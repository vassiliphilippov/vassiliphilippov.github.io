break even

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈivən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈiːvən / (BrE)
to reach a point where neither a profit nor a loss is made; to have gains equal to losses.

Examples

Tonight at 8 o'clock the Cardinals, who gave the Pirates as much trouble as anyone did in 1960, breaking even with them, will get their first 1961 shot at baseball's world champions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profit - to make a financial gain
  • Antonym: lose money - to experience a financial loss

2. VERB

/ breɪk ˈivən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈiːvən / (BrE)
to have no profit or loss at the end of a financial period; to earn enough money to cover costs

Examples

In many cases that statement "We break even on our downtown operation and make money on our branches "would be turned around if the cost analysis were recalculated on terms less prejudicial to the old store.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profit - financial gain after covering all expenses
  • Antonym: lose money - to experience financial loss in business or investment
