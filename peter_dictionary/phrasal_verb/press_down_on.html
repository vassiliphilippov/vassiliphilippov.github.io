press down on

1. VERB

/ prɛs daʊn ɑn / (AmE), / prɛs daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
To apply force to something from above, making it move down or feel increased pressure.

Examples

The burden of his secret was pressing down on him, as it was on Lieutenant Beckstrom and his six enlisted men.

Synset Examples

The snow bore down on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear down on - to move toward with pressing force or determination
  • Antonym: lift off - to remove pressure or weight from
