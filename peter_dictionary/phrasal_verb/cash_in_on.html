cash in on

1. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to take advantage of a situation or opportunity, especially to make money or gain a benefit

Examples

How can you cash in on this fast-growing type of outlet and still maintain relationships with older existing outlets which are still important?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalize on - to use something to one's advantage for gain
  • Synonym: exploit - to make full use of or benefit from, often selfishly

Etymology

From the verb 'cash in' (to exchange for cash) + 'on', figuratively extended to exploiting opportunities for gain.
