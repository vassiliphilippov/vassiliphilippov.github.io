impinge on

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɪndʒ ɑn / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒ ɒn / (BrE)
to hit or strike against something; to come into sudden contact with something

Examples

Heat of condensation (work function) plus kinetic energy of the electrons impinging on the anode.
Although he pointed out that mandatory legislation impinging on home rule is basically distasteful, he added that the vital interest in election results transcended county lines.

Synset Examples

The car hit a tree
He struck the table with his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • Synonym: collide - to come into forceful contact with something

Etymology

From Latin *impingere*, meaning 'to strike against,' from *in-* (upon) + *pingere* (to fasten).
