seal off

1. VERB

/ siːl ɔf / (AmE), / siːl ɒf / (BrE)
to close or make something airtight or watertight, especially to prevent the passage of gas, liquid, or people

Examples

Purified inactive chlorine was then added from one of the tubes described above and the mixture frozen out and sealed off in a flask equipped with a break seal.
The desired amounts of inactive chlorine and radioactive chlorine were likewise condensed in these cells on the vacuum line following which they were frozen down and the manifold as a whole was sealed off.
The reactants for the gas phase experiments were first frozen out in a side arm attached to the manifold and then allowed to distill slowly into the manifold of pre-cooled reaction cells before sealing off.

Synset Examples

seal the windows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close off - to block or prevent access to a place

2. VERB

/ siːl ɔf / (AmE), / siːl ɒf / (BrE)
to close an area, building, or container tightly to prevent entry, exit, or movement into or out of it, especially for security or safety reasons

Examples

A clot forms at the site, seals off the flow of blood to the heart and provokes a heart attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockade - to block or seal off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving

3. VERB

/ siːl ɔf / (AmE), / siːl ɒf / (BrE)
to prevent people or things from entering or leaving an area by blocking all access to it

Examples

Sealed off, brick by brick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockade - to block or seal off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving
  • Antonym: open up - to remove barriers so that access is possible
