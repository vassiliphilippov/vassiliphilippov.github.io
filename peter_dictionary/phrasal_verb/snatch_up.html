snatch up

1. VERB

/ snætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / snætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to seize or grasp something quickly and eagerly

Examples

Then he was striding across the room, his thoughts confused but the worry building swiftly inside him as he snatched up the note.

Synset Examples

Before I could stop him the dog snatched the ham bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take or seize suddenly
  • Synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
