come after

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to occur later than something; to happen as a result or consequence of something else

Examples

The announcement came after a period of sharp deterioration in East-West relations.
What comes after Trujillo is now the puzzle.

Synset Examples

A terrible tsunami followed the earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follow - to come or go after in time, sequence, or order
  • Antonym: precede - to come before in time or order
