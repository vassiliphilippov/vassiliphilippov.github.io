drag out

1. VERB

/ dræɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dræɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something last longer than necessary; to prolong or extend the duration of something, often tiresomely or unnecessarily

Examples

He stopped every few minutes and leaned on his shovel as he studied the horizon, but nothing happened, each day dragging out with monotonous calm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolong - to make something continue longer than usual or necessary
  • Synonym: extend - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: shorten - to make something shorter in duration
