jot down

1. VERB

/ dʒɑt daʊn / (AmE), / dʒɒt daʊn / (BrE)
To write something quickly or briefly, especially as a reminder or note.

Examples

When she came back Eugenia was sitting at the kitchen table with a pencil and envelope jotting down words and figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note down - write something quickly so as not to forget it
  • Synonym: scribble - write carelessly or hurriedly

Etymology

From 'jot' (to write quickly), itself from Latin 'iota', the smallest letter in the Greek alphabet, indicating a very small amount.
