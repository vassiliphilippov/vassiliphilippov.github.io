come forward

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / kʌm ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to offer yourself or volunteer for a task or to provide information, usually in a public or official context

Examples

One night, at the close of the evening service, he came forward, left his resentment at the altar and gave his heart to God.

Synset Examples

Young people should step to the fore and help their peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volunteer - to offer to do something without being forced or paid
  • Synonym: step forward - to come forward or offer oneself

2. VERB

/ kʌm ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / kʌm ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to make oneself known, especially by offering help, information, or to volunteer for something

Examples

Will he come forward and be sworn, sir?

Synset Examples

Young people should step to the fore and help their peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step forward - to move or present oneself to offer help or information
