win back

1. VERB

/ wɪn bæk / (AmE), / wɪn bæk / (BrE)
to recover or regain something or someone that was lost, often through effort or persuasion

Examples

By reducing rates as much as 60 per cent, it and its associated railroads hope to win back some of the business they have lost to truckers and barge lines.
It will early know why the party won n't win back city votes.

Synset Examples

We got back the money after we threatened to sue the company
He got back his son from the kidnappers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to get back something that was lost or taken away
  • Antonym: lose - to no longer have something because it has been taken away or destroyed
