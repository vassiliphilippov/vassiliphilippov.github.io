live with

1. VERB

/ lɪv wɪð / (AmE), / lɪv wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or tolerate something, especially something unpleasant or difficult, and adjust to it

Examples

I gotta live with that.

Synset Examples

I shall have to accept these unpleasant working conditions
I swallowed the insult
She has learned to live with her husband's little idiosyncrasies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accept - to receive or agree to something willingly
  • Synonym: tolerate - to allow the existence or occurrence of something without interference
  • Synonym: put up with - to tolerate or endure (something)
