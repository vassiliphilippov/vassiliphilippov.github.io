bug out

1. VERB

/ bʌɡ aʊt / (AmE), / bʌɡ aʊt / (BrE)
(of eyes) to bulge or stick out, typically as a reaction of surprise, fear, or excitement.

Examples

The lady's eyes, unmagnified, bugged out.

Synset Examples

His eyes popped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pop out - to protrude or bulge outward suddenly (of eyes, e.g. in surprise)
  • Antonym: sink in - (of eyes) to recede or become less prominent, the opposite of bulge out

Etymology

From the noun 'bug' (insect), used in American English slang to mean 'protrude' in reference to the prominent eyes of some insects.
