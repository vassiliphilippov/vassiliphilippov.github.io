hike up

1. VERB

/ haɪk ʌp / (AmE), / haɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to pull or lift up (especially a piece of clothing) quickly or sharply

Examples

Maude swooped up the cup and hiked up her top hoop as if about to take off with a racing start.

Synset Examples

He hitched up his socks and pants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hitch up - to pull up or tighten (something, especially clothing)
