edge up

1. VERB

/ ɛdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / ɛdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to move gradually or carefully into a space, often by pushing or maneuvering oneself

Examples

In the meantime, six sandals, stained an ocher, the same color as Pompeii's shaved hair, edged up close to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge in - to move gradually into or toward something, especially in a confined space
  • Synonym: squeeze in - to force oneself or something into a limited space
