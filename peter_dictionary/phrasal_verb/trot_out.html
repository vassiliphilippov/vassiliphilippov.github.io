trot out

1. VERB

/ trɑt aʊt / (AmE), / trɒt aʊt / (BrE)
to produce or display something, especially repeatedly or in a way that lacks originality, usually for inspection, discussion, or admiration

Examples

"Poor Mr. Thynne, he always has to be trotted out for my encouragement ".

Synset Examples

His novel trots out a rich heiress
always able to trot out some new excuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show off - to display or exhibit something proudly
  • Synonym: parade - to display something in a conspicuous way, often to impress others

Etymology

From 'trot' (to move briskly) + 'out'; figuratively extended to mean 'bring out to be seen'.
