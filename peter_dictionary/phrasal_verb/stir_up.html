stir up

1. VERB

/ stɪr ʌp / (AmE), / stɜː ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something, such as dust, water, or animals, to rise, move, or become active by disturbing the surface or surroundings

Examples

A red-tailed hawk flew in behind them and stayed there, watching for any snakes or rabbits that they might stir up from the side of the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rouse - to cause to become active; awaken

2. VERB

/ stɝː ʌp / (AmE), / stɜː ʌp / (BrE)
to deliberately provoke or incite strong feelings, trouble, or action in a group of people

Examples

The crisis was artificially stirred up by the Kremlin (Wall Street) and the Red Army (Pentagon) egged on by the West Germans (East Germans).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incite - to encourage or stir up (violent or unlawful behavior)
  • Antonym: calm down - to make a situation or feelings less intense; to reduce agitation

3. VERB

/ stɝ ʌp / (AmE), / stə ʌp / (BrE)
to arouse or excite strong feelings, emotions, or passions in someone or among a group

Examples

Evidently the war drum beating and hysteria so painstakingly being stirred up in the West have been planned long in advance.

Synset Examples

The ostentatious way of living of the rich ignites the hatred of the poor
The refugees' fate stirred up compassion around the world
Wake old feelings of hatred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke - to cause a strong emotional reaction, especially anger
  • Antonym: calm down - to make or become less excited or agitated
