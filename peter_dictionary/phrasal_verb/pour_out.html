pour out

1. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to make a liquid or substance flow out of a container, especially by tipping or emptying it

Examples

But is that not like going to a chemistry laboratory and blindly pouring out liquids and powders from an array of bottles and then, after stirring, expecting a new wonder drug inevitably to result?

Synset Examples

the sommelier decanted the wines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decant - to gradually pour liquid from one container into another, especially without disturbing the sediment

2. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to make a liquid flow from a container by tipping or holding it so the contents move out

Examples

She had swished away, she had been gone for a long time probably when Sarah suddenly realized that she ought to stop her, pour out the coffee, so no one would drink it.

Synset Examples

effused brine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pour - to cause liquid to flow; to flow rapidly

3. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to express (feelings or thoughts) freely and without holding back, often in a flood of words or emotions.

Examples

The judge listened quietly as the young woman poured out her frustrations - then discussing with her the possibility of seeking aid from Family Service before going to a lawyer.
On the anniversary of her father's death she poured out with agonized tears her feelings of guilt about not having attended his funeral.

Synset Examples

The woman poured out her frustrations as the judge listened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vent - to give free expression to (emotion), especially anger or frustration

4. VERB

/ pɔr aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to flow forth or be emitted in large quantities, especially suddenly or forcefully; to be disgorged.

Examples

The sail, ragged though it was, still had enough surface to catch some of the ocean of power being poured out from the nursery stars.

Synset Examples

The crowds spilled out into the streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill out - to flow or be disgorged from a container
