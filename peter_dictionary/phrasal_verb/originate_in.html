originate in

1. VERB

/ əˈrɪdʒ.əˌneɪt ɪn / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.neɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to have as a source, starting point, or place of origin; to come from a particular place or situation.

Examples

Recent interferometer measurements (Radhakrishnan and Roberts, 1960) have shown the 960 - Mc emission of Jupiter to be partially polarized and to originate in a region of larger diameter than the visible disk.

2. VERB

/ əˈrɪdʒəˌneɪt ɪn / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to have as a source or starting point; to begin or arise in a particular place, situation, or from something

Examples

Do you hear the words coming from my mouth?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derive from - to take or receive from a specific source

Etymology

From Latin 'originari' (to rise, begin), from 'origo' (beginning, source) + English preposition 'in'.
