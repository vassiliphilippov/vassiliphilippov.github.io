run out

1. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
To leave a place suddenly and quickly, usually in a hurry or abruptly.

Examples

"I'm mad ", shouted Payne, as he ran out into the hall.
He picked up the powder canister and ran out.
It was so pretty and artless that she felt like a child again and would have enjoyed running out barefoot to play on the wet grass with all the growing things, but Doaty never permitted bare feet and she was decidedly not a child but une femme d' un certain age.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - to move or leave suddenly and quickly
  • Synonym: rush out - to leave a place quickly or in a hurry

2. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
(of a supply or resource) to be used up or finished so that none remains.

Examples

But time is running out, and many of Stravinsky's admirers begin to fear that he will never find terra firma.
Red Hogan's patience ran out.
I saw that I would soon run out of buildings at this rate, so I decided to take another measure - the whole state of Pennsylvania.
I had been among the top third in my class at N. Y. U., had wanted desperately to go to medical school, but I 'd run out of money and energy at the same time.
We ran out of money and we have n't eaten for two days ".

Synset Examples

Our supplies finally ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplete - to use up the supply or resources of something
  • Synonym: exhaust - to use up completely or drain of resources
  • Antonym: replenish - to fill something up again

3. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
(of something such as a period of time, contract, document, etc.) to expire or cease to be valid.

Examples

She did n't want to be the only one with a stove in her room, especially as her life span was nearly run out anyway, and she insisted that Hope have the heater.

Synset Examples

My passports expired last month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expire - cease to be valid, typically of documents, contracts, etc.

4. VERB

/ rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
to come to an end because nothing is left; to become used up or exhausted

Examples

You're out some smokes, son.

Synset Examples

Our supplies finally ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expire - to come to an end (of validity or duration)
  • Synonym: finish - to use up completely
  • Degree Lower: run short - to have less than is needed; nearly used up
