take down

1. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
To move something or someone from a higher to a lower position.

Examples

Each brass handle and hinge shone for his reward, and he knew how to get at the dust in the china flowers and how to take down the long glass drops which hung from the chandelier.
They love to dust, scrub, polish, wax floors, move the furniture around from place to place, take down the curtains, put up new ones and have themselves a real ball.
He had taken a carbine down from the wall and it trailed from his hand, the stock bumping on the wood floor.

Synset Examples

take down the vase from the shelf

Related Terms

  • Antonym: put up - move something to a higher position

2. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
To write something down; to make a written record of something spoken or observed.

Examples

You taking this **down**, new fish?

Synset Examples

she noted everything the teacher said that morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - write something down; record
  • Synonym: jot down - write down quickly
  • Synonym: write down - put something in writing; record

3. VERB

/ teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
To move something or someone from a higher to a lower position.

Examples

Take that tub of shit **down** to the infirmary.

Synset Examples

take down the vase from the shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lower - move something to a lower position
