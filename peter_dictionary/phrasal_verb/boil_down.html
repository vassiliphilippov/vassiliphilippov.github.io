boil down

1. VERB

/ bɔɪl daʊn / (AmE), / bɔɪl daʊn / (BrE)
(of an issue, argument, etc.) To be reducible to or summarized as the most important or essential aspect.

Examples

This is an issue which boils down to a matter of opinion, depending on whether you 're an American or National fan and anti or pro Yankee.

Synset Examples

The proposal boils down to a compromise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum up - to give the main points or summarize

Etymology

Metaphorical use of the literal 'boil down' (to condense by boiling) in the late 19th century, applied to ideas or arguments.
