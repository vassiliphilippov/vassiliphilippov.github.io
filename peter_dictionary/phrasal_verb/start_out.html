start out

1. VERB

/ stɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / stɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place or begin a journey, especially at the beginning of an activity, journey, or process

Examples

Once she has made up her mind, the queen starts out by constructing, in her chosen abode, a small "floor "of dried grass or some woolly material.
Winston enjoyed seeing him start out; he wore his clothes with style.

Synset Examples

The family took off for Florida

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set out - to begin a journey or course of action
  • Synonym: depart - to leave, especially in order to start a journey
  • Antonym: arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey

2. VERB

/ stɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / stɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
to begin a course of action or a journey; to take the first step toward doing something

Examples

It had n't started out that way.
The year will probably start out with segregation still the most troublesome issue.
The new President is in no position to start out his dealings with Moscow by issuing callable bluffs.
And trailers for boats are not what they started out to be ten years ago.
He tells of three men who started out on a trip across a single paddock, a ten-by-ten-mile square owned by a sheep grazer.

Synset Examples

We began working at dawn
Who will start?
Get working as soon as the sun rises!
The first tourists began to arrive in Cambodia
He began early in the day
Let's get down to work now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begin - to start doing something; to take the first step
  • Synonym: commence - to begin; to start something formally
  • Antonym: finish - to bring to an end; complete
