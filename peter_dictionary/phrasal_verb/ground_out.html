ground out

1. VERB

/ ɡraʊnd aʊt / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd aʊt / (BrE)
(in baseball) to be put out after hitting the ball on the ground, typically resulting in a throw to first base before the batter reaches it.

Examples

After Al Paschal grounded out, Jay Cooke walked and Jim McDaniel singled home McAuliffe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strike out - be put out after three strikes in baseball
