dole out

1. VERB

/ doʊl aʊt / (AmE), / dəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something, especially money or food, in small amounts to several people

Examples

She doled out what Glendora vaguely guessed were the right amounts of dried peas, eggs, cornmeal, a little salt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribute - to give out shares or portions of something

Etymology

From the verb 'dole' (to give out in charity), from Middle English 'dol', meaning portion or share.

2. VERB

/ doʊl aʊt / (AmE), / dəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something, especially in small quantities, often to several people

Examples

But instead of delivering the ration - either in actual commodities or in cash - at intervals of perhaps two weeks or a month, the Belgians felt obliged to dole it out more often.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present
dole out some money
shell out pocket money for the children
deal a blow to someone
the machine dispenses soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribute - to give out or spread something to people
  • Synonym: dispense - to give out or provide (especially something official or medical)

Etymology

From the noun 'dole', meaning a portion or share given out, itself from Middle English, from Old English 'dāl' (part, share).
