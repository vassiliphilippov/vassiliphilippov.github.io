back down

1. VERB

/ bæk daʊn / (AmE), / bæk daʊn / (BrE)
to withdraw from a stance, demand, or commitment, especially after initially insisting on it; to concede or yield in an argument or confrontation

Examples

The smoke from that chimney rose as sluggishly as smoke from any other, and hung as sadly in the drizzle, creeping back down along the sopping canvas of the roof.
During the next five years the leaders of the Fair Deal reluctantly backed down from the optimistic expectations of the New Deal.

Synset Examples

The bully had to back down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give in - to cease to resist or yield
  • Antonym: stand firm - to refuse to change a decision or position
