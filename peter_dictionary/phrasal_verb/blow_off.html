blow off

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ˈɔf / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈɒf / (BrE)
(of part of something) to come loose and be forcefully detached due to an explosion or sudden strong force.

Examples

Though Bob Carroll seemed to have had his head practically blown off by the exploding grenade, he lived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear off - to remove something by force
