jump off

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɔf / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒf / (BrE)
(of a person, group, or event) to begin quickly and often successfully.

Examples

The Pirates jumped off to an 11 - 3 start by May 1 last year, when the Redbirds as well as the Dodgers held them even over the season.

Synset Examples

The freshman jumped off to a good start in his math class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get off to a good start - to make a good beginning; to start successfully
