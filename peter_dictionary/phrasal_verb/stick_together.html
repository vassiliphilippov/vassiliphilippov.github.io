stick together

1. VERB

/ stɪk təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / stɪk təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
(of people) to remain united and support each other, especially during difficulty or adversity.

Examples

Those O'Dwyers had that Irish clannishness that made them stick together in spite of politics and everything.

Synset Examples

The two families stuck together throughout the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand by - to support or remain loyal to someone
  • Antonym: split up - to separate or stop being united
