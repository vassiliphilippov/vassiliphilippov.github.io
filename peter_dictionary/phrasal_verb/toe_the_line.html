toe the line

1. VERB

/ toʊ ðə laɪn / (AmE), / təʊ ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to conform to rules or standards, or do what is expected by authority

Examples

They 'll toe the line.
How could he exert authority over them - make them toe the line, as he had to - if he knuckled under to this small-town clown?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conform - to behave according to socially accepted conventions or standards
  • Antonym: rebel - to resist authority or control; to act against rules

Etymology

Originates from the practice of lining up with one's toes against a line, especially in military or athletic contexts.
