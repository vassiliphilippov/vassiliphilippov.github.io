run off

1. VERB

/ rʌn ɒf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
To force someone or something to leave a place, often using threats or pressure.

Examples

My father ran him off here six years ago ".

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase away - to force someone or something to leave quickly
  • Synonym: drive away - to make someone or something leave a place or stop being involved
  • Antonym: invite - to ask someone to come, the opposite of forcing away

2. VERB

/ rʌn ɔf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place or person suddenly and secretly, often taking something or someone with you

Examples

Max, in a fit of despair, takes Alicia and runs off for two marvelous weeks in Burbank (Fink calls it "the most wonderful and lovely fourteen days in my whole life "), at the end of which Alicia tragically contracts Parkinson's disease and dies.
He had the feeling that he should abandon the car and run off somewhere to hide.

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection
  • Synonym: flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger

3. VERB

/ rʌn ɔf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
to leave suddenly and unexpectedly, especially in a hurry or secretly

Examples

Then she continued with energy, "I myself did not see her until a week after she had run off to find the father.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - to leave suddenly and quickly, often to escape something
  • Synonym: flee - to run away from a place or danger quickly
  • Antonym: linger - to remain or stay in a place longer than necessary

4. VERB

/ rʌn ɔf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and quickly, often unexpectedly or without telling others

Examples

And she runs off.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - to move or run away suddenly and quickly
  • Synonym: dash off - to leave or depart quickly and suddenly
  • Antonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary

Synset Examples

6. VERB

/ rʌn ɔf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
To leave secretly with someone you love, especially to get married without permission or announcement.

Examples

And without so much as an apology... ...you ran off with that sideshow phony.

Synset Examples

The young couple eloped and got married in Las Vegas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elope - to run away secretly in order to get married, especially without parental consent
