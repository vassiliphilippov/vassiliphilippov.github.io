rear back

1. VERB

/ rɪr bæk / (AmE), / rɪə bæk / (BrE)
(of an animal) to rise upright on the hind legs, especially suddenly; also, to suddenly move one's body backward, often in surprise or fear.

Examples

He is apt to rear back and claim his rights.

Synset Examples

the frightened horse reared back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recoil - to suddenly draw back, especially in fear, surprise, or disgust
