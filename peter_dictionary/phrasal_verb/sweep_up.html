sweep up

1. VERB

/ swip ʌp / (AmE), / swiːp ʌp / (BrE)
To become involved or carried along by a situation or events, often without intending to be.

Examples

Everyone in Salem, saint and sinner alike, is swept up by it.

Synset Examples

They were swept up by the events
don't drag me into this business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroil - to involve someone deeply in an argument, conflict, or situation
  • Synonym: entangle - to become caught up in something, especially a difficult situation
