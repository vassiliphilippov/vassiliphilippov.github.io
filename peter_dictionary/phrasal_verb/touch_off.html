touch off

1. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something sudden to begin, especially violence, trouble, or a strong reaction; to trigger or ignite.

Examples

On a military mission for his native Virginia the youthful George Washington touched off the French and Indian War, then guarded his colony's frontier as head of its militia.
That touched off a total stampede.
A sizable supply of powder had been touched off.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - to cause something to start or happen suddenly
  • Synonym: set off - to cause a device, process, or event to start
  • Synonym: ignite - to cause something to start burning or to begin suddenly
