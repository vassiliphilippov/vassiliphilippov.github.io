draw off

1. VERB

/ drɔ ɑf / (AmE), / drɔː ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling or drawing it away, especially a covering or layer

Examples

Angelina placed the tray on the table and with a flick of dark wrist drew off the cloth.

Synset Examples

She placed the tray down and drew off the cloth
draw away the cloth that is covering the cheese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
  • Synonym: strip off - to remove a layer or covering from something, usually by pulling
  • Antonym: put on - to place something on something else, especially clothing or a covering (opposite in context)
