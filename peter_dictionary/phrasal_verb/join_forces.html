join forces

1. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
to work together with another person or group in order to achieve a shared goal

Examples

Sam Spade joins forces with a band of adventurers in search of a priceless jeweled statue of a falcon; but when the bird is found at last, it turns out to be a fake.
Contrarily, Republican "volunteers "go their separate ways, and thus far have given no indication that they 'd be willing to join forces under a single directorate, except in the most loose-knit fashion.

Synset Examples

The soprano and the pianist did not get together very well
We joined forces with another research group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperate - to work together with someone for a common purpose
  • Synonym: collaborate - to work jointly with others, especially in an intellectual endeavor
  • Antonym: go separate ways - to stop working together and pursue individual goals
