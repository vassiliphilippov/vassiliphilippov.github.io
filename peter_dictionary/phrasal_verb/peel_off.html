peel off

1. VERB

/ pil ɔf / (AmE), / piːl ɒf / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering or layer from something, or to come away in a layer or strip

Examples

Andrei peeled off five one-thousand-zloty notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - to remove all coverings or possessions from something
  • Synonym: unpeel - to remove the peel or outer layer from

2. VERB

/ pil ɑf / (AmE), / piːl ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something, such as a layer of clothing or a covering, especially with some effort or difficulty
