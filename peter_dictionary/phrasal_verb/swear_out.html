swear out

1. VERB

/ swɛr aʊt / (AmE), / swɛə aʊt / (BrE)
(of a legal authority or private citizen) to formally obtain or apply for the issuance of a warrant, summons, or similar legal document by making a sworn statement to a court or magistrate.

Examples

The sheriff and District Attorney Mills hastily swore out a number of warrants against men who had been riding about armed, according to signed statements by Chavez and Dr. I. P. George, and ordered Deputy Barney Clark of Raton to rescue the posseman.

Synset Examples

He was processed by the sheriff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: issue - to officially provide or distribute a legal document
