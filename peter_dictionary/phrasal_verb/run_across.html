run across

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / rʌn əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to meet or find someone or something by chance

Examples

I found a trooper once the Apache had spread-eagled on an ant hill, and another time we ran across some teamsters they 'd caught, tied upside down on their own wagon wheels over little fires until their brains was exploded right out o' their skulls.
They ran across Mr. Rayburn and the youngster expressed a desire to get the Speaker's autograph.

Synset Examples

I'll probably see you at the meeting
How nice to see you again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come across - to find or meet by chance
