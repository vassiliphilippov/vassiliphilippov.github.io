about-face

1. VERB

/ əˈbaʊt ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / əˈbaʊt ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
To turn so as to face the opposite direction, typically by turning 180 degrees, especially on command.

Examples

Killpath peered through half closed lids at his reflection, thrust up his chin in a gesture of satisfaction and about-faced.
The steady roll of the drums had sounded behind him as he walked between the endless ranks of the men he had commanded, and each man about-faced and turned his back as the officer approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn around - to move so as to face the opposite direction

Etymology

From the military command 'about face', meaning to turn and face the opposite direction.
