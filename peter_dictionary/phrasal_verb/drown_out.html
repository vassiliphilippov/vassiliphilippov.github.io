drown out

1. VERB

/ draʊn aʊt / (AmE), / draʊn aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent a sound from being heard by making a louder noise

Examples

The beehive voices, for no one could bear silence, drowned out the sound of Mrs. Lincoln's weeping.
"It does n't drown out the dialogue ", I explained.

Synset Examples

The noise from the ice machine drowned out the music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overpower - to be so strong or loud as to dominate others
