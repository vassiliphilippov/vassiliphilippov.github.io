sink in

1. VERB

/ sɪŋk ɪn / (AmE), / sɪŋk ɪn / (BrE)
(of a fact, idea, or feeling) to become fully understood or absorbed in one's mind or emotions.

Examples

At last, sitting there, in the familiar surroundings, the truth began to sink in.
But I want this to sink in awhile.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - (of a fact or idea) to be understood or noticed
