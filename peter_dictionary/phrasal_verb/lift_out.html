lift out

1. VERB

/ lɪft aʊt / (AmE), / lɪft aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from a place by lifting it, especially when it is among or inside other things

Examples

He pulled it over, climbed up, and lifted out the big volume, almost losing his balance from the weight of it.

Synset Examples

scoop the sugar out of the container

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take out - to remove something from a place
  • Antonym: put in - to place something inside another thing
