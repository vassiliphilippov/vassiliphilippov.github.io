branch out

1. VERB

/ bræntʃ aʊt / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to expand or extend one's activities, interests, or business into new areas; to diversify

Examples

The lyricist's father was a lawyer who had branched out into real estate.

Synset Examples

The company diversified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diversify - to make or become more varied or diverse

Etymology

From the noun 'branch' + 'out', figuratively referring to the way a tree develops new branches; used idiomatically since the late 19th century.
