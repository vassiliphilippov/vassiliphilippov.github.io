go about

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to begin to deal with or do something, especially in a particular way

Examples

The men did not object to his sketching them while they went about their work, but no one could be persuaded to come to his studio to pose.
But, how does one go about the job of finding and destroying mobile forces?
"You 'd like to hear how I go about it ".

Synset Examples

approach a task
go about a difficult problem
approach a new project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set about - to begin doing something with energy or determination
  • Synonym: tackle - to make a determined effort to deal with a problem or task
