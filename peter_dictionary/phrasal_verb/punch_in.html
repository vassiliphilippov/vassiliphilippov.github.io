punch in

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ ɪn / (AmE), / pʌntʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to record the time of one's arrival at work, especially by using a time clock or similar device.

Examples

It was an awkward hour, but I did n't have to punch any time clock, and it only meant that sometimes I had to stay a couple of hours later at the drawing board to finish up a job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clock in - to record the time you arrive at work
  • Antonym: punch out - to record the time you leave work

Etymology

From 'punch' (to press or strike), referring to operating a punch card time clock, plus the adverb 'in.'
