sneak out

1. VERB

/ sniːk aʊt / (AmE), / sniːk aʊt / (BrE)
To leave a place quietly and secretly, in order not to be noticed.

Examples

As it was, while Central wrangled among itself, Robert Tanner sneaked out of the pilots' barracks, out to the field and over to Helva's slim metal hull.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip away - leave quietly or secretly
