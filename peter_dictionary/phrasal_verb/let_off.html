let off

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɑf / (AmE), / lɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to excuse someone from a duty, punishment, or obligation; to allow someone to avoid something they would normally have to do or face

Examples

They went free that very day!

Synset Examples

Please excuse me from this class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - to release from a duty or punishment
  • Antonym: punish - to impose a penalty or negative consequence
