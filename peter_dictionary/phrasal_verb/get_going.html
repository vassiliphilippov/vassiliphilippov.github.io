get going

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to begin to move or to start doing something; to set something in motion

Examples

"Well, let's get going ", she said impatiently.
I figure if I can get any kind of publicity campaign going, I 'll land him on TV - you know, one of those favorite horses for some Western hero.

Synset Examples

I start at eight in the morning
Ready, set, go!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin or set out on an activity
  • Antonym: stop - to cease moving or doing something
