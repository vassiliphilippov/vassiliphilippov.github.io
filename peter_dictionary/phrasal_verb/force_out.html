force out

1. VERB

/ fɔrs aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːs aʊt / (BrE)
To make someone leave a place, especially by law, authority, or pressure.

Examples

They tell me that within a week, you'’re going to move Klingman **out**.

Synset Examples

The landlord evicted the tenants after they had not paid the rent for four months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evict - to lawfully expel someone from property
  • Synonym: expel - to force someone to leave a place
