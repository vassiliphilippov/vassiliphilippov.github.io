move in on

1. VERB

/ muːv ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / muːv ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to approach someone or something in a way that is often intrusive, threatening, or encroaching.

Examples

They moved in on him, crowded him from all sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - to press closely together; to encroach upon
  • Antonym: back off - to move away to avoid intruding or threatening
