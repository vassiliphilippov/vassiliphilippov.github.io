go over

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an event, idea, etc.) to be received or happen in a particular way; to have a specified outcome, especially in terms of approval or acceptance.

Examples

My host went over and stared out the window at his peacocks; then he turned to me.
That might go over.

Synset Examples

how did your talk go over?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be received - to be accepted or responded to (in a certain way)

2. VERB

/ goʊ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / gəʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to examine or check something carefully in order to determine its accuracy, quality, or condition

Examples

He and the prime minister would be back from the window, seated at Mahzeer's desk; they would be going over papers Mahzeer had saved as excuse for just such a meeting.

Synset Examples

check the brakes
Check out the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check - to examine something to ensure accuracy or condition
  • Synonym: inspect - to examine something closely
  • Synonym: review - to assess or examine again or in detail

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to fall forward and down, often from a standing or upright position

Examples

The man went over without sound, falling to the bare floor.

Synset Examples

The old woman went over without a sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topple - to become unsteady and fall down
  • Synonym: fall down - to lose balance and drop to the ground

4. VERB

/ goʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to examine or review something carefully in order to check its quality, accuracy, or condition

Examples

I hope you don't mind the way I keep going over this Barzini business.

Synset Examples

check the brakes
Check out the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check - to examine so as to determine accuracy, quality, or condition
  • Synonym: inspect - to look at something closely to assess its condition or quality
