scoop out

1. VERB

/ skuːp aʊt / (AmE), / skuːp aʊt / (BrE)
to remove the inside of something, or make something hollow, by using a scoop or similar tool

Examples

If your pool is located on or near sloping ground, it may have natural drainage which is certainly more desirable than to be faced with the annual expense and labor of first pumping out the water and then scooping out all the debris.

Synset Examples

scoop out a melon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollow out - to make something hollow by removing the inside
