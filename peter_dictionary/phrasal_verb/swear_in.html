swear in

1. VERB

/ swɛr ɪn / (AmE), / swɛə ɪn / (BrE)
to administer an official oath to someone, especially when they take up an office or duty

Examples

On Friday he will go to Portland for the swearing in of Dean Bryson as Multnomah County Circuit Judge.

Synset Examples

The speaker of the House swore in the new President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaugurate - formally admit someone to office

Etymology

From 'swear' (to make a solemn statement or promise), combined with 'in' to denote entering into a position under oath.
