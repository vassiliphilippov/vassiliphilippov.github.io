shut up

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to tell someone to stop talking or making noise, often abruptly or forcefully

Examples

"Shut up.

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be quiet - to make little or no noise; to stop talking
  • Synonym: silence - to cause to become silent or not to speak

2. ADJ

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
To be or become closely confined, locked, or kept inside a place so that escape or movement is restricted.

Examples

The women, keeping their voices low as they worked around the house or sat in the living room, sounded like chickens shut up in a coop for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits; limit, restrict
  • Antonym: release - to set someone or something free from confinement

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'shut up', meaning to close something completely so nothing can enter or leave.

3. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
(of a person) to stop talking or make someone stop talking, often used as an impolite command.

Examples

"You shut up!
"If it gave me pleasure to say hard things ", he wrote, "I would shut up forever ".

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be quiet - to stop making noise or talking
  • Antonym: speak up - to speak more loudly or express opinions openly

4. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
To confine someone or something in a place so that escape is impossible; to lock in.

Examples

Whoever it was had meant to shut him up in here, had followed him and waited till the courthouse and square were deserted.

Synset Examples

The parents locked her daughter up for the weekend
She locked her jewels in the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confine - keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: let out - to allow to leave a place; release

5. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
To tell someone to stop talking or to be quiet, often in a brusque or rude manner.

Examples

Shut up!

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be quiet - to stop talking or making noise
  • Synonym: hush - to make someone be silent or quieter

6. VERB

/ ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
To stop talking or to tell someone to stop talking, often in a forceful or rude way.

Examples

You're a real nice girl and I like you a lot, but right now, shut up.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be quiet - To stop talking or make less noise
  • Antonym: speak up - To begin talking or speak more loudly
