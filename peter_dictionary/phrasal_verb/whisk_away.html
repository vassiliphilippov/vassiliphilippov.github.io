whisk away

1. VERB

/ wɪsk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɪsk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To remove or take someone or something away quickly and suddenly.

Examples

He shares with Mr. Morse a parody of the college anthems he once sang while his second song is whisked away from him by Virginia Martin, a girl with a remarkably expressive yip in her voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snatch - to seize something quickly and suddenly

Etymology

From the verb 'whisk' (to move briskly or quickly) + 'away'.
