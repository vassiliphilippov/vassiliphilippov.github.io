play up

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ʌp / (AmE), / pleɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to emphasize or make something appear more important, noticeable, or prominent than it actually is

Examples

The West Berlin crisis is being played up artificially because it is needed by the United States to justify its arms drive ".

Synset Examples

The introduction highlighted the speaker's distinguished career in linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or be more noticeable
  • Antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
