run around

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(especially of children or animals) to play in an energetic, noisy, and lively way, moving from place to place.

Examples

Four or five of the cousins from East Texas were about his age, so naturally they ran around together.
All they could think of was to run around in circles, kicking their legs out.
"Running around in the moonlight almost naked and slugging a man with a rock "?

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and behave in a lively, energetic way
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly and energetically
