pass through

1. VERB

/ pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
To travel or move through a place on the way to somewhere else, often without stopping for long.

Examples

At 2130 hours they had passed through the barbed wire at the point of departure.

Synset Examples

The tourists moved through the town and bought up all the souvenirs;
Some travelers pass through the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass by - to go past something without stopping or interacting
  • Synonym: go through - to move into and across something

3. VERB

/ pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
to move through or go across something, often referring to an obstacle, barrier, or line, especially in a military context.

Examples

He moved only about 30 feet after the 240 - grain slug hit him - and this was after the bullet had passed through a sapling.

Related Terms
