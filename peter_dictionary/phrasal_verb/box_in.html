box in

1. VERB

/ bɑks ɪn / (AmE), / bɒks ɪn / (BrE)
To enclose or confine someone or something so that movement or options are restricted, as if inside a box.

Examples

The Senator had boxed them in with their own restrictions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - to catch or confine someone so they cannot escape
  • Antonym: free - to release from confinement or restriction
