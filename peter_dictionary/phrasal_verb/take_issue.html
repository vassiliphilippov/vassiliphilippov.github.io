take issue

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / teɪk ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
to disagree or express opposition to something or someone

Examples

I must, in fairness, take issue with his premise, primarily because the so-called scandals in labor unions were very much connected with business scandals.

Synset Examples

I beg to differ!
She disagrees with her husband on many questions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or be in accord
  • Synonym: disagree - to have a different opinion or not agree
