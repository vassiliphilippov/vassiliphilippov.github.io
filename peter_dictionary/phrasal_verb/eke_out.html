eke out

1. VERB

/ ik aʊt / (AmE), / iːk aʊt / (BrE)
to make a supply or resource last longer by using or adding to it frugally, often when it is scarce; to supplement something deemed insufficient.

Examples

(It makes no real difference that Braque's collage is on paper and eked out in charcoal, while Picasso's is on canvas and eked out in oil.)
(It makes no real difference that Braque's collage is on paper and eked out in charcoal, while Picasso's is on canvas and eked out in oil.)

Synset Examples

He eked out his meager pay by giving private lessons
Braque eked out his collages with charcoal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplement - to add an extra element to something, especially to make up for a deficiency

Etymology

From Old English *ēcan* (to increase), related to 'eke' meaning to add.
