zip up

1. VERB

/ zɪp ʌp / (AmE), / zɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to close something, especially an item of clothing, with a zipper

Examples

The fat man removed his apron, put on a greasy and wrinkled jacket, and zipped it over his paunch.

Synset Examples

Zip up your jacket--it's cold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unzip - to open something by undoing a zipper
