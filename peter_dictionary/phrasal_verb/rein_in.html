rein in

1. VERB

/ reɪn ɪn / (AmE), / reɪn ɪn / (BrE)
to stop or slow down a horse or oneself by pulling on the reins; to cause to slow or halt movement, especially by control or restraint

Examples

They reined in before the town marshal's office, a box sized building on Main Street.
They reined in there, Brannon remaining in the saddle while Hogan went to look for Jesse Macklin in the hotel dining room.

Synset Examples

They reined in in front of the post office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrain - to hold back from action; keep in check or under control

Etymology

From the noun 'rein', from Old French 'resne', from Latin 'retinere' (to hold back).
