hunker down

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋkər daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə daʊn / (BrE)
to lower your body by bending your knees and sitting close to your heels, often to wait, take shelter, or prepare for something difficult

Examples

Then he hunkered down on the heels of his handmade boots, peered into the orderly chaos of axle, shock absorber, and spring.

Synset Examples

In some cultures, the women give birth while squatting
The children hunkered down to protect themselves from the sandstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - to crouch close to the ground by bending the knees
  • Antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet

Etymology

Origin of "hunker" uncertain, but possibly from Scottish dialect.
