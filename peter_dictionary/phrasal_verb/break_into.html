break into

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / breɪk ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to suddenly begin to do something, especially to express or utter something spontaneously, such as starting to sing, laugh, cry, etc.

Examples

He almost broke into tears.
The mouth frequently breaks into a disarming smile.
Everywhere he went in town, people sidled up, gave him the guttural bit or broke into a frightening Tarzan yodel.

Synset Examples

break into a yodel
break into a song
break into tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst into - to suddenly begin to produce or display (something, especially an emotion or action)

2. VERB

/ breɪk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / breɪk ˈɪntʃu / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or vehicle) to suddenly begin moving at a different speed or in a different way, especially more quickly.

Examples

He took one step, two, broke into a trot and then into a run.
He broke into a dogtrot, breathing heavily, streaming with sweat.

Synset Examples

The dancers broke into a cha-cha
The horse broke into a gallop
