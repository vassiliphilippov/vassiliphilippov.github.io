shout out

1. VERB

/ ʃaʊt aʊt / (AmE), / ʃaʊt aʊt / (BrE)
to say or cry something in a loud voice, especially suddenly or to attract attention

Examples

An old gentlemen next to me held a Boy Scout bugle to his lips and blasted away at every fourth step and during the interim shouted out, "V for Victory "!

Synset Examples

she cried with pain when the doctor inserted the needle
I yelled to her from the window but she couldn't hear me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly, e.g. in anger, pain, or excitement
  • Synonym: scream - to make a loud, high cry because of pain, fear, or excitement
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak or say something very quietly
