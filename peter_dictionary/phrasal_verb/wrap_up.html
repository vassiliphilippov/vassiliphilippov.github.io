wrap up

1. VERB

/ ræp ʌp / (AmE), / ræp ʌp / (BrE)
to cover or enclose something or someone completely, often by folding or arranging material around as protection

Examples

I heard o' Texas cattlemen wrappin' a cow thief up in green hides and lettin' the sun shrink' em and squeeze him to death.

Synset Examples

wrap the baby before taking her out
Wrap the present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or upon, as to protect or conceal
  • Antonym: unwrap - to remove a covering from something
