in for

1. ADJ

/ ɪn fɔr / (AmE), / ɪn fə / (BrE)
(be) in for something: certain to experience or receive something, especially something unpleasant or unexpected.

Examples

He was not going to lose the mission by default, and whatever reason Kayabashi had for bringing his little sight-seeing group to the mission, he was going to be in for a surprise.
Looks like we might be in for a speck of trouble ".
"You 're in for trouble ".

Synset Examples

he knew he was in for a licking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: up against - facing or about to deal with (something difficult)
  • Synonym: get what's coming - to receive the (usually bad) consequences one deserves
