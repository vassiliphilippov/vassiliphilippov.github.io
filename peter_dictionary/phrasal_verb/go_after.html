go after

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to pursue or follow someone or something in order to catch or chase them; to attempt to obtain or attack.

Examples

He thought of Joe Harris, the nigger who had gone after his sister.
Once he went after Hans and dumped his pot over the banister.
"We 're going after ice ", Howard said, "and thought maybe you 'd go along and keep us company ".
Ben and Lucien have gone after them.
Caucusing, the liberals decided to go after Colmer, which actually was the more drastic course, since seniority in the House is next to godliness.

Synset Examples

The policeman chased the mugger down the alley
the dog chased the rabbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch or overtake
  • Synonym: pursue - to follow with the aim of catching or achieving

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈæftər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to try to obtain or achieve something; to pursue or seek.

Examples

It should have been nearly as easy for her to remember that as it was for Big Hans to remember going after the axe while he was still spattered with Pa's yellow sick insides.

Synset Examples

pursue a hobby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursue - to follow or chase in order to catch or achieve something

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to pursue someone or something with the intention of catching, obtaining, or achieving them or it

Examples

Go after him, go on!

Synset Examples

The policeman chased the mugger down the alley
the dog chased the rabbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch or catch up with
  • Antonym: flee - to run away from a pursuer or danger
