jump out

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp aʊt / (AmE), / dʒʌmp aʊt / (BrE)
To be very noticeable or immediately catch attention, often by contrast with what surrounds it.

Examples

Left in their previous atom-like smallness, they would have cut away too abruptly into depth; and the broad, opaque shapes of pasted paper would have been isolated in such a way as to make them jump out of plane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand out - be easily noticeable, clearly visible or prominent
