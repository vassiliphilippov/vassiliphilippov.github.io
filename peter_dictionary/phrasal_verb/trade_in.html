trade in

1. VERB

/ treɪd ɪn / (AmE), / treɪd ɪn / (BrE)
to give something you own, such as a car or appliance, to a seller as part payment for something newer or more valuable

Examples

"This morning, we allowed a farmer $ 600 on the old picker he traded in on a new $ 2700 model.

Synset Examples

trade in an old car for a new one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - to give something and receive something of the same kind in return
