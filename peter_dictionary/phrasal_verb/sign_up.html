sign up

1. VERB

/ saɪn ʌp / (AmE), / saɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to officially agree to participate in something or join an organization, usually by writing one's name or agreeing in writing

Examples

The promoter who wanted to sign him up for the circus asked him how he was able to do it.
My husband enters the motel office, signs up for a room, and them solemnly asks the proprieter if he accepts pets.

Synset Examples

They signed two new pitchers for the next season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enroll - to officially register or sign up for a course, activity, or organization
  • Synonym: register - to record or enroll your name officially for something

2. VERB

/ saɪn ʌp / (AmE), / saɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to put your name on an official list to become a member of a group, join an activity, or participate in something

Examples

Sustaining members may sign up at $ 25 for the ten-concert season; annual members may attend for $ 16.

Synset Examples

Sign up for yoga classes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - to put your name on an official list
  • Synonym: enroll - formally join a class or activity
