walk about

1. VERB

/ wɔk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to walk without a particular destination or aim, especially in a relaxed or leisurely manner

Examples

Would you permit Juanita to walk about the grounds with me for a short spell, Mrs. Lattimer "?

Synset Examples

we were walking around in the garden
after breakfast, she walked about in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
  • Synonym: roam - to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically
