step up

1. VERB

/ stɛp ʌp / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase the amount, speed, or intensity of something; to make something greater, stronger, or more effective

Examples

Also, the program for fleet modernization will be stepped up in 1961 causing an increase in expenditures.
To step up the denomination's program, the Rev. Mr. Brandt suggested the vision of 8000 new Assemblies of God churches in the next 10 years.

Synset Examples

let's rev up production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Synonym: accelerate - to make something happen or move faster
  • Antonym: reduce - to make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
