take for granted

1. VERB

/ teɪk fər ˈɡræn.tɪd / (AmE), / teɪk fə ˈɡrɑːn.tɪd / (BrE)
to accept or assume something as true without questioning or verifying it

Examples

I do mean, however, that I take them for granted, and that everything I shall be saying would appear quite idiotic against any contrary assumptions.
There was no money for tuition, for clothes, for all the things you apparently take for granted.

Synset Examples

I assume his train was late

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assume - to accept something as true without proof
  • Synonym: presume - to suppose something is true based on probability
