walk around

1. VERB

/ wɔk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to walk with no particular goal or destination, often in a leisurely or casual manner

Examples

Or: "I walk around the house a lot ".
Through the door, he had seen Mr. Jack walking around, waiting for Miss Ada.
He had stood at a little distance, studying her, as though he would walk around next and look at the back of her head.
Calenda was not the sort who walked around without one of his "boys "close at hand.
"They 're an expensive English shoe for walking around a lot.

Synset Examples

we were walking around in the garden
after breakfast, she walked about in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
  • Degree Lower: pace - to walk back and forth repeatedly, often due to anxiety or deep thought

2. VERB

/ wɔk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move on foot so as to go around or encircle something

Examples

He grinned, nodded, and walked around to the front of the car.
I 'll slide out and walk around the block back to the cocktail lounge on Biscayne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumnavigate - to go completely around something, especially by water or air
