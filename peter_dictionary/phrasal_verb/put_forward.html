put forward

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / pʊt ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to present an idea, suggestion, plan, or proposal for consideration or discussion

Examples

It reached its ultimate philosophical statement in notions of "state will "put forward by the Germans, especially by Hegel, although political philosophers will recognize its origins in the rejected doctrines of Hobbes.

Synset Examples

I submit to you that the accused is guilty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propose - to suggest a plan or idea for consideration
  • Synonym: suggest - to mention an idea or plan for somebody to think about
