take to be

1. VERB

/ teɪk tə bi / (AmE), / teɪk tə bi / (BrE)
to regard or consider (someone or something) as being a particular thing; to look on as.

Examples

At once my ears were drowned by a flow of what I took to be Spanish, but - the driver's white teeth flashing at me, the road wildly veering beyond his glistening hair, beyond his gesticulating bottle - it could have been the purest Oxford English I was half hearing; I would n't have known the difference.
A few literary men defended what they took to be an emphasis on the poetry at the expense of the drama, but the response was mainly hostile and quite violent.

Synset Examples

she looked on this affair as a joke
He thinks of himself as a brilliant musician
He is reputed to be intelligent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regard as - to consider or view in a particular way
  • Synonym: consider - to think about as a particular thing or in a particular light

2. VERB

/ teɪk tə bi / (AmE), / teɪk tə bi / (BrE)
to consider or regard someone or something as being a particular kind of person or thing, often mistakenly

Examples

I could see why some of the boys took him **for** snobby.

Synset Examples

she looked on this affair as a joke
He thinks of himself as a brilliant musician
He is reputed to be intelligent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake for - to wrongly think that one person or thing is another
