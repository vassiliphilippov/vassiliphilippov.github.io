start up

1. VERB

/ stɑrt ʌp / (AmE), / stɑːt ʌp / (BrE)
to begin operating or functioning, especially after being stopped; to make something begin to work, such as a machine, engine, or system

Examples

Consider it as a standby setup, at negligible cost, for those emergencies when the furnace quits, a blizzard holds up fuel delivery, or for cool summer mornings or evenings when you do n't want to start up your whole heating plant.
When the two cars were equidistant from him, the station wagon started up again and the Ford gathered speed.

Synset Examples

We simply could not start the engine
start up the computer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shut down - to stop operating or functioning, especially machinery, systems, or businesses
  • Synonym: turn on - to cause a device or system to begin operating by switching it on
  • Synonym: activate - to make something active or operative
