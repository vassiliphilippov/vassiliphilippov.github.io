wave off

1. VERB

/ weɪv ɔf / (AmE), / weɪv ɒf / (BrE)
To dismiss something, especially a suggestion, question, or concern, as unimportant or not worthy of consideration.

Examples

THE MODEST AND HAPPY Spahn waved off his new laurels as one of those good days.

Synset Examples

He waved off suggestions of impropriety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush off - to dismiss someone or something abruptly or casually
  • Synonym: ignore - to intentionally disregard or pay no attention to something
