plump for

1. VERB

/ plʌmp fɔr / (AmE), / plʌmp fə / (BrE)
to choose or support someone or something, especially after considering alternatives

Examples

In the end, Picasso and Braque plumped for the representational, and it would seem they did so deliberately.

Synset Examples

He plumped for the Labor Party
I backed Kennedy in 1960

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - to support or endorse someone or something

Etymology

Possibly from a dialect usage of 'plump' meaning to decide suddenly or cast a vote.
