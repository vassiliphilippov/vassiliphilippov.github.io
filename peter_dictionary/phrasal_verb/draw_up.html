draw up

1. VERB

/ drɔ ʌp / (AmE), / drɔː ʌp / (BrE)
(of a person) to straighten oneself, especially in a way that shows dignity or pride.

Examples

"I soon came back to my senses ", he said, aloud, to the young blizzard, proudly, drawing himself up, as if making a report to some important superior.

Synset Examples

He drew himself up when he talked to his superior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straighten up - to make the body straight or erect

2. VERB

/ drɔː ʌp / (AmE), / drɔː ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare and write out plans, documents, or detailed arrangements

Examples

On Monday, the Hughes concern was formally declared bankrupt after its directors indicated they could not draw up a plan for reorganization.

Synset Examples

frame a policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - to prepare a preliminary version of a document

3. VERB

/ drɔː ʌp / (AmE), / drɔː ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange or form people or things into a particular order or formation, especially in a controlled or military manner

Examples

A wide-ranging, bipartisan force - from Minnesota's Democratic Hubert Humphrey to Massachusetts' Republican Leverett Saltonstall - was drawn up against a solid phalanx of Southern Democrats, who have traditionally used the filibuster to stop civil rights bills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line up - to arrange in a straight row or in proper order

4. VERB

/ drɔ ˈʌp / (AmE), / drɔː ˈʌp / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or its driver) to come to a stop or bring a vehicle to a stop.

Examples

The car drew up alongside him and stopped.

Synset Examples

He pulled up the car in front of the hotel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull up - stop a vehicle
