trim down

1. VERB

/ trɪm daʊn / (AmE), / trɪm daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or extent of something, usually to make it neater or more efficient

Examples

Baltimore's bulky spring training contingent now gradually will be reduced as Manager Paul Richards and his coaches seek to trim it down to a more streamlined and workable unit.

Synset Examples

reduce your daily fat intake
The employer wants to cut back health benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduce - to make something smaller in size or amount
  • Synonym: cut back - to reduce the amount of something
