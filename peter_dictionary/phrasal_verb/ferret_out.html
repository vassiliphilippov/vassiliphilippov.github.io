ferret out

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛrət aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to search for and discover something through persistent effort or investigation

Examples

I even ferreted out the materials from which shields were made - linden wood covered with leather - so I 'd get the light reflections accurate.

Synset Examples

She ferreted out the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track down - to find someone or something after searching
  • Synonym: root out - to find and remove something or someone

Etymology

From 'ferret', referring to the animal used to drive creatures from hiding, extended to mean to search out.
