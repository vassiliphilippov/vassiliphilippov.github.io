stand up

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to rise to a standing position from a sitting or lying position

Examples

They had been sitting too long to be able to stand up easily.
Clara Harris, one of the guests in the box, stood up and demanded water.
She stood up, smoothing her hair down, straightening her clothes, feeling a thankfulness for the enveloping darkness outside, and, above everything else, for the absence of the need to answer, to respond, to be aware even of Stowey coming in or going out, and yet, now that she was beginning to cook, she glimpsed a future without him, a future alone like this, and the pain made her head writhe, and in a moment she found it hard to wait for Lucretia to come with her guests.
Sighing, Leona dropped the paper and stood up.
Then Miss Ada had stood up, rustling and rustling, and gone upstairs.

Synset Examples

The audience got up and applauded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sit down - to move from a standing to a sitting position
  • Synonym: get up - to rise from bed or a sitting/lying position; stand up

2. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to put something into an upright position so that it is standing vertically

Examples

Honest I could feel the hair stand up on the back of my neck like a dog's that is going to get into a fight.
I stood up and began pacing.

Synset Examples

Can you stand the bookshelf up?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set up - to place something in an upright position or to assemble

3. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to refuse to yield or retreat in the face of criticism, opposition, or attack; to maintain one's position or beliefs firmly

Examples

Yet, paradoxically, according to Dr. Maurice Linden, many wives despise their husbands for not standing up to them.
"You mean anyone who stood up for his rights ", Curt said.
Brahm's Sonata in A, although also vigorous, stood up well under the two artists' strong, large-scale treatment.
How are the roads, and how will they stand up?
(I watched it on television late one night last week and it "stands up "remarkably well, even twenty years later.)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resist - to withstand the force or effect of something
  • Synonym: withstand - to remain undamaged or unaffected by; resist
  • Antonym: give in - to yield or surrender under pressure
  • Antonym: back down - to yield in an argument or pressure

4. VERB

/ stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to defend oneself or others against attack, criticism, or pressure

Examples

"Do you feel you can stand up to the next legislative session and defend this contract "? asked Mrs. Grace O. Peck, representative from Multnomah County, of the commission chairman, Joseph E. Harvey Jr..
Now we have stood up to the Communists; we are stronger and more self-confident - and Low cannot so easily put us to rights.

Synset Examples

He stood up for his friend
She stuck up for the teacher who was accused of harassing the student

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick up for - to support or defend someone or something, especially when it is criticized or attacked
  • Antonym: give in - to stop resisting; to surrender or yield, especially under pressure or attack
