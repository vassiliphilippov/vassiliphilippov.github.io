block out

1. VERB

/ blɑk aʊt / (AmE), / blɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to plan the arrangement and timing of musical numbers, scenes, or other elements within a theatrical production

Examples

After we got a script and the spots for the songs were blocked out, we 'd get together for an hour or so every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to organize or arrange in advance
