bring up

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to move someone to a higher position or rank, especially in an organization or sports team

Examples

Stengel inherited DiMaggio, Rizzuto, but he brought up Mantle from Class C to the majors, from Joplin to New York.

Synset Examples

This player was brought up to the major league

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promote - to raise to a higher position or rank

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
(of a sudden event or realization) to cause someone to stop what they are doing or thinking abruptly.

Examples

The week after Manassas the sound of horses in the yard brought Kate up in shock from an afternoon's rest when she saw the Federal soldiers from her upstairs window.

Synset Examples

The noise brought her up in shock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halt - to bring or come to a stop
  • Synonym: startle - to cause someone to stop suddenly with surprise

3. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to move or lift something or part of the body to a higher position

Examples

She brought up her free hand to hit him, but this time he was quicker.

Synset Examples

Raise your hands
Lift a load

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to lift something to a higher position
  • Antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position

4. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
To introduce a subject or topic for discussion.

Examples

Possibly, the coconut containing dessert had brought up bitter problems of administration.
I make this observation about the lady, Miss Judy Garland, because she brought up the subject herself in telling a story about a British female reporter who flattered her terribly in London recently and then wrote in the paper the next day:
When the husband drops the case and returns to his wife, both seem sorry they brought the matter up in the first place.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mention - refer to something briefly or casually
  • Synonym: raise - to bring forward for discussion
  • Antonym: ignore - to intentionally not bring up a topic

5. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to care for and educate (a child) until they are grown up; to raise (children)

Examples

"We were brought up that way "was one statement which won general assent.
It was their conviction that the people should be "brought up together ", a grade at a time, until in some indefinite future some might be ready to tackle history, economics and political science.

Synset Examples

raise a family
bring up children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to care for children until they are adults; synonym for 'bring up' in this sense
  • Synonym: rear - to care for and educate a child while they are growing up; synonym for 'bring up'

6. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
To mention or introduce a subject, idea, or topic for discussion.

Examples

- Sollozzo would lose our ass. - What about the negotiator? - He's playing pinochle with my men.

Synset Examples

raise the question of promotions
bring up an unpleasant topic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to bring forward a subject or question for discussion
  • Synonym: bring out - to make something apparent or introduce into conversation
