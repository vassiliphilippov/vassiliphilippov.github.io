rule in

1. VERB

/ ruːl ɪn / (AmE), / ruːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of a court or other authority) to include as valid, relevant, or permissible, or to decide in someone's favor according to rules or judgment.

Examples

The golfing fathers ruled in his favor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rule out - decide to exclude or dismiss as a possibility
