wind up

1. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
To arrive in a particular situation or condition, often unexpectedly or as a result of events; to end up.

Examples

So he wound up with a dozen.
"Chicago and All That Jazz "may have wound up satisfying neither the confirmed fan nor the inquisitive newcomer.
Sometimes I got on the wrong car and did n't get to school at all, but wound up at the ocean, or some other dismal place, and had to spend the day there.
It was a bad place for Roberts to wind up, but Roberts had asked for it.

Synset Examples

He ended up marrying his high school sweetheart
he wound up being unemployed and living at home again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end up - to eventually reach a particular place or condition

2. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
(of a baseball pitcher) to make the preliminary swinging movement with the arm before throwing the ball.

Examples

The pitcher wound up and the ball came in straight for Mike's head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch - to throw a baseball to a batter

3. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
(informal) To reach a particular place or situation as a result of a course of events; to finally be or do something, often unexpectedly.

Examples

And I wound up in here.

Synset Examples

He ended up marrying his high school sweetheart
he wound up being unemployed and living at home again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end up - to eventually reach a particular situation or outcome
