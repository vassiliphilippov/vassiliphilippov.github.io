palm off

1. VERB

/ pɑm ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːm ɒf / (BrE)
To give or sell something by making it appear more valuable or genuine than it really is, usually with the intention to deceive.

Examples

If a union cannot perform this function, then collective bargaining is being palmed off by organizers as a gigantic fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foist - to pass off (something spurious) as genuine

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'palm' (to conceal in the palm of the hand) + 'off'.
