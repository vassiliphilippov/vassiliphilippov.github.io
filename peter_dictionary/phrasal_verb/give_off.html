give off

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to produce or emit something, especially as a by-product or in the form of heat, light, a smell, or a quality

Examples

But they deny the values of the city - the crowded, competitive, tolerant city, the "melting pot "which gave off so many of the most admirable American qualities.
But though I boasted and gave off a dapper front, I was beneath it all frightened.

Synset Examples

The big cities gave off so many wonderful American qualities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emit - to send out or give off (such as an odor, heat, or light)

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to produce and send out a substance, such as light, heat, a smell, or a gas

Examples

Before this week is out... ... we are going to knock down their doors... ... and shed the light of the law on that nest of vipers.

Synset Examples

The ozone layer blocks some harmful rays which the sun emits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emit - to send out (light, heat, sound, gas, etc.)
