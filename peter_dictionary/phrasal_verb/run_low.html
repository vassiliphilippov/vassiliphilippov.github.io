run low

1. VERB

/ ɹʌn ˈloʊ / (AmE), / ɹʌn ˈləʊ / (BrE)
To approach depletion or become nearly used up, especially relating to a supply of something.

Examples

We're running low on Hexlite.

Synset Examples

The money had gone after a few days
Gas is running low at the gas stations in the Midwest

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: run out - to use up a supply so that nothing remains
  • Synonym: be short of - to not have enough of something needed
