knock out

1. VERB

/ nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to destroy, remove, or break something forcefully, often so that it no longer functions or is in place

Examples

The chimney of the hut across from him was surmounted by a beef barrel with ends knocked out.

Synset Examples

The windows were knocked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - break something into pieces violently
  • Synonym: demolish - destroy or ruin a structure completely

2. VERB

/ nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone unconscious by hitting them forcefully, especially in boxing or a fight

Examples

"I was hit - knocked out.
"Why the two damned fools got all excited when they saw the bottle, and knocked each other out cold ", Shayne said good-humoredly.

Synset Examples

the boxing champion knocked out his opponent in a few seconds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stun - to make someone unconscious or unable to think clearly, usually by a blow to the head
  • Antonym: revive - to bring someone back to consciousness

3. VERB

/ nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to destroy, disable, or eliminate something, especially by force or effort

Examples

The tines broke off under Jess's twisting, and he swung the handle in an attempt to knock Curt's brains out.
What targets have we successfully knocked out?
Like hell he 'd wait - and supposing the radar-controlled gun got knocked out?
Most of the Junkers were above the blinding light of the flares, and the radar-controlled shore gun had been knocked out by one of the first sticks of bombs.

Synset Examples

knock out a target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroy - to ruin completely or put an end to the existence of something
