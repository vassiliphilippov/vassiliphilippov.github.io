buy off

1. VERB

/ baɪ ɑf / (AmE), / baɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to use money or gifts to bribe someone so they act in your favor or overlook wrongdoing

Examples

In 1959, the State Senate finally clued into the fact they couldn't buy him **off** with just a $200 check.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to give money or favors to influence someone dishonestly
