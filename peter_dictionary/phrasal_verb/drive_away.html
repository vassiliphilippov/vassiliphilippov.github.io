drive away

1. VERB

/ draɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / draɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To force someone or something to leave a place, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

I drove him away.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispel - to make something, especially a feeling or belief, disappear
  • Synonym: chase away - to force someone or something to go away

to force someone or something to leave a place, either physically or figuratively

Examples

Synset Examples
