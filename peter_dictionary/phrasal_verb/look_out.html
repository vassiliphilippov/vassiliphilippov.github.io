look out

1. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt / (BrE)
To be vigilant, attentive, or careful, especially in order to avoid danger or trouble.

Examples

He looked out of the tent at the company street.
He looked out through windowpanes turned a faint violet by sun and weather, looked out at King's Bridge toward Westchester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beware - be cautious and alert to danger
  • Synonym: watch out - be careful or alert
