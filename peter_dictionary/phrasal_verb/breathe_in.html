breathe in

1. VERB

/ brið ɪn / (AmE), / briːð ɪn / (BrE)
to take air into your lungs through your nose or mouth; to inhale

Examples

When they had gone, he stood for a minute breathing in the mustiness of old paper and leather which the busily thrumming air conditioner could n't quite dispel.

Synset Examples

Inhale deeply
inhale the fresh mountain air
The patient has trouble inspiring
The lung cancer patient cannot inspire air very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhale - to breathe in; to draw in air
  • Antonym: breathe out - to expel air from the lungs; to exhale
