open fire

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to begin shooting weapons, especially guns or artillery

Examples

A British officer had come aboard and told him that in case of enemy air attack he was not to open fire until bombs were actually dropped.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cease fire - to stop shooting or firing weapons
  • Synonym: fire - to shoot a gun or other weapon

2. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to begin shooting a weapon, especially guns or firearms, at people or things

Examples

Any man who opens fire on Jack Napier will answer to me.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cease fire - to stop shooting weapons
