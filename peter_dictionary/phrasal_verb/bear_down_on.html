bear down on

1. VERB

/ bɛr daʊn ɑn / (AmE), / beə daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to move toward someone or something in a determined or threatening way, often rapidly or forcefully; originally, of a ship, to sail towards another vessel

Examples

They bore down on him.
They would be coming for him next, bearing down on him from both directions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come nearer in distance or time
  • Antonym: recede - to move back or away from
