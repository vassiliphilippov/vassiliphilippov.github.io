call back

1. VERB

/ kɔl bæk / (AmE), / kɔːl bæk / (BrE)
to officially request the return of something that has been issued, sent out, or produced, often because of a problem or for further action; to recall.

Examples

We hoped that its practitioners and teachers might be put on some sort of reserve list and called back for refresher courses each year or so.

Synset Examples

recall the defective auto tires
The manufacturer tried to call back the spoilt yoghurt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to order the return of a product or person

2. VERB

/ kɔl bæk / (AmE), / kɔːl bæk / (BrE)
to remember or recall something from memory; to bring to mind again

Examples

Clayton tried to call back the face of the man he had known.

Synset Examples

I can't remember saying any such thing
I can't think what her last name was
can you remember her phone number?
Do you remember that he once loved you?
call up memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remember - to bring to mind an awareness of someone or something from the past
  • Antonym: forget - to be unable to remember
