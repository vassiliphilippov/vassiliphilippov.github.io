rule out

1. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
To exclude something as a possibility; to decide that something is not possible or not appropriate.

Examples

We can escape from such a difficulty by ruling out the animal as not constituting a trial, but such a solution is not always satisfactory.
Now on the subjectivist view, this answer is ruled out.
At the same time the President took pains not to rule out an eventual meeting with the Soviet leader.
Import quotas are n't ruled out where the national interest is involved.
A completely amorphous institution would be a contradiction in terms; to escape this fate, it must rule some things out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclude - to deliberately not include something
  • Antonym: allow - to permit something to be possible

2. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
To decide that something is impossible or not suitable, and so stop considering it as a possible option.

Examples

He walked rapidly along the buildings scanning their facades: one was a club - that was out; two others he ruled out because all their windows were lighted.

Synset Examples

John was ruled out as a possible suspect because he had a strong alibi
This possibility can be eliminated from our consideration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eliminate - to remove something from consideration or exclude
  • Antonym: consider - to think about something as a possible option

3. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
to decide that something is not possible or not suitable, especially after careful consideration or according to rules

Examples

In fact, some sensitives rule this out, preferring to consider their expression as strictly extra-sensory perception (ESP), on this side of the "veil ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclude - to keep out; deny the inclusion of

4. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
To decide that something is not possible or not suitable, and therefore stop considering it.

Examples

Cause of death has been attributed to a violent allergic reaction... ... although authorities have not ruled out the possibility of drug use.

Synset Examples

John was ruled out as a possible suspect because he had a strong alibi
This possibility can be eliminated from our consideration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eliminate - to remove from consideration
  • Antonym: consider - to think about or take into account
