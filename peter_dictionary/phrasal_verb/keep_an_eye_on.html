keep an eye on

1. VERB

/ kip ən aɪ ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp ən aɪ ɒn / (BrE)
to watch or monitor someone or something carefully and attentively, usually for safety or supervision.

Examples

If a concessionaire runs the cafeteria, keep an eye out for quality and price.
Conchita kept an eye on the twins and little Elena, trying to keep them from falling into the creek by which they persisted in playing.
And accompanying adults are urged to keep an alert and sensible eye on their responsibilities.

Synset Examples

Keep an eye on the baby, please!
The world is watching Sarajevo
She followed the men with the binoculars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - to look at or observe attentively over a period of time
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or observe something
  • Antonym: ignore - to intentionally not pay attention to someone or something
