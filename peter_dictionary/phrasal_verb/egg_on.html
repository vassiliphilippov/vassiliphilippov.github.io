egg on

1. VERB

/ ɛɡ ɑn / (AmE), / ɛɡ ɒn / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to do something, especially something unwise or mischievous

Examples

The crisis was artificially stirred up by the Kremlin (Wall Street) and the Red Army (Pentagon) egged on by the West Germans (East Germans).

Synset Examples

The other children egged the boy on, but he did not want to throw the stone through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incite - to urge or stir up someone to action, especially unlawful or violent action
  • Antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Old Norse 'eggia' (to incite), not related to 'egg' (the food).
