pick on

1. VERB

/ pɪk ɑn / (AmE), / pɪk ɒn / (BrE)
to repeatedly treat someone unfairly, criticize, or bully them, especially in a way that is perceived as unnecessary or unjust

Examples

For over a hundred years Southerners have felt that the North was picking on them.
It's infuriating, this feeling that one is being picked on, continually, constantly.

Synset Examples

Don't always pick on your little brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone perceived as vulnerable
  • Synonym: harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
