prick up

1. VERB

/ prɪk ʌp / (AmE), / prɪk ʌp / (BrE)
(of ears or someone/animal) to raise or lift suddenly, especially in response to a sound or signal of interest or alertness.

Examples

It was Giselle, the fille de chambre, come to clean the room, and while she stood before him with ears pricked up and regard all curiosity, explaining her errand, Alex could see from the corner of his eye the doctor doing all he could to calm the displeased bird.

Synset Examples

The dog pricked up his ears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perk up - to become more lively or attentive

Etymology

From the verb 'prick' (to pierce or cause to stand erect), used since Middle English.
