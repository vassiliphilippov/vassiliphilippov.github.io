cheer up

1. VERB

/ tʃɪr ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone feel happier or more cheerful; to lift someone's spirits

Examples

It always brings a smile to my face.

Synset Examples

She tried to cheer up the disappointed child when he failed to win the spelling bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Synonym: console - to comfort someone who is sad or disappointed
  • Antonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm

Etymology

From 'cheer' (Middle English, from Old French 'chere' meaning face, countenance, later 'mood') + 'up' (preposition and adverb).
