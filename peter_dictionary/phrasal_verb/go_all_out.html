go all out

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔl aʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to make the greatest possible effort; to do something with maximum enthusiasm or energy

Examples

Maria goes all out as a Druid princess who gets two-timed by a Roman big shot.

Synset Examples

The cast gives full measure every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give it your all - to make your maximum effort; try as hard as possible
  • Antonym: take it easy - to act or proceed in a relaxed or gentle way; to not make a strong effort
