go deep

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ dip / (AmE), / ɡəʊ diːp / (BrE)
to extend or have a significant influence, effect, or importance; to reach far beyond the surface or the obvious

Examples

It is, I insist, hard to define the Rayburn contribution to our political civilization because it is so massive and so widespread and so complicated, and because it goes so deep.

Synset Examples

His accomplishments go far

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go far - to extend in influence, range, or importance
