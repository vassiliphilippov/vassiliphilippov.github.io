hold still for

1. VERB

/ hoʊld stɪl fɔr / (AmE), / həʊld stɪl fə / (BrE)
to tolerate, accept, or allow something, especially something unpleasant or undesired

Examples

"No divinity will hold still for that.

Synset Examples

I won't stand for this kind of behavior!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put up with - to tolerate or endure something
  • Synonym: stand for - to tolerate, accept, or permit (something)
  • Synonym: tolerate - to allow or endure without interference
  • Antonym: refuse - to decline to accept or allow
