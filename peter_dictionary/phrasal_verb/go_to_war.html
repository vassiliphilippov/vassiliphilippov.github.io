go to war

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə wɔr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə wɔː / (BrE)
to begin fighting in a war; to engage in armed conflict with another group, nation, or state.

Examples

I think it is essential, however, to pinpoint here the difference between the two concepts of sovereignty that went to war in 1861 - if only to see better how imperative is our need today to clarify completely our far worse confusion on this subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make war - to engage in or declare war; to fight a war against someone
  • Antonym: make peace - to stop fighting and establish peaceful relations

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə wɔr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə wɔː / (BrE)
to engage in active warfare against another country or group; to begin hostilities

Examples

I want Sollozzo, or we go to war... -

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make war - to engage in warfare against an enemy
  • Degree Lower: fight - to engage in physical combat
  • Synonym: declare war - to formally announce engagement in war
  • Antonym: make peace - to end hostilities and establish peace
