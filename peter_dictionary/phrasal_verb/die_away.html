die away

1. VERB

/ daɪ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / daɪ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually become less strong, loud, or intense until it stops or disappears

Examples

The storms of the past had died away, and the great upheaval which was to mark the following century had not yet begun to disturb men's minds.
The music died away discordantly.

Synset Examples

The storm abated
The rain let up after a few hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade away - to gradually become weaker or disappear
  • Synonym: diminish - to become or cause to be less
  • Antonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
