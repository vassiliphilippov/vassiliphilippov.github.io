rake up

1. VERB

/ reɪk ʌp / (AmE), / reɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to bring something unpleasant or embarrassing, especially from the past, to public attention again; to revive memories of something best forgotten.

Examples

If some future Khrushchev decided to rake up the misdeeds of his revered predecessor, would not the factory workers pass the same resolutions applauding his dispossession?

Synset Examples

He raked up the misdeeds of his predecessor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehash - to discuss or present something again without any new ideas
  • Antonym: suppress - to prevent something from being known or expressed
