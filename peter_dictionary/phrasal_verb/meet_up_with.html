meet up with

1. VERB

/ mit ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / miːt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to arrange to see and spend time with someone, typically by plan or appointment

Examples

I wrote her that I 'd met up with Eileen and that old bonds had proved too strong and asked her to send my clothes down by express.
"Just let me meet up with that damned bushwhackin' coward face-to-face "! he exploded.

Synset Examples

She met up with her former lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together with someone, either by chance or arrangement

2. VERB

/ mit ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / miːt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to meet someone by arrangement or appointment, usually for social or practical reasons

Examples

And then I'll meet with Don Barzini and Tattaglia.

Synset Examples

She met up with her former lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together with someone, especially by arrangement
