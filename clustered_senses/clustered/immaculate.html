immaculate

Summary

  • perfectly clean, neat, or without any flaws or mistakes
    • ADJ: spotlessly clean; flawless
    • ADJ: free from stain or blemish
    • ADJ: perfect; without any fault or flaw
perfectly clean, neat, or without any flaws or mistakes

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmækjələt / (AmE), / ɪˈmakjʊlət / (BrE)
perfectly clean and neat; free from dirt or flaws.
spotlessly clean; flawless

Examples

His handwriting was immaculate; every letter was perfectly formed and evenly spaced.
She wore an immaculate white dress that looked as if it had never been touched.
The kitchen was immaculate, with every surface gleaming and not a speck of dust in sight.

Synset Examples

the apartment was immaculate
in her immaculate white uniform
a spick-and-span kitchen
their spic red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean or pure; without blemish or stain
  • Antonym: dirty - covered or marked with an unclean substance; not clean

Etymology

From Latin immaculatus (unstained, spotless), from in- (not) + maculatus (spotted).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmækjələt / (AmE), / ɪˈmækjʊlət / (BrE)
perfectly clean or free from any stain or blemish; spotless.
free from stain or blemish

Examples

His handwriting was so immaculate that it looked like printed text.
She always kept her white dress immaculate for important occasions.
The living room was immaculate, with not a speck of dust in sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean or pure; without any stain or blemish
  • Antonym: stained - marked or discolored by a foreign substance

Etymology

From Latin 'immaculatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'maculatus' (spotted, stained).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈmækjələt / (AmE), / ɪˈmakjʊlət / (BrE)
perfectly clean, neat, or free from mistakes or faults; without any flaws or errors.
perfect; without any fault or flaw

Examples

Her white shirt was immaculate after she spent an hour cleaning it.
His academic record remained immaculate throughout his university career.
The chef prided himself on maintaining an immaculate kitchen at all times.

Synset Examples

faultless logic
speaks impeccable French
timing and technique were immaculate
an immaculate record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flawless - without any flaws or imperfections
  • Synonym: perfect - without fault or defect; as good as possible
  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; having faults or defects

Etymology

From Latin immaculatus, from in- (not) + maculatus (spotted, stained), from macula (spot).
