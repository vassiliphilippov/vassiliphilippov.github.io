A type of fabric or an item made by interlacing yarn with needles or by machine, often characterized by its stretch and flexibility.

1. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
A type of fabric that is produced by interlacing yarns using the process of knitting, often characterized by its stretch and flexibility.
fabric produced by knitting

Examples

He chose a lightweight knit for the summer top.
The sweater is made from a soft wool knit.
This shop sells both woven and knit fabrics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woven - fabric made by weaving threads over and under each other

Etymology

From Old English *cnyttan* (to knot).

2. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
A piece of fabric or an item created by interlacing yarn with needles or by machine in a series of connected loops.
fabric or item made by knitting

Examples

She admired the intricate knit of the handmade blanket.
The sweater's tight knit made it especially warm in winter.
This scarf has a loose knit that gives it a casual look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knitting - the action or process of knitting yarn into fabric
  • Antonym: weave - a fabric or cloth created by interlacing threads at right angles

Etymology

From Old English 'cnyttan' (to tie in a knot).
