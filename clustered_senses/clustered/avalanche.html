A sudden and overwhelming quantity of things occurring or appearing at once, especially in large numbers.
sudden overwhelming quantity or influx

Examples

After posting his job advertisement, he received an avalanche of applications.
The celebrity was met with an avalanche of fan mail after her performance.
There was an avalanche of complaints following the company’s announcement.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

1. NOUN

/ ˈæv.əˌlæntʃ / (AmE), / ˈæv.əl.ɑːnʃ / (BrE)
A mass of snow, ice, and sometimes mud that rapidly slides down a mountainside.
mass of snow/ice sliding downhill

Examples

Heavy snowfall increased the risk of an avalanche on the steep slopes of the mountain.
Several skiers were caught in a deadly avalanche in the Alps last winter.
The rescue team rushed to the village after an avalanche blocked the main road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landslide - mass of earth or rock moving down a slope

Etymology

From French 'avalanche,' possibly from Savoyard dialect, from Provençal 'lavanca' (torrent of snow).

1. VERB

/ ˈævəˌlæntʃ / (AmE), / ˈævəlɑːnʃ / (BrE)
(of snow) to accumulate into a large mass and move rapidly down a mountainside.
(of snow) gather into a mass and slide down

Examples

Every spring, as the temperature rises, the snow can avalanche suddenly and without warning.
If the snowpack is unstable, it may avalanche after a loud noise or heavy snowfall.
The rescue team saw a massive wall of snow avalanche down the mountain toward the village.

Related Terms
