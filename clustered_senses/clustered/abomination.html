abomination

Summary

  • A person, thing, or action that causes strong disgust, hatred, or moral revulsion, often because it is considered extremely wrong or offensive.
    • NOUN: intense hatred and disgust
    • NOUN: something extremely disgusting or offensive
    • NOUN: loathsome or detestable person or thing
A person, thing, or action that causes strong disgust, hatred, or moral revulsion, often because it is considered extremely wrong or offensive.

1. NOUN

/ əˌbɑːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌbɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of intense hatred and disgust; something that causes this strong revulsion
intense hatred and disgust

Examples

He looked upon the vandalized church as an abomination.
Many considered the new law an abomination that should be repealed at once.
The idea of cheating was an abomination to her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loathing - strong feeling of hatred or disgust
  • Antonym: delight - great pleasure or satisfaction

Etymology

From Latin abominatio(n-), from abominari (to deprecate as ill-omened, detest).

2. NOUN

/ əˌbɑməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌbɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a thing or action that is considered extremely disgusting or morally offensive
something extremely disgusting or offensive

Examples

Many regarded the cruel practice as a moral abomination that must be outlawed.
She considered lying to be an absolute abomination in their family.
The dumping of toxic waste in the river was seen as an abomination by the local community.

Synset Examples

his treatment of the children is an abomination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocity - a shockingly cruel or brutal act
  • Antonym: delight - something that gives great pleasure

Etymology

From Latin abominatio (a loathing, detestation), from abominari (to deprecate as an ill omen).

3. NOUN

/ əˌbɑːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌbɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes disgust or hatred; a loathsome or detestable person or thing
loathsome or detestable person or thing

Examples

He acted as if she were some kind of abomination that had no place in his life.
Some considered the traitor an abomination and refused even to speak his name.
The dictator was viewed as an abomination by much of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - a cruel or wicked person
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person

Etymology

From Latin abominatio(n-), from abominari ('to shun as a bad omen').
