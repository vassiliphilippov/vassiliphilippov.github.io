1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something known, especially knowledge or truth, by a divine or supernatural agency
divine disclosure of knowledge

Examples

Many religions are founded on the belief in divine revelation.
She described her vision as a sudden revelation that transformed her life.
The prophet claimed that the message was a revelation received directly from God.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disclosure - the action of making new or secret information known
  • Antonym: concealment - the action of hiding something or preventing it from being known

Etymology

From Latin 'revelatio', from 'revelare' meaning 'to lay bare, reveal'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a surprising or enlightening disclosure of something previously unknown
an astonishing disclosure

Examples

His book is filled with personal revelation and insight.
It came as a revelation to learn that she could sing so beautifully.
The scientist's discovery was a revelation that changed the course of research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disclosure - the act of making new or secret information known
  • Antonym: secret - something that is kept or meant to be kept unknown or unseen by others

Etymology

From Latin revelatio (disclosure), from revelare (to reveal).
