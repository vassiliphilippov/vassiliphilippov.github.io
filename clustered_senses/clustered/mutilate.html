to mutilate

Summary

    • VERB: injure or destroy severely

1. VERB

/ ˈmjuːtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to damage or injure something or someone severely, especially by cutting off or destroying parts
to damage or injure severely

Examples

Careless editing can sometimes mutilate the meaning of an original text.
The artist was devastated when vandals mutilated his sculpture in the park.
The rescue team found that the animal had been severely mutilated by a trap.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disfigure - to spoil the appearance of something or someone
