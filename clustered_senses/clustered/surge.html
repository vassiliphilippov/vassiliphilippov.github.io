A sudden and powerful movement or increase, especially of water, people, emotion, or numbers. (noun)

1. NOUN

/ sɝdʒ / (AmE), / sɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a large and sudden powerful movement of water, especially a great sea wave or swell
a large sea wave or swell

Examples

A powerful surge of water flooded the low-lying villages after the storm.
Fishermen were warned of the dangerous surge approaching the coast.
The sudden surge crashed over the seawall, drenching everyone nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a moving ridge on the surface of water, especially the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'surgere' (to rise, spring up).
