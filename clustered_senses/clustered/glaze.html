glaze

Summary

  • to cover food with a smooth, shiny coating, especially one made from sugar or another sweet substance
    • VERB: to coat food with a sweet, shiny layer
  • a thin, shiny coating, often savory or sweet, applied to the surface of food to give flavor and a glossy finish
    • NOUN: shiny coating applied to food
  • to cover something with a smooth, shiny coating, especially with a liquid that hardens to a gloss
    • VERB: cover with a shiny coating
  • A smooth, often shiny coating applied to ceramics, metal, or fabric to protect or decorate the surface.
    • NOUN: shiny coating for ceramics or metal
    • NOUN: glossy finish on fabric
  • to fit or supply windows, doors, or other openings with glass
    • VERB: fit or supply with glass
  • to become glassy or to take on a smooth, shiny appearance, often as a result of a change in emotion, attention, or condition
    • VERB: become glassy in appearance

1. VERB

/ ɡleɪz / (AmE), / ɡleɪz / (BrE)
to cover food with a smooth, shiny coating, especially one made from sugar or another sweet substance
to coat food with a sweet, shiny layer

Examples

After baking, let the cake cool before you glaze it with chocolate.
She learned how to glaze donuts so they have a perfect, glossy finish.
The chef will glaze the fruit tart with apricot jam for extra shine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frost - cover with icing or a similar sweet coating

Etymology

From Middle English glasen, from Old English glæs (glass); used for the shiny appearance similar to glass.
