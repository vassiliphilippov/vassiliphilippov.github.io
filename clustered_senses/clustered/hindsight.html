1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪnd.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪnd.saɪt / (BrE)
the ability to understand and interpret an event or situation only after it has happened
understanding after an event

Examples

In hindsight, it was a mistake to invest in that company.
She realized in hindsight that she should have asked more questions.
With the benefit of hindsight, we can see where the project went wrong.

Synset Examples

hindsight is always better than foresight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foresight - the ability to predict or prepare for future events

Etymology

From hind (‘behind’) + sight; modelled after 'foresight'.
