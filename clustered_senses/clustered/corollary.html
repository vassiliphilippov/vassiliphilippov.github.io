corollary

Summary

  • A result or effect that follows naturally from something else; a logical or practical consequence.
    • NOUN: natural consequence or result

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəˌleri / (AmE), / kəˈrɒləri / (BrE)
A result or effect that follows naturally from something else; a logical or practical consequence.
natural consequence or result

Examples

A rise in energy prices is often a corollary of increasing international demand.
In economic theory, a corollary to market competition is the reduction of prices for consumers.
One corollary of the new law is that fewer people will qualify for assistance.

Synset Examples

blind jealousy is a frequent corollary of passionate love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence, outcome, or effect

Etymology

From Latin corollarium (money paid for a garland), later meaning 'additional provision', from corolla (small garland).

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɑːləri / (AmE), / kəˈrɒləri / (BrE)
A proposition that follows directly from a proven statement or theorem.
proposition directly derived from another

Examples

One important corollary of this law is that energy is never lost, only transformed.
The professor explained that the statement was not a new claim, but a direct corollary from established principles.
This result is a corollary of the previous theorem in the chapter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Degree Higher: theorem - a general proposition not self-evident but proved by a chain of reasoning

Etymology

From Latin corollarium (money paid for a garland, gratuity, consequence), from corolla (garland).
