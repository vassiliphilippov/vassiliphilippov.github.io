curfew

Summary

  • an official order requiring people to remain indoors or off the streets after a specified time, usually issued for security or safety reasons
    • NOUN: official restriction on going outdoors after a set time
  • A signal, especially a bell, announcing the time when curfew restrictions begin, traditionally requiring people to be indoors.
    • NOUN: bell or signal marking curfew time
  • the specific time in the evening when people must be indoors or clear the streets, as required by law or custom
    • NOUN: the required time to be off the streets

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜr.fju / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.fjuː / (BrE)
an official order requiring people to remain indoors or off the streets after a specified time, usually issued for security or safety reasons
official restriction on going outdoors after a set time

Examples

After the riot, the police enforced a strict curfew throughout the downtown area.
Teens often complain about their parents setting an early curfew on weekends.
The city imposed a curfew starting at 8 p.m. to ensure public safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lockdown - a state of isolation or restricted access instituted for safety or security

Etymology

From Old French 'cuevrefeu' meaning 'cover fire', originally referring to a signal to extinguish fires at a set time in the evening.

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.fjuː / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.fjuː / (BrE)
the specific time in the evening when people must be indoors or clear the streets, as required by law or custom
the required time to be off the streets

Examples

As the curfew approached, the usually busy square became quiet and deserted.
The city declared that the curfew would begin at 10 p.m. each night.
We hurried home so we wouldn’t be caught outside after the curfew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curfew - an order requiring people to remain indoors at certain times

Etymology

From Old French 'cuevrefeu' (cover fire), originally referring to a bell signaling when to extinguish fires; later to time restrictions.
