illicit

Summary

  • Not allowed by law, rules, or social norms; forbidden or not permitted.
    • ADJ: not allowed by law or morality

Not allowed by law, rules, or social norms; forbidden or not permitted.

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
not allowed by law, morality, or social convention; especially referring to actions or relationships considered morally wrong or forbidden by society.
not allowed by law or morality

Examples

The novel explores the consequences of illicit love in a conservative society.
The politician was forced to resign after his illicit affair became public knowledge.
They met in secret to continue their illicit relationship.

Synset Examples

an illicit association with his secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law
  • Antonym: lawful - conforming to the law

Etymology

From Latin 'illicitus', from 'in-' (not) + 'licēre' (to be allowed).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
forbidden by law, rules, or custom; not allowed or permitted
forbidden by law or rules

Examples

The movie explores the consequences of an illicit relationship.
The police cracked down on the illicit sale of counterfeit goods at the market.
They were arrested for their involvement in illicit activities.

Synset Examples

an illegitimate seizure of power
illicit trade
an outlaw strike
unlawful measures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legal - permitted by law; lawful
  • Antonym: lawful - conforming to, permitted by, or recognized by law or rules
  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law or statute

Etymology

From Latin 'illicitus', from 'in-' (not) + 'licitus' (lawful).
