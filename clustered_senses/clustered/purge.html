1. NOUN

/ pɝdʒ / (AmE), / pɜːdʒ / (BrE)
an act of removing unwanted substances, impurities, or people from a place, organization, or substance, especially by a process of cleansing or elimination
removal of unwanted elements by cleansing

Examples

After the scandal, there was a massive purge within the organization to restore public trust.
The new government ordered a purge of corrupt officials from the administration.
The water treatment process includes a purge to remove harmful bacteria and sediment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleanse - to clean thoroughly; to remove impurities
  • Antonym: contaminate - to make something impure by adding harmful substances

Etymology

From Old French *purgier*, from Latin *purgare* (to cleanse, to purify).
