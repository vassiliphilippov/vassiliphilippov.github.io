vibe

Summary

  • to have a mutual understanding, connection, or positive feeling with someone or something; to resonate emotionally, often used informally
    • VERB: to feel a mutual emotional connection
  • A distinctive emotional quality, atmosphere, or mood that is sensed instinctively about a place, situation, person, or type of music.
    • NOUN: distinctive emotional atmosphere or feeling
    • NOUN: atmosphere or mood of a place or music
to have a mutual understanding, connection, or positive feeling with someone or something; to resonate emotionally, often used informally

1. VERB

/ vaɪb / (AmE), / vaɪb / (BrE)
to have a mutual understanding, connection, or positive feeling with someone or something; to resonate emotionally, often used informally
to feel a mutual emotional connection
informal

Examples

Are you vibing with this music, or should we change it?
I just don't vibe with his sense of humor, so we rarely hang out.
They really vibed at the party and ended up talking all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connect - to form a relationship or feel affinity with someone
  • Synonym: get along - to have a harmonious relationship
  • Antonym: clash - to be in conflict or not get along

Etymology

Shortened from 'vibration,' popularized in African-American Vernacular English and 20th-century slang.
