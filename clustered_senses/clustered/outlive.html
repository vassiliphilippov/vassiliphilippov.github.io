to outlive

Summary

  • to live longer than someone or something; to remain alive after someone or something else has died or ended
    • VERB: live longer than
to live longer than someone or something; to remain alive after someone or something else has died or ended

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈlɪv / (AmE), / aʊtˈlɪv / (BrE)
to live longer than someone or something; to remain alive after someone or something else has died or ended
live longer than

Examples

Despite his illness, he managed to outlive everyone in his family.
No one expected the old oak tree to outlive so many generations of villagers.
Some technologies become obsolete and are outlived by their successors.

Synset Examples

She outlived her husband by many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survive - to continue to live after someone or something else has died or ended

Etymology

From out- (beyond) + live (to remain alive). First used in the 16th century.
