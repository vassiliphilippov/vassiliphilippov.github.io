taunt

Summary

  • to repeatedly mock, tease, or criticize someone in a way that is intended to provoke or upset them
    • VERB: mock or tease persistently
  • A remark, comment, or action intended to mock, provoke, or deride someone.
    • NOUN: mocking or provoking remark
to repeatedly mock, tease, or criticize someone in a way that is intended to provoke or upset them

1. VERB

/ tɑnt / (AmE), / tɔːnt / (BrE)
to repeatedly mock, tease, or criticize someone in a way that is intended to provoke or upset them
mock or tease persistently

Examples

He was taunting me all day about my new haircut.
She taunted her brother by repeatedly reminding him of his mistake.
The opposing fans taunted the team with rude chants after the loss.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful way
  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone

Etymology

Probably from French 'tant' ('so much'), used in the phrase 'tant pour tant', later influenced by the verb form.

1. NOUN

/ tɑnt / (AmE), / tɔːnt / (BrE)
A remark, comment, or action intended to mock, provoke, or deride someone.
mocking or provoking remark

Examples

His cruel taunt left her feeling humiliated in front of the entire class.
Ignoring the bully's latest taunt, he walked away without responding.
The crowd hurled taunts at the opposing team during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - A rude or disrespectful remark or action; synonym
  • Antonym: praise - To express warm approval or admiration; antonym

Etymology

Probably from Old French tant (so much), later influenced by obsolete verb 'taunt' (to reproach).
