The state of being forgotten, unknown, or disregarded.

1. NOUN

/ əˈblɪviən / (AmE), / əˈblɪviən / (BrE)
The state of being completely forgotten or unknown; total forgetfulness.
state of being forgotten

Examples

After years of isolation, her achievements faded into oblivion.
He drank to escape the pain and find oblivion at the bottom of the bottle.
Without proper care, historical sites often fall into oblivion.

Synset Examples

he sought the great oblivion of sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amnesia - loss of memory
  • Antonym: remembrance - the action of remembering

Etymology

From Latin 'oblivio' (forgetfulness), from 'oblivisci' (to forget).

2. NOUN

/ əˈblɪviən / (AmE), / əˈblɪviən / (BrE)
a state in which someone or something is not remembered, used, or thought about; the condition of being forgotten or disregarded
state of being forgotten or ignored

Examples

After decades out of the spotlight, the band faded into oblivion.
Many ancient civilizations have disappeared into oblivion, leaving behind only ruins.
The actor's career fell into oblivion after a string of unsuccessful films.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgetfulness - the state of being unable to recall or remember
  • Antonym: remembrance - the act of remembering or recalling something

Etymology

From Latin 'oblivio' (forgetfulness, oblivion), from 'oblivisci' (to forget).
