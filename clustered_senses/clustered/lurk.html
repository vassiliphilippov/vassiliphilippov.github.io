to lurk

Summary

  • to read or observe discussions on an online forum, chat room, or social media site without actively contributing or making your presence known
    • VERB: observe online discussions without participating
  • To remain hidden or move about secretly, often with a negative or suspicious purpose, such as waiting to ambush or go unnoticed.
    • VERB: to be present secretly or without purpose
    • VERB: wait hidden to attack
    • VERB: move or wait secretly, in hiding

1. VERB

/ lɝk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to read or observe discussions on an online forum, chat room, or social media site without actively contributing or making your presence known
observe online discussions without participating
informal

Examples

I like to lurk on the forum before deciding whether to join the conversation.
Many new users lurk in chat rooms to get a feel for the group.
She had been lurking on the website for weeks before making her first post.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: post - make a public contribution to an online forum or discussion

To remain hidden or move about secretly, often with a negative or suspicious purpose, such as waiting to ambush or go unnoticed.

1. VERB

/ lɝːk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to remain in or around a place secretly or without any obvious purpose, often waiting or observing unnoticed
to be present secretly or without purpose

Examples

Security asked anyone lurking in the hallway without a pass to leave.
The teenagers would often lurk near the playground after dark.
Who is that man lurking outside our building every evening?

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loiter - to stand or wait around idly without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: hang around - to stay in a place without doing anything in particular

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *lurken* (to lie hidden).

2. VERB

/ lɝk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to wait hidden, especially in order to attack someone or do something wrong
wait hidden to attack

Examples

He was caught lurking behind the bushes, waiting for someone to pass by.
The thief decided to lurk in the shadows until his target arrived.
Wolves often lurk near watering holes, hidden from view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - to lie in wait to make a surprise attack
  • Degree Lower: hide - to conceal oneself

Etymology

Probably from Middle English luren or lorken, of obscure origin.

3. VERB

/ lɜrk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to wait or move about secretly, often with the intention of ambush or remaining unnoticed
move or wait secretly, in hiding

Examples

A cat may lurk in the shadows, waiting for an unsuspecting mouse.
Predators often lurk underwater, remaining still until their prey approaches.
She noticed someone lurking near the entrance and alerted security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - to conceal oneself or stay out of sight
  • Synonym: ambush - to attack by surprise after hiding and waiting
  • Antonym: emerge - to come out into view after being hidden

Etymology

From Middle English *lurken*, likely of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish *lurke* (to lie in wait).
