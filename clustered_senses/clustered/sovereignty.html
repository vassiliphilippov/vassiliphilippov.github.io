Examples

International law often debates issues of sovereignty when one state interferes in another's affairs.

The treaty transferred sovereignty of the region from one country to another.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsɑvərənti / (AmE), / ˈsɒvərənti / (BrE)
(in law and digital policy) the claimed authority of a state to control, regulate, or require storage and handling of digital data and information located or processed within its jurisdiction.
authority over digital data
legal

Examples

Many countries claim sovereignty over user data stored on servers within their borders.
Debates about cloud computing often focus on national sovereignty concerning cross-border data flows and access.
The law asserted digital sovereignty by requiring companies to keep citizens' records on local servers.
