incumbent

Summary

  • The person who currently holds an office or official position.
    • NOUN: current officeholder
  • (of a person) currently holding a particular office or position, especially in politics or business.
    • ADJ: currently holding office or position
  • (of a duty) necessary for someone as a responsibility or obligation; morally binding.
    • ADJ: necessary as a duty or responsibility
  • (of an object or stratum) lying or resting on something else, especially in scientific or geological contexts.
    • ADJ: resting or lying on something else

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌmbənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmbənt / (BrE)
The person who currently holds an office or official position.
current officeholder

Examples

She defeated the incumbent in a closely contested mayoral race.
The incumbent faces a tough re-election battle this year.
Voters often compare the incumbent with the challenger during campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: challenger - a person who competes against the incumbent for an office

Etymology

From Latin incumbens, present participle of incumbere (to lean or press upon).
