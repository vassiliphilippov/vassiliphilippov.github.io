desolation

Summary

  • A state of complete destruction, ruin, or emptiness, especially in a place or landscape.
    • NOUN: total destruction or devastation
    • NOUN: state of ruin or destruction
    • NOUN: a state of bleak emptiness or abandonment
  • A feeling of deep sadness and emptiness caused by being abandoned or left alone.

A state of complete destruction, ruin, or emptiness, especially in a place or landscape.

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an event or state resulting in complete destruction or ruin; severe devastation
total destruction or devastation

Examples

After the hurricane, scenes of desolation stretched for miles along the coast.
The forest fires left a trail of desolation in their wake.
The war brought utter desolation to the once vibrant city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - severe and overwhelming damage or destruction
  • Synonym: ruin - the state of being destroyed or badly damaged

Etymology

From Latin 'desolatio', from 'desolare' (to leave alone, lay waste).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being ruined, decayed, or destroyed, often resulting in emptiness or barrenness.
state of ruin or destruction

Examples

After the wildfire, the landscape was marked by desolation and silence.
The once-thriving town fell into desolation after the factory closed.
The war left nothing behind but desolation throughout the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - the process or state of being destroyed or decayed
  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being successful, especially economically

Etymology

From Latin desolatio (abandonment, devastation), from desolare (to leave alone, lay waste).

1. NOUN

sadness due to being abandoned

Examples

The announcement filled him with desolation, cutting all hope from his heart.
When his friends moved away, he was left in complete desolation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - deep sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Latin desolatio (abandonment), from desolare (to leave alone, desert).
