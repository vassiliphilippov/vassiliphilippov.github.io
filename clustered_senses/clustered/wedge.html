A piece or object with a thick end that tapers to a thin edge or point, often used to split, secure, or hold things in place; also refers to a wedge-shaped piece of something.

1. NOUN

/ wɛdʒ / (AmE), / wɛdʒ / (BrE)
a piece of solid material with a thick end that tapers to a thin edge, used for forcing things apart, securing or tightening things, or as a simple tool to split or lift objects
piece of material used to force things apart

Examples

A small wedge of metal was placed between the rocks to pry them apart.
He used a wedge of wood to hold the door open.
The axe blade acts as a wedge to split the logs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: block - a solid piece of material with flat surfaces, often rectangular

Etymology

Old English wecg, related to Dutch wig and German Wekke.
