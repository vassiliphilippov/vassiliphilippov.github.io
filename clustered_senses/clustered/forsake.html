to abandon someone or something, especially when they most need your help; to leave in a time of need.

1. VERB

/ fərˈseɪk / (AmE), / fəˈseɪk / (BrE)
to abandon someone or something, especially when they most need your help; to leave in a time of need.
abandon someone in need

Examples

After the scandal, many of his supporters chose to forsake him.
She promised never to forsake her friends during difficult times.
The city seemed forsaken by all hope after the disaster.

Synset Examples

The mother deserted her children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something behind, especially permanently
  • Synonym: desert - to leave someone without help or in a difficult situation
  • Antonym: support - to give help or assistance to someone

Etymology

From Old English 'forsacan' (to renounce, refuse), from for- (away, off) + sacan (to dispute).
