  • a state of nervous confusion or agitation

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌstə / (BrE)
a state of nervous confusion or agitation
state of confused agitation

Examples

His arrival caused a slight fluster among the staff, who were not expecting him.
She was in such a fluster that she forgot her keys at home.
Trying to answer all the questions at once put him into a fluster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of being unclear or disordered
  • Antonym: composure - calmness and self-control

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete verb meaning to hurry or confuse, origin uncertain, possibly imitative.

1. VERB

/ ˈflʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌstə / (BrE)
to make someone nervous, confused, or agitated, often so that they cannot think or act clearly
to make or become confused or nervous

Examples

She spoke confidently and didn't allow the interruption to fluster her.
The sudden announcement seemed to fluster the new manager during the press conference.
Try not to let the tough questions fluster you during your job interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful and relaxed

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from obsolete 'flust' (to hurry), influenced by 'bluster'.

2. VERB

/ ˈflʌstər / (AmE), / ˈflʌstə / (BrE)
to make someone nervous, confused, or upset, often so that they are unable to think clearly
make nervous or confused

Examples

Do not fluster the presenter with difficult questions.
He tried to remain calm, but the unexpected news flustered him.
Her sudden arrival seemed to fluster everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agitate - to make someone feel anxious or disturbed
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone feel relaxed and peaceful

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete Dutch *flusteren* (to hurry).
