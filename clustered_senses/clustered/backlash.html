backlash

Summary

  • a sudden backward movement in response to an impact, often referring to physical, mechanical, or engineering contexts
    • NOUN: a backward movement after impact
  • A strong and adverse reaction by many people, especially to a social or political event or statement.
    • NOUN: strong adverse reaction to events
    • NOUN: a strong negative reaction from the public
  • (of an action or statement) to have an unintended negative effect that rebounds on the originator; to backfire.
    • VERB: have an unintended negative rebound effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.læʃ / (BrE)
a sudden backward movement in response to an impact, often referring to physical, mechanical, or engineering contexts
a backward movement after impact

Examples

If the hammer strikes too hard, a backlash may occur, potentially causing damage to the tool.
Proper maintenance can reduce backlash and keep the system functioning smoothly.
The engineer noticed a backlash in the gear mechanism when the machine suddenly stopped.

Etymology

From back + lash, originally referring to a sharp backward movement of a whip.
