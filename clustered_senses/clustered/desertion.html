desertion

Summary

  • The act of leaving or abandoning someone, a place, or a responsibility, especially when one is expected to stay or support.
    • NOUN: the act of abandoning or giving up
    • NOUN: leaving someone or something you should support
The act of leaving or abandoning someone, a place, or a responsibility, especially when one is expected to stay or support.

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝːʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving or abandoning someone, a place, or a responsibility, especially without intending to return; the act of giving something up
the act of abandoning or giving up

Examples

His sudden desertion of the project surprised the whole team.
She felt a sense of desertion when her friends left her alone.
The desertion of the post by the soldiers was considered a serious offense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of leaving something or someone permanently and without intention to return

Etymology

From Latin 'desertio', from 'deserere' (to abandon).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝːʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving or withdrawing support from someone or something to whom one owes allegiance or responsibility, especially when this causes harm or difficulty
leaving someone or something you should support

Examples

After his father's desertion, Mark had to take care of his siblings.
The desertion of the soldiers caused panic among their comrades.
There was widespread anger at the desertion of the community by local officials.

Synset Examples

his abandonment of his wife and children left them penniless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of leaving someone or something completely and finally
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal or faithful to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'desertio', from 'deserere' (to abandon).
