liege

Summary

  • a feudal lord to whom allegiance and service are owed, especially in the context of the medieval feudal system
    • NOUN: feudal lord entitled to service
  • a person who holds land under the feudal system and owes allegiance and service to a lord
    • NOUN: feudal vassal or subject
  • (historical, archaic) Owing or owed feudal allegiance and service; loyal to a feudal superior.
    • ADJ: owing feudal allegiance; loyal to a lord

1. NOUN

/ lidʒ / (AmE), / liːdʒ / (BrE)
a feudal lord to whom allegiance and service are owed, especially in the context of the medieval feudal system
feudal lord entitled to service

Examples

He served his liege faithfully throughout the long and turbulent wars.
In the castle hall, the knights pledged their loyalty to their liege.
The liege summoned all his vassals for a council of war.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vassal - a person who held land from a feudal lord and owed him allegiance

Etymology

From Old French *lige*, from Medieval Latin *ligius* (bound, obliged).

1. NOUN

/ liːdʒ / (AmE), / liːdʒ / (BrE)
a person who holds land under the feudal system and owes allegiance and service to a lord
feudal vassal or subject

Examples

During the Middle Ages, every liege had certain duties and rights defined by law.
The liege was required to swear loyalty to his feudal lord before receiving the fief.
The king summoned his lieges to aid in the defense of the realm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vassal - a person in a subordinate position who owes allegiance and service to a lord
  • Antonym: lord - a man of noble rank or high office, especially in feudal times

Etymology

From Old French *lige*, from Medieval Latin *ligius* (entitled, legitimate, or faithful).
