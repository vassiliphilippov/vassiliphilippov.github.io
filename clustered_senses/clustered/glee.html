1. NOUN

/ ɡli / (AmE), / ɡliː / (BrE)
a feeling or expression of great delight or joy; merriment
great joy or delight

Examples

He danced around the room in pure glee after hearing the good news.
She could barely contain her glee when she opened the acceptance letter.
The children shouted with glee as they ran through the sprinklers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - a feeling of great pleasure and satisfaction
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering or unhappiness

Etymology

From Old English *glēo* (music, entertainment, joy), related to Middle English *gle* (joy, pleasure).

1. NOUN

/ ɡliː / (AmE), / ɡliː / (BrE)
great delight, often with a sense of malicious satisfaction at someone else's misfortune or discomfort.
malicious or delighted satisfaction

Examples

She could barely hide her glee at her rival's embarrassing mistake.
The critics watched with glee as the company experienced another setback.
There was a look of glee on his face when he learned of their defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or joy
  • Synonym: schadenfreude - pleasure derived from another person's misfortune
  • Antonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment

Etymology

From Old English gléo (entertainment, music, joy).
