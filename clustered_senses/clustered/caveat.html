caveat

Summary

  • (law) A formal notice filed with a court or official to suspend a proceeding until the filer is given a hearing.
    • NOUN: formal legal notice to suspend proceedings
  • a warning or caution about something, especially to prevent misunderstanding or discourage certain actions
    • NOUN: a warning or caution
(law) A formal notice filed with a court or official to suspend a proceeding until the filer is given a hearing.

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.vi.ˌæt / (AmE), / ˈkæv.i.ˌæt / (BrE)
(law) A formal notice filed with a court or official to suspend a proceeding until the filer is given a hearing.
formal legal notice to suspend proceedings
legal

Examples

A caveat was placed on the inheritance until all claims could be heard.
Before the property could be sold, a caveat had to be resolved by the court.
The lawyer filed a caveat with the court to prevent the immediate approval of the will.

Synset Examples

a caveat filed against the probate of a will

Etymology

From Latin caveat, meaning 'let him beware'.
