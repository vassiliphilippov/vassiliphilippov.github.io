witless

Summary

  • (used after verbs such as 'scare' or 'bore') extremely; to a very great degree.
    • ADJ: to the utmost degree (in phrases like 'scared witless')
  • (of a person) lacking sense, intelligence, or good judgment; foolish or stupid.
    • ADJ: lacking sense or intelligence
(used after verbs such as 'scare' or 'bore') extremely; to a very great degree.

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtlɪs / (BrE)
(used after verbs such as 'scare' or 'bore') extremely; to a very great degree.
to the utmost degree (in phrases like 'scared witless')

Examples

She was bored witless by the dull lecture on accounting principles.
The children screamed witless when the roller coaster took its first big drop.
The hikers were lost and scared witless by the noises in the dark forest.

Synset Examples

he was scared witless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shitless - to an extreme degree (in phrases such as 'bored shitless')

Etymology

From 'wit' meaning 'intelligence' + '-less'.

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtləs / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking sense, intelligence, or good judgment; foolish or stupid.
lacking sense or intelligence

Examples

His witless behavior often got him into trouble.
Only a witless person would leave their keys in the car with the engine running.
The witless remark caused everyone in the meeting to cringe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensible - having or showing good sense

Etymology

From Old English 'wit' (understanding, sense) + '-less' (without).
