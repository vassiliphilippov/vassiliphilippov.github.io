to provide and serve food and drinks, especially for events or groups, often as a professional service

1. VERB

/ ˈkeɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
to provide and serve food and drinks, especially for social events such as parties and banquets
provide food for events

Examples

She has decided to cater private parties on weekends.
The company will cater the wedding for 150 guests.
They often cater corporate events and business meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provide - to supply something that is needed or wanted
  • Synonym: supply - to make something available to someone as needed

Etymology

From French *acater* (to buy, provide), from Latin *acceptare* (to accept, receive).

2. VERB

/ ˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
to provide what is needed or desired, especially food and drink, for an event or group of people
provide what is needed, especially food or services

Examples

The restaurant can cater for large parties and corporate events.
They hired a company to cater their wedding reception.
We always try to cater to the needs of our customers.

Synset Examples

The hostess provided lunch for all the guests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provide - to supply or make available what is needed

Etymology

From French 'acater' (to buy), from Latin 'acceptare' (to accept, receive).

3. VERB

/ ˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
to provide and serve food and drinks for an event or gathering, often as a professional service
provide food and service for events

Examples

If you want us to cater your event, please contact us two weeks in advance.
Many families choose to cater big celebrations rather than cook themselves.
The hotel will cater the conference with breakfast and lunch each day.

Synset Examples

We hired a company to cater the wedding reception.
They decided to cater their own party to save money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply - to provide something that is needed or wanted
  • Synonym: serve - to bring food or drink to people

Etymology

From French 'acater' (to buy), from Latin 'acceptare' (to accept).
