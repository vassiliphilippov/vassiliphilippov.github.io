to force or remove something or someone from a place or position where it is fixed or established

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈlɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒ / (BrE)
to force or knock something or someone out of its place or position
force from position

Examples

Criticism may dislodge established beliefs, leading to meaningful change.
He used a stick to dislodge the ball from the tree branches.
The wind was strong enough to dislodge several tiles from the roof.

Synset Examples

Shift one's position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away or get rid of it
  • Synonym: displace - to move something from its usual or proper place
  • Antonym: secure - to fix or attach firmly so it cannot be displaced

Etymology

From Old French *deslogier* (to remove from a place), from *des-* (dis-) + *logier* (to lodge).

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈlɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒ / (BrE)
to remove or force (someone or something) from a position or place where they have been firmly established
remove from a position or place

Examples

After much effort, I finally managed to dislodge the stuck drawer.
Strong winds can easily dislodge loose tiles from the roof.
The rescue workers had to dislodge the debris before reaching the survivors.

Synset Examples

The new employee dislodged her by moving into her office space

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - take away from a place or position
  • Synonym: evict - force someone to leave a place or position
  • Antonym: anchor - fix firmly in position; to secure

Etymology

From Old French 'deslodger', from 'des-' (dis-) + 'loger' (to lodge).
