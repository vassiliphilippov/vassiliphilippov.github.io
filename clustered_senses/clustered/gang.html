1. NOUN

/ ɡæŋ / (AmE), / ɡæŋ / (BrE)
a group of workers who work together, especially on manual or industrial tasks
group of workers

Examples

A gang of builders was busy laying bricks at the new site.
He led a gang of electricians during the factory renovation project.
The road repair gang started work at dawn to avoid the traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crew - a group of people who work together, especially on a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From Old English *gang* (a going, journey, way), related to Old Norse *gangr*, German *Gang* (a going, walk).
