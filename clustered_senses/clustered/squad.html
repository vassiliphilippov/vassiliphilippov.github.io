A small group of people organized for a particular purpose, such as a team in sports, a military unit, or a group of police officers.

1. NOUN

/ skwɑd / (AmE), / skwɒd / (BrE)
a small military unit consisting of about 8 to 12 soldiers, typically forming part of a platoon
small military unit

Examples

During the exercise, the squad practiced moving in formation and communicating silently.
Only one squad managed to reach the top of the hill before dawn.
The leader assigned each squad a specific section of the perimeter to guard.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: platoon - a larger military unit consisting of several squads

Etymology

From French 'escouade', from Spanish 'escuadra', meaning 'squadron'.

2. NOUN

/ skwɑd / (AmE), / skwɒd / (BrE)
a small group of police officers trained to deal with a particular type of crime or issue
specialized police unit

Examples

A bomb disposal squad was called to the scene after the suspicious package was found.
The anti-fraud squad investigates financial crimes and scams.
The city formed a special squad to tackle drug-related crimes in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - a group forming part of a larger organization

Etymology

From French 'escouade', from Spanish 'escuadra' (square, squad).

3. NOUN

/ skwɑd / (AmE), / skwɒd / (BrE)
a small group of people organized for a particular purpose, especially a team in sports or a unit in the military or other organizations
small group or sports team

Examples

Our squad practiced every evening to prepare for the tournament.
The coach selected his best squad for the final match.
The new player was excited to join the basketball squad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: team - a group of people forming one side in a competitive game or sport

Etymology

From French 'escouade', from Spanish 'escuadra' (squad, section), originally from Latin 'exquadra' (a square).
