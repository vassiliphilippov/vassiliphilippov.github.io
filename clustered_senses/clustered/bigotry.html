1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪɡətri / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡətri / (BrE)
intolerance toward those who hold different opinions or belong to different groups.
intolerance; prejudice against differing groups

Examples

He spoke out against religious bigotry during his speech.
Her actions were motivated by deep-seated bigotry and fear of outsiders.
Racial bigotry continues to be a serious issue in many parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tolerance - the willingness to accept others' differences
  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas

Etymology

From French *bigoterie* (bigoted behavior).
