1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛtrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛtrɪmənt / (BrE)
Harm or damage; a loss or injury to something or someone.
harm or damage

Examples

Lack of sleep will be to your detriment in the long run.
They continued their argument to the detriment of the project's success.
Working late every night can be a serious detriment to your health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harm - physical or mental damage or injury
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something

Etymology

From Latin 'detrimentum' (a rubbing or wearing away, loss, damage).
