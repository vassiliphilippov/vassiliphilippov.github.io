(Grammar) a word or phrase, such as 'some', 'many', or 'fifteen', that indicates quantity.
word expressing quantity in grammar

Examples

'Much' is a common quantifier used with uncountable nouns.
The quantifier in 'Several students arrived early' is 'several'.
Words like 'all', 'few', and 'many' are quantifiers in English grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determiner - (Grammar) a word that introduces a noun and limits its meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'quantus' (how much) + '-fier' (maker).

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːntɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
(logic) a word or expression, such as 'some', 'all', or 'no', used to indicate the quantity of subjects to which a proposition applies.
word expressing quantity in logic

Examples

In formal logic, a quantifier determines how many objects a proposition refers to.
The quantifier 'some' indicates the existence of at least one example.
The statement 'all dogs are mammals' contains the quantifier 'all'.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: universal quantifier - a quantifier expressing 'for all' or 'every'
  • Degree Lower: existential quantifier - a quantifier expressing 'there exists' or 'some'

Etymology

From Latin *quantificare* (to make quantity), from *quantus* (how much).
