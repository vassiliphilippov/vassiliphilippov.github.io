stale

Summary

  • no longer new, interesting, or original; lacking freshness or originality
    • ADJ: no longer new or original
  • No longer fresh, pleasant, or good to eat or use, usually because of age or exposure to air.
    • ADJ: not fresh; deteriorated by age

1. ADJ

/ steɪl / (AmE), / steɪl / (BrE)
no longer new, interesting, or original; lacking freshness or originality
no longer new or original

Examples

Many students found the teacher's lectures stale and uninspiring.
The comedian's jokes had become stale after years of repetition.
The headline was already stale by the time it appeared in the newspaper.

Synset Examples

moth-eaten theories about race
stale news

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - new or original in character; recently created or experienced
  • Synonym: trite - (of a remark or idea) lacking originality or freshness

Etymology

From Old English 'stæl' meaning 'place', later associated with 'old' or 'not fresh'.

1. ADJ

/ steɪl / (AmE), / steɪl / (BrE)
No longer fresh, pleasant, or good to eat or use, usually because of age or exposure to air.
not fresh; deteriorated by age

Examples

Nobody wanted to drink the stale coffee that had been sitting in the pot for hours.
The cookies became stale after being left out overnight.
We threw away the stale bread because it was too hard to eat.

Synset Examples

stale bread
the beer was stale

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - recently made, obtained, or prepared; not stale

Etymology

From Old French 'estal' (station, position), later from Old English 'stæl' meaning 'stall, stable'.
