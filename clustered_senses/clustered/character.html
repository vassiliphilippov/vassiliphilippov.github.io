character

Summary

  • the set of qualities or features that make something or someone distinctive; the individual nature of a person, place, or thing
    • NOUN: distinctive quality or nature
  • The combination of qualities and traits that form the moral and ethical nature of a person; the quality of having strong moral principles, integrity, and good reputation.
    • NOUN: a person's moral and ethical qualities
    • NOUN: good moral reputation; integrity
  • a person, especially one with distinctive or unusual traits, often considered eccentric or unique
    • NOUN: person with distinctive or eccentric traits
  • A part played by an actor in a play, film, or television show, or a person represented in a work of fiction.
    • NOUN: part played by an actor
    • NOUN: person in a fictional work
  • a written or printed symbol, such as a letter, numeral, or punctuation mark, used to represent speech or writing
    • NOUN: written symbol; letter or sign
  • (Genetics) a structural or functional attribute of an organism, such as eye color or blood type, determined by a gene or group of genes.
    • NOUN: genetic attribute determined by genes
  • a formal statement, often by a previous employer, recommending a person's qualifications and dependability to a potential employer
    • NOUN: letter of recommendation or reference

Examples
