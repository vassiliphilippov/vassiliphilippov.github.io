A state of agreement or harmony between people, groups, or opinions.

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɔrd / (AmE), / əˈkɔːd / (BrE)
a state of harmony or agreement; sympathetic compatibility between people or groups
state of harmony or agreement

Examples

Despite their differences, the diplomats worked in perfect accord to resolve the crisis.
Her words were in accord with her actions, which gained her everyone's trust.
The new manager brought the team into greater accord with one another.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discord - lack of harmony or agreement
  • Synonym: harmony - the quality of forming a pleasing and consistent whole

Etymology

From Old French 'acord', from Latin 'accordare' meaning 'to bring into agreement'.
