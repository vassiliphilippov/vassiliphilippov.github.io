sway

Summary

  • a movement or inclination to one side, especially a side-to-side or pitching motion
    • NOUN: side-to-side or pitching movement
  • To move or cause to move slowly or rhythmically backward and forward or from side to side; to walk or move in a swinging or rocking motion.
    • VERB: move back and forth
    • VERB: move or walk with a swinging motion
    • VERB: move back and forth or sideways
  • power or influence that controls or affects people, decisions, or events.
    • NOUN: controlling influence or power
  • to influence someone so that they change their opinion or decision
    • VERB: influence someone's opinion or decision

1. NOUN

/ sweɪ / (AmE), / sweɪ / (BrE)
a movement or inclination to one side, especially a side-to-side or pitching motion
side-to-side or pitching movement

Examples

A gentle sway of the bridge could be felt in the wind.
He adjusted his stance to match the sway of the dance floor crowd.
The ship's sway made it difficult for the passengers to walk steadily.

Etymology

From Old Norse sveigja (to bend, sway).
