buttress

Summary

  • a structure built against a wall to support or strengthen it, usually made of stone or brick
    • NOUN: wall support structure
  • to support, strengthen, or reinforce something, especially an argument or position
    • VERB: support or strengthen
  • to support or reinforce a structure with a projecting support built against its wall

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtrəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrɪs / (BrE)
a structure built against a wall to support or strengthen it, usually made of stone or brick
wall support structure

Examples

Medieval architects often designed churches with flying buttresses for additional support.
Rainwater ran down the old brick buttress at the side of the building.
The cathedral's massive buttress helps prevent the walls from collapsing outward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that bears the weight of another thing or keeps it upright

Etymology

From Old French *bouterez*, related to *bouter* (to thrust against).
