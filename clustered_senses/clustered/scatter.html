To throw, spread, or distribute things over a wide area in a random or irregular way.

1. VERB

/ ˈskædɚ / (AmE), / ˈskætə / (BrE)
to throw or distribute things so that they cover a wide area, often in a random or irregular way
to spread things over an area

Examples

Autumn leaves were scattered by the wind across the park.
She likes to scatter birdseed in the garden every morning.
The children scattered their toys all over the living room.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to distribute something over an area or surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'scateren', likely of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish 'skatre', Norwegian 'skatra'.
