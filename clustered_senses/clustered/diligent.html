diligent

Summary

  • Showing careful, persistent, and thorough effort in work or duties.
    • ADJ: showing persistent care in tasks
    • ADJ: careful and persistent in work or effort
Showing careful, persistent, and thorough effort in work or duties.

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪlədʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Showing care and perseverance in doing work or carrying out duties.
showing persistent care in tasks

Examples

He received an award for his diligent work as a volunteer.
She is a diligent student who always completes her assignments on time.
The team made a diligent effort to solve the complex problem.

Synset Examples

a diligent detective investigates all clues
a diligent search of the files

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrious - hard-working; showing effort and persistence
  • Antonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy; idle

Etymology

From Latin 'diligens', present participle of 'diligere' meaning 'to value, esteem, love'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪlədʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
showing careful and persistent effort or work, especially in paying attention to detail
careful and persistent in work or effort

Examples

His diligent efforts at work soon earned him a promotion.
She is a diligent student who always completes her assignments on time.
The manager praised the team for their diligent attention to every detail of the project.

Synset Examples

a diligent (or patient) worker
with persevering (or patient) industry she revived the failing business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrious - hard-working and diligent
  • Antonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy; opposite of diligent

Etymology

From Latin 'diligens', present participle of 'diligere' (to value, esteem, love, choose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01740371-s
Synset POS: s
