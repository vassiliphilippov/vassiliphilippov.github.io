pristine

Summary

  • Extremely clean, fresh, and in an untouched or unspoiled state; spotless and as if new.
    • ADJ: spotlessly clean and unused
    • ADJ: immaculately clean or untouched
Extremely clean, fresh, and in an untouched or unspoiled state; spotless and as if new.

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪstin / (AmE), / ˈprɪstiːn / (BrE)
extremely clean and unused; spotless and fresh as if new
spotlessly clean and unused

Examples

After hours of cleaning, the kitchen was finally pristine again.
She wore a pristine white dress to the ceremony.
The rental car was delivered in pristine condition, without a scratch.

Synset Examples

handed her his pristine white handkerchief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from stain or blemish
  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean, without any stain
  • Antonym: dirty - covered or marked with an unclean substance

Etymology

From Latin pristinus (former, original, primitive).

in a clean and untouched state; completely free from dirt, contamination, or decay
immaculately clean or untouched

Examples
