fruition

Summary

  • The realization or achievement of something desired, such as a plan, project, or goal, often with the satisfaction that comes from it.
    • NOUN: realization or achievement of something desired
    • NOUN: enjoyment or satisfaction from use or possession
  • The state or process of bearing fruit; the period when fruit is produced by a plant.
    • NOUN: the bearing of fruit (botany)
The realization or achievement of something desired, such as a plan, project, or goal, often with the satisfaction that comes from it.

1. NOUN

/ fruˈɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / fruˈɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the state or action of something being realized or accomplished; the fulfillment or achievement of a plan, project, or desire
realization or achievement of something desired

Examples

After years of dedication, her research finally came to fruition with the release of the new drug.
Many ideas are discussed, but only a few ever reach fruition.
The team's hard work will come to fruition when the project is completed.

Synset Examples

the victory was the realization of a whole year's work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realization - the achievement of something desired or planned

Etymology

From Late Latin *fruitionem* (enjoyment, use), from Latin *frui* (to enjoy).

1. NOUN

/ fruˈɪʃən / (AmE), / fruˈɪʃən / (BrE)
The state or process of bearing fruit; the period when fruit is produced by a plant.
the bearing of fruit (botany)

Examples

After months of care, the vineyard finally came to fruition with abundant grape clusters.
In agriculture, timing is crucial to ensure that crops reach fruition before the weather turns cold.
The apple trees reached fruition in early September, filling the orchard with ripe fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripeness - state of being fully developed or mature, especially as fruit

Etymology

From Late Latin *fruitionem* (enjoyment, use), from Latin *frui* (to enjoy).
