fester

Summary

  • (of a sore or wound) to become infected and generate pus; to become worse or more intense, especially through long-term neglect.
    • VERB: (of a wound) to become infected and produce pus
  • A sore or wound that has become inflamed and produced pus.
    • NOUN: an inflamed sore with pus
(of a sore or wound) to become infected and generate pus; to become worse or more intense, especially through long-term neglect.

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛstɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛstə / (BrE)
(of a sore or wound) to become infected and generate pus; to become worse or more intense, especially through long-term neglect.
(of a wound) to become infected and produce pus

Examples

Her wounds began to fester after several days without medical care.
If not properly treated, the cut may fester and lead to serious infection.
The untreated injury continued to fester beneath the bandage.

Synset Examples

her wounds are festering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppurate - to form or discharge pus

Etymology

From Old French *festier*, from Latin *festāre* (to rot).

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛstər / (AmE), / ˈfɛstə / (BrE)
A sore or wound that has become inflamed and produced pus.
an inflamed sore with pus

Examples

If a fester is not properly cleaned, it can lead to a serious infection.
The nurse carefully examined the patient's fester to determine the best course of treatment.
The untreated wound turned into a painful fester on his leg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abscess - a swollen area in body tissue containing pus

Etymology

From Old French *festre* (to fester), from Latin *fistula* (pipe, ulcer).
