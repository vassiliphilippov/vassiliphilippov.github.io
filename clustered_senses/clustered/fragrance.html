A pleasant, sweet, or distinctive smell; a pleasing scent.

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪɡrəns / (AmE), / ˈfreɪɡrəns / (BrE)
a pleasant, sweet smell; a pleasing scent
pleasant, sweet smell

Examples

Even after he left, the fragrance of his cologne lingered in the air.
She chose a candle with a vanilla fragrance for the living room.
The fragrance of blooming roses filled the entire garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aroma - a distinctive, typically pleasant smell
  • Antonym: odor - a particular smell, especially an unpleasant one
  • Synonym: perfume - a pleasant-smelling liquid worn on the body

Etymology

From Old French 'fragrance', from Latin 'fragrantia' (sweet smell), from 'fragrare' (to smell sweet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪɡrəns / (AmE), / ˈfreɪɡrəns / (BrE)
a pleasant and distinctive smell, especially one that is sweet or agreeable
pleasant or sweet smell

Examples

Freshly baked bread has a comforting fragrance that fills the entire house.
Many people wear a signature fragrance as part of their daily routine.
The garden was filled with the fragrance of blooming roses on a warm summer evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scent - a distinctive smell, especially a pleasant one
  • Synonym: perfume - a pleasant smell, especially one that is artificial or used as a cosmetic
  • Antonym: odor - a smell, especially an unpleasant one

Etymology

From Latin 'fragrantia', from 'fragrare' (to smell sweet).
