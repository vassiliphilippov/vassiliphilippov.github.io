done or shown openly; not hidden, secret, or concealed

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈvɜrt / (AmE), / əʊˈvɜːt / (BrE)
done or shown openly; not hidden, secret, or concealed
openly visible; not hidden

Examples

She showed overt affection for her children, hugging them in public.
The company made an overt attempt to seek public approval for its new policy.
There was overt hostility between the two rival teams during the match.

Synset Examples

an overt lie
overt hostility
overt intelligence gathering
open ballots

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed; hidden or secret

Etymology

From Old French *overt* (opened), from Latin *apertus* (open).
