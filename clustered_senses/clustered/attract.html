1. VERB

/ əˈtrækt / (AmE), / əˈtrækt / (BrE)
to cause someone to have a liking or interest in something; to draw attention or appeal to someone or something
to draw interest or attention

Examples

Bright colors often attract children's attention.
The aroma of freshly baked bread attracted people to the bakery.
The museum hopes to attract more visitors with its new exhibition.

Synset Examples

The idea of a vacation appeals to me
The beautiful garden attracted many people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repel - to drive or force back; to be distasteful to
  • Synonym: appeal - to be attractive or interesting

Etymology

From Latin attrahere, from ad- (to) + trahere (to draw).

1. VERB

/ əˈtrækt / (AmE), / əˈtrækt / (BrE)
(of a force or object) to cause something to move toward itself or prevent it from moving away, especially as in physics.
cause to move toward; pull in (physics)

Examples

Charged objects can attract or repel each other depending on their polarity.
The Sun's gravity attracts the planets, keeping them in orbit.
The magnet will attract any pieces of metal nearby.

Synset Examples

the gravitational pull of a planet attracts other bodies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repel - to drive or force (something) away; to push away, especially in physics

Etymology

From Latin 'attrahere' meaning 'to draw toward.'
