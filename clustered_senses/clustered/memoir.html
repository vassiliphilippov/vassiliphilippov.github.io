1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmwɑr / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.wɑː / (BrE)
A written account in which someone describes experiences from their own life.
written account of one's own life

Examples

He spent years writing his memoir about his travels across Asia.
Her best-selling memoir gives insight into her childhood struggles.
The author reveals personal secrets in her latest memoir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autobiography - an account of a person's life written by that person, usually more comprehensive than a memoir

Etymology

From French 'mémoire' (memory, reminiscence), from Latin 'memoria' (memory).

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmwɑr / (AmE), / ˈmɛmwɑː / (BrE)
An essay or written account on a scientific or scholarly topic, often published as part of academic literature.
scholarly or scientific essay

Examples

His memoir addressed advances in medical research over the last decade.
She published a memoir on the early theories of electricity.
The journal featured a memoir summarizing the latest archaeological findings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treatise - a written work dealing formally with a subject
  • Synonym: monograph - a detailed scholarly paper on a single subject

Etymology

From French *mémoire* (memorandum, note), ultimately from Latin *memoria* (memory).
