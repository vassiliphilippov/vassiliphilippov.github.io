definitive

Summary

  • Clearly defined, final, and not open to doubt or change.
    • ADJ: clearly defined or decisive
    • ADJ: final or conclusive; decisive
  • (of a book, study, or work) recognized as the most authoritative and complete; considered to be the best of its kind.
    • ADJ: most authoritative or complete
Clearly defined, final, and not open to doubt or change.

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɪnɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪnɪtɪv / (BrE)
Clearly defined, formulated, or decided; not vague or doubtful.
clearly defined or decisive

Examples

She gave a definitive answer, leaving no room for confusion.
The committee reached a definitive conclusion after reviewing all the evidence.
The company's policy now has definitive guidelines for employee conduct.

Synset Examples

the plain and unequivocal language of the laws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation
  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin definitīvus (defining, setting boundaries), from dēfīnīre (to define).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɪnətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(of a conclusion or settlement) reached decisively and with authority; final and not able to be changed.
final or conclusive; decisive

Examples

The judge delivered a definitive ruling that ended the long-standing dispute.
This edition is considered the definitive version of the novel.
We are waiting for a definitive answer to know how to proceed.

Synset Examples

a definitive verdict
a determinate answer to the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: final - coming at the end; last in a series or process
  • Antonym: tentative - not certain or fixed; provisional

Etymology

From Latin definitīvus ('finite, final'), from dēfīnīre ('to limit, fix').
