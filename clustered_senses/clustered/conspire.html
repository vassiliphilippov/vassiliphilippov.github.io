to conspire

Summary

  • To secretly plan with others to do something harmful, illegal, or deceitful.
    • VERB: make secret plans; plot together
    • VERB: to secretly plan something illegal or harmful
To secretly plan with others to do something harmful, illegal, or deceitful.

1. VERB

/ kənˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / kənˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to make secret plans with others to do something unlawful or harmful
make secret plans; plot together

Examples

A group of employees decided to conspire against their boss by leaking confidential documents.
Detectives discovered that the suspects had conspired for months before carrying out the theft.
The two nations were accused of trying to conspire to manipulate the election results.

Synset Examples

They conspired to overthrow the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - secretly make plans to carry out an illegal or harmful act
  • Synonym: scheme - make secret plans, often for deceitful or illegal purposes

Etymology

From Latin conspirare (to agree, plot), from com- (together) + spirare (to breathe).

2. VERB

/ kənˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / kənˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to secretly plan together to do something harmful, illegal, or deceitful
to secretly plan something illegal or harmful

Examples

Authorities arrested the group after learning they were conspiring to commit fraud.
It was discovered that they had conspired for months to rig the competition.
Several employees conspired to steal sensitive information from the company.

Synset Examples

The two companies conspired to cause the value of the stock to fall

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: collaborate - to work together on an activity or project, often with a neutral or positive connotation
  • Synonym: connive - to secretly allow or be involved in wrongdoing, often with passive approval

Etymology

From Latin conspirare (to plot, act in harmony), from con- (together) + spirare (to breathe).
