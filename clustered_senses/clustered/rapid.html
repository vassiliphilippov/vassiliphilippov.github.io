1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪd / (AmE), / ˈrapɪd / (BrE)
a section of a river where the water moves very fast and often becomes rough
fast-flowing section of a river

Examples

Fish often struggle to swim upstream through a powerful rapid.
The kayak overturned in the dangerous rapid.
They scouted the rapids before starting their white-water rafting trip.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: waterfall - a steep fall or flow of water in a river

Etymology

From Latin rapidus (seizing quickly, swift).
