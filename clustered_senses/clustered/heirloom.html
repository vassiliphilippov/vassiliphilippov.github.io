1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌlum / (AmE), / ˈeə.luːm / (BrE)
a valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations and is passed down from one generation to the next
object passed down in a family

Examples

She wore her grandmother’s heirloom necklace on her wedding day.
That painting is an heirloom that has been handed down for centuries.
The antique clock has become a cherished heirloom in their family.

Etymology

From Middle English *heirlome*, from heir + lome (tool), originally meaning a hereditary device or possession.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌlum / (AmE), / ˈeə.luːm / (BrE)
A valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations and is passed down from one generation to the next; in law, property inseparable from an inheritance by custom.
item passed down in a family

Examples

The silverware set became an heirloom after my grandmother left it to my mother.
They treat the old painting as an heirloom, preserving it for future generations.
This clock is a family heirloom that has been handed down for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - property or money received from someone after their death
  • Synonym: bequest - something given or left by will

Etymology

From Middle English 'erloum', from Old French 'erleon', from heriter (to inherit).
