1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑl.əˌtud / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
The state of being alone, especially when this is peaceful or desired rather than caused by others.
the state of being alone

Examples

After a hectic week, she craved some solitude in the mountains.
In the quiet of solitude, he found peace and clarity.
Writers often seek solitude to concentrate on their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loneliness - the sadness that comes from being alone
  • Antonym: company - the state of being with other people

Etymology

From Latin 'solitudo' (loneliness, solitude), from 'solus' (alone).

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.lə.tud / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
A place where one is alone or isolated from others; a solitary or secluded spot.
a solitary or secluded place

Examples

He found peace in the solitude of the forest.
She escaped to a solitude far from the city to collect her thoughts.
The old monastery stands in complete solitude on the mountain ridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from other people
  • Antonym: crowd - a large number of people together

Etymology

From Latin solitudo (loneliness, desert), from solus (alone).
