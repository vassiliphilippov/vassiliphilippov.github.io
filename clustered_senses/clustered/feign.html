to feign

Summary

  • to pretend to have a feeling, condition, or reaction, usually to deceive
    • VERB: pretend or give a false appearance of
    • VERB: pretend or fake (with intent to deceive)
to pretend to have a feeling, condition, or reaction, usually to deceive

1. VERB

/ feɪn / (AmE), / feɪn / (BrE)
to pretend to have a particular feeling, condition, or quality; to give a false appearance of something
pretend or give a false appearance of

Examples

During the meeting, I had to feign confidence even though I was nervous.
He tried to feign interest, but it was clear he was bored.
She managed to feign surprise when she heard the news.

Synset Examples

She assumed indifference, even though she was seething with anger
he feigned sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: simulate - to imitate the appearance or character of something
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic

Etymology

From Old French 'feign-, stem of feindre', from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, invent, pretend).

2. VERB

/ feɪn / (AmE), / feɪn / (BrE)
to pretend to feel or be affected by something, usually with the intention to deceive
pretend or fake (with intent to deceive)

Examples

He tried to feign surprise, but everyone saw through his act.
It is unwise to feign interest in something you truly dislike.
She would often feign illness to avoid going to work.

Synset Examples

He feigned that he was ill
He shammed a headache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to make something appear true when it is not
  • Synonym: fake - to simulate or not be genuine
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be

Etymology

From Old French feindre, from Latin fingere (to shape, invent, pretend).
