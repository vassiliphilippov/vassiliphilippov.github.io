aloof

Summary

  • Distant, reserved, or uninvolved in manner or attitude; not friendly or forthcoming.
    • ADV: in a distant or reserved manner

Distant, reserved, or uninvolved in manner or attitude; not friendly or forthcoming.

1. ADV

/ əˈluf / (AmE), / əˈluːf / (BrE)
In a way that is distant, reserved, or uninvolved, especially in attitude or manner.
in a distant or reserved manner

Examples

During the meeting, she remained aloof, avoiding participation in the discussion.
Even when praised for her achievements, she responded aloof, keeping her emotions hidden.
He watched the festivities aloof, preferring not to join in the celebration.

Synset Examples

the local gentry and professional classes had held aloof for the school had accepted their sons readily enough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distantly - in a way that is emotionally or physically far apart
  • Antonym: warmly - in a friendly or enthusiastic way

Etymology

From Middle English 'alof' (to windward), from 'a-' (on) + 'loof' (windward direction).

2. ADJ

/ əˈluːf / (AmE), / əˈluːf / (BrE)
Not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant in manner.
distant or unfriendly in manner

Examples

Despite their efforts to include her, she stayed aloof and hardly spoke.
His aloof attitude made it difficult for coworkers to get to know him.
She remained aloof during the meeting, rarely engaging with anyone else.

Synset Examples

stood apart with aloof dignity
a distant smile
he was upstage with strangers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distant - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and remote
  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; showing friendship

Etymology

From Dutch 'loef' meaning 'the weather side of a ship', suggesting turning away.
