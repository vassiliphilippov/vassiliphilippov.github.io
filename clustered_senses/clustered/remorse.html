1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈmɔːs / (BrE)
A deep and painful feeling of regret or guilt for a wrongdoing or misdeed.
deep regret or guilt

Examples

Despite her apology, his remorse was clearly visible on his face.
He showed genuine remorse for his actions in court.
She was filled with remorse after lying to her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment over something one has done or failed to do
  • Synonym: contrition - the state of feeling remorseful and penitent
  • Synonym: guilt - a feeling of responsibility or remorse for a wrong
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Late Latin remorsum (a biting back), from remordēre, re- (again) + mordēre (to bite).
