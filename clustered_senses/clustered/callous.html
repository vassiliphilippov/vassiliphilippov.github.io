callous

Summary

  • showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others; emotionally unfeeling.
    • ADJ: emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
  • (of skin) having become tough and thick, especially through repeated use or friction; having calluses.
    • ADJ: having tough, thickened skin
  • to make someone insensitive, emotionally unfeeling, or unresponsive, especially to the suffering of others.
    • VERB: make emotionally insensitive
showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others; emotionally unfeeling.

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæləs / (AmE), / ˈkæləːs / (BrE)
showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others; emotionally unfeeling.
emotionally insensitive or unfeeling

Examples

Her callous remark about the accident shocked everyone in the room.
It was callous of him to ignore his friend's distress.
The manager's callous attitude made the employees feel unappreciated.

Synset Examples

a callous indifference to suffering
cold-blooded and indurate to public opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitive - lacking sensitivity to others' feelings
  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

From Latin callosus (thick-skinned, hard).

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əs / (BrE)
(of skin) having become tough and thick, especially through repeated use or friction; having calluses.
having tough, thickened skin

Examples

She barely noticed the pain because her feet were already callous from years of dancing barefoot.
The gardener's callous fingers showed the signs of a lifetime spent with tools.
Years of working outdoors gave him callous hands that felt like leather.

1. VERB

/ ˈkæloʊs / (AmE), / ˈkaləs / (BrE)
to make someone insensitive, emotionally unfeeling, or unresponsive, especially to the suffering of others.
make emotionally insensitive

Examples

Repeated disappointments may callous one's outlook on life over time.
The violent environment tended to callous young recruits before they realized it.
Years of harsh conditions can callous a person against the pain of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harden - to make or become more severe or less sympathetic
  • Antonym: soften - to make or become more gentle or sympathetic

Etymology

From Latin 'callosus' (thick-skinned, hardened).
