hypostatization

Summary

  • The act of regarding or treating an abstract concept as if it were a concrete or real thing.
    • NOUN: treating the abstract as concrete
The act of regarding or treating an abstract concept as if it were a concrete or real thing.

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɑːstətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪˌpɒstətʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of regarding or treating an abstract concept as if it were a concrete or real thing.
treating the abstract as concrete

Examples

In literature, the hypostatization of emotions allows writers to personify abstract feelings.
Some critics accuse historians of hypostatization when they treat nations as if they act with personal intentions.
The hypostatization of concepts such as justice or freedom is common in philosophical discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reification - the process of regarding something abstract as a material thing

Etymology

From Greek 'hypostasis' (substance, foundation) via Late Latin 'hypostatizatio'.
