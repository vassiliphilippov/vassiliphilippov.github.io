retaliation

Summary

  • the act of doing something harmful or unpleasant to someone because they have done something harmful or unpleasant to you
    • NOUN: act of returning harm for harm
the act of doing something harmful or unpleasant to someone because they have done something harmful or unpleasant to you

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌtæliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌtæliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of doing something harmful or unpleasant to someone because they have done something harmful or unpleasant to you
act of returning harm for harm

Examples

Fear of retaliation kept many people silent about the abuse.
He warned that any attack would result in swift retaliation.
The company took retaliation against workers who spoke out about unsafe conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the act of harming someone in return for being harmed
  • Synonym: reprisal - an act of retaliation; especially in war
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the act of stopping feeling anger or resentment towards someone for an offense

Etymology

From Latin retaliare (to pay back in kind).
