precarious

Summary

  • In a dangerous, unstable, or insecure position; likely to fail, fall, or change suddenly; not safe or secure.
    • ADJ: dangerous or unstable
    • ADJ: not secure or stable; giving no reassurance
    • ADJ: not secure; unstable or risky
In a dangerous, unstable, or insecure position; likely to fail, fall, or change suddenly; not safe or secure.

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
in a dangerous, unstable, or insecure position; likely to collapse or fail
dangerous or unstable

Examples

After the storm, the bridge was in a precarious condition and unsafe for traffic.
She balanced the vase on a precarious stack of books.
They found themselves in a precarious financial situation after losing their jobs.

Synset Examples

a parlous journey on stormy seas
a perilous voyage across the Atlantic in a small boat
the precarious life of an undersea diver
dangerous surgery followed by a touch-and-go recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: secure - free from danger or threat

Etymology

From Latin 'precarius' (obtained by entreaty, hence dependent on favor), via French 'précaire'.

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
(of a situation or state) not secure; affording no ease or reassurance; likely to change or collapse suddenly.
not secure or stable; giving no reassurance

Examples

After months of negotiation, the two sides finally agreed to a precarious truce.
Her financial situation became increasingly precarious after losing her job.
The ladder was balanced in a precarious position against the wall.

Synset Examples

a precarious truce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - not steady or secure; likely to change or collapse
  • Antonym: secure - fixed or stable; not likely to give way

Etymology

From Latin precarius (obtained by prayer or entreaty, hence uncertain).

3. ADJ

/ prɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
not securely held or in a dangerous position; likely to fall, collapse, or fail; uncertain or unstable due to risk or difficulty
not secure; unstable or risky

Examples

Her financial situation has been precarious ever since she lost her job.
The climbers found themselves in a precarious position on the narrow ledge.
The poorly constructed bridge looked precarious after the storm.

Synset Examples

a shaky marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - likely to change or fail; not firm or fixed
  • Antonym: secure - fixed or fastened so as not to give way, become loose or be lost

Etymology

From Latin precarius (obtained by entreaty, depending on favor), from prex, precis (prayer).
