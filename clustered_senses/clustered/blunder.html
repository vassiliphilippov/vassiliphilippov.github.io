blunder

Summary

  • to make a careless or serious mistake, especially because of confusion or ignorance
    • VERB: make a serious or careless mistake
  • a careless or embarrassing mistake, often causing embarrassment or problems
    • NOUN: an embarrassing mistake
  • to say something impulsively and often foolishly, without careful consideration
    • VERB: speak impulsively or foolishly
  • to move or progress in a clumsy or confused way, often without clear direction
    • VERB: move clumsily or blindly
to make a careless or serious mistake, especially because of confusion or ignorance

1. VERB

/ ˈblʌndər / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
to make a careless or serious mistake, especially because of confusion or ignorance
make a serious or careless mistake

Examples

He blundered into the wrong meeting room by mistake.
She realized she had blundered only after seeing the disappointed faces around her.
The company blundered when it released a faulty product to the public.

Synset Examples

I blundered during the job interview

Related Terms

  • Synonym: err - make a mistake or error
  • Synonym: misjudge - form a wrong opinion or conclusion

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *blundra* (to shut one's eyes).

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
a careless or embarrassing mistake, often causing embarrassment or problems
an embarrassing mistake

Examples

Calling the wrong name at the ceremony was an unforgettable blunder.
His speech was ruined by a major blunder in the introduction.
The new employee made a costly blunder during her first week at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake, especially one made by failing to notice something
  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional act or remark causing embarrassment
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose, opposite of mistake

Etymology

From Middle English blonder (to stumble), of uncertain origin; perhaps Scandinavian.
