baton

Summary

  • a short, thin stick or staff carried by officials as a symbol of their office or authority
    • NOUN: staff symbolizing official authority
  • a hollow tube that is passed from one runner to another in a relay race
    • NOUN: relay race tube
  • A thin, tapered rod used by a conductor to direct an orchestra or choir.
    • NOUN: conductor's rod for leading music
  • a short, thick stick carried and used as a weapon by police officers
    • NOUN: police officer's club or stick
  • a thin, hollow metal rod that is twirled or carried by a drum major or majorette as part of a marching band performance
    • NOUN: rod twirled by drum major or majorette
a short, thin stick or staff carried by officials as a symbol of their office or authority

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtɑn / (AmE), / bəˈtɒn / (BrE)
a short, thin stick or staff carried by officials as a symbol of their office or authority
staff symbolizing official authority

Examples

During the ceremony, the official raised his baton to signal the commencement.
The parade marshal carried a silver baton to lead the procession.
The police chief's baton was displayed on the desk as a mark of office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scepter - an ornamented staff held by a sovereign as a symbol of authority

Etymology

From French 'bâton' (stick, staff), from Old French, from Late Latin 'bastō' (stick).
