chaperone

Summary

  • to accompany and supervise someone, especially to ensure proper behavior in social situations
    • VERB: to accompany and supervise
  • a person, often an older woman, who accompanies and supervises a young woman or a group of young people to ensure proper behavior, especially in social situations
    • NOUN: person who accompanies and supervises young people
to accompany and supervise someone, especially to ensure proper behavior in social situations

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæpəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃæpərəʊn / (BrE)
to accompany and supervise someone, especially to ensure proper behavior in social situations
to accompany and supervise

Examples

Parents often chaperone school dances to ensure everyone behaves appropriately.
She chaperoned her younger brother at the birthday party.
Two teachers were assigned to chaperone the students on the field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - to accompany someone to provide protection or guidance

Etymology

From French 'chaperon' (protector, hood), originally referring to a woman who accompanies a younger unmarried woman in public.

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæpəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃapərəʊn / (BrE)
a person, often an older woman, who accompanies and supervises a young woman or a group of young people to ensure proper behavior, especially in social situations
person who accompanies and supervises young people

Examples

As a chaperone, she made sure the students behaved during the field trip.
Her mother acted as a chaperone when she attended the party.
The dance committee requires each group to have a chaperone present during the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - a person who accompanies someone for protection or as a companion
  • Synonym: guardian - someone who protects or looks after another, especially a minor

Etymology

From French 'chaperon' (protector, hood), ultimately from Late Latin 'cappa' (cape).
