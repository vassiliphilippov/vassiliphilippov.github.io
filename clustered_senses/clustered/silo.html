1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪləʊ / (BrE)
A tall, cylindrical structure, typically made of concrete or metal, used for storing silage or grain on a farm.
cylindrical tower for storing silage or grain

Examples

After the harvest, the workers inspected the silo to ensure it was properly sealed.
Modern farms often have more than one silo to store different types of feed.
The farmer filled the silo with freshly harvested corn for the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granary - a storehouse for threshed grain

Etymology

From Spanish 'silo' (pit for holding grain), from Latin 'sirus', of Greek origin.

/ ˈsaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləʊ / (BrE)
An isolated collection or repository of information within an organization that is not easily shared or integrated with other parts, hindering communication or data use.
isolated repository of organizational data

Examples

The company's departments worked in silos, so teams rarely shared important customer data.
IT began a project to break down the silo between marketing and sales within six months.
Analysts warned that creating more isolated silos would increase costs and slow decision-making across divisions.
