1. NOUN

/ ˈvælər / (AmE), / ˈvælə / (BrE)
great courage, especially in the face of danger in battle; bravery regarded as a heroic quality
heroic courage in battle

Examples

She demonstrated great valor when she rescued the children from the burning building.
Stories of valor have inspired generations to act courageously.
The soldier was commended for his valor during the fierce fighting.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage to face danger

Etymology

From Latin 'valorem', accusative of 'valor' (value, worth), from 'valere' (to be strong).
