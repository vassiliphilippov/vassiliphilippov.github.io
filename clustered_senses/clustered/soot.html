soot

Summary

  • a black powdery or flaky substance consisting mainly of amorphous carbon, produced by the incomplete burning of organic matter, often collecting on surfaces as a residue
    • NOUN: black residue from burning
  • to cover or stain with soot.
    • VERB: cover or stain with soot

1. NOUN

/ sʊt / (AmE), / sʊt / (BrE)
a black powdery or flaky substance consisting mainly of amorphous carbon, produced by the incomplete burning of organic matter, often collecting on surfaces as a residue
black residue from burning

Examples

Excessive soot in the chimney can be a fire hazard.
The artist used ground soot as pigment for his ink drawings.
The walls above the fireplace were coated with a thick layer of soot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - the powdery residue left after the burning of a substance

Etymology

From Old English 'sōt', related to Dutch 'zoot' and German 'Ruß'.
