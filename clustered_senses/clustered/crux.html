crux

Summary

  • the most important or decisive point of an issue, problem, or argument
    • NOUN: the main or crucial point
the most important or decisive point of an issue, problem, or argument

1. NOUN

/ krʌks / (AmE), / krʌks / (BrE)
the most important or decisive point of an issue, problem, or argument
the main or crucial point

Examples

Finding the crux of the issue can help resolve even the most complex problems.
Once she identified the crux of the argument, the solution became clear to everyone.
The crux of the matter is whether we have enough money to finish the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the most important quality of something; its basic nature
  • Synonym: gist - the main point or essence of a matter
  • Antonym: triviality - a thing of little importance or significance

Etymology

From Latin crux (cross, trouble, perplexity), originally referring to a cross, then a difficult point.
