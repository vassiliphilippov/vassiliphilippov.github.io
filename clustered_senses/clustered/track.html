A path, course, or mark made by people, animals, or vehicles; includes roads, trails, railway lines, racecourses, and visible traces left on a surface.

1. NOUN

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
a rough path or road, especially one made by repeated use or for a particular purpose
rough path or road

Examples

A narrow dirt track led from the village to the top of the hill.
Recent rains had made the track almost impassable by vehicles.
The hikers followed the track through the dense forest until they reached the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trail - a path, track, or unpaved lane or road
  • Degree Higher: road - a wide way leading from one place to another
  • Synonym: path - a way or track laid down for walking or made by continual treading

Etymology

From Middle French trac, from Old French trac 'mark, trace', from tracer 'to trace'.
