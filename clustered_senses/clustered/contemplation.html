contemplation

Summary

  • The act of thinking deeply or looking thoughtfully at something for a long time.
    • NOUN: thoughtful observation or reflection
    • NOUN: calm, lengthy, serious consideration
The act of thinking deeply or looking thoughtfully at something for a long time.

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.t̬əmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of looking thoughtfully at something for a long time, often in deep reflection or concentration
thoughtful observation or reflection

Examples

After a period of contemplation, she decided to change her career path.
He sat in contemplation before the painting, absorbed in its detail.
The quiet park was the perfect place for his evening contemplation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration

Etymology

From Latin contemplatio (act of looking at, contemplation), from contemplari (to observe, consider).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of thinking about something in a calm and serious way for a long time
calm, lengthy, serious consideration

Examples

After hours of contemplation, she finally made her decision.
He spends each morning in quiet contemplation before starting his day.
The mountain view inspired a moment of deep contemplation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Synonym: meditation - deep thinking or focus, especially for relaxation or spiritual purposes
  • Antonym: distraction - a thing that prevents someone from concentrating

Etymology

From Latin contemplatio (act of looking at, contemplation), from contemplari (to gaze attentively).
