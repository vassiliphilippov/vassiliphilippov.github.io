cognate

Summary

  • a person related to another by blood, especially sharing a common ancestor
    • NOUN: person related by blood or ancestry
  • (of people) related by blood; descended from the same ancestor.
    • ADJ: related by blood
  • (of qualities, ideas, or things) related in origin, nature, or character; having a common ancestor or source.
    • ADJ: related in origin or character
  • (of words, languages, etc.) having the same origin or a common ancestral language.
    • ADJ: having the same linguistic origin
  • a word that has the same linguistic derivation as another; a word descended from the same ancestral language as another word
    • NOUN: word sharing ancestry with another

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːɡ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
a person related to another by blood, especially sharing a common ancestor
person related by blood or ancestry

Examples

Anthropologists traced the siblings' status as cognates through their maternal lineage.
As a cognate, she could claim inheritance rights under local law.
Most members of the royal family are distant cognates rather than direct descendants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnate - a person related on the father's side

Etymology

From Latin 'cognatus' (related by birth), from 'co-' (together) + 'gnatus' (born).
