to mingle

Summary

  • To mix or blend things, elements, or groups together so that they become combined or less distinct.
    • VERB: become mixed or combined
    • VERB: to mix or combine together
  • to become involved with or take part in an activity, group, or situation, especially in a way that mixes one's presence or participation with others
    • VERB: become involved with others or with something
To mix or blend things, elements, or groups together so that they become combined or less distinct.

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪŋɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
to be or become mixed together, especially so that the individual parts are difficult to distinguish
become mixed or combined

Examples

As the evening progressed, the sounds of laughter and music mingled in the air.
Her thoughts mingled with memories from her childhood.
The paint colors mingled on the artist's palette, creating a unique shade.

Synset Examples

His words jumbled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend different things together
  • Synonym: jumble - to mix together in a confused or untidy way

Etymology

From Middle English menglen, frequentative of Old English mengan (to mix), related to Old Norse menga.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to mix or combine things together, or to be mixed or combined; to blend different elements or groups.
to mix or combine together

Examples

Guests began to mingle at the party, creating a lively atmosphere.
Her laughter mingled with the music coming from the next room.
The chef likes to mingle different spices to create unique flavors.

Synset Examples

resourcefully he mingled music and dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix two or more things completely together
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not mix

Etymology

From Middle English 'menglen', frequentative of 'mengen' (to mix), from Old English 'mengan'.
