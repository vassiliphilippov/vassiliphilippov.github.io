blush

Summary

  • A pink or red powder or cream applied to the cheeks to give a healthy or rosy appearance.
    • NOUN: cosmetic for coloring cheeks
  • A reddening or rosy color of the cheeks, especially as a sign of emotion (such as embarrassment or shame) or good health.
    • NOUN: reddening of the face from emotion
    • NOUN: a healthy rosy color in the cheeks
  • To become red or rosy in the face, especially from emotion (such as embarrassment or shame) or from physical causes (such as cold).
    • VERB: become rosy or reddish in color
    • VERB: turn red from embarrassment or shame

1. NOUN

/ blʌʃ / (AmE), / blʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden reddening of the face caused by embarrassment, guilt, shame, or modesty
reddening of the face from emotion

Examples

A deep blush spread across his cheeks as he realized his mistake.
Her face was covered with a blush when he complimented her in front of everyone.
She tried to hide her blush by turning away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flush - a sudden reddening of the skin, often from emotion or exertion

Etymology

From Old French 'bluschir', from a Germanic origin meaning 'to shine' or 'become red'.

2. NOUN

/ blʌʃ / (AmE), / blʌʃ / (BrE)
a rosy or pinkish color, especially in the cheeks, regarded as a sign of good health or vitality
a healthy rosy color in the cheeks

Examples

A warm blush rose to her cheeks as she walked in the crisp morning air.
His skin had the natural blush of youth and good health.
The child’s face was lit up with a healthy blush after playing outside all day.

Etymology

From Middle English 'blushen', from Old English 'blyscan' (to blush).
