relentless

Summary

  • Never stopping or becoming less intense; persistent, severe, or determined.
    • ADJ: never stopping; persistent
    • ADJ: never-ending and intense; impossible to appease
Never stopping or becoming less intense; persistent, severe, or determined.

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈlɛntləs / (AmE), / rɪˈlɛn(t)ləs / (BrE)
never stopping or becoming less severe, intense, or strong; persistent and determined.
never stopping; persistent

Examples

Despite relentless criticism, she continued pursuing her goals.
He admired her relentless determination to succeed.
The relentless sun beat down on the desert without mercy.

Synset Examples

the relentless beat of the drums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing to exist or endure over a prolonged period
  • Synonym: ceaseless - without stopping or pausing; unending
  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy or pity
  • Antonym: relenting - becoming less severe, harsh, or strict

Etymology

From re- (intensive prefix) + obsolete 'relent' (to melt, soften) + -less.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈlɛntləs / (AmE), / rɪˈlɛntləs / (BrE)
Continuing in a severe or extreme way without stopping or becoming less intense; impossible to be appeased or stopped.
never-ending and intense; impossible to appease

Examples

Despite his injury, he showed relentless determination to finish the race.
Her relentless pursuit of justice inspired the entire community.
The storm was relentless, battering the coast for hours without a break.

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexorable - impossible to stop or prevent
  • Synonym: unyielding - not giving way under pressure; firm or inflexible
  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy or pity

Etymology

From re- + obsolete 'lent' (soft, gentle), from Latin 'lentus' (slow, flexible).
