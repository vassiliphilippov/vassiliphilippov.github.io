to startle

Summary

  • to move or jump suddenly as a result of surprise or alarm
    • VERB: move suddenly in surprise or alarm
  • to cause someone to react suddenly, often by surprise or fear, prompting them into action or alertness
    • VERB: cause someone to react suddenly by surprise
to move or jump suddenly as a result of surprise or alarm

1. VERB

/ ˈstɑrtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtl̩ / (BrE)
to move or jump suddenly as a result of surprise or alarm
move suddenly in surprise or alarm

Examples

He tends to startle easily when someone enters the room unexpectedly.
I didn't mean to startle you with my sudden laughter.
The loud noise made her startle and drop her book.

Synset Examples

She startled when I walked into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - make a quick, nervous movement in reaction to surprise or fear
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Middle English stertlen (to jump up), frequentative of sterten (to jump).

1. VERB

/ ˈstɑːrtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtl̩ / (BrE)
to cause someone to react suddenly, often by surprise or fear, prompting them into action or alertness
cause someone to react suddenly by surprise

Examples

A sudden clap of thunder startled everyone at the picnic.
He was startled into action by the unexpected announcement.
The alarm clock startled her into jumping out of bed immediately.

Synset Examples

the loud noise startled him awake
galvanized into action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surprise - to cause (someone) to feel mild astonishment or shock
  • Synonym: galvanize - to shock or excite (someone) into taking action

Etymology

From Middle English stertlen, frequentative of sterten (to leap up).
