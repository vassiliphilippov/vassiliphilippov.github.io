rack

Summary

  • a rib section of the forequarter of veal, pork, lamb, or mutton, often including several ribs and prepared as a cut of meat
    • NOUN: rib section of veal, pork, lamb, or mutton
  • A device used as an instrument of torture to stretch or dislocate victims' bodies, especially by pulling on limbs.
    • NOUN: torture device for stretching victims
    • NOUN: device for torture by stretching
  • to torture someone by stretching them on the rack, a device formerly used for inflicting pain and extracting confessions.
    • VERB: torture on the rack
  • To cause someone severe emotional or mental anguish; to torment, distress, or strain greatly, either literally or figuratively.
    • VERB: torment emotionally or mentally
    • VERB: to stretch or strain to the limit
  • a frame or stand used for holding, displaying, or storing objects
    • NOUN: frame or stand for holding things
  • to move or adjust (a camera, lens, or device) using a rack and pinion mechanism
    • VERB: adjust with a rack and pinion
  • to obtain something, especially money, through force, threats, or intimidation
    • VERB: obtain by force or intimidation
  • Destruction or collapse, especially as used in the phrase 'rack and ruin.'
    • NOUN: destruction; collapse
  • to draw off (wine or other liquid) from the lees or sediment, usually into another container
    • VERB: draw off liquid from sediment
  • to place or arrange items, especially balls in a game such as pool, in or on a rack
    • VERB: arrange or place items in a rack
  • a rapid gait of a horse in which each foot strikes the ground separately
    • NOUN: a rapid horse gait
  • (of a horse) to move at a fast, smooth, four-beat gait called a 'rack'.
    • VERB: move at a fast, smooth gait (horse)
  • (of a ship) to run before a gale; to move swiftly with the wind from behind, especially under storm conditions.
    • VERB: to run before a gale (nautical)
