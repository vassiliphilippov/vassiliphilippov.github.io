1. NOUN

/ næɡ / (AmE), / næɡ / (BrE)
an old horse or one that is worn out from hard work
an old, worn-out horse

Examples

Despite its age, the nag still managed to pull the cart up the hill.
She rode an old nag across the countryside, enjoying the slow pace.
The farmer led the tired nag back to the stable after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack - a horse intended for ordinary riding, often old or worn out

Etymology

Possibly from Middle Low German *nagghe* (small horse); related to Old Norse *naggi* (pony).

1. NOUN

/ næɡ / (AmE), / næɡ / (BrE)
a person who habitually finds fault or complains, especially in a persistent and annoying way
person who constantly complains or criticizes

Examples

Don't be a nag—I've already promised I'll finish my homework.
He tried to ignore his roommate, who was becoming more of a nag each day about the dishes.
Her mother was such a nag that Mark dreaded family dinners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who frequently complains
  • Antonym: encourager - a person who gives support and praise

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian nagga (to gnaw, find fault).
