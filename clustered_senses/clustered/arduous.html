arduous

Summary

  • Very difficult and requiring a lot of effort, often over a long period or to the point of exhaustion.
    • ADJ: difficult and requiring much effort
    • ADJ: difficult and requiring great effort
    • ADJ: requiring great effort; hard to do
Very difficult and requiring a lot of effort, often over a long period or to the point of exhaustion.

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrdʒuəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːdjuəs / (BrE)
very difficult and requiring a lot of effort, especially over a long period of time.
difficult and requiring much effort

Examples

Climbing the mountain proved an arduous challenge for the team.
She finally completed the arduous journey across the desert.
Writing the entire report was an arduous process that took several weeks.

Synset Examples

the arduous work of preparing a dictionary

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or needing little effort

Etymology

From Latin arduus (steep, difficult).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑrdʒuəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːdjuːəs / (BrE)
involving or requiring a lot of effort; very difficult and tiring
difficult and requiring great effort

Examples

After several arduous months of training, she finally completed the marathon.
Learning a new language can be an arduous process that requires patience and determination.
The team faced an arduous journey through the mountains in freezing conditions.

Synset Examples

his final, straining burst of speed
a strenuous task
your willingness after these six arduous days to remain here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strenuous - requiring great exertion or effort
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or taxing

Etymology

From Latin arduus (steep, difficult).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑrdʒuəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːdjuːəs / (BrE)
involving or requiring a lot of effort and hard work, often to the point of exhaustion
requiring great effort; hard to do

Examples

Climbing the mountain was an arduous journey that tested everyone's endurance.
She found it arduous to balance her job with evening classes, but she never gave up.
The project required arduous hours of research and dedication from the entire team.

Synset Examples

heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grueling - extremely tiring and demanding
  • Synonym: laborious - requiring considerable effort and time
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or requiring much effort

Etymology

From Latin arduus (steep, difficult).
