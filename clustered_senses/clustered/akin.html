related by blood

Examples

Many people in the village are akin to one another.
The two families were akin, having descended from the same ancestors.
Though distant cousins, they are still technically akin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: related - connected by blood or family

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *ācynn* (of the same kin), from 'a-' (on, of) + 'kin' (family, race).

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɪn / (AmE), / əˈkɪn / (BrE)
having a similar quality or character; related in nature, feeling, or appearance
similar in quality or character

Examples

A sudden sense of joy, akin to relief, washed over her.
His remarks were akin to an apology, though he never actually said sorry.
The siblings' interests are akin, especially their love for classical music.

Synset Examples

a feeling akin to terror
kindred souls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similar - having a resemblance in appearance, character, or quantity
  • Antonym: different - not the same as another; unlike

Etymology

From Middle English, variant of 'akin to,' from 'of kin' meaning related by blood.
