innate

Summary

  • Describes a quality, ability, or characteristic that exists in a person or animal from birth, not learned or acquired; inborn or natural.
    • ADJ: inborn; not learned
    • ADJ: present at birth; not acquired
    • ADJ: inborn; existing naturally, not learned
Describes a quality, ability, or characteristic that exists in a person or animal from birth, not learned or acquired; inborn or natural.

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪt / (BrE)
(of a quality, ability, or characteristic) existing in a person or animal from birth rather than acquired or learned.
inborn; not learned

Examples

Her innate sense of justice guided her choices throughout life.
Humans are thought to have an innate fear of snakes and spiders.
Some children have an innate musical ability that becomes evident at an early age.

Synset Examples

an unconditioned reflex

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquired - developed or learned, not present from birth
  • Synonym: inborn - existing from birth; innate

Etymology

From Latin 'innatus' (inborn), past participle of 'innasci' (to be born in).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪt / (BrE)
(of a quality or ability) present at birth; not acquired or learned later.
present at birth; not acquired

Examples

Certain reflexes are innate, not learned.
He has an innate ability to connect with animals.
Her innate sense of rhythm was evident even as a child.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquired - developed after birth rather than being present from birth
  • Synonym: inborn - existing from birth

Etymology

From Latin 'innatus' (inborn), from 'in-' (in) + 'nasci' (to be born).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪt / (BrE)
(of a quality or ability) existing naturally rather than being learned or acquired; inborn.
inborn; existing naturally, not learned

Examples

Confidence seemed almost innate in her, as if she was born with it.
His innate curiosity led him to explore science from a young age.
She has an innate sense of rhythm that makes her a gifted dancer.

Synset Examples

a natural leader
a born musician
an innate talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inborn - existing from birth, not acquired
  • Antonym: acquired - learned or developed, not innate

Etymology

From Latin 'innatus', past participle of 'innasci' (to be born in).
