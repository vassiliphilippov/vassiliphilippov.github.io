To prevent or hinder someone or something from making progress or moving freely.

1. VERB

/ ˈhæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæmpə / (BrE)
to prevent someone from easily achieving something or from moving freely; to hinder or obstruct
hinder or obstruct

Examples

Heavy traffic can hamper emergency vehicles during peak hours.
Her recovery was hampered by complications after surgery.
Strict regulations may hamper the growth of small businesses.

Synset Examples

The brace I have to wear is hindering my movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone to do something
  • Synonym: obstruct - to block or get in the way of progress
  • Antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *hamperen* (to impede), of obscure origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈhæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæmpə / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from making progress or moving freely
prevent progress or free movement

Examples

Heavy traffic can hamper the arrival of emergency vehicles during rush hour.
Her recovery was hampered by an infection after the operation.
Strict regulations may hamper innovation in the tech industry.

Synset Examples

He was hampered in his efforts by the bad weather
the imperialist nation wanted to strangle the free trade between the two small countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen or someone to do something
  • Synonym: impede - to delay or stop the progress of something
  • Antonym: facilitate - to make a process or action easier

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *hamperen* (to restrain), perhaps altered from *hamper* (basket), itself of uncertain origin.

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmpər / (AmE), / ˈhæmpə / (BrE)
a large basket, typically with a lid, used for carrying or storing items such as laundry or picnic supplies
basket with a cover, for storage or carrying

Examples

She placed the dirty clothes in the hamper by the bathroom door.
The laundry hamper was overflowing after a busy week.
We packed sandwiches and fruit in a hamper for our picnic in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket - a container used to hold or carry things, typically made from interwoven materials

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'hamper', meaning 'case for goblets', from Old French 'hanapier' (case for cups).
