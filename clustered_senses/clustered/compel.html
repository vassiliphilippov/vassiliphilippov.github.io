to compel

Summary

  • to force someone to do something, or to make something necessary because of circumstances
    • VERB: force someone to do something
    • VERB: to force or make necessary
to force someone to do something, or to make something necessary because of circumstances

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpɛl / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛl / (BrE)
to force someone to do something; to require or oblige someone to act in a certain way
force someone to do something

Examples

Circumstances beyond his control compelled him to find a new job.
She felt compelled to apologize after realizing her mistake.
The law can compel companies to provide accurate information to consumers.

Synset Examples

We compel all students to fill out this form

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coerce - to force someone to do something by use of threats or pressure
  • Antonym: persuade - to cause someone to do something by reasoning or argument
  • Synonym: force - to make someone do something against their will

Etymology

From Latin compellere (to drive together, force), from com- (together) + pellere (to drive).

2. VERB

/ kəmˈpɛl / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛl / (BrE)
to force or make something necessary by the strength, seriousness, or effect of circumstances
to force or make necessary

Examples

His illness compelled him to take a leave of absence from work.
Severe financial pressures compel many families to reduce their expenses.
The evidence compelled a thorough investigation by the police.

Synset Examples

the water shortage compels conservation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: require - to need or demand as necessary

Etymology

From Latin compellere, from com- (together) + pellere (to drive).
