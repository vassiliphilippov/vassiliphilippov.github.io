An opinion or conclusion formed without enough evidence; a guess based on incomplete information.

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛktʃər / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃə / (BrE)
an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information or insufficient evidence
speculation based on incomplete evidence

Examples

Her theory was dismissed as mere conjecture without supporting data.
Much of the initial report is conjecture, awaiting confirmation from further investigation.
The historian's argument relies heavily on conjecture rather than solid facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is known for sure

Etymology

From Latin conjectura (interpretation, guess), from conicere (to throw together).

1. VERB

/ kənˈdʒɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃə / (BrE)
to form an opinion or conclusion on the basis of incomplete information; to guess without sufficient evidence
form an opinion without full evidence

Examples

Historians often conjecture about the causes of ancient wars due to limited evidence.
It is difficult to conjecture what might have happened without eyewitness reports.
The committee could only conjecture as to why the project was abandoned.

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - to estimate or suppose something without sufficient information
  • Synonym: speculate - to form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Antonym: know - to have knowledge or be certain about something

Etymology

From Latin conjecturare (to infer, guess), from conjectura (conclusion, inference).
