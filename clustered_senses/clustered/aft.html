At or toward the rear (stern) of a ship or the tail of an aircraft.

1. ADJ

/ æft / (AmE), / ɑːft / (BrE)
(nautical, aeronautical) Situated at or toward the stern of a ship or the tail of an aircraft.
toward the rear (in ships or aircraft)

Examples

Passengers seated in the aft section of the plane may feel more turbulence.
The crew quarters are located aft near the engine room.
The ship's galley is just aft of the main mast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forward - situated at or toward the bow or nose (of a ship or aircraft)

Etymology

From Middle English *aft*, from Old English *æftan* (behind, at the back).
