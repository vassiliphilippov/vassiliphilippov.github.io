To surprise or astonish someone greatly; to shock or amaze.

1. VERB

/ ˈflæbɚˌɡæst / (AmE), / ˈflabəɡɑːst / (BrE)
To surprise or astonish someone greatly; to shock or amaze.
to astonish or greatly surprise

Examples

His incredible performance never fails to flabbergast the audience.
I was flabbergasted when I saw the size of the bill.
The news of her sudden resignation truly flabbergasted everyone in the office.

Synset Examples

This boggles the mind!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: amaze - to fill with great surprise or wonder

Etymology

Origin uncertain; first attested late 18th century, possibly a fanciful formation.
