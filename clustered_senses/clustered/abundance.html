abundance

Summary

  • A very large quantity of something; more than is needed.
    • NOUN: a very large quantity or supply
  • (Chemistry) The ratio of the total mass of an element in the earth's crust to the total mass of the earth's crust, typically expressed as a percentage or in parts per million.
    • NOUN: ratio of an element in earth's crust (chemistry)
  • (Physics) The ratio of the number of atoms of a particular isotope of an element to the total number of atoms of all its isotopes.
    • NOUN: ratio of atoms of an isotope to total isotopes

1. NOUN

/ əˈbʌn.dəns / (AmE), / əˈbʌn.dəns / (BrE)
A very large quantity of something; more than is needed.
a very large quantity or supply

Examples

Despite the drought, this year's harvest produced an abundance of fresh vegetables.
During the holidays, there is an abundance of food and good cheer.
She was overwhelmed by the abundance of choices at the market.

Synset Examples

an age of abundance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plenty - a large or sufficient amount or quantity; more than enough
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply; shortage

Etymology

From Latin abundantia (fullness, copiousness), from abundare (to overflow).
