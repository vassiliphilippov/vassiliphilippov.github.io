gnarly

Summary

  • (of a surface, especially of old trees or people) having a rough, knotted, or twisted appearance, often with knobs or protuberances.
    • ADJ: having a rough, knotted appearance
  • informal slang: (of an event, action, or thing) extremely impressive or exciting; more generally, intensely extreme (can be positive—excellent—or negative—dangerous or severe—depending on context).
    • ADJ: slang: extremely impressive or extreme

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑrli / (AmE), / ˈnɑːli / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially of old trees or people) having a rough, knotted, or twisted appearance, often with knobs or protuberances.
having a rough, knotted appearance

Examples

Her gnarly hands revealed years of hard work in the fields.
The old oak's gnarly branches twisted high above the ground.
We carefully avoided the tree's gnarly roots that jutted out of the path.

Synset Examples

a knobbed stick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnarled - rough and knotted, especially with age
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From the word 'gnarl', possibly imitative in origin, referring to a protuberant knot on a tree.
