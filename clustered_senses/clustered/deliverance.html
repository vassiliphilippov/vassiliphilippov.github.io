1. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvɚrəns / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvərəns / (BrE)
the action of being rescued or set free from danger, harm, or distress
rescue or liberation from danger or harm

Examples

After hours stranded at sea, the sailors felt immense relief at their deliverance.
The political leader promised the deliverance of the people from oppression.
Their prayers were answered with the deliverance of the hostages from captivity.

Synset Examples

work is the deliverance of mankind
a surgeon's job is the saving of lives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - the act of saving someone from danger or harm
  • Synonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free
  • Antonym: captivity - the state of being imprisoned or confined

Etymology

From Old French deliverance, from Latin deliberare (to set free).
