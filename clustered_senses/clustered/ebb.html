ebb

Summary

  • the movement of the tide away from the land, out to sea
    • NOUN: the outgoing flow of the tide
  • (of tide, water, or other things) to flow back or recede; to move away from the land or diminish in intensity or amount.
    • VERB: flow back or recede (as a tide)
  • a gradual decline or lessening, especially of strength, power, or quantity
    • NOUN: gradual decline or lessening
  • (of a quality, feeling, or state) to gradually decrease or decline; to recede or diminish.
    • VERB: to recede, decline, or diminish
the movement of the tide away from the land, out to sea

1. NOUN

/ ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
the movement of the tide away from the land, out to sea
the outgoing flow of the tide

Examples

At ebb, many rocks and shells are exposed on the beach.
The fishermen waited until the ebb before setting out to sea.
The strong ebb made it difficult for the small boat to return.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flood - the incoming flow of the tide

Etymology

From Old English 'ebba', related to Old High German 'ebba' (ebb).
