to appease

Summary

  • to calm or pacify someone by giving in to their demands or by doing something to please them, often to maintain peace or avoid conflict
    • VERB: to pacify or make peace by yielding
    • VERB: to calm or placate someone
  • to make a feeling or desire less strong, often by giving what is wanted; to calm or satisfy
    • VERB: to calm or satisfy a feeling or desire

1. VERB

/ əˈpiz / (AmE), / əˈpiːz / (BrE)
to calm or pacify someone by giving in to their demands or by making concessions, often to maintain peace or avoid conflict
to pacify or make peace by yielding

Examples

History shows that attempts to appease aggressive leaders often fail.
She bought him a gift to appease his anger after their argument.
The government tried to appease the protesters by promising reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacify - to calm or bring peace to a person or situation
  • Antonym: aggravate - to make a situation worse or more tense

Etymology

From Old French apaisier, based on pais (peace), from Latin pax (peace).

2. VERB

/ əˈpiz / (AmE), / əˈpiːz / (BrE)
to make someone less angry or hostile, often by giving in to their demands or by doing something to please them
to calm or placate someone

Examples

Governments sometimes appease critics with minor policy changes.
She bought her son a toy to appease his temper tantrum.
The company tried to appease the protesters by agreeing to some of their demands.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollify - to calm or soothe someone's anger or anxiety
  • Antonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: enrage - to make someone extremely angry

Etymology

From Old French 'apaisier', from Vulgar Latin *adpaciare, based on Latin 'pax' (peace).
