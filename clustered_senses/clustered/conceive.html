1. VERB

/ kənˈsiv / (AmE), / kənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to form or develop an idea, plan, or intention in the mind
form or create an idea in the mind

Examples

A solution to the problem was finally conceived after hours of discussion.
She conceived the idea while traveling abroad last year.
The architect conceived an innovative design for the new museum.

Synset Examples

He conceived of a robot that would help paralyzed patients
This library was well conceived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent (a complex procedure, system, or mechanism)
  • Synonym: imagine - to form a mental image or concept of
  • Antonym: forget - to fail to remember, to not bring to mind

Etymology

From Latin 'concipere' (to take in, conceive), from 'com-' (together) + 'capere' (to take).
