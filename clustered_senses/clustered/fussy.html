Examples

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌsi / (AmE), / ˈfʌsi / (BrE)
Easily annoyed or irritable; difficult to please or quick to become upset.
easily annoyed or irritable

Examples

She gets fussy if her coffee isn't made just right in the morning.
Some people are more fussy than others about travel delays.
The baby became fussy whenever it was time for a nap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or made angry
  • Antonym: placid - not easily upset or excited; calm

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌsi / (AmE), / ˈfʌsi / (BrE)
Excessively concerned with unimportant details or requiring close attention; hard to please, especially about minor matters.
exact about small details; hard to please

Examples

He is fussy when it comes to choosing his clothes for important meetings.
Our toddler has become very fussy about what she eats lately.
The guests at the restaurant were so fussy that the chef had to remake several dishes.

Synset Examples

a finicky eater
fussy about clothes
very particular about how her food was prepared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particular - fussy or exacting about details or preferences
  • Synonym: fastidious - very attentive to and concerned about accuracy and detail
  • Antonym: easygoing - relaxed and tolerant; not difficult to please

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly from fuss + -y, late 19th century.
