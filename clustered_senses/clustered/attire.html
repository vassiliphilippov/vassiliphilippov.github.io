1. NOUN

/ əˈtaɪər / (AmE), / əˈtaɪə / (BrE)
clothes, especially fine or formal ones, or clothing worn for a particular purpose or occasion.
clothing for a particular purpose or occasion

Examples

Proper attire is required for entry into the exclusive restaurant.
She wore elegant attire to the gala, catching everyone's attention.
The soldiers changed into their battle attire before heading to the field.

Synset Examples

formal attire
battle dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothing - clothes collectively; garments
  • Synonym: outfit - a set of clothes worn together, especially for a specific occasion
  • Synonym: dress - clothing, especially of a specific kind or for a specific occasion

Etymology

From Old French *atirier* (to arrange, prepare).

1. VERB

/ əˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / əˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to dress in special or formal clothes, especially to look attractive or impressive
dress in special clothes

Examples

He attired the children in matching outfits for the holiday photo.
She attired herself in a beautiful gown for the ceremony.
They attired themselves in traditional costume for the annual festival.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put on clothes, especially formal or special clothes
  • Antonym: undress - to remove clothes from oneself

Etymology

From Old French *atirer* (arrange, prepare), from Vulgar Latin *attirare*, from *ad-* (to) + *tirare* (pull).
