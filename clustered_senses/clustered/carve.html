to carve

Summary

  • To shape, form, or mark something by cutting material, often using tools; includes making objects or engraving patterns or letters.
    • VERB: form by cutting into shape
    • VERB: engrave or cut by chipping away at a surface
  • to cut (meat, a piece of meat, etc.) into slices or pieces, especially before serving
    • VERB: cut food, especially meat, into pieces
  • (in computing, esp. digital forensics) to recover or extract files or data fragments from raw digital storage by identifying file signatures and reconstructing the data.
    • VERB: recover files from raw storage
To shape, form, or mark something by cutting material, often using tools; includes making objects or engraving patterns or letters.

1. VERB

/ kɑrv / (AmE), / kɑːv / (BrE)
to form (something) by cutting material into a particular shape, often using tools
form by cutting into shape

Examples

Each student tried to carve a pumpkin with a different face for the festival.
He spent hours trying to carve a statue that resembled an ancient warrior.
The artist will carve a delicate bird from a single block of wood.

Synset Examples

Carve a flower from the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpt - to shape or form something by carving or molding material

Etymology

From Old English 'ceorfan' (to cut, carve); related to Old High German 'kerban'.

(in computing, esp. digital forensics) to recover or extract files or data fragments from raw digital storage by identifying file signatures and reconstructing the data.
recover files from raw storage

Examples

The forensics team managed to carve deleted photos from the suspect's damaged hard drive.

Their incident response playbook recommends carving files by signature when file systems are unreadable.
