to concede

Summary

  • To admit, often reluctantly, that something is true or that you might be wrong.
    • VERB: to admit wrongdoing or truth
    • VERB: admit or yield, often reluctantly
  • to admit that you have lost in a contest, election, or argument; to acknowledge defeat
    • VERB: admit defeat; acknowledge loss
  • to surrender or give up something, especially unwillingly, to another person or group
    • VERB: surrender or yield to another
To admit, often reluctantly, that something is true or that you might be wrong.

1. VERB

/ kənˈsid / (AmE), / kənˈsiːd / (BrE)
to admit, often reluctantly, that something is true or that one has done something wrong
to admit wrongdoing or truth

Examples

After hours of questioning, she conceded that she had lied to her boss.
Despite his best efforts to avoid the topic, he finally conceded that he had made a mistake.
The politician was forced to concede his involvement in the scandal.

Synset Examples

She confessed that she had taken the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confess - to admit to a wrongdoing or a fault
  • Antonym: deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin concedere, from con- (altogether) + cedere (to go, yield).

1. VERB

/ kənˈsid / (AmE), / kənˈsiːd / (BrE)
to admit that you have lost in a contest, election, or argument; to acknowledge defeat
admit defeat; acknowledge loss

Examples

After realizing he could not catch up, the mayoral candidate finally conceded the election.
The losing side refused to concede until every last vote was counted.
When it became clear they had lost, the team graciously conceded to their opponents.

Synset Examples

The candidate conceded after enough votes had come in to show that he would lose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give up or surrender, especially in a contest or confrontation
  • Antonym: win - to be victorious in a contest or competition

Etymology

From Latin 'concedere' meaning 'to yield, give up, grant'.

1. VERB

/ kənˈsid / (AmE), / kənˈsiːd / (BrE)
to surrender or give up something, especially unwillingly, to another person or group
surrender or yield to another

Examples

After a long discussion, she finally conceded the remote control to her brother.
The general was forced to concede the city to enemy forces after the siege.
They refused to concede their territory despite mounting pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give way or surrender under pressure
  • Synonym: relinquish - to give up or abandon, especially unwillingly
  • Antonym: retain - to keep possession of something; not give up

Etymology

From Latin concedere (to yield, grant), from con- (completely) + cedere (to go, yield).
