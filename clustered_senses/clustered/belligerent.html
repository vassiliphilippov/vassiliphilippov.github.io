/ bəˈlɪdʒərənt / (AmE), / bɛˈlɪdʒərənt / (BrE)

hostile or aggressive

Examples

His belligerent attitude made negotiations difficult.
She responded in a belligerent tone to the criticism.
The customer became belligerent when his order was delayed.

Synset Examples

aggressive acts against another country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; characterized by aggression
  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving conflict or hostility; calm

Etymology

From Latin 'belligerans', present participle of 'belligerare' (to wage war), from 'bellum' (war) + 'gerere' (to wage).

1. ADJ

/ bəˈlɪdʒərənt / (AmE), / bɛˈlɪdʒərənt / (BrE)
(of a country or group) engaged in war or conflict, especially as recognized by international law.
engaged in war or conflict

Examples

During the conflict, both belligerent powers sought support from neutral countries.
Several belligerent nations signed the peace treaty to end hostilities.
The status of a belligerent state grants certain rights and duties under international law.

Synset Examples

belligerent (or warring) nations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neutral - not taking part in a war or conflict

Etymology

From Latin belligerans, present participle of belligerare ‘to wage war’, from bellum ‘war’ + gerere ‘to wage’.

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪdʒərənt / (AmE), / bəˈlɪdʒərənt / (BrE)
a person, group, or nation engaged in war or conflict
person or group involved in conflict

Examples

Both belligerents agreed to a ceasefire after months of negotiation.
International law protects civilians but imposes strict rules on belligerents during armed conflicts.
The United Nations acted as a mediator between the belligerents in the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combatant - person engaged in fighting or warfare
  • Antonym: noncombatant - person not engaged in fighting or warfare

Etymology

From Latin *belligerans* (waging war), from *bellum* (war) + *gerere* (to wage).
