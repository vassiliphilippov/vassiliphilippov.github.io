The intention or desire to cause harm, pain, or suffering to others.

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪs / (AmE), / ˈmælɪs / (BrE)
the intention or desire to cause harm or suffering to others
desire to harm others

Examples

He acted with such malice that it shocked everyone in the room.
The letter was written with clear malice towards her former employer.
There was no malice in his actions, only clumsy misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spite - the desire to hurt, annoy, or offend someone
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Old French 'malice', from Latin 'malitia' (badness, ill-will), from 'malus' (bad).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmælɪs / (AmE), / ˈmælɪs / (BrE)
The desire to cause pain, injury, or distress to another; intention or wish to do harm.
desire to harm others

Examples

He denied that there was any malice behind his comment.
She acted with such malice that nobody trusted her motives.
The anonymous letter was written out of pure malice and jealousy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spite - a desire to hurt, annoy, or offend someone
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Old French 'malice', from Latin 'malitia' (badness, ill-will), from 'malus' (bad).
