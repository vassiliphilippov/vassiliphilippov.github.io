legend

Summary

  • A brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration, chart, or map, often clarifying symbols or elements shown.
    • NOUN: explanatory text for illustration or map
  • a traditional story sometimes popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated, especially one involving mythical or supernatural beings or events
    • NOUN: mythical or supernatural story
  • a person who is famous and admired for their extraordinary achievements or abilities
    • NOUN: famous person known for great achievements
A brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration, chart, or map, often clarifying symbols or elements shown.

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒənd / (BrE)
A brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration, chart, or map, often clarifying symbols or elements shown.
explanatory text for illustration or map

Examples

Always check the legend on a map to understand the symbols.
Each figure in the report includes a legend explaining the different colored lines.
The legend beneath the graph made the data much easier to interpret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caption - A title or brief explanation accompanying an illustration or photo

Etymology

From Latin legenda (things to be read), from legere (to read).

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒənd / (BrE)
a traditional story sometimes popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated, especially one involving mythical or supernatural beings or events
mythical or supernatural story

Examples

According to local legend, the lake is guarded by a mythical creature.
Each country has its own unique legend explaining how the world began.
The legend of King Arthur has inspired countless books and movies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - a traditional story, especially one concerning the early history of a people or explaining a natural or social phenomenon; typically involving supernatural beings or events
  • Synonym: folktale - a story originating in popular culture, typically passed on by word of mouth
  • Antonym: history - the study of past events, particularly human affairs; also, a factual account of past events

Etymology

From Old French 'legende', from Medieval Latin 'legenda' (things to be read).
