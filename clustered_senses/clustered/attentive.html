attentive

Summary

  • Paying close and careful attention to someone or something, or being careful to notice and respond to needs or details.
    • ADJ: paying close attention
    • ADJ: giving close attention or care
Paying close and careful attention to someone or something, or being careful to notice and respond to needs or details.

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɛn.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.tɪv / (BrE)
paying close and careful attention to someone or something
paying close attention

Examples

An attentive reader will notice the subtle clues in the novel.
She is always attentive to the needs of her elderly neighbors.
The teacher appreciated how attentive the students were during her lecture.

Synset Examples

heedful of the warnings
so heedful a writer
heedful of what they were doing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention; distracted
  • Synonym: heedful - taking careful notice of; attentive

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'attentivus', from Latin 'attendere' (to attend).

2. ADJ

/ əˈtɛn.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.tɪv / (BrE)
paying close attention to something or taking care to notice and respond to needs or details
giving close attention or care

Examples

She was always attentive to her children's needs, making sure they were safe and happy.
The students sat attentive during the important announcement from the principal.
The waiter was so attentive that he noticed when our glasses were nearly empty.

Synset Examples

attentive to details
the nurse was attentive to her patient
an attentive suitor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention; negligent
  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice or respond to things
  • Synonym: observant - quick to notice or perceive things

Etymology

From Latin 'attentivus', from 'attendere' meaning 'to attend'.
