The act of betraying someone or something; betrayal of trust or loyalty.

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃəri / (BrE)
the act or practice of betraying someone or something; deliberate betrayal of trust or confidence
deliberate betrayal of trust

Examples

His treachery was eventually revealed, shocking everyone who had trusted him.
She could not forgive the treachery of her closest friend.
Stories of political treachery fill the pages of history books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayal - the act of betraying someone or something
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal or faithful

Etymology

From Old French 'tricherie', from 'trichier' (to cheat), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃəri / (BrE)
the act of betraying someone or something; betrayal of trust or loyalty
betrayal of trust

Examples

A sense of treachery filled the room when the truth came out.
The spy was executed for his treachery against the government.
They could not forgive her treachery after she revealed their secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayal - the act of being disloyal or betraying trust
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal or faithful

Etymology

From Old French 'tricherie' (cheating, trickery), from 'tricher' (to cheat), of Germanic origin.
