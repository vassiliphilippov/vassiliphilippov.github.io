retrograde

adjective
becoming worse; deteriorating
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
becoming worse or returning to an earlier, less advanced state; deteriorating or regressing.

Examples

retrograde step progress
Instead of moving forward, the negotiations took a retrograde turn, undoing earlier progress.
Many critics argued that the new law represented a retrograde step for civil rights.
The company's retrograde policies have led to a decline in innovation and morale.

Related Terms

  • antonym – progressive: moving forward or advancing to a better, more developed state
  • synonym – regressive: becoming less advanced; returning to a former or less developed state
