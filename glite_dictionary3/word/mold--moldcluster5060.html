to mold

verb
to shape or influence
Pronunciation: / moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
to shape or influence something or someone; to give direction or form to

Examples

to mold a mind
Her early experiences helped to mold her character.
Teachers can mold the attitudes of young people in their classrooms.
The campaign was designed to mold public opinion on the issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym – determine: to directly influence or decide the outcome of something
  • antonym – mislead: to guide someone in a wrong direction
  • synonym – shape: to influence or determine the form of something
