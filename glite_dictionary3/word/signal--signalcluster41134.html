signal

noun
nonverbal action conveying a message
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnəl /; / ˈsɪɡ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl /; / ˈsɪɡ.nəl / (BrE)
an action, gesture, event, or sound that communicates information or prompts someone to act

Examples

signal with a hand
She gave him a quick nod, which he took as a signal to start talking.
The lifeguard raised his arm as a signal for everyone to leave the pool.
The runner waited for the starter’s signal before sprinting down the track.
She gave a subtle signal to her friend to leave the party early.
The loud cheers were a signal that the concert was about to begin.
The referee blew the whistle as a signal for the players to start the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cue: a signal to an actor or performer to begin a specific action
  • synonym – gesture: a movement of part of the body, especially a hand or the head, to express an idea or meaning
  • synonym – prompt: a signal or act that encourages someone to take action
