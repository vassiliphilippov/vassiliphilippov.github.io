MacGuffin

noun
plot device driving the story
Pronunciation: / məˈɡʌf.ɪn / (AmE), / məˈɡʌf.ɪn / (BrE)
(in film and literature) an object, event, or character in a story that serves to set and keep the plot in motion despite usually lacking intrinsic importance.

Examples

the MacGuffin drives story
In many thrillers, the MacGuffin is quickly forgotten once the action begins.
The director cleverly used a lost painting as the MacGuffin to spark the entire adventure.
The mysterious briefcase was the MacGuffin that motivated the characters throughout the film.
