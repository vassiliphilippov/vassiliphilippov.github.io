chromosphere

noun
layer of the sun's atmosphere
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊ.məˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.məˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the gaseous layer of the sun's atmosphere between the photosphere and the corona, visible as a reddish glow during a total solar eclipse

Examples

solar chromosphere
During the total solar eclipse, the chromosphere appeared as a thin red ring around the moon.
Scientists study the chromosphere to better understand solar flares and other phenomena.
The chromosphere is not usually visible from Earth except during an eclipse.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – corona: the outermost layer of the sun's atmosphere above the chromosphere
  • degree lower – photosphere: the luminous surface layer of the sun below the chromosphere
