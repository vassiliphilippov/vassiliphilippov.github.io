defective

adjective
markedly below normal in structure, function, or ability
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
Markedly subnormal in structure, function, intelligence, or behavior; not functioning or developing normally.

Examples

defective vision
Some students have defective vision and require corrective glasses.
Speech therapy was recommended for his defective articulation.
The child was born with defective hearing, which made learning to speak difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abnormal: deviating from what is normal or usual
  • antonym – normal: conforming to a standard; regular or expected
