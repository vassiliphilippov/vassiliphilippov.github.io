to plank

verb
to set down heavily or abruptly
Pronunciation: / plæŋk / (AmE), / plæŋk / (BrE)
to put or drop something or oneself down heavily or abruptly, often making a noise

Examples

to plank oneself down
After a long day, she planked herself down on the soft couch and sighed.
He planked the suitcase onto the floor, startling everyone in the room.
The chef planked the wooden board on the counter with a loud thud.

  • synonym – slam: to put or throw down with force
