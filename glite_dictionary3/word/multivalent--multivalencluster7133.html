multivalent

adjective
having more than one valence
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌl.tɪˈveɪ.lənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈveɪ.lənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element, ion, or molecule) having more than one valence, or a valence of three or higher.

Examples

multivalent ion forms
A multivalent vaccine targets multiple strains of a virus in one shot.
Iron is multivalent, existing in both Fe2+ and Fe3+ ionic states.
This compound is multivalent and can form several types of bonds.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bivalent: having a valence of two
  • antonym – monovalent: having a valence of one
