open

adjective
willing to consider new ideas
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən /; / ˈoʊ.pən / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən /; / ˈəʊ.pən / (BrE)
frank, honest, or receptive; willing to consider new ideas or not hiding thoughts or feelings

Examples

open mind

They remained open to the possibility of a last-minute change in the schedule.
She insisted on holding all discussions in open meetings.
The government made an open declaration of its intentions.
There was open hostility between the two groups.
Her criticism was very open and heard by everyone in the company.
The mayor gave an open apology to all citizens after the incident.
They organized an open meeting to discuss the new policy.
