mycelium

noun
network of fungal threads (hyphae)
Pronunciation: / maɪˈsiliəm / (AmE), / mɪˈsiːlɪəm / (BrE)
the network of fine, branching filaments (hyphae) that make up the main growing part of a fungus, usually found in soil or organic material

Examples

mycelium in soil
Scientists studied the mycelium to better understand how fungi decompose organic matter.
The mycelium spread rapidly through the damp, decaying leaves on the forest floor.
You can sometimes see white mycelium growing on old bread as it begins to rot.

  • degree lower – hypha: a single thread-like filament forming part of the mycelium of a fungus
