offsides

adjective
illegally beyond prescribed line/area (sports)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsaɪdz / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsaɪdz / (BrE)
In sports, illegally beyond the prescribed line or area, or ahead of the ball or puck when not permitted by the rules.

Examples

offsides at the line
If you're offsides at the snap, the play is automatically stopped by the referee.
The player was called offsides after crossing the blue line too early.
Their goal didn't count because someone was offsides during the attack.

  • synonym – offside: Illegally beyond a prescribed line or area in a game (variant)
  • antonym – onside: In a legal position according to the rules of the game (sports)
