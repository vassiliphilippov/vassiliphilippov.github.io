hubris

noun
excessive pride or arrogance
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuː.brɪs / (AmE), / ˈhjuː.brɪs / (BrE)
Excessive pride or self-confidence, often leading to downfall or misfortune.

Examples

dangerous hubris
Critics accused the company of hubris after it dismissed warnings about safety risks.
Many classic tragedies revolve around a hero whose hubris leads to disastrous consequences.
The leader's hubris ultimately caused his downfall when he ignored the advice of his advisors.

  • synonym – arrogance: an attitude of superiority manifested in an overbearing manner or in presumptuous claims
  • antonym – humility: a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness
