to prostrate

verb
render helpless or defenceless
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.treɪt / (BrE)
to make someone helpless or defenceless; to reduce to a state of physical or mental weakness

Examples

to prostrate an enemy
The dictator's army prostrated the resistance within days.
The economic crisis prostrated many small businesses, leaving them unable to recover.
Years of war had prostrated the once-proud nation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – empower: to give someone power or authority
  • synonym – overwhelm: to defeat or overpower someone completely
