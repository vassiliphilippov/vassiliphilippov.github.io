fret

noun
state of agitation or worry
Pronunciation: / frɛt / (AmE), / frɛt / (BrE)
a state of anxiety or agitation caused by persistent worry

Examples

in a fret about work
His constant fret over deadlines made it hard to relax.
She couldn't hide her fret about the upcoming interview.
Try not to fall into a fret every time plans change unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anxiety: a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • antonym – calm: a state of peace and lack of agitation
