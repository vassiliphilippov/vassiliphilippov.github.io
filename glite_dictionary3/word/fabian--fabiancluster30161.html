Fabian

adjective
relating to Fabianism or the Fabian Society
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Fabianism or the Fabian Society, a British socialist movement advocating gradual and reformist rather than revolutionary means to social change.

Examples

Fabian approach
Her ideas on economic policy are distinctly Fabian in their emphasis on progress through incremental steps.
The Fabian approach to social reform favored gradual change through legislative measures.
The organization was founded by a group of Fabian intellectuals in London.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reformist: favoring gradual reform over revolution
  • antonym – revolutionary: favoring rapid or radical political or social change
