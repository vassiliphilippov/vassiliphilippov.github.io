blackmail

noun
extortion by threatening disclosure
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈblæk.meɪl / (BrE)
the crime of demanding money or another benefit from someone by threatening to reveal damaging or embarrassing information about them

Examples

blackmail case
He was arrested for attempting to blackmail a politician with sensitive photographs.
She lived in constant fear of blackmail after her secret was discovered.
The company became a victim of blackmail after hackers accessed confidential files.

Related Terms

  • synonym – extortion: the act of obtaining something, especially money, through force or threats
