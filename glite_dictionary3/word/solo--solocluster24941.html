to solo

verb
fly an aircraft alone (pilot term)
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləʊ / (BrE)
(of a pilot) to fly an aircraft alone, without a co-pilot or passengers.

Examples

to solo an airplane
After months of instruction, she finally got permission to solo for the first time.
He soloed yesterday and couldn't stop talking about the experience.
Student pilots typically solo after demonstrating basic flying skills.
