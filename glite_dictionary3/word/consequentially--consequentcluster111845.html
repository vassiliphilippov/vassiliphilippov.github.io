consequentially

adverb
as a result; with consequence
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnsɪˈkwɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃəli / (BrE)
As a result; in a manner that produces a consequence or outcome.

Examples

consequentially lost the chance
She missed the deadline and consequentially lost the opportunity.
The experiment was flawed, and consequentially, the results were unreliable.
The new law was poorly defined and consequentially led to widespread confusion.
