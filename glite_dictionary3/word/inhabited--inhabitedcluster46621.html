inhabited

adjective
having residents; lived in
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhæbɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) having people or animals living there; lived in.

Examples

inhabited island
Scientists study the most remote inhabited areas of the planet.
The island was once densely inhabited, but now it is deserted.
The old castle remains partially inhabited by caretakers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – occupied: being lived in or used by people
  • antonym – uninhabited: not lived in; without residents
