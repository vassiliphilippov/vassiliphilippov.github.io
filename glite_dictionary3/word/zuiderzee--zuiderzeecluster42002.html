Zuiderzee

proper noun
former inlet of the North Sea in the Netherlands
Pronunciation: / ˈzaɪdərˌzeɪ / (AmE), / ˈzʌɪdə(ɹ)ˌzeɪ / (BrE)
a former large inlet of the North Sea on the northern coast of the Netherlands, sealed off by a dam in 1932 to form the IJsselmeer

Examples

the Zuiderzee in Holland
Before it was dammed, the Zuiderzee was a bustling waterway for Dutch trade.
Historic maps of the Netherlands still show the outline of the old Zuiderzee.
The creation of the IJsselmeer marked the end of the Zuiderzee as an open inlet.
