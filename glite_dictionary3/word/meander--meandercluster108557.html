to meander

verb
move in a winding course
Pronunciation: / miˈændɚ / (AmE), / miˈændə / (BrE)
to move in a winding or indirect course, especially over a long distance

Examples

to meander along path
The river meanders gently through the valley, creating scenic bends along its banks.
Tourists meandered through the narrow streets, discovering hidden shops and cafés along the way.
We spent the afternoon meandering around the botanical gardens, enjoying every unexpected turn.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wander: to move aimlessly or without a fixed course
  • synonym – wind: to follow a curving or twisting path
