squirearchy

noun
class of landowning gentry
Pronunciation: / ˈskwaɪrˌɑrki / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪərɑːki / (BrE)
A class of country gentlemen or landowners, collectively regarded as forming a rural ruling class.

Examples

the squirearchy opposes change
He criticized the squirearchy for resisting social reforms that would benefit the poor.
The influence of the squirearchy was evident in rural political decisions of the 18th century.
Victorian novels often depict the squirearchy as guardians of tradition and local power.
