alluvion

noun
gradual addition of land by water
Pronunciation: / əˈluːvi.ən / (AmE), / əˈluːvɪən / (BrE)
The gradual addition of land to a shore or bank, typically by the action of water depositing sediment or by the sea receding.

Examples

alluvion adds land
Disputes can arise when alluvion changes property boundaries over time.
The coastline expanded due to the gradual process of alluvion.
The sudden alluvion created fertile land along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alluvium: soil or material deposited by running water
