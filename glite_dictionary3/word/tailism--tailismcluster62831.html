tailism

noun
tendency to follow others in decisions
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈteɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to follow others rather than take initiative or lead, especially in decision-making or group settings

Examples

group tailism problem
A culture of tailism can prevent an organization from innovating and adapting to change.
Critics accused the manager of tailism when he refused to introduce any new ideas.
His tailism in decision-making often led to missed opportunities for the team.

  • synonym – follower: a person who follows the lead or example of another
  • antonym – leadership: the act of leading or guiding others
