choana

noun
posterior nasal opening to nasopharynx
Pronunciation: / koʊˈænə / (AmE), / kəʊˈænə / (BrE)
either of the two posterior openings at the back of the nasal cavity that connect to the nasopharynx

Examples

nasal choana opening
During embryonic development, the choana forms as part of the separation of the nasal and oral cavities.
In humans, each choana serves as a passageway for air between the nasal cavity and the pharynx.
The doctor examined the patient's choana for any signs of blockage or swelling.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nostril: either of the two external openings of the nose
