immediate

adjective
of the present time or place
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
existing or occurring at the present moment or in the present place; happening, being, or applied without delay.

Examples

immediate area around
Only authorized personnel are allowed in the immediate area around the construction site.
The immediate concern was ensuring everyone's safety during the emergency.
We must address the immediate needs of the community before planning long-term projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym – current: belonging to the present time; happening now
  • antonym – delayed: happening or arriving later than expected or usual
  • synonym – instant: happening or existing at once; immediate
