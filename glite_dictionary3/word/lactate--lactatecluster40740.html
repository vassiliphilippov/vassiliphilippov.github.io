to lactate

verb
produce lactic acid (biochemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæk.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæk.teɪt / (BrE)
(in biochemistry) to produce or form lactate (lactic acid or its salts) as a metabolic byproduct, especially during anaerobic glycolysis.

Examples

cells lactate quickly
During intense exercise, muscle cells lactate quickly when oxygen supply is insufficient.
Researchers measured how tumor tissues lactate under hypoxic laboratory conditions.
When bacteria ferment sugar anaerobically, they often lactate as a metabolic byproduct.
