bare

adjective
without a finish or covering
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / bɛə /; / beə / (BrE)
plain, simple, or undecorated; empty of contents or embellishment

Examples

bare walls
He admired the grain of the bare wood before applying any stain.
Leaving the table bare risks it getting stained from spills.
The walls were left bare, awaiting a final layer of paint.
She preferred a bare table setting, using only essential dishes and utensils.
The living room had bare walls, with not a single picture or decoration.
The new office design is intentionally bare, focusing on simplicity and function over decoration.
After moving out, the apartment was left bare.
The bare closet hinted that no one lived there anymore.
The bare shelves made the room feel deserted.
