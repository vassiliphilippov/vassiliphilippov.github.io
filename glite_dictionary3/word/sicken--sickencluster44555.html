to sicken

verb
cause strong aversion or disgust
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪkən /; / ˈsɪk.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkən /; / ˈsɪk.ən / (BrE)
to make someone feel sick, ill, or disgusted

Examples

to sicken someone deeply
Her blatant dishonesty truly sickens me.
Many people were sickened by the corruption revealed in the report.
The gruesome details of the crime scene sickened everyone present.
Seeing the moldy food on the table began to sicken the guests.
The cruelty described in the report genuinely sickened her.
The foul odor of the garbage would sicken anyone with a sensitive stomach.
The graphic images in the documentary sicken many viewers.
The smell of chemicals in the factory can sicken workers over time.
The spoiled meat sickened everyone at the picnic.
