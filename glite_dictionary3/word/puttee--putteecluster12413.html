puttee

noun
strip of cloth legging, esp. for soldiers
Pronunciation: / pʌˈti / (AmE), / pʊˈti / (BrE)
A long strip of cloth that is wound spirally around the lower leg from ankle to knee, especially as part of a soldier's uniform, notably in World War I.

Examples

puttee on a soldier
A collection of World War I memorabilia included a pair of worn puttees.
He struggled to keep his puttee in place while traversing the muddy trenches.
The soldier wrapped his puttee tightly around his leg before marching into the field.
