hypervolaemia

noun
abnormally increased blood volume
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərvəˈlimiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəːvɒˈliːmɪə / (BrE)
An abnormal increase in the volume of circulating blood in the body.

Examples

hypervolaemia after surgery
Surgical patients are closely monitored to prevent the development of hypervolaemia during recovery.
The patient was diagnosed with hypervolaemia following symptoms of swelling and high blood pressure.
Treatment for hypervolaemia often includes diuretics to help reduce fluid levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hypervolemia: a blood disorder consisting of an increase in the volume of circulating blood (US spelling)
  • antonym – hypovolaemia: a decrease in the volume of circulating blood
