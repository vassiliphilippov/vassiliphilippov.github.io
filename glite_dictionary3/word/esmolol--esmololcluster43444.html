esmolol

noun
short-acting intravenous beta blocker drug
Pronunciation: / ˈɛs.məˌlɔl / (AmE), / ˈɛz.məˌlɒl / (BrE)
A short-acting intravenous beta blocker used primarily to treat cardiac arrhythmias.

Examples

esmolol injection for heart
Because esmolol acts quickly and wears off fast, it is ideal for acute cardiac cases.
During surgery, esmolol was used to manage sudden spikes in blood pressure.
The doctor administered esmolol to control the patient's rapid heart rate.
