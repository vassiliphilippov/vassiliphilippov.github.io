circuitous

adjective
long and indirect (route or journey)
Pronunciation: / sɚˈkjuːɾiəs / (AmE), / səˈkjuːɪtəs /; / sɜːˈkjuːɪtɪəs / (BrE)
not direct; taking a longer or less straightforward path (used for routes, journeys, speech, or actions)

Examples

circuitous route
His explanation was so circuitous that I struggled to understand his main point.
The hikers took a circuitous trail through the forest to enjoy the scenery.
We drove along a circuitous route to avoid the construction on the main road.
He gave a circuitous answer, avoiding the real issue.
Her circuitous way of telling stories often frustrated listeners.
The manager's circuitous explanation only confused the team further.
