split

adjective
cut lengthwise along the grain (wood, etc.)
Pronunciation: / splɪt / (AmE), / splɪt / (BrE)
(especially of wood) cut lengthwise along the grain, often resulting in two or more pieces.

Examples

split wood piece
The carpenter selected a piece of split oak for the repair job.
They needed split bamboo poles to build the fence.
We stacked the split logs neatly beside the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cleft: split, divided, or partially divided into two; often refers to fissures in wood
  • antonym – unsplit: not split or divided; left whole
