to carry

verb
have visible marks or features on the surface
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˈkæri / (BrE)
to have marks, traces, or features clearly visible on the surface or on the skin

Examples

carry a scar
Her smooth skin carried no hint of the illness she had overcome.
That painting carries the marks of centuries of neglect.
The old soldier still carries the scars of battle on his face.
