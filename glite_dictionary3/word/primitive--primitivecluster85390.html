primitive

adjective
relating to something early, simple, or crude
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪm.ə.tɪv /; / ˈprɪm.ə.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
belonging to an early stage of development; simple, not advanced, or not developed (can refer to technology, societies, biology, or objects)

Examples

primitive tools
Early computers were primitive compared to today's sophisticated machines.
Many isolated tribes still use primitive tools made from stone and wood.
The explorers faced primitive living conditions in the remote jungle.
Anthropologists have studied primitive societies to understand early human social structures.
Some anthropologists argue that the term primitive can be misleading or pejorative.
The art of many primitive cultures is celebrated for its symbolism and simplicity.
Scientists studied the primitive structure of the ancient plant to learn about its evolution.
The fossil reveals a primitive species that existed over 200 million years ago.
The platypus is a primitive mammal with features not found in more evolved animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – archaic: very old or old-fashioned; belonging to an earlier period
  • synonym – crude: in a natural or raw state; not yet processed or refined
  • antonym – modern: relating to the present or recent times, especially as opposed to the remote past
  • synonym – naive: showing a lack of experience or formal training, especially in art
  • antonym – sophisticated: developed to a high degree of complexity or refinement
