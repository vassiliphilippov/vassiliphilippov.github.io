to invoke

verb
summon into action or existence, as if by magic
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪnˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to call upon a spirit, deity, or higher power for help, support, or to make something happen, often through prayer or ritual

Examples

to invoke a spirit
Ancient legends say one can invoke rain by performing a sacred dance.
The magician claimed to invoke a protective force around the audience.
The shaman tried to invoke the spirits during the ritual.
During the ceremony, the priest invoked blessings upon the newlyweds.
In moments of crisis, many people invoke the help of a higher power.
The villagers would invoke the goddess before setting out to sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appeal: to make a serious or urgent request for help or support
  • synonym – conjure: to call upon or command a spirit by invocation or magic
  • synonym – summon: to call to appear, especially by supernatural means
