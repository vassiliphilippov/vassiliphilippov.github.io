prohibitionist

noun
advocate of banning alcoholic drinks
Pronunciation: / proʊˌhɪbɪˈʃənɪst / (AmE), / prəʊˌhɪbɪˈʃənɪst / (BrE)
A person who advocates or supports the prohibition of the manufacture and sale of alcoholic drinks.

Examples

prohibitionist leader
As a dedicated prohibitionist, she campaigned tirelessly against alcohol sales.
Many prohibitionists believed that banning liquor would benefit society as a whole.
The prohibitionist movement gained momentum in the early twentieth century.
