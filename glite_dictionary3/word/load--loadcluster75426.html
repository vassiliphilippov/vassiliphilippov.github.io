load

noun
amount that can be carried at one time
Pronunciation: / loʊd / (AmE), / ləʊd / (BrE)
an amount or weight of something that is carried, moved, or processed at one time, especially by a person, vehicle, or machine

Examples

a load of laundry
She did three loads of laundry in one afternoon.
The elevator has a maximum load capacity that must not be exceeded.
The truck carried a full load of gravel to the construction site.
After hiking for hours with a full load on his back, he needed to rest.
That bridge was not designed to handle such a large load of traffic.
The truck was carrying a heavy load of bricks to the construction site.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burden: something that is carried; a load, especially something heavy
  • synonym – cargo: goods carried by a vehicle
  • antonym – unload: to remove goods or cargo from a vehicle
