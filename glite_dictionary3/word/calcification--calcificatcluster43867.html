calcification

noun
hardening of tissue by calcium salts
Pronunciation: / ˌkælsəfɪˈkeɪʃən /; / ˌkæl.sə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkælsɪfɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n /; / ˌkæl.sɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which calcium salts are deposited in tissue, causing it to harden, or an area that has become hardened in this way (medical/biological)

Examples

tissue calcification
A small calcification was detected in the lung tissue during the scan.
Progressive calcification of the arteries can lead to serious health problems.
The X-ray revealed a calcification near the patient's shoulder joint.
A bone spur is often the result of excessive calcification over time.
Geologists study the calcification of coral reefs to track changes in the ocean environment.
The doctor explained that calcification in the arteries can increase the risk of heart disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decalcification: the loss or removal of calcium or calcium salts in tissue
  • synonym – ossification: the process of bone formation or turning into bone
