to total

verb
destroy beyond repair (esp. a vehicle)
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊ.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl / (BrE)
to damage something completely so that it cannot be repaired, especially a vehicle

Examples

to total a car
He accidentally totaled his first car on his way home from college.
She was furious when she saw her brother had totaled her favorite bicycle.
The insurance company declared the vehicle totaled after the severe accident.
