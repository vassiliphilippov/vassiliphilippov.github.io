Henry James

proper noun
American-British novelist (1843–1916)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛnri ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(1843–1916) An American-born writer who became a British citizen, known for his novels exploring consciousness and perception, including 'The Portrait of a Lady' and 'The Turn of the Screw'.
