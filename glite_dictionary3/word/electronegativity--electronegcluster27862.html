electronegativity

noun
ability to attract electrons in a bond (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrəˌnɛɡəˈtɪvɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌnɛɡəˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The ability of an atom or radical to attract electrons toward itself when forming a chemical bond.

Examples

high electronegativity value
Chemists use the concept of electronegativity to predict the nature of chemical bonds between atoms.
Fluorine has the highest electronegativity of all the elements in the periodic table.
The difference in electronegativity between oxygen and hydrogen explains why water is a polar molecule.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electron affinity: the amount of energy released when an electron is added to a neutral atom or molecule
  • antonym – electropositivity: tendency of an atom to donate electrons in a chemical bond
