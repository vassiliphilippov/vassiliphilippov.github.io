heuristic

adjective
enabling discovery; guiding investigation
Pronunciation: / hjuˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / hjʊəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
(of a method or process) enabling or serving to discover or learn something for oneself, especially by allowing for intuitive judgment or trial and error rather than following strict rules.

Examples

heuristic method
A heuristic approach can sometimes find solutions faster than strict algorithms in complex problems.
He used a heuristic method to generate new ideas instead of following a set process.
Medical diagnosis often involves heuristic reasoning based on experience and observation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – algorithmic: following fixed rules or procedures, opposite of heuristic
