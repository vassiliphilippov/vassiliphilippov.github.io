analogous

adjective
similar in function but different in origin
Pronunciation: / əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
(especially in biology) having similar function but a different evolutionary origin or structure.

Examples

analogous structures
In biology, the fins of a fish and the flippers of a whale are considered analogous structures.
The engineer explained that these two mechanisms are analogous in how they solve similar problems.
The wings of bats and the wings of insects are analogous, not homologous.

Related Terms

  • antonym – homologous: having the same evolutionary origin but a different or similar function
