usurper

noun
one who takes power illegally
Pronunciation: / juˈsɝːpɚ / (AmE), / juˈsɜːpə / (BrE)
a person who seizes a position of power or authority unlawfully or by force

Examples

the usurper king
History often paints the usurper as a villain who displaced the rightful king.
The country was ruled by a usurper who claimed the throne after a coup.
The general was labeled a usurper after declaring himself president without an election.

  • antonym – legitimate ruler: rightful holder of a position, office, or rights
  • synonym – usurp: to seize and hold a position, office, or rights by force or without legal right
