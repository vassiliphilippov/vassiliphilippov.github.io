meddlesomeness

noun
tendency to interfere; being meddlesome
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛd.əl.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.əl.səm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being inclined to interfere in other people's affairs or matters that do not concern one

Examples

meddlesomeness in private matters
He was resented for his meddlesomeness in family affairs that were not his concern.
Her meddlesomeness has created tension in the office, as she constantly interferes with others' projects.
The meddlesomeness of the committee members made it difficult to make independent decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discretion: the quality of behaving or speaking in such a way as to avoid causing offense or revealing confidential information
  • synonym – interference: the act of interfering or meddling in others' affairs
  • synonym – intrusiveness: the quality of being unwelcome or intrusive
