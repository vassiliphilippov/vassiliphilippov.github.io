Shavuot

noun
Jewish festival commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈvu.oʊt / (AmE), / ʃəˈvu.ɒt / (BrE)
A Jewish holy day celebrated on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments to Moses on Mount Sinai.

Examples

Shavuot holiday celebration
Shavuot marks the end of the counting of the Omer period in the Jewish calendar.
Many Jewish families gather to celebrate Shavuot with prayers and traditional dairy foods.
On Shavuot, it is customary to read the Book of Ruth in synagogue services.
