pantheism

noun
worship tolerating all gods (rare)
Pronunciation: / ˈpænθiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpanθɪɪzəm / (BrE)
(rare) The worship or belief system that acknowledges or accepts all gods.

Examples

ancient pantheism beliefs
He studied the history of pantheism as a form of tolerant worship in diverse civilizations.
In some ancient cultures, pantheism allowed for the peaceful coexistence of different deities and religious practices.
The rare tradition of pantheism reflects a society open to embracing all gods equally.
