to arraign

verb
accuse or criticize openly
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈreɪn / (BrE)
to accuse or criticize someone, especially to call them publicly to account for a fault or failing

Examples

to arraign a leader
He was arraigned in the media for his lack of leadership.
Several critics arraigned the government for its handling of the crisis.
The manager was swiftly arraigned for the team's poor performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accuse: to charge someone with a fault or offense
  • antonym – praise: to express approval or admiration of someone or something
