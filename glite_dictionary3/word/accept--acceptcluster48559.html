to accept

verb
tolerate an unpleasant situation
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to tolerate or resign oneself to (an unpleasant situation or condition) without protest

Examples

to accept the situation
After losing the election, he had to accept the results and move on with his life.
Many people struggled to accept the changes brought about by the new policy.
She found it difficult at first, but eventually learned to accept her new responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently; to bear up under difficult circumstances
  • antonym – reject: to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • synonym – tolerate: to allow something that one does not like or agree with
