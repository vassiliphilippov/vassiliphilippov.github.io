indefeasible

adjective
cannot be annulled or made void
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈfisəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfiːzəbl / (BrE)
impossible to annul, void, or undo; not able to be made invalid or lost

Examples

indefeasible legal right
Citizenship, once granted under these circumstances, is considered indefeasible by law.
He claimed an indefeasible right to the property, regardless of any subsequent disputes.
Her claim to the inheritance was found to be indefeasible after the court's decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym – revocable: capable of being revoked or cancelled
  • antonym – voidable: able to be declared invalid or void
