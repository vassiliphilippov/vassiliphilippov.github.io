inferior

noun
character printed below another
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
A character or symbol that is set or printed slightly below and to the side of another character, often used in mathematical or chemical notation.

Examples

inferior number
In chemical formulas, the number of atoms is indicated using an inferior next to the element symbol.
The equation included an inferior to denote the base of the logarithm.
Typographers pay special attention to the alignment of each inferior character.

Related Terms

  • synonym – subscript: A character written below the line, particularly in mathematics or chemistry
