M-theory

noun
theory unifying string theories in physics
Pronunciation: / ɛm ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / ɛm ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
a theoretical framework in physics that unifies all consistent versions of superstring theory within an eleven-dimensional universe

Examples

M-theory in physics
Many physicists hope that M-theory will provide a unified description of all fundamental forces in the universe.
Some researchers suggest that M-theory could one day reconcile general relativity with quantum mechanics.
The complexities of M-theory challenge even the most brilliant minds in particle physics.
