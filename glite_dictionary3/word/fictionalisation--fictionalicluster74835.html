fictionalisation

noun
process of making something fictional
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of turning real events, people, or facts into fiction, or a work that does this

Examples

fictionalisation of real events
Her novel is a fictionalisation of her grandmother's experiences during the war.
Some critics argue that the fictionalisation of biographies distorts important facts.
The fictionalisation of historical events can make them more accessible to modern readers.
Her latest novel is a fictionalisation of true events from World War II.
Many biographies include some degree of fictionalisation to engage readers.
The film received criticism for its fictionalisation of the historical record.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adaptation: the process of adapting something for a new use, often in the arts
  • antonym – nonfiction: writing that is factual rather than imaginary or fictional
