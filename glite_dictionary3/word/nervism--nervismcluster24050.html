nervism

noun
theory that the nervous system regulates all processes
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The theory that all behavioral and physiological processes are controlled by the nervous system, as proposed by Pavlov.

Examples

nervism theory explained
Modern neuroscience owes much to the early ideas of nervism championed by Russian scientists.
Pavlov's experiments with conditioned reflexes helped establish nervism as a central principle in physiology.
The doctrine of nervism emphasizes the regulatory role of the nervous system in all bodily functions.
