to equalize

verb
make equal, especially scores
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make things equal, especially to make a score or amount the same for all sides or participants

Examples

to equalize the score
After trailing for most of the match, she equalized with a stunning goal in the last minute.
The team managed to equalize just before halftime, bringing the score to 2–2.
They are hoping to equalize the scores in the next game.

Related Terms

  • synonym – level: to make a score or amount equal
  • antonym – surpass: to go beyond in amount or degree, often a score
