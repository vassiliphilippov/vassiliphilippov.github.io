Hinayana

noun
school of Buddhism focused on personal salvation
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪniˈjɑːnə /; / hiːnəˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌhɪniˈjɑːnə /; / hɪnəˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
a historical term for the early, conservative school of Buddhism, now considered offensive and avoided because it is disrespectful (often used to refer to Theravada Buddhism, contrasted with Mahayana)
