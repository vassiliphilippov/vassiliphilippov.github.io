shibah

noun
seven-day Jewish mourning period
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiːvə / (AmE), / ˈʃiːvə / (BrE)
In Judaism, a period of seven days of mourning observed after the death of a close relative.

Examples

observe shibah together
After his grandmother passed away, the family gathered to observe shibah.
It is customary to bring food to a house where shibah is being observed.
They visited their friends who were sitting shibah for their father.
