toxemia

noun
pregnancy condition with high blood pressure, swelling, and protein in urine
Pronunciation: / tɑkˈsimiə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
An abnormal medical condition occurring during pregnancy, characterized by high blood pressure, swelling, and protein in the urine.

Examples

pregnancy toxemia symptoms
Swelling and high blood pressure can be early indicators of toxemia.
The doctor closely monitored her for signs of toxemia during the last trimester of pregnancy.
Untreated toxemia can pose risks to both the mother and the baby.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preeclampsia: A more common term for the same pregnancy complication as toxemia
