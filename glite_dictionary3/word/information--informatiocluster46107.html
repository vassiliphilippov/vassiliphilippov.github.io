information

noun
quantitative measure of uncertainty (theory)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(communication theory) A quantitative measure of the uncertainty or surprise associated with a particular outcome, usually expressed in bits.

Examples

information in bits
In digital communication, information is measured in bits that represent the reduction of uncertainty.
Scientists use entropy to quantify the amount of information in a random variable.
The information content of a message depends on how unpredictable the outcome is.

Related Terms

  • synonym – entropy: (in information theory) measure of information content or uncertainty
