to prognosticate

verb
predict or foretell a future event
Pronunciation: / prɑgˈnɑstəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / prɒgˈnɒstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to predict or foretell a future event, especially using signs, indications, or omens

Examples

to prognosticate the future
It is difficult to prognosticate the outcome of such a complex negotiation.
Many analysts attempt to prognosticate how the stock market will perform each year.
The gray clouds seemed to prognosticate the arrival of a heavy storm.
Dark clouds on the horizon often prognosticate a coming storm.
Many experts attempt to prognosticate the outcome of the election based on early voting data.
The economic indicators prognosticate a period of slow growth ahead.
