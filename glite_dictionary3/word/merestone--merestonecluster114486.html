merestone

noun
historic stone boundary marker
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛrˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɪə.stəʊn / (BrE)
A historic term for a boundary marker made of a pile of stones topped by an upright slab, used to mark land limits.

Examples

merestone marks boundary
Ancient maps often reference a merestone as a key boundary point.
During the legal dispute, the location of the original merestone became crucial evidence.
The old merestone at the edge of the field marked the division between two estates.
