Rome

noun
leadership of the Roman Catholic Church
Pronunciation: / roʊm / (AmE), / rəʊm / (BrE)
The leadership or authority of the Roman Catholic Church, often referred to by metonymy from its location in the city of Rome.

Examples

authority of Rome
He traveled to Rome to seek guidance from the Church's highest authorities.
Many important decisions about doctrine come directly from Rome.
The bishop followed the directives issued by Rome regarding church policies.
