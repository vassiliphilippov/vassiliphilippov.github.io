maniclike

adjective
resembling mania of manic-depressive illness
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪkˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkˌlaɪk / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of the mania observed in manic-depressive illness, especially in terms of heightened energy, activity, or mood.

Examples

maniclike energy burst
After three sleepless nights, he entered a maniclike state, working frenetically on multiple projects.
Her maniclike behavior during the meeting surprised her colleagues, who were used to her being reserved.
The patient’s maniclike symptoms included rapid speech and an unusually elevated mood.
