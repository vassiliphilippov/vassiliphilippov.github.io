hollow

noun
small valley between hills or mountains
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
a small, sheltered valley or a space or depression that is sunken below the surrounding surface, especially one naturally formed

Examples

forest hollow valley
After the storm, the stream swelled and filled the hollow with rushing water.
Deer gathered each morning in the misty hollow at the forest’s edge.
They found a quiet hollow to set up their campsite away from the wind.
He found shelter in a small hollow beneath the hillside.
Rainwater collected in the hollow left by the fallen rock.
The animal crawled into the hollow at the base of the old tree.
