corona

noun
electrical discharge around a conductor
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊnə / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊnə / (BrE)
an electrical discharge caused by ionization of the surrounding air near a conductor, often visible as a faint glow

Examples

corona discharge glow
A visible corona formed around the high-voltage power lines during the thunderstorm.
Engineers measured the current produced by the corona to monitor electrical discharge levels.
The sharp points of the wire produced a corona that glowed in the dark laboratory.
