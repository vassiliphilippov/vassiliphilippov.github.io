helicopter

noun
aircraft with rotating overhead blades
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛlɪˌkɑptər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlɪˌkɒptə / (BrE)
an aircraft with rotating overhead blades that lift and propel it, allowing for vertical takeoff and landing

Examples

a helicopter lands
He watched as the helicopter hovered above the city skyline.
News crews often use a helicopter to capture live footage of traffic.
The rescue team arrived by helicopter within minutes of the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym – airplane: an aircraft with fixed wings propelled by engines
  • synonym – autogyro: a type of aircraft that uses an unpowered rotor for lift
