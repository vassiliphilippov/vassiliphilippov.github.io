Thetis

proper noun
Greek sea nymph, mother of Achilles
Pronunciation: / ˈθiːtɪs / (AmE), / ˈθiːtɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, one of the fifty sea nymphs known as the Nereids; mother of Achilles by Peleus.

Examples

Thetis in Greek myth
In Homer’s Iliad, Thetis pleads with Zeus on behalf of her son Achilles.
Many stories depict Thetis as a caring but protective mother to Achilles.
The ancient Greeks feared the wrath of Thetis and honored her in their prayers.
