incorruptness

noun
resistance to data tampering
Pronunciation: / ɪn.kəˈrʌpt.nəs / (AmE), / ɪn.kəˈrʌpt.nəs / (BrE)
(in computing) the property of data, files, or a system that they cannot be tampered with or altered; data integrity or immutability.

Examples

data incorruptness
The blockchain's incorruptness ensures that records cannot be altered without network consensus.
Engineers designed the archive with checksums to guarantee the incorruptness of long-term backups.
Regulatory auditors praised the system's incorruptness because every transaction was cryptographically signed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – data integrity: the accuracy and consistency of stored data; freedom from corruption or unintended change
  • synonym – immutability: the state of being unchangeable or unable to be altered once recorded
