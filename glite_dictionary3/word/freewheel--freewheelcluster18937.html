freewheel

noun
device allowing wheels to spin freely
Pronunciation: / ˈfriˌwil / (AmE), / ˈfriːwiːl / (BrE)
a device, especially on the rear wheel of a bicycle, that allows the wheel to turn freely when the pedals are not being turned

Examples

bicycle freewheel mechanism
A stuck freewheel can prevent the rear wheel from spinning properly.
After replacing the freewheel, the bike rode much more smoothly.
The bicycle's freewheel lets you coast downhill without pedaling.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coaster brake: a type of brake integrated into the hub of a bicycle that's activated by pedaling backward
