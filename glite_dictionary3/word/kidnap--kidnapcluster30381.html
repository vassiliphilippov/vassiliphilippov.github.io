to kidnap

verb
abduct someone by force for ransom
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪdˌnæp / (AmE), / ˈkɪd.næp / (BrE)
to take someone away illegally by force, often in order to demand money or other conditions for their release

Examples

to kidnap a child
Police believe the gang planned to kidnap the wealthy businessman for ransom.
She was kidnapped while walking home from school.
They threatened to kidnap the ambassador's family if their demands were not met.
