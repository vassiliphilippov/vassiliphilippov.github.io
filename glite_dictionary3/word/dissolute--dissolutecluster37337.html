dissolute

adjective
lacking moral restraint; immoral
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈdɪsəluːt / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) lacking moral restraint; living in a way that is considered immoral or wild.

Examples

dissolute lifestyle
Despite his dissolute lifestyle in his youth, he eventually became a respected community leader.
Many historians describe the 18th-century court as notoriously dissolute and scandal-prone.
The novel’s main character is a dissolute nobleman who squanders his fortune on parties and gambling.

Related Terms

  • antonym – virtuous: having or showing high moral standards
