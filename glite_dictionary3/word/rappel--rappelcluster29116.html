rappel

noun
a descent down a cliff or wall using a rope
Pronunciation: / rəˈpɛl / (AmE), / raˈpɛl / (BrE)
a descent down a vertical surface, such as a cliff or wall, using a rope anchored above and wrapped around the body for control, especially in mountaineering or climbing

Examples

a rappel down a cliff
Each climber took turns making the rappel as the sun began to set.
She prepared for the final rappel down the steep rock face.
The most thrilling part of the hike was a hundred-foot rappel into the canyon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abseil: to descend a rock face or other near-vertical surface by using a rope
