secure

adjective
firmly fixed; not likely to fail
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkjʊr / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
firmly fixed or fastened; not likely to fail, break, or give way under stress or use.

Examples

secure grip
Her grip on the railing was secure despite the strong winds.
Make sure the lid is secure before carrying the box.
The scaffolding was not secure and posed a hazard to workers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unstable: likely to give way, fall, or fail; not secure
