interreflection

noun
reciprocal reflection between surfaces
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntərɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
reciprocal reflection of light between two or more surfaces, especially when each surface reflects light back to the other(s)

Examples

interreflection of light surfaces
In thin lenses, interreflection between the surfaces can cause slight optical artifacts.
The lighting model accounts for interreflection between walls, making the room look more realistic.
When two mirrors face each other, repeated interreflection creates a tunnel of images.
