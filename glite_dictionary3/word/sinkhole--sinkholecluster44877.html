sinkhole

noun
decoy server that traps malicious traffic
Pronunciation: / \u02c8s\u026a\u014bk\u02ccho\u028al / (AmE), / \u02c8s\u026a\u014bk\u02cch\u0259\u028al / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) a server, service, or network address configured to attract, capture, and isolate malicious or unwanted internet traffic (such as botnet communications) for analysis or to prevent further harm.

Examples

internet sinkhole
Security researchers deployed a sinkhole to capture and analyze botnet command traffic overnight.
The ISP redirected infected devices into a sinkhole to prevent spam campaigns from reaching customers.
After discovering the domain's malicious behavior, the team registered and pointed it at a sinkhole.

Related Terms

  • synonym – honeynet: a network of honeypots designed to monitor and analyze malicious activity
  • synonym – honeypot: a decoy computer or service set up to attract attackers and study their behavior
