to sleep

verb
to rest in an unconscious state
Pronunciation: / sliːp / (AmE), / sliːp / (BrE)
to rest with eyes closed and mind and body inactive, usually at night, in a state in which one is not conscious and the body recovers strength

Examples

to sleep well
He couldn't sleep because of the loud thunderstorm outside.
It is important to sleep at least eight hours each night for good health.
Sometimes I sleep late on weekends to catch up on rest.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – doze: to sleep lightly or for a short time
  • degree lower – nap: to sleep for a short period, especially during the day
  • synonym – slumber: to sleep, especially peacefully or deeply
  • antonym – wake: to emerge or cause to emerge from sleep; to be or become awake
