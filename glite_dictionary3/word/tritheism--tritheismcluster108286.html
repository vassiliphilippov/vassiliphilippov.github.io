tritheism

noun
belief in three separate gods in Christianity
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪθiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪθiːɪzəm / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the doctrine or belief that the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit are three separate gods, rather than three persons of one God.

Examples

belief in tritheism
During the council, the bishops condemned tritheism as a heresy.
Some critics have accused certain interpretations of the Trinity of bordering on tritheism.
Theologians debated whether his views constituted tritheism or orthodox Trinitarianism.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monotheism: belief in the existence of only one God
  • synonym – polytheism: belief in or worship of more than one god
