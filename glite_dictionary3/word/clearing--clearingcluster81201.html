clearing

noun
removal of suspicion or doubt
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing suspicion or doubt, especially regarding a person's guilt or integrity

Examples

clearing of charges
He finally achieved the clearing of all charges against him in court.
Her public clearing by the authorities restored her reputation.
The new evidence led to the clearing of her name after years of suspicion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accusation: a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • synonym – exoneration: the act of officially clearing someone from blame or fault
