motivative

adjective
causing or encouraging action
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊt̬ɪˌveɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈməʊtɪˌveɪtɪv / (BrE)
causing or tending to cause someone to take action; impelling action or behavior

Examples

motivative effect on people
Her motivative approach encourages employees to reach new goals.
Some philosophers argue that moral statements are primarily motivative rather than descriptive.
The professor gave a motivative speech that inspired students to join the campaign.
