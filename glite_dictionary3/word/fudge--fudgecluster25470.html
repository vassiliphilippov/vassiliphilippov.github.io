to fudge

verb
alter information dishonestly
Pronunciation: / fʌdʒ / (AmE), / fʌdʒ / (BrE)
to change or present information dishonestly or inaccurately, often to deceive people

Examples

to fudge the numbers
He was caught fudging the details in his official statement.
If you fudge the results, the report will be inaccurate.
The accountant tried to fudge the numbers to hide expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fabricate: to invent or make up something, often with intent to deceive
  • synonym – falsify: to alter information or evidence deliberately to mislead
  • synonym – misrepresent: to give a false or misleading account of something
