organic

adjective
relating to carbon-based chemical compounds
Pronunciation: / ɔːrˈɡænɪk /; / ɔrˈɡænɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪk / (BrE)
relating to chemical compounds containing carbon, or derived from living things

Examples

organic molecules
Chemists study the properties of organic molecules to understand living systems.
Hydrocarbons are organic compounds commonly found in fuel and natural gas.
Plastic materials can be derived from organic sources such as plants.
Only organic compounds can be synthesized by living cells.
Scientists tested the soil for organic matter to determine its fertility.
The fossils were discovered within layers of organic material deposited millions of years ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inorganic: not containing carbon or not derived from living matter (opposite of organic in chemistry)
