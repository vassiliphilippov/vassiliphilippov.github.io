vena cerebri interna

noun
a major deep cerebral vein
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪnə səˈriːbraɪ ɪnˈtɜrnə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə səˈrebrɪ ɪnˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
either of a pair of veins that run along the midline of the brain and unite to form the great cerebral vein, draining deep structures of the cerebrum

Examples

vena cerebri interna blockage
Blockage of the vena cerebri interna can lead to serious neurological deficits.
During the operation, the surgeon clearly identified the two venae cerebri internae.
The vena cerebri interna plays a crucial role in draining blood from the deep structures of the brain.
