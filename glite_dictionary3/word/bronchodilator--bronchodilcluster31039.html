bronchodilator

noun
drug widening bronchial tubes
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɑːŋkoʊˈdaɪˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkəʊˈdaɪleɪtə / (BrE)
a drug that relaxes and widens the bronchial tubes in the lungs, making breathing easier

Examples

use a bronchodilator inhaler
Before exercise, he inhaled a bronchodilator to prevent shortness of breath.
People with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease often use a bronchodilator to improve their breathing.
The doctor prescribed a bronchodilator to help manage her asthma symptoms.

Related Terms
