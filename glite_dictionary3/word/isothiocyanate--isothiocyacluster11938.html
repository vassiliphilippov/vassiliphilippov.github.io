isothiocyanate

noun
chemical compound found in pungent plants
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊˌθaɪoʊˈsaɪəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˌθaɪəˈsʌɪəneɪt / (BrE)
any of a family of chemical compounds containing the –N=C=S group, found in plants such as horseradish and mustard and responsible for their pungent flavor.

Examples

isothiocyanate compound in mustard
Certain vegetables like broccoli and watercress are rich in isothiocyanate compounds.
Scientists are studying the potential role of isothiocyanate in cancer prevention.
The pungent taste of horseradish comes from an isothiocyanate released when the root is grated.
