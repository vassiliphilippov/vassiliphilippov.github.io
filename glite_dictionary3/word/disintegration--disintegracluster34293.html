disintegration

noun
breakdown of a radioactive substance
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈteɡrəʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈteɡrəʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a radioactive substance spontaneously breaks apart, emitting ionizing radiation

Examples

radioactive disintegration
Radioactive disintegration plays a crucial role in nuclear power generation.
Scientists measure the rate of disintegration to determine the age of ancient rocks.
The disintegration of uranium atoms releases energy in the form of radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – radioactive decay: the process in which an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation
