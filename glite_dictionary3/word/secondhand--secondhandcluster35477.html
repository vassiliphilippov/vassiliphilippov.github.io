secondhand

adjective, adverb
not from the original source
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkəndˌhænd /; / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəndˌhænd /; / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of information or knowledge) not obtained directly from the original source; learned through someone else

Examples

secondhand news
Much of what we know about ancient times is secondhand information.
She only had a secondhand account of the event, so some details might be missing.
The news he shared was clearly secondhand and not very reliable.
I only know about the incident secondhand, through friends who were at the event.
Most of my information came secondhand from colleagues who read the report.
She heard the news secondhand rather than from the person involved.
