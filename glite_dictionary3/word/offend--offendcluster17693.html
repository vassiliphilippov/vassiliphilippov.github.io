to offend

verb
to hurt someone's feelings
Pronunciation: / əˈfɛnd / (AmE), / əˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel hurt, upset, or annoyed by something you say or do

Examples

to offend a friend
I didn't mean to offend you with my comment.
She was offended when she wasn't invited to the party.
Try not to offend anyone with your jokes.
He accidentally offended his colleagues with his harsh criticism.
I hope my comments did not offend you.
Some people are easily offended by jokes.
Her rude comments offended everyone at the dinner table.
Some people are easily offended by jokes about sensitive topics.
The sight of the dirty kitchen really offends me.
