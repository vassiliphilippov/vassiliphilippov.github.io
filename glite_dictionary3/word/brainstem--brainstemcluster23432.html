brainstem

noun
central trunk of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪnˌstɛm / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnˌstɛm / (BrE)
the central trunk of the brain, continuous with the spinal cord and consisting of the medulla oblongata, pons, and midbrain

Examples

the brainstem controls breathing
Basic life functions are controlled by the brainstem.
Damage to the brainstem can affect breathing and heart rate.
The surgeon explained that the tumor was located near the brainstem.
