nilpotent

adjective
(math) becomes zero at some power
Pronunciation: / nɪlˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / nɪlˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
(of a mathematical element) becoming equal to zero when raised to a certain power.

Examples

nilpotent matrix property
A matrix is called nilpotent if some power of it equals the zero matrix.
In linear algebra, nilpotent operators play an important role in simplifying systems.
The algebraic structure contains several nilpotent elements, each vanishing under repeated multiplication.

Related Terms

  • antonym – idempotent: (of a mathematical element) unchanged when operated on by itself
