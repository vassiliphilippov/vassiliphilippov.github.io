polyzoa

noun
colonial aquatic animals (zooids)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A group of small aquatic animals that live in colonies, each consisting of many zooids, found in marine or freshwater environments.

Examples

marine polyzoa colonies
Many polyzoa species help purify water by filtering out microscopic particles.

The reef was teeming with polyzoa, forming intricate patterns on the rocks.
