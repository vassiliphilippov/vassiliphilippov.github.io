overcapitalisation

noun
excessive capitalization of a business
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚˌkæp.ɪ.təl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˌkæp.ɪ.tələ.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The situation in which a business has issued more shares or obtained more capital than its assets or earnings justify, often resulting in reduced returns to shareholders.

Examples

overcapitalisation of a company
A history of overcapitalisation made the firm vulnerable during the economic downturn.
Analysts warned that rapid expansion could cause overcapitalisation and financial instability.
The company suffered from overcapitalisation, leading to poor returns for its investors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – undercapitalisation: inadequate capitalization of a business
