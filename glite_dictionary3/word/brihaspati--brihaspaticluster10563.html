Brihaspati

proper noun
Hindu god of ritual devotion and wisdom
Pronunciation: / briːˈhʌspəti / (AmE), / briːˈhʌspəti / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the personification of ritual devotion and the preceptor of the gods; regarded as the god of wisdom and eloquence.

Examples

Brihaspati guides the gods
Brahmins often invoke Brihaspati during sacred rituals to seek wisdom and eloquence.
Scholars study the hymns dedicated to Brihaspati to understand Vedic concepts of devotion.
The ancient texts describe Brihaspati as the chief priest and counselor to the gods.
