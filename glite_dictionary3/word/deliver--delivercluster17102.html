to deliver

verb
perform an action (e.g., attack, blow)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlɪvɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvə / (BrE)
to give, present, or perform something such as a speech, idea, or official decision

Examples

to deliver a speech
She practiced her speech many times before she had to deliver it to the audience.
The boxer managed to deliver a powerful punch in the final round.
The general ordered the troops to deliver a swift attack at dawn.
He practiced for weeks to deliver his ideas clearly and confidently.
She was nervous before she had to deliver her speech to the large audience.
The professor will deliver a lecture on climate change tomorrow evening.
After hours of deliberation, the jury was ready to deliver a decision.
It took several months before the committee could deliver its final report.
The judge will deliver her verdict tomorrow morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – execute: to carry out or perform an action
  • synonym – present: to formally give or introduce (a speech or idea) to others
  • synonym – render: to formally give or announce (an opinion, decision, or judgment)
  • antonym – withhold: to refrain from giving or performing
