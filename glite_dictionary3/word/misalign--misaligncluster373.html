to misalign

verb
to arrange incorrectly; to be out of alignment
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsəˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to place or arrange improperly, so that the correct relative position is not maintained

Examples

to misalign the parts
If you misalign the gears, the engine won't run properly.
If your printer heads become misaligned, the text and images will print incorrectly.
Poor installation can cause the doors to misalign over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – align: to place or arrange in a straight line or in correct relative positions
