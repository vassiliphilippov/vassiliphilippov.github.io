to resile

verb
formally withdraw from an agreement or statement
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪl /; / rɪˈzʌɪl / (BrE)
(formal) to withdraw from or abandon a promise, agreement, or position, especially officially or legally

Examples

to resile from an agreement
He cannot legally resile from the contract without facing penalties.
Once the promise is made, it is difficult to resile from it without damage to one's reputation.
The company decided to resile from its earlier agreement due to unforeseen circumstances.
He found it difficult to resile from positions he had defended for years.
The politician refused to resile from her controversial statements despite public scrutiny.
Under pressure, the witness chose to resile from his original testimony.
