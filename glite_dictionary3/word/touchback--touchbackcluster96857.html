touchback

noun
football play where ball enters and stays in end zone
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌtʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃˌbæk / (BrE)
(in American football) a situation in which the ball is kicked into the defending team's end zone and is not returned, resulting in the ball being brought out to a designated spot on the field.

Examples

kick results in touchback
The coach instructed the kicker to aim for a touchback to prevent a return.
The kickoff resulted in a touchback, so the offense started at their 25-yard line.
Whenever the ball sails over the end zone, it is ruled a touchback.
