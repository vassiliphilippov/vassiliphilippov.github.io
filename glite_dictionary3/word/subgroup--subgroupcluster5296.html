subgroup

noun
smaller group within a larger group
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsʌbɡruːp / (BrE)
a smaller group that is part of a larger group, often with its own distinguishing features or functions

Examples

a subgroup of people
Chimpanzees in the wild often form subgroups based on age, gender, or dominance.
In the survey, a subgroup of participants showed a different response than the majority.
The committee split into smaller subgroups to tackle different tasks.
