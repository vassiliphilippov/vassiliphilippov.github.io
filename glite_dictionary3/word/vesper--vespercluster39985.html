Vesper

noun
Venus as the evening star
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛspər / (AmE), / ˈvɛspə / (BrE)
A poetic or literary name for the planet Venus when it appears as the evening star, visible at sunset in the western sky.

Examples

Vesper shines brightly
Early astronomers charted the movements of Vesper with great care.
In ancient poetry, Vesper often symbolizes the coming of night.
The bright Vesper hung low in the western sky as the sun set.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Hesperus: Venus as the evening star; another literary/poetic name
  • antonym – Phosphorus: Venus as the morning star; poetic/literary term for Venus at dawn
