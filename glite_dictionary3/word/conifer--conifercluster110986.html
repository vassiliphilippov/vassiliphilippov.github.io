conifer

noun
cone-bearing tree or shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnɪfər / (AmE), / ˈkɒnɪfə / (BrE)
A tree or shrub that produces cones and has needle-shaped or scale-like leaves, belonging to the group of gymnosperms.

Examples

conifer forest
Many conifer species can thrive in cold, mountainous regions.
The forest was dominated by tall conifers such as pines and firs.
Unlike most deciduous trees, a conifer does not lose its needles in winter.

  • synonym – evergreen: A plant that retains green leaves all year round
