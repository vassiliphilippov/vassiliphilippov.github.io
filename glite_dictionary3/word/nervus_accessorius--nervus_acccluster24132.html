nervus accessorius

noun
accessory cranial nerve
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs əkˈsɛsəri.əs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs əkˈsɛsəri.əs / (BrE)
a cranial nerve that arises from both cranial and spinal roots, uniting to form a single nerve; also known as the accessory nerve.

Examples

nervus accessorius injury
Damage to the nervus accessorius can cause weakness in certain neck muscles.
In anatomy class, we studied the pathway of the nervus accessorius.
The surgeon carefully identified the nervus accessorius during the procedure to avoid nerve injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accessory nerve: the eleventh cranial nerve, controlling certain shoulder and neck muscles
