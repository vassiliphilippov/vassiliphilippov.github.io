Pius VII

proper noun
pope from 1800 to 1823, clashed with Napoleon
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪ.əs səˈvɛnθ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.əs səˈvɛnθ / (BrE)
(1742–1823) Italian pope from 1800 to 1823, known for his conflicts with Napoleon, signing a concordat with France, and his imprisonment and later return to Rome.

Examples

Pope Pius VII imprisoned
Pius VII was taken prisoner by Napoleon in 1809 and only returned to Rome years later.
As pope, Pius VII faced immense challenges, including pressure from European powers.
The concordat between Pius VII and Napoleon normalized relations between the Catholic Church and France.
