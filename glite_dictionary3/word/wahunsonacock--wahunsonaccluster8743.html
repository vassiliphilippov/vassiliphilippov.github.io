Wahunsonacock

proper noun
Native American chief; father of Pocahontas
Pronunciation: / wɑːˌhuːnˈsɑːnəkɑːk / (AmE), / wɑːˌhuːnˈsɒnəkɒk / (BrE)
A Native American chief and founder of the Powhatan confederacy of tribes in eastern Virginia, and the father of Pocahontas (circa 1550–1618).

Examples

Chief Wahunsonacock of Powhatan
Wahunsonacock was a powerful leader who united more than thirty tribes in the tidewater region of Virginia.
Historians believe that Wahunsonacock played a key role in the early interactions between Native Americans and English settlers.
Pocahontas was the daughter of Wahunsonacock, also known as Chief Powhatan.
