speculativeness

noun
quality of being speculative or risky (finance)
Pronunciation: / spɛk.jə.lə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / spɛk.jʊˈleɪ.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being speculative, especially with regard to financial risk or uncertainty of outcomes

Examples

financial speculativeness
He assessed the speculativeness of the new venture before deciding to invest.
Regulations were introduced to reduce the speculativeness in the stock market.
The speculativeness of certain investments makes them attractive to risk-tolerant individuals.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conservatism: the quality of being conservative, avoiding unnecessary risks (especially in finance)
  • synonym – riskiness: the quality of involving risk or danger; lack of safety
