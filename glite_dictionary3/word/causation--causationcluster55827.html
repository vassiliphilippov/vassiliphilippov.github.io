causation

noun
the act or process of causing something
Pronunciation: / kɔˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kɔːˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of causing something to happen or exist; the relationship between cause and effect

Examples

causation of events
Scientists study causation to understand how various factors influence outcomes in experiments.
The lawyer argued that insufficient evidence existed to prove causation in the case.
There was no clear causation between the two incidents, according to the investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cause: something that brings about a result; reason for an event
  • antonym – effect: a result or outcome produced by a cause
