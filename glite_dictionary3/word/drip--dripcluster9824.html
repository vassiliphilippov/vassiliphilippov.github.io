to drip

verb
to let fall in drops
Pronunciation: / drɪp / (AmE), / drɪp / (BrE)
to let liquid fall in small drops, or for liquid to fall in small drops

Examples

to drip water
Please drip some wax onto the envelope to seal it.
She dripped a few drops of water onto the canvas for a special effect.
The faucet continued to drip after the plumber left.
After washing his hands, water would drip from his fingers as he reached for a towel.
Rainwater began to drip from the edge of the old roof.
The paint continued to drip onto the floor, forming a colorful puddle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dribble: to let or fall in drops, often irregularly
  • antonym – gush: to flow out suddenly or forcefully
  • synonym – trickle: to flow in a small stream or in drops
