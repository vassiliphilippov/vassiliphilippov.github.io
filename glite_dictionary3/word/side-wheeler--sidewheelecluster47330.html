side-wheeler

noun
paddle steamer with wheels on both sides
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌwɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌwiːlə / (BrE)
a paddle steamer with a paddle wheel on each side, used primarily in the 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

historic side-wheeler steamboat
In the 1800s, a side-wheeler was a common sight on the Mississippi River.
The old side-wheeler navigated the river with ease, her paddles churning the water on both sides.
Tourists marveled at the restored side-wheeler during the annual steamboat festival.
