Saccharomycetaceae

noun
family of yeasts (fungi)
Pronunciation: / ˌsækəroʊˌmaɪsəˈtæsiː / (AmE), / ˌsakərəʊˌmaɪsɪˈtɛːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi comprising the typical yeasts, characterized by reproduction through budding and fermentation of carbohydrates.

Examples

Saccharomycetaceae yeast family
Most commercial baking yeasts belong to the Saccharomycetaceae family.
Researchers are studying the Saccharomycetaceae to learn more about fermentation processes.
