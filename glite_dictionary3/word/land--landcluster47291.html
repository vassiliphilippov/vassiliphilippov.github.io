to land

verb
come down and rest after being in the air
Pronunciation: / lænd / (AmE), / lænd / (BrE)
to come down and rest on a surface after moving through air or water, or to bring something or someone to shore or ground

Examples

The smugglers decided to land the cargo at night to avoid detection.

Passengers land at the harbor before making their way into the city.
The ship landed safely on the island after a week at sea.
