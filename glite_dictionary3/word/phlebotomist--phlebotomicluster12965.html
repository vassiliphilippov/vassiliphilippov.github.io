phlebotomist

noun
person trained to draw blood
Pronunciation: / fləˈbɑː.t̬ə.mɪst / (AmE), / flɪˈbɒ.tə.mɪst / (BrE)
A person trained to draw blood from patients, usually for medical testing, transfusions, or donations.

Examples

phlebotomist draws blood
Hospitals employ a dedicated phlebotomist to collect blood samples efficiently and safely.
If you have a fear of needles, let the phlebotomist know before the procedure begins.
The phlebotomist greeted me warmly before preparing to take my blood.
