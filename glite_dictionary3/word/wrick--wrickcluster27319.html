to wrick

verb
twist or sprain a joint suddenly
Pronunciation: / rɪk / (AmE), / rɪk / (BrE)
to twist a part of the body, especially a joint, suddenly and painfully so as to sprain it

Examples

to wrick your back
Be careful not to wrick your neck when looking up suddenly.
He managed to wrick his ankle while jogging in the park.
She wricked her wrist trying to open the stubborn jar lid.
