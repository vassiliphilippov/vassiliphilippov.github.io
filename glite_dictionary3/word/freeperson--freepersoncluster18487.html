freeperson

noun
person not a serf or slave
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːˌpɜːrsən / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
A person who is not a serf or a slave; someone who possesses personal freedom and is not owned by another.

Examples

a freeperson in society
In medieval society, a freeperson could own land and move at will.
Once granted his freedom, he proudly declared himself a freeperson.
The distinction between a freeperson and a serf was fundamental in feudal Europe.

Related Terms

  • antonym – serf: an agricultural laborer bound to the land under the feudal system
  • antonym – slave: a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
