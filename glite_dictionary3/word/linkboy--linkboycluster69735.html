linkboy

noun
boy who carried a torch for pedestrians (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋkˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋk.bɔɪ / (BrE)
(historical) a boy hired to carry a torch or lantern to light the way for pedestrians at night.

Examples

a linkboy with torch
A linkboy waited outside the theater, hoping to earn a coin guiding theatergoers home.
In 18th-century London, a linkboy could guide you safely through dark, dangerous streets.
The wealthy often hired a linkboy to carry a torch when venturing out after dusk.
