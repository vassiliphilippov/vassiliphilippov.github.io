aged

adjective
having a specified age
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒd / (AmE), / eɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having reached or possessing a specified age; used before stating the number of years someone has lived.

Examples

aged 10 years
Children aged five and under are admitted free.
My grandmother adopted a cat aged three years.
The boy, aged ten, won first prize in the competition.
