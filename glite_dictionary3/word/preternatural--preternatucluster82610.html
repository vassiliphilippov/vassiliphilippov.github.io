preternatural

adjective
beyond what is natural; supernatural
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːtərˈnætʃərəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːtəˈnatʃ(ə)rəl / (BrE)
very unusual or unnatural; beyond what is normal or ordinary, sometimes seeming supernatural

Examples

preternatural ability
He moved with a preternatural grace that seemed almost impossible for a human.
Her preternatural ability to sense danger saved her life more than once.
The storm had a preternatural intensity that left the entire town in awe.
A preternatural silence fell over the room after the announcement.
His preternatural memory allowed him to recall details most people would forget.
She possessed a preternatural calm even in the face of danger.

Related Terms

  • antonym – natural: existing in or derived from nature; not made or caused by humankind
  • antonym – ordinary: normal, not unusual
  • synonym – supernatural: attributed to forces beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature
  • synonym – uncanny: strange or mysterious in an unsettling way
