convoy

noun
group of vehicles traveling together
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.vɔɪ /; / ˈkɑnˌvɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
a group of vehicles or ships traveling together, usually for protection

Examples

a convoy of trucks
A long convoy of trucks stretched along the highway, delivering relief aid to the city.
During the snowstorm, the buses traveled in a convoy to ensure none got stranded.
The supply convoy was escorted by several military vehicles for safety.
During wartime, a convoy of merchant ships set sail under the protection of several destroyers.
Pirates attacked the convoy at dawn, hoping to capture the valuable cargo.
The navy organized a convoy to ensure vital supplies reached the mainland safely.
