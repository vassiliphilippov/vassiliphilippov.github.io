holdover

noun
something surviving from the past
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌəʊvə / (BrE)
something that remains or survives from an earlier time or previous system

Examples

a holdover from history
Many traditions at the school are holdovers from the 19th century.
The building's ornate design is a holdover from a bygone era.
The policy is a holdover from an earlier administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leftover: something that remains after the rest has been used or dealt with
  • synonym – relic: an object or custom that has survived from the past
