escape

noun
leakage or release of a fluid
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp / (BrE)
an act or instance of a substance, such as a liquid or gas, leaking or being released from a container or confined space

Examples

an escape of steam
A slight escape of coolant was observed during the inspection.
Detecting any escape of natural gas is crucial for safety in the laboratory.
The engineers quickly sealed the pipe to stop the escape of toxic chemicals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leak: an unintended hole or crack that allows the escape of fluid
  • synonym – spill: an act of something, especially liquid, flowing out accidentally
