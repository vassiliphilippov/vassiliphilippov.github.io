chinchilla

noun
a thick, soft wool-cotton fabric
Pronunciation: / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə / (BrE)
A thick, twilled fabric made of wool and cotton, often with a soft, napped surface resembling that of chinchilla fur.

Examples

chinchilla fabric jacket
Designers often use chinchilla for winter garments because of its warmth and texture.
The coat was made from a luxurious chinchilla that felt soft to the touch.
This antique blanket is woven from genuine chinchilla fabric.
