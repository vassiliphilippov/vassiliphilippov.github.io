reference

noun
what a word or expression refers to
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈrɛf.ər.əns / (BrE)
the relationship between a word or phrase and the object or concept it refers to (in linguistics or philosophy)

Examples

reference in language
In philosophy of language, the reference of 'Venus' is the planet itself.
The reference of the word 'cat' is the class of all cats.
Understanding the reference of a term is key to analyzing its use in a sentence.
Ambiguity can arise when the reference of a pronoun is unclear in a sentence.
In semantics, the reference of a word defines what it actually refers to in the real world.
Philosophers often debate the reference of proper names and how meaning is attached to them.
