agrypnia

noun
religious vigil before a feast day
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrɪpniə / (AmE), / əˈɡrɪpniə / (BrE)
a vigil or night-long religious service held before certain feast days, especially in Eastern Christian traditions.

Examples

all-night agrypnia service
An agrypnia often includes prayers, readings, and hymns lasting through the night.
Many Orthodox Christians attend the agrypnia on the night before Easter.
The church was beautifully decorated for the agrypnia in celebration of the feast day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vigil: a period of keeping awake, typically for devotional purposes
