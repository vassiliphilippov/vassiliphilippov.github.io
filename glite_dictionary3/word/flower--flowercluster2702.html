to flower

verb
produce or yield flowers (of a plant)
Pronunciation: / ˈflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce or yield flowers; to bloom.

Examples

to flower in spring
Many desert plants only flower after a heavy rainfall.
The apple tree flowered beautifully this year, promising a good harvest.
The tulips in the garden flower every spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bloom: (of a plant) to produce flowers; to be in flower
  • antonym – wither: (of a plant) to become dry and shrivelled; lose freshness and vitality
