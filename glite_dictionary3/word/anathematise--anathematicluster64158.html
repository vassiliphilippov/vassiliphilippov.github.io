to anathematise

verb
to formally curse or condemn
Pronunciation: / əˈnæθəməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæθəmətaɪz / (BrE)
to curse, condemn, or declare to be evil, often with formal authority or threat of divine punishment

Examples

to anathematise an idea
Some critics anathematise modern art, dismissing it as corrupt or meaningless.
The priest anathematised the heretic before the assembled congregation.
Throughout history, societies have anathematised those who broke sacred laws.

  • synonym – curse: to call for harm or misfortune upon
