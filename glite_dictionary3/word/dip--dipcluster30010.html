to dip

verb
take a small amount from (resources)
Pronunciation: / dɪp / (AmE), / dɪp / (BrE)
to take a small amount from a supply, especially money or resources, often when this is not done regularly or is needed to cover an extra cost

Examples

to dip into savings
I had to dip into my savings to pay for the repairs.
She often dips into her emergency fund for unexpected bills.
When business was slow, they reluctantly dipped into company reserves.

Related Terms

  • synonym – draw on: to use information, experience, money etc. to help you do something
  • synonym – withdraw: to take money out of an account or a supply
