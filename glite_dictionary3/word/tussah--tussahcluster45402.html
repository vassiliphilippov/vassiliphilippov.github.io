tussah

noun
wild silk-producing moth
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌ.sə / (AmE), / ˈtʌ.sə / (BrE)
An oriental moth that produces brownish silk, commonly used in the production of wild silk fabrics.

Examples

tussah silk fabric
She wore a beautiful scarf made from tussah silk.
The tussah moth is native to India and is prized for its strong, natural silk.
Wild silk from tussah moths is coarser than that produced by domesticated silkworms.
