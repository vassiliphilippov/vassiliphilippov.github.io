to suspend

verb
temporarily bar from school or office
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to officially prevent someone from attending work, school, or an activity for a limited period, usually as a punishment or while a decision is made

Examples

to suspend a student
If you continue to arrive late, they may be forced to suspend you.
She was suspended from her job while the accusations were investigated.
The principal decided to suspend the student for breaking the school rules.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – expel: to officially force someone to leave a school or organization permanently
