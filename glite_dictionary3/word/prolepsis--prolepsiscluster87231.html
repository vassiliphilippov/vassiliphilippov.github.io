prolepsis

noun
rhetorical device: answering objections in advance
Pronunciation: / proʊˈlɛpsɪs / (AmE), / prəʊˈlɛpsɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical or literary device in which objections are anticipated and answered in advance.

Examples

prolepsis in argument
By employing prolepsis, the essayist anticipated the main objections to her theory.
In literature, authors often employ prolepsis to strengthen their narratives by preemptively countering criticism.
The speaker used prolepsis to address arguments that his opponents might raise later in the debate.

  • synonym – anticipation: the act of anticipating; expectation or prediction
