backband

noun
band supporting shafts on a harnessed horse
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbækˌbænd / (BrE)
A broad band that passes over the back of a horse and supports the shafts of a vehicle, used as part of a harness.

Examples

backband on a horse
A worn backband could cause the shafts to slip during a long journey.
He replaced the old leather backband with a new one to improve the horse's comfort.
The coachman checked the backband to ensure it was secure before starting the carriage.
