bladder

noun
flexible sac for holding liquid or gas
Pronunciation: / ˈblædər / (AmE), / ˈbladə / (BrE)
a hollow, flexible sac in the body or a device that can expand to hold liquid or gas, especially the organ in humans and animals that collects and stores urine.

Examples

full bladder
A fish often uses its swim bladder to control buoyancy in the water.
Inflating the small bladder inside the soccer ball gives it its bounce.
The doctor explained that the bladder stores urine before it is passed from the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym – urinary bladder: the specific bladder organ for storing urine in humans and animals
