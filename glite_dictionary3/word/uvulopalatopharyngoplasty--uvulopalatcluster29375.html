uvulopalatopharyngoplasty

noun
throat surgery to treat snoring or sleep apnea
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːvjəloʊˌpælət̬oʊˌfærɪnˈɡɒpləsti / (AmE), / ˌjuːvjələʊˌpælətəʊˌfærɪŋˈɡɒpləsti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove or reshape tissue in the throat, especially the uvula, soft palate, and pharynx, to improve airflow and treat severe snoring or sleep apnea

Examples

undergo uvulopalatopharyngoplasty surgery
After undergoing uvulopalatopharyngoplasty, her snoring was significantly reduced.
Many patients recover quickly from uvulopalatopharyngoplasty and notice improved quality of sleep.
The doctor recommended uvulopalatopharyngoplasty to treat his severe obstructive sleep apnea.
