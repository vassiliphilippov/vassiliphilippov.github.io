haemoglobinuria

noun
presence of hemoglobin in urine
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˌɡloʊbəˈnjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈnjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of hemoglobin in the urine, usually as a result of the breakdown of red blood cells.

Examples

haemoglobinuria after malaria
Acute haemoglobinuria can occur following severe malaria or incompatible blood transfusion.
Dark-colored urine can be a sign of haemoglobinuria in patients with certain blood disorders.
The doctor ordered tests to confirm whether haemoglobinuria was present after the patient reported red-tinted urine.
