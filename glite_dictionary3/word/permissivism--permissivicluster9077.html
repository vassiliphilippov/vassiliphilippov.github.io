permissivism

noun
lenient attitude toward social behavior
Pronunciation: / pərˈmɪs.ɪˌvɪz.əm / (AmE), / pəˈmɪs.ɪ.vɪz.əm / (BrE)
The principle or practice of allowing or tolerating a wide range of social behavior, often characterized by lenience or a lack of strict rules.

Examples

social permissivism
Critics argue that excessive permissivism may erode traditional values.
Some parents worry that permissivism leads to a lack of discipline in children.
The era of the 1960s is often associated with a rise in social permissivism.

Related Terms

  • antonym – strictness: the quality of being strict, opposite to lenience
  • synonym – tolerance: acceptance of different views or behaviors
