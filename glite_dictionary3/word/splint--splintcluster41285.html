splint

noun
device for immobilizing body part
Pronunciation: / splɪnt / (AmE), / splɪnt / (BrE)
a rigid or flexible device applied to a limb or part of the body to prevent movement and protect it, especially after injury or surgery

Examples

splint for a broken arm
After the accident, a splint was applied to her leg until further treatment could be done.
Children sometimes need a splint to support their injured fingers.
The nurse used a splint to keep his fractured arm stable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cast: a hard protective shell for immobilizing a broken bone
