to sulk

verb
be silently moody; show displeasure
Pronunciation: / sʌlk / (AmE), / sʌlk / (BrE)
to be silently moody or show displeasure, often by withdrawing or refusing to talk

Examples

to sulk alone
After being told he couldn't go to the party, he began to sulk in his room.
Rather than argue, he chose to sulk silently in the corner.
She would sulk for hours whenever she lost a game.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pout: to push out one's lips, especially in annoyance or to show displeasure
