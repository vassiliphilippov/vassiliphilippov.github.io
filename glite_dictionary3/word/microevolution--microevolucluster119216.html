microevolution

noun
small-scale evolutionary changes within a species
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˌiː.vəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
Evolutionary change within a species or small group of organisms, especially over a relatively short period, involving small genetic variations.

Examples

microevolution in bacteria
Antibiotic resistance in bacteria is a clear example of microevolution in action.
Scientists study microevolution by observing genetic changes within populations over successive generations.
The Galapagos finches are often cited as a textbook case of microevolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym – macroevolution: major evolutionary changes at or above the species level
