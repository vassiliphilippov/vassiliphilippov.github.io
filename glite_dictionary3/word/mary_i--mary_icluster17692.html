Mary I

proper noun
Queen of England, 1553–1558, restored Catholicism
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛri ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1516–1558) The Queen of England from 1553 to 1558, known for restoring Roman Catholicism and persecuting Protestants; daughter of Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon, and wife of Philip II of Spain.

Examples

Mary I restored Catholicism
Mary I was succeeded by her half-sister Elizabeth I, ushering in a new era for England.
During the rule of Mary I, hundreds of Protestants were executed for their religious beliefs.
Many historians debate whether Mary I's reign truly reversed the Protestant Reformation in England.
