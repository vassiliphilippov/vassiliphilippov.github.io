sporozoite

noun
motile, infective stage of a sporozoan
Pronunciation: / spɔɹ.əˈzoʊ.aɪt / (AmE), / spɔː.rəˈzəʊ.aɪt / (BrE)
a motile, infective form of a sporozoan parasite produced in the life cycle stage following sporogony

Examples

sporozoite infects blood
The malaria parasite enters the human body in the form of a sporozoite injected by a mosquito.
Under the microscope, scientists observed the movement of each sporozoite within the infected cell.
Vaccines targeting the sporozoite stage aim to prevent the progression of malaria in the host.
