carbamide

noun
chemical compound in urine; fertilizer component
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːr.bəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.maɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed from ammonia and carbon dioxide, found as the main solid component of urine, and used in fertilizers, animal feed, and plastics
