dumbly

adverb
without speaking; silently
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌmli / (AmE), / ˈdʌmli / (BrE)
In a way that shows an inability or unwillingness to speak; without saying anything.

Examples

sit dumbly silent
He stared dumbly at the broken vase, unable to find words.
She nodded dumbly in agreement, not trusting herself to speak.
The children waited dumbly for instructions, their faces full of uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • synonym – silently: in a quiet or noiseless manner
  • antonym – vocally: with the use of one's voice
