to leapfrog

verb
progress with sudden large advances
Pronunciation: / ˈliːp.frɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈliːp.frɒɡ / (BrE)
to move or progress by making sudden, significant advances rather than gradual steps

Examples

to leapfrog into success
By taking online courses, he was able to leapfrog into a senior position much sooner than expected.
The company hopes to leapfrog its competitors by investing in cutting-edge research.
The new wireless technology could allow some countries to leapfrog traditional wired infrastructure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overtake: to move ahead of someone or something by surpassing
