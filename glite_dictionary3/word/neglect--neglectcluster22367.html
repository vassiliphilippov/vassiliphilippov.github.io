to neglect

verb
fail to do something; leave undone
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡlɛkt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛkt / (BrE)
to fail to do something, especially something you are responsible for, often through carelessness or forgetting

Examples

to neglect to finish homework
She often neglected to call her parents, even though they worried about her.
They were criticized for neglecting important safety procedures during construction.
You should not neglect your duties at work, as it can have serious consequences.
He forgot to do his assignment because he neglected to write it down in his planner.
If you neglect to include all relevant documents, your application may be delayed.
The committee neglected reviewing several important proposals at the last meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – attend to: to deal with or take care of something
  • synonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym – omit: to leave out or fail to include
  • synonym – overlook: fail to notice or do something
