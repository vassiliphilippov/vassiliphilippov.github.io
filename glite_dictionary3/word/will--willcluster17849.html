will

noun
legal document for property after death
Pronunciation: / wɪl / (AmE), / wɪl / (BrE)
a legal document that states what is to happen to a person's property after they die

Examples

will and testament
After her death, the lawyer read the contents of her will to the family.
She left her entire estate to charity in her will.
They discovered a handwritten will among his personal papers.
