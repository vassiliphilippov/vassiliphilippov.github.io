to carry

verb
have with you or on your person
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˈkæri / (BrE)
to hold and move someone or something from one place to another; to transport or support while moving

Examples

to carry a bag
During the hike, she carried a first aid kit in her backpack at all times.
He always carries his wallet in his back pocket.
I usually carry a notebook to jot down ideas whenever they come to me.
Buses carry thousands of commuters into the city each morning.
Pipes carry fresh water to all the apartments in the building.
She asked me to carry her bags upstairs.
A powerful breeze can carry seeds to distant fields.
The explosion carried debris across the entire street.
The strong current was able to carry the boat far from the shore.
