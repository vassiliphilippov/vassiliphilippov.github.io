demoralisation

noun
loss of morale and disorder
Pronunciation: / dɪˌmɔrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which people or groups lose confidence, hope, or morale, often leading to discouragement or confusion

Examples

demoralisation of workers
The sudden policy changes caused confusion and demoralisation among the staff.
The team's repeated losses led to complete demoralisation within the locker room.
Widespread layoffs created a sense of demoralisation across the company.
After weeks of setbacks, a sense of demoralisation spread through the community.
The constant criticism led to complete demoralisation within the team.
Widespread layoffs caused significant demoralisation among the staff.

Related Terms

  • antonym – order: an organised, controlled state; opposite of disorder
