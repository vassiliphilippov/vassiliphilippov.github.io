easterly

noun
wind from the east
Pronunciation: / ˈistɚli / (AmE), / ˈiːstəli / (BrE)
A wind blowing from the east.

Examples

a strong easterly
A strong easterly blew across the coastline, bringing cooler air to the region.
Fishermen delayed their departure because a persistent easterly was making the seas rough.
The crops suffered after several days of a dry easterly sweeping through the valley.
