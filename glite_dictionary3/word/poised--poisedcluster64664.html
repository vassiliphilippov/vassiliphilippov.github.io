poised

adjective
calm and self-assured
Pronunciation: / pɔɪzd / (AmE), / pɔɪzd / (BrE)
having a calm and assured manner, in full control of oneself, especially in difficult situations

Examples

poised under pressure
Despite the pressure of the interview, she remained poised and answered every question confidently.
Even in the midst of chaos, their leader appeared poised and in complete control.
He gave a poised performance that impressed both judges and audience alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collected: calm and self-controlled; not nervous or agitated
  • antonym – flustered: agitated or confused; lacking composure
  • synonym – self-possessed: having control of one's feelings or actions; composed
