to ping

verb
make a sharp, ringing noise
Pronunciation: / pɪŋ / (AmE), / pɪŋ / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound (like metal being struck or an engine knocking)

Examples

to ping against metal
Raindrops ping against the metal roof during the storm.
She pinged the glass lightly to test if it was cracked.
The pebble pinged off the windshield with a sharp sound.
A stray bullet pinged against the lamp post during the commotion.
She paused as her phone pinged with a new notification.
The tiny stone pinged off the metal fence, making a sharp noise.
If your car pings under acceleration, it may need higher-octane gasoline.
They noticed the old sedan pinging as it climbed the mountain road.
When I switched to cheaper fuel, the engine began to ping on steep hills.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – clang: to make a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • synonym – knock: produce a sharp sound in an engine due to improper combustion
  • synonym – pink: (of an engine) to make a light metallic knocking sound (chiefly British)
  • synonym – ring: to make a clear resonant or vibrating sound
