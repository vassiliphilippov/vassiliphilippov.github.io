to produce

verb
bring a work (film, play, book) into public release
Pronunciation: / prəˈdus / (AmE), / prəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to organize and finance the creation of a work, especially a film, play, or book, and bring it into public release or distribution

Examples

to produce a movie
After years of work, they finally produced their debut novel.
She hopes to produce an independent film next year.
The company will produce a series of educational podcasts this fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym – publish: to prepare and issue a book, journal, or other material for public sale
  • synonym – release: to make a product available to the public
