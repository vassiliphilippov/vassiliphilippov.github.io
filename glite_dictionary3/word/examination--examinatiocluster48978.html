examination

noun
formal systematic questioning
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˌzæm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzæm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally and systematically questioning someone, especially in an official or academic context

Examples

police examination of witness
During the police examination, each suspect was questioned thoroughly.
Her examination of the evidence revealed several inconsistencies.
The lawyer's examination of the witness lasted over an hour.
