litter

noun
chair or bed carried by bearers
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
a vehicle consisting of a chair or bed carried on two poles by bearers, used for transporting people, especially in former times

Examples

royal litter ride
In ancient Rome, wealthy citizens often traveled in a litter when they went to public events.
The explorers constructed a makeshift litter to transport their wounded companion out of the jungle.
The injured nobleman was carried through the streets on a litter borne by four servants.
