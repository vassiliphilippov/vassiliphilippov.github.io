to conduct

verb
allow passage of heat, electricity, etc.
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to allow heat, electricity, sound, etc., to pass through or along something; to serve as a medium for transmission

Examples

conduct electricity
Copper wires conduct electricity very efficiently, making them ideal for use in wiring.
Materials like wool and fiberglass do not conduct heat well, which makes them good insulators.
The metal pan conducts heat evenly, allowing food to cook more consistently.

Related Terms

  • synonym – transmit: to send or convey from one person or place to another
