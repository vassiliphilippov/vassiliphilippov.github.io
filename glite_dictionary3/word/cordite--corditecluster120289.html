cordite

noun
type of smokeless gunpowder
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.daɪt / (BrE)
a smokeless explosive powder made from nitroglycerin, guncotton, and petroleum jelly, used chiefly as a propellant in cartridges.

Examples

cordite smell after gunfire
A faint smell of cordite lingered in the air after the guns were fired.
Factories producing cordite had to follow strict safety protocols to prevent explosions.
The army supplied their soldiers with cartridges filled with cordite for the new rifles.
