shopper

noun
person comparing competitors' prices for a store
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑpər / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpə / (BrE)
a person employed by a store to shop at competitors' stores to compare prices and merchandise

Examples

mystery shopper
A skilled shopper can help a retail chain stay competitive by reporting on trends at other stores.
Many companies hire a shopper to secretly gather detailed information from their competitors.
The shopper visited several rival stores to note differences in product displays and pricing.
