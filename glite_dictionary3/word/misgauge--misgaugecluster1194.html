to misgauge

verb
judge or estimate incorrectly
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
To judge or estimate something incorrectly.

Examples

to misgauge the time
I completely misgauge the amount of time needed to finish the project.
It's easy to misgauge the size of the room from the photos.
She misgauged his reaction and was surprised by his anger.
