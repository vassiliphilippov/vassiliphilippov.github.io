treacherous

adjective
guilty of betrayal or deception
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (BrE)
guilty of or involving betrayal or deception; dangerously untrustworthy.

Examples

treacherous friend
Political alliances in ancient times were often treacherous and short-lived.
She was shocked by her friend's treacherous behavior during the argument.
The hikers were warned about the treacherous paths that could become slippery after rain.
