Jezebel

proper noun
cruel queen in the Old Testament
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzəbəl / (BrE)
The wife of Ahab, king of Israel, known from the Old Testament as a cruel and immoral queen who promoted the worship of Baal and persecuted the prophets of Israel.

Examples

Queen Jezebel of Israel
Many regard Jezebel as a symbol of wickedness and idolatry in ancient Israel.
Some literary works use the name Jezebel to represent a manipulative or immoral woman.
The story of Jezebel is often cited in sermons warning against moral corruption.
