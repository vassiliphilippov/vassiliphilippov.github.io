wise

adjective
improperly forward or impudent (informal)
Pronunciation: / waɪz / (AmE), / waɪz / (BrE)
(informal) behaving in a way that is boldly disrespectful or cheeky, especially by showing impudence or sass toward authority.

Examples

wise remark
Don’t get wise with me, young man!
If you get wise with your boss, you might get fired.
She was punished for being wise to the teacher during class.

Related Terms

  • synonym – impertinent: rude and not showing respect
  • synonym – impudent: not showing proper respect; impertinent
  • antonym – respectful: showing politeness or deference
