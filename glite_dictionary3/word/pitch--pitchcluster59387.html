to pitch

verb
try to sell or offer for sale
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
to try to sell or present something, or to persuade people to buy or support an idea

Examples

to pitch an idea
Dozens of companies will pitch their new ideas at the trade fair next week.
He used to pitch encyclopedias door to door in the summer.
Street vendors pitch their products to passing tourists in the busy square.
She carefully pitched her presentation to engage a professional audience.
The marketing campaign was pitched at young adults looking for adventure.
You should pitch your offer to match the client's interests and needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aim: to direct something at a particular target or audience
  • synonym – peddle: to sell goods, often by traveling from place to place
  • synonym – tailor: to make or adapt something for a particular purpose or person
