to entrench

verb
occupy a trench or secured position (military)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
(of troops or individuals) to establish oneself in a position of protection, especially by digging or occupying a trench or secured defensive area.

Examples

to entrench behind defenses
After hours of digging, the platoon was well entrenched and ready for any attack.
The soldiers began to entrench themselves as nightfall approached.
To withstand the enemy assault, the unit quickly entrenched behind sandbags and barbed wire.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fortify: to make a place stronger and safer against attack
  • antonym – surrender: to stop resisting an enemy; to yield
