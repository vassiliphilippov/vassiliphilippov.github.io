inward

adjective
existing in the mind; mental
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnwɚd / (AmE), / ˈɪnwəd / (BrE)
(of feelings, thoughts, etc.) existing in the mind; mental or spiritual rather than external or physical.

Examples

inward feelings grow
His response was calm, but his inward emotions were in turmoil.
Meditation helps people explore their inward thoughts and feelings.
She felt an inward sense of relief when she heard the news.

Related Terms

  • synonym – internal: existing or occurring inside the mind or body
  • antonym – outward: relating to things external to the mind; external or visible
