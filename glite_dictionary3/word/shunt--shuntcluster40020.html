shunt

noun
low-resistance conductor to divert electric current
Pronunciation: / ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
a conductor of low resistance connected in parallel with another device to divert part of the electric current

Examples

current shunt
A precise shunt is essential to protect sensitive components from too much current.
The engineer installed a shunt to measure the current flowing through the circuit.
You can use a shunt for current measurement in high-power circuits.
