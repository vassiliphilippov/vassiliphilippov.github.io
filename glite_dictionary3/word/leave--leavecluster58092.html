to leave

verb
not change or interfere with
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liːv / (BrE)
to allow something to remain unchanged, undisturbed, or in its current state; to refrain from taking or interfering with

Examples

leave it alone
I wish people would leave the artwork alone instead of trying to improve it.
Please leave your shoes at the door to keep the floor clean.
We decided to leave the garden untouched and let the wildflowers grow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disturb: to interfere with or disrupt
  • antonym – take: to remove or carry away
