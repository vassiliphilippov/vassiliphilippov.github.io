hero

noun
principal character in a story
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
The principal character in a play, movie, novel, or poem, typically the one with whom the audience identifies most closely.

Examples

hero of the story
Audiences often cheer for the hero as the central figure in the story.
In classical tragedies, the hero is not always morally perfect but is always central to the plot.
The hero of the novel faces many challenges before finding happiness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – antagonist: the main opponent of the hero or protagonist in a story
  • synonym – protagonist: the main character in a story or drama
