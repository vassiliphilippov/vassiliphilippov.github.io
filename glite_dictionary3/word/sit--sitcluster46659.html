to sit

verb
hold a formal meeting or session
Pronunciation: / sɪt / (AmE), / sɪt / (BrE)
(of a committee, court, parliament, etc.) to hold a formal meeting or session in order to conduct official business.

Examples

sit in session
Parliament sat for several hours debating the new law.
The committee will sit next Thursday to discuss the proposal.
The court usually sits every weekday during the trial season.
