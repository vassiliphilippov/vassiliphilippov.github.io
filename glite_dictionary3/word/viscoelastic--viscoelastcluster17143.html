viscoelastic

adjective
having both viscous and elastic properties
Pronunciation: / ˌvɪskoʊɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌvɪskəʊɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
(of a material) showing both viscous and elastic characteristics when undergoing deformation.

Examples

viscoelastic material properties
Rubber is often described as viscoelastic because it stretches and flows to some extent when stressed.
Some polymers exhibit viscoelastic behavior, meaning they have both solid-like and liquid-like responses to force.
The researchers tested the viscoelastic properties of the new synthetic material under different temperatures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – elastic: able to return to original shape after stretching or compression
  • synonym – viscous: having a thick, sticky consistency
