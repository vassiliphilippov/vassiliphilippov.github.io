blusterer

noun
person who speaks boastfully or indiscreetly
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌstərər / (AmE), / ˈblʌstərə / (BrE)
a person who speaks in a loud, boastful, or indiscreet manner, often causing trouble or drawing attention

Examples

loud blusterer at work
Everyone in the office tried to avoid the blusterer because he often revealed sensitive information.
She saw him as a blusterer who valued attention more than discretion.
The blusterer dominated the meeting, interrupting others with his loud and careless remarks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – braggart: someone who boasts about achievements or possessions
  • synonym – bully: a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate others
  • antonym – quietist: a person who is calm and refrains from making loud or indiscreet statements
