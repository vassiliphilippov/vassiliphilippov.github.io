ritonavir

noun
pharmacokinetic booster drug

(in medicine) a pharmacokinetic booster: a drug, often given in low dose, used to increase the blood levels of other drugs by inhibiting the liver enzyme cytochrome P450 3A4 (CYP3A4).

Examples

ritonavir as drug booster
Clinicians commonly prescribe low-dose ritonavir to boost the levels of other protease inhibitors.
In the study, adding ritonavir increased plasma concentrations of the antiviral by inhibiting CYP3A4.
The treatment guideline recommends using ritonavir as a pharmacokinetic booster in certain combination therapies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – CYP3A4 inhibitor: a substance that reduces the activity of the liver enzyme CYP3A4, slowing drug breakdown
  • synonym – booster: a drug given to increase the effect or blood levels of another drug (pharmacokinetic booster)
