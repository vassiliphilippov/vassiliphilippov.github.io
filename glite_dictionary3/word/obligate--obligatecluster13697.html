to obligate

verb
commit money/resources to fulfill an obligation
Pronunciation: / ˈɑblɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒblɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to commit or earmark (resources, especially money) for a particular purpose, usually to meet a legal or formal requirement

Examples

to obligate funds
The contract requires the company to obligate resources for employee training.
The government must obligate funds before the end of the fiscal year.
We have already obligated most of the grant money for this project.

Related Terms

  • synonym – earmark: to designate something for a specific purpose
