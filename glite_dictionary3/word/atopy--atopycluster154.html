atopy

noun
genetic tendency to develop allergies
Pronunciation: / ˈætəpi / (AmE), / ˈatəpi / (BrE)
A genetic tendency to develop allergic reactions, such as asthma, eczema, or hay fever, which become apparent shortly after exposure to an allergen.

Examples

family history of atopy
A family history of atopy increases the risk of developing allergic diseases like asthma or dermatitis.
People with atopy may develop allergic reactions such as asthma within minutes of exposure to certain triggers.
The doctor diagnosed the child with atopy after reviewing her recurring eczema and hay fever symptoms.

  • synonym – allergy: an abnormal reaction by the immune system to a usually harmless substance
