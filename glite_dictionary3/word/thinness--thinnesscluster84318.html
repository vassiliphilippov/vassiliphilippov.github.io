thinness

noun
state of being thin (not thick)
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having little thickness or width (of a physical object or material)

Examples

thinness of the ice
He commented on the thinness of the ice covering the pond.
The thinness of the book surprised me, as I expected it to be much thicker.
The thinness of the wire makes it difficult to see.
She was worried about the thinness of the wall between the two rooms.
The thinness of the ice made it dangerous to walk across the pond.
This fabric is prized for its exceptional thinness and softness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slenderness: the quality of being slender, usually referring to a gracefully thin appearance
  • antonym – thickness: the dimension through an object as opposed to its length or width; opposite of thinness
