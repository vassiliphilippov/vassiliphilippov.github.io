acrocyanosis

noun
bluish discoloration of extremities
Pronunciation: / ˌækroʊˌsaɪəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌakrɔʊˌsʌɪəˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A bluish discoloration of the extremities, such as fingers or toes, caused by poor circulation, often triggered by cold or emotional stress.

Examples

acrocyanosis of fingers
People with acrocyanosis may experience discomfort in their hands and feet during winter.
The doctor diagnosed her with acrocyanosis after noticing her blue-tinged fingers in the cold room.
Wearing gloves prevented the acrocyanosis from worsening while she was outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cyanosis: bluish discoloration of the skin due to lack of oxygen in the blood
