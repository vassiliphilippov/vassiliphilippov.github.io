to gall

verb
become or make sore from rubbing
Pronunciation: / ɡɔl / (AmE), / ɡɔːl / (BrE)
(of skin or body part) to become or make sore through rubbing or friction.

Examples

to gall the skin
Hiking for hours in wet boots can gall your feet.
The constant movement of the strap galled his shoulder, leaving a raw patch of skin.
The rough saddle began to gall the horse's back after a long ride.
