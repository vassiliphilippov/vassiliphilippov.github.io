dreadnought

noun
early 20th-century battleship with uniform big guns
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛdˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.nɔːt / (BrE)
A battleship of the early 20th century, characterized by having large guns of the same caliber and superior firepower and speed compared to previous battleships.

Examples

a dreadnought battleship
Historians often debate the strategic impact of dreadnoughts during World War I.
The launch of the dreadnought revolutionized naval warfare in the early 20th century.
The museum featured a detailed model of a British dreadnought from 1910.
