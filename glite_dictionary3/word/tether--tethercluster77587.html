tether

noun
restraint for tying an animal
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛðər / (AmE), / ˈtɛðə / (BrE)
a rope, chain, or similar restraint used to tie an animal so that it can only move within a certain area

Examples

dog tether
After grazing, the goat chewed through its tether and wandered off.
The horse was secured to a post with a tether.
They found a broken tether in the field where the cow had been tied.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leash: a cord or strap to control an animal, especially a dog
  • antonym – untether: to release from a tether
