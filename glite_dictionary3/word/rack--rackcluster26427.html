to rack

verb
to run before a gale (nautical)
Pronunciation: / ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
(of a ship) to run before a gale; to move swiftly with the wind from behind, especially under storm conditions.

Examples

ship racks before wind
As the storm intensified, they decided to rack southward, letting the wind carry them.
The captain ordered the crew to rack with the gale to avoid being broadsided by the waves.
The old schooner began to rack before the gale, gaining speed as the wind howled astern.
