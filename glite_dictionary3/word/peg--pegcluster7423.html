peg

noun
device for tuning a stringed instrument
Pronunciation: / pɛɡ / (AmE), / pɛɡ / (BrE)
a device on a stringed instrument, such as a violin or guitar, that can be turned to adjust the pitch of the strings by tightening or loosening them

Examples

guitar peg tuner
Carefully adjusting each peg, he ensured the lute was perfectly tuned.
She tightened the violin's peg to bring the string up to pitch.
The guitar's peg slipped, causing it to go out of tune during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tuning peg: a peg specifically designed for tuning a stringed instrument
