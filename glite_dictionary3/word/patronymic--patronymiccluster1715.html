patronymic

adjective
derived from a father's or ancestor's name
Pronunciation: / ˌpæt.rəˈnɪm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpat.rəˈnɪm.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a name) derived from the name of a father or ancestor, especially by the addition of a prefix or suffix.

Examples

patronymic naming system
Icelandic last names are often patronymic, reflecting the father's first name.
In many cultures, a patronymic surname is inherited from the father.
The study focused on the use of patronymic naming systems in different regions.
