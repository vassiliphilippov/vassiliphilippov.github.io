Thule

proper noun
ancient name for a distant northern land
Pronunciation: / ˈθuːli / (AmE), / ˈθjuːli / (BrE)
an ancient name for the farthest north region of the known world, used by Greek and Roman writers to refer to a distant northern land, often identified with Scandinavia or Iceland.

Examples

ancient Thule land
Ancient writers described Thule as the northernmost edge of the known world.
Some believe that Thule refers to Iceland, while others suggest it was Norway or Greenland.
The location of Thule has been debated by historians for centuries.
