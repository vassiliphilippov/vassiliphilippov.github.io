to direct

verb
to aim an activity or product at a specific group
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt, daɪˈrɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt, daɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to address or target something (such as a letter, product, or event) to a specific person, group, or purpose

Examples

to direct a letter
The company decided to direct its new product toward college students.
They are directing the event specifically at families with children.
This advertising campaign is directed at young professionals interested in technology.
Before sending the invitation, make sure you direct it correctly.
Please direct the envelope clearly to avoid any delivery mistakes.
She carefully directed the package to her friend's new address.
