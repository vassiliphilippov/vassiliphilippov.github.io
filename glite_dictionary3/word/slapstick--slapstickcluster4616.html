slapstick

noun
theatrical noise-making device
Pronunciation: / ˈslæpˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈslæp.stɪk / (BrE)
A device made of two flat pieces of wood joined by a hinge, used in theatrical performances to produce a loud slapping noise, especially to simulate a blow without causing injury.

Examples

theater slapstick prop
A traditional slapstick can make a surprisingly loud noise when struck together.
Stagehands prepared the slapstick before the performance to ensure the sound effects were convincing.
The actor used a slapstick to create the sound of a punch during the comedy scene.
