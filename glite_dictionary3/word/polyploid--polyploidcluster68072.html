polyploid

noun
organism/cell with extra chromosome sets
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌplɔɪd / (BrE)
(genetics) an organism or cell having more than two complete sets of chromosomes.

Examples

a polyploid organism
Because of its polyploid nature, this species is unusually resistant to certain diseases.
Many cultivated plants, such as wheat, are naturally polyploid and possess genetic advantages as a result.
Scientists created a polyploid strain of fish to study the effects of extra chromosomes on growth rates.
