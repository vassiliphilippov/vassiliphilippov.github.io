to cohabit

verb
live together as an unmarried couple
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
(of two people) to live together and have a sexual relationship, especially without being married to each other.

Examples

to cohabit as partners
In some countries, couples who cohabit for several years gain legal rights similar to married couples.
It has become more common for partners to cohabit rather than marry immediately.
They decided to cohabit before making any plans to get married.

Related Terms

  • synonym – live together: to share a home with someone, usually as a couple
