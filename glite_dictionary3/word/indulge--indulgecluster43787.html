to indulge

verb
allow oneself or others to enjoy fully
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌldʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒ / (BrE)
to allow yourself to enjoy something you like, especially something that is a luxury or not necessary

Examples

to indulge in chocolate
He likes to indulge in a long, hot bath after a stressful day.
Parents sometimes indulge their children by buying them too many toys.
She tried not to indulge her cravings for chocolate every night.
After a long week, she likes to indulge in a decadent dessert.
He knew he shouldn't indulge his craving for chocolate, but he couldn't resist.
On vacation, we sometimes indulge ourselves by sleeping in late every morning.
Even on a strict diet, she sometimes indulges in dessert.
He decided to indulge his children with an extra hour of playtime.
She likes to indulge in a piece of chocolate after dinner.
