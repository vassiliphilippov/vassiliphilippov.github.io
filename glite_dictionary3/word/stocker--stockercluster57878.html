stocker

noun
young animal kept to be fattened or matured
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑkər / (AmE), / ˈstɒkə / (BrE)
a young domestic animal, especially a steer or heifer, kept until it is fattened or matured for further use such as breeding or slaughter

Examples

stocker cattle farm
After six months, the stocker calves had gained enough weight for market.
