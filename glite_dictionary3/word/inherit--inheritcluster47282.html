to inherit

verb
receive genetically from parents
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪt / (BrE)
to receive a quality, characteristic, or trait genetically from one's parents or ancestors

Examples

to inherit blue eyes
Genetically transmitted diseases can be inherited from either parent.
Many children inherit their eye color from their parents.
She inherited her father's sense of humor and her mother's artistic talent.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acquire: to come into possession of something, especially non-genetically
