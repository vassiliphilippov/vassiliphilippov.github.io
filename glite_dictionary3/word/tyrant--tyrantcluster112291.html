tyrant

noun
usurping ruler in ancient Greece
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt / (BrE)
(in ancient Greece) a ruler who seized power and governed without legal right to it, often ruling oppressively.

Examples

ancient Greek tyrant
Many tyrants in ancient Greece began as champions of the people but became oppressive.
The city was once ruled by a tyrant who took power by force.
The legacy of the Athenian tyrant Peisistratus is still debated by historians.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dictator: a ruler with total power, typically one who has obtained control by force
