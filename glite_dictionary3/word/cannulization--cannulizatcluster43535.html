cannulization

noun
insertion of a cannula or tube
Pronunciation: / ˌkæn.jə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.jʊ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of inserting a cannula or tube into a hollow body organ, typically to administer or remove fluids

Examples

cannulization of a vein
Complications may arise during cannulization, particularly in patients with small or fragile veins.
