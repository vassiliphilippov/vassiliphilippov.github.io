legitimate

adjective
recognized as lawful (child or marriage)
Pronunciation: / lɪˈdʒɪtəmət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
(of a child or marriage) recognized as lawful by law or custom, especially born or carried out within legal or official standards.

Examples

legitimate child
He was the king’s only legitimate son, born within wedlock.
In the past, only legitimate children could inherit their parents’ property.
Their marriage was finally declared legitimate by the church.
