tushery

noun
writing of poor quality with archaic words
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːʃəri / (AmE), / ˈtʊʃəri / (BrE)
(informal, often derogatory) Writing of poor quality, especially that which uses affected or excessive archaic language.

Examples

old-fashioned tushery
His penchant for medieval jargon only contributed to the tushery in his poetry.
Readers quickly tired of the tushery that filled many Victorian romance novels.
The novel was dismissed by critics as mere tushery, lacking genuine substance.
