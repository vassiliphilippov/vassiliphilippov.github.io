punchboard

noun
gambling board with holes for prize slips
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌntʃ.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃ.bɔːd / (BrE)
a small board perforated with holes containing slips of paper, used in gambling games where participants pay to punch out a slip for a chance to win a prize

Examples

play a punchboard game
He tried his luck at the punchboard, hoping to find the winning slip.
Old gambling halls often featured a punchboard as a popular game of chance.
The carnival set up a punchboard where players could win small prizes.
