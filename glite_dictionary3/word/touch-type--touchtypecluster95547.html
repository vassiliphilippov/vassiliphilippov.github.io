to touch-type

verb
type without looking at keys
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌtʃ taɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ tʌɪp / (BrE)
to type on a keyboard without looking at the keys, using muscle memory to locate the letters

Examples

to touch-type fast
Even with his eyes closed, he could easily touch-type long paragraphs.
Many employers expect applicants to touch-type at a certain speed.
She learned to touch-type in school, which made her much faster at taking notes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hunt-and-peck: type by searching for each key individually
