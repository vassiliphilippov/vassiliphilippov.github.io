to trifurcate

verb
divide into three parts
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪfərˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪfəˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to divide or split into three branches or parts

Examples

to trifurcate into branches
At the old oak tree, the path trifurcates into three separate trails through the forest.
During embryonic development, the main bronchus trifurcates to supply air to all regions of the lung.
The river trifurcates just before entering the delta region, creating a patchwork of smaller streams.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – bifurcate: to divide into two branches or parts
