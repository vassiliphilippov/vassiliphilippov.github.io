ionosphere

noun
upper atmospheric layer with high ion concentration
Pronunciation: / aɪˈɑːnəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnəsfɪə / (BrE)
The outer region of the Earth's atmosphere, beginning about 50 kilometers above the surface, that contains a high concentration of ions and free electrons and is able to reflect radio waves.

Examples

the ionosphere reflects radio
Radio signals can bounce off the ionosphere, enabling long-distance communication.
Scientists study the ionosphere to better understand space weather and its impact on Earth.
Solar storms can cause disturbances in the ionosphere, affecting GPS and radio reception.
