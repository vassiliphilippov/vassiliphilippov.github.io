drift

noun
mass of material heaped by wind or water
Pronunciation: / drɪft / (AmE), / drɪft / (BrE)
a large mass of material, such as sand or snow, that has been piled up by the wind or by water currents

Examples

drift of snow
A drift of sand blocked the path after the storm.
Heavy snow had formed a huge drift outside the front door.
The boat was caught against a drift of debris in the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sand dune: a hill of sand built by wind or water
  • synonym – snowdrift: a bank of snow heaped up by the wind
