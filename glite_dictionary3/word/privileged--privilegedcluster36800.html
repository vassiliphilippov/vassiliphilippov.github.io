privileged

adjective
not subject to usual rules or penalties
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪvəlɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of information, communication, or actions) not subject to usual rules or penalties, especially protected from disclosure or legal repercussions.

Examples

privileged information access
As a journalist, she could not be forced to reveal her privileged sources in court.
Lawyers are often careful to keep privileged communications with their clients confidential.
The judge ruled that the document was privileged and could not be admitted as evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confidential: meant to be kept private or secret
  • synonym – protected: kept safe from harm or unauthorized access
  • antonym – unprotected: not shielded from harm or disclosure
