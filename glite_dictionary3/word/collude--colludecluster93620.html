to collude

verb
secretly cooperate for deceitful or illegal purpose
Pronunciation: / kəˈlud / (AmE), / kəˈluːd / (BrE)
to secretly work together with others in order to deceive or cheat someone

Examples

to collude with rivals
It is illegal for companies to collude on pricing to limit competition.
The officials were found to collude in rigging the election results.
They were accused of colluding with criminal organizations to avoid taxes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – collaborate: work together, often used neutrally or positively
  • synonym – conspire: secretly plan together to do something harmful or illegal
  • synonym – plot: to secretly plan to do something illegal or harmful
