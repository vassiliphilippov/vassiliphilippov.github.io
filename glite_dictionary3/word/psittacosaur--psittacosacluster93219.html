psittacosaur

noun
primitive, hornless ceratopsian dinosaur
Pronunciation: / sɪˈtækəˌsɔr / (AmE), / sɪˈtakəsɔː / (BrE)
a small, primitive dinosaur of the genus Psittacosaurus, characterized by a parrot-like beak, long hind limbs, short forelimbs, and lacking horns but having the beginnings of a frill; thought to have been bipedal.

Examples

a psittacosaur fossil
A psittacosaur would have used its beak to clip plants close to the ground.
Recent discoveries revealed the possible coloration of a juvenile psittacosaur.
The museum displays a nearly complete psittacosaur skeleton from the Early Cretaceous period.
