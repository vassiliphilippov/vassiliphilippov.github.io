terrace

noun
a row of joined houses (esp. British)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
a row of houses built in a similar style and joined together along their side walls, or the street on which such houses face

Examples

terrace houses
Each terrace on this street has its own unique character.
The family moved into a beautiful terrace near the city center.
They painted the front door of their terrace bright blue to stand out.
