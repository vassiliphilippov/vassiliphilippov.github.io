trembling

noun
a shaky or quivering movement
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmblɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid, slight shaking movement, often caused by fear, excitement, weakness, or cold

Examples

uncontrollable trembling
He tried to hide his trembling, but his nervousness was clear.
The cold wind caused a trembling throughout his whole body.
There was a noticeable trembling in her hands as she spoke.

Related Terms

  • synonym – quivering: a slight, rapid shaking movement
  • synonym – shaking: the act of moving something or someone up and down or back and forth with quick motions
