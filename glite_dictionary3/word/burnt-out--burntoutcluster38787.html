burnt-out

adjective
exhausted from prolonged stress or overwork
Pronunciation: / bɝːnt aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnt aʊt / (BrE)
Extremely tired or exhausted, especially as a result of prolonged stress or overwork.

Examples

burnt-out worker
After months of working overtime, he felt completely burnt-out and needed a vacation.
Many teachers become burnt-out by the end of the school year.
She realized she was burnt-out and took some time off to recover.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exhausted: very tired; depleted of energy
  • antonym – refreshed: full of energy after rest
