Mendeleyev

proper noun
Russian chemist, creator of periodic table
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛn.dəˈleɪ.ɛf / (AmE), / ˌmɛndɪˈleɪɛf / (BrE)
(1834–1907) A Russian chemist who developed the periodic table of the chemical elements and predicted the existence of several new elements.

Examples

Mendeleyev developed table
Mendeleyev is best known for creating the first widely recognized periodic table of elements.
Many chemistry students learn about Mendeleyev's contributions in their studies.
The predictions Mendeleyev made about undiscovered elements were later confirmed by scientific discoveries.
