beneficiary

noun
person/entity benefiting from an action (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛnəˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃəri / (BrE)
a person or organization that receives money, help, or other benefits from something (such as a will, insurance, or an action); also used in linguistics for the receiver of an action

Examples

beneficiary of a will
Identifying the beneficiary helps clarify for whom an action is performed.
In the sentence 'She baked him a cake,' 'him' is the beneficiary of the action.
Linguists often analyze who acts as the beneficiary in different sentence structures.
Each policyholder must name at least one beneficiary when signing up for insurance.
The orphanage is the main beneficiary of the charity's annual fundraiser.
The primary beneficiary of the trust will receive monthly payments for life.
