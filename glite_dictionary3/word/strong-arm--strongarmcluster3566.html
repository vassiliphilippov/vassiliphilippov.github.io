to strong-arm

verb
use force or threats to coerce
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɔŋ ɑrm /; / ˈstrɔŋ ˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋ ɑːm /; / ˈstrɒŋ ˌɑːm / (BrE)
to use force or threats to make someone do something; to bully or coerce

Examples

to strong-arm a person

She refused to be strong-armed into signing the agreement.
The company tried to strong-arm its supplier into lowering prices.

The company was accused of strong-arming officials to win the contract.
The gang tried to strong-arm the shopkeeper into paying for protection.
Politicians sometimes strong-arm their opponents to gain an advantage.

The union claimed the company tried to strong-arm workers into signing the new contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bully: to intimidate or mistreat someone with force or threats
  • synonym – coerce: to make someone do something by force or threats
  • synonym – intimidate: to frighten or threaten someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something
  • antonym – persuade: to convince someone by argument rather than force
