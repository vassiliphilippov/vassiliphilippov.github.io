faculty

noun
inherent mental or physical power
Pronunciation: / ˈfækəlti / (AmE), / ˈfækəlti / (BrE)
an inherent mental or physical power, especially one of the mind's natural abilities such as reasoning, memory, or perception

Examples

mental faculty
As people age, some may worry about losing their mental faculties.
He maintained full control of his faculties until the end of his life.
Imagination is one faculty that distinguishes humans from other animals.

  • antonym – incapacity: lack of ability; inability
