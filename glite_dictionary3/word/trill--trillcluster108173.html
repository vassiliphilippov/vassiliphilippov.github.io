trill

noun
rapid alternation between two musical notes
Pronunciation: / trɪl / (AmE), / trɪl / (BrE)
a rapid alternation between a note and the note a semitone or tone above it, used as a musical ornament

Examples

trill in melody
Many Baroque pieces feature an elaborate trill in their melodies.
She struggled to master the trill required in the violin solo.
The pianist played a beautiful trill at the end of the movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tremolo: a trembling effect produced by rapid repetition of a single note or rapid alternation between two notes
