to occupy

verb
take control of a place by force
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊpaɪ / (BrE)
(of an army or force) to enter and take control of a place, especially by military means.

Examples

occupy a city
During the war, foreign troops occupied several territories for years.
The army plans to occupy the capital city by the end of the week.
The decision to occupy the region sparked widespread protests.

Related Terms

  • synonym – invade: to enter a place by force, especially for conquest
  • antonym – retreat: to withdraw troops from an area
