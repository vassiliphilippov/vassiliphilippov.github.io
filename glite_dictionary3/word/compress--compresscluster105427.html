compress

noun
cloth pad applied to body
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛs / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛs / (BrE)
A cloth pad or dressing, sometimes medicated, applied firmly to a part of the body to relieve discomfort, reduce fever, or stop bleeding.

Examples

cold compress
Doctors often recommend using a moist compress for minor injuries.
She placed a cold compress on his forehead to reduce the fever.
The nurse applied a warm compress to the swollen area to ease the pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dressing: a piece of material applied to a wound to promote healing
