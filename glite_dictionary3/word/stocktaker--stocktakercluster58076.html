stocktaker

noun
person who counts inventory
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑkˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to count and record the amount of goods or materials in stock, typically in a shop or warehouse

Examples

experienced stocktaker in a warehouse
As a stocktaker, she is responsible for counting all the items in the warehouse.
The company hired a stocktaker to perform the annual inventory check.
The store needed an experienced stocktaker to organise their inventory records.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inventory clerk: person who is responsible for maintaining records of goods in stock
