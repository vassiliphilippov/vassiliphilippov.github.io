demonstrative

adjective
serving to demonstrate or indicate
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɑnstrətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv / (BrE)
showing something clearly or expressing feelings openly

Examples

demonstrative behavior
Her demonstrative tone made it clear that she supported the proposal.
In grammar, a demonstrative pronoun is used to point to specific things.
The teacher used a demonstrative gesture to show the correct answer on the board.
He is very demonstrative and always hugs his friends when greeting them.
The children in her class are unusually demonstrative about their excitement.
Unlike his reserved brother, Mark is openly demonstrative with his feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expressive: showing one's emotions or feelings openly
  • synonym – indicative: suggesting or showing something
  • antonym – reserved: reluctant to show emotions or feelings openly
