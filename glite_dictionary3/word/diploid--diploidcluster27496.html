diploid

adjective
having two sets of chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent.

Examples

diploid organism
In plants, the diploid phase alternates with the haploid phase during the life cycle.
Most human cells are diploid, containing a total of 46 chromosomes.
The fertilized egg is the first diploid cell of an organism.

Related Terms

  • antonym – haploid: having a single set of chromosomes
