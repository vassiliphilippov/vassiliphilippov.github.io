kaoliang

noun
type of Chinese sorghum
Pronunciation: / kaʊˈliːˌɑŋ / (AmE), / kaʊˈliːˌɑŋ / (BrE)
A type of sorghum grown in China and Manchuria, with small white or brown grains used for food and dry stalks used for fodder, fuel, or thatching.

Examples

kaoliang grain harvest
A traditional Chinese spirit called baijiu is sometimes distilled from kaoliang grain.
Farmers in northern China rely on kaoliang for both food and livestock feed.
The dry stalks of kaoliang are often used for thatching roofs in rural villages.
