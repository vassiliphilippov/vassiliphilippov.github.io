Alexander the Great

proper noun
king of Macedon; ancient conqueror
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪgˈzændɚ ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændə ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(356–323 BC) King of Macedon; a military leader who conquered much of the ancient world, including Greece, Egypt, and Persia, and founded Alexandria.

Examples

Alexander the Great conquered Persia
After conquering Persia, Alexander the Great founded the city of Alexandria in Egypt.
Many historians regard Alexander the Great as one of the greatest military strategists in history.
The exploits of Alexander the Great have inspired countless legends and stories throughout history.
