internalisation

noun
incorporation of values or attitudes within oneself
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɝːnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making attitudes, values, or beliefs a part of one's own thinking or behavior

Examples

internalisation of values
His internalisation of company values made him a trusted leader among his peers.
The internalisation of social norms begins at an early age through family and education.
Therapy often focuses on the internalisation of positive self-beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adoption: the act of taking up or accepting something as one's own
  • synonym – assimilation: the process of taking in and fully understanding information or ideas
