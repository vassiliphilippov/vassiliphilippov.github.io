to surcharge

verb
print new denomination on stamp or note
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to print a new denomination or value on a stamp or banknote, typically by overprinting

Examples

to surcharge a stamp
Collectors highly value rare stamps that were surcharged after new denominations were introduced.
The government surcharged several banknotes when the currency was revalued.
The postal authority decided to surcharge the old stamps due to the change in postage rates.
