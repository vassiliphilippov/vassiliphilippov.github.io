to rescue

verb
take forcibly from legal custody
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛskju /; / ˈrɛs.kju / (AmE), / ˈrɛskjuː /; / ˈres.kjuː / (BrE)
to save someone or something from danger, harm, or an unpleasant situation (including taking someone from legal custody)

Examples

to rescue a trapped person
A group of armed men tried to rescue the prisoners while they were being transferred.
It was illegal to rescue someone who had been duly arrested by the authorities.
The rebels plotted to rescue their leader from police custody.
He risked his life to rescue the stranded hikers during the storm.
The firefighters managed to rescue the family from the burning building.
Volunteers worked tirelessly to rescue animals affected by the flood.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abandon: to leave someone or something in a dangerous or difficult situation
  • synonym – liberate: to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression
  • synonym – save: to keep someone or something safe from danger or harm
