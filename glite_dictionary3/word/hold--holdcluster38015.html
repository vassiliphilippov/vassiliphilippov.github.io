to hold

verb
consider or believe; have an opinion
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
to have, maintain, or express an opinion, belief, or quality

Examples

hold an opinion
Do you hold him responsible for what happened?
Many experts hold that exercise is essential for good health.
She held the belief that education could change society.
She was held by a verbal agreement to remain with the company for two years.
The contract holds all parties responsible for maintaining confidentiality.
The manager will hold you to your commitment to finish the project on time.
Her speech held a message of hope for everyone listening.
The museum holds an impressive collection of modern art.
This theory holds several important implications for future research.
