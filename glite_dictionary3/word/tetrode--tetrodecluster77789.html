tetrode

noun
vacuum tube with four electrodes
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəʊd / (BrE)
a vacuum tube with four electrodes, used especially in electronic circuits to amplify or switch signals

Examples

vacuum tetrode tube
A tetrode differs from a triode by having an additional screen grid between the plate and the control grid.
Engineers replaced the damaged tetrode in the amplifier circuit to restore proper function.
The old radio used a tetrode to boost the weak signals it received.
