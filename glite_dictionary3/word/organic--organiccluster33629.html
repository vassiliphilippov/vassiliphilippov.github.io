organic

adjective
of a disease, due to physical bodily causes
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈɡænɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or disorder) relating to or caused by physical changes in an organ or tissue of the body, rather than psychological causes.

Examples

organic disease
Distinguishing between organic and functional illnesses is important for proper medical treatment.
Some forms of dementia are considered organic diseases because they result from physical changes in brain tissue.
The doctor diagnosed her with an organic disorder, indicating a physical problem with the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – functional: of a disease, with no known physical cause; due to psychological or unknown factors
