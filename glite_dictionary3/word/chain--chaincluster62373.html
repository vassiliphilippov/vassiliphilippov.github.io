to chain

verb
to connect or link in a series
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn / (AmE), / tʃeɪn / (BrE)
to connect, fasten, or secure something with a chain

Examples

to chain a bike
He chained the bicycles to the fence so they would not be stolen.
The engineer chained the wagons for extra security during transportation.
The prisoners were chained together and led away.
Please chain the gates when you leave the park.
She carefully chained the suitcase to her chair at the station.
They had to chain the bikes outside the building to prevent theft.

Related Terms

  • synonym – link: to connect or join together
  • synonym – shackle: to restrain with chains or shackles
  • antonym – unchain: to release from a chain
  • antonym – unfasten: to release from being fastened, to detach
