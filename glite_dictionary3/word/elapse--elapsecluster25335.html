to elapse

verb
(of time) to pass or go by
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæps / (AmE), / ɪˈlæps / (BrE)
(of time) to pass or go by, especially in a regular or unnoticed way.

Examples

as hours elapse
As weeks elapsed, the investigation made little progress.
Five years may elapse before construction is completed.
Several hours elapsed before anyone noticed the problem.
