to remainder

verb
sell surplus books cheaply
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmeɪndər / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪndə / (BrE)
(of a publisher) to sell off unsold copies of a book at a reduced price, usually as surplus stock.

Examples

to remainder old books
Copies of the novel were remaindered following disappointing sales.
Many bookshops sell remaindered stock at heavily discounted prices.
The publisher decided to remainder the unsold titles after a year in stores.
