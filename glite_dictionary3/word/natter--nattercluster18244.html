to natter

verb
talk casually; chat
Pronunciation: / ˈnæt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈnætə / (BrE)
to talk casually about unimportant matters; to chat in a friendly, informal way

Examples

to natter with friends
She loves to natter with her colleagues during lunch breaks.
They were nattering away in the kitchen while the children played outside.
We met in the park and nattered for an hour about our weekend plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chat: to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • degree higher – gossip: to talk habitually about others, often involving secrets or rumors
