hachure

noun
shading with multiple crossing lines
Pronunciation: / hɑːˈʃʊr / (AmE), / haˈʃʊə / (BrE)
a method of shading using closely spaced parallel or crossed lines, especially in cartography or drawing to indicate slope or depth

Examples

hachure lines on maps
Mastering hachure is essential for effective topographical drawing.
Old maps often show slopes by means of hachure rather than contour lines.
The artist used hachure to create the illusion of shadow on the mountains.
