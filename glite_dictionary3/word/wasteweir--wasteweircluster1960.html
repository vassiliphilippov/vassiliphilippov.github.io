wasteweir

noun
channel for overflow water at dams
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪstˌwɪr / (AmE), / ˈweɪstˌwɪə / (BrE)
a channel or passage designed to carry excess water over or around a dam or other obstruction to prevent flooding or structural damage

Examples

wasteweir overflow water
Excess water is safely diverted through the wasteweir during heavy rainfall.
Many dams have a wasteweir as an essential safety feature.
The engineer checked the wasteweir to ensure it was not blocked by debris.
