wad

noun
small lump of chewable substance
Pronunciation: / wɑd / (AmE), / wɒd / (BrE)
a small lump or bundle of something chewable, such as tobacco or gum, formed for placing in the mouth

Examples

wad of gum
He spat the wad onto the ground after losing the taste.
He took a fresh wad of chewing tobacco from his pocket and put it in his mouth.
She always keeps a wad of gum in her bag to chew after meals.
