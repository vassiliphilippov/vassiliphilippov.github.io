platysma

noun
broad neck muscle involved in jaw movement
Pronunciation: / pləˈtɪzmə / (AmE), / pləˈtɪzmə / (BrE)
either of two broad, thin muscles located on each side of the neck, involved in moving the mouth and jaw

Examples

platysma muscle in neck
Damage to the platysma may affect the movement of the lower lip and jaw.
Surgeons must be careful not to injure the platysma during certain neck procedures.
The platysma muscle can be seen tensing when some people grimace or strain their neck.
