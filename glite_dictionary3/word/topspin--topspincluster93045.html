topspin

noun
forward spin on a ball in sports
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑpˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌspɪn / (BrE)
A forward spin given to a ball, especially in sports like tennis, causing it to dip and bounce forward after hitting the ground.

Examples

topspin on the ball
A well-placed shot with topspin is difficult for opponents to return.
She added topspin to her forehand to make the ball dip quickly over the net.
The coach taught him how to generate more topspin on his serves.

Related Terms

  • antonym – backspin: a backward spin imparted to a ball for a reverse effect
  • degree lower – side spin: spin applied to a ball causing it to curve sideways
