case

noun
legal dispute or proceeding in court
Pronunciation: / keɪs / (AmE), / keɪs / (BrE)
a legal matter, investigation, or problem that needs to be solved, especially in court or by the police

Examples

court case
She was involved in a complex case that lasted for months.
The judge concluded the case after hearing all the evidence from both sides.
The lawyer took on the case despite its many difficulties.
She was assigned to investigate a complicated fraud case.
The detective worked late into the night to crack the case.
The police closed the case after finding new evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lawsuit: a claim or dispute brought to a law court for adjudication
  • synonym – mystery: something that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain, often requiring investigation
  • antonym – solved: having found a solution, especially to a problem or mystery
  • synonym – trial: a formal examination of evidence by a court
