alternate

adjective
every second one; every other
Pronunciation: / ˈɔl.tɚ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.neɪt / (BrE)
happening or appearing every other time, or in a regular pattern with things taking turns

Examples

alternate days of work
Alternate pages of the book are printed in color.
The class meets on alternate Saturdays throughout the semester.
We take alternate routes to avoid the morning traffic.
During the meeting, speakers had alternate turns to share their ideas.
He felt alternate waves of hope and despair as he waited for the results.
The alternate rain and sunshine made for a strange spring day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alternating: happening in succession, one after the other
  • antonym – consecutive: following continuously in an unbroken sequence
