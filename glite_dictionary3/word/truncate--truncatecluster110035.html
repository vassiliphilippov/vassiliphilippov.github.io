to truncate

verb
replace a corner with a plane (geometry)
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌŋkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋkeɪt / (BrE)
(in geometry) To replace a corner of a shape, especially a solid, with a plane, forming a new face.

Examples

to truncate a corner
Mathematicians often truncate the vertices of a polyhedron to create a new geometric figure.
To achieve the desired shape, the sculptor decided to truncate the sharp corners of the block.
You can truncate each corner of the cube by slicing it with a plane perpendicular to the diagonal.
