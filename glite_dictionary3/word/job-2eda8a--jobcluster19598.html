to job

verb
arrange work to be done by a contractor
Pronunciation: / dʒɑb / (AmE), / dʒɒb / (BrE)
to arrange for work to be done by an outside contractor, rather than performing it oneself

Examples

to job out work
Many government projects are now jobbed to private contractors to save costs.
The company decided to job out the cleaning services to a local firm.
They regularly job maintenance work instead of hiring full-time staff.
