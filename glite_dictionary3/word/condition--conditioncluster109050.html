condition

noun
an assumption for validity or effect
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃ.ən /; / kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ən /; / kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a requirement, assumption, or clause that must be met for something to happen, especially in contracts or agreements

Examples

condition of the contract
His offer included a strict condition that no changes be made.
Payment is subject to the condition that the work is completed on time.
The contract will only be valid under this condition.
Before signing the agreement, she carefully reviewed all the conditions.
Most rental contracts include several conditions to protect both the tenant and the landlord.
The sale will only proceed if the conditions outlined are met by both parties.

  • synonym – proviso: a condition or stipulation in a contract or agreement
  • synonym – requirement: something essential or necessary
  • synonym – term: a word or phrase used to describe a requirement in an agreement
