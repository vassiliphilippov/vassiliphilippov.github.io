fin

noun
appendage for swimming in fish
Pronunciation: / fɪn / (AmE), / fɪn / (BrE)
a flattened appendage on the body of a fish or other aquatic animal, used for swimming and maintaining balance

Examples

fish fin
A dolphin's dorsal fin is often visible when it swims near the surface.
The shark used its powerful fin to steer swiftly through the water.
Tropical fish often have brightly colored fins that help them attract mates.
