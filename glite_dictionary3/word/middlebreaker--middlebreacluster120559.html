middlebreaker

noun
double moldboard plow for splitting soil
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əlˌbreɪ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əlˌbreɪ.kə / (BrE)
a type of moldboard plow fitted with two moldboards, designed to move soil to both sides of a central furrow, commonly used for opening rows in fields

Examples

middlebreaker plow field
Many cotton farmers rely on a middlebreaker during early planting to create wide, clean seed beds.
The farmer used a middlebreaker to open neat furrows across the field in spring.
With its double moldboards, a middlebreaker efficiently separates the soil to both sides of the new row.
