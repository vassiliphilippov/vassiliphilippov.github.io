to depress

verb
make someone feel sad or down
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛs / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛs / (BrE)
to make someone feel sad and without hope or enthusiasm

Examples

to depress a person
Hearing about the layoffs really depressed her.
It's easy to feel depressed during the long winter months.
The endless rain can depress even the most optimistic people.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cheer up: to make or become more cheerful or happy
  • synonym – discourage: to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
