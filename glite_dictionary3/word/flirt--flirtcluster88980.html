to flirt

verb
act carelessly or lightly with something
Pronunciation: / flɝːt / (AmE), / flɜːt / (BrE)
to behave carelessly or irresponsibly with something, especially with ideas, feelings, or danger, often without serious intention

Examples

to flirt with danger
He liked to flirt with danger by taking unnecessary risks.
She often flirted with the idea of quitting her job, but never did it.
The politician was accused of flirting with extremist views to gain attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym – toy: to act without seriousness or play with something lightly
  • synonym – trifle: to treat something as unimportant or act with little seriousness
