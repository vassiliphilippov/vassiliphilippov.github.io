posse

noun
temporary group acting as police
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːsi / (AmE), / ˈpɒsi / (BrE)
A group of people summoned by a sheriff to help in maintaining law and order, often temporarily; a group acting as an unofficial police force.

Examples

sheriff's posse rides
In old Western movies, a posse would ride out after outlaws.
The sheriff rounded up a posse to track down the fugitives.
The town quickly formed a posse when the bank was robbed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – militia: a military force raised from the population to supplement a regular army; often temporary or emergency service
