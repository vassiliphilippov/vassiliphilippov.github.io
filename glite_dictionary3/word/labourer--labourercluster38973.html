labourer

noun
manual worker; someone who does physical labour
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbərər / (AmE), / ˈleɪbərə / (BrE)
a person whose work involves hard physical effort, especially work that does not require special training or skill

Examples

factory labourer
He found work as a labourer in a local factory.
Many farms hire seasonal labourers during harvest time.
The construction labourer worked long hours in the hot sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym – laborer: an alternative spelling of 'labourer', mainly used in American English
