foam

noun
mass of small bubbles on liquid
Pronunciation: / foʊm / (AmE), / fəʊm / (BrE)
A mass of small bubbles formed in or on the surface of a liquid, often as a result of agitation or the presence of certain substances.

Examples

soap foam bubbles
He popped the soap bubbles in the foam while taking a bath.
The barista carefully spooned the foam onto the top of the cappuccino.
Waves crashing against the rocks produced a thick foam along the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – froth: a mass of small bubbles formed on a liquid
  • synonym – scum: a layer of froth or bubbles on liquid, often with impurities
