to wear

verb
become damaged through use or friction
Pronunciation: / wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə /; / weə / (BrE)
to become damaged, thin, or weaker as a result of continuous use or friction

Examples

to wear thin
After years of heavy use, the carpet began to wear in the middle.
Continuous exposure to wind and rain made the sign wear faster than expected.
The edges of the old book will wear if you handle them roughly.
After years of use, the engine began to wear and needed replacement.
If you don't oil the hinges, they will eventually wear and become noisy.
This carpet will wear quickly if too many people walk on it.

  • synonym – degrade: to break down or deteriorate in quality
  • synonym – erode: to gradually wear away (especially by natural forces)
  • synonym – wear out: to use something until it becomes unusable
