to excite

verb
raise to a higher energy level (physics/chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪt / (BrE)
(of an atom, molecule, or particle) to raise to a higher energy level, typically by absorbing energy.

Examples

to excite atoms
A laser is often used to excite the molecules in these experiments.
Heating the gas will excite the atoms to emit visible light.
Ultraviolet light can excite electrons in some materials.
