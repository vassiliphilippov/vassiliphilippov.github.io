to flounce

verb
move or walk dramatically
Pronunciation: / flaʊns / (AmE), / flaʊns / (BrE)
to move or walk in a dramatic, exaggerated, or angry way, often to attract attention

Examples

to flounce out angrily
After reading the email, she flounced away in protest.
He flounced into the room and threw his bag onto the chair.
She flounced out of the meeting, slamming the door behind her.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stomp: to walk with heavy, forceful steps, often angrily
