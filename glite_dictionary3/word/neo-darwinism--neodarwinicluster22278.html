neo-Darwinism

noun
modern theory of evolution involving genetics
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊˈdɑːr.wɪ.nɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈdɑː.wɪ.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A modern evolutionary theory that explains the origin of new species through genetic mutations and natural selection, building on Darwin's original ideas with genetics.

Examples

modern neo-Darwinism theory
Advances in genetics have strongly reinforced the principles of neo-Darwinism.
