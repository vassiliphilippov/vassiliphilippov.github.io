pruning

noun
cut-off part of a plant
Pronunciation: / ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
a branch or stem that has been cut off a plant, usually to encourage healthy growth

Examples

pile of prunings
After the gardener finished, he collected the prunings into a large pile for composting.
The prunings from the rose bushes can sometimes be used to propagate new plants.
We burned the prunings to keep the orchard tidy at the end of the season.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trimming: the act of cutting away unwanted parts, especially from plants
