mantis

noun
predatory insect with upright forelegs
Pronunciation: / ˈmæntɪs / (AmE), / ˈmæntɪs / (BrE)
A predatory insect with a long body and large eyes, known for holding its forelegs upright as if in prayer.

Examples

praying mantis
Gardeners appreciate the mantis for its ability to control insect pests naturally.
She was fascinated by the delicate manner in which the mantis cleaned its forelegs.
The mantis sat motionless on the leaf, waiting to catch its prey.
