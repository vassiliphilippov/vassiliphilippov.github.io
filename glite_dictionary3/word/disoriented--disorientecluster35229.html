disoriented

adjective
confused about place, time, or identity
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔɹiˌɛntɪd /; / dɪsˈɔriˌɛntɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔː.ri.ˌɛn.tɪd /; / dɪsˈɔːrɪəntɪd / (BrE)
unable to think clearly or not knowing where you are or what is happening, often feeling confused or lost

Examples

feel disoriented after spinning
After spinning around in circles, I felt completely disoriented and had to sit down.
The loud noise and flashing lights caused him to become disoriented during the emergency.
Traveling across time zones can leave you disoriented for a few days.
After moving to a new country, she often felt disoriented and unsure of her place among her peers.
In large cities, newcomers can become disoriented by unfamiliar customs and social expectations.
The rapid changes in technology have left some older adults feeling socially disoriented.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alienated: feeling isolated or estranged from a group or society
  • synonym – confused: unable to think clearly or to understand what is happening or what somebody is saying
  • antonym – oriented: having a clear understanding of where you are, who you are, or what is happening
