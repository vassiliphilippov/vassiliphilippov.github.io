Cherokee

noun
a member of an Indigenous people of the US
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛrəˌki / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrəki / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people originally from the southeastern United States, especially the Appalachian Mountains, now chiefly living in Oklahoma

Examples

a Cherokee leader
Many Cherokee attended the annual powwow to celebrate their heritage.
She is proud to be a descendant of the Cherokee.
The Cherokee were forcibly relocated to Oklahoma during the Trail of Tears.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Iroquoian: a group of Indigenous peoples and languages related to the Cherokee
