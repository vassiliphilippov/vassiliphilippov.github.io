subjectivist

noun
person believing in subjectivism
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʒɛktɪvɪst / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛktɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that knowledge and values are dependent on or influenced by personal experiences or perceptions rather than objective reality

Examples

a subjectivist philosopher
As a subjectivist, she often emphasized the importance of personal experience when interpreting art.
Many critics of the subjectivist perspective claim it leads to moral relativism.
The philosopher was a well-known subjectivist who argued that truth depends on individual perception.

Related Terms

  • antonym – objectivist: a person who believes that knowledge and values are based on objective reality
