acervulus

noun
small asexual fruiting body of fungi
Pronunciation: / əˈsɝːvjələs / (AmE), / əˈsɜːvjʊləs / (BrE)
A small, cushion-like asexual fruiting body formed by certain fungi, usually on plant hosts, composed of a dense mat of hyphae.

Examples

acervulus on plant leaf
Researchers observed multiple acervuli scattered across the host's epidermis.
The fungus forms an acervulus on the surface of the infected leaf.
Under the microscope, you can see an acervulus developing from the plant tissue.
