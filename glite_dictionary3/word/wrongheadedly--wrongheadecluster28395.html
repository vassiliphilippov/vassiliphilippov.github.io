wrongheadedly

adverb
in a stubbornly misguided way
Pronunciation: / ˌrɔŋˈhɛdɪdli / (AmE), / ˌrɒŋˈhɛdɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows stubborn adherence to wrong or misguided opinions or actions.

Examples

wrongheadedly refuse advice
He wrongheadedly insisted on his plan despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary.
She wrongheadedly believed that ignoring the problem would make it disappear.
The policy was wrongheadedly defended by its supporters in parliament.
