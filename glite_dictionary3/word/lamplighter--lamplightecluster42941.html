lamplighter

noun
person who lights street lamps (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæmpˌlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlamplʌɪtə / (BrE)
(historical) A person employed to light and extinguish streetlights, especially when gas lamps were used.

Examples

old lamplighter on street
Before electric lights, the lamplighter was a familiar and important figure in towns.
Children watched in fascination as the lamplighter made his rounds each evening.
In the old city, the lamplighter would appear at dusk to light every street lamp.
