to challenge

verb
raise a formal objection in court
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
to question, dispute, or formally object to something, especially in law or authority

Examples

to challenge a decision
She intends to challenge the witness during the cross-examination on the grounds of bias.
The defense attorney decided to challenge the admissibility of the evidence presented by the prosecution.
The lawyer formally challenged the composition of the jury, claiming it was not representative.
He bravely challenged the official explanation, seeking more transparency.
Some scientists challenge the results of the study, arguing that the methodology was flawed.
The lawyer decided to challenge the witness's testimony in court.
