to deign

verb
do something beneath one's dignity
Pronunciation: / deɪn / (AmE), / deɪn / (BrE)
to do something that one considers to be beneath one's dignity, often reluctantly or with condescension

Examples

to deign to help
He wouldn't deign to eat at such an ordinary restaurant.
She barely deigned to glance at the documents handed to her.
The queen did not deign to reply to the commoner's question.

Related Terms

  • synonym – condescend: to show feelings of superiority; to act in a patronizing way
