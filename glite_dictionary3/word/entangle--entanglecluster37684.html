to entangle

verb
twist together into a confusing mass
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaŋɡl / (BrE)
to twist together or entwine so that it becomes difficult to separate or straighten out; to make into a confusing mass

Examples

to entangle wires
Be careful not to entangle the fishing lines.
Her hair became entangled with seaweed while she was swimming.
The kitten managed to entangle itself in the ball of yarn.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tangle: to become or make twisted together in a messy mass
  • antonym – untangle: to free from a tangle or confusing mass
