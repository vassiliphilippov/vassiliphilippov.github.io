racketeer

noun
person involved in organized crime
Pronunciation: / ˌræk.əˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌræk.ɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who engages in criminal activity, especially one who obtains money by fraud, extortion, or other illegal means

Examples

notorious racketeer
He started out as a small-time racketeer before joining a powerful crime syndicate.
Jurors heard testimony about how the racketeer laundered money through fake businesses.
The police arrested a notorious racketeer accused of running an extortion ring.
