paleolith

noun
stone tool from Paleolithic period
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪliəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈpalɪəˌlɪθ / (BrE)
A stone tool made and used during the Paleolithic period.

Examples

ancient paleolith tool
A single paleolith can reveal much about the daily lives of early humans.
Archaeologists discovered a paleolith buried deep within the ancient cave.
Many museums display paleoliths as key exhibits from the prehistoric era.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – microlith: a small stone tool, typically from the Mesolithic period
