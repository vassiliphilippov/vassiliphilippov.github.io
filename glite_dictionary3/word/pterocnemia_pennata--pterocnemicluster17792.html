Pterocnemia pennata

noun
a South American flightless bird (Darwin's rhea)
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛroʊkˈnɛmiə pɛˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɒkˈniːmɪə pɛˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A species of large, three-toed, flightless bird native to South America, also known as Darwin's rhea.

Examples

The Pterocnemia pennata is found in grasslands from Peru to the Strait of Magellan.
Unlike the common rhea, the Pterocnemia pennata is slightly smaller and adapted to colder climates.
