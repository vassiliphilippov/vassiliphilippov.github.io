papistic

adjective
relating to the Roman Catholic Church (archaic, critical)
Pronunciation: / pəˈpɪstɪk / (AmE), / pəˈpɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the Pope or the Roman Catholic Church, especially in a critical or disparaging sense; of or supporting Roman Catholicism.

Examples

papistic beliefs
His opponents accused him of supporting papistic traditions in the royal court.
Such papistic practices were viewed with suspicion in seventeenth-century England.
The pamphlet was filled with papistic rhetoric against the Protestant movement.
