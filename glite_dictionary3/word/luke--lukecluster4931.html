Luke

proper noun
New Testament apostle; author of the third Gospel
Pronunciation: / luːk / (AmE), / luːk / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the apostle closely associated with St. Paul, traditionally regarded as the author of the third Gospel.

Examples

Luke the apostle
Many scholars believe that Luke was a physician before he became an evangelist.
The Gospel according to Luke is the third book of the New Testament.
Tradition holds that Luke accompanied St. Paul on several missionary journeys.
