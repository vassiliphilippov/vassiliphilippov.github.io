Edo

proper noun
historic name for Tokyo, Japan
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːdəʊ / (BrE)
the former name of Tokyo, which served as the capital and largest city of Japan, and was the country's economic and cultural center during the Tokugawa period (1603–1868)

Examples

Edo period
During the Tokugawa period, Edo grew into one of the largest cities in the world.
Many historic neighborhoods in modern Tokyo were originally part of Edo.
The shogunate established its government in Edo in the early 17th century.
