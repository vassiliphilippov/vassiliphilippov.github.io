waddle

noun
a clumsy, swaying walk
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑd.əl / (AmE), / ˈwɒd.əl / (BrE)
a way of walking with short steps and a clumsy swaying motion from side to side, like that of a duck

Examples

a slow waddle walk
He crossed the yard with a slow waddle, resembling a duck on land.
Her playful waddle down the hallway made the children giggle.
The penguin's distinctive waddle charmed all the visitors at the zoo.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stagger: to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
