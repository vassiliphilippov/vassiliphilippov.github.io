dash

noun
punctuation mark (– or —) for separation or joining words
Pronunciation: / dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
A punctuation mark (– or —) used to separate parts of a sentence, indicate a pause, or join words, especially in compound words or when dividing words at the end of a line.

Examples

use a dash in writing
He added a dash between the two parts of the word at the end of the line.
Please use a dash instead of a hyphen in this sentence.
The dash can signal an abrupt break in thought or structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hyphen: punctuation mark (‐) used for joining words or splitting at line breaks
