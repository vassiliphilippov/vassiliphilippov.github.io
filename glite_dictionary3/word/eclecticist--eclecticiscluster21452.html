eclecticist

noun
person who chooses from diverse sources
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklɛktɪsɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪsɪst / (BrE)
a person who selects ideas, styles, or methods from a variety of sources rather than adhering to a single system or tradition

Examples

famous eclecticist thinker
As an eclecticist, she incorporates theories from both classical and modern traditions in her research.
Many architects consider themselves eclecticists, blending historic and contemporary elements in their designs.
The panel included an eclecticist who advocated drawing from multiple philosophical viewpoints.
