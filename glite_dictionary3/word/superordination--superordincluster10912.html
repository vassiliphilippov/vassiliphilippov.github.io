superordination

noun
relation of being higher in rank or class
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˌɔːrdɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The state or relation of being superordinate; specifically, the semantic or hierarchical relation of belonging to a higher rank, class, or category.

Examples

superordination in a hierarchy
A clear understanding of superordination is vital in analyzing taxonomic classifications.
In biology, the superordination of mammals over reptiles is shown by their evolutionary traits.
The concept of superordination is essential in both linguistics and organizational hierarchy.
