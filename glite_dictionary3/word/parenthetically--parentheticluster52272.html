parenthetically

adverb
in a parenthetical or aside manner
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrənˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌpærənˈθetɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is expressed as a side remark or additional information, often within parentheses or as an aside.

Examples

parenthetically mention a fact
He parenthetically explained the historical context before moving on to the main topic.
She mentioned parenthetically that the report would be finished by Friday.
The author parenthetically notes that more research is needed in this area.

Related Terms

  • synonym – incidentally: used to add a remark, often as an aside
