dipolar

adjective
having two opposite electric or magnetic poles
Pronunciation: / daɪˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / daɪˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
(of a molecule or system) having two equal and opposite electric charges or magnetic poles separated by a small distance.

Examples

dipolar molecule structure
Some molecules exhibit dipolar characteristics, making them effective at dissolving ionic substances.
The dipolar nature of the magnet creates a strong magnetic field between the poles.
Water is a dipolar molecule, which accounts for many of its unique properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monopolar: having a single electric or magnetic pole
  • synonym – polar: having a positive and a negative pole
