echoencephalography

noun
ultrasound imaging of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkoʊɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌɛkəʊɛnˌsɛfəˈlɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A noninvasive medical procedure that uses ultrasound waves to examine the structure and anatomy of the brain.

Examples

echoencephalography brain scan
Before MRI became widespread, echoencephalography was commonly used to assess brain injuries.
Recent advances in echoencephalography have improved the detection of brain abnormalities in infants.
The neurologist ordered an echoencephalography to help diagnose the patient's symptoms.
