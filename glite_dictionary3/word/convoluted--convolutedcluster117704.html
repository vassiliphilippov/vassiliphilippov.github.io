convoluted

adjective
rolled or coiled upon itself (botanical)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnvəˌlutɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvəˌluːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf, petal, or similar structure) rolled longitudinally upon itself, especially in the bud.

Examples

convoluted leaf structure
Some grasses produce convoluted leaves that unroll as they mature.

The flower has a convoluted petal structure when in bud form.
