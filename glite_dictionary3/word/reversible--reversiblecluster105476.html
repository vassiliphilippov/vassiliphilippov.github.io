reversible

adjective
able to be changed back
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrsəbəl /; / rɪˈvɜːrsəbəl /; / rɪˈvɜrsəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsəbəl /; / rɪˈvɜːsəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be changed back to a previous state or condition; capable of being reversed

Examples

reversible medical change
Fortunately, the surgery is reversible if complications arise.
Her decision was reversible, so she changed her mind later on.
The company took a reversible action, allowing them to return to their original plan.
Some medical conditions are reversible with appropriate treatment.
The damage caused by the medication is reversible if caught early.
This jacket is reversible, allowing you to wear it two different ways.

The process of melting and freezing water is reversible under standard conditions.
This medication causes side effects, but most of them are reversible after you stop taking it.
