brumous

adjective
foggy; misty; filled with mist
Pronunciation: / ˈbruːməs / (AmE), / ˈbruːməs / (BrE)
(literary) filled with, resembling, or abounding in fog or mist; foggy or misty.

Examples

a brumous morning
A brumous dawn shrouded the city in a thick, grey haze.
The brumous weather made it difficult to see across the field.
Writers often use brumous scenes to evoke a sense of mystery or melancholy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foggy: full of or characterized by fog
  • synonym – misty: characterized by or full of mist
