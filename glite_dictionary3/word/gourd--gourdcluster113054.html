gourd

noun
a vine bearing hard-rinded fruits
Pronunciation: / ɡɔrd / (AmE), / ɡɔːd / (BrE)
A climbing or trailing plant of the family Cucurbitaceae, typically producing fruits with hard rinds, some of which are edible or used for ornamental or practical purposes.

Examples

gourd plant climbing
Different species of gourd can be grown for decorative, culinary, or practical uses.
He carved a water container from a dried gourd found in the fields.
The gourd vines quickly spread across the garden fence by midsummer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pumpkin: a large rounded orange-yellow fruit with a thick rind, edible flesh, and many seeds
  • synonym – squash: an edible fruit also from the family Cucurbitaceae
