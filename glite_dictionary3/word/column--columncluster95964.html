column

noun
a line of units one after another
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑləm /; / ˈkɑːləm / (AmE), / ˈkɒləm / (BrE)
a line or group of people, vehicles, or things arranged one behind another or in a long, vertical shape

Examples

column of cars
A long column of cars followed the leader through the city.
The soldiers marched in a column down the main street.
They stood in a single column waiting to buy tickets at the booth.
A column of ants marched towards the crumbs left on the floor.
A thick column of black smoke rose above the burning factory.
The chemist carefully poured the liquid into a narrow column of powder.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pillar: an upright structure of stone, brick, etc., used as a support
  • antonym – row: a line of things or people side by side
  • synonym – tower: a tall, narrow structure
