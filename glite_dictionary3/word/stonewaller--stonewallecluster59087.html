stonewaller

noun
person who obstructs or delays progress
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnwɔlər / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnwɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately refuses to cooperate, answer questions, or who obstructs progress by delaying tactics such as lengthy speeches

Examples

a skilled stonewaller
In political negotiations, a skilled stonewaller can frustrate even the most persistent opponents.
Rather than address the issue, she became a stonewaller, deflecting every question with lengthy statements.
The committee accused the witness of being a stonewaller who refused to give straight answers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cooperator: someone who willingly works with others or complies
  • synonym – obstructionist: someone who deliberately delays or prevents progress
