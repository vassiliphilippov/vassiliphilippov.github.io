gens

noun
Roman clan; family descended from a common male ancestor
Pronunciation: / dʒɛnz / (AmE), / dʒɛnz / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) a family or clan consisting of all those who shared the same name and claimed descent from a common male ancestor.

Examples

Roman gens name
Each Roman citizen belonged to a particular gens, identified by their family name.
Several members of the gens were involved in politics during the late Roman Republic.
The patrician gens claimed legendary origins tracing back to heroes and gods.
