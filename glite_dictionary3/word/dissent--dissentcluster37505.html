dissent

noun
judicial disagreement with majority opinion
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
(Law) A judge's opinion that disagrees with the majority decision of the court.

Examples

a judge's dissent
Her dissent became a key reference in later court decisions.
The Supreme Court's ruling included one notable dissent from Justice Lee.
The judge issued a strong dissent challenging the reasoning of the majority opinion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concurrence: A statement by a judge agreeing with the majority but for different reasons
  • antonym – majority opinion: The official decision agreed to by more than half the judges
