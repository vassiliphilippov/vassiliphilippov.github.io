Herod the Great

proper noun
king of Judea; biblical figure
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrəd ðə ˈɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəd ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
(74–4 BC) King of Judea known for his extensive building projects and, according to the New Testament, his order to kill all male infants in Bethlehem in an attempt to eliminate the infant Jesus.

Examples

Herod the Great ruled Judea
Historians debate the extent of Herod the Great's cruelty and his political achievements.
Many ancient buildings in Judea were constructed under the reign of Herod the Great.
The story of Herod the Great is central to the early chapters of the Gospel of Matthew.
