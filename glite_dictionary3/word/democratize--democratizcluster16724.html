to democratize

verb
make more democratic by reform
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to make a country, organization, or system more democratic by allowing everyone to participate equally

Examples

to democratize a country
Many activists are pushing to democratize education and make it accessible to all.
Several nations in the region have begun to democratize after years of authoritarian rule.
The new government vowed to democratize the country's political system.
Efforts to democratize society met resistance from traditional power holders.
Many countries sought to democratize after the fall of the dictatorship.
Reforms were introduced to democratize the electoral process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – authoritarianize: to make more authoritarian in nature or rule
  • antonym – autocratize: to make more autocratic or less democratic
  • synonym – liberalize: to make laws or rules less strict; to make freer or more open
