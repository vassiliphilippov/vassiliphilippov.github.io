Latinate

adjective
derived from or imitative of Latin
Pronunciation: / ˈlætɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈlatɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of words, phrases, or style) derived from or imitative of Latin, especially in vocabulary or form.

Examples

Latinate vocabulary
Many English scientific terms are Latinate in origin, having been adapted from Latin.
The author's Latinate style made the prose sound formal and scholarly.
The vocabulary of law is often heavily Latinate, reflecting its historical roots.
