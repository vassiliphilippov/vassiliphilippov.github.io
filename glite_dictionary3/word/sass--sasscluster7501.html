to sass

verb
answer back rudely or cheekily
Pronunciation: / sæs / (AmE), / sæs / (BrE)
to answer or speak to someone in a rude or cheeky manner, especially to someone in authority

Examples

to sass your parents
Don't sass your mother when she's giving you instructions.
If you sass the coach again, you'll have to leave practice early.
The employee was reprimanded for sassing his supervisor during the meeting.
