psilophyte

noun
extinct primitive vascular plant
Pronunciation: / sɪˈlɑfˌaɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒf.aɪt / (BrE)
an extinct group of primitive vascular plants of the order Psilophytales, important in the early evolution of land plants

Examples

fossil psilophyte
Fossils of psilophytes provide valuable information about the evolution of early vascular plants.
The psilophyte Rhynia is often studied in paleobotany courses.
Unlike modern ferns, psilophytes lacked true leaves and roots.
