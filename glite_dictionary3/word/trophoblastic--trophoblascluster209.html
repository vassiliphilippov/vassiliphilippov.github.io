trophoblastic

adjective
relating to the trophoblast (embryonic cell layer)
Pronunciation: / ˌtroʊfəˈblæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrɒfəˈblæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the trophoblast, the outer layer of cells in the early embryo that contributes to placenta formation.

Examples

trophoblastic cells in embryo
Choriocarcinoma is a type of trophoblastic cancer that can develop during pregnancy.
Some pregnancy tests detect trophoblastic cells to confirm the early stages of gestation.
The researchers observed abnormal trophoblastic proliferation in the tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trophoblast: the outer cell layer of the early embryo that forms the placenta
