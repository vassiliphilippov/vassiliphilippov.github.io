crystallinity

noun
degree of structural order in a solid
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪstəˈlɪnəti / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəˈlɪnɪti / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The degree to which a solid material has a well-ordered, crystalline structure.

Examples

high crystallinity material
Reduced crystallinity in the material led to increased flexibility and transparency.
Scientists measured the crystallinity of the new mineral sample to determine its purity.
The crystallinity of the polymer affects its mechanical and thermal properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym – amorphous: lacking a definite crystalline structure
  • synonym – crystalline: having the structure and form of a crystal
