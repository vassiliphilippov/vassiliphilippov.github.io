narrow

adjective
thorough and detailed (examination, scrutiny, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
(of attention, scrutiny, etc.) taking great care and being attentive to small details; thorough and meticulous.

Examples

narrow examination
A narrow examination of the evidence proved crucial in solving the case.
Her narrow attention to detail revealed errors others had missed.
The committee conducted a narrow scrutiny of each application before making their decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – careful: taking care in one's actions or decisions
  • antonym – casual: not thorough or detailed; done without much attention
  • synonym – meticulous: showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
