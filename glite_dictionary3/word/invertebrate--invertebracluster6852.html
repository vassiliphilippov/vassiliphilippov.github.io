invertebrate

noun
animal lacking a backbone
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɜrtəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
an animal that does not have a backbone or spinal column, such as an insect, worm, or mollusk; not used as a precise scientific classification

Examples

an invertebrate like worm
Earthworms are invertebrates that play a crucial role in soil health.
Jellyfish are one of the most well-known groups of invertebrates in the ocean.
Unlike mammals, invertebrates do not have a backbone.

  • antonym – vertebrate: an animal with a backbone or spinal column
