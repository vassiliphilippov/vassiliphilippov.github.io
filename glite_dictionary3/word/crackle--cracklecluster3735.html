crackle

noun
fine cracks in glaze of ceramics
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkræk.əl / (BrE)
a network of fine cracks in the glaze of china, pottery, or porcelain, often occurring naturally or as a decorative effect

Examples

crackle in pottery glaze
Experts examined the crackle to determine the age of the ceramic plate.
Some collectors prize the crackle found on certain antique porcelains.
The vase had a beautiful crackle that added character to its surface.
