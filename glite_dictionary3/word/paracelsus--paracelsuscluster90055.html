Paracelsus

proper noun
Swiss physician and alchemist (1493–1541)
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈsɛlsəs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈsɛlsəs / (BrE)
(c. 1493–1541) Swiss physician and alchemist noted for introducing chemical remedies and advocating the treatment of diseases based on external causes, rather than the imbalance of humors.

Examples

Paracelsus changed medicine
Paracelsus revolutionized medicine by promoting the use of chemical substances to treat diseases.
Many historians consider Paracelsus to be the father of toxicology because of his pioneering research.
The theories of Paracelsus challenged the traditional Galenic view of medicine in Renaissance Europe.
