torrential

adjective
relating to a torrent or its action
Pronunciation: / tɔˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / tɒˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by the rapid, forceful flow characteristic of a torrent; especially used in scientific contexts to describe processes or features shaped by rushing water.

Examples

torrential river flow
Engineers studied the area’s torrential landscape before planning the new dam.
Some plant species have developed torrential adaptations to survive in rapidly flowing streams.
The steep valleys showed signs of torrential erosion after the flash flood.

Related Terms

  • synonym – torrent: a strong and fast-moving stream of water or other liquid
