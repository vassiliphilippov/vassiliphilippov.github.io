flaskful

noun
the amount a flask holds
Pronunciation: / ˈflæskˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈflɑːskˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of liquid that fills a flask; the quantity a flask will hold.

Examples

a flaskful of tea
He poured himself a flaskful of hot tea before heading out into the cold.
Only one flaskful of the sample remained after the experiment.
She measured a flaskful of water for the recipe.
