to clinch

verb
settle decisively; finalize
Pronunciation: / klɪntʃ / (AmE), / klɪntʃ / (BrE)
to succeed in achieving or concluding something finally or decisively, especially after uncertainty or negotiation

Examples

to clinch a deal
After weeks of negotiations, the company managed to clinch the contract.
Her incredible performance helped the team clinch victory in the final minutes.
We need one more signature to clinch the deal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – finalize: to make final or complete, especially in discussion or negotiation
  • antonym – lose: to fail to achieve or secure something one aimed for
  • synonym – secure: to make certain or ensure the obtaining of something
