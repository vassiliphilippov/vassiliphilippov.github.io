mutatis mutandis

adverb
with necessary changes made
Pronunciation: / mjuˈteɪtɪs mjuˈtændɪs / (AmE), / mjuːˈtɑːtɪs mjuːˈtandɪs / (BrE)
with the necessary changes having been made; allowing for necessary alterations.

Examples

mutatis mutandis to a new case
Any reference to the previous agreement applies mutatis mutandis to this contract.
Laws governing public institutions apply, mutatis mutandis, to private organizations as well.
The procedures for staff appointments are, mutatis mutandis, applicable to all departments.
