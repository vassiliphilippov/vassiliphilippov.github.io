fibre

noun
moral strength and character
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
moral strength and integrity in a person's character; the inner qualities that guide a person's ethical actions and decisions.

Examples

moral fibre

She showed remarkable moral fibre when faced with a difficult decision.
The job requires someone with strong fibre and unwavering principles.
