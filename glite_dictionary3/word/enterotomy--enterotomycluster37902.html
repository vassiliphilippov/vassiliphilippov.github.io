enterotomy

noun
surgical incision into the intestine
Pronunciation: / ˌɛntəˈrɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the intestine, often to provide access or create a permanent opening through the abdominal wall

Examples

perform an enterotomy procedure
An enterotomy is sometimes required for diagnostic exploration during abdominal surgery.
Complications can arise after a complicated enterotomy procedure.
The surgeon performed an enterotomy to remove the intestinal obstruction.
