obscene

adjective
sexually suggestive or indecent
Pronunciation: / əbˈsin / (AmE), / əbˈsiːn / (BrE)
offensive or indecent, especially in a sexual way or against accepted standards of morality

Examples

obscene phone calls
She received a series of obscene phone calls that made her feel uncomfortable.
Some people found his jokes obscene, while others thought they were just harmless fun.
The magazine was banned for publishing obscene images considered offensive by authorities.
Her artwork was labeled as obscene due to its explicit imagery.
Some critics argued that the performance was obscene and not suitable for broadcast.
The committee found the film's content to be obscene and banned it from theaters.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decent: conforming to accepted standards of morality or respectability
  • synonym – indecent: not conforming with accepted standards of morality, especially sexual morality
  • synonym – lewd: crude and offensive in a sexual way
  • synonym – salacious: having or conveying undue or inappropriate interest in sexual matters
