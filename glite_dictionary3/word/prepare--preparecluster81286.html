to prepare

verb
in music, to introduce a dissonant note as a consonance
Pronunciation: / prɪˈpɛr / (AmE), / prɪˈpɛə / (BrE)
(in music) to lead up to and soften a dissonant note by first presenting it as a consonant note in the preceding chord.

Examples

to prepare a chord
In measure 10, the composer chose to prepare the seventh before introducing the dissonance.
It is important to prepare the dissonance to achieve a pleasing musical effect.
The harmony expertly prepares each discordant note, creating a smooth transition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – resolve: to follow a dissonant note or chord with a consonant one, completing the musical progression
