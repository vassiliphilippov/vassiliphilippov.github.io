phlegmatic

adjective
having an unemotional temperament
Pronunciation: / flɛɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / flɛɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of a person) having or showing a calm, unemotional temperament.

Examples

phlegmatic attitude
Despite the chaos around him, John stayed phlegmatic throughout the ordeal.
He remained phlegmatic even in the face of bad news.
Her phlegmatic response surprised everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – emotional: showing strong emotions
  • synonym – stoic: enduring pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaining
