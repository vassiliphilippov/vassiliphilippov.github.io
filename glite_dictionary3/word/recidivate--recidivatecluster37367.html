to recidivate

verb
to return to bad or criminal behavior
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɪdəˌveɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪdɪveɪt / (BrE)
to relapse into previous bad or criminal behavior, especially after having been treated or punished

Examples

to recidivate after prison
After his release from prison, he struggled not to recidivate despite many temptations.
Efforts to prevent criminals from recidivating include education and job programs.
Statistics show that a significant percentage of offenders recidivate within three years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reform: to abandon bad or criminal ways and improve morally
  • synonym – relapse: to fall back into a former state or practice, especially a bad one
