synecdochic

adjective
relating to synecdoche (figure of speech)
Pronunciation: / sɪˌnɛkˈdɑkɪk / (AmE), / sɪˌnɛkˈdɒkɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving synecdoche, a figure of speech in which a part is used for the whole or the whole for a part.

Examples

synecdochic expression
Her synecdochic use of 'the crown' to mean 'the monarchy' adds a layer of symbolism.
In this passage, a synecdochic expression is used, substituting 'wheels' for 'car'.
The poet's synecdochic reference to 'all hands on deck' effectively conveyed urgency.
