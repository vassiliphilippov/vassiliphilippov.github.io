to expropriate

verb
to take property away, often by authority
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsproʊprieɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprəʊprieɪt / (BrE)
(of a government or authority) to take away property from its owner, often for public use or benefit, usually with compensation.

Examples

to expropriate land
During the revolution, many farms were expropriated by the state.
The government decided to expropriate undeveloped land for the new highway project.
They argued that it was unfair to expropriate private property without proper compensation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confiscate: to take or seize someone's property with authority
  • antonym – return: to give something back to its original owner
  • synonym – seize: to take hold of something suddenly and forcibly, especially by official order
