agitating

adjective
causing anger or irritation
Pronunciation: / ˈædʒɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈadʒɪteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing or likely to cause anger or resentment; provoking disturbance or irritation.

Examples

agitating speech
Her agitating remarks turned the discussion into a heated argument.
The agitating news report sparked widespread public outrage.
Waiting in line for hours without explanation was an agitating experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calming: making someone feel less angry or upset
  • synonym – provoking: causing annoyance or anger
