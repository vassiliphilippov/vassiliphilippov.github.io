banding

noun
color artifact in digital images
Pronunciation: / ˈbændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪŋ / (BrE)
a digital-imaging artifact in which smooth color or tone transitions appear as distinct parallel bands, typically caused by limited color depth, quantization, or compression.

Examples

color banding in images
The screenshot exhibited obvious color banding in the shadows due to low bit depth.
When he increased the contrast, large gradients developed noticeable banding that ruined the smooth transition.
Photographers often apply dithering to exported files to minimize visible banding in subtle color transitions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – artifact: an unintended defect or distortion in a digital image
  • synonym – posterization: loss of smooth gradation between tones causing abrupt changes and visible bands
