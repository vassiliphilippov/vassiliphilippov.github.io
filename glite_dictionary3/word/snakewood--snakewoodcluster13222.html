snakewood

noun
tropical American tree with patterned wood
Pronunciation: / ˈsneɪkˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk.wʊd / (BrE)
a tropical American tree (Brosimum guianense) with large peltate leaves and hollow stems, valued for its hard, patterned wood.

Examples

snakewood patterned wood
Many violin bows are prized when they are made from rare snakewood.
Some antique furniture features inlays of snakewood due to its decorative grain.
The craftsman used snakewood to carve the handle of the luxury walking cane.
