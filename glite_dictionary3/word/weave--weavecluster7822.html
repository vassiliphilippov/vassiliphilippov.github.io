to weave

verb
make cloth by interlacing threads
Pronunciation: / wiːv / (AmE), / wiːv / (BrE)
to make fabric or combine things by interlacing threads or elements together

Examples

to weave cloth
He spent hours weaving threads to make a colorful tapestry.

The villagers wove beautiful blankets out of wool.
She used fine threads to weave a beautiful silk scarf.
Spiders weave intricate webs to catch their prey.
The author managed to weave several storylines into a single narrative.

Related Terms

  • synonym – braid: to interweave strands, as in hair or thread
  • synonym – intertwine: to twist or weave together
  • synonym – knit: to make fabric by interlocking loops of yarn
  • antonym – untangle: to separate things that have been woven or tangled together
