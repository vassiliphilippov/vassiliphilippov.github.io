oviraptorid

noun
a type of advanced theropod dinosaur
Pronunciation: / oʊ.vɪˈræp.tər.ɪd / (AmE), / əʊ.vɪˈræp.tər.ɪd / (BrE)
A member of a group of advanced carnivorous theropod dinosaurs, characterized by toothless, parrot-like beaks and often elaborate crests.

Examples

fossil oviraptorid skeleton
Scientists believe that some oviraptorids may have brooded their eggs much like modern birds.
The diet of an oviraptorid is still debated among paleontologists.
The newly discovered fossil turned out to be an oviraptorid with a remarkably well-preserved crest.
