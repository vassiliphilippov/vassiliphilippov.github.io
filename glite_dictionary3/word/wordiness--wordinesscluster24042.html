wordiness

noun
use of too many words; verbosity
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdi.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of using or containing more words than necessary, especially so as to be tedious or verbose

Examples

avoid wordiness in writing
Good writing avoids unnecessary wordiness to communicate ideas clearly.
His lectures are criticized for their wordiness and lack of focus.
The report's excessive wordiness made it difficult to find the main points.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conciseness: the quality of being brief and to the point
  • synonym – verbosity: the quality of using more words than needed
