to confabulate

verb
fabricate memories unconsciously (medical)
Pronunciation: / kənˈfæbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfabjʊleɪt /; / kənˈfabjəleɪt / (BrE)
to make up stories or information to fill gaps in memory or knowledge, often without realizing it (used in psychology and AI)

Examples

to confabulate false memories
After his stroke, Mark began to confabulate about his childhood, inserting stories that were not true.
It is important for clinicians to recognize when their patients confabulate memories rather than intentionally lie.
Patients with certain brain injuries may confabulate details, sincerely believing in events that never happened.
When asked about obscure events, the chatbot began to confabulate details that sounded believable but were false.
The assistant confabulated an entire citation instead of admitting it had no reliable source for the claim.
Users reported the virtual agent confabulating answers when its knowledge cutoff prevented it accessing current facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fabricate: to invent or make up information or stories
  • synonym – hallucinate (AI): in AI, to produce statements that are not true or grounded in training data
