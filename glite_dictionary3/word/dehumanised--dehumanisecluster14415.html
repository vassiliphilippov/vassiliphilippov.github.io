dehumanised

adjective
treated as less than human
Pronunciation: / diˈhjuːmənˌaɪzd / (AmE), / diːˈhjuːmənʌɪzd / (BrE)
Made to lose human qualities such as compassion, individuality, or emotion; treated as less than human.

Examples

dehumanised by harsh treatment
Many prisoners described feeling dehumanised by the harsh conditions and lack of respect.
She spoke out against policies that left vulnerable groups feeling dehumanised and powerless.
The film portrayed a society in which people were increasingly dehumanised by technology.
