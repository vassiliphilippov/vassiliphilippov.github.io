keratomalacia

noun
corneal softening due to vitamin A deficiency
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrətoʊməˈleɪʃiə / (AmE), / ˌkɛrətəʊməˈleɪsɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition involving softening, drying, and ulceration of the cornea, typically caused by a deficiency of vitamin A.

Examples

keratomalacia from vitamin A deficiency
Ophthalmologists in developing countries often encounter cases of keratomalacia due to dietary deficiencies.
The patient's blurred vision was found to be caused by advanced keratomalacia.
Vitamin A supplementation is crucial in preventing keratomalacia in malnourished children.
