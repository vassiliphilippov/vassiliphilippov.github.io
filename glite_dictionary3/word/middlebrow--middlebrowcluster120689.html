middlebrow

noun
someone with moderately cultured tastes
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əlˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əlˌbraʊ / (BrE)
a person whose tastes or interests are regarded as neither highly intellectual nor very unsophisticated, but in between

Examples

a middlebrow reader
Despite being called a middlebrow, she enjoyed a wide range of books from popular to literary fiction.
His plays were dismissed as middlebrow entertainment, appealing to neither critics nor the masses.
The magazine aimed to attract the middlebrow reader with articles on art, travel, and culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – highbrow: a person with highly cultured or intellectual tastes
  • antonym – lowbrow: a person with unsophisticated, unrefined tastes
