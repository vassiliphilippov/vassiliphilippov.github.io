hijab

noun
Muslim modest dress or headscarf
Pronunciation: / hɪˈdʒɑːb / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒɑːb / (BrE)
The practice, among some Muslim women, of dressing modestly in public, typically by wearing a headscarf covering the hair, neck, and sometimes the shoulders.

Examples

hijab rules
Debate about the hijab in public schools continues in several countries.
Many women choose to wear the hijab as a sign of modesty and faith.
She explained the cultural importance of the hijab at the seminar.
