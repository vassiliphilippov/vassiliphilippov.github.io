curious

adjective
eager to know or learn; inquisitive
Pronunciation: / ˈkjʊriəs / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəs / (BrE)
eager to know or learn something; showing a desire to investigate or find out more, especially about things that do not directly concern oneself

Examples

curious about details
He grew curious about his family's history and decided to do some research.
Many people stopped to take a curious look at the new sculpture in the town square.
The curious toddler kept asking questions about everything she saw in the park.

Related Terms

  • antonym – indifferent: lacking interest or concern
  • synonym – inquisitive: showing curiosity; eager to know or ask questions
