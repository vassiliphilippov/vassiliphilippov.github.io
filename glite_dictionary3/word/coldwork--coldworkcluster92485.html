to coldwork

verb
shape metal without heat
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊldˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊldˌwɜːk / (BrE)
to shape or form (metal) without the use of heat, usually by hammering, rolling, or bending at room temperature.

Examples

to coldwork metal
Metals that have been coldworked often exhibit increased hardness and strength.
The technician decided to coldwork the steel sheet for greater precision.
They coldwork aluminum parts to achieve the desired dimensions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hotwork: shape or form metal using heat
