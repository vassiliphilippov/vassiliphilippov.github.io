paralyzed

adjective
unable to move or act (physically or metaphorically)
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpærəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Unable to move or act, either physically due to paralysis or metaphorically due to fear, shock, or other causes.

Examples

paralyzed with fear
After the accident, his legs were paralyzed and he needed a wheelchair.
She was paralyzed with fear and couldn't move a muscle.
The city was paralyzed by a sudden snowstorm that blocked all the roads.

Related Terms

  • antonym – active: able to move or act
  • synonym – immobilized: unable to move
