to shelter

verb
protect or give refuge to (verb)
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛltɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛltə / (BrE)
to protect someone or something by giving them a place to stay or by shielding them from danger, bad weather, etc.

Examples

to shelter refugees
Residents opened their homes to shelter the victims of the flood.
The mountain cave sheltered them from the harsh winter winds.
We sheltered from the sudden downpour under a large oak tree.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expose: to leave without shelter or protection
  • synonym – protect: to keep someone or something safe from harm or injury
