to explode

verb
increase rapidly and uncontrollably
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsploʊd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊd / (BrE)
(of numbers, amounts, or phenomena) to increase rapidly and uncontrollably; to grow or multiply very quickly.

Examples

explode in number
Housing prices exploded after the new technology hub was built nearby.
Interest in electric vehicles is exploding as more models become available.
The number of online users has exploded in the past decade.

Related Terms

  • antonym – plummet: decrease suddenly and quickly
  • synonym – skyrocket: increase very rapidly
  • synonym – soar: rise or increase very quickly
