libertinism

noun
self-indulgent, unrestrained lifestyle
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbərtɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪbətɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A lifestyle or attitude characterized by self-indulgence, lack of moral restraint, and often sexual promiscuity or disregard for conventional morality.

Examples

moral libertinism
Historians debate whether the decline of the empire was accelerated by social libertinism.
Many critics viewed the rock star's lifestyle as the epitome of libertinism.
The novelist's early works were influenced by the ideals of libertinism prevalent in 18th-century France.
