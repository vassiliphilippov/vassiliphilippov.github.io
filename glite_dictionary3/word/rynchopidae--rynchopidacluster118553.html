Rynchopidae

noun
family of birds known as skimmers
Pronunciation: / rɪnˈkɒpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / rɪnˈkɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds, commonly known as skimmers, consisting of a single genus Rynchops, characterized by their long wings and unique bill shape used for skimming the water surface for food.
