valve

noun
device in brass instrument to change pitch
Pronunciation: / vælv / (AmE), / vɑːlv / (BrE)
a device that controls the flow of a liquid, gas, or air by opening, closing, or partially blocking a passage (including in musical instruments)

Examples

valve controls water
Modern French horns use rotary valves to change pitch smoothly.
The trumpeter pressed the third valve to reach a lower note.
When the valve stuck, the musician was unable to play the correct note.
Each radiator has a valve to control the flow of hot water.
If you turn this valve clockwise, it will shut off the gas supply.
The plumber replaced the faulty valve to stop the leak in the pipe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slide: a device in certain brass instruments for altering the length of the air column
  • synonym – stopcock: a valve for regulating the flow of a liquid or gas through a pipe
  • synonym – tap: a device for controlling the flow of liquid, especially water, from a pipe
