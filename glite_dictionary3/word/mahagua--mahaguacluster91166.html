mahagua

noun
tropical tree of Cuba and Jamaica; timber source
Pronunciation: / məˈhɑːɡwə / (AmE), / məˈhɑːɡwə / (BrE)
An upright forest tree native to Cuba and Jamaica, known for its variably hairy leaves, orange-yellow or orange-red flowers, and moderately dense timber used in cabinetwork and gunstocks.

Examples

mahagua timber furniture
In some parts of the Caribbean, mahagua trees are valued for their beautiful blossoms.
The cabinetwork in the old house was made from locally sourced mahagua wood.
The craftsman preferred mahagua for its durability when making furniture.
