leukocytosis

noun
abnormal increase in white blood cells
Pronunciation: / luːkoʊsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / luːkəʊsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal increase in the number of white blood cells in the blood, often due to infection or certain diseases

Examples

leukocytosis after infection
A doctor may order further tests if leukocytosis is found in your blood work.
Certain bacterial infections can cause leukocytosis, signaling the body's immune response.
In leukemia, leukocytosis can become dangerously high and requires medical attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym – leukopenia: a decrease in the number of white blood cells
