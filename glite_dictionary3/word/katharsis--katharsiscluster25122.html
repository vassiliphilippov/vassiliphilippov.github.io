katharsis

noun
emotional release or cleansing
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑr.sɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑː.sɪs / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis and literary theory) the process of releasing, and thereby providing relief from, strong or repressed emotions.

Examples

emotional katharsis
Artists often speak of experiencing katharsis through the act of creating their work.
Group therapy can sometimes act as a means of katharsis for participants dealing with trauma.
The play provides a sense of katharsis for the audience as unresolved tensions are dramatically released.
