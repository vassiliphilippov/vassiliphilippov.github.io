lame

adjective
weak or unconvincing (excuse, argument, etc.)
Pronunciation: / leɪm / (AmE), / leɪm / (BrE)
(of an excuse, argument, etc.) pathetically lacking in force, effectiveness, or conviction; feeble or unconvincing.

Examples

lame excuse
Her lame argument failed to persuade the committee.
That was a lame excuse for being late and no one believed him.
They gave a lame explanation that didn't make any sense.

Related Terms

  • synonym – feeble: lacking in strength or effectiveness
  • antonym – strong: having great force or effectiveness
  • synonym – weak: lacking strength, power, or intensity
