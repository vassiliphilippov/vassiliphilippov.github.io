to sophisticate

verb
to adulterate or make impure by deception
Pronunciation: / səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to alter something in order to make it impure, especially with the intention to deceive

Examples

to sophisticate food
Authorities discovered that the merchant had sophisticated the olive oil with lesser oils.
Efforts to sophisticate the product were quickly uncovered by industry inspectors.
Unscrupulous manufacturers sometimes sophisticate wine by adding artificial flavorings.
