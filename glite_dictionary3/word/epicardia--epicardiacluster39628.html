epicardia

noun
section of esophagus near the stomach
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkɑrdiə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkɑːdiə / (BrE)
the short section of the esophagus that extends from the diaphragm down to the stomach

Examples

epicardia damage
Damage to the epicardia can lead to swallowing difficulties and discomfort.
Inflammation in the epicardia may be detected during an endoscopic procedure.
The surgeon carefully examined the epicardia for any signs of abnormal tissue.
