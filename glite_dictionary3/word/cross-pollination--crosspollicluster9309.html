cross-pollination

noun
pollination between different flowers
Pronunciation: / krɑs ˌpɑlɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˌpɒlɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The transfer of pollen from the anther of one flower to the stigma of another, resulting in fertilization.

Examples

cross-pollination by bees
Bees and other insects play a crucial role in cross-pollination between different plants.
Gardeners often encourage cross-pollination to improve the genetic diversity of their flowers.
Many fruit trees require cross-pollination to produce a good harvest of apples.

Related Terms

  • antonym – self-pollination: pollination within the same flower or plant
