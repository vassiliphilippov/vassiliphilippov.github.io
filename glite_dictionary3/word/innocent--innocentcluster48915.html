innocent

noun
a person lacking knowledge of evil
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnəsənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnəs(ə)nt / (BrE)
a person who is unaware of or lacks experience with evil, corruption, or wrongdoing, often regarded as pure or naïve

Examples

a true innocent
He was a true innocent, untouched by the harsh realities of the world.
Many stories use the character of an innocent to highlight the contrast between good and evil.
The innocent wandered through the city, unaware of the dangers lurking in the shadows.

Related Terms

  • antonym – culprit: a person guilty of wrongdoing or crime
  • synonym – naive: a person lacking worldly experience, simple and trusting
