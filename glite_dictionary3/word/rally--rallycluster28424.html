rally

noun
large gathering for support or enthusiasm
Pronunciation: / ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
a large public meeting, especially one held to support a particular idea, party, or cause, or to arouse enthusiasm

Examples

political rally
Organizers hoped the rally would motivate more people to volunteer for the campaign.
The politician spoke to a cheering crowd at the city’s main rally.
Thousands attended the rally to show support for the new environmental policy.
