farthingale

noun
hooped frame worn under skirts (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːrðɪŋˌgeɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɑːθɪŋɡeɪl / (BrE)
A hooped petticoat or framework worn under a skirt to extend it horizontally, especially fashionable among European women in the 16th and 17th centuries.

Examples

farthingale under a dress
During the Renaissance, it was fashionable for women to wear a farthingale to achieve a wide silhouette.
Museum exhibits of period costumes often include at least one farthingale from the 16th century.
The queen's elaborate gown was supported by a stiff farthingale beneath the skirt.
