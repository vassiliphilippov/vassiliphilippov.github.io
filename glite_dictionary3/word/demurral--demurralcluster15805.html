demurral

noun
formal legal objection
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɝːəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌrəl / (BrE)
(law) a formal objection or protest, especially to an opponent's pleading in a court of law.

Examples

legal demurral
Despite several demurrals, the judge allowed the evidence to be admitted.
Her demurral to the proposal led to a lengthy discussion among the parties.
The lawyer filed a demurral in response to the plaintiff's complaint.
