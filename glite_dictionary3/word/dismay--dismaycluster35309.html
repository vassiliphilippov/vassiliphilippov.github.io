dismay

noun
sudden fear or anxiety
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmeɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈmeɪ / (BrE)
a sudden or strong feeling of fear, worry, shock, or disappointment, usually caused by something unexpected or difficult

Examples

in dismay at news
She looked at the broken vase in dismay, unsure of what to do next.
The news of the approaching storm filled the villagers with dismay.
To my dismay, the plan had completely fallen apart at the last minute.
He felt a wave of dismay when he realized he had forgotten his keys.
Sarah looked at the broken vase in dismay, unsure of how to explain it.
The teacher's decision to cancel the trip was met with dismay by the students.
