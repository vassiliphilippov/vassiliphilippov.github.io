anaphase

noun
stage of cell division when chromosomes separate
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈanəˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the stage of cell division (meiosis or mitosis) during which chromosomes move toward opposite poles of the spindle

Examples

anaphase of mitosis
During anaphase, the chromosomes are pulled apart to opposite sides of the cell.
Scientists observed defects in anaphase that led to abnormal cell division.
The proper separation of chromosomes in anaphase is essential for genetic stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meiosis: type of cell division that reduces chromosome number by half
  • degree lower – metaphase: stage before anaphase when chromosomes line up at the cell's equator
  • synonym – mitosis: type of cell division resulting in two identical daughter cells
  • degree higher – telophase: stage after anaphase when chromosomes arrive at the poles and the cell begins to divide
