to mark

verb
insert punctuation marks into text
Pronunciation: / mɑrk / (AmE), / mɑːk / (BrE)
to make a symbol, sign, or notation to identify, distinguish, or indicate something (including punctuation, check marks, or labeling)

Examples

to mark answers
Before publishing, the editor will mark the text with the appropriate punctuation.
She carefully marked the essay, adding commas and full stops where needed.
You should always mark your sentences clearly so that readers can understand your meaning.
Please mark each item on the checklist after you complete it.
She carefully marked the correct answers on her exam paper.
You need to mark the tasks you have finished so we can track progress.
After she called everyone, she marked their names off the attendance list.
Once the task was done, he marked it off his checklist.
We mark attendees off the guest list as they arrive at the event.
