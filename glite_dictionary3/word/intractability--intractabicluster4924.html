intractability

noun
being difficult to manage or control
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtræktəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtræktəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to manage, influence, or control

Examples

intractability of a person
His intractability made it impossible to negotiate a compromise.
Political intractability often leads to deadlock in government.
The intractability of the problem frustrated even the most experienced engineers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – manageability: the quality of being easy to manage or control
  • synonym – stubbornness: quality of being unwilling to change or yield
