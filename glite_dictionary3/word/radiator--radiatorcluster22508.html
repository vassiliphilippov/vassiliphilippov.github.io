radiator

noun
device for transferring heat from fluids
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪ.diˌeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.dɪˌeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a device, often part of a vehicle or heating system, consisting of a metal structure through which hot fluids are circulated to transfer heat to the surrounding air

Examples

car radiator leak
He replaced the old radiator in his living room with a modern, efficient model.
Steam rises from the radiator as the central heating system warms the house.
The car overheated because the radiator was leaking coolant.
