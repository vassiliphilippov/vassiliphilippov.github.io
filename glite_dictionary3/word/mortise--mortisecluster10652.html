mortise

noun
rectangular hole for a tenon
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrtɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪs / (BrE)
a rectangular hole cut into a material to receive a matching projection (tenon), forming a joint

Examples

mortise joint piece
Each leg of the table fits tightly into a mortise for stability.
She used a chisel to shape the mortise in the oak beam.
The carpenter cut a mortise into the door frame to fit the hinge snugly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tenon: a projection on the end of a piece of wood that fits into a mortise
