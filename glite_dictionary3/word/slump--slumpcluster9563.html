to slump

verb
fall sharply in value or amount
Pronunciation: / slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
(of prices, values, or activity) to decrease suddenly and by a large amount.

Examples

slump in prices
Sales of the product slumped following the negative press coverage.
Share prices slumped by nearly 20% in just one day.
The economy slumped into recession after a sharp fall in demand.

Related Terms

  • synonym – plummet: to fall or drop straight down at high speed (especially in value/price)
  • antonym – surge: to rise suddenly and powerfully, especially in price or value
