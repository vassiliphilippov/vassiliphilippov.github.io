shoulder

noun
strip of land beside a road
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊldɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
a narrow strip of land, often unpaved, along the side of a road, used especially in emergencies or for stopping vehicles.

Examples

road shoulder
After his tire blew out, he pulled over onto the shoulder to change it.
Traffic slowed down as an accident blocked the shoulder near the next exit.
Walking along the shoulder of the highway can be very dangerous at night.
