ADA-SCID

noun
immunodeficiency disorder due to ADA mutation
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈeɪ ɛs ˈsiː ˈaɪ ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈeɪ ˌɛs ˌsiː ˌaɪ ˌdiː / (BrE)
A form of severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID) caused by a mutation in the gene encoding the enzyme adenosine deaminase.

Early detection of ADA-SCID can improve long-term health outcomes for affected children.
