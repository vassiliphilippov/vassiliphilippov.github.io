direct

adjective
without intervening steps or intermediaries
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt, daɪˈrɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt, daɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
being straight, immediate, or without anything or anyone in between (such as a route, flight, consequence, or relationship)

Examples

direct flight
A direct flight from New York to London saves a lot of time compared to one with layovers.
He had direct contact with the CEO during the negotiations.
Many plants thrive when placed in direct sunlight for several hours each day.
The driver chose the direct route to save time.
The missile scored a direct hit on the target.
There is a direct flight from London to New York.
His injury was a direct consequence of the unsafe working conditions.
The new law had a direct impact on small businesses across the country.
The weather was a direct cause of the flight delays.
