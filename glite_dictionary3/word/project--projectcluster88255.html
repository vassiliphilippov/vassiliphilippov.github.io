to project

verb
make a voice or sound carry clearly
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to show, express, or present an emotion, quality, idea, or oneself so that it is clearly noticed by others, including making one's voice carry

Examples

to project your voice
If you don't project when presenting, your words might get lost in the room.
Please project your voice so that everyone in the back can hear you.
The actor learned to project his speech on stage without using a microphone.
Although she was nervous, she managed to project confidence during the interview.
He tries to project a positive attitude even in difficult situations.
The actor skillfully projected deep sorrow through his performance.
During the interview, she tried to project herself as confident and knowledgeable.
He decided to project a new marketing strategy at the board session.
The company will project its vision for the next decade at the annual meeting.
