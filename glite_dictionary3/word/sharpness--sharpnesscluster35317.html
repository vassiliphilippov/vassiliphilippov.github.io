sharpness

noun
intense, keenly felt quality
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑːrp.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːp.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being intensely and keenly felt, often in a way that causes emotional pain or distress

Examples

sharpness of criticism
He was unprepared for the sharpness of their criticism.
The sharpness of her grief was evident in every word she spoke.
The sharpness of his disappointment faded only after several months.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acuteness: the quality of being strong, severe, or intense
  • antonym – mildness: the quality of being gentle or not strong in effect
