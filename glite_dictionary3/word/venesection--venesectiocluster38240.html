venesection

noun
surgical opening of a vein
Pronunciation: / ˌviːnəˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌviːnɪˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making a surgical incision into a vein, especially to withdraw blood for therapeutic purposes

Examples

venesection for blood removal
Following the diagnosis, the physician recommended venesection to reduce the patient's iron levels.
Historically, venesection was commonly used to treat various illnesses by removing blood.
Modern venesection is performed under sterile conditions to minimize infection risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – phlebotomy: the act of cutting or puncturing a vein to draw blood
