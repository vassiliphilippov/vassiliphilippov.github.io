to distance

verb
move far ahead of others
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
to move or develop ahead of others, especially in a competition or race

Examples

to distance a rival
By the halfway mark, he managed to distance his main competitor.
She tried to distance herself from the chasing pack during the final mile.
The lead swimmer began to distance the rest of the field after the first lap.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lag behind: to fall behind in movement or progress
  • synonym – outpace: to move faster than or leave behind
