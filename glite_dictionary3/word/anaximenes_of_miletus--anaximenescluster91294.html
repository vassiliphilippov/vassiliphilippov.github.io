Anaximenes of Miletus

proper noun
pre-Socratic Greek philosopher of air as the origin of all things
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.əkˈsɪm.ə.niz əv maɪˈliː.təs / (AmE), / ˌan.ækˈsɪm.ɪ.niːz əv mɪˈliː.təs / (BrE)
(c. 585–c. 528 BC) A pre-Socratic Greek philosopher from Miletus, associated with Anaximander, who believed air is the fundamental substance of the universe.
