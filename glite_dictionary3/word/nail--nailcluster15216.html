to nail

verb
to arrest or capture (someone, informal)
Pronunciation: / neɪl / (AmE), / neɪl / (BrE)
(informal) To arrest or capture someone, especially in the act of wrongdoing.

Examples

to nail a thief
Authorities hope to nail the main suspects before they leave the country.
It took a while, but they were able to nail him on tax evasion charges.
The police finally managed to nail the burglar after weeks of surveillance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arrest: to take someone into legal custody
  • synonym – nab: to catch or seize suddenly
