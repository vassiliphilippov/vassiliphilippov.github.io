stative

adjective
denoting a state (not an action)
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb or participial adjective) expressing a state or condition, rather than an action or process.

Examples

stative verb type
'Love' is usually considered a stative verb because it describes a feeling, not an action.
In English, the verb stative describes conditions like 'know' or 'believe', not actions.
Some verbs have both stative and dynamic uses, depending on the context.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dynamic: (of verbs) expressing an action or a process rather than a state
