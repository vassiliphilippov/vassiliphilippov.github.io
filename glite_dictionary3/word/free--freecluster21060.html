free

adjective
not fixed; able to move
Pronunciation: / friː /; / fri / (AmE), / friː / (BrE)
not attached, fastened, or fixed; able to move or act independently (including scientific contexts)

Examples

free hand
He worked until the jammed drawer finally came free.
She tried to pull her foot free from the muddy ground.
The latch broke, and the gate swung free in the wind.
A free electron can move through a conductor and contribute to electric current.
Ozone decomposes to release free oxygen atoms in the upper atmosphere.
Pure hydrogen exists in nature as free molecules rather than being chemically bound to other elements.
