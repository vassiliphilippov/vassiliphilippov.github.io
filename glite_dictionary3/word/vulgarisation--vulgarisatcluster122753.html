vulgarisation

noun
making something coarse or unrefined
Pronunciation: / ˌvʌlɡərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something coarse, less refined, or more commonplace, often leading to a loss of original quality or sophistication

Examples

vulgarisation of art
Many critics argue that the vulgarisation of classical art diminishes its cultural significance.
Some believe that reality television has led to the vulgarisation of modern entertainment.
The vulgarisation of public discourse has been a subject of concern for educators.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coarsening: the process of making something less delicate or refined
  • antonym – refinement: the process of making something more elegant or cultured
