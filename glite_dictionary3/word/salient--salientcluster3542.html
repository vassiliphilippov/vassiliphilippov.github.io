salient

noun
military position projecting toward enemy
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.li.ənt / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.li.ənt / (BrE)
(military) a part of a defensive line or fortification that projects outward towards the enemy, making it more exposed to attack.

Examples

enemy salient
The general ordered reinforcements to defend the vulnerable salient in the line.
The regiment held the salient despite heavy enemy fire.
Troops stationed at the salient were at greater risk of encirclement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – re-entrant: an inwardly pointing angle on a defensive line, opposite of salient
