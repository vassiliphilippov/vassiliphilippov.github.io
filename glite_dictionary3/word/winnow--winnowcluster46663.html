to winnow

verb
blow away or remove with air
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ / (BrE)
to separate grain from chaff or to remove something unwanted by blowing air; to separate valuable parts from what is worthless

Examples

to winnow the chaff
After threshing, they would winnow the grain to separate it from the husks.
Strong gusts of wind helped winnow the dry leaves from the yard.
The farmer used a large fan to winnow the chaff from the wheat.
Ancient communities would winnow their grain by tossing it into the wind.
She spent the afternoon winnowing rice in the blazing sun.
The farmers used large fans to winnow the harvested wheat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scatter: to throw loosely about; distribute irregularly
  • synonym – separate: to set or keep apart
  • synonym – sift: examine or sort carefully, especially to select what is valuable
