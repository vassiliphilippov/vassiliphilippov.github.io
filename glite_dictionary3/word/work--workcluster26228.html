to work

verb
cause (something) to happen
Pronunciation: / wɝk / (AmE), / wɜːk / (BrE)
to cause something to happen or to occur as a result of one's actions, especially something notable or specific

Examples

to work a miracle
Her speech worked a profound change in public opinion.
Sometimes you really need luck to work a miracle in such tough situations.
The magician managed to work a spectacular trick for the audience.

  • synonym – effect: to bring about (a result); to cause to occur
  • antonym – prevent: to stop something from happening
