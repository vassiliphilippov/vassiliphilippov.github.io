to discard

verb
throw away; get rid of
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd / (BrE)
to throw something away or get rid of it because it is no longer wanted or needed

Examples

to discard old clothes
After the accident, he had to discard his damaged bicycle helmet.
She discarded the old papers she no longer needed.
You should discard all food past its expiration date.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dispose: to get rid of something, especially by throwing it away
  • antonym – keep: to retain possession of
