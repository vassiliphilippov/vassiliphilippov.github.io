to empanel

verb
select or enlist (esp. jurors) for a panel
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈpænəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpanəl /; / ɪmˈpæn(ə)l / (BrE)
to select or enroll people, especially jurors, to form a panel or official list for duty

Examples

to empanel a jury
Before the proceedings can start, the judge must empanel the committee.
It took several hours to empanel a panel of impartial jurors.
The court will empanel a new jury for the upcoming trial.
It can take several hours to empanel enough people for a large case.
She was empanelled as a prospective juror in the district court.
The court began to empanel a jury for the high-profile trial.
