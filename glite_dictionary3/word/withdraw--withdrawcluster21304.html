to withdraw

verb
to remove oneself from others
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔ / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔː / (BrE)
to become less involved with people or activities, often by keeping away or becoming emotionally distant

Examples

to withdraw emotionally
After losing his job, he began to withdraw and rarely spoke to his friends.
During stressful times, he tends to withdraw from social gatherings.
She would often withdraw into her room to be alone with her thoughts.
After the accident, he began to withdraw from his friends and family.
Many students withdraw from social activities during stressful periods.
She withdrew emotionally following the loss of her job.
