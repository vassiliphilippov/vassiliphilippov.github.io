hydrocele

noun
fluid accumulation in a sac, usually scrotum
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌsil / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrəsiːl / (BrE)
A medical condition in which serous fluid accumulates in a body sac, most commonly in the scrotum.

Examples

hydrocele in the scrotum
Doctors confirmed the diagnosis of hydrocele after performing an ultrasound exam.
In infants, a hydrocele often resolves without treatment within the first year.
The surgeon explained that the painless swelling was caused by a hydrocele.
