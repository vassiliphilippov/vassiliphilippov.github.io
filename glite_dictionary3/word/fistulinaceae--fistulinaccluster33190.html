Fistulinaceae

noun
a family of fungi distinguished by separate tubes
Pronunciation: / fɪsˌtuːləˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / fɪsˌtjʊlɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi closely related to Polyporaceae, distinguished by separate tubes on the undersurface of the cap

Examples

Fistulinaceae mushroom family
Members of the Fistulinaceae family are known for their unique tube-shaped pores.
Mycologists differentiate Fistulinaceae from Polyporaceae based on the structure of the tubes under the cap.
The beefsteak fungus, a notable member of Fistulinaceae, is edible and widely studied.
