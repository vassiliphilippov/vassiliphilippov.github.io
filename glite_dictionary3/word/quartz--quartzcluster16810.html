quartz

noun
a hard mineral (silicon dioxide) found in rocks
Pronunciation: / kwɔrts / (AmE), / kwɔːts / (BrE)
a hard, typically colorless or white mineral consisting of silicon dioxide, found widely in many rocks and used as a gemstone and in industry

Examples

clear quartz crystal
Fine yellow sand is usually composed mostly of quartz grains.
Geologists discovered a large vein of quartz inside the mountain.
The countertop was made entirely of quartz, giving the kitchen a modern look.
