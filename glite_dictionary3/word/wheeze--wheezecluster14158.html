wheeze

noun
a whistling or husky breathing sound
Pronunciation: / wiːz / (AmE), / wiːz / (BrE)
A sound made while breathing that is husky or whistling, typically caused by some obstruction of the air passages.

Examples

loud wheeze
A persistent wheeze is often a symptom of asthma in children.
I could hear the faint wheeze of his breath as he slept.
The patient’s wheeze was clearly audible during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rattle: a harsh, rattling breathing sound, often linked with respiratory distress
