resistant

adjective
not affected or harmed by something
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
not affected or harmed by something; able to withstand the effects of something (such as heat, chemicals, disease, or influence)

Examples

resistant to disease
She remained resistant to all their arguments for change.
The bacteria are increasingly resistant to antibiotics, making them harder to treat.
This material is highly resistant to water and stains.
Some plastics are highly resistant to heat and chemicals, making them suitable for industrial use.
The scientists developed a resistant strain of wheat that thrives in dry climates.
These bacteria are resistant to many common antibiotics, making infections harder to treat.
Engineers developed a plastic that is resistant to both heat and chemicals.
The new phone case is highly resistant to scratches and stains.
This raincoat is resistant to water, keeping you dry even in a heavy downpour.

Related Terms

  • antonym – absorbent: able to soak up liquids easily
  • synonym – immune: protected or exempt from a disease or the effects of something
  • synonym – impervious: not allowing a substance to pass through or be affected by
  • antonym – susceptible: likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by something
  • synonym – tolerant: able to endure specified conditions or treatment
