epicycle

noun
small circle moving around a larger circle
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɪˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪˌsaɪkəl / (BrE)
A small circle whose center moves around the circumference of a larger circle, used to describe certain orbital motions.

Examples

epicycle in planetary motion
By tracing an epicycle around a deferent, the model could account for retrograde motion.
Mathematicians can generate complex curves, such as an epicycloid, using an epicycle.
The ancient astronomers used the concept of an epicycle to explain the apparent motion of planets.
