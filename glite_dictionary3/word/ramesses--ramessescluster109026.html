Ramesses

proper noun
any of 11 kings of ancient Egypt
Pronunciation: / ˈræməsiːz / (AmE), / ˈræməsiːz / (BrE)
Any of eleven kings of ancient Egypt who reigned between 1315 and 1090 BC, most famously Ramesses II.

Examples

Ramesses the Great
Many scholars regard Ramesses II as one of the greatest pharaohs of ancient Egypt.
Several mummies of Ramesses have been unearthed and studied by archaeologists.
The temple at Abu Simbel was built by Ramesses to demonstrate his power.
