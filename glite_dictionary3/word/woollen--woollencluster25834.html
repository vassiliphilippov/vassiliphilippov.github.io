woollen

noun
fabric made from sheep's wool
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊlən / (AmE), / ˈwʊlən / (BrE)
A fabric made from the hair of sheep, especially one with a soft, fuzzy texture, typically used for clothing.

Examples

soft woollen fabric
During winter, I prefer woollen over synthetic fabrics for their comfort.
She wore a light woollen to keep warm in the chilly weather.
The shop sells high-quality woollen imported from Scotland.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cotton: soft, white, fibrous substance from cotton plants, used for fabric
  • synonym – wool: the soft, curly hair from sheep or similar animals
