to silkscreen

verb
to print using a silkscreen process
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlkˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌskriːn / (BrE)
to print or produce an image or design by means of a silkscreen process.

Examples

to silkscreen a T-shirt
He spent all afternoon silkscreening limited edition prints for his loyal customers.
Posters for the event were silkscreened in bright, bold colors at the local print shop.
The artist decided to silkscreen her designs onto a series of T-shirts for the exhibition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – screen-print: to print by forcing ink through a prepared screen of fine material
