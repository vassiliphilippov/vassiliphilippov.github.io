suggestiveness

noun
quality of evoking ideas or thoughts
Pronunciation: / səˈdʒɛstɪvnəs / (AmE), / səˈdʒɛstɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to evoke ideas, thoughts, or associations in the mind; the power to prompt suggestions or stimulate thought

Examples

the suggestiveness of a novel
He admired the suggestiveness of the novel, which prompted him to reflect deeply on its themes.
The painting's suggestiveness made every viewer interpret its meaning differently.
There was a certain suggestiveness in the way she described the setting, hinting at unspoken events.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suggestive: making people think of ideas or associations, especially indirectly
