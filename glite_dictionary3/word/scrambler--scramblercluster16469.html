scrambler

noun
device for scrambling and unscrambling signals
Pronunciation: / ˈskræmbəlɚ / (AmE), / ˈskræmbələ / (BrE)
a device that alters a signal, especially speech, to make it unintelligible during transmission and restores it to its original form at reception

Examples

scrambler device signal
He spoke through a scrambler, making his voice impossible to understand without special equipment.
New digital scramblers offer advanced protection against eavesdropping.
The military uses a scrambler to secure their radio communications.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encryptor: a device or program that encodes information to prevent unauthorized access
