eschalot

noun
small mild onion used for seasoning
Pronunciation: / ˌɛʃəˈlɑt / (AmE), / ˌeʃəˈlɒt / (BrE)
A type of onion plant producing small, clustered, mild-flavored bulbs used as a seasoning in cooking.

Examples

chopped eschalot in salad
Chopped eschalot adds a delicate flavor to many French dishes.
The chef prefers using eschalots instead of regular onions in vinaigrettes.
You can substitute eschalot with spring onion if you can’t find any.
