ferromagnetism

noun
magnetic property of certain metals
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛroʊˈmæɡnəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊˈmæɡnətɪzəm / (BrE)
The property exhibited by certain materials, such as iron, nickel, or cobalt, which allows them to become strongly magnetized in a magnetic field and retain their magnetism even after the field is removed.

Examples

strong ferromagnetism
Iron owes its strong magnetic properties to ferromagnetism.
Scientists measured the temperature at which ferromagnetism disappears in nickel samples.
The discovery of ferromagnetism in certain alloys led to advances in electrical engineering.

Related Terms

  • antonym – antiferromagnetism: property where adjacent ions or atoms have opposite magnetic moments
  • degree lower – paramagnetism: property of materials that are weakly attracted by a magnetic field
