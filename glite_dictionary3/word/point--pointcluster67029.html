to point

verb
indicate, be a sign of
Pronunciation: / pɔɪnt / (AmE), / pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to indicate, show, or aim at something by extending a finger, object, or weapon, or by directing attention

Examples

to point a finger
All signs point to a possible change in management.
His symptoms point to an underlying health issue.
Rising prices point to increased demand in the market.
He points out historical landmarks during the tour.
She pointed to the sign at the end of the street.
The teacher pointed in the direction of the library.
Always point the tool away from yourself when using it.
He pointed the gun at the target before pulling the trigger.
She pointed the flashlight toward the dark hallway to see better.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aim: to direct a weapon or other object at a target
  • synonym – brandish: to wave or exhibit a weapon in a threatening way
  • synonym – direct: to guide or cause to move towards a particular goal or target
  • synonym – gesture: to make a motion to express or direct
  • synonym – indicate: to show, suggest, or point out
  • antonym – misdirect: to direct something wrongly or badly
  • synonym – signal: to convey a warning or information
