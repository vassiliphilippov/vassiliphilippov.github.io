endemic

noun
disease regularly present in an area
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
A disease or condition regularly found among particular people or in a certain area.

Examples

endemic disease
After several decades, the disease is now considered an endemic rather than an emerging threat.
Malaria remains an endemic in certain regions of Africa, affecting millions every year.
The yellow fever endemic has shaped public health policies in the affected country.

Related Terms

  • antonym – epidemic: A widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time.
  • degree higher – pandemic: An outbreak of a disease prevalent over a whole country or the world.
