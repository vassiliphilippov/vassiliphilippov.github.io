to abridge

verb
make shorter while keeping the main parts
Pronunciation: / əˈbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to make a book, play, text, or speech shorter by removing parts while preserving its essential meaning

Examples

to abridge a book
He is abridging the lengthy speech to fit the time limit.
The author decided to abridge the novel for younger readers.
The legal document was abridged to make it easier to understand.

Related Terms

  • antonym – extend: to make something longer or larger in scope
  • synonym – shorten: to make something shorter in length, duration, or extent
