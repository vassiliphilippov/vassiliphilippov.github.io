basophilia

noun
tendency of cells to stain with basic dyes
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪsəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbeɪsəˈfɪliə / (BrE)
the tendency of cells or cellular structures to stain readily with basic dyes, often used in histology and pathology.

Examples

basophilia in blood cells
Certain white blood cells exhibit basophilia when stained in the laboratory.
Cytoplasmic basophilia is an important diagnostic feature in some diseases.
The pathologist noted marked basophilia in the liver tissue sample.
