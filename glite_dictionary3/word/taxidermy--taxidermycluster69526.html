taxidermy

noun
art of mounting animal skins
Pronunciation: / ˈtæksɪˌdɜrmi / (AmE), / ˈtaksɪˌdɜːmi / (BrE)
the art or process of preparing, stuffing, and mounting the skins of animals to make them appear lifelike

Examples

taxidermy animal display
He developed an interest in taxidermy after watching documentaries about wildlife.
Many hunters have their trophies preserved through taxidermy to display them at home.
The museum displayed an impressive collection of taxidermy, including a lifelike lion and several rare birds.
