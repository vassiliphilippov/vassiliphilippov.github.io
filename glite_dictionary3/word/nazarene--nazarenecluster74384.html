Nazarene

noun
early name for a Christian
Pronunciation: / ˌnæzəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌnazəˈriːn / (BrE)
an early Christian, especially a member of a Jewish-Christian sect that followed Jesus and kept Jewish law

Examples

early Nazarene follower
In the earliest decades after Jesus’s death, supporters of his teachings were often called Nazarenes by others.
Some Roman officials distinguished between Jews and Nazarenes in their records of religious sects.
The Acts of the Apostles refers to Paul as a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes.
Historians believe that the Nazarenes maintained most traditional Jewish customs while worshipping Jesus.
Some scholars argue that the beliefs of the Nazarenes influenced later Christian sects.
The early Nazarenes were distinct in rejecting the Epistles of St. Paul.
