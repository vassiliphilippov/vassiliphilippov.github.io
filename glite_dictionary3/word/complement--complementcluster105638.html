complement

noun
a group of immune-response enzymes in serum
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmpləmənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
a group of enzymes in blood serum that work together as part of the immune response by enhancing the body's ability to fight infection

Examples

complement proteins in blood
Blood tests measured the patient's complement levels to assess her immune function.
The complement system helps defend the body against bacteria and viruses.
The doctor explained that a low complement level could indicate an immune system problem.
