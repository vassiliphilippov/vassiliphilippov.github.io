Theodosius I

proper noun
Roman emperor, last to rule united Empire
Pronunciation: / θiəˈdoʊziəs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / θɪəˈdəʊziəs ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
the Roman emperor (379–395), the last to rule over both the eastern and western halves of the Roman Empire, noted for declaring Christianity the official state religion and banning pagan practices.

Examples

Theodosius I ruled Rome
Theodosius I is remembered for making Christianity the official religion of the Roman Empire.
During the reign of Theodosius I, the empire was still under a single ruler before it permanently split.
The ban on pagan rituals under Theodosius I marked a turning point in the empire's religious policies.
