gratuity

noun
a tip for services rendered
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈtuːəti / (AmE), / ɡrəˈtjuːəti / (BrE)
a sum of money given to someone for providing a service, as a tip or token of appreciation, in addition to the basic payment

Examples

Some restaurants include a gratuity in the total bill for large parties.
The waiter was pleased with the generous gratuity left by the guests.
When paying the bill, she added a 15% gratuity for excellent service.
She received a gratuity from the company for her outstanding service.
The waiter smiled when the customers left a generous gratuity on the table.
Upon retirement, he was given a substantial gratuity as a token of appreciation.
