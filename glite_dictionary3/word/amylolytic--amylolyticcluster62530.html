amylolytic

adjective
relating to starch breakdown
Pronunciation: / ˌæmɪloʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæmɪləʊˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or causing the breakdown of starch into sugars, typically by enzymatic action.

Examples

amylolytic enzyme action
Certain bacteria possess amylolytic enzymes that break down starch in the digestive tract.
Researchers are studying amylolytic processes to improve industrial fermentation methods.
The amylolytic activity of the solution was measured to determine the rate of starch decomposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amylase: an enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of starch into sugars
