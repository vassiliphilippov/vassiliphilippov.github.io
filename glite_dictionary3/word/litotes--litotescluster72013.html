litotes

noun
affirmation by negating the contrary
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtəˌtiz / (AmE), / lɪˈtəʊtiːz / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which an affirmative is expressed by negating its opposite, used for rhetorical effect or understatement.

Examples

litotes in writing
By saying he was "not unhappy," she recognized the use of litotes in his speech.
English speakers frequently use litotes for emphasis, such as saying "she's not bad-looking."
The author used litotes when he called a disastrous event "not uncommon."

Related Terms

  • synonym – understatement: the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
