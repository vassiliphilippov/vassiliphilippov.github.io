drawn-out

adjective
spoken or sounded slowly, with prolonged vowels
Pronunciation: / drɔn aʊt / (AmE), / drɔːn aʊt / (BrE)
(of speech) spoken slowly with vowels lengthened more than usual; prolonged in time or sound.

Examples

drawn-out speech
He replied to every question in a slow, drawn-out manner, stretching every vowel.
Her drawn-out explanation made the meeting feel even longer.
The teacher's drawn-out speech made it hard for students to stay focused.

Related Terms

  • antonym – brief: lasting only a short time
  • synonym – prolonged: lasting for a longer time than usual
