Lethe

noun
river of forgetfulness in Greek myth
Pronunciation: / ˈliːθi / (AmE), / ˈliːθi / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a river in Hades whose waters caused the souls of the dead to forget their earthly lives and sufferings.

Examples

the Lethe river
In ancient texts, a soul would drink from the Lethe before entering a new life.
Poets often invoke the Lethe as a metaphor for oblivion or forgetfulness.
She wished she could immerse her painful memories in the waters of Lethe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – River Styx: another river in Hades; associated with the boundary between Earth and the Underworld
