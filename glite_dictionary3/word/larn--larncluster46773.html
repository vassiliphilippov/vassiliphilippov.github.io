to larn

verb
acquire knowledge or skill (dialect)
Pronunciation: / lɑrn / (AmE), / lɑːn / (BrE)
(dialect) To learn; to gain knowledge or skill through study or experience.

Examples

to larn new skills
He never did larn to swim, despite living by the river all his life.
She reckoned she could larn him a thing or two about farming.
You’ll larn a lot here if you pay attention to the old folks.
