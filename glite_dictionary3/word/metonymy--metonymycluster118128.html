metonymy

noun
figure of speech; substitution by related word
Pronunciation: / məˈtɑnəmi / (AmE), / mɪˈtɒnɪmi / (BrE)
The use of the name of one thing to refer to something else that is closely related to it, such as using 'crown' to mean 'the monarchy.'

Examples

metonymy in language
Calling business executives ‘suits’ is an example of metonymy in everyday language.
In literature, authors often use metonymy to create vivid imagery and association.
The phrase ‘the White House’ is a metonymy for the U.S. president or the executive branch.

  • synonym – synecdoche: a figure of speech in which a part is used to represent the whole or vice versa
