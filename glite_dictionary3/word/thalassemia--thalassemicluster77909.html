thalassemia

noun
inherited blood disorder causing anemia
Pronunciation: / ˌθæl.əˈsiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌθæl.əˈsiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A hereditary blood disorder characterized by abnormal hemoglobin production, leading to anemia.

Examples

Children with thalassemia may require regular blood transfusions throughout their lives.
