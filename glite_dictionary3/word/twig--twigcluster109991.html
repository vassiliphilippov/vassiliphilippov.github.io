twig

noun
small, thin tree branch
Pronunciation: / twɪɡ / (AmE), / twɪɡ / (BrE)
a small, thin branch growing from a tree or shrub, typically one of the current or previous year

Examples

snap a twig
He snapped a twig when he walked through the thick underbrush.
She found a twig on the ground and used it to draw in the dirt.
The bird built its nest from small twigs and leaves.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – branch: a part of a tree that grows out from the trunk or from a bough
  • synonym – stick: a thin piece of wood, often fallen from a tree
