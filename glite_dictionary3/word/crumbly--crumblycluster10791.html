crumbly

adjective
easily breaking into small pieces
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌmbli / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmbli / (BrE)
easily breaking into small pieces or fragments; apt to fall apart into crumbs or powder.

Examples

crumbly cheese
Ancient bricks can become crumbly after years of exposure to the elements.
She picked up a crumbly piece of cheese from the platter.
The freshly baked cake was so crumbly that it fell apart when I tried to cut it.
