impertinent

adjective
lively or boldly playful in manner
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɚtɪnənt /; / ɪmˈpɝː.tən.ənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːtɪnənt /; / ɪmˈpɜː.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
rude, disrespectful, or boldly forward (sometimes in a playful or cheeky way)

Examples

impertinent remark to teacher
Her impertinent sense of humor kept the party lively and unexpected.
The child's impertinent questions made everyone in the room laugh.
There was an impertinent charm in his quick wit and ready smile.
Children can sometimes be impertinent without realizing they are being disrespectful.
It was impertinent of her to question the teacher's decision in front of everyone.
The reporter's impertinent questions angered the politician.
