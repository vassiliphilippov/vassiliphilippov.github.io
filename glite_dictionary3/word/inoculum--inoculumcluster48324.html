inoculum

noun
substance to induce immunity
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɑːkjələm / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjʊləm / (BrE)
A substance containing viruses, toxins, or immune serum that is introduced into the body to stimulate or increase immunity to a particular disease.

Examples

inoculum injection
A small amount of inoculum was injected into the patient's arm to initiate an immune response.
Researchers carefully measured the inoculum before adding it to the culture medium.
The laboratory prepared an inoculum containing weakened forms of the virus for testing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vaccine: a substance used to stimulate the production of antibodies and provide immunity against one or several diseases
