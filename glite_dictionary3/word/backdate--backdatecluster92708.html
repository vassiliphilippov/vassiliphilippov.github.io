to backdate

verb
make effective from an earlier date
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæk.deɪt / (BrE)
to make effective or applicable from a date prior to the present or to the date of actual issue

Examples

to backdate a contract
Her pay increase was backdated to January as promised.
The new policy will be backdated to the beginning of the year.
They decided to backdate the contract for administrative purposes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – antedate: to assign to an earlier date; to date before the actual time
