cenogenesis

noun
introduction of novel embryonic features
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːnoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
The introduction, during embryonic development, of features or structures not present in the ancestral evolutionary history of the species.

Examples

cenogenesis in embryos
Comparing cenogenesis and palingenesis helps clarify patterns of embryonic development across different species.
Some embryologists debate the significance of cenogenesis in the development of complex organisms.
The formation of the mammalian placenta is considered a classic example of cenogenesis in evolutionary biology.

Related Terms

  • antonym – palingenesis: reappearance of ancestral characteristics during development
