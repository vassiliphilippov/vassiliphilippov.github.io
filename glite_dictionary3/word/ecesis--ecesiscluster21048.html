ecesis

noun
establishment of an organism in a new habitat
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsiːsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈsiːsɪs / (BrE)
(Ecology) The process by which a plant or animal becomes established in a new habitat.

Examples

ecesis of new plants
Many invasive species undergo ecesis quickly, outcompeting native flora and fauna.
Successful ecesis depends on the suitability of soil and climate conditions in the area.
The ecesis of mangrove seedlings is critical for the formation of new coastal forests.
