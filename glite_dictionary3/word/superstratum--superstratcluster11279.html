superstratum

noun
dominant invading language influencing natives
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pɚˈstreɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstreɪ.təm / (BrE)
A language imposed by a later conquering or invading people on an indigenous population, which influences the native language by contributing features to it.

Examples

linguistic superstratum
In English, Norman French acted as a superstratum after the Norman Conquest.
Latin was the superstratum in Gaul, shaping the local language that became French.
The influence of a superstratum can dramatically alter the vocabulary and grammar of the indigenous language.

Related Terms

  • antonym – substratum: a native or previously existing language that influences an incoming dominant language
