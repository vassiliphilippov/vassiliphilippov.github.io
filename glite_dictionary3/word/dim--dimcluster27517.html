dim

adjective
slow to learn or understand
Pronunciation: / dɪm / (AmE), / dɪm / (BrE)
(of a person) slow to understand or lacking intelligence; not quick-witted.

Examples

dim student
Although Tom is friendly, he can be a bit dim when it comes to solving problems.
He's not dim; he just needs a little more time to understand the material.
She worried that the teacher thought her son was dim because he was quiet in class.
