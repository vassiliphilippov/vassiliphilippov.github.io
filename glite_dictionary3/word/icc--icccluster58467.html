ICC

noun
International Criminal Court
Pronunciation: / aɪ si si / (AmE), / aɪ siː siː / (BrE)
abbreviation for International Criminal Court, the permanent international court (established by the Rome Statute, 2002) that prosecutes individuals for genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes.

Examples

ICC war crimes
The ICC issued arrest warrants for several war crimes suspects after a lengthy investigation.
Many governments debated whether to cooperate with the ICC during the cross-border conflict resolution talks.
Victims' groups urged the ICC to speed up its inquiry into alleged crimes and provide justice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – International Criminal Court: the permanent international tribunal that prosecutes individuals for the gravest international crimes
