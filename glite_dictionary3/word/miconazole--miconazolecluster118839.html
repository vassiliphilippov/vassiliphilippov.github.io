miconazole

noun
antifungal drug for skin/mucous infections
Pronunciation: / maɪˈkɑːnəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / mɪˈkɒnəzəʊl / (BrE)
a synthetic antifungal drug, usually administered as a nitrate, used to treat fungal infections of the skin and mucous membranes

Examples

miconazole cream treatment
Some people experience mild irritation when using miconazole for the first time.
The doctor prescribed miconazole cream to treat the patient's athlete's foot.
Topical miconazole is often recommended for fungal infections in the mouth or vaginal area.
