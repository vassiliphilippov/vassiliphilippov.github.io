ladanum

noun
resin from rockroses used in perfumery
Pronunciation: / ˈlæd.ən.əm / (AmE), / ˈlæd.ən.əm / (BrE)
a soft, sticky, dark resin obtained from certain Mediterranean rockroses, used in perfumes as a fixative

Examples

ladanum perfume ingredient
Extracts of ladanum have been prized since ancient times for their rich scent.
The perfumer used ladanum to give the fragrance a deep, warm base note.
The sticky ladanum was collected from the leaves of the cistus shrub in the Mediterranean.
