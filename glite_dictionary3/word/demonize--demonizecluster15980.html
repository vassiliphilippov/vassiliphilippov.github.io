to demonize

verb
portray as evil or demonic
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdiːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to portray or treat someone or something as evil, wicked, or threatening, often unfairly

Examples

to demonize a group
Parents shouldn't demonize video games without understanding their content.
Some politicians try to demonize their opponents to gain public support.
The media was criticized for attempting to demonize certain groups.
It's easy to demonize someone you don't understand or agree with.
Some media outlets tend to demonize certain groups to provoke fear or anger.
We should try to understand others instead of quickly demonizing them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deify: to treat or worship someone as a god (opposite direction of meaning)
  • antonym – humanize: to portray as more human or with human qualities
  • synonym – vilify: to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
