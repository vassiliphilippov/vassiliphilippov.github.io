biennial

noun
plant living two years
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɛniəl / (AmE), / baɪˈɛniəl / (BrE)
A plant that completes its life cycle in two years, typically flowering and producing fruit in the second year before dying.

Examples

biennial plant type
Carrots are technically biennial plants, but they are usually harvested after one growing season.
Foxglove is a classic example of a biennial because it flowers only in its second year.
Gardeners often plant biennials one year and enjoy their blooms the next spring.

Related Terms

  • antonym – annual: a plant that completes its life cycle in one year
  • antonym – perennial: a plant that lives for more than two years
