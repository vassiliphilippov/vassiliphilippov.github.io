teeter

noun
playground apparatus (seesaw)
Pronunciation: / ˈtitər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtə / (BrE)
A playground apparatus consisting of a long board balanced on a central support, with children sitting at each end to ride it up and down.

Examples

ride a teeter board
At recess, the most popular plaything was the old wooden teeter.
She hurt her knee when she slid off the teeter too quickly.
The children laughed as they rode the teeter in the playground.
