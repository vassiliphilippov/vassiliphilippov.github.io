barratry

noun
offense of inciting lawsuits
Pronunciation: / ˈbærətri / (AmE), / ˈbærətri / (BrE)
the offense of persistently instigating groundless lawsuits or quarrels, especially for personal gain; vexatious litigation.

Examples

persistent barratry lawsuits
Historically, barratry was considered a serious offense to prevent the abuse of legal systems.
She was charged with barratry for repeatedly pursuing unfounded lawsuits against her neighbors.
The lawyer was disbarred after repeated accusations of barratry in the courts.
