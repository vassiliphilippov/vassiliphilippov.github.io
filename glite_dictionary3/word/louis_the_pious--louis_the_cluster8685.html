Louis the Pious

proper noun
Frankish emperor (778–840), son of Charlemagne
Pronunciation: / ˈluːi ðə ˈpaɪəs / (AmE), / ˈluːiː ðə ˈpaɪəs / (BrE)
(778–840) Third son of Charlemagne; king of France and Germany and Holy Roman Emperor from 814 to 840, known for his devoutness and efforts to maintain the Carolingian Empire.

Examples

Louis the Pious ruled the empire
Louis the Pious succeeded Charlemagne as Holy Roman Emperor in 814.
Many historians study Louis the Pious for his role in the division of the Carolingian Empire.
The reign of Louis the Pious was marked by religious reforms and internal strife.
