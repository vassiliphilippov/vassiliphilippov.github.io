beatified

adjective
declared blessed by the Catholic Church
Pronunciation: / biˈætɪfaɪd / (AmE), / biˈætɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) officially declared to be blessed and thus worthy of veneration, usually as a step toward sainthood.

Examples

beatified by the Church
After decades of devotion, the priest was finally beatified by the Church.
Mother Teresa was beatified by Pope John Paul II in 2003.
The church held a special ceremony in honor of the newly beatified individual.
