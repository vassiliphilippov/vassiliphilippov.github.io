lieutenant

noun
assistant or deputy with delegated authority
Pronunciation: / luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
A person who acts as an assistant or deputy, especially with authority to exercise power when their superior is absent.

Examples

trusted lieutenant to a boss
He served as the CEO's trusted lieutenant during the company's reorganization.
The mayor relied on his lieutenant to oversee the budget committee.
When the director was away, her lieutenant managed the operations smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assistant: a person who helps or supports someone else
  • synonym – deputy: a person who is appointed to assist or act for another
  • antonym – superior: a person of higher rank or authority
