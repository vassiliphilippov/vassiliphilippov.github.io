cloud

noun
visible mass of particles in air
Pronunciation: / klaʊd / (AmE), / klaʊd / (BrE)
a large group or mass of small things, such as dust, smoke, insects, or blossoms, floating or collected together in the air

Examples

cloud of dust
A cloud of dust followed the car down the dirt road.
A thick cloud of smoke rose from the burning building.
He vanished behind a cloud of chalk that filled the air.
A cloud of locusts darkened the sky as it passed overhead.
He inhaled a cloud of smoke when the door opened.
The garden was filled with a cloud of white blossoms in the spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cluster: a group of similar things positioned closely together
  • synonym – mist: a collection of tiny water droplets in the air causing reduced visibility
  • synonym – plume: a long cloud of smoke or vapor
  • synonym – swarm: a large group of insects moving together
