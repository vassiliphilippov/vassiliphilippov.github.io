flounce

noun
exaggerated, jerky manner of walking
Pronunciation: / flaʊns / (AmE), / flaʊns / (BrE)
an act of walking with exaggerated, jerky, or showy motions, especially to display anger or annoyance

Examples

a dramatic flounce
His flounce across the stage made everyone laugh.
She left the room in a dramatic flounce, slamming the door behind her.
With a theatrical flounce, she tossed her scarf and stormed away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stalk: to walk angrily or stiffly
  • synonym – strut: a proud, stiff-minded walk, often to show off
