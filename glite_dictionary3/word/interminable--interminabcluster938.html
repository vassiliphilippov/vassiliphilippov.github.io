interminable

adjective
endlessly long or tedious
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɝːmənəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːmɪnəbl / (BrE)
lasting for a very long time and seeming never to end; often used when the length causes boredom or annoyance.

Examples

interminable wait
His stories were so interminable that people often lost interest before he finished.
She grew weary of the interminable rain that had fallen for weeks.
The interminable meeting dragged on for hours, with no conclusion in sight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – brief: lasting only a short time
  • synonym – endless: having or seeming to have no end or limit
  • synonym – eternal: lasting or existing forever; without end
