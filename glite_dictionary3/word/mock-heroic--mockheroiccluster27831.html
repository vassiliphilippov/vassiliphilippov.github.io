mock-heroic

noun
satirical imitation of heroic poetry
Pronunciation: / mɑk hɪˈroʊɪk / (AmE), / mɒk hɪˈrəʊɪk / (BrE)
A literary or satirical imitation of the style and conventions of heroic poetry, often to ridicule or satirize its subject.

Examples

a mock-heroic poem
Jonathan Swift is well known for his mock-heroic poems, which make fun of the classical form.
The author's new novel employs a mock-heroic tone to satirize the conventions of epic tales.
The play's mock-heroic style highlights the absurdity of the characters' grandiose ambitions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burlesque: a literary or dramatic work that seeks to ridicule by means of grotesque exaggeration or comic imitation
  • synonym – parody: an imitation of the style of a particular writer, artist, or genre with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
