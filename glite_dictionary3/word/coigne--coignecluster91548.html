coigne

noun
printer's wedge for locking up type
Pronunciation: / kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
An adjustable metal or wooden wedge used by printers to securely lock a form in a chase.

Examples

metal coigne wedge
He carefully tapped the coigne into position to hold the type firmly.
The old pressroom still had a drawer full of wooden coignes.
Without a coigne, the layout may shift during printing.
