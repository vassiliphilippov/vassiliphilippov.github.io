arteria lingualis

noun
artery supplying the tongue
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə lɪŋˈɡweɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə lɪŋˈɡweɪlɪs / (BrE)
a blood vessel arising from the external carotid artery that supplies blood to the underside of the tongue

Examples

arteria lingualis supplies tongue
Blockage of the arteria lingualis can lead to impaired function of the tongue.
During surgery on the floor of the mouth, care must be taken not to damage the arteria lingualis.
The arteria lingualis supplies oxygenated blood to the muscles beneath the tongue.
