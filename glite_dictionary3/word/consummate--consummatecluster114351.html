to consummate

verb
to bring to perfection or completion
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnsəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsjʊmeɪt / (BrE)
to make something complete or perfect; to bring to a high degree of skill, excellence, or completion

Examples

to consummate a plan
After years of preparation, her vision was consummated in the opening of her dream restaurant.
The peace talks were consummated by the signing of the historic treaty.
Their dedication and hard work finally consummated the project, delivering a flawless launch.

  • synonym – perfect: to make completely free from faults or defects; make perfect
