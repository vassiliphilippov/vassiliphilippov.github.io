to jam

verb
block electronic or radio signals
Pronunciation: / dʒæm / (AmE), / dʒam / (BrE)
to deliberately block or interfere with the reception of radio or electronic signals

Examples

to jam radio signals
Pirates were able to jam the ship's communications with powerful transmitters.
The government tried to jam the foreign radio broadcasts during the conflict.
They are developing new ways to prevent enemies from jamming military radar.

Related Terms

  • synonym – block: prevent passage or movement; used for objects or signals
  • synonym – interfere: to prevent something from working smoothly
