Neoplatonist

noun
adherent of Neoplatonism
Pronunciation: / niː.oʊˈpleɪtənɪst / (AmE), / niː.əʊˈpleɪtənɪst / (BrE)
A person who adheres to the philosophical ideas of Neoplatonism, a school of thought influenced by Plato and later developed in the 3rd century AD.

Examples

famous Neoplatonist philosopher
As a Neoplatonist, she believed in the existence of an underlying spiritual reality.

Plotinus is often regarded as the most influential Neoplatonist in ancient philosophy.
