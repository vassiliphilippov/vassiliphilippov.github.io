visceral

adjective
based on instinctive or emotional response
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪsəɹəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl / (BrE)
(of feelings or reactions) based on deep, instinctive emotion rather than on reason or logic.

Examples

visceral reaction
His visceral dislike of the politician wasn't based on any particular policy.
She had a visceral reaction to the disturbing news, unable to explain her feelings logically.
The movie provoked a visceral fear in the audience, far beyond what words could express.

Related Terms

  • synonym – instinctive: based on instinct or innate feeling
  • antonym – rational: based on logic or reasoning
