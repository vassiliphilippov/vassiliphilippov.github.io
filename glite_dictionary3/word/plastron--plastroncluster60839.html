plastron

noun
metal breastplate worn under mail
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstrɑn / (AmE), / ˈplæstrɒn / (BrE)
A metal breastplate worn beneath a coat of mail, especially as part of historical armor.

Examples

During the museum tour, the guide pointed out a medieval plastron used by soldiers.
Historians believe the plastron was essential for absorbing the force of sword strikes.
The knight wore a plastron under his mail to protect himself in battle.
