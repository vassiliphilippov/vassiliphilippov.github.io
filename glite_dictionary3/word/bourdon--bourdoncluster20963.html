bourdon

noun
drone pipe of bagpipes or organ
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːdɑn / (AmE), / ˈbʊədɒn / (BrE)
a pipe of the bagpipe (or organ) that is tuned to produce a single continuous tone, usually at a fixed pitch; a drone pipe

Examples

bourdon pipe sound
He adjusted the bourdon on his bagpipes to achieve a more resonant drone.
The low, steady hum of the bourdon is unmistakable in traditional Scottish music.
The organ's largest pipe, called the bourdon, produced a powerful, deep note.
