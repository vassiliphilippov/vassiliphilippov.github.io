randomization

noun
process of making random; arranging by chance
Pronunciation: / ˌrændəmɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrandəmʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of making something random, especially by arranging or selecting in a way that each outcome is equally likely; deliberate assignment or arrangement to minimize bias, often used in scientific experiments.

Examples

randomization in experiments
Proper randomization in a clinical trial is crucial for scientific validity.
The randomization of participants helps ensure that the results are not biased.
Without randomization, patterns in the data could influence the outcome of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – determinism: the opposite principle, where events are determined and predictable
  • synonym – randomness: the quality or state of lacking a predictable order or plan
