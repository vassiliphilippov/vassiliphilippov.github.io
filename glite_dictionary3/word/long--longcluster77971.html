long

adjective
financial term for holding assets expecting price rise
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋ / (BrE)
(in finance) holding securities or commodities with the expectation that their price will rise, or relating to a position benefiting from a price increase.

Examples

long position stocks
She maintained a long position in gold futures throughout the year.
The investor decided to go long on technology stocks ahead of the quarterly earnings reports.
Traders who are long in oil benefit when the price of crude rises.

Related Terms

  • antonym – short: selling securities or commodities not owned, expecting a price drop (financial antonym)
