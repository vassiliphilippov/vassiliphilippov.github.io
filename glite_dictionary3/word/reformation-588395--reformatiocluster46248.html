reformation

noun
major improvement or change in institutions
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛfəˈrmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making significant changes or improvements to an institution or practice, especially in social, political, or religious contexts

Examples

reformation of society
After years of corruption, there was an urgent need for reformation within the organization.
The new government promised a complete reformation of the education system.
The period was marked by a reformation in religious practices across the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deterioration: the process of becoming worse
  • synonym – reform: to make changes in order to improve something
