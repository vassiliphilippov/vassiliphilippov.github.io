to jag

verb
make a jagged cut or edge
Pronunciation: / dʒæɡ / (AmE), / dʒæɡ / (BrE)
to make a jagged cut or edge; to cut notches or teeth into something

Examples

to jag the edge
Carefully jag the edges of the paper to give it a handmade appearance.
She used scissors to jag the fabric for a decorative effect.
The metal was jagged to create a serrated knife blade.

  • antonym – smooth: to make even or free from roughness
