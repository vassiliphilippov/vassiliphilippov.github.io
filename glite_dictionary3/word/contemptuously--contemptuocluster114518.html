contemptuously

adverb
in a disdainful or disrespectful way
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmptʃuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptjʊəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows disdain or lack of respect.

Examples

to speak contemptuously
He contemptuously dismissed the critics' concerns as unimportant.
She glared contemptuously at the suggestion, making it clear she disagreed.
The director spoke contemptuously about his rival's achievements.
