schnorchel

noun
submarine air intake device
Pronunciation: / ˈʃnɔːrkəl / (AmE), / ˈʃnɔːkəl / (BrE)
A retractable air intake and exhaust device used on submarines, allowing them to operate submerged for long periods by providing an air passage to the surface.

Examples

schnorchel on a submarine
Engineers inspected the damaged schnorchel after the vessel returned to port.
German U-boats were equipped with a schnorchel to help them evade detection while remaining submerged.
The invention of the schnorchel significantly extended the operational capabilities of submarines during World War II.
