to distort

verb
alter so as to mislead
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːt / (BrE)
to change the meaning, truth, or accuracy of something so that it is false or misleading

Examples

to distort the truth
He often distorts the truth to make his stories more interesting.
The newspaper was accused of distorting the facts to suit its agenda.
The report had been distorted by repeated retelling until it was hardly recognizable.
Don't let your admiration distort your view of his actions.
Her anger began to distort her judgment in the dispute.
Painful memories can sometimes distort our perception of the past.

  • synonym – falsify: to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
