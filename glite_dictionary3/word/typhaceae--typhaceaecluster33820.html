Typhaceae

noun
family of perennial marsh plants (cattails)
Pronunciation: / taɪˈfeɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / tɪˈfeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of perennial marsh plants with creeping rootstocks and long, linear leaves, commonly known as the cattail family.

Examples

Typhaceae marsh plants
Scientists studied the Typhaceae family to understand their adaptation to marshy habitats.
The Typhaceae are often found in wetlands throughout temperate regions of the world.
The most familiar members of the Typhaceae are the common cattails seen alongside ponds and lakes.
