katabatic

adjective
wind moving downward a slope due to cooling
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈbætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbatɪk / (BrE)
(of a wind or air current) moving downward or down a slope, usually as a result of cooling, especially at night.

Examples

katabatic wind descends
During the night, a katabatic wind descended the mountain, clearing the fog.
Strong katabatic winds swept down the valley after sunset, making the temperature drop rapidly.
The Antarctic coast is known for its fierce katabatic storms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anabatic: (of a wind) moving upward, especially up a slope, due to warming of the surface
