to sovietise

verb
make adopt Soviet systems
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.ə.taɪz / (BrE)
to make a country, institution, or system adopt the systems, principles, or control of the former Soviet Union

Examples

to sovietise a country
After the revolution, the new leadership tried to sovietise the educational system.
Policies designed to sovietise agriculture led to widespread resistance.
Several Eastern European countries were rapidly sovietised in the late 1940s.
After World War II, the USSR attempted to sovietise many countries in Eastern Europe.
It took decades to reverse the policies used to sovietise the region.
The new regime began to sovietise education, industry, and the media.
