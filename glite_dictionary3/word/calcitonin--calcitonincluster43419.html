calcitonin

noun
thyroid hormone lowering blood calcium
Pronunciation: / ˌkælsɪˈtoʊnɪn / (AmE), / ˌkælsɪˈtəʊnɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that lowers calcium levels in the blood and inhibits bone resorption

Examples

calcitonin hormone release
Calcitonin helps regulate calcium levels in the body by decreasing bone resorption.
High calcitonin can be a marker for certain types of thyroid cancer.
The doctor measured the patient's calcitonin levels to assess thyroid function.
