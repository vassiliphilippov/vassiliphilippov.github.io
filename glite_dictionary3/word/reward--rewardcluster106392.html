to reward

verb
give something in recognition of actions
Pronunciation: / rɪˈwɔrd / (AmE), / rɪˈwɔːd / (BrE)
to give something (such as money, a benefit, or recognition) to someone for doing something good, helpful, or successful

Examples

to reward good behavior
She was rewarded for her honesty when she returned the lost wallet.
The company decided to reward its best employees with a bonus.
We should always reward good behavior in children to encourage it.
He believes that good behavior should always be rewarded rather than ignored.
The company decided to reward her exceptional performance with a bonus.
Volunteers are often rewarded for their hard work at a special ceremony.
