to buzz

verb
to fly low over (aircraft)
Pronunciation: / bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to fly low over a place, especially to create noise or attract attention.

Examples

to buzz a building
A small propeller plane buzzed the stadium, drawing everyone's attention.
Helicopters buzzed low over the forest as part of the rescue operation.
The fighter jets buzzed the city before the parade began.

Related Terms

  • synonym – skim: to move quickly just above a surface
  • antonym – soar: to fly or rise high in the air
