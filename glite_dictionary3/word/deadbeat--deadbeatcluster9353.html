deadbeat

noun
someone who does not pay debts
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌbit / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌbiːt / (BrE)
a person who habitually fails to pay debts or meet financial obligations

Examples

lazy deadbeat
Banks have strict policies to avoid lending money to deadbeats.
She accused him of being a deadbeat for never settling his credit card bills.
The landlord was frustrated with yet another deadbeat tenant who refused to pay rent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defaulter: a person who fails to fulfill a financial obligation
  • antonym – paymaster: someone responsible for paying others or who regularly pays debts
