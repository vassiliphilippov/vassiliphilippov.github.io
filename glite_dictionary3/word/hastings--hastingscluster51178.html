Hastings

proper noun
battle in 1066 leading to Norman Conquest
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪstɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈheɪstɪŋz / (BrE)
the Battle of Hastings, a decisive conflict in 1066 in which William the Conqueror defeated the Saxons, leading to the Norman Conquest of England.

Examples

Battle of Hastings
Many historians consider Hastings to be one of the most significant battles in English history.
The Battle of Hastings marked the beginning of Norman rule in England.
William the Conqueror emerged victorious at Hastings in 1066.
