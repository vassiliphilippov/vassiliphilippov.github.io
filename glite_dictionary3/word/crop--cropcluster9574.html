to crop

verb
(of land) yield a crop
Pronunciation: / krɑp / (AmE), / krɒp / (BrE)
to produce or grow crops; to prepare land for growing crops

Examples

to crop the field
If managed correctly, the land can crop for decades without degradation.
The fertile valley crops abundantly each summer.
This kind of soil crops well even in dry years.
Farmers begin to crop the fields as soon as the ground thaws in spring.
Properly cropping the land is essential for a good harvest.
The community worked together to crop the abandoned lot for their vegetable project.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fallow: leave land unseeded after plowing for a period
  • synonym – harvest: gather (a crop) as a harvest
