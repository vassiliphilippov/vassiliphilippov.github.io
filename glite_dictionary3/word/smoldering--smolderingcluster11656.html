smoldering

adjective
barely suppressed emotion, esp. anger
Pronunciation: / ˈsmoʊldərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or expression) showing barely suppressed strong emotion, especially anger or passion.

Examples

smoldering anger
His smoldering resentment was obvious to anyone who cared to notice.
She sat at the table with a smoldering look that betrayed her hidden rage.
There was a smoldering tension in the room after the argument.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: not showing strong emotion or angry feelings
  • synonym – seething: filled with intense but unexpressed anger
