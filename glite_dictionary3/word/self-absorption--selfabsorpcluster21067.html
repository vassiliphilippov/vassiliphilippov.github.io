self-absorption

noun
preoccupation with oneself
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf əbˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
The state of being excessively preoccupied with one's own thoughts, feelings, or interests, often to the exclusion of others.

Examples

deep self-absorption
His self-absorption made it hard for him to maintain close friendships.
The artist's self-absorption was evident in her work, which rarely reflected the world around her.
Too much self-absorption can lead to isolation from others.

Related Terms

  • synonym – egocentrism: the inability to see a situation from another person's point of view
  • antonym – selflessness: concern more with the needs and wishes of others than with one's own
