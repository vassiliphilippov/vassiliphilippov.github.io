authority

noun
an authoritative written work or source
Pronunciation: / əˈθɔr.ə.t̬i /; / əˈθɔrəti / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ə.ti /; / ɔːˈθɒrəti / (BrE)
a person or written work that is recognized as an expert or the most reliable source on a subject

Examples

authority on history
Her research paper cited the leading authority on ancient Greek philosophy.
In legal matters, his textbook has become the standard authority for students.
This biography is considered the authority on 19th-century Russian literature.
Dr. Martin is an authority on medieval literature and often lectures at international conferences.
The museum consulted an authority in ancient art to verify the authenticity of the sculptures.
You should ask an authority in finance if you want reliable investment advice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expert: a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • antonym – novice: a person who is new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • synonym – reference: a book or source used for information
