Mohican

noun
member of a Native American people of the Hudson valley
Pronunciation: / moʊˈhiːkən / (AmE), / məʊˈhiːkən / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian-speaking Native American people formerly living in the Hudson River valley and eastward to the Housatonic River

Examples

a Mohican person
He traced his ancestry back to a Mohican woman born in the 18th century.
Many Mohican descendants now live in Wisconsin.
The Mohican tribe once occupied vast areas along the Hudson River.
