Ojibwa

noun
Native American people of the Great Lakes region
Pronunciation: / oʊˈdʒɪbwɑ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪbwə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the area around Lake Superior, extending from Canada into the northern United States

Examples

an Ojibwa elder
He is studying the language and culture of the Ojibwa for his anthropology research.
Many Ojibwa live in communities throughout Canada and the northern United States.
The Ojibwa have a rich tradition of storytelling and music.
