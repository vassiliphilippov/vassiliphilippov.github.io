to snare

verb
catch in a trap or by deception
Pronunciation: / snɛr / (AmE), / snɛə / (BrE)
to catch or trap someone or something, especially by trickery or with a device

Examples

to snare a rabbit
Many fish are snared in the nets each season.
She was snared by his clever scheme and lost her savings.
The hunter tried to snare a rabbit with a homemade trap.
He tried to snare her with promises he couldn't keep.
Investigators snared the corrupt official using a secret recording.
The clever advertisement snared many unsuspecting customers into buying unnecessary products.
