to demonstrate

verb
show or prove clearly
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛm.ənˌstreɪt /; / ˈdɛmənˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛm.ən.streɪt /; / ˈdɛmənstreɪt / (BrE)
to show or prove that something is true or exists, especially by giving evidence, examples, or experiments

Examples

to demonstrate clear evidence
Her commitment to the project demonstrates her dedication and reliability.
The experiment clearly demonstrated how the chemical reaction takes place.
The study was designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the new vaccine.
Careful testing helped demonstrate the safety of the new medication.
He used statistics to demonstrate the effectiveness of the new policy.
The scientist was able to demonstrate her theory with a practical experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disprove: to show that something is not true or is false
  • synonym – prove: to establish the truth or validity of something by evidence or argument
