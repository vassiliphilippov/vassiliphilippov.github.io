borehole

noun
narrow drilled hole (esp. for exploration)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːhəʊl / (BrE)
A narrow hole drilled into the ground, especially for exploratory, geological, or environmental investigation.

Examples

drill a borehole
Drilling a borehole is often the first step in mineral exploration projects.
The company installed a water borehole to supply the remote village.
The scientists extracted core samples from the borehole for further analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – well: A shaft sunk into the ground to obtain water, oil, or gas
