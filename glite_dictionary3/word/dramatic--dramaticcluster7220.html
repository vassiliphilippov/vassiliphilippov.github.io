dramatic

adjective
powerful and expressive operatic singing voice
Pronunciation: / drəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a singer or singing voice) marked by great power, expressiveness, and a highly theatrical or emotional style, especially in opera.

Examples

dramatic soprano
Casting a singer with a dramatic voice can transform the emotional impact of an opera.
She auditioned with her dramatic tenor, impressing the judges with both power and nuance.
The role calls for a dramatic soprano with the stamina to project over the orchestra.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lyric: of a singing voice that is light, flexible, and expressive
