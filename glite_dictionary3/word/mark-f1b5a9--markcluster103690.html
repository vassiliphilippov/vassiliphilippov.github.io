to mark

verb
make marks or underline text
Pronunciation: / mɑrk / (AmE), / mɑːk / (BrE)
to make a visible sign, line, spot, or impression on a surface (such as drawing, scratching, staining, or decorating)

Examples

to mark a page
Please mark any questions you find confusing so we can discuss them.
She carefully marked each key point with a red pen.
The teacher asked the students to mark the important sentences in the passage.
Before joining the pieces of clay, the potter will mark the surface with a sharp tool.
To prevent the knife from slipping, mark the plastic with shallow lines first.
You should mark the wood to remember where to cut it later.
The childhood accident marked his left hand for life.
The illness marked her face, leaving faint lines across her cheeks.
Years of hard labor marked his body with scars.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blemish: to spoil the appearance by marking with a flaw
  • antonym – erase: to remove a mark or writing
  • synonym – highlight: to emphasize or make prominent in text
  • synonym – scar: to leave a permanent mark or scar on
  • synonym – score: to make cuts or lines on a surface, especially as a preparatory or decorative measure
  • synonym – stain: to leave a colored mark on something, often accidentally
  • synonym – underline: to draw a line under text for emphasis
