to vent

verb
expose to cool/fresh air
Pronunciation: / vɛnt / (AmE), / vɛnt / (BrE)
to expose something to cool or fresh air in order to cool or freshen it

Examples

to vent a room
It's important to vent winter clothes before packing them away.
Please vent the room by opening the windows for a while.
She vented the basement to remove the musty smell.

Related Terms

  • synonym – air: to expose something to the open air for the purpose of drying, cooling, or removing odor
  • synonym – ventilate: to cause fresh air to enter and move around a place or thing
