vetch

noun
climbing plant of the genus Vicia
Pronunciation: / vɛtʃ / (AmE), / vɛtʃ / (BrE)
any of various climbing plants of the genus Vicia, having compound leaves ending in tendrils and small flowers, often grown for forage or as a soil improver

Examples

vetch plant cover
Farmers often plant vetch as a cover crop to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
Purple vetch blossoms carpeted the edge of the meadow in spring.
Some species of vetch are used as animal fodder due to their high nutritional value.
