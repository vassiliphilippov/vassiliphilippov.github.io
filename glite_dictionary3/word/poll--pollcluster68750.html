to poll

verb
ask for votes or opinions
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
to ask people for their opinions or votes, especially in an official or organized way

Examples

to poll people
The organization will poll members to determine their preferred candidate.
The senator polled more than 60% of the total votes in last year's election.
They plan to poll residents about the new community project.
Every year, the organization polls thousands of students about their career plans.
Researchers will poll a representative sample of voters before the election.
The company polled employees to determine their preferences about remote work.
