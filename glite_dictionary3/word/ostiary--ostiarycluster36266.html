ostiary

noun
minor Holy Order in the pre-reformed Western Church
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːstiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪəri / (BrE)
the lowest of the minor Holy Orders in the unreformed Western Church, formerly responsible for the church door, now suppressed by the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

ostiary receives order
Historical records describe the duties of an ostiary during ecclesiastical ceremonies.
In medieval times, the ostiary was responsible for watching over the doors of the church.
The role of ostiary was eventually abolished in the Roman Catholic Church.
