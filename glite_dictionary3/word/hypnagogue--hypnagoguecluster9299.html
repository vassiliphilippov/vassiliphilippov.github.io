hypnagogue

noun
agent that induces sleep or drowsiness
Pronunciation: / hɪpˈnæɡɔɡ / (AmE), / hɪpˈnæɡəg / (BrE)
An agent or substance that induces drowsiness or brings about sleep.

Examples

hypnagogue medicine
Natural herbs such as valerian root are sometimes considered mild hypnagogues.
The doctor prescribed a hypnagogue to help the patient manage insomnia.
The effectiveness of a hypnagogue can vary greatly from person to person.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedative: a substance promoting calm or inducing sleep
  • antonym – stimulant: an agent that increases physiological or nervous activity
