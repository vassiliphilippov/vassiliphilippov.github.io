to breeze

verb
blow gently and lightly (of wind)
Pronunciation: / briːz / (AmE), / briːz / (BrE)
(of the wind) to blow gently and lightly.

Examples

to breeze past trees
A soft wind breezed across the open field at dawn.
Cool air often breezes through the attic windows during the summer.
Palm trees gently breezed in the warm evening air.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gust: a sudden strong rush of wind
  • synonym – waft: to move lightly or smoothly through the air
