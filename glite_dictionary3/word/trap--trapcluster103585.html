trap

noun
something that deceives or ensnares unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / træp / (AmE), / træp / (BrE)
a situation or thing that tricks, catches, or ensnares someone unexpectedly, including an ambush

Examples

fall into a trap
He walked right into the trap set by the fraudsters.
The competition was full of trap questions meant to mislead the participants.
The contract was a clever trap for those who didn't read the fine print.
He waited in silence, perfectly still, until his trap sprung on the unsuspecting intruder.
She realized too late that she had walked into a trap laid by her rivals.
The soldiers set a trap for the enemy patrol in the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ambush: an act of hiding to make a surprise attack
  • synonym – pitfall: a hidden or unsuspected danger or difficulty
  • synonym – snare: something that entangles or traps by deception
