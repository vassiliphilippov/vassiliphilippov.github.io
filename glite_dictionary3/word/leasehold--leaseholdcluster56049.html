leasehold

noun
land or property held under a lease
Pronunciation: / ˈliːsˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈliːshəʊld / (BrE)
Land or property that is held or occupied by lease from the freeholder.

Examples

leasehold property rights
Many London flats are available on a leasehold rather than a freehold basis.
The leasehold was sold to a new tenant when the original lease expired.
They purchased the leasehold on the apartment for a fixed period of 99 years.
