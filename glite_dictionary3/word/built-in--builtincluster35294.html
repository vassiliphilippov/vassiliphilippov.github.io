built-in

adjective
forming an essential part; inherent
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪltˈɪn /; / ˈbɪltˌɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɪltˈɪn /; / ˈbɪlt.ɪn / (BrE)
forming a permanent or essential part of something; included as an integral feature, not separate or added later

Examples

built-in feature
Respect for others should be a built-in value of any civil society.
The new camera comes with several built-in features, such as Wi-Fi and GPS.
There is a built-in advantage to having experience in this field.
Many cars now come with built-in navigation systems as standard equipment.
She admired the built-in bookshelves that maximized the use of space in the living room.
The kitchen features a built-in dishwasher for added convenience.
