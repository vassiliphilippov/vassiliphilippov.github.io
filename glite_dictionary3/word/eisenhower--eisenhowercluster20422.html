Eisenhower

proper noun
U.S. general and 34th president
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪzənˌhaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪzənˌhaʊə / (BrE)
(Dwight D.) Eisenhower (1890–1969), U.S. Army general and statesman; the 34th President of the United States, who supervised the Allied invasion of Normandy and the defeat of Nazi Germany in World War II.

Examples

President Eisenhower
As Supreme Commander, Eisenhower planned the D-Day landings in 1944.
During his presidency, Eisenhower established the Interstate Highway System.
Many historians credit Eisenhower with maintaining peace during the tense years of the Cold War.
