Adelges piceae

noun
insect pest of fir trees
Pronunciation: / əˈdɛldʒiːz ˈpaɪsi.i / (AmE), / əˈdɛldʒiːz ˈpiːsi.iː / (BrE)
A species of wingless aphid-like insect that infests and can kill fir trees, notably the Balsam Fir and Fraser Fir.

Examples

Adelges piceae attacks firs
Foresters monitor stands of fir for the presence of Adelges piceae to limit tree losses.
Infestations of Adelges piceae have caused extensive damage to Fraser Fir populations in the southern Appalachians.
The spread of Adelges piceae in Christmas tree farms has led to increased management efforts.
