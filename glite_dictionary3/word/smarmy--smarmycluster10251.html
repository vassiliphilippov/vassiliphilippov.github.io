smarmy

adjective
excessively ingratiating or insincerely flattering
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈsmɑːmi / (BrE)
behaving in a way that seems polite, kind, or pleasing but is unpleasant because it is not sincere; excessively ingratiating or flattering.

Examples

smarmy smile
He greeted the guests with a smarmy smile that made everyone uncomfortable.
Her boss's smarmy compliments felt forced and insincere.
The car salesperson's smarmy charm didn't fool me for a second.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sincere: genuine, honest, not pretending or insincere
  • synonym – unctuous: excessively flattering or insincerely earnest
