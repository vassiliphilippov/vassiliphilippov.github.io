to sensualize

verb
attribute to the senses
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sjʊ.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
To attribute something to the senses or to treat as originating in sensation.

Examples

to sensualize an idea
His theory attempts to sensualize morality by explaining it as a product of bodily desires.
Some philosophers sensualize all knowledge, claiming it originates solely from sensory experience.
The poet sought to sensualize abstract ideas by linking them to physical sensations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sensual: relating to or affecting the senses rather than the intellect
