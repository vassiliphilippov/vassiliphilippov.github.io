to sibilate

verb
pronounce with a hissing sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪbəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪleɪt / (BrE)
to make or pronounce a hissing or sibilant sound, especially like 's' or 'sh', either in speech or as a sign of disapproval

Examples

to sibilate a word
She sibilated each syllable of the tongue-twister, making the phrase sound almost musical.
Some speakers of Spanish noticeably sibilate when pronouncing certain words.
The actor intentionally sibilated the 's' in 'snake' for dramatic effect.
During the play, the villain was sibilated at by the audience.
She sibilated his name through clenched teeth, anger evident in every syllable.
The snake sibilated a warning as we approached the nest.
He sibilated in annoyance when the dog barked during his speech.
The audience began to sibilate loudly at the speaker's controversial remarks.
When the actor forgot his lines, a few people in the crowd started to sibilate from the back rows.
