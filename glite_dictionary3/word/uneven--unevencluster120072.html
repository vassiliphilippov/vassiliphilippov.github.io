uneven

adjective
not fairly matched as opponents
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
(of a contest or competitors) not fairly matched in strength, ability, or resources, so that one side has a clear advantage.

Examples

uneven match
Many critics felt the match was too uneven to be truly exciting.
The final was an uneven contest, with one team dominating from the start.
The negotiations turned out to be uneven, as one party held all the leverage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – even: fairly matched in strength or ability; equal
  • synonym – lopsided: heavily unbalanced or favoring one side, often used for contests or results
  • synonym – one-sided: unfairly or overwhelmingly favoring one side; not balanced
