to perforate

verb
make holes for easy separation
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜr.fəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fər.eɪt / (BrE)
to make a line of small holes in something, especially to make it easier to tear

Examples

to perforate a page
Many notebooks have perforated pages for quick removal.
Please perforate the form along the dotted line before handing it in.
The tickets were perforated so they could easily be torn apart.
