to pole

verb
propel a boat with a pole
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
to move or propel a boat or raft by pushing against the bottom of the water with a long pole.

Examples

to pole a boat
The boatman expertly poled the raft across the shallow river.
Visitors can pole along the canals to enjoy the scenery at their own pace.
We decided to pole our canoe through the marshy waters instead of paddling.

Related Terms

  • synonym – punt: to propel a boat by pushing with a pole against the bottom of the river
