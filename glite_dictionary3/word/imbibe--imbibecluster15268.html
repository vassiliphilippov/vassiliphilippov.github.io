to imbibe

verb
absorb gas, light, or heat into a solution
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbaɪb / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaɪb / (BrE)
(of a substance) to absorb gas, light, or heat into a solution.

Examples

to imbibe heat
Certain types of charcoal can imbibe gases from the air.
Some solutions imbibe heat when undergoing chemical reactions.
The dye is able to imbibe light, making it useful for solar cells.
