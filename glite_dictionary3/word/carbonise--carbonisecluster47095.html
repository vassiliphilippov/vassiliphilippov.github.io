to carbonise

verb
convert into carbon by heating
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːr.bəˌnaɪz /; / ˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.naɪz /; / ˈkɑːbənʌɪz / (BrE)
to turn a substance into carbon or combine it with carbon, usually by heating or chemical treatment

Examples

to carbonise wood
Certain manufacturing techniques carbonise fabric to make it more durable.
Engineers developed a process to carbonise wood for use in water filters.
If paper is left too long in the kiln, the heat will carbonise it completely.
Some fuels are made by carbonising organic material at high temperatures.
Steel can be strengthened when it is carbonised during manufacturing.
The process will carbonise the surface of the metal, making it harder and more durable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – carbonize: convert into carbon, usually by heating (US spelling)
  • synonym – carburize: add carbon to a metal, especially steel, by heating
  • antonym – decarburize: remove carbon from a metal or substance
