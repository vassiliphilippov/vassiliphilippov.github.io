garrison

noun
troops stationed to guard a place
Pronunciation: / ˈɡær.ə.sən / (AmE), / ˈɡær.ɪ.sən / (BrE)
a group of soldiers stationed at a place, or the fortified place itself, to defend it

Examples

garrison of soldiers
A new garrison was stationed at the border fort to strengthen its defenses.
After the battle, the victorious army left a small garrison in the captured town.
The garrison held out bravely during the long siege.
During the siege, food supplies in the garrison quickly ran out.
The old stone garrison still stands as a reminder of the town's military past.
The soldiers marched into the garrison at dawn to begin their watch.

Related Terms

  • synonym – army: a large organized body of armed personnel trained for war, especially on land
  • antonym – civilian: a person not in the armed services or police force
  • synonym – fort: a fortified building or military post
  • synonym – outpost: a small military camp or position at some distance from the main force
