slur

noun
a curved line denoting legato in music
Pronunciation: / slɜːr / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
(in music) a curved line placed over or under a group of notes to indicate that they are to be played or sung smoothly and connectedly (legato).

Examples

slur over the notes
Don't forget to draw the slur when you copy the music by hand.
Playing the passage under a slur gives it a more flowing, connected sound.
The composer marked a slur over the first four notes of the melody.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tie: a curved line in music connecting two of the same note indicating they are played as a single note
