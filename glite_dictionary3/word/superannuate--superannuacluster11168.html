to superannuate

verb
retire due to age or infirmity
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpɚˈæn.ju.eɪt /; / suːpərˈæn.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pərˈæn.ju.eɪt /; / suːpərˈanjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to retire someone from a job because of old age or physical incapacity

Examples

to superannuate an employee
After forty years of dedicated service, the company chose to superannuate the old engineer.
Organizations often superannuate employees who are no longer able to perform their duties due to age.
She was superannuated last year when new retirement regulations were introduced.
He was superannuated from the police force after reaching the age limit for service.
Many workers fear being superannuated before they are ready to retire.
The company decided to superannuate several long-serving employees this year.
