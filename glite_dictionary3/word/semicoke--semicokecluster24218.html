semicoke

noun
residue from low-temperature coal carbonization
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmiˌkoʊk / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪkəʊk / (BrE)
A solid, carbon-rich residue obtained by carbonizing coal at a relatively low temperature and used as a smokeless fuel.

Examples

semicoke as smokeless fuel
After the carbonization process, a large amount of semicoke was left as residue.
Burning semicoke results in a clean and efficient source of heat.
The factory uses semicoke because it produces less smoke than regular coal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coke: a solid carbonaceous material derived from destructive distillation of coal
