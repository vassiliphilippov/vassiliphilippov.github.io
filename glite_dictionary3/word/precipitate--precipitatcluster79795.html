precipitate

adjective
done hastily or without thought
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪpɪtət / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtət / (BrE)
(of an action) done with excessive haste or without careful consideration.

Examples

precipitate decision making
A precipitate withdrawal of troops could destabilize the region even further.
Critics argued that the government's decision was precipitate and poorly planned.
His precipitate response surprised everyone, as they expected a more thoughtful reply.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deliberate: done with careful thought or consideration
  • synonym – hasty: done with excessive speed or urgency
