lacewood

noun
decorative variegated wood from sycamore or similar trees
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪswʊd / (AmE), / ˈleɪswʊd / (BrE)
a hard, tough, and elastic wood with a sometimes variegated or speckled appearance, often derived from the sycamore tree or related species, used for furniture and decorative work

Examples

lacewood furniture piece
He installed lacewood panels in the office to achieve an elegant, textured effect.
Many luthiers value lacewood for its beauty and durability in making musical instruments.
The cabinet was crafted from lacewood, giving it a unique spotted pattern.
