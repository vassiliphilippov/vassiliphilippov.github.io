to force-feed

verb
make someone eat by force
Pronunciation: / fɔrs fid / (AmE), / fɔːs fiːd / (BrE)
to make someone eat food by using force, especially when they are unwilling or unable to eat themselves.

Examples

to force-feed a patient
Doctors had to force-feed the patient when she refused to eat for several days.
He was force-fed nutritious food after weeks of illness left him too weak to eat.
The activists protested the decision to force-feed prisoners who were on hunger strike.

Related Terms

  • synonym – feed: to give food to a person or animal
