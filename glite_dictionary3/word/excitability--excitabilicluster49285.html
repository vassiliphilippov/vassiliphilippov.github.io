excitability

noun
quality of being easily excited
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily excited, especially emotionally or physiologically

Examples

emotional excitability
Her high excitability often led her to react strongly to minor events.
Muscle excitability is crucial for proper bodily movement and response.
The excitability of children can make classroom management challenging at times.
