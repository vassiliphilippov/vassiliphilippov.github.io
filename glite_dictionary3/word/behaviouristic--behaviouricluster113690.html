behaviouristic

adjective
relating to behaviorism in psychology
Pronunciation: / bɪˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / bɪˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of behaviorism, a theory in psychology that emphasizes observable and measurable behaviors rather than internal mental states.

Examples

behaviouristic psychology approach
Her research is rooted in behaviouristic psychology, often referencing classic conditioning experiments.
Many early 20th-century psychologists promoted behaviouristic methods to study learning and behavior.
The behaviouristic approach focuses solely on observable actions and disregards thoughts and emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – behaviorist: someone who advocates or practices behaviorism
  • antonym – cognitivist: someone who emphasizes internal mental processes; supporter of cognitivism
