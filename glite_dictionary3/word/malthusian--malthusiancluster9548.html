Malthusian

adjective
relating to Malthus or his theories
Pronunciation: / mælˈθuːziən / (AmE), / malˈθjuːziən / (BrE)
Relating to the theories of Thomas Malthus, especially the idea that population growth tends to outpace food supply, leading to societal constraints.

Examples

Malthusian population theory
His arguments were rooted in classical Malthusian theory about population pressure.
Many environmentalists warn of a Malthusian crisis if population growth is not controlled.
The economist presented a Malthusian view on food scarcity and resource management.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neo-Malthusian: relating to modern adaptations of Malthusian theory, especially concerning population control
