Maimonides

proper noun
medieval Jewish philosopher and scholar
Pronunciation: / maɪˈmɑːnɪˌdiz / (AmE), / maɪˈmɒnɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
(1135–1204) A Spanish-born Jewish philosopher, rabbi, and physician who is regarded as the greatest Jewish scholar of the Middle Ages and who codified Jewish law in the Mishneh Torah.

Examples

Maimonides codified law
Maimonides had a profound influence on Jewish thought and law throughout the centuries.
