carry-over

noun
transfer of a learned skill to another situation
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæriˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the transfer or application of a skill or knowledge learned in one situation to another similar situation

Examples

carry-over of skills
Athletes often benefit from the carry-over of strength training into improved sports performance.
The carry-over of reading skills from one language to another can help bilingual children excel.
There's significant carry-over from classroom learning to practical work tasks.
