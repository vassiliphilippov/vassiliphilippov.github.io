to universalise

verb
make universal; apply generally
Pronunciation: / juˌnɪvərsəˈlaɪz / (AmE), / juːˌnɪvəːsəˈlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something universal; to apply or regard something as true or relevant in all cases or situations

Examples

to universalise a principle
Great philosophers often try to universalise specific human experiences in their theories.
She hopes her novel will universalise the struggles faced by people in her community.
The company attempted to universalise its standards across all international branches.

Related Terms

  • synonym – generalize: to make general; draw general conclusions
  • antonym – particularize: to make particular or specific
