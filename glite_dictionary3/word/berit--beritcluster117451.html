berit

noun
Jewish rite of circumcision
Pronunciation: / bəˈriːt / (AmE), / bəˈriːt / (BrE)
The Jewish religious ceremony of circumcision performed on a male child, usually on the eighth day after birth.

Examples

berit ceremony
A rabbi was invited to perform the berit for their newborn son.
Attending a berit is considered an important event in Jewish communities.
The family held the berit according to traditional Jewish customs.
