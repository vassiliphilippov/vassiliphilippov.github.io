prophylactic

adjective
preventing disease or infection
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.fləˈkæl.tɪk /; / ˌproʊfləˈktɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.fləˈklækt.ɪk /; / ˌprəʊfləˈktɪk / (BrE)
intended to prevent disease or protect against harm; preventive

Examples

prophylactic vaccine
Many vaccines have a prophylactic effect by protecting people from future illnesses.
The doctor recommended a prophylactic antibiotic before surgery to reduce the risk of infection.
Wearing masks can serve as a prophylactic measure during a flu outbreak.
She advocated for the prophylactic use of mosquito nets to ward off malaria.
Some vaccines have a prophylactic function, helping to prevent infection before exposure.
The building's security system is a prophylactic measure against theft.

Related Terms

  • antonym – curative: able to cure disease or restore health
  • synonym – preventive: intended to stop something undesirable, like disease, from happening
