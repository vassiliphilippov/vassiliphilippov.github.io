defaulter

noun
one who forfeits a match
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔltər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
a contestant or team who fails to compete in a match or forfeits by not appearing or by violating the rules

Examples

defaulter in a match
After missing three games, the team was labeled as defaulters in the tournament records.
In some sports, a defaulter is automatically disqualified from further competition.
The referee declared him a defaulter when he failed to show up for the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forfeit: to lose or give up something, especially as a penalty
