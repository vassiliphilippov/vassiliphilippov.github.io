unaccountable

adjective
not subject to control or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbl̩ / (BrE)
Not subject to control, supervision, or responsibility; not required to justify actions or decisions.

Examples

unaccountable leaders
He preferred to operate in an unaccountable manner, avoiding rules and scrutiny.
The new agency was largely unaccountable to the public, raising concerns about oversight.
Without checks and balances, officials can become unaccountable and abuse their power.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accountable: required to justify actions or decisions; responsible
