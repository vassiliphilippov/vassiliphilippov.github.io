tunicate

noun
marine animal with saclike body
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːnɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnɪkət / (BrE)
a primitive marine animal with a saclike, unsegmented body, belonging to the subphylum Tunicata, often having a notochord only during its larval stage

Examples

marine tunicate animal
As a larva, the tunicate swims freely before attaching itself to a surface and transforming into its adult form.
Some tunicates filter plankton from the water by drawing it through their saclike bodies.
The scientist collected several tunicates from the rocky tidal pool for closer examination.
