transalpine

noun
person from the other side of the Alps (from Italy)
Pronunciation: / trænzˈælpaɪn / (AmE), / tranzˈalpaɪn / (BrE)
a person living on or coming from the far side of the Alps from the perspective of Italy, especially in historical contexts

Examples

transalpine tribes
Julius Caesar described the Gauls as transalpine peoples in his Commentaries.
The transalpine armies were known for their fierce resistance to Roman rule.
Trade with the transalpine was crucial for the Roman Empire's economy.
