to dichotomize

verb
divide into two groups
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to divide or separate into two distinct or opposing groups or kinds

Examples

to dichotomize opinions
It's unhelpful to dichotomize people as successes or failures.
Scholars often dichotomize historical events as either progressive or regressive.
The debate tends to dichotomize issues into black and white, ignoring complexities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – divide: to separate into parts or groups
  • antonym – unify: to make or become united, uniform, or whole
