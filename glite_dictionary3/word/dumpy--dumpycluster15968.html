dumpy

adjective
short and plump; thick-set
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmpi / (BrE)
short and thick or fat in a way that looks unattractive or clumsy (used for people or things)

Examples

dumpy little person
A dumpy gray pigeon hopped along the window ledge, searching for crumbs.
She felt self-conscious about her dumpy figure in the group photo.
The dumpy little cottage sat at the end of the lane, surrounded by wildflowers.
She was a dumpy woman with a friendly smile and twinkling eyes.
The dumpy legs of the pony made it look awkward but endearing.
The dumpy little house sat at the end of the lane, surrounded by overgrown bushes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lean: thin, especially in a healthy or attractive way
  • antonym – slender: gracefully thin
  • synonym – squat: short and thick-set in form or stature
  • synonym – stocky: broad and sturdily built
  • synonym – stumpy: short and thick or broad, usually in a way that lacks grace
