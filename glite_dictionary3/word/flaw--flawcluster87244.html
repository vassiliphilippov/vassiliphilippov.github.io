flaw

noun
imperfection or fault in an object
Pronunciation: / flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
a small fault or imperfection in an object or machine that makes it less perfect or less useful

Examples

a flaw in glass
Even the slightest flaw can affect the performance of the device.
The engineer discovered a major flaw in the design of the engine.
There was a tiny flaw in the glass that caused it to crack.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blemish: a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance of something
  • synonym – defect: a fault or imperfection that makes something less valuable
