coop

noun
housing cooperative (member-owned)
Pronunciation: / kuːp / (AmE), / kuːp / (BrE)
a housing cooperative: a building or residential complex owned and managed collectively by its members, who hold shares and have the right to occupy a unit.

Examples

housing coop
They joined a coop to live in affordable, member-run housing.
The tenants voted to renovate the coop and improve the shared heating system.
As a new member, she bought shares and received a right to occupy a coop apartment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – co-op: short form of cooperative; a business or organisation owned and run jointly by its members
  • synonym – housing cooperative: a residential organisation where residents own shares and control management
