MIPS

noun
CPU speed measurement unit
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪps / (AmE), / ˈmɪps / (BrE)
A unit for measuring the execution speed of a computer's CPU, equal to one million instructions per second.

Examples

MIPS rating
Some modern microcontrollers operate at only a few MIPS, but are still effective for many embedded tasks.
The new processor can handle up to 500 MIPS, making it one of the fastest in its class.
When comparing two CPUs, engineers often use MIPS ratings as a rough indicator of performance.
