to break

verb
enter illegally or without authorization
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to enter someone's property, whether physical or digital, without permission, often with the intent to steal or commit a crime

Examples

to break into a house
Hackers broke into the database and accessed sensitive information.
She called the police when she realized someone had broken into her apartment.
The police believe someone broke into the office during the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burgle: to enter a building illegally with intent to commit a crime, especially theft
  • degree lower – enter: to come or go into a place
