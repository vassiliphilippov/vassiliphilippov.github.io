Dorothy Hodgkin

proper noun
English chemist and Nobel laureate
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔːrəθi ˈhɑːdʒkɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒrəθi ˈhɒdʒkɪn / (BrE)
(1910–1994) English chemist renowned for using X-ray crystallography to determine the structures of important biochemical substances, including penicillin, vitamin B12, and insulin.

Examples

Dorothy Hodgkin discovered insulin
Dorothy Hodgkin was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1964 for her work on the structure of vitamin B12.
Many regard Dorothy Hodgkin as one of the most influential chemists of the twentieth century.
The pioneering research of Dorothy Hodgkin revolutionized the field of X-ray crystallography.
