to superinfect

verb
to infect an already infected organism or cell
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌsjuːpərɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to infect an organism, cell, or tissue that is already infected, especially with a similar or related microorganism or virus

Examples

to superinfect an infected cell
Researchers observed that the virus could superinfect cells already harboring a latent infection.
The doctor warned that improper antibiotic use might superinfect wounds with resistant microorganisms.
The patient was found to superinfect a previously infected tissue with a second strain of bacteria.
