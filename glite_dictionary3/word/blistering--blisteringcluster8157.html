blistering

adjective
extremely harsh or scathing (of speech or writing)
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪstɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of remarks or criticism) extremely harsh, severe, or scathing in tone or effect.

Examples

blistering criticism
His blistering remarks left the audience stunned and silent.
She delivered a blistering speech attacking the government's policies.
The director faced blistering criticism from both fans and the media after the film's release.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mild: gentle or not severe, especially in speech or criticism
  • synonym – scathing: extremely critical or harsh in speech or writing
  • synonym – vitriolic: filled with bitter criticism or malice
