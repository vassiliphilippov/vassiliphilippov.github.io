tornado

noun
a violent, rotating windstorm
Pronunciation: / tɔrˈneɪ.doʊ / (AmE), / tɔːˈneɪ.dəʊ / (BrE)
A violent, localized windstorm occurring over land, characterized by a rotating, funnel-shaped cloud that often causes severe damage.

Examples

a tornado destroys houses
Many people sought shelter when the tornado warning sirens sounded.
Photographers captured dramatic images of the tornado stretching from the clouds to the ground.
The tornado swept through the town, leaving a trail of destruction in its path.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cyclone: a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure
  • synonym – hurricane: a severe tropical storm with strong winds and heavy rain
