self-aggrandizement

noun
the act of exaggerating one's own importance
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf əˈɡrændɪzmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈɡrændɪzmənt / (BrE)
The act of promoting oneself as more important, powerful, or influential than one really is.

Examples

constant self-aggrandizement
His constant self-aggrandizement made him unpopular among his colleagues.
Instead of focusing on teamwork, she engaged in self-aggrandizement during the meeting.
The biography criticized the politician for his public self-aggrandizement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boastfulness: talking a lot about one's achievements or abilities
  • antonym – humility: the quality of having a modest view of one's importance
