rapacity

noun
predatory behavior; subsisting on live prey
Pronunciation: / rəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / rəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
The quality or behavior of subsisting on live prey, especially in reference to predatory animals such as birds of prey.

Examples

animal rapacity
Naturalists have long studied the rapacity of owls in relation to the local mouse population.
Some species of hawks are known for their rapacity toward smaller birds.
The rapacity of the eagles was observed as they hunted live fish in the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym – predation: the act of preying upon other animals for food
