wormcast

noun
cylindrical mass of soil from a worm
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜrmˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmkɑːst / (BrE)
a cylindrical mass of soil excreted by an earthworm or lugworm, often found on the surface of soil or sand.

Examples

wormcast on grass
After the rain, the garden was dotted with fresh wormcasts on the lawn.
Fishermen often look for wormcasts on the beach to find bait.
The presence of wormcasts indicates healthy soil full of earthworm activity.
