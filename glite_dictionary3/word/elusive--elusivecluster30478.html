elusive

adjective
difficult to catch or achieve
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
difficult to find, catch, or achieve, especially by being skillful at avoiding capture or detection.

Examples

elusive criminal
She devised an elusive plan to escape from the fortress unnoticed.
The criminal remained elusive despite months of intense searching by the police.
The fox proved too elusive for the hunters to capture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catchable: able to be caught easily
  • synonym – evasive: tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, or escape capture
