dialysis

noun
separation of substances by diffusion through a membrane
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæləsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈalɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process of separating substances in a solution by passing them through a semipermeable membrane, which allows only certain molecules to pass through

Examples

dialysis in a laboratory
Dialysis is often used in laboratories to purify proteins from a mixture of substances.
In medical treatments, dialysis mimics the function of healthy kidneys by removing waste from the blood.
The process of dialysis depends on a semipermeable membrane to separate small molecules from larger ones.
