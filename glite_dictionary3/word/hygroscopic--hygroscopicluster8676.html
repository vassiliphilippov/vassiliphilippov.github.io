hygroscopic

adjective
absorbing moisture from the air
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪɡrəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪɡrəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) tending to absorb moisture from the air.

Examples

hygroscopic salt crystals
Salt is a hygroscopic substance, so it absorbs water from humid air.
Some medications must be kept dry because they are hygroscopic and can degrade if exposed to moisture.
The hygroscopic nature of the material makes it ideal for use in desiccants.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – deliquescent: (of a substance) tending to dissolve and become liquid by absorbing moisture from the air
  • antonym – hydrophobic: repelling water or not absorbing moisture
