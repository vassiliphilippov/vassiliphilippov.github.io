to roughcast

verb
apply textured plaster to a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌfˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈrʌfkɑːst / (BrE)
to coat a wall or surface with roughcast, a coarse plaster mixture of lime, cement, gravel, and water, giving a textured finish

Examples

to roughcast a wall
It took the mason all day to roughcast the garden wall.
The builders will roughcast the exterior walls to give the house a rustic look.
They roughcasted the old stone cottage as part of the renovation project.
