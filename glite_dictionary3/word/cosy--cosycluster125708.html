cosy

adjective
suggesting close and possibly secret collaboration
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
(of a relationship or situation) marked by an unusually close and comfortable association that may suggest secrecy or collusion, especially for improper or dishonest reasons.

Examples

cosy relationship
Some journalists accused the mayor of being too cosy with business leaders.
The deal had a cosy feel, raising suspicions of under-the-table arrangements.
There was a cosy relationship between the company and the regulators.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collusive: involving secret or illegal cooperation
  • synonym – complicit: involved with others in an illegal or morally wrong activity
