to scrape

verb
to scratch repeatedly
Pronunciation: / skreɪp / (AmE), / skreɪp / (BrE)
to scratch, rub, or damage a surface (including skin) by rubbing it against something rough or hard, sometimes making a noise or a mark

Examples

to scrape a surface
He scraped his nails along the chalkboard during class.
She scraped at the old paint with a knife until the surface was clean.
The cat scraped at the armchair until threads came loose.
He carefully scraped a simple pattern into the wooden bench with his pocketknife.
The children scraped their initials into the wet cement sidewalk.
Years ago, people would scrape warnings onto cave walls to alert others of danger.
Be careful not to scrape your knees when you climb that tree.
He scraped his hand on the rough wall.
She scraped her elbow when she slipped on the pavement.
