alternation

noun
repeated switching between states or things
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːl.t̬ɚˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːl.təˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the repeated change from one thing or state to another and back again

Examples

alternation of moods
His moods were characterized by an alternation of optimism and doubt.
The orchestra's performance was marked by a dramatic alternation between soft and loud passages.
There is a regular alternation of day and night due to the rotation of the earth.
