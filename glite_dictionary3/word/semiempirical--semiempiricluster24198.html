semiempirical

adjective
partly theoretical, partly observed/experimental
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌɛmˈpɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌɛmˈpɪərɪkəl / (BrE)
Based partly on theoretical calculation and partly on observation or experiment.

Examples

semiempirical method
Her thesis focused on the limitations of semiempirical methods in predicting chemical reactions.
Many semiempirical formulas are used in chemistry to estimate molecular properties.
The researchers used a semiempirical approach, combining experimental data with mathematical models.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – empirical: based solely on observation or experience
  • degree higher – theoretical: based entirely on theory or calculations, not direct observation
