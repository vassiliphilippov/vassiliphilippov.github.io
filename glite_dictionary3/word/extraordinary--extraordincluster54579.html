extraordinary

adjective
holding a special additional official function
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstrɔrdiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrɔːdənəri / (BrE)
(of an official) serving a special or unusual function in addition to those of regular officials, often appearing in official titles.

Examples

extraordinary envoy
As an envoy extraordinary, she had authority beyond that of a regular diplomat.
He was named minister extraordinary with special powers during the crisis.
The president appointed an ambassador extraordinary to represent him at the summit.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ordinary: normal; not having special rank or function
