hematolysis

noun
destruction of red blood cells
Pronunciation: / hiˌmætəˈlɑləsɪs / (AmE), / hiˌmatəˈlɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the destruction of red blood cells, resulting in the release of hemoglobin into the surrounding fluid

Examples

hematolysis in blood samples
Improper handling of blood samples can sometimes lead to hematolysis, which may affect test results.
The laboratory tests confirmed that hematolysis was responsible for the low hemoglobin levels.
The patient's symptoms were traced back to rapid hematolysis caused by an autoimmune disorder.
