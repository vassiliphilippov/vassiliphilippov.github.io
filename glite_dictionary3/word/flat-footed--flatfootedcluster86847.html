flat-footed

adjective
with feet flat on the ground
Pronunciation: / flæt ˈfʊt̬ ɪd / (AmE), / flæt ˈfʊt ɪd / (BrE)
Standing or moving with the soles of the feet flat on the ground, not on the toes.

Examples

standing flat-footed in place
In martial arts, remaining flat-footed can make you less agile.
She walked flat-footed across the room, making no attempt to sneak quietly.
The children were told to stand flat-footed during the performance, not on their toes.
