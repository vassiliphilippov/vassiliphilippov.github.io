miscible

adjective
capable of being mixed (liquids)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪbəl / (BrE)
(of liquids) able to be mixed together to form a single homogeneous substance without separating.

Examples

miscible liquids together
Alcohol and water are completely miscible in all proportions.
Oil and vinegar are not miscible, so they separate into two layers.
The chemist tested which solvents were miscible with each other.

Related Terms

  • antonym – immiscible: (of liquids) not forming a homogeneous mixture when mixed
