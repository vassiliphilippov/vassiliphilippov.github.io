Shiism

noun
branch of Islam recognizing Ali as Muhammad's successor
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiːˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃiːɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the branch of Islam that holds Ali as the rightful successor to Muhammad and rejects the legitimacy of the first three caliphs

Examples

Shiism in Iran
Shiism is the predominant sect in Iran, shaping its culture and politics.
Many followers of Shiism observe particular rituals during the month of Muharram.
The history lecture covered the origins of Shiism and its difference from Sunnism.
