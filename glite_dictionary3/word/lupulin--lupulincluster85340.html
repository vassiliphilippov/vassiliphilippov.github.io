lupulin

noun
powder from hop plant flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈluːpjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈl(j)uːpjʊlɪn / (BrE)
A yellowish, powdery substance found on the strobiles (female flowers) of the hop plant, used in brewing and medicine.

Examples

lupulin in hop flowers
Scientists examined the chemical properties of lupulin extracted from freshly harvested hops.
Some herbalists recommend lupulin for its purported calming effects.
The brewer stressed the importance of using high-quality lupulin for a richer flavor in the beer.
