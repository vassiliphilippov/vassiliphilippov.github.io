lemma

noun
subsidiary proposition used in proofs
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛmə / (AmE), / ˈlɛmə / (BrE)
A subsidiary proposition assumed to be true, used as an intermediate step in the proof of another proposition, especially in mathematics and logic.

Examples

prove a lemma
Before stating the theorem, let me present an important lemma that we will use.
Proving this lemma will help us understand the structure of the subsequent arguments.
The mathematician introduced a lemma to simplify the proof of the main theorem.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – corollary: A proposition that follows with little or no proof from one already proven.
  • degree higher – theorem: A statement that has been proven on the basis of previously established statements, such as other theorems and accepted axioms.
