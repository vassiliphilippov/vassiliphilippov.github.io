to collide

verb
come into conflict; be incompatible
Pronunciation: / kəˈlaɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, interests, or qualities) to be incompatible or to come into conflict with each other.

Examples

collide over ideas
Sometimes personalities collide within a close-knit team, causing tension.
The director's artistic vision collided with the producer's commercial goals.
Their interests collide on many important issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clash: to be incompatible or in conflict, especially in appearance or qualities
  • antonym – harmonize: to be in agreement or compatible
