offprint

noun
separately printed article from another publication
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌprɪnt / (BrE)
a separately printed article or portion that originally appeared within a larger publication, such as a journal or book

Examples

offprint of an article
He requested an offprint from the publisher for his records.
Many librarians archive offprints for specialized research collections.
The scholar distributed an offprint of her article at the conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reprint: a new impression of an article or book, especially a later printing
