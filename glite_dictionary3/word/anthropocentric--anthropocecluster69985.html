anthropocentric

adjective
human-centered perspective
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌanθrəpəˈsentrɪk / (BrE)
regarding humans as the most important element of existence, often interpreting or evaluating things in human terms

Examples

anthropocentric worldview
Environmental policies often reflect anthropocentric values rather than considering the needs of all species.
Many critics argue that our anthropocentric approach to nature has led to environmental problems.
The novel challenges traditional anthropocentric thinking by imagining intelligence in non-human life forms.

  • degree lower – egocentric: seeing everything in relation to oneself
