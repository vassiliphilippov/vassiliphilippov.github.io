shrapnel

noun
exploding shell with scattered pellets
Pronunciation: / ˈʃræp.nəl / (AmE), / ˈʃrap.nəl / (BrE)
A type of artillery shell filled with lead pellets that explodes in flight, scattering its fragments over a wide area.

Examples

shrapnel from explosion
A piece of shrapnel lodged in his arm during the battle.
Doctors removed shrapnel fragments from his leg after the explosion.
The soldiers took cover as the shrapnel from the exploding shell rained down around them.

  • synonym – fragmentation bomb: bomb designed to break into fragments upon explosion
