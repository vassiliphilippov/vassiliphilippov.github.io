thrombectomy

noun
surgical removal of a blood clot
Pronunciation: / θrʌmˈbɛktəmi / (AmE), / θrɒmˈbɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove a blood clot from a blood vessel

Examples

thrombectomy surgery
A thrombectomy may be necessary when medications fail to dissolve a blood clot.
After her stroke, she underwent an emergency thrombectomy to remove the blockage.
The surgeon performed a thrombectomy to restore blood flow in the patient's artery.
