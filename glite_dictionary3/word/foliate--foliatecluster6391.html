to foliate

verb
produce or grow leaves (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.li.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.li.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce or grow leaves.

Examples

to foliate in spring
After being pruned, the plant began to foliate again within a few weeks.
In spring, many shrubs foliate rapidly, covering their branches in fresh greenery.
Some species foliate earlier than others, depending on the climate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – defoliate: to deprive of leaves, typically as a result of disease, chemical application, or environmental factors
