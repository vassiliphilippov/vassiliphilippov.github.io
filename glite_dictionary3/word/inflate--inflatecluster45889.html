to inflate

verb
decompress data (computing)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪt / (BrE)
(in computing) to decompress data that has been compressed, especially to expand a DEFLATE-based compressed data stream back to its original form.

Examples

to inflate a file
The program will inflate the compressed stream to recover the original image.
After downloading, the installer inflated the archive to unpack the application files.
The library is inflating data blocks as it reads from the compressed file.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decompress: to restore compressed data to its original form
  • antonym – deflate: to compress data (the opposite operation in computing contexts)
