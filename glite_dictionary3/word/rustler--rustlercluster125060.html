rustler

noun
livestock thief, esp. of cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌs.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌs.lə / (BrE)
a person who steals livestock, especially cattle, typically by driving them away illegally

In the old West, becoming a rustler was a quick way to get yourself into serious trouble.
The sheriff organized a posse to catch the rustler who had been stealing cattle from local ranches.
