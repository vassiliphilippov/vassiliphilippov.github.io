unofficially

adverb
without official approval
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that is not officially approved, authorized, or recognized

Examples

unofficially in charge
Unofficially, she runs the office despite her junior title.
He was unofficially appointed to manage the team before the formal announcement.
The event has unofficially started, even though the organizers are still setting up.
Unofficially, he serves as the treasurer for the committee.
She acted unofficially as the team leader during the project.
The decision was made unofficially before the meeting even started.
