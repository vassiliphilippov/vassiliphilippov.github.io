root

noun
embedded part of a tooth, nail, or hair
Pronunciation: / ruːt / (AmE), / ruːt / (BrE)
the embedded part of a bodily structure, such as a tooth, nail, or hair, that is attached to and supported by underlying tissue

Examples

root of a tooth
Damage to the nail root can affect how the nail grows back.
Pulling out a hair often hurts because the root is still attached to the scalp.
The dentist found an infection at the root of her tooth.

Related Terms

  • antonym – crown: the visible part of a tooth above the gum line
