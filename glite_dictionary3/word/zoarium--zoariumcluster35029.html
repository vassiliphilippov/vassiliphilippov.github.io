zoarium

noun
colony of zooids
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈɛəriəm / (AmE), / zəʊˈɛəriəm / (BrE)
A colony formed by a number of zoologically similar or identical individuals (zooids) living together, especially in certain invertebrates such as bryozoans.

Examples

zoarium of bryozoans
In bryozoans, each zoarium consists of many interconnected individuals.
Scientists studied the structure of the fossilized zoarium to learn more about ancient marine life.
The coral's zoarium provided shelter for countless small marine creatures.
