to flavour

verb
add or enhance taste
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
To add or enhance the taste of something by using seasonings or other substances.

Examples

to flavour the sauce
The bread was lightly flavoured with rosemary, giving it a distinct aroma.
The chef expertly flavoured the soup with fresh herbs and garlic.
You can flavour the yogurt with honey or fruit to make it sweeter.
