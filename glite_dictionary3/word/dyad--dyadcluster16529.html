dyad

noun
pair of two related items
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌæd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ad / (BrE)
A pair of two entities or items that are closely associated or regarded as a unit.

Examples

a dyad of partners
A dyad can be found in music, where only two notes are played together.
In sociology, a dyad represents the smallest possible social group.
The scientific study focused on the interaction within each dyad of participants.
