crossfire

noun
gunfire from crossing directions
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑsˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire from two or more directions so that the lines of fire cross, making it difficult for someone to avoid being hit

Examples

caught in the crossfire
During the robbery, a bystander was injured in the crossfire between the police and the suspects.
The reporter hesitated to enter the conflict zone, fearing she might get caught in the crossfire.
The soldiers were trapped in the crossfire and had nowhere to escape.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ambush: a surprise attack, especially from a hidden position
