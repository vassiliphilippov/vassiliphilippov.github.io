nestled

adjective
positioned closely and comfortably
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛsəld / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəld / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or object) settled or positioned closely and comfortably against or within something, as if for affection or protection.

Examples

nestled in soft blankets
She found the kitten nestled in a pile of old blankets.
The tiny cottage was nestled among the pine trees by the lake.
The village is nestled in a quiet valley far from the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exposed: not protected or sheltered from the environment or danger
  • synonym – snuggled: settled closely and comfortably in a warm or protective way
