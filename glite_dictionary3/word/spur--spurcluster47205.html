spur

noun
railway line branching from main line
Pronunciation: / spɝː / (AmE), / spɜː / (BrE)
A secondary railway line branching off from a main or trunk line.

Examples

railway spur
Engineers plan to build a spur to connect the factory with the trunk line.
Freight trains frequently use the spur to bypass the busy main tracks.
The new industrial park is served by a dedicated spur from the main railway.
