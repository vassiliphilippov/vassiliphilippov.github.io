to blister

verb
criticize harshly
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very harshly; to attack verbally with severe criticism

Examples

to blister a politician
During the debate, she blistered her opponent's record on healthcare reform.
His boss blistered him for missing multiple deadlines on the project.
The critic blistered the director's latest film for its lack of originality.

  • synonym – scold: to criticize angrily
