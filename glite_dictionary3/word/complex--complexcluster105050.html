complex

noun
psychological pattern of repressed feelings
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pleks / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) a group of repressed emotions, thoughts, and desires that unconsciously influence a person's behavior.

Examples

an inferiority complex
He overcame his fear complex through years of counseling and self-reflection.
Her inferiority complex made social interactions stressful and difficult.
The therapist believed that childhood trauma had contributed to his guilt complex.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neurosis: a mild mental illness involving stress and anxiety, but not delusions or hallucinations
