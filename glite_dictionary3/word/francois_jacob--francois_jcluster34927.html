Francois Jacob

proper noun
French biochemist and Nobel laureate
Pronunciation: / frɑ̃ːˈswa ʒæˈkɔb / (AmE), / frɑ̃ːˈswa ʒaˈkɒb / (BrE)
A French biochemist (1920–2013) known for his pioneering work on the genetic regulation of cellular processes; won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1965 with Jacques Monod and André Lwoff.

Francois Jacob discovered genes
Francois Jacob made significant contributions to our understanding of gene expression and regulation.
