dissident

noun
person opposed to official policy
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (BrE)
a person who opposes official policy, especially that of an authoritarian state or organization

Examples

political dissident
Many dissidents fled the country to escape persecution.
She became known as a leading dissident in the movement for democratic reform.
The government imprisoned several prominent dissidents who had spoken out against its policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conformist: a person who conforms to accepted behaviour or established practices
