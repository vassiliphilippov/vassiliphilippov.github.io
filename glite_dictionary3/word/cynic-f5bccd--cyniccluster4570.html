cynic

noun
person distrustful of others' motives
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪk / (BrE)
a person who believes that people are motivated purely by self-interest rather than acting for honorable or unselfish reasons

Examples

a cynic about motives
Although some call him a cynic, he considers himself just realistic about human nature.
Sarah is a cynic who doubts that anyone ever acts out of genuine kindness.
The company hired a cynic to challenge assumptions and uncover hidden motives.
