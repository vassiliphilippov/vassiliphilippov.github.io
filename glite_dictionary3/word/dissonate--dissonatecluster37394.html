to dissonate

verb
cause to sound harsh or discordant
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsəneɪt / (BrE)
to cause to sound harsh, discordant, or unpleasant

Examples

to dissonate metal pipes
He worried that the metal pipes would dissonate if not tuned properly.
In careless hands, the violin can easily dissonate and unsettle the audience.
The unexpected mix of instruments seemed to dissonate rather than harmonize on stage.
