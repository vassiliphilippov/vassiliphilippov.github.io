wafer

noun
thin disk of bread used in religious ceremonies
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪ.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.fə / (BrE)
A thin disk of unleavened bread, especially one used in the Christian celebration of the Eucharist or other religious services.

Examples

holy wafer at church
During Mass, the priest distributed the sacred wafer to the congregation.
She received the wafer on her tongue as part of the Eucharist ceremony.
The church ordered special wafers for use in its communion services.
