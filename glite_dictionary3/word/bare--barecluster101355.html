bare

adjective
lacking shelter or resources; bleak
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / beə / (BrE)
(of land or a place) lacking shelter, vegetation, or resources; bleak and unprotected.

Examples

bare land
Her apartment was cold and bare, with no curtains or rugs to soften it.
The bare mountain ridge stretched for miles without a tree in sight.
They struggled to survive in the bare countryside, where little grew.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barren: (of land) too poor to produce vegetation; unproductive
  • synonym – desolate: (of a place) deserted and bleak
  • antonym – fertile: (of land or soil) capable of producing abundant vegetation or crops
