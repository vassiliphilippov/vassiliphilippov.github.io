to scamper

verb
move quickly or playfully with small steps
Pronunciation: / ˈskæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈskampə / (BrE)
to move quickly and playfully, especially with small, hurried steps

Examples

to scamper across grass
A squirrel scampered up the tree to escape the barking dog.
She scampered down the hallway, her footsteps echoing behind her.
The children scampered across the playground as soon as the bell rang.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dawdle: to move slowly and waste time
  • synonym – scurry: to move quickly with short, hurried steps
