tranquilliser

noun
drug that relieves tension
Pronunciation: / træŋˈkwɪl.aɪ.zər / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪl.ɪ.zə / (BrE)
A drug used to reduce stress or tension without reducing mental clarity, typically prescribed to manage anxiety or agitation.

Examples

take a tranquilliser pill
After taking the tranquilliser, he felt calmer but still fully alert.
Some people misuse tranquillisers without understanding the risks involved.
The doctor prescribed a mild tranquilliser to help her cope with anxiety.
