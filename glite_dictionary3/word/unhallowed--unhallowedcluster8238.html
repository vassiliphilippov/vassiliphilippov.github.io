unhallowed

adjective
not made holy or sacred
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhæloʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈhaləʊd / (BrE)
(of a place or object) not made holy or consecrated; not blessed or regarded as sacred.

Examples

unhallowed ground
Stories warned of spirits wandering on unhallowed earth where no prayers had been spoken.
The villagers avoided the unhallowed ruins that stood on the hill.
Vampires were believed to rest only in unhallowed ground, far from sacred places.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hallowed: made holy; consecrated; blessed
