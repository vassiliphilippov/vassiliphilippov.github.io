to gum

verb
to apply gum or a sticky substance
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
to cover, fill, or make something sticky with gum or a sticky substance

Examples

to gum paper edges
If you gum the edges of the paper, they will stick together firmly.
She gummed the envelope shut before mailing her letter.
The technician gummed the labels onto the packages for extra security.
If you let sugar sit in water, it will eventually gum together and form a sticky mess.
Residue from the spilled drink began to gum the machine's gears, causing it to malfunction.
The printer often gums up if you use cheap paper.
