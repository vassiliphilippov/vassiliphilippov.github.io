subsoil

noun
layer of soil beneath topsoil
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
the layer of soil beneath the topsoil and above the bedrock, usually containing less organic matter and more minerals

Examples

subsoil layer
During construction, the workers had to dig through several layers of subsoil.
The roots of many plants extend deep into the subsoil to find water and nutrients.
Unlike the richer topsoil, subsoil often has a denser texture and fewer organisms.
