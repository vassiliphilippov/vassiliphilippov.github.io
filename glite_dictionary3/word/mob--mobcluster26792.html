to mob

verb
press together in a crowd
Pronunciation: / mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
(of people) to move in a large crowd, pressing closely together, often in a way that causes disorder or makes movement difficult.

Examples

to mob a celebrity
During the sale, customers mobbed the store, hoping to find bargains.
Fans began to mob the entrance when the band arrived at the venue.
Security struggled to control the crowd as people mobbed around the stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crowd: to gather tightly or press closely together
  • synonym – pack: to fill a space tightly, as with people or things
