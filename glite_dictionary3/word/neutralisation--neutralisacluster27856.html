neutralisation

noun
removal of threat, often by killing (euphemism)
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(euphemism, especially in military or intelligence contexts) The removal of a threat by killing or destroying it, often in a covert operation.

Examples

neutralisation of a target
Officials refused to comment on the neutralisation that took place in the border region.
The military announced the neutralisation of several high-profile targets during the operation.
The term neutralisation is often used to obscure the harsh reality of such missions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – elimination: the act of removing or getting rid of something or someone
