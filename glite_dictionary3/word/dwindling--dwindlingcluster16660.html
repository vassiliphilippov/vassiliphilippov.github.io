dwindling

noun
gradual decrease or decline
Pronunciation: / ˈdwɪndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdwɪndlɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of gradually decreasing in size, amount, or strength.

Examples

the dwindling of resources
Many environmentalists are concerned about the dwindling of natural resources.
She felt a sense of loss at the dwindling of family traditions.
The dwindling of the town's population worried the community leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decline: a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • antonym – increase: the process of becoming larger or greater over time
