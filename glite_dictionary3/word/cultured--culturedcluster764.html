cultured

adjective
grown in a laboratory culture
Pronunciation: / \u02c8k\u028cl.t\u0343\u0283\u025ad / (AmE), / \u02c8k\u028cl.t\u0283\u0259d / (BrE)
(in biology and medicine) (of cells, tissues, or microorganisms) grown or maintained in a laboratory culture medium rather than in a living organism.

Examples

cultured cells in lab
Researchers used cultured neurons to study the drug's effects in vitro.
The technician stained the cultured bacteria to check for antibiotic resistance under the microscope.
Biotech companies often test products on cultured human cells before conducting clinical trials in people.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cell culture: the process of growing cells in an artificial nutrient medium for research or medical use
  • synonym – in vitro: in an artificial environment outside a living organism (commonly used for laboratory experiments)
