ground

noun
electrical connection to earth or zero voltage
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a connection between an electrical device and the earth or another large conducting body, providing a path for electric current and serving as a reference point of zero voltage

Examples

ground wire connection
Always connect the green wire to the ground for safety.
Lightning rods provide a direct path to ground in case of a strike.
The instructions specify attaching the device chassis to ground to prevent electric shock.
