geotropism

noun
response of living organisms to gravity
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːˈɑːtrəˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːˈɒtrəˌpɪzəm / (BrE)
the growth or movement of a living organism in response to gravity, especially the downward growth of roots and the upward growth of shoots in plants

Examples

plant geotropism response
Positive geotropism causes roots to grow towards the center of the Earth.
Researchers studied how microgravity in space affects geotropism in seedlings.
The downward growth of plant roots is a clear example of geotropism.
