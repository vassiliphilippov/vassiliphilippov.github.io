fictitiously

adverb
in an invented or imaginary manner
Pronunciation: / fɪkˈtɪʃəsli / (AmE), / fɪkˈtɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is invented, imagined, or not real; in a fictional manner

Examples

fictitiously create a story
His alibi was constructed fictitiously, with no basis in reality.
The author claimed that the diary had been written fictitiously to enhance the story.
The company fictitiously inflated its earnings to impress investors.
She fictitiously claimed to be a famous actress in order to impress her friends.
The city is portrayed fictitiously in the movie, with invented landmarks and characters.
The novel describes events that occur fictitiously, not based on true history.

  • synonym – fictitious: not real or true; imaginary or invented
