quick-frozen

adjective
frozen rapidly for preservation
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪkˌfroʊzən / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌfrəʊzən / (BrE)
(of foods) frozen very rapidly to preserve flavor and nutritional value.

Examples

quick-frozen vegetables
Many vegetables at the supermarket are quick-frozen to keep them fresh.
The taste of quick-frozen fish is often superior to that of slower frozen varieties.
These peas have been quick-frozen to lock in their nutrients.
