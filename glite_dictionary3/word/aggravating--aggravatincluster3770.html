aggravating

adjective
making worse or more serious
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəveɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Making a situation, problem, or condition worse or more serious.

Examples

aggravating the problem
His constant criticism was only aggravating the tense atmosphere in the office.
Loud noises can be aggravating to people with migraines.
Missing the deadline will have an aggravating effect on the project's outcome.
