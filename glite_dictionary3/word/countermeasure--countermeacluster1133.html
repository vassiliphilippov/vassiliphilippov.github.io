countermeasure

noun
action taken to prevent another action
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌmɛʒə / (BrE)
An action or device intended to prevent or reduce the effect of another action, especially a hostile one.

Examples

countermeasure against attack
After the enemy launched missiles, the navy deployed several countermeasures to defend the ship.
The government implemented new countermeasures to protect against cyberattacks.
Vaccines act as a countermeasure to the spread of infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym – measure: an action taken as a means to an end
