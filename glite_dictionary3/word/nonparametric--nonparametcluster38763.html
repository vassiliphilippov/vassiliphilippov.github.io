nonparametric

adjective
not involving statistical parameters
Pronunciation: / ˌnɒnˌpærəˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌpærəˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
(of statistical methods) not involving any assumption about the distribution of the underlying population or estimation of its parameters.

Examples

nonparametric statistical test
Many statistical tests are nonparametric, requiring no assumption of a normal distribution.
Researchers used nonparametric methods to analyze the survey data.
You may prefer a nonparametric approach when the underlying data distribution is unknown.

Related Terms

  • antonym – parametric: involving parameters in a statistical model
