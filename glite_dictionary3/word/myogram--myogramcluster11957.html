myogram

noun
graphical record of muscle activity
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.oʊˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.əʊ.ɡræm / (BrE)
A graphical record showing the activity or contraction of muscles, usually produced by a myograph.

Examples

myogram of muscle activity
A myogram can reveal abnormal patterns of muscle contraction during testing.
During the experiment, the scientist recorded a myogram to analyze muscle response.
The doctor studied the myogram to diagnose the patient's muscular disorder.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electromyogram: a record of muscle activity detected by electric signals
