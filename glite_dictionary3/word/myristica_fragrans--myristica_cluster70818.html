Myristica fragrans

noun
tropical tree yielding nutmeg and mace
Pronunciation: / maɪˌrɪstɪkə ˈfreɪɡrænz / (AmE), / maɪˌrɪstɪkə ˈfreɪɡrænz / (BrE)
An East Indian evergreen tree, widely cultivated in tropical regions, whose aromatic seeds are the source of the spices nutmeg and mace.

Examples

Myristica fragrans tree
Myristica fragrans is native to the Banda Islands but is now cultivated throughout the tropics.
Many botanists study Myristica fragrans for its economic and culinary importance.
The aromatic seeds of Myristica fragrans are ground to produce nutmeg, a popular spice.

Related Terms
