blade

noun
flat part of a leaf
Pronunciation: / bleɪd / (AmE), / bleɪd / (BrE)
the broad, flat part of a leaf, especially in grasses, as distinct from the stalk (petiole)

Examples

blade of grass
A palm tree's blade is wide and fan-shaped, making it easy to recognize.
He carefully cut the blade from the stem for his plant collection.
The blade of the grass shimmered with morning dew.
