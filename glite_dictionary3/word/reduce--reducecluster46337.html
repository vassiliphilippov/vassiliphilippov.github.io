to reduce

verb
bring to a weaker or lower state
Pronunciation: / rɪˈduːs /; / rɪˈdus / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to bring someone or something to a lower, weaker, or more submissive state or position

Examples

reduced to tears
After months without rain, the drought reduced the once-thriving farmland to barren desert.
The dictator reduced the rebellious region to submission through force.
The new policy reduced the employees to accepting lower wages and longer hours.
After the court decision, he was reduced to the status of an ordinary employee.
The scandal reduced the mayor to a position of disgrace.
They reduced her to tears with their cruel remarks.
After the surgeon reduced the dislocated shoulder, the patient felt relief.
The broken bone was carefully reduced and secured with pins.
The doctor needed to reduce the fracture immediately to prevent complications.
