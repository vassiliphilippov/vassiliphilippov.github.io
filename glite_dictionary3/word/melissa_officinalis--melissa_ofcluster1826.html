Melissa officinalis

noun
herb with lemon-scented leaves (lemon balm)
Pronunciation: / məˌlɪsə ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / məˌlɪsə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A bushy perennial herb of the mint family, native to Europe and Central Asia, with small white or yellowish flowers and lemon-scented leaves, commonly known as lemon balm.

Examples

Melissa officinalis herbal tea
Melissa officinalis is often used in herbal teas for its calming properties.
Gardeners love growing Melissa officinalis because its lemony scent attracts bees.
The leaves of Melissa officinalis can be used fresh or dried in various culinary dishes.
