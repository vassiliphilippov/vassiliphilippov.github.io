white

adjective
having turned white (of hair)
Pronunciation: / waɪt / (AmE), / waɪt / (BrE)
(of hair, face, or skin) having turned white or very pale, typically from age, emotion, or loss of pigment

Examples

white hair
After years of stress, his once dark beard has become almost completely white.
As she aged, more and more white strands could be seen in her hair.
The white hairs at his temples gave him a distinguished appearance.
His face turned white after he saw the accident.
She grew white with fear as she listened to the terrible news.
The child became white as a sheet when the doctor entered the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ashen: very pale from illness or emotion
  • antonym – black: (of hair) being black in color, not affected by aging
  • synonym – blanched: paled, especially from shock or illness
  • antonym – flushed: having a reddish color from heat or emotion; the opposite of pale
  • degree lower – gray: (of hair) having turned gray, often due to age
  • synonym – livid: discolored, ashen or pale, as from shock or illness
