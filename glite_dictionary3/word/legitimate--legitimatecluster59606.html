legitimate

adjective
based on reasonable grounds; valid
Pronunciation: / lɪˈdʒɪt̬.ə.mət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪt.ɪ.mət / (BrE)
based on facts, evidence, or reasonable grounds; justified or valid

Examples

legitimate concern
His fears about the new policy are legitimate given the recent changes.
She made a legitimate argument based on the available evidence.
There are legitimate reasons to be concerned about the project's success.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reasonable: fair and sensible; based on good judgment
  • antonym – unfounded: not based on fact or reason
  • synonym – valid: having a sound basis in logic or fact
