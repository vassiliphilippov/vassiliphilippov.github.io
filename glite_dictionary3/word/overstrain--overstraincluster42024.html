to overstrain

verb
strain or exert excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstreɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to stretch or exert (something or oneself) excessively, often to the point of damage or exhaustion.

Examples

to overstrain your muscles
If you overstrain your back while lifting, you may injure yourself.
She tends to overstrain herself by taking on too many responsibilities at once.
The old bridge collapsed after being overstrained by heavy traffic.
