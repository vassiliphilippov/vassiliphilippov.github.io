to shift

verb
change in pronunciation systematically
Pronunciation: / ʃɪft / (AmE), / ʃɪft / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) to change systematically in pronunciation as part of historical development in a language.

Examples

to shift a sound
Grimm’s Law describes how Germanic consonants shifted from those in Proto-Indo-European.
Some linguists study how sounds shift in regular patterns across related languages.
Vowel sounds in Middle English shifted, leading to the Great Vowel Shift.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mutate: to undergo or cause to undergo change in form or nature, especially of language sounds
