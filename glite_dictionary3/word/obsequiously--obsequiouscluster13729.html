obsequiously

adverb
in a servile or excessively eager manner
Pronunciation: / əbˈsiː.kwi.əs.li / (AmE), / əbˈsiː.kwɪ.əs.li / (BrE)
In a manner that shows too great a willingness to serve or obey someone; excessively eager to please or obey.

Examples

to obsequiously flatter a boss
His coworkers disliked how obsequiously he volunteered for every task the boss mentioned.
She smiled obsequiously at the manager, hoping for a promotion.
The waiter hovered obsequiously at their table, anticipating their every need.
