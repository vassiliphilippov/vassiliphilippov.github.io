Monophysite

noun
adherent of Monophysitism (one-nature doctrine)
Pronunciation: / məˈnɒfəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfɪsaɪt / (BrE)
A person who adheres to the doctrine that Christ has a single, divine nature rather than both a divine and a human nature.

Examples

Monophysite church member
A noted theologian of the time was accused of being a Monophysite by his opponents.
Several Eastern churches had many Monophysite members after the debates over Christology.
The council condemned the Monophysite doctrine as heretical in the sixth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Dyophysite: adherent of the doctrine that Christ has two natures, divine and human
  • antonym – Nestorian: adherent of the doctrine that Christ exists as two persons, one divine and one human
