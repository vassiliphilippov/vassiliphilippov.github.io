to degrade

verb
reduce land level by erosion
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡreɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of land) to be worn down or reduced in level by erosion or other natural processes.

Examples

to degrade the soil
If deforestation continues unchecked, it will degrade the soil and lower the land surface.
Severe flooding can degrade riverbanks and alter natural landscapes.
Years of heavy rainfall have gradually degraded the hillside.
