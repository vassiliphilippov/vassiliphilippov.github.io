Saccharomyces cerevisiae

noun
yeast species used in baking and brewing
Pronunciation: / sæk.əˌroʊ.maɪˌsiz sər.əˈvɪs.i.i / (AmE), / sæk.əˌrɒ.mɪˌsiz sə.rəˈvɪs.i.aɪ / (BrE)
a species of yeast widely used in baking and brewing to ferment sugars, producing carbon dioxide and alcohol
