to hitchhike

verb
travel by getting free rides from motorists
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪtʃˌhaɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃˌhʌɪk / (BrE)
to travel by getting free rides in passing vehicles, especially by standing at the side of the road and signaling to drivers

Examples

to hitchhike across Europe
After college, she decided to hitchhike across Europe for the summer.
Many travelers still hitchhike as a way to save money and meet new people.
They managed to hitchhike from the city to the coast before nightfall.
