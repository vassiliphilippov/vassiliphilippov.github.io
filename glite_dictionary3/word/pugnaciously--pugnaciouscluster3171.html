pugnaciously

adverb
in an aggressive or combative manner
Pronunciation: / pʌgˈneɪʃəsli / (AmE), / pʌɡˈneɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows a readiness or eagerness to fight or argue

Examples

to argue pugnaciously
He replied pugnaciously to every criticism, never backing down from an argument.
She defended her ideas pugnaciously, unwilling to admit she might be wrong.
The journalist pugnaciously questioned the politician during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aggressively: in a forceful or hostile manner
  • antonym – peacefully: in a calm or non-confrontational manner
