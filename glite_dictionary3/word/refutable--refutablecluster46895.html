refutable

adjective
capable of being disproved
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfjuːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be shown to be false or incorrect; capable of being disproved.

Examples

refutable claim
A scientific hypothesis should always be refutable through observation or testing.
His theory is refutable and can be tested by further experiments.
The statement was so vague that it was barely refutable by any evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disprovable: able to be disproved
  • antonym – irrefutable: impossible to prove false
