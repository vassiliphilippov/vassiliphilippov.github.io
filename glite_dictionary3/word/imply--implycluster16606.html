to imply

verb
express indirectly
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ɪmˈplaɪ /; / ɪmˈplʌɪ / (BrE)
to suggest or indicate something without saying it directly

Examples

imply a hidden meaning
Are you trying to imply that I made a mistake?
Her tone seemed to imply that something was wrong, even if she didn't say it outright.
The article implies that the new policy may fail without directly saying so.
By questioning her alibi, the detective was implying that she could be the culprit.
Her tone seemed to imply that the manager was at fault.
The article did not directly accuse him but strongly implied his involvement in the scandal.
