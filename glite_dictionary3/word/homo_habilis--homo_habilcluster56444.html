Homo habilis

noun
extinct early human species
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊmoʊ ˈhæbəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ ˈhæbɪlɪs / (BrE)
An extinct species of early human found in East Africa, recognized for having some advanced humanlike features and believed to have lived about 2.4 to 1.6 million years ago.

Examples

Homo habilis fossils discovered
Compared to earlier hominids, Homo habilis had a larger brain capacity.
Scientists believe Homo habilis used simple tools for various tasks.
The discovery of Homo habilis fossils shed new light on human evolution.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – Australopithecus: an earlier genus of hominids to which Homo habilis is related
  • degree higher – Homo erectus: a later and more advanced human species following Homo habilis
