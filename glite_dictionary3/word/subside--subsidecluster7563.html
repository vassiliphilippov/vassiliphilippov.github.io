to subside

verb
become less intense; die down
Pronunciation: / səbˈsaɪd / (AmE), / səbˈsaɪd / (BrE)
(of a feeling, pain, noise, or activity) to become less intense, violent, or severe; to wear off or die down.

Examples

pain subsides slowly
After the thunderstorm, the winds finally subsided and the streets became quiet.
As the excitement began to subside, people returned to their normal routines.
The swelling in his ankle gradually subsided after he applied an ice pack.

Related Terms

  • synonym – diminish: to become or make something less
  • antonym – intensify: to become or make something stronger or more extreme
