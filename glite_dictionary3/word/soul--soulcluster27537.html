soul

noun
genre of African-American popular music
Pronunciation: / soʊl / (AmE), / səʊl / (BrE)
A style of popular music originating in the African-American community, blending elements of gospel, rhythm and blues, and jazz, especially prominent in the 1960s and 1970s.

Examples

soul music hits
Classic soul artists like Aretha Franklin and Marvin Gaye had a huge influence on popular music.
She grew up listening to soul records from the 1970s on her parents' stereo.
The powerful vocals and emotional depth are hallmarks of soul music.
