modillion

noun
ornamental architectural bracket under a cornice
Pronunciation: / moʊˈdɪliən / (AmE), / məʊˈdɪliən / (BrE)
an ornamental bracket or block placed beneath the projecting part of a cornice, especially in classical architecture

Examples

modillion under cornice
Above the columns, a row of elegant modillions supports the extended cornice.
Several modillions had to be replaced during the restoration of the 18th-century façade.
The architect chose to adorn the building's cornice with carved modillions for a more classical appearance.
