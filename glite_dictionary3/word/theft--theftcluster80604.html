theft

noun
unlawful taking of property
Pronunciation: / θɛft / (AmE), / θɛft / (BrE)
The act of taking another person's property without permission or legal right and with the intent to keep it.

Examples

theft of money
Employees are trained to report any suspected theft immediately.
She was arrested and charged with theft after the incident in the store.
The theft of valuable paintings shocked the whole community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burglary: unlawful entry into a building, especially to commit theft
  • antonym – give: to freely transfer the possession of something to someone
  • synonym – larceny: the legal term for theft, especially in American law
  • synonym – robbery: the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
