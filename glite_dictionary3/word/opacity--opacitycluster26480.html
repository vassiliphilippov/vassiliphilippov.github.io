opacity

noun
obscurity or incomprehensibility of meaning
Pronunciation: / oʊˈpæsəti / (AmE), / əʊˈpæsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to understand because of unclear or obscure meaning

Examples

opacity of writing
Her explanation was marked by such opacity that few listeners could follow her logic.
Many critics complained about the opacity of the novel's language and symbolism.
The opacity of the legal document made it almost impossible to determine its true meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clarity: the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • synonym – obscurity: the state of being unclear or difficult to understand
