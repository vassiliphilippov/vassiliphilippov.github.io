to overrun

verb
to go past a limit or boundary
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚˈrʌn /; / ˌoʊvərˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈrʌn /; / ˌəʊvəˈrʌn / (BrE)
to go beyond or past the intended limit or boundary, or to flow/run over the edge of something

Examples

to overrun the limit
If you overrun the end of the page, your text will be cut off.
The meeting overran its scheduled time by half an hour.
The plane overran the runway during landing because of the wet conditions.
If you add too much data, the buffer might overrun and cause an error.
The river began to overrun its banks after the heavy rainfall.
The saucepan will overrun if you keep boiling the milk unattended.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exceed: to go beyond a set limit
  • synonym – overflow: (of a liquid) to flow over the brim or limit of a container
