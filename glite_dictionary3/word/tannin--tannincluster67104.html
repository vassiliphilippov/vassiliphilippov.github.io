tannin

noun
plant substance used in tanning and medicine
Pronunciation: / ˈtænɪn / (AmE), / ˈtænɪn / (BrE)
a yellowish or brownish organic substance found in certain plants, used in tanning leather and in medicine, and contributing to the astringency of some foods and drinks such as wine and tea

Examples

tannin in tea
Oak bark is a rich source of tannin used for tanning leather.
Red wine contains tannin, which gives it a dry, astringent taste.
Too much tannin in tea can make it taste bitter and mouth-puckering.
