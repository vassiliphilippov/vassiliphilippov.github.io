quantisation

noun
division into discrete units, especially in physics
Pronunciation: / kwɑn.təˌzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kwɒn.tɪˌzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of dividing something into discrete units or levels, especially in the context of quantum theory or digital signal processing

Examples

quantisation of signals
Digital audio requires quantisation to convert analog signals into digital form.
Einstein’s work led to the quantisation of light as discrete particles called photons.
The quantisation of energy levels explains many strange phenomena in atomic physics.

Related Terms

  • synonym – quantization: the act of dividing into quanta or expressing in terms of quantum theory (American spelling)
