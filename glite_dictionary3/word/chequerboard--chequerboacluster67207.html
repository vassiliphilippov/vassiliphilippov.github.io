chequerboard

noun
board with 64 alternating colored squares
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəbɔːd / (BrE)
A board marked with 64 squares of two alternating colors, used for playing chess or draughts.

Examples

chequerboard pattern
A black-and-white chequerboard pattern covered the surface of the table.
He set up the chess pieces carefully on the chequerboard before the game began.
The vintage chequerboard was made from polished wood and ivory inlays.
