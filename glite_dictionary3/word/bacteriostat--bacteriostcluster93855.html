bacteriostat

noun
agent inhibiting bacterial growth
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɪəriəˌstæ t / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriəˌstat / (BrE)
A chemical or biological agent that inhibits the growth of bacteria without necessarily killing them.

Examples

bacteriostat in medicine
Adding a bacteriostat to the solution helped prevent contamination during the experiment.
Some disinfectants act as a bacteriostat rather than killing bacteria outright.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of the new bacteriostat in various bacterial cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – antibiotic: a substance used to kill or inhibit bacteria
  • antonym – bactericide: an agent that kills bacteria
