overture

noun
preliminary event or introduction
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚˌtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətjʊə / (BrE)
an event, action, or proposal that introduces or precedes something more important; a preliminary step or introduction

Examples

overture to a story
The first chapter serves as an overture to the themes developed in the novel.
The peace talks began with an overture from the new ambassador.
The welcome speech was the overture to an evening of celebration.

Related Terms

  • antonym – epilogue: a section or speech at the end of a book or play serving as a comment or conclusion
  • synonym – preliminary: an action or event done in preparation for something more important
  • synonym – prelude: an action or event serving as an introduction to something more important
