to crawl

verb
show excessive submission or fear
Pronunciation: / krɔl / (AmE), / krɔːl / (BrE)
to behave in an excessively humble or submissive way, often out of fear or in order to gain favor

Examples

to crawl for favor
After making a mistake, he started to crawl and beg for forgiveness.
He would crawl to his boss, hoping for a promotion.
She refused to crawl before anyone, no matter their status.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grovel: to behave in a way that shows too much respect or fear toward someone in authority
