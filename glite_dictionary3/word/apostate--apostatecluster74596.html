apostate

noun
person who renounces a faith or cause
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːsteɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɒsteɪt / (BrE)
a person who renounces or abandons their religion, political party, or cause, especially in a way considered disloyal or traitorous

Examples

apostate leaves religion
He was labeled an apostate after abandoning the church to join a rival group.
Once a committed activist, she became an apostate to the movement after the scandal.
The regime hunted down every known apostate and punished them harshly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loyalist: a person who remains loyal to a cause or party
  • synonym – traitor: a person who betrays their country, cause, or friends
