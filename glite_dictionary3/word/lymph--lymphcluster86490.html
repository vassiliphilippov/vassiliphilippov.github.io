lymph

noun
bodily fluid in the lymphatic system
Pronunciation: / lɪmf / (AmE), / lɪmf / (BrE)
a clear fluid resembling plasma, containing white blood cells, that circulates through the lymphatic system and drains into the bloodstream

Examples

lymph circulates
Lymph flows through vessels and nodes, helping to fight infections in the body.
Swelling of the ankle can occur when the lymph does not drain properly.
The surgeon carefully drained the excess lymph from the swollen tissue.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – blood plasma: the pale yellow liquid component of blood holding blood cells in suspension
  • synonym – serum: the clear, yellowish fluid that remains after blood has clotted
