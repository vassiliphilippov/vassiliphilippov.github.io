Succoth

noun
Jewish festival commemorating the Israelites' shelter in the wilderness
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːkoʊθ, ˈsʌkoʊθ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkɒθ, ˈsuːkɒθ / (BrE)
A major Jewish festival beginning on the 15th of Tishri, commemorating the shelter of the Israelites during their 40 years in the wilderness.

Examples

Succoth festival celebration
During Succoth, it is customary to invite guests to share meals in the temporary shelter.
Many Jewish families build a Succoth booth in their backyard to celebrate the festival.
The celebration of Succoth lasts for seven days and includes various rituals and prayers.
