to flap

verb
to act in an agitated or nervous way
Pronunciation: / flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
to show agitation or anxiety, often by making a fuss or acting nervously

Examples

to flap about problems
She tends to flap whenever there's unexpected news at work.
There's no need to flap—we have plenty of time.
Try not to flap if things don't go as planned.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: not showing agitation or nervousness
  • synonym – fuss: to show unnecessary or exaggerated concern or excitement
  • synonym – panic: to feel or show sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety
