array

noun
arrangement of antennas for directionality
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪ / (AmE), / əˈreɪ / (BrE)
An arrangement of antennas or aerials, positioned and spaced to produce specific directional characteristics for signal transmission or reception.

Examples

array of antennas
A phased array is commonly used in radar systems for precise tracking.
Adjusting the spacing in the array greatly improved the reception quality.
The engineers installed an array of antennas to boost the radio signal strength.
