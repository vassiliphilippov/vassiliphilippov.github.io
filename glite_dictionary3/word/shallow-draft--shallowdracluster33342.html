shallow-draft

adjective
(of a vessel) with little draft, suited for shallow water
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæloʊ dræft / (AmE), / ˈʃæləʊ drɑːft / (BrE)
(of a boat or vessel) having a keel that does not extend far below the waterline, allowing navigation in shallow waters.

Examples

shallow-draft boat
Because of its shallow-draft design, the boat could approach the sandy shoreline easily.
The shallow-draft ferry is ideal for crossing the bay where the water is not deep.
They designed a shallow-draft barge to transport goods upriver during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deep-draft: (of a vessel) having a keel that extends deep below the waterline
  • synonym – flat-bottomed: (of a boat) having a flat hull, often with very shallow draft
