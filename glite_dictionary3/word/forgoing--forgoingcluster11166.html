forgoing

noun
the act of giving up or renouncing
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving up, renouncing, or surrendering something such as a possession, right, or privilege

Examples

forgoing a right
His forgoing of the inheritance surprised everyone in the family.
Successful athletes sometimes credit their achievements to the forgoing of personal pleasures.
The forgoing of certain privileges can lead to greater equality in society.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abandonment: the act of giving up something completely
  • synonym – renunciation: the act of formally rejecting or giving up something
