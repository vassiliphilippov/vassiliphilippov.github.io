ideology

noun
visionary or imaginary theorization
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
Imaginative or visionary theorizing, especially when considered unrealistic or speculative.

Examples

abstract ideology
Her argument relied on ideology rather than on evidence or facts.
Some critics viewed his utopian plans as a product of pure ideology.
The proposal was dismissed as mere ideology, lacking any practical foundation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – realism: the attitude of accepting facts and dealing with them practically
  • synonym – theory: a set of ideas intended to explain something
