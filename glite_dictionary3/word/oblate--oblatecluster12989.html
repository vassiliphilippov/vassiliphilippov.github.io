oblate

adjective
flattened at the poles (of spheroids)
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊˌbleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a spheroid or object) having the equatorial diameter greater than the polar diameter; flattened at the poles.

Examples

oblate planet shape
Some planets are more oblate than others because they spin faster.
The Earth is not a perfect sphere; it is actually oblate due to its rotation.
The satellite's data confirmed that the planet had an oblate shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym – prolate: having the polar diameter greater than the equatorial diameter; elongated at the poles
