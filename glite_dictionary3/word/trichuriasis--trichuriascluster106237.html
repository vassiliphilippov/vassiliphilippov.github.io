trichuriasis

noun
disease from whipworm infestation
Pronunciation: / trɪˌkjʊrˈaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / trɪˌkjʊərˈaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation with whipworm (Trichuris trichiura), common in tropical regions with poor sanitation.

Examples

trichuriasis infection symptoms
Children in endemic regions are at greater risk of trichuriasis due to contaminated soil and water supplies.
Public health campaigns in rural areas focus on preventing trichuriasis through improved sanitation and hygiene education.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with trichuriasis after noticing symptoms of chronic diarrhea and abdominal pain.
