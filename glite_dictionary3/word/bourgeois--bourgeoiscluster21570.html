bourgeois

adjective
of or relating to the middle class
Pronunciation: / bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the middle class, especially with reference to materialistic values or conventional attitudes

Examples

bourgeois lifestyle
Her bourgeois upbringing influenced her tastes and preferences throughout her life.
Many consider bourgeois lifestyles to be overly concerned with comfort and material possessions.
The play criticizes the narrow values often associated with bourgeois families.
Critics mocked the play for its bourgeois sentimentality and lack of originality.
She rejected his bourgeois values in favor of a more adventurous lifestyle.
The decor was tasteful but rather bourgeois for his eclectic taste.
His tastes were seen as distinctly bourgeois, favoring comfort and security over radical change.
In Marxist theory, bourgeois values are often contrasted with those of the proletariat.
The revolutionaries denounced the bourgeois attitudes of the wealthy elite.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bohemian: socially unconventional, especially in an artistic way
  • synonym – conventional: based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed
  • synonym – middle-class: related to the social group between the upper and working classes
  • antonym – proletarian: relating to or characteristic of the working class (Marxist sense)
