microeconomics

noun
study of individual economic units
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌɛkəˈnɑmɪks / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˌiːkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of economics that studies the behavior and decisions of individual consumers, households, and firms.

Examples

microeconomics of markets
An introductory course in microeconomics covers topics such as supply and demand and market equilibrium.
She chose to specialize in microeconomics because she was interested in how individual businesses make decisions.
The professor explained how microeconomics differs from macroeconomics in its focus on small-scale economic actors.
