soapfish

noun
a slimy-skinned fish of warm Atlantic waters
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊpˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish of the family Grammistidae, found in warm Atlantic coastal waters of America, characterized by slimy, mucus-covered skin.

Examples

soapfish with slimy skin
Soapfish secrete a protective mucus that makes their skin feel slippery to the touch.
