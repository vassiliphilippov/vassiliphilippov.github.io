rankness

noun
luxuriant or excessive growth
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋknəs / (AmE), / ˈræŋknəs / (BrE)
The quality of producing abundant and vigorous growth, especially of vegetation.

Examples

wild rankness in grass
After years of neglect, rankness had overtaken the once carefully trimmed lawn.
Farmers must sometimes fight the rankness of weeds that thrive in fertile soil.
The rankness of the garden after weeks of rain made it hard to find the path.

Related Terms

  • antonym – barrenness: the state of being unproductive or infertile
  • synonym – luxuriance: the property of being lush and rich in growth
