romanticization

noun
the act of idealizing or sentimentalizing
Pronunciation: / roʊˌmæntɪsɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˌmæntɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something seem more appealing, idealized, or sentimental than it really is

Examples

romanticization of history
His romanticization of the past makes it difficult for him to accept present realities.
The romanticization of rural life often ignores the hardships faced by farmers.
There is a growing concern about the romanticization of war in popular culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – idealization: the act of regarding something as perfect or better than in reality
  • antonym – realism: the practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly
