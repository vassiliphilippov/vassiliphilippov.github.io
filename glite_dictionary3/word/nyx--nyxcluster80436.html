Nyx

proper noun
goddess of the night in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / nɪks / (AmE), / nɪks / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of the night, daughter of Chaos and counterpart of the Roman Nox.

Examples

Nyx in Greek mythology
According to legend, Nyx emerged from Chaos at the beginning of creation.
Artists often depict Nyx as a shadowy figure cloaked in darkness.
In ancient hymns, mortals would pray to Nyx for restful sleep.
