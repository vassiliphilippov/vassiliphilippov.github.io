supposititious

adjective
based on supposition or conjecture
Pronunciation: / səˌpɑzɪˈtɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˌpɒzɪˈtɪʃəs / (BrE)
Based primarily on supposition, surmise, or conjecture rather than on proven or established facts.

Examples

supposititious evidence
His argument was built upon supposititious evidence rather than concrete facts.
Much of the story is supposititious, created from sparse and unreliable records.
The historian rejected the supposititious claims about the king's secret life.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conjectural: based on conjecture rather than knowledge
  • antonym – factual: concerned with what is actually the case
  • synonym – hypothetical: based on or serving as a hypothesis
