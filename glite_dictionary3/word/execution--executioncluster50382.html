execution

noun
putting someone to death as punishment
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of putting a condemned person to death, especially as punishment for a crime

Examples

public execution of a criminal
He was sentenced to death, but his execution was postponed pending a final appeal.
News of the execution sparked protests from international human rights groups.
The execution of the prisoner was carried out at dawn under strict security.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capital punishment: the legally authorized killing of someone as punishment for a crime
  • antonym – pardon: the action of forgiving or being forgiven for an offense
