Constance

proper noun
church council (1414–1418) ending the Great Schism
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.stəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stəns / (BrE)
the council of 1414–1418 that ended the Great Schism in the Roman Catholic Church by resolving papal disputes and promoting church reform.

Examples

the Council of Constance
Historians often cite Constance as a major effort to address corruption in medieval Catholicism.
Many important reforms were enacted during Constance to restore unity within the Church.
The Council of Constance marked a turning point in church history.
