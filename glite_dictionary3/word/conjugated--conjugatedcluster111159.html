conjugated

adjective
(chem.) with alternating double and single bonds
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑndʒəˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an organic compound) containing two or more double bonds each separated by a single bond.

Examples

conjugated double bonds
Benzene is an example of a conjugated hydrocarbon with alternating double and single bonds in its ring structure.
The dye molecule absorbs light because it has a conjugated system of double bonds.
Vitamin A contains a long conjugated chain, which is essential for its biological activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unconjugated: (of an organic compound) not having alternating double and single bonds
