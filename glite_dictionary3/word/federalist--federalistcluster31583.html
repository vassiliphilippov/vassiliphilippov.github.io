Federalist

noun
member of U.S. Federalist Party
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlɪst / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the Federalist Party, a former political party in the United States that advocated a strong central government in the late 18th and early 19th centuries

Examples

leading Federalist politician
Alexander Hamilton was a leading Federalist and a principal author of The Federalist Papers.
During the early years of the republic, the Federalists and Democratic-Republicans clashed over the shape of the new government.
Many Federalists supported the ratification of the U.S. Constitution in 1787.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Anti-Federalist: a person who opposed the adoption of the U.S. Constitution and a strong federal government
