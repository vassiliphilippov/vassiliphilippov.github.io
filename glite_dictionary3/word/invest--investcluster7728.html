to invest

verb
endow with a quality or ability (formal)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɛst / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛst / (BrE)
to formally give someone power, authority, office, or a special quality, often with a ceremony

Examples

to invest someone with power
She was invested with great authority upon her appointment to the role.
The constitution invests the president with the power to veto legislation.
Tradition invests the ceremony with deep symbolic meaning.
The board voted to invest the committee with the necessary authority to make decisions.
The new constitution will invest the president with greater powers.
Traditionally, the ceremony was held to formally invest the monarch with the crown.
He was invested with the powers of the presidency after taking the oath of office.
The board will invest her as the new CEO during a formal ceremony next week.
Traditionally, the monarch invests new knights in an elaborate ritual at the palace.

  • antonym – divest: to deprive someone of power, rights, or possessions
  • synonym – endow: to provide with a quality, ability, or asset
  • synonym – install: place someone formally in an office or position
