to clear

verb
sell to get rid of goods
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
to sell something, especially goods, in order to get rid of them, often at reduced prices

Examples

to clear stock
The company managed to clear its entire warehouse stock during the sale.
The store is trying to clear last season's shoes by offering deep discounts.
They need to clear the remaining inventory before the new line arrives.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dispose of: get rid of, especially by selling or throwing away
  • antonym – keep: retain possession of; not sell or dispose of
