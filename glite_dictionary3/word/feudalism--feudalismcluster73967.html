feudalism

noun
platform-controlled economic system
Pronunciation: / c8fjud0d59l6az59m / (AmE), / c8fjud0d59l6az(59)m / (BrE)
(figurative, modern) a social or economic arrangement in which powerful platform owners or companies exercise near-feudal control over workers, users, or services, extracting value while limiting mobility and choice.

Examples

modern feudalism economy
Many commentators now call the dominant app-store model feudalism, with companies controlling access and fees tightly.
Workers argue that gig platforms create modern feudalism where loyalty pays little and mobility is limited.
Policy papers warn that unchecked platform power risks turning the internet into digital feudalism for consumers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gig economy: a labor market characterized by short-term contracts or freelance work often mediated by digital platforms
  • synonym – platform capitalism: an economic model in which digital platforms extract value from users and workers through control of data, access, and fees
