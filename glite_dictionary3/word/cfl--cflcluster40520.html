CFL

noun
compact fluorescent lamp; energy-saving light bulb
Pronunciation: / si ɛf ɛl / (AmE), / siː ɛf ɛl / (BrE)
an energy-saving light bulb that uses a compact fluorescent tube to produce light, commonly used as an alternative to incandescent bulbs

Examples

CFL light bulb
A single CFL can last much longer than a traditional incandescent bulb.
The environmental impact of disposing of a broken CFL is a concern due to its mercury content.
We replaced all the old bulbs with CFL units to save on electricity bills.
