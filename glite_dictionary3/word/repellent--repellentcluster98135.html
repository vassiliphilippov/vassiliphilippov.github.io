repellent

noun
the power to repel
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
the power or quality of repelling; a force or property that drives something away or prevents approach

Examples

social repellent
Her attitude acted as a social repellent, discouraging people from approaching her.
The chemical had a strong repellent that kept insects away from the crops.
This lotion contains a natural repellent effective against mosquitoes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – attraction: the act or power of drawing toward oneself
