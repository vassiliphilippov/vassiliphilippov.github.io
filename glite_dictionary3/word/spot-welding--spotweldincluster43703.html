spot-welding

noun
joining metals by welding at points
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑt ˌwɛldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɒt ˌwɛldɪŋ / (BrE)
A method of joining metal surfaces by welding them together at a series of small, closely spaced points.

Examples

spot-welding metal sheets
Automobile manufacturers often rely on spot-welding for assembling car bodies.
Proper spot-welding ensures strong and reliable joints in sheet metal fabrication.
The technician used spot-welding to attach the metal panels quickly and efficiently.
