to snick

verb
hit with the edge of the bat (cricket)
Pronunciation: / snɪk / (AmE), / snɪk / (BrE)
(in cricket) to hit the ball with a glancing blow from the edge of the bat, often resulting in a catch behind the wicket.

Examples

to snick the ball
He snicked the delivery, resulting in a sharp catch at slip.
She tried not to snick the ball, but it brushed the edge of her bat.
The batsman managed to snick the ball just past the wicketkeeper's gloves.

  • synonym – edge: to strike the ball with the edge of the cricket bat
