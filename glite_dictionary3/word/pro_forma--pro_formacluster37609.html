pro forma

adjective
adjusted or projected financial figures
Pronunciation: / pro8a c8f54rm59 / (AmE), / pr598a c8f54d0m59 / (BrE)
(in business and accounting) describing financial statements or figures that are hypothetical, adjusted, or projected and do not represent actual historical results.

Examples

pro forma financial statement
The company reported pro forma revenue that excluded one-off costs to show underlying growth.
Analysts adjusted their models after reviewing the firm's pro forma statements for next quarter.
Investors were sceptical when management presented pro forma earnings that removed restructuring expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adjusted earnings: earnings that have been modified to exclude certain items to show underlying performance
  • synonym – projected: estimated or forecasted values for future periods
