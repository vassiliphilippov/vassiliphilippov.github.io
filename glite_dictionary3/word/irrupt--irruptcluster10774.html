to irrupt

verb
increase rapidly or suddenly (esp. of populations)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of a population or group) to increase rapidly and in an uncontrolled or sudden manner, often after a period of stability.

Examples

to irrupt in numbers
After several years of scarcity, the lemming population suddenly irrupted across the tundra.
Ecologists observed that the locusts periodically irrupt, causing widespread crop damage.
Invasive plants can irrupt when environmental conditions favor their rapid growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erupt: to burst out or break forth suddenly, often used for volcanoes or violent outbursts
