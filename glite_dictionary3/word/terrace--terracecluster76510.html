to terrace

verb
form land into step-like levels
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
to form (land) into flat, level areas resembling steps, typically for the purpose of cultivation or landscaping

Examples

to terrace a hillside
Farmers terrace the hillsides to prevent erosion and create more arable land.
The ancient civilization terraced the slopes to maximize their agricultural output.
They are currently terracing the steep garden to plant rows of vegetables.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flatten: to make flat or flatter
  • synonym – level: to make a surface or area flat
