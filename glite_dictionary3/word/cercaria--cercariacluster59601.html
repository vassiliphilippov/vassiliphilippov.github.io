cercaria

noun
larval stage of a trematode worm
Pronunciation: / sərˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / sɜːˈkeəriə / (BrE)
the free-swimming, tadpole-shaped larval stage of a trematode worm, which possesses a tail that is lost upon maturing into the adult form

Examples

swimming cercaria stage
Scientists studied the behavior of the cercaria as it left the snail host.
