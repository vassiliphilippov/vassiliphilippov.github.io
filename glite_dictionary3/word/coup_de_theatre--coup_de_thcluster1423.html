coup de theatre

noun
sudden dramatic event in theatre
Pronunciation: / ku də teɪˈɑtrə / (AmE), / kuː də teɪˈɑːtrə / (BrE)
an unexpected and dramatic turn of events in a play or narrative, often used to impress or surprise the audience

Examples

a coup de theatre surprises audience
A sudden confession provided a coup de theatre in the second act.
Directors often strive for a coup de theatre to keep viewers engaged.
The play's ending was a true coup de theatre that left the audience speechless.
