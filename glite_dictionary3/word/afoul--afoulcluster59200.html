afoul

adjective
in a tangled or entangled state
Pronunciation: / əˈfaʊl / (AmE), / əˈfaʊl / (BrE)
in a state of entanglement or collision, especially of lines, ropes, or sails on a ship; involved in conflict or difficulty.

Examples

ropes afoul on deck
Several ropes on the deck were afoul and had to be carefully untangled.
The fishing nets became afoul during the storm, causing a long delay.
The ship sailed into the harbor with its flags hanging afoul after the rough sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear: free from obstruction or entanglement
  • synonym – entangled: twisted together in a confused mass
