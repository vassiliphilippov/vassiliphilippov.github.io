idiosyncrasy

noun
distinctive personal characteristic or habit
Pronunciation: / ˌɪdi.oʊˈsɪŋ.krə.si / (AmE), / ˌɪdi.əˈsɪŋ.krə.si / (BrE)
A distinctive or peculiar characteristic, habit, or mannerism unique to an individual.

Examples

personal idiosyncrasy
Everyone has at least one idiosyncrasy that makes them unique.
Her habit of singing to plants is just one idiosyncrasy among many.
The chef’s idiosyncrasy of wearing mismatched socks became his trademark.
