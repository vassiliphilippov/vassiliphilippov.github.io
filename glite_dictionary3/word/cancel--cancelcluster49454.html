to cancel

verb
make something invalid or void
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl / (BrE)
to make something no longer valid, active, or in effect (such as a ticket, law, or entry)

Examples

to cancel a ticket

Please cancel the order if payment is not received within a week.
The bank may cancel your check if there is a mistake on it.
A court can cancel a law if it is found unconstitutional.
Due to unforeseen circumstances, the company had to cancel the contract with their supplier.
The government decided to cancel the outdated regulations after much debate.
If you make a mistake, just cancel the entry by clicking the delete button.
She asked the company to cancel her subscription immediately.
The document will cancel any previous agreements between the parties.
