wedge

noun
piece of material used to force things apart
Pronunciation: / wɛdʒ / (AmE), / wɛdʒ / (BrE)
a piece of solid material with a thick end that tapers to a thin edge, used for forcing things apart, securing, splitting, or as a block; also, any object or piece with a wedge (triangular) shape

Examples

a wedge of wood
A small wedge of metal was placed between the rocks to pry them apart.
He used a wedge of wood to hold the door open.
The axe blade acts as a wedge to split the logs.
She placed a wedge of cheese on the plate, its triangular shape clearly visible.
The carpenter used a wooden wedge to split the log apart.
The doorstop had the form of a wedge, keeping the door from closing.
A wooden wedge was inserted under the door to keep it open.
Make sure to use a wedge when parking on a slope to prevent accidents.
The mechanic placed a wedge behind the car's wheel to stop it rolling.

Related Terms

  • antonym – block: a solid piece of material with flat surfaces, often rectangular
  • synonym – chock: a block or wedge placed under an object to prevent movement
  • degree higher – prism: a geometric solid with two identical ends and flat sides
