openbill

noun
type of stork with distinctive bill
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.pən.bɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən.bɪl / (BrE)
A stork with a bill whose upper and lower parts touch only at the base and tip, commonly found in Africa and Asia.

Examples

an openbill stork
Birdwatchers were excited to spot an Asian openbill near the river.
The African openbill feeds primarily on snails in wetlands.
The unique beak of the openbill helps it extract its prey from shells.
