a.m.

adverb
before noon; time indicator
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
used with clock times to show the time is between midnight and noon (before 12:00)

Examples

a.m. sharp
Our appointment is set for 7 a.m. tomorrow.
She usually exercises early in the a.m..
The train is scheduled to arrive at 9:30 a.m..
Her flight departs at 7:30 a.m. sharp.
I usually wake up at 6 a.m. on weekdays.
The meeting is scheduled for 10 a.m. tomorrow.
