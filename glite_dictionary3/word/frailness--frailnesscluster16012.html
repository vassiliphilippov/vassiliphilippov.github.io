frailness

noun
state of being weak or delicate
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfreɪlnəs / (BrE)
the state of being weak or delicate in health or body, especially as a result of old age

Examples

physical frailness
After his illness, a certain frailness lingered in his voice and movements.
The frailness of her grandfather became more evident during the cold winter months.
The doctor was concerned about the frailness of the elderly patient's bones.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fragility: the quality of being easily broken or damaged; delicacy
  • antonym – robustness: the quality of being strong and healthy; sturdiness
