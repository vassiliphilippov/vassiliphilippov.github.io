to euphemize

verb
use a euphemism
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːfəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːfɪˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to refer to something using a mild or indirect word or expression instead of one considered too harsh or blunt

Examples

to euphemize bad news
Politicians often euphemize failures to avoid public backlash.
Some people euphemize unpleasant topics to make them sound less harsh.
The company chose to euphemize layoffs by calling them 'workforce adjustments'.

  • synonym – euphemism: a mild or indirect word substituted for one considered harsh or blunt
