swarm

noun
large moving crowd
Pronunciation: / swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
a large group of people or things moving together in a busy or uncontrolled way

Examples

swarm of people
A swarm of reporters gathered outside the courthouse waiting for a statement.
A swarm of tourists filled the museum lobby in the morning.
Security struggled to control the swarm rushing toward the stage after the concert.
