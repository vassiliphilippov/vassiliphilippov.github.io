camachile

noun
thorny tropical American tree with edible pods
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɑːtʃiːl / (AmE), / kəˈmæʃɪli / (BrE)
A thorny tropical American tree (Pithecellobium dulce) with yellow flowers, sickle-shaped edible pods, and valued for its timber, dye, and gum.

Examples

camachile tree shade
A row of camachile trees lined the rural road, providing shade and fruit for the travelers.
Farmers harvest the yellow dye from the bark of the camachile tree.
The camachile is often cultivated for its sweet, edible pods in tropical countries.
