determinism

noun
philosophical doctrine that events are predetermined
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɝːmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
(philosophy) The theory that all events, including human actions, are determined by causes external to the will, and that individuals have no real power to choose or control their actions.

Examples

philosophical determinism
She questioned whether determinism leaves any room for moral responsibility.
Some scientists adopt determinism as a framework for understanding natural laws.
The concept of determinism has sparked centuries of philosophical debate about human freedom.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fatalism: belief that all events are predetermined and inevitable
  • antonym – free will: the power of acting without constraint; making choices
