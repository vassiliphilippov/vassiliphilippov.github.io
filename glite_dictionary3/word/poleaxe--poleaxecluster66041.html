to poleaxe

verb
to strike down forcefully
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊˌlæks / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlˌæks / (BrE)
to strike down or knock someone to the ground as if with a heavy weapon

Examples

to poleaxe an opponent
She felt as though she had been poleaxed by the shocking announcement.
The boxer was poleaxed by a powerful right hook.
The news of his dismissal poleaxed him into silence.
