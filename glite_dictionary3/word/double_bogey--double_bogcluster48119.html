to double bogey

verb
score two over par (golf)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
(in golf) to score two strokes over par for a hole.

Examples

double bogey the seventh hole
After missing a short putt, he double bogeyed the par-three seventh hole.
If you double bogey the easiest hole, it can ruin your entire round.
She was disappointed to double bogey the final hole after playing so well all day.
