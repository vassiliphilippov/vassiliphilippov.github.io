to represent

verb
describe or present as an example
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to describe, present, or show something as an example or in a particular way

Examples

to represent a situation
In his speech, he represented the city as a model of sustainability.
She represented the theory as a breakthrough in modern science.
The historian represented the battle as a turning point in the war.
Each team represents a different region of the country.
The painting represents the style of early Renaissance art.
This statue represents the ideals of freedom and unity.
He tried to represent the feeling of joy in his poem.
It is hard to represent such complex ideas in a single picture.
Some theories represent knowledge as mental images or concepts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – depict: to show or describe something in words or pictures
  • synonym – embody: to be an expression or give a tangible form to an idea, quality, or feeling
  • synonym – exemplify: to be a typical example of something
  • antonym – misrepresent: to give a false or misleading account
  • synonym – present: to bring something before someone's attention or to the mind
  • synonym – symbolize: to represent or stand for something
