diplomatic

adjective
tactful; skilled in handling sensitive matters
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (BrE)
having or showing skill in dealing with people or sensitive situations without causing offense; tactful.

Examples

diplomatic answer
He gave a diplomatic response to avoid offending either side in the dispute.
It's important to be diplomatic when offering criticism to your colleagues.
She handled the difficult conversation in a very diplomatic manner, avoiding any hurt feelings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – blunt: uncompromisingly forthright and direct
  • synonym – tactful: showing skill and sensitivity in dealing with others
