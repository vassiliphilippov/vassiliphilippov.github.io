John Lackland

proper noun
King of England (1199–1216); signer of Magna Carta
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈlækˌlænd / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈlak.lənd / (BrE)
(1167–1216) King of England from 1199 to 1216, the youngest son of Henry II, known for signing the Magna Carta after pressure from the barons and for losing English possessions in France.

Examples

John Lackland signed charter
John Lackland was forced by his barons to put his seal on the Magna Carta in 1215.
After the death of Richard I, John Lackland ascended the English throne.
Many historians consider John Lackland one of England's most controversial monarchs.
