extenuating

adjective
making an offence seem less serious
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnjuːeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of factors or circumstances) serving to lessen the severity or culpability of an action; making an offence seem less serious.

Examples

extenuating circumstances
His actions, while wrong, were influenced by extenuating factors beyond his control.
If there are any extenuating reasons for your lateness, please explain them to the committee.
The judge considered the extenuating circumstances before making her decision.
