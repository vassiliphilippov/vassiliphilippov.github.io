stare

noun
a fixed, prolonged look
Pronunciation: / stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə / (BrE)
A long and fixed look with eyes wide open, often showing surprise, curiosity, or hostility.

Examples

a cold stare
After the embarrassing incident, she felt everyone's stare upon her.
He gave me a cold stare when I walked into the room.
The stranger's intense stare made him feel uneasy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gaze: a steady and intent look
  • degree lower – glance: a quick look
