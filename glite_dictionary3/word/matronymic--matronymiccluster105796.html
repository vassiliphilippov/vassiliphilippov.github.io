matronymic

noun
name from mother's or maternal ancestor's name
Pronunciation: / ˌmætrəˈnɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˌmætrəʊˈnɪmɪk / (BrE)
A name derived from the name of one's mother or a maternal ancestor.

Examples

matronymic surname
Her last name is a matronymic, passed down from her mother's side of the family.
Historical records sometimes list both a patronymic and a matronymic for each individual.
In some cultures, a matronymic is used instead of a patronymic to reflect maternal lineage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – patronymic: a name derived from the name of one's father or a paternal ancestor
