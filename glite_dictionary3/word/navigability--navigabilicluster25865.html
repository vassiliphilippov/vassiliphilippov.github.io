navigability

noun
suitability for passage by ships or aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˌnævəɡəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌnavɪɡəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being suitable for the passage of ships or aircraft; the state of being navigable.

Examples

navigability of a river
Engineers conducted a survey to determine the navigability of the canal for large freight vessels.
The navigability of the river was crucial for the town's development as a trade center.
The treaty addresses issues concerning the navigability of international waterways for military aircraft.

Related Terms

  • synonym – navigable: (of a waterway or route) able to be sailed on or through by ships or boats
