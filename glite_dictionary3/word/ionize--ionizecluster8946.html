to ionize

verb
convert into ions
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪəˌnaɪz /; / ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to cause an atom or molecule to become an ion, or to become an ion, by gaining or losing electrons

Examples

to ionize a gas
High energy radiation can ionize gases in the upper atmosphere.
The chemist used electricity to ionize the sample solution.
Water molecules can ionize under certain conditions, producing hydrogen and hydroxide ions.
Certain gases will ionize readily when exposed to high-voltage electricity.
In mass spectrometry, molecules are often ionized to facilitate detection.
When heated sufficiently, the air between the electrodes will ionize and conduct electricity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discharge: to neutralize an ion by gaining or losing electrons; opposite of ionize
  • synonym – ionise: to become converted into ions (British spelling)
