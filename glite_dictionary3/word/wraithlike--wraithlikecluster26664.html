wraithlike

adjective
ghostly or insubstantial in appearance
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪθˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈreɪθˌlaɪk / (BrE)
having a thin, shadowy, or insubstantial appearance; resembling a wraith or ghost.

Examples

wraithlike figure
A wraithlike mist curled around the old gravestones at dawn.
The dancers moved in a wraithlike manner across the dimly lit stage.
Under the pale moonlight, his features were wraithlike and unclear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ghostly: having qualities of a ghost; eerie or spectral
  • antonym – substantial: having substance; real and solid, the opposite of insubstantial
