regular

noun
member of the regular army
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
a soldier who serves as a member of a country's permanent, professional army, as opposed to reserve forces or militias

Examples

regular soldier
He served as a regular in the army for ten years before retiring.
The regulars were deployed to the conflict zone alongside reservists.
Unlike the volunteers, the regular soldiers had undergone extensive training.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reservist: a soldier belonging to a reserve force
