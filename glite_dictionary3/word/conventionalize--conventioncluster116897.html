to conventionalize

verb
make conform to conventions or norms
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something follow established conventions, styles, or customs

Examples

to conventionalize a design
Many artists conventionalize natural forms to create patterns in their work.
Over time, language tends to conventionalize expressions that were once novel.
The architect chose to conventionalize the design, adhering to industry standards.
Company protocol has conventionalized even casual conversations among employees.
Some artists refuse to conventionalize their work, preferring innovative techniques.
The new dance form was quickly conventionalized to suit traditional audiences.

  • synonym – conform: to comply with rules, standards, or laws
  • antonym – individualize: to make something distinctive or individual
  • synonym – stylize: to design or represent something according to a style rather than naturalistic depiction
