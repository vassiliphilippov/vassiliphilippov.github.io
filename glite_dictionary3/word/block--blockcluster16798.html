to block

verb
impede movement (esp. in sports)
Pronunciation: / blɑk / (AmE), / blɒk / (BrE)
to impede or stop the movement or progress of an opponent, a ball, or something similar, especially in sports or games

Examples

to block a shot
She jumped up to block her opponent's serve during the volleyball match.
The defender blocked the shot at the last second, saving his team a goal.
The player skillfully blocked the ball, preventing it from reaching the net.

Related Terms

  • antonym – allow: to let something happen or proceed; antonym in the context of letting through
  • synonym – obstruct: to block or close up by an obstacle; synonym in the context of preventing movement
