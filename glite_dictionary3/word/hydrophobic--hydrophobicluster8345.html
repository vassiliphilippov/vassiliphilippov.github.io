hydrophobic

adjective
repelling or not absorbing water
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
tending to repel or fail to mix with water; not absorbing or dissolving in water

Examples

hydrophobic surface coating
Some fabrics are treated to become hydrophobic so they resist stains and moisture.
The duck's feathers are naturally hydrophobic, keeping it dry in the water.
This chemical coating is highly hydrophobic and prevents water from adhering to the surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hydrophilic: tending to attract and mix with water
  • synonym – water-repellent: resistant to absorbing water
