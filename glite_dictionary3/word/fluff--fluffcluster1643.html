fluff

noun
unimportant or trivial material
Pronunciation: / flʌf / (AmE), / flʌf / (BrE)
unimportant or trivial material, especially in writing, speech, or broadcast content

Examples

fluff in a magazine
Don't waste your time reading all that fluff in the magazine.
Her speech sounded good but was full of fluff and lacked substance.
The report was mostly fluff with very little real analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nonsense: ideas or words that have no meaning or value
  • antonym – substance: the quality of being important, valid, or significant
