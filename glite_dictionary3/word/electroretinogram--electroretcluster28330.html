electroretinogram

noun
test recording retinal electrical activity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlektroʊrəˈtɪnəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ɪˌlektrəʊˌretɪnəˈɡram / (BrE)
a test that records the electrical responses of the retina when exposed to light, used to assess retinal function

Examples

After the electroretinogram, the ophthalmologist explained the results to the patient.
