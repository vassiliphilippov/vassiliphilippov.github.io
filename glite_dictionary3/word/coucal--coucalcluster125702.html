coucal

noun
ground-dwelling Old World cuckoo
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːkəl / (AmE), / ˈkuːkəl / (BrE)
A ground-dwelling cuckoo of the Old World, noted for its long, dagger-like hind claw.

Examples

coucal bird species
A coucal was spotted in the tall grasses near the riverbank early this morning.
The distinctive call of the coucal can be heard at dawn in many Asian wetlands.
Unlike most cuckoos, the coucal builds its own nest rather than laying eggs in other birds' nests.
