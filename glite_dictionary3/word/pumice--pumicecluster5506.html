pumice

noun
light, porous volcanic rock used as abrasive
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌ.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌm.ɪs / (BrE)
a light, porous volcanic rock formed when lava cools rapidly, often used as an abrasive for cleaning, polishing, or smoothing surfaces

Examples

pumice stone
She used a pumice stone to smooth the rough skin on her heels.
The pumice is so light that it can float on water.
Volcanic ash and pumice covered the village after the eruption.
