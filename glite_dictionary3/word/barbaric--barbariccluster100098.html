barbaric

adjective
extravagantly bold or crudely rich, lacking refinement
Pronunciation: / bɑˈbærɪk / (AmE), / bɑːˈbærɪk / (BrE)
(of style or decoration) extravagantly bold, unrestrained, and crudely rich or elaborate; lacking refinement.

Examples

barbaric style
His paintings were known for their barbaric colors and intense energy.
The architect's choice of barbaric ornamentation clashed with the delicate structure of the building.
The room was filled with a barbaric display of gold, velvet, and crystal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ornate: elaborately decorated
  • antonym – refined: elegant and cultured in appearance, manner, or taste
