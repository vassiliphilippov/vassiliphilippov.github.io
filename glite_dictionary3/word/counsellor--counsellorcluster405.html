counsellor

noun
lawyer; legal advisor
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl.ə / (BrE)
A lawyer who gives legal advice or represents clients in a court of law.

Examples

counsellor in court
She consulted her counsellor before making any legal decisions.
The defense counsellor presented a compelling argument to the jury.
The judge addressed the counsellor directly during the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attorney: a person appointed to act for another in legal matters; lawyer
  • synonym – barrister: a lawyer entitled to practice as an advocate, particularly in higher courts
  • synonym – solicitor: a lawyer who gives legal advice and prepares legal documents, especially in Britain
