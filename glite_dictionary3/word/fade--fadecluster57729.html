fade

noun
golf shot curving right (right-handed)
Pronunciation: / feɪd / (AmE), / feɪd / (BrE)
(in golf) a shot that curves slightly to the right for a right-handed player, as a result of sidespin.

Examples

a fade shot
He adjusted his swing to achieve a gentle fade instead of a straight shot.
Her coach taught her how to hit a reliable fade when playing into the wind.
Many professional golfers use a fade to control the placement of the ball on the fairway.

Related Terms

  • antonym – draw: a golf shot that curves to the left for a right-handed player
  • degree higher – slice: a golf shot that curves sharply to the right for a right-handed player
