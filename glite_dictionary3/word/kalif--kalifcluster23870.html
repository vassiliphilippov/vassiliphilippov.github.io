kalif

noun
Islamic civil and religious leader
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlɪf / (AmE), / ˈkeɪlɪf / (BrE)
the civil and religious leader of a Muslim state, regarded as the successor to the Prophet Muhammad and representative of Allah on earth

Examples

the kalif of Baghdad
As kalif, he was responsible for both spiritual and administrative matters.
Many historians have studied the influence of each kalif during the spread of Islam.
The Umayyad dynasty established the title of kalif as the central authority in the Islamic world.
