invasive

adjective
gradually intrusive or unwelcome
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
tending to intrude gradually or without permission, often in a way that is unwelcome or harmful

Examples

invasive personal questions
She found the reporter's questions to be uncomfortably invasive.
The invasive weeds quickly took over the garden, crowding out the native plants.
There was growing concern about the invasive presence of tourists in the quiet village.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encroaching: intruding or trespassing gradually beyond proper limits
  • synonym – intrusive: tending to intrude; interrupting or disturbing
  • antonym – welcoming: friendly or inviting, the opposite of intrusive
