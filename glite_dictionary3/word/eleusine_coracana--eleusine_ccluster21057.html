Eleusine coracana

noun
tropical cereal grass; finger millet
Pronunciation: / əˌluːˈsiːni kɔːˈrɑːkənə / (AmE), / ɛˌluːˈsiːni kɒˈrækənə / (BrE)
A tropical cereal grass native to Asia and Africa, known for its small grains that are ground into flour and used as a staple food, especially in India and parts of Africa.

Examples

Eleusine coracana flour bread
Eleusine coracana is valued in India for making flatbreads and porridge.
Farmers in Africa cultivate Eleusine coracana because it thrives in dry conditions.
The nutritional content of Eleusine coracana makes it a popular health food ingredient.
