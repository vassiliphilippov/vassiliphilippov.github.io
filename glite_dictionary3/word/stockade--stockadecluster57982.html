stockade

noun
defensive barrier of wooden posts
Pronunciation: / stɑːˈkeɪd / (AmE), / stɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a strong fence made of close-set wooden posts or stakes, built as a defensive barrier around a place

Examples

stockade fence posts
Prisoners were kept inside a stockade during the war.
The old frontier town was surrounded by a wooden stockade for security.
