fief

noun
land held under feudal system
Pronunciation: / fiːf / (AmE), / fiːf / (BrE)
A piece of land granted by a lord to a vassal under the feudal system in exchange for service and loyalty.

Examples

fief of a knight
During the Middle Ages, a fief was commonly granted in return for military service.
Each fief had its own peasants, who worked the land for the lord.
The knight managed a large fief given to him by the king.
