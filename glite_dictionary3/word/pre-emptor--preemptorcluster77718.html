pre-emptor

noun
one who acquires land by preemption
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priˈɛmptə / (BrE)
a person who acquires land or property by preemption, especially under certain legal provisions giving priority rights

Examples

pre-emptor of land
As a pre-emptor, he was entitled to purchase the property at a reduced rate.
Historical records often mention pre-emptors settling on frontier lands.
The pre-emptor filed his claim before the government opened the land to the public.
