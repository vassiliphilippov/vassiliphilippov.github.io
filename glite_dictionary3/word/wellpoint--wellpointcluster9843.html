wellpoint

noun
perforated tube for groundwater collection
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛlˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈwɛl.pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a perforated tube or pipe driven into the ground to collect groundwater for pumping or drainage

Examples

wellpoint system installation
Wellpoints are often used as a temporary dewatering method during excavations.
Each wellpoint is connected to a header pipe that leads to the main pump.
The contractor installed a wellpoint system to lower the groundwater at the construction site.
