plaster

noun
adhesive dressing for wounds
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstɚ /; / ˈplæstər / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
a medical material used to protect or treat the body, such as a cast, adhesive bandage, or poultice

Examples

plaster for cuts
Do you have a plaster? I think I'm getting a blister on my heel.
He put a plaster on his finger after he cut it while chopping vegetables.
The nurse quickly covered the small scrape with a plaster.
After the injury, a clay plaster was placed on his arm to reduce inflammation.
In the past, people would use a mustard plaster as a home remedy for chest congestion.
The doctor applied a hot plaster to her swollen knee to relieve the pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – band-aid: an adhesive bandage for covering small wounds (mainly American English)
  • synonym – poultice: a soft, moist mass applied to the body to relieve soreness
