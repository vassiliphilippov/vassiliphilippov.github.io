to depolarise

verb
eliminate polarization
Pronunciation: / diːˈpoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / diːˈpəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to eliminate or reduce the polarization of something, especially in a cell or an electrical field

Examples

to depolarise a neuron
Certain drugs can depolarise neurons, affecting how signals are transmitted in the brain.
Researchers used electrical stimulation to depolarise the cardiac muscle.
The nerve cell membranes depolarise rapidly during the transmission of a signal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – depolarize: alternative spelling (American variant) of 'depolarise', meaning to eliminate polarization
  • antonym – polarise: cause to acquire polarity or polarization
