to mobilise

verb
to cause to move or set in motion
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to move or be put into motion; to make ready for movement or action

Examples

to mobilise a patient
Doctors were able to mobilise the patient after several weeks of physical therapy.
The campaign aims to mobilise volunteers across the country for environmental clean-up projects.
The government decided to mobilise resources to help the victims of the disaster.

Related Terms

  • synonym – move: to change position; to go from one place to another
