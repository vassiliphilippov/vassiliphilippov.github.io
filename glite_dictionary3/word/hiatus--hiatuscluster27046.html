hiatus

noun
gap or missing part in a manuscript or sequence
Pronunciation: / haɪˈeɪt̬əs / (AmE), / haɪˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
An interruption or missing section in a manuscript, text, or sequence, especially where part of the material is lost.

Examples

hiatus in a manuscript
Because of the hiatus in the record, the history of that period is unclear.
Scholars noted a hiatus in the poem, indicating part of the original text had been lost.
There was a significant hiatus in the ancient manuscript where several pages were missing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gap: an unfilled space or interval; a break in continuity
