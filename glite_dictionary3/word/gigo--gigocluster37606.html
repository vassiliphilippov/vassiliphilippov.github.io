GIGO

noun
computing rule: poor input yields poor output
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
(computing) the principle that incorrect or poor-quality input will produce faulty output; garbage in, garbage out.

GIGO in software
Many programming errors are the result of GIGO, emphasizing the need for careful data entry.
