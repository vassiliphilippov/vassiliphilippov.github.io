self-restraint

noun
control of one's own impulses or desires
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf rɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf rɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
The ability to control one's own emotions, impulses, or desires; restraint imposed on oneself.

Examples

show self-restraint under pressure
Her self-restraint in refusing the tempting offer was admirable.
It took a great deal of self-restraint for him not to shout back during the argument.
Maintaining self-restraint is essential when dealing with difficult situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – impulsiveness: tendency to act on impulse rather than restraint
  • synonym – self-control: control of one's own emotions and actions
