vacuously

adverb
in a meaningless or empty way
Pronunciation: / ˈvæk.ju.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈvæk.ju.əs.li / (BrE)
In a manner that shows a lack of thought, intelligence, or meaning.

Examples

vacuously stare ahead
He nodded vacuously, giving no sign he truly understood the question.
She stared vacuously at the television screen, barely registering what was on.
The students sat vacuously in class, not paying attention to the lecture.
