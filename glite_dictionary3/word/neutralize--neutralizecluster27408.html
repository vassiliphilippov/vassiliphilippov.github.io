to neutralize

verb
counteract or offset the effect of
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something ineffective or harmless by counteracting its effect

Examples

to neutralize a problem
Adding a base will neutralize the acidity of the solution.
She tried to neutralize his arguments by presenting strong evidence.
The government introduced new measures to neutralize the impact of the economic crisis.
Drinking milk can neutralize the acid from spicy foods.
The new policy aims to neutralize the influence of lobbyists in government decisions.
The team tried to neutralize their competitor's advantage with improved technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – counteract: to act against something so as to reduce its force or neutralize it
  • antonym – intensify: to increase the strength or effect of something
  • antonym – reinforce: to strengthen or increase the effect of something
