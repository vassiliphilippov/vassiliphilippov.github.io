conceivability

noun
the quality of being conceivable
Pronunciation: / kənˌsiːvəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌsiːvəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being conceivable or able to be imagined or believed.

Examples

conceivability of an idea
He doubted the conceivability of her explanation given the evidence.
Philosophers have long debated the conceivability of a world without physical laws.
The conceivability of life on other planets fascinates many scientists.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inconceivability: the state of not being conceivable
  • synonym – possibility: the state or fact of being possible
