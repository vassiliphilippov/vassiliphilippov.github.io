to mechanise

verb
make automatic or routine; make monotonous
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an activity or process) automatic or routine, especially to the point of being monotonous

Examples

to mechanise daily tasks
His daily routine had become so mechanised that he barely noticed the passage of time.
Over time, long meetings can mechanise innovation by discouraging spontaneous thinking.
The new manager was determined not to mechanise the creative process.

Related Terms

  • synonym – automatize: to make automatic, especially by using machines or automation
  • antonym – humanize: to make something less mechanical and more humane
