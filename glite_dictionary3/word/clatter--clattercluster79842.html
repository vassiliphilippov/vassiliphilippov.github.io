clatter

noun
loud, rattling noise
Pronunciation: / ˈklæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklætə / (BrE)
a loud, continuous rattling or banging noise, often caused by hard objects striking each other rapidly

Examples

clatter of dishes
She was woken by the clatter of footsteps in the hallway.
The clatter of dishes in the kitchen signaled that dinner was nearly ready.
The old cart moved down the street with a loud clatter of wheels.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clang: a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • synonym – rattle: a rapid succession of short, sharp noises
