aeon

noun
divine being or power in Gnosticism
Pronunciation: / ˈiːˌɑn, ˈeɪˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈiːən, ˈiːɒn / (BrE)
(In Gnosticism) A divine being or power emanating from the Supreme Being, each playing a role in the structure and operation of the universe.

Examples

divine aeon
According to the text, the last aeon was responsible for shaping the material world.
In Gnostic belief, each aeon represents a stage in the process of universal creation.
Scholars often debate the number and hierarchy of aeons within different Gnostic sects.
