to counterpoint

verb
compose music with contrasting melodies
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to compose or arrange (music) with two or more independent melodic lines; to set in contrast, especially in music.

Examples

to counterpoint melodies
In his latest piece, he counterpointed a gentle piano theme with energetic strings.
She enjoys counterpointing different rhythms to create a complex musical texture.
The composer skillfully counterpoints the flute melody with the cello line.
