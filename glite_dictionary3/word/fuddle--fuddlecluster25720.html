to fuddle

verb
confuse or bewilder
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈfʌd.əl / (BrE)
to confuse or perplex someone; to make someone unable to think clearly

Examples

fuddle your mind
Don't let all these technical details fuddle you; the process is actually straightforward.
She tried to explain, but her words only fuddled the children more.
The complex instructions utterly fuddled him during the exam.

Related Terms

  • synonym – befuddle: to make someone unable to think clearly; to confuse
  • antonym – clarify: to make something clearer or easier to understand
  • synonym – confuse: to make someone unable to think clearly or to mix up
