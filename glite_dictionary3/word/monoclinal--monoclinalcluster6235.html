monoclinal

adjective
of strata inclined in one direction
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈklaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈklaɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a geological structure) having all rock layers or strata inclined in the same direction.

Examples

monoclinal rock layers
A monoclinal structure can often be recognized by the uniform tilt of sedimentary layers.
The region's monoclinal fold is an important feature for geologists studying the area's tectonic history.
Their survey confirmed that the basin had a distinct monoclinal formation running northeast.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anticlinal: of strata sloping downward from a central crest
  • antonym – synclinal: of strata sloping upward from a central trough
