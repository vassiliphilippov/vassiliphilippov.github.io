pole

noun
old unit of length (16.5 feet)
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
an old unit of length or land measurement, also known as a rod or perch

Examples

one pole length
A length of one pole is equal to 16.5 feet, which was once a common surveyor's unit.
Old maps sometimes list distances in poles rather than in feet or meters.
The farmer measured the boundary as six poles long.
Early land deeds often record plots in units such as the pole or rod.
He inherited a parcel described as ten poles square.
The property was measured at sixteen poles wide by twenty poles long.
