lying-in

noun
childbirth; period of labor and delivery
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪɪŋˈɪn / (AmE), / ˌlaɪɪŋˈɪn / (BrE)
the process of childbirth, from the onset of labor contractions to the delivery of the baby; sometimes used to refer also to the period of bed rest after childbirth

Examples

lying-in period after birth
During her lying-in, she was cared for by midwives and family members.
Historically, many women spent several weeks in lying-in after the birth of a child.
The hospital used to have a special lying-in ward for mothers during childbirth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delivery: the act of giving birth to a baby
  • synonym – labor: the process of childbirth from the onset of contractions to birth
