acid-loving

adjective
thriving in acidic conditions
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd ˌlʌvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌlʌvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) thriving in or requiring a relatively acidic environment, especially one with a pH well below 7.

Examples

acid-loving plants
Blueberries are a well-known example of acid-loving crops.
Gardeners often add peat moss to accommodate acid-loving rhododendrons.
Some plants are acid-loving and will flourish only in soils with a low pH.
