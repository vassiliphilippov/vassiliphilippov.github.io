galvanisation

noun
stimulation with an electric current
Pronunciation: / ˌɡælvənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡælvənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the stimulation of living tissue with a galvanic (direct) electric current, especially for medical or physiological purposes

Examples

medical galvanisation treatment
Doctors once used galvanisation as a treatment for paralysis and other nervous disorders.
Early experiments with galvanisation demonstrated the effects of electricity on nerve cells.
The physiotherapist applied galvanisation to stimulate muscle contraction in the patient's leg.
