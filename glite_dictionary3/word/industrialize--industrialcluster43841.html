to industrialize

verb
develop industry; become industrial
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌstriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to develop industry in a country, region, or process, especially by using machinery and mass production

Examples

to industrialize a country
As countries industrialize, their economies often experience rapid growth and urbanization.
Government incentives helped the region industrialize after the war, leading to higher employment rates.
Some developing nations have yet to fully industrialize and remain largely agricultural.
Efforts to industrialize traditional crafts met with resistance from local artisans.
Many countries sought to industrialize their agriculture during the 20th century.
The region rapidly industrialized following the construction of several large factories.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deindustrialize: to reduce industrial activity or dismantle industries
  • synonym – mechanize: to introduce machinery into a process
  • synonym – urbanize: to make or become urban, to develop towns and cities
