Hyperion

proper noun
Titan in Greek mythology, father of Helios
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɪriən / (AmE), / hɪˈpɪərɪən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a Titan who was the son of Gaea and Uranus, and the father of Helios, Selene, and Eos.

Examples

Hyperion the Titan
In the epic, Hyperion stands as one of the twelve original Titans.
Many ancient poets described Hyperion as the personification of the sun before Helios.
The myth of Hyperion connects him with the dawn, the sun, and the moon through his children.
