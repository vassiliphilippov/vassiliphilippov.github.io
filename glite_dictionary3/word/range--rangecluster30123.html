to range

verb
move freely over an area
Pronunciation: / reɪndʒ / (AmE), / reɪndʒ / (BrE)
to move or travel freely over an area, often without a fixed route or purpose

Examples

to range freely
During the day, the shepherd's flock would range over the hills.
Migratory animals often range great distances to find food and shelter.
The wild horses range across the open plains in search of grazing land.

Related Terms

  • synonym – roam: to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically, especially over a wide area
  • synonym – wander: to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way
