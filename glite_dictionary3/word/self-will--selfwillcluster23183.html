self-will

noun
determined insistence on one's own ideas or desires
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈwɪl / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈwɪl / (BrE)
the strong determination to do what you want, even if others advise against it

Examples

strong self-will
Despite persistent objections, his self-will led him to start the business his own way.
Her self-will made it difficult for her to accept others' advice even in challenging situations.
The child's strong self-will sometimes clashed with the rules at school.
He is often admired for his self-will, refusing to give up even when others doubt him.
Her self-will helped her overcome many obstacles despite the challenges she faced.
Too much self-will can sometimes make it hard to cooperate with others.
