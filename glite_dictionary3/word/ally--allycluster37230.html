to ally

verb
join or unite for a common purpose
Pronunciation: / əˈlaɪ / (AmE), / əˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to join or unite with another person, group, or country for a common purpose or in support of a cause

Examples

to ally with partners
After years of competition, the two companies decided to ally to develop new technology.
He refused to ally with any political party, preferring to remain independent.
Several small nations chose to ally themselves with the larger neighboring country for protection.
