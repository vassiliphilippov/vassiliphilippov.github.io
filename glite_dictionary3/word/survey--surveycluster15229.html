to survey

verb
inspect or look over carefully
Pronunciation: / sərˈveɪ / (AmE), / səˈveɪ / (BrE)
to look at or examine something carefully and thoroughly; to inspect or consider in detail

Examples

to survey the room
He surveyed his desk before starting work to ensure everything was in order.
She took a moment to survey the crowd before stepping onto the stage.
The scientist surveyed the landscape with great interest, making careful notes in her journal.
As she entered the museum, she surveyed the paintings carefully before moving on.
He paused to survey the room, taking in every detail before making his decision.
The committee will survey the current policies and recommend changes if needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inspect: to look at something closely to assess its condition or quality
  • synonym – scan: to look at all parts of something carefully in order to detect some feature
