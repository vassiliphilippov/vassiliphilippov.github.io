straining

noun
distorting something's intended meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of interpreting or presenting something in a way that distorts its original meaning or intent

Examples

straining the truth
Critics accused him of straining the author's words to suit his theory.
His constant straining of the facts made his argument less credible.
The lawyer's straining of the contract's language was obvious to the judge.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – interpretation: the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something (without distortion)
  • synonym – misrepresentation: the action of giving a false or misleading account of something
