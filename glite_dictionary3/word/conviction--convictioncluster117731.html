conviction

noun
formal finding of guilt in a court
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
(Law) a formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense, made by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law.

Examples

conviction in court
A criminal conviction can have serious consequences for future employment.
His conviction for fraud resulted in a five-year prison sentence.
The lawyer appealed the conviction on the grounds of insufficient evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acquittal: a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they have been charged
