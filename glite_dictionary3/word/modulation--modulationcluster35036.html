modulation

noun
modifying a carrier wave to transmit a signal
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Electronics) The process of transmitting a signal by varying a carrier wave's amplitude, frequency, or phase using the signal.

Examples

modulation of radio signal
Frequency modulation is widely used in radio broadcasting.
The engineer explained how modulation allows multiple signals to be transmitted over the same channel.
Understanding amplitude modulation is vital for students of telecommunications.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demodulation: the process of extracting the original information-bearing signal from a modulated carrier wave
