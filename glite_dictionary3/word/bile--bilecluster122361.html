bile

noun
digestive fluid aiding fat digestion
Pronunciation: / baɪl / (AmE), / baɪl / (BrE)
A bitter greenish-yellow digestive fluid secreted by the liver and stored in the gallbladder, which helps to break down fats during digestion.

Examples

bile from the liver
A blockage in the bile duct can prevent bile from reaching the digestive tract.
Doctors examined the patient's bile to check for signs of liver disease.
The liver produces bile to help digest fats in the small intestine.
