to spin

verb
move in a winding course (of liquid)
Pronunciation: / spɪn / (AmE), / spɪn / (BrE)
to turn around quickly or cause something to turn rapidly; to move in a winding or twisting course; to become or make something uncontrolled or chaotic

Examples

to spin around
A narrow river spins its way across the countryside, twisting between fields and forests.
The mountain stream spun through the rocky gorge, carving a winding path over centuries.
Tiny rivulets of water spun down the windowpane during the heavy rainstorm.
Dancers would spin across the stage in perfect synchronization.
The little girl loves to spin in circles until she gets dizzy.
The top continued to spin for more than a minute before falling over.
Could you spin the wheel to see who wins the prize?
He decided to spin the globe to pick his next travel destination.
She likes to spin her ring absent-mindedly while thinking.
