bridge

noun
ship's command platform
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ / (BrE)
the raised platform on a ship from which it is steered and commanded, typically where the captain and officers operate

Examples

bridge of a ship
Access to the bridge is usually restricted to certain crew members for safety reasons.
Modern vessels are equipped with advanced navigation systems on the bridge.
The captain stood on the bridge, overseeing the crew below as the ship entered the harbor.
