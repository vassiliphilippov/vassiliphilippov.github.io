compost

noun
decayed organic material for fertilizer
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːm.poʊst / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pɒst / (BrE)
A mixture of decaying plant matter and manure used to fertilize and improve the soil.

Examples

rich compost soil
Kitchen scraps and yard waste can be turned into compost for your garden.
Many farmers use compost as a natural alternative to chemical fertilizers.
The gardener spread compost over the flower beds to enrich the soil.
