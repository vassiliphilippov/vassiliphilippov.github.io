regeneration

noun
feedback in phase with input (technical)
Pronunciation: / rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(technical) The process in which feedback is in phase with and augments the input, especially in electronics or signal processing.

Examples

regeneration in electronics
By adjusting the amount of regeneration, the radio operator enhanced reception clarity.
Certain oscillators use regeneration to maintain continuous wave generation.
The regeneration in the amplifier circuit increases the overall signal strength.
