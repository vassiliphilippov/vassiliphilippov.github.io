discount

noun
interest deducted in advance on a loan
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
Interest deducted in advance from the principal of a loan, typically calculated on an annual basis.

Examples

discount on loan
A bill of exchange is often sold at a discount reflecting the interest deducted in advance.
By applying a discount, the lender collects its interest before the borrower receives the funds.
The bank charged a discount on the loan, reducing the amount I received.
