scrubber

noun
software that cleans data
Pronunciation: / c8skr8cb59r / (AmE), / c8skr8cb59 / (BrE)
(in computing) a program or process that cleans data by detecting and removing errors, duplicates, or inconsistent values to improve data quality.

Examples

data scrubber
The data pipeline includes a scrubber that removes duplicate and invalid customer records before analysis.
Engineers ran the nightly scrubbers to standardize formats and correct typos across millions of entries.
During ingestion, the scrubber flagged inconsistent dates and normalized values for the downstream model.

Related Terms

  • synonym – data cleaning: the process of detecting and correcting (or removing) corrupt or inaccurate records from a dataset
  • synonym – deduplication: the process of finding and removing duplicate records in a dataset
