to amortize

verb
pay off a debt or expense over time
Pronunciation: / ˈæmərˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈmɔːtʌɪz / (BrE)
to gradually pay off a debt or spread the cost of an asset over a period of time, often through regular payments

Examples

to amortize a loan
Businesses often amortize the cost of equipment on their financial statements.
She carefully amortized her student debt by making fixed monthly installments.
The company decided to amortize the loan over ten years to reduce monthly payments.

  • synonym – liquidate: to settle or pay off a debt or obligation
