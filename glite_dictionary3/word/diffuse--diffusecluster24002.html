diffuse

adjective
lacking conciseness; wordy
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːs / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) lacking conciseness; wordy or overly spread out in style.

Examples

diffuse writing style
Her speech was interesting but rather diffuse, lacking a clear point.
Some readers found the novel's narrative too diffuse to be engaging.
The author's diffuse writing style made the report difficult to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • synonym – verbose: using or expressed in more words than needed
