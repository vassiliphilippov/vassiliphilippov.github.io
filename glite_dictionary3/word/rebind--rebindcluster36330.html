to rebind

verb
remap domain name to a different IP (DNS rebinding)
Pronunciation: / ccrid0c8ba6and / (AmE), / ccrid0c8ba6and / (BrE)
(in computing, especially cybersecurity) to change or remap the IP address that a domain name resolves to, typically as part of a DNS rebinding technique used to bypass same-origin protections or reach internal network addresses.

Examples

to rebind a domain name
Attackers may rebind a domain to a local IP to bypass browser same-origin protections.
During the penetration test she recorded evidence of rebinding that allowed access to the internal web service.
Configuring a short TTL makes it easier for attackers to rebind domains during DNS-based attacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – DNS rebinding: a web-based attack technique that manipulates DNS responses to make a domain name resolve to different IPs, often to access internal network resources.
  • synonym – remap: to change the mapping of one address or identifier to another (for example, domain to IP).
