crag

noun
steep, rugged rock or cliff
Pronunciation: / kræɡ / (AmE), / kræɡ / (BrE)
a steep or rugged rock or cliff, especially one projecting upward or outward from a mountain or shore

Examples

rocky crag
After a long climb, we rested beneath the shelter of a jutting crag.
Mist clung to the crag as waves crashed against its base.
The eagle built its nest high on a dangerous crag overlooking the valley.
