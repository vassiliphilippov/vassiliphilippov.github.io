affidavit

noun
sworn written legal statement
Pronunciation: / ˌæfəˈdeɪvɪt / (AmE), / ˌæfɪˈdeɪvɪt / (BrE)
A written statement sworn to be true and made under oath before an authorized official, used as evidence in court or legal proceedings.

Examples

sworn affidavit
Her claim was supported by an affidavit stating that she was present at the scene.
The witness submitted an affidavit confirming her account of the incident.
You must sign the affidavit in front of a notary public for it to be valid.
