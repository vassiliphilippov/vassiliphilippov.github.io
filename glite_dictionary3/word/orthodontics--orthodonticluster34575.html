orthodontics

noun
branch of dentistry for correcting teeth
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːrθəˈdɑntɪks / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈdɒntɪks / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry that deals with the prevention and correction of irregularities of the teeth and jaws

Examples

orthodontics for straight teeth
Advancements in orthodontics have made braces more comfortable and less noticeable.
After her visit to the clinic, her dentist recommended she consider orthodontics to straighten her teeth.
Children often begin orthodontics in their early teens to correct misaligned teeth.
