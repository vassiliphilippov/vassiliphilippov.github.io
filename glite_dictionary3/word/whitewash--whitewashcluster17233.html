whitewash

noun
lime-based wall paint
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a liquid mixture of lime and water used to whiten walls and other surfaces

Examples

whitewash on walls
He noticed that the fence was covered with flaking whitewash.
The old cottage was given a fresh coat of whitewash to brighten it up.
They applied whitewash to the cellar walls for a clean, traditional look.
