ballyhoo

noun
loud or flashy publicity
Pronunciation: / ˈbæliˌhu / (AmE), / ˈbæliˌhuː / (BrE)
blatant or sensational promotion or publicity, especially when exaggerated or noisy.

Examples

media ballyhoo
Despite the ballyhoo surrounding the product launch, sales remained disappointing.
He was tired of all the ballyhoo and preferred to focus on the work itself.
The new movie's release was accompanied by a great deal of ballyhoo in the media.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hype: excessive or exaggerated publicity
  • synonym – publicity: notice or attention given to someone or something by the media
  • antonym – understatement: the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it is
