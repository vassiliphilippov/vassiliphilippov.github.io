myxoma virus

noun
a poxvirus causing gelatinous tumors
Pronunciation: / mɪkˈsoʊmə ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / mɪkˈsəʊmə ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
A poxvirus closely related to the smallpox virus that causes benign gelatinous tumors, particularly in rabbits, and is sometimes associated with similar effects in humans.

Examples

myxoma virus infects rabbits
Scientists studied how the myxoma virus evolved to become less lethal over time.
The myxoma virus was used in Australia to control wild rabbit populations.
The presence of myxoma virus in laboratory rabbits led to visible tumor growths.
