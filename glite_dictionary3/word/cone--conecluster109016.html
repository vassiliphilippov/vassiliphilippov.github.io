cone

noun
seed-bearing structure in plants
Pronunciation: / koʊn / (AmE), / kəʊn / (BrE)
a seed- or spore-producing structure in certain plants, typically consisting of overlapping scales or bracts arranged around a central axis, as found in pine trees and some ferns

Examples

pine cone
Each cone contains numerous seeds that are released when it matures.
Some species of spruce develop a different type of cone for pollen and seeds.
The pine tree dropped a large cone covered in sticky resin onto the forest floor.
