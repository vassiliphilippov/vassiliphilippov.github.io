simvastatin

noun
cholesterol-lowering medication
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈvæstətɪn / (AmE), / sɪmˈvæstətɪn / (BrE)
a medication taken orally to lower cholesterol levels in the blood, often prescribed to reduce the risk of heart attacks and other cardiovascular events.

Examples

take simvastatin tablets
Many patients take simvastatin after a heart attack to prevent future cardiovascular problems.
Some people experience muscle pain as a side effect of simvastatin.
The doctor prescribed simvastatin to help control my cholesterol levels.
