transposition

noun
rearrangement of power lines (electricity)
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(electricity) The rearrangement of power lines to minimize the mutual effects of capacitance and inductance between them.

Examples

transposition of cables
A transposition of the cables was necessary to balance the inductive and capacitive effects in the network.
In order to reduce interference, engineers performed a transposition of the high-voltage transmission lines.
Proper transposition helps to ensure the stability and efficiency of electricity distribution systems.
