to undermine

verb
hollow out or erode at the base
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈmaɪn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈmaɪn / (BrE)
to make a hollow or cavity beneath something, especially by gradually removing supporting material; to erode the base of.

Examples

undermine the foundation
Continuous flooding can undermine the foundations of a bridge, threatening its stability.
Over time, the river began to undermine the steep banks, creating small caves.
Workers discovered that erosion had undermined a section of the tunnel roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erode: to gradually wear away soil, rock, or land
