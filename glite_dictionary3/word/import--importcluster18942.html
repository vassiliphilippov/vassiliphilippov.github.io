import

noun
essential meaning or significance
Pronunciation: / ˈɪm.pɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pɔːt / (BrE)
the meaning, significance, or importance of something, especially what is implied

Examples

import of a message
The import of his speech was not immediately clear to the audience.
The judge explained the import of the new law to the jury.
You should not underestimate the import of these legal changes.
She did not fully grasp the import of his words until much later.
The court's decision is of great import for international law.
These findings are of little import to the overall conclusions of the study.
His comments were vague, but their import became clear in hindsight.
She only understood the full import of his words after he had left.
The import of the new policy was not immediately apparent to the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym – consequence: a result or effect of an action or condition, also used for importance
  • synonym – implication: something suggested but not directly expressed
  • antonym – insignificance: lack of importance or meaning
  • synonym – meaning: what is meant by a word, text, concept, or action
  • synonym – significance: the quality of being significant; meaningfulness
