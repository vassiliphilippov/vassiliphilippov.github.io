bid

noun
bridge: declared number of tricks
Pronunciation: / bɪd / (AmE), / bɪd / (BrE)
(in bridge) a statement indicating the number of tricks a player commits to try to win in a round.

Examples

a bid of four spades
After a long discussion with his partner, he made a bold bid of four hearts.
Her opening bid was one no-trump, surprising the other players.
You are required to make your bid before play can begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym – call: (in cards) a declaration made during bidding
  • antonym – pass: (in bridge) to decline making a bid
