retrofit

noun
component added after manufacture
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌfɪt / (BrE)
a component, system, or accessory that is added to an existing product, structure, or system after its original manufacture to improve or update it

Examples

energy-saving retrofit
Adding a new filtration system as a retrofit can extend the life of industrial equipment.
Many homes have had a retrofit to meet updated safety standards.
The old factory received a retrofit to improve its energy efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – upgrade: an improvement or enhancement of existing equipment
