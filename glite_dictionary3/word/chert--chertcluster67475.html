chert

noun
a hard, fine-grained silica rock
Pronunciation: / tʃɝt / (AmE), / tʃɜːt / (BrE)
A hard, fine-grained sedimentary rock composed mainly of microcrystalline quartz, often occurring as nodules or layers in limestone.

Examples

layer of chert
Fossils are sometimes preserved in layers of chert within limestone.
Native Americans used chert to make arrowheads and tools.
The geologist collected several samples of chert from the riverbed.
