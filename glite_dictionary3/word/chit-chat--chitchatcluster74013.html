to chit-chat

verb
talk casually or socially
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪtˌtʃæt / (BrE)
to talk socially in a light and informal way, often without exchanging important information

Examples

to chit-chat with friends
She was chit-chatting with her neighbor when I walked by.
The colleagues like to chit-chat by the coffee machine during their breaks.
We sat in the garden and chit-chatted until the sun went down.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chat: to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • degree higher – gossip: to talk about the personal lives of others, often spreading rumors
