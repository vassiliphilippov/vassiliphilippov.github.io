to seize

verb
take or capture by force
Pronunciation: / siz / (AmE), / siːz / (BrE)
to take hold of, capture, or take control of something or someone suddenly and often by force

Examples

to seize power
Protesters attempted to seize control of the building.
The general ordered his troops to seize the enemy camp overnight.
The police managed to seize the stolen goods during the raid.
After the king's death, his cousin attempted to seize the throne.
He tried to seize the company's assets after the owner passed away.
The rebels planned to seize the government buildings before dawn.
As soon as the opportunity arose, they seized it without hesitation.
He tried to seize her hand as she walked past, but she pulled away.
The police managed to seize the stolen goods moments before they left the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capture: to take control of someone or something by force
  • synonym – grasp: to take hold of something firmly
  • antonym – liberate: to set free from control or imprisonment, especially by force
  • antonym – release: to set free or let go
  • synonym – usurp: to take a position of power or importance illegally or by force
