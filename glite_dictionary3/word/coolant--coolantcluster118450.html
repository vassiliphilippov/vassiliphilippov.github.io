coolant

noun
substance for cooling engines or systems
Pronunciation: / ˈkuː.lənt / (AmE), / ˈkuː.lənt / (BrE)
a liquid or gas used to reduce or control the temperature of a system by absorbing and transferring heat away from it, especially in engines or machinery

Examples

coolant in an engine
A special coolant circulates through the computer's components to prevent overheating.
Always ensure your car has enough coolant before taking a long trip.
The mechanic checked the engine's coolant level during the routine service.

Related Terms

  • synonym – refrigerant: a substance used for cooling, especially in refrigerators and air conditioners
