stake

noun
post used to mark a position or boundary
Pronunciation: / steɪk / (AmE), / steɪk / (BrE)
a strong post or pole, usually made of wood or metal, driven into the ground to mark a position, support something, or as part of a structure

Examples

stake in the ground
A tall stake at the corner showed where the fence would be built.
Each runner started behind a wooden stake marking the beginning of the course.
The surveyors drove a stake into the ground to indicate the property boundary.
She hammered a stake into the ground to mark the property boundary.
The tomato plants were supported by a wooden stake in each pot.
Vampires in folklore are said to be killed by driving a stake through their hearts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – post: an upright piece of wood or metal fixed in the ground, usually to support something or mark a location
