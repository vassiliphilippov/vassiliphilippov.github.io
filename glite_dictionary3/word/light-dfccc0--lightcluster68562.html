light

adjective
of low atomic weight (science)
Pronunciation: / laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
(of an element or compound) having atomic weight less than or equal to the average; not containing heavy isotopes.

Examples

light element
In nuclear reactors, light elements are preferred as moderators due to their atomic characteristics.
Ordinary water is composed of light hydrogen and oxygen atoms.
Scientists compare the properties of light and heavy water in experiments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heavy: (of an element or compound) having atomic weight greater than average
