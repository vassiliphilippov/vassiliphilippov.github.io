KiB

noun
unit of 1024 bytes (digital storage)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪ.baɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪ.baɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1024 bytes, used chiefly in computing

Examples

file size in KiB
A small text file may take up only a few KiB of storage.
The log file is approximately 256 KiB in size.
This hard drive has a capacity of 500,000 KiB.
