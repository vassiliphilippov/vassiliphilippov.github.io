scalability

noun
ability to grow or expand effectively
Pronunciation: / ˌskeɪləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌskeɪləˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The ability of a system, process, or product to handle increased workload or to be expanded to accommodate growth.

Examples

scalability of a system
Cloud computing services are known for their flexibility and scalability.
Investors are interested in startups with a clear plan for scalability as demand increases.
The scalability of the new software allows it to support thousands of users as the company grows.
