Nahuatl

noun
indigenous people of central Mexico
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːwɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈnɑːwətəl / (BrE)
A member of any of the indigenous peoples of central Mexico, especially those descended from the Aztecs.

Examples

Nahuatl people
A Nahuatl can trace their ancestry to the ancient Aztec civilization.
Many Nahuatl still live in villages throughout central Mexico.
Some Nahuatl have preserved their traditional customs and language.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Aztec: a member of the indigenous people dominant in central Mexico before the Spanish conquest
