to cauterise

verb
burn tissue to stop bleeding/infection
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to burn or sear tissue, typically to stop bleeding or prevent infection, using a hot instrument, electric current, or a chemical agent

Examples

to cauterise a wound
After removing the mole, the doctor used a heated tool to cauterise the wound.
In the past, soldiers’ injuries were often cauterised on the battlefield to stop bleeding.
New advances allow surgeons to cauterise tissue more quickly and precisely during operations.
