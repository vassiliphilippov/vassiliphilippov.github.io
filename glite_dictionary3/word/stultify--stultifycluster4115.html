to stultify

verb
cause to appear foolish
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌltɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstʌltɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to make someone appear foolish, absurd, or incompetent (sometimes in a legal context)

Examples

to stultify an opponent
His constant interruptions only served to stultify his position during the debate.
She worried that asking basic questions might stultify her in front of her colleagues.
The complicated procedure could easily stultify an otherwise intelligent participant.
In rare legal cases, the defense may attempt to stultify the defendant in order to challenge a contract.
She feared that admitting such an error would stultify her in front of her colleagues.
The policy seemed designed to stultify innovation rather than encourage it.

  • antonym – dignify: make someone or something seem worthy or impressive
  • synonym – humiliate: make someone feel ashamed or foolish
