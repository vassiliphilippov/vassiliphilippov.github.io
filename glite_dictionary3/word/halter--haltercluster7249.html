halter

noun
balancing organ in some insects
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltə / (BrE)
either of the small, knobbed structures behind the wings of dipterous insects, used to maintain balance during flight

Examples

insect halter balance
Scientists studied the movement of the halter to understand insect flight mechanics.
The halter of a fly are essential for its ability to stabilize in the air.
Unlike other insect wings, the halter are reduced to small, club-shaped structures.
