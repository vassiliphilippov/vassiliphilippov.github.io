to ground

verb
teach the basics of a subject
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to teach someone the basic principles or to base something on a foundation

Examples

to ground students in basics
All new employees are grounded in the values and history of the company during orientation.
His teachers grounded him thoroughly in the fundamentals of mathematics.
She helps ground her students in the basics before moving on to more advanced topics.
His criticism was grounded in careful research and extensive reading.
She grounds her opinions in both logic and experience.
The decision must be grounded on the relevant laws and regulations.
