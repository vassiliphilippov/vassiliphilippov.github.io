panhandler

noun
a person who begs for money in public
Pronunciation: / ˈpænˌhændlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpænˌhændlə / (BrE)
a person who begs for money from strangers in public places, typically by asking passersby directly

Examples

street panhandler
A panhandler stopped me downtown and asked if I could spare some change.
City officials are discussing new ways to assist panhandlers and address homelessness.
He often gives food instead of money to panhandlers he meets on his way to work.
