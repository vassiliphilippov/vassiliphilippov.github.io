clannishness

noun
tendency to stick to one's group
Pronunciation: / ˈklæn.ɪʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈklæn.ɪʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the tendency to associate closely with a particular group and avoid outsiders

Examples

family clannishness
Her sense of clannishness led her to trust only her childhood friends.
Some critics accused the old elite of clannishness and reluctance to accept outsiders.
The clannishness of the group made it difficult for newcomers to feel welcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exclusiveness: the quality of excluding others; being exclusive
  • antonym – openness: willingness to accept or include others
