to welt

verb
to strike and raise a mark on the skin
Pronunciation: / wɛlt / (AmE), / wɛlt / (BrE)
to strike someone hard enough to raise a ridge or mark on the skin, especially with a whip or rod

Examples

to welt a person
He welted the carpet with a stick, raising clouds of dust.
She claimed the guard welted her after she tried to escape.
The coach threatened to welt the boys if they misbehaved again.

Related Terms

  • antonym – caress: to touch or stroke gently
  • synonym – flog: to beat severely with a whip or rod
  • synonym – whip: to strike with a whip as punishment
