lipoid

noun
oily organic compound in cells
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪpɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪpɔɪd / (BrE)
an oily organic compound that is insoluble in water but soluble in organic solvents; essential as a structural component of living cells, alongside proteins and carbohydrates

Examples

lipoid compound in cells
A deficiency in certain lipoid compounds can affect brain development.
Many vitamins are lipoid-soluble, meaning they dissolve in fats but not in water.
The researchers studied the lipoid content of the cell membranes.
