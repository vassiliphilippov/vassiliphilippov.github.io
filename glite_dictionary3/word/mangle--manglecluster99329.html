to mangle

verb
damage or injure severely
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡl / (BrE)
to damage or injure something or someone severely by cutting, tearing, or crushing

Examples

to mangle a hand
He mangled his hand in a terrible car accident.
The machine could easily mangle your clothes if you are not careful.
The report was so badly written that it seemed to mangle the facts.
She was lucky to survive, though the accident had mangled both her legs.
The animal was found mangled after the attack by a much larger predator.
The machinery accidentally mangled his hand, leaving permanent damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disfigure: to spoil or damage the appearance of something or someone
  • antonym – heal: to become healthy again; to make well after injury
  • synonym – maul: to wound or handle roughly, often causing injury
  • synonym – mutilate: to inflict serious damage or injury
  • antonym – repair: to restore something damaged to a good condition
