chronological

adjective
arranged by order of time
Pronunciation: / krəˈnɑlədʒɪkəl / (AmE), / krəˈnɒlədʒɪkəl / (BrE)
arranged in the order in which events occurred in time

Examples

chronological order of events
She listed all the events in chronological order for clarity.
The novel tells the story in a chronological sequence, starting from the protagonist's childhood.
Your chronological age is the number of years you have lived since birth.

Related Terms

  • antonym – random: made, done, or happening without method or conscious decision
  • synonym – sequential: arranged or following in a sequence, especially a logical one
