demineralisation

noun
removal of minerals from liquid
Pronunciation: / diˌmɪnəɹəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing minerals and mineral salts from a liquid, especially from water

Examples

demineralisation of water
Demineralisation of drinking water helps to prevent scale buildup in household appliances.
After demineralisation, the water is suitable for laboratory experiments.
The plant uses a sophisticated demineralisation system to purify industrial water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – remineralisation: the process of restoring minerals to a liquid or material
