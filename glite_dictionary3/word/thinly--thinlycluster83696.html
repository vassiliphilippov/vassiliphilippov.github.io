thinly

adverb
without sincerity or enthusiasm
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪnli / (AmE), / ˈθɪnli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows little force, sincerity, or enthusiasm.

Examples

thinly hidden anger
He thinly smiled at the joke but remained otherwise silent.
She thinly disguised her disappointment when she heard the news.
The apology was offered thinly and failed to convince anyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – faintly: in a way that is not strong or clear
