ruggedness

noun
quality of being uneven or rough
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being uneven or rough, especially in relation to land or natural features.

Examples

ruggedness of terrain
Despite the ruggedness of these mountains, several rare plants thrive here.
The ruggedness of the terrain made it challenging for hikers to reach the summit.
You can appreciate the natural ruggedness of the coastline from this viewpoint.

Related Terms

  • synonym – roughness: The state or quality of having an uneven or irregular surface
  • antonym – smoothness: The quality of being even and without roughness
