kinesis

noun
biological movement in response to stimulus
Pronunciation: / kəˈniːsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈniːsɪs / (BrE)
movement of an organism or cell in response to a stimulus, without a specific direction toward or away from the source

Examples

kinesis in animals
In humid environments, woodlice exhibit kinesis by moving more rapidly to avoid desiccation.
The scientist studied the kinesis of bacteria exposed to light.
Unlike taxis, kinesis involves undirected movement in response to environmental conditions.
