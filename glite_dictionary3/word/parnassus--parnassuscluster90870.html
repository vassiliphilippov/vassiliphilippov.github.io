Parnassus

proper noun
mountain in Greek mythology, home of the Muses
Pronunciation: / pɑːrˈnæsəs / (AmE), / pɑːˈnæsəs / (BrE)
A mountain in central Greece, regarded in Greek mythology as the home of the Muses and the symbolic center of poetry and the arts.

Examples

Parnassus of poetry
Artists in ancient Greece often dedicated their music to the spirits of Parnassus.
Many poets dream of climbing Parnassus and being inspired by the Muses.
The legend of Parnassus is central to the history of Western literature.
