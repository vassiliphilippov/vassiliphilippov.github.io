prefect

noun
chief officer or magistrate
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈpriːfɛkt / (BrE)
a senior official or chief officer, especially in some countries' governments or police forces

Examples

prefect of police
Every region in ancient Rome was governed by a prefect.
The prefect implemented stricter measures to improve city security.
The prefect of Paris police addressed the public after the incident.
