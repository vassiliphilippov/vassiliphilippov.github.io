bluegrass

noun
a type of grass with bluish-green leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of several grasses of the genus Poa, commonly found in temperate regions, noted for their bluish-green stems or leaves.

Examples

bluegrass lawn grass
Farmers often plant bluegrass to prevent soil erosion on slopes.
