biplane

noun
aircraft with two pairs of wings
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈbaɪpleɪn / (BrE)
an aircraft with two sets of wings, one above the other, commonly used in the early 20th century

Examples

old biplane aircraft
A restored biplane is now on display at the aviation museum.
Early aviators often flew biplanes because their dual wings provided extra lift.
The biplane soared gracefully above the fields during the airshow.
