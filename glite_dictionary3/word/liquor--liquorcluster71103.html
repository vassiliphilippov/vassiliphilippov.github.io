liquor

noun
cooking liquid from meat or vegetables
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
the liquid in which vegetables or meat have been cooked, often used as a broth or base for soups and sauces

Examples

liquor from cooked beans
After boiling the beans, she used the flavorful liquor to make soup.
Ham is often served with some of its cooking liquor poured over the top.
The chef recommended saving the vegetable liquor for sauces.

Related Terms

  • synonym – broth: liquid in which meat, fish, or vegetables have been simmered
  • synonym – stock: flavored liquid base for soups and sauces, made by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables
