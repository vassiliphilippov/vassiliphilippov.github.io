to defeat

verb
prevent passage of a proposal or plan
Pronunciation: / dəˈfit /; / dɪˈfit / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
to win against someone or something, or to stop a plan or proposal from succeeding

Examples

to defeat an opponent

The city council will likely defeat the proposal if enough members oppose it.
The committee voted to defeat the controversial bill.
He hopes to defeat the defending champion in tomorrow’s race.
It took her years to defeat her fear of public speaking.
The team managed to defeat its rivals in the final match.
