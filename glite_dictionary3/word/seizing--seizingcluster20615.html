seizing

noun
small cord for fastening ropes together (nautical)
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
A small cord or line used in lashing together two or more ropes, especially in maritime contexts.

Examples

rope seizing
Before climbing, check that each seizing is tight and in good condition.

The sailor used a seizing to secure the two ropes at the joint.
