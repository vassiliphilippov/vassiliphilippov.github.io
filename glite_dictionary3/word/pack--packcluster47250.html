to pack

verb
wrap or fill body part for treatment
Pronunciation: / pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
to wrap a part of the body with a material such as blankets, sheets, or compresses, or to fill a wound with materials, typically for the purpose of treatment, covering, or absorbing fluids

Examples

to pack a wound
After the dental surgery, the dentist packed the socket to control bleeding.
Doctors sometimes pack an injured limb in ice to reduce swelling.
The nurse carefully packed the wound with sterile gauze.
