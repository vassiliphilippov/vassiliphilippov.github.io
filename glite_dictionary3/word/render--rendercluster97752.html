to render

verb
formally surrender or give up
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛn.dɚ /; / ˈrɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɛn.də /; / ˈrɛndə / (BrE)
to formally give up, hand over, or surrender something or someone, especially to an authority or as required

Examples

to render the documents
She refused to render the confidential documents to the investigating authority.
The army was forced to render the captured fort to the enemy at sunrise.
Under the treaty, the government must render the escaped fugitives to their country of origin.
After losing the case, the company had to render the building to the government.
He was required to render an account of his expenses after the business trip.
The court ordered the defendant to render the disputed property to its rightful owner.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deliver: to hand over officially or formally
  • antonym – retain: to keep possession of, not surrender
  • synonym – surrender: to give up possession or control to another
