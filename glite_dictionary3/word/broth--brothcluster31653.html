broth

noun
thin soup made from meat, fish, or vegetables
Pronunciation: / brɑθ /; / brɑːθ / (AmE), / brɒθ / (BrE)
a savory liquid made by boiling or simmering meat, fish, vegetables, or grains in water, often used as a base for soups and sauces

Examples

Many traditional recipes start with a simple broth as the base.
