skidder

noun
worker who moves logs with a skid
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪdə / (BrE)
a worker in logging who uses a skid to move logs from where they are felled to a collection point

Examples

skidder moves timber
In the early morning, the skidder prepared his equipment for another long day in the forest.
Many skidders take pride in their ability to move logs efficiently and safely.
The skidder guided the heavy logs down the slope to the loading area.
