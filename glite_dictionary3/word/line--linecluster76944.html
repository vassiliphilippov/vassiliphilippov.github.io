line

noun
a row of people or things, one behind another
Pronunciation: / laɪn / (AmE), / laɪn / (BrE)
a row or formation of people or things arranged in a straight line, or a boundary or mark in sports or military

Examples

line of people
A long line of cars stretched down the highway during rush hour.
Please form a line so everyone can get their tickets easily.

The referee checked if the puck had crossed the goal line.
The tennis ball landed just inside the baseline line.
Enemy forces broke through the defensive line during the night.
Heavy fighting continued on the eastern line for several days.
The soldiers took up positions along the line before sunrise.
