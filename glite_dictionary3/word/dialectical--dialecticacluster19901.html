dialectical

adjective
relating to logical argument or dialectic
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the art or practice of investigating or discussing the truth of opinions, especially by means of logical argumentation (dialectic).

Examples

dialectical reasoning
Debates about ethics often involve dialectical reasoning to analyze different perspectives.
The philosopher discussed the issue using a dialectical approach.
The theory evolved through a dialectical process of argument and counterargument.
