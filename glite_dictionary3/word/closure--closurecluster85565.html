to closure

verb
end debate by calling for a vote
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊʒər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
To terminate a debate or discussion (especially in a legislative body) by calling for an immediate vote.

Examples

to closure a debate
A member of parliament moved to closure the proceedings and proceed directly to a vote.
Once the opposition finished their remarks, the speaker closured the floor to end further arguments.
The chairman decided to closure the debate after three hours of discussion.
