burrawong

noun
Australian evergreen cycad plant
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrəˌwɒŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌrəˌwɒŋ / (BrE)
A large, attractive, palmlike evergreen cycad native to New South Wales, Australia, known for its glossy leaves and edible seeds.

Examples

burrawong plant leaves
In some gardens, the burrawong is prized for its striking foliage and unique shape.
Many indigenous Australians used the seeds of the burrawong for food after careful preparation.
The burrawong can live for several centuries and adds a prehistoric look to the landscape.
