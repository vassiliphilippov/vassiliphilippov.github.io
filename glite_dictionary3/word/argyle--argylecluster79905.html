argyle

noun
covered metal vessel for hot gravy
Pronunciation: / ˈɑr.ɡaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɑː.ɡaɪl / (BrE)
a covered metal vessel for serving gravy, with a detachable insert for hot water to keep the contents warm

Examples

silver argyle vessel
He polished the antique argyle before the guests arrived for the formal meal.
The silver argyle on the table kept the gravy piping hot throughout dinner.
Victorian households often used an argyle to serve sauces and gravies in style.
