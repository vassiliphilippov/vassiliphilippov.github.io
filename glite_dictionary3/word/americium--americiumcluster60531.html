americium

noun
radioactive chemical element (Am)
Pronunciation: / əˌmɛrˈɪsiəm / (AmE), / əˌmɛrˈɪsiəm / (BrE)
a radioactive metallic chemical element (symbol Am), produced artificially by bombarding uranium with neutrons or helium ions; a transuranic element of the actinide series used in smoke detectors and scientific research.

Examples

americium in smoke detectors
Because americium is radioactive, it must be handled with great care.
Scientists use americium as a source of alpha particles in laboratory experiments.
The smoke detector in my kitchen contains a small amount of americium.
