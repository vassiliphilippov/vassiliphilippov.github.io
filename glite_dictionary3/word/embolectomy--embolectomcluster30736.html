embolectomy

noun
surgical removal of an embolus
Pronunciation: / ˌɛm.boʊˈlɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌɛm.bəˈlɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove an embolus, usually from an artery, to restore blood flow

Examples

embolectomy for blocked artery
Following the embolectomy, blood flow was successfully restored to the patient's limb.
Prompt embolectomy can be life-saving in cases of acute arterial embolism.
The surgeon performed an embolectomy to clear the blocked artery.
