to short

verb
cheat by underpaying
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to give someone less money than they are owed, especially deliberately; to cheat by underpaying

Examples

to short a customer
If you feel you have been shorted, you should talk to the manager immediately.
The cashier accidentally shorted me five dollars when giving me change.
Tourists are sometimes shorted by dishonest taxi drivers in foreign cities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defraud: to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • synonym – underpay: to pay someone less than what is due
