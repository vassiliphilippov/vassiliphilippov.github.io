sagamore

noun
Algonquian tribal chief
Pronunciation: / ˈsæɡəˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈsæɡəmɔː / (BrE)
(historical) A chief or leader of a North American tribe or confederation, especially among Algonquian peoples.

Examples

sagamore of the tribe
Legends say that the sagamore bravely defended his people against rival bands.
Several tribes recognized the authority of a single sagamore in times of crisis.
