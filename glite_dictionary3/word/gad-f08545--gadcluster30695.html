to gad

verb
wander or travel for fun without purpose
Pronunciation: / ɡæd / (AmE), / ɡæd / (BrE)
to go from place to place, especially in a carefree or restless way, for enjoyment or entertainment

Examples

to gad around town
Instead of working, he spent the afternoon gadding around the city with his friends.
On weekends, she loves to gad about town, discovering new cafes and boutiques.
Travel writers often gad from country to country in search of stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meander: to wander aimlessly, often with leisure
  • synonym – roam: to move about aimlessly or without any destination
