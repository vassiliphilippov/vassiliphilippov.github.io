logical

adjective
coherent; orderly and consistent
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑdʒɪkəl /; / ˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
showing clear, consistent, and reasonable structure or reasoning; making sense

Examples

logical argument
For a story to be believable, its plot must be logical and flow naturally.
Her essay presented a logical progression of ideas that was easy to follow.
The architect's design was praised for being both beautiful and logical.
Given her experience and skills, it was a logical decision to hire her for the job.
It was logical to bring an umbrella because the weather forecast predicted rain.
The argument follows a logical sequence, making it easy to understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coherent: (of a logical whole) holding together or making sense as a whole
  • antonym – illogical: not logical; lacking sound reasoning
  • synonym – reasonable: having sound judgment; fair and sensible
