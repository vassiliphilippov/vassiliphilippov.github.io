two-dimensionality

noun
state of having two dimensions
Pronunciation: / tuː dɪˌmɛnʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / tuː dɪˌmɛnʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the property of existing or being measurable in two dimensions, typically length and width, but lacking depth

Examples

two-dimensionality of a shape
In computer graphics, two-dimensionality limits the movement of objects to a plane.
Mathematics often explores the two-dimensionality of geometric figures such as squares and circles.
The two-dimensionality of the painting made it look flat compared to a sculpture.
