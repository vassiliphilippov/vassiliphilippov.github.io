epiphytic

adjective
growing on another plant (not parasitic)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpəˈfɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) living on the surface of another plant and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air, rain, or debris accumulating around it, but not parasitic.

Examples

epiphytic orchid plant
Many orchids are epiphytic, thriving on the branches of rainforest trees.
Some mosses display epiphytic behavior, growing harmlessly on the bark of larger plants.
The epiphytic ferns added a lush green layer to the trunks of the old oaks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – parasitic: (of a plant or organism) living on another for sustenance and causing harm
