to abduct

verb
seize and carry off by force
Pronunciation: / æbˈdʌkt / (AmE), / əbˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to take someone away illegally by force or deception, often to demand a ransom or for other criminal purposes

Examples

to abduct a child
Authorities believe the missing child may have been abducted on her way home from school.
He was shocked to discover a plot to abduct members of the royal family.
The criminals planned to abduct the wealthy businessman's daughter for ransom.

Related Terms

  • synonym – kidnap: to take someone away illegally by force, often to get a ransom
