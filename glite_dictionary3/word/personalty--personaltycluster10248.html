personalty

noun
movable property; not real estate
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrsənəlti / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəlti / (BrE)
(law) movable property; all personal possessions as opposed to real estate (immovable property).

Examples

personalty assets
All the deceased's personalty must be identified for probate purposes.
The will specified how his personalty would be divided among his children.
Unlike real estate, personalty includes items such as cars and jewelry.
