megrim

noun
severe recurring headache; migraine
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːɡrɪm / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡrɪm / (BrE)
A severe recurring headache, often affecting one side of the head; historically used for what is now called a migraine.

Examples

a megrim headache
She suffered from a megrim that left her bedridden for hours.
The doctor suspected her frequent megrim attacks were related to stress.
Victorian novels sometimes mention a megrim as an excuse for avoiding social events.
