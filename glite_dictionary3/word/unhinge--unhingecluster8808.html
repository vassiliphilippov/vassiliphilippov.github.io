to unhinge

verb
make mentally disturbed or upset
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhɪndʒ / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɪndʒ / (BrE)
to disturb the normal operation of someone's mind; to make someone mentally unbalanced, anxious, or upset

Examples

to unhinge a mind
Constant stress at work can unhinge even the most stable person.
Losing her closest friend nearly unhinged her with grief.
The shocking revelation seemed to unhinge him completely.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: to make someone peaceful or less anxious
  • synonym – derange: to cause mental disturbance or insanity
  • synonym – disturb: to interrupt the normal function or peace of mind
