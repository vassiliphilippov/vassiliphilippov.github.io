to abrogate

verb
formally repeal or abolish
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.roʊ.ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæb.rə.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to formally repeal or abolish a law, right, or agreement; to revoke officially

Examples

to abrogate a law
A treaty may be abrogated by mutual consent of the parties involved.
The company attempted to abrogate its contractual obligations after the merger.
The government decided to abrogate the outdated regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – annul: to declare invalid (an official agreement, decision, or result)
  • antonym – enact: to make (a law) officially valid; to put into effect
  • synonym – repeal: to officially revoke (a law or act)
