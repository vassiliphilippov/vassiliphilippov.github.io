Mathew B. Brady

proper noun
U.S. Civil War-era photographer
Pronunciation: / ˈmæθjuː bi ˈbreɪdi / (AmE), / ˈmæθjuː biː ˈbreɪdi / (BrE)
(1823–1896) A pioneering American photographer, renowned for his portraits and as the official photographer of the Union during the American Civil War.

Examples

Mathew B. Brady photographed soldiers
Mathew B. Brady is credited with documenting the horrors of the Civil War through his remarkable photographs.

The legacy of Mathew B. Brady continues to influence photography and historical documentation today.
