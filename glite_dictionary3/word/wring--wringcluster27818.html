to wring

verb
twist and compress (esp. hands in distress)
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
to twist and compress something, especially one's hands, usually as an expression of pain, distress, or anxiety

Examples

to wring your hands
He wrung his hands while trying to find the right words to say.
In moments of grief, people often wring their hands unconsciously.
She began to wring her hands as she waited anxiously for the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clutch: to grasp or hold tightly (esp. in fear, pain, or anxiety)
  • antonym – relax: to make or become less tense or rigid
