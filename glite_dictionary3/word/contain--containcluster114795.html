to contain

verb
prevent the spread or progress of something
Pronunciation: / kənˈteɪn / (AmE), / kənˈteɪn / (BrE)
to keep something, such as danger, disease, or emotions, under control or within limits; to prevent it from spreading or getting out

Officials struggled to contain the wildfire as strong winds continued to drive it north.

Troops were deployed to contain the insurgency in the eastern region.

She took a deep breath, trying to contain her anger during the meeting.
