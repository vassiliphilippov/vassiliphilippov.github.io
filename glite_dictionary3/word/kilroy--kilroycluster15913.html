Kilroy

noun
fictitious figure in WWII graffiti
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪlˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɪl.rɔɪ / (BrE)
a fictitious person whose name appeared in graffiti, especially 'Kilroy was here', popular among American servicemen during World War II to indicate their presence or passage

Examples

Kilroy was here
Soldiers would scrawl 'Kilroy was here' on walls to show they had been there first.
The mysterious figure of Kilroy fascinated both American troops and civilians.
The phrase 'Kilroy was here' became famous during the Second World War.
