minder

noun
government-assigned watcher of visitors
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndə / (BrE)
a person, especially in certain countries with authoritarian governments, assigned to accompany and monitor foreign visitors

Examples

government minder
During the conference, a government-assigned minder followed the journalists everywhere they went.
Foreign delegations were often accompanied by a minder to control what they saw and whom they spoke to.
Our tour guide was friendly, but the official minder kept a close eye on our group.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chaperone: a person who accompanies and looks after another, often to supervise or guide
  • synonym – escort: a person who goes with someone to give protection or guidance
