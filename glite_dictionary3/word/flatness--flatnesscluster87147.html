flatness

noun
lack of contrast or gloss
Pronunciation: / ˈflætnəs / (AmE), / ˈflætnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having little or no contrast, with a lack of highlights or gloss

Examples

flatness in painting
Artists sometimes use flatness to create a muted or subdued mood in their paintings.
She disliked the flatness of the paint, preferring a finish with more shine.
The flatness of the photograph made it appear dull and lifeless.

Related Terms

  • antonym – glossiness: the quality of being shiny and reflective
  • synonym – matteness: the state of having a dull, non-reflective surface
