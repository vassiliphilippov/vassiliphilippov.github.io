botonee

adjective
heraldic cross with button-like ends
Pronunciation: / bəˈtoʊni / (AmE), / bəˈtəʊni / (BrE)
(in heraldry) having a cluster of three rounded projections, resembling buttons or knobs, at the end of each arm (of a cross).

Examples

botonee cross design
A cross botonee is commonly seen in medieval heraldic designs.
In the church window, a botonee cross was prominently displayed.
The family crest features a cross botonee at its center.
