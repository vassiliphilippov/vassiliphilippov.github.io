nitrogenase

noun
enzyme for nitrogen fixation
Pronunciation: / naɪˈtroʊdʒəˌneɪs / (AmE), / naɪˈtrɒdʒəˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme found in nitrogen-fixing microorganisms that catalyzes the conversion of atmospheric nitrogen into ammonia

Examples

nitrogenase enzyme activity
Nitrogenase is essential for the process of nitrogen fixation in certain bacteria.
Researchers study how nitrogenase enables microorganisms to convert nitrogen gas into usable ammonia.
Without nitrogenase, plants could not access sufficient nitrogen from the soil.
