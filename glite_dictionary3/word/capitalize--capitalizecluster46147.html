to capitalize

verb
gain advantage from a situation
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to gain an advantage from a situation, often by using it to one’s own benefit (usually used with 'on')

Examples

to capitalize on success
After the scandal broke, the opposition party tried to capitalize on the government’s misfortune.
Savvy investors capitalize on market fluctuations to maximize their profits.
She managed to capitalize on her networking skills to land a new job.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exploit: to make full use of and derive benefit from (a resource or situation)
  • synonym – take advantage of: to use an opportunity to achieve results, sometimes unfairly
