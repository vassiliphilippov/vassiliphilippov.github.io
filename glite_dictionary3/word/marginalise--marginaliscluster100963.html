to marginalise

verb
to treat as unimportant or peripheral
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrdʒənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat a person, group, or concept as insignificant or peripheral; to push to the margins of society or a group

Examples

to marginalise minority groups
Her ideas were often marginalised during meetings, despite their value.
Many refugees feel that society has marginalised them, leaving them without support or opportunities.
Policies that marginalise certain communities can have lasting negative consequences.

Related Terms

  • antonym – empower: to give power or authority to someone
  • synonym – exclude: to deliberately leave out or deny access
