transposition

noun
moving a term to the other side of an equation
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Mathematics) the process of moving a quantity from one side of an equation to the other with a change of sign.

Examples

transposition of terms
Incorrect transposition of terms can lead to errors in solving equations.
The teacher explained the concept of transposition to help students rearrange algebraic expressions.
When solving equations, transposition is often used to isolate the variable.
