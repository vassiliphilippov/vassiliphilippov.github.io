hypoglycaemia

noun
abnormally low blood sugar
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊɡlaɪˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the level of sugar in the blood falls abnormally low, often due to excessive insulin or inadequate food intake.

Examples

hypoglycaemia episode
A poor diet can sometimes result in episodes of hypoglycaemia.
People with diabetes must monitor their blood sugar to avoid hypoglycaemia.
Symptoms of hypoglycaemia include sweating, confusion, and shakiness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hyperglycaemia: an excess of glucose in the bloodstream
