dolphin

noun
a large food and game fish (mahi-mahi)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːlfɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪn / (BrE)
A large, slender food and game fish (Coryphaena hippurus) found in warm seas, especially around Hawaii, known for its brightly colored body and also called mahi-mahi or dorado.

Examples

fresh dolphin fillet
Fishermen in Hawaii often target dolphin for both sport and food.
The chef prepared a delicious grilled dolphin with tropical salsa.
Unlike the mammal, the dolphin fish is best known for its dazzling colors and speed.
