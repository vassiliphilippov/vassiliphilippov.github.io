nemesis

noun
an agent of downfall or retribution
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛmɪsɪs / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the source of someone's downfall or undoing, especially one that cannot be avoided

Examples

nemesis brings defeat
For years, his arrogant attitude proved to be his greatest nemesis.
Procrastination is often the nemesis of productivity.
Rising sea levels are a potential nemesis for many coastal cities.
