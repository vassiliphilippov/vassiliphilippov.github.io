newsworthiness

noun
quality making something suitable for news coverage
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːzˌwɝːði.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌwɜː.ði.nəs / (BrE)
the quality that makes an event, story, or subject sufficiently interesting or important to merit coverage in the news

Examples

newsworthiness of a story
During the meeting, they debated the newsworthiness of several breaking stories.
Some stories are published mainly because of their sensational newsworthiness, not their importance.
The editor questioned the newsworthiness of the celebrity gossip piece submitted by the reporter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – newsworthy: interesting or important enough to be reported as news
