teacupful

noun
the amount a teacup holds
Pronunciation: / ˈtiˌkʌpˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌkʌpˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to what a teacup can hold, used as a measure especially in recipes.

Examples

teacupful of milk
Add a teacupful of milk to the mixture and stir well.
She measured out a teacupful of flour for the cake.
The recipe calls for one teacupful of sugar.
