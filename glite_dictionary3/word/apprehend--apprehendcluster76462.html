to apprehend

verb
arrest or take into custody
Pronunciation: / ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌaprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to arrest or take someone into custody, especially by legal authority

Examples

to apprehend a suspect
After a long chase, authorities finally apprehended the fugitive near the city border.
It took several days for investigators to apprehend all members of the criminal gang.
The police managed to apprehend the thief before he escaped with the valuables.
