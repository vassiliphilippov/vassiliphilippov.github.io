specialization

noun
biological adaptation of a body part
Pronunciation: / ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which a body part or structure becomes adapted for a specific function.

Examples

cell specialization
During embryonic development, specialization allows stem cells to become muscle or nerve cells.
Nerve cells exhibit a high degree of specialization for transmitting electrical signals.
The specialization of bird beaks enables them to access different food sources.

  • synonym – differentiation: process by which cells or tissues become specialized
