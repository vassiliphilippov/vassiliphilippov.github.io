nontransmissible

adjective
not able to be transmitted (esp. disease)
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑntrænzˈmɪsəbəl /; / ˌnɑn.trænzˈmɪs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒntrænzˈmɪsəbəl /; / ˌnɒn.trænzˈmɪs.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
not able to be passed or spread from one person or organism to another (such as a disease or characteristic)

Examples

nontransmissible disease
It is important to distinguish between nontransmissible and infectious diseases when developing health policies.
One of the main focuses of public health is the prevention of nontransmissible diseases such as diabetes.
Unlike the common cold, heart disease is a nontransmissible condition.
Doctors reassured the public that the condition is nontransmissible and poses no infectious risk.
Genetic disorders can be classified as nontransmissible if they cannot be passed down to offspring.
Some traits are nontransmissible, meaning they are acquired, not inherited.
