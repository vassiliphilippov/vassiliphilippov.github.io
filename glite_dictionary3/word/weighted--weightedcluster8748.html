weighted

adjective
adjusted by importance or value
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪd / (BrE)
adjusted by giving different values, importance, or factors more influence than others

Examples

weighted voting system
Our club uses a weighted voting system to give larger groups more say in decisions.
The weighted scores ensure that more important tasks influence the final result more heavily.
The report includes both simple and weighted averages for comparison.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unweighted: not adjusted by importance or value
