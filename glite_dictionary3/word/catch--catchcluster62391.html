to catch

verb
reach in time for transport or event
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / katʃ / (BrE)
to reach in time so as not to miss (a form of transport, event, etc.)

Examples

to catch a bus
Did you manage to catch the beginning of the show last night?
I have to catch a train at 7 o'clock, so I can't be late.
She ran to catch the bus before it left the stop.

Related Terms

  • antonym – miss: to fail to reach in time for something such as a bus or event
