ethnologist

noun
a specialist in ethnology; cultural anthropologist
Pronunciation: / ɛθˈnɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ɛθˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies or is an expert in ethnology, the branch of anthropology that analyzes cultures and their characteristics, relationships, and development.

Examples

ethnologist studies cultures
A leading ethnologist delivered a lecture on the social structures of Pacific Island communities.
As an ethnologist, she published several influential papers on kinship patterns in Africa.
The ethnologist spent years living among the Amazonian tribe to document their cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anthropologist: a scientist who studies humans and their societies, past and present
  • synonym – ethnology: the scientific study of the characteristics and differences of peoples and cultures
