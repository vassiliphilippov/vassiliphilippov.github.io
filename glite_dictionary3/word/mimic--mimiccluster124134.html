mimic

adjective
imitating or resembling something
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪk / (BrE)
imitating or resembling something, especially in order to appear like it; constituting an imitation.

Examples

mimic butterfly wings
Some insects have developed mimic coloration to resemble dangerous species and avoid predators.
The museum displayed mimic artifacts created to give visitors a lifelike sense of the originals.
The performance featured dazzling mimic battles to create a sense of historical realism.

Related Terms

  • antonym – authentic: genuine; not a copy or imitation
  • synonym – imitative: tending to imitate or copy something
