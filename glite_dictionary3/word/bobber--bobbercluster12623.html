bobber

noun
fishing line float
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑbər / (AmE), / ˈbɒbə / (BrE)
a small float, typically made of cork or plastic, attached to a fishing line to indicate when a fish bites

Examples

fishing bobber
A red-and-white bobber drifted lazily on the surface of the lake.
He watched the bobber carefully, waiting for it to dip below the water.
The fish tugged the line, pulling the bobber under.

Related Terms

  • synonym – float: fishing device that stays on the surface of water to indicate a bite
