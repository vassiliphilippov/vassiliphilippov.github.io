to librate

verb
to oscillate before stopping
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪˌbreɪt / (BrE)
to oscillate or move back and forth, especially before coming to a complete stop

Examples

to librate like a pendulum
As the pendulum slowed, it began to librate before settling to a stop.
The bell continued to librate gently after being struck.
The hanging sign would librate in the wind before finally becoming still.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oscillate: move or swing back and forth at a regular speed
  • synonym – vibrate: move continuously and rapidly back and forth
