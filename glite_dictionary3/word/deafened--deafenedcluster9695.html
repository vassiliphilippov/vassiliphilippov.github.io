deafened

adjective
having lost the ability to hear
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfənd / (AmE), / ˈdɛfənd / (BrE)
Having lost the ability to hear, either completely or partially, especially as a result of noise or injury.

Examples

deafened by loud noise
He was deafened by the explosion and fitted with a hearing aid.
Many workers became deafened after years in the noisy factory.
She was temporarily deafened after standing too close to the speakers at the concert.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deaf: unable to hear, lacking the sense of hearing
  • antonym – hearing: the ability to perceive sound
