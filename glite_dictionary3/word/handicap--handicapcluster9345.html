to handicap

verb
predict winner and set odds (esp. in racing)
Pronunciation: / ˈhændiˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈhandɪkap / (BrE)
to attempt to predict the outcome of a contest, especially a horse race, and set odds for or against a participant

Examples

to handicap a race
Bookmakers often handicap matches to set fair odds for punters.
Experts will handicap the upcoming derby based on each horse's recent performances.
He has been handicapping sporting events for over a decade and is known for his accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forecast: to estimate or calculate in advance, especially as regards competition outcomes
  • synonym – predict: to say what will happen in the future, especially based on knowledge or experience
