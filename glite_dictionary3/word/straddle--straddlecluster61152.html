straddle

noun
noncommittal or equivocal position
Pronunciation: / ˈstræd.əl / (AmE), / ˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
a position or policy of refusing to commit fully to a single side or opinion, especially to avoid taking risks or offending others

Examples

a political straddle
By maintaining a straddle, the company avoided alienating any part of its customer base.
His constant straddle between opposing opinions frustrated his colleagues.
The politician's straddle on the key issue disappointed both sides of the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – commitment: the state or quality of being dedicated to a cause or activity
  • synonym – neutrality: the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
