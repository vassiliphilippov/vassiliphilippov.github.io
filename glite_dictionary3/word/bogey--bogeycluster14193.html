to bogey

verb
score one over par (golf)
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
(in golf) to score one stroke over par on a hole.

Examples

to bogey the last hole
He bogeyed the last three holes and missed qualifying for the tournament.
If you bogey every hole, you'll finish well above par.
She managed to bogey the 18th hole after a tricky putt.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – birdie: to score one stroke under par in golf
  • degree lower – par: the number of strokes a skilled golfer is expected to take for a hole or course
