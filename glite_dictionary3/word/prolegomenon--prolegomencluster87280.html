prolegomenon

noun
introductory essay to a book or treatise
Pronunciation: / proʊˌlɛˈɡɑməˌnɑn / (AmE), / prəʊˌlɛˈɡɒmɪˌnɒn / (BrE)
an introductory essay or critical discussion at the beginning of a book or treatise, intended to introduce its themes or scope

Examples

prolegomenon to a book
A brief prolegomenon can help readers understand the aims of a philosophical treatise.
In his prolegomenon, the editor outlined the historical background of the text.
The author's prolegomenon provided valuable context for the complex arguments that followed.
