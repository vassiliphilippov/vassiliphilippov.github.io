Sturmabteilung

noun
Nazi paramilitary militia, 1921–1943
Pronunciation: / ʃtʊrmˈaːptaɪlʊŋ / (AmE), / ʃtʊəmˈaptaɪlʊŋ / (BrE)
The Nazi militia formed by Adolf Hitler in 1921, which played a key role in his rise to power but was later overshadowed by the SS after 1943.

Examples

the Sturmabteilung militia
After 1943, the Sturmabteilung lost most of its influence within the Nazi Party.
Many members of the Sturmabteilung were purged during the Night of the Long Knives.
The Sturmabteilung played a central role in Hitler’s consolidation of power in the early 1930s.
