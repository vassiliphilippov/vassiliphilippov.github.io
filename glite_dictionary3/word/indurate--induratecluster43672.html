to indurate

verb
to become fixed or established
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndjʊˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪndjʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of attitudes, customs, etc.) to become firmly established or fixed and resistant to change.

Examples

to indurate old habits
Old prejudices can indurate if left unchallenged over time.
Over centuries, these traditions began to indurate and resist outside influence.
The social norms of the village slowly indurated into unchanging customs.
