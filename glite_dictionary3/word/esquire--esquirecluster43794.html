esquire

noun
title of respect for a man, below a knight
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskwaɪər / (AmE), / ɪˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
a title of respect placed after a man's name, originally for members of the English gentry ranking just below a knight

Examples

Mr. Smith, Esquire
He signed the letter as John Smith, Esquire, to show his professional status.
Most British lawyers use Esquire after their names in formal correspondence.
The title Esquire was traditionally used for members of the English gentry.

Related Terms

  • synonym – squire: young nobleman acting as an attendant to a knight; also a country gentleman
