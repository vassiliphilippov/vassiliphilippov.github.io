subsidized

adjective
supported by public funds
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.sɪˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd / (BrE)
Partly paid for by public funds to reduce the cost for users or recipients.

Examples

subsidized housing
He moved into a subsidized apartment after losing his job.
Many families rely on subsidized child care to balance work and home responsibilities.
The museum offers subsidized tickets for students and senior citizens.
