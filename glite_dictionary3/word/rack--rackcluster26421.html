to rack

verb
torment emotionally or mentally
Pronunciation: / ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
to strain, torment, or cause someone severe emotional or mental anguish; to stretch or stress greatly

Examples

to rack your brain
Guilt continued to rack him long after the incident.
She was racked with worry about her son's health.
The memory of his mistake would rack his mind for years.
He racked his brains trying to remember her name, but it just wouldn't come to him.
She racked her memory for any clue that could explain the sudden disappearance.
The country has been racked by years of civil war and instability.
