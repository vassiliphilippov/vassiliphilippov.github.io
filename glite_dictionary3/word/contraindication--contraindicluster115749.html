contraindication

noun
reason to avoid medical treatment
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.trəˌɪn.dəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˌɪn.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(medicine) A condition or factor that serves as a reason to withhold a certain medical treatment or procedure due to the harm it would cause to the patient.

Examples

serious contraindication
A known allergy to penicillin is a clear contraindication for its use.
Before prescribing, doctors carefully assess for any possible contraindication to the procedure.
Pregnancy is a contraindication for taking this particular medication.

Related Terms

  • antonym – indication: A reason to use a specific treatment or procedure
