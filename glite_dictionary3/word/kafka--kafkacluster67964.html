Kafka

proper noun
Czech novelist and short-story writer
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːfkə / (AmE), / ˈkæfkə / (BrE)
Franz Kafka (1883–1924), a Czech-born German-speaking novelist and short-story writer whose works explore themes of alienation and surreal, nightmarish complexity.

Examples

Kafka novels
Many literary critics consider Kafka's novels to be among the most influential of the twentieth century.
Students in literature classes often study the works of Kafka to understand modern existentialism.
The term 'Kafkaesque' comes from the strange and unsettling worlds depicted by Kafka.
