to sustain

verb
provide with necessities or support
Pronunciation: / səˈsteɪn / (AmE), / səˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to provide support, necessities, or nourishment to keep someone or something alive, existing, or continuing; to maintain something over time

Examples

sustain a family
After he lost his job, Mark struggled to sustain his family and cover the basic bills.
Plants need sunlight and water to sustain their growth throughout the year.
The grant will help sustain community programs that support low-income families.
It is important to sustain efforts to improve public health over time.
She tried to sustain the note for as long as possible during her vocal performance.
The company was able to sustain its growth for several years.
Fresh water and fruit sustain the isolated community on the island.
The hikers sustained themselves on nuts and dried fruit during their trek.
This diet barely sustains the prisoners' basic needs for survival.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cease: to bring or come to an end
  • synonym – continue: to persist or keep going
  • synonym – maintain: to keep in existence or continuance; preserve
  • synonym – nourish: to provide with the food or other substances necessary for growth and health
  • synonym – prolong: to make something last longer
  • antonym – starve: to suffer or die from lack of food
  • synonym – support: to bear all or part of the weight; to give assistance to
