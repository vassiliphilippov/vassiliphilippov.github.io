trawl

noun
long fishing line with many hooks
Pronunciation: / trɔl / (AmE), / trɔːl / (BrE)
a long fishing line with many shorter lines and hooks attached, usually suspended between buoys, used for catching fish

Examples

trawl fishing line
After several hours, they pulled up the trawl filled with a variety of sea creatures.
He lowered the trawl into the water, making sure every hook was properly baited.
The fishermen checked the trawl each morning to see how many fish they had caught.
