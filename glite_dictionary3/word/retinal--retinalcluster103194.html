retinal

noun
a light-sensitive pigment in the retina
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛt.ən.əl / (AmE), / ˈrɛt.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
a yellow to red pigment derived from vitamin A, found in the retina and essential for vision

Examples

retinal pigment
Retinal plays a critical role in the conversion of light to visual signals in the eye.
Vitamin A deficiency can affect the production of retinal and lead to vision problems.
When exposed to light, retinal changes shape, triggering a nerve impulse.
