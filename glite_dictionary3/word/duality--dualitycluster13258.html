duality

noun
the quality of having two parts or natures
Pronunciation: / duˈæləti / (AmE), / djʊˈalɪti / (BrE)
The state of having two different or opposite parts or features; a division into two contrasting or complementary aspects.

Examples

the duality of human nature
Quantum physics often deals with the duality of particles behaving both as waves and as discrete objects.
The novel explores the duality of human nature through its main characters.
There is a duality in her personality: she is both reserved and outgoing depending on the situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dichotomy: a division into two mutually exclusive or contradictory groups
  • antonym – unity: the state of being united or forming a whole
