endorphin

noun
natural brain chemical with pain-relieving effects
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdɔɹfɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɔːfɪn / (BrE)
a neurochemical produced naturally in the brain that acts as a pain reliever and promotes a feeling of well-being

Examples

release endorphin after exercise
Laughter and positive social interactions can boost endorphin release, improving mood.
Scientists discovered that endorphin levels rise after vigorous exercise, which helps explain the 'runner's high.'
The body's natural endorphin production is thought to help reduce stress and pain.
