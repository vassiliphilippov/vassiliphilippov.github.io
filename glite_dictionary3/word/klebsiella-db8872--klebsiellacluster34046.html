klebsiella

noun
genus of disease-causing bacteria
Pronunciation: / klɛb.siˈɛl.ə / (AmE), / klɛb.siˈɛl.ə / (BrE)
a genus of nonmotile, rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria, some species of which can cause respiratory and other infections in humans

Examples

klebsiella infection
Klebsiella pneumoniae is a common cause of hospital-acquired pneumonia.
Infections caused by klebsiella can be difficult to treat due to antibiotic resistance.
