to spare

verb
use something frugally or with restraint
Pronunciation: / spɛr / (AmE), / spɛə / (BrE)
to give or allow something that you can afford to give, or to use something carefully and not waste it

Examples

to spare some change
During the drought, everyone had to spare water as much as possible.
She spared her words, choosing to speak only when necessary.
We must spare our dwindling food supplies to make them last the whole journey.
Can you spare a few moments to talk before the meeting starts?
I'm afraid I can't spare any cash right now, as I need it for bills.
She kindly spared some old clothes for the shelter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conserve: to use something carefully to avoid waste
  • synonym – donate: to give something, often to charity or in a helpful way
  • antonym – waste: to use something carelessly or extravagantly
