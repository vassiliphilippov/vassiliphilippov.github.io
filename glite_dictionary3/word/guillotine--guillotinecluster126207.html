guillotine

noun
execution device with falling blade
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪl.ə.tin / (AmE), / ˈɡɪl.ə.tiːn / (BrE)
a device with a heavy, angled blade held between upright posts, used for beheading people, especially as a form of execution

Examples

the guillotine blade
A replica of the guillotine is displayed in the museum's section on capital punishment.
During the French Revolution, many prisoners were executed by the guillotine.
The invention of the guillotine was intended to make executions more humane.
