hesitant

adjective
unwilling or slow to act; uncertain
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛzɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈhɛzɪtənt / (BrE)
slow to act or speak because of uncertainty or lack of confidence.

Examples

hesitant to answer
Despite the offer, she remained hesitant to accept the job.
His hesitant steps revealed his uncertainty about the decision.
She gave a hesitant reply, unsure of what to say next.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decisive: having the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently
  • synonym – reluctant: unwilling or slow to act; hesitant
