John James Audubon

proper noun
American ornithologist and bird artist
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈdʒeɪmz ˈɔdəˌbɑn / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈdʒeɪmz ˈɔːdəbɒn / (BrE)
(1785–1851) An American ornithologist and artist renowned for his detailed illustrations of North American birds, particularly in his work 'The Birds of America'.

Examples

John James Audubon painted birds
John James Audubon traveled across the United States to observe and paint hundreds of bird species.
Her collection includes a rare edition of John James Audubon's 'The Birds of America'.
Many bird conservation societies are named after John James Audubon in honor of his legacy.
