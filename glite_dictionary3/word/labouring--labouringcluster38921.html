labouring

adjective
doing hard or unpleasant work
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) engaged in hard or arduous work, especially work that is physically demanding or unpleasant.

Examples

labouring workers
Many labouring miners toiled deep underground to extract coal for the community.
She admired the resilience of labouring mothers working tirelessly to support their families.
The labouring workers spent hours in the fields every day under the hot sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drudging: doing tedious, menial, or unpleasant work
  • antonym – idle: not working or active
  • synonym – toiling: engaged in hard, continuous work
