Argus

proper noun
giant with one hundred eyes in Greek myth
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrɡəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a giant with one hundred eyes who was appointed by Hera to guard the heifer Io and was killed by Hermes.

Examples

Argus guards Io
Hera chose Argus to guard Io because he never fully slept.
In Greek mythology, Argus was famous for his hundred watchful eyes.
The legend of Argus inspired the phrase 'ever watchful.'
