Hibiscus elatus

noun
Caribbean forest tree with valuable timber
Pronunciation: / haɪˈbɪskəs ɪˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs ɪˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
an erect forest tree native to Cuba and Jamaica, with variably hairy leaves and orange-yellow or orange-red flowers, valued for its moderately dense timber used in cabinetwork and gunstocks
