naive

adjective
art/style by untrained creator, simple in form
Pronunciation: / naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
(of art or style) produced by someone without formal training, characterized by simplicity or a childlike quality.

Examples

naive painting style
Critics praised the naive style of his drawings for their freshness and sincerity.
Her naive sculptures possess a raw, honest charm often missing from academic works.
The exhibition features several naive paintings by local, self-taught artists.

Related Terms

  • synonym – primitive: art or style produced in a simple, unsophisticated manner, often by self-taught artists
