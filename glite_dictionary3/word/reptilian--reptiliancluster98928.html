reptilian

noun
cold-blooded vertebrate of class Reptilia
Pronunciation: / rɛpˈtɪliən / (AmE), / rɛpˈtɪliən / (BrE)
any cold-blooded vertebrate belonging to the class Reptilia, including snakes, lizards, turtles, crocodiles, and extinct forms such as dinosaurs

Examples

ancient reptilian fossils
Scientists discovered fossilized remains of a new reptilian species in the desert.
Some reptilian traits have remained largely unchanged for millions of years.
The Komodo dragon is the largest living reptilian on Earth today.
