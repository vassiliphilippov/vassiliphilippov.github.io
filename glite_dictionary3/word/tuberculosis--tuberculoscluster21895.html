tuberculosis

noun
infectious disease, primarily of the lungs
Pronunciation: / tuˌbɜrkjəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / tjuˌbɜːkjʊˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis, typically affecting the lungs but capable of involving other parts of the body; symptoms include fever, coughing, and the formation of small lesions

Examples

tuberculosis infection
Early screening and treatment can significantly improve the prognosis for patients with tuberculosis.
She was hospitalized for several weeks due to a severe case of tuberculosis.
The doctor explained that tuberculosis is most often spread through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs.
