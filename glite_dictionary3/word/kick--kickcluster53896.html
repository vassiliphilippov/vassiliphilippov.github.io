to kick

verb
spring back suddenly (often of guns, machinery)
Pronunciation: / kɪk / (AmE), / kɪk / (BrE)
(of a gun or machine) to spring back suddenly as a result of a forceful thrust or impact.

Examples

the machine kicks back
Be careful—the machinery may kick unexpectedly if something jams.
The old rifle kicked hard against his shoulder when fired.
The pistol kicks less than the shotgun, making it easier for beginners.
