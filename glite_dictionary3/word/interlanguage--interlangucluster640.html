interlanguage

noun
language used for communication between speakers of different native languages
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪntəlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a language or dialect systematically used by speakers of different native languages to communicate with each other

Examples

interlanguage for communication
English often serves as an interlanguage for international business meetings.
Swahili developed as an interlanguage among traders on the East African coast.
The conference was held in French, the chosen interlanguage for all participants.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lingua franca: a language systematically used to make communication possible between people not sharing a native language
