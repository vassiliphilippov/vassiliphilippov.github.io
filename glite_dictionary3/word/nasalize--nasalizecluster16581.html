to nasalize

verb
pronounce a sound through the nose
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪzəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəlaɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce a sound, especially a vowel, with air escaping through the nose by lowering the velum

Examples

to nasalize a vowel
In French, the letter 'n' after a vowel will often cause it to be nasalized.
Some regional accents tend to nasalize certain vowels more than others.
The speech coach taught her not to nasalize her consonants when speaking.

Related Terms

  • antonym – denasalize: to pronounce a sound without nasal resonance
  • synonym – nasal: a sound produced with air flowing through the nose
