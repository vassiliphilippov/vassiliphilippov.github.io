oilrig

noun
structure for drilling oil or gas
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪlˌrɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌrɪɡ / (BrE)
A large structure with equipment used to drill for oil or gas, either on land or at sea.

Examples

offshore oilrig
An explosion on the oilrig caused significant environmental damage.
The workers spent months living on the oilrig in the North Sea.
They installed a new type of drill on the oilrig to increase efficiency.
