foulness

noun
state of being dirty or filthy
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being dirty, filthy, or offensive, especially from accumulated waste or refuse.

Examples

foulness of the streets
She was appalled by the foulness that covered the city streets after the festival.
The foulness of the abandoned building made it uninhabitable for anyone.
Years of neglect had led to a level of foulness that was hard to remove.

  • synonym – filth: Extremely dirty or unpleasant condition
