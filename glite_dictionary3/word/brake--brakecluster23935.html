brake

noun
thing that slows or restrains progress
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
A thing that restrains, hinders, or slows progress or development.

Examples

brake on progress
Strict regulations can serve as a brake to business innovation.
The sudden rise in oil prices acted as a brake on economic growth.
The team's lack of experience proved to be a brake on their progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catalyst: something that accelerates a process
  • synonym – impediment: something that obstructs or slows progress
  • synonym – restraint: something that limits freedom or action
