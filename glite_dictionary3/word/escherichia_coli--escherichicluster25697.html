Escherichia coli

noun
bacterium found in intestines, can cause illness
Pronunciation: / ˌɛʃəˈrɪkiə ˈkoʊˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛʃəˈrɪkiə ˈkəʊlaɪ / (BrE)
A species of bacterium commonly found in the intestines of humans and other animals; some strains may cause illness in humans.

Examples

Escherichia coli infection
Proper handwashing helps prevent infections from harmful strains of Escherichia coli.
