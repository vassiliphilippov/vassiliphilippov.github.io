canalis cervicis uteri

noun
cervical canal in the female reproductive system
Pronunciation: / kəˈneɪlɪs ˌsɜːrˈviːs juːˈtɛraɪ / (AmE), / kəˈneɪlɪs ˌsɜːvɪs juːˈtɛraɪ / (BrE)
A spindle-shaped canal in the cervix that extends from the uterus to the vagina.

Examples

The canalis cervicis uteri is lined with mucus-secreting cells to protect the uterus from infections.
