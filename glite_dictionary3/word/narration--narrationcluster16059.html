narration

noun
rhetorical section presenting facts
Pronunciation: / nəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / nəˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(in rhetoric) the part of a speech following the introduction, in which the facts of the case are presented.

Examples

narration in a speech
Following the introduction, the speaker moved smoothly into the narration of the case.
In classical rhetoric, the narration lays out the background necessary for the argument.
The lawyer's narration was clear and concise, detailing all relevant facts.
