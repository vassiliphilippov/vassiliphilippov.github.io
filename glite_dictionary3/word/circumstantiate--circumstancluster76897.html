to circumstantiate

verb
provide circumstantial evidence
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrkəmˈstænʃieɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstænʃieɪt / (BrE)
To provide or support with circumstantial evidence; to make clear by giving details or specific facts.

Examples

to circumstantiate a claim
She failed to circumstantiate her accusations with any actual facts or context.
The lawyer attempted to circumstantiate his client's alibi with several witnesses.
The report circumstantiated its claims with a wealth of supporting details.
