unbroken

adjective
not plowed or cultivated (of land)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
(of land or farmland) not plowed or cultivated; left in its natural state.

Examples

unbroken land
Much of the region remains unbroken, home to native plants and wildlife.
The developers bought a vast expanse of unbroken land at the edge of town.
The settlers gazed across the unbroken prairie, planning where they would first begin to farm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – plowed: (of land) land that has been turned over with a plow
