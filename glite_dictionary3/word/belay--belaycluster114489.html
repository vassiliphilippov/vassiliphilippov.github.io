to belay

verb
secure a boat by tying it to something
Pronunciation: / bəˈleɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
to secure or fasten a rope by winding it around an object, especially for safety in climbing or to tie up a boat

Examples

to belay a rope
Always belay the rope securely to prevent the vessel from drifting away.
The sailor quickly belayed the boat to the dock before unloading the cargo.
They belayed the yacht to a sturdy cleat on the pier.
She belaying the rope quickly as her friend ascended the cliff.
The climber carefully belayed the rope around the sturdy rock to ensure safety.
You must always belay your partner properly before they start to climb.
