displacement

noun
movement from an original position
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the movement of something from its original position, especially measured as a straight line from start to end

Examples

displacement in distance
In physics, displacement is measured as a straight line from the starting point to the end point.
The earthquake caused the displacement of several buildings from their foundations.
The sudden displacement of the train tracks disrupted the entire railway service.
Her calculations showed the displacement of the particle after five seconds of motion.
In physics, displacement is different from distance because it has both magnitude and direction.
The displacement of the car was measured as 100 meters east from its original position.
