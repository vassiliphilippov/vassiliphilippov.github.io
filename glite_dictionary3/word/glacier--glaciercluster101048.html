glacier

noun
slowly moving mass of ice
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈɡlæsiə / (BrE)
a large mass of ice that moves slowly over land, typically forming in areas where more snow falls than melts

Examples

melting glacier
Climate change has caused many glaciers around the world to shrink.
Hikers marveled at the massive glacier nestled between the mountains.
The Columbia glacier is retreating rapidly due to rising temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – iceberg: a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier or ice shelf
