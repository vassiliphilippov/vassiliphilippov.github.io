to dim

verb
become vague or less clear
Pronunciation: / dɪm / (AmE), / dɪm / (BrE)
(of something perceived or understood) to become vague, indistinct, or less clear.

Examples

to dim memories
As memories of their childhood friendship dim, they find themselves growing apart.
Over time, the details of the legend have begun to dim in people's minds.
The line between right and wrong can dim in difficult situations.

  • antonym – sharpen: become clearer or more distinct
