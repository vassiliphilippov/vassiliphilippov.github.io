liberation

noun
the act of freeing someone or something
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of setting someone or a group free from control, oppression, or discrimination

Examples

liberation of people
She fought tirelessly for the liberation of women from oppression.
The army's arrival brought liberation to the prisoners of war.
The country's liberation was celebrated by all its citizens.
Feminist groups continue their fight for women's liberation worldwide.
Many artists have expressed themes of liberation in their work.
The civil rights movement played a major role in the liberation of African Americans.

Related Terms

  • antonym – captivity: the condition of being imprisoned or confined
  • synonym – emancipation: the process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • synonym – freedom: the state of being free rather than in confinement or under physical restraint
  • antonym – oppression: prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
