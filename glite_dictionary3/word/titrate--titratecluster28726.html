to titrate

verb
adjust medication dose gradually
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
(medicine) to adjust the dose of a drug gradually, increasing or decreasing it until the desired therapeutic effect or tolerance is reached.

Examples

to titrate a dose
The doctor will titrate the patient's antidepressant slowly until symptoms are controlled without severe side effects.
Nurses spent weeks titrating the opioid regimen to find a balance between pain relief and safety.
After starting prednisone, his physician titrated the dose down over several days to reduce withdrawal risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adjust: to change something slightly in order to achieve the desired fit, appearance, or result (applied here to doses).
  • synonym – titration: the process of adjusting or measuring dose/concentration; in medicine, adjusting drug dosage.
