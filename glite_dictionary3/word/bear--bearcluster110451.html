to bear

verb
to be pregnant with (a child)
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / beə / (BrE)
to give birth to or produce offspring, or (of a plant) to produce fruit or flowers

Examples

to bear a child
In the poem, the queen bears a son destined to rule the kingdom.
It is not easy to bear a child in times of war.
She was said to be bearing twins that summer.
Every year, the ewe bears a pair of lambs in the spring.
She bravely bore three children during the war.
She has borne him a healthy son.
Some fruit trees bear fruit only after several years of growth.
That old rose bush still bears beautiful flowers every spring.
The hen bore several chicks during the summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – give birth: to produce offspring; to have a baby
  • synonym – produce: to bring forth or yield, especially as a result of natural growth or development
