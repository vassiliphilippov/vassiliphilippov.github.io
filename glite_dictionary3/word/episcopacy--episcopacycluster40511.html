episcopacy

noun
the body of bishops or their system
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɪskəˌpæsi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪskəpəsi / (BrE)
the collective body of bishops in a church, or the system of church government overseen by bishops

Examples

episcopacy in the church
Debates over episcopacy have caused major divisions among Protestant denominations.
He studied the structure and influence of episcopacy in Anglican tradition.
The rise of episcopacy in the early church shaped much of Christian history.
