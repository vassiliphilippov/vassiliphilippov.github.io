blowback

noun
recirculated misinformation from own intelligence
Pronunciation: / ˈbloʊˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌbæk / (BrE)
misinformation that returns to the country where it originated after being spread abroad by its own intelligence services

Examples

blowback from propaganda
Analysts expressed concern about blowback from disinformation campaigns targeting foreign media.
Recent reports highlighted how blowback can undermine domestic trust in official sources.
The government's secret operation led to unexpected blowback when the false story resurfaced at home.
