to chaffer

verb
to chat casually and inconsequentially
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæfər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːfə / (BrE)
to talk in a friendly way without sharing anything important; to engage in light, inconsequential conversation

Examples

to chaffer with friends
The two old friends sat on the porch and chaffered until dusk.
They chaffered by the market stall, exchanging stories along with their goods.
While waiting for the train, the travelers chaffered about the weather and their plans.

  • synonym – chitchat: casual conversation about unimportant things
