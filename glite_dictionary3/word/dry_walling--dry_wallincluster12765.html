dry walling

noun
building stone walls without mortar
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ ˌwɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌwɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of building stone walls without using mortar to bind the stones together

Examples

dry walling stone fences
Skilled craftsmen are required for dry walling because it demands precision in placing each stone.
The landscape is dotted with old walls constructed using the dry walling method.
Traditional farmers practiced dry walling to build field boundaries that would last for generations.
