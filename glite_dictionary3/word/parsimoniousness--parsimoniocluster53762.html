parsimoniousness

noun
extreme reluctance to spend money
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrsɪˈmoʊniəsnəs /; / ˌpɑːrsəˈmoʊniəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːsɪˈməʊniəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very unwilling to spend money or use resources; extreme stinginess or frugality

Examples

her parsimoniousness with money
Despite her wealth, her parsimoniousness was evident in her reluctance to spend money.
His parsimoniousness made him unpopular among friends who expected more generosity.
The company's parsimoniousness led to outdated equipment and unhappy staff.
Because of his parsimoniousness, he preferred to repair broken appliances instead of buying new ones.
Her parsimoniousness was evident in her careful budgeting and reluctance to make unnecessary purchases.
The company's parsimoniousness helped it survive during tough economic times, though employees sometimes complained about the lack of perks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – frugality: the quality of being economical with money or resources
  • antonym – generosity: the quality of being generous; willingness to give
  • synonym – stinginess: the quality of being unwilling to spend money
