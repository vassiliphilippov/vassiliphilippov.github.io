firewall

noun
aviation: application of maximum thrust
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwɔːl / (BrE)
(colloquial, aviation) The act of applying maximum thrust by pushing the throttle fully forward.

Examples

to firewall the throttle
In an emergency, it's common to move the throttle to the firewall for a quick escape.
The pilot pushed the throttle to the firewall and the jet surged forward.
With the runway running out, she slammed the lever all the way to the firewall.
