toxin

noun
poison produced by living organisms
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous substance produced by certain microorganisms, plants, or animals, especially as a result of their metabolism and growth

Examples

dangerous toxin
After the snakebite, the doctor treated her to counteract the toxin in her bloodstream.
Certain bacteria release a toxin that can cause food poisoning if ingested.
Some plants produce a natural toxin to deter animals from eating them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – poison: a substance that is capable of causing the illness or death of a living organism when introduced or absorbed
