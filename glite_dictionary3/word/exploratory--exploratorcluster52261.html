exploratory

adjective
intended to explore or investigate
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsplɔːrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɒrətəri / (BrE)
serving or intended to explore or investigate something; undertaken to gain new knowledge or information

Examples

exploratory surgery
Doctors recommended an exploratory surgery to determine the cause of the pain.
Scientists embarked on an exploratory mission to study the newly discovered cave system.
The company conducted an exploratory survey to assess the market potential for its new product.

Related Terms

  • antonym – confirmatory: serving to confirm or verify something
  • synonym – investigative: carrying out investigation; curious to obtain information
