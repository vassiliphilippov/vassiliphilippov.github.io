hadrosaurus

noun
a duck-billed herbivorous dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˌhædrəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌhædrəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a large herbivorous dinosaur with a duck-billed snout, belonging to a group of bipedal ornithischian dinosaurs, which may have been partly aquatic

Examples

a hadrosaurus fossil
Fossils of the hadrosaurus have been found in North America and date back to the Cretaceous period.
Scientists believe the hadrosaurus may have used its webbed feet to swim in prehistoric swamps.
The distinctive duck-bill of the hadrosaurus made it easily recognizable among other dinosaurs.
