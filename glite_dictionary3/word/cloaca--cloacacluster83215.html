cloaca

noun
waste pipe for sewage or water
Pronunciation: / kloʊˈeɪkə / (AmE), / kləʊˈeɪkə / (BrE)
A waste pipe or conduit for carrying away sewage or surface water, especially in historical contexts.

Examples

ancient cloaca system
Archaeologists found the remains of a cloaca beneath the ruins of the old settlement.
Regular maintenance of the cloaca was essential to prevent flooding and disease in medieval towns.
