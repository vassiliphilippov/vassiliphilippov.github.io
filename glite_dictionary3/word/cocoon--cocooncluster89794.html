to cocoon

verb
wrap protectively as if in a cocoon
Pronunciation: / kəˈkun / (AmE), / kəˈkuːn / (BrE)
to wrap or envelop (someone or something) protectively as if in a cocoon

Examples

to cocoon a child
During the blizzard, she cocooned herself in blankets to stay warm.
I like to cocoon on the couch with a good book when it rains.
The puppy cocooned in a pile of towels, safe and snug.

  • synonym – shield: to protect from danger or harm
