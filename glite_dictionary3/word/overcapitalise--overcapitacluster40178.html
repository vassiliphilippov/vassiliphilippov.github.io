to overcapitalise

verb
provide too much capital to a business
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to give a business more capital (money or value) than it really needs or deserves

Examples

to overcapitalise a company
He warned that to overcapitalise could hurt long-term profits and reduce financial flexibility.
Small businesses sometimes overcapitalise, tying up more funds than are necessary for their operations.
The company could be in trouble if they continue to overcapitalise new projects with unrealistic investment.
Financial regulators warn against attempts to overcapitalise firms for short-term market advantages.
Some investors worry that the company will overcapitalise its new branches, leading to inefficient use of funds.
The auditors discovered that management had overcapitalised several assets, inflating the company's balance sheet.

Related Terms

  • antonym – undercapitalise: supply a business with insufficient capital
