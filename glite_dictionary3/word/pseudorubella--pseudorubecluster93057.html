pseudorubella

noun
viral disease with brief rash in children
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊruˈbɛlə / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊruːˈbɛlə / (BrE)
A viral disease of infants and young children, marked by a sudden high fever, mild sore throat, and a brief pinkish rash.

Examples

pseudorubella rash in children
Cases of pseudorubella are often mistaken for more serious illnesses like measles or rubella.
Parents should be aware that pseudorubella usually resolves on its own without complications.
The doctor diagnosed the child with pseudorubella after noticing the brief rash and high fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym – roseola: a viral disease of young children with sudden high fever and rash
  • antonym – rubella: a contagious viral infection causing rash (German measles)
