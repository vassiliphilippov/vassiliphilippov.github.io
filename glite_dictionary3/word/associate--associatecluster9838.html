associate

noun
a subordinate member in an organization or company
Pronunciation: / əˈsoʊsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsɪət / (BrE)
a person with lower or subordinate membership or rank in a society, institution, or business, often below that of a full member or partner

Examples

associate position
Most associates in the law office handle research and basic client meetings under supervision.
She started as an associate at the accounting firm before becoming a partner five years later.
The university offers associate membership to people who do not meet the full criteria.

Related Terms

  • antonym – partner: a full member or owner in a professional firm
