to outwear

verb
to exhaust through overuse or strain
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈwɛr / (AmE), / aʊtˈwɛə / (BrE)
to exhaust or tire (someone or oneself) through overuse, great strain, or prolonged effort

Examples

to outwear a worker
She tried not to let the stress of her job outwear her over time.
The ten-mile run completely outwore the inexperienced runners.
They feared the relentless pace would outwear even the strongest members of the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exhaust: to make someone extremely tired
  • antonym – refresh: to give someone new energy or strength
