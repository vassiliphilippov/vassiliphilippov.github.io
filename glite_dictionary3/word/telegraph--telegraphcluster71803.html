to telegraph

verb
send a message by telegraph
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛl.əˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.ɪ.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to send a message by telegraph or cable, especially using electrical signals over a distance

Examples

to telegraph a message
He promised to telegraph the latest news as soon as he arrived.
In those days, people often telegraphed family members about important events.
The company will telegraph the confirmation to your office tomorrow.
