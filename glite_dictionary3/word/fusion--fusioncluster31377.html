fusion

noun
merging of adjacent sounds or syllables in speech
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / ˈfjʊːʒən / (BrE)
the process by which adjacent sounds, syllables, or words merge into a single sound or form, especially in speech

Examples

vowel fusion in speech
In connected speech, vowel fusion can often occur, making the words harder to distinguish.
Some dialects show more extensive fusion of syllables than the standard language.
The linguist studied cases of fusion between consonant and vowel sounds in fast casual speech.
