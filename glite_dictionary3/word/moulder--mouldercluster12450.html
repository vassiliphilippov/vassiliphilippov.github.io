to moulder

verb
decay or break down gradually
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈməʊldə / (BrE)
to decay or break down slowly, especially as a result of natural processes

Examples

to moulder in a barn
After years of neglect, the farm equipment began to moulder in the damp barn.
If left outside, wooden fences will gradually moulder away.
The old books were left to moulder in the abandoned library.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decay: to break down or deteriorate over time
  • synonym – decompose: to break down into basic elements by natural processes
  • synonym – rot: to decay or decompose through the action of bacteria and fungi
