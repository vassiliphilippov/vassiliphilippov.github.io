to wind

verb
to coil or wrap around
Pronunciation: / waɪnd / (AmE), / waɪnd / (BrE)
to wrap, coil, or twist something around an object or into a spiral shape

Examples

to wind a rope
He wound the hose neatly around the wall bracket after finishing the garden work.
She carefully wound the long scarf around her neck before going outside.
The electrician winds the wire around the metal rod to form a coil.
Children wound long garlands around the maypole during the spring celebration.
She began to wind the flowers into a wreath for the festival.
The bride's hair was wound with delicate strands of ivy and ribbon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coil: to wind into continuous, regularly spaced rings one above the other or spiral
  • antonym – unwind: to undo or remove a winding or twisting
  • antonym – unwrap: to remove what is wrapped around something
