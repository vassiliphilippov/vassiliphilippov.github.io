to give

verb
cause to happen; be responsible for
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv / (AmE), / ɡɪv / (BrE)
to produce, cause, or result in something (such as an effect, feeling, or sound)

Examples

give a result
Her dedication and hard work gave the project a chance to succeed.
His quick thinking gave us a real advantage in the negotiation.
The new evidence gave the investigation a fresh direction.
His risky actions gave us quite a scare.
That movie gave me an idea for my next project.
The new medication may give you relief from pain.
The new well will give enough water for the entire village.
Their small shop gives some extra income to the family.
This old apple tree still gives a good crop every year.
