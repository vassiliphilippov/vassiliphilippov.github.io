to overstock

verb
stock excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstɑk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstɒk / (BrE)
to supply or fill with more stock than is needed or can be sold or used

Examples

to overstock a store
If you overstock on perishable goods, you might end up with a lot of waste.
The store tends to overstock its shelves before the holiday season, hoping for high demand.
They realized they had overstocked their warehouse after sales dropped unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – understock: to stock with too little supply
