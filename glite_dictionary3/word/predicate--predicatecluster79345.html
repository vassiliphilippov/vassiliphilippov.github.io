to predicate

verb
declare an attribute or quality
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpriːdɪˌkeɪt /; / ˈprɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to state, affirm, or declare something about a subject (in logic, grammar, or general use)

Examples

to predicate a statement
Ancient philosophers often predicated rationality as the chief virtue of mankind.
The expert's report predicated the claimant's success on the strength of the new evidence.
The teacher predicates her grading system on students' mastery of the coursework.
In the sentence 'All roses are flowers,' the word 'flowers' is predicated of 'roses.'
Many philosophers predicate existence as a fundamental property of certain objects.
The professor explained how the term 'mortal' is predicated of humans in classic logical propositions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – affirm: to state something as a fact
  • synonym – assert: to state or declare positively and often forcefully
  • antonym – deny: to state that something is not true
