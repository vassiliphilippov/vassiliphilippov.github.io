diplomacy

noun
skill in managing affairs or relations
Pronunciation: / dɪˈploʊməsi / (AmE), / dɪˈpləʊməsi / (BrE)
the skill of dealing with people or situations in a sensitive and effective way to avoid conflict or achieve a goal

Examples

show diplomacy at work
Good diplomacy can resolve conflicts without resorting to force.
He lacks the diplomacy needed for dealing with sensitive situations.
She handled the tense negotiations with remarkable diplomacy.
His lack of diplomacy made many meetings more tense than they needed to be.
It takes diplomacy to negotiate difficult family situations without causing arguments.
She showed great diplomacy in resolving the conflict between the two departments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bluntness: lack of sensitivity or diplomacy
  • synonym – tact: ability to say or do things without offending
