juggle

noun
deceptive manipulation or rearrangement
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡl / (BrE)
an act of manipulating or rearranging things in order to deceive or create a misleading impression

Examples

a juggle of facts
Political campaigns often involve a juggle of promises and facts.
The company's financial juggle was eventually uncovered by auditors.
Through a clever juggle of schedules, she made it appear as though she had more free time.
