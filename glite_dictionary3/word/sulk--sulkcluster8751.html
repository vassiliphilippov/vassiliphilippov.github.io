sulk

noun
a sullen mood or state of withdrawal
Pronunciation: / sʌlk / (AmE), / sʌlk / (BrE)
a period or state of being sullenly silent or withdrawn, often because of annoyance or disappointment

Examples

in a sulk after defeat
He spent the afternoon in a sulk after losing the game.
Their argument ended with him going off in a sulk.
When ignored, she often retreats into a sulk and refuses to talk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mood: a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • degree higher – tantrum: an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration
