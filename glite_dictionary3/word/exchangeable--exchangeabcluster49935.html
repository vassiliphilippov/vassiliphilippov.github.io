exchangeable

adjective
finance: able to be exchanged for a specified asset
Pronunciation: / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbl / (BrE)
(finance) (of a security or financial instrument) able to be exchanged, under specified terms, for a particular asset (typically shares) or another security.

Examples

exchangeable bonds
The company issued exchangeable bonds that can be traded for shares of its subsidiary next year.
Investors bought the exchangeable note to gain stock exposure without immediate ownership of the company.
After five years the fund may sell assets or make them exchangeable for equity under the agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – convertible: a security that can be converted into shares of the issuer; a related financial mechanism, though 'exchangeable' often refers to exchange into a specified third-party asset.
  • synonym – recoverable: to be able to be recovered or reclaimed; included to show related financial/legal dispositions rather than exact equivalence.
