bastardization

noun
action of declaring illegitimacy
Pronunciation: / ˌbæ.stɚ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɑː.stə.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of declaring someone illegitimate, especially as a child not legally recognized by both parents; the state of being rendered a bastard

Examples

bastardization of children
After the court's decision, the bastardization of the heirs was officially recognized.
In earlier centuries, bastardization could have severe consequences for inheritance rights.
The annulment led to the bastardization of all children born within that union.

Related Terms

  • antonym – legitimation: the process of making legitimate or lawful, especially a child
