Salicornia europaea

noun
a salt-marsh plant known as glasswort or samphire
Pronunciation: / sæ.ləˈkɔr.ni.ə juˈroʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / sal.ɪˈkɔː.ni.ə juːˈrəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
a fleshy salt-marsh plant with jointed stems and tiny scale-like leaves, found in coastal regions of Europe and North America; formerly used in glassmaking, also known as common glasswort or marsh samphire

Examples

Salicornia europaea in salt marshes
Historically, people burned Salicornia europaea to obtain soda ash for glassmaking.
In the salt marsh, Salicornia europaea forms dense green carpets in late summer.
Many chefs now use Salicornia europaea as a delicate, salty garnish for seafood dishes.
