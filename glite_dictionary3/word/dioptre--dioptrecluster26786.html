dioptre

noun
unit of lens refractive power
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɒptɚ / (AmE), / daɪˈɒptə / (BrE)
a unit of measurement of the refractive power of a lens, equal to the reciprocal of the focal length measured in meters

Examples

dioptre of a lens
A lens with a focal length of one meter has a refractive power of one dioptre.
My new glasses have lenses with a strength of minus two dioptres each.
Ophthalmologists measure the curvature of the cornea in dioptres.
