carnage

noun
mass killing; slaughter
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the killing of a large number of people, especially in a violent or brutal way

Examples

war carnage
Newspapers reported the scale of carnage in the aftermath of the terrorist attack.
The battlefield was littered with the evidence of carnage after the brutal conflict.
The city tried to recover from the carnage caused by the devastating explosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bloodshed: the killing or wounding of people, usually during a violent event
  • synonym – massacre: the deliberate and brutal killing of many people
  • synonym – slaughter: the killing of a large number of people or animals violently
