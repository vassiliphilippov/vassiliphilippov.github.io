to chink

verb
to fill cracks or small gaps
Pronunciation: / tʃɪŋk / (AmE), / tʃɪŋk / (BrE)
to fill small cracks or narrow openings, especially in a wall or between logs, with a sealing material such as caulking or plaster.

Examples

to chink a wall
He spent the afternoon chinking the cracks in the stone wall with mortar.
The workers carefully chinked the gaps between the logs in the old cabin.
They chink every small gap to keep out the cold wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – caulk: to seal a gap or seam with a waterproof filler
