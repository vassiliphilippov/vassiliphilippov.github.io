threadbare

adjective
worn so thin that threads show through
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛdˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈθrɛd.bɛə / (BrE)
(of cloth, clothing, or furnishings) having the nap worn away, with threads showing through due to age or use; shabby or worn-out.

Examples

threadbare old sofa
After years of use, the living room sofa had become threadbare and uncomfortable.
He wore a threadbare coat that barely kept out the cold winds.
The hotel carpets were so threadbare that you could see the floor underneath.

Related Terms

  • antonym – new: not existing before; recently made
  • synonym – shabby: in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • synonym – worn: damaged and thin because of continuous use
