hypostasis

noun
theological term: person of the Trinity
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑːstəˌsɪs / (AmE), / hʌɪˈpɒstəsɪs / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) Any of the three persons of the Trinity, especially the person of Christ in whom divine and human natures are united.

Examples

the hypostasis of Christ
In Christian doctrine, each hypostasis of the Trinity is distinct yet shares the same essence.
The union of divine and human in Christ is known as the hypostasis or hypostatic union.
Theologians debated for centuries how many hypostases exist within the Godhead.
