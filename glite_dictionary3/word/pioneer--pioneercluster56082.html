pioneer

noun
early settler in a new area
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈnɪə / (BrE)
one of the first people to settle in a new area or territory, preparing it for others to follow

Examples

early pioneer settlers
As pioneers, they faced harsh winters and scarce resources in the unfamiliar land.
Many pioneers traveled hundreds of miles to start new lives in the American West.
The town's museum honors the early pioneers who built the first homes in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – colonist: a person who settles in a new colony
  • synonym – settler: a person who moves to a new area to live, especially as one of the first people there
