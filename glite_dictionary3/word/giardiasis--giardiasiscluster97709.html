giardiasis

noun
intestinal infection by Giardia protozoa
Pronunciation: / dʒiɑrˈdaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / dʒɑːˈdaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
An intestinal infection caused by the protozoan Giardia, typically contracted from contaminated food or water, and marked by symptoms such as diarrhea, nausea, bloating, and abdominal discomfort.

Examples

giardiasis infection symptoms
Contaminated water is a common source of giardiasis outbreaks in many regions.
Symptoms of giardiasis can include persistent diarrhea and stomach cramps.
The doctor diagnosed her with giardiasis after she returned from a trip abroad.
