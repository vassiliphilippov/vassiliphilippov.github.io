transliteration

noun
writing words in another alphabet
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænz.lɪˌtɪr.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænz.lɪˌtɪə.rɪˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing the letters or words of one alphabet in the corresponding letters of another alphabet

Examples

transliteration of Russian names
Proper transliteration is essential for researchers working with ancient manuscripts.
Some historical texts are only available in transliteration, not in the original script.
The transliteration of Russian names into English can vary significantly.
