to diddle

verb
handle or move something absent-mindedly
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈdɪd.əl / (BrE)
to move, handle, or manipulate something, especially absent-mindedly or without purpose

Examples

to diddle with keys
He likes to diddle with gadgets he finds around the house.
She absent-mindedly diddled the pen cap while thinking about her essay.
Stop diddling with the remote and pay attention to the movie.
