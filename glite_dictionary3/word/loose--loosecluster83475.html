to loose

verb
set free; release from confinement
Pronunciation: / lus /; / luːs / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
to release, set free, or make less tight

Examples

to loose a bird
She loosed the bird from its cramped cage and watched it fly away.
The guards finally loosed the prisoners after the peace agreement was signed.
The king loosed the captives as an act of mercy.
She decided not to loose her anger in public, instead choosing to remain calm.
The dictator threatened to loose his army on the rebels if they did not surrender.
They feared that unguarded research could loose powerful new diseases into the world.
His grip on the handle loosed as he relaxed his hand.
The tension in the cable gradually loosed during the night.
When the knot loosed, the package began to fall apart.

Related Terms

  • synonym – release: to set free, as from captivity or restraint
  • antonym – restrain: to keep something under control or within limits
  • antonym – tighten: to make or become tight or tighter
  • synonym – unleash: to suddenly release a force, feeling, or action
