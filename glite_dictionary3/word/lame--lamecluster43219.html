to lame

verb
to deprive of use of a limb
Pronunciation: / leɪm / (AmE), / leɪm / (BrE)
to cause someone or an animal to lose the use of a limb, especially a leg, making them unable to walk properly

Examples

to lame an animal
He warned that a careless blow could easily lame a working animal.
The disease threatened to lame thousands of cattle in the region.
The horse was badly injured and the fall could lame it for life.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cripple: to cause severe and permanent injury to someone, making them unable to use a limb or walk
