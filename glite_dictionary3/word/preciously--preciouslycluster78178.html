preciously

adverb
very; extremely (archaic/literary)
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃəsli / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃəsli / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) Used to emphasize a small amount or degree; very or extremely.

Examples

preciously few answers
She received preciously few responses to her invitation.
There is preciously little time left to complete the project.
They offered preciously little help when we needed support.

  • antonym – scarcely: only just; almost not
  • synonym – very: to a high degree; extremely
