sketchiness

noun
lack of detail; incompleteness
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛtʃ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskɛtʃ.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being incomplete, lacking in detail, or not fully developed.

Examples

sketchiness of the plan
Because of the sketchiness of the plan, investors were reluctant to get involved.
Critics pointed out the sketchiness of the article's research and called for more sources.
The sketchiness of the witness's description made it difficult for police to identify the suspect.
