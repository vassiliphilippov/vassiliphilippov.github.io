to upset

verb
defeat unexpectedly (esp. in sports)
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈsɛt / (AmE), / ʌpˈsɛt / (BrE)
to defeat (an opponent) unexpectedly, especially when the opponent is favored to win

Examples

to upset the champion
Fans cheered when their local player upset the world number one in the first round.
It was a huge story when the outsider upset the favorite to win the tournament.
The underdog team upset the champions in a dramatic final.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose: to be defeated (opposite sense)
  • synonym – shock: to cause sudden upset or surprise
  • synonym – surprise: to do something unexpected
