aetiologist

noun
disease cause specialist
Pronunciation: / ˌiːtiˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌiːtɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A medical specialist who studies the causes or origins of diseases.

Examples

aetiologist studies diseases
Her research as an aetiologist has advanced the understanding of rare infectious diseases.
Hospitals often consult an aetiologist when a new illness emerges without a clear cause.
