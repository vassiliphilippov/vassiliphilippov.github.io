ghost

noun
spirit of a dead person
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
the spirit or soul of a dead person that appears to the living, typically as a visible or tangible figure

Examples

see a ghost
As a child, he was afraid that a ghost might appear while he was alone.
Many cultures have stories about a ghost haunting a particular location.
She claimed to have seen a ghost in the old house late at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym – corporeal being: a living being with a physical body, as opposed to a ghost
  • synonym – phantom: a ghostly appearing figure; something seen, heard, or sensed but having no physical reality
  • synonym – spirit: the non-physical part of a person, often believed to live on after death
