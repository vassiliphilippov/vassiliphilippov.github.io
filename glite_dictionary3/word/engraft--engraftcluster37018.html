to engraft

verb
join plant parts to grow together
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡræft / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to insert or join a part of one plant into another so that they grow together as a single plant

Examples

to engraft a branch
In spring, skilled gardeners often engraft apple branches onto sturdy rootstocks.
The horticulturist showed us how to engraft a healthy stem onto a diseased tree.
They successfully engrafted a rare rose onto a more common variety.

Related Terms

  • synonym – graft: to insert a shoot or twig into another plant so that they grow together
