to unsubstantialize

verb
make immaterial or incorporeal
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.səbˈstæn.ʃi.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səbˈstæn.ʃɪə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) immaterial or incorporeal; to deprive of substance or physical reality

Examples

to unsubstantialize a story
Legends often unsubstantialize historical figures, turning them into myths.
She worried that digital art might unsubstantialize the experience of painting on canvas.
The philosopher argued that abstract thought can unsubstantialize the physical world.
