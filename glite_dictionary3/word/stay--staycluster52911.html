stay

noun
temporary judicial suspension of proceedings
Pronunciation: / steɪ / (AmE), / steɪ / (BrE)
A judicial order that temporarily stops a legal proceeding or the enforcement of a court judgment until a specific event occurs or the order is changed.

Examples

legal stay
A temporary stay of execution was granted to allow for new evidence to be considered.
The company received a stay from the court, pausing the collection of debts until the case was resolved.
The judge issued a stay on the construction project pending further review.

Related Terms

  • synonym – injunction: a court order requiring a party to do or refrain from doing a specific act
