to overexpose

verb
expose to too much light (photography)
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose film, a photo, or an image to too much light, causing it to become too bright or washed out

Examples

to overexpose a photo
If you overexpose your photos, they will appear washed out and lack detail.
She tried not to overexpose the image while adjusting the camera settings.
The bright sunlight overexposed the film, ruining all of the vacation pictures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – underexpose: expose film or image to insufficient light
