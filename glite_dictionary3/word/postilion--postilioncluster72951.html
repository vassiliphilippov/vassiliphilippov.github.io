postilion

noun
rider guiding carriage horses
Pronunciation: / pəˈstɪliən / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪlɪən / (BrE)
A person who rides the leading horse on the left side of a pair (or team) drawing a carriage, especially when there is no coachman.

Examples

postilion leads carriage
During their journey, they relied on a postilion to control the powerful team of horses.
The grand carriage was led by a skilled postilion dressed in fine livery.
Without a coachman, the postilion expertly guided the horses through the busy streets.
