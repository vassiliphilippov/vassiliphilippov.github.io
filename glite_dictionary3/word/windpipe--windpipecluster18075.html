windpipe

noun
trachea; airway tube in the neck
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌpaɪp / (BrE)
the tube in the neck and chest that carries air from the larynx to the lungs; the trachea

Examples

windpipe injury
After inhaling the smoke, he started coughing because it irritated his windpipe.
Doctors inserted a tube into her windpipe to help her breathe during the operation.
If a piece of food goes down the windpipe instead of the esophagus, it can cause choking.
