nitrile

noun
synthetic rubber used for gloves and seals
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtraɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtraɪl / (BrE)
a synthetic rubber material (acrylonitrile butadiene rubber) used for gloves, seals, hoses, and other items requiring oil and chemical resistance.

Examples

nitrile gloves for safety
Hospitals switched to nitrile gloves for better chemical resistance and latex allergy safety.
The mechanic prefers nitrile gloves when handling solvents and greasy engine parts.
A damaged nitrile seal caused oil to leak from the pump during operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acrylonitrile butadiene rubber (NBR): the industrial name for the copolymer commonly called nitrile rubber
  • synonym – nitrile rubber: a synthetic rubber (acrylonitrile butadiene rubber) resistant to oils and chemicals
