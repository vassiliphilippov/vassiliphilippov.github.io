intermediate

adjective
in the middle of a range or scale
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
being between two other things, levels, or points; not at either extreme

Examples

intermediate level class
She signed up for the intermediate Spanish class because she wasn't a beginner but not yet advanced.
They purchased a bike that was suitable for intermediate riders, not for absolute beginners.
This job requires an intermediate level of computer skills to complete the tasks effectively.
She is enrolled in an intermediate French class, not beginner or advanced.
The train made several intermediate stops between the two cities.
There are three intermediate stages in the project before completion.

  • antonym – initial: occurring at the beginning; first in a series
