to disquiet

verb
to make uneasy or anxious
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkwaɪ.ət / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwaɪ.ət / (BrE)
to make someone worried, uneasy, or disturbed

Examples

to disquiet a group
Her silence began to disquiet those around her.
Nothing seemed to disquiet him, even in difficult times.
The troubling headlines served only to disquiet the public further.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disturb: to interrupt the peace or calm; to bother
  • antonym – reassure: to comfort or restore confidence to someone
