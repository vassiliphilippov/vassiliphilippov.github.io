hallah

noun
Jewish braided egg bread
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑlə / (AmE), / ˈxɑːlə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a type of white bread made with eggs and leavened with yeast, often braided and glazed with eggs before baking, traditionally eaten on the Sabbath and Jewish holidays.

Examples

fresh hallah bread
At the bakery, I bought a beautifully braided hallah for the holiday.
Each slice of hallah was light and slightly sweet.
She baked a fresh hallah for the Sabbath dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym – challah: Alternate spelling for the same bread, usually now more common in English
