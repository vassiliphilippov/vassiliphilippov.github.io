heterometabolous

adjective
undergoing incomplete metamorphosis (insects)
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈmɛtəboʊləs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈmɛtəbələs / (BrE)
(of an insect) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis in which the nymph closely resembles the adult and there is no pupal stage.

Examples

heterometabolous insect nymph
Grasshoppers are heterometabolous insects, developing from nymphs that look much like the adults.
In heterometabolous species, there is no pupal stage between the nymph and adult forms.
Unlike butterflies, which are holometabolous, cockroaches exhibit a heterometabolous life cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym – holometabolous: (of an insect) undergoing complete metamorphosis, including a pupal stage
