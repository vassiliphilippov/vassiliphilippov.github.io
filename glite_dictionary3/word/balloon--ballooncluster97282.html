to balloon

verb
to become inflated or rapidly increase
Pronunciation: / bəˈluːn / (AmE), / bəˈluːn / (BrE)
(of an object or a quantity) to become swollen, puffed out, or rapidly increase in size or volume, like a balloon being inflated.

Examples

to balloon in size
As the wind caught them, the curtains began to balloon outwards.
Her workload ballooned after her colleague left the company.
Rising prices have caused the budget to balloon in recent years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expand: to increase in size, number, or importance
  • antonym – shrink: become or make smaller in size or amount
