to pasteurise

verb
heat food to kill microorganisms
Pronunciation: / ˈpæs.tʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpæs.tjə.raɪz / (BrE)
to heat food or liquid to a specific temperature for a set period in order to kill harmful microorganisms and extend shelf life

Examples

to pasteurise milk
Before bottling, the cider is pasteurised to destroy any unwanted bacteria.
Fruit juices are often pasteurised to increase their shelf life without adding preservatives.
The dairy now pasteurises all its milk to ensure it is safe to drink.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sterilize: to make free from living microorganisms; to render sterile
