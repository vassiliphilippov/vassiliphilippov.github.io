mayonnaise

noun
thick creamy condiment
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.əˌneɪz / (AmE), / ˌmeɪ.əˈneɪz / (BrE)
A thick, creamy sauce made from egg yolks, oil, and vinegar or lemon juice, used as a condiment.

Examples

mayonnaise on a sandwich
She spread mayonnaise on her sandwich before adding the lettuce and tomato.
The potato salad tastes better with a bit of mayonnaise mixed in.
Would you like ketchup or mayonnaise with your fries?
