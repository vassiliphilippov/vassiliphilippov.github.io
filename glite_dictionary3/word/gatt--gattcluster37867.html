GATT

noun
UN treaty and agency promoting international trade
Pronunciation: / ɡæt / (AmE), / ɡæt / (BrE)
an international agreement under the United Nations, created to promote trade by reducing tariffs and import quotas; also refers to the agency governing this agreement

Examples

GATT agreement
Many countries benefited from membership in the GATT before the founding of the World Trade Organization.
The GATT negotiations led to significant reductions in international trade barriers.
The principles established by GATT continue to influence modern trade agreements.
