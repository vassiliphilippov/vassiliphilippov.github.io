to goose

verb
give a burst of fuel/power
Pronunciation: / ɡus / (AmE), / ɡuːs / (BrE)
(informal) To give a sudden burst of fuel or power to (an engine or vehicle), causing it to accelerate abruptly.

Examples

to goose the engine
He goosed the engine and shot ahead of the other cars.
Try not to goose the throttle when driving on icy roads.
Whenever we hit a long straight, she would goose the truck just for fun.
