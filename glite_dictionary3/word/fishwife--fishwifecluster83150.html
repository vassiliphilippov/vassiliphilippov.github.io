fishwife

noun
woman who sells fish
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃˌwaɪf / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌwaɪf / (BrE)
A woman who sells fish, typically at a market; sometimes used informally to describe a coarse or loud-mouthed woman.

Examples

market fishwife
Despite being a respected fishwife, she was known for her quick wit and sharp tongue.
The fishwife shouted out the freshest catches to passersby in the bustling market.
The novel's protagonist was once a humble fishwife struggling to support her family.
