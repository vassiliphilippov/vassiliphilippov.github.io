enmeshed

adjective
entangled or deeply involved
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈmɛʃt / (AmE), / ɪnˈmɛʃt / (BrE)
involved in or caught up in something so completely as to be difficult to escape; entangled, as if caught in a net.

Examples

enmeshed in problems
He was so enmeshed in his work that he neglected his family and friends.
She found herself enmeshed in office politics that she had tried to avoid.
The company became enmeshed in a series of legal disputes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – entangled: twisted together or entwined, making escape difficult
