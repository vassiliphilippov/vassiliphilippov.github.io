to cram

verb
study intensively before an exam
Pronunciation: / kræm / (AmE), / kræm / (BrE)
to study very hard and quickly for an exam, usually in a short period of time

Examples

to cram for exams
I had to cram for my chemistry exam last night because I didn't study earlier.
Many students cram the night before finals instead of spreading out their studying.
She crammed all weekend to prepare for her medical school entrance test.
He always tries to cram the night before his exams, but it rarely works well.
The teacher offered extra sessions to help students cram for the finals.
We stayed up late to cram for the biology test.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bone up: to study intensively or review material quickly
  • synonym – swot: to study very hard, especially for exams
