evacuation

noun
orderly removal of people for safety
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of moving people from a dangerous place to a safer place, especially in an organized way for safety.

Examples

emergency evacuation
Authorities coordinated a mass evacuation to ensure everyone's safety.
During the fire alarm, an orderly evacuation of the building took place.
The city ordered the evacuation of residents ahead of the approaching hurricane.
