lumpectomy

noun
breast-conserving surgery to remove a tumor
Pronunciation: / lʌmˈpɛktəmi / (AmE), / lʌmˈpɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for removing a tumor from the breast without removing much of the surrounding tissue or lymph nodes

Examples

lumpectomy for breast tumor
After her diagnosis, she chose to undergo a lumpectomy instead of a full mastectomy.
Doctors recommended a lumpectomy followed by radiation therapy for optimal results.
The lumpectomy was successful, and she was able to return home the next day.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mastectomy: surgical removal of the whole breast
