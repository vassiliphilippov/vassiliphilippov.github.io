chapelgoer

noun
Protestant not in the Church of England
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæpəlˌɡoʊər / (AmE), / ˈtʃæpəlˌɡəʊə / (BrE)
(in England) a Protestant who attends a chapel, especially one who is not a member of the Church of England.

Examples

chapelgoer attends service
As a lifelong chapelgoer, he cherished the traditions of his Nonconformist community.
Her family had been chapelgoers for generations, attending services every Sunday.
Many chapelgoers in 19th-century England faced social and political discrimination.
