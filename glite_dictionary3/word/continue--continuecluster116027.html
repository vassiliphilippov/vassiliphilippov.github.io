to continue

verb
do something repeatedly without stopping
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪn.ju / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.juː / (BrE)
to keep doing something or not stop; to persist or move ahead without interruption

Examples

to continue working hard
Despite the criticism, she continued giving her speeches with confidence.
If you continue asking the same question, you'll eventually get an answer.
The rain continued for days, causing widespread flooding in the region.
If you continue working at this pace, you'll finish early.
She decided to continue her studies despite the challenges she faced.
The rain will continue throughout the night, according to the weather forecast.
After a short break, the hikers continued along the mountain trail toward the summit.
The parade continued for miles through the city, attracting large crowds.
You must continue straight ahead until you reach the traffic lights.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cease: to stop an action or activity
  • antonym – halt: to bring or come to an abrupt stop
  • synonym – persist: to continue firmly in a course of action despite difficulty
  • synonym – proceed: to go forward or onward, especially after an interruption
  • antonym – stop: to cease continuing or halt an activity
