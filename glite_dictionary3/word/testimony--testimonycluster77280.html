testimony

noun
formal statement as evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛstəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪməni / (BrE)
a formal written or spoken statement given as evidence, especially in a court of law

Examples

court testimony
According to his testimony, he was at home on the night of the incident.
His testimony was crucial in proving the defendant's innocence.
The witness gave her testimony before the judge and jury during the trial.
His testimony was crucial in convicting the defendant of the crime.
The jury listened intently to the testimony of each expert called to the stand.
The witness gave her testimony calmly before the entire court.

Related Terms

  • antonym – denial: a statement that something is not true
  • synonym – evidence: information or objects that support the truth of something, especially in court
