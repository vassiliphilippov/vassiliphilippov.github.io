ideal

adjective
pertaining to the doctrine of ideas as reality
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdiːəl / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəl / (BrE)
Relating to the philosophical doctrine that ideas or the mind are the ultimate reality, as opposed to material objects; associated with idealism.

Examples

ideal philosophy theory
Her arguments were grounded in an ideal conception of reality, not in physical phenomena.
Some schools of thought believe in an ideal world that is fundamentally mental rather than material.
The philosopher explained an ideal system where only ideas truly exist.
