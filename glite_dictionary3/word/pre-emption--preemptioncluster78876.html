pre-emption

noun
government right to appropriate property
Pronunciation: / priˈɛm(p)ʃən /; / priˈɛmpʃən / (AmE), / priːˈɛmpʃən /; / priˈɛmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the right or act of claiming, purchasing, or seizing something before others, often by law or agreement
