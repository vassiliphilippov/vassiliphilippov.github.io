period

noun
time for one complete cycle
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
the time it takes to complete one full cycle of a regularly repeating event or phenomenon, such as a wave or oscillation

Examples

period of a wave
A pendulum’s period increases with its length.
The period of Earth’s rotation is approximately twenty-four hours.
The scientist measured the period of the sound wave using specialized equipment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – frequency: the number of cycles of a repeating event per unit of time
