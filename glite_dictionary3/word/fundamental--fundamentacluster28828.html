fundamental

noun
lowest tone in a harmonic series
Pronunciation: / ˌfʌn.dəˈmɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌfʌn.dəˈmɛn.tl̩ / (BrE)
the lowest tone in a harmonic series, from which the overtones are integer multiples, forming the perceived pitch of a musical sound

Examples

fundamental frequency
Many instruments reinforce both the fundamental and its harmonics to produce a richer sound.
The fundamental determines the pitch you hear when you play a note on a piano.
When the fundamental is missing, the brain can still perceive its pitch from the overtones.
