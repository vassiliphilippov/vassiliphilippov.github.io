licence

noun
freedom from usual rules or conventions
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
freedom to depart from rules or conventions, sometimes in an excessive or unrestrained way

Examples

artistic licence
Certain comedians are given more licence in their jokes than others.
The director took considerable artistic licence with the story's historical events.
The poet's use of grammar shows a deliberate licence to create a unique style.
He confused artistic freedom with licence to offend traditional values.
Some critics argue that media licence can threaten social stability if left unchecked.
The new policy was condemned for granting licence to irresponsible behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym – liberty: the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions
  • synonym – license: freedom to deviate from rules; US spelling variant
  • antonym – restraint: limitation or control over behavior or expression
