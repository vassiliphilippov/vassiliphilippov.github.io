kith

noun
friends and acquaintances
Pronunciation: / kɪθ / (AmE), / kɪθ / (BrE)
(archaic) Friends and acquaintances collectively, especially used in the phrase 'kith and kin'.

Examples

kith and kin
Although he traveled far, he never forgot his kith back home.
He invited all his kith to the wedding, making it a truly joyous occasion.
In olden days, one's kith and kin were central to daily life.
