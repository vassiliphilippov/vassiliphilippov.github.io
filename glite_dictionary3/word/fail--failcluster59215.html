to fail

verb
stop working or operating (machine, system)
Pronunciation: / feɪl / (AmE), / feɪl / (BrE)
to stop working, become weaker, or not function as expected (machines, systems, health, etc.)

Examples

fail suddenly
If the brakes fail while driving, it can be extremely dangerous.
My refrigerator failed last night and ruined all the food inside.
The old laptop finally failed during the presentation, leaving us without our slides.
As he aged, his eyesight began to fail and reading became more difficult.
Her strength seemed to fail after the long illness.
You could see that the old building was starting to fail structurally over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – break down: (of a machine or vehicle) stop functioning
  • synonym – deteriorate: to become progressively worse
  • antonym – improve: to become better or make better
  • synonym – malfunction: to fail to function or operate properly
  • antonym – work: function or operate as intended
