actinomorphous

adjective
radially symmetrical (especially of flowers)
Pronunciation: / ækˌtɪnəˈmɔr.fəs / (AmE), / ækˌtɪnəˈmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
(of a flower or organism) capable of being divided into symmetrical halves by any longitudinal plane passing through the axis; radially symmetrical.

Examples

actinomorphous flower shape
In botany, actinomorphous flowers can be divided into two equal halves by any plane through their center.
The daisy is an example of an actinomorphous flower, as it is radially symmetrical.
Unlike orchids, which are zygomorphic, a buttercup's actinomorphous blooms display symmetry from multiple angles.

Related Terms

  • antonym – zygomorphic: having bilateral symmetry (in botany)
