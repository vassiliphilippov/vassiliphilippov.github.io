to devalue

verb
to lose in value
Pronunciation: / diˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / diˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) to lose in value; to become less valuable, especially in terms of exchange rates or worth.

Examples

to devalue a currency
Experts warned that the policy could cause the local currency to devalue against the dollar.
If the company continues to perform poorly, its stock may devalue significantly.
Many currencies tend to devalue during periods of economic instability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – appreciate: to increase in value over time
  • synonym – depreciate: to decrease in value over time
