self-effacement

noun
modesty; not drawing attention to oneself
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf əˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the quality of not drawing attention to oneself; the act or habit of making oneself inconspicuous or withdrawing into the background

Examples

quiet self-effacement
Despite her achievements, she showed remarkable self-effacement in group discussions.
His self-effacement made him well-liked among colleagues who valued his humility.
She preferred self-effacement over boasting about her accomplishments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – humility: a modest or low view of one's own importance
  • synonym – modesty: the quality of being unassuming in the estimation of one's abilities
  • antonym – self-assertion: the act of expressing or defending one's rights or views forcefully
