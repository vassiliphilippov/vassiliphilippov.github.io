trunnel

noun
wooden peg for fastening timbers
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌnəl / (BrE)
A wooden peg used to fasten timbers, especially in shipbuilding, which swells when wet to hold the timbers tightly together.

Examples

wooden trunnel fastener
After rain soaked the hull, each trunnel expanded, creating a tight seal between the timber planks.
In traditional wooden boat construction, a trunnel is often used instead of metal fastenings.
The shipbuilder drove a trunnel through the beams to secure them during the restoration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – treenail: wooden peg used to fasten pieces of timber together
