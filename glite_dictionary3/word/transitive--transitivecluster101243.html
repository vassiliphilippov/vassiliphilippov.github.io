transitive

adjective
verb requiring a direct object
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb) taking a direct object; requiring a direct object to complete its meaning.

Examples

transitive verb example
Can you give an example of a transitive verb in a sentence?
In the sentence 'She reads books,' the verb 'reads' is transitive because it takes an object.
Most English verbs are either transitive, intransitive, or can be both depending on context.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intransitive: (of a verb) not taking a direct object
