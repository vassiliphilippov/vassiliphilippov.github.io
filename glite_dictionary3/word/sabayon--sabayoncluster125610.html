sabayon

noun
foamy custard-like dessert
Pronunciation: / sæˈbaɪ.ən / (AmE), / sæˈbaj.ɒ̃ / (BrE)
A light, foamy dessert made by whisking egg yolks, sugar, and wine (often Marsala) over gentle heat, served hot or chilled.

Examples

sabayon dessert sauce
For a summer treat, try a chilled sabayon over poached peaches.

The chef served a warm sabayon with fresh berries for dessert.
