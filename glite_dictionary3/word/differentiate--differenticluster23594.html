to differentiate

verb
adapt teaching for individual learners
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪt / (BrE)
(in education) to modify teaching, tasks, materials, or assessment so they match the different needs, abilities, or interests of individual learners.

Examples

to differentiate instruction methods
Educators differentiate instruction to meet diverse student needs, using varied materials and pacing.
In her classroom, the teacher differentiated activities so each child could work at an appropriate level.
Schools often differentiate assessment methods to fairly evaluate students with varied learning profiles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adapt: to change teaching or materials to suit particular learners or situations
  • synonym – personalize: to design learning experiences that meet an individual learner's needs or preferences
  • antonym – standardize: to make teaching or assessment the same for all learners; opposite of tailoring instruction
