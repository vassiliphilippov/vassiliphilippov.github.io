to habituate

verb
to make used to something
Pronunciation: / həˈbɪtʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / həˈbɪtjueɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or oneself used to something, especially so that it no longer seems strange or new

Examples

to habituate yourself
It takes several weeks to habituate yourself to a new work schedule.
Over time, residents became habituated to the constant noise from the nearby highway.
Wild animals can habituate to human presence if they are exposed often enough.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accustom: to make familiar with something through repeated experience
  • synonym – desensitize: to make less sensitive to something, especially through repeated exposure
