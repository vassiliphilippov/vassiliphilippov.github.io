thermosphere

noun
atmospheric layer above mesosphere
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜːrmoʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the region of the Earth's atmosphere that lies between the mesosphere and the exosphere, characterized by increasing temperature with height

Examples

Earth's thermosphere layer
Auroras are often visible in the thermosphere due to interactions between solar wind and atmospheric gases.
Temperatures in the thermosphere can rise dramatically because of absorption of high-energy radiation.
The thermosphere extends from about 85 kilometers to 600 kilometers above the Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – mesosphere: the atmospheric layer just below the thermosphere
