anvil

noun
middle ear bone; incus
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.vəl / (AmE), / ˈæn.vɪl / (BrE)
a small bone in the middle ear that is located between the malleus and the stapes and transmits sound vibrations; also called the incus

Examples

the anvil bone
Damage to the anvil can result in hearing loss or reduced sound conduction.
Medical students must learn the function of the anvil in the auditory system.
The anvil receives vibrations from the malleus and passes them to the stapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – incus: the medical term for the anvil bone in the middle ear
  • degree lower – malleus: the hammer-shaped bone in the middle ear, adjacent to the anvil
  • degree higher – stapes: the stirrup-shaped bone in the middle ear, adjacent to the anvil
