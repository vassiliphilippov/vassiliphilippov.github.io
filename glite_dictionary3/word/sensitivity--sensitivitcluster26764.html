sensitivity

noun
data/information sensitivity (security)
Pronunciation: / \u02ccs\u025bn.s\u0259\u02c8t\u026av.\u0259.ti / (AmE), / \u02ccs\u025bns\u026a\u02c8t\u026av\u026ati / (BrE)
the degree to which information, data, or communications are considered sensitive and require protection, access controls, or special handling in security and privacy contexts.

Examples

data sensitivity
The company's sensitivity to customer data prompted a major overhaul of its security practices.
The new policy assigns a high sensitivity rating to any files containing personal identifiers.
Before sharing documents externally, check their sensitivity level and apply appropriate encryption.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confidentiality: the principle of keeping information secret and only accessible to authorized people
  • synonym – data classification: the process of categorizing information based on its level of sensitivity and required protections
  • synonym – privacy: the state of being free from unauthorized observation or intrusion, especially regarding personal information
