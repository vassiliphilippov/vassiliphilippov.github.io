Ciliata

proper noun
class of protozoa with cilia
Pronunciation: / sɪliˈeɪtə / (AmE), / sɪlɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a class of single-celled protozoa characterized by the presence of cilia or hairlike appendages during some stage of their life cycle

Examples

class Ciliata organisms
Many species in the class Ciliata live in freshwater and use cilia for movement and feeding.
Researchers have studied the reproductive processes of Ciliata to understand cell division.
The diversity within the Ciliata makes them important subjects in evolutionary biology.
