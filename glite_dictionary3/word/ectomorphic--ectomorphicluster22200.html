ectomorphic

adjective
having a slender, lightly built physique
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.toʊˈmɔr.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təʊˈmɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
(of a person) having a slender body build with little fat or muscle and relatively long limbs.

Examples

an ectomorphic body type
Doctors described him as ectomorphic, with slim limbs and little body fat.
Her ectomorphic frame made her stand out among the dancers.
Many successful long-distance runners have an ectomorphic body type that favors endurance over strength.

Related Terms

  • antonym – endomorphic: having a round or fat body type
  • antonym – mesomorphic: having a muscular and well-built body type
