declamatory

adjective
pompous or theatrical in speech or style
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklæməˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈklæm.ə.tər.i / (BrE)
using speech or writing that is loud, pompous, or intended to impress, often in an exaggerated or theatrical way.

Examples

declamatory speech
He delivered his argument in such a declamatory tone that it overshadowed his actual points.
Her declamatory style of recitation made the poetry seem exaggerated and theatrical.
The politician's declamatory speeches were meant to impress the crowd, but often sounded insincere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bombastic: using high-sounding but unimportant or meaningless language
  • antonym – measured: deliberate and restrained in speech or style
