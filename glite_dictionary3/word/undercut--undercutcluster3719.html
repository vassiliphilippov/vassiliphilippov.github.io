to undercut

verb
hit a ball downward for backspin or lift (sports)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkʌt / (BrE)
(in sports such as golf, tennis, or hockey) to strike the ball obliquely downward so as to give it a backspin or elevation.

Examples

to undercut the ball
By undercutting the golf ball, he managed to land it softly on the green.
She undercuts her shots in table tennis to keep her opponent off balance.
The player skillfully undercut the ball to make it bounce higher over the net.

  • synonym – slice: to hit (a ball) so that it curves as a result of spin
