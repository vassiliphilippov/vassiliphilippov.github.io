diatomic

adjective
consisting of two atoms
Pronunciation: / daɪˈætəmɪk / (AmE), / daɪˈætəmɪk / (BrE)
(of a molecule or substance) consisting of or relating to two atoms in each molecule.

Examples

diatomic oxygen molecule
Hydrogen is the lightest diatomic element, forming H₂ molecules.
Oxygen exists in the atmosphere as a diatomic molecule (O₂).
Unlike diatomic nitrogen, ozone is a triatomic molecule made of three oxygen atoms.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – monatomic: a molecule or substance consisting of single atoms
  • degree higher – triatomic: a molecule made up of three atoms
