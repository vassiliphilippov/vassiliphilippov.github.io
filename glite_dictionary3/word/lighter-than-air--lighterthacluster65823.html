lighter-than-air

adjective
able to float in air because less dense than air
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtər ðæn ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə ðən ˈeə / (BrE)
(of an aircraft or object) able to rise and float in air because it is less dense than the air it displaces, as with balloons and airships.

Examples

lighter-than-air balloon
Airships, as lighter-than-air craft, were widely used before the development of airplanes.
Designing lighter-than-air vehicles requires careful control of buoyant gases.
The hot air balloon is a classic example of a lighter-than-air aircraft.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heavier-than-air: (of an aircraft) relying on aerodynamic lift, not buoyancy
