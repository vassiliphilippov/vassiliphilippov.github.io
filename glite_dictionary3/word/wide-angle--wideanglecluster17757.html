wide-angle

adjective
having a wide field of view (lens or shot)
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪdˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdˌæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
(of a lens or camera shot) having or relating to a field of view that is wider than usual, especially one achieved by a special lens.

Examples

wide-angle lens photo
A wide-angle view makes the room appear much larger than it actually is.
The film opens with a stunning wide-angle shot of the city skyline.
The photographer used a wide-angle lens to capture the whole mountain range in a single shot.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fisheye: (of a lens) with an extremely wide field of view
  • antonym – telephoto: (of a lens) with a longer focal length and narrower field of view than standard
