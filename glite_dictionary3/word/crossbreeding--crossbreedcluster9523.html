crossbreeding

noun
the act of producing hybrids by mixing breeds or species
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of mixing different breeds, species, or varieties of animals or plants to produce hybrids with specific traits

Examples

crossbreeding new plants
Crossbreeding has been used to develop new strains of disease-resistant crops.
Concerns about crossbreeding sometimes arise when native species are threatened by introduced breeds.
Farmers often rely on crossbreeding to improve the productivity of their livestock.
Crossbreeding cattle can result in offspring with improved milk production and disease resistance.
Plant scientists often use crossbreeding to create new varieties of crops with desirable traits.
The crossbreeding of different dog breeds has led to many popular hybrids like Labradoodles.
