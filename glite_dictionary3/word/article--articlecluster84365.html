article

noun
a grammatical word marking specificity (a/an/the)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a grammatical word (such as 'the', 'a', or 'an') used with a noun to show whether the noun refers to something specific or unspecific

Examples

definite article
In the sentence 'I saw a dog,' the word 'a' is an indefinite article.
Some languages, such as Russian, do not use an article before nouns.
The definite article 'the' is used when referring to something specific that both speaker and listener know about.

Related Terms

  • synonym – determiner: a word or phrase that introduces a noun and limits or clarifies its meaning
