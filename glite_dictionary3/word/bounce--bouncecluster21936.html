to bounce

verb
be refused and returned (of payment)
Pronunciation: / baʊns / (AmE), / baʊns / (BrE)
(of a cheque or payment) to be refused and returned by a bank because there is not enough money or for another reason

Examples

bounce a check
Be sure to deposit enough money, or your rent check might bounce.
Her payment was bounced by the bank due to an error in the account details.
If you write a check with insufficient funds, the bank will bounce it.
Her rent check bounced, so she was charged an additional fee.
If there aren't enough funds in your account, the payment will bounce.
The bank notified him that his check had bounced.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear: (of a check) to be successfully processed by a bank
  • synonym – reject: to refuse to accept or approve something
