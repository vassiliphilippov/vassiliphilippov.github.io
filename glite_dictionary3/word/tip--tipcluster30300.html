to tip

verb
give advice or inside information
Pronunciation: / tɪp / (AmE), / tɪp / (BrE)
to give someone secret or advance information, often to help them or warn them about something

Examples

to tip the police off
A neighbor tipped the police off to suspicious activity in the area.
He tipped the journalist about the company's financial troubles before the story broke.
She tipped her friend about the upcoming job opening.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inform: to tell someone about something, especially officially
  • synonym – warn: to tell someone about a possible danger or problem
