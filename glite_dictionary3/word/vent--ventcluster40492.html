vent

noun
an opening in the earth that emits lava or gases
Pronunciation: / vɛnt / (AmE), / vɛnt / (BrE)
a fissure or opening in the earth's crust (or on the surface of a planet) through which molten lava and gases escape

Examples

volcanic vent
An underwater volcanic vent supports a unique ecosystem of marine life.
Molten lava spewed from the main vent during the eruption.
Scientists studied the gases emitted by the vent to learn more about the volcano's activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – volcano: a mountain or hill with a crater through which lava, rock fragments, and gases erupt
