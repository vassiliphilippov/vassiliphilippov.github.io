homozygous

adjective
having identical alleles for a gene
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊməˈzaɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməˈzaɪɡəs / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having identical alleles for a particular gene or genes at corresponding chromosomal locations.

Examples

homozygous gene pair
A homozygous genotype can result in the expression of recessive traits.
If both parents contribute a red-eye allele, the offspring will be homozygous for that trait.
Scientists identified several individuals in the population as homozygous for the mutated gene.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heterozygous: having two different alleles for a particular gene
