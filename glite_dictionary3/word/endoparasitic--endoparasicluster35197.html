endoparasitic

adjective
living inside a host (as a parasite)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a parasite) living inside the internal organs of an animal or plant.

Examples

endoparasitic worms
Endoparasitic worms can cause serious diseases in their hosts if left untreated.
Some insects have a life cycle that includes an endoparasitic larval stage inside other animals.
The veterinarian identified the illness as an endoparasitic infection in the dog's intestine.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ectoparasitic: (of a parasite) living on the surface of the host
