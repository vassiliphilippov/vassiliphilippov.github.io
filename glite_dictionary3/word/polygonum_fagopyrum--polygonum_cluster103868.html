Polygonum fagopyrum

noun
annual Asian plant (buckwheat) with edible triangular seeds
Pronunciation: / pəˌlɑːɡənum fəˌɡɑːpɪˈruːm / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪɡənəm fæˌɡəʊpɪˈruːm / (BrE)
An annual Asian plant, commonly known as buckwheat, with clusters of small pinkish white flowers and small edible triangular seeds used whole or ground into flour.

Examples

Polygonum fagopyrum thrives in cool climates and is valued for its short growing season.
Farmers in Russia often cultivate Polygonum fagopyrum for both its flowers and its nutritious seeds.
