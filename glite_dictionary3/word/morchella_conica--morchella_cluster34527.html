Morchella conica

noun
a species of edible morel mushroom
Pronunciation: / mɔːrˈkɛlə ˈkɒnɪkə / (AmE), / mɔːˈkɛlə ˈkɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of edible morel mushroom, distinguished by its pitted, conical cap which is fused to the stalk, appearing grey when young and turning black as it ages.

Examples

Morchella conica mushroom dish
Chefs prize Morchella conica for its unique flavor and texture in gourmet dishes.
Professional foragers eagerly seek out the distinctive Morchella conica each spring in forested areas.
The Morchella conica features a cap attached directly to its stem, unlike some other morels.
