climb-down

noun
admission of error or change of position
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪmˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmˌdaʊn / (BrE)
an act of admitting that you were wrong or changing your stated opinion or position

Examples

public climb-down
After public outcry, the minister's apology was seen as a major climb-down.
His statement was a clear climb-down from his earlier hardline stance.
The company made a climb-down on its controversial policy after widespread criticism.
