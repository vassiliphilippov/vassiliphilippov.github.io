activism

noun
direct action for a cause
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæktɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of taking direct and vigorous action, especially to achieve a political or social goal.

Examples

activism for change
Her commitment to environmental activism led her to organize several successful community clean-up campaigns.
In recent years, online activism has become a powerful tool for raising awareness about social justice issues.
Student activism played a significant role in bringing about changes in university policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – passivism: the policy or practice of accepting events without active response or resistance
  • synonym – protest: an organized public demonstration expressing disapproval or objection
