detector

noun
device extracting modulation from radio signal
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
An electronic component that extracts modulation signals from a radio carrier wave, used in radio receivers.

Examples

radio detector circuit
A malfunctioning detector can cause loss of audio in AM radios.
Modern receivers typically use a semiconductor diode as the detector.
The radio's detector converts the signals into audible sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – demodulator: a device that separates the original information-bearing signal from a modulated carrier wave
