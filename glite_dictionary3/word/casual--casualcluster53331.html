casual

adjective
done without plan or offhand
Pronunciation: / ˈkæʒuəl / (AmE), / ˈkaʒjʊəl /; / ˈkæʒjʊəl / (BrE)
done in an informal, offhand, or unplanned way; not showing much care or preparation

Examples

casual remark
He made a casual comment about the weather as he entered the room.
The journalist gathered information through casual conversations with local residents.
Their approach to the meeting was a little too casual for such an important discussion.
His casual glance at the report missed several important errors.
She gave the room a casual inspection before leaving.
The meeting ended with only a casual discussion of the major issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cursory: done quickly with little attention to detail
  • antonym – deliberate: done consciously and intentionally
  • synonym – offhand: done without preparation or forethought
  • antonym – thorough: carried out with great care and completeness
