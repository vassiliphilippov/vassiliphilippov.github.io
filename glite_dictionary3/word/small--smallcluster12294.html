small

adjective
faint or quiet voice
Pronunciation: / smɔl / (AmE), / smɔːl / (BrE)
(of a voice, sound, etc.) quiet, faint, or barely audible, often conveying gentleness or subtlety.

Examples

small voice
A small voice within me told me to keep going despite the challenge.
He spoke in a small voice, afraid to interrupt the meeting.
Her small voice was barely heard above the noise in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – faint: (of a sound) barely perceptible or weak
  • antonym – loud: producing a lot of sound; easily heard
