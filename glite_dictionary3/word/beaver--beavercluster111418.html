beaver

noun
large semi-aquatic rodent that builds dams
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːvər / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə / (BrE)
a large semiaquatic rodent with webbed hind feet and a broad, flat tail, known for building dams and lodges in rivers and streams

Examples

wild beaver dam
In North America, the beaver population once declined due to the fur trade.
The beaver built an impressive dam across the stream near our campsite.
We watched a beaver swim out to collect sticks for its lodge.
