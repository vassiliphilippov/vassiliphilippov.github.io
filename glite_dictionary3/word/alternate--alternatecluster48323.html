alternate

adjective
of leaves, arranged singly on alternating sides
Pronunciation: / ˈɔl.tɚ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.neɪt / (BrE)
(of leaves, branches, etc.) arranged first on one side and then on the other along an axis, not in pairs.

Examples

alternate leaves pattern
Many trees have alternate leaves that grow on opposite sides of the stem in a staggered pattern.
Some species display alternate leaf patterns, while others have leaves arranged in opposite pairs.
The botanist identified the plant by its alternate arrangement of branches along the central axis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – opposite: (of leaves, etc.) arranged in pairs on either side of an axis
