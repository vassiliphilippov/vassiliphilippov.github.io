to puff

verb
make proud or conceited
Pronunciation: / pʌf / (AmE), / pʌf / (BrE)
to act or speak in a boastful or self-important way, or to make someone feel proud or conceited

Examples

to puff up pride
Compliments from his colleagues puffed his ego even more.
Don't let their praise puff you up too much.
Her recent success seemed to puff her with pride.
He continued to puff about his achievements, even though no one seemed impressed.
She would puff at her colleagues whenever her ideas were challenged.
The manager puffed at the meeting, dismissing all suggestions but his own.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boast: to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • antonym – deflate: to make someone less confident or less proud
  • antonym – humble: having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance
  • synonym – inflate: to make someone or something feel or seem more important or impressive
