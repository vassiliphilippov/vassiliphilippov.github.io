to demythologize

verb
remove mythic elements from a text
Pronunciation: / diːˌmɪˈθɑːləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / diːˌmɪˈθɒləˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to remove or interpret away the mythical or supernatural elements in a text, often to reveal historical or rational content

Examples

to demythologize a story
Modern interpreters often demythologize stories to separate fact from fiction.
Some scholars seek to demythologize ancient religious texts to better understand their historical context.
The professor attempted to demythologize the legendary accounts in the epic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mythologize: to render or explain as a myth
