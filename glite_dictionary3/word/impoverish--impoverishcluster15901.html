to impoverish

verb
make poor; take away strength or value
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɑvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒvərɪʃ / (BrE)
to make a person or place poor, or to take away strength, value, or quality from something

Examples

to impoverish a country
Excessive taxation may impoverish communities over time, leading to social unrest.
Overfarming has gradually impoverished the soil, leaving it unable to support healthy crops.
The loss of natural resources can impoverish a nation both economically and ecologically.
Heavy taxes can impoverish entire communities over time.
The economic crisis threatens to impoverish thousands of families.
War and corruption may impoverish even the wealthiest of nations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deprive: to take something away from someone
  • antonym – enrich: to make richer or improve in quality or value
  • synonym – impoverished: reduced to poverty; made poor
  • antonym – improve: to make or become better; opposite of impoverish in some contexts
