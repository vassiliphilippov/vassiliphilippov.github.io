stupor

noun
a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
Pronunciation: / ˈstuː.pɚ /; / ˈstuːpər / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.pə /; / ˈstjuːpə / (BrE)
a state in which a person is almost unconscious and unable to think or feel properly, often unresponsive or not fully aware

Examples

in a stupor after shock
His grief left him in a stupor from which he struggled to recover.
She sat in a stupor for hours after hearing the news of her dismissal.
The patient remained in a stupor after the accident, barely responding to questions.
Alcohol had left him in such a deep stupor that he did not remember the events of the night.
She remained in a stupor for hours after the accident, barely responding to anyone around her.
The patient was found in a stupor, unable to move or speak clearly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – alertness: the state of being fully aware and responsive
  • degree higher – coma: a state of deep unconsciousness from which a person cannot be awakened
  • synonym – daze: a state in which someone cannot think clearly
  • synonym – shock: a sudden upsetting experience and the feeling of surprise and distress it causes
