to succour

verb
to help in difficulty
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
to give help or assistance to someone in a difficult or distressing situation

Examples

to succour a friend
He was grateful to the neighbors who succoured his family after the accident.
In the old tales, brave knights would succour those in distress.
The Red Cross volunteers succoured the victims of the flood with food and shelter.
