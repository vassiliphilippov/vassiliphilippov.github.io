live-bearer

noun
fish that gives birth to live young
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪv ˌbɛr.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪv ˌbeə.rə / (BrE)
a small, usually brightly colored fish that gives birth to live young, commonly found in warm fresh or brackish waters and sometimes used for mosquito control

Examples

live-bearer fish tank
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep live-bearers like guppies and mollies for their vibrant colors and easy breeding.
The live-bearer is known for giving birth to fully formed, free-swimming young instead of laying eggs.
To control mosquito larvae, some communities introduce live-bearers into stagnant ponds.
