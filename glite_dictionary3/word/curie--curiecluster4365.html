Curie

proper noun
famous scientist; Nobel laureate in chemistry and physics
Pronunciation: / ˈkjʊri / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəri / (BrE)
Marie Curie (1867–1934), a French chemist and physicist born in Poland, renowned for her research on radioactivity and the discovery of radium and polonium; recipient of two Nobel Prizes.

Examples

Curie received Nobel
Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains an inspiration in science.

The discoveries made by Curie and her husband revolutionized the understanding of radioactivity.
