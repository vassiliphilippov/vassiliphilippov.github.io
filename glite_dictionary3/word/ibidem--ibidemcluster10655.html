ibidem

adverb
used in citations to mean 'in the same place'
Pronunciation: / ɪˈbɪdɛm / (AmE), / ɪˈbɪdɛm / (BrE)
used in citations to indicate that a source has already been cited directly above; in the same place.

Examples

ibidem in a citation
For all subsequent references, he simply wrote ibidem to avoid repeating the same citation.
In the bibliography, after the first detailed entry, the author used ibidem for repeated sources.
She checked the footnote, which read 'ibidem', to confirm it referred to the earlier citation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ibid.: abbreviation of 'ibidem', meaning 'in the same place' in citations
