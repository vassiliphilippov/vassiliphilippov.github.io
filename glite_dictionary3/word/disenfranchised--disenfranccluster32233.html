disenfranchised

adjective
deprived of rights, especially voting
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfræn.tʃʌɪzd / (BrE)
deprived of the rights of citizenship, especially the right to vote; lacking power or influence in society.

Examples

disenfranchised voters
Feeling disenfranchised, the citizens demanded fairer representation in parliament.
Many minority groups remained disenfranchised long after legal barriers were removed.
The new laws further marginalized already disenfranchised communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – enfranchised: given the rights of citizenship or vote
