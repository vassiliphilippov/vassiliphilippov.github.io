brininess

noun
degree of saltiness in a solution
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbraɪni.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or degree of being salty, especially due to the presence of salt in a liquid

Examples

strong brininess in soup
Excessive brininess can overpower the delicate flavors in a dish.
Some people enjoy the brininess of pickled foods.
The brininess of the seawater made it unsuitable for drinking.
