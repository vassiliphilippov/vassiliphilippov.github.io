to heave

verb
bend or bulge out of shape (under pressure or heat)
Pronunciation: / hiv /; / hiːv / (AmE), / hiːv / (BrE)
to rise, bulge, or move up and down in a rhythmic or swelling motion

Examples

the ground heaves up
If the floorboards start to heave, it could be due to moisture swelling the wood.
The frozen ground will often heave in winter, damaging the pavement above.
The road began to heave as the intense summer heat built up.
Beyond the harbor, the ocean seemed to heave in great, rolling waves.
Her chest would heave with every sob, rising and falling uncontrollably.
The sea began to heave as the storm approached.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buckle: to bend, warp, or collapse under pressure or heat
  • synonym – surge: to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward
  • synonym – swell: to rise in a rounded shape, often moving in waves
  • synonym – warp: to become bent or twisted out of shape
