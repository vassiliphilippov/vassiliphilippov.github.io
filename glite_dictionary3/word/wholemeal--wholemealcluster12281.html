wholemeal

adjective
made from whole wheat grain
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊlˌmil / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlˌmiːl / (BrE)
(of flour or bread) made from whole grains of wheat, including the bran and germ, rather than refined or white flour.

Examples

wholemeal bread
Adding wholemeal flour to recipes can make them healthier.
She prefers wholemeal pasta because it's higher in fiber than regular pasta.
The bakery offers a range of wholemeal breads and rolls.

Related Terms

  • synonym – whole wheat: (of flour or bread) made using the entire wheat grain; chiefly American term
