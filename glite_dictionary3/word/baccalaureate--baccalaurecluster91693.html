baccalaureate

noun
undergraduate academic degree
Pronunciation: / ˌbækəˈlɔːriət / (AmE), / ˌbakəˈlɔːrɪət / (BrE)
an academic degree awarded to someone who has successfully completed undergraduate studies at a college or university

Examples

baccalaureate degree
Completing a baccalaureate is a prerequisite for entering graduate school.
She received her baccalaureate from a prestigious university in France.
The ceremony celebrated students who had earned their baccalaureate degrees.
