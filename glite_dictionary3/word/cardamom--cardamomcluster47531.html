cardamom

noun
aromatic spice from plant seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdəməm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdəməm / (BrE)
An aromatic spice made from the seeds of several plants of the genus Elettaria or Amomum, used as a flavoring in cooking and baking.

Examples

cardamom spice powder
In Indian cuisine, cardamom is often used in both sweet and savory dishes.
She added a pinch of cardamom to the cake batter to enhance its flavor.
The aroma of cardamom filled the kitchen as the tea brewed on the stove.
