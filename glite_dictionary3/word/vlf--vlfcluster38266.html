VLF

noun
very low frequency (radio)
Pronunciation: / ˌviː ˌɛl ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌviː ˌɛl ˈɛf / (BrE)
a radio frequency in the range between 3 and 30 kilohertz, known as very low frequency

Examples

VLF radio signals
Researchers monitor VLF signals to study space weather and atmospheric disturbances.
Submarines often use VLF transmissions to communicate while submerged.
The VLF band is important for distant radio navigation and time signals.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – ELF: extremely low frequency (radio waves below 3 kilohertz)
  • degree higher – LF: low frequency (radio waves between 30 and 300 kilohertz)
