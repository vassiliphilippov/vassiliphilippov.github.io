periwig

noun
man's wig popular in the 17th–18th centuries
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌwɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈpɛrəˌwɪɡ / (BrE)
a wig for men, especially popular in the 17th and 18th centuries, often worn as a symbol of social status or fashion
