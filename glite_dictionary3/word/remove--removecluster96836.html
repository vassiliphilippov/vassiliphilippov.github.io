to remove

verb
to stay away or leave (formal)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmuːv / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːv / (BrE)
(formal) To stay away from a place, or to leave it, especially deliberately or for a particular reason.

Examples

to remove oneself quietly
At the sight of the approaching storm, the guests removed from the garden to the house.
He removed to Paris after finishing his studies in London.
Upon hearing the heated discussion, she quietly removed herself from the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absent: to stay away deliberately or leave a place
  • synonym – depart: to leave, especially in order to start a journey
