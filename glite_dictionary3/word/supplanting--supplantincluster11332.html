supplanting

noun
act of replacing someone by unfair means
Pronunciation: / səˈplæn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / səˈplɑːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking the place of another, especially by unfair or underhanded means

Examples

supplanting a rival leader
He achieved success through the supplanting of his rivals using dubious tactics.
Many feared the supplanting of local traditions by foreign influences.
The supplanting of the old management by the new team came as quite a shock to employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dispossession: the act of depriving someone of possession or occupancy
  • synonym – replacement: the action of replacing someone or something
