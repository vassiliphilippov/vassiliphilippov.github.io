divisionism

noun
Neo-Impressionist painting technique using color dots
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
a technique in Neo-Impressionist painting in which colors are separated into individual dots or patches that interact optically when viewed from a distance

Examples

divisionism in painting
Many Neo-Impressionist painters adopted divisionism to achieve new effects with light and color.
Seurat was a major figure in the development of divisionism during the late nineteenth century.
The artist explained how divisionism creates the illusion of blended colors from a distance.
