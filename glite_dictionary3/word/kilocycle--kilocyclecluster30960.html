kilocycle

noun
unit of frequency (1,000 cycles/sec)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪloʊˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɪləʊˌsaɪkəl / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one thousand cycles per second (1 kHz), chiefly used in measuring radio frequencies; now largely replaced by 'kilohertz'.

Examples

kilocycle radio frequency
Old manuals often use the term kilocycle instead of kilohertz.
The radio station broadcasts at a frequency of 800 kilocycles.
The technician adjusted the transmitter to 1,200 kilocycles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – kilohertz: a unit of frequency equal to 1,000 hertz (cycles per second)
