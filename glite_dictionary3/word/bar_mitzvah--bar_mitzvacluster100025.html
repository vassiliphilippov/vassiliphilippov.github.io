to bar mitzvah

verb
initiate (a boy) in the bar mitzvah ceremony
Pronunciation: / bɑr ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / bɑː ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
to confirm or initiate a Jewish boy at the age of religious maturity (usually 13) in the bar mitzvah ceremony.

Examples

to bar mitzvah a boy
He was bar mitzvahed at his family's synagogue last spring.
Many boys bar mitzvah at the age of thirteen, marking their coming of age in the Jewish community.
The family traveled to Israel so their son could bar mitzvah at the Western Wall.
