incongruously

adverb
in an out-of-place or inappropriate manner
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑŋɡruəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒŋɡruəsli / (BrE)
In a way that seems out of place, inappropriate, or not in harmony with the surroundings or expectations.

Examples

incongruously dressed for the party
A cow stood incongruously on the steps of the city courthouse, attracting stares from passersby.
Her bright pink shoes looked incongruously cheerful at the somber funeral.
The ancient castle was decorated incongruously with neon lights for the festival.
