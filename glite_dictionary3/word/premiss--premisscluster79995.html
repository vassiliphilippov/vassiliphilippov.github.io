premiss

noun
proposition assumed as basis for argument
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
A statement or proposition that is assumed to be true and from which a conclusion is drawn, especially in reasoning or argument.

Examples

premiss of an argument
Before reaching a conclusion, she carefully considered each premiss of the logic puzzle.
If either premiss is false, the entire syllogism collapses.
The argument is valid only if the first premiss is actually true.
