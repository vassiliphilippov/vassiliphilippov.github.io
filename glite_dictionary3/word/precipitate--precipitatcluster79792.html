to precipitate

verb
separate as solid particles from solution
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪpɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to separate out from a solution as solid particles, usually as a result of a chemical reaction.

Examples

precipitate solid particles
Adding sodium hydroxide causes the metal ions to precipitate as a solid.
Silver chloride will precipitate out of the solution when two reagents are mixed.
When the temperature drops, calcium carbonate may precipitate from seawater.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dissolve: to cause to become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution
