neutralisation

noun
making something ineffective or nullified
Pronunciation: / nuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / njuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The action of making something ineffective or nullifying its effect, especially by applying an opposing force or influence.

Examples

neutralisation of a threat
His quick response led to the neutralisation of the crisis before it escalated.
The neutralisation of the toxin prevents any harm to humans.
They focused on the neutralisation of hostile forces in the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym – counteraction: the act of opposing or mitigating an effect
  • antonym – reinforcement: the act of strengthening or supporting an effect
