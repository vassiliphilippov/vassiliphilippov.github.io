diaphanous

adjective
very light, almost transparent; delicate
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæfənəs / (AmE), / daɪˈæfənəs / (BrE)
(of fabric or material) very light, delicate, and almost transparent; so thin as to allow light to pass through.

Examples

diaphanous fabric dress
Morning dew clung to the diaphanous spiderwebs stretched across the grass.
The bride wore a diaphanous veil that softly shimmered in the sunlight.
The dancer moved gracefully in a gown of diaphanous silk.

Related Terms

  • antonym – opaque: not letting light through; not transparent or translucent
  • synonym – sheer: very thin or transparent (of fabric)
