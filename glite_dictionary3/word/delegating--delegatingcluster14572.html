delegating

noun
assigning authority or tasks to others
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛləˌɡeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪɡeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving responsibility or authority to someone else, especially a subordinate, to make certain decisions or complete tasks

Examples

delegating important tasks
Effective delegating can help leaders focus on more important tasks.
Poor delegating often results in confusion and low productivity.
The manager believes in delegating to empower her team members.
