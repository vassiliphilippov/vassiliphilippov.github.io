indivisibility

noun
impossibility of division by an integer (math)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˌvɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪzɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
In arithmetic, the property of being impossible to divide by a particular integer without leaving a remainder.

Examples

indivisibility of numbers
Mathematicians study the indivisibility of numbers as part of number theory.
The indivisibility of seven by three means there is always a remainder.
The concept of indivisibility is essential for understanding prime numbers.
