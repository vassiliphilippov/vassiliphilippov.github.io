to breathe

verb
expel gases or odors
Pronunciation: / brið / (AmE), / briːð / (BrE)
to expel gases or odors, especially into the air

Examples

breathe a smell
Old books sometimes breathe a musty odor when you open them after years in storage.
The cave breathed a cold, damp mist with every gust of wind.
The factory breathed noxious fumes into the night air, polluting the surroundings.
