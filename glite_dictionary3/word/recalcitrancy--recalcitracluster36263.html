recalcitrancy

noun
stubborn resistance to authority
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkælsɪtrənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈkælsɪtrənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being stubbornly uncooperative or resistant to authority or control

Examples

recalcitrancy of students
Despite repeated efforts, the recalcitrancy of the wild horses made them difficult to train.
His recalcitrancy during negotiations slowed the progress toward an agreement.
The recalcitrancy of the teenager frustrated her teachers and parents alike.
