haywire

adjective
(informal) mentally confused or erratic
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪˌwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌwaɪə / (BrE)
(informal) not working or behaving normally; out of control or malfunctioning (used for people, machines, or situations)

Examples

go haywire suddenly
After a week without sleep, his thoughts seemed to go completely haywire.
Sarah went haywire when she heard the unexpected news and started laughing uncontrollably.
The children went haywire during the birthday party, running and shouting everywhere.
Ever since the power outage, the computer has gone completely haywire.
Her plans went haywire after the unexpected storm ruined the festival.
When the signal started acting haywire, the technician tried to reset the equipment.
