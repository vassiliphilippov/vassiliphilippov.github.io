Powhatan

proper noun
Indian chief and founder of the Powhatan Confederacy
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.hə.tæn / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.hə.tæn / (BrE)
an Indian chief (c. 1550–1618) who founded the Powhatan Confederacy of tribes in eastern Virginia and was the father of Pocahontas

Examples

Chief Powhatan leader
Chief Powhatan played a key role in the early years of English settlement at Jamestown.
Historians still debate the full impact that Powhatan had on Native and colonial relations.
The story of Pocahontas frequently references her father, Powhatan.
