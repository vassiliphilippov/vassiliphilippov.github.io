aspartame

noun
artificial calorie-free sweetener
Pronunciation: / əˈspɑːrˌteɪm / (AmE), / əˈspɑːteɪm / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound used as an artificial, calorie-free sweetener, made from aspartic acid and phenylalanine.

Examples

aspartame sweetener
Many diet sodas are sweetened with aspartame instead of sugar.
Some people avoid aspartame due to reports of possible side effects.
The packet said it contained aspartame as the primary sweetening agent.
