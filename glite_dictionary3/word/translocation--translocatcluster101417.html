translocation

noun
transport of dissolved material within a plant
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the movement of dissolved substances from one part of a plant to another, especially through the phloem

Examples

plant translocation process
A blockage in the phloem can affect translocation and harm the wellbeing of the entire plant.
Scientists studied the rate of translocation to understand how nutrients move during plant growth.
The process of translocation ensures that sugars produced in the leaves are distributed to other parts of the plant.
