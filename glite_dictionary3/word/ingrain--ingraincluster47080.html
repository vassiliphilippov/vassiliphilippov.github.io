to ingrain

verb
to fix an idea or habit firmly
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡreɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪn / (BrE)
to firmly establish an idea, habit, or behavior in a person so that it becomes a lasting part of them

Examples

to ingrain habits in children
It is important to ingrain good study habits in children from an early age.
The coach worked hard to ingrain discipline and teamwork in his players.
Years of training had ingrained the safety procedures into her routine.
It is important to ingrain good study habits in students early on.
Regular training helps ingrain safe practices into employees' daily routines.
She worked hard to ingrain a sense of responsibility in her children.

Related Terms

  • antonym – eradicate: to remove or destroy something completely, especially a habit or idea
  • synonym – implant: to fix an idea or attitude firmly in someone's mind
  • synonym – instill: to gradually but firmly establish an idea or attitude in a person's mind
