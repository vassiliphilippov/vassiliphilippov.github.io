sophistry

noun
subtle, deceiving reasoning
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːfəstri / (AmE), / ˈsɒfəstri / (BrE)
the use of clever but false or misleading arguments, especially with the intention to deceive

Examples

political sophistry
Debaters must avoid sophistry and rely on sound reasoning instead.
She saw through his sophistry and refused to be persuaded by his clever words.
The politician's argument was dismissed as mere sophistry by his opponents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fallacy: a mistaken belief or error in reasoning
  • antonym – truthfulness: the state of being honest and not intending to deceive
