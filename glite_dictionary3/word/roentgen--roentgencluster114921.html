roentgen

noun
unit of radiation exposure
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛntɡən / (AmE), / ˈrɛntɡən / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for exposure to ionizing radiation, defined as the amount that produces one electrostatic unit of charge in one cubic centimeter of dry air.

Examples

roentgen measurement unit
Older equipment sometimes reported radiation in roentgen units rather than sieverts or grays.
Researchers recorded a dose of several roentgens during the experiment.
The patient's exposure was measured in roentgens to ensure it stayed within safe limits.
