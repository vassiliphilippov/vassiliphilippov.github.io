placebo

noun
inactive substance given as treatment
Pronunciation: / pləˈsiboʊ / (AmE), / pləˈsiːbəʊ / (BrE)
a substance with no therapeutic effect, often given to a patient or used as a control in testing new drugs.

Examples

placebo effect in trials
Some people experience improvement in symptoms simply because they believe the placebo is an actual drug.
The new drug was tested against a placebo to determine its real effectiveness.
The patients in the control group received a placebo instead of the active medication.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nocebo: a substance that causes negative effects, opposite of placebo
