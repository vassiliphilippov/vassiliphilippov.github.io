enchained

adjective
bound or held by chains
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtʃeɪnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃeɪnd / (BrE)
bound or confined with chains; held captive by physical or metaphorical restraints

Examples

enchained by rules
She felt enchained by the rules of her strict upbringing.
The ancient relic was enchained to prevent it from being stolen.
The prisoners were enchained in the dark, damp cell for days.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shackled: restrained or confined, especially with shackles
  • antonym – unchained: released from chains; set free
