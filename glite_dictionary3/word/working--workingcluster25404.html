working

adjective
sufficient for practical use or purpose
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
serving as a preliminary, provisional, or adequate version or understanding that is used for now, even if not final or perfect

Examples

working idea
Do you have a working understanding of how the new software operates?
She has a working knowledge of French, enough to travel and get by on her own.
The plan needs a working majority to be approved by the board.
The team used a working model to demonstrate the system before the final design was approved.
This document is only a working copy and will be revised before publication.
We need a working definition of success for this project.
Scientists often operate under a working hypothesis until further evidence is found.
The committee prepared a working draft to discuss at the meeting.
This schedule is just a working plan; it may change as the project develops.

Related Terms

  • antonym – final: coming at the end, not provisional
  • synonym – functional: capable of operating or serving a purpose
  • synonym – preliminary: coming before the main action, not final
  • synonym – provisional: arranged or existing for the present, possibly to be changed later
  • synonym – tentative: not certain or fixed; provisional
