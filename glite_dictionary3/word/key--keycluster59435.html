key

noun
piece of information for encryption/decryption
Pronunciation: / kiː / (AmE), / kiː / (BrE)
(in computing and cryptography) a piece of information (often a string of bits or characters) used to encrypt or decrypt data or to control access to encrypted information.

Examples

encryption key
The secure message required a secret key to decrypt the attachment sent by her colleague.
Companies store private keys in hardware devices to prevent attackers from stealing sensitive credentials.
When setting up the VPN, she generated a new key and shared its public part with IT.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encryption key: a value used to transform plaintext into ciphertext or vice versa; effectively the same concept as a cryptographic key.
  • synonym – password: a secret word or phrase used for authentication; sometimes used like a key to access protected data, though technically different from cryptographic keys.
