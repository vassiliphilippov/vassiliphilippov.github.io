macroelement

noun
chemical element needed in large amounts
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.roʊˈɛl.ə.mənt / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊˈɛl.ɪ.mənt / (BrE)
a chemical element essential to the body and required in relatively large amounts for normal physiological functions

Examples

macroelement in nutrition
Calcium is considered a macroelement because it is needed in large quantities by the body.
Sodium and potassium are two important macroelements in human nutrition.
The deficiency of any macroelement can lead to significant health problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym – micronutrient: an element or compound needed in very small amounts for growth
  • antonym – trace element: a chemical element needed by the body in minute amounts
