to thunder

verb
to speak loudly and forcefully
Pronunciation: / ˈθʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈθʌndə / (BrE)
to make or produce a loud, deep, powerful noise (such as shouting, moving, or general noise)

Examples

to thunder a command
"Silence!" the judge thundered, bringing the courtroom to order.
She thundered at her team to work harder before the deadline.
The coach thundered instructions to the players from the sidelines.
Crowds thundered their approval after the winning goal was scored.
The train thundered past the station, shaking the ground.
Waves thundered against the cliffs during the storm.
A freight train thundered past the station late at night, startling the waiting passengers.
The bus thundered down the empty highway, making the windows rattle.
The horses thundered across the field as the race began.

Related Terms

  • synonym – roar: to say something in a loud, deep voice
  • synonym – rumble: to make a continuous deep, resonant sound
  • synonym – shout: to speak very loudly, often due to anger or excitement
  • antonym – whisper: to speak very softly or make a soft sound
