valency

noun
capacity to interact in biological processes
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪlənsi / (AmE), / ˈveɪlənsi / (BrE)
(Biology) The relative capacity of a biological molecule, such as an antigen or substrate, to react or interact with another molecule.

Examples

valency of a molecule
A change in valency may alter the biological response to a particular medication.
Researchers studied the valency of viral proteins to assess their interactions in the host.
The valency of an antibody determines how effectively it can bind to antigens.
