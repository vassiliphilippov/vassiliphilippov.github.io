eclectic

noun
person choosing from various sources
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (BrE)
a person who selects ideas, styles, or tastes from a diverse range of sources rather than adhering to a single system or style

Examples

eclectic thinker
Among philosophers, he was regarded as an eclectic who embraced multiple schools of thought.
As an eclectic, she never limited herself to one genre in her art collection.
The magazine editor was an eclectic, always curious to explore new trends and voices.

Related Terms

  • synonym – eclectic: selecting ideas or styles from a broad range of sources (adjective)
