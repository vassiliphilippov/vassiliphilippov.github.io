presumptuous

adjective
excessively forward or bold
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzʌmptʃuəs / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmptjʊəs / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) failing to observe the limits of what is permitted or appropriate, often by being excessively bold or forward.

Examples

presumptuous question
Don't you think it's presumptuous to offer advice without being asked?
Her presumptuous attitude quickly alienated her colleagues.
It was rather presumptuous of him to assume he would be invited to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forward: bold or brash beyond what is socially acceptable
  • antonym – modest: having a humble or reserved manner
