to tucker

verb
to tire out completely
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌkɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʌkə / (BrE)
(informal) To make someone very tired; to exhaust someone completely.

Examples

to tucker out children
By the end of the day, the children were completely tuckered.
That long hike really tuckered me out.
This heat will tucker you if you’re not careful.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exhaust: to make someone very tired
  • antonym – refresh: to make someone feel less tired or more energetic
