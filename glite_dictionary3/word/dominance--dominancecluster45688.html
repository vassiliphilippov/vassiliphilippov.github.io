dominance

noun
genetic expression of one allele over another
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəns / (BrE)
(Genetics) The phenomenon in which one allele of a gene pair is expressed in the phenotype while the other is not.

Examples

gene dominance pattern
In Mendelian genetics, dominance explains why some traits mask the presence of others.
Incomplete dominance results in a blending of parental traits rather than one dominating.
The concept of dominance is crucial in understanding patterns of inheritance.
