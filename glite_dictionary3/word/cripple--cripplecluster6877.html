cripple

noun
person unable to walk normally (offensive)
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpəl / (BrE)
(offensive) A person who is unable to walk properly because of injury or disability to the legs or back.

Examples

a cripple in a wheelchair
He rejected the label cripple and preferred to be known by his name.
In the past, society often isolated anyone labeled a cripple.
The old term cripple is now considered highly offensive and should be avoided.

Related Terms

  • antonym – able-bodied: not disabled; having no injury or impairment
  • synonym – invalid: a person made weak or disabled by illness or injury
