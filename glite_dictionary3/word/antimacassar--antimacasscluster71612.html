antimacassar

noun
cloth protecting chair from hair oils
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.məˈkæs.ər / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.məˈkæs.ə / (BrE)
A small, decorative cloth placed on the back or arms of a chair to protect the upholstery from hair oils.

Examples

antimacassar on a chair
My grandmother's living room always had a antimacassar on each seat to protect them from hair cream.
She carefully washed and ironed the lace antimacassars before putting them back on the chairs.
The antique armchair was adorned with a delicate antimacassar to spare its upholstery from stains.
