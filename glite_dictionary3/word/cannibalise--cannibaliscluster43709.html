to cannibalise

verb
reduce one's own product sales
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.ɪ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ɪ.bəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to reduce or take sales, demand, or market share away from one of a company's existing products by introducing another product or variant from the same company.

Examples

to cannibalise sales
The launch of the budget model was meant to cannibalise sales of the premium version within months.
When the cheaper model arrived, it quickly cannibalised several of the company's midrange product sales.
Market research warned against cannibalising starter products, because internal competition could harm brand growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – divert: to cause something (such as demand or sales) to be used for a different product or purpose
  • synonym – erode: to gradually reduce or wear away (e.g., market share or sales)
