rout

noun
a disorderly crowd; mob
Pronunciation: / raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
a disorderly or tumultuous crowd of people, especially one engaged in unlawful or violent activity

Examples

rout of rioters
A rout of peasants gathered outside the city gates, demanding justice.
Medieval chronicles often describe a rout breaking into castles during times of unrest.
The angry rout surged through the town square, shouting and waving banners.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mob: a large crowd of people, especially one that is disorderly or intent on causing trouble
  • antonym – peace: a state of tranquility or order
  • synonym – rabble: a disorderly crowd; a mob
