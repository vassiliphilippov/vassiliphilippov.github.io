factitious

adjective
artificial; not natural
Pronunciation: / fækˈtɪʃəs / (AmE), / fækˈtɪʃəs / (BrE)
not produced by natural forces; artificial rather than natural

Examples

factitious feelings
She doubted the sincerity of his feelings, suspecting them to be factitious rather than genuine.
Some economists warned that the growth figures were factitious and would not be sustained.
The increase in sales was due to a factitious demand created by clever marketing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – artificial: made or produced by human beings rather than occurring naturally
  • antonym – natural: existing in or derived from nature; not made or caused by humankind
