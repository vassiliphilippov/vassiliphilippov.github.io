ratite

noun
flightless bird with a flat breastbone
Pronunciation: / ˈræt.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈræt.aɪt / (BrE)
any of a group of large, flightless birds with a flat breastbone lacking a keel, such as ostriches, emus, cassowaries, kiwis, and rheas

Examples

ratite bird group
Ostriches and emus are examples of ratites, a group of flightless birds.
Scientists believe that ratites evolved independently on different continents.
The flat breastbone is a key feature that distinguishes ratites from flying birds.
