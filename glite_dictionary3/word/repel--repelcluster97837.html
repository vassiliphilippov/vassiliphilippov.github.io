to repel

verb
to reject outright or bluntly
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛl / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛl / (BrE)
to reject or refuse (someone or something) in a blunt or abrupt manner

Examples

to repel an offer
He tried to make amends, but she repelled all his attempts at reconciliation.
She repelled his request to join the club without hesitation.
The company repelled repeated offers to be acquired by a competitor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accept: to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • synonym – reject: to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something or someone
