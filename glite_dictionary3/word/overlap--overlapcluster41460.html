overlap

noun
partial coincidence in time
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚˈlæp /; / ˌoʊvərˈlæp / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈlæp /; / ˌəʊvəˈlæp / (BrE)
a situation or area where two or more things, events, or ideas coincide, share features, or have something in common

Examples

overlap of ideas
An overlap of the two events meant we had to choose which one to attend.
The meeting times have some overlap, which might cause conflicts for attendees.
There was a significant overlap in their work schedules during the summer.
Despite their differences, their proposals had some overlap.
The overlap between the two theories suggests they may be describing similar phenomena.
There is significant overlap in the skills needed for both jobs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coincidence: the occurrence of events at the same time
  • antonym – exclusive: not shared; having nothing in common
  • synonym – intersection: a point or set where things overlap or cross
