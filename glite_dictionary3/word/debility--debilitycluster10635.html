debility

noun
physical weakness or frailty
Pronunciation: / dəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
A state of physical weakness, especially as the result of illness or old age.

Examples

chronic debility
After a long illness, he was left with a lingering debility that affected his daily activities.
Elderly people are often more vulnerable to debility during the winter months.
The debility of the patient made it difficult for her to walk unassisted.
