papist

noun
offensive term for Roman Catholics
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpɪst / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpɪst / (BrE)
(offensive) A Roman Catholic, especially one who is an ardent supporter of the pope and papal authority, often used derogatorily.

Examples

a strict papist
He was accused of being a papist because of his loyalty to Rome.
Some anti-Catholic literature of the era is filled with the word papist.
The term papist was often used derisively in 17th-century England.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Catholic: a member of the Roman Catholic Church
  • antonym – Protestant: a member of any branch of Christianity rejecting papal authority
