justiciary

noun
historical high judicial officer
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈstɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪʃəri / (BrE)
(historical) A high judicial officer, especially in Scotland or England, with authority over legal matters; a judge having jurisdiction over justice.

Examples

a justiciary judge
He traced his lineage to a twelfth-century justiciary of England.
Records show that the Scottish justiciary held significant power during the Middle Ages.
The ancient justiciary was responsible for maintaining order across the realm.
