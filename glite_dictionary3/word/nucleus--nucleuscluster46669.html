nucleus

noun
central core of a comet
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The solid central part of a comet, made of ice and frozen gases, which vaporizes as the comet approaches the Sun, forming the coma and tail.

Examples

nucleus of a comet
Astronomers observed the nucleus breaking apart after the comet's close passage to Jupiter.
Only the nucleus is visible when the comet is far from the Sun.
The nucleus of a comet contains icy material that vaporizes as it nears the sun.
