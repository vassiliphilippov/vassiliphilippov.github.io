encrustation

noun
decorative overlay or inlay layer
Pronunciation: / ɛnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a decorative coating or layer of contrasting material applied to a surface, often as an inlay or overlay

Examples

gold encrustation on altars
Medieval churches often feature gold encrustation on their altars and walls.
Restorers carefully cleaned the stone to preserve the original encrustation.
The jewelry box had an elegant encrustation of mother-of-pearl on its lid.
