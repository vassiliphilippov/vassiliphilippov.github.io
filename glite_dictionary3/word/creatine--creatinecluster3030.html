creatine

noun
energy-supplying compound in muscles
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːəˌtin / (AmE), / ˈkriːətiːn / (BrE)
an amino acid-like compound found in vertebrate muscle tissue, where it helps supply energy for muscle contraction

Examples

creatine supplement
Athletes often take creatine supplements to improve their performance and increase muscle mass.
High levels of creatine in the bloodstream may indicate muscle damage or excessive supplementation.
The body naturally produces creatine in the liver, kidneys, and pancreas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – creatinine: a breakdown product of creatine, excreted in urine
