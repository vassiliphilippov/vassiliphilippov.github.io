Calypso

proper noun
Greek mythological sea nymph
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪp.soʊ / (AmE), / kəˈlɪp.səʊ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a sea nymph who detained Odysseus on her island for seven years.

Examples

myth of Calypso
According to Greek legend, Calypso kept Odysseus on her island for seven years.
Homer's epic recounts how Calypso fell in love with Odysseus.
Some scholars interpret the story of Calypso as a symbol of temptation and captivity.
