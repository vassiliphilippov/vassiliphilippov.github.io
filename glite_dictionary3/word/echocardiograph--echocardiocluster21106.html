echocardiograph

noun
ultrasound heart imaging device
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkoʊˈkɑrdioʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊˈkɑːdiəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A medical instrument that uses ultrasound waves to create images of the heart and detect its abnormalities.

Examples

use an echocardiograph machine
A portable echocardiograph is especially useful in emergency situations.
Modern hospitals are equipped with the latest echocardiograph technology for cardiac assessments.
The doctor used an echocardiograph to examine the patient's heart function.
