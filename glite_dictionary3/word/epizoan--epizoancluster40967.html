epizoan

noun
external parasitic organism
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
an organism that lives on the surface of another animal as an external parasite

Examples

marine epizoan
Marine biologists studied the barnacles as epizoans living on the whale's skin.
Some epizoans, such as lice and fleas, can transmit diseases to their hosts.
The tick is a common epizoan that feeds on the blood of mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ectoparasite: a parasite that lives on the surface of its host
  • antonym – endoparasite: a parasite that lives inside the host's body
