condenser

noun
electrical device for storing charge
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
an electrical device that stores an electric charge, typically consisting of two conductors separated by an insulator; also known as a capacitor

Examples

condenser in circuit
A condenser can smooth out voltage fluctuations in an electrical circuit.
Older manuals often use the term condenser instead of capacitor.
The engineer replaced the faulty condenser in the radio circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capacitor: an electrical device that stores energy in an electric field
