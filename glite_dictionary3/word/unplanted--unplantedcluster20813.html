unplanted

adjective
not planted; without crops or plants
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈplæntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈplɑːntɪd / (BrE)
(of land) not sown with seeds or not having plants growing on it.

Examples

unplanted field
Several garden beds were left unplanted so the soil could recover.
The farmer inspected the unplanted section of his land for future crops.
The field remained unplanted throughout the growing season due to unfavorable weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym – planted: with seeds or plants established in the ground
