tapir

noun
large nocturnal mammal with a fleshy snout
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
a large, mostly nocturnal mammal with a heavy body and a flexible, fleshy snout, native to tropical America and Southeast Asia

Examples

wild tapir
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered tapir populations in South America.
Few people have seen a wild tapir because it is mostly active at night.
The tapir used its long snout to search for fruit on the forest floor.
