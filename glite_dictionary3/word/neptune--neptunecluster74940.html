Neptune

proper noun
Roman god of the sea
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛp.tuːn / (AmE), / ˈnɛp.tjuːn / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the sea, equivalent to the Greek god Poseidon.

Examples

Neptune god of the sea
In Roman mythology, Neptune was believed to control storms and earthquakes at sea.
Many ancient temples were dedicated to Neptune, where sailors prayed for safe journeys.
The artist depicted Neptune with his trident, emerging from the waves.
