misplay

noun
baseball defensive mistake
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
(Baseball) a mistake by a defensive player in failing to make an out when a routine play would have sufficed.

Examples

costly misplay
Despite his earlier misplay, the player redeemed himself with a spectacular catch.
One costly misplay can change the momentum of a baseball game.
The shortstop's misplay in the seventh inning cost the team a crucial run.
