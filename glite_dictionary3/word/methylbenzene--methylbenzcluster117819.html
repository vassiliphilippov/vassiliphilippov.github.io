methylbenzene

noun
colorless flammable liquid hydrocarbon (toluene)
Pronunciation: / ˌmiːθəlˈbɛnzin / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəlˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid hydrocarbon obtained from petroleum or coal tar, used as a solvent and in high-octane fuels; also known as toluene
