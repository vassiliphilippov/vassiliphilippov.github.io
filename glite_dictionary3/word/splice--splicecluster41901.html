to splice

verb
join by interweaving strands
Pronunciation: / splaɪs / (AmE), / splaɪs / (BrE)
to join two or more pieces of rope, wire, film, or similar material together by intertwining, weaving, or fastening their ends

Examples

to splice two ropes
Before sailing, the crew took time to splice the frayed rope.
She learned how to splice film reels during her training at the studio.
The electrician carefully spliced the wires together to restore the connection.
Carefully splicing the cables ensured a stable electrical connection.
The technician used special tape to splice the torn film segments back together.
They needed to splice the two ropes to make one long, strong rope for the rescue.
