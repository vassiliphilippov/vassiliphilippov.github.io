shade

noun
area of partial darkness caused by blocked light
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪd / (AmE), / ʃeɪd / (BrE)
an area where light is blocked, creating darkness or coolness (including in art or drawing)

Examples

cool shade
He moved his chair into the shade because it was too hot in the direct sunlight.
The children played in the shade while their parents relaxed nearby.
We sat in the shade of the large oak tree to escape the midday sun.
Proper use of shade makes the objects in a drawing appear three-dimensional.
The artist carefully added shade to the portrait to create depth and realism.
Using different pencils, he practiced drawing shade under the tree's branches.

Related Terms

  • antonym – highlight: an area in a picture or design that is strongly illuminated
  • synonym – shadow: a dark area or shape produced by a body coming between rays of light and a surface
