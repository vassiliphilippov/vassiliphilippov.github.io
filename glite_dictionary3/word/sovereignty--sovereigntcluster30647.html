sovereignty

noun
supreme authority of a state
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːvrənti /; / ˈsɑvərənti / (AmE), / ˈsɒvrənti /; / ˈsɒvərənti / (BrE)
the authority of a state to govern itself and control its own affairs, including legal or digital matters within its borders

Examples

national sovereignty
Debates over national sovereignty often arise in the context of international organizations.
Many countries fought hard to gain their sovereignty from colonial powers.
The treaty affirmed the sovereignty of each nation over its territory.
Many countries claim sovereignty over user data stored on servers within their borders.
Debates about cloud computing often focus on national sovereignty concerning cross-border data flows and access.
The law asserted digital sovereignty by requiring companies to keep citizens' records on local servers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – autonomy: the right or condition of self-government
  • antonym – dependency: a territory that is subject to the authority of another state
  • synonym – jurisdiction: the official power to make legal decisions and judgments in a particular area or over particular people or things
