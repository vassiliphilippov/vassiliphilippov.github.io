to reappraise

verb
assess again or review value
Pronunciation: / ˌriəˈpreɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈpreɪz / (BrE)
to appraise or assess something again, especially in order to reconsider its value or importance

Examples

to reappraise a decision
It is necessary to reappraise our investment strategy in light of recent market changes.
The bank decided to reappraise the property before approving the new mortgage.
The government will reappraise the old tax system to ensure fairness.
