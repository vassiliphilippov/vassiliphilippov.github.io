totipotency

noun
ability of a cell to form all cell types or a complete organism
Pronunciation: / toʊˈtɪpətənsi / (AmE), / təʊˈtɪpətənsi / (BrE)
the ability of a single cell, especially in early development, to develop into a complete organism or any of its parts by giving rise to all cell types

Examples

totipotency of a cell
In mammals, totipotency is lost soon after the first few cell divisions.
Researchers study totipotency to better understand how organisms develop from a single cell.
The concept of totipotency is essential for advances in cloning technologies.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – multipotency: ability of a cell to develop into multiple, but limited, cell types
  • degree lower – pluripotency: ability of a cell to develop into most, but not all, cell types
