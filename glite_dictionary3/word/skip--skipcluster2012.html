to skip

verb
move with light hops
Pronunciation: / skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to move by hopping or leaping lightly and quickly, often in a playful way

Examples

to skip down the street
She loves to skip down the street on sunny days.
The children skipped across the playground, laughing as they went.
They skipped lightly over the puddles to avoid getting their shoes wet.
Children were skipping and laughing in the sunny park.
He skipped across the puddles, barely getting his shoes wet.
The little girl loves to skip down the path on her way to school.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hop: to move by jumping on one foot
  • synonym – jump: to push oneself off a surface and into the air by using the muscles in one's legs and feet
  • antonym – walk: to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
