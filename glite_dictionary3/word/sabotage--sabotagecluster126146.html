to sabotage

verb
deliberately destroy or hinder
Pronunciation: / ˈsæbəˌtɑdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsæbətɑːʒ / (BrE)
to deliberately destroy, damage, or obstruct something, especially for political or military advantage

Examples

to sabotage a plan
During the strike, some workers sabotaged factory equipment to stop production.
Someone tried to sabotage the experiment by tampering with the data.
The employees threatened to sabotage the machinery if their demands were not met.

Related Terms

  • antonym – protect: to keep safe from harm or injury
  • synonym – wreck: to deliberately destroy or severely damage something
