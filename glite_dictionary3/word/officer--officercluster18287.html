officer

noun
person holding position of authority or trust
Pronunciation: / ˈɑː.fɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ɪ.sə / (BrE)
a person who is appointed or elected to a position of authority or trust within an organization, such as a company, club, or government body

Examples

club officer
Every club must have at least three officers: a president, a treasurer, and a secretary.
He served as an officer of the charity organization for over ten years.
She was elected as an officer of the student council for the next academic year.
