nervus glossopharyngeus

noun
ninth cranial nerve; pharynx and tongue innervation
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜːrvəs ˌɡlɒsəfəˈrɪndʒiəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ˌɡlɒsəfəˈrɪndʒiːəs / (BrE)
the ninth cranial nerve, responsible for providing sensory innervation to the pharynx and back of the tongue, as well as motor fibers to muscles that elevate the pharynx and larynx; contains parasympathetic fibers to the otic ganglion

Examples

nervus glossopharyngeus controls swallowing
Damage to the nervus glossopharyngeus can result in difficulty swallowing and altered taste sensation.
During the anatomy lecture, the professor described the course and function of the nervus glossopharyngeus.
Stimulation of the nervus glossopharyngeus plays a role in the gag reflex.
