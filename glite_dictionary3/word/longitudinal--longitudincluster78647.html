longitudinal

adjective
measured over a long period of time
Pronunciation: / ˌlɑːn.dʒəˈtuː.dɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˌlɒn.dʒɪˈtjuː.dɪ.nəl / (BrE)
(of research or data) involving repeated observations or measurements of the same subjects over an extended period of time.

Examples

longitudinal study over years
A longitudinal analysis often reveals trends that cross-sectional studies may miss.
Researchers collected data from the same subjects at several points in this longitudinal project.
The longitudinal study tracked thousands of participants for more than twenty years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cross-sectional: Observations or measurements at a single point in time (research design)
