mulish

adjective
obstinately stubborn
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːlɪʃ / (BrE)
Unreasonably stubborn or obstinate, especially in refusing to change one's mind or attitude.

Examples

mulish refusal to change
Despite everyone's advice, he remained mulish and refused to listen.
Her mulish insistence on doing things her way caused several delays.
The child sat with a mulish expression, unwilling to apologize.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flexible: willing to change or compromise
  • synonym – obstinate: refusing to change behavior or ideas
  • synonym – stubborn: unwilling to change one's mind or attitude
