collar

noun
figurative restraint or restriction
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
a restriction or limit, especially one imposed to control or restrain behavior or activity (figurative)

Examples

collar on freedom
He argued that government intervention serves as a collar on innovation.
The board decided to put a collar on executive bonuses to avoid excessive payouts.
The new regulations placed a collar on risky trading practices.
