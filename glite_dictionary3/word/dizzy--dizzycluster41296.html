dizzy

adjective
frivolous or scatterbrained (person)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪzi / (AmE), / ˈdɪzi / (BrE)
(of a person) silly, scatterbrained, or lacking seriousness; frivolous or flighty.

Examples

dizzy person at work
He tended to act a little dizzy when the discussion turned serious.
Her dizzy remarks made everyone laugh at the meeting.
The movie portrays a dizzy heiress who doesn't care about responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – serious: thoughtful, solemn, not playful or silly
  • synonym – silly: showing a lack of good sense or judgment; frivolous
