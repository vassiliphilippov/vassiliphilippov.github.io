haematocele

noun
swelling from blood in a body cavity
Pronunciation: / hɪˈmætəˌsil / (AmE), / hɪˈmætəˌsiːl / (BrE)
a swelling caused by blood collecting in a body cavity, especially in the membrane covering the testis

Examples

haematocele in the scrotum
After the injury, a haematocele formed, causing considerable discomfort and swelling.
The surgeon identified a haematocele during the examination of the patient's scrotum.
Treatment of a haematocele often involves draining the accumulated blood.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hematoma: a localized swelling filled with blood outside blood vessels
