Regency

noun
British period (1811–1820, Prince Regent)
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənsi / (BrE)
the period in British history from 1811 to 1820 when the future George IV ruled as Prince Regent during King George III's incapacity

Examples

the Regency period
Jane Austen's novels were written during the Regency.
Many social customs of the Regency era are depicted in historical dramas.
The Regency period is famous for its distinctive architecture and styles.
