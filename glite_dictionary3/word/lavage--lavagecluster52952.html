lavage

noun
medical washing out of an organ
Pronunciation: / ləˈvɑʒ / (AmE), / laˈvɑːʒ / (BrE)
the washing out of a hollow organ, especially the stomach, by flushing with water or another fluid

Examples

lavage of the stomach
Pulmonary lavage can help diagnose and treat certain lung conditions.
The doctor performed a gastric lavage to remove the toxins from her stomach.
The patient underwent lavage after accidental ingestion of a harmful substance.
