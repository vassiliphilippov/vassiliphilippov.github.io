wobbly

adjective
unsteady or shaky
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑbli / (AmE), / ˈwɒbli / (BrE)
unsteady and likely to shake or move from side to side, often because of weakness or a defect

Examples

wobbly chair
After spinning in circles, she stood up and took a few wobbly steps.
He felt nervous crossing the wobbly old bridge over the stream.
The table became wobbly after one of the legs broke.

Related Terms

  • antonym – steady: firm, not shaking or moving
  • synonym – unstable: not firmly fixed; likely to move or fall
