rhymester

noun
writer of (often inferior) rhymes
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪm.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈraɪm.stə / (BrE)
a person who writes rhymes, especially a poet regarded as minor or inferior

Examples

talentless rhymester
Many critics dismissed him as nothing more than a rhymester with little real talent.
The literary magazine refused to publish the rhymester's latest batch of verses.
Though called a rhymester by her rivals, she continued to write playful poetry for children.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bard: a poet, especially one reciting epics
  • synonym – poet: a person who writes poems
  • synonym – poetaster: an inferior poet; a writer of mediocre verse
