to grin

verb
smile broadly, showing the teeth
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪn / (BrE)
to smile broadly, often by showing the teeth, sometimes as a sign of amusement, pleasure, or embarrassment

Examples

to grin with delight
He grinned sheepishly as he admitted his mistake.
She couldn't help but grin when she saw the surprise party waiting for her.
The children were grinning from ear to ear after receiving their gifts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – frown: to make a facial expression showing disapproval or displeasure
  • synonym – smile: to make a facial expression of pleasure or amusement, often without showing teeth
