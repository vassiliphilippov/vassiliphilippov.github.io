limerick

noun
humorous five-line poem
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmɚɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪmərɪk / (BrE)
a humorous poem of five lines with a rhyme scheme of aabba, typically with a witty or absurd subject

Examples

A classic limerick often begins with 'There once was a man from...'.
