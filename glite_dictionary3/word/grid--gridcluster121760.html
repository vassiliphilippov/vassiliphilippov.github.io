grid

noun
metal battery plate for conductance/support
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
a perforated or corrugated metal plate in a storage battery, used as a conductor and support for the active material

Examples

battery grid plate
Damaged grids can cause a loss of capacity in lead-acid batteries.
The grid serves both as a conductor and as a support for the active material within each cell.
The positive grid in the battery must be made of lead alloy to resist corrosion.
