watcher

noun
person who keeps vigil by the sick or dead
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person who keeps a devotional vigil by the bedside of a sick or dead person

Examples

watcher by the bedside
Each family member took turns as a watcher during the long illness.
The watcher sat silently through the night, praying beside the dying man.
Tradition required a watcher to stay by the body until burial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vigil: a period of keeping awake, especially to keep watch or pray
