to stem

verb
originate from; arise from
Pronunciation: / stɛm / (AmE), / stɛm / (BrE)
(of a problem, event, etc.) to arise or originate from a particular cause or situation.

Examples

stem from a cause
Her anxiety stemmed from a difficult childhood.
Many English words stem from Latin or Greek roots.
Our challenges often stem from poor communication within the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – derive: to come from or be developed from something
  • synonym – originate: to have a specified beginning; arise
