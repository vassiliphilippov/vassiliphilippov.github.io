to last

verb
to persist for a period
Pronunciation: / læst / (AmE), / lɑːst / (BrE)
to continue for a period of time; to persist or remain in existence or survive

Examples

to last for years
Let's hope the sunny weather lasts until the weekend so we can go hiking.
The celebrations lasted all night and brought the whole community together.
Their friendship has lasted for over twenty years, despite living in different countries.
During the harsh winter, only a few villagers managed to last.
He wondered how much longer his strength would last as the journey continued.
Many species are unable to last without a steady food supply.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cease: to come to an end; to stop
  • synonym – endure: to continue or persist for a long time, especially despite difficulties
  • antonym – perish: to die, especially in a sudden or untimely way
  • synonym – survive: to continue to live or exist, especially after coming close to dying or being destroyed
