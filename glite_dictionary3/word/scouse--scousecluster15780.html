scouse

noun
stew of meat, vegetables, and hardtack
Pronunciation: / skaʊs / (AmE), / skaʊs / (BrE)
A traditional stew of meat, vegetables, and hardtack, originally eaten by sailors and closely associated with Liverpool.

Examples

a bowl of scouse
Beef or lamb is usually used as the main ingredient in traditional scouse.
Many people in Liverpool still cook scouse as a hearty family meal.
The sailors looked forward to a hot bowl of scouse after a long day at sea.
