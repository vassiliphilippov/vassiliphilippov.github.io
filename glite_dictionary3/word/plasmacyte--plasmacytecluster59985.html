plasmacyte

noun
white blood cell type; develops from B lymphocytes
Pronunciation: / ˈplæsməˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈplazməˌsaɪt / (BrE)
A type of white blood cell that develops from a B lymphocyte in response to a specific antigen, typically found in bone marrow and sometimes in the blood.

Examples

plasmacyte in bone marrow
High counts of plasmacytes may indicate certain bone marrow diseases.
Under the microscope, the doctor identified several plasmacytes in the patient's bone marrow sample.
When exposed to the antigen, a B cell can mature into a plasmacyte and produce antibodies.
