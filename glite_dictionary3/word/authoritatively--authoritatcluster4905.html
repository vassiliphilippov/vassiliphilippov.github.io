authoritatively

adverb
in a commanding or authoritative manner
Pronunciation: / əˌθɔːrɪˈteɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒrɪtətɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that shows confidence, authority, and is likely to be respected or obeyed.

Examples

authoritatively explain rules
He authoritatively explained the company's new policy to the staff.
The guide authoritatively answered every question about the historic site.
The judge addressed the courtroom authoritatively, leaving no doubt about her control.

  • synonym – confidently: in a self-assured manner
