precocial

adjective
born or hatched in an advanced, developed state
Pronunciation: / prɪˈkoʊʃəl / (AmE), / prɪˈkəʊʃəl / (BrE)
(of young birds or animals) born in an advanced state, with the ability to see and move around soon after birth or hatching.

Examples

precocial chicks walk
Ducks and chickens are precocial birds, able to walk and feed themselves soon after hatching.
The precocial young of the species left the nest less than a day after birth.
Unlike altricial mammals, many ungulates are precocial and stand within minutes of being born.

Related Terms

  • antonym – altricial: (of young animals) born or hatched in an undeveloped state, requiring care from parents
