muffled

adjective
wrapped up for warmth, protection, or secrecy
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈmʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) wrapped up in coverings, especially for warmth, protection, or secrecy, so as to be difficult to see or recognize.

Examples

muffled in blankets
She sat muffled in her blanket, barely visible in the dim light.
The children were muffled in layers of scarves and coats before going outside.
The spy stayed muffled in a heavy coat and hat to avoid recognition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exposed: not covered or protected
  • synonym – swaddled: wrapped tightly with cloth, especially a baby
