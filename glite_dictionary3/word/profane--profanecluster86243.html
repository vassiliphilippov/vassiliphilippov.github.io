to profane

verb
corrupt morally by intemperance or sensuality
Pronunciation: / prəˈfeɪn / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn / (BrE)
to corrupt morally, especially through indulgence in sensual pleasures or intemperance

Examples

to profane young minds
He was accused of trying to profane the youth with his radical ideas.
Some claimed that unchecked luxury could easily profane their society’s moral values.
The decadent lifestyle threatened to profane even the most upright citizens.
