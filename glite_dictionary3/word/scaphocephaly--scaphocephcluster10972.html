scaphocephaly

noun
skull malformation: long and narrow head
Pronunciation: / skæfəˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / skæfəʊˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by an abnormally long and narrow skull, often due to premature fusion of skull bones.

Examples

scaphocephaly in infants
Early detection of scaphocephaly can help prevent complications with brain development.
Surgery may be required in severe cases of scaphocephaly to correct the skull deformity.
The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with scaphocephaly after observing the unusual shape of his skull.
