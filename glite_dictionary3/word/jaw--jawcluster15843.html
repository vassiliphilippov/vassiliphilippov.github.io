jaw

noun
bony structure that frames the mouth
Pronunciation: / dʒɑ / (AmE), / dʒɔː / (BrE)
either of the two bony parts in the head that frame the mouth and hold the teeth, especially the lower movable part

Examples

broken jaw
He broke his jaw during the football match and needed surgery.
Snakes can unhinge their jaw to swallow prey much larger than their heads.
The dentist examined her upper and lower jaw for any signs of infection.
A shark’s powerful jaw can crush the shell of a sea turtle.
After being hit by the ball, his jaw was sore and swollen.
The dentist examined the patient's jaw to check for any signs of infection.

  • synonym – maxilla: the upper jawbone in vertebrates
