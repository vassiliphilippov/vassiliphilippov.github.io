directionality

noun
property of sensitivity in specific directions
Pronunciation: / dɪˌrɛkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / dɪˌrɛkʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the property of being more sensitive or responsive in a particular direction than in others, especially in microphones or antennas

Examples

directionality in microphones
Different types of microphones offer varying degrees of directionality depending on their intended use.
Engineers tested the antenna's directionality to ensure it received signals from the correct source.
The directionality of the microphone helps reduce background noise from the sides and rear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – directivity: the quality of being directional, especially sensitivity or response in a specific direction
