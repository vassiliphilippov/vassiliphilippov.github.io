polymerase

noun
enzyme that synthesizes DNA or RNA
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪməˌreɪs / (AmE), / pəˈlɪməˌreɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that assists in the synthesis of DNA or RNA molecules by assembling nucleotides in a sequence complementary to an existing strand

Examples

polymerase chain reaction
Researchers used a heat-resistant polymerase to amplify DNA in the lab.
The polymerase enzyme plays a crucial role in DNA replication during cell division.
When the polymerase malfunctions, genetic diseases can sometimes result.
