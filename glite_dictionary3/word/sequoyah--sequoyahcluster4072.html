Sequoyah

proper noun
Cherokee inventor of the Cherokee syllabary
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
(c. 1770–1843) A Cherokee silversmith who invented the Cherokee syllabary, a system of writing for the Cherokee language.

Examples

Sequoyah invented writing
Sequoyah is celebrated for developing the Cherokee writing system that enabled his people to preserve their language.
Historians recognize Sequoyah as one of the few people to create an original writing system.
Many schools and places in the United States are named after Sequoyah.
