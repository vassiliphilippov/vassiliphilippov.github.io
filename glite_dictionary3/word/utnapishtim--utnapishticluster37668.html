Utnapishtim

proper noun
Mesopotamian flood hero; immortal
Pronunciation: / ut.nəˈpɪʃ.tɪm / (AmE), / uːt.nəˈpɪʃ.tɪm / (BrE)
a hero in Mesopotamian mythology, favored by the gods, who survived the great flood and was granted immortality; the grandfather of Gilgamesh

Examples

Utnapishtim survives the flood
In the Epic of Gilgamesh, Utnapishtim reveals the secret of immortality to Gilgamesh.
Scholars have long debated the historical origins of Utnapishtim and the flood narrative.
The story of Utnapishtim closely resembles the biblical tale of Noah's Ark.
