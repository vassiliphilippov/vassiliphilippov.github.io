to pinch

verb
cut the tip off a plant
Pronunciation: / pɪntʃ / (AmE), / pɪntʃ / (BrE)
to squeeze or press something tightly between the fingers, often causing discomfort or to shape or seal something by pressing with the fingers

Examples

to pinch a finger
Gardeners often pinch young shoots to encourage more flowers.

Potters often pinch the clay to create textured designs on bowls and cups.
She used her fingers to pinch the edges of the pie crust into a decorative pattern.
You should pinch the fabric to make pleats along the seam.
If you pinch the dough, it will seal the edges better.
She pinched his arm to get his attention during the movie.
The child cried out when his sister pinched him on the cheek.
