to salute

verb
express praise or admiration
Pronunciation: / səˈlut / (AmE), / səˈluːt / (BrE)
to show respect, honor, or greet someone, including by praising, toasting, or acknowledging them

Examples

to salute a hero
Everyone stood to salute the extraordinary achievements of the team.
I would like to salute all the volunteers who made this event possible.
The president publicly saluted the scientists for their groundbreaking work.
Everyone at the table stood to salute the retiring professor.
He raised his glass to salute the team's achievements.
Let us salute the newlyweds on their special day!
Crowds gathered to salute those who sacrificed their lives in service.
The president will salute the heroes of the nation during the annual parade.
The soldiers saluted their fallen comrades with a solemn ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – commend: to praise formally or officially
  • antonym – criticize: to express disapproval or point out faults
  • antonym – disregard: to pay no attention to or fail to show respect
  • synonym – greet: to say hello or welcome someone
  • synonym – honor: to show great respect, especially in a formal way
  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to someone
  • synonym – praise: to express warm approval or admiration
  • synonym – toast: to propose or drink to someone's health or honor
