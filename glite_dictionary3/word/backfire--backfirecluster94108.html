to backfire

verb
make a loud noise due to engine malfunction
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
(of an engine or vehicle) to make a loud explosive noise, typically caused by fuel igniting outside the combustion chamber.

Examples

to backfire loudly
As the bus approached, its engine suddenly backfired, startling the people nearby.
My old car backfires every time I start it on a cold morning.
You could hear the motorcycle backfiring as it sped down the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – misfire: fail to fire or ignite as intended (engines, firearms)
