chromaesthesia

noun
sensation of colors from nonvisual stimuli
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊməˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊməˈθiːziə / (BrE)
a condition in which the perception of colors is triggered by nonvisual stimuli, such as sounds or numbers

Examples

chromaesthesia with music
Her chromaesthesia made conversations a uniquely colorful experience, as every voice produced a different hue.
Research into chromaesthesia helps scientists understand the connections between sensory pathways in the brain.
Some musicians with chromaesthesia see vivid colors whenever they hear certain notes or chords.

Related Terms

  • synonym – synesthesia: a condition in which stimulation of one sensory pathway leads to automatic experiences in a second sensory pathway
