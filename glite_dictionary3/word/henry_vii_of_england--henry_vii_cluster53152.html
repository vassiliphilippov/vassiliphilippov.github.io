Henry VII of England

proper noun
first Tudor king of England, 1485–1509
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛnri ðə ˈsɛvənθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˌhɛnri ði ˈsɛvənθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
the first Tudor king of England (reigned 1485–1509), who defeated Richard III at Bosworth Field and united the houses of York and Lancaster by marrying Elizabeth of York.

Examples

Henry VII of England united England
Henry VII of England established the Tudor dynasty after defeating Richard III at the Battle of Bosworth Field.
By marrying Elizabeth of York, Henry VII of England effectively united the feuding houses of Lancaster and York.
Many historians credit Henry VII of England with restoring stability to a country riven by civil war.
