persuasion

noun
a personal belief or opinion
Pronunciation: / pərˈsweɪʒən / (AmE), / pəˈsweɪʒən / (BrE)
A personal belief or opinion, especially one that is held without proof or certainty.

Examples

political persuasion
Among people of every persuasion, there was agreement on the need for change.
Despite belonging to a different persuasion, they remained good friends.
He is a man of liberal persuasion and strong convictions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conviction: a firmly held belief or opinion
  • synonym – opinion: a belief or judgment not based on absolute certainty
