exempt

adjective
free from an obligation or duty
Pronunciation: / ɪgˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) free from an obligation or liability to which others are subject.

Examples

exempt from duty
Certain medical conditions may make you exempt from military service.
Children under the age of five are exempt from ticket charges.
She was exempt from the final exam because of her outstanding grades.

Related Terms

  • antonym – liable: subject to an obligation or duty
