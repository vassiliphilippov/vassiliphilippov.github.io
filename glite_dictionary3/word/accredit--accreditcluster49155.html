to accredit

verb
to ascribe or assign credit
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / əˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to officially recognize, approve, or certify someone or something as meeting certain standards or being responsible for something

Examples

to accredit an institution
Historians accredit the invention to an anonymous artisan from the 14th century.
Many people accredit their success to hard work and perseverance.
The film's director was accredited with the innovative visual effects.
Only certified inspectors are accredited to evaluate the site for safety regulation compliance.
The program is currently seeking to be accredited by a recognized professional association.
The university was accredited by a national education board last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authorize: to give official permission for something
  • antonym – blame: to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • synonym – certify: to formally attest or confirm as true or meeting a standard
  • synonym – credit: to ascribe an achievement or quality to someone
  • antonym – discredit: to harm the reputation of or deny credibility
