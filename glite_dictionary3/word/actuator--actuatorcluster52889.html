actuator

noun
device that activates movement or action
Pronunciation: / ˈæktʃuˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.eɪ.tər / (BrE)
A mechanical device that causes a machine or system to operate or move automatically when activated by a signal.

Examples

electric actuator
A faulty actuator prevented the automatic door from opening.
Hydraulic actuators are widely used in industrial machinery for powerful linear movement.
The robotic arm uses an actuator to lift and move heavy objects precisely.

  • synonym – motor: a machine that supplies motive power for a device
