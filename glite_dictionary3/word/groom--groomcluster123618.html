to groom

verb
prepare or train for a future role
Pronunciation: / ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
to prepare or train someone for a particular future role or function

Examples

to groom a future leader
He was groomed from a young age to inherit the family business.
The company is grooming her for a leadership position in the future.
They groomed their children to excel in both academics and athletics.
