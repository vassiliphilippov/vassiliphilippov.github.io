idiocy

noun
extreme stupidity or, formerly, severe mental disability
Pronunciation: / ˈɪdiəsi / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəsi / (BrE)
extreme stupidity or folly; (historically, now offensive) the state of having very low intelligence or mental capacity.

Examples

utter idiocy
His reckless decision showed a remarkable level of idiocy.
In the past, the term idiocy was used in medical contexts, but it is now considered offensive and outdated.
The film’s plot was criticized for its sheer idiocy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intelligence: the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • synonym – stupidity: lack of intelligence or common sense
