thorny

adjective
difficult, complicated (problem or issue)
Pronunciation: / ˈθɔrni / (AmE), / ˈθɔːni / (BrE)
(of a problem or situation) full of difficulties or complications; causing problems or debate.

Examples

thorny issue
Despite his experience, he found himself challenged by the thorny legal complexities of the case.
She avoided answering the thorny questions raised during the interview.
The committee struggled with the thorny issue of how to allocate funding fairly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – difficult: not easy; requiring great effort
  • antonym – simple: easy to understand or deal with
