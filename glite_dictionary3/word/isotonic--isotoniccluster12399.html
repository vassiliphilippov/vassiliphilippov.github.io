isotonic

adjective
having equal osmotic pressure (in solutions)
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈtɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈtɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of solutions) having the same or equal osmotic pressure, especially as applied to biological fluids.

Examples

isotonic solution balance
An isotonic drink helps maintain fluid balance during intense exercise.
Saline used in medical treatments is usually isotonic with human blood.
The experiment compared the effects of isotonic and hypertonic solutions on red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – hypotonic: (of a solution) having a lower osmotic pressure than another solution
