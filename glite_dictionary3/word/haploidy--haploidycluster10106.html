haploidy

noun
single set of chromosomes (genetics)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæplɔɪdi / (AmE), / ˈhæplɔɪdi / (BrE)
the condition of having a single set of unpaired chromosomes in a cell or organism

Examples

haploidy in gametes
In humans, sperm and egg cells are characterized by haploidy.
Many fungi exhibit haploidy during part of their life cycle.
The transition from haploidy to diploidy is a key event during fertilization.

Related Terms

  • antonym – diploidy: condition of having two complete sets of chromosomes
  • synonym – haploid: having a single set of unpaired chromosomes
