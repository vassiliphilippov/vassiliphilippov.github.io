fiberoptics

noun
light transmission through glass or plastic fibers
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbərˈɑptɪks / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbərˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
The transmission of light signals through thin glass or plastic fibers, mainly used for high-speed data communication and imaging.

Examples

fiberoptics technology
Advances in fiberoptics have revolutionized medical imaging and telecommunications.
Many internet service providers use fiberoptics for faster and more reliable connections.
