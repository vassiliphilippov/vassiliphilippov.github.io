burnish

noun
smooth, shiny surface quality
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪʃ / (BrE)
a smooth and shiny quality, especially on a surface as a result of polishing or rubbing

Examples

burnish of silver
A fresh burnish on the silverware gave it a mirror-like gleam.
The artist admired the burnish of the finished bronze sculpture.
The marble floor had lost its burnish after years of heavy use.

Related Terms

  • antonym – matte: a surface without shine or gloss
  • synonym – sheen: a soft, smooth shine on a surface
