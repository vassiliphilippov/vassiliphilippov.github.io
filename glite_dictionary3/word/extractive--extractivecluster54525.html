extractive

adjective
prioritizing short-term profit over sustainability
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
(of an economic or political system) prioritizing short-term profit or resource extraction over long-term sustainability, often benefiting a small elite.

Examples

extractive economic system
Critics argued that the extractive economic system undermined opportunities for sustainable growth.
Policies favoring extractive practices can lead to environmental degradation and social inequality.
The country's extractive institutions have concentrated wealth and power in the hands of a few.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inclusive: (of a system) designed to benefit a broad base of the population; encouraging participation or inclusion
