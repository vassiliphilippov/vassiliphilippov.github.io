licorice

noun
plant with sweet, edible roots
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (BrE)
A deep-rooted, coarse-textured plant native to the Mediterranean region, with blue flowers and pinnately compound leaves, cultivated for its long, sweet roots.

Examples

licorice plant roots
Blue flowers bloom on the licorice in early summer.
Farmers in southern Europe cultivate licorice for commercial purposes.
The licorice plant is grown for its sweet root, often used in candies and herbal teas.
