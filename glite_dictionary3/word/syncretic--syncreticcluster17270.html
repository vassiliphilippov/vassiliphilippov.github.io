syncretic

adjective
combining different beliefs or systems
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈkrɛtɪk / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkrɛtɪk / (BrE)
combining different beliefs, philosophies, or practices, especially in religion, into a new or unified system

Examples

syncretic religious beliefs
Her research focused on syncretic religions that emerged in the Caribbean after colonization.
The country's culture is syncretic, blending elements from various indigenous and colonial traditions.
The music festival showcased a syncretic mix of jazz, hip hop, and traditional folk rhythms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – eclectic: deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources
