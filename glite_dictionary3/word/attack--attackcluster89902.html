to attack

verb
begin an aggressive action
Pronunciation: / əˈtæk / (AmE), / əˈtæk / (BrE)
to use force, violence, or aggressive action to harm, damage, or defeat someone or something (including military, sports, or destructive processes); or to act aggressively against someone or something

Examples

to attack a target
During the second half, the team tried to attack down the right wing.
Hackers may attack company networks using sophisticated software.
The army planned to attack at sunrise when the enemy least expected it.
She felt as though her ideas were being attacked during the heated debate.
The dog suddenly attacked the mail carrier as he walked up the driveway.
The virus attacked his immune system, leaving him very weak.
Strong acids may attack the surface of the metal pipes over time.
The bacteria can attack healthy tissue if left untreated.
The virus began to attack his immune system, making him very ill.

  • synonym – damage: to cause physical harm to something
  • antonym – defend: to protect from attack or to resist aggression
