generalissimo

noun
supreme military commander
Pronunciation: / dʒəˌnɛrəlɪˈsimoʊ / (AmE), / dʒəˌnɛrəlɪˈsiːməʊ / (BrE)
the officer with the highest command authority in an army or armed forces, especially one exercising supreme control over multiple armies or countries

Examples

the generalissimo commands armies
After years of conflict, the generalissimo declared victory and called for peace.
During the war, he was appointed generalissimo of the allied forces.
The generalissimo exercised absolute authority over the entire military hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – commander-in-chief: the person with supreme command of armed forces
  • degree lower – field marshal: a high-ranking army officer, usually second only to supreme commander
