disfranchisement

noun
loss of right or privilege, especially to vote
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪz.mənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪz.mənt / (BrE)
the act or state of being deprived of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote

Examples

disfranchisement of voters
Historical records show widespread disfranchisement during the early 20th century in the southern states.
Legal battles sought to challenge the disfranchisement imposed by discriminatory regulations.
The new laws resulted in the disfranchisement of thousands of eligible voters.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disenfranchisement: the state of being deprived of the right to vote
  • antonym – enfranchisement: granting of voting rights or other privileges
