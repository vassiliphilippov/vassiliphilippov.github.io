sub-assembly

noun
unit assembled separately for larger product
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.əˈsɛm.bli / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.əˈsɛm.bli / (BrE)
a group of parts assembled together as a unit, intended to be incorporated with other units into a final manufactured product

Examples

sub-assembly of engine parts
Each sub-assembly is thoroughly tested before it becomes part of the main product line.
The gear system is produced as a sub-assembly before being installed in the transmission.
