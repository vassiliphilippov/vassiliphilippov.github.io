to rape

verb
violently strip or destroy (figurative)
Pronunciation: / reɪp / (AmE), / reɪp / (BrE)
(figurative) To violently and disastrously take away or destroy something's possessions or beauty, especially with reference to land or a country.

Examples

to rape the land
Centuries of war have raped the countryside, leaving little of its former beauty.
Critics argue that unchecked mining rapes the land for profit.
The occupying forces raped the village of all its valuable artwork and treasures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – devastate: to destroy much or most of something
  • synonym – ruin: to damage or destroy something so badly that it loses all value, use, or pleasure
