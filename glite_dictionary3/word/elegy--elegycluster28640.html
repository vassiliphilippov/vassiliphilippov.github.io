elegy

noun
mournful poem for the dead
Pronunciation: / ˈɛl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛl.ɪ.dʒi / (BrE)
a poem or song composed as a lament for someone who has died, expressing sorrow or mourning

Examples

elegy for a friend
During the memorial service, she read an elegy dedicated to her grandfather.
Many English poets have composed an elegy to honor those lost in war.
The poet wrote a touching elegy in memory of his friend.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dirge: a funeral song or tune of mourning
  • synonym – lament: a passionate expression of grief, especially as a song or poem
  • antonym – panegyric: a speech or piece of writing in praise of someone, especially the dead
