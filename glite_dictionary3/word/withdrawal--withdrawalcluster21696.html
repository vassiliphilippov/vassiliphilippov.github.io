withdrawal

noun
the act of withdrawing
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔəl / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːəl / (BrE)
the act of removing or taking away something or someone from a place or situation, including medical removal

Examples

withdrawal of troops
After hours of negotiation, the army announced the withdrawal of its troops.
His withdrawal from the competition disappointed many of his fans.
The sudden withdrawal of investment caused concern among the employees.
After the withdrawal of the tumor, the patient recovered steadily.
Strict procedures are followed during the withdrawal of tissue samples for testing.
The nurse performed the withdrawal of blood quickly and painlessly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advance: the act of moving forward to a position; the opposite of withdrawal
  • synonym – retreat: the act of moving back or withdrawing, especially in a military context
