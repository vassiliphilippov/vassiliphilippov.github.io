to proof

verb
allow dough to rise before baking
Pronunciation: / pruːf /; / pruf / (AmE), / pruːf / (BrE)
to allow dough to rise or to activate yeast before baking

Examples

to proof bread dough
After proofing overnight in the fridge, the dough was ready to bake.
If you don't proof the dough properly, the bread will turn out dense.
You need to proof the dough for an hour before shaping it into loaves.
Be sure to proof the yeast before adding it to the dough.
Let the mixture stand for ten minutes to proof the yeast before baking.
She forgot to proof the active dry yeast, so the bread did not rise.
