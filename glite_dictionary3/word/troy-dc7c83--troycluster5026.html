troy

noun
system of weights for precious metals
Pronunciation: / trɔɪ / (AmE), / trɔɪ / (BrE)
a system of weights used for precious metals and gemstones, in which the pound consists of 12 ounces, each ounce being 480 grains

Examples

troy ounce weight
A troy pound contains 12 ounces instead of the usual sixteen.
Silver is measured in troy ounces rather than avoirdupois ounces.
The price of gold is often quoted per troy ounce.
