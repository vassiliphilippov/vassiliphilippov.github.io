to demarcate

verb
set or mark boundaries
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɑːrˌkeɪt /; / diˈmɑrˌkeɪt / (AmE), / diːˈmɑːkeɪt /; / dɪˈmɑːkeɪt / (BrE)
to set or mark the boundaries or limits of something

Examples

to demarcate a border
A fence was erected to demarcate the two farms from one another.
Clear road markings demarcate the bicycle lane from the main traffic area.
The surveyors will demarcate the property lines before construction begins.
Clearly demarcating responsibilities ensures that every team member knows their role.
The fence was built to demarcate the boundary between the two properties.
The river demarcates the official border between the two countries.
