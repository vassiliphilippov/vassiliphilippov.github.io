to surf

verb
browse casually (esp. on the internet or TV)
Pronunciation: / sɜrf /; / sɝf / (AmE), / sɜːf / (BrE)
to look around or browse casually, especially through information or channels such as on the internet or television, without a specific purpose

Examples

to surf the internet
He spent hours surfing channels, hoping to find something interesting to watch.
Many people surf online stores without any intention of buying.
She likes to surf the internet in the evenings to discover new recipes.
He likes to surf late at night, never settling on one show for long.
I was surfing through the channels when I stumbled upon an old movie.
Instead of watching the news, she just surfed until she got bored.

  • synonym – channel-hop: to repeatedly change television channels
