megaloblast

noun
abnormally large red blood cell
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡələˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡələʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
an unusually large red blood cell, typically found in certain types of anemia such as pernicious anemia or folic acid deficiency

Examples

abnormal megaloblast cell
A deficiency in vitamin B12 may result in the production of megaloblasts.
Microscopic examination revealed several megaloblasts among the red blood cells.
The presence of megaloblasts in the blood is a hallmark of certain types of anemia.
