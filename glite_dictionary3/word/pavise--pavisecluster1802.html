pavise

noun
large medieval oblong shield
Pronunciation: / pəˈviz / (AmE), / pəˈviːz / (BrE)
a large, heavy, oblong shield used in the Middle Ages to protect the whole body, originally carried but sometimes placed in a fixed position

Examples

medieval pavise shield
Archers would hide behind a pavise while reloading their crossbows on the battlefield.
During the siege, soldiers placed the pavise upright to shield themselves from arrows.
The museum displayed a well-preserved 15th-century pavise with elaborate heraldic designs.
