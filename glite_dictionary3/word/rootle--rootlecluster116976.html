to rootle

verb
dig in the ground with the snout
Pronunciation: / ˈrutl̩ / (AmE), / ˈruːtl̩ / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a pig) to dig in the ground with the snout in search of food.

Examples

to rootle in the soil
The pig began to rootle through the soil searching for truffles.
Wild boars often rootle in the undergrowth looking for food.
You could see the piglets rootling happily in the muddy pen.
