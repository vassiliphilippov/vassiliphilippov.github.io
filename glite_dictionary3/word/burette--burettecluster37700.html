burette

noun
laboratory measuring glass tube
Pronunciation: / bjʊˈrɛt / (AmE), / bjʊəˈrɛt / (BrE)
a thin, graduated glass tube with a tap at the bottom, used in laboratories for measuring and dispensing precise amounts of liquid, especially in titrations

Examples

fill a burette with liquid
Always rinse a burette with the solution it will dispense to ensure accurate measurements.
Reading the meniscus at eye level is crucial when using a burette in titrations.
The chemist carefully filled the burette with the titrant before beginning the experiment.
