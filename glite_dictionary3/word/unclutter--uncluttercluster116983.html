to unclutter

verb
remove clutter or obstructions
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈklʌtər / (AmE), / ʌnˈklʌtə / (BrE)
to remove unnecessary items or obstructions from a place, making it tidy or easier to use

Examples

to unclutter a room
I need to unclutter my workspace before I can start the project.
She decided to unclutter her living room by getting rid of old magazines and boxes.
We spent the afternoon helping our parents unclutter the garage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clutter: to fill a space in an untidy way with many things
  • synonym – declutter: remove clutter or unnecessary things
