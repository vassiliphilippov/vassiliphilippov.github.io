to make

verb
cause (something) to happen
Pronunciation: / meɪk / (AmE), / meɪk / (BrE)
to cause something to happen, exist, or be in a particular state; to force or cause someone to do something

Examples

to make someone laugh

Just seeing her smile made the whole evening worthwhile.
Your kind words really made my day.
She tried to make her explanation as simple as possible for everyone to understand.
Strong winds can make the sea extremely dangerous for small boats.
The good news really made my day brighter.
