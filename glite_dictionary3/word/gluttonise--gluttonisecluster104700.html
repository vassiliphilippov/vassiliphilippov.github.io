to gluttonise

verb
eat excessively and greedily
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlʌt.ən.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌt.ən.aɪz / (BrE)
To eat excessively and without restraint, often in a greedy manner.

Examples

to gluttonise at dinner
During the feast, he couldn't help but gluttonise on the endless selection of food.
She regretted gluttonising after feeling uncomfortably full all evening.
Whenever the holidays come around, some people tend to gluttonise far more than they intend.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fast: to refrain from eating
  • synonym – gorge: to eat greedily or to excess
  • synonym – overeat: to eat more food than necessary or healthy
