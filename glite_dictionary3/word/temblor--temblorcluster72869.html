temblor

noun
an earthquake or tremor
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛm.blɔr / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.blɔː / (BrE)
an earthquake; a shaking and vibration of the earth's surface caused by underground movement or volcanic activity

Examples

strong temblor
A powerful temblor struck the coastal region, causing widespread damage.
Residents felt the temblor just after midnight, sending them rushing outdoors.
The city was still recovering from last year’s temblor when another one hit.
