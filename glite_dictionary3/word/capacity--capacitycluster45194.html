capacity

noun
ability to do or produce; maximum amount
Pronunciation: / kəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / kəˈpæsəti /; / kəˈpæsɪti / (BrE)
the maximum amount that something can contain, produce, or hold (such as space, volume, or data storage)

Examples

maximum capacity
Due to limited capacity, only a few vehicles can cross the bridge at a time.
She has a remarkable capacity for learning new languages quickly.
The stadium has a seating capacity of 50,000 people.
Cloud services often offer additional capacity for an extra monthly fee.
Due to its low capacity, the old hard disk could not store large video files.
The capacity of this USB drive is 128 gigabytes, which is enough for most users.
During the pandemic, restaurants operated below full capacity due to safety regulations.
The factory increased its capacity by upgrading its machinery last year.
This machine is designed to work at maximum capacity for eight hours a day.

  • synonym – limit: the greatest amount possible or allowed in a particular situation
  • synonym – output: the amount produced by a person, machine, or industry
  • synonym – storage: the process or state of keeping data for use in a computer
  • synonym – volume: the amount of space that a substance or object occupies
