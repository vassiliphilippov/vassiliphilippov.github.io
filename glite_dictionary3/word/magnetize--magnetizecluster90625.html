to magnetize

verb
attract or influence strongly
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmaɡnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to attract or influence strongly, as if by a magnet

Examples

to magnetize a crowd
Her performance managed to magnetize everyone in the room.
The charismatic leader could magnetize crowds wherever he went.
The story was so intriguing that it seemed to magnetize my attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym – captivate: to attract and hold the attention of someone by being interesting or charming
  • antonym – repel: to drive or force away
