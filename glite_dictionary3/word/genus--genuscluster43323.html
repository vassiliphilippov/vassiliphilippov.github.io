genus

noun
biological taxonomic rank above species
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
(biology) A taxonomic rank used in the classification of organisms, ranking above species and below family, and consisting of related species.

Examples

genus of animals
Roses belong to the genus Rosa, which contains over a hundred species.
Scientists discovered a new genus of bacteria in the soil sample.
The genus Panthera includes lions, tigers, and leopards.
