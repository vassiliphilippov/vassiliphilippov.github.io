minesweeping

noun
the removal of marine mines
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnˌswiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnˌswiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of detecting and removing underwater mines, especially to make an area of water safe for navigation

Examples

minesweeping operations at sea
After the conflict, international teams focused on minesweeping to ensure safe passage for commercial ships.
Modern technology has greatly improved the efficiency of minesweeping in dangerous waters.
The navy carried out extensive minesweeping operations before reopening the harbor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mine clearance: the process of detecting and removing land or sea mines
  • antonym – minelaying: the act of placing mines in water or on land
