to reorder

verb
place an order for more goods or services
Pronunciation: / riˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / riˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to place an order again for goods or services, especially when the original supply has run out

Examples

to reorder supplies
After selling out of the product, we decided to reorder it from our supplier.
If you finish your prescription, you can easily reorder your medication online.
The store had to reorder the popular CD several times to keep up with demand.
