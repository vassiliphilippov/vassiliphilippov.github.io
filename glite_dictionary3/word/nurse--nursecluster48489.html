to nurse

verb
care for an illness or injury with special attention
Pronunciation: / nɝs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
to care for or tend to someone who is ill, injured, or in need of attention, in order to help them recover or grow

Examples

to nurse a patient
He has been nursing a sprained ankle for weeks now.
Many people try to nurse a cold with tea and rest.
She stayed home to nurse her son through the flu.
After his surgery, several professionals nursed him back to health.
During the epidemic, he nursed dozens of patients every day.
She decided to nurse her elderly mother at home rather than putting her in a care facility.
After surgery, he nursed his wounds and followed all of the doctor’s instructions.
He nursed his ankle by resting and avoiding any pressure on it.
She carefully nursed the sick plant back to health with water and sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym – care: to look after or provide for the needs of someone
  • antonym – neglect: to fail to care for properly
  • synonym – tend: to care for or look after someone or something
  • synonym – treat: to deal with an illness medically or with care
