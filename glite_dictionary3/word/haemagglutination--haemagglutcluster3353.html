haemagglutination

noun
clumping of red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˌɡluːtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The clumping together of red blood cells, usually as a result of a specific antibody or viral agent.

Examples

haemagglutination test result
A positive haemagglutination result indicated the presence of the pathogen.
Detection of antibodies was achieved through a haemagglutination assay.
The virus can cause haemagglutination when mixed with human erythrocytes in the lab.
