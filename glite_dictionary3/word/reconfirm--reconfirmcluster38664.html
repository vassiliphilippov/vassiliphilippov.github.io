to reconfirm

verb
confirm again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈfɜrm / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to confirm something again, especially to make sure that an earlier arrangement or decision is still valid or correct

Examples

to reconfirm a booking
Passengers are advised to reconfirm their bookings 24 hours before departure.
Please reconfirm your attendance by Friday to ensure your spot is reserved.
She called the hotel to reconfirm their reservation before leaving.
