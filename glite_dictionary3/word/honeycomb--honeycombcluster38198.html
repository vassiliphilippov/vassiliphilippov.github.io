honeycomb

noun
beeswax structure of hexagonal cells
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌniˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌkəʊm / (BrE)
a structure of hexagonal cells made by bees from wax, used to store honey and larvae

Examples

bees build honeycomb
Fresh honeycomb can be eaten as a sweet treat or spread on toast.
She marveled at the perfect symmetry of the honeycomb filled with golden honey.
The bees built a new honeycomb in the corner of the hive.
