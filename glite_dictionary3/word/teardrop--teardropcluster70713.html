teardrop

noun
a single drop of a tear
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪrˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈtɪəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A single drop of the salty liquid that forms in the eye as a result of emotion or irritation and runs down the cheek when a person cries.

Examples

a single teardrop
A single teardrop slid down her cheek as she listened to the sad news.
He noticed a teardrop clinging to her eyelashes just before she turned away.
The artist painted a portrait with a brilliant blue teardrop on the subject's face.

  • synonym – tear: the liquid secreted by glands in the eye
