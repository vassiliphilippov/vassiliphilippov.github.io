to canonise

verb
treat as sacred or saintly
Pronunciation: / ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkanənʌɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone as if they are sacred or saintly, often giving them special status or reverence

Examples

to canonise a hero
In his writings, he canonised her as the ideal woman.
Some critics argue that society tends to canonise certain celebrities regardless of their real achievements.
The film appears to canonise its protagonist, portraying her as flawless and beyond reproach.

Related Terms

  • synonym – idolize: to admire or revere excessively
  • antonym – vilify: to speak or write about with strong criticism or hatred
