declarative

noun
mood expressing statements or facts
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklɛrəˌtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈklærətɪv / (BrE)
a grammatical mood that is used to make statements or express facts

Examples

declarative mood grammar
A declarative is used to make statements rather than ask questions or give commands.
Most basic English sentences are in the declarative mood.
She wrote the sentence as a declarative, not as a question.

  • synonym – indicative: a grammatical mood used for factual statements
