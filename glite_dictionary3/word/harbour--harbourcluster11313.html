to harbour

verb
keep a feeling or thought, esp. negative, in mind
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
to keep or hold a thought, feeling, or idea in your mind for a long time, often secretly

Examples

to harbour bad feelings
He harboured hopes of reconciliation, despite their long separation.
She has harboured resentment towards her former manager for years.
Try not to harbour negative thoughts about yourself.
For years, he harboured doubts about his decision to move abroad.
It is dangerous to harbour negative thoughts for too long.
She tried not to harbour any resentment towards her former boss.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bear: to carry or hold (a feeling, such as a grudge)
  • synonym – entertain: to consider or allow (an idea or feeling) to remain in the mind
  • antonym – let go: to stop holding onto a feeling or grudge
  • synonym – nurse: to keep or foster a feeling, especially negative, in one's mind
