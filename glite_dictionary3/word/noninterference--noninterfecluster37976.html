noninterference

noun
policy of not getting involved in others' disputes
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːnˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
the principle or policy of not becoming involved in the disputes or affairs of other countries or people

Examples

noninterference policy abroad
He argued that noninterference in the internal affairs of other nations promotes international stability.
Many leaders advocate for noninterference as a key principle of international law.
The country's policy of noninterference has helped maintain peaceful relations with its neighbors.
