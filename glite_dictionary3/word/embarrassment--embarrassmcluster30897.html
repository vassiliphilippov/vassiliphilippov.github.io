embarrassment

noun
event causing embarrassment
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbɛrəsmənt /; / ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsmənt / (BrE)
a feeling or situation of awkwardness, self-consciousness, or shame

Examples

public embarrassment
He tried to laugh off the embarrassment of forgetting her name at the event.
The failed presentation was a major embarrassment for the company.
Winning the award was a delight, but tripping on stage was an unexpected embarrassment.
Despite the embarrassment of forgetting his lines, he continued his speech gracefully.
He felt a surge of embarrassment when his mistake was pointed out in front of the whole class.
Her face turned red with embarrassment after she realized her shirt was inside out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – humiliation: a feeling or event that makes someone feel very ashamed or foolish
  • antonym – pride: a feeling or event that makes someone feel proud, opposite of embarrassment
  • synonym – shame: a painful feeling of humiliation or distress caused by consciousness of wrong or foolish behavior
