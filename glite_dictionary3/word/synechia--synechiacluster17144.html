synechia

noun
adhesion of iris to lens or cornea
Pronunciation: / sɪˈniːkiə / (AmE), / sɪˈniːkɪə / (BrE)
an adhesion of the iris to the lens or cornea of the eye, often resulting from injury, surgery, or disease

Examples

synechia after eye surgery
Following his eye surgery, he developed a synechia that impaired his vision.
Prompt treatment of glaucoma can help prevent complications such as synechia.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed synechia after observing adhesions between the patient's iris and lens.
