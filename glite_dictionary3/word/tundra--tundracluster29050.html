tundra

noun
treeless Arctic plain with frozen subsoil
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌn.drə / (AmE), / ˈtʌn.drə / (BrE)
a vast, flat, treeless region in the Arctic, where the subsoil is permanently frozen and only low vegetation grows

Examples

Arctic tundra landscape
In summer, the snow melts on the tundra, revealing wildflowers and mosses.
Researchers traveled across miles of frozen tundra to collect their data.
The Arctic tundra is home to unique animals adapted to the cold climate.
