to falter

verb
to walk unsteadily; trip repeatedly
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑltər /; / ˈfɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
to move or walk in an unsteady or hesitant way, as if about to fall or lose strength

Examples

falter while walking
Exhausted after the long hike, she began to falter along the rocky path.
He faltered as he tried to make his way through the darkness, nearly losing his balance.
The injured athlete faltered down the corridor, barely able to stay upright.
As the ground shook, the tall stack of books started to falter and sway.
He began to falter as he crossed the rocky path, nearly losing his balance.
The runner seemed to falter at the halfway mark, but she quickly regained her pace.

Related Terms

  • antonym – recover: to regain balance or composure after faltering
  • synonym – stumble: to trip or lose balance while walking; usually used as a synonym
