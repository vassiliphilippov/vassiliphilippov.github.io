abetment

noun
act of encouraging or supporting wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / əˈbɛtmənt / (AmE), / əˈbɛtmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging or supporting someone to do something wrong or illegal; the act of urging someone on

Examples

abetment of a crime
His abetment of the crime made him equally guilty in the eyes of the law.
Laws often punish abetment of an offense as seriously as the offense itself.
The manager was accused of abetment in the financial scandal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deterrence: the act of discouraging an action through fear or doubt
  • synonym – incitement: the act of urging someone to act, especially unlawfully
