bark

noun
a sailing ship with three or more masts
Pronunciation: / bɑrk / (AmE), / bɑːk / (BrE)
a sailing ship with three or more masts, typically with the foremast and mainmast rigged square and the mizzenmast rigged fore-and-aft

Examples

old bark ship
Historians restored the bark to showcase traditional shipbuilding techniques.
The captain commanded a sturdy bark on her maiden voyage across the Atlantic.
The old bark sailed around the world before being retired to a museum.
