aborigine

noun
indigenous person native to a place
Pronunciation: / ˌæbəˈrɪdʒəni / (AmE), / ˌabəˈrɪdʒəni / (BrE)
a member of the original inhabitants of a particular place, especially before colonization or settlement by others

Examples

Australian aborigine
Many aborigines face challenges in preserving their cultural heritage.
The aborigine spoke about his community's history and traditions.
The land was once inhabited by aborigines before foreign settlers arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym – native: a person born in a specified place; indigenous inhabitant
  • antonym – settler: a person who moves to a new place, often displacing indigenous people
