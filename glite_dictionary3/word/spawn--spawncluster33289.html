to spawn

verb
produce or give rise to
Pronunciation: / spɑn / (AmE), / spɔːn / (BrE)
to produce or generate something, especially suddenly or in large numbers; to give rise to.

Examples

to spawn new ideas
Her unique approach spawns innovative solutions to complex problems.
Rumors quickly spawned panic throughout the small town.
The success of the film spawned numerous sequels and spin-offs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – destroy: to put an end to something's existence
  • synonym – generate: to produce or bring into existence
  • synonym – trigger: to cause something to begin or happen
