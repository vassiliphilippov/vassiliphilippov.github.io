excursive

adjective
tending to stray from the main subject
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskɜrsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɜːsɪv / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) tending to stray from the main subject or to move freely over a wide range of topics.

Examples

excursive writing style
Her excursive essays often wander through philosophy, history, and literature in a single page.
The professor’s excursive lecture touched upon numerous subjects unrelated to the course topic.
Their conversation became increasingly excursive, ranging from science to art and back again.

Related Terms

  • synonym – digressive: tending to depart from the main subject
  • synonym – discursive: moving from topic to topic without order
