acromegalia

noun
disorder causing bone enlargement (hands, feet, face)
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.rəʊ.məˈɡeɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌak.rəʊ.mɪˈɡeɪ.li.ə / (BrE)
A disorder characterized by abnormal enlargement of the bones in the hands, feet, and face, caused by excessive growth hormone production, usually due to a pituitary gland tumor.

Early treatment of acromegalia can help prevent permanent bone changes.
Symptoms of acromegalia often include headaches and enlarged hands and feet.
