erethism

noun
abnormally high bodily sensitivity
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrəˌθɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪθɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
An abnormal or excessive degree of irritability or sensitivity to stimulation in an organ or part of the body.

Examples

nervous erethism
Certain neurological disorders may present with erethism as a primary symptom.
Mercury poisoning can cause erethism, with symptoms such as irritability and mood changes.
The patient was diagnosed with erethism after reporting heightened sensitivity to light and sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – irritability: the quality of being easily irritated or excited, especially of a body part
