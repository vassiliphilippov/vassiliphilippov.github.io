Eocene

noun
geological epoch 56–34 million years ago
Pronunciation: / ˈiːoʊˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˈiːəʊsiːn / (BrE)
the geological epoch lasting from about 56 to 34 million years ago, marked by the emergence of many modern mammal families

Examples

the Eocene epoch
During the Eocene, Earth's climate was warmer and tropical forests extended much farther north than today.
Many fossil discoveries from the Eocene provide insight into the evolution of early mammals.
The end of the Eocene saw significant changes in fauna due to climate cooling.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – Paleocene: earlier epoch of the Paleogene Period, before Eocene
