downstream

adjective
in the direction a stream flows
Pronunciation: / ˌdaʊnˈstriːm / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the direction in which a stream or river flows, or situated further along the course of a stream or river from a given point.

Examples

downstream village
Fishermen prefer spots downstream where the water is deeper.
The pollution spreads downstream from the factory, affecting nearby towns.
Wildlife was more abundant in the downstream areas of the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym – upstream: in the opposite direction to a stream's current
