subterfuge

noun
deceptive trick or excuse
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.tɚˌfjuːdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.tə.fjuːdʒ / (BrE)
a trick, excuse, or dishonest plan used to hide the true purpose of something or to achieve an aim by deception

Examples

clever subterfuge
He used a subterfuge to sneak out of the house without his parents noticing.
Her explanation was clearly a subterfuge designed to avoid answering the real question.
The company's claim to be environmentally friendly was just a subterfuge to attract more customers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deception: the act of deceiving someone
  • antonym – honesty: the quality of being truthful and sincere
