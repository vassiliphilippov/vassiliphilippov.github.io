Mustafa Kemal

proper noun
Turkish statesman, founder of modern Turkey
Pronunciation: / muːˈstɑːfə kɛˈmɑːl / (AmE), / mʊˈstɑːfə kɪˈmɑːl / (BrE)
the founder and first President of modern Turkey (1881–1938), who abolished the Ottoman caliphate and established a secular state

Examples

Mustafa Kemal founded Turkey
Mustafa Kemal played a pivotal role in transforming the Ottoman Empire into the Republic of Turkey.
Many historians consider Mustafa Kemal one of the most influential leaders of the twentieth century.
The reforms introduced by Mustafa Kemal had a lasting impact on Turkish society.
