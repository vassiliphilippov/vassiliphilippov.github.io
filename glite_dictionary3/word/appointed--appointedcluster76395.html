appointed

adjective
fixed or set officially
Pronunciation: / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a time, place, etc.) fixed or set officially, especially by an authority or by prior agreement.

Examples

appointed time
Everyone should arrive at the appointed location by noon.
The ceremony will begin at the appointed time regardless of the weather.
We met at the appointed hour to discuss the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym – designated: officially assigned or set for a purpose
  • antonym – random: made, done, or happening without method or conscious decision
  • synonym – scheduled: arranged or planned according to a timetable
