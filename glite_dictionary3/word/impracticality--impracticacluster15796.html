impracticality

noun
unsuitability for practical use
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpræk.tɪˈkælə.ti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpræk.tɪˈkæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unworkable or unsuitable for practical use or application.

Examples

the impracticality of a plan
Despite its beauty, the design was criticized for its impracticality in everyday use.
The impracticality of the proposed solution became evident during the first test.
They abandoned the plan due to its overwhelming impracticality and high costs.
