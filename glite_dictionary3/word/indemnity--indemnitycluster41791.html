indemnity

noun
money paid as compensation for loss or injury
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
A sum of money paid as compensation for loss, damage, or injury, especially under the terms of an insurance policy or legal contract.

Examples

indemnity payment received
She received a large indemnity for the injuries sustained in the accident.
The contract specified an indemnity in case of unforeseen losses.
The insurance company paid an indemnity to cover the damages after the fire.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compensation: something, typically money, awarded to someone as recompense for loss, injury, or suffering
  • synonym – damages: money claimed or awarded in compensation for a loss or injury
  • antonym – penalty: a punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract
