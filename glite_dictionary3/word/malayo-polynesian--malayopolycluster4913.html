Malayo-Polynesian

noun
language branch within Austronesian family
Pronunciation: / məˌleɪoʊˌpɑliˈniʒən / (AmE), / məˌleɪəʊˌpɒlɪˈniːʒən / (BrE)
the branch of the Austronesian language family spoken across a region from Madagascar to the central Pacific, including languages such as Malay, Tagalog, and Maori

Examples

Malayo-Polynesian language family
Madagascar's main language, Malagasy, is part of the Malayo-Polynesian family.
Many languages spoken in Indonesia belong to the Malayo-Polynesian group.
Scholars have traced the migration patterns of Malayo-Polynesian peoples across the Pacific.
