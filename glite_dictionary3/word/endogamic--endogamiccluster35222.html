endogamic

adjective
able to be fertilized by same-species pollen
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.doʊˈɡæ.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈɡæ.mɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) characterized by or suitable for fertilization by pollen from another flower of the same species.

Examples

endogamic plant species
Botanists observed that endogamic species often have mechanisms to encourage intra-species pollination.
Certain plants are endogamic, allowing cross-fertilization within the same species.
The endogamic nature of this flower increases its chances of successful pollination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exogamic: characterized by fertilization involving pollen from a different species
