to manoeuvre

verb
make tactical military or naval movements
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvɚ /; / məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnuːvə / (BrE)
to move or act skillfully or strategically, especially to achieve a particular goal or to guide something carefully

Examples

to manoeuvre a car
The commander ordered his soldiers to manoeuvre into a defensive position at dawn.
The general decided to manoeuvre his troops around the enemy's left flank.
Their fleet manoeuvred skillfully to avoid a direct confrontation.
He is always manoeuvring behind the scenes to influence decisions.
She manoeuvred carefully to secure the job she wanted.
The politician tried to manoeuvre himself into a position of power.
As the crowd grew, he carefully manoeuvred his way to the exit.
She manoeuvred her car into a tight parking space without any trouble.
The captain skilfully manoeuvred the ship through the narrow channel.
