monochromat

noun
person with complete color blindness
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkroʊmæt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrəʊmat / (BrE)
a person who is completely color-blind, able to perceive only shades of one color

Examples

a monochromat sees gray
In studies, a monochromat experiences a world very different from someone with normal vision.
Researchers tested how the monochromat navigated tasks where others relied on color cues.
The monochromat could only distinguish objects based on brightness, not color.
