to fly

verb
be widely or rapidly disseminated
Pronunciation: / flaɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ / (BrE)
to move, change, or spread very quickly (such as time, news, emotions, or people)

Examples

fly by quickly
Accusations can fly when emotions run high in a heated debate.
Rumors about the merger began to fly within hours.
Wild theories flew around the office after the news broke.
As the concert began, the hours seemed to fly.
I can't believe how quickly the weeks fly by during summer vacation.
Time tends to fly when you're having fun.
He tends to fly from happiness to frustration in a matter of seconds.
She can fly into a temper at the slightest provocation.
Sometimes children fly into a rage when they don't get what they want.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dart: to move suddenly and quickly in a particular direction
  • antonym – drag: (of time) to pass very slowly
  • antonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary, opposite of moving quickly away
  • synonym – rush: to move with urgent haste
  • synonym – snap: to suddenly lose self-control, especially by becoming angry
