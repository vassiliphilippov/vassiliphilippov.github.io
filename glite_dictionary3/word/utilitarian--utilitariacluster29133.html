utilitarian

adjective
practical; focused on usefulness
Pronunciation: / juːˌtɪlɪˈtɛriən /; / juˌtɪlɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / juːˌtɪlɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
designed to be useful or practical rather than attractive or comfortable

Examples

utilitarian design
Many modern buildings have a utilitarian appearance, focusing on efficiency over style.
She preferred a utilitarian approach to kitchen design, choosing only what was necessary.
The furniture in the office is strictly utilitarian, lacking any decorative features.
His designs are more utilitarian than decorative, focusing on function above all else.
She chose a utilitarian backpack that prioritized comfort and durability over fashion.
The apartment was furnished in a utilitarian style, with only the essentials for daily living.

  • antonym – aesthetic: concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty
  • synonym – functional: designed to be practical and useful rather than attractive
