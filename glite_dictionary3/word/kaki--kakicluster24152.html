kaki

noun
Asian tree with edible orange fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːki / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Asia, especially Japan and China, bearing large red or orange edible astringent fruit; also called Japanese persimmon.

Examples

ripe kaki fruit
Dried kaki slices are popular snacks in East Asian countries.
He bought a box of ripe kakis from the market to make a traditional dessert.
The kaki tree in their garden was heavy with bright orange fruit in the autumn.
