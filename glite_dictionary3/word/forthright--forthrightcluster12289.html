forthright

adjective
direct and honest in speech or manner
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrθˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθˌraɪt / (BrE)
(of a person, speech, or manner) direct, honest, and outspoken; not attempting to hide anything or avoid the truth.

Examples

forthright answer
He gave a forthright response, leaving no room for misunderstanding.
Her forthright comments during the meeting impressed everyone with their honesty.
It's refreshing to meet someone so forthright about their opinions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blunt: uncompromisingly forthright; direct in speech or manner
  • synonym – candid: truthful and straightforward; frank
  • antonym – evasive: tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly
  • antonym – subtle: delicate or indirect in meaning or intent
