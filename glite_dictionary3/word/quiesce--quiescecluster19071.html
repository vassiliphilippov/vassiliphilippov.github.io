to quiesce

verb
pause operations for maintenance (computing)
Pronunciation: / kwic85bs / (AmE), / kwic85bs / (BrE)
(in computing) to put a computer system, service, or storage into a controlled, temporarily quiet state by pausing or suspending normal operations so that maintenance, backups, or snapshots can be performed safely.

Examples

to quiesce a system
The IT team decided to quiesce the database to perform a safe backup.
Before taking the snapshot, the administrator quiesced all virtual machines to avoid data corruption.
Modern storage systems support APIs that let applications quiesce services during online upgrades.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pause: to stop activity for a short time
  • antonym – resume: to continue an activity after a pause
  • synonym – suspend: to temporarily stop or interrupt an operation or activity
