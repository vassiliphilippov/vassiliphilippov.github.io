to reconstitute

verb
form again; give new structure to
Pronunciation: / riˈkɑn.stəˌtut / (AmE), / riˈkɒn.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
to form or create something again or with a new structure after it has been changed or destroyed

Examples

to reconstitute a group
After the breakup, he worked hard to reconstitute his life and career.
Engineers plan to reconstitute the damaged bridge using stronger materials.
The government decided to reconstitute the advisory board after several members resigned.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dissolve: to formally end or disband an organization
  • synonym – restructure: to give a new structure or organization to something
