rabbet

noun
rectangular groove for joining pieces
Pronunciation: / ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a rectangular groove cut along the edge or end of a piece of wood or other material to receive another piece and form a joint

Examples

rabbet joint edge
She measured the width of the rabbet before attaching the frame.
The carpenter cut a rabbet along the edge of the board to fit the back panel.
To create a strong corner joint, a rabbet is often used in cabinet making.

  • synonym – groove: a long narrow cut or depression in a surface
