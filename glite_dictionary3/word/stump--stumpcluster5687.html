to stump

verb
make political campaign speeches while traveling
Pronunciation: / stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
to travel around a district or region making political speeches, especially as part of a campaign

Examples

to stump for votes
He spent three weeks stumping in small rural towns before the election.
She stumps tirelessly, meeting constituents and delivering her message in every district.
The senator will stump across the Midwest to rally support for her policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – campaign: to work in an organized and active way toward a political goal, often by traveling and making speeches
