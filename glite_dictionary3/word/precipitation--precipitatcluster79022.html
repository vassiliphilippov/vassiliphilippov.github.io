precipitation

noun
unexpected hastening of an event
Pronunciation: / prəˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the sudden acceleration or hastening of an event or process, often unexpectedly

Examples

precipitation of change
Historians argue over the precipitation of revolution by the unpopular new policy.
The CEO's sudden resignation led to the precipitation of sweeping changes within the company.
Their disagreement resulted in the precipitation of a hasty decision.
