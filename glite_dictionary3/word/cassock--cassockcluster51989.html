cassock

noun
long robe worn by clergy
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsək / (AmE), / ˈkæsək / (BrE)
a long, close-fitting, ankle-length garment worn by priests or choristers, usually as part of church dress

Examples

black cassock
He noticed a tear near the hem of his old black cassock.
Many choristers wore a red cassock beneath their white surplices during the festival service.
The priest adjusted his cassock before entering the church for the morning Mass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – surplice: a loose white outer ecclesiastical vestment, worn over a cassock
