to immaterialize

verb
make immaterial or incorporeal
Pronunciation: / ɪməˈtɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪməˈtɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to render immaterial or incorporeal; to cause to lose physical substance or form

Examples

to immaterialize a body
In some theories, consciousness could immaterialize the mind, separating it from the body.
The villain's body began to immaterialize, vanishing into thin air as we watched.
The wizard chanted a spell to immaterialize the locked door before us.

Related Terms

  • antonym – materialize: to become material or take physical form
