workaholism

noun
compulsive excessive working
Pronunciation: / ˌwɝː.kəˈhɑː.lɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌwɜː.kəˈhɒl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The compulsion to work excessively and compulsively, often at the expense of other aspects of life.

Examples

struggle with workaholism
He finally recognized that his workaholism was straining his relationships.
Her workaholism led to stress and burnout despite her many achievements at the office.
Some companies mistakenly value workaholism over work-life balance.
