jackhammer

noun
power tool for breaking hard surfaces
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒækˌhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌhamə / (BrE)
A powerful tool powered by compressed air or electricity, used for breaking up hard surfaces such as concrete or pavement.

Examples

use a jackhammer on concrete
He wore ear protection while operating the jackhammer at the construction site.
Noise from the jackhammer could be heard all the way down the street.
The workers used a jackhammer to break up the old concrete sidewalk.
