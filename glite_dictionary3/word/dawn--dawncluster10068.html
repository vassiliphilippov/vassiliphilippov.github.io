to dawn

verb
become light at daybreak
Pronunciation: / dɔn / (AmE), / dɔːn / (BrE)
(of day) to begin to grow light as the sun rises; to start becoming daytime.

Examples

the sky dawned bright
As the sky began to dawn, birds started singing throughout the forest.
It doesn't dawn until after six in winter here.
They waited patiently for the world to dawn before setting out on their journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym – daybreak: the time in the morning when light first appears
  • antonym – dusk: the period just after sunset when it is becoming dark
