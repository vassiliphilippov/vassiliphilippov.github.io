squeaky

adjective
making a high-pitched sound
Pronunciation: / ˈskwiki / (AmE), / ˈskwiːki / (BrE)
having or making a high-pitched, often shrill sound, like that of a mouse or a hinge

Examples

squeaky voice
She spoke in a squeaky voice that was barely audible above the noise.
The old floorboards were squeaky and creaked with every step.
Their shoes became squeaky after walking in the rain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – raspy: harsh or grating in sound
  • synonym – shrill: having a high-pitched and piercing quality
