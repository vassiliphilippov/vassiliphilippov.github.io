cliff-hanging

adjective
causing suspense or excitement
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪfˌhæŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪfˌhæŋɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a situation or event) causing great suspense or excitement because the outcome is uncertain until the last moment.

Examples

cliff-hanging ending
She enjoys reading cliff-hanging thrillers that keep her guessing until the last page.
The election results were so cliff-hanging that people stayed up all night to hear the outcome.
The final moments of the match were truly cliff-hanging, keeping everyone on the edge of their seats.

Related Terms

  • antonym – predictable: not causing suspense; easily anticipated outcome
  • synonym – suspenseful: causing a feeling of suspense; exciting uncertainty
