endband

noun
a strip or cord reinforcing a book's spine
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈɛndˌbænd / (BrE)
A small cord or strip of material attached near the spine of a book, especially in hardcover bindings, to reinforce the structure and sometimes add decoration.

Examples

endband on a book
A professionally bound volume often includes a tightly sewn endband for added durability.
Repairing the endband took patience, but it restored the book to its former strength.
The old book's endband was intricately decorated with colorful threads.
