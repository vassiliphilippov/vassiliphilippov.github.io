caffein

noun
bitter stimulant found in coffee and tea
Pronunciation: / ˈkæfiˌin / (AmE), / ˈkæfiːn / (BrE)
a bitter substance found in coffee, tea, and some other plants that acts as a stimulant to the central nervous system

Examples

caffein in coffee
Research shows that caffein can affect sleep patterns if consumed late in the day.
Some energy drinks contain high levels of caffein to boost alertness.
The caffein in tea tends to produce a milder effect compared to coffee.
