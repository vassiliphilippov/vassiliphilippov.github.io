catabatic

adjective
downward-moving wind or air current
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈbætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbatɪk / (BrE)
(of a wind or air current) moving downward or down a slope, especially as a result of cooling, usually at night.

Examples

catabatic wind flows
Coastal regions sometimes experience cold catabatic winds after sunset.
Mountain climbers encountered a strong catabatic wind late at night, making their descent more difficult.
The rapid drop in temperature triggered a catabatic flow down the valley.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anabatic: (of a wind or air current) moving upward or up a slope
