MAOI

noun
type of antidepressant drug
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmˌeɪˌoʊˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛmˌeɪˌəʊˈaɪ / (BrE)
any of a group of antidepressant drugs that inhibit the activity of monoamine oxidase enzymes in the brain, thereby increasing the levels of certain neurotransmitters

Examples

take an MAOI medication
After other treatments failed, her doctor prescribed an MAOI to help manage her depression.
Patients taking an MAOI must avoid certain foods due to potential dangerous interactions.
The psychiatrist discussed the benefits and risks of using an MAOI with the patient.
