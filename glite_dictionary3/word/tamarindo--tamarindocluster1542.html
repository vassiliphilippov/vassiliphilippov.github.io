tamarindo

noun
tropical fruit with tangy pulp
Pronunciation: / ˌtæməˈrindoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtaməˈrɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A large tropical pod fruit with a tangy pulp, used in cooking or eaten fresh, especially in Latin American and Caribbean cuisines.

Examples

tamarindo candy flavor
The chef made a refreshing drink from tamarindo and sugar.
The curry gets its sour flavor from the addition of tamarindo paste.
You can find tamarindo candies in many Latin American grocery stores.
