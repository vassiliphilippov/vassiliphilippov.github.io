union

noun
political unit formed by joining
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːniən /; / ˈjun.jən / (AmE), / ˈjuːniən /; / ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
a political unit formed by joining together countries, states, or organizations; or a symbol of such a union on a flag

Examples

union of countries
After years of negotiation, several small countries formed a lasting union.
The European Union is a prominent example of a modern political union.
The United States was originally a union of thirteen colonies.
Many flags display a union to signify the coming together of different states or regions.
On the British flag, the union represents the historic merging of England and Scotland.
The blue field in the upper corner of the United States flag is called the union.
