fix

noun
illegal or unfair exemption by influence
Pronunciation: / fɪks / (AmE), / fɪks / (BrE)
an illegal or unfair arrangement by which someone receives special treatment or exemption, usually as a result of bribery or corruption

Examples

a fix for a ticket
He tried to get a fix for his speeding ticket by calling in a favor.
Rumors spread about a fix giving the company an unfair tax break.
The senator was accused of arranging a fix to avoid prosecution.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corruption: dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power
  • synonym – favor: an act of kindness or preferential treatment
  • antonym – punishment: the act of punishing someone for an offense
