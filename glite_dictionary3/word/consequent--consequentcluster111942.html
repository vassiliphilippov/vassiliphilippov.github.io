consequent

adjective
resulting from something
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnsɪkwənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪkwənt / (BrE)
Happening as a result or effect of something else.

Examples

consequent effect
His error led to a consequent loss of confidence among the staff.
She failed the test, with consequent disappointment among her friends.
There was an earthquake and a consequent disruption of transport services.

Related Terms

  • antonym – causal: relating to or acting as a cause; not resultant, but connected by cause-and-effect
  • synonym – ensuing: happening immediately after something as a result
  • synonym – resultant: resulting from something; produced as a result
