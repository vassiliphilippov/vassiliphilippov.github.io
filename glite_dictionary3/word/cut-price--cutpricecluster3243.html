cut-price

adjective
selling at a reduced price
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌpraɪs / (BrE)
(of goods or services) sold at a price lower than the usual or standard cost.

Examples

cut-price ticket
She bought a cut-price ticket to the concert online.
Supermarkets often offer cut-price deals on groceries near their expiration date.
The store is famous for its cut-price electronics during holiday sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discounted: sold at a reduced price
  • antonym – full-price: sold at the usual or original price
