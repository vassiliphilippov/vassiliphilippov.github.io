telomere

noun
protective end of a chromosome
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛl.əˌmɪr / (AmE), / ˈtiː.ləˌmɪə / (BrE)
the region at each end of a eukaryotic chromosome that protects it from deterioration or fusion with neighboring chromosomes

Examples

chromosome telomere
Every time a cell divides, its telomeres become slightly shorter.
Researchers have discovered that shorter telomeres are associated with aging and increased risk of disease.
Scientists are studying ways to slow the shortening of telomeres to delay the aging process.
