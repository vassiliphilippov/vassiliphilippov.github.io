disinclined

adjective
unwilling; reluctant
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪnd / (BrE)
Unwilling or reluctant to do something, often because of mild dislike or disapproval.

Examples

to feel disinclined to help
After a long day's work, he was disinclined to go out again.
Many voters are disinclined to support changes they do not fully understand.
She seemed disinclined to accept his invitation to dinner.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inclined: having a tendency or preference for something
  • synonym – reluctant: unwilling and hesitant; not wanting to do something
