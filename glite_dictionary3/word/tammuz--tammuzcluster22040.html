Tammuz

proper noun
Sumerian-Babylonian god of vegetation
Pronunciation: / tɑːˈmuːz / (AmE), / ˈtæmʊz / (BrE)
An ancient Sumerian and Babylonian god of pastures and vegetation, who was the consort of Inanna.

Examples

god Tammuz worship
Many ancient rituals in Mesopotamia honored Tammuz as the god who died and was reborn each year.
Some stories describe Tammuz as the husband of the goddess Inanna.
The annual mourning for Tammuz reflected the cycle of life, death, and renewal in nature.
