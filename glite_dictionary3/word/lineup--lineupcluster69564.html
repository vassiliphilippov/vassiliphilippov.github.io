lineup

noun
police identification procedure
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪ.nʌp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.nʌp / (BrE)
a line of people arranged by police for inspection or identification, especially to help witnesses identify a suspect

Examples

police lineup identification
After the robbery, the police conducted a lineup to help find the perpetrator.
Five men stood in the lineup, waiting to be identified by the victim.
The witness picked the suspect out of the lineup at the police station.
