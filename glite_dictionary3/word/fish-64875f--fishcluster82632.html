fish

noun
cold-blooded aquatic vertebrate
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ / (AmE), / fɪʃ / (BrE)
A cold-blooded aquatic animal that has a backbone, usually breathes through gills, and typically has fins and scales.

Examples

tropical fish
She keeps a tank of tropical fish in her living room.
The river is popular with anglers hoping to catch big fish.
We saw a school of brightly colored fish swimming near the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mammal: a warm-blooded vertebrate animal distinguished by the possession of hair or fur, mammary glands
  • synonym – shark: a large predatory fish with a cartilaginous skeleton
