tightness

noun
lack of space or looseness
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having little space for movement or being held firmly in place; being crowded or packed closely together

Examples

tightness of space
Due to the tightness of the schedule, there was no time for a break.
She felt a tightness in her chest after running up the stairs.
The tightness of the lid made it hard to open the jar.
Due to the tightness of the seating arrangement, it was difficult to move around.
The tightness in the small elevator made everyone uncomfortable.
The tightness of the crowd at the concert created an intense atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compression: the act of pressing something tightly together
  • synonym – crowdedness: the state of being full of people or things
  • antonym – looseness: the quality of not being tight; more room for movement
  • antonym – roominess: the quality of having ample space
