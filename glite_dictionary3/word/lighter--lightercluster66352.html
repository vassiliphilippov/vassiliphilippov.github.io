to lighter

verb
transport goods in a flat-bottomed boat
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə / (BrE)
(of a ship or cargo) to transport or transfer goods in a flat-bottomed boat, especially from a ship to the shore or to another vessel.

Examples

to lighter goods ashore
Due to the shallow harbor, containers were lightered ashore by small boats.
Special vessels are designed to lighter oil from tankers at sea.
The crew used a barge to lighter cargo from the anchored ship to the quay.
