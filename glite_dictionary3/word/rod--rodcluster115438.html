rod

noun
unit of length equal to 16.5 feet
Pronunciation: / rɑd / (AmE), / rɒd / (BrE)
a former unit of length equal to 16.5 feet (approximately 5.03 meters), used especially in surveying land.

Examples

measure a rod length
A fence post was placed every rod along the property line.
Maps from the 1800s sometimes use the rod as a unit of distance.
The land was measured as ten rods wide by twenty rods long.
