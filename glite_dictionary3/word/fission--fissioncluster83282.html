fission

noun
cell division; reproduction by splitting
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
Reproduction in which a single-celled organism divides into two roughly equal and independent cells.

Examples

cell fission process
During fission, the parent cell splits in half to form two new cells.
Many bacteria reproduce rapidly through fission, allowing colonies to expand quickly.
Unicellular organisms like amoebas multiply by binary fission.

Related Terms

  • antonym – budding: form of asexual reproduction where new organism grows from body of parent
  • synonym – mitosis: cell division resulting in two genetically identical nuclei
