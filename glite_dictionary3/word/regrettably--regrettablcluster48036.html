regrettably

adverb
unfortunately; regrettably
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛtəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈɡretəbli / (BrE)
Used to express that something is unfortunate or to be regretted.

Examples

regrettably missed the chance
Regrettably, she missed the last train and had to spend the night at the station.
Regrettably, we will have to cancel the outdoor concert due to heavy rain.
He explained, regrettably, that no more tickets were available for the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fortunately: used to express that something is lucky or fortunate
  • synonym – unfortunately: used to express that something is regrettable or unlucky
