to demoralise

verb
to lower someone's confidence or morale
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɔːr.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make someone lose confidence, hope, or spirit; to discourage or lower someone's morale

Examples

to demoralise a team
Hearing about the project's cancellation can really demoralise those who worked hard.
The constant criticism began to demoralise the entire team.
The soldiers were demoralised by the series of defeats.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discourage: to make someone less confident or hopeful
  • antonym – encourage: to give someone confidence or hope
