to calm

verb
make quiet or less disturbed
Pronunciation: / kɑm / (AmE), / kɑːm / (BrE)
to make or become peaceful, quiet, or less agitated

Examples

to calm nerves
He closed his eyes and took deep breaths to calm his nerves before speaking.
She tried to calm the frightened child before the storm started.
The teacher managed to calm the class after the fire alarm went off.
After a heated argument, it took him several minutes to calm down.
She tried to calm her nerves before giving the speech.
The crowd began to calm once the announcement was made.
Strong medication was used to calm the agitated child in the emergency room.
The doctor had to calm her with a mild sedative so she could sleep.
The nurse tried to calm the anxious patient before the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agitate: to make someone troubled or nervous
  • synonym – sedate: to calm or quiet with a sedative
  • synonym – settle down: to become quieter or less active
  • synonym – soothe: to gently calm or relieve anxiety or pain
