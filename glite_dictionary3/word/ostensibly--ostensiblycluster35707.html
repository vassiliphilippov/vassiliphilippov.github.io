ostensibly

adverb
apparently, but perhaps not really
Pronunciation: / əˈstɛn.sə.bli / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛn.sɪ.bli / (BrE)
as something appears or is claimed to be, but possibly not actually so

Examples

ostensibly supports the idea
He ostensibly agreed with the plan, though he opposed it in private.
The company was ostensibly founded to improve education, but its real purpose was profit.
The trip was ostensibly for research, but they spent most of their time sightseeing.

Related Terms

  • antonym – actually: in fact; really
  • synonym – apparently: as it appears or seems; seemingly
  • synonym – seemingly: appearing to be something, especially when that is not actually the case
