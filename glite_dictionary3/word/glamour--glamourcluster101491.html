glamour

noun
alluring beauty or charm
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmə / (BrE)
An attractive or exciting quality that makes someone or something seem appealing, often in a romantic or showy way; alluring beauty or charm.

Examples

Hollywood glamour
Despite her job's apparent glamour, the work was actually exhausting and routine.
Many people are drawn to the glamour of city life, dreaming of fame and excitement.
The actress's glamour captivated audiences everywhere she went.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allure: the quality of being powerfully attractive or charming
  • antonym – plainness: the quality of not being attractive or glamorous
