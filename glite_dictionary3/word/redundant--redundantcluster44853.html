redundant

adjective
not needed; more than necessary
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌndənt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndənt / (BrE)
Not needed or no longer useful because there is more than is necessary.

Examples

redundant staff
After the company installed new software, many clerical jobs became redundant.
Having three different locks on the same door seems a bit redundant.
The last paragraph in your essay is redundant and could be removed to improve clarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – essential: absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • synonym – superfluous: unnecessary, especially through being more than enough
