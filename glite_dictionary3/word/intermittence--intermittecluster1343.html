intermittence

noun
the state of being intermittent
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚˈmɪt.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈmɪt.əns / (BrE)
The quality of being intermittent; the condition of occurring at irregular intervals or being repeatedly interrupted.

Examples

rain intermittence
Only the intermittence of the rain allowed us to complete the outdoor event in fits and starts.
She was frustrated by the intermittence of his attention during their conversation.
The intermittence of the signal made it difficult to maintain a stable connection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – continuity: the state of being continuous, without interruption
  • synonym – intermittency: the fact or state of being intermittent
