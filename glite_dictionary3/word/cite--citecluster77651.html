cite

noun
a short note recognizing a source
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
a brief note acknowledging the source of information or a quoted passage, especially in academic or scholarly works

Examples

add a cite note
Every reference in her thesis included a proper cite to the original author.
He forgot to add a cite for the data he used in his report.
The editor asked the writer to provide a cite for the historical claim.

Related Terms

  • synonym – citation: a formal reference to a published or unpublished source
