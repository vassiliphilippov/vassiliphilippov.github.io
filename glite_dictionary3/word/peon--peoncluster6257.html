peon

noun
person who does menial work
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːɑn / (AmE), / ˈpiːɒn / (BrE)
A person who does menial or unskilled work, especially someone of low social or employment status.

Examples

a peon at work
During the colonial era, many peons worked on plantations under harsh conditions.
She felt like a peon after being assigned all the tedious tasks in the office.
The factory relies heavily on the labor of peons for its daily operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – laborer: a person doing unskilled manual work
  • synonym – servant: a person employed to perform duties for others, especially a household worker
