prelim

noun
graduate school examination
Pronunciation: / ˈpriː.lɪm / (AmE), / ˈpriː.lɪm / (BrE)
an examination taken by graduate students, usually early in their program, to assess their suitability to continue their studies

Examples

pass a prelim exam
All PhD candidates must pass their prelim before starting their dissertation research.
Failure to pass the prelim can result in dismissal from the graduate program.
She studied for months to ensure she would do well on her prelim.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preliminary examination: an examination taken as a prerequisite to continue graduate studies
