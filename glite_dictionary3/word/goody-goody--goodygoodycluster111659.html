goody-goody

noun
overly virtuous or well-behaved person
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʊdi ˌɡʊdi / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdi ˌɡʊdi / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an overly virtuous or well-behaved manner, especially to gain favor with authority figures

Examples

the class goody-goody
Don't be such a goody-goody; it's okay to have a little fun sometimes.
He was teased for being a goody-goody who never broke the rules.
Nobody liked the new student because she acted like a goody-goody in every class.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brown-noser: someone who flatters authority to gain favor
  • synonym – teacher's pet: a student who seeks favor by being obedient or helpful to the teacher
