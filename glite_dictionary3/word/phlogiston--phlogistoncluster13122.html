phlogiston

noun
hypothetical substance in obsolete combustion theory
Pronunciation: / floʊˈdʒɪstən / (AmE), / fləˈdʒɪstən / (BrE)
A hypothetical substance once thought to exist in all combustible materials and released during burning, according to an obsolete scientific theory.

Examples

phlogiston theory debate
In the eighteenth century, chemists debated the existence of phlogiston as part of the combustion process.
Lavoisier disproved the theory of phlogiston, revolutionizing the science of chemistry.
The idea that phlogiston was released during burning was ultimately replaced by the discovery of oxygen.
