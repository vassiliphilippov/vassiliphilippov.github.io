bossy

adjective
domineering; fond of ordering others
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːsi / (AmE), / ˈbɒsi / (BrE)
Fond of giving people orders and tending to tell others what to do, often in a domineering or unpleasant way.

Examples

a bossy manager
He was becoming increasingly bossy at work, always telling others what to do.
Her bossy attitude made it difficult for her colleagues to work with her.
The older sibling can sometimes be bossy and insist on getting their own way.

Related Terms

  • antonym – authoritative: able to be trusted as accurate or true; considered to be in control
  • synonym – domineering: asserting one's will over another in an arrogant way
  • antonym – submissive: ready to conform to the authority or will of others
