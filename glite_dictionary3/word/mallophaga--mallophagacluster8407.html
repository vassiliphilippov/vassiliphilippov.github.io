Mallophaga

noun
biting lice (parasitic insects)
Pronunciation: / ˌmæləˈfeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈfəːɡə / (BrE)
A former order of parasitic insects, commonly known as biting lice, that live on birds and some mammals, feeding on skin, feathers, or hair.

Examples

Mallophaga on birds
Researchers found several species of Mallophaga infesting the feathers of migratory birds.
The host-specific nature of Mallophaga makes them useful for studying parasite-host relationships.
Veterinarians sometimes identify Mallophaga during routine examinations of pet birds.
