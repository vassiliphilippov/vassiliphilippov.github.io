spawn

noun
eggs of fish, amphibians, or molluscs
Pronunciation: / spɑn / (AmE), / spɔːn / (BrE)
the mass of eggs laid by fish, amphibians, or molluscs, often in water

Examples

fish spawn in rivers
Each spring, the river is filled with the spawn of salmon returning to their birthplace.
Scientists collected samples of frog spawn from the pond for their research.
The water's surface shimmered with the fresh spawn laid by the molluscs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – roe: eggs of fish, especially when used as food
