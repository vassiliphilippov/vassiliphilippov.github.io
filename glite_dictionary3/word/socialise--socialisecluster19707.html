to socialise

verb
prepare for social life; teach social norms
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to train or prepare someone (such as a child or animal) to behave in a way that is acceptable in society

Examples

to socialise a child
Parents play a crucial role in helping their children socialise from an early age.
Schools provide opportunities for students to socialise and learn accepted social norms.
Some animals, like wolves, need to socialise with their pack to develop normal behavior.
It is important to socialise children so they learn accepted manners and values.
Teachers play a key role in helping students socialise properly within the classroom.
The puppy was socialised with other dogs from a young age to prevent aggression.

Related Terms

  • synonym – civilize: to bring out of a savage or uneducated state; to improve socially
  • synonym – educate: to give intellectual, moral, and social instruction to someone
  • synonym – indoctrinate: to teach someone to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • antonym – isolate: to cause (a person or place) to be or remain alone or apart from others
  • antonym – ostracize: to exclude from a society or group
