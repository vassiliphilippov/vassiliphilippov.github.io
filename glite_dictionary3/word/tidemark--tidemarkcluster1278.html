tidemark

noun
line marking tide's highest or lowest reach
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪdˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdmɑːk / (BrE)
a visible line left on the shore or an object marking the highest or lowest point reached by the tide

Examples

tidemark on the wall
A faint brown tidemark indicated how high the water had risen during the storm surge.
At low tide, you could see the tidemark clearly on the rocks.
The boat was stained below the tidemark after weeks in the harbor.
