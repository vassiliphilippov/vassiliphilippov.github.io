forbearing

adjective
patient and restrained under adversity
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈbɛərɪŋ / (BrE)
showing patient self-control and restraint, especially under adversity; slow to express anger or resentment

Examples

forbearing attitude
Even when criticized unfairly, she responded with forbearing calm.
His forbearing attitude helped ease tensions during the stressful meeting.
She remained forbearing throughout the long argument, never raising her voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – irritable: easily annoyed or angered
  • synonym – patient: able to accept or tolerate delays, problems, or suffering without becoming annoyed or anxious
