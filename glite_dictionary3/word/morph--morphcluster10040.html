to morph

verb
change shape (esp. in computer animation)
Pronunciation: / mɔrf / (AmE), / mɔːf / (BrE)
to change or transform smoothly from one form, shape, or character to another, especially using computer animation

Examples

to morph into a new shape
In the film, one character morphs into another right before our eyes.
The designer used software to morph the company logo into a variety of shapes.
We watched as the animation slowly morphed a cat into a lion.
Advanced software can morph two faces into one seamless image.
During the music video, the lead singer morphed into an eagle and flew away.
The character was designed to morph into different creatures throughout the movie.
Her mood seemed to morph with the weather.
Over the years, his hobby began to morph into a successful business.
The caterpillar will eventually morph into a butterfly.
