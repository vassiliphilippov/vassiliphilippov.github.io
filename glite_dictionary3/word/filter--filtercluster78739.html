filter

noun
electrical device for frequency selection
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.tər / (BrE)
an electrical device that selectively allows certain frequencies to pass while attenuating others from a signal

Examples

audio filter circuit
A high-pass filter blocks signals below a certain frequency, allowing only higher frequencies through.
Modern radios often use a digital filter to improve sound clarity.
The audio engineer installed a filter to remove low-frequency noise from the recording.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bandpass filter: a filter that allows only a specific range of frequencies to pass
