ripping

noun
copying data from a disc to digital files
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing) The process of copying audio or other data from an optical disc (such as a CD or DVD) to a computer or other digital storage, producing digital files.

Examples

ripping a CD
The ripping of his CD collection to MP3 took all afternoon.
She praised the software's fast ripping and accurate metadata tagging for albums.
After buying a used DVD, he asked about the legality of ripping movies for personal use.

Related Terms

  • antonym – burn: to write data onto a CD or DVD (opposite process of copying from a disc)
  • synonym – rip: to copy audio or data from a disc to a digital file (verb)
