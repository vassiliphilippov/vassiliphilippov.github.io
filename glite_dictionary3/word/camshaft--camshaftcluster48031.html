camshaft

noun
shaft operating valves in engines
Pronunciation: / ˈkæmˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈkamʃɑːft / (BrE)
A shaft in an engine to which cams are attached, used to operate the intake and exhaust valves.

Examples

camshaft controls valves
Proper timing of the camshaft is essential for efficient engine operation.
The camshaft controls the opening and closing of the engine's valves.
The mechanic replaced the broken camshaft to restore the engine's performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crankshaft: shaft in an engine that translates piston motion to rotation
