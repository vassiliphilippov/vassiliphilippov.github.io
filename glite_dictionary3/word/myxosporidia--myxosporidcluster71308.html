Myxosporidia

noun
order of aquatic spore-forming parasites
Pronunciation: / mɪkˌsoʊspəˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / mɪksəʊspɒˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
An order of microscopic, spore-forming parasites (protozoans) that infect aquatic animals, especially fish, and are characterized by complex spores with infective sporoplasms and polar capsules.

Examples

Myxosporidia infect fish
In aquaculture, outbreaks caused by Myxosporidia can result in significant losses of farmed fish.
Scientists studying fish diseases often encounter parasites classified within the Myxosporidia.
The life cycle of Myxosporidia is complex and involves both vertebrate and invertebrate hosts.
