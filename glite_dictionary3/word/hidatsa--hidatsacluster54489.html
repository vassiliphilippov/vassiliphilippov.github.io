Hidatsa

noun
a member of the Hidatsa Indigenous people
Pronunciation: / hɪˈdætsə / (AmE), / hɪˈdætsə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people of the Sioux group, formerly living along the Missouri River in western North Dakota

Examples

Hidatsa tribe member
A Hidatsa elder told stories of their people's ancestors and migration.
She is researching the history and traditions of the Hidatsa.
The Hidatsa were skilled farmers as well as hunters along the Missouri River.
