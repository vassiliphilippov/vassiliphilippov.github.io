lysogenisation

noun
integration of phage DNA into a bacterium
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪsəˌdʒɛnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsɒdʒɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a bacterium acquires a phage that integrates its genetic material into the bacterial genome, resulting in a lysogenic state

Examples

lysogenisation of bacteria
Following lysogenisation, the bacterium can reproduce normally while carrying the integrated viral genome.
Researchers measured the rate of lysogenisation among different strains of E. coli in the experiment.
The process of lysogenisation allows certain viruses to persist harmlessly within bacterial cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lysis: the disintegration of a cell by rupture of its wall or membrane
  • synonym – lysogeny: state of a bacterium containing an integrated phage genome
