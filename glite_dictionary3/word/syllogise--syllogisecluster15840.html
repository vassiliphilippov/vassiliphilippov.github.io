to syllogise

verb
reason by syllogisms
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪləˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to reason or argue using syllogisms (a form of logical reasoning where a conclusion is drawn from two given or assumed propositions)

Examples

to syllogise logically
It is not always effective to syllogise about real-world situations that involve uncertainty.
Students are trained to syllogise when studying classical logic in university.
The philosopher would often syllogise to demonstrate the logical structure of his arguments.
