Frankenstein

noun
a creation that harms its creator
Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkənˌstaɪn / (BrE)
A creation or agency that escapes the control of its creator and causes harm to them.

Examples

a Frankenstein invention
His once-promising invention turned into a Frankenstein that ruined his career.
Some fear that artificial intelligence could become a modern Frankenstein if left unchecked.
The new technology became a Frankenstein, spiraling out of control and threatening its inventors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – monster: a large, frightening imaginary creature; used figuratively for anything powerful and uncontrollable
