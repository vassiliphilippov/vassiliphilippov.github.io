backslider

noun
person who relapses into bad habits
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌslaɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌslaɪdə / (BrE)
a person who relapses into bad habits or undesirable ways of behaving, especially after a period of improvement or reform

Examples

a backslider returns
After a few months of sobriety, he became a backslider and started drinking again.
She promised not to be a backslider this time after overcoming her old habits.
The church offered support to help backsliders return to the faith.

Related Terms

  • synonym – recidivist: a person who relapses into criminal or antisocial behavior
  • synonym – sinner: a person who transgresses religious or moral law
