oligodendria

noun
glial tissue forming myelin in CNS
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊlɪɡoʊˈdɛndriə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəʊˈdɛndriə / (BrE)
Tissue composed of glial cells whose sheetlike extensions form the myelin sheath around nerve fibers in the central nervous system.

Examples

oligodendria in brain tissue
Damage to the oligodendria can result in the loss of myelin, affecting nerve transmission.
Multiple sclerosis is characterized by the destruction of oligodendria and their myelin sheaths.
Scientists are studying how oligodendria regenerate after central nervous system injuries.
