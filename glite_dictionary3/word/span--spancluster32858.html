span

noun
section of a bridge or structure crossing an obstacle
Pronunciation: / spæn / (AmE), / spæn / (BrE)
the distance, period, or part that stretches between two points in space or time (such as a bridge span, a distance, or a period of time)

Examples

a span of bridge
A new span was added to accommodate increased traffic over the highway.
Engineers measured the length of the span crossing the river.
The central span of the bridge was lifted for repairs last month.
The bridge has a central span of 200 meters.
The falcon's wings had an impressive span when fully extended.
Within the short span of an hour, everything changed.
A human life span can vary greatly depending on circumstances.
Over the span of his career, he won several awards.
The project was completed within a span of two weeks.
