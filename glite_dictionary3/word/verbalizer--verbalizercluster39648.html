verbalizer

noun
person who expresses in words or speeches
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝː.bə.laɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.bə.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who expresses ideas or information in words, especially one who delivers speeches or is notably talkative

Examples

gifted verbalizer
At the conference, the keynote verbalizer captivated the audience with her eloquence.
He was always the verbalizer in group discussions, articulating everyone's thoughts.
The company hired a skilled verbalizer to explain their new product to the public.

Related Terms

  • antonym – listener: a person who listens, rather than speaks
  • synonym – orator: a person who gives speeches, especially formal or public ones
  • synonym – speaker: a person who speaks, especially in public
