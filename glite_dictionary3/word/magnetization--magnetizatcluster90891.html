magnetization

noun
the process of becoming magnetic
Pronunciation: / ˌmæɡnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɡnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process by which a material becomes magnetic, either temporarily or permanently

Examples

process of magnetization
After exposure to a magnetic field, the rod showed a clear increase in magnetization.
Demagnetizing a material involves reversing its magnetization process.
The magnetization of iron can be achieved by rubbing it with a strong magnet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – magnetism: the physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces between objects
