doily

noun
small ornamental mat for dishes or bowls
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɪli / (AmE), / ˈdɔɪli / (BrE)
A small ornamental mat, typically round and made of lace or linen, placed under a dish, bowl, or other object to protect the surface or for decoration.

Examples

doily under a plate
Every plate at the tea party rested on a dainty white doily.
She placed a doily under the teapot to protect the wooden table.
The antique cake stand was decorated with a delicate lace doily.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mat: a piece of material placed on a surface to protect it or for decoration
