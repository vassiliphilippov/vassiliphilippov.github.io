Jean Paul Marat

proper noun
French revolutionary leader (1743–1793)
Pronunciation: / ʒɑ̃ pɔl məˈrɑ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ː pɔːl məˈra / (BrE)
(1743–1793) A French revolutionary leader, noted for his role in overthrowing the Girondists; assassinated in his bath by Charlotte Corday.

Examples

Jean Paul Marat led revolution
Jean Paul Marat was a central figure in the radical phase of the French Revolution.
Charlotte Corday famously assassinated Jean Paul Marat while he was taking a medicinal bath.
Historians debate the legacy and influence of Jean Paul Marat on revolutionary politics.
