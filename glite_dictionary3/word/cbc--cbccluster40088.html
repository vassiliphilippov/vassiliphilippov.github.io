CBC

noun
blood test measuring cell counts
Pronunciation: / si bi si / (AmE), / siː biː siː / (BrE)
a blood test that counts the number of white and red blood cells and platelets in a given volume of blood

Examples

CBC blood test
A CBC is often part of a routine health examination.
The doctor ordered a CBC to check for signs of infection.
Your CBC results indicate that your platelet count is within the normal range.

Related Terms

  • synonym – full blood count: blood test measuring cell components (primarily in British usage)
