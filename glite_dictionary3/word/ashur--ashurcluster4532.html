Ashur

proper noun
chief god of the Assyrians
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈɑːʃʊə / (BrE)
the chief god of the Assyrians, associated with military power and empire, and identified with the Babylonian god Anshar

Examples

Ashur god of Assyria
Reliefs from Nineveh often depict Ashur as a powerful figure guiding the Assyrian kings.
Scholars believe that the worship of Ashur played a crucial role in unifying the Assyrian Empire.
The ancient Assyrians built temples to honor Ashur, their chief deity.
