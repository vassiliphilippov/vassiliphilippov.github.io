immateriality

noun
state of being irrelevant or unimportant
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmæt̬.ɪ.ɚˈæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˌmæt.ɪəˈræl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being irrelevant or having no importance to the matter at hand; complete irrelevance requiring no further consideration

Examples

immateriality of evidence
During the trial, the judge ruled that the witness's statement was of immateriality to the case.
His objections were dismissed due to their clear immateriality in the discussion.
The defense lawyer argued that the evidence lacked relevance and was therefore of total immateriality.
