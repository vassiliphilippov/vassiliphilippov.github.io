thermolabile

adjective
easily destroyed by heat
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜr.moʊˈleɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məˈleɪ.baɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance) easily altered, decomposed, or destroyed by heat, especially in chemical, physical, or biological contexts.

Examples

thermolabile enzyme
A thermolabile compound must be stored at low temperatures to maintain its effectiveness.
Many vitamins are thermolabile, which means they can be destroyed during cooking.
This enzyme is thermolabile and loses its activity quickly at temperatures above 40°C.

Related Terms

  • antonym – thermostable: not easily destroyed or changed by heat
