preventiveness

noun
quality of preventing something
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvɛntɪvnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or capacity of preventing, hindering, or acting as an obstacle to something.

Examples

preventiveness of early action
Recent studies emphasize the preventiveness of early screenings in reducing disease rates.
She questioned the preventiveness of the new policy measures against cyber attacks.
The preventiveness of safety regulations has saved countless lives over the years.
