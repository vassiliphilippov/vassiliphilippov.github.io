host

noun
person introducing speakers at an event
Pronunciation: / hoʊst / (AmE), / həʊst / (BrE)
a person who receives or entertains guests at an event, at home, or as an innkeeper

Examples

host a dinner party
As the host, she made sure the event ran smoothly from start to finish.
The host welcomed everyone and introduced the keynote speaker.
The awards ceremony began with the host thanking the sponsors and judges.
In the novel, the host offered rooms to weary pilgrims each night.
The host made sure the fire was always burning in the main hall.
The old host greeted travelers warmly as they entered the bustling inn.
As the host of the dinner, he introduced all the guests to each other.
It is the responsibility of the host to ensure there is enough food and drink for everyone.
She was an excellent host and made everyone feel welcome at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym – emcee: a person who acts as master of ceremonies
  • antonym – guest: a person invited to attend an event
  • synonym – hostess: a woman who invites and entertains guests
  • synonym – innkeeper: the landlord or manager of an inn
