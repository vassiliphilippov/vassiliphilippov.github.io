exogamic

adjective
relating to marrying outside a social group
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.səˈɡæm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səˈɡæm.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by the custom of marrying only outside a specific clan, tribe, or social group.

Examples

exogamic marriage rule
Among some cultures, exogamic marriages are essential for forming alliances with neighboring communities.
The tribe followed an exogamic tradition, requiring its members to marry outside their own group.
Their exogamic customs helped prevent inbreeding and encouraged social cohesion.
