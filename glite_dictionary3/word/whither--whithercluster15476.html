whither

adverb
to what place; to where (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪðər / (AmE), / ˈwɪðə / (BrE)
(archaic) To what place or to which place; to where something leads or goes.

Examples

whither goest thou

She asked herself whither this path might ultimately lead.
The poet mused on whither the winds would carry his message.
