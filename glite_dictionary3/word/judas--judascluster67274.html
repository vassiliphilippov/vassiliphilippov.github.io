Judas

noun
traitor under the guise of friendship
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːdəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəs / (BrE)
a person who betrays a friend or companion, especially under the appearance of loyalty or friendship

Examples

call him a Judas
Calling him a Judas, the team never trusted him again after the betrayal.
He was branded a Judas after revealing confidential information to the rival company.
She felt like a Judas when she broke her promise to her closest friend.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loyalist: a person who remains loyal to a cause or person
  • synonym – traitor: a person who betrays someone or something, such as a friend, cause, or principle
