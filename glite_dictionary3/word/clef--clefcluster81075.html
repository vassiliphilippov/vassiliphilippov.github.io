clef

noun
symbol showing note pitch on a music staff
Pronunciation: / klɛf / (AmE), / klɛf / (BrE)
A symbol at the beginning of a musical staff that indicates the pitch of the notes to be played or sung.

Examples

clef symbol on staff
Every staff must start with a clef to show the pitch of the notes.
Please make sure you read the music in the correct clef or you may play the wrong notes.
The violin music begins with a clef symbol at the start of each line.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – bass clef: a clef placing F below middle C on the second-highest line of the staff
  • synonym – staff: a set of five horizontal lines and four spaces that represent different musical pitches
  • degree higher – treble clef: a clef placing G above middle C on the second-lowest line of the staff
