NMR

noun
nuclear magnetic resonance technique
Pronunciation: / ɛn ˌɛm ˈɑr / (AmE), / ɛn ˌɛm ˈɑː / (BrE)
a technique that exploits the magnetic properties of certain atomic nuclei to determine the structure of molecules, often used in chemistry and medical imaging

A doctor may order an NMR scan to obtain detailed images of body tissues.
Advances in NMR have revolutionized modern spectroscopy.
