four-hitter

noun
baseball game with only four hits allowed by pitcher
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr ˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher allows the opposing team only four hits.

Examples

The rookie pitcher threw a brilliant four-hitter against the league's top team.
Yesterday's four-hitter secured his spot in the starting rotation.
