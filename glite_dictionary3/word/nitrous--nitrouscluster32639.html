nitrous

noun
nitrous oxide for engine boost
Pronunciation: / c8na6atr59s / (AmE), / c8n6atr59s / (BrE)
(informal) nitrous oxide (NOS), a gas injected into an internal-combustion engine to produce a temporary increase in power.

Examples

nitrous boost
The race car's driver activated the nitrous for a sudden burst of speed down the straight.
They filled the toolbox with small tanks of nitrous before the weekend's tuning session.
Mechanics warned that using too much nitrous could seriously damage the engine's pistons.

Related Terms

  • synonym – NOS: abbreviation for nitrous oxide system used to increase engine power
  • synonym – nitrous oxide: a chemical compound (N2O) used medically as an anesthetic and in engines to boost power
