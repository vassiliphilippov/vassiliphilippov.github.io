nonverbal

adjective
not using words or language
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
communicated or expressed without spoken or written language, using gestures, facial expressions, or other non-word cues

Examples

nonverbal communication
Her nonverbal cues made it clear she was upset.
Much communication between people is actually nonverbal in nature.
The psychologist administered a nonverbal reasoning test to the child.
Children often express their feelings through nonverbal communication, such as smiling or crying.
She relied on nonverbal cues like eye contact and gestures during the interview.
The teacher noticed the nonverbal signals of confusion among her students.
