foolproof

adjective
incapable of failure or misuse
Pronunciation: / ˈfuːlˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈfuːlˌpruːf / (BrE)
(of a design, method, or plan) so simple, reliable, or well-constructed that it cannot go wrong or be used incorrectly.

Examples

foolproof plan
Her recipe is so foolproof that anyone can bake a perfect cake on the first try.
The new software comes with a foolproof installation process that even beginners can handle.
We need a foolproof plan to make sure nothing goes wrong during the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym – faulty: prone to mistakes or failure
  • synonym – reliable: consistently good in quality or performance
  • synonym – unfailing: always effective or dependable
