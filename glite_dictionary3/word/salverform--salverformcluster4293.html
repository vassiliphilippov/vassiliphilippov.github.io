salverform

adjective
having a slender tube and expanded tip (flower shape)
Pronunciation: / ˈsælvərˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈsælvəˌfɔːm / (BrE)
(of a flower) having a slender tube and an abruptly expanded, flat tip, as in certain gamopetalous corollas.

Examples

salverform flower shape
A classic example of a salverform blossom is found in the genus Primula.
Botanists often describe the phlox flower as salverform due to its distinctive shape.
The periwinkle has a salverform corolla, with petals that spread out flat at the top of a narrow tube.
