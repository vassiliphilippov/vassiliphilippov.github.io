to recall

verb
cause attention to return from distraction
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔl / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːl / (BrE)
to remember or bring something back into your mind

Examples

to recall a memory
A sharp knock at the door recalled me from my wandering thoughts.
His stern voice recalled the children from their playful daydreams.
The sudden ringing of the phone recalled her to the present moment.
He tried to recall exactly what he had said during the meeting.
I can still recall the excitement of my first concert.
Whenever she smells bread baking, it recalls memories of her childhood.
He could easily recall the details of that important meeting.
I can't recall where I put my keys this morning.
She tried to recall the name of her first-grade teacher.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forget: to fail to remember
  • synonym – remember: to have in or be able to bring to one's mind an awareness of something
  • synonym – reminisce: to recall or talk about past experiences
