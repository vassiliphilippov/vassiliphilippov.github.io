Limacidae

noun
family of terrestrial land slugs
Pronunciation: / laɪˈmæsɪdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈmæsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of air-breathing land slugs in the class Gastropoda, known for lacking shells and being found in moist environments

Examples

Limacidae slugs
Many gardeners are concerned about Limacidae because these slugs eat young plants.
Members of the Limacidae family can be identified by their elongated bodies and lack of shells.
The Limacidae are distributed throughout Europe and parts of Asia.
