regionalism

noun
foreign policy focusing on specific regions
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdʒənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A policy or principle that prioritizes the interests of a particular geographic region, especially in international affairs.

Examples

regionalism policy debate
Critics argue that excessive regionalism can undermine broader international cooperation.
Her thesis explores the impact of regionalism on economic development in Southeast Asia.
The country's regionalism influenced its decision to strengthen ties with neighboring states.
