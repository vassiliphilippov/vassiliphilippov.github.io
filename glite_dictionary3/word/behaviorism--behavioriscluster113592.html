behaviorism

noun
psychological theory emphasizing observable behavior
Pronunciation: / bɪˈheɪvjəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərɪzəm / (BrE)
an approach in psychology that focuses on observable and measurable behaviors rather than internal mental processes

Examples

behaviorism in psychology
Many early experiments in learning were grounded in behaviorism.
Some critics argue that behaviorism neglects the role of thoughts and feelings.
The principles of behaviorism influenced the development of modern psychotherapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cognitive psychology: the branch of psychology concerned with mental processes
  • synonym – psychology: the scientific study of the mind and behavior
