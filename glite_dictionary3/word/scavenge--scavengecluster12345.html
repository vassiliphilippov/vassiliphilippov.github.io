to scavenge

verb
remove refuse or unwanted material
Pronunciation: / ˈskævɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈskævɪndʒ / (BrE)
to remove refuse or unwanted material from a place, especially in order to keep it clean

Examples

to scavenge city streets
Each morning, workers scavenge the streets to collect debris left by pedestrians.
Teams were hired to scavenge the park after the festival.
Volunteers scavenged the riverbank, removing plastic bottles and other trash.
