deixis

noun
context-dependent reference in language
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪksɪs / (AmE), / ˈdiːksɪs / (BrE)
the use of words or phrases (such as pronouns or adverbs) whose meaning depends on the context of the speaker and situation, especially relative to time, place, or person

Examples

deixis in pronouns
Pronouns like 'I,' 'you,' and 'this' all rely on deixis for their interpretation.
The word 'here' is an example of deixis because its meaning changes depending on who is speaking and where.
Understanding deixis helps linguists study how language connects to context in communication.

  • synonym – reference: the act of mentioning or alluding to something; general linguistic reference
