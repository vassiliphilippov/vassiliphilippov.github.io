human chorionic gonadotropin

noun
hormone detected in pregnancy tests
Pronunciation: / ˌhjuːmən kɔːˈriːɒnɪk gəˌnædəˈtroʊpɪn / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmən kɒːˈriːɒnɪk gəˌnædəˈtrəʊpɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the placenta early in pregnancy, used as the basis for certain pregnancy tests
