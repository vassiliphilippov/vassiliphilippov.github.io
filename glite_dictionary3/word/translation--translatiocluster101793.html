translation

noun
genetics: mRNA-directed protein synthesis
Pronunciation: / trænzˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / trænsˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which genetic information encoded in messenger RNA directs the synthesis of a specific protein at a ribosome in the cytoplasm

Examples

translation of mRNA
During translation, ribosomes assemble amino acids into a polypeptide chain.
Mutations in mRNA can disrupt translation and lead to faulty proteins.
The process of translation is essential for protein production in living cells.
