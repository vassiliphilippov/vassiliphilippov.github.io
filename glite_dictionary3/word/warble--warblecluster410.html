warble

noun
swelling in animals caused by botfly larvae
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːbəl / (BrE)
A lumpy swelling or abscess under the skin of domestic mammals, caused by the larvae of a botfly or warble fly.

Examples

warble under skin
Each spring, farmers check their cattle for signs of warbles.
The veterinarian examined the cow and discovered a large warble on its back.
Treatment is necessary to prevent complications caused by the presence of a warble under the skin.
