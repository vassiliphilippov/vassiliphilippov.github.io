billiards

noun
cue sport played on a cloth-covered table
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪl.jɚdz / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.jədz / (BrE)
any of several games played on a rectangular table with a cloth surface and cushioned edges, in which players use cue sticks to strike balls

Examples

play billiards with friends
He spent Saturday night with friends playing billiards at the local club.
She excels at both billiards and other cue sports.
The rules of billiards differ slightly from those of pool and snooker.
