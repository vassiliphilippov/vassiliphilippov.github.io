proenzyme

noun
inactive enzyme precursor
Pronunciation: / proʊˈɛnzaɪm / (AmE), / prəʊˈɛnzaɪm / (BrE)
an inactive substance that is converted into an active enzyme by a chemical change, usually by removing part of its molecule

Examples

inactive proenzyme form
A genetic mutation may prevent a proenzyme from being properly converted into its active form.
During inflammation, the body rapidly converts this proenzyme into an enzyme to fight infection.
The pancreas secretes proenzyme forms that are activated in the digestive tract.
