popularism

noun
music tailored for the majority's taste
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑpjələrˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊlărɪzəm / (BrE)
Music that is designed to appeal to the general public's taste and understanding.

Examples

popularism in music
Classical composers sometimes resisted popularism to retain complexity and sophistication in their work.
Some critics argue that popularism sacrifices artistic integrity for commercial success.
The rise of popularism in the 20th century shaped the direction of mainstream music.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pop music: a genre of popular music with mass appeal
  • antonym – populism: political approach aiming to represent ordinary people (not musical sense)
