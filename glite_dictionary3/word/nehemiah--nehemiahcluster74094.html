Nehemiah

proper noun
book of the Old Testament; Jewish leader
Pronunciation: / niːəˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / niːɪˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament recounting how Nehemiah, a Jewish official at the court of Artaxerxes I, led the rebuilding of Jerusalem after the Babylonian Captivity.
