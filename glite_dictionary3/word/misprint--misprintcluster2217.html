to misprint

verb
print incorrectly; make an error in printed text
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈprɪnt / (AmE), / mɪsˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to print something incorrectly, especially by making a mistake in spelling, layout, or content during printing or publishing

Examples

to misprint a word
If you misprint the number, customers may not be able to contact us.
Newspapers often have to issue corrections when they misprint facts in an article.
The publisher accidentally misprinted the author's name on the book cover.

Related Terms

  • antonym – print: to produce text or images on paper using a machine
