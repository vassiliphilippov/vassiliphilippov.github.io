catheterisation

noun
insertion of a catheter into the body
Pronunciation: / kəˌθɛtərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌθiːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the medical procedure of inserting a catheter into the body to allow drainage, injection of fluids, or access by surgical instruments

Examples

catheterisation after surgery
After her surgery, she required catheterisation for a short period.
Cardiac catheterisation is a key diagnostic tool in modern medicine.
The doctor performed catheterisation to help drain the patient's bladder.
