dioecious

adjective
male and female on separate individuals (biology)
Pronunciation: / daɪˈiʃəs / (AmE), / daɪˈiːsɪəs / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal species) having male and female reproductive organs on separate individuals.

Examples

dioecious plant species
Holly plants are dioecious, so both male and female plants are needed to produce berries.
Some animals, including most mammals, are dioecious, with separate males and females for reproduction.
Unlike monoecious corn, spinach is a dioecious plant species.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monoecious: having male and female reproductive organs on the same individual
