to hook

verb
hit a ball with spin to travel left
Pronunciation: / hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
(in sports) to hit a ball so that it spins and travels to the left, especially unintentionally.

Examples

to hook a golf shot
He tends to hook his drives on the golf course.
She accidentally hooked her shot, sending the ball into the rough.
The batter hooked the ball to the left boundary for four runs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – slice: hit a ball so that it curves to the right
