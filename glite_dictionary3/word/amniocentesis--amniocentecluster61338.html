amniocentesis

noun
test involving extraction of amniotic fluid
Pronunciation: / ˌæmniˌoʊsɛnˈtisɪs / (AmE), / ˌamni.əʊ.senˈtiː.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which amniotic fluid is taken from the uterus of a pregnant woman, usually after the 15th week of pregnancy, to test for fetal abnormalities or certain conditions

Examples

amniocentesis test results
Because of her age, she was offered amniocentesis during her second trimester.
Some parents choose amniocentesis to find out about possible health issues before birth.
The doctor recommended an amniocentesis to check for genetic disorders in the developing baby.
