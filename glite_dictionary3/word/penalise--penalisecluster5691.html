to penalise

verb
impose a penalty or punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to subject someone to a penalty or punishment for a fault or offense

Examples

to penalise a player
The new law could penalise small businesses with heavy fines.
The referee decided to penalise the player for a handball during the match.
You will be penalised if you submit your assignment after the deadline.

  • antonym – reward: to give a reward or compensation for good behavior or achievement
