to laugh

verb
make sounds showing amusement
Pronunciation: / læf / (AmE), / lɑːf / (BrE)
to make sounds and movements of the face and body that express amusement or happiness

Examples

to laugh out loud
Everyone laughed when the cat jumped onto the table.
His jokes always make me laugh out loud.
She couldn't help laughing at the funny movie.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – chuckle: to laugh quietly or to oneself
  • antonym – cry: to shed tears, often as an antonym in the phrase 'laugh or cry'
  • degree lower – giggle: to laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way
