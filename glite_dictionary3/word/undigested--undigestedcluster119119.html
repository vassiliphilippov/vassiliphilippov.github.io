undigested

adjective
not mentally absorbed or analyzed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.dɪˈdʒɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈdʒɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, facts, or information) not thoroughly considered, analyzed, or integrated mentally; not absorbed or assimilated by the mind.

Examples

undigested information overload
He quoted an undigested mass of statistics without putting them into context.
The lecture left me with an undigested collection of theories and no clear understanding.
Too much undigested information can overwhelm rather than enlighten readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – assimilated: fully incorporated or absorbed into the mind
