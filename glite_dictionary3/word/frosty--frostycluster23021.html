frosty

adjective
pleasantly cold and invigorating (weather)
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɑsti / (AmE), / ˈfrɒsti / (BrE)
cold or covered with frost; having a thin layer of ice crystals or a chilly feeling (of weather, air, or surfaces)

Examples

frosty morning
The frosty morning air made her cheeks tingle as she walked to work.
The mountains were especially beautiful in the frosty autumn air.
We enjoyed a frosty evening stroll under a clear, starry sky.
A frosty layer had formed on the car windshield overnight.
She wiped her name onto the frosty window with her finger.
The grass was frosty and crunched beneath our boots at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crisp: (of weather or air) notably fresh, cool, and bracing
  • synonym – icy: covered with or consisting of ice
  • synonym – nippy: (informal, of weather) rather cold; chilly
