countertransference

noun
therapist's emotional reaction to a patient
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntɚtrænzˈfɜːrən(t)s / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntətrænsˈfɜːr(ə)ns / (BrE)
the emotional reaction and projection of feelings by a psychoanalyst or therapist towards a patient, often as a result of the therapist's own unconscious feelings and experiences

Examples

countertransference in therapy
Proper supervision can help clinicians manage feelings of countertransference in their work.
The therapist became aware of her own countertransference towards her client during the difficult session.
Unrecognized countertransference may influence the course of therapy if not appropriately addressed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – transference: redirection of a patient's emotions to the therapist
