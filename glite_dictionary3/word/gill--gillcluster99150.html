gill

noun
British imperial capacity unit (5 fluid ounces)
Pronunciation: / ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
a unit of liquid measurement, used in Britain and the US, equal to about 4 or 5 fluid ounces

Examples

gill of milk
A gill of ale was traditionally served in English taverns.
He measured the spirits precisely, pouring a gill into the jug.
The recipe calls for half a gill of milk.
A gill is not a commonly used measure in modern American cooking, but it can still be found in old cookbooks.
He poured out half a gill of brandy for each of his guests.
The recipe calls for one gill of milk, which is the same as four ounces.
