governing

adjective
having authority or control
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌvərnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪŋ / (BrE)
Having authority to make and enforce rules, laws, or decisions; exercising control over an organization, country, or group.

Examples

governing body
Many countries have a governing body that oversees professional sports leagues.
The governing committee decided to update the organization's code of conduct.
The governing officials were responsible for implementing the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ruling: having control or authority over; currently in power
  • antonym – subordinate: lower in rank or authority
