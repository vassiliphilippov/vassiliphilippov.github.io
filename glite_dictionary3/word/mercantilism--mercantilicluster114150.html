mercantilism

noun
economic policy favoring regulated national wealth
Pronunciation: / mərˈkæntɪˌlɪzəm /; / mərˈkæn.tɪˌlɪ.zəm / (AmE), / mɜːˈkæntɪlɪz(ə)m /; / mɜːˈkæn.tɪ.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
an economic theory and system, especially in 16th-18th century Europe, that promoted government control of trade to increase a nation's wealth and power
