to underrun

verb
to pass below something
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈrʌn / (BrE)
to run or pass underneath something.

Examples

to underrun a bridge
In this method, the pipeline will underrun the existing structure.
The cable must underrun the bridge to avoid interfering with traffic above.
The river underruns the walkway for several miles before emerging on the other side.

Related Terms

  • antonym – overrun: to run or pass over or above something
