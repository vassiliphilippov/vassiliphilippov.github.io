croaky

adjective
rough or hoarse, like a frog's sound
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊki / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊki / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) having a rough, hoarse, or rasping quality, like that of a frog or crow.

Examples

croaky voice
After cheering all night, her croaky voice could barely be heard.
He woke up with a croaky throat and struggled to speak clearly.
The old man's croaky laugh echoed around the room.
