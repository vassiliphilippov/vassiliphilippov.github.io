reducer

noun
pipe fitting joining different diameters
Pronunciation: / rəˈdusɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
A fitting used to join two pipes of different diameters by reducing the size from a larger to a smaller bore.

Examples

pipe reducer fitting
A metal reducer was used to adapt the old system to the new, narrower piping.
The plumber installed a reducer to connect the large main pipe to the smaller branch pipe.
When joining pipes of different sizes, a reducer ensures a secure fit and smooth water flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adapter: fitting used to connect pipes of different types or sizes
