granuloma

noun
small inflammatory tissue growth
Pronunciation: / ˌɡræn.jəˈloʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊˈləʊ.mə / (BrE)
A small area of inflammation in tissue, often caused by infection, injury, or inflammation, typically consisting of a collection of immune cells.

Examples

granuloma in lung tissue
Granulomas can form as a response to chronic infections like tuberculosis.
