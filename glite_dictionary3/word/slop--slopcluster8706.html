to slop

verb
walk through mud or mire
Pronunciation: / slɑp / (AmE), / slɒp / (BrE)
to walk through mud or mire, often making a splashing or squelching sound

Examples

to slop through mud
She hates to slop around in the wet yard, but her dog insists on going out.
The children slopped across the muddy path after the rainstorm.
We had to slop through the soggy field to reach the barn.

Related Terms

  • synonym – splash: move making splashing noises and scattering liquid or mud
  • synonym – trudge: walk slowly with heavy steps, often in difficult conditions
  • synonym – wade: walk with effort through water or another substance
