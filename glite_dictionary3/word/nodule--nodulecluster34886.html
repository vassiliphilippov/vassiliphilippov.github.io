nodule

noun
small rounded mineral lump in rock
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑdʒul / (AmE), / ˈnɒdjuːl / (BrE)
a small rounded lump of mineral substance, often harder than the surrounding rock or sediment, especially found in geological formations

Examples

mineral nodule in rock
Over time, a nodule can form as minerals precipitate around a small core in sediment.
Some manganese nodules on the ocean floor contain valuable metals like nickel and copper.
The geologist carefully examined the nodule embedded in the sandstone layer.
