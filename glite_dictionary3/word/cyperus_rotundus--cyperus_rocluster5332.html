Cyperus rotundus

noun
a perennial sedge with edible tubers
Pronunciation: / saɪˈpɪərəs roʊˈtʌndəs / (AmE), / sɪˈpiːərəs rəʊˈtʌndəs / (BrE)
a widely distributed perennial sedge with small edible nutlike tubers, commonly considered a weed in many regions

Examples

Cyperus rotundus weed problem
Cyperus rotundus is often considered one of the world's worst weeds due to its resilience.
Gardeners struggle to remove Cyperus rotundus because of its persistent underground tubers.
Traditional medicine in some cultures uses the tubers of Cyperus rotundus for various remedies.
