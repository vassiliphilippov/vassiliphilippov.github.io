facultative

adjective
able to exist in more than one condition
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.əlˌteɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / fəˈkʌl.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of an organism or process) able to function, survive, or exist under more than one set of conditions, not restricted to a single mode of life or activity.

Examples

facultative bacteria growth
Scientists studied the facultative adaptations that allow certain plants to thrive in dry and wet environments.
Some fungi are facultative parasites, capable of living off both living hosts and dead organic matter.
Unlike obligate anaerobes, facultative bacteria can survive in both the presence and absence of oxygen.

Related Terms

  • antonym – obligate: restricted to a single condition or mode of life
