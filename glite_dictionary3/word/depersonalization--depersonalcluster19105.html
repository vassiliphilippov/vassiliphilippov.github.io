depersonalization

noun
feeling of detachment from one's self
Pronunciation: / diˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a psychological condition in which a person feels detached from their own thoughts, feelings, or sense of identity, often experiencing a sense of unreality or strangeness

Examples

experience depersonalization symptoms
After a traumatic experience, John often struggled with depersonalization and felt disconnected from his emotions.
People experiencing depersonalization may describe feeling as if they are outside their own bodies.
Prolonged stress and anxiety can sometimes trigger episodes of depersonalization.

Related Terms

  • synonym – derealization: a feeling that the external world is unreal or strange
