fixation

noun
obsessive unhealthy preoccupation
Pronunciation: / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
an unhealthy or abnormal mental focus on something or someone, or a state where development is stuck at a certain stage

Examples

fixation on success
Ever since he lost the game, he developed a fixation with improving his performance.
Her fixation on healthy eating made it difficult for her to enjoy meals with friends.
His fixation on achieving perfection sometimes hindered his progress.
Psychoanalysts believe that a fixation can influence behavior throughout life.
Some adults display a childhood fixation that hinders emotional growth.
The patient's fixation at the oral stage explained certain habits.

Related Terms

  • synonym – obsession: an idea or thought that continually preoccupies the mind
  • synonym – regression: a return to a former or less developed state, used in psychology
