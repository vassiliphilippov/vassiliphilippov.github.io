gestalt

noun
organized whole; unified pattern
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈstɑlt / (AmE), / ɡəˈʃtɑːlt / (BrE)
an organized whole in which the whole is seen as more than just the sum of its parts, especially in the context of perception or psychology

Examples

a gestalt view
In therapy, the concept of gestalt emphasizes perceiving the client's experience as a whole.
The artist arranged the shapes to create a striking gestalt that captivated the audience.
The team's performance had a unique gestalt that could not be understood by looking at individual roles alone.
