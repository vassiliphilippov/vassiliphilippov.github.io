overflow

noun
content beyond a container (UI/CSS)
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌfləʊ / (BrE)
(in computing, especially web design and user interfaces) content that extends beyond the visible area of a container; also the CSS property that controls whether such content is clipped, hidden, or scrollable.

Examples

overflow content box
The CSS overflow property determines whether content is clipped, visible, or scrollable inside a box.
After resizing the window, an unexpected overflow caused the sidebar text to be cut off.
Developers often inspect overflow to decide if a scrollbar or responsive layout is needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clip: in UI design, to cut off content at the container boundary so the excess is not visible
  • synonym – scroll: to move content so parts that extend beyond the visible area become visible
