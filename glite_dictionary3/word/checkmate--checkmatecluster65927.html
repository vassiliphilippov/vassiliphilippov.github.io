to checkmate

verb
win the game of chess by putting the king in inescapable threat
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (BrE)
to place an opponent's king under attack in such a way that it cannot escape, thus winning the game of chess

Examples

to checkmate the king
If you don't defend your king properly, you may be checkmated quickly.
Kasparov skillfully checkmated his opponent in less than twenty moves.
She plans her strategy carefully to checkmate her rivals in the tournament.
