herbicide

noun
plant-killing chemical agent
Pronunciation: / ˈhɝːbɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɜːbɪˌsaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to destroy unwanted plants or to prevent their growth.

Examples

spray herbicide on weeds
Improper use of herbicide can damage valuable crops and harm the environment.
Many modern herbicides are developed to target specific types of invasive plants.
The farmer sprayed herbicide on the field to control the spread of weeds.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fertilizer: a chemical to promote plant growth
  • synonym – pesticide: a chemical used for killing pests, including insects and weeds
  • synonym – weedkiller: a chemical for killing weeds specifically
