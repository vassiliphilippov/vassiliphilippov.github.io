radiolucent

adjective
allowing X-rays to pass through
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪ.di.oʊˈluː.sənt / (AmE), / ˌreɪ.dɪ.əʊˈluː.sənt / (BrE)
Allowing X-rays or other forms of radiation to pass through with little or no absorption, appearing dark or transparent on radiographic images.

Examples

radiolucent area on X-ray
Dentists look for radiolucent spots in teeth X-rays to identify possible cavities.
On the X-ray, the bone appeared solid while the radiolucent tissue showed up as a darker area.
The surgeon noted that the implant material was highly radiolucent, making it difficult to detect on follow-up scans.

Related Terms

  • antonym – radiopaque: preventing X-rays from passing through; appearing white on radiographs
