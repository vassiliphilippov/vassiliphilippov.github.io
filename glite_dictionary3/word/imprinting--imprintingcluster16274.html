imprinting

noun
early-life learning leading to lasting behavior
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a process in the early life of an animal in which a rapid form of learning establishes long-lasting behavioral responses to specific individuals or objects, often of their own species

Examples

animal imprinting process
Many animals rely on imprinting to recognize their parents after birth.
Research shows that imprinting occurs during a critical period soon after hatching.
The ducklings followed the scientist everywhere, a classic case of imprinting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conditioning: learning process of forming associations between stimuli and responses
  • synonym – habituation: process of becoming accustomed to a stimulus through repeated exposure
