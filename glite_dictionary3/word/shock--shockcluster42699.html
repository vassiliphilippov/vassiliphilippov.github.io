shock

noun
serious medical condition of circulatory collapse
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒk / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the body collapses or nearly collapses due to insufficient blood flow and oxygen delivery to tissues, typically marked by a rapid heartbeat, pale skin, and low blood pressure.

Examples

medical shock emergency
Doctors monitor vital signs closely to detect any signs of shock.
Immediate treatment for shock is essential in cases of severe injury.
The patient went into shock after losing a large amount of blood.

  • synonym – collapse: sudden loss of strength or consciousness
  • antonym – stability: the state of being steady or not likely to change suddenly
