pavis

noun
large medieval shield for full-body protection
Pronunciation: / pəˈviːs / (AmE), / pəˈviːs / (BrE)
A large, heavy, oblong shield used in the Middle Ages, designed to protect the entire body; it was originally carried but sometimes set up as stationary protection.

Examples

medieval pavis shield
During the siege, the archers took cover behind a pavis while reloading their crossbows.
Knights often used a pavis to protect themselves from volleys of arrows.
The museum's medieval collection includes a well-preserved pavis from the 15th century.
