censorious

adjective
harshly critical; fault-finding
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈsɔriəs / (AmE), / sɛnˈsɔːriəs / (BrE)
Tending to find fault or criticize others harshly; expressing strong disapproval or censure.

Examples

censorious remarks
He was known for his censorious attitude toward modern art.
Her censorious remarks discouraged further discussion among the group.
The professor grew censorious when discussing students' lack of preparation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – approving: showing or feeling approval
  • synonym – critical: inclined to judge severely and find fault
