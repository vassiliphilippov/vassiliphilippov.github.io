sop

noun
piece of food for dipping in liquid
Pronunciation: / sɑp / (AmE), / sɒp / (BrE)
a piece of food, such as bread, used for dipping into a liquid like soup or sauce

Examples

sop of bread
A sop is often used to absorb the last bit of stew at the bottom of the bowl.
He tore off a sop of bread and dipped it into his soup.
She gave the dog a sop soaked in gravy.
He dipped a sop into his soup before taking a bite.
In medieval times, people often used sops to soak up sauces and broths.
She offered me a sop of bread to taste the broth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dunk: to dip food quickly into a liquid before eating it
