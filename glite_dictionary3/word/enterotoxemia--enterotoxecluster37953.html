enterotoxemia

noun
disease in cattle and sheep from intestinal toxins
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.tə.roʊ.tɑkˈsiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌɛn.tə.rəʊ.tɒkˈsiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A disease in cattle and sheep caused by the absorption of bacterial toxins from the intestines.

Examples

enterotoxemia in sheep
Outbreaks of enterotoxemia can cause significant losses in sheep flocks if not controlled.
The veterinarian diagnosed the sudden deaths as being due to enterotoxemia.
Vaccination is an effective method to prevent enterotoxemia in young lambs.
