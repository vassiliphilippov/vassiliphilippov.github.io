vena facialis

noun
vein of the face (medical term)
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.nə fəˈseɪ.li.əs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə fəˈseɪ.li.əs / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the face; also called the facial vein in anatomical terminology.

Examples

vena facialis drains face
During surgery, the vena facialis must be carefully identified to prevent excessive bleeding.
Facial infections can sometimes spread to the vena facialis, posing a risk of more serious complications.
The vena facialis is responsible for carrying blood away from the superficial structures of the face.
