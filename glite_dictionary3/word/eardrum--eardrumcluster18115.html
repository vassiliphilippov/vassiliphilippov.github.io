eardrum

noun
membrane in the ear for hearing
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrˌdrʌm / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌdrʌm / (BrE)
the thin membrane inside the ear that vibrates when sound waves reach it, allowing hearing to occur

Examples

ruptured eardrum
A loud explosion can damage the eardrum and cause hearing loss.
She suffered a ruptured eardrum after diving into the pool.
The doctor used an otoscope to check my eardrum for signs of infection.
