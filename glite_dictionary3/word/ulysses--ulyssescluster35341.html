Ulysses

proper noun
Roman name of Odysseus, Greek hero
Pronunciation: / juˈlɪsiz / (AmE), / juˈlɪsiːz / (BrE)
The Roman name for Odysseus, the legendary Greek king of Ithaca and hero of Homer's epic poem the Odyssey.

Examples

Ulysses in mythology
In Roman mythology, Ulysses is celebrated for his cunning and resourcefulness.
Many scholars believe that James Joyce named his novel after Ulysses to draw parallels with Odysseus's journey.
Statues of Ulysses can be found in museums around the world, highlighting his importance in classical mythology.
