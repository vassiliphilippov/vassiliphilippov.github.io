combine

noun
group of companies controlling production and competition
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌɪn / (BrE)
a group of independent companies or organizations that join together to control the production and distribution of a product or service and reduce competition

Examples

a business combine
A number of smaller firms united to form a powerful combine in the steel industry.
Government intervention broke up the tobacco combine to encourage greater competition.
The oil combine was accused of fixing prices across several countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cartel: a group of businesses that work together to fix prices or limit production
  • synonym – trust: a large group of businesses joined together to control the market
