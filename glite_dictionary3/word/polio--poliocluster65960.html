polio

noun
an infectious viral disease (poliomyelitis)
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
An acute infectious disease caused by the poliovirus, leading to inflammation of nerve cells in the brain stem and spinal cord, and sometimes resulting in paralysis.

Examples

polio vaccine
Efforts to eradicate polio have significantly reduced the number of new cases worldwide.
He lost the use of his legs because he contracted polio as a child.
Many children were vaccinated to prevent polio outbreaks in the 1950s.
