regulator

noun
official or body overseeing an activity or industry
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
an official or organization responsible for controlling and supervising a particular industry or area of public interest

Examples

financial regulator
A regulator must ensure companies comply with safety standards.
New guidelines were quickly approved by the environmental regulator.
The financial regulator imposed new rules to prevent market manipulation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deregulator: a person who removes or lessens regulations, often the opposite role
  • synonym – overseer: someone who supervises others, especially workers
  • synonym – supervisor: a person who supervises activities or people
