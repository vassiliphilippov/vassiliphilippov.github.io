election

noun
process of voting for a position
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛk.ʃən /; / ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.ʃən /; / ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a formal process in which people vote to choose someone for a public office or position, or the state of having been elected

Examples

presidential election
Many international observers monitored the election to ensure fairness.
She was declared the winner of the mayoral election by a narrow margin.
Voter turnout in the recent election was higher than expected.
After her election, she thanked everyone who had supported her campaign.
The election of the new chairman was announced to the board members.
They held a party to celebrate his recent election to the city council.

Related Terms

  • antonym – appointment: the act of assigning someone to a position without an election
  • synonym – referendum: a general vote by the electorate on a single political question
  • synonym – vote: an act of expressing a choice or decision in an election
