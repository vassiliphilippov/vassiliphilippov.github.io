lignite

noun
a soft, brownish coal used as fuel
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪɡˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡnaɪt / (BrE)
a soft, brownish-black form of coal that is intermediate in quality between peat and bituminous coal, used mainly as fuel.

Examples

burn lignite fuel
Compared to bituminous coal, lignite contains more moisture and releases less heat.
Large deposits of lignite were discovered in the region during the geological survey.
The power plant burns lignite to generate electricity for the nearby cities.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – bituminous coal: a higher-grade, black coal than lignite, used as fuel
  • degree lower – peat: partially decayed plant material used as fuel, earlier stage than lignite
