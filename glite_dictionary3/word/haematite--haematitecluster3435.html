haematite

noun
a reddish-black iron ore mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːməˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhiːmətaɪt / (BrE)
A reddish-black mineral consisting of ferric oxide, and the main source of iron.

Examples

shiny haematite stone
Jewellery made of polished haematite has a metallic sheen and deep color.
The miners extracted haematite from the ancient quarry, using it to produce iron.
You can easily identify haematite by its reddish streak when scraped on a tile.
