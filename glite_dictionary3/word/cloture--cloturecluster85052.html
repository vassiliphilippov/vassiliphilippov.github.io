to cloture

verb
end debate by vote (parliamentary)
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
to bring a debate to an end by calling for an immediate vote, especially in a legislative assembly.

Examples

to cloture a debate
A two-thirds majority was required to cloture the debate and proceed to a vote.
The chairman decided it was necessary to cloture after hours of discussion.
The senator moved to cloture the lengthy debate on the bill.

Related Terms

  • synonym – closure: the act of ending a debate and proceeding to vote, especially in British contexts
