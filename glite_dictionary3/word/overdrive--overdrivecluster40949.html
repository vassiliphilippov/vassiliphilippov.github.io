overdrive

noun
temporary electronic drive boost to speed response
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌdraɪv / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌdraɪv / (BrE)
(in electronics) a feature or circuit that temporarily increases the electrical drive to a component (for example, display pixels) to make it respond or change state faster.

Examples

overdrive setting
Many LCDs include an overdrive setting that sharpens fast-moving images.
The monitor's overdrive noticeably reduced motion blur during fast-paced gaming sessions.
Engineers enabled the overdrive circuit to push pixels to their next state faster.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boost: a temporary increase in power or signal to improve performance or output
