Kenneth Kaunda

proper noun
first president of Zambia
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛnəθ kaʊˈʊndə / (AmE), / ˈkɛnɪθ kaʊˈʊndə / (BrE)
(1924–1999) Zambian statesman who led the country to independence and served as its first president from 1964 to 1991.

Examples

Kenneth Kaunda led Zambia
Kenneth Kaunda played a pivotal role in Zambia's transition to independence.
In 1991, Kenneth Kaunda peacefully handed over power after losing the presidential election.
Many streets and institutions in Zambia are named after Kenneth Kaunda in honor of his legacy.
