trouble

noun
angry disturbance or difficulty
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
a situation in which there is conflict, disturbance, or difficulty, often involving anger or inconvenience

Examples

cause trouble
If you cause trouble again, you'll be asked to leave.
The festival was fun until some trouble broke out near the entrance.
There was trouble at the meeting when tempers flared over the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disturbance: an interruption of a peaceful state
  • synonym – fuss: unnecessary commotion or excitement, especially over something unimportant
  • antonym – peace: a state of quiet and calm, the opposite of disturbance or trouble
