staining

noun
dyeing specimens for microscopic study
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of applying a dye to biological specimens to enhance their visibility under a microscope

Examples

staining microscope slides
Accurate staining is essential for distinguishing different types of bacteria under the microscope.
After staining, the slides were examined to identify the presence of specific proteins.
The pathologist used a special staining technique to highlight abnormal cells in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bleaching: the process of removing color from something
  • synonym – dyeing: the process of coloring materials with a dye
