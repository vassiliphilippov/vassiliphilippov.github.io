traction

noun
market adoption or momentum
Pronunciation: / ˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
(business) the degree to which a product, service, or idea is gaining acceptance, users, customers, or momentum in the market.

Examples

traction in the market
After months of testing, the new app finally gained traction with a steady stream of active users.
Investors asked for metrics showing traction before agreeing to the Series A funding round.
Their social campaign created enough traction that several retailers reached out to stock the product.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adoption: the act of beginning to use or accept a product, service, or idea
  • synonym – momentum: the force or speed of movement; used metaphorically for growing progress or activity
