to discredit

verb
damage the reputation of
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to harm the reputation of someone or something; to make people stop believing or trusting in them

Examples

to discredit a scientist
False information was spread to discredit the scientist's work.
He tried to discredit his rival by revealing old mistakes.
The scandal threatened to discredit several senior officials.
Attempts to discredit the witness failed due to lack of evidence.
She feared that the scandal would discredit her achievements in the eyes of the public.
The newspaper article attempted to discredit the scientist by questioning his research methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defame: to damage the reputation of; slander or libel
  • antonym – vindicate: to clear someone of blame or suspicion
