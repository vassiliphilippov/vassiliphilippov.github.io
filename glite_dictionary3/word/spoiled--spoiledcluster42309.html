spoiled

adjective
pampered or overindulged (esp. child)
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a child) having a character or disposition that has been negatively affected by excessive pampering or indulgence.

Examples

spoiled child
Many people believe that only children are more likely to be spoiled.
The parents' constant gifts made their son a truly spoiled child.
The teacher found it difficult to manage the spoiled students in her new class.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disciplined: (of a person) having self-control and good habits from guidance and correction
  • synonym – indulged: (of a person) given too much of what they want, often in an unhealthy way
