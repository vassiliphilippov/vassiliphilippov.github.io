multipotent

adjective
able to develop into several cell types
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
(of a cell, especially a stem cell) able to develop into several different types of cells within a particular group.

Examples

multipotent stem cell
Multipotent stem cells in the bone marrow can form several kinds of blood cells.
Researchers are exploring the therapeutic uses of multipotent stem cells in regenerative medicine.
Unlike pluripotent cells, multipotent cells have a more limited differentiation potential.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – pluripotent: able to develop into nearly all cell types
  • degree higher – totipotent: able to develop into all cell types, including embryonic and extraembryonic
