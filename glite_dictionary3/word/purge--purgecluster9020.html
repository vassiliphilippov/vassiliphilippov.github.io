to purge

verb
remove (esp. for political reasons)
Pronunciation: / pɝdʒ / (AmE), / pɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to remove someone from an organization, often for political reasons or as part of a group expulsion

Examples

to purge officials
After the government changed hands, several officials were purged from their positions for opposing the new regime.
She was purged from the committee due to her outspoken views.
The party regularly purges its ranks of anyone suspected of disloyalty.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expel: to force someone to leave an organization or country
  • synonym – oust: to remove someone from a position of power or responsibility
