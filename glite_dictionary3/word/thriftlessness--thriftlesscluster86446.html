thriftlessness

noun
wastefulness; lack of thrift
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɪftləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈθrɪftləsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being wasteful and lacking in the careful use of resources, especially money.

Examples

thriftlessness with money
His thriftlessness has led to years of financial difficulties.
She regretted her youth's thriftlessness once she started saving for her future.
The company suffered from management's thriftlessness and poor planning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – thrift: the quality of using resources carefully and not wastefully
  • synonym – wastefulness: the trait of using resources carelessly or inefficiently
