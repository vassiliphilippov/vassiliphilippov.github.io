to covenant

verb
enter into a formal agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvənənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌvənənt / (BrE)
to make or enter into a formal agreement or contract, especially one that is legally binding

Examples

In 1650, the Scottish nobles covenanted with the king for religious freedoms.
The landlord and tenant covenant to respect the terms set out in the lease.
The two companies covenanted not to compete with each other for five years.

The two companies covenanted to share research findings for the duration of the project.
They covenanted together to preserve the ancient traditions of their community.
