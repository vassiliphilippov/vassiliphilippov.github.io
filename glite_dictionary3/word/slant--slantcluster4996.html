to slant

verb
present with a bias
Pronunciation: / slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to present information or a situation in a way that shows a particular bias or point of view

Examples

to slant a story
He slants his reports to appeal to a younger audience.
It's difficult to find media sources that don't slant their coverage in some way.
The newspaper slanted the story to favor one political candidate over the other.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bias: to give a settled and often prejudiced outlook to something; to influence unfairly
  • synonym – distort: to change the way something is presented, often making it inaccurate
