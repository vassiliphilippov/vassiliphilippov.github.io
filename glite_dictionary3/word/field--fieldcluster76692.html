to field

verb
to answer or handle (question/problem) successfully
Pronunciation: / fild / (AmE), / fiːld / (BrE)
to answer or deal with (a question or problem) expertly and successfully, especially in a public setting

Examples

to field questions
Despite the sudden criticism, he fielded it with skill and confidence.
During the press conference, the politician fielded challenging questions about government policy.
She fielded multiple complaints from customers without losing her composure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – handle: to deal with or manage a situation, problem, or person
  • synonym – parry: to deflect or ward off, especially a criticism or question
