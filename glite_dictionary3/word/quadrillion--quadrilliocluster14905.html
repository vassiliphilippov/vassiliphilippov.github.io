quadrillion

noun
a number equal to 1 with 24 zeros (UK)
Pronunciation: / kwɑˈdrɪliən / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrɪliən / (BrE)
the number represented as 1 followed by 24 zeros; in the UK, equal to a million millions of millions (10^24)

Examples

quadrillion in UK maths
A quadrillion is a thousand raised to the power of eight in the British system.
In historical British texts, a quadrillion refers to a 1 with 24 zeros.
The concept of a quadrillion is used in astronomical calculations in the UK.

  • degree higher – quintillion: the number represented as 1 followed by 30 zeros (UK) or 18 zeros (US)
  • degree lower – trillion: the number represented as 1 followed by 18 zeros (UK) or 12 zeros (US)
