Mnemosyne

proper noun
Greek goddess of memory, mother of the Muses
Pronunciation: / niˈmɑːsəni / (AmE), / nɪˈmɒsəni / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the Titaness who was the goddess of memory and the mother of the Muses.

Examples

Mnemosyne in mythology
Mnemosyne was considered the embodiment of memory in Greek mythology.
According to legend, Zeus and Mnemosyne were the parents of the nine Muses.
Many ancient poets invoked Mnemosyne at the beginning of their works.
