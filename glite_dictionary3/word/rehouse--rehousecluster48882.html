to rehouse

verb
provide new accommodation
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈhaʊz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhaʊz / (BrE)
to provide someone with a new home, especially after they have lost or had to leave their previous one

Examples

to rehouse a family
After the fire, the charity worked quickly to rehouse all displaced residents.
The city plans to rehouse low-income tenants in safer, modern apartments.
The government promised to rehouse the families affected by the flood.
