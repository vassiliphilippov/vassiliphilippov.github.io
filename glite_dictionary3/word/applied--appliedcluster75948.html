applied

adjective
relating to practical use rather than theory
Pronunciation: / əˈplaɪd / (AmE), / əˈplaɪd / (BrE)
(of a subject of study) concerned with practical applications in the real world, rather than theory or fundamental principles.

Examples

applied science
He chose to specialize in applied mathematics rather than focusing on abstract theory.
Her research bridges the gap between applied psychology and clinical practice.
The university offers several applied science programs aimed at solving real-world issues.

  • antonym – theoretical: concerned with theory rather than practical application
