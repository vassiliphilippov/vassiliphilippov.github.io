to decant

verb
pour liquid, separate sediment
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkænt / (AmE), / dɪˈkɑːnt / (BrE)
to pour liquid from one container into another, especially to separate it from sediment

Examples

to decant wine
After fermentation, the scientist decanted the liquid into a clean flask.
She slowly decanted the broth to leave the solids behind in the pot.
The sommelier carefully decanted the red wine to avoid disturbing the sediment.

  • synonym – pour: to cause a liquid to flow from a container
