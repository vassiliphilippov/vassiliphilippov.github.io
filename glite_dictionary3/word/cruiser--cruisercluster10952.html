cruiser

noun
large warship between battleship and destroyer
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːzər / (AmE), / ˈkruːzə / (BrE)
a large, fast warship, ranking below a battleship but above a destroyer in size

Examples

naval cruiser
During World War II, several cruisers played critical roles in naval battles.
The fleet included a modern cruiser equipped with advanced radar systems.
The navy commissioned a new cruiser to enhance their maritime defenses.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – battleship: the largest and most heavily armed type of warship
  • degree lower – destroyer: a small, fast, and maneuverable warship
