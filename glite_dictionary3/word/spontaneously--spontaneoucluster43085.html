spontaneously

adverb
without advance preparation
Pronunciation: / spɑnˈteɪniəsli / (AmE), / spɒnˈteɪniəsli / (BrE)
in a natural, unplanned, or unforced way; without being planned or caused by something else

Examples

to act spontaneously
He volunteered spontaneously to help after hearing about the problem.
Her laughter erupted spontaneously, surprising everyone in the room.
The children began to sing spontaneously as they walked through the park.
Flowers sometimes bloom spontaneously in the wild without any human intervention.
The audience began to applaud spontaneously after his inspiring speech.
The children started singing spontaneously, surprising their parents with an impromptu concert.
