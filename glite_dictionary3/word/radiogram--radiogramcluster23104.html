radiogram

noun
image produced by X-rays or gamma rays
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəˌɡræm / (BrE)
a photographic image produced on a radiosensitive surface by radiation other than visible light, especially by X-rays or gamma rays

Examples

radiogram of the chest
A radiogram of her lungs showed signs of pneumonia.
The dentist requested a radiogram to assess the condition of the teeth.
The doctor examined the radiogram to check for any fractures in the patient's arm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – X-ray: an image produced using X-rays (also the process)
  • synonym – radiograph: an image produced by X-rays or similar radiation; synonym for radiogram
