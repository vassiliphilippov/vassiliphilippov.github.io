trophobiosis

noun
symbiotic relation where protection is traded for food
Pronunciation: / ˌtroʊfoʊbaɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtrəʊfəʊbʌɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A symbiotic relationship in which one organism protects another in exchange for a food product provided by the latter.

Examples

trophobiosis with ants
Trophobiosis can significantly influence the population dynamics of the partners involved.
Ants and aphids often engage in trophobiosis, with ants protecting aphids in exchange for honeydew.
The study focused on trophobiosis between certain beetles and fungi in rainforest ecosystems.
