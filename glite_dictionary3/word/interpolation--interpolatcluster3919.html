interpolation

noun
musical reuse by re-recording
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɜrpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(in music) the practice of incorporating a melody, hook, or phrase from a preexisting song into a new recording by re‑performing and re‑recording it (rather than using a direct sample of the original sound recording).

Examples

song interpolation technique
The artist's latest track features an interpolation of a classic chorus, replayed with a fresh arrangement.
Because the song used an interpolation, the original songwriter received credit and royalty payments.
Labels often prefer an interpolation when samples are too expensive or complex to license.

Related Terms

  • synonym – re-record: to perform and record a musical part again rather than using the original recording.
  • antonym – sample: to use a portion of an existing sound recording directly in a new song (contrasts with interpolation).
