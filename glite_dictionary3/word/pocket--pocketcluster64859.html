pocket

noun
bowling: ideal spot among pins
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
(bowling) the space between the headpin and the adjacent pins where a bowled ball is most likely to result in a strike.

Examples

bowling pocket
If you miss the pocket, you're less likely to get a clean strike.
Skilled bowlers aim for the pocket to increase their chances of scoring a strike.
The ball landed perfectly in the pocket, knocking down all ten pins.
