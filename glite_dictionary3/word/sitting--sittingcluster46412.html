sitting

adjective
stationary and easily attacked
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) stationary and therefore easily attacked, criticized, or captured; defenseless or vulnerable.

Examples

sitting duck target
He felt like a sitting duck waiting for the critics to review his work.
The slow-moving convoy was a sitting target for enemy attacks.
With the security system down, the building became a sitting target for thieves.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defenseless: unable to protect oneself from attack
  • antonym – invulnerable: impossible to harm or damage
