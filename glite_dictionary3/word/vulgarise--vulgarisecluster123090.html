to vulgarise

verb
make less refined or debase
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪz /; / ˈvʌlɡɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to make something less refined, more common, or crude

Examples

to vulgarise modern culture
Many critics believe that reality television has vulgarised modern culture.
Some people worry that translating poetry may vulgarise its original beauty.
The tabloid articles have vulgarised the discussion of complex political issues.
It is disappointing when artists deliberately vulgarise their work to attract a larger audience.
Some reality TV shows tend to vulgarise public taste.
The comedian's jokes vulgarised the otherwise elegant event.
