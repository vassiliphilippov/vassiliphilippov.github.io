prothrombin

noun
blood protein precursor to thrombin
Pronunciation: / proʊˈθrɑːmbɪn / (AmE), / prəʊˈθrɒmbɪn / (BrE)
a protein in blood plasma that is an inactive precursor of thrombin, essential for blood clotting

Examples

prothrombin level test
A deficiency in prothrombin can lead to excessive bleeding disorders.
The doctor measured the prothrombin level to assess the patient's blood clotting ability.
Vitamin K is essential for the synthesis of prothrombin in the liver.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thrombin: an enzyme that converts fibrinogen into fibrin during blood clotting
