turbulent

adjective
agitated or disturbed (of liquid/gas)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrbjələnt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊlənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) moving in a violently irregular or agitated way; characterized by disturbance or turbulence.

Examples

turbulent water
During the storm, the usually still lake became noticeably turbulent.
Strong winds created turbulent currents that made swimming dangerous.
The boat struggled to stay afloat in the turbulent waters of the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: not agitated; free from disturbance (of liquid/gas)
