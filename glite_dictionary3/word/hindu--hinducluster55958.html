Hindu

noun
native or inhabitant of Hindustan (India)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪn.du / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.duː / (BrE)
(historical) A native or inhabitant of Hindustan or India, especially before the partition and in non-religious contexts.

Examples

a Hindu trader
During the colonial era, many Hindus migrated to work in other parts of the British Empire.
Old census records classified residents of the region as Hindus regardless of their religion.
She traced her ancestry to a long line of Hindus living near the Ganges.
