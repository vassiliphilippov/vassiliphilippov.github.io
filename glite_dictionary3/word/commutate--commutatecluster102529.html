to commutate

verb
reverse current direction (electricity)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmjəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊteɪt / (BrE)
To reverse the direction of an alternating electric current each half cycle in order to produce a unidirectional current.

Examples

to commutate electric current
Early electric motors used mechanical switches to commutate the current.
Engineers must carefully commutate the flow in high-power converters to prevent electrical faults.
The device is designed to commutate the alternating current into a steady direct current output.

  • synonym – rectify: to convert alternating current to direct current
