firsthand

adjective, adverb
direct from original source
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝstˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstˌhænd / (BrE)
directly from the original source or by personal experience (used as both adjective and adverb)

Examples

firsthand experience

Traveling abroad offers the opportunity to gain firsthand experience of different cultures.
He wanted to see the results firsthand before making a decision.
Journalists report news firsthand to ensure accuracy.
She experienced the excitement of the concert firsthand.
