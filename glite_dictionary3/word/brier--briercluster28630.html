brier

noun
prickly woody vine of eastern U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.ə / (BrE)
A very prickly woody vine native to the eastern United States, with tough, round stems, shiny leathery leaves, and small clusters of black inedible berries.

Examples

woody brier vine
Birds often build nests among the protective thorns of the brier.
He cut away the tangled brier vines that had overtaken the old fence.
The hiker's progress was slowed by a thick patch of brier blocking the trail.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bramble: any prickly shrub, especially wild rose or blackberry
