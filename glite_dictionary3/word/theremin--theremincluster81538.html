theremin

noun
electronic musical instrument played without touch
Pronunciation: / ˈθɛrəmɪn / (AmE), / ˈθɛːrəmɪn / (BrE)
an electronic musical instrument played by moving the hands in proximity to two antennas to control pitch and volume, without physical contact.

Examples

play the theremin on stage
Clara Rockmore is known for her virtuoso performances on the theremin.
He demonstrated how to play the theremin by moving his hands near its antennas.
The eerie sound of the theremin is often heard in science fiction movie soundtracks.
