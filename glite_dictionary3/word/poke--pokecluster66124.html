to poke

verb
stir by prodding or pushing
Pronunciation: / poʊk / (AmE), / pəʊk / (BrE)
to push, prod, or stir something or someone quickly with a finger, stick, or similar object; or to make a hole or mark by doing this

Examples

to poke a stick
Every few minutes, someone would poke the coals to keep them hot.
He poked the ashes to find any remaining embers.
She used a stick to poke the dying fire back to life.
During the game, someone kept trying to poke me to get my attention.
He couldn't resist the urge to poke her in the side as a joke.
She warned her son not to poke the dog with a stick.
Be careful not to poke a hole in the bag with your pencil.
He accidentally poked his finger through the soft paper.
The children tried to poke a stick into the anthill to see what would happen.
