redundance

noun
state of being unnecessary or surplus
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌndəns / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of being superfluous or unnecessary; excess or surplus

Examples

a redundance of words
In his speech, there was a noticeable redundance of similar points made several times.
Streamlining the process eliminated much of the redundance in the current system.
The technical report suffered from redundance and could have been half as long.

Related Terms

  • antonym – necessity: the fact of being necessary or indispensable
  • synonym – redundancy: the state of being not needed or superfluous
