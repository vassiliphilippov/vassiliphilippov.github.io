to overextend

verb
to do or use too much; to exceed limits
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈstɛnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈstɛnd / (BrE)
to try to do more than one is able to, causing strain or difficulty; to commit beyond reasonable limits

Examples

to overextend your finances
Be careful not to overextend your finances during the holiday season.
He overextended himself by taking on multiple projects at once.
The company overextended its resources and struggled to meet demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conserve: to use carefully and not excessively, thus preventing strain
  • synonym – overreach: to go beyond what is intended or allowed, especially in ambition or effort
  • synonym – overstretch: to strain by extending too far, especially resources or capacity
