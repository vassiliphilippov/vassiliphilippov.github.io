to whitewash

verb
cover with a whitening mixture (walls)
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtwɒʃ / (BrE)
to cover a surface, especially a wall or building, with a white liquid mixture used for whitening and disinfecting

Examples

to whitewash a fence
In preparation for the festival, locals spent days whitewashing every building in the village.
The caretaker whitewashed the walls before the grand reopening of the hall.
They decided to whitewash the old fence to make it look fresh and clean.
