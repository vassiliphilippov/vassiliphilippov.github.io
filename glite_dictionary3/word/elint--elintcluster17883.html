ELINT

noun
electronic intelligence from electromagnetic emissions
Pronunciation: / ˈiːˌlɪnt / (AmE), / ˈiːˌlɪnt / (BrE)
intelligence gathered by detecting and analyzing electromagnetic emissions, excluding those from radioactive sources, typically for military or espionage purposes

Examples

ELINT data analysis
Advanced aircraft are equipped with ELINT sensors to gather crucial battlefield information.
Analysis of intercepted ELINT reveals the presence of new missile defense systems.
The military used ELINT to monitor enemy radar installations along the border.

Related Terms

  • synonym – SIGINT: intelligence from interception of signals, including communications and electronic emissions
