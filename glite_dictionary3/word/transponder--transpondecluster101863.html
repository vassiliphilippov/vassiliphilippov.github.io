transponder

noun
device that automatically responds to signals
Pronunciation: / trænˈspɑndər / (AmE), / trænˈspɒndə / (BrE)
an electronic device that receives a specific signal and automatically transmits a specific reply, used especially in communications and navigation systems

Examples

satellite transponder signal
A satellite transponder receives signals from Earth and retransmits them to ground stations.
Modern car keys often contain a transponder that communicates with the vehicle's ignition system.
The aircraft's transponder sent an identification code to air traffic control.

  • synonym – beacon: a device transmitting a signal for guidance
