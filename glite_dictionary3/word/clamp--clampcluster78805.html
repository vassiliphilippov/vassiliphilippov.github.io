to clamp

verb
impose or enforce forcefully
Pronunciation: / klæmp / (AmE), / klæmp / (BrE)
to impose or enforce (something, such as a restriction or punishment) forcefully or suddenly

Examples

to clamp strict rules
Authorities are clamping harsh penalties on those who break the rules.
Police quickly clamped a ban on street gatherings during the emergency.
The government decided to clamp strict regulations on the industry after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – impose: to force something (such as a rule, tax, or punishment) to be accepted or put in place
