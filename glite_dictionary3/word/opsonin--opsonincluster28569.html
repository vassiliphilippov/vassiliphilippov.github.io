opsonin

noun
substance aiding phagocyte action
Pronunciation: / ˈɑpsənɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒpsənɪn / (BrE)
a substance, especially an antibody in blood serum, that binds to foreign particles and makes them more easily absorbed or destroyed by phagocytes

Examples

opsonin in blood
Deficiency in opsonin production can lead to increased susceptibility to certain infections.
Scientists measured the level of opsonin to assess the body's response to infection.
The presence of opsonin in the serum enhanced the destruction of bacteria by the immune system.
