retention

noun
the ability to hold liquid
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the ability or power of a material or substance to hold or retain liquid

Examples

retention of water
Modern diapers are engineered for maximum liquid retention.
Sandy soils have poor retention of moisture compared to loamy soils.
The clay in the field improves the soil's retention of water during dry weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absorption: the process by which a substance takes in another substance, such as a liquid
  • antonym – permeability: the quality of a material that allows liquids to pass through it
