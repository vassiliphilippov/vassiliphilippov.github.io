to relieve

verb
make less worried by sharing trouble
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to make a person feel less worried or troubled by telling or sharing something that is causing anxiety

Examples

to relieve oneself by talking
After days of anxiety, Jack relieved himself by talking honestly with his family.
He finally relieved himself of his secret by confessing to his friend.
She relieved her mind of worry by telling her supervisor about the mistake.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alleviate: to make something less severe or serious
  • synonym – unburden: to relieve oneself of a burden, especially by revealing something troubling
