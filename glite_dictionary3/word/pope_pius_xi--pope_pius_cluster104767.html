Pope Pius XI

proper noun
Pope who ruled 1922–1939; signed Lateran Treaty
Pronunciation: / poʊp ˌpaɪəs ðə ˈɛlɪvənθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˌpaɪəs ði ˈɛlɪvənθ / (BrE)
(1857–1939) the head of the Roman Catholic Church from 1922 to 1939, known for signing the Lateran Treaty recognizing Vatican City as an independent state.

Pope Pius XI played a crucial role in establishing the sovereignty of Vatican City in 1929.
