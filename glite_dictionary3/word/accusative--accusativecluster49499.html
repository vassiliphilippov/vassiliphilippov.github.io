accusative

noun
grammatical case for direct objects
Pronunciation: / əˈkjuːzətɪv / (AmE), / əˈkjuːzətɪv / (BrE)
the grammatical case used for a noun or pronoun that serves as the direct object of a verb

Examples

accusative case noun
English does not typically mark the accusative case except in some pronouns, like 'him' or 'her'.
In Latin, the accusative case marks the direct object of an action verb.
Students of German must learn the endings used for nouns in the accusative case.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nominative: the grammatical case for the subject of a verb
