approximation

noun
imprecise or incomplete account
Pronunciation: / əˌprɑksɪˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌprɒksɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
something that is close to the real thing but not exact, such as a value, answer, or description

Examples

rough approximation
Her description gave a rough approximation of the meeting's events but lacked specific detail.
The official numbers are not yet available, so we have to work with an approximation.
This report is only an approximation of what actually happened that day.
Her painting captures an approximation of the original landscape's colors.
The map is a close approximation of the island's geography.
This measurement is only an approximation, not the exact value.
Even a quick approximation can be useful when exact data is unavailable.
The engineer's approximation of the bridge's length was very close to the actual measurement.
We only have a rough approximation of how many people will attend the event.
