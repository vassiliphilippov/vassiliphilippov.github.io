Cassia occidentalis

noun
tropical shrub, coffee adulterant
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsiə ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (BrE)
A tropical shrub with unpleasant odor and many leaves, whose seeds are sometimes used as an adulterant in coffee; also known as coffee senna.

Examples

Cassia occidentalis shrub
Cassia occidentalis is often found growing in waste places and along roadsides in tropical regions.
Because of its toxic properties, Cassia occidentalis should not be used in traditional herbal remedies without caution.
Some unscrupulous traders have mixed Cassia occidentalis seeds with coffee beans to increase profits.
