atmosphere

noun
unit of pressure (atm)
Pronunciation: / ˈæt.məˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈæt.məsˌfɪə / (BrE)
A unit of pressure equal to the pressure needed to support a column of mercury 760 millimeters high at sea level and 0°C (equivalent to 101,325 pascals).

Examples

one atmosphere pressure
At sea level, the standard atmosphere is used as a reference for many scientific calculations.
Pressure in the diving tank exceeded two atmospheres, making it essential to monitor safety protocols.
The experimental chamber was kept at one atmosphere of pressure.

  • synonym – bar: unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals
