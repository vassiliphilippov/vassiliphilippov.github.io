to rip

verb
copy multimedia from a disc to digital files
Pronunciation: / rɪp / (AmE), / rɪp / (BrE)
to copy audio, video, or other data from an optical disc (such as a CD or DVD) to a computer or other digital storage, often converting it into files.

Examples

to rip a CD
He ripped his CD collection to his computer to listen offline.
Before the flight she ripped several DVDs into MP4 files to watch on her tablet.
They are ripping tracks from the disc and saving them as lossless FLAC files.

Related Terms

  • synonym – copy: to make a duplicate of digital data or files
  • synonym – extract: to obtain data from a larger source, such as files from a disc or archive
