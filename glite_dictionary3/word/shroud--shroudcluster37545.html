to shroud

verb
to cover or conceal like a shroud
Pronunciation: / ʃraʊd / (AmE), / ʃraʊd / (BrE)
to cover, conceal, or obscure something as if with a shroud

Examples

to shroud in fog
A thick fog shrouded the mountain, hiding it from view.
Darkness shrouded the city as the power went out.
The artist's intentions were shrouded in mystery.
A thick fog shrouded the harbor, making navigation difficult for the ships.
Dark clouds shrouded the mountain peaks, creating an eerie atmosphere.
The details of the event remain shrouded in secrecy even after many years.

  • synonym – envelop: to wrap up, cover, or surround completely
  • antonym – reveal: to make known or visible
  • antonym – uncover: to remove a covering; reveal
