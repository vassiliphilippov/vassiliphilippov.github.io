potency

noun
capacity for coming into being
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊ.tən.si / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tən.si / (BrE)
the inherent capacity or potential for something to come into existence or to be developed

Examples

potency for change
Philosophers have debated the potency of mind to shape reality.
The soil’s potency for nurturing life was evident in the lush garden.
This idea still had great potency, though it had not yet been realized.
