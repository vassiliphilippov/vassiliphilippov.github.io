to robocall

verb
place automated prerecorded calls
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊbəˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbəˌkɔːl / (BrE)
to place automated, prerecorded telephone calls to many people, typically for advertising, reminders, or political messages.

Examples

to robocall voters
The campaign plans to robocall thousands of voters with a reminder before election day.
Companies sometimes robocall customers late at night with offers to renew their subscriptions.
The charity robocalled supporters yesterday, asking for donations to fund its new program.

Related Terms

  • synonym – automated call: a telephone call made automatically using a computerized system to deliver a prerecorded message
  • synonym – spam: to send the same unwanted message to many people (by email, phone, etc.)
