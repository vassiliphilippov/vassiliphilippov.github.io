to glamourise

verb
make something appear glamorous
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæmɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmərʌɪz /; / ˈɡlæməraɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem more glamorous, attractive, or exciting than it really is, often by exaggerating or idealizing it

Examples

to glamourise a lifestyle
Some ads glamourise smoking, ignoring its health risks.
The film tends to glamourise the lives of criminals.
They tried to glamourise their holiday photos with special filters and effects.
Some advertisements glamourise unhealthy food to attract young customers.
The film tends to glamourise the lives of gangsters, ignoring their violent crimes.
We shouldn't glamourise the idea of working long hours at the expense of our health.

Related Terms

  • antonym – downplay: to make something seem less important or significant than it is
  • synonym – glamorize: to make something appear glamorous and attractive (American spelling)
  • synonym – romanticize: to make something appear more romantic or ideal than it really is
