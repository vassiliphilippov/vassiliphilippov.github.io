to overwrite

verb
replace existing data with new data
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈraɪt / (BrE)
to write new data on top of existing data, erasing what was previously there, especially in a computer file or storage medium

Examples

to overwrite a file
Be careful not to overwrite your important documents when saving new versions.
If you overwrite a file, the original data cannot be easily recovered.
The software warns you before you overwrite an existing file.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delete: to remove or eliminate, especially computer data or text
  • synonym – erase: to remove data or information, especially from a computer
