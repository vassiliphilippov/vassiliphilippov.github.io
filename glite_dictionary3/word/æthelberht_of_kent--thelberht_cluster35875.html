Æthelberht of Kent

proper noun
early Anglo-Saxon king of Kent
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθəlˌbɑrt əv kɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɛθəlbɜːt əv kɛnt / (BrE)
(c. 552–616) The Anglo-Saxon king of Kent, notable for being one of the first English kings converted to Christianity and for issuing the earliest known code of English law.

Examples

Aethelberht of Kent meets Augustine
Æthelberht of Kent was converted to Christianity after meeting Saint Augustine.
Many historians credit Æthelberht of Kent with paving the way for the spread of Christianity in southern England.
The legal code established by Æthelberht of Kent is one of the earliest in England.
