to inscribe

verb
draw a figure inside another, touching at many points
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to draw or construct a figure inside another so that it touches the boundary at as many points as possible, especially in geometry

Examples

to inscribe a circle
In geometry, a regular polygon may be inscribed within a circle.
The task was to inscribe the largest possible square within the rectangle.
You can inscribe a circle in a triangle so that it touches all three sides.

Related Terms

  • antonym – circumscribe: draw around a figure so as to contain it within the boundary
