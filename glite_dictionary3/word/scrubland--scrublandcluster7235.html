scrubland

noun
land covered with shrubs or bushes
Pronunciation: / ˈskrʌbˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈskrʌblənd / (BrE)
An area of land covered with low, stunted shrubs and bushes, usually in regions unsuitable for cultivation.

Examples

dry scrubland area
After the fire, the area slowly regenerated into a patch of scrubland.
Many animals have adapted to survive in the harsh conditions of the Australian scrubland.
The vast scrubland stretched for miles, dotted with thorny bushes and hardy grasses.
