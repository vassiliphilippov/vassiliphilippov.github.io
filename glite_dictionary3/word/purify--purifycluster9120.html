to purify

verb
make morally or spiritually pure
Pronunciation: / ˈpjʊrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone morally or spiritually pure; to free from sin or guilt

Examples

to purify the soul
Many believe that acts of charity can purify the heart.
She prayed that the ritual would purify her soul.
The confession was intended to purify him of his guilt.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cleanse: to make someone or something clean or pure, especially in a spiritual or moral sense
  • antonym – defile: to make something dirty or impure, especially in a moral or spiritual sense
