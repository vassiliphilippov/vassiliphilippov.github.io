mobilisation

noun
preparation and assembly for war or emergency
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊbɪləˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌmoʊ.bə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌməʊ.bɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of gathering and organizing people or resources for action, especially in preparation for war, emergencies, or a particular purpose

Examples

mobilisation of troops
Rapid mobilisation of resources was crucial during the natural disaster.
The country's extensive mobilisation efforts surprised international observers.
The government ordered the mobilisation of all available troops along the border.
Effective disaster response relies on the mobilisation of local and national resources.
Rapid mobilisation of volunteers helped control the flooding in the city.
The government ordered the mobilisation of military units along the border.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demobilisation: the act of standing down or disbanding forces or activities
  • synonym – mobilization: preparation and assembly for war or emergency (American spelling)
