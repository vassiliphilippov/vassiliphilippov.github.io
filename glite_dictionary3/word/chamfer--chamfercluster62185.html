chamfer

noun
a sloping edge or surface, not at a right angle
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæm.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.fə / (BrE)
A sloping edge or corner formed by cutting off the edge of a material, where two surfaces meet at an angle other than 90 degrees.

Examples

chamfer edge
The craftsman created a smooth chamfer along the edge of the shelf for a neater look.
This tool allows you to cut a precise chamfer on wooden boards.
You should use a file to make a slight chamfer on the metal before assembly.
