faithful

adjective
accurately representing an original
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθfəɫ / (BrE)
exactly representing or following the original; accurate and true to the source or facts.

Examples

faithful copy
Her translation is remarkably faithful to the tone and meaning of the original text.
The artist produced a faithful reproduction of the ancient manuscript.
We admired the faithful depiction of historical events in the documentary.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accurate: free from error; correct or precise
  • antonym – unfaithful: not remaining true to the original; inaccurate or false
