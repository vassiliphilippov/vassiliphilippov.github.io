to loft

verb
propel or launch into the air
Pronunciation: / lɑft / (AmE), / lɒft / (BrE)
to send or launch something high into the air, especially a ball or object

Examples

to loft a ball
A strong wind lofted the kite higher than anyone expected.
Engineers lofted a weather balloon to collect data from the upper atmosphere.
The golfer skillfully lofted the ball over the bunker and onto the green.
He lofts his shots to give his teammates more time to prepare.
The striker lofted the ball over the goalkeeper's head.
With one swift movement, she lofted the tennis ball high over the net.

Related Terms

  • antonym – drop: to let something fall, the opposite action to lofting
  • antonym – ground: to hit or play a ball so it stays on the ground
  • synonym – hurl: to throw something with force
  • synonym – launch: to send or shoot something into the air or space
