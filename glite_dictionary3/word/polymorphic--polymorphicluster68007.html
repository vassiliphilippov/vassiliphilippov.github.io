polymorphic

adjective
crystallizing in multiple structural forms
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of a compound) capable of crystallizing in two or more different forms having distinct structures.

Examples

polymorphic crystals
Pharmaceutical companies must consider polymorphic behavior when designing stable drug formulations.
Scientists studied the polymorphic transitions in the mineral as temperature changed.
The compound can exist in several polymorphic forms, each with different properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym – polymorphous: (of a compound) occurring in several forms or types
