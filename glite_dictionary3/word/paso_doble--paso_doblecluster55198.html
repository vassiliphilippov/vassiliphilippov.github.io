paso doble

noun
lively Spanish march music for dance/bullfights
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːsoʊ ˈdoʊbleɪ / (AmE), / ˌpæsəʊ ˈdəʊbleɪ / (BrE)
A style of lively march music, originally from Spain, composed for dancing the paso doble and often played at bullfights.

Examples

paso doble music performance
Every year, the festival opens with a lively paso doble played by the town band.
The dramatic rhythm of a paso doble energizes the dancers' performance.
The orchestra played a traditional paso doble as the matadors entered the arena.
