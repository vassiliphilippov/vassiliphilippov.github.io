to garrote

verb
strangle with an iron collar or wire
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈrɑːt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
to strangle someone, especially with an iron collar or wire as a method of execution or murder

Examples

to garrote a victim
During the Spanish Inquisition, many prisoners were garrotted as a method of execution.
In colonial times, criminals were sometimes sentenced to be garroted in public.
The assassin garroted his target in complete silence, leaving no evidence behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – strangle: to kill by squeezing the throat
  • synonym – suffocate: to die or cause to die from lack of air
