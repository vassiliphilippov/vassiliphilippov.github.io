ceremoniously

adverb
with formality and ceremony
Pronunciation: / sɛrəˈmoʊniəsli / (AmE), / sɛrəˈməʊniəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is formal and follows established customs or rituals, often with elaborate ceremony.

Examples

ceremoniously present an award
She ceremoniously handed him the keys to the new office.
The ambassador was greeted ceremoniously at the palace gates.
They ceremoniously cut the ribbon to open the new community center.

Related Terms

  • antonym – informally: without ceremony or formality
