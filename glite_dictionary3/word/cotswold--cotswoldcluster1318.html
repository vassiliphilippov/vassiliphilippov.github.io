Cotswold

noun
breed of long-wooled sheep from England
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtsˌwoʊld / (AmE), / ˈkɒts.wəʊld / (BrE)
a breed of sheep with long wool, originally from the Cotswold Hills in England

Examples

Cotswold sheep breed
A flock of Cotswold grazed peacefully on the hillside.
Farmers in the region have bred Cotswold sheep for centuries.
The Cotswold is prized for its high-quality long wool.
