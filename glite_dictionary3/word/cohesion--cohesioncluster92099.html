cohesion

noun
botanical process of plant parts growing together
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhiʒən / (AmE), / kəʊˈhiːʒən / (BrE)
(Botany) The process by which normally separate parts of a plant, such as petals, grow together.

Examples

petal cohesion growth
Botanists studied the cohesion of stamens in that species of lily.
Petal cohesion results in a tubular flower structure in certain plants.
Some flowers display cohesion when their petals merge during development.

Related Terms

  • antonym – adhesion: the process by which different plant organs stick together
