localization

noun
adapting products or content for a specific locale

The process of adapting a product, service, or content for use in a particular locale, including translation, cultural and format changes, and compliance with local laws or conventions.

Examples

localization of software
The team's localization of the app included translated text, date formats, and right-to-left layout support.
Before launching in Japan, they hired specialists to handle the localization and cultural testing.
Effective localization can increase user engagement by making software feel natural to local customers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adaptation: the modification of something to suit a new use or condition, especially for different cultural contexts
  • synonym – translation: the rendering of text or speech from one language into another, often a core part of localization
