subordination

noun
the act of making someone or something subordinate
Pronunciation: / səbˌɔrdnˈeɪʃən /; / səbˌɔːrdəˈneɪʃən /; / səˌbɔrdəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃn /; / səˌbɔːdɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act, process, or state of being in a lower or secondary position, or being under the authority or control of another; obedience or submissiveness to authority

The military is known for its strict subordination of lower ranks to higher authority.
