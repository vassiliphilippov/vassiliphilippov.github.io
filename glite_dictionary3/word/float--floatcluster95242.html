float

noun
stocks available for public trading
Pronunciation: / floʊt / (AmE), / fləʊt / (BrE)
the total number of a company's shares that are available for trading by the public on the open market

Examples

public float shares
A stock with a small float can experience more price volatility.
Investors often look at the float to gauge how many shares are actually available for trading.
The company's float increased after they issued more shares to the public.
