to comport

verb
behave or conduct oneself
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɔrt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to behave or conduct oneself in a particular way, especially as expected or appropriate

Examples

comport himself well
He failed to comport himself in a manner befitting a leader.
She always comports herself with great dignity at public events.
You are expected to comport yourself professionally during the conference.
As an ambassador, she must comport herself with professionalism at all times.
He always tries to comport himself with dignity, even in difficult situations.
The students failed to comport themselves appropriately at the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – behave: to act in a particular way, especially to be good
  • antonym – misbehave: to behave badly or improperly
