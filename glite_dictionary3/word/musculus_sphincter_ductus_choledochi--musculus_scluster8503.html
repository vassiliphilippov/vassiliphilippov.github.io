musculus sphincter ductus choledochi

noun
muscle sphincter of the common bile duct
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˈsfɪŋk.tɚ ˈdʌk.təs koʊ.ləˈdoʊ.kaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˈsfɪŋk.tə ˈdʌk.təs kɒ.ləˈdəʊ.kɪ / (BrE)
The smooth muscle sphincter located at the end of the common bile duct, regulating the flow of bile into the duodenum.

Examples

musculus sphincter ductus choledochi controls bile
A spasm of the musculus sphincter ductus choledochi can cause pain and obstruct bile drainage.
During surgery, the surgeon inspected the musculus sphincter ductus choledochi for any abnormalities.
The musculus sphincter ductus choledochi controls the flow of bile into the small intestine.
