monotype

noun
taxonomic group with one member
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.nə.taɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.taɪp / (BrE)
(biology) A taxonomic group containing only one member, such as a genus with a single species.

Examples

a monotype genus
In some cases, a monotype can provide important insight into evolutionary history.
Many rare plants are classified as a monotype within their genus.
The bird genus Apteryx is a monotype because it contains only one living species.
