Chrysanthemum balsamita

noun
aromatic Eurasian herb used in cooking and tea
Pronunciation: / krɪˌsænθəməm bælˈsæmɪtə / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθɪməm bælˈsæmɪtə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb with a tansy-like scent and yellow button-shaped flowers, used as a potherb, salad green, and sometimes for tea or potpourri.

Examples

Chrysanthemum balsamita leaves in salad
Gardeners often grow Chrysanthemum balsamita for its aromatic leaves and attractive yellow blooms.
Historically, Chrysanthemum balsamita was used in salads and as a remedy in folk medicine.
Some herbal teas are flavored with Chrysanthemum balsamita, known for its slightly minty aroma.
