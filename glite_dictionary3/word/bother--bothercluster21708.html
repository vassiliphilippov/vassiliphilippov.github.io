to bother

verb
to confuse or puzzle
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːðər / (AmE), / ˈbɒðə / (BrE)
to make someone feel confused, perplexed, or puzzled

Examples

to bother your mind
Don't let those details bother you; focus on the main idea.
Her contradictory statements bothered me and left me uncertain about her intentions.
The professor's complex explanation only bothered the students further.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confuse: to make someone unable to think clearly or understand
  • antonym – enlighten: to make someone understand something clearly
  • synonym – perplex: to make someone feel completely baffled or puzzled
