to punch

verb
make a hole with a tool
Pronunciation: / pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to make a hole in something using a special tool, or to drive or force something through with strong pressure

Examples

to punch a hole
Please punch holes in these documents before filing them.
She punched a ticket at the entrance using a handheld tool.
The machine automatically punches holes in each sheet for binding.
A jagged rock punched through the sole of his boot while he was hiking.
A rusty nail punched right through the wooden plank.
The sharp branch punched a hole in the tent fabric during the storm.

  • synonym – thrust: to push something suddenly or violently in a specified direction
