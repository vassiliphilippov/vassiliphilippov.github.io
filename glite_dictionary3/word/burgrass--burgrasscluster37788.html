burgrass

noun
a grass with bur-like seed heads (genus Cenchrus)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any grass of the genus Cenchrus, known for its bristly, bur-like seed heads that can stick to animals and clothing

Examples

burgrass seeds stick
Cattle can sometimes avoid areas overgrown with burgrass due to its spiky burs.
Gardeners often remove burgrass because its sticky seeds cling to clothing and animal fur.
The farmer found that burgrass quickly spread across the pasture, making it difficult for livestock to graze.
