vacillation

noun
indecision, frequent changes of mind
Pronunciation: / ˌvæsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvæsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the inability to decide between different opinions or actions; indecision, especially as expressed by frequent changes of mind or hesitation

Examples

constant vacillation
Despite pressure to choose quickly, her vacillation was evident in every conversation.
His vacillation over the job offer frustrated both employers and friends.
The committee's vacillation delayed the implementation of the new policy.
