to presage

verb
to be a sign or omen of
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
to be a sign or warning of; to foreshadow or indicate as an omen.

Examples

to presage trouble
A sudden drop in temperature may presage a coming storm.
Many feared the economic downturn would presage further job losses.
The ominous clouds presaged the arrival of the hurricane.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foreshadow: to indicate or warn of a future event
  • synonym – portend: to be a sign or warning that something momentous is likely to happen
  • synonym – predict: to declare or indicate in advance, especially on the basis of special knowledge
