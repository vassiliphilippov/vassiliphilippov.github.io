to trammel

verb
to restrict or hinder
Pronunciation: / ˈtræməl / (AmE), / ˈtræməl / (BrE)
to restrict, limit, or catch someone or something, as if in a net or trap

Examples

to trammel progress
Freedom of speech should not be trammeled by unnecessary laws.
The teacher did not want to trammel the students' creativity with too many rules.
They argued that new regulations would trammel the company's ability to innovate.
She felt her creativity was trammeled by rigid rules at work.
The bird became trammeled in the discarded fishing net.
The regulations trammel small businesses, making it hard for them to grow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – free: to release from restriction or confinement
  • synonym – impede: to hinder or slow the progress of something
  • synonym – restrain: to prevent someone from doing something; to hold back
  • synonym – trap: to catch (an animal or person) in a trap
