social

adjective
relating to communities or groups
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl /; / ˈsoʊ.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl /; / ˈsəʊ.ʃəl / (BrE)
relating to society, communities, or the way people or animals live and interact together

Examples

social behavior
Humans are considered social animals because they naturally live in groups.
Many animals display social behaviors such as cooperation and communication.
The study examined how social environments affect human behavior.
Her research focuses on social change and its effects on urban areas.
Most social problems require cooperation between government and the community.
These laws were created to address social inequality and injustice.
Ants are highly social creatures that work together for the survival of the colony.
Many birds are social animals, gathering in flocks during migration.
Wolves display social behavior by living and hunting in packs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – individual: relating to a single person or thing, not society
  • synonym – sociable: willing to talk and engage with others
  • synonym – societal: relating to society or social organization
  • antonym – solitary: (of animals) tending to live alone rather than in groups
