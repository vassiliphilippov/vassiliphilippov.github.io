plagiocephaly

noun
skull malformation with an oblique axis
Pronunciation: / ˌpleɪɡioʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌpleɪɡiəʊˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A congenital malformation of the skull in which the main axis of the skull is oblique.

Examples

infant plagiocephaly
Parents were advised to reposition their baby's head during sleep to help prevent plagiocephaly.
Surgery is sometimes recommended in severe cases of plagiocephaly to correct the skull shape.
The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with plagiocephaly after noticing an uneven shape to the skull.
