to lot

verb
divide land into parcels
Pronunciation: / lɑt / (AmE), / lɒt / (BrE)
to divide land or property into separate parcels or lots, especially for the purpose of sale or development

Examples

to lot land
After acquiring the property, they lotted it into ten smaller sections for resale.
Developers will lot the estate to create separate homes with their own boundaries.
The company decided to lot the farmland before selling it to individual buyers.
