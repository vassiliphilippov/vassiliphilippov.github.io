trepang

noun
edible sea cucumber from Asia-Pacific
Pronunciation: / trəˈpæŋ / (AmE), / trɪˈpæŋ / (BrE)
A sea cucumber of warm coastal waters, especially from Australia to Asia, used as food in East and Southeast Asian cuisines.

Examples

dried trepang dish
Dried trepang is considered a delicacy in many parts of China.
Fishermen collect trepang along the coral reefs for export to Asian markets.
The soup was flavored with slices of trepang and various Chinese herbs.
