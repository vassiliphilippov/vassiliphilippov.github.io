ironic

adjective
humorously sarcastic or mocking
Pronunciation: / aɪˈrɑːnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒnɪk / (BrE)
Expressing an idea, remark, or situation in a way that is deliberately contrary to what is expected, often to be humorously sarcastic or mocking.

Examples

ironic comment
He gave her an ironic smile after she missed the obvious answer.
Her ironic comments during the debate made the audience laugh.
The ironic tone of his letter made it clear he was not truly angry.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sarcastic: using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym – sincere: genuine; not ironic or mocking
