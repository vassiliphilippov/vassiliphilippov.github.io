Haggadah

noun
non-legal Talmudic literature of Jewish tradition
Pronunciation: / hɑˈɡɑdə / (AmE), / hɑˈɡɑdə / (BrE)
Talmudic literature that is not concerned with laws but forms part of the Jewish tradition, often comprising stories, legends, and moral teachings.

Examples

Jewish Haggadah stories
Many Jewish stories are derived from the Haggadah rather than legal texts.
She studied both the legal Halakhah and the narrative sections of the Haggadah.
The rabbi explained a passage from the Haggadah during the discussion.
