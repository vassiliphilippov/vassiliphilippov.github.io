caudate

noun
lizard-like amphibian (salamander or newt)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
an amphibian of the order Caudata, typically resembling a lizard and comprising salamanders and newts

Examples

caudate salamander group
Both newts and salamanders are considered caudates due to their tails and body shape.
Many caudates inhabit moist woodlands where they are rarely seen during the day.
The research focused on the regeneration abilities in several species of caudate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anuran: an amphibian of the order Anura (frogs and toads), lacking a tail
  • synonym – salamander: an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance
