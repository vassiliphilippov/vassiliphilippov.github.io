Babism

noun
19th-century Iranian religious sect and doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːˌbɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbɑːˌbɪzəm / (BrE)
The religious doctrine and practices of a 19th-century Iranian sect founded by the Báb, characterized by the belief in continuing revelation and reforms such as forbidding polygamy and slavery.

Babism religious movement
Many of the reforms introduced by Babism were considered radical at the time.
