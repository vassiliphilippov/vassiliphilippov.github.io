ditch

noun
long, narrow trench in the ground
Pronunciation: / dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)
A long, narrow trench dug in the ground, typically to carry water or mark the boundary of an area.

Examples

deep ditch
He lost his balance and accidentally fell into a muddy ditch.
Rainwater collected in the ditch by the side of the road after the storm.
The farmer dug a ditch along the edge of the field to help with drainage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trench: a long, narrow excavation, especially one used in warfare or construction
