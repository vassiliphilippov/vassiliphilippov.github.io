commonplace

adjective
lacking originality; overused
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.mənˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌpleɪs / (BrE)
(of a remark, idea, or phrase) overused and therefore unoriginal; lacking freshness or originality through frequent use.

Examples

commonplace idea
Critics described the movie's plot as predictable and commonplace.
He avoided commonplace topics in his writing, seeking fresh and unique ideas instead.
The speech was full of commonplace expressions that failed to inspire the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hackneyed: (of a phrase or idea) lacking significance through having been overused
  • antonym – original: new and not derived from anything else
  • synonym – trite: lacking originality or freshness, due to overuse
