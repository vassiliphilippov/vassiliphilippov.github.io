G

noun
DNA nucleotide, deoxyguanylate
Pronunciation: / dʒi /; / dʒiː / (AmE), / dʒi /; / dʒiː / (BrE)
a component of DNA or RNA, either as the base guanine or as a nucleotide containing guanine

Examples

G base in DNA

During DNA sequencing, the G nucleotide pairs with cytosine.
Scientists labeled the G position to track the genetic sequence.
A mutation that changes a G to an A can have significant effects.
RNA strands also include G, which always pairs with cytosine.
