reaction

noun
mental response to an event or stimulus
Pronunciation: / riˈækʃən / (AmE), / riˈækʃən / (BrE)
an emotional, mental, or behavioral response to something that happens, including feelings, attitudes, or actions taken in response

Examples

emotional reaction
Her immediate reaction to the announcement was disbelief and surprise.
His reaction to the criticism was to reflect on his approach and make improvements.
The novel provoked a strong reaction among critics and readers alike.
Her nervous reaction was obvious during the job interview.
His reaction to the surprise party was one of genuine delight.
The news provoked a mixed reaction from the public.
Her early novels were a reaction to the traditional roles assigned to women in society.
His outspokenness was a direct reaction to years of being ignored.
The new art movement emerged as a reaction against strict academic standards.
