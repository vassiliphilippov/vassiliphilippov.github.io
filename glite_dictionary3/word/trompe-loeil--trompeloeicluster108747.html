trompe-l'oeil

adjective
art technique creating optical illusion
Pronunciation: / trɔmp lɔɪ / (AmE), / trɒmp lɜːl / (BrE)
a style of painting or art designed to deceive the viewer into believing that the depicted objects are real

Examples

trompe-l'oeil painting
A stunning trompe-l'oeil mural gave the impression of a grand staircase where none existed.
Museums often display works that feature trompe-l'oeil to fascinate visitors with visual tricks.
The artist's use of trompe-l'oeil made the painted fruit look almost edible.

  • synonym – illusionism: artistic practice of creating illusions of reality
