Lupinus subcarnosus

noun
a Texan blue-flowered lupine species, toxic to livestock
Pronunciation: / luˈpaɪnəs sʌbˌkɑːrˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / ljuːˈpaɪnəs sʌbˌkɑːˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A low-growing annual herb native to southwestern United States (especially Texas), with silky foliage and blue flowers; known to cause livestock poisoning in the region.
