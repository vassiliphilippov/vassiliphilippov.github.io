subunit

noun
fractional monetary unit
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a monetary unit that is a fixed fraction, usually one hundredth, of the main unit of currency

Examples

currency subunit
In some countries, the subunit is no longer used because of inflation.
Most currencies divide the basic unit into a subunit, such as cents or pence.
The euro cent is a subunit of the euro.
