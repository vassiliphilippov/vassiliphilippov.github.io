telescope

noun
optical instrument for seeing distant objects
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument that makes distant objects appear closer and larger, using lenses or mirrors to magnify their images

Examples

look through a telescope
He used his telescope to observe the craters on the moon.
The astronomer adjusted the telescope to focus on a distant galaxy.
With a powerful telescope, you can see the rings of Saturn from your backyard.

Related Terms

  • synonym – binoculars: an instrument with two eyepieces for viewing distant objects
  • antonym – microscope: an instrument for viewing very small objects
