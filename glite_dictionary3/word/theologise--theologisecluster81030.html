to theologise

verb
discuss or interpret theologically
Pronunciation: / θiˈɑlədʒaɪz /; / ˈθiəloʊˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / θɪˈɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to discuss, interpret, or reason about something from a theological (religious) point of view

Examples

to theologise about faith
He tends to theologise every discussion about morality, viewing all actions through a religious lens.
Some writers theologise historical events, interpreting them as part of a divine plan.
The professor cautioned against attempts to theologise political debates in the classroom.
He would rather act than theologise about every aspect of faith.
Scholars often theologise about the nature of salvation and divinity.
They spent the afternoon theologising on the challenges faced by the modern church.
