infidel

noun
person who does not believe in a particular religion
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.fə.dəl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.dəl / (BrE)
a person who does not believe in a particular religion, especially Christianity or Islam, or who is regarded by members of a religion as unfaithful or unorthodox

Examples

an infidel enemy
During the Crusades, knights fought against those they called infidels in the Holy Land.
He was labeled an infidel because he followed a different faith.
The term infidel has often been used in religious conflicts throughout history.

Related Terms

  • antonym – believer: person who accepts a particular faith
  • synonym – heretic: person who holds beliefs contrary to the orthodox doctrine of a religion
