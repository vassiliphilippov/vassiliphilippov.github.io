precedent

noun
earlier example guiding future actions
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdənt / (BrE)
An earlier event or action that is regarded as an example or guide to be considered in subsequent similar circumstances.

Examples

set a precedent
Lawyers often cite precedent to support their arguments in court.
The court's decision set a significant precedent for future legal cases.
There is no precedent for such a rapid technological change in history.

Related Terms

  • synonym – example: something that serves as a pattern or model
  • antonym – following: coming after or next
