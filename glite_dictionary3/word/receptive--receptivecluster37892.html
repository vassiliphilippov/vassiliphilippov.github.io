receptive

adjective
Able to absorb liquid
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
Able to absorb liquid or take up a substance, especially without repelling it.

Examples

receptive to ink
The artist prefers paper that is highly receptive to ink for detailed drawings.
The filter has a receptive surface that allows rapid absorption of water.
This type of fabric is especially receptive to dye, resulting in brighter colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absorbent: able to soak up liquid easily
  • antonym – repellent: resisting or preventing absorption
