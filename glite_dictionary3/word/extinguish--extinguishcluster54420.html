to extinguish

verb
put out a fire, flame, or light
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to put out a fire, flame, light, or something burning (like a cigarette) so it stops burning or shining

Examples

to extinguish a fire
It took the firefighters hours to extinguish the burning building.
Please extinguish the candles before leaving the room.
The rain finally helped to extinguish the flames in the forest.
He quickly extinguished his cigar by pressing it into the sand.
Please extinguish all smoking materials before boarding the bus.
She extinguished her cigarette in the ashtray before entering the non-smoking area.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignite: to set on fire; to cause to start burning
  • antonym – light: to ignite or cause to begin burning (e.g. a cigarette)
  • synonym – quench: to put out or suppress a fire, flame, or feeling
  • synonym – stub out: to extinguish (a cigarette or similar item) by crushing the burning end against a surface
