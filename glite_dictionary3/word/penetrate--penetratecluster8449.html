to penetrate

verb
move into or through (something)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪt /; / ˈpɛnɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪ.treɪt /; / ˈpɛnɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to enter or pass into or through something, especially with effort or difficulty

Examples

to penetrate a wall
Light failed to penetrate the heavy curtains in the old mansion.
Scientists use special drills to penetrate deep layers of rock for research.
The search team struggled to penetrate the thick jungle due to tangled vines.
It took years for new technology to penetrate rural areas.
No sound could penetrate the thick walls of the ancient castle.
The sunlight penetrated the dense canopy of the forest.
