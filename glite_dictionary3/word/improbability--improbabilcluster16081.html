improbability

noun
quality of being improbable
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌprɑːbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒbəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unlikely to happen or be true.

Examples

sheer improbability
Given the team's track record, their victory was an utter improbability to many fans.
Scientists were surprised by the improbability of the two rare species meeting in the wild.
The improbability of winning the lottery does not stop people from buying tickets every week.
