metacentre

noun
point indicating a vessel's stability (shipbuilding)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtəˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəsɛntə / (BrE)
(Shipbuilding) The point where a vertical line through the center of buoyancy of a floating body in equilibrium intersects a corresponding line when the body is tilted to one side; the distance of the metacentre above the center of gravity indicates the stability of the vessel.

Examples

metacentre of a ship
Engineers adjusted the cargo distribution to raise the metacentre and improve safety.
Knowledge of the metacentre is essential in the design of stable vessels.
The ship's metacentre was carefully calculated to ensure maximum stability at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buoyancy: the ability of an object to float in water or air
  • synonym – center of gravity: the point where the total weight of a body is considered to act
  • antonym – instability: state of being unstable, lacking stability
