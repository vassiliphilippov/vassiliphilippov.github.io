counterevidence

noun
evidence that contradicts a claim
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
Evidence that tends to disprove or contradict a particular claim or hypothesis.

Examples

counterevidence against a claim
Despite clear counterevidence, she refused to change her theory.
Scientific progress often involves the collection of counterevidence to test existing models.
The defense attorney presented counterevidence that challenged the witness's testimony.
