rustication

noun
banishment to the countryside
Pronunciation: / ˌrʌstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of sending someone away from a particular place, especially as a punishment, to live in the countryside; banishment to rural areas

Examples

rustication as punishment
After the scandal, the official went into rustication in his family's rural estate.
His rustication was seen as both a punishment and an opportunity for reflection.
The rebellious student faced rustication for repeatedly violating college rules.
