to transubstantiate

verb
change bread and wine into body and blood (theology)
Pronunciation: / ˌtræn.səbˈstæn.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌtræn.səbˈstæn.ʃieɪt / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) to change the bread and wine of the Eucharist into the body and blood of Christ.

Examples

to transubstantiate bread
Catholics believe that the priest can transubstantiate the bread and wine during Mass.
Many theologians have debated how the bread and wine actually transubstantiate in the sacrament.
The doctrine claims that the elements transubstantiate into the real presence of Christ.
