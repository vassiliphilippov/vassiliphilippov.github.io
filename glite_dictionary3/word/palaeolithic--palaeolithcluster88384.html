Palaeolithic

noun
early phase of the Stone Age
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaliəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
The early phase of the Stone Age, lasting from about 2.5 million years ago to the end of the last Ice Age, characterized by the use of primitive stone tools.

Examples

Palaeolithic period tools
Archaeologists discovered Palaeolithic tools in the cave, revealing details about early human life.
Art from the Palaeolithic era, such as cave paintings, provides insight into prehistoric cultures.
The Palaeolithic period is marked by the use of simple stone implements and hunter-gatherer societies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Neolithic: the later part of the Stone Age, when ground or polished stone weapons and implements prevailed
