dewdrop

noun
a drop of dew
Pronunciation: / ˈduˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A small drop of dew that forms on cool surfaces, usually seen in the early morning.

Examples

morning dewdrop
A single dewdrop sparkled on the tip of the grass blade in the morning sunlight.
Each dewdrop reflected the colors of the sunrise like a tiny prism.
She knelt down to examine the delicate dewdrops that covered the petals of the rose.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dew: moisture condensed from the atmosphere, especially at night, and deposited in the form of small drops on any cool surface
