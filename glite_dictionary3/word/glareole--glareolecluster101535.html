glareole

noun
Old World shorebird of the Glareolidae family
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɛr.iˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɛə.ri.əʊl / (BrE)
An Old World shorebird of the family Glareolidae, having long pointed wings and short legs, and closely related to the coursers.

Examples

a glareole bird
Several species of glareole can be found throughout Africa and southern Europe.
The glareole is known for its agile flight and insect-catching abilities.
The ornithologist spotted a flock of glareoles near the gravel beds.

  • synonym – courser: a related bird of the family Glareolidae, but with longer legs
