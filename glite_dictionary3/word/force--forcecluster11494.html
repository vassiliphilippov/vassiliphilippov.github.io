force

noun
baseball play where a runner is put out at a base
Pronunciation: / fɔrs / (AmE), / fɔːs / (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which a base runner who is required to advance is put out by a fielder holding the ball while touching the base before the runner arrives.

Examples

force play at second
The shortstop made a quick throw to second for the force.
The team practiced turning double plays starting with the force at second base.
With two outs, he was tagged out on a force at home plate.
