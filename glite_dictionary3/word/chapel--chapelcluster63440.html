chapel

noun
small place of Christian worship
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæpəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæpəl / (BrE)
A small building or room used for Christian worship, typically with its own altar, and often attached to a larger church or institution.

Examples

school chapel
Each morning, the students gather in the school chapel for assembly and prayer.
The hospital has a small chapel where patients and staff can pray or reflect.
They held their wedding ceremony in a centuries-old chapel overlooking the valley.
