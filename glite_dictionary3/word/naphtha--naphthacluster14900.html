naphtha

noun
volatile flammable liquid hydrocarbon mixture
Pronunciation: / ˈnæfθə / (AmE), / ˈnafθə / (BrE)
A volatile, flammable liquid mixture of hydrocarbons, used mainly as a solvent or fuel in industry.

Examples

refined naphtha fuel
Some types of lighter fluid contain highly refined naphtha as their main ingredient.
The lab stored large quantities of naphtha for use as an industrial solvent.
You must keep naphtha away from open flames due to its flammable nature.
