to parallel

verb
to match or be similar to
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌlɛl /; / ˈpærəˌlɛl / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌlɛl /; / ˈparəlɛl / (BrE)
to be similar to or match something in a way that shows a correspondence or duplication

Examples

to parallel her success
Her achievements parallel those of the greatest scientists in history.
No other city has ever paralleled the rapid growth of Shanghai in recent decades.
The plot of this movie closely parallels events from the director's own life.
Her career paralleled that of her mentor in many respects.
History often parallels literature in surprising ways.
The challenges faced by the two companies closely parallel each other.

  • synonym – match: to be equal to or correspond with in quality, amount, or type
  • synonym – resemble: to be like or similar to
