pyrethrum

noun
insecticidal powder from chrysanthemum plants
Pronunciation: / paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / pɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
A substance obtained from the dried flower heads of certain chrysanthemum plants, used as an insecticide.

Examples

pyrethrum insect spray
Organic farmers often prefer pyrethrum as a natural alternative to synthetic pesticides.
The gardeners used pyrethrum to control the aphid population in the greenhouse.
The powder is made by grinding the dried flower heads of pyrethrum plants.
