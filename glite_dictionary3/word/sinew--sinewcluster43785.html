sinew

noun
tendon; tissue connecting muscle to bone
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnu / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuː / (BrE)
a strong cord of tough, inelastic tissue that connects a muscle to a bone; a tendon

Examples

sinew of the arm
Ancient bowmakers often used animal sinew to string their bows for added strength.
The athlete's injury was caused by a torn sinew in his leg.
The strength of the arm depends not just on muscle, but also on the sinew that anchors it to the bone.
