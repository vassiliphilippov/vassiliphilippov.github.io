megabit

noun
unit of digital information (1,000,000 bits)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡ.əˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡ.ə.bɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000 (one million) bits, commonly used to measure data transfer rates

Examples

download speed in megabits
A file size of 10 megabits will take about one second to download at high speed.
Most internet providers advertise download speeds in megabits per second.
One megabit contains one million individual bits of digital data.
