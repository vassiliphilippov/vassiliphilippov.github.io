knockabout

noun
type of sloop without bowsprit
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑk.əˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.ə.baʊt / (BrE)
a type of sloop with a simplified rig and no bowsprit, designed for ease of handling

Examples

knockabout sailboat
He preferred sailing a knockabout because it was easier to manage alone.
Several knockabouts were anchored in the harbor, their simple rigs easily recognizable.
The old knockabout handled the choppy waters with ease during the regatta.
