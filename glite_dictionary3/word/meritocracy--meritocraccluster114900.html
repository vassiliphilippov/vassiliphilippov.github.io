meritocracy

noun
system where advancement is by merit
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɑk.rə.si /; / ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɑː.krə.si / (AmE), / ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɒk.rə.si / (BrE)
a system or society where people are chosen or advance based on their abilities and merits, not their social class or wealth

Examples

modern meritocracy
Critics argue that a true meritocracy is difficult to achieve in an unequal society.
Many believe a meritocracy encourages competition and innovation in the workplace.

Critics argue that true meritocracy is difficult to achieve in practice.
Many believe that a meritocracy encourages hard work and rewards talent irrespective of background.
The university aspires to be a meritocracy, admitting students purely on academic achievement.
