out

noun
dismissal in baseball; batter or runner fails to reach base
Pronunciation: / aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
(Baseball) a failure by a batter or runner to reach a base safely, resulting in a dismissal from play for that turn.

Examples

get an out in baseball
He was tagged between second and third base and called out by the umpire.
The team managed to get two outs in the ninth inning, holding onto their lead.
You only get three outs per inning before the teams switch sides.
