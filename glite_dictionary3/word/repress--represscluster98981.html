to repress

verb
produce a new pressing (records)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈprɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛs / (BrE)
(in music and media production) to produce a new pressing or run of physical copies (especially vinyl records) of a previously released recording.

Examples

to repress a record
The label plans to repress the classic album next month to meet fan demand.
After the first run sold out, they repressed the vinyl with updated sleeve art.
Collectors were excited because the company is repressing the band's rare EP on heavyweight vinyl.

Related Terms

  • related – pressing: a single production run of physical records; the batch of copies produced at once
  • synonym – reissue: to release a recording or publication again, often with revisions or additional material
