indigo

noun
deep blue dye from plants or synthetic sources
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndɪˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a deep blue dye obtained from certain plants or produced synthetically, used especially for coloring fabrics

Examples

indigo dye cloth
Farmers in India have cultivated plants for indigo production for centuries.
Modern jeans are often colored using synthetic indigo.
The traditional fabric was dyed with indigo to achieve its rich blue color.
