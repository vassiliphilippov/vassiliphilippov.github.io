abrogation

noun
official cancellation or repeal
Pronunciation: / ˌæbrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌabrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the official or legal act of repealing, abolishing, or cancelling a law, agreement, or practice

Examples

abrogation of a law
After much debate, the parliament voted in favor of the abrogation of the controversial law.
His legal team argued that the contract's abrogation was not justified by the circumstances.
The abrogation of the treaty led to renewed tensions between the two countries.
