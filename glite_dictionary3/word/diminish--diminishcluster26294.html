to diminish

verb
make less important; reduce reputation
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem less important, impressive, or valuable; to reduce in authority, dignity, or reputation

Examples

to diminish someone's reputation
Criticism from the press did not diminish the leader's reputation among supporters.
Don't allow others to diminish your sense of self-worth.
His comments were intended to diminish her achievements in the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym – belittle: to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • synonym – deprecate: to express disapproval of or make little of
  • antonym – exalt: to raise someone in rank, power, or character
