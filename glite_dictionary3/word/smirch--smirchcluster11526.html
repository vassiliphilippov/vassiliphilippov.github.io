to smirch

verb
make dirty or stained by smearing
Pronunciation: / smɝtʃ / (AmE), / smɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to make something dirty or stained by smearing; to soil or tarnish

Examples

to smirch clean clothes
His reputation was smirched by false rumors spread online.
She accidentally smirched her dress with greasy hands while cooking.

Be careful not to smirch your clothes while painting the fence.
The mistake was minor, but it threatened to smirch his professional image.
The politician's rivals tried to smirch her reputation with false rumors.
