flashy

adjective
bright and conspicuous in a showy way
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃi / (AmE), / ˈflæʃi / (BrE)
attracting attention by being bright, showy, or extravagant, often in a way that is considered tasteless

Examples

She preferred elegant, simple clothing rather than flashy dresses covered in sequins.
The car's flashy paint job made it stand out from all the others on the street.
His flashy gold watch was meant to impress everyone at the party.
Many people thought the new car design was too flashy for their tastes.
The singer wore a flashy outfit covered in sequins and bright colors.
