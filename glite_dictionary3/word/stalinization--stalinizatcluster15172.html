Stalinization

noun
the adoption of Stalin's policies and practices
Pronunciation: / ˌstɑːlɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the adoption or imposition of the policies and practices associated with Joseph Stalin, especially involving centralized control, repression, and the spread of Soviet-style communism

Examples

forced Stalinization of countries
After the war, Stalinization was implemented in several countries by force.
Intellectuals often suffered under the Stalinization of cultural institutions.
The rapid Stalinization of Eastern Europe altered political landscapes across the continent.

Related Terms

  • antonym – de-Stalinization: the process of eliminating the influence of Stalin and his policies
