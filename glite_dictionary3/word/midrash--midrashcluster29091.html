Midrash

noun
Jewish scriptural commentary
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.rɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.raʃ / (BrE)
a traditional Jewish commentary or interpretation of parts of the Hebrew Scriptures, often attached to the biblical text

Examples

classic Midrash story
During our studies, we discussed how the Midrash influences modern Jewish thought.
Many rabbis refer to the Midrash to help explain difficult passages in the Torah.
The Midrash often adds stories and interpretations that are not found in the original biblical text.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Talmud: the central text of Rabbinic Judaism consisting of the Mishnah and Gemara
  • synonym – commentary: an explanatory series of comments or annotations, especially on a text
