deadly

adjective
causing spiritual death or loss of grace
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdli / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli / (BrE)
(in religious context) causing spiritual death or loss of divine grace; regarded as extremely serious or mortal in the eyes of a religious tradition.

Examples

deadly sin
According to church doctrine, pride is considered a deadly sin.
In medieval theology, jealousy was classified as a deadly offense against divine grace.
She confessed to committing one of the deadly sins during her youth.

Related Terms

  • antonym – venial: (of a sin) regarded as less serious and pardonable
