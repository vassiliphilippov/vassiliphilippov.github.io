touchy

adjective
sensitive or delicate (situation)
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌtʃi / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃi / (BrE)
(of a subject or situation) requiring careful handling due to sensitivity or potential to cause disagreement or offense; delicate.

Examples

touchy subject
He avoided mentioning the touchy topic of politics during the meeting.
It's a touchy subject, so we should discuss it carefully and with respect.
Negotiating the contract proved to be a touchy process for everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delicate: requiring careful treatment; sensitive or fragile
  • synonym – ticklish: difficult to deal with; requiring caution
