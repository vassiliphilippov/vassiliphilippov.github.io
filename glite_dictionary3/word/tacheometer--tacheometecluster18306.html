tacheometer

noun
surveying instrument for rapid measurement
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːkiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌtækiˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a surveying instrument resembling a theodolite, designed to make rapid measurements of distance, direction, and elevation

Examples

tacheometer survey work
A tacheometer allows for the simultaneous measurement of horizontal and vertical angles.
Modern surveying tools often include a digital tacheometer for enhanced accuracy and speed.
The surveyor used a tacheometer to quickly determine the distance between the two points.
