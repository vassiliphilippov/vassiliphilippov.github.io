wheel

noun
driving force or mechanism (metaphoric)
Pronunciation: / wiːl / (AmE), / wiːl / (BrE)
a person, group, or force that provides the driving energy and direction for an organization or process; a controlling power (used metaphorically)

Examples

driving wheel of change
Change will only happen when the right wheel is set in motion within the organization.
He was a key wheel behind the new marketing strategy.
The wheels of government can turn slowly after an election.
