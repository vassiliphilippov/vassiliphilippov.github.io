whip

noun
party official enforcing legislative discipline
Pronunciation: / wɪp / (AmE), / wɪp / (BrE)
a legislator appointed by a political party to ensure party discipline, enforce attendance, and secure votes within a legislative body

Examples

party whip
As a whip, she was responsible for maintaining party discipline in Parliament.
Several MPs were persuaded by the whip to support the government's position.
The chief whip asked all members to attend the vote on the bill.

Related Terms

  • antonym – backbencher: a legislator who does not hold governmental office and is not a frontbench spokesperson
  • synonym – party leader: the head official of a political party within a legislative body
