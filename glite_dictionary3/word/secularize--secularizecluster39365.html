to secularize

verb
transfer from religious to civil control
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkjələrˌaɪz /; / ˈsɛkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to make something non-religious or remove it from religious control or influence

Examples

to secularize a church
Many monasteries were secularized in the eighteenth century, transferring their land to the state.
The government decided to secularize several church-owned schools during the reform.
The movement aimed to secularize health care, taking hospitals out of religious control.
Many historic churches have been secularized and turned into museums.
Reforms in the country aimed to secularize its laws and institutions.
The new government set out to secularize the nation's education system.
