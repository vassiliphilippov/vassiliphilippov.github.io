madrona

noun
Pacific coastal evergreen tree with reddish berries and bark
Pronunciation: / məˈdroʊnə / (AmE), / məˈdrəʊnə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, with glossy leathery leaves, orange-red edible berries, and reddish bark; its wood is used in furniture and the bark in tanning.

Examples

madrona tree bark
The madrona tree's red bark peels away in thin sheets, revealing smooth wood underneath.
The unique hardness of madrona wood makes it valuable for furniture making.
Wildlife often feed on the orange-red berries of the madrona during the fall.
