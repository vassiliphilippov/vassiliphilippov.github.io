rollover

noun
transfer of retirement funds to another plan
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the transfer of funds from one pension plan or retirement account to another without incurring a tax penalty

Examples

rollover a retirement account
After leaving her job, she opted for a rollover to move her 401(k) into an IRA.
The financial advisor explained how a rollover works for employer-sponsored retirement plans.
You must complete the rollover within 60 days to avoid taxes and penalties.
