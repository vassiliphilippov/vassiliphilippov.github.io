cochin

noun
breed of large Asian chicken
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.tʃɪn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.tʃɪn / (BrE)
An Asian breed of large domestic chicken characterized by dense plumage and feathered legs, valued for its ornamental and meat qualities.

Examples

cochin chicken breed
A single cochin hen can produce a considerable number of large brown eggs each season.
Many poultry shows feature cochin breeds in the ornamental category.
The farmer kept a flock of cochin chickens for their impressive feathering and calm temperament.
