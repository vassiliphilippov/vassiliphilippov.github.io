to ordinate

verb
appoint to a religious or clerical office
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪˌneɪt / (BrE)
to appoint someone to a clerical or religious office, especially as a priest or minister

Examples

to ordinate a priest
After years of study, she was finally ordinated as a minister in her church.
Many families attended the ceremony where their loved one was ordinated.
The bishop will ordinate three new priests this weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ordain: to make someone a priest or minister by a special ceremony
