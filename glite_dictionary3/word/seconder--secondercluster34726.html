seconder

noun
person who supports a formal motion
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛk.ənd.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ənd.ə / (BrE)
a person who formally supports a proposal or motion so that it can be discussed or voted on

Examples

seconder of a motion
She agreed to act as seconder for the new policy resolution.
The chairman asked if there was a seconder for the motion.
Without a seconder, the proposal could not be debated.

Related Terms

  • antonym – proposer: person who formally puts forward a motion or proposal
