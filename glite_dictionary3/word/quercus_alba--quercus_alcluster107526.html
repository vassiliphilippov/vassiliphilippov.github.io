Quercus alba

noun
species of white oak tree
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜːrkəs ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈalba / (BrE)
A large, slow-growing deciduous tree of eastern North America, known as the white oak, with stout spreading branches and leaves usually having seven rounded lobes; valued for its strong, durable hardwood.
