retractable

adjective
able to be drawn back or withdrawn
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtræktəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈtræktəbl / (BrE)
able to be drawn back, withdrawn, or pulled in when not in use.

Examples

retractable leash
Our dog leash is retractable, letting the dog roam further when it's safe.
Some ballpoint pens are retractable, so you can protect the tip when not writing.
The airplane is equipped with retractable landing gear for better aerodynamics during flight.

  • antonym – extendable: able to be extended
