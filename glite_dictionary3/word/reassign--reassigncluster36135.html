to reassign

verb
transfer to a new role or location at work
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈsaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to transfer someone to a different job, position, or place of work within an organization

Examples

to reassign an employee
After the project ended, he was reassigned to the company’s headquarters in London.
Due to staff shortages, several nurses were reassigned to different wards last month.
The manager decided to reassign her to a new department to develop her skills.
