nimbus

noun
a rain-bearing dark cloud
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪm.bəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪm.bəs / (BrE)
A dark grey cloud that brings rain; a type of rain cloud.

Examples

dark nimbus cloud
A dark nimbus hung over the city, threatening a heavy downpour by evening.
Artists often depict gods with a glowing nimbus, but here it refers to a rain cloud.
As the nimbus swept in, the temperature dropped and rain began to fall.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cumulonimbus: a dense, towering, vertical cloud associated with thunderstorms and heavy precipitation
  • synonym – stratus: a low-level cloud forming a uniform, grey layer, often bringing light rain or drizzle
