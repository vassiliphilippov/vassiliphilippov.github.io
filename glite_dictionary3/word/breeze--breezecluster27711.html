to breeze

verb
to move quickly and easily
Pronunciation: / bɹiz / (AmE), / bɹiːz / (BrE)
to move or proceed quickly and easily; to accomplish something with little difficulty.

Examples

to breeze through work
He breezed into the room as if he owned the place.
She managed to breeze through the interview without any nervousness.
The team breezed past their opponents to reach the finals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sail: to move smoothly and easily
  • antonym – struggle: to proceed with difficulty or effort
