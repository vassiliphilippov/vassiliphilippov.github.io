stall

noun
delaying or misleading tactic
Pronunciation: / stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
a deliberate tactic used to mislead someone or to delay an action or decision

Examples

clever stall
He used a clever stall to keep the negotiations going.
Her excuse was just a stall to buy more time.
The coach called a timeout as a stall to disrupt the opposing team's momentum.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delay: the act of causing something to happen later than planned
  • synonym – prevarication: the action of evading the truth; delaying or misleading
