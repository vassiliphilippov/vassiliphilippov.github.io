vulcanisation

noun
process of hardening rubber with sulphur
Pronunciation: / ˌvʌlkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of treating rubber or rubberlike materials with sulphur at high temperatures to improve elasticity and strength or to harden them

Examples

vulcanisation of rubber
Charles Goodyear is credited with inventing the process of vulcanisation in the 19th century.
During vulcanisation, sulphur is added to raw rubber, making it more resilient and elastic.
The vulcanisation of rubber revolutionised the tire industry and increased the durability of many products.
