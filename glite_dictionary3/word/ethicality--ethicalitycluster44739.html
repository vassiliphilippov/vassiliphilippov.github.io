ethicality

noun
quality of being ethical
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌiːθɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being ethical; conformity to accepted moral standards of conduct.

Examples

ethicality of actions
She questioned the ethicality of the company's advertising practices.
The ethicality of using animals in research is a serious concern for many scientists.
There is an ongoing debate about the ethicality of genetic engineering in humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym – immorality: the state of not conforming to moral standards
  • synonym – morality: principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong
