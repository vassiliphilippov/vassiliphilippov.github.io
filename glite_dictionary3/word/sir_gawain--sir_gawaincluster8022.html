Sir Gawain

proper noun
Arthurian knight; Arthur's nephew
Pronunciation: / sɚ ɡəˈweɪn / (AmE), / sə ɡəˈweɪn / (BrE)
A nephew of King Arthur and one of the knights of the Round Table in Arthurian legend, renowned for his bravery and courtesy.

Examples

Sir Gawain accepts challenge
Sir Gawain accepted the Green Knight's challenge when no one else dared to speak.
In many stories, Sir Gawain is praised for his courtesy and sense of honor.
Legends recount Sir Gawain's unwavering loyalty to King Arthur and his court.

  • synonym – Sir Lancelot: Arthurian knight known for his affair with Queen Guinevere
  • antonym – Sir Mordred: Arthurian knight, often depicted as Arthur's traitorous nephew
