reverberance

noun
quality of being resonant or echoing
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrbɚəns / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbərəns / (BrE)
The quality of having a loud, deep, and resonant sound, often with echoes or repeated reverberations.

Examples

deep reverberance
She was struck by the reverberance of his voice as it filled the hall.
The reverberance of the cathedral's acoustics made every note linger in the air.
The empty gym had a peculiar reverberance that made footsteps sound louder.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dullness: lacking resonance or brightness in sound
  • synonym – resonance: the quality of being deep, full, and reverberating
