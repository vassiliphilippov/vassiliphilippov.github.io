naturalization

noun
introduction of non-native species
Pronunciation: / ˌnætʃərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃrəlʌɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
the process of introducing animals or plants to a new region where they are not native, allowing them to establish and thrive

Examples

naturalization of species
Ecologists monitor the naturalization of non-indigenous fish in local rivers.
Several invasive plant species spread rapidly after their naturalization in North America.
The naturalization of rabbits in Australia has had a major impact on the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – eradication: the complete removal of a species from an area
  • synonym – introduction: the act of bringing a species into a new area
