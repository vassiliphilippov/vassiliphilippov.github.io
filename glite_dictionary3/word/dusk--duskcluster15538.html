dusk

noun
time just after sunset
Pronunciation: / dʌsk / (AmE), / dʌsk / (BrE)
The period of time just after the sun has set and before it becomes completely dark.

Examples

at dusk birds sing
Birds often become more active at dusk as they prepare to roost for the night.
The streetlights flickered on as dusk settled over the city.
We took a long walk at dusk and watched the sky turn deep blue.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dawn: the time of day when light first appears, before sunrise
  • synonym – twilight: the time between sunset and darkness
