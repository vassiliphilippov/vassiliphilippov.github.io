Madame Curie

proper noun
Nobel-winning scientist; pioneer of radioactivity
Pronunciation: / mæˌdæm ˈkjʊri / (AmE), / ˌmad(ə)m ˈkjʊəri / (BrE)
A French chemist and physicist (born in Poland) who won two Nobel prizes for her work on radioactivity and the discoveries of radium and polonium (1867–1934).

Examples

Madame Curie discovered radium
Madame Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize in any field.
Many universities have buildings named after Madame Curie to honor her achievements.
The work of Madame Curie revolutionized our understanding of radioactivity.
