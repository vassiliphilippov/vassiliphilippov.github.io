corkage

noun
charge for bringing your own wine to a restaurant
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.kɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.kɪdʒ / (BrE)
a charge made by a restaurant or similar establishment for serving wine that a customer has brought, rather than purchased on the premises

Examples

corkage fee
Before making a reservation, ask about their corkage policy.
The restaurant charges a corkage fee if you bring your own wine.
We enjoyed a nice dinner and paid a small corkage to have our favorite bottle served.
