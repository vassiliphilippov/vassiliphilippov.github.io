trainload

noun
amount carried by a train
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪnˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnləʊd / (BrE)
an amount of goods or materials that can be carried by a single train

Examples

a trainload of coal
It took three trainloads of steel to supply the new construction project.
The factory received a trainload of coal every week during the winter.
They shipped the harvested grain by the trainload to the processing plant.
