backstop

noun
the catcher (baseball position)
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstɒp / (BrE)
(Baseball) The catcher, the player who positions behind home plate to catch pitches not hit by the batter.

Examples

skilled backstop catcher
As a skilled backstop, he prevented many wild pitches from reaching the back fence.
The rookie backstop showed impressive reflexes behind home plate.
The team's starting backstop was sidelined with a shoulder injury.
