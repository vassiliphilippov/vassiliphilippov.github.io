to deputize

verb
appoint as substitute or representative
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛpjəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛpjʊtʌɪz / (BrE)
to appoint someone as a substitute or give someone the power to act on behalf of another, especially officially or temporarily

Examples

to deputize an assistant
In his absence, he will deputize his assistant to run the meeting.
She was deputized to represent her manager at the conference.
The sheriff decided to deputize several townspeople during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appoint: to choose someone for a job or position
