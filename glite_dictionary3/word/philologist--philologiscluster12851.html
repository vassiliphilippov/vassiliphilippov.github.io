philologist

noun
scholar of language and classical texts
Pronunciation: / fɪˈlɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scholar who studies language in written historical sources, often with a focus on classical or ancient languages and texts

Examples

philologist studies texts
As a philologist, she contributed to our understanding of Latin literature.
Many philologists debate the true meaning of texts written during the Middle Ages.
The philologist spent many years studying ancient Greek manuscripts.
