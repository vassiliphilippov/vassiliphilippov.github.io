hydrodynamics

noun
study of the motion of fluids
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of science concerned with the forces acting on or exerted by fluids in motion

Examples

hydrodynamics of water flow
Advanced knowledge of hydrodynamics is necessary to design efficient marine vessels.
She is a professor specializing in hydrodynamics at the university.
The simulation software can model the complex hydrodynamics of ocean currents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fluid dynamics: the study of the movement of liquids and gases
  • antonym – hydrostatics: the branch of science concerned with fluids at rest
