to polemicise

verb
engage in heated argument or controversy
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɛmɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to engage in a heated argument or controversy, especially on political or intellectual issues

Examples

to polemicise about politics
He tended to polemicise in his writing, challenging prevailing views with fierce arguments.
Instead of seeking common ground, she chose to polemicise on divisive topics in her column.
The professors would often polemicise over the finer points of philosophy during their meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agree: to have the same opinion
  • synonym – argue: to give reasons for or against something
  • synonym – debate: to discuss or argue about something formally
