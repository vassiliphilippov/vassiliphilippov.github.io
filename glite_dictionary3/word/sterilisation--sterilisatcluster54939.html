sterilisation

noun
process of eliminating all microorganisms
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɛrɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something free from all living bacteria and other microorganisms, usually by using heat or chemicals

Examples

sterilisation of equipment
Proper sterilisation ensures that laboratory equipment is free from contamination.
The sterilisation of surgical instruments is crucial before any operation.
They use high-pressure steam for the sterilisation of glassware in the research facility.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – disinfection: the process of reducing harmful microorganisms to a safe level, but not necessarily all
  • synonym – sterilization: the process of eliminating all microorganisms (American spelling)
