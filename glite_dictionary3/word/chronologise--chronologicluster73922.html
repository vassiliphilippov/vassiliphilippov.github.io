to chronologise

verb
arrange events in time order
Pronunciation: / krəˈnɑːlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / krəˈnɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or establish the order of events in accordance with their occurrence in time

Examples

to chronologise historical events
It was difficult to chronologise the ancient artifacts with limited data available.
Researchers worked to chronologise the discoveries made during the expedition.
The historian attempted to chronologise every significant event of the 19th century.
