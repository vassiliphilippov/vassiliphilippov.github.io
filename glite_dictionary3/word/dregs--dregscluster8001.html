dregs

noun
sediment at the bottom of a liquid
Pronunciation: / drɛɡz / (AmE), / drɛɡz / (BrE)
the small solid particles that settle at the bottom of a liquid, especially after brewing or fermentation

Examples

dregs of coffee
After finishing his coffee, he noticed the dregs collected at the bottom of the cup.
The wine was cloudy, with dregs left after fermentation.
They poured out the tea, leaving the bitter dregs behind.
