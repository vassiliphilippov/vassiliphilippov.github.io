risk-free

adjective
without risk; completely safe
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪsk fri / (AmE), / ˈrɪsk friː / (BrE)
Completely safe and not involving any risk or chance of loss.

Examples

risk-free investment
He chose the risk-free route, preferring safety over potential gains.
Investing in government bonds is often considered a risk-free option for conservative investors.
No investment is truly risk-free, but some have much lower risks than others.

Related Terms

  • antonym – risky: involving the possibility of loss or danger
  • synonym – safe: not likely to cause harm or loss
