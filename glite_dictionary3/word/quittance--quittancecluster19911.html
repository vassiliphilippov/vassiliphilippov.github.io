quittance

noun
formal receipt releasing from a debt or claim
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪtns / (AmE), / ˈkwɪt.əns / (BrE)
a formal document or receipt certifying that a person has been released from an obligation, debt, or claim

Examples

quittance document received
He kept the quittance as proof that the debt had been settled.
The landlord provided a quittance after all outstanding rent was paid.
Without a quittance, she could not confirm that her loan was fully repaid.

Related Terms

  • synonym – receipt: a written acknowledgement of receiving something, such as money or goods
