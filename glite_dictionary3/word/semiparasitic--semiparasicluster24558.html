semiparasitic

adjective
partially parasitic (of plants)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) partially parasitic, especially in obtaining water or nutrients from another plant but also able to photosynthesize.

Examples

semiparasitic plant species
Many mistletoes are considered semiparasitic because they can photosynthesize but still rely on their host plants for water and nutrients.
Some plants display a semiparasitic relationship, drawing sustenance from hosts without being entirely dependent.
The semiparasitic nature of rhinanthus makes it unique among wildflowers in meadows.
