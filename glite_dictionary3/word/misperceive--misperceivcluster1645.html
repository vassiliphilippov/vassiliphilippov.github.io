to misperceive

verb
perceive incorrectly
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪspərˈsiv / (AmE), / ˌmɪspəˈsiːv / (BrE)
to perceive or interpret something incorrectly; to misunderstand what one sees or senses

Examples

to misperceive a situation
If you stand at the end of the pool, you might misperceive the depth of the water.
It's easy to misperceive someone's intentions when communication is unclear.
She tends to misperceive criticism as a personal attack, even when it's meant to help.
