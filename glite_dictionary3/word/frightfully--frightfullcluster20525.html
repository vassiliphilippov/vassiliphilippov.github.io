frightfully

adverb
very; extremely (emphasizer)
Pronunciation: / ˈfraɪtfəli / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtfəli / (BrE)
(especially British English) used to emphasize the degree of something, similar to 'very' or 'extremely'.

Examples

frightfully cold outside
It was frightfully cold last night, and the pipes nearly froze.
She looked frightfully pale after hearing the shocking news.
The food at that restaurant was frightfully expensive, so we decided not to eat there.

  • antonym – slightly: to a small degree
  • synonym – terribly: used as an intensifier, meaning 'very' or 'extremely'
