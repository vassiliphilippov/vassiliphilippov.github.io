Fenusa pusilla

noun
species of sawfly affecting birch trees
Pronunciation: / fəˈnuːsə pjuːˈsɪlə / (AmE), / fɛˈnjuːsə pjuːˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A small black sawfly native to Europe, now established in eastern United States, whose larvae mine birch leaves and can cause significant defoliation.
