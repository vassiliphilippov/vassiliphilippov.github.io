Hagada

noun
non-legal Talmudic tradition
Pronunciation: / həˈɡɑːdə / (AmE), / həˈɡɑːdə / (BrE)
the sections of Talmudic literature comprising stories, legends, and ethical teachings, as distinct from legal writings

Examples

study the Hagada stories
Scholars study the Hagada to understand the cultural and spiritual life of ancient Jewish communities.
The rabbi often included stories from the Hagada in his sermons to illustrate moral lessons.
Unlike Halacha, which prescribes legal rules, the Hagada focuses on folklore and ethics.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Halacha: the body of Jewish law contained in the Talmud and other texts
