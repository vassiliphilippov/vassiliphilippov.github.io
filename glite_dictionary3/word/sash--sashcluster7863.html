sash

noun
window frame holding the panes
Pronunciation: / sæʃ / (AmE), / sæʃ / (BrE)
a framework, often movable, that holds the panes of glass in a window and fits into the window frame

Examples

sash window frame
Each sash in the double-hung window can slide up and down independently.
He painted the sash to match the rest of the window frame.
The old house still had its original sash windows, complete with delicate wooden frames.

Related Terms

  • synonym – casement: a window that is attached to its frame by hinges
