fraternisation

noun
friendly association, especially with an enemy
Pronunciation: / fræˌtɜrnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / frəˌtɜːnɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of associating with others in a friendly or brotherly way, especially with members of an opposing side or enemy

Examples

fraternisation with enemy soldiers
Military rules often forbid fraternisation between officers and enemy soldiers during wartime.
Rumors of fraternisation between rival football fans worried the organizers before the final match.
The company discouraged fraternisation between management and staff outside the workplace.
