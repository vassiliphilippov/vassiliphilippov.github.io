to conduct

verb
to lead or guide someone somewhere
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to lead or guide someone to a particular place, especially in a formal or careful manner

Examples

to conduct guests
An attendant conducted the visitors to their seats before the ceremony started.
The guide conducted us through the ancient ruins, explaining their history along the way.
The manager conducted the applicants to the conference room for the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym – escort: to go with someone to protect or guide them
  • antonym – follow: to go after or behind someone
  • synonym – lead: to go with or guide someone to a place
