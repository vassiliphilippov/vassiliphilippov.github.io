commedia dell'arte

noun
Italian improvised theatre of 16th–18th centuries
Pronunciation: / koʊˌmeɪdiə dɛˈlɑrteɪ / (AmE), / kɒˌmeɪdiə dɛˈlɑːteɪ / (BrE)
a form of Italian theatre from the 16th to 18th centuries, featuring improvised performances based on stock characters and scenarios

Examples

classic commedia dell'arte performance

Stock characters like Harlequin and Pantalone originated from commedia dell'arte.
The influence of commedia dell'arte can still be seen in contemporary comedy and theatre.
