to narcotise

verb
administer narcotics to induce sleep or relieve pain
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːrkəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkətaɪz / (BrE)
to administer a narcotic drug to someone, especially to induce sleep or relieve pain

Examples

to narcotise a patient
Emergency teams may narcotise victims who have suffered severe trauma.
He was narcotised to keep him calm during the surgery.
The doctor decided to narcotise the patient before the painful procedure.
