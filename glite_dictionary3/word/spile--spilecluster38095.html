spile

noun
support column driven into the ground
Pronunciation: / spaɪl / (AmE), / spaɪl / (BrE)
a column or stake of wood, steel, or concrete driven into the ground to support a structure or retain earth

Examples

spile supports foundation
The engineer recommended inserting a spile to stabilize the bridge foundation.
Without a strong spile, the retaining wall would not have held against the shifting soil.
Workers drove a steel spile deep into the riverbed to support the pier.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pile: a heavy beam or post driven vertically into the bed of a river, soft ground, etc., to support a structure
