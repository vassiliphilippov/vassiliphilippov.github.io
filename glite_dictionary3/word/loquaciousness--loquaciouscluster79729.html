loquaciousness

noun
the quality of being talkative
Pronunciation: / loʊˈkweɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ləˈkweɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very talkative or tending to talk a great deal

Examples

her loquaciousness at parties
Despite her loquaciousness, she always listened carefully when others spoke.
He was criticized for his loquaciousness during meetings, often speaking at length on every topic.
Her loquaciousness made her the center of attention at every party.

Related Terms

  • synonym – loquacity: talkativeness; the tendency to talk a lot
  • antonym – reticence: the quality of being reserved or not revealing thoughts or feelings readily
