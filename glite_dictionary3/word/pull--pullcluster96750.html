to pull

verb
to restrain a horse from winning a race
Pronunciation: / pʊl / (AmE), / pʊl / (BrE)
(of a jockey or driver) to rein in (a horse) so as to prevent it from winning a race, usually dishonestly.

Examples

to pull a horse
If a driver tries to pull a horse, they risk severe penalties.
The racing commission investigated whether he had intentionally pulled his mount.
There were suspicions that the jockey might pull the horse during the final lap.
