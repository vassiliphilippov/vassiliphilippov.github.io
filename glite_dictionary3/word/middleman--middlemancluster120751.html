middleman

noun
wholesaler or intermediary in trade
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əlˌmæn /; / ˈmɪdəlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl.mæn /; / ˈmɪdəlˌman / (BrE)
a person or company that acts as an intermediary, arranging deals or buying and reselling goods between producers and buyers

Examples

middleman in a deal
Acting as a middleman, she bought goods in bulk and resold them to small businesses.
Many farmers rely on a middleman to distribute their produce to grocery stores.
The company eliminated the middleman to sell products directly to customers.
He acted as a middleman to help negotiate the contract between the two companies.
If you need an introduction, I can put you in touch with a reliable middleman.
Large companies often prefer to deal directly with suppliers, cutting out the middleman.

Related Terms

  • antonym – client: a person who receives assistance or services, as opposed to the provider
  • synonym – go-between: a person who acts as an intermediary between two people or groups
  • synonym – intermediary: a person or organization acting as a link between people or groups
  • antonym – retailer: a person or business that sells goods to the public
  • synonym – wholesaler: a person or company selling goods in large quantities at low prices to retailers
