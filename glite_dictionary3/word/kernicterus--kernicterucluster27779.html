kernicterus

noun
brain damage in newborns due to bilirubin
Pronunciation: / kɝːˈnɪktərəs / (AmE), / kɜːˈnɪktərəs / (BrE)
A rare type of brain damage in newborns caused by the accumulation of bilirubin (a bile pigment) in the brain tissue, leading to yellow staining and injury.

Examples

kernicterus in newborns
Cases of kernicterus have become rare due to better screening and management of bilirubin levels in infants.
Prompt treatment of newborn jaundice is vital to prevent complications like kernicterus.
The baby was diagnosed with kernicterus after showing signs of severe jaundice and neurological damage.
