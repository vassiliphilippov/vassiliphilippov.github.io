to enable

verb
make able; provide means or power
Pronunciation: / ɪˈneɪbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪbl̩ / (BrE)
to make someone or something able to do something; to give the necessary means, power, or opportunity

Examples

to enable someone to act
A grant from the government enabled the museum to reopen after renovations.
Better technology is enabling doctors to diagnose diseases earlier than ever before.
This new training program will enable employees to work more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disable: to make unable to function or act
  • synonym – empower: to give someone the authority or power to do something
