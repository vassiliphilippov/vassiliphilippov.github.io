thermojunction

noun
junction of two metals creating voltage
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A junction between two different metals at which a voltage is generated, typically used in thermocouples to measure temperature.

Examples

thermojunction in a thermocouple
A thermojunction forms the core of every thermocouple device.
The sensor relies on a thermojunction to produce a measurable voltage when heated.
Variations in temperature at the thermojunction result in changes in output voltage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thermocouple: a device made of two joined dissimilar metals used to measure temperature
