preamble

noun
introductory statement to a law or constitution
Pronunciation: / priˈæm.bəl / (AmE), / priˈæm.bəl / (BrE)
an introductory statement, especially to a statute or constitution, that explains its purpose and underlying philosophy

Examples

preamble to a constitution
Every new statute in that country includes a preamble explaining the background.
The US Constitution begins with a famous preamble that outlines its purposes.
The committee argued over the wording of the preamble for several days.
