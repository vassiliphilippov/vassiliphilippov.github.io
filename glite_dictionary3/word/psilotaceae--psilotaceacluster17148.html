Psilotaceae

noun
family of primitive, leafless ferns
Pronunciation: / saɪloʊtəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / sɪləʊtəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A small family of primitive, fern-like plants with nearly leafless stems and tiny scalelike leaves, found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.

Examples

Psilotaceae family plants
Botanists have debated the evolutionary position of Psilotaceae among ferns.
Some species within the Psilotaceae family can be found growing in tropical forests.
The Psilotaceae are characterized by their simple stems and lack of true roots.
