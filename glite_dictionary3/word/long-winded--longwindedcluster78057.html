long-winded

adjective
tediously lengthy or verbose
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈwɪndɪd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈwɪndɪd / (BrE)
using or expressed in too many words; tediously lengthy or verbose.

Examples

long-winded speech
His long-winded explanation only made the meeting drag on longer than necessary.
She tends to give long-winded answers when a simple yes or no would suffice.
The professor's lectures were often so long-winded that students struggled to stay focused.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: expressing much in few words
  • synonym – verbose: using more words than needed
