respect

noun
being admired or esteemed
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɛkt /; / rəˈspɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
a feeling or condition of admiration or esteem for someone or something, or courteous regard for others

Examples

respect for teachers
She has earned the respect of her colleagues through years of hard work.
The teacher commanded respect from everyone in the classroom.
Winning the championship brought him great respect in the community.
His honesty earned him the respect of his colleagues.
She lost all respect for him after learning the truth.
Teachers deserve respect from their students in the classroom.
It is important to show respect to people from all walks of life.
She earned the respect of her colleagues through hard work and integrity.
The teacher commands respect in the classroom because of her fairness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – admiration: a feeling of great respect and approval
  • antonym – contempt: lack of respect; a feeling that someone or something is inferior
  • synonym – deference: polite submission and respect
  • antonym – disregard: lack of consideration or respect
  • antonym – disrespect: lack of respect or courtesy
  • synonym – esteem: high regard or respect
