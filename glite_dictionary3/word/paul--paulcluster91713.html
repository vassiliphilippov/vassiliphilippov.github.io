Paul

proper noun
Christian apostle and New Testament writer
Pronunciation: / pɔl / (AmE), / pɔːl / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) a Christian apostle and missionary, originally named Saul, who played a leading role in the early spread of Christianity and authored several Epistles.

Examples

Apostle Paul
Paul traveled widely, preaching the message of Christianity to non-Jews across the Roman Empire.
Although Paul was not one of the original twelve Apostles, he is often considered equally influential.
Many scholars believe that the writings of Paul have had a profound impact on Christian theology.
