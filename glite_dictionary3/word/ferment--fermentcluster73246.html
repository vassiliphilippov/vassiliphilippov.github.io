to ferment

verb
cause to undergo fermentation
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to cause or undergo fermentation (a chemical change, especially in food or drink, often making alcohol or causing souring)

Examples

to ferment grapes
Some foods, such as kimchi, ferment naturally due to the bacteria present.
The winery will ferment the grape juice for several weeks to create a rich flavor.
They ferment soybeans to make traditional Japanese miso paste.
If left unrefrigerated, the juice will quickly ferment and become undrinkable.
If milk ferments, it develops a sour taste and must be discarded.
The cream began to ferment after sitting out on the counter for hours.
