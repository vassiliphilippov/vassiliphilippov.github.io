debilitating

adjective
causing serious weakness or loss of strength
Pronunciation: / dɪˈbɪlɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈbɪlɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing a serious weakness or loss of strength and energy, often to the extent of interfering with normal activity.

Examples

debilitating illness
Chronic pain can be debilitating, affecting every aspect of a person's daily life.
The country faced a debilitating drought that damaged crops and affected millions.
The disease had a debilitating effect on her health, leaving her bedridden for months.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enervating: causing someone to feel drained of energy
  • antonym – invigorating: making someone feel stronger or more energetic
