musculus adductor brevis

noun
short inner thigh muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskjələs ædˈdʌktər ˈbrɛvɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs adˈdʌktə ˈbrɛvɪs / (BrE)
a short muscle in the inner thigh that draws the leg inward toward the body

Examples

musculus adductor brevis injury
An injury to the musculus adductor brevis can cause pain on the inside of the thigh.
Physical therapists often assess the strength of the musculus adductor brevis after a groin strain.
The musculus adductor brevis plays a key role in bringing the thighs together during movement.
