vocation

noun
a person's occupation or calling
Pronunciation: / voʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of suitability for a particular career or occupation, especially one to which a person is specially drawn or trained

Examples

true vocation in teaching
After years of study, she finally found her true vocation as a teacher.
He feels that nursing is more than just a job—it's his vocation.
Many people struggle to discover their vocation in life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – avocation: a hobby or minor occupation, something done in addition to one's main job
  • synonym – profession: a paid occupation, especially one that requires advanced education and training
