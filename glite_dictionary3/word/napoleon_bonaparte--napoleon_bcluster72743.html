Napoleon Bonaparte

proper noun
French military leader and emperor (1769–1821)
Pronunciation: / nəˌpoʊliˌoʊn ˈbɒnəˌpɑrt / (AmE), / nəˌpəʊliˈəʊn ˈbɒnəˌpɑːt / (BrE)
(1769–1821) French military leader and emperor who rose to prominence during the French Revolution and ruled much of Europe in the early 19th century.

Examples

Napoleon Bonaparte led France
Napoleon Bonaparte established the Napoleonic Code, which influenced legal systems worldwide.
Many artists and writers have depicted Napoleon Bonaparte in their works, fascinated by his rise and fall.
The defeat of Napoleon Bonaparte at Waterloo marked the end of his reign.
