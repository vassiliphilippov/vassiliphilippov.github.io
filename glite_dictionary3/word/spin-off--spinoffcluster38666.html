spin-off

noun
derivative company, product, or media work
Pronunciation: / spɪn ˈɔf / (AmE), / spɪn ˈɒf / (BrE)
a new company, product, television programme, or other work that is developed from an existing one, often to use its technology, characters, or market appeal.

Examples

spin-off show
The tech company created a successful spin-off to market a new security product globally.
Fans eagerly watched the new spin-off that focused on a supporting character's life after the finale.
The university formed a research spin-off to commercialize the laboratory's renewable energy technology products.

Related Terms

  • synonym – derivative: a product or work based on another existing work
  • synonym – offshoot: something that has developed from something larger or earlier
  • antonym – original: the primary or first work or product from which a spin-off is derived
