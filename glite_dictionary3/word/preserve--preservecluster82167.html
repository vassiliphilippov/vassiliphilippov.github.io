to preserve

verb
prevent food from decaying
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
to prevent food from decaying or spoiling, especially by treating it with special substances or procedures

Examples

to preserve food
Farmers often preserve their harvest to ensure food is available in winter.
Salt was commonly used to preserve meat before refrigerators existed.
You can preserve fruit by turning it into jam or jelly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – spoil: (of food) to become unfit for eating; to rot or decay
