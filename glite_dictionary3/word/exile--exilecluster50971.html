exile

noun
forced removal from one's native country
Pronunciation: / ˈɛɡ.zaɪl, ˈɛk.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪl, ˈɛɡzaɪl / (BrE)
the state or act of being expelled from one's native country, typically as a punishment or for political reasons

Examples

exile from home
After the revolution, he faced exile and was forced to live abroad for years.
Many artists produced their most influential work while in exile.
The threat of exile was used to silence political dissent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – banishment: the act of ordering someone to leave and stay away from a place
  • synonym – deportation: the act of expelling a foreigner from a country
  • synonym – expatriation: the act of banishing a person from their native country
