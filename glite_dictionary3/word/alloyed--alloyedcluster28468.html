alloyed

adjective
(of metal) mixed with inferior element
Pronunciation: / əˈlɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈlɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a metal) mixed with another, often inferior, substance to reduce its purity or value.

Examples

alloyed with cheaper metal
Coins are often alloyed to make them more durable and less expensive.
The engineer explained that alloyed metals can lose some of their original value.
The gold in this ring is alloyed with copper to increase its strength.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unalloyed: (of a metal) not mixed with any other substance; pure
