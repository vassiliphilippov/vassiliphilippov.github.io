front

noun
outward appearance to conceal feelings
Pronunciation: / frʌnt / (AmE), / frʌnt / (BrE)
a false or outward appearance used to hide true feelings or intentions, or a person who serves as a cover for something secret or illegal

Examples

a brave front
Despite her worries, she put on a brave front for her friends.
He maintained a cheerful front, even though he was struggling inside.
Underneath his confident front, he was actually quite nervous.
Everyone suspected he was merely a front for the criminal organization.
The company was just a front for laundering money.
The real boss remained hidden, using a front to conduct business.
