to glaze

verb
to coat food with a sweet, shiny layer
Pronunciation: / ɡleɪz / (AmE), / ɡleɪz / (BrE)
to cover a surface with a smooth, shiny liquid that dries hard, especially on food or ceramics

Examples

to glaze a cake
After baking, let the cake cool before you glaze it with chocolate.
She learned how to glaze donuts so they have a perfect, glossy finish.
The chef will glaze the fruit tart with apricot jam for extra shine.
Before baking, you should glaze the scones with a thin layer of milk.
She carefully glazed the pottery to give it a glossy finish.
The chef likes to glaze the ham with honey before baking it.
