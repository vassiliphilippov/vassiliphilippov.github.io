stamen

noun
male organ of a flower
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪmən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪmən / (BrE)
the male reproductive organ of a flower, typically consisting of a filament and an anther in which pollen is produced

Examples

stamen of a flower
Bees collect pollen from the stamen of many different flowers.
If you examine a rose closely, you can easily spot each stamen surrounding the central pistil.
The lily's stamen is covered with yellow pollen.
