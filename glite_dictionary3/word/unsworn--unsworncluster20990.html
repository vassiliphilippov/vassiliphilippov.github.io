unsworn

adjective
not subject to an oath or affirmation
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈswɔrn / (AmE), / ʌnˈswɔːn / (BrE)
(of a statement, person, etc.) not made or bound by an oath, especially before a court of law.

Examples

unsworn statement in court
Her statement was considered unsworn evidence and carried less legal weight.
The unsworn witness answered the questions freely in the courtroom.
The judge explained that unsworn affidavits are not admissible in this case.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sworn: having taken an oath; bound by an oath
