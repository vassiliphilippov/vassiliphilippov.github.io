velouté

noun
white sauce made with stock
Pronunciation: / vəˈluːteɪ / (AmE), / vəˈluːteɪ / (BrE)
A white sauce made with stock instead of milk, thickened with a roux, used as a base in classical French cuisine.

Examples

veloute sauce base
A classic velouté serves as a base for many French soups and sauces.
He thickened the sauce with a roux to achieve the perfect velouté consistency.
The chef prepared a delicate velouté to accompany the roasted chicken.
