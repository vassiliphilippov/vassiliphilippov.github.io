insular

adjective
narrow-minded or isolated in outlook
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.sjələr / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
having a narrow or limited outlook; not interested in new or different ideas, cultures, or people

Examples

insular attitude
An insular community often struggles to adapt to change from the outside world.
Growing up in a small village, his views remained quite insular for many years.
The company was criticized for its insular approach to global markets.
His insular attitude made it difficult for him to accept new ideas.
She criticized the organization's insular perspective and urged them to be more open-minded.
The community grew increasingly insular after decades without outside contact.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cosmopolitan: familiar with or at ease in many different countries and cultures
  • antonym – open-minded: willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • synonym – parochial: having a limited or narrow outlook or scope
