to dispossess

verb
to deprive of property or land
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
to deprive someone of the possession of land, property, or other belongings, especially real estate

Examples

to dispossess people of land
Colonial powers often dispossessed local people of their ancestral territories.
The company tried to dispossess tenants who could not pay the increased rent.
The family was dispossessed of their land after the court ruling.
