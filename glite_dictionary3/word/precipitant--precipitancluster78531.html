precipitant

noun
substance causing a precipitate (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tənt / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tənt / (BrE)
A substance or agent that causes a solid (precipitate) to form from a solution during a chemical reaction.

Examples

precipitant in solution
Changing the pH of the mixture can determine which precipitant is most effective.
The lab technician carefully chose the correct precipitant to isolate the desired compound.
When silver nitrate is added to the solution, it acts as a precipitant for chloride ions.
