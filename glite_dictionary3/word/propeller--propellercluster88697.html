propeller

noun
rotating device moving air or water
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɛlər / (AmE), / prəˈpɛlə / (BrE)
A mechanical device with rotating blades that pushes against air or water to move a vehicle such as a boat or aircraft.

Examples

propeller blades spinning
A helicopter's lift is provided by its main propeller.
Engineers designed a more efficient propeller for the new aircraft.
The boat's propeller was damaged by underwater rocks.
