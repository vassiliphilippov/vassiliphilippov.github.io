crampon

noun
spiked device for boots to grip ice or snow
Pronunciation: / ˈkræm.pɑn / (AmE), / ˈkræm.pɒn / (BrE)
an iron or steel framework with spikes, attached to boots to prevent slipping on ice or snow, especially during climbing

Examples

crampon on boots
Modern crampons are lighter and more durable than earlier models.
The mountaineer adjusted his crampon before tackling the icy slope.
Without a crampon, walking on the glacier would have been nearly impossible.
