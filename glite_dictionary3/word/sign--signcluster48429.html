to sign

verb
make sign of the cross for blessing
Pronunciation: / saɪn / (AmE), / saɪn / (BrE)
(in Christian traditions) to make the sign of the cross over someone in order to invoke divine protection or consecrate them.

Examples

to sign the cross
She signed herself as she entered the cathedral.
The bishop signed the congregation to bestow a blessing.
The priest signed each child before they left the church.
