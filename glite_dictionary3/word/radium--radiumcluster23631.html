radium

noun
a radioactive metallic chemical element
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a highly radioactive metallic element that occurs in small amounts in uranium ores, formerly used in luminous paints and medical treatments

Examples

radium in luminous paint
Marie Curie dedicated much of her life to isolating and studying radium.
The discovery of radium revolutionized the field of radiation therapy for cancer treatment.
Watches from the early 20th century often contained radium to make their hands glow in the dark.

Related Terms

  • synonym – radon: a radioactive gas formed by the decay of radium
  • synonym – uranium: a radioactive metallic element used as fuel in nuclear reactors
