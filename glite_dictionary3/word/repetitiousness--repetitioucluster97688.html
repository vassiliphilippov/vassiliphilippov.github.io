repetitiousness

noun
the quality of being repetitious
Pronunciation: / rɛpəˈtɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / rɛpɪˈtɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being repetitious, especially in speech or writing; the tendency to repeat things excessively, often leading to boredom or verbosity.

Examples

avoid repetitiousness in writing
Editors often eliminate repetitiousness from drafts to improve clarity and style.
Some listeners complained about the repetitiousness in the musical arrangement.
The repetitiousness of his speech made it difficult for the audience to stay engaged.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conciseness: the quality of being brief and to the point
  • synonym – verbosity: the quality of using more words than needed
