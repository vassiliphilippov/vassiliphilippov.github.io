abstract

adjective
existing as an idea, not physically
Pronunciation: / ˈæbˌstrækt / (AmE), / ˈæb.strækt / (BrE)
existing as an idea or in thought, not as a physical or practical thing; theoretical

Examples

abstract idea
He struggled to explain the abstract principles behind the scientific theory.
Philosophy often deals with abstract concepts that are difficult to define.
The idea of freedom is an abstract notion that means different things to different people.
His research focused more on abstract theories than on practical applications.
Many philosophy courses require students to engage in abstract reasoning about complex concepts.
The debate soon became too abstract for most participants to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concrete: existing in a material or physical form, not abstract
  • synonym – theoretical: concerned with the theory rather than practice
