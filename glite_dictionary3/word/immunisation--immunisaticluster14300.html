immunisation

noun
process of making immune to disease
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a person or animal is made immune to a disease, especially through inoculation or vaccination.

Examples

immunisation against disease
Many countries require proof of immunisation before children start school.
Routine childhood immunisation helps prevent serious illnesses.
The government's immunisation program has successfully reduced the incidence of measles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – immunization: process of making immune to disease (American English spelling)
  • synonym – vaccination: administration of a vaccine to produce immunity
