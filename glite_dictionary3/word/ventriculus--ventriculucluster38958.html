ventriculus

noun
muscular stomach in birds/reptiles
Pronunciation: / vɛnˈtrɪkjələs / (AmE), / vɛnˈtrɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
the thick-walled, muscular stomach of birds and reptiles, located below the crop and specialized for grinding food

Examples

bird ventriculus
Many seed-eating birds rely on a strong ventriculus to grind hard grains before digestion.
Unlike mammals, birds possess a distinct organ called the ventriculus that aids in breaking down food.
Veterinarians examined the lizard’s ventriculus to diagnose its digestive problems.
