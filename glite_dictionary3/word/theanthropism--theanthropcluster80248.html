theanthropism

noun
doctrine that Jesus was both human and divine
Pronunciation: / θiˈænθɹəˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / θiˈænθɹəˌpɪzəm / (BrE)
(Theology) The doctrine that Jesus Christ was both fully human and fully divine, representing a union of the human and the divine.

Examples

theanthropism in Christology
Some early church debates focused on interpretations of theanthropism and the nature of Christ.
The Council addressed heresies that challenged theanthropism in explaining the relationship between Jesus's divinity and humanity.
The doctrine of theanthropism is central to many branches of Christian theology.
