nongregarious

adjective
not living or growing in groups
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɡrɪˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɡrɪˈɡɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
(of plants or animals) not living or growing in groups or colonies; solitary in habits or growth.

Examples

nongregarious animals
Certain nongregarious insects, like solitary bees, do not form colonies.
Some cacti remain nongregarious, spacing themselves apart rather than growing in clusters.
The nongregarious habits of the bird make it difficult to observe in the wild.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gregarious: (of people, animals, or plants) living in groups or communities
