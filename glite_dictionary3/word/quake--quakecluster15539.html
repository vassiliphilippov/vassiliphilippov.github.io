to quake

verb
shake or tremble rapidly
Pronunciation: / kweɪk / (AmE), / kweɪk / (BrE)
to shake or tremble with quick, sometimes violent movements (can refer to people, objects, or the earth)

Examples

to quake with fear
His hands were quaking in the freezing cold.
She tried to speak, but her voice quaked with fear.
The ground began to quake beneath their feet as the earthquake started.
During the night, the city quaked due to the unexpected earthquake.
The ground began to quake after the powerful explosion.
Villagers watched in fear as the earth started to quake beneath their feet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shake: to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • synonym – shudder: shake or tremble convulsively, typically as a result of fear or revulsion
  • antonym – steady: to make or become firm, fixed, or stable; not shaking or moving
  • synonym – tremble: shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety or excitement
