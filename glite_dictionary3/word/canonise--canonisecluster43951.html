to canonise

verb
declare someone a saint officially
Pronunciation: / ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkanənaɪz / (BrE)
officially declare (a dead person) to be a saint, especially by the Roman Catholic Church.

Examples

to canonise a saint
He was officially canonised for his lifelong dedication to helping the poor.
Many early Christian martyrs were canonised centuries after their deaths.
The Vatican announced plans to canonise the beloved nun.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – beatify: declare (a dead person) to be blessed, a step before canonisation
  • degree higher – sainthood: the state or condition of being recognized as a saint
