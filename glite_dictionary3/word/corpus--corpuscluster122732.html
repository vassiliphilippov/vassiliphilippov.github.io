corpus

noun
collection of written texts
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrpəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpəs / (BrE)
a collection of written texts, especially the entire works of a particular author or a body of writing on a specific subject

Examples

corpus of texts
Her research focused on a corpus of Shakespeare's plays.
The linguist created a corpus of modern American English to study language change.
The publisher released the author's complete corpus in a new edition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anthology: a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing
