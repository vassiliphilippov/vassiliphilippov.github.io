Iroquois

proper noun
member of a North American Native people
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrəkˌwɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪərəkwɔɪ / (BrE)
A member of any of a group of Native American peoples formerly living in what is now New York State, especially those belonging to the Iroquois League, which played a major role in early American history.

Examples

Iroquois people group
Several Iroquois leaders played crucial roles in negotiations with European settlers.
The Iroquois were known for their powerful confederacy and influence in early American history.
Today, descendants of the Iroquois continue to preserve their cultural traditions and language.
