sight

noun
something seen; visual perception
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
something that is seen, especially something impressive, beautiful, or remarkable

Examples

beautiful sight
At the carnival, every sight and sound seemed new and exciting.
The children were amazed by the sight of the snow-covered mountains.
The parade was a sight to behold, with its vibrant costumes and floats.
It was a beautiful sight to see the mountains covered in snow.
The children laughed at the strange sight of the parade elephants marching down Main Street.
Tourists gathered to take photos of the famous sight during their city walk.

  • antonym – sound: something that is heard, as opposed to something seen

  • synonym – view: the ability to see something or the range of vision
  • synonym – vision: the faculty or state of being able to see
