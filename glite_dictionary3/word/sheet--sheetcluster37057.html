to sheet

verb
fall or pour heavily (of rain, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ʃit / (AmE), / ʃiːt / (BrE)
(of rain, snow, etc.) to fall or pour heavily and continuously, as in a wide unbroken stream.

Examples

rain sheets down
During the storm, the wind drove the snow to sheet across the fields.
The rain began to sheet down in the early hours of the morning.
Thunder crashed as water sheeted off the roof and into the streets.
