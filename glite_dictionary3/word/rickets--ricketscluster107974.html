rickets

noun
childhood bone disease from vitamin D deficiency
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪkɪts / (AmE), / ˈrɪkɪts / (BrE)
A childhood disease caused by vitamin D deficiency, leading to softening and weakening of the bones.

Examples

rickets in children
A lack of sunlight and poor diet may cause rickets in young children.
Early symptoms of rickets include bone pain and delayed growth.
Public health campaigns have greatly reduced the incidence of rickets in developed countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – osteomalacia: softening of the bones, especially in adults, usually due to vitamin D deficiency
