meagreness

noun
state of being meager or insufficient
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːɡɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡənəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being meager; the condition of being lacking in quantity, fullness, or richness.

Examples

meagreness of resources
Critics pointed out the meagreness of evidence presented at the trial.
Despite the meagreness of her resources, she managed to support her family.
The meagreness of his meal left him hungry for more.

  • antonym – abundance: a very large quantity of something
  • synonym – meagerness: state of being meager (alternative US spelling)
  • synonym – poverty: the state of being extremely poor
