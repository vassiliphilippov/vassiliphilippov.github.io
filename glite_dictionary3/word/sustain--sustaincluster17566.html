to sustain

verb
to suffer or experience (an injury, etc.)
Pronunciation: / səˈsteɪn / (AmE), / səˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to experience or suffer (an injury, loss, or other unpleasant event)

Examples

sustain an injury
He sustained multiple fractures in the car accident.
Several buildings sustained damage in the earthquake.
The athlete sustained a serious knee injury during the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym – incur: to become subject to something unwelcome or unpleasant as a result of actions
  • synonym – suffer: to experience or be subjected to something bad or unpleasant
