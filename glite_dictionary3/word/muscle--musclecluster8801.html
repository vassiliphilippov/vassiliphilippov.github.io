to muscle

verb
force one's way by strength
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
to force one's way into or through something by using strength or forceful tactics

Examples

to muscle past guards
He muscled his way past the security guard to enter the concert hall.
Several companies tried to muscle into the growing tech market.
She muscled her way through the crowd to get to the front of the line.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barge: move forcefully or roughly
