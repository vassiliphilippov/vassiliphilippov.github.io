gurnard

noun
bottom-dwelling coastal fish with armored head and fins
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɝːnərd / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːnəd / (BrE)
a bottom-dwelling coastal fish with a spiny, armored head and fingerlike pectoral fins, which it uses to crawl along the sea bottom; found in shallow waters of the Atlantic and Mediterranean.

Examples

gurnard fish species
A gurnard uses its fingerlike fins to crawl along the ocean floor in search of food.
Chefs often prepare gurnard in Mediterranean seafood stews because of its firm texture.
The fisherman displayed a bright red gurnard caught near the rocky shoreline.
