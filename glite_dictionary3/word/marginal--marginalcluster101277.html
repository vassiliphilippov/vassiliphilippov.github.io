marginal

adjective
of minimal or questionable quality
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪnəl /; / ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
of questionable, limited, or minimal quality, amount, or difference; barely acceptable or sufficient

Examples

marginal improvement
Because his skills were marginal, he struggled to keep up with the team.
Her performance in the class was considered marginal by the teacher.
The research produced only marginal results, raising doubts about its validity.
He passed the exam with only a marginal difference above the minimum requirement.
The benefits of the new policy are marginal at best, according to some analysts.
The team's marginal lead was enough to secure them a place in the finals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mediocre: of only average quality; not very good
  • synonym – negligible: so small or unimportant as to be not worth considering; insignificant
  • antonym – substantial: of considerable importance, size, or worth
