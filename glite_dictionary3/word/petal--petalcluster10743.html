petal

noun
a colored part of a flower
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛtəl / (BrE)
A typically brightly colored, thin part of a flower that surrounds its reproductive organs, forming part of the perianth.

Examples

petal of a flower
Brightly colored petals attract pollinators to the flower.
Each petal glistened with morning dew in the sunlight.
The rose's petal felt soft and delicate between her fingers.

Related Terms
