to accept

verb
be able to receive or hold something
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
(of an object or surface) to be designed or able to receive, contain, or be compatible with something.

Examples

accept new parts
The projector will accept video input from multiple sources.
The soil did not accept the new type of fertilizer very well.
This charger can accept both USB-C and micro USB cables.
