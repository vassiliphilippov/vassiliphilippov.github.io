fledgling

adjective
young and inexperienced
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) young and inexperienced; at an early stage of development or activity.

Examples

fledgling artist
As a fledgling chef, she was eager to learn from her mentors.
Despite his fledgling status, he showed great promise as a journalist.
The fledgling company quickly adapted to the challenges of the marketplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym – experienced: having gained knowledge or skill in a particular field
  • synonym – novice: a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
