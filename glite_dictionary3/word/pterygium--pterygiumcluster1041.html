pterygium

noun
growth of conjunctiva on the eye
Pronunciation: / təˈrɪdʒiəm / (AmE), / təˈrɪdʒiəm / (BrE)
a benign growth of the conjunctiva in the eye, usually in the shape of a wedge, that may extend onto the cornea, often due to irritation or sun exposure

Examples

pterygium eye growth
Protecting your eyes from UV light can reduce the risk of developing a pterygium.
Some people require surgery if a pterygium begins to interfere with their vision.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed the patient with a pterygium after noticing a wedge-shaped growth on his eye.
