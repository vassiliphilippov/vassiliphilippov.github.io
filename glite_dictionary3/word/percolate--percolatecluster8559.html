to percolate

verb
move through a porous material
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːkəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkəleɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) to move gradually through a porous material or substance.

Examples

to percolate through soil
After the rainstorm, water began to percolate through the soil into the underground reservoirs.
Chemicals from the landfill may percolate into the groundwater, causing environmental concerns.
It can take hours for the coffee to percolate through the filter, creating a rich and strong brew.

Related Terms

  • synonym – filter: to pass a liquid, gas, light, or sound through a device to remove unwanted substances
  • synonym – seep: to flow or leak slowly through porous material or small holes
