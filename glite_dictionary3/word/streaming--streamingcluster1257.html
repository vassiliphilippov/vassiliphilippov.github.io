streaming

adjective
continuous transmission of audio, video, or data
Pronunciation: / ˈstriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of audio, video, or data) transmitted or received over the internet or a network in a continuous flow, allowing access as earlier parts are still being sent or processed.

Examples

streaming video online
Educational institutions now offer streaming lectures for remote students.
Many users prefer streaming music rather than downloading files to their devices.
With streaming video technology, viewers can start watching a movie before it has fully downloaded.

Related Terms

  • antonym – download: transfer (data or programs) from a server to a local device
