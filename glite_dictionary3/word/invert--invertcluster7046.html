to invert

verb
turn upside down or inside out
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɝt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to turn something upside down, inside out, or to reverse its normal order or position

Examples

to invert a cake
Before serving the cake, invert the pan to release it onto a plate.
He accidentally inverted the image while editing the photo.
To see the hidden message, you must invert the colors on the screen.
He decided to invert the colors on the image to create a dramatic effect.
If you invert the order of the numbers, the calculation will give you a different result.
To ask a yes/no question in English, you often need to invert the subject and the auxiliary verb.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flip: to turn over or cause to turn over with a sudden quick movement
  • antonym – restore: to bring something back to its original condition
  • synonym – reverse: to turn something the opposite way or change to the opposite direction
  • antonym – upright: in a vertical position; not inverted
