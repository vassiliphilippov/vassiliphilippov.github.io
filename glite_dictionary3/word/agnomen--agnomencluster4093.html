agnomen

noun
an added name or honorary epithet
Pronunciation: / æɡˈnoʊ.mən / (AmE), / æɡˈnəʊ.mən / (BrE)
an additional name or epithet added to a person's name, especially in ancient Rome or as an honorary title (e.g. 'Ferdinand the Great')

Examples

agnomen 'the Great'
His achievements earned him the agnomen 'the Wise' among his peers.
In ancient Rome, a victorious general might be awarded the agnomen 'Africanus'.
The title ‘the Great’ served as an agnomen to many rulers throughout history.
