fallaciousness

noun
the state of being fallacious
Pronunciation: / fəˈleɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / fəˈleɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being fallacious; the characteristic of being based on mistaken belief or unsound reasoning

Examples

fallaciousness of an argument
Academics often debate the fallaciousness of various historical claims.
She pointed out the fallaciousness in the opposing lawyer’s reasoning during the trial.
The fallaciousness of his argument became clear when the evidence was examined.
