laryngectomy

noun
surgical removal of the larynx
Pronunciation: / ˌlærɪŋˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌlarɪŋˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove part or all of the larynx, often performed to treat laryngeal cancer

Examples

undergo laryngectomy surgery
A laryngectomy can significantly affect a person's ability to speak and breathe normally.
After her laryngectomy, she learned to speak using an electronic device.
The patient required a total laryngectomy due to advanced cancer.

  • synonym – tracheostomy: a surgical opening in the trachea to assist breathing
