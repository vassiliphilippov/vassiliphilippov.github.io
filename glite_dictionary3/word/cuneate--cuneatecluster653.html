cuneate

adjective
narrowly triangular leaf shape
Pronunciation: / kjuˈniː.eɪt / (AmE), / kjuːˈniː.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) narrowly triangular in shape, wider at the tip and tapering toward the base.

Examples

cuneate leaf base
Oak leaves are often lobed, but some have a distinctly cuneate shape.
The botanist noted the cuneate base of the leaf in her report.
The petal margins taper into a cuneate base connecting to the stem.

  • synonym – wedge-shaped: having the shape of a wedge; similar to cuneate
