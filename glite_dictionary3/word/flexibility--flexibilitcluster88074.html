flexibility

noun
ability to adapt or change easily
Pronunciation: / ˌflɛksəˈbɪlɪti /; / ˌflɛksəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌflɛksɪˈbɪlɪti /; / ˌflɛksɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able or willing to change or adapt easily to different situations or ideas

Examples

show flexibility at work
Her flexibility allowed her to thrive in the rapidly changing environment.
Remote work provides greater flexibility for managing family responsibilities.
The company values flexibility when assigning projects to employees.
Employers often value flexibility when hiring for fast-paced environments.
Her flexibility allowed her to adapt quickly to the new team's working style.
Negotiations succeeded because both sides showed a willingness to compromise and some flexibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adaptability: the ability to adjust to new conditions
  • antonym – inflexibility: the quality of being unwilling to change or compromise
  • antonym – rigidity: the quality of being inflexible or unyielding
