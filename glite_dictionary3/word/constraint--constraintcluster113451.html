constraint

noun
act of restricting or limiting
Pronunciation: / kənˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / kənˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
the act of constraining or restricting someone or something; the use or threat of force to limit freedom or behavior

Examples

legal constraint
He acted under severe constraint imposed by the threat of punishment.
Social constraint shaped his behavior at the formal event.
The law acts as a constraint on government power.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freedom: the power or right to act, speak, or think without hindrance or restraint
  • synonym – restraint: a measure or condition that keeps someone or something under control
