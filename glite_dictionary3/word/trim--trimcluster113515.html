trim

noun
equilibrium position of an aircraft
Pronunciation: / trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
the attitude or position of an aircraft in flight when allowed to assume its own natural orientation, especially when all controls are neutral

Examples

aircraft trim
Changes in fuel load can affect an aircraft's trim and must be compensated for.
Maintaining proper trim can reduce pilot fatigue during long flights.
The pilot adjusted the controls to achieve the best trim for level flight.
