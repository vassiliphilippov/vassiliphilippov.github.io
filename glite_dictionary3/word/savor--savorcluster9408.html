to savor

verb
to enjoy or appreciate fully
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪvər /; / ˈseɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈseɪvə / (BrE)
to enjoy or appreciate something fully, especially by taking time to experience it

Examples

to savor every moment
He tried to savor every bite of the delicious dessert.
She sat back to savor the moment after receiving the award.
You should savor your achievements and not rush to the next challenge.
He paused to savor the victory before joining his teammates in celebration.
She closed her eyes to savor the rich aroma of the freshly brewed coffee.
They ate slowly, wanting to savor every bite of the delicious meal.
