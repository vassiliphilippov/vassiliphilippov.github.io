hyperlipidemia

noun
excess fats in the blood (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌlɪpəˈdiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəlɪpɪˈdiːmɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an abnormally high concentration of fats or lipids in the blood.

Examples

hyperlipidemia diagnosis
Certain genetic factors can increase the risk of developing hyperlipidemia.
Patients with hyperlipidemia are often advised to modify their diet and exercise regularly.
The doctor ordered blood tests to check for hyperlipidemia after reviewing his patient's medical history.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hypercholesterolemia: high cholesterol in the blood
  • antonym – hypolipidemia: low levels of lipids in the blood
