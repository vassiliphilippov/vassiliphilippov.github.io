orthogonal

adjective
at right angles; perpendicular
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈθɑɡənl̩ / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒɡənl̩ / (BrE)
(of lines, axes, or planes) at right angles to each other; perpendicular.

Examples

orthogonal lines
In geometry, the term orthogonal describes two vectors that meet at a right angle.
The grid lines on the map are orthogonal to each other, forming perfect squares.
The motion of the particle was measured along three orthogonal axes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – parallel: side by side and having the same distance continuously between them
  • synonym – perpendicular: at an angle of 90° to a given line, plane, or surface
