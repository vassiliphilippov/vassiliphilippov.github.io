to moralise

verb
improve someone's morals
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to improve the morals or moral character of (someone or a group) through teaching or admonishment

Examples

to moralise young people
Some parents believe it is their duty to moralise their children from a young age.
The film aims to moralise audiences without being overly preachy.
The teacher tried to moralise her students by discussing the value of honesty.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preach: to give moral advice, especially in a self-righteous or tedious way
