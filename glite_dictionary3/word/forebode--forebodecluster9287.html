to forebode

verb
predict, especially of something bad
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈboʊd / (AmE), / fɔːˈbəʊd / (BrE)
to predict or indicate a future event, especially something unpleasant or bad

Examples

to forebode trouble
Some critics forebode a decline in the housing market next year.
The dark clouds forebode an approaching storm.
The eerie silence seemed to forebode trouble for the expedition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foretell: to predict or tell something before it happens
  • synonym – predict: to state what will happen in the future
  • synonym – prophesy: to say that a specified thing will happen in the future
