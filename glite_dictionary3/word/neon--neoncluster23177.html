neon

noun
inert gaseous chemical element
Pronunciation: / ˈniːˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless chemical element (symbol Ne) that is an inert gas and glows red when electrified, used especially in signs and lighting

Examples

neon sign
A neon lamp produces a characteristic red-orange light in advertisements.
Scientists isolate neon from the air by fractional distillation of liquid air.
The neon in the sign glowed brightly throughout the night.
Natural neon is rare on Earth and is usually obtained from the air.
Scientists use neon to fill tubes for colorful lighting effects.
The bright neon in the sign made the words glow vividly at night.
