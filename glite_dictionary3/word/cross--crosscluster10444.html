cross

noun
mark of intersecting lines
Pronunciation: / krɑs / (AmE), / krɒs / (BrE)
A mark or figure consisting of two lines that intersect each other, typically at right angles.

Examples

red cross mark
A red cross was painted on the door for identification.
He made a small cross in the corner of the page to remember where he left off.
The teacher asked each student to draw a cross on their test paper.
