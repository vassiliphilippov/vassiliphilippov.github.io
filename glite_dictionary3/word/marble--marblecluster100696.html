marble

noun
hard, polished rock used in sculpture/building
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl / (BrE)
A hard, crystalline metamorphic rock that can be polished to a high sheen, commonly used for sculpture and as a building material.

Examples

polished marble floor
She admired the marble columns that lined the ancient temple.
The elegant floors of the grand hotel were made of polished marble.
The statue was carved from a single block of marble and stands in the museum.

Related Terms

  • synonym – granite: a very hard, granular, crystalline igneous rock used as a building stone
  • degree lower – limestone: a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate
