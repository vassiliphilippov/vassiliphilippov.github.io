to resist

verb
withstand the force or effect of something
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪst / (BrE)
to remain unaffected by or withstand the effect or force of something (such as damage, disease, or change)

Examples

resist water damage
Modern fabrics can resist stains much more effectively than older materials.
The walls were built strong enough to resist even the fiercest storms.
These materials are designed to resist extreme temperatures and harsh conditions.
After the transplant, her immune system began to resist the new heart tissue.
Doctors monitor patients closely to see if their bodies will resist the transplanted organ.
Immunosuppressive drugs are used to prevent the body from resisting foreign tissues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endure: to suffer something difficult or painful patiently
  • synonym – reject: (medicine) For the body to attack and fail to accept a transplanted organ or tissue
  • antonym – succumb: to give way to force or pressure
