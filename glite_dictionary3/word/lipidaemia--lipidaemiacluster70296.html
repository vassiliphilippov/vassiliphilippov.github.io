lipidaemia

noun
excess lipids in the blood
Pronunciation: / lɪˌpɪdˈiːmiə / (AmE), / lɪˌpɪdˈiːmiə / (BrE)
the presence of an abnormally high concentration of lipids in the blood

Examples

lipidaemia in blood
Patients with uncontrolled diabetes often develop lipidaemia as a secondary condition.
Routine blood tests can reveal lipidaemia, which may increase the risk of heart disease.
The doctor diagnosed her with lipidaemia and recommended dietary changes to lower her cholesterol.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hyperlipidemia: abnormally high levels of lipids in the blood (American spelling)
