cotangent

noun
trigonometric ratio: adjacent/opposite
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈtændʒənt / (BrE)
The trigonometric function that is the ratio of the length of the side adjacent to a given angle to the length of the side opposite in a right-angled triangle.

Examples

cotangent of an angle
In trigonometry, you can find the cotangent by dividing the adjacent side by the opposite side.
She calculated the cotangent to solve the triangle problem.
The cotangent of an angle is the reciprocal of the tangent.

  • synonym – sine: the trigonometric ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse in a right triangle
  • antonym – tangent: the trigonometric ratio of opposite to adjacent side in a right triangle
