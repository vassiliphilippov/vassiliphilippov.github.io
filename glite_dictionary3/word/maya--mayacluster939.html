Maya

noun
member of an ancient Mesoamerican civilization
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous people of Mexico and Central America, especially known for their ancient civilization in regions like Yucatán, Guatemala, and Belize

Examples

ancient Maya civilization
Archaeologists have discovered new temples built by the Maya in the jungle.
Many descendants of the Maya still live in present-day Guatemala and speak their traditional languages.
The ancient Maya were known for their remarkable achievements in mathematics and astronomy.
Archaeologists have studied the ruins left by the ancient Maya civilization for decades.
Many Maya continue to preserve their traditional customs and language in present-day Guatemala.
The modern Maya face challenges in maintaining their cultural heritage amidst globalization.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Aztec: indigenous people of central Mexico with a distinct language and history
  • synonym – Inca: indigenous people of South America, especially Peru, with a distinct language and empire
  • synonym – Mayan: relating to the Maya people or their language
