bailor

noun
person who entrusts goods in bailment
Pronunciation: / beɪˈlɔr / (AmE), / beɪˈlɔː / (BrE)
a person who entrusts goods or property to another (the bailee) for a specific purpose without transferring ownership

Examples

bailor gives property
A bailor may reclaim their property once the bailment purpose is fulfilled.
As the bailor, she trusted the company to store her antiques safely.
The bailor must inform the bailee of any defects in the property.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bailee: person who receives property from a bailor in trust
