hogged

adjective
of a ship: sagging at both ends
Pronunciation: / hɑgd / (AmE), / hɒgd / (BrE)
(of a ship) weakened so that both the bow and stern sag downward, causing the ends to be lower than the middle.

Examples

hogged ship hull
A hogged ship is dangerous because it can compromise the hull's integrity.
After years at sea, the old vessel became hogged and needed extensive repairs.
Inspectors noticed the freighter was hogged, with both bow and stern drooping lower than the center.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hogging: the upward curving of a ship's hull in the middle due to structural stress
  • synonym – sag: to sink, droop, or bend downwards, especially in the middle from weight or weakness
