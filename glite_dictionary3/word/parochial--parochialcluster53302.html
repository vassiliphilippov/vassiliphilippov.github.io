parochial

adjective
narrow in outlook or scope
Pronunciation: / pəˈroʊkiəl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəl / (BrE)
having a limited or narrow outlook or scope, often focusing only on local or minor concerns rather than broader or more general issues.

Examples

parochial attitude
Critics argued that his parochial view prevented the city from adapting to global changes.
She was frustrated by the organization's parochial attitude toward innovation.
The debate was marked by a parochial focus, ignoring the wider national implications.

  • synonym – insular: having a narrow or limited outlook; isolated
