circle

noun
tier of seats in a theater above the orchestra
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
(in a theater or opera house) a curved section or tier of seats, usually the first tier above the stalls or orchestra.

Examples

theater circle
The circle was almost full by the time the orchestra began to play.
They had excellent seats in the circle for the opening night performance.
We prefer sitting in the circle because the view of the stage is much better.
