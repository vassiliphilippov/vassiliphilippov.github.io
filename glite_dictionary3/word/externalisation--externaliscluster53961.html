externalisation

noun
attributing to outside causes
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɜrnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of attributing one's own thoughts, feelings, or motives to external sources or outside causes.

Examples

externalisation of blame
His externalisation of blame prevented him from recognizing his own mistakes.
In psychology, externalisation is a defense mechanism seen in patients with certain disorders.
The persistent externalisation of problems can be a barrier to personal growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – projection: psychological process of attributing one's feelings to others
