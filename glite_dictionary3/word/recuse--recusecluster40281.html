to recuse

verb
disqualify oneself (legal, esp. judge)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkjuz / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuːz / (BrE)
(of a judge or official) to remove yourself or be removed from a legal case or decision because of a possible conflict of interest or bias

Examples

to recuse yourself from a case
If the prosecutor has a conflict of interest, he must recuse himself from the case.
The judge decided to recuse herself from the trial due to a personal connection to the defendant.
There were calls for the committee chair to recuse herself to ensure an impartial decision.
If you feel you cannot be impartial, you must recuse yourself from the proceedings.
The defense attorney filed a motion to recuse the presiding judge.
