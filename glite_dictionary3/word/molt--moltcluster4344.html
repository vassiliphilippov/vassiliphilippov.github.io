to molt

verb
shed feathers, hair, skin, or shell
Pronunciation: / moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to shed old feathers, hair, skin, or shell to make way for new growth.

Examples

to molt feathers
Every year, birds molt their old feathers to prepare for migration.
Lobsters will molt several times as they grow larger.
My pet snake began to molt and soon shed all its skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shed: to lose or cast off a covering, such as hair or feathers, naturally
