correlated

adjective
having a mutual relationship
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
having a mutual relationship or connection with something else, such that changes in one are associated with changes in the other.

Examples

correlated factors
High income and education level are often correlated in sociological studies.
Scientists discovered that the presence of the gene was strongly correlated with increased risk of disease.
The two variables are correlated, indicating that changes in one influence the other.
