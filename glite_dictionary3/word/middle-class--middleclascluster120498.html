middle-class

adjective
of or relating to the social middle class
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əlˌklæs / (AmE), / ˌmɪd.əlˈklɑːs / (BrE)
belonging to or characteristic of the social group between the working class and the wealthy, typically having moderate income and education

Examples

middle-class family life
He grew up in a middle-class environment that emphasized education and hard work.
Many middle-class families aspire to own their own homes.
The neighborhood is primarily middle-class, with well-kept houses and good schools.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – lower-class: belonging to the social group with the lowest status and wealth
  • degree higher – upper-class: belonging to the social group with the highest status and wealth
