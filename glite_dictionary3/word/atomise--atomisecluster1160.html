to atomise

verb
destroy with bombs or great force
Pronunciation: / ˈætəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈætəmaɪz / (BrE)
to destroy something completely by using bombs or other powerful weapons, reducing it to small particles or fragments

Examples

to atomise a city
If the order is given, the military can atomise the enemy position within seconds.
The airstrike atomised the entire warehouse, leaving nothing behind.
The old building was atomised by a controlled demolition, sending dust and debris into the sky.

  • synonym – annihilate: destroy utterly; obliterate
  • synonym – vaporize: destroy instantly by turning into vapor, e.g., via explosion
