to inflame

verb
set on fire; cause to burn
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm / (BrE)
to cause something to start burning or to catch fire

Examples

to inflame dry wood
A single spark was enough to inflame the pile of old newspapers.
The match could easily inflame the dry grass, so be careful.
The protesters threatened to inflame the government building if their demands were not met.
Be careful with matches, as spilled gasoline may inflame quickly.
If the fabric is too close to the candle, it could easily inflame.
Several dry bushes suddenly inflamed during the intense heatwave.
