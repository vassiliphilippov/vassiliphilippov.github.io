noticeably

adverb
in a way that is easy to notice
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊtɪsəbli / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtɪsəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is easy to see or recognize; in a manner that attracts notice or attention.

Examples

noticeably different
She was noticeably upset after receiving the news.
The patient's condition improved noticeably after the new treatment began.
The weather has become noticeably warmer since last week.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inconspicuously: in a way that is not clearly visible or attracting attention
  • synonym – obviously: in a way that is easily perceived or understood
