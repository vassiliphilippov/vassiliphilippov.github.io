propitiously

adverb
in a favorable or auspicious manner
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɪʃəsli / (AmE), / prəˈpɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows good signs for success; favorably or auspiciously.

Examples

propitiously begin a project
He launched his new business propitiously, with a large order from a major client.
The negotiations began propitiously, with both sides expressing eagerness to cooperate.
The weather was propitiously clear for the outdoor wedding ceremony.
