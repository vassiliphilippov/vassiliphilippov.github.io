sweep

noun
long oar for large/open boat
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiːp / (BrE)
a long oar used for rowing large boats, especially open boats

Examples

a boat sweep
He manned a sweep on the port side of the open barge.
Old fishing vessels often relied on a heavy sweep when sails were useless.
The sailors took up the sweep and rowed their boat toward the shore.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oar: a pole with a flat blade used to row or steer a boat
  • degree lower – paddle: a short pole with a broad blade used to move a small boat
