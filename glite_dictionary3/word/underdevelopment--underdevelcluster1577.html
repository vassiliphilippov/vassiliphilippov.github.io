underdevelopment

noun
insufficient film processing; low contrast
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˌdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
in photography, inadequate processing of film resulting in images with insufficient contrast

Examples

underdevelopment of film
Improper timing during processing often leads to underdevelopment in photographic film.
Photographers must avoid underdevelopment to ensure proper contrast in their prints.
The negative suffered from underdevelopment, making the image appear washed out and lacking detail.

Related Terms

  • antonym – overdevelopment: excessive processing of film resulting in too much contrast
