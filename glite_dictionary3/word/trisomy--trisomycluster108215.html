trisomy

noun
condition with an extra chromosome
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌsoʊ.mi / (AmE), / ˈtrʌɪ.sə.mi / (BrE)
A chromosomal abnormality in which there is an extra chromosome, resulting in one more than the normal number in a cell.

Examples

trisomy 21 diagnosis
Doctors can detect certain types of trisomy through prenatal genetic testing.
Down syndrome is an example of a condition caused by trisomy 21.
The risks of trisomy increase with maternal age.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monosomy: chromosomal abnormality with one fewer chromosome
