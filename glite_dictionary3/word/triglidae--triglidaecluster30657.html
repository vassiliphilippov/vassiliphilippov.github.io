Triglidae

noun
family of fishes: gurnards and sea robins
Pronunciation: / traɪˈɡlɪdiː / (AmE), / trɪˈɡlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes that includes the gurnards and sea robins, often divided into the subfamilies Triglinae and Peristediinae.

Examples

Researchers found that Triglidae are common in the coastal waters of the Mediterranean Sea.
The family Triglidae includes several species known for their armored, wing-like pectoral fins.
Within the Triglidae, the subfamily Triglinae is often called the true sea robins.
