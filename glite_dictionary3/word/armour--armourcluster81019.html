armour

noun
protective animal or plant covering
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a tough, rigid protective covering on an animal or plant, such as a shell or thickened skin, that defends against predators or injury

Examples

animal armour shell
The armadillo's armour helps protect it from predators in the wild.
The beetle's armour is made up of hardened exoskeletal plates.
The turtle retracts into its armour when threatened by danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shell: hard outer covering of certain animals such as mollusks and turtles
