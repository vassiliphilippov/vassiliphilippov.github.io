de-icer

noun
substance for melting or preventing ice
Pronunciation: / diˈaɪsɚ / (AmE), / diˈaɪsə / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to remove or prevent the formation of ice, commonly applied to surfaces such as roads, sidewalks, and aircraft.

Examples

de-icer on roads
City workers spread de-icer on the roads to prevent accidents.
He keeps a bottle of de-icer in his car during winter.
The airport staff sprayed de-icer on the plane's wings before takeoff.
