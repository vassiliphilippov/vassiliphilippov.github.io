to cringe

verb
draw back in fear or pain
Pronunciation: / krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
to shrink back or draw back, usually because of fear, pain, or embarrassment

Examples

to cringe in fear
She couldn’t help but cringe at the loud crash behind her.
The dog would cringe whenever someone raised their voice.
Whenever he sees needles, he tends to cringe instinctively.
He cringed in the corner, afraid to speak.
She could not help but cringe before the intimidating boss.
The dog would cringe whenever it heard a loud noise.

  • synonym – cower: to crouch down in fear
  • synonym – flinch: to make a quick, nervous movement as an instinctive reaction to fear, pain, or surprise
  • synonym – recoil: to suddenly spring or flinch back in fear, horror, or disgust
  • antonym – stand tall: to behave in a confident, unafraid manner
