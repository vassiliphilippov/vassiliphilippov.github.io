caskful

noun
amount a cask holds
Pronunciation: / ˈkæskˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːskˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to the contents of a full cask.

Examples

a caskful of wine
After the harvest, the brewer filled a caskful with fresh cider.
The cellar held several caskfuls of aged sherry waiting to be bottled.
They ordered a caskful of wine for the wedding feast.
