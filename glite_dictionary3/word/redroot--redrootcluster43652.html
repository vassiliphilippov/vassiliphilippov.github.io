redroot

noun
perennial North American plant with red root
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.ruːt / (BrE)
A perennial woodland plant native to North America, noted for its red root and sap, bearing a single lobed leaf and white flower in early spring; its rootstock is used as a stimulant and expectorant in herbal medicine.

Examples

wild redroot plant
In early spring, the forest floor is dotted with the white flowers of redroot.
The distinctive red sap of redroot makes it easy to identify among woodland plants.
The herbalist recommended using redroot for its expectorant properties to help with chest congestion.
