weed

noun
unwanted plant competing with crops
Pronunciation: / wid / (AmE), / wiːd / (BrE)
A wild plant growing where it is not wanted and in competition with cultivated plants.

Examples

pull weeds
After a few weeks, weeds began to take over the once-pristine vegetable garden.
Crabgrass is a common weed that thrives in lawns and competes with grass.
The farmer spent hours pulling weeds to protect his crops from being choked out.
