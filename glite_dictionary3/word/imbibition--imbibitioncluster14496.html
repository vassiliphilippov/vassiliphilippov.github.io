imbibition

noun
absorption of liquid by a solid or gel
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.bəˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.bɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Chemistry, biology) The process by which a solid or gel absorbs a liquid, often causing swelling.

Examples

imbibition of water
Gelatin increases in size through imbibition when placed in water.
In some materials, imbibition causes notable swelling due to water intake.
The imbibition of water by seeds is essential for germination.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absorption: the process of taking in a substance
