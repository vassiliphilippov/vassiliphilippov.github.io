passive

noun
grammatical voice with acted-upon subject
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv / (BrE)
(grammar) the form or voice of a verb in which the subject is acted on rather than performing the action, as in 'The ball was thrown by the boy.'

Examples

use the passive
Many students have trouble identifying the passive in complex sentences.
The passive can sometimes make writing seem less direct or clear.
Writers are often advised to avoid the passive in favor of the active voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – active: the grammatical voice where the subject performs the action
