to platitudinize

verb
utter platitudes or trite remarks
Pronunciation: / plæ.tɪˈtjuː.dəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / pləˈtɪt.juː.dɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to speak or write in platitudes; to express oneself using dull or trite remarks

Examples

to platitudinize in speeches
Journalists criticized the president for platitudinizing during the important press conference.
The lecture was dull because the professor just platitudinized for an hour.
Whenever he speaks in meetings, he tends to platitudinize instead of offering real solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – platitude: a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
