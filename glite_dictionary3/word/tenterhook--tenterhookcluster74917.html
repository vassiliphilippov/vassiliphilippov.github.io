tenterhook

noun
hook for stretching cloth on a frame
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛntərˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈtɛntəhʊk / (BrE)
one of a series of hooks used to stretch and hold cloth on a frame during manufacturing or drying

Examples

tenterhook for cloth
Before modern processes, weavers relied on tenterhook devices to prevent shrinkage in their cloth.
In the old mill, iron tenterhooks lined the wooden frames for stretching wool.
The fabric was carefully secured to the tenterhooks so it would dry evenly.
