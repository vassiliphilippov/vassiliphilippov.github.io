to deplume

verb
strip of honors or attributes
Pronunciation: / dɪˈplum / (AmE), / dɪˈpluːm / (BrE)
to strip someone or something of honors, possessions, or distinguishing attributes

Examples

to deplume a champion
After the scandal, the committee decided to deplume him of his titles and privileges.
The organization was deplumed of its awards after the corruption revelations came to light.
Witnessing his fall from grace, his peers watched as he was deplumed of every honor he held.
