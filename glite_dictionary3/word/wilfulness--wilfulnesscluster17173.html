wilfulness

noun
stubborn disobedience; self-willed nature
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfʊlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being determined to do what one wants, even if it is disobedient or defiant of discipline

Examples

child's wilfulness
Despite warnings, her wilfulness led her to ignore the rules.
His wilfulness often got him into trouble at school.
The manager grew frustrated with the wilfulness of the new recruits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – obedience: the quality of being compliant and following rules
  • synonym – stubbornness: the quality of being stubborn or hard to move
