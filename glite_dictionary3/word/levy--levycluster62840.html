levy

noun
an imposed tax or fee
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
an amount of money, such as a tax or fee, that is officially imposed and collected by an authority

Examples

tax levy
A special levy was introduced to fund the construction of the new bridge.
Failure to pay the levy on time can result in additional penalties.
The government announced a new levy on sugar-sweetened drinks.
