Ithunn

proper noun
Norse goddess of spring and youth
Pronunciation: / ɪˈθʊn / (AmE), / ɪˈθʊn / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the goddess of spring and rejuvenation, wife of Bragi, who guarded the apples that gave the gods eternal youth.

Examples

Ithunn guards apples
According to the legend, Ithunn kept the magical apples that granted the gods their youth.
In many poems, Ithunn is praised for her role in preserving the vitality of Asgard's deities.
The theft of Ithunn's apples caused great concern among the gods.

  • synonym – Bragi: Norse god of poetry and husband of Ithunn
