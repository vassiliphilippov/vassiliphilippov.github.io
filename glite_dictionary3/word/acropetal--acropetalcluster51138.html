acropetal

adjective
developing from base to apex, especially in plants
Pronunciation: / əˈkroʊpətl / (AmE), / əˈkrɒpɪtəl / (BrE)
(of leaves or flowers) developing or opening in succession from the base towards the apex.

Examples

acropetal growth pattern
In acropetal order, new leaves appear progressively higher up the stem.
Many species exhibit acropetal development during the early stages of growth.
The plant's acropetal flowering ensures that the oldest blooms are found near the base.

Related Terms

  • antonym – basipetal: developing or opening in succession from apex to base
