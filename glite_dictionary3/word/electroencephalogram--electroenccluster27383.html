electroencephalogram

noun
graphic record of brain's electrical activity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlektroʊɪnˈsɛfələˌɡræm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊɪnˈsɛfələɡræm / (BrE)
a graphic record of the electrical activity of the brain, produced by electrodes placed on the scalp

Examples

electroencephalogram test result
An electroencephalogram is often used to diagnose epilepsy and other neurological disorders.
She was nervous about having an electroencephalogram, but the procedure was painless.
The doctor reviewed the electroencephalogram to check for abnormal brain activity.
