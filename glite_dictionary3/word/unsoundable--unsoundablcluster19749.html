unsoundable

adjective
too deep to measure
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsaʊndəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊndəbl / (BrE)
(of a body of water or chasm) too deep to be measured; impossible to determine the depth of.

Examples

unsoundable abyss
Legends spoke of an unsoundable abyss hidden deep within the jungle.
The mariner claimed the pit was unsoundable, its depths beyond the reach of any line.
When the crew tried to measure the trench, it proved unsoundable even with their longest rope.

  • synonym – bottomless: having no apparent bottom; very deep
