khadi

noun
coarse Indian hand-spun cotton cloth
Pronunciation: / kɑːˈdi / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdi / (BrE)
A coarse, hand-spun and hand-woven cotton cloth made in India, traditionally associated with Indian independence and craftsmanship.

Examples

traditional khadi cloth
Mahatma Gandhi popularized khadi as a symbol of self-reliance and Indian independence.
Many people in India still wear khadi garments, especially on national holidays.
The artisan demonstrated how khadi was spun and woven by hand.
