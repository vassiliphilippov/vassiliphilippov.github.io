to aggrieve

verb
cause to feel sorrow or distress
Pronunciation: / əˈɡriv / (AmE), / əˈɡriːv / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel sorrow, distress, or grief; to afflict or oppress

Examples

to aggrieve the family
It would aggrieve him to hear such sad news about his hometown.
She did not mean to aggrieve her friends with her harsh words.
The unfair treatment will only aggrieve the employees further.

Related Terms

  • antonym – console: to comfort someone who is distressed
  • synonym – grieve: to feel or cause to feel deep sorrow
