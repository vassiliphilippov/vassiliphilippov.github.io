plough

noun
farm tool for turning soil
Pronunciation: / plaʊ / (AmE), / plaʊ / (BrE)
a large farming tool with one or more heavy blades used for turning and breaking up soil in preparation for planting seeds

Examples

old plough in a field
Before tractors, oxen often pulled the plough through the heavy soil.
The farmer used a plough to prepare the field for the spring planting.
The old wooden plough is now displayed in the village museum.
