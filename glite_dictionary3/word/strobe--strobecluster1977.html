strobe

noun
flashing light instrument to freeze motion
Pronunciation: / stroʊb / (AmE), / strəʊb / (BrE)
a scientific instrument that produces rapid flashes of light, often used to make moving objects appear stationary by synchronizing with their motion

Examples

strobe light flashes
Photographers often rely on a strobe to freeze fast-moving subjects in action shots.
The technician used a strobe to study the motion of the rotating fan blades.
Under the influence of the strobe, the dancer's movements appeared almost surreal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stroboscope: an instrument used to make a cyclically moving object appear to slow down or stop by illuminating it with short bursts of light
