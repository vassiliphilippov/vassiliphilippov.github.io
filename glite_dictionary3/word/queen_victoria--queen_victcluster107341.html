Queen Victoria

proper noun
British queen (1837–1901), Victorian era monarch
Pronunciation: / kwiːn vɪkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / kwiːn vɪkˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
the queen of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland from 1837 to 1901, and the empress of India from 1876; known for her long reign and for the Victorian era named after her

Examples

Queen Victoria ruled Britain
Queen Victoria reigned for over 63 years, marking one of the longest reigns in British history.
During the reign of Queen Victoria, Britain became the world's foremost global power.
Many inventions and social changes emerged during the era named after Queen Victoria.
