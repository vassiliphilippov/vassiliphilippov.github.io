to smear

verb
damage reputation with false statements
Pronunciation: / smɪr / (AmE), / smɪə / (BrE)
to damage the reputation of someone by making false or malicious statements about them

Examples

to smear a rival
She refused to smear her coworkers, even when pressured to spread lies about them.
The politician claimed that his opponent tried to smear his reputation during the debate.
The tabloid attempted to smear several celebrities with unfounded allegations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defame: to damage the reputation of someone by making false statements
  • antonym – praise: to express warm approval or admiration of someone
  • synonym – slander: to make false spoken statements that damage someone's reputation
