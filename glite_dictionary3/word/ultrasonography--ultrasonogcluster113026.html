ultrasonography

noun
imaging using ultrasound waves
Pronunciation: / ˌʌl.trə.soʊˈnɑː.ɡrə.fi / (AmE), / ˌʌl.trə.səʊˈnɒ.ɡrə.fi / (BrE)
A technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce images of internal body structures, commonly used in medical diagnosis.

Examples

medical ultrasonography scan
Advances in ultrasonography have improved the detection of abdominal diseases.
The doctor recommended ultrasonography to monitor the development of the baby during pregnancy.
Veterinarians often use ultrasonography to examine animals' internal organs without surgery.
