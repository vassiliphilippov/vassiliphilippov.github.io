to throw

verb
to confuse or perplex
Pronunciation: / θroʊ /; / θɹoʊ / (AmE), / θrəʊ / (BrE)
to confuse or unsettle someone; to make someone unable to think clearly or act decisively

Examples

to throw someone off
Her sudden question completely threw me during the interview.
I was thrown by the unexpected change in the schedule.
The complicated instructions can really throw you if you’re not careful.
His unexpected reaction threw me completely off balance during the meeting.
The sudden change of plans threw everyone for a loop.
The surprise announcement really threw her and she didn't know how to respond.
