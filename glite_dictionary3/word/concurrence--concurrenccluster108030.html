concurrence

noun
agreement in opinion or result
Pronunciation: / kənˈkɝːəns /; / kənˈkɜr.əns / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrəns /; / kənˈkʌr.əns / (BrE)
the state of agreeing, working together, or cooperating with others

Examples

concurrence of opinion
The committee reached concurrence after a lengthy discussion.
The study's findings were in concurrence with previous research results.
There was a strong concurrence among the experts regarding the cause of the problem.
Effective policy requires the concurrence of all major stakeholders.
Government funding was granted in concurrence with private investment to support the initiative.
The success of the project depended on the concurrence of several different departments.
