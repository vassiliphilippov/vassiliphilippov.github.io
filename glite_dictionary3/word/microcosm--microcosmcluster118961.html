microcosm

noun
a miniature model of something larger
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkrəˌkɑzəm / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrə(ʊ)ˌkɒzəm / (BrE)
a small, representative system or community that reflects the characteristics of a larger whole

Examples

microcosm of society
Her novel portrays a microcosm of Victorian England through the residents of one small village.
The classroom can be seen as a microcosm of society, reflecting its diversity and challenges.
This aquarium acts as a microcosm of the ocean's ecosystem, complete with coral and small fish.

Related Terms

  • antonym – macrocosm: the whole of a complex structure, especially the world or universe, contrasted with a microcosm
