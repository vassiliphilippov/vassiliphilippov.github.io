saturated

adjective
having all valence bonds filled (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsatʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an organic compound) having no double or triple bonds between carbon atoms; all available valence bonds are filled, typically with hydrogen.

Examples

saturated fat
A saturated hydrocarbon contains only single bonds between its carbon atoms.
Butter and lard are high in saturated fats.
Many nutritionists advise limiting your intake of saturated fat.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unsaturated: having double or triple bonds between carbon atoms (chemistry)
