ratlin

noun
rope rung on a ship's rigging
Pronunciation: / ˈræt.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈræt.lɪn / (BrE)
(nautical) A small horizontal rope fastened between the shrouds of a sailing ship, forming steps of a ladder for climbing aloft.

Examples

climb the ratlin ropes
She scrambled up the ratlins with practiced ease, reaching the top sail.
The sailor placed his foot on a ratlin and began to climb up the mast.
Years of weather had frayed the old ratlins on the port side.
