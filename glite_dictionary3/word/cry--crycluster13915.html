to cry

verb
reach a condition by crying
Pronunciation: / kraɪ / (AmE), / kraɪ / (BrE)
to shed tears, usually because of sadness, pain, or strong emotion

Examples

to cry tears
After the breakup, she cried herself hoarse.
He was so exhausted that he cried himself to sleep.
The child cried himself into a headache before his mother could comfort him.
After hearing the bad news, he couldn't help but cry uncontrollably.
She tried not to cry during the sad movie, but tears streamed down her face.
The baby began to cry when it was hungry.

Related Terms

  • antonym – laugh: to make sounds of amusement, often the antonym of crying in emotional expression
  • synonym – weep: to shed tears, especially as an expression of sorrow
