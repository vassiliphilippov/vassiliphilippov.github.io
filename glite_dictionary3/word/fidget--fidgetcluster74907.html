fidget

noun
restless or agitated feeling with small movements
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdʒət / (AmE), / ˈfɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
a feeling of restlessness or agitation, often resulting in small, nervous movements

Examples

have the fidgets
Children often get the fidgets when they're bored and have to sit still.
He tried to hide his fidget, but kept tapping his foot nervously.
The long lecture gave me the fidgets after an hour.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: a peaceful or relaxed state, free from agitation
  • synonym – restlessness: the state of being unable to relax or remain still
