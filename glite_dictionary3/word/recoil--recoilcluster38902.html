to recoil

verb
to have a negative effect on the originator
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔɪl / (BrE)
(of an action) to have an unintended negative effect on the person or group who initiated it; to backfire.

Examples

to recoil on him
His attempt to deceive others eventually recoiled upon himself.
The new policy recoiled on the company, leading to public criticism.
Threatening legal action may recoil on you if the facts are not clear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backfire: (of an action) to have the opposite effect to that intended, causing harm to the originator
