to seel

verb
sew up the eyelids of birds (falconry)
Pronunciation: / siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
to sew up the eyelids of hawks and falcons, especially to render them temporarily blind for training purposes

Examples

to seel a hawk's eyes
After a few weeks, the stitches used to seel the hawk's eyelids were carefully removed.
In traditional falconry, it was common practice to seel the young bird's eyes during training.
The handler decided to seel the falcon temporarily to keep it calm.
