to refracture

verb
break a previously mended bone again
Pronunciation: / riˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / riˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
to break a bone again after it has healed improperly, usually to correct the original problem

Examples

to refracture a bone
After examining the X-ray, the doctor decided to refracture the poorly healed bone.
Sometimes it is necessary to refracture a limb in order to ensure proper healing.
The surgeon had to refracture her wrist to correct the misalignment.

  • synonym – fracture: to break a bone or hard material
