specular

adjective
mirror-like or highly reflective
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
reflecting light in a manner similar to a mirror; having a shiny, mirror-like surface

Examples

specular reflection surface
Raindrops on the road can create dangerous specular highlights that dazzle drivers at night.
The gemstone was admired for its specular sheen under direct light.
The scientist noted the specular reflection from the polished metal surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym – diffuse: scattering light in many directions, opposite of specular
  • synonym – reflective: capable of reflecting light, but not necessarily in a mirror-like manner
