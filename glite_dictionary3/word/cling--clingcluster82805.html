to cling

verb
hold on tightly
Pronunciation: / klɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪŋ / (BrE)
to hold on tightly or stick closely to someone or something, either physically or by being hard to remove

Examples

to cling to a rock
He clung to the edge of the rock, afraid to let go.
She clings to her beliefs, even when others disagree.
The frightened kitten clung to its owner's sweater during the thunderstorm.
Even after several washes, the smell clings to the fabric.
The wet shirt clung to his back after the rainstorm.
Tiny bits of paper cling to the adhesive tape.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adhere: to stick fast to a surface or substance
  • antonym – detach: to separate or remove from something else
  • synonym – grip: to hold something tightly
  • antonym – release: to let go of something held
  • synonym – stick: to adhere to a surface or object
