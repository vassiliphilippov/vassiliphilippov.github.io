clear-eyed

adjective
perceptive and objective
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌaɪd / (BrE)
having an ability to think clearly and objectively; able to perceive and judge things accurately and without emotion

Examples

clear-eyed judgment
Despite the excitement, he remained clear-eyed about the potential risks involved.
Her clear-eyed analysis of the situation helped the company make the right choice.
The biography provides a clear-eyed look at the challenges the artist faced throughout her career.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clear-sighted: able to perceive and understand things clearly
  • synonym – perspicacious: having a ready insight into and understanding of things
  • antonym – unimaginative: not using or showing imagination or creativity (contrasting lack of insight/creativity)
