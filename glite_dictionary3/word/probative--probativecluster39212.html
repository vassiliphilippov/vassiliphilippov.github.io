probative

adjective
providing or tending to prove; evidential (esp. legal)
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊ.bə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.bə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of evidence or information) providing proof or helping to establish the truth of a statement or allegation, especially in a legal context.

Examples

probative evidence in court
If the probative effect of the evidence outweighs any prejudice, it should be admitted.
The attorney argued that the photographs had significant probative value.
The judge found the witness's testimony highly probative in establishing the facts of the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym – evidential: relating to or providing evidence
  • antonym – irrelevant: not related to what is being discussed or proved; lacking significance
