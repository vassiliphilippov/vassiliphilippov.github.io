NREM

noun
stage of sleep without rapid eye movement
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnˌɑːrˌiːˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛnˌɑːrˌiːˈɛm / (BrE)
the phase of sleep in which there are no rapid eye movements or dreaming, typically accounting for most of a normal night's sleep

Examples

NREM sleep stage
Children typically spend a higher percentage of the night in NREM sleep than adults.
Most deep restorative sleep occurs during NREM rather than REM stages.
Sleep studies have shown that NREM sleep is crucial for physical recovery.
