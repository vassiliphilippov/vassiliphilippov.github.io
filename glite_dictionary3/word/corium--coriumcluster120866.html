corium

noun
the deep vascular layer of skin
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔriəm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːriəm / (BrE)
the deep, vascular inner layer of the skin, lying beneath the epidermis; dermis.

Examples

thick corium layer
Beneath the thin outer skin lies the corium, which contains many blood vessels and nerves.
Burns that reach the corium are considered serious and require immediate medical attention.
The surgeon carefully separated the corium from the surrounding tissues during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dermis: the thick layer of living tissue below the epidermis
