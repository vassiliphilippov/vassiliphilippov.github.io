oarfish

noun
long deep-sea fish with red dorsal fin
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, elongated deep-water fish with a red dorsal fin, often reaching lengths of 20 to 30 feet, found in tropical and temperate oceans.

Examples

giant oarfish sighting
Many myths about sea serpents may be based on sightings of the elusive oarfish.
Scientists were excited when a giant oarfish washed ashore on the Californian coast.
The oarfish is rarely seen by humans because it lives at great ocean depths.
