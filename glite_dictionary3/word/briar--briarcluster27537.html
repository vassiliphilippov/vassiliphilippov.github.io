briar

noun
prickly Eurasian wild rose
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə / (BrE)
a prickly plant or bush, such as a wild rose or woody vine, with thorny stems

Examples

thorny briar bush
She carefully picked her way through the briar, avoiding the sharp thorns on the stems.
The briar bushes in the hedgerow were covered with bright pink blossoms in spring.
The scent of crushed briar leaves filled the air as they walked through the meadow.
After the storm, thick briar blocked the entrance to the old garden.
Birds sometimes build nests among the briar for protection from predators.
The path was overgrown with briar, making it difficult to walk through without getting scratched.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bramble: a prickly, scrambling vine or shrub, particularly of the genus Rubus
  • synonym – rose: a woody perennial plant of the genus Rosa, known for its fragrant flowers
