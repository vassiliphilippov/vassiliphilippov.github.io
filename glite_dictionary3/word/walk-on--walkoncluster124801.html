walk-on

noun
minor non-speaking acting role
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔk ɑn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɒn / (BrE)
A small acting role in a play, film, or television show, often without spoken lines.

Examples

a walk-on role
He landed a walk-on in a popular television series.
Many aspiring actors start their careers with walk-on roles in theatres.
She made a brief walk-on during the final scene of the movie.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cameo: brief appearance by a well-known person in a performance
  • antonym – leading role: main or principal part in a dramatic production
