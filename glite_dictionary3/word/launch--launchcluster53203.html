to launch

verb
begin vigorously
Pronunciation: / lɔntʃ /; / lɑntʃ / (AmE), / lɔːntʃ / (BrE)
to start or introduce something new, such as a project, activity, or career, often with energy or publicity

Examples

to launch a product
After the announcement, the CEO launched into a passionate speech about the company's future.
He launched immediately into his explanation, hardly pausing to take a breath.
She launched into a new fitness regime with great enthusiasm.
He hopes to launch his political career by running for local office.
She decided to launch her own business after years of experience in the industry.
The university plans to launch a new research center next year.
A major campaign was launched to raise awareness about climate change.
She will launch her own business next year after finishing her studies.
The company decided to launch a new training program for employees.
