Anglican

noun
follower of the Anglican Church
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡlɪkən / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlɪkən / (BrE)
a member of the Church of England or of a church that is in communion with it, adhering to its doctrine and practices

Examples

Anglican believer
As an Anglican, he values both scripture and tradition in his faith.
Many Anglicans around the world celebrate traditional holidays in similar ways.
She was raised as an Anglican and attends services every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Protestant: a member of any of the Western Christian churches separate from the Roman Catholic Church, following the principles of the Reformation
