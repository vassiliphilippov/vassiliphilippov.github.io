coloured

adjective
having non-white skin (dated, offensive)
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(of people) having skin that is not white, especially as historically used to classify people of mixed race in South Africa or, formerly, to refer to Black people; now considered offensive or dated in many contexts.

Examples

coloured community
He grew up in a community where most of the residents were identified as coloured.
Historical documents often referred to people of mixed ancestry as coloured in South Africa.
The term coloured is now considered offensive and is largely avoided in modern usage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – black: having dark skin pigmentation, especially of African descent
  • antonym – white: having light skin pigmentation, especially of European descent
