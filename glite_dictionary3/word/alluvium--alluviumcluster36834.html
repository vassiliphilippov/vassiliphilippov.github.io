alluvium

noun
sediment deposited by water
Pronunciation: / əˈluːviəm / (AmE), / əˈljuːvɪəm / (BrE)
Loose clay, silt, sand, or gravel that has been transported by running water and deposited, especially in a riverbed, floodplain, or delta.

Examples

alluvium in riverbed
After the flooding, a thick layer of alluvium covered the crops in the valley.
Geologists collected samples of alluvium to study changes in the stream's flow over time.
The fertile soils of the Nile Delta are composed largely of alluvium deposited by the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sediment: matter that settles to the bottom of a liquid
