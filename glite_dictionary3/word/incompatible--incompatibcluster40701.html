incompatible

adjective
unable to work together (medical, drugs, muscles)
Pronunciation: / ɪn.kəmˈpæt.ə.bəl /; / ˌɪnkəmˈpætəbəl / (AmE), / ɪn.kəmˈpæt.ɪ.bəl /; / ˌɪnkəmˈpætəbəl /; / ˌɪnkəmˈpætəbl /; / ˌɪnkəmˈpætɪbəl / (BrE)
not able to exist, work, or be used together without conflict; unable to combine or function harmoniously (applies to things, substances, ideas, devices, etc.)

Examples

incompatible devices
Certain muscle groups are incompatible and cannot contract at the same time.
These two antibiotics are incompatible and should not be administered together.
When taken together, the drugs have incompatible effects that can reduce their effectiveness.
Some chemicals are incompatible and must never be mixed for safety reasons.
The paints proved incompatible, resulting in a lumpy and uneven surface.
Water and oil are incompatible and will not form a uniform solution.
His decision to arrive late in casual clothes was completely incompatible with the company's formal culture.
Smoking is incompatible with the health regulations of this facility.
Such language is incompatible with the respectful tone expected in our meetings.
