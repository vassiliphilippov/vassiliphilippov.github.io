clutch

noun
a group collected or dealt with together
Pronunciation: / klʌtʃ / (AmE), / klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a group of things or people collected or occurring together, especially those to be dealt with at the same time

Examples

clutch of letters
A clutch of letters waited on the kitchen table.
A clutch of problems emerged just before the project's deadline.
She interviewed a clutch of job applicants in one afternoon.
