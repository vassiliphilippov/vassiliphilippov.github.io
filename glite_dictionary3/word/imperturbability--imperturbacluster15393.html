imperturbability

noun
calmness; being unruffled
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɜrtɚbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːtəˌbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of remaining calm and unshaken, especially under pressure or in difficult situations

Examples

remarkable imperturbability
Even in the midst of chaos, her imperturbability impressed everyone around her.
He handled the heated argument with remarkable imperturbability, never raising his voice or losing control.
The pilot's imperturbability during the emergency was critical to the safe landing.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agitation: a state of anxiety or nervous excitement; the opposite of calmness
  • synonym – composure: the state of being calm and in control of oneself
  • synonym – equanimity: mental calmness and evenness of temper, especially in difficult situations
