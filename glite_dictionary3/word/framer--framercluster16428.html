framer

noun
person who drafts a law or plan
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪ.mər / (AmE), / ˈfreɪ.mə / (BrE)
a person who drafts or formulates a new law, plan, or system, especially as part of a founding or constitution-making process

Examples

framer of the constitution
Modern legislators often study the work of the original framers for inspiration.
She was recognized as one of the principal framers of the peace agreement.
The framers of the Constitution debated every detail of the new government.
