gobbler

noun
someone who eats quickly and greedily
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑblər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblə / (BrE)
A person who eats quickly and greedily, often swallowing large mouthfuls at a time.

Examples

fast gobbler
He's such a gobbler that his plate is always empty first.
The teacher warned the students not to be gobblers during lunch and to chew their food properly.
Watching her brother eat like a gobbler made her lose her appetite.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glutton: a person who eats and drinks excessively or voraciously
  • antonym – nibbler: a person who eats small amounts slowly
