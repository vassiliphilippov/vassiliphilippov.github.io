prolific

adjective
highly productive; abundant in production
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɪfɪk / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, or person) producing much fruit, foliage, offspring, or many works; highly productive.

Examples

prolific rabbit
Rabbits are known to be prolific breeders, often producing many litters annually.
She is a prolific writer who publishes several novels each year.
The old apple tree in our yard has always been incredibly prolific every summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – barren: not producing or incapable of producing offspring or fruit
  • synonym – fruitful: producing good or useful results; productive
  • synonym – productive: producing or achieving a lot; effective
