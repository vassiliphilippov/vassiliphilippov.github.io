earth

noun
the planet we live on
Pronunciation: / ɝːθ / (AmE), / ɜːθ / (BrE)
the planet on which humans live; the world or the place where people exist, especially as opposed to heaven or the afterlife

Examples

planet earth

Climate change is affecting the whole earth, not just one country.
The earth takes about 365 days to orbit the sun.
He believed in focusing on the here and now, on matters of earth rather than the afterlife.
Many religious texts contrast the transience of earth with the promises of heaven.
Monks are taught to renounce the pleasures of earth to seek spiritual enlightenment.
It felt like paradise on earth when they finally reunited.
Many religions distinguish between earth, Heaven, and Hell as different realms of existence.
Some believe that our actions on earth determine our fate in the afterlife.
