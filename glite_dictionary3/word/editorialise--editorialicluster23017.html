to editorialise

verb
insert personal opinions into an objective statement
Pronunciation: / ˌɛdəˈtɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈtɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to express personal opinions or interpretation in what is supposed to be an objective report or statement

Examples

to editorialise in news articles
Editors reminded contributors not to editorialise in scientific reports.
Rather than simply reporting the facts, she tended to editorialise in her articles.
The journalist was criticized for choosing to editorialise during the news broadcast.
