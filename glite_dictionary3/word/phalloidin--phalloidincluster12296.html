phalloidin

noun
toxic peptide from death cap mushroom
Pronunciation: / fæˈlɔɪdɪn / (AmE), / fæˈlɔɪdɪn / (BrE)
a highly toxic cyclic peptide found in the death cap mushroom (Amanita phalloides), belonging to the group of phallotoxins, used in cell biology and toxicology.

Examples

phalloidin staining cells
Phalloidin is well known for its role in disrupting cellular functions by binding to actin.
Due to its toxicity, phalloidin must be handled with great care in the laboratory.
The scientist used phalloidin to stain actin filaments in the cell culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – phallotoxin: a class of toxins of which phalloidin is a member
