mistrial

noun
invalid or inconclusive trial
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsˌtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌtraɪəl / (BrE)
A trial that is invalid or inconclusive, especially due to an error in the proceedings or inability of the jury to reach a verdict.

Examples

mistrial declared by judge
A mistrial can occur if critical evidence is mishandled during proceedings.
Because of a procedural error, the defendant’s case ended in a mistrial.
The judge declared a mistrial after the jury failed to reach a unanimous decision.
