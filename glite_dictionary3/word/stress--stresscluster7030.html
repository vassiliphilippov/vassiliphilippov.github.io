stress

noun
force per unit area (physics)
Pronunciation: / stɹɛs / (AmE), / stɹɛs / (BrE)
(Physics) The force per unit area applied to a material, producing strain or deformation.

Examples

stress on a material
Engineers must calculate the stress that a bridge's beams can safely withstand.
High stress in the cable could lead to structural failure if not properly managed.
When a metal bar is bent, the stress concentrates at the point of maximum curvature.

Related Terms

  • synonym – strain: (Physics) the deformation of a material due to applied stress
