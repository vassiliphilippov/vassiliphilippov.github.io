Mishnah

noun
first written collection of Jewish oral traditions
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪʃ.nə / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃ.nə / (BrE)
the first major written collection of Jewish oral traditions, forming the first part of the Talmud, compiled around AD 200.

Examples

study the Mishnah text
Many Jewish scholars dedicate years to the study of the Mishnah and its commentaries.
She referenced a passage from the Mishnah in her research on Jewish law.
The Mishnah forms the core text of Rabbinic Judaism, alongside the Gemara.
