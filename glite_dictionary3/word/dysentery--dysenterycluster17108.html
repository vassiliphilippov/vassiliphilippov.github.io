dysentery

noun
intestinal infection causing severe diarrhea
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈdɪsəntri / (BrE)
a serious disease of the intestines causing severe diarrhea with the presence of blood and mucus, often resulting from infection

Examples

dysentery outbreak
During the expedition, several members became ill with dysentery.
Poor water sanitation can lead to the spread of dysentery among local populations.
The outbreak of dysentery in the camp required immediate medical attention.
