IDDM

noun
insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus (Type 1 diabetes)
Pronunciation: / aɪ diː diː ɛm / (AmE), / aɪ diː diː ɛm / (BrE)
abbreviation for insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus, a form of diabetes requiring regular insulin administration due to the body's inability to produce insulin

Examples

IDDM diagnosis in children
Children diagnosed with IDDM often require careful monitoring of their blood sugar levels.
Managing IDDM involves a balance of diet, exercise, and daily insulin injections.
The risk of complications from IDDM can be reduced with proper medical care.

Related Terms

  • antonym – NIDDM: non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus; Type 2 diabetes
  • synonym – Type 1 diabetes: autoimmune diabetes marked by a lack of insulin production
