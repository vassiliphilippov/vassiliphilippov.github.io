catecholamine

noun
group of related hormones and neurotransmitters
Pronunciation: / ˌkæt̬ɪˈkoʊləˌmin / (AmE), / ˌkætɪˈkəʊləmiːn / (BrE)
any of a group of chemicals, such as epinephrine and norepinephrine, produced mainly by the adrenal glands and acting as hormones or neurotransmitters

Examples

catecholamine levels in blood
Doctors measured the level of catecholamines in his blood to assess adrenal function.
Some tumors can cause excessive production of catecholamine, leading to high blood pressure.
The body releases catecholamines in response to stress or danger.
