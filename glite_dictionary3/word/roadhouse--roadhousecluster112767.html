roadhouse

noun
restaurant/bar on a main road, often with entertainment
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊdˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an inn or restaurant, usually located outside a town or on a main road, where meals, drinks, and entertainment such as dancing or gambling may be provided

Examples

roadhouse with live music
During the 1920s, many roadhouses offered illegal gambling along with drinks.
The old roadhouse on Highway 7 is famous for its Saturday night dances.
They stopped at a roadhouse for dinner and live music after a long drive.
