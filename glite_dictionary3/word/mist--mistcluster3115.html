to mist

verb
spray or cover with fine droplets
Pronunciation: / mɪst / (AmE), / mɪst / (BrE)
to spray or cover with fine droplets, or to become misty or unclear because of fine droplets

Examples

to mist plants
After exercising, he likes to mist his face with cool water.
She used a bottle to mist the delicate leaves every morning.
The florist gently misted the flowers to keep them fresh.
Heavy fog can mist the headlights, making driving dangerous.
The photographer worried that the rain would mist his lens and ruin the shot.
The windows began to mist over as the temperature dropped.
The bathroom mirror misted after my hot shower.
The greenhouse windows often mist over on chilly mornings.
When I stepped outside, my glasses immediately misted up.
