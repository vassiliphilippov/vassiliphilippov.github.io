ballottement

noun
palpatory technique for detecting floating objects in the body
Pronunciation: / bəˈlɒtmənt / (AmE), / bəˈlɒtmənt / (BrE)
a clinical technique in which a floating object in the body, such as a fetus, is felt by a quick tap that causes it to rebound against the examining finger

Examples

ballottement test knee
A positive ballottement sign can help confirm the presence of excess fluid in the knee joint.
Obstetricians are trained to perform ballottement as part of assessing fetal health.
The doctor used ballottement to estimate the position of the fetus during the examination.
