alveolus

noun
tiny air sac in the lungs
Pronunciation: / ælˈvioʊləs / (AmE), / ælˈviːələs / (BrE)
A tiny air sac in the lungs where the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place.

Examples

lung alveolus
Damage to the alveolus can impair breathing in patients with lung disease.
Each alveolus is surrounded by a network of tiny capillaries.
Oxygen passes from each alveolus into the surrounding blood vessels in the lungs.
