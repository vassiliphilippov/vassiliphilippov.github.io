blabber

noun
person who reveals secrets, esp. to authorities
Pronunciation: / ˈblæbər / (AmE), / ˈblæbə / (BrE)
a person who reveals confidential information, especially to authorities.

Examples

office blabber
No one wanted to be labeled a blabber after last year’s incident.
She was called a blabber for telling the principal about the secret meeting.
The gang refused to trust anyone they thought might be a blabber.

Related Terms

  • synonym – informant: a person who provides confidential information to authorities
