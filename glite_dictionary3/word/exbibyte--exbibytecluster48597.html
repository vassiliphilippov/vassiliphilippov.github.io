exbibyte

noun
unit of digital information (2^60 bytes)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksbɪˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛksbɪˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,024 pebibytes or 2^60 bytes, used to measure digital storage capacity.

Examples

exbibyte of data
Scientific simulations can generate several exbibytes of data that must be archived and analyzed.
The new data center has a storage capacity of over two exbibytes, making it one of the largest in the world.
Transferring an exbibyte of data over the internet would take years with current technology.
