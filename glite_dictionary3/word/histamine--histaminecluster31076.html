histamine

noun
biological compound involved in immune and allergic responses
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪstəˌmin / (AmE), / ˈhɪstəmiːn / (BrE)
a compound formed in the body from histidine, which causes the expansion of blood vessels and stimulates gastric secretion, especially during allergic reactions

Examples

histamine release in allergy
High levels of histamine can cause symptoms such as itching and swelling.
Some allergy medications work by blocking the effects of histamine.
When pollen is detected, the body releases histamine, leading to sneezing and watery eyes.
