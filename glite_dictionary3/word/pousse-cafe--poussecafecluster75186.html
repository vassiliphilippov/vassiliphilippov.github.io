pousse-cafe

noun
layered after-dinner liqueur drink
Pronunciation: / pus kæˈfeɪ / (AmE), / puːs kæˈfeɪ / (BrE)
A small after-dinner drink consisting of several liqueurs carefully poured so as to remain in separate layers.

Examples

colorful pousse-cafe layers
After dinner, she ordered a pousse-cafe to finish her meal with something sweet and colorful.
He skillfully prepared a pousse-cafe, pouring each liqueur slowly to create distinct layers.
The dessert menu included a classic pousse-cafe made with brandy, crème de menthe, and cherry liqueur.
