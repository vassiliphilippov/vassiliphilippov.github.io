fictionalization

noun
work based on real events but written as fiction
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of creating a fictional version of real events or people, or a work that presents real events as fiction

Examples

fictionalization of real events
Her latest novel is a fictionalization of her grandfather's experiences during the war.
The movie is a fictionalization of true events from the 1960s civil rights movement.
There is ongoing debate about how much creative liberty a fictionalization should take with historical facts.
His documentary blurs the line between fact and fictionalization.
Many movies are based on the fictionalization of real-life crimes.
The author’s fictionalization of historical events brought new interest to the story.
