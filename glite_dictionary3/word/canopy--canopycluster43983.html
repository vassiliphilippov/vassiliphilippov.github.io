to canopy

verb
to cover with a canopy
Pronunciation: / ˈkænəpi / (AmE), / ˈkanəpi / (BrE)
to cover or shelter with or as if with a canopy

Examples

to canopy a garden
A thick layer of leaves canopied the forest floor, creating patches of shade.
The ancient king's throne was canopied with silk to denote his status.
Vines had grown over the old gazebo, canopying the entire structure in green.

  • antonym – uncover: to remove a cover from
