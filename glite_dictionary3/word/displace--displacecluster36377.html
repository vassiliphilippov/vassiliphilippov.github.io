to displace

verb
remove someone from a job or position
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave or move from their usual place, or to take the place of someone or something

Examples

to displace workers
Automation may displace many factory workers in the coming years.
She was displaced from her role after the department merged with another.
The company had to displace several managers during the reorganization.
In some regions, electric vehicles are beginning to displace traditional gasoline cars.
Rising water levels could displace local populations along the river.
The new manager will displace several old policies that are no longer effective.
She feared that a new manager would displace her from her current position.
Technological innovations often displace older methods and practices in the industry.
The construction of the new highway will displace hundreds of residents from their homes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dismiss: to remove someone from their job officially; to fire
  • synonym – evict: to force someone to leave a place, especially a home
  • synonym – fire: to dismiss someone from their job
  • antonym – hire: to employ someone for a job
  • synonym – move: to change the place or position of something or someone
  • synonym – relocate: to move to a new place or position
  • synonym – replace: to take the place of something that is no longer used or needed
  • synonym – supersede: to take the place of; to replace
