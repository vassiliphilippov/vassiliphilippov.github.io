antibiosis

noun
harmful association between organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tɪ.baɪˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌantɪ.baɪˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
an association between two organisms in which one is adversely affected by the other or by its metabolic products

Examples

antibiosis between bacteria
Researchers observed antibiosis between certain bacteria and their competitors in the petri dish.
The production of antibiotics by fungi is a classic example of antibiosis in nature.
Understanding antibiosis can help develop new strategies to control harmful microbes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – symbiosis: a close and long-term biological interaction between two different organisms
