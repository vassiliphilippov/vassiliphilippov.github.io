ignobleness

noun
quality of being ignoble or base
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈnoʊb(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnəʊb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being ignoble; lacking nobility of character or purpose; baseness.

Examples

moral ignobleness
His betrayal revealed a shocking degree of ignobleness that no one had suspected.
She regretted the moment of ignobleness that led her to act selfishly.
The story illustrates the ignobleness often found in those obsessed with power.

Related Terms

  • synonym – baseness: the quality of lacking moral principles
  • antonym – nobility: the quality of being noble in character or rank
