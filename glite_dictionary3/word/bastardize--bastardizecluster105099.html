to bastardize

verb
corrupt or lower the quality of something
Pronunciation: / ˈbæstɚˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədaɪz / (BrE)
to lower the quality or value of something by adding unwanted elements or by changing it in a way that makes it less pure or true to its original form

Examples

to bastardize a tradition
He refused to bastardize his family's recipes just to appeal to modern tastes.
Many critics argue that reality television has bastardized the art of documentary filmmaking.
When companies try to mass-produce traditional crafts, they often bastardize the original designs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrupt: to make something less pure or honest
  • synonym – debase: to lower in quality or character
