chromesthesia

noun
a type of synesthesia involving color sensations
Pronunciation: / kroʊməˈθiʒə / (AmE), / krəʊmɛsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
a form of synesthesia in which sounds or other nonvisual stimuli evoke the perception of colors

Examples

chromesthesia with music
For some people with chromesthesia, every musical note appears as a different color.
Scientists have developed tests to measure the consistency of color associations in chromesthesia.
The artist described how her chromesthesia influences her choice of colors when painting.
