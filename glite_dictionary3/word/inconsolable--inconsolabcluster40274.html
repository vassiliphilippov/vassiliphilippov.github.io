inconsolable

adjective
unable to be comforted
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkənˈsoʊləbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈsəʊləbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their grief) unable to be comforted; deeply and persistently sad.

Examples

inconsolable after loss
After losing their beloved pet, the children were inconsolable for days.
His inconsolable grief worried his friends, who didn't know how to help him.
She was inconsolable at her father's funeral, unable to speak through her tears.

Related Terms

  • antonym – comforted: having received comfort or consolation
  • synonym – heartbroken: overwhelmed by great sadness or grief
