to settle

verb
arrange or put in order
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl /; / ˈsɛtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl /; / ˈsɛtl̩ / (BrE)
to arrange or place things or yourself in a particular position or order

Examples

to settle belongings
After unpacking, he settled his belongings in neat rows on the dresser.
She carefully settled the books on the shelf, arranging them by size.
The waiter settled the plates and cutlery on the table before serving the food.
After a long journey, he finally settled himself by the fireplace.
She settled into her favorite armchair with a sigh of relief.
The bird settled on the branch and tucked its wings.
