to downsize

verb
reduce workforce; lay off staff
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (BrE)
to reduce the number of employees in a company or organization, usually to cut costs

Examples

to downsize a company
After the merger, several departments were forced to downsize.
Many workers were downsized as the factory struggled to remain profitable.
The company plans to downsize its staff by fifteen percent this year.
After the merger, management decided to downsize the marketing department.
Many employees were worried that the firm might downsize before the end of the year.
The company was forced to downsize after several quarters of declining profits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expand: to increase in size or number (especially of a business or staff)
  • synonym – lay off: to dismiss employees, usually for economic reasons
