to tack

verb
turn a sailing vessel into the wind
Pronunciation: / tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
to change the direction of a sailing boat by turning its bow through the wind, or to change direction or approach suddenly (literal or figurative)

Examples

to tack the boat
The captain ordered the crew to tack as the wind shifted suddenly.
The yacht tacked smoothly, impressing everyone on board with its maneuverability.
To reach the harbor, we had to tack several times against the strong breeze.
If the project runs into trouble, we may need to tack and reconsider our strategy.
Midway through the negotiations, she tacked and took a much harder stance.
When criticism grew, the government decided to tack and adopt a new approach.

Related Terms

  • antonym – jibe: to change the course of a sailing vessel by turning the stern through the wind
  • antonym – persist: to continue steadily in an action or course
  • synonym – veer: to change direction suddenly
