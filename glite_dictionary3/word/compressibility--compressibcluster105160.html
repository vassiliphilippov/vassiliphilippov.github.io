compressibility

noun
ability to be compressed
Pronunciation: / kəmˌprɛsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kəmˌprɛsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The ability of a substance or material to decrease in volume when pressure is applied.

Examples

compressibility of gases
Engineers must consider the compressibility of materials when designing underwater structures.
The compressibility of gases allows them to be stored in pressurized containers.
Water has much lower compressibility than air, making it more resistant to being squeezed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – incompressibility: the property of being unable to be compressed
