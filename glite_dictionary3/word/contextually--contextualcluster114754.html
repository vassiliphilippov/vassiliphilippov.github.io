contextually

adverb
in a context-dependent way
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛkstʃuəli / (AmE), / kənˈtɛkstjʊəli / (BrE)
In a way that depends on or relates to the context.

Examples

to understand contextually
Historical data should be analyzed contextually rather than in isolation.
Some words are only offensive when used contextually in a certain way.
The meaning of the phrase must be interpreted contextually to avoid misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym – context: the circumstances that form the setting for an event or statement
