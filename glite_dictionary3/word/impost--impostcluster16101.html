impost

noun
tax or duty on imports/exports
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pəʊst / (BrE)
a tax or duty imposed on imported or exported goods; money collected under a tariff

Examples

customs impost tax
Colonial ports often collected an impost on all imported products.
Merchants protested against the new impost, claiming it would harm trade.
The government increased the impost on luxury goods to generate more revenue.
