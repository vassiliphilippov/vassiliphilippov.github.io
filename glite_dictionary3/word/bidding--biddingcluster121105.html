bidding

noun
command or request to act or attend
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
an order or command given by someone, especially a person with authority

Examples

at the bidding of a leader
He changed jobs at the bidding of his mentor.
She attended the meeting at the chairman's bidding.
The king summoned his advisors at his bidding.
He left the party early, doing his mother's bidding without complaint.
She only attended the meeting at her manager's bidding.
The knight acted at the king's bidding and rode into battle without hesitation.
