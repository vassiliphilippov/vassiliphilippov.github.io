denigrating

adjective
disparaging or damaging (of statements)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛnəˌɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛnɪɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a statement or remark) intended to harm someone's reputation, often by being unjustly critical or untrue.

Examples

denigrating comments
She was upset by the denigrating article that misrepresented her work.
Such denigrating comments can seriously damage a person's reputation.
The politician was criticized for making denigrating remarks about his opponents during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defamatory: meant to harm someone's reputation by making false statements
