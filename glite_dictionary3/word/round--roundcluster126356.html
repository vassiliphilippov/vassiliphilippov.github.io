to round

verb
express as a round number
Pronunciation: / raʊnd / (AmE), / raʊnd / (BrE)
to express or adjust a number to the nearest whole number or to a specified degree of accuracy

Examples

to round a number
The cashier will round your change to the nearest dollar.
When estimating costs, it's common to round up the final amount.
You should always round your answer to two decimal places in this calculation.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – round down: to decrease a number to the nearest whole number or specified place below
  • synonym – round off: to make a number simpler by approximating it to the nearest round number
  • degree higher – round up: to increase a number to the nearest whole number or specified place
