reproach

noun
disgrace; state of shame
Pronunciation: / rɪˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
an expression of criticism, disapproval, or shame; the state of being in disgrace

Examples

reproach from parents
Living in poverty is no reproach if one behaves with dignity.
She feared that her actions would bring reproach upon her family name.
The scandal was a source of reproach for the entire organization.
He looked at his friend with a glance of reproach after she broke her promise.
Her voice was filled with reproach as she addressed her careless teammate.
The manager’s mild reproach made the staff aware of their mistake.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disgrace: Loss of reputation or respect, often as a result of a dishonorable action
  • antonym – honor: High respect or esteem; the opposite of disgrace
  • antonym – praise: expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym – rebuke: an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
