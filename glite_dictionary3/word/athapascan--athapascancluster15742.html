Athapascan

noun
a person of an Athapaskan-speaking Indigenous group
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (BrE)
A member of any of the Indigenous peoples of North America who speak an Athapaskan language, especially those living in subarctic regions of western Canada and central Alaska.

Examples

an Athapascan person
Many Athapascan peoples have rich oral storytelling traditions passed down through generations.
She studied the migration patterns of early Athapascan groups across Alaska and Canada.
The Athapascan tribes adapted to the harsh subarctic climate with ingenuity and resilience.
