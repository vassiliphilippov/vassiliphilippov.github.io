magnetically

adverb
by use of magnetism
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
By means of magnetism; using or relating to magnetic forces or properties.

Examples

magnetically store data
The data is stored magnetically on hard drives in the computer lab.
The trains are magnetically levitated above the tracks for a smoother ride.
This substance can be magnetically separated from the non-magnetic materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym – magnetism: The physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces.
