Thrace

proper noun
ancient region in Balkan Peninsula
Pronunciation: / θreɪs / (AmE), / θreɪs / (BrE)
an ancient region in southeastern Europe, situated in the eastern Balkan Peninsula, historically known for wine production; it was colonized by the Greeks and became a Roman province. Today, its territory is divided among Bulgaria, Greece, and Turkey.

Examples

ancient Thrace region
Thrace was known in antiquity for its excellent wines and strategic location.
Modern political borders have divided Thrace among three different countries.
Some ancient Greek colonies were established along the coast of Thrace.
