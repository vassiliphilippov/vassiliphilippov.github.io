amoeba

noun
single-celled water-dwelling organism
Pronunciation: / əˈmibə / (AmE), / əˈmiːbə / (BrE)
a single-celled organism that lives in water or moist environments and moves by forming temporary projections called pseudopods

Examples

microscopic amoeba
An amoeba absorbs food particles from its surroundings.
Scientists observed the amoeba moving by extending its pseudopods.
The lake water contained several amoebae under the microscope.
