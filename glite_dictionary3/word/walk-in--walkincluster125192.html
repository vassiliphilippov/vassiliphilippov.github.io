walk-in

noun
an assured or easy victory
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɪn / (BrE)
an easy or assured victory, especially in an election

Examples

walk-in victory
It was clear from the polls that the mayoral race would be a walk-in for the incumbent.
Some expected the game to be a walk-in, but it turned out to be very close.
The candidate's popularity made the election a walk-in for her party.
