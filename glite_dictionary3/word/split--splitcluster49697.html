split

noun
dividing shares to increase number without changing value
Pronunciation: / splɪt / (AmE), / splɪt / (BrE)
a share of money or valuables that has been divided between people, or an increase in the number of a corporation's outstanding shares by issuing more shares to current shareholders

Examples

split of profits
After the split, each shareholder owned twice as many shares as before.
Analysts predicted that a stock split could boost trading volume.
The company declared a three-for-one split to attract more investors.
Each member of the group received an equal split of the stolen goods.
He demanded his split before they divided the money.
She refused to give him his split after the job was done.
