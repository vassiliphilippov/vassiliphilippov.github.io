Romany

noun
member of an itinerant ethnic group originally from India
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊməˌni, ˈrɑməni / (AmE), / ˈrɒməni / (BrE)
a member of a traditionally itinerant people originating from northern India, now living worldwide, especially in Europe, North Africa, and North America, noted for their distinct language and culture

Examples

a Romany person
A Romany community settled in the outskirts of the city, maintaining their distinct way of life.
Many Romany migrated across Europe over the centuries, adapting to different cultures.
The Romany have a rich oral tradition of music and storytelling.
