Saint James the Apostle

proper noun
apostle of Jesus in the New Testament
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz ði əˈpɑstl̩ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz ði əˈpɒstl̩ / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, brother of John, and traditionally considered the author of the Epistle of James.
