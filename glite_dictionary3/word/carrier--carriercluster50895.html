carrier

noun
organism with masked recessive gene
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriər /; / ˈkæriɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæriə / (BrE)
an organism (person or animal) that carries a disease or a gene without showing symptoms, and can pass it to others

Examples

disease carrier person
Both parents were asymptomatic carriers of the disease, increasing the risk for their children.
Genetic counseling can help identify if someone is a carrier of inherited disorders.
She found out she was a carrier for the cystic fibrosis gene after genetic testing.
A blood test revealed he was a carrier of the infection.
Some animals can act as a carrier of viruses that affect humans.
Typhoid Mary was a famous carrier who spread disease despite having no symptoms herself.

Related Terms

  • synonym – heterozygote: an organism having two different alleles for a particular gene
  • synonym – vector: organism that transmits a pathogen without being affected
