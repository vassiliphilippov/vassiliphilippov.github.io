hypobetalipoproteinemia

noun
hereditary disorder causing low beta-lipoproteins
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˌbeɪtəˌlaɪpoʊˌproʊtiˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpə(ʊ)ˌbiːtəˌlɪpəʊprəʊˈtiːniːə / (BrE)
A hereditary disorder characterized by abnormally low levels of beta-lipoproteins, lipids, and cholesterol in the blood.

Her family history revealed several cases of hypobetalipoproteinemia diagnosed in childhood.
