to connote

verb
suggest or imply an additional meaning
Pronunciation: / kəˈnoʊt / (AmE), / kəˈnəʊt / (BrE)
to express or suggest an additional meaning or idea in addition to the literal one; to imply or state indirectly

Examples

connote a hidden meaning
Calling the apartment "cozy" connotes that it may actually be quite small.
His smile connoted more than just politeness; it hinted at genuine affection.
The color white often connotes purity or innocence in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – denote: to be the literal or primary meaning; to indicate directly
