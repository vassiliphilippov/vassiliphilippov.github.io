little

adjective
small in quantity or degree
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl / (BrE)
(used with mass nouns) Small in quantity or degree; not much or almost none. When used with 'a', means at least some.

Examples

little hope remained
During the drought, little rain fell for several weeks.
He has little patience for unnecessary delays.
There was little interest in the proposal during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – few: small in number (used with count nouns)
  • antonym – much: large amount or degree (used as a quantifier with mass nouns)
  • degree higher – some: an unspecified quantity; at least a small amount
