Invar

noun
iron-nickel alloy with low thermal expansion
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌvɑr / (AmE), / ˈɪn.vɑː / (BrE)
an alloy of iron and nickel with a very low coefficient of thermal expansion, used where dimensional stability is required, such as in instruments and measuring devices.

Examples

Invar measuring instruments
Many scientific instruments use Invar to maintain precise calibrations over time.
The measuring tape was made of Invar to ensure its length would not change with temperature fluctuations.
