hydromel

noun
drink of honey and water
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌmɛl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəʊˌmɛl / (BrE)
A drink made by diluting honey in water, which becomes mead when fermented.

Examples

sweet hydromel drink
In ancient times, hydromel was valued for its sweetness and supposed medicinal qualities.
The monks prepared hydromel by mixing fresh honey with spring water.
When hydromel is allowed to ferment, it turns into mead.
