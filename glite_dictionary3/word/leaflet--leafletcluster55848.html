leaflet

noun
one part of a compound leaf (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈliːflət / (AmE), / ˈliːflɪt / (BrE)
a single segment or division of a compound leaf in plants; not an entire leaf but part of one that shares a stalk with others

Examples

fern leaflet
Each leaflet on the fern is intricately shaped and contributes to the overall beauty of the plant.
The scientist explained that a compound leaf consists of several leaflets attached to a single central stem.
When identifying the tree, look closely at how many leaflets make up each compound leaf.
