to ascend

verb
move to a higher position or status
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛnd / (AmE), / əˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to move to a higher or more important position or status (such as in a job or becoming a monarch)

Examples

to ascend the throne
After years of hard work, she finally ascended to the position of CEO.
He hopes to ascend the ranks and become a senior manager within five years.
Their company rapidly ascended to become a leader in the tech industry.
After her father's death, she ascended to the throne and became queen.
Historians record the exact year when Queen Victoria ascended to the British throne.
Upon the king’s abdication, his son ascended as the new monarch.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abdicate: to give up the throne or royal power
  • synonym – advance: to move forward or make progress, especially in a career
  • antonym – descend: to move to a lower position or status
  • synonym – rise: to move upwards in rank or status
