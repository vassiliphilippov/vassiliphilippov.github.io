antimuon

noun
antiparticle of a muon
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈmjuːɑn / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈmjuːɒn / (BrE)
the antiparticle of a muon, which decays into a positron, a neutrino, and an antineutrino

Examples

an antimuon decays
Researchers used the decay of the antimuon to study fundamental interactions in physics.
The antimuon is commonly produced in high-energy particle collisions.
When a antimuon encounters a muon, they can annihilate each other and release energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – antiparticle: a subatomic particle with the same mass as another particle but opposite electric charge and other differences
  • antonym – muon: an elementary particle similar to the electron, with negative electric charge and a greater mass
