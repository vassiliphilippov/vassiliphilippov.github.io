contingency

noun
possible future event or condition
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
a possible event or situation that might happen, especially one that could cause problems or depends on other things

Examples

contingency plan
Insurance provides financial protection against unexpected contingencies.

Management is required to have a contingency plan in case of unexpected events.

Their travel plans are subject to weather-related contingency.
