shack

noun
small, crudely built shelter
Pronunciation: / ʃæk / (AmE), / ʃæk / (BrE)
A small, roughly built shelter or dwelling, often made from inexpensive or salvaged materials.

Examples

old shack by the river
During the storm, we took shelter in an old shack by the river.
He built a makeshift shack out of wood and metal scraps.
They lived in a shack on the edge of town with no electricity or running water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cabin: a small, simple dwelling, usually in a rural area
  • synonym – hut: a small, simple, single-story house or shelter
