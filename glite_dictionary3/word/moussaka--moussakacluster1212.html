moussaka

noun
Greek/Middle Eastern baked dish with eggplant and meat
Pronunciation: / muˈsɑkə / (AmE), / muːˈsɑːkə / (BrE)
A baked dish of eggplant and ground meat, usually lamb, layered with tomatoes, onions, and spices, and topped with a creamy white sauce and eggs.

Examples

traditional moussaka recipe
My grandmother taught me how to make traditional moussaka from scratch.
The menu offers vegetarian moussaka as an alternative to the classic lamb version.
We ordered moussaka at the Greek restaurant, and it was delicious.
