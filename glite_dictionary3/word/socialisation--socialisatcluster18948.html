socialisation

noun
the process of learning societal norms
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person learns and adopts the customs, values, and behaviors of their society or social group

Examples

socialisation of children
Primary socialisation takes place within the family, teaching children basic values and expectations.
Rapid changes in technology have affected the way socialisation occurs among teenagers.
Schools play a significant role in the socialisation of young people.
