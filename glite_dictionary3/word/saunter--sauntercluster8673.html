to saunter

verb
walk slowly and leisurely
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːntə / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, without hurry or apparent purpose

Examples

to saunter down the street
He often saunters into the office late, carrying a cup of coffee.
She decided to saunter through the park, enjoying the warm afternoon sun.
The couple sauntered along the beach, not in any hurry to reach their destination.

  • antonym – stride: to walk with long, decisive steps
  • synonym – stroll: to walk in a slow and relaxed way
