to fluff

verb
make hair look fuller by ruffling
Pronunciation: / flʌf / (AmE), / flʌf / (BrE)
to make something (such as hair or fur) appear fuller, softer, or fluffier by shaking, brushing, or arranging it

Examples

to fluff a pillow
If you fluff your curls after drying, they will look bouncier and thicker.
She always fluffs her hair to make it look more voluminous before going out.
The stylist fluffed my bangs for a softer, natural effect.
Cold weather makes some animals fluff up their fur to stay warm.
She paused to fluff the pillows on the couch before guests arrived.
The parrot likes to fluff its feathers after a bath.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ruffle: to disturb the smoothness or regularity of something (e.g., feathers)
