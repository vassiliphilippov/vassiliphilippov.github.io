to reflect

verb
think deeply or carefully
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkt / (BrE)
to think deeply or carefully about something, especially when considering or analyzing it

Examples

to reflect on life
After the meeting, she took time to reflect on what had been discussed.
As I listened to the old song, I couldn't help but reflect on my childhood.
He likes to reflect before making important decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contemplate: to look thoughtfully for a long time at; to think deeply about something
  • synonym – ponder: to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • synonym – speculate: to form a theory or conjecture about a subject without firm evidence
