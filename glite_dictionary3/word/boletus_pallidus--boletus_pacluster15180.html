Boletus pallidus

noun
a species of bolete mushroom
Pronunciation: / boʊˈliː.təs pəˈlɪdəs / (AmE), / bəˈliː.təs pəˈlɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of bolete mushroom with an off-white to dingy brown cap, a similarly colored stalk, and pale greenish yellow tubes under the cap.

Examples

Boletus pallidus mushroom cap
Mushroom foragers often seek the Boletus pallidus in deciduous woodlands during summer.
The Boletus pallidus is easily recognized by its pale cap which darkens with age.
The tubes on the underside of the Boletus pallidus turn a pale greenish yellow as the fungus matures.
