plausibility

noun
apparent reasonableness or credibility
Pronunciation: / plɔːzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / plɔːzəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of seeming reasonable or probable; the appearance of being true or credible

Examples

plausibility of the story
He considered the plausibility of her story before deciding to believe her.
Some experts questioned the plausibility of the proposed theory.
The plausibility of his explanation convinced the jury of his innocence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – credibility: the quality of being trusted and believed in
  • antonym – implausibility: the state of being unlikely or not believable
