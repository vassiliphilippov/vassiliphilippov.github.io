cusp

noun
triangular flap of a heart valve
Pronunciation: / kʌsp / (AmE), / kʌsp / (BrE)
A thin, triangular flap forming part of a heart valve, allowing blood to flow in one direction.

Examples

heart valve cusp
A damaged cusp can lead to a leaky heart valve and impaired circulation.
Surgeons examined the aortic valve's cusps for any signs of abnormality during the procedure.
The mitral valve contains two cusps that control blood flow between the heart's chambers.
