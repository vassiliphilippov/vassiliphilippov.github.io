cartelization

noun
formation of a business cartel
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrˌtɛlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtɛlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which businesses in an industry form a cartel, agreeing to control production, prices, or marketing to reduce competition

Examples

cartelization of companies
Economists warn that cartelization stifles innovation and harms consumers.
Government policies aim to prevent the cartelization of major financial markets.
The cartelization of the oil industry led to higher prices for consumers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cartel: a group of businesses or nations that agree to limit competition
  • antonym – competition: the act of competing in business, often regarded as the opposite of cartelization
