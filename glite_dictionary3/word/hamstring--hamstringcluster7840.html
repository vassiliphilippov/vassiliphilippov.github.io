to hamstring

verb
to make powerless or ineffective
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæm.strɪŋ / (BrE)
to make someone or something powerless or ineffective, especially by restricting their activity or capability

Examples

to hamstring progress
Bureaucratic red tape continues to hamstring efforts to improve public services.
New rules may hamstring small businesses trying to compete with larger firms.
The team's progress was hamstrung by budget cuts and staff shortages.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cripple: to severely limit the functionality, efficiency, or power of something
  • antonym – strengthen: to make stronger, more powerful, or more effective
