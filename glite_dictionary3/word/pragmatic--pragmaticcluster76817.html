pragmatic

adjective
relating to pragmatism (philosophy)
Pronunciation: / prægˈmætɪk / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the theory of pragmatism, a philosophical approach that evaluates theories or beliefs in terms of the success of their practical application.

Examples

pragmatic philosophy
Her research centers on pragmatic principles rather than abstract theory.
Many debates in education policy now include a pragmatic perspective rooted in real-world outcomes.
The pragmatic approach to philosophy emphasizes the practical consequences of ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pragmatism: philosophical theory that truth is measured by practical effects
