to manoeuver

verb
perform a tactical movement (military/naval)
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnuːvə /; / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
to move, guide, or position something or someone skillfully, especially to gain an advantage

Examples

to manoeuver a vehicle
The general manoeuvered his troops into a better position for attack.
The navy attempted to manoeuver around the enemy fleet at dawn.
They are manoeuvering carefully to avoid direct confrontation with the opposition forces.
He manoeuvered his way through the crowded room to reach the exit.
She skillfully manoeuvered the car through the narrow streets of the old city.
The captain manoeuvered the ship away from the rocks just in time.

  • synonym – maneuver: alternative spelling, same meaning (US)
  • synonym – steer: to guide or control the course of a vehicle or vessel
