infarct

noun
area of dead tissue from blocked blood supply
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɑːkt / (BrE)
An area of dead tissue in the body caused by a lack of blood supply, usually due to an obstruction in the blood vessels.

Examples

infarct in the brain
Brain tissue surrounding the infarct may still be salvageable with prompt intervention.
Early treatment can help limit the damage caused by a myocardial infarct.
The CT scan revealed a small infarct in the patient's left temporal lobe.

  • synonym – necrosis: the death of body tissue
