trochlear

noun
fourth cranial nerve of the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊ.kli.ər / (AmE), / ˈtrɒk.li.ə / (BrE)
either of the two cranial nerves (the fourth pair) that control the superior oblique muscles of the eyes

Examples

trochlear nerve damage
An ophthalmologist examined the patient's trochlear function to check for any abnormalities in eye rotation.
Damage to the trochlear nerve can affect eye movement and result in double vision.
The trochlear nerve is the smallest of the cranial nerves, but it has the longest intracranial course.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abducens nerve: sixth cranial nerve, controlling lateral eye movement
  • synonym – oculomotor nerve: third cranial nerve, controlling most eye movements
