haymaking

noun
the process of making hay
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of cutting grass and drying it to make hay, typically as animal feed.

Examples

summer haymaking
Every summer, the farmers spend weeks engaged in haymaking to prepare for winter.
Traditional haymaking requires both good weather and careful timing to ensure the best quality hay.
With advances in machinery, haymaking has become faster and less labor-intensive than in the past.
