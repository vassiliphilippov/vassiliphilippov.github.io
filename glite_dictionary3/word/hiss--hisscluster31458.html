to hiss

verb
move with a sharp sibilant or whooshing sound
Pronunciation: / hɪs / (AmE), / hɪs / (BrE)
to make a sharp, hissing sound, like a snake or to show disapproval

Examples

to hiss like a snake
The rockets hissed overhead, leaving trails of smoke in the sky.
The train began to hiss as it pulled into the station.
Winds hissed through the gaps in the old wooden door.
Some critics hissed at the end of the disappointing play.
The audience began to hiss loudly when the speaker made a controversial remark.
The crowd started hissing as the band performed an unpopular encore.
She tried not to hiss at the cat, but its behavior was infuriating.
Some people hiss when they disapprove instead of booing.
The audience began to hiss loudly when the unpopular speaker took the stage.
