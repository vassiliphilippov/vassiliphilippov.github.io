armilla

noun
ancient astronomical instrument of hoops
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈmɪlə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪlə / (BrE)
An ancient astronomical instrument made of metal hoops representing circles of the celestial sphere, used by early astronomers to determine the positions of stars.

Examples

armilla for star positions
Museums often display an armilla to illustrate early astronomical practices.
The ancient astronomer studied the stars carefully with his armilla.
Using an armilla, scholars were able to map out constellations centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym – armillary sphere: a model of objects in the sky consisting of a series of rings
