fata morgana

noun
complex optical mirage
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑtə mɔrˈɡɑnə / (AmE), / ˌfɑːtə mɔːˈɡɑːnə / (BrE)
A complex form of superior mirage, especially one seen on the horizon at sea or over land, producing multiple images, often distorted, usually seen in the Strait of Messina.

Examples

a fata morgana mirage
Hikers were momentarily tricked by a fata morgana that made the distant mountains look much closer than they were.
Sailors in the Mediterranean sometimes saw a fata morgana shimmering above the horizon.
The mysterious castle in the distance turned out to be a fata morgana caused by unusual atmospheric conditions.
