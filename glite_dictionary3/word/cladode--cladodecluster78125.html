cladode

noun
flattened, leaf-like stem
Pronunciation: / ˈklæˌdoʊd / (AmE), / ˈklæd.əʊd / (BrE)
A flattened stem that resembles and functions as a leaf, common in certain plants.

Examples

cladode of a cactus
In some plants, cladodes help conserve water by reducing surface area.
Scientists studied the cladode structure to understand its role in desert adaptation.
The cactus has cladodes that carry out photosynthesis in place of leaves.
