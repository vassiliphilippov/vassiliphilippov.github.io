coding

noun
the act of writing in code or cipher
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of writing in code or cipher; the process of converting plain information into a coded form for secrecy or communication

Examples

coding secret messages
Her expertise in coding allowed her to protect confidential data from unauthorized access.
Manual coding of messages was time-consuming before the advent of computers.
The coding of sensitive information was essential during wartime to maintain security.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decoding: the act of converting coded text back into its original form
  • synonym – enciphering: the act of converting a message into code or cipher
