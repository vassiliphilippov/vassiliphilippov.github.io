two-channel

adjective
using two audio channels for sound
Pronunciation: / ˈtuˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
(of audio equipment) using two separate channels for reproducing sound, typically to create a sense of stereo or spatial effect.

Examples

two-channel stereo system
Many home theaters start with a basic two-channel setup before expanding to surround sound.
She prefers two-channel recordings because they sound more immersive than mono tracks.
The new amplifier supports two-channel output for a true stereo experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mono: sound reproduction using a single channel
  • synonym – stereo: sound reproduction using two or more channels for spatial effect
