duple

adjective
consisting of two parts or beats
Pronunciation: / ˈduːpəl / (AmE), / ˈdjuːpəl / (BrE)
consisting of two parts, elements, or beats; especially relating to musical time having two beats per measure.

Examples

duple meter music
In music theory, duple rhythms have two beats per measure.
The composer chose a duple meter to give the piece a steady, march-like quality.
The waltz is commonly in triple time, while a march is usually in duple time.
