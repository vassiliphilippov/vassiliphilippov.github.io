to devalue

verb
reduce or remove the value of
Pronunciation: / diˈvælju /; / diˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / diˈvæljuː /; / diˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
to reduce the value, importance, or worth of something; to make something less respected or valuable

Examples

to devalue hard work
He felt the feedback unfairly devalued his hard work and achievements.
New evidence may devalue the historical significance of the artifact.
Rapid inflation can devalue a country's currency.
A significant scratch can devalue a car considerably.
Constant criticism will only devalue her confidence over time.
Many people believe that mass production has devalued the uniqueness of traditional crafts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – appreciate: to increase the value of something
  • synonym – debase: to reduce in quality or value; to degrade
  • synonym – depreciate: to diminish in value over time or to reduce the worth of something
