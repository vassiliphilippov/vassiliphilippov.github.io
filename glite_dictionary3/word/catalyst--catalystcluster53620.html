catalyst

noun
substance that speeds up a chemical reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkætəlɪst / (BrE)
A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without undergoing any permanent change itself.

Examples

chemical catalyst
Adding a catalyst can speed up the chemical reaction significantly.
In biology class, we learned how an enzyme acts as a catalyst in our bodies.
The presence of a platinum catalyst made the industrial process much more efficient.

  • synonym – enzyme: a protein that acts as a biological catalyst
