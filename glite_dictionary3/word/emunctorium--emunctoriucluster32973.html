emunctorium

noun
cloth used to wipe chalice in Eucharist
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmʌŋkˈtɔriəm / (AmE), / ɪˌmʌŋkˈtɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A white linen cloth used to wipe the chalice during the Eucharist after each communicant partakes.

Examples

emunctorium wipes chalice
During the Mass, an altar server handed the emunctorium to the officiating priest.
The church sacristan ensured each emunctorium was freshly laundered before the service.
The priest carefully used the emunctorium to wipe the chalice after each communicant received the wine.
