flutter

noun
quick, light back-and-forth movement
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌtɚ /; / ˈflʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə / (BrE)
a quick, light, repeated movement back and forth or up and down (as with wings, leaves, curtains, or similar things)

Examples

a gentle flutter of leaves
A gentle flutter of leaves caught her attention as she walked through the park.
A sudden flutter of wings startled the group of hikers.
The flutter of his heartbeat was visible on the monitor.
A sudden flutter in the curtains caught my attention.
The flutter of bird wings could be heard at dawn.
There was a gentle flutter of leaves outside the window.

  • antonym – stillness: the absence of movement or motion
  • synonym – vibration: a rapid back-and-forth motion
