flavivirus

noun
a virus of the Flavivirus genus
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪ.vɪˌvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪ.vɪˌvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
A virus belonging to the genus Flavivirus of the family Flaviviridae, which includes several important animal and human pathogens such as the viruses that cause yellow fever, dengue, and Zika.

Examples

flavivirus infection
Scientists are researching vaccines to prevent diseases caused by flaviviruses.
The Zika virus is a well-known flavivirus spread by mosquitoes.
Yellow fever, caused by a flavivirus, has led to outbreaks in several countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arbovirus: a virus transmitted by arthropods such as mosquitoes and ticks
