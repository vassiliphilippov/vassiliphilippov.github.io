to allow

verb
make possible; not prevent
Pronunciation: / əˈlaʊ / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ / (BrE)
to let something happen, to permit, or to make something possible

Examples

to allow animals to escape
Leaving the gates open could allow animals to escape from the enclosure.
The new software will allow users to access their files remotely.
Wearing a raincoat allowed her to walk in the storm without getting wet.
Pets are not allowed inside the apartment building.
The teacher allowed the students to use their notes during the test.
We do not allow food or drinks in the library.
Low interest rates allow more people to buy homes than before.
The rules allow students to choose their own topics for the final project.
This shortcut allows for faster completion of the task.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forbid: to order not to do something; prohibit
  • synonym – permit: to make something possible or give permission for something
  • antonym – preclude: to make impossible; to prevent
  • antonym – prevent: to stop something from happening
