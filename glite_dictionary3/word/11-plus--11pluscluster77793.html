11-plus

noun
British school selection exam for 11–12 year olds
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛvən plʌs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛvən plʌs / (BrE)
(formerly in Britain) an examination taken by students aged 11 or 12 to determine entry to grammar schools.

Examples

pass the 11-plus exam
In the 1950s, children would study hard for the 11-plus to qualify for grammar school.
Many parents hired tutors to help prepare their children for the 11-plus exam.
Since failing the 11-plus, she attended the local secondary modern school.
