vena angularis

noun
anatomical vein in the face
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪ.nə æŋˈɡjʊə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə æŋˈɡjuː.ə.rɪs / (BrE)
A short vein formed by the junction of the supraorbital vein and the supratrochlear vein, which continues as the facial vein.

Examples

vena angularis facial vein
An infection in the region of the vena angularis can spread to deeper facial veins.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the vena angularis.
The vena angularis is an important vessel near the inner corner of the eye.

  • degree higher – facial vein: vein formed from the continuation of the vena angularis, draining the face
