tarsus

noun
bones of the ankle and heel
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrsəs / (AmE), / ˈtɑːsəs / (BrE)
the group of bones in the foot between the metatarsus and the leg, forming the ankle and heel in humans.

Examples

broken tarsus bone
After the accident, swelling appeared in the region of her right tarsus.
Birds have a highly modified tarsus compared to humans.
The doctor explained that a fracture in the tarsus could affect mobility for several months.
