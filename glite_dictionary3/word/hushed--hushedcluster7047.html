hushed

adjective
quiet; spoken softly
Pronunciation: / hʌʃt / (AmE), / hʌʃt / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) quiet and spoken in a soft or subdued tone, often to avoid being overheard.

Examples

hushed voice
She spoke in a hushed voice so as not to wake the baby.
The audience listened in hushed silence as the orchestra played softly.
Their hushed conversation drifted through the quiet library.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loud: (of sound) producing much noise
  • synonym – muted: (of sound) quiet or soft in tone
  • synonym – subdued: (of sound) softened and quieted
