homopteran

noun
insect with membranous wings (Homoptera)
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑptərən / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒptərən / (BrE)
an insect belonging to the former order Homoptera, characterized by membranous forewings and hind wings

Examples

a homopteran insect
Aphids are a common type of homopteran that can damage plants in gardens and farms.
Researchers studied the behavior of several homopteran species to understand their life cycle.
The museum has a large collection of homopteran specimens from around the world.

  • degree higher – hemipteran: insect of the order Hemiptera, which now usually includes former homopterans
