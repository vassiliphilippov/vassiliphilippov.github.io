to pump

verb
to flow intermittently in spurts
Pronunciation: / pʌmp / (AmE), / pʌmp / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) to flow or move in spurts or with an intermittent motion, as if driven by a pump.

Examples

pump blood
During vigorous exercise, adrenaline causes blood to pump more forcefully through the veins.
The engine failed, and oil would no longer pump regularly into the machinery.
Water began to pump in short bursts through the damaged pipe.
