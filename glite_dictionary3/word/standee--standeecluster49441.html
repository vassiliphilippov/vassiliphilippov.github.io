standee

noun
person standing where seating is unavailable
Pronunciation: / stænˈdi / (AmE), / stænˈdiː / (BrE)
a person who stands in a place where others might sit, such as a passenger standing on a crowded bus or train, or a spectator standing in a theater's standing room

Examples

bus standee
A notice at the theater entrance limited the number of standees allowed in the balcony.
The bus driver announced that no more standees would be permitted for safety reasons.
The train was so crowded that many standees had to hold onto the overhead rails.
