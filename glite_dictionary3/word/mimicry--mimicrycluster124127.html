mimicry

noun
resemblance for protection in animals
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪmɪkri / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪkri / (BrE)
the close resemblance of one species to another species or to natural objects, often to gain protection from predators

Examples

animal mimicry
Many harmless butterflies use mimicry to look like poisonous species and avoid being eaten.
Scientists study mimicry to understand how animals adapt to their environment for survival.
The phenomenon of mimicry can also be seen in plants that resemble stones to escape herbivores.

Related Terms

  • synonym – camouflage: the act of concealing the identity of something by modifying its appearance
  • synonym – imitation: the act of copying the behavior or appearance of someone or something
