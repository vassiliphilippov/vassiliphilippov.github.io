incompressibility

noun
non-compressibility of data (information theory)
Pronunciation: / cc6ank59mccpr5bs59c8b6al59ti / (AmE), / cc6ank59mccpr5bs59c8b6al59ti / (BrE)
(in information theory and theoretical computer science) the property of a data sequence or object that it cannot be made shorter by any effective algorithm under a given formal model; often used in connection with Kolmogorov complexity and arguments about randomness or lower bounds.

Examples

incompressibility of data
Researchers relied on the incompressibility of the random string to prove a lower bound.
The lecture explained Kolmogorov incompressibility and why true randomness resists algorithmic compression.
Because of its incompressibility, the data set could not be reduced by common compression tools.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Kolmogorov complexity: a measure of the shortest program or description that produces a string; high complexity corresponds to incompressibility
  • antonym – compressibility: the ability of data or a string to be reduced in length by an algorithm; opposite property to incompressibility in this sense
