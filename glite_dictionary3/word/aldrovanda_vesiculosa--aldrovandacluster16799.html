Aldrovanda vesiculosa

noun
carnivorous aquatic plant species
Pronunciation: / ˌældroʊˈvændə vəˌsɪkjəˈloʊsə / (AmE), / ˌaldrəʊˈvandə vəˌsɪkjʊˈləʊsə / (BrE)
a floating aquatic carnivorous plant found in Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia, having whorls of 6 to 9 leaves ending in hinged lobes that trap small aquatic creatures

Examples

Aldrovanda vesiculosa traps insects
Aldrovanda vesiculosa uses rapid leaf movement to catch small aquatic animals like water fleas.
Habitat destruction has made Aldrovanda vesiculosa rare in many parts of its natural range.
Scientists study Aldrovanda vesiculosa to better understand plant carnivory and adaptation.
