phase

noun
distinct state of matter in a system
Pronunciation: / feɪz / (AmE), / feɪz / (BrE)
a distinct state of matter within a system, identical in chemical composition and physical form, separated from other states by a boundary

Examples

solid phase change
Ice and liquid water are different phases of H₂O.
The solution separated into an oil phase and a water phase.
The substance remains in a solid phase below zero degrees Celsius.

Related Terms

  • synonym – state: a condition or mode of being, especially regarding matter (solid, liquid, gas, etc.)
