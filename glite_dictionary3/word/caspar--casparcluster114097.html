Caspar

proper noun
one of the Three Wise Men in Christian tradition
Pronunciation: / ˈkæspɑr / (AmE), / ˈkaspɑː / (BrE)
(in Christian tradition) one of the three Magi or Wise Men from the East who visited the infant Jesus, traditionally described as an Indian scholar who brought gold as a gift.

Examples

Caspar brought gold
According to tradition, Caspar presented gold to the newborn Jesus.
During the Christmas pageant, Matthew played the part of Caspar.
The depiction of Caspar in Renaissance art often shows him as an elder with a crown.
