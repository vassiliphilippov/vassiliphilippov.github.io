semipermeable

adjective
selectively permeable (membrane)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈpɝmiəbəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈpɜːmiəbəl / (BrE)
(of a membrane) allowing certain substances to pass through while preventing others from passing.

Examples

semipermeable membrane filter
Many filters use a semipermeable surface to separate specific particles from a solution.
Osmosis occurs across a semipermeable barrier, letting water through but blocking larger molecules.
The cell membrane is semipermeable, allowing nutrients to enter while keeping harmful substances out.
