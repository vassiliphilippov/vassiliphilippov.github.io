to straiten

verb
to squeeze together; make narrow
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtən / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən / (BrE)
to squeeze or press together tightly; to make narrow or restricted

Examples

to straiten a passage
Intense pressure straitened the passage, making it difficult to breathe.
She straitened her grip on the rope as she climbed higher.
The tailor straitened the waist of the jacket to give it a slimmer fit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compress: to press or squeeze together; to make more compact
  • antonym – loosen: to make less tight or confined; to release pressure
