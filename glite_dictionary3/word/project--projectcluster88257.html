to project

verb
transfer ideas between domains
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to plan, predict, or imagine something as possible or likely to happen, or to transfer ideas from one context to another

Examples

to project future results
Her ability to project mathematical concepts into art inspired her students.
It is not always valid to project Western legal principles onto non-Western societies.
The researchers projected economic theories onto biological systems to develop their models.
She was asked to project possible outcomes for the marketing campaign.
The company will project its financial growth for the next decade.
Urban planners often project new developments to accommodate future population increases.
It can be difficult to project the consequences of your actions far into the future.
She tried to project herself in a leadership role within the organization.
Sometimes people project their own hopes and fears onto others.
