behind

adverb
in debt or late in paying
Pronunciation: / bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
late, less advanced, or not keeping up with others (in time, progress, or payment); showing an earlier time than correct (for clocks)

Examples

fall behind schedule
She is still behind on her student loan payments after graduating last year.
The business fell behind with its tax bills and faced penalties.
They were constantly behind in paying their rent, which worried the landlord.
After missing several deadlines, the project slipped behind schedule.
If you don't practice regularly, you'll quickly fall behind your classmates.
The company was losing money and had started to lag behind its rivals.
If the kitchen clock is behind, we might miss the train.
My watch is behind by ten minutes, so I was late.
The wall clock was behind, so we reset it to the right time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fast: (of a clock) showing a time later than the correct time
  • synonym – in arrears: late in paying money that is owed
  • synonym – lag: to fall behind in progress or development
  • synonym – slow: (of a clock) showing a time earlier than the correct time
