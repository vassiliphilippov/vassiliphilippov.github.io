fireclay

noun
heat-resistant clay for firebricks
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌkleɪ / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪə.kleɪ / (BrE)
A type of clay that is highly resistant to heat and is used for making firebricks, crucibles, and other refractory materials.

Examples

fireclay bricks
Because of its durability, fireclay is often used in kilns and fireplaces.
Skilled workers shape fireclay into various forms for industrial use.
The bricks were made from fireclay to withstand the intense heat of the furnace.
