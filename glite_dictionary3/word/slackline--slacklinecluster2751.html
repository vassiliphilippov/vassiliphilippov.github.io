slackline

noun
partially tensioned line for balance or walking
Pronunciation: / ˈslækˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈslækˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A length of flat webbing or rope that is partially tensioned and suspended above the ground, used for balancing, walking, or performing tricks.

Examples

walk on a slackline
He spent the afternoon practicing tricks on his slackline.
She set up the slackline between two trees in the park.
Walking on a slackline requires focus and good balance.
