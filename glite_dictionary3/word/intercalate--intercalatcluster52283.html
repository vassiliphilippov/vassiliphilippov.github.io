to intercalate

verb
insert extra days/months into a calendar
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrkəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːkəleɪt / (BrE)
to insert an extra period of time, such as a day or month, into a calendar

Examples

to intercalate a month
Historically, certain cultures would intercalate a month to keep the lunar and solar years aligned.
Some calendars intercalate an extra day every four years to account for the Earth's orbit.
To prevent the calendar from drifting, the council decided to intercalate seven days into the schedule.
