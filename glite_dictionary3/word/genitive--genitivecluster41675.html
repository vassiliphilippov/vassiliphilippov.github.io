genitive

noun
grammatical case of possession
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtɪv / (BrE)
the grammatical case used to show ownership or a relationship similar to ownership

Examples

genitive case ending
In some languages, nouns change form in the genitive case to indicate possession.
The English possessive 's' is a remnant of the genitive case from Old English.
You can recognize the genitive by the use of 'of' or the apostrophe-s in English.
