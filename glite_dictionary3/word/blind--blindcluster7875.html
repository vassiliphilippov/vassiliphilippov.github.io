to blind

verb
make less visible or obvious
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd / (AmE), / blaɪnd / (BrE)
to make something less visible, obvious, or clear by comparison or by concealing it

Examples

to blind others to faults
Her success tended to blind people to her faults.
The beauty of the painting can sometimes blind viewers to its deeper meaning.
The new stadium's bright lights blind the stars in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym – obscure: to make something difficult to see, understand, or perceive
  • antonym – reveal: to make something known or visible
