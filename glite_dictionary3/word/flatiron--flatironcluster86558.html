flatiron

noun
old-fashioned iron heated on a stove
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈflætˌaɪən / (BrE)
a type of iron, typically with a flat, heavy base, that was heated on a stove and used for pressing clothes, especially before electric irons became common

Examples

old flatiron for clothes
Before electric models, people would reheat the flatiron several times during ironing.
She used a heated flatiron to smooth out her dress before the wedding.
The old flatiron sat on the kitchen shelf as a reminder of days gone by.
