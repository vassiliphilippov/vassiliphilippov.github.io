biota

noun
all animal and plant life of a region
Pronunciation: / baɪˈoʊtə / (AmE), / baɪˈəʊtə / (BrE)
the animal and plant life of a particular region considered as a whole

Examples

biota of a region
Conservationists study the biota of wetlands to understand ecosystem health.
Human activities have a significant impact on the biota of tropical rainforests.
The Galápagos Islands are famous for their unique biota that inspired Darwin's theory of evolution.
