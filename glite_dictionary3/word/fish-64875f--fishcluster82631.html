to fish

verb
try to get something indirectly
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ / (AmE), / fɪʃ / (BrE)
to try to get something such as information, praise, or a response by hinting or asking indirectly.

Examples

to fish for information
He often fishes for information in conversations without being direct.
Reporters kept fishing for more details about the incident.
She was clearly fishing for compliments after showing us her new dress.
