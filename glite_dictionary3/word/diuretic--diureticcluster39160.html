diuretic

noun
substance that promotes urine production
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
A substance that increases the production of urine and promotes the removal of excess water from the body.

Examples

take a diuretic pill
Caffeine acts as a mild diuretic, increasing how often you urinate.
Many athletes avoid using diuretics due to the risk of dehydration.
The doctor prescribed a diuretic to help reduce her high blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – antidiuretic: substance that reduces urine production
  • synonym – laxative: substance that stimulates bowel movements
