to neutralise

verb
make chemically neutral
Pronunciation: / ˈnutʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a substance chemically neutral, especially by adding an acid to a base or vice versa

Examples

to neutralise an acid
The chemist needed to neutralise the acidic solution before disposing of it.
They used baking soda to neutralise the spilled vinegar on the floor.
You can neutralise a base by slowly adding an acid to it.
