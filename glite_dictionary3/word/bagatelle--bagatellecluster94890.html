bagatelle

noun
table game using cues and balls
Pronunciation: / ˌbæɡəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌbaɡəˈtɛl / (BrE)
A table game in which players use short cues to strike balls into holes, often guarded by wooden pegs, with penalties for knocking over the pegs.

Examples

play bagatelle game
He became fascinated with bagatelle after visiting a vintage games exhibition.
The rules of bagatelle require caution, as knocking over the pegs leads to penalties.
