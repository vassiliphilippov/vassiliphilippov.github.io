endoparasite

noun
parasite living within host body
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.doʊˌpær.əˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dəʊˌpær.ə.saɪt / (BrE)
a parasite that lives inside the body of its host, especially within the internal organs or tissues

Examples

intestinal endoparasite
Roundworms are a common type of endoparasite found in many animal species.
The veterinarian explained that the dog's illness was caused by an endoparasite living in its intestines.
Unlike ectoparasites, an endoparasite can be much harder to detect without laboratory tests.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ectoparasite: a parasite that lives on the surface of its host
