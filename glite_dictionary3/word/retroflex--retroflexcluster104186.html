to retroflex

verb
bend or turn backward
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌflɛks / (BrE)
to bend or turn something backward or back upon itself, especially in medical or scientific contexts

Examples

to retroflex an endoscope
In some surgical procedures, the doctor must retroflex the tip of the endoscope to see certain areas clearly.
The plant's leaves can retroflex in response to environmental stress.
This muscle allows the tongue to retroflex during speech articulation.
