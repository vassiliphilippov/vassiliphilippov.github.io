St. John

proper noun
apostle; author of biblical texts
Pronunciation: / seɪnt dʒɑn / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn / (BrE)
(In the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, traditionally regarded as the author of the fourth Gospel, three epistles, and the Book of Revelation.

Many believe that St. John authored the Book of Revelation while exiled on Patmos.
