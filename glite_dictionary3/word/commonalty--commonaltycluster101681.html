commonalty

noun
the general population excluding clergy or nobility
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmənəlti / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənəlti / (BrE)
the ordinary people who are not members of the clergy or nobility; the general populace, especially in historical or feudal societies.

Examples

the commonalty in society
During the revolution, the commonalty demanded better living conditions from the ruling elite.
Poets often wrote about the struggles of the commonalty in feudal times.
The medieval commonalty had few legal rights compared to the aristocracy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – aristocracy: the highest class in certain societies, especially hereditary nobility
  • synonym – commoners: persons not of noble or clerical rank
