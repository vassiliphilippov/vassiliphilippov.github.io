phytotoxin

noun
plant-derived poisonous substance
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪtəˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪtəʊˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A poisonous substance produced by a plant, especially one similar in effect to bacterial toxins.

Examples

phytotoxin in plants
Certain molds can produce a phytotoxin that is harmful to crops and livestock.
Researchers are studying the phytotoxin to develop new methods of pest control.
The scientist isolated a phytotoxin from the leaves of the plant for further testing.
