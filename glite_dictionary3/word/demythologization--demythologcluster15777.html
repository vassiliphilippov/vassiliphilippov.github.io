demythologization

noun
reinterpretation to remove mythical elements
Pronunciation: / diˌmɪθələˌdʒaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌmɪθəˌlɒdʒaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the reinterpretation of a message, especially a religious one, to remove mythological elements and express it in rational terms

Examples

demythologization of religious stories
His approach to theology included a rigorous demythologization of miracles and supernatural events.
Many scholars debated the value of demythologization in making religious texts accessible to a rational audience.
The process of demythologization seeks to reinterpret biblical stories in a way that is meaningful for modern readers.
