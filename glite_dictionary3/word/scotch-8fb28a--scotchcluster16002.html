to scotch

verb
make a small cut or score
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ / (AmE), / skɒtʃ / (BrE)
to make a small cut or score into something, especially superficially

Examples

to scotch a surface
Be careful not to scotch the wood when you are trimming the edges.
The car's tire was scotched after running over a sharp stone.
Use a sharp knife to scotch the surface lightly before painting.
