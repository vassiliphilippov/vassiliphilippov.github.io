to confound

verb
mistake one thing for another
Pronunciation: / kənˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / kənˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
to mistake one thing for another; to confuse or mix up (things or people)

Examples

to confound facts
Historians sometimes confound the facts when records are incomplete or unclear.
It is easy to confound these two species of birds because they look so similar.
The witness confounded the suspect with another man in her testimony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confuse: to mistake one thing for another; to fail to distinguish between things
