parapraxis

noun
unintentional mistake in speech or action
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈpræksɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈpraksɪs / (BrE)
A minor, unintended mistake in speech, writing, or action, often revealing unconscious thoughts or motives.

Examples

a parapraxis in speech
Forgetting your keys might just be a simple parapraxis rather than a sign of serious memory issues.
She made a parapraxis by calling her boss 'mom' during the meeting.
The psychologist explained the parapraxis as a window into her subconscious mind.
