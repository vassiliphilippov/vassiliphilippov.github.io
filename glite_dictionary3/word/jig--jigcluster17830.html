jig

noun
fishing lure jerked in water
Pronunciation: / dʒɪɡ / (AmE), / dʒɪɡ / (BrE)
A type of fishing lure with one or more hooks, typically moved up and down in the water to attract fish.

Examples

fishing jig lure
Many anglers prefer a jig when fishing in deep water for walleye.
The fisherman attached a colorful jig to his line before casting into the river.
Using a weighted jig, she caught several bass near the lake's edge.
