to naturalize

verb
to adapt to a new environment or culture
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnatʃərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt or accustom something to a new environment, culture, or context, so that it seems normal or typical there

Examples

to naturalize foreign words
Foreign words are often naturalized into a language when they are commonly used.
The French recipe has been naturalized for American tastes over the years.
The ancient myths were naturalized into the culture of the new country.

  • synonym – assimilate: to absorb and integrate into a wider society or culture
