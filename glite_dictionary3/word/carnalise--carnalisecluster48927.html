to carnalise

verb
debase through sensual gratification
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
To debase or corrupt by subjecting to sensual or sexual gratification; to make carnal.

Examples

to carnalise pure ideas
His critics accused him of trying to carnalise spiritual beliefs for personal gain.
The writer argued that modern society tends to carnalise even the most sacred traditions.
They feared that commercial influence would carnalise art, stripping it of lofty ideals.

Related Terms

  • antonym – spiritualise: to make spiritual; contrast with 'carnalise'
