wheel

noun
instrument of torture (historical)
Pronunciation: / wil / (AmE), / wiːl / (BrE)
an instrument of torture, historically used to stretch, disjoint, or mutilate victims, often by binding them to a large wheel

Examples

torture wheel
Historians describe how the wheel was used to stretch the bodies of prisoners during medieval times.
In the museum, you can see a replica of the infamous torture wheel on display.
The condemned man was sentenced to death by the wheel, a fate dreaded across Europe for centuries.
