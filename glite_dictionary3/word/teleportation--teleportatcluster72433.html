teleportation

noun
instantaneous transportation of matter by dematerializing and reassembling
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləpɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪpɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the hypothetical process of instantaneously transporting a person or object from one place to another without traversing the physical space between them

Examples

instant teleportation travel
Many science fiction stories feature teleportation as a convenient way for characters to travel instantly.
Researchers have discussed whether teleportation could ever become possible using quantum mechanics.
The villain escaped capture with the help of advanced teleportation technology.
