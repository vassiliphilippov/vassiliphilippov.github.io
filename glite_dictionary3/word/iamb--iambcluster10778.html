iamb

noun
a metrical unit of two syllables (unstressed-stressed)
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪæm / (AmE), / ˈaɪam / (BrE)
A metrical foot in poetry consisting of two syllables, the first unstressed and the second stressed.

Examples

iamb in poetry
In English verse, the iamb is one of the most common metrical feet.
Shakespeare often wrote his plays in lines of iambs, producing a familiar beat.
The poet structured every line with a distinct iamb to create a rhythmic flow.
