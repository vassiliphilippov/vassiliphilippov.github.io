voltmeter

noun
instrument for measuring voltage
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊltˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈvɒltˌmiːtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the electrical potential difference between two points in an electric circuit.

Examples

voltmeter reading voltage
A digital voltmeter provides a clear numerical reading of the potential difference.
The electrician used a voltmeter to check the wiring for any electrical faults.
You should always connect the voltmeter in parallel to measure the voltage accurately.
