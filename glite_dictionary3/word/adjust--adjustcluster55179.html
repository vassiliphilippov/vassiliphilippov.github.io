to adjust

verb
adapt to new conditions
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to change or adapt oneself to new or different conditions

Examples

to adjust to change
After moving to a new city, it took her months to adjust to the different climate.
Employees often find it difficult to adjust when a company changes its management style.
It is important that children adjust quickly to being back in school after the holidays.
After moving to a new country, it took her a while to adjust to the culture.
His eyes needed time to adjust to the dimly lit room.
You may need to adjust your expectations when starting a new job.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acclimate: to become accustomed to a new environment
  • synonym – adapt: to change in order to suit new conditions
  • antonym – resist: to refuse to accept or adjust to change
