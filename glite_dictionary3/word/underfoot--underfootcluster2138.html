underfoot

adverb
beneath the feet
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈfʊt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈfʊt / (BrE)
beneath one's feet; on the ground where people walk or stand.

Examples

muddy underfoot
After the rain, the grass was wet and muddy underfoot.
Careful, the floor is slippery underfoot after cleaning.
They felt the crunch of dry leaves underfoot as they walked through the woods.
