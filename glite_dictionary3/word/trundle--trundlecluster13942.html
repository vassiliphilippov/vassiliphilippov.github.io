to trundle

verb
move slowly and heavily, often on wheels
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌndl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌndl̩ / (BrE)
to move slowly and heavily, often making a rumbling or clattering noise, typically on wheels.

Examples

to trundle down road
He watched as the delivery van trundled down the narrow street, barely fitting between the parked cars.
The child trundled her toy wagon across the backyard, laughing as it bumped over the grass.
The old cart trundled along the bumpy country road, its contents rattling with every jolt.
