keratocele

noun
hernia of the cornea
Pronunciation: / kəˈrædəˌsil / (AmE), / kəˈrætəsiːl / (BrE)
A hernia of the cornea in which part of the corneal tissue bulges out through a weakened area.

Examples

keratocele of cornea
A keratocele may occur as a result of previous corneal injury or disease.
Prompt surgical intervention is often required to treat a keratocele and prevent complications.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed the patient with a keratocele after observing the bulge in the cornea.
