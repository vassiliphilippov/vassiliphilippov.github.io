Jakob Grimm

proper noun
German philologist and folklorist (1785–1863)
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑkoʊb ˈɡrɪm / (AmE), / ˈjɑːkɒb ˈɡrɪm / (BrE)
(1785–1863) The older of the two Grimm brothers, known for collecting German fairy tales and for formulating Grimm's law in linguistics.

Examples

Jakob Grimm collected tales
Jakob Grimm was instrumental in collecting and publishing traditional German fairy tales with his brother.
Grimm's law, named after Jakob Grimm, describes consonant shifts in Germanic languages.
Many linguists credit Jakob Grimm for his pioneering work in comparative philology.
