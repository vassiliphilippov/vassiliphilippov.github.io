cornsilk

noun
silky threads atop an ear of corn
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnˌsɪlk / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌsɪlk / (BrE)
the long, silky, thread-like strands found protruding from the tip of an ear of maize (corn), which are the styles of the female flowers

Examples

cornsilk on corn
She carefully removed the cornsilk before boiling the sweet corn for dinner.
Some herbal teas are made using dried cornsilk for their purported health benefits.
When peeling an ear of corn, you often find cornsilk tangled among the kernels.
