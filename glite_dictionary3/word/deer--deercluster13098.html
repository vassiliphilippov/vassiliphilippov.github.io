deer

noun
hoofed mammal, male has antlers
Pronunciation: / dɪr / (AmE), / dɪə / (BrE)
A large, usually shy, hoofed mammal found in forests and grasslands, the male of which typically has solid antlers that are shed and regrown each year.

Examples

wild deer
A family of deer grazed peacefully at the edge of the forest.
During our hike, we spotted a deer darting through the trees.
The male deer shed his antlers at the end of winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – elk: a large species of deer found in northern regions
  • synonym – moose: the largest species of deer, native to northern America and Eurasia
