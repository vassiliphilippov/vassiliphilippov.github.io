spillway

noun
channel for excess water near a dam
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
a channel or passage built to carry excess water over or around a dam, especially to prevent flooding.

Examples

spillway of a dam
Engineers inspected the spillway for signs of damage after the recent storm.
Heavy rains forced water to surge over the spillway and into the river below.
The dam's spillway was designed to cope with exceptionally high water levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sluice: an artificial channel for conducting water, often with a gate to regulate flow
