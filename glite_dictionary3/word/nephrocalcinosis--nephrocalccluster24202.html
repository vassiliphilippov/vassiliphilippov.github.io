nephrocalcinosis

noun
calcium deposits in kidney tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛfroʊˌkælsɪˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrəʊˌkælsɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which abnormal calcium deposits form in the tissue of the kidneys, often leading to impaired kidney function and blood in the urine.

Examples

nephrocalcinosis in the kidneys
Doctors often monitor electrolytes in patients at risk of nephrocalcinosis to prevent further kidney damage.
Inherited metabolic disorders can sometimes lead to early-onset nephrocalcinosis in children.
The ultrasound revealed extensive nephrocalcinosis that explained the patient's declining kidney function.
