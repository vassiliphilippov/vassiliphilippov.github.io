gavage

noun
feeding through a tube into the stomach
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈvɑʒ / (AmE), / ɡaˈvɑːʒ / (BrE)
The process of feeding a person or animal by delivering a nutrient solution directly into the stomach, typically through a tube passed through the nose or mouth.

Examples

gavage feeding tube
In severe cases of anorexia, gavage may be medically necessary.
The doctor decided to start gavage to ensure the patient received adequate nutrition.
Veterinarians often use gavage to feed young or sick animals unable to eat on their own.
