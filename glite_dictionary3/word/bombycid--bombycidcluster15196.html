bombycid

noun
silk-producing Asiatic moth
Pronunciation: / bɑmˈbɪsɪd / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɪsɪd / (BrE)
A moderate-sized Asiatic moth of the family Bombycidae, whose larvae feed on mulberry leaves and produce silk.

Examples

bombycid moth larvae
Scientists study the bombycid to improve sericulture and silk production techniques.
Several species of bombycid moths are native to Asia, especially China and India.
The bombycid is most famous for its silk-producing larva, known as the silkworm.
