off-grid

adjective
not connected to main utilities or networks
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈɡrɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈɡrɪd / (BrE)
Not connected to or using the public supply of electricity, water, or communication networks.

Examples

off-grid house
Many environmentalists dream of building an off-grid house to minimize their impact on nature.
The retreat offers an off-grid experience, free from phones and modern distractions.
They decided to live off-grid in a remote cabin with solar panels and a well.

Related Terms

  • antonym – on-grid: connected to public utility networks
  • synonym – self-sufficient: able to provide for oneself without outside help
