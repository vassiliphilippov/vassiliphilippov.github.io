dromaeosaur

noun
fast, carnivorous dinosaur family
Pronunciation: / droʊˈmiː.əˌsɔr / (AmE), / drəʊˈmiː.əˌsɔː / (BrE)
A member of a family of swift, bipedal, carnivorous dinosaurs with a sickle-shaped claw, typically from the Cretaceous period.

Examples

dromaeosaur fossil discovery
Scientists discovered new fossils belonging to an unknown species of dromaeosaur in Mongolia.
The dromaeosaur is often depicted as a swift predator in paleontological illustrations.
Unlike most other theropods, the dromaeosaur possessed a large, curved claw on each foot.
