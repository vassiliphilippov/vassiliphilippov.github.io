cornhusker

noun
person who husks corn
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnˌhʌskər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌhʌskə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to remove the husks from ears of corn

Examples

skilled cornhusker
As a skilled cornhusker, she could strip hundreds of ears each day.
During harvest season, the cornhusker worked long hours in the field.
Machines have replaced the traditional role of the cornhusker in many modern farms.
