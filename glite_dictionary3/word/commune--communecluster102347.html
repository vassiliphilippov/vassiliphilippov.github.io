to commune

verb
to communicate intimately or spiritually
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjun / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːn / (BrE)
to share an intimate or spiritual feeling or connection with someone or something, especially in a deep, meaningful way

Examples

to commune with nature
Many people seek quiet places to commune with their spiritual beliefs.
She found peace as she communed with the forest during her long walk.
The artist often communes with her innermost thoughts before beginning to paint.

  • synonym – connect: to join or associate with something or someone
  • antonym – isolate: to separate from others or from connection
