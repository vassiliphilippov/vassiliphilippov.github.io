Activase

noun
trademarked thrombolytic drug for dissolving clots
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪˌveɪs / (AmE), / ˈæktɪˌveɪs / (BrE)
A trademarked medical drug used as a thrombolytic agent to dissolve blood clots, particularly in the treatment of acute myocardial infarction.

Examples

Activase injection for clots
Doctors administered Activase to rapidly break down the blood clot.
The patient received Activase shortly after arriving at the emergency room to treat the heart attack.
Timely injection of Activase can significantly improve recovery outcomes after a stroke.
