prelims

noun
material before the main text of a book
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːlɪmz / (AmE), / ˈpriːlɪmz / (BrE)
the sections of a book that come before the main text, such as the title page, copyright information, and preface

Examples

prelims of a book
Editors often check the prelims carefully for errors before printing the book.
The prelims include the table of contents and the acknowledgments.
You can find the copyright page in the prelims of most publications.
