scavenger

noun
chemical agent removing impurities
Pronunciation: / ˈskævəndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈskævɪndʒə / (BrE)
a chemical substance added to a mixture to react with and neutralize impurities or unwanted substances

Examples

scavenger removes impurities
A scavenger is added to the fuel to remove traces of lead that could harm the engine.
Adding a scavenger ensures that impurities in the solution do not affect the final product.
The manufacturer recommended using a scavenger to prevent unwanted reactions during the chemical process.
