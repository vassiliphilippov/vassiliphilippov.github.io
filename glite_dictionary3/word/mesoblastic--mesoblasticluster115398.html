mesoblastic

adjective
relating to the mesoderm
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛzəˈblæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːzəˈblæstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or derived from the mesoderm, the middle layer of an embryo in early development.

Examples

mesoblastic tissue formation
Certain congenital diseases can arise from defects in mesoblastic development.
The mesoblastic layer differentiates into several specialized cell types.
The mesoblastic tissue eventually forms the muscles and bones of the embryo.
