to clear

verb
to make something understandable
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
to make (something) free from confusion or ambiguity; to remove doubt or uncertainty

Examples

to clear a question
Can you clear this point in your presentation?
He tried to clear the misunderstanding between the two departments.
The instructions weren't obvious, so I asked her to clear them up for me.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clarify: to make something less confusing and more comprehensible
  • antonym – obscure: to make unclear or difficult to understand
