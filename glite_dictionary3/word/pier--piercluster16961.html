pier

noun
bridge support column or pillar
Pronunciation: / pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
a vertical supporting structure in a building or bridge, such as a solid part between openings or supporting a bridge span

Examples

bridge pier
During construction, each pier must be precisely located to support the spans above.
Engineers inspected the concrete pier for signs of cracks or erosion.
The damaged pier weakened the overall stability of the bridge.
Between each pair of windows stood a slender pier that divided the light.
In classical architecture, a pier is often used to carry the load of heavy masonry arches.
The arched doorway was flanked by a thick pier supporting the vaulted ceiling.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abutment: a structure supporting the end of a bridge span
  • synonym – column: a vertical, cylindrical supporting pillar
