mother

noun
substance formed in vinegar fermentation
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
a stringy, slimy substance composed of yeast cells and bacteria that forms during fermentation, particularly in vinegar production.

Examples

mother in vinegar
After a few weeks, a mother formed at the bottom of the cider jar.
Some people add a piece of mother to start a new batch of homemade vinegar.
The presence of mother is a sign of natural, unfiltered vinegar.
