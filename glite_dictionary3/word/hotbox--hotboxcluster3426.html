hotbox

noun
overheated railroad journal bearing
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑtˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌbɒks / (BrE)
A journal bearing of a railroad car or similar vehicle that has become overheated, often due to a lack of lubrication.

Examples

railroad hotbox failure
A sudden hotbox can cause damage and safety hazards on railroads.
Maintenance crews had to replace the hotbox before the train could continue its journey.
The train was delayed because a hotbox was discovered on one of the freight cars.
