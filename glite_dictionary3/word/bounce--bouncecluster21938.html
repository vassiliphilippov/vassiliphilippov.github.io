bounce

noun
a light, upward or forward movement
Pronunciation: / baʊns / (AmE), / baʊns / (BrE)
a quick, springy movement upwards or forwards, or the quality of being able to rebound or move in a lively way

Examples

a lively bounce
She walked into the room with a bounce in her step, clearly excited.
The ball gave a surprising bounce when it hit the concrete floor.
With every bounce, the rubber ball seemed to go higher and higher.
After hitting the sidewalk, the basketball lost much of its bounce.
The bounce of the tennis ball surprised her opponent.
The cat jumped off the table with a graceful bounce.
Her hair had a natural bounce that made it look healthy and full.
The new running shoes gave his step an extra bounce.
The tennis ball lost its bounce after being used for several games.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fall: move downward, typically rapidly and freely
  • synonym – jump: a movement upwards or forwards through the air
  • synonym – rebound: to spring back after hitting a surface
  • synonym – resilience: the capacity to recover quickly or spring back into shape
  • synonym – springiness: the quality of being able to spring back after being pressed or stretched
