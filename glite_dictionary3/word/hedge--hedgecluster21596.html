hedge

noun
fence made of shrubs or bushes
Pronunciation: / hɛdʒ / (AmE), / hɛdʒ / (BrE)
a fence or boundary formed by a row of closely planted shrubs or bushes, often used to mark property lines or enclose gardens

Examples

tall hedge row
A neatly trimmed hedge surrounded the front lawn, giving the house a tidy appearance.
Birds often build their nests in the dense branches of a hedge.
They planted a hedge along the driveway for privacy and decoration.
