conductance

noun
capacity to conduct electricity
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌktəns / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktəns / (BrE)
the ability of a material to conduct electricity, measured as the reciprocal of electrical resistance

Examples

electrical conductance value
As temperature increases, the conductance of the solution rises.
Copper has a high conductance, making it ideal for electrical wiring.
Measuring the conductance helps engineers assess circuit efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conductivity: the degree to which a material conducts electricity
  • antonym – resistance: opposition to the flow of electric current
