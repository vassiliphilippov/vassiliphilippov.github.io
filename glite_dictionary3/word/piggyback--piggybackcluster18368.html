to piggyback

verb
add or combine with something else
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪɡiˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡiˌbæk / (BrE)
to add or combine something so that it functions or is considered together with something else, often by attaching it or taking advantage of an existing opportunity or structure

Examples

to piggyback a campaign
Some companies piggyback their marketing campaigns on trending events to reach more people.
The resolution will piggyback several existing proposals to gain broader support.
You can piggyback your request onto the main application to save time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attach: to fasten or join one thing to another
  • synonym – combine: to join or mix two or more things together
