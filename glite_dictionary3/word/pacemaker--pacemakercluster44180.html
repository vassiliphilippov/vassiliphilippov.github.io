pacemaker

noun
an implanted electronic heart device
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a small electronic device implanted in the body to regulate an abnormal heart rhythm by producing electrical impulses

Examples

implanted pacemaker device
After surgery, his heart was regulated by a pacemaker to ensure a steady rhythm.
Battery replacement is sometimes required for an implanted pacemaker.
The doctor explained that a pacemaker might be necessary for patients with severe arrhythmia.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defibrillator: a device that restores normal heartbeat by sending an electric pulse to the heart
