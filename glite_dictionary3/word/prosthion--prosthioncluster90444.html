prosthion

noun
craniometric point on the maxilla
Pronunciation: / prɑːsˈθaɪ.ən / (AmE), / prɒsˈθɪ.ən / (BrE)
the most forward point in the center of the alveolar process of the maxilla, used as a reference in craniometric measurements

Examples

prosthion measurement point
In forensic anthropology, the prosthion is crucial for identifying human remains.
Researchers marked the prosthion to compare skull measurements across different populations.
The dentist examined the alveolar process near the prosthion for signs of bone loss.
