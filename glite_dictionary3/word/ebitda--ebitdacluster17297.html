EBITDA

noun
financial measure of profit before certain deductions
Pronunciation: / iːˌbɪtˈdɑː / (AmE), / iːˌbɪtˈdɑː / (BrE)
Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization; a financial measure used to assess a company's operating performance.

Examples

EBITDA margin
In merger discussions, EBITDA is often cited as a key metric for comparing businesses.
Many investors use EBITDA to evaluate the financial health of a company before choosing to invest.
The company reported a higher EBITDA this quarter, indicating improved operating performance.
