gross

noun
total amount before deductions
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
the total amount of money or income before any deductions, such as expenses or taxes, are subtracted

Examples

gross income
Always check if a salary figure refers to the gross or net amount.
His annual gross was over $80,000, but he took home much less after taxes.
The report showed the company's gross for the quarter had increased by ten percent.

Related Terms

  • antonym – net: the amount remaining after deductions are made
