to contract

verb
formally agree; enter into a contract
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / kənˈtrækt /; / kənˈtrɑːkt / (BrE)
to make a formal agreement, especially a legally binding one, or to hire/engage someone by contract

Examples

to contract a worker
After careful consideration, they contracted an expert to oversee the project.
The school district will contract a new bus service for next year.
We decided to contract with a local company for the renovations.
The company decided to contract a new software developer to lead the project.
The festival organizers are contracting security staff for the event.
They contracted several artists to paint murals throughout the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – agree: to have the same opinion; to consent
  • synonym – hire: to employ someone for wages or salary
