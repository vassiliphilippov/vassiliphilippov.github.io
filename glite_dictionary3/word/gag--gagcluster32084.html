to gag

verb
prevent from speaking out
Pronunciation: / ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)
to prevent someone from expressing their opinions freely, especially by using force or authority

Examples

to gag the press
She felt her right to speak was being gagged by strict company policies.
The government tried to gag the media from reporting on the protests.
The judge issued an order to gag all parties involved in the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – muzzle: to prevent someone from expressing their opinions
  • synonym – silence: to force someone to stop speaking or expressing an opinion
