to disinherit

verb
to prevent from inheriting
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪt / (BrE)
to prevent someone, especially by making a will, from inheriting money or property after your death

Examples

to disinherit a child
He was shocked to learn that his aunt had disinherited him from her will.
She threatened to disinherit anyone who opposed her wishes.
The billionaire decided to disinherit his son after their falling out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disown: to refuse to acknowledge as one's own or maintain any connection with
  • antonym – inherit: to receive property or money from someone after their death
