C-horizon

noun
soil layer above bedrock
Pronunciation: / si həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / si həˈraɪzən / (BrE)
the soil layer beneath the B-horizon and above the bedrock, composed mainly of weathered parent rock material

Examples

deep C-horizon layer
Scientists collected samples from the C-horizon to analyze its mineral composition.
The C-horizon contains partially disintegrated rock and marks the transition to unweathered bedrock.
Unlike the upper layers, the C-horizon plays little role in supporting plant growth.
