Miami

noun
member of an extinct Native American people
Pronunciation: / maɪˈæmi / (AmE), / maɪˈæmi / (BrE)
A member of an extinct Native American people, part of the Algonquian linguistic group, who formerly lived in northern Indiana and southern Michigan.

Examples

Miami tribe member
Artifacts from the Miami have been found throughout northern Indiana.
She has studied the culture and language of the Miami people extensively.
The Miami were skilled farmers and hunters before their dispersal in the 19th century.
