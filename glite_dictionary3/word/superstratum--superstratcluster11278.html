superstratum

noun
a layer superimposed on another
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pɚˈstreɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstreɪ.təm / (BrE)
A stratum or layer that is superimposed upon another, especially in geological or linguistic contexts.

Examples

rock superstratum
A thin superstratum of volcanic ash covered the older sedimentary rock.
Researchers analyzed each superstratum to determine changes in soil composition over time.
The superstratum of loanwords influenced the evolution of the local language.

  • antonym – substratum: a stratum or layer beneath another
