polemic

noun
person who argues combatively, especially in theology
Pronunciation: / poʊˈlɛmɪk / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪk / (BrE)
a person who strongly attacks or defends a particular opinion or doctrine, especially in theological or philosophical argument

Examples

a skilled polemic debater
As a seasoned polemic, she never shied away from challenging established views.
In the 18th century, a polemic would often write treatises attacking rival theologians.
The famous polemic was known for his fierce debates on matters of faith.
