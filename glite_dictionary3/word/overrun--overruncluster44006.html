to overrun

verb
take control by force; defeat
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈrʌn / (BrE)
(especially of armed forces) to seize control of and defeat a place or position by force, typically quickly and completely.

Examples

to overrun a city
During the war, enemy troops overran several border towns before being stopped.
Rebels overran the government stronghold after a fierce battle.
The invading army overran the capital in just two days.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capture: to take control of a place or person by force
  • antonym – retreat: to withdraw from enemy forces as a result of defeat
