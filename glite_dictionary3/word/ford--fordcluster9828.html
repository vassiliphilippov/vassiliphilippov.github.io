to ford

verb
cross a shallow part of a river
Pronunciation: / fɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːd / (BrE)
to cross a river or stream at a shallow place where it can be walked or driven across

Examples

to ford a stream
Early explorers would often ford the river instead of building bridges.
The hikers carefully forded the shallow section of the creek.
We had to ford the stream to reach the campsite on the other side.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wade: to walk through water or another substance that impedes movement
