to release

verb
make available to the public for sale or use
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlis / (AmE), / rɪˈliːs / (BrE)
to prepare and make available for public distribution or sale, or to make information available to the public

Examples

to release a product
The company will release its new smartphone next month to great anticipation.
The latest software update was released to customers yesterday.
They plan to release the documentary in theaters nationwide this summer.
After years of litigation, the bank finally agreed to release the frozen accounts.
Authorities must release the confiscated assets to their rightful owner.
The company decided to release its financial reserves to fund new research projects.
She decided to release her findings to the press after months of research.
The company refused to release details about the new product until the launch date.
The government will release the official report tomorrow morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – publish: to prepare and issue a book, magazine, or other material for public sale or circulation
  • antonym – withhold: to keep (assets or information) back or not make available
