lien

noun
legal right to take someone's property for a debt
Pronunciation: / ˈliːən / (AmE), / ˈliːən / (BrE)
a legal right to keep possession of another person's property until a debt owed by that person is discharged

Examples

lien on property
If you fail to pay your taxes, the government may place a lien on your property.
The bank holds a lien on the car until the loan is paid off.
The contractor filed a lien against the house after he was not paid for his work.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encumbrance: a claim against property, such as a mortgage or lien
