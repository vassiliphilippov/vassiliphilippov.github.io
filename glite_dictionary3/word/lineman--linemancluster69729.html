lineman

noun
surveyor's assistant marking positions
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪn.mæn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn.mæn / (BrE)
A surveyor's assistant responsible for marking positions with a range pole during surveying operations, usually in the field.

Examples

survey lineman
During the field survey, a lineman played a crucial role in marking the property boundaries.
The lineman carefully placed the range pole at the exact coordinates specified by the chief surveyor.
To become a skilled lineman, one must understand basic surveying instruments and measurements.
