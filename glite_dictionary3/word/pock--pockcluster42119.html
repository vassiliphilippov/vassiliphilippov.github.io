pock

noun
a blister or mark from an eruptive disease
Pronunciation: / pɑk / (AmE), / pɒk / (BrE)
a small blister, pustule, or mark left on the skin by an eruptive disease, such as smallpox

Examples

a pock scar
After her illness, a single pock remained on her forehead as a faint reminder.
Old paintings sometimes depict people with at least one visible pock from the disease.
The doctor examined the pock on the patient's cheek, which was a remnant of smallpox.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scar: a mark left on the skin or within body tissue after a wound or injury has healed
