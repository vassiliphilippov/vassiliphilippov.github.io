fiduciary

noun
person managing assets for another
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃiːəri / (BrE)
a person who has the legal and ethical responsibility to manage someone else’s assets or interests on their behalf

Examples

fiduciary manages money
A fiduciary who violates trust can face serious legal consequences.
As a fiduciary, she must always act in her clients’ best interests.
Banks often serve as a fiduciary when managing investment funds.

Related Terms

  • antonym – beneficiary: person for whose benefit assets are managed
  • synonym – trustee: person or entity that holds assets for the benefit of another
