to flyspeck

verb
inspect for tiny errors
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌspɛk / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌspɛk / (BrE)
to examine something very carefully in order to find very small mistakes or faults, often in a way that is unnecessary or overly detailed.

Examples

to flyspeck a report
Auditors will flyspeck the report for any small errors before approving the final draft.
She flyspecked every paragraph, catching several punctuation mistakes the team had missed.
Instead of improving, he tended to flyspeck colleagues' proposals during meetings, slowing decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nitpick: to find fault with small or unimportant details
  • synonym – scrutinize: to examine something very carefully
