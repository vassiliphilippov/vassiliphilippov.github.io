to escape

verb
avoid or not suffer something
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp / (BrE)
to avoid something unpleasant, harmful, or a duty

Examples

to escape punishment
Several employees escaped the layoffs thanks to the company's new project.
She managed to escape injury in the car accident.
We were lucky to escape the worst of the storm that hit our area.
He thought he could escape responsibility, but the truth soon caught up with him.
Many people try to escape the consequences of their actions, but it rarely works forever.
The suspect managed to escape punishment by providing a clever alibi.

Related Terms

  • synonym – avoid: to keep away from; to prevent from happening to oneself
  • synonym – evade: avoid something, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • antonym – experience: to undergo or feel the effects of (used here as antonym: to not escape)
  • antonym – face: to accept or deal with consequences or punishment
  • synonym – get away with: to succeed in avoiding punishment for something
