Goidelic

noun
branch of Celtic languages (Irish, Scottish Gaelic, Manx)
Pronunciation: / ɡɔɪˈdɛlɪk / (AmE), / ɡɔɪˈdɛlɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Celtic languages consisting of Irish, Scottish Gaelic, and Manx, spoken in Ireland, Scotland, and the Isle of Man

Examples

Goidelic languages group
Irish and Scottish Gaelic both belong to the Goidelic language group.
Manx, though once extinct, has been revived as part of the Goidelic family.
The Goidelic languages are distinct from the Brythonic branch spoken in Wales and Brittany.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Brythonic: the other main branch of Celtic languages, including Welsh, Breton, and Cornish
