rain

noun
rapid outpouring or succession (figurative)
Pronunciation: / reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
a rapid or abundant fall or outpouring of things happening in quick succession, often used figuratively.

Examples

rain of applause
After her promotion, she received a rain of congratulations from colleagues.
The new policy led to a rain of complaints at the customer service desk.
The speech triggered a rain of protests from the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shower: a sudden, short period in which a lot of things fall or are given
