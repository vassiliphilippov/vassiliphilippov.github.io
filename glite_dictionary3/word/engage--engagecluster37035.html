to engage

verb
to interlock or become operative (machinery)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to cause a machine or part to become active, interlocked, or in position for use

Examples

to engage the gear
Before starting the car, make sure the gear is properly engaged.
He forgot to engage the handbrake, and the car rolled backward.
When the lever is engaged, the machine starts to operate.
Before starting the machine, ensure that all safety locks are engaged.
She engaged the clutch before shifting into first gear.
You must engage the parking brake whenever you park on a hill.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disengage: to separate or release (something that has been engaged)
