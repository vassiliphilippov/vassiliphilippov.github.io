to mortice

verb
join wood using tenon and mortise
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔr.tɪs /; / ˈmɔːr.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tɪs / (BrE)
to cut a hole (mortice) in wood or other material, or to join pieces of wood using a mortice and tenon joint

Examples

to mortice a joint
After cutting the slots, he began morticing the joints for the window frame.
The carpenter expertly morticed the beams together to form a sturdy frame.
The craftsman taught his apprentice how to mortice two timbers securely.
A carpenter will carefully mortice the joint to ensure a perfect fit.
She morticed the frame before attaching the tenon.
They are morticing the wood for the new door frame.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tenon: a projection on the end of a piece of wood that is inserted into a mortise
