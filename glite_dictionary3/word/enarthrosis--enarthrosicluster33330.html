enarthrosis

noun
type of freely movable joint; ball-and-socket
Pronunciation: / ɛnɑrˈθroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɛnɑːˈθrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A freely movable joint in which the rounded head of one bone fits into a rounded cavity in another, allowing movement in all directions; a ball-and-socket joint.

Examples

hip enarthrosis
Damage to the hip's enarthrosis can lead to restricted movement and pain.
The shoulder is an example of an enarthrosis, allowing the arm to move freely in multiple directions.
Unlike the hinge joint, the enarthrosis permits rotation and a greater range of motion.
