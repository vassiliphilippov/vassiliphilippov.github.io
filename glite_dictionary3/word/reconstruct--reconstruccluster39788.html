to reconstruct

verb
form a mental picture of the past
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt /; / ccrid0k59nc8str8ckt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt /; / ccrid0k59nc8str8ckt / (BrE)
to form an idea or representation of something from available information, either in your mind or using data (including in technical or computational contexts)

Examples

to reconstruct an event
After listening to the witnesses, she managed to reconstruct the accident in her mind.

By reconstructing sonar returns, scientists created detailed maps of the submerged wreck site.
