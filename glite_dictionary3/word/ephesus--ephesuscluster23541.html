Ephesus

noun
Christian ecumenical council in 431 AD
Pronunciation: / ˈɛfəsəs / (AmE), / ˈɛfɪsəs / (BrE)
the third ecumenical council of the Christian church, held in 431, which declared Mary as the Mother of God and condemned Pelagianism.

Examples

Ephesus church council
At Ephesus, church officials debated the nature of Christ and the status of Mary.
Many historians study the outcomes of Ephesus to understand theological disputes of the fifth century.
The Council of Ephesus played a pivotal role in shaping early Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Council of Nicaea: the first ecumenical council of the Christian church, held in 325
  • antonym – Pelagianism: the doctrine condemned at the Council of Ephesus
