podsol

noun
acidic soil with leached layer in cool climates
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑdˌsɒl / (AmE), / ˈpɒd.sɒl / (BrE)
a soil type found in temperate to cold, moist climates, characterized by an organic mat over a grey, leached layer, typically occurring under coniferous or heath vegetation

Examples

podsol soil layer
Because of its low fertility, podsol is not suitable for most crops without soil treatment.

The podsol was evident by its ashy, leached horizon and acidic conditions.
