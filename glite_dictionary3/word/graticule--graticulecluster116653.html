graticule

noun
marking lines in optical instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrætɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈɡrætɪkjuːl / (BrE)
a network of fine lines, dots, cross hairs, or wires placed in the eyepiece of an optical instrument to assist with measurement or positioning

Examples

graticule crosshairs alignment
She adjusted the graticule so the crosshairs aligned perfectly with the specimen.
The microscope’s graticule helped the biologist measure tiny cellular structures with precision.
The telescope’s graticule made it easier to estimate the distance between stars.
