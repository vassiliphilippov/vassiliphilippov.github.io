tauon

noun
massive subatomic lepton
Pronunciation: / ˈtaʊˌɑn, ˈtaʊˌɒn / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle that is a lepton with a very large mass, heavier than an electron or muon

Examples

a tauon particle
Researchers accelerated particles to high energies to observe the decay of a tauon.
The discovery of the tauon provided new insight into the structure of matter.
Unlike the electron, the tauon has a much shorter lifetime before it decays.
