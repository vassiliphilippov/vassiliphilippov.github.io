derisive

adjective
expressing ridicule or contempt
Pronunciation: / dɪˈraɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪsɪv / (BrE)
showing that you think someone or something is ridiculous or not worthy of respect; expressing contempt or ridicule.

Examples

derisive laughter
His proposal was met with derisive comments from the rest of the team.
She let out a derisive laugh when she heard the ridiculous rumor.
The comedian's derisive jokes targeted the celebrities in the front row.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mocking: making fun of someone in a cruel way; derisive
  • antonym – respectful: showing respect or admiration; politely considerate
