distrustful

adjective
inclined to doubt or suspect
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtrʌstfəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrʌstfəl / (BrE)
having or showing a lack of trust; inclined to doubt or suspect others' motives or honesty.

Examples

distrustful of strangers
He grew increasingly distrustful after being betrayed by a close friend.
Her distrustful glance suggested she didn't believe his explanation.
People can become distrustful if their privacy is repeatedly violated.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suspicious: having or showing a cautious distrust of someone or something
  • antonym – trustful: having or marked by complete trust or confidence
