to steady

verb
make or keep stable or balanced
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
to make or become stable, balanced, or fixed so that it does not shake, move, or change suddenly

Examples

to steady the ladder
Before taking the photograph, she took a deep breath to steady her shaking hands.
He steadied the ladder while his friend climbed to reach the roof.
She placed her hand on the railing to steady herself as she climbed the steps.
He paused on the narrow step to steady himself.
She reached out to steady the vase before it fell off the shelf.
The doctor gently steadied the patient as they stood up.

Related Terms

  • synonym – secure: to fix or fasten something so it cannot move
  • antonym – shake: to move quickly up and down or from side to side
  • synonym – stabilize: to make or become unlikely to change, fall, or fail
  • antonym – wobble: to move with an unsteady motion
