counterbore

noun
tool for enlarging top of a hole
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəbɔː / (BrE)
A drill bit or tool used to enlarge the upper part of a hole so that a screw or bolt head can be recessed below or flush with the surface.

Examples

counterbore tool bit
A special counterbore is required for hardware that needs to be recessed.
Before inserting the screw, make a counterbore so the head will sit flush with the surface.
The mechanic used a counterbore to prepare the hole for the bolt head.
