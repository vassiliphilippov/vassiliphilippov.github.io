salmonella

noun
type of bacteria causing food poisoning
Pronunciation: / ˌsælməˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˌsælməˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped bacteria that cause food poisoning and typhoid fever in humans.

Examples

salmonella infection
After eating undercooked chicken, she contracted salmonella and was bedridden for days.
Health officials issued a warning about a salmonella outbreak linked to contaminated eggs.
Proper food handling can help prevent salmonella infection in your kitchen.
