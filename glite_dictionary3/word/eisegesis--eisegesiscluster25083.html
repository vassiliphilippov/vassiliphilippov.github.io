eisegesis

noun
interpretation of a text based on personal ideas
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈdʒiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪsɪˈdʒiːsɪs / (BrE)
the interpretation of a text, especially scripture, according to one's own ideas rather than the intended meaning

Examples

eisegesis of scripture
Careful study tries to avoid eisegesis and instead seeks to understand the author's original intent.
Much academic debate arises from eisegesis, where interpreters inject personal beliefs into ancient texts.
The scholar was accused of eisegesis for reading his own views into the biblical passage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exegesis: the objective explanation or interpretation of a text, especially scripture
