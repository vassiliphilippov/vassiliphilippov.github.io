purgatory

noun
temporary state of suffering
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətəri / (BrE)
A temporary state or place of great suffering or torment, especially one that must be endured before relief or improvement.

Examples

emotional purgatory
After losing his job, he described the months spent searching for work as a purgatory.
For many, the bureaucracy involved in immigration can be a bureaucratic purgatory.
The long wait in the hospital's emergency room felt like a kind of purgatory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – paradise: a state or place of perfect happiness
  • synonym – torment: a state of great mental or physical suffering
