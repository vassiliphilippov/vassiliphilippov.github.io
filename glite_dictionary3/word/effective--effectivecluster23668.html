effective

adjective
real or actual, not just theoretical
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of something abstract, such as a rule, demand, or amount) existing in fact, not just in theory; real or actual.

Examples

effective amount
After adjusting for inflation, the effective cost of the project was much higher than anticipated.
The country’s effective population is lower than its total population due to emigration.
The policy looks good on paper, but its effective impact remains to be seen.

  • antonym – theoretical: existing only in theory, not in fact
