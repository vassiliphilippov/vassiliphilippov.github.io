middling

adjective
average or moderate in quality
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdlɪŋ / (BrE)
of average or moderate quality; neither very good nor very bad

Examples

middling exam results
Her performance on the exam was middling, neither outstanding nor disappointing.
The film received middling reviews from most critics, who found it neither bad nor remarkable.
We stayed at a middling hotel—nothing special, but comfortable enough.

Related Terms

  • synonym – average: typical or ordinary in quality
  • antonym – excellent: of the highest quality
  • synonym – mediocre: of only moderate quality; not very good
