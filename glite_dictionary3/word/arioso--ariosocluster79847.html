arioso

noun
melodic passage between recitative and aria (music)
Pronunciation: / ˌɑriˈoʊsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɑːrɪˈəʊsəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) a short vocal passage that is more melodic than a recitative but not as formally structured as an aria.

Examples

In the opera, the arioso bridged the transition from recitative to aria.
Mozart often wrote an arioso to highlight a character's inner feelings.
