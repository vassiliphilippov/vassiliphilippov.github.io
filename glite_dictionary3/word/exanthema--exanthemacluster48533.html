exanthema

noun
disease-related skin eruption or rash
Pronunciation: / ɛɡˌzænˈθimə / (AmE), / ɛkˌsænˈθiːmə / (BrE)
a skin eruption or rash that appears as a symptom of a disease, especially an infectious one

Examples

exanthema after infection
A sudden exanthema may signal the onset of a viral infection.
Scarlet fever is characterized by a distinctive exanthema over the trunk and limbs.
The child developed an exanthema after contracting measles.

Related Terms

  • antonym – enanthema: an eruption or rash occurring on mucous membranes
  • synonym – rash: an area of reddened, irritated, or swollen skin
