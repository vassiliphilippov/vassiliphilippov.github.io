neurohormone

noun
hormone released by nerve cells
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by nerve cells and released into the blood in response to nerve impulses, often affecting distant organs.

Examples

release neurohormone into blood
Norepinephrine acts as a neurohormone to influence heart rate and blood pressure.
Scientists discovered a new neurohormone that plays a role in stress responses.
The release of vasopressin as a neurohormone helps regulate water balance in the body.
