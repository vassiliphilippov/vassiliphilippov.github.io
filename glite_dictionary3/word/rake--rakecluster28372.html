to rake

verb
move or sweep through with a raking motion
Pronunciation: / reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
to move, search, or sweep through something with a sweeping motion, as with hands, eyes, or light

Examples

to rake through papers
He raked through the pile of old photographs looking for a picture of his grandfather.
She raked her fingers through her hair absentmindedly as she read the letter.
The searchlight raked the dark alley, illuminating every corner.
He raked the shelves with his eyes, searching for the missing book.
His gaze raked the room, taking in the scene in a matter of seconds.
She raked through the document, hoping to spot any obvious errors.
At sunrise, beams of sunlight raked the valley, illuminating the mist.
Enemy machine guns raked the advancing soldiers as they crossed the open field.
The warship raked the shoreline with a barrage of heavy artillery fire.
