to shadow

verb
make seem less important by comparison
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊ / (BrE)
To make (someone or something) seem less important or impressive by comparison.

Examples

to shadow her success
Her outstanding performance shadowed the efforts of the other team members.
The rapid success of the startup shadowed many established firms in the industry.
The tragedy shadowed all their previous accomplishments.
