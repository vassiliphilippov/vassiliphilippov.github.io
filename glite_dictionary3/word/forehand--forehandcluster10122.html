forehand

noun
sports stroke with palm facing forward
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːhænd / (BrE)
(in sports) a stroke in which the palm of the hand faces in the direction of the stroke, especially in tennis, badminton, or squash.

Examples

tennis forehand shot
He hit the ball across the court with a powerful forehand.
Her forehand is the strongest part of her game.
To improve your forehand, focus on your grip and wrist movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – backhand: a stroke made with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke, as in tennis
