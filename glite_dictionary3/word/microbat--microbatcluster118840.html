microbat

noun
an insect-eating bat with large ears
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌbæt / (BrE)
A type of bat, typically with large ears, that primarily feeds on insects and is found worldwide.

Examples

microbat eats insects
Researchers observed a colony of microbats emerging at dusk to feed.
The microbat uses echolocation to navigate and hunt insects in the dark.
Unlike fruit bats, a microbat relies heavily on its sense of hearing.
