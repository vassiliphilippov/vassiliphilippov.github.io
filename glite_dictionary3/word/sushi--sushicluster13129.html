sushi

noun
Japanese dish of vinegared rice and fish
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːʃi / (AmE), / ˈsuːʃi / (BrE)
a Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as raw fish and vegetables, often wrapped in seaweed

Examples

eat sushi with chopsticks
He prefers sushi made with salmon rather than tuna.
Making sushi at home requires fresh ingredients and some practice.
We ordered a platter of sushi for lunch at the new Japanese restaurant.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – maki: sushi with ingredients rolled in rice and seaweed
  • degree lower – nigiri: a type of sushi with a slice of fish atop a mound of rice
  • antonym – sashimi: thinly sliced raw fish, often served without rice
