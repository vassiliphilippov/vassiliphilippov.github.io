electrocardiograph

noun
device for recording heart's electrical activity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɑrdioʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈkɑːdiəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to record the electrical activity of the heart over a period of time

Examples

electrocardiograph machine
An electrocardiograph can detect irregular heartbeats and other abnormalities quickly.
Modern hospitals are equipped with advanced electrocardiographs to assist in diagnosing cardiac conditions.
The doctor used an electrocardiograph to monitor the patient's heart activity during the surgery.
