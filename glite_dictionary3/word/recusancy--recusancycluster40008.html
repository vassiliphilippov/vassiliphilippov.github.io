recusancy

noun
refusal to submit to authority (historical, often religious)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkjuːzənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuːzənsi / (BrE)
Refusal to submit to established authority, especially the refusal of Roman Catholics to attend services of the Church of England in post-Reformation England.

Examples

recusancy laws enforced
During Elizabethan England, recusancy was punishable by heavy fines and imprisonment.
Historians have studied the impact of recusancy on English Catholic families in the 16th century.
The government viewed acts of recusancy as a threat to state security and religious unity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conformity: compliance with established standards or authority
