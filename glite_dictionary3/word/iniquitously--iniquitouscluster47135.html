iniquitously

adverb
in a wicked or unjust manner
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɪkwɪtəsli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪkwɪtəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is extremely unfair or morally wrong.

Examples

iniquitously treat people
Many believed the laws were enforced iniquitously against certain groups.
She felt iniquitously treated by her employers after the incident.
The government was accused of acting iniquitously during the land reforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – righteously: in a morally right or justifiable manner
  • synonym – unjustly: in a manner that is not just or fair
