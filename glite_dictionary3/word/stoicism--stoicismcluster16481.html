Stoicism

noun
ancient Greek philosophical system teaching endurance of hardship
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
An ancient Greek school of philosophy founded by Zeno, teaching that virtue, the highest good, is based on knowledge, and that the wise live in harmony with the divine Reason that governs nature, remaining indifferent to fortune, pleasure, and pain.

Examples

ancient Stoicism philosophy
Many leaders have been inspired by Stoicism and its emphasis on rational self-control.
The principles of Stoicism are still applied in modern self-help and therapy literature.
Through reading about Stoicism, she learned how to cope with challenges calmly.
