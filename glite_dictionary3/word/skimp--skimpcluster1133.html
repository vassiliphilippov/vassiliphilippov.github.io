to skimp

verb
provide less than enough; be sparing
Pronunciation: / skɪmp / (AmE), / skɪmp / (BrE)
to provide or use less than is needed, often to save money, time, or effort; to be stingy or do something with too little effort or resources

Examples

Related Terms

  • synonym – cut corners: to do something in the easiest or cheapest way, often sacrificing quality
  • synonym – scrimp: to be excessively economical or parsimonious
  • antonym – splurge: to spend freely or extravagantly
  • antonym – squander: to spend or use something wastefully
  • synonym – stint: to provide an inadequate amount, to be frugal with
