prosiness

noun
the state of being dull or commonplace
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊzinəs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dull, commonplace, or lacking in imaginative or poetic qualities; the state of being prosy

Examples

everyday prosiness
Despite the prosiness of the topic, the lecturer managed to make it engaging.
Readers complained about the prosiness of the novel’s style.
The prosiness of his speech made it difficult to stay interested.

Related Terms

  • antonym – poeticism: the quality or character of poetry or poetic expression
  • synonym – prosaicness: the quality of being prosaic or unimaginative
