measure

noun
segment of music defined by beats
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
(music) A segment of time in a piece of music defined by a given number of beats, separated on a staff by bar lines.

Examples

measure in music
Each chorus in the song lasts exactly sixteen measures.
The composer wrote a difficult passage in the fourth measure of the piece.
The drummer kept a steady rhythm for eight measures before the melody began.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bar: segment of music marked by bar lines, synonymous with 'measure' (chiefly British)
