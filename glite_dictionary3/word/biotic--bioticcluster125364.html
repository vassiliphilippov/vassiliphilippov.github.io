biotic

adjective
relating to living organisms
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / bʌɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from living organisms, especially in their ecological relationships.

Examples

biotic factors
Climate change affects both biotic and abiotic factors in natural habitats.
Scientists study how biotic interactions influence the diversity of species.
The biotic components of an ecosystem include plants, animals, and microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abiotic: not derived from living organisms; nonliving components of an environment
