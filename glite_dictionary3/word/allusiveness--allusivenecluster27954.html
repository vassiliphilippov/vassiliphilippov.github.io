allusiveness

noun
quality of being indirectly referential
Pronunciation: / əˈluːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈluːsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of tending to make indirect or implied references, rather than direct statements

Examples

literary allusiveness
His speech was marked by great allusiveness, requiring attentive listening to catch all the references.
Some critics praise the allusiveness of the novel, while others find it vague.
The allusiveness of her poetry invites readers to uncover hidden meanings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allusiveness: tendency to refer indirectly to something
  • antonym – directness: quality of being straightforward and explicit
