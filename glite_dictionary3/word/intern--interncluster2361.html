to intern

verb
confine or detain, especially during war
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to confine or detain someone, especially during wartime, without trial, usually for political or military reasons

Examples

to intern enemy civilians
Authorities may intern civilians if they are suspected of posing a threat to security.
During the war, the government decided to intern all enemy nationals living near the coast.
Many political dissidents were interned without trial during that period.
