hypallage

noun
reversal of syntactic relation between words
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpælədʒi / (AmE), / hɪˈpalədʒi / (BrE)
A literary device in which the syntactic relationship between two words is reversed, often resulting in an unusual or striking expression.

Examples

hypallage in poetry
He pointed out the clever use of hypallage in the line 'the dancing leaves of autumn.'
Poets often use hypallage to create vivid and surprising imagery in their writing.
The phrase “restless night” is an example of hypallage, since it is the person, not the night, who is restless.
