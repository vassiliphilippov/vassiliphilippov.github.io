to overrefine

verb
refine excessively or with undue subtlety
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to make something too refined or subtle, often to the point of diminishing its value or clarity

Examples

to overrefine an idea
If you overrefine your argument, it may become too abstract for the audience to follow.
She realized she had overrefined her design, making it less practical and appealing.
Some critics argue that modern art tends to overrefine basic principles, losing its emotional impact.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overcomplicate: to make something more complicated than necessary
  • degree lower – refine: to make something more pure or improve by making small changes
