transplant

noun
medical operation moving organ/tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænsˌplænt /; / trænˈsplænt / (AmE), / ˈtræns.plɑːnt /; / trænˈsplɑːnt / (BrE)
a medical operation in which an organ or tissue is moved from one body to another, or the organ/tissue itself

Examples

kidney transplant
After the transplant, the patient's body gradually accepted the new kidney.
The hospital specializes in heart transplant surgery for children and adults.
Thousands of people are on the waiting list for an organ transplant each year.
After the transplant, he had to take medication for the rest of his life.
Doctors monitored the transplant closely for signs of rejection.
The patient received a transplant to replace her failing kidney.
