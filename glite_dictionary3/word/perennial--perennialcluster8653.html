perennial

adjective
lasting for a very long time
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛniəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəl / (BrE)
lasting or recurring for a long time; enduring or continually happening

Examples

perennial problem
Drought is a perennial problem in this region.
Her perennial optimism makes her a joy to be around.
They are engaged in a perennial struggle for independence.
Budget overruns are a perennial issue in large-scale construction projects.
He voiced perennial concerns about the lack of progress in negotiations.
The region faces perennial water shortages due to its dry climate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – eternal: lasting forever; having no end
  • antonym – occasional: happening from time to time, but not regularly
  • synonym – persistent: continuing firmly or obstinately over a prolonged period
  • antonym – temporary: lasting for only a limited period; not permanent
