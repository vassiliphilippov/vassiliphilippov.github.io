colonisation

noun
establishment of colonies
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒlənʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of establishing control over a territory and settling it with people from another country

Examples

colonisation of new lands
European colonisation transformed the Americas in the 16th century.
Historians continue to debate the long-term impact of colonisation on indigenous populations.
The colonisation of large parts of Africa had profound cultural and political effects.
