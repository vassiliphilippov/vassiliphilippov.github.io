defence

noun
psychological mechanism to reduce anxiety
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
(Psychiatry) An unconscious psychological process that serves to reduce anxiety arising from instinctive desires or conflicts.

Examples

psychological defence mechanism
Repression is considered a common psychological defence according to Freud's theories.
Some therapists focus on helping clients become aware of their unconscious defence strategies.
The patient's use of humor was identified as a defence against anxiety.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coping mechanism: a strategy or process for managing stress or anxiety
  • synonym – defense mechanism: an unconscious mental process that protects a person from anxiety
