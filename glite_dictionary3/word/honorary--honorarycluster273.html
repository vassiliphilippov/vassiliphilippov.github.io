honorary

adjective
given as an honor without duties or payment
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnəˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəri / (BrE)
(of a title, degree, position, etc.) given as an honor without the usual requirements, responsibilities, or payment.

Examples

honorary degree
He holds an honorary position on the board and does not participate in daily operations.
She was awarded an honorary doctorate by the university for her contribution to science.
The club made her an honorary member because of her long-standing support.

  • synonym – ceremonial: relating to or used for formal events and ceremonies; not involving real power or duties
