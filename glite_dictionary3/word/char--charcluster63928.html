to char

verb
burn slightly so as to blacken surface
Pronunciation: / tʃɑr / (AmE), / tʃɑː / (BrE)
to burn or blacken the surface of something, especially food or material, so that it becomes dark or burnt

Examples

to char the toast
Be careful not to char the toast; it should be golden, not black.
If you char the peppers over an open flame, their skins will come off easily.
The log began to char around the edges as the campfire grew hotter.
Heat from the campfire can easily char your marshmallow if you aren't careful.
If the bread stays in the toaster too long, it will char on the outside.
Lightning struck the tree and began to char its thick trunk.
Be careful not to char the meat, or it will taste bitter.
He likes to char the meat over an open flame to give it extra flavor.
Some chefs intentionally char the meat for a crunchy, smoky crust.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blacken: to make or become black or dark, especially by burning
  • synonym – burn: to cook with excessive heat so the surface becomes black
  • synonym – scorch: to burn the surface of something without setting it on fire
