glaring

adjective
extremely obvious or conspicuous, especially of faults
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɛɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡleə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fault or mistake) very obvious and easily noticed, particularly because it is bad or shocking.

Examples

glaring mistake
Despite its glaring flaws, the project moved forward.
The journalist pointed out a glaring error in the official report.
There was a glaring inconsistency between his words and his actions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – egregious: outstandingly bad; shocking
  • synonym – flagrant: noticeably wrong or offensive
  • antonym – subtle: not obvious; difficult to detect or notice
