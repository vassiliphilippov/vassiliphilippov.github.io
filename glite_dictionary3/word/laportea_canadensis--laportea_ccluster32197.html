Laportea canadensis

noun
a North American stinging woodland plant
Pronunciation: / ləˈpɔr.ti.ə kəˈnæd.ənsɪs / (AmE), / ləˈpɔː.tɪ.ə kəˈnæd.ənsɪs / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant of rich woods, known for its stinging hairs and used for textile fibers; also called Canadian wood nettle.

Examples

Laportea canadensis stinging plant
Laportea canadensis grows abundantly in moist, shady forests throughout eastern North America.
Some indigenous peoples used fibers from Laportea canadensis to make strong fishing nets and textiles.
The stinging hairs of Laportea canadensis can cause skin irritation when touched.
