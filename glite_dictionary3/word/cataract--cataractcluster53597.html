cataract

noun
clouding of the lens of the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt̬.əˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈkæt.ər.ækt / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the lens of the eye becomes cloudy, causing impaired vision

Examples

cataract surgery
Her blurry vision was eventually traced to a cataract forming on the lens.
Many older adults develop a cataract in one or both eyes over time.
The surgeon recommended removing the cataract to restore the patient's vision.
