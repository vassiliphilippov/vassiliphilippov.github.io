hold

noun
power or influence over others
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
an act of grasping, gripping, or the power or influence over others

Examples

strong hold
His charm gave him a hold on the audience throughout the speech.
She has a strong hold over her team, motivating them to achieve results.

He lost his hold on the wet ladder and slipped down.
She kept a firm hold on her child's hand while crossing the street.
The wrestler had a tight hold around his opponent.
