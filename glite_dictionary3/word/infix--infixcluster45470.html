infix

noun
an affix inserted within a word
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪks / (BrE)
An affix inserted into the middle of a word, rather than at its beginning or end.

Examples

infix '-um-' example
In Tagalog, the word 'sulat' can take the infix '-um-' to become 'sumulat'.
Some languages use an infix to convey tense or aspect within a verb stem.
The linguistics professor explained how an infix differs from a prefix or suffix.
