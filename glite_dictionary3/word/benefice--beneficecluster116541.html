to benefice

verb
to grant a benefice (to someone)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛnɪfɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪs / (BrE)
to endow (a person, especially a member of the clergy) with a benefice

Examples

to benefice a priest
It was once common practice to benefice scholars who contributed significantly to the church.
Many churches historically beneficed their clergy to ensure their financial security.
The bishop decided to benefice the young priest for his dedicated service.
