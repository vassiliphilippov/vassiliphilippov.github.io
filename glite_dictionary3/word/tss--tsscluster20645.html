TSS

noun
toxic shock syndrome; acute bacterial infection
Pronunciation: / ti ɛs ɛs / (AmE), / tiː ɛs ɛs / (BrE)
a serious and sometimes fatal acute condition caused by bacterial infection (often Staphylococcus), characterized by high fever, rash, low blood pressure, and organ failure, commonly associated with tampon use or surgical wounds

Examples

TSS from tampon use
After reading about TSS in a health magazine, she became more cautious with menstrual products.
Awareness campaigns about TSS encourage women to change tampons regularly and avoid prolonged use.
The doctor quickly diagnosed her with TSS after she developed a high fever and low blood pressure.
