temperate

adjective
mild or moderate climate or weather
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
(of weather or climate) having moderate temperatures; not subject to extremes of heat or cold.

Examples

temperate climate
Many species of plants thrive only in temperate zones where the weather is generally mild.
She moved to a temperate coastal town to escape the harsh winters of the north.
The Mediterranean is known for its temperate climate, with warm summers and mild winters.

Related Terms

  • antonym – extreme: weather or climate with intense conditions
