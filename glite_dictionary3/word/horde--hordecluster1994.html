horde

noun
nomadic community or tribe
Pronunciation: / hɔrd / (AmE), / hɔːd / (BrE)
A large group or tribe, especially one of nomads in Central Asia or Eastern Europe, typically moving together as a community.

Examples

the Mongol horde
In ancient times, the Mongol horde swept across much of Asia and Europe.
Legend tells of a horde that established a great empire by uniting rival clans.
The historians studied the movement of each horde across the steppes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tribe: a social group consisting of people with the same culture, language, or ancestor
