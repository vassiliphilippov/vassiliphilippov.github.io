crankiness

noun
the state of being irritable or eccentric
Pronunciation: / ˈkræŋkinəs / (AmE), / ˈkraŋkɪnɪs / (BrE)
A tendency to be easily irritated or to behave in an eccentric and fussy manner.

Examples

morning crankiness
His crankiness in the mornings makes conversations a challenge until he has his coffee.
The teacher's unexpected crankiness surprised her usually patient students.
We laughed at Grandpa's harmless crankiness and his odd little habits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – even-temperedness: the state of being calm and not easily upset
  • synonym – irritability: the quality of being easily annoyed or angered
