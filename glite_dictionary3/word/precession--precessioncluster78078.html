precession

noun
wobbling motion of rotating body
Pronunciation: / priˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the slow movement of the axis of a spinning body, such as a top or planet, around another axis, causing the rotation axis to trace out a cone

Examples

axial precession movement
Astronomers account for axial precession when tracking the positions of stars over centuries.
Gyroscopes demonstrate precession when a force is applied to their spinning rotor.
The Earth's precession causes a gradual shift in the orientation of its axis.
