pinon

noun
low-growing North American pine
Pronunciation: / ˈpiː.njɑːn / (AmE), / ˈpiː.njɒn / (BrE)
A low-growing pine tree native to western North America, known for producing edible seeds called pine nuts.

Examples

pinon pine tree
A mature pinon may only reach fifteen feet but can live for centuries.
She collected pinon nuts from beneath the low, spreading trees.
The pinon forests provide vital habitat for wildlife in the southwest.
