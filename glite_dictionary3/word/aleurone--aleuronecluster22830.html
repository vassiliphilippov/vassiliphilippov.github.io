aleurone

noun
protein layer in seed endosperm
Pronunciation: / əˈlʊrˌoʊn / (AmE), / əˈljuː.rəʊn / (BrE)
a granular protein layer found in the outermost layer of the endosperm of many seeds and cereal grains, important in plant development and seed biology

Examples

aleurone layer in seeds
Scientists study the aleurone to understand how nutrients are mobilized in grains.
The aleurone layer releases enzymes during seed germination.
Wheat's aleurone contains important proteins that contribute to its nutritional value.
