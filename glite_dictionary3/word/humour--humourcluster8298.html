humour

noun
the quality of being amusing or comical
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmər / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
the quality of being funny or amusing, or the ability to appreciate or express what is funny

Examples

British humour
British humour is often based on understatement and irony.
His dry humour always made meetings more enjoyable.
She used her sense of humour to diffuse tense situations.
A good teacher often uses humour to keep students engaged in lessons.
Despite all the stress at work, she never loses her humour.
He has a dry humour that not everyone immediately recognizes.
Her sense of humour made difficult situations more bearable.
Some people have a dry humour that is not immediately obvious.
The comedian's clever humour appealed to audiences of all ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym – humor: the quality of being funny or amusing (American spelling)
  • antonym – seriousness: the quality of being solemn or earnest, lack of humour
  • synonym – wit: the ability to use words and ideas in a quick and inventive way to create humour
