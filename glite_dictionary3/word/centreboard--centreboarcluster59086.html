centreboard

noun
retractable keel fin on sailboats
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.təˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a retractable, flat board or fin attached to the keel of a sailboat, lowered into the water to prevent sideways drift from wind pressure

Examples

centreboard of a sailboat
After hitting shallow water, they quickly raised the centreboard to avoid damage.
Many small dinghies are designed with a retractable centreboard for greater versatility.
The centreboard makes it easier to sail upwind by preventing the boat from drifting sideways.
