teratogenic

adjective
causing birth defects during development
Pronunciation: / ˌter.ə.toʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌter.ə.təʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or agent) able to cause developmental malformations or birth defects in an embryo or fetus.

Examples

teratogenic drugs
Exposure to teratogenic chemicals should be avoided by expectant mothers.
Researchers are studying which environmental factors are most teratogenic in animals.
The physician warned that some medications can have teratogenic effects during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non-teratogenic: not causing developmental malformations or birth defects
