to splatter

verb
make liquid fall in scattered drops
Pronunciation: / ˈsplætər /; / ˈsplæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplætə / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to splash or be thrown in drops, often in a scattered or messy way

Examples

to splatter sauce
She accidentally splattered soup all over the stovetop while stirring too quickly.
The rain began to splatter against the window panes as the storm moved in.
Water splatters everywhere when you drop ice cubes into a hot pan.
Be careful not to splatter paint all over the floor while you work.
Hot oil can splatter if you cook at too high a temperature.
She accidentally splattered ketchup on her white shirt.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spatter: to scatter small drops of liquid onto a surface
  • synonym – spill: to accidentally or deliberately let liquid flow out of a container
  • synonym – splash: to cause liquid to fly about in drops
