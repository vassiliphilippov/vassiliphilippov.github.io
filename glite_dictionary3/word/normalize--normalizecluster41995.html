to normalize

verb
heat-treat to desired hardness or texture
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a material, such as metal or glass, attain a desired consistency, texture, or hardness by gradually heating and then cooling it

Examples

to normalize metal
Engineers recommend that you normalize the alloy if you want uniformity in texture and hardness.
Modern production lines often normalize glass to remove internal stresses caused during shaping.
To ensure the steel frame is strong and flexible, manufacturers normalize the metal before use.

Related Terms

  • synonym – temper: to improve the hardness or elasticity of a material by heating and cooling
