dissociative

adjective
causing or relating to dissociation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsoʊʃiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsiətɪv / (BrE)
(especially in psychology or medicine) tending to cause or relating to dissociation, particularly of mental processes or identity.

Examples

dissociative disorder
Certain drugs can have dissociative effects on perception and consciousness.
Researchers are studying the dissociative nature of some mental health conditions.
The patient exhibited symptoms of a dissociative disorder after the traumatic event.

Related Terms

  • antonym – associative: tending to associate or connect; the opposite of dissociative
