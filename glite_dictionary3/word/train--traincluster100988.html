to train

verb
drag or allow to drag along the ground
Pronunciation: / treɪn / (AmE), / treɪn / (BrE)
(of a garment or similar object) to drag or allow to trail loosely along the ground or another surface behind one.

Examples

ribbon trains behind
During the parade, the dancer's ribbon trained along the pavement in colorful swirls.
His long cloak trained in the dust as he entered the old mansion.
The bride's gown trained elegantly behind her as she walked down the aisle.
