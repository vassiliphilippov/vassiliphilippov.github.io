orangery

noun
greenhouse for orange trees
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrəndʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒəri / (BrE)
a building or greenhouse, especially one from the 17th to 19th centuries, where orange trees and other citrus plants are grown, typically in cooler climates

In the eighteenth century, wealthy Europeans built grand orangeries to house their exotic fruit trees.
