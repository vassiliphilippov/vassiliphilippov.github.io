lime

noun
adhesive used to trap birds
Pronunciation: / laɪm / (AmE), / laɪm / (BrE)
A sticky substance, often made from birdlime, smeared on branches to trap small birds.

Examples

sticky lime trap
Coating the branch with lime, he set the trap and waited patiently.
Hunters once used lime as a traditional method for catching songbirds.
The poacher covered the twigs with lime to snare unsuspecting birds.
