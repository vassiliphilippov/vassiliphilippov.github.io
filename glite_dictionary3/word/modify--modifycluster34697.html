to modify

verb
add a modifier to a constituent
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(in linguistics) To add a word or phrase that restricts or adds to the meaning of another word or phrase within a sentence.

Examples

to modify a noun
A prepositional phrase can modify a noun to give more specific information.
Adverbs are often used to modify verbs in a sentence.
In the phrase 'very tall man,' the word 'very' modifies the adjective 'tall.'
