occupied

adjective
under foreign military control after invasion
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a country, region, or area) seized and controlled by a foreign military force as a result of invasion.

Examples

occupied city
All communication was censored in the occupied city after the invasion.
During the war, many civilians fled the occupied territories to seek safety elsewhere.
International law sets out rules for how an occupied area should be governed.
