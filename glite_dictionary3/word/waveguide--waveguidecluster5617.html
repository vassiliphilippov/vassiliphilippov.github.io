waveguide

noun
structure guiding light in photonics
Pronunciation: / c8we6avcc61a6ad / (AmE), / c8we6avcc61a6ad / (BrE)
A physical structure (often dielectric or on‑chip) that confines and guides light (optical or infrared waves) for communication, sensing, or photonic circuits.

Examples

optical waveguide
The silicon waveguide guides infrared light across the photonic chip for high-speed data transmission.
To connect the laser to the circuit, they aligned the fiber to the on-chip waveguide with great care.
During testing, the lab measured scattering losses in the polymer waveguide at visible wavelengths.

Related Terms

  • synonym – optical fiber: a flexible strand of glass or plastic that guides light for communications; a common type of optical waveguide
