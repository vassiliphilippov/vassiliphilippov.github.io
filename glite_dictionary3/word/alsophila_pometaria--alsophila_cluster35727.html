Alsophila pometaria

noun
species of North American moth
Pronunciation: / ælˈsoʊfɪlə poʊˈmiːtəriə / (AmE), / ælˈsɒfɪlə pəˈmiːtərɪə / (BrE)
A North American moth species whose grey-winged males and wingless females produce larvae known as fall cankerworms.

Gardeners often monitor outbreaks of Alsophila pometaria to protect their trees from defoliation.
Male Alsophila pometaria moths are easily recognized by their grey wings, while the females are wingless.
