to scratch

verb
gather money or resources with difficulty
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ / (BrE)
to gather money or resources with difficulty, especially over time and in small amounts

Examples

to scratch together money
After years of hard work, she finally managed to scratch together enough savings for a down payment.
He tried to scratch enough money to pay the rent this month.
Many artists have to scratch a living teaching and taking odd jobs.
