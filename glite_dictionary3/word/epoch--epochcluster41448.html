epoch

noun
reference date in astronomy
Pronunciation: / ˈiː.pɑk / (AmE), / ˈiː.pɒk / (BrE)
(Astronomy) An arbitrarily fixed date used as a reference point for calculating or recording the positions of celestial bodies.

Examples

astronomical epoch date
Astronomers use a standard epoch to ensure data consistency across observations.
Celestial coordinates must be corrected according to the epoch of observation.
The star's position was measured at the epoch of January 1, 2000.
