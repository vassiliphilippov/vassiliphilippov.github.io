altocumulus

noun
type of medium-altitude cloud
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.toʊˈkjuː.mjə.ləs / (AmE), / ˌæl.təʊˈkjuː.mjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
a type of white or gray cloud composed of rounded masses or rolls, found at medium altitudes in the sky (about 2 to 6 km or 6,500 to 20,000 feet above the ground)

Examples

altocumulus clouds in sky
A band of altocumulus clouds stretched across the morning sky.
Meteorologists often study altocumulus formations to predict incoming weather changes.
The airplane flew just below a layer of altocumulus clouds.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – cumulus: a type of puffy, white cloud with a flat base, found at lower altitudes
