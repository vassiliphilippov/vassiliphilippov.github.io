dogfight

noun
fierce, disorderly fight (esp. aerial battle)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔgˌfaɪt /; / ˈdɔɡˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒg.faɪt /; / ˈdɒɡfaɪt / (BrE)
a fiercely contested fight, argument, or competition, often involving aggressive or intense rivalry

Examples

fierce dogfight for victory
A violent dogfight broke out between rival gangs downtown last night.
The movie featured an intense dogfight scene with vintage fighter planes.
The two pilots became engaged in a dogfight high above the city.
The final few minutes of the game turned into a real dogfight between the teams.
The two candidates engaged in a heated dogfight during the televised debate.
There was a prolonged dogfight over who would secure the contract.
