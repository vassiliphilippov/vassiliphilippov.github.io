cud

noun
regurgitated food chewed by ruminants
Pronunciation: / kʌd / (AmE), / kʌd / (BrE)
partly digested food returned from the stomach to the mouth of a ruminant animal to be chewed again

Examples

chew the cud slowly
After grazing, sheep often rest and chew their cud in groups.
Farmers watch to ensure their cattle are bringing up cud as a sign of good health.
The cow chewed its cud lazily in the afternoon sun.
