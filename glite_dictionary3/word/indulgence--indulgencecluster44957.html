indulgence

noun
foolish or senseless behavior
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (BrE)
foolish or senseless behavior; an act of allowing oneself to do something unwise or imprudent.

Examples

a reckless indulgence
His weekend gambling spree was another indulgence that left him regretting his choices.
She apologized for her momentary indulgence in gossip at the office party.
Sometimes a small indulgence is harmless, but repeated carelessness can cause real problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym – folly: lack of good sense or judgment; foolish action
  • antonym – prudence: wise or cautious behavior; exercising sound judgment
