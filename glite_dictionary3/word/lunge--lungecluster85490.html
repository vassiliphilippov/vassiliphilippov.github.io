lunge

noun
sudden forward movement
Pronunciation: / lʌndʒ / (AmE), / lʌndʒ / (BrE)
a sudden forward movement, especially to attack, reach, or seize something (such as in sports or fencing)

Examples

a quick lunge
He made a quick lunge to catch the falling glass.
The cat's lunge caught the mouse by surprise.
With a sudden lunge, the fencer attacked his opponent.
A quick, well-timed lunge can catch your opponent off guard.
During practice, she worked on the speed and accuracy of her lunge.
The fencer executed a perfect lunge to score the winning point.

Related Terms

  • antonym – retreat: the act of withdrawing or moving back
  • synonym – thrust: a sudden forward movement; to push or drive with force
