to crow

verb
to utter a shrill call like a rooster
Pronunciation: / kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
(of a cock or similar bird) to utter a loud, shrill call, especially at dawn; also, to make a sound resembling this.

Examples

to crow at dawn
Every morning, the cocks crow before sunrise in this village.
The rooster began to crow at the break of dawn, waking the whole farm.
You could hear the peacocks crowing loudly from across the zoo.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cackle: to make a sharp, broken noise, like a hen
