to preordain

verb
to determine or decide in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɔːrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ˌpriːɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to decide, arrange, or determine something in advance, often by a higher power or by fate

Examples

to preordain the future
It is unwise to assume that success is preordained for any project.
Many believe that our destinies are preordained and cannot be changed.
The course of events seemed preordained by tradition and circumstance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – predestine: to determine the outcome or course of events in advance, especially by divine will
