to pluck

verb
to overcharge; swindle
Pronunciation: / plʌk / (AmE), / plʌk / (BrE)
to overcharge or swindle (someone), especially by charging an unreasonably high price

Examples

to pluck a customer
He felt he had been plucked after seeing the bill for his meal.
The taxi driver tried to pluck us by doubling the fare.
Tourists are often plucked in popular shopping areas if they're not careful.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overcharge: to charge (someone) too high a price for goods or a service
  • synonym – rip off: to cheat someone by charging too much
  • antonym – undercharge: to charge less than the correct or expected price
