to scandalize

verb
shock or offend morally
Pronunciation: / ˈskæn.dəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskæn.də.laɪz / (BrE)
to shock or offend someone by behavior that is considered immoral or improper

Examples

to scandalize the public
His rude remarks scandalized everyone at the dinner table.
She feared her bold choices would scandalize her traditional family.
The artist's controversial painting scandalized the conservative community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – offend: to make someone feel upset or insulted
  • synonym – shock: to surprise and upset someone deeply
