to cover

verb
to take over someone else's duties temporarily
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to take someone's place and temporarily do their work or fulfill their responsibilities

Examples

to cover for someone

He agreed to cover her shift during the busy holiday season.
She is covering for our secretary who is ill this week.
