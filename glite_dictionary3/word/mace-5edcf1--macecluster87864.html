mace

noun
ceremonial staff of authority
Pronunciation: / meɪs / (AmE), / meɪs / (BrE)
a ceremonial staff carried as a symbol of authority or office, often used in formal events or by officials

Examples

ceremonial mace
The ornate mace is kept in a special case at the city hall.
The university's chancellor carried the mace during the graduation ceremony.
Traditionally, the mace signifies the speaker's authority in parliament.
