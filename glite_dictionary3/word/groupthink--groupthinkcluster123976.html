groupthink

noun
uncritical group decision making
Pronunciation: / ˈɡruːpˌθɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈɡruːpˌθɪŋk / (BrE)
The practice of thinking or making decisions as a group, often resulting in uncritical acceptance of prevailing views and discouragement of individual creativity or responsibility.

Examples

danger of groupthink
Avoiding groupthink is important for making effective decisions in any organization.
She challenged the groupthink that stifled innovation in her department.
The company's failure was largely attributed to groupthink among its executives.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conformity: behavior in accordance with socially accepted conventions
  • antonym – individualism: the habit or principle of being independent and self-reliant
