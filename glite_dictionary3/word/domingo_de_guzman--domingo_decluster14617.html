Domingo de Guzman

proper noun
Spanish priest; founder of the Dominican Order
Pronunciation: / doʊˈmiŋɡoʊ deɪ ɡuˈzmɑːn / (AmE), / dəˈmɪŋɡəʊ deɪ ɡʊzˈmæn / (BrE)
A Spanish priest who founded the Dominican Order, also known as the Black Friars, around 1170–1221.
