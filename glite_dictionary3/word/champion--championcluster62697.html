to champion

verb
act as a defender or supporter
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən / (BrE)
to publicly support, protect, or fight for a person, belief, or cause

Examples

to champion a cause
He was known to champion the cause of the poor throughout his career.
She has long championed the rights of children in her community.
The organization champions environmental protection and sustainable development.

Related Terms

  • synonym – advocate: to publicly support or recommend a particular cause or policy
  • antonym – oppose: to actively resist or refuse to support
