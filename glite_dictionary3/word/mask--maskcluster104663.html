mask

noun
something used to conceal the truth
Pronunciation: / mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
an action, behavior, or quality used to hide the truth about something or someone

Examples

a mask for feelings
His cheerful attitude was just a mask for his disappointment.
She wore a mask of confidence to hide her nervousness during the interview.
The company’s promises of change turned out to be a mask for maintaining the status quo.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cover: something that covers or conceals the real nature of something
  • synonym – disguise: something that is used to hide something else
  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
