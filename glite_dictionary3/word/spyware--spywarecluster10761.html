spyware

noun
software that collects data secretly
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈspaɪˌwɛə / (BrE)
software that secretly collects information from a computer without the user's knowledge or permission, often for malicious purposes

Examples

spyware infection
Many users unknowingly install spyware when downloading free programs from the internet.
The company's IT department detected several types of spyware on employee computers.
You should update your antivirus software regularly to protect against spyware and other threats.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – adware: software that automatically displays or downloads advertising
  • synonym – malware: software designed to damage or disrupt a computer system
  • degree higher – ransomware: malicious software that blocks access to a system until a ransom is paid
