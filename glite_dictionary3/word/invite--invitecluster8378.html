to invite

verb
be attractive or tempting; provoke a response
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪt / (BrE)
(of an object or situation) to be attractive or appealing in a way that encourages a particular response or action.

Examples

invite conversation
Her friendly smile seemed to invite conversation from everyone she met.
The fresh scent of coffee in the morning can invite even the sleepiest person to wake up early.
The open gate invited curious children to explore the beautiful garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tempt: to try to entice or attract someone to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
