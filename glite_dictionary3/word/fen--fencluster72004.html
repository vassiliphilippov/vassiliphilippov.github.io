fen

noun
grassy low-lying wetland
Pronunciation: / fɛn / (AmE), / fɛn / (BrE)
A low-lying wetland with grassy vegetation, often serving as a transition zone between land and water.

Examples

wet fen area
Conservationists are working to restore the natural water levels in the ancient fen.
She walked for miles across the flat, open fens of East Anglia.
The rare orchid thrives in the fen where the soil is always moist.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bog: wet, spongy ground with decaying vegetation, more acidic than a fen
  • synonym – marsh: an area of low-lying wetland, often waterlogged and with grasses
