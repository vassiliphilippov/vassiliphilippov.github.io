to truss

verb
tie or bind tightly with ropes
Pronunciation: / trʌs / (AmE), / trʌs / (BrE)
to tie or bind firmly, especially with ropes or similar materials, usually to prevent movement

Examples

to truss a box
Before transporting the boxes, workers had to truss them securely with cord.
Rescuers quickly trussed the injured climber to keep him still on the stretcher.
The chef began to truss the chicken before roasting it in the oven.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bind: to tie or fasten something firmly
  • antonym – untie: to undo or release from a tied position
