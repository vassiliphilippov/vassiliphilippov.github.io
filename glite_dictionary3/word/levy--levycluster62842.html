to levy

verb
impose and collect officially (e.g., taxes, fines)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
(of an authority) to impose and collect a tax, fee, fine, or other charge officially.

Examples

to levy a tax
If you fail to pay, the agency may levy the amount directly from your account.
The court has the authority to levy fines against violators of the law.
The government decided to levy a new tax on luxury goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collect: to gather or receive (something due)
  • synonym – impose: to officially order something (such as a tax) to be paid or obeyed
