measly

adjective
contemptibly small in amount
Pronunciation: / ˈmizli / (AmE), / ˈmiːzli / (BrE)
(of an amount) contemptibly small; not nearly enough.

Examples

a measly amount
All she got for her efforts was a measly piece of cake.
He was disappointed to receive a measly fifty dollars for his month's work.
The company made a measly donation to the charity, which barely made a difference.

Related Terms

  • synonym – miserable: pitifully small or inadequate
  • synonym – paltry: (of an amount) very small or meager
  • antonym – substantial: of considerable amount, size, or importance
