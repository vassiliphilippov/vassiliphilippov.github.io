to press

verb
to weigh heavily or be oppressive (figuratively)
Pronunciation: / prɛs / (AmE), / prɛs / (BrE)
(of a feeling, problem, or responsibility) to be oppressive or weigh heavily on someone, causing worry or distress.

Examples

guilt presses on
Guilt can press heavily upon the conscience, making it hard to find peace.
The responsibility to care for his family continued to press on him every day.
Thoughts of the looming deadline pressed on her mind throughout the weekend.
