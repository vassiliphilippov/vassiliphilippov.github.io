to preponderate

verb
be greater in importance or influence
Pronunciation: / priˈpɑndəˌreɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒndəreɪt / (BrE)
to be greater in number, force, influence, or importance than something else; to outweigh

Examples

to preponderate over disadvantages
In her view, the disadvantages did not preponderate sufficiently to warrant rejection.
In the final analysis, the benefits preponderate over the risks involved.
When making decisions, long-term considerations often preponderate against short-term gains.

  • synonym – outweigh: to be more important or significant than
