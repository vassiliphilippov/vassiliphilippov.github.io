to sustain

verb
support with evidence
Pronunciation: / səˈsteɪn / (AmE), / səˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to establish or strengthen (an argument, claim, or idea) by providing supporting evidence or facts

Examples

sustain an argument
His testimony sustained the case against the accused.
New facts have recently sustained the original claim.
The latest research sustains her theory about climate change.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contradict: to assert the opposite of a statement or claim
  • synonym – corroborate: to confirm or give support to a statement or theory
  • synonym – support: to provide evidence or justification for something
