to hot-work

verb
shape metal while hot
Pronunciation: / hɑt wɜrk / (AmE), / hɒt wɜːk / (BrE)
to shape (metal) by rolling, pressing, forging, or otherwise working it while it is hot.

Examples

to hot-work steel
Industrial forges often hot-work metal to achieve the desired strength and shape.
She hot-worked the iron, reshaping it on the anvil while still glowing red.
The machinists hot-work the steel bars before they cool down.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cold-work: to shape metal while it is cold
