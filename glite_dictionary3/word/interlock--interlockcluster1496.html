interlock

noun
vehicle safety device preventing engine start
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪntəlɒk / (BrE)
a safety device in a vehicle that prevents the engine from starting unless certain conditions are met

Examples

interlock device in car
Authorities required him to install an interlock after his conviction for drunk driving.
Many modern cars are equipped with an interlock that requires the brake pedal to be pressed before starting.
The mechanic explained how the faulty interlock was preventing the engine from turning over.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignition interlock: a device fitted to vehicle ignitions to prevent starting unless a breathalyzer test is passed
