fillet

noun
bundle of sensory nerve fibers to the thalamus
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlət / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪt / (BrE)
a bundle of sensory nerve fibers in the nervous system, especially one that transmits information to the thalamus

Examples

fillet of nerve
Damage to the medial fillet can result in loss of touch sensation in certain areas of the body.
Neuroscientists studied the role of the fillet in processing tactile information.
The lateral fillet transmits important sensory signals to the thalamus.
