Jansenism

noun
Roman Catholic doctrine emphasizing predestination and grace
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒænsəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒænsənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The Roman Catholic doctrine and movement founded on the teachings of Cornelis Jansen, emphasizing predestination, original sin, and the necessity of divine grace for salvation.

Examples

Jansenism in theology
Her research focuses on the theological differences between Jansenism and other Catholic movements of the seventeenth century.
The doctrines of Jansenism were condemned by several papal bulls.
The spread of Jansenism in France led to considerable controversy within the Catholic Church.
