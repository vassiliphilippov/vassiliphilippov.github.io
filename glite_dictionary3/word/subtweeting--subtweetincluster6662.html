subtweeting

noun
tweeting about someone without tagging them
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈtwitɪŋ / (AmE), / sʌbˈtwiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of posting a tweet that refers to a particular person without tagging them, so they are unlikely to be notified directly

Examples

subtweeting a friend
Subtweeting is a passive-aggressive way to air grievances online.
He accused her of subtweeting him after their argument.
Many people consider subtweeting to be a form of indirect communication.
