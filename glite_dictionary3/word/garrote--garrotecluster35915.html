garrote

noun
instrument for strangulation or execution
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈrɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
An instrument, usually a cord or wire with handles, used for execution or strangulation by tightening around a person's neck.

Examples

a garrote device
Historically, the garrote was employed in public executions in Spain.
The assassin used a garrote to silently eliminate his target.
The victim had marks on his neck consistent with the use of a garrote.

Related Terms

  • synonym – strangle: to kill by squeezing the throat
