demonization

noun
portraying as evil or wicked
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of portraying someone or something as wicked or evil, often unjustly or for propaganda purposes

Examples

demonization of a group
Media coverage contributed to the demonization of the company following the scandal.
Some historians criticize the demonization of rival nations during wartime propaganda.
The demonization of immigrants in political discourse has sparked widespread debate.
