counterplot

noun
plan to thwart another plot
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌplɑt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌplɒt / (BrE)
A secret scheme or plan intended to thwart or undermine another plot.

Examples

secret counterplot revealed
The detective launched a counterplot to foil the villain's scheme.
The story became more intriguing when a new counterplot emerged among the rivals.
Unbeknownst to the conspirators, a counterplot was already in motion.
