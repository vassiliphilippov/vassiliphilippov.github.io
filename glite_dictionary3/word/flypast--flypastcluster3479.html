flypast

noun
a ceremonial low-level flight of aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌpæst / (AmE), / ˈflaɪpɑːst / (BrE)
a ceremonial flight by one or more aircraft at a low altitude over an area, usually in front of spectators, especially as a salute or in commemoration

Examples

spectacular flypast
A flypast by the Royal Air Force was organized to mark the Queen's birthday.
Spectators gathered early to secure a good view of the flypast during the national celebration.
The crowd was thrilled as the flypast of vintage planes roared overhead.
