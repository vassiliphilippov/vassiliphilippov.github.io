absolutist

adjective
supporting total or absolute power, esp. in government
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.sə.lu.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.ljuː.tɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or supporting the principle of absolute, unrestricted power, especially in government or politics.

Examples

absolutist government style
Her approach to governance was criticized as excessively absolutist and intolerant of dissent.
Many historians have debated whether the emperor's policies truly reflected an absolutist ideology.
The new regime followed an absolutist model, granting power to a single ruler.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authoritarian: favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • antonym – democratic: relating to or supporting democracy or its principles
