knight

noun
chess piece shaped like a horse's head
Pronunciation: / naɪt / (AmE), / naɪt / (BrE)
A chess piece shaped like a horse's head that moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular.

Examples

chess knight
Many beginners forget how the knight moves in chess.
The knight captured the opponent's bishop with a clever move.
You need to move your knight to protect your king.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bishop: A chess piece that moves diagonally
  • synonym – pawn: A chess piece that moves forward one square, with special rules
  • synonym – rook: A chess piece that moves in straight lines vertically or horizontally
