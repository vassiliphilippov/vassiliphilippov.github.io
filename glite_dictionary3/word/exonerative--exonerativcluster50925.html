exonerative

adjective
absolving from blame or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɑːnəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒnərətɪv / (BrE)
Providing absolution from blame or responsibility; tending to clear someone of wrongdoing.

Examples

exonerative evidence
An exonerative statement from the witness shifted the course of the investigation.
The jury found the evidence to be exonerative, clearing the defendant of all charges.
The report contained several exonerative details that supported the accused's innocence.
