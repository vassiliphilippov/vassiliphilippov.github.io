to stray

verb
to wander from a path or course
Pronunciation: / streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
to move away from the correct place, path, or topic; to wander without a clear direction or aim

Examples

to stray from topic
His thoughts often stray during long meetings.
If you stray from the trail, it's easy to get lost in the woods.
She warned her son not to stray too far from the playground.
During the meeting, he began to stray from the main agenda and discussed unrelated issues.
Her thoughts would often stray when she was tired, making it hard to concentrate on her work.
Try not to stray from the topic when answering the exam questions.
After the storm, several sheep strayed from the flock and wandered into the forest.
During the recession, many workers strayed from city to city in search of employment.
Hungry cats sometimes stray through neighborhoods looking for leftover food.

Related Terms

  • synonym – digress: to depart from the main subject in speech or writing
  • synonym – drift: to be carried slowly by a current of air or water; to move aimlessly
  • antonym – focus: to concentrate attention or effort; opposite of stray in this context
  • synonym – roam: to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically
  • antonym – stay: to remain in the same place
  • synonym – wander: to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way
