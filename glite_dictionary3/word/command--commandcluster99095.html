to command

verb
give an authoritative order
Pronunciation: / kəˈmænd / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
to give an authoritative order to someone; to direct or instruct someone to do something

Examples

to command someone
She commands everyone in the office with confidence and authority.
The general commanded the troops to advance toward the city at dawn.
The teacher commanded the students to stop talking and pay attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forbid: to order someone not to do something; prohibit
  • synonym – order: to tell someone to do something; to instruct
