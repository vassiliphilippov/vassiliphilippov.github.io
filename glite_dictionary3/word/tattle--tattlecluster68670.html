to tattle

verb
reveal secrets, often in a childish manner
Pronunciation: / ˈtætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtætl̩ / (BrE)
to reveal confidential information or secrets, especially in a childish or petty way

Examples

to tattle on classmates
Children sometimes tattle on each other to their teachers.
It's considered rude to tattle on your coworkers without good reason.
She promised not to tattle about the surprise party.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blab: to reveal secrets or talk carelessly
  • synonym – gossip: to talk about other people's private affairs
  • antonym – keep quiet: to not speak or reveal information
