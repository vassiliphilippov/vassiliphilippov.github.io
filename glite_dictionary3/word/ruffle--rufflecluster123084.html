to ruffle

verb
walk with a proud, showy gait
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.əl / (BrE)
to walk with a proud or showy gait, often in an attempt to impress others

Examples

to ruffle down the hall

He ruffled down the hallway, eager for everyone to notice his new suit.
She ruffled past the crowd, head held high and shoulders back.
