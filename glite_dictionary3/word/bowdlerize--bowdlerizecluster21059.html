to bowdlerize

verb
to censor by omitting offensive parts
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊd.lɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbaʊd.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
to remove or alter parts of a book, play, or other work that are considered offensive or inappropriate, often resulting in a less effective or weakened version

Examples

to bowdlerize a book
Some critics argue that films are bowdlerized to avoid controversy, which diminishes their impact.
Teachers debated whether to bowdlerize certain scenes from the play during the school performance.
The publisher decided to bowdlerize the classic novel to make it suitable for children.

  • synonym – censor: to examine and suppress or remove unacceptable parts of a text
