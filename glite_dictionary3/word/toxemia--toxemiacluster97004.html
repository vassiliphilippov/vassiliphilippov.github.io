toxemia

noun
blood poisoning by bacterial toxins
Pronunciation: / tɑkˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which toxins produced by bacteria enter the bloodstream, leading to blood poisoning.

Examples

toxemia from infection
Doctors monitored her closely for any signs of toxemia during her recovery.
Early recognition of toxemia is vital for effective treatment and patient survival.
The patient developed toxemia following a severe bacterial infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sepsis: a serious medical condition caused by the body's response to infection in the bloodstream
