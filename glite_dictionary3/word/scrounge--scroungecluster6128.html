to scrounge

verb
search for or collect food or provisions
Pronunciation: / skraʊndʒ / (AmE), / skraʊndʒ / (BrE)
to search for or collect something, especially food or provisions, by foraging or making do with what is available, often in a resourceful or opportunistic way.

Examples

to scrounge food scraps
After losing his job, he would scrounge meals from restaurant leftovers downtown.
During the harsh winter, the fox had to scrounge for food wherever it could find it.
The stray cats often scrounge around garbage bins looking for scraps.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forage: to search widely for food or provisions
