to score

verb
compose or arrange music for a score
Pronunciation: / skɔr / (AmE), / skɔː / (BrE)
to write music in the form of a score, especially for a particular production such as a film, play, or performance

Examples

to score a movie
John Williams scored many famous movies, including the 'Star Wars' series.
She was hired to score the soundtrack for the new science fiction film.
The composer is currently scoring a Broadway musical.
