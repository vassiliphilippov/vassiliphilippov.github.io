to stifle

verb
suppress or restrain
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪfəl /; / ˈstaɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈstaɪfəl /; / ˈstaɪ.fəl / (BrE)
to hold back or suppress an emotion, reaction, or development so it is not shown or does not grow

Examples

to stifle laughter
He struggled to stifle his anger during the heated debate.
Sometimes, it's necessary to stifle your laughter in serious situations.
Strict rules can stifle creativity in the workplace.
He could barely stifle a yawn after working late into the night.
Maria attempted to stifle her anger when she heard the unfair comment.
She tried to stifle her laughter during the serious meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – encourage: to give support or confidence, the opposite of restraining or suppressing
  • antonym – express: to show or reveal an emotion or reaction
  • synonym – repress: to subdue or hold back an emotion
  • synonym – suppress: to put an end to something forcibly; to prevent development or expression
