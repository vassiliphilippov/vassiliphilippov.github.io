mutism

noun
inability or unwillingness to speak
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuː.tɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tɪzəm / (BrE)
the condition of being unable or unwilling to speak, especially due to a physical or psychological reason

Examples

selective mutism
Her sudden mutism puzzled the doctors, as she was previously very talkative.
Selective mutism is often observed in children who experience extreme social anxiety.
The diagnosis of mutism was confirmed after a series of psychological evaluations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – muteness: the quality or state of being mute; inability to speak
