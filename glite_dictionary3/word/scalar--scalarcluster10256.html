scalar

adjective
having only magnitude, not direction
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪlər / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlə / (BrE)
relating to a quantity that has only magnitude and no direction, such as mass or speed

Examples

scalar measurement type
In physics, a scalar quantity like temperature can be described by a single number.
Mass is a classic example of a scalar property in science.
Unlike force, which is a vector, energy is a scalar and does not involve direction.
