ataxia

noun
lack of muscle coordination
Pronunciation: / əˈtækiə / (AmE), / əˈtæksɪə / (BrE)
the inability to coordinate voluntary muscle movements, often resulting in unsteady movements and a staggering gait

Examples

ataxia affects movement
Alcohol intoxication can temporarily cause ataxia and impaired walking.
Genetic disorders such as Friedreich's ataxia affect the nervous system and movement.
The patient's diagnosis of ataxia explained her difficulties with balance and coordination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – coordination: the ability to use different parts of the body together smoothly and efficiently
  • degree higher – dyskinesia: impairment of voluntary movement, a broader term
