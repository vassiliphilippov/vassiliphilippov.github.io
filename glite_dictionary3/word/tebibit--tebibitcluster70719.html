tebibit

noun
unit of digital information (2^40 bits)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 gibibits, or 2^40 (1,099,511,627,776) bits.

Examples

one tebibit of data
A tebibit is commonly used to measure data transfer in high-speed networks.
File sizes are often represented in gibibits or tebibits depending on their magnitude.
The server's backup was over one tebibit in size, requiring several hours to complete.
