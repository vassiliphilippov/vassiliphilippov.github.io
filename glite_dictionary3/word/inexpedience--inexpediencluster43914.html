inexpedience

noun
unsuitability or impracticality for a purpose
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unsuitable or unwise for achieving a desired end; lack of expedience.

Examples

political inexpedience
His decision was criticized for its sheer inexpedience and lack of foresight.
The board considered the inexpedience of implementing such a risky policy at this time.
They debated the inexpedience of further investment, given the current economic climate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expedience: the quality of being convenient and practical despite possibly being improper or immoral
