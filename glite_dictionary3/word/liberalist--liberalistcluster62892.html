liberalist

noun
person advocating liberal principles
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪb.ər.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɪb.ər.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates liberal principles, especially in politics, favoring progress, reform, and the protection of civil liberties

Examples

a liberalist politician
As a proud liberalist, she campaigned tirelessly for social justice and equal rights for all citizens.
Many 19th-century thinkers were considered liberalists because they promoted political and economic freedoms.
The debate featured a liberalist arguing for educational reforms and a conservative who opposed them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conservative: a person favoring traditional views and values with caution about change
  • synonym – liberal: a person favoring individual liberty and government protections for civil rights
