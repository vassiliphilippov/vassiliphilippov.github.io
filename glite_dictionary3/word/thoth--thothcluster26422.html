Thoth

proper noun
Egyptian god of wisdom and writing
Pronunciation: / θoʊθ / (AmE), / təʊθ / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian god of the moon, wisdom, writing, and the arts, often depicted with the head of an ibis; regarded as the scribe of the gods.

Examples

Thoth the scribe god
In ancient Egyptian mythology, Thoth was revered as the inventor of writing and the scribe of the gods.
Legends say that wisdom and knowledge were gifts bestowed upon humanity by Thoth.
Priests of Thoth often wore ibis feathers to honor their deity.
