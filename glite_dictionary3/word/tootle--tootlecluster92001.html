tootle

noun
light casual musical sound
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtəl / (AmE), / ˈtuːtəl / (BrE)
a light, casual sound produced by playing a musical instrument, especially a wind instrument, in an informal or cheerful manner

Examples

a cheerful tootle
A faint tootle echoed from the bandstand while the musicians tuned their instruments.
The tootle of a clarinet drifted softly from the next room as the sun set.
We heard the merry tootle of trumpets as the parade came down the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – toot: a short, sharp sound, especially from a horn or whistle
