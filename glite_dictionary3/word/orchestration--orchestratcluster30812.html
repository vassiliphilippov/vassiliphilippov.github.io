orchestration

noun
arrangement of events for effect
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrkəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːkɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the careful arrangement or coordination of events or elements to achieve a particular effect or outcome

Examples

clever orchestration of events
She admired the clever orchestration of the surprise party, which left everyone speechless.
The orchestration of the annual fundraiser required months of planning and teamwork.
The company's orchestration of the product launch was praised for its flawless execution.
