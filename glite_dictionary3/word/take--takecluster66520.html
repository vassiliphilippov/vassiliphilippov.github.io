take

noun
money earned from sales or transactions
Pronunciation: / teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
the amount of money earned from a particular activity, especially the sale of goods or property; profit or income from transactions

Examples

the take from sales
After taxes, the take from the land sale was less than anticipated.
The band's concert take exceeded all expectations this year.
The weekly take at the restaurant has been steadily increasing.

  • antonym – loss: the state of losing money instead of making a profit
  • synonym – profit: financial gain from business or investment
  • synonym – revenue: income, especially that of a company or organization
