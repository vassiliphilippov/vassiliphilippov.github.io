solecism

noun
socially awkward or tactless act
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.lɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪˌsɪz.əm / (BrE)
a socially awkward or tactless act; a breach of good manners or etiquette

Examples

social solecism
Forgetting to introduce your guests can be seen as a social solecism.
His comment at the dinner table was an embarrassing solecism that made everyone uncomfortable.
Making a joke at a funeral is considered a serious solecism in most cultures.
