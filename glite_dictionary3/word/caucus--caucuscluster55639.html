to caucus

verb
meet to select candidates or set policy
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔkəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəs / (BrE)
to meet as a group, especially within a political party, to select candidates or decide on policy

Examples

to caucus with members
After caucusing for hours, the group finally agreed on a candidate.
Several lawmakers caucused privately ahead of the public vote.
The committee will caucus tomorrow to discuss their endorsement.

  • synonym – convene: to come together for a meeting
