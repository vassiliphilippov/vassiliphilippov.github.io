synchroniser

noun
instrument for matching periodic motions
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪzə / (BrE)
an instrument that indicates whether two periodic motions are synchronous, especially one that enables a pilot to synchronize the propellers of a multi-engine plane

Examples

synchroniser for propellers
A faulty synchroniser can result in uncomfortable noise and uneven engine performance.
Modern aircraft are often equipped with a synchroniser for safer engine operation.
The pilot used the synchroniser to adjust the engines and minimize vibrations.
