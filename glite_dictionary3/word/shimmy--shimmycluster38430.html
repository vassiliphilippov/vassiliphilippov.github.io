shimmy

noun
vigorous ragtime/jazz dance with shaking movements
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
a lively dance, typically performed to ragtime or jazz music, involving vigorous shaking of the shoulders and hips

Examples

the shimmy dance
She learned to shimmy at a jazz club downtown.
The Charleston and the shimmy were popular dances during the Roaring Twenties.
The band played upbeat ragtime tunes, encouraging everyone to try the shimmy.
