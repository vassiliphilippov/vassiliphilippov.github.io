teleost

noun
a type of bony fish
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛliˌɔst, ˈtiːliˌɑst / (AmE), / ˈtiːlɪɒst / (BrE)
a fish belonging to the large subclass Teleostei, characterized by a bony skeleton and comprising the vast majority of modern fishes.

Examples

teleost fish species
As a teleost, the goldfish exhibits unique adaptations among bony fishes.
Most modern fish species, such as trout and salmon, are teleosts with fully ossified skeletons.
The study focused on the jaw structure of several teleost species living in freshwater.
