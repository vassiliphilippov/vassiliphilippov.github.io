to plough

verb
move forcefully through something
Pronunciation: / plaʊ / (AmE), / plaʊ / (BrE)
to move forcefully or steadily through a substance or area as if cutting through it, like a plow through soil

Examples

to plough through snow
He ploughed his way through the crowd to reach the exit.
The ferry ploughed through the thick fog, barely visible from the shore.
The runner ploughed on through the mud despite the heavy rain.

  • synonym – plow: US spelling of 'plough', same verb sense
