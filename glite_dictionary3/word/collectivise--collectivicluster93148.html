to collectivise

verb
bring under collective or state control
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪvaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvaɪz / (BrE)
to bring farms, businesses, or other enterprises under collective or state control, especially as an official policy

Examples

to collectivise farms
After the revolution, the new government sought to collectivise all agricultural land.
Many farms were forcibly collectivised during the period of rapid industrialization.
The policy to collectivise small industries was met with considerable opposition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – privatize: transfer from state or collective to private ownership
