abwatt

noun
CGS unit of power
Pronunciation: / æbˈwɑt / (AmE), / æbˈwɒt / (BrE)
A unit of power in the centimetre-gram-second (CGS) electromagnetic system, equal to the power produced by a current of one abampere flowing across a potential difference of one abvolt; equivalent to one ten-thousandth of a watt.

Examples

abwatt of power
One abwatt equals exactly 0.00001 watts in the SI system.
Physicists rarely use the abwatt today, preferring watts for most applications.
The abwatt is used in older scientific literature to specify small amounts of power.
