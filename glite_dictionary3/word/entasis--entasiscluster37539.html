entasis

noun
convex curve in a column's shaft
Pronunciation: / ˈɛntəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛntəsɪs / (BrE)
A slight convex curve in the shaft of a column, used to correct the optical illusion of concavity when the column's sides are straight.

Examples

entasis in a column
Modern architects sometimes use entasis to echo classical design in contemporary buildings.
The ancient Greeks perfected entasis to create visually appealing columns in their temples.
Without entasis, the columns would appear slightly concave to the human eye.
