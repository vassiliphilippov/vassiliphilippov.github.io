to cancel

verb
decide an event will not happen
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl / (BrE)
to decide that a planned event or arrangement will not take place; to call off something that was scheduled

Examples

to cancel a meeting
The school decided to cancel classes due to the heavy snowfall.
They had to cancel the concert because the singer was ill.
We should cancel our dinner reservation if we can't make it on time.
