traditionalist

adjective
strongly conservative, resistant to change
Pronunciation: / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
Having a strong adherence to traditional views, especially in a way that is conservative and resistant to change.

Examples

traditionalist attitude
Despite growing calls for reform, the school's traditionalist policies did not change.
Her traditionalist attitude often put her at odds with the more progressive members of the group.
The committee remained traditionalist in its approach, refusing to consider any new proposals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conservative: favoring traditional views and values; resistant to change
  • antonym – progressive: favoring innovation and change
