Gasterophilus

noun
genus of horse botflies
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæstəˈrɑfɪləs / (AmE), / ˌɡæstəˈrɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of botflies whose larvae infest the digestive tract of horses and related animals.

Examples

Gasterophilus larvae infestation
Gasterophilus larvae can cause significant discomfort in horses by attaching to their stomach lining.
The lifecycle of Gasterophilus involves the adult fly laying eggs on the horse’s body.
Veterinarians often recommend treatments to control Gasterophilus infestations in stabled horses.
