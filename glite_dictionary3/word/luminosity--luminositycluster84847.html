luminosity

noun
perceived brightness in digital images
Pronunciation: / luːməˈnɑːsəti / (AmE), / luːmɪˈnɒsəti / (BrE)
(in digital imaging) the perceived brightness of an image, pixel, or area, often represented as a separate luminosity (luma) channel used in image processing and color grading.

Examples

luminosity channel
In image editing, luminosity controls how bright the highlights and shadows appear without changing color.
The photographer adjusted the luminosity mask to brighten the model's face while keeping the background colors intact.
Colorists often separate color from luminosity to grade footage while preserving natural hues and contrast.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brightness: the perceived amount of light coming from an object or image
  • synonym – luma: the brightness component of a video or image signal, separate from chrominance (color)
  • synonym – luminance: a technical measure of the amount of light emitted or reflected in a direction, used in imaging and display technology
