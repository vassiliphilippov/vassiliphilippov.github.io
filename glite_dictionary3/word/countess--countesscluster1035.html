countess

noun
female noble ranking as count or earl
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tɪs / (BrE)
A noblewoman holding the rank of count or earl, or the wife of a count or earl.

Examples

the elegant countess
As a young countess, she was admired for her grace and charity.
The countess attended the royal banquet in a stunning gown.
The ancient castle once belonged to a famous countess of the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – earl: a British nobleman ranking above a viscount and below a marquess
