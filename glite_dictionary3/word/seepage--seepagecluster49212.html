seepage

noun
slow leaking of liquid or gas
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsiːpɪdʒ / (BrE)
The slow movement or escape of a liquid or gas through small holes or porous material.

Examples

seepage through walls
Seepage from the cracked pipe caused a small pool of water in the basement.
Engineers are monitoring the seepage of groundwater into the excavation site.
The dam was inspected for signs of seepage after the heavy rain.
