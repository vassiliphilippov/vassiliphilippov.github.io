reciprocation

noun
alternating back-and-forth movement
Pronunciation: / rɪˌsɪprəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌsɪprɒˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
alternating back-and-forth movement, typically in a straight line, as occurs in certain mechanical systems

Examples

reciprocation of motion
The reciprocation in the mechanical arm must be perfectly timed to maintain efficiency.
The reciprocation of the saw blade makes it suitable for cutting through tough materials.
The engine operates through the reciprocation of its pistons within the cylinders.
