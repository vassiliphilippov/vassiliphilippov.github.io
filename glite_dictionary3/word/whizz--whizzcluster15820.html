to whizz

verb
move quickly, often with a sound
Pronunciation: / wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
to move very quickly, often making a humming, buzzing, or swishing sound

Examples

A red sports car whizzed by us on the highway.

A bullet whizzed overhead, making everyone duck for cover.
The bike whizzed past us, leaving only a faint sound behind.
You could hear the arrow whizz through the air during the archery contest.
