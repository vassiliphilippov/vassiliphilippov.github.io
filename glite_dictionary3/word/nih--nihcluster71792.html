NIH

noun
bias against external ideas
Pronunciation: / ɛn aɪ eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɛn aɪ eɪtʃ / (BrE)
Initialism for 'Not Invented Here' — an informal term for a tendency to reject ideas, products, or solutions developed outside one's own organisation and to prefer in-house alternatives.

Examples

NIH attitude problem
The startup suffered from NIH, rejecting useful third-party tools and building everything in-house instead.
Managers blamed the project's delay on NIH that prevented them from adopting proven solutions.
To overcome NIH, the team invited external experts to show how third-party software could help.

Related Terms

  • antonym – open-mindedness: willingness to consider new ideas or opinions
  • synonym – parochialism: a limited or narrow outlook, especially focused on local concerns and unwilling to consider outside ideas
