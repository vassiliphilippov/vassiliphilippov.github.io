spell

noun
a short period of time
Pronunciation: / spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
a period of time during which something happens, especially a particular activity or condition

Examples

a short spell of rain
After a brief spell in the hospital, he returned home.
She needs to sit down after a long spell on her feet.
We had a sudden spell of warm weather in early March.
After a long spell at the factory, he was glad to take a break.
She took her spell at the reception desk before lunch.
You should rest now; I'll take over for a spell.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shift: a period of work in a job, especially one of several periods in which a day is divided
  • synonym – stint: a period of time spent doing a particular job or activity
  • synonym – stretch: an uninterrupted period of time
