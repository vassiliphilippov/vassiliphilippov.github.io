pretence

noun
false claim or display
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛns /; / ˈpriːtns / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
an attempt to make something that is not true appear true; a false claim, display, or show (often to deceive)

Examples

false pretence
His apparent joy was only a pretence to hide his disappointment.
She made a pretence of being interested in the conversation.
They dropped all pretence of politeness and argued openly.
His apology was pure pretence and lacked real sincerity.
They dropped all pretence of being friends once the competition started.
Under the pretence of helping, he was trying to gather information.
His friendliness was nothing but a pretence to gain their trust.
She dropped her pretence of indifference as soon as he entered the room.
The company maintained the pretence of prosperity despite facing severe financial troubles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deception: the act of deceiving someone
  • synonym – guise: an external form, appearance, or manner of presentation, typically concealing the true nature of something
  • antonym – honesty: the quality of being truthful and sincere

  • antonym – reality: the state of things as they actually exist
  • antonym – truth: the true or actual state of a matter; opposite of falsehood or pretence
