flush

noun
period of greatest prosperity/productivity
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden flow or surge of liquid, emotion, or prosperity

Examples

a flush of excitement
Farmers enjoyed a brief flush of abundance after the rains.
The company experienced a flush of success during the late 1990s.
This is the flush of youth, when possibilities seem endless.
A flush of adrenaline rushed through him before he stepped onto the stage.
He felt a flush of excitement as he crossed the finish line first.
Winning the award gave her a sudden flush of pride and joy.
A sudden flush of water swept down the street after the heavy rain.
She heard the flush of the toilet from the next room.
There was a flush of red as blood began to flow from the wound.

Related Terms

  • antonym – boredom: the state of feeling bored and lacking excitement
  • synonym – gush: a sudden rapid flow, especially of liquid
  • synonym – outpouring: an act of flowing out rapidly
  • synonym – peak: The highest point of something, especially times of success or activity
  • synonym – rush: a sudden feeling of excitement or exhilaration
  • synonym – thrill: a sudden strong feeling of excitement or pleasure
  • antonym – trough: A period of low activity or success, opposite of peak or flush
