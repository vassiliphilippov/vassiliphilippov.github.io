earth

noun
classical element; one of four physical substances
Pronunciation: / ɜrθ / (AmE), / ɜːθ / (BrE)
(in ancient and medieval philosophy) one of the four substances once thought to compose the physical universe, associated with the qualities of coldness and dryness and with the humour black bile.

Examples

element earth
According to ancient philosophers, earth was considered one of the four fundamental elements.
Alchemy described earth as cold and dry, representing stability and materiality.
In medieval medicine, earth was associated with black bile and melancholy.
