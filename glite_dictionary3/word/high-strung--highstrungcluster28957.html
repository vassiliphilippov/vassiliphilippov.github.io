high-strung

adjective
very nervous or easily agitated
Pronunciation: / haɪˈstrʌŋ / (AmE), / haɪˈstrʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) very nervous, easily upset, or quick to become tense or agitated.

Examples

a high-strung person
He's a little too high-strung to enjoy working in such a chaotic environment.
Her high-strung nature made her sensitive to even slight criticism.
The high-strung horse shied away from every unexpected sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym – relaxed: calm and free from tension
  • synonym – tense: nervous or unable to relax
