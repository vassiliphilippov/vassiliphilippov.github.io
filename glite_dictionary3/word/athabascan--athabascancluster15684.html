Athabascan

noun
member of a North American indigenous group
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈbæskən / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈbæskən / (BrE)
a member of any of the indigenous peoples of North America who speak an Athapaskan language and traditionally live in the subarctic regions of western Canada and central Alaska

Examples

an Athabascan person
Many Athabascan people continue to practice traditional fishing methods in Alaska.
She learned about Athabascan culture during her travels through western Canada.
The Athabascan languages include a variety of dialects spoken across North America.
