to introject

verb
unconsciously absorb ideas or attitudes
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.tɹoʊˌdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.trəʊˌdʒɛkt / (BrE)
(in psychology) to unconsciously incorporate the attitudes or ideas of others into one's own personality or mental life.

Examples

to introject beliefs
Children often introject parental values without realizing it.
In therapy, he discovered he had introjected his father's critical voice.
Patients may unconsciously introject negative beliefs from significant others.
