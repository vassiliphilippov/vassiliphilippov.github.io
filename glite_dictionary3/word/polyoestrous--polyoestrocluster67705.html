polyoestrous

adjective
having multiple estrous periods each year
Pronunciation: / ˌpɒliˈɛstrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈiːstrəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) having more than one period of estrus in a single year.

Examples

polyoestrous animals breed
Domestic cats are polyoestrous, which is why they can have multiple litters annually.
Many rodents are polyoestrous, breeding several times throughout the year.
Unlike dogs, which are usually monoestrous, cows are polyoestrous and go into heat multiple times a year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monoestrous: having only one period of estrus per year
