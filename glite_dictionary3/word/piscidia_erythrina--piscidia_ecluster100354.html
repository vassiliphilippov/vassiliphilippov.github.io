Piscidia erythrina

noun
a tropical tree yielding fish poisons
Pronunciation: / pɪˈsɪdiə ɛrɪˈθraɪnə / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪdiə ɛrɪˈθrʌɪnə / (BrE)
a small tree native to the West Indies and Florida, having large pinnate leaves, panicles of red-striped purple to white flowers, and curly winged seedpods, known for yielding fish poisons
