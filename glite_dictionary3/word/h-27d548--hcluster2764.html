h

noun
Planck's constant in physics
Pronunciation: / eɪtʃ / (AmE), / eɪtʃ / (BrE)
the constant of proportionality relating the energy of a photon to its frequency, known as Planck's constant; approximately 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ joule-seconds

Examples

h in physics
Modern physics equations frequently include h as a fundamental constant.
Scientists use h to determine the energy of photons emitted by atoms.
The value of h is essential for calculations in quantum mechanics.
