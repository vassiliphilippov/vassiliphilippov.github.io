bareboating

noun
chartering a boat without crew or provisions
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrˌboʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌbəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of chartering a boat without crew or provisions, so that you supply your own crew and provisions.

Examples

bareboating in the Caribbean
Many people enjoy bareboating because it offers more freedom and adventure than a crewed charter.
They learned navigation basics before bareboating through the Greek islands.
We decided to go bareboating in the Caribbean for our summer vacation.
