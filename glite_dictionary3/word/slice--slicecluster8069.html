to slice

verb
hit a ball with spin to alter its path
Pronunciation: / slaɪs / (AmE), / slaɪs / (BrE)
to hit a ball in sports so that it spins and curves away from its expected path

Examples

to slice the ball
He managed to slice the ball just over the net during the tennis match.
If you slice the golf ball, it will veer sharply to the right.
She accidentally sliced the shot, sending it out of bounds.
He accidentally sliced his drive and the golf ball curved sharply to the right.
If you slice the tennis ball, it will drop quickly after crossing the net.
Jess tried to slice the shot to surprise her opponent with a sudden change in direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cut: to hit a ball so that it moves sharply and with spin, as in cricket or tennis
  • antonym – hook: to hit a ball so it curves in the opposite direction (especially in golf)
