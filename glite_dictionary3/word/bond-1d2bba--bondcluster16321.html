bond

noun
restraint or tie confining movement
Pronunciation: / bɑnd / (AmE), / bɒnd / (BrE)
a thing used to restrain someone, such as ropes, chains, or bands, especially to confine a prisoner or limit freedom

Examples

iron bonds
Historical accounts often describe escaped slaves breaking their bonds under the cover of darkness.
She felt emotionally trapped, as if invisible bonds kept her from moving forward.
The prisoner struggled against the bond that held his wrists tightly together.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bondage: the state of being held in captivity or restraint
  • antonym – freedom: the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants
  • synonym – shackle: a device used to restrain a prisoner's limbs
  • synonym – tie: a thing used to bind or fasten
