to disqualify

verb
declare someone ineligible or unfit
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to officially make someone ineligible or unfit for something, such as a competition, activity, or position, usually because of breaking rules or not meeting requirements

Examples

to disqualify a player
Breaking the rules could disqualify a contestant from the competition.
If you arrive late for the exam, they may disqualify you from taking it.
The referee had to disqualify the athlete after discovering the foul.
Any criminal conviction will automatically disqualify an applicant for the position.
If you lie on your application, it may disqualify you from the scholarship program.
The presence of peanuts in the sauce could disqualify it for people with allergies.
