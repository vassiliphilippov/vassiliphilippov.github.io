Akhbari

noun
member of a Shiite Muslim movement in 17th-century Iraq
Pronunciation: / ɑːkˈbɑːri / (AmE), / ɑːkˈbɑːri / (BrE)
a follower or member of a 17th-century Shiite Muslim movement in Iraq that upheld strict adherence to religious texts and tradition, in opposition to the Usuli school

Examples

an Akhbari scholar
During the 17th century, the Akhbari movement was influential among Shiite scholars in Iraq.
Modern Shiite jurisprudence distinguishes between Akhbari and Usuli doctrines.
The Akhbari school emphasizes strict adherence to traditions rather than independent reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Usuli: adherent of a rival Shiite school emphasizing reasoned interpretation of law
