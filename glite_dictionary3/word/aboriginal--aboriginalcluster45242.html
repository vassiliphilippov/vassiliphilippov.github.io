aboriginal

noun
an indigenous person of a place
Pronunciation: / ˌæb.əˈrɪdʒ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌab.əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
a member of the original inhabitants of a region, especially before colonization or settlement by another group

Examples

aboriginal people
Many aboriginals maintain unique languages and cultural traditions.
Museums now feature exhibitions highlighting the histories of local aboriginals.
The new policy aims to protect the rights of aboriginals in remote areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – native: a person born in a particular place or associated with a place by birth
  • antonym – settler: a person who moves to a new region and establishes a permanent residence, often contrasting with indigenous peoples
