to riffle

verb
to flip through pages quickly
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
to turn over pages quickly or to move paper or thin material with a light, rapid motion

Examples

to riffle the pages
Before handing over the book, she riffling quickly to check for any notes inside.
He absentmindedly riffled the pages of his notebook during the meeting.
She riffled through the stack of magazines, searching for a particular article.
A gentle breeze riffling the curtains caught her attention.
His fingers riffled rapidly through the stack of cards.
She watched as the wind riffled the pages of her notebook.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flick: to move or make something move with a sudden quick movement
  • synonym – flutter: to move with quick, light movements, especially repeatedly up and down or from side to side
  • synonym – leaf through: to turn pages of a book or magazine quickly
  • synonym – thumb through: to look through pages of something quickly
