background

noun
less noticeable context or setting
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the part of a situation or scene that is less noticeable or important, serving as a setting or context for something else

Examples

stay in the background
She prefers to stay in the background at social events rather than draw attention.
The city skyline provided a beautiful background for their wedding photos.
The music played softly in the background while the guests chatted.

Related Terms

  • antonym – foreground: the most prominent or important part of a scene or situation
