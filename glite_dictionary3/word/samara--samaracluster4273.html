samara

noun
winged dry fruit, e.g. of maple
Pronunciation: / səˈmɛrə / (AmE), / səˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
a dry, winged fruit that does not split open when mature, commonly produced by trees such as ash, elm, or maple

Examples

samara from a maple
Each samara usually contains a single seed encased within its papery, wing-like structure.
In biology class, we examined a samara to learn how seeds disperse in the wind.
The samara of the maple tree spins as it falls, carrying the seed farther from the parent tree.
