allochronic

adjective
existing at different geological times
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈkrɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkrɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of taxa) existing or occurring at different geological times.

Examples

allochronic species separation
Although their anatomy is similar, the birds are allochronic in origin, separated by millions of years.
Fossil records help paleontologists determine whether similar organisms are allochronic or contemporaneous.
The two species are considered allochronic because they lived during different geological periods.

Related Terms

  • antonym – synchronous: existing or occurring at the same time
