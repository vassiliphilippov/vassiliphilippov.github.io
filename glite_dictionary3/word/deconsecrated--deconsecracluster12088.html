deconsecrated

adjective
no longer consecrated or sacred
Pronunciation: / diːˈkɑːnsɪˌkreɪtɪd / (AmE), / diːˈkɒnsɪˌkreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a building or object) having had its consecration formally removed, making it no longer sacred.

Examples

deconsecrated church building
After being deconsecrated, the cemetery grounds could be developed for other uses.
The deconsecrated church was converted into a community arts center.
They held a concert in the once-sacred but now deconsecrated chapel.

Related Terms

  • antonym – consecrated: having been made or declared sacred
