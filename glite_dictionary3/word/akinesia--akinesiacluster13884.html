akinesia

noun
loss of voluntary movement
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪkɪˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˌeɪkɪˈniːziə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the absence or loss of voluntary movement, typically resulting from temporary paralysis.

Examples

akinesia in Parkinson's
Some forms of Parkinson's disease lead to episodes of akinesia, where patients freeze in place.
The patient displayed akinesia after the stroke, unable to move his right arm.
Treatment was started to address the severe akinesia noticed during the neurological exam.

Related Terms

  • synonym – paralysis: loss of the ability to move part or most of the body
