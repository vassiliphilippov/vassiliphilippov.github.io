hell

noun
the world of the dead (religious concept)
Pronunciation: / hɛl / (AmE), / hɛl / (BrE)
(in various religions) a place where the souls of the dead are believed to go, often as a realm of punishment or the underworld.

Examples

In some beliefs, hell is divided into different regions for different kinds of souls.
Many ancient cultures imagined hell as a place beneath the earth where souls are judged.
