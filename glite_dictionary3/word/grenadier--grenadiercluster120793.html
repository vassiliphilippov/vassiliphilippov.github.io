grenadier

noun
deep-sea fish with a long tail and large head
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛnəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a deep-sea fish with a large head, elongated body, and long tapering tail, found in deep ocean waters

Examples

deep-sea grenadier fish
A biologist studied the peculiar features of the newly discovered species of grenadier.
Some fishermen specialize in catching the elusive grenadier during deep-sea expeditions.
The grenadier is commonly found at depths of over one thousand meters in the Atlantic Ocean.
