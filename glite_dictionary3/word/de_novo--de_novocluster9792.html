de novo

adjective
spontaneous; not inherited (genetics)
Pronunciation: / də ˈnoʊvoʊ / (AmE), / deɪ ˈnəʊvəʊ / (BrE)
(in genetics and medical contexts) of a mutation or genetic variant: arising newly in an individual and not inherited from either parent.

Examples

de novo mutation
The diagnosis identified a de novo mutation in the gene, absent from both parents' DNA.
Researchers reported several de novo variants that likely explain the child's developmental delay.
Whole-genome sequencing can reveal previously undetected de novo changes contributing to rare diseases.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inherited: transmitted genetically from parents to offspring; opposite of a de novo mutation
  • synonym – novel: new or not previously observed; often used of mutations or variants
