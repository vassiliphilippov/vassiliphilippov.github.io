retroflex

adjective
pronounced with the tongue tip turned back
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtrəˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəˌflɛks / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) pronounced with the tip of the tongue curled back toward the hard palate.

Examples

retroflex consonant sound
In Hindi, the letter 'ट' represents a retroflex sound distinct from the English 't'.
Some Indian and Australian Aboriginal languages use many retroflex consonants in their phonetic inventory.
The teacher demonstrated how to produce a retroflex stop by curling the tongue back.
