offside

noun
rule infringement; illegal position (sport)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔf.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒf.saɪd / (BrE)
(in sports) a rule infringement in which a player occupies an illegal position on the field, especially in football (soccer) or hockey.

Examples

called for offside foul
Controversy erupted when the referee failed to notice an apparent offside during the match.
Players must always be aware of the offside rule to avoid penalties.
The striker was called for offside just before scoring the goal.
