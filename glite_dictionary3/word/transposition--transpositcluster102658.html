transposition

noun
changing a musical key
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(music) the action of changing the key of a piece of music by raising or lowering its pitch.

Examples

transposition of keys
Digital pianos often have a transposition function to quickly change the pitch.
He performed the piece in a different key using transposition.
The sheet music required transposition to fit the vocal range of the singer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – modulation: the process of changing from one key to another in music
