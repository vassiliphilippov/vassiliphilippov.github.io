XY

noun
male chromosomal complement (genetics)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛks ˈwaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛks ˈwaɪ / (BrE)
the normal set of sex chromosomes found in males, consisting of one X and one Y chromosome

Examples

XY chromosomes in males
Abnormalities in the XY chromosomes can lead to genetic conditions.
Human males typically have an XY chromosomal pattern.
The XY genotype determines male biological sex in most mammals.
