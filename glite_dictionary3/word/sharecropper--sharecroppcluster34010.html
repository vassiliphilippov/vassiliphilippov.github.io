sharecropper

noun
tenant farmer paying rent as crop share
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛrˌkrɑpər / (AmE), / ˈʃeəˌkrɒpə / (BrE)
a farmer, especially in the southern United States, who raises crops on land owned by someone else and gives a portion of the crop as rent.

Examples

sharecropper on a farm
After the Civil War, many families became sharecroppers on southern plantations.
As a sharecropper, he had to give half his harvest to the landowner.
The life of a sharecropper was often marked by hard labor and poverty.

Related Terms

  • antonym – landowner: a person who owns land, especially one who leases it to others
  • synonym – tenant farmer: a person who farms rented land
