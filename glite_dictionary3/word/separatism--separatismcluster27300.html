separatism

noun
doctrine of complete separation (e.g. church and state)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpɚəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərətɪzəm / (BrE)
The advocacy or practice of keeping two groups, institutions, or principles—such as church and state—strictly separate from each other.

Examples

religious separatism
Debates about separatism frequently arise whenever religious influence is seen in public education policies.
Many supporters of separatism argue for a strict division between religious organizations and government.
The country's constitution is based on the principle of separatism between church and state.

Related Terms

  • synonym – separationism: the belief in keeping things separate, especially church and state
