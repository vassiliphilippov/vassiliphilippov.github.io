to puff

verb
breathe noisily after exertion
Pronunciation: / pʌf / (AmE), / pʌf / (BrE)
to blow or breathe out air forcefully or noisily, especially after exertion

Examples

to puff after running
After just a few minutes of running, he was already puffing heavily.
She puffed up the hill, struggling to catch her breath.
They paused at the top, puffing and laughing at how out of shape they felt.
He puffed loudly as he tried to catch his breath after the run.
She puffs at the candles, hoping to blow all of them out at once.
The child puffed at the dandelion to send its seeds flying through the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gasp: to catch one's breath with an open mouth, typically from exertion, shock, or pain
  • synonym – huff: to blow out loudly, especially in anger or exertion
  • synonym – pant: to breathe quickly and heavily, typically after exertion
