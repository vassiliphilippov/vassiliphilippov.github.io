to interiorise

verb
incorporate within oneself; make internal or personal
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɪriəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriəraɪz / (BrE)
to incorporate something within oneself, making it an internal or personal part of one’s beliefs, thoughts, or identity

Examples

to interiorise new values
As children grow, they often interiorise the values taught by their parents.
She gradually interiorised the principles of the new culture after living abroad for several years.
Therapists help clients interiorise positive self-beliefs to support mental health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – internalize: to make something a part of one’s inner self or beliefs
