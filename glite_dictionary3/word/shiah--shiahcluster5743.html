Shiah

noun
member of the Shi'a branch of Islam
Pronunciation: / ʃiˈɑ / (AmE), / ʃiˈɑː / (BrE)
A member of the branch of Islam that regards Ali and his descendants as the legitimate successors to Muhammad, chiefly found in Iran and parts of the Middle East.

Examples

devout Shiah Muslim
As a Shiah, he observes different religious practices than many of his Sunni friends.
Most Iranians identify as Shiah Muslims, following the teachings of Ali.
The division between Shiah and Sunni Muslims dates back to the early days of Islam.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Shiite: alternative spelling and more common form for 'Shiah', referring to a member of the same branch of Islam
  • antonym – Sunni: a member of the largest branch of Islam, differing from Shiah in certain religious beliefs and practices
