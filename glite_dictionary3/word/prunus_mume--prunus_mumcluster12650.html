Prunus mume

noun
East Asian tree with fragrant blossoms and small fruits
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːnəs ˈmuːmi / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˈmjuːmiː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to East Asia, cultivated for its fragrant white or pink blossoms and small, yellow, plum-like fruits; commonly known as the Japanese apricot or ume.

Examples

Prunus mume blossoms in spring
The Prunus mume trees bloom early in spring, adding delicate pink and white flowers to the garden.
The fruit of the Prunus mume is commonly used to make umeboshi, a type of pickled plum.
Traditional Japanese art often features the blossoms of Prunus mume as a symbol of perseverance and renewal.
