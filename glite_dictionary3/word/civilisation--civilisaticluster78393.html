civilisation

noun
process of advanced social development
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɪv.ə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɪv.ɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of a society becoming advanced in culture, organization, and manners

Examples

modern civilisation
Education is often considered a fundamental part of societal civilisation.
Some historians argue that trade was crucial to the civilisation of early societies.
The rise of Athens marked a new phase in the civilisation of ancient Greece.
Good conversation and generosity are the true marks of civilisation.
His manners and taste are the very model of civilisation.
She aspired to a level of civilisation in both thought and behavior.
