uncertain

adjective
not consistent or dependable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɝːtən / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːtən /; / ʌnˈsɜːtn̩ /; / ʌnˈsɜːtn / (BrE)
not definite, reliable, or predictable; subject to change or not confirmed

Examples

uncertain future
He has an uncertain memory of what happened that night.
His future at the company remains uncertain after the recent changes.
The weather is always uncertain in early spring, making planning difficult.
Due to uncertain economic conditions, many businesses are reluctant to invest.
Her future after graduation remains uncertain despite her many achievements.
The weather in spring can be quite uncertain and difficult to predict.
Her test results were uncertain, so further examination was required.
It is uncertain whether the game will be played because of the weather.
The future of the project remains uncertain due to financial constraints.
