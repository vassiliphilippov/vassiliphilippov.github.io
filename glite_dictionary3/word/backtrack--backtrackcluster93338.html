to backtrack

verb
go back along a previous route
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈbæk.træk / (BrE)
to go back along the same route that one has just taken; to retrace one's steps or course

Examples

to backtrack along the path
After realizing her mistake, she backtracked to the previous intersection to try a different route.
The lost dog backtracked to the spot where it had last seen its owner.
When they reached a dead end, the group had to backtrack several miles to find the correct path.

  • synonym – retrace: to go back over a route again
