Luffa acutangula

noun
tropical climber yielding edible fruit and loofahs
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊfə ˌɑːkuːˈtænɡjʊlə / (AmE), / ˈlʌfə ˌækjuːˈtæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to Pakistan and widely cultivated in tropical regions for its edible fruit and fibrous mature gourds, used as natural sponges.

Examples

Luffa acutangula plant fruit
Botanists often distinguish Luffa acutangula from similar species by its ridged, angled fruit.
Farmers in Southeast Asia grow Luffa acutangula for both its edible young fruits and its fibrous gourds.
The mature fruit of the Luffa acutangula is dried and used as a natural scrubbing sponge.
