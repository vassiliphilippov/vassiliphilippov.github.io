margin

noun
collateral deposited with a broker
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
the amount of money or collateral a customer must deposit with a broker when borrowing funds to buy securities

Examples

margin account funds
Because the price of the stocks dropped, his margin account was at risk of a call.
Buying on margin can lead to higher gains, but it also increases potential losses.
The investor was required to deposit a margin to purchase shares on credit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collateral: property or assets pledged as security for a loan
  • synonym – leverage: use of borrowed money to increase potential return on investment
