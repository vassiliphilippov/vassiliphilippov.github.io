sacrifice

noun
baseball out that advances runners
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs / (BrE)
In baseball, an out made by a batter to advance one or more base runners, especially through a bunt or fly ball.

Examples

baseball sacrifice bunt
After the sacrifice, both runners were in scoring position.
He laid down a perfect sacrifice to help his team score.
The coach signaled for a sacrifice to move the runner to third base.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sacrifice bunt: a bunt in baseball intended to advance runners at the expense of being put out
  • synonym – sacrifice fly: a fly ball in baseball resulting in a run scored after a catch
