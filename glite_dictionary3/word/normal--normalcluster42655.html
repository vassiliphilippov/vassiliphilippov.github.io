normal

adjective
at right angles; perpendicular
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔr.məl / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.məl / (BrE)
(of a line, plane, or surface) forming a right angle to a given line, plane, or surface; perpendicular.

Examples

normal to a surface
In geometry, a line drawn normal to a surface is perpendicular to that surface at the point of contact.
The force acts normal to the wall, minimizing any tangential effect.
The technician aligned the laser beam normal to the glass plate for maximum transmission.

Related Terms

  • antonym – oblique: not forming a right angle; slanting
  • synonym – perpendicular: at an angle of 90° to a given line, plane, or surface
