nucleolus

noun
cell nucleus structure involved in ribosome production
Pronunciation: / nuˈkli oʊ ləs / (AmE), / njuːˈkliː əl əs / (BrE)
A small, dense, round structure in the nucleus of a cell, containing RNA and involved in the production of ribosomes.

Examples

nucleolus inside nucleus
Abnormalities in the nucleolus can be indicative of certain diseases.
The nucleolus is essential for the assembly of ribosomes within the cell.
Under the microscope, the nucleolus appears as a dark, round spot inside the nucleus.
