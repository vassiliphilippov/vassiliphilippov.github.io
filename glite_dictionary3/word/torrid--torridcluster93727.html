torrid

adjective
extremely hot and dry
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔrɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɒrɪd / (BrE)
Extremely hot and dry, often to an uncomfortable degree.

Examples

torrid heat
The athletes suffered under the torrid afternoon sun during the marathon.
They struggled through the torrid desert, desperate for water and shade.
With each torrid summer, the drought in the region grew more severe.

  • antonym – cool: moderately cold; the opposite of hot
  • synonym – scorching: extremely hot, like burning
  • synonym – sweltering: oppressively hot; uncomfortably hot
