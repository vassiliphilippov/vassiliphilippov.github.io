bound

noun
boundary line or limit
Pronunciation: / baʊnd / (AmE), / baʊnd / (BrE)
a line, limit, or boundary that marks the edge or extent of something (physical or abstract)

Examples

bound of the property
His land lies within the bounds of the village.
The river forms the eastern bound of the property.
They crossed the bounds of the national park without realizing it.
Freedom of speech has its bounds, even in a free society.
His actions were clearly outside the bounds of professional conduct.
The team pushed their skills to the bounds of human endurance.
Her curiosity knew no bound when it came to exploring new ideas.
The property’s bound is marked by a row of tall hedges.
We must respect the bound between public and private land.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boundary: the edge or limit that separates two things or areas
  • synonym – extent: the degree to which something has spread; the range covered
  • antonym – infinite: without limits or end
  • synonym – limit: the point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass
