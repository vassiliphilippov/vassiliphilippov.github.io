to fluff

verb
bungle; make a mess of
Pronunciation: / flʌf / (AmE), / flʌf / (BrE)
to make a mess of or bungle something, especially by making a careless mistake

Examples

to fluff an answer
He completely fluffed his lines during the performance and had to start over.
If you fluff the interview, you might not get the job.
She fluffed her speech by forgetting the main points halfway through.

Related Terms

  • synonym – botch: to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • synonym – mishandle: to manage or deal with something incorrectly or ineffectively
  • antonym – succeed: to accomplish something successfully
