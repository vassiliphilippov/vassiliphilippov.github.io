to assign

verb
attribute or credit something
Pronunciation: / əˈsaɪn / (AmE), / əˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to attribute, credit, or classify something as belonging to a person, cause, category, or source

Examples

to assign a cause
Many historians assign this painting to the early works of Picasso.
Some people assign too much importance to luck in achieving success.
The critics assign the novel's popularity to its relatable characters.
Many critics assign symbolic meaning to the colors used in the painting.
People often assign blame without knowing all the facts.
The teacher assigned different roles to each student in the play.
After much debate, the curator assigned the artifact to the Neolithic period.
She assigned the document to the 'confidential' file in the company records.
The committee will assign each project to a relevant category based on its scope.
