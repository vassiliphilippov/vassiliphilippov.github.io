Haftorah

noun
scriptural reading from the Prophets in Jewish service
Pronunciation: / hɑfˈtoʊrə / (AmE), / hɑːfˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
A short reading from the Prophets, recited in Jewish synagogues on the Sabbath and festivals after the Torah reading.

Examples

Haftorah reading in synagogue
Each week, a different Haftorah is chosen to complement the Torah portion just read.
Sara was honored with reading the Haftorah at her bat mitzvah.
The rabbi explained the significance of the Haftorah during the Sabbath service.
