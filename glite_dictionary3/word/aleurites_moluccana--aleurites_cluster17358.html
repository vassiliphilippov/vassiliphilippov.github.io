Aleurites moluccana

noun
tree species yielding candlenuts and oil
Pronunciation: / əˌlurɪˌtiz məˌlʌˈkænə / (AmE), / əˌljʊərɪˌtiːz məˌlʌˈkænə / (BrE)
a large tree native to southeastern Asia whose nuts provide oil used in varnishes and whose kernels are strung together locally to make candles; also known as the candlenut tree

Examples

Aleurites moluccana tree oil
Known as the candlenut tree, Aleurites moluccana is cultivated for both its ornamental value and practical uses.
The oil extracted from Aleurites moluccana nuts is widely used in making varnishes.
Traditional Hawaiian leis sometimes include the seeds of Aleurites moluccana as decoration.
