fledged

adjective
having developed feathers (birds)
Pronunciation: / flɛdʒd / (AmE), / flɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a bird) having grown the feathers necessary for flight; often used after 'fully' or in combination with another word.

Examples

fully fledged bird
A newly fledged robin perched on the garden fence.
By spring, the sparrows were fully fledged and ready to fly.
The fledged chicks left the nest for the first time yesterday.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unfledged: (of a bird) not yet having developed feathers
