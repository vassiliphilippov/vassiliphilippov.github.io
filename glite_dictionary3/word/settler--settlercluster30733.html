settler

noun
person who moves to a new territory to live
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛtlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtlə / (BrE)
a person who moves to a new country, colony, or area and establishes residence there, often as part of a group colonizing the region

Examples

early settler
As a settler, she faced many hardships adapting to the new land.
Many settler communities still celebrate the founding of their towns.
The first settlers arrived in the valley in the early 1800s.

Related Terms

  • synonym – colonist: a person who settles in a new colony
