Chaldaean

noun
wise man in occult learning (Babylonian)
Pronunciation: / kælˈdiən / (AmE), / kalˈdiːən / (BrE)
A wise man skilled in occult learning, especially one from ancient Babylonia.

Examples

ancient Chaldaean astrologer
In ancient times, a Chaldaean was revered for his knowledge of the stars and mystical arts.
The king consulted a Chaldaean to interpret the strange dreams that troubled his sleep.
The wise Chaldaean studied sacred texts by candlelight, seeking hidden truths.

Related Terms

  • synonym – magi: a member of a priestly caste skilled in astrology and magic, especially in ancient Persia and Babylonia
