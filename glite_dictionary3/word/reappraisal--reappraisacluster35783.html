reappraisal

noun
psychological emotion-regulation strategy
Pronunciation: /  ri preaz l / (AmE), /  ri preaz l / (BrE)
(in psychology) a cognitive emotion-regulation strategy in which a person changes their interpretation of a situation or stimulus in order to alter its emotional impact.

Examples

emotional reappraisal technique
Therapists teach clients reappraisal to reinterpret stressful events and reduce negative emotions during sessions.
The study measured participants' use of reappraisal when viewing upsetting images in the laboratory.
After practice, her habitual reappraisals of criticism led to lower anxiety and better sleep.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cognitive reappraisal: a psychological strategy of changing how one thinks about a situation to change emotional responses
  • antonym – suppression: in emotion regulation, inhibiting emotional expression rather than changing how one interprets the situation
