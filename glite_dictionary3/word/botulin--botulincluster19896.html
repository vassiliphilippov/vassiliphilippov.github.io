botulin

noun
toxin causing botulism
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːtʃəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒtʃʊlɪn / (BrE)
a highly potent toxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum, which causes botulism and can be used as a bioweapon

Examples

botulin toxin danger
Botulin is one of the most potent toxins known to science.
Improperly canned foods can sometimes contain deadly botulin.
Researchers studied the effects of botulin exposure in laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – botulinum toxin: the neurotoxin produced by Clostridium botulinum, commonly used in medicine and cosmetics
  • degree higher – botulism: a severe illness caused by botulin poisoning
