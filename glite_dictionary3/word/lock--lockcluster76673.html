to lock

verb
build canal locks for navigation
Pronunciation: / lɑk / (AmE), / lɒk / (BrE)
to construct locks (canal enclosures) to enable the passage of boats and ships through differing water levels

Examples

to lock a river
Engineers had to lock the river to allow ships to reach the inland port.
In the 19th century, many rivers were locked to improve trade routes.
They plan to lock the canal, making it easier for barges to travel between towns.

Related Terms

  • synonym – canalize: to convert into or provide with a canal or channel
