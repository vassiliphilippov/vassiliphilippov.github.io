middlewoman

noun
female lead in a minstrel line
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female performer in the middle of a minstrel line who acts as a principal speaker, engaging with the other performers in dialogue

Examples

middlewoman leading dialogue
She was chosen to be the middlewoman because of her quick thinking and strong stage presence.
The middlewoman kept the audience entertained with her witty remarks during the performance.
Traditionally, the middlewoman would initiate most of the banter on stage.
