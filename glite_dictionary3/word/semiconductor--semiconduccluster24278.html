semiconductor

noun
substance with conductivity between conductor and insulator
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛm.i.kənˈdʌk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɛm.i.kənˈdʌk.tə /; / ˌsɛm.i.kənˈdʌkt.ə / (BrE)
a material (such as silicon) whose ability to conduct electricity is between that of a conductor and an insulator, used in electronic devices
