autoicous

adjective
having separate male and female reproductive clusters on one plant
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈtɔɪkəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɔɪkəs / (BrE)
(of a plant, especially a moss or liverwort) having male and female reproductive organs in separate clusters on the same individual.

Examples

autoicous moss species
Researchers noted that autoicous species exhibited both archegonia and antheridia on a single plant.
Some mosses are autoicous, producing male and female organs in separate clusters on the same stem.
The autoicous condition increases the likelihood of self-fertilization in liverworts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dioicous: having male and female reproductive organs on separate plants
  • degree lower – monoicous: having male and female organs in the same cluster on the same plant (esp. mosses)
