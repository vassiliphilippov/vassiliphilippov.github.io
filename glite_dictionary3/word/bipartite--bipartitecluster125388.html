bipartite

adjective
divided into two parts nearly to the base
Pronunciation: / baɪˈpɑːrtaɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈpɑːtaɪt / (BrE)
(especially of leaves or anatomical structures) divided into two distinct parts or sections, almost to the base.

Examples

bipartite leaf structure
Many fungi have bipartite fruiting bodies that are easily recognized.
The insect’s bipartite antennae help distinguish it from similar species.
The leaf is bipartite, with each segment extending almost to the midrib.
