ripe

adjective
advanced in age
Pronunciation: / raɪp / (AmE), / raɪp / (BrE)
(of a person's age) advanced; having reached a notably old age.

Examples

ripe old age
At a ripe old age, he remained active and curious about the world.
He retired at the ripe age of eighty-five, surrounded by his grandchildren.
She lived to the ripe age of 98, still enjoying every day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aged: having lived for a specified number of years; advanced in years
  • antonym – young: having lived or existed for only a short time; not old
