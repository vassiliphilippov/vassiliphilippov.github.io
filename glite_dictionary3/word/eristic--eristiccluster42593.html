eristic

noun
a person who enjoys argument or controversy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
a person who argues or disputes, often for the sake of argument rather than reaching the truth

Examples

an eristic debater
It is difficult to reach consensus when an eristic dominates the discussion.
Some television hosts act as eristics, provoking controversies for higher ratings.
The philosopher was an eristic who preferred endless debate to clear answers.
