squint

noun
abnormal alignment of the eyes
Pronunciation: / skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
A condition in which the eyes are not properly aligned with each other, resulting in a deviation from parallel gaze.

Examples

a squint condition
Her left eye had a noticeable squint from birth.
Regular eye exams can help detect a squint at an early stage.
The child was diagnosed with a squint that required corrective surgery.
