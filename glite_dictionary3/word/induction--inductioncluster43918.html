induction

noun
production of electric current by magnetic field
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the production of an electric current or electromotive force in a circuit by a changing magnetic field or by a varying current in a neighboring circuit

Examples

electromagnetic induction
Faraday's law explains how induction produces electricity in a coil of wire.
The transformer relies on induction to transfer energy between its windings.
When the magnetic field changed, induction created a current in the secondary circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electromagnetic induction: production of electricity by a varying magnetic field
