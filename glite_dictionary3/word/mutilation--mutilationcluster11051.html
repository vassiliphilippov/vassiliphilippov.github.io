mutilation

noun
severe injury causing disfigurement or loss of a body part
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of severely injuring or damaging a body, especially by removing a limb or other important part, often causing disfigurement.

Examples

mutilation of body
He was left with permanent mutilation as a result of the accident.
Legislation was passed to prevent the ritual mutilation of children.
The victim suffered extensive mutilation during the attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amputation: the removal of a limb, typically by surgery
  • synonym – maiming: the act of wounding or injuring seriously, especially by depriving of the use of a limb
