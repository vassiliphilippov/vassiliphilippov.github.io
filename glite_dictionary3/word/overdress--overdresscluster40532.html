to overdress

verb
wear excessively formal or elaborate clothes
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to put on clothes that are too formal, elaborate, or showy for a particular occasion

Examples

to overdress for parties
He tends to overdress for casual gatherings, making everyone else feel underdressed.
She realized she had overdressed when she saw everyone else in jeans and T-shirts.
Try not to overdress for the interview; business casual is appropriate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dress up: to put on formal or smart clothes
  • antonym – underdress: to wear clothes that are too casual or not formal enough for an occasion
