Assumption

noun
Virgin Mary's ascent into heaven (Catholic feast)
Pronunciation: / əˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / əˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the celebration of the Virgin Mary being taken up into heaven at the end of her earthly life.

Examples

Assumption feast day
Catholics around the world celebrate the Assumption on August 15 each year.
Many believers attended mass to commemorate the Assumption of the Virgin Mary.
The church was decorated with flowers in honor of the Assumption.

  • synonym – Dormition: the Eastern Orthodox celebration of Mary's death and assumption into heaven
