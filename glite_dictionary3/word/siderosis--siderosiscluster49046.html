siderosis

noun
lung disease from inhaled iron dust
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪdəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪdəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A lung disease caused by the inhalation of iron dust, leading to fibrosis, especially among welders and other metal workers.

Examples

siderosis in welders
Long-term exposure to iron dust in the factory led to a diagnosis of siderosis.
The patient's chest X-ray showed signs consistent with siderosis.
Welders are at higher risk of developing siderosis due to airborne particles.
