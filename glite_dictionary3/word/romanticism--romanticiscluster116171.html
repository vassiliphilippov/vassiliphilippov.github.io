Romanticism

noun
literary and artistic movement (late 18th–early 19th century)
Pronunciation: / roʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A movement in literature and art during the late 18th and early 19th centuries that emphasized inspiration, subjectivity, and the primacy of nature over civilization.

Examples

Romanticism in literature
Romanticism influenced painters to depict dramatic landscapes and powerful emotions.
Many scholars study Romanticism to understand how art responded to changes in society and science.
The poetry of Wordsworth and Coleridge is often cited as the foundation of Romanticism in England.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Classicism: Artistic and literary movement emphasizing order, harmony, and reason, in contrast to Romanticism
  • degree lower – Naturalism: Literary and artistic movement seeking to depict reality as it is, often differing from Romanticism's idealism
