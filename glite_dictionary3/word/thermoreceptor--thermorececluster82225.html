thermoreceptor

noun
temperature-sensing nerve receptor
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜr.moʊ.rɪˈsɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məʊ.rɪˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
a sensory receptor that detects changes in temperature, responding to heat or cold

Examples

skin thermoreceptor
Damage to thermoreceptors can impair a person's ability to sense environmental temperature changes.
Our sense of touch relies partly on thermoreceptors to distinguish hot from cold objects.
Some animals have highly sensitive thermoreceptors in their skin to detect subtle temperature changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nociceptor: a sensory receptor for painful stimuli
