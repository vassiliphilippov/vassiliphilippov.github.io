to dangle

verb
hang or swing loosely
Pronunciation: / ˈdæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to hang or swing loosely, especially so as to be able to move freely

Examples

dangle from a branch
A single earring dangled from her left ear, catching the light as she turned.
Colorful paper lanterns dangled from the ceiling, casting a festive glow on the room.
She let her feet dangle over the edge of the dock while she watched the sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hang: be suspended or hold something so as to allow free movement
