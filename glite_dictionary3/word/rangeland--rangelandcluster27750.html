rangeland

noun
land for grazing livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪndʒˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒˌlænd / (BrE)
land covered with natural vegetation and suitable for grazing livestock, such as cattle or sheep.

Examples

rangeland for cattle
Fire management is a key concern for landowners who depend on healthy rangeland for their livestock.
Much of the western United States is covered by rangeland used for grazing cattle.
The government implemented new policies to protect native plants on public rangeland.
