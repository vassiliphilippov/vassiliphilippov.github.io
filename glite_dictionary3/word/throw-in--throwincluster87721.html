throw-in

noun
a method of putting the ball back into play in sports
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
an act of throwing the ball back into play, especially in rugby or other sports after it has gone out of bounds

Examples

a throw-in in football
His powerful throw-in helped his team gain a significant advantage.
Players quickly lined up for the throw-in to restart the game.
The referee signaled for a throw-in after the ball went out of bounds.
