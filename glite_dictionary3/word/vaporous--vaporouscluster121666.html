vaporous

adjective
filled with vapor; misty
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpɚəs /; / ˈveɪpərəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəs / (BrE)
filled with or resembling vapor; misty, hazy, or insubstantial (can also mean vague or dreamlike)

Examples

vaporous morning air
Early morning sunlight filtered through the vaporous air above the river.
The swamp had a vaporous atmosphere that clung to everything.
The valley remained vaporous after the heavy rain, shrouding the trees in mist.
His vision of the future remained vaporous and ill-defined, like a dream fading at dawn.
She wore a vaporous scarf that floated elegantly around her shoulders.
The mountaintops were hidden by vaporous clouds drifting across the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foggy: filled or abounding with fog
  • synonym – misty: full of or covered with mist
  • antonym – solid: firm and stable in shape; not liquid or gas
