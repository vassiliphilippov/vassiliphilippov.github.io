imperishableness

noun
quality of being unable to decay
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛr.ɪ.ʃə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛr.ɪ.ʃə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being able to decay or be destroyed

Examples

imperishableness of materials

Many ancient myths speak of the imperishableness of the gods.
