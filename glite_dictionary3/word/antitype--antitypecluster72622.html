antitype

noun
opposite or contrasting type
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ti.taɪp / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.taɪp / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the opposite or direct contrast of another type

Examples

antitype of kindness
Her cheerful demeanor was the antitype of his constant gloom.
Some literary characters are written as the antitype of the protagonist, serving as their foil.
The architect designed the new building as the antitype of the historic one across the street.
