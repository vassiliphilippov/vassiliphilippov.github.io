impression

noun
set of copies printed at one time
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
all the copies of a book, newspaper, etc., printed at one time from the same setting of type.

Examples

impression of book
Each impression of the catalogue had a unique cover design.
Only a limited impression of this rare edition was produced in 1923.
The publisher released a second impression after the first run sold out quickly.
