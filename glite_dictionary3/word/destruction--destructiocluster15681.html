destruction

noun
the act of destroying something completely
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Examples

destruction of buildings
The earthquake caused the destruction of several historic buildings in the city.
The fire resulted in the complete destruction of the warehouse.
Years of war have led to the widespread destruction of the countryside.
The wildfire caused the complete destruction of several homes and thousands of acres of forest.
They were shocked by the destruction of their neighborhood after the hurricane passed.
War often leads to the destruction of historic buildings and cultural monuments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – creation: the act of bringing something into existence (opposite of destruction)
  • synonym – devastation: great destruction or damage
  • synonym – ruin: severe damage or destruction of something
