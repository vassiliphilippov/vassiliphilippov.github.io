maypole

noun
decorated pole for May Day dances
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a tall pole decorated with flowers and ribbons, around which people traditionally dance as part of celebrations on May Day

Examples

dance around the maypole
Children danced around the maypole, weaving colorful ribbons together during the festival.
Every year, the village erects a maypole in the town square for the May Day celebration.
The tradition of dancing around the maypole dates back hundreds of years in Europe.
