to order

verb
give a command or directive
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ /; / ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to give a command or instruction; to direct someone to do something

Examples

to order someone
He orders his staff around as if he's a general.
She ordered the soldiers to stand at attention.
The manager ordered his team to finish the report by noon.
He was ordering everyone around as if he were the boss.
She ordered her assistant to prepare the documents by noon.
The sergeant ordered the soldiers to line up in formation.
It is the government's job to order public activities to ensure safety and fairness.
Parents often try to order their children's behavior with clear guidelines and rules.
The council decided to order the closure of bars after midnight to maintain peace.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ask: to request someone to do something (without authority)
  • synonym – command: to give an authoritative order
  • synonym – control: to exercise restraint or direction over something
  • antonym – disorder: to disrupt the order or arrangement; remove regulation
  • synonym – instruct: to give directions or orders
  • synonym – regulate: to control or supervise by means of rules and regulations
