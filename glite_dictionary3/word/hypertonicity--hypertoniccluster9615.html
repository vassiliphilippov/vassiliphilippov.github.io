hypertonicity

noun
higher osmotic pressure (biology/medicine)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pɚ.təˈnɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pə.təˈnɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The condition of having a higher osmotic pressure than another solution, often used in medicine and biology.

Examples

solution hypertonicity
Dehydration can result from the hypertonicity of extracellular fluid, causing water to move out of cells.
Medical students must understand the effects of hypertonicity on red blood cells during osmosis experiments.
The laboratory compared the hypertonicity of several saline solutions under controlled conditions.

Related Terms
