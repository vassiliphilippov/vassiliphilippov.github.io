heathen

adjective
not acknowledging Abrahamic religions
Pronunciation: / ˈhiðən / (AmE), / ˈhiːðən / (BrE)
(of a person or people) not acknowledging or believing in the God of Christianity, Judaism, or Islam; pagan.

Examples

heathen customs
In medieval Europe, those who did not follow Christianity were often called heathen.
The missionaries sought to convert the heathen populations in foreign lands.
The tribe practiced rituals that were considered heathen by neighboring Christian communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pagan: not belonging to any major world religion, especially Christianity, Judaism, or Islam
