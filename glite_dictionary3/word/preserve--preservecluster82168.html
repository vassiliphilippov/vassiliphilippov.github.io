to preserve

verb
keep for private hunting, fishing, etc.
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
to keep (an area or resource) undisturbed for personal or private use, especially for hunting, shooting, or fishing

Examples

to preserve a lake
Local landowners have preserved their lake to ensure exclusive fishing rights.
The wealthy family preserves the entire woodland for their annual hunting trips.
They hope to preserve the marsh, allowing only a select group access during duck season.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conserve: to protect (something, especially the environment) from harm or destruction
