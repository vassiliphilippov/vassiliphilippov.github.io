tariff

noun
government tax on imports or exports
Pronunciation: / ˈter.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈtær.ɪf / (BrE)
A tax or duty imposed by a government on imported or exported goods.

Examples

tariff on imports
Many countries negotiate trade agreements to reduce or eliminate tariffs between them.
Raising the tariff on agricultural products sparked a heated political debate.
The government introduced a new tariff on steel imports to protect domestic manufacturers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – duty: a government tax, especially on imports
  • antonym – subsidy: government financial support to an industry (opposite of a tax)
