speciousness

noun
deceptive appearance of truth
Pronunciation: / ˈspiːʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈspiːʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of seeming to be true, right, or plausible while actually being false or deceptive

Examples

speciousness of an argument
Critics pointed out the speciousness in his reasoning, which initially seemed convincing.
Her argument had an air of speciousness that misled many in the audience.
The speciousness of the claim was revealed once the facts were carefully examined.
