Girondist

noun
moderate political group member during the French Revolution
Pronunciation: / dʒɪˈrɑndɪst / (AmE), / dʒɪˈrɒndɪst / (BrE)
a member of the moderate republican political group in France during the French Revolution, known for opposing the radical Jacobins and eventually being overthrown by them

Examples

a Girondist leader
During the French Revolution, several Girondists were executed during the Reign of Terror.
Historians often compare the Girondist and Jacobin factions to highlight the divisions among revolutionaries.
The political philosophy of the Girondists favored decentralization and moderate reform.
