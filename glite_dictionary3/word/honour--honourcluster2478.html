honour

noun
a woman's chastity (archaic/literary)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) A woman's chastity or sexual purity, regarded as a matter of reputation or virtue.

Examples

lose her honour
Defending one's honour was often depicted as a matter of life and death in ancient tales.
In the old novel, the family was concerned with restoring their daughter's honour after the scandal.
She felt her honour had been called into question by the townsfolk's cruel gossip.
