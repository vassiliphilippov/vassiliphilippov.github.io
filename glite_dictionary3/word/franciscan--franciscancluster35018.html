Franciscan

noun
member of the Franciscan religious order
Pronunciation: / frænˈsɪskən / (AmE), / frænˈsɪskən / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic religious order founded by St. Francis of Assisi, known for vows of poverty and wearing a distinctive habit.

Examples

Franciscan monk robe
During the festival, the Franciscan wore a simple grey robe and sandals.
Many people sought advice from the wise old Franciscan who lived at the monastery.
The Franciscan led morning prayers in the small village chapel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – friar: a member of any of certain religious orders of men, especially in the Roman Catholic Church
