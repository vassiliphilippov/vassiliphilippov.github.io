brilliance

noun
intense brightness of light
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪljəns / (AmE), / ˈbrɪl.jəns / (BrE)
a very bright light, especially one that is much brighter than what the eyes are accustomed to

Examples

brilliance of the light
A sudden brilliance shone through the window, lighting up the entire room.
He shielded his eyes from the brilliance of the stadium floodlights.
The brilliance of the midday sun made it hard to see the road ahead.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glare: harsh, dazzling light that is difficult to look at
