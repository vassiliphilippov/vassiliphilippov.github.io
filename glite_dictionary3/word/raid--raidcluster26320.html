to raid

verb
make a sudden incursion to seize goods
Pronunciation: / reɪd / (AmE), / reɪd / (BrE)
to take or search for something quickly or without permission, often by entering a place suddenly

Examples

to raid a village
During the war, enemy soldiers would frequently raid the border towns for supplies.
The bandits raided the village at dawn, taking everything of value.
Vikings used to raid coastal settlements throughout Europe in search of treasure.
After midnight, the children quietly raided the kitchen for snacks.
She often raids her roommate's closet without asking.
The bear raided the campsite searching for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym – invade: to enter a place or country by force, often with an army
  • synonym – loot: to steal things, especially during chaos
  • synonym – plunder: to steal goods, typically using force
  • antonym – retreat: to withdraw from enemy forces or an area
