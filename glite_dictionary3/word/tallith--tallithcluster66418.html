tallith

noun
Jewish prayer shawl with knotted fringes
Pronunciation: / tɑːˈlit / (AmE), / ˈtælɪθ / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a shawl with specially knotted fringes at each corner, worn by Jewish men during morning prayers.

Examples

wear a tallith in prayer
Each boy received a new tallith for his Bar Mitzvah ceremony.
He wore his tallith proudly during the morning service.
The rabbi explained the meaning of the fringes on the tallith.
