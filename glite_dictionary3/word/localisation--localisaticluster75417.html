localisation

noun
adapting products for local markets
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of translating and adapting software, websites, multimedia, or other products so they are linguistically and culturally appropriate for a particular language, region, or market.

Examples

localisation of software
The localisation of the app into French boosted user engagement across France and Canada.
Our team is responsible for the localisation of video games, adapting content and interfaces for diverse cultures.
Before launch, the company budgeted heavily for localisation to ensure legal and cultural suitability in each market.

Related Terms

  • synonym – localization: US spelling of 'localisation', same meaning
  • synonym – translation: the process of converting text from one language into another; part of but narrower than localisation
