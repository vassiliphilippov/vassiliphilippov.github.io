extension

noun
act of expanding scope or availability
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act of expanding the range, influence, or availability of something, such as a program or belief

Examples

extension of a program
The charity is considering an extension of its programs to include more communities.
The government announced the extension of free healthcare services to rural areas.
There was strong support for the extension of the deadline to complete the project.
Many religions owe their success in part to the extension of their beliefs across continents and cultures.
The extension of democracy to rural regions changed the political landscape of the country.
The extension of this practice into urban communities brought about significant social change.

  • synonym – expansion: the process of becoming greater in size, number, or amount
  • antonym – restriction: the act of limiting or narrowing scope
