rima vocalis

noun
space between the true vocal folds
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪmə voʊˈkælɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːmə vəʊˈkɑːlɪs / (BrE)
the space between the two true vocal folds in the larynx, through which air passes during speech and breathing

Examples

rima vocalis opening
Damage to the rima vocalis can affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
During an examination, the doctor observed that the rima vocalis was abnormally narrow.
The flow of air through the rima vocalis is essential for producing vocal sounds.
