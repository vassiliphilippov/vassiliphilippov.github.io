self-aggrandisement

noun
promoting one's own importance or power
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf əˈɡræn.daɪz.mənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈɡræn.daɪz.mənt / (BrE)
the act of promoting oneself as more important, powerful, or influential than is warranted

Examples

political self-aggrandisement
Critics accused the politician of self-aggrandisement during her campaign.
His constant self-aggrandisement annoyed his colleagues, who preferred humility in the workplace.
The speech was seen as an exercise in self-aggrandisement rather than genuine leadership.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modesty: the quality of having a moderate view of one's own importance
  • synonym – self-promotion: the act of promoting oneself, one's abilities, or one's achievements
