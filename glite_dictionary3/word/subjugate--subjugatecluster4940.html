to subjugate

verb
bring under control; make subservient
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbdʒəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒʊɡeɪt / (BrE)
to bring a person or group under control or domination, especially by force

Examples

to subjugate a people
She refused to let fear subjugate her will and determination.
The conquerors sought to subjugate the indigenous people to their rule.
Throughout history, empires have tried to subjugate neighboring nations for resources and power.
Activists fought against the laws that sought to subjugate minority groups.
The dictator used fear and violence to subjugate his people.
Throughout history, powerful nations have often tried to subjugate weaker ones for their own gain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dominate: to have control or power over; to rule
  • antonym – liberate: to set free from oppression or control
  • synonym – oppress: to keep someone in hardship, especially by the unjust exercise of authority
