Polygonum orientale

noun
annual plant with crimson flower spikes
Pronunciation: / pɑːˈlɪɡənəm ɔːrɪɛnˈteɪliː / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪɡənəm ɔːrɪɛnˈtɑːliː / (BrE)
An annual plant with broad ovate leaves and slender drooping spikes of crimson flowers, native to southeastern Asia and Australia, and naturalized in North America; also known as prince's-feather.

Examples

Polygonum orientale crimson flowers
Polygonum orientale is valued in ornamental gardens for its striking crimson flower spikes.
Gardeners often refer to Polygonum orientale by its popular name, prince's-feather.
The naturalized populations of Polygonum orientale have become common in some parts of North America.
