COMINT

noun
intelligence from intercepted communications
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.mɪnt / (BrE)
intelligence information obtained from foreign communications intercepted by parties other than the intended recipients, especially for military or security purposes

Examples

COMINT operations
Advances in encryption technology have made COMINT operations increasingly complex.
Intercepted COMINT has led to the prevention of several major attacks in recent years.
The agency specializes in analyzing COMINT to gather crucial information about overseas threats.
