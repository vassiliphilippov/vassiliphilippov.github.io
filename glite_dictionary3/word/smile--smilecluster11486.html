to smile

verb
express with a smile
Pronunciation: / smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
to make a happy or friendly expression by turning up the corners of the mouth, often to show pleasure, friendliness, or amusement

Examples

to smile warmly
He smiled his approval at her suggestion, putting everyone at ease.
She smiled her gratitude to the audience after receiving the award.
The teacher smiled encouragement to the nervous student before the performance.
He always smiles at strangers to make them feel welcome.
She couldn't help but smile when she received the good news.
The children smiled brightly as they posed for the picture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – frown: to furrow the brow in an expression of displeasure or concentration
  • synonym – grin: to smile broadly, often showing the teeth
