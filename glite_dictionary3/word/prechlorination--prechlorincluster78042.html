prechlorination

noun
chlorination before another process
Pronunciation: / priːˌklɔːrəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / priːˌklɒrɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the addition of chlorine to water or another substance before a subsequent chemical process, often to aid purification or disinfection

Examples

prechlorination of water
Concerns about by-product formation have led some experts to reconsider the use of prechlorination.

The process of prechlorination can help control algae growth in municipal reservoirs.
