pantheism

noun
the belief that God and the universe are identical
Pronunciation: / ˈpænθiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpanθɪɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that God is identical with the universe, or that the universe is a manifestation of God.

Examples

nature and pantheism
He explored the philosophy of pantheism in his latest book, arguing that nature and divinity are inseparable.
Her interest in pantheism led her to study various spiritual traditions that view the universe as sacred.
Some poets have expressed pantheism through their work, depicting the natural world as divine.

Related Terms

  • antonym – atheism: the disbelief or lack of belief in the existence of God or gods
  • degree higher – panentheism: the belief that God is greater than the universe and includes and interpenetrates it
  • antonym – theism: the belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially a personal God as creator and ruler of the world
