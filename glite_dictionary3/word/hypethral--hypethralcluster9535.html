hypethral

adjective
open to the sky (esp. in architecture)
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpiːθrəl / (AmE), / hɪˈpiːθrəl / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having no roof, or an area that is partly or entirely open to the sky, especially as seen in classical architecture.

Many classical sanctuaries were designed as hypethral spaces to create a connection with the sky.
