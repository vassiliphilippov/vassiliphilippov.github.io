entablature

noun
horizontal structure above columns in classical buildings
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtæblətʃər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæblətʃə / (BrE)
(Architecture) The horizontal structure supported by columns in a classical building, situated above the column capitals and below the roof, typically consisting of the architrave, frieze, and cornice.

Examples

classical entablature design
Classical buildings are easily recognized by their distinctive columns and entablature.
Restoration work focused on repairing the damaged entablature above the marble columns.
The decorative motifs on the entablature added elegance to the ancient temple.
