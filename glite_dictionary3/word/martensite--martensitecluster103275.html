martensite

noun
hard, brittle steel microstructure
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtənˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtənsaɪt / (BrE)
A hard, brittle form of steel with a needle-like microstructure, formed when austenite is rapidly cooled, with carbon dissolved in alpha-iron.

Examples

hardened martensite steel
Improper tempering can make martensite too brittle for use in tools.
Quenching the steel in water results in the formation of martensite.
The exceptional hardness of the blade is due to its high martensite content.

Related Terms

  • antonym – austenite: A solid solution of carbon in gamma-iron, precursor phase in steel
