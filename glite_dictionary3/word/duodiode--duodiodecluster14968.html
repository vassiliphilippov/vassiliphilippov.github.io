duodiode

noun
electron tube with two diodes
Pronunciation: / duːˈoʊˌdaɪ.oʊd / (AmE), / ˌdjuː.əʊˈdaɪ.əʊd / (BrE)
an electron tube that contains two diodes within the same envelope, each with either a common or separate cathode.

Examples

duodiode vacuum tube
A duodiode can perform separate rectification tasks within a single component.
Early televisions often contained a duodiode as part of their signal detection system.
The radio engineer replaced the faulty duodiode in the amplifier circuit.
