bevel

noun
graphical effect simulating a sloped edge
Pronunciation: / c8b5bv59l / (AmE), / c8b5bv59l / (BrE)
(in digital design) a visual effect or shading applied to a flat element that simulates a sloped, raised, or recessed edge to create the appearance of depth.

Examples

bevel effect
The app button uses a subtle bevel to suggest depth on the flat surface.
In Photoshop she added a soft bevel to the icon to make it look raised.
Modern UI guidelines warn against heavy bevels because they can make interfaces look dated.

Related Terms

  • related – chamfer: a physical beveled edge or corner; related concept in manufacturing and design.
  • synonym – emboss: to give a surface a raised or recessed relief; a similar graphic effect often used with bevels.
