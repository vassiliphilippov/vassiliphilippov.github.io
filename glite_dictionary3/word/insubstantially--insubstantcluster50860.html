insubstantially

adverb
in a manner lacking substance or strength
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.səbˈstæn.ʃəli / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səbˈstæn.ʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks strength, substance, or solidity; not substantially or with little importance.

Examples

insubstantially supported argument
Many of his arguments were insubstantially supported and lacked persuasiveness.
The evidence against the defendant was insubstantially presented, failing to make a strong case.
The novel's theme is insubstantially developed, leaving readers unsatisfied.

  • antonym – substantially: in a substantial or significant way
