hawsepipe

noun
hole for anchor rope on a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔzˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɔːzˌpaɪp / (BrE)
A hole in the deck or hull of a ship through which the anchor chain or rope passes.

Examples

hawsepipe on a ship
Maintenance included cleaning the hawsepipe to prevent buildup of debris around the anchor chain.
The crew inspected the hawsepipe to make sure there were no obstructions before lowering the anchor.
Years of saltwater exposure had rusted the old ship's hawsepipe.
