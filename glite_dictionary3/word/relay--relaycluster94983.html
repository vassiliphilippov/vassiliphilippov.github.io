relay

noun
the act of passing something along
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈriːleɪ / (BrE)
the act of passing something along from one person or group to another, especially in order to accomplish a task more efficiently or continuously

Examples

relay of supplies
A seamless relay of supplies kept the expedition moving without delay.
The relay of information from the field office to headquarters was crucial to the investigation.
The volunteers formed a relay to move the sandbags to the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym – handover: the act of giving control or responsibility to another person
  • synonym – transmission: the act of sending something from one person or place to another
  • antonym – withhold: to refuse to give something to someone
