base

noun
a chemical compound reacting with acids
Pronunciation: / beɪs / (AmE), / beɪs / (BrE)
a chemical compound that reacts with acids to form a salt and water and typically turns litmus blue; examples include oxides, hydroxides, and ammonia

Examples

strong base solution
A base can neutralize an acid, resulting in the formation of a salt and water.
Ammonia is a common household base found in many cleaning products.
In chemistry class, we learned that a base turns red litmus paper blue.
