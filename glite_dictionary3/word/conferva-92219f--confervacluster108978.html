conferva

noun
filamentous freshwater alga forming scum
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɜrvə / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːvə / (BrE)
a filamentous freshwater green alga, especially of the former genus Conferva, which grows in still or stagnant water forming scum; now often refers to algae of the genus Tribonema or similar forms

Examples

green conferva algae
A thick layer of green conferva formed along the edges of the old well.
Scientists sampled the conferva from the stagnant pool to study its structure.
The pond's surface was covered in conferva after weeks of hot weather.
