steam

noun
water vapor from boiling or evaporation
Pronunciation: / stiːm / (AmE), / stiːm / (BrE)
Water in the form of an invisible vapor produced by boiling or evaporation, especially when diffused in the air.

Examples

hot steam rising
After a long run in the cold, her breath looked like steam in the frosty air.
The kitchen filled with steam as the water boiled on the stove.
You could see steam rising from the hot coffee mug.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – mist: tiny droplets of water in the air
  • synonym – vapor: diffused matter in the form of a mist or smoke, especially as a suspended fluid in air
