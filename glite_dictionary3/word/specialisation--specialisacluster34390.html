specialisation

noun
biological adaptation of body part or cell
Pronunciation: / ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which a body part or cell becomes adapted to perform a specific function.

Examples

cell specialisation process
During embryonic development, specialisation allows cells to take on unique roles in the body.
Nerve cells exhibit extreme specialisation to transmit electrical signals efficiently.
The specialisation of leaves into thorns helps protect certain plants from herbivores.

Related Terms

  • synonym – differentiation: (Biology) The process by which cells or tissues change to serve a specific function
