to prepossess

verb
to impress or influence positively beforehand
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / ˌpriː.pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
to influence or impress someone beforehand in a positive way, often creating a favorable opinion before actual experience

Examples

to prepossess with charm
Her kindness and intelligence prepossessed everyone she met at the interview.
His confident manner seemed to prepossess the judges before the competition even began.
The charming decor immediately prepossessed guests in favor of the restaurant.
