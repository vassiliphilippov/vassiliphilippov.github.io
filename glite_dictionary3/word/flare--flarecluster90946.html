to flare

verb
to widen or spread outward
Pronunciation: / flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
to become wider or spread outward at one end or edge, especially gradually

Examples

to flare outwards
The road flares out into a broad plaza at the city center.
The sleeves of her dress flare at the wrists, giving it a dramatic touch.
The vase flares at the top, allowing flowers to spread beautifully.

Related Terms

  • antonym – taper: to become gradually narrower at one end
