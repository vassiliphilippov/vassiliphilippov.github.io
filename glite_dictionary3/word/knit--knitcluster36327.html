to knit

verb
gather or contract into folds or wrinkles
Pronunciation: / nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
to draw together into small wrinkles or folds; to contract and form creases or furrows

Examples

knit his brows
A worried frown knit his forehead into deep lines.
She knit her brows in concentration as she read the letter.
Stress can often make people knit their features unconsciously.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pucker: to contract into wrinkles or small folds
  • antonym – smooth: to make surface even, remove wrinkles
