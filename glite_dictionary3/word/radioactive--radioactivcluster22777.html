radioactive

adjective
emitting or related to radioactivity
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv / (BrE)
emitting or relating to the emission of ionizing radiation or particles as a result of the decay of unstable atomic nuclei

Examples

radioactive material
Caution signs warned that the area contained radioactive substances.
Scientists studied the radioactive waste produced by the nuclear plant.
The lab requires special precautions when handling radioactive materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym – radioactive decay: the process by which an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation
  • synonym – radioactive isotope: an isotope that emits radiation as it decays
  • antonym – stable: not emitting radiation, not subject to radioactive decay
