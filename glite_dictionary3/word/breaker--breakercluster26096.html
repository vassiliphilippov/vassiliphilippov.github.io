breaker

noun
electrical safety device for circuit overloads
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkə / (BrE)
a device that automatically stops the flow of electricity in a circuit as a safety measure when the circuit becomes overloaded

Examples

circuit breaker
A power surge caused the breaker to shut off the electricity in the kitchen.
If the toaster trips the breaker again, you'll need to check for an electrical fault.
She flipped the main breaker off before replacing the light fixture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circuit breaker: a device that automatically interrupts an electric circuit
  • synonym – fuse: a safety device that melts to break an overloaded circuit
