contextualism

noun
theory emphasizing the importance of context
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛkstʃuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈtɛkstʃuəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that the context in which an idea, word, or event occurs is fundamental to its meaning or interpretation.

Examples

linguistic contextualism
Linguists often use contextualism to interpret ambiguous sentences in spoken language.
Philosophers who support contextualism argue that meaning depends heavily on situational factors.
The debate between contextualism and absolutism is central to epistemology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – relativism: theory that points of view have no absolute truth, only relative value
