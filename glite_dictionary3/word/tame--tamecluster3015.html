tame

adjective
domesticated; not wild
Pronunciation: / teɪm / (AmE), / teɪm / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) not wild; brought under control or domesticated by humans.

Examples

tame animals
After several generations of breeding, the foxes became increasingly tame.
The farmer keeps a flock of tame sheep in the meadow.
These tame blueberries are easier to harvest than wild ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym – wild: living or growing in a natural, untamed state
