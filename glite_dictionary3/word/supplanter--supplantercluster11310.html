supplanter

noun
one who takes another's place illicitly
Pronunciation: / səˈplæn.tɚ / (AmE), / səˈplæn.tə / (BrE)
a person who wrongfully or illegally takes the place of another

Examples

a supplanter takes power
After the king's sudden death, a supplanter seized the throne, claiming it by force.
He was branded a supplanter after taking credit for his colleague's ideas.
History is filled with stories of the supplanter unseating the rightful heir.

Related Terms

  • synonym – usurper: someone who seizes power or position without legal right
