Sabbath

noun
weekly day of religious rest
Pronunciation: / ˈsæb.əθ / (AmE), / ˈsæb.əθ / (BrE)
A day of rest and religious observance observed weekly by members of various religions, such as Saturday for Jews, Sunday for most Christians, and Friday for Muslims.

Examples

the Sabbath day
Many families gather together on the Sabbath to share a special meal and rest from work.
Observing the Sabbath is an important tradition in Judaism and Christianity.
She refrains from using electrical devices during the Sabbath to honor her religious beliefs.

  • synonym – holy day: a day solemnly dedicated to a religious observance
