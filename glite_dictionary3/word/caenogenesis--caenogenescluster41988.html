caenogenesis

noun
novel trait introduction during embryonic development
Pronunciation: / siːnoʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (AmE), / siːnɪˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the introduction during embryonic development of characters or structures not present in the earlier evolutionary history of the strain or species; evolutionary addition of novel features, such as the development of the placenta in mammals

Examples

caenogenesis in embryos
Because caenogenesis adds new features not found in ancestors, it plays a crucial role in evolutionary development.
Some scientists argue that placental evolution in mammals can be understood through the concept of caenogenesis.
The development of a yolk sac in mammals is an example of caenogenesis in evolutionary history.
