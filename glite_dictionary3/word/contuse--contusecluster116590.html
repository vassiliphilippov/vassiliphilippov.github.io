to contuse

verb
to injure tissue or bone, causing a bruise
Pronunciation: / kənˈtuz / (AmE), / kənˈtjuːz / (BrE)
to injure the underlying soft tissue or bone without breaking the skin, typically resulting in a bruise

Examples

to contuse a muscle
Doctors explained that the patient had contused his ribs, which caused significant pain but no fracture.
She contused her shoulder in the accident, but there was no open wound.
The player managed to contuse his thigh during the match, resulting in a deep bruise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bruise: to injure tissue and cause a discoloration without breaking the skin
