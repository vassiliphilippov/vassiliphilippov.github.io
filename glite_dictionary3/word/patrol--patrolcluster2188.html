to patrol

verb
to guard an area by moving around it
Pronunciation: / pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to guard or monitor an area by moving around it regularly to maintain security or order

Examples

to patrol the streets
Security officers were patrolling the building after the alarm was triggered.
Soldiers patrol the border to prevent illegal crossings.
The police patrolled the neighborhood every night to keep it safe.
