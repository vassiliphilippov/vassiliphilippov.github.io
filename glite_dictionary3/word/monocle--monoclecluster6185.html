monocle

noun
single eyepiece for one eye
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkəl / (BrE)
A single round lens for one eye, typically used to correct vision, and held in place by the muscles around the eye.

Examples

wear a monocle
After his monocle slipped, he caught it quickly and placed it back over his eye.
He adjusted his monocle before reading the letter aloud to the group.
The professor's monocle gave him an air of old-fashioned sophistication.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contact lens: a lens placed directly on the surface of the eye
  • synonym – spectacles: a pair of lenses worn to correct or assist eyesight
