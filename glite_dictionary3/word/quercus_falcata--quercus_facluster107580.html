Quercus falcata

noun
a species of southern red oak tree
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs færˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs falˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, also known as southern red oak, with deeply lobed, narrow, falcate leaves and wood similar to northern red oaks.
