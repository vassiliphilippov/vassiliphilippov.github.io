phenomenal

adjective
relating to a phenomenon; observable
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑː.mə.nəl / (AmE), / fəˈnɒm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or connected with a phenomenon or phenomena; observable or perceptible, especially as distinct from theoretical or underlying causes.

Examples

phenomenal world
Her research focused on the phenomenal aspects of perception rather than abstract theories.
In philosophy, the distinction between noumenal and phenomenal reality is an important one.
The study examined phenomenal changes in weather patterns over fifty years.
