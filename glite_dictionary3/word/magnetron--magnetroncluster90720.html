magnetron

noun
microwave-generating vacuum tube
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡ.nəˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.nɪ.trɒn / (BrE)
a type of vacuum tube in which a stream of electrons from a central cathode to a cylindrical anode is controlled by crossed magnetic and electric fields, used chiefly in the generation of microwaves

Examples

magnetron in a microwave
Early radar equipment often used a magnetron to produce high-frequency signals.
Technicians replaced the faulty magnetron to repair the communications device.
The magnetron in a microwave oven generates the microwaves that heat your food.
