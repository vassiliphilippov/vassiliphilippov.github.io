to assimilate

verb
make similar or absorb into a group
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪməleɪt / (BrE)
to become similar to others, especially by integrating into a group or culture

Examples

to assimilate into society
It can take years for immigrants to fully assimilate into a new society.
Many schools strive to help new students assimilate into the local culture.
The government attempted to assimilate minority communities by promoting a unified national identity.
It can take years for migrants to fully assimilate to their new surroundings.
Newcomers often strive to assimilate into the local culture to feel at home.
Some students assimilate quickly, while others maintain their distinct cultural identities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – differentiate: to make or become different in the process of development
  • synonym – integrate: to combine into a whole; to become part of a group
