ensilage

noun
fermented fodder stored in a silo
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.sɪˌlɑdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.sɪ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
fodder, usually grass or other crops, harvested while green and preserved through partial fermentation in a silo for feeding livestock.

Examples

ensilage for cattle feed
Modern dairies often depend on high-quality ensilage for year-round nutrition.
Poorly made ensilage can spoil and lead to health issues in livestock.
The farmer prepared ensilage from freshly cut corn to feed his cattle during winter.
