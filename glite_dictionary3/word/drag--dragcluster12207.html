to drag

verb
walk without lifting feet
Pronunciation: / dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to move slowly, with effort, or reluctance; to lag behind or to continue for a long, tedious time

Examples

drag your feet
He was so tired that he could only drag his feet across the floor.
She dragged her way home after a long day at work.
The old man dragged himself down the corridor, clearly exhausted.
After running the marathon, he could only drag himself back to his apartment.
She watched him drag his feet as he reluctantly headed to work.
The meeting seemed to drag on for hours without any conclusion.
As the afternoon dragged by, she grew increasingly bored with the slow progress.
The film dragged in the middle, making it difficult to stay engaged.
The meeting seemed to drag on forever, leaving everyone restless by the end.

  • synonym – lag: to fall behind in movement, progress, or development
  • antonym – lead: to be ahead or in front of others
  • synonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary, or to delay leaving; to last a long time
  • antonym – rush: to move or progress with great speed
  • synonym – trail: to move or lag slowly behind
  • synonym – trudge: walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically from exhaustion
