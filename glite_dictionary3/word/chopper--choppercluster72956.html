chopper

noun
a high-bouncing ground ball in baseball
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑp.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒp.ə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a batted ball that strikes the ground and bounces high in the air.

Examples

high chopper hit
He hit a chopper over the pitcher's mound and made it to first base safely.
The batter was lucky that his chopper took a strange bounce past the infielder.
With runners on base, a chopper can be difficult for the defense to handle.
