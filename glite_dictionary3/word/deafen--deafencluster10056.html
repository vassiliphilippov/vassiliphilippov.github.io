to deafen

verb
make unable to hear
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfən / (AmE), / ˈdɛfən / (BrE)
to make someone unable to hear or to make it impossible to hear because of a very loud noise

Examples

to deafen with noise
Continuous loud music can deafen people if they are not careful.
The alarm's shrill sound threatened to deafen anyone nearby.
The explosion was so loud it almost deafened me.
Fireworks exploded all around, threatening to deafen the crowd.
The roar of the engine would deafen anyone standing nearby.
Their laughter was so loud it almost seemed to deafen us.

  • synonym – deafening: extremely loud (sound or noise)
