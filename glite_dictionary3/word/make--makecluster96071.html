to make

verb
to act as if about to do something
Pronunciation: / meɪk / (AmE), / meɪk / (BrE)
to move, proceed, or act as if to do something

Examples

make to leave
He made to leave the room, but stopped at the door.
The cat made as if to jump onto the table, then changed its mind.
When I entered, she made to speak, but hesitated.
After the concert, we had to make our way through the crowded hall.
She managed to make her way up the steep hillside before sunset.
They hurried to make their way to the exit during the fire drill.

  • antonym – retreat: to move back or withdraw
