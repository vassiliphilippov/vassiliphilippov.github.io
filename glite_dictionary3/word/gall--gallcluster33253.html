gall

noun
digestive fluid from the liver
Pronunciation: / ɡɔl / (AmE), / ɡɔːl / (BrE)
A bitter yellowish fluid secreted by the liver and stored in the gallbladder, which helps to digest fats.

Examples

gall in liver
Gall is essential for the digestion and absorption of dietary fats.
Doctors discovered an excess of gall in her system during the medical exam.
The liver continuously produces gall, which is stored in the gallbladder until needed.
