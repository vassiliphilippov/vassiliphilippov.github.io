contumaciously

adverb
in a stubbornly rebellious way
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntjuˈmeɪʃəsli / (AmE), / ˌkɒntjuˈmeɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a stubborn and rebellious manner, especially continually defying authority.

Examples

contumaciously refuse orders
He contumaciously resisted every attempt at discipline in the classroom.
The defendant acted contumaciously by ignoring the judge’s orders.
The witness contumaciously refused to answer the prosecutor's questions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – obediently: in a manner that obeys rules or authority
  • synonym – rebelliously: in a way that shows a refusal to obey authority
