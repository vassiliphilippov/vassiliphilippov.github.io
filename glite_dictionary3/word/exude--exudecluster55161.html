to exude

verb
release a liquid or substance slowly
Pronunciation: / ɪgˈzud / (AmE), / ɪgˈzjuːd / (BrE)
to release or discharge (a liquid or substance) slowly and steadily, often in drops or a small quantity

Examples

exude sweat
After running in the sun, she began to exude sweat from every pore.
Certain trees exude sap when their bark is damaged.
The walls of the cave exuded moisture even in the dry season.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ooze: to flow or leak out slowly; to exude
  • synonym – seep: to flow or leak slowly through porous material
