kangaroo

noun
large leaping marsupial of Australia
Pronunciation: / ˌkæŋɡəˈru / (AmE), / ˌkaŋɡəˈruː / (BrE)
A large, plant-eating marsupial native to Australia and New Guinea, with powerful hind legs for leaping and a long, thick tail.

Examples

wild kangaroo
A group of kangaroos was spotted hopping across the field at sunset.
The kangaroo is recognized as a national symbol of Australia.
Young kangaroos, called joeys, spend several months in their mother's pouch after birth.
