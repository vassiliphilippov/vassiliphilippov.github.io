manroot

noun
American perennial plant with large starchy root
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈmænˌruːt / (BrE)
A tropical American climbing or prostrate perennial plant with a large starchy root, sometimes believed to be a source of the sweet potato.

Examples

manroot plant root
Some botanists once believed that the sweet potato was derived from the manroot.
The manroot produces an enormous tuber that is often harvested for its starch.
The climbing vines of the manroot can cover large areas in tropical regions.
