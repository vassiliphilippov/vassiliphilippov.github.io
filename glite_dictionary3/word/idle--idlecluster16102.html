idle

adjective
not working; inactive
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.dl̩ /; / ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.dl̩ /; / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
not working, not active, or not being used (about people, things, or resources)

Examples

idle workers
He spent most of the summer idle, lounging by the pool.
The factory has been idle since last month due to a shortage of materials.
Their equipment lay idle while repairs were being made.
Leaving your savings as idle money means missing out on possible interest.
Many companies hold idle funds in their accounts, waiting for better investment opportunities.
The idle capital in the bank could have been used for business expansion.
During the economic downturn, many skilled people remained idle for months.
Several idle workers gathered outside the closed factory, hoping for news about reopening.
The government's goal is to reduce the number of idle youths in the city.

  • antonym – employed: (of a person) having a paid job
  • synonym – inactive: not engaging in or involving any activity
  • synonym – unemployed: (of a person) without a paid job but available to work
