buzzard

noun
a New World vulture; large scavenging bird
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌz.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈbʌz.əd / (BrE)
A large scavenging bird, especially a New World vulture common in South and Central America and the southern United States.

Examples

circling buzzard
A buzzard landed on the fence post, scanning the area for food.
The buzzard is often mistaken for other birds of prey in the region.
We saw a buzzard circling high above the desert looking for prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vulture: a large bird of prey feeding mainly on carrion
