to fuel

verb
stimulate or intensify (figurative sense)
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːəl / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəl / (BrE)
To make something increase or become more intense, especially an emotion, argument, or activity.

Examples

to fuel anger
His anger was fueled by years of injustice and frustration.
Rumors about the merger have fueled speculation among investors.
The politician's comments only fueled further controversy in the media.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dampen: make less strong or intense
  • synonym – provoke: to cause a reaction or feeling
  • synonym – stimulate: encourage or increase activity or interest
