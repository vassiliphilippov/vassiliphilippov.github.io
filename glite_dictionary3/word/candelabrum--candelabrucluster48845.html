candelabrum

noun
ornamental branched candlestick
Pronunciation: / ˌkændəˈleɪbrəm / (AmE), / ˌkandɪˈlɑːbrəm / (BrE)
An ornamental branched candlestick that holds several candles or lights.

Examples

ornate candelabrum centerpiece
A golden candelabrum stood elegantly on the mantelpiece, holding seven white candles.
She polished the antique candelabrum until it gleamed in the light.
The grand dining room was lit by a large candelabrum on the table.
