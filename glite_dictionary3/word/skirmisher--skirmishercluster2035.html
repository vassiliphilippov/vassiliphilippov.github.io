skirmisher

noun
person engaged in minor fighting, often military
Pronunciation: / ˈskɝː.mɪʃ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɜː.mɪʃ.ə / (BrE)
a person, especially a soldier, who is involved in minor fighting or preliminary battles, often ahead of the main force

Examples

enemy skirmisher attacks
In the early hours, a lone skirmisher fired a warning shot across the valley.
The skirmisher advanced quietly, scanning the terrain for signs of enemy movement.
The battalion sent out several skirmishers to probe the enemy's defenses.

  • synonym – scout: a person sent ahead to gather information, especially in the military
