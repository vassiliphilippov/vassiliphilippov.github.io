to affranchise

verb
grant freedom from slavery or servitude
Pronunciation: / əˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / əˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
to grant freedom to someone, especially from slavery or servitude

Examples

to affranchise a slave
After years of negotiation, the government decided to affranchise all bonded workers.
Some reformers petitioned the king to affranchise the nation's serfs.
The movement aimed to affranchise those held in involuntary servitude.

Related Terms

  • synonym – emancipate: to set free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • synonym – enfranchise: to grant freedom or rights, particularly the right to vote
  • antonym – enslave: to make someone a slave; deprive of freedom
