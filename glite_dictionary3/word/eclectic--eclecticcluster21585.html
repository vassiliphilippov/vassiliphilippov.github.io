eclectic

adjective
selecting from a variety of sources
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (BrE)
deriving ideas, style, or taste from a diverse range of sources rather than following a single system

Examples

eclectic mix of styles
He decorated his apartment in an eclectic style, mixing vintage and modern pieces.
Her eclectic taste in music ranges from classical to heavy metal.
The restaurant is known for its eclectic menu, offering dishes from around the world.

  • synonym – selective: choosing carefully from various options
