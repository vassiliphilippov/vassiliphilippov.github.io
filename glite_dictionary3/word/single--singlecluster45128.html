single

noun
a base hit in baseball reaching first base
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
(Baseball) A hit that allows the batter to reach first base safely.

Examples

single to first base
He swung at the first pitch and got a single to start the inning.
Jake hit a sharp single into left field, moving the runner to third.
The crowd cheered as she scored the winning run on a single.
