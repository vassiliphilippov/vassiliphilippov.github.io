Franz Joseph Haydn

proper noun
Austrian composer, major influence on classical symphony
Pronunciation: / fræns ˈdʒoʊzəf ˈhaɪdən / (AmE), / frans ˈdʒəʊzɪf ˈhaɪdən / (BrE)
(1732–1809) An Austrian composer known for his prolific output and major influence on the classical form of the symphony and string quartet.

Examples

Franz Joseph Haydn composed symphonies
Franz Joseph Haydn composed over one hundred symphonies, shaping the future of classical music.
Many music historians consider Franz Joseph Haydn the father of the string quartet.
The influence of Franz Joseph Haydn can be heard in the works of Mozart and Beethoven.
