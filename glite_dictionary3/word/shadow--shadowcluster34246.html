shadow

noun
dominant presence overshadowing someone
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
a persistent and dominant presence, especially one that overshadows or limits someone else's actions or recognition

Examples

shadow of his brother
He worked tirelessly, always in the shadow of his elder brother's achievements.
The company's innovative ideas remained in the shadow of its larger competitor.
The young artist felt trapped in the shadow of her famous predecessor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – influence: the power to affect someone or something
  • antonym – limelight: the center of public attention
