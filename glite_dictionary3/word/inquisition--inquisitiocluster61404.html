Inquisition

noun
historical Catholic tribunal to suppress heresy
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A former tribunal of the Roman Catholic Church (1232–1820) established to identify and punish heresy.

Examples

the Inquisition punished heresy
During the Inquisition, many people lived in fear of being investigated for their beliefs.
Historians study the records of the Inquisition to understand the extent of religious persecution in Europe.
The Inquisition was notorious for using torture to force confessions from accused heretics.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heresy: belief or opinion contrary to orthodox religious doctrine
