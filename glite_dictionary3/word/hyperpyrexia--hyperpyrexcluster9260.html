hyperpyrexia

noun
extremely high fever
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərpaɪˈrɛksiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəpɪəˈksiːə / (BrE)
an abnormally high fever, especially one exceeding 41°C (105.8°F), frequently occurring in children

Examples

dangerous hyperpyrexia episode
Doctors treated the patient's hyperpyrexia urgently to prevent complications.
In rare cases, hyperpyrexia can lead to seizures if not managed quickly.
The child was admitted to the hospital with hyperpyrexia after three days of illness.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – fever: an abnormally high body temperature, usually due to illness
  • synonym – hyperthermia: dangerously high body temperature, especially due to outside heat
