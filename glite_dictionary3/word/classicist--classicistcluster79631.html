classicist

noun
student of ancient Greek and Latin
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsəsɪst / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪsɪst / (BrE)
A person who studies or is an expert in the languages, literature, and culture of ancient Greece and Rome.

Examples

classicist scholar
As a classicist, he spends much of his time translating Latin texts into English.
She is a renowned classicist who has published many articles on ancient Greek poetry.
The university hired a new classicist to expand its ancient studies program.

Related Terms

  • synonym – classic: a work of ancient Greek or Roman literature; or something judged to be outstanding
