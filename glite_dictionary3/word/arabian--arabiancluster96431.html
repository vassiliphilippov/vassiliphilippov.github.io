Arabian

noun
a person from the Arabian Peninsula or nearby regions
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪ.bi.ən / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.bi.ən / (BrE)
a member of a Semitic people originally from the Arabian Peninsula and surrounding regions, especially one who speaks Arabic and inhabits the Middle East or North Africa

Examples

an Arabian person
As an Arabian, he retains strong ties to his family's heritage in the region.
Many Arabians travel to celebrate Eid with their relatives across the Middle East.
The ancient Arabians played a key role in the development of early mathematics and astronomy.
