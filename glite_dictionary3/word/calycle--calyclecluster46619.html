calycle

noun
group of bracts resembling a calyx
Pronunciation: / ˈkælɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəl / (BrE)
a small group of bracts resembling a calyx, especially as found beneath the flowers of plants such as carnations or hibiscus

Examples

calycle under flower
Botanists examined the calycle structure beneath the hibiscus blooms for research.
In some species, the calycle can be mistaken for the flower's calyx due to its similar appearance.
The calycle of the carnation is composed of several green bracts that protect the flower.
