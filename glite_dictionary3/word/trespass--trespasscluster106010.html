to trespass

verb
make unfair or excessive use of
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛspæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs / (BrE)
(formal) To make unfair or excessive use of someone’s time, rights, or goodwill.

Examples

to trespass upon kindness
He apologized for trespassing upon her kindness.
I hope I do not trespass on your patience with this long explanation.
She felt that the reporter had trespassed upon her privacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encroach: to gradually take or use more of something than is rightfully yours
  • synonym – invade: to intrude or encroach upon
