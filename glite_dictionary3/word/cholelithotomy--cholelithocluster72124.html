cholelithotomy

noun
surgical removal of gallstone from gallbladder
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊləˌlɪˈθɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌkəʊlɪlɪˈθɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving removal of a gallstone through an incision in the gallbladder

Examples

cholelithotomy surgery performed
A cholelithotomy may be recommended when gallstones cannot be dissolved or broken by less invasive methods.
Recovery after a cholelithotomy is usually quicker than after a cholecystectomy.
The surgeon performed a cholelithotomy to remove the patient's gallstones.

  • synonym – gallstone: a small hard crystalline mass formed in the gallbladder
