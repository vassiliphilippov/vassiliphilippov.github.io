fission

noun
splitting of a nucleus with energy release
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A nuclear reaction in which a heavy atomic nucleus splits into two or more smaller nuclei, with the release of energy.

Examples

nuclear fission reaction
Nuclear fission powers many modern reactors for electricity generation.
Scientists study fission to better understand how nuclear weapons work.
The process of fission produces hazardous radioactive waste.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fusion: a nuclear reaction in which two light nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus
