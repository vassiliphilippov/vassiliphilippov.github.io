to plunder

verb
take or use dishonestly, esp. ideas or work
Pronunciation: / ˈplʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndə / (BrE)
to take or use something, especially ideas, work, or property, dishonestly or illegally, often without permission or credit

Examples

to plunder ideas
He openly admitted to plundering themes from classic novels in his screenplay.
Many companies have faced lawsuits for plundering patented technology.
The author was accused of plundering ideas from earlier works without giving credit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appropriate: to take something for one's own use, often without permission
  • antonym – borrow: to take and use something with the intention of returning it
  • synonym – steal: to take something that does not belong to you without permission
