to bulk

verb
stick out or form a bulge
Pronunciation: / bʌlk / (AmE), / bʌlk / (BrE)
to swell, bulge, or stick out in a noticeable way, or to cause something to do so

Examples

bulk under clothes

A mound of earth bulked up at the edge of the field.
The strange shape bulked visibly beneath the tarp.
Heavy fillings can bulk the envelope, making it look uneven.
The dampness began to bulk the pages of the old book.
The starch caused the pudding to bulk as it cooked.
