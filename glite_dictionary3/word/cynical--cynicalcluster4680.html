cynical

adjective
distrustful of others' motives
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪkəl / (BrE)
Distrusting or disparaging the motives of others; believing that people are motivated purely by self-interest.

Examples

cynical about politics
After years of disappointment, she became increasingly cynical about politics.
His cynical attitude made it hard for him to trust anyone's good intentions.
Many people grow cynical after witnessing repeated scandals in business and government.

Related Terms

  • antonym – optimistic: hopeful and confident about the future
  • synonym – skeptical: not easily convinced; having doubts or reservations
