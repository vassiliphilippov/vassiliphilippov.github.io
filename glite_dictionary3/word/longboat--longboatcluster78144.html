longboat

noun
largest boat on a sailing vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌbəʊt / (BrE)
the largest boat carried aboard a merchant sailing vessel, traditionally used for carrying cargo or passengers between ship and shore

Examples

a longboat carries people
During the storm, everyone gathered in the longboat for safety.
Old sailing vessels often relied on the longboat for important missions ashore.
The crew lowered the longboat to ferry supplies from the shore to the ship.
