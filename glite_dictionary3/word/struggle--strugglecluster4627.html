to struggle

verb
engage in a fight or conflict
Pronunciation: / ˈstrʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌɡl / (BrE)
to be engaged in a fight or conflict, often with difficulty or resistance

Examples

to struggle against enemies
As the rebels struggled to gain control, the conflict intensified.
She struggled against her captor, refusing to give up hope.
The two teams struggled fiercely for dominance throughout the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contend: to compete or struggle in order to win or achieve something
  • synonym – fight: to take part in a violent struggle; engage in combat
  • antonym – surrender: to give up or cease to resist an opponent
