fibre

noun
compressed material; leather substitute
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A leatherlike material made by compressing layers of paper or cloth, used especially in manufacturing.

Examples

fibre suitcase
Many suitcases in the past were constructed from compressed fibre for durability.
The case was made of fibre instead of real leather, making it more affordable.
The technician repaired the equipment using a sheet of fibre as a replacement panel.
