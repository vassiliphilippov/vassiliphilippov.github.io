sealed

adjective
kept secret or undisclosed
Pronunciation: / siːld / (AmE), / siːld / (BrE)
kept secret or undisclosed for the time being; not to be revealed until a specified time or circumstance.

Examples

sealed envelope
After the sealed bid auction, the results were announced to the public.
In tournaments, a sealed move is made when a game is adjourned and play is to be resumed later.
The general handed her sealed orders that she could not open until reaching the destination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – open: not concealed or secret; accessible to all
  • synonym – secret: kept hidden or not known; not disclosed
