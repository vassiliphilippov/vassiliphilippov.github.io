stray

adjective
isolated or scattered (not close together)
Pronunciation: / streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
(of occurrences or items) appearing in isolation or scattered, not close together in time or place.

Examples

stray bullet
After the party, some stray balloons lingered in the garden.
He picked up the stray coins from the floor.
Only a few stray comments were made during the meeting.

Related Terms
