primitively

adverb
in relation to origin or beginning
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪmətɪvli / (AmE), / ˈprɪmɪtɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to the original state, beginning, or earliest form of something.

Examples

primitively organized society
Rights were primitively assigned according to family hierarchy.
The custom was primitively religious in origin but gradually lost its sacred nature.
The species was primitively aquatic before adapting to life on land.
