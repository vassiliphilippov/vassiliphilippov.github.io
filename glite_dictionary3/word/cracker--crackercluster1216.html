cracker

noun
small explosive firework
Pronunciation: / ˈkrækər / (AmE), / ˈkrækə / (BrE)
A firework consisting of a small explosive charge and fuse in a heavy paper casing, producing a loud noise when set off.

Examples

set off a cracker
Lighting a cracker in the street is illegal in some cities due to safety concerns.
She bought a box of colorful crackers for the New Year's celebration.
The children were excited to light a cracker during the holiday festival.
