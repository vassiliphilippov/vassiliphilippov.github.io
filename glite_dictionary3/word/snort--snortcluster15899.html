snort

noun
rough noise made through the nose
Pronunciation: / snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
a sudden, harsh noise made by forcing air through the nose, often to show emotion such as amusement, anger, or contempt

Examples

a loud snort
His comment was met with a snort from the audience.
She gave a loud snort of disapproval when she heard the news.
With an indignant snort, he turned and left the room.
His joke was met with a snort from the back of the room.
She let out a loud snort at the ridiculous suggestion.
Unable to hide her contempt, she made a rude snort of laughter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – giggle: a light, silly laugh
  • synonym – grunt: a low, short guttural sound, often to express effort or displeasure
  • synonym – snicker: a suppressed laugh, often disrespectful or mocking
  • synonym – sniff: to draw air audibly through the nose to detect a smell or express emotion
  • antonym – sob: to cry noisily, often the opposite emotional outburst of laughing disrespectfully
