to beckon

verb
summon by a gesture
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛk.ən / (AmE), / ˈbɛk.ən / (BrE)
to make a gesture (with your hand, arm, or head) to encourage someone to come closer or follow

Examples

to beckon a friend over
She beckoned to the waiter to bring the bill.
Standing at the door, she beckoned her friend inside.
The old man beckoned me over with a gentle wave of his hand.
He beckoned to his friend from across the crowded room.
She stood at the door, beckoning for us to enter.
The teacher beckoned the students to gather around her desk.
