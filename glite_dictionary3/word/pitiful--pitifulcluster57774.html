pitiful

adjective
deserving or causing pity
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪfəl / (BrE)
deserving or causing feelings of pity; arousing compassion because of distress or inadequacy

Examples

pitiful little child
Her pitiful attempt at an apology only made things worse.
The abandoned puppy was in such a pitiful state that everyone wanted to help.
We donated to the charity because the conditions in the refugee camp were truly pitiful.

Related Terms

  • antonym – admirable: inspiring approval or respect, the opposite of pitiful
  • synonym – pathetic: arousing pity, especially through vulnerability or sadness
  • synonym – pitiable: deserving pity, usually because of misfortune
  • synonym – wretched: in a very unhappy or unfortunate state
