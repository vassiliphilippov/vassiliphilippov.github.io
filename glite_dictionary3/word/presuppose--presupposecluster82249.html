to presuppose

verb
require as a precondition
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːsəˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌpriːsəˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to assume or require something as a necessary condition before something else can happen or be discussed

Examples

to presuppose trust
Effective communication presupposes mutual trust between the parties involved.
Her argument presupposed that everyone shared the same values.
The theory presupposes a basic understanding of quantum mechanics.
Effective communication presupposes a shared language and common ground.
Her argument presupposed that everyone agreed with her values.
The theory seems to presuppose a basic understanding of biology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assume: to suppose something to be true without proof
  • synonym – imply: to suggest something as a logical consequence
