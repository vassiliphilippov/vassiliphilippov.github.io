proclivity

noun
natural tendency or inclination
Pronunciation: / proʊˈklɪvəti / (AmE), / prəˈklɪvɪti / (BrE)
a natural tendency to behave in a particular way or to like a particular thing, especially something bad

Examples

proclivity for trouble
Despite his parents' concerns, he showed an early proclivity for music.
He has a proclivity for exaggeration, which sometimes gets him into trouble.
Her proclivity for punctuality made her a reliable team member.

Related Terms

  • antonym – aversion: a strong dislike or disinclination
  • synonym – predilection: a preference or special liking for something
