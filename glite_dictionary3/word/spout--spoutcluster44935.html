to spout

verb
speak loudly or excitedly at length
Pronunciation: / spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
to speak in a loud, excited, or emotional manner, often for a long time and without much thought

Examples

to spout nonsense loudly
He began to spout nonsense about conspiracy theories at the dinner table.
She tends to spout facts quickly when she’s excited about a topic.
The politician would spout promises during every debate, but rarely kept any.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pontificate: express one's opinions in a pompous and dogmatic way
  • synonym – rant: to speak or shout at length in a wild, impassioned way
