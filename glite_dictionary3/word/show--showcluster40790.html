to show

verb
provide evidence; demonstrate or prove
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ʃəʊ / (BrE)
to prove, demonstrate, or provide evidence for something (including facts, research, or measurements)

Examples

to show the results
His calm response shows his experience in handling pressure.
Medical tests showed no trace of the infection in her body.
The results of the experiment show that the treatment is effective.
Her research showed that the proposed method significantly improved patient outcomes.
The lawyer attempted to show that his client was innocent by presenting new evidence.
The scientist was able to show the effectiveness of the new vaccine through rigorous testing.
Her medical records show a clear improvement over the past year.
The diary shows his distress that evening.
The evidence showed that the company was not at fault.

Related Terms

  • synonym – demonstrate: to show clearly, especially by giving proof or evidence
  • antonym – disprove: to show something is false by providing evidence
  • synonym – prove: demonstrate the truth or existence of something by evidence or argument
  • synonym – read: (of a gauge or meter) to indicate a value
  • synonym – register: (of an instrument) to indicate a value
