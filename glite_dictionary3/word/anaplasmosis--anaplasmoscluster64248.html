anaplasmosis

noun
tick-borne bacterial disease in humans
Pronunciation: / \u02cc\u00e6n\u0259pl\u00e6z\u02c8mo\u028as\u026as / (AmE), / \u02cc\u00e6n\u0259pl\u00e6z\u02c8m\u0259\u028as\u026as / (BrE)
(in humans) a tick-borne infectious disease caused by Anaplasma bacteria, especially Anaplasma phagocytophilum, typically causing fever, headache and muscle aches; also called human granulocytic anaplasmosis.

Examples

human anaplasmosis infection
Public health alerts advised residents after several anaplasmosis cases were reported in humans.
Doctors recommended antibiotics as soon as they suspected anaplasmosis following the tick bite.
Because symptoms can be vague, people with fever after hiking were tested for anaplasmosis.
