Eurocentrism

noun
belief in European superiority; Western-centric view
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊroʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that Europe and Europeans are superior to others, or the tendency to interpret the world in terms of European or Western values and experiences.

Examples

Eurocentrism in history books
Some historians argue that Eurocentrism distorts our understanding of world history.
The curriculum has been criticized for its Eurocentrism and lack of diversity.
The museum is making efforts to move away from Eurocentrism in its exhibits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Afrocentrism: viewing world history or culture from the perspective of African people and values
  • synonym – ethnocentrism: belief in the inherent superiority of one's own ethnic group or culture
