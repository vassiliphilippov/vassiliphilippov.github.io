extortion

noun
an exorbitant or unfair charge
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɔɹʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
an exorbitant or excessively high charge or price demanded for something, especially one perceived as unfair or unreasonable

Examples

extortionate prices
Many travelers complained about the extortion at local tourist shops.
The plumber's bill was pure extortion for such a simple job.
Ticket prices during the holidays amount to extortion when demand is high.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bargain: something bought or offered for sale at a favorable price
  • synonym – rip-off: an act of overcharging or swindling someone
