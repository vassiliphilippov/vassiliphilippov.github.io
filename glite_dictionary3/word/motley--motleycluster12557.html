motley

adjective
multicolored; variegated
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑtli / (AmE), / ˈmɒtli / (BrE)
having sections or patches of different, usually bright, colors; multicolored or variegated in appearance

Examples

motley scarf colors
She wore a motley scarf that brightened up her winter coat.
The artist's studio was filled with a motley assortment of colorful fabrics and paints.
The parade featured a motley collection of flags and costumes from around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monochrome: having or consisting of only one color
  • synonym – particolored: having different colors in different parts
  • synonym – piebald: having irregular patches of two colors, typically black and white
