to roar

verb
act in a wild, noisy, or disorderly way
Pronunciation: / rɔr / (AmE), / rɔː / (BrE)
to behave or move in a noisy, wild, or disorderly way; to proceed with great commotion

Examples

bikers roar into town
A gang of bikers roared into the quiet town, startling the residents with their antics.
Every Saturday night, young crowds would roar through the downtown streets, celebrating loudly.
The fans roared out of the stadium after their team won the championship.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rampage: to rush around in a violent and uncontrollable manner
  • synonym – revel: to engage in lively and noisy festivities
