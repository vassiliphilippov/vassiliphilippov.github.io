to reconvict

verb
convict again, especially after retrial
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈvɪkt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɪkt / (BrE)
to convict (someone) again, especially after a retrial or following a previous conviction that was overturned

Examples

to reconvict a criminal
After a lengthy retrial, the jury decided to reconvict the accused.
If new evidence is found, the court may reconvict the defendant.
The prosecution is seeking to reconvict him after the original verdict was overturned.
