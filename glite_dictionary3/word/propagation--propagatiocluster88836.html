propagation

noun
spreading of ideas or practices
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːpəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of spreading ideas, beliefs, or practices so that they are adopted in new areas or among more people

Examples

propagation of ideas
He studied the propagation of cultural practices through migration.
Religious propagation was a central goal of the missionaries during that era.
The propagation of new technologies depends on effective communication and public acceptance.
