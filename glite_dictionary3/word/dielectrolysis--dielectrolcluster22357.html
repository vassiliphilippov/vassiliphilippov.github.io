dielectrolysis

noun
movement of charged particles in a colloid by electric field
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪɪlɛkˈtrɑləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪɪlɛkˈtrɒləsɪs / (BrE)
the movement of charged particles within a colloid under the influence of an electric field, where positively charged particles migrate to the cathode and negatively charged particles to the anode
