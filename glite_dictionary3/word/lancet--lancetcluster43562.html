lancet

noun
pointed arch in Gothic architecture
Pronunciation: / ˈlæn.sət / (AmE), / ˈlæn.sɪt / (BrE)
an acutely pointed arch, especially one of the narrow, tall type commonly used in Gothic architecture

Examples

Gothic lancet window
Each lancet in the nave is adorned with intricate stained glass panels.
The architect specified a series of tall lancets to enhance the building's vertical emphasis.
The cathedral's windows are framed by elegant lancet arches that reach towards the sky.
