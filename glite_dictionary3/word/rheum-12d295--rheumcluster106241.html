rheum

noun
watery discharge from eyes or nose
Pronunciation: / rum / (AmE), / ruːm / (BrE)
a watery or thin discharge from the mucous membranes, especially from the eyes or nose

Examples

rheum in the eyes
During his cold, a sticky rheum kept forming around his nostrils.
Older people sometimes notice more rheum in their eyes after sleeping.
She wiped the rheum from the corner of her eye with a tissue.
