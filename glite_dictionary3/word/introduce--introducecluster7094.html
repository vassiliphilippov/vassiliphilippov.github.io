to introduce

verb
to bring in something or someone new
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to bring or present something or someone new into a place, group, or situation for the first time

Examples

to introduce a new product
The company will introduce innovative products to attract more customers.
The school plans to introduce new technology into its classrooms next year.
They decided to introduce a new member to the committee during the meeting.
She introduced the composer to the audience before the performance began.
The company will introduce its new smartphone at the conference next week.
The director hopes to introduce several talented young actors in his latest film.
Smartphones were first introduced to the market in the late 1990s.
The chef is introducing several innovative dishes to the restaurant's menu this season.
The company plans to introduce a new line of eco-friendly products next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – implement: put (a plan, decision, etc.) into effect
  • synonym – launch: to start or present something for the first time, especially a product
  • antonym – remove: to take something out from a place or environment
  • synonym – unveil: to reveal or present something to the public for the first time
