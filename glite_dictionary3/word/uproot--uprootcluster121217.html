to uproot

verb
force to leave homeland
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈrut /; / ʌpˈɹut / (AmE), / ʌpˈruːt /; / ʌpˈɹuːt / (BrE)
to remove people or things completely from their usual place or to destroy something deeply established

Examples

to uproot a family
Economic hardship can sometimes uproot entire communities, scattering residents far from their homeland.
She was uprooted from her village and forced to start over in a foreign country.
The civil war uprooted thousands of families from their ancestral homes.
Activists are working hard to uproot the causes of inequality in the community.
Decades-old customs were suddenly uprooted by rapid modernization in the country.
The new government set out to uproot corruption from every level of the administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – displace: to force someone to leave their home, especially because of war or disaster
  • synonym – eliminate: to remove or get rid of something
  • synonym – eradicate: to destroy or get rid of something completely
  • antonym – preserve: to keep something in its original state
