to inhere

verb
to exist essentially in something
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɪr / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪə / (BrE)
(of a quality or characteristic) to exist essentially or permanently in something.

Examples

to inhere in qualities
A sense of purpose inheres in every great work of art.
Moral responsibility does not inhere in objects themselves, but in how people use them.
The right to express oneself inheres in all individuals.

  • synonym – inherent: existing as a permanent or essential attribute
