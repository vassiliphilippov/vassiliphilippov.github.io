pithouse

noun
underground or semi-underground shelter
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɪtˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A shelter partly or wholly dug into the ground, with a roof, used for human habitation or for sheltering animals or livestock.

Examples

ancient pithouse remains
Many artifacts were discovered inside the remains of a pithouse on the site.
Some traditional communities still build a pithouse to shelter livestock from extreme weather.
The ancient people constructed a pithouse to stay warm during harsh winters.
