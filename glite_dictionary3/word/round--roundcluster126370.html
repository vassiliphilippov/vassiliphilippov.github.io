to round

verb
to attack or criticize sharply
Pronunciation: / raʊnd / (AmE), / raʊnd / (BrE)
to criticize or attack someone or something sharply in speech or writing, especially suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

to round on critics
He unexpectedly rounded on his teammates after their defeat, blaming them for the loss.
In her editorial, she rounded against the government's new education policy.
The senator rounded on his critics during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym – criticize: to express disapproval of someone or something
  • antonym – praise: to express approval or admiration of someone or something
