tempering

noun
moderation or moderation process
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something less severe or extreme; moderation.

Examples

tempering anger
Her tempering influence prevented the meeting from becoming confrontational.
The tempering of criticism with empathy can foster better communication.
Through careful tempering, the harsh policy was made more acceptable to the public.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intensification: the process of making something stronger or more severe
  • synonym – moderation: the process of making something less extreme
