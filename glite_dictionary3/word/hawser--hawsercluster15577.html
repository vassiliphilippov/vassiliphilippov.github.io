hawser

noun
thick nautical rope or cable
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔzər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːzə / (BrE)
a thick, strong rope or cable used for mooring or towing a ship

Examples

hawser for a ship
After years exposed to saltwater, the old hawser finally needed to be replaced.
The crew used a hawser to secure the ship to the dock during the storm.
They attached a hawser to the tugboat to tow the barge upriver.
