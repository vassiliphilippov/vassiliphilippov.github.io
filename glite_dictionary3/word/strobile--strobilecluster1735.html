strobile

noun
cone-shaped reproductive structure in plants
Pronunciation: / ˈstroʊ.baɪl / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊ.baɪl / (BrE)
A cone-shaped structure consisting of overlapping scales or bracts that bear ovules or spores, found in certain plants such as conifers and some ferns.

Examples

pine strobile
A mature strobile can disperse its spores over a wide area.
Botanists examined the strobile to study its reproductive cycle.
The pine tree produces a strobile containing its seeds.
