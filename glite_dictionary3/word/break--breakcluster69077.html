to break

verb
make obedient or trained
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to weaken, destroy, or make someone or something lose spirit, strength, or resolve

Examples

to break a wild horse
It took months to break the wild mustang, but eventually it accepted a rider.
Some companies try to break new employees with strict rules and long hours.
The coach's harsh methods were meant to break the team's rebellious attitude.
After several weeks of intense questioning, he finally broke and confessed.
Machines often break when pushed beyond their designed limits.
She tried hard not to break under the enormous pressure of the competition.
The harsh interrogation was intended to break the prisoner's spirit.
The loss of his family nearly broke him, but he eventually found the strength to recover.
Years of constant failure can break even the most determined individuals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crush: to completely defeat or destroy someone's spirit or hopes
  • synonym – demoralize: to cause someone to lose confidence or hope
  • synonym – discipline: to train someone to obey rules or a code of behavior
  • antonym – encourage: to inspire someone with confidence or hope, opposite of subduing
  • synonym – snap: to suddenly lose self-control under pressure
  • synonym – tame: to make an animal less wild and more obedient
  • antonym – withstand: to resist or not give way to pressure or stress
