unmasking

noun
exposure of a fraud or impostor
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of revealing the true character or identity of someone, especially when exposing deception or fraud

Examples

unmasking a criminal
After months of suspicion, the detective's careful investigation resulted in the unmasking of the impostor.
Her testimony was crucial in the unmasking of the corporate fraud.
The journalist's article led to the unmasking of the corrupt officials.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cover-up: an attempt to prevent the public from discovering information about a serious crime or mistake
  • synonym – exposure: the act of making something visible or revealing a secret, especially a wrongdoing
