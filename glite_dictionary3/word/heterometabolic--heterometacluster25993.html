heterometabolic

adjective
incomplete insect metamorphosis
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˌmɛtəˈbælɪk / (BrE)
(of an insect) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, with the nymph resembling the adult and no pupal stage present.

Examples

heterometabolic insect development
Grasshoppers are heterometabolic insects, developing through egg, nymph, and adult stages without a pupal phase.
Mayflies display heterometabolic development, meaning juveniles are similar in form to the adults.
Unlike butterflies, heterometabolic insects skip the pupal stage entirely.

Related Terms

  • antonym – holometabolic: undergoing complete metamorphosis with distinct larval, pupal, and adult stages
