wrangler

noun
person who prepares and cleans data
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋɡlɚ / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡlə / (BrE)
(in data science and analytics) a person who collects, cleans, transforms, and organizes raw or messy data to make it suitable for analysis.

Examples

data wrangler
The company hired a wrangler to preprocess and clean the large consumer dataset before analysis.
As a wrangler, she spent the morning merging tables and fixing inconsistent values.
Our team of wranglers rebuilt the pipeline to automate messy data extraction tasks nightly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – data cleaning: the process of detecting and correcting (or removing) corrupt or inaccurate records from a dataset; a core task of data wrangling.
  • synonym – data engineer: a professional who designs and builds systems for collecting, storing, and processing data; role often overlaps with data wrangling.
