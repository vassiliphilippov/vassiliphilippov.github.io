curtilage

noun
enclosed land around a house or building
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜrt̬ɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the land immediately surrounding a house or building, often enclosed, considered as forming one enclosure with it

Examples

curtilage of a house
Local regulations prevent building any new structures within the curtilage of a listed house.
The court had to determine whether the garage was within the curtilage of the home.
The garden was considered part of the curtilage and was therefore private property.
