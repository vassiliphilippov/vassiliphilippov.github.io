spirant

adjective
fricative speech sound
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərənt / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced by forcing air through a narrow channel, leading to audible friction; fricative.

Examples

spirant sound type
In English, the spirant sounds include 'f', 'v', 's', and 'z'.
Some children initially replace spirant consonants with stops in early speech development.
The transition from a stop to a spirant is called spirantization in phonology.
