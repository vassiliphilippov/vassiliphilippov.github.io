Cockaigne

noun
mythical land of luxury and ease
Pronunciation: / kɑˈkeɪn / (AmE), / kɒˈkeɪn / (BrE)
(in medieval legend) an imaginary land of great luxury, ease, and idleness, where physical comforts and pleasures are always at hand.

Examples

land of Cockaigne
In medieval tales, the peasants dreamed of Cockaigne, where rivers ran with wine and food never ran out.
Scholars study references to Cockaigne to understand medieval attitudes toward wealth and desire.
The poem describes a journey to Cockaigne, an idyllic realm of endless feasting and leisure.
