to truss

verb
to support structurally, usually with beams
Pronunciation: / trʌs / (AmE), / trʌs / (BrE)
to support or reinforce a structure by using a framework of beams or bars

Examples

to truss a roof
Engineers will truss the new bridge to ensure its stability during storms.
The old barn was trussed with heavy timbers to prevent collapse.
To increase the span, the roof must be properly trussed using steel supports.

  • synonym – reinforce: to strengthen or support with additional material
