flux

noun
field lines in physics, especially magnets
Pronunciation: / flʌks / (AmE), / flʌks / (BrE)
the lines of force in a field, especially the magnetic or electric field surrounding a permanent magnet or a moving charged particle

Examples

magnetic flux lines
Engineers measured the magnetic flux to design the new motor.
In a simple bar magnet, the flux flows from the north to the south pole outside the magnet.
The strength of the flux around the coil affects the induced current.

Related Terms

  • synonym – field: the region influenced by a particular force, such as magnetic or electric
