Bacteroides

noun
genus of gut bacteria
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɛrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / bækˈtɛə.rɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
A genus of Gram-negative, rod-shaped, anaerobic bacteria that do not produce endospores or pigment, commonly found in the intestines of humans and animals.

Examples

Bacteroides species are among the most abundant bacteria in the human gut microbiome.
