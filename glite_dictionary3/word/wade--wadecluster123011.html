to wade

verb
walk through shallow water
Pronunciation: / weɪd / (AmE), / weɪd / (BrE)
to walk through water or another substance that is not deep, especially with effort

Examples

to wade through water
She waded into the pond to rescue the ducklings.
The children enjoyed wading in the shallow water at the beach.
We had to wade through the stream to reach the picnic site.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ford: to cross (a river or stream) at a shallow place
