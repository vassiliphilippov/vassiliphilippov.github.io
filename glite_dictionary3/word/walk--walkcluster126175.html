walk

noun
baseball: advance to first base after four balls
Pronunciation: / wɑk / (AmE), / wɔːk / (BrE)
(Baseball) an award to a batter of first base after receiving four balls outside the strike zone during an at-bat.

Examples

walk in baseball
The batter earned a walk after the pitcher threw four consecutive balls.
The team's strategy resulted in more walks than hits during the series.
With two outs and the bases loaded, he drew a crucial walk to tie the game.
