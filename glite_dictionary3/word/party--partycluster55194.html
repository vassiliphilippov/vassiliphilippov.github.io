party

noun
person or group in a legal case
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a person or group involved in a legal proceeding as a participant, such as a plaintiff or defendant

Examples

party to a contract
Each party must submit evidence to support their case.
The party of the second part agrees to the conditions stated above.
The contract was signed by both parties involved in the dispute.

  • synonym – litigant: a person involved in a lawsuit
  • degree lower – plaintiff: the party who initiates a lawsuit
