to twinkle

verb
shine intermittently; flash or glitter
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to shine or emit light with a flickering or sparkling effect

Examples

to twinkle like stars
She watched as his eyes twinkled with amusement.
The Christmas lights twinkled all along the street.
The stars twinkle brightly in the night sky.
Holiday lights twinkling in the trees made the street look magical.
She noticed how the sunlight twinkled on the surface of the lake.
The stars twinkle brightly on a clear winter night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flash: shine with a sudden, brief light
  • synonym – glimmer: to shine with a weak or unsteady light
  • synonym – glitter: shine with bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • degree higher – shine: to produce or reflect light
  • synonym – sparkle: to shine with flashes of light
