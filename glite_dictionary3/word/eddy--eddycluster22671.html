eddy

noun
a small whirlpool or whirlwind
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdi / (AmE), / ˈɛdi / (BrE)
a small whirlpool or whirlwind formed when a fluid current doubles back on itself

Examples

a swirling eddy
Dust rose in an eddy as the wind whipped through the alley.
Leaves spun in a tiny eddy at the edge of the pond.
The canoe drifted dangerously close to an eddy beneath the bridge.
