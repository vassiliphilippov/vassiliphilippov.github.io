adenosine

noun
nucleoside component of nucleic acids
Pronunciation: / əˈdɛnəˌsin / (AmE), / əˈdiːnəˌsiːn / (BrE)
a nucleoside formed by the combination of adenine and ribose, found in all living cells as a component of nucleic acids and important biomolecules such as ATP and ADP

Examples

cellular adenosine function
Adenosine is an essential nucleoside in many cellular processes, especially those involving energy transfer.
During strenuous exercise, levels of adenosine in the blood may increase, causing fatigue.
Researchers are studying how adenosine regulates sleep and wakefulness in the human brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ATP: adenosine triphosphate, an energy-carrying molecule
  • synonym – adenine: a purine base found in DNA and RNA
