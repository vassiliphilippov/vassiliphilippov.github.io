Thespis

proper noun
originator of Greek tragedy, Greek poet
Pronunciation: / ˈθɛs.pɪs / (AmE), / ˈθɛs.pɪs / (BrE)
A Greek poet of the sixth century BC, traditionally regarded as the originator of Greek tragedy.

Examples

Thespis created tragedy
According to ancient sources, Thespis was the first person to appear on stage as an actor.
Many scholars credit Thespis with revolutionizing Greek theater by introducing dialogue.
Theatrical performances that honor Thespis are sometimes called Thespian in tradition.
