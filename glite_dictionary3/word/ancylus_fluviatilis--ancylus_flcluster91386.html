Ancylus fluviatilis

noun
minute conical freshwater snail
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.sɪ.ləs fluː.vi.əˈtaɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈæn.sɪ.ləs fluː.vɪ.ˈa.tɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A minute, conical freshwater snail that superficially resembles a limpet and lives on freshwater plants.

Examples

Ancylus fluviatilis on rocks
Ancylus fluviatilis is commonly found on rocks and aquatic plants in clean, fast-flowing streams.
Freshwater biologists study the distribution of Ancylus fluviatilis to monitor ecosystem health.
The limpet-like appearance of Ancylus fluviatilis often leads to confusion with true marine limpets.
