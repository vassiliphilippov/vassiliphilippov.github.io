migratory

adjective
habitually moving from place to place
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪɡrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / mʌɪˈɡreɪtəri / (BrE)
(of a person or group) habitually moving from place to place, especially to find seasonal work.

Examples

migratory workers travel
During the harvest season, many migratory workers come to the region for temporary jobs.
Farmers depend on migratory labor to pick crops at the right time.
The migratory lifestyle can be challenging for families with young children.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nomadic: wandering from place to place without a fixed home
  • antonym – settled: having a permanent place of residence
