phosphorescence

noun
persistent emission of light after excitation
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːsfəˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəˈrɛsəns / (BrE)
the emission of light by a substance that continues after the source of excitation has been removed

Examples

phosphorescence in minerals
Certain minerals display phosphorescence when exposed to ultraviolet light, glowing after the light source is removed.
The glow from the jellyfish is an example of phosphorescence in nature.
