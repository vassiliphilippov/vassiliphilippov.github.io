to totalise

verb
combine into a total or whole
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊ.t̬ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.tə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make into a total; to combine or sum up different parts into a single entity or concept

Examples

to totalise different ideas
Can we totalise these different perspectives into one coherent philosophy?
It is difficult to totalise the data when the sources are so varied.
The theory seeks to totalise individual experiences into a unified system.
