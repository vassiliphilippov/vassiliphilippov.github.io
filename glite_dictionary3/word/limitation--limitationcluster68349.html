limitation

noun
legal time limit for bringing a claim
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) A legally defined period of time after which legal actions or claims cannot be brought forward in court.

Examples

limitation period law
Due to the limitation in the contract, any disputes must be raised within two years.
The court dismissed the case because the limitation period had expired.
You should check the limitation period before filing a lawsuit to ensure your claim is valid.

Related Terms

  • synonym – statute of limitations: a law prescribing the time limit for legal actions
