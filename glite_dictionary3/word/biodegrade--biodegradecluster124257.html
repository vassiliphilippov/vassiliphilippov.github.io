to biodegrade

verb
break down naturally by organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊdɪˈɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪəʊdɪˈɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) to be broken down naturally by living organisms, especially bacteria, into harmless compounds.

Examples

to biodegrade in soil
Many types of packaging are designed to biodegrade quickly in landfills.
Not all plastic containers will naturally biodegrade in the environment.
The chemical is safe because it can biodegrade in a matter of weeks.
