grazed

adjective
scraped or lightly injured (skin/surface)
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪzd / (AmE), / ɡreɪzd / (BrE)
(of skin or a surface) scraped or lightly injured in passing, especially so the top layer is damaged but not deeply cut.

Examples

grazed knee
After falling off his bike, he looked down at his grazed knees and winced.
She wore a bandage on her grazed hand from accidentally scraping it against the brick wall.
The football player returned to the field with a heavily grazed elbow after a rough tackle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abraded: worn away or scraped off, typically of a surface
  • degree higher – cut: having a wound made by something sharp (deeper than grazed)
  • synonym – scraped: having surface layers removed by rubbing or abrasion
