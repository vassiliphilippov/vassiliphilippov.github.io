to canton

verb
divide into administrative districts (cantons)
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɒn / (BrE)
(of a country or region) to divide or be divided into cantons (administrative districts).

Examples

to canton a region
Debates continue about whether to canton the province along linguistic lines.
Following the civil war, leaders decided to canton the territory to better administer resources.
The nation was cantoned into several autonomous regions, each with its own local government.

  • synonym – partition: to divide into parts or sections
