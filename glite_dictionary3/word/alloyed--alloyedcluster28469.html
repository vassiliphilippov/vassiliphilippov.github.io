alloyed

adjective
combined with other metals
Pronunciation: / əˈlɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈlɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a metal) combined with one or more other elements to improve or alter its properties.

Examples

alloyed for strength
By using alloyed copper, the manufacturer enhanced the product's conductivity.
Coins are often made from alloyed metals to prevent corrosion.
The alloyed steel was chosen for its strength and durability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unalloyed: not combined with other metals or elements
