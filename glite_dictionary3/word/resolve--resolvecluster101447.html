to resolve

verb
decide or settle after discussion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
to settle, solve, or bring a problem, dispute, or question to a conclusion

Examples

to resolve a problem
After hours of negotiation, the two companies finally resolved their differences.
The committee will meet next week to resolve the issue.
We discussed the matter thoroughly and eventually resolved to take action.
Can you resolve this puzzle before the timer runs out?
It took days to resolve all of the technical issues with the new software.
The scientist was able to resolve the complex equation after hours of work.
After several hours of discussion, the committee finally resolved the issue.
The two companies were able to resolve their differences without going to court.
We hope to resolve this matter quickly and fairly.

  • synonym – answer: to find a solution, reply, or result
  • synonym – decide: to make a choice or come to a conclusion
  • antonym – prolong: to make something last longer; to delay resolution
  • synonym – settle: to resolve or reach an agreement about (an argument or problem)
  • synonym – solve: to find the answer to a problem or question
