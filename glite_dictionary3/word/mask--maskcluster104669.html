to mask

verb
apply a bitwise or image selection
Pronunciation: / mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
(in computing) to apply a mask to data or an image, using a bitwise or pixel-wise operation to select, hide, or protect parts of the data.

Examples

to mask information digitally
The software will mask customer IDs before exporting the dataset for testing.
She masked the background pixels to keep only the subject for automated analysis.
During preprocessing they masked noisy channels so the model trained on clean inputs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bitwise AND: an operation often used to apply a binary mask to select bits in a value
  • synonym – filter: to remove or select parts of data according to specified criteria
