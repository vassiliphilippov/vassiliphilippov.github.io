fording

noun
crossing a shallow river or stream
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of crossing a stream or river at a shallow place, typically by wading, driving, or riding through it

Examples

fording a river
Before the bridge was built, fording was the only way to cross to the other side.
During the journey, several vehicles became stuck while fording the swollen river.
The hikers spent hours fording icy mountain streams on their way to the campsite.
