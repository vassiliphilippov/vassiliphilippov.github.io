pythium

noun
a genus of plant-pathogenic fungi
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪθiəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪθiəm / (BrE)
Any fungus of the genus Pythium, many species of which are pathogenic to plants and cause root rot and related diseases.

Examples

pythium root rot
Preventing pythium outbreaks is essential for maintaining healthy greenhouse plants.
Scientists studied pythium under the microscope to understand how it caused root rot in seedlings.
The farmers lost much of their crop to pythium infection during the unusually wet season.
