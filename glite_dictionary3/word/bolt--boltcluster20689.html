to bolt

verb
leave suddenly and quickly
Pronunciation: / boʊlt / (AmE), / bəʊlt / (BrE)
to leave suddenly and quickly, especially in order to escape

Examples

to bolt from the room
Frightened by the sudden noise, she bolted from the room.
The dog bolted out the gate as soon as it opened.
When the fire alarm sounded, everyone bolted for the exit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flee: to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary
  • synonym – run: to move swiftly on foot, often to escape or leave quickly
