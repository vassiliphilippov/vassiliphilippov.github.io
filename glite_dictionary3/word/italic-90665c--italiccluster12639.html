italic

noun
handwriting with right-slanting letters
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
a style of writing or typeface in which the letters slant to the right, often used for emphasis or distinction

Examples

italic handwriting style
Learning italic can improve the legibility of your handwriting.
She practiced her italic until her slanted letters looked elegant.
The invitation was written entirely in italic, making it look formal.
Please print the book titles in italic for the bibliography.
Scientific names are traditionally written in italic in academic articles.
The word was set in italic to emphasize its importance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – italic: typeface with slanted letters for emphasis
  • synonym – oblique: style of typeface similar to italic but sloped without script features
  • antonym – roman: upright style of typeface, as opposed to italic
