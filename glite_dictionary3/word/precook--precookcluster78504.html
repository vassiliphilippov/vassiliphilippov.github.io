to precook

verb
to cook in advance, before final preparation
Pronunciation: / priˈkʊk / (AmE), / priˈkʊk / (BrE)
to cook food partially or completely in advance, so that final preparation takes less time

Examples

to precook pasta
Many instant meals only require heating because the ingredients are already precooked.
She spent Sunday afternoon precooking several dishes for the week ahead.
You should precook the chicken before adding it to the casserole.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parboil: to partially boil food before further cooking
