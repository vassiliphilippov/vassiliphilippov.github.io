intellectualization

noun
defense mechanism using reasoning to avoid emotion
Pronunciation: / ɪn.t̬əlˌɛk.tʃu.əl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪn.təˌlɛk.tʃu.ə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(psychology) The use of reasoning or logic to avoid confronting emotional stress or internal conflict.

Examples

intellectualization of feelings
By relying on intellectualization, he could discuss his trauma without ever addressing how it affected him.
During therapy, she recognized her habit of intellectualization as a way to avoid dealing with painful feelings.
The professor’s tendency toward intellectualization made it difficult for him to connect emotionally with his students.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rationalization: using logical explanations to justify behaviors or feelings
