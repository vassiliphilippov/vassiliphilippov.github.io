valve

noun
a flap controlling fluid flow in organs
Pronunciation: / vælv / (AmE), / vɑːlv / (BrE)
a structure in a hollow organ, such as the heart, that acts as a flap to allow fluid to flow in only one direction

Examples

heart valve problem
Blood flows through the heart in a single direction thanks to the valve structure.
Doctors replaced the damaged heart valve during surgery.
When the mitral valve fails, blood may leak backward into the atrium.
