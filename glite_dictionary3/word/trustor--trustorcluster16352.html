trustor

noun
person who creates a trust
Pronunciation: / trʌsˈtɔr / (AmE), / trʌsˈtɔː / (BrE)
(law) a person who creates a trust by transferring property to a trustee for the benefit of a beneficiary.

Examples

trustor signs trust
Both the trustor and the trustee must sign the document to make the trust legally binding.
It is essential that the trustor clearly specifies the powers granted to the trustee.
The trustor transferred ownership of the property to the trustee under the terms of the agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – settlor: person who settles property on a trust (synonym of trustor)
