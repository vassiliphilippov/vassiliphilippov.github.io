Agrobacterium tumefaciens

noun
plant-pathogenic bacterium
Pronunciation: / ˌæɡroʊbækˈtɪəriəm tjuːməˈfeɪʃiːnz / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəʊbækˈtɪəriəm tjuːməˈfeɪsiːnz / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes crown gall disease in plants by transferring part of its DNA to the plant's genome

Examples

Agrobacterium tumefaciens infects plants
Agrobacterium tumefaciens is frequently used in genetic engineering to introduce new genes into plants.
Researchers study Agrobacterium tumefaciens to better understand how bacteria transfer DNA to other organisms.
The presence of Agrobacterium tumefaciens can cause tumors to form on the roots and stems of infected plants.
