to snafu

verb
to cause confusion or chaos
Pronunciation: / snæˈfu / (AmE), / snæˈfuː / (BrE)
to cause to be in a state of complete confusion or disarray, especially as a result of error or mismanagement

Examples

to snafu the plan
If we don’t communicate clearly, we could snafu the whole operation.
The delivery was snafuing our plans for the grand opening.
The last-minute changes completely snafued the project schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym – muddle: to bring into a disordered or confusing state
  • antonym – organize: to arrange or order systematically
