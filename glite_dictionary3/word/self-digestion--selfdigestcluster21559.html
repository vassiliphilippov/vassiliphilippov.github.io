self-digestion

noun
breakdown of tissue by internal processes
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf dɪˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a plant or animal's own tissues are broken down by internal mechanisms, especially enzymes produced within the organism.

Examples

postmortem self-digestion
After death, self-digestion can cause rapid tissue breakdown in the body.
Damage to the pancreas can lead to self-digestion of the organ's own tissues.
The process of self-digestion helps seeds use stored nutrients during germination.

Related Terms

  • synonym – autolysis: the destruction of cells or tissues by their own enzymes
