glamorization

noun
making something appear more attractive
Pronunciation: / ˌɡlæmɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡlæmərʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something or someone appear more attractive or exciting, often in a superficial or unrealistic way

Examples

the glamorization of crime
Many documentaries seek to avoid the glamorization of criminal activity.
The glamorization of violence in movies can have a negative impact on viewers.
The fashion industry's glamorization of unrealistic beauty standards has been widely criticized.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glamorize: to make something more attractive or exciting than it really is
  • synonym – idealization: the act of regarding something as perfect or better than reality
  • antonym – realism: the representation of things as they actually are
