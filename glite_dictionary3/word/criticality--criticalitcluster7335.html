criticality

noun
state of being critical; self-sustaining nuclear reaction
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪtɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌkrɪtɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being critical; especially the point at which a nuclear reaction becomes self-sustaining

Examples

nuclear criticality event
Engineers closely monitored the criticality tests during the experiment.
Reaching criticality is a crucial step in operating a nuclear power plant safely.
The reactor reached criticality and began to sustain a chain reaction.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – subcritical: having a level below that required for a nuclear reaction to be self-sustaining
  • degree higher – supercritical: having a level above that required for a nuclear reaction to be self-sustaining
