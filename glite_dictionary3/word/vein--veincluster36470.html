vein

noun
blood vessel carrying blood to the heart
Pronunciation: / veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
a blood vessel that carries blood towards the heart, as distinct from an artery

Examples

vein in the arm
Oxygen-poor blood returns to the heart through the veins.
Some people have prominent veins visible on their hands.
The doctor inserted the needle gently into the vein in her arm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – artery: a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
