belles-lettres

noun
aesthetic or artistic literary writing
Pronunciation: / bɛl ˈlɛtrə(z) / (AmE), / bɛl ˈlɛtrə(z) / (BrE)
Literary works written and valued for their aesthetic, artistic qualities rather than for purely informative or practical purposes.

Examples

classic belles-lettres works
Her library is filled with works of belles-lettres, from classic novels to essays admired for their style.
Many critics believe that the study of belles-lettres helps develop a deeper appreciation of language.
The professor specialized in belles-lettres, teaching courses on poetry and literary essays.
