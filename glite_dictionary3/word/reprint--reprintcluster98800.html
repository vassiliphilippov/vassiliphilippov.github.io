to reprint

verb
print again; produce a new edition
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈprɪnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to print (a book, article, or other published material) again, especially in a new edition or after the original run has sold out

Examples

to reprint an article
If this journal article is accepted, we may reprint it in a special anniversary issue.
Many authors hope their out-of-print books will one day be reprinted for new audiences.
The publisher decided to reprint the classic novel after its popularity surged online.

Related Terms

  • synonym – print: to produce books, newspapers, or other materials by pressing inked type or plates onto paper
