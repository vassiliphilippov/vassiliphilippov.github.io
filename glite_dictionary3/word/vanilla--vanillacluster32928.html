vanilla

adjective
plain or basic; lacking extras
Pronunciation: / vəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
used to describe something that is plain, ordinary, or lacking special features or extras; basic.

Examples

vanilla version
His proposal was criticized for being too vanilla and lacking innovation.
Many consumers prefer the vanilla model because it is more affordable and easier to use.
The software comes in both a vanilla version and an advanced edition with extra features.

Related Terms

  • synonym – basic: simple or fundamental, without extra features
  • antonym – fancy: elaborate or with many extra features
  • synonym – plain: not decorated or elaborate; simple
