detritus

noun
fragments or remains of something destroyed
Pronunciation: / dəˈtraɪdəs /; / dəˈtraɪtəs / (AmE), / dɪˈtraɪtəs / (BrE)
loose fragments, debris, or waste left after something is destroyed, broken down, or worn away (such as rocks, silt, or other remains)

Examples

detritus from destruction
After the landslide, the valley was covered in detritus from the collapsed hillside.
Bits of plastic and other detritus littered the beach after the storm.
The archaeologists sifted through the detritus of the ancient settlement to find clues about daily life.
A layer of detritus covered the forest floor after the heavy rain.
Geologists studied the detritus at the base of the cliffs to analyze the effects of erosion.
The river carried detritus downstream, depositing it along the banks.
