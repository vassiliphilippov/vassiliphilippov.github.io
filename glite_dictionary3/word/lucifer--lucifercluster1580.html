Lucifer

proper noun
chief spirit of evil; adversary of God
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsɪfər / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪfə / (BrE)
in Judeo-Christian and Islamic belief, the chief spirit of evil and adversary of God, also known as Satan or the Devil, regarded as the tempter of humankind and ruler of Hell

Examples

Lucifer tempts humans
In literature, Lucifer is often portrayed as a cunning tempter or fallen angel.
Many religious texts describe Lucifer as the rebellious angel cast down from heaven.
The figure of Lucifer has inspired countless interpretations in art and popular culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – God: the principal deity in monotheistic traditions; opposite of evil
  • synonym – Satan: the chief evil spirit, figure representing the Devil
