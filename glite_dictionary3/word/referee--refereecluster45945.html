referee

noun
person assessing manuscripts for publication
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
a person who reads manuscripts and assesses their suitability for publication, as in academic or scientific journals

Examples

referee a journal article
As a referee, she provided detailed feedback on the manuscript's methodology and clarity.
The editor thanked the anonymous referee for his constructive comments.
The journal requires each paper to be evaluated by at least one referee.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reviewer: person who assesses the quality of written work
