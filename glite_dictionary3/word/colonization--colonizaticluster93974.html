colonization

noun
the act of establishing colonies
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of establishing control over the indigenous people of an area and settling it as a colony

Examples

colonization of new lands
European colonization changed the political and cultural landscape of many continents.
Space agencies are discussing the potential colonization of Mars in the future.
The colonization of Australia had a profound impact on the indigenous population.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decolonization: the act of withdrawing from a colony, leaving it independent
  • synonym – settlement: the process of establishing a new community in a new place
