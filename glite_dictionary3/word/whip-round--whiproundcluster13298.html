whip-round

noun
informal collection of money (UK)
Pronunciation: / wɪp ɹaʊnd / (AmE), / wɪp raʊnd / (BrE)
(British) an informal collection of money from a group of people, typically for a charitable or benevolent purpose.

Examples

whip-round for charity
After the meeting, someone suggested a whip-round for charity.
There was a whip-round at the pub to help the injured player.
We organised a whip-round to buy a farewell gift for our colleague.
