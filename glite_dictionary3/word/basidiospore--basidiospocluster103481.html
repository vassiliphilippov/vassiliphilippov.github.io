basidiospore

noun
sexually produced fungal spore
Pronunciation: / bəˈsɪdi.oʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / bəˈsɪdɪəˌspɔː / (BrE)
A spore produced sexually by certain fungi, typically borne on a basidium.

Examples

basidiospore formation in fungi
After germination, the basidiospore develops into a new fungal mycelium.
Some fungi produce millions of basidiospores to ensure successful dispersal and colonization.
The basidiospore is released from the basidium during the reproductive cycle of many mushrooms.
