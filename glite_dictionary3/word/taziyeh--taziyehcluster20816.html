Ta'ziyeh

noun
Iranian Shi'a religious drama
Pronunciation: / tɑːˈziːjeɪ / (AmE), / tɑːˈziːjeɪ / (BrE)
a traditional Iranian religious drama, performed as a theatrical pageant, that depicts events from the Battle of Kerbala and expresses themes of religious passion and mourning, especially in Shi'a Islam

Examples

Ta'ziyeh performance
Each year, thousands gather to watch the Ta'ziyeh performances commemorating the martyrdom of Imam Hussein.
Scholars have studied the Ta'ziyeh as a unique form of religious theatre in Iran.
The emotional impact of the Ta'ziyeh is heightened by its powerful music and stylized acting.
