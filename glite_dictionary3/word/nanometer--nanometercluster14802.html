nanometer

noun
unit of length, one billionth of a meter
Pronunciation: / ˈnænəˌmitɚ / (AmE), / ˈnænəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (1 × 10⁻⁹ meters), commonly used in scientific contexts.

Examples

one nanometer wide
Modern computer chips have transistors only a few nanometers wide.
Scientists use nanometer-scale measurements to study tiny particles and biological structures.
The wavelength of visible light ranges from about 400 to 700 nanometers.
