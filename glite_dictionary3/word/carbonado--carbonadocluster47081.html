carbonado

noun
inferior, dark industrial diamond
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrbəˈnɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbənˈɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A dark, opaque variety of diamond, usually of lower quality, used mainly for industrial purposes such as drilling and polishing.

Examples

industrial carbonado diamond
The mining company exported carbonado for use in the manufacturing of drill bits.
They replaced the standard drill tip with a carbonado to cut through the hard rock.
Unlike clear gemstones, carbonado is valued for its toughness in industrial applications.
