to gape

verb
stare in amazement, mouth open
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
to look at something with your mouth open in surprise or wonder

Examples

to gape at wonders
The children could only gape at the magician's amazing tricks.
Tourists gaped at the towering skyscrapers as they entered the city.
We stood there gaping in disbelief at the sudden snowfall in June.
