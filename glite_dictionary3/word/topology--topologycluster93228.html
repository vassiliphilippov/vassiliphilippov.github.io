topology

noun
study of body regions in anatomy
Pronunciation: / təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of anatomical regions of the body and the relationships among structures within those regions, such as muscles, nerves, and arteries

Examples

topology of the body
A detailed knowledge of topology helps surgeons plan complex operations more safely.
His research focuses on the topology of the brain, mapping out the connections between various anatomical structures.
Medical students often study topology to understand how different organs relate to each other in a given region.
