to triple-tongue

verb
play fast notes on a wind instrument using rapid tonguing
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪp.əlˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.əlˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
to play fast notes on a wind instrument by rapidly articulating the tongue in a specific sequence, enabling quick, repeated tonguing of notes

Examples

to triple-tongue a fast passage
During the rehearsal, she triple-tongued the notes as the composer intended.
It takes practice to triple-tongue cleanly at such high speeds.
The flutist skillfully triple-tongued during the rapid passages of the concerto.
