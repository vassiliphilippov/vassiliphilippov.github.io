to cauterise

verb
make emotionally insensitive or deaden morals
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (BrE)
To make someone insensitive or emotionally hardened; to deaden feelings or morals.

Examples

to cauterise feelings
Repeated disappointments can cauterise a person's ability to trust others.
She feared that constant criticism might cauterise her enthusiasm.
Years of conflict had cauterised his sense of compassion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – harden: make or become more emotionally callous or less sensitive
  • antonym – soften: make or become less severe or more sensitive
