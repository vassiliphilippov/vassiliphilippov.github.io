nacre

noun
the iridescent layer inside some shells
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪkər / (AmE), / ˈneɪkə / (BrE)
the hard, smooth, iridescent inner layer of the shell of some mollusks, used in making pearls and mother-of-pearl objects

Examples

nacre in shells
Artisans carve delicate buttons out of nacre obtained from mollusk shells.
Pearl jewelry often displays the beautiful iridescence of nacre.
The nacre lining the oyster shell shimmered in the sunlight.
