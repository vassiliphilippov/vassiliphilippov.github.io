to sphacelate

verb
(of tissue) undergo necrosis
Pronunciation: / ˈsfækəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsfækəˌleɪt / (BrE)
(of tissue) to undergo necrosis; to become gangrenous and die.

Examples

to sphacelate tissue
If untreated, the infected tissue may sphacelate and require surgical removal.
Pathologists observed that the damaged skin had sphacelated within a few days.
The limb began to sphacelate after the loss of blood supply.
