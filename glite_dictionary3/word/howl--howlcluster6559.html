to howl

verb
make a loud, mournful noise (wind, water, vehicles)
Pronunciation: / haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
(of wind, water, or vehicles) to make a long, loud, mournful sound; to produce a noise similar to an animal's howl.

Examples

the wind howls outside
All night long, the ocean waves howled against the rocky cliffs.
During the storm, the wind began to howl through the narrow streets.
The train howled as it sped over the mountain pass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – roar: to make a loud, deep noise, as of a lion or water
  • antonym – whisper: to make a soft, rustling noise, as of wind
