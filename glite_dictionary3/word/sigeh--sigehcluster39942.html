sigeh

noun
temporary marriage in Shiite Islam
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːɡeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsiːɡeɪ / (BrE)
In Shiite Islam, a form of temporary marriage permitted in Iran, allowing a couple to stipulate terms and duration, which can range from a few minutes to 99 years.

Examples

sigeh marriage contract
In some Iranian cities, couples may choose a sigeh arrangement rather than a traditional marriage.
She entered into a sigeh contract for a fixed period as allowed by local religious authorities.
The practice of sigeh is often discussed in debates about Iranian family law.
