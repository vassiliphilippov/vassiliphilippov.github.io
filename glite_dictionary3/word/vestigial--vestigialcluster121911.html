vestigial

adjective
small remnant of a formerly functional part or organ
Pronunciation: / vɛˈstɪdʒiəl / (AmE), / vɛˈstɪdʒɪəl / (BrE)
(of a part or organ) remaining as a small or incomplete remnant of something that was once well developed or functioning in the ancestors of an organism.

Examples

vestigial tail
Some snakes have vestigial legs, remnants from their evolutionary ancestors.
The appendix is often considered a vestigial organ in humans.
The whale's pelvis is a vestigial structure with no apparent function.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rudimentary: not fully developed or basic in form
