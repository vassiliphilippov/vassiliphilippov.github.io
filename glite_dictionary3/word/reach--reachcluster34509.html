reach

noun
extent or limits of influence or effect
Pronunciation: / riːtʃ / (AmE), / riːtʃ / (BrE)
the distance, area, or extent within which something can be achieved, influenced, or has power (including ability, influence, or media exposure)

Examples

global reach
New policies may extend the reach of healthcare to more people.
The mountain cabin was out of the reach of cellphone signals.
The virus is spreading beyond the reach of current containment measures.
Most people consider space travel to be far beyond the reach of ordinary citizens.
She stretched the reach of her influence to include the entire company.
Success in that field is within the reach of those willing to work hard.
International sanctions limited the reach of the organization’s activities.
Modern technology has greatly increased the reach of educational programs.
The new policy extends the reach of the government into rural areas.
