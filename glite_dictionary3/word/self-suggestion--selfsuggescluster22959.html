self-suggestion

noun
mental self-influence for self-improvement
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf səˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf səˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
the practice of influencing one's own thoughts, feelings, or behavior by mentally repeating positive ideas, often as a method of self-improvement

Examples

positive self-suggestion
By practicing self-suggestion, she hoped to boost her self-confidence and overcome anxiety.
The technique of self-suggestion involves repeating positive phrases to oneself daily.
Émile Coué popularized self-suggestion as a method for achieving personal goals.
