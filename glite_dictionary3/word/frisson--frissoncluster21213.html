frisson

noun
sudden thrill or shiver of emotion
Pronunciation: / friˈsɑn / (AmE), / friˈsɒ̃ / (BrE)
A sudden strong feeling of excitement or fear, often mixed with pleasure; a thrilling shiver of emotion.

Examples

a frisson of excitement
A sudden frisson ran through the crowd when the lights flickered.
He spoke in a whisper that sent a frisson down her spine.
She felt a frisson of anticipation as the concert began.

  • synonym – chill: A feeling of coldness or sudden fear/shivering
  • synonym – thrill: A sudden feeling of excitement and pleasure
