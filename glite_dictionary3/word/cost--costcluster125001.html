cost

noun
value or worth of something, usually in money
Pronunciation: / kɑst / (AmE), / kɒst / (BrE)
the amount of money or resources needed to buy or do something

Examples

high cost
Before selling the car, he checked its current cost in the market.
She was surprised by the high cost of the antique vase at auction.
The cost of gold continues to rise due to inflation.
Before starting the project, make sure to calculate the total cost involved.
The cost of living in the city has risen dramatically in recent years.
There is always a cost to cutting corners, whether in money, time, or quality.

  • synonym – price: the amount of money expected, required, or given in payment for something
  • synonym – value: the monetary worth of something
