disorientation

noun
wild delusion, often drug-induced
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which someone experiences a wild delusion, especially one induced by a hallucinogenic drug

Examples

drug-induced disorientation
During the experiment, some participants reported vivid disorientation and altered perceptions of reality.
Prolonged use of the drug can lead to episodes of disorientation characterized by wild delusions.
The patient suffered from disorientation after ingesting the hallucinogenic mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delusion: a false belief or judgement about external reality
  • synonym – hallucination: an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
