debenture

noun
certificate acknowledging a debt
Pronunciation: / dɪˈbɛntʃɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈbɛntʃə / (BrE)
A certificate or document issued by a company or government acknowledging a debt and promising repayment with interest at a later date.

Examples

company debenture certificate
Holding a debenture means you are a creditor of the company.
The company raised capital by issuing debentures to investors.
Unlike shares, a debenture does not confer any ownership in the issuing firm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bond: a certificate issued by a government or company promising to repay borrowed money with interest
