otoscope

noun
medical instrument for ear examination
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊtəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəskəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument with a magnifying lens and light, used to examine the external ear canal and eardrum.

Examples

otoscope examination of ear
An otoscope allows the physician to view the eardrum clearly.
During the examination, she gently inserted the otoscope into his ear canal.
The doctor used an otoscope to check the child’s ear for infection.
