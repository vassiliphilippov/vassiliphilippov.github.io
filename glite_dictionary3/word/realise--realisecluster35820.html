to realise

verb
convert an asset into cash
Pronunciation: / ˈriːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to get money by selling something or from a business transaction

Examples

to realise a profit
She hopes to realise a substantial sum from the sale of her late uncle's property.
The company decided to realise some of its assets to raise funds.
The paintings were realised at auction for more than expected.
She hopes to realise enough money from the sale to pay off her debts.
The company managed to realise a significant profit last quarter.
Through careful investments, they realised a substantial income over the year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acquire: to buy or obtain an asset or property
  • synonym – earn: receive money as payment for work or investment
  • synonym – liquidate: to convert assets into cash, especially to pay debts
  • antonym – lose: fail to keep or achieve money, profit, or income
