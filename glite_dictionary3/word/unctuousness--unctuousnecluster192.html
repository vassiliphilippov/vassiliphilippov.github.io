unctuousness

noun
insincere or overly earnest behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈʌŋk.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʌŋk.tjʊ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of expressing insincere earnestness or excessive smoothness, often intended to persuade or impress

Examples

fake unctuousness in speech
He responded with such unctuousness that it was impossible to know if he was sincere.
Her unctuousness during the meeting made everyone question her true motives.
The politician's unctuousness was evident in his exaggerated praise for his hosts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sincerity: honesty and genuineness in expression or feeling
  • synonym – unctuous: excessively or insincerely flattering or earnest (adjective)
