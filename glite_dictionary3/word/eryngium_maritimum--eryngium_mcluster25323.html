Eryngium maritimum

noun
a species of sea holly (plant)
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɪndʒiəm məˈrɪtɪməm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪndʒiəm məˈrɪtɪməm / (BrE)
A European evergreen perennial plant of the parsley family, with twisted spiny leaves, commonly known as sea holly; its roots were once used as an aphrodisiac.
