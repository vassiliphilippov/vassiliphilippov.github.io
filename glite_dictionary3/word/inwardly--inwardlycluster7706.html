inwardly

adverb
privately; inside oneself
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnwərdli / (AmE), / ˈɪnwədli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to one's inner thoughts or feelings, rather than outwardly or visibly.

Examples

inwardly feel angry
Inwardly, she was raging, but she kept a calm expression on her face.
Although the group agreed, inwardly he disagreed with their decision.
He smiled at the news, but inwardly he felt anxious and uncertain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – outwardly: in a way that is apparent or shown to others
