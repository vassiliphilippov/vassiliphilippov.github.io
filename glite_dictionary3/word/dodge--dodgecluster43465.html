to dodge

verb
avoid answering or dealing with
Pronunciation: / dɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɒdʒ / (BrE)
to avoid answering a question, dealing with an issue, or fulfilling a responsibility, especially by being evasive or cunning

Examples

to dodge a question
She often dodges her responsibilities by shifting the blame to others.
The senator tried to dodge the question during the press conference.
Whenever his boss asks about the project, he tends to dodge the issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym – confront: to face up to and deal with a problem or difficult situation
  • synonym – evade: to escape or avoid by cleverness or trickery
