to monopolize

verb
to have exclusive control over something
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑː.pə.laɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒp.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to have or take complete control over something so that others cannot share or use it

Examples

to monopolize the conversation
He tends to monopolize the conversation, rarely letting others speak.
Large companies sometimes monopolize the technology sector, leaving little room for competition.
OPEC has often tried to monopolize the oil market.
After acquiring two competitors, the company monopolized the local grocery business.
During meetings, he tends to monopolize the conversation and not let others speak.
The large corporation tried to monopolize the entire telecommunications market.

Related Terms

  • synonym – control: to have power over something
  • synonym – dominate: to have a commanding influence or control over; synonym of monopolize
  • antonym – share: to have or use something jointly with others
