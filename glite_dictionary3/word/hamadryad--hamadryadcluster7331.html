hamadryad

noun
tree nymph in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˌhæməˈdraɪæd / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈdrʌɪad / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a nymph or spirit that lives in and is intimately connected to a particular tree, especially an oak.

Examples

ancient hamadryad spirit
According to legend, a hamadryad would die if her tree was cut down.
In mythology, every tree had its own guardian hamadryad.
The poet described a hamadryad emerging gracefully from the ancient oak.

  • synonym – dryad: a tree nymph in Greek mythology (not restricted to a particular tree)
