transudation

noun
slow passage of fluid through membranes
Pronunciation: / ˌtræn.sjuˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræn.sjʊˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which fluid slowly passes through a membrane or vessel wall, especially from blood vessels, due to pressure differences rather than inflammation.

Examples

transudation through a membrane
Doctors analyzed the fluid to determine whether it was caused by transudation or exudation.
During heart failure, transudation leads to accumulation of fluid in the lungs.
The presence of protein-poor fluid indicated that transudation rather than inflammation was occurring.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exudation: the process of oozing out fluid, typically as a result of inflammation
