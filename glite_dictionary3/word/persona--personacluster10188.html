persona

noun
outward personality or facade
Pronunciation: / pərˈsoʊnə / (AmE), / pəˈsəʊnə / (BrE)
(in Jungian psychology) the aspect of someone's character that is presented to or perceived by others, as opposed to their true self.

Examples

public persona
His cheerful persona hid feelings of anxiety and insecurity.
Many celebrities develop a persona that is quite different from their true selves.
She maintained a professional persona at work, but her friends knew she was much more relaxed outside the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym – facade: an outward appearance maintained to conceal a less pleasant reality
  • synonym – mask: a covering used to conceal the face, figuratively used for a facade
  • antonym – self: the true or inner identity of a person
