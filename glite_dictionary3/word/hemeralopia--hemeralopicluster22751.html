hemeralopia

noun
inability to see in bright light
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˌrəˈloʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌræˈləʊpiə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by difficulty seeing clearly in bright light.

Examples

hemeralopia in sunlight
Patients with hemeralopia often struggle to see outdoors on a sunny day.
Sunglasses may help individuals suffering from hemeralopia manage their symptoms.
The doctor diagnosed him with hemeralopia after he complained of vision problems in bright environments.
