to untune

verb
cause to lose composure
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtun / (AmE), / ʌnˈtjuːn / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose their composure or emotional stability

Examples

to untune the mind
Grief can untune a person's inner harmony, leaving them vulnerable and shaken.
It takes a great shock to untune her normally placid demeanor.
The sudden news was enough to untune even the calmest of minds.
