rime

noun
white deposit of ice crystals
Pronunciation: / raɪm / (AmE), / raɪm / (BrE)
A white, icy deposit formed by the rapid freezing of water droplets on cold surfaces, especially outdoors.

Examples

rime on branches
After the blizzard, a layer of rime coated the telephone wires.
The trees were covered with a delicate rime after the cold, foggy night.
We woke to find the windows glazed with rime, sparkling in the morning sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym – frost: a thin layer of ice formed on surfaces when water vapor freezes
  • synonym – hoarfrost: a type of frost that forms feathery white ice crystals
