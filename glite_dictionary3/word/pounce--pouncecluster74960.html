to pounce

verb
swoop down to attack or seize
Pronunciation: / paʊns / (AmE), / paʊns / (BrE)
to move suddenly down on something, especially in order to attack or seize it

Examples

to pounce on prey
Reporters pounced on the celebrity as soon as she left the building.
The cat waited silently before it pounced on the unsuspecting mouse.
The child watched the hawk pounce from the tree onto its prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym – swoop: to sweep or move rapidly downward through the air
