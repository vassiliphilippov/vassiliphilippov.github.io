catalase

noun
an enzyme that breaks down hydrogen peroxide
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˈkatəleɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme found in most living cells that catalyzes the decomposition of hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen

Examples

A deficiency in catalase activity can lead to the accumulation of toxic substances in the body.
Scientists often use catalase as a marker to study cellular metabolism.
