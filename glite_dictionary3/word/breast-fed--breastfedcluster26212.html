breast-fed

adjective
fed with milk from the mother's breast
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛstˌfɛd / (AmE), / ˈbrɛstˌfɛd / (BrE)
(of an infant) fed with milk from the mother's breast rather than from a bottle or other source.

Examples

breast-fed baby
A breast-fed child may have a stronger immune system.
Many doctors encourage mothers to have their babies breast-fed for the first six months.
She was proud that all three of her children were breast-fed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bottle-fed: (of an infant) fed with milk from a bottle rather than the mother's breast
