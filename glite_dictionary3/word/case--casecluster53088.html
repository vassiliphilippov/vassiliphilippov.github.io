case

noun
standard quantity in a container
Pronunciation: / keɪs / (AmE), / keɪs / (BrE)
A standard quantity of goods packaged together in a box or container, often used as a unit for selling or shipping.

Examples

case of water
He stacked another case of bottled water in the storeroom.
The supermarket sells soda by the case at a discount.
We ordered a case of wine for the party.
