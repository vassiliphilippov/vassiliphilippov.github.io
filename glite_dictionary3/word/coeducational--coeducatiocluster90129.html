coeducational

adjective
having both male and female students
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˌɛdʒuˈkeɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˌɛdjuˈkeɪʃənl̩ / (BrE)
(of a school or educational system) allowing or attended by both male and female students.

Examples

coeducational school
Many parents prefer coeducational schools because they believe they better prepare students for real-world interactions.
She transferred from a single-sex school to a coeducational one last year.
The university became coeducational in the 1960s, allowing women to enroll for the first time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – single-sex: (of a school) attended by members of only one sex
