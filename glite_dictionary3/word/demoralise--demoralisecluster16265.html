to demoralise

verb
corrupt morally or by intemperance
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɔːrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to corrupt someone morally, especially through indulgence in sensuality or intemperance

Examples

to demoralise young people
He warned that excessive wealth could demoralise even the most principled leaders.
It is dangerous to demoralise young people by encouraging reckless drinking and partying.
Some believed the decadent lifestyle would demoralise the entire community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrupt: to cause someone or something to become dishonest or immoral
  • antonym – uplift: to improve morally or spiritually
