Creole

noun
descendant of French ancestors in Louisiana
Pronunciation: / ˈkriː.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈkriːəʊl / (BrE)
(in the southern United States, especially Louisiana) a person descended from French ancestors, often with a distinct cultural and linguistic heritage.

Examples

a Creole person in Louisiana
As a Creole, she is proud of her French and Spanish ancestry.
Several generations of Creole families have lived in New Orleans since the eighteenth century.
The festival celebrates Creole traditions through music, food, and dance.
