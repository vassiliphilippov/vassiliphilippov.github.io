kalemia

noun
excessive potassium in the blood
Pronunciation: / kəˈliːmiə / (AmE), / kəˈliːmiə / (BrE)
the presence of an abnormal amount of potassium in the blood, especially in excess

Examples

kalemia in blood test
Doctors monitor electrolyte levels closely to detect kalemia or other imbalances early.
The patient was diagnosed with kalemia after blood tests revealed elevated potassium levels.
Untreated kalemia can lead to serious cardiac complications if not managed promptly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – hyperkalemia: an abnormally high potassium level in the blood
  • degree lower – hypokalemia: an abnormally low potassium level in the blood
