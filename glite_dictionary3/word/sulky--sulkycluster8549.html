sulky

adjective
sullen or moodily silent
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌlki / (AmE), / ˈsʌlki / (BrE)
Bad-tempered or silently moody and unsociable, especially as a result of disappointment or anger.

Examples

sulky child
After being told she couldn't go to the party, she became sulky and refused to speak.
He sat in the corner with a sulky expression, ignoring everyone around him.
It's hard to have a good time when someone is in a sulky mood.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cheerful: noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym – sullen: bad-tempered and sulky; gloomy
