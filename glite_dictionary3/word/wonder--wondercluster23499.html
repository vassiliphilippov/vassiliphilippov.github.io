to wonder

verb
wish to know; be curious
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʌndə / (BrE)
to wish to know something, to be curious, or to feel uncertain about something

Examples

to wonder why
Do you ever wonder why the sky is blue?
I often wonder what life is like in other countries.
She couldn't help but wonder if she had made the right decision.
I often wonder if I made the right decision moving to this city.
She wondered about the reasons for his sudden departure.
You might wonder whether you'll ever achieve your dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym – doubt: to feel uncertain or lack conviction
  • synonym – ponder: to think about or consider carefully
  • synonym – speculate: to form ideas without firm evidence
