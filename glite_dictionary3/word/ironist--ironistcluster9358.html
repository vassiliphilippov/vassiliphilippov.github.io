ironist

noun
person who uses irony or sarcasm
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪɚnɪst / (AmE), / ˈaɪərənɪst / (BrE)
a person who uses irony, sarcasm, or ridicule, especially in writing or speech, often to expose folly or criticize.

Examples

famous ironist writer
As an accomplished ironist, she often highlighted the absurdities of society in her columns.
Modern literature is full of ironist characters who mock traditional values.
The famous ironist delighted audiences with his clever, biting remarks.
