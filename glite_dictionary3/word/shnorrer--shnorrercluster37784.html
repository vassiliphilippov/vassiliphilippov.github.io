shnorrer

noun
person who habitually scrounges from others
Pronunciation: / ˈʃnɔːrər / (AmE), / ˈʃnɒrər / (BrE)
a person who habitually seeks to obtain something without paying or giving anything in return; a scrounger, especially one who exploits others' generosity

Examples

a shnorrer at parties
Don't be a shnorrer; contribute something to the group.
He's known as a shnorrer who always shows up empty-handed at parties.
The club quickly tired of the habitual shnorrer asking for free tickets.
