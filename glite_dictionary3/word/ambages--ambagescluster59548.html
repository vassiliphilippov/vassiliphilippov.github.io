ambages

noun
roundabout or mysterious ways
Pronunciation: / ˈæmˌbeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈæmˌbeɪdʒiːz / (BrE)
(archaic) Indirect or roundabout ways of speaking or acting; circumlocutions or enigmatic methods.

Examples

speak in ambages
The monk spoke in such ambages that his true intentions were never revealed.
Tired of legal ambages, the witness demanded a straightforward answer.
Victorian authors sometimes delighted in the ambages of diplomatic conversation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circumlocution: the use of many words where fewer would do, especially in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive
