cyrilla

noun
shrub/small tree with white flowers and colorful foliage
Pronunciation: / sɪˈrɪlə / (AmE), / saɪˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and Brazil, grown for its slender clusters of white flowers and orange or crimson foliage.
