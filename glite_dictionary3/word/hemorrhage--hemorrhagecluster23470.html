to hemorrhage

verb
to bleed heavily or uncontrollably
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛmɚˌɹɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɛmərɪdʒ / (BrE)
to lose blood heavily from a ruptured blood vessel, often in a serious or uncontrolled way

Examples

to hemorrhage badly
After the accident, he began to hemorrhage from a deep wound in his leg.
Doctors rushed to control the artery before she could hemorrhage further.
Without immediate surgery, the patient might hemorrhage to death internally.

  • synonym – bleed: to lose blood from the body
