mixture

noun
food or drink made by mixing ingredients
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
something made by combining different things, such as a food, drink, group, or substance

Examples

mixture of juice
He poured a strange mixture of juice and soda into his glass.
She served a delicious mixture of berries and cream for dessert.
The chef created a unique mixture of spices for the soup.
Her salad was a colorful mixture of fresh vegetables and fruits.
The festival offered a lively mixture of music, food, and crafts from around the world.
There was a mixture of excitement and nervousness among the students before the exam.
Air is a mixture of several different gases, including oxygen and nitrogen.
The chemist carefully prepared a mixture of two liquids for the experiment.
Unlike a compound, a mixture does not have components chemically bonded together.
