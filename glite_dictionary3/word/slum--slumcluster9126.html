to slum

verb
to visit poor areas out of curiosity or novelty
Pronunciation: / slʌm / (AmE), / slʌm / (BrE)
(of someone from a higher social class) to spend time in impoverished areas or among people of lower socio-economic status, often out of curiosity or a sense of adventure, sometimes in a condescending or insensitive manner.

Examples

to slum with locals
Critics accuse celebrities who slum at charity events of being patronizing.
She sometimes likes to slum by eating at fast-food restaurants instead of her usual fancy spots.
The wealthy tourists decided to slum in the city's rundown neighborhood for the evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slum it: to put up with uncomfortable or less luxurious conditions, usually temporarily
