to infiltrate

verb
enter secretly to spy or cause harm
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.fɪlˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪl.treɪt / (BrE)
to secretly enter or become part of a group, organization, or place, often to get information or do harm (including military or espionage contexts)

Examples

to infiltrate a group
Posing as a volunteer, she managed to infiltrate the organization undetected.
The police feared that criminal gangs might infiltrate the protest groups.
They worked overtime to ensure no one could infiltrate their security system.
During the night, several soldiers attempted to infiltrate behind enemy lines to gather intelligence.
It was their mission to infiltrate the camp and disrupt communications before the main assault.
The special forces unit managed to infiltrate the enemy's defensive perimeter without being detected.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exfiltrate: to withdraw covertly from an organization or area
  • synonym – penetrate: to go into or through something, especially with force or effort
  • synonym – spy: to secretly collect information about an enemy or competitor
