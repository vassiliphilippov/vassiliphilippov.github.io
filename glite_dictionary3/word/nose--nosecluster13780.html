nose

noun
figurative symbol of inquisitiveness
Pronunciation: / noʊz / (AmE), / nəʊz / (BrE)
used figuratively to refer to inquisitiveness or curiosity, especially in expressions meaning to interfere in other people's affairs

Examples

nose in business
He always has his nose in other people's business and can't help meddling.
She poked her nose into the conversation, wanting to know every detail.
Try to keep your nose out of things that don't concern you.

Related Terms

  • synonym – curiosity: eagerness to know or learn something
  • antonym – indifference: lack of interest or curiosity
