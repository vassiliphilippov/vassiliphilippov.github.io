Insectivora

noun
former order of insect-eating mammals
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌsɛktəˈvɔrə / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛktɪˈvɔːrə / (BrE)
a former order of small, primarily insect-eating mammals including shrews, moles, hedgehogs, and tenrecs

Examples

Insectivora classification group
Early zoologists grouped many small, sharp-toothed mammals as members of Insectivora.
Shrews and hedgehogs were once classified together in the order Insectivora.
The order Insectivora has been largely replaced by the more accurate order Eulipotyphla in modern taxonomy.
