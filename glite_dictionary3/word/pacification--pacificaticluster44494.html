pacification

noun
actions to suppress insurgency
Pronunciation: / ˌpæs.ə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæs.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the efforts by a government to suppress or eliminate insurgency and resistance, often through military or police action combined with efforts to restore peace and order

Examples

military pacification campaign
Critics argue that pacification often leads to civilian unrest if not paired with genuine reform.
During the occupation, the army focused on pacification rather than outright confrontation.
The government launched a major pacification campaign in the troubled region to quell the uprising.

  • synonym – counterinsurgency: military or political action taken to combat insurgency
