botanical

noun
plant-derived drug or substance
Pronunciation: / bəˈtænɪkəl / (AmE), / bəˈtænɪkəl / (BrE)
a substance or drug obtained from parts of a plant, such as its bark, root, or leaves, especially used for medicinal purposes

Examples

herbal botanical supplement
Many health supplements are made from botanical extracts found in rare plants.
Researchers are studying a new botanical that may help lower blood pressure.
The clinic specializes in treatments using traditional botanical medicines.
