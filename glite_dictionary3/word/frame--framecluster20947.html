frame

noun
independent window in computer interface
Pronunciation: / freɪm / (AmE), / freɪm / (BrE)
a section of a computer screen or web page that is one of two or more distinct windows, which can be scrolled or manipulated independently from the others

Examples

frame on a website
Many websites once used frames to display navigation on the side and content in the main area.
Some modern browsers limit the use of frames due to potential security issues.
The document opened in a separate frame so users could scroll without leaving the homepage.

  • synonym – window: a separate viewing area on a computer display
