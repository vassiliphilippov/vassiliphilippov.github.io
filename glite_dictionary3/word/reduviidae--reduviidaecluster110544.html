Reduviidae

noun
family of predatory assassin bugs
Pronunciation: / rɪˈduːviˌɪdiː / (AmE), / rɛˈdjuːvɪˌɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory insects known as assassin bugs, characterized by their elongated bodies and a prominent, curved proboscis used for piercing prey.
