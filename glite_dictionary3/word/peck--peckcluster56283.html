to peck

verb
bother with trivial complaints
Pronunciation: / pɛk / (AmE), / pɛk / (BrE)
to bother someone persistently with trivial complaints or repeated criticism

Examples

to peck at someone
He felt exhausted after his mother pecked him all evening about his untidy room.
If you keep pecking at me over little things, I'll never get anything done.
She tends to peck at her coworkers with constant minor complaints.
