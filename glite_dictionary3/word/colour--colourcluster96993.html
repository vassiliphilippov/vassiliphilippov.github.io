colour

noun
vividness; interest; variety (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
interest, excitement, or vividness; a false or misleading outward appearance

Examples

colour in a story
His speech lacked colour, making it difficult for the audience to stay interested.
She brought a lot of colour to the office with her creativity and humor.
The novel was full of colour and kept readers engaged from start to finish.
Despite his colour of innocence, everyone suspected he was involved.
He tried to give his story a colour of truth, but the facts did not support him.
The law was bent to provide a colour of legitimacy to the dubious practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dullness: the quality of being uninteresting or lacking excitement
  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • synonym – semblance: the outward appearance or apparent form of something, especially when the reality is different
  • synonym – vividness: the quality of being strikingly bright or intense
