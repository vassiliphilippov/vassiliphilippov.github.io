citizen

noun
legal member of a country or state
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtɪzən / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪzən / (BrE)
A person who is a legal member of a particular country or state, either by birth or by naturalization, and who has the rights and responsibilities associated with that status.

Examples

citizen of a country
As a citizen of France, she is entitled to live and work anywhere in the European Union.
Every citizen has the right to vote in national elections.
The mayor addressed the concerns of every citizen during the town hall meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – alien: a foreigner or non-citizen living in a country
  • synonym – subject: a person under the rule of a monarch
