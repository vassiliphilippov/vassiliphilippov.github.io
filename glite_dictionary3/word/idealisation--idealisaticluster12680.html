idealisation

noun
psychiatric defense mechanism of perceiving extremes
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdiːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) A defense mechanism in which a person regards someone or something they feel ambivalent about as either entirely good or entirely bad, splitting their perception into two extreme representations.

Examples

idealisation in therapy
Children often resort to idealisation when they have mixed feelings about their parents.
In clinical settings, idealisation is seen as a way to manage conflicting emotions toward significant others.
The therapist explained that idealisation made it hard for Clara to accept flaws in her partner.

Related Terms

  • antonym – devaluation: defense mechanism involving viewing someone as all bad
