to snitch

verb
secretly inform on someone
Pronunciation: / snɪtʃ / (AmE), / snɪtʃ / (BrE)
to secretly inform on someone, especially to the authorities or a person in power, often betraying a trust

Examples

to snitch on friends
He promised not to snitch on his friends, no matter what happened.
If you snitch to the teacher, the class will lose trust in you.
She got in trouble at school because someone snitched on her.

Related Terms

  • synonym – betray: to be disloyal to someone by giving information about them
  • synonym – inform: to give information about someone's wrongdoing to an authority
