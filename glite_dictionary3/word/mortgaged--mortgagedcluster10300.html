mortgaged

adjective
subject to a mortgage or financial obligation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrɡɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of property or an asset) subject to a mortgage; burdened with financial or legal obligations to a lender.

Examples

mortgaged house
His family's business was so mortgaged that they struggled to make interest payments.
Nearly every building on that street is mortgaged to the bank.
She refused to buy a heavily mortgaged property.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encumbered: restricted or burdened, especially with legal or financial obligations
  • antonym – freehold: property owned without any mortgage or financial burden
