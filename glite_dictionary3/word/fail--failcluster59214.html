to fail

verb
not achieve a passing grade
Pronunciation: / feɪl / (AmE), / feɪl / (BrE)
to not pass a test, exam, or course, or to judge someone as not passing

Examples

fail an exam
Despite his best efforts, he failed the driving test again.
If you don't study, you might fail the exam.
To graduate, students cannot fail more than two classes in a year.
If you don't submit your assignment on time, your professor may fail you.
She was disappointed when the instructor decided to fail her on the driving test.
The strict marking scheme caused several students to be failed this semester.
