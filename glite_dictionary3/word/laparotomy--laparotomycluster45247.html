laparotomy

noun
surgical incision into the abdominal wall
Pronunciation: / ˌlæpəˈrɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌlæpəˈrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the abdominal wall, typically to examine the organs inside the abdomen

Examples

laparotomy procedure
A laparotomy may be required if less invasive diagnostic methods fail to reveal the problem.
After the laparotomy, the doctors were able to remove the diseased tissue successfully.
The surgeon performed a laparotomy to investigate the cause of the patient's abdominal pain.
