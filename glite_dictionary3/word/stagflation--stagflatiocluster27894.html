stagflation

noun
simultaneous inflation and economic stagnation
Pronunciation: / stæɡˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / stæɡˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
A situation in which an economy experiences stagnant growth, high unemployment, and rising prices at the same time.

Examples

period of stagflation
Economists are worried that stagflation could return if inflation keeps rising while economic growth slows.
Policymakers found it difficult to combat stagflation because traditional tools for fighting inflation could worsen unemployment.
The country suffered from stagflation during the 1970s, with soaring prices and high unemployment rates.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deflation: a decrease in the general price level of goods and services
  • synonym – inflation: a rise in the general level of prices
