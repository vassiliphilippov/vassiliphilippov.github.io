to enfeoff

verb
grant land in feudal society
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfiːf / (AmE), / ɪnˈfiːf / (BrE)
(historical, legal) To grant someone possession of land, especially as a vassal, in exchange for a pledge of service in feudal society.

Examples

to enfeoff a knight
Many nobles were enfeoffed as a reward for their service in battle.
The king decided to enfeoff his loyal knight with a large parcel of land.
Under feudal law, a lord could enfeoff several vassals to secure his lands.

Related Terms

  • synonym – invest: to formally give someone a rank, authority, or possession (historical sense)
