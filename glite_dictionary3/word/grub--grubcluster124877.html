to grub

verb
search or dig busily
Pronunciation: / ɡrʌb / (AmE), / ɡrʌb / (BrE)
to search or dig about busily, especially in the ground or among other things, often using the hands or tools

Examples

to grub in soil
Farmers grub in the field early each morning to remove weeds.
She grubbed through old boxes in the attic searching for her grandmother's photos.
The children began to grub in the dirt looking for worms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dig: break up and move earth, or search for something by digging
