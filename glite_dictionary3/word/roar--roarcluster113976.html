to roar

verb
speak very loudly and forcefully
Pronunciation: / rɔr / (AmE), / rɔː / (BrE)
to say something in a very loud and forceful voice

Examples

to roar a command
"Don't you dare touch that!" she roared angrily across the room.
He roared at the children to finish their homework before dinner.
The coach roared instructions from the sidelines as the game heated up.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bellow: to shout in a deep loud voice
  • antonym – whisper: to speak very softly
  • synonym – yell: to shout loudly
