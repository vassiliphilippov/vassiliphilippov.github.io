confusion

noun
mistake from taking one thing for another
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a mistake made when one thing is wrongly taken for another, leading to misunderstandings or misidentification

Examples

confusion of names
His handwriting caused confusion when the teacher mixed up two students' assignments.
The company rebranded its product to avoid confusion with a competitor.
There was confusion at the airport when two passengers had the same name.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clarity: the quality of being clear and easy to understand or distinguish
  • synonym – misidentification: the act of identifying someone or something incorrectly
