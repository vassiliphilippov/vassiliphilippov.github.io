to rumour

verb
circulate unverified information
Pronunciation: / ˈruːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈruːmə / (BrE)
to circulate or spread information or reports that may not be true

Examples

to rumour bad news
It was widely rumoured that the company would be sold.
The media rumoured that the actor was secretly married.
Throughout the office, people rumoured about the manager's sudden resignation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deny: to declare something is not true; opposite of spreading rumors
  • synonym – gossip: to spread personal or sensational rumors
