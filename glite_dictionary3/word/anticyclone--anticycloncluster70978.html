anticyclone

noun
area of high atmospheric pressure with outward-spiraling winds
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈsaɪ.kloʊn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsaɪ.kləʊn / (BrE)
A weather system with high atmospheric pressure at its center, around which winds move outward in a spiral; in the Northern Hemisphere, these winds spiral clockwise, and in the Southern Hemisphere, counterclockwise.

Examples

a strong anticyclone
A strong anticyclone brought sunny weather to the entire region for several days.
During winter, an anticyclone can cause clear skies and colder temperatures.
In the Northern Hemisphere, winds in an anticyclone rotate in a clockwise direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cyclone: a weather system with low atmospheric pressure at its center and inward-spiraling winds
