center

noun
focus of interest or attention
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə / (BrE)
the most important or main part of something; the focus of attention or activity

Examples

center of attention
After her impressive speech, she became the center of everyone’s attention.
He always tries to be the center of any conversation.
The new exhibit was the center of attraction at the museum opening.
At the center of the novel is a powerful question about identity.
She quickly summarized the center of his argument in just a few words.
The center of the debate focused on the need for education reform.

  • synonym – essence: the most important quality or feature of something
  • synonym – focus: the main point of interest or activity
  • antonym – periphery: the outer edge or boundary of something, especially where attention is not focused
