deep-eyed

adjective
having eyes set deep in the sockets
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːpˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌaɪd / (BrE)
Having eyes that are set deep in the sockets, giving a sunken or hollow appearance.

Examples

deep-eyed man
His deep-eyed gaze made him look older and more mysterious.
Many classical sculptures depict deep-eyed figures to accentuate their emotion and depth.
The artist painted the old, deep-eyed fisherman with remarkable detail.
