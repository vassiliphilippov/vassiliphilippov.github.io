Aramean

noun
historical Semitic people of Aram/Mesopotamia
Pronunciation: / ˌærəˈmiːən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmiːən / (BrE)
a member of a Semitic people who inhabited Aram and parts of Mesopotamia from the 11th to the 8th centuries BC

Examples

an Aramean tribe
Ancient inscriptions mention the migration of the Aramean tribes into Mesopotamia.
Scholars continue to study the language and culture of the Arameans today.
The Aramean influence can be seen in the religious practices of their neighbors.
