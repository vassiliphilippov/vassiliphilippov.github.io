nativeness

noun
native-like quality of language
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪ.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the degree to which spoken or written language (including translations or machine-generated text) resembles the language of a native speaker; native-like quality in speech or text.

Examples

language nativeness
Researchers evaluated the model's nativeness to measure how convincingly it mimicked native speaker writing.
The translation team adjusted idioms to improve the nativeness of the localized user interface.
During the exam, tutors rated students' spoken nativeness alongside grammar and pronunciation for a full assessment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – native-likeness: the quality of being like the language produced by a native speaker
