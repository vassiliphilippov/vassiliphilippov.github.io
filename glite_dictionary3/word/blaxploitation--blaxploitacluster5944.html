blaxploitation

noun
the use of Black stereotypes, especially in films
Pronunciation: / blækˌsplɔɪˈteɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / blakˌsplɔɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the exploitation of Black people, especially by featuring stereotyped roles in films or other media, often for commercial gain

Examples

blaxploitation movies
During the 1970s, Hollywood produced a wave of blaxploitation movies targeting urban audiences.
Many critics argue that blaxploitation films reinforced negative stereotypes of African Americans.
While some see blaxploitation as empowering, others view it as problematic and offensive.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exploitation: the action of treating someone unfairly in order to benefit from their work
