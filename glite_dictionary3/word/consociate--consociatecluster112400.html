to consociate

verb
to unite or join together
Pronunciation: / kənˈsoʊʃieɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
to bring or come into association; to unite or join together for a particular purpose

Examples

to consociate groups
During the crisis, the organizations consociated to offer aid and support to those affected.
Several small companies decided to consociate in order to compete with larger firms.
The proposal would consociate community leaders in a joint effort to improve local schools.

Related Terms

  • synonym – associate: to connect or join with others; to link together
