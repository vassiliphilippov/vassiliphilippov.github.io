wired

adjective
having wires for electricity or communication
Pronunciation: / waɪɚd / (AmE), / waɪəd / (BrE)
Equipped with wire or wires, especially for electric or telephone service.

Examples

wired for electricity
Our new house isn't wired for cable television yet, so we'll have to wait.
The entire office building is fully wired for high-speed internet.
This old neighborhood is not well wired, making modern upgrades difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym – wireless: using radio signals instead of wires for connections
