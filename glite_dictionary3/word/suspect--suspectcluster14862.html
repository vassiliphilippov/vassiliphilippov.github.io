to suspect

verb
believe to be true or probable
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to believe something is true or likely without having definite proof

Examples

to suspect a problem
Although he had no proof, he suspected that his friend was hiding something.
I suspect she knows more than she is telling us.
Many people suspect that the company will announce layoffs soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – doubt: to feel uncertain about something
  • synonym – suppose: to think that something is likely to be true
