saturation

noun
maximum absorption or response state
Pronunciation: / ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsatʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the state in which a system or substance cannot absorb or respond to any more of something due to having reached its maximum capacity

Examples

market saturation point
After a certain point, the market achieved saturation and growth stagnated.
Humidity climbed to saturation, making air feel heavy and dense.
The amplifier reached saturation and no further increase in input changed the output.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desaturation: the process of removing saturation or reducing it
  • degree higher – supersaturation: the state of being more highly concentrated than normally possible under given temperature and pressure
