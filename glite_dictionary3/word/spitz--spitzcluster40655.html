spitz

noun
stocky, pointed-muzzle dog breed group
Pronunciation: / spɪts / (AmE), / spɪts / (BrE)
A breed or group of stocky, heavy-coated dogs native to northern regions, characterized by pointed muzzles, erect ears, and a curled furry tail.

Examples

spitz dog breed
Many families choose a spitz for its energetic and loyal nature.
Sam's new puppy is a rare Finnish spitz with a beautiful red coat.
The spitz has a thick coat that helps it withstand cold climates.
