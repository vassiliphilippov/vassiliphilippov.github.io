schizocarp

noun
a dry fruit that splits into seed-containing parts
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪzəˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈskɪz.ə.kɑːp / (BrE)
A dry fruit that splits at maturity into two or more parts, each containing a single seed.

Examples

schizocarp splits open
Many members of the parsley family form a schizocarp as their fruit.
The maple tree produces a schizocarp that splits into two winged seeds.
When mature, the schizocarp breaks apart, allowing each seed to be dispersed separately.
