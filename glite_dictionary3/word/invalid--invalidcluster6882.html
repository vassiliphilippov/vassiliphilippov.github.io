to invalid

verb
retire due to disability or illness
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnvəˌleɪd /; / ˈɪnvəˌleɪd, ɪnˈvælɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəlɪd /; / ˈɪnvəlɪd, ɪnˈvælɪd / (BrE)
to remove someone from service or make them unable to work or live normally due to illness or injury

Examples

to invalid a soldier
After his injury, the army invalided him out of service.
Many firemen were invalided during the hazardous operation.
She was invalided home after a serious illness while abroad.
A serious fall while mountaineering invalided him for the rest of his life.
She was invalided by a rare disease in her youth.
The wounded soldier was invalided home after the battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cripple: to cause severe and permanent injury to someone, making them unable to move or work
  • synonym – disable: to make unable to use part of the body or unable to do something
  • synonym – discharge: to officially make someone leave a job, especially for health reasons or at the end of service
  • synonym – retire: to leave one's job and stop working, usually because of age or health
