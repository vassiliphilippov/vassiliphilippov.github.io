to confab

verb
talk informally; have a casual conversation
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.fæb / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fæb / (BrE)
to talk informally or have a casual conversation, especially about unimportant matters

Examples

to confab about plans
After the meeting, we stayed to confab about our weekend plans.
The two colleagues would often confab by the water cooler about the latest office news.
While waiting for the bus, they started to confab about nothing in particular.
