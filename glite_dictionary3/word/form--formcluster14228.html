to form

verb
make or shape for a purpose
Pronunciation: / fɔrm / (AmE), / fɔːm / (BrE)
to make, create, or shape something; to establish or influence the nature or character of something

Examples

to form a plan
She carefully formed the dough into small rolls before baking them.
The committee was formed to address the neighborhood's concerns.
The sculptor used clay to form the statue's delicate features.
It took only an hour to form a club for amateur painters.
Several students formed a study group before the final exams.
The committee decided to form a new sub-group to tackle the issue.
She used her hands to form the clay into a small bowl.
Teachers can form a student's attitude toward learning.
The government is working to form new policies on climate change.

  • antonym – dissolve: to officially end or break up an organization or assembly
  • synonym – establish: to set up or found something, such as an institution
  • synonym – imprint: to impress or stamp (something) firmly on the mind or memory
  • synonym – instill: to gradually but firmly establish (an idea or attitude) in a person's mind
  • synonym – mold: to form something by shaping or putting into a mold
  • synonym – shape: to give a particular form or shape to something
