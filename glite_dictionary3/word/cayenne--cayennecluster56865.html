cayenne

noun
hot red pepper spice
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈɛn / (AmE), / kaɪˈɛn / (BrE)
A hot-tasting red powder made from ground dried pods and seeds of certain varieties of capsicum peppers, used as a spice.

Examples

cayenne powder spice
Add a pinch of cayenne to give the soup an extra kick.
She accidentally used too much cayenne in the recipe, making the dish very spicy.
The chef sprinkled cayenne over the roasted vegetables for added heat.
