kiloton

noun
unit of explosive power (1,000 tons of TNT)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪl.ə.tʌn / (AmE), / ˈkɪl.ə.tɒn / (BrE)
A unit of explosive power equal to the energy released by 1,000 tons of TNT, commonly used to describe the strength of nuclear weapons.

Examples

kiloton explosion
A kiloton is a common measurement used to describe the force of atomic blasts.
Estimates suggest the explosion was equivalent to several kilotons of TNT.
The nuclear bomb had a yield of nearly 20 kilotons.
