rapport

noun
a relationship of mutual trust and understanding
Pronunciation: / ræˈpɔr / (AmE), / ræˈpɔː / (BrE)
a close and harmonious relationship in which people understand each other's feelings or ideas and communicate well

Examples

strong rapport with colleagues
Building rapport with clients is essential for long-term business success.
The teacher quickly developed a strong rapport with her students.
They had an easy rapport that made working together enjoyable.
