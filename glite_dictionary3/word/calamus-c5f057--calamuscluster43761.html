calamus

noun
the hollow base of a feather's shaft
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləməs / (AmE), / ˈkæləməs / (BrE)
the hollow, tubular lower part of a feather shaft that attaches to the skin of a bird

Examples

bird calamus shaft
During preening, the bird cleaned dirt from the calamus and the rest of its feathers.
The calamus anchors the feather firmly in the bird's skin.
You can see the calamus at the base of this wing feather.
