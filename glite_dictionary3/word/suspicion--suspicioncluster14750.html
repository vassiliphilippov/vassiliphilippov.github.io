suspicion

noun
the state of being suspected
Pronunciation: / səˈspɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being suspected or thought likely to have done something wrong or illegal

Examples

fall under suspicion
After the incident, he fell under suspicion and faced many questions from the authorities.
His behavior aroused suspicion among his colleagues, who began to watch him closely.
There was no concrete evidence, but a cloud of suspicion hung over her for months.

Related Terms

  • antonym – certainty: the state of being completely confident or having no doubt
  • synonym – doubt: a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
