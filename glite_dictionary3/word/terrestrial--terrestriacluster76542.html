terrestrial

adjective
living or existing on land
Pronunciation: / təˈrɛstriəl / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəl / (BrE)
relating to land (as opposed to water or air); living or growing on land

Examples

terrestrial animals

Most orchids are terrestrial, but some species grow on trees.
The rainforest is home to a wide variety of terrestrial animals.
Some species of plants are terrestrial, while others are adapted to aquatic environments.
The terrestrial habitat supports many different types of mammals and insects.
Unlike their marine relatives, these animals are strictly terrestrial and cannot survive underwater.
