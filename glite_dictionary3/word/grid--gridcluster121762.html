grid

noun
system for electrical power distribution
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
a system of interconnected high-voltage transmission lines through which electrical power is distributed across a large area or region

Examples

power grid failure
A sudden storm caused the entire grid to lose power, plunging neighborhoods into darkness.
Many remote communities are not connected to the main grid and rely on local generators.
Upgrading the national grid is essential to support renewable energy sources like wind and solar.

Related Terms

  • antonym – off-grid: not connected to the main electrical grid
  • synonym – power grid: system of electrical transmission lines for distributing power
