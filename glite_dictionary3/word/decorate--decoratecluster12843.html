to decorate

verb
award an official honor or medal
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəreɪt / (BrE)
to award (someone) an official mark of honor, such as a medal, for distinguished achievement or service

Examples

decorate a soldier
After many years of service, she was finally decorated by the president.
He felt proud to be decorated for his contributions to the mission.
The general decided to decorate the soldier for his extraordinary bravery.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disgrace: to take away honor or cause shame
  • synonym – honor: to formally recognize someone's achievements or service
