to survive

verb
continue to live after danger or hardship
Pronunciation: / sərˈvaɪv / (AmE), / səˈvaɪv / (BrE)
to continue to live or exist after a dangerous or difficult event, such as an accident, illness, or disaster

Examples

to survive an accident
After the accident, he struggled to survive despite his injuries.
It is a miracle that the hikers managed to survive in the wilderness for a week.
The old traditions still survive in some rural communities.
After the plane crash, only a few passengers survived the ordeal.
Doctors told her she might not survive the operation, but she made a full recovery.
The ancient tree has survived many storms over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • antonym – die: to stop living
  • synonym – endure: to remain in existence; to last through hardship
  • antonym – perish: to die, especially in an accident or disaster
