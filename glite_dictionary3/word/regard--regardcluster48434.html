to regard

verb
look at attentively
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
to look at someone or something attentively; to observe closely

Examples

to regard someone closely
He regarded her with curiosity as she spoke.
She paused to regard the painting before moving on to the next exhibit.
The cat sat on the windowsill and regarded the birds outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
  • synonym – observe: to watch something carefully
