IDA

noun
UN agency providing aid to poor countries
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ di ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ diː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an agency of the United Nations affiliated with the World Bank, responsible for providing interest-free loans and grants to the world's poorest countries.

Examples

IDA funding projects
Many countries rely on assistance from the IDA to improve public health programs.
The IDA approved several new grants to support rural development projects.
The IDA works closely with the World Bank to provide low-interest loans to developing nations.
