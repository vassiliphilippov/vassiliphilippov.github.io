tenement

noun
run-down apartment building
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnəmənt / (BrE)
A large building divided into apartments, especially one that is in poor condition and provides housing at low cost for people with little money.

Examples

tenement building
Many immigrants lived in crowded tenements in New York City during the early 20th century.
She worked as a social worker in the city's most neglected tenements.
The old tenement was condemned because of its unsafe living conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – apartment building: a building containing several separate apartments for people to live in
  • degree higher – slum: a very poor and crowded area, often with run-down housing
