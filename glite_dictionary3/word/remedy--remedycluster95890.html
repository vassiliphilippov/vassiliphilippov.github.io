to remedy

verb
correct or fix a problem or fault
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛmədi /; / ˈrɛm.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈrɛmɪdi /; / ˈrɛm.ɪ.di / (BrE)
to correct, fix, or provide a solution for a problem, fault, or bad situation

Examples

to remedy a problem
A new plan was introduced to remedy deficiencies in the education system.
She tried to remedy the error before anyone noticed.
The government is working to remedy the economic disparities in the region.
He hopes to remedy the situation before it gets worse.
Laws were created to remedy social injustices in the community.
The doctor tried several treatments to remedy her persistent cough.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alleviate: to make a problem or symptoms less severe
  • synonym – cure: to restore health; eliminate a disease or problem
  • synonym – rectify: to put right; correct
  • antonym – worsen: to make or become worse
