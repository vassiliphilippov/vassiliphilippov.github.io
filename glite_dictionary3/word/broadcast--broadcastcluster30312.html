to broadcast

verb
sow seeds by scattering widely
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to sow seeds by scattering them widely over the surface of the ground, especially by hand

Examples

to broadcast seeds
Farmers used to broadcast seeds across the fields in early spring.
She broadcast grass seed by hand over the lawn to ensure even coverage.
The traditional method involves broadcasting wheat seed before lightly raking the soil.
