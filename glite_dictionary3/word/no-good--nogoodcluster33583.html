no-good

adjective
worthless or invalid (of a check)
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊˌɡʊd / (AmE), / ˌnəʊˈɡʊd / (BrE)
(of a check) not valid or accepted, especially because there are insufficient funds in the account.

Examples

no-good check
After writing a no-good check, he had to quickly deposit more money to cover it.
She was embarrassed when the store rejected her no-good check at the register.
The bank called to inform me that my last payment was a no-good check.

Related Terms

  • antonym – valid: legally or officially acceptable
  • synonym – worthless: having no value, use, or importance
