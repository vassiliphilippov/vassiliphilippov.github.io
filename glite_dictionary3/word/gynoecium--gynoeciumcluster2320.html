gynoecium

noun
female reproductive part of a flower
Pronunciation: / dʒaɪˈniːsiəm / (AmE), / gɪˈniːsɪəm / (BrE)
The female reproductive part of a flower, consisting of one or more carpels.

Examples

gynoecium of a flower
In roses, the gynoecium forms the central part of the blossom that produces seeds.
Scientists examined the development of the gynoecium under different light conditions.
The gynoecium is composed of several fused carpels in most flowering plants.
