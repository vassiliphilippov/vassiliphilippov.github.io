tourniquet

noun
device to stop blood flow
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.nɪ.kət / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪ.ket / (BrE)
A device, typically a tight band, used to stop the flow of blood through an artery by applying pressure to a limb.

Examples

apply a tourniquet tightly
She quickly fashioned a makeshift tourniquet out of her scarf to stop the blood flow.
The paramedic wrapped a tourniquet above the injured man's knee to control the bleeding.
You should only use a tourniquet in emergencies when other methods do not work.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bandage: a strip of cloth used to bind a wound or protect an injured part of the body
