countercoup

noun
overthrow of a government born from a coup
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌkuː / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌkuː / (BrE)
a sudden and decisive action that overthrows a government which itself gained power through a coup d'état

Examples

military countercoup
After the coup, rival generals attempted a countercoup to regain power.
The fragile government was overthrown by a swift countercoup just months after taking control.
The military staged a countercoup to remove the newly established regime.
