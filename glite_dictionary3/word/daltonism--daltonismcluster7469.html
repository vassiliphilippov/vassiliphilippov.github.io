Daltonism

noun
type of color blindness affecting green vision
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔltənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɔːltənɪzəm / (BrE)
a type of color blindness characterized by reduced sensitivity to green light, making it difficult to distinguish between green and purplish-red hues

Examples

Daltonism color blindness
Daltonism often makes it hard for individuals to tell the difference between green and purple colors.
Because of Daltonism, the artist uses labels to identify paint colors.
He was diagnosed with Daltonism after struggling to differentiate traffic lights.
