snakehead

noun
North American marsh plant with showy flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈsneɪkˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkhɛd / (BrE)
A showy perennial plant of marshlands in eastern and central North America, with waxy lance-shaped leaves and flowers that are creamy white on the lower part and pale pink to deep purple on the upper parts.

Examples

snakehead flowers bloom
Gardeners value the snakehead for its hardiness and striking appearance.
The snakehead blooms in late summer, bringing vibrant color to the marshlands.
