overage

noun
excess of money or merchandise
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a surplus amount of money or goods that is present and exceeds what was expected or recorded

Examples

overage in inventory
Inventory overage can indicate errors in stocking or record-keeping.
The accountant found an overage in the cash register at the end of the day.
They were pleased to discover a small overage after completing the year-end inventory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – shortage: an amount by which something is less than expected or needed
