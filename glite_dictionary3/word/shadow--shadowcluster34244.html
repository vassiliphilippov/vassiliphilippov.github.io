shadow

noun
inseparable companion
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ /; / ˈʃadəʊ / (BrE)
a person who follows or observes someone closely, often secretly (like a spy or companion)

Examples

shadow companion
During the summer internship, Emma became the manager's shadow, eager to learn everything she could.
Ever since he arrived, Tom has been his older brother's shadow, rarely leaving his side.
Some teachers encourage new staff to be a shadow to more experienced colleagues during their first week.
Government agents often use a shadow to track people of interest.
She noticed she had a shadow as she walked down the street.
The private investigator assigned a shadow to keep an eye on the suspect.

Related Terms

  • synonym – detective: a person employed to find information about crimes or people
  • synonym – tail: a person who follows someone for surveillance
  • antonym – target: a person being followed by a shadow
