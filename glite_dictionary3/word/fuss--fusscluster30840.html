fuss

noun
petty quarrel or commotion
Pronunciation: / fʌs / (AmE), / fʌs / (BrE)
unnecessary or excessive excitement, activity, or complaint, especially about something unimportant

Examples

make a fuss
He got into a fuss with his sister over who would use the bathroom first.
She made such a fuss over a tiny mistake in the report.
There was no need for all that fuss about the seating arrangement.
All this fuss about nothing is exhausting to watch.
Please don't cause a fuss when we leave early.
She made a big fuss over his birthday present.
The children caused a fuss when they couldn't find their toys.
There was a lot of fuss in the hallway after the fire alarm went off.
Why make such a fuss over a minor mistake?

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: absence of agitation or excitement
  • synonym – commotion: a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • synonym – uproar: a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
