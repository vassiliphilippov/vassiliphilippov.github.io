to jump

verb
increase suddenly and significantly
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to increase suddenly and significantly (in amount, value, or status)

Examples

jump in prices
Attendance jumped by 30% at the concert this year.
Fuel costs jumped after the new tax was introduced.
The company's profits jumped to record highs last quarter.
After years of dedication, he finally jumped to a senior management position.
The company's stock jumped to a new high after the merger announcement.
The film jumped from obscurity to international success almost overnight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – plummet: to decrease suddenly and steeply
  • synonym – rise: to move from a lower position to a higher one; increase in rank or status
  • synonym – soar: to rise rapidly; to increase quickly to a high level
