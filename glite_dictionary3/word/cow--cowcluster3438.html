to cow

verb
to intimidate or subdue
Pronunciation: / kaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊ / (BrE)
to make someone submit or behave in a certain way by frightening or intimidating them

Examples

to cow a group
He tried to cow his rivals with harsh words, but they stood their ground.
She was not easily cowed and spoke up for her beliefs.
The protesters refused to be cowed by threats from the authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – daunt: to make someone feel intimidated or apprehensive
  • antonym – hearten: to give courage or confidence to
  • synonym – intimidate: to frighten or overawe someone, especially to make them do what one wants
