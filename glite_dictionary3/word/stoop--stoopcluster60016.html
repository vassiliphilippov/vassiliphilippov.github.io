to stoop

verb
lower oneself morally or act dishonorably
Pronunciation: / stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
to lower oneself by doing something considered morally wrong or undignified; to act beneath one's own standards of honor or dignity

Examples

to stoop to cheating
I refuse to stoop to their level, even if they insult me.
She wouldn't stoop to cheating just to win the contest.
They expected him to stoop so low, but he maintained his integrity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – debase oneself: to lower one's moral standards or dignity
  • antonym – rise above: to refuse to be affected by or lower oneself
  • synonym – sink: to lower oneself in conduct or quality
