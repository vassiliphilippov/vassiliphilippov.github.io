eyepiece

noun
lens or lenses to look through on an optical instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌpis / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌpiːs / (BrE)
the part of an optical instrument, such as a microscope or telescope, that you look through, consisting of a combination of lenses placed at the viewing end

Examples

eyepiece of a microscope
Make sure the eyepiece is clean before you start the experiment.
She adjusted the eyepiece of the microscope to get a clearer view of the specimen.
The telescope’s eyepiece was foggy, making it hard to see the stars clearly.
