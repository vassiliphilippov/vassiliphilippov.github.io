natural

noun
music: symbol cancelling a sharp or flat
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃ(ə)rəl / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl / (BrE)
A musical symbol (♮) used to cancel a previous sharp or flat and restore the original pitch of a note.

Examples

natural sign
In the key signature, a natural clears any accidental effects from previous measures.
The pianist noticed a natural sign on the score, cancelling the earlier sharp.
When you see a natural after a flat, it means to play the note at its original pitch.
