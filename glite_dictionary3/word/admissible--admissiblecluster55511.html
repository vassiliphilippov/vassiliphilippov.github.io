admissible

adjective
acceptable; allowed, especially as evidence
Pronunciation: / ədˈmɪsəbl / (AmE), / ədˈmɪsəbl / (BrE)
acceptable or valid, especially as evidence in a court of law; deserving to be admitted.

Examples

admissible evidence
Certain testimony was deemed not admissible due to lack of relevance.
Only admissible evidence will be considered during the trial.
The judge ruled that the document was admissible in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inadmissible: not acceptable or valid, especially as evidence
