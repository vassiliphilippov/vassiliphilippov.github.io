to emboss

verb
raise a design in relief
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈbɑs / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒs / (BrE)
to decorate a surface by raising a design above the surrounding area, often by pressing or stamping

Examples

to emboss a card
Many wedding invitations are embossed with elegant designs for a tactile effect.
She learned how to emboss her initials onto the stationery at the craft workshop.
The artist used a special tool to emboss the leather cover with intricate patterns.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deboss: to stamp a design into a surface so that it is recessed
  • antonym – engrave: to carve or cut a design into a surface
