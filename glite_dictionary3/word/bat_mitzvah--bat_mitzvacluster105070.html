to bat mitzvah

verb
to confirm a girl in Judaism at coming of age
Pronunciation: / bɑt ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / bæt ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
to confirm a girl in the Jewish faith through the bat mitzvah ceremony, marking her coming of age at 12 or 13

Examples

to bat mitzvah a girl
He helped bat mitzvah his niece by reading a blessing during the ceremony.
Her daughter will bat mitzvah next spring at their synagogue.
Many families choose to bat mitzvah their daughters as part of a large celebration.
