string

noun
hypothetical subatomic particle (string theory)
Pronunciation: / strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
(physics) a hypothetical one-dimensional subatomic particle, regarded as a concentration of energy with the dynamic properties of a flexible loop, especially in string theory.

Examples

a string in physics
In modern physics, the fundamental particles are sometimes modeled as strings rather than point-like objects.
Physicists hope that studying strings will provide new insights into the nature of the universe.
The properties of a string in string theory differ greatly from those of conventional particles.
