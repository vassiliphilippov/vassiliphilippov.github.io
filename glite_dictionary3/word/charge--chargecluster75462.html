to charge

verb
place a heraldic bearing on
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to place a heraldic bearing or device on a shield, banner, or similar surface

Examples

to charge a shield with a symbol
He decided to charge the shield with a red star to honor his ancestors.
Many medieval banners were charged with animals, plants, or geometric shapes.
The family coat of arms was charged with three golden lions.
