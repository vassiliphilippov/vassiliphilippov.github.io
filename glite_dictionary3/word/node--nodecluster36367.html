node

noun
point where orbit crosses a plane (astronomy)
Pronunciation: / noʊd / (AmE), / nəʊd / (BrE)
(astronomy) A point at which the orbit of a celestial body crosses a reference plane, especially the ecliptic.

Examples

orbital node
Eclipses can only occur when the Sun is near a node of the Moon's orbit.
The Moon passes through a node twice every orbit, once ascending and once descending.
The intersection points, or nodes, are significant in predicting celestial events.
