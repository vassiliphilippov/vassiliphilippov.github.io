rudiment

noun
undeveloped or vestigial body part
Pronunciation: / ˈruːdəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈruːdɪmənt / (BrE)
an undeveloped or vestigial part of the body, especially one that was functional at an earlier stage of development

Examples

body rudiment
A genetic mutation can cause a rudiment to become prominent during development.
In birds, the tiny bones are a rudiment of lost limbs.
The appendix is sometimes considered a rudiment inherited from our ancestors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vestige: a small remaining part of something that once existed or was more noticeable
