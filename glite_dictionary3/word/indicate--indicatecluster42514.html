to indicate

verb
be a sign or evidence of
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.dɪˌkeɪt /; / ˈɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪt /; / ˈɪndɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to show, suggest, or be a sign that something is true or likely

Examples

indicate a problem
A fever can indicate an underlying infection.
Poor sales figures usually indicate problems with marketing or product quality.
The presence of smoke may indicate a fire nearby.
Early symptoms often indicate the onset of the disease and should not be ignored.
Her nervous tone may indicate that she is unsure about the decision.
The data indicate a significant increase in temperature over the past century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contradict: to deny or be in conflict with evidence
  • synonym – demonstrate: to show evidence or prove something
  • synonym – denote: to be a sign of; to indicate
  • synonym – signal: to be a sign or warning of something
  • synonym – suggest: to imply or indicate as a possibility
