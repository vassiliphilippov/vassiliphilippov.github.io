to rationalise

verb
remove irrational quantities (maths)
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
(Mathematics) To eliminate irrational numbers, especially roots, from the denominator of a fraction or from an expression.

Examples

to rationalise a fraction
In mathematics, students often learn how to rationalise fractions containing square roots.
The equation can be rationalised to make further calculations easier.
To simplify the denominator, you need to rationalise the expression.
