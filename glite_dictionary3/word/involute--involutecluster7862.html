involute

adjective
closely coiled (shells, axis obscured)
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.vəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈɪn.və.ljuːt / (BrE)
(of certain shells) closely coiled so that the axis is hidden or not visible.

Examples

involute shell coils
The involute form makes it difficult to see the inner coils of the sea snail's shell.
The ammonite fossil displays an involute shell, with the whorls tightly wound and the center hidden.
Unlike evolute shells, involute shells show no visible central axis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – evolute: (of shells) coiled so that the whorls are not closely coiled and the axis is visible
