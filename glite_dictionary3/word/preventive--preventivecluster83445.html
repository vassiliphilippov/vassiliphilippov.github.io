preventive

adjective
intended to prevent or hinder
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
intended to stop something bad, especially disease or problems, from happening or getting worse

Examples

preventive health care
Many public health campaigns focus on preventive measures rather than cures.
She believes regular exercise is a preventive strategy against many illnesses.
The doctor recommended a preventive treatment to reduce the risk of infection.
Many doctors emphasize the importance of preventive care to avoid serious illnesses.
Regular check-ups are a common preventive measure to maintain good health.
The clinic offers several preventive services, including vaccinations and screenings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – curative: serving to cure disease
  • synonym – precautionary: intended to prevent harm or risk
  • synonym – prophylactic: designed to prevent disease
  • antonym – remedial: intended as a remedy
