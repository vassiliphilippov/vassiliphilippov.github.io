slothfulness

noun
habitual laziness; reluctance to work
Pronunciation: / ˈsloʊθ.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsləʊθ.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being lazy and unwilling to work or make an effort

Examples

chronic slothfulness
Her slothfulness kept her from completing even the simplest tasks on time.
Overcoming slothfulness can be challenging without strong motivation.
The manager noticed a culture of slothfulness developing among the staff.

Related Terms

  • antonym – diligence: careful and persistent work or effort
  • synonym – laziness: the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy
