polygene

noun
a gene affecting traits jointly with others
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːliˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that individually has little effect on phenotype but combines with others to produce observable variation in a trait

Examples

polygene controls trait
Plant breeders often select for desired traits controlled by multiple polygenes rather than a single gene.
Researchers found that each polygene contributed a small effect to the overall phenotype.
The height of an individual is determined in part by the action of many polygenes.

  • synonym – gene: a unit of heredity that influences a specific characteristic
