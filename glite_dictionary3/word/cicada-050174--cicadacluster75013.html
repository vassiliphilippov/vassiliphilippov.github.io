cicada

noun
noisy insect with large wings
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkeɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
A stout-bodied insect with large, transparent wings, known for the loud, high-pitched sound produced by males.

Examples

singing cicada
A single male cicada can produce a remarkably loud sound to attract females.
In summer, the sound of cicadas fills the air in the countryside.
The shed skin of a cicada was found clinging to the tree's bark.

  • synonym – insect: a small arthropod animal with six legs and usually wings
