to buck

verb
resist or oppose
Pronunciation: / bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
To resist or oppose something, especially a general direction, expectation, or authority.

Examples

to buck the trend
Despite criticism, the company decided to buck the trend and invest in new technology.
She always tries to buck conventional wisdom and find her own solutions.
Some people admire those who buck authority and stand up for what they believe in.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oppose: to actively resist or fight against something
  • antonym – submit: to accept or yield to a superior force or to the authority or will of another
