Albatrellus ovinus

noun
a whitish edible fungus found under conifers
Pronunciation: / æl.bəˈtrɛl.əs oʊˈviː.nəs / (AmE), / æl.bəˈtrel.əs əʊˈviː.nəs / (BrE)
A species of edible fungus with a whitish, often circular cap, a white pore surface with small pores, and a central white stalk, found under conifers; not commonly eaten.

Examples

Albatrellus ovinus mushroom cap
Although Albatrellus ovinus is edible, it is not as popular as other wild mushrooms in cooking.
