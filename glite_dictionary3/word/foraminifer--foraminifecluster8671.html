foraminifer

noun
marine microorganism with shell and pseudopods
Pronunciation: / fəˌræməˈnɪfər / (AmE), / fəˌræmɪˈnɪfə / (BrE)
A single-celled marine microorganism with a calcareous shell containing openings through which pseudopods extend.

Examples

marine foraminifer shell
A single foraminifer can produce a remarkably intricate shell structure.
Sediment samples revealed thousands of tiny foraminifers from the ancient seabed.
The scientist examined the foraminifer under a powerful microscope.
