to hatch

verb
emerge from an egg
Pronunciation: / hætʃ / (AmE), / hætʃ / (BrE)
to come out of an egg, or to help eggs produce young animals (including sitting on eggs to incubate them)

Examples

to hatch from an egg
If all goes well, the fish will hatch within a week.
The chicks hatched from their eggs early this morning.
Turtle eggs usually hatch after about two months of incubation.
Many birds take turns to hatch the eggs until they are ready to break open.
Penguins must hatch their eggs in extreme cold by balancing them on their feet.
The mother hen will hatch her eggs by sitting on them for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – emerge: to come out into view or existence
  • synonym – incubate: to keep eggs warm so they develop and hatch
