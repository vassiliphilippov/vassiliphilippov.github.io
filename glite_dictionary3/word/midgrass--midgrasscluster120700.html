midgrass

noun
grass of medium height, especially prairie forage
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of several types of grass of medium height, especially those that dominated undisturbed prairie regions in the United States and serve as key forage grasses in temperate zones

Examples

midgrass prairie plants
Ranchers often manage fields of midgrass to support cattle grazing throughout the year.
Restoring midgrass areas is important for preserving native prairie ecosystems.
The Great Plains were once dominated by midgrass species, providing crucial forage for bison.
