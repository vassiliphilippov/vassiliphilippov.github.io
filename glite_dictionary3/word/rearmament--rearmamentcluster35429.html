rearmament

noun
the act of arming again
Pronunciation: / riˈɑrməmənt / (AmE), / riˈɑːməmənt / (BrE)
the process of equipping a country, group, or individual with new or additional weapons and military equipment, especially after a period of disarmament

Examples

rearmament of the army
He protested against the rearmament of previously disarmed nations after the peace treaty.
Many historians debate the impact of Germany's rearmament prior to World War II.
The government announced a new policy of rearmament in response to rising threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym – armament: the process of equipping with weapons
  • antonym – disarmament: the act of reducing or withdrawing military forces or weapons
