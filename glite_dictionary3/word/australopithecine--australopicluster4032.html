australopithecine

noun
extinct early humanlike bipedal primate
Pronunciation: / ˌɔstrələˈpɪθɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˌɒstrələˈpɪθɪˌsiːn / (BrE)
an extinct member of the genus Australopithecus, a group of bipedal primates that lived in Africa from about 4 to 1 million years ago and are among the earliest known hominids

Examples

an australopithecine ancestor
Fossil evidence suggests that australopithecines were among the first hominids to walk upright.
Paleoanthropologists have discovered several skulls belonging to australopithecine species in East Africa.
The brain of an australopithecine was significantly smaller than that of modern humans, but it showed signs of development.
