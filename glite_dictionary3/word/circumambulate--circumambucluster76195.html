to circumambulate

verb
walk around something
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrkəmˈæmbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈæmbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to walk all the way around something, especially as part of a ritual or ceremony

Examples

to circumambulate a temple
Each morning, the monks circumambulate the sacred statue in silent meditation.
For centuries, pilgrims have circumambulated holy sites as an act of devotion.
Visitors are required to circumambulate the temple in a clockwise direction.
