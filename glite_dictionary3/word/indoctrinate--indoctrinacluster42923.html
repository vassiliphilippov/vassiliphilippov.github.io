to indoctrinate

verb
teach beliefs uncritically
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɑktrəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɒktrɪneɪt / (BrE)
to teach a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically, especially with the aim of influencing their opinions or behavior

Examples

to indoctrinate young children
Some cults attempt to indoctrinate members from an early age.
Teachers should educate, not indoctrinate, their students.
The regime sought to indoctrinate young people with its ideology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brainwash: to make someone adopt radically different beliefs by using persistent pressure
