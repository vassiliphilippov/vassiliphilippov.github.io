dislodgement

noun
forced removal from a position
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈlɑdʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒm(ə)nt / (BrE)
the act of removing or being removed from a position, often by force or pressure, especially from a place of advantage

Examples

dislodgement of rocks
After the storm, there was significant dislodgement of rocks from the mountainside.
Improper handling can result in the dislodgement of the delicate sensors within the device.
The dislodgement of enemy troops from the hill was critical to the victory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – installation: the act of placing in position
  • synonym – removal: the act of taking something away
