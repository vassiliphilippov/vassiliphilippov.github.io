do-nothing

adjective
inactive; unwilling to act or take responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈduːˌnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈduːˌnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) unwilling or unable to take any action or responsibility; inactive or ineffective.

Examples

do-nothing attitude
His critics accused him of being a do-nothing manager who avoided tough decisions.
Many citizens were frustrated with the do-nothing attitude of the council.
Voters are unlikely to support another do-nothing administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inactive: not engaging in or involving any or much physical activity; not active
  • synonym – indolent: wanting to avoid activity or exertion; lazy
  • antonym – proactive: creating or controlling a situation by causing something to happen rather than responding to it after it has happened
