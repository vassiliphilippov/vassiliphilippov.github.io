to silhouette

verb
depict as dark outline against light
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪl.uˈɛt / (BrE)
to represent or show as a dark shape or outline against a lighter background

Examples

to silhouette a figure
A bird was silhouetted in the window as the sun set behind it.
Photographers often silhouette their subjects to create dramatic effects.
The tree was silhouetted against the evening sky.
A lone figure was silhouetted by the headlights as he crossed the road.
Lightning briefly silhouetted the mountains on the horizon.
The tree was silhouetted against the orange glow of the sunset.
