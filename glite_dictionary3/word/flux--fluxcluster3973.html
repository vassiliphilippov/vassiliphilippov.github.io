to flux

verb
move or flow smoothly in large numbers
Pronunciation: / flʌks / (AmE), / flʌks / (BrE)
(of people or things) to move or flow smoothly and continuously, especially in a large number, as if in a stream.

Examples

to flux out crowds
After the concert, fans fluxed out of the arena in waves.
As the meeting ended, employees fluxed toward the cafeteria for lunch.
During the evacuation, people fluxed through the exits in an orderly fashion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flow: to move steadily and continuously in a stream
