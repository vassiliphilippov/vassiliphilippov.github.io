parthenocarpy

noun
seedless fruit development (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrθəˈnɑːrpi / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθəˈnɒːpi / (BrE)
(in botany) the natural or artificial development of fruit without fertilization, resulting in seedless fruit.

Examples

parthenocarpy in bananas
Bananas found in most supermarkets are the result of parthenocarpy, which explains why they lack seeds.
Most commercial seedless grapes are produced using parthenocarpy techniques.
Scientists encourage parthenocarpy in crops to produce seedless fruits for easier consumption.
