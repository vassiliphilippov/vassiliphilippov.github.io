to pinch

verb
steal (informal)
Pronunciation: / pɪntʃ / (AmE), / pɪntʃ / (BrE)
(informal) To steal something, especially something small or of minor value.

Examples

to pinch a wallet
He got caught trying to pinch some chocolate bars from the corner store.
She realized her phone had been pinched at the party last night.
Someone managed to pinch my wallet while I was shopping in the market.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give back: to return something that was taken or borrowed
  • synonym – steal: to take something that belongs to someone else without permission or legal right
  • synonym – swipe: (informal) to steal something quickly or secretly
