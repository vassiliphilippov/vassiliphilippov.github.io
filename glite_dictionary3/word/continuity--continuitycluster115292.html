continuity

noun
uninterrupted duration or existence
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntɪˈnuːɪti /; / ˌkɑːntəˈnuːəti / (AmE), / ˌkɒntɪˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being continuous, unbroken, or uninterrupted over time

Examples

maintain continuity over time
Her daily meditation helped her maintain a sense of continuity through stressful times.
Historians debate the continuity of cultural practices from ancient to modern societies.
The film's story lost its continuity due to several abrupt flashbacks.
After the merger, maintaining continuity of services became a top priority.
Good teachers provide continuity in their students' learning experience.
The director ensured continuity between different scenes in the movie.
