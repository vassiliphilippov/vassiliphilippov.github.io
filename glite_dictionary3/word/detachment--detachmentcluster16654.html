detachment

noun
lack of emotional involvement
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
the state of being emotionally or personally distant; a lack of emotional involvement, bias, or connection

Examples

emotional detachment
His detachment from the situation allowed him to make fair decisions.
Journalists are often expected to write with detachment and objectivity.
She maintained a sense of detachment during the heated discussion.
His emotional detachment made it hard for others to get close to him.
The mountain village's detachment from the rest of the world gave it a unique charm.
The physical detachment of the house from the main building offered added privacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – attachment: the condition of being attached or connected
  • antonym – engagement: emotional or personal involvement
  • synonym – indifference: lack of interest, concern, or sympathy
  • synonym – isolation: the process or fact of being alone or kept apart
  • synonym – separation: the action or state of moving or being moved apart
