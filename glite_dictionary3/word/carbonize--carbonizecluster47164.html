to carbonize

verb
convert into carbon by heating or burning
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to convert or be converted into carbon, especially by heating or burning; to char

Examples

to carbonize wood
If you carbonize organic waste, it can be used as a soil amendment.
Industrial processes can carbonize wood to produce charcoal.
Too much heat will carbonize the surface of the meat, leaving it charred.

Related Terms

  • synonym – char: to partially burn so as to blacken the surface; to convert to charcoal
  • synonym – incinerate: to burn something completely to ashes
