to introduce

verb
to begin with a preface or introduction
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to begin or mark the start of something, such as a speech, event, or new era

Examples

to introduce a topic
He will introduce his talk with some background information about the project.
She likes to introduce her presentations with a funny story to engage the audience.
The book is introduced by a heartfelt letter from the author.
The discovery of penicillin introduced a new era in modern medicine.
The invention of the internet introduced unprecedented changes to global communication.
The leader's reforms introduced significant improvements in education throughout the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conclude: to bring something to an end, often with closing remarks
  • synonym – preface: to provide an introduction to a book or speech
  • synonym – usher in: to mark the start of something new, especially an era or event
