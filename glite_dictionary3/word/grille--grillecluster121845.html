grille

noun
small screened opening in a door or window
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
A small opening covered with a grating, often in a door or window, through which transactions or communications can take place.

Examples

ticket grille
A metal grille in the office door allows staff to communicate with visitors securely.
She slipped the paperwork through the narrow grille built into the reception window.
The teller spoke to me through the grille at the bank counter.

  • synonym – screen: a mesh or grating that covers an opening
