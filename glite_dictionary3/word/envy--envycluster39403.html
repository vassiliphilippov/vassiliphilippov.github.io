envy

noun
resentful desire for what someone else has
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.vi / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vi / (BrE)
a feeling of discontent or resentful longing for what someone else has

Examples

strong envy
He tried to mask his envy behind a polite smile.
She couldn't hide her envy when she saw her friend's new car.
Their success was the object of envy among their competitors.
Consumed by envy, he could not celebrate his friend's achievements.
Her envy was evident when she saw her colleague receive the promotion.
In many religions, envy is considered to be a serious sin.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – admiration: a feeling of respect and approval
  • degree higher – covetousness: intense desire to possess something that belongs to someone else
  • synonym – jealousy: resentment against a rival or against someone enjoying success or advantage
