noctuid

noun
a type of nocturnal moth, agricultural pest
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑktjuˌɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɒktjʊɪd / (BrE)
A dull-colored, medium-sized, nocturnal moth belonging to the family Noctuidae, whose larvae are often harmful agricultural pests.

Examples

noctuid moth species
Farmers must monitor their crops for noctuid larvae, which can devastate young plants.
Many noctuids are attracted to light, making them common around porch lamps at night.
The cornfields suffered heavy damage when a large population of noctuids invaded late in the season.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cutworm: larval stage of some noctuid moths, known as agricultural pests
