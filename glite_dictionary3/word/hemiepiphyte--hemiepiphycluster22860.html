hemiepiphyte

noun
plant epiphytic part of its life
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmiˈɛpɪˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˌhiːmiˈɛpɪfaɪt / (BrE)
a plant that lives as an epiphyte for part of its life cycle, attaching to another plant without permanently rooting in the soil

Examples

a hemiepiphyte plant on tree
Researchers discovered a rare hemiepiphyte species growing on the trunks of old trees.
Some hemiepiphytes begin their lives high in the canopy and later send roots to the ground.
The strangler fig is a well-known hemiepiphyte found in tropical forests.
