neocolonialism

noun
economic and political control of less developed countries
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊ.kəˈloʊ.ni.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊ.kəˈləʊ.ni.ə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the use of economic, political, or cultural pressures by a powerful country to control or influence other, typically less developed, countries, especially former colonies

Examples

neocolonialism in Africa
She wrote her thesis on the impact of neocolonialism in Africa.
Some critics argue that foreign aid can sometimes be a tool of neocolonialism.
The new trade agreements were seen as a form of neocolonialism by local activists.

Related Terms

  • synonym – colonialism: the policy or practice of acquiring control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically
