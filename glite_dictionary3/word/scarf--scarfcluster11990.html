scarf

noun
joint where wood or metal pieces lock end-to-end
Pronunciation: / skɑrf / (AmE), / skɑːf / (BrE)
A joint formed by notching the ends of two pieces of wood or metal so that they lock together end-to-end.

Examples

scarf joint wood
A well-made scarf ensures the timber sections are securely joined.
Engineers used a metal scarf to connect the structural girders.
The beams were joined with a scarf to make them long enough for the roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym – splice: joining two pieces together, especially by overlapping
