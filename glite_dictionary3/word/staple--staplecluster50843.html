staple

noun
textile fiber used for yarn
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪpəl / (BrE)
A natural fiber, such as raw cotton, wool, hemp, or flax, that can be twisted together to form yarn.

Examples

cotton staple length
Cotton with a longer staple is generally considered better for making fine yarn.
Textile manufacturers often blend different types of staple to achieve specific fabric properties.
The quality of the wool is determined by the length and strength of its staple.
