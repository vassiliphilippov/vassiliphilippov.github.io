Flavius Claudius Julianus

proper noun
Roman Emperor who restored paganism (331?–363)
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪ.vi.əs ˈklɔː.di.əs dʒuˈleɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪ.vi.əs ˈklɔː.dɪ.əs dʒuːˈlɪə.nəs / (BrE)
Roman Emperor (331?–363), nephew of Constantine the Great, who briefly restored paganism and opposed Christianity.

Examples

Flavius Claudius Julianus Roman emperor
Flavius Claudius Julianus became emperor in 361 and attempted to revive classical paganism in the Roman Empire.
Despite a short reign, Flavius Claudius Julianus left a significant mark on Roman religious history.
Historians often debate the legacy of Flavius Claudius Julianus and his policies towards Christianity.
