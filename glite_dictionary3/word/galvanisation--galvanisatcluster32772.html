galvanisation

noun
coating metal with zinc or by galvanic current
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæl.və.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡæl.və.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of coating iron or steel with a layer of zinc to protect it from rusting, or the process of applying a protective metal coating using a galvanic current

Examples

galvanisation of steel
After galvanisation, the metal surface appeared shiny and resistant to corrosion.
Modern construction relies heavily on galvanisation to extend the lifespan of iron products.
The galvanisation of steel beams is essential to prevent rust in humid environments.
