manipulatively

adverb
in a manipulative way
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪpjəˌleɪtɪvli / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊlətɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that seeks to influence or control others, often in a clever or devious way.

manipulatively control people

She spoke manipulatively to get what she wanted from the team.
The ad campaign was designed manipulatively to influence public opinion.
