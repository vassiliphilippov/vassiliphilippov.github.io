pinnatifid

adjective
leaf divided nearly to midrib; not separate leaflets
Pronunciation: / pɪˈnætɪˌfɪd / (AmE), / pɪˈnatɪfɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf) divided nearly to the midrib in broad segments, but not separated into distinct leaflets.

Examples

pinnatifid leaf shape
Many ferns have pinnatifid fronds, creating a feather-like appearance without full division.
The pinnatifid leaves of the dandelion are deeply lobed but not separated into individual leaflets.
When identifying plants, the term pinnatifid is used to describe broad, deeplycut leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pinnate: (of a leaf) having leaflets arranged on either side of a central stem
