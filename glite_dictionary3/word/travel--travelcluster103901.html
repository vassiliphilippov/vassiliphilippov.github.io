to travel

verb
move from one place to another
Pronunciation: / ˈtrævəl / (AmE), / ˈtrævəl / (BrE)
to move from one place to another, especially over a distance; to go on a journey; to spread or be communicated from place to place

Examples

sound travels far
After the storm, the sound of the waves traveled miles inland.
News of his resignation traveled quickly through the company.
She loves to travel to new countries every summer and experience different cultures.

  • antonym – stay: to remain in a place
