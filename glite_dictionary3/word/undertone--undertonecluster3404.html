undertone

noun
implicit or subdued meaning or feeling
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌtəʊn / (BrE)
a subtle or underlying quality, especially an implicit feeling or meaning beneath what is openly stated

Examples

undertone of anger
Her calm words had an undertone of irritation that was hard to miss.
The novel is light-hearted, but with a darker undertone throughout.
There was a political undertone to his speech, even though he never mentioned any parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nuance: a subtle difference in or shade of meaning
