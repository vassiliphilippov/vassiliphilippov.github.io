diestrum

noun
pause between estrous cycles (animals)
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɛstrəm / (AmE), / daɪˈɛstrəm / (BrE)
A period of reproductive inactivity or quiescence between estrous cycles in animals with several cycles per breeding season.

Examples

diestrum stage in dogs
During the diestrum stage, the female dog is not receptive to mating.
Hormone levels typically decrease during diestrum, signaling the end of the fertile period.
Veterinarians monitor the diestrum phase to help manage breeding cycles in livestock.

Related Terms

  • antonym – estrus: a recurring period of sexual receptivity and fertility in female mammals
