CMV

noun
a herpes virus causing cell enlargement and illness
Pronunciation: / siː ɛm viː / (AmE), / siː ɛm viː / (BrE)
a type of herpes virus, cytomegalovirus, that causes enlarged epithelial cells and can lead to serious illness or birth defects, especially in people with weakened immune systems

Examples

CMV infection
Patients with weakened immune systems are particularly vulnerable to severe CMV complications.
Routine screening for CMV is recommended for organ transplant recipients.
The newborn was diagnosed with CMV infection after developing persistent jaundice.
