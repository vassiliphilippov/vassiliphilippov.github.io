poisonberry

noun
herb with poisonous black berries
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzənˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənˌbɛri / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb, naturalized in America, with white flowers and hairy poisonous leaves, producing black berries that can be poisonous or edible.

Examples

poisonberry plant leaves
I mistook the black poisonberries for edible fruit after my hike.
The gardener warned us not to touch the poisonberry because of its toxic leaves.
Wild birds sometimes eat the poisonberries without being harmed, but they can be dangerous for people.
