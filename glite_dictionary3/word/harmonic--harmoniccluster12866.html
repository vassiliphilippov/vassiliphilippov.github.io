harmonic

adjective
relating to sympathetically induced vibrations
Pronunciation: / hɑːrˈmɑːnɪk / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a vibration) occurring as a result of the vibrations of a nearby or connected body, often by resonance or sympathy.

Examples

harmonic vibration
In physics, a harmonic response occurs when an object vibrates in sympathy with another.
The experiment showed how a tuning fork could induce a harmonic oscillation in a second fork.
The piano string produced a harmonic vibration in the nearby guitar, causing its strings to sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sympathetic: relating to vibrations produced by or in response to another vibration
