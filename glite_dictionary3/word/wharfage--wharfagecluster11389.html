wharfage

noun
platform extending from shore to water for ships
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrfɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːfɪdʒ / (BrE)
A platform built out from the shore into the water and supported by piles, used for providing access to ships and boats.

Examples

cargo on wharfage
Cargo was swiftly moved from the trucks onto the wharfage before being loaded onto the ship.
The old wharfage has been renovated to accommodate larger vessels.
The port authorities charge a fee for use of the wharfage facilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dock: a platform for loading and unloading ships
  • synonym – pier: a structure projecting into water used as a landing place or promenade
