degressive

adjective
decreasing by stages or steps
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a process, rate, or amount) decreasing by stages or in steps, especially at a diminishing rate.

Examples

degressive payment steps
Certain subsidies are granted on a degressive basis, decreasing over time.
The degressive pattern of the scale means that reductions are made at each step.
The company applied a degressive tax rate, so larger incomes were taxed incrementally less.

Related Terms

  • antonym – progressive: increasing or advancing by stages
