nondisjunction

noun
failure of chromosomes to separate in cell division
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪsˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪsˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
failure of homologous chromosomes or sister chromatids to separate properly during cell division, resulting in abnormal chromosome numbers in daughter cells

Examples

nondisjunction during meiosis
Cells affected by nondisjunction can have either too many or too few chromosomes.
Down syndrome is often caused by nondisjunction during meiosis.
Scientists studied the effects of nondisjunction in fruit flies to better understand chromosomal disorders.
