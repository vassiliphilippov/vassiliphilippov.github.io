to inactivate

verb
make inactive; disable
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make something inactive; to cause to stop functioning or operating

Examples

to inactivate a virus
Certain environmental conditions may inactivate the enzyme, stopping its normal function.
Researchers found a chemical that can inactivate the virus in laboratory tests.
The technician will inactivate the account until the issue is resolved.

Related Terms

  • antonym – activate: to make active or operative
  • synonym – deactivate: to make inactive or inoperative
