infirmity

noun
physical or mental weakness (esp. from age)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɜrməti / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːməti / (BrE)
physical or mental weakness, especially caused by old age or illness

Examples

chronic infirmity
After his long illness, John struggled with a lingering infirmity that limited his activities.
Despite her physical infirmities, she remained cheerful and active in the community.
The infirmity of old age made it difficult for her to walk unassisted.

Related Terms

  • synonym – frailty: the condition of being weak or delicate
  • antonym – robustness: the quality of being strong and healthy
