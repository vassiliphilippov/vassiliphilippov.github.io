to flit

verb
move lightly and quickly
Pronunciation: / flɪt / (AmE), / flɪt / (BrE)
to move swiftly and lightly from place to place; to dart or skim quickly

Examples

to flit from flower
A shadow flitted across the wall as the bird passed the window.
Her thoughts would often flit to memories of her childhood during quiet moments.
The butterfly seemed to flit from one flower to another without resting.

  • antonym – linger: stay in a place longer than necessary
