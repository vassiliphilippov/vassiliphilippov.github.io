thoracocentesis

noun
removal of fluid from the chest
Pronunciation: / ˌθɔːr.ə.koʊ.sɛnˈtiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌθɔː.rə.kəʊ.sɛnˈtiː.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure involving the removal of fluid from the chest cavity, usually for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes

Examples

thoracocentesis procedure performed
Before diagnosing the cause of the effusion, a thoracocentesis was necessary.
The doctor performed a thoracocentesis to relieve pressure in the patient's chest.
Ultrasound guidance was used to ensure the thoracocentesis was performed safely.
