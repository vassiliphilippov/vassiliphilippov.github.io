abstract

noun
general idea or concept
Pronunciation: / ˈæbˌstrækt / (AmE), / ˈab.strækt / (BrE)
A general idea or concept not associated with any specific instance; something that exists only as a thought or theory.

Examples

philosophical abstract
Beauty is an abstract that is hard to define precisely.
Philosophy often deals with abstract rather than concrete realities.
She prefers to think in terms of practical outcomes, not in the abstract.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concrete: actual thing or instance; the opposite of abstract
