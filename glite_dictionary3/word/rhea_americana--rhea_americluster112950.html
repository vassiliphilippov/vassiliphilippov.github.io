Rhea americana

noun
large flightless South American bird
Pronunciation: / ˌriːə ˌæməˈrɪkənə / (AmE), / ˌriːə ˌaməˈrɪkənə / (BrE)
A large, fast-running, flightless bird native to South America, resembling an ostrich but with three toes.

Examples

Rhea americana running fast
Farmers in Argentina sometimes keep Rhea americana for their eggs and feathers.

Unlike ostriches, Rhea americana have three toes on each foot.
