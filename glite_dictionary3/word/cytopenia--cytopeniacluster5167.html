cytopenia

noun
deficiency of blood cells
Pronunciation: / saɪtəˈpiniə / (AmE), / saɪtəˈpiːniə / (BrE)
a deficiency of one or more types of cells in the blood

Examples

cytopenia in blood tests
Certain medications can lead to cytopenia as a side effect, necessitating close blood monitoring.
Doctors monitor for cytopenia in cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy.
The patient was diagnosed with cytopenia after routine blood tests showed low cell counts.
