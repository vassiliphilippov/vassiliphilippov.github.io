holdout

noun
person refusing agreement in negotiation
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌaʊt /; / ˈhəʊld.aʊt / (BrE)
a person or group who refuses to agree to something, especially in negotiations, often hoping for better terms

Examples

contract holdout
After months of talks, the most prominent holdout eventually signed the contract.
She became known as the last holdout against the company's takeover plan.
The union's chief holdout finally agreed to the proposal after several days of negotiations.
Despite generous offers, several holdouts declined to sell their property to the developers.
She was the last holdout in the contract negotiations, refusing to sign until her demands were met.
The city's teachers became holdouts after months of talks with the school board failed to produce an agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compromiser: someone who is willing to make concessions
  • synonym – obstructionist: a person who deliberately delays or prevents progress, especially in negotiations
