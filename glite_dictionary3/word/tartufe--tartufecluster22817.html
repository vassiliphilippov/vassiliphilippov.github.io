Tartufe

noun
religious hypocrite (from Molière)
Pronunciation: / tɑːrˈtuːf / (AmE), / tɑːˈtjuːf / (BrE)
A hypocrite who feigns religious virtue or piety, especially after the character in Molière's play.

Examples

a Tartufe in church
He was exposed as a Tartufe, preaching morality while secretly profiting from corruption.
Like Molière's Tartufe, she hid her ambitions behind a façade of devotion.
Literary critics often refer to political charlatans as modern Tartufes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hypocrite: a person who pretends to have certain beliefs or feelings
