scruplesome

adjective
having strong moral principles
Pronunciation: / ˈskruːpəlsəm / (AmE), / ˈskruːpl̩səm / (BrE)
having or showing strong moral principles that prevent one from acting dishonestly or unethically

Examples

scruplesome person
He was admired for his scruplesome conduct in business dealings.
Her scruplesome nature prevents her from lying, even in difficult situations.
Though the temptation was great, his scruplesome conscience would not let him cheat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scrupulous: very concerned to avoid doing wrong
