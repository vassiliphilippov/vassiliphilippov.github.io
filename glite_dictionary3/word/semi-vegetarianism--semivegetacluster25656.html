semi-vegetarianism

noun
mainly vegetarian diet with occasional meat or fish
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmi ˌvɛdʒəˈtɛriˌænɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi ˌvɛdʒɪˈteəriːənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A dietary practice of mostly following a vegetarian diet, but occasionally including meat or fish for social, cultural, pragmatic, or nutritional reasons.

Examples

semi-vegetarianism with occasional meat
Semi-vegetarianism allows for occasional flexibility at family gatherings and social events.
His transition to semi-vegetarianism was motivated by environmental concerns, though he still eats fish occasionally.
Many people practice semi-vegetarianism to enjoy the health benefits of a plant-based diet without completely giving up meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flexitarianism: A mostly vegetarian diet with occasional inclusion of meat or fish
  • degree lower – vegetarianism: The practice of abstaining from the consumption of meat
