pisha paysha

noun
Yiddish-origin two-player card game
Pronunciation: / ˈpiʃə ˈpeiʃə / (AmE), / ˈpiʃə ˈpeɪʃə / (BrE)
A card game of Yiddish origin for two players, typically one adult and one child, where players alternate drawing cards to build up or down from a face-up card, and the winner is the one with the fewest cards when the deck is finished.

Examples

play pisha paysha cards
He taught his niece how to play pisha paysha using an old deck of cards.
In some families, pisha paysha is a beloved holiday tradition.
The children spent the afternoon playing pisha paysha with their grandmother.
