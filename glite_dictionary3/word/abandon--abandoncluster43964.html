to abandon

verb
leave a place or property empty
Pronunciation: / əˈbændən / (AmE), / əˈbændən / (BrE)
to leave someone, something, or a place behind permanently, often irresponsibly or when needed

Examples

to abandon a child

Many drivers abandon their vehicles when snow makes the roads impassable.
The residents had to abandon the village due to flooding.

It is illegal to abandon a child or leave them without proper care.
She felt betrayed when her father abandoned the family and never returned.
After the storm, many residents were forced to abandon their homes.
She would never abandon her family, no matter how difficult life became.
They had to abandon the search when night fell.

  • antonym – retain: to keep possession of something or someone
