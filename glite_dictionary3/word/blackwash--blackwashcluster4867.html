blackwash

noun
attack on someone's reputation
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
an attempt to deliberately damage or destroy someone's reputation, especially by making false or unfair allegations

Examples

political blackwash campaign
After the rumor spread, many saw it as nothing more than a blackwash orchestrated by her rivals.
The article was dismissed as a blackwash aimed at undermining the politician’s credibility.
The company faced a blackwash in the media following the unfounded accusations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – libel: the written defamation of someone's character
  • synonym – slander: the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
