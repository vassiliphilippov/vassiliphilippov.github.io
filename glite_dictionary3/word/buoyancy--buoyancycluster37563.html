buoyancy

noun
ability to float in fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔɪ.ən.si /; / ˈbɔɪənsi / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ.ən.si /; / ˈbɔɪənsi / (BrE)
the ability or tendency of something to float in water, air, or another fluid

Examples

buoyancy in water
Salt water increases the buoyancy of swimmers, making it easier to float.
Submarines control their buoyancy to dive or rise in the ocean.
The buoyancy of the boat kept it safely above the water's surface.
Helium gives the blimp enough buoyancy to float above the stadium.
She faced her challenges with the same buoyancy that always kept her spirits high.
The buoyancy of the balloon allowed it to rise gently into the sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym – density: the degree of compactness of a substance, often contrasted with buoyancy
  • synonym – floatation: the process or capability of floating on water or other liquid
