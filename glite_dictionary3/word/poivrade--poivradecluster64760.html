poivrade

noun
peppery French sauce for game meats
Pronunciation: / pwɑːˈvrɑːd / (AmE), / pwaˈvʁad / (BrE)
A French sauce, typically made for game meats such as venison, prepared with browned vegetables, meat trimmings, marinade, and a generous quantity of pepper.

Examples

poivrade sauce for venison
She served roast duck with a classic poivrade for an authentic French dinner.
The chef prepared a rich poivrade to complement the flavor of the venison.
Traditional poivrade sauce relies on both pepper and aromatic vegetables for depth of flavor.
