wickiup

noun
Native American brush hut
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪkiˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɪkiˌʌp / (BrE)
A domed hut or lodge made with a frame of poles covered with brush, matting, or grass, used traditionally by some Native American peoples of the southwestern United States.

Examples

traditional wickiup shelter
Park rangers explained how a traditional wickiup was made from available desert materials.
The Apache family built a wickiup to use as shelter during their travels.
We saw a reconstructed wickiup at the museum's outdoor exhibit.
