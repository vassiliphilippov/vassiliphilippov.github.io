common-law

adjective
based on common law system
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.mən lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən lɔː / (BrE)
Based on or relating to the common law system, as opposed to statutory or civil law.

Examples

common-law system
A judge made a ruling based on common-law principles rather than written statutes.
Certain common-law rights exist even if not explicitly stated in legislation.
The country operates under a common-law legal system influenced by British traditions.
