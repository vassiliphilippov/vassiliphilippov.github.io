to rice

verb
push food through a ricer
Pronunciation: / raɪs / (AmE), / raɪs / (BrE)
to push (food, especially cooked vegetables) through a ricer so that it becomes the consistency of rice

Examples

to rice potatoes
After boiling the potatoes, she likes to rice them for a fluffier texture.
Many chefs recommend that you rice cauliflower to use as a low-carb alternative.
To make the gnocchi, first rice the cooked potatoes and let them cool.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mash: to crush food into a soft, pulpy mass, often with a masher
