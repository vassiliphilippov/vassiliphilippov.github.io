thinned

adjective
made less thick, diluted
Pronunciation: / θɪnd / (AmE), / θɪnd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) made less thick or dense, typically by mixing with water or another thinner.

Examples

thinned paint mixture
Before serving, she added water to the soup so it became thinned and less creamy.
The artist used thinned paint to achieve a translucent effect on the canvas.
The whiskey tasted unusually light because it had been thinned with water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concentrated: (of a liquid) made stronger or thicker by removing water or other diluting agent
  • synonym – diluted: (of a liquid) made weaker or thinner by adding water or another solvent
