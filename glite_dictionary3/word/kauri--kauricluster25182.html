kauri

noun
tall New Zealand timber tree
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊri / (BrE)
A tall timber tree of New Zealand, known for its straight-grained white wood.

Examples

giant kauri tree
A single fallen kauri can yield enough timber for several large boats.
Craftsmen prize kauri wood for its smooth texture and durability.
The ancient kauri forests are protected as part of New Zealand's natural heritage.
