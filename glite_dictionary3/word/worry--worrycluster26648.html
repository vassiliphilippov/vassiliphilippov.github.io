to worry

verb
to make anxious or troubled
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝː.i / (AmE), / ˈwʌr.i / (BrE)
to make someone feel anxious or troubled about actual or potential problems

Examples

to worry your friend
His constant complaints worried her more than he realized.
It's not good to worry yourself sick about things beyond your control.
Please don't worry your parents with such bad news.
