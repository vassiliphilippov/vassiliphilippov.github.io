to cover

verb
place something over or on top
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvɚ /; / ˈkʌvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to put something over or on top of something else to protect, hide, or enclose it; to form a layer over something

Examples

to cover the pot
Please cover the pot while the soup is simmering.
She covered her face with her hands in embarrassment.
The picnic table was covered with a bright red cloth.
She used a blanket to cover the sleeping child.
The plastic sheet covered the furniture during the renovation.
This paint covers the wall very evenly and hides all imperfections.
Clouds cover the sky before it starts to rain.
Please cover the pot with a lid to keep the soup warm.
She gently covered her sleeping baby with a blanket.
