inosculation

noun
joining of structures (medical/biological)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪˌnɑːskyəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɒskyʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the natural or surgical joining of parts or branches of tubular structures, such as blood vessels or tree branches, so that they become continuous

Examples

Botanists studied the inosculation between the two tree branches, where they fused over time.
