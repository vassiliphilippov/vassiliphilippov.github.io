fine

noun
monetary penalty for breaking rules or laws
Pronunciation: / faɪn / (AmE), / faɪn / (BrE)
A sum of money that must be paid as a punishment for breaking a law or rule.

Examples

pay a fine
If you do not return your library books on time, you will have to pay a fine.
She had to pay a heavy fine for speeding in a school zone.
The company was given a fine for polluting the river.
