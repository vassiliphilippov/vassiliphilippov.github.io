stapedectomy

noun
surgical removal of the stapes bone
Pronunciation: / ˌsteɪpəˈdɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌsteɪpɪˈdɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove the stapes bone from the middle ear, usually to improve hearing

Examples

undergo stapedectomy surgery
A stapedectomy is often performed to treat otosclerosis, a condition that affects the bones of the middle ear.
Complications following a stapedectomy are rare but can include vertigo or taste disturbance.
The patient experienced significant improvement in hearing after her stapedectomy.
