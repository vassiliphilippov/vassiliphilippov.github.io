DDI

noun
drug–drug interaction
Pronunciation: / diː diː aɪ / (AmE), / diː diː aɪ / (BrE)
A drug–drug interaction: an interaction between two or more medications that changes their effects or increases the risk of harm.

Examples

DDI between medicines
Before starting the new antibiotic, the pharmacist checked for any possible DDI with her current medications.
Clinical guidelines recommend reviewing patients' prescriptions to avoid harmful DDI that increase bleeding risk.
Electronic prescribing systems warn doctors automatically when a potential DDI could reduce drug effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drug interaction: an interaction between medicines that alters their effects or side effects
