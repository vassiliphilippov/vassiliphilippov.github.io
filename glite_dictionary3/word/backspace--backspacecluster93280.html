to backspace

verb
move cursor/carriage one space back
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbækˌspeɪs / (BrE)
to move the cursor or carriage backward by one space, especially to erase the previous character when using a keyboard or typewriter

Examples

to backspace to erase
If you make a mistake while typing, just backspace to erase the error.
Remember to backspace over any extra spaces before saving the document.
She quickly backspaced the last sentence and started again.
