Jakob Ludwig Karl Grimm

proper noun
German folklorist and linguist; elder Grimm brother
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːˌkoʊb ˈlʊdvɪɡ ˈkɑrl ɡrɪm / (AmE), / ˈjɑː.kɒb ˈlʊd.vɪɡ ˈkɑːl ɡrɪm / (BrE)
(1785–1863) German philologist, linguist, and folklorist, best known for co-authoring Grimm's Fairy Tales and formulating Grimm's law of linguistics.

Examples

Jakob Ludwig Karl Grimm collected tales
Jakob Ludwig Karl Grimm played a major role in collecting and publishing traditional German folk tales.

Some fairy tales attributed to the Brothers Grimm were first published by Jakob Ludwig Karl Grimm.
