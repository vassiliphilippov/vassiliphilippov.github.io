madness

noun
rash or foolish behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈmædnəs / (AmE), / ˈmædnəs / (BrE)
rash or foolish behavior that shows a lack of good judgment

Examples

act of madness
Driving on icy roads with bald tires is an act of madness.
It would be sheer madness to invest all your money in one company.
Some people said it was madness to attempt the climb alone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – folly: lack of good sense; foolishness
  • antonym – wisdom: the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
