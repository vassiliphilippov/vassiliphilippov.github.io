despicably

adverb
in a despicable or contemptible way
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɪkəbli / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪkəbli / (BrE)
In a manner deserving strong dislike or contempt; in a way that is morally reprehensible or disgraceful.

Examples

despicably cruel
His actions during the trial were despicably dishonest and manipulative.
She was treated despicably by her supervisor for simply voicing her concerns.
The villain in the story behaved despicably toward his closest friends.
