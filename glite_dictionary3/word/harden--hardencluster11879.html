to harden

verb
make a system more secure (computing)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdən / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdən / (BrE)
(computing) to make a computer system, application, or network more secure by applying configuration changes, removing or disabling vulnerable services, and enforcing stricter access controls.

Examples

to harden a server
System administrators harden servers by disabling unnecessary services and applying security patches regularly.
Before deployment, the team hardened the application configuration to reduce attack surface and permissions.
After the breach, engineers focused on hardening endpoints and updating default passwords across devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym – secure: to protect a system or data from attack, loss, or unauthorized access
  • antonym – vulnerable: open to attack, damage, or unauthorized access
