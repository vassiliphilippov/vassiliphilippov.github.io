normalisation

noun
imposition of standards or regulations
Pronunciation: / ˌnɔːrmələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɔːməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of establishing or imposing standards or regulations to make something conform to a norm or standard

Examples

normalisation of standards
International normalisation helps ensure products are safe and compatible worldwide.
Some industries resist normalisation due to a desire for unique local methods.
The normalisation of procedures improved efficiency throughout the organization.
