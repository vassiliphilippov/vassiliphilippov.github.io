kingbird

noun
large American flycatcher bird
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪŋˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A large American flycatcher of the genus Tyrannus, known for its aggressive defense of its territory.

Examples

kingbird catches insects
During our hike, we spotted a kingbird perched on a fence post, scanning for insects.
Many birdwatchers are drawn to the vibrant calls of the kingbird during breeding season.
The kingbird is famous for fearlessly chasing away much larger birds from its nest.
