to enlighten

verb
make clear; give knowledge or understanding
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈlaɪtən /; / ɪnˈlaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtən /; / ɪnˈlaɪtn / (BrE)
to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject or situation; to clarify something

Examples

to enlighten the class
After reading the book, she felt enlightened about the subject's history.
Could you enlighten me as to why this change was necessary?
The teacher tried to enlighten us about the complex theory during the lecture.
Her lecture helped enlighten the audience about climate change.
If you have time, could you enlighten me on how this system works?
Reading widely can enlighten your perspective on many issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clarify: to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible
  • antonym – confuse: to make something unclear or difficult to understand
  • synonym – inform: to give information; to tell
  • antonym – mislead: to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
