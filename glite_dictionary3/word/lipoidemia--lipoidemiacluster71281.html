lipoidemia

noun
excess lipids in the blood
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪpoʊɪˈdiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌlɪpɔɪˈdiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an excessive amount of lipids (fats) in the blood.

Examples

lipoidemia diagnosis result
Patients with lipoidemia are at an increased risk of developing cardiovascular disease.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check for lipoidemia after noticing high cholesterol levels.
Treatment of lipoidemia often involves dietary changes and medication.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hyperlipidemia: an excess of lipids in the blood
  • antonym – hypolipidemia: an abnormally low concentration of lipids in the blood
