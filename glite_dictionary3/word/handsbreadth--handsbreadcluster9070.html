handsbreadth

noun
unit of length based on a hand's width
Pronunciation: / ˈhændzˌbrɛdθ / (AmE), / ˈhændzbrɛdθ / (BrE)
a unit of length based on the width of the human hand, traditionally used as a rough measurement equal to about 3 or 4 inches (8–10 cm)

Examples

a handsbreadth wide
He moved just a handsbreadth closer to get a better view.
The window measured only a handsbreadth wide, letting in little light.
There was barely a handsbreadth between the two parked cars.
