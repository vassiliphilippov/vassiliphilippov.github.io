offstage

adjective
concealed from public view; secret
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
not visible or known to the public; happening secretly or in private, away from public attention

Examples

offstage talks
Much of politics happens offstage and never reaches the public.
The company's offstage strategies often surprised competitors.
There were many offstage negotiations that determined the outcome.

Related Terms

  • antonym – public: open to all people; not private
  • synonym – secret: not known or seen or not meant to be known or seen by others
