procession

noun
orderly movement of people or vehicles
Pronunciation: / prəˈsɛʃ.ən /; / prəˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈsɛʃ.ən /; / prəˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a group of people, animals, or vehicles moving forward in an orderly, formal way, especially as part of a ceremony or public event

Examples

funeral procession
A long procession of cars followed the hearse through the streets.
The annual religious festival featured a colorful procession through the city center.
The graduation procession moved slowly across the campus lawn.
A funeral procession moved slowly along the street toward the cemetery.
A long procession of cars followed the bride and groom from the church.
The children watched the colorful procession pass by during the holiday festival.
