to let

verb
allow someone to rent property
Pronunciation: / lɛt / (AmE), / lɛt / (BrE)
to allow someone to use or occupy property under a contract, usually for rent

Examples

to let a house
She decided to let her flat while she traveled abroad.
The cottage was let to students for the summer.
They have let their house on a one-year contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lease: to grant possession and use of property by contract, especially for rent
  • synonym – rent out: to allow someone to use property in exchange for payment
