metazoan

noun
multicellular animal; non-protozoan
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
any animal belonging to the subkingdom Metazoa, comprising all multicellular animals except protozoans and sponges.

Examples

a metazoan animal
Modern biologists study the development of metazoan embryos to understand multicellularity.
Most animals familiar to us, from insects to mammals, are classified as metazoan organisms.
The discovery of ancient metazoan fossils has shed light on the evolution of early animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym – protozoan: single-celled organism of the former group Protozoa
