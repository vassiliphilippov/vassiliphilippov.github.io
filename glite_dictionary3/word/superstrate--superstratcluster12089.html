superstrate

noun
dominant invading language influencing another
Pronunciation: / ˈsuː.pərˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌstreɪt / (BrE)
A language introduced by later invaders or dominant groups that is imposed on an indigenous population and contributes features to their language.

Examples

superstrate language influence
Portuguese functioned as the superstrate in many West African pidgin languages.
The French language served as a superstrate in the formation of Middle English.
When examining the vocabulary of creole languages, linguists often identify the superstrate influence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – substrate: a language spoken by an indigenous population that is influenced by a dominant invading language
