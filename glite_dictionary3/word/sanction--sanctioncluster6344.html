to sanction

verb
give official approval
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋk.ʃən /; / ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.ʃən /; / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
to give official permission or approval for an action, especially by an authority

Examples

to sanction a project
No building will be sanctioned without full safety inspections.
The government refused to sanction the use of the drug until further tests had been completed.
The university would not sanction the student protest unless it complied with campus rules.
The board refused to sanction the merger without additional information.
The committee decided to sanction the new research project after reviewing the proposal.
The government has chosen to sanction stricter emissions standards for factories.

  • antonym – forbid: to refuse to allow; to prohibit

  • antonym – prohibit: to formally forbid something by law, rule, or authority
