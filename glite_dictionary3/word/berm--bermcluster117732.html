berm

noun
narrow ledge or shelf at a slope
Pronunciation: / bɝm / (AmE), / bɜːm / (BrE)
a narrow ledge, shelf, or raised strip of land, typically at the top or bottom of a slope, often used to control water flow or provide support

Examples

earth berm
A berm was built along the riverbank to prevent flooding during heavy rains.
Hikers often stop to rest on the berm at the top of the trail.
The construction crew added a small berm to direct runoff away from the road.
