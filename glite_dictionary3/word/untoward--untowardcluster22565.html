untoward

adjective
improper; socially inappropriate
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɔːd / (BrE)
(of behavior or events) not in keeping with accepted standards of what is proper or appropriate in polite society; improper or inappropriate.

Examples

untoward behavior
Any untoward conduct at the formal dinner will not be tolerated.
His comments at the meeting were considered untoward and offended several participants.
The principal addressed the student's untoward behavior during the assembly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decent: conforming to accepted standards; proper
  • synonym – improper: not in keeping with accepted standards of behavior
