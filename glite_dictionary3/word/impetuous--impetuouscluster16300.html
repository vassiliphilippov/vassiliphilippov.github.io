impetuous

adjective
moving with great force or violence
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛtʃuəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛtjʊəs / (BrE)
(of movement, force, or action) moving with great energy or violence; forceful or rapid.

Examples

impetuous river flow
During the storm, impetuous gusts of wind battered the coastal village.
He watched the impetuous waves crash relentlessly against the rocks.
The impetuous river rushed through the narrow gorge, churning up debris in its path.

  • antonym – gentle: having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament or character
  • synonym – violent: using or involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill
