rich

adjective
pleasantly full and mellow (sound, color, etc.)
Pronunciation: / rɪtʃ / (AmE), / rɪtʃ / (BrE)
(of a color, sound, or similar quality) strong, deep, full, or intense

Examples

rich color
He admired the rich tones of the old violin.
The orchestra produced a rich and harmonious sound during the concert.
The singer's rich voice captivated the audience.
He chose a rich burgundy tie to go with his suit for the wedding.
The artist blended several pigments to create a rich shade of blue for the ocean.
The living room was painted a rich emerald green that made it feel cozy and inviting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pale: (of a color) light and not intense
  • synonym – resonant: (of sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • antonym – thin: (of sound) lacking fullness or richness
  • synonym – vivid: (of a color) intensely deep or bright
