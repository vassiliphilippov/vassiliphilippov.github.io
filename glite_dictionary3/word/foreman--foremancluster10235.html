foreman

noun
leader and spokesperson of a jury
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmən / (BrE)
the member of a jury who is chosen to act as its leader and spokesperson

Examples

foreman of jury
After a long deliberation, the foreman announced the decision to the court.
The foreman delivered the jury's verdict in a clear and steady voice.
The judge asked the foreman if the jury had reached a unanimous verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foreperson: gender-neutral term for the leader of a jury
