to luff

verb
to turn a sailing vessel toward the wind
Pronunciation: / lʌf / (AmE), / lʌf / (BrE)
to turn a boat or sail toward the wind so that the sails flap or lose power

Examples

to luff the sails
If you luff too much, the sails will lose their power and begin to flap.
She skillfully luffed the yacht to avoid hitting the buoy.
The captain ordered the crew to luff as the wind shifted suddenly.
If you point the boat too far into the wind, the jib will luff and lose power.
The skipper shouted for the crew to trim the sails before they started to luff.
When the wind shifted suddenly, the mainsail began to luff near the harbor entrance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bear away: to steer a sailing vessel away from the wind
