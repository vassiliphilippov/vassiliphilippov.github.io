congested

adjective
abnormally full of blood or fluid
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛstɪd / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) abnormally full of blood or other fluid, often causing swelling or discomfort.

Examples

congested nose
After the injury, her ankle became congested and swollen.
Her sinuses were congested due to a severe cold.
The doctor explained that his lungs were partially congested with blood.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear: not blocked or filled up; unobstructed
  • synonym – swollen: (of a part of the body) enlarged by fluid retention or inflammation
