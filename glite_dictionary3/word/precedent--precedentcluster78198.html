precedent

noun
an earlier legal decision serving as authority
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdənt / (BrE)
(in law) a previous case or legal decision that may be or (in common law) must be followed in subsequent similar cases.

Examples

legal precedent
Lawyers searched for a precedent that would support their client's case.
Setting a new precedent can shape future interpretations of the law.
The court relied on the precedent set by an earlier ruling when making its decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authority: a source able to supply or verify information, or having the power to influence or decide
  • synonym – example: something that serves as a model or reference for others
