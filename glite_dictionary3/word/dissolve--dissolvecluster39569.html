dissolve

noun
film editing transition between scenes
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
a gradual transition between two scenes in a film, where the next scene appears as the previous one fades out

Examples

film dissolve effect
Editors often use a dissolve to suggest the passage of time.
That old movie features a dissolve every time the location changes.
The director chose a dissolve to connect the two emotional scenes smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fade: a gradual transition to or from a blank image or sound, often used in film or TV
