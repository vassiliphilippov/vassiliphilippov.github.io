to insinuate

verb
introduce oneself subtly
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɪn.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪn.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
to introduce or insert oneself or something into a situation or group in a subtle, often indirect way

Examples

to insinuate oneself into a group
By volunteering for small tasks, she began to insinuate herself into the leader’s inner circle.
He managed to insinuate himself into the exclusive club despite not being invited.
The undercover agent tried to insinuate himself into the organization to learn more about their activities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – insert: to put something into something else
  • synonym – intrude: to enter without permission or welcome
