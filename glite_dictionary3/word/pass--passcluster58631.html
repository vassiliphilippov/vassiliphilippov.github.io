to pass

verb
move over or through something
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
to move something (such as a hand or eyes) over, along, or through the surface or length of something else

Examples

to pass a hand
He passed his eyes along the bookshelf, searching for the right title.
She passed her hands gently over the fabric to feel its texture.
The jeweler passed a cloth through the ring to polish it carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glide: to move smoothly and continuously over a surface
  • synonym – stroke: to move one's hand with gentle pressure over a surface, typically repeatedly
