to snub

verb
ignore or reject bluntly
Pronunciation: / snʌb / (AmE), / snʌb / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or reject someone in a way that shows disrespect or disapproval

Examples

to snub a friend
After not receiving an invitation, she felt deeply hurt that her friends had snubbed her.
He snubbed our offer without even considering it.
The actor snubbed reporters outside the theater, refusing to answer any questions.
Despite being greeted warmly, the celebrity snubbed the fans waiting outside the theatre.
He snubbed his old friend at the party by pretending not to recognize him.
She felt hurt when her colleague snubbed her in front of the entire team.

  • synonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
