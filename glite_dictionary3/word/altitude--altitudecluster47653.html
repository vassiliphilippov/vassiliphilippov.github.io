altitude

noun
angular distance above the horizon
Pronunciation: / ˈæltɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈaltɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the angular distance of a celestial object above the horizon, measured in degrees

Examples

altitude of the sun
The altitude of the sun determines the length of the shadow.
The star reached its highest altitude at midnight.
To measure a planet’s altitude, point your telescope above the horizon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – azimuth: angular measurement in a horizontal plane, perpendicular concept in navigation
  • synonym – elevation: angular height above the horizon in astronomy
