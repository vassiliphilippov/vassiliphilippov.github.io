inflated

adjective
pretentious or grandiose (especially of language)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪtəd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of language, ideas, or claims) pretentious or excessively grand in style or tone.

Examples

inflated language
His inflated claims about his achievements made him sound arrogant and untrustworthy.
She was known for her inflated speeches, filled with complicated words and grandiose ideas.
The politician's inflated promises were met with skepticism by the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bombastic: high-sounding but with little meaning; inflated
  • antonym – modest: not excessively grand or boastful; humble
  • synonym – pretentious: attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit than is actually possessed
