tranquilizer

noun
anxiety-reducing drug
Pronunciation: / ˈtræŋkwəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtræŋkwɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a drug that calms a person and reduces anxiety or tension without causing significant loss of mental clarity

Examples

take a tranquilizer pill
He refused to take a tranquilizer because he was worried about its side effects.
Some animals receive a tranquilizer before undergoing surgery to keep them calm.
The doctor prescribed a tranquilizer to help her manage her anxiety.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedative: a drug that calms or induces sleep
  • antonym – stimulant: a drug that increases alertness or energy
