to entrap

verb
to catch or trap using deceit
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtræp / (AmE), / ɪnˈtræp / (BrE)
to catch or trick someone into a trap, especially by using deception or skillful planning

to entrap a criminal

The police managed to entrap the criminal by setting up a clever sting operation.
Wild animals can often be entrapped by nets or snares left in the forest.
