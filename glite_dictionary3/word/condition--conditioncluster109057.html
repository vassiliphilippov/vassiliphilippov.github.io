to condition

verb
base analysis on specified variables (statistical)
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(in statistics and data analysis) to restrict, base, or compute an analysis given particular variables or known values; to evaluate quantities (such as probabilities or estimates) conditional on specified information.

Examples

to condition on variables
We condition the analysis on age and income to control for demographics.
When estimating causal effects, researchers condition on baseline covariates to reduce confounding bias.
The model was conditioned on observed test scores before predicting students' future performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conditional probability: the probability of an event given that another event has occurred or given known values
  • synonym – control: to adjust or account for variables in an analysis to reduce confounding
