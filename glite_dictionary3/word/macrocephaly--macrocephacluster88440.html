macrocephaly

noun
abnormally large head (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a person has an abnormally large head, without increased intracranial pressure and with symmetrical overgrowth

Examples

diagnosed with macrocephaly
Genetic counseling was recommended for families with a history of macrocephaly.
The pediatrician examined the infant and diagnosed macrocephaly based on the unusually large head circumference.
Unlike hydrocephalus, macrocephaly does not typically present with increased pressure inside the skull.
