to joke

verb
say something to make others laugh
Pronunciation: / doʊk /; / dʒoʊk / (AmE), / dəʊk /; / dʒəʊk / (BrE)
to say or do things to make people laugh; to speak or act humorously or playfully

Examples

to joke with friends
He likes to joke with his friends to lighten the mood.
Please tell me you’re only joking about moving abroad!
When the meeting grew tense, she tried to joke but nobody laughed.
He loves to joke with his colleagues to lighten the mood.
Please don't take it seriously—I was only joking.
She was clearly joking when she said she'd move to the moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jest: to speak or act in a joking or playful way; to make fun
  • antonym – mock: to make fun of someone, often unkindly
  • synonym – tease: to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful way
