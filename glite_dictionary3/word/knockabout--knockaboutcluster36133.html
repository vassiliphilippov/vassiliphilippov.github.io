knockabout

adjective
boisterous, physical humor or action
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑk.əˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.əˌbaʊt / (BrE)
(of entertainment or activity) full of rough, boisterous, and energetic humor or action, often in a physical or slapstick manner.

Examples

knockabout comedy
The comedian was famous for his knockabout humor, which always had the audience in stitches.
The play featured a lot of knockabout action, with actors chasing each other across the stage.
They enjoyed the film’s knockabout comedy, filled with pratfalls and physical gags.
