Donatello

proper noun
Florentine Renaissance sculptor
Pronunciation: / ˌdoʊnəˈtɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˌdɒnəˈtɛləʊ / (BrE)
(1386–1466) An Italian sculptor from Florence, renowned for his lifelike sculptures and a major figure of the early Renaissance.

Examples

Donatello sculpture masterpiece
During our trip to Florence, we admired several works by Donatello in the museums.
Many art historians consider Donatello to be one of the pioneers of Renaissance sculpture.
The lifelike quality of Donatello's bronze David set a new standard for artists.
