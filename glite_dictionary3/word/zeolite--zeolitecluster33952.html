zeolite

noun
a type of hydrated aluminosilicate mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈziːəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈziːəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
any of a group of glassy minerals composed of hydrated aluminosilicates of calcium, sodium, or potassium, typically found in volcanic rocks and used as molecular sieves or ion-exchange agents

Natural zeolite deposits are often mined for use in water purification and agriculture.
