hemoprotein

noun
protein containing heme group
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊˈproʊtiːn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
A protein with a heme group (containing iron and porphyrin) as its prosthetic group, essential in various biological processes such as oxygen transport and cellular respiration.

Examples

hemoprotein in blood cells
Cytochromes, a large family of hemoproteins, play a key role in cellular respiration.
Hemoglobin is a well-known hemoprotein responsible for carrying oxygen in the blood.
Researchers studied the structure of the bacterial hemoprotein to better understand its function.
