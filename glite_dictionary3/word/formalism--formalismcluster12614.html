formalism

noun
theory that formal statements lack intrinsic meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrməˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The theory that formal statements, such as those in logic or mathematics, lack intrinsic meaning, and their symbols serve mainly to demonstrate useful forms or structures.

Examples

mathematical formalism
In discussions of logic, formalism opposes views that ascribe inherent meaning to mathematical language.
Some philosophers adhere to formalism, arguing that mathematical statements are just manipulations of symbols without meaning.
The debate between intuitionism and formalism shaped much of twentieth-century mathematical philosophy.
