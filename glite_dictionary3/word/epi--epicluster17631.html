EPI

noun
Eysenck Personality Inventory; personality test
Pronunciation: / ˌiː.piːˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː.piːˈaɪ / (BrE)
a self-report questionnaire designed to assess personality traits based on Hans Eysenck's factor analysis, focusing on dimensions such as extraversion and neuroticism

Examples

EPI personality test
After completing the EPI, the subject discussed her results with the counselor.
Psychologists often administer the EPI to measure levels of extraversion and neuroticism.
The EPI was used in the study to evaluate participants' personality types.
