to burn

verb
become sunburned by sunlight
Pronunciation: / bɝn / (AmE), / bɜːn / (BrE)
to damage or injure something by heat, fire, or radiation (including sunburn)

Examples

to burn your skin
Despite the cloudy weather, she still managed to burn during her afternoon walk.
Don't forget your sunscreen or you'll burn after only an hour at the beach.
I tend to burn very quickly if I don't wear a hat on sunny days.
Be careful not to burn your fingers when taking the tray out of the oven.
She accidentally burned the toast while making breakfast this morning.
Too much exposure to the sun can burn your skin.
