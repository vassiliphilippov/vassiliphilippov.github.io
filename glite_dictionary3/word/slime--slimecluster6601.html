slime

noun
thick, sticky substance
Pronunciation: / slaɪm / (AmE), / slaɪm / (BrE)
a thick, slippery or sticky substance, often unpleasant, that is viscous in nature

Examples

green slime
After the storm, slime coated the sidewalk and made it slippery.
The children played with homemade slime in bright, vibrant colors.
The pond was covered in a thick layer of green slime.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – grime: dirt that forms a layer on the surface of something, often sticky or greasy
  • synonym – mucus: a slimy substance produced in the body, especially by mucous membranes
  • synonym – ooze: soft, slimy mud or a similar viscous substance
