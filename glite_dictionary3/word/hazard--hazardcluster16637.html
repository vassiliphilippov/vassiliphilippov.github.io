to hazard

verb
put at risk; expose to danger
Pronunciation: / ˈhæzɚd /; / ˈhæzərd / (AmE), / ˈhæzəd /; / ˈhazəd / (BrE)
to risk something or put it in danger

Examples

to hazard your money
He decided to hazard everything on a single investment.
She was not willing to hazard a guess about the outcome.
They did not want to hazard their safety by entering the abandoned building.
He did not hazard a response, fearing he might say the wrong thing.
If you hazard your reputation on this project, you must be certain of its success.
She decided to hazard a guess, even though she wasn't sure of the answer.

  • synonym – risk: to expose to danger or loss
