execution

noun
completion of a legal document by signing
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksɪˈkjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The formal act of signing, sealing, and sometimes delivering a legal document, such as a contract or deed, to make it legally valid and enforceable.

Examples

formal execution of a contract
Delays in the execution of the agreement can have serious legal consequences.
Proper execution of the deed is required for it to be considered legally binding.
The execution of the contract took place in the presence of both parties and their lawyers.
