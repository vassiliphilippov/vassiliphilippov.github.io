obscureness

noun
quality of being unclear or hard to see
Pronunciation: / əbˈskjʊrnəs / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊənɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being unclear or hard to see, especially due to insufficient light

Examples

obscureness in darkness
Rain and fog added to the obscureness of the gloomy evening.
She stumbled in the obscureness before the lights were turned on.
The obscureness of the room made it difficult to find the missing keys.
