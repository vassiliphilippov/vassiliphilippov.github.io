to waver

verb
emit unsteady or fluctuating sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪvə / (BrE)
(of a sound) to become unsteady, varying in loudness or pitch.

Examples

a waver in sound
As the wind increased, the distant siren seemed to waver between high and low pitches.
Her voice began to waver as she sang the final notes of the song.
The old radio's signal would sometimes waver, making it hard to hear clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – quaver: (of a voice) to shake or tremble, often when nervous or emotional
