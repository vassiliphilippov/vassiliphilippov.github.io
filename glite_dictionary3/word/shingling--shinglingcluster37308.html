shingling

noun
sediment with uniformly tilted flat pebbles
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
(Geology) Sediment consisting of flat pebbles that are uniformly tilted in the same direction.

Examples

shingling in sediment
Evidence of shingling was found in the ancient delta sediments, revealing the direction of the paleocurrents.
The geologist noted clear shingling in the riverbed deposits, with all the pebbles slanting eastward.
The presence of shingling can indicate water flow patterns during the time of sediment deposition.
