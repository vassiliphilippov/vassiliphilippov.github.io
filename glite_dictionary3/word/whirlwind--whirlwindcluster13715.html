whirlwind

noun
a fast, spinning column of air
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝlˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlˌwɪnd / (BrE)
A column of air moving rapidly around and over itself in a vertical movement over the Earth's surface, often causing swirling debris.

Examples

a whirlwind of dust
A sudden whirlwind swept leaves and dust high into the air.
As we hiked, we saw a small whirlwind dancing across the field.
The farm was damaged when a powerful whirlwind passed through the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cyclone: a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure
  • synonym – tornado: a violent rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground
