stinkbird

noun
ill-smelling South American bird (hoatzin)
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a crested, ill-smelling South American bird (the hoatzin), whose young have claws on the first and second digits of their wings

Examples

the stinkbird in the Amazon
Local villagers often spot the stinkbird along the riverbanks in the Amazon Basin.
The stinkbird is easily recognized by its pungent odor and distinctive crest.
Young stinkbirds use their wing claws to climb among the branches.
