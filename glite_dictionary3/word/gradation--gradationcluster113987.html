gradation

noun
relative position in a graded series
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡreɪˈdeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a slight difference or change in degree or intensity seen as part of a continuous sequence; the relative position within such a series

Examples

color gradation
The artist carefully blended the colors to achieve subtle gradations on the canvas.
The sky displays beautiful gradations of blue as the sun sets.
There are many gradations of responsibility within the company, each with distinct duties.
