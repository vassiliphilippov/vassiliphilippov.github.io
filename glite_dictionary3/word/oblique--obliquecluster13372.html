oblique

noun
any grammatical case other than nominative
Pronunciation: / əˈbliːk / (AmE), / əˈbliːk / (BrE)
(in grammar) any case of a noun, pronoun, or adjective other than the nominative, often used to indicate a role other than the subject of a sentence.

Examples

oblique case usage
In Latin, the accusative is considered an oblique case.
Many modern English pronouns only preserve remnants of the oblique case.
The student found it challenging to distinguish between the nominative and oblique cases.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nominative: the grammatical case used for a subject of a verb
