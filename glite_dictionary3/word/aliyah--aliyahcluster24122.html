aliyah

noun
Jewish immigration to Israel
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈliːjə / (AmE), / aˈliːjə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) the immigration of Jews to Israel as a return to their ancestral homeland.

Examples

make aliyah to Israel
After graduating, David decided to make aliyah and start a new life in Israel.
Many families consider aliyah not only a physical journey but also a spiritual one.
The government's programs are designed to encourage aliyah from North America and Europe.
