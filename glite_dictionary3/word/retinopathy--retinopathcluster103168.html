retinopathy

noun
disease of the retina
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtɪˈnɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˌrɛtɪˈnɒpəθi / (BrE)
A disease of the retina that may result in loss of vision.

Examples

retinopathy in diabetes
Diabetic retinopathy is a common cause of vision loss in adults.
If left untreated, retinopathy can lead to permanent blindness.
The ophthalmologist specializes in diagnosing and treating various forms of retinopathy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – maculopathy: disease of the macula of the retina
  • synonym – retinitis: inflammation of the retina
