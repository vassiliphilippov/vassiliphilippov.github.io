to pilfer

verb
steal small items repeatedly
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪlfɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlfə / (BrE)
to steal things of relatively little value, especially in small quantities or on several occasions

Examples

to pilfer small items
He was caught trying to pilfer office supplies from the storeroom.
She had a habit of pilfering snacks from the kitchen at night.
Tourists are warned not to pilfer coins from the wishing well.
