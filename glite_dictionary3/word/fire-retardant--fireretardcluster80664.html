fire-retardant

adjective
resistant to catching fire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪər rɪˌtɑrdnt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə rɪˌtɑːdənt / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) difficult to burn or slow to catch fire, often specially treated to resist fire.

Examples

fire-retardant fabric
All children's sleepwear must be made from fire-retardant materials to ensure safety.
The curtains in the theater are fire-retardant to reduce the risk of fire spreading.
They applied a fire-retardant coating to the wooden beams during construction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flame-resistant: not easily ignited or burned by fire
  • antonym – flammable: easily set on fire; combustible
