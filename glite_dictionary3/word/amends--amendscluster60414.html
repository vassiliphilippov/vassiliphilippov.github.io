amends

noun
compensation for loss, injury, or wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛndz / (AmE), / əˈmɛndz / (BrE)
something (such as money or actions) given or done to make up for a loss, injury, or wrongdoing; compensation or reparation

Examples

make amends for mistakes
Making amends to those he had hurt was the first step in his recovery.
She offered to pay amends for the damage she had caused to the car.
The company made financial amends after the lawsuit was settled.
After the accident, he offered to pay for the repairs as amends.
She wanted to make amends for her harsh words by writing him a letter of apology.
They spent years trying to make amends for their mistakes in the past.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compensation: something, typically money, awarded to someone in recognition of loss or suffering
  • antonym – offense: a breach of a law or rule; an illegal act (contrast, relates to cause of making amends)
  • synonym – penance: voluntary self-punishment as an outward expression of repentance for having done wrong
  • synonym – reparation: making amends for a wrong by paying money or helping those who have been wronged
