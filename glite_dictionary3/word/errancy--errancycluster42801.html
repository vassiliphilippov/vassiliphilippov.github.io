errancy

noun
tendency to err; fallibility
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrənsi / (AmE), / ˈɛrənsi / (BrE)
the tendency to make mistakes or to be fallible

Examples

repeated errancy at work

Philosophers debate whether errancy is an essential quality of human nature.
The judge acknowledged the errancy of human decisions in the legal process.
