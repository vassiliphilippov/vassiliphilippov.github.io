calendar

noun
system for organizing and dividing the year
Pronunciation: / ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ɪn.də / (BrE)
A system for organizing days for social, religious, commercial, or administrative purposes, defining the beginning, length, and divisions of the year.

Examples

solar calendar
Ancient civilizations each developed their own calendar to track the seasons and agricultural cycles.
Because of differences in the lunar and solar calendar, some holidays fall on varying dates each year.
The Gregorian calendar is the most widely used system of timekeeping in the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym – almanac: an annual calendar containing important dates and information
