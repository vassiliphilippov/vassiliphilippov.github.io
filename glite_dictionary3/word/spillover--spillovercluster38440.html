spillover

noun
indirect effect, especially in economics
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪlˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈspɪlˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an indirect or secondary effect of an activity or policy, especially when the effect extends beyond the original context; in economics, an unintended effect of public expenditure on other sectors or areas.

Examples

spillover effects
The spillover effects of the policy were felt throughout neighboring industries.
There was a significant spillover from government investment into the private sector.
Tourism growth can create a spillover that benefits the whole local economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contagion: the spread of effects or influence from one area to another, often used in finance
  • synonym – externality: an economic side effect or consequence that affects others
