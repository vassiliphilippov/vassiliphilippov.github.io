cyclopaedia

noun
historical term for encyclopedia
Pronunciation: / saɪkləˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / sʌɪkləˈpiːdɪə / (BrE)
a comprehensive reference work containing articles on a wide range of topics, often arranged alphabetically; chiefly an old or British spelling of 'encyclopedia'

Examples

cyclopaedia of science
Many authors in the 18th century contributed to the famous Chambers's Cyclopaedia.
She consulted the cyclopaedia to write her research paper on ancient civilizations.
The library houses a rare Victorian cyclopaedia with hand-colored illustrations.
