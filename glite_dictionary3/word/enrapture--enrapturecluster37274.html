to enrapture

verb
to fill with intense delight
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈræptʃər / (AmE), / ɪnˈræptʃə / (BrE)
to give intense pleasure or joy to; to hold completely spellbound by delight

Examples

to enrapture an audience
He was enraptured by the breathtaking view from the mountain top.
Her storytelling skills enrapture children and adults alike.
The beauty of the music enraptured the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – captivate: to attract and hold the interest of someone deeply
  • synonym – entrance: to fill someone with wonder and delight, holding their attention
