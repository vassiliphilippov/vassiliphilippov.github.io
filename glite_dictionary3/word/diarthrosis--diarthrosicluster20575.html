diarthrosis

noun
freely movable joint
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.ɑrˈθroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ɑːˈθrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
Anatomy. A freely movable joint, such as the shoulder, hip, or knee, in which the bones are separated by a fluid-filled cavity.

Examples

diarthrosis in the knee
Damage to a diarthrosis can significantly limit a person's mobility.
Most joints in the limbs are classified as diarthroses, providing a wide range of movement.
The knee is an example of a diarthrosis that allows for both flexion and extension.
