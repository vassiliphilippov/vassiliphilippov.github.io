restraint

noun
rule or condition limiting freedom
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
a rule, condition, or measure that limits or restricts freedom, action, or movement

Examples

legal restraint rule
The government imposed new restraints on overseas investment to protect domestic industries.
There are strict restraints on what prisoners can do during their free time.
Without proper restraint, companies could exploit natural resources without regard for the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freedom: the power or right to act, speak, or think without hindrance or restraint
  • synonym – restriction: a limiting condition or measure
