midrib

noun
the central vein of a leaf
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌrɪb / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.rɪb / (BrE)
the main or central vein running through the middle of a leaf, providing support and transporting nutrients

Examples

midrib of a leaf
Damage to the midrib can affect the entire structure of the leaf.
The midrib of the leaf is often thicker and more prominent than the other veins.
Water and nutrients are transported along the midrib to the rest of the leaf.
