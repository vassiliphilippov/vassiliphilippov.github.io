Bartolommeo Eustachio

proper noun
Italian anatomist and pioneer of anatomy
Pronunciation: / baˌrtoʊ.loʊˈmeɪ.oʊ juˌstæˈkioʊ / (AmE), / baˌtə.lɒˈmeɪ.oʊ juˌstæˈkioʊ / (BrE)
(1520–1574) Italian anatomist, regarded as one of the founders of modern anatomy, noted for his detailed descriptions of the ear and the heart.

Examples

Bartolommeo Eustachio described anatomy
Bartolommeo Eustachio was one of the first to describe the structures of the inner ear in detail.
Medical students study the discoveries made by Bartolommeo Eustachio in their anatomy courses.
The Eustachian tube, named after Bartolommeo Eustachio, connects the middle ear to the nasopharynx.
