to layer

verb
arrange or form in layers
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪə / (BrE)
to arrange or form into layers; to place material or substances in distinct levels, one on top of another

Examples

to layer clothes
The artist will layer several coats of paint to achieve the desired effect.
They carefully layered leaves and soil to build a compost heap.
To create the cake, first layer the sponge and cream alternately.
