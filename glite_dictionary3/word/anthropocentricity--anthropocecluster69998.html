anthropocentricity

noun
viewing reality from a human-centered perspective
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpoʊˌsɛnˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəʊˌsɛnˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
The tendency or practice of interpreting or regarding the world in terms of human values and experiences.

Examples

anthropocentricity in thinking
Many environmentalists criticize anthropocentricity for its role in ecological degradation.
Rejecting anthropocentricity, some thinkers advocate for a more biocentric viewpoint.
The philosopher argued that anthropocentricity limits our understanding of other forms of life.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anthropocentrism: the belief that humans are the central or most important entity
  • antonym – ecocentrism: a belief that prioritizes ecological concerns above human concerns
