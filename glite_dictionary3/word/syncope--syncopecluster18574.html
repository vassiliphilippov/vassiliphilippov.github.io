syncope

noun
temporary loss of consciousness; fainting
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkəpi / (BrE)
a temporary loss of consciousness caused by a sudden decrease in blood flow to the brain

Examples

syncope after standing
During the marathon, several runners suffered from syncope due to dehydration and heat.
If you have a history of syncope, consult a doctor for a thorough cardiovascular evaluation.
The patient experienced syncope after standing up too quickly from the hospital bed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fainting: losing consciousness for a short time due to lack of blood to the brain
