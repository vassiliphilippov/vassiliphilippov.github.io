Yucca glauca

noun
a species of yucca plant in central U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈjukə ˈɡlaʊkə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ˈɡlaʊkə / (BrE)
A species of yucca native to the west central United States, characterized by a clump of grasslike leaves at the base and a tall stalk bearing a cluster of small whitish flowers.
