Doctor

noun
saint recognized for major theological contributions (Catholicism)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a title given to certain saints recognized for their significant contribution to theology or doctrine through their writings or teachings.

Examples

Doctor of the Church
Only a select few saints have been honored with the title of Doctor for their doctrinal contributions.
Saint Augustine was declared a Doctor of the Church for his influential writings.
The teachings of Saint Theresa, a Doctor of the Church, continue to inspire many.
