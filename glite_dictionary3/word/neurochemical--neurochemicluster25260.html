neurochemical

noun
chemical affecting nervous system activity
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
a chemical substance that influences or is involved in the activity of the nervous system, especially neurotransmitters and other molecules affecting brain function

Examples

neurochemical imbalance
An imbalance in neurochemicals can lead to mental health disorders such as depression.
Drugs that affect neurochemical activity are sometimes used to treat neurological diseases.
Researchers are studying how certain neurochemicals influence mood and behavior.
