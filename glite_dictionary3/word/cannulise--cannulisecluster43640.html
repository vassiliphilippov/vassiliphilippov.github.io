to cannulise

verb
insert a cannula or tube medically
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.jə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ju.laɪz / (BrE)
to insert a cannula or tube into a body cavity, duct, or vessel, typically for medical procedures

Examples

to cannulise a vein
It is important to cannulise the artery carefully to avoid complications.
Nurses are well trained to cannulise small veins in pediatric patients.
The doctor will cannulise the patient's vein before starting the infusion.
