to doctor

verb
to falsify or adulterate
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
to change or falsify something, especially in order to deceive or make it less pure

Examples

to doctor a document
He tried to doctor the photographs so no one would notice the mistake.
It was discovered that someone had doctored the evidence before the trial.
The reports were carefully doctored to hide the financial losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adulterate: to make something impure by adding inferior substances
  • synonym – falsify: to alter information or evidence for deception
