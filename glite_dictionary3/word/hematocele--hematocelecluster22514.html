hematocele

noun
swelling caused by blood in a body cavity
Pronunciation: / hɪˈmætəˌsil / (AmE), / hɪˈmætəsiːl / (BrE)
A swelling resulting from blood collecting in a body cavity, especially in the membrane covering the testis.

Examples

hematocele in the scrotum
A hematocele often requires careful evaluation to rule out other causes of scrotal swelling.
Following the injury, the patient developed a hematocele that needed surgical intervention.
The doctor diagnosed the painful swelling as a hematocele after conducting an ultrasound.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – edema: swelling caused by fluid accumulation in the tissues
  • synonym – hematoma: a localized collection of blood outside blood vessels
