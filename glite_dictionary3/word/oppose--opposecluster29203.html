to oppose

verb
set in contrast or opposition
Pronunciation: / əˈpoʊz / (AmE), / əˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to set people or things in opposition or rivalry, especially in competition or debate

Examples

oppose an opponent
In the debate, she opposed his arguments point by point, highlighting the flaws in his logic.
The artist opposed warm and cool colors in the painting to create a dramatic effect.
These two theories are often opposed to illustrate the different schools of thought in philosophy.
Reality TV shows frequently oppose contestants with conflicting personalities to create drama.
The manager would often oppose team members to encourage healthy rivalry and innovation.
The principal decided to oppose the top students from each class in a debate competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – align: to place or arrange in a line with or alongside; the opposite of setting in opposition
  • synonym – contrast: to compare in such a way as to emphasize differences
  • synonym – match: to oppose or set against for competition
  • synonym – pit: to set in direct competition or conflict with
  • antonym – support: to back up or strengthen rather than oppose
