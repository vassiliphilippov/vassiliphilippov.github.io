untoward

adjective
unfavorable or inappropriate
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɔːd / (BrE)
unexpected, inappropriate, or unfavorable, often causing trouble or difficulty.

Examples

untoward event
Nothing untoward happened during their journey, despite the stormy weather.
She apologized if her remarks seemed untoward during the meeting.
The company assured customers that no untoward activity had been detected on their accounts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adverse: having a negative or unfavorable effect
  • antonym – favorable: beneficial or advantageous
