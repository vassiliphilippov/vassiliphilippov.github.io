matzo

noun
unleavened bread eaten during Passover
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmatsəʊ / (BrE)
A thin, crisp unleavened bread traditionally eaten by Jews during Passover.

Examples

matzo during Passover
During Passover, Jewish families abstain from eating regular bread and eat matzo instead.
She used matzo meal to make traditional dumplings for the holiday soup.
The supermarket stocked several varieties of matzo in preparation for the festival.
