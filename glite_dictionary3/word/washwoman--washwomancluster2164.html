washwoman

noun
woman who washes others' clothes for a living
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑʃˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to wash other people's clothes, typically working from her own home

Examples

hardworking washwoman
In the nineteenth century, being a washwoman was one of the few paid jobs available to poor women.
My grandmother worked as a washwoman to provide for her family.
The local washwoman collected laundry every Monday and returned it by the end of the week.

Related Terms

  • synonym – laundress: a woman employed to wash and iron clothes
