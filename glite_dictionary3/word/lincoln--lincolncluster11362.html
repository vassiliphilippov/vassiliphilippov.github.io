Lincoln

proper noun
16th President of the United States
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋkən / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkən / (BrE)
(Abraham Lincoln) the 16th President of the United States (1809–1865), who led the nation during the Civil War and abolished slavery.

Examples

President Lincoln speech
Many historians regard Lincoln as one of the greatest American presidents for his leadership during the Civil War.
The Gettysburg Address, delivered by Lincoln, remains one of the most famous speeches in U.S. history.
Visitors can explore the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C., which honors his legacy.
