newspeak

noun
deliberately misleading or manipulative language
Pronunciation: / ˈnuˌspik / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌspiːk / (BrE)
language that is deliberately ambiguous or contradictory, used to mislead and manipulate the public, especially for political purposes.

Examples

political newspeak
After reading the proposal, she was struck by the amount of newspeak and empty jargon it contained.
Critics accused the corporation of using newspeak to avoid answering difficult questions.
The government press release was filled with newspeak designed to obscure the real issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym – doublethink: the acceptance of two contradictory ideas or beliefs at the same time, especially as a result of political indoctrination
