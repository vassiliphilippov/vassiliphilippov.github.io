white

adjective
covered with snow
Pronunciation: / waɪt / (AmE), / waɪt / (BrE)
of the color that reflects nearly all visible light and has no hue; the color of fresh snow, milk, or paper; glowing or shining with a very bright, pale color

Examples

white snow
After the storm, the mountains were suddenly white and beautiful.
She dreamed of a white Christmas spent by the fire.
We woke up to see the white fields sparkling in the morning sun.
A white glow radiated from the kiln's opening as the pottery fired.
The lava emerged white and blinding from the volcano's vent.
The metal rod turned white in the furnace, indicating it was ready to be shaped.
She wore a beautiful white dress to the ceremony.
Snow fell overnight, covering everything in a white blanket.
The white walls made the room look bright and spacious.

Related Terms

  • antonym – black: of the very darkest color by the absence of reflected light
  • degree lower – ivory: a creamy-white color, like that of ivory
  • degree higher – snow-white: perfectly or pure white, like snow
  • synonym – snowy: covered with snow
  • synonym – white-hot: heated to such a high temperature that it emits white light
