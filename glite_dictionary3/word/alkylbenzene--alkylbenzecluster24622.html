alkylbenzene

noun
compound with alkyl group on benzene ring
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.kəlˈbɛn.ziːn / (AmE), / ˌalkɪlˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
An organic compound consisting of one or more alkyl groups attached to a benzene ring.

Examples

linear alkylbenzene compound
Linear alkylbenzenes are considered more biodegradable than their branched counterparts.
Many synthetic processes involve attaching an alkyl group to a benzene ring to form an alkylbenzene.
