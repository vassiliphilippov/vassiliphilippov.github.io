to club

verb
join resources for a shared goal
Pronunciation: / klʌb / (AmE), / klʌb / (BrE)
(of people) to unite or combine resources for a shared purpose, often to pool money or effort.

Examples

to club together money
If we all club in, we can afford a nicer dinner.
Several neighbors clubbed together to purchase new playground equipment for the park.
They decided to club together to buy their friend a birthday present.

  • synonym – pool: to combine resources or efforts for a common purpose
