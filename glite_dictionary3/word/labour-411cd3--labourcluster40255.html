labour

noun
workers' unions and their movement
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
Organized workers, their unions, or the movement aimed at improving their working conditions and status.

Examples

labour union
Labour fought for better wages and safer working conditions in the early twentieth century.
He has been a member of the Labour movement since his youth.
The new policy was criticized by Labour for weakening workers' rights.

Related Terms

  • antonym – management: the people in charge of running a company or organization, often opposed to labour
  • synonym – union: an organized association of workers formed to protect and further their rights and interests
