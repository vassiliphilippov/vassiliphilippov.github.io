dyspnea

noun
difficult or labored breathing
Pronunciation: / dɪspˈniə / (AmE), / dɪspˈniːə / (BrE)
difficult or labored breathing; shortness of breath, usually as a symptom of a medical condition.

Examples

severe dyspnea episode
Asthma attacks are often characterized by severe dyspnea and wheezing.
People with heart failure may experience dyspnea even at rest.
The patient reported dyspnea after climbing a single flight of stairs.
