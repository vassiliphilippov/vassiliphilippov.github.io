embryo

noun
early-stage developing organism
Pronunciation: / ˈɛm.bri.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bri.əʊ / (BrE)
An animal or human organism in the early stages of development before birth or hatching, especially before the major organs and body systems have formed.

Examples

human embryo development
At this stage, the embryo is just a cluster of cells.
Many animals have distinct embryo stages before they are born or hatched.
The scientist studied the development of the embryo under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – fetus: a later stage of development in mammals after the embryo stage
