to sparkle

verb
be lively or brilliant; exhibit virtuosity
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑrkəl / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkl̩ / (BrE)
to be lively, brilliant, or to show great skill or virtuosity; to be full of energy and charm

Examples

to sparkle with energy
Her witty remarks always sparkle during social gatherings.
The pianist's performance sparkled with energy and precision.
Their conversation sparkled with humor and clever insights.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coruscate: to flash or sparkle; to be brilliant or showy in style
  • antonym – dull: lacking brightness, liveliness, or brilliance
  • synonym – scintillate: to emit flashes of light; to be brilliant or dazzling
