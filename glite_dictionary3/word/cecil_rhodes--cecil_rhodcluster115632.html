Cecil Rhodes

proper noun
British colonial statesman and financier (1853–1902)
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːsəl ˈroʊdz / (AmE), / ˈsiːsəl ˈrəʊdz / (BrE)
(1853–1902) British colonial statesman and financier in South Africa, who made a fortune in gold and diamond mining, played a significant role in British imperial expansion in the region, and established the Rhodes Scholarship.

Examples

Cecil Rhodes diamond mining
Cecil Rhodes amassed a considerable fortune through diamond mining in South Africa.
Many buildings and institutions in southern Africa were named after Cecil Rhodes.
The prestigious Rhodes Scholarship was established by the will of Cecil Rhodes.
