to control

verb
place under legal or official restrictions
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to have power over or direct the actions or behavior of people, things, or situations; to manage or command

Examples

to control a situation
New laws were introduced to control the use of pesticides in agriculture.
Some countries strictly control access to the internet for political reasons.
The government decided to control the sale of certain pharmaceuticals due to safety concerns.
As a manager, he likes to control every aspect of the project.
She found it difficult to control her emotions during the meeting.
The dictator tried to control the media to shape public opinion.
It is important to control the temperature during the experiment.
Parents should control what their young children watch on television.
The manager must control every step of the process to maintain quality.
