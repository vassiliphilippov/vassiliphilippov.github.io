epithelial

adjective
relating to epithelium (body tissue)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈθiliəl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈθiːliəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of epithelium, the thin tissue forming the outer layer of a body's surface and lining hollow structures within the body.

Examples

epithelial cells
Certain cancers originate from epithelial cells in the body.
Researchers are studying how epithelial layers repair themselves after injury.
The doctor examined a sample of epithelial tissue taken from the patient's throat.
