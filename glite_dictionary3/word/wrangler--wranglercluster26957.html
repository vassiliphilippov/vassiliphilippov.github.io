wrangler

noun
person who takes care of saddle horses
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person, especially a cowboy, whose job is to take care of saddle horses on a ranch or during a cattle drive

Examples

horse wrangler
As a wrangler, she was responsible for feeding and grooming all the saddle horses on the ranch.
The wrangler rounded up the horses at dawn to prepare for the day's ride.
The young cowboy dreamed of becoming a skilled wrangler one day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cowboy: a man who herds and tends cattle, usually on horseback, especially in the western US
