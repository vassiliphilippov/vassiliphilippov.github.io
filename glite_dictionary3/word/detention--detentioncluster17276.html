detention

noun
state of being confined, usually temporarily
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
the state of being kept confined, especially as a punishment or while awaiting a decision

Examples

police detention
His family was concerned about his prolonged detention without formal charges.
School detention was given to students who arrived late.
The suspect was held in detention for several hours before being released.

Related Terms

  • synonym – imprisonment: the state of being confined, especially for a long time or as a punishment
  • antonym – release: the act of setting someone free from confinement
