quo warranto

noun
legal inquiry into authority to hold office or rights
Pronunciation: / kwoʊ wəˈræn.toʊ / (AmE), / kwəʊ wəˈran.təʊ / (BrE)
a legal proceeding that inquires by what authority a person has the right to hold an office, franchise, or liberty

Examples

quo warranto case in court
A quo warranto proceeding was filed to question his right to exercise certain privileges.
The court issued a quo warranto to challenge the legitimacy of the mayor's appointment.
The judge scheduled a hearing for the quo warranto against the elected official.
