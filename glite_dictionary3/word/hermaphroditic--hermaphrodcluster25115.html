hermaphroditic

adjective
having both male and female reproductive organs
Pronunciation: / hɝːˌmæf.rəˈdɪ.tɪk /; / hɚˌmæf.rəˈdɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / hɜːˌmæf.rəˈdɪ.tɪk /; / hɜːˌmæf.rəˈdɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
having both male and female reproductive organs in the same individual (used for animals and plants)

Examples

hermaphroditic flowers
Many flowering plants are hermaphroditic, meaning each flower contains both stamens and pistils.
Some species exhibit a hermaphroditic reproductive system as an adaptation to isolated habitats.
The hermaphroditic nature of certain algae allows them to self-fertilize.
Many earthworms are hermaphroditic, meaning each individual can produce both eggs and sperm.
Some fish species exhibit hermaphroditic traits and can change sex during their lives.
The hermaphroditic flowers of the plant produce both pollen and ovules.

  • synonym – monoecious: having both male and female reproductive organs in the same plant or individual
