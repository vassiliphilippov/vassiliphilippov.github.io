icefall

noun
steep, cascading part of a glacier
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌfɔːl / (BrE)
A steep section of a glacier where ice flows rapidly over a drop, resembling a frozen waterfall.

Examples

steep icefall on glacier
From a distance, the icefall sparkled in the morning sun, a curtain of jagged blue and white.
Many expeditions have been delayed at the Khumbu icefall due to shifting ice and hidden crevasses.
The climbers carefully navigated the dangerous icefall on their ascent of the glacier.
