bubo

noun
swollen lymph node (in certain infections)
Pronunciation: / ˈbjuːboʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːbəʊ / (BrE)
an inflamed and swollen lymph node, especially one occurring in certain infectious diseases such as plague, gonorrhea, or tuberculosis

Examples

painful bubo
A tender bubo can develop in the groin during the early stages of bubonic plague.
Swollen buboes are characteristic of certain bacterial infections, requiring prompt medical attention.
The doctor identified a large bubo in the patient's armpit, raising concern for possible plague infection.
