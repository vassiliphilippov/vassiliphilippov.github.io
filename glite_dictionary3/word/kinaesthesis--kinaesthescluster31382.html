kinaesthesis

noun
awareness of body movement and position
Pronunciation: / ˌkɪnəsˈθisɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɪsˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
the perception or awareness of the movement and position of parts of the body

Examples

kinaesthesis during movement
Children develop kinaesthesis as they learn to crawl, walk, and run.
Damage to certain nerves can impair a person’s kinaesthesis, making movement coordination difficult.
Professional dancers often have a highly developed sense of kinaesthesis.
