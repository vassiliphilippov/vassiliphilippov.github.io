implication

noun
something implied or suggested
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a meaning, conclusion, or result that is suggested or can be inferred but is not directly stated

Examples

implication of her words
Her words carried an implication that he was not welcome.
The new law has serious implications for the local community.
We need to consider the possible implications of our actions before proceeding.
Although he didn't say it outright, the implication of his words was clear.
Her tone carried an implication that she doubted his honesty.
The policy change has important implications for the future of the company.

Related Terms

  • antonym – explicit meaning: a meaning stated clearly and in detail
  • antonym – explicit statement: something stated clearly and directly
  • synonym – hint: a slight or indirect suggestion
  • synonym – inference: a conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning
  • synonym – insinuation: an indirect or covert suggestion, often of something negative
  • synonym – suggestion: an idea put forward for consideration
