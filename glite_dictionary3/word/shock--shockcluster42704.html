to shock

verb
gather sheaves of grain in upright bundles
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒk / (BrE)
to gather together sheaves of grain and set them upright in a field to dry

Examples

to shock the wheat
After the harvest, the workers would shock the wheat in neat rows across the field.
Farmers often shocked their grain to let it dry before threshing.
It was common practice to shock oats by hand in the late summer.
