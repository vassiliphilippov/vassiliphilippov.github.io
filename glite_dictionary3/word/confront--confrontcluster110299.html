to confront

verb
face or deal with directly
Pronunciation: / kənˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / kənˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to face or deal with a difficult situation, person, or thing directly and boldly

Examples

to confront a problem
It's important to confront your fears rather than run from them.
She finally confronted the issue that had been troubling her for months.
The manager had to confront the team about their poor performance.
She confronted her fear of heights by climbing the tall tower.
The two leaders finally confronted each other after months of tension.
When he opened the door, he was suddenly confronted by a barking dog.
