sepulcher

noun
stone burial chamber or tomb
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpəlkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛp(ə)lkə / (BrE)
a small room or chamber, typically made of stone, in which a dead person is buried

Examples

ancient sepulcher
Archaeologists carefully examined the contents of the underground sepulcher.
The king’s body was laid to rest in a grand marble sepulcher.
They discovered an ancient sepulcher beneath the ruins of the old church.
