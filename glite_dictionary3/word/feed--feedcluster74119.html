to feed

verb
to supply or provide
Pronunciation: / fid / (AmE), / fiːd / (BrE)
to support, promote, or increase a feeling, quality, or process by supplying what is needed (often figurative)

Examples

to feed an idea
Her determination feeds her ambition to succeed.
Rumors like these only feed the public's anxiety about safety.
The data you enter will feed directly into the results analysis.
He would often feed his mind with interesting books and lectures.
On vacation, she loved to feed her eyes on the colorful street markets.
Tourists flock to the viewpoint to feed their souls on the majestic scenery.
Compliments from her boss kept feeding his confidence.
Gossip only feeds her insecurities, making them worse over time.
Negative comments fed the team's doubts before the final match.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gratify: to give pleasure or satisfaction to
  • synonym – nourish: to provide with substances necessary for growth; often used figuratively
  • antonym – starve: to deprive something of what is needed, especially sustenance or resources
  • synonym – supply: to provide or make available what is needed
