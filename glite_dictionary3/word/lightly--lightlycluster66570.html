lightly

adverb
without due consideration
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
without seriousness, concern, or careful thought; in a casual or unconcerned way

Examples

take advice lightly
Don't take these warnings lightly; they could have serious consequences.
He did not make that accusation lightly; he had proof to support his claim.
You shouldn't dismiss her concerns lightly.
He was hurt, but took the rejection lightly and moved on quickly.
She dismissed the criticism lightly, refusing to let it upset her.
They treat such matters lightly and rarely show any concern.

  • antonym – seriously: with seriousness or gravity
