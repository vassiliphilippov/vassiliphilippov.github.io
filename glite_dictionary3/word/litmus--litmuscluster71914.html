litmus

noun
dye used to test acidity or alkalinity
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtməs / (AmE), / ˈlɪtməs / (BrE)
a blue dye obtained from lichens, used as a simple test to indicate acidity or alkalinity by changing color

Examples

litmus paper test
Blue litmus paper is commonly used as a simple acid test in laboratories.
The chemist used litmus to quickly determine the pH of the liquid.
When dipped in vinegar, litmus paper turns red, indicating the solution is acidic.

Related Terms

  • synonym – indicator: a substance that changes colour to indicate the presence of an acid or a base
