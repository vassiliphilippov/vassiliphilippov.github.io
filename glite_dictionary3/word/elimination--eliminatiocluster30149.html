elimination

noun
removal of a variable in equations
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing an unknown variable from a set of mathematical equations by combining them in a way that one variable is cancelled out

Examples

elimination method
By applying elimination, the algebra students reduced the equations to a single unknown.
Mathematicians often prefer elimination over substitution when dealing with multiple variables.
The method of elimination is commonly used to solve systems of linear equations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – substitution: the process of replacing one variable with equivalent expressions in equations
