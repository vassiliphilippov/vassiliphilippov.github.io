Arianism

noun
heretical Christian doctrine taught by Arius
Pronunciation: / ˈɛəriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛəriəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
The Christian doctrine, regarded as heretical, taught by Arius in the 4th century, asserting that the Son is subordinate to the Father.

Examples

Arianism controversy
Debates about Arianism shaped the development of Christian doctrine during the fourth century.
Many bishops opposed Arianism because it denied the full divinity of Christ.
The council condemned Arianism as a heresy in early Christianity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Trinitarianism: Christian doctrine asserting the co-equality of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit
