excusably

adverb
in a justifiable or forgivable way
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskjuːzəbli / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzəbli / (BrE)
In a way that can be justified or forgiven; to a degree that is worthy of excuse.

Examples

excusably late
He arrived excusably late due to the severe traffic jam.
She was excusably upset after hearing the disappointing news.
The team's mistakes were excusably made under high pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inexcusably: in a manner not capable of being excused or justified
