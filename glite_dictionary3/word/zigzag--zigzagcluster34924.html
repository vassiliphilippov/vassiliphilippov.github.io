to zigzag

verb
move or travel in sharp alternately changing turns
Pronunciation: / ˈzɪɡˌzæɡ / (AmE), / ˈzɪɡˌzæɡ / (BrE)
to move or travel forward by turning sharply first in one direction and then in another alternately.

Examples

to zigzag down a road
She watched the plane zigzag through the clouds to avoid turbulence.
The deer zigzagged across the field to escape the predator.
The path zigzags up the steep hillside, making the climb easier.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meander: to follow a winding and turning course
  • synonym – swerve: to turn aside abruptly from a straight course
