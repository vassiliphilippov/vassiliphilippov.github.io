serratus posterior inferior

noun
back muscle at lower thoracic-lumbar junction
Pronunciation: / səˈreɪtəs pɑːˈstɪriɚ ɪnˈfɪriɚ / (AmE), / səˈreɪtəs pɒˈstɪəriə ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
a thin, quadrilateral muscle located at the junction of the thoracic and lumbar regions of the back, which helps counteract the pull of the diaphragm on the lower ribs.

Examples

serratus posterior inferior muscle
During the dissection, the serratus posterior inferior was clearly visible beneath the latissimus dorsi.
Injury to the serratus posterior inferior can affect lower rib movement during breathing.
The serratus posterior inferior helps stabilize the lower ribs against the action of the diaphragm.
