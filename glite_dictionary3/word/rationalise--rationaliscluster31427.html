to rationalise

verb
think logically; use reason
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to think logically and use reason when making decisions or solving problems

Examples

to rationalise a decision
If we rationalise this process, we can avoid unnecessary mistakes.
It is important to rationalise your options before making a major life decision.
She tried to rationalise her fears by looking for logical explanations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – emotionalize: to base judgments or actions on emotions rather than reason
  • synonym – reason: to think, understand, and form judgments logically
