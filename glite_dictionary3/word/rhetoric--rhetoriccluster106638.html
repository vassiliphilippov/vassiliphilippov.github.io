rhetoric

noun
effective or persuasive use of language
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛt̬ərɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɛtərɪk / (BrE)
the art or skill of using language effectively to influence or persuade people, especially in speaking or writing

Examples

political rhetoric
Critics accused the company of empty rhetoric instead of real action on climate change.
She studied ancient rhetoric to improve her own persuasive speaking skills.
The politician's rhetoric was designed to inspire hope among his supporters.
Ancient philosophers wrote treatises on the rules and principles of rhetoric.
She studied rhetoric at university to improve her public speaking skills.
The politician’s mastery of rhetoric helped him persuade the audience.

  • synonym – oratory: skill or eloquence in public speaking
  • antonym – plain speaking: speaking in a direct, clear, and straightforward manner
