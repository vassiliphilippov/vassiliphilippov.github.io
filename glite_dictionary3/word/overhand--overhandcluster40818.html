overhand

adjective
performed with hand moving down from above shoulder
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəhænd / (BrE)
(of a stroke, throw, etc.) made with the hand brought forward and down from above shoulder level.

Examples

overhand throw motion
She preferred using an overhand stroke in her tennis serve.
Swimmers often use the overhand technique in freestyle events.
The pitcher delivered an overhand throw, surprising the batter.
