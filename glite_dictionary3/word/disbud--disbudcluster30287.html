to disbud

verb
remove undeveloped horn buds of cattle, etc.
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈbʌd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbʌd / (BrE)
to remove or destroy the undeveloped horn buds of a young animal, especially cattle, to prevent horn growth

Examples

to disbud young calves
After the vet disbudded the calves, they were returned to the pasture.
Farmers often disbud calves at an early age to prevent injuries and simplify handling.
It is recommended that you disbud young goats when they are just a few days old.
