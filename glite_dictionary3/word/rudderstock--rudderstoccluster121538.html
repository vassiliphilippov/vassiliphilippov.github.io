rudderstock

noun
vertical post of a rudder
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdərˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈrʌdəˌstɒk / (BrE)
the vertical post at the forward edge of a rudder to which the rudder is attached and which enables it to pivot

Examples

rudderstock damage report
A crack in the rudderstock may let water into the hull, which is a serious safety concern.
If the rudderstock is damaged, steering the vessel becomes nearly impossible.
The shipwright examined the rudderstock for signs of wear before beginning maintenance.
