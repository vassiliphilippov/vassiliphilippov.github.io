back-geared

adjective
equipped with gears to reduce spindle speed
Pronunciation: / bækˈɡɪrd / (AmE), / bækˈɡɪəd / (BrE)
(of a machine, especially a lathe) fitted with a set of gears that reduces the speed of the main spindle relative to the driving pulley.

Examples

back-geared lathe
A back-geared mechanism allows slower spindle speeds for cutting harder materials.
Many traditional lathes are back-geared to offer a wider range of operating speeds.
The workshop acquired a back-geared lathe for improved precision in metalworking.
