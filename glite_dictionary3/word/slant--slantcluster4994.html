to slant

verb
lie or move at a slope
Pronunciation: / slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to slope, lean, or move at an angle instead of being straight or level

Examples

to slant sideways
A bright beam of sunlight slanted through the window in the early morning.
Her handwriting slanted across the page at a steep angle.
The roof slants sharply to allow rainwater to drain away.
He slanted forward to hear what the speaker was saying.
She slanted the painting so it would catch the light better.
The old tree slanted dangerously after the storm.
As the old bookshelf ages, it starts to slant forward due to the weight of the books.
During the earthquake, the building began to slant dangerously to one side.
The rays of the setting sun slant through the windows in the late afternoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lean: to incline or bend from a straight position
  • antonym – level: to make or become level or even
  • synonym – slope: to be inclined from a horizontal or vertical line
  • synonym – tilt: to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • antonym – upright: in a vertical position, not slanted
