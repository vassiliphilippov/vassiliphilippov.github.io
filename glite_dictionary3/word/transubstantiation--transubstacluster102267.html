transubstantiation

noun
change of bread and wine into Christ's body and blood (Catholic doctrine)
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænsəbˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən /; / ˌtræn.səbˌstæn.ʃiˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænsʌbstænʃɪˈeɪʃən /; / ˌtræns.ʌbˌstæn.ʃiˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the Christian belief, especially in Roman Catholicism, that during the Eucharist the bread and wine become the body and blood of Christ while still looking like bread and wine
