Wassermann

noun
blood test for detecting syphilis
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːsərˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈwasəmæn / (BrE)
a blood test formerly used to detect syphilis by checking for the presence of antibodies to the syphilis bacterium.

Examples

Wassermann blood test
A positive Wassermann result required further confirmatory testing in many cases.
Before modern tests, the Wassermann was commonly used for diagnosing syphilis.
The doctor ordered a Wassermann to screen for syphilis in the patient.
