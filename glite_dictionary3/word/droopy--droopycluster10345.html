droopy

adjective
hanging down limply, weak or tired
Pronunciation: / ˈdruːpi / (AmE), / ˈdruːpi / (BrE)
Hanging down limply, as from exhaustion or weakness; not firm or upright.

Examples

droopy eyelids
His eyelids became droopy as he struggled to stay awake during the lecture.
She wore a droopy hat that shaded her eyes from the sun.
The flowers looked droopy after days without water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – perky: brisk, lively, or upright
  • synonym – sagging: hanging down loosely
