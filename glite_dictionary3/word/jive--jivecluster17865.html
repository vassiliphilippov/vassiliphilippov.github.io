jive

noun
1930s jazz style with flowing rhythms
Pronunciation: / dʒaɪv / (AmE), / dʒaɪv / (BrE)
a style of jazz music played by big bands, popular in the 1930s, characterized by flowing rhythms and less complex arrangements than later jazz styles
