to mound

verb
to form into a pile or raised mass
Pronunciation: / maʊnd / (AmE), / maʊnd / (BrE)
to form (a substance or material) into a pile or raised mass; to heap up into a rounded elevation

Examples

to mound potatoes
After the flood, workers mounded earth along the riverbank to prevent further damage.
She carefully mounded the mashed potatoes on each plate before serving.
The gardener mounded the soil around the base of the plant to protect its roots.
