to lionize

verb
treat as a celebrity; praise publicly
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlaɪənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone as a celebrity; to give someone a great deal of public attention or praise

Examples

to lionize a famous person
Critics warned against lionizing business leaders without considering the impact of their decisions.
Many fans lionized the novelist, flocking to her book signings and public appearances.
The city council chose to lionize the local athlete after his remarkable achievements.

Related Terms

  • synonym – celebrate: to publicly acknowledge someone with praise or honor
  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
