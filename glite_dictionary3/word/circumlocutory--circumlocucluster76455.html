circumlocutory

adjective
using unnecessarily wordy or indirect language
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝːkəmˈloʊkjəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈlɒkjʊtəri / (BrE)
using many words where fewer would do, especially in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive; roundabout in expression.

Examples

circumlocutory answer
Her circumlocutory explanation left everyone more confused than before.
Rather than give a direct answer, he became increasingly circumlocutory in his responses.
The legal document was so circumlocutory that it took hours to decipher.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: using few words; not including extra or unnecessary information
  • synonym – periphrastic: using indirect or roundabout forms of expression
