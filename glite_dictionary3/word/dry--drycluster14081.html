dry

adjective
prohibiting sale of alcohol
Pronunciation: / draɪ / (AmE), / draɪ / (BrE)
(of a place or law) opposed to or prohibiting the production and sale of alcoholic beverages.

Examples

dry county law
After the election, the town stayed dry despite some opposition from younger residents.
Several southern counties remain dry and do not permit the sale of beer or wine.
The law establishing a dry zone was enacted in 1920 and is still in effect today.
