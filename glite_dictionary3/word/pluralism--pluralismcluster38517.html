pluralism

noun
holding more than one church benefice
Pronunciation: / ˈplʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈplʊərəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
The practice, especially in the Christian Church, of one person holding more than one benefice or church office at the same time.

Examples

church pluralism practice
He was accused of pluralism after accepting two benefices in neighboring parishes.
In the Middle Ages, pluralism was a common issue, with clergy holding multiple church positions.
The Council attempted to curb pluralism by enforcing stricter rules about church appointments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – benefice: a permanent church appointment, typically involving property and income
