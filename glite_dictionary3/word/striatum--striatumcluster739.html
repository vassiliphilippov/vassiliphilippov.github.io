striatum

noun
striped region in the brain
Pronunciation: / straɪˈætəm / (AmE), / straɪˈɑːtəm / (BrE)
a region of the brain consisting of striped bands of white and grey matter, located in front of the thalamus in each cerebral hemisphere and comprising the caudate nucleus and the lentiform nucleus

Examples

the striatum of the brain
Damage to the striatum can contribute to neurological disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
Researchers studied the activity of the striatum during learning tasks in the laboratory.
The striatum plays a crucial role in coordinating movement and reward processing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – caudate nucleus: a C-shaped structure in the striatum involved in learning and memory
  • synonym – lentiform nucleus: part of the striatum, involved in movement control
