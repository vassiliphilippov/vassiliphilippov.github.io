recalcitrance

noun
biomass resistance to breakdown
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (AmE), / rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (BrE)
(in biomass science and biofuel research) the inherent resistance of plant biomass to chemical or enzymatic deconstruction, which hinders conversion to fermentable sugars.

Examples

biomass recalcitrance problem
Researchers work to reduce recalcitrance in biomass to improve biofuel yields in pilot plants.
High lignin content increases plant cell wall recalcitrance, making enzymatic breakdown more difficult.
The team pretreated the straw to lower recalcitrance before fermentation in the lab.

Related Terms

  • antonym – digestibility: the ease with which biomass is broken down into fermentable sugars; often considered the opposite of recalcitrance
  • synonym – resistance to deconstruction: the difficulty of breaking down biomass into its component sugars and polymers
