Akhenaton

proper noun
Egyptian pharaoh who promoted sun worship
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈkɛnəˌtɑːn / (AmE), / ˌækɪˈneɪtən / (BrE)
an early ruler of ancient Egypt (died in 1358 BC), known for rejecting traditional gods and introducing the worship of the sun deity Aten

Examples

Akhenaton ruled Egypt
Akhenaton is remembered for establishing the worship of Aten as Egypt’s primary religion.
Many historians credit Akhenaton with starting one of history's earliest monotheistic reforms.
The reign of Akhenaton marked a dramatic shift in artistic and religious traditions in ancient Egypt.
