to reclaim

verb
cause someone to abandon a wrong way of life
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkleɪm / (AmE), / rɪˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to persuade or cause someone to give up a wrong, immoral, or harmful way of life and adopt a better one

Examples

to reclaim a person
His aunt tried tirelessly to reclaim him from a life of addiction.
Many religious groups see it as their mission to reclaim those who have lost their moral way.
The organization hopes to reclaim young people involved in crime and guide them toward productive lives.

Related Terms

  • antonym – corrupt: to cause someone to become morally depraved
  • synonym – reform: to improve someone's behavior or morals
