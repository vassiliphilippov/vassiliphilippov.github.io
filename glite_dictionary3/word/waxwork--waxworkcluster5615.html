waxwork

noun
twining shrub with yellow capsules and scarlet seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈwæksˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈwaksˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a twining shrub native to North America, known for its yellow capsules containing scarlet seeds; also called American bittersweet (Celastrus scandens)

Examples

waxwork shrub berries
Gardeners often plant waxwork for its colorful yellow capsules and scarlet seeds.
In autumn, the bright berries of waxwork are especially striking against the foliage.
The hedge was covered with waxwork twining over the old fence.
