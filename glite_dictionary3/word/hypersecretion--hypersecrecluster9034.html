hypersecretion

noun
excessive production by a gland or organ
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərsɪˈkriːʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəsɪˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
the production of an abnormally large amount of a substance by a gland or organ, especially in the body

Examples

excessive hypersecretion of hormones
Chronic hypersecretion of hormones can disrupt normal metabolic processes in the body.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with hypersecretion of gastric acid, leading to frequent heartburn.
The condition is treated by medications that regulate glandular hypersecretion.
