presage

noun
an omen or feeling about the future
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a sign, feeling, or omen that something (usually bad) will happen

Examples

dark presage
His sudden illness was a presage that troubled everyone in the office.
Many believed that the eclipse served as a dark presage for the king's fate.
The gathering storm clouds were seen as a presage of impending disaster.
Many regarded the black cat crossing their path as a presage of misfortune.
The strange lights in the sky were believed to be a presage of disaster.
The sudden drop in temperature was seen as a presage of the coming storm.
