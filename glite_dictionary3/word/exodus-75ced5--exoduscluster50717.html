exodus

noun
a mass departure of people
Pronunciation: / ˈɛɡ.zə.dəs / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sə.dəs / (BrE)
a situation in which a large number of people leave a place at the same time, especially to escape from danger or difficult conditions

Examples

massive exodus of people
Following the hurricane, there was a massive exodus from the coastal areas to safer regions.
The country saw an exodus of skilled workers seeking better opportunities abroad.
The ongoing conflict triggered an exodus of refugees across the border.

Related Terms

  • synonym – migration: movement of people from one place to another, often in large groups
  • antonym – return: the act of coming or going back to a place; opposite of exodus
