to burnish

verb
to polish and make shiny
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝː.nɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪʃ / (BrE)
to polish a surface by rubbing, especially to make it shiny

Examples

to burnish metal surface
After hours of work, she managed to burnish the silver candlestick until it gleamed.
He used a soft cloth to burnish his shoes before the interview.
The craftsman would carefully burnish the wooden table to achieve a glassy finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym – polish: to make the surface of something smooth and shiny by rubbing it
  • antonym – tarnish: to make or become less bright or shiny
