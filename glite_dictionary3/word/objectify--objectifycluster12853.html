to objectify

verb
make external or concrete
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛktɪˌfaɪ, ɑb- / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something external, objective, or concrete; to represent an idea or feeling as an object

Examples

to objectify feelings
Language helps us objectify our internal experiences.
Philosophers often try to objectify abstract concepts for clarity.
The artist attempted to objectify her emotions through sculpture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abstract: to consider something theoretically or separately from something else (opposite direction: from concrete to abstract)
  • synonym – externalize: to make external or visible; to express outwardly
