breakwater

noun
barrier to protect shore from waves
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
A barrier built out into the sea to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves.

Examples

breakwater protects harbor
Engineers designed the breakwater to reduce coastal erosion along the beach.
Fishermen like to cast their lines from the end of the breakwater.
The new breakwater will help shield the harbor from severe storm surges.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jetty: A structure that projects into the water to influence the current or tide or to protect a harbor
  • synonym – pier: A platform supported on pillars or girders leading out from the shore into a body of water
