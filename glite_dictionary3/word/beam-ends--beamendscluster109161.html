beam-ends

noun
the ends of a ship's deck beams
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːmˌɛndz / (AmE), / ˈbiːmˌɛndz / (BrE)
(Nautical) The ends of the transverse deck beams of a ship or boat; often used in the phrase 'on her beam-ends' to mean a vessel heeled over so far that the deck is nearly vertical.

Examples

on her beam-ends
During the storm, the yacht was thrown onto her beam-ends by a huge wave.
Old sailors believed that once a vessel was on her beam-ends, survival was uncertain.
The captain shouted as the ship lurched toward its beam-ends in the high seas.
