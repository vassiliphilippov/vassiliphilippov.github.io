legitimation

noun
making lawful; granting legal status
Pronunciation: / lɪˌdʒɪtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪtəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something lawful or giving it legal status

Examples

legitimation of activities
He campaigned for the legitimation of certain previously prohibited activities.
Marriage resulted in the legitimation of their children under the law.
The legitimation of the new government required constitutional amendments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – delegitimation: the process of removing legitimacy or legal status
  • synonym – legalization: the process of making something legal
