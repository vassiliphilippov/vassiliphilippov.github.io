camel

noun
desert-dwelling humped mammal
Pronunciation: / ˈkæməl / (AmE), / ˈkæməl / (BrE)
A large, cud-chewing mammal with a humped back, adapted to life in desert regions and used as a draft or saddle animal.

Examples

ride a camel
A fully grown camel can travel for days without water.
Farmers in North Africa often use a camel to transport goods across long distances.
The camel is well adapted to survive in the harsh conditions of the desert.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – bactrian camel: a camel with two humps, found in Central Asia
  • degree lower – dromedary: a camel with a single hump, found mainly in Africa and Arabia
