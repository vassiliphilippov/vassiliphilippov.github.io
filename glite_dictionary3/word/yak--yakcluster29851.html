to yak

verb
talk a lot, often about trivial things
Pronunciation: / jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
to talk incessantly and sometimes trivial matters, often in an informal or trivial way

Examples

to yak for hours
He tends to yak on the phone for hours every evening.
She was still yaking when everyone else had finished eating.
We sat around the coffee table yakking about old times.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be quiet: to not speak, to remain silent
  • synonym – chatter: to talk quickly and continuously, usually about trivial matters
  • synonym – prattle: to talk at length about insignificant things
