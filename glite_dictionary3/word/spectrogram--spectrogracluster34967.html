spectrogram

noun
visual record of a spectrum
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛktɹəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəɡram / (BrE)
a visual or photographic representation showing the intensity of different frequencies in a spectrum, often used to analyze sound or electromagnetic waves.

Examples

spectrogram of sound waves
A spectrogram of the bird's song revealed detailed frequency patterns.
Musicians use a spectrogram to visualize and understand complex sound waves.
The scientist studied the spectrogram to analyze the composition of the star's light.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spectrum: a band of colors, as seen in a rainbow, produced by separation of components of light; or a range of something
