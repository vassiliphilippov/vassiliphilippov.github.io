to induce

verb
produce electric current by magnetic or electrostatic means
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdus / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to produce an electric current in a conductor by the influence of a changing magnetic or electric field.

Examples

to induce a current
A moving magnet can induce a current in a coil of wire.
Alternating current is often induced by rotating a conductor in a magnetic field.
Physicists demonstrated how changing the magnetic field would induce electricity in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – generate: to produce electricity or energy
  • antonym – suppress: to prevent or stop (as in suppressing a current)
