lunatic

adjective
insane, as if affected by the moon (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈluːnəˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈljuːnətɪk / (BrE)
(archaic) Affected with insanity, originally believed to be caused by the phases of the moon.

Examples

lunatic mind
In medieval Europe, some believed a person could become lunatic during a full moon.
The old hospital once treated many patients labeled as lunatic by society.
Under the archaic belief, her strange behavior was thought to be lunatic in origin.
