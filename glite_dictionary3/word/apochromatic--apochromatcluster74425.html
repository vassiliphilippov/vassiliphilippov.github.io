apochromatic

adjective
corrected for chromatic and spherical aberration
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈkroʊmətɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈkrɒmətɪk / (BrE)
(of a lens or optical system) corrected for chromatic and spherical aberrations, producing images with minimal color distortion.

Examples

apochromatic lens design
An apochromatic telescope is often favored by astrophotographers for its superior image quality.
She replaced the standard lens with an apochromatic one to reduce color fringing in her photos.
The microscope comes with an apochromatic objective for enhanced clarity.
