vapour

noun
process of becoming gas/vapor
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.pə / (BrE)
The process of becoming a gas, especially from a liquid or solid state; the action or state of vaporizing.

Examples

water turning to vapour
After the rain, the sun quickly turned the puddles into vapour.
Fog is formed when water vapour condenses in the air.
The process of boiling involves the formation of vapour from liquid water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – condensation: process of a gas becoming a liquid
  • synonym – vapor: process of becoming a gas (American spelling)
