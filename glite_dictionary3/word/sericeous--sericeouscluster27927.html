sericeous

adjective
covered with fine, silky hairs
Pronunciation: / səˈriːʃəs / (AmE), / səˈriːsiəs / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially of a plant or animal) covered with fine, soft, silky hairs or down.

Examples

sericeous leaf surface
Botanists examine whether a specimen is sericeous to help with accurate identification.
Some caterpillars appear sericeous due to the fine, silky hairs on their bodies.
The leaves of the plant are sericeous, giving them a silvery sheen in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – glabrous: lacking hairs or down; smooth
  • synonym – pubescent: covered with soft, short hairs
