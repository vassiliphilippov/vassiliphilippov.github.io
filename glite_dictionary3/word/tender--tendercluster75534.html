tender

adjective
very young or immature
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndɚ /; / ˈtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə /; / ˈtendə / (BrE)
very young, inexperienced, or not fully mature; easily damaged or not hardy

Examples

tender age
At such a tender age, children can be easily influenced by their surroundings.
He showed musical talent from a tender age.
The calf was separated from its mother at a very tender age.
Early spring frosts can easily kill tender plants in the garden.
Many tropical flowers are too tender to survive in temperate climates.
You should bring all tender seedlings indoors before the first frost.
