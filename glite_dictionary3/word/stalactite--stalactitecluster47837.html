stalactite

noun
cave formation hanging from ceiling
Pronunciation: / stəˈlæk.taɪt / (AmE), / stəˈlæk.taɪt / (BrE)
A tapering structure hanging from the roof of a cave, formed of calcium carbonate deposited by dripping water.

Examples

stalactite in a cave
A single stalactite hung from the cavern ceiling, glistening in the flashlight beam.
Over thousands of years, water droplets gradually form a stalactite from the minerals they carry.
Visitors to the cave admired the enormous stalactites sparkling in the cool, damp air.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stalagmite: a deposit rising from the floor of a cave, formed from dripping minerals
