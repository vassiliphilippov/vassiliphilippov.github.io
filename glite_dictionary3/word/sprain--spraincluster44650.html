sprain

noun
injury to a joint's ligaments
Pronunciation: / spreɪn / (AmE), / spreɪn / (BrE)
an injury to a joint caused by stretching or tearing its ligaments, resulting in pain and swelling

Examples

ankle sprain
Applying ice immediately can help reduce swelling from a sprain.
He suffered a sprain after twisting his ankle during the game.
The doctor examined my wrist and confirmed it was just a sprain, not a fracture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – strain: an injury involving muscles or tendons rather than ligaments
