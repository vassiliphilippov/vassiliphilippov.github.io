power-assisted

adjective
aided by mechanical or electrical power
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊɚ əˌsɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈpaʊər əˌsɪstɪd / (BrE)
using additional mechanical or electrical power to assist or replace manual effort, especially in machines or vehicles.

Examples

power-assisted steering
Many modern cars come with power-assisted steering for easier maneuvering.
The hospital bed is power-assisted to help nurses adjust its position more easily.
Using a power-assisted bicycle allows her to travel longer distances without quite as much effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym – manual: operated or done by hand without power assistance
