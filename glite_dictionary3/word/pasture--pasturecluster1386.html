pasture

noun
grazing land for livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈpæstʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstʃə / (BrE)
An area of land covered with grass or other plants, suitable for the grazing of livestock such as cattle or sheep.

Examples

green pasture land
Farmers rotate their herds from one pasture to another to keep the grass healthy.
The cattle spent the afternoon grazing in the pasture near the river.
The rolling green pasture stretched as far as the eye could see.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meadow: a field of grass, especially one used for hay
