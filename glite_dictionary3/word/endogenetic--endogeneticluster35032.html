endogenetic

adjective
formed beneath Earth's surface (geology)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndədʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of rocks) formed or occurring beneath the surface of the Earth, especially as a result of internal geological processes.

Examples

endogenetic rock formation
Geologists distinguish between endogenetic and exogenetic processes when classifying rock formations.
Most igneous rocks are considered endogenetic because they originate from magma beneath the crust.
The formation of granite is a classic endogenetic geological process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exogenetic: of rocks formed at or near the Earth's surface
