acousma

noun
illusory perception of nonverbal sounds
Pronunciation: / əˈkaʊzmə / (AmE), / əˈkaʊzmə / (BrE)
A sensory illusion involving the perception of strange, nonverbal sounds that have no external source.

Examples

unexplained acousma sounds
Hearing an unexplained buzzing or humming is a common form of acousma.
In psychiatric evaluation, acousma should be distinguished from hallucinations involving voices.
The patient reported experiencing frequent acousma during quiet moments in the evening.
