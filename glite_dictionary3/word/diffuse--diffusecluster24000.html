to diffuse

verb
to spread widely through something
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjus /; / dɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to spread or distribute something widely so that it covers a large area or reaches many people

Examples

to diffuse information
Rumors about the merger quickly diffused throughout the company.
Sunlight diffuses across the room in the early morning.
The smell of freshly baked bread began to diffuse through the entire house.
As the chemical started to diffuse, its concentration decreased at the source.
The crowd slowly diffused into the surrounding streets after the concert.
The scent of perfume began to diffuse through the entire room.
His research helped diffuse new ideas throughout academic circles.
Social media can diffuse rumors rapidly across the globe.
The organization aims to diffuse vital health information in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concentrate: to gather together in one place
  • synonym – disperse: to scatter in different directions
  • synonym – disseminate: to spread information, ideas, or knowledge widely
  • synonym – penetrate: to spread into or throughout; to pass into or through
  • synonym – permeate: to spread throughout something; to pass through
  • synonym – spread: to cause information or substances to cover a wider area or become known by more people
