overpressure

noun
transient excess air pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.vərˌprɛʃ.ər / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəˌprɛʃ.ə / (BrE)
a sudden or temporary increase in air pressure above the normal atmospheric level, often resulting from an explosion or shock wave

Examples

overpressure from explosion
