allotropic

adjective
existing in different forms (elements)
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.əˈtrɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈtrɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical element) existing in two or more different forms, with different physical or chemical properties, in the same physical state.

Examples

allotropic forms of carbon
Diamond and graphite are allotropic forms of carbon.
Oxygen displays allotropic behavior, existing as both O2 and O3 molecules.
The discovery of allotropic modifications often led to advances in materials science.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allotrope: a different form of the same element; e.g., diamond and graphite are allotropes of carbon
