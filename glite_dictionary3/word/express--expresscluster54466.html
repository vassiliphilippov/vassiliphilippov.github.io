to express

verb
communicate or show (ideas, feelings)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
to show or communicate a feeling, idea, or quality through words, actions, or other means

Examples

to express feelings openly
He expressed his apology with a sincere look and a gentle tone.
People can express themselves through art as well as through speech.
She found it difficult to express her true feelings to her family.
Her eyes expressed more gratitude than she could put into speech.
Music often expresses emotions that are difficult to describe.
The artist's new sculpture expresses deep sorrow without using any words at all.
He found it difficult to express himself clearly in English.
It's important to express your feelings honestly in a relationship.
The artist expressed her emotions through her paintings.
