tyrosinemia

noun
inherited disorder of tyrosine metabolism
Pronunciation: / ˌtaɪrəˌsiˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌtɪərəʊsɪˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder in which the body is unable to properly break down the amino acid tyrosine, often leading to liver and kidney problems and intellectual disability.

Examples

tyrosinemia affects liver
Early treatment of tyrosinemia can prevent severe liver and kidney complications.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of tyrosinemia.
The infant was diagnosed with tyrosinemia after laboratory tests revealed elevated tyrosine levels in the blood.
