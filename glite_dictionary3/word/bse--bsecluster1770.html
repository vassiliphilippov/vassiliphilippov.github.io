BSE

noun
fatal cattle disease ('mad cow disease')
Pronunciation: / ˌbiː ˌɛs ˈiː / (AmE), / ˌbiː ˌɛs ˈiː / (BrE)
a fatal neurological disease of cattle, known as bovine spongiform encephalopathy, that causes degeneration of the central nervous system and is sometimes called 'mad cow disease'

Examples

BSE outbreak in cattle
Scientists continue to study how BSE is transmitted and how to prevent further cases.
Tests confirmed that the sick cows had BSE, raising concern among consumers and farmers alike.
The government imposed new controls after an outbreak of BSE was discovered in the region.
