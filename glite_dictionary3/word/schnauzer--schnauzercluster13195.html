schnauzer

noun
a German breed of wiry-coated dog
Pronunciation: / ˈʃnaʊzər / (AmE), / ˈʃnaʊzə / (BrE)
a breed of sturdy, medium to large German dog with a wiry coat and a distinctive blunt, bearded muzzle, used as a ratter, guard dog, or police dog

Examples

friendly schnauzer dog
Her family decided to adopt a schnauzer because of its loyal and protective nature.
Many police units use the schnauzer for its intelligence and ability to be trained.
The schnauzer greeted everyone at the door, wagging its tail energetically.
