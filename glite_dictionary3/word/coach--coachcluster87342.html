coach

noun
horse-drawn carriage for passengers
Pronunciation: / koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a large, enclosed horse-drawn carriage, typically pulled by four horses and driven by a coachman, used for carrying passengers

Examples

ride in a coach carriage
In the 18th century, wealthy travelers often journeyed long distances by coach.
The old coach was displayed in the museum as an example of luxurious transportation.
The royal family traveled to the palace in a magnificent coach.
