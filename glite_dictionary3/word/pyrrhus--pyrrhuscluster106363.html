Pyrrhus

proper noun
King of Epirus, famed for costly victories over Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪrəs / (BrE)
(c. 318–272 BC) King of Epirus, known for defeating the Romans in two costly battles that gave rise to the term 'Pyrrhic victory'.

Examples

Pyrrhus of Epirus
Pyrrhus famously remarked that another such victory would ruin him after defeating the Romans.
Historians often cite Pyrrhus when discussing battles with devastating costs.
The reputation of Pyrrhus as a formidable general spread throughout the ancient Mediterranean world.
