assimilatory

adjective
capable of absorbing or incorporating substances
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪməˌleɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪlətəri / (BrE)
capable of taking substances (such as gases, light, or liquids) into a solution or being incorporated as part of a process, especially in biological or chemical contexts.

Examples

assimilatory process in cells
Certain bacteria play an assimilatory role by incorporating nitrogen into their cells.
Photosynthesis is an assimilatory process involving the uptake of light energy.
The assimilatory function of the leaves enables plants to convert carbon dioxide into organic compounds.

  • synonym – assimilative: promoting or related to assimilation
