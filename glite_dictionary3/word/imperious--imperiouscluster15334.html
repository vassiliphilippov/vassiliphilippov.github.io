imperious

adjective
arrogant and domineering
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɪriəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəs / (BrE)
having or showing an arrogant and domineering attitude; expecting obedience and treating others as inferior

Examples

imperious tone of voice
His imperious manner made it clear he expected everyone to follow his orders.
She gave an imperious wave of her hand, dismissing their concerns without a thought.
The manager spoke in an imperious tone that discouraged any disagreement from his team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – haughty: arrogantly superior and disdainful
  • antonym – humble: having or showing a modest or low view of one’s importance
  • synonym – supercilious: behaving as though one thinks one is superior to others
