to busy

verb
occupy oneself with an activity
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
to occupy oneself with an activity or task, often to avoid idleness

Examples

to busy oneself cooking
During the long summer, he busied himself with learning new recipes.
Many retirees busy themselves with volunteer work after leaving their jobs.
She likes to busy herself with gardening in her free time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – engage: to participate or become involved in
  • synonym – occupy: to fill or take up (a space, time, or attention)
