to cross-examine

verb
question a witness after the opposing side
Pronunciation: / krɔs ɪɡˈzæmɪn / (AmE), / krɒs ɪɡˈzæmɪn / (BrE)
to formally question a witness in a court of law, especially after they have already been questioned by the opposing side

Examples

to cross-examine a witness
During the trial, several experts were called to the stand and cross-examined extensively.
It is the lawyer's job to cross-examine every witness carefully.
The defense attorney began to cross-examine the prosecution's key witness.

  • synonym – interrogate: to question thoroughly, especially by the police or in a formal context
