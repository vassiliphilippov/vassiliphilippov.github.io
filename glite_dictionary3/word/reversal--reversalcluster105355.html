reversal

noun
a major change in attitude or principle
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜːrsəl /; / rɪˈvɜr.səl / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsəl /; / rɪˈvɜː.səl / (BrE)
a complete change in attitude, principle, or point of view

Examples

reversal of opinion
After years of supporting the policy, the government announced a reversal in its position.
His speech marked a reversal of his previous views on climate change.
The company's sudden reversal left many employees confused about the future.
A reversal of the court's decision would set a significant legal precedent.
After months of progress, the patient experienced a reversal of his health improvements.
The sudden reversal in policy surprised everyone in the organization.

  • synonym – inversion: a reversal of position, order, form, or relationship
  • synonym – turnaround: an abrupt or unexpected change, especially in policy or opinion
