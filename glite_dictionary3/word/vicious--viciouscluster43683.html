vicious

adjective
morally wrong or wicked
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
morally wrong, wicked, or deserving strong criticism

Examples

vicious character
His vicious character made it impossible for others to trust him.
She condemned the vicious behavior displayed by the leaders of the criminal gang.
The corrupt dictator was known for his vicious disregard for human rights.
Covering up the problem instead of fixing it was a vicious mistake by the company.
Such vicious behavior has no place in civil society and must be condemned.
The judge called the defendant's actions vicious and imposed the maximum sentence allowed by law.
