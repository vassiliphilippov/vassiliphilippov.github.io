whistler

noun
person who makes a loud high sound
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪslɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪslə / (BrE)
a person who produces a loud high sound, especially by forcing air through their lips or a device

Examples

talented whistler
As a skilled whistler, she could mimic many bird calls perfectly.
Growing up, he became known in the village as the best whistler around.
The referee blew his whistle, but the loudest whistler in the crowd drowned him out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blow: to produce a sound by forcing air through the mouth
