to disperse

verb
separate light into a spectrum
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɝs / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːs / (BrE)
to separate light into its component spectral colors, typically by passing it through a prism or similar medium

Examples

to disperse light
A droplet of water can disperse sunlight, creating a miniature spectrum similar to a prism.
Astronomers use specialized equipment to disperse starlight and study its various wavelengths.
When white light passes through a prism, the glass disperses it into a rainbow of colors.
