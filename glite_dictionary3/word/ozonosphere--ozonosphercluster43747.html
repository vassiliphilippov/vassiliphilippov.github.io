ozonosphere

noun
layer of atmosphere rich in ozone
Pronunciation: / oʊˈzoʊnəsfɪr / (AmE), / əʊˈzəʊnəsfɪə / (BrE)
The layer of the Earth's stratosphere that contains a high concentration of ozone and absorbs most of the sun's ultraviolet radiation.

Examples

Earth's ozonosphere
A thinning ozonosphere could lead to an increase in skin cancers and environmental harm.
Scientists monitor the ozonosphere for signs of ozone depletion.
The ozonosphere plays a crucial role in protecting living organisms from harmful ultraviolet rays.
