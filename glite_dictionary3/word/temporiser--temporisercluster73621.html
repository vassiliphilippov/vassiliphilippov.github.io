temporiser

noun
someone who delays action to gain time
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəraɪzə / (BrE)
A person who avoids making a decision or taking action in order to gain time or await a more favorable opportunity.

Examples

clever temporiser
A skilled temporiser, she deflected every question until she could learn more.
As a notorious temporiser, he rarely gave direct answers in meetings.
The committee's temporiser stalled the vote, hoping for new developments next week.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decisive person: a person who makes decisions quickly and firmly
  • synonym – procrastinator: a person who delays or postpones action
