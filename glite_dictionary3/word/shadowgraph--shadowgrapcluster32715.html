shadowgraph

noun
image produced by X-rays or other radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a photographic image made on a sensitive surface by radiation other than visible light, especially X-rays or gamma rays

Examples

shadowgraph X-ray image
A shadowgraph can reveal hidden flaws that are invisible to the naked eye.
Engineers used a shadowgraph to study the internal structure of the metal component.
The doctor examined the shadowgraph of the patient's lungs to look for signs of pneumonia.
