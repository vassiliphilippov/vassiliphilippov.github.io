lockstitch

noun
type of interlocking machine stitch
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑk.stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɒk.stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A type of machine stitch in which the top thread interlocks with the bobbin thread, commonly used in sewing machines.

Examples

lockstitch sewing machine
Most sewing machines use a lockstitch for strong, durable seams.
She learned how to adjust the tension to perfect the lockstitch on her new machine.
The lockstitch is preferred for its ability to prevent unraveling.

Related Terms

  • antonym – chainstitch: a type of sewing stitch where a single thread forms chain-like loops
