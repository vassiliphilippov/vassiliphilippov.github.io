to motion

verb
formally propose an action
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
to propose a formal action or suggestion, especially in a meeting or assembly

Examples

to motion to adjourn
After a lengthy debate, the committee motioned to close nominations.
During the meeting, she motioned to postpone the discussion until next week.
He motioned that the board should approve the new budget.

Related Terms

  • synonym – propose: to put forward a suggestion for consideration
  • synonym – second: to formally support a motion or proposal
