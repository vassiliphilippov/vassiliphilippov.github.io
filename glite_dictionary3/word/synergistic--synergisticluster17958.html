synergistic

adjective
linked to the doctrine of synergism (theology)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪnɚˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the theological doctrine of synergism, which holds that human will cooperates with divine grace in the process of salvation.

Examples

synergistic salvation
Her essay examines the implications of a synergistic approach to human free will and divine grace.
Some denominations hold a firmly synergistic view of how believers attain salvation.
The council's debate focused on whether salvation was synergistic or monergistic in nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monergistic: relating to the doctrine that salvation is entirely the work of divine grace without human cooperation
  • synonym – synergism: the theological doctrine that salvation involves cooperation between divine grace and human will
