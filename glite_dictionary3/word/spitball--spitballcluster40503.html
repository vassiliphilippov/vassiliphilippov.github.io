spitball

noun
chewed paper ball used as a projectile
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪtˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈspɪt.bɔːl / (BrE)
A small ball of chewed paper, often made by chewing a piece of paper and shaping it into a sphere, typically used as a playful projectile.

Examples

shoot a spitball
After lunch, the desks were littered with spitballs and scraps of paper.
During class, Jake secretly threw a spitball at his friend across the room.
The teacher caught the student making a spitball and sent him to the principal's office.
