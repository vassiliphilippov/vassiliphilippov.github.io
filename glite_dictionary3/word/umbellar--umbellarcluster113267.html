umbellar

adjective
resembling or bearing umbels (flower clusters)
Pronunciation: / ʌmˈbɛl.ər / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɛl.ə / (BrE)
(of plants or inflorescences) bearing, made up of, or resembling an umbel (a cluster of flowers with stalks of nearly equal length that spread from a common center).

Examples

umbellar flower cluster
Carrots and parsley display classic umbellar patterns in their flowering structures.
Researchers studied several species with umbellar inflorescences in Mediterranean habitats.
The plant's umbellar arrangement of small white flowers makes it easily identifiable in the field.

Related Terms

  • antonym – racemose: having inflorescences in racemes, a different flower cluster form
  • synonym – umbellate: having or resembling an umbel; arranged in umbels
