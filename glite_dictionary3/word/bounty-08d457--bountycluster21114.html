bounty

noun
reward or payment, often by a government
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
a payment or reward given for doing something, such as capturing someone or reporting a problem

Examples

bounty on a criminal
A bounty of $5,000 was placed on the fugitive’s head.
He joined the army to collect the enlistment bounty they were offering at the time.
The government offered a bounty on invasive species to encourage their removal.
The company offered a generous bounty to anyone who found and reported critical security bugs.
Independent researchers earned a small bounty after responsible disclosure of the website vulnerability.
Many startups run public bounty programs to encourage reporting before attackers exploit flaws.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bug bounty: a program run by an organisation that offers bounties for reports of security vulnerabilities
  • synonym – incentive: something that motivates action, often monetary
  • synonym – reward: something given in recognition of service or effort
