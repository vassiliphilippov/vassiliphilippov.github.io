to stamp

verb
categorize or label by stereotype
Pronunciation: / stæmp / (AmE), / stæmp / (BrE)
to label, classify, or assign a characteristic to someone or something, often in a fixed or stereotyped way

Examples

to stamp a person
He hated being stamped as an outsider because of his accent.
It's wrong to stamp all teenagers as irresponsible.
She was unfairly stamped a troublemaker by the teachers.
Her generosity stamped her as a truly kind person.
His accent immediately stamped him as a newcomer to the city.
This victory stamps the team as one of the season's favorites.
