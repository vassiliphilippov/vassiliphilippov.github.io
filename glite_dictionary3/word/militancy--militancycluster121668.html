militancy

noun
aggressive activism or support for a cause
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being aggressively active in support of a cause, especially to achieve political or social change

Examples

political militancy
She admired his militancy in fighting for workers' rights.
The group's increasing militancy made negotiations difficult for the government.
There was a noticeable rise in militancy among student protestors last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aggression: hostile or violent behavior or attitudes
  • antonym – passivity: acceptance of what happens, without active response or resistance
