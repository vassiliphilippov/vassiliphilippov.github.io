overgrowth

noun
excessive or abnormal growth (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
excessive growth or enlargement of an organ, tissue, or body part, often resulting from abnormal hormone production or disease.

Examples

overgrowth of tissue
A pituitary tumor can cause overgrowth of bones and tissues, leading to health complications.
The doctor diagnosed the swelling as an overgrowth resulting from a hormonal imbalance.
The patient's overgrowth of facial features was attributed to acromegaly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hypertrophy: increase in the size of an organ or tissue due to enlargement of its cells
