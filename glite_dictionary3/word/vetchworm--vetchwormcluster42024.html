vetchworm

noun
caterpillar pest of crops
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛtʃˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈvɛtʃˌwɜːm / (BrE)
The caterpillar of a noctuid moth, noted for being highly destructive to crops such as corn, cotton, and tomatoes.

Examples

vetchworm destroys crops
Farmers took measures to protect their tomato crops from a sudden outbreak of vetchworms.
Research shows that the vetchworm prefers moist conditions for laying its eggs.
The vetchworm can quickly destroy entire fields of young corn if not controlled early.
