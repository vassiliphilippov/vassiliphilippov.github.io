utriculus

noun
small sac in the inner ear
Pronunciation: / juːˈtrɪkjʊləs / (AmE), / juːˈtrɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
a small sac in the inner ear into which the semicircular canals open, involved in the body's sense of balance

Examples

inner ear utriculus
Damage to the utriculus can lead to problems with balance and spatial orientation.
Fluid movement within the utriculus triggers nerve signals that help maintain equilibrium.
The utriculus and saccule both play a vital role in detecting linear acceleration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – saccule: another small sac in the inner ear, paired with the utriculus
