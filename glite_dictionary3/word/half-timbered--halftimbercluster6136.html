half-timbered

adjective
with exposed timber framing and infilled panels
Pronunciation: / ˌhæfˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
(of a building) having visible wooden framework with the spaces between filled with masonry or plaster, characteristic of Tudor and similar architecture.

Examples

half-timbered house
Many picturesque English villages feature half-timbered houses dating back to the 16th century.
Restorers worked carefully to preserve the half-timbered structure during the renovation.
The half-timbered façade gives the cottage a distinctive, old-world charm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – timber-framed: having a structural frame made of timber
