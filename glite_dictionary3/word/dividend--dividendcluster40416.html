dividend

noun
sum paid to shareholders from a company's profits
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪvɪdɛnd / (BrE)
A sum of money paid regularly (typically quarterly) by a corporation to its shareholders out of its profits or reserves.

Examples

dividend payment
He invested in stocks that provide a steady dividend income.
Shareholders were pleased when the company announced a higher dividend for the second quarter.
The board decided to cut the dividend to preserve cash during the downturn.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loss: money lost in business, opposite of profit/dividend
  • synonym – profit: financial gain made in business
