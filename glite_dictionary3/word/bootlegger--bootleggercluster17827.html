bootlegger

noun
seller of unauthorized media copies
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡə / (BrE)
a person who makes, copies, or distributes unauthorized recordings or other media (such as films, music, or software) for sale or distribution.

Examples

music bootlegger
Authorities arrested a bootlegger who distributed unauthorized films on DVD in the city.
Fans bought concert recordings from a bootlegger outside the venue despite the band's protests.
The cybersecurity team identified a bootlegger selling pirated software through several international websites.

Related Terms

  • antonym – authorized distributor: a person or company licensed to distribute media lawfully
  • synonym – pirate: to make, distribute, or sell unauthorized copies of media or software
