predicator

noun
word or phrase expressing the predicate in a sentence
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a word or phrase, especially a verb, that serves as the predicate of a sentence and expresses something about the subject

Examples

predicator in a sentence
In the sentence 'She sings,' the verb 'sings' acts as the predicator.
Many sentences in English require a predicator to be grammatically complete.
The predicator determines the structure and meaning of a clause.
