Gustav Fechner

proper noun
German physicist and founder of psychophysics
Pronunciation: / ˈgʊs.tɑːf ˈfɛk.nər / (AmE), / ˈɡʊstɑːf ˈfɛk.nə / (BrE)
(1801–1887) A German physicist and philosopher who founded psychophysics and formulated Fechner's law, building on the work of E. H. Weber.

Gustav Fechner developed psychophysics
Gustav Fechner played a crucial role in the development of experimental psychology.

The influence of Gustav Fechner can be seen in modern sensory research.
