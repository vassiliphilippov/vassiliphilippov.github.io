retrogressive

adjective
going from better to worse
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
Returning to an earlier, less advanced, or worse state; declining or deteriorating.

Examples

retrogressive policy change
Many saw the reforms as retrogressive, reversing hard-won civil rights.
The director argued against retrogressive changes that would harm student achievement.
The new policy was criticized as retrogressive because it undid years of progress in healthcare.

Related Terms

  • antonym – progressive: favoring or implementing social reform or new ideas; moving forward
  • synonym – regressive: tending to return to a former or less developed state
