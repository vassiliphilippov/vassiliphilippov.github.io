eminence

noun
bony protrusion for muscle or ligament attachment
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnəns / (BrE)
a raised area or projection on a bone, typically serving as a site for the attachment of a muscle or ligament

Examples

bony eminence
An anatomical eminence can be palpated just above the knee joint.
The greater trochanter is a large eminence on the femur for muscle attachment.
The surgeon identified the bony eminence before making the incision.
