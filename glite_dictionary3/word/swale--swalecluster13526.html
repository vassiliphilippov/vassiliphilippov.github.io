swale

noun
a low, marshy area between ridges
Pronunciation: / sweɪl / (AmE), / sweɪl / (BrE)
A low-lying, often marshy or moist area, especially one found between ridges or hills.

Examples

wet swale
After heavy rain, water collected in the swale between the two hills.
The trail followed a swale that separated the grassy ridges.
Wildflowers flourished in the moist swale at the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym – depression: a sunken or lower area of land
  • antonym – ridge: an elevated landform often bordering a swale
