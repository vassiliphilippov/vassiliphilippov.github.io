uppity

adjective
arrogantly self-assertive; presumptuous
Pronunciation: / ˈʌp.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈʌp.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) arrogantly self-assertive or presumptuous; behaving as if one is more important than is appropriate or expected.

Examples

an uppity employee
He grew rather uppity after getting his promotion and stopped listening to his team.
If you get uppity in meetings, you might alienate your colleagues.
The new intern was a bit too uppity for most people’s taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arrogant: having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • antonym – humble: having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
