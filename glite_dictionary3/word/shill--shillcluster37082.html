shill

noun
one who feigns interest to promote something
Pronunciation: / ʃɪl / (AmE), / ʃɪl / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be a genuine customer or supporter, typically to promote a product, service, or event and encourage others to participate or buy

Examples

fake shill at auction
At the auction, the shill kept upping the bids to create excitement.
It was obvious he was just a shill for the company, praising every product they sold.
The casino hired a shill to pretend to win big and attract more gamblers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decoy: a person who entices others into danger or traps
  • synonym – stooge: a person who serves merely to support or assist others, especially in a subordinate or compliant way
