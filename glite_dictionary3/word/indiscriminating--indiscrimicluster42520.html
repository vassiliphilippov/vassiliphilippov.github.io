indiscriminating

adjective
not showing discernment or discrimination
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪsˈkrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
not showing careful judgment or the ability to distinguish differences; lacking discrimination.

Examples

indiscriminating taste in music
He had an indiscriminating taste in music, enjoying almost every genre without preference.
Her indiscriminating generosity meant she gave to every cause, regardless of its merit.
The company's indiscriminating hiring practices led to a mix of skilled and unskilled workers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discriminating: having or showing refined taste or good judgment
