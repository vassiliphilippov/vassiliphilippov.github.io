naked

adjective
without protection or cover
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪkəd /; / ˈneɪkɪd / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪd / (BrE)
not covered or protected; bare or exposed to harm or the elements

Examples

naked branches
The crops were naked to the harsh winter winds after the trees were cut down.
The island stood naked to the sea, with no cliffs or trees for shelter.
Without her armor, she felt naked before her opponents on the battlefield.
The naked branches stood stark against the winter sky.
They had to climb across the naked rocks to reach the water.
Without its usual blanket of snow, the hillside looked naked and exposed.
