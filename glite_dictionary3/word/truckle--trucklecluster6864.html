to truckle

verb
to act in a subservient or cringing way
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkəl / (BrE)
to act in a subservient or overly obedient way, especially to someone in authority

Examples

to truckle to authority
He would truckle to anyone in authority, hoping for a promotion.
She refused to truckle to pressure from her colleagues and stayed true to her ideals.
Some politicians truckle to powerful interests instead of standing by their principles.
He refused to truckle to his boss's unreasonable demands.
She despised those who truckled to authority out of fear.
Some politicians truckle to special interests instead of standing up for what is right.

Related Terms

  • antonym – defy: to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • synonym – fawn: to flatter or behave obsequiously in order to gain favor
  • synonym – kowtow: to act in an excessively subservient manner
  • synonym – submit: to accept or yield to a superior force or to the authority of another
  • synonym – yield: to give way to pressure or submit to another's authority
