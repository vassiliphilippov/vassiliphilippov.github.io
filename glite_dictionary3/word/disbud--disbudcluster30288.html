to disbud

verb
remove some buds for flower quality
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈbʌd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbʌd / (BrE)
to remove some buds from a plant so that the remaining flowers grow larger or of better quality

Examples

to disbud flower plants
It is important to disbud chrysanthemum plants early in the growing season.
She spent the afternoon carefully disbudding her dahlias to ensure a beautiful display.
To encourage larger roses, gardeners often disbud the weaker side shoots.
