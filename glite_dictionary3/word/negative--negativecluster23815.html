to negative

verb
vote against; withhold approval
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
to vote against or refuse to give assent or approval to something, especially in a formal or official context.

Examples

to negative a proposal
He threatened to negative any bill that increased taxes.
If the council chooses to negative the motion, it will not become policy.
The committee decided to negative the proposed amendment after a lengthy debate.
