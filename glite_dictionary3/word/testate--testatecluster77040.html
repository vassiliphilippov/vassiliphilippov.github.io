testate

noun
person who made a will
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛsteɪt / (BrE)
A person who has made a legally valid will before death.

Examples

the testate leaves property
The court determined the testate's capacity to make a valid will at the time of signing.
The property was distributed according to the wishes expressed by the testate in the document.
When a testate dies, the executor must follow the instructions outlined in the will.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intestate: a person who has died without making a will
