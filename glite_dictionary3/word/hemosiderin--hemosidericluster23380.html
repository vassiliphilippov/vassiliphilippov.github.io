hemosiderin

noun
iron-containing pigment from hemoglobin breakdown
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊˈsɪdərɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˈsɪdərɪn / (BrE)
a granular brown pigment consisting of iron oxide, formed from the breakdown of hemoglobin and found in tissues, especially in cases of excess iron storage or bleeding

Examples

hemosiderin deposits in tissue
Excessive accumulation of hemosiderin can indicate problems with iron metabolism or chronic bleeding.
Staining techniques can highlight hemosiderin in liver biopsies, helping to diagnose certain disorders.
The pathologist identified hemosiderin deposits in the tissue sample under the microscope.
