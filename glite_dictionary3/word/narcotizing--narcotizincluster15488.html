narcotizing

adjective
inducing stupor or narcosis
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːr.kəˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.kəˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Inducing stupor or a narcotic state; causing drowsiness or unconsciousness.

Examples

narcotizing effect
She avoided painkillers with narcotizing properties, preferring to stay alert after the operation.
The narcotizing fumes from the chemicals quickly made the workers drowsy.
The anesthesia had a narcotizing effect on the patient, causing him to fall into a deep sleep.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedative: having a calming or sleep-inducing effect
  • antonym – stimulant: causing increased alertness or activity, opposite of narcotizing
