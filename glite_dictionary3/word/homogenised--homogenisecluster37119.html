homogenised

adjective
made uniform by blending elements
Pronunciation: / həˈmɑːdʒəˌnaɪzd /; / həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪzd /; / həˈmɒdʒəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) made uniform in composition or character throughout, usually by blending or processing

Examples

homogenised milk
During the process, various ingredients were thoroughly homogenised to create a uniform mixture.
Milk is often homogenised to ensure the fat is evenly distributed throughout the liquid.
The company produces homogenised fruit juice to provide a smooth and consistent texture.
Many dairy products are homogenised to ensure a smooth texture and consistent flavour.
She preferred unprocessed food over homogenised alternatives found in supermarkets.
The curriculum has become so homogenised that schools across the country offer nearly identical courses.
