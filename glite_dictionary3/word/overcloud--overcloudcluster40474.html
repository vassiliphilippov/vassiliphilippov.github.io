to overcloud

verb
become covered with clouds
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklaʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklaʊd / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) to become covered with clouds, making it cloudy or overcast.

Examples

the sky overclouds
As the temperature dropped, the horizon seemed to overcloud rapidly.
By mid-morning, the sun had vanished as heavy gray clouds overclouded the skyline.
The sky began to overcloud just before the afternoon storm arrived.
