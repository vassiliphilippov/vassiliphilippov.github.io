parliament

noun
national legislative assembly
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlɪmənt / (BrE)
the supreme legislative body in certain countries, responsible for making and passing laws

Examples

parliament passes laws
Canada's parliament consists of two main chambers: the House of Commons and the Senate.
She was elected to parliament in her country's recent general election.
The British parliament convened to debate the new law.
