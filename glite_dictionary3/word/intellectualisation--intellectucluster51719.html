intellectualisation

noun
psychiatric defense mechanism using logic to avoid emotion
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˌlɛktʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntɪˌlɛktjuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) The defense mechanism by which a person uses reasoning or logic to avoid confronting emotional stress or internal conflict.

Examples

intellectualisation of feelings
His reliance on intellectualisation prevented him from addressing his true feelings about the loss.
In therapy, she tended to use intellectualisation to discuss traumatic events without emotional involvement.
Some people resort to intellectualisation as a way of coping with personal conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rationalization: defense mechanism offering a logical explanation to justify unacceptable feelings or behavior
