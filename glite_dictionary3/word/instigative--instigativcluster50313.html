instigative

adjective
provoking or inciting action
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnstɪˌɡeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪnstɪɡətɪv / (BrE)
Tending to provoke or incite others to take action, especially towards rebellion or unrest.

Examples

instigative speech
His instigative behavior encouraged the group to challenge the rules.
The article was criticized for its instigative tone, which some thought might cause unrest.
The politician's instigative remarks led to heated protests across the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inciting: encouraging people to take action, especially illegal action
  • synonym – provocative: tending to provoke or arouse
