to die

verb
lose sparkle or freshness (wine or beer)
Pronunciation: / daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
(of wine or beer) to lose its sparkle or bouquet; become flat or stale.

Examples

die after opening
After sitting open all night, the champagne had died and lost its fizz.
If wine is left uncorked for too long, it will eventually die and taste dull.
The beer began to die after it was poured and forgotten on the counter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pall: to lose strength or freshness; become less interesting or appealing
