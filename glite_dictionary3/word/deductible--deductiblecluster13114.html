deductible

noun
initial expense paid before insurance covers remaining costs
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʌktəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktəbl̩ / (BrE)
a specified amount of money that the insured must pay before an insurance company will pay a claim

Examples

insurance deductible amount
After she met her health plan’s deductible, the insurance began to cover most of her costs.
Our car insurance has a $500 deductible, so we pay that amount before coverage starts.
You can lower your monthly premium by choosing a higher deductible.

Related Terms

  • synonym – excess: amount payable by an insured person before insurance benefits apply (British usage)
