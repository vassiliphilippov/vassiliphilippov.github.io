bathometer

noun
instrument for measuring water depth
Pronunciation: / bəˈθɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / bəˈθɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the depth of water, especially in oceans or lakes

Examples

bathometer reading depth
A bathometer is essential equipment for oceanographic surveys and submarine navigation.
Early explorers relied on the bathometer to create accurate charts of sea depths.
The research team lowered a bathometer into the lake to determine its maximum depth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – depth gauge: an instrument for measuring the depth of something, especially water
  • synonym – sounding line: a line or wire weighted at one end and marked at intervals, used for measuring water depth
