to pierce

verb
make a hole in or through
Pronunciation: / pɪrs / (AmE), / pɪəs / (BrE)
to make a hole in or through something, or to go through something with a sharp object

Examples

to pierce a hole
She watched as the surgeon skillfully pierced the swollen tissue with a needle.
Sunlight pierced the clouds, illuminating the valley below.
The sharp thorn pierced his finger, making him yelp in pain.
A loud scream pierced the silence of the night.
Sunlight pierced through the dense clouds as the storm cleared.
The needle pierced his skin quickly but painlessly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – penetrate: to go into or through something
  • synonym – puncture: to make a small hole in something
  • antonym – seal: to close tightly so as not to let in or out; opposite of pierce in some uses
  • synonym – stab: to push a pointed object into someone or something
