to remonstrate

verb
to protest or object forcefully
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɑnstreɪt /; / rɪˈmɑːnstreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒnstreɪt / (BrE)
to protest or argue forcefully against something; to express strong objection or disapproval

Examples

to remonstrate with a decision
He remonstrated vigorously when accused of cheating during the exam.
Several employees remonstrated with the manager about the new overtime policy.
She remonstrated that the decision was unfair and demanded a reconsideration.
He did not hesitate to remonstrate with the manager about the poor service he had received.
The principal remonstrated with the students for being late to class so often.
When she saw her brother break the vase, she felt compelled to remonstrate angrily.
Several members of the audience remonstrated against the speaker's controversial remarks.
The employees remonstrated with management over the sudden changes to their work schedules.
When she received the unfair penalty, she remonstrated that she had followed all the rules.
