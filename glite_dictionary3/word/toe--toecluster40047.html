to toe

verb
hit a golf ball with the club's toe
Pronunciation: / toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
to hit a golf ball with the toe (outer end) of the club rather than the center of the clubface

Examples

to toe a shot
Golfers sometimes toe a shot when their stance is off balance.
He was frustrated after he toed his drive on the first hole.
She accidentally toed the ball and it veered sharply to the right.
Golfers sometimes toe the ball when their stance is too far from the club.
He accidentally toed his shot, sending the ball veering off to the right.
She toed the drive, resulting in a short, awkward shot.

Related Terms

  • antonym – shank: to hit a golf ball with the club's hosel, producing an unwanted shot
