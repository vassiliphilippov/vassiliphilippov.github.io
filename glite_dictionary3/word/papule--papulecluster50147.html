papule

noun
small inflamed skin bump (no pus)
Pronunciation: / ˈpæp.jul / (AmE), / ˈpap.juːl / (BrE)
a small, raised, inflamed area on the skin that does not produce pus, often seen in conditions like chickenpox

Examples

itchy papule
A papule usually does not contain pus, distinguishing it from other types of skin lesions.
After being exposed to poison ivy, small itchy papules appeared on her skin.
The doctor examined the patient's arm and noted several papules clustered near the elbow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pustule: a small inflamed skin elevation containing pus
