white-lipped

adjective
having white lips (from fear, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt.lɪpt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt.lɪpt / (BrE)
Having pale or white lips, especially as a result of fear, terror, or shock.

Examples

white-lipped with fear
He was white-lipped after hearing the shocking news.
She stood white-lipped with terror as the storm raged outside.
The white-lipped child clung tightly to her mother's hand.
