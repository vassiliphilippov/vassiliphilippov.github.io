morpheme

noun
smallest meaningful language unit
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrˌfim / (AmE), / ˈmɔːfiːm / (BrE)
The smallest meaningful unit of language, which cannot be divided into smaller meaningful parts.

Examples

a single morpheme
Every prefix and suffix you add creates a new morpheme in the word.
Linguists often analyze sentences by breaking them down into their smallest morpheme components.
The word 'unhappiness' contains three morphemes: 'un-', 'happy', and '-ness'.
