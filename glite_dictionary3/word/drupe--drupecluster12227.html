drupe

noun
fleshy fruit with a single stone
Pronunciation: / druːp / (AmE), / druːp / (BrE)
A fleshy fruit with a single hard stone that contains the seed, such as a cherry or olive.

Examples

a drupe fruit
Cherries and peaches are classified as drupes in botanical terms.
The drupe of the olive tree is harvested to produce both oil and table olives.
The almond is actually a drupe, not a true nut.

Related Terms

  • antonym – berry: a small, pulpy, and often edible fruit
