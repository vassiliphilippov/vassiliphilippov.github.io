dodder

noun
a parasitic leafless vine (Cuscuta)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːdər / (AmE), / ˈdɒdə / (BrE)
a leafless parasitic plant of the genus Cuscuta with thin, twining, yellow or whitish stems that obtains nourishment from other plants

Examples

dodder plant stems
Gardeners must remove dodder quickly to stop it from spreading to healthy plants.
The field was overrun with dodder, choking the growth of the crops.
The yellow threads of dodder are easy to spot as they twine around their host.
