Osiris

proper noun
Egyptian god of the underworld
Pronunciation: / oʊˈsaɪrɪs / (AmE), / əʊˈsaɪrɪs / (BrE)
The Egyptian god of the underworld and judge of the dead, husband and brother of Isis, and father of Horus.

Examples

Osiris the Egyptian god
According to legend, Osiris was resurrected by his wife, Isis, after being slain by his brother.
Many ancient myths describe Osiris as the ruler of the afterlife in Egyptian religion.
The annual festival in honor of Osiris symbolized death and rebirth.
