clasp

noun
act of holding tightly
Pronunciation: / klæsp / (AmE), / klɑːsp / (BrE)
an act of holding something tightly with one's hand or arms

Examples

clasp of arms
He loosened his clasp on her shoulders when she seemed uncomfortable.
Her clasp on the child's hand reassured him as they crossed the street.
With a sudden clasp, the mountaineer caught the edge and pulled himself up.
