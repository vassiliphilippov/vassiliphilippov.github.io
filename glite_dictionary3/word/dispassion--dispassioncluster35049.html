dispassion

noun
absence of strong emotion; objectivity
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpæʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈpæʃən / (BrE)
The state of being unemotional, impartial, and not influenced by strong feelings or personal involvement.

Examples

dispassion in judgment
He spoke about the accident with surprising dispassion, as if he had not been involved.
Her scientific research was praised for its dispassion and lack of subjective influence.
The judge handled the case with remarkable dispassion and fairness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bias: preference or prejudice, not objective
  • synonym – objectivity: the quality of being objective and impartial
