to civilise

verb
make more refined or cultured
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪv.əˌlaɪz /; / ˈsɪv.ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or society more cultured, refined, or advanced in manners, behavior, or way of life

Examples

to civilise bad habits
She worked hard to civilise his uncouth manners.
The art class aims to civilise the students' sense of aesthetics.
Travel and literature can civilise the mind and broaden one's horizons.
Efforts were made to civilise the indigenous population by introducing new laws and customs.
Missionaries sought to civilise the region, believing it would benefit the local people.
The teacher hoped to civilise the unruly class through education and discipline.

Related Terms

  • antonym – barbarise: to make barbarous or to become barbaric
  • antonym – barbarize: to make less civilized or more barbaric
  • synonym – cultivate: to improve or refine the mind, taste, or manners
  • synonym – enlighten: to give someone greater knowledge and understanding
