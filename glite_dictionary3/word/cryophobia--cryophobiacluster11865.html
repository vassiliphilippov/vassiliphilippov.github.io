cryophobia

noun
abnormal fear of cold or freezing
Pronunciation: / ˌkraɪ.oʊˈfoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / ˌkraɪ.əˈfəʊ.bi.ə / (BrE)
An extreme or irrational fear of cold or freezing, often recognized as a psychological condition.

Examples

cryophobia in winter
Her cryophobia was so severe that she kept her home’s heating on even during mild temperatures.
People with cryophobia tend to avoid cold beverages and chilly weather at all costs.
The patient’s cryophobia made winter a particularly stressful time of year.
