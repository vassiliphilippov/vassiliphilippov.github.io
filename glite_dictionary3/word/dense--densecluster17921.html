dense

adjective
having high relative density
Pronunciation: / dɛns / (AmE), / dɛns / (BrE)
having a high relative density or specific gravity; containing a lot of matter in a small volume.

Examples

dense metal
Lead is a particularly dense metal, much heavier than aluminum or copper.
Materials with a dense structure tend to conduct heat better.
The liquid in the beaker appeared more dense than water, causing the objects to float.

Related Terms

  • synonym – heavy: having great weight or density
  • antonym – light: having little weight, of low density
