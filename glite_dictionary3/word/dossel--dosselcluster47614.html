dossel

noun
ornamental altar hanging in a church
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːsəl / (AmE), / ˈdɒsəl / (BrE)
an ornamental hanging of rich fabric placed behind the altar of a church or at the sides of the chancel, used as a decorative feature in ecclesiastical settings

Examples

dossel behind altar
Restorers carefully preserved the faded dossel as part of the church's historical interior.
The ancient cathedral displayed a magnificent dossel of crimson velvet behind the altar.
The embroidered dossel added a sense of grandeur to the chancel area.
