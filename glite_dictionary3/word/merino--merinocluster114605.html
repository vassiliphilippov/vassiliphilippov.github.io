merino

noun
breed of sheep known for fine wool
Pronunciation: / məˈriːnoʊ / (AmE), / məˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
A breed of white sheep originally from Spain, valued for its heavy fleece of exceptionally fine wool.

Examples

merino sheep breed
Australian farmers have successfully bred merino sheep for generations.
He keeps a flock of merinos on his farm for both wool and breeding purposes.
The merino is prized for its high-quality wool across the textile industry.
