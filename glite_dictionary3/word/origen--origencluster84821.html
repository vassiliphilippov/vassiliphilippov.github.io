Origen

proper noun
Greek Christian theologian and philosopher (c. 185–254)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrədʒən / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪdʒən / (BrE)
(c. 185–254) A Greek philosopher and theologian who reinterpreted Christian doctrine in light of Neoplatonism; many of his ideas were later considered unorthodox.

Examples

Origen early theologian
Origen was one of the most influential early Christian theologians.
Many of Origen's teachings were later declared heretical by the Church.
The writings of Origen deeply influenced later Christian thought.
