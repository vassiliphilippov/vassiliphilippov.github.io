show

noun
outward display or demonstration
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ʃəʊ / (BrE)
an outward display, pretense, or demonstration intended to create a particular impression or to hide true feelings

Examples

a show of strength
Despite his show of confidence, he was quite nervous inside.
Her apology seemed more like a show than genuine remorse.
The politician made a great show of listening to the public's concerns.
She kept up a brave show of confidence during the interview.
The ceremony was all for show and had little real substance.
Their generosity was merely for show, not out of genuine concern.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appearance: the way that someone or something looks
  • synonym – display: an act of showing something, often deliberately
  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
