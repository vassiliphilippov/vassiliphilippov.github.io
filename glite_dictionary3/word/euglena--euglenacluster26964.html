Euglena

noun
genus of single-celled green algae
Pronunciation: / juːˈɡliːnə / (AmE), / juːˈɡliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of single-celled green algae with a single flagellum, found in fresh and saltwater, known for their ability to photosynthesize and move.

Examples

Euglena under microscope
In biology class, we observed how Euglena can both photosynthesize and move using its flagellum.
Researchers studied the movement of Euglena in response to light exposure.
The microscope revealed dozens of Euglena swimming actively in the water sample.
