Pavlovian

adjective
relating to Pavlov or conditioned reflexes
Pronunciation: / pævˈloʊ.vi.ən / (AmE), / pævˈlɒv.i.ən / (BrE)
Relating to the work of the Russian physiologist Ivan Pavlov, especially his theories and experiments on conditioned reflexes.

Examples

Pavlovian response
Her fear of water was almost Pavlovian, triggered immediately by the sound of waves.
Psychologists studied Pavlovian conditioning to understand how habits are formed.
The dog exhibited a Pavlovian response when it heard the bell ring.

Related Terms

  • synonym – classical conditioning: a type of learning in which a stimulus acquires the capacity to evoke a response originally evoked by another stimulus
  • antonym – operant conditioning: a type of learning where behavior is controlled by consequences, opposed to Pavlovian conditioning
