yet

adverb
to a greater degree (in comparisons)
Pronunciation: / jɛt / (AmE), / jɛt / (BrE)
to a greater degree or extent; used to emphasize a comparative, especially in set phrases such as 'a yet sadder tale' or 'yet another problem'.

Examples

yet another problem
Her voice grew yet softer as she shared the sad news.
It was yet more evidence that the problem was widespread.
The team faced yet another unexpected challenge before the project was complete.
