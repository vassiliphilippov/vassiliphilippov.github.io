chameleon

noun
color-changing lizard of Africa and Madagascar
Pronunciation: / kəˈmiliən / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliən / (BrE)
A small, slow-moving lizard found mainly in Africa and Madagascar, known for its ability to change the color of its skin and for its long, projectile tongue used to catch prey.

Examples

chameleon changes color
A chameleon can shoot its tongue out quickly to catch insects.
Many people are fascinated by the chameleon's ability to adapt its appearance.
The chameleon changed its color to blend in with the green leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lizard: a type of reptile with scaly skin and four legs
