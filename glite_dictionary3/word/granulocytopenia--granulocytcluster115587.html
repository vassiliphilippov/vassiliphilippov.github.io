granulocytopenia

noun
very low granulocyte count in blood
Pronunciation: / ɡræn.jə.loʊ.saɪ.təˈpiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ɡran.jʊ.ləʊ.saɪ.təˈpiː.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an abnormally low number of granulocytes in the blood, often resulting from radiation or drug therapy.

Examples

acute granulocytopenia case
Acute granulocytopenia can lead to life-threatening complications if not treated promptly.
Doctors ordered a blood test to check for granulocytopenia after the patient showed unusual fatigue and fever.
Following chemotherapy, she developed granulocytopenia and was monitored closely for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – leukopenia: Low white blood cell count
  • synonym – neutropenia: Abnormally low number of neutrophils in the blood
