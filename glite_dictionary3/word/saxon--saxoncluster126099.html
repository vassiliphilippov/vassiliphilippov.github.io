Saxon

noun
member of an early Germanic people in England
Pronunciation: / ˈsæk.sən / (AmE), / ˈsæk.sən / (BrE)
a member of a Germanic people who settled in England in the early Middle Ages and, along with the Angles and Jutes, formed the Anglo-Saxon population; dominant in England until the Norman Conquest

Examples

ancient Saxon people
After the Norman Conquest, the influence of the Saxons on English law and language began to wane.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts belonging to the Saxon settlers in southern England.
The Saxons played a crucial role in shaping early English society and culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Angle: a member of a Germanic people who settled in Britain and helped form the Anglo-Saxons
  • degree higher – Anglo-Saxon: a person of English descent from the period after the merging of Angles, Saxons, and Jutes
