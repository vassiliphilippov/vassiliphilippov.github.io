hogchoker

noun
a small inedible North American flatfish
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔgˌtʃoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈhɒgˌtʃəʊkə / (BrE)
a small flatfish (Trinectes maculatus) found in coastal streams from Maine to Texas, considered useless as food due to its tough texture

Examples

a hogchoker fish
Despite its abundance, the hogchoker is rarely eaten due to its poor taste and tough skin.
Fishermen often catch hogchokers by accident while netting for more valuable species.
The muddy creek was full of catfish, sunfish, and the occasional hogchoker.
