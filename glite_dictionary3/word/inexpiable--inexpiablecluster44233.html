inexpiable

adjective
impossible to atone for or forgive
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɛkspiəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛkspiəbəl / (BrE)
(of a fault or offense) so serious that it cannot be atoned for or forgiven.

Examples

inexpiable crime
He felt a deep, inexpiable guilt for his betrayal of trust.
Some transgressions are considered inexpiable no matter how much regret is shown.
The crime was deemed inexpiable by both the community and the law.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgivable: able to be forgiven
  • synonym – unpardonable: impossible to pardon or forgive
