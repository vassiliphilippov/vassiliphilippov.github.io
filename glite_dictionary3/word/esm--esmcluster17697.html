ESM

noun
detection of enemy electromagnetic signals (military)
Pronunciation: / i ɛs ɛm / (AmE), / iː ɛs ɛm / (BrE)
(abbreviation) Electronic Support Measures; the use of electronic systems to detect, intercept, or locate sources of electromagnetic radiation, mainly for immediate threat recognition in military operations.

Examples

ESM detects signals
Modern warships rely heavily on ESM to detect and analyze hostile radars.
The importance of ESM has increased with the growing complexity of electronic warfare.
The radar operators used ESM equipment to identify potential threats on the battlefield.

  • synonym – electronic warfare: military action involving the use of electromagnetic spectrum to attack or impede the enemy
