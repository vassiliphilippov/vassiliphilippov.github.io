Fabiana imbricata

noun
a South American flowering shrub with medicinal uses
Pronunciation: / fəˌbiːɑːnə ɪmˌbrɪkətə / (AmE), / fəˌbiːɑːnə ɪmˌbrɪkətə / (BrE)
A shrub native to South America, particularly Peru and Chile, known for its small tubular pink to lavender flowers and tonic, diuretic properties derived from its leaves.
