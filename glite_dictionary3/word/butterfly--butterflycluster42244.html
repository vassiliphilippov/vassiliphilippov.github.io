butterfly

noun
a swimming stroke with both arms moving together
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
A swimming stroke performed by throwing both arms forward together out of the water while the legs kick up and down in a dolphin-like motion.

Examples

butterfly swimming stroke
Michael Phelps won a gold medal in the 100-meter butterfly at the Olympics.
She has been practicing her butterfly to improve her speed in competitions.
The butterfly requires great strength and coordination to master.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backstroke: a swimming stroke executed while lying on the back
  • synonym – breaststroke: a swimming stroke performed on the chest with the arms moving in halves of a heart shape and the legs doing a frog kick
  • synonym – freestyle: a category of swimming stroke where any method is allowed, usually the front crawl
