disadvantageously

adverb
in an unfavorable or harmful way
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.əd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs.li / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that causes harm, loss, or is unfavorable to someone or something.

Examples

treated disadvantageously

The new policy affected local businesses disadvantageously.
The rule changes operate disadvantageously for newcomers to the field.
