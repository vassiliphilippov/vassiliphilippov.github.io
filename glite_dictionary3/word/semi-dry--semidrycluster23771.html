semi-dry

adjective
somewhat dry; moderately dry
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈdraɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈdraɪ / (BrE)
Moderately dry; not completely dry but not wet either.

Examples

semi-dry pavement
After the rain, the pavement was only semi-dry and still a bit slippery.
Make sure the mop is semi-dry before you use it on the wooden floor.
The winemaker described the rosé as semi-dry with a hint of sweetness.
