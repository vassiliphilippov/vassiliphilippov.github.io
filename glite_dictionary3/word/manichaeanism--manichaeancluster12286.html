Manichaeanism

noun
ancient dualistic religion founded by Mani
Pronunciation: / ˌmænɪˈkiːəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
An ancient religion founded by the prophet Mani in the third century CE, combining elements of Zoroastrianism, Babylonian folklore, Buddhism, and Christianity, especially known for its dualistic principles of light and darkness.

Examples

ancient Manichaeanism religion
Manichaeanism spread from Persia to the Roman Empire and influenced later religious movements.
Scholars study Manichaeanism to understand its impact on early Christian thought.
The dualism of light and darkness is central to the teachings of Manichaeanism.
