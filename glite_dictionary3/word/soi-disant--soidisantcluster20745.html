soi-disant

adjective
self-styled; so-called (often falsely)
Pronunciation: / swɑː diˈzɑnt / (AmE), / swɑː diˈzɑ̃ / (BrE)
Describing someone who claims or styles themselves as having a particular title, rank, or quality, often without justification or recognition by others.

Examples

soi-disant expert
His soi-disant credentials did not withstand close scrutiny.
Many doubted the soi-disant prince's claim to royalty.
The soi-disant expert offered bizarre advice that no reputable doctor would endorse.

Related Terms

  • synonym – self-styled: claiming a particular title or status for oneself, often without right
  • synonym – so-called: used to express doubt or skepticism about someone's claims
