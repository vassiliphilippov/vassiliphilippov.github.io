Jesuitry

noun
practices or theology of the Jesuits
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛzjuˌɪtri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjʊɪtri / (BrE)
The theology, teachings, or practices of the Jesuits, often implying clever or subtle reasoning, especially casuistry.

Examples

clever Jesuitry arguments
His reputation for subtle Jesuitry made people wary of his true intentions.
Some critics saw the ruling as an example of legal Jesuitry, twisting law to fit convenience.
The pamphlet accused the church leaders of Jesuitry in their arguments about doctrine.
