languisher

noun
person who languishes
Pronunciation: / ˈlæŋɡwɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪʃə / (BrE)
A person who loses vigor or vitality, or suffers from neglect, hopelessness, or prolonged weakness.

Examples

a lonely languisher
In her poetry, she describes the languisher trapped in a world without hope.
The languisher sat by the window, yearning for freedom from his isolation.
The novel's protagonist is a languisher whose dreams slowly fade amidst adversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – languish: to lose or lack vitality; to become weak or feeble
