Joseon

proper noun
Korea's name under Japanese rule (1910–1945)
Pronunciation: / tʃoʊˈsʌn / (AmE), / tʃəʊˈsɒn / (BrE)
the name used for Korea as a Japanese province from 1910 to 1945 during Japanese colonial rule

Examples

Joseon under Japanese rule
During the colonial period, Korea was officially known as Joseon by the Japanese authorities.
Many historical texts from 1910 to 1945 refer to the peninsula as Joseon.
The policies imposed in Joseon had a profound impact on Korean society and culture.
