Gita

noun
sacred Hindu scripture; part of the Mahabharata
Pronunciation: / ˈgiːtə / (AmE), / ˈɡiːtə / (BrE)
the sacred Hindu scripture, also known as the 'song of God,' found in the Mahabharata, presenting a dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna on life, duty, and spiritual wisdom.

Examples

the Gita scripture
During the discussion, she quoted from the Gita to make her point about duty.
Many students study the Gita to learn about Hindu philosophy and moral dilemmas.
The Gita offers profound insights into human nature and the pursuit of righteousness.
