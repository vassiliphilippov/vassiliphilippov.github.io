newton

noun
SI unit of force
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtən / (BrE)
the SI unit of force, equal to the force that imparts an acceleration of one meter per second squared to a mass of one kilogram

Examples

500 newtons of force
Engineers calculated that the structure could withstand forces up to 500,000 newtons.
One newton is the amount of force needed to accelerate a one kilogram mass by one meter per second squared.
The force required to move the object was measured in newtons.
