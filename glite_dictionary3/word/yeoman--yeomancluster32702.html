yeoman

noun
historical small landowner
Pronunciation: / ˈjoʊmən / (AmE), / ˈjəʊmən / (BrE)
(historical) A man who owned and cultivated his own land, ranking below the gentry in former English society.

Examples

yeoman farmer
A yeoman in medieval England would often work his own fields alongside hired help.
Shakespeare sometimes described the loyalty and honesty of a yeoman.
The social class of the yeoman played a vital role in rural communities.
