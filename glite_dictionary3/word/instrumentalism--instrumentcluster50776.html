instrumentalism

noun
theory that ideas are instruments for practical action
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A theory in philosophy which holds that ideas are tools or instruments for action, and their value is measured by how well they work in practice.

Examples

philosophical instrumentalism
In philosophy class, we discussed how instrumentalism differs from realism and idealism.
John Dewey was a leading proponent of instrumentalism in American philosophy.
Some critics argue that instrumentalism places too much emphasis on practical outcomes rather than theoretical truth.
