precaution

noun
the act or trait of being careful in advance
Pronunciation: / prɪˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
care taken in advance to prevent harm or danger; the quality of being cautious or careful

Examples

natural precaution
His natural precaution kept him from making rash decisions.
She acted with great precaution when handling the chemicals.
Taking every possible precaution is wise before starting a new medicine.
Out of an abundance of precaution, the company tested all the fire alarms.
Wearing gloves and a mask was a sensible precaution during flu season.
You should always take extra precaution when walking home alone at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym – carelessness: lack of care or attention; negligence
  • synonym – caution: care to avoid danger or mistakes
  • synonym – prudence: the quality of being prudent or careful; good judgment
