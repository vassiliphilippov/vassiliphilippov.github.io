Tuberaceae

noun
family of fungi including truffles
Pronunciation: / tuːbəˈreɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / tjuːbəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi whose fruiting bodies resemble tubers, varying in size from that of an acorn to that of a large apple, and including truffles.

Examples

Tuberaceae family fungi
Mycologists study the Tuberaceae to better understand the ecology of truffle-producing fungi.
Several species within the Tuberaceae have symbiotic relationships with tree roots.
The Tuberaceae family is best known for its edible truffles, prized in gourmet cuisine.
