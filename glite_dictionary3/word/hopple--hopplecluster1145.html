to hopple

verb
strap a horse's legs to control gait
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːpəl / (AmE), / ˈhɒpəl / (BrE)
to strap the foreleg and hind leg on the same side of a horse together, in order to keep those legs moving in unison

Examples

to hopple a horse
Care must be taken not to hopple the horse too tightly, to avoid injury.
Some standardbred horses are hoppled for certain types of harness racing events.
The trainer decided to hopple the young horse so it would learn to pace.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hobble: to fasten together the legs of (a horse, etc.) to prevent free motion; to restrain
