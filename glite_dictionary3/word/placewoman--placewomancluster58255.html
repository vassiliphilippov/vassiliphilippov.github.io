placewoman

noun
female appointee (disparaging)
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪswʊmən / (AmE), / ˈpleɪswʊmən / (BrE)
A female who is appointed to a position, often implying she holds the post due to connections rather than merit, used with a disparaging tone.

Examples

political placewoman
He dismissed her achievements, unfairly labeling her as a mere placewoman in the organization.
Many critics argue that she is just a placewoman serving the interests of the party leadership.
The board was dominated by placewomen and placemen with little independent authority.
