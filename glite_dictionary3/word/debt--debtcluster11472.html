debt

noun
future cost from quick or suboptimal design
Pronunciation: / d5bt / (AmE), / d5bt / (BrE)
A metaphorical obligation in computing and software engineering representing the extra future work and cost required to fix or improve systems because of earlier shortcuts, quick fixes, or suboptimal design choices.

Examples

technical debt grows
The project accrued technical debt after rushed coding, increasing later maintenance work.
Managers often accept debt to meet a deadline, but it multiplies and slows releases.
They scheduled time each sprint to pay down accumulated debt and clean up the codebase.

Related Terms

  • synonym – liability: a responsibility or obligation that may result in future cost or effort
  • synonym – maintenance cost: future work and expense required to fix, improve, or maintain software or systems
