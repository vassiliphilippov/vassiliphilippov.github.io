cryptorchism

noun
failure of testes to descend
Pronunciation: / krɪpˈtɔːr kɪzəm / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɔːk ɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which one or both testes fail to move into the scrotum before birth.

Examples

cryptorchism in newborn boys
If left untreated, cryptorchism can increase the risk of infertility and testicular cancer.
Surgery is often recommended to correct cryptorchism in young boys.
The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with cryptorchism after noticing an undescended testicle during the examination.
