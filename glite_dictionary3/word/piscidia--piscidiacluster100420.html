Piscidia

proper noun
genus of shrubs used for fish poisons
Pronunciation: / pɪˈsɪdiə / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪdiə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs or small trees with indehiscent pods containing black seeds; the roots and bark are used as fish poisons.
