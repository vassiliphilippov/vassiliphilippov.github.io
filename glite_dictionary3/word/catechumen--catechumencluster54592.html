catechumen

noun
person undergoing Christian instruction before baptism
Pronunciation: / ˌkætɪˈkjuːmən / (AmE), / ˌkatɪˈkjuːmən / (BrE)
a person who is being taught the basic principles of Christianity before baptism

Examples

new catechumen class
As a catechumen, she attended weekly lessons on the fundamentals of the Christian faith.
In early Christianity, a catechumen could spend years studying before being baptized.
The church holds special classes for each catechumen preparing for baptism.
