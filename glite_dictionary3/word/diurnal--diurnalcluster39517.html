diurnal

adjective
active during the day
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɝːnəl / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːnəl / (BrE)
active or occurring during the daytime rather than at night

Examples

diurnal animals
Many flowers are diurnal and only open when the sun is out.
Owls are nocturnal, but most birds are diurnal and active in daylight.
The scientist studied the diurnal patterns of the animal’s behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nocturnal: active at night rather than during the day
