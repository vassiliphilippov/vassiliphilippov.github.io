Afro-American

noun
American of African ancestry
Pronunciation: / ˌæfroʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
A person in the United States whose ancestors were African, particularly one descended from enslaved Black Africans.

Examples

Afro-American leader
In the 1960s, the term Afro-American gained popularity among civil rights groups.
Langston Hughes was a prominent Afro-American poet and social activist.
The museum features an exhibit on Afro-American culture and history.
