to blow

verb
(of electrical parts) to break or stop working
Pronunciation: / bloʊ / (AmE), / bləʊ / (BrE)
to break, burst, or fail suddenly, especially due to pressure or electrical overload

Examples

to blow a fuse
If you turn on too many lights at once, you might blow a circuit breaker.
The lightbulb suddenly blew and the room went dark.
The old radio stopped working because the fuse blew.
If the pipe blows again, we'll need to call a plumber right away.
The old balloon blew as soon as it touched the rough surface.
When the tire blew on the highway, we had to pull over immediately.
