false

adjective
not faithful; untrustworthy
Pronunciation: / fɔls / (AmE), / fɔːls / (BrE)
Not loyal or trustworthy in devotion or affection; unfaithful or treacherous.

Examples

false friend
Despite his charming words, his promises turned out to be false and empty.
History is filled with tales of false allies who betrayed their companions.
She realized too late that he was a false friend who only cared about himself.
