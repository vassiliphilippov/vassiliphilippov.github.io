ESR

noun
blood test for inflammation (medical)
Pronunciation: / i ɛs ɑr / (AmE), / i ɛs ɑː / (BrE)
abbreviation for erythrocyte sedimentation rate; a blood test measuring how quickly red blood cells settle in a test tube, used to detect inflammation.

Examples

ESR blood test
A high ESR may indicate an underlying disease such as infection or autoimmune disorder.
Her ESR was elevated, prompting further diagnostic tests.
The doctor ordered an ESR to check for signs of inflammation.
