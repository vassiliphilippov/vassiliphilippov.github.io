to close

verb
come to an end; conclude
Pronunciation: / kloʊz / (AmE), / kləʊz / (BrE)
to end or finish something (such as an event, meeting, business deal, market session, or game)

Examples

to close a meeting
As the meeting closed, everyone stood to applaud the guest speaker.
The ceremony will close with a speech from the president.
The exhibition will close at the end of September.
After weeks of negotiation, we finally closed the deal yesterday.
She was relieved when they closed the agreement without further delays.
The company expects to close on the new office space next month.
Shares closed slightly lower after a day of volatile trading.
The Dow Jones index closed at a record high yesterday.
The company’s stock closed at $42 per share on Friday.

Related Terms

  • antonym – begin: to start or commence
  • antonym – cancel: to call off or annul a business deal or agreement
  • synonym – end: to come to an end; to finish
  • synonym – finalize: bring something to a conclusion, especially an agreement
  • synonym – finish: to bring an activity or event to an end
  • antonym – open: (of a financial market) to begin trading at a particular price
  • synonym – save: (baseball) an act of a relief pitcher preserving a lead to win the game
