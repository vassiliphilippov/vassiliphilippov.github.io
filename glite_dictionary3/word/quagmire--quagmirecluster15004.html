quagmire

noun
soft, wet, muddy ground
Pronunciation: / ˈkwæɡˌmaɪr / (AmE), / ˈkwæɡˌmaɪə / (BrE)
an area of soft, wet, muddy ground that gives way underfoot

Examples

muddy quagmire
After the flood, the fields became a treacherous quagmire for days.
Many animals make their homes in the quagmire at the edge of the forest.
The hiker got stuck in the quagmire after a night of heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bog: wet, spongy ground, often a natural habitat
  • antonym – dry land: land that is not wet or boggy
  • synonym – swamp: a wetland with standing water and vegetation
