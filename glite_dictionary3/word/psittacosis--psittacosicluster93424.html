psittacosis

noun
disease from birds causing atypical pneumonia
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪtəˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪtəkəʊˈsɪs /; / ˌsɪtəˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Chlamydia psittaci, usually found in birds but can be transmitted to humans, causing flu-like symptoms
