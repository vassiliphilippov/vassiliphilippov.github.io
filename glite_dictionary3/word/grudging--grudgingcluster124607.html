grudging

adjective
unwilling or reluctant
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
given or done unwillingly or with reluctance

Examples

grudging approval
His grudging admiration became obvious when he praised the team's efforts.
She offered a grudging apology after being confronted about her mistake.

He made a grudging donation to the charity, showing little enthusiasm.
She gave a grudging apology after being confronted by her manager.
The company offered a grudging pay increase to its employees.
