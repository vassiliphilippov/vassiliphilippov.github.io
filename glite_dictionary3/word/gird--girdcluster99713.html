to gird

verb
put a belt or girdle around
Pronunciation: / ɡɜrd /; / ɡɝd / (AmE), / ɡɜːd / (BrE)
to encircle or bind something with a belt, band, or similar object; to surround closely

Examples

gird the waist
As the storm approached, they girded the tents tightly to withstand the wind.
Before the race, he decided to gird himself with a supportive belt.
The walls gird the old city, protecting it from invaders.
Clouds girded the mountaintop, hiding it from view.
She girded the package with a thick ribbon before sending it.
The knight girded his sword around his waist before battle.

  • synonym – surround: to encircle on all sides
  • antonym – unbind: to remove or release from a binding or restraint
