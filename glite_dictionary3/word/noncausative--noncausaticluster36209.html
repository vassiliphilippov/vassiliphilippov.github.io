noncausative

adjective
not expressing causation (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɔːzəˌtɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɔːzətɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb or construction) not expressing a causative action; not causing something to happen.

Examples

noncausative verb form
In English, the verb 'fall' is considered noncausative, while 'drop' is causative.
Many languages distinguish between causative and noncausative verb forms.
The noncausative use of 'open' describes an event happening without an agent.
