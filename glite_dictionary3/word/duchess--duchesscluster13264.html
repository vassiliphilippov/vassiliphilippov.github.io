duchess

noun
female noble title; wife of a duke
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌtʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃ.ɪs / (BrE)
a woman who holds the rank of duke in her own right or is the wife of a duke

Examples

the duchess of York
After her husband's death, she became the reigning duchess of the estate.
Children waved excitedly as the duchess passed by in her carriage.
The duchess greeted her guests with grace and elegance during the royal banquet.
