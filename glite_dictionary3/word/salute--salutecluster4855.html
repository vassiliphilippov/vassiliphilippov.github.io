to salute

verb
make a formal military gesture of respect
Pronunciation: / səˈlut / (AmE), / səˈluːt / (BrE)
to make a formal gesture of respect or greeting, especially in the military, by raising the hand to the forehead or taking a prescribed position

Examples

to salute an officer
All soldiers are required to salute their commanding officers.
She saluted smartly, following the proper military protocol.
The cadet stood at attention and saluted as the general entered the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – greet: to address someone with expressions of recognition or welcome
  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to someone
