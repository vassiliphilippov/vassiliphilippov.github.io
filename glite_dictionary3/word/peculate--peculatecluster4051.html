to peculate

verb
to embezzle; misuse entrusted property
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛkjʊˌleɪt / (BrE)
to embezzle or fraudulently take for one's own use property or money entrusted to one's care

Examples

to peculate public money
For years, he managed to peculate small sums from the trust without detection.
It was discovered that the manager had tried to peculate funds from the organization.
The auditor warned that anyone who attempted to peculate company assets would face prosecution.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embezzle: to steal or misappropriate money placed in one's trust
