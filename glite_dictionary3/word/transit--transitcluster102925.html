to transit

verb
revolve a surveyor's telescope to reverse direction
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.zɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
(of the telescope of a surveying instrument) to revolve about its horizontal axis in order to reverse its direction.

Examples

to transit the telescope
Before making the next reading, be sure to transit the instrument carefully.
He quickly transited the scope to ensure accurate alignment with the new reference point.
The surveyor transited the telescope to take measurements in the opposite direction.
