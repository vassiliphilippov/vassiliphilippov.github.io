subscript

noun
character written below another
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
a character, symbol, or number written or printed just below and to the right of another character, typically to indicate a modified meaning

Examples

subscript number in formula
In chemical formulas, the oxygen in H2O has a subscript 2 to indicate two hydrogen atoms.
Please write the index as a subscript so it is clear you're referring to elements of the sequence.
The subscript in this equation helps distinguish between different variables.

Related Terms

  • antonym – superscript: a character or symbol written above and to the side of another character
