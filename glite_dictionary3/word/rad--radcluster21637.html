rad

noun
unit of plane angle (radian)
Pronunciation: / ræd / (AmE), / ræd / (BrE)
a unit of plane angle equal to the angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc equal in length to the radius; short for radian, approximately 57.295 degrees

Examples

angle of 1 rad
The angle was measured as two rad in the experiment.
This calculator can display results in either degrees or rad.
Trigonometric functions are often evaluated in rad rather than degrees.

Related Terms

  • antonym – degree: a unit for measuring angles, equal to 1/360 of a full rotation
  • synonym – radian: the full form of 'rad' as a unit of angle
