highbrow

adjective
culturally or intellectually superior
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbraʊ / (BrE)
intellectually or culturally superior; relating to people or activities considered highly cultured or educated.

Examples

highbrow art exhibition
His taste in music became increasingly highbrow after college.
She prefers highbrow literature to popular novels.
The museum is known for its highbrow exhibitions that attract art enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intellectual: relating to the intellect or to high-level thinking
  • antonym – lowbrow: not highly intellectual or cultured
