distressingly

adverb
in a way that causes distress or discomfort
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes distress, anxiety, or unhappiness; unpleasantly.

Examples

distressingly frequent events
Her memory loss became distressingly frequent over the past year.
It was distressingly clear that the project would not be finished on time.
The city's pollution was distressingly apparent during our visit.
