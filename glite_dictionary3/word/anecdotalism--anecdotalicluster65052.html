anecdotalism

noun
tendency to use anecdotes
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ɪkˈdoʊ.t̬ə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪkˈdəʊ.tə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The tendency to use or rely on anecdotes, often at the expense of systematic analysis or comprehensive evidence.

Examples

anecdotalism in arguments
A focus on anecdotalism can overshadow the importance of scientifically gathered data.
Critics accused the report of anecdotalism rather than providing robust statistical evidence.
The book's anecdotalism made it entertaining but sometimes lacked deeper analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anecdotal: Based on or consisting of anecdotes rather than data or analysis
