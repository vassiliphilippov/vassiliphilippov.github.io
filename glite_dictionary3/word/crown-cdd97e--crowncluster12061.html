to crown

verb
to formally make someone king or queen
Pronunciation: / kraʊn / (AmE), / kraʊn / (BrE)
to ceremonially declare (someone) king or queen, usually by placing a crown on their head during a formal event

Examples

to crown a king
She was crowned queen in a grand and solemn gathering.
The archbishop will crown the new monarch during the ceremony.
The tradition is to crown the king with a jeweled gold crown.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dethrone: to remove (a monarch) from the throne
  • synonym – enthrone: to seat (someone) on a throne, especially as a monarch
