firecracker

noun
small explosive firework
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌkrækər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌkrækə / (BrE)
a small explosive charge in a heavy paper casing, ignited by a fuse, used as a firework or noisemaker

Examples

light a firecracker
It can be dangerous to hold a firecracker after lighting the fuse.
She lit a string of firecrackers to celebrate the New Year.
The children laughed when the firecracker exploded loudly in the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – firework: a device containing combustible chemicals that causes a spectacular explosion when ignited
