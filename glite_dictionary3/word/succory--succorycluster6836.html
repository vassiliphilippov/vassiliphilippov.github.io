succory

noun
perennial plant (chicory), edible roots and leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkəri / (AmE), / ˈsʌkəri / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the daisy family with blue flowers, cultivated for its edible roots and leaves used in salads; also known as chicory.

Examples

succory leaves salad
Farmers often grow succory for its roots, which are roasted and ground as a coffee substitute.
Fresh succory leaves add a pleasant bitterness to mixed green salads.
Wild succory can be found blooming along roadsides in the summer.
