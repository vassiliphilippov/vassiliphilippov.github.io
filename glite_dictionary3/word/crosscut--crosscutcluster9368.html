to crosscut

verb
to cut across, usually diagonally
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌt / (BrE)
to cut across something, especially at a diagonal or right angle to its main axis or grain

Examples

to crosscut wood
The carpenter decided to crosscut the plank to fit it into the frame.
Using a sharp saw, she crosscut the logs at an angle for the project.
You should always measure twice before you crosscut any expensive materials.
