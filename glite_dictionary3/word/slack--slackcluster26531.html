slack

noun
coal dust and small fragments
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
fine coal dust and small fragments of coal mixed with dirt, especially the residue left after screening coal through a sieve

Examples

slack coal
After screening, only a heap of slack remained at the bottom of the sieve.
He sold the slack to local farmers, who used it for heating.
The workers shoveled the slack into piles to be removed from the coal yard.
