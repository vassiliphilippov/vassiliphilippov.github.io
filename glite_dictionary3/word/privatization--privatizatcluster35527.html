privatization

noun
transfer from public to private ownership
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪ.və.t̬əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpraɪ.və.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of transferring ownership or control of a business, service, or property from the public sector to private individuals or organizations.

Examples

privatization of public services
Critics argue that privatization can lead to higher prices for essential services.
Several countries have pursued privatization to improve efficiency in their economies.
The government announced the privatization of the national railway system.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nationalization: transfer of private assets into state ownership
