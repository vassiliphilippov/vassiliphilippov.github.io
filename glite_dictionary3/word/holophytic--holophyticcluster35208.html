holophytic

adjective
obtaining nourishment by photosynthesis
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊləˈfɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒləˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of organisms) obtaining nourishment by synthesizing organic matter from inorganic substances, as green plants do through photosynthesis.

Examples

holophytic nutrition method
Organisms with a holophytic lifestyle require sunlight to synthesize organic compounds from inorganic materials.
Some algae are holophytic, producing their own food through photosynthesis.
The holophytic mode of nutrition allows green plants to survive in a range of environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – autotrophic: (of organisms) able to produce their own food from inorganic substances
