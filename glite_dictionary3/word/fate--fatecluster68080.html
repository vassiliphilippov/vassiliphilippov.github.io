to fate

verb
determine or designate beforehand
Pronunciation: / feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
to decree or designate (someone or something) to a particular outcome, typically by destiny or a higher power

Examples

to fate a meeting
It seemed as if their meeting was fated from the start.
Some believed he was fated to lead the country through difficult times.
The tragedy was fated to happen, despite all their precautions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – destine: to intend or determine beforehand; to decree as a future event
