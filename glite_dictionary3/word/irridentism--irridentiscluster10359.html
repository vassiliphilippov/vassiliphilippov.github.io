irridentism

noun
belief in reclaiming related territories
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛdəntɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈred.ən.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The political principle or policy that a country should reclaim and control territories inhabited by people who share its ethnic or historical ties.
