to lose

verb
to fail to keep in sight or mind
Pronunciation: / luːz / (AmE), / luːz / (BrE)
to be unable to see, find, or keep track of someone or something; to let someone or something go out of your sight or mind

Examples

to lose sight
If we take this shortcut, we might lose the tour group in the crowd.
She often gets so absorbed in thought that she can lose track of what's happening around her.
The detective managed not to lose sight of the suspect for most of the chase.

Related Terms

  • antonym – find: to come upon by searching; to discover something lost
