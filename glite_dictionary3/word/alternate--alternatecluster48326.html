alternate

noun
a substitute or replacement person
Pronunciation: / ˈɔl.tɚ.nət / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.nət / (BrE)
a person designated to take the place of another if necessary; a substitute or replacement

Examples

alternate on a jury
If one of the jurors cannot serve, an alternate will be called in.
She was chosen as an alternate for the Olympic team in case someone was injured.
There is always an alternate available to replace a missing player on the roster.
