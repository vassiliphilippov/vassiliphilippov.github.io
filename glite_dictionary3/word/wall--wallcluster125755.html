wall

noun
membrane or tissue enclosing a structure
Pronunciation: / wɔl / (AmE), / wɔːl / (BrE)
a layer of tissue or membrane that forms the boundary or lining enclosing an organ or structure in the body.

Examples

stomach wall
Muscle contractions help move food along the intestinal wall.
The doctor explained that a perforation in the stomach wall could be dangerous.
The heart's walls are made up of several layers of tissue.
