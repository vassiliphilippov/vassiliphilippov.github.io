alidad

noun
surveying instrument for drawing lines of sight
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌdæd / (BrE)
A surveying instrument used with a plane table, consisting of a straightedge with sights, for drawing lines of sight to distant objects and measuring angles.

Examples

alidad for plane table
The alidad is an essential tool for traditional plane table surveying.
The surveyor carefully adjusted the alidad to align with the distant church steeple.
Using the alidad, the engineers traced accurate lines on the map.
