downswing

noun
downward movement in a golf swing
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌswɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌswɪŋ / (BrE)
the part of a golf swing in which the club is swung downward toward the ball after reaching its highest point

Examples

golf downswing motion
A quick downswing can lead to a slice if not controlled properly.
Improving your downswing will help you achieve greater distance with your drives.
