crushing

noun
forceful suppression or defeat
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of forcefully suppressing or defeating something or someone, especially by authority or with overwhelming power

Examples

crushing of opposition
Her novel describes the crushing of freedom during the dictatorship era.
The government responded to the protests with a swift and decisive crushing of dissent.
There was a brutal crushing of the workers' strike in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – encouragement: the act of giving support, confidence, or hope
  • synonym – quelling: the act of putting an end to something, typically by force
  • synonym – suppression: the act of preventing something from continuing or being expressed
