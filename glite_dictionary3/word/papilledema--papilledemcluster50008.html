papilledema

noun
swelling of the optic disc in the eye
Pronunciation: / ˌpæpɪlɪˈdimə / (AmE), / ˌpapɪlɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
swelling of the optic disc, where the optic nerve enters the eye, usually due to increased intracranial or intraocular pressure

Examples

papilledema from high pressure
Prolonged papilledema can lead to permanent vision loss if not addressed promptly.
The ophthalmologist detected papilledema during a routine eye examination.
The patient was referred to a neurologist after signs of papilledema were observed.
