knowing

adjective
having or showing secret or inside information
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
showing that you have secret or inside information; suggestive of private knowledge or understanding

Examples

knowing look
She gave him a knowing look as she handed him the letter.
The detective offered a knowing smile when asked about the case.
With a knowing nod, he indicated that he understood the hidden message.

Related Terms

  • antonym – innocent: not showing knowledge or suspicion; lacking secret knowledge
