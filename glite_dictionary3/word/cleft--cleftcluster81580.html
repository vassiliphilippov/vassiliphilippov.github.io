cleft

adjective
split or divided (esp. anatomically)
Pronunciation: / klɛft / (AmE), / klɛft / (BrE)
divided or split into two or more parts, often along a natural line (used for anatomical features, objects, or leaves)

Examples

cleft chin
He smiled, revealing a prominent cleft chin.
Her baby was diagnosed with a cleft palate that would require surgery.
The log was wedged open with a cleft stick.
Some ferns display cleft fronds that appear almost divided into separate segments.
The botanist pointed out the cleft leaves as a key feature of the species.
The maple tree's leaves are distinctly cleft, giving them a characteristic shape.
