hobbler

noun
someone who walks with a limp
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːblər / (AmE), / ˈhɒblə / (BrE)
a person who walks with a limp or an unsteady, hobbling gait

Examples

old hobbler walking
After his injury, the old man became a familiar hobbler along the village road.
Despite being a hobbler, she refused to let her limp stop her from exploring the city.
The marathon included not just runners but also the occasional hobbler determined to finish.
