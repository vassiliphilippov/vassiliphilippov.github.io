fishplate

noun
metal plate for joining rails or beams
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌpleɪt / (BrE)
a metal plate used to join two rails or beams together at their ends, typically by bolting along the sides

Examples

fishplate joins rails
A missing fishplate can compromise the safety of the entire rail track.
Over time, the bolts on the fishplate may loosen and need tightening.
The repair crew installed a new fishplate to secure the broken rail joint.
