well-fed

adjective
properly nourished; having eaten enough
Pronunciation: / wɛl ˈfɛd / (AmE), / wɛl ˈfɛd / (BrE)
Having eaten enough good food; properly nourished or plump from sufficient or abundant food.

Examples

well-fed child
After several hearty meals, he looked much more well-fed than before.
The well-fed children played happily in the park.
The farmer's cows are always well-fed and healthy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – malnourished: suffering from lack of proper nutrition
  • synonym – plump: having a full, rounded shape, often from being well-fed
  • antonym – undernourished: not receiving enough food for good health
