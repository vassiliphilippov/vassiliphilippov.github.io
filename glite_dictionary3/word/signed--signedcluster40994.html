signed

adjective
bearing a digital signature
Pronunciation: / saɪnd / (AmE), / saɪnd / (BrE)
(of data, software, or an electronic document) bearing a digital (cryptographic) signature that verifies the identity of the signer and ensures the item has not been altered.

Examples

signed document
The firmware update is signed to verify it comes from the publisher.
She refused to install the signed driver until she confirmed the developer's certificate.
The email contained a signed attachment proving the message had not been altered in transit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authenticated: verified as genuine, confirming the identity of the sender or origin.
  • antonym – unsigned: not bearing a digital signature; not verified cryptographically.
