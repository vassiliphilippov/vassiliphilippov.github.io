despondence

noun
state of discouragement or hopelessness
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɑn.dəns / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒn.dəns / (BrE)
A state of being discouraged and without hope; low spirits from loss of confidence or courage.

Examples

deep despondence
A wave of despondence swept over her after receiving the bad news.
Despite his despondence, he managed to find the strength to try again.
Many people struggled with despondence during the long winter months.

Related Terms

  • synonym – despondency: a state of being despondent; depression of spirits
  • antonym – hopefulness: having or showing hope
