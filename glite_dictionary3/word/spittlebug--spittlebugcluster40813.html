spittlebug

noun
froth-producing plant insect
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪt̬.əl.bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈspɪt.əl.bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small leaping herbivorous insect that lives in and secretes a mass of protective froth, often found on plants.

Examples

spittlebug on plants
A single leaf may house several spittlebugs, each hidden in their own bubble of spittle.
Gardeners often notice the frothy homes of the spittlebug on stems and leaves in spring.
The spittlebug covers itself with a foam to protect from predators and drying out.
