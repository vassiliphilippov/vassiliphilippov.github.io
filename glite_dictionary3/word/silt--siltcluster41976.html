to silt

verb
become blocked with fine sediment
Pronunciation: / sɪlt / (AmE), / sɪlt / (BrE)
(of a river, harbor, etc.) to become filled or blocked with silt.

Examples

to silt up a canal
If the canals are not maintained, they may eventually silt and become unusable.
Many small harbors silt after heavy rains, making navigation difficult.
Over the years, the reservoir began to silt, reducing its water storage capacity.
