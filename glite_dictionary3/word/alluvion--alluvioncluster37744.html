alluvion

noun
overflowing of water onto land
Pronunciation: / əˈluːviən / (AmE), / əˈluːvɪən / (BrE)
the overflowing of a body of water onto normally dry land, especially as a natural process

Examples

alluvion floods fields
After the alluvion, the banks were covered with nutrient-rich silt.
Certain legal disputes arose over land created by the gradual alluvion of the river.
The river's alluvion transformed the normally arid fields each spring.

Related Terms

  • antonym – drought: a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall
  • synonym – flood: an overflow of a large amount of water beyond its normal limits
  • synonym – inundation: an overwhelming flood of water
