Picariae

noun
obsolete bird classification group
Pronunciation: / pɪˈkɛəriˌi / (AmE), / pɪˈkɛəriːiː / (BrE)
A former taxonomic grouping used in bird classification, nearly equivalent to the order Coraciiformes, which includes species such as kingfishers and bee-eaters.

Examples

Picariae bird group
Many species now classified under Coraciiformes were formerly placed in the Picariae.
Modern ornithology has largely replaced the use of Picariae with more precise taxonomic terms.
The classification of birds once included the Picariae as a major group.
