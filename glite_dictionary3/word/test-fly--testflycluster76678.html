to test-fly

verb
fly an aircraft to assess performance or safety
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛst flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛst flaɪ / (BrE)
to fly an aircraft in order to evaluate its performance, safety, or systems, often under experimental or new conditions

Examples

to test-fly a new plane
He test-flew several aircraft models during his career as an aviation engineer.
Only experienced pilots are allowed to test-fly experimental jets.
She was chosen to test-fly the new prototype before it went into production.
