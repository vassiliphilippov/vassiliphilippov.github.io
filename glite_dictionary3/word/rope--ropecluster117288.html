rope

noun
thick, strong cord for tying or pulling
Pronunciation: / roʊp / (AmE), / rəʊp / (BrE)
a thick, strong cord made by twisting together strands of fiber, used for tying, pulling, lifting, or securing things

Examples

climbing rope
Climbers must check that the rope is strong enough to support them.
He used a rope to tie the boat to the dock.
The package was secured with a length of rope.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cord: a thin, flexible string or rope made from twisted strands
  • degree lower – string: a thinner length of cord often used for tying or binding
