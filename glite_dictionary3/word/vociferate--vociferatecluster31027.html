to vociferate

verb
to shout or speak very loudly
Pronunciation: / voʊˈsɪfəˌreɪt / (AmE), / vəʊˈsɪfəreɪt / (BrE)
to shout, say, or utter something loudly, especially with strong emotion or protest

Examples

to vociferate a protest
Demonstrators vociferated their demands for justice outside the courthouse.
She vociferated her objections in the middle of the meeting, startling everyone in the room.
The crowd began to vociferate as the speaker addressed the controversial topic.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shout: to speak or call out loudly
  • antonym – whisper: to speak very softly or quietly
  • synonym – yell: to cry out loudly, especially in pain, anger, or excitement
