LCD

noun
liquid crystal display technology
Pronunciation: / ɛl siː diː / (AmE), / ɛl siː diː / (BrE)
a flat display technology that uses liquid crystal cells to control light, commonly used in computer screens, televisions, and digital watches

Examples

LCD screen display
Many older laptops are equipped with LCD displays, which are thinner than traditional CRT monitors.
She prefers phones with an LCD screen because of their longer battery life.
The price of LCD televisions has dropped significantly in the last decade.

Related Terms

  • antonym – LED: a display that uses light-emitting diodes, another flat-panel technology
  • synonym – liquid crystal display: full form of LCD; a screen technology using liquid crystals
