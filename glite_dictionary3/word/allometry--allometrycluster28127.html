allometry

noun
scaling of variables with system size
Pronunciation: / 59c8lm59tri / (AmE), / 59c8lf6atri / (BrE)
(in complex systems and urban studies) the study of how measurable variables (for example, infrastructure, economic output, or network properties) scale with the size of a system, often expressed by power-law relationships.

Examples

allometry in city size
Researchers found that road length scales with population according to urban allometry, following a sublinear power law.
City planners apply allometry to predict infrastructure needs as smaller towns grow into larger metropolitan areas.
Studies of social networks use allometry to describe how communication patterns change with system size.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scaling law: a mathematical relationship showing how one quantity changes with the size of a system
  • synonym – urban scaling: the study of how various urban indicators change systematically with city population size
