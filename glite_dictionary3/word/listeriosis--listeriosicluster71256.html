listeriosis

noun
infectious disease by Listeria bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪstɚiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪstəriˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Listeria monocytogenes, affecting humans and animals, and often serious for newborns, pregnant women, or those with weakened immune systems

Examples

listeriosis infection risk
Outbreaks of listeriosis have been traced back to contaminated dairy products and deli meats.
Pregnant women are advised to avoid certain foods to reduce their risk of contracting listeriosis.
Symptoms of listeriosis can be severe in people with weakened immune systems.
