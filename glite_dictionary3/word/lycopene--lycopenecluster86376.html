lycopene

noun
a red pigment in tomatoes and red fruits
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪkəˌpin / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkəpiːn / (BrE)
a red carotenoid pigment found especially in tomatoes and other red fruits and vegetables, thought to have antioxidant properties

Examples

lycopene in tomatoes
Some studies suggest that lycopene may help reduce the risk of certain cancers.
Tomatoes are an excellent source of lycopene, which gives them their distinctive red color.
Watermelon is another fruit that contains a high amount of lycopene.
