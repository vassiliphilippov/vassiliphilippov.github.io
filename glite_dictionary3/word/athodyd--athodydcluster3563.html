athodyd

noun
type of simple jet engine
Pronunciation: / əˈθoʊdɪd / (AmE), / əˈθɒdɪd / (BrE)
A simple type of jet engine that operates with no moving parts and must be launched at high speed to function.

Examples

athodyd jet engine
An athodyd requires an initial burst of speed to become operational.
Early experiments with the athodyd paved the way for modern ramjet engines.
The missile was powered by an athodyd that could only start after being dropped from an aircraft.
