smooth

adjective
(music) connected, without breaks between notes
Pronunciation: / smuð / (AmE), / smuːð / (BrE)
moving, happening, or performed in an even, graceful, and uninterrupted way; free from sudden changes or problems

Examples

smooth movement
Her violin solo was remarkably smooth, with every note seamlessly connected.
The pianist played the passage in a smooth and flowing manner.
To achieve the desired effect, the conductor asked for a more smooth interpretation of the melody.
She admired the dancer's smooth transitions from one move to the next.
The skater's smooth glide across the ice captivated the audience.
With a smooth stride, the cat leapt gracefully onto the windowsill.
Everything ran smooth during the grand opening event, impressing all the guests.
Her transition into the new company was surprisingly smooth owing to her prior experience.
The negotiations went smooth and concluded within a week.
