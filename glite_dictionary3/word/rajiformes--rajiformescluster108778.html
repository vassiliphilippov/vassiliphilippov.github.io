Rajiformes

noun
order of flattened-bodied cartilaginous fish
Pronunciation: / ˌrædʒəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌreɪdʒɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of cartilaginous fish with flattened bodies, including rays, skates, guitarfishes, and sawfishes

Examples

Rajiformes species diversity
Many marine biologists study Rajiformes to understand the evolution of cartilaginous fishes.
Skates and rays, both members of the Rajiformes, inhabit oceans around the world.
The Rajiformes are distinguished by their flattened bodies and wing-like pectoral fins.
