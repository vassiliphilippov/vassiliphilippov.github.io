barosaurus

noun
a giant, long-necked Jurassic dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˌbæɹoʊˈsɔɹəs / (AmE), / ˌbæɹəʊˈsɔːɹəs / (BrE)
a large, long-necked dinosaur of the late Jurassic period, known for its enormous size and long tail, possibly growing as tall as a five-story building

Examples

giant barosaurus skeleton
A full-grown barosaurus may have weighed as much as 20 tons.
Scientists believe that barosaurus used its long neck to reach treetops for food.
The skeleton of a barosaurus towers over visitors at the natural history museum.
