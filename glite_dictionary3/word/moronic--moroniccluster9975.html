moronic

adjective
very foolish or stupid; historical offensive term
Pronunciation: / məˈrɑnɪk / (AmE), / məˈrɒnɪk / (BrE)
very foolish or stupid; formerly used, now offensive, to refer to a person with a mental age of between eight and twelve years.

Examples

moronic mistake
He made a moronic comment that angered everyone in the room.
It's hard to believe someone could act so moronic in a serious situation.
Their plan was so moronic that it was doomed to fail from the start.
