to storm

verb
to act violently or angrily
Pronunciation: / stɔrm / (AmE), / stɔːm / (BrE)
to behave with sudden and intense force or anger, often moving quickly and aggressively

Examples

to storm out angrily
She stormed out of the meeting after hearing the criticism.
The coach stormed into the locker room and shouted at the players.
When angry, he tends to storm around the house, slamming doors.

  • antonym – calm: to behave in a relaxed or peaceful manner
  • synonym – rage: to behave with intense anger or violence
