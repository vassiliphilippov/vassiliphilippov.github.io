pyrosis

noun
medical term for heartburn
Pronunciation: / paɪˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A painful burning sensation in the chest caused by backflow of stomach acid irritating the esophagus; commonly known as heartburn.

Examples

pyrosis after eating
Certain medications can help relieve the discomfort of pyrosis.
Gastroesophageal reflux disease is a common cause of chronic pyrosis.
The patient complained of frequent pyrosis after eating spicy foods.
