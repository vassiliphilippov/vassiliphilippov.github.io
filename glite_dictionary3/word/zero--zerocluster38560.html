zero

noun
the number 0; additive identity
Pronunciation: / ˈziroʊ /; / ˈzɪəɹoʊ / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
the number 0; the mathematical value that, when added to another number, leaves it unchanged

Examples

zero plus five
Adding zero to any number leaves it unchanged.
In mathematics, zero is known as the additive identity.
The sum of five and zero is still five.
If you add zero to any number, the value stays the same.
In the equation 5 + zero = 5, zero is the additive identity.
The temperature dropped to zero degrees last night.
