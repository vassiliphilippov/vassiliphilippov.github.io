boarder

noun
person lodging and eating in someone's home
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə / (BrE)
a person who pays to live and have meals in someone else's house

Examples

house boarder
After Mary lost her job, she became a boarder in Mrs. White's house to save money.
Each boarder had their own room but ate meals together with the host family.
The family decided to take in a boarder to help cover their bills.
