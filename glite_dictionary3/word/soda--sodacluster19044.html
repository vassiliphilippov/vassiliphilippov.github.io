soda

noun
sodium carbonate, used in industry
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
A sodium salt of carbonic acid, commonly used in the manufacture of soap, glass, and paper; sodium carbonate.

Examples

washing soda
Chemists use soda as a cleaning agent because of its alkalinity.
Soap manufacturers rely on soda to help break down fats during production.
The factory produces large quantities of soda for use in making glass and paper.
