woodworm

noun
larva of a wood-boring insect
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of an insect that feeds on and bores through wood, often causing structural damage to wooden objects or buildings

Examples

woodworm in furniture
He was disappointed to find that woodworm had damaged the old floorboards.
Signs of woodworm include small holes in wooden beams and fine sawdust nearby.
The antique furniture had to be treated after woodworm was discovered in the legs.
