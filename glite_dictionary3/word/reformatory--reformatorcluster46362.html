reformatory

adjective
tending or intended to reform
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔːrməˌtɔːri / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmətəri / (BrE)
Tending or intended to reform, especially by improving character or behavior.

Examples

reformatory measures
Her approach was both punitive and reformatory, focusing on changing behavior.
Some view strict curfews as a reformatory measure for troubled youth.
The prison implemented reformatory policies to encourage inmate rehabilitation.

  • synonym – reformative: having the intention or effect of reforming
