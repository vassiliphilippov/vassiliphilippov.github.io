polydactyly

noun
extra fingers or toes; birth defect
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈdæk.tɪ.li / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdæktɪli / (BrE)
A rare birth defect in which a person has more than the usual number of fingers or toes.

Examples

polydactyly in newborns
During surgery, the doctor explained the options for correcting polydactyly to the child's parents.
Genetic testing can help determine the cause of polydactyly in families with a history of the condition.
The baby was born with polydactyly, having six toes on each foot.
