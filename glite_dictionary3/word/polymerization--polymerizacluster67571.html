polymerization

noun
process of forming a polymer from monomers
Pronunciation: / pəˌlɪmɚɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌlɪməraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which small molecules called monomers are combined to form a polymer or polymeric compound

Examples

polymerization of monomers
Heat is often used to initiate the polymerization process in certain resins.
Scientists studied the effects of catalysts on the rate of polymerization.
The polymerization of ethylene produces polyethylene, a widely used plastic.
