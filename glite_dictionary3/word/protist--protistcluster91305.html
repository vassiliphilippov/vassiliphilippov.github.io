protist

noun
unicellular eukaryotic microorganism
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊ.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tɪst / (BrE)
any member of a diverse group of mostly unicellular eukaryotic organisms that are not animals, plants, or fungi, often living in water or moist environments

Examples

single-celled protist
Amoebas are well-known examples of protists with unique movement abilities.
Many protist species can be found in freshwater ponds and lakes.
Scientists study protists to better understand early eukaryotic evolution.
