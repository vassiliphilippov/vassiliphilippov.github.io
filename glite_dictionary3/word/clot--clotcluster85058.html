to clot

verb
to thicken or solidify (of a liquid)
Pronunciation: / klɑt / (AmE), / klɒt / (BrE)
to thicken or form lumps, especially when a liquid like blood or milk becomes solid or semi-solid

Examples

to clot in blood
If the blood does not clot properly, it can lead to excessive bleeding.
Milk will clot when lemon juice is added to it.
The wound was slow to clot, prolonging the bleeding.
If milk is left out at room temperature, it can clot and spoil.
The rennet caused the cream to clot during the cheese-making process.
Under certain conditions, protein in the liquid will begin to clot quickly.
After the injury, blood began to clot around the wound.
If left to stand, milk will eventually clot and separate.
The solution can clot when exposed to air for too long.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coagulate: to change from a fluid to a thickened mass
  • synonym – curdle: to separate or cause to separate into curds or lumps
  • antonym – dissolve: to become incorporated into a liquid; opposite of coalesce
  • antonym – liquefy: to make or become liquid
