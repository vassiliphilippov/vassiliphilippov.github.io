Italic

noun
Indo-European language branch including Latin
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Latin and related ancient languages such as Oscan and Umbrian

Examples

Italic language group
Latin is the most well-known member of the Italic language group.
Scholars have studied the evolution of Italic languages through inscriptions and historical texts.
The ancient Italic peoples spoke languages now largely extinct.
