to maintain

verb
to assert or affirm firmly
Pronunciation: / meɪnˈteɪn / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn / (BrE)
to state or assert something firmly, especially when others disagree

Examples

to maintain innocence
He maintained that his decision was based on sound principles, not personal feelings.
She maintained that she had never seen him before, despite the evidence presented.
The company maintains that its product is safe to use under all conditions.
He maintained in court that he had never seen the document before.
She maintains her innocence despite the accusations against her.
The company maintained that it had followed all legal procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assert: to state a fact or belief forcefully
  • antonym – deny: to state that something is not true
