monad

noun
indivisible metaphysical entity (philosophy)
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊnæd / (AmE), / ˈməʊnæd / (BrE)
(in philosophy) A singular metaphysical entity, considered indivisible and fundamental, from which all material properties are derived.

Examples

Leibnizian monad
According to Leibniz, every monad reflects the entire universe in its own way.
Some philosophers argue that the soul is a monad, indivisible and eternal.
The concept of the monad has fascinated metaphysicians for centuries.
