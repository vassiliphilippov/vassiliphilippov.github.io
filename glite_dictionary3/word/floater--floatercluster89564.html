floater

noun
insurance policy for movable property
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtɚ / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtə / (BrE)
an insurance policy that covers loss of movable property regardless of its location, such as jewelry or cameras

Examples

floater insurance policy
Many homeowners add a floater for valuable items not covered by standard policies.
She bought a floater to make sure her expensive necklace was insured even while traveling.
The floater provided full coverage for his camera, whether at home or abroad.
