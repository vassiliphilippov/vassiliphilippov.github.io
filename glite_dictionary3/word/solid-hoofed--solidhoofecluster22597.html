solid-hoofed

adjective
having unsplit, solid hooves (of animals)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑlɪd huːft / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd huːft / (BrE)
(of an animal) having hooves that are solid and not split into two parts.

Examples

solid-hoofed animal
Ancient wild horses were among the earliest solid-hoofed creatures domesticated by humans.
Horses are solid-hoofed animals, unlike cows or deer.
The veterinarian explained that the solid-hoofed nature of donkeys makes their hooves more resistant to certain infections.
