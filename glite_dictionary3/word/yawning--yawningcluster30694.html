yawning

noun
involuntary open-mouthed breath, often from tiredness
Pronunciation: / ˈjɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
An involuntary intake of breath with the mouth wide open, typically caused by tiredness, boredom, or drowsiness.

Examples

to stop yawning
His yawning was so contagious that everyone in the room soon started to feel sleepy.
She tried to stifle her yawning during the long lecture, but it was difficult to stay alert.
The teacher noticed the yawning among students after lunch and decided to take a short break.

Related Terms

  • synonym – yawn: an act of opening the mouth wide and inhaling due to tiredness or boredom
