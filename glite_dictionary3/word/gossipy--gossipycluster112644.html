gossipy

adjective
talking eagerly about others' private affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːsəpi / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪpi / (BrE)
Enjoying or characterized by talking about the private affairs of others, especially in a casual or unconstrained way.

Examples

a gossipy friend
Her gossipy nature made her popular at the office but also occasionally caused trouble.
The gossipy conversation at the cafe was filled with rumors and speculation.
They found the magazine too gossipy for their taste and preferred more serious journalism.
