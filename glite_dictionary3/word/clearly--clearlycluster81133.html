clearly

adverb
in an obvious, easy-to-understand way
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrli / (AmE), / ˈklɪəli / (BrE)
in a way that leaves no doubt; obviously or without question

Examples

clearly a mistake

She spoke clearly so that everyone could understand her instructions.
The evidence clearly shows that he was not involved in the crime.
It was clearly a mistake to overlook the details in the report.
She clearly enjoys her work, judging by her enthusiasm every day.
The instructions were clearly explained, so everyone understood what to do.
