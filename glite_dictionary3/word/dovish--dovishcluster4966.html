dovish

adjective
favoring peace; opposed to war
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.vɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.vɪʃ / (BrE)
favoring peaceful policies or opposed to aggression and war, especially in politics or diplomacy

Examples

dovish foreign policy
His dovish stance on foreign policy has attracted support from peace activists.
The senator is considered dovish because of her consistent opposition to military intervention.
While some party members are more dovish, others strongly favor increasing the defense budget.
