to smell

verb
seem to suggest a particular quality
Pronunciation: / smɛl / (AmE), / smɛl / (BrE)
to seem to have a particular quality or to suspect something, especially in a non-literal or intuitive way

Examples

smells of trouble
His explanation smells of dishonesty and makes me doubt his motives.
That apology smells of insincerity to most people.
The proposal smelled of corruption from the very beginning.
I smelled a hint of dishonesty in his response.
She can always smell trouble before it happens.
The journalist smelled corruption as soon as she read the report.

Related Terms

  • antonym – overlook: to fail to notice or detect something
  • synonym – reek: to give off a strong impression of something, usually unpleasant
  • synonym – sense: to become aware of or perceive; to detect something not always tangible
  • synonym – suspect: to believe something is likely or true, especially something negative
