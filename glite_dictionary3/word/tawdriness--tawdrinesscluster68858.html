tawdriness

noun
showy cheapness; gaudiness
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔdɹi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːdɹi.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being cheap and gaudy, especially in a way that is meant to be showy or attractive

Examples

the tawdriness of decorations
Despite its tawdriness, the neon-lit casino attracted crowds looking for excitement.
She was embarrassed by the tawdriness of her flashy new dress when compared to the elegant outfits of the others.
The tawdriness of the decorations made the party feel more like a cheap carnival than a classy event.

Related Terms

  • antonym – elegance: the quality of being stylish and graceful, opposite of tawdriness
  • synonym – gaudiness: flashiness, showy brightness that lacks taste
