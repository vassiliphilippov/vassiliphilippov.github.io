illiberal

adjective
restricting civil liberties (political)
Pronunciation: / 6AC8l6AB5A59l / (AmE), / 6AC8l6AB59r59l / (BrE)
Relating to a political system that holds elections or democratic forms but restricts civil liberties, independent media, or political opposition.

Examples

illiberal democracy
The government has become increasingly illiberal, restricting protests and controlling the media.
Observers warned that the new laws signalled an illiberal turn in the country's politics.
Many voters accepted the illiberal measures because they promised security over civil liberties.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authoritarian: a political system with strong central power and limited political freedoms; often similar in practice.
  • antonym – liberal democracy: a democratic system that protects civil liberties, rule of law, and pluralism; often treated as the opposite of an illiberal system.
