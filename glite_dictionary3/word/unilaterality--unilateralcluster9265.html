unilaterality

noun
one-sidedness; unilateral quality
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː.nəˌlæt.əˈræl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˌlæt.əˈræl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unilateral or one-sided.

Examples

unilaterality of a decision
Many critics pointed out the unilaterality of the government's new policy.
The contract was criticized for its unilaterality and lack of mutual benefit.
We need to overcome the unilaterality in our discussions to achieve a fair agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bilaterality: the state or quality of being bilateral; having two sides
  • synonym – unilateral: involving only one side or party
