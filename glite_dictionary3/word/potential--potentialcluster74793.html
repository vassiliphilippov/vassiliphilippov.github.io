potential

noun
difference in electric charge (volts)
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
the difference in electric charge between two points in a circuit, measured in volts

Examples

electric potential difference
A battery provides a potential that drives current through the circuit.
If there is no potential difference, no current will flow.
The potential across the resistor was measured to be five volts.
