sharp

adjective
high-pitched or piercing (of a sound)
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrp / (AmE), / ʃɑːp / (BrE)
(of a sound) having or emitting a high-pitched, piercing, or shrill tone.

Examples

sharp whistle sound
A sharp whistle pierced the morning silence.
Her voice turned sharp when she shouted across the playground.
The sharp cry of the baby startled everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mellow: (of a sound) pleasantly smooth or soft; not harsh or sharp
  • synonym – piercing: (of a sound) very high and loud, making you feel uncomfortable
  • synonym – shrill: high-pitched and piercing in sound
