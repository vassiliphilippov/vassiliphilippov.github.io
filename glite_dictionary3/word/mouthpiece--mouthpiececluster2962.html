mouthpiece

noun
spokesperson or representative
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊθˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (BrE)
a person, group, or publication that speaks on behalf of another person or group, especially as a representative or spokesperson.

Examples

mouthpiece for the government
She refused to be used as a mouthpiece for management's unpopular decisions.
Some papers have become a political party's mouthpiece rather than independent voices.
The company's lawyer acted as their mouthpiece during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spokesperson: a person who speaks on behalf of another or for a group
