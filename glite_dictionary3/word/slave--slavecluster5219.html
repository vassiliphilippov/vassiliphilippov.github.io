slave

noun
person dominated by influence or person
Pronunciation: / sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
a person who is strongly controlled by something or someone, or who works extremely hard (used figuratively)

Examples

slave to work
He became a slave to his work, neglecting everything else in his life.
She realized she was a slave to her own perfectionism.
Some people are slaves to technology, unable to put their phones down.
Ever since the new manager started, I've been a slave to my desk, working late every night.
He became a slave to his job, rarely taking time off for himself.
Those students are slaves to their studies during the exam period.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drudge: a person who does hard, menial, or tedious work
  • antonym – master: a person with control or power over others; the opposite of being dominated
  • degree lower – servant: a person employed to perform domestic duties for others
  • synonym – toiler: a person who works very hard, especially at dull or difficult tasks
