ill-tempered

adjective
easily annoyed; irritable
Pronunciation: / ɪl ˈtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ɪl ˈtɛmpəd / (BrE)
Easily annoyed or made angry; irritable or grumpy.

Examples

an ill-tempered person
After a long, stressful day, he became quite ill-tempered and impatient.
Children tend to avoid the ill-tempered neighbor who yells at anyone passing his house.
The ill-tempered manager snapped at his staff over minor mistakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – good-tempered: having a cheerful or agreeable disposition
  • synonym – irritable: easily annoyed or angered
