Cox-1

noun
enzyme regulating prostaglandins in body
Pronunciation: / kɑks wʌn / (AmE), / kɒks wʌn / (BrE)
an enzyme, also known as cyclooxygenase-1, that helps regulate the production of prostaglandins, particularly those that protect the stomach lining and support kidney function

Examples

Cox-1 enzyme activity
Doctors often consider the effects on Cox-1 when prescribing NSAIDs to patients.
Many common painkillers inhibit Cox-1 as well as Cox-2.
Research has shown that Cox-1 plays a key role in protecting stomach tissue.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cyclooxygenase-2: related enzyme involved in prostaglandin production, often targeted by different drugs
  • synonym – prostaglandin: group of compounds involved in inflammation and other processes, regulated by Cox-1
