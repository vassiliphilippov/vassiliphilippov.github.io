marmite

noun
a polarizing thing
Pronunciation: / c8m51rccma6at / (AmE), / c8m51d0ma6at / (BrE)
Something, especially a cultural product or idea, that causes strongly divided opinions; a love-it-or-hate-it thing.

Examples

marmite effect
The new art-house film was a marmite that critics either loved or despised.
Her proposal became a workplace marmite, splitting the team into supporters and critics.
Some see the gadget as essential, while others call it a marmite that wastes money.
