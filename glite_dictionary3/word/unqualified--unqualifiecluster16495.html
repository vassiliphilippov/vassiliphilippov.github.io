unqualified

adjective
lacking necessary qualifications or training
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkwɑlɪfaɪd /; / ʌnˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not having the necessary qualifications, skills, or authority for a job, activity, or position

Examples

unqualified worker
Allowing an unqualified worker to operate machinery can be dangerous.
Because he was unqualified for the position, the company decided not to hire him.
She felt unqualified to give advice on the complicated legal issue.
As the candidate lacked the required certification, he was deemed unqualified for the legal proceedings.
Because she was still a minor, she was unqualified to sign the contract.
The evidence was dismissed because the witness was unqualified to testify in court.

  • antonym – qualified: having the necessary qualifications or training
