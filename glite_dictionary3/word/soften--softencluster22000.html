to soften

verb
become more yielding or flexible
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔf.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɒf.ən / (BrE)
to become more willing to change one's opinion or attitude as a result of influence or pressure; to yield or relent

Examples

to soften your opinion
After hours of negotiation, the manager finally began to soften his position on the contract terms.
Public opposition to the new law appeared to soften following the recent government announcements.
She refused at first, but her heart started to soften after hearing his sincere apology.

Related Terms

  • antonym – harden: to become less sympathetic or more resistant to influence
  • synonym – yield: to give way under pressure or influence
