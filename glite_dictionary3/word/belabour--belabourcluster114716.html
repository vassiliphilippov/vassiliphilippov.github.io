to belabour

verb
to beat soundly or repeatedly
Pronunciation: / bɪˈleɪbər / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪbə / (BrE)
To beat someone or something forcefully and repeatedly.

Examples

to belabour an enemy
Angry at the thief, the crowd proceeded to belabour him before the police arrived.
In the old tales, warriors would belabour their enemies in fierce combat.
The villain began to belabour his helpless victim with a stick.
