diaglyph

noun
sunken or depressed carving or engraving
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəɡlɪf / (BrE)
a carving or engraving in which the design is sunken below the surface of the stone or gem, as opposed to a raised relief (cameo)

Examples

diaglyph carving technique
Ancient artists used the diaglyph technique to depict scenes by engraving them below the surface of gemstones.
The museum’s collection includes a rare Roman diaglyph displaying intricate mythological figures.
Unlike a cameo, a diaglyph features a recessed image carved into the stone.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cameo: a gem or stone carving where the design is in raised relief
  • synonym – intaglio: engraving technique with an incised or sunken design
