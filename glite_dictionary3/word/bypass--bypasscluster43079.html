bypass

noun
surgically created shunt
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.pɑːs / (BrE)
a passage created surgically to divert the flow of blood or other bodily fluids around a blocked or damaged section

Examples

bypass surgery
A coronary artery bypass can significantly reduce the risk of heart attacks.
Doctors recommended a gastric bypass procedure to help her lose weight.
The patient underwent a bypass to restore blood flow to his heart.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shunt: a tube or passage that diverts fluid from one path to another
