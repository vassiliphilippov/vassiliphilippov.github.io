to suspect

verb
believe someone is guilty (verb)
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to believe that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing, or is untrustworthy, without certain proof

Examples

to suspect a criminal
Investigators continue suspecting foul play in the mysterious disappearance.
Many people suspected the manager but had no concrete evidence.
The police suspect him of involvement in the robbery.
After her keys went missing, Jane started to suspect her roommate.
I can't help but suspect that she knows more than she's telling us.
The police began to suspect his story after finding inconsistencies in his account.

  • synonym – doubt: to feel uncertain about something
