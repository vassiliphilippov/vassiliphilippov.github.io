to virilise

verb
cause to develop male characteristics
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪrɪlʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a hormone or drug) to cause someone, especially a female, to develop male physical characteristics.

Examples

to virilise a woman
Cushing's syndrome sometimes virilises children, leading to unusual hair growth and a deeper voice.
Excessive androgen hormones may virilise female patients with certain medical conditions.
Hormone therapy can virilise individuals undergoing gender transition to male.

Related Terms

  • synonym – masculinize: to make or become more masculine in characteristics
