to develop

verb
move a chess piece to an active position
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɛləp / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləp / (BrE)
(in chess) to move a piece to a more active or strategic position, especially in the opening

Examples

to develop chess pieces
Beginners are taught to develop their pieces early in the game.
In this opening, White should develop the knights before the bishops.
She quickly developed her rook to control the open file.
A strong player will develop their knights and bishops early in the game.
If you fail to develop quickly, your opponent may gain a significant advantage.
It is important to develop your pieces rather than leaving them on their original squares.
