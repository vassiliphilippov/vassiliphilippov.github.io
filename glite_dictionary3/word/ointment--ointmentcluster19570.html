ointment

noun
medicated or soothing skin preparation
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔɪnt.mənt /; / ˈɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a smooth, thick or oily substance that is rubbed on the skin to heal, soothe, or moisturize, often containing medicine

Examples

ointment for wounds
After the insect bite, he gently rubbed some ointment on the irritated area.
She uses a soothing ointment to treat her dry skin during winter.
The doctor recommended applying the ointment twice a day to help heal the cut.
She applied the ointment to her ankle to reduce the swelling.
The doctor prescribed an antibiotic ointment for the wound.
Using a soothing ointment helped relieve the itching and redness.
