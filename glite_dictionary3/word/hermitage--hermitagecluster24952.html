hermitage

noun
dwelling of a hermit
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜrmɪtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːmɪtɪdʒ / (BrE)
A place where a hermit lives, especially a secluded dwelling for religious or contemplative solitude.

Examples

remote hermitage
A weathered path led to a stone hermitage perched atop the hill.
The old monk found peace in his remote hermitage deep in the forest.
Throughout history, many spiritual figures have sought solitude in a hermitage.
