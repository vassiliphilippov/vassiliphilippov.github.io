antique

adjective
old-fashioned or out of fashion
Pronunciation: / ænˈtik / (AmE), / ænˈtiːk / (BrE)
(of style, appearance, or ideas) old-fashioned or out of fashion; not in contemporary use.

Examples

antique style clothes
His views on technology are decidedly antique and not suited to our modern world.
She wore a dress with an antique style that stood out at the formal event.
The furniture in the lobby has a rather antique appearance, reminiscent of the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modern: relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past
  • synonym – outmoded: no longer fashionable or useful
