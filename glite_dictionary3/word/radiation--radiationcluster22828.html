radiation

noun
biological spread into new habitats
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
in biology, the process by which a group of organisms diversifies and spreads into new habitats, often resulting in the formation of new species.

Examples

evolutionary radiation of species
Darwin's finches are a classic example of evolutionary radiation on the Galápagos Islands.
Island environments often encourage the radiation of animals into unique ecological niches.
The radiation of mammals after the extinction of dinosaurs led to many new species.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adaptive radiation: the rapid evolution of diversely adapted species from a common ancestor
