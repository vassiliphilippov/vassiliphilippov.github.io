to fossilise

verb
become mentally inflexible
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːsəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɒsɪlaɪz / (BrE)
(figurative) To become mentally rigid or set in one's ways, unwilling to adapt or change.

Examples

to fossilise old ideas
His attitudes have fossilised since his youth, making him indifferent to change.
If you never try new things, your mind can fossilise over time.
Some institutions tend to fossilise and resist innovative ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ossify: to become rigid or fixed, especially in habits or opinions
