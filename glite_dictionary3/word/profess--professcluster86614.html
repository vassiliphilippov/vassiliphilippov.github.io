to profess

verb
claim expertise or teaching role
Pronunciation: / prəˈfɛs / (AmE), / prəˈfɛs / (BrE)
to claim to be an expert in or teacher of a particular subject or profession, especially publicly

Examples

to profess a subject
Dr. Lee professes physics and enjoys mentoring students.
He professes to be an expert in Renaissance art.
She professes organic chemistry at the university.
