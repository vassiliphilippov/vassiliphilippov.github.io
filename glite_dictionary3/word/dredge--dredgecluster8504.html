dredge

noun
machine for removing material from riverbeds or channels
Pronunciation: / drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
a large machine or vessel equipped with a power shovel used to remove sand, mud, or other material from the bottom of rivers, harbours, or other bodies of water.

Examples

a dredge machine
A massive dredge slowly scooped sand from the bottom of the river to keep shipping lanes deep.
The crew used a dredge to clear silt from the harbor entrance after the storm.
When the channel began to fill with debris, engineers brought in a dredge to restore navigation.
