deflation

noun
reduction of token supply (cryptocurrency)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and tokenomics) a mechanism or state in which the total supply of a digital token decreases over time, typically because tokens are permanently removed or 'burned', creating greater scarcity.

Examples

token deflation event
The project's constant token buybacks produced a steady deflation that increased scarcity and investor interest.
After the smart contract burned millions of coins, the community welcomed the resulting deflation.
Some economists warn that token deflation can lead to hoarding and reduced network activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inflation: an increase in token supply or in general price levels; opposite concept to token deflation
  • synonym – token burn: a process that permanently removes tokens from circulation to reduce supply
