pitch

noun
dark, sticky substance from tar
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
a dark, sticky, thick substance obtained as a residue from distilling tar, used for waterproofing and paving

Examples

black pitch tar
Ships in ancient times were often coated with pitch to prevent leaks.
The smell of pitch lingered in the air long after the road was paved.
The workers spread hot pitch on the roof to make it waterproof.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tar: a dark, thick, flammable liquid obtained from organic substances, often used similarly
