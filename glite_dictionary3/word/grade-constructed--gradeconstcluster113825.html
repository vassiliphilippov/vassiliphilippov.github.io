grade-constructed

adjective
constructed at ground level
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪd kənˈstrʌktɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd kənˈstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
(of a road or structure) constructed at ground level, rather than elevated or sunken.

Examples

grade-constructed road
A grade-constructed crossing can sometimes present safety concerns for vehicles and pedestrians.
The city is planning a grade-constructed bypass to minimize construction costs.
Unlike overpasses, grade-constructed roads do not require expensive supporting structures.
