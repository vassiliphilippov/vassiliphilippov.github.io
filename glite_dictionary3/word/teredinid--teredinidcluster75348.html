teredinid

noun
wood-boring marine bivalve mollusc
Pronunciation: / təˈrɛdəˌnɪd / (AmE), / təˈrɛdɪnɪd / (BrE)
A wormlike marine bivalve mollusc that bores into wood, such as piers and ships, using shell-adapted boring mechanisms; shipworm.

Examples

teredinid damages wood
Scientists study teredinids to understand how they degrade submerged wood.

The wooden hull of the ship was destroyed by teredinids over time.
