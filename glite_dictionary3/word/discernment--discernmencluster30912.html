discernment

noun
ability to perceive hidden things
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɜrn.mənt /; / dɪˈsɝnmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːn.mənt /; / dɪˈsɜːnmənt / (BrE)
the ability to notice, understand, and judge things well, especially when it is not obvious or easy to do so

Examples

keen discernment
A critic needs great discernment to appreciate truly original works of art.
Her discernment allowed her to spot the subtle flaws in the plan that others missed.
Through careful discernment, the investigator uncovered evidence that had gone unnoticed.
Good leaders rely on discernment when making important decisions for their team.
Her discernment in choosing trustworthy friends set her apart from her peers.
I admire his discernment in separating facts from opinions.
A person with discernment can usually sense when something is wrong.
She handled the delicate situation with great discernment and tact.
The critic’s discernment was evident in his thoughtful review of the play.
