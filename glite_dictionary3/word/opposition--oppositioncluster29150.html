opposition

noun
main political party not in power
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the principal political party or group opposing the party in power, especially in a legislature, and prepared to assume office if elected

Examples

the opposition party
After the election, the leader of the opposition promised to continue holding the government accountable.
In the parliamentary system, the opposition plays a vital role in scrutinizing legislation.
The opposition criticized the new policy introduced by the ruling party.

Related Terms

  • antonym – government: the party currently holding political office
