infantry

noun
foot soldiers; army branch fighting on foot
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.fən.tri / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fən.tri / (BrE)
soldiers or a branch of an army trained and equipped to fight on foot, rather than on horseback or in vehicles.

Examples

infantry soldiers
During the parade, the infantry marched in perfect formation ahead of the tanks.
Modern infantry units are equipped with advanced rifles and body armor.
The infantry advanced steadily across the battlefield despite heavy gunfire.
