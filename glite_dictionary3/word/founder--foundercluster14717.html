to founder

verb
to stumble and nearly fall (verb)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to stumble and nearly fall, often collapsing as a result of exhaustion, injury, or weakness.

Examples

runner founders from exhaustion
After hours of walking through the snow, the team began to founder from fatigue.
He watched as the runner started to founder in the final meters of the race.
The exhausted horse began to founder on the muddy track.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stumble: to trip or momentarily lose balance while walking or running
