acarid

noun
tiny mite-like arachnid
Pronunciation: / ˈækəˌrɪd / (AmE), / ˈakərɪd / (BrE)
A very small arachnid, often parasitic on animals or plants, related to ticks and including mites.

Examples

acarid infestation
Farmers often struggle to control acarid infestations in their crops.
Some species of acarid can cause allergic reactions in humans.
The microscope revealed the tiny acarid responsible for the outbreak.
