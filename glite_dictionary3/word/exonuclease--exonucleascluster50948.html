exonuclease

noun
enzyme removing nucleotides from nucleic acids
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.soʊˈnuː.kli.eɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊˈnjuː.kli.eɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that removes nucleotides one by one from the end of a nucleic acid chain

Examples

exonuclease activity in DNA
Researchers discovered a new exonuclease that plays a role in genetic recombination.
