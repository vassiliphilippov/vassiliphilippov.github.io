cleared

adjective
free from objects or obstructions
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrd / (AmE), / ˈklɪəd / (BrE)
(of an area or space) having been made free from objects, obstructions, or unwanted material, especially vegetation, debris, or fixtures.

Examples

cleared land
After the storm, most streets were still blocked, but a few had already been cleared.
Farmers used the cleared field to plant their crops early in the season.
The rescue team quickly made their way along the cleared path.

Related Terms

  • antonym – uncleared: not free from objects or obstructions
