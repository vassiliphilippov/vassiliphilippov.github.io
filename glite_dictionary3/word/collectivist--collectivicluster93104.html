collectivist

adjective
supporting collective or communal ownership
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪvɪst / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by the principle or practice of collective ownership, especially in regard to property or means of production.

Examples

collectivist society
Some critics argue that collectivist policies can stifle personal initiative.
The new law had a distinctly collectivist approach to land distribution.
Their society was governed by collectivist ideals, prioritizing the needs of the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – communal: shared by all members of a community
  • antonym – individualist: favoring individual rather than collective action or ownership
