scale

noun
ratio of representation to actual size
Pronunciation: / skeɪl / (AmE), / skeɪl / (BrE)
The ratio between the actual size of something and its representation, especially on maps or in models.

Examples

scale on map
If the scale is inaccurate, the model will not match the real object.
The architect built the house at a scale of one inch to every ten feet.
This map uses a scale of 1:100,000 to represent the landscape.

Related Terms

  • synonym – proportion: the relationship in size or amount between things
