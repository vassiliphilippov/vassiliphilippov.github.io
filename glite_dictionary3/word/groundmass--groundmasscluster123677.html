groundmass

noun
matrix of fine crystals in igneous rock
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌmæs / (BrE)
(Geology) The compact, fine-grained crystalline material within which larger crystals are embedded in an igneous rock.

Porphyritic rocks typically show visible crystals embedded in a much finer groundmass.
