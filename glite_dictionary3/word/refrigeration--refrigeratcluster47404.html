refrigeration

noun
process of cooling for preservation
Pronunciation: / rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of cooling or freezing something, especially food, to preserve it

Examples

refrigeration of food
Advances in refrigeration have made it possible to safely store vaccines at low temperatures.
After the power outage, failure of refrigeration led to spoilage of the meat and dairy products.
Modern supermarkets rely on refrigeration to keep perishable foods fresh for longer periods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – freezing: the process of making something very cold so that it becomes solid
  • antonym – heating: the process of making something hot or warmer
