gruff

adjective
deep and harsh-sounding (voice or sound)
Pronunciation: / ɡrʌf / (AmE), / ɡrʌf / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) low and rough, often sounding unfriendly, harsh, or as if produced by illness or emotion.

Examples

gruff voice
After shouting all day, her speech became gruff and raspy.
The dog's gruff barking startled everyone in the house.
The old man greeted us with a gruff voice, making him seem intimidating at first.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hoarse: rough and harsh in sound, usually due to illness or overuse
  • antonym – soft: gentle and not harsh in sound
