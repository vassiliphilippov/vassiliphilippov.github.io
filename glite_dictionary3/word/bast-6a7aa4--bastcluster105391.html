bast

noun
plant tissue for food transport
Pronunciation: / bæst / (AmE), / bɑːst / (BrE)
(Botany) Plant tissue that transports synthesized food from the leaves to other parts of the plant, consisting mainly of sieve tubes.

Examples

bast tissue transport
Damage to the bast can severely affect a plant's ability to distribute food produced by photosynthesis.
Scientists studied the structure of bast to understand how plants move carbohydrates.
The bast of the tree plays a crucial role in transporting nutrients throughout the plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym – phloem: vascular tissue in plants that transports sugars and nutrients
