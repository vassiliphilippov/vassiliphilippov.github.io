Word

noun
the divine Word; Jesus Christ in Christian theology
Pronunciation: / wɜrd / (AmE), / wɜːd / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the divine Word of God; Jesus Christ, regarded as the second person of the Trinity and the incarnation of God's word.

Examples

the Word made flesh
In Christian doctrine, the Word was made flesh and lived among us.
Many theologians have debated the meaning of the term 'Word' in the context of the Trinity.
The Gospel of John refers to Christ as the Word who existed from the beginning.
