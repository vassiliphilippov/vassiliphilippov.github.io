condenser

noun
device that converts vapor to liquid
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛnsɚ /; / kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a device that cools vapor and turns it into liquid, used in machines like air conditioners or steam turbines

Examples

condenser in air conditioner
After repairing the condenser, the air conditioning unit worked efficiently again.
Most distillation setups require a condenser to recover the purified liquid.
The condenser in the laboratory cooled the steam into water for collection.
After the steam passes through the turbine, a condenser turns it back into water.
The air conditioning unit uses a condenser to cool and liquefy the refrigerant gas.
They installed a new condenser in the laboratory's distillation apparatus.
