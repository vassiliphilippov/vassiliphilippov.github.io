bellyband

noun
cloth band worn around the waist
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛliˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌbænd / (BrE)
a cloth band or wrap worn around the waist or abdomen by people, especially for infants or during pregnancy, to support or protect the belly

Examples

bellyband for babies
Many traditional cultures use a bellyband for infants during the first few weeks of life.
She bought a soft cotton bellyband for her daughter after the baby's birth.
The nurse wrapped a bellyband around the newborn baby to protect his navel.
She wore a maternity bellyband to support her growing belly during late pregnancy.
After the birth, the nurse recommended a soft postpartum bellyband to ease abdominal discomfort.
Many runners use a compression bellyband during exercise to support core muscles after surgery.
