spirant

noun
a type of fricative consonant
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈspaɪrənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by breath forcing its way through a narrow opening in the vocal tract; a fricative

Examples

spirant consonant example
Linguists classify both 's' and 'z' as spirant consonants.
Some languages have more spirant sounds than others, making pronunciation challenging for learners.
The sound of 'f' in 'fun' is a classic example of a spirant.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fricative: a consonant produced by forcing air through a narrow passage (such as 'f' or 's')
