Georgy Zhukov

proper noun
Soviet WWII general and military leader
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛr.dʒi ˈʒuː.kɑf / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.dʒi ˈʒuː.kɒf / (BrE)
(1896–1974) Soviet general and military leader during World War II, known for directing major counteroffensives including at Stalingrad and the capture of Berlin.

Examples

Georgy Zhukov commanded armies
Georgy Zhukov played a pivotal role in the defeat of Nazi Germany during World War II.
Many historians regard Georgy Zhukov as one of the most talented military strategists of the 20th century.
