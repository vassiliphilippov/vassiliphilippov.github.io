ohm

noun
SI unit of electrical resistance
Pronunciation: / oʊm / (AmE), / əʊm / (BrE)
the SI unit of electrical resistance, equal to the resistance between two points of a conductor when a potential difference of one volt produces a current of one ampere

Examples

one ohm resistance
The circuit measured a resistance of 47 ohms, which was well within the required range.
The multimeter displayed zero ohms, indicating a short circuit.
You should use a resistor with a higher ohm value to reduce current flow.
