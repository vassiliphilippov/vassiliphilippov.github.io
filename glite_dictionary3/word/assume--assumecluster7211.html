to assume

verb
seize control or rights without authority
Pronunciation: / əˈsum / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to take control of, take on, or accept a role, duty, responsibility, or obligation

Examples

to assume responsibility
After the CEO resigned, the vice president quickly assumed control of the company.
He tried to assume authority over the board, even though he wasn't elected.
The colonists assumed the right to govern themselves, defying the king's orders.
After the mayor resigned, she agreed to assume the office temporarily.
He was ready to assume the role of team leader after the promotion.
The new CEO will assume her responsibilities on Monday.
If you assume the loan, you must also make all future payments.
She agreed to assume responsibility for her brother's medical bills.
The new company will assume all outstanding debts after the merger.
