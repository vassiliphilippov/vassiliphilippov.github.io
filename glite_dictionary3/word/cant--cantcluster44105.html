cant

noun
specialized jargon of a group
Pronunciation: / kænt / (AmE), / kænt / (BrE)
the specialized jargon or language characteristic of a particular group, especially one used to exclude or mislead others.

Examples

cant of thieves
Much of the prisoners' cant was confusing to outsiders.
Some historical documents are written in the cant of secret societies.
The two thieves communicated in cant so the police would not understand them.
