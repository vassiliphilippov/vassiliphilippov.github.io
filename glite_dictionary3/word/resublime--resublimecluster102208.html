to resublime

verb
to sublime again (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˌriːsəˈblaɪm / (AmE), / ˌriːsəˈblaɪm / (BrE)
to cause to sublimate again, especially to change a solid directly into a gas (or vice versa) a second or further time

Examples

to resublime a solid
After cooling, the substance was heated again to resublime it and remove any remaining impurities.
If the crystals are not pure enough, simply resublime them as needed.
The experiment required us to resublime the compound to ensure its purity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sublime: to change a solid directly into a gas without becoming liquid
