alkalinity

noun
quality of being alkaline; pH above 7
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.kəˈlɪn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈlɪn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a pH above 7; the capacity of a solution to neutralize acids.

Examples

high alkalinity water
Limestone contributes to the natural alkalinity found in groundwater.
Soil alkalinity can affect the growth of certain types of plants.
The alkalinity of the water was measured to ensure it was safe for fish.
