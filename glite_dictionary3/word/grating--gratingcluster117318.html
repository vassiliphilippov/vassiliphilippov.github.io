grating

noun
optical device dispersing light into a spectrum
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an optical device with many parallel grooves that disperses light or other electromagnetic radiation into its component wavelengths, producing a spectrum

Examples

optical grating device
By rotating the grating, you can analyze various wavelengths of emitted light.
Modern spectrometers often rely on a grating to disperse incoming electromagnetic radiation.
The scientist used a grating to separate the different colors of the laser beam.

Related Terms

  • synonym – diffraction grating: an optical device made of a surface with closely spaced lines used to separate light into a spectrum
