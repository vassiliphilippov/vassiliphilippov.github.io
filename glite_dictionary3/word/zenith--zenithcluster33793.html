zenith

noun
point in the sky directly overhead
Pronunciation: / ˈziːnɪθ / (AmE), / ˈziːnɪθ / (BrE)
the point in the sky or celestial sphere directly above an observer, opposite the nadir

Examples

zenith in the sky
Ancient cultures used the zenith point to mark important times of the year.
Astronomers often measure the angle between a star and the zenith to determine its position.
At noon, the sun reached its zenith, directly overhead in the sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nadir: the point on the celestial sphere directly below the observer, opposite the zenith
