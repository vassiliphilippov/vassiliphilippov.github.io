to lounge

verb
sit or recline comfortably
Pronunciation: / laʊndʒ / (AmE), / laʊndʒ / (BrE)
to sit, lie, or spend time in a place in a relaxed or lazy way

Examples

to lounge on a sofa
After a long day, she liked to lounge on the balcony and read a book.
He lounged in his favorite chair, completely at ease.
We spent the afternoon lounging by the pool, enjoying the sunshine.
It's not allowed to lounge in the hotel lobby unless you are a guest.
Several people were lounging near the entrance, waiting for their friends to arrive.
The teenagers would often lounge around the shopping mall after school.

Related Terms

  • synonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary, typically because of a reluctance to leave
  • synonym – loiter: to stand or wait around idly without apparent purpose
  • synonym – recline: to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
  • synonym – sprawl: to sit or lie with arms and legs spread out in a relaxed way
