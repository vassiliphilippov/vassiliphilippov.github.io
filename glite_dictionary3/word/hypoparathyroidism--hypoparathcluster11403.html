hypoparathyroidism

noun
medical condition of low parathyroid hormone
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˌpær.əˈθaɪ.rɔɪd.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəʊ.pær.əˈθaɪ.rɔɪd.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the parathyroid glands secrete insufficient parathyroid hormone, resulting in abnormally low calcium levels in the blood.

Examples

hypoparathyroidism affects calcium
After surgery, the patient developed hypoparathyroidism and required calcium supplements.
Chronic hypoparathyroidism can lead to muscle cramps and tingling sensations.
Doctors diagnosed him with hypoparathyroidism following his complaints of persistent fatigue and weakness.
