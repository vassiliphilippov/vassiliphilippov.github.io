retreat

noun
act of withdrawing or moving back
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtrit / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːt / (BrE)
the act of moving back or withdrawing, especially to avoid danger or in a military context

Examples

a quick retreat
After her embarrassing mistake, she beat a quiet retreat from the stage.
The hikers planned their retreat once they noticed the storm approaching.
The soldiers made a hasty retreat when the enemy launched a counterattack.
At the sound of the retreat, the soldiers began to fall back from the front lines.
During the exercise, a simulated retreat was announced to practice coordinated pullbacks.
The bugler played the retreat, signaling a tactical withdrawal from the enemy's position.
After suffering heavy losses, the army had no choice but to retreat to safer ground.
The retreat was organized quickly to prevent total defeat.
The general ordered a retreat when the enemy forces proved overwhelming.
