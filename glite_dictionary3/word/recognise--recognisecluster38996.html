to recognise

verb
accept authority or validity
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪz /; / ˈrɛkəgˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛkəɡnʌɪz /; / ˈrɛkəɡnaɪz / (BrE)
to officially accept, approve, or acknowledge the status, authority, or credentials of someone or something

Examples

recognise a new country
He refused to recognise their right to rule over the land.
Most countries do not recognise the new government as legitimate.
The international community finally recognised her as the nation's true leader.
Only officially accredited bodies can recognise new professional qualifications in this field.
The council voted to recognise the organization as an official partner.
The university refused to recognise the foreign diploma as equivalent to a local degree.
