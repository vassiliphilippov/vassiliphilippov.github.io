federalism

noun
system of government with shared state and central powers
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a system of government in which several states or regions retain certain powers but are united by a central authority as a federation

Examples

American federalism debate
Debates about federalism have shaped many countries' constitutions.
Some argue that federalism allows local governments to better address regional concerns.
The United States is often cited as an example of federalism in practice.
