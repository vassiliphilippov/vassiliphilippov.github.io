wit

noun
keen intelligence; mental sharpness
Pronunciation: / wɪt / (AmE), / wɪt / (BrE)
the ability to think quickly and make clever or amusing remarks; mental sharpness and humor

Examples

quick wit
Her wit allowed her to solve the problem in seconds.
His quick wit made him popular among his classmates.
You need wit as well as courage to survive in the city.
He was admired for his quick wit and clever jokes.
Her wit delighted everyone at the dinner party.
The comedian’s sharp wit kept the audience laughing throughout the show.

  • synonym – humor: the quality of being amusing or entertaining
  • synonym – intelligence: the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
