isogamy

noun
fusion of similar gametes (biology)
Pronunciation: / aɪˈsɑɡəmi / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒɡəmi / (BrE)
(biology) reproduction by the fusion of gametes that are similar in size and structure.

Examples

isogamy in algae
Isogamy was observed as a primitive condition in the evolution of sexual reproduction.
Many algae species reproduce through isogamy, where gametes are identical in form.
The study compared isogamy and anisogamy in different protist groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anisogamy: reproduction by the fusion of dissimilar gametes
