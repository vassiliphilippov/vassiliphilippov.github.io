flood

noun
the act of flooding or overflowing
Pronunciation: / flʌd / (AmE), / flʌd / (BrE)
a large amount of water (or another substance) that covers land or a place, often causing damage; also, the act or period of such flooding

Examples

a flood in the street
A broken water main caused a flood in the basement.

The sudden flood destroyed the crops and forced villagers to evacuate.
After heavy rains, the town was left underwater due to a sudden flood.
Every spring, the plains are rejuvenated by the annual flood.
The river burst its banks, causing a severe flood that damaged many homes.
At flood, the riverbanks were completely underwater, making passage impossible.
Taken at the flood, their opportunity brought great success.
The sailors waited until the flood before attempting to enter the harbor.
