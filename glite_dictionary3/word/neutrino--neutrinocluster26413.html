neutrino

noun
elementary neutral particle
Pronunciation: / nuˈtrinoʊ / (AmE), / njuːˈtriːnəʊ / (BrE)
A subatomic particle with no electric charge and extremely small mass, which rarely interacts with other matter.

Examples

a neutrino particle
Detecting a single neutrino among billions of other particles is an extraordinary challenge.
Scientists confirmed that the neutrino has a tiny but non-zero mass.
The sun produces countless neutrinos that pass through the Earth every second.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electron: a stable subatomic particle with a negative electric charge
  • synonym – neutron: a subatomic particle with no net electric charge, found in atomic nuclei
