vedalia

noun
pest-controlling ladybird beetle
Pronunciation: / vəˈdeɪliə / (AmE), / vəˈdeɪliə / (BrE)
A small ladybird beetle (Rodolia cardinalis), native to Australia, introduced to other regions to control scale insects.

Examples

vedalia beetle
After introducing the vedalia to the new environment, the scale insect population dropped significantly.
Farmers rely on the vedalia for natural pest control, reducing the need for chemical pesticides.
The vedalia beetle was released to combat the infestation of scale insects in citrus orchards.
