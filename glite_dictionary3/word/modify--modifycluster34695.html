to modify

verb
make small changes to something
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make small changes to something, usually in order to improve it or make it more suitable for a particular purpose

Examples

to modify a plan
She modified her speech after receiving feedback from the audience.
The software allows users to easily modify their documents.
We should modify our plans to account for the new restrictions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – adjust: to make minor changes to fit or suit conditions
  • synonym – alter: to change or make different in some respect
  • degree higher – transform: to make a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
