to top

verb
reach the highest point of something
Pronunciation: / tɑp / (AmE), / tɒp / (BrE)
to reach or be at the highest point or part of something

Examples

to top the hill
She topped the staircase and gazed out over the city.
The climbers topped the ridge just as the sun began to set.
We cheered as we finally topped the last hill of the marathon.
The athlete managed to top the bar in the high jump without grazing it.
The drone easily topped the fence as it moved across the field.
The river narrowly topped the rocks without causing any splashes.
A bright star tops the Christmas tree during the holidays.
A delicate sculpture topped the ornate building, drawing everyone's attention upwards.
The sundae was topped with a cherry and some whipped cream.
