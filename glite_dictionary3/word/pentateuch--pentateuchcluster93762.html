Pentateuch

noun
the first five books of the Hebrew Bible
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛn.təˌtjuːk / (AmE), / ˈpɛntəˌtjuːk / (BrE)
the first five books of the Hebrew Bible, comprising Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy, considered as a single unit

Examples

the Pentateuch in Judaism
Each book of the Pentateuch begins with a distinct theme and purpose.
Many scholars have debated the authorship of the Pentateuch for centuries.
The Pentateuch contains laws, narratives, and religious teachings central to Judaism and Christianity.
