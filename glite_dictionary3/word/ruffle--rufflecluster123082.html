to ruffle

verb
disturb or upset someone
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.əl / (BrE)
to upset or disturb the composure of someone; to make someone feel annoyed or less calm

Examples

to ruffle his feelings
Her critical remarks did not ruffle him at all.
The manager's decision will likely ruffle a few feathers among the staff.
Try not to ruffle anyone with your blunt comments during the meeting.
Despite their attempts, nothing could ruffle her calm today.
Her critical remarks did not ruffle him in the slightest.
The bad news seemed to ruffle his usually steady composure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • antonym – soothe: to gently calm or relieve someone's feelings
  • synonym – upset: to make someone unhappy, worried, or angry
