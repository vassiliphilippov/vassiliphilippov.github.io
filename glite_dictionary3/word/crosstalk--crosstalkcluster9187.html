crosstalk

noun
unwanted signal interference
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔːsˌtɑːk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌtɔːk / (BrE)
undesired interference caused by signals from one circuit or channel being unintentionally transferred to another

Examples

crosstalk between channels
Engineers worked hard to minimize crosstalk between the different audio channels on the mixing board.
High levels of crosstalk in network cables can seriously affect data transmission quality.
The telephone lines suffered from noticeable crosstalk, making conversations unclear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – interference: unwanted disturbance in a signal or system
  • antonym – isolation: the absence or prevention of signal coupling
  • synonym – noise: random or unwanted signals that interfere with communication
