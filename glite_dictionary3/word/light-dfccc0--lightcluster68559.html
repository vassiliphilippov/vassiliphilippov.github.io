light

adjective
loose or sandy in texture (soil)
Pronunciation: / laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
(of soil) loose, large-grained, and easily workable; not heavy or clayey in texture.

Examples

light soil
Sandy areas often have light soil, making them easy to dig but requiring more frequent watering.
The gardener preferred light soil for planting carrots because it allowed for better root development.
The plants struggled in the clay but thrived once we moved them to light soil.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heavy: (of soil) dense, clayey, and hard to work
