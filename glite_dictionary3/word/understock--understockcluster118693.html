to understock

verb
to supply with too little stock
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈstɑk / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstɒk / (BrE)
to supply or fill with less than the usual or desired amount of stock or inventory

Examples

to understock store shelves
If you understock the shelves before a sale, you risk losing potential business.
Last winter, the pharmacy understocked cold medicine, leading to shortages.
The store tended to understock certain popular items, frustrating many customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – overstock: to supply with more than the usual or desirable quantity
