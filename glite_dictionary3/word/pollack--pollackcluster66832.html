pollack

noun
flesh of North Atlantic fish (like cod)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlæk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlæk / (BrE)
The lean white flesh of a North Atlantic fish, similar to cod, used as food.

Examples

grilled pollack fillet
Grilled pollack fillets are a healthy source of protein and omega-3 fatty acids.
Many people substitute pollack for cod in traditional recipes because of its mild flavor.
The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh pollack from the local market.
