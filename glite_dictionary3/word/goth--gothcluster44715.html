Goth

noun
uncouth or ill-bred person (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ɡɑθ / (AmE), / ɡɒθ / (BrE)
(Archaic) A crude, uncouth, or ill-bred person who lacks culture or refinement.

Examples

uncultured Goth person
In the refined company, his manners revealed him to be something of a Goth.
Some Victorians called their less cultured colleagues Goth as a playful insult.
The critics dismissed him as a mere Goth with no appreciation for literature or art.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barbarian: an uncivilized or uncultured person
  • antonym – gentleman: a courteous, cultured, or refined man
  • synonym – philistine: a person who is indifferent to culture or the arts
