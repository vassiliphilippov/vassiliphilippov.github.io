Joliot-Curie

proper noun
French nuclear physicist (1900–1958)
Pronunciation: / ʒoʊlioʊ kʊˈri / (AmE), / ʒɒliɒ kjʊˈriː / (BrE)
Frédéric Joliot-Curie (1900–1958), French nuclear physicist, assistant to Marie Curie, and husband of Irène Joliot-Curie, who together discovered artificial radioactivity.

Joliot-Curie Nobel Prize
Frédéric Joliot-Curie was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1935 alongside his wife.
