gravel

noun
small stones and pebbles
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrævəl / (AmE), / ˈɡravəl / (BrE)
Small, loose rock fragments and pebbles, often used to cover roads, paths, or as a construction material.

Examples

gravel road surface
After the rain, tiny pieces of gravel washed onto the road.
The path was covered with gravel to make it easier to walk on.
They laid fresh gravel in the driveway to improve drainage.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cobble: a stone larger than gravel but smaller than a boulder
  • synonym – pebble: a small, rounded stone
  • degree lower – sand: fine particles smaller than gravel
