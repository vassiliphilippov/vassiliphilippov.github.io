seeming

adjective
appearing to be, not necessarily real
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
appearing to be something, especially when it may not be so; apparent rather than real.

Examples

seeming honesty
Her seeming indifference masked a deep concern for the outcome.
The seeming simplicity of the task was deceiving—many struggled to complete it.
We were fooled by his seeming generosity, only to realize his motives were selfish.

Related Terms

  • synonym – apparent: seeming to be true but possibly not
  • antonym – genuine: truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • synonym – ostensible: stated or appearing to be true, but not necessarily so
