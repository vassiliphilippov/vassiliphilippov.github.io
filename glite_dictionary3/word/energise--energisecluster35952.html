to energise

verb
raise to a higher energy level
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to be raised to a higher energy level, especially in a physical or chemical process

Examples

to energise atoms
Adding heat will energise the molecules, causing them to move more rapidly.
Microwave radiation can energise water molecules, making them vibrate faster.
Scientists use lasers to energise electrons and observe their reactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – de-energise: to remove energy or reduce to a lower energy state
  • synonym – excite: to increase the energy of atoms or molecules
