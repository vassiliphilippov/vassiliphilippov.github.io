Mibit

noun
unit of information: 1,048,576 bits
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,048,576 (2^20) bits, typically used in computing and data transfer.

Examples

Mibit file size
A file size of 20 Mibits will take only seconds to download on a fast network.
The modem can transfer data at a rate of 5 Mibits per second.
To avoid confusion, the engineer always specified values in Mibits rather than just 'megs'.
