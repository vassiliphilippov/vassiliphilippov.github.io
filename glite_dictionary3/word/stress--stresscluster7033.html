stress

noun
special importance or emphasis
Pronunciation: / strɛs / (AmE), / strɛs / (BrE)
special importance or emphasis given to a particular point, idea, or aspect

Examples

stress on safety
Her teacher laid particular stress on the importance of clear handwriting.
In his speech, the president put stress on national unity over division.
The manager placed stress on the need for punctuality during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – emphasis: special weight or forcefulness given to something
  • antonym – understate: to make something seem less important; to play down the importance
