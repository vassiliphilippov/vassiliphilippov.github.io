spackle

noun
paste for filling cracks and holes in plaster
Pronunciation: / ˈspækəl / (AmE), / ˈspakəl / (BrE)
A powder made of gypsum plaster and glue that, when mixed with water, forms a paste used to fill cracks and holes in plaster or drywall.

Examples

apply spackle to wall
After mixing the spackle, she applied it smoothly with a putty knife.
He used spackle to repair the small holes in the wall before painting.
Make sure the spackle is completely dry before sanding the surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym – putty: a soft, malleable paste used to fill small holes or cracks in wood or glass
