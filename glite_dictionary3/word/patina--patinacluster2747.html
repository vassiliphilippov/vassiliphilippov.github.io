patina

noun
acquired surface or quality; aura
Pronunciation: / pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə / (BrE)
a surface or appearance that develops on something with age or experience, especially as a result of exposure or handling; often used figuratively to describe an acquired characteristic or aura

Examples

patina of experience
A patina of dust covered the furniture after the long winter absence.
The old book possessed a patina of wisdom and charm that made it more appealing.
Years in the city had given him a patina of worldliness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sheen: a soft glow on a surface, often from age or polish
