intransigency

noun
unwillingness to compromise
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrænsədʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unwilling to compromise or change one's views; stubborn refusal to agree or yield.

Examples

political intransigency
His intransigency on this issue made negotiations almost impossible.
Political progress was hampered by the intransigency of both parties.
The union's intransigency delayed agreement on the new contract for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flexibility: willingness to change or compromise
  • synonym – intransigence: unwillingness to compromise; stubbornness
