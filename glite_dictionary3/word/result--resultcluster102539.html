to result

verb
to end or happen as a consequence
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzʌlt / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌlt / (BrE)
to happen or occur as a consequence of something else

Examples

to result from action
His carelessness resulted in an accident.
Late payment may result in a penalty fee.
The argument resulted in a broken friendship.
If the machine overheats, a breakdown could result.
Nothing significant will result from these negotiations unless both sides make concessions.
Serious injuries can result when safety procedures are ignored in the workplace.
