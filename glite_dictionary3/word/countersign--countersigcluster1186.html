countersign

noun
secret word or phrase for authentication
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsaɪn / (BrE)
a secret word or phrase used to identify or authenticate a member of a group, especially as a means of security or access control

Examples

secret countersign word
Every night the countersign changes to prevent unauthorized entry.
The guard asked for the countersign before allowing us to enter the camp.
Without the correct countersign, the message could not be delivered to headquarters.

Related Terms

  • synonym – challenge: a question or demand for identification, especially in security contexts
  • synonym – password: a secret word or phrase for authentication, especially for access to a computer system
