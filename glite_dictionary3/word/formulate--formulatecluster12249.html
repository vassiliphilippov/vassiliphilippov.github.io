to formulate

verb
develop or state an idea systematically
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to create, develop, or express an idea, plan, or theory carefully and clearly

Examples

to formulate a plan
It is important to formulate clear objectives before starting any research project.
She took several days to carefully formulate her proposal before presenting it to management.
The scientists worked together to formulate a new theory about the origin of the universe.
He struggled to formulate his feelings in a way that others could understand.
It took her some time to formulate her response during the meeting.
Scientists often formulate hypotheses before beginning their experiments.
After months of research, the scientists were able to formulate a new theory about climate change.
She struggled to formulate an effective response to the unexpected question.
The committee will formulate a plan to improve public transportation in the city.

  • synonym – devise: to plan or invent by careful thought
  • synonym – express: to put thoughts or feelings into words
