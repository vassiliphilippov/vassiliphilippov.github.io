libration

noun
oscillation of a celestial body in astronomy
Pronunciation: / laɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
(astronomy) a real or apparent slow oscillation of a celestial body, especially a moon or satellite, allowing different areas to be visible over time.

Examples

libration of the moon
Scientists study the libration of artificial satellites to improve their stability in orbit.
The apparent libration of the Moon means we can see slightly more than half of its surface from Earth.
Through libration, distant regions near the lunar limb become visible for limited periods.
