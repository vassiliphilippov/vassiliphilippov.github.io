Bishop Wulfila

proper noun
bishop who translated the Bible into Gothic
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪʃəp wʊlˈfiːlə / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəp wʊlˈfiːlə / (BrE)
(c. 311–383) A Christian bishop of Cappadocian descent known for translating the Bible from Greek into Gothic and reputedly inventing the Gothic alphabet.

Examples

Bishop Wulfila translated Bible
Bishop Wulfila played a crucial role in spreading Christianity among the Visigoths.
Historians credit Bishop Wulfila with creating the first Gothic alphabet for his translation work.
The translation of the Bible by Bishop Wulfila was a milestone for the Gothic language and Christianity among the Goths.
