circumspect

adjective
cautious; considering possible consequences
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːkəmˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌspɛkt / (BrE)
careful to think about the possible risks or consequences before acting or deciding; cautious and prudent

Examples

circumspect decision making
Doctors are increasingly circumspect when prescribing new medications to their patients.
She approached every investment with a circumspect attitude, always researching thoroughly before making decisions.
The CEO remained circumspect during the press conference, refusing to make any bold statements.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cautious: being careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • antonym – reckless: not thinking about the possible risks or consequences; careless
