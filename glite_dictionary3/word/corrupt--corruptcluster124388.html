to corrupt

verb
make immoral or depraved
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to become immoral, dissolute, or morally depraved, especially by leading into bad habits or actions

Examples

to corrupt a child
His wealth and power eventually began to corrupt his once honest character.
Some believe that exposure to violent media can corrupt young children.
The controversial book was said to corrupt the minds of impressionable readers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – debauch: to lead someone into immoral behavior
  • antonym – improve: to make someone or something better or morally upright
  • synonym – subvert: to undermine someone's morals or principles
