ghostfish

noun
eellike Atlantic bottom fish
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊstˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊstˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
An eellike Atlantic bottom-dwelling fish with a large, almost vertical mouth.

Examples

deep-sea ghostfish
Researchers discovered a rare ghostfish off the coast of the Atlantic last summer.
The ghostfish spends much of its life near the ocean floor, hunting for prey.
With its pale coloring, the ghostfish can be difficult to spot in its deep-sea habitat.
