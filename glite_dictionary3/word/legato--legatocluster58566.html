legato

adjective, adverb
smoothly and connectedly (music)
Pronunciation: / ləˈɡɑːtoʊ /; / lɪˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / lɛˈɡɑːtəʊ /; / lɪˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) played or sung in a smooth, connected way, with each note flowing into the next without breaks

Examples

play legato notes
Please play the next section legato rather than staccato.
The composer indicated that this phrase should be sung legato for emotional effect.
The violinist performed the passage legato, creating a seamless, flowing sound.
Her legato singing style gave the song an elegant and graceful quality.
In piano music, phrases marked legato should be performed with smoothly connected notes.
The violinist played the entire melody legato, creating a warm, flowing sound.

Related Terms
