herniation

noun
abnormal protrusion through tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌhɝːniˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɜːniˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the abnormal protrusion of an organ or tissue through a rupture or opening in the surrounding muscle or membrane

Examples

herniation of a disc
A sudden movement may increase the risk of herniation in vulnerable individuals.
Abdominal herniation can cause severe pain and requires prompt medical attention.
The MRI revealed a disc herniation pressing against the patient's spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hernia: an abnormal exit of tissue or an organ through the wall of the cavity that normally contains it
