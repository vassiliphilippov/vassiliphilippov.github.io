liner

noun
inner protective covering
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪnər /; / ˈlaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
a material or layer that covers and protects the inside surface of something, such as a trash can or the inside of a garment

Examples

trash liner
I always use a trash liner to keep the bin clean.
She sewed a soft liner inside the purse to protect the contents.
The gloves come with a removable fleece liner for added warmth.
Many luxury handbags have a leather exterior and a fabric liner for extra durability.
She replaced the worn liner in her jacket with a new one.
The coat's silk liner makes it comfortable to wear even in cold weather.
