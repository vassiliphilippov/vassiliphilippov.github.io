sidelight

noun
navigation light on a boat's side
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.laɪt / (BrE)
a light on the port or starboard side of a vessel, indicating its direction to other vessels at night, typically red on the port side and green on the starboard side

Examples

sidelight on a ship
The captain checked that the ship’s sidelights were functioning before leaving the harbor.
The red and green sidelights help pilots determine a ship’s orientation on dark waters.
Without working sidelights, a vessel risks not being seen by other boats at night.
