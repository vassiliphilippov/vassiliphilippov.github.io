potshot

noun
an easy or casual criticism or attack
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑtˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɒtʃɒt / (BrE)
A criticism or attack that is easily made and not carefully considered, often aimed at an easy target.

Examples

take potshots at rivals
Instead of offering solutions, he just took potshots at his opponents.
She grew tired of the constant potshots directed at her work.
The article was full of potshots at the company's leadership.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attack: an aggressive criticism or action against someone or something
  • antonym – compliment: a polite expression of praise or admiration
