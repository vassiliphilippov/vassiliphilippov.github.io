sensitisation

noun
increased sensitivity to a substance
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɛn.sə.tɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming sensitive to a substance (such as an allergen or antigen), often resulting in a stronger reaction on later exposure

Examples

sensitisation to allergens
After repeated exposure to pollen, the patient's sensitisation led to severe allergic reactions.
Scientists studied the sensitisation process in order to develop better allergy treatments.
The sensitisation of the immune system made subsequent contact with the substance dangerous.
Medical researchers monitored the effects of sensitisation over several weeks of treatment.
Repeated sensitisation can lead to allergic reactions in some individuals.
The sensitisation of laboratory animals is necessary before testing certain vaccines.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitisation: the process of making less sensitive or nonreactive, often to an allergen
  • synonym – sensitization: the process or state of becoming sensitive to a substance (American spelling)
