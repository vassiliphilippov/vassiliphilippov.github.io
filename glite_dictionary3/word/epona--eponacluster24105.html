Epona

proper noun
Celtic goddess of horses
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpoʊnə / (AmE), / ɪˈpəʊnə / (BrE)
(in Celtic and Roman mythology) a goddess chiefly associated with horses, mules, and asses.

Examples

Epona goddess of horses
Equestrians sometimes pay tribute to Epona for good fortune in their competitions.
Many ancient statues depict Epona being surrounded by horses and holding a cornucopia.
The festival honoring Epona was celebrated by both the Romans and the Celts.
