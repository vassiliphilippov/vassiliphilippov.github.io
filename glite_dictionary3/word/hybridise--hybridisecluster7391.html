to hybridise

verb
create hybrids by crossbreeding
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪbrəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪbrɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to breed animals or plants by mating individuals from different species, breeds, or varieties to produce a hybrid

Examples

to hybridise two plants
Attempts to hybridise the wild cats resulted in a new breed with unique traits.
Scientists often hybridise different plant species to develop new, resistant crops.
Some gardeners hybridise roses to achieve more vibrant colors and hardier plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crossbreed: to breed animals or plants of different breeds or varieties together
  • antonym – inbreed: to breed from closely related people or animals
  • synonym – interbreed: to breed or reproduce within a different species, breed, or variety
