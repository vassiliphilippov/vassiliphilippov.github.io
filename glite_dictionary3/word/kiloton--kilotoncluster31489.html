kiloton

noun
unit of weight, 1,000 tons
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪləˌtʌn / (AmE), / ˈkɪlətʌn / (BrE)
A unit of weight equal to one thousand tons (typically 2,000,000 pounds or about 907,185 kilograms); also used to describe the explosive power of nuclear weapons equivalent to one thousand tons of TNT.

Examples

kiloton of steel
A plant with a capacity of five kilotons processes a vast amount of material each year.
Scientists measured the explosion's force at just under one kiloton of TNT.
The bomb had a yield of twenty kilotons, causing widespread devastation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – megaton: a unit of explosive power equal to one million tons of TNT
  • degree lower – ton: a unit of weight equal to 2,000 pounds (US) or 1,016 kilograms (UK)
