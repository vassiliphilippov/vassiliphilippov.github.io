denial

noun
refusal to grant a request
Pronunciation: / dəˈnaɪ.əl /; / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act of refusing to accept, agree to, or grant something; saying that something is not true or rejecting a request or claim

Examples

denial of a request
After repeated denials of their application, they decided to appeal.
The denial of service caused widespread frustration among users.
The manager's denial of our request for more time surprised everyone.
Her constant denial of the allegations only made people more suspicious.
His denial of the facts delayed the resolution of the problem.
The company issued a denial after rumors of a merger surfaced.

Related Terms

  • antonym – admission: the act of confessing to or acknowledging something is true
  • synonym – refusal: the act of declining to accept or grant something
  • synonym – rejection: the act of dismissing or refusing a proposal, idea, or statement
