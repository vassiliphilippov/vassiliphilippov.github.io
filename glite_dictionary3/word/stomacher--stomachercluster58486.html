stomacher

noun
ornamental V-shaped garment panel (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌməkər / (AmE), / ˈstʌməkə / (BrE)
a V-shaped piece of stiffened material worn over the chest and stomach, typically as part of a woman's dress in the 16th to 18th centuries

Examples

stomacher with jewels
A richly decorated stomacher was often a symbol of wealth in the Elizabethan era.
She wore a silk gown with a jeweled stomacher at the royal ball.
The museum's collection includes a beautifully embroidered stomacher from the 17th century.
