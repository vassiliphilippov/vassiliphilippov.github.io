wash

noun
erosion of soil or gravel by water
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ / (AmE), / wɒʃ / (BrE)
the gradual wearing away and removal of soil or gravel by the action of water, especially from a surface such as a road or field

Examples

rain wash on roads
Heavy rains can cause substantial wash on unpaved mountain roads, leading to dangerous driving conditions.
Repeated wash from the spring floods had carved deep grooves into the gravel path.
The construction crew worked to repair the wash left behind after the river overflowed its banks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erosion: the process by which soil and rock are removed from the Earth's surface by wind or water flow
