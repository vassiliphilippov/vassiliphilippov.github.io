microsensor

noun
very small sensor device
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌsɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌsɛnsə / (BrE)
A very small device, usually ranging from submicrometer to millimeter size, that detects and converts physical or chemical quantities into electrical signals, often with higher accuracy and lower power consumption than larger sensors.

Examples

microsensor detects gas
Researchers developed a microsensor capable of detecting low concentrations of toxic gas.
The microsensor's accuracy makes it ideal for use in aerospace engineering.
The new medical implant contains a microsensor to monitor blood pressure in real time.

  • synonym – sensor: a device that detects or measures physical properties
