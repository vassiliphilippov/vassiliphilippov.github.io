vindictively

adverb
in a revengeful or spiteful manner
Pronunciation: / vɪnˈdɪktɪvli / (AmE), / vɪnˈdɪktɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner motivated by a desire for revenge; acting out of spite or a wish to retaliate.

Examples

to speak vindictively
He spoke vindictively about his former partner, unable to let go of the past.
She acted vindictively after being unfairly dismissed, seeking to damage her employer's reputation.
The character in the novel plotted vindictively against those he believed had wronged him.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgivingly: in a forgiving, merciful manner
  • synonym – spitefully: in a way that shows malice or desire to harm
