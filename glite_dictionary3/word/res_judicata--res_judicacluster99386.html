res judicata

noun
matter settled by court; cannot be retried
Pronunciation: / reɪs ˌdʒuːdɪˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / reɪs ˌdʒuːdɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
(Law) A matter that has been adjudicated by a competent court and therefore may not be pursued further by the same parties.

Examples

res judicata applies in court
Because the issue had already been decided, it was subject to res judicata.
The court dismissed the claim on the grounds of res judicata.
The doctrine of res judicata prevents parties from relitigating the same dispute.
