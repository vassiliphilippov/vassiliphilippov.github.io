light

adjective
not great in degree or amount
Pronunciation: / laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
gentle, not strong or intense; small in amount, degree, or intensity

Examples

light rain
After the accident, the injuries to the crew were surprisingly light.

We only received a light dusting of snow overnight, so driving conditions are fine.

Her voice was so light that it could barely be heard over the music.
The surgeon used a light touch to avoid damaging the tissue.
Although he used to smoke heavily, he's now just a light smoker.
She is a light eater and rarely has large meals.
We enjoyed a light supper before the concert.
