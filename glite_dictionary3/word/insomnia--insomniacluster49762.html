insomnia

noun
inability to sleep; sleeplessness
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɑːmniə / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɒmniə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person regularly finds it difficult to fall asleep or stay asleep.

Examples

chronic insomnia
After weeks of insomnia, she finally went to see a sleep specialist.
He struggled with insomnia throughout his university years.
Stress and anxiety are common causes of insomnia in adults.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hypersomnia: excessive sleepiness
  • synonym – sleeplessness: the state of being unable to sleep
