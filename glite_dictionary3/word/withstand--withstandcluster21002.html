to withstand

verb
remain unaffected by; resist successfully
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈstænd / (AmE), / wɪðˈstænd / (BrE)
to resist or endure something difficult, damaging, or forceful without being harmed or affected

Examples

withstand strong pressure
She had to withstand strong criticism from her colleagues.
The old fortress managed to withstand several sieges over the centuries.
This material can withstand extreme temperatures.
He managed to withstand the intense pressure from his opponents and kept calm.
The bridge was designed to withstand earthquakes and strong winds.
These materials are engineered to withstand extreme temperatures without deteriorating.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently or remain firm under hardship
  • synonym – resist: to withstand the action or effect of something
  • antonym – succumb: to fail to withstand; to yield to a superior force
