trematode

noun
parasitic flatworm with external suckers
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛməˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmətəʊd / (BrE)
a parasitic flatworm of the class Trematoda, typically having suckers for attaching to a host

Examples

trematode parasite worm
A trematode uses specialized suckers to attach itself to its host.

The liver fluke is a type of trematode that infects mammals.
