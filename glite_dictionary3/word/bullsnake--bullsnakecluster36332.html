bullsnake

noun
large harmless rodent-eating North American snake
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a large harmless North American snake, feeding on rodents and often found in burrows; it is a type of gopher snake (Pituophis catenifer sayi)

Examples

a bullsnake in a burrow
A bullsnake may hiss loudly and mimic a rattlesnake when threatened, but it is not venomous.
The bullsnake helps control the rodent population on many farms.
We found a large bullsnake basking in the sun near its burrow.
