puffer

noun
type of inflatable, often poisonous fish
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌfər / (AmE), / ˈpʌfə / (BrE)
a marine fish with a spiny, elongated body that can inflate itself into a globe; some species are poisonous

Examples

puffer fish inflates
Divers often spot the spiny puffer hiding among coral reefs.
Some species of puffer contain toxins that can be lethal to humans.
The puffer inflated its body to scare away predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fugu: a species of pufferfish eaten as sushi; highly poisonous if not prepared correctly
