opsonization

noun
immune process aiding phagocytosis of microbes
Pronunciation: / ˌɑp.sə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒp.sə.nʌɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which opsonins bind to an invading microorganism, making it more susceptible to phagocytosis by immune cells

Examples

opsonization of bacteria
Antibodies play an important role in opsonization, making bacteria more readily attacked by phagocytes.
C3b is a key protein involved in opsonization during the complement cascade.
The process of opsonization enhances the immune system's ability to clear pathogens.

Related Terms

  • synonym – opsonin: a substance that enhances phagocytosis of antigens by marking them for immune response
  • synonym – phagocytosis: the ingestion of bacteria or other material by phagocytes
