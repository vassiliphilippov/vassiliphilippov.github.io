deadening

noun
the act of making something dull or ineffective
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛd.ənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛd.ənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something less effective, interesting, or lively, often by making it routine or monotonous

Examples

deadening effect
Many complained about the deadening effect of bureaucracy on creativity.
Routine paperwork can have a deadening influence on innovative thinking.
The repetitive nature of the task contributed to the deadening of his enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dulling: the act of making something dull or less sharp/vivid
  • antonym – enlivening: the act of making something more lively or interesting
