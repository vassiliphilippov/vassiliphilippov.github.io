consanguinity

noun
blood relationship; kinship by descent
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.sæŋˈɡwɪ.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sæŋˈɡwɪ.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state of being related by blood; the quality of being descended from the same ancestor.

Examples

consanguinity between siblings
Ancient kingdoms often forged alliances through marriage, even when consanguinity was close.
Genealogists study family trees to determine the degree of consanguinity among relatives.
The laws prohibit marriage between close consanguinity to prevent genetic disorders.
