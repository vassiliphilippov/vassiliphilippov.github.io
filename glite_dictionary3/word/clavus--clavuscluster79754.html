clavus

noun
hard, thickened skin area on the toe; corn
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪ.vəs / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.vəs / (BrE)
a hard, thickened area of skin, especially on the toes, caused by continual pressure or friction, typically from ill-fitting shoes; a type of corn

Examples

clavus on toe
People who wear tight shoes are more likely to develop a clavus.
The doctor diagnosed the painful growth on his toe as a clavus.
Treatment for a clavus often involves relieving the pressure causing it.
