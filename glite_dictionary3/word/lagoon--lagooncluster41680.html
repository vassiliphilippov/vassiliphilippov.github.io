lagoon

noun
shallow water separated by a barrier
Pronunciation: / ləˈɡun / (AmE), / ləˈɡuːn / (BrE)
A shallow body of water separated from a larger sea or lake by a barrier such as a sandbank or coral reef.

Examples

tropical lagoon
A coral reef helped to form a beautiful lagoon just off the coast.
The calm waters of the lagoon were ideal for swimming and fishing.
Wild birds gathered each morning at the edge of the lagoon to feed.
