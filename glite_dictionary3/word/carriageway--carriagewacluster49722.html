carriageway

noun
part of a road for one-way traffic
Pronunciation: / ˈkærɪdʒˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒˌweɪ / (BrE)
A part of a road or highway intended for vehicles, usually one of the separated roads for traffic moving in a single direction, often divided into lanes.

Examples

dual carriageway
Most British motorways have a central reservation dividing the two carriageways.
Please keep to the left carriageway while the right side is being repaired.
The accident blocked the northbound carriageway for several hours yesterday.
