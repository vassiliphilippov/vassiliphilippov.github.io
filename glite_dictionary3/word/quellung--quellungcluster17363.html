quellung

noun
swelling of microorganism capsule after antibody reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈkvɛlʊŋ / (AmE), / ˈkvɛlʊŋ / (BrE)
A swelling of the capsule surrounding a microorganism caused by reaction with a specific antibody, used as the basis for certain identification tests.

Examples

quellung reaction test
A positive quellung test can confirm the presence of Streptococcus pneumoniae.
Researchers observed the quellung phenomenon under the microscope after adding the specific antiserum.
The quellung reaction is an important diagnostic tool in microbiology.
