warm

adjective
close to finding something sought
Pronunciation: / wɔrm / (AmE), / wɔːm / (BrE)
(of a seeker) close to finding or discovering something, especially used in informal contexts when someone is searching for an answer or a hidden object.

Examples

warm guess
The teacher said I was warm when I picked up the right book.
When you guessed Paris, you were getting warm, but it's actually Rome.
You're getting warm; the keys are somewhere near that shelf.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cold: far from the object sought or the correct answer
