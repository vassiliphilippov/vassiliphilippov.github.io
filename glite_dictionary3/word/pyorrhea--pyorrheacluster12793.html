pyorrhea

noun
discharge of pus (esp. gums)
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪəˈriə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈriːə /; / ˌpʌɪəˈrɪə / (BrE)
a serious gum disease that causes pus to be discharged from the gums and can lead to loose teeth

Examples

pyorrhea of gums
Symptoms such as bleeding and pyorrhea often indicate advanced gum disease.
The dentist diagnosed the patient with pyorrhea after noticing pus oozing from his gums.
Untreated pyorrhea can lead to tooth loss and serious complications.
Chronic pyorrhea is more common in people with poor oral hygiene.
The dentist diagnosed her gum problems as pyorrhea and started a treatment plan.
Untreated pyorrhea can eventually result in the loss of multiple teeth.
