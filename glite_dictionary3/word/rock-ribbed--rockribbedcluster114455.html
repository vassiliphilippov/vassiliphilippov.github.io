rock-ribbed

adjective
uncompromising or staunch (often in politics)
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑk ˌrɪbd / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrɪbd / (BrE)
(of a person or their beliefs) very firm, uncompromising, or stubbornly conservative, especially in politics.

Examples

rock-ribbed conservative
He was known as a rock-ribbed conservative who rarely strayed from his party's positions.
Her rock-ribbed commitment to tradition impressed some and frustrated others.
The senator's rock-ribbed beliefs made bipartisan cooperation challenging.
