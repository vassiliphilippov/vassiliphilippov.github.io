radicle

noun
small rootlike anatomical structure
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
an anatomical structure resembling a small root or rootlet, such as a fibril of a nerve

Examples

nerve radicle
Damage to a sensory radicle can result in loss of sensation in the affected area.
Microscopic examination revealed several radicles extending from the nerve fiber.
The spinal nerve radicle branches off from the main nerve trunk.
