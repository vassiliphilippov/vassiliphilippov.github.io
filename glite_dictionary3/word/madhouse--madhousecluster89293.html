madhouse

noun
pejorative term for insane asylum; chaotic place
Pronunciation: / ˈmædˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈmadˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A pejorative term for a mental institution, typically used to refer to an insane asylum; also used figuratively for a place or situation that is very chaotic or noisy.

Examples

a real madhouse inside
After the news broke, the office turned into a madhouse with phones ringing nonstop.
Back in the nineteenth century, people with mental illnesses were often sent to a madhouse.
Black Friday at the store was a complete madhouse this year.

  • synonym – bedlam: noisy and chaotic place, originally an asylum
