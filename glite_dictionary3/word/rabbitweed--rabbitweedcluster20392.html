rabbitweed

noun
a low-growing sticky subshrub of the US Southwest
Pronunciation: / ˈræbɪtˌwid / (AmE), / ˈræbɪtˌwiːd / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub native to the southwestern United States, with narrow linear leaves, slender branches, and many tiny yellow flower heads.

Examples

wild rabbitweed plant
Gardeners in the Southwest sometimes struggle to control rabbitweed in their yards.
