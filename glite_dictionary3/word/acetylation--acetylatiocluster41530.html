acetylation

noun
addition of an acetyl group to a molecule
Pronunciation: / əˌsiːtɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsiːtɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing an acetyl group into a molecule, especially in biochemical reactions

Examples

acetylation of proteins
Aspirin works by causing the acetylation of certain enzymes.
Histone acetylation plays a crucial role in regulating gene expression.
The acetylation of proteins can affect their function and stability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deacetylation: removal of an acetyl group from a molecule
