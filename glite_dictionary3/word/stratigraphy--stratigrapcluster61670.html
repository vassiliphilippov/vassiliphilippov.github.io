stratigraphy

noun
study of rock layers in geology
Pronunciation: / strəˈtɪɡrəfi / (AmE), / strəˈtɪɡrəfi / (BrE)
the branch of geology concerned with the study of rock layers (strata) and their sequence, distribution, age, and relationship to geological history

Examples

study of stratigraphy
Modern stratigraphy uses advanced dating methods to analyze the age of sedimentary deposits.
She specialized in stratigraphy, focusing on reconstructing Earth's ancient environments from rock layers.
Understanding stratigraphy is essential for oil and gas exploration.
