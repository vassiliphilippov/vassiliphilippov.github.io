to decarbonize

verb
reduce greenhouse-gas emissions
Pronunciation: / diˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈkɑːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to reduce or eliminate carbon dioxide or other greenhouse-gas emissions from a system, sector, process, or economy, typically through changes in technology, energy sources, or policy.

Examples

to decarbonize the economy
The city announced plans to decarbonize its transportation sector by 2030.
Many manufacturers are trying to decarbonize production by switching to renewable electricity.
By investing in solar farms, the utility has begun to decarbonize its electricity supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decarbonise: British spelling of 'decarbonize'; same meaning of lowering greenhouse-gas emissions.
  • synonym – reduce emissions: to make fewer greenhouse gases enter the atmosphere; often used interchangeably in climate policy.
