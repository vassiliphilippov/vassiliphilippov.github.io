glabrescent

adjective
becoming hairless or smooth
Pronunciation: / ɡləˈbrɛsənt / (AmE), / ɡləˈbrɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a surface, typically in plants or animals) lacking hair or becoming hairless; tending to become smooth and free from hairs or similar growths.

Examples

glabrescent plant stem
In spring, the stems appear fuzzy, but by summer they are almost completely glabrescent.
Many amphibians display glabrescent skin as they develop into adults.
The leaves of the plant are initially hairy but become glabrescent as they mature.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glabrous: having no hairs or projections; smooth (especially in plants or animals)
