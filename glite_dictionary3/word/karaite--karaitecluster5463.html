Karaite

noun
member of a Jewish sect rejecting the Talmud
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkærəˌaɪt / (BrE)
a member of a Jewish sect, originating in the eighth century, that accepts only the Hebrew Scriptures as authoritative and rejects the Talmud, with origins in Iraq

Examples

Karaite community leader
Ancient manuscripts from Karaite scholars offer unique interpretations of the Hebrew Scriptures.
The Karaite community celebrates its holidays according to the biblical calendar.
Unlike Rabbinic Jews, Karaites do not follow the traditions of the Talmud.
