inexpediency

noun
unsuitability for a purpose
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənsi / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unsuitable or unwise for achieving a particular purpose

Examples

political inexpediency
Despite its popularity, the project's costs revealed its fundamental inexpediency.
She argued against the decision, citing both its moral issues and its inexpediency.
The committee weighed the inexpediency of implementing the policy without broader consultation.
