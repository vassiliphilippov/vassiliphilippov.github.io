underwriter

noun
person or organization that guarantees new securities
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person or organization, especially a banker, that undertakes to finance or guarantee new securities or other financial ventures by taking on risk, often for a fee

Examples

underwriter guarantees bonds
As an underwriter, she assessed the risks involved in issuing new corporate bonds.
Major underwriters often build relationships with large institutional investors to ensure the success of new securities.
The investment bank acted as the principal underwriter for the company's initial public offering.
