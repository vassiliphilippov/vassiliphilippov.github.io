exon

noun
gene segment coding for proteins
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkˌsɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛksɒn / (BrE)
a segment of a DNA or RNA molecule containing information coding for a protein or peptide sequence, typically separated by introns within a gene

Examples

In most eukaryotic genes, exons and introns alternate along the DNA strand.
