to discourage

verb
try to prevent or oppose
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkɝːɪdʒ /; / dɪˈskɝːɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒ /; / dɪˈskʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to try to prevent something or make someone less likely to do something, especially by making them lose confidence or showing disapproval

Examples

to discourage bad habits
Heavy taxes on soda are meant to discourage excessive sugar consumption.
Parents often discourage their children from smoking by explaining its dangers.
The government aims to discourage reckless driving through stricter penalties.
Her failure on the test did not discourage her from studying harder next time.
Negative criticism can discourage employees from sharing innovative ideas at work.
Please do not discourage your child from trying new things, even if they seem difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dissuade: to persuade someone not to do something
  • antonym – encourage: to give support, confidence, or hope to someone; to help something happen
