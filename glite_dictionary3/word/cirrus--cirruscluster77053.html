cirrus

noun
thin, wispy high-altitude cloud
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪr.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɪr.əs / (BrE)
A type of thin, wispy white cloud that forms at high altitudes, typically composed of ice crystals.

Examples

cirrus cloud formation
Meteorologists predict fair weather when only cirrus clouds are present in the sky.
On clear days, you can sometimes see cirrus clouds far above the other clouds.
The jet left a trail that soon turned into a thin strip of cirrus.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cumulus: a dense, fluffy cloud with a flat base
  • antonym – stratus: a low, gray cloud layer covering the sky
