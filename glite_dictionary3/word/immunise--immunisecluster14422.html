to immunise

verb
grant legal immunity from prosecution
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪmjʊnaɪz / (BrE)
(legal) To grant someone legal immunity from prosecution, typically in exchange for testimony or cooperation.

Examples

to immunise a witness
Authorities agreed to immunise her so she could provide crucial information in court.
He refused to immunise the defendant, fearing it might undermine the investigation.
The witness was immunised in return for testifying against the main suspect.

  • antonym – prosecute: institute legal proceedings against a person
