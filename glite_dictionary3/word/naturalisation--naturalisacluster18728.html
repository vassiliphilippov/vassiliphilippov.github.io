naturalisation

noun
adaptation of borrowed word's pronunciation
Pronunciation: / ˌnætʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃ(ə)rələˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process by which a borrowed word is adapted in pronunciation to fit the phonological system of the borrowing language.

Examples

naturalisation of words
Linguists study the patterns of naturalisation in different languages to understand their phonological rules.
Over time, naturalisation has made many foreign words sound completely native in English.
The naturalisation of 'piano' led to its current English pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assimilation: process by which sounds become similar to neighboring sounds
