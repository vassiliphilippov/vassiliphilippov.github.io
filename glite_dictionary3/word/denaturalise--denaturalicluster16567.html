to denaturalise

verb
remove citizenship status
Pronunciation: / diˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to officially remove someone's rights and status as a citizen of a country

Examples

to denaturalise a citizen
After the investigation, the government decided to denaturalise the individual for lying on his citizenship application.
Several war criminals were denaturalised in the 1980s as new evidence emerged about their past.
The government has strict rules about when it can denaturalise someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – denationalize: to deprive of national rights or characteristics; often used synonymously
