dissection

noun
cutting apart for study
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of cutting something apart in order to study its internal parts, especially in a scientific or medical context

Examples

dissection of a frog
Careful dissection of the heart revealed signs of disease.
The article provided a detailed dissection of the proposed law's weaknesses.
The biology students performed a dissection of a frog to examine its internal organs.
