sleaze

noun
cheap vulgarity or sordid behavior
Pronunciation: / sliz / (AmE), / sliːz / (BrE)
Low quality or moral standards, especially involving vulgarity or cheapness; behavior or things considered sordid or disreputable.

Examples

sleaze and corruption
He built his fortune on the sleaze and corruption of the entertainment industry.
Many were shocked by the sleaze in the latest tabloid stories about the scandal.
The politician tried to distance himself from any hint of sleaze during his campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decency: the quality of being proper and respectable
  • synonym – sordidness: the quality of being dirty or morally degraded
