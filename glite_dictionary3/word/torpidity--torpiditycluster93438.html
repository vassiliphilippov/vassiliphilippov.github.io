torpidity

noun
state of mental and physical inactivity
Pronunciation: / tɔrˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / tɔːˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
a state of being mentally and physically inactive or lacking energy

Examples

deep torpidity
After the accident, she experienced a short period of torpidity before gradually becoming more responsive.
The patient's torpidity made it difficult for doctors to assess his level of consciousness.
Winter often induces a kind of torpidity in certain animals, similar to hibernation.
After his illness, a feeling of torpidity lingered for weeks, making it hard for him to resume work.
Her torpidity lifted when she began exercising regularly and following a healthier routine.
The novelist described the city's summer torpidity in vivid detail, emphasizing the stifling heat and sluggish days.
