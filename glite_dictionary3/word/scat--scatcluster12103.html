scat

noun
jazz singing with improvised nonsense syllables
Pronunciation: / skæt / (AmE), / skæt / (BrE)
a style of jazz singing in which the singer improvises melodies and rhythms using nonsense syllables instead of words, often imitating instrumental sounds

Examples

scat singing jazz
He practiced scat to master the vocal rhythms of classic jazz tunes.
Her remarkable ability to scat added an exciting dimension to the band's performance.
In jazz, scat allows singers to improvise as if their voice were an instrument.
