whispering

adjective
low, continuous, indistinct sound
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪspərɪŋ / (BrE)
making a low, continuous, indistinct sound, resembling a whisper; often used of wind, water, or voices.

Examples

whispering wind
A whispering stream ran behind the cottage, barely louder than a breath.
The whispering waves made the shore seem peaceful all night long.
We listened to the whispering wind as it moved through the trees at dusk.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – hushed: softened to a quiet or low volume
  • synonym – murmuring: making a soft, low, continuous sound
