to beat

verb
make or create by pounding or trampling
Pronunciation: / biːt /; / bit / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
to make a path or drive out animals by striking, treading, or using force repeatedly

Examples

to beat a path
She beat a narrow trail through the tall grass to reach the river.
The villagers often beat new paths through the dense jungle for easier access.
With each step, the hikers slowly beat a pathway up the hillside.
Guides beat the reeds along the riverbank to drive out waterfowl.
The hunters beat the bushes vigorously to flush out pheasants.
We beat the undergrowth for nearly an hour before any rabbits appeared.
As we climbed the mountain, a fierce wind beat into our faces, making progress difficult.
Heavy rain beat against the windows throughout the night.
The afternoon sun beat down on the crowded beach, forcing everyone to seek shade.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flush: to cause animals to leave cover by sudden movement or noise
  • synonym – forge: to form or make (something) by concentrated effort
  • synonym – hammer: strike or hit repeatedly with force (of natural forces)
  • synonym – pound: strike repeatedly or heavily (of rain, wind, etc.)
