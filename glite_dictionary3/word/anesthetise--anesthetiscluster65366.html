to anesthetise

verb
administer anesthesia to induce loss of sensation
Pronunciation: / əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈniːsθɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to administer an anesthetic to a person or animal so as to induce loss of sensation or consciousness, especially to enable a medical procedure

Examples

to anesthetise a patient
It is important to anesthetise the patient thoroughly before performing surgery.
The dentist will anesthetise your gum before the extraction begins.
Veterinarians often anesthetise animals prior to complex procedures.
