decontamination

noun
removal of harmful substances
Pronunciation: / diˌkɑntəˌmeɪˈʃən / (AmE), / diˌkɒntəˌmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing harmful substances, such as chemicals, radioactive materials, or microorganisms, from a person, place, or object

Examples

decontamination process
Decontamination procedures were carried out after the chemical spill to ensure the area was safe.
After removing the hazardous waste, a full decontamination of the site was performed.
The hospital has strict decontamination protocols to prevent the spread of infection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contamination: the presence of a harmful or unwanted substance
  • synonym – decontaminate: to remove contaminants from something
