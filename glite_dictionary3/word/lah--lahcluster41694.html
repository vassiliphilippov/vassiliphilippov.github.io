lah

particle
Southeast Asian English discourse particle
Pronunciation: / lɑ / (AmE), / lɑː / (BrE)
A sentence-final discourse particle used in Southeast Asian English varieties to add emphasis, soften a statement or request, reassure, or signal casual friendliness.

Examples

add lah for emphasis
Don't worry about the meeting, lah, everyone knows how busy you are today.
She smiled and said lah to make her disagreement sound less harsh and friendlier.
If you're tired, just go home lah — nobody will blame you for leaving early tonight.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lor: another Singlish/Manglish sentence-final particle expressing resignation, emphasis, or casual tone.
