autogamy

noun
self-fertilization in plants
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈtɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒɡəmi / (BrE)
Self-fertilization, especially in flowering plants, where pollen from a flower fertilizes ovules of the same flower or plant.

Examples

autogamy in plants
In autogamy, self-pollination ensures that a single plant can produce seeds.
Some species of plants rely on autogamy to reproduce in isolated environments.
The study explored how autogamy affects genetic diversity in wildflowers.
