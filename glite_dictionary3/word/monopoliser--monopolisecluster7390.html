monopoliser

noun
person who dominates attention or conversation
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑː.pə.laɪ.zər / (AmE), / məˈnɒ.pə.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who dominates a conversation, attention, or group interaction so that others have little chance to speak or take part

Examples

monopoliser of conversation
During the meeting, the monopoliser spoke for forty minutes and left no time for questions.
Everyone avoided inviting the monopoliser because they always dominated every party conversation.
She became known as the office monopoliser after taking credit for several team projects.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contributor: a person who adds to a discussion or activity rather than dominating it
  • synonym – monopolist: someone who takes exclusive control; used both for economic monopolies and figurative domination
