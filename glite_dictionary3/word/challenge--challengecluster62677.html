to challenge

verb
demand identification or authorization
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
to stop someone and demand their name, identity, or authorization, especially as a security measure

Examples

to challenge a visitor
Airport staff routinely challenge passengers who enter restricted areas without proper ID.
She was challenged by a police officer as she tried to enter the backstage area.
The guard challenged the visitor before allowing him inside the building.
