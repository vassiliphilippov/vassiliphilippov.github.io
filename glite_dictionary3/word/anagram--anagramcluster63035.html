anagram

noun
word rearranged from another
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈanəɡram / (BrE)
a word or phrase formed by rearranging the letters of another word or phrase

Examples

anagram of a word
'Listen' is an anagram of 'silent'.
She loves solving anagram puzzles in her free time.
The word anagram comes from the Greek, meaning to transcribe letters differently.
