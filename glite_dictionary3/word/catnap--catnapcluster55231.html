catnap

noun
a short, light sleep
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt.næp / (AmE), / ˈkæt.næp / (BrE)
A short, light sleep, usually taken during the day and not in bed.

Examples

a quick catnap
After a long meeting, she took a catnap on the office couch.
He closed his eyes and enjoyed a quick catnap during his lunch break.
Sometimes a brief catnap in the afternoon can boost your energy.
