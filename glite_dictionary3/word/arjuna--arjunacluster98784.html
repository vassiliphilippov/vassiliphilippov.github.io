Arjuna

proper noun
Hindu warrior prince in the Bhagavad-Gita
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈdʒunə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒuːnə / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, a warrior prince in the Bhagavad-Gita to whom Krishna reveals spiritual teachings about the nature of existence and the path to knowing God.

Examples

Arjuna in the Bhagavad-Gita
In Hindu epics, Arjuna is renowned for his courage and skill as an archer.
Many scholars study the teachings given to Arjuna in the Bhagavad-Gita for their spiritual insights.
The dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna remains central to interpretations of Hindu philosophy.
