shadfly

noun
short-lived flying insect; mayfly
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃædˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A slender insect with delicate, transparent wings, whose aquatic larval stage is followed by a very short-lived terrestrial adult stage.

Examples

a shadfly swarm
A shadfly only lives for a couple of days in its adult form before dying.
Every summer, large numbers of shadflies gather near the riverbanks to mate and lay eggs.
The presence of shadflies often signals a healthy aquatic ecosystem.
