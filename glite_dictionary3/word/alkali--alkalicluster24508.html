alkali

noun
water-soluble basic compound
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.kəˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪ / (BrE)
a compound, typically a hydroxide or carbonate of an alkali metal, that dissolves in water to form a strongly basic solution and turns litmus blue

Examples

strong alkali solution
Potassium hydroxide is a strong alkali that is often used in industry.
The laboratory experiment demonstrated how an alkali turns red litmus paper blue.
When dissolved in water, an alkali can neutralize acids to produce salt and water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acid: a substance that reacts with a base to form a salt and water
  • synonym – base: a substance that reacts with acids to form salts and water
