to retire

verb
cause a player/team to leave the field in sports
Pronunciation: / rəˈtaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
(in sports) to cause a player or team to be out or to end their turn at play

Examples

After two quick outs, the team was able to retire the batter on a fly ball.
The bowler retired the batsman with a fast yorker.
The pitcher managed to retire the side in the seventh inning without giving up a run.
If they retire the next hitter, they will win the game.
The pitcher retired three batters in a row with fastballs.
