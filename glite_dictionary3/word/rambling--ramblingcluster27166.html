rambling

adjective
digressive; lacking clear focus
Pronunciation: / ˈræm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) lengthy and confused or inconsequential, often digressing from the main point or covering a wide range of topics.

Examples

rambling speech
He tends to give rambling answers that drift away from the original question.
Her rambling speech made it difficult to understand her main argument.
The book is fascinating but becomes quite rambling in the later chapters.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • synonym – discursive: digressing from subject to subject
