flush

adjective
having plenty of money or resources
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
having plenty of money or resources; well supplied with funds or possessions of value

Examples

flush with cash
After winning the lottery, she was suddenly flush with cash and could buy anything she wanted.
Although he once struggled, he's now flush and living comfortably.
The company is particularly flush this quarter thanks to record sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym – affluent: having a great deal of money; wealthy
  • antonym – poor: lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable
  • synonym – wealthy: having a lot of money, possessions, or resources
