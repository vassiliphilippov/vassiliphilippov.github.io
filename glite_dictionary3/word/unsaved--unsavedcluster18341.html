unsaved

adjective
not having received religious salvation
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈseɪvd / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvd / (BrE)
not having received salvation, especially in a Christian religious sense; in danger of eternal punishment in Hell.

Examples

the unsaved souls
He believed it was his duty to pray for the unsaved in his community.
Many missionaries devote their lives to helping the unsaved find faith.
The preacher warned that the unsaved would face eternal consequences.
