Humpty Dumptyism

noun
belief that broken things can be restored
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmpti ˈdʌmptiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhʌmpti ˈdʌmptiɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or attitude that things, especially fragile situations or systems, can be restored after falling apart, often used with skepticism.

Examples

political Humpty Dumptyism
Her speech was criticized for its naïve Humpty Dumptyism, ignoring the permanent damage already done.
Some critics warn against Humpty Dumptyism in efforts to fix deeply flawed institutions.
The politician's optimism about restoring the divided country was dismissed as Humpty Dumptyism.
