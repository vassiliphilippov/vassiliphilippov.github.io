worldliness

noun
concern with worldly or material affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːldlinɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːldlinɪs / (BrE)
Concern with material or worldly affairs, often to the neglect of spiritual or moral values.

Examples

excessive worldliness in life
His sermons warned against excessive worldliness and encouraged a focus on faith.
Monks often take vows to renounce worldliness and dedicate themselves to prayer.
She rejected the worldliness of city life and sought spiritual fulfillment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – materialism: focus on material possessions and physical comfort over spiritual values
  • antonym – spirituality: concern with the soul, spirit, or religious values over material matters
