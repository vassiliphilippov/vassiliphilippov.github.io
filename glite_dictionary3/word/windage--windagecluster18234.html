windage

noun
gap between projectile and bore in gun
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndɪdʒ / (BrE)
the space between the projectile of a smoothbore gun and the interior surface of the gun's bore, allowing for gases to escape around the projectile when fired

Examples

windage in a gun barrel
Gunmakers worked to minimize windage to improve accuracy and power.
The excessive windage reduced the musket's firing efficiency.
When the windage is too great, much of the gunpowder's force is lost.
