intubation

noun
insertion of a tube into the body (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tuˈbeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuːˈbeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the medical procedure of inserting a tube into a hollow organ, such as the trachea, to keep it open or deliver medication, air, or fluids

Examples

emergency intubation procedure
Emergency intubation was performed to secure the airway before surgery.
Proper training is essential to ensure safe and effective intubation in critical care situations.
The patient required intubation after experiencing severe breathing difficulties.

Related Terms

  • antonym – extubation: removal of a previously inserted tube from a body organ
