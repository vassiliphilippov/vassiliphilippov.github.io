Protozoa

noun
group of single-celled microscopic organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a former phylum of single-celled microscopic organisms, traditionally considered animals, that includes amoebas, ciliates, flagellates, and sporozoans

Examples

microscopic Protozoa species
Diseases like malaria are caused by certain Protozoa that invade the human body.
Scientists observed several Protozoa under the microscope to study their unique methods of movement.
Textbooks now often classify Protozoa differently, but the term is still commonly used.

Related Terms

  • synonym – protist: any organism belonging to the kingdom Protista, including protozoans and certain algae
