quadrantanopia

noun
blindness in a quarter of the visual field
Pronunciation: / kwɑːˌdræntəˈnoʊpiə / (AmE), / kwɒˌdræntəˈnəʊpiə / (BrE)
blindness or loss of vision in one quarter of the visual field, usually resulting from damage to the visual pathway in the brain.

Examples

quadrantanopia after stroke
After her stroke, she experienced partial quadrantanopia that made it difficult to read.
Damage to the optic radiation often results in quadrantanopia.
The patient's visual exam revealed quadrantanopia in the upper right corner of his field of vision.
