to demineralize

verb
remove minerals or salts
Pronunciation: / diˈmɪnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmɪnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to remove minerals or salts from something, especially from water or biological tissue

Examples

to demineralize water
Before using the water in the steam iron, you should demineralize it to prevent limescale buildup.
Exposure to acids can demineralize tooth enamel, leading to cavities.
The laboratory uses special filters to demineralize water for experiments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – remineralize: add minerals or salts back to something
