locust

noun
migratory grasshopper that forms swarms
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.kəst / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəst / (BrE)
A large, mainly tropical grasshopper with short antennae that migrates in vast swarms and can cause extensive damage to crops.

Examples

swarm of locusts
A swarm of locusts devoured the farmers' entire wheat crop in just a few hours.
During periods of drought, locust plagues can become a serious threat to agriculture.
The villagers tried to protect their fields from the oncoming locust swarm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grasshopper: plant-eating insect with long hind legs for jumping
