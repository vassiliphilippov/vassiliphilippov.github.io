to reenact

verb
to act out or perform again
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.ɪˈnækt / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪˈnækt / (BrE)
to act out or perform a past event again, especially as a play, recreation, or for education

Examples

to reenact a battle
During the festival, actors reenact the legendary battle for the audience.
Every summer, volunteers reenact scenes from the town's colonial past.
The students reenacted the famous courtroom trial for their history project.
During the festival, actors reenact the famous battle to educate and entertain visitors.
Police sometimes reenact crimes to better understand how they happened.
The students reenacted the signing of the Declaration of Independence for their history project.
