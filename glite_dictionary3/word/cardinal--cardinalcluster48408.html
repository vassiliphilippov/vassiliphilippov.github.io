cardinal

adjective
expressing numerical quantity, not order
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrdɪnl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnl̩ / (BrE)
(of a number) expressing quantity rather than order, such as one, two, or three (as opposed to first, second, third).

Examples

cardinal number
In linguistics, a cardinal numeral indicates quantity but not position.
Most children learn the cardinal numbers before they understand ordinal numbers.
The teacher explained the difference between cardinal and ordinal numbers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ordinal: denoting position or order, not quantity, as in first, second, third
