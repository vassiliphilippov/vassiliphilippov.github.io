play

noun
movement or free space in a mechanism
Pronunciation: / pleɪ / (AmE), / pleɪ / (BrE)
the amount of movement or freedom to move in a part of a machine or system before it begins to function or change position

Examples

door hinge play
Check that there is minimal play in the door hinge to prevent rattling.
If the lever has any play, it might not operate the valve correctly.
There was too much play in the steering wheel, so it needed to be fixed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slack: the part of a rope, chain, or mechanism that is loose or not taut
