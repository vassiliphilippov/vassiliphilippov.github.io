distributary

noun
branch of a river that leaves the main stream
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɪb.jəˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪb.jʊ.tər.i / (BrE)
A branch of a river that flows away from the main stream and does not rejoin it, commonly found in deltas.

Examples

distributary river branch
As the river approached the sea, it split into several distributary channels.
Salinity levels in the distributary can vary greatly depending on the season.
The Nile Delta is famous for its many distributaries spreading across the landscape.

Related Terms

  • antonym – tributary: a river or stream that flows into a larger river
