empiricist

noun
believer in knowledge from experience
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈpɪrɪsɪst / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪst / (BrE)
A philosopher or thinker who believes that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience.

Examples

famous empiricist philosopher
As an empiricist, she argued that all theories must be tested through observation and experiment.
Many scientists adopt an empiricist approach, trusting evidence gathered by the senses.
The famous philosopher John Locke is often regarded as a leading empiricist.

  • antonym – rationalist: a philosopher who emphasizes reason over experience
