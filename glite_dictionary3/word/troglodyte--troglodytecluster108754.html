troglodyte

noun
person who lives in a cave
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɑːɡləˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɒɡ.lə.daɪt / (BrE)
a person who lives in a cave, especially as a member of a prehistoric or primitive people; sometimes used figuratively to describe someone regarded as old-fashioned or reclusive

Examples

prehistoric troglodyte
He called his boss a troglodyte for refusing to use modern technology at work.
In some remote regions, troglodytes still dwell in cave houses as their ancestors did centuries ago.
The archaeologist explained that the ancient village was once inhabited by troglodytes who carved homes into the cliffs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hermit: a person living in solitude, often for religious reasons
  • synonym – recluse: a person who lives a solitary life and tends to avoid others
