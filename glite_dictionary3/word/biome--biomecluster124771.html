biome

noun
large ecological community with characteristic climate and plants
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.oʊm / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.əʊm / (BrE)
a large natural community of plants and animals occupying a major habitat, characterized by its dominant plant life and prevailing climate

Examples

forest biome
Deserts are a type of biome characterized by low rainfall and sparse vegetation.
Scientists study how climate change is affecting each biome differently.
The Amazon rainforest is a biome with an incredible diversity of species.
