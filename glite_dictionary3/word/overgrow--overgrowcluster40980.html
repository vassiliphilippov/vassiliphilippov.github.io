to overgrow

verb
grow across or spread over
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to grow over or cover something, especially with plants or vegetation spreading uncontrollably

Examples

plants overgrow ruins
Ancient ruins were almost completely overgrown by moss and ferns.
If left unchecked, weeds will overgrow the vegetable garden by midsummer.
The backyard fence was quickly overgrown with flowering vines.
If left unchecked, bamboo will quickly overgrow the entire backyard.
The old garden began to overgrow with weeds after months of neglect.
The path to the river had overgrown so much that it was hardly visible anymore.
