to shear

verb
to be deformed by shearing force
Pronunciation: / ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
to become deformed as a result of forces that cause layers or particles to slide past each other, especially in response to a shearing strain

Examples

to shear rock layers
During an earthquake, geological layers may shear along fault lines.
If the force is too great, the bolt will shear rather than bend.
When subjected to extreme pressure, the metal can shear and lose its original shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deform: to change shape, especially by being subjected to force
