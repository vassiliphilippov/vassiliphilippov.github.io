entomophilous

adjective
pollinated by insects (of plants)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.təˈmɑː.fə.ləs / (AmE), / ˌɛn.təˈmɒ.fɪ.ləs / (BrE)
(of flowering plants) pollinated by insects, especially in reference to the means of pollination.

Examples

entomophilous flowers
Many orchids are entomophilous, relying exclusively on bees for pollination.
The entomophilous flowers attract butterflies with their bright colors and sweet nectar.
Unlike wind-pollinated grasses, entomophilous species depend on insects to transfer their pollen.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anemophilous: pollinated by wind (of plants)
  • antonym – zoophilous: pollinated by animals other than insects (of plants)
