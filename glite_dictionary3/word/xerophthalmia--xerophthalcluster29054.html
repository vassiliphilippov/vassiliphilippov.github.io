xerophthalmia

noun
dryness of the eyes (medical condition)
Pronunciation: / zɪrˌɑfˈθælmiə / (AmE), / ˌzɪərɒfˈθalmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by abnormal dryness of the conjunctiva and cornea, often due to vitamin A deficiency

Examples

xerophthalmia from vitamin A deficiency
Public health programs aim to reduce xerophthalmia by providing vitamin A supplements.
The doctor diagnosed the child with xerophthalmia caused by severe vitamin A deficiency.
Untreated xerophthalmia can lead to serious eye complications and even blindness.
