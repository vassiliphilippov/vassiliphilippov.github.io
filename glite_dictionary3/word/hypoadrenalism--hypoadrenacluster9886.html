hypoadrenalism

noun
underactivity of the adrenal cortex
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊəˈdriːnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊəˈdriːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by decreased function of the cortex of the adrenal gland, often characterized by anemia, weakness, and darkening of the skin.

Examples

diagnose hypoadrenalism symptoms
Darkening of the skin can be a classic symptom of hypoadrenalism.
The patient was diagnosed with hypoadrenalism after several months of unexplained fatigue and weight loss.
Treatment for hypoadrenalism typically involves hormone replacement therapy.
