to focus

verb
become clear or come into focus
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to become clear or sharp, or to make something clear or sharp (such as an image or attention)

Examples

to focus clearly
As the fog lifted, the distant mountains gradually focused in the morning light.
It took a few seconds for the camera lens to focus after adjusting the settings.
The image on the screen focused suddenly, revealing the details of the painting.
He tried to focus his emotions and remain calm during the interview.
She needed to focus her thoughts before responding to the difficult question.
The group discussion helped focus everyone's ideas on finding a solution.

Related Terms

  • antonym – blur: to become less clear or less distinct
  • synonym – concentrate: to direct one's attention or effort towards a particular activity, subject, or problem
