enculturation

noun
adoption of a culture's behaviors and norms
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the gradual process by which an individual learns and adopts the values, customs, and behaviors of a particular culture, often beginning in childhood

Examples

enculturation of children
Immigrants sometimes experience multiple stages of enculturation as they adapt to a new society.
Teachers play an important role in the enculturation of students into the values of their community.
The process of enculturation begins in early childhood and continues throughout life.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acculturation: the process of cultural change and psychological change that results following meeting between cultures
