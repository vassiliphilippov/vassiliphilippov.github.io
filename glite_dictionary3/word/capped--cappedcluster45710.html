capped

adjective
having a cap or top covering
Pronunciation: / kæpt / (AmE), / kapt / (BrE)
covered on top as if with a cap or crown, often used with a modifier to indicate the type of covering

Examples

snow-capped mountains
At dawn, cloud-capped hills appeared mysterious and enchanting.
She admired the red-capped mushrooms growing at the edge of the forest.
The city's skyline was dominated by snow-capped peaks shimmering in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – uncapped: not covered with a cap or top
