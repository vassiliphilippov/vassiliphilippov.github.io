to fossilise

verb
convert into a fossil
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːsəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɒsɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert or be converted into a fossil; to become preserved in the form of a fossil

Examples

to fossilise ancient bones
Ancient plants fossilised over millions of years beneath layers of sediment.
If bones are quickly buried, they may fossilise instead of decaying.
Some trilobites fossilised so well that their delicate features are still visible today.

Related Terms

  • synonym – petrify: to change organic matter into a stony substance; to turn to stone
