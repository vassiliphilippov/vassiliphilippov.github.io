flow

noun
the rate at which fluid moves
Pronunciation: / floʊ / (AmE), / fləʊ / (BrE)
the steady and continuous movement or supply of something, such as a liquid, gas, people, things, information, or ideas; or to move or progress freely and smoothly like a stream

Examples

steady flow of water
A steady flow of water is crucial for the operation of the turbine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – current: a flow, especially in figurative senses like current of thought
  • antonym – ebb: the movement of the tide out to sea; decline or decrease
  • synonym – rate: a measure of how quickly something happens
  • synonym – stream: a continuous flow of liquid, air, or gas
