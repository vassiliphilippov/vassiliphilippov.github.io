dead

noun
the most intense or quietest part of a period
Pronunciation: / dɛd / (AmE), / dɛd / (BrE)
the period when something, especially cold or silence, is most intense or complete, often used in expressions like 'the dead of night' or 'the dead of winter'

Examples

the dead of night
During the dead of winter, the village lay silent under a blanket of snow.
It is dangerous to swim in the lake during the dead of winter, when the ice is thinnest.
She woke up in the dead of night to a strange noise outside.
