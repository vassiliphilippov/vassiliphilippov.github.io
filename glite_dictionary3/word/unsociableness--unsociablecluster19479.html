unsociableness

noun
quality of being unsociable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsoʊʃəblnɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsəʊʃəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unsociable; a tendency to avoid social interaction or companionship

Examples

his unsociableness at parties
His unsociableness made it difficult for him to make new friends at college.
Some people interpret unsociableness as rudeness, but it often stems from shyness.
The writer's unsociableness was mistaken for arrogance by many of her peers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sociability: the quality of being sociable; a tendency to seek or enjoy companionship
