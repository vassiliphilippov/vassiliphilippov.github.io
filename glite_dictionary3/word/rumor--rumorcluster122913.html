rumor

noun
unverified information passed between people
Pronunciation: / ˈruː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈruː.mə / (BrE)
information or a story that is passed from person to person, often of uncertain or doubtful truth.

Examples

spread a rumor
Is there any truth to the rumor we heard last night?
She denied the rumor that she was leaving the company.
The rumor about the new manager spread quickly through the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fact: a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym – gossip: casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people
