diestrous

adjective
in a period of reproductive inactivity
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɛstrəs / (AmE), / daɪˈɛstrəs / (BrE)
(of certain animals) in a phase of sexual inactivity between periods of estrus during the breeding season; having more than one estrous cycle per breeding season.

Examples

diestrous female animals
Researchers noted that the wolf pack's females were all diestrous during late autumn.
The mare was examined during her diestrous period to monitor reproductive health.
Veterinarians can identify the diestrous phase through hormonal changes and behavioral signs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anestrous: when an animal is not having any reproductive cycles
  • antonym – estrous: in a period of sexual receptivity
