Hunkpapa

noun
a division of the Teton Sioux people
Pronunciation: / hʌŋˈpɑːpə / (AmE), / hʌŋˈpɑːpə / (BrE)
A member of a division of the Teton Sioux people, originally living in the western Dakotas, known for resisting white expansion into the northern Great Plains.

Hunkpapa Lakota people
Many Hunkpapa descendants continue to live in the Dakotas today.

The Hunkpapa played a major role in the Battle of the Little Bighorn.
