spiritualism

noun
belief in communication with spirits
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that the spirits of dead people can communicate with the living, especially through a medium

Examples

spiritualism and seances
After losing her father, she became interested in spiritualism and attended séances hoping to make contact.
Many people seek comfort and answers through spiritualism, believing they can communicate with loved ones who have passed away.
The rise of spiritualism in the 19th century led to a surge in mediums and psychic investigators.
