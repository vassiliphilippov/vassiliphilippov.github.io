seriousness

noun
quality of causing fear or distress
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of a situation or event that causes fear, distress, or concern.

Examples

seriousness of illness
Doctors explained the seriousness of the patient's condition to the family.
We failed to realize the seriousness of the environmental crisis until it was almost too late.
When she saw the seriousness of the accident, she called for help immediately.

  • synonym – severity: the condition of being very bad, serious, or dangerous
