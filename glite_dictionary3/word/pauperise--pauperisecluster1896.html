to pauperise

verb
to make extremely poor
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔːpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɔːpəraɪz / (BrE)
to reduce someone or something to extreme poverty; to make a person or group very poor

Examples

to pauperise a country
Many argued that the harsh reforms would only pauperise the nation further.
The collapse of the local industry threatened to pauperise entire communities.
Unfair taxation policies can quickly pauperise small farmers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – enrich: to make someone wealthy or richer
  • synonym – impoverish: to make poor; to reduce to poverty
