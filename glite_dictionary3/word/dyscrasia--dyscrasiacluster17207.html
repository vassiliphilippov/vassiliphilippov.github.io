dyscrasia

noun
abnormal bodily condition (medical)
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkreɪziə / (AmE), / dɪsˈkreɪzɪə / (BrE)
A pathological or abnormal condition of the body, especially marked by an imbalance or abnormality in the constitution of the blood or bodily fluids.

Examples

blood dyscrasia
Certain medications may lead to blood dyscrasia as a side effect, requiring careful monitoring.
Her doctors were concerned about an underlying dyscrasia when she showed persistent abnormal blood test results.
The patient was diagnosed with a rare dyscrasia that affected the composition of his blood.
