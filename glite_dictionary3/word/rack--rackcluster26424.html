to rack

verb
draw off liquid from sediment
Pronunciation: / ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
to draw off (wine or other liquid) from the lees or sediment, usually into another container

Examples

to rack wine
After fermenting, the winemaker will rack the wine into clean barrels to separate it from the sediment.
It's important to rack the cider carefully to avoid disturbing the lees at the bottom.
They racked the port several times during aging to improve its clarity.
