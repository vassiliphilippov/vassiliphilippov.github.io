to resort

verb
turn to as a solution, often as a last option
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːt / (BrE)
to turn to and adopt a course of action, especially one that may be undesirable, as a means of achieving something or solving a problem

Examples

to resort to violence
Due to the storm, we may have to resort to using candles for light.
She promised not to resort to lying, no matter the circumstances.
When negotiations failed, the team had to resort to legal action.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fall back on: to rely on or use something as a backup when needed
  • synonym – turn to: to seek help, advice, or a solution from someone or something
