to DJ

verb
perform as a disc jockey
Pronunciation: / diː ˈdʒeɪ / (AmE), / diː ˈdʒeɪ / (BrE)
to act as a disc jockey by selecting, mixing, and playing recorded music for an audience, often at events or on radio.

Examples

to DJ a club night
He loves to DJ at local clubs on Saturday nights.
Several famous musicians have DJed at that festival over the years.
She started DJing at college parties before moving to larger venues.

  • synonym – disc jockey: a person who plays recorded music for an audience
