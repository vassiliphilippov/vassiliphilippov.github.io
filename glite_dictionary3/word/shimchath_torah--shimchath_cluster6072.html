Shimchath Torah

noun
Jewish festival marking Torah reading completion
Pronunciation: / ʃɪmˌxɑt ˈtɔrə / (AmE), / ʃɪmˌxɑt ˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
A Jewish festival celebrated on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri, marking the completion and recommencement of the annual cycle of Torah readings.

Examples

Shimchath Torah celebration
During Shimchath Torah, it is customary to read the last passage of the Torah and immediately begin again from Genesis.
Many families celebrate Shimchath Torah by dancing with the Torah scrolls at the synagogue.
The festive atmosphere of Shimchath Torah brings the entire congregation together in joyful celebration.
