to rusticate

verb
temporarily suspend from a university (Britain)
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(Britain, historical) To suspend temporarily from a college or university, especially as a punishment.

Examples

to rusticate a student
After being caught cheating on his exams, the university decided to rusticate him for a term.
If you break the rules again, the college may have no choice but to rusticate you.
Several students were rusticated for their involvement in the protest.
