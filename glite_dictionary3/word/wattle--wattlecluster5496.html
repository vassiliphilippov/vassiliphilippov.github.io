wattle

noun
Australasian tree yielding slender branches
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑt.əl / (AmE), / ˈwɒt.əl / (BrE)
Any of various Australasian trees, especially acacias, that produce slender stems or branches used for making fences, shelters, or similar structures.

Examples

Australian wattle tree
Many traditional Australian shelters were constructed using local wattle trees.
Some wattle species have become important in land restoration projects due to their fast growth.
The farmer cut several wattle poles to repair the old stockyard fence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acacia: genus of trees and shrubs often called wattles; many yield suitable poles
