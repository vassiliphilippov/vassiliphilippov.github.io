sterile

adjective
free from microorganisms (medical and hygiene)
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.aɪl / (BrE)
completely free from living microorganisms, especially harmful bacteria or viruses; using or relating to methods that keep something free from pathogens

Examples

sterile equipment
All surgical equipment must be sterile before use on a patient.
She wore gloves and a mask to keep everything as sterile as possible during the procedure.
The laboratory maintains a sterile environment to prevent contamination of experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aseptic: free from contamination caused by harmful bacteria, viruses, or other microorganisms
  • antonym – contaminated: having been made impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
