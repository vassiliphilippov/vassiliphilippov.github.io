to plant

verb
put seeds or plants in the ground
Pronunciation: / plænt / (AmE), / plɑːnt / (BrE)
to put seeds, plants, or other living things (like fish) into the ground or water so they can grow

Examples

to plant seeds
Every spring, we plant vegetables in our backyard garden.
If you plant the seeds too deep, they may not sprout.
She helped her grandfather plant strawberries along the fence.
Environmentalists regularly plant fish in local lakes and streams to boost native populations.
The conservation team will plant thousands of young trout in the river next spring.
They planted several species of fish to help restore the ecosystem after the drought.
