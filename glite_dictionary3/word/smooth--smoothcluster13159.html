to smooth

verb
make smooth or smoother
Pronunciation: / smuð / (AmE), / smuːð / (BrE)
to make a surface or object smooth or shinier, especially by rubbing or pressing

Examples

to smooth the fabric
After painting, he began smoothing the walls for a perfect finish.
He used sandpaper to smooth the rough edges of the table.
She gently smoothed the wrinkles from the fabric with her hand.
He smoothed his shoes with a cloth before the ceremony.
She smoothed the silverware until it gleamed in the light.
The artisan carefully smoothed the wooden table to give it a polished finish.

  • antonym – roughen: make or become rough
  • synonym – shine: to make something bright or glossy by rubbing
