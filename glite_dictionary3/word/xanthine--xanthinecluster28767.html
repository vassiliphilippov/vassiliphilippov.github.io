xanthine

noun
chemical compound; nucleic acid metabolite
Pronunciation: / ˈzænθiːn / (AmE), / ˈzanθiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, crystalline compound produced by the metabolism of nucleic acids, serving as a precursor of uric acid and found in various body tissues and fluids

Examples
