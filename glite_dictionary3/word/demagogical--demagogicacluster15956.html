demagogical

adjective
Like or relating to a demagogue
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛməˈɡɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdɛməˈɡɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a demagogue; appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than rational argument.

Examples

demagogical speech
Critics accused the leader of using demagogical language to manipulate public opinion.
Some articles decried the demagogical rhetoric dominating the campaign debates.
The candidate's demagogical tactics appealed to voters' fears rather than their hopes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – demagogic: relating to a demagogue; manipulative or inflammatory
