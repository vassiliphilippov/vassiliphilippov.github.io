profiteer

noun
person who makes excessive profits
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːfɪˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌprɒfɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who makes excessive profits, especially by taking advantage of shortages or crises

Examples

war profiteer
During the crisis, several profiteers were caught selling essential medicines at outrageous prices.
He was labeled a profiteer after accumulating wealth by hoarding and reselling fuel during the drought.
The government introduced strict laws to punish profiteers exploiting food shortages.

Related Terms

  • antonym – humanitarian: a person who seeks to promote human welfare, often contrasted with profiteer in crisis situations
  • synonym – speculator: someone who engages in risky financial transactions to make quick, large profits
