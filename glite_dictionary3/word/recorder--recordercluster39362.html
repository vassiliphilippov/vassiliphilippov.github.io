recorder

noun
a woodwind instrument with finger holes
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a tubular woodwind instrument with eight finger holes and a fipple mouthpiece, played by blowing into one end and covering the holes to produce different notes

Examples

play the recorder
He brought his wooden recorder to the Renaissance fair.
She learned to play the recorder in her elementary school music class.
The recorder is often used in early music ensembles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flute: a woodwind instrument played by blowing across a hole
