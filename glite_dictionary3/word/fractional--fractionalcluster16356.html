fractional

adjective
of coins or currency: less than basic unit
Pronunciation: / ˈfrækʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl / (BrE)
(of money) constituting a division or an aliquot part of the basic monetary unit; relating to coins or currency worth less than the standard unit.

Examples

fractional currency
Collectors often seek rare fractional notes issued during times of financial crisis.
In many countries, fractional coins like nickels and dimes are used for small transactions.
The U.S. government once issued fractional currency to make change during coin shortages.

  • synonym – divisional: relating to coins that are fractions of a monetary unit
