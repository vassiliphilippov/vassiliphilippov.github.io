dialyzer

noun
medical device for separating substances in dialysis
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪəlʌɪzə / (BrE)
a medical device that separates substances in a solution by allowing only certain molecules to pass through a semipermeable membrane, typically used in dialysis treatment

Examples

hemodialysis dialyzer
After each treatment, the nurse carefully cleaned the dialyzer to ensure its effectiveness.
Modern dialyzers are designed to increase efficiency and patient comfort during dialysis sessions.
The dialyzer is crucial in filtering toxins from the blood of patients with kidney failure.
