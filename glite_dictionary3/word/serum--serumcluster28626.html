serum

noun
watery fluid from clotted blood
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəm / (BrE)
the clear, yellowish fluid that separates from blood when it clots, containing no blood cells or clotting proteins but rich in dissolved proteins

Examples

serum protein levels
A drop in serum protein levels can indicate certain health conditions.
Doctors checked the patient's serum for signs of infection.
The laboratory technician separated the serum from the blood sample for further testing.
