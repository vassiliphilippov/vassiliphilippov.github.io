silkwood

noun
tropical tree yielding silky fiber and edible fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlkˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌwʊd / (BrE)
A fast-growing tropical American evergreen tree with white flowers and fleshy edible fruit; its bark yields a silky fiber used for making ropes, and its wood is valued for making staves.

Examples

silkwood tree fruit
Local farmers appreciate the silkwood for both its edible fruit and useful timber.
She harvested silky fibers from a silkwood tree to weave baskets.
The bark of the silkwood is processed to make durable cordage.
