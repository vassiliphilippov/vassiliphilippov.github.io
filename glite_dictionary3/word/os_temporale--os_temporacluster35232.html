os temporale

noun
bone forming side of cranium; temporal bone
Pronunciation: / oʊs tɛmˈpɔreɪli / (AmE), / ɒs tɛmˈpɔːrəli / (BrE)
A thick bone forming the side of the human cranium and encasing the inner ear; also known as the temporal bone.

Examples

os temporale fracture
Fractures of the os temporale can result in hearing loss or dizziness.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the os temporale and its relation to the jaw joint.
The os temporale is crucial for protecting the organs of hearing and balance.
