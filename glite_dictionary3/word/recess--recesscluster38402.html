recess

noun
a break or pause from activity or work
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.ses /; / rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈses /; / ˈriː.ses / (BrE)
a period when an activity is temporarily stopped, especially a break during work, school, or official meetings

Examples

school recess
After hours of negotiations, the committee called a recess to rest and regroup.
She used the short recess between meetings to make a quick phone call.
The students eagerly waited for recess so they could play outside.
Congress went into recess for the summer holiday.
The court took a recess before announcing its verdict.
The meeting was called to order again after a brief recess.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adjournment: a formal suspension of a meeting or session to a later time
  • synonym – break: a short period of rest from work or activity
  • synonym – pause: a short stop in an activity
  • antonym – session: a period during which a meeting or activity is actively occurring
