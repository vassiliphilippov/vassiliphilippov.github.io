to fix

verb
illegally arrange outcome of event
Pronunciation: / fɪks / (AmE), / fɪks / (BrE)
to influence or arrange the outcome of an event, competition, or decision by dishonest or illegal means, usually to benefit oneself or others unfairly

Examples

to fix a match
He spent years in jail for fixing horse races.
The players were accused of trying to fix the football match.
There was an investigation into whether someone had fixed the outcome of the election.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rig: to dishonestly arrange the outcome of something, especially a contest or election
