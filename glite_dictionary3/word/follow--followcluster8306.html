to follow

verb
keep informed or up to date about
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːloʊ /; / ˈfɑloʊ /; / ˈfɑː.loʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ /; / ˈfɒl.əʊ / (BrE)
to keep informed about, observe, or track the progress or activity of someone or something, including watching or monitoring them

Examples

to follow the news
It's important to follow developments in your area of expertise.
Many people follow their favorite authors on social media for updates.
She likes to follow the latest news in environmental science.
I couldn't help but follow every move he made during the performance.

The detectives followed the suspect's actions from a distance.
It is illegal for private citizens to follow others without their consent.
The detective was assigned to follow the suspect after he left the hotel.
Undercover agents follow criminal networks to gather evidence for investigation.
