to pulp

verb
reduce to a soft, shapeless mass
Pronunciation: / pʌlp / (AmE), / pʌlp / (BrE)
to crush or remove the soft, moist part of something, making it into a shapeless mass

Examples

to pulp fruit
After blending, the fruit was completely pulped.
The chef pulped the tomatoes to make the sauce smooth.
They pulp wood to manufacture paper products.
After boiling the tomatoes, you should pulp them to make a smooth sauce.
It can be tricky to pulp a mango without wasting much of the fruit.
The machine is used to pulp oranges for juice production.
