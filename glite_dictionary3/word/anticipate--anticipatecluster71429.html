to anticipate

verb
to realize or expect in advance
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt /; / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt /; / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪt / (BrE)
to expect or prepare for something before it happens; to look forward to something

Examples

anticipate a problem
Economists anticipate an increase in inflation next year.
She could anticipate his reaction before he even spoke.
We must anticipate possible problems and prepare solutions in advance.
He anticipated hearing from his friend and checked his phone repeatedly.
Many students anticipate the results of the exam with nervousness.
She anticipated the party all week with growing excitement.
Many people anticipate a rise in fuel prices over the summer.
She could anticipate the outcome of the meeting from their earlier discussions.
The company did not anticipate such rapid growth this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – doubt: to feel uncertain or lack belief that something will happen
  • antonym – dread: to anticipate with fear or apprehension
  • synonym – expect: to regard something as likely to happen
  • synonym – predict: to say what will happen in the future
  • antonym – react: respond to something after it happens; antonym in the context of 'beforehand' vs 'after'
