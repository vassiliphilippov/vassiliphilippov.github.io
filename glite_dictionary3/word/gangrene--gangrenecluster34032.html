to gangrene

verb
(of tissue) undergo necrosis
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæŋˌɡrin / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋɡriːn / (BrE)
(of tissue) to undergo necrosis, typically caused by infection or lack of blood supply.

Examples

to gangrene quickly
Doctors warned that without prompt care, the tissue could gangrene rapidly.
If the wound is left untreated, it might gangrene and require amputation.
When a limb ceases to receive blood flow, it may begin to gangrene.
