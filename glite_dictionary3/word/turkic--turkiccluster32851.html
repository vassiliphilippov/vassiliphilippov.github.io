Turkic

noun
subfamily of related Central Asian languages
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrkɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkɪk / (BrE)
a group of closely related languages spoken in central and western Asia, including Turkish, Uzbek, and Kazakh, forming a subfamily of the Altaic language family

Examples

Turkic languages
Linguists have studied the common features of Turkic tongues for centuries.
The Turkic languages are spoken by millions of people across Eurasia.
Uzbek is one of several Turkic languages found in Central Asia.
