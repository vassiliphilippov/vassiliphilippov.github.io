standoff

noun
successful defensive stand or resistance
Pronunciation: / ˈstændˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈstændɒf / (BrE)
an act of resisting or repelling an attack, especially by successfully maintaining a defensive position

Examples

standoff with police
After several hours, the standoff between protesters and police ended without violence.
The soldiers held a standoff at the bridge, preventing enemy forces from advancing.
Their defensive strategy resulted in a standoff, stopping the opposition's attack cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defense: the act of protecting against attack
  • antonym – retreat: an act of withdrawing from an attack or confrontation
