to gee

verb
(of animals) turn to the right
Pronunciation: / dʒiː /; / dʒi / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
to command or cause a horse or other draft animal to turn to the right

Examples

to gee a horse
The farmhand shouted for the mule to gee at the fork in the road.
When the lead dog refused to gee, the sled veered off course.
With a sharp command, she made the oxen gee into the next field.
At his command to gee, the team of mules obediently swung to the right.
She had to gee the horse to avoid the muddy path on the left.
The farmer shouted 'gee!' and the oxen turned right at the end of the field.
