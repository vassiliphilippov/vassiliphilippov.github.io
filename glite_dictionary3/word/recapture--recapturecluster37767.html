to recapture

verb
take back by force
Pronunciation: / riˈkæptʃər / (AmE), / riˈkæptʃə / (BrE)
to take back possession or control of something or someone, especially after losing it

Examples

to recapture a city
After a week-long siege, the army managed to recapture the city from the rebels.
Authorities worked tirelessly to recapture the escaped prisoners before they could cross the border.

After escaping from the zoo, the tiger was quickly recaptured by keepers.
The authorities were able to recapture the fugitive after a week-long search.
The team hopes to recapture the title they lost last year.
