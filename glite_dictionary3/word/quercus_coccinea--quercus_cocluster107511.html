Quercus coccinea

noun
species of oak tree with scarlet autumn leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs kɑkˈsiniə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs kɒkˈsiːnɪə / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous oak tree native to eastern North America, known for its deeply lobed leaves that turn bright scarlet in autumn.
