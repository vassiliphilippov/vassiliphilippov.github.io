hobgoblin

noun
mischievous supernatural creature in folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑbˌɡɑblɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒbˌɡɒblɪn / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, grotesque, and mischievous supernatural creature believed to cause trouble for humans.

Examples

a mischievous hobgoblin
Children believed that a hobgoblin lived in the woods behind the old house.
In the story, a mischievous hobgoblin played tricks on the villagers every night.
Many superstitions warn travelers to beware of the forest hobgoblin after dark.
