immunoelectrophoresis

noun
technique for separating antigens and antibodies
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəʊɪˌlɛktrəfəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊɪˌlɛktrəʊfɒˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
A laboratory technique that combines electrophoresis and immunodiffusion to separate and identify antigens and antibodies in a sample.

Examples

immunoelectrophoresis test results
Immunoelectrophoresis is often employed to detect specific antibodies in a serum sample.
