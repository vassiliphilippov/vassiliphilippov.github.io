to obturate

verb
block or close a passage
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːb.tʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.tjʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
to block or close a passage or opening, especially to prevent the flow of something through it

Examples

to obturate a pipe
Engineers designed the valve to obturate the flow of gas in an emergency.
Mucus may obturate the nasal passage during a severe cold.
The dentist used a special material to obturate the root canal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – obstruct: to block or hinder a passage; prevent movement through
  • antonym – unblock: to remove a blockage from a passage
