cirque

noun
steep, semicircular mountain basin
Pronunciation: / sɜrk / (AmE), / sɜːk / (BrE)
a steep, bowl-shaped, semicircular basin with steep walls found on a mountain, often formed by glacial erosion and sometimes containing a lake

Examples

glacial cirque
Each cirque in the range held a small, sparkling tarn.
Scientists studied the formation of the cirque to understand the region's glacial history.
The hikers reached the rim of the cirque and admired the view of the glacial lake below.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrie: a Scottish term for a cirque or glacially-eroded basin
