to foliate

verb
number the pages (of a book)
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.li.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.li.eɪt / (BrE)
to number the pages of a book or manuscript

Examples

to foliate a manuscript
Before restoration, experts foliated the entire collection for ease of study.
It is important to foliate each sheet so researchers can reference individual pages.
The archivist began to foliate the old manuscript for proper cataloguing.
