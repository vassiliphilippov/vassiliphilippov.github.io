automatism

noun
automatic, unconscious behavior
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑːməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒmətɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the performance of actions without conscious thought or intention, especially as seen in certain medical or psychological conditions such as epilepsy

Examples

medical automatism episode
During his seizure, the patient exhibited automatism such as lip-smacking and fidgeting.
Some lawyers claim automatism as a defense when a crime is committed during sleepwalking.
The neurologist explained that automatism can be a symptom of certain types of epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – automaticity: the quality of being performed automatically, often through learning or habit
