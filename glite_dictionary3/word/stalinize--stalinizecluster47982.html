to stalinize

verb
make conform to Stalin's policies
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑː.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɑː.lɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to transform (a country, political system, or institution) in accordance with the policies and practices of Joseph Stalin, especially involving authoritarian control, repression, and centralization

Examples

to stalinize a country
Following the revolution, the new leadership began to stalinize the party's structure and policies.
Historians debate how quickly the Eastern Bloc countries were stalinized after World War II.
The government attempted to stalinize education by centralizing control and suppressing independent thought.
