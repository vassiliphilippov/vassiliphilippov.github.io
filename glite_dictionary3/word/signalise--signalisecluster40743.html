to signalise

verb
make something conspicuous or notable
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz /; / ˈsɪɡnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something noticeable or draw special attention to it

Examples

to signalise an achievement
He hoped his generous donation would signalise his commitment to the cause.
The award signalised her achievements in the field of science.
The organization decided to signalise the anniversary with a special celebration.
Her courage was signalised in the official report for all to see.
It is important to signalise the main changes in the policy document.
The author signalises the theme of loss repeatedly throughout the novel.

  • antonym – obscure: to make less visible or noticeable
  • synonym – signalize: make conspicuous or noteworthy; US spelling
