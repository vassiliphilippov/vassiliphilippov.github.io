to die

verb
become indifferent to something
Pronunciation: / daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
to become indifferent to or no longer affected by (often used in a spiritual or figurative sense)

Examples

die to old dreams
After years of disappointment, she finally died to her ambitions for fame.
He chose to die to worldly pleasures and devote himself to spiritual growth.
Some people die to material concerns and find deeper meaning in simple living.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignore: to pay no attention to; disregard
  • synonym – renounce: to formally give up or reject something
