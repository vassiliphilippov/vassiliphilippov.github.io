to trip

verb
cause to activate; trigger (device or reaction)
Pronunciation: / trɪp / (AmE), / trɪp / (BrE)
to cause (a mechanism or device) to activate or operate, often by triggering a switch or reaction

Examples

to trip a breaker
A power surge can trip the circuit breaker and shut off the electricity.
If you trip the alarm, security will be called immediately.
Press the red button to trip the emergency shutoff system.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deactivate: to make (something, such as a device or system) inactive or ineffective
  • synonym – trigger: to cause a device, process, or action to begin
