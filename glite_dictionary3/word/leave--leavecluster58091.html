to leave

verb
entrust to someone
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liːv / (BrE)
to put something or someone into the care or responsibility of another person, including by will after death

Examples

to leave money to family
I will leave my dog with my neighbor while I am on vacation.
Parents often leave their children in the care of relatives during emergencies.
She left her responsibilities to her assistant before going on leave.
After his death, their father left the house to his daughters.
He plans to leave everything to charity in his will.
My grandmother left me her pearls when she passed away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bequeath: to leave personal estate to someone by a will
  • synonym – entrust: to assign the responsibility for doing something to someone
  • antonym – inherit: to receive property from someone after their death
  • antonym – relinquish: to give up control of something
