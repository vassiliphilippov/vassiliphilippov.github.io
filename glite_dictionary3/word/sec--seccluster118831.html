SEC

noun
US agency regulating securities markets
Pronunciation: / ɛs i si / (AmE), / ɛs iː siː / (BrE)
an independent agency of the United States federal government responsible for enforcing federal securities laws and regulating the securities industry to protect investors.

Examples

the SEC investigates fraud
After the latest financial scandal, the SEC increased its oversight of investment firms.
Many investors look to the SEC for guidance on financial regulations.
The SEC brought charges against the company for insider trading.
