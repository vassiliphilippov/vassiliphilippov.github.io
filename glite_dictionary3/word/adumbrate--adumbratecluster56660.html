to adumbrate

verb
hint at; suggest partially
Pronunciation: / ˈædʌmˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈædʌmbreɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or disclose (something) partly or indirectly; to hint at or give a faint outline of

Examples

to adumbrate future plans
By mentioning recent events, she adumbrated a coming announcement without revealing the details.
His speech adumbrated the economic challenges that would soon emerge.
The author adumbrates the novel's tragic ending in the opening chapter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – imply: to indicate or suggest without being explicitly stated
  • synonym – insinuate: to suggest or hint (something) in an indirect way
