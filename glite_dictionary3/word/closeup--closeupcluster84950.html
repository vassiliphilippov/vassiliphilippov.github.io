closeup

noun
photograph taken at close range
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊs.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkləʊs.ʌp / (BrE)
A photograph or film shot taken at a very short distance from the subject, showing detail.

Examples

closeup of a face
Her new profile picture is a stunning closeup of her face.
The director decided to use a closeup to highlight the actor's emotional expression.
The magazine featured a closeup of the intricately designed wedding ring.
