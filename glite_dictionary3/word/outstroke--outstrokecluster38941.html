outstroke

noun
piston movement toward crankshaft
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
The movement of an engine piston toward the crankshaft during its cycle.

Examples

outstroke of piston
During each outstroke, the piston moves toward the crankshaft, completing part of the combustion cycle.
Excessive friction during the outstroke can lead to wear on the piston rings.
The engine's outstroke is carefully timed to maximize efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym – instroke: the stroke of an engine piston moving away from the crankshaft
