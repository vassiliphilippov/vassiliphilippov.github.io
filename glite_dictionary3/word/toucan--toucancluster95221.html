toucan

noun
large tropical bird with a big colorful beak
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːˌkæn / (AmE), / ˈtuːkən / (BrE)
A large tropical American bird with brightly colored plumage and a very large, thin-walled beak, known for eating fruit and living in trees.

Examples

colorful toucan bird
Children were fascinated by the toucan's enormous beak at the zoo.
Many species of toucan feed mainly on fruit, but also eat insects and eggs.
The brightly colored toucan perched high in the rainforest canopy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hornbill: large tropical bird with a long, curved bill, similar in appearance to toucans
