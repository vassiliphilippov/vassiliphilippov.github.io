oscillogram

noun
graph from an oscillograph
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪləˌɡræm / (AmE), / əˈsɪləˌɡram / (BrE)
A recording or graphical representation produced by an oscillograph, showing variations in electrical signals or other quantities over time.

Examples

oscillogram of voltage
After the experiment, the technician printed out an oscillogram of the voltage fluctuations.
Please compare these oscillograms to detect any irregular patterns in the signal.
The scientist analyzed the oscillogram to identify anomalies in the waveform.
