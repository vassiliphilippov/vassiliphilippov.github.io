pitch

noun
quality of sound perceived as high or low
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
the quality of a sound governed by the frequency of its vibrations, determining how high or low it is perceived

Examples

pitch of a note
Adjusting the frequency changes the pitch of the note produced.
Some animals can hear sounds at a pitch far beyond human capability.
The opera singer hit a remarkably high pitch during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tone: musical or vocal sound with reference to its pitch, quality, and strength
