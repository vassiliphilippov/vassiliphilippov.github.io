set

noun
descent of a heavenly body below the horizon
Pronunciation: / sɛt / (AmE), / sɛt / (BrE)
the descent of a celestial body below the horizon, especially the sun or moon

Examples

after set of sun
Photographers often wait until just after the set for unique lighting.
The hikers planned to return by the set of the moon.
We reached camp before the set of the sun.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rise: the act of a celestial body appearing above the horizon
  • synonym – sunset: the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon
