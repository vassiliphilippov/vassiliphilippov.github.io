cgs

noun
centimeter–gram–second measurement system
Pronunciation: / si ˌdʒi ˈɛs / (AmE), / siː dʒiː ˈes / (BrE)
a system of measurement based on centimeters, grams, and seconds, used especially in science before the widespread adoption of the SI system

Examples

Electromagnetic equations can appear much simpler when expressed in cgs units.
In older physics textbooks, the cgs system is commonly used for units of force.
