Hussite

noun
follower of John Huss's religious reforms
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhʌsaɪt / (BrE)
A follower of the religious movement led by John Huss in Bohemia during the early 15th century, advocating for church reforms.

Examples

a Hussite reformer
As a Hussite, he advocated for reform and criticized church corruption.
Many Hussites opposed certain doctrines of the Catholic Church in the fifteenth century.
The Hussite leaders played a crucial role in shaping Bohemian religious history.
