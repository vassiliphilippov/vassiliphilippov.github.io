simple

adjective
botany: undivided leaf
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl / (BrE)
(Botany) Describing a leaf that is undivided, i.e. not divided into leaflets or smaller subdivisions.

Examples

simple leaf
The rose bush has simple leaves that are oval in shape.
This plant is easily identified by its simple leaf structure, without any lobes or divisions.
Unlike maple trees, dogwoods often have simple leaves with smooth edges.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compound: (Botany) Describing a leaf that is divided into distinct leaflets.
