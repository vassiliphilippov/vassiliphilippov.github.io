to flick

verb
look quickly through pages
Pronunciation: / flɪk / (AmE), / flɪk / (BrE)
to look quickly through a book, magazine, or other written material by turning the pages lightly or rapidly

Examples

to flick through pages
As the bell rang, the students flicked through their notes one last time.
He likes to flick through old photo albums on rainy days.
She flicked through the magazine while waiting for her coffee.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leaf through: to turn the pages of a book or magazine quickly, looking at them casually
  • synonym – thumb through: to quickly look through the pages of a book, magazine, etc.
