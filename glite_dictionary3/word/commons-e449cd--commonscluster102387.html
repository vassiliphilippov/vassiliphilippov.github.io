commons

noun
shared digital/knowledge resources
Pronunciation: / c8k51d0m59nz / (AmE), / c8k52m59nz / (BrE)
a shared pool of digital or knowledge resources (such as datasets, software, texts, or media) that a community maintains and makes available under open licences for collective use and reuse.

Examples

digital commons
Researchers publish their datasets into the commons to ensure open access and reproducibility.
The university library curated a new digital commons where students share open textbooks and course materials.
Community volunteers maintain the software and metadata in the commons for everyone's benefit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – open access: free, immediate, online availability of scholarly outputs and resources without most copyright restrictions
  • antonym – private property: resources owned and controlled by individuals or corporations, typically restricted from communal use
