to reap

verb
obtain or receive as a result
Pronunciation: / riːp / (AmE), / riːp / (BrE)
to cut and gather crops (especially grain) from a field, or to receive something as a result of your actions or efforts

Examples

to reap the rewards
After years of hard work, she finally reaped the rewards of her dedication.
The company hopes to reap substantial profits from the new product line.
You only reap what you sow, so make wise choices.
After months of hard work, the company began to reap the benefits of its new strategy.
Farmers reap wheat in late summer, when the grain is fully ripe.
If you sow kindness, you will eventually reap rewards in return.
