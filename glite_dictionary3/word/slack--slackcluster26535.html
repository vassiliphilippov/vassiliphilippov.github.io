slack

adjective
(of water) moving slowly or barely moving
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
(of water or a tide) flowing with little speed or force, especially at the turning of the tide.

Examples

slack tide
At slack tide, the river seemed calm and almost motionless.
Fishing is often best during slack water, when the current slows before changing direction.
The boat waited for slack tide to safely pass through the narrow channel.

  • synonym – still: (of water) not flowing or moving
