tribune

noun
Roman official elected by plebeians
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪˌbjuːn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪ.bjuːn / (BrE)
In ancient Rome, an official elected by the plebeians to protect their interests and rights.

Examples

Roman tribune
As a tribune, he had the power to veto decisions harmful to the common people.
The tribune spoke passionately on behalf of the plebeians in the assembly.
The Roman Senate often clashed with the tribunes over policy matters.
