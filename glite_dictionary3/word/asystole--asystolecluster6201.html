asystole

noun
absence of heartbeat; heart stops
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪstoʊli / (AmE), / əˈsɪstəli / (BrE)
a condition in which the heart stops beating due to a failure of the ventricles to contract, resulting in the absence of a heartbeat and usually leading to death if not treated immediately

Examples

asystole cardiac arrest
Emergency responders are trained to recognize and treat asystole as a medical emergency.
If left untreated, asystole can quickly lead to brain damage or death.
The patient experienced asystole during surgery, requiring immediate resuscitation efforts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cardiac arrest: sudden cessation of heart activity
  • degree lower – ventricular fibrillation: irregular, rapid heart rhythm often preceding asystole
