frontier

noun
wilderness at country’s settled edge
Pronunciation: / frʌnˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪə / (BrE)
an area of land at the edge of a settled region, especially where it borders uninhabited wilderness or is being explored or developed

Examples

wild frontier land
During the 19th century, the American frontier moved steadily westward.
Life on the frontier was often harsh and unpredictable, demanding resilience from its inhabitants.
The settlers ventured into the frontier in search of new opportunities and land.

Related Terms

  • synonym – border: a line separating two political or geographical areas
  • degree lower – hinterland: the remote or less developed parts of a country
