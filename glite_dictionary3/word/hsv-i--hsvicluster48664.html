HSV-I

noun
herpesvirus causing oral herpes
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈviː ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈviː ˈwʌn / (BrE)
a herpesvirus (herpes simplex virus type 1) that typically causes oral herpes, such as cold sores around the mouth

Examples

HSV-I infection
HSV-I is the most common cause of cold sores around the lips and mouth.
Doctors diagnose oral herpes by detecting the presence of HSV-I in a patient's lesion.
Unlike HSV-2, HSV-I is more frequently transmitted by nonsexual contact.
