Phoradendron flavescens

noun
North American mistletoe species
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔːrəˈdɛndrən fləˈvɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌfɒrədɛnˈdrɒn fləˈvɛsəns / (BrE)
a North American species of mistletoe that grows on deciduous trees and is traditionally associated with Christmas in the United States; it can significantly weaken its host plant.

Examples

Phoradendron flavescens on trees
Phoradendron flavescens can weaken oak trees by parasitizing their branches.
During the holidays, many people hang Phoradendron flavescens in their homes as the American mistletoe.
Unlike European mistletoe, Phoradendron flavescens is native to North America and grows on deciduous trees.
