to dredge

verb
coat food with dry ingredient before cooking
Pronunciation: / drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
to lightly coat food with a dry ingredient such as flour or breadcrumbs before cooking

Examples

to dredge chicken pieces
Before frying, dredge the fish fillets in seasoned flour for a crispier texture.
The chef quickly dredged the cutlets in breadcrumbs before placing them in the pan.
You should always dredge the chicken in cornstarch to help the sauce stick better.
