buttressed

adjective
supported by buttresses or braces
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtrəst / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrɪst / (BrE)
supported or strengthened by means of buttresses or braces, especially in architecture.

Examples

buttressed wall
Her argument was buttressed by solid evidence and reliable sources.
The hilltop castle, buttressed against strong winds, stood for centuries.
The medieval cathedral was buttressed by towering stone supports along its walls.

  • antonym – unsupported: not given support or reinforcement; without props
