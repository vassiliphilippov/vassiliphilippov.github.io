to avianise

verb
modify microorganisms by chick embryo culture
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪviəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈeɪvɪənaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt or modify microorganisms by repeated culture in the developing chick embryo, typically to change their properties for research or vaccine production

Examples

to avianise a virus
By avianising the pathogen, scientists observed changes in its virulence.
Researchers avianised the microorganism through several passages in chick embryos.
The laboratory was able to avianise the influenza virus for vaccine development.
