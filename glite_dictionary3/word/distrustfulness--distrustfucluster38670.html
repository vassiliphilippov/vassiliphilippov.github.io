distrustfulness

noun
lack of trust; suspicion
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtrʌst.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrʌst.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unwilling to trust others; lack of trust or suspicion

Examples

deep distrustfulness
Her distrustfulness made it difficult for her to form close friendships.
The manager's distrustfulness led him to monitor his employees closely.
There was a general atmosphere of distrustfulness in the community after the scandal.
