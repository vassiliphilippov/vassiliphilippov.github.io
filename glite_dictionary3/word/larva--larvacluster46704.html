larva

noun
immature form of an animal before metamorphosis
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːrvə / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.və / (BrE)
An immature, free-living form of an animal that is distinctly different in form or structure from the adult and must undergo metamorphosis to reach maturity, especially in insects, amphibians, and some fish.

Examples

larva of a butterfly
Frog eggs hatch into a larva called a tadpole before turning into adult frogs.
Most insects go through a larva stage before becoming adults.
The caterpillar is the larva of a butterfly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – adult: the mature, fully developed stage of an animal
  • synonym – nymph: an immature form of some insects which resembles the adult but is smaller and lacks fully developed wings or reproductive organs
  • degree higher – pupa: the life stage after larva in some insects, typically non-feeding and undergoing transformation to adult
