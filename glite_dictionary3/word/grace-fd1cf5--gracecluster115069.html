grace

noun
extra time given beyond a deadline
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪs / (BrE)
an additional period of time allowed after a deadline to fulfill an obligation without penalty

Examples

grace period
Her utility company granted her a grace so she could pay her bill later.
The bank offered a three-month grace after the loan's due date due to hardship.
You have a ten-day grace on your rent payment before late fees apply.
