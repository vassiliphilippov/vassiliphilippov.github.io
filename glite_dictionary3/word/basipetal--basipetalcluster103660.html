basipetal

adjective
opening from apex toward base (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪsəˈpɛtəl / (AmE), / ˌbeɪsɪˈpiːtəl / (BrE)
(of leaves or flowers) developing or opening in succession from the apex toward the base.

Examples

basipetal flower growth
Researchers observed basipetal development in the inflorescence under laboratory conditions.
Some plants exhibit basipetal maturation, where older leaves are found at the base of the stem.
The flower buds of this species open in a basipetal sequence, starting from the tip and moving downward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acropetal: (of growth or development) proceeding from the base toward the apex
