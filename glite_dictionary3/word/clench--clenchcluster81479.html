to clench

verb
squeeze or close tightly
Pronunciation: / klɛntʃ / (AmE), / klɛntʃ / (BrE)
to close or squeeze something tightly, especially with your hands, teeth, or muscles

Examples

clench your fist
He clenched his jaw angrily during the argument.
She tried to clench her fists to hide the trembling.
You could see her hands clenching in frustration as she waited.
During the tense moment, he found himself clenching his jaw involuntarily.
He clenched his fists when he heard the bad news.
She clenched the steering wheel tightly as the car skidded on the ice.
