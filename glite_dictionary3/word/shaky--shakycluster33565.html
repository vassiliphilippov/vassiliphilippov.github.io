shaky

adjective
not secure; likely to fail
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪki / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪki / (BrE)
not firm or secure; likely to fail or experience problems

Examples

shaky future
After the scandal, the company's future looked shaky at best.
His finances have been shaky since he lost his job.
They repaired their shaky alliance to present a united front.

Related Terms

  • synonym – insecure: not securely fixed; not stable
  • antonym – solid: stable and secure; not likely to fall or fail
  • synonym – unstable: not stable; likely to change or fall apart
