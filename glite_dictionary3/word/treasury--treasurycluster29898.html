Treasury

noun
U.S. government debt security
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
A government bond or other negotiable debt security issued by the United States government, which guarantees timely payment of interest and principal.

Examples

Treasury bond investment
Many investors consider Treasury bonds to be among the safest investments available.
She diversified her portfolio with stocks and Treasurys to reduce risk.
The yield on a ten-year Treasury rose sharply after the economic report.
