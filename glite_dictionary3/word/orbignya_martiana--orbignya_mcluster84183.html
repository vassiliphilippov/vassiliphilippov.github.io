Orbignya martiana

noun
Brazilian palm with valuable nuts and oil
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈbɪnjə mɑrˈtiːənə / (AmE), / ɔːˈbɪnjə mɑːˈtɪənə / (BrE)
A tall feather palm native to northern Brazil, known for its hard-shelled nuts that produce valuable oil and a type of vegetable ivory.

Examples

Orbignya martiana palm tree
Botanists studied the Orbignya martiana and its contribution to local ecosystems in northern Brazil.
The durable seeds of Orbignya martiana are often used for carving decorative items.
The nuts of the Orbignya martiana are harvested to extract both oil and vegetable ivory.
