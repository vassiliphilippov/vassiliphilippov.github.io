recession

noun
withdrawal of clergy and choir after a service
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the formal withdrawal of the clergy and choir from the chancel to the vestry at the end of a church service

Examples

church recession
After the recession, the congregation quietly left the church.
The choir sang a hymn during the recession at the close of the service.
The organist played a solemn piece for the recession of the clergy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – procession: the movement of clergy and choir into the chancel at the beginning of a service
