neuroma

noun
a tumor of nerve tissue
Pronunciation: / nuˈroʊmə / (AmE), / njʊəˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
A benign growth or tumor originating from nerve tissue, typically composed of nerve cells and fibers.

Examples

neuroma in the foot
A neuroma can cause pain, tingling, or numbness depending on its location.
Morton's neuroma is a common condition affecting the nerves between the toes.
The patient was diagnosed with a neuroma in her left foot.
