plate

noun
cut of beef from lower forequarter
Pronunciation: / pleɪt / (AmE), / pleɪt / (BrE)
the cut of meat from the thin, lower section of the forequarter of an animal, especially beef

Examples

beef plate cut
Chuck, rib, and plate are all cuts from the forequarter of beef.
Short ribs are often cut from the plate section of the cow.
The butcher trimmed the plate before slicing it for stewing meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brisket: cut of meat from the breast or lower chest of beef or veal
  • degree higher – flank: cut of meat from the side of the animal, below the loin and rib
