to reprieve

verb
temporarily relieve; postpone punishment or difficulty
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpriv / (AmE), / rɪˈpriːv / (BrE)
to postpone or temporarily relieve someone from something unpleasant, especially from punishment or an expected difficulty

Examples

to reprieve the team
Heavy rain reprieved the farmers for a week from the worst of the drought.
The governor decided to reprieve the prisoner, granting her a few more days of life.
The judge may reprieve the sentence until new evidence is considered.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delay: to put off to a later time
  • synonym – postpone: to arrange for something to take place at a later date
