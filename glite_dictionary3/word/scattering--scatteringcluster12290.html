scattering

noun
a small number spread out randomly
Pronunciation: / ˈskæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A small number of things spread irregularly or at random over an area or among other things.

Examples

scattering of applause
A scattering of applause came from the back of the room.
He noticed a scattering of freckles across her nose.
There was only a scattering of houses along the remote country road.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abundance: a very large quantity of something
  • synonym – sprinkling: small number of things scattered or distributed
