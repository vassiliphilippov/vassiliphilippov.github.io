Homoptera

noun
former order of insects (aphids, cicadas, etc.)
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑp.tə.rə / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒp.tə.rə / (BrE)
a former order of insects that includes aphids, cicadas, leafhoppers, whiteflies, and related species, now usually divided among other groups such as Hemiptera

Examples

Cicadas are a well-known group within the Homoptera, recognized by their loud mating calls.
Many scientists now classify Homoptera as part of the order Hemiptera.
The Homoptera include pests such as aphids and whiteflies, which can damage crops.
