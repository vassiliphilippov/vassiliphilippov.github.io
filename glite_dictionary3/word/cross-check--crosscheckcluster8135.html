to cross-check

verb
verify by comparison with other sources
Pronunciation: / krɔs ˈtʃɛk / (AmE), / krɒs ˈtʃɛk / (BrE)
to verify information by comparing it with information from different sources

Examples

to cross-check facts
Always cross-check the numbers in your report to avoid errors.
Journalists often cross-check their facts with multiple sources before publishing an article.
She cross-checked the details with another witness to ensure the statement was accurate.
