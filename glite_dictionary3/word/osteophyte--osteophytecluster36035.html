osteophyte

noun
abnormal bony outgrowth
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːstiəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒstiəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
a small abnormal bony outgrowth, typically forming on the edge of a bone, often associated with joint disease

Examples

osteophyte on a joint
An osteophyte can cause pain by pressing on surrounding tissues or nerves.
Surgical removal of an osteophyte may be necessary if it interferes with joint movement.
The X-ray revealed a large osteophyte on the patient's knee joint.

  • synonym – bone spur: another term for an osteophyte, a bony outgrowth
