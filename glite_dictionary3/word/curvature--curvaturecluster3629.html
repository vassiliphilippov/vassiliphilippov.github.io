curvature

noun
rate a curve changes direction
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝː.vɚ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.və.tʃə / (BrE)
the rate at which the direction of a curve changes at a particular point; a measure of how sharply a curve bends

Examples

calculate curvature mathematically
Mathematicians use calculus to precisely calculate the curvature at any given point on a curve.
The curvature of the mirror allowed it to focus light to a single point.
The curvature of the road made it difficult for large vehicles to navigate safely.

  • antonym – straightness: the quality or state of being straight
