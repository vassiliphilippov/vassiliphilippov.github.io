adjacency

noun
being next to or touching
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒeɪsənsi / (AmE), / əˈdʒeɪsənsi / (BrE)
the state of being next to or very near something else; proximity or closeness, especially so close as to be touching

Examples

adjacency of rooms
Care was taken to ensure the adjacency of rooms for proper emergency access.
In mathematics, adjacency is important when analyzing graphs or networks.
The adjacency of the two buildings made it easy for employees to move between them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – distance: the amount of space between two points
  • synonym – proximity: nearness in space, time, or relationship
