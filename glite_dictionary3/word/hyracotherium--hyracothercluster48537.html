Hyracotherium

proper noun
extinct early horse ancestor
Pronunciation: / haɪˌrækəˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / hʌɪˌrakəˈθɪərɪəm / (BrE)
an extinct genus of small hoofed mammals, formerly known as Eohippus, considered the earliest ancestor of modern horses

Examples

Hyracotherium fossil discovery
Scientists consider Hyracotherium to be one of the earliest ancestors of modern horses.
The fossil remains of Hyracotherium have been discovered in both Europe and North America.
The small size and multi-toed feet of Hyracotherium distinguish it from present-day horses.
