Arawak

noun
Indigenous people of South America and the Caribbean
Pronunciation: / ˈærəˌwæk / (AmE), / ˈærəwæk / (BrE)
a member of a group of Indigenous peoples native to northeastern South America and the Caribbean

Examples

an Arawak person
Artifacts from the Arawak have been found across the Caribbean islands.
Historians believe the Arawak were among the first peoples encountered by Columbus.
Many modern descendants of the Arawak still live in Guyana and Suriname.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Carib: another Indigenous group native to the Caribbean, often associated with the Arawak
