widow

noun
woman whose husband has died
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
A woman whose husband has died, especially one who has not remarried.

Examples

elderly widow
After her husband's passing, she became a widow at the age of 34.
Many charities offer support to the spouses and children of widows.
The widow wore black as a sign of mourning during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym – widower: a man whose wife has died and has not remarried
