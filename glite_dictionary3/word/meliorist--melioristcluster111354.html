meliorist

noun
someone who advocates improvement or reform
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːliərɪst / (AmE), / ˈmiːlɪərɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that the world can be made better by human effort and advocates reform.

Examples

committed meliorist for change
As a committed meliorist, she constantly seeks ways to improve education in her community.
During the debate, the meliorist argued for gradual, thoughtful reform rather than radical upheaval.
Some critics see the meliorist approach as too optimistic about the pace of social change.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conservative: a person who resists change and prefers traditional views
  • synonym – reformer: a person who makes changes to something in order to improve it
