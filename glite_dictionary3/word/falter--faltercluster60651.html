to falter

verb
become weak or unsteady
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔltər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
to become weaker or less certain; to lose strength, confidence, or determination

Examples

falter in confidence
Her confidence began to falter when she faced unexpected challenges.
If their resolve falters now, the project may never be completed.
The runner started to falter as exhaustion set in during the final lap.

Related Terms

  • antonym – strengthen: to become stronger or more firm
  • synonym – waver: to go back and forth between choices or opinions, to become unsteady
