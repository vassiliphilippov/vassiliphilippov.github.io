enol

noun
organic compound with a hydroxyl and double bond
Pronunciation: / ˈiːˌnɑl / (AmE), / ˈiːˌnɒl / (BrE)
an organic compound containing a hydroxyl group directly attached to a carbon atom that is also double-bonded to another carbon atom

Examples

an enol compound
Chemists often study enol intermediates because of their unique reactivity.
The enol content affects the stability of some pharmaceutical drugs.
The enol form of the compound can easily convert to a keto form under certain conditions.
