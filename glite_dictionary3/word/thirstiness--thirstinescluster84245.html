thirstiness

noun
dryness; lack of moisture (esp. from drought)
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜrstinɪs / (AmE), / ˈθɜːstinɪs / (BrE)
a condition of dryness due to a lack of moisture, especially resulting from a long-term absence of rainfall

Examples

thirstiness of soil
After months without rain, the thirstiness of the soil became apparent.
Deserts are defined by their extreme thirstiness and sparse vegetation.
The thirstiness of the region makes farming nearly impossible without irrigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aridity: extreme dryness, especially of climate
