blinder

noun
side vision restriction for horses
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndə / (BrE)
A piece of leather or similar material attached to a horse's harness to restrict its side vision and prevent distraction.

Examples

blinder on a horse
The coachman adjusted the blinder on the left side to keep the horse focused on the road.
The stable hand made sure both blinders were securely attached to the harness before the parade.
Wearing a blinder helps prevent the horse from being startled by movements at its sides.
