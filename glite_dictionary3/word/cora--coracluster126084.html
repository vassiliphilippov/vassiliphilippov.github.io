Cora

proper noun
Greek goddess, queen of the underworld
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the daughter of Zeus and Demeter, who became queen of the underworld after her abduction by Pluto; also known as Persephone and identified with the Roman Proserpina.

Cora in Greek myth
Artists have often depicted Cora gathering flowers before her abduction.

The story of Cora explains the changing of the seasons in ancient Greek mythology.
