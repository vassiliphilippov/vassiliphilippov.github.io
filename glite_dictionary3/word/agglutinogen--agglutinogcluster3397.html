agglutinogen

noun
antigen causing agglutinin production
Pronunciation: / əˈɡluːtənˌoʊdʒən / (AmE), / əˈɡluːtɪnədʒən / (BrE)
a substance, typically an antigen, that stimulates the production of specific agglutinins (antibodies) in the body

Examples

agglutinogen on cells
Blood type is determined by the presence or absence of specific agglutinogens on the surface of red blood cells.
Researchers identified a new agglutinogen involved in the rare blood group.
The introduction of a foreign agglutinogen can trigger an immune response in the patient.
