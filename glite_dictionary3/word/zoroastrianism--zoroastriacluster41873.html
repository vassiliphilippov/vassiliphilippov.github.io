Zoroastrianism

noun
ancient Persian religion
Pronunciation: / ˌzɔːroʊˈæstriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌzɒrəʊˈæstriənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an ancient Persian religion founded by Zoroaster in the 6th century BC, emphasizing the duality of good and evil and set forth in the Zend-Avesta
