pole

noun
either of Earth's axis intersection points
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
either of the two points on the Earth's surface where its axis of rotation meets the surface (the North Pole and the South Pole), or the corresponding points in the sky

Examples

North Pole point
Earth's rotation is defined by the line connecting its two poles.
Scientists set up a research station near the South pole.
The North pole is covered by sea ice throughout the year.
At the pole, the sun can remain above or below the horizon for months at a time.
Explorers have made dangerous journeys to reach the pole under extreme conditions.
The Earth's magnetic field is strongest near each geographic pole.

Related Terms

  • synonym – axis: an imaginary line about which a body rotates
  • antonym – equator: the imaginary line midway between the poles dividing the Earth into northern and southern hemispheres
