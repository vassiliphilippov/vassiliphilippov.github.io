to overleap

verb
defeat oneself by going too far
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈliːp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈliːp / (BrE)
(formal) To defeat or harm oneself by going too far or exceeding limits.

Examples

to overleap the limit
By ignoring advice and pushing ahead, she overleapt and ended up failing.
In his ambition, the politician tried to overleap, ultimately losing the support of his party.
One must be careful not to overleap and ruin a success by excessive risk-taking.
