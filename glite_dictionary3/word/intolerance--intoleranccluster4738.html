intolerance

noun
unwillingness to accept differing views or beliefs
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɑːlɚəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒl(ə)rəns / (BrE)
Unwillingness to accept or respect people with different opinions, beliefs, or practices.

Examples

intolerance of differences
His intolerance for opposing viewpoints made discussions with him very difficult.
Social media can sometimes fuel intolerance instead of promoting understanding.
The organization was founded in response to religious intolerance in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bigotry: intolerance toward those who hold different opinions
  • antonym – tolerance: willingness to accept different opinions or behaviors
