viscousness

noun
resistance of a liquid to flow
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪskəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪskəsnɪs / (BrE)
the property of a liquid that resists flow due to internal friction; viscosity

Examples

high viscousness liquid
The viscousness of honey makes it flow very slowly, especially when cold.
Water has much less viscousness than syrup, which is why it spreads out easily.
When testing lubricants, engineers often measure their viscousness under different temperatures.
