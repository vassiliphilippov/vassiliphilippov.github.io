megalith

noun
large stone in prehistoric monument
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəlɪθ / (BrE)
A large stone that forms part of a prehistoric monument or structure, especially in western Europe.

Examples

ancient megalith monument
A single megalith can weigh several tons and may have required many people to transport.
Archaeologists believe the megalith was erected during the Neolithic period.
Stonehenge is perhaps the most famous site built with megaliths in Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – monolith: large single upright block of stone, often shaped or used as a monument
