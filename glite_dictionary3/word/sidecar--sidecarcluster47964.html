sidecar

noun
auxiliary container for applications
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdkɑː / (BrE)
(in computing) a small auxiliary software component or container that runs alongside a primary application to provide supporting services such as logging, monitoring, proxying, or data collection, commonly used in microservices and container orchestration.

Examples

sidecar container service
In Kubernetes, the team deployed a sidecar to collect logs and forward them to the ELK stack.
The security architect recommended adding sidecars to handle encryption for each microservice independently.
During testing, they noticed the sidecar caused higher resource use and adjusted CPU limits accordingly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – container: a lightweight, standalone software package that includes an application and its dependencies
