reflector

noun
optical telescope with a concave mirror
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛktər / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktə / (BrE)
an optical telescope that uses a large concave mirror to form an image, which is then magnified by an eyepiece

Examples

reflector telescope mirror
He assembled his first homemade reflector in his garage at the age of fifteen.
Many observatories prefer a reflector because of its superior image quality and lower cost for large apertures.
The astronomer inspected the night sky through his reflector, searching for new stars.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reflecting telescope: telescope using a mirrored system to gather and focus light
  • antonym – refractor: optical telescope that uses lenses to form images
