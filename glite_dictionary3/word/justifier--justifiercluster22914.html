justifier

noun
person who argues in support of something
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who supports or defends a policy, action, or institution by providing reasons or arguments

Examples

justifier of a policy
As a prominent justifier of government intervention, she often debated with her critics on television.
He does not want to be seen merely as a justifier of unpopular corporate practices.
Some historians have acted as justifiers of colonialism in their writings.
