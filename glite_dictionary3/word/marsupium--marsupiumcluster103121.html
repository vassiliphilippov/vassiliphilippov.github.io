marsupium

noun
pouch in marsupials for carrying young
Pronunciation: / mɑrˈsuːpiəm / (AmE), / mɑːˈsuːpiəm / (BrE)
an external abdominal pouch in most marsupials where the newborn offspring are kept and suckled

Examples

marsupium of a kangaroo
Scientists observed the opossum using its marsupium to carry and protect its young.
The kangaroo's marsupium provides a safe place for its joey to develop after birth.
Unlike placental mammals, marsupials nurse their young inside the marsupium rather than a womb.
