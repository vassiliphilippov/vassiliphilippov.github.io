redwood

noun
giant Californian coniferous tree
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
either of two very large coniferous trees native to California, especially the coastal redwood (Sequoia sempervirens) and the giant sequoia (Sequoiadendron giganteum), known for their great height and reddish bark.

Examples

ancient redwood tree
A single redwood can live for more than a thousand years.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining stands of redwood trees.
The ancient redwood forests of California attract visitors from around the world.
