retread

noun
remolded used tire
Pronunciation: / riˈtrɛd / (AmE), / ˈriːtrɛd / (BrE)
a used tire that has been remolded to replace the worn tread, extending its service life

Examples

retread tire
After examining the damage, she decided to install a retread on the rear axle.
Many trucks use retreads to reduce their overall operating expenses.
The mechanic recommended a retread as a cost-effective alternative to buying a new tire.

  • antonym – new tire: a tire that has never been used before
