complicity

noun
involvement as an accomplice in wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / kəmˈplɪsɪti / (BrE)
the state of being involved with others in an illegal activity or wrongdoing

Examples

complicity in a crime
She denied any complicity in the cheating scandal at the university.
The investigation revealed his complicity in the fraudulent scheme.
The judge considered the defendant’s complicity when delivering the sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accomplice: a person who helps another commit a crime
  • antonym – innocence: the state of not being involved in wrongdoing
