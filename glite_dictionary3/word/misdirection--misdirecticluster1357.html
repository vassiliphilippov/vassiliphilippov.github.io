misdirection

noun
incorrect legal instruction to a jury
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsdəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
an incorrect instruction or guidance given to a jury by a judge, especially one that may affect the verdict

Examples

jury misdirection
Justice was compromised due to misdirection affecting the jury’s verdict.
The court found that misdirection had occurred during the summing up to the jury.
The defense appealed the case on the grounds of misdirection by the trial judge.
