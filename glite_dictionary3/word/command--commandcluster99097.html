to command

verb
overlook from a high position
Pronunciation: / kəˈmænd / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
(of a place or position) to have a view over an area from a higher position; to overlook.

Examples

command a view
From the balcony, you command a sweeping view of the city below.
The fortress commands the entire valley, offering a strategic advantage.
The lighthouse commands the bay, making it visible from miles away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overlook: to have a view over; to look down upon from above
