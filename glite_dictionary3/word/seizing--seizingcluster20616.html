seizing

noun
the act of gripping firmly
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gripping or holding something firmly, especially with the hands or similar appendages

Examples

seizing a hand
Her seizing of his hand was so tight that it left marks.
Octopuses use the rapid seizing of prey with their tentacles to catch food.
The sudden seizing of the steering wheel prevented the car from veering off the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grasping: the act of taking and holding something firmly
  • antonym – letting go: the act of releasing or ceasing to hold something
