dehiscent

adjective
splitting open at maturity to release seeds
Pronunciation: / dɪˈhɪsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈhɪsənt / (BrE)
(of fruits, seed pods, or anthers) splitting open spontaneously at maturity to release their contents, especially seeds.

Examples

dehiscent seed pod
Many legumes produce dehiscent pods that split open to scatter seeds.
Some wildflowers have dehiscent capsules that help them propagate widely.
The dehiscent fruit releases its seeds when it matures in late summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – indehiscent: not splitting open to release seeds at maturity
