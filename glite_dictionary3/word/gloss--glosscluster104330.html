to gloss

verb
explain or translate words in a text
Pronunciation: / ɡlɑs / (AmE), / ɡlɒs / (BrE)
to provide an explanation or translation for a word or phrase, especially in the margin or between the lines of a text

Examples

to gloss a word
She glossed the poem in the margins to help her classmates understand the archaic vocabulary.
The linguist glossed each line of the ancient manuscript for his students.
When studying Old English, the professor glosses challenging words in the text.
In your assignment, you are required to gloss any Latin phrases you include.
The teacher glossed each unfamiliar term with a brief translation above the line.
This edition glosses many archaic expressions so modern readers can understand the text.

Related Terms

  • synonym – annotate: to add explanatory notes or comments to a text
  • synonym – explain: to make something clear or easy to understand
