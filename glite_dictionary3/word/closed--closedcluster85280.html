closed

adjective
requiring union membership (of workplace)
Pronunciation: / kloʊzd / (AmE), / kləʊzd / (BrE)
(of a workplace or shop) requiring employees to be members of a labor union as a condition of employment.

Examples

closed shop for workers
Labor laws sometimes restrict the creation of closed workplaces.
Many workers appreciated the security of employment in a closed environment.
The factory became a closed shop, only hiring members of the union.

Related Terms

  • antonym – open: allowing employment regardless of union membership
