wrinkled

adjective
having lines or folds; marked by wrinkles
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪŋkəld / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkəld / (BrE)
having small lines or folds, usually caused by creasing, aging, or not being smoothed out (used for skin, fabric, clothing, etc.)

Examples

wrinkled shirt
His shirt looked very wrinkled after a long day of travel.
She tried to iron the wrinkled napkins before setting the table.
The old man's wrinkled hands shook as he wrote his letter.
His pants were wrinkled because he forgot to iron them before leaving.
She wore a wrinkled shirt to the meeting after pulling it from the laundry basket.
The wrinkled bed sheets made the room look untidy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – creased: having lines or folds, especially from being pressed or crumpled
  • antonym – smooth: having an even and regular surface; without wrinkles
