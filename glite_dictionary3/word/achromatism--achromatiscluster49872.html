achromatism

noun
the absence of chromatic color
Pronunciation: / əˈkroʊmətɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmətɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or state of being without color; the absence of chromatic color, especially in optics or vision

Examples

achromatism in vision
Scientists study achromatism to understand visual perception in animals with color blindness.
The achromatism of the glass ensures a true-to-life view through the telescope.
The designer chose elements that emphasized achromatism for a minimalist aesthetic.
