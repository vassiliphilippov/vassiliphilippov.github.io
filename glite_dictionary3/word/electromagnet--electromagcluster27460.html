electromagnet

noun
temporary magnet by electric current
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛk.troʊˌmæg.nət / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəʊˈmæɡ.nət / (BrE)
A magnet created by passing an electric current through a coil of wire surrounding an iron core, making the core temporarily magnetic.

Examples

powerful electromagnet
He built a simple electromagnet with a battery, some copper wire, and a nail for his science project.
The crane uses a powerful electromagnet to lift heavy metal objects in the scrapyard.
When the switch is turned on, the electromagnet attracts the iron lever and completes the circuit.

  • synonym – magnet: an object that produces a magnetic field
