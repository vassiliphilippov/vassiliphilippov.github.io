to forge

verb
shape or form with effort, often metalwork
Pronunciation: / fɔrdʒ / (AmE), / fɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to shape or create something with effort, especially by heating and hammering metal, or to develop ideas, plans, or objects

Examples

to forge a new path
Innovators must forge new paths in their industries to succeed.
She forged a sculpture from scrap metal in her studio.
The blacksmith carefully forged a sword from the hot iron.
The artist managed to forge a sculpture from scrap metal she found in the yard.
They had to forge a shelter using branches and leaves when they got lost in the forest.
Under pressure, he was able to forge a plan with very limited resources.
After much debate and discussion, the committee managed to forge a new strategy for the project.
Great leaders are often those who forge visionary plans in times of crisis.
The company needed to forge a compelling explanation for their sudden growth.
