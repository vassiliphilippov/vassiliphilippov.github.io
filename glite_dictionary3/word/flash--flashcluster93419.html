flash

noun
gaudy outward display
Pronunciation: / flæʃ / (AmE), / flæʃ / (BrE)
a display of conspicuous and often tasteless showiness intended to attract attention

Examples

a bit of flash
He prefers a bit of flash to stand out from the crowd.
His outfit was all about flash, with gold chains and glittering shoes.
The party was filled with flash but lacked genuine warmth.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modesty: the quality of being modest, not showy or ostentatious
  • synonym – showiness: the quality of being bright, flashy, or ostentatious
