to transpirate

verb
pass through pores as a gas or vapor
Pronunciation: / trænˈspaɪr.eɪt / (AmE), / trænˈspaɪə.reɪt / (BrE)
(of a gas or vapor) to pass through the pores or interstices of a substance or tissue; to be released by transpiration.

Examples

to transpirate water vapor
Certain chemicals are able to transpirate from soil into the surrounding air.
The process allows oxygen to transpirate through semipermeable membranes in laboratory experiments.
Water vapor can transpirate through the leaf tissues into the atmosphere.
