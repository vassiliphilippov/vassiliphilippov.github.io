binding

adjective
legally obligatory; enforceable
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an agreement or promise) having legal force; legally obligatory or enforceable.

Examples

binding legal contract
A binding contract ensures the terms are legally enforceable.
The agreement is binding on both parties once they sign it.
This offer is binding until the expiration date specified in the document.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enforceable: able to be imposed or carried out by law
  • antonym – nonbinding: not having legal force; not obligatory
