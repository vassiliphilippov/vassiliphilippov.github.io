take-up

noun
act of tightening, absorbing, or reeling in
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈteɪk ʌp / (BrE)
the action or process of tightening, absorbing, or drawing in something, such as slack in a rope or absorption of a substance

Examples

take-up of slack
Proper take-up of thread is essential for smooth sewing machine operation.
Rapid take-up of water was observed in the dry soil during the first rainfall.
The take-up of slack in the cable ensured the load was secure.
