Dias

proper noun
Portuguese explorer, rounded Cape of Good Hope
Pronunciation: / diˈɑs, ˈdaɪəs / (AmE), / diˈas, ˈdaɪəs / (BrE)
Bartolomeu Dias (c. 1450–1500), Portuguese explorer who was the first European to round the Cape of Good Hope in 1488, opening the sea route from Europe to Asia.

Examples

Dias rounded Africa
Dias successfully navigated around the Cape of Good Hope, paving the way for future voyages to India.
Many historians credit Dias with opening new opportunities for European trade with Asia.
The achievements of Dias are still studied in schools as major milestones in the Age of Exploration.
