heuristic

noun
problem-solving rule or method
Pronunciation: / hjuˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / hjʊəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
a rule or method for problem-solving, learning, or discovery that employs practical, experience-based techniques rather than guaranteed logical procedures

Examples

simple heuristic
A simple heuristic can help you choose the shortest route to your destination.
Doctors often apply heuristics when diagnosing patients with complex symptoms.
The programmer used a heuristic to speed up the search process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – algorithm: a step-by-step procedure for calculations or problem-solving
  • synonym – rule of thumb: a broadly accurate principle based on experience or practice
