conclave

noun
secret or private meeting
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnˌkleɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kleɪv / (BrE)
a secret or private meeting, especially one where important decisions are made

Examples

secret conclave
After the crisis, party leaders called a conclave to plan their next move.
The board of directors held a conclave to choose the company's new CEO.
The scientists met in a secret conclave to discuss their groundbreaking discovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assembly: a group gathered together for a common purpose
  • synonym – meeting: an assembly of people, especially for discussion or decision-making
  • antonym – public meeting: an open gathering accessible to anyone, not secret
