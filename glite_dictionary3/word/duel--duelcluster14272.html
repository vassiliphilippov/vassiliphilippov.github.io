duel

noun
formal fight to settle a dispute
Pronunciation: / ˈduːəl / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəl / (BrE)
a formal fight between two people, often with weapons, arranged in advance to settle a dispute or point of honor

Examples

duel with pistols
Challenging someone to a duel was once considered an honorable way to resolve disputes.
The outcome of the duel changed the course of their families’ histories.
The two generals agreed to settle their differences in a duel at dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym – combat: a fight, especially during a war
  • degree lower – contest: a struggle or fight to win something
  • synonym – duelist: a person who takes part in a duel
