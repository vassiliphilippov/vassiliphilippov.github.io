isoagglutinogen

noun
antigen causing isoagglutinin production
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊəˈɡluːtɪnədʒən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊəˈɡluːtɪnədʒən / (BrE)
an antigen present in some individuals of the same species that can stimulate the production of, or react with, an isoagglutinin, especially in the context of blood transfusion or immunology

Examples

isoagglutinogen on cells
Blood typing relies on the detection of isoagglutinogen antigens on the surface of red blood cells.
Researchers studied how different isoagglutinogen variants affect blood transfusion compatibility.
The presence of an isoagglutinogen in donor blood can cause a serious immune reaction in a recipient with incompatible blood type.
