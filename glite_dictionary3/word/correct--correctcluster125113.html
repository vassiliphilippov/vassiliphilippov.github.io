to correct

verb
(of markets/prices) to fall in value after adjustment
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
(of a market or prices) to fall in value, usually after a period of rise, due to adjustment to realistic levels.

Examples

to correct prices
After months of rapid growth, the market finally began to correct this quarter.
Analysts predicted that the housing market would correct after years of steady increases.
The sudden surge in stock prices was followed by a sharp correcting phase.
