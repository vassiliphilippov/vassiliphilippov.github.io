antbird

noun
South American bird that feeds on ants
Pronunciation: / ˈæntˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈæntˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A dull-colored bird native to South America, known for feeding on ants, often by following army ant swarms.

Examples

antbird follows ants
Scientists observed a rare antbird following a column of ants through the jungle.
Several species of antbird depend on army ants to flush insects from the leaf litter.
The antbird perched quietly near the forest floor, watching for movement.
