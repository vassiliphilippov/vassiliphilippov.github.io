to overbalance

verb
to outweigh in importance or effect
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to outweigh or be more significant than something else; to have greater importance or effect

Examples

overbalance good intentions
His desire for revenge overbalanced his sense of justice.
Sometimes, practical concerns overbalance our emotional responses in difficult decisions.
The risks of the investment overbalance the potential rewards, making it too dangerous.

Related Terms

  • antonym – counterbalance: to act against something with equal force or importance
  • synonym – outweigh: to be more important or significant than something else
