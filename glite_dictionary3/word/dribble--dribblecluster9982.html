to dribble

verb
move a ball with repeated touches
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪbəl / (BrE)
to move (a ball) forward by repeatedly tapping or bouncing it with hands or feet, especially in sports such as basketball or soccer

Examples

to dribble a basketball
As he approached the goal, he continued to dribble until he found an opening.
She skillfully dribbled the ball past two defenders before taking a shot.
You must learn to dribble quickly to be effective in basketball.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pass: to move the ball to a teammate by throwing or kicking it (in sports)
  • antonym – shoot: to attempt to score a goal or point by sending the ball toward the target
