progressive

noun
supporter of social and political reform
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports social reform, progress, and the protection of civil liberties, especially in politics

Examples

a progressive politician
Many progressives advocate for stronger environmental policies and climate change action.
She describes herself as a progressive who supports universal healthcare and education reform.
The new mayor is considered a leading progressive in the city’s political landscape.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conservative: a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
  • synonym – liberal: a person open to new behavior or opinions, often favoring civil liberties and reform
