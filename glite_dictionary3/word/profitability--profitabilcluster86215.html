profitability

noun
capacity to produce a profit
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːfɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌprɒfɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which a business or activity produces a profit or financial gain

Examples

profitability of a company
Investors closely examine a firm's profitability before making decisions.
Rising costs have affected the profitability of many small businesses.
The company is reviewing its operations to improve overall profitability.

  • antonym – loss: when a business spends more than it earns
  • synonym – profit: money gained in business or by selling things
