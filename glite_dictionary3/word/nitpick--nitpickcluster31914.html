to nitpick

verb
criticize minor details
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪtˌpɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɪtˌpɪk / (BrE)
to criticize or find fault with small or unimportant details, often in a petty or excessive way

Examples

to nitpick small mistakes
Rather than appreciate my effort, he started to nitpick about my handwriting.
She tends to nitpick when stressed, pointing out even the tiniest flaws in our work.
Try not to nitpick every detail of the report; focus on the main issues instead.
He likes to nitpick about grammar mistakes in every email he receives.
Instead of helping, she would rather nitpick every small flaw in my presentation.
Stop trying to nitpick my cooking; just enjoy the meal.
