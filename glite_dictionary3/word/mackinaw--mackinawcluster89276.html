mackinaw

noun
thick, plaid woolen cloth
Pronunciation: / ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmækɪˌnɔː / (BrE)
A heavy woolen cloth that is thickly napped and felted, often featuring a plaid pattern, used especially for coats and blankets.

Examples

mackinaw woolen cloth
He wore a red-and-black mackinaw to keep warm in the snowy woods.
She spread her grandfather's old mackinaw coat over the sled.
The thick mackinaw blanket was perfect for cold winter nights at the cabin.
