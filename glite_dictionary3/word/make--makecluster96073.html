to make

verb
appoint to a position or role
Pronunciation: / meɪk / (AmE), / meɪk / (BrE)
to appoint or regard someone as having a particular role, quality, or status; to make a judgment or consider

Examples

make someone captain
He was made captain of the team for his leadership skills.
The board made her the new CEO after a unanimous vote.
They made him chairman of the committee last year.
She always makes a big deal of minor issues.
The press tried to make it a scandal, but there was no truth to the story.
They tried to make him a scapegoat for the team's failure.
After hearing the arguments, she tried to make sense of the situation.
Can you make any conclusions from this data?
It's difficult to make an accurate estimate without more information.
