to spawn

verb
to lay eggs (fish, amphibians)
Pronunciation: / spɔn / (AmE), / spɔːn / (BrE)
(of fish, amphibians, etc.) to lay eggs in water.

Examples

to spawn in water
Every spring, the trout swim upstream to spawn in the shallow gravel beds.
If water conditions are right, goldfish may spawn several times in a season.
Scientists observed hundreds of frogs returning to the pond to spawn after the first rainfall.
