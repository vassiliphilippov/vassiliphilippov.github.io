Horatio Alger

proper noun
American author of rags-to-riches stories
Pronunciation: / həˈreɪʃioʊ ˈældʒər / (AmE), / hɒˈreɪʃioʊ ˈældʒə / (BrE)
(1832–1899) A United States author of popular adventure stories for boys, best known for tales in which virtue and hard work enable poor young men to rise from poverty.

Examples

Horatio Alger story
Critics sometimes dismiss such upward mobility stories as mere Horatio Alger myths.
His story is a real-life Horatio Alger tale, rising from poverty to great success.
Many young Americans grew up reading Horatio Alger novels, which inspired them to believe in the value of hard work.
