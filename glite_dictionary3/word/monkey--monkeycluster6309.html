to monkey

verb
secretly tamper with or alter
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
to tamper with or fiddle with something, often in a secretive, playful, or ineffective way

Examples

to monkey with controls
Don't monkey with the settings on my computer.
He was accused of monkeying with the company accounts.
The mechanic warned them not to monkey around under the hood without proper training.
He was supposed to fix the car, but he just monkeyed with it for hours.
The kids spent the afternoon monkeying around in the backyard instead of doing their chores.
They've been monkeying around in the garage, probably building something silly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fiddle: to adjust, manipulate, or alter something, especially dishonestly
  • synonym – idle: to spend time doing nothing or little of importance
  • synonym – tamper: to interfere with something in order to cause damage or make unauthorized alterations
  • antonym – work: to engage in productive activity
