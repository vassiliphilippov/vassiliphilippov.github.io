Ojibway

noun
member of an Algonquian Indigenous people
Pronunciation: / oʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (BrE)
A member of an Indigenous Algonquian people living mainly around the northern Great Lakes region, especially in Canada and the northern United States.

Examples

an Ojibway elder
Many Ojibway still maintain their traditional languages and customs today.
She is proud of her heritage as an Ojibway woman.
The Ojibway lived in the forests and near the lakes of North America.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Chippewa: an alternative name for the Ojibway people, primarily in the United States
