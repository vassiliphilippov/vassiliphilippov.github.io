to condense

verb
remove water to concentrate
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛns / (AmE), / kənˈdɛns / (BrE)
to remove water or moisture from a substance, usually to make it thicker or more concentrated

Examples

to condense milk
The recipe says to condense the soup before adding it to the casserole.
They condensed the tomato sauce by boiling off the excess water.
To make fudge, you often condense the milk by simmering it slowly.
