pervasively

adverb
in a pervasive manner
Pronunciation: / pərˈveɪsɪvli / (AmE), / pəˈveɪsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that spreads widely throughout an area or a group of people; in a manner that is pervasive.

Examples

pervasively influence society
Corruption can act pervasively, affecting every level of government.
Digital technology is now integrated pervasively in everyday life.
The smell of fresh paint lingered pervasively throughout the house for days.
