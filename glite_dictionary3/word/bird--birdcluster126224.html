bird

noun
feathered, usually flying animal
Pronunciation: / bɝd / (AmE), / bɜːd / (BrE)
A warm-blooded, egg-laying vertebrate animal distinguished by feathers, wings, and a beak, and (typically) able to fly.

Examples

colorful bird
A hummingbird is the smallest species of bird found in North America.
Several colorful birds gathered at the feeder in the backyard.
The bird soared gracefully above the mountains, searching for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym – avian: relating to birds
  • antonym – mammal: warm-blooded vertebrate of a different class, not egg-laying, with hair or fur
