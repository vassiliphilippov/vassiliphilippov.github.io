mosaicism

noun
condition with genetically distinct cell populations
Pronunciation: / moʊˈzeɪ.sɪ.zəm / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A condition in which an organism has two or more populations of cells with different genetic makeup, resulting from mutations occurring after fertilization.

Examples

genetic mosaicism in cells
Because of mosaicism, two individuals with the same mutation may have different symptoms.
Recent research has revealed that mosaicism is more common than previously believed in human tissues.
The genetic disorder was traced to mosaicism detected in some of the patient's cells.
