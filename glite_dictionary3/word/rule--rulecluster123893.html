to rule

verb
govern; exercise authority
Pronunciation: / ruːl / (AmE), / ruːl / (BrE)
to govern, control, or have authority over people or a place

Examples

to rule a country
For many decades, the dictator ruled the small nation with an iron fist.
The queen ruled her country wisely and brought peace to her people.
Who will rule after the king steps down from the throne?
Despite efforts to diversify, one political party rules the region.
In the world of modern technology, innovation often rules over tradition.
Social media platforms rule the market for online advertising these days.

  • antonym – obey: to comply with the command, direction, or request of a person or law
  • synonym – predominate: to be the most common or important
  • synonym – reign: to hold royal office; rule as king or queen
