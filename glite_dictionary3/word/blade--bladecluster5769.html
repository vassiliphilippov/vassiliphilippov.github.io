blade

noun
thin, narrow piece (e.g., of lint, grass)
Pronunciation: / bleɪd / (AmE), / bleɪd / (BrE)
a thin, flat part of something, especially a body part like the shoulder blade or tongue, or a narrow piece resembling a blade of grass

Examples

blade of confetti
Each blade of confetti shimmered as it floated to the ground.
He brushed a blade of lint off his jacket before the meeting.
There was a single blade of straw stuck to her boot after the walk.
Anatomy students studied the structure of each blade in detail.
She felt a sharp pain near the blade of her tongue.
The surgeon carefully examined the blade of the patient's shoulder.

Related Terms

  • synonym – filament: a slender threadlike object or fiber
  • synonym – shoulder blade: the large, flat bone at the back of the shoulder
  • synonym – strand: a single thin length of something such as thread, fiber, or hair
