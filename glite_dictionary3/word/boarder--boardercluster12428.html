boarder

noun
person who forcibly boards a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə / (BrE)
a person who forcibly enters a ship, especially during an attack or hostile action

Examples

enemy boarder
Historical naval battles often involved hand-to-hand combat between boarders and defenders.
Pirates acted as boarders, climbing onto merchant vessels to plunder their cargo.
The crew prepared themselves to repel the boarders as enemy ships approached.
