to caramelize

verb
(of sugar) turn into caramel when heated
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəməˌlaɪz /; / ˈkærəˌməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærəməlaɪz /; / ˈkærəm(ə)laɪz / (BrE)
to heat sugar or foods with sugar until they turn brown and develop a sweet, rich flavor

Examples

to caramelize onions
As the onions cook slowly, they caramelize and develop a sweet, rich aroma.
If you heat sugar too long, it will caramelize and turn dark brown.
The chef allowed the sauce to caramelize for extra flavor.
If you caramelize the nuts, they will have a rich, toasty flavor.
The onions will slowly caramelize and become sweet after about twenty minutes.
To make the sauce, gently caramelize the sugar in a heavy-bottomed pan.
