paternalism

noun
controlling others for their supposed good
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɜrnəlɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or policy of people in authority restricting the freedom and responsibilities of those subordinate to them, supposedly in their best interest

Examples

paternalism in government
Historically, colonial powers often justified their control by claiming a duty of paternalism toward local populations.
Some critics argue that government welfare programs are a form of paternalism that undermines personal responsibility.
The company's paternalism was evident in its strict rules and oversight of employees' lives.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – authoritarianism: enforcement of strict obedience to authority
  • degree lower – maternalism: policy or practice of care in a motherly way
