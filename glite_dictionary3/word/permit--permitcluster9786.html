to permit

verb
allow; give permission
Pronunciation: / pərˈmɪt / (AmE), / pəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allow or give permission for something to happen

Examples

to permit entry
My parents will not permit me to stay out late on weekdays.
No food or drink is permitted inside the lecture hall.
The city recently permitted music events in public spaces.
Install drains to permit excess water to leave the area easily.
Leaving the window open permitted the cold air to come inside.
The new regulations permit companies to operate in this region.
Parents must permit their children to participate in the field trip.
The principal did not permit late entries after the exam began.
You are not permitted to smoke inside the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allow: to let someone do something or let something happen
  • antonym – forbid: to order someone not to do something; to refuse to allow
