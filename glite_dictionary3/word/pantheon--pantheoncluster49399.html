pantheon

noun
temple to all the gods (antiquity)
Pronunciation: / ˈpænθiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈpænθiən / (BrE)
(in antiquity) A temple dedicated to all the gods, especially in ancient Rome or Greece.

Examples

ancient pantheon temple
In antiquity, rulers would often commission a pantheon to honor the gods of their city.
The Pantheon in Rome is one of the best-preserved buildings from ancient times.
