IPV

noun
inactivated polio vaccine
Pronunciation: / aɪ pi vi / (AmE), / aɪ piː viː / (BrE)
a vaccine against poliomyelitis consisting of inactivated polio virus, administered by injection to induce immunity

Examples

IPV vaccine injection
Children in several countries now routinely receive the IPV as part of their immunization schedule.
The introduction of IPV has played a major role in the reduction of polio cases worldwide.
Unlike the oral vaccine, IPV poses no risk of vaccine-derived polio.

Related Terms

  • antonym – OPV: oral poliovirus vaccine, a live vaccine given orally
