transdermic

adjective
applied or absorbed through the skin
Pronunciation: / trænsˈdɜrmɪk / (AmE), / trænzˈdɜːmɪk / (BrE)
(of medication or treatment) administered through the unbroken skin, typically by direct application such as creams, ointments, or time-release patches.

Examples

transdermic patch treatment
Some hormone therapies are administered using transdermic methods to maintain consistent blood levels.
The doctor prescribed a transdermic patch to deliver the medication steadily over 24 hours.
This cream allows for transdermic absorption of the active ingredient, avoiding the digestive system.
