purportedly

adverb
allegedly; as claimed
Pronunciation: / pərˈpɝːtɪdli / (AmE), / pəˈpɔːtɪdli / (BrE)
as is claimed or alleged, but not necessarily proven to be true

Examples

purportedly involved in crime
He was purportedly seen at the scene of the crime, though no evidence supports this claim.
The new medicine is purportedly effective, but more research is needed to confirm its benefits.
The policy was introduced to improve safety, purportedly in response to recent accidents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allegedly: as is claimed to be the case or have taken place, without proof
