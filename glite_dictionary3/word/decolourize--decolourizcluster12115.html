to decolourize

verb
make less colored or white
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkʌlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌləraɪz / (BrE)
to remove or lighten the color of something; to cause to become white or less colored

Examples

to decolourize fabric
Repeated washing will gradually decolourize bright garments.
Sunlight can decolourize fabrics left outdoors for too long.
The laboratory used chemicals to decolourize the solution before analysis.

  • synonym – bleach: to make something white or lighter by removing color
  • antonym – discolor: to change or spoil the color of
