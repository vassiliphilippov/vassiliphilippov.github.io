to load

verb
add inferior substances; adulterate
Pronunciation: / loʊd / (AmE), / ləʊd / (BrE)
to make something impure or inferior by adding a foreign or lower-quality substance, often by replacing valuable ingredients with cheaper ones

Examples

to load food with fillers
Some dishonest vendors load olive oil with cheaper vegetable oils to reduce costs.
The company was caught trying to load its products with cheaper fillers.
Worried about quality, officials tested whether suppliers had loaded the wine with additives.
