to creak

verb
make a harsh, squeaking sound
Pronunciation: / krik / (AmE), / kriːk / (BrE)
to make a harsh, high-pitched, squeaking sound, especially when being moved or when pressure is applied

Examples

to creak when opening
If you creak the chair back and forth, it becomes very annoying.
She slowly opened the window and heard it creak on its rusty hinges.
The old floorboards creak whenever someone walks across the hallway.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – groan: make a deep, drawn-out sound, often due to strain
  • synonym – squeak: make a short, high-pitched sound
