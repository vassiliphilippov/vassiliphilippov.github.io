to cognise

verb
be aware of or know
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡˌnaɪz / (BrE)
To be aware of or possess knowledge about something; to recognize or understand.

Examples

to cognise the truth
He gradually cognised the truth about his heritage.
It took me some time to cognise the full implications of the announcement.
Many fail to cognise the complexity of the situation at first glance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to something; disregard
  • synonym – know: to be aware of or have knowledge through experience or information
