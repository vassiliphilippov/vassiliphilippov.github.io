cytotoxic

adjective
toxic to cells
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪtəˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪtəˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) toxic to cells; capable of damaging or killing living cells.

Examples

cytotoxic drugs
Some chemotherapy medications are highly cytotoxic and can cause significant side effects.
The cytotoxic activity of the compound was confirmed in multiple experiments.
The researchers tested several cytotoxic drugs against the cancer cells in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – noncytotoxic: not harmful to cells
  • synonym – toxic: poisonous or harmful to living organisms
