suborder

noun
biological classification below order
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɔːdə / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic category that ranks below an order and above a family, used to further classify organisms.

Examples

suborder of mammals
Each suborder may contain several families based on shared characteristics.
Ornithologists study the suborder Passeri, which includes songbirds.
The primates are divided into two main suborders: Strepsirrhini and Haplorhini.
