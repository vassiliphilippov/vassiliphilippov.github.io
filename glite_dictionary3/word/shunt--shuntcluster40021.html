to shunt

verb
to divert with an electrical shunt
Pronunciation: / ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
to provide with or divert current by means of an electrical shunt, especially to protect a circuit or direct electricity along a particular path

Examples

to shunt electricity
If the voltage rises too high, the system will automatically shunt the flow away from sensitive components.
In certain medical devices, surgeons shunt excess fluid using electrical means.
The technician decided to shunt the current to prevent damage to the circuit.

  • synonym – bypass: to pass or go around; to avoid by diversion
