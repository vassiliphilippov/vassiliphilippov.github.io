unbloody

adjective
without bloodshed or violence
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈblʌdi / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈblʌdi / (BrE)
(of an event or process) not involving violence or bloodshed; achieved without causing physical harm or blood loss.

Examples

unbloody revolution
It was one of the rare, unbloody battles in the region’s history.
The revolution was remarkably unbloody, with hardly any casualties reported.
They were relieved that the election led to an unbloody transition of power.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bloody: involving or characterized by bloodshed
