deckhand

noun
ship crew member doing manual labor
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈdɛkˌhænd / (BrE)
a member of a ship's crew whose job is to perform unskilled manual labor on deck

Examples

a deckhand cleans the deck
After a long shift, the deckhand rested in the crew quarters.
She started her maritime career as a deckhand on a ferry.
The deckhand scrubbed the deck in preparation for the day's passengers.
