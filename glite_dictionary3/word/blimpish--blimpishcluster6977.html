blimpish

adjective
ultraconservative and pompous (often British)
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪmpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblɪmpɪʃ / (BrE)
Pompously ultraconservative and nationalistic, especially in a narrow-minded or old-fashioned way.

Examples

blimpish attitude
His blimpish attitudes often clashed with the views of the younger generation.
She dismissed his blimpish opinions as outdated and inflexible.
The newspaper column was criticized for its blimpish nostalgia for the empire's former glory.
