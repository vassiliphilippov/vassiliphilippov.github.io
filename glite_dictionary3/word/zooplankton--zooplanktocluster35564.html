zooplankton

noun
tiny aquatic animals drifting in water
Pronunciation: / ˌzoʊ.əˈplæŋk.tən / (AmE), / ˌzəʊ.əˈplæŋk.tən / (BrE)
Small, often microscopic, aquatic animals that drift in water, forming the animal component of plankton, such as tiny crustaceans and fish larvae.

Examples

marine zooplankton
A sudden increase in zooplankton can affect the clarity and quality of lake water.
Baleen whales feed primarily on zooplankton such as krill and copepods.
Researchers collected samples of zooplankton to study the aquatic food chain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – phytoplankton: plant-like microscopic organisms that form the plant component of plankton
