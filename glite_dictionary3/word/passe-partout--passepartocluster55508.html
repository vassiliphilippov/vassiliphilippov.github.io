passe-partout

noun
master key for many locks
Pronunciation: / pæs pɑːrˈtu / (AmE), / pɑːs pɑːˈtuː / (BrE)
a master key that can open a number of different locks; especially a key that opens every door in a building or establishment

Examples

passe-partout key for doors
After losing the original, the hotel manager used the passe-partout to enter the guests' rooms.
Only trusted employees are given access to the passe-partout for security reasons.
The caretaker carried a passe-partout that unlocked every office in the building.
