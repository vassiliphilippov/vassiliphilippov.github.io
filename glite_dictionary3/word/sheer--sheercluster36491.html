sheer

adjective
(of fabric) very thin and almost transparent
Pronunciation: / ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
(of fabric or material) very thin and almost transparent; allowing light to pass through easily.

Examples

sheer fabric dress
Butterflies have sheer wings that glimmer in the morning light.
She wore a sheer silk blouse that revealed the outline of her camisole underneath.
The curtains were made from a sheer white fabric that let in plenty of sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – opaque: not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • degree lower – translucent: allowing light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through; semi-transparent
  • synonym – transparent: allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen
