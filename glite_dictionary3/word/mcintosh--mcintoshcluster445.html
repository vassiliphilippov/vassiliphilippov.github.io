McIntosh

noun
variety of apple from the northeastern US
Pronunciation: / məkˈɪn.tɑʃ / (AmE), / məkˈɪn.tɒʃ / (BrE)
A variety of early-ripening apple with white flesh and red skin, popular in the northeastern United States; often eaten raw or used for applesauce.
