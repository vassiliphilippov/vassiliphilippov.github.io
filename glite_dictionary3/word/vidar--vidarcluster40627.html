Vidar

proper noun
Norse god, avenger of Odin
Pronunciation: / ˈviː.dɑr / (AmE), / ˈviː.dɑː / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the Aesir gods, the son of Odin, known for avenging his father's death by killing Fenrir during Ragnarok.

Examples

Vidar avenges Odin
According to legend, Vidar avenged Odin by slaying the wolf Fenrir at Ragnarok.
In Norse mythology, Vidar is known for his strength and silence.
Many ancient texts describe Vidar as one of the few gods to survive Ragnarok.
