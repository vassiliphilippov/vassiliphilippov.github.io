minority

noun
group differing from the majority
Pronunciation: / maɪˈnɔrəti / (AmE), / mɪˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
A group of people whose race, ethnicity, religion, or political beliefs differ from those of a larger group in the same society, often resulting in less power or representation.

Examples

minority group
As a member of a religious minority, he sometimes felt isolated at school.
The new policy was designed to support the minority communities in the city.
The organization advocates for equal rights for all racial minority groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym – majority: the greater part or number; the group with the largest number of members
