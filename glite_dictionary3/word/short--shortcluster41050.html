short

adjective
insufficient in quantity
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
not having enough of something; insufficient in quantity or amount

Examples

short supply
After the drought, food supplies became short in the region.
I'm short of cash until I get paid next week.
The hospital was short on nurses during the night shift.
He complained that the sack of potatoes was a few pounds short of what he paid for.
Make sure you don't pour a short measure of oil into the recipe.
The shopkeeper was accused of giving short change to customers.

  • synonym – inadequate: not sufficient; lacking the quality or quantity required
  • synonym – light: underweight or not as heavy as required, especially in trade
  • antonym – plentiful: existing in or yielding great quantities
  • synonym – scant: barely sufficient in amount
  • synonym – scarce: in short supply; not enough available
