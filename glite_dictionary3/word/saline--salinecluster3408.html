saline

noun
salt solution used in medicine
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪliːn / (BrE)
a solution of salt (sodium chloride) in distilled water, commonly used in medicine to match the salt concentration of body fluids

Examples

sterile saline solution
After cleaning the wound with saline, the doctor applied a bandage.
Physiological saline is often used to rinse contact lenses safely.
The nurse prepared a fresh bag of saline for the patient’s IV drip.
