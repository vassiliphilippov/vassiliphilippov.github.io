neophobia

noun
fear of novelty or new things
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
An extreme or irrational fear of anything new or unfamiliar; a fear of novelty.

Examples

neophobia towards change
Corporate neophobia can hinder innovation and adaptation to changing markets.
Some children display neophobia when introduced to unfamiliar foods at an early age.
The scientist studied animal neophobia to understand avoidance behaviors toward new objects.
