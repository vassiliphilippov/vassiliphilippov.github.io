Gracillariidae

noun
family of leaf-miner moths
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˌsɪl.əˈraɪ.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɡræˌsɪl.əˈraɪ.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small moths whose larvae are known as leaf miners, feeding within leaves and creating distinctive mines.

Examples

Gracillariidae leaf miners
Larvae of Gracillariidae create winding tunnels visible on the leaves of their host plants.
Many agricultural scientists study the Gracillariidae because of the damage their leaf-mining larvae can cause to crops.
The Gracillariidae family contains over 2,000 species of small moths found worldwide.
