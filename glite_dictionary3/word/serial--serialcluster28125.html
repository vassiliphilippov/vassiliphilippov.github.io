serial

adjective
in regular succession; arranged as a series
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
happening, arranged, or done in a series or sequence, one after another

Examples

serial killer case
His serial meetings with the director ensured no details were overlooked.
The orchestra held a serial concert series each month during the summer.
The painter produced a serial set of images documenting every phase of the project.
She became famous for her serial novels that kept readers eagerly anticipating the next installment.
The police investigated a serial arsonist who had set fires in several neighborhoods.
There was a serial pattern to the burglaries, all occurring on consecutive nights.
In computing, serial processing means instructions are handled one after the other.
The serial execution of tasks ensures that each one is completed before the next begins.
Unlike parallel systems, serial approaches may be slower but are simpler to design.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intermittent: occasional; not continuous or steady
  • antonym – parallel: (of computing) involving the simultaneous execution of operations
  • synonym – sequential: arranged in a sequence; following in order
