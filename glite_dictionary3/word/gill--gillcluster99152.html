gill

noun
radiating structure under mushroom cap
Pronunciation: / ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
any of the thin, radiating, leaflike structures on the underside of the cap of a mushroom or similar fungus, where spores are produced

Examples

mushroom gills
Careful examination revealed the gills beneath the mushroom cap were lined with tiny spores.
Moisture tends to collect on the gills, aiding the dispersal of spores in humid conditions.
The identification key uses the color of the gills to help distinguish between edible and poisonous species.
