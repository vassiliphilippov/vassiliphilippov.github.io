rigour

noun
harsh or severe conditions; hardship
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
severe or harsh conditions that are difficult to endure; hardship or harshness.

Examples

rigour of winter
During the long war, civilians endured the rigour of daily shortages and curfews.
The rigour of the high-altitude climate challenged even the most experienced explorers.
Training for the marathon exposed him to the rigour of early morning runs in the winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hardship: severe suffering or difficulty
  • synonym – harshness: the quality of being unpleasantly rough or severe
  • antonym – mildness: the quality of being not severe or harsh (contrasting property)
