assimilative

adjective
able to absorb or take in substances
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪməˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪlətɪv / (BrE)
able to absorb or incorporate substances such as gases, light, or liquids into a solution or into itself.

Examples

assimilative pigment in plants
Chlorophyll is an assimilative pigment in plants that captures light energy for photosynthesis.
Soil with higher assimilative capacity can filter and break down pollutants more effectively.
The assimilative properties of activated charcoal help it remove toxins from the digestive tract.
