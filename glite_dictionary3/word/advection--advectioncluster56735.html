advection

noun
horizontal transfer of heat or properties in the atmosphere
Pronunciation: / ædˈvɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ædˈvɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
(Meteorology) the horizontal movement of heat, moisture, or other atmospheric properties, especially by wind in the atmosphere.

Examples

horizontal advection of heat
Meteorologists track the advection of pollutants to predict air quality issues.
The advection of moist air can lead to fog formation along coastlines.
Weather forecasts often mention advection to explain sudden changes in temperature.

Related Terms

  • antonym – convection: the vertical movement of heat or properties in a fluid or the atmosphere
