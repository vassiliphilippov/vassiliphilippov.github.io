to bolt

verb
move or run suddenly and quickly
Pronunciation: / boʊlt / (AmE), / bəʊlt / (BrE)
to move or run suddenly and quickly, often to escape

Examples

to bolt out the door
She bolted out of the door when the fire alarm went off.
Startled by the sudden noise, the cat bolted under the bed.
When he heard the thunder, the horse bolted from the stable.
As soon as the dog saw the open gate, it tried to bolt with its owner's shoe.
Hearing footsteps behind him, the burglar decided to bolt with the stolen necklace.
The employee tried to bolt with confidential files from the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abscond: to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest
  • synonym – dash: to move suddenly and quickly somewhere
  • synonym – escape: to get away from a place where one is being held or confined
  • antonym – freeze: to suddenly stop moving, often due to fear or surprise
  • antonym – remain: to stay in the same place; not to leave or escape
