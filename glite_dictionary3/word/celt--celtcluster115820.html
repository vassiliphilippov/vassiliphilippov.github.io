Celt

noun
member of ancient European people
Pronunciation: / kɛlt, sɛlt / (AmE), / kɛlt, sɛlt / (BrE)
a member of a group of ancient peoples who lived in parts of Europe, especially in Britain, Ireland, France, and Spain, before and during Roman times

Examples

ancient Celt tribes
Many Celts migrated to Ireland during the Iron Age.
She traced her ancestry back to a Celt who lived in what is now France.
The ancient Celt communities left rich archaeological remains across Europe.
