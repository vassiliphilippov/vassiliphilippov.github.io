high-necked

adjective
having a high neckline (of a garment)
Pronunciation: / haɪˈnɛkt / (AmE), / haɪˈnɛkt / (BrE)
(of a garment) having a neckline that rises high up the neck, often covering most or all of the neck.

Examples

high-necked sweater
His favorite sweater is high-necked and made from soft wool.
She wore a high-necked dress to keep warm in the chilly weather.
The collection features several elegant high-necked blouses suitable for formal occasions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low-necked: (of a garment) having a low or plunging neckline
