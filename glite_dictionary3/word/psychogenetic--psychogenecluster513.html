psychogenetic

adjective
due to psychological cause (esp. disorder)
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkə(ʊ)dʒɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, or caused by, psychological factors rather than physical ones, especially regarding the origin of a disorder.

Examples

psychogenetic illness cause
A thorough evaluation is needed to distinguish between psychogenetic and physiological causes of the illness.
Some researchers believe that dissociative disorders are psychogenetic in nature.
The patient exhibited symptoms with no organic basis, indicating a possible psychogenetic origin.
