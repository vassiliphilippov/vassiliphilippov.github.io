deputy

noun
member of lower house (legislature, esp. France)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛpjəti / (AmE), / ˈdɛpjʊti / (BrE)
a member of the lower house of the legislative assembly in certain countries, especially France

Examples

French deputy parliament
Each deputy is elected to represent a specific constituency in the lower house.
Several deputies proposed amendments to the new education bill.
The French deputy addressed the National Assembly during a heated debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – member of parliament: an elected member of a parliament (general legislative body)
  • antonym – senator: a member of a senate, usually the upper chamber in a bicameral legislature
