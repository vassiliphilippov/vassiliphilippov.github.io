perpendicular

adjective
nearly vertical; very steep
Pronunciation: / ˌpɚ.pənˈdɪk.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.pənˈdɪk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
(of a slope or surface) so steep as to be nearly vertical.

Examples

perpendicular cliff face
A perpendicular drop separated the mountain trail from the valley below.
The perpendicular cliffs made access by boat nearly impossible.
The hikers were daunted by the perpendicular wall of rock before them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – horizontal: parallel to the horizon; not vertical
  • synonym – vertical: upright or at a right angle to the horizon
