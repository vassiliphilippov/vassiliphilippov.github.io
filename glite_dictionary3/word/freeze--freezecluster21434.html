freeze

noun
temporary suspension or halt in activity
Pronunciation: / friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
a temporary stop or suspension of an activity, process, or change, especially as imposed by authority

Examples

a wage freeze
After months of inflation, workers were frustrated by the wage freeze imposed by management.
Negotiators called for a freeze in nuclear weapons development to ease international tensions.
The government announced a freeze on public sector hiring for the next fiscal year.
A freeze on rent increases was introduced to help struggling tenants.
Due to economic uncertainty, the company implemented a hiring freeze across all departments.
The government announced a freeze on public sector salaries to control spending.
