Erlang

noun
unit of traffic intensity in telecommunications
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrˌlæŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜː.læŋ / (BrE)
a unit of traffic intensity used in telecommunications, equal to the continuous use of one voice path.

Examples

one Erlang of traffic
In telephony, a circuit carrying calls for sixty minutes in an hour is said to have one Erlang of traffic.
Our network sees about five Erlangs of call volume during peak times.
The engineer calculated the needed lines using Erlang formulas.
