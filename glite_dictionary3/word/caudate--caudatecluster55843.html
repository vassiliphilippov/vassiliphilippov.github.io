caudate

noun
tail-shaped brain structure
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
a tail-shaped mass of gray matter forming part of the basal ganglia, located in a lateral ventricle of the brain

Examples

caudate nucleus brain
MRI scans revealed a lesion in the patient's left caudate nucleus.
Researchers are studying the role of the caudate in learning and memory.
The doctor explained that damage to the caudate can affect a person's movement and behavior.
