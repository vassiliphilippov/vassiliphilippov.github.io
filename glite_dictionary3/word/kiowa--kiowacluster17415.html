Kiowa

noun
member of Southwestern Native American people
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪ.oʊ.wə / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.əʊ.wə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the southwestern United States, especially Oklahoma, Texas, and New Mexico

Examples

a Kiowa elder
Many Kiowa still maintain traditional ceremonies and crafts.
She interviewed a prominent Kiowa elder for her research on Native American cultures.
The history of the Kiowa people is deeply connected to the Great Plains.
