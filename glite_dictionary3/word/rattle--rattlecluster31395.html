rattle

noun
short, loud sound in chest (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈræɾl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrætl / (BrE)
A rapid series of short, loud sounds, especially as heard in the chest with a stethoscope in certain respiratory conditions.

Examples

chest rattle sound
A persistent chest rattle can indicate an underlying respiratory problem.
The doctor noticed a rattle in the patient's chest during the examination.
The nurse listened closely for any sign of a rattle through the stethoscope.
