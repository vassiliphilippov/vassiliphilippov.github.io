Palestine

proper noun
former British mandate in the Middle East
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.ə.staɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ɪ.staɪn / (BrE)
a former British mandate territory on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean, divided between Jordan and Israel in 1948.

Examples

British Palestine mandate
Many families in Palestine were affected by the division of territory after World War II.
The League of Nations granted Britain a mandate over Palestine following the First World War.
The end of the British mandate in Palestine in 1948 led to major changes in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Israel: country formed in part from the former British Mandate of Palestine
  • synonym – Transjordan: historical region east of the Jordan River, created from part of the British Mandate of Palestine
