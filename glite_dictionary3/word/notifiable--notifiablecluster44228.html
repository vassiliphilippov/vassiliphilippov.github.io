notifiable

adjective
required to be officially reported
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊ.t̬ɪˈfaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ.tɪˈfaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a disease, condition, or event) required by law or regulation to be reported to the authorities.

Examples

notifiable disease case
Influenza is a notifiable disease in many countries, meaning doctors must report cases to health authorities.
The law states that any notifiable incident must be logged within 24 hours.
Tuberculosis became a notifiable condition to better control its spread.
