repayable

adjective
required to be paid back
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / rɪˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a sum of money) required to be paid back; capable of being repaid.

Examples

repayable loan amount
All advances made under this agreement are repayable on demand.
Scholarships are given as grants and are not repayable by the student.
The loan is repayable over five years with monthly installments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non-repayable: not required to be paid back
  • synonym – returnable: able or allowed to be returned, often for a refund or deposit
