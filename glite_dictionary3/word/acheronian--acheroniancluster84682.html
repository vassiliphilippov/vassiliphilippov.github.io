Acheronian

adjective
dark or hellish; gloomy (from myth)
Pronunciation: / ˌækəˈroʊniən / (AmE), / ˌækəˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
dark, gloomy, or infernal; resembling the lower world as described in Greek mythology, particularly the river Acheron.

Examples

Acheronian darkness
He described his nightmares as an endless journey through Acheronian shadows and despair.
The explorers hesitated before entering the Acheronian cave, fearing what lay in the darkness.
Thick mist shrouded the landscape in an Acheronian gloom, making the village feel otherworldly.

Related Terms
