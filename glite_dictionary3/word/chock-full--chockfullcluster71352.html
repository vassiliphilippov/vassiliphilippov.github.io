chock-full

adjective
completely full or packed
Pronunciation: / tʃɑk ˈfʊl / (AmE), / tʃɒk ˈfʊl / (BrE)
Completely full; packed to capacity.

Examples

chock-full of people
During the sale, the store became chock-full of eager shoppers.
Her suitcase was chock-full after she packed all her new clothes.
The display case was chock-full of colorful pastries and cakes.
