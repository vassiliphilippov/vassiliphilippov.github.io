demoralization

noun
loss of morale; discouragement
Pronunciation: / dɪˌmɔrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / dɪˌmɔrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of discouragement, confusion, or loss of morale, especially in a person or group

Examples

demoralization of workers
Continuous criticism can result in demoralization and reduced productivity.
The team's repeated losses led to a sense of demoralization among the players.
There was demoralization in the office after the company announced major layoffs.
Continued management failures resulted in the demoralization of the entire department.
The sudden loss of leadership created demoralization and chaos in the organization.
The team's crushing defeat led to a sense of demoralization among the players.
