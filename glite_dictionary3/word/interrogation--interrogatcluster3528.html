interrogation

noun
signal sent to trigger transponder reply
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.təˈɹoʊ.ɡəˌʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈɹɒ.ɡəˌʃən / (BrE)
A transmission sent to elicit a response from a transponder, especially in telecommunications and aviation systems.

Examples

radio interrogation
Modern air traffic control relies on rapid interrogation and response cycles to track multiple aircraft.
The radar system sends an interrogation to aircraft transponders to determine their identity and altitude.
When the beacon receives the interrogation, it automatically transmits a coded reply signal.
