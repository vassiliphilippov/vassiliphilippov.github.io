calm

adjective
weather or water without wind or disturbance
Pronunciation: / kɑm / (AmE), / kɑːm / (BrE)
not disturbed, nervous, or agitated; peaceful and still (describing people, weather, or water)

Examples

calm weather
After the storm passed, the lake was calm and glassy, perfect for rowing.
The calm sea stretched out to the horizon, undisturbed by even the slightest breeze.
They sailed safely thanks to the calm weather predicted for the afternoon.
Despite the chaos around him, his expression remained perfectly calm.
She spoke in a calm and reassuring tone throughout the meeting.
Try to stay calm if you find yourself in a stressful situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agitated: feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • synonym – serene: calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • antonym – stormy: (of weather) characterized by storms, wind, or turbulence
  • synonym – tranquil: free from disturbance; calm
