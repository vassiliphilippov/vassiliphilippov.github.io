labor-intensive

adjective
requiring much manual labor
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.bɚ ɪnˌtɛn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə ɪnˌtɛn.sɪv / (BrE)
(of a task, process, or industry) requiring a high amount of manual labor but little capital.

Examples

labor-intensive farming
Farming can be highly labor-intensive in areas without modern machinery.
Switching to automated systems reduced the labor-intensive nature of the operation.
The production of handmade carpets is a labor-intensive process that relies heavily on skilled workers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – capital-intensive: requiring large capital investment but little labor
