new

adjective
inexperienced; lacking training
Pronunciation: / nu / (AmE), / njuː / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking experience or training in a particular activity or position.

Examples

new employee
As a new teacher, she often asked her colleagues for advice.
Being new to the sport, he made a few beginner mistakes.
The new employees are still learning how to operate the machines safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym – experienced: having knowledge or skill from doing something for a long time
  • synonym – inexperienced: having little knowledge or experience in a particular area
