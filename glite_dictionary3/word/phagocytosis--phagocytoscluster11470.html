phagocytosis

noun
process of engulfing and digesting particles by cells
Pronunciation: / ˌfəɡəˌsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfaɡəˌsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which certain cells engulf and digest microorganisms and cellular debris, serving as an important defense against infection

Examples

phagocytosis of bacteria
Defects in phagocytosis can increase susceptibility to infections.
Macrophages use phagocytosis to remove bacteria from the bloodstream.
The process of phagocytosis involves the engulfment of foreign particles by immune cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endocytosis: the process of taking substances into a cell by engulfing them in a membrane
  • antonym – exocytosis: the process of expelling substances from a cell via vesicles fusing with the plasma membrane
