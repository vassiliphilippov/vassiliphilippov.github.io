realisation

noun
sale of assets to obtain cash
Pronunciation: / riˌæləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of selling assets, such as stocks or property, in order to convert them into cash, or the money obtained from such a sale

Examples

realisation of assets
After the realisation of his shares, he invested the money in real estate.
The administrator handled the realisation of the estate efficiently, ensuring all debts were settled.
The bankrupt company's properties were sold off in a realisation to pay creditors.
