hormone

noun
chemical messenger from an endocrine gland
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
A chemical substance produced by an endocrine gland and carried in the bloodstream to affect specific tissues or organs.

Examples

hormone levels rise
Cortisol is a stress hormone released during challenging situations.
Doctors may prescribe synthetic hormone treatments for patients with imbalances.
Puberty is triggered by a complex interaction of hormones within the body.
