instrument

noun
person used by another to achieve a goal
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.strə.mənt /; / ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.strə.mənt /; / ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
a person or thing used by someone to achieve a goal; a means to an end

Examples

instrument of change
He was merely an instrument for carrying out their plans, not a partner.
She became an instrument in the hands of the powerful politician.
The rebels accused him of being an instrument of foreign interests.
She used her charm as an instrument to get what she wanted.
The media became an instrument of political influence during the election.
The new policy served as an instrument for social change in the country.
