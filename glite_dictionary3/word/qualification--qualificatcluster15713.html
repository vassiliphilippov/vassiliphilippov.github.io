qualification

noun
limiting or modifying statement
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑː.lɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkwɑː.lə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒ.lɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statement or condition that limits or modifies what was previously said or proposed

Examples

qualification to a statement
He gave his support for the plan, but with one important qualification.
Her endorsement of the project came with several qualifications about its feasibility.
No qualification was mentioned in his recommendation letter.
She agreed to support the proposal, though with a qualification concerning funding details.
The prime minister added a qualification to his earlier statement to clarify his position.
Without any qualification, his assertion sounded too sweeping and absolute.
