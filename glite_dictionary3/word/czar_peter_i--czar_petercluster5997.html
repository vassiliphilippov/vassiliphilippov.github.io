Czar Peter I

proper noun
czar of Russia who modernized the nation
Pronunciation: / zɑr ˈpiːtər ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / zɑː ˈpiːtə ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(also known as Peter the Great) The czar of Russia from 1682 to 1725, known for implementing major reforms to modernize Russia and expanding its territory, including founding St. Petersburg.

Examples

Czar Peter I modernized Russia
Czar Peter I is remembered for drastically modernizing the Russian military and government.
Many historians credit Czar Peter I with transforming Russia into a major European power.
The city of St. Petersburg was founded by Czar Peter I as part of his westernizing reforms.
