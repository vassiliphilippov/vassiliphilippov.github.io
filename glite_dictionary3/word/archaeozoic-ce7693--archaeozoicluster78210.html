archaeozoic

adjective
early Precambrian era; ancient geological period
Pronunciation: / ˌɑr.ki.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ki.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the earlier of two divisions of the Precambrian era, characterized by the oldest known rocks and life forms.

Examples

archaeozoic rocks
Geologists have discovered fossils of archaeozoic bacteria in ancient rock layers.
Some of the oldest rocks on Earth are classified as archaeozoic in origin.
The archaeozoic eon predates the proterozoic and includes Earth's earliest known life.
