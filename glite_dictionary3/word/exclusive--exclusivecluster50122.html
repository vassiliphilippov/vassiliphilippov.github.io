exclusive

adjective
not shared; focused on one thing
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪv / (BrE)
(of attention, devotion, etc.) directed solely to one object, person, or purpose; not shared or divided among several.

Examples

exclusive attention
His exclusive devotion to science meant he rarely pursued other interests.
She gave her exclusive attention to her daughter's needs during the illness.
The club rewarded him with their exclusive loyalty for years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – shared: divided among several people or things
  • synonym – undivided: not divided; giving all attention to something
