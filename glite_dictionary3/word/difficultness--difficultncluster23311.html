difficultness

noun
the quality of being difficult
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪfələtnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪkəlt.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being hard to do, deal with, or understand; difficulty.

Examples

the difficultness of a task
Despite the difficultness of the project, the team managed to deliver it on time.
She underestimated the difficultness of hiking up the steep mountain trail alone.
The difficultness of the math problem challenged even the brightest students in the class.
