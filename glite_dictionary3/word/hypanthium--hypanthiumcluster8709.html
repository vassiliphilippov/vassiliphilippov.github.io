hypanthium

noun
floral structure bearing sepals and stamens
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpænθiəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpanθɪəm / (BrE)
a cuplike, ringlike, or tubular floral structure that bears the sepals, stamens, and calyx, found in some plants such as those in the Rosaceae family

Examples

hypanthium of a rose
A swollen hypanthium can sometimes help attract pollinators to a flower.
Botanists studied the structure of the hypanthium to classify various members of the rose family.
The cherry blossom's hypanthium is particularly prominent during early flowering.
