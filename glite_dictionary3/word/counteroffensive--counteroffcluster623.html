counteroffensive

noun
large-scale defensive military attack
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a large-scale military attack launched by a defending force to regain the initiative from an attacking force

Examples

launch a counteroffensive
After weeks of retreat, the general planned a massive counteroffensive that changed the course of the war.
International news covered the government's surprise counteroffensive in great detail.
The army launched a counteroffensive to push back the invading forces.
