pervaporation

noun
membrane technique for separating liquids
Pronunciation: / pɝːˌvæpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / pɜːˌvæpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A membrane separation process in which a liquid mixture is partially vaporized through a semipermeable membrane, commonly used to separate or concentrate solutions.

Examples

pervaporation membrane process
Pervaporation is often used to remove water from ethanol in the production of biofuels.
Recent studies focus on improving pervaporation membranes to better separate complex mixtures.
The efficiency of pervaporation depends on the nature of the membrane used.
