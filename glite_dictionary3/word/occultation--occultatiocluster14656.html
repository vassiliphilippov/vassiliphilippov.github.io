occultation

noun
when one celestial body blocks another
Pronunciation: / əˌkʌlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒkʌlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the event in which one celestial body passes in front of another, completely or partially obscuring it from view

Examples

lunar occultation event
An occultation occurs when a planet, moon, or asteroid passes in front of another object in the sky.
Astronomers observed the occultation of Jupiter by the Moon last night.
The rare occultation provided valuable data about the distant star.
