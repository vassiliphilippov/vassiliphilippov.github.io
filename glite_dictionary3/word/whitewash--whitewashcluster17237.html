whitewash

noun
defeat with no points scored by loser
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtwɒʃ / (BrE)
a defeat in which the losing person or team fails to score any points, especially in games or sports.

Examples

a whitewash victory
It was their third consecutive whitewash this season, much to the disappointment of their fans.
The team suffered a whitewash in the finals, failing to score a single goal.
The visitors were handed a humiliating whitewash by the home team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shutout: a game in which one side prevents the other from scoring
