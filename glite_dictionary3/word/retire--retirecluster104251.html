to retire

verb
stop working or participating after a period
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtaɪɚ /; / rəˈtaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to leave your job or stop working permanently, usually because of age or after a long period of service

Examples

to retire from work
After forty years of teaching, she decided to retire last spring.
He plans to retire from international competition after this season.
Many athletes retire in their thirties due to physical demands.
After forty years with the company, he decided to retire and move to the countryside.
Many people plan carefully for their finances before they retire.
She hopes to retire by the age of sixty and travel the world.

  • antonym – continue: to keep working and not retire
  • synonym – quit: to leave a job or position voluntarily
  • synonym – resign: to voluntarily leave a job or position
