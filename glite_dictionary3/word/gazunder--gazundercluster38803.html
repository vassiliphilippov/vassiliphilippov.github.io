to gazunder

verb
lower an agreed offer, esp. for property
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈzʌndər / (AmE), / ɡəˈzʌndə / (BrE)
to reduce an already agreed offer (especially on a property) just before contracts are exchanged, often considered unethical

Examples

to gazunder the price
After months of negotiation, the seller was furious when the agent attempted to gazunder the price.
If you gazunder someone, you might lose their trust completely.
The buyer tried to gazunder my offer at the last minute, hoping I would still agree to the deal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gazump: to raise the asking price after accepting an initial offer, often in property deals
