seal

noun
marine mammal with flippers
Pronunciation: / siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
a large marine mammal with a streamlined body and flippers, usually found in cold regions, that comes ashore to breed

Examples

seal swims
A group of seals lay basking on the rocks by the shore.
During breeding season, hundreds of seals gather on the remote beaches.
The seal dived gracefully into the freezing water below the ice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sea lion: a large marine mammal similar to a seal but with external ear flaps
  • synonym – walrus: a large marine mammal related to seals, with tusks and whiskers
