spoiler

noun
device on aircraft wing to reduce lift and increase drag
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a hinged device on the upper surface of an aircraft wing that can be raised to reduce lift and increase drag, usually to assist in landing or controlling descent

Examples

airplane spoiler
Modern jetliners are equipped with multiple spoilers for improved control.
The pilot deployed the spoiler to help slow the plane during landing.
When the spoiler was raised, the aircraft's descent rate increased noticeably.
