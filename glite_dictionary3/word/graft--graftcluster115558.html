graft

noun
transplanted tissue or organ in surgery
Pronunciation: / ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a piece of living tissue or an organ transplanted from a donor to a recipient in surgical procedures

Examples

skin graft surgery
After the accident, doctors used a skin graft to help her wounds heal.
The surgeon carefully placed the graft to restore blood flow to the damaged artery.
There is always a risk that the body may reject the transplanted graft.

Related Terms

  • synonym – transplant: an operation in which an organ or tissue is moved from one body to another
