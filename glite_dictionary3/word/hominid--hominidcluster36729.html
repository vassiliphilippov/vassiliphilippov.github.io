hominid

noun
member of the Hominidae family (humans and great apes)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːmɪnɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒmɪnɪd / (BrE)
a member of the biological family Hominidae, which includes humans, great apes, and their ancestors

Examples

extinct hominid species
Recent discoveries have revealed new species of extinct hominids in Africa.
Some scientists study ancient hominid fossils to learn about human evolution.
The gorilla is considered a hominid, along with orangutans and chimpanzees.
