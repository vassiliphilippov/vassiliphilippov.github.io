muddiness

noun
cloudy or unclear quality
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌd.i.nəs /; / ˈmʌdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being cloudy, unclear, or lacking clarity, especially in liquids, sound, explanations, or thoughts

Examples

muddiness of an explanation
The muddiness of his explanation left everyone more confused than before.
The muddiness of the river increased after the storm washed soil into the water.
There was a muddiness in the sound, making the speaker difficult to understand.
After a sleepless night, she faced the meeting with mental muddiness.
The muddiness of his thoughts prevented him from making a firm decision.
The report was criticized for the muddiness of its logic and arguments.
