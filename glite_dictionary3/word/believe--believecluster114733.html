to believe

verb
to consider or suppose something to be true
Pronunciation: / bɪˈliv / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to consider or suppose something to be true or to have an opinion about someone or something in a particular way

Examples

to believe someone honest
He believes this to be a fair decision.
Many people believe her to be the most talented actor in the company.
The coach believes Thomas is ready for the main team.

Related Terms

  • antonym – doubt: to feel uncertain about something; lack belief
  • synonym – think: to form an opinion about something or someone
