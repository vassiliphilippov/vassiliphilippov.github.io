deadhead

noun
a vehicle traveling empty (transport)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (BrE)
a train, bus, or taxi that is traveling empty, especially to or from a location to pick up passengers or cargo

Examples

deadhead train journey
Railway companies try to minimize deadhead trips for cost efficiency.
The bus made the return journey as a deadhead after dropping off the last passengers.
The taxi driver complained about too many deadhead miles at the end of his shift.
