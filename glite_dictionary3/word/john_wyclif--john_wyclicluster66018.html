John Wyclif

proper noun
English theologian and church reformer (1328–1384)
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈwɪklɪf / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈwɪklɪf / (BrE)
(c.1328–1384) English theologian and reformer who criticized the doctrines and practices of the Roman Catholic Church, laying groundwork for the Protestant Reformation.
