scarabaean

noun
stout-bodied beetle with spiked head
Pronunciation: / skəˈræbiən / (AmE), / skəˈræbiːən / (BrE)
any of numerous stout-bodied beetles with heads bearing horny spikes, typically belonging to the family Scarabaeidae.

Examples

large scarabaean beetle
Many farmers consider the scarabaean a pest because of its habit of feeding on crops.
The entomologist spent years studying the diverse species of scarabaean beetles in the rainforest.
The museum displayed a large scarabaean fossil recovered from ancient Egyptian ruins.
