ecumenicism

noun
doctrine promoting Christian unity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkjuːməˈnɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌkjuːmɪˈnɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Christianity) the doctrine or principle promoting cooperation and better understanding among different Christian denominations, aiming for universal Christian unity.

Examples

ecumenicism among churches
Her church community was deeply committed to ecumenicism and frequently participated in interfaith events.
Many religious leaders advocate ecumenicism to foster dialogue and mutual respect among denominations.
The rise of ecumenicism in the 20th century transformed relationships between Protestant and Catholic churches.
