collider

noun
particle accelerator for colliding beams
Pronunciation: / kəˈlaɪdər / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪdə / (BrE)
a type of particle accelerator in which two beams of particles are made to collide head-on at high speeds

Examples

particle collider experiment
A new collider is being planned to exceed the energy of current particle accelerators.
Scientists use a collider to study the fundamental particles of the universe.
The Large Hadron Collider is one of the most sophisticated scientific instruments ever built.
