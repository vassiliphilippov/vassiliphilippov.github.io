semiparasite

noun
a plant partially parasitic on others
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmipaɹəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈpærəsaɪt / (BrE)
a plant that derives some of its nutrients from another living plant but also contains chlorophyll and can photosynthesize

Examples

semiparasite on a tree
Scientists studied the relationship between the host tree and the semiparasite growing on its branches.
The mistletoe is a well-known semiparasite that still performs some photosynthesis.
Unlike true parasites, a semiparasite can produce its own food through sunlight.
