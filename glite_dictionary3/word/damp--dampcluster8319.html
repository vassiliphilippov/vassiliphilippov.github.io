damp

adjective
slightly wet
Pronunciation: / dæmp / (AmE), / damp / (BrE)
slightly wet, often in a way that is uncomfortable or undesirable

Examples

damp clothes after rain
His hair was still damp from the morning shower.
She hung her damp clothes outside to dry in the sun.
The kitchen floor felt damp after I finished mopping it.

  • antonym – dry: free from moisture or liquid; not wet
  • synonym – moist: slightly wet, often in a pleasant sense
  • degree higher – wet: covered or saturated with water or another liquid
