outport

noun
subsidiary port outside a main port
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌpɔːt / (BrE)
a port built outside or away from a main port, often in deeper water and farther from the center of trade, typically to handle larger ships or overflow traffic

Examples

outport for large ships
Goods were transferred from the main port to the outport for loading onto larger vessels.
The city developed an outport to accommodate growing international shipping traffic.
The construction of a modern outport revitalized the region's maritime industry.
