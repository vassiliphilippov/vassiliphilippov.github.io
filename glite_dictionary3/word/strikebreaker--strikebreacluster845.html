strikebreaker

noun
person who works during a strike
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪkˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a person who continues to work or is hired to work while regular employees are on strike, especially to replace those who are striking

Examples

factory strikebreaker
During the factory strike, several strikebreakers were brought in to keep production running.
Hiring a strikebreaker can increase tensions between management and striking workers.
The union members viewed strikebreakers with suspicion and anger.

  • synonym – scab: a contemptuous term for a strikebreaker
