to besiege

verb
overwhelm with demands or attention
Pronunciation: / bɪˈsidʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒ / (BrE)
to overwhelm someone with a large number of requests, questions, or persistent attention

Examples

to besiege a celebrity
After the match, autograph seekers besieged the football players outside the stadium.
Reporters besieged the politician as he left the courthouse, firing questions from every direction.
Since announcing her new book, fans have besieged her with letters and emails every day.
After her promotion, she was besieged with requests for advice from her colleagues.
He felt besieged by doubts as the deadline approached.
Journalists besieged the actor with questions about his new film.

Related Terms

  • synonym – harass: to subject someone to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • synonym – overwhelm: to give someone too much of a thing to deal with
  • synonym – pester: repeatedly annoy someone with requests or demands
  • antonym – reassure: to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears
