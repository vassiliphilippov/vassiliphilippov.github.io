to huckster

verb
to peddle goods from place to place
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌkstər / (AmE), / ˈhʌkstə / (BrE)
to sell or offer goods for sale in a petty, aggressive, or itinerant manner; to peddle goods from place to place

Examples

to huckster cheap goods
He huckstered homemade jewelry at fairs across the region last summer.
Street performers often huckster small items to tourists along the boardwalk.
The vendors would huckster their wares in every village they passed through.
