unfavourableness

noun
the quality of being unfavourable
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈfeɪvərəblnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈfeɪvərəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unfavourable; lack of encouragement or likelihood of success.

Examples

unfavourableness of results
She was concerned about the unfavourableness of the report’s findings.
The unfavourableness of the weather forced them to cancel the picnic.
The project's unfavourableness made investors hesitant to participate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – favourableness: the quality of being favourable; supportive or likely to result in success
  • synonym – unfavourable: not likely to lead to success; negative or adverse
