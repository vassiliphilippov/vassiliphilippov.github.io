impregnation

noun
process of saturating with a substance
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.prɛgˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prɛɡˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of completely saturating a material with another substance, typically to alter its properties or protect it

Examples

impregnation of wood
Engineers researched the impregnation of concrete with sealants to prevent water damage.
The impregnation of fabrics with flame-retardant chemicals increases their safety in public spaces.
The impregnation of the leather made the boots both waterproof and long-lasting.
