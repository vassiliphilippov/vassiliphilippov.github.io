scrim

noun
open-weave fabric used as curtain or backdrop
Pronunciation: / skrɪm / (AmE), / skrɪm / (BrE)
a strong, loosely woven fabric, often used as a curtain or backdrop in theatres or for reinforcement in building and upholstery

Examples

scrim curtain on stage
A torn scrim can quickly spoil the illusion in a theatrical production.
Light passing through the scrim revealed the performers waiting behind it.
The stage crew hung a scrim at the front of the stage to create a dreamy effect.
