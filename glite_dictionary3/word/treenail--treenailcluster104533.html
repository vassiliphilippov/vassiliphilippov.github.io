treenail

noun
wooden peg for fastening timbers
Pronunciation: / ˈtriˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈtriːneɪl / (BrE)
A wooden peg used to fasten timbers together in shipbuilding and carpentry, which swells when wet to hold the timbers tightly.

Examples

wooden treenail joint
Moisture caused the treenail to swell, locking the timbers in place.
Shipbuilders drove each treenail carefully to ensure a tight hold between the beams.
The hull of the old ship was held together by sturdy treenails.
