to de-emphasize

verb
reduce importance or emphasis
Pronunciation: / diˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / diˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (BrE)
to reduce the importance or emphasis given to something

Examples

to de-emphasize a problem
In her speech, she de-emphasized the problems and focused on the achievements.
Some modern teaching methods de-emphasize rote memorization in favor of critical thinking.
The company tried to de-emphasize its role in the controversy during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • antonym – emphasize: to give special importance or prominence to something
