homogenisation

noun
process of making uniform or consistent
Pronunciation: / həˌmɑːdʒənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / hɒˌmɒdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something uniform or consistent throughout, especially by breaking down differences in composition or structure

Examples

homogenisation of milk
Milk undergoes homogenisation to ensure an even texture and taste.
The homogenisation of products across global markets can diminish local cultures.
There are concerns about the homogenisation of news coverage among different media outlets.

Related Terms

  • antonym – diversification: the process of making or becoming more diverse or varied
  • synonym – homogenization: the American spelling of 'homogenisation'
