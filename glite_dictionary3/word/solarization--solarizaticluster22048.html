solarization

noun
exposure to the rays of the sun
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊlərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of exposing something, especially soil or an object, to the rays of the sun, often to achieve a particular effect such as sterilization or treatment

Examples

solarization of soil
Proper solarization requires covering the area with clear plastic for several weeks in the summer.
Repeated solarization can help sterilize soil in greenhouses.
The solarization of the field eliminated many harmful pathogens before planting.
