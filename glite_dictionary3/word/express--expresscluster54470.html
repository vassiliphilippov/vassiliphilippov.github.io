to express

verb
manifest a gene or genetic trait
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
(of a gene or genetic trait) to be shown or manifested in the phenotype due to gene activity.

Examples

to express a gene
Gene mutations can cause an organism to express an unusual trait.
Only certain plants express the resistance gene under stress conditions.
Some individuals do not express symptoms despite carrying the mutation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – suppress: to prevent the expression of a gene
