Rustbelt

noun
U.S. region of declining industry
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌstˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈrʌstˌbɛlt / (BrE)
A region in the northeastern and midwestern United States characterized by the decline of formerly powerful manufacturing industries, especially steel or textiles.

Examples

the Rustbelt region
After the factories closed, unemployment soared throughout the Rustbelt.
Economic recovery in the Rustbelt remains a challenge for policy makers.
Many families left the Rustbelt in search of better job opportunities elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Sunbelt: A region in the southern U.S. noted for its economic growth and warm climate
