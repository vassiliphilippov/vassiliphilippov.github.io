square-rigged

adjective
having square sails as main sails
Pronunciation: / skwɛr rɪgd / (AmE), / skwɛə rɪgd / (BrE)
(of a ship) having square sails as the principal sails.

Examples

square-rigged sailing ship
A square-rigged vessel requires a skilled crew to handle its complex sail arrangements.
Most merchant vessels of that era were square-rigged to take advantage of prevailing winds.
The museum features a beautifully restored square-rigged ship from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fore-and-aft rigged: rigged with sails set along the line of the keel rather than square to it
