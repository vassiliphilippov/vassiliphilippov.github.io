neutral

adjective
having no net electric charge
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəl /; / ˈnjuːtrəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəl / (BrE)
having neither a positive nor a negative electric charge, or not chemically reactive (science/technical)

Examples

neutral atom charge
A neutral atom has an equal number of protons and electrons, resulting in no overall charge.
In a salt solution, the sodium ion is positive and the chloride ion is negative, but water molecules are generally neutral.
Neutrons are neutral particles found in the nucleus of an atom.
Because helium is a neutral gas, it does not easily participate in chemical reactions.
Some metals appear shiny and neutral because they are resistant to corrosion.
The laboratory uses neutral substances to prevent unwanted side reactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – charged: having a positive or negative electric charge
  • synonym – inert: lacking the ability to move or react; chemically inactive
