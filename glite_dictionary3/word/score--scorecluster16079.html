to score

verb
achieve a result or grade
Pronunciation: / skɔr / (AmE), / skɔː / (BrE)
to gain points, goals, or marks in a game, test, or competition; or to assign a grade or rank to something

Examples

to score a goal
He managed to score above ninety percent in mathematics.
Many students hope to score higher on their second attempt.
She studied hard to score well on her final exams.
If you score the highest points, you will win the game.
She managed to score three goals in the first half of the match.
The team struggled but finally scored just before halftime.
Independent judges score the finalists to determine who wins the prize.
Teachers often score exams shortly after they are completed.
The committee will score each application based on several criteria.
