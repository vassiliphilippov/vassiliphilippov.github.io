distortion

noun
alteration of a signal's original waveform
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɔrʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
a change or deformation in the waveform of an acoustic or electrical signal, typically resulting in an undesirable or altered sound

Examples

sound distortion
Audio engineers work to minimize distortion in high-fidelity sound systems.
Old tape recordings often suffer from unwanted distortion due to equipment limitations.
The guitarist adjusted the amp to create a heavy distortion effect during the solo.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clarity: clearness of sound or signal, opposite of distortion
  • synonym – noise: unwanted sound or interference in a signal
