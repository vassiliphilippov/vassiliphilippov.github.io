subduedness

noun
the state of being subdued or restrained
Pronunciation: / səbˈdudnəs / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being quiet, restrained, or showing patient endurance, especially in response to difficulty or adversity

Examples

emotional subduedness
Despite the chaos around him, his subduedness helped to calm the team.
Her subduedness in the face of criticism was truly admirable.
The subduedness of her response showed remarkable self-control.

Related Terms

  • synonym – patience: the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, problems, or suffering without becoming annoyed
