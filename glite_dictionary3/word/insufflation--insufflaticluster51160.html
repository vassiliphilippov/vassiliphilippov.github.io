insufflation

noun
medical procedure of blowing air or powder into the body
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnsəˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsʌˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
(medicine) The act of blowing air, gas, or medicated powder into a body cavity, especially the lungs, for medical purposes.

Examples

abdominal insufflation
Laparoscopic surgeries often require abdominal insufflation with carbon dioxide to provide adequate working space.
Some nasal medications are administered by insufflation, delivering the powder directly into the nostrils.
The anesthesiologist carefully performed insufflation to ensure the patient’s lungs were properly inflated during surgery.
