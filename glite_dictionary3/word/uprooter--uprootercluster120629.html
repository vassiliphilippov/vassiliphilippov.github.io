uprooter

noun
one who removes or destroys completely
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈruːtər / (AmE), / ʌpˈruːtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that removes, destroys, or wipes out something, especially by pulling it out by the roots or eradicating it completely

Examples

forest uprooter
Environmental activists protested against the uprooters of ancient woodlands for the new highway.
He became known as an uprooter of old traditions in the company.
The storm was a fierce uprooter of trees along the coastline.
