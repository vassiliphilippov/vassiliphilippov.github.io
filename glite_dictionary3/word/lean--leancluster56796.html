to lean

verb
be inclined; tend toward something
Pronunciation: / lin / (AmE), / liːn / (BrE)
to have a tendency or be inclined toward a particular opinion, action, or condition

Examples

to lean toward an idea
Her medical advice tends to lean in favor of natural remedies over pharmaceuticals.
Many voters lean toward supporting the independent candidate in this election.
The committee is leaning toward postponing the project until next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – incline: to have a preference, tendency, or disposition; to be likely toward something
  • antonym – resist: to withstand the effect of something; to not be inclined
  • synonym – tend: to regularly or frequently behave in a particular way or have a certain characteristic
