to liberate

verb
release as a result of a reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a chemical reaction or process) to release (a gas, energy, or other substance).

Examples

to liberate gas
Heating limestone liberates carbon dioxide gas as a byproduct of decomposition.
The reaction liberates a considerable amount of energy in the form of heat.
When water splits during electrolysis, it liberates hydrogen and oxygen gases.

Related Terms

  • synonym – release: allow something to move, escape, or be set free
