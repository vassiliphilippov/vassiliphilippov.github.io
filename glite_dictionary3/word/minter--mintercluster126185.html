minter

noun
person who coins or stamps money
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪntər / (AmE), / ˈmɪntə / (BrE)
a person, especially a skilled worker, who produces coins by stamping or coining them, usually at a mint

Examples

minter of coins
At the old mint, visitors can see a demonstration of how a minter would strike coins by hand.
Historically, the minter played a vital role in ensuring the quality of a nation's currency.
The minter carefully inspected each coin for imperfections before approving them for circulation.

  • synonym – mintmaster: the official in charge of a mint
