organicistic

adjective
relating to organicism
Pronunciation: / ɔːrˌɡænɪˈsɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˌɡanɪˈsɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of organicism; tending toward viewing systems as organic wholes rather than merely collections of parts.

Examples

organicistic worldview
Her approach to urban planning was decidedly organicistic, emphasizing the city as a living organism.
The philosopher advanced an organicistic view of society, seeing it as more than the sum of its parts.
This theory adopts an organicistic perspective to explain the interactions within an ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym – organicism: the view that a system is a complex, organic whole
