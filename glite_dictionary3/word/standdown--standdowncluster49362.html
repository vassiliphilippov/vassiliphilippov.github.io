standdown

noun
temporary halt of military action
Pronunciation: / ˈstændˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈstand.daʊn /; / ˈstandˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a period when military or emergency operations or alertness are temporarily stopped or relaxed

Examples

military standdown period
After the intense fighting, both sides agreed to a standdown to allow humanitarian aid to reach civilians.
Following the standdown, troops were able to rest and resupply before the next phase of operations.
The general ordered a standdown until further instructions from headquarters.
After the successful completion of the operation, the commander ordered a standdown for the troops.
The emergency services remained on standdown once the situation was under control.
There was a scheduled standdown at the missile base following routine drills.

Related Terms

  • antonym – alert: state of heightened readiness, especially in the military
  • synonym – ceasefire: an agreement to stop fighting temporarily
