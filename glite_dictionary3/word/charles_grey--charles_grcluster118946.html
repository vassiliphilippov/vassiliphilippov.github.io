Charles Grey

proper noun
British Prime Minister and reformer (1764–1845)
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑrlz ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
(1764–1845) British Prime Minister, Earl Grey, noted for passing social reforms including the abolition of slavery in the British Empire.

Examples

Charles Grey abolished slavery
Charles Grey played a key role in abolishing slavery in the British Empire.
Many historians regard Charles Grey as a major architect of British modern democracy.
