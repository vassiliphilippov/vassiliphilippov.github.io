arytenoid

noun
cartilage in the larynx related to vocal cords
Pronunciation: / ˌærɪˈtinoɪd / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈtiːnɔɪd / (BrE)
either of two small cartilages at the back of the larynx to which the vocal cords are attached, important in voice production

arytenoid cartilage in larynx
Damage to the arytenoid may cause hoarseness or difficulty breathing.
