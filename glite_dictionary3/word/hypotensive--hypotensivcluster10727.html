hypotensive

noun
person with low blood pressure
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a person who has abnormally low blood pressure

Examples

hypotensive person
As a hypotensive, she is advised to avoid medications that lower blood pressure.
Several hypotensives responded well to dietary changes in the clinical study.
The doctor explained that being a hypotensive can sometimes cause dizziness or fainting.
