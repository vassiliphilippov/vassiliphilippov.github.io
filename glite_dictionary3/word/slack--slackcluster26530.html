slack

noun
period of decline in performance or quality
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
a period when performance, activity, or quality declines or becomes less effective

Examples

a slack in sales
The factory manager was concerned about the recent slack in quality control.
There was a noticeable slack in production during the holiday months.
They worked hard to recover from the slack in employee performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – falloff: a decrease in quantity, quality, or activity
  • synonym – slump: a sudden severe or prolonged fall in performance or quality
