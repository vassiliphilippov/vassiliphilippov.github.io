paleocerebellum

noun
anterior lobe of the cerebellum
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊˌsɛrəˈbɛləm / (AmE), / ˌpaliəʊˌsɛrəˈbɛləm / (BrE)
the part of the cerebellum consisting mainly of the anterior lobe, regarded as one of the earliest parts of the mammalian hindbrain to evolve

Examples

the paleocerebellum of mammals
Comparative studies show that the paleocerebellum is highly conserved across many mammalian species.
Damage to the paleocerebellum can result in muscle tone abnormalities and coordination issues.
Researchers are investigating the role of the paleocerebellum in both sensory and motor functions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – archicerebellum: the oldest part of the cerebellum, primarily related to balance
  • degree higher – neocerebellum: the most recently evolved part of the cerebellum
