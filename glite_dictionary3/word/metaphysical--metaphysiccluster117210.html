metaphysical

adjective
beyond physical or natural laws
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting realities that are beyond what is perceptible to the senses or beyond the laws of nature.

Examples

metaphysical world
She was fascinated by metaphysical questions about the nature of existence and consciousness.
Some philosophers debate the existence of metaphysical forces that lie beyond scientific explanation.
The story explores metaphysical realms where the boundaries of reality and imagination blur.

Related Terms

  • antonym – physical: relating to material nature or the body
  • synonym – supernatural: existing or occurring outside the laws of nature
