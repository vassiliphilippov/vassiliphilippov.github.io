to transistorise

verb
equip with transistors (electronics)
Pronunciation: / trænˈzɪstəˌraɪz / (AmE), / trænˈzɪstəraɪz / (BrE)
To equip (an electronic circuit or device) with transistors, replacing earlier components such as valves or tubes.

Examples

to transistorise old equipment
Engineers are working to transistorise older vacuum tube equipment for greater efficiency.
In the 1960s, many amplifiers were transistorised to enhance performance.
The company decided to transistorise its radios to improve durability and reduce size.
