garnishment

noun
court order to withhold wages or assets
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a legal process by which a court orders part or all of a person's wages or assets to be withheld and sent to someone to whom they owe money

Examples

court garnishment order
After the lawsuit, her savings account was subject to garnishment to satisfy the judgment.
Employers are legally required to comply with a garnishment order when they receive one from the court.
The court issued a garnishment to collect the unpaid debt directly from his paycheck.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attachment: the legal seizure of property to secure a judgment
