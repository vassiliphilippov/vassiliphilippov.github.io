sandman

noun
mythical figure who brings sleep to children
Pronunciation: / ˈsændˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈsændˌman / (BrE)
an imaginary being in folklore, said to sprinkle sand in children's eyes to make them sleepy and encourage them to fall asleep

Examples

the sandman brings sleep
After reading a bedtime story, the boy hoped the sandman would bring him pleasant dreams.
In many cultures, the sandman is a comforting figure associated with peaceful sleep.
The sandman is said to visit children at night and help them fall asleep.
