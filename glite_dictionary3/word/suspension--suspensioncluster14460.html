suspension

noun
mixture of fine particles in a fluid
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛnʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃən / (BrE)
a mixture in which fine particles are dispersed throughout a fluid, supported by buoyancy without dissolving

Examples

suspension of particles
Blood is a complex fluid containing cells in suspension.
The laboratory test required a suspension of the drug in saline.
The muddy water was a suspension of clay and silt particles.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – colloid: a mixture where very small particles are evenly dispersed in another substance
  • antonym – solution: a mixture where a substance is dissolved in another
