to nucleate

verb
form into a nucleus or core
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to form into a nucleus or central core, or to cause something to do so

Examples

to nucleate ice crystals
During freezing, water molecules nucleate to form the initial ice crystals.
Scientists observed how proteins nucleate to create complex cellular structures.
The new material began to nucleate crystals around tiny impurities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aggregate: to form or group into a cluster or mass
  • antonym – disperse: to cause to break up and spread in all directions
