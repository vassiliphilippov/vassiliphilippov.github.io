crosswind

noun
wind blowing across a path
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔs.wɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs.wɪnd / (BrE)
a wind blowing at an angle or perpendicular to the direction of travel of an aircraft, ship, or vehicle

Examples

strong crosswind
Drivers need to be careful when a crosswind pushes against their vehicles on the highway.
Sailing can be challenging when a sudden crosswind catches the boat.
The airplane struggled to land safely in a strong crosswind.

Related Terms

  • antonym – headwind: wind blowing directly against the direction of travel
  • antonym – tailwind: wind blowing in the same direction as travel, from behind
