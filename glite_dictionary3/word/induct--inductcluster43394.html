to induct

verb
produce electric current by induction
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to produce an electric current in a conductor by the influence of a changing magnetic or electric field

Examples

to induct electric current
A changing magnetic field can induct an electric current in a nearby coil.
Engineers designed the device to induct current as the magnetic field varies.
The oscillating circuit will induct voltage into the secondary winding.
