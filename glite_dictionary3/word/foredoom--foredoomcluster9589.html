to foredoom

verb
to doom in advance
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈduːm / (AmE), / fɔːˈduːm / (BrE)
to doom or condemn in advance; to predetermine to a negative fate

Examples

to foredoom a plan
His ambition and pride foredoomed his quest for power to end in tragedy.
Some believed the peace talks were foredoomed by mutual distrust on both sides.
The villain seemed foredoomed to fail from the very beginning of the story.
