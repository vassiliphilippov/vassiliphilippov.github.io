waterpower

noun
energy from moving or falling water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ərˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpaʊə / (BrE)
The energy or force that can be generated from moving or falling water, especially as a source of mechanical power or electricity.

Examples

waterpower plant
Many countries invest in waterpower as a renewable energy source.
The efficiency of waterpower depends on the volume and speed of the flowing water.
The old mill was operated by waterpower from the nearby river.
