cross-eye

noun
eyes turning inward (strabismus)
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌaɪ / (BrE)
A condition in which one or both eyes turn inward toward the nose; a form of strabismus.

Examples

cross-eye condition
Because of his cross-eye, he found it difficult to focus on nearby objects.
Surgery can sometimes help people born with cross-eye achieve better alignment.
The doctor diagnosed her with cross-eye and recommended corrective lenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – strabismus: a condition in which the eyes do not properly align with each other
  • antonym – wall-eye: strabismus in which one or both eyes turn outward
