to leverage

verb
use resources to maximum advantage
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛv.ə.rɪdʒ /; / ˈlɛvərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈliː.vər.ɪdʒ /; / ˈliːvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
to use something you have (such as resources, skills, or influence) to achieve the best result or gain an advantage

Examples

to leverage your skills
Many companies leverage their assets to finance new projects.
She plans to leverage her professional connections to grow the business.
The startup leveraged existing technology to create an innovative product.
The company hopes to leverage its technology to gain a competitive edge.
They plan to leverage their experience to expand into new markets.
We must leverage every available resource to achieve our goals.
