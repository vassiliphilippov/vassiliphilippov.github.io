roller

noun
a ground ball in baseball
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A batted ball that rolls along the ground, especially through the infield.

Examples

ground roller ball
During the game, the shortstop charged a tricky roller and made a great play.
He fielded the roller cleanly and threw to first for the out.
The batter hit a slow roller to third base, giving the runner time to reach first.
