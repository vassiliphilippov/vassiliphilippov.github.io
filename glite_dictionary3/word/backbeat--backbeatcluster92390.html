backbeat

noun
accented beat in music, especially on 2 and 4
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌbit / (AmE), / ˈbækˌbiːt / (BrE)
A strong rhythmic accent on the second and fourth beats of a measure, especially in popular music.

Examples

strong backbeat rhythm
Her new single features a catchy backbeat that makes it easy to dance to.
Many rock and roll songs rely on a strong backbeat to get the audience moving.
The drummer emphasized the backbeat to give the song a driving rhythm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – downbeat: the first beat of a measure, often emphasized
  • synonym – offbeat: an unaccented beat or a weak beat between strong beats
