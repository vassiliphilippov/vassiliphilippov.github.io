flatness

noun
lack of flavor or freshness (food/drink)
Pronunciation: / ˈflætnəs / (AmE), / ˈflætnəs / (BrE)
lack of liveliness, freshness, or excitement; dullness in taste, style, or expression

Examples

flatness of soda
Many people dislike the flatness of day-old soda compared to a freshly opened can.
The flatness of the soup was apparent until she added a pinch of salt and herbs.
The chef tasted the sauce and immediately noticed its flatness, deciding to add more lemon.
She criticized the flatness in his tone during the speech.
Some readers appreciate the flatness of Hemingway's prose for its simplicity.
The flatness of the music made the concert rather uninspiring.

  • synonym – dullness: lack of interest or excitement; the state of being dull
  • antonym – liveliness: the quality of being full of life or spirit
