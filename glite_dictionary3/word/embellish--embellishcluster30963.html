to embellish

verb
make more attractive by adding details
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈbɛlɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛl.ɪʃ / (BrE)
to make something more attractive by adding decorative details or features; to enhance with details, often in a way that improves or exaggerates the original

Examples

to embellish a story
He tends to embellish the facts when telling anecdotes to make them more entertaining.
She liked to embellish her stories with vivid descriptions and a touch of humor.
The artist decided to embellish the mural with gold leaf accents.
