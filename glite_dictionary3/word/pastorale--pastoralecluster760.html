pastorale

noun
musical piece evoking rural life
Pronunciation: / pæstəˈrɑːleɪ / (AmE), / ˌpæstəˈrɑːli / (BrE)
A musical composition, typically with a gentle and lilting character, intended to evoke scenes or feelings of rural life.

Examples

a gentle pastorale melody
Beethoven’s Symphony No. 6, also known as the Pastorale, is famous for its depiction of nature.
The gentle melody of the pastorale reminded the audience of peaceful countryside scenes.
The orchestra performed a beautiful pastorale to open the concert.
