Pseudepigrapha

noun
ancient Jewish texts falsely ascribed to biblical figures
Pronunciation: / suːˌdɛpɪˈgrɑːfə / (AmE), / suːˌdɛpɪˈgrɑːfə / (BrE)
Ancient Jewish writings from about 200 BC to AD 200, attributed falsely to biblical figures, many of which are apocalyptic.

Examples

ancient Pseudepigrapha texts
Many of the Pseudepigrapha offer insights into the hopes and beliefs of Second Temple Judaism.
Scholars debate which writings should be included in the Pseudepigrapha collection.
The apocalyptic visions found in the Pseudepigrapha influenced later Christian literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Apocrypha: biblical or related writings not forming part of the accepted canon of Scripture
