to fraternise

verb
associate or be friendly, often with an enemy
Pronunciation: / ˈfrætərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfrætənaɪz / (BrE)
to associate or form a friendship with someone, especially when it is regarded as improper or unusual, such as with an enemy

Examples

to fraternise with the enemy
During the war, soldiers were forbidden to fraternise with the local population.
Rumours spread that the ambassador had fraternised with opposition leaders during the summit.
She was criticised for fraternising with people considered outsiders by the group.

Related Terms

  • synonym – associate: spend time with or connect socially
  • antonym – avoid: stay away from, not associate with
  • synonym – consort: habitually associate with (someone), typically with the disapproval of others
