to denaturalize

verb
deprive of citizenship
Pronunciation: / diˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to deprive (someone) of the rights and duties of citizenship, typically by legal process

Examples

to denaturalize a citizen
After his criminal conviction, the government decided to denaturalize him.
Many feared they could be denaturalized during the political crackdown.
The law allows the authorities to denaturalize individuals who obtained citizenship through fraud.

Related Terms

  • antonym – naturalize: to grant citizenship to someone
