to plunge

verb
cause to be immersed or involved deeply
Pronunciation: / plʌndʒ / (AmE), / plʌndʒ / (BrE)
to put or push something or someone suddenly and forcefully into a substance, especially a liquid

Examples

to plunge into water
He plunged the vegetables into boiling water for a quick blanch.
Her coach plunged her into intensive training ahead of the competition.
The sudden crisis plunged the company into chaos.
He plunged his hands into the icy river to rinse them off.
She quickly plunged the cloth into the bucket of soapy water.
The chef plunges the vegetables into boiling water to blanch them.
He plunged the knife into the soil to plant the sapling.
She took a deep breath and plunged her hands into the icy water.
The diver plunged straight into the pool from the highest platform.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dip: to put something quickly into a liquid and take it out again
  • synonym – immerse: to deeply involve or dip completely into
