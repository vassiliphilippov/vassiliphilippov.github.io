syncytium

noun
multinucleated cell mass with no internal boundaries
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈsɪtiəm / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɪtɪəm / (BrE)
A mass of cytoplasm containing multiple nuclei enclosed within a single membrane, without internal cell boundaries, as seen in certain tissues such as muscle fibers.

Examples

muscle syncytium
Skeletal muscle tissue develops as a syncytium, allowing coordinated contraction.
Some viruses stimulate cells to join together, forming a large syncytium.
The heart's atrial muscle acts as a functional syncytium to enable synchronized beating.
