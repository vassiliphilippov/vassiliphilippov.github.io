to overtake

verb
be overcome by (emotion or feeling)
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈteɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈteɪk / (BrE)
(of emotions or sensations) to affect someone strongly and suddenly, so that they are unable to control their reaction.

Examples

overtake with fear
A sudden wave of panic overtook her as she entered the dark alley.
As exhaustion overtook him, he could barely keep his eyes open.
He was overtaken by grief when he heard the news.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overwhelm: to have a strong emotional effect on
