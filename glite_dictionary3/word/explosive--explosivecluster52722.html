explosive

adjective
liable to explode; sudden or violent
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
able or likely to explode, or likely to cause a sudden violent outburst or change (literal or figurative)

Examples

explosive reaction
His explosive reaction to the news shocked his friends.
The scientist handled the explosive chemicals with great caution.
There was an explosive argument in the meeting that caught everyone by surprise.
His comments turned what was already an explosive debate into a shouting match.
The manager warned that the meeting could become explosive if tempers flared.
The political climate in the region remains explosive after last week's unrest.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • synonym – volatile: liable to change rapidly, especially for the worse; unstable
