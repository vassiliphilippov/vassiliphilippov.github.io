desynchronisation

noun
loss of synchronization or timing
Pronunciation: / diˌsɪŋkrənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition in which two or more things fail to occur at the same time or lose their coordinated timing

Examples

desynchronisation of signals
Network issues can lead to desynchronisation between audio and video streams.
Researchers observed desynchronisation in brain activity during the test.
The medication caused a desynchronisation of the patient's circadian rhythms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – synchronisation: the process of happening at the same time or at the same rate
