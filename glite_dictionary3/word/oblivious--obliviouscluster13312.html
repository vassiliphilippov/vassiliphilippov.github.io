oblivious

adjective
unaware; failing to notice
Pronunciation: / əˈblɪviəs / (AmE), / əˈblɪvɪəs /; / əˈblɪviəs / (BrE)
not aware of or not noticing what is happening around you

Examples

oblivious to danger
Despite the warning signs, the hikers remained oblivious to the approaching storm.
He walked down the street, oblivious to the rain soaking his clothes.
She was so oblivious to the noise that she continued reading her book.
Children are often oblivious to the worries that trouble adults.
He walked through the crowded room, completely oblivious to the curious stares he received.
The driver was so distracted that she seemed oblivious of the traffic light turning red.
