Pergamon

proper noun
ancient Greek city in Asia Minor
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrɡəmɒn / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡəmən / (BrE)
an ancient Greek city in western Asia Minor, now part of modern-day Turkey, noted for its cultural and historical significance, especially as the origin of parchment.

Examples

ancient Pergamon ruins
Archaeologists unearthed new artifacts from the ruins of Pergamon in western Turkey.
The library of Pergamon was renowned throughout the ancient world for its vast collection of manuscripts.
The technique of making parchment is said to have originated in Pergamon.
