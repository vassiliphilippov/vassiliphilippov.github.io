to closet

verb
shut in a small space for work or discussion
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑzət / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to shut oneself or someone else in a private room or small space, especially for concentrated work or discussion

Examples

to closet oneself away
He closeted his team in the study to brainstorm new strategies.
She closeted herself with her research notes in preparation for the big presentation.
The board members closeted themselves in the meeting room for hours to finalize the contract.
