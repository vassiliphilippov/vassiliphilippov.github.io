legitimation

noun
the act of making legitimate, often legally
Pronunciation: / lɪˌdʒɪtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪtɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone or something legitimate, especially in law; the action of granting lawful status or rights, often used in the context of legitimizing a person's status at birth

Examples

legitimation of birth
He obtained letters of legitimation from the king and secured his inheritance rights.
In some legal systems, legitimation confers all the rights of a legitimate child.
The legitimation of the child was completed after the parents married.
