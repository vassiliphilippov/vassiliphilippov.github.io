to refuse

verb
not allow someone to have something
Pronunciation: / rəˈfjuz / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to not allow or deny someone something they want, such as permission, entry, or an object

Examples

refuse a request
His parents refuse him sweets before dinner.
The club refused her entry because she was underage.
The teacher often refuses students extensions on their homework assignments.
Due to safety concerns, the organizers had to refuse entry to latecomers.
He felt hurt when they decided to refuse his application for membership.
The club may refuse anyone who does not follow the dress code.

Related Terms

  • antonym – admit: to allow entry or membership
  • synonym – deny: to refuse to give or grant something to someone
  • antonym – grant: to give or allow someone what they ask for
  • synonym – reject: to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something or someone
