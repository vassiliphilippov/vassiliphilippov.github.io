to depress

verb
cause prices or values to fall
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛs / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛs / (BrE)
to cause something (such as prices, levels, or activity) to fall, become lower, or be reduced

Examples

to depress prices
A sudden drop in demand can depress the entire stock market.
Heavy exports of steel may depress domestic prices for months.
The oversupply of wheat helped depress prices worldwide.
Freezing temperatures can depress the pressure inside a tire.
High taxes may depress business investment in the region.
The drought greatly depressed the water levels in the lake.
Certain drugs may depress the activity of the nervous system.
High interest rates can depress investment in the housing market.
The government raised taxes to depress consumer spending.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lower: to make or become less in amount, level, or value
  • antonym – raise: to cause prices or values to increase
  • antonym – stimulate: to increase the activity or force of something
