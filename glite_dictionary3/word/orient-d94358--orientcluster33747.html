to orient

verb
familiarize with new surroundings or situations
Pronunciation: / ˈɔriˌɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɔːriənt / (BrE)
to familiarize someone with new surroundings, situations, or information so they can adapt or function effectively

Examples

to orient new employees
A guide was assigned to orient the visitors to the museum's layout.
It took her some time to orient herself after moving to a different country.
The company provides training sessions to help new employees orient themselves to their roles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acclimate: to adapt to a new climate, environment, or situation
  • antonym – disorient: to make someone lose their sense of direction or feel confused
