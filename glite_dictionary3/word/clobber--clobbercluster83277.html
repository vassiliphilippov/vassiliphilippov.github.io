to clobber

verb
hit hard and repeatedly
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑbɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɒbə / (BrE)
to hit someone or something very hard and repeatedly

Examples

to clobber an opponent
If you don't duck, he's going to clobber you with that bat.
The champion clobbered his opponent in the final round of the match.
They were clobbering the punching bag during their intense workout.
