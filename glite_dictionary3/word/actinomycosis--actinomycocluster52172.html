actinomycosis

noun
chronic bacterial disease with abscesses
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.təˌnoʊ.maɪˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪ.nəʊ.maɪˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a chronic bacterial disease characterized by the formation of painful abscesses, most commonly in the mouth, lungs, or gastrointestinal tract, often resulting in swelling and discharge of pus

Examples

actinomycosis infection symptoms
Humans can contract actinomycosis through direct contact with infected animals or contaminated materials.
The veterinarian diagnosed the swollen jaw in the cow as actinomycosis.
Treatment for actinomycosis typically involves a long course of antibiotics to fully resolve the infection.
