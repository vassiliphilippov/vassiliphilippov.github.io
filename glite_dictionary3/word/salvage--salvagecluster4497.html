salvage

noun
property saved from disaster or damage
Pronunciation: / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ / (BrE)
property or goods that have been saved from damage, especially from a shipwreck, fire, or disaster

Examples

salvage goods
After the fire, only a few items of salvage could be recovered from the ruins.
Much of the salvage from the wreck was auctioned off to collectors.
The ship's salvage was carefully inventoried by the insurance company.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loss: property or goods that have been destroyed or cannot be recovered
  • synonym – wreckage: debris or remains of something destroyed
