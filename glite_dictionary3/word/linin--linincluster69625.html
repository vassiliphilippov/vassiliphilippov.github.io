linin

noun
obsolete term for nuclear matrix material
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
(obsolete) A network of viscous material in the cell nucleus, formerly thought to support chromatin granules.

Examples

linin in cell nucleus
Although linin is no longer a scientific term, it appears in older biology literature.
Early cytologists believed that linin formed a supportive framework within the cell nucleus.
The theory involving linin has since been replaced by a more accurate understanding of nuclear structure.
