coarse-grained

adjective
broad, low-detail granularity
Pronunciation: / kɔrsˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / kɔːsˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
Having a relatively large or broad level of detail; characterized by low granularity or general categories rather than fine distinction (often said of data, analysis, policies, or system design).

Examples

coarse-grained analysis
The team adopted a coarse-grained approach to the market study to prioritize speed over detail.
In distributed systems, developers must decide between coarse-grained and fine-grained locking strategies for performance.
Policy makers often use coarse-grained categories in census forms to simplify data collection and analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fine-grained: having small, detailed distinctions or a high level of detail; opposite of coarse-grained
  • synonym – low-resolution: lacking detail or fine distinctions; similar in meaning when describing data or representations
