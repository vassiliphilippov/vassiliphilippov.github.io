to return

verb
respond in kind; reciprocate
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɝn / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to respond or reply to an action, gesture, or statement

Examples

to return a favor
If someone helps you, it's nice to return the favor in the future.
She was delighted when he returned her affection with a smile.
When praised, it's polite to return the compliment.
He returned that he had no knowledge of the incident.
When asked about his plans, he returned with a question of his own.
When challenged, she always returns with a clever remark.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to a question or remark
  • synonym – reciprocate: to respond to a gesture or action by making a corresponding one
  • synonym – reply: to respond, especially in speech or writing
  • synonym – retort: to answer in a sharp, witty, or angry manner
