mitre

noun
ceremonial headdress for bishops
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə / (BrE)
A tall, folding headdress, usually pointed at the front and back, worn by bishops and certain other clergy during ceremonies.

Examples

bishop's mitre hat
During the procession, the golden mitre gleamed under the cathedral lights.
