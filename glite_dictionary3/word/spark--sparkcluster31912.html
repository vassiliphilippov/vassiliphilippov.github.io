spark

noun
small sign of a feeling or quality
Pronunciation: / spɑrk / (AmE), / spɑːk / (BrE)
a small but noticeable amount of a feeling, quality, or liveliness that could grow into something more

Examples

spark of hope
He still showed a spark of hope, despite all the setbacks.
The teacher noticed a spark of curiosity in the students during the experiment.
There was a spark of excitement in her eyes when she saw the results.
Despite her age, a spark of joy lit up her face whenever she laughed.
The old man still had a spark of playfulness in his smile.
There was a mischievous spark in her eyes as she told the joke.
