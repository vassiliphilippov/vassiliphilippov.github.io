reprinting

noun
an unchanged reissued publication
Pronunciation: / riˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
A published work, such as a book, that has been printed again in an unchanged form and offered for sale.

Examples

first reprinting of a book
The first reprinting of the novel sold out within weeks of its release.
The publisher announced a reprinting due to high demand from readers.
This reprinting includes no changes from the original version published ten years ago.

  • synonym – edition: a particular form or version of a published text
