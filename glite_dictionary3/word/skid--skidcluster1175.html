skid

noun
plank used for sliding objects
Pronunciation: / skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
one of a pair of long, flat planks or supports, placed on the ground to make a track for rolling or sliding heavy objects

Examples

wooden skid support
A wooden skid can prevent cargo from tipping during transport.
Engineers used a pair of skids to slide the heavy machinery into position.
The workers placed a skid under the crate to help move it across the floor.
