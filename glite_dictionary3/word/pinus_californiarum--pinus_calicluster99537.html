Pinus californiarum

proper noun
species of small pine tree with a single needle per fascicle
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪnəs ˌkælɪˌfɔrniˈærəm / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs ˌkælɪˌfɔːnɪˈɑːrəm / (BrE)
A species of very small pine tree, similar to the Rocky Mountain pinyon but distinguished by having a single needle per fascicle; found in a range similar to Parry's pinyon.

Examples

Pinus californiarum pine tree
Botanists have identified Pinus californiarum in several protected areas across southern California.
Recent conservation efforts aim to preserve the rare habitat of Pinus californiarum.
The unique single-needle clusters of Pinus californiarum distinguish it from other regional pinyon pines.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Pinus monophylla: Rocky Mountain pinyon, an allied species with similar ecological range
  • synonym – Pinus quadrifolia: Parry's pinyon, a similar pine species in the same region
