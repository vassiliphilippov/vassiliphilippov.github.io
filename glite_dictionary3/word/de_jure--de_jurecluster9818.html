de jure

adjective, adverb
according to law; by right
Pronunciation: / deɪ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / di ˈdʒʊəreɪ /; / deɪ ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
by law or according to legal right (not necessarily in practice); used as an adjective or adverb

Examples

de jure government
He is the de jure owner of the property, though others reside there.
Slavery was abolished de jure in the 19th century, but discrimination persisted.
The country had a de jure government, but the military held power in reality.
Although he was the de jure owner of the company, his partner managed all its operations.
Racial segregation was abolished de jure, but remained in practice for many years.
Their marriage was not recognized de jure until new laws were passed.
