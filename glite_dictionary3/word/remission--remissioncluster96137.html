remission

noun
legal referral of a case to another court
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The act of referring a legal case to another court or authority for decision.

Examples

case remission order
Requests for remission are sometimes made when jurisdictional issues arise.
The defense sought remission so that a higher court could hear the matter anew.
The judge granted a remission of the case to the appellate court for further consideration.
