to hectograph

verb
make copies using a hectograph
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛktəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɛktəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to reproduce or copy (something) using a hectograph duplicator, an early device for making multiple copies of written or drawn material

Examples

to hectograph a letter
Before photocopiers became common, people would hectograph newsletters at home.
The teacher used to hectograph exam sheets for the entire class.
They hectographed dozens of handouts for the club meeting.
