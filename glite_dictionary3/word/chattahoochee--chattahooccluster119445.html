Chattahoochee

proper noun
river in southeastern United States
Pronunciation: / ˌtʃætəˈhuːtʃi / (AmE), / ˌtʃætəˈhuːtʃi / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States that rises in northern Georgia and flows southwest and south, joining the Flint River at the Florida border to form the Apalachicola River

Examples

the Chattahoochee River
Many outdoor enthusiasts enjoy kayaking along the Chattahoochee.
The Chattahoochee River forms part of the border between Georgia and Alabama.
The city of Columbus, Georgia, lies on the banks of the Chattahoochee.
