backstop

noun
precaution or safeguard for emergencies
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstɒp / (BrE)
a precaution or safeguard put in place to prevent failure or address emergencies if other measures do not work

Examples

financial backstop support
She served as a backstop for the team, ready to help if anything went wrong.
The government created a financial backstop to reassure the markets during the crisis.
The policy was intended as a backstop in case the primary plan failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contingency: a possible emergency or unexpected event; something done to prepare for this
  • synonym – safeguard: something that protects against possible danger or trouble
