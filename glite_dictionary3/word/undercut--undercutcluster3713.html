to undercut

verb
cut away material from underside
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈkʌt /; / ˌʌndərˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌt /; / ˌʌndəˈkʌt / (BrE)
to cut away material from the underside of something, often to create an overhang or to weaken or remove it (used in carpentry, mining, or general objects)

Examples

to undercut a block
Carefully undercutting the wood allows for more dramatic shadows in the finished carving.
The sculptor had to undercut the marble to achieve the intricate details in the statue.
To create the illusion of depth, the artisan undercut the edges of the relief panel.
Before removing the stone, the miners had to undercut the vein of ore.
The carpenter carefully undercut the wood to fit it around the pipe.
To harvest the turf properly, workers often undercut the bottom layer first.
