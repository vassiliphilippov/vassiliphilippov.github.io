vulcanite

noun
a hard, vulcanized rubber
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈvʌlkənaɪt / (BrE)
A hard, nonresilient form of rubber produced by vulcanizing natural rubber with sulfur, used in manufacturing and dentistry.

Examples

vulcanite denture base
Dentists once crafted dentures from vulcanite because of its hardness and moldability.
Historically, vulcanite was used to make combs and jewelry before plastics were common.
The pipes were fitted with vulcanite mouthpieces for durability.
