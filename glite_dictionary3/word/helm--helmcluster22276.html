helm

noun
steering mechanism of a vessel
Pronunciation: / hɛlm / (AmE), / hɛlm / (BrE)
the steering mechanism of a vessel, such as a wheel or tiller, used to guide its direction

Examples

the helm of a ship
He handed over the helm to his first mate during the night watch.
The captain stood at the helm, calmly steering the ship through the storm.
With one hand on the helm, she navigated the narrow channel carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rudder: a flat piece, usually of wood, metal, or plastic, attached vertically to the stern of a boat for steering
