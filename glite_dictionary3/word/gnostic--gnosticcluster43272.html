Gnostic

noun
adherent of Gnosticism
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
A person who advocates or adheres to Gnosticism, an ancient religious movement emphasizing esoteric knowledge of spiritual truth.

Examples

Gnostic thinker
As a Gnostic, he devoted his life to the pursuit of spiritual truth.
Some Gnostics believed that salvation came through secret knowledge.
The early Christian church struggled with the influence of Gnostic teachings.
