to trim

verb
of an aircraft, be in flight equilibrium
Pronunciation: / trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
to adjust the control surfaces of an aircraft to achieve balance or maintain a desired attitude during flight

Examples

to trim an airplane
A well-balanced aircraft will trim at its cruising speed without constant input.
If the elevator is set correctly, the plane should trim automatically after takeoff.
The pilot adjusted the controls so the airplane would trim during cruise.
Modern planes can automatically trim themselves for level flight.
She carefully trimmed the aircraft to maintain a steady altitude.
The pilot had to trim the airplane before the long flight.
