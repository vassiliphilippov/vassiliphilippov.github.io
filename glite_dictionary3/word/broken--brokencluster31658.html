broken

adjective
not fulfilled; violated (of promises, contracts, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkən / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən / (BrE)
(especially of promises or contracts) having been violated, disregarded, or not fulfilled.

Examples

broken promise
Many voters become cynical after too many broken campaign promises.
She felt betrayed by all the broken promises he had made to her.
The company faced legal action due to broken contracts with their partners.

Related Terms

  • synonym – breached: violated, especially in the context of contracts
  • antonym – fulfilled: carried out as promised or expected
