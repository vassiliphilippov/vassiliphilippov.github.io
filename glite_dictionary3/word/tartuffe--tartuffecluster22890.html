Tartuffe

noun
hypocrite who pretends to religious piety
Pronunciation: / tɑrˈtuf / (AmE), / tɑːˈtjuːf / (BrE)
a hypocrite who feigns religious virtue or piety, especially for personal gain, after the character in Molière's play

Examples

a Tartuffe in church
He called his opponent a Tartuffe for preaching family values while living a secret double life.
In literature classes, students often study the character of Tartuffe as the archetype of religious hypocrisy.
The journalist exposed the politician as a modern-day Tartuffe, masking selfish motives with pious words.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hypocrite: one who pretends to have moral or religious beliefs they do not possess
