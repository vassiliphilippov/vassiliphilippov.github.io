angiohemophilia

noun
rare inherited bleeding disorder
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒioʊˌhiːmoʊˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌandʒiəʊˌhiːməˈfɪliːə / (BrE)
a rare inherited bleeding disorder, also known as von Willebrand's disease, characterized by a deficiency of a coagulation factor and mucosal bleeding

Examples

angiohemophilia diagnosis
The hematologist diagnosed the patient with angiohemophilia after several episodes of unexplained nasal bleeding.
Treatment for angiohemophilia often involves replacing the deficient blood clotting factor.
Unlike classic hemophilia, angiohemophilia can affect both males and females equally.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – hemophilia: a group of disorders causing impaired blood clotting
  • synonym – von Willebrand disease: a common inherited disorder of blood clotting; another common term for angiohemophilia
