kinship

noun
family relationship by blood, marriage, or adoption
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪnˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɪnʃɪp / (BrE)
(in anthropology) the state of being related by blood, marriage, or adoption; relationships among family members.

Examples

family kinship ties
Adoption can create legal kinship ties where no biological connection exists.
Anthropologists study patterns of kinship to understand how societies organize families.
The sense of kinship between the two tribes was strengthened by years of intermarriage.
