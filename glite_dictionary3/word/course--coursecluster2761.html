to course

verb
hunt game with hounds
Pronunciation: / kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
(of people) to hunt (game animals, especially hares) with hounds by sight rather than scent.

Examples

to course a hare
Greyhounds are traditionally bred and trained to course small game by sight.
In medieval England, nobles would often course hares across the open fields.
The sport of coursing was once popular among the English aristocracy.
