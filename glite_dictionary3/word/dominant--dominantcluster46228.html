dominant

adjective
gene that masks the effect of others
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːmɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
(of a gene) producing the same observable trait whether paired with an identical or a different allele.

Examples

dominant gene trait
A child only needs one dominant gene from either parent to express the trait.
Brown eyes are typically a dominant genetic trait.
In pea plants, the dominant allele determines flower color, even if only one copy is present.

Related Terms

  • antonym – recessive: (of a gene) producing its effect only when paired with an identical allele
