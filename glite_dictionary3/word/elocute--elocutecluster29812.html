to elocute

verb
to speak in an elocutionary manner
Pronunciation: / ˈɛləˌkjut / (AmE), / ˈɛləˌkjuːt / (BrE)
To speak or recite in an elocutionary manner, often with particular attention to clarity and expression.

Examples

to elocute a poem
During the competition, she elocuted the poem with precision and feeling.
It's important to elocute clearly when addressing a large crowd.
The speaker elocuted each line as if performing in front of an audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – declaim: to speak rhetorically or with strong feeling
  • synonym – recite: to repeat aloud from memory, often formally
