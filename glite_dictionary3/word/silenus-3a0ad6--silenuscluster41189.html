silenus

noun
minor woodland deity, companion of Dionysus
Pronunciation: / saɪˈliːnəs / (AmE), / saɪˈliːnəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of the minor woodland gods, companions of Dionysus, often depicted as old men, similar to satyrs.

Examples

ancient silenus statue
A silenus was often shown in ancient Greek art as an elderly, drunken figure with a horse's tail.
According to legend, the silenus accompanied Dionysus through the forests and countryside.
In the myth, a silenus gives wise advice to King Midas after being found asleep in a vineyard.
