monochromia

noun
complete color blindness
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkroʊmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrəʊmiə / (BrE)
A complete inability to perceive colors, where colors can only be distinguished by their brightness.

Examples

monochromia affects vision
Due to his monochromia, artistic activities involving color were especially challenging for him.
People with monochromia see the world only in varying shades of light and dark.
The genetic basis for monochromia is different from that of partial color blindness.
