dispossession

noun
the act of being deprived of property
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspoʊˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of depriving someone of land, property, or possessions, especially by legal process

Examples

dispossession of land
His dispossession was carried out following a lengthy court battle.
Laws were enacted to prevent the dispossession of indigenous lands.
The family faced dispossession after failing to pay the mortgage for several months.
