to plant

verb
place secretly for observation or deception
Pronunciation: / plænt / (AmE), / plɑːnt / (BrE)
to place someone or something secretly in a particular position for the purpose of observation, deception, or obtaining information

Examples

to plant evidence
Police managed to plant a listening device in the suspect's office.
She suspected someone had planted false evidence in her room.
The agency decided to plant a spy inside the enemy organization.

  • synonym – install: put something in position for use or operation
  • antonym – remove: take something away from a location
