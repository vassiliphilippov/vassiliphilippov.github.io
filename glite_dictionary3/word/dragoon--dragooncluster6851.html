to dragoon

verb
to force or subjugate by force
Pronunciation: / drəˈɡun / (AmE), / drəˈɡuːn / (BrE)
to force or coerce someone to do something, especially by threats, pressure, or force

Examples

to dragoon workers
He felt his colleagues were being dragooned into accepting the new regulations against their will.
The government attempted to dragoon the population into supporting its policies by deploying the army.
The protesters refused to be dragooned into submission by the show of force.
He refused to be dragooned by his peers into making a risky investment.
She felt she had been dragooned into organizing the event against her will.
The company tried to dragoon its employees into working overtime.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coerce: to force someone to do something by using threats or force
  • antonym – persuade: to convince someone by reasoning or pleading
