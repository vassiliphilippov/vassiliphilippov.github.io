indiscreet

adjective
lacking discretion or good judgment
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈskrit / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskriːt / (BrE)
not careful in speech or action, especially so as to reveal secrets or offend; lacking discretion or good judgment

Examples

indiscreet comment
He was indiscreet in discussing confidential information with a stranger.
Her indiscreet remarks at the party embarrassed everyone.
It would be indiscreet to share those details without permission.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discreet: careful and prudent in speech or action; keeping things confidential
