amygdalotomy

noun
surgical severing of amygdala fibers
Pronunciation: / əˌmɪɡdəˈlɑːtəmi / (AmE), / əˌmɪɡdəˈlɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which fibers of the amygdala are severed to reduce symptoms such as extreme uncontrollable violence

Examples

perform amygdalotomy surgery
Due to its risks, amygdalotomy is rarely performed in modern neurosurgery.
