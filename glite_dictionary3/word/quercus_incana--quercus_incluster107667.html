Quercus incana

noun
a shrubby oak tree of the southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs ɪnˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ɪnˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A small, semi-evergreen shrubby oak tree native to the southeastern United States, with hairy young branches and leaves that taper to a slender bristly point.
