paralysis

noun
loss of ability to move a body part
Pronunciation: / pəˈræləsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈralɪsɪs / (BrE)
Loss of the ability to move all or part of the body and sometimes to feel anything in it, caused by illness, injury, or damage to the nerves.

Examples

suffer paralysis
After the stroke, paralysis affected the right side of his face.
Temporary paralysis can occur after certain medical procedures.
The accident left her with complete paralysis in both legs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – immobility: the state of not being able to move
  • synonym – palsy: partial or complete loss of movement or uncontrollable tremor
