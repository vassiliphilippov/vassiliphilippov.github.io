hardening

noun
securing computer systems
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdənɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing and information security) the process of making a computer system, network, or application more secure by configuring settings, removing unnecessary services, applying patches, and reducing its attack surface.

Examples

hardening server security
The security team began hardening of our servers to fend off attacks.
Server hardening includes removing unnecessary services and closing open network ports proactively.
As part of compliance, auditors checked the company's hardening checklist and patch schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym – securing: the act of protecting systems or data against threats and unauthorized access
  • synonym – system hardening: making a system more secure by applying configuration changes and updates
