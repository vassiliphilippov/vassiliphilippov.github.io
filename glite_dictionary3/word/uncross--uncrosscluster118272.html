to uncross

verb
move from crossed to uncrossed position
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to move (a part of the body or two objects) from a crossed to an uncrossed position

Examples

to uncross your legs
He uncrossed his legs to stretch them out under the table.
Please uncross your fingers so we can start the exercise.
She nervously uncrossed her arms as she spoke.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cross: to place one thing over another, especially forming an X shape
