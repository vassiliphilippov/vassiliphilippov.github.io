consonant

noun
speech sound other than a vowel
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a speech sound produced by obstructing or restricting the flow of air somewhere in the vocal tract, as opposed to a vowel

Examples

consonant sound
In the word 'cat', both 'c' and 't' are consonants.
She found it difficult to pronounce words with many consonants in a row.
The letter 'b' represents a consonant sound in English.

Related Terms

  • antonym – vowel: a speech sound produced without significant constriction of the airflow, as opposed to a consonant
