technophobe

noun
person who avoids technology
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛk.nəˌfoʊb / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.nə.fəʊb / (BrE)
A person who dislikes, fears, or avoids modern technology.

Examples

technophobe avoids gadgets
Despite being a technophobe, she learned to order groceries online during the pandemic.
My grandfather is a self-proclaimed technophobe who refuses to use a smartphone.
The company offered training sessions to help technophobes adapt to the new software.

Related Terms

  • synonym – luddite: someone opposed or resistant to technological change
  • antonym – technophile: a person who enthusiastically embraces new technology
