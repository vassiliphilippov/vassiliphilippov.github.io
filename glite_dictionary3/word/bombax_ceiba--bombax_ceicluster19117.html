Bombax ceiba

noun
East Indian silk cotton tree
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑmbæks ˈseɪbə / (AmE), / ˈbɒmbaks ˈsiːbə / (BrE)
An East Indian silk cotton tree, known for yielding seed fibers that are of lower quality than kapok.

Examples

Bombax ceiba tree flowers
In rural India, the Bombax ceiba is valued for its medicinal and economic uses.
The fibers produced by the Bombax ceiba are commonly used for stuffing pillows and mattresses.
Unlike kapok, the fiber from the Bombax ceiba is considered less resilient but still useful for insulation.
