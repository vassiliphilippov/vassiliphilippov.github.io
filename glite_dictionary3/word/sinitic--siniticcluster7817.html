Sinitic

noun
subgroup of Sino-Tibetan languages
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the group of Sino-Tibetan languages that includes Chinese and its varieties.

Examples

Sinitic languages
Linguists have debated the classification of Sinitic languages for decades.
Mandarin, Cantonese, and Hakka are considered Sinitic languages within the Sino-Tibetan family.
The spread of Sinitic dialects has greatly influenced the vocabulary of neighboring regions.
