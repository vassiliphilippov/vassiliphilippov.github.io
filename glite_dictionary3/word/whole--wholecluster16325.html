whole

adjective
having both the same parents (of siblings)
Pronunciation: / hoʊl / (AmE), / həʊl / (BrE)
(of siblings) having the same mother and father; sharing both parents.

Examples

whole brother
Although they were whole brothers, their personalities were quite different.
She is my whole sister, not my half-sister.
The law grants inheritance rights only to whole siblings in this situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – half: (of siblings) having one parent in common, either mother or father, but not both
