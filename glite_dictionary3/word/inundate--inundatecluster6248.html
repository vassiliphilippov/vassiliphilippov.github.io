to inundate

verb
to flood or cover completely with water
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.ʌn.deɪt /; / ˈɪnʌnˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.ʌn.deɪt / (BrE)
to flood or overwhelm something with a large amount, especially of water

Examples

to inundate a city
After the heavy rain, the river quickly inundated the surrounding fields.
During the hurricane, entire neighborhoods were inundated with seawater.
If the dam breaks, the valley will be inundated within hours.
Heavy rains can easily inundate low-lying neighborhoods within hours.
If left unchecked, the river could inundate the entire valley.
We were inundated with emails after the announcement went public.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flood: to cover with water, typically due to excessive rain or overflowing
  • synonym – overwhelm: to overpower or flood with something, especially emotions or things
