Judas Maccabaeus

proper noun
Jewish leader who led revolt in Judea, 2nd century BC
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒudəs ˌmækəˈbiəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəs ˌmækəˈbiːəs / (BrE)
(c. 161 BC) Jewish leader who led a revolt against Seleucid rule in Judea, recapturing Jerusalem; subject of the books I and II Maccabees.

Examples

Judas Maccabaeus led a revolt
Handel composed an oratorio called Judas Maccabaeus, celebrating the hero's victories.
Many Jewish families discuss Judas Maccabaeus during Hanukkah celebrations.
The story of Judas Maccabaeus is recounted in the first and second books of Maccabees.
