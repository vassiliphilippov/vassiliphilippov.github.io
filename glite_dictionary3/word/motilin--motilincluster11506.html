motilin

noun
gastrointestinal hormone stimulating digestion
Pronunciation: / moʊˈtɪlɪn / (AmE), / məʊˈtɪlɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced in the gastrointestinal tract that stimulates smooth muscle contractions in the stomach and small intestine

Examples

motilin hormone release
Motilin plays a crucial role in regulating digestive motility between meals.
Scientists discovered that motilin levels rise just before the stomach starts to contract.
Some drugs can affect the body's production of motilin, influencing gastrointestinal movement.
