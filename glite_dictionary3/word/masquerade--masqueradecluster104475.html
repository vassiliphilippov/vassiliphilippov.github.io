masquerade

noun
false show or pretense
Pronunciation: / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a false outward appearance or display intended to conceal the truth; a pretense

Examples

emotional masquerade
Her kindness was merely a masquerade to hide her real intentions.
The company’s commitment to the environment turned out to be a masquerade.
Tired of the masquerade, he decided to reveal his true feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – facade: a deceptive outward appearance
  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • antonym – sincerity: the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
