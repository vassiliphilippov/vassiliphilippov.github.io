flashback

noun
sudden, vivid re-experience of a past event
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌbak / (BrE)
a sudden and vivid memory or recurrence of a past experience, especially one brought on by hallucinogenic drugs or trauma

Examples

trauma flashback experience
After taking the drug, some users experienced a flashback several months later.
Hearing the song gave her a vivid flashback to her childhood summers.
The war veteran suffered a terrifying flashback that made him relive the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • antonym – amnesia: partial or total loss of memory
  • synonym – memory: the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
  • synonym – reminiscence: a recollection of a past event
