harbour

noun
sheltered port for ships
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
A sheltered area of water next to the coast where ships can anchor safely, especially for loading or unloading cargo.

Examples

busy harbour area
Fishing boats crowded the small harbour after the storm.
The ancient harbour was once a vital trading center.
The ship waited outside the harbour for permission to dock.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anchorage: a place where vessels anchor
  • antonym – open sea: the part of the sea that is far from land or shelter
  • synonym – port: a town or city with a harbor where ships load or unload, especially one where customs officers are stationed
