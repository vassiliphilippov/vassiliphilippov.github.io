ellwand

noun
historical measuring rod (an ell long)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛlˌwænd / (AmE), / ˈɛlˌwɒnd / (BrE)
a rod about one ell (approximately 45 inches) in length, used as a standard unit of official measurement, especially in England and Scotland historically

Examples

ellwand for measuring cloth
Craftsmen ensured their cloth met regulations by measuring it with an ellwand.
In medieval times, every town was required to keep an ellwand for trade.
The ellwand was often displayed in market squares as a reference standard.

Related Terms

  • synonym – yardstick: a measuring stick one yard long, used as a standard of measurement
