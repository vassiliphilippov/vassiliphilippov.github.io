grip

noun
traction or friction between surface and object
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪp / (AmE), / ɡrɪp / (BrE)
the traction or friction between a moving object and the surface it moves on, especially referring to how well a vehicle's tires hold the road

Examples

tire grip
High-performance tires provide better grip in wet conditions.
The car lost its grip on the icy road and started to skid.
The cyclist praised the tires for their excellent grip during sharp turns.

Related Terms

  • synonym – traction: the adhesive friction of a wheel or tire on a surface
