irredeemable

adjective
impossible to reform or correct
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈdiməbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbl / (BrE)
impossible to reform, improve, or correct; incapable of being saved or made better.

Examples

irredeemable criminal behavior
After years of criminal behavior, he was dismissed as irredeemable by the authorities.
Some critics argued that the novel's protagonist was irredeemable, lacking any qualities worth saving.
The damage to the environment was deemed irredeemable after the spill.

Related Terms

  • synonym – incorrigible: not able to be corrected or reformed
  • antonym – redeemable: able to be saved, improved, or corrected
