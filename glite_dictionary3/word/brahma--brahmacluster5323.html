Brahma

noun
a breed of heat-resistant cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmə / (BrE)
a large breed of heat- and tick-resistant humped cattle, originally developed in the U.S. by crossbreeding Indian cattle, used mainly for crossbreeding and beef production

Examples

Brahma cattle breed
At the livestock show, the prize-winning bull was a massive Brahma.
Many farmers cross Brahma with other cattle breeds to improve hardiness and meat quality.
The rancher decided to breed Brahma cattle because of their resistance to heat and ticks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Brahman: a breed of zebu cattle originating from India, used in the development of Brahma cattle
