backdoor

noun
secret or unofficial means of access
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈbæk.dɔː / (BrE)
a secret or unofficial way of gaining access to a place, organization, or position, often by circumventing normal procedures or rules

Examples

find a backdoor way
By using personal connections, she gained entry to the club through a backdoor.
He was accused of using a backdoor to secure the contract.
The hacker found a backdoor into the company's internal network.

Related Terms

  • antonym – front door: open, official, or direct means of access
  • synonym – loophole: means of evading a rule or regulation
  • synonym – shortcut: an alternative and faster way of doing something
