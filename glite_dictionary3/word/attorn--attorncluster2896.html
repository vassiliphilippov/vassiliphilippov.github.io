to attorn

verb
acknowledge a new landowner as landlord (legal, historical)
Pronunciation: / əˈtɜrn / (AmE), / əˈtɔːn / (BrE)
(legal, historical) To formally acknowledge a new landowner as one's landlord, especially by tenants.

Examples

to attorn to a landlord
Historically, it was common practice for villagers to attorn before the local magistrate.
The lease stated that the tenant must attorn upon notice of a change in ownership.
Upon sale of the estate, the tenants were required to attorn to the new landlord.
