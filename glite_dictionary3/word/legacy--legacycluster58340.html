legacy

noun
gift of property left by will
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛɡəsi / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡəsi / (BrE)
money, property, or digital assets left to someone in a will after a person dies

Examples

legacy of money
She used her legacy from her aunt to start a new business.
The legacy consisted of several valuable paintings and family heirlooms.
Under his grandfather's will, Mark received a small legacy.
Estate planners now advise clients to include their legacy of social media and passwords in wills.
After her death, the family struggled to access her online photos because her legacy was poorly documented.
Many services offer tools to help users plan their digital legacies and assign account managers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bequest: a gift of personal property by will
  • antonym – devise: a gift of real property by will
  • synonym – digital assets: files, accounts, cryptocurrencies, and other electronic property with value or personal significance
  • synonym – digital estate: the totality of someone's online accounts, data, and digital property
  • degree higher – inheritance: assets received from someone after their death
