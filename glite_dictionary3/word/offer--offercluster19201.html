to offer

verb
say you will do something; volunteer
Pronunciation: / ˈɑfər /; / ˈɑː.fɚ /; / ˈɔfɚ /; / ˈɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈɒfə /; / ˈɒf.ə / (BrE)
to say you are willing to do something, propose an action, or express willingness (including to help, to negotiate, to pay, to resign, or to marry)

Examples

to offer help
I offered to watch their children so they could go out for dinner.
She offered to pick up some groceries on her way home from work.
When no one else would help, he offered to stay late and finish the project.
He finally offered to her after years of courtship.
In a touching ceremony, he offered his hand in marriage.
When James offered, it took her completely by surprise.
She offered a generous sum for the rare book at the auction.
The company decided to offer $10,000 to purchase the antique car.
We are planning to offer more than the asking price to secure the house.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bid: to offer a price, especially at an auction
  • synonym – propose: to ask someone to marry you
  • degree higher – threaten: to state one's intention to cause harm or take action as a warning
  • synonym – volunteer: to freely offer to do something
