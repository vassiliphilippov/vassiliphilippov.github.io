republic

noun
political system with elected representatives
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
a country or political system where the people or their elected representatives hold power, and there is no king or queen

Examples

modern republic
After gaining independence, the country decided to form a republic governed by elected officials.
Many ancient nations, such as Rome, became a republic after overthrowing their monarchs.
The United States is often described as a republic rather than a pure democracy.
France became a republic after the monarchy was abolished.
In a republic, citizens elect leaders to represent their interests.
Ireland is an example of a republic in Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – democracy: a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • antonym – monarchy: a system of government with a monarch at the head
