postposition

noun
a word placed after what it governs (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊst.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A word or grammatical element placed after the word or phrase it governs, especially a type of adposition occurring after its complement; also, the act of placing one element after another.

In some languages, a postposition is used instead of a preposition to indicate relationships between nouns.
