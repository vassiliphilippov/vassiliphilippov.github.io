crash

noun
sudden economic or stock market collapse
Pronunciation: / kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
a sudden large decline in business activity or stock prices, especially one that triggers further failures

Examples

stock market crash
After the housing market crash, property values fell dramatically across the country.
Many investors lost their savings during the stock market crash of 1929.
The economic crash led to widespread unemployment and bankruptcies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – boom: a period of rapid economic growth or increasing prices
  • synonym – collapse: a sudden complete failure of an institution, company, or system
