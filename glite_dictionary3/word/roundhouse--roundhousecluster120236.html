roundhouse

noun
circular locomotive repair building
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊndˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈraʊndˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A circular building used for servicing and repairing locomotives, typically with rail tracks radiating from a central turntable.

Examples

railway roundhouse
Engineers guided the train into the roundhouse for routine maintenance.
Several locomotives were stored in the massive roundhouse overnight.
The old roundhouse is now a railway museum open to visitors.
