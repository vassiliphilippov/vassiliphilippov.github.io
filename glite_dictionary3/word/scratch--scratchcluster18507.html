to scratch

verb
remove by erasing or crossing out
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ / (BrE)
to remove, delete, or cancel something, such as erasing writing or calling off an event

Examples

to scratch a plan
He quickly scratched his comment from the notes after realizing it was incorrect.
If you make a mistake, just scratch the word and write it again.
Let me scratch that last idea—I don't think it will work.
Due to unforeseen circumstances, we had to scratch the afternoon meeting.
Let's scratch that idea for now and revisit it later.
The organizers decided to scratch the concert because of bad weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym – call off: to cancel plans or an event
  • synonym – cancel: to decide that something planned will not happen
  • synonym – delete: to remove or eliminate, especially written or digital material
  • antonym – retain: to keep or not remove
  • antonym – schedule: to arrange or plan an event
  • synonym – strike: to delete or cross out (a word or remark)
