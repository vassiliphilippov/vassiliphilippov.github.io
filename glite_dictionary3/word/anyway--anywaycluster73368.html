anyway

adverb
used to add or explain; change of subject
Pronunciation: / ˈɛniˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛnɪweɪ / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that explains or supports a previous statement or adds information, often after an interruption or change of subject.

Examples

anyway, moving on
I'm not sure how much it will cost; anyway, we need to fix the car.
She didn't want to go out, but anyway, her friends insisted.
We missed the train, but anyway, we arrived in time for dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anyhow: used similarly to introduce or emphasize a statement regardless of preceding circumstances
  • synonym – in any case: used to indicate that something remains true regardless of other factors
  • antonym – nevertheless: used to introduce a contrasting statement
