temperamental

adjective
liable to mood swings
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛmpəɹəˈmɛnɾəl /; / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
likely to change mood or behavior suddenly; unpredictable or moody

Examples

temperamental machine
Although the car is powerful, it can be temperamental and difficult to start in cold weather.
Her temperamental nature made working with her challenging at times.
The director found the actor too temperamental to cast in the leading role.
Her temperamental pet bird sometimes sings beautifully and other times refuses to make a sound.
Our old car is so temperamental that you never know if it will start.
The temperamental machine refused to work whenever we needed it most.

  • antonym – stable: not likely to change suddenly; steady
  • synonym – unpredictable: not able to be predicted; changeable in behavior or outcomes
