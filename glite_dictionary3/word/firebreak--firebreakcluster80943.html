firebreak

noun
a cleared strip to stop spread of fire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbreɪk / (BrE)
a strip of land that has been cleared of vegetation to prevent or slow the spread of fire in forests or grasslands

Examples

forest firebreak
A wide firebreak encircled the campsite, ensuring everyone's safety during wildfire season.
Authorities recommended creating a firebreak around the village to reduce the risk of forest fires.
The firefighters quickly cleared a firebreak to protect the nearby homes from the advancing flames.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fireguard: a strip of land cleared to prevent the spread of fire, especially in Britain
