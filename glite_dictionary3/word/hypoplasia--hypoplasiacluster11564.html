hypoplasia

noun
underdevelopment of tissue or organ
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˈpleɪ.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəˈpleɪ.zɪ.ə / (BrE)
abnormal underdevelopment of a tissue or organ due to a reduced number of cells

Examples

hypoplasia of tissue
Congenital hypoplasia of the enamel can lead to increased tooth sensitivity.
Researchers are studying the causes of limb hypoplasia in animals exposed to certain chemicals.
The patient was diagnosed with optic nerve hypoplasia, resulting in impaired vision.

Related Terms

  • antonym – atrophy: gradual decline or wasting of tissue or organ
  • antonym – hyperplasia: increase in the amount of tissue due to cell proliferation
