goat

noun
person subject to ridicule or blame
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ɡəʊt / (BrE)
a person who is the victim of ridicule, blame, or pranks, especially in a group setting

Examples

become the goat of the group
After the team's loss, Mark became the goat of the locker room, teased endlessly by his teammates.
Every group of friends seems to have a goat who is always the target of practical jokes.
He didn't mind playing the goat at parties as long as everyone was having fun.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scapegoat: a person who is blamed for the wrongdoings, mistakes, or faults of others
