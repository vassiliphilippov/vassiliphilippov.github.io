relict

noun
remnant of an earlier geological formation
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪkt / (BrE)
(Geology) A physical feature that remains from a previous geological formation after other parts have been removed or eroded away.

Examples

ancient relict landform
Scientists studied the rock formation, a relict of volcanic activity from millions of years ago.
The isolated hill is a relict from an ancient mountain range that has mostly eroded away.
This valley contains several relicts that provide evidence of past geological processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – remnant: a small remaining quantity or fragment of something
