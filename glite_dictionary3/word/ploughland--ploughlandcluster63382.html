ploughland

noun
land cultivated by ploughing
Pronunciation: / ˈplaʊ·lænd / (AmE), / ˈplaʊ·lænd / (BrE)
Arable land that is tilled by ploughing and used for growing crops.

Examples

fertile ploughland
During the drought, little could be grown on the ploughland despite repeated efforts.
Much of the farm's ploughland was planted with wheat and barley this season.
Over the years, the old forest was transformed into productive ploughland.
