astigmatism

noun
eye defect causing blurred vision
Pronunciation: / əˈstɪɡməˌtɪzəm /; / əˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (BrE)
a defect of the eye or lens that causes blurred or distorted vision because the eye does not focus light evenly

Examples

astigmatism affects vision
Corrective lenses can help people with astigmatism see more clearly.
If left untreated, astigmatism can make reading and driving difficult.
She was diagnosed with astigmatism after complaining of frequent headaches and blurred vision.
Her doctor explained that her blurred vision was caused by astigmatism, which affected the shape of her eye.
Many people wear corrective lenses to improve their astigmatism.
The optometrist detected a mild astigmatism during the routine eye examination.
