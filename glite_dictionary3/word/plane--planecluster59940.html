to plane

verb
smooth or shape with a plane
Pronunciation: / pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
to smooth or shape a surface by shaving off thin layers, especially using a carpenter's plane

Examples

to plane a wooden board
Before painting the door, he carefully planed its edges to ensure a perfect fit.
She spent the afternoon planing down the wooden surface to remove any uneven spots.
The carpenter planes the rough boards until they are smooth enough for furniture making.
He spent the afternoon planing the rough door to make it fit the frame.
The carpenter carefully planed the edge of the table until it was perfectly straight.
You should plane the board before joining the pieces together.
