drenching

noun
the act of soaking something or someone
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛn.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɛn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something or someone completely wet, typically by soaking or saturating with liquid

Examples

a heavy drenching
After that drenching, the tent was no longer waterproof.
She got a thorough drenching walking home in the thunderstorm.
The sudden drenching of the field delayed the soccer match for hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym – drying: the act of removing moisture from something
  • synonym – soaking: the act of making something thoroughly wet
