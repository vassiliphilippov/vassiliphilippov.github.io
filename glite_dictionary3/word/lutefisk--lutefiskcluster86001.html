lutefisk

noun
Scandinavian dried cod dish treated with lye
Pronunciation: / ˈluːtəˌfɪsk / (AmE), / ˈluːtəfɪsk / (BrE)
A traditional Scandinavian dish made from dried cod that has been soaked in a lye solution and then boiled, resulting in a gelatinous texture.

Examples

traditional lutefisk dish
Every Christmas, my grandmother prepares lutefisk as part of our holiday feast.
Many people are wary of lutefisk because of its strong smell and unusual texture.
The restaurant specializes in traditional Norwegian dishes, including lutefisk and lefse.
