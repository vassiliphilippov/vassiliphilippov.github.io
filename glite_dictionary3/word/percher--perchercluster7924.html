percher

noun
bird with feet adapted for perching
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a bird with feet adapted for perching, especially one formerly classified in the now largely abandoned order Passeriformes.

Examples

percher bird species
Some scientists no longer classify certain birds as perchers due to changes in taxonomy.
The forest is home to many species of percher that fill the air with birdsong.
The robin is a common percher found in gardens and woodlands.

Related Terms

  • synonym – passerine: songbird or perching bird of the order Passeriformes
