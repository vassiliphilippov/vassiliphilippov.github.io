Nootka

noun
a member of the Wakashan Indigenous people
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtkə / (AmE), / ˈnuːtkə / (BrE)
A member of the Wakashan Indigenous people living on Vancouver Island and in the Cape Flattery region of northwestern Washington.

Examples

Nootka people
Anthropologists have studied the culture of the Nootka for more than a century.
Many Nootka now refer to themselves as Nuu-chah-nulth.
The Nootka have a rich tradition of whale hunting and cedar carving.
