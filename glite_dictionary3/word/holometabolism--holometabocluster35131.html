holometabolism

noun
complete metamorphosis in insects
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊloʊməˈtæbəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɒləʊməˈtæbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a type of insect development involving complete metamorphosis, with distinct egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages

Examples

holometabolism in butterflies
Beetles are an example of insects that develop through holometabolism.
Butterflies undergo holometabolism, experiencing four distinct life stages from egg to adult.
The process of holometabolism distinguishes some insect orders from those with incomplete metamorphosis.
