ambulacrum

noun
area on echinoderm underside with tube feet
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.bjəˈleɪ.krəm / (AmE), / ˌam.bjʊˈleɪ.krəm / (BrE)
one of the five radial areas on the underside of an echinoderm, such as a sea star or sea urchin, where the tube feet are located

Examples

sea star ambulacrum
Damage to the ambulacrum can impair the movement and feeding abilities of a sea urchin.
Scientists observed regeneration occurring first in a single ambulacrum before spreading to adjacent areas.
The starfish extends its tube feet along each ambulacrum to move across the sea floor.
