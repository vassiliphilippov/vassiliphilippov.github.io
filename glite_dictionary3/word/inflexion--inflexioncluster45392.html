inflexion

noun
grammatical word form change
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
a change in the form of a word, often by adding a suffix, to express different grammatical functions such as tense, mood, person, or number

Examples

inflexion of a verb
English verbs undergo inflexion to indicate tense, such as adding '-ed' for the past.
In Latin, noun inflexion shows the grammatical case and number.
