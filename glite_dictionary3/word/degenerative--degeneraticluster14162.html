degenerative

adjective
causing progressive deterioration (medical)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) characterized by progressive deterioration of tissues, organs, or cells, leading to a loss of function.

Examples

degenerative disease
Alzheimer's disease is a degenerative condition affecting the brain.
Many forms of arthritis are classified as degenerative diseases.
The doctor explained that her illness was degenerative and would worsen over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – progressive: (of a disease) increasing in severity or extent
  • antonym – regenerative: able to renew or restore tissues or organs
