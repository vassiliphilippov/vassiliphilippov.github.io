echoencephalograph

noun
ultrasound device for brain imaging
Pronunciation: / ˌekoʊɛnˈsɛfələˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌɛkəʊɛnˈsɛfələˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument that uses ultrasound waves to create an image of the brain and detect abnormalities

Examples

an echoencephalograph scan
Advancements in technology have made the echoencephalograph more accurate for brain diagnostics.
Doctors used an echoencephalograph to examine head trauma after the accident.
The echoencephalograph revealed a small lesion in the patient's frontal lobe.
