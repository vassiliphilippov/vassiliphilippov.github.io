earplug

noun
plug inserted in ear for protection
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
A small piece of material, such as cotton, wax, or rubber, that is inserted into the ear canal to protect the ear from water, noise, or dust.

Examples

to insert an earplug
Before swimming, he always put in an earplug to keep water out of his ears.
She wore an earplug in each ear to block out the loud construction noise.
The doctor recommended using an earplug to prevent further ear infections.

Related Terms

  • synonym – earmuff: a cushioned covering for the ears, especially to protect against cold or noise
