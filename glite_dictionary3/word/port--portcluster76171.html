to port

verb
(of a ship) to reach a harbor
Pronunciation: / pɔrt / (AmE), / pɔːt / (BrE)
to bring a ship into a harbor or port, or to land at a port

Examples

to port a ship
After a week at sea, the ship finally ported in Lisbon.
The vessel is scheduled to port at Singapore tomorrow.
They ported at dawn, eager to disembark and explore the coastal city.
The captain had to port the vessel during the heavy storm.
The cargo ship was ported just before sunrise.
They successfully ported the yacht despite the dense fog.
