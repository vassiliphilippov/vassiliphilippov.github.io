academic

adjective
focused on learning's minor or formal aspects
Pronunciation: / ˌækəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌakəˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
concerned with ideas, theories, or details that are not practical or relevant to real situations; theoretical or hypothetical

Examples

academic debate
His arguments tended to be rather academic and disconnected from real-world concerns.
Some professors are criticized for their academic approach that values theory over application.
The debate soon became academic, focusing on minute historical details rather than practical solutions.
Her objections were deemed academic because the project had already started.
The answer to that is purely academic since the law is no longer enforced anyway.
Whether they would have succeeded is an academic question at this point.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pedantic: excessively concerned with minor details or rules
  • antonym – practical: concerned with actually doing or using something rather than theories or ideas
  • synonym – theoretical: concerned with or involving the theory of a subject rather than its practical application
