hypothetic

adjective
based on hypothesis or supposition
Pronunciation: / haɪpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
based primarily on a hypothesis or conjecture rather than proven evidence; supposed or assumed for the sake of argument or investigation.

Examples

hypothetic situation
Her explanation was entirely hypothetic, lacking any supporting data.
Scientists created a hypothetic scenario to test the effects of climate change on crops.
The threat was deemed hypothetic and not based on real intelligence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conjectural: based on or involving conjecture rather than knowledge or evidence
  • synonym – hypothetical: based on or serving as a hypothesis; supposed but not necessarily real or true
  • synonym – supposititious: based on assumption rather than actual fact
