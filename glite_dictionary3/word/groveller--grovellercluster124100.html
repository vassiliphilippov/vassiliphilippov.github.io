groveller

noun
person who acts in an excessively submissive way
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɑːvələr / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒvələ / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an excessively submissive way, often showing lack of self-respect to gain favor or approval

Examples

office groveller
No one respected him because he acted like a groveller around his boss.
She refused to become a groveller just to get a promotion.
The king surrounded himself with grovellers who told him only what he wanted to hear.
