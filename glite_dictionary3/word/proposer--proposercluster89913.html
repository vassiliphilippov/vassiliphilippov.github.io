proposer

noun
process that suggests a value in consensus
Pronunciation: / prəˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
(in distributed computing) a process or node that proposes a value to be chosen by a consensus protocol, initiating a proposal that other participants may accept or reject.

Examples

proposer node in protocol
In Paxos, the proposer suggests a value and then waits for acceptors to reply.
During a leader change, multiple proposers may send proposals, so the system needs tie-breaking rules.
The system discarded the update when the proposer failed to obtain a majority of acceptances.

  • synonym – initiator: a process or agent that starts an action or proposal in a protocol
