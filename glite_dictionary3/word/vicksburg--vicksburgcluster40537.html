Vicksburg

noun
a key Civil War battle in 1863
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪksˌbɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈvɪks.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
a decisive battle in the American Civil War in 1863, in which Union forces besieged and captured the city of Vicksburg, leading to Confederate surrender after nearly seven weeks

battle of Vicksburg
General Grant's strategy during the battle of Vicksburg is still studied in military academies today.

The Siege of Vicksburg marked a turning point in the American Civil War.
