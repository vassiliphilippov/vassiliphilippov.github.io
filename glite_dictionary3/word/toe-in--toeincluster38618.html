toe-in

noun
front wheels angled inward (vehicle alignment)
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
the alignment of the front wheels of a vehicle so that they are closer together at the front than at the back, often to improve stability or handling

Examples

toe-in adjustment
After the alignment, the mechanic adjusted the toe-in to meet the manufacturer's specifications.
Excessive toe-in can cause tires to wear unevenly and reduce fuel efficiency.
Many racing teams carefully monitor toe-in to optimize vehicle handling on the track.

Related Terms

  • antonym – toe-out: the alignment of the front wheels angled outward at the front compared to the rear
