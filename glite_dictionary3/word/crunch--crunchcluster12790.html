crunch

noun
a critical shortage or decisive moment
Pronunciation: / krʌntʃ / (AmE), / krʌntʃ / (BrE)
a critical situation in which something important, such as time, money, or resources, is in short supply and urgent decisions must be made

Examples

a time crunch
As the deadline approached, the project team faced a severe time crunch.
Many families experienced a budget crunch after the economic downturn.
The company had to lay off workers during the financial crunch.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crisis: a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • synonym – emergency: a serious or dangerous situation needing immediate action
