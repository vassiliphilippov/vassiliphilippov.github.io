friar

noun
member of a religious order
Pronunciation: / ˈfraɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a male member of a religious order, especially one who originally depended solely on charity for support

Examples

Catholic friar
As a young man, he chose to become a friar and devote his life to service.
The friar greeted us warmly, sharing stories of his work in the community.
The friar walked through the village, collecting food and clothing for the needy.
