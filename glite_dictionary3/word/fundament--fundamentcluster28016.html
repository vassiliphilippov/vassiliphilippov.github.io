fundament

noun
basic underlying principle or foundation
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌndəmənt / (AmE), / ˈfʌndəmənt / (BrE)
a basic underlying principle, assumption, or foundation on which something is based or developed

Examples

fundament of an idea
Every argument in science should have a clear fundament rooted in evidence.
The theory lacks a solid fundament and is therefore difficult to accept.
Without a moral fundament, laws can quickly become unjust.

Related Terms

  • synonym – basis: the underlying support or starting point for something
  • synonym – foundation: the underlying basis or principle for something
  • antonym – superstructure: the part of a building or concept built on top of the foundation
