irruptive

adjective
igneous rock solidified underground
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
(of igneous rock) having solidified beneath the earth's surface, as in granite, diorite, or gabbro; describing rocks formed by the intrusion and solidification of magma within the crust.

Examples

irruptive rock formation
Granite is an irruptive rock that forms deep within the earth's crust.
The geologists collected samples of irruptive rocks for further study.
Unlike volcanic basalt, irruptive rocks cool slowly underground.

Related Terms

  • antonym – extrusive: (of igneous rock) formed from lava that solidifies on the earth's surface
  • synonym – intrusive: (of igneous rock) formed when magma solidifies beneath the earth's surface
