to introduce

verb
present formally (e.g. legislation) before a group
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to formally present something, such as a bill or piece of legislation, for consideration by a legislative body or official group

Examples

to introduce a bill
She will introduce the proposal at tomorrow's board meeting for further discussion.
The committee decided to introduce several amendments during the session.
The senator plans to introduce a new bill that would reform healthcare.

Related Terms

  • synonym – propose: to put forward (an idea, plan, or proposal) for consideration
  • antonym – withdraw: to remove something previously presented or proposed
