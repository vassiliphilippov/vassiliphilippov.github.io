ESR

noun
electron spin resonance spectroscopy
Pronunciation: / ˌiː ˌɛs ˈɑːr / (AmE), / ˌiː ˌɛs ˈɑː / (BrE)
a technique in which microwaves are absorbed by paramagnetic substances to study their electronic structure, also known as electron spin resonance

Examples

ESR spectroscopy analysis
Routine ESR measurements are critical in identifying free radicals in chemistry research.
Scientists used ESR to investigate the molecular structure of the sample.
The ESR spectrum revealed details about the material's unpaired electrons.
