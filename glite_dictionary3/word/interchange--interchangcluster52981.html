interchange

noun
highway junction on different levels
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a junction of highways on different levels that allows vehicles to move from one to another without crossing paths with other traffic streams

Examples

highway interchange
During rush hour, the interchange can become a major bottleneck.
Take the first exit at the interchange to get onto the main highway.
The new interchange was built to reduce congestion and improve traffic flow.

  • antonym – intersection: a point where two or more roads meet or cross at the same level
