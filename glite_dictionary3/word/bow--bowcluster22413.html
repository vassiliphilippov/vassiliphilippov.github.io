bow

noun
performers acknowledging applause at end of show
Pronunciation: / baʊ / (AmE), / baʊ / (BrE)
an appearance by performers at the end of a show to acknowledge the audience's applause, usually marked by bending forward

Examples

take a bow onstage
After the orchestra finished playing, the conductor gave a deep bow to the cheering audience.
She received a standing ovation and made a graceful bow.
The actors took a final bow before leaving the stage.

  • synonym – curtain call: an appearance by performers at the end of a performance to receive applause
