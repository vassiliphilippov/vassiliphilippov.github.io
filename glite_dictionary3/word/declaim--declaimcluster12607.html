to declaim

verb
speak forcefully against something
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkleɪm / (AmE), / dɪˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to speak or recite in a forceful, passionate, or theatrical way, often to make a point or for dramatic effect

Examples

to declaim a speech
During the debate, she declaimed passionately against corruption and injustice.
He often declaims about environmental issues at public gatherings.
The activist declaimed against the new policy in front of a large crowd.
Rather than simply read the speech, he chose to declaim it passionately from memory.
She would often declaim poetry at local gatherings, mesmerizing her audience.
The actor began to declaim Shakespeare’s lines with dramatic gestures and a booming voice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – denounce: to publicly declare something to be wrong or evil
  • antonym – mumble: to speak in a low indistinct manner
  • synonym – orate: to speak formally in public, often at length
  • antonym – praise: to express approval or admiration of
  • synonym – recite: to repeat aloud from memory before an audience
