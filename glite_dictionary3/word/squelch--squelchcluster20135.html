to squelch

verb
suppress or crush completely
Pronunciation: / skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to suppress or crush something completely, especially opposition or unwanted ideas

Examples

to squelch criticism
He attempted to squelch any criticism by threatening those who spoke out.
Rumors spread quickly, but the company was able to squelch them with an official statement.
The government tried to squelch the protests before they could gain momentum.

Related Terms

  • antonym – encourage: to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym – quash: to reject or suppress forcefully
