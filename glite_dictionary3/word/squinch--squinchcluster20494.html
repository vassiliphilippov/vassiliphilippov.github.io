squinch

noun
small arch across wall angle (architecture)
Pronunciation: / skwɪntʃ / (AmE), / skwɪntʃ / (BrE)
A small arch or set of arches built across the interior angle of two walls, especially to support a dome or spire above.

Examples

squinch under a dome
Careful restoration of the medieval squinch revealed its original decorative carvings.

Without a sturdy squinch, the dome would not have been adequately supported.
