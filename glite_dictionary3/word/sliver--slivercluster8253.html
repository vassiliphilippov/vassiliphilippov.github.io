sliver

noun
thin sharp fragment (wood/glass/metal)
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɪvə / (BrE)
a small, thin piece or slice that has been cut or broken off from something (such as wood, glass, metal, or food)

Examples

a sliver of glass
A sliver of wood stuck out from the old fence post.
After the vase shattered, there were slivers of porcelain all over the floor.
She carefully removed the sliver of glass from her finger.
A thin sliver of cheese was all that was left on the plate.
She pulled a sliver of wood out of her finger after handling the old fence.
Sunlight entered the room through a narrow sliver between the curtains.
