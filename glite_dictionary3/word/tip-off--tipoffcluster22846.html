tip-off

noun
secret advance warning or information
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪp ˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈtɪp ˌɒf / (BrE)
A secret warning or piece of information about something, especially something illegal or surprising, that is going to happen.

Examples

police tip-off
Journalists claimed they acted on a tip-off from someone inside the company.
Thanks to an anonymous tip-off, the authorities raided the illegal warehouse.
The police received a tip-off about the planned robbery and managed to stop it in time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alert: an announcement that something requires attention
  • synonym – warning: advance notice about impending danger or problem
