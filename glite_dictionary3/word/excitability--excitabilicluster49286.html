excitability

noun
excessive responsiveness to stimulation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsʌɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively responsive or sensitive to stimulation, especially in an organ or body part

Examples

nerve excitability
Certain neurological disorders are characterized by abnormal excitability in brain regions.
Researchers studied the excitability of cardiac tissue after exposure to certain chemicals.
The increased excitability of nerve cells can lead to muscle spasms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insensitivity: lack of sensitivity or response to stimuli
  • synonym – irritability: the quality of being easily irritated or stimulated
