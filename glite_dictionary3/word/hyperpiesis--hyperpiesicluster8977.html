hyperpiesis

noun
persistent high blood pressure (no known cause)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərpaɪˈiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəpaɪˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition of persistently high blood pressure with no identifiable cause.

Examples

chronic hyperpiesis diagnosis
Long-term hyperpiesis increases the risk of stroke, heart attack, and kidney failure if left untreated.
The patient was diagnosed with hyperpiesis after repeated blood pressure tests showed consistently elevated readings.
Unlike secondary hypertension, hyperpiesis has no identifiable underlying cause and is often managed with medication.
