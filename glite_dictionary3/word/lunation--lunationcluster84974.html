lunation

noun
period between new moons
Pronunciation: / luˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / luːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the period of time between one new moon and the next, typically about 29.5 days

Examples

a lunation cycle
Ancient calendars were often based on the lunation rather than the solar year.
Each lunation lasts just over 29 days, marking the lunar cycle.
The festival is held on the second lunation after the spring equinox.

  • synonym – synodic month: a month measured by successive new moons (synonym for 'lunation')
