extricable

adjective
able to be freed or disentangled
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkstrɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrɪkəbl / (BrE)
Able to be freed or disentangled from a difficulty or constraint.

Examples

extricable from trouble
Fortunately, the legal issue proved extricable after further investigation.
She found herself in a complex situation, but it was not entirely extricable.
The knot was so tight that it was barely extricable without cutting the rope.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inextricable: impossible to disentangle or escape from
