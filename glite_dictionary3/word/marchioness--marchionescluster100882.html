marchioness

noun
wife or widow of a marquis; female marquis
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːrʃəˈnɛs / (AmE), / ˌmɑːʃəˈnɛs / (BrE)
a noblewoman who is the wife or widow of a marquis, or who holds the rank of marquis in her own right (ranked below a duchess and above a countess)

Examples

the marchioness of Devonshire
After her husband's death, she became the Dowager marchioness of Devonshire.
As a marchioness, she managed several estates across the countryside.
The marchioness attended the royal banquet wearing an elegant velvet gown.
As a marchioness, she participated in many social and charitable events.
In the novel, the marchioness is portrayed as intelligent and compassionate.
The marchioness hosted a grand ball at her country estate.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – countess: the wife or widow of a count or earl, or a woman holding that rank herself
  • degree higher – duchess: the wife or widow of a duke, or a woman holding the rank of duke in her own right
  • synonym – marquis: a nobleman ranking above a count and below a duke
