cynically

adverb
in a cynical manner
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnɪkli / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a belief that people are motivated purely by self-interest or a lack of trust in human sincerity or integrity.

Examples

cynically exploit rules
He cynically exploited the loophole to benefit himself.
She cynically dismissed their promises as empty words.
The reporter cynically questioned the sincerity of the politician's apology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cynical: distrusting or disparaging the motives of others; believing that people are motivated by self-interest
