to reserve

verb
arrange or book in advance
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɝv /; / rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
to arrange for something to be kept for your use at a future time; to book in advance

Examples

to reserve a table
She reserved a seat on the afternoon flight to Paris.
We decided to reserve a table at the new restaurant for Friday night.
You should reserve your tickets early, as the event is very popular.
I'd like to reserve a table for four at seven o'clock, please.
It's important to reserve your tickets early for popular shows.
They reserved a seat for her at the front of the auditorium.

Related Terms

  • synonym – book: to arrange for something, such as a seat or room, to be kept for you
  • antonym – cancel: to annul a reservation or prior arrangement
  • synonym – hold: to set aside for someone; to reserve
