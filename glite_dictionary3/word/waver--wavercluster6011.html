to waver

verb
sway or move in wavelike pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvɚ /; / ˈweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈweɪvə / (BrE)
to move or sway unsteadily back and forth; to shake or tremble slightly

Examples

to waver in the wind
Her voice seemed to waver with emotion as she spoke.
The candle flame began to waver in the breeze.
The tall grass wavered gently under the summer wind.
A shadow seemed to waver on the wall as the headlights passed by.
The flame of the candle began to waver in the breeze.
The old flag wavered in the wind, barely holding onto the pole.
He wavered on the edge of the platform, uncertain whether to step forward.
Her voice began to waver as she described the difficult situation.
You could see the candle flame waver in the drafty room.
