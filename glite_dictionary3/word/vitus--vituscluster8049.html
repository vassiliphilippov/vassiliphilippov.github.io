Vitus

proper noun
Christian martyr and patron saint
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈviːtəs / (BrE)
A Christian martyr and the patron saint of those who suffer from epilepsy and Sydenham's chorea, who died around 300 CE.

Examples

Saint Vitus feast
According to legend, Vitus was martyred during the Roman persecution of Christians.
Many people pray to Vitus for protection from epilepsy and related ailments.
The feast of Vitus is celebrated on June 15th in many Christian traditions.
