immunosuppressor

noun
drug reducing immune response
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˈsəprɛsər / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˈsʌprɛsə / (BrE)
a drug that reduces the strength of the body's immune response

Examples

immunosuppressor drug therapy
After his transplant, the patient was given an immunosuppressor to prevent organ rejection.
Prolonged use of an immunosuppressor can increase the risk of infections.
The doctor prescribed an immunosuppressor as part of the treatment for the autoimmune disorder.
