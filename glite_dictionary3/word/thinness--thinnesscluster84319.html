thinness

noun
the quality of being thin or not thick
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not thick; the low viscosity or density of a liquid or substance

Examples

thinness of the sauce
He commented on the thinness of the sauce, wishing it were a bit richer.
She was surprised by the thinness of the gravy compared to what she usually makes.
The thinness of the paint made it easy to apply evenly over the surface.

  • antonym – thickness: the quality of being thick or dense
