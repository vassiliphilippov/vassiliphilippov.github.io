ebonite

noun
hard vulcanized rubber
Pronunciation: / ˈɛbəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛbənaɪt / (BrE)
a hard, nonresilient form of rubber produced by vulcanizing natural rubber with high amounts of sulfur, used in electrical insulation and other products

Examples

ebonite rod
Electrical insulators were once commonly manufactured from ebonite due to its nonconductive properties.
He collected vintage cameras, some of which featured ebonite parts.
The old fountain pen was made of ebonite, which gave it a distinctive feel.
