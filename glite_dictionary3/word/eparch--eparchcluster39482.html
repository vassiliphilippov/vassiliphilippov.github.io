eparch

noun
provincial governor in ancient Greece or Byzantium
Pronunciation: / ˈiːˌpɑrk / (AmE), / ˈiːˌpɑːk / (BrE)
the governor or prefect of an eparchy in ancient Greece or in the Byzantine Empire; a provincial administrator.

Examples

eparch of a province
An edict from the eparch was treated as law in the eparchy.
During the Byzantine era, the eparch of Constantinople oversaw the city's markets.
