solitary

noun
isolation of a prisoner from others
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑlɪˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪtəri / (BrE)
short for 'solitary confinement'; the condition of being held alone in a prison cell apart from other prisoners.

Examples

in solitary confinement
After the fight, he was placed in solitary for three days.
She dreaded the thought of spending months in solitary.
The prisoner appealed the decision to keep him in solitary.

Related Terms

  • synonym – solitary confinement: the isolation of a prisoner from others as a punishment
