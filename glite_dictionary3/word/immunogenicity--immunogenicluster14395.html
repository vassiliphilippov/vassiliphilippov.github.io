immunogenicity

noun
ability to induce immune response
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˌdʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˌdʒəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
The ability of a substance, such as an antigen or vaccine, to provoke an immune response in the body.

Examples

immunogenicity of a vaccine
Factors such as dose and administration route can affect the immunogenicity of therapeutic drugs.
Researchers are testing the immunogenicity of the new vaccine in clinical trials.
The immunogenicity of the protein makes it a suitable candidate for further development.

Related Terms

  • synonym – antigenicity: Capacity of a substance to bind specifically with antibodies or T-cell receptors
  • degree higher – immunity: The body's ability to resist infection or disease
