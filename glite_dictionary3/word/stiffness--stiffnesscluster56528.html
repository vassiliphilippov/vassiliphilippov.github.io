stiffness

noun
firmness or rigidity in purpose or behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪfnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪfnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strict, severe, or unyielding in attitude, behavior, or rules

Examples

stiffness of rules
Despite his usual stiffness, he managed to appear friendly at the event.
He admired her stiffness and determination to stick to her beliefs.
Her stiffness in negotiations made compromise difficult for both sides.
His stiffness with the rules left little room for compassion.
The stiffness of her teaching style made her students nervous about making mistakes.
There was a certain stiffness in his approach to discipline that some saw as overly harsh.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flexibility: willingness to change or compromise
  • antonym – leniency: the quality of being more merciful or tolerant
  • synonym – rigidity: the quality or state of being stiff and unyielding
  • synonym – sternness: the quality of being strict, serious, and unrelenting
