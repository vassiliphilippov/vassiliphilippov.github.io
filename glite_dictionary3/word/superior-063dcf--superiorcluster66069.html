superior

adjective
not influenced by; beyond influence
Pronunciation: / suˈpɪriɚ /; / suˈpɪriər / (AmE), / suˈpɪəriə /; / suːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
not affected or influenced by something; above or beyond the influence of a particular factor or emotion

Examples

superior to criticism
He believed himself superior to the criticism of his peers.
The judge must remain superior to bias and personal feelings.
This law applies to everyone; no one is superior to it.
Even when criticized harshly, she remained superior to negativity and continued her work.
He prided himself on being superior to petty arguments that distracted others.
The committee must act superior to outside pressures to maintain its integrity.
