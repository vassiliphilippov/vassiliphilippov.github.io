idleness

noun
state of being inactive; unemployment
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪdlnəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪdlɪnɪs /; / ˈaɪdlənɪs / (BrE)
the state of not working or being inactive, sometimes because of laziness

Examples

period of idleness
During the recession, many workers faced unexpected idleness as factories shut down.
He filled his hours of idleness by learning new skills online.
Long periods of idleness can affect a person's sense of purpose and motivation.
He regretted his years of idleness after realizing how much he could have accomplished.
Rather than spend the afternoon in idleness, she decided to volunteer at the shelter.
Repeated idleness can lead to a loss of ambition and motivation.
