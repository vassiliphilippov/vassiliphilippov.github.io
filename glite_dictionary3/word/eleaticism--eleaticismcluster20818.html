Eleaticism

noun
school of ancient Greek philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˌɛliˈætɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛliːˈatɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
The philosophical doctrines and teachings of the Eleatic school of ancient Greece, especially characterized by the rejection of change and the emphasis on unity.

Examples

ancient Eleaticism philosophy
Many ancient philosophers engaged with the principles of Eleaticism in their own enquiries.
Plato's dialogues frequently grapple with notions central to Eleaticism, such as the concept of unchanging reality.
The core argument of Eleaticism is the denial of multiplicity and change in the universe.
