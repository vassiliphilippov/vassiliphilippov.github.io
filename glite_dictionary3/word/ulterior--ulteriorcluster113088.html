ulterior

adjective
beyond what is immediate or obvious
Pronunciation: / ʌlˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ʌlˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
existing beyond what is obvious or stated, especially a reason or motive that is hidden or not openly shown

Examples

ulterior motive hidden
His reasons for investing in the project are ulterior and not immediately clear to everyone.
She wondered if there was an ulterior motive behind his generous offer.
The debate strayed into issues ulterior to the original topic.
He suspected his friend had an ulterior motive for offering to help.
She assured us there was no ulterior agenda behind her proposal.
The company's generosity seemed suspicious, leading some to wonder about its ulterior intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – covert: not openly acknowledged or displayed; secret
  • synonym – hidden: not immediately obvious or visible; concealed
  • antonym – overt: open and not secret; easily observed
