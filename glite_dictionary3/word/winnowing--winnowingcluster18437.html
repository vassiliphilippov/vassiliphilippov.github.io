winnowing

noun
separating grain from chaff
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪnoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of separating grain from chaff, typically by means of wind or air movement

Examples

winnowing grain
After harvest, winnowing was often a communal activity in the village.
The winnowing left piles of clean wheat ready for storage.
Traditional farmers performed winnowing by tossing the grain into the air to let the wind carry away the chaff.
