Brassica napus

noun
plant grown for seeds and forage
Pronunciation: / brəˈsɪkə ˈneɪpəs / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈneɪpəs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce rapeseed oil, and also grown as a forage crop.
