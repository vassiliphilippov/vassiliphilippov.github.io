groundswell

noun
sudden shift in public opinion
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌswɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌswɛl / (BrE)
A rapid and significant change in public opinion or political sentiment, often occurring spontaneously and without obvious leadership.

Examples

groundswell of support
A sudden groundswell of public opinion forced lawmakers to reconsider the proposed law.
The company responded to the groundswell of customer complaints by improving its services.
There has been a groundswell of support for environmental reforms in recent years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – backlash: a strong adverse reaction, especially in politics or opinion
  • synonym – upsurge: a sudden increase or rise, especially of emotion or activity
