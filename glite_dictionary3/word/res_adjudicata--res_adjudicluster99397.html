res adjudicata

noun
legal matter already decided by court
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪs ˌædʒuːdɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌreɪz ˌædjuːdɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A matter that has already been decided by a court and cannot be raised or litigated again between the same parties.

Examples

res adjudicata applies in court
Because of res adjudicata, the plaintiff could not bring the same issue to court again.
The court dismissed the case, citing res adjudicata since the matter had already been decided.
The defense argued that the lawsuit was barred by res adjudicata.
