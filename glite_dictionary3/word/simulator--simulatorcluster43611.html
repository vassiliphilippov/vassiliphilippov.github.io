simulator

noun
device or program for training by imitation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪm.jəˌleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.jʊ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
A machine or program that models real-life conditions for training, research, or testing purposes.

Examples

simulator for pilots
Driving schools often rely on a simulator before allowing learners on the actual road.
Medical students practiced emergency procedures with the help of a virtual simulator.
The airline uses a high-tech simulator to train its pilots.
