prescript

noun
official rule or directive
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈpriːskrɪpt / (BrE)
an official or prescribed rule, direction, or order, especially one that guides or governs conduct or action

Examples

prescript of conduct
Every religious community has its own prescripts that members are expected to follow.
He acted according to the prescript set forth by ancient tradition.
The company's code of ethics served as a prescript for employee behavior.
