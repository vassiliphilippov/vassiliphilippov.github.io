synergism

noun
interaction producing a greater combined effect
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪn.ɚˌdʒɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.əˌdʒɪz.əm / (BrE)
the interaction of two or more agents or forces so that their combined effect is greater than the sum of their individual effects

Examples

synergism between drugs
Muscles can exhibit synergism to produce more powerful movements than they could alone.
Scientists are studying the synergism observed when certain chemicals are combined.
The synergism between the two drugs led to a much faster recovery than expected.

Related Terms

  • synonym – synergy: the combined power of a group that achieves more than individuals
