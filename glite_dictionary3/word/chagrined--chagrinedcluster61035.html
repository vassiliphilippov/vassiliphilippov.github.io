chagrined

adjective
distressed or humiliated; embarrassed
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈɡrɪnd / (AmE), / ʃəˈɡrɪnd / (BrE)
feeling distressed or humiliated because of disappointment, failure, or embarrassment.

Examples

chagrined by a mistake
He felt chagrined after being corrected in front of the whole class.
She was chagrined to discover she had made a basic error in her report.
They looked chagrined when their plan failed to impress the judges.

  • antonym – proud: feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of one's achievements
