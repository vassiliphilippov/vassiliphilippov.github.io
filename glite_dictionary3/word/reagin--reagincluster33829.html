reagin

noun
antibody causing allergic reactions
Pronunciation: / riˈeɪɡɪn / (AmE), / riːˈeɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
An antibody, specifically immunoglobulin E, that is produced in response to allergens and causes allergic reactions by releasing histamine and other substances.
