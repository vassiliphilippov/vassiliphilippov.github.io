hematocytopenia

noun
low red blood cell count
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˌsoʊtəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊsaɪtəˈpiːniə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally low number of red blood cells in the blood.

Examples

hematocytopenia causes fatigue
Hematocytopenia can lead to symptoms such as fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath.
The doctor diagnosed him with hematocytopenia after reviewing his blood test results.
Treatment for hematocytopenia depends on the underlying cause of the condition.
