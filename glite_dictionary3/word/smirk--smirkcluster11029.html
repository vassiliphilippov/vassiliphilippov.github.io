to smirk

verb
smile smugly or derisively
Pronunciation: / smɝk / (AmE), / smɜːk / (BrE)
to smile in a self-satisfied, smug, or irritatingly knowing way, often expressing smugness or scorn

Examples

to smirk at someone
He smirked at the question, clearly pleased with himself.
She couldn't help but smirk when her rival tripped onstage.
Stop smirking; it's not as clever as you think.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grin: to smile broadly, showing the teeth
  • antonym – scowl: to frown in an angry or bad-tempered way
