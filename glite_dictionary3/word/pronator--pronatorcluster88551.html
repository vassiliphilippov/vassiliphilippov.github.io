pronator

noun
a muscle causing pronation
Pronunciation: / proʊˈneɪtər / (AmE), / prəˈneɪtə / (BrE)
a muscle that produces or assists in pronation, especially turning the forearm or hand so the palm faces downward or backward

Examples

pronator muscle injury
An injury to the pronator can limit rotation of the forearm.
Physical therapy focused on strengthening the pronator may help recovery.
The pronator muscles are responsible for turning the palm downward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – supinator: a muscle that produces or assists in supination (turning the palm upward)
