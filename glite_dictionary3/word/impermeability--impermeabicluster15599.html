impermeability

noun
state of not letting liquid/gas pass through
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɜːrmiəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːmɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not allowing liquids or gases to pass through it.

Examples

impermeability of fabric
Gore-Tex fabric is valued for its balance of breathability and impermeability to rain.
The impermeability of the concrete ensured that no water leaked into the basement.
The engineers tested the impermeability of the dam to guarantee its safety.
