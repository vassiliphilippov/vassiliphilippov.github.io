diversion

noun
military distraction maneuver
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a military attack or maneuver intended to distract the enemy and draw defense away from the principal point of attack

Examples

military diversion attack
By launching a diversion, they successfully drew attention away from the real assault.
The general ordered a diversion on the eastern flank to confuse the enemy.
The success of the mission depended on the effectiveness of the initial diversion.
