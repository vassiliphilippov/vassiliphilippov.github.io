Imuran

noun
trademarked immunosuppressive drug
Pronunciation: / ˈɪm.jəˌræn / (AmE), / ˈɪmjʊˌran / (BrE)
A trademarked immunosuppressive drug (azathioprine) used to prevent the rejection of transplanted organs.

Examples

Imuran after transplant
After her kidney transplant, she was prescribed Imuran to prevent organ rejection.
Patients taking Imuran need regular blood tests to monitor for side effects.
The doctor explained that Imuran can weaken the immune system to protect new organs.
