connexion

noun
act of joining or linking things
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛk.ʃən /; / kəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈnɛk.ʃən /; / kəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of joining things together or the device that connects them

Examples

wire connexion
Proper connexion of the wires is essential for the system to function.
She felt a strange connexion with the stranger she met on the train.
The connexion between the computer and the printer was lost during the storm.
If the connexion is loose, the signal may become unreliable or noisy.
Older British texts often use the word connexion where modern usage prefers 'connection.'
The electrician installed a new connexion between the two wires to ensure the circuit was complete.

Related Terms
