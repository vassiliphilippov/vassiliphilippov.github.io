vacant

adjective
not occupied; without a current occupant
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a position, seat, or place) not occupied; having no current occupant or incumbent.

Examples

vacant seat
After months of searching, they finally filled the vacant seat on the council.
That house on the corner has been vacant for years.
The company announced that the manager's position was vacant after the resignation.

  • antonym – occupied: being used or held by someone
