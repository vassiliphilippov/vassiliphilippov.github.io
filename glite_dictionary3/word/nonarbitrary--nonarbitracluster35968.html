nonarbitrary

adjective
not based on personal opinion or randomness
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈɑrbəˌtrɛri / (AmE), / nɒnˈɑːbɪtrəri / (BrE)
not based on personal choice or random decision; having an objective or determined basis

Examples

nonarbitrary rule
A nonarbitrary distinction can be justified by evidence or reasoning.
He argued that the classification system was nonarbitrary and based on measurable criteria.
The rules for selecting participants must be nonarbitrary to ensure fairness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – arbitrary: based on random choice or personal whim rather than any reason or system
