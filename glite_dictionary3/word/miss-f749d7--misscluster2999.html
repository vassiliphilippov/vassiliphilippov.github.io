to miss

verb
fail to experience or undergo
Pronunciation: / mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
to fail to experience, undergo, or be affected by something, especially something unpleasant or significant

Examples

to miss the storm
I was lucky to miss the traffic jam during rush hour.
She missed the worst part of the storm by leaving early.
We missed the flu this season, even though many of our friends got sick.
