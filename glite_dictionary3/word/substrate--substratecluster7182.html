substrate

noun
a substance acted upon by an enzyme
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.streɪt / (BrE)
a substance on which an enzyme or ferment acts in a chemical reaction

Examples

enzyme substrate reaction
Glucose can act as a substrate for several important metabolic enzymes.
The enzyme binds to its substrate to catalyze the reaction efficiently.
The specificity of an enzyme depends on the shape of its substrate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enzyme: a protein that catalyzes a chemical reaction
  • antonym – product: the substance formed as a result of a chemical reaction
