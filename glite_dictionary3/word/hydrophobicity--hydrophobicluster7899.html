hydrophobicity

noun
tendency to repel water
Pronunciation: / haɪdroʊfəˈbɪsɪti / (AmE), / hʌɪdrəʊfəˈbɪsɪti / (BrE)
the property of repelling water; the tendency of a substance to avoid or not absorb water

Examples

hydrophobicity of a surface
Researchers measured the hydrophobicity of the nanoparticles using a contact angle test.
Surface hydrophobicity is important in the design of anti-fouling coatings.
The hydrophobicity of the material makes it ideal for waterproof clothing.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hydrophilicity: the property of attracting or absorbing water
