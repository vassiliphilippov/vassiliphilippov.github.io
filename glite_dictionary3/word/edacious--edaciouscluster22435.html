edacious

adjective
greedy or voracious (esp. for food)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ɪˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having a great appetite for food; devouring food in large quantities; voracious.

Examples

edacious appetite
After the long hike, she felt edacious and finished two large pizzas by herself.
The edacious guests emptied every serving dish within minutes of their arrival.
Vultures are known for their edacious feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rapacious: aggressively greedy or grasping
  • synonym – ravenous: extremely hungry
  • synonym – voracious: having a very eager approach to food or a pursuit
