phosphate

noun
old-fashioned carbonated fruit drink
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
A sweet, carbonated soft drink, popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, made with fruit syrup and a small amount of phosphoric acid.

Examples

lemon phosphate drink
He remembered the sharp, tangy taste of a lemon phosphate from his childhood.
