to unman

verb
cause to lose courage or self-control
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmæn / (AmE), / ʌnˈmæn / (BrE)
to cause (someone) to lose courage or self-control; to make someone feel less brave or less confident

Examples

to unman a brave person
She found the ordeal so stressful that it nearly unmanned her.
The haunting noise in the old house unmans even the bravest visitors.
The unexpected criticism completely unmanned him during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cow: to frighten someone into submission or silence
  • antonym – encourage: to give someone confidence or support
