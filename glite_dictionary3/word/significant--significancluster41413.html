significant

adjective
statistically meaningful; not due to chance
Pronunciation: / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a result, difference, or effect) too great or correlated to be due to chance alone; indicating a real or systematic relationship, especially in statistics.

Examples

significant result
A significant correlation was found between exercise frequency and improved mood.
The results of the experiment were significant at the 0.05 level, indicating a strong effect.
There was no significant change in the patients' blood pressure after treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insignificant: not large or important enough to be considered statistically meaningful
