to dash

verb
to discourage or ruin hope/confidence
Pronunciation: / dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
to destroy or ruin someone's hopes, courage, or enthusiasm suddenly

Examples

to dash hopes
He was completely dashed by the harsh criticism in the review.
Her hopes of winning were dashed when she heard the final results.
The news of the accident dashed our excitement for the trip.
Her injury dashed her hopes of playing in the final match.
The disappointing news dashed all his dreams of winning the competition.
The sudden announcement dashed any remaining optimism among the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crush: to destroy completely, particularly hopes or spirit
  • synonym – daunt: to make someone feel intimidated or apprehensive
  • synonym – discourage: cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • antonym – fulfill: to bring about or realize, especially expectations or hopes (opposite meaning)
  • synonym – shatter: to suddenly destroy something abstract, such as hope or confidence
