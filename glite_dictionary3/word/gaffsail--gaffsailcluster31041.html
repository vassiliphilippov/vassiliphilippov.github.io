gaffsail

noun
four-sided sail supported by a gaff
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæfˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡæfˌseɪl / (BrE)
A four-sided, fore-and-aft sail that is supported by a gaff along its upper edge.

Examples

gaffsail on a yacht
He expertly trimmed the gaffsail to maximize the yacht's speed.
The schooner was rigged with a large gaffsail to catch the afternoon breeze.
Traditional fishing boats often use a gaffsail for better maneuverability.
