thievishly

adverb
in the manner of a thief; stealthily
Pronunciation: / ˈθiːvɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈθiːvɪʃli / (BrE)
In a way that resembles or is characteristic of a thief; stealthily or with the intent to steal.

Examples

look thievishly around
He glanced thievishly at the unattended bag near the door.
She moved thievishly in the darkness, careful not to make a sound.
The cat padded thievishly through the kitchen, eyeing the fish on the counter.
