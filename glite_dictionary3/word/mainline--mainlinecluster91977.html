to mainline

verb
inject a drug into a vein
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪn.laɪn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪn.laɪn / (BrE)
to inject a drug directly into a vein, especially as a method of taking narcotics

Examples

to mainline heroin
Authorities warn that mainlining can lead to life-threatening infections and overdoses.
She has been mainlining heroin for years, putting her health at serious risk.
Some addicts prefer to mainline drugs because the effects are stronger and quicker.
