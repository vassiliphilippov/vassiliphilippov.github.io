shunt

noun
a passage for diverting bodily fluid
Pronunciation: / ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ʃʌnt / (BrE)
a medical device or passage used to divert bodily fluids from one place to another

Examples

brain shunt
A ventricular shunt can help relieve pressure caused by hydrocephalus.
Doctors used a shunt to bypass the blocked artery.
The surgeon inserted a shunt to redirect the flow of cerebrospinal fluid.
A malfunctioning shunt can lead to serious complications if not detected early.
Doctors inserted a shunt to help drain fluid from the brain.
The patient required a shunt to relieve pressure caused by excess cerebrospinal fluid.

Related Terms

  • synonym – catheter: a flexible tube inserted into the body to remove or inject fluids
