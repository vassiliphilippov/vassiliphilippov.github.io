coadjutor

noun
assistant bishop (in church)
Pronunciation: / koʊˈædʒətər / (AmE), / kəʊˈadʒʊtə / (BrE)
A bishop appointed to assist and often succeed another bishop in their duties.

Examples

coadjutor bishop
He was appointed coadjutor to the aging bishop of the diocese.
The coadjutor played a significant role in church administration.
Upon the bishop's retirement, the coadjutor automatically assumed his position.

  • synonym – assistant: a person who helps someone else
