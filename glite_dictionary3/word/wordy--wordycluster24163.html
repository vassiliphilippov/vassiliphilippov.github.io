wordy

adjective
using too many words
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdi / (BrE)
using or containing more words than are necessary, often making communication less clear or effective

Examples

a wordy explanation
His explanations became increasingly wordy, making it hard for the students to follow his main point.
The report was so wordy that I struggled to find the important details.
Try to avoid wordy sentences when writing your essay to improve clarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: using only a few words; brief and to the point
  • synonym – verbose: using more words than needed; long-winded
