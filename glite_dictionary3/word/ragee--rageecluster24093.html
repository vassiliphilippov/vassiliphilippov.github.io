ragee

noun
East Indian cereal grass (finger millet)
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈreɪdʒi / (BrE)
An East Indian cereal grass (finger millet) whose seeds yield a somewhat bitter flour, used as a staple in parts of Asia and Africa.

Examples

ragee flour bread
Because of its hardiness, ragee is an important crop in arid regions.
In southern India, ragee porridge is a common breakfast dish.
The villagers harvested ragee to grind into flour for their daily bread.
