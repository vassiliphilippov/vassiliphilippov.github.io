speakeasy

noun
secret bar during Prohibition era
Pronunciation: / ˈspiˌkizi / (AmE), / ˈspiːkiːzi / (BrE)
(during Prohibition in the United States) a secret or illegal bar where alcoholic drinks were sold and consumed.

Examples

hidden speakeasy bar
During the 1920s, many New Yorkers flocked to the local speakeasy to socialize and drink.
She discovered a hidden speakeasy behind an unmarked door downtown.
The film depicted the glamour and danger of life inside a speakeasy during Prohibition.
