diesis

noun
typographical symbol (‡) for footnotes
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A typographical symbol (‡) used in printing to indicate a third cross-reference or footnote, following the asterisk (*) and dagger (†).

Examples

diesis symbol in text
If you encounter a diesis (‡) in the text, check the corresponding note at the bottom.
The editor used a diesis to mark the third footnote on the page.
When both an asterisk and a dagger have already been used, a diesis is the next typographical mark.
