domatium

noun
plant structure providing shelter for insects or fungi
Pronunciation: / doʊˈmeɪtiəm / (AmE), / dəˈmeɪtɪəm / (BrE)
A part of a plant, such as a leaf, that has been modified to provide shelter for insects, mites, or fungi.

Examples

domatium in a leaf
Biologists observed fungi growing inside the domatium of several species of Acacia.
Some plants develop a domatium to protect beneficial mites living on their leaves.
The ant colony occupied the domatium at the base of the plant's leaf.
