to devitalize

verb
make less lively or energetic
Pronunciation: / diˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something less lively or less energetic; to deprive of vitality or vigor

Examples

to devitalize a community
Economic downturns often devitalize small businesses and neighborhoods.
Many believe pollution can devitalize urban communities over time.
The harsh working conditions gradually devitalized the entire staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enervate: to weaken or drain of energy
  • antonym – invigorate: to give energy or life to something
