hyperbolism

noun
use of hyperbole
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɜrbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / hʌɪˈpɜːbəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the use of exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally; hyperbole.

Examples

use of hyperbolism in stories
Her description of the storm was pure hyperbolism, making it sound far worse than it was.
In political speeches, hyperbolism is often used to persuade the audience.
The poet's frequent hyperbolism gives his verses a heightened sense of drama.
