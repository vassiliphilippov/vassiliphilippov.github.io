stilted

adjective
unnaturally formal or stiff
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈstɪltɪd / (BrE)
(of language, speech, or behavior) too formal and not natural, often in a way that seems awkward or forced.

Examples

stilted conversation
His acceptance speech sounded stilted and rehearsed, lacking genuine emotion.
She wrote a stilted letter of thanks that felt more like a formality than sincere gratitude.
The conversation between the two strangers was stilted at first, but soon became more relaxed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – awkward: not smooth or graceful; causing discomfort or difficulty
  • antonym – natural: not artificial; relaxed and unaffected in manner or style
