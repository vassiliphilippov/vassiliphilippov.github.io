to pension

verb
grant a retirement pension
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən / (BrE)
to grant or pay a pension to someone, especially as a benefit after retirement or long service.

Examples

to pension a worker
After thirty years as a professor, the university finally pensioned him upon his retirement.
She was pensioned off at age sixty and now enjoys her time traveling.
The company pensioned several long-serving employees last year after closing the factory.

Related Terms

  • synonym – retire: to leave one's job and cease to work, usually due to age or long service
