to unplug

verb
disconnect from digital devices
Pronunciation: / ənˈplʌɡ / (AmE), / ənˈplʌɡ / (BrE)
to stop using electronic devices or online services, especially deliberately and temporarily, in order to rest or reduce stress.

Examples

to unplug from technology
She decided to unplug from social media for a whole weekend and feel calmer.
After work he unplugged his phone and went hiking with no notifications.
Many health experts recommend unplugging from devices before bed to improve sleep quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disconnect: to end or interrupt a connection, including connections to devices or networks.
  • antonym – reconnect: to resume a connection to devices, networks, or online services; opposite of unplug (in this sense).
