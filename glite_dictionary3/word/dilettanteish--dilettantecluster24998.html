dilettanteish

adjective
superficial or amateurish interest
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪləˈtæntɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌdɪləˈtæntɪʃ / (BrE)
Showing a superficial or amateurish interest, especially in the arts or a field of study; lacking depth or serious commitment.

Examples

dilettanteish approach to art
Critics dismissed the novel as a dilettanteish attempt at literary fiction.
He was respected in business, but his art collection was assembled in a rather dilettanteish manner.
Her dilettanteish approach to photography meant she rarely studied the technical aspects deeply.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amateurish: lacking professional skill or polish
  • antonym – proficient: skilled and competent
