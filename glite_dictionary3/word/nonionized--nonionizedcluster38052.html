nonionized

adjective
not converted into ions
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈaɪ.əˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈaɪ.əˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not converted into ions; existing in a form without electrical charge separation.

Examples

nonionized form of a drug
At a neutral pH, the drug remains mostly nonionized and is absorbed more efficiently.
The difference in absorption rates is due to the nonionized form of the compound.
Water molecules are nonionized unless they are dissociated by an electrolyte.

  • antonym – ionized: converted into ions
