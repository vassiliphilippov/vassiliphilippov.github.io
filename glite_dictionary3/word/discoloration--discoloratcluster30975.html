discoloration

noun
change of color to a duller or faded state
Pronunciation: / dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a change from the original color to a faded, dull, or less attractive color, or a mark where this has happened

Examples

discoloration on fabric
She noticed a slight discoloration around the edges of the old photograph.
The discoloration on the fabric was caused by sunlight exposure over several years.
Water damage led to the discoloration of the ceiling tiles.
She tried to remove the discoloration from her white shirt without success.
The plumber explained that the brown discoloration in the water was caused by rust in the pipes.
There was a noticeable discoloration on the old photograph where the sun had faded it.
