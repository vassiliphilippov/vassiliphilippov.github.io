wrecker

noun
someone who deliberately causes destruction or sabotage
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes destruction or sabotages, especially by intentionally wrecking property or causing accidents

Examples

property wrecker
Authorities believe the shipwreck was caused by a wrecker seeking to loot the cargo.
Several wreckers were arrested for planning to destroy the railroad bridge.
The spy acted as a wrecker by sabotaging the machinery in the factory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rescuer: someone who saves others, often from wrecks
  • synonym – saboteur: a person who engages in sabotage
