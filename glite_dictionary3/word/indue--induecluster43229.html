to indue

verb
to endow or provide with a quality
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdu / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuː / (BrE)
(archaic) To endow or provide (someone or something) with a quality, ability, or attribute.

Examples

to indue someone with power
By constant practice, education can indue a person with great wisdom.
The ceremony will indue her with the authority she needs to govern.
They believed the sacred object indued them with supernatural powers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deprive: to take away from; to remove a quality or attribute
  • synonym – endow: to give a quality or ability to someone or something
