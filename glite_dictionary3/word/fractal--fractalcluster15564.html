fractal

noun
self-similar geometric pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈfræk.təl / (AmE), / ˈfræk.təl / (BrE)
A complex geometric shape in which each part has the same statistical character as the whole, often used to describe patterns that are self-similar at different scales; especially in mathematics.

Examples

fractal pattern
Many natural structures, such as snowflakes and ferns, display fractal patterns.
The coastline appears to be a fractal because it looks similar whether you zoom in or out.
The mathematician explained how a fractal can be generated by repeating a simple process.
