hematoma

noun
swelling of clotted blood under tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈtoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtəʊmə / (BrE)
A localized swelling in the body caused by an accumulation of blood, usually clotted, in tissue after blood vessels are broken.

Examples

hematoma after injury
After the fall, a large hematoma developed near his elbow, causing discomfort.
It can take several weeks for a hematoma to completely disappear without medical intervention.
The doctor examined the hematoma on her leg and recommended rest.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bruise: An injury appearing as a discoloration of the skin due to bleeding underneath
