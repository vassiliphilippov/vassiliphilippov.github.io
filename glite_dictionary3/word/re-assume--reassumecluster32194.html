to re-assume

verb
take on again after a break
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈsuːm / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to take on (a role, responsibility, behavior, etc.) again after a period away from it

Examples

to re-assume a role
After a two-year hiatus, she decided to re-assume her position as chairperson.
He tried to re-assume his daily routine after recovering from surgery.
Once the crisis was over, the manager could re-assume control of the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resume: to take up or begin again after an interruption
