Ch'in Shih Huang Ti

proper noun
first Emperor of unified China, 3rd century BC
Pronunciation: / ʧɪn ʃɪ hwɑŋ ti / (AmE), / ʧɪn ʃɪ hwæŋ ti / (BrE)
the first Emperor of the Qin dynasty who unified China, commissioned much of the Great Wall, and established standardized systems of currency, weights, and laws; died 210 BC.

Examples

Ch'in Shih Huang Ti unified China
Ch'in Shih Huang Ti is credited with unifying China and commissioning the beginnings of the Great Wall.
Many reforms introduced by Ch'in Shih Huang Ti continue to influence China today.
The reign of Ch'in Shih Huang Ti marked a turning point in Chinese history.
