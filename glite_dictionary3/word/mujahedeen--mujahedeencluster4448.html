mujahedeen

noun
Muslim guerrilla fighters (esp. in jihad)
Pronunciation: / muˌdʒɑːhəˈdin / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn / (BrE)
A group of Muslim guerrilla fighters engaged in a jihad, especially in conflict against non-Muslim forces or secular governments.

Examples

the mujahedeen fighters
Many members of the mujahedeen later became influential leaders in the region.
News reports covered the training camps operated by the mujahedeen.
The mujahedeen resisted the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan in the 1980s.
