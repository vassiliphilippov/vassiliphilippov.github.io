undogmatic

adjective
not dogmatic or rigid in beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.dɔɡˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɒɡˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
not adhering strictly to a set of beliefs or principles; open-minded and not dogmatic, especially concerning religion or ideology.

Examples

undogmatic approach
He is known for his undogmatic views on religious matters.
Her undogmatic approach to teaching encourages students to think independently.
The committee valued an undogmatic stance when discussing philosophical issues.
