de-escalation

noun
reduction of conflict intensity
Pronunciation: / diˌɛskəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌɛskəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing the intensity or seriousness of a conflict, crisis, or dangerous situation

Examples

de-escalation of conflict
Immediate de-escalation of the conflict was necessary to prevent further casualties.
Successful de-escalation strategies can help avoid full-scale war.
The ambassador called for de-escalation and urged both sides to return to negotiations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defuse: to make a tense situation less dangerous or volatile
  • antonym – escalation: an increase in the intensity or seriousness of a conflict or situation
