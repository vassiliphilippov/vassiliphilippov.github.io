strapado

noun
a method of torture involving suspension by the wrists
Pronunciation: / strəˈpɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / strəˈpɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A method of torture in which a person's hands are tied behind their back, and they are hoisted by a rope attached to their wrists and then dropped so that their fall is abruptly stopped, causing intense pain and possible injury.

Examples

the strapado torture method
Historical records describe the strapado as a particularly brutal form of torture.

The prisoner was subjected to the strapado during the inquisition.
