Pangloss

proper noun
incurably optimistic person (from Voltaire)
Pronunciation: / ˈpænˌɡlɔs / (AmE), / ˈpaŋɡlɒs / (BrE)
An incurable or naively optimistic person, especially as exemplified by the tutor in Voltaire's satire 'Candide'.

Examples

eternal Pangloss optimist
Despite the constant setbacks, John remained a true Pangloss, always expecting the best possible outcome.
Even in the face of disaster, his Pangloss-like optimism kept the team's spirits high.
She called him a modern Pangloss for his unwavering belief that everything would work out in the end.
