clam

noun
burrowing marine mollusk with a shell
Pronunciation: / klæm / (AmE), / klæm / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a shell that closes tightly, living buried in sand or mud, especially along the seashore

Examples

clam shell on beach
She ordered a bowl of clam chowder at the seaside restaurant.
The fishermen caught several clams to sell at the market.
We dug in the sand and found a large clam near the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mussel: a different type of bivalve mollusk, often found in freshwater or saltwater habitats
  • synonym – oyster: a marine bivalve mollusk with a rough shell, cultivated for food and pearls
