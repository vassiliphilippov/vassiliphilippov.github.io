kutch

noun
tannin extract from Pacific mangrove bark
Pronunciation: / kʌtʃ / (AmE), / kʌtʃ / (BrE)
a tannin extract obtained from the bark of various mangrove trees in Pacific regions, used in tanning and dyeing

Examples

kutch extract for tanning
The chemical analysis revealed that the kutch extract was rich in valuable tannins.
The tannery imported high-quality kutch for use in the leather dyeing process.
Traditional textile industries in the region relied on kutch as a principal tanning agent.
