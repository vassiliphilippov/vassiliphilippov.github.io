senior

adjective
greater in rank or age
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːniɚ /; / ˈsiːniər / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə / (BrE)
having a higher rank, age, or status than others; superior in position

Examples

senior manager
Despite her young age, she quickly became a senior engineer at the firm.
He was appointed as the senior manager in the department.
The senior members of the team guided the new recruits.
Discounts are available for senior citizens over the age of sixty-five.
My grandmother is a senior member of the local book club.
The hospital provides special care for its senior patients.
