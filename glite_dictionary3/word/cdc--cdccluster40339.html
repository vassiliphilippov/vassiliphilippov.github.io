CDC

noun
U.S. federal agency for disease control
Pronunciation: / si di si / (AmE), / siː diː siː / (BrE)
the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, a U.S. federal agency responsible for the investigation, diagnosis, control, and prevention of diseases

Examples

the CDC guidelines
According to the CDC, vaccination rates are on the rise this year.
Scientists at the CDC are studying the recent outbreak.
The CDC issued new guidelines to help prevent the spread of infectious diseases.
