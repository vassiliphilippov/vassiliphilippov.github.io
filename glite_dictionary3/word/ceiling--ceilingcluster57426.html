ceiling

noun
maximum altitude an aircraft can reach
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the maximum altitude at which an aircraft can fly under specified conditions

Examples

aircraft ceiling high
Due to engine limitations, its operational ceiling is lower than that of similar jets.
Pilots must be aware of their aircraft's ceiling for safety reasons.
The aircraft can reach a ceiling of 40,000 feet under optimal conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – altitude: height above a given level, especially sea level
  • antonym – floor: the minimum altitude permitted or achievable
