marabout

noun
large African carrion-eating stork
Pronunciation: / ˈmær əˌbu / (AmE), / ˈmær əˌbuː / (BrE)
A large African stork (Leptoptilos crumeniferus) with black and white plumage, known for eating carrion and for its downy underwing feathers used in trimming garments.

Examples

marabout stork feathers
A huge marabout soared overhead as the safari group watched in awe.
Fashion designers once prized the soft feathers of the marabout for clothing trims.
The marabout can often be seen wading in shallow lakes searching for food.
