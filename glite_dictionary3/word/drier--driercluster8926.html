drier

noun
substance that promotes drying
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪər / (AmE), / ˈdraɪə / (BrE)
A substance that promotes or accelerates the process of drying, especially by absorbing moisture.

Examples

chemical drier
Calcium chloride acts as an effective drier in many industrial processes.
Silica gel is a common drier used in packaging to keep products moisture-free.
The laboratory stores chemicals with a drier to prevent water absorption.

Related Terms

  • synonym – desiccant: a substance used to remove moisture
