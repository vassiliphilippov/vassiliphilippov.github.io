extemporaneous

adjective
done without preparation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɛmpəˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛmpərəˈneɪəs / (BrE)
Spoken or done without preparation; improvised.

Examples

extemporaneous speech
His extemporaneous remarks added energy to the meeting but lacked depth.
She gave an extemporaneous performance when the scheduled singer fell ill.
The speaker delivered an extemporaneous address in response to an unexpected question.

Related Terms

  • synonym – impromptu: done without being planned or rehearsed
  • synonym – improvised: created or performed spontaneously without preparation
  • antonym – prepared: done with planning and forethought
