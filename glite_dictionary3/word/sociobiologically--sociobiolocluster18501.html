sociobiologically

adverb
in relation to sociobiology
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊsiˌoʊbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiəʊbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to sociobiology, that is, from the perspective of the study of the biological basis of social behavior.

Examples

sociobiologically explain behavior
She analyzed the mating rituals sociobiologically to reveal their adaptive significance.
Some behaviors can be explained sociobiologically, rather than purely culturally or environmentally.
The scientist interpreted animal interactions sociobiologically, focusing on evolutionary advantages.
