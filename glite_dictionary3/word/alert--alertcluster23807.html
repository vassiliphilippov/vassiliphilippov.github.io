alert

adjective
quick, mentally attentive
Pronunciation: / əˈlɝːt /; / əˈlɜrt / (AmE), / əˈlɜːt / (BrE)
quick to notice and respond to things; mentally attentive and responsive

Examples

alert mind
After a good night's rest, he felt alert and ready for the day.

You need to stay alert during the meeting to catch any important changes.
After a good night's sleep, she felt more alert than usual.
Parents need to be alert to any changes in their children's behavior.
The security guard remained alert throughout his shift.
