leukopenia

noun
abnormally low white blood cell count
Pronunciation: / ˌluːkoʊˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˌluːkəˈpiːniə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally low level of white blood cells in the blood.

Examples

leukopenia after chemotherapy
Certain chemotherapy treatments can cause leukopenia as a side effect.
People with leukopenia are more vulnerable to infections due to their weakened immune system.
The patient was diagnosed with leukopenia after a routine blood test revealed a low white cell count.

Related Terms

  • antonym – leukocytosis: an increase in the number of white blood cells
