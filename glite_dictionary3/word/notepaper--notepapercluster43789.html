notepaper

noun
paper for writing notes or letters
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊtˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
paper used for writing short notes or letters, typically in smaller sheets than standard writing paper

Examples

notepaper for messages
He scribbled a message on a piece of notepaper and left it on her desk.
She bought some flower-patterned notepaper to write thank-you letters.
The drawer was filled with envelopes and unused notepaper.
