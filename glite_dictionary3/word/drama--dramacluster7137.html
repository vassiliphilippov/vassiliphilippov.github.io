drama

noun
theatrical literary genre
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdrɑː.mə / (BrE)
A literary genre that consists of works intended to be performed on stage by actors; plays and other theatrical works.

Examples

drama genre
Shakespeare's most famous drama is often considered to be Hamlet.
She prefers reading drama to novels or poetry.
The school is putting on a modern drama about teenage life.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – comedy: a type of drama intended to amuse and entertain
  • degree lower – farce: a light, humorous dramatic work with exaggerated situations
  • synonym – play: a written work designed for theatrical performance; synonymous with drama in some contexts
  • degree higher – tragedy: a type of drama dealing with serious or sorrowful themes
