wittingly

adverb
knowingly; deliberately
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that is done with full knowledge, awareness, or intention; deliberately.

Examples

wittingly break the law
It was clear that he wittingly misled the investigators.
She wittingly omitted certain details from her report.
The law punishes those who wittingly violate safety regulations.
