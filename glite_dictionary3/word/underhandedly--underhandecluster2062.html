underhandedly

adverb
in a secret and deceitful way
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈhændɪdli / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈhændɪdli / (BrE)
in a secret and deceitful way; slyly and secretly, often to achieve an unfair advantage

Examples

to act underhandedly
He acted underhandedly to secure the promotion, even spreading rumors about his rival.
She felt betrayed when she learned her friends had underhandedly excluded her from the trip.
The company was accused of underhandedly altering the terms of the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym – openly: in an open or public way; not concealed
  • synonym – secretly: without others knowing; in secret
