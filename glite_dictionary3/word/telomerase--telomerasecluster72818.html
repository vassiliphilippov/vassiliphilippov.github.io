telomerase

noun
enzyme that adds telomeres to chromosomes
Pronunciation: / təˈlɑmɚˌeɪs / (AmE), / tɪˈlɒmərˌeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme found in eukaryotic cells that adds telomeres to the ends of chromosomes after cell division

Examples

telomerase enzyme activity
Researchers studied how telomerase repairs the ends of chromosomes after replication.
Scientists discovered that telomerase activity is higher in certain cancer cells.
The absence of telomerase can lead to the gradual shortening of chromosomes.
