behaviorist

noun
psychologist subscribing to behaviorism
Pronunciation: / bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (BrE)
a psychologist who studies and interprets behavior according to the principles of behaviorism, focusing on observable actions rather than internal mental states

Examples

behaviorist studies actions
As a behaviorist, she believed that people's actions were shaped by their environments.
Modern therapy often incorporates insights from behaviorist approaches alongside other psychological schools.
The behaviorist conducted experiments on animal learning using reward and punishment.
