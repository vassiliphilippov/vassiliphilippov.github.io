acrocentric

adjective
chromosome with off-center centromere
Pronunciation: / ˌækrəˈsentrɪk / (AmE), / ˌækrəˈsentrɪk / (BrE)
(of a chromosome) having the centromere located close to one end, resulting in one very short (p) arm and one very long (q) arm.

Examples

acrocentric chromosome type
An acrocentric chromosome has a centromere that is positioned near one end.
Human chromosomes 13, 14, 15, 21, and 22 are classified as acrocentric.
The researchers observed that the cell had several acrocentric chromosomes under the microscope.
