shorthorn

noun
English breed of short-horned cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrthɔrn / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːthɔːn / (BrE)
A breed of cattle originating in England, characterized by short horns and raised for both milk and meat.

Examples

shorthorn cattle breed
Some farmers prefer the dual-purpose qualities of the shorthorn over other cattle breeds.
The shorthorn is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
The farm introduced a new herd of shorthorn cattle to improve both milk and beef production.
