apriorism

noun
belief in a priori principles; doctrine of innate knowledge
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪˈpraɪəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈprʌɪəˌrɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief that certain principles or knowledge are known independently of experience, especially the doctrine that knowledge is based on self-evident or presupposed principles.

Examples

philosophical apriorism
Critics of apriorism argue that knowledge must be tested through observation and experiment.
Some schools of thought reject apriorism in favor of empirical approaches to knowledge.
The philosopher's argument was grounded in apriorism, emphasizing truths independent of experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym – empiricism: the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience
