propagandistic

adjective
relating to propaganda
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːpəɡænˈdɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəɡænˈdɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of propaganda, especially by promoting a particular cause or point of view in a biased or misleading way.

Examples

propagandistic news coverage
Some news outlets present information in a highly propagandistic manner to influence public opinion.
The film was criticized for its propagandistic tone and lack of impartiality.
The regime distributed propagandistic posters to sway the population.

Related Terms

  • synonym – biased: unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • antonym – objective: not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
