palingenesis

noun
reappearance of ancestral traits in development
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪnˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpælɪnˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the appearance during embryonic development of characteristics or structures that were present in the evolutionary ancestors of a species

Examples

palingenesis in embryos
Research into palingenesis has provided insight into the origins of complex body plans.
Some biologists argue that palingenesis can reveal the evolutionary pathways of vertebrates through embryonic stages.
The theory of palingenesis suggests that certain embryonic structures replicate ancestral forms.
