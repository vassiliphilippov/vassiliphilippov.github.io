cackle

noun
sound made by a hen or harsh laughter
Pronunciation: / ˈkæk.əl /; / ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkæk.əl /; / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
a loud, harsh, high-pitched sound or laugh, especially made by a hen or by a person laughing in a shrill way

Examples

a loud cackle
A farmer can often tell when a hen has just laid an egg by her distinctive cackle.
The morning was quiet except for the occasional cackle from the chicken coop.
We heard the hen's cackle echoing through the barnyard after she laid her egg.
After hearing the joke, he burst into a loud cackle that drew attention.
Her cackle echoed throughout the room, startling everyone nearby.
The movie villain's evil cackle sent shivers down my spine.
Amid the cackle of students, the teacher struggled to regain their attention.
The room was filled with the cackle of gossipy neighbors.
Their cackle echoed down the hallway, making it hard to concentrate.
