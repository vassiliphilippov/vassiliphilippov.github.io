conservativism

noun
support for traditional values and opposition to change
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝː.və.tɪ.vɪz.əm / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪ.vɪz.əm / (BrE)
A political or theological belief system favoring the maintenance of traditional institutions and values and opposing radical change.

Examples

political conservativism
Debates between conservativism and progressivism have shaped government policies for decades.
His political philosophy is grounded in conservativism and a deep respect for longstanding traditions.
The rise of conservativism in recent years has influenced social and economic reforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conservatism: preference for traditional values and institutions; opposition to rapid change
  • antonym – progressivism: support or advocacy of social reform and new ideas
