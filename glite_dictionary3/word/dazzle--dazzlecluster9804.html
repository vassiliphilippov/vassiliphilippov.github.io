to dazzle

verb
to blind temporarily with intense light
Pronunciation: / ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose clear vision, especially by shining a very bright light into their eyes

Examples

to dazzle with headlights
Drivers can easily be dazzled by oncoming headlights at night.
The camera flash dazzled her, making it hard to see for a few seconds.
The sun dazzled him as he stepped out of the dark theater.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blind: to prevent someone from seeing, either temporarily or permanently
