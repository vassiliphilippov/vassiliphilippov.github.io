calcifugous

adjective
growing in acidic (lime-poor) soil
Pronunciation: / ˌkælsəˈfjuːɡəs / (AmE), / ˌkalsɪˈfjuːɡəs / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing or living in acidic soil, not thriving in soils rich in lime or calcium carbonate.

Examples

calcifugous plants thrive
Blueberries are an example of a calcifugous crop that prefers acidic soil conditions.
Gardeners often choose calcifugous plants for areas with high rainfall and acidic ground.
Many heather species are calcifugous and will not grow well in alkaline soils.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calcicole: (of a plant) thriving in lime-rich soils
