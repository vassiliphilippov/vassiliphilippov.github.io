unconscious

adjective
done without awareness or intention
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkɑːnʃəs /; / ʌnˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
not aware of something or not noticing it; done without realizing or intending (of actions or people)

Examples

unconscious habit
Many habits, like tapping your fingers, are unconscious and hard to break.
Much of our behavior is guided by unconscious motives we do not fully understand.
She gave an unconscious smile, not realizing she was being watched.
He seemed unconscious of the effect his words had on others.
She walked past the painting, completely unconscious of its artistic value.
The children were laughing, unconscious of the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – automatic: performed without conscious thought or intention
  • antonym – aware: knowing about something; conscious of
  • antonym – deliberate: done consciously and intentionally
  • synonym – ignorant: lacking knowledge or awareness in general; uneducated or unsophisticated
