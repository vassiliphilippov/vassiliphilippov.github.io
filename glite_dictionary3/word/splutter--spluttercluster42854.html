to splutter

verb
speak hastily with spitting sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈsplʌtɚ /; / ˈsplʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplʌtə / (BrE)
to speak or say something in a rushed or confused way, often with spitting or popping sounds, especially due to strong emotion

Examples

to splutter words
"I... I never said that!" he spluttered in protest.
She spluttered with anger when accused of lying.
The teacher spluttered out instructions amidst the chaos.
He began to splutter angrily when accused of lying.
She spluttered her tea in surprise at the news.
The engine spluttered and stalled in the cold morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spit: to eject saliva forcefully from the mouth
  • synonym – sputter: to make a series of soft explosive sounds
  • synonym – stammer: to speak with sudden pauses and repeated sounds or words, often due to nervousness
  • synonym – stutter: to speak with involuntary repetition of sounds, usually due to a speech disorder or nervousness
