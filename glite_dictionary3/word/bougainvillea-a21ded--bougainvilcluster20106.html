bougainvillea

noun
tropical ornamental vine with colorful bracts
Pronunciation: / ˌbuːɡənˈvɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbʊɡənˈvɪliə / (BrE)
A tropical South American woody vine or shrub of the genus Bougainvillea, commonly grown for its brightly colored flower bracts.

Examples

bougainvillea plant
In warm climates, bougainvillea can be seen cascading over fences and trellises.
She planted a bougainvillea near the porch, hoping its pink bracts would brighten the entrance.
The garden was filled with vibrant bougainvillea climbing the old stone wall.
