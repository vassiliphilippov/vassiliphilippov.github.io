monophonic

adjective
having a single melodic musical line
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of music) consisting of a single melodic line without accompaniment or harmonies.

Examples

monophonic chant melody
Early Gregorian music is primarily monophonic, lacking harmony or counterpoint.
The chant was sung in a monophonic style, with no instrumental accompaniment.
Unlike polyphonic compositions, monophonic pieces focus on a single musical line.

Related Terms

  • antonym – homophonic: having one main melody with accompanying chords or parts
  • antonym – polyphonic: having two or more independent melodic lines
