register

noun
distinctive pitch range in music
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdʒɪstər / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstə / (BrE)
In music, a particular range of pitch characterized by a distinct quality of sound produced by the human voice, an instrument, or a pipe organ stop.

Examples

high register voice
Each pipe organ stop produces a different register, adding richness to the music.
She trained her voice to sing comfortably in the higher register required for the role.
The piece shifts from the violin's low register to its sparkling upper notes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – octave: an interval spanning eight notes or a pitch range in music
  • synonym – tessitura: the general range of pitches in a piece of music or vocal part
