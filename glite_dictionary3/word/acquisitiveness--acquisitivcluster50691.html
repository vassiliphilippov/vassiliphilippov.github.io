acquisitiveness

noun
desire to acquire and possess things
Pronunciation: / əˈkwɪzətɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈkwɪzɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
a strong desire to acquire and possess things, especially material goods

Examples

strong acquisitiveness for money
His acquisitiveness led him to collect rare and expensive artwork from around the world.
Some critics argue that modern society encourages acquisitiveness at the expense of deeper values.
There is a fine line between ambition and acquisitiveness in business.

Related Terms

  • antonym – generosity: the quality of being kind and generous, especially in giving
  • synonym – greed: an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
