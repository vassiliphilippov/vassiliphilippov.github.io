to occlude

verb
to block or close up
Pronunciation: / əˈkluːd / (AmE), / əˈkluːd / (BrE)
to block or close up a passage or opening, especially to prevent the passage of a fluid or air

Examples

to occlude an artery
A buildup of plaque can occlude the arteries, leading to serious health problems.
If a filter becomes clogged, it may occlude the flow of water entirely.
The surgeon had to occlude the blood vessel to stop the bleeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym – block: to stop something from moving through a space
  • synonym – obstruct: to be or get in the way of; hinder
  • antonym – unblock: to remove a blockage from something
