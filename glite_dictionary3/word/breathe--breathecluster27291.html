to breathe

verb
(of wine) develop flavor by exposure to air
Pronunciation: / brið / (AmE), / briːð / (BrE)
(of wine) to develop its full flavor by being exposed to air, typically after uncorking.

Examples

let wine breathe
Allowing the vintage port to breathe brings out its complex aromas.
Experts suggest that red wine should breathe for a while after opening.
The sommelier recommended letting the Bordeaux breathe before serving.

  • synonym – aerate: to expose a liquid, especially wine, to air
