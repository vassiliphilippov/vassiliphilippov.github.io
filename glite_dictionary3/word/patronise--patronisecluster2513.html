to patronise

verb
be a regular customer of a business
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpatrənʌɪz /; / ˈpætrənaɪz / (BrE)
to regularly use the services of a business or be a customer of a shop, restaurant, etc.

Examples

to patronise a restaurant
Art lovers regularly patronise the gallery to support new exhibitions.
She prefers to patronise small, family-owned restaurants rather than big chains.
We always patronise the local bakery when we're in town.
If more customers patronise this café, it will be able to stay open longer.
Many locals choose to patronise small family-run shops instead of big supermarkets.
She has patronised that bakery for more than ten years.

  • synonym – patronize: to regularly use the services of a business (American spelling)
  • synonym – support: to give business or custom to
