federation

noun
union of states into one political entity
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forming a political unity from separate states, colonies, or provinces, each retaining control over its internal affairs

Examples

the federation process
Talks are ongoing about the federation of several neighboring states for economic cooperation.
The creation of the Australian federation in 1901 united the colonies into a single nation.
The process of federation allowed each province to have its own laws while sharing a central government.

Related Terms

  • antonym – confederation: an organization of states or groups united for a common purpose, often with looser ties than a federation
