reedbird

noun
migratory American songbird
Pronunciation: / ˈridˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈriːdˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A migratory American songbird, especially the bobolink, often found in marshy or reedy areas.

Examples

American reedbird in marsh
Scientists tracked the movements of the reedbird to understand its migration patterns.
The song of the reedbird fills the marshes every spring as it migrates north.
We spotted a reedbird perched quietly among the cattails at dawn.
