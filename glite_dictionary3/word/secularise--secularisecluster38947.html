to secularise

verb
make non-religious; remove religious influence
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
To make something secular; to remove religious or spiritual influence from it.

Examples

to secularise education
He argued that modern art tends to secularise themes that were once deeply spiritual.
Over the years, many traditional holidays have become more secularised in their celebration.
The government decided to secularise the education system, removing religious instruction from schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sanctify: make holy or sacred
  • synonym – secularize: make something secular; American spelling
