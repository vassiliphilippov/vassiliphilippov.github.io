to decolorise

verb
to remove or lighten color
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkʌlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌləraɪz / (BrE)
to cause to lose color; to make white or lighter in color

Examples

to decolorise fabric
Prolonged exposure to sunlight can decolorise outdoor furniture.
They used peroxide to decolorise their hair before dyeing it.
This chemical will decolorise the fabric within minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bleach: to make something white or lighter by removing color
  • antonym – stain: to mark or discolor with something that is not easily removed
