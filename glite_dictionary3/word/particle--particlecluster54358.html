particle

noun
a tiny piece or amount
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrtɪkəl /; / ˈpɑːr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪkl /; / ˈpɑː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a very small piece or amount of something, especially in science or physics

Examples

a particle of dust
Not a particle of dust could be found on the polished shelves.
She felt a particle of doubt but pushed it aside quickly.
There wasn’t a particle of truth in his excuse.
Dust is made up of countless tiny particles too small to see with the naked eye.
Physicists study how each particle interacts with others at the quantum level.
The electron is a fundamental particle in atomic structure.
