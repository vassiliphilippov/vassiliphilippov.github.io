whipping

noun
diagonal sewing stitch on fabric edge
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A sewing stitch worked over the edge of fabric in a series of diagonal passes to prevent fraying.

Examples

whipping stitch sewing
Learning the whipping stitch is essential for basic hand sewing.
She used a whipping stitch along the seam to stop the fabric from unraveling.
The edges were finished with whipping to give the blanket a neat appearance.
