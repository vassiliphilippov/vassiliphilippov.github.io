to nobble

verb
cheat or trick someone out of something
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒbl / (BrE)
to obtain something from someone by deceit; to cheat or trick someone out of something

Examples

to nobble money
He tried to nobble me out of my share of the profits, but I caught on quickly.
It was reported that the accountant had nobbled several clients over the years.
The new owner was wary of anyone who might attempt to nobble him during the business transition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defraud: to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • synonym – swindle: to cheat someone out of money or property
