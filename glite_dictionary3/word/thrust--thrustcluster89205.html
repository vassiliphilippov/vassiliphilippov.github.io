thrust

noun
a stab or blow with a pointed weapon
Pronunciation: / θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
a strong, sudden push or blow, especially with a hand, arm, or sharp object

Examples

thrust of a sword
She parried the sword and countered with a powerful thrust to his side.
The assassin ended the struggle with a single thrust of his dagger.
With a swift thrust, the fencer scored a point against his opponent.
He scared off the stray dog with a thrust of his walking stick.
The angry coach made a sharp thrust in the air to emphasize his point.
With a sudden thrust, she pointed her finger at the map to indicate their destination.
