promythium

noun
introductory section before a story
Pronunciation: / proʊˈmɪθiəm / (AmE), / prəʊˈmɪθiəm / (BrE)
A short introductory section or preface that comes before the main story, especially in a fable or literary work.

Examples

promythium before story
In classical literature, the promythium would often reveal the lesson before the tale began.
The ancient manuscript includes a promythium that gives context to the fable.
The teacher asked students to analyze the promythium for clues about the author’s intent.
