Pythagoras

proper noun
ancient Greek philosopher and mathematician
Pronunciation: / paɪˈθæɡərəs / (AmE), / pɪˈθæɡərəs / (BrE)
(c. 580–500 BC) A Greek philosopher and mathematician known for establishing the Pythagorean theorem and making significant contributions to mathematics and philosophy.
