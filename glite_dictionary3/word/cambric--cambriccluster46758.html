cambric

noun
finely woven white linen or cotton fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈkæm.brɪk / (AmE), / ˈkæm.brɪk / (BrE)
a finely woven, lightweight white linen or cotton fabric, often used for shirts, handkerchiefs, and lingerie

Examples

soft cambric fabric
Old letters were wrapped carefully in a piece of white cambric.
She wore a delicate dress made of cambric for the summer evening.
The store sells handkerchiefs crafted from fine cambric.

Related Terms

  • synonym – batiste: a fine, soft, lightweight fabric similar to cambric
  • synonym – linen: a fabric made from flax, often used in textiles
