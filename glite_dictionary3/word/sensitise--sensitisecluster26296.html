to sensitise

verb
make more sensitive or responsive
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something more aware of or responsive to certain things, such as events, feelings, or substances

Examples

to sensitise the skin
Exposure to certain chemicals can sensitise the skin, causing allergic reactions.
Repeated criticism can sensitise a person to negative feedback.
The school organized a workshop to sensitise students to environmental issues.
It is important to sensitise medical staff to patients' emotional needs.
Teachers are working to sensitise students to issues of diversity and inclusion.
The campaign aimed to sensitise the public to the dangers of smoking.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitise: make less sensitive or less aware
  • antonym – desensitize: to make less sensitive or responsive
  • synonym – sensitize: alternate spelling (American English) of 'sensitise'
