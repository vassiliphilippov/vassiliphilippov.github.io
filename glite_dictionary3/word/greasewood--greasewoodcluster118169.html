greasewood

noun
spiny shrub of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrisˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːswʊd / (BrE)
A low, hardy, much-branched spiny shrub (Sarcobatus vermiculatus), commonly found in alkaline soils of western North America.

Examples

greasewood bush
In the dry valleys, greasewood grows in large tangled thickets along with sagebrush.
Ranchers often clear greasewood to make way for grazing cattle.
The silvery leaves of greasewood reflect the intense sunlight of the Nevada desert.
