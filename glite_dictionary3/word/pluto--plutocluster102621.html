Pluto

proper noun
Roman god of the underworld
Pronunciation: / ˈpluːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpluːtəʊ / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the underworld and ruler of the dead, equivalent to the Greek god Hades.

Examples

Pluto god underworld
In ancient Roman stories, Pluto presided over the realm of the dead.
Many myths describe Pluto as a stern but just ruler of the underworld.
Unlike some gods, Pluto was seldom depicted on Roman temples.
