thyroxin

noun
thyroid hormone regulating metabolism
Pronunciation: / θaɪˈroʊksɪn / (AmE), / θaɪˈrɒksɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolism by controlling the rate of oxidation in cells

Examples

thyroxin hormone level
A deficiency in thyroxin can lead to symptoms of hypothyroidism.
Synthetic thyroxin is often prescribed for patients with underactive thyroid glands.
The doctor checked her thyroxin levels to assess her thyroid function.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thyroxine: alternative spelling of 'thyroxin', the same thyroid hormone
  • synonym – triiodothyronine: another thyroid hormone affecting metabolism, often abbreviated as T3
