parathormone

noun
hormone from parathyroid glands
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈθɔːrˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈθɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the parathyroid glands that regulates the levels of calcium and phosphorus in the blood

Examples

parathormone controls calcium
A deficiency of parathormone can lead to dangerously low calcium levels in the body.
Doctors measured the patient's parathormone levels to assess her parathyroid gland function.
Overproduction of parathormone may cause elevated blood calcium and bone problems.
