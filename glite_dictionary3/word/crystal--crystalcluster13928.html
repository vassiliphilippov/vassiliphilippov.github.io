crystal

noun
quartz component in electronics
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl / (BrE)
a crystalline material, especially a piece of quartz, used as a component in electronic devices such as oscillators, radios, and watches, due to its ability to precisely control frequencies

Examples

crystal radio component
A damaged crystal can cause the circuit to malfunction.
If the crystal in your watch fails, it will stop keeping time accurately.
The radio uses a crystal for frequency stabilization.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oscillator: an electronic circuit that generates a repetitive signal
