lymphoblast

noun
immature precursor of a lymphocyte
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmfəˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈlɪmfəˌblɑːst / (BrE)
an immature cell that develops into a lymphocyte during the formation of blood cells

Examples

lymphoblast in bone marrow
Acute lymphoblastic leukemia is characterized by the rapid production of abnormal lymphoblasts.
Doctors observed an increased number of lymphoblasts in the patient's blood sample.
During early immune system development, a lymphoblast undergoes differentiation to become various types of lymphocytes.
