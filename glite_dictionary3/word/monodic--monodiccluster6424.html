monodic

adjective
having a single vocal part (music)
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːdɪk / (AmE), / məˈnɒdɪk / (BrE)
(of music) having a single melodic line or vocal part, without accompaniment or harmony.

Examples

monodic chant
A monodic chant can create a powerful and intimate musical experience.
Early church music was predominantly monodic, emphasizing the clarity of a single melody.
The composer wrote a monodic piece that showcased a solo soprano voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – polyphonic: having two or more independent melodic lines
