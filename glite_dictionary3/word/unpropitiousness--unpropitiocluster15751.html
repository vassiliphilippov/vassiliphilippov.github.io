unpropitiousness

noun
state of being unlikely to succeed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.prəˈpɪʃ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prəˈpɪʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unlikely to lead to success or favorable results.

Examples

unpropitiousness of a plan
His plan was marked by a certain unpropitiousness, as many warned it would not work.
Investors sensed the unpropitiousness of the market and decided to withdraw their funds.
The unpropitiousness of the weather made their journey even more difficult.
