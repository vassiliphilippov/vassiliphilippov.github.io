motorial

adjective
conveying signals away from CNS (medical)
Pronunciation: / moʊˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / məˈtɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
(of nerves or nerve impulses) conveying information away from the central nervous system, especially to muscles; efferent.

Examples

motorial nerve signals
Damage to the motorial pathways can lead to weakness or loss of movement in affected muscles.
Researchers studied how motorial impulses affect voluntary muscle contractions.
The motorial nerves transmit signals to your muscles, allowing you to move your limbs.
