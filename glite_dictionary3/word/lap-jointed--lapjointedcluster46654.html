lap-jointed

adjective
joined by overlapping to be flush
Pronunciation: / ˈlæp ˌdʒɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæp ˌdʒɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of two parts) joined together by overlapping, so that their surfaces are flush.

Examples

lap-jointed wooden frame
A lap-jointed frame provides a seamless and sturdy finish in woodworking projects.
The carpenter ensured that all the beams were lap-jointed for maximum strength.
These two pieces are lap-jointed, which makes the connection almost invisible.

Related Terms

  • antonym – butt-jointed: joined by butting ends together rather than overlapping
