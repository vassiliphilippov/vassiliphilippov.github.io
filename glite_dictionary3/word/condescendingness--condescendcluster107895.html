condescendingness

noun
quality of being condescending
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːndɪˈsɛndɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒndɪˈsɛndɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving in a way that shows you consider yourself more important or superior to others, often by being courteous in a patronizing manner

Examples

condescendingness at work
Despite his condescendingness, he believed he was being genuinely kind.
His condescendingness was evident in the way he spoke to the junior staff.
There was a hint of condescendingness in her tone that made everyone uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – condescension: an attitude of patronizing superiority; disdain
  • antonym – humility: modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness
