pull

noun
influence or special advantage
Pronunciation: / pʊl / (AmE), / pʊl / (BrE)
influence, power, or special advantage that allows someone to achieve things more easily than others

Examples

political pull
Despite his lack of qualifications, his pull in the company landed him the job.
It takes serious pull to arrange a meeting with the CEO on such short notice.
She got her son into the best school because she has a lot of pull in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clout: influence or power, especially in politics or business
  • synonym – influence: the capacity to have an effect on something or someone
  • synonym – leverage: the power to influence people or situations
