aphonic

adjective
unable to produce voice; only whispering
Pronunciation: / əˈfɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / əˈfɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
unable to produce voice due to injury or illness, capable only of whispered speech

Examples

aphonic after illness
After the surgery, he was aphonic and could only communicate by whispering.
Laryngitis left her aphonic for a week, unable to speak above a faint whisper.
The patient remained aphonic due to severe vocal cord damage.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – dysphonic: having difficulty in speaking due to a physical disorder
  • synonym – voiceless: lacking voice or sound (general)
