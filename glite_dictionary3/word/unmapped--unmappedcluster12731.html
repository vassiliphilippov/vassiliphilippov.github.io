unmapped

adjective
not aligned to reference genome
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmæpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈmæpt / (BrE)
(in genomics/bioinformatics) not successfully aligned or assigned to a location in a reference genome or annotation during sequence analysis.

Examples

unmapped DNA sequences
The sequencing pipeline flagged thousands of unmapped reads after quality filtering and adapter trimming.
Researchers investigated why so many microbial sequences remained unmapped against the human reference genome.
Low-complexity or contaminated reads often end up unmapped, requiring special handling in the analysis workflow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mapped: assigned or aligned to a specific location in a reference genome; opposite of unmapped in sequencing analysis.
  • synonym – unaligned: not aligned to a reference sequence or genome; often used for reads that fail to map.
