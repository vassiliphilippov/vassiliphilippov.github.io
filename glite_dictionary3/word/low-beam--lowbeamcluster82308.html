low-beam

adjective
headlight setting directing light downward
Pronunciation: / loʊ bim / (AmE), / ləʊ biːm / (BrE)
(of headlights) set to a position that directs the light downward to avoid dazzling other drivers, used especially when there is oncoming traffic.

Examples

low-beam headlights on
It is safer to use low-beam lights in foggy weather.
Many cars automatically change from high-beam to low-beam when they detect oncoming traffic.
Please switch to low-beam headlights when driving through the city at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym – high-beam: headlights set to the highest, brightest position
