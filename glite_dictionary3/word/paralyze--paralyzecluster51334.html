to paralyze

verb
make powerless; disable
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈparəˌlʌɪz /; / ˈpærəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something unable to move or act, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

to paralyze a person
Excessive regulations can sometimes paralyze innovation in a company.
If fear paralyzes you, you may be unable to respond in an emergency.
The sudden strike paralyzed the city's transportation system for days.
Sometimes anxiety can paralyze people from making important decisions.
The sudden accident seemed to paralyze the entire city with shock.
The venom can paralyze its prey within minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cripple: to make ineffective or powerless
  • synonym – immobilize: to make unable to move
  • antonym – revive: to bring back to activity or effectiveness
  • antonym – stimulate: to encourage or cause something to become more active
