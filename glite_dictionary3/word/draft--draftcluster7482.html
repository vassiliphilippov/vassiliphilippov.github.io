to draft

verb
prepare a preliminary version
Pronunciation: / dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
to prepare a preliminary version or outline of a document, plan, or drawing

Examples

to draft a letter
He spent the morning drafting his resignation letter.
She was asked to draft a proposal for the new marketing campaign.
The committee will draft a set of guidelines for future research projects.
Engineers drafted the preliminary layout of the bridge before construction began.
She was asked to draft a blueprint for the new community center.
The architects will draft several proposals before finalizing the building plan.

Related Terms

  • synonym – design: to create the plans or drawings for something before it is made
  • antonym – finalize: to complete or bring something to a finished state
  • synonym – outline: to give a general description or summary of something
  • synonym – sketch: to make a rough drawing or outline of something
