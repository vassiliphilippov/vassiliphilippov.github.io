mitigative

adjective
making less severe or serious
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪtəˌɡeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈmɪtɪɡətɪv / (BrE)
Reducing the severity or seriousness of something, especially pain, sorrow, or harmful effects.

Examples

mitigative effect
His kind words served a mitigative role, soothing her after the loss.
Policies with a mitigative effect can prevent environmental disasters from becoming catastrophic.
The doctor prescribed a mitigative treatment to help ease her chronic pain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exacerbating: making a problem, situation, or feeling worse
  • synonym – mitigating: making less severe or intense; reducing harshness
