mantispid

noun
predatory mantis-like insect; larvae parasitize spider and wasp nests
Pronunciation: / mænˈtɪspɪd / (AmE), / mænˈtɪspɪd / (BrE)
A predatory insect of the family Mantispidae, resembling a mantis, whose larvae are parasitic in the nests of spiders and wasps.

Examples

a mantispid insect
Larvae of the mantispid are known to infiltrate the egg sacs of spiders and feed on their contents.
Researchers discovered a new species of mantispid in the tropical rainforest.
The mantispid is easily mistaken for a praying mantis due to its raptorial forelegs.
