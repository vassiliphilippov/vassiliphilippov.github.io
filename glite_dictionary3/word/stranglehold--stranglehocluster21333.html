stranglehold

noun
wrestling hold that restricts breathing
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡəlˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡəlˌhəʊld / (BrE)
A wrestling hold in which the arms are pressed against the opponent's windpipe, restricting their breathing.

Examples

stranglehold wrestling move
His use of the stranglehold was both impressive and intimidating.
She broke free from the stranglehold just as the referee counted to three.
The wrestler applied a stranglehold to his opponent, forcing him to surrender.

  • synonym – chokehold: a wrestling hold involving pressure on the neck
