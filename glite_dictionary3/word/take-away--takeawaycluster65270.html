take-away

noun
main point or lesson
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈteɪkəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a main point, lesson, or important piece of information that someone learns from a talk, meeting, experience, or text.

Examples

If there's one take-away from our conversation, it's that communication must improve across departments.
