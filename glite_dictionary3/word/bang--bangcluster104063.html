bang

adverb
directly; squarely; exactly
Pronunciation: / bæŋ / (AmE), / bæŋ / (BrE)
directly or squarely; used to emphasize that something happens exactly or straight into something else.

Examples

bang into the wall
He ran bang into the door and dropped his books.
She arrived bang on time for the meeting.
The dart hit bang in the center of the target, amazing everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slap: directly; exactly (used similarly as an adverb, e.g. 'slap bang in the middle')
