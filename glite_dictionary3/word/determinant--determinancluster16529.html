determinant

noun
matrix used in solving equations
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɝː.mɪ.nənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nənt / (BrE)
A square matrix whose numerical value can be computed and is used especially in solving systems of simultaneous linear equations.

Examples

matrix determinant
Calculating the determinant of a matrix is a fundamental step in linear algebra.
If the determinant equals zero, the matrix is singular and the equations may not have a unique solution.
The value of the determinant helps determine whether a system of linear equations has a unique solution.
