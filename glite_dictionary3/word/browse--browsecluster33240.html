to browse

verb
(of animals) feed on leaves or twigs
Pronunciation: / braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
(of animals) to feed on leaves, twigs, or other high-growing vegetation, especially in a leisurely or selective way.

Examples

deer browse leaves
During winter, the elk browsed the shrubs for food.
Goats often browse in the rocky fields instead of grazing on grass.
The deer browse on the lower branches of the bushes every morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – graze: (of animals) to feed on growing grass or herbage
