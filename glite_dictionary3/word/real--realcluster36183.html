real

adjective
adjusted for inflation (economics)
Pronunciation: / ˈriːəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl / (BrE)
(of a value, cost, or amount) calculated with inflation or other factors considered, representing true purchasing power rather than nominal value.

Examples

real value
During periods of high inflation, real wages may fall even if nominal wages rise.
Economists use real GDP to compare economic growth over time.
The report showed that real prices for consumer goods have remained stable for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nominal: (economics) existing in name only, or not adjusted for inflation
