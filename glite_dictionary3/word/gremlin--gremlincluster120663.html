gremlin

noun
mischievous mythical creature
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɛmlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛmlɪn / (BrE)
(in folklore) a mischievous imaginary creature said to cause problems, especially with machinery and aircraft.

Examples

a gremlin in the engine
After hearing strange noises in the night, she wondered if a gremlin had paid her a visit.
Some engineers joked that a gremlin was hiding in the engine, causing it to fail repeatedly.
The pilots blamed the malfunction on a mischievous gremlin tampering with their instruments.
