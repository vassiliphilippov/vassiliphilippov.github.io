kapok

noun
tropical tree yielding silky fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpɒk / (BrE)
A large tropical tree (Ceiba pentandra) with a thick, deeply ridged trunk, producing pods containing seeds covered in silky fibers used as a stuffing material.

Examples

tall kapok tree
Many pillows are stuffed with kapok for a natural and soft filling.
The kapok tree can grow over 200 feet tall in some rainforests.
Traditional life vests were once filled with kapok fibers because of their buoyancy.
