to double

verb
call 'double' in a card game
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl / (BrE)
(in certain card games) to make a demand for a card or suit, particularly by calling 'double' to increase the stakes or challenge the bid.

Examples

to double a bid
He decided not to double because he wasn't certain about his partner's cards.
If you double in this round, you must be sure of your hand.
She confidently chose to double her opponent's bid at the bridge table.
