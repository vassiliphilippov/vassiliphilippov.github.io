sprinkling

noun
baptism by scattering water
Pronunciation: / ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
(Rare) The act or method of baptizing by scattering a small amount of water on a person's head instead of immersing.

Examples

baptism by sprinkling
Historical records indicate that sprinkling became more common in Europe after the Middle Ages.
Some denominations accept sprinkling while others insist on full immersion during baptism.
The church practiced sprinkling as the preferred form of baptism for infants.

Related Terms

  • synonym – baptism: Christian rite of admission and adoption often by water
  • antonym – immersion: baptism by fully submerging in water
