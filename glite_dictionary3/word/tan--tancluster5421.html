tan

noun
trigonometric ratio (opposite/adjacent side)
Pronunciation: / tæn / (AmE), / tæn / (BrE)
the ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the side adjacent to it in a right-angled triangle; short for 'tangent' in trigonometry

Examples

tan of an angle
In trigonometry class, we learned how to calculate the tan of an angle.
The value of tan theta increases rapidly as theta approaches 90 degrees.
To find the height of the tree, she used the tan of 45 degrees.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cotangent: the reciprocal trigonometric function (adjacent/opposite)
  • synonym – tangent: the trigonometric function relating the angle to its opposite and adjacent sides
