to pack

verb
arrange items in a container
Pronunciation: / pæk / (AmE), / pæk /; / pak / (BrE)
to put things into a container or arrange them for storage or transport; to load an animal or person with a burden

Examples

to pack a suitcase
Could you pack these clothes into the suitcase for me?
She always packs her lunch before leaving for work.
They packed their belongings into boxes for the move.
Farmers would pack their animals with goods to take to the market.
She helped her father pack the donkeys before they set out at dawn.
The guide began to pack the mules for the long journey.

  • antonym – unpack: to remove things from a container or suitcase
