emission

noun
release of electrons from atoms
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the process by which electrons are released from atoms, typically as a result of energy absorption or excitation

Examples

electron emission rate
Researchers measured the emission rate of electrons under different experimental conditions.
The emission of electrons from the surface of the cathode is known as thermionic emission.
X-rays are produced by the emission of electrons when certain metals are struck by high-energy particles.

  • antonym – absorption: the process by which something is taken in or absorbed
  • synonym – ejection: the act of being thrown out or expelled
