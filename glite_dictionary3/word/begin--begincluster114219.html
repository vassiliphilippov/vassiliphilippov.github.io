to begin

verb
start to speak or say
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn / (BrE)
to start to say something, especially used to introduce speech or a statement

Examples

begin a speech
'Let me explain,' he began in a calm voice.
She paused a moment, then began, 'Thank you all for coming.'
The speaker began by greeting the audience warmly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – finish: to bring something to an end; the opposite of starting
  • synonym – start: to commence an action or activity; also used with speech
