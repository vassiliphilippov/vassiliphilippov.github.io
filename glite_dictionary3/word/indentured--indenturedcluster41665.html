indentured

adjective
bound by contract to work
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛntʃɚd / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛntʃəd / (BrE)
bound by contract, especially to work for another for a specified period

Examples

indentured servant
For seven years, he worked as an indentured servant on a farm in Virginia.
Many immigrants came to the colonies as indentured workers, hoping for a better life after their service.
The indentured laborers signed contracts that restricted their freedom of movement.
