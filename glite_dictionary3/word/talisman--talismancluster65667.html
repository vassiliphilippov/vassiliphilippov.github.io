talisman

noun
object believed to provide magical protection or luck
Pronunciation: / ˈtæl.ɪz.mən / (AmE), / ˈtæl.ɪz.mən / (BrE)
an object, typically a piece of jewelry, believed to have magical powers and to bring good luck or protection to its owner

Examples

lucky talisman
He kept the talisman in his pocket as a source of comfort during difficult times.
Many cultures believe that carrying a talisman can ward off evil spirits.
She wore a talisman around her neck to keep bad luck away.
