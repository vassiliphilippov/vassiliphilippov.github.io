disciplinarian

noun
person enforcing strict discipline
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ə.pləˈnɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪ.plɪˈneə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a person who believes in or enforces strict discipline, often demanding exact conformity to rules and regulations

Examples

strict disciplinarian
As a disciplinarian, the headmaster was both respected and feared by the students.
Her father was a strict disciplinarian who believed in rigid house rules.
Some coaches are seen as tough disciplinarians, demanding effort and punctuality from their teams.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lenient: tolerant; not strict in enforcing discipline or rules
  • synonym – martinet: a strict disciplinarian, especially in the armed forces
