Saint Jude

proper noun
Apostle; patron of lost causes
Pronunciation: / seɪnt dʒuːd / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒuːd / (BrE)
One of the twelve Apostles in the New Testament, traditionally invoked in prayer for desperate or hopeless situations.

Examples

pray to Saint Jude
Many people pray to Saint Jude when they are facing seemingly impossible situations.
She lit a candle and whispered a prayer to Saint Jude for help with her financial troubles.
The church held a special service honoring Saint Jude, the patron of desperate cases.
