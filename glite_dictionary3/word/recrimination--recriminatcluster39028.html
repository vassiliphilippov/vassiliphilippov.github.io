recrimination

noun
mutual accusations; counter-accusations
Pronunciation: / rɪˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making accusations against someone who has accused you; mutual accusations exchanged between parties

Examples

bitter recrimination
After the scandal, recrimination between the two parties made cooperation impossible.
The meeting ended in a flurry of recriminations as both sides blamed each other for the failure.
Their heated argument descended into bitter recriminations rather than finding a solution.
