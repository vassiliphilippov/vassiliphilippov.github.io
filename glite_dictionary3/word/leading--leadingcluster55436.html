leading

noun
thin metal strip for spacing in printing
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
A thin strip of metal used in traditional printing to separate lines of type and determine spacing between them.

Examples

leading between lines
The printer adjusted the leading to create more space between the lines.
Too much leading can make a page look sparse and difficult to read.
Traditional letterpress printing required manual insertion of leading between each row of type.
