casework

noun
individualized social work intervention
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪs.wɝːk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs.wɜːk / (BrE)
the close study and management of an individual's or family's problems by a social worker or other professional to provide diagnosis and treatment

Examples

social worker casework
Effective casework requires building trust with the family being assisted.
She documented every stage of her casework to better track the family's progress.
The social worker specialized in casework with troubled teenagers.
