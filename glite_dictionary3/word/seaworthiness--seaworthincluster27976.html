seaworthiness

noun
fitness of a vessel to navigate the sea
Pronunciation: / ˈsiˌwɝːðɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌwɜːðɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being fit or suitable for a vessel to travel safely on the sea.

Examples

seaworthiness of a ship
Insurance will not cover damages if seaworthiness is not proven at the start of the voyage.
Questions were raised about the seaworthiness of the old fishing vessel.
The seaworthiness of the ship was confirmed after a thorough inspection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unseaworthiness: the state of not being fit to travel on the sea
