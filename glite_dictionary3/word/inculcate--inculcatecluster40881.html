to inculcate

verb
teach or instill by repetition
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌlˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌlkeɪt / (BrE)
to teach and instill an idea, attitude, or habit through persistent instruction or repetition

Examples

to inculcate good habits
Parents often inculcate good manners in their children from an early age.
Religious leaders frequently inculcate moral values through their sermons.
The teacher tried to inculcate a sense of responsibility in her students.

Related Terms

  • synonym – instill: to gradually but firmly establish an idea or attitude
