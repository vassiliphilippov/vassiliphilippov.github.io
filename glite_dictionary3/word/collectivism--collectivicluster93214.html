collectivism

noun
ownership and control by the people as a group
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
the political or social principle of collective ownership and control of the means of production and distribution, rather than by individuals.

Examples

support for collectivism
He opposed collectivism, arguing that individual rights should come before group interests.
Many political movements in the 20th century were influenced by collectivism and aimed to redistribute wealth.
Under strict collectivism, factories and farms are owned by the state rather than private individuals.
