zoonosis

noun
disease transmissible from animals to humans
Pronunciation: / zoʊəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / zəʊ.əˈnəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A disease that can be transmitted to humans from animals.

Examples

zoonosis from animals
Avian influenza is a zoonosis that poses a potential threat to public health.
Rabies is a well-known zoonosis that can be transmitted from dogs to humans.
Scientists are concerned about the emergence of new zoonoses due to increased human-wildlife interaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anthroponosis: a disease transmissible from humans to animals
