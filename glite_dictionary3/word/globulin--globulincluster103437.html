globulin

noun
a group of proteins in blood, milk, muscle, and seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɑbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of simple proteins found in blood plasma, milk, muscle, and seeds, which are soluble in salt solutions and serve various biological functions

Examples

globulin blood test
Blood tests often measure globulin levels to diagnose immune system disorders.
Some plant seeds are rich in globulin, making them a good source of protein.
There are several types of globulin present in human blood plasma, including alpha, beta, and gamma globulins.
