Aranyaka

noun
class of ancient Hindu religious treatises
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈrʌn.jə.kə / (AmE), / əˈræn.jə.kə / (BrE)
any of a class of ancient Hindu treatises, closely related to the Brahmanas, that were meant to be studied and expounded by hermits in the solitude of the forest

Examples

an Aranyaka text
Scholars often study the Aranyaka to understand early Indian spiritual practices.
The Aranyaka emphasizes meditation and contemplation in the forest, away from society.
The Aranyaka texts bridge the ritualistic Brahmanas and the philosophical Upanishads.
