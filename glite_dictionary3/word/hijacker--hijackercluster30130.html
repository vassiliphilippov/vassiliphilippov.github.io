hijacker

noun
malicious program or attacker that controls browsers
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌdʒækər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒækə / (BrE)
(in computing, cybersecurity) a program or attacker that takes control of a web browser or online session, typically to redirect traffic, display unwanted pages or adverts, or steal information.

Examples

browser hijacker program
A rogue browser extension acted as a hijacker, redirecting users to counterfeit login pages.
The company's security team removed a hijacker that was replacing search results with adverts on every machine.
After the update, she discovered the homepage changed by a hijacker and reported it to IT.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hacker: a person who uses technical skills to access systems; a hijacker can be an attacker who hijacks browsers or sessions.
  • synonym – malware: software designed to harm, exploit, or gain unauthorized access to computers; a hijacker is often a form of malware.
