ketose

noun
a sugar with a ketone group
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkiːtəʊz / (BrE)
a monosaccharide sugar that contains a ketone group as its functional group rather than an aldehyde group

Examples

ketose sugar type
Fructose is a common example of a ketose sugar found in fruit.
Some ketoses are important intermediates in metabolic pathways.
The classification of a sugar as a ketose depends on the presence of the ketone functional group.
