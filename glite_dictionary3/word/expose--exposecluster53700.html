to expose

verb
reveal falseness or pretension, often with ridicule
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to reveal information or secrets that were previously hidden or to show that something is false or not genuine

Examples

to expose a lie
Her documentary exposes the myths surrounding the popular diet fad.
The book aims to expose the so-called expert's lack of real credentials.
The journalist set out to expose the fraud behind the company's claims.
Her new book will expose several secrets about the celebrity's private life.
The investigation hopes to expose the truth behind the financial scandal.
The journalist worked for months to expose the corruption within the company.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conceal: to hide something from view or keep it secret
  • synonym – debunk: to expose the falseness or hollowness of a myth, idea, or belief
  • synonym – reveal: to make known something previously secret or unknown
  • synonym – unmask: to expose the true character or hidden facts about someone or something
