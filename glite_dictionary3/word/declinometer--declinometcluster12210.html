declinometer

noun
instrument for measuring magnetic declination
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkləˈnɑmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌdɛklɪˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring magnetic declination, especially the angle between geographic and magnetic north

Examples

A declinometer can help navigators determine the angle between true north and magnetic north.
Magnetic surveys often require a declinometer for accurate readings.
The research team used a declinometer to map changes in the Earth's magnetic field.
