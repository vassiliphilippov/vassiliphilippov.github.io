gonorrhea

noun
sexually transmitted disease
Pronunciation: / ˌɡɑːnəˈriːə / (AmE), / ˌɡɒnəˈriːə / (BrE)
a sexually transmitted disease caused by infection with the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae, typically resulting in inflammation and discharge from the genital tract

Examples

gonorrhea infection
Rates of gonorrhea have increased in several countries over the past decade.
The doctor diagnosed him with gonorrhea after reviewing his symptoms and lab results.
Untreated gonorrhea can lead to serious health complications, especially in women.
