endometrium

noun
mucous membrane lining the uterus
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈmitriəm / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈmiːtrɪəm / (BrE)
the mucous membrane lining the uterus, which thickens during the menstrual cycle and is shed during menstruation if pregnancy does not occur

Examples

thickened endometrium
A healthy endometrium is crucial for the successful implantation of a fertilized egg.
During menstruation, the endometrium is shed from the body.
The thickness of the endometrium is often measured during fertility assessments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endometrial: relating to the endometrium
  • synonym – myometrium: the muscular tissue of the uterus
