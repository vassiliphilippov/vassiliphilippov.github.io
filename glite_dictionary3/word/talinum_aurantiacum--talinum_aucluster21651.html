Talinum aurantiacum

noun
a succulent plant with edible fleshy roots
Pronunciation: / təˈlaɪnəm ɔːˌræntiˈeɪkəm / (AmE), / təˈlaɪnəm ɔːˌræn.tiˈɑː.kəm / (BrE)
A plant native to the southwestern United States, with fleshy roots, erect stems, narrow succulent leaves, and one reddish-orange flower in each upper leaf axil; known for its edible fleshy roots once cooked by Indigenous peoples.

Examples

Talinum aurantiacum edible roots
Talinum aurantiacum thrives in sandy soils across the southwestern United States.
Indigenous peoples historically cooked and ate the roots of Talinum aurantiacum.
The narrow leaves and reddish-orange flowers distinguish Talinum aurantiacum from related species.
