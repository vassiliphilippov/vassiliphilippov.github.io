fanaticism

noun
excessive zeal and intolerance
Pronunciation: / fəˈnæt.ɪˌsɪz.m̩ / (AmE), / fəˈnæt.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively enthusiastic or intolerant, especially with an inability to accept opposing views

Examples

religious fanaticism
His fanaticism made any rational discussion impossible.
Religious fanaticism has often led to conflict throughout history.
Some political groups are criticized for their fanaticism and refusal to compromise.

Related Terms

  • antonym – tolerance: willingness to accept different beliefs or opinions
  • synonym – zealotry: fanatical and uncompromising pursuit of ideals
