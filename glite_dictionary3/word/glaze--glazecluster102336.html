to glaze

verb
become glassy in appearance
Pronunciation: / ɡleɪz / (AmE), / ɡleɪz / (BrE)
to become glassy or to take on a smooth, shiny appearance, often as a result of a change in emotion, attention, or condition

Examples

eyes glaze over
After hours of studying, his eyes began to glaze with exhaustion.
Her expression would glaze whenever the conversation turned to technical topics.
The frog's skin appeared to glaze in the morning sunlight.
