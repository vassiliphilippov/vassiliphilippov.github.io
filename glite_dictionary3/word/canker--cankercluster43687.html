canker

noun
plant fungal disease causing bark damage
Pronunciation: / ˈkæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈkæŋkə / (BrE)
A fungal disease of woody plants that causes localized damage to the bark, often resulting in dead areas and potential weakening of branches or stems.

Examples

tree canker
Fruit trees are often susceptible to canker if the weather is damp and warm.
If left untreated, canker can kill entire sections of a tree.
The gardener pruned away the branches infected with canker.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blight: a plant disease caused by fungi or bacteria resulting in wilting, blight, or death
