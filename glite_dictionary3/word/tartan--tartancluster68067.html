tartan

noun
checked woolen cloth pattern, often Scottish
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːr.tən / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.tən / (BrE)
A woolen cloth woven in one of several patterns of colored checks and intersecting lines, traditionally associated with Scottish clans.

Examples

tartan pattern scarf
He bought a scarf in his family's tartan colors.
She wore a traditional Scottish dress made of tartan.
The museum displayed several kilts, each featuring a different tartan.

  • synonym – plaid: a pattern of crisscrossed horizontal and vertical bands in multiple colors
