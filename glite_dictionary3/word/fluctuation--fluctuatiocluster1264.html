fluctuation

noun
wave-like motion, especially in liquids
Pronunciation: / ˌflʌk.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌflʌk.tjʊˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a continuous motion of rising and falling or moving up and down in a wave-like manner, especially in liquids or surfaces.

Examples

fluctuation of waves
The fluctuation of the ocean made the boat rise and fall gently.
The ship was designed to withstand the fluctuation of the waves during storms.
We could see the fluctuation of the water's surface as the wind picked up.

  • synonym – undulation: a smooth, wave-like motion
