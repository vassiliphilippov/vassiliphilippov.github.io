to boomerang

verb
to return to the origin, like a boomerang
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːmɚˌræŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːməræŋ / (BrE)
(of an object or action) to return to the place or person of origin, especially unexpectedly or with unintended consequences.

Examples

to boomerang on someone
Her harsh words eventually boomeranged and hurt her own reputation.
If you spread rumors, they might boomerang back to you.
The new policy boomeranged on the government, creating more issues than it solved.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rebound: to bounce back after hitting something
  • synonym – ricochet: to rebound off a surface in a sequence
