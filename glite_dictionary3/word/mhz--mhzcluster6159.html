MHz

noun
unit of frequency (one million hertz)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡəˌhɝts / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəhɜːts / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one million hertz (cycles per second)

Examples

MHz radio frequency
The radio station broadcasts at a frequency of 100 MHz.
This processor runs at 500 MHz, making it much faster than older models.
Wi-Fi networks commonly operate in the 2400 MHz band.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – GHz: unit of frequency equal to one billion hertz
  • synonym – hertz: the unit of frequency in the International System of Units (SI), symbol Hz
  • degree lower – kHz: unit of frequency equal to one thousand hertz
