to melt

verb
become less visible or distinct
Pronunciation: / mɛlt / (AmE), / mɛlt / (BrE)
to gradually blend into or disappear in a smooth or seamless way

Examples

to melt into shadows

The colors on the horizon melted into one another at sunset.
As the night fell, the shadows melted into the darkness of the room.
The chameleon seemed to melt into the green leaves around it.
The guests tried to melt into the background to avoid attention.
