triggerfish

noun
a type of tropical marine fish
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪɡərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A deep-bodied tropical marine fish with rough skin and a large spine in the first dorsal fin that can be raised or locked in place.

Examples

colorful triggerfish
A triggerfish uses its sharp teeth to crack open sea urchins and crustaceans.
Some species of triggerfish can be aggressive when protecting their nests from intruders.
The vibrant triggerfish darted among the coral reefs, displaying its bright colors.
