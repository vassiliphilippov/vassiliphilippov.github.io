taxonomy

noun
hierarchical classification for information
Pronunciation: / tækˈsɑnəmi / (AmE), / tækˈsɒnəmi / (BrE)
a structured, usually hierarchical system for classifying and organizing items, topics, or metadata in information systems, libraries, or catalogs.

Examples

product taxonomy
The e-commerce team redesigned the product taxonomy to make items easier to find.
A clear taxonomy for the knowledge base reduced support calls and sped up customer onboarding.
Librarians used a rich taxonomy to tag articles, improving search relevance across the archive.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ontology: a formal representation of concepts and their relationships used to structure information
