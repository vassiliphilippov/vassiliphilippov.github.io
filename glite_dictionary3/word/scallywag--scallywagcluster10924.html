scallywag

noun
white Southerner supporting Reconstruction (US history)
Pronunciation: / ˈskæliˌwæɡ / (AmE), / ˈskælɪwæɡ / (BrE)
(US historical) a white Southerner who collaborated with Northern Republicans during Reconstruction, often perceived as acting out of self-interest.

Examples

scallywag politician
During Reconstruction, many Northerners distrusted the motives of every local scallywag.
History books discuss the role of scallywags in post-Civil War Southern society.
The politician was accused of being a scallywag for supporting radical reforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – carpetbagger: a Northerner who moved to the South after the Civil War for personal gain
