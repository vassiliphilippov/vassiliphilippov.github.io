resultant

adjective
due to a previous cause or event
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzʌltənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌltənt / (BrE)
occurring as a result or consequence of something; following as an effect.

Examples

resultant effect
Costs were reduced, and the resultant savings helped the company survive.
Pollution was an unfortunate resultant effect of rapid industrial expansion.
The resultant damage from the storm was much worse than expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym – causal: expressing or indicating a cause
  • synonym – consequent: following as a result or effect
