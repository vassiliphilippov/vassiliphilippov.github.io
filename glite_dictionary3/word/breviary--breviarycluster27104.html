breviary

noun
book of daily Christian prayers (esp. Catholic)
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛviˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈbriːvɪəri / (BrE)
a book containing the prayers, hymns, psalms, and readings to be recited daily by members of certain Christian religious orders, especially in the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

breviary of prayers
A worn leather breviary lay on the monk’s desk, pages marked with careful notes.
Many medieval breviaries survived as beautifully illuminated manuscripts in museums.
The priest opened his breviary and began to recite the morning prayers.
