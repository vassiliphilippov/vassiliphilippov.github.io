gastrocnemius

noun
large calf muscle in the lower leg
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæstroʊkˈniːmiəs / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrɒkˈniːmɪəs / (BrE)
the large muscle at the back of the lower leg that forms the main part of the calf and is responsible for flexing the foot downward

Examples

gastrocnemius muscle injury
Injury to the gastrocnemius may cause pain when pushing off with the foot.
Stretching the gastrocnemius can help relieve calf tightness after running.
The athlete strained his gastrocnemius while sprinting during practice.
