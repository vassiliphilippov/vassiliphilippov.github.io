upper

noun
stimulant drug increasing energy
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpər / (AmE), / ˈʌpə / (BrE)
A drug, especially a central nervous system stimulant, that increases energy and decreases appetite; often used to treat conditions such as narcolepsy or depression.

Examples

take an upper pill
After his depression diagnosis, he was prescribed an upper to help with his low energy.
Some students misuse uppers to stay awake during stressful exam periods.
The doctor warned her that taking an upper could interfere with her sleep.

Related Terms

  • antonym – downer: A drug that reduces or depresses mental or physical activity; a sedative.
  • synonym – stimulant: A substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity in the body.
