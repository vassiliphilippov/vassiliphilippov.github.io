antagonism

noun
interference with a chemical's action by another (biochemistry)
Pronunciation: / ænˈtæɡəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
(Biochemistry) A phenomenon in which the action of one chemical substance is interfered with or inhibited by another having a similar structure.

Examples

drug antagonism
In biochemistry, antagonism occurs when one substance blocks the action of another.
Researchers observed antagonism as the presence of the second compound diminished the first's physiological effect.
The antagonism between two drugs reduced the effectiveness of the treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – synergism: interaction of substances to produce a combined effect greater than the sum of their separate effects
