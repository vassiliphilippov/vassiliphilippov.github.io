cannulisation

noun
insertion of a cannula into an organ
Pronunciation: / ˌkæn.jə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.juː.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the insertion of a cannula or tube into a hollow body organ, typically to provide access or drainage

Examples

cannulisation of a vein
Repeated cannulisation may cause irritation or scarring of the vessel wall.
Skilled cannulisation is essential in many emergency medical procedures.
The cannulisation of the vein was performed to begin intravenous therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cannulation: the process of inserting a cannula, especially the spelling in American English
