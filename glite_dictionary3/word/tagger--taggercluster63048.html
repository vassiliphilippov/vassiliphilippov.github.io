tagger

noun
software that auto-assigns content tags
Pronunciation: / ˈtægər / (AmE), / ˈtægə / (BrE)
(computing) a software tool or feature that automatically assigns descriptive tags or keywords to digital content (such as photos, documents, or posts) to aid searching, filtering, or organization.

Examples

photo tagger
The photo tagger automatically assigned location and face keywords so she could find pictures quickly.
Many museums now use a tagger to add subject keywords to digital collections for public searches.
The platform's smart taggers suggested trending hashtags when I uploaded my latest blog post.

Related Terms

  • synonym – annotator: a tool or person that adds notes, labels, or metadata to data for analysis or organization
  • synonym – classifier: a program that categorizes data automatically, often using machine learning
