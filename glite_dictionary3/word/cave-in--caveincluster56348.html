cave-in

noun
sudden collapse into a hollow beneath
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪv ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪv ɪn / (BrE)
a sudden collapse of a structure or surface into a hollow space beneath it, often referring to the ground or a building giving way unexpectedly

Examples

dangerous cave-in
Heavy rain caused a cave-in along the old riverbank, swallowing several trees.
Rescuers worked through the night to clear debris left by the cave-in.
The miners were trapped underground after a cave-in blocked the main tunnel.
