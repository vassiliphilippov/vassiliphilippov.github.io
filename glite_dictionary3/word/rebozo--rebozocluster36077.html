rebozo

noun
traditional Latin-American scarf or shawl
Pronunciation: / ɾeɪˈboʊzoʊ / (AmE), / reɪˈbəʊzəʊ / (BrE)
a long woolen or linen scarf, traditionally worn by Latin-American women, which covers the head and shoulders and may also be used as a sling for carrying a baby

Examples

colorful rebozo scarf
At the festival, many women wore colorful rebozos as part of their traditional attire.
She draped her rebozo over her shoulders for warmth during the cool evening.
The mother used her rebozo as a sling to carry her baby while she worked.
