creosote

noun
oily liquid from wood tar, antiseptic
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːəˌsoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkriːəsəʊt / (BrE)
a dark, oily liquid used to preserve wood and as an antiseptic; can be made from wood tar or coal tar

Examples

creosote wood posts
Exposure to creosote fumes can be hazardous to your health.
Historically, creosote was used as an antiseptic for wounds and infections.
The fence posts were treated with creosote to prevent rot and decay.
Exposure to creosote requires safety precautions due to its toxic properties.
Railroad ties are often treated with creosote to prevent decay and insect damage.
The smell of creosote is strong and distinctive, lingering on freshly preserved wood.
