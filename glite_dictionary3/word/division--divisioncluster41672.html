division

noun
disagreement causing group split
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
Disagreement or hostility that separates members of a group or organization, often leading to a split.

Examples

division between people
Religious differences can often cause division between communities.
Their handling of the crisis led to division among senior staff members.
There was a deep division within the political party over policy changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discord: lack of agreement or harmony between people
  • antonym – unity: the state of being united or joined as a whole
