to will

verb
to decree or ordain
Pronunciation: / wɪl / (AmE), / wɪl / (BrE)
to decide, intend, or cause something to happen by using your will or authority

Examples

to will something
In ancient texts, it is written that fate wills the destinies of all men.
Some believe that God wills everything that happens in the universe.
The king willed that a new law be implemented immediately.
Despite the obstacles, she willed herself to finish the race.
He willed the door to open, but nothing happened.
They firmly willed a better future for their children.
