to call

verb
read a list of names to check attendance
Pronunciation: / kɔl /; / kɑl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
to request, order, or summon someone or something to come, to gather, or to do something, especially formally or officially

Examples

to call a meeting
At the start of each meeting, the secretary would call the roll.
Please call the list to ensure everyone has arrived.
The teacher began to call the names of the students to see who was present.
The opposition party called a vote of no confidence in the government.
The principal called an emergency meeting after the incident.
The referee called a timeout to allow the players to rest.
The chairman decided to call an emergency meeting to address the crisis.
The union leader called a strike to begin at midnight.
They called a press conference to announce the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – call the roll: to read out names on a roll to check attendance
  • antonym – cancel: to annul or call off something previously ordered
  • synonym – convene: to bring people together for a meeting
  • antonym – dismiss: to send people away from a meeting or assembly
  • synonym – summon: to order someone to be present
