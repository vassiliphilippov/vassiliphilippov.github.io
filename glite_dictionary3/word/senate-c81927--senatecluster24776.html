senate

noun
upper chamber of a legislature
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnət / (AmE), / ˈsɛnət / (BrE)
The smaller, usually upper, chamber of a legislative body, often possessing high legislative powers; especially the upper house in countries with a bicameral system.

Examples

the senate passes laws
In the United States, the Senate consists of 100 members, two from each state.
The senate passed the bill after a long and heated debate.
The ancient Roman senate wielded immense influence over the governance of the Republic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – house of representatives: lower legislative chamber in some countries, such as the US
  • synonym – parliament: national legislative body, especially in the UK
