to muck

verb
clear dirt or waste, esp. in mining
Pronunciation: / mʌk / (AmE), / mʌk / (BrE)
to remove dirt, waste, or refuse, especially from a mine or similar place

Examples

to muck a mine
After the blasting, teams entered the shaft to muck the loose rubble.
It took all morning to muck out the old coal mine.
The workers were assigned to muck the debris from the tunnel.
