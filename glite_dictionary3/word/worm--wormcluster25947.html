to worm

verb
move with twisting motions
Pronunciation: / wɜrm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
to move with twisting or wriggling motions, especially while trying to get free or make progress through a narrow space

Examples

to worm through a hole
She tried to worm out of the tight corner by twisting her body.
The child managed to worm his way through the gap in the fence.
The snake began to worm its way across the forest floor, searching for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wriggle: to twist and turn with quick movements, especially to get free
  • synonym – writhe: to twist and turn with quick wriggling movements
