double dagger

noun
typographical symbol for footnotes (‡)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˈdæɡər / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈdaɡə / (BrE)
a typographical symbol (‡) used to indicate a third cross reference or footnote in printed text, following the asterisk (*) and the dagger (†)

Examples

double dagger footnote symbol
In academic texts, the double dagger is often used after the asterisk and dagger for cross-references.
Please refer to the double dagger symbol at the bottom of the page for additional information.
The third footnote in the document was marked with a double dagger instead of a number.
