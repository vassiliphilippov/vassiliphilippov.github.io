dragon

noun
mythical fire-breathing reptilian creature
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈdraɡən / (BrE)
A large imaginary creature, typically represented as a reptilian monster with wings, claws, and the ability to breathe fire, found in the mythologies and folklore of Europe and Asia.

Examples

fire-breathing dragon monster
According to legend, the ancient dragon guarded a mountain of gold.
Children listened in awe as their grandfather described a battle with a fire-breathing dragon.
The knight set out to slay the fierce dragon that terrorized the village.
