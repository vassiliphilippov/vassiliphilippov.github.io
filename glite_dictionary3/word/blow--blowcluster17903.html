to blow

verb
expel air forcefully through the mouth
Pronunciation: / bloʊ / (AmE), / bləʊ / (BrE)
to expel air forcefully from the mouth or nose, including to play a wind instrument, cool something, or clear the nose

Examples

to blow out candles
He tried to blow out the candle from across the table.
If you blow gently into the flute, you can make a soft sound.
She told the children to blow on their food so it wouldn't burn their tongues.
As the wind blew through the pipe, it created a haunting melody.
She blew a note on her saxophone to start the performance.
The children eagerly blew on their whistles during the parade.
He had never learned to blow a saxophone properly.
She loves to blow the trumpet at school concerts.
The musician blew a few notes to warm up before the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exhale: to breathe out air from the lungs
  • antonym – inhale: to draw air into the lungs
  • synonym – play: to perform music on an instrument
  • synonym – sound: to make a sound, like an instrument or object
