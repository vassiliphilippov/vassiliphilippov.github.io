standoff

noun
a tied or undecided contest
Pronunciation: / ˈstændˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈstændɒf / (BrE)
a situation in a contest or competition where the score is tied and no winner is decided

Examples

standoff in chess
After nine rounds of chess, the tournament finished in a standoff between the top two players.
The debate resulted in a standoff, as neither side could convince the other of their argument.
The match ended in a tense standoff, with both teams unable to score in extra time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – draw: a contest that ends with neither side winning
  • synonym – tie: a result in which two or more competitors finish with the same score
  • antonym – win: to achieve victory in a contest
