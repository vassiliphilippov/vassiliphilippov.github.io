to overprice

verb
to price too high
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpraɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpraɪs / (BrE)
to set the price of something higher than it is worth or higher than is reasonable

Examples

to overprice a product
If you overprice your house, it might stay on the market for months.
Many people avoid that shop because they always overprice their goods.
The company was accused of having overpriced its new smartphone compared to competitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overcharge: to charge someone too much for something
  • antonym – underprice: to price something too low
