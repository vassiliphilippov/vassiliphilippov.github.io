swoop

noun
rapid slide in music
Pronunciation: / swup / (AmE), / swuːp / (BrE)
(music) A rapid sliding movement up or down the musical scale, typically used as an expressive effect.

Examples

a swoop on the clarinet
Her performance was marked by expressive swoops on the clarinet.
The composer's use of swoop in the melody gave the piece a lively character.
The violinist added a dramatic swoop between the two phrases.
