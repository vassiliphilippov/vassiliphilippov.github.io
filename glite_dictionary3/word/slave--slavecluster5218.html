slave

noun
person legally owned by another
Pronunciation: / sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
a person who is legally owned by another person and is forced to work for them without pay or freedom

Examples

slave owner
In the 19th century, many slaves in America sought liberation through the Underground Railroad.
She was determined to escape a life as a slave and find her freedom.
The ancient Greeks and Romans often kept slaves in their households.

Related Terms

  • antonym – master: a person who owns a slave or has control over others
  • degree lower – servant: a person employed to perform domestic duties
