intercessio

noun
Roman magistrate's right of veto
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈtʃɛsioʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtʃɛsɪəʊ / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) the right of veto possessed by certain magistrates, allowing them to prevent the actions of other magistrates or bodies.

Examples

intercessio blocks a law
An understanding of intercessio is crucial to studying the checks and balances of Roman governance.
During the Roman Republic, the tribunes exercised their intercessio to block laws they deemed unfair.
The use of intercessio was one way that the plebeians could counter the power of patrician officials.
