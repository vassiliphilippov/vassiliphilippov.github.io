seigneur

noun
man of rank; feudal lord
Pronunciation: / seɪˈnjɜr / (AmE), / sɛɪˈnjɜː / (BrE)
a man of rank or authority, especially a feudal lord in pre-revolutionary France or other French-speaking regions

Examples

powerful seigneur
During the French colonial period, each seigneur was granted a large tract of land.
The seigneur ruled over his land and collected dues from the peasantry.
The villagers paid homage to their local seigneur in the annual festival.
