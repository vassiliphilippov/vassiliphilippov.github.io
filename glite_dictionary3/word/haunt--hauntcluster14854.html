to haunt

verb
recur constantly or trouble persistently
Pronunciation: / hɑnt /; / hɔnt / (AmE), / hɔːnt / (BrE)
to keep coming into someone's mind or to cause worry or anxiety

Examples

to haunt the mind
His strange behavior began to haunt her thoughts every day.
She felt as if her mistakes would forever haunt her.
The memory of that tragic night still haunts him years later.
Many believe the old mansion is haunted by the ghost of its former owner.
The fear of failure can haunt even the most successful people.
The painful memory continued to haunt him for years after the accident.

  • synonym – plague: to trouble or annoy persistently
  • synonym – stalk: to pursue or approach stealthily or obsessively
  • synonym – torment: to cause persistent pain or anguish
