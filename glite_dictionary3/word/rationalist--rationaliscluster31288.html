rationalist

adjective
relating to rationalism (philosophy)
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəˌlɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of rationalism, especially the philosophical doctrine that reason is the chief source of knowledge

Examples

rationalist philosophy
His arguments were deeply rooted in rationalist principles, emphasizing logic over emotion.
The university offers a course in rationalist thought and its influence on modern science.
This book provides an overview of rationalist approaches to philosophical questions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – empiricist: relating to empiricism, the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience
