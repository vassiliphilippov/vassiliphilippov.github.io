severity

noun
excessive harshness or strictness
Pronunciation: / səˈvɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪˈvɛrəti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely strict, harsh, or severe, especially in attitude, judgment, or punishment

Examples

severity of punishment
Her severity as a judge made her both respected and feared in the courtroom.
Many employees complained about the severity of the company's new disciplinary policies.
The principal was known for the severity of his punishments for breaking school rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym – harshness: the quality of being extremely unkind or cruel
  • antonym – leniency: the quality of being more merciful or tolerant
