right-winger

noun
player on the right wing (sports)
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋə / (BrE)
a player who plays on the right wing of a team in sports such as football (soccer), hockey, or rugby, responsible for attacking or defending on the right flank.

Examples

football right-winger
The right-winger sprinted down the flank and delivered a perfect cross into the box.
Fans cheered when the right-winger cut inside and curled the ball into the top corner.
Our coach asked the right-winger to track back and support the full-back defensively.

Related Terms

  • antonym – left-winger: a player who operates on the left wing; positional opposite in many sports.
  • synonym – winger: a player who plays on either wing (left or right) in certain team sports.
