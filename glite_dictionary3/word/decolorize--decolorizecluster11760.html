to decolorize

verb
remove or lighten color
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkʌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌləraɪz / (BrE)
to remove the color from something; to cause to become white or lighter in color

Examples

to decolorize a solution
After the experiment, the solution began to decolorize and turn almost clear.
Sunlight can slowly decolorize old photographs displayed near windows.
The chemicals will decolorize the fabric during the cleaning process.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bleach: to make something white or lighter by removing color
  • antonym – stain: to mark or change the color of something
