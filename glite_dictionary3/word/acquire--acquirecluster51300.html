to acquire

verb
gain knowledge or skills
Pronunciation: / əˈkwaɪɚ /; / əˈkwaɪər / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to gain or come to have something (such as an object, skill, quality, or characteristic), often through effort or experience

Examples

to acquire new skills
Children acquire language naturally at a young age.
He hopes to acquire new skills during the training program.
It takes time to acquire proficiency in a foreign language.
After years of saving, she finally acquired her dream house.
It can take decades to acquire expertise in a specialized field.
The company has recently acquired several smaller businesses to expand its market reach.
Children often acquire language skills simply by being immersed in conversation.
He acquired a love for classical music after attending several concerts.
Over time, she acquired a reputation for fairness and integrity among her colleagues.
