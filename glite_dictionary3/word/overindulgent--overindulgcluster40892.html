overindulgent

adjective
excessively allowing indulgence
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (BrE)
Allowing someone, especially a child, to have or do too much of what they want; excessively indulgent.

Examples

overindulgent parent
His overindulgent behavior led to the child developing bad habits.
Some parents become overindulgent and let their children do whatever they want.
The overindulgent aunt bought her nieces expensive gifts every week.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – indulgent: willing to allow excessive leniency or generosity
  • antonym – strict: demanding that rules are closely followed, not lenient
