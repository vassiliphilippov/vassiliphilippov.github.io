float

noun
air sac in fish for buoyancy
Pronunciation: / floʊt / (AmE), / fləʊt / (BrE)
an object or device that stays on the surface of water, or a part of a fish that helps it stay up in water

Examples

fishing float
If the float is damaged, the fish may struggle to stay upright in water.
Scientists studied the float to learn how fish control their buoyancy.
Some fish adjust the amount of gas in their float to swim at different depths.
A small wooden float drifted slowly across the pond.
Children played in the pool with a bright pink float.
The fisherman attached a float to his line to help him see when he had a bite.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buoy: an anchored floating object as a navigation marker
  • antonym – sink: to go below the surface of water and not float
  • synonym – swim bladder: an internal gas-filled organ in fish that controls buoyancy
