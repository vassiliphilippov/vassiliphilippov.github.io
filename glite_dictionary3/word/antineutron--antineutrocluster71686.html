antineutron

noun
antiparticle of a neutron
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈnuː.trɑn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈnjuː.trɒn / (BrE)
the antiparticle of a neutron, having the same mass as a neutron but opposite baryon number and magnetic moment

Examples

detect an antineutron particle
Physicists have detected the presence of a single antineutron in a particle accelerator experiment.
The antineutron has the same mass as a neutron but an opposite magnetic moment.
When a antineutron collides with a neutron, they annihilate each other.
