to occupy

verb
to live in (a place)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ /; / ˈɑː.kjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪ /; / ˈɒ.kjʊˌpaɪ /; / ˈɒkjʊpaɪ / (BrE)
to be in, live in, or fill a place or space; to take up space or time

Examples

occupy an apartment
After the renovation, the family will occupy the newly expanded apartment.
Several graduate students occupy the upper floor of the old house.
She was delighted to occupy a small cottage by the sea for the summer.
Her collection of books occupies all the available shelves in the room.
The city completely occupies the island, leaving no space for forests.
Water occupies every part of the valley during the rainy season.
The exhibition occupies the entire first floor of the museum.
The renovation work occupied nearly two months of our time.
This project will occupy most of my weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym – consume: use up (time or space)
  • synonym – fill: to make something full; to occupy a space completely
  • synonym – inhabit: to live in a place
  • antonym – vacate: to leave a place that was previously occupied
