butyrin

noun
glyceride of butyric acid
Pronunciation: / ˈbjuːtərɪn / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtɪrɪn / (BrE)
Any of three esters formed from glycerol and butyric acid, especially the triglyceride present in butter.

Examples

butyrin in butter
Chemists study butyrin to understand the properties of dairy fats.
The main fat in butter is butyrin, which is a triglyceride of butyric acid.
When butyrin is hydrolyzed, it releases butyric acid and glycerol.
