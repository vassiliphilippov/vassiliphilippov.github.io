to absorb

verb
take in and understand information or ideas
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔrb /; / əbˈzɔrb, əbˈsɔrb / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːb /; / əbˈzɔːb, əbˈsɔːb / (BrE)
to take in or incorporate information, ideas, or influences so that they become part of you or a group

Examples

to absorb new ideas
Children absorb languages more easily when they are young.
It usually takes time to absorb new information and make it your own.
She quickly absorbed the details of the complex case.
Many traditions were absorbed into the dominant culture over time.
She quickly absorbed the new information presented in class.
The small town absorbed a wave of newcomers after the factory opened.
Some of the higher costs are absorbed into the overall budget, reducing their impact.
The new company policy will absorb all previous regulations into a single comprehensive set.
When the two departments merged, the smaller group was absorbed by the larger one.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assimilate: to integrate or absorb into a larger group or culture
  • antonym – forget: to fail to remember information or ideas
  • synonym – incorporate: to combine or include as part of something
  • synonym – integrate: to combine with another to form a whole
  • antonym – separate: to set or keep apart, not combining into a whole
  • synonym – understand: to grasp the meaning of something mentally
