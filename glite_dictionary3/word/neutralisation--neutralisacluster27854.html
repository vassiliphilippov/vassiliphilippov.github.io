neutralisation

noun
chemical reaction of acid and base to form salt and water
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːtrəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A chemical reaction in which an acid and a base react to form a salt and water, usually by combining hydrogen ions with hydroxide ions.

Examples

neutralisation of acid
Neutralisation occurs when hydrochloric acid is mixed with sodium hydroxide, resulting in salt and water.
In a titration, the endpoint indicates complete neutralisation of the acid by the base.
The process of neutralisation is important in treating acidic industrial waste before disposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neutralize: to make a substance neutral or to counteract acidity or alkalinity
