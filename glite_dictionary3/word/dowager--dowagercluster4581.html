dowager

noun
widow with inherited title or property
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊ.ədʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.ɪdʒ.ə / (BrE)
A widow, especially one who holds property or a title received from her deceased husband.

Examples

wealthy dowager
At the gala, the elderly dowager wore a string of pearls inherited from her late spouse.
Many novels feature a dowager as a formidable matriarch controlling the family's fortune.
The dowager presided over the estate with grace after her husband's passing.
