assimilator

noun
a person who absorbs knowledge or beliefs
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪməˌleɪtər / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪˌleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who learns or adopts knowledge, beliefs, or behaviors, often by absorbing them from others or from the surrounding culture

Examples

quick assimilator
As a quick assimilator, she mastered new languages with ease.
Cultural assimilators often adapt rapidly to new environments.
The education system values students who are active assimilators of information.

Related Terms

  • synonym – learner: a person who is learning a subject or skill
  • antonym – teacher: someone who teaches or imparts knowledge
