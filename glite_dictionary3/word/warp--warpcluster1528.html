to warp

verb
to bend or twist out of shape
Pronunciation: / wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
to bend or twist out of shape, especially due to heat, pressure, damp, or stress

Examples

to warp a wooden board
Don't leave your vinyl records in the sun or they might warp.
Repeated exposure to heat can warp plastic containers.
The wooden floor began to warp after the pipe burst and flooded the basement.

  • synonym – distort: to twist out of the true meaning or shape
  • antonym – straighten: to make straight or restore to original shape
