cross hair

noun
perpendicular lines for aiming in optical devices
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔs hɛr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs heə / (BrE)
either of two fine, mutually perpendicular lines in the sighting plane of an optical instrument, used for aiming or calibration

Examples

cross hair in a scope
The engineer used the cross hair for precise calibration of the instrument.
The sniper lined up the target carefully in his cross hair.
Through the microscope, she adjusted the cross hair to focus on the specimen.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reticle: a grid or pattern, often of lines, used for sighting in optical instruments
