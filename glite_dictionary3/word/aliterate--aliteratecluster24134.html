aliterate

noun
person disinclined to read literature
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / əˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
a person who is able to read but chooses not to read, especially books or other literary works

Examples

an aliterate adult
Despite being aliterate, he excelled in other areas of learning.
Many aliterates have the ability to read but simply choose not to do so for pleasure.
The rise of visual media has contributed to the growth of the aliterate population.
