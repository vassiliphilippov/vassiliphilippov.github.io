interstate

adjective
between or involving different states
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsteɪt / (BrE)
Involving or relating to mutual relations between states, especially within a single country such as the United States.

Examples

interstate trade agreement
The interstate agreement improved cooperation between state governments.
The interstate highway system transformed long-distance travel across the country.
There are new regulations governing interstate trade within the United States.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intrastate: existing or occurring within a single state
