to disassociate

verb
stop being associated with someone or something
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈsoʊsi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈsəʊsieɪt / (BrE)
to break or end an association or connection with a person, group, or idea; to separate oneself from something

Examples

to disassociate oneself from group
After the scandal, she decided to disassociate herself from the political party.
He tried to disassociate his personal opinions from his professional role.
The company made efforts to disassociate its brand from the controversial campaign.
