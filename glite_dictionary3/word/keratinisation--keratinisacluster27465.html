keratinisation

noun
formation of keratin in cells
Pronunciation: / kəˌrætəˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kɪəˌrætɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which keratin is formed and deposited in cells, causing them to become tough and horny, as seen in nails, hair, and the outer layer of skin

Examples

keratinisation of skin cells
Abnormal keratinisation can lead to various skin disorders such as psoriasis.
Researchers studied the keratinisation of epithelial tissues in mammals.
The process of keratinisation is essential for the formation of strong, protective nails.
