to anathematize

verb
to curse or condemn as evil
Pronunciation: / əˈnæθəməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæθəmətaɪz / (BrE)
to formally curse or condemn as evil, often with a threat of divine punishment

Examples

to anathematize evil acts
In his speech, the preacher anathematized those who would lead others astray.
Some critics anathematize modern art for abandoning traditional values.
The church leaders chose to anathematize the heretical teachings during the council.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bless: to invoke or confer divine favor upon; opposite of curse
  • synonym – excommunicate: to officially exclude from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church
