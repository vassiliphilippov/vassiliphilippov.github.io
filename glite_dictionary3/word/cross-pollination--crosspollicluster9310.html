cross-pollination

noun
mixing of diverse ideas or influences
Pronunciation: / krɔs ˌpɑləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˌpɒlɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of introducing ideas or influences from different fields, people, or sources in order to create something new or foster innovation.

Examples

cross-pollination of ideas
Artists benefit greatly from the cross-pollination that happens when different creative circles interact.
The cross-pollination of ideas from different disciplines led to a groundbreaking invention.
The company's success comes from the cross-pollination of talent from various cultural backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fertilization: the action of fertilizing an egg or plant
  • antonym – inbreeding: reproduction from closely related individuals, especially in plants or animals
