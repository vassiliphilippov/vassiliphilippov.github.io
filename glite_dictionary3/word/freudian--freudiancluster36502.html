Freudian

noun
follower of Freud's theories
Pronunciation: / frɔɪˈdiːən / (AmE), / frɔɪˈdiːən / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices the theories of Sigmund Freud, especially in psychology or psychoanalysis

Examples

a Freudian psychologist
As a committed Freudian, she interpreted the patient's dreams for hidden meanings.
Many modern therapists are influenced by Freudian thought, even if they don't follow it strictly.
The debate between the Freudian and behaviorist approaches continues in psychology.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Jungian: a follower of Carl Jung's theories
  • synonym – psychoanalyst: a therapist trained in Freudian psychoanalysis
