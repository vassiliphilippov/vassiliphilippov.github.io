evasive

adjective
deliberately vague or indirect
Pronunciation: / ɪˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
tending to avoid giving direct or clear answers, often so as to hide the truth or avoid commitment

Examples

evasive answer
Her evasive remarks left everyone unsure about her true intentions.
Rather than admitting his mistake, he became increasingly evasive during the meeting.
When asked about the missing funds, the politician gave an evasive response that failed to answer the main question.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ambiguous: open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • antonym – direct: saying things in a clear and straightforward way
