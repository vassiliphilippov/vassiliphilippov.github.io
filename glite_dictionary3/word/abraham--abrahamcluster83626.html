Abraham

proper noun
first patriarch in the Hebrew Bible and religious figure
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.brəˌhæm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.brəˌham / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the first patriarch of the Hebrews, regarded as the father of the Jewish, Christian, and Islamic peoples through his son Isaac.

Examples

the story of Abraham
Many scholars study the story of Abraham to understand the origins of three major religions.
Muslims, Christians, and Jews all regard Abraham as a key ancestral figure.
The faith of Abraham is often cited as a model of obedience and trust in God.
