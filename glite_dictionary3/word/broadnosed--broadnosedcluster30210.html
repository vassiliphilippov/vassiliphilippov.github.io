broadnosed

adjective
having a wide nose
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔdˌnoʊzd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌnəʊzd / (BrE)
Having a broad or wide nose; used especially to describe certain animals, such as New World monkeys, or people with a wide nose.

Examples

broadnosed monkey
He was recognized by his broadnosed features and friendly smile.
Many broadnosed bats inhabit the forests of the Amazon basin.
The broadnosed monkeys of South America are easily distinguished by their widely spaced nostrils.

Related Terms

  • antonym – narrow-nosed: having a narrow nose; especially relating to Old World monkeys with close-set nostrils
