bootleg

noun
illegally-made whiskey or liquor
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɛg / (AmE), / ˈbuːt.lɛg / (BrE)
Whiskey or other alcoholic liquor made and distributed illegally, especially during Prohibition in the United States.

Examples

bootleg whiskey
Authorities confiscated several barrels of bootleg hidden in the barn.
During Prohibition, people risked arrest to buy bootleg from hidden speakeasies.
The bootleg he offered tasted surprisingly smooth for illegal whiskey.

Related Terms

  • antonym – legal alcohol: alcoholic beverage produced and sold legally
  • synonym – moonshine: illegally made distilled alcohol, especially corn whiskey
