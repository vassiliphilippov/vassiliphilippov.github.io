contract

noun
legally binding agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑntrækt / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
a written or spoken agreement between two or more parties that is legally enforceable

Examples

sign a contract
If you break the contract, you could face legal consequences.
The company signed a contract with its new supplier last week.
They negotiated the terms of the contract for several weeks before agreeing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – agreement: a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement
  • antonym – disagreement: lack of consensus or harmony
  • synonym – treaty: a formal agreement between states
