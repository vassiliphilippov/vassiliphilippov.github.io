decay

noun
gradual decrease (e.g. of charge)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
a gradual decrease in the magnitude of something, especially stored charge or current, over time

Examples

charge decay
In electronic circuits, decay of charge can affect overall performance.
Scientists measured the decay of the current over several milliseconds.
The electrical signal showed a rapid decay after the power was switched off.

  • antonym – increase: the process of becoming larger or greater
