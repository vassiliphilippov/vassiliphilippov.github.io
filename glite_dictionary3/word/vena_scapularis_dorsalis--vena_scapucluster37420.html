vena scapularis dorsalis

noun
a vein near the scapula draining into major neck veins
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.nə skæˌpjəˈlɛər.ɪs dɔrˈsæl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə skæp.jʊˈleə.rɪs dɔːˈsæl.ɪs / (BrE)
A vein that accompanies the descending scapular artery and is a tributary of the subclavian or external jugular vein.

Examples

vena scapularis dorsalis injury
An injury to the vena scapularis dorsalis can lead to excessive bleeding in the shoulder region.
During the surgery, the vena scapularis dorsalis was carefully preserved by the physician.
The vena scapularis dorsalis drains blood from the muscles around the scapula.
