planaria

noun
freshwater flatworm able to regenerate
Pronunciation: / pləˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / pləˈneəriə / (BrE)
a small, free-swimming freshwater flatworm noted for its remarkable ability to regenerate lost body parts, commonly used in laboratory research

Examples

planaria regeneration experiment
In biology class, we observed how planaria regrow their tails after being cut.
Many scientists study planaria to understand the process of regeneration.
Some planaria can even develop two heads if they are sliced in half lengthwise.
