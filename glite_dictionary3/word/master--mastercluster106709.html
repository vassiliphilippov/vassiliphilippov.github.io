master

noun
person with authority or control
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstər /; / ˈmæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə /; / ˈmɑː.stə / (BrE)
a person who has authority or control over others; a leader or commander

Examples

master of servants
He was the undisputed master of the household, making all of the important decisions.
The sailors obeyed their master without hesitation.
Under the old system, each apprentice answered directly to the master.
As ship's master, she was responsible for the crew and passengers.
The master assigned new tasks to each apprentice at the beginning of the day.
The master ensured that the project was completed according to his exacting standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ruler: a person who governs or exercises authority over an area or people
  • antonym – servant: one who is employed to perform duties for others, especially in a household
  • antonym – subordinate: a person under the authority of another
  • synonym – supervisor: a person who oversees others' work
