ornate

adjective
elaborately decorated in style or language
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈneɪt / (AmE), / ɔːˈneɪt / (BrE)
(of language, writing, or style) using unusual words and complex constructions; elaborately or excessively decorated, especially in literary or rhetorical style.

Examples

ornate language
His speech was so ornate that it was difficult for the audience to follow the main point.
The author's ornate prose was filled with elaborate metaphors and sophisticated vocabulary.
Victorian writing is often criticized for its ornate and excessive style.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flowery: (of language) full of elaborate literary or rhetorical effects
  • antonym – simple: (of language or style) plain, not elaborate or ornate
