to featherbed

verb
hire more workers than necessary
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛðɚˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəbɛd / (BrE)
To provide more jobs than are necessary, often to comply with union demands or prevent unemployment.

Examples

to featherbed workers
Management resisted pressure to featherbed despite demands from labor unions.
Some critics argue that featherbedding increases operational costs without improving productivity.
The company was accused of featherbedding to keep union members employed.

  • antonym – understaff: to employ too few staff
