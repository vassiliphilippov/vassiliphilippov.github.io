lachrymator

noun
tear-producing gas or chemical
Pronunciation: / ˈlækrɪˌmeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlæk.rɪ.meɪ.tə / (BrE)
A chemical substance, especially a gas, that causes the eyes to produce tears without causing permanent damage, often used for crowd control.

Examples

lachrymator gas release
Exposure to the lachrymator caused everyone's eyes to water uncontrollably.
Police deployed a lachrymator to disperse the demonstrators in the square.
The military uses a lachrymator as a non-lethal crowd control agent.
