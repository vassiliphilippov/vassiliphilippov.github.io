weeder

noun
person who removes weeds
Pronunciation: / ˈwidər / (AmE), / ˈwiːdə / (BrE)
a person, especially a farmhand, whose job is to remove weeds from fields, gardens, or other areas

Examples

field weeder
Modern technology has made the traditional role of the weeder less common.
She was hired as a weeder during the summer at the local farm.
The weeder spent hours clearing the vegetable patch of invasive plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym – farmhand: a worker employed to do agricultural work on a farm
