to interest

verb
attract or engage someone's curiosity
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
to attract the attention or curiosity of someone; to cause someone to want to know more about something

Examples

to interest a child
He tried to interest his children in classical music at an early age.
The new art exhibit really interested her, so she decided to visit the museum.
The science program is designed to interest young students in environmental issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bore: to make someone feel uninterested or weary
  • synonym – fascinate: to attract and hold the attention of someone intensely
