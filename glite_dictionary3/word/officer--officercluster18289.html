to officer

verb
to command as an officer
Pronunciation: / ˈɑfəsɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
to act as an officer by directing or commanding others, especially in a military or organizational context

Examples

to officer a team
Several senior members were brought in to officer the expedition through the desert.
She was chosen to officer the new unit because of her extensive leadership experience.
The battalion was officered by veterans with years of combat experience.
