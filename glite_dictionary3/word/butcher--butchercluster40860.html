to butcher

verb
spoil or do something very badly
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃə / (BrE)
to do something very badly, often damaging, ruining, or spoiling it through incompetence or clumsy handling.

Examples

to butcher a speech
The editor butchered the final cut, leaving out essential scenes.
A nervous speaker often butchers their lines when everyone is watching.
We warned them not to butcher the historical facts in the article.

Related Terms

  • synonym – botch: to do something badly or carelessly
  • antonym – fix: to repair or correct something that is wrong
  • synonym – mangle: to severely damage or spoil something
