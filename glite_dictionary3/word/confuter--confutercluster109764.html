confuter

noun
person who disproves an argument
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjutər / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːtə / (BrE)
A person who refutes or disproves a statement or argument by presenting contrary evidence or reasoning.

Examples

confuter of arguments
During the debate, the confuter systematically dismantled each of her opponent's claims.
Every good scientific community values a careful confuter who questions accepted ideas.
