to drydock

verb
move ship into drydock
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌdɒk / (BrE)
to move (a ship) into a drydock for repairs or maintenance.

Examples

to drydock a vessel
After several months at sea, the oil tanker was drydocked for essential repairs.
The engineers are preparing to drydock the cruise liner for maintenance over the winter.
The shipyard plans to drydock the vessel for routine inspection next week.
