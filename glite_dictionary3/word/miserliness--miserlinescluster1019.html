miserliness

noun
extreme unwillingness to spend money
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪzərlɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪːzəlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unwilling to spend money; excessive stinginess

Examples

extreme miserliness
Her miserliness was evident when she refused to donate even a small amount to charity.
People often criticized his miserliness, saying he only cared about saving money, not helping others.
The old man's miserliness prevented him from enjoying the simple pleasures of life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – generosity: the quality of being generous
  • synonym – stinginess: unwillingness to spend or give
