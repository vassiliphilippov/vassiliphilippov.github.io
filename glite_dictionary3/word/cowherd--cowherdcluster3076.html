cowherd

noun
person who looks after cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊˌhɝd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌhɜːd / (BrE)
a person whose job is to tend cattle, often on horseback, especially on a ranch or farm

Examples

cowherd on horseback
A skilled cowherd must be able to ride a horse and handle livestock.
During the festival, the local cowherds demonstrated their cattle-wrangling techniques.
The cowherd guided the cattle across the open plains at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cowboy: a person who herds and tends cattle, especially in the western US, traditionally on horseback
  • antonym – shepherd: a person who tends sheep
