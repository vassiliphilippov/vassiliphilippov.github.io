azotaemia

noun
accumulation of nitrogenous waste in blood
Pronunciation: / ˌæz.oʊˈtiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌaz.əʊˈtiː.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the accumulation of nitrogenous waste products, such as urea, in the blood, which are normally excreted by the kidneys.

Examples

chronic azotaemia diagnosis
Doctors monitor azotaemia closely in patients undergoing dialysis.
If left untreated, chronic azotaemia can progress to more severe forms of kidney disease.
The blood test revealed an elevated level of azotaemia, indicating possible kidney dysfunction.
