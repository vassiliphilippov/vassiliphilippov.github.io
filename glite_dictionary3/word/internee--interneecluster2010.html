internee

noun
person held in detention, especially in war
Pronunciation: / ɪn.tɚˈni / (AmE), / ɪn.tɜːˈniː / (BrE)
a person who is held in confinement, especially during a war, without trial or formal charges, often for political or military reasons

Examples

internee in a camp
During the war, thousands of internees were held in camps far from their homes.
Family members waited anxiously for news about the released internees.
The rights of internees during conflicts have been the subject of international debate.
