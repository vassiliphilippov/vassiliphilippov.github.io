to enshrine

verb
hold as sacred or highly respected
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈʃraɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃraɪn / (BrE)
to preserve or treat something as sacred, important, or worthy of great respect

Examples

to enshrine a tradition
Ancient traditions are enshrined in the rituals of this festival.
Her memory was enshrined in the hearts of those who loved her.
The constitution enshrines the fundamental rights of all citizens.
Her achievements are enshrined in the memories of her students.
Many rights are enshrined in the country's constitution.
The relic was enshrined in the temple for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deify: to worship or regard as a god
  • synonym – entomb: to place in a tomb, typically as a form of reverence or burial
  • antonym – profane: to treat with irreverence or disrespect
