Piedmont

proper noun
plateau region between the coastal plain and Appalachians
Pronunciation: / ˈpiˌɑdmɑnt / (AmE), / ˈpiːdmɒnt / (BrE)
a plateau region in the eastern United States, situated between the Atlantic coastal plain and the Appalachian Mountains, extending from New Jersey to Alabama

Examples

US Piedmont plateau
After leaving the Appalachian foothills, the river flows through the Piedmont before reaching the coast.
Many major cities, including Atlanta, are located within the Piedmont.
The fertile soil of the Piedmont region makes it ideal for agriculture.
