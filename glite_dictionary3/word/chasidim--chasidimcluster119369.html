Chasidim

noun
Jewish religious sect from 18th-century eastern Europe
Pronunciation: / xɑːˈsiːdɪm / (AmE), / xaˈsiːdɪm / (BrE)
A sect of Orthodox Jews originating in eastern Europe in the late 18th century, noted for strict observance of Mosaic law and distinct religious practices.
