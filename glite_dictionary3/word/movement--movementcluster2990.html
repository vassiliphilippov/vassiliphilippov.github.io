movement

noun
illusion of motion from rapid images
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
an optical illusion of motion produced when a rapid succession of still images or lights is viewed, as in film or animation

Examples

illusion of movement
Old cartoons create the illusion of movement by showing a series of drawings in rapid sequence.
Thanks to the optical trickery, we perceive continuous movement in films made from still frames.
The flashing billboard gave a convincing sense of movement to passing drivers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – motion: the process of moving or being moved, often used for physical or visual movement
