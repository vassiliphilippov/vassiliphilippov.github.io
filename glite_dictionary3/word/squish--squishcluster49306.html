to squish

verb
walk with a wet, sucking sound
Pronunciation: / skwɪʃ / (AmE), / skwɪʃ / (BrE)
to walk with a soft, wet, sucking sound, especially through mud or something similarly squelchy

Examples

to squish through mud
He could feel the mud squishing beneath his feet with every step.
My boots squished as I walked across the soggy field after the rain.
They squished through the marsh to reach the other side before dusk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – squelch: to make a soft sucking sound, as when walking through mud
  • antonym – tiptoe: to walk quietly and carefully on one's toes
  • synonym – trudge: to walk with slow, heavy steps, often through something difficult like mud
