blanket

adjective
applying broadly to all cases or members
Pronunciation: / ˈblæŋ.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈblæŋ.kɪt / (BrE)
Including or affecting all members of a group; applying broadly without exceptions.

Examples

blanket ban
Critics argue that a blanket policy fails to consider individual circumstances.
The government imposed a blanket ban on outdoor events to prevent the spread of disease.
There was blanket coverage of the incident in the media.

Related Terms

  • synonym – across-the-board: Applying to all categories or people; without exceptions
