to bracket

verb
classify or group together
Pronunciation: / ˈbræk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbræk.ɪt / (BrE)
to classify or group people or things together, especially because they have similar characteristics

Examples

to bracket people by age
In the study, participants were bracketed based on age and education.
The government bracketed families according to their income levels.
They tend to bracket all jazz musicians together, ignoring the differences between styles.
