chunking

noun
grouping information for memory
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃʌŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
(psychology) The process of grouping smaller units of information into larger, meaningful units to aid memory and understanding.

Examples

chunking information pieces
Effective chunking of tasks helps improve productivity and reduces cognitive overload.
Our brains use chunking to process phone numbers as three smaller groups rather than ten individual digits.
When studying for exams, chunking information into categories can make it easier to remember.
