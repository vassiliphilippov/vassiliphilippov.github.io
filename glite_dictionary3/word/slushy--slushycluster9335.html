slushy

adjective
like melting snow; wet and soft
Pronunciation: / ˈslʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈslʌʃi / (BrE)
Partially melted, wet, and soft, like melting snow or containing much slush.

Examples

slushy snow
He trudged through the slushy ground, his boots getting soaked with each step.
The sidewalks were covered in a thick, slushy layer after the winter storm.
The weather warmed up, turning the snow into a slushy mess.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slush: partially melted snow or ice
