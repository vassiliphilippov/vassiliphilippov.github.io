vibrato

noun
musical effect with rapid pitch variations
Pronunciation: / vɪˈbrɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / vɪˈbrɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(music) a pulsating effect in singing or playing an instrument, made by slight, rapid variations in pitch.

Examples

vibrato in singing
She practiced controlling her vibrato to achieve a more consistent sound.
The singer's use of vibrato gave the performance added emotional depth.
The violinist's delicate vibrato made the melody shimmer beautifully.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tremolo: a similar musical effect, but produced by rapid repetition of a note or change in volume, not pitch
