to abominate

verb
to intensely dislike or detest
Pronunciation: / əˈbɑːməˌneɪt /; / əˈbɑːmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈbɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to feel intense hatred or strong dislike for something; to detest or loathe

Examples

to abominate bad behavior
He openly abominated the corrupt officials who betrayed their country.
Many people abominate cruelty to animals and speak out against it.
She abominates dishonesty in any form, no matter how small.
He openly abominated the company's unethical business practices.
Many people abominate any form of animal cruelty and work to prevent it.
She abominates dishonesty in both personal and professional relationships.
