to forge

verb
move with sudden speed or energy
Pronunciation: / fɔrdʒ / (AmE), / fɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to move forward with determination or energy, especially when facing difficulties

Examples

to forge ahead
As the company faced new challenges, it forged forward with energy and determination.
She forged past the other runners just before crossing the finish line.
The cyclist suddenly forged ahead of the pack in the final lap.
Despite the storm, the hikers continued to forge ahead through the thick forest.
She decided to forge ahead with her studies, even after facing several setbacks.
The company hopes to forge ahead in the international market despite fierce competition.
