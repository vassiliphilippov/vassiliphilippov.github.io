Valhalla

noun
hall of the slain in Norse mythology
Pronunciation: / vælˈhɑlə / (AmE), / vælˈhæl.ə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the great hall of Odin, where the souls of warriors who died bravely in battle are received.

Examples

Valhalla of fallen warriors
According to legend, only the bravest warriors are welcomed into Valhalla after death.
In Norse sagas, Odin presides over the great feasts in Valhalla.
Many fantasy authors reference Valhalla as a symbol of an eternal reward for heroes.
