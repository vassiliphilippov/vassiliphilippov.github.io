kite

noun
a toy flown in the wind on a string
Pronunciation: / kaɪt / (AmE), / kaɪt / (BrE)
a light frame covered with paper, plastic, or cloth, often in various shapes, designed to be flown in the air at the end of a string as a toy

Examples

fly a kite
He made a colorful kite out of sticks and paper.
On a windy day, it's perfect to fly a kite in the park.
The children ran across the field, trying to get their kite airborne.
