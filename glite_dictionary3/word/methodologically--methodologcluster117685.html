methodologically

adverb
in a systematic or methodological manner
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛθədəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmɛθədəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on a system of methods or principles, especially in academic or scientific work

Examples

methodologically analyze data
If we analyze these cases methodologically, patterns begin to emerge.
The researchers approached the problem methodologically, breaking it down into clear, logical steps.
To ensure valid results, the experiment must be conducted methodologically.
