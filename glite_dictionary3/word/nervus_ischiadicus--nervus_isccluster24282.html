nervus ischiadicus

noun
large nerve in the thigh; sciatic nerve
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs ɪsˈkiː.aˌdɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ɪsˈkɪː.ə.dɪ.kəs / (BrE)
the large nerve arising from the sacral plexus that runs down the thigh and divides into the common peroneal and tibial nerves; also called the sciatic nerve

Examples

nervus ischiadicus injury
An injury to the nervus ischiadicus can cause severe pain and muscle weakness in the leg.
Compression of the nervus ischiadicus may lead to symptoms commonly known as sciatica.
The surgeon carefully located the nervus ischiadicus before proceeding with the operation.
