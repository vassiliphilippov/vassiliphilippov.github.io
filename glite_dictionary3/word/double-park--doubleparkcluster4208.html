to double-park

verb
park beside another parked vehicle
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəlˈpɑrk / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈpɑːk / (BrE)
to park a vehicle beside another vehicle that is already parked at the side of a road, often blocking traffic or violating parking laws

Examples

to double-park a car
She was fined for double-parking outside the grocery store.
The delivery truck double-parked and blocked all the traffic.
You can't double-park on this street during rush hour.
