Fomor

noun
Celtic mythological sea demon or giant
Pronunciation: / foʊˈmɔr / (AmE), / fəʊˈmɔː / (BrE)
(in Celtic mythology) a member of a race of sea demons or giants associated with chaos and hostile natural forces.

ancient Fomor legends
According to legend, the Fomor fought fiercely against the early settlers of Ireland.
Many stories portray the Fomor as embodiments of destructive natural forces.
The Fomor were often depicted as monstrous sea beings in Celtic tales.
