renin

noun
enzyme from kidneys affecting blood pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.nɪn / (AmE), / ˈriː.nɪn / (BrE)
a proteolytic enzyme secreted by the kidneys that catalyzes the formation of angiotensin, thereby helping regulate blood pressure

Examples

renin levels in blood
A mutation affecting renin production can lead to abnormal blood pressure.
Doctors measured the patient's renin levels to assess his blood pressure regulation.
Secretion of renin by the kidneys plays a crucial role in the body's response to low blood volume.
