to govern

verb
control by rules or regulations
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
to control or strongly influence how something happens, or to establish rules or standards for something

Examples

to govern behavior
Strict standards govern the production of organic food in this country.
The school board governs all policies regarding student behavior and dress code.
These procedures are governed by federal law to ensure fairness.
His dedication to fairness governs his decisions at work.
Principles of honesty and respect govern her daily interactions with others.
Religious beliefs often govern the conduct of communities around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym – control: to exercise restraint or direction over; dominate
  • antonym – follow: to act according to rules or advice (as an antonym in some contexts, when a person is not governed by something)
  • synonym – regulate: to control or maintain according to rules or principles
