cannulation

noun
insertion of a cannula into a body organ
Pronunciation: / ˌkænjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkanjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of inserting a cannula or tube into a hollow body organ, such as a blood vessel

Examples

cannulation of a vein
Arterial cannulation is necessary for continuous blood pressure monitoring in critical care.
Proper technique is crucial to minimize complications during cannulation.
The surgeon performed cannulation of the vein to administer fluids during the operation.
