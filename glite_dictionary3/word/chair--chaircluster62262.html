to chair

verb
preside over a meeting or event
Pronunciation: / tʃɛr / (AmE), / tʃɛː /; / tʃɛə / (BrE)
to act as the person in charge of a meeting, event, or organization; to preside over

Examples

to chair a discussion
As a senior professor, she often chairs important academic panels.
He will chair tomorrow's board meeting in the CEO's absence.
She was invited to chair the annual conference this year.
After ten years, he continues to chair the advisory board with dedication.
It is an honor to chair such a distinguished panel of experts.
She was asked to chair the committee during the annual review process.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lead: to be in charge or command of a group, organization, or activity
  • synonym – moderate: to act as the moderator or leader of a discussion or meeting
  • synonym – preside: be in charge of a formal meeting, ceremony, or event
