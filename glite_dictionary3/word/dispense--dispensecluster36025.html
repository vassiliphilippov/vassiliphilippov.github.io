to dispense

verb
to grant an exemption from a rule
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpɛns / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛns / (BrE)
to give official permission for someone not to follow a rule, law, or usual requirement; to grant an exemption or dispensation

Examples

to dispense with rules
After much deliberation, the authority dispensed him from attending the mandatory meetings.
Special circumstances may allow officials to dispense individuals from jury duty.
The bishop dispensed her from the usual fasting rules due to her illness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – enforce: to make someone obey a rule or law
  • synonym – exempt: to free someone from an obligation or rule
