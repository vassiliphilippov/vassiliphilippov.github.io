bakehouse

noun
place where baked goods are made or sold
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪk.haʊs / (BrE)
A place where baked goods such as bread, cakes, and pastries are made or sold.

Examples

local bakehouse
He works nights at the local bakehouse preparing pastries and croissants for the café.
The smell of fresh bread filled the bakehouse every morning as the ovens were fired up.
The village bakehouse is famous for its traditional sourdough loaves.
