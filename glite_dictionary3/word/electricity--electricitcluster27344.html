electricity

noun
a form of energy from moving electric charge
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti /; / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (BrE)
a form of energy produced by the movement of charged particles (such as electrons), used to power devices, machines, and lighting

Examples

electricity powers lights
During the thunderstorm, the electricity in our house went out for hours.
Many household appliances would not work without electricity.
Solar panels can generate electricity from sunlight.
During the experiment, the students observed how electricity flows through a simple circuit.
Modern households rely on electricity to operate appliances and lighting.
The power outage left the entire neighborhood without electricity for several hours.

Related Terms

  • synonym – current: flow of electric charge
  • synonym – power: energy that is used to provide light, heating, or to work machines
  • degree lower – static electricity: electric charge at rest
