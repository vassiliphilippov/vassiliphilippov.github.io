inlet

noun
a narrow arm of water leading inland
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈɪnlɛt / (BrE)
A narrow arm of the sea, lake, or river that leads inland from a larger body of water, often situated between rocky headlands.

Examples

coastal inlet
Storms often deposit driftwood along the edges of the inlet.
The boat anchored in the sheltered inlet surrounded by rocky cliffs.
They explored a narrow inlet that reached deep into the forested coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bay: a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward
  • antonym – outlet: a point where water flows out of a lake, river, or other body of water
