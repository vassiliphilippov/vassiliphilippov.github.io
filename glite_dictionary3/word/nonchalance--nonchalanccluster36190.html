nonchalance

noun
calm lack of concern
Pronunciation: / nɑnʃəˌlɑns / (AmE), / ˈnɒnʃələns / (BrE)
A calm and relaxed manner, showing a lack of anxiety, enthusiasm, or concern.

Examples

cool nonchalance
His nonchalance during the emergency frustrated everyone else.
Julia strolled into the meeting with an air of nonchalance as if nothing was urgent.
She answered the difficult question with surprising nonchalance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anxiety: a feeling of worry or unease
  • synonym – indifference: lack of interest or concern
