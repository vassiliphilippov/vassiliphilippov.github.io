polychete

noun
marine bristle worm (annelid)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.liˌkiːt / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪˌkiːt / (BrE)
a mostly marine annelid worm possessing both sexes and paired appendages (parapodia) with bristles

Examples

marine polychete worm
Marine biologists study polychetes to understand their role in aquatic ecosystems.
Several species of polychete were discovered living near hydrothermal vents.
The distinctive bristles on a polychete help it to move along the ocean floor.
