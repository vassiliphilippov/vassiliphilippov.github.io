longitudinal

adjective
running or extending lengthwise
Pronunciation: / ˌlɔndʒəˈtudənəl / (AmE), / ˌlɒndʒɪˈtjuːdɪnəl / (BrE)
running or extending lengthwise rather than across

Examples

longitudinal beam in car
The car's frame is reinforced by a longitudinal beam running down its center.
The researchers observed changes by taking longitudinal measurements over several years.
The spinal cord is protected by longitudinal grooves in the vertebrae.

Related Terms

  • antonym – transverse: running or lying across, not lengthwise
