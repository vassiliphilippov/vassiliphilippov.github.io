incriminatingly

adverb
in a manner suggesting guilt
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkrɪmɪˌneɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɪmɪˌneɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that suggests someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing.

Examples

look incriminatingly at someone
He smiled incriminatingly when asked about the missing money.
She looked incriminatingly at the pile of documents on his desk.
The witness spoke incriminatingly about the defendant's presence at the crime scene.
