postponement

noun
the action or time of delaying
Pronunciation: / poʊstˈpoʊnmənt / (AmE), / pəʊstˈpəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act or period of delaying something so that it happens later than planned

Examples

unexpected postponement
Due to technical problems, the meeting experienced yet another postponement.
The concert's postponement disappointed many eager fans.
The unexpected rain led to a postponement of the football match.
Any postponement of the court case will inconvenience the witnesses.
Her request for a postponement was granted, so the hearing will be next week.
The meeting was subject to postponement due to bad weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advancement: the act of bringing something forward to an earlier time
  • synonym – delay: the period of causing something to happen later than planned
