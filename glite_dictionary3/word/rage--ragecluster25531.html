to rage

verb
be violent; act with great intensity
Pronunciation: / reɪdʒ / (AmE), / reɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of a storm, fire, or other force) to act or continue with great violence or intensity.

Examples

storm raged outside
A blizzard raged outside, making travel impossible for hours.
As the battle raged on, the soldiers fought bravely amid chaos and noise.
The wildfire continued to rage for days, consuming thousands of acres of forest.
