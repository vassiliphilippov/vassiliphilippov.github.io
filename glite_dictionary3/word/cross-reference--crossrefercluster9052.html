cross-reference

noun
reference to another place in the same work
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔs ˌrɛf.əˌrɛns / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs ˌrɛf.ərəns / (BrE)
a note or direction in a book or other text that directs the reader to related information elsewhere in the same work

Examples

cross-reference in a book
A helpful cross-reference guided me to the correct chapter.
