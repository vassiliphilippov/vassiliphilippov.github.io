riffle

noun
a small wave or ripple on liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
A small wave or ripple on the surface of water or other liquid, often caused by a slight disturbance such as wind or a gentle current.

Examples

a gentle riffle on water
A gentle riffle disturbed the surface of the pond as the breeze picked up.
Raindrops created a riffle across the otherwise still water.
The riffle of the stream caught the sunlight and sparkled throughout the afternoon.
