to unwind

verb
reverse winding or twisting
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwʌɪnd /; / ʌnˈwaɪnd / (BrE)
to make something that is twisted, coiled, or tangled become straight or loose

Examples

to unwind a rope
Carefully, he unwound the cords tangled around the chair legs.
Could you help me unwind this spool of thread so it doesn't get knotted?
She began to unwind the long scarf from around her neck.
After hours of effort, he managed to unwind the tangled hose in the garden.
It's not easy to unwind a ball of yarn once it's gotten knotted.
She tried to unwind the knot in the necklace with gentle fingers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – untangle: to make something that is tangled become straight or untwisted
  • antonym – wind: to twist or coil something (such as thread or yarn) around a spool or object
