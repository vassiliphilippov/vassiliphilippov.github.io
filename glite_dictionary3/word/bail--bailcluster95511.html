to bail

verb
secure release by paying money (legal)
Pronunciation: / beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
to pay money or provide security to release someone from jail until their court appearance

Examples

to bail a friend
He was able to bail his friend out of jail after the hearing.
The lawyer promised to bail her client that evening if the judge agreed.
They struggled to bail their son by raising enough money for the required security.
Her family quickly raised the money to bail her out of jail.
If you can't afford to bail yourself, you may have to stay in custody until the trial.
The judge decided to bail the defendant after his lawyer arranged payment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – detain: to keep someone in custody
  • synonym – release: to set free from confinement
