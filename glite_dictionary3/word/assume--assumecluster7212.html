to assume

verb
suppose without proof
Pronunciation: / əˈsuːm / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to suppose something to be true without proof; to take something for granted

Examples

to assume the answer
Don't just assume she knows what to do—make sure to explain it clearly.
I assume you'll be joining us for dinner tonight.
People often assume things without having all the facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – doubt: to feel uncertain about; to disbelieve
  • synonym – presume: suppose something is true on the basis of probability
