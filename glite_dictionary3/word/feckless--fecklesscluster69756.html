feckless

adjective
lacking effectiveness or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛklɪs /; / ˈfɛkləs / (AmE), / ˈfɛklɪs /; / ˈfɛkləs / (BrE)
lacking determination, responsibility, or strength of character; ineffective or irresponsible

Examples

feckless young man
Her feckless attitude toward her studies led to poor grades.
The company's feckless response to the crisis caused customers to lose trust.
The project failed due to his feckless leadership and lack of direction.
Critics often describe the administration as feckless in the face of crisis.
His feckless attitude made him an unreliable partner in group projects.
The manager was frustrated with his feckless assistant, who missed every important deadline.
