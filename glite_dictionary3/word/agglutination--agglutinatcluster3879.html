agglutination

noun
clumping of particles in a liquid
Pronunciation: / əˌɡluːtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which small particles or cells clump together to form larger masses, especially in a liquid (often in biology or medicine)
