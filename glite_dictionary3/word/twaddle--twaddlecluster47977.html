to twaddle

verb
to talk trivial nonsense at length
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɒdəl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and at length about trivial or insignificant matters; to chatter or babble nonsensically

Examples

to twaddle about nothing
He tends to twaddle on about sports statistics during meetings.
Please stop twaddling; we're trying to focus on the main issue.
She twaddled for an hour about her weekend plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym – babble: to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • synonym – prattle: to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
