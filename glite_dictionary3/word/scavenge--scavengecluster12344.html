to scavenge

verb
feed on dead or discarded matter
Pronunciation: / ˈskævəndʒ / (AmE), / ˈskævɪndʒ / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) to feed on dead or decaying matter, or on discarded refuse.

Examples

to scavenge dead animals
The hyenas began to scavenge once the lions abandoned their kill.
Urban foxes often scavenge through rubbish bins for food scraps.
Vultures scavenge on carcasses left behind by predators in the savanna.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forage: searching widely for food or provisions
  • antonym – graze: to eat grass in a field (for herbivores)
