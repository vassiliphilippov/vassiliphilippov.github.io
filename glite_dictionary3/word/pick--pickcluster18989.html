pick

noun
horizontal yarn in weaving
Pronunciation: / pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
(in weaving) the yarn or thread that is woven horizontally across the warp threads to form fabric.

Examples

weaving pick thread
A broken pick can weaken the structure of woven material.
The quality of the fabric depends on the number of picks per inch.
The weaver adjusted the tension to ensure each pick was even.

Related Terms

  • antonym – warp: the set of lengthwise threads held in tension on a loom
