ectodermal

adjective
relating to the embryonic outer cell layer
Pronunciation: / ˌɛktoʊˈdɝːməl / (AmE), / ˌɛktəˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from the ectoderm, the outermost layer of cells in the embryo

Examples

ectodermal tissue layer
During development, the nervous system arises from ectodermal tissue.
Scientists studied the ectodermal origins of certain cell types in the laboratory.
Some rare genetic disorders affect the growth of ectodermal structures such as hair, skin, and nails.

Related Terms

  • antonym – endodermal: relating to the endoderm, the innermost layer of an embryo
  • antonym – mesodermal: relating to the mesoderm, the middle layer of an embryo
