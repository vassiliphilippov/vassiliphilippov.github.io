investor

noun
person who invests money for gain
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɛstər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstə / (BrE)
a person or organization that puts money into something, such as a business or property, with the expectation of making a financial gain

Examples

investor buys shares
As an experienced investor, he carefully analyzes market trends before making decisions.
Many investors are looking to diversify their portfolios this year.
The investor was pleased with the profits from her stocks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – debtor: a person who owes a sum of money
  • synonym – shareholder: a person who owns shares in a company
  • synonym – speculator: a person who invests in stocks, property, etc., hoping to make a profit quickly
