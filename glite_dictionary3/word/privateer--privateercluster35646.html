privateer

noun
privately owned warship with government authorization
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪvəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌpraɪvəˈtɪə / (BrE)
A privately owned armed ship authorized by a government to attack enemy ships during wartime.

Examples

armed privateer ship
A captured privateer was often repurposed for use in the navy.
Many fortunes were made by privateers during the 18th century.
The government issued letters of marque to turn his ship into a privateer.
