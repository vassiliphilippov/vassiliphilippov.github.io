tangible

adjective
able to be proved or shown as fact
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
(of evidence, facts, or results) able to be clearly shown, proved, or recognized; real and definite.

Examples

tangible evidence
The lawyer presented tangible evidence that convinced the jury of his client's innocence.
There was no tangible proof linking him to the crime.
We need tangible results before we invest more money in this project.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intangible: not able to be touched or easily defined; not having physical presence
