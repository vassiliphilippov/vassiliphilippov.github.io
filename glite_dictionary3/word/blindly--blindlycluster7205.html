blindly

adverb
without thought or consideration
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndli / (BrE)
Without thinking, planning, or considering the consequences; without a rational basis or due preparation.

Examples

follow blindly advice
Making decisions blindly can often lead to regrettable mistakes.
She followed his advice blindly, not questioning his motives at all.
They invested their money blindly, without researching the company first.

Related Terms

  • synonym – recklessly: without regard for danger or consequences
  • antonym – thoughtfully: with careful consideration or attention
