tone

noun
pitch quality that distinguishes words (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / toʊn / (AmE), / təʊn / (BrE)
(linguistics) a pitch or change in pitch of the voice that distinguishes words in tonal languages.

Examples

word tone change
In Vietnamese, a single syllable can have multiple meanings depending on its tone.
Learning to differentiate the four tones in Cantonese can be challenging for beginners.
Mandarin is a language in which each syllable has a specific tone that changes the meaning of a word.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intonation: the rise and fall of the voice in speaking, not lexical in most languages
