to trace

verb
read or make out with difficulty
Pronunciation: / treɪs / (AmE), / treɪs / (BrE)
to read or make out with difficulty, especially when writing is unclear or ancient

Examples

to trace old writing
It took hours for the botanist to trace the faint handwriting in the field notebook.
She carefully traced the old inscription on the monument.
The historian tried to trace the meaning of the faded manuscript.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decipher: to convert or interpret something hard to read or understand
  • synonym – interpret: to explain or understand the meaning of something, especially ambiguous
