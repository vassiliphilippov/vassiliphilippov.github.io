to inhabit

verb
to live in or occupy a place
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhabɪt /; / ɪnˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
to live in or be present in a place (used for people, animals, or things)

Examples

to inhabit a city
In the past, nomadic tribes inhabited these deserts.
Many rare species inhabit the rainforests of South America.
Polar bears inhabit the icy regions of the Arctic.
Dreams and hopes inhabit the hearts of young people everywhere.
Lively traditions inhabit every corner of the ancient town.
Sweet memories inhabit this house, making it feel alive with the past.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desert: to leave a place so that it is no longer inhabited
  • synonym – occupy: to reside in or take possession of a place
