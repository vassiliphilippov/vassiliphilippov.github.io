halide

noun
a salt formed with a halogen
Pronunciation: / ˈhælaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhælʌɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed when a halogen reacts with a metal or another element, resulting in a salt

Examples

halide compound
Chemists often study the properties of each halide to understand its reactivity.
Silver bromide is a halide commonly used in photographic film.
Table salt, or sodium chloride, is technically a halide.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – halogen: any of a group of five chemically related nonmetallic elements, including fluorine, chlorine, bromine, iodine, and astatine
