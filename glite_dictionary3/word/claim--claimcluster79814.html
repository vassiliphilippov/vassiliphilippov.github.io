to claim

verb
to cause the loss of, especially life
Pronunciation: / kleɪm / (AmE), / kleɪm / (BrE)
(of an event or circumstance) to cause the loss of something, especially life; to take as a result, often undesirably.

Examples

to claim a life
The disease claims thousands of people every year.
The storm claimed several lives along the coast.
Thousands of jobs were claimed by the economic downturn.
