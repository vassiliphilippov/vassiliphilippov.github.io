patrilineally

adverb
by descent through the male line
Pronunciation: / ˌpætrɪˈlɪniəli / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈlɪniəli / (BrE)
By descent through the male line; in a way that follows the father's lineage.

Examples

patrilineally inherit property
Certain titles are transferred patrilineally, following the male bloodline.
Inheritance in this society is passed patrilineally from father to son.
Many genealogical traditions trace family origins patrilineally rather than through the mother's side.
