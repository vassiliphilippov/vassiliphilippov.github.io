myeloblast

noun
immature bone marrow cell forming white blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.ə.loʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪ.ələʊˌblast / (BrE)
a large immature cell found in bone marrow that develops into a type of white blood cell (granulocyte)

Examples

myeloblast in bone marrow
A myeloblast can be distinguished from other cells by its large nucleus and scant cytoplasm.
In acute myeloid leukemia, the number of myeloblasts increases abnormally in the bone marrow.
The pathologist noted several myeloblasts present in the smear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erythroblast: a precursor cell to red blood cells
  • degree higher – leukocyte: a white blood cell; immune system cell in the blood
