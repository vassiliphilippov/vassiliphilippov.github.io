Cassia alata

noun
tropical shrub with medicinal uses
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsiə əˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub native to the Americas, bearing yellow flowers and large leaves whose juice is used in traditional medicine to treat ringworm and poisonous bites.
