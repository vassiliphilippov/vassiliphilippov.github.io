triune

adjective
three in one; united trio (esp. theological)
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪ.jun / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.juːn / (BrE)
(especially in Christian theology) Being three in one; having three parts or aspects that are unified in a single entity.

Examples

triune God
Some theologians have written extensively about the triune nature of the divine.
The concept of a triune being is meant to explain the relationship between the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
The doctrine of the triune God is central to most branches of Christianity.
