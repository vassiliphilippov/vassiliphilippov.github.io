majolica

noun
decorative tin-glazed pottery
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒɑlɪkə / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒlɪkə / (BrE)
a type of highly decorated earthenware pottery coated with a glaze containing tin oxide, often brightly colored and intricately painted

Examples

colorful majolica pottery
Artisans recreated traditional majolica using time-honored glazing techniques.

The museum has an impressive collection of sixteenth-century majolica plates from Italy.
