ex cathedra

adverb
with full official authority
Pronunciation: / ɛks kəˈθiːdrə / (AmE), / ɛks kəˈθiːdrə / (BrE)
with the full authority that comes from one's official position, especially used of statements made by the Pope or other high officials

Examples

ex cathedra statement
His decisions, made ex cathedra, carried the weight of unquestioned authority.
The bishop addressed the congregation ex cathedra, leaving no room for debate.
When the CEO spoke ex cathedra about company policy, everyone listened carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authoritatively: in a commanding and influential manner
