overtime

adverb
beyond the usual time
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətaɪm / (BrE)
Beyond the regular or scheduled time; longer than originally planned or intended.

Examples

stay overtime
Because the project was delayed, we had to stay overtime to finish it.
He often works overtime during busy seasons at the company.
The team played overtime to break the tie in the championship match.
