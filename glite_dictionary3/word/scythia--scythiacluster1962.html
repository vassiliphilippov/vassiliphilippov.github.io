Scythia

proper noun
ancient Eurasian region inhabited by Scythians
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪθiə / (AmE), / ˈsɪθɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region of Eurasia, inhabited by the Scythians from the 8th to 4th centuries BC, stretching from the Black Sea to the Aral Sea

Examples

ancient Scythia region
Ancient historians often described the vast steppes of Scythia as mysterious and dangerous.
The nomadic tribes of Scythia were known for their skill in horseback riding and archery.
Trade routes passed through Scythia, connecting Europe and Asia in antiquity.
