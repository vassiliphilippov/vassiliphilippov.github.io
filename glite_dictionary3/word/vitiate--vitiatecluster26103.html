to vitiate

verb
make legally invalid or ineffective
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃieɪt /; / ˈvɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to spoil, weaken, or make something less effective or invalid (such as a document, agreement, or process)

Examples

to vitiate a contract
A procedural error can vitiate the legal validity of an agreement.
