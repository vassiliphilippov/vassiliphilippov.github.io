anhydride

noun
compound formed by removal of water
Pronunciation: / ænˈhaɪˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ænˈhɪd.raɪd / (BrE)
A chemical compound formed from another compound (or compounds) by removing water, especially one derived from an acid.

Examples

anhydride chemical compound
Maleic anhydride is widely used in the manufacture of resins and coatings.
Some acids form an anhydride when heated and dehydrated.
The chemist synthesized an anhydride to study its reactivity with water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acid: a substance with a pH less than 7, often forming anhydrides
