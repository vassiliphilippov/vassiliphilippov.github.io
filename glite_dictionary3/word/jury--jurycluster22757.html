jury

noun
group that decides a court case
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɝː.i / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊə.ri / (BrE)
A group of people sworn to decide the facts and deliver a verdict in a court trial based on the evidence presented.

Examples

jury in court
It took the jury only thirty minutes to find the defendant not guilty.
The jury deliberated for several hours before reaching a verdict.
The defense attorney carefully explained the evidence to the jury during the trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym – judge: person in a court who presides and ensures law is followed
