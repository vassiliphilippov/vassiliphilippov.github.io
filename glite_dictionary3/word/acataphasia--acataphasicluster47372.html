acataphasia

noun
neurological disorder affecting speech organization
Pronunciation: / ˌækətəˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˌækətəˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A disorder in which impairment of the central nervous system causes an inability to formulate organized statements or express oneself coherently.

Examples

acataphasia after stroke
Following a severe stroke, the patient developed acataphasia and struggled to form complete sentences.
Neurologists often assess for acataphasia during cognitive examinations of brain injury patients.
The speech therapist worked with individuals affected by acataphasia to improve their communication skills.
