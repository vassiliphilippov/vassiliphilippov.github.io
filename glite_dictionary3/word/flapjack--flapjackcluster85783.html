flapjack

noun
thin cake fried on a griddle
Pronunciation: / ˈflæpˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈflæp.dʒæk / (BrE)
A flat cake made from a thin batter, fried on both sides on a griddle; also known as a pancake in North America.

Examples

flapjack with syrup
He topped his flapjack with butter and maple syrup.
She made fresh flapjacks for breakfast every Sunday morning.
The diner is famous for its enormous blueberry flapjacks.
