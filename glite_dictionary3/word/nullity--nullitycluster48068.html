nullity

noun
the state of being legally void
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈnʌl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
an act, condition, or thing that is legally void or has no validity; the state of being null, especially in legal contexts

Examples

legal nullity
Any law passed without proper authority is considered a nullity.
He argued that the proceedings amounted to a legal nullity.
The court declared the contract a nullity due to fraud.

Related Terms

  • synonym – invalidity: the state of not being legally valid
  • antonym – validity: the state of being legally valid
