classicism

noun
art/literature style inspired by Greece and Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈklasɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A style and movement in art, literature, and architecture, especially of the 17th and 18th centuries, emphasizing order, rationality, and restraint, taking inspiration from the art and culture of ancient Greece and Rome.

Examples

embrace classicism in art
His paintings rejected modern trends and instead embraced classicism as a source of inspiration.
Many poets of the Enlightenment era were influenced by classicism and modeled their works after ancient texts.
The architecture of the building reflects the principles of classicism with its symmetrical form and restrained decoration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neoclassicism: a revival of classicism in the arts, especially in the 18th century
  • antonym – romanticism: an artistic movement emphasizing emotion and individualism, reaction to classicism
