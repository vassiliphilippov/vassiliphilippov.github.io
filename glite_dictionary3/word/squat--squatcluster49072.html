to squat

verb
illegally occupy a building
Pronunciation: / skwɑt / (AmE), / skwɒt / (BrE)
to occupy a building or dwelling illegally, usually without the owner's permission

Examples

to squat in buildings
Several families squatted in the vacant apartments during the winter.
The activists decided to squat in the abandoned building to protest housing shortages.
They squatted illegally for months before the authorities intervened.
