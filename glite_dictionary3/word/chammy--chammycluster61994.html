chammy

noun
soft suede leather, originally from chamois
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæmi / (AmE), / ˈtʃæmi / (BrE)
a soft, pliable suede leather, originally made from the skin of the chamois antelope but now usually from sheepskin

Examples

soft chammy cloth
After washing the car, she dried it with a soft chammy to prevent streaks.
He wiped the window clean with a damp chammy.
The mechanic keeps a chammy in his toolbox for polishing the car.
