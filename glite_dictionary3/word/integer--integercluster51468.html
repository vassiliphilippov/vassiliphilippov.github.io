integer

noun
whole number (incl. negatives and zero)
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.t̬ɚ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
a whole number; a number without fractions, including zero, positive numbers, and negative numbers

Examples

positive integer
Every even integer is divisible by 2.
Negative numbers like -3 are also considered an integer.
Zero is the only integer that is neither positive nor negative.
