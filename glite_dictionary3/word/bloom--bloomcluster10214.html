bloom

noun
powdery deposit on a surface
Pronunciation: / bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
A thin, powdery or cloudy deposit that forms on the surface of certain materials such as varnish, chocolate, fruit, or glass.

Examples

bloom on grapes
A whitish bloom may appear on grapes as they ripen, which is a natural protective coating.
Improper storage conditions can cause a bloom to form on the surface of chocolate.
The antique glassware was covered with a faint bloom after years of disuse.
