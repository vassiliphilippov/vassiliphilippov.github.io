to hustle

verb
move busily or energetically
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌs.əl /; / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
to move or make someone move quickly and energetically, sometimes with urgency or force

Examples

to hustle down the street
After the bell rang, students began to hustle from class to class.
The nurses had to hustle to get all the patients ready on time.
We watched the cooks hustle in the busy kitchen before dinner was served.
Police hustled the protesters away from the scene to restore order.
She was hustled into the waiting car before anyone noticed her.
The security guards hustled the celebrity through the back door to avoid the crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bustle: to move in an energetic and busy manner
  • antonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary
  • synonym – rush: to move with urgent haste
