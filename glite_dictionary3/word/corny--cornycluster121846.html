corny

adjective
unoriginal and clichéd; overused
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔɹni / (AmE), / ˈkɔːni / (BrE)
trying to be significant or original in a way that is dull, sentimental, or clichéd; lacking freshness or originality

Examples

corny joke
Despite its corny message, the play was genuinely moving for some audience members.
He told a corny joke that made everyone groan rather than laugh.
The film's ending felt corny and predictable, ruining what could have been a powerful moment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hackneyed: overused and unoriginal; trite
  • antonym – original: novel and creative; not derived from something else
  • synonym – trite: lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
