tooth

noun
means of enforcement; effectiveness
Pronunciation: / tuθ / (AmE), / tuːθ / (BrE)
the power to enforce or carry out an action, rule, or law; effectiveness given by the ability to enforce or apply consequences

Examples

teeth in a law
The agreement gave the commission additional teeth to enforce its rules.
The new policy has real teeth, allowing regulators to issue fines for violations.
Without teeth, the environmental laws are just empty promises.

  • synonym – power: the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way
