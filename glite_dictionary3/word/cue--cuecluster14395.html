to cue

verb
to prompt someone with the next words (theatre, speech)
Pronunciation: / kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
to give a prompt or reminder to someone, especially an actor or speaker, by suggesting the next words when they have been forgotten or are not fully learned

Examples

to cue an actor
If you forget your lines during the play, the stage manager will cue you.
She stood just offstage, ready to cue the actors if they stumbled.
When he hesitated in the middle of his speech, his assistant quietly cued him with the next sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – prompt: to supply someone with a forgotten word or line during a performance
