scapular

noun
religious shoulder garment; part of monastic habit
Pronunciation: / ˈskæpjələr / (AmE), / ˈskæpjʊlə / (BrE)
a long, wide piece of woolen cloth with an opening for the head, worn over the shoulders as part of a monastic habit; also, a similar devotional garment in certain Christian traditions

Examples

wear a scapular garment
Pilgrims were given a scapular to wear as a sign of devotion.
She explained the significance of the brown scapular to the visitors.
The monk wore his scapular as part of his daily habit.
