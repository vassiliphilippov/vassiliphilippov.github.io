rationalisation

noun
systematic organization or planning
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃ.ə.nəl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌræʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌræʃ.ə.nəlaɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the systematic organization or reorganization of something according to a plan, logic, or scientific principles, often to increase efficiency

Examples

rationalisation of costs
Government rationalisation of public spending has affected many social programs.
The rationalisation of the library's cataloging system simplified the search process for users.
The company's rationalisation of its workflow led to greater efficiency and productivity.
After the merger, management began a programme of rationalisation to eliminate overlapping roles.
Many staff were concerned that rationalisation would result in job losses.
The rationalisation of the manufacturing process led to significant cost reductions for the company.

  • synonym – rationalization: systematic organization; American spelling of rationalisation
  • synonym – streamlining: making a business or process more efficient
