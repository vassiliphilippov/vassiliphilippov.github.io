harness

noun
straps fastening something to the body
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrnɪs /; / ˈhɑrnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːnɪs / (BrE)
a set of straps and fittings used to fasten something to a person or animal for safety or to attach it to something else

Examples

safety harness
A child safety harness can prevent falls from high chairs or strollers.
Always make sure your harness is securely fastened before climbing.
The skydiver's harness kept him safely attached to the parachute.
She cleaned the leather harness after a long day in the fields.
The farmer checked the harness before hitching the horse to the wagon.
Without a proper harness, the oxen couldn't pull the heavy load safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tackle: equipment for a specific purpose, sometimes for animals
  • synonym – yoke: a wooden crosspiece fastened over the necks of animals to pull a plow or cart
