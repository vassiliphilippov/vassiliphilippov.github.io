gorgonian

noun
type of branching coral
Pronunciation: / ɡɔrˈɡoʊniən / (AmE), / ɡɔːˈɡəʊniən / (BrE)
A type of coral characterized by a horny or calcareous, often branching, skeleton, commonly found in warm seas.

Examples

gorgonian coral reef
A gorgonian colony can provide shelter for many small marine creatures.
Divers marveled at the brilliant colors of the gorgonian corals swaying in the current.
The brittle structure of a gorgonian makes it sensitive to disturbances in its environment.
