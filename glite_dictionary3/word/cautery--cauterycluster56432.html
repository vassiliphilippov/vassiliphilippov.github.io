cautery

noun
medical destruction of tissue by burning or freezing
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːtəɹi / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəri / (BrE)
A medical procedure that involves burning, freezing, or otherwise destroying tissue to stop bleeding or remove abnormal growths.

Examples

to perform cautery treatment
Certain wounds are still treated with cautery to prevent infection.
Electrosurgical cautery is commonly employed in dermatology for minor procedures.
The surgeon used cautery to stop the bleeding during the operation.

  • synonym – cauterization: The process or act of cauterizing tissue
