echovirus

noun
group of enteroviruses causing various illnesses
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈiːkəʊˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of enteroviruses that can cause various diseases, such as viral meningitis, mild respiratory illness, and diarrhea, especially in newborns.

Examples

echovirus infection symptoms
Outbreaks of echovirus infections can occur in childcare settings during the summer.
The doctor explained that echovirus is sometimes responsible for mild respiratory illnesses in children.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of echovirus in the cerebrospinal fluid sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enterovirus: any virus of the genus Enterovirus, to which echoviruses belong
