inexpedient

adjective
not suitable or advisable
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənt /; / ˌɪnˌɛkˈspiːdiənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənt /; / ˌɪnɛkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
not suitable, advisable, or practical in a situation; unwise or not a good idea

Examples

inexpedient decision

She argued that such an action was politically inexpedient and might lead to unintended consequences.
