extrusive

adjective
formed by volcanic eruption (geology)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstruːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstruːsɪv / (BrE)
(of igneous rock) formed by the eruption of molten material through the Earth's surface, typically resulting in fine-grained rocks such as basalt.

Examples

extrusive igneous rock
Basalt is an extrusive igneous rock commonly formed from rapidly cooled lava flows.
Geologists classified the ash deposit as extrusive because it originated from a volcanic eruption.
The difference between intrusive and extrusive rocks lies in where they solidify.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intrusive: (of rock) formed from magma cooled and solidified beneath the Earth's surface
