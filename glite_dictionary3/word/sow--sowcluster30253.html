to sow

verb
plant seeds
Pronunciation: / soʊ / (AmE), / səʊ / (BrE)
to plant seeds in or on the ground so that they will grow

Examples

to sow seeds
If you sow the seeds too deeply, they may not sprout.
In spring, farmers sow wheat in their fields.
She sowed wildflowers along the border of the garden.
Farmers sow wheat in the spring to ensure a good harvest in the autumn.
If you sow the seeds too closely, the plants may not have enough space to grow.
She carefully sowed wildflower seeds along the edge of the garden path.
