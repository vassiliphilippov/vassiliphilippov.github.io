unpalatably

adverb
in an unpleasant or disagreeable way
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpælətəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈpælətəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is unpleasant or disagreeable, especially to the taste or mind.

Examples

to say unpalatably truth
He delivered the news unpalatably, without trying to soften the blow.
Some truths are unpalatably harsh, but they must be accepted.
The medicine was unpalatably bitter, forcing her to pinch her nose while swallowing it.
