reformer

noun
person seeking reform or improvement
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a person who advocates or works for change and improvement in a system, society, or institution

Examples

social reformer
Florence Nightingale was a famous reformer who transformed nursing practices.
The new education reformer argued for smaller class sizes and modern curricula.
Throughout history, every major social movement had at least one determined reformer leading the way.
