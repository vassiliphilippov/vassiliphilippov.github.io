whitecap

noun
a wave with a white, frothy crest
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌkap / (BrE)
a wave in the sea or on a lake whose crest is broken and appears white due to the wind

Examples

choppy whitecaps
Fishing boats bobbed among the whitecaps as the storm rolled in.
From the beach, we could see whitecaps stretching to the horizon.
The strong wind turned the surface of the lake into a field of whitecaps.
