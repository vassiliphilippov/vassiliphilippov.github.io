lift

noun
a wave elevating water or ground
Pronunciation: / lɪft / (AmE), / lɪft / (BrE)
A rise or elevation of the surface, such as water or ground, caused by a wave or surge.

Examples

wave lift
A strong lift from the passing wave washed over the sandbar.
The earthquake triggered a sudden lift in the ground near the fault line.
We noticed a gentle lift on the lake as the wind picked up.

Related Terms

  • antonym – subsidence: the process of sinking to a lower level; opposite of a rise
  • synonym – surge: a strong, wave-like forward movement, often of water
