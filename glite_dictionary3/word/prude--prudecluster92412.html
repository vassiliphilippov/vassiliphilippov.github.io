prude

noun
excessively modest or proper person
Pronunciation: / pruːd / (AmE), / pruːd / (BrE)
a person who is excessively proper or modest, especially in matters relating to sex or morality

Examples

old-fashioned prude
Calling someone a prude can be hurtful if they are simply reserved by nature.
He didn't want to seem like a prude, but he was uncomfortable with the raunchy jokes.
She was considered a prude by her more liberal friends because of her conservative views.

Related Terms

  • antonym – libertine: a person who behaves without moral principles or a sense of responsibility, especially in sexual matters
  • synonym – puritan: a person with strict moral or religious views, especially concerning pleasure
