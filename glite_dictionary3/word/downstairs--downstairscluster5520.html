downstairs

adverb
on or to a lower floor
Pronunciation: / ˌdaʊnˈstɛrz / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈsteəz / (BrE)
on or to a lower floor of a building, especially the one directly below the main or upper floor

Examples

go downstairs quietly
If you're looking for the kitchen, it's downstairs next to the dining room.
She hurried downstairs when she heard the doorbell.
The children are playing downstairs while the adults talk in the living room.
