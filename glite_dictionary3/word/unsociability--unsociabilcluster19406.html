unsociability

noun
tendency to avoid social interaction
Pronunciation: / ʌnˌsoʊʃiəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ʌnˌsəʊʃɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unsociable, tending to avoid companionship or social interaction

Examples

his unsociability at parties
His unsociability made it difficult for him to form lasting friendships at work.
She attributed her unsociability to shyness rather than hostility.
The unsociability of the neighbor became more apparent as he declined every invitation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sociability: the tendency to seek and enjoy companionship; friendliness
