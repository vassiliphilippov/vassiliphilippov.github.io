testator

noun
person who has made a will
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛsteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtɛsteɪtə / (BrE)
A person who has made a will or given a legacy.

Examples

testator leaves a will
If the testator changes their mind, they can always revoke or modify their will.
In order for the will to be valid, the testator must sign it in the presence of witnesses.
The testator specified in his will that his estate should be divided equally among his three children.
