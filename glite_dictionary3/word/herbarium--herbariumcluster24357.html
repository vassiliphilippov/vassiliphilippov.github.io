herbarium

noun
collection of preserved plant specimens
Pronunciation: / hɜrˈbɛriəm / (AmE), / hɜːˈbɛːrɪəm / (BrE)
A collection of preserved and systematically arranged plant specimens, usually dried and mounted, for scientific study and reference.

Examples

herbarium plant collection
Researchers visited the herbarium to compare their findings with historical plant records.
The university's herbarium houses over a million specimens collected from around the world.
Volunteers spend hours cataloging plants in the local herbarium.

Related Terms

  • synonym – botanical garden: garden dedicated to the collection, cultivation, and display of a wide range of plants
