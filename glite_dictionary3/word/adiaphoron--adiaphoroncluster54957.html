adiaphoron

noun
ritual not required or forbidden by scripture
Pronunciation: / ˌædiˈæfərɑn / (AmE), / ˌædiˈæfərɒn / (BrE)
A religious observance or practice regarded as neither commanded nor forbidden by sacred scriptures, and therefore considered optional or a matter of individual conscience.

Examples

religious adiaphoron practice
Many reformers argued that festival observance was an adiaphoron rather than a doctrinal requirement.
Some controversies in the church arise from misunderstandings about what qualifies as an adiaphoron.
The council ruled that wearing specific colors of vestments was an adiaphoron and left it to local customs.
