scantiness

noun
insufficient or revealing clothing
Pronunciation: / ˈskæn.ti.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈskæn.ti.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of a garment being very small, light, or revealing, so that it covers little of the body.

Examples

scantiness of clothing
Despite the scantiness of his costume, the performer moved confidently across the stage.
Some critics argue that the scantiness of runway fashions is growing each year.
The scantiness of her dress turned several heads at the formal event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – skimpiness: the quality of clothing providing little coverage; meagerness or lack of material
