to crystallise

verb
make definite or clear; give form
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make an idea, plan, or situation clear and definite; to become clear in your mind

Examples

to crystallise an idea
As the discussion went on, his thoughts began to crystallise.
It took her several weeks to crystallise her vision for the project.
The leader helped crystallise everyone's ideas into a clear proposal.
After the discussion, her ideas began to crystallise into a clear plan.
His explanation crystallised the complex issue for everyone present.
The meeting helped crystallise the company's goals for the next year.

  • antonym – obscure: to make something less visible or less clear
  • synonym – solidify: to make or become hard or solid; become definite
