tocopherol

noun
a fat-soluble vitamin E compound; antioxidant
Pronunciation: / toʊˈkɑːfəˌrɒl / (AmE), / təˈkɒfərəʊl / (BrE)
A fat-soluble vitamin (vitamin E) that is essential for normal reproduction and acts as a key antioxidant in the body, neutralizing free radicals.

Examples

tocopherol supplement capsule
A deficiency of tocopherol can lead to neurological problems and reproductive issues in animals.
Many skincare products contain tocopherol for its antioxidant properties.
Vitamin E is often listed as tocopherol on ingredient labels for dietary supplements.
