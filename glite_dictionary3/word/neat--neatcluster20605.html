neat

adjective
impressively clever but superficial
Pronunciation: / nit / (AmE), / niːt / (BrE)
superficially impressive or clever, but lacking in depth or attention to underlying complexities

Examples

neat solution
It's a neat solution on the surface, but it doesn't solve the underlying problem.
She presented a neat argument, yet it oversimplified the complexity of the situation.
The proposal seemed neat, but when examined closely, it failed to address the real issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – facile: appearing neat and comprehensive only by ignoring the true complexities
  • synonym – slick: attractive or impressive but often lacking in depth
