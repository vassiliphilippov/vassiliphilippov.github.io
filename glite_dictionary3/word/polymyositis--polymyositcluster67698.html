polymyositis

noun
inflammatory muscle disease
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːlimˌaɪəˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪmʌɪəˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by inflammation and weakness of multiple muscles, especially in the limbs and neck, often causing pain and swelling.

Examples

polymyositis muscle weakness
Although rare, polymyositis can severely impact a person's ability to perform daily activities.
The patient was diagnosed with polymyositis after experiencing persistent muscle weakness and pain.
Treatment for polymyositis often involves corticosteroids to reduce inflammation.
