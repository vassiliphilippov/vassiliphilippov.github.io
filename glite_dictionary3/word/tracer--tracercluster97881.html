tracer

noun
radioactive substance for medical tracing
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪsɚ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsə / (BrE)
a radioactive substance introduced into the body to study metabolic or other biological processes, especially in radiology or medical research

Examples

radioactive tracer injection
A radioactive tracer was injected to help visualize the blood flow in the patient's heart.
Doctors used a tracer to monitor the spread of the drug throughout the body.
The PET scan relies on a tracer to highlight areas of abnormal cell activity.
