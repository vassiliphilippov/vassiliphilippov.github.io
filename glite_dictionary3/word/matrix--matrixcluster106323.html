matrix

noun
substance in which tissue cells are embedded
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (BrE)
the material or substance in an organism in which tissue cells are embedded

Examples

matrix of bone tissue
Bone strength depends in large part on the composition of its matrix.
Collagen fibers are found within the matrix of connective tissue.
The extracellular matrix provides structural support to surrounding cells.
