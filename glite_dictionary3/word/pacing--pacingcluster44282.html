pacing

noun
walking back and forth with slow, steady steps
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of walking back and forth with slow, regular steps, especially because of anxiety or deep thought

Examples

nervous pacing
His pacing calmed him as he prepared for the final exam.
She was pacing the room, anxiously waiting for the phone to ring.
The constant pacing in the hallway distracted the other students.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stride: a long, decisive step or the manner of walking
