to foreshorten

verb
reduce in scope, keep essentials
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / fɔːˈʃɔːtn / (BrE)
to reduce something in length, duration, or scope while keeping its essential parts

Examples

to foreshorten a meeting
Due to time constraints, the conference had to foreshorten several scheduled presentations.
The author decided to foreshorten the novel by removing secondary storylines.
The committee voted to foreshorten the meeting but address all key issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lengthen: make something longer or more extensive
  • synonym – shorten: make something shorter or briefer
