to ret

verb
soak plant fibres to separate them
Pronunciation: / rɛt / (AmE), / rɛt / (BrE)
to soak flax, hemp, or jute in water or other liquid to separate the fibres from the woody part of the stem

Examples

to ret flax fibers
Farmers used large vats to ret their hemp before processing it into ropes.
In order to extract the fibres, workers must ret the flax for several days.
The traditional method was to ret the jute in slow-moving streams.
