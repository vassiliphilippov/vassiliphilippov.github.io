monosomy

noun
absence of one chromosome from a pair
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.nəˌsoʊ.mi / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.səʊ.mi / (BrE)
A chromosomal abnormality in which one chromosome of a pair is absent from the normal diploid number.

Examples

Certain severe forms of monosomy are not compatible with life.
Doctors can diagnose monosomy through genetic testing and analysis of chromosomes.
