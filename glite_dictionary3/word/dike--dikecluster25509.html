dike

noun
a barrier to hold back water
Pronunciation: / daɪk / (AmE), / daɪk / (BrE)
an embankment or barrier built to prevent the passage or overflow of water from a river, sea, or other body of water

Examples

dike holds water
A breach in the dike could result in severe flooding of the surrounding area.
Engineers inspected the dike after the heavy rain to ensure it had not been damaged.
The Netherlands is famous for its complex system of dikes that protect low-lying land from the sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dam: a barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level
  • synonym – embankment: a mound of earth or stone built to hold back water or support a road or railway
  • synonym – levee: an embankment built to prevent a river from overflowing
