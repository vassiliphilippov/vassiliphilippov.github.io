drift

noun
slow movement off course
Pronunciation: / drɪft / (AmE), / drɪft / (BrE)
a slow movement or force that carries something along, especially by wind or water

Examples

boat drift movement
A cross-current caused the sailboat's drift toward the rocky shore.
Due to strong winds, the plane experienced significant drift from its flight path.
The captain constantly monitored the drift of the ship to ensure it stayed on course.
Particles in the air are subject to the drift caused by the wind.
The boat was carried away by the strong drift of the river.
You could feel the drift pulling you slowly downstream as you swam.

Related Terms

  • antonym – correction: an adjustment to return to the intended course
  • synonym – current: a body of water or air moving in a definite direction, especially through a surrounding body of water or air in which there is less movement
  • synonym – deviation: departure from a set course or standard
