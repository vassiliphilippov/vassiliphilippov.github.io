microsome

noun
cellular granule for protein synthesis
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkrəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrəˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a tiny granule in the cytoplasm of a cell where protein synthesis occurs under the direction of messenger RNA (mRNA), often formed during cell fractionation

Examples

cell microsome fraction
Microsome fractions are useful in examining cellular enzyme activity.
