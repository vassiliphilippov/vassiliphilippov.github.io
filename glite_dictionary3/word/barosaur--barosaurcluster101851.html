barosaur

noun
large long-necked Jurassic dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛɹ.əˌsɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.rəˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur of the genus Barosaurus, believed to have stood as tall as a five-story building, from the late Jurassic period

Examples

giant barosaur skeleton
A barosaur could be as tall as a modern five-story building.
Scientists believe the barosaur used its long neck to reach vegetation high in trees.
The skeleton of a barosaur towers over visitors at the natural history museum.
