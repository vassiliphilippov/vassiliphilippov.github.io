lugsail

noun
type of four-cornered sail
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌɡˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈlʌɡ.seɪl / (BrE)
A four-cornered sail that is set from a yard mounted obliquely to the mast.

Examples

lugsail on a boat
She admired the unique shape of the lugsail as it caught the afternoon breeze.
The small fishing boat was rigged with a lugsail for easier handling in strong winds.
Traditional Scottish skiffs often use a lugsail as their primary sail.
