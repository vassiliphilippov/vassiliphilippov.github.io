to novate

verb
replace a legal obligation with a new one
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊveɪt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊveɪt / (BrE)
(Law) To replace an existing legal obligation with a new one, especially by substituting parties or changing terms.

Examples

to novate a contract
If both sides consent, you can novate the lease and substitute a new tenant.
The debt was novated after the merger, so the buyer took on the liability.
The parties agreed to novate the original contract, transferring the obligations to a new company.
