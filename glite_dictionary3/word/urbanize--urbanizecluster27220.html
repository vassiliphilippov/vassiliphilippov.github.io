to urbanize

verb
make more city-like; develop as urban area
Pronunciation: / ˈɝː.bə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make a place, person, or thing more like a city, either by developing it or by giving it city-like habits or ways of life

Examples

to urbanize a rural area
Government policies can urbanize remote areas by improving infrastructure and attracting businesses.
Many small towns have begun to urbanize rapidly due to economic growth.
The decision to urbanize the farmland was controversial among local residents.
Efforts to urbanize rural communities often involve social and cultural changes.
Pigeons have become highly urbanized, thriving in metropolitan areas worldwide.
Wild animals can sometimes be urbanized through ongoing exposure to city environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – civilize: to bring to a stage of social, cultural, and moral development
  • antonym – ruralize: to make more rural in character
  • synonym – urban: relating to a city or town
