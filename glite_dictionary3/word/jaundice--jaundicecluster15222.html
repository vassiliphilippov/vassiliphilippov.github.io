to jaundice

verb
to affect with jaundice or prejudice
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔndɪs /; / ˈdʒɑndɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːndɪs / (BrE)
to influence or distort someone's view or attitude with bitterness, envy, or prejudice

Examples

to jaundice a view
His opinions were jaundiced by years of unresolved conflict.
Repeated disappointments began to jaundice her perception of her colleagues.
The bitter experience had jaundiced his outlook on life.
Her recent experiences had jaundiced her views toward the project.
Long years of isolation had jaundiced his outlook on life.
We should avoid letting personal feelings jaundice our impartiality.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bias: to influence in a particular, often unfair, direction
  • synonym – prejudice: to influence (someone) with an unreasonable bias
