vocative

noun
grammatical case for addressing someone
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːkətɪv / (AmE), / ˈvɒkətɪv / (BrE)
(in some languages) the grammatical case used when addressing someone or something directly.

Examples

vocative case ending
Greek learners must learn to recognize and use the vocative correctly.
In Latin, the vocative is used when calling someone by name.
The vocative case does not exist in modern English, but is present in many other languages.
