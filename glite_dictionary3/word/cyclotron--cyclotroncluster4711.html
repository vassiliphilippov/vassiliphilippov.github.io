cyclotron

noun
a type of particle accelerator
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkləˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkləˌtrɒn / (BrE)
a machine used to accelerate charged particles to high energies by means of a constant magnetic field and a rapidly varying electric field, commonly used in physics research

Examples

powerful cyclotron machine
Ernest Lawrence is credited with inventing the cyclotron in the 1930s.
Medical isotopes are often produced using a cyclotron in hospitals.
The research laboratory installed a new cyclotron to study atomic nuclei.
