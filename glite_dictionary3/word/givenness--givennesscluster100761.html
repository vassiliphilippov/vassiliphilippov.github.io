givenness

noun
state of being assumed or taken for granted
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪvənnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvənnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being assumed, accepted, or taken for granted as a basis for argument or discussion.

Examples

givenness in context
In linguistics, givenness plays a crucial role in understanding how information is communicated in discourse.
She questioned the givenness of traditional social norms in her essay.
The givenness of certain facts forms the basis of logical reasoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assumption: something that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof
  • synonym – presupposition: something tacitly assumed beforehand; an implicit assumption
