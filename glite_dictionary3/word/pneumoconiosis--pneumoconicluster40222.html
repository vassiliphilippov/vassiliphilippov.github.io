pneumoconiosis

noun
lung disease from inhaling dust
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːmoʊˌkoʊniˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməʊˌkəʊnɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic lung disease caused by the inhalation of metallic or mineral dust, leading to inflammation or fibrosis of the lung tissue.

Examples

pneumoconiosis from dust exposure
Early detection of pneumoconiosis can improve the quality of life for affected workers.
Miners are at high risk of developing pneumoconiosis after years of exposure to coal dust.
The government introduced new safety regulations to prevent pneumoconiosis among industrial employees.
