overload

noun
excessive electrical load
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌloʊd /; / ˌoʊvərˈloʊd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvələʊd /; / ˌəʊvəˈləʊd / (BrE)
an excessive amount or load that is more than what is safe, normal, or manageable

Examples

electrical overload
An overload can trip the circuit breaker to prevent electrical fires.
If you plug too many devices into one outlet, you risk causing an overload.
The technician traced the power failure back to an overload in the main supply line.
After taking on three new projects, he was feeling the overload at work.
Mental overload can lead to increased stress and burnout.
The electrical circuit failed due to an overload of appliances being used at once.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burden: a load, typically a heavy one; something carried or endured
  • synonym – overcurrent: an excessive current in an electrical circuit
  • synonym – overwhelm: to give too much of something; to overpower or overload
  • antonym – underload: a load that is less than normal or desired
