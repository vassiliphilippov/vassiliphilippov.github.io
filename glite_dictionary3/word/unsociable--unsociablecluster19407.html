unsociable

adjective
not enjoying company; withdrawn
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsoʊʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsəʊʃəbəl / (BrE)
not enjoying or seeking the company of others; withdrawn or reserved in social situations.

Examples

unsociable person at parties
Her unsociable manner made it difficult for her to make new friends at work.
James can be quite unsociable at parties, preferring to sit quietly in a corner.
The neighbors are friendly, but this block is generally known for being unsociable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – introverted: shy, reserved, or inwardly focused
  • antonym – sociable: enjoying the company of others; friendly
