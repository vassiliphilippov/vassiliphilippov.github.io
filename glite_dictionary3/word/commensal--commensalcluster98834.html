commensal

adjective
living in a commensal relationship
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛnsəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɛnsəl / (BrE)
(of organisms) living together in a relationship in which one benefits while the other is neither helped nor harmed.

Examples

commensal bacteria in gut
Many bacteria in the human gut are considered commensal rather than harmful.
Most fungi in soil have a commensal relationship with plant roots.
The remora is a commensal fish that attaches itself to larger marine animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mutualistic: living together for mutual benefit
  • antonym – parasitic: living at the expense of another, causing harm
