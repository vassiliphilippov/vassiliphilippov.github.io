wingman

noun
supporting pilot in a flying formation
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪŋˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋmæn / (BrE)
a pilot who flies outside and behind the leader in a formation, supporting the lead aircraft, especially in military aviation

Examples

wingman in a fighter jet
Each wingman maintained his position precisely, ensuring the formation stayed tight.
In combat, a wingman provides cover and protects the lead pilot from enemy attacks.
The squadron commander relied on his trusted wingman during complex maneuvers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – copilot: pilot who assists the main pilot in operating an aircraft
  • antonym – leader: pilot flying at the front of a formation
