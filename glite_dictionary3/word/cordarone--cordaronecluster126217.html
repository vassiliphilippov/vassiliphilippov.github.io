Cordarone

noun
antiarrhythmic drug for heart rhythm disorders
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəˌrəʊn / (BrE)
A trademarked antiarrhythmic medication used to treat serious heart rhythm disorders when other treatments are ineffective, known for potentially severe side effects.

Examples

Cordarone heart treatment
After her arrhythmia was diagnosed, the doctor prescribed Cordarone as a last resort.
Because of its serious side effects, Cordarone is only used when other medications fail.
Patients on Cordarone require regular monitoring to check for potential complications.
