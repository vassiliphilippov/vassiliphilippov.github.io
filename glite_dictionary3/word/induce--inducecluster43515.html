to induce

verb
cause to occur rapidly
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdus / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to cause something to happen or arise, especially as a result of an action or situation

Examples

to induce a reaction
Exposure to the allergen can induce a severe reaction within minutes.
High stress levels sometimes induce headaches very quickly.
The doctor used medication to induce labor when the baby was overdue.
Exposure to cold temperatures can sometimes induce illness.
His harsh words could induce anxiety in some listeners.
The government's new policy may induce inflation in the coming months.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cause: to make something happen, especially something negative
  • synonym – precipitate: to cause to happen suddenly or sooner than expected
  • antonym – prevent: to stop something from happening
  • synonym – provoke: to cause a reaction or feeling to occur suddenly
