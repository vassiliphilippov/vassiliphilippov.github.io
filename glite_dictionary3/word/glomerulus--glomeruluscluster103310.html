glomerulus

noun
capillary network in the kidney
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊˈmɛrjələs / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈmɛrjʊləs / (BrE)
A small network of capillaries in the kidney, located within the Malpighian body, that acts as a filter during urine formation.

Examples

kidney glomerulus
Blood enters the glomerulus, where waste products are filtered out to begin the process of urine formation.
Damage to the glomerulus can impair the kidney's ability to filter blood effectively.
Doctors use special tests to check how well your glomerulus is working when evaluating kidney health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nephron: functional unit of the kidney containing the glomerulus
