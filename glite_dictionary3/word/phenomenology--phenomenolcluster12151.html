phenomenology

noun
philosophy of conscious experience
Pronunciation: / fəˌnɑməˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fɪˌnɒmɪˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A branch of philosophy concerned with the study of structures of human experience and consciousness, without focusing on considerations of objective reality.

Examples

study of phenomenology
Her research explores the phenomenology of memory and subjective experience.
Many psychologists have been inspired by Husserl’s phenomenology in their study of consciousness.
The philosophy department offers a seminar on phenomenology and its influence on modern thought.
