barrenness

noun
state of being unproductive
Pronunciation: / ˈbær.ə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbær.ən.nəs / (BrE)
The state of being unproductive or incapable of producing anything of value.

Examples

land barrenness
She struggled with the emotional impact of barrenness after years of trying to conceive.
The barrenness of the desert made it almost impossible for any crops to grow.
The novel highlights the barrenness of ambition without purpose or compassion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fertility: the quality of being able to produce abundantly
  • synonym – sterility: the condition of being unable to produce offspring or vegetation
