unrimed

adjective
not having rhyme (in verse)
Pronunciation: / ˈʌnˌraɪmd / (AmE), / ˈʌnˌraɪmd / (BrE)
(of verse) not having rhyme; not ending in rhyming words.

Examples

unrimed verse
Much of Milton's poetry is written in unrimed verse.
The playwright experimented with unrimed lines to achieve a more natural dialogue.
Traditional haikus are unrimed, focusing on syllable count rather than rhyme.
