to roost

verb
(of a bird) settle or rest
Pronunciation: / ɹust / (AmE), / ɹuːst / (BrE)
to rest or sleep in a place, especially used for birds; sometimes used figuratively for people or things

Examples

to roost in trees
At dusk, hundreds of crows roost in the old oak tree behind the house.
Small sparrows often roost together on telephone wires during cold nights.
We watched the flock of starlings roost on the barn roof as the sun set.
At dusk, hundreds of birds roost in the tall trees near the lake.
During the storm, the chickens roosted safely inside their coop.
Every evening, bats roost together in the old barn rafters.
