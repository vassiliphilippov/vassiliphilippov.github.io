takeover

noun
acquisition of corporate control
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the act of gaining control of a company by buying its shares or merging with it

Examples

company takeover
Share prices rose sharply following news of the planned takeover.
The board of directors approved the takeover after lengthy negotiations.
The company resisted a hostile takeover by a larger competitor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acquisition: the act of acquiring control of a company
  • antonym – divestiture: the act of selling subsidiary assets or businesses
  • synonym – merger: the combination of two companies into one
