to plane

verb
move fast across water's surface
Pronunciation: / pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
(of a boat) to move quickly across the surface of water so that much of the hull lifts clear of the water due to speed.

Examples

to plane across water
The speedboat began to plane as it picked up speed across the lake.
We watched the surfers' boards planing smoothly over the waves.
When a boat planes, it glides over the water with little resistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – skid: to slide without rotating (esp. a vehicle or boat)
