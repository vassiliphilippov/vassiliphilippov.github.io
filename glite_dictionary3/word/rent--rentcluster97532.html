to rent

verb
let property in return for payment
Pronunciation: / rɛnt / (AmE), / rɛnt / (BrE)
to pay or receive payment for the temporary use of property, land, equipment, or goods

Examples

to rent an apartment
She is planning to rent her spare room to students every summer.
The owners rented the apartment to a young couple last month.
They decided to rent their house while they traveled overseas for a year.
Many tourists rent bicycles to explore the city.
She rented a small office downtown for her new business.
We decided to rent a cabin by the lake for our summer holiday.
The company rents office space in the city center.
We decided to rent a car for our trip across the country.
You can rent movies online instead of buying them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hire: to pay money to use something or employ someone temporarily
  • synonym – lease: to let property for a specified period in return for payment
  • synonym – let: allow someone to use property in return for payment (chiefly British)
