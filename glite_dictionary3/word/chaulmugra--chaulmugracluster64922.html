chaulmugra

noun
East Indian tree yielding medicinal oil
Pronunciation: / tʃɔlˈmuɡrə / (AmE), / tʃɔːlˈmuːɡrə / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Hydnocarpus wightiana) whose oily seeds are the source of chaulmoogra oil, formerly used in the treatment of leprosy.

Examples

chaulmugra tree seeds
Botanists have long studied the properties of chaulmugra due to its medicinal uses.
Local traditions in India and Burma have included the oil from chaulmugra in their healing practices.
The seeds of the chaulmugra tree were once a primary treatment for leprosy.
