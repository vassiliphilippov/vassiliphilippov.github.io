to exposit

verb
explain in detail; expound
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɑzɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪt / (BrE)
to explain or set forth an idea or meaning in detail, especially in a formal or scholarly way

Examples

to exposit an idea
In his lengthy article, he exposited the relationship between art and society.
She was invited to exposit her views on the subject at the international conference.
The professor took time to exposit the complex philosophical theories to his students.
During the lecture, he exposited the historical background of the poem at length.
She often exposits complex theories in a way that is easy to understand.
The professor began to exposit the principles of quantum mechanics for the new students.

Related Terms

  • synonym – elucidate: to make clear; to explain
  • synonym – expound: to present and explain systematically and in detail
  • synonym – interpret: to explain the meaning of something
  • antonym – obscure: to make unclear or difficult to understand
