to glean

verb
gather leftovers from harvest
Pronunciation: / ɡlin / (AmE), / ɡliːn / (BrE)
to gather (especially grain or other produce) left by reapers in a field after the main harvest

Examples

to glean leftover grain
After the main harvest, villagers returned to the fields to glean the remaining wheat.
Many birds glean seeds from the ground after the reapers depart.
She would glean leftover grapes from the vineyards for her family.
