vapour

noun
suspension of particles in air
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpər / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə / (BrE)
A visible suspension of fine particles of liquid or solid in the air, such as mist or smoke.

Examples

morning vapour rising
In the early morning, a thin vapour rose from the surface of the lake.
The chemist demonstrated how the substance turned into vapour when heated.
You could see the vapour escaping from the kettle as it boiled.
