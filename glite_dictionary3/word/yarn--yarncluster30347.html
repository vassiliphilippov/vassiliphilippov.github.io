to yarn

verb
tell a tall or fanciful story
Pronunciation: / jɑrn / (AmE), / jɑːn / (BrE)
to tell a long or imaginative story, often one that is exaggerated or implausible

Examples

to yarn about adventures
After dinner, Grandpa loved to yarn about his adventures at sea.
She could yarn so well that everyone believed her wildest stories.
The campers sat around the fire and began to yarn late into the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – story: an account of imaginary or real people and events told for entertainment
  • synonym – tale: a narrative that relates the details of some real or imaginary event, incident, or case; story
