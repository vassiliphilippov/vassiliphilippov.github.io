to invoke

verb
cite as authority, law, or principle
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪnˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to refer to a law, authority, or principle in support of an action or argument

Examples

to invoke a law
During the debate, she invoked the Constitution to support her position.
Facing criticism, the manager invoked company policy to justify his decision.
The lawyer invoked an outdated statute to defend his client.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appeal to: to make a serious or urgent request to an authority or principle
  • synonym – cite: to refer to as evidence or authority
