fault

noun
mistake caused by poor judgment or carelessness
Pronunciation: / fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
a mistake, wrong action, or the state of being to blame for something bad

Examples

at fault for a problem
He always tries to find a fault in other people's work instead of his own.
She admitted her fault and apologized to everyone affected.
The accident was entirely my fault because I wasn’t paying attention.
Don't blame yourself for something that isn't your fault.
I know it was my fault that we missed the train.
The accident was entirely her fault, not yours.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blame: Responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • synonym – blunder: a stupid or careless mistake
  • antonym – credit: Recognition for a good act or achievement (opposite of fault in responsibility)
  • synonym – error: something done incorrectly through ignorance or carelessness
  • synonym – mistake: an action or decision that is wrong or produces a result that was not intended
