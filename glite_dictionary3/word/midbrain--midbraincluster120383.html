midbrain

noun
middle part of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌbreɪn / (BrE)
the middle part of the brain, located beneath the cerebral hemispheres and above the hindbrain, involved in visual and auditory processing, motor control, and other functions

Examples

the midbrain controls movement
Damage to the midbrain can affect balance and motor control.
Scientists studied how the midbrain processes information from our senses.
The midbrain plays an important role in regulating eye movements and auditory responses.
