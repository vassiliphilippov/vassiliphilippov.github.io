satirical

adjective
using humor to criticize or mock
Pronunciation: / səˈtɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / səˈtɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
using humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize or expose people's stupidity or vices, especially in the context of contemporary politics and other topical issues.

Examples

satirical cartoon
He uses satirical humor to highlight political corruption in his stand-up routines.
Her satirical play exposes the absurdities of contemporary society.
The newspaper published a satirical cartoon lampooning the government's new policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – earnest: serious in intention or effort, without satire or mockery
  • synonym – ironic: expressing meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite
  • synonym – sarcastic: using irony to mock or convey contempt
