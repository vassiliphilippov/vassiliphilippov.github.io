jack

noun
nautical flag showing a ship's nationality
Pronunciation: / dʒæk / (AmE), / dʒak / (BrE)
A small flag flown at the bow of a ship, typically used to indicate the vessel's nationality.

Examples

ship jack flag
A blue jack fluttered at the bow, signaling the ship's country of origin.
Every morning, the crew hoisted the jack along with the ensign.
The British warship raised its jack before sailing out of the harbor.
