Nikita Khrushchev

proper noun
Soviet leader, premier 1953–1964
Pronunciation: / nɪˈkitə kruːˈʃtʃɛf / (AmE), / nɪˈkiːtə ˈkrʊʃtʃɛf / (BrE)
(1894–1971) Soviet statesman and premier, leader of the Soviet Union from 1953 to 1964, known for denouncing Stalin and initiating de-Stalinization.

Examples

Nikita Khrushchev led USSR
Nikita Khrushchev delivered his famous secret speech denouncing Stalin's crimes in 1956.
During the Cuban Missile Crisis, Nikita Khrushchev played a crucial role in negotiating with the United States.
Many historians credit Nikita Khrushchev with beginning the process of de-Stalinization in the Soviet Union.
