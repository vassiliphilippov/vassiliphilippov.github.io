unrealism

noun
representing no concrete objects; abstract style
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a style or quality of representation that does not refer to concrete objects or specific examples; the state or quality of being unrealistic or abstract.

Examples

literary unrealism
Her story was criticized for its unrealism and lack of believable details.
Modern art often embraces unrealism to evoke emotions rather than depict the real world.
The movie's visual unrealism gave it a dreamlike atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym – realism: representation of things as they actually are
