to cantillate

verb
recite or sing with musical intonation
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.tɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to recite or sing words, especially prayers or psalms, with a musical intonation or chant

Examples

to cantillate a prayer
During the service, the priest cantillated the psalms in a melodic tone.
Students were taught how to cantillate passages from ancient scriptures.
The rabbi cantillated the blessing, filling the synagogue with solemn music.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chant: to repeat or sing a phrase continuously or rhythmically, often in a religious context
  • synonym – intonate: to utter with a particular tone or modulation of the voice
