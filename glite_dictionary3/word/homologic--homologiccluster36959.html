homologic

adjective
similar in evolutionary origin but not function
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑlədʒɪk / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒlədʒɪk / (BrE)
(of organs or body parts) having a similar evolutionary origin, even if their functions differ.

Examples

homologic body parts
Even though their functions differ, these bones are considered homologic in origin.
Scientists study homologic features to understand evolutionary relationships between species.
The wings of a bird and the forelegs of a lizard are homologic structures.

  • synonym – homologous: having the same evolutionary origin
