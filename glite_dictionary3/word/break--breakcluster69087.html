to break

verb
make opening shot in cue sports
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
(in billiards, snooker, or similar games) to make the first shot that scatters the arranged balls at the start of a game.

Examples

to break the balls
After he broke, the balls scattered perfectly across the table.
It's your turn to break, so take your shot carefully.
She always manages to break with plenty of power at the start of the game.
