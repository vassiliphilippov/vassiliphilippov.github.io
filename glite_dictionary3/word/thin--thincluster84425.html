to thin

verb
become less thick or thinner
Pronunciation: / θɪn / (AmE), / θɪn / (BrE)
to make or become less thick, less dense, or more diluted; to lose weight or become slimmer

Examples

to thin the sauce
Add more water if the sauce starts to thin too much.
The crowd began to thin as the evening wore on.
The fog will thin out by midday, making driving conditions safer.
After months of exercise and dieting, she began to thin noticeably.
He was trying to thin down before the summer holidays.
Wild animals often thin during harsh winters when food is scarce.
She thinned the paint by adding more solvent to get the right consistency.
Some people prefer to thin their coffee with milk to reduce its strength.
You can thin the sauce with a little water if it is too rich.
