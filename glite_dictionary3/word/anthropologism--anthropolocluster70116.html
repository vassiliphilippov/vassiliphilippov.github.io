anthropologism

noun
belief that culture fully shapes human nature
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəˈpɑlədʒɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌanθrəˈpɒlədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
The viewpoint that human nature is entirely shaped and determined by the surrounding culture, allowing no independent human agency.
