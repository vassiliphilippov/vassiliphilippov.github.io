chokehold

noun
complete control over something
Pronunciation: / ˈt͡ʃoʊkˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈt͡ʃəʊkˌhəʊld / (BrE)
complete control or dominance over a person, group, or situation, restricting freedom or activity

Examples

chokehold on power
His influence gave him a virtual chokehold over the city's law enforcement agencies.
Many feel that the new regulations have put a chokehold on small business innovation.
The company maintained a chokehold on the local market, blocking out any competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stranglehold: complete power and control over someone or something
