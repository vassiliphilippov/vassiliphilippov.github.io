to flex

verb
to bend or curve
Pronunciation: / flɛks / (AmE), / flɛks / (BrE)
to bend or make something bend, especially so it is no longer straight (can refer to a body part or an object)

Examples

to flex a stick
Glass can flex slightly before it shatters under stress.
The metal rod began to flex under the weight of the heavy boxes.
The tree branch will flex in strong winds but rarely snaps.
She started to flex her ankles before going for a run.
The doctor asked him to flex his elbow to test the joint's mobility.
You should flex your fingers to improve blood circulation.
Gently flex the plastic sheet so it fits around the curve.
The athlete was able to flex the steel bar with his bare hands.
You shouldn't flex the wood too much or it might snap.
