paracentesis

noun
removal of abdominal fluid
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəsɛnˈtisɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəsenˈtiːsɪs / (BrE)
A medical procedure in which fluid is removed from the abdomen using a needle or catheter, typically for diagnosis or relief of pressure.

Examples

paracentesis procedure abdomen
After the paracentesis, laboratory tests were conducted on the extracted fluid.
Repeated paracentesis may be necessary for patients with chronic ascites.
The doctor performed a paracentesis to relieve the patient's abdominal swelling.
