constriction

noun
tightness or pressure in the body
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of tightness or pressure in a part of the body, such as the chest or throat

Examples

constriction in the chest
After running up the stairs, he complained of a constriction in his chest.
Panic attacks often produce a sensation of constriction in the body.
She noticed a strange constriction in her throat when she became anxious.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tightness: the state of being tight or under tension, especially in the body
