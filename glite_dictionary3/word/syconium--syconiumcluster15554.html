syconium

noun
the fruit of the fig, a hollow structure containing many fruitlets
Pronunciation: / saɪˈkoʊniəm / (AmE), / sɪˈkəʊnɪəm / (BrE)
A fleshy, hollow, multiple fruit characteristic of figs, formed from an enlarged inflorescence enclosing numerous small fruitlets inside.

Examples

fig syconium
Each syconium contains hundreds of tiny flowers on the inside surface.
The edible portion of a fig is known botanically as a syconium.
The structure of the syconium is essential for the unique pollination process of figs.
