to grant

verb
admit or concede a point
Pronunciation: / ɡrænt / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnt / (BrE)
to admit or concede (a fact, argument, or point), often reluctantly

Examples

to grant a point
Even though I disagree with your conclusion, I grant that your logic makes sense.
I grant you that it was a difficult decision, but it was necessary.
She refused to grant him the point, insisting her answer was correct.

Related Terms

  • synonym – concede: to admit that something is true or valid; to acknowledge
  • antonym – deny: to declare untrue; to refuse to admit the truth or existence of something
