shipworm

noun
wood-boring marine mollusc
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a marine mollusc with a wormlike body that bores into wood, damaging piers and ships; a type of bivalve.

Examples

shipworm damages wood
Scientists study shipworms to understand how they digest wood in marine environments.
The old pier's beams had been severely damaged by shipworms.
Wooden ships often fell victim to shipworm infestations during long ocean voyages.

Related Terms

  • synonym – teredo: another name for the shipworm; the genus to which most shipworms belong
