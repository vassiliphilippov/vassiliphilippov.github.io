adiabatic

adjective
occurring without heat exchange
Pronunciation: / ˌædiəˈbætɪk / (AmE), / ˌædɪəˈbatɪk / (BrE)
(of a process) occurring without any loss or gain of heat with the surroundings.

Examples

adiabatic process in physics
During adiabatic expansion, the temperature of the gas drops rapidly.
Physicists often use adiabatic processes to approximate idealized thermodynamic behavior.
The engine operates under adiabatic conditions, so no heat is transferred to the surroundings.

  • antonym – isothermal: occurring at constant temperature
