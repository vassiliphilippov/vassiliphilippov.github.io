lobbyist

noun
person who seeks to influence legislators
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑː.bi.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.i.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who is employed to try to influence legislators to support legislation that benefits the employer or client

Examples

lobbyist influences politicians
New regulations require every lobbyist to register their activities with the government.
She became a lobbyist after years of working in public relations.
The lobbyist met with several senators to argue in favor of the proposed tax bill.
