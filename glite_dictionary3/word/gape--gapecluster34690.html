to gape

verb
be or become wide open
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
to be or become wide open, often in a way that is noticeable or surprising

Examples

gape wide open
A hole gaped in the roof after the storm.
The cave gaped before them, dark and mysterious.
The door began to gape after the strong wind hit it.
