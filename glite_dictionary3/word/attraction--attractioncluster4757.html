attraction

noun
force causing objects to draw together
Pronunciation: / əˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
the force that draws one object toward another, especially due to physical or natural laws, such as gravity or magnetism

Examples

gravitational attraction
Gravitational attraction keeps the planets in orbit around the sun.
Scientists studied the attraction of opposite electric charges in the experiment.
The attraction between the two magnets was strong enough to snap them together.

Related Terms

  • antonym – repulsion: the force by which objects push away from each other
