t-scope

noun
psychological instrument for brief visual stimuli
Pronunciation: / ti skoʊp / (AmE), / ti skəʊp / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used in psychology to present visual stimuli for very brief periods

Examples

t-scope in experiments
A malfunctioning t-scope can affect the results of visual perception experiments.
Many classic attention studies relied on the t-scope for accurate timing of stimuli.
