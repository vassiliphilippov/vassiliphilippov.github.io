autoradiograph

noun
photographic image from radiation emissions
Pronunciation: / ˌɔtoʊˈreɪdioʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈreɪdiəʊˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a photograph or image produced on a photographic film or plate by the pattern of radioactive emissions from a specimen placed in contact with it

Examples

autoradiograph of tissue sample
Researchers analyzed the autoradiograph to determine protein synthesis rates in the tissue.
The autoradiograph revealed the distribution of the radioactive tracer in the sample.
The presence of labeled DNA was clearly visible on the autoradiograph after several hours of exposure.
