to fit

verb
be the correct size or shape
Pronunciation: / fɪt / (AmE), / fɪt / (BrE)
to be the correct size or shape for someone or something; to go into a space correctly; to insert or adjust something so it is accommodated or occupies the available space

Examples

to fit the shoes
Do these shoes fit comfortably, or are they too tight?
The key will only fit one specific lock.
This jacket doesn't fit me anymore after I lost weight.
The key won't fit in the lock because it's bent.
These books just fit perfectly on the shelf.
This new shirt doesn't fit me anymore.
Can you fit all these books into that small bag?
Not everyone will fit comfortably around the table, so we might need extra chairs.
She tried to fit her schedule around her new job.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adjust: to change something slightly to make it more suitable
  • synonym – alter: to change something, usually slightly, so that it is different but still recognizably the same thing
  • synonym – jam: to push forcibly into a limited space
  • synonym – match: to go well with something or someone; be harmonious or compatible
  • antonym – misfit: to be the wrong size or shape and not fit properly
  • synonym – squeeze: to force into a tight space
  • synonym – suit: to be right or appropriate for someone or something
