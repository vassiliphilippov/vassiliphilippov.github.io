OTC

adjective
not traded on a stock exchange
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ ti ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌəʊ tiː ˈsiː / (BrE)
(of securities) not traded on an organized stock exchange but dealt with directly between parties, typically via a dealer network.

Examples

OTC stock trading
He specializes in trading OTC securities for emerging tech companies.
Many investors buy OTC stocks for companies that do not meet the requirements of major exchanges.
The OTC market is typically less regulated than the traditional stock exchanges.
