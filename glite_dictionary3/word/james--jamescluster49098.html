James

proper noun
disciple of Jesus; New Testament author
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of Jesus' disciples, the brother of John, and traditionally considered the author of the Epistle of James.

Examples

James the Apostle
James and his brother John were among the first disciples called by Jesus.
In many paintings, James is depicted holding a pilgrim’s staff.
The Epistle of James offers practical advice for Christian living.

Related Terms

  • synonym – John: another disciple and brother of James in the New Testament
