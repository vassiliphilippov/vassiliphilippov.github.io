madcap

adjective
impulsive; acting without thought
Pronunciation: / ˈmædˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈmad.kæp / (BrE)
acting quickly and without thinking about the consequences; impulsive or reckless.

Examples

madcap adventure
She has a madcap sense of adventure that often gets her into trouble.
The play is filled with madcap antics and unpredictable moments.
Their madcap plan to drive across the country without a map ended in disaster.
