to inject

verb
introduce something new
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to introduce or add something, such as an idea, quality, or element, into something else

Examples

to inject new ideas
His enthusiasm injected much-needed energy into the team.
She injects fresh ideas into every project she works on.
The director injected humor into an otherwise serious film.
He would often inject his own opinions during meetings, sometimes derailing the discussion.
She tried to inject some humor into the tense conversation.
The writer decided to inject a plot twist at the last minute to surprise readers.
