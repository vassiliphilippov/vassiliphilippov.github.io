candlenut

noun
seed of the candlenut tree; source of oil
Pronunciation: / ˈkændlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌnʌt / (BrE)
the oily seed of the candlenut tree, used as a source of oil in varnishes and sometimes for ornamental or culinary purposes

Examples

candlenut oil seed
In some cultures, a whole candlenut was strung and burned like a candle.
She roasted the candlenut before adding it to the traditional dish.
The varnish was made more durable with oil extracted from the candlenut.
