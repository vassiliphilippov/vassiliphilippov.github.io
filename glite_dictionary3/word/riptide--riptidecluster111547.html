riptide

noun
strong, turbulent water current
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪp.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪp.taɪd / (BrE)
a strong, fast, and often dangerous current of water, especially in the sea or river, that can pull swimmers away from the shore

Examples

dangerous riptide
He was carried far out to sea by a sudden riptide while surfing.
Swimmers are advised to avoid this area because of a dangerous riptide that sometimes forms.
The warning flags were raised due to reports of a strong riptide near the beach.
A sudden riptide made it difficult for the surfers to get back to shore.
He explained how to escape a riptide by swimming parallel to the coast.
Swimmers should beware of the riptide that can pull them out to sea.
