tinning

noun
process of coating with tin
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of coating or covering a surface with a thin layer of tin to protect it from corrosion

Examples

tinning metal surfaces
Food cans often undergo tinning to prevent rust and extend shelf life.
Poor tinning can lead to rapid corrosion in industrial equipment.
The plumber recommended tinning the copper pipe ends before soldering.

Related Terms

  • synonym – galvanizing: coating iron or steel with a protective layer of zinc
