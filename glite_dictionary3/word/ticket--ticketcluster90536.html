ticket

noun
summons or fine notice for an offense
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
an official notice given to someone by a police officer for breaking a law, especially for a traffic offense, usually requiring payment of a fine or a court appearance

Examples

parking ticket fine
If you park there, you'll probably end up with a ticket.
She got a ticket for speeding on her way to work.
The officer handed him a ticket for running a red light.
