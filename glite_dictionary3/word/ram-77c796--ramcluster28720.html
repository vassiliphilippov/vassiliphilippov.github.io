to ram

verb
pack or fill tightly
Pronunciation: / ræm / (AmE), / ræm / (BrE)
to force or pack something or someone into a place, state, or action, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

to ram clothes in
Commuters rammed onto the train during rush hour.
The students rammed their belongings into boxes before the big move.
They ram as many clothes as possible into their suitcases for the trip.
He rammed his ideas into the conversation, ignoring everyone else's opinion.
She tried to ram the importance of honesty into her children.
The coach rammed discipline into the team before the match.
