bandoleer

noun
shoulder belt for carrying ammunition
Pronunciation: / bændəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌbændəˈlɪə / (BrE)
A broad belt, usually fitted with small pockets or loops for cartridges, worn over the shoulder by soldiers to carry ammunition.

Examples

bandoleer with bullets
He filled each pocket of the bandoleer with gleaming cartridges.
Old photographs showed revolutionaries with rifles and a bandoleer of bullets.
The soldier slung his bandoleer across his chest before heading into battle.
