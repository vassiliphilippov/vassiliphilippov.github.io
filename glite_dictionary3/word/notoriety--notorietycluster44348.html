notoriety

noun
being famous for something bad
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊtəˈraɪəti / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtəˈraɪəti / (BrE)
The state of being famous or well known for something negative or undesirable.

Examples

gain notoriety for crime
Her sudden notoriety was the result of a controversial statement she made in the interview.
The criminal gained notoriety throughout the country for his daring escape from prison.
The restaurant tried to overcome its notoriety for poor hygiene by implementing strict cleanliness standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym – infamy: the state of being well known for a bad quality or deed
  • antonym – renown: the condition of being known or talked about by many people, typically for something good
