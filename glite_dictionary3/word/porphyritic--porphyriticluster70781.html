porphyritic

adjective
rock texture with large crystals
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔrfəˈrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɔːfəˈrɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of igneous rocks) having a texture characterized by large crystals embedded in a fine-grained groundmass.

Examples

porphyritic rock texture
Many volcanic rocks are porphyritic, containing phenocrysts set in a fine matrix.
The geologist identified the stone as porphyritic due to its large, well-formed crystals.
The granite sample displayed a porphyritic texture with prominent feldspar crystals.
