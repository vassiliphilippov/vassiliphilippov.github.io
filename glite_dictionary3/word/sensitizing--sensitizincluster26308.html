sensitizing

noun
making an organism sensitive by repeated injections
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making an organism sensitive to a substance, especially by repeated injections of that substance.

Examples

sensitizing of animals
In laboratory studies, sensitizing can help researchers observe specific allergic reactions.
Repeated sensitizing is often necessary before the immune response becomes detectable.
The treatment involved the sensitizing of patients to a potential allergen using controlled doses.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitization: the process of reducing sensitivity in an organism to a substance
