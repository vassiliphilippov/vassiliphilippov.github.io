Apocrypha

noun
biblical books outside the Hebrew canon
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːkrəfə / (AmE), / əˈpɒkrɪfə / (BrE)
(in the context of the Bible) the group of ancient books included in the Septuagint and Vulgate versions of the Old Testament but not found in the Hebrew canon, and considered non-canonical by most Protestant denominations.

Examples

the Apocrypha books
Scholars frequently debate the historical value of the Apocrypha.
She studied passages from the Apocrypha in her theology class.
The Catholic Bible includes the Apocrypha, while most Protestant versions do not.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Deuterocanonical: biblical books considered canonical by some but not all Christian traditions
  • antonym – canonical: accepted as part of the official canon, especially of Scripture
