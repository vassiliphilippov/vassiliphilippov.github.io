shortening

noun
fat used in baking
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrt.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt.nɪŋ / (BrE)
Fat, such as butter, lard, or solid vegetable fat, used in baking to make pastry or dough crumbly or flaky.

Examples

shortening in baking
She measured out the shortening and mixed it with the flour for the dough.
The recipe calls for a cup of shortening to make the cookies tender.
Using shortening instead of butter results in a flakier pie crust.

Related Terms

  • synonym – butter: a yellow fat spread made from milk or cream
  • synonym – lard: fat from the abdomen of a pig, used in cooking and baking
  • synonym – margarine: a butter substitute made from vegetable oils or animal fats
