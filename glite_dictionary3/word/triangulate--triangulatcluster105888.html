to triangulate

verb
survey or locate using triangulation
Pronunciation: / traɪˈæŋ.jə.leɪt /; / traɪˈæŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈaŋ.gjʊ.leɪt /; / traɪˈaŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to determine a location or distance by using the method of triangulation (measuring with triangles), especially in surveying or navigation

Examples

to triangulate a position
Scientists used GPS data to triangulate the epicenter of the earthquake.
The engineer was asked to triangulate the exact position of the new building site.
The research team triangulated the area using satellite imagery and reference points.
Astronomers often triangulate the distance to nearby stars by observing them from different positions in Earth's orbit.
Surveyors triangulate the position of landmarks to create accurate maps.
The team will triangulate the height of the hill using measurements from three fixed points.

  • synonym – triangulation: the process of surveying by measuring triangles
