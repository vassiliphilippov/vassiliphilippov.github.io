to assume

verb
pretend to have a quality or feeling
Pronunciation: / əˈsum / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to pretend to have or to put on a particular quality, feeling, appearance, role, or position

Examples

to assume an identity
He tried to assume an air of confidence, though he felt nervous.
In the meeting, he assumed a friendly attitude despite their argument.
She managed to assume indifference, hiding her true feelings.
In myths, gods often assume the shapes of animals to interact with humans.
She assumed a cheerful tone as she delivered the news to the team.
The actor assumed an angry expression during the dramatic scene.
At the coronation, the monarch assumed the royal robes before entering the hall.
In the play, the actor assumes the costume of a medieval knight.
She quietly assumed her mask and cloak before leaving the palace.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adopt: to take up or start to use something—such as a manner, style, or attitude
  • synonym – affect: to put on a false display of a quality, feeling, or attitude
  • synonym – don: to put on (an article of clothing)
  • synonym – feign: to pretend to be affected by a feeling, state, or injury
