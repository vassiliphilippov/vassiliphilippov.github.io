appellative

adjective
relating to naming or designation
Pronunciation: / əˈpɛlətɪv / (AmE), / əˈpɛlətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the act of naming or giving names; serving to name or designate.

Examples

appellative function words
In early childhood, the appellative faculty develops as children learn to assign words to objects.
Some rituals have an appellative purpose, symbolically bestowing names or titles during the ceremony.
The appellative function of language is especially evident in how we name new inventions.
