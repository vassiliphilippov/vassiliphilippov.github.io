self-appointed

adjective
assuming a role or title on one's own initiative
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) having given oneself a particular role, title, or responsibility without being asked or officially chosen.

Examples

self-appointed leader
A self-appointed committee began investigating the issue, even though no one asked them to.
He acted as a self-appointed expert on every subject discussed at the meeting.
The city was tired of the self-appointed leaders who spoke without consulting anyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym – official: appointed or authorized by a recognized authority
  • synonym – self-declared: stating or claiming something about oneself without official recognition
