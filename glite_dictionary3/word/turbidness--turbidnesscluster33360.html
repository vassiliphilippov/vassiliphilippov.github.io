turbidness

noun
state of being turbid or cloudy
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.bɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bɪd.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being turbid; muddiness or cloudiness caused by suspended particles or stirred sediment.

Examples

turbidness of water
Laboratory tests measured the turbidness of the drinking water to ensure safety standards were met.
The turbidness of the river increased after the heavy rainfall washed soil into the water.
The pond's turbidness made it difficult to see the fish swimming beneath the surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clarity: the quality of being clear, transparent, or pure
  • synonym – turbidity: the quality or state of being turbid; cloudiness or haziness in a liquid
