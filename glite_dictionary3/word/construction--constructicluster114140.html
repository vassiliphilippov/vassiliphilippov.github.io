construction

noun
interpretation or explanation
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
an interpretation or explanation of the meaning of something, or the process of forming an idea by combining elements

Examples

put a construction on words
His remarks could be given a hostile construction, though that was not his intention.
The court put a narrow construction on the new law, limiting its effect.
The passage is ambiguous and open to several different constructions.
His construction of the argument was both innovative and persuasive.
Language acquisition involves the construction of complex mental representations.
The philosopher's construction of reality challenged conventional theories.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deconstruction: the process of breaking down concepts into parts
  • synonym – formulation: the action of devising or creating something
  • synonym – interpretation: the act of explaining the meaning of something
  • antonym – misinterpretation: incorrect or mistaken explanation of meaning
