wantonness

noun
lack of restraint or control
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːntənnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɒntənnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking restraint or control; reckless freedom from inhibition or worry

Examples

reckless wantonness
His wantonness led him to ignore the consequences of his actions.
She was criticized for the wantonness with which she spent her inheritance.
The wantonness of their celebrations shocked many in the quiet town.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abandon: reckless freedom from inhibition or worry
  • antonym – restraint: self-control and discipline
