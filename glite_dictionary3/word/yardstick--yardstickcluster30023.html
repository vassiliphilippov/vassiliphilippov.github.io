yardstick

noun
a standard for comparison
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːrdˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌstɪk / (BrE)
a standard or criterion by which something is judged or measured

Examples

yardstick for success
Educational achievement is often used as a yardstick for intelligence, though this is debatable.
Profits alone should not be the only yardstick for evaluating a company's success.
The committee lacked a clear yardstick to judge the effectiveness of the new policy.
