rhinophyma

noun
enlargement of the nose due to skin thickening
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪnəˈfaɪmə / (AmE), / ˌraɪnəˈfaɪmə / (BrE)
a marked enlargement of the nose with thickened, reddened skin and visible blood vessels, often related to advanced rosacea

Examples

rhinophyma with swollen nose
Some people mistakenly believe rhinophyma is solely caused by alcohol consumption, but it can occur without drinking.
The patient's rhinophyma had developed gradually, resulting in a bulbous and reddened nose.
Treatment for rhinophyma may include surgical procedures to reshape the affected nose.
