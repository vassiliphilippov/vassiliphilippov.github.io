pulsar

noun
rotating neutron star emitting regular pulses
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌlˌsɑr / (AmE), / ˈpʌlˌsɑː / (BrE)
A highly magnetized, rotating neutron star that emits regular pulses of electromagnetic radiation, typically detected as radio waves.

Examples

rotating pulsar star
Astronomers detected regular radio signals from the newly observed pulsar in the distant galaxy.
Some pulsars rotate hundreds of times per second, making them among the fastest spinning objects in the universe.
The discovery of the first pulsar was one of the most significant achievements in modern astronomy.

  • synonym – neutron star: an extremely dense, small star composed mostly of neutrons
