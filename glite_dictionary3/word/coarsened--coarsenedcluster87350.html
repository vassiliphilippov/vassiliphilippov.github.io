coarsened

adjective
made rough or less refined
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrsənd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsənd / (BrE)
made rough, crude, or less refined, often through lack of skill or exposure to harsh conditions.

Examples

coarsened manners
His manners had coarsened over the years he spent working in the mines.
The coarsened language in the novel reflects the characters’ tough environment.
The coarsened surface of the wood was difficult to polish without extra effort.

  • antonym – refined: improved or made more polished or elegant
