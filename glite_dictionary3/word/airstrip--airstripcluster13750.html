airstrip

noun
simple landing strip for aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈerˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈeəˌstrɪp / (BrE)
A strip of ground set aside for the landing and taking off of aircraft, usually without the full services of a regular airport.

Examples

remote airstrip
After the storm, the airstrip was cleared of debris so flights could resume.
The pilot safely landed the small plane on the airstrip in the remote village.
They built a temporary airstrip for emergency relief operations.
