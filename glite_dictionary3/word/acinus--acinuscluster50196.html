acinus

noun
small sac in a compound gland
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈæsɪnəs / (BrE)
a small sac or saclike cavity in a compound gland, where secretions are produced

Examples

gland acinus structure
Damage to the salivary acini can affect saliva production.
Each acinus in the pancreas produces digestive enzymes that are released into the ducts.
The gland's acini can be seen clearly under the microscope due to their rounded structure.
