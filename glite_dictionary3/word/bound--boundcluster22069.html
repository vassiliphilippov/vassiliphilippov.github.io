bound

adjective
obligated or compelled (by law, duty, or oath)
Pronunciation: / baʊnd / (AmE), / baʊnd / (BrE)
obligated or required to do something, especially by law, duty, or contract

Examples

bound by law
As a sworn officer, he is bound to uphold the law at all times.
She felt morally bound to tell the truth, no matter the consequences.
The committee members are bound by strict confidentiality agreements.
As citizens, we are bound by law to pay taxes honestly.
He felt bound by his promise to help his friend move on Saturday.
She is bound by contract to work for the company for two more years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – free: not bound or constrained; at liberty
  • synonym – obligated: required by law, contract, or moral duty
