rhinosporidiosis

noun
rare infection of the nose or mucous membranes
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪ.noʊˌspɔːr.ɪ.diˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌraɪ.nəʊ.spɒ.rɪ.diˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a rare chronic infection, usually of the nose or mucous membranes, caused by the organism Rhinosporidium seeberi, often acquired in stagnant water

Examples

rhinosporidiosis infection nose
Doctors warned swimmers in the region about the risk of contracting rhinosporidiosis from stagnant ponds.
Effective treatment for rhinosporidiosis often requires surgical removal of polyps.
The patient was diagnosed with rhinosporidiosis after presenting with nasal growths and bleeding.
