to guard

verb
take precautions to prevent something
Pronunciation: / ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɡɑːd / (BrE)
to take precautions in order to prevent or avoid an unwanted consequence

Examples

to guard against danger
Doctors warn patients to guard against infection after surgery.
She tried to guard against making promises she couldn't keep.
You should always guard against identity theft when shopping online.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expose: to leave unprotected or vulnerable
  • synonym – protect: to keep safe from harm or injury
