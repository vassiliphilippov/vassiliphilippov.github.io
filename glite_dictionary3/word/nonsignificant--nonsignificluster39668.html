nonsignificant

adjective
not statistically significant
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt / (BrE)
Not statistically significant; likely due to chance rather than reflecting a real effect or difference.

Examples

nonsignificant result
A nonsignificant result suggests that the observed effect could be due to random variation.
After analysis, the differences between the two groups proved to be nonsignificant.
The change in blood pressure was nonsignificant according to the study results.
