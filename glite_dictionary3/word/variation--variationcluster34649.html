variation

noun
angle between magnetic and true north
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛə.riˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the angle at a specific location between magnetic north and true north, important for navigation

Examples

magnetic variation angle
If you don't account for the local variation, your compass reading could lead you off course.
Navigators must check the current variation before setting their bearings.
The variation in this region is about 7 degrees east of true north.
