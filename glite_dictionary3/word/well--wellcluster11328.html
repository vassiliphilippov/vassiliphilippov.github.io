well

noun
an abundant source (especially metaphorical)
Pronunciation: / wɛl / (AmE), / wɛl / (BrE)
A place or source from which something is supplied abundantly, especially used metaphorically to refer to a rich supply of something.

Examples

well of inspiration
His imagination proved to be an endless well of inspiration for his writing.
She was a well of kindness and patience, always ready to help others.
The old teacher was a well of knowledge and wisdom in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fount: a source of desirable quality or commodity
  • synonym – spring: a natural source (often of water), also used metaphorically for an origin
