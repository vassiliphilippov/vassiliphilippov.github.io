vena femoralis

noun
major vein of the thigh
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪnə fəˈmɔrəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə fɛˈmɒrəlɪs / (BrE)
the vein that accompanies the femoral artery in the thigh and continues as the external iliac vein; responsible for draining blood from the lower limb

Examples

the vena femoralis in thigh
A blood clot in the vena femoralis can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
An ultrasound revealed a blockage in the patient's vena femoralis.
During surgery, the vena femoralis must be carefully exposed to avoid injury.
