moat

noun
durable competitive advantage (business)
Pronunciation: / moʊt / (AmE), / məʊt / (BrE)
In business, a durable competitive advantage that protects a company's market share and profits from rivals.

Examples

company's moat advantage
Investors praised the startup's strong moat, which protected its market share against new competitors.
The company's patented technology created a wide moat that rivals found difficult to cross.
Analysts looked for businesses with recurring revenue, pricing power, and a durable moat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barrier to entry: an obstacle that makes it difficult for new firms to enter an industry
  • synonym – competitive advantage: a condition or set of factors that makes a company more successful than its rivals
