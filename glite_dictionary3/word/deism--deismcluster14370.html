deism

noun
belief in a non-intervening God based on reason
Pronunciation: / ˈdiː.ɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪ.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The belief that God exists and created the world but does not intervene in its workings, with faith based on reason rather than on revelation or religious authority.

Examples

deism rejects miracles
Deism rejects miracles and prophecies as sources of religious knowledge.
He studied the history of deism and its impact on European philosophy.
Some Enlightenment thinkers advocated deism as a way to reconcile science and faith.

Related Terms

  • antonym – atheism: disbelief or lack of belief in the existence of God or gods
  • antonym – theism: belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially a creator who intervenes in the universe
