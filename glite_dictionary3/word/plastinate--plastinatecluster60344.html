to plastinate

verb
preserve tissue with plastics
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈplæstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to preserve biological tissue by replacing water and fat with plastics, especially for teaching and research purposes

Examples

to plastinate a body
After the brain was plastinated, students could examine its anatomy without risk of decay.
Scientists often plastinate animal specimens to study their internal structures in detail.
The medical school decided to plastinate several organs for display in the anatomy lab.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embalm: to preserve (a body) from decay, usually with chemicals
