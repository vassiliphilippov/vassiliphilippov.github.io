eyewash

noun
superficial or deceptive measure
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a statement, action, or measure that is intended to placate or impress people but is superficial or insincere and lacks real substance or effect.

Examples

political eyewash
Many voters dismissed the committee's changes as eyewash rather than meaningful safety reform.
The CEO's apology felt like mere eyewash, a public relations move with no real consequences.
After the scandal, small regulatory updates seemed to be eyewash while problems remained unresolved.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lip service: insincere expression of support or concern without real action
  • antonym – meaningful reform: genuine changes that have substantial effect
  • synonym – window dressing: an action or change made only to give a good appearance
