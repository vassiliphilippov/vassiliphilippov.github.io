hemochromatosis

noun
iron overload disorder
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊˌkroʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˌkrəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which excessive iron accumulates in the body, especially in the tissues, potentially causing bronzed skin, liver enlargement, diabetes, and joint abnormalities.

Examples

Early diagnosis of hemochromatosis is crucial for effective management and prevention of organ damage.
