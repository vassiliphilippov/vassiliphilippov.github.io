caravan

noun
group traveling together (often with animals)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrəˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈkærəvæn / (BrE)
A group of people, especially traders or pilgrims, traveling together in a line with animals or vehicles for safety across a desert or through dangerous regions.

Examples

caravan of camels
A caravan of camels and traders camped beneath the stars after a long day of travel.
Merchants formed a caravan for protection as they carried goods along the Silk Road.
The caravan moved slowly across the desert, winding between the dunes under the blazing sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym – convoy: a group of vehicles or ships traveling together for protection
  • synonym – train: a group of people, animals, or vehicles traveling together
