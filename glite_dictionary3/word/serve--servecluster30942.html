to serve

verb
to function for a purpose or provide a service
Pronunciation: / sɝv / (AmE), / sɜːv / (BrE)
to be used for a particular purpose, to fulfill a function, or to have a particular effect or result

Examples

serve a purpose
Solar panels serve both to reduce energy costs and to protect the environment.
The central heating system serves all apartments in the building.
This drawer serves as a safe place for important documents.
Her previous experience will serve her well in her new position.
The study room can serve as an office if needed.
These old boxes serve a useful purpose during the move.
A short note will serve to explain the situation.
If you don't have any flour, cornmeal will serve in this recipe.
This chair will serve until we can buy a new one.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contribute: to help bring about a result
  • synonym – do: be enough or adequate for a purpose
  • antonym – fail: not succeed in being enough or adequate
  • synonym – function: to have a particular function or role
  • synonym – suffice: to be enough; to meet the needs
