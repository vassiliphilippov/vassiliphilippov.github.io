to fetch

verb
be sold for a certain price
Pronunciation: / fɛtʃ / (AmE), / fɛtʃ / (BrE)
(of something for sale) to be sold for a particular price.

Examples

fetch a high price
Even in poor condition, the classic car fetched enough to fund his holiday.
His rare comic book collection fetched a surprisingly high price when he sold it.
That antique vase fetched over a thousand pounds at last night's auction.
