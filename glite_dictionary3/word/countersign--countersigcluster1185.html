countersign

noun
confirming signature on a document
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌsaɪn / (BrE)
a second signature added to a document to confirm or endorse it after the initial signature

Examples

countersign on paperwork
Every disbursement over a certain amount needs a countersign from the finance office.
The manager's countersign was required before the contract could be finalized.
Without the necessary countersign, the document was considered incomplete by the auditors.

  • synonym – signature: a person's name written as confirmation or approval
