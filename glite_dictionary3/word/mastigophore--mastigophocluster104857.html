mastigophore

noun
protozoan with whiplike appendages
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstəɡəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈmæstɪɡəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a usually nonphotosynthetic, free-living protozoan with whiplike appendages, some of which can be pathogens of humans or other animals

Examples

a mastigophore swims
Some species of mastigophore are known to cause diseases in humans.
The lake water sample was teeming with active mastigophores.
The researcher identified the mastigophore under a microscope by its distinct flagella.
