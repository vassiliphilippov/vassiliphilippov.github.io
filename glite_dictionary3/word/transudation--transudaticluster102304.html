transudation

noun
fluid passing through a membrane or tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌtræn.suˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræn.sjuːˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A fluid that passes through a membrane or tissue due to hydrostatic or osmotic pressure, especially one that has filtered out of blood vessels but is not the result of inflammation.

Examples

transudation in heart failure
Low-protein transudation can occur in cases of heart failure.
The physician collected a sample of transudation from the patient's pleural cavity for analysis.
Unlike an exudate, transudation is typically clear and low in cellular content.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exudate: fluid that seeps out of blood vessels, often due to inflammation
