to save

verb
accumulate money for future use
Pronunciation: / seɪv / (AmE), / seɪv / (BrE)
to keep or retain something, especially for future use or for someone else

Examples

to save money
By saving regularly, she was able to buy a new car.
I try to save a little each month for emergencies.
They saved enough money to go on a long vacation.

Please save my place in line while I run to the restroom.
They plan to save the option of reopening negotiations if necessary.

She saves her children's drawings in a folder to look back on in the future.
They decided to save the best wine for a special occasion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hoard: to collect and keep a large amount of something for future use, often secretly
  • synonym – keep: to retain possession of or maintain for future use
  • synonym – reserve: to keep something for a particular purpose or person
  • antonym – spend: pay out money for goods or services
  • synonym – store: to put something away and keep it for future use
