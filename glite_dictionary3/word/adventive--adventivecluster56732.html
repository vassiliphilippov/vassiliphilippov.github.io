adventive

adjective
not native or fully established (biological)
Pronunciation: / ædˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / ədˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) not native to a place and not fully established, but locally or temporarily naturalized.

Examples

adventive plant species
Many adventive plants are found along railway lines, where they can thrive temporarily.
The botanist noted that the adventive grass species had not spread far beyond the garden.
The region's ecosystem includes several adventive insects that arrived through imported goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – introduced: brought to an area by humans, not native
  • antonym – native: originating or naturally occurring in a place
