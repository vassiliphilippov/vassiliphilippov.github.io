color

noun
deceptive appearance or pretense
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
an appearance or pretense that hides the true nature of something, often with the intent to mislead

Examples

color of innocence
He offered his actions a color of innocence, though he knew they were wrong.
The report was given the color of precision, but key facts were missing.
Under the color of official business, they advanced their own interests.

Related Terms

  • synonym – guise: an external form concealing the true nature
  • synonym – semblance: an outward appearance that is deliberately misleading
  • antonym – truth: the real facts; opposite of a misleading appearance
