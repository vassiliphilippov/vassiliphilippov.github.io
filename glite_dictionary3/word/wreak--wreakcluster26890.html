to wreak

verb
cause (especially harm or destruction) to happen
Pronunciation: / rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
to cause something, especially something violent or harmful, to happen

Examples

to wreak havoc
His reckless actions may wreak unintended consequences for everyone involved.
If released, the virus could wreak widespread damage in the population.
The storm wreaked havoc across the coastal villages, destroying homes and businesses.

  • synonym – inflict: impose something (usually negative) on someone or something
