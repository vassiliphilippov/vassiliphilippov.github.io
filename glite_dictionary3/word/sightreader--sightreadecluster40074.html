sightreader

noun
person who reads or performs music at first sight
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪtˌriːdər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtˌriːdə / (BrE)
a person, especially a musician, who is able to perform or read music at first sight without prior preparation

Examples

skilled sightreader pianist
Although he struggled with improvisation, he was a gifted sightreader in the orchestra.
As a talented sightreader, she could play any sheet music set before her.
It's important for a professional musician to be an excellent sightreader.
