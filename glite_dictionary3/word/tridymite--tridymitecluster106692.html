tridymite

noun
a mineral form of silica
Pronunciation: / traɪˈdɪm.aɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈdɪm.ɪt / (BrE)
a mineral form of silica (SiO₂), occurring as thin, often hexagonal plates or crystals, commonly in volcanic rocks

Examples

tridymite crystals in rock
Geologists discovered a layer of tridymite in the volcanic rock samples from the region.
Microscopic examination revealed that the thin, plate-like crystals were actually tridymite.
The presence of tridymite indicates the rock was formed at relatively low pressures and high temperatures.
