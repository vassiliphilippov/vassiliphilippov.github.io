PI

noun
antiviral drug for HIV; protease inhibitor
Pronunciation: / piː aɪ / (AmE), / piː aɪ / (BrE)
an antiviral drug, specifically a protease inhibitor, used to treat HIV by blocking the activity of HIV protease and preventing viral replication, often as part of combination therapy

Examples

PI blocks HIV
Combination therapy often includes more than one PI to improve treatment outcomes.
Research has led to the development of several effective PIs in fighting resistant strains of HIV.
The doctor prescribed a new PI as part of the patient's HIV treatment regimen.

Related Terms

  • synonym – protease inhibitor: class of drugs that inhibit protease enzymes, used in HIV treatment
