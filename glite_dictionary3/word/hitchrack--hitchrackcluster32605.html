hitchrack

noun
rail for tying horses
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪtʃˌræk / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃˌræk / (BrE)
A fixed horizontal rail or post to which horses are tied to prevent them from wandering away.

Examples

tie horse to hitchrack
Children played near the hitchrack while their parents tended to business inside.
He tied the mare to the hitchrack while he went into the general store.
The old stable still had a hitchrack out front for tying up horses.
