Phytolacca americana

noun
North American perennial plant with poisonous berries and root
Pronunciation: / faɪtəˌlækə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / faɪtəˌlækə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A tall, coarse perennial plant native to North America, with small white flowers and blackish-red berries on long drooping clusters; its young stems are edible, but its berries and roots are poisonous.
