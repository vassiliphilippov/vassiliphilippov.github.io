to maintain

verb
keep to something correctly or closely
Pronunciation: / meɪnˈteɪn / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn / (BrE)
to keep to something regularly or consistently, such as a pattern, schedule, or record

Examples

to maintain focus
He tried to maintain focus during the long and complicated meeting.
It is important to maintain a steady rhythm when running long distances.
We must maintain accurate records for all our transactions.
Each employee must maintain accurate records of their hours worked.
It is good practice to maintain a diary during fieldwork.
She diligently maintains her research notes after every experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – keep: to continue doing something or to stick to a schedule or pattern
  • antonym – lose track: to stop keeping count or awareness of something
  • synonym – record: to set down in writing for future reference
