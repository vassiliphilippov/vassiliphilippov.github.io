recapture

noun
government seizure of excess profits
Pronunciation: / riˈkæp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / riˈkæp.tʃə / (BrE)
the legal seizure by a government of profits exceeding a fixed limit, especially under financial or tax regulations

Examples

tax recapture rules
Tax recapture provisions limit how much profit can be retained by private entities.
The company faced a recapture of excess profits after an audit by the tax authorities.
The new law broadened the scope of recapture to include various investment gains.
