to thrust

verb
impose forcefully or insistently
Pronunciation: / θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
to impose something forcefully or insistently on someone, especially against their will

Examples

to thrust opinions on people
He always tries to thrust his opinions on others, even when they're not relevant.
The teacher thrust extra assignments on the class right before the holidays.
They resented having unfamiliar customs thrust upon them by outsiders.

Related Terms

  • synonym – force: to make someone do something by using strength or power
  • synonym – impose: to forcefully place a burden or idea on someone
