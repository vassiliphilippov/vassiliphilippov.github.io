to destress

verb
relieve mental stress
Pronunciation: / diˈstrɛs / (AmE), / diˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to reduce or relieve mental or emotional stress; to relax and unwind (often by doing pleasant or calming activities).

Examples

to destress after work
After work she took a long walk to destress before preparing dinner for the family.
He destressed over the weekend with yoga classes and a friend's quiet cabin retreat.
Spa evenings and reading by the fire are her favorite ways of destressing after a busy week.

Related Terms

  • synonym – relax: to become less tense, anxious, or stressed
  • antonym – stress: to put mental or emotional strain on; the opposite of destress
  • synonym – unwind: to relax after a period of stress or work
