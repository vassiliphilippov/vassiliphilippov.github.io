curettage

noun
surgical scraping for tissue removal
Pronunciation: / kjʊˈrɛt̬ɪdʒ / (AmE), / kjʊəˈrɛtɪdʒ / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which tissue or growths are removed from a bodily cavity, especially the uterus, by scraping with a curette

Examples

curettage of uterus
A curettage is often done under local or general anesthesia, depending on the patient's needs.
Following a miscarriage, curettage may be recommended to ensure all tissue is cleared.
The doctor performed a curettage to remove abnormal tissue from the uterus.
