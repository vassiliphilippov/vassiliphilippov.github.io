set-aside

adjective
reserved or saved in advance
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt əˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛt əˌsaɪd / (BrE)
(of something) reserved or saved for a particular purpose or person, especially in advance.

Examples

set-aside seats for guests
All tickets for the front row were set-aside for VIP guests.
The council has a set-aside budget for community projects.
There are funds set-aside for emergency expenses if needed.
