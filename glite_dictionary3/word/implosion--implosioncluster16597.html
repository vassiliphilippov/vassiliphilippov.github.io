implosion

noun
initial phase in stop consonant articulation
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈploʊʒən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpləʊʒən / (BrE)
The initial phase during the articulation of a stop consonant, when the airflow is briefly blocked in the vocal tract.

Examples

speech implosion
A linguist may analyze the timing of the implosion when studying speech sounds.
Accurate recording equipment is needed to clearly capture the subtle implosion in some languages.
The implosion of a stop consonant is followed by a sudden release of air.
