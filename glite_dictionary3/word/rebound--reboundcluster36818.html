rebound

noun
movement back after impact
Pronunciation: / ˈriˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈriːbaʊnd / (BrE)
a sudden movement back after hitting something; a reaction when force causes something to return to its original position or direction

Examples

rebound off a wall
After striking the wall, the ball took a sharp rebound toward the players.
The basketball made a loud rebound off the backboard before dropping into the net.
The car's tire suffered a rebound after hitting the curb, causing a slight jolt.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bounce: the movement of something springing back after hitting a surface
