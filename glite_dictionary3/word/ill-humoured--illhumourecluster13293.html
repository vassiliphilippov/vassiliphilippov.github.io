ill-humoured

adjective
having a surly or irritable temper
Pronunciation: / ˌɪlˈhjuːmɚd / (AmE), / ˌɪlˈhjuːməd / (BrE)
having a surly, unfriendly, or irritable disposition; easily annoyed or inclined to be brusque.

Examples

ill-humoured reply
Despite his ill-humoured remarks, his colleagues knew he meant no real harm.
Her ill-humoured tone made it clear she didn't want to continue the conversation.
The ill-humoured waiter provided little warmth or welcome during our meal.
