to aim

verb
direct or intend toward a goal
Pronunciation: / eɪm / (AmE), / eɪm / (BrE)
to point or direct something (such as a weapon or object) at a target

Examples

to aim a gun
He aimed his remarks at the entire audience, hoping to inspire everyone.
She aimed to finish the project before the deadline.
The company is aiming its new product line at young professionals.
It's dangerous to aim a loaded gun at anyone, even as a joke.
She aimed the water hose at the flowers to give them a good soaking.
The photographer carefully aimed his camera at the hummingbird.

Related Terms

  • synonym – direct: to cause to move or be aimed toward a position or goal
  • degree higher – fire: to discharge a weapon at a target
  • synonym – point: to direct or aim an object towards something
  • synonym – target: to select as the object of an action or intention
