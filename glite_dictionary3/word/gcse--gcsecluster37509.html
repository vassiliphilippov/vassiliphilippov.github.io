GCSE

noun
British school qualification for 15–16 year olds
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː siː ˈiː / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː siː ˈiː / (BrE)
(in the UK) a qualification awarded for passing an exam in a particular subject, usually taken by students aged 15–16 in secondary school.

Examples

GCSE exam results
Having a GCSE in English is a basic requirement for many jobs.
Most students in England take their GCSEs at the end of Year 11.
