myringa

noun
membrane of the ear; eardrum
Pronunciation: / mɪˈrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
the thin membrane that separates the outer ear from the middle ear and vibrates in response to sound; the eardrum

Examples

myringa infection
Inflammation of the myringa can occur during an ear infection.
Rupture of the myringa often results in hearing loss and discomfort.
The surgeon carefully inspected the myringa for signs of perforation before proceeding with the operation.
