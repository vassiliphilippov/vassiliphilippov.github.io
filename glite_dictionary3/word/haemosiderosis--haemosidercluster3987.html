haemosiderosis

noun
abnormal deposit of hemosiderin in tissues
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊˌsɪdəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊsɪdəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormal deposits of hemosiderin in body tissues, often associated with diseases like thalassemia or hemochromatosis.
