still

noun
tranquil silence or calm (literary)
Pronunciation: / stɪl / (AmE), / stɪl / (BrE)
a period or state of deep silence and calm, especially used in a poetic or literary sense

Examples

the still of night
In the still of the morning, the village felt untouched by time.
She longed for the peaceful still before dawn.
There was a profound still in the house after midnight.
