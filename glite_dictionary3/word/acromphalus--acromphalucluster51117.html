acromphalus

noun
abnormal protrusion of the navel
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɑm.fə.ləs / (AmE), / əˈkrɒm.fə.ləs / (BrE)
An abnormal protrusion of the navel, sometimes considered an early sign of an umbilical hernia.

Examples

infant acromphalus
In rare cases, acromphalus may progress to a full umbilical hernia if untreated.
Parents should seek medical advice if they notice signs of acromphalus in their newborn.
The doctor diagnosed the infant with acromphalus during a routine checkup.
