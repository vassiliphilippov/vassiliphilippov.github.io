platypus

noun
small aquatic mammal with a bill and webbed feet
Pronunciation: / ˈplætɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈplætɪpəs / (BrE)
a small, aquatic mammal native to Australia and Tasmania, with dense fur, a broad bill, webbed feet, and a flat tail; the only living species of the family Ornithorhynchidae.

Examples

a platypus swims
The platypus is famous for its duck-like bill and egg-laying habits.
The platypus is one of the few mammals that lay eggs.
We saw a platypus swimming in the river during our trip to Tasmania.
