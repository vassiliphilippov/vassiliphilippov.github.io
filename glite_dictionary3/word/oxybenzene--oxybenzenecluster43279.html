oxybenzene

noun
a toxic chemical compound; phenol
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksiˈbɛnzin / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
A toxic, white, crystalline acidic compound derived from benzene, used in manufacturing and as an antiseptic; poisonous if ingested.

Examples

oxybenzene in plastics
Oxybenzene is widely used in the manufacture of plastics and pharmaceuticals due to its disinfectant properties.
Laboratory protocols require the proper storage of oxybenzene because of its potential health hazards.
You should always handle oxybenzene with care, as it is highly toxic if swallowed or inhaled.
