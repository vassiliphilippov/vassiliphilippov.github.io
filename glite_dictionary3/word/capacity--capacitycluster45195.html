capacity

noun
ability to store electric charge
Pronunciation: / kəˈpæs.ə.ti / (AmE), / kəˈpæs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the ability of a component, device, or system to store electric charge, measured as capacitance

Examples

electric capacity
Electrolytic capacitors have a higher capacity compared to ceramic ones.
Measuring the capacity of a device is crucial in designing electronic circuits.
