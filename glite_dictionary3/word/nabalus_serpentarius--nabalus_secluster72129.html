Nabalus serpentarius

noun
a perennial herb of the U.S. with pinkish flowers and thick leaves
Pronunciation: / nəˈbeɪləs ˌsɜːrpənˈtɛəriəs / (AmE), / nəˈbɑːləs ˌsɜːpɛnˈtɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the southern and eastern United States, with drooping clusters of pinkish flowers and thick basal leaves resembling a lion's foot; sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes.
