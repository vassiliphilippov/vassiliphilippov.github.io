leafhopper

noun
sap-sucking jumping insect
Pronunciation: / ˈliːfˌhɑpər / (AmE), / ˈliːfˌhɒpə / (BrE)
A small jumping insect that sucks the sap from plants, often causing damage to crops.

Examples

leafhopper damages crops
A leafhopper can transmit diseases between plants as it feeds on their sap.
Farmers often use pesticides to control leafhopper populations in their fields.
Some species of leafhopper have colourful patterns that help them blend into foliage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cicada: large insect of the same order as leafhoppers, notable for its loud song
  • synonym – planthopper: similar sap-sucking insect that resembles a leafhopper
