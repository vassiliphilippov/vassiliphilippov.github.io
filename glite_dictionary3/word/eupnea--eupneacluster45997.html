eupnea

noun
normal, unlabored breathing
Pronunciation: / juːpˈniːə / (AmE), / juːpˈniːə / (BrE)
normal, unlabored breathing; natural breathing at rest without difficulty or distress.

Examples

steady eupnea
After surgery, the nurse monitored the patient's eupnea to ensure her breathing remained steady and calm.
During sleep, people experience eupnea unless disrupted by medical conditions like sleep apnea.
Proper eupnea is an important sign of general health and wellness in newborns.
