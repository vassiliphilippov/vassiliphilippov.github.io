to abash

verb
make embarrassed or self-conscious
Pronunciation: / əˈbæʃ / (AmE), / əˈbaʃ / (BrE)
to make someone feel embarrassed or self-conscious

Examples

to abash a friend
His rude comment did not abash her in the slightest.
She tried to speak confidently, but his intense gaze abashed her.
The unexpected applause seemed to abash the shy performer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embarrass: to cause someone to feel awkward or self-conscious
  • antonym – encourage: to give someone confidence, opposite of cause embarrassment
  • synonym – shame: to make someone feel ashamed or humiliated
