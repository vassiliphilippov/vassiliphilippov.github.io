flaccid

adjective
soft, limp, lacking firmness
Pronunciation: / ˈflæs.ɪd, ˈflæk.sɪd /; / ˈflæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈflæs.ɪd, ˈflæk.sɪd /; / ˈflæs.ɪd / (BrE)
soft and lacking firmness or strength; hanging limply, especially due to weak muscles or lack of tone

Examples

flaccid muscles
After weeks in a cast, his leg muscles were noticeably flaccid and weak.
The balloon hung flaccid after most of the air escaped.
The leaves became flaccid due to a lack of water.
After weeks without exercise, his muscles became noticeably flaccid.
Her argument was flaccid, lacking any real evidence or strength.
The lettuce leaves were flaccid after sitting in the fridge for too long.
