endogamous

adjective
having fertilization within the same species
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdɑɡəməs / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒɡəməs / (BrE)
(of reproduction) characterized by fertilization involving pollen or gametes from another flower or individual of the same species.

Examples

endogamous breeding system
Research has shown that endogamous breeding promotes genetic stability within isolated populations.
Some plant species are endogamous, requiring fertilization by pollen from flowers of the same kind.
Unlike exogamous plants, endogamous species rarely hybridize with different species.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exogamous: (of reproduction) involving fertilization from a different species or population
