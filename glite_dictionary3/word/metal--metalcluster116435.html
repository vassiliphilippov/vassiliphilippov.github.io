metal

noun
a shiny, conductive chemical element
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a chemical element that is typically a shiny, solid material, conducts heat and electricity, and can be shaped or formed into sheets or wires

Examples

shiny metal surface
Aluminum is a lightweight metal used in making aircraft and cans.
Copper is a good conductor, making it an important metal for electrical wiring.
Gold is a precious metal valued for its rarity and beauty.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonmetal: an element that does not have the characteristic properties of metals
