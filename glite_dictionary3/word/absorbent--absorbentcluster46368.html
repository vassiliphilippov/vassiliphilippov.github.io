absorbent

noun
material that soaks up liquids or gases
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔrbənt, æbˈzɔrbənt / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbənt, æbˈzɔːbənt / (BrE)
a material that is able to soak up liquids or gases easily

Examples

use an absorbent pad
Activated charcoal acts as an absorbent for toxins in some medical treatments.
Paper towels are a common absorbent found in most kitchens.
The laboratory technician used an absorbent to collect the spilled liquid.

Related Terms

  • antonym – adsorbent: a material that accumulates substances on its surface rather than absorbing them
