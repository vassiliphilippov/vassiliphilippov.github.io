forgery

noun
fake copy made to deceive
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒəri / (BrE)
a copy of an object, document, or work of art that is made or altered with the intent to deceive by representing it as the genuine original

Examples

a forgery of a painting
Experts examined the signature and quickly determined it was a forgery.
That document is an obvious forgery and cannot be trusted.
The art dealer was arrested for selling a forgery instead of a genuine painting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – authentic: genuine; not a copy or imitation
  • synonym – counterfeit: an imitation made to deceive; especially of money or documents
