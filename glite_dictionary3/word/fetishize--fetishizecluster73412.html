to fetishize

verb
to treat with excessive devotion
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛtɪʃˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛtɪʃaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or regard something with excessive or irrational devotion, often attributing to it special powers or importance; to make a fetish of something

Examples

to fetishize tradition
He fetishized ancient rituals, insisting on following every detail precisely.
Some people fetishize certain objects, attributing to them a power they do not possess.
The media often fetishizes youth and beauty in its advertisements.

Related Terms

  • antonym – devalue: to lower or dismiss the value or importance of something
  • synonym – idolize: to admire or revere excessively
