to commercialise

verb
make something commercial or for profit
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɝːʃəlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something available for profit or to manage in a way intended to make money, often by turning activities, services, or ideas into commercial enterprises

Examples

to commercialise a product
Some artists refuse to commercialise their work, preferring creative freedom over profit.
The company plans to commercialise its latest medical technology next year.
The once-traditional village has started to commercialise its cultural festivals to attract tourists.
