to sup

verb
take small sips or spoonfuls
Pronunciation: / sʌp / (AmE), / sʌp / (BrE)
to take liquid or soft food into the mouth a little at a time, especially by drinking or eating with a spoon

Examples

to sup hot soup
He supped the hot soup carefully, blowing on each spoonful before tasting it.
She likes to sup a cup of herbal tea while reading in the evening.
The elderly man supped slowly, savoring each bite of the stew.
