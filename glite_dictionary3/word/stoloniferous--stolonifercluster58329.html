stoloniferous

adjective
producing stolons (runners)
Pronunciation: / ˌstoʊləˈnɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌstəʊləˈnɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of a plant) producing stolons; having stems that grow horizontally along or just below the ground and form new plants at the nodes.

Examples

stoloniferous grass species
Many species of grass are stoloniferous and can rapidly spread over a lawn.
Some weeds are particularly invasive due to their stoloniferous nature.
The stoloniferous growth of strawberries allows them to colonize new areas efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stolon: a horizontal stem that takes root at intervals to form new plants
