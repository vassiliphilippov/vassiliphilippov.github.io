all

determiner
referring to the whole quantity, number, or every member
Pronunciation: / ɔl / (AmE), / ɔːl / (BrE)
used to refer to the whole quantity or number of something, or every one of a group or class

Examples

all children together
All children must be accompanied by an adult.
All the students passed the test with excellent grades.
She worked all day without taking a break.

Related Terms

  • synonym – every: used to refer to all members of a group considered individually
  • antonym – none: not any; no part or person
  • antonym – some: used to refer to an unspecified part or quantity of a whole
