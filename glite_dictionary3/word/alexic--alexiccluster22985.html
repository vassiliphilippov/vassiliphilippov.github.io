alexic

noun
person with alexia (reading disorder)
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛksɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɛksɪk / (BrE)
A person who has alexia, a disorder characterized by the loss of the ability to read.

Examples

an alexic patient
After his stroke, he became alexic and required extensive rehabilitation to communicate.
Researchers studied how alexic patients process written language differently from the general population.
The clinic has recently started a program to support individuals who are alexic.

  • synonym – alexia: loss of the ability to read; acquired reading disorder
