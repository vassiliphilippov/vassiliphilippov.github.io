gossip

noun
rumor or talk about others' private affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːsɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪp / (BrE)
information about the private affairs of others, often of a sensational or malicious nature, shared for entertainment or intrigue.

Examples

gossip about celebrities
She was often the subject of village gossip after moving in with her boyfriend.
The latest gossip at the office spread quickly among the employees.
Their conversation turned into an hour of sharing juicy gossip about celebrities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fact: a thing known or proved to be true, not mere report or speculation
  • synonym – rumor: an unverified piece of information spread among people
