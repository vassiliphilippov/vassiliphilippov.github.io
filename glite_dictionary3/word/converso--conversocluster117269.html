converso

noun
Jew or Moor converted to Christianity (medieval Iberia)
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛrsoʊ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːsəʊ / (BrE)
(historical) A Jew or Moor in medieval Spain or Portugal who professed conversion to Christianity, especially to avoid persecution or expulsion.

Examples

a converso in Spain
A number of conversos eventually rose to prominent positions in Spanish society.
Many conversos continued to practice their original faith in secret despite public conversion.
The Inquisition targeted conversos suspected of heresy during the late 15th century.
