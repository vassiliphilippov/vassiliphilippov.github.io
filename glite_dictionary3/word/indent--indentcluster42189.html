to indent

verb
to set text in from the margin
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnt / (BrE)
to set in (a line or lines of text) from the margin, typically at the beginning of a paragraph

Examples

to indent a line
If you forget to indent your code, it might be hard to read.
Please indent each paragraph at the start of your essay.
You should indent the first line to improve readability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – outdent: to set text outwards from the usual margin
