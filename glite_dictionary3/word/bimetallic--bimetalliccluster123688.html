bimetallic

adjective
relating to use of two metals in a monetary system
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪməˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪmɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving a monetary system based on the use of two metals, typically gold and silver, as legal tender at a fixed ratio.

Examples

bimetallic standard
Advocates of bimetallic standards believed it would stabilize currency values by using both metals.
Debates about the bimetallic monetary system were central to 19th-century economic policy.
The country adopted a bimetallic standard, allowing both gold and silver coins to circulate as money.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monometallic: using a monetary standard based on a single metal
