to erode

verb
remove soil or rock gradually
Pronunciation: / ɪˈroʊd / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊd / (BrE)
(of natural forces) to gradually wear away or remove soil, rock, or land.

Examples

to erode the coastline
If left unchecked, acid rain will erode the limestone statues in the park.
Over time, the constant flow of water eroded the riverbank.
Strong winds can quickly erode exposed hillsides in the desert.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deposit: to lay down material, especially by natural forces—a process opposite to erosion
  • synonym – wear away: to gradually remove material by friction or erosion
