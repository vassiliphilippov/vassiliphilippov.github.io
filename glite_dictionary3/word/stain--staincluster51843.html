to stain

verb
to color with a liquid dye or tint
Pronunciation: / steɪn / (AmE), / steɪn / (BrE)
to color, dye, or mark something with a liquid, often leaving a permanent or hard-to-remove spot or discoloration

Examples

to stain wood
The craftsman carefully stained the glass for the church windows.
They decided to stain the old wooden floor a deep cherry color.
You can stain the fabric to give it a unique appearance.
Be careful not to stain your shirt with the spaghetti sauce.
Coffee can easily stain teeth if consumed regularly over time.
The ink bottle spilled and stained the carpet permanently.
A spill of red wine can easily stain a white carpet if not cleaned up quickly.
Repeated handling began to stain the old photograph’s edges with oily fingerprints.
The scandal threatened to stain the politician’s career for years to come.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bleach: to make something lighter or whiter in color
  • antonym – cleanse: to make something clean or pure
  • synonym – dye: to color or stain with a dye
  • synonym – spot: to mark with small areas of a different color
  • synonym – sully: to damage the purity or integrity of something
  • synonym – tarnish: to lose or cause to lose luster or purity, often used for metals or reputations
