fanatic

noun
person with extreme enthusiasm
Pronunciation: / fəˈnætɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnætɪk / (BrE)
a person who shows extreme or irrational enthusiasm for a cause, often disregarding reason or opposing evidence

Examples

sports fanatic at games
It's important not to become a fanatic about your personal beliefs and shut out other perspectives.
She was considered a health fanatic, following online trends with unquestioning devotion.
The political rally attracted more than one fanatic who wouldn't listen to any opposing arguments.

  • antonym – moderate: a person holding moderate views, especially in politics
