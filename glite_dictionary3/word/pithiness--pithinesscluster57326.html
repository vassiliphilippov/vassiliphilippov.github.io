pithiness

noun
concise and forceful expression
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪθi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪθɪnəs / (BrE)
The quality of expressing much in few words; concise and forceful expression in speech or writing.

Examples

remark of pithiness
Her pithiness impressed the audience, as she managed to summarize the complex topic in a single sentence.
The pithiness of his comments made them stand out during the discussion.
Writers often strive for pithiness to make their arguments more effective and memorable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conciseness: the quality of being brief but comprehensive
  • antonym – verbosity: the quality of using more words than needed
