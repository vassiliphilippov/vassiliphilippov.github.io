refractivity

noun
property describing extent of refraction
Pronunciation: / rɪˌfrækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / rɪˌfrækˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
The degree to which a substance or medium refracts light or other electromagnetic waves, expressed as its index of refraction.

Examples

refractivity of glass
Scientists measured the refractivity of the new glass to determine its suitability for lenses.
The refractivity of air changes with temperature and pressure, affecting how light bends through the atmosphere.
Water's high refractivity makes objects appear closer than they actually are when viewed underwater.

Related Terms

  • synonym – refractive index: A measure of how much light is bent when entering a material
