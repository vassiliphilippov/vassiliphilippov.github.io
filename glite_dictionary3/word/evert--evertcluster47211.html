to evert

verb
turn something inside out
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvɝt / (AmE), / ɪˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to turn something inside out or turn the inner surface outward, especially in a medical or anatomical context

Examples

to evert a body part
Certain sea creatures can evert their stomachs to digest food externally.
The surgeon needed to evert the eyelid to examine the tissue underneath.
To clean the pocket thoroughly, gently evert the fabric so the inside is exposed.
