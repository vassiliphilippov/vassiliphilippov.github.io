tallow

noun
hard animal fat used in candles, soap, etc.
Pronunciation: / ˈtæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtaləʊ / (BrE)
a hard, white fat rendered from the suet of animals, especially cattle or sheep, used in making soap, candles, and lubricants

Examples

tallow candle
Early settlers often used tallow to make their own soap.
The factory shipped barrels of tallow for use in industrial lubricants.
The old candle was made of tallow and gave off a distinctive smell.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suet: hard fat from around the kidneys of cattle and sheep
