condonation

noun
forgiveness of an offense as if it never occurred
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.doʊˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dəˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of forgiving or overlooking an offense, treating it as if it had not happened, especially in a legal context

Examples

condonation of wrongdoing
His repeated condonation of her mistakes only encouraged further errors.
Legal condonation can sometimes prevent a spouse from seeking divorce for past misconduct.
The court's condonation of the defendant's actions was met with public outrage.
