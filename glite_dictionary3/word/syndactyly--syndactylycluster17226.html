syndactyly

noun
condition: fused fingers or toes
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈdæktəli / (AmE), / sɪnˈdæktɪli / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which two or more fingers or toes are joined together, either partially or completely, by webbing or fusion

Examples

syndactyly of fingers
Genetic counseling may be recommended for families with a history of syndactyly.
Her newborn son was diagnosed with syndactyly affecting his left hand.
The surgeon explained that syndactyly could often be corrected with an operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – polydactyly: condition of having more than the normal number of fingers or toes
  • synonym – webbed: having the fingers or toes joined by a membrane
