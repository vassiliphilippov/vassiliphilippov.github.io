cascade

noun
sudden outpouring of something
Pronunciation: / kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kasˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a small waterfall, or a sudden, plentiful flow of something resembling a waterfall

Examples

cascade of water
A cascade of sparks erupted from the fireworks display, illuminating the night sky.
She broke down, and a cascade of tears streamed down her face.
There was a cascade of laughter when the comedian delivered his punchline.
A gentle cascade flowed over the moss-covered rocks, creating a soothing sound.
Small fish darted beneath the cool water of the cascade as it tumbled into the pool below.
The hikers were delighted to discover a beautiful cascade hidden in the forest.

  • synonym – waterfall: A steep fall or flow of water in a river or stream
