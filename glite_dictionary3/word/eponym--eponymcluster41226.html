eponym

noun
person after whom something is named
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpəˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛpənɪm / (BrE)
a person after whom something (such as a place, invention, or disease) is named, or the name itself

Examples

eponym of a city
Constantine I is the eponym for Constantinople.
The Alzheimer's disease gets its name from its eponym, Dr. Alois Alzheimer.
The city of Alexandria takes its eponym from Alexander the Great.
Down's syndrome serves as an eponym for the English physician John Down.
The disease known as Parkinson's is an eponym named after Dr. James Parkinson.
The word "sandwich" is an eponym derived from the Earl of Sandwich.
