to derecognise

verb
withdraw official approval or recognition
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈrɛk.əɡˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈrɛk.əɡˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to withdraw official approval or recognition from a person, group, or entity; to cause to be no longer approved or accepted, especially in a formal, legal, or diplomatic context.

Examples

to derecognise an organization
The charity was derecognised by the council due to financial irregularities.
The university decided to derecognise the club after it violated several policies.
When a government derecognises a foreign state, diplomatic relations are usually suspended.

Related Terms

  • antonym – recognise: accept or approve officially
  • synonym – withdraw: to remove or take away, especially officially
