apraxic

adjective
affected by lack of coordinated movements
Pronunciation: / əˈpræksɪk / (AmE), / əˈpraksɪk / (BrE)
Unable to perform coordinated movements as a result of a central nervous system disorder.

Examples

apraxic patient struggles
An apraxic individual may find even routine tasks frustratingly difficult.
Motor tests revealed that John was apraxic, unable to execute simple commands.
The patient became apraxic following the stroke, struggling to tie his shoes.
