sumac

noun
a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus
Pronunciation: / ˈsuː.mæk / (AmE), / ˈsjuː.mæk / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus, especially those species with reddish berries, some of which are used as spices or for ornamental purposes; usually refers to non-poisonous varieties.

Examples

sumac spice powder
Many Middle Eastern dishes use ground sumac as a tangy spice.
The sumac in the garden produces clusters of bright red berries every autumn.
We planted a sumac tree along the edge of the driveway for its vibrant fall colors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – poison ivy: a poisonous plant of the genus Toxicodendron, once considered part of Rhus
