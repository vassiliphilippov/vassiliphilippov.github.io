to overcloud

verb
make unclear or obscure
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklaʊd /; / ˌoʊvɚˈklaʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklaʊd / (BrE)
to make something less clear or harder to understand; to obscure or cloud the meaning of something

Examples

to overcloud the truth
As the years passed, memory began to overcloud the details of the event.
Personal bias may overcloud his ability to judge the facts objectively.
Unnecessary technical jargon can overcloud a simple explanation.
His lengthy explanation only overclouded the main point of the meeting.
The debate became confusing when unrelated issues overclouded the topic at hand.
Try not to overcloud your argument with irrelevant data.
