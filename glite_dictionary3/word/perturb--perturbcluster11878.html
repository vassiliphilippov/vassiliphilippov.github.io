to perturb

verb
to make anxious or unsettled
Pronunciation: / pərˈtɜrb /; / pərˈtɝːb / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːb / (BrE)
to disturb or unsettle someone, making them anxious, uneasy, or confused

Examples

to perturb a person
His constant questioning began to perturb the teacher during the discussion.
She tried not to let the harsh criticism perturb her focus on the project.
The unexpected announcement seemed to perturb even the most confident investors at the meeting.
International events can perturb the balance of power in unexpected ways.
The loud noise outside perturbed her concentration, making it hard to finish her work.
The sudden announcement seemed to perturb even the most experienced staff members.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: to make someone tranquil and free from disturbance
  • synonym – disturb: to interrupt or bother, causing anxiety or discomfort
  • synonym – unsettle: to make someone feel anxious or uneasy
