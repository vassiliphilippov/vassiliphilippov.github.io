to pierce

verb
make a way through; penetrate
Pronunciation: / pɪrs / (AmE), / pɪəs / (BrE)
to make a way through or into something, especially by cutting or penetrating

Examples

to pierce the forest
A narrow path pierced the dense woods, leading to the river.
Sunlight pierced the clouds after the heavy rain.
The headlights pierced the thick fog as the car moved forward.

Related Terms

  • synonym – penetrate: to go into or through something, especially when difficult
  • synonym – perforate: to make a hole through something
