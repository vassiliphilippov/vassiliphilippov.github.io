ghostwriter

noun
someone who writes for another credited as author
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊstˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊstˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to write material for someone else who is named as the author

Examples

ghostwriter for a celebrity
He made his living as a ghostwriter for politicians and business leaders.
Many bestselling books are actually written by a ghostwriter, not the person credited.
The celebrity hired a ghostwriter to help finish her memoir.
