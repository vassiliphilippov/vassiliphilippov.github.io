co-option

noun
selection of a new member by existing group
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɑːpʃən /; / koʊˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
the process of appointing or selecting someone to a committee, board, or group by decision of the current members, rather than by open election

Examples

co-option onto a board
Several committee vacancies were filled through co-option rather than by open election.
Some organizations allow for co-option when an urgent replacement is required.
The club used co-option to fill the vacant seat on its board.
Critics argued that co-option undermined the democratic process by bypassing a vote.
He refused the co-option onto the board, preferring to be elected by his peers.
The committee secured her membership through co-option rather than an open election.
