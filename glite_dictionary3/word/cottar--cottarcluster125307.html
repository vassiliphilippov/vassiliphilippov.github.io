cottar

noun
wedge or pin fastener
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒtə / (BrE)
a fastener consisting of a wedge or pin that is inserted through a slot to hold two other pieces together

Examples

cottar pin fastener
A hammered-in cottar keeps the crank firmly attached in many old bicycles.
The axle was secured by a cottar that prevented it from slipping out of place.
You need to remove the cottar before disassembling the wheel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cotter pin: a split pin used to fasten parts together
