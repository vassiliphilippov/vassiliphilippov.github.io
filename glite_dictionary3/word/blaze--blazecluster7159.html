to blaze

verb
shine with intense brightness
Pronunciation: / bleɪz / (AmE), / bleɪz / (BrE)
to burn or shine with a very bright and intense light or flame

Examples

to blaze with light
A lantern blazed in the distance, guiding us through the darkness.
Her eyes blazed with excitement as she shared the news.
The fireworks began to blaze in the night sky, dazzling the crowd.
Dozens of candles blazed in the old cathedral, casting dancing shadows on the walls.
The campfire continued to blaze long into the night, warming everyone gathered around.
The wildfire blazed across the hillside, threatening homes and forcing people to evacuate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flame: to burn with a visible flame
  • synonym – flare: to burn with a sudden intensity of light or flame
  • synonym – glow: to produce a steady light without flames
  • antonym – smolder: to burn slowly with no flame
