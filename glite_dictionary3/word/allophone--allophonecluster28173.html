allophone

noun
variant sound of a phoneme
Pronunciation: / ˈæləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈaləfəʊn / (BrE)
(Linguistics) any of the different spoken sounds or variants that represent a single phoneme in a language, but do not change meaning.

Examples

allophone of a phoneme
In English, the sounds [t] and [tʰ] are allophones of the phoneme /t/.
Native speakers often do not notice the different allophones in their own language.
The pronunciation of 'l' in 'leaf' and 'feel' shows different allophones.
