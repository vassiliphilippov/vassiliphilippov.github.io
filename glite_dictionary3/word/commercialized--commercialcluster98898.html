commercialized

adjective
done mainly for profit
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɜrʃəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Organized, developed, or done chiefly for financial profit, often losing original value or character as a result.

Examples

commercialized art exhibition
He was disappointed by the commercialized atmosphere of the art exhibition.
Many believe that the once-sacred festival has become far too commercialized in recent years.
Tourist destinations are often commercialized as they become more popular and attract business investments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – commodified: treated as a commodity, especially in a way that loses original value
