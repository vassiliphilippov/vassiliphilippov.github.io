Landsmål

noun
Norwegian language standard based on dialects
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːn(d)ˌsmɔl / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌsmɔːl / (BrE)
one of the two official written standards of the Norwegian language, based largely on rural dialects and later renamed Nynorsk.

Examples

Landsmaal language form
Many government documents were translated into Landsmål after it became an official language.
The debate between supporters of Landsmål and Bokmål has lasted for generations.
The poet wrote his works in Landsmål to capture the spirit of rural Norway.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Bokmål: the other official written standard of Norwegian, based on Danish and urban speech
  • synonym – Nynorsk: the modern name for Landsmål; the New Norwegian written language
