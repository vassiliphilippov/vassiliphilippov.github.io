bagging

noun
ensemble method (bootstrap aggregating)
Pronunciation: / ˈbæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
(in statistics and machine learning) bootstrap aggregating: an ensemble method that trains multiple models on different bootstrap (resampled) subsets of the data and combines their outputs (for example by averaging or voting) to reduce variance and improve stability.

Examples

bagging method models
In machine learning, bagging reduces model variance by training multiple predictors on bootstrap samples.
Researchers applied bagging to improve stability when small changes caused large prediction differences.
Their production pipeline uses bagging with decision trees to increase accuracy on noisy datasets.
