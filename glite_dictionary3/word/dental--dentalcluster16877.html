dental

noun
consonant made with tongue at teeth or gum ridge
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛn.t̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.təl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with the tip of the tongue against or near the upper front teeth or the gum ridge.

Examples

dental sound
Certain languages distinguish between dental and alveolar consonants in their phonetics.
He struggled to pronounce the dental sounds common in Spanish.
The "th" sound in "think" is an example of a dental in English.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alveolar: a consonant articulated with the tongue against the alveolar ridge (ridge behind the upper front teeth)
