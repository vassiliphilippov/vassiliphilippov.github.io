pentatone

noun
five-note musical scale
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛntəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɛntətəʊn / (BrE)
A musical scale consisting of five notes, typically formed by omitting the fourth and seventh degrees of the standard diatonic scale.

Examples

pentatone scale
Learning the pentatone helps beginning musicians to improvise simple yet effective tunes.
Many folk songs around the world are based on the pentatone rather than the full diatonic scale.
Some Asian music traditions make extensive use of the pentatone in their melodies.
