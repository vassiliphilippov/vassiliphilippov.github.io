carry-over

noun
undivided corporate profits carried forward
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the accumulated and undivided profits of a corporation remaining after dividends and reserves have been accounted for

Examples

carry-over of profits
A significant carry-over can indicate financial stability within a corporation.
Auditors reviewed the carry-over to ensure all profits were properly accounted for.
The company's large carry-over provided a buffer for future investments.
