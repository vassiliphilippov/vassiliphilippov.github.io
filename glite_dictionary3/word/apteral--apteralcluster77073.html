apteral

adjective
having columns at one or both ends of a building
Pronunciation: / ˈæp.tər.əl / (AmE), / ˈæp.tər.əl / (BrE)
(of a building, especially a classical temple) having columns at one or both ends but not along the sides.

Examples

apteral temple design
The ancient temple is apteral, with columns gracing only its front facade.
The architect chose an apteral format to create a simpler, more focused entrance.
Unlike the peripteral style, the apteral design omits columns from the sides of the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amphiprostyle: having columns at both ends but not along the sides
  • antonym – peripteral: having a single row of columns on all sides of a building
