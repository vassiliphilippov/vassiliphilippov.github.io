to tenure

verb
grant permanent employment (esp. in academia)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.jɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjə / (BrE)
to grant someone a permanent position, especially as a teacher or professor at a university or college

Examples

to tenure a professor
After years of dedicated research and teaching, Professor Kim was finally tenured by the university.
Many young academics hope to be tenured before they turn forty.
The committee will decide next month whether to tenure the assistant professor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dismiss: remove someone from their job or position
