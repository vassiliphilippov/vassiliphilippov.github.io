detribalisation

noun
abandonment of tribal customs for urban ways
Pronunciation: / diˌtraɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌtraɪbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌtrʌɪbəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n /; / diˌtraɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which people from tribal communities lose their traditional customs, identity, or social structures, often by adopting modern or urban ways of living

Examples

detribalisation of native people
Historians have documented the detribalisation of native populations during the colonial era.
Many indigenous communities struggled with detribalisation due to government assimilation policies.
The effects of detribalisation can be seen in the loss of traditional languages and customs.
Anthropologists have documented the effects of detribalisation on cultural identity in many countries.
The government's assimilation policy led to widespread detribalisation among indigenous populations.
Urban migration often results in the detribalisation of rural communities as traditional practices are lost.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acculturation: the process of adopting the cultural traits or social patterns of another group
  • antonym – tribalisation: the process of making or becoming organized around tribes or tribal customs
