blackleg

noun
person who works during a strike
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌlɛg / (AmE), / ˈblak.lɛg / (BrE)
a person who continues to work or is employed in place of others during a strike; a strikebreaker

Examples

a blackleg during a strike
Calling someone a blackleg was considered a serious insult among the striking miners.
During the dispute, the company hired blacklegs to keep production going.
Several workers accused him of being a blackleg when he refused to join the strike.

  • synonym – scab: a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike; strikebreaker
