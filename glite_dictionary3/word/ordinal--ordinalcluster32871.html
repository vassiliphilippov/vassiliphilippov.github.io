ordinal

adjective
indicating order in a series
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrdənl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪn(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a number or position) indicating the order of something in a series, such as first, second, or third.

Examples

ordinal number position
He explained the difference between ordinal and cardinal numbers to the students.
In the list, each item is assigned an ordinal number.
The contestants finished in ordinal order from first to last.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cardinal: a number indicating quantity rather than order
