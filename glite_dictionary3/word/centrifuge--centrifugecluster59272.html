to centrifuge

verb
to separate by spinning at high speed
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɪˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
to rotate something at high speed in order to separate liquids from solids, or different substances from each other, by centrifugal force

Examples

to centrifuge a sample
Before testing the mixture, they are centrifuging it for ten minutes.
The lab technician will centrifuge the samples before analyzing them.
We centrifuged the blood to separate the plasma from the cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spin: to turn around quickly, especially in a circular motion
