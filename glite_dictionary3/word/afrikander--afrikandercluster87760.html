Afrikander

noun
descendant of Dutch settlers in South Africa
Pronunciation: / ˌæfrɪˈkændər / (AmE), / ˌaf.rɪˈkan.də / (BrE)
a white native of South Africa, particularly the Cape Province, descended from Dutch settlers and typically speaking Afrikaans; also known as an Afrikaner

Examples

an Afrikander family
Many Afrikanders trace their ancestry back to the Dutch settlers of the 17th century.
She identified herself as an Afrikander, proud of her family's heritage and language.
The Afrikander community played a significant role in South African history.
