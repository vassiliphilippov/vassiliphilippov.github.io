mydriasis

noun
abnormal dilation of the pupil
Pronunciation: / maɪˈdraɪəsɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈdrɪəsɪs / (BrE)
the abnormal or excessive dilation of the pupil, especially as a result of drugs, injury, or decreased light

Examples

mydriasis after medication
Certain medications can cause temporary mydriasis as a side effect.
In low light conditions, mydriasis helps more light enter the eye.
The doctor noted mydriasis in the patient's right eye after the head injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym – miosis: abnormal contraction of the pupil
