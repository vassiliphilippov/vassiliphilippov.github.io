superpower

noun
powerful state with global influence
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌpaʊə / (BrE)
A state that is extremely powerful and has the ability to influence events around the world.

Examples

global superpower
During the Cold War, the United States and the Soviet Union were considered superpowers.
Many experts believe that the emergence of new superpowers could shift the global balance of power.
Some countries aspire to achieve superpower status through rapid economic and military growth.
