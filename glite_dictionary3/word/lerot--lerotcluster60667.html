lerot

noun
a dormouse of southern Europe and northern Africa
Pronunciation: / lɪˈroʊ / (AmE), / lɪˈrəʊ / (BrE)
a dormouse species (Eliomys quercinus) found in southern Europe and northern Africa, noted for its distinctive black eye markings and bushy tail.

Examples

wild lerot
The lerot builds its nest in tree holes and feeds on fruits and insects.
The bushy tail and facial markings make the lerot easy to identify in the wild.
We spotted a lerot darting along a stone wall at dusk.
