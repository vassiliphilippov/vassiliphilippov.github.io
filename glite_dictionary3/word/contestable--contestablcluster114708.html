contestable

adjective
able to be disputed or challenged
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛstəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈtɛstəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be disputed or challenged; open to dispute.

Examples

contestable claim
Some of the study's conclusions are contestable, as they lack sufficient supporting evidence.
That interpretation of the law is certainly contestable and may not hold up in court.
The results of the election are highly contestable due to irregularities in the voting process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – indisputable: impossible to dispute or question
