incision

noun
a cut or depression on a surface
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a narrow cut or depression scratched or carved into a surface, especially in stone or metal as decoration or marking

Examples

decorative incision
Ancient pottery often features decorative incisions made with simple tools.
Artists sometimes use a stylus to create fine incision marks on metal plates.
The archaeologist examined the faint incisions on the cave wall for clues about early humans.
