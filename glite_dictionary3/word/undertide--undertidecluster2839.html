undertide

noun
subsurface water current
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌtaɪd / (BrE)
a current flowing beneath the surface layer of a body of water

Examples

strong undertide
Divers must be cautious of the undertide when exploring coral reefs.
The undertide can be strong and unpredictable in this part of the river.
Watch for signs of a strong undertide before swimming far from shore.

Related Terms

  • synonym – undertow: a current below and moving in a different direction from a surface current, especially when near the shore
