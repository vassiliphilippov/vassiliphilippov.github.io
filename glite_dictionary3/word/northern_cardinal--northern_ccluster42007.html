northern cardinal

noun
North American songbird with a crest
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrðərn ˈkɑrdənəl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈkɑːdɪn(ə)l / (BrE)
a North American songbird with a crest and a thick bill, found especially in wooded areas; the male has bright red plumage and the female is mostly brown with red highlights.

Examples

northern cardinal singing in trees
A bright red northern cardinal perched on the backyard feeder, attracting everyone's attention.
Male northern cardinals are famous for their striking red feathers and black face mask.
The song of the northern cardinal is commonly heard across much of eastern North America.
