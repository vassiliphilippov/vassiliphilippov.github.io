vaporisation

noun
process of becoming vapor
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪpərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveɪpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a substance changes from a liquid or solid into a vapor.

Examples

vaporisation of water
During the experiment, the vaporisation of the chemical was carefully measured.
Efficient vaporisation is crucial in the operation of steam engines.
The vaporisation of water begins to occur rapidly at 100°C.

Related Terms

  • antonym – condensation: the process of vapor turning back into liquid
  • synonym – evaporation: the process of a liquid turning into vapor, usually at a temperature below its boiling point
