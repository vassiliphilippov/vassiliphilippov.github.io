to gutter

verb
flow in thin, irregular streams
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow in a thin, irregular stream or streams, often down a surface.

Examples

water guttered down
Rainwater guttered along the cracked windowpane during the storm.
Tears guttered down her face as she remembered the tragedy.
Wax guttered down the candle, leaving uneven trails on the table.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trickle: to flow or fall in a thin, slow, or irregular stream
