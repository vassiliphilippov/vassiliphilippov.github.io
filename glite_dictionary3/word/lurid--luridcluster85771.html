lurid

adjective
gruesome; sensationally shocking
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊr.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈljʊə.rɪd / (BrE)
shocking or sensational, especially because of explicit details about crime or scandal

Examples

lurid crime story
She was fascinated by lurid stories of criminal exploits and scandals.
The newspaper described the lurid details of the crime in shocking detail.
The trial exposed a lurid side of the city's nightlife that few people had seen.
She was fascinated by lurid stories of scandal and crime.
The movie is full of lurid violence that some viewers found disturbing.
The newspaper was criticized for publishing lurid details about the murder case.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ghastly: horribly shocking or frightful
  • synonym – gruesome: causing horror or disgust; grisly
  • antonym – pleasant: giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
  • antonym – restrained: subtle or not excessively showy; the opposite of lurid in this sense
  • synonym – sensational: causing great public interest and excitement
