to bumble

verb
move awkwardly and unsteadily
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to move or walk in an awkward, unsteady, or confused manner, often tripping or staggering

Examples

to bumble down stairs
After spraining his ankle, he could only bumble down the hallway, clinging to the wall for support.
In the darkness, she bumbled around the room searching for the light switch.
The children bumble across the uneven field, laughing as they trip over tufts of grass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stumble: to trip or lose one's balance while walking or running
  • synonym – totter: to move in a feeble or unsteady way
