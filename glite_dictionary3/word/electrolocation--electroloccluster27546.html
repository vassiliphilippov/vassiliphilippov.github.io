electrolocation

noun
ability to locate objects via electric fields
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrələʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability of certain animals to detect and locate objects in their environment by sensing natural electric fields

Examples

fish electrolocation prey
Scientists study how certain fish use electrolocation to navigate and find prey in murky waters.
Some aquatic animals rely on electrolocation rather than sight to understand their surroundings.
The ability of sharks to detect hidden animals is due in part to electrolocation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electroreception: the detection of electric fields by living organisms
