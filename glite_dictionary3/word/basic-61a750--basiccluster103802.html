basic

adjective
slang: unoriginal; mainstream
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsɪk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk / (BrE)
Informal, slang: unoriginal, predictable, or overly conventional, especially used to criticise someone's tastes, behaviour, or choices as mainstream and lacking imagination.

Examples

basic style
Many critics called the album basic, arguing it offered few original sounds or ideas.
She admitted to liking pumpkin spice and considered herself a little basic among friends.
Rather than risky design, the brand released yet another basic black tee that sold quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mainstream: conventional or widely accepted by the general public
  • antonym – original: new, inventive, or not derived from something else
  • synonym – unoriginal: lacking new or different ideas; not original
