solid

noun
firm or stable state of matter
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑlɪd /; / ˈsɑː.lɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd /; / ˈsɒl.ɪd / (BrE)
a substance or material that is not a liquid or gas; a state of matter with a definite shape and volume

Examples

solid state
At room temperature, iron is a solid.
The chemist compared the characteristics of the solid and the liquid.
Water can exist as a solid, liquid, or gas depending on temperature.
At room temperature, water exists as a liquid, but ice is the solid form.
Diamond is a solid with an extremely high melting point.
The experiment compared the properties of the material in its solid and liquid states.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gas: a substance in a state in which it expands freely to fill any available space
  • antonym – liquid: a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
