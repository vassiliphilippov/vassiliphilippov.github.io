marsupial

noun
mammal with a pouch for young
Pronunciation: / mɑrˈsuːpiəl / (AmE), / mɑːˈsuːpiəl / (BrE)
A mammal whose females carry and feed their young in a pouch on the abdomen.

Examples

marsupial mother pouch
Most marsupials give birth to tiny, undeveloped young that crawl into their mother's pouch.
The kangaroo is perhaps the most famous marsupial native to Australia.
The koala, another well-known marsupial, spends much of its life in eucalyptus trees.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monotreme: egg-laying mammal such as the platypus
  • antonym – placental: mammal whose young develop inside the womb with a placenta
