grummet

noun
metal ring for reinforcing a hole
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌmət / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmɪt / (BrE)
A metal ring used to reinforce a small hole, allowing cords or lines to pass through and be attached.

Examples

grummet in a sail
Sailmakers attached a grummet to reinforce the eyelet at the sail's corner.
The canvas cover was secured with cords threaded through each grummet.
Without a proper grummet, the rope quickly frayed against the sharp edge of the hole.
