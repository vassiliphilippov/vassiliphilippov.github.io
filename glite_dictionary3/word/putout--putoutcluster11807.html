putout

noun
baseball out from fielding
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈpʊt.aʊt / (BrE)
(in baseball) an out made by a defensive player through a fielding play, rather than by a strikeout.

Examples

putout by a fielder
After the fly ball was caught, the outfielder tallied another putout for his stats.
His quick reflexes contributed to a difficult putout at second base.
The shortstop recorded six putouts during yesterday's game.

Related Terms

  • antonym – strikeout: an out caused by three strikes in baseball
