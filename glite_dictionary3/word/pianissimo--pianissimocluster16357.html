pianissimo

noun
(music) very soft or quiet passage
Pronunciation: / piˌɑːnɪˈsɪmoʊ / (AmE), / piˌænɪˈsɪməʊ / (BrE)
(in music) a direction to play very softly; the passage of music to be performed at a very low volume.

Examples

pianissimo marking in music
The composer marked this section pianissimo to create a sense of delicate calm.
The haunting melody begins at pianissimo and gradually grows louder.
The orchestra played the final bars pianissimo, barely above a whisper.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forte: (music) loud or strong; direction to play loudly
  • degree higher – mezzo-forte: (music) moderately loud
  • degree higher – piano: (music) softly; a direction to play softly
