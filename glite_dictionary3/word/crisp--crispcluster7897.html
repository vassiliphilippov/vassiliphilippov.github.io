crisp

adjective
concise, to-the-point (speech or writing)
Pronunciation: / krɪsp / (AmE), / krɪsp / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or something seen or heard) clear, sharply defined, and concise

Examples

crisp clear answer
He gave a crisp answer that left no room for doubt.
Her report was praised for its crisp prose and straightforward conclusions.
The manager's crisp instructions made the task easy to understand.
Her voice was crisp and clear over the loudspeaker, easy for everyone to understand.
On a clear morning, the mountain appeared in crisp detail against the sky.
The photographer admired the crisp lines of the newly developed picture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – curt: rudely brief in speech or manner
  • antonym – fuzzy: lacking clarity or distinctness, blurred
  • synonym – sharp: clear and distinct, especially in sound or image
  • synonym – terse: using few words, often in a way that seems unfriendly
  • antonym – verbose: using more words than needed; wordy
