QCD

noun
theory of strong interactions in particle physics
Pronunciation: / ˌkjuː siː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌkjuː siː ˈdiː / (BrE)
abbreviation for quantum chromodynamics, a theory describing the strong interaction between quarks and gluons, fundamental to the structure of protons and neutrons.

Examples

QCD theory in physics
Graduate students are required to take a course in QCD to understand the behavior of hadrons.
Lattice QCD calculations are used to predict the masses of exotic particles.
Recent experiments have provided more evidence supporting QCD as the standard model for strong interactions.
