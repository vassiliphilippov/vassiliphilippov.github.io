leucopenia

noun
abnormally low white blood cell count
Pronunciation: / ˌluːkəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˌljuːkəˈpiːniə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an abnormally low number of white blood cells in the blood.

Examples

leucopenia after chemotherapy
A blood test revealed leucopenia in the patient after her treatment.
Chemotherapy can sometimes cause leucopenia, making patients more susceptible to infection.
Doctors monitor white blood cell counts to watch for signs of leucopenia in immunocompromised individuals.

Related Terms

  • antonym – leukocytosis: abnormally high white blood cell count
  • synonym – leukopenia: abnormally low white blood cell count; American English spelling
