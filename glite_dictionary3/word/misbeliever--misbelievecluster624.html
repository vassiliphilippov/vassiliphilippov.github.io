misbeliever

noun
person with unorthodox religious beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsbɪˈliːvər / (AmE), / ˌmɪsbɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
a person whose religious beliefs differ from the accepted doctrine of the Roman Catholic Church; a heretic or unbeliever.

Examples

a misbeliever faces punishment
In medieval times, a misbeliever could face severe punishment for their views.
The inquisitors accused several villagers of being misbelievers during the trial.
To the devout, anyone questioning church dogma was labeled a misbeliever.
