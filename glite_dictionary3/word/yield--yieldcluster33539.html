to yield

verb
move aside to make room
Pronunciation: / jiːld / (AmE), / jiːld / (BrE)
to move or allow oneself to be moved in order to make space for someone or something

Examples

to yield the right-of-way
As the two groups approached, one side decided to yield and made space for the other.
Please yield if someone needs to get past you in the aisle.
The driver refused to yield to oncoming traffic at the intersection.

Related Terms

  • synonym – give way: move aside or make space for someone or something
  • synonym – make room: move to allow space for another
  • antonym – stand firm: be unwilling to move aside or give up space
