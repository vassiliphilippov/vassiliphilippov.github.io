to gull

verb
to deceive or trick
Pronunciation: / ɡʌl / (AmE), / ɡʌl / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone; to fool them

Examples

to gull a victim
He tried to gull investors with a fake business scheme.
She warned her friends not to be gulled by emails claiming they had won a lottery.
Tourists are often gulled by street vendors selling counterfeit goods.
He was clever enough not to be gulled by their flattery.
Many investors were gulled by the scheme's promises of quick riches.
She managed to gull him into believing her story.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deceive: to make someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym – dupe: to deceive or trick, especially by misleading
  • antonym – enlighten: to give someone greater knowledge or understanding, opposite of deceive
  • synonym – trick: to deceive someone, often as a joke or for personal gain
