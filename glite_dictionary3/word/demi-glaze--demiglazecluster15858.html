demi-glaze

noun
rich French brown sauce
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛmiˈɡleɪz / (AmE), / ˌdɛmiˈɡleɪz / (BrE)
A rich brown sauce in French cuisine, made by simmering sauce Espagnole with extra beef stock until reduced and seasoned with wine or sherry.

Examples

demi-glaze sauce for steak
He drizzled demi-glaze over the steak for added flavor.
The chef prepared a demi-glaze to accompany the roasted duck breast.
You can create a delicious demi-glaze by reducing beef stock with wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – demi-glace: the French spelling for the same rich, reduced brown sauce
