comparative

noun
grammar term: higher degree of adjective/adverb
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpæɹ.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpæɹ.ət.ɪv / (BrE)
the form of an adjective or adverb that expresses a higher degree of the quality, usually formed in English by adding '-er', or by using 'more' or 'less' before the word

Examples

comparative form
In the sentence 'She is taller than her brother,' 'taller' is a comparative.
Some adjectives form the comparative by using 'more' or 'less' before the word.
Teachers often ask students to identify the comparative forms in a list of adjectives.
