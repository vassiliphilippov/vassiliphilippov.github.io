bill

noun
a cutting tool with a hooked blade
Pronunciation: / bɪl / (AmE), / bɪl / (BrE)
a tool with a curved or hooked blade used for cutting branches or pruning trees and shrubs

Examples

pruning bill
He sharpened the blade of the bill before heading out to prune the orchard.
Medieval soldiers often carried a bill as a weapon in addition to its use as a tool.
The gardener reached for his bill to clear the undergrowth near the hedge.
