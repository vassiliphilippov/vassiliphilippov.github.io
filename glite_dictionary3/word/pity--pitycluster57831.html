to pity

verb
feel sympathy for someone’s suffering
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
to feel or express sympathy for someone else’s suffering or misfortune

Examples

to pity a friend
I pitied him when I heard about his loss, but there was little I could do to help.
She couldn't help but pity the injured bird as it struggled to fly.
They pitied the homeless man, offering him food and warm clothing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sympathize: to feel or express compassion; to share in someone's feelings
