to superimpose

verb
place one thing over another
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərɪmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to place or lay one thing over another, typically so that both are still visible

Examples

to superimpose text on image
The editor was able to superimpose the text onto the background image for the cover.
They superimposed old maps over current satellite images to study changes in the landscape.
When you superimpose the graphs, it's easier to compare the data trends.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overlay: to cover the surface of something with a layer
  • antonym – underlie: to lie or be situated under something
