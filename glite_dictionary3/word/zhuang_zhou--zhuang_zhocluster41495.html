Zhuang Zhou

proper noun
4th-century Chinese philosopher, founder of Taoist ideas
Pronunciation: / dʒwɑŋ dʒoʊ / (AmE), / dʒwɑːŋ dzəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 4th century BCE) A Chinese philosopher whose works and ideas form a major foundation of Taoist philosophy.
