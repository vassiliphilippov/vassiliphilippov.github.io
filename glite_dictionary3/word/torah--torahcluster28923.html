Torah

noun
Jewish sacred scroll; first five books of Hebrew Bible
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔːrə / (AmE), / ˈtɔːrə / (BrE)
The scroll containing the first five books of the Hebrew Scriptures, used in Jewish religious services.

Examples

Torah scroll in synagogue
Every synagogue has at least one Torah that is read on special occasions.
He studied the Torah to deepen his understanding of Jewish law.
The rabbi carefully unrolled the Torah during the service.
