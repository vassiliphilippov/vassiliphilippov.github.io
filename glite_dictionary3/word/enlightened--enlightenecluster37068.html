enlightened

adjective
showing understanding or insight
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈlaɪtnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtnd / (BrE)
having or showing a full understanding of a situation or problem; showing insight or awareness.

Examples

enlightened leader
An enlightened leader listens to the concerns of the people.
She appreciated working for such an enlightened employer.
The new policy reflects a more enlightened approach to education.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignorant: lacking knowledge or awareness
  • synonym – informed: having knowledge of a subject
