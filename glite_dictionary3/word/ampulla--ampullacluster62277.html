ampulla

noun
dilated part of a bodily canal or duct
Pronunciation: / æmˈpʌlə / (AmE), / æmˈpʌlə / (BrE)
A dilated, flask-like portion of a canal or duct in the body, especially in the semicircular canals of the ear.

Examples

the ampulla of a duct
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully examined the ampulla for any signs of blockage.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the ampulla within the ear.
The ampulla of the semicircular canal plays a crucial role in our sense of balance.
