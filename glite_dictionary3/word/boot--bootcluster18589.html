to boot

verb
to kick forcefully
Pronunciation: / buːt / (AmE), / buːt / (BrE)
to kick someone or something forcefully, especially with the foot

Examples

to boot a ball
He was so angry that he booted the ball across the field.
If you don't move quickly, the coach might boot you off the team.
The player booted his opponent in the shin during the match.
