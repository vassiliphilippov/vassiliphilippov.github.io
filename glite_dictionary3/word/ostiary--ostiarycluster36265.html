ostiary

noun
doorkeeper or usher, especially in a church
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊstiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪəri / (BrE)
a person appointed to guard the entrance of a church or building, especially an usher or doorkeeper in early Christian churches

Examples

ostiary opens door
During the ceremony, the ostiary quietly opened the heavy door for the bishop.
In early Christian communities, the ostiary was responsible for guarding the entrance to sacred spaces.
The ancient church appointed an ostiary to welcome worshippers and keep order at the entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – doorkeeper: a person who guards or controls access to a door
  • synonym – usher: a person who shows people to their seats, especially in a church or theater
