thyrotoxicosis

noun
excess thyroid hormone condition
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪ.roʊˌtɑk.sɪˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌθaɪ.rəʊ.tɒk.sɪˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by the excessive production of thyroid hormones by the thyroid gland, often leading to symptoms such as rapid heartbeat and weight loss.

Examples

thyrotoxicosis symptoms appear
Her doctor explained that thyrotoxicosis results from an overactive thyroid gland producing too much hormone.
The patient was diagnosed with thyrotoxicosis after presenting symptoms such as rapid heart rate and excessive sweating.
Untreated thyrotoxicosis can lead to serious complications, including heart problems and bone loss.
