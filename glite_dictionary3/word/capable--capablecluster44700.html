capable

adjective
permitting; open to something
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a thing or situation) potentially susceptible to or allowing a specified action or state; open to.

Examples

capable of causing change
Such an agreement is capable of causing confusion among those involved.
The data is capable of being misread if examined out of context.
This statement is capable of several interpretations depending on the reader's perspective.

Related Terms

  • synonym – open: allowing or susceptible to something (as in 'open to interpretation')
  • synonym – susceptible: likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
