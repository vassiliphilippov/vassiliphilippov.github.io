to board

verb
get onto a vehicle
Pronunciation: / bɔrd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
to get onto or enter a vehicle such as a train, bus, ship, or aircraft

Examples

to board a plane
Passengers are asked to board the train five minutes before departure.
She boarded the bus and found a seat near the back.
We will board the plane as soon as the gate opens.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disembark: to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle
  • synonym – embark: to go on board a ship or aircraft
