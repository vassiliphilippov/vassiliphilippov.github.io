submission

noun
legal argument or statement
Pronunciation: / səbˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) an argument or statement presented by a lawyer to a judge or jury as part of a case.

Examples

legal submission
During the trial, her submission was that the contract was not enforceable.
The defense counsel made a submission that the evidence should be excluded.
The judge invited final submissions from both parties before reaching a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym – argument: a reason or set of reasons presented to support a case or opinion
