aliyah

noun
being called to read from the Torah in synagogue
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈliːə / (AmE), / ɑːˈliːə / (BrE)
(Judaism) the honor of being called up in the synagogue to recite or read a portion of the Torah during a service.

Examples

receive an aliyah honor
Each week, different members of the congregation are given an aliyah during the service.
It is customary for those celebrating a special occasion to receive an aliyah in the synagogue.
She felt deeply honored when she was invited for an aliyah at her nephew's bar mitzvah.
