menstruum

noun
menstrual discharge; menstruation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnstruːəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstruːəm / (BrE)
the monthly discharge of blood from the uterus of nonpregnant women, occurring from puberty to menopause; menstruation

Examples

monthly menstruum flow
In ancient medical texts, the term menstruum frequently appears instead of the modern word menstruation.
References to a woman's menstruum can often be found in historical literature and documents.
The regular flow of the menstruum was once considered essential for a woman's health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – menses: the monthly flow of blood and cellular debris from the uterus
  • synonym – menstruation: the monthly process of discharging blood from the uterus
