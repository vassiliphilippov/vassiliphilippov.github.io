gastrin

noun
hormone stimulating stomach acid
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæstrɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪn / (BrE)
a polypeptide hormone produced by the stomach lining that stimulates the secretion of gastric acid, especially in response to the presence of food

Examples

gastrin hormone release
Abnormally high gastrin may indicate a disorder of the stomach lining.
Doctors measured the patient’s gastrin levels to diagnose a possible ulcer.
The release of gastrin increases gastric acid production after eating.

Related Terms

  • synonym – secretin: hormone that stimulates the pancreas and regulates stomach acid
  • antonym – somatostatin: hormone that inhibits the secretion of gastrin and other hormones
