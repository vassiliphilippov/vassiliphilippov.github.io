Seminole

noun
member of a Native American group in Florida
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmɪˌnoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəʊl / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally from the southeastern United States, especially Florida, descended from Muskhogean-speaking tribes.

Examples

Seminole tribe member
He learned about the history of the Seminole people in his American studies class.
Several Seminole traditions are still practiced in parts of Florida today.
The Seminole resisted forced relocation during the Second Seminole War.
