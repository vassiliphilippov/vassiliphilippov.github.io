commensal

noun
organism living in association with another without dependency
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛn.səl / (AmE), / kəˈmɛn.səl / (BrE)
an animal or plant that lives in close association with another of a different species, without being dependent on it or harming it

Examples

commensal animal with host
A commensal may benefit from the host's presence without providing any return benefit.
In the study, scientists identified several commensals living alongside coral reefs.
The barnacle is a classic commensal that attaches to whales without affecting them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mutualist: an organism that is part of a relationship where both partners benefit
  • antonym – parasite: an organism that lives in or on another organism and benefits at the host's expense
  • synonym – symbiont: an organism living in a close, often long-term, biological interaction with another
