to play

verb
move or operate freely within limits (esp. in machinery)
Pronunciation: / pleɪ / (AmE), / pleɪ / (BrE)
(of a part, especially in a machine) to move or operate freely within certain limits and without being tightly fixed.

Examples

to play in socket
If the axle plays in the socket, it may cause uneven wear.
The gear must play smoothly in its housing for the mechanism to work.
There was too much play in the joint, so it had to be tightened.
