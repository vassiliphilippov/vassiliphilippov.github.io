outwork

noun
outer defensive structure of a fortification
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a subsidiary defensive structure situated outside the main area of a fortification, often used to provide extra protection.

Examples

Archaeologists discovered the remains of an ancient outwork surrounding the city.
During the siege, the enemy first attacked the outwork before reaching the main fortress walls.
