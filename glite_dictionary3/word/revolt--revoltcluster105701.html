revolt

noun
organized rebellion against authority
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvoʊlt / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊlt / (BrE)
an organized attempt to oppose and remove the authority of a government or ruler, often using force

Examples

a revolt by workers
Many were imprisoned for participating in the revolt against the monarchy.
The revolt finally ended after months of fierce fighting and negotiation.
The government struggled to suppress the revolt that spread through the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rebellion: an act of violent or open resistance to an established government or ruler
  • antonym – submission: the action of accepting or yielding to a superior force or authority
  • synonym – uprising: an act of resistance or revolt, usually by a group against authority
