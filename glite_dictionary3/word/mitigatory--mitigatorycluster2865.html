mitigatory

adjective
serving to lessen pain or sorrow
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪtəˌɡeɪtɔri / (AmE), / ˈmɪtɪɡətəri / (BrE)
Having the effect of making pain, sorrow, or a negative situation less severe or easier to bear.

Examples

mitigatory effect
His apology had a mitigatory effect on the tense atmosphere in the room.
The doctor prescribed a mitigatory treatment to help manage her chronic pain.
They argued that there were mitigatory circumstances surrounding his actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – aggravating: making a situation or feeling worse
  • synonym – mitigating: having the effect of making less severe
