to dry-dock

verb
bring a ship into a drydock
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌdɒk / (BrE)
to bring (a ship or boat) into a drydock in order to carry out repairs, cleaning, or inspection below the waterline.

Examples

to dry-dock a ship
Every five years, we dry-dock the ferry to inspect its hull and repaint it.
The crew had to dry-dock the vessel for urgent repairs after the accident.
They are dry-docking the container ship next week for a major overhaul.
