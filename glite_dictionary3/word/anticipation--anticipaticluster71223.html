anticipation

noun
the act of predicting or expecting
Pronunciation: / ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expecting or predicting something, often by reasoning about what will happen in the future

Examples

careful anticipation
She made her plans with careful anticipation of possible obstacles.
The team waited in anticipation for the judge's decision.
There was a feeling of anticipation in the air before the concert began.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expectation: a strong belief that something will happen
  • antonym – surprise: an unexpected event or fact
