Marantaceae

noun
family of tropical perennial herbs (Marantaceae)
Pronunciation: / ˌmærənˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌmærænˈteɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical perennial herbs with usually starchy underground stems (rhizomes), including the arrowroot and prayer plants.

Examples

Marantaceae family plants
Botanists have classified arrowroot under the Marantaceae family due to its distinctive characteristics.
Many plants in the Marantaceae family are prized for their ornamental foliage.
The starchy roots of some Marantaceae species are used as food in tropical regions.
