candour

noun
honesty; openness
Pronunciation: / ˈkændər / (AmE), / ˈkændə / (BrE)
the quality of being open, honest, and straightforward in speech or expression

Examples

speak with candour
He spoke with refreshing candour about the challenges he faced.
Her candour during the interview impressed everyone on the panel.
Sometimes candour can be mistaken for rudeness if not delivered thoughtfully.
His candour in the meeting earned him the respect of his colleagues.
Journalists admired the politician's rare candour during the press conference.
She spoke with remarkable candour about her mistakes and regrets.

Related Terms

  • synonym – candor: openness and honesty in expression; chiefly American spelling
  • antonym – deceit: the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • synonym – honesty: the quality of being truthful and sincere
  • antonym – insincerity: the quality of being not honest or genuine
