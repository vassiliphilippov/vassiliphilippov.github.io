to garner

verb
to acquire or collect, especially by effort
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrnər / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnə / (BrE)
to collect, obtain, or gather something, especially after effort

Examples

to garner support
He worked hard to garner support for the new policy.
Her research has garnered widespread recognition in academic circles.
The novel garnered praise from critics and readers alike.
He worked hard to garner valuable experience in the field over several years.
She managed to garner enough support to launch her new project.
The movie garnered critical acclaim from reviewers worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acquire: to gain or obtain something
  • synonym – collect: to bring things together from different places or people
  • synonym – earn: to receive or deserve as a result of effort
  • synonym – gather: to bring things or people together in one place
  • antonym – lose: to fail to keep or maintain; to have taken away from you
