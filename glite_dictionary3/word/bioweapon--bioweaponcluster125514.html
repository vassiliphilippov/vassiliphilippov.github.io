bioweapon

noun
weapon using biological agents
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.oʊˌwɛp.ən / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊˌwɛp.ən / (BrE)
A weapon that uses biological agents, such as bacteria or viruses, to cause harm or death to people, animals, or plants.

Examples

deadly bioweapon attack
He argued that using a bioweapon would have catastrophic global consequences.
International treaties ban the development and stockpiling of bioweapons.
The government increased security at labs to prevent the theft of bioweapons materials.

Related Terms
