self-induction

noun
generation of EMF in a circuit by current change
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the production of an electromotive force in a circuit when the current in that circuit changes, usually measured in henries

Examples

self-induction in a circuit
Rapid switching in circuits can cause large voltage spikes due to self-induction.
The principle of self-induction is essential in the design of transformers and inductors.
The unit of self-induction is called the henry.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mutual induction: induction of EMF in one circuit by another nearby circuit
