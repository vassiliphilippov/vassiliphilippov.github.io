Dominican

adjective
of Saint Dominic or the Dominican order
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɪnɪkən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnɪkən / (BrE)
of or relating to Saint Dominic or the Dominican order, a Roman Catholic religious order founded by Saint Dominic in the early 13th century.

Examples

Dominican monastery
He admired the architecture of the Dominican monastery in the old city.
Several Dominican priests contributed to the university's founding.
The Dominican tradition emphasizes preaching and teaching.
