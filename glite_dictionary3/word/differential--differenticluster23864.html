differential

noun
instantaneous rate of change in calculus
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a quantity representing the instantaneous rate of change of one variable with respect to another, especially as the result of differentiation in calculus

Examples

a differential in calculus
In advanced calculus, the concept of a differential provides a foundation for integration.
The differential of y with respect to x is commonly written as dy/dx.
To solve the equation, first compute the differential of the function.

Related Terms

  • synonym – derivative: the measure of how a function changes as its input changes; result of differentiation
