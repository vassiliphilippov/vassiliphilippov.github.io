tarantism

noun
disorder marked by dancing after spider bite
Pronunciation: / təˈræn.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / təˈræn.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A nervous disorder, historically believed to be caused by the bite of a tarantula or wolf spider, characterized by an uncontrollable urge to dance.

Examples

tarantism dance frenzy
In the 17th century, outbreaks of tarantism led to community-wide dance rituals in southern Italy.
Some historians suggest that tarantism was a social phenomenon rather than a genuine medical condition.
The legend of tarantism fascinated many, with reports of victims dancing wildly to cure themselves.
