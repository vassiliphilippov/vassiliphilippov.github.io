plain

adjective, adverb
obvious or clear; easy to perceive
Pronunciation: / pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
clear, obvious, or easy to understand; expressed in a direct way (also: clearly, obviously)

Examples

plain language
His disappointment was plain to see.
It was plain from her expression that she was upset.
The solution to the problem became plain after he explained it.
She was plain delighted by the surprise party.
The evidence is plain there for anyone to see.
You are plain wrong about how this works.
His memo was written in plain language, leaving no room for misunderstanding.
She gave a plain answer, not attempting to sugarcoat the facts.
Sometimes the plain truth is exactly what people need to hear, even if it stings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ambiguous: open to more than one interpretation; not clear
  • synonym – apparent: clearly visible or understood; obvious
  • synonym – candid: truthful and straightforward
  • antonym – evasive: tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, indirect
  • synonym – obvious: easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • synonym – obviously: in a way that is easy to see or understand; clearly
  • synonym – plainly: clearly; in a way that is easy to see, hear, or understand
  • synonym – unvarnished: not embellished, straightforward and direct
