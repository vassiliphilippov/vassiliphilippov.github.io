dried-out

adjective
thoroughly dry; lacking moisture
Pronunciation: / ˌdraɪdˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌdraɪdˈaʊt / (BrE)
thoroughly dry, having lost all or most moisture.

Examples

dried-out leaves
After being left in the sun for days, the leaves were completely dried-out.
She threw away the dried-out markers that no longer worked.
The old wooden fence became dried-out and started to crack.

Related Terms

  • synonym – desiccated: thoroughly dried, especially by removal of moisture
  • antonym – moist: slightly wet; not completely dry
