to process

verb
move or march in formal procession
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
to move or march in a formal line, especially as part of a ceremony or procession

Examples

to process down the aisle
Dignitaries processed solemnly through the city in honor of the event.
The choir processed down the aisle at the start of the service.
The graduates processed into the auditorium as music played.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parade: to march or walk in a formal group, often ceremonially
  • antonym – recess: to move out in a formal or ceremonial procession
