impolitic

adjective
not wise or prudent
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɑlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒlɪtɪk / (BrE)
not wise or prudent; not showing good judgment, especially in a particular situation

Examples

impolitic decision
Declining the invitation was seen as an impolitic move by the committee.
His impolitic remark caused discomfort in the room.
It would have been impolitic to voice her doubts at that moment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – politic: wise or prudent, especially in practical matters
  • synonym – prudent: acting with care and thought for the future
