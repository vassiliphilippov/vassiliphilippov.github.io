sensitisation

noun
increased psychological sensitivity to stimuli
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychology) the process by which a person or organism becomes increasingly responsive or sensitive to certain events or stimuli, especially emotional ones.

Examples

sensitisation to emotional events
After the traumatic event, she experienced sensitisation to loud noises and crowds.
Repeated exposure to violence in media can lead to sensitisation to aggressive behavior.
Therapists monitor patients for sensitisation during exposure therapy for anxiety disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitisation: the process of becoming less sensitive to a stimulus or event
