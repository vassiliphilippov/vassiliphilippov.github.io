pompous

adjective
excessively grand or self-important
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːmpəs /; / ˈpɑːm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpəs /; / ˈpɒm.pəs / (BrE)
having or showing an exaggerated sense of self-importance, often in a pretentious or overly formal way

Examples

pompous speech
He walked into the room with a pompous air, as if expecting applause.
The ceremony was unnecessarily pompous, complete with trumpets and elaborate robes.
The speech sounded far too pompous for such a simple occasion.
Critics called the ceremony overly formal and pompous.
He gave a pompous speech that made everyone roll their eyes.
The professor's pompous attitude discouraged students from asking questions.
