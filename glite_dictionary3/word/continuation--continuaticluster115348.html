continuation

noun
act or result of prolonging something
Pronunciation: / kənˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkənˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of something continuing or being extended without stopping

Examples

continuation of a project
She was grateful for the continuation of her scholarship for another year.
The continuation of the talks was agreed upon by all parties after a short break.
Unforeseen events forced the continuation of the project beyond its original deadline.
She welcomed the continuation of peaceful negotiations.
The continuation of the project depends on additional funding.
Unforeseen circumstances threatened the continuation of the concert.

  • antonym – interruption: a break in continuity or duration
  • antonym – resumption: the act of starting again after a pause
