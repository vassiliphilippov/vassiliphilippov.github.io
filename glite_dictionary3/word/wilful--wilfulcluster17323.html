wilful

adjective
deliberate; intentional (especially negative)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfʊl / (BrE)
deliberately doing something, especially when it is disobedient or goes against rules or authority

Examples

wilful disobedience
Her wilful disregard for the rules led to her suspension from school.
His wilful decision to ignore the warning put everyone at risk.
The court found him guilty of wilful damage to property.
He was dismissed for his wilful disregard of company policies.
Her wilful refusal to follow the rules frustrated her teachers.
The child's wilful behavior often led to conflict at home.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accidental: happening by chance, not planned
  • synonym – intentional: done on purpose; deliberate
  • synonym – willful: Deliberately disobedient or stubborn (American English spelling)
