tendentiously

adverb
in a biased or partisan manner
Pronunciation: / tɛnˈdɛnʃəsli / (AmE), / tɛnˈdɛnʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a strong and deliberate bias or tendency, especially in support of a particular point of view.

Examples

tendentiously present facts
He accused the documentary of presenting facts tendentiously to sway public opinion.
The article was written tendentiously, favoring one side of the debate over the other.
The news channel reported the event tendentiously, omitting key details.

Related Terms

  • antonym – objectively: in a way not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
  • synonym – tendentious: expressing or intending to promote a particular cause or point of view, especially a controversial one
