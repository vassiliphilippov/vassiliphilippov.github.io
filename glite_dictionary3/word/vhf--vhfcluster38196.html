VHF

noun
radio frequency range (30–300 MHz)
Pronunciation: / ˌviː ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌviː ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (BrE)
a range of radio frequencies from 30 to 300 megahertz, commonly used for television, radio, and communications

Examples

VHF radio signal
Many television and radio stations broadcast on the VHF band.
Older TV antennas are often designed to receive VHF signals.
The pilot switched to the VHF radio to communicate with air traffic control.
