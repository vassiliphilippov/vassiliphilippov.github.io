to heel

verb
follow closely behind
Pronunciation: / hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to follow closely behind someone, usually at the heels.

Examples

to heel beside
He told his excited puppy to heel during their walk in the park.
The detective instructed his partner to heel as they entered the building.
The guide dog was trained to heel at his master's side at all times.
