module

noun
detachable spacecraft compartment
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.juːl / (BrE)
a detachable compartment of a spacecraft, designed for a specific purpose such as crew living space, experiments, or propulsion

Examples

space module
During re-entry, the command module separated from the rest of the spacecraft.
Engineers designed a new module to be attached to the International Space Station.
The lunar module safely landed the astronauts on the Moon's surface.
