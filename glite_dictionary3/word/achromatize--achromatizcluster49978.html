to achromatize

verb
make free from color distortion (optics)
Pronunciation: / əˈkroʊməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmətaɪz / (BrE)
to make (a lens or optical instrument) free from chromatic aberration; to remove or correct unwanted color effects

Examples

to achromatize a lens
It is necessary to achromatize the objective lens to eliminate color fringes.
Modern microscopes achromatize their lenses to produce clear images.
Optical engineers work to achromatize telescopes for more accurate observations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – chromatize: to add color or produce color effects
