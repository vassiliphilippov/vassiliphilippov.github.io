Muskogee

noun
a Native American people; core of the Creek Confederacy
Pronunciation: / mʌsˈkoʊgi / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊgi / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in Georgia and eastern Alabama, who formed the core of the Creek Confederacy.

Examples

Muskogee people history
Historians study the role of the Muskogee in the formation of the Creek Confederacy.
Many Muskogee descendants today live in Oklahoma and preserve their cultural traditions.
The Muskogee language is still spoken by some elders in the community.
