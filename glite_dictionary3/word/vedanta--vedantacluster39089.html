Vedanta

noun
Hindu philosophy based on the Upanishads
Pronunciation: / veɪˈdɑn.tə / (AmE), / vɛˈdɑːn.tə / (BrE)
A school of Hindu philosophy based on the teachings of the Upanishads, emphasizing the ultimate unity of the individual soul (Atman) and the absolute (Brahman).

Examples

Vedanta philosophy teachings
Many modern spiritual teachers draw inspiration from Vedanta philosophies.
She studied Vedanta at the university to understand its teachings about the self.
The concept of non-duality is central to Vedanta thought.
