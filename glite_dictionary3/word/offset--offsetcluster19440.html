offset

noun
horizontal shoot from plant base; forms new plants
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)
a short horizontal shoot from the base of a plant, capable of developing into a new individual plant

Examples

offset of a plant
Some houseplants develop offsets at their base, making them easy to multiply.
Strawberries reproduce quickly by sending out offsets that root nearby.
The gardener separated the offsets from the parent plant to propagate new succulents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – runner: a long, slender stem that grows horizontally along the ground and roots to form new plants
