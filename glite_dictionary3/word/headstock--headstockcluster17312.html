headstock

noun
stationary support in a machine tool
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌstɒk / (BrE)
The fixed part of a machine tool, such as a lathe, that supports and drives a revolving part like a spindle or chuck.

Examples

headstock of a lathe
He adjusted the headstock to ensure the workpiece rotated smoothly.
Proper maintenance of the headstock is crucial for accurate machining.
The headstock of the lathe houses the main spindle and the change gears.
