Platonic

adjective
relating to Plato or his philosophy
Pronunciation: / pləˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / pləˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to Plato or his philosophy, especially in reference to ideas such as the existence of ideal forms or the nature of reality as discussed by Plato.

Examples

Platonic philosophy
His lecture offered a detailed analysis of Platonic dialogues and their influence on Western thought.
Many Renaissance thinkers were inspired by Platonic ideas when developing their theories.
The concept of ideal forms is a central aspect of Platonic philosophy.
