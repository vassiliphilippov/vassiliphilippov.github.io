to square

verb
to be in agreement; to match
Pronunciation: / skwɛr / (AmE), / skweə / (BrE)
to make things agree, match, or be consistent with each other

Examples

to square the facts
His account does not square with the evidence we have so far.
She tried to make her actions square with her beliefs.
The results don't square with my calculations, so something must be wrong.
He tried to square his beliefs with his actions, but struggled to find consistency.
Her story didn't square with the facts presented at the trial.
The data must square with the company's financial statements before we can approve the report.
