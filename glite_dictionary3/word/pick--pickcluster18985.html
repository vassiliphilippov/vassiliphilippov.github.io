to pick

verb
eat small amounts or take small bites
Pronunciation: / pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
to eat food by taking small bites or by eating a small amount at a time, often without finishing a full meal

Examples

to pick at food
He just picked at the salad during lunch, barely touching it.
She tends to pick at her food instead of eating a full meal.
When nervous, David often picks at his dinner and leaves most of it on the plate.

  • synonym – nibble: to eat in small bites
