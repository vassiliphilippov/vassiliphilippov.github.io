to take

verb
buy or select something
Pronunciation: / teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
to choose, select, or buy something

Examples

to take a seat

She decided to take the blue dress instead of the red one.
He always takes the blue marker when given a choice.
She couldn't decide which dessert to take from the tray.
You may take whichever seat you prefer in the room.
