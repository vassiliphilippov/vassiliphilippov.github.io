marlite

noun
metamorphic rock similar to marl
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːl.aɪt / (BrE)
A metamorphic rock with approximately the same composition as marl, typically formed by the alteration of marl under heat and pressure.

Examples

layer of marlite
Marlite often forms in regions where both clay and limestone are present in the underlying strata.

Unlike pure limestone, marlite contains significant amounts of clay minerals.
