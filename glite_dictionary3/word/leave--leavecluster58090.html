to leave

verb
cause to be in a state or condition
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liːv / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to remain, be left behind, or be in a particular state as a result

Examples

leave someone happy
His generous donation left the charity deeply grateful.
The experience left a lasting impression on everyone present.
The tragic news left her in shock for days.
Cutting the fruit leaves a sticky residue on your hands.
The floodwaters left a thick layer of mud across the street.
The red wine left an ugly stain on the tablecloth.
His absence leaves only one candidate for the position.
The new schedule leaves enough time for us to finish everything.
Their decision leaves me with no other choice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allow: to give permission for something to happen
  • synonym – leave behind: to cause to remain after one's departure
  • synonym – permit: to officially allow something to happen
  • synonym – remain: to be left after other parts have been removed
  • antonym – remove: to take something away so none remains
  • synonym – render: to cause to be or become; to make
  • synonym – subtract: to take away a part, sometimes leaving a remainder
