laminectomy

noun
surgical removal of vertebral arch
Pronunciation: / ˌlæməˈnɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌlamɪˈnɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove the bony arch (lamina) of one or more vertebrae, often to relieve pressure on the spinal cord or nerves

Examples

laminectomy surgery
A laminectomy may be recommended if conservative treatments for back pain are unsuccessful.
Recovery after a laminectomy typically involves physical therapy and rest.
The patient underwent a laminectomy to relieve pressure on his spinal cord.
