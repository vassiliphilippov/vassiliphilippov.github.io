opsonisation

noun
process making microorganisms easier to ingest by immune cells
Pronunciation: / ˌɑpsəˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpsənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which opsonins bind to microorganisms, making them more susceptible to being ingested and destroyed by phagocytes

Examples

opsonisation of bacteria
Complement proteins aid in the opsonisation of invading pathogens, enhancing their clearance.
Defects in opsonisation can lead to increased susceptibility to infections.
The opsonisation of bacteria is a crucial step in the body's immune defense.

Related Terms

  • synonym – opsonin: a molecule that binds to foreign particles and makes them more susceptible to phagocytosis
  • synonym – phagocytosis: the process by which phagocytes engulf and digest microorganisms
