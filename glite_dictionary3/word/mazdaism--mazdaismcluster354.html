Mazdaism

noun
ancient Persian religion founded by Zoroaster
Pronunciation: / ˈmæz.deɪ.ɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmaz.deɪ.ɪzəm / (BrE)
the religion founded by Zoroaster in ancient Persia, centered on the worship of Ahura Mazda and the duality of good and evil

Examples

ancient Mazdaism religion
In Mazdaism, Ahura Mazda is seen as the supreme god and source of all that is good.
Scholars have studied Mazdaism to better understand ancient Persian society and its beliefs.
The teachings of Mazdaism emphasize the perpetual struggle between the forces of good and evil.
