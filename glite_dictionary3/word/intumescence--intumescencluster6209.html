intumescence

noun
increase in volume of a substance when heated
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuːˈmɛs.əns / (BrE)
the increase in volume of a substance when heated, often with the release of water, especially as seen in certain chemicals or materials

Examples

intumescence of fireproof coating
The fire safety coating relied on intumescence to expand and protect the underlying surface.
The process of intumescence is critical for certain fire-retardant materials.
Upon heating, the mineral underwent intumescence and released steam.
