flock

noun
church congregation
Pronunciation: / flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
a group of people who are together, especially as members of a church or gathered for a purpose

Examples

flock of churchgoers
He devoted his life to guiding his flock through times of joy and hardship.
The flock gathered every Sunday to listen to the sermon.
The pastor encouraged his flock to support the local community project.
A flock of tourists gathered near the entrance to the museum.
Every morning, a flock of students waits for the school bus at the corner.
The shepherd guided his flock through the narrow pass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – congregation: a group of people assembled for religious worship
  • synonym – crowd: a large number of people gathered together
  • synonym – herd: a large group of animals, especially hoofed mammals, that live, feed, or migrate together
  • synonym – swarm: a large group of insects, especially flying ones, moving together
