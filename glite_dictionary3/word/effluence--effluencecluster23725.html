effluence

noun
the act or process of flowing out
Pronunciation: / ˈɛfluəns / (AmE), / ˈɛfluəns / (BrE)
the process or act of flowing out, especially of a liquid or other substance

Examples

effluence of water
Scientists are studying the effluence from underwater vents in the ocean.
Strict regulations control the effluence of waste from chemical plants.
The factory was fined for the effluence of pollutants into the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym – influx: the process of flowing in
