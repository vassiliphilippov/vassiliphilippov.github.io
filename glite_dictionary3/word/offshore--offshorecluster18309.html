offshore

adjective, adverb
away from shore or land
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈʃɔr /; / ˈɔfˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈʃɔː /; / ˈɒfʃɔː / (BrE)
away from the shore or at some distance from land, especially in the sea

Examples

offshore oil platform
A storm warning was issued for vessels operating offshore.
Seabirds often hunt for fish several miles offshore where the water is deeper.
The boat drifted offshore during the night, far from the harbor lights.
Strong winds pushed the fishing boats offshore, making their return difficult.
The company is exploring new offshore oil fields in the North Sea.
We spent a week on an offshore island that was only accessible by boat.
