to seal

verb
make definite and irrevocable
Pronunciation: / siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
to decide or determine something so that it cannot be changed; to make something certain and irreversible

Examples

to seal his fate
By confessing, he may have sealed his fate.
The board's decision will seal the company's future.
The peace treaty effectively sealed the end of hostilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – finalize: to make final or definite
  • antonym – reverse: to undo or change a decision or action
