beriberi

noun
disease caused by lack of vitamin B1
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛriˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛriˈbɛri / (BrE)
A disease caused by a deficiency of thiamine (vitamin B1), characterized by weakness, nerve degeneration, and heart problems.

Examples

beriberi disease symptoms
A diet lacking in whole grains and meats can sometimes lead to beriberi.
Doctors diagnosed him with beriberi after he showed symptoms of severe weakness and nerve pain.
Thiamine supplements are used to treat patients suffering from beriberi.
