maliciously

adverb
in a malicious manner
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪʃəsli / (AmE), / məˈlɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a desire to harm someone or cause trouble.

Examples

maliciously spread rumors
He maliciously destroyed his rival's work out of envy.
She maliciously spread rumors about her coworker to damage her reputation.
The critic maliciously mocked the author's latest book in his review.

Related Terms

  • antonym – benevolently: in a kind and well-meaning manner
  • synonym – spitefully: in a spiteful manner
