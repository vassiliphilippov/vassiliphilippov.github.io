to foil

verb
to enhance by contrast
Pronunciation: / fɔɪl / (AmE), / fɔɪl / (BrE)
to enhance (something) by providing a contrast; to make something stand out by being different

Examples

to foil dark colors
Delicate white lace foiled the deep color of the fabric, creating a striking effect.
The dark mountains foil the brightness of the sky at sunset.
The vibrant purple dress foiled her pale complexion beautifully.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contrast: to be strikingly different from something else, typically in a way that is clear or obvious
