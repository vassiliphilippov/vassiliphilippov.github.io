Tristram

proper noun
legendary knight in Arthurian romance
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪstrəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪstrəm / (BrE)
A legendary knight of the Middle Ages, nephew to the king of Cornwall, who, according to legend, was fated to fall in love with Iseult after they drank a magical love potion.

Examples

Tristram and Iseult
In medieval tales, Tristram is renowned for both his bravery and his doomed love.
The tragic romance of Tristram and Iseult has inspired countless poets and writers.
Wagner's opera 'Tristan und Isolde' is based on the story of Tristram and Iseult.
