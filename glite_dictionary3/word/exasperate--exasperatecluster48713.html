to exasperate

verb
to make worse; to aggravate
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzæspəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːspəreɪt / (BrE)
(rare) To make a problem, situation, or feeling worse; to aggravate.

Examples

to exasperate a problem
Adding salt to the wound would exasperate the situation even further.
His careless remarks exasperated the ongoing conflict between the two teams.
The excessive noise only exasperated his headache.
