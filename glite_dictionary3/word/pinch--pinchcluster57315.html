pinch

noun
a difficult or straitened circumstance
Pronunciation: / pɪntʃ / (AmE), / pɪntʃ / (BrE)
a difficult or distressing situation, especially one needing quick action or involving hardship

Examples

the pinch of poverty
During the winter, the pinch of unemployment became even more severe for the community.
Many families are feeling the pinch as prices for essentials continue to rise.
The charity aims to help those experiencing the pinch of poverty.
He managed to stay calm during the pinch and solved the problem quickly.
In a pinch, you have to trust your instincts to make the best decision.
The team came together when the pinch was at its worst.
