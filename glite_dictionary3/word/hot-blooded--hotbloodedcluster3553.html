hot-blooded

adjective
prone to strong emotions or passion
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑtˈblʌdəd / (AmE), / ˌhɒtˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
Having strong feelings or emotions; easily aroused to passion, anger, or enthusiasm.

Examples

hot-blooded young man
He was known as a hot-blooded young man who easily lost his temper.
Her hot-blooded nature made her both passionate and unpredictable.
The debate turned hot-blooded as emotions ran high among the participants.
