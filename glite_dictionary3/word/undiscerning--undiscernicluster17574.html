undiscerning

adjective
not able to make careful judgments
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪˈsɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈsɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
lacking the ability to make careful judgments or to notice fine distinctions; not discerning.

Examples

an undiscerning reader
Her undiscerning reading of the report caused her to miss several important details.
Only an undiscerning viewer would fail to appreciate the clever symbolism in the film.
The undiscerning customer bought the imitation product without noticing the differences.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discerning: having or showing good judgment
  • synonym – indiscriminate: done at random without careful judgment
