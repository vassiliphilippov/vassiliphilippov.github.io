formalization

noun
the act of making something formal
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrmələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːmələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something formal, especially by defining official rules, structures, or procedures

Examples

formalization of rules
In mathematics, the formalization of theories helps ensure clarity and precision.
The formalization of the agreement required both parties to sign a written contract.
The organization began the formalization of its procedures to standardize its operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – informalization: the process of making something less formal
  • synonym – institutionalization: the process of establishing something as a norm or institution
