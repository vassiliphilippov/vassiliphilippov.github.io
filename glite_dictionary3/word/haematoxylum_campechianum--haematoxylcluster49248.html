Haematoxylum campechianum

noun
tropical tree yielding logwood dye
Pronunciation: / hiːməˈtɑksələm kæmˌpeɪʃiˈænəm / (AmE), / hiːməˈtɒksɪləm kamˌpɛkɪˈanəm / (BrE)
a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, with spiny branches, bipinnate leaves, and yellow flowers; its hard reddish-brown heartwood is used to make a black dye, known as logwood.
