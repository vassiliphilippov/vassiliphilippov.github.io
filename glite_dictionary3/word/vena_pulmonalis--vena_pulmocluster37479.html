vena pulmonalis

noun
pulmonary vein (carries blood from lungs)
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnə ˌpʌlməˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːnə ˌpʌlməˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
Any of the four veins that carry oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left atrium of the heart; also called a pulmonary vein.
