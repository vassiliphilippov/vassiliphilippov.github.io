to cannulate

verb
insert a cannula or tube for medical use
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.ju.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ju.leɪt / (BrE)
to insert a cannula or tube into a body part, usually a blood vessel or cavity, for medical purposes

Examples

to cannulate a vein
She watched as the nurse skillfully cannulated the infant's tiny blood vessel.
The surgeon had to cannulate the patient's artery to administer the medication directly.
To perform the procedure, first cannulate the vein using a sterile technique.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decannulate: remove a cannula or tube from a body part
