cartilage

noun
firm elastic connective tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a firm, elastic connective tissue found in various parts of the body, especially in joints, that is more flexible than bone

Examples

cartilage in the knee
As people age, the cartilage in their joints can wear down, leading to arthritis.
Sharks have skeletons made entirely of cartilage rather than bone.
The surgeon explained that damage to the knee cartilage could cause long-term problems.
