realism

noun
philosophical doctrine that universals exist objectively
Pronunciation: / ˈriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that abstract concepts or universals exist independently of the mind and of their names.

Examples

metaphysical realism
Debates between realism and nominalism shaped the development of Western metaphysics.
In medieval philosophy, realism argues that universals have a real existence beyond language and thought.
