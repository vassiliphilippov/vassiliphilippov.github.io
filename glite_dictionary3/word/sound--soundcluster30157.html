to sound

verb
measure water depth
Pronunciation: / saʊnd / (AmE), / saʊnd / (BrE)
to measure the depth of (a body of water) using a sounding line or similar device

Examples

to sound the depth
Before mapping the coastline, they would regularly sound to determine the varying depths.
It is important to sound the water to avoid running aground near the shore.
The sailors prepared to sound the channel before entering the unfamiliar harbor.
