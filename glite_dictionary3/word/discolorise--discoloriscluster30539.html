to discolorise

verb
cause to become lighter in color
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkʌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌl.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to become white or lighter in color; to bleach or remove its color

Examples

to discolorise fabric
Exposing the painting to harsh light may discolorise its vibrant colors over time.
Sunlight can discolorise fabrics left in the window for too long.
The chemical treatment will discolorise the stains and restore the shirt's original shade.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bleach: to make something white or lighter in color by using chemicals or sunlight
  • antonym – darken: to make something darker in color
