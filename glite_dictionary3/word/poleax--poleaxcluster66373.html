to poleax

verb
to knock down or strike decisively
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊˌlæks / (AmE), / ˈpəʊˌlæks / (BrE)
to hit, knock down, or fell someone or something as if with a poleax; to strike or overwhelm decisively

Examples

to poleax an opponent
A stray branch poleaxed the hiker, knocking him to the ground.
The boxer was poleaxed by a sudden uppercut in the third round.
The shocking news poleaxed him into silence for several minutes.
