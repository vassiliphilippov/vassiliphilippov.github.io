complementary

adjective
mutually exclusive and exhaustive (linguistics, logic)
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpləˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
(of words or propositions) so related that each is the negation of the other, together covering all possibilities.

Examples

complementary antonyms
In linguistics, a pair of complementary antonyms together exhaust all possible states.
In logic, 'alive' and 'dead' are considered complementary terms.
The words 'true' and 'false' are classic examples of complementary propositions.
