ganoid

noun
primitive fish with bony, shiny scales
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæ.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡæ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a primitive fish with thick, bony scales covered by a shiny layer; especially referring to members of the order Ganoidei

Examples

ganoid fish species
Certain freshwater fishes, like the gar, are considered living ganoids.
Fossils of ganoids provide valuable insight into the evolution of vertebrates.
The scales of a ganoid are thick, diamond-shaped, and remarkably shiny.

Related Terms

  • antonym – teleost: modern bony fish, contrasted with primitive ganoid fishes
