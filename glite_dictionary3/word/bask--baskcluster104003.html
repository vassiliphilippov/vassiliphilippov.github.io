to bask

verb
take pleasure from praise or success
Pronunciation: / bæsk / (AmE), / bɑːsk / (BrE)
to take great pleasure or satisfaction from something, especially attention, praise, or success

Examples

to bask in praise
After winning the award, she basked in the admiration of her colleagues.
He likes to bask in the attention whenever he achieves something remarkable.
The singer basked in the applause of her adoring fans after the concert.

Related Terms

  • synonym – luxuriate: to enjoy oneself without restraint
  • synonym – revel: to take intense pleasure or satisfaction
