drum

noun
part of a vehicle's braking system
Pronunciation: / drʌm / (AmE), / drʌm / (BrE)
a hollow cylindrical part, usually made of cast iron, attached to a wheel and forming a component of drum brakes in vehicles

Examples

brake drum replacement
Replacing a worn brake drum can restore stopping power and reduce noise.
Rust had built up inside the drum, affecting the braking performance.
The mechanic inspected the drum for excessive wear and damage.
