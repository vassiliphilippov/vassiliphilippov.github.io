to touch

verb
add a slight amount of color
Pronunciation: / tʌtʃ / (AmE), / tʌtʃ / (BrE)
to add a small amount of color to something; to give a slight tint or hue

Examples

to touch cheeks with color
A trace of blush touched her cheeks, giving her a healthy glow.
Autumn had touched the maples with shades of crimson and orange.
The painter touched the edges of the canvas with gold to highlight the design.
