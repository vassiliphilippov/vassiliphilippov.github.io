eupneic

adjective
having normal, unlabored breathing
Pronunciation: / juˈpniɪk / (AmE), / juːpˈniːɪk / (BrE)
(of breathing) normal and unlabored; able to inhale and exhale air without difficulty.

Examples

eupneic breathing pattern
After the operation, the patient was monitored to ensure she remained eupneic throughout the night.
Most healthy adults are eupneic at rest, with an even breathing rate.
The doctor noted that the infant was calm and eupneic during the examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dyspneic: having difficulty in breathing; shortness of breath
