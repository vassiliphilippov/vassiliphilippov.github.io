CBR

noun
cosmic background radiation (cosmology)
Pronunciation: / siː biː ɑːr / (AmE), / siː biː ɑː / (BrE)
the cooled remnant radiation from the early universe, observable today as a faint background glow with a temperature of about 2.7 kelvin; cosmic background radiation

Examples

CBR from space
Astronomers use the CBR to gain insights into the origin and evolution of the universe.
Sensitive instruments aboard satellites have mapped tiny fluctuations in the CBR across the sky.
The discovery of the CBR provided strong evidence supporting the Big Bang theory.
