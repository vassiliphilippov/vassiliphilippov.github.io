to conduct

verb
to direct or manage an activity or operation
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌkt / (AmE), / kənˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to organize and direct a particular activity; to manage, guide, or control something, especially business, an event, or proceedings

Examples

to conduct a meeting
He learned how to conduct business negotiations with international clients.
She was asked to conduct the meeting in her manager's absence.
The company will conduct an internal investigation to address the issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym – manage: to be in charge of, to direct or control
  • synonym – supervise: to oversee or direct the work or tasks of others
