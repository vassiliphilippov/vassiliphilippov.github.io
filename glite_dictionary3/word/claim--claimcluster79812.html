to claim

verb
assert something as true
Pronunciation: / kleɪm / (AmE), / kleɪm / (BrE)
to say that something is true, especially without giving proof

Examples

to claim to know
He claimed to be an expert, but he knew very little about the topic.
She claimed that she had never seen him before in her life.
The company claims its product is the most effective on the market.
Do you intend to claim credit for this new marketing strategy?
Several authors claimed the concept, but only one was recognized for it.
She tried to claim the invention as her own, but someone else had already patented it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assert: state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • antonym – deny: state that something is not true
  • antonym – disclaim: to deny or renounce a claim; antonym for 'claim'
