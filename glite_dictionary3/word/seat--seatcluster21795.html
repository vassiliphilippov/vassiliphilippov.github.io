to seat

verb
to provide with seats or seating
Pronunciation: / sit /; / siːt / (AmE), / siːt / (BrE)
to provide seats for people, arrange where people sit, or show someone to a seat; (of a building, vehicle, etc.) to have seats for a certain number of people; to cause someone to sit down

Examples

to seat the guests
The organizers seated the guests according to their ticket numbers.
The theater can seat over 1,000 people for special events.
This lecture hall seats comfortably up to three hundred students.
The concert hall seats more people after the recent renovations.
The stadium seats 50,000 spectators for major events.
This bus seats up to 40 passengers comfortably.
Please wait here until the hostess seats you at your table.
The teacher seated the students in alphabetical order for the ceremony.
The usher seated the guests as they arrived at the theater.
