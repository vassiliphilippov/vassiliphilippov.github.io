waxing

noun
gradual increase (especially of the moon)
Pronunciation: / ˈwæksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwæksɪŋ / (BrE)
a gradual increase in the size, strength, or extent of something, especially the illuminated portion of the moon as seen from Earth

Examples

waxing strength
Farmers once used the waxing and waning of the moon to plan their planting.
In ancient times, people celebrated the waxing of the days after the winter solstice.
The waxing of the moon is clearly visible each night after the new moon.
