to scrimp

verb
economize or manage with limited resources
Pronunciation: / skrɪmp / (AmE), / skrɪmp / (BrE)
to spend very little money, especially by making extreme efforts to economize or get by with as little as possible

Examples

to scrimp on food
During college, she learned to scrimp on meals and expenses.
Many families scrimp during hard economic times to make ends meet.
They had to scrimp and save for years to afford a house.

Related Terms

  • synonym – skimp: to spend or use less of something than is necessary
