unsynchronised

adjective
not synchronized; not occurring together
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɪŋkrənaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɪŋkrənaɪzd / (BrE)
not happening or operating at the same time; lacking coordination in time.

Examples

unsynchronised audio
Due to technical issues, the audio and video became unsynchronised.
Efforts failed when the group's movements remained unsynchronised despite several rehearsals.
The two dancers were noticeably unsynchronised during the performance.
