ideology

noun
set of guiding beliefs or ideas
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌʌɪdɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a set of ideas or beliefs that form the basis of a political, economic, or other system, especially those held by a particular group or society

Examples

political ideology
Education can play a vital role in shaping the ideology of young people.
His ideology clashed with that of the organization he worked for.
The political party is driven by a strict ideology that influences all its decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – creed: a system of religious belief or faith
  • synonym – doctrine: a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group
  • synonym – dogma: a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym – pragmatism: a practical approach to problems and affairs (opposite in some political contexts)
