to wheedle

verb
persuade by flattery or gentle urging
Pronunciation: / ˈwidəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːdəl / (BrE)
To influence or persuade someone by gentle urging, flattering, or coaxing.

Examples

to wheedle a favor
He managed to wheedle his way into the exclusive party.
She tried to wheedle an extra piece of cake from her grandmother.
The salesperson attempted to wheedle the customer into buying the expensive suit.

Related Terms

  • antonym – blunt: speak in a direct, sometimes harsh way
  • synonym – cajole: persuade by flattery or deceit
  • synonym – coax: persuade gently and persistently
