retiring

adjective
leaving a position or office
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) about to leave or having recently left a job, position, or office, especially as a result of reaching the usual age for leaving that position.

Examples

retiring manager
A retiring director will be succeeded by a new appointee next month.
The retiring president delivered a farewell speech at the company's annual meeting.
They hosted a party in honor of their retiring colleague.

Related Terms

  • synonym – former: having previously held a certain office or position
  • antonym – incumbent: currently holding a specified office or position
