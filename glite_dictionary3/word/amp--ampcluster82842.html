AMP

noun
nucleotide important in metabolism
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˌɛm ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌem ˈpiː / (BrE)
a nucleotide found in muscle cells and important in metabolism, reversibly convertible to ADP and ATP

Examples

AMP in muscle cells
During intense exercise, AMP is converted to ADP and ATP to provide energy.
Muscle fatigue can be related to the amount of AMP present in the cells.
Researchers measured the increase in AMP concentrations after physical activity.
