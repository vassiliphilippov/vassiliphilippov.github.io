fraternity

noun
group of people in the same profession
Pronunciation: / frəˈtɜrnəti / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
all the people who share a particular profession or occupation, regarded as a group; a professional or occupational brotherhood

Examples

medical fraternity members
She was highly respected by the legal fraternity for her integrity and expertise.
The fraternity of scientists welcomed new research in their field.
The entire journalism fraternity mourned the loss of their colleague.

  • antonym – individual: a single person rather than a collective group
