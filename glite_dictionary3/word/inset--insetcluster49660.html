to inset

verb
to set in or embed
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌsɛt / (BrE)
to set or place something into a space or within another object, often so that it fits flush or is embedded.

Examples

to inset glass into door
A small screen was inset at the bottom corner of the monitor for additional information.
Shelves are frequently inset into alcoves to save space.
The designer decided to inset a panel of stained glass into the front door.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embed: to fix (an object) firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • antonym – remove: to take something out from its place
