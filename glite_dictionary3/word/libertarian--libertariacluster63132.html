libertarian

noun
advocate of individual liberty/minimal government
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪbərˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates the doctrine of libertarianism, emphasizing individual freedom and minimal government intervention

Examples

a libertarian politician
As a proud libertarian, she opposes most government regulations.
Many libertarians advocate for strong protection of civil liberties.
The debate featured a libertarian arguing for lower taxes and less intervention.
