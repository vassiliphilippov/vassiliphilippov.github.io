to feather

verb
turn an oar blade parallel to the water
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛðər /; / ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
to turn the blade of an oar or paddle so it is parallel to the water to reduce wind resistance

Examples

to feather an oar
Properly feathering the oar reduces wind resistance and makes the boat faster.
She struggled to feather the blade smoothly as she learned to row.
The coxswain reminded the crew to feather their oars at the end of each stroke.
Rowers are taught to feather the oar at the end of each stroke.
She always remembers to feather her paddle to minimize wind resistance.
When you feather the blade, you make your paddling more efficient.
