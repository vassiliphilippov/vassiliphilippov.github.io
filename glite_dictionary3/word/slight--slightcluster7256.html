slight

adjective
small in amount or importance
Pronunciation: / slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
small in degree, amount, or importance; not strong or substantial

Examples

slight difference
She felt a slight pain in her shoulder after carrying the heavy bag.
The difference in taste was slight, but I could tell the chocolate was a bit richer.
There is a slight possibility of rain this afternoon, but most people expect sunshine.
Her influence on the decision was slight compared to her colleagues'.
The evidence against the suspect was only slight, so the case was dismissed.
There is a slight chance of rain tomorrow, but it's mostly sunny.

Related Terms

  • synonym – insignificant: too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • synonym – little: small in amount; not much
  • synonym – minor: lesser in importance, seriousness, or significance
  • antonym – substantial: of considerable importance, size, or worth
  • synonym – trivial: of little value or importance
