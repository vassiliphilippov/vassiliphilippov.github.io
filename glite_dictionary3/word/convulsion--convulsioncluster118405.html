convulsion

noun
violent, involuntary muscle contraction
Pronunciation: / kənˈvʌlʒən /; / kənˈvʌlʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvʌlʃən /; / kənˈvʌlʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a sudden, violent, and involuntary movement of the body or muscles, often caused by illness; also, a sudden, uncontrollable outburst

Examples

a convulsion during illness
Some fevers in young children can trigger a convulsion lasting several minutes.
The patient experienced a severe convulsion after the medication was administered.
Witnesses described the child falling to the ground and going into a convulsion.
A violent convulsion shook his body before he lost consciousness.
Her joke sent the whole room into convulsions of laughter.
The patient suffered a convulsion after being exposed to the flashing lights.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: stillness or lack of sudden movement
  • synonym – seizure: a sudden attack of illness, especially epilepsy or another disorder with convulsions
  • synonym – spasm: a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
