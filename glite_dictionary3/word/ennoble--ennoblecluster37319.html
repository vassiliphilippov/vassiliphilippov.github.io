to ennoble

verb
make someone a noble or give a title
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
to give someone a noble title or rank; to make someone a member of the nobility

Examples

to ennoble a person
Several artists were ennobled after making significant contributions to the empire.
The king decided to ennoble his most loyal advisor for outstanding service.
The queen plans to ennoble distinguished citizens during the annual ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym – degrade: to lower in dignity or rank
  • synonym – elevate: to raise to a higher rank or position
