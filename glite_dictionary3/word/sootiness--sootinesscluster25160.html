sootiness

noun
the state of being dirty with soot
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːt̬i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsuː.ti.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being covered with or resembling soot; blackness or dirtiness caused by soot

Examples

heavy sootiness
After the fire, the air was filled with a sootiness that clung to everything in the house.
The sootiness of the ceiling revealed years of burning candles inside the church.
The old fireplace added an unmistakable sootiness to the living room walls.
