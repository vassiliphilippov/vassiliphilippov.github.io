to vituperate

verb
criticize harshly; abuse verbally
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈtuːpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / vɪˈtjuːpəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to criticize or speak about someone or something in a harshly abusive or scolding manner

Examples

to vituperate a colleague
It was unprofessional for the manager to vituperate his staff in front of customers.
She refused to vituperate her political opponents, preferring a more dignified approach.
Some critics tend to vituperate new artists rather than offer constructive feedback.

Related Terms

  • antonym – praise: to express warm approval or admiration of
  • synonym – vilify: to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
