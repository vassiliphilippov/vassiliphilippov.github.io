to tame

verb
make less wild; domesticate
Pronunciation: / teɪm / (AmE), / teɪm / (BrE)
to make an animal, plant, land, or behavior less wild or easier to control; to domesticate or bring under control

Examples

to tame a wild animal
Farmers often tame wild plants to grow them as crops.
Humans have managed to tame wolves, turning them into domestic dogs.
It took years to tame the wild horse.
It takes a lot of patience to tame a parrot that has lived in the jungle.
Scientists hope to tame the aggressive instincts of captive wolves through selective breeding.
The trainer was able to tame the wild horse after several months of careful work.
It took several weeks to tame the dog's aggressive tendencies.
Strict rules were used in an attempt to tame the boys' rowdy behavior.
The trainer managed to tame the rebellious horse with consistent discipline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discipline: to train or control by enforcing rules and punishing misbehavior
  • synonym – domesticate: to make an animal or plant suitable for home or farm life
  • synonym – punish: to inflict a penalty for an offense or wrongdoing
  • antonym – wild: (of animals) living in a natural state, not tamed or domesticated
