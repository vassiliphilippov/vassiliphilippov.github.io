to chuck

verb
throw away; discard (informal)
Pronunciation: / tʃʌk / (AmE), / tʃʌk / (BrE)
(informal) to throw or discard something carelessly or without much accuracy

Examples

to chuck a ball
Don't just chuck your homework in the bin without looking at it!
He chucked the broken umbrella into the trash as soon as he got home.
She decided to chuck all her old clothes before moving to the new apartment.
Don't just chuck your jacket on the floor; hang it up.
He chucked his keys onto the table as soon as he walked in.
She chucked the letter into the bin without reading it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discard: To throw something away or get rid of it
  • antonym – keep: To retain or not dispose of something
  • synonym – throw: to propel something through the air with force
  • synonym – toss: to throw lightly, often in a casual manner
