lacquer

noun
hard, glossy protective coating
Pronunciation: / ˈlækər / (AmE), / ˈlakə / (BrE)
a hard, shiny substance or coating used to protect or decorate wood, metal, or other surfaces

Examples

lacquer finish on wood
Many traditional Asian artworks are finished with black or red lacquer.
The antique table was restored with a fresh coat of lacquer to enhance its shine.
The guitar’s glossy lacquer made it stand out under the stage lights.

Japanese art often features intricate designs embedded in lacquer surfaces.
The craftsman coated the wooden box with several layers of lacquer to achieve a glossy sheen.

  • synonym – varnish: a liquid used to provide a hard, clear, shiny surface coating
