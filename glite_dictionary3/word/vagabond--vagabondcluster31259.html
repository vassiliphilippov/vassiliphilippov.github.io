to vagabond

verb
wander aimlessly; move from place to place
Pronunciation: / ˈvæɡəˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈvaɡəbɒnd / (BrE)
(archaic) To move about aimlessly or wander from place to place, often in search of food or work.

Examples

to vagabond across countries
He spent years vagabonding across Europe in search of inspiration.
In the aftermath of the war, families vagabonded from town to town to survive.
The protagonist vagabonds through the countryside, never staying in one place for long.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drift: move passively, aimlessly, or involuntarily into a situation or condition
  • synonym – roam: move about aimlessly or without any fixed destination
  • antonym – settle: adopt a fixed or permanent place to live
