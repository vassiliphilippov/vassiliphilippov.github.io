bipinnate

adjective
having doubly pinnate leaflets
Pronunciation: / baɪˈpɪneɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈpɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having leaflets that are themselves divided in a pinnate arrangement, forming a double feather-like structure.

Examples

bipinnate leaf structure
By comparing the pinnate and bipinnate leaves, students could easily see the difference in leaf structure.
Many fern species have bipinnate leaves, creating a delicate, feathery appearance.
The botanist noted that the acacia's bipinnate foliage allowed more sunlight to reach the plant's lower branches.
