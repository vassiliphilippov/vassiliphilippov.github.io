inferential

adjective
based on inference, not direct statement
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.fəˈrɛn.ʃəl /; / ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈrɛn.ʃəl /; / ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or based on inference, reasoning, or drawing conclusions rather than direct observation

Examples

inferential reasoning
Her report was full of inferential statements that relied on interpreting the available data.
Much of our understanding of ancient history is inferential, pieced together from archaeological finds.
The lawyer's inferential evidence was persuasive, even though there was no direct proof.
His argument was based on inferential reasoning rather than direct observation.
Juries are often required to draw inferential conclusions from the evidence presented.
The scientist made an inferential leap from the observed data to her final theory.
His conclusion is not based on direct evidence, but on inferential reasoning.
Legal arguments often rely on inferential interpretations of the facts.
The scientist presented inferential data supporting her theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deductive: reasoned from general to specific; based on deduction
  • antonym – inductive: characterized by deriving general principles from particular facts or instances
  • antonym – intuitive: based on what one feels to be true rather than on reasoning or inference
