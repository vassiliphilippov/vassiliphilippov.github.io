Proteaceae

noun
family of shrubs and trees, mainly Australian and South African
Pronunciation: / proʊˌtiːˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / prəʊˌtiːˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of mainly Australian and South African shrubs and trees, characterized by leathery leaves and usually clustered tetramerous flowers; the family includes many ornamental plants.

Examples

Proteaceae family plants
Gardeners often cultivate members of Proteaceae for their unusual and attractive flowers.
Many species in the Proteaceae are adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
The Proteaceae family includes the well-known Australian banksia and the South African protea.
