contrivance

noun
an artificial or forced arrangement
Pronunciation: / kənˈtraɪvəns / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvəns / (BrE)
an artificial, ingenious, or forced arrangement of details, often making something seem unnatural or unrealistic

Examples

plot contrivance twist
Critics noted that the story's plot twists were more contrivance than genuine surprise.
His explanation seemed like a clever contrivance designed to avoid the real issue.
The movie's ending relied on a last-minute contrivance that felt unconvincing to many viewers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – artifice: clever or cunning devices or expedients, especially as used to trick or deceive others
  • synonym – device: a thing made or adapted for a particular purpose, especially a mechanical or electronic one
  • antonym – naturalness: the quality of being natural or not contrived
