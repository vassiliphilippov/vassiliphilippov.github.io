protoactinium

noun
rare radioactive metallic element (Pa)
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.toʊ.ækˈtɪn.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.tə.ækˈtɪn.i.əm / (BrE)
A short-lived radioactive metallic element (Pa), formed from uranium and decaying into actinium and then into lead.

Examples

protoactinium element
Protoactinium is extremely rare and was first discovered in uranium ores.
Scientists must take precautions when handling protoactinium due to its radioactivity.
The decay chain of uranium-238 includes the formation of protoactinium.
