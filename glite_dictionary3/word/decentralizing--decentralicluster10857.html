decentralizing

adjective
moving away from central control
Pronunciation: / diˈsɛntrəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈsɛntrəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
moving or tending away from a central authority or point; characterized by the distribution of functions, powers, or people away from a central location or control.

Examples

decentralizing power structures
A decentralizing trend can be seen in modern information networks, reducing the risks of single-point failures.
Many advocates believe that decentralizing power improves efficiency and responsiveness in public services.
The company is adopting a decentralizing approach to management, giving more responsibility to regional offices.

Related Terms

  • antonym – centralizing: concentrating control or authority at a central point
