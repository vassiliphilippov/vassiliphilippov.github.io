arteriolosclerosis

noun
hardening of arterioles
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪriˌoʊloʊsklɪˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriˌəʊləʊsklɪˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which the walls of the arterioles become thickened and hardened, often leading to reduced blood flow.

Examples

arteriolosclerosis in small arteries
Chronic hypertension can result in arteriolosclerosis, which impairs blood flow to various organs.
