conservative

adjective
cautious or deliberately low (estimate, judgement)
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝː.və.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
deliberately low or cautious so as to avoid excess, especially in estimates or judgements.

Examples

conservative estimate
The accountant provided a conservative estimate of the company's annual profits.
Their conservative calculations ensured that the project stayed well within budget.
We took a conservative approach when forecasting next year's sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cautious: showing care to avoid danger or mistakes
  • antonym – liberal: generous or not strict, especially in estimates
