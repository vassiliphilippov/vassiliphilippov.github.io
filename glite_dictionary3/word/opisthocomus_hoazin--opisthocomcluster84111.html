Opisthocomus hoazin

noun
a crested, ill-smelling South American bird
Pronunciation: / oʊˌpɪsθəˈkoʊməs hoʊˈætsɪn / (AmE), / əˌpɪsθəˈkəʊməs həʊˈætsɪn / (BrE)
a crested, foul-smelling South American bird (the hoatzin) whose young possess claws on the first and second digits of their wings

Examples

Opisthocomus hoazin bird
The Opisthocomus hoazin is found in swamps and mangroves along the Amazon and Orinoco rivers.
The diet of the Opisthocomus hoazin consists mainly of leaves and vegetation.
Unlike any other bird, young Opisthocomus hoazin have claws on their wings to help them climb.
