foliated

adjective
having thin layered structure (rock)
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊliˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of rock, especially metamorphic rock) having thin, leaflike layers or strata.

Examples

foliated rock layers
Many metamorphic rocks are foliated, displaying distinct parallel layers.
Slate, a foliated metamorphic rock, splits easily along its thin layers.
The geologist pointed out the foliated structure of the schist.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonfoliated: (of rocks) not having thin, leaflike layers
