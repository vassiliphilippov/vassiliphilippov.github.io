undercoat

noun
fine inner layer of animal fur
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkəʊt / (BrE)
the dense, soft layer of fur beneath the longer, coarser guard hairs of an animal's coat

Examples

thick undercoat fur
Cats shed their undercoat in the spring as the weather becomes warmer.
Some breeds of dogs have a thick undercoat that protects them from cold weather.
The groomer carefully brushed out the loose undercoat from the husky.

Related Terms

  • antonym – guard hair: the longer, coarser outer hairs in an animal's coat
