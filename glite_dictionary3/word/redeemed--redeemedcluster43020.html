redeemed

adjective
saved from sin; spiritually delivered
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdimd / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːmd / (BrE)
(in religious contexts) saved from sin, error, or evil; delivered from the consequences of sin through spiritual salvation.

Examples

redeemed by grace
In many hymns, believers sing about being redeemed by grace.
She felt redeemed after confessing her sins and seeking forgiveness.
The preacher spoke about how the congregation was redeemed through faith.

Related Terms

  • antonym – damned: condemned to eternal punishment or destruction
