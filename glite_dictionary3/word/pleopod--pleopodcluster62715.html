pleopod

noun
abdominal appendage of aquatic crustaceans
Pronunciation: / ˈpliːəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈpliːəˌpɒd / (BrE)
one of a pair of abdominal limbs in certain aquatic crustaceans, adapted mainly for swimming and, in females, for carrying eggs

Examples

pleopod of a shrimp
During breeding season, the female shrimp's pleopods are covered with tiny eggs.
Researchers observed the movement of the crab's pleopods as it swam across the tank.
The crayfish used its pleopods to circulate water around its eggs.
