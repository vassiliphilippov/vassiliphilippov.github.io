to encompass

verb
include within a scope; surround
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌmpəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmpəs / (BrE)
to include a wide range of things, ideas, or people; to surround or cover something completely

Examples

encompass many topics
Her interests encompass both science and literature.
The festival encompasses music, film, and art from around the world.
The law encompasses all forms of discrimination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exclude: to leave out; not include
  • synonym – include: to contain as part of a whole
