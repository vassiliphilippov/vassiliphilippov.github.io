voile

noun
light, semi-transparent fabric
Pronunciation: / vɔɪl / (AmE), / vwɑːl / (BrE)
A lightweight, semi-transparent fabric, often made of cotton or polyester, used especially for making curtains, dresses, and blouses.

Examples

voile curtain fabric
Many summer blouses are crafted from lightweight voile fabrics for coolness and comfort.
She chose a white voile curtain to let in some light while maintaining privacy.
The dress was made of soft blue voile and fluttered gently in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chiffon: A light, sheer fabric typically made from silk or nylon
  • synonym – organza: A thin, plain weave, sheer fabric traditionally made from silk
