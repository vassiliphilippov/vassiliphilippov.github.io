lettercard

noun
folded postcard with message inside
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtərˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəkɑːd / (BrE)
A postcard consisting of a folded sheet, so that the written message is on the inside and can be sealed before mailing.

Examples

send a lettercard home
My grandmother used to send me a lettercard every summer from France.
The museum exhibited a collection of vintage lettercards from the early twentieth century.
You should fold the lettercard along the lines before sealing it for mailing.
