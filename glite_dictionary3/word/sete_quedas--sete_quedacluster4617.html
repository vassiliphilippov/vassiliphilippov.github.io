Sete Quedas

proper noun
former waterfall on the Brazil-Paraguay border
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛteɪ ˈkeɪdəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛteɪ ˈkeɪdəs / (BrE)
a former series of impressive waterfalls on the Paraná River, on the border between Brazil and Paraguay, submerged after the construction of the Itaipu Dam in 1982

Examples

Sete Quedas waterfalls
Sete Quedas was submerged under water after the construction of the Itaipu Dam in the 1980s.
Hundreds of tourists once visited Sete Quedas every year to admire its spectacular cascades.
The natural beauty of Sete Quedas made it one of South America's most famous waterfalls before it disappeared.
