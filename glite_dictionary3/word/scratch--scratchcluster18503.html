to scratch

verb
carve or mark a surface
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ /; / skɹætʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ /; / skɹatʃ / (BrE)
to scrape, cut, or mark a surface or object by rubbing with something sharp or rough

Examples

to scratch a surface
Over the years, many visitors have scratched messages into the old stone wall.
She carefully scratched a design onto the metal surface with a sharp tool.
They scratched their initials into the wooden bench in the park.
Some people find that wearing synthetic fabrics scratches their skin uncomfortably.
The label inside the shirt scratched me all day until I finally cut it off.
This wool scarf scratches my neck and makes it hard to concentrate.
Be careful not to scratch the table when you move those heavy boxes.
He accidentally scratched his new watch against the wall.
Her cat likes to scratch the door when it wants to come inside.
