takedown

noun
a move to bring an opponent to the mat (wrestling)
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈteɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
(in wrestling) an act of bringing an opponent down to the mat from a standing position, typically scoring points.

Examples

a wrestling takedown
A well-executed takedown can change the momentum of a wrestling bout.
He scored a quick takedown in the first round to earn two points.
The referee awarded her a point for each successful takedown during the match.
