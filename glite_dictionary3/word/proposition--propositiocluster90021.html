proposition

noun
(logic) statement that is true or false
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(logic) A statement or assertion that affirms or denies something and is either true or false.

Examples

logical proposition
If the proposition is false, then the argument cannot be sound.
Mathematicians often prove the truth or falsity of a proposition.
The philosopher discussed whether the proposition was logically valid.
