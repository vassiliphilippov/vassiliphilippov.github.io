to hydrolyze

verb
to break down by reaction with water
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrəlʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to undergo or cause to undergo a chemical reaction in which it is broken down by water.

Examples

to hydrolyze a compound
Cellulose does not easily hydrolyze in the human digestive tract.
Some enzymes can hydrolyze fats into glycerol and fatty acids.
The catalyst helps to hydrolyze the peptide bonds in proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decompose: to break down into simpler parts or elements
  • antonym – synthesize: to combine elements to form a compound
