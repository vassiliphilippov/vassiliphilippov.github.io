franchise

noun
a right or privilege granted by government
Pronunciation: / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
a statutory right or privilege granted by a government to a person or group, especially the right to vote in public elections

Examples

the right to franchise
After years of protest, the government finally granted the franchise to all adult citizens.
Many countries extended the franchise to women in the early twentieth century.
The movement for civil rights was focused on expanding the franchise and ending discriminatory laws.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disenfranchisement: being deprived of the right to vote
  • synonym – suffrage: the right to vote in political elections
