to ground

verb
hit a baseball along the ground
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(in sports) to hit or throw a ball so that it travels along or touches the ground

Examples

to ground a ball
After grounding into a double play, he was visibly frustrated.
She usually grounds the ball to the right side when facing left-handed pitchers.
The batter grounded to shortstop for the final out of the inning.
During the last play, he quickly grounded the ball to stop the clock.
It's a penalty if you ground the ball without a receiver nearby.
The quarterback decided to ground the ball to avoid a sack.

  • synonym – spike: to throw the ball forcefully to the ground (American football)
