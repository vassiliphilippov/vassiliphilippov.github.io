to polemise

verb
engage in controversial debate
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɛmɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmʌɪz / (BrE)
To engage in a controversial debate or dispute, especially in writing or speech.

Examples

to polemise against ideas
She often polemises against mainstream economic theories in her columns.
The two professors polemised fiercely in academic journals over the interpretation of the text.
They continued to polemise about foreign policy well into the evening.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agree: have the same opinion
  • synonym – argue: to give reasons for or against something
  • synonym – debate: to discuss opposing reasons; to argue
