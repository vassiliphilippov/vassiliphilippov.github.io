tumbril

noun
farm cart; historical execution cart
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌm.brəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.brɪl / (BrE)
a two-wheeled cart, especially one for carrying dung on a farm; historically, such carts were used to transport prisoners to the guillotine during the French Revolution

Examples

old tumbril cart
During the French Revolution, prisoners were carried to their fate in a tumbril.
He studied the museum's weathered tumbril, imagining its somber journeys through Paris's streets.
The old tumbril creaked as it carried manure across the muddy field.
