fertilisation

noun
union of male and female gametes
Pronunciation: / ˌfɝːtɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a male gamete (sperm or pollen) unites with a female gamete (egg or ovule) to form a new organism

Examples

fertilisation of an egg
After fertilisation, the embryo begins to develop rapidly.
Scientists study fertilisation to better understand genetic inheritance.
The fertilisation of the ovule by pollen is crucial in plant reproduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conception: the process of becoming pregnant involving fertilisation
  • synonym – pollination: transfer of pollen to fertilise plants
  • antonym – sterilisation: process that makes an organism unable to reproduce
