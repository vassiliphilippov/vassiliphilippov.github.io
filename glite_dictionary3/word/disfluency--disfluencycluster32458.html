disfluency

noun
lack of fluency in speech or writing
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfluənsi / (AmE), / dɪsˈfluːənsi / (BrE)
A lack of smoothness or skill in speaking or writing, often resulting in hesitations, repetitions, or pauses.

Examples

speech disfluency
Researchers often analyze disfluency to gain insight into cognitive processes during speech.
The speaker's disfluency was noticeable through frequent pauses and repeated words.
Writing without any disfluency makes the text much easier to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fluency: the quality of being fluent, smooth and effortless in speech or writing
