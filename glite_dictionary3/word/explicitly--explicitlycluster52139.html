explicitly

adverb
clearly and directly
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsplɪsɪtli / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɪsɪtli / (BrE)
in a clear and direct manner, leaving no room for confusion or doubt

Examples

explicitly state rules
She explicitly told her students not to use mobile phones during class.
The contract stated explicitly that late payments would incur a fee.
The rules are explicitly outlined in the instruction manual.

Related Terms

  • antonym – implicitly: in a way that is not directly stated or expressed
