polyp

noun
small growth on mucous membrane
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.lɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪp / (BrE)
a small, typically benign, vascular growth that forms on the surface of a mucous membrane, such as in the nose, colon, or uterus

Examples

nasal polyp removal
A nasal polyp can cause breathing difficulties and may require removal.
If left untreated, some types of polyps have the potential to develop into cancer.
The doctor discovered a polyp during the patient's routine colonoscopy.
