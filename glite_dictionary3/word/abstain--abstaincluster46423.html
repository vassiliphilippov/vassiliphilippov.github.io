to abstain

verb
deliberately choose not to do or consume
Pronunciation: / əbˈsteɪn / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately choose not to do or consume something, especially something considered undesirable or unhealthy

Examples

to abstain from alcohol
Because he was driving, Tom chose to abstain from drinking alcohol at the party.
During Lent, many people abstain from eating meat as a sign of devotion.
She decided to abstain from dessert in order to stick to her diet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – refrain: to stop oneself from doing something
