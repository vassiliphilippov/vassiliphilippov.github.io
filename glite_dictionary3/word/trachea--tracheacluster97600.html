trachea

noun
airway tube in the throat
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪ.ki.ə / (AmE), / ˈtræki.ə / (BrE)
the tube in the throat with cartilaginous rings that carries air from the larynx to the bronchi in the lungs

Examples

human trachea
A blockage in the trachea can make breathing difficult.
Air passes through the trachea before reaching the lungs.
The doctor examined the patient's trachea for signs of injury.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – bronchus: major air passages branching from the trachea into the lungs
  • synonym – windpipe: another term for the trachea
