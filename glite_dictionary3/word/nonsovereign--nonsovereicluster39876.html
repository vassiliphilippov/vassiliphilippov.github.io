nonsovereign

adjective
not having full self-government
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɑv.əˌrɪn / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɒv(ə)rɪn / (BrE)
(of peoples or political entities) not possessing full self-governing authority; subject to control by external powers.

Examples

nonsovereign territory status
Many territories remain nonsovereign and are governed by a colonial power.
Several nonsovereign states participate in international events with restricted rights.
The status of a nonsovereign nation often limits its ability to negotiate independently.

  • antonym – sovereign: having full independent authority or self-government
