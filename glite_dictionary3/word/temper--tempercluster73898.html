to temper

verb
adjust pitch of a piano or instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpər / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to adjust the pitch of (a piano or other musical instrument) to achieve the desired tuning, especially by altering the tuning to fit a particular temperament

Examples

to temper a piano
After years of experience, she could temper a piano quickly and accurately.
He carefully tempered the piano before the concert to achieve a well-tuned sound.
The technician will temper the instrument to ensure every note is perfectly balanced.
