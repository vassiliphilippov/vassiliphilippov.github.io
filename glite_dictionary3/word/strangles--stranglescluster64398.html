strangles

noun
bacterial disease of horses
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡəlz / (AmE), / ˈstraŋɡlz / (BrE)
An acute infectious disease of horses, caused by Streptococcus bacteria, resulting in swelling and inflammation of the mucous membranes, especially of the respiratory tract.

Examples

strangles infection in horses
A vaccine is available to help protect horses from contracting strangles.
Quarantine is essential when strangles is detected in a stable to prevent the spread of infection.
The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with strangles after noticing the swollen lymph nodes.
