to requite

verb
to repay or return (a favor or feeling)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkwaɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪt / (BrE)
to make repayment for or return (a favor, service, wrongdoing, love, etc.)

Examples

to requite a favor
He longed to requite her love, but could not find the right words.
She hoped to requite his kindness with a thoughtful gift.
Their loyalty was requited by generous rewards from the company.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgive: to stop feeling resentment towards someone
  • synonym – repay: to pay back money or a favor
  • synonym – retaliate: to take revenge or return an attack
