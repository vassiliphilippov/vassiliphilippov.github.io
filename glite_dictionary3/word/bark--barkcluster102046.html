bark

noun
outer covering of tree trunks and roots
Pronunciation: / bɑrk / (AmE), / bɑːk / (BrE)
the tough, protective outer covering of the stems and roots of trees and other woody plants

Examples

tree bark
He peeled the bark away to reveal the smooth wood beneath.
Some medicines are derived from the bark of tropical trees.
The bark of the old oak tree was thick and deeply grooved.
