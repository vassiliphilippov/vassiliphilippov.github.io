grumbling

adjective
continuous, low-pitched throbbing sound
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) continuous, full, and low-pitched; resembling a persistent rumble or growl.

Examples

grumbling engine noise
A grumbling sound came from the old engine as it started up.
The storm brought a grumbling noise that seemed to shake the windows all night.
We listened to the distant, grumbling thunder as the rain began to fall.
