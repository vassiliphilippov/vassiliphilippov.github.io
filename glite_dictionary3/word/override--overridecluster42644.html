override

noun
the act of overruling or canceling
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌraɪd / (BrE)
an act of overruling, canceling, or counteracting the effect or authority of something

Examples

override a veto
Congress voted to override the president's veto, nullifying the original decision.
The judge issued an override to prevent the law from taking effect.
There is a manual override for the automatic safety system in case of emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overrule: to rule against or reject the decision of another
  • antonym – validate: to make valid or confirm the effectiveness of something
