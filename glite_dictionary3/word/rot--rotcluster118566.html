to rot

verb
become physically weaker due to decline
Pronunciation: / rɑt / (AmE), / rɒt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to become physically weaker, often over time, especially because of illness, neglect, or confinement.

Examples

to rot in prison
After years in captivity, the prisoners began to rot in their cells, rarely seeing sunlight.
If you don't eat properly while ill, your body can slowly rot from weakness.
Left untreated, she feared her grandfather would simply rot away in the nursing home.

Related Terms

  • synonym – waste away: to gradually become thinner and weaker, often due to illness
