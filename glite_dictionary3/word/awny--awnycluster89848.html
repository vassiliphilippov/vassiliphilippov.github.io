awny

adjective
having bristlelike appendages (esp. in grasses)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔni / (AmE), / ˈɔːni / (BrE)
(of a plant) having awns; bearing bristlelike or hairlike appendages on the flowering parts, especially in grasses and cereals.

Examples

awny wheat variety
Compared to other varieties, this wheat is more awny, making it distinctive among local crops.
Researchers collected several awny grasses from the meadow for their experiment.
The botanist identified the species as awny due to its prominent bristles on the seed heads.
