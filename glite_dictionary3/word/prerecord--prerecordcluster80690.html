to prerecord

verb
record in advance (for broadcast)
Pronunciation: / priː.rɪˈkɔrd / (AmE), / priː.rɪˈkɔːd / (BrE)
to record (audio or video content) in advance of its broadcast or public presentation

Examples

to prerecord a show
The host prerecorded her opening remarks earlier in the day.
They are prerecording several episodes to get ahead of schedule.
We decided to prerecord the interview to avoid technical issues during the live broadcast.

Related Terms

  • synonym – record: to capture audio, video, or data for later use
