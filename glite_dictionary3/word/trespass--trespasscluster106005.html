trespass

noun
unlawful entry onto property
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛs.pæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛs.pæs / (BrE)
The act of entering someone's land or property without permission or legal right.

Examples

trespass on private property
A sign was posted to warn people against trespass.
The company filed a lawsuit for trespass following repeated unauthorized entry onto its property.
The farmer accused the hikers of trespass after finding them on his land.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intrusion: the act of wrongfully entering upon, seizing, or taking possession of the property of another
