fibre

noun
indigestible plant material aiding digestion
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
Indigestible plant material found in food that helps stimulate movement in the intestines and aids digestion.

Examples

dietary fibre
A high-fibre diet can help prevent constipation and other digestive issues.
Whole grains, fruits, and vegetables are good sources of dietary fibre.
You should eat plenty of fibre to maintain a healthy digestive system.
