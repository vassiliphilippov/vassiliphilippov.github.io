guanine

noun
a base in DNA and RNA; pairs with cytosine
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwɑˌnin / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːniːn / (BrE)
a purine base found in DNA and RNA that pairs with cytosine in the structure of nucleic acids

Examples

guanine base in DNA
In RNA, guanine always pairs with cytosine just like it does in DNA.
Scientists discovered that guanine is one of the four main bases in genetic material.
The DNA sequence showed that guanine was paired with cytosine in several locations.
