neck

noun
narrow part of an object
Pronunciation: / nɛk / (AmE), / nɛk / (BrE)
the narrow part of an object, such as a bottle or instrument, that resembles the neck of a person or animal in position or form

Examples

neck of a bottle
She held the bottle by its neck and poured the juice.
The guitar has a long neck that makes it easy to play higher notes.
The vase broke at the neck, leaving sharp edges.
