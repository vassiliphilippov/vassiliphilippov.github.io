Lysimachus

proper noun
Macedonian general under Alexander the Great
Pronunciation: / laɪˈsɪməkəs / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪməkəs / (BrE)
(c.355–281 BC) A Macedonian general who served under Alexander the Great and later became ruler of Thrace, Asia Minor, and Macedonia.

Examples

Lysimachus ruled Thrace
Lysimachus was one of the prominent Diadochi after Alexander the Great's death.
After the battle of Ipsus, Lysimachus gained control over much of Asia Minor.
The coins issued by Lysimachus often depicted Alexander as a god.
