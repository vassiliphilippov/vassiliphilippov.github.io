to dissertate

verb
talk or write formally at length
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsərˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsəteɪt / (BrE)
to speak or write at length and in a formal, detailed manner about a topic, often in an academic context

Examples

to dissertate on literature
During the seminar, she dissertated extensively about modernist literature.
He tends to dissertate when asked about his research, providing far more detail than necessary.
The professor dissertated on the philosophical implications of artificial intelligence for over an hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expound: to explain a theory or idea in detail
  • synonym – lecture: to deliver an educational talk to an audience, especially students
