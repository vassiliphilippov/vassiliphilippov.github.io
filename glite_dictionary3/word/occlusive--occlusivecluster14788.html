occlusive

noun
consonant formed by blocking then releasing airflow
Pronunciation: / əˈkluːsɪv / (AmE), / əˈkluːsɪv / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced by completely blocking the airflow in the vocal tract and then suddenly releasing it; also known as a stop.

Examples

occlusive consonant sound
In French, the word begins with a voiced occlusive rather than a fricative.
Phoneticians study how the place of articulation affects the quality of an occlusive.
The English sounds /p/, /t/, and /k/ are all examples of occlusives.
