wide-open

adjective
lax in enforcing laws or rules
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪd ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
(of a place or system) not strictly regulated or controlled; unusually lax in enforcing laws or rules.

Examples

wide-open city
Corruption was rampant in the wide-open port town of the 1920s.
Some politicians accused the local government of maintaining a wide-open approach to gambling laws.
The city became wide-open during Prohibition, with illegal bars operating everywhere.
