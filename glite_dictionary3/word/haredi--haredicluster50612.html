Haredi

noun
strictly Orthodox Jewish sect member
Pronunciation: / hɑːˈreɪdi / (AmE), / hɑːˈreɪdi / (BrE)
A member of any of several strict Orthodox Jewish sects that reject modern secular culture and often do not recognize the spiritual authority of the modern state of Israel.

Examples

Haredi community
He grew up in a Haredi household in Jerusalem, far removed from modern secular influences.
Several Haredi communities maintain strict religious practices regardless of their country's secular laws.
The Haredi population in Israel has been steadily increasing over the past few decades.
