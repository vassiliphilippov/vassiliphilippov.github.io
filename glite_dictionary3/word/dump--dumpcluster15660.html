to dump

verb
sell goods at artificially low prices
Pronunciation: / dʌmp / (AmE), / dʌmp / (BrE)
to sell goods at artificially low prices, especially in order to undermine competitors or gain market share

Examples

to dump goods
After losing market share, the manufacturer decided to dump excess stock overseas.
Several countries imposed tariffs to prevent foreign firms from dumping steel at below-market prices.
The company was accused of dumping cheap products in the European market.

Related Terms

  • synonym – undersell: to sell goods at a lower price than a competitor
