well

adverb
with high probability; very likely
Pronunciation: / wɛl / (AmE), / wɛl / (BrE)
used as an adverb to indicate that something is very likely or probable; in all likelihood.

Examples

could well happen
He could well become the next president if the polls are correct.
It might well snow before the end of the month.
You may well find it hard to finish the project on time.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – possibly: might happen or be true, but less certain than 'probably'
  • synonym – probably: in all likelihood; almost certainly
