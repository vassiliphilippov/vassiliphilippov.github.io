veracious

adjective
accurate and truthful
Pronunciation: / vəˈreɪʃəs / (AmE), / vəˈreɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a statement, account, etc.) accurate and truthful; not containing or affected by falsehood.

Examples

veracious account
Historians praised his work for its veracious treatment of the facts.
The reporter provided a veracious account of the events as they unfolded.
We rely on veracious data to make informed decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accurate: correct and without any mistakes
  • antonym – false: not true; incorrect
  • synonym – truthful: telling or expressing the truth
