salty

adjective
annoyed, bitter (slang)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔlti / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlti / (BrE)
informal, slang: annoyed, irritated, or bitter about something, often after losing or being treated unfairly.

Examples

salty attitude
After the referee's call, she stayed quiet but looked clearly salty about the decision.
He got salty online after losing the game and complained to his teammates.
Don't be salty just because you didn't win; try practicing for the next match.

Related Terms

  • synonym – annoyed: feeling slightly angry or irritated
  • antonym – unbothered: not upset or affected by something
