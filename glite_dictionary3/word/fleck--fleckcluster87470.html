fleck

noun
a small spot or fragment
Pronunciation: / flɛk / (AmE), / flɛk / (BrE)
a very small spot, mark, or fragment, especially one that stands out from its surroundings

Examples

fleck of paint
A fleck of dust landed on the white tablecloth.
He rubbed at the fleck of paint on his shirt sleeve.
Tiny flecks of glass were scattered across the floor.
Each lily pad was adorned with a single fleck of dew in the morning sunlight.
She noticed a fleck of blue paint on her shirt after finishing the mural.
The old photograph had a fleck of dust that blurred one corner of the image.

Related Terms

  • synonym – speck: a tiny spot or small particle
  • synonym – spot: a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture
  • synonym – streak: a long, thin mark, typically one made by a different substance or color
