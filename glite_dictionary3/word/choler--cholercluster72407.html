choler

noun
an old medical humor linked to anger
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
(historical) In pre-modern medicine, one of the four bodily humors, believed to be secreted by the liver and to cause irritability and anger.

Examples

yellow choler humor
His rash behavior was often attributed to an abundance of choler in his system.
Physicians of the Middle Ages believed that balancing one's choler was essential for good health.
The excess of choler was once thought to make a person quick-tempered and irritable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anger: strong feeling of annoyance, displeasure, or hostility
  • degree lower – phlegm: another of the four bodily humors, believed to cause sluggishness
