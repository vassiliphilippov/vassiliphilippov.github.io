to erect

verb
build or put up (a structure)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to build or put up something, especially in an upright position (such as a building, wall, or statue)

Examples

to erect a building
A statue was erected in the center of the park to honor the founder.
Engineers are erecting a temporary bridge while repairs are made to the old one.
The workers will erect a new office building downtown next year.
A statue was erected in honor of the famous scientist in the city square.
The workers will erect a new fence along the property line next week.
They erected a tent in the field before the rain started.

Related Terms

  • synonym – construct: to build or form by putting together parts
  • antonym – demolish: to pull down or destroy (a building or structure)
