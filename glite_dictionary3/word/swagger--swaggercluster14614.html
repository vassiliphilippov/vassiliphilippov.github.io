to swagger

verb
walk confidently to impress
Pronunciation: / ˈswæɡər /; / ˈswæɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈswæɡə / (BrE)
to walk or behave in a confident and sometimes arrogant or boastful way, often to impress others

Examples

to swagger down the street
He tried to swagger into the meeting, pretending to be in control.
She couldn't help but swagger a little after winning the award.
The actors swagger across the stage, embodying their confident characters.
He likes to swagger into meetings as if he owns the company.
She was swaggering about her recent promotion at every opportunity.
The young athlete swaggered across the field, soaking up the applause.

Related Terms

  • antonym – humble: to act in a modest or unassuming way
  • degree lower – saunter: to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, without hurry or effort
  • synonym – strut: to walk with a stiff, erect, and proud gait
