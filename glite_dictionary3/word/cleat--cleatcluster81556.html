cleat

noun
projection on shoe sole to prevent slipping
Pronunciation: / klit / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
a projecting piece of metal or leather attached to the sole of a shoe to prevent slipping, especially in sports footwear

Examples

cleat on a shoe
A broken cleat can increase the risk of slipping on wet grass.
She replaced the worn-out cleats on her running shoes for better grip.
The football player's cleat left a distinctive mark on the muddy field.
