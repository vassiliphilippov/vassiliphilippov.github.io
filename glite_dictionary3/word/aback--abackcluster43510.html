aback

adverb
of sails, having wind against the front
Pronunciation: / əˈbæk / (AmE), / əˈbak / (BrE)
(of a ship's sails) with the wind pressing against the forward side, causing the ship to slow or stop; said of sails.

Examples

aback by strong wind
If the helmsman turns too quickly, the jib will be aback, causing the vessel to stall.
The sudden squall caught us off guard, and soon the sails were aback.
With all yards aback, progress was halted until the crew could adjust the rigging.
