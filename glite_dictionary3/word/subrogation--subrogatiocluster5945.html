subrogation

noun
legal substitution of creditor rights
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbroʊˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) the substitution of one party for another as creditor, giving the substitute party the legal rights and remedies of the original creditor.

Examples

subrogation of rights
After payment, the lender exercised subrogation rights against the debtor.
The insurance company claimed subrogation to recover funds from the party at fault.
Through subrogation, the firm assumed all legal claims of the original creditor.
