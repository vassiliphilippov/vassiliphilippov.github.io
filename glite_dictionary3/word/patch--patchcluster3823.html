patch

noun
small contrasting area or section
Pronunciation: / pætʃ / (AmE), / pætʃ / (BrE)
a small area that is different from what surrounds it, such as in color, material, or a piece of ground

Examples

a patch of color
Leopards are known for the patches on their coats.
There was a patch of bright blue on the otherwise gray wall.
We found several patches of ice on the sidewalk this morning.
She grew strawberries in a small patch behind the house.
The children searched for frogs in the vegetable patch.
We planted herbs in a sunny patch of the garden.
