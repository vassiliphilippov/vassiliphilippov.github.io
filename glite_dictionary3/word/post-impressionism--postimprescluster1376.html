Post-Impressionism

noun
French art movement after Impressionism
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊst ɪmˈprɛʃ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst ɪmˈprɛʃ.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
a movement in Western painting, especially in France, that developed in the late 19th century as both an extension of Impressionism and a rejection of some of its limitations, emphasizing symbolic content, structure, and order.

Examples

Post-Impressionism in painting
Many art critics believe that Post-Impressionism helped pave the way for modern abstract art.

The influence of Post-Impressionism can be seen in the bold colors and expressive brushwork of the painting.
