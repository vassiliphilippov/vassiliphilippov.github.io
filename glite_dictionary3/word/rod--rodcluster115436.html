rod

noun
rod-shaped bacterium
Pronunciation: / rɑd / (AmE), / rɒd / (BrE)
a bacterium having a cylindrical or elongated shape; a rod-shaped bacterium

Examples

rod-shaped bacteria
E. coli is a commonly studied rod found in the intestines of humans and animals.
Staining techniques can help distinguish a rod from other types of bacteria, such as cocci.
When viewed under a microscope, the rod appears as a straight, elongated cell.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bacillus: a rod-shaped bacterium (also used as a synonym in biology)
  • antonym – coccus: a spherical bacterium (contrasting bacterial shape)
