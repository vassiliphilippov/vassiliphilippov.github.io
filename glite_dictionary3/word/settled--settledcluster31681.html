settled

adjective
stable; not likely to change
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əld / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
(of a situation, condition, or period) stable and unlikely to change; established or fixed.

Examples

settled weather pattern
After days of rain, we finally enjoyed a week of settled weather.
Her routine became more settled after she moved to the countryside.
The political situation remains settled, with no major changes expected.

Related Terms

  • synonym – steady: firm and unwavering; regular and not likely to change
  • antonym – unstable: likely to change or not firmly fixed
