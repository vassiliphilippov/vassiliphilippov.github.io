to encode

verb
convert information into code
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkoʊd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊd / (BrE)
to convert information or data into a particular code, especially so that it can be processed by a computer or transmitted securely

Examples

to encode data
Modern digital cameras can encode pictures directly into JPEG format.
Scientists often encode experimental data before storage to ensure accuracy and security.
The system will encode your message, making it unreadable without the proper key.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decode: to convert coded data back into its original form
