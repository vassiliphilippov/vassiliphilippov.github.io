diminished

adjective
reduced in size or strength (body part)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
made or become smaller, weaker, or less in size, strength, importance, or value

Examples

diminished strength
After her accident, the strength in her right hand was diminished.
Muscle tissue can become diminished if it is not exercised regularly.
The patient's left leg was noticeably diminished after months in a cast.
Her influence in the company has diminished over the last year.
The medication helped restore hearing that had previously been diminished.
The patient's vision was diminished after the accident.
After the scandal, the company's reputation was diminished in the eyes of investors.
He left the meeting feeling diminished by the sharp criticism he received.
Many felt that their achievements were diminished by comparisons to previous generations.
