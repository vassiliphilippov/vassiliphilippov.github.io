period

noun
division of geological time
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
A division of geological time, longer than an epoch and shorter than an era, during which a specific system of rocks was formed.

Examples

Jurassic period in history
Scientists study rock layers to determine the period in which they formed.
The Jurassic period is famous for its abundance of dinosaurs.
This mineral deposit dates back to the Devonian period.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – epoch: a subdivision of a period in geological time
  • degree higher – era: a major division of geological time, longer than a period
