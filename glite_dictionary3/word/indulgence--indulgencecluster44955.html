indulgence

noun
allowing someone's wishes, often too much
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (BrE)
the act or habit of allowing someone to have or do what they want, especially too much or too often

Examples

parental indulgence
His grandmother's indulgence made him a very spoiled child.
Teachers should balance indulgence with clear expectations.
Too much indulgence can lead to a lack of discipline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leniency: the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected
  • antonym – strictness: the quality of being rigorous or not allowing leniency
