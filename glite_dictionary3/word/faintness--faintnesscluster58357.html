faintness

noun
the state of being weak or feeble
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪntnəs /; / ˈfeɪnt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪntnɪs /; / ˈfeɪnt.nəs / (BrE)
a state of physical weakness or dizziness, as if about to faint

Examples

faintness in legs
He felt a sudden faintness in his limbs and had to sit down.
The faintness of the signal made it difficult to hear the message clearly.
There was a faintness in her voice as she spoke after the long illness.
During the long ceremony, several people complained of faintness and needed to sit down.
She felt a sudden faintness as she stood up quickly from her chair.
The patient's faintness was caused by dehydration and lack of food.
