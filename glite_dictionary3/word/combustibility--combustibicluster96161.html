combustibility

noun
capacity to catch fire and burn
Pronunciation: / kəmˌbʌstəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kəmˌbʌstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being capable of catching fire and burning; the ability of a substance to ignite and sustain combustion.

Examples

high combustibility
Because of its high combustibility, gasoline must be handled with extreme caution.
Scientists evaluated the combustibility of several household chemicals in the laboratory.
The combustibility of the materials used in construction is carefully tested for safety regulations.
