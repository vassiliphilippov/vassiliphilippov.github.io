to contrive

verb
devise or invent after mental effort
Pronunciation: / kənˈtraɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪv / (BrE)
to invent, devise, or plan something in a clever or skillful way

Examples

to contrive a clever plan
After hours of discussion, the team managed to contrive a viable solution to the problem.
She contrived an excuse to avoid attending the meeting.
They are trying to contrive a system that will save both time and money.
He contrived a clever solution to the complex problem.
She managed to contrive a meeting between the two leaders despite their tight schedules.
They had to contrive a way to get past the locked gate without being noticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – concoct: to invent or devise, especially in a clever or deceitful way
  • synonym – devise: to plan or invent by careful thought
  • synonym – plan: to decide on and arrange in advance
