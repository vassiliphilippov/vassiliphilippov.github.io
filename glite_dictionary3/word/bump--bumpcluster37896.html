to bump

verb
reduce in rank or position
Pronunciation: / bʌmp / (AmE), / bʌmp / (BrE)
to remove or force someone from a position or place, or to demote someone

Examples

to bump someone down
After the reorganization, several employees were bumped to lower positions.
Due to repeated mistakes, he was bumped from manager to assistant.
The company decided to bump her down to a junior role after the audit.
Several passengers were bumped from the overbooked flight at the last minute.
The senior manager was bumped from her corner office to make way for the new CEO.
When the team expanded, Mark was bumped off his project to make space for a new leader.

Related Terms

  • synonym – demote: to lower someone in rank or position
  • synonym – dislodge: to force someone out of a position or place
  • antonym – promote: to raise to a higher rank or position
