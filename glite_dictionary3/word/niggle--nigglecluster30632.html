to niggle

verb
worry or fuss unnecessarily
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪɡl / (BrE)
to worry or fuss over something in a way that is excessive or unnecessary

Examples

to niggle at worries
It started to niggle at him that he might have forgotten to lock the door.
She tends to niggle over every small detail, even when it's not important.
Stop letting minor issues niggle you so much and try to relax.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dwell: to linger over or worry about something repeatedly
  • synonym – fuss: to show unnecessary concern or excitement
