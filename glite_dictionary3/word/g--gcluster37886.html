G

noun
unit of digital information (gigabyte)
Pronunciation: / dʒi / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information storage equal to about one billion bytes (gigabyte)

Examples

64 G storage
My phone only has 64 G of storage, so I have to delete photos regularly.
Our internet plan comes with a data limit of 100 G per month.
The video game requires at least 20 G of free space on your hard drive.
My laptop comes with a 512 G hard drive, which is plenty for my needs.
She transferred over 100 G of photos to her backup disk.
The software update requires at least 2 G of free disk space.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – MB: A unit of digital information equal to 1,048,576 bytes (megabyte), smaller than gigabyte
  • degree higher – TB: A unit of digital information equal to 1,099,511,627,776 bytes (terabyte), larger than gigabyte
  • synonym – gigabyte: unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bytes
