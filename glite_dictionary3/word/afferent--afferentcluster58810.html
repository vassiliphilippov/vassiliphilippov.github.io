afferent

noun
nerve carrying signals to CNS
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.ɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
a nerve that carries sensory impulses toward the central nervous system

Examples

sensory afferent nerve
Damage to the afferent pathways may result in loss of feeling or reflexes.
Sensory information from the skin is transmitted to the brain by afferent fibers.
The doctor explained that an injury to an afferent nerve can reduce sensation in that area.

Related Terms

  • antonym – efferent: a nerve that carries impulses away from the central nervous system
