smattering

noun
slight or superficial understanding
Pronunciation: / ˈsmæt̬.ɚ.rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A small amount of knowledge about a subject or language; a slight or superficial understanding.

Examples

a smattering of French
He has only a smattering of Spanish, just enough to ask for directions.
She gained a smattering of knowledge about art history from her weekend classes.
The article provides only a smattering of information about the new technology.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expertise: deep or thorough knowledge in a subject
  • synonym – glimmer: a faint or slight indication of something, especially knowledge
