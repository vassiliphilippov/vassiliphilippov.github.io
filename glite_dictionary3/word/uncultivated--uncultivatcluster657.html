uncultivated

adjective
lacking refinement or education
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌltəˌveɪtɪd /; / ʌnˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking education, refinement, or cultural development

Examples

an uncultivated person
Despite his wealth, he remained uncultivated and uninterested in the arts.
His uncultivated manners made him stand out at the formal dinner.
She considered his interests too narrow and uncultivated for her taste.
He felt uncultivated next to his well-read and sophisticated colleagues.
Her uncultivated manners set her apart in the refined company she found herself in.
Some critics regarded him as uncultivated because he showed little interest in literature or the arts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cultivated: having or showing good education, taste, and manners
  • antonym – cultured: having refined taste, manners, and education
  • synonym – ignorant: lacking knowledge or awareness
