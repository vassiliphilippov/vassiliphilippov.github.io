to collar

verb
seize or grab by the neck or collar
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
to seize, grasp, or arrest someone, especially by grabbing their collar or neck (can be informal for police arrest)

Examples

to collar a suspect
He collared me after the meeting to ask a few questions.
She quickly collared her dog before it could run into the street.
The officer managed to collar the suspect as he tried to escape.
After months of investigation, the police finally collared the infamous jewel thief.
The detective managed to collar the suspect before he left the train station.
The security guard collared two shoplifters near the exit and called the police.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arrest: to seize and take into custody by legal authority
  • synonym – grab: to seize suddenly and roughly
  • antonym – release: to set free or let go
