legislature

noun
law-making body
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtʃə / (BrE)
A group of people with the power to make, change, or repeal laws in a country or region.

Examples

national legislature
A two-thirds majority of the legislature is required to change the constitution.
Elections for the national legislature will be held next spring.
The new healthcare bill was approved by the state legislature after weeks of debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parliament: an assembly of representatives that makes laws, especially in the UK and Commonwealth
