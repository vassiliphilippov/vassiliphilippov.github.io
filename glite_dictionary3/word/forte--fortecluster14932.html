forte

adverb
loudly (in music)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrteɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːteɪ / (BrE)
(in music) played or sung loudly; used as a direction to perform a passage with force or intensity.

Examples

play forte suddenly
In this section, the music shifts suddenly from piano to forte.
The composer marked the chorus to enter forte to increase the drama.
The conductor signaled for the brass section to play forte in the next passage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – piano: in music: softly or quietly
