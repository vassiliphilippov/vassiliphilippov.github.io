to obsess

verb
to preoccupy or trouble the mind continuously
Pronunciation: / əbˈsɛs / (AmE), / əbˈsɛs / (BrE)
to fill or dominate the mind of someone continually and in a troubling way; to be persistently preoccupied with something

Examples

to obsess over problems
He let worries about his career obsess him day and night.
If you obsess over small details, you'll never finish the project.
Memories of the incident continued to obsess her for years.
He tends to obsess about every small detail before an important meeting.
She started to obsess with the idea of moving to a different city.
Some people obsess over social media likes and comments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – haunt: to continually trouble or preoccupy the mind
  • antonym – ignore: to pay no attention to
  • synonym – preoccupy: to absorb or occupy the mind to the exclusion of other thoughts
  • synonym – worry: to experience mental distress or agitation
