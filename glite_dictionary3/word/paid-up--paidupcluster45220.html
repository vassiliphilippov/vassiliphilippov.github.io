paid-up

adjective
having paid in advance; fully paid
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪd ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˌpeɪd ˈʌp / (BrE)
Having paid the full amount required in advance, especially for membership, insurance, or shares.

Examples

paid-up member
All paid-up members can attend the annual meeting for free.
Only paid-up shareholders are eligible to vote at the meeting.
She has paid-up insurance, so her policy is valid until next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unpaid: not yet paid or settled
