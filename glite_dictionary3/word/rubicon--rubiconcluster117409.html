Rubicon

noun
point of no return; decisive commitment
Pronunciation: / ˈruː.bɪ.kɑn / (AmE), / ˈruː.bɪ.kɒn / (BrE)
a point of no return, where a decision or action commits one irrevocably to a course of events

Examples

cross the Rubicon
After signing the contract, the team had passed the Rubicon and could not back down.
Crossing the Rubicon meant accepting whatever consequences would follow.
When she resigned from her job, she knew she had crossed her personal Rubicon.
