slick

noun
thin layer of oil on water
Pronunciation: / slɪk / (AmE), / slɪk / (BrE)
a thin layer of oil, grease, or other substance on a surface, especially on water, or a smooth, slippery area or patch

Examples

oil slick
An oil slick covered much of the harbor after the tanker accident.
Cleanup crews worked to contain the floating slick before it reached the coastline.
The ducks struggled to escape the thick slick spreading over the pond.
After the oil spill, a thick slick covered the surface of the water.
He slipped on the icy slick at the entrance to the building.
You could feel the slick left by the cleaning product on the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oil spill: a release of liquid petroleum into the environment, especially marine areas
  • synonym – slickness: the quality of being smooth and slippery
