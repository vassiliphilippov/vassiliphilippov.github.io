to sponsor

verb
to officially support or back a proposal or project
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑnsər / (AmE), / ˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
to support, take responsibility for, or provide money for a person, project, or activity

Examples

to sponsor a project
If no one is willing to sponsor the proposal, it cannot be considered for funding.
Several countries have decided to sponsor the peace talks in the region.
The senator agreed to sponsor the new environmental legislation in Congress.
Several major brands sponsor the annual music festival.
She decided to sponsor a child in need through a charitable organization.
The company agreed to sponsor the local football team for the next season.

  • synonym – patronize: to give financial or other support to a person or organization
  • synonym – support: to give assistance or backing to something
