to upscale

verb
make more upscale; upgrade quality/market
Pronunciation: / 8cpccke6al / (AmE), / 8cpccke6al / (BrE)
to make (something) more stylish, expensive, or appealing to wealthier customers; to raise the quality, price, or market position of something.

Examples

to upscale a hotel
The hotel chain announced plans to upscale its properties to attract luxury travelers.
They decided to upscale the brand by improving packaging and raising prices next season.
Local councils are accused of upscaling neighborhoods, pushing out long-time residents through higher rents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gentrify: to renovate and improve a district so that it attracts wealthier residents (often displacing earlier residents)
  • synonym – upgrade: to improve or raise something to a higher standard or level
