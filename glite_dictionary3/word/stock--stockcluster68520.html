to stock

verb
supply with livestock
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɒk / (BrE)
to supply a farm or area with animals; to supply a body of water with fish

Examples

to stock a pond
After purchasing the land, they stocked it with cows and horses.
The farmer stocked the pasture with a new herd of sheep.
To improve productivity, agricultural programs often help families stock their farms with livestock.
Officials regularly stock lakes to maintain healthy fish populations for anglers.
Several conservation groups helped stock the pond to support local fishing efforts.
The community decided to stock the river with trout every spring.

  • synonym – restock: to replenish with a new stock
