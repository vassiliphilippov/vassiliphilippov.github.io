to sweep

verb
move across quickly and smoothly
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiːp / (BrE)
to move or make a movement in a wide, smooth, or gliding arc, or to gesture broadly

Examples

to sweep across the room
A chilly wind swept through the empty streets late at night.
A wave of relief swept over her when she heard the good news.
The lights swept across the dark auditorium as the curtains opened.
He swept through the airport terminal, barely glancing at the people around him.
Spotlights swept across the crowd during the concert’s opening act.
The grand staircase allowed her to sweep into the ballroom with elegance.
He swept his hand across the table, gesturing for everyone to leave.
She made a dramatic entrance, sweeping her arm to indicate the whole room.
The dancer swept around the stage with elegant, flowing movements.

  • synonym – glide: move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort
