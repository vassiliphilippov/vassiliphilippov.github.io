to retouch

verb
make small changes to improve appearance
Pronunciation: / riˈtʌtʃ / (AmE), / riˈtʌtʃ / (BrE)
to improve or alter a photograph, picture, or other image, especially by making slight changes to its appearance

Examples

to retouch a photo
He spent hours retouching the old family photographs on his computer.
Many magazines retouch photographs to remove small imperfections from the models' faces.
The artist decided to retouch the portrait to make the colors more vivid.
