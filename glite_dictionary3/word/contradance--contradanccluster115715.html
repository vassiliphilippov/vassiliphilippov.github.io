contradance

noun
folk dance with couples in lines or sets
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.trəˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trəˌdɑːns / (BrE)
a type of folk dance in which couples are arranged in sets or lines and perform a sequence of figures, often facing one another

Examples

contradance with partners
In a classic contradance, couples switch partners as they move up and down the set.
She learned the steps of the contradance at a local folk dance workshop.
The festival featured a lively contradance with musicians playing traditional tunes.
