Sr.

noun
title for older of two with the same name
Pronunciation: / siː ˈɑːr / (AmE), / siː ˈɑː / (BrE)
A title added after a man's name to indicate he is the older of two family members, usually father and son, who share the same name.

Examples

John Smith Sr.
After retiring, Martin Wells Sr. handed the business over to his namesake son.
Please address the invitation to Robert Lee, Sr., not his son.
The company was founded by Sr. John Thompson in 1923.

  • antonym – Jr.: title for the younger of two with the same name
