hospitable

adjective
favorable to life or growth (of a place)
Pronunciation: / hɑˈspɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / hɒˈspɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of an environment, place, etc.) providing favorable conditions for life, growth, or activity.

Examples

hospitable environment
Desert plants have adapted to survive in a less hospitable climate than most.
Mars is currently not hospitable to human life due to its harsh conditions.
The scientist searched for a hospitable environment to study new forms of bacteria.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inhospitable: not providing favorable conditions for life or growth
