crater

noun
bowl-shaped depression from impact
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.tə / (BrE)
A large, bowl-shaped hole in the ground or on a planet's surface, typically caused by a meteorite or bomb impact.

Examples

crater from explosion
After the explosion, a huge crater appeared in the middle of the street.
Scientists studied the crater to learn more about the asteroid impact.
The crater left by the meteorite was over a kilometer wide.

Related Terms

  • synonym – caldera: a large volcanic crater, typically formed by collapse after an eruption
