scrooge

noun
a person who is very stingy
Pronunciation: / skruːdʒ / (AmE), / skruːdʒ / (BrE)
a person who is very stingy and unwilling to spend money or share with others

Examples

a scrooge with money
Despite his wealth, he lived like a scrooge and rarely bought anything new.
Don't be such a scrooge; it's just a small donation for charity.
Every office has at least one scrooge who refuses to contribute to the holiday party.

Related Terms

  • synonym – miser: a person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible
  • synonym – skinflint: a person who is extremely reluctant to spend money
  • antonym – spendthrift: a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way
