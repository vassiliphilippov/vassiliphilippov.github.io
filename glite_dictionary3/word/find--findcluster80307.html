to find

verb
realize oneself to be in a state or place
Pronunciation: / faɪnd / (AmE), / faɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person) to perceive or realize that oneself is in a particular condition or place, often unexpectedly or unintentionally.

Examples

find yourself alone
After a long walk, we found ourselves lost in the woods.
He found himself regretting the decision he made years ago.
She found herself unexpectedly promoted to manager after just a year at the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discover: to learn or find out something previously unknown
  • antonym – lose: to fail to keep or maintain; cease to have, either physically or in an abstract sense
