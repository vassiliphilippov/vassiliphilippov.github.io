circumnavigation

noun
traveling around something completely
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝː.kəmˌnæv.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˌnæv.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of traveling all the way around something, such as the Earth, especially by ship or airplane

Examples

circumnavigation of the globe
Advances in technology have made circumnavigation by air much faster than by sea.
Her solo circumnavigation of the globe inspired adventurers around the world.
The first successful circumnavigation of the world was completed by Magellan's crew in the 16th century.
