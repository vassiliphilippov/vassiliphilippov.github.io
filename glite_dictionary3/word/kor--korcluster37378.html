kor

noun
ancient Hebrew unit of capacity
Pronunciation: / kɔr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
an ancient Hebrew unit of capacity equal to 10 baths or 10 ephahs, used chiefly in biblical and historical contexts

Examples

a kor of grain
A kor was a common measurement mentioned in Old Testament accounts.
In ancient times, a kor of grain could feed an entire village for weeks.
The temple records listed several kors of olive oil among the offerings.
