omerta

noun
Mafia code of silence
Pronunciation: / oʊˈmɛr.tɑ / (AmE), / əʊˈmɛə.tə / (BrE)
A code of silence observed by members of the Mafia, especially a refusal to cooperate with authorities about criminal activities.

Examples

omerta code of silence
Breaking the omerta is considered an act of betrayal within Mafia circles.
Many witnesses in the trial remained silent, bound by the tradition of omerta.
The gang operated under an unbreakable omerta, making it nearly impossible for the police to gather evidence.
