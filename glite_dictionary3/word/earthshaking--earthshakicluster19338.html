earthshaking

adjective
extremely significant or impactful
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely significant or important; having a very great impact or influence.

Examples

earthshaking event
Her resignation was an earthshaking moment for the entire organization.
No earthshaking changes are expected in this year's budget.
The scientist's discovery was considered an earthshaking event in the field of physics.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insignificant: too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • synonym – momentous: of great importance or significance, especially in having a bearing on future events
  • synonym – world-shaking: having an effect significant enough to affect the entire world
