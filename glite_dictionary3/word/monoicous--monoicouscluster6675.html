monoicous

adjective
having male and female reproductive organs on one individual
Pronunciation: / məˈnɔɪ.ɪ.kəs / (AmE), / məˈnɔɪ.ɪ.kəs / (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) having both male and female reproductive organs on the same individual.

Examples

monoicous plant species
Some liverworts are monoicous and can produce both types of gametes on the same plant.
The monoicous nature of the species facilitates self-fertilization in isolated habitats.
When studying mosses, distinguishing between monoicous and dioicous forms is important for understanding their reproduction.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dioicous: having male and female reproductive organs on separate individuals
