to federalise

verb
join to form a federation
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to join together into a federation or to unite states or groups for a common purpose

Examples

to federalise several states
Efforts to federalise the region met with strong support from local leaders.
Several provinces agreed to federalise after lengthy negotiations.
The two countries decided to federalise in order to strengthen their economic ties.
After the revolution, several small states decided to federalise for greater strength and unity.
The region was formally federalised in the mid-nineteenth century, creating a new central government.
There are ongoing debates about whether to federalise parts of the health care system.
