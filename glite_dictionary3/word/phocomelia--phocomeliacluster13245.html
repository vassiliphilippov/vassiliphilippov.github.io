phocomelia

noun
congenital limb abnormality (hands/feet attach near trunk)
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊ.kəˈmiː.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.kəˈmiː.li.ə / (BrE)
A rare congenital condition in which the upper part of the arms or legs is missing, causing the hands or feet to be attached close to the trunk; often associated with thalidomide use during pregnancy.

Examples

phocomelia birth defect
Doctors studied photographs of patients with phocomelia to understand the effects of congenital limb abnormalities.
Thalidomide exposure during pregnancy has been linked to cases of phocomelia in newborns.
The child was born with phocomelia, resulting in severely shortened arms.
