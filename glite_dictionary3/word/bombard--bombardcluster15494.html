to bombard

verb
attack with persistent requests or questions
Pronunciation: / bɑmˈbɑrd / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːd / (BrE)
to direct a continuous stream of questions, requests, or other messages at someone, often in an overwhelming way

Examples

to bombard someone with questions
Journalists bombarded the politician with questions as she left the building.
My inbox was bombarded with promotional emails after I signed up for the newsletter.
The teacher was bombarded with complaints after announcing the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barrage: to overwhelm with a large amount of something, especially questions or criticism
  • degree lower – pester: to trouble or annoy someone with frequent or persistent requests or interruptions
