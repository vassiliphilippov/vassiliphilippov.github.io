reject

noun
person or thing that is rejected
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
a person or thing that has been dismissed as inadequate, unacceptable, or inferior

Examples

a reject from school
After the auditions, she felt like a reject but tried not to lose hope.
Clearance bins often contain items that are factory rejects with minor flaws.
He was labeled a social reject in high school, but went on to find his own crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym – candidate: a person being considered for something (as contrast; one is chosen, one rejected)
  • synonym – outcast: a person who has been rejected by society
