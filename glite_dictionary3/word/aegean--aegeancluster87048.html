Aegean

noun
sea between Greece and Turkey
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒiːən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
the part of the Mediterranean Sea located between Greece and Turkey, known for its historical significance as a trade route for ancient civilizations

Examples

the Aegean Sea
Ancient traders frequently sailed across the Aegean to exchange goods and ideas.
Many beautiful islands are scattered throughout the Aegean Sea.
The clear blue waters of the Aegean attract visitors from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Mediterranean: the sea connected to the Atlantic Ocean and surrounded by the Mediterranean region
