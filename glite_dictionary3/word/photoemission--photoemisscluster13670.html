photoemission

noun
emission of electrons due to light exposure
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
The emission of electrons from a material's surface when it is exposed to light, especially from metals.

Examples

photoemission from metal surface
Recent advances in photoemission allow for detailed imaging of atomic structures.
Scientists studied photoemission to understand the behavior of electrons in semiconductors.
The photoemission from the metal surface was measured using sensitive detectors.
