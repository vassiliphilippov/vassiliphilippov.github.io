Darius the Great

proper noun
King of Persia, 550–486 BC
Pronunciation: / dəˈraɪəs ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / dəˈraɪəs ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 550–486 BC) King of Persia who expanded the Persian Empire and invaded Greece, suffering defeat at the Battle of Marathon.

Examples

Darius the Great ruled Persia
Darius the Great ruled the Persian Empire at its height, overseeing vast territory and diverse peoples.
At the Battle of Marathon, Darius the Great faced a surprising defeat by the Athenians.
Many achievements in infrastructure and administration are credited to Darius the Great.
