bain-marie

noun
pan for gently heating food
Pronunciation: / ˌbæn məˈri / (AmE), / ˌbæ̃ məˈriː / (BrE)
a large container of hot water, in which smaller pans are placed to keep food warm or to cook food gently

Examples

bain-marie for melting chocolate
Custards are often cooked in a bain-marie to prevent curdling.
She kept the sauce warm by setting the bowl in a bain-marie during the dinner.
The chef placed the chocolate in a bain-marie to melt it slowly.
