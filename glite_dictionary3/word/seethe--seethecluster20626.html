to seethe

verb
show intense, unexpressed anger or agitation
Pronunciation: / siːð / (AmE), / siːð / (BrE)
to be filled with intense but unexpressed anger or agitation

Examples

to seethe with anger
After the meeting, several employees were quietly seething at the manager's remarks.
Although he appeared composed, his mind continued to seethe over the unfair decision.
She tried to remain calm, but inside she was seething with frustration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boil: to become very angry or agitated
  • synonym – simmer: stay almost at the point of boiling, to feel anger or excitement that is kept hidden
