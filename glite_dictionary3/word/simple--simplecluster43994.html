simple

adjective
innocent or credulous in a childlike way
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪm.pəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.pəl / (BrE)
having or showing a natural, childlike innocence or credulity; lacking sophistication or worldliness.

Examples

simple faith
He gave a simple answer, believing everyone would take his word.
Her simple faith in people was both charming and a little worrying.
There was a simple joy in her laughter, untouched by cynicism.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – gullible: easily deceived or tricked because of trusting others too much
  • synonym – naive: innocent and lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment
