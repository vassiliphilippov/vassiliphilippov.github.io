to maintain

verb
keep something in a particular state
Pronunciation: / meɪnˈteɪn / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn / (BrE)
to cause to continue in a particular state, position, or activity

Examples

to maintain a healthy diet
It is important to maintain a healthy diet throughout your life.
She works hard to maintain her reputation for honesty.
The company struggled to maintain its position as a market leader.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abandon: to give up completely or stop maintaining
  • synonym – preserve: to keep something in its original state or in good condition
  • synonym – sustain: to keep something going over time
