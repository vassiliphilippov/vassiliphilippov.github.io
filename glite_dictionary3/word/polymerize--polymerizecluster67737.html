to polymerize

verb
cause to form a polymer (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪməˌraɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪməraɪz / (BrE)
to form a polymer by joining smaller molecules (monomers) together in a chemical reaction

Examples

to polymerize plastic materials
Chemists can polymerize different compounds to produce new materials with unique properties.
Heat will polymerize the monomer solution into a solid plastic.
When exposed to light, the resin will polymerize quickly, forming a hard finish.
Certain plastic materials polymerize when exposed to ultraviolet light.
Chemists can polymerize monomers to create new synthetic fibers.
If the solution does not polymerize properly, the final product will be weak.
