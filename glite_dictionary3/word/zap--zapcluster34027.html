to zap

verb
to strike suddenly or attack with force
Pronunciation: / zæp / (AmE), / zæp / (BrE)
to strike, attack, kill, or destroy suddenly and with force, especially using electricity or a weapon

Examples

to zap an enemy
He quickly zapped the wasp with an electric fly swatter before it could sting anyone.
In the video game, you gain points every time you zap an alien spaceship.
The fighter jets zapped the enemy camp with precision-guided missiles.
Lightning zapped the tree in an instant, splitting it in two.
The comedian's jokes zap the audience with their quick, biting wit.
The movie hero zapped his enemies with a laser gun.
If you touch that wire, you could get zapped and seriously hurt.
In the video game, players must zap the monsters before they reach the base.
The hero aimed his laser gun and zapped the alien enemy in a flash.
