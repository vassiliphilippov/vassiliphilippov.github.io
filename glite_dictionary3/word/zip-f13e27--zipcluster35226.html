zip

noun
fastening device with sliding tab
Pronunciation: / zɪp / (AmE), / zɪp / (BrE)
a fastening device consisting of two strips of fabric with interlocking metal or plastic teeth and a sliding tab to open or close them

Examples

zip on a jacket
He struggled to close the zip on his suitcase because it was too full.
She fixed the broken zip on her jacket before leaving the house.
The zip on my backpack got stuck halfway up.

Related Terms

  • synonym – zipper: a fastening device with interlocking teeth, especially in American English
