thermogravimeter

noun
instrument measuring liquid density and temperature
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜːrmoʊˈɡrævɪˌmiːtɚ / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈɡrævɪmiːtə / (BrE)
An instrument that combines a hydrometer and a thermometer to measure both the density and temperature of a liquid.

Examples

thermogravimeter checks density
A thermogravimeter is commonly used in labs that study the physical properties of liquids.
Before beginning the experiment, she calibrated the thermogravimeter to ensure accurate readings.
The thermogravimeter allowed the scientist to record both the temperature and density of the solution in real time.
