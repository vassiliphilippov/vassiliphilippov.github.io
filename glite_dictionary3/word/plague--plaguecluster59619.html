plague

noun
deadly epidemic disease
Pronunciation: / pleɪɡ / (AmE), / pleɪɡ / (BrE)
a deadly infectious disease that spreads quickly and kills many people (especially the historical disease caused by Yersinia pestis)

Examples

the plague spreads rapidly
Doctors struggled to control the plague as it swept through the crowded city.
In history books, one can read about the devastation caused by the plague.
The Black Death was a plague that wiped out millions in medieval Europe.
After the plague was discovered in the village, health officials rushed to contain its spread.
Fleas carried by rats were responsible for transmitting the plague to humans.
The plague devastated many European cities during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym – epidemic: a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community
  • synonym – pandemic: an epidemic that has spread over multiple countries or continents
  • synonym – pestilence: a fatal epidemic disease, especially bubonic plague
