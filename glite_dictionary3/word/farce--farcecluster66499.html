farce

noun
type of comedy with exaggerated situations
Pronunciation: / fɑrs / (AmE), / fɑːs / (BrE)
A comedy that uses exaggerated, improbable situations and broad satire to entertain.

Examples

stage farce
He laughed at the farce unfolding on stage, unable to take the plot seriously.
Many critics dismissed the trial as a complete farce lacking any seriousness.
The play was a farce full of mistaken identities and slapstick humor.
