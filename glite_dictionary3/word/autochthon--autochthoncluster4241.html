autochthon

noun
original inhabitant of a region
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈtɑːkθɑn / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkθɒn / (BrE)
a member of the earliest known inhabitants of a region; an original or indigenous inhabitant.

Examples

ancient autochthon people

Some myths describe kings as direct descendants of the land's autochthon.
The aboriginal tribes were considered the autochthon of the island.
