to bug

verb
install a device to secretly listen
Pronunciation: / bʌɡ / (AmE), / bʌɡ / (BrE)
to secretly install a device in order to listen to conversations in a place or on a communication line

Examples

to bug a room
He suspected that someone had bugged his office to eavesdrop on his meetings.
She was worried her apartment might be bugged after noticing some strange noises during her calls.
The police bugged the suspect's phone calls to gather more evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tap: to listen secretly to someone's phone conversations
