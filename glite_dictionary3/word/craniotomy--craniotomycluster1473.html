craniotomy

noun
surgical opening of the skull
Pronunciation: / ˌkreɪniˈɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌkreɪniˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which a bone flap is temporarily removed from the skull to access the brain

Examples

craniotomy surgery
Before the craniotomy, the medical team explained the risks and benefits to the family.
Recovery after a craniotomy can take several weeks, depending on the complexity of the surgery.
The neurosurgeon performed a craniotomy to remove the patient's brain tumor.
