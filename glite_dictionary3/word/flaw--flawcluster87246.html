flaw

noun
defect or weakness in character
Pronunciation: / flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
a defect or weakness in a person's character that makes them less perfect or effective

Examples

a flaw in character
Despite his many achievements, everyone knew he had a flaw in his judgment.
Her only serious flaw was her quick temper, which sometimes caused problems at work.
The hero's tragic flaw ultimately led to her downfall in the story.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fault: a character defect or shortcoming
  • antonym – virtue: a good quality or advantage in someone's character
