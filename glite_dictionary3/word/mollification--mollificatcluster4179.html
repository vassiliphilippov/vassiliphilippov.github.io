mollification

noun
the act of appeasing or calming someone
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of calming or appeasing someone, especially to make them less angry or hostile

Examples

mollification of anger
After some mollification, the angry neighbors agreed to a compromise.

The manager used gentle mollification to calm the frustrated customers.

Her gentle words served as a mollification to his wounded pride.
The manager's prompt mollification of the angry customer prevented the situation from escalating.
