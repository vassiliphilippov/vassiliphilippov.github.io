stocks

noun
wooden device for punishing offenders
Pronunciation: / stɑks / (AmE), / stɒks / (BrE)
a device consisting of a heavy wooden frame with holes for securing the feet (and sometimes the hands), formerly used to punish offenders by public confinement

Examples

in the stocks for punishment
In medieval times, criminals were often placed in the stocks in the town square for public humiliation.
The villagers laughed and jeered as the thief sat helplessly in the stocks.
Tourists can see a restored set of stocks in front of the old courthouse.
