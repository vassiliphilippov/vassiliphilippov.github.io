buckram

noun
stiff cotton fabric for bookbinding or clothing
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkrəm / (AmE), / ˈbʌkrəm / (BrE)
a coarse, stiff cotton fabric, often stiffened with glue, used in bookbinding and to reinforce or stiffen clothing

Examples

buckram fabric lining
Her new dress had a layer of buckram to help it hold its shape.
The old book was bound in buckram to provide extra durability.
They repaired the torn spine of the book using buckram fabric.
