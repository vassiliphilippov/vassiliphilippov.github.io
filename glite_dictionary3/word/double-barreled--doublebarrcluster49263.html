double-barreled

adjective
having two barrels (usually of a gun)
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəl ˈbær.əld / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈbær.əld / (BrE)
(of a gun) having two barrels mounted side by side or one above the other, typically to allow two shots to be fired in succession.

Examples

double-barreled shotgun
He inherited a vintage double-barreled shotgun from his grandfather.
Polishing the old double-barreled firearm became part of his weekend routine.
The hunters preferred a double-barreled rifle for its reliability and quick follow-up shot.
