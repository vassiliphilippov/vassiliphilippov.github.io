shiftiness

noun
quality of being changeable or unpredictable
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪftinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪftinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being changeable or unpredictable, especially in direction.

Examples

shiftiness of wind
Pilots must account for the shiftiness of air currents during takeoff.
The shiftiness of the wind made sailing much more difficult.
The weather's shiftiness forced the organizers to postpone the outdoor event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – instability: state of being likely to change or not stay the same
