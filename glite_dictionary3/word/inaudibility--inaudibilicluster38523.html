inaudibility

noun
quality of being inaudible
Pronunciation: / ɪˌnɑːdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌnɔːdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to hear.

Examples

inaudibility of speech
He spoke with such inaudibility that no one could understand his message.
The inaudibility of her whisper was intentional, ensuring only her closest friend could hear.
The inaudibility of the high-frequency tones made the experiment challenging for researchers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – audibility: the quality of being heard
  • synonym – inaudible: unable to be heard
