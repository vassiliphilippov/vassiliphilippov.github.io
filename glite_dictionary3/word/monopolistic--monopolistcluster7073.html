monopolistic

adjective
having exclusive commercial control
Pronunciation: / məˌnɑːpəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / məˌnɒpəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
having or relating to exclusive control over a commercial activity, typically by possession or legal right

Examples

monopolistic business practices
Laws are in place to prevent monopolistic control over essential resources.
Some economists argue that monopolistic competition can hinder innovation.
The company was accused of adopting monopolistic practices to dominate the market.

Related Terms

  • antonym – competitive: characterized by competition, not exclusive control
  • synonym – monopoly: the exclusive possession or control of the supply of or trade in a commodity or service
