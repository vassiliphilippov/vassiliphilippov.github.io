payable

noun
an amount owed in business accounts
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪəbl / (BrE)
(Accounting) A sum of money that is owed by a business for goods or services received on credit; an amount recorded as a liability in an account.

Examples

payables report
He is responsible for managing all the payables and ensuring timely payments.
The company's payables have increased significantly this quarter.
Their accountant reconciles receivables and payables at the end of each month.

Related Terms

  • antonym – receivable: an amount owed to a business by others
