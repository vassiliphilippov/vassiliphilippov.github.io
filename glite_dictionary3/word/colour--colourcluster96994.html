colour

noun
quark property in particle physics
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
(Physics) A property of quarks that determines their interactions in the strong nuclear force, typically described as coming in three types (such as red, green, blue).

Examples

colour charge of quark
In particle physics, each quark possesses a colour charge that is essential for strong interactions.
Physicists use the term colour to distinguish the different states of quark charge.
The concept of colour explains why quarks are always confined within hadrons.
