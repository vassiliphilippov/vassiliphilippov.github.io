to face

verb
confront with (problem, accusation, etc.)
Pronunciation: / feɪs / (AmE), / feɪs / (BrE)
to deal with or confront a difficult or unpleasant situation or person directly

Examples

to face a problem
We were forced to face some difficult questions during the meeting.

You must face the consequences of your actions sooner or later.
After losing his job, he had to face the reality of his financial situation.
It's important to face your fears instead of running from them.
She finally faced the truth about their relationship.
Despite criticism, he chose to face his opponents head-on.
She had to face her fears before giving the speech.
The two teams will face each other in the finals next week.

  • synonym – confront: to face someone or something directly, especially in opposition or challenge
  • antonym – evade: to avoid dealing with or facing something
