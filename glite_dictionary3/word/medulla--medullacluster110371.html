medulla

noun
lower part of the brainstem
Pronunciation: / məˈdʌlə / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌlə / (BrE)
the lower part of the brainstem, continuous with the spinal cord, responsible for regulating vital functions such as breathing and heartbeat

Examples

the medulla controls breathing
A stroke affecting the medulla may have life-threatening consequences.
Damage to the medulla can result in the loss of basic bodily functions such as breathing.
The medulla controls involuntary activities like heartbeat and blood pressure.
