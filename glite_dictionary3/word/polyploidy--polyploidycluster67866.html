polyploidy

noun
state of having extra chromosome sets
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈplɔɪdi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈplɔɪdi / (BrE)
the condition of having more than two complete sets of chromosomes in a cell or organism

Examples

polyploidy in plants
Many plants exhibit polyploidy, resulting in larger fruits or flowers.
Researchers found evidence of polyploidy in several species of fish.
The phenomenon of polyploidy is a significant factor in plant evolution.
