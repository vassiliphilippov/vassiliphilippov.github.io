tranquillizer

noun
drug that calms without impairing clarity
Pronunciation: / træŋˈkwɪl.aɪ.zər / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪl.ɪ.zə / (BrE)
A drug that is used to reduce anxiety, stress, or tension without significantly impairing mental clarity.

Examples

mild tranquillizer
He was given a mild tranquillizer to calm his nerves during the flight.
Some people misuse tranquillizers as a way to cope with everyday stress.
The doctor prescribed a tranquillizer to help reduce her anxiety before the surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedative: a drug that calms or induces sleep
  • antonym – stimulant: a drug that increases activity or alertness
