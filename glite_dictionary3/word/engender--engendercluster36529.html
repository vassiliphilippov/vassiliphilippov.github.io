to engender

verb
cause to exist or develop
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɛndɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
to cause a feeling, situation, or condition to exist or develop

Examples

to engender strong feelings
A sense of fear may engender hostility in the community.
Her unfair treatment engendered resentment among her colleagues.
The policy changes are likely to engender greater trust between staff and management.

  • synonym – cause: to make something happen; bring about
  • antonym – destroy: to put an end to the existence of something
  • synonym – generate: to bring into existence; produce
