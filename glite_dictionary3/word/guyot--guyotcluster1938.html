guyot

noun
flat-topped underwater volcanic mountain
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪ.jɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡiː.jɒt / (BrE)
an isolated, flat-topped seamount of volcanic origin, typically found in ocean basins, especially in the Pacific Ocean.

Examples

deep-sea guyot
Many marine species inhabit the slopes of a guyot in deep ocean waters.
Scientists discovered a guyot while mapping the Pacific Ocean floor.
The summit of the guyot was once above sea level before erosion flattened it.
