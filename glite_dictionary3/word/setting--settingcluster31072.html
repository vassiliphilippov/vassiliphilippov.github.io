setting

noun
arrangement of scenery in plays or films
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement of scenery and properties on a stage or film set to represent the place where a play, movie, or other performance takes place

Examples

setting for a play
She admired the elaborate setting that transformed the stage into a medieval castle.
The setting for the final act was a lavish Victorian drawing room.
The film’s realistic setting helped immerse the audience in the story.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backdrop: a painted cloth hung at the back of a theater stage as part of the setting
  • synonym – scenery: the painted backdrops and other stage elements used to create a setting
