to slump

verb
fall or decline suddenly and markedly
Pronunciation: / slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
to fall, sink, or sit down heavily and suddenly, often from tiredness or weakness

Examples

to slump into a chair
After running the marathon, she slumped onto the bench, exhausted.
Share prices slumped to their lowest level in five years.
The company's profits slumped after a series of poor investments.
After a long day, he slumped into his chair and closed his eyes.
In disappointment, she slumped against the wall, unable to hide her frustration.
The exhausted runner slumped to the ground after crossing the finish line.
After the long hike, he slumped into his chair, exhausted from the effort.
During the boring lecture, several students began slumping in their seats.
She slumped against the wall, barely able to keep her eyes open.
