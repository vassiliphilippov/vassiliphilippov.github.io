snatch

noun
unlawful act of abduction or kidnapping
Pronunciation: / snætʃ / (AmE), / snætʃ / (BrE)
(law) The unlawful act of capturing and carrying away a person against their will and holding them in false imprisonment; abduction or kidnapping.

Examples

the snatch case
He faced serious charges for his role in the snatch and illegal confinement of the businessman.
Laws have become stricter to deter the snatch of children by strangers.
The police arrested several suspects in connection with the snatch of an heiress from her home.
