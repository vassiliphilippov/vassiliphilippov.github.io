pneumococcus

noun
bacterium causing pneumonia
Pronunciation: / nuːmoʊˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / njuːməʊˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a spherical bacterium (Streptococcus pneumoniae) that causes pneumonia and other infectious diseases in humans and some animals

Examples

pneumococcus infection
In the early 20th century, many pneumonia cases were traced to the pneumococcus.
The doctor explained that the infection was caused by the pneumococcus.
Vaccines have been developed to protect against diseases caused by pneumococcus.
