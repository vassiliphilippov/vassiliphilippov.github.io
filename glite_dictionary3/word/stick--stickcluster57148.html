stick

noun
threat of punishment or penalty
Pronunciation: / stɪk / (AmE), / stɪk / (BrE)
A threat of punishment or penalty used as a means of persuasion or control, often in contrast to a reward ('carrot').

Examples

stick and carrot
If employees are only motivated by the stick, they may feel resentful and disengaged.
Managers sometimes rely too much on the stick instead of finding positive incentives.
The government used the stick of strict fines to enforce compliance with the new law.

Related Terms

  • antonym – carrot: something offered as a reward to induce desired behavior
