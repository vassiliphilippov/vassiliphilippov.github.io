staged

adjective
deliberately arranged for effect
Pronunciation: / steɪdʒd / (AmE), / steɪdʒd / (BrE)
deliberately arranged or planned to produce a particular effect, rather than being spontaneous or genuine.

Examples

staged scene
Many reality TV shows include staged scenes to increase drama.
The company denied that the protest was staged to gain publicity.
The photos looked too perfect and clearly staged for the campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym – spontaneous: done without planning or premeditation; occurring naturally
