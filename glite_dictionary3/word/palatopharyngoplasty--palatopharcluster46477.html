palatopharyngoplasty

noun
surgical treatment for snoring or sleep apnea
Pronunciation: / ˌpæl.ə.toʊˌfær.ɪnˈɡɑː.plə.sti / (AmE), / ˌpæl.ə.təʊˌfær.ɪŋˈɡɒ.plə.sti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of excess tissue from the palate and oropharynx to widen the airway, typically performed to treat severe snoring or obstructive sleep apnea

Examples

palatopharyngoplasty surgery for sleep
After her palatopharyngoplasty, she noticed a significant improvement in her breathing at night.
The effectiveness of palatopharyngoplasty varies depending on the underlying cause of the patient's snoring.
The patient underwent a palatopharyngoplasty to address his severe obstructive sleep apnea.
