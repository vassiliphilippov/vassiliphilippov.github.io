hyalinisation

noun
conversion of tissue to a glassy, translucent state
Pronunciation: / haɪəlɪˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪəˌlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state in which tissue becomes hyaline, typically as a result of pathological change

Examples

hyalinisation of tissue
Excessive hyalinisation can lead to stiffening of the blood vessels.
Pathologists noted hyalinisation of the glomeruli under the microscope.
The biopsy confirmed the presence of hyalinisation in the lung tissue.
