brachydactylia

noun
abnormal shortness of fingers and toes
Pronunciation: / ˌbrækiˌdækˈtɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbrakɪˌdakˈtɪlɪə / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by abnormal shortness of the fingers and toes.

Examples

brachydactylia in children
Doctors identified brachydactylia in several members of the patient's family.
Research has linked certain gene mutations to the development of brachydactylia.
The genetic disorder known as brachydactylia results in unusually short fingers from birth.
