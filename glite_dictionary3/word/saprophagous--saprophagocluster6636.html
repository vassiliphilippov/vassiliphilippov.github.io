saprophagous

adjective
feeding on decaying animal matter
Pronunciation: / səˈprɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / səˈprɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
(of certain animals) feeding on dead or decaying animal matter.

Examples

saprophagous beetles
Many beetles are saprophagous, feeding on decaying plant and animal materials in the forest floor.
The larvae of some flies are saprophagous, helping to recycle nutrients in the ecosystem.
Vultures are not saprophagous; rather, they are considered scavengers that feed specifically on carrion.

  • synonym – scavenging: feeding on dead animal or organic matter
