to dangle

verb
cause to hang loosely
Pronunciation: / ˈdæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to cause to hang or swing loosely, usually so as to move freely in the air

Examples

to dangle a toy
He dangled the puppet in front of the children, making them laugh.
She dangled her keys above the dog's head to get its attention.
The fisherman dangled a worm from his line over the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hang: to suspend or be suspended by attachment from above
