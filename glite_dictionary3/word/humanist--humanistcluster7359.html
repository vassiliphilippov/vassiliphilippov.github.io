humanist

adjective
marked by humanistic values; devoted to human welfare
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the philosophy of humanism, which emphasizes human dignity, rationality, and human welfare, often in preference to religious beliefs

Examples

humanist philosophy
Her humanist approach to education inspires respect for every student.
The award honored organizations for their humanist contributions to society.
They believe that humanist principles should guide social policy and government.
Her humanist views led her to promote secular education and critical thinking.
Many scientists advocate a humanist approach that values reason and evidence over tradition.
The debate contrasted humanist principles with more theologically based arguments.

  • synonym – humanitarian: concerned with human welfare
  • antonym – religious: relating to or believing in religion
