predetermination

noun
doctrine of all events being decided in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɝː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Theology) The doctrine that all events, especially regarding salvation, have been decided in advance by divine will.

Examples

predetermination by God
Calvinists emphasize predetermination as an essential part of their religious doctrine.
Some people find comfort in believing in predetermination, trusting that everything happens for a reason.
The concept of predetermination has been debated by theologians for centuries.
