Herrenvolk

noun
race believing itself entitled to rule others (Nazi context)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrənˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈhɛʁənfɔlk / (BrE)
(historical) A race or people that regards itself as superior and entitled to dominate other races; especially associated with the ideology of Nazi Germany.

Examples

Nazi Herrenvolk ideology
Some historians analyze the Herrenvolk mentality in the context of colonial empires.
The belief in a Herrenvolk led to widespread discrimination and atrocities.
The ideology of the Herrenvolk played a central role in Nazi propaganda and policy.
