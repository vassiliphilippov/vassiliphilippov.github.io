anadromous

adjective
fish migrating from sea to fresh water to spawn
Pronunciation: / əˈnædrəməs / (AmE), / əˈnædrəməs / (BrE)
(of fish) migrating up rivers from the sea to spawn.

Examples

anadromous salmon migration
Many salmon are anadromous, returning to freshwater rivers to lay their eggs each year.
Restoration of anadromous fish runs has become a major conservation goal in many countries.
The anadromous habits of shad allow them to thrive in both saltwater and freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catadromous: (of fish) migrating down rivers to the sea to spawn
