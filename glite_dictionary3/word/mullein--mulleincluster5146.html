mullein

noun
tall plant with woolly leaves and spikes of flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪn / (BrE)
a tall plant of the genus Verbascum, with large woolly leaves and spikes of yellow, white, or purplish flowers, commonly found in Eurasia and North Africa

Examples

mullein plant flowers
Gardeners often grow mullein for its striking spikes of yellow flowers.
Some herbal teas include dried mullein leaves as an ingredient.
The hillside was dotted with mullein rising above the grasses.
