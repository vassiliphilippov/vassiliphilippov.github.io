Axis

proper noun
World War II alliance (Germany, Italy, Japan, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈæk.sɪs / (BrE)
the alliance of Germany, Italy, and later Japan and other nations during World War II, opposed to the Allies.

Examples

the Axis powers
During World War II, the Axis powers sought to expand their territories across Europe and Asia.
Japan became part of the Axis after signing the Tripartite Pact in 1940.
The defeat of the Axis marked a turning point in world history.
