cycloserine

noun
adjunct in exposure therapy
Pronunciation: / sa6aklo8ac8s6arid0n / (AmE), / sa6akl598ac8s6a59rid0n / (BrE)
(in psychiatry and clinical psychology) a drug (often the D-enantiomer) given as an adjunct to exposure-based therapies to enhance fear extinction and learning, acting on NMDA receptors.

Examples

cycloserine aids therapy
Researchers gave patients a low dose of cycloserine before exposure sessions to speed fear extinction.
Several trials tested whether cycloserine enhances cognitive behavioural therapy outcomes for social anxiety disorder.
Clinicians cautiously used cycloserine as an adjunct, noting timing and dose influence treatment effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym – D-cycloserine: the D-enantiomer of cycloserine, frequently used in research and clinical trials as a cognitive enhancer in exposure therapy.
  • synonym – NMDA receptor partial agonist: a substance that partially activates NMDA receptors and can influence learning and memory processes relevant to extinction.
