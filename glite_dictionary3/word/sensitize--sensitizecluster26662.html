to sensitize

verb
make sensitive to light (photography)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make a material sensitive to light, usually of a particular color, by coating it with a photographic emulsion

Examples

to sensitize photographic film
Before use, you need to sensitize the film so that it can capture images in low light.
The technician sensitized the photographic plates with a special emulsion to increase their responsiveness to blue light.
To create color prints, the paper must be sensitized to specific wavelengths of light.
