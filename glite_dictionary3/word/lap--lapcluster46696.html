to lap

verb
to lie partly over something
Pronunciation: / læp / (AmE), / læp / (BrE)
(of objects) to lie partly over or along the edge of something or over one another.

Examples

to lap edges
Beams lap at the joint to form a stronger structure.
The pages of the book lapped slightly, making it hard to turn them smoothly.
The tiles are designed to lap over each other for better protection from rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overlap: to cover partly the same area as something else
