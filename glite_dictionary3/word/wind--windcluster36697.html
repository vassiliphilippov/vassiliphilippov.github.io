wind

noun
moving air in the atmosphere
Pronunciation: / wɪnd / (AmE), / wɪnd / (BrE)
A natural movement of air, especially in the Earth's atmosphere, often with some force, from an area of high pressure to an area of low pressure.

Examples

strong wind
After the storm, a cold wind blew in from the north, chilling everyone outside.
The wind whipped across the open fields, making it hard to walk.
You can hear the wind howling through the trees on winter nights.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – breeze: a gentle or light wind
  • degree higher – gale: a very strong wind
