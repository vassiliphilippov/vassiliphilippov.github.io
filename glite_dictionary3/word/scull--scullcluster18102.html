scull

noun
short oar used in rowing
Pronunciation: / skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
an oar used to move a boat, either as a pair of short oars or a single long oar at the back of the boat

Examples

scull oar
A single scull is different from a sweep oar, as it is shorter and handled with one hand.
He gripped each scull firmly, ready to propel the boat across the lake.
The athlete dipped her sculls into the water with smooth, practiced strokes.
A wooden scull rested against the stern, ready to be used for the journey.
She learned how to operate the scull efficiently to travel upstream.
The fisherman used a single scull to maneuver his boat through the narrow channel.

  • degree lower – paddle: a short pole with a broad blade at one or both ends, used to move a small boat through water
