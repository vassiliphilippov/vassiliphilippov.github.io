hydatid

noun
fluid-filled cyst from tapeworm larvae
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ.də.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪ.də.tɪd / (BrE)
A cyst filled with fluid, formed as a result of infestation by tapeworm larvae, especially in echinococcosis.

Examples

hydatid cyst in liver
Doctors discovered a large hydatid in her liver during the ultrasound examination.
Surgical removal of the hydatid was necessary to prevent complications.
The presence of multiple hydatids indicated a severe case of echinococcosis.
