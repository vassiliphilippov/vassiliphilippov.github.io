all-encompassing

adjective
covering everything; comprehensive
Pronunciation: / ˌɔl ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɔːl ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (BrE)
Including or covering everything or everyone; comprehensive in scope or content.

Examples

all-encompassing view
Her research takes an all-encompassing approach to the subject, covering history, culture, and science.
The new law provides an all-encompassing framework for environmental protection.
We need an all-encompassing plan to address all aspects of the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym – comprehensive: including all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • antonym – limited: restricted in size, amount, or extent; not covering everything
