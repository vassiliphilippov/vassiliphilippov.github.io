to poll

verb
cut upper branches of trees
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
to cut the upper branches of (a tree) to promote dense growth of new shoots; to convert into a pollard

Examples

to poll a tree
Every spring, the gardener polls the willow trees along the riverbank.
The farmer decided to poll the old oaks to encourage new growth.
They polled the trees in the orchard last year to improve their shape and health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pollard: a tree with the upper branches cut back to promote growth
