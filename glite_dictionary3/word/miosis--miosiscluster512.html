miosis

noun
cell division forming gametes/spores
Pronunciation: / maɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the process of cell division in sexually reproducing organisms that results in four nuclei, each with half the chromosome number, forming reproductive cells such as gametes or spores

Examples

cell miosis
During miosis, the chromosome number in the cells is reduced by half.
If miosis fails, gametes may end up with an abnormal number of chromosomes.
The process of miosis is essential for the formation of eggs and sperm in animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meiosis: alternative, more common spelling of the term miosis
  • antonym – mitosis: cell division where the nucleus divides into two genetically identical nuclei
