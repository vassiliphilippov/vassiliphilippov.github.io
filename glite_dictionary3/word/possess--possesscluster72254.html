to possess

verb
(of emotion/idea) to control or dominate someone
Pronunciation: / pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
(of an emotion, idea, or impulse) to take control of or strongly influence someone; to dominate or overwhelm (a person’s mind, spirit, or actions).

Examples

possess a person
A sudden fear possessed him as he stepped onto the stage.
During the heated debate, anger seemed to possess every participant.
What possessed you to make such a reckless decision?

Related Terms

  • synonym – control: to exercise restraining or directing influence over
  • synonym – overwhelm: to overpower completely in mind or feeling
