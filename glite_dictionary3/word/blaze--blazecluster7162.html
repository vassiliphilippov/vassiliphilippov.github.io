to blaze

verb
mark a path by making tree marks
Pronunciation: / bleɪz / (AmE), / bleɪz / (BrE)
to indicate or mark a path, especially in forests, by making cuts or marks (blazes) on trees

Examples

to blaze a trail
In the dense forest, rangers blaze trails to help hikers find their way.
They carefully blazed the route by cutting marks in the tree bark every hundred meters.
While exploring the hillside, we decided to blaze a new path back to the campsite.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trailblaze: to mark or pioneer a new path, especially through wild or unexplored territory
