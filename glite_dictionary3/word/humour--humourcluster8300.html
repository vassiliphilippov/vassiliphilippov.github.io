humour

noun
state of mind or mood (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
A temporary state of mind or mood; disposition or temperament, especially as affecting a person's behavior. Now chiefly archaic or literary.

Examples

bad humour
Depending on his humour, he could be charming or irritable.
He was in no humour to listen to advice after the argument.
Her humour changed suddenly from cheerful to sullen.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mood: a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • synonym – temper: a person's state of mind seen in terms of their being angry or calm
