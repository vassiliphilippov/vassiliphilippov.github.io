consecration

noun
the act of making something sacred
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑnsɪˈkreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsɪˈkreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something sacred or dedicating it formally to a religious purpose, often through a special ceremony

Examples

consecration of a church
Following its consecration, the altar became the focal point of the church.
The consecration of the new temple drew worshippers from all over the country.
The bishop presided over the consecration ceremony last Sunday.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desecration: the act of treating something sacred with disrespect
  • synonym – sanctification: the process of making or declaring something holy
