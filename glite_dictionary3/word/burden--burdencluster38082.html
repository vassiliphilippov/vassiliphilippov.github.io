to burden

verb
to weigh down or load heavily
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːdən /; / ˈbɝːdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdən /; / ˈbɜːdn̩ / (BrE)
to give someone a heavy load, responsibility, or worry

Examples

to burden a friend
She did not want to burden her friends with her personal problems.
Taxes can sometimes burden small businesses, making it hard for them to grow.
The mule was burdened with heavy sacks as it climbed the mountain path.
Please let me know if I ever burden you with too many requests.
She didn't want to burden her colleagues with extra work during the busy season.
The manager decided not to burden the team with additional responsibilities before the deadline.

  • antonym – relieve: to remove a burden or responsibility from someone
