Acarina

noun
order of mites and ticks
Pronunciation: / ˌækəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / ˌækəˈriːnə / (BrE)
An order of arachnids comprising mites and ticks, many of which are microscopic and some of which are parasites of plants, animals, or humans.

Examples

Acarina include mites
Biologists study Acarina to understand their role as parasites and decomposers.
Many species within Acarina live in soil or decaying matter.
Ticks and mites are both classified under the order Acarina.
