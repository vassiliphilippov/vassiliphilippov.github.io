to defile

verb
to make dirty or impure
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfaɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪl / (BrE)
to make something dirty, impure, or to damage its reputation or honor (literally or figuratively)

Examples

to defile a place
He was fined for defiling the public park with graffiti.
Industrial waste continued to defile the once pristine lake.
Tourists should respect the local culture and not defile sacred sites.
His political rivals tried to defile his reputation with false accusations.
The vandals defiled the ancient temple with graffiti and litter.
The water became defiled by industrial waste from the nearby factory.
A single careless remark might defile years of hard-earned trust.
False accusations can easily defile an innocent person's reputation.
He would never defile her good name with such baseless rumors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contaminate: to make something impure by adding something harmful or unwanted
  • synonym – pollute: to make air, water, or soil dirty or harmful
  • antonym – purify: to remove harmful substances and make clean
  • synonym – sully: to damage the purity or integrity of; to soil
  • synonym – tarnish: to make or become less bright or less good; to diminish
