fair

adverb
in accordance with rules or laws; without cheating
Pronunciation: / fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
In a way that is in accordance with the rules, laws, or standards and without fraud or cheating.

Examples

to play fair
He prefers to win fair rather than by deception.
The teams competed fair for the championship, respecting all the rules.
They insisted that the election be conducted fair and square.

Related Terms

  • synonym – justly: in a morally right or fair way
  • antonym – unfairly: in a way that is not just or according to the rules; with cheating
