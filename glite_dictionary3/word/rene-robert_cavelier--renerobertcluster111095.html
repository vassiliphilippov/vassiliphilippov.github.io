Rene-Robert Cavelier

proper noun
French explorer who claimed Louisiana for France
Pronunciation: / rəˌneɪ ˈroʊbər ˌkævəˈlieɪ / (AmE), / rəˌneɪ ˈrɒbə ˌkævəlˈjeɪ / (BrE)
(1643–1687) A French explorer who claimed Louisiana for France and explored large areas of North America.

Examples

Rene-Robert Cavelier explored America
Rene-Robert Cavelier led an expedition down the Mississippi River in the late seventeenth century.
Many history books discuss the impact of Rene-Robert Cavelier on the colonization of North America.
The state of Louisiana was claimed for France by Rene-Robert Cavelier in 1682.
