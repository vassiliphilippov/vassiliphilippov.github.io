pigment

noun
coloring substance in tissues
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a substance that gives color to plant or animal tissues

Examples

pigment in plant cells
Chlorophyll is the green pigment found in plants.
Melanin is the pigment responsible for skin color in humans.
Some animals can change their pigment to blend into their environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bleach: a substance that removes color or whitens
  • synonym – dye: a natural or synthetic substance used to add color, especially in textiles
