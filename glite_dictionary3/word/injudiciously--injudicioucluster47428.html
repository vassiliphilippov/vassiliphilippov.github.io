injudiciously

adverb
without good judgment
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.dʒuˈdɪʃ.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dʒuˈdɪʃ.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of good judgment or care.

Examples

injudiciously spent money
He spent the company's funds injudiciously, leading to financial troubles.
She injudiciously revealed confidential information to the press.
The medicine was prescribed injudiciously and caused serious side effects in some patients.
