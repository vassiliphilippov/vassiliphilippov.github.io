to settle

verb
become quiet, fixed, or established
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬l̩ /; / ˈsɛt̬.əl /; / ˈsɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl̩ /; / ˈsɛt.əl /; / ˈsɛtəl / (BrE)
to become calm, quiet, stable, or to stop moving after activity or uncertainty

Examples

to settle down
After an hour, the excited crowd finally began to settle and listen to the speaker.

The dust from the renovation work will settle in a few hours.

A thick fog settled on the lake, obscuring the view.

Let the sediment settle at the bottom of the glass before drinking.
The bird circled for a moment before it settled on the branch.
The dust began to settle after the breeze died down.

  • synonym – stabilize: to make or become steady and unlikely to change suddenly
