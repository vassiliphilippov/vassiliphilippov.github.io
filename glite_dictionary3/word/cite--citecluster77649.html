to cite

verb
formally commend or honor
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
to formally recognize or praise someone for an achievement, often by naming them in an official statement or document

Examples

cite a hero
Several soldiers were cited for bravery after the rescue operation.
The committee cited Dr. Lee for his groundbreaking research in medicine.
The mayor cited her for her volunteer work during the annual awards ceremony.
