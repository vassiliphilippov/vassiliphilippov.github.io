prejudgment

noun
judgment formed before evidence is known
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt / (BrE)
A judgment or opinion formed before all the necessary evidence is known or considered.

Examples

quick prejudgment
Her prejudgment of the situation resulted in an unfair evaluation.
The committee cautioned against making any prejudgment before hearing all the facts.
We must avoid prejudgment and keep an open mind during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – prejudice: an opinion formed without sufficient knowledge or reason
