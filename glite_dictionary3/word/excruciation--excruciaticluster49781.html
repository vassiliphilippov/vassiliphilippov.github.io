excruciation

noun
the infliction of extreme pain or suffering
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌskruːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌskruːʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the infliction of extremely intense pain or suffering; severe torture or agony

Examples

the excruciation of prisoners
Medieval punishments often involved the deliberate excruciation of prisoners.
She described the chronic pain as a kind of daily excruciation she could barely endure.
The excruciation of the victim was evident in his anguished cries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – torture: the action of inflicting severe pain as punishment or to force someone to do or say something
