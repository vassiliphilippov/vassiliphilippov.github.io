mammon

noun
wealth regarded as an evil influence
Pronunciation: / ˈmæmən / (AmE), / ˈmamən / (BrE)
wealth or material riches regarded as having a corrupting or evil influence, especially when considered as an object of worship or greed.

Examples

worship mammon
He was accused of worshipping mammon instead of caring about his family.
Many believe that modern society is driven by mammon and materialism.
The preacher warned his congregation against serving both God and mammon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – altruism: selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • synonym – avarice: extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • synonym – greed: an intense and selfish desire for wealth, power, or food
