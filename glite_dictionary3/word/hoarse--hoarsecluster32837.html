hoarse

adjective
rough, harsh-sounding voice
Pronunciation: / hɔrs / (AmE), / hɔːs / (BrE)
(of a voice) sounding rough and harsh, typically as a result of a sore throat, shouting, or strong emotion.

Examples

hoarse voice
After catching a cold, Mark's hoarse cough worried his friends.
His voice sounded hoarse after cheering loudly at the match.
She spoke in a hoarse whisper that barely carried across the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – husky: (of a voice) sounding low and slightly rough
  • antonym – smooth: (of a voice) free from roughness or harshness
