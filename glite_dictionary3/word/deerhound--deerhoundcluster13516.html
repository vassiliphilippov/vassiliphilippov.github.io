deerhound

noun
large Scottish hunting dog breed
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪəhaʊnd / (BrE)
A very large, rough-coated breed of dog originally bred in Scotland for hunting deer; known as the royal dog of Scotland.

Examples

Scottish deerhound dog
Her family has bred deerhounds for generations, specializing in show dogs.
The Scottish deerhound is admired for its grace and gentle temperament.
They adopted a young deerhound from a rescue shelter last year.
