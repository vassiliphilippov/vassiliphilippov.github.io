scattershot

adjective
wide-ranging and haphazard
Pronunciation: / ˈskæt̬ɚˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈskætəˌʃɒt / (BrE)
covering a wide range of things or ideas in a random or haphazard way, rather than being focused or systematic

Examples

scattershot approach
Critics praised its ambition but found the film too scattershot in its themes.
His scattershot approach to studying left him confused about the main points.
The report offered scattershot recommendations rather than a clear plan.

Related Terms

  • antonym – focused: concentrated on a particular area or subject
