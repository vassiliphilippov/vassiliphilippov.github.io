to wharf

verb
to provide with a wharf
Pronunciation: / wɔrf / (AmE), / wɑːf / (BrE)
to provide (a place, especially a riverbank or shore) with a wharf or to build a wharf at (a place)

Examples

to wharf a riverbank
In the 19th century, engineers wharfed the riverbanks to encourage commercial development.
Local authorities plan to wharf the harbor to support economic growth in the region.
They decided to wharf the entire waterfront to accommodate the new trade ships.
