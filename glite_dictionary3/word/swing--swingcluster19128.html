to swing

verb
move or hang back and forth while suspended
Pronunciation: / swɪŋ / (AmE), / swɪŋ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move back and forth or from side to side, often while hanging or attached at one point

Examples

to swing gently
She watched the necklace swing gently from her hand as she raised it.
The lamp above the table began to swing softly in the breeze.
The porch swing would swing back and forth with each gust of wind.
He likes to swing his arms as he walks down the street.
She swung her hips as she danced across the floor.
The monkey swung gracefully from branch to branch in the trees.
As the car approached the corner, the driver had to swing left to avoid a pothole.
She told him to swing back around and pick up the dropped glove.
The door slowly began to swing open with the wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dangle: to hang or swing loosely
  • synonym – sway: to move slowly or rhythmically backward and forward or from side to side
  • synonym – swerve: to turn aside abruptly from a straight course
