cleavers

noun
a scrambling annual herb (Galium aparine)
Pronunciation: / ˈkliːvərz / (AmE), / ˈkliːvəz / (BrE)
a scrambling annual plant with slender stems covered in hooked bristles, found in hedgerows and waste places in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere; Galium aparine.

Examples

wild cleavers plant
Herbalists sometimes use dried cleavers to prepare traditional teas for health purposes.
In spring, cleavers can quickly overrun gardens and hedgerows with their sprawling growth.
The stems of cleavers cling to clothing and animal fur thanks to their tiny hooked bristles.
