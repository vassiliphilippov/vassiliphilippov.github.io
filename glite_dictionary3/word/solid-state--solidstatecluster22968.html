solid-state

adjective
using semiconductor materials (electronics)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑlɪd steɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd steɪt / (BrE)
describing electronic devices or technology that use solid materials (especially semiconductors like transistors or integrated circuits) instead of vacuum tubes or moving parts

Examples

solid-state drive
A solid-state drive is faster and more reliable than a traditional hard disk drive.
Most modern radios are solid-state, making them more durable and energy-efficient.
The amplifier uses solid-state technology instead of old vacuum tubes.
Many modern computers include a solid-state drive, which is faster and more reliable than traditional hard drives.
Researchers are making advances in solid-state battery technology to improve electric vehicles.
The solid-state components replaced vacuum tubes in radios, making them smaller and more durable.

Related Terms

  • antonym – liquid-state: relating to the properties of liquid materials
  • synonym – solid-state physics: the study of the physical properties of solid materials
  • antonym – vacuum tube: an electronic device based on vacuum tubes, as opposed to solid-state components
