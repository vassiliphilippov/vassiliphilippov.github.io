shower

noun
sudden burst or fall of items
Pronunciation: / ˈʃaʊər / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊə / (BrE)
A sudden and plentiful fall or burst of things, such as petals, sparks, or tears, likened to rain.

Examples

shower of confetti
A shower of confetti fell over the happy couple as they left the church.
She burst into a shower of tears as soon as she heard the news.
The campfire sent up a shower of golden sparks into the night sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cascade: a small waterfall or something resembling one in flow or appearance
  • synonym – downpour: a heavy fall of rain, also used more generally for things that fall heavily
