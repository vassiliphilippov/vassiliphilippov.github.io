column

noun
vertical glass tube for chromatography
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːləm / (AmE), / ˈkɒləm / (BrE)
a vertical glass tube used in column chromatography, through which a mixture passes and components are separated into colored bands

Examples

chromatography column
After loading the sample onto the column, the different pigments formed distinct colored bands.
If the column is packed too tightly, the flow rate of the solvent will be reduced.
The chemist carefully filled the column with silica gel before starting the separation process.
