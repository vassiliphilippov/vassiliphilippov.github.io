magdalen

noun
a reformed prostitute
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡdələn / (AmE), / ˈmæɡdələn / (BrE)
A woman who has been a prostitute and has reformed her ways; often used allusively in reference to Mary Magdalene.

Examples

fallen magdalen
In Victorian literature, the figure of the magdalen is often portrayed with sympathy.
She was known as a true magdalen, having transformed her life completely after leaving the streets.
The institution offered shelter and rehabilitation to any magdalen seeking a new life.
