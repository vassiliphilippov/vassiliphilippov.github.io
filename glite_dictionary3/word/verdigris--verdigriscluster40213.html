verdigris

noun
blue-green pigment made from copper salts
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrdɪˌɡris / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪɡriːs / (BrE)
A blue or green powder used as a pigment, typically composed of copper salts formed by the action of acetic acid on copper, and historically used in paints and dyes.

Examples

verdigris pigment powder
Copper roofs often develop a layer of verdigris after years of exposure to the elements.
In medieval manuscripts, verdigris was commonly used to create vibrant green colors.
The artist mixed verdigris into the paint to achieve a rich turquoise hue.

  • synonym – patina: a green or brown film on the surface of bronze or similar metals, produced by oxidation
