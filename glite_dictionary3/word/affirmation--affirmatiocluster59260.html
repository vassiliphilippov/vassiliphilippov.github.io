affirmation

noun
a solemn declaration made instead of an oath
Pronunciation: / ˌæfɚˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in law and religion) a solemn declaration, formally made as an alternative to an oath, by a person who objects to swearing an oath on religious or ethical grounds.

Examples

make a legal affirmation
By signing the legal affirmation, she formally accepted the truth of her statement.
Some people prefer to take an affirmation because of their personal beliefs.
The witness made an affirmation instead of swearing an oath in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym – oath: a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
