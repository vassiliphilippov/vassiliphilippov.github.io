to emaciate

verb
to become abnormally thin or weak
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmeɪ.si.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmæ.sɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
To become abnormally thin or weak, often because of illness or lack of food.

Examples

to emaciate from illness
After weeks of starvation, the prisoners began to emaciate rapidly.
If left untreated, chronic illnesses can emaciate even the healthiest individuals.
The disease caused her to emaciate despite her efforts to eat well.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fatten: to make or become fatter or more nourished
  • synonym – waste away: to gradually become very weak or thin, especially from illness
