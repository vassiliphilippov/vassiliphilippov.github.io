to gauffer

verb
create wavy fabric with a heated iron
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔfər / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːfə / (BrE)
to make fabric wavy or crimped by pressing it with a heated goffering iron.

Examples

to gauffer fabric edges
She learned how to gauffer lace using an old-fashioned heated iron.
The milliner decided to gauffer the ribbons to create a decorative effect.
Traditional costumes often feature collars or cuffs that have been gauffered for added texture.
