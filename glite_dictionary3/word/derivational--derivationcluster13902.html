derivational

adjective
relating to word formation from a base
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛr.əˈveɪ.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌdɛr.ɪˈveɪ.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
relating to the formation of new words by adding prefixes or suffixes to a base word, indicating a semantic relationship between the new word and its base.

Examples

derivational suffix
In English, adding '-er' to 'teach' is a derivational process.
Linguists often analyze derivational suffixes to understand how words are formed.
The difference between 'quick' and 'quickly' is explained by derivational morphology.
