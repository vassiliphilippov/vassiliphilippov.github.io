Trypetidae

noun
family of fruit flies (insect)
Pronunciation: / traɪˈpɛtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / traɪˈpetɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fruit flies, some of which are leaf miners, formerly used in classification but now usually included in the family Tephritidae.

Examples

Trypetidae fruit flies
Modern taxonomy places species once included in Trypetidae within Tephritidae.
Scientists used to classify many fruit flies under the Trypetidae family.
The Trypetidae were studied extensively for their role as agricultural pests.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – Drosophilidae: family of small fruit flies, distinct from Trypetidae
  • synonym – Tephritidae: current family of fruit flies in modern classification
