pastry

noun
baked food made of dough
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪstri / (AmE), / ˈpeɪstri / (BrE)
a food made from a mixture of flour, fat, and water, either as the dough or as baked goods made from it (such as pies, tarts, or croissants)

Examples

pastry dough
He learned how to make puff pastry at culinary school.
She enjoyed a fresh pastry with her morning coffee.
The bakery displays a delicious selection of sweet and savory pastries.
He skillfully rolled out the pastry for a homemade apple pie.
The bakery is famous for its delicious pastry doughs and tarts.
Too much handling can make the pastry tough instead of light and flaky.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bread: food made of dough and baked, but typically not rich in fat like pastry
  • synonym – cake: sweet baked food made from a mixture of flour, sugar, eggs, and fat
  • synonym – pie crust: the baked outer shell of a pie, often made from pastry
