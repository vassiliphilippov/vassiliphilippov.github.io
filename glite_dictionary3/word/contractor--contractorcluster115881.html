contractor

noun
party to a contract
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræktər / (AmE), / kənˈtræktə / (BrE)
a person or company hired under a contract to provide goods, services, or do a job (often in construction)

Examples

building contractor
Before starting the project, the contractor and the client signed a detailed contract.
If the contractor breaches the contract, they may be liable for damages.
The contractor must fulfill all obligations specified in the agreement.
As a general contractor, she manages several crews on each job site.
That company is a well-known contractor specializing in commercial construction projects.
The homeowner hired a contractor to renovate the kitchen and bathroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym – builder: a person who constructs buildings
  • antonym – client: the recipient in a contractual agreement
  • degree lower – subcontractor: a person or company hired by a contractor to fulfill part of a contract
