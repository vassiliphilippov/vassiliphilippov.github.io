counterglow

noun
faint night-sky light opposite the Sun
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
a faint patch of light in the night sky, seen directly opposite the Sun, caused by the reflection of sunlight from interplanetary dust

Examples

see the counterglow at night
On clear nights away from city lights, some observers can detect the counterglow near the ecliptic.
Scientists study the counterglow to learn more about interplanetary dust and micrometeoroids.
The counterglow is often too faint to see with the naked eye under most conditions.
