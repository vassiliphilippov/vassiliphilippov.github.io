flux

noun
substance for removing metal impurities
Pronunciation: / flʌks / (AmE), / flʌks / (BrE)
A substance added to molten metals to combine with impurities so they can be easily removed.

Examples

add flux to metal
Adding a flux helps purify the molten iron by binding with unwanted substances.
Borax is commonly used as a flux in the process of soldering metals.
The blacksmith explained that the flux would make dross rise to the surface for easy removal.
