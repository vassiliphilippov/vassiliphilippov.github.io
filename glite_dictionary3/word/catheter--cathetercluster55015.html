catheter

noun
medical: a tube for fluid passage in the body
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθətər / (AmE), / ˈkæθətə / (BrE)
a thin, flexible tube inserted into the body to allow the passage of fluids or to keep a passageway open

Examples

catheter insertion procedure
After surgery, a urinary catheter was necessary for several days.
The doctor inserted a catheter to drain excess fluid from the bladder.
The nurse carefully checked the position of the catheter during the patient's recovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cannula: a small tube inserted into a body cavity, duct, or vessel
