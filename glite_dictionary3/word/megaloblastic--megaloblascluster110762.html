megaloblastic

adjective
relating to large immature red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡələˈblæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələˈblæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by the presence of unusually large immature red blood cells (megaloblasts), typically used in reference to certain types of anemia.

Examples

megaloblastic anemia
A deficiency in vitamin B12 can lead to megaloblastic changes in the bone marrow.
The doctor diagnosed her with megaloblastic anemia after examining her blood cells.
Treatment for megaloblastic anemia often includes supplementation of folic acid or vitamin B12.

Related Terms

  • synonym – megaloblastic anemia: a type of anemia characterized by large, abnormal red blood cells
