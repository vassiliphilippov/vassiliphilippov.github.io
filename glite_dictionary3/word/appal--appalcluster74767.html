to appal

verb
to shock or horrify greatly
Pronunciation: / əˈpɔl / (AmE), / əˈpɑːl /; / əˈpɔːl / (BrE)
to shock or horrify someone very much; to fill with strong feelings of fear, shock, or disgust

Examples

to appal the crowd
I was utterly appalled by the conditions in the hospital.
The violence in the movie appalled many viewers.
Their lack of compassion continues to appal me.
Her lack of empathy often appals her colleagues.
The cruelty of the dictator's actions continued to appal the international community.
We were appalled by the filthy conditions in the restaurant.

  • synonym – disgust: cause to feel revulsion or strong disapproval
  • synonym – horrify: to cause someone to feel horror or shock
