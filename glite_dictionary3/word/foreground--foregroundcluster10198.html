to foreground

verb
make prominent or noticeable
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to make something more prominent, noticeable, or important in attention or consideration

Examples

to foreground an issue
In her analysis, she foregrounds social and cultural influences on the text.
The exhibition foregrounds the work of female artists from the twentieth century.
The report foregrounded the urgent need for environmental reform.

Related Terms

  • synonym – highlight: to emphasize something as particularly important or prominent
  • antonym – obscure: to make less visible or less prominent
