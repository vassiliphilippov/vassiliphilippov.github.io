to mold

verb
become moldy due to dampness
Pronunciation: / moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to become covered with mold and begin to decay, especially because of dampness; to spoil due to humidity.

Examples

bread molds quickly
After a week of rain, the bread began to mold in the cupboard.
If you leave old fruit in a warm, damp place, it will quickly mold.
The papers in the basement molded because the room was so humid.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rot: to decay or decompose as a result of bacterial or fungal action
  • synonym – spoil: to become unfit for consumption due to decay or the action of microorganisms
