brambly

adjective
covered with or like brambles
Pronunciation: / ˈbræmbli / (AmE), / ˈbræmbli / (BrE)
(of land or vegetation) covered with or resembling brambles; thickly overgrown with brambles or similar undergrowth.

Examples

brambly hedge
She wore long sleeves to protect herself from the brambly hedge.
The path became difficult to walk as it wound through a brambly thicket.
They cleared the brambly area at the back of the garden to plant vegetables.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear: free of undergrowth or obstructions
  • synonym – thorny: covered with thorns
