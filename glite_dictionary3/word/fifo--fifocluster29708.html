FIFO

noun
inventory accounting method (first in, first out)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪfəʊ / (BrE)
a method of inventory accounting in which the earliest acquired items are assumed to be sold or used first

Examples

FIFO inventory method
FIFO is often preferred in industries where products are perishable or have expiration dates.
Many companies use FIFO to value their inventory for tax purposes.
Under FIFO, the cost of goods sold reflects the cost of the oldest inventory items.

Related Terms

  • antonym – LIFO: inventory accounting method (last in, first out)
