to localise

verb
adapt for a local market
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt a product, piece of content, or software so it is suitable for a particular country or region, typically including translation, cultural adjustments, formatting changes, and regulatory compliance.

Examples

to localise an app
The company plans to localise its app for the Japanese market before launch next spring.
They hired translators and designers to localise product packaging for several European countries.
If you want more users, you must localise the website and adapt content to local customs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – localize: adapt for a particular country or region (American spelling of 'localise').
  • synonym – translate: to express the meaning of text in another language; often a key part of localisation.
