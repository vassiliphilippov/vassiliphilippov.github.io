iridescent

adjective
displaying shifting, rainbow-like colors
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈdɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdɛsənt / (BrE)
showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles or in different lights, like a rainbow or peacock feathers

Examples

iridescent peacock feathers
She admired the iridescent colors of the peacock's feathers.
The artist painted the fish with an iridescent glaze to make them shimmer.
The surface of the soap bubble appeared iridescent in the sunlight.
She wore an iridescent scarf that shimmered in many hues depending on the light.
The soap bubble gleamed with an iridescent sheen as it floated in the sun.
The surface of the abalone shell is naturally iridescent, reflecting a spectrum of colors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dull: lacking brightness or sheen
  • synonym – lustrous: having a soft glow, reflecting light evenly and efficiently
  • antonym – matte: dull and lacking shine or gloss
  • synonym – opalescent: showing varying colors like an opal
  • synonym – pearlescent: having a luster resembling that of pearl
