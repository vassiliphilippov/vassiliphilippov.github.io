to choke

verb
struggle to breathe or speak from emotion
Pronunciation: / tʃoʊk / (AmE), / tʃəʊk / (BrE)
to suppress, restrain, or stifle something such as an emotion, creativity, or expression; or to have difficulty speaking or breathing because of strong emotion

Examples

to choke back tears
He began to choke with sorrow as he recalled the tragic events.
She tried to speak but choked up when she saw the crowd.
The news made him choke with a mix of surprise and joy.
Strict regulations can choke innovation in the industry.
The constant criticism began to choke her creativity.
Too many rules may choke a child's imagination.
He managed to choke his disappointment and congratulated the winner.
She tried to choke her laughter during the meeting.
The leader worked hard to choke any dissent within the organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym – express: to convey or show a feeling or thought
  • synonym – gasp: to inhale suddenly, especially with surprise or difficulty
  • synonym – stifle: to restrain or stop something from developing
  • synonym – suffocate: to prevent something from growing by depriving it of necessary conditions
  • synonym – suppress: to put an end to the expression or activity of something
