to dehumanize

verb
deprive of human qualities or dignity
Pronunciation: / diˈhjuː.məˌnaɪz /; / diˈhjuːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈhjuː.mə.naɪz /; / diˈhjuːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat a person or group as if they are not human, by removing their dignity, individuality, or warmth

Examples

to dehumanize a group
Propaganda is often used to dehumanize enemy groups during wartime.
Some argue that excessive technology in the workplace may dehumanize employees.
The harsh conditions of the prison system can dehumanize inmates and staff alike.
Intensive factory work can dehumanize employees by turning their labor into a set of monotonous tasks.
Long hours and endless paperwork can quickly dehumanize even the most dedicated professionals.
Many critics argue that modern bureaucracy tends to dehumanize public services, making them rigid and impersonal.
