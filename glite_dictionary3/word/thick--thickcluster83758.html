thick

adjective
dense in consistency
Pronunciation: / θɪk / (AmE), / θɪk / (BrE)
dense or closely packed; having a lot of something in one place, making it hard to see or pass through

Examples

thick fog
Early morning travelers struggled to see through the thick fog covering the highway.
She poured the thick smoothie into a tall glass, impressed by its creamy texture.
The stew became thick after simmering for hours on the stove.
It was almost impossible to push through the thick hedge surrounding the old house.
The thick undergrowth made it difficult for the hikers to find the path.
We wandered through the thick forest until the sunlight disappeared.
A cat's eyes glowed in the thick shadow beneath the porch.
Fog hung over the city, making the thick darkness almost impenetrable.
We stumbled through the thick night, unable to see more than a few steps ahead.
