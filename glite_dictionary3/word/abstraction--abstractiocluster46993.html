abstraction

noun
preoccupation or deep absorption in thought
Pronunciation: / əbˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
the state of being preoccupied or absorbed in thought to the exclusion of other things

Examples

lost in abstraction
His abstraction during meetings was becoming a concern to his manager.
Lost in abstraction, she didn’t hear the question directed at her.
The painter's abstraction was apparent, as he barely noticed anything around him.
