internationalisation

noun
the act of placing under international control
Pronunciation: / ɪn.tɚˌnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪn.təˌnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of bringing something, such as an area or issue, under the control or regulation of multiple international authorities

Examples

internationalisation of a region
After years under local authority, the internationalisation of the region was proposed by the United Nations.
Debates about the internationalisation of space law have intensified in recent years.
The internationalisation of the canal was supported by several countries to ensure open access.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nationalisation: the act of bringing something under national government control
