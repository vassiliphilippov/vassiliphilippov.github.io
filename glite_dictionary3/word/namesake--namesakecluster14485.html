namesake

noun
person or thing with the same name
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪmˌseɪk / (AmE), / ˈneɪmseɪk / (BrE)
a person or thing that has the same name as another or is named after another

Examples

famous namesake
She met another woman at the event who happened to be her namesake.
The school was named after its founder's namesake, John Smith.
Unlike his famous namesake, William Shakespeare, he was not interested in literature.
