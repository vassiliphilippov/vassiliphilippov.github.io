to synchronise

verb
cause to match in time or rate
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪz /; / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪz / (BrE)
to make two or more things happen, move, or operate at the same time or in coordination

Examples

to synchronise movements
Before starting the experiment, all team members had to synchronise their watches.
