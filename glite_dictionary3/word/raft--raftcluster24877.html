raft

noun
large concrete foundation slab
Pronunciation: / ræft / (AmE), / rɑːft / (BrE)
a foundation consisting of a large, solid slab of reinforced concrete, used to support structures on soft or unstable ground

Examples

concrete raft foundation
A raft foundation helps distribute the load of the structure over a wide area.
Engineers recommended a raft for the building due to the soft, shifting soil.
The new apartment complex was built on a raft to ensure stability on the marshy site.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slab foundation: a foundation made from a single concrete slab
