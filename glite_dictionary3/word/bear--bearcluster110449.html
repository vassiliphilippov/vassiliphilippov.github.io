to bear

verb
contain; have within
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / beə / (BrE)
to carry, hold, support, or have something (physically or metaphorically), or to display a quality, mark, or characteristic; also, to conduct or comport oneself in a particular way

Examples

bear a heavy load
The envelope bore a strange symbol inside its lining.
The old bottle still bears the scent of perfume after so many years.
This document bears an important message hidden in the text.
For many years, he bore the responsibility of headmaster.
She proudly bears the title of Professor Emeritus.
The constitution states that only citizens may bear public office.
His letter bore the president’s signature.
She bears a striking resemblance to her grandmother.
The statue bears an inscription from the 19th century.
