accelerando

noun
increase in musical tempo
Pronunciation: / əksɛləˈrændoʊ / (AmE), / æksɛləˈrændəʊ / (BrE)
an increase in the speed or tempo of music, or a passage played with gradually increasing speed

Examples

orchestra plays accelerando
During the accelerando, the musicians had to closely follow the conductor’s tempo changes.
The composer marked an accelerando in the third measure of the score.
The orchestra executed a dramatic accelerando towards the end of the piece.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ritardando: a gradual decrease in tempo
