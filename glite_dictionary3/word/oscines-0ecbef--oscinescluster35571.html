oscines

noun
suborder of typical songbirds
Pronunciation: / ˈɒsɪniːz / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪniːz / (BrE)
A suborder of birds known as the typical songbirds, characterized by their developed vocal organs allowing complex songs.

Examples

oscines sing songs
Scientists have long studied the complex vocalizations produced by oscines.
The majority of familiar backyard birds, such as robins and sparrows, are classified as oscines.
Unlike suboscines, oscines are capable of learning new songs throughout their lives.

Related Terms

  • synonym – passerines: the larger order of perching birds to which oscines belong
