strangled

adjective
suppressed or choked-off (sound or emotion)
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡəld / (BrE)
(of a sound, emotion, or action) suppressed or produced with difficulty, as if choked off or constrained.

Examples

strangled cry
A strangled laugh escaped her lips as she tried not to disturb the meeting.
His voice emerged in a strangled whisper, barely audible above the noise.
She let out a strangled sob when she heard the bad news.
