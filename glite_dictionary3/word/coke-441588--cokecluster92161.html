coke

noun
solid fuel made from coal
Pronunciation: / koʊk / (AmE), / kəʊk / (BrE)
A solid, carbon-rich fuel derived from heating coal in the absence of air, used chiefly in industry, especially for smelting iron ore.

Examples

coke for steelmaking
High-quality coke is essential for efficient iron production.
The process of making coke involves heating coal without air.
The steel plant uses coke to fuel its blast furnaces.
