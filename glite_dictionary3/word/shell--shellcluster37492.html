to shell

verb
attack with explosive shells
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl / (AmE), / ʃɛl / (BrE)
to fire explosive shells at a place or people, typically as a military attack

Examples

to shell a city
As the city was shelling intensively, residents fled to safer areas.
Government troops continued to shell rebel positions along the border.
The village was shelled repeatedly during the night by enemy forces.

  • antonym – ceasefire: a suspension of active hostilities in warfare
