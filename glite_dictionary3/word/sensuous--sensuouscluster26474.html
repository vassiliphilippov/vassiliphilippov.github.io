sensuous

adjective
deriving pleasure from beauty
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.su.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sjuː.əs / (BrE)
Taking delight in or deriving pleasure from beauty, especially as perceived by the senses.

Examples

sensuous beauty
She was drawn to the sensuous beauty of the painting's colors and textures.
The sensuous music filled the room with a sense of warmth and comfort.
Their garden was designed to provide a sensuous experience for visitors, full of fragrant flowers and soft grasses.

  • antonym – sensual: relating to physical or sexual pleasure
