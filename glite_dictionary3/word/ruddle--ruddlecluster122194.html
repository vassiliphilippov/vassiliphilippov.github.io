to ruddle

verb
to color with red ochre
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌdəl / (BrE)
to color or redden something with, or as if with, red ochre

Examples

to ruddle stone walls
Centuries ago, artists would ruddle cave walls with vivid imagery.
Her cheeks seemed to ruddle with excitement on the brisk winter morning.
The shepherd would ruddle his sheep to identify them in the flock.
