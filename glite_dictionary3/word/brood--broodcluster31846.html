brood

noun
a group of young animals born at one time
Pronunciation: / bruːd / (AmE), / bruːd / (BrE)
a group of young animals, especially birds, produced or cared for at one time by a parent or parents

Examples

a brood of chicks
The duck and her brood swam across the pond in a neat line.
The hen protected her brood from danger as they wandered through the yard.
When the fox approached, the mother bird gathered her brood under her wings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clutch: a group of eggs or young birds hatched at one time
  • synonym – litter: a group of young animals born to an animal at one time, especially mammals
