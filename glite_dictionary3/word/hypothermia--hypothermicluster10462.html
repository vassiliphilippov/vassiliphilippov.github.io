hypothermia

noun
dangerously low body temperature
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈθɜrmiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəʊˈθɜːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the body temperature drops significantly below normal, often due to prolonged exposure to cold

Examples

hypothermia from cold weather
If hypothermia occurs, it is important to warm the person gradually and seek medical help immediately.
The hiker was treated for hypothermia after being stranded overnight in freezing temperatures.
Winter swimmers risk developing hypothermia if they stay in cold water for too long.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hyperthermia: abnormally high body temperature
