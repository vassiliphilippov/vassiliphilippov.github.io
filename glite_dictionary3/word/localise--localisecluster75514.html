to localise

verb
identify the location of something
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to identify, determine, or specify the location of something

Examples

to localise a fault
Engineers worked all night to localise the fault in the network.
It often takes time to localise the origin of persistent noise in old buildings.
The doctors managed to localise the infection to one area of the lung.
His sense of smell helped him localise the leaking gas quickly.
Producers decided to localise the drama in contemporary London.
The research team was able to localise the source of the infection to a small village.
