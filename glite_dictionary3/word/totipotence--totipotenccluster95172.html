totipotence

noun
the ability of a cell to form a complete organism
Pronunciation: / toʊˈtɪpətəns / (AmE), / təʊˈtɪpətəns / (BrE)
the ability of a single cell to develop into all the different cell types of an organism or into a complete organism

Examples

totipotence of a cell
Loss of totipotence in animal cells marks a key step in embryonic differentiation.
Researchers studied the totipotence of certain plant cells to regenerate whole plants.
The totipotence of a fertilized egg allows it to develop into a complete organism.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – pluripotency: the ability of a cell to develop into several cell types but not a whole organism
