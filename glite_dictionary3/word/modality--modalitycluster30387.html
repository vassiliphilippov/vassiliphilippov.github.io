modality

noun
a particular mode or manner; sense or form
Pronunciation: / moʊˈdæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / məʊˈdæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a particular mode in which something exists or is experienced, especially a sensory or linguistic mode such as possibility, necessity, or obligation

Examples

sensory modality experience
Doctors considered different treatment modalities to determine the most effective approach for the patient.
In linguistics, modality refers to the expression of possibility or necessity in a sentence.
The experiment explored each modality of human perception, including sight, sound, and touch.
