straggle

noun
disorderly or scattered group
Pronunciation: / ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡəl / (BrE)
a group of people or things that are spread irregularly or scattered in a disorderly way

Examples

a straggle of sheep
A straggle of sheep wandered away from the main flock.
After the parade, a straggle of people loitered in the main square.
We passed a straggle of cottages on our hike through the valley.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cluster: a group of closely packed things
