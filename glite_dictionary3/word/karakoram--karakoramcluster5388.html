Karakoram

proper noun
mountain range in northern Kashmir
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrəˈkɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌkærəkˈɔːrəm / (BrE)
a mountain range in northern Kashmir, extending from the Hindu Kush and containing the world’s second highest peak, K2.

Examples

the Karakoram mountains
Climbers from around the globe travel to the Karakoram for its challenging mountains and breathtaking scenery.
Glaciers in the Karakoram are among the largest outside the polar regions.
The Karakoram range is home to some of the world’s highest peaks, including K2.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Hindu Kush: a mountain range to the west of the Karakoram in central Asia
