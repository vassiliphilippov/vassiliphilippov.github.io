diminutive

noun
word form indicating smallness or affection
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɪn.jə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪn.jʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
A word formed with a suffix or alteration to indicate smallness, endearment, or triviality, such as '-let' in 'booklet'.

Examples

diminutive suffix
Adding '-ette' at the end of a word often forms a diminutive in English.
In Russian, the name 'Sasha' is a diminutive of 'Alexander'.
The word 'duckling' is a classic example of a diminutive created by the suffix '-ling'.
