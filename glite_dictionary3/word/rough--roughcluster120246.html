rough

adjective
aggressive or prone to violence
Pronunciation: / rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
(of a person or group) ready and able to use force or violence; aggressive or tough in character.

Examples

rough neighborhood
He grew up in a rough neighborhood where fights were common.
Some parts of the city are known for their rough inhabitants.
The team was full of rough players who never backed down from a challenge.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gentle: kind and not likely to use force or violence
  • synonym – tough: strong and able to withstand hardship or force, sometimes aggressive
