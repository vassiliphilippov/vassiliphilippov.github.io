to beg

verb
evade the issue; assume a point (in argument)
Pronunciation: / bɛɡ / (AmE), / bɛɡ / (BrE)
(of a statement or argument) to evade a direct answer or to assume the truth of the point at issue, especially in the phrase 'beg the question'.

Examples

beg the question
Rather than addressing the issue directly, he chose to beg the question.
She accused her opponent of attempting to beg the point in the debate.
The politician tried to beg the question instead of providing a clear answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assume: to suppose something without proof
  • synonym – evade: to avoid giving a direct answer
