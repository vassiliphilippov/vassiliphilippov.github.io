ontology

noun
knowledge organization system in computer science
Pronunciation: / ɑnˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
(in computer science) a structured framework that organizes knowledge within a specific domain, detailing entities, concepts, and relationships, typically in a hierarchical way.

Examples

ontology for data organization
Researchers developed an ontology to structure all the information for the digital library project.
The AI system was built using a complex ontology that mapped out medical concepts and their relationships.
Without a shared ontology, different systems have trouble exchanging and understanding data.

  • synonym – taxonomy: classification system, especially in science
