domain

noun
area under rule or control
Pronunciation: / doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
A territory or area under the rule or control of a particular ruler, government, or authority.

Examples

domain of a king
Each feudal lord governed his own domain independently from the throne.
The company expanded its domain to other countries in the region.
The king's domain included vast forests and several towns.
