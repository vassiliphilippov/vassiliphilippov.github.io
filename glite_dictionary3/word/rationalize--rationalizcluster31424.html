to rationalize

verb
explain or justify with reasons
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃ.ə.nəl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to attempt to explain or justify behavior or an attitude with logical reasons, even if these are not true or appropriate

Examples

to rationalize bad behavior
He often rationalizes his mistakes rather than admitting to them.
It's easy to rationalize bad habits by saying everyone else does it too.
She tried to rationalize her decision to skip the meeting, but her manager was not convinced.

Related Terms

  • synonym – excuse: to attempt to lessen the blame for
  • synonym – justify: to show or prove to be right or reasonable
