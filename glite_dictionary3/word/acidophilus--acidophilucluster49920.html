acidophilus

noun
probiotic bacterium for gut health
Pronunciation: / ˌæsɪˈdɑːfɪləs / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈdɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A type of bacterium used to ferment dairy products such as yogurt, and as a probiotic to promote gut health.

Examples

acidophilus bacteria in yogurt
Consuming foods rich in acidophilus may help maintain a balanced gut microbiome.
Doctors often recommend taking acidophilus supplements after antibiotics to restore healthy bacteria.
Many types of yogurt contain live acidophilus cultures beneficial for digestion.
