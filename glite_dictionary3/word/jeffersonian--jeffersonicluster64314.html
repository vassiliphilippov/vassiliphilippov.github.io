Jeffersonian

noun
follower of Thomas Jefferson's ideas
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛfərˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛfəˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the ideas and principles of Thomas Jefferson, especially those related to democracy and government

Examples

a Jeffersonian thinker
Many Jeffersonians opposed the expansion of federal power in the early United States.
Modern political debates often echo the concerns of Jeffersonians regarding individual liberty.
The historian wrote extensively about influential Jeffersonians during the early 19th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Federalist: a supporter of a strong central government, often contrasted with Jeffersonians
