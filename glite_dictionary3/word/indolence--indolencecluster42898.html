indolence

noun
habitual laziness; avoidance of work
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.də.ləns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dələns / (BrE)
the quality of being habitually lazy or avoiding work; a tendency to be inactive due to dislike of exertion

Examples

chronic indolence
His indolence prevented him from finishing the project on time.
Rising temperatures during summer often encourage a sense of indolence among workers.
She blamed her poor grades on her own indolence and lack of motivation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – diligence: careful and persistent work or effort
  • synonym – laziness: the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy
