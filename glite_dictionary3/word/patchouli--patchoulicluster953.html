patchouli

noun
fragrant tropical plant; source of perfume oil
Pronunciation: / pəˈtʃuːli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃuːli / (BrE)
A small shrubby plant native to tropical Asia, especially India, whose strongly scented leaves yield a fragrant oil used in perfumes and incense.

Examples

patchouli plant leaves
Many perfumes use patchouli oil as a base note for its distinctive fragrance.
She cultivated patchouli in her garden to harvest the aromatic leaves.
The earthy aroma of patchouli filled the room as she burned the scented candle.
