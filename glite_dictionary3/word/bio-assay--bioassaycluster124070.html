bio-assay

noun
scientific test of biological activity
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.oʊˌæseɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊˌæseɪ / (BrE)
A scientific test that measures the biological activity of a substance by observing its effects on living organisms and comparing the results to a standard.

Examples

bio-assay of a drug
Scientists use a bio-assay to compare the antibacterial effects of different compounds.
The bio-assay revealed that the plant extract significantly slowed the growth of the bacteria.
The drug was subjected to a bio-assay to determine its potency and safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assay: a test or analysis, especially of a chemical or biological substance
