editing

noun
targeted modification of DNA
Pronunciation: / ˈedɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈedɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(in genetics) the process of making deliberate, targeted changes to an organism's DNA or genome, using techniques such as CRISPR to alter specific genes or traits.

Examples

gene editing technology
Researchers are refining gene editing techniques to correct genetic mutations.
Clinical trials test whether editing embryos can safely prevent inherited disorders in future generations.
The lab used CRISPR for precise editing of plant genomes to increase drought resistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gene editing: the targeted modification of specific genes within an organism's DNA
  • synonym – genome editing: the alteration of an organism's complete set of DNA to change traits or functions
