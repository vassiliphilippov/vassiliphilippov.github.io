to detribalise

verb
cause loss of tribal cultural identity
Pronunciation: / diˈtraɪbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈtraɪbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause members of a tribe to lose their cultural identity or distinctiveness

Examples

to detribalise a community
Government policies attempted to detribalise indigenous communities through education and relocation programs.
Some argue that rapid urbanization can unintentionally detribalise rural societies, leading to the loss of ancestral heritage.
The missionaries' efforts in the region aimed to detribalise the local population by replacing traditional customs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acculturate: to cause to adopt the cultural traits of another group
  • synonym – assimilate: to cause to become part of a different society, country, etc.
