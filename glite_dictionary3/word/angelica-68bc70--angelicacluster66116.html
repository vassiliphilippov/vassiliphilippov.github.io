angelica

noun
a tall herb of the genus Angelica
Pronunciation: / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
a tall plant of the parsley family, with compound leaves and small white or greenish flowers, belonging to the genus Angelica, some species of which are used in cooking and herbal medicine

Examples

wild angelica plant
She added dried angelica to the herbal tea blend for its unique flavor.
The candied stems of angelica are often used to decorate cakes and desserts.
Wild angelica grows abundantly along the riverbanks in northern Europe.
