stout-billed

adjective
having a strong, thick beak
Pronunciation: / staʊt bɪld / (AmE), / staʊt bɪld / (BrE)
(of a bird) having a strong and thick beak.

Examples

stout-billed finch
A stout-billed bird can often be recognized by the broad base of its beak.
Many finches are stout-billed, which helps them eat seeds efficiently.
The stout-billed parrot uses its strong beak to crack open hard nuts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – thin-billed: having a slender, narrow beak
