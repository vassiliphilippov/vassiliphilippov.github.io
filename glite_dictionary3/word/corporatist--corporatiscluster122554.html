corporatist

noun
supporter of corporatism
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrpərətɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɔːp(ə)rətɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates corporatism, an economic or political system in which interest groups such as businesses, labor, and government work together in collective associations.

Examples

a corporatist thinker
As a lifelong corporatist, she believed in strong partnerships between government and industry.
Critics accused the administration of favoring corporatist interests over the needs of ordinary citizens.
The new policy delighted the corporatist factions within the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corporatism: the control of a state or organization by large interest groups
