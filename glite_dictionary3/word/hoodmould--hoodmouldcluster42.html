hoodmould

noun
projecting stone moulding above windows or doors
Pronunciation: / ˈhʊdmuːld / (AmE), / ˈhʊdməʊld / (BrE)
An external architectural moulding of stone, projecting above a doorway, window, or arch, designed to throw off rainwater.

Examples

hoodmould above window
A decorative hoodmould added both charm and practicality to the 19th-century manor's facade.
Restorers carefully repaired the crumbling hoodmould over the arched entrance.
The old church features a hoodmould above each stained glass window to protect them from rain.
