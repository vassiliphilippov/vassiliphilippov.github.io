locater

noun
person who marks land claim boundaries
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who determines or fixes the boundaries of land claims, especially in legal or historical contexts.

Examples

locater of land boundaries
A skilled locater was essential during the gold rush to avoid disputes over land.
Several land owners hired a locater to resolve the boundary conflict.
The locater used old maps to determine the original property lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym – surveyor: a person whose job is to measure land and mark property boundaries
