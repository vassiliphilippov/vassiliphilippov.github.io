Halogeton glomeratus

noun
a toxic annual herb, harmful to livestock
Pronunciation: / ˌhæləˈdʒiːtən ɡloʊˈmɛrətəs / (AmE), / ˌhælədʒiːˈtɒn ɡlɒˈmɛrətəs / (BrE)
a coarse annual herb native to Eurasia, introduced to North America, known for its high oxalate content which makes it dangerous to grazing animals such as sheep and cattle

Examples

Halogeton glomeratus invades rangelands
Halogeton glomeratus has invaded many western rangelands, creating serious problems for ranchers and their livestock.
Sheep can suffer kidney damage after grazing in areas dense with Halogeton glomeratus.
The spread of Halogeton glomeratus is often linked to overgrazing and poor land management practices.
