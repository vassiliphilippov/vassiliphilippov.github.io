bivalent

adjective
having a valence of two
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.vəˌlɛnt / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.və.lənt / (BrE)
(of an atom, ion, or molecule) having a valence of two; capable of forming two bonds.

Examples

bivalent atom bonds
In some reactions, the ion acts as a bivalent species, forming two covalent bonds.
Oxygen is a bivalent element because it forms two chemical bonds in most compounds.
The vaccine is bivalent, targeting two different strains of the virus.
