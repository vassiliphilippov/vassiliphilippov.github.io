aldosterone

noun
a hormone regulating salt and water balance
Pronunciation: / ælˈdɑstəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ælˈdɒstərəʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the adrenal cortex that regulates the balance of sodium and potassium and helps control blood pressure

Examples

aldosterone hormone levels
Aldosterone plays an important role in regulating blood pressure and fluid balance.
Patients with Addison's disease may have low levels of aldosterone.
Your doctor may order tests to check your aldosterone levels if you show symptoms of hormone imbalance.
