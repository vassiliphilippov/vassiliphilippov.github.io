adjuvant

noun
substance that boosts medical treatment
Pronunciation: / ˈædʒəvənt / (AmE), / ˈadʒʊvənt / (BrE)
A substance added to a drug or vaccine to increase its effectiveness.

Examples

adjuvant in a vaccine
Doctors may use an adjuvant to enhance the effects of chemotherapy.
Researchers tested a new adjuvant in combination with standard treatments.
The vaccine contained an adjuvant to stimulate a stronger immune response.
