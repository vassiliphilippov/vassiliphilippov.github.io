patronizing

adjective
condescending in manner or speech
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of behavior, speech, etc.) showing an apparent kindness or helpfulness that betrays a feeling of superiority; condescending.

Examples

patronizing attitude
His patronizing tone annoyed everyone at the meeting.
Please don't be so patronizing—I know what I'm doing.
She gave me a patronizing smile that made me feel like a child.

Related Terms

  • synonym – condescending: showing that you believe you are more intelligent or better than other people
  • antonym – respectful: showing politeness or honor toward someone or something
