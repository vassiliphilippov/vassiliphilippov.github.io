to capitalize

verb
convert reserve funds into capital
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtəlaɪz /; / ˈkapɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
in business or accounting, to provide a business with capital, convert funds into capital, or treat money spent as a capital asset

Examples

to capitalize a company
After years of saving profits, the firm plans to capitalize the accumulated funds.
Several companies capitalized their retained earnings by issuing new shares to shareholders.
The board decided to capitalize the company's reserves to fund expansion.
Accountants must decide whether to capitalize or expense these expenditures based on established rules.
Certain development costs can be capitalized and depreciated over time according to accounting standards.
The company decided to capitalize the cost of new equipment instead of recording it as an expense.
Startups often seek to capitalize their businesses by attracting venture capital and loans.
The company was capitalized through a mix of private investment and bank loans.
The partners decided to capitalize the new firm with both equity and debt.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expense: to record money spent as an expense in accounting
  • synonym – fund: to provide money for a business or project
