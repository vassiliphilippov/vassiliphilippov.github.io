diapedesis

noun
movement of blood cells through capillary walls
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.ə.pəˈdiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ə.pɪˈdiː.sɪs / (BrE)
the movement of blood cells, especially white blood cells, out of the bloodstream by passing through the intact walls of capillaries into surrounding tissue

Examples

diapedesis of cells
During inflammation, diapedesis allows white blood cells to reach areas of tissue injury.
Researchers studied how diapedesis is regulated at the cellular level in healthy and diseased tissues.
The process of diapedesis is crucial for the immune response to infection.

  • synonym – extravasation: the leakage of blood, lymph, or other fluid from a vessel into the surrounding tissue
