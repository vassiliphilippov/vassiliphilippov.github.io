stoneware

noun
type of hard, nonporous ceramic
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌweə / (BrE)
Ceramic ware made from clay and fired at a high temperature until it becomes hard, vitrified, and nonporous.

Examples

stoneware mug
She collects antique stoneware jars from the nineteenth century.
The artist prefers working with stoneware because of its durability.
The plates are made from stoneware, making them safe for use in the oven.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – earthenware: pottery made from clay and fired at a lower temperature, typically porous
  • degree higher – porcelain: hard, white, translucent ceramic fired at even higher temperatures
