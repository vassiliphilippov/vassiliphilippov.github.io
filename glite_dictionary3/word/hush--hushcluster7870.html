to hush

verb
make someone or something quiet
Pronunciation: / hʌʃ / (AmE), / hʌʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something quiet, or to become quiet or silent

Examples

to hush the crowd
He tried to hush the crowd so the speaker could begin.
She put a finger to her lips to hush everyone during the movie.
The librarian hushed the noisy students quickly.
After a few minutes, the children finally hushed and listened to the story.
The audience immediately hushed when the doors opened.
The teacher raised her hand and the class gradually hushed to hear the announcement.
Please hush for a moment while I answer the phone.
The forest hushed as the sun began to set, leaving only the sound of cricket chirps.
When the teacher entered, the students quickly hushed and sat down.

Related Terms
