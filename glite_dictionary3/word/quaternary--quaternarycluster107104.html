Quaternary

noun
geological period of last 2 million years
Pronunciation: / kwəˈtɝː.nɛr.i / (AmE), / kwəˈtɜː.nəri / (BrE)
the geological period covering the last 2 million years, including the present time, characterized by the development of humans and repeated glaciations.

Examples

the Quaternary period
Fossils found in the Quaternary deposits include early humans and large mammals.
Scientists study ice cores to learn more about the Earth's climate during the Quaternary.
The Quaternary period saw dramatic climate changes that shaped modern ecosystems.
