magnification

noun
exaggeration of importance or significance
Pronunciation: / ˌmæɡ.nə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɡ.nɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of making something seem more important, significant, or impressive than it really is; exaggeration.

Examples

magnification of problems
His constant magnification of minor issues made the meeting last twice as long as necessary.
The politician's statement was a clear magnification of the problem to influence public opinion.
There was considerable magnification of the risks involved, causing unnecessary worry.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exaggeration: the act of making something seem larger or more important than it is
