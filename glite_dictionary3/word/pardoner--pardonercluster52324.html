pardoner

noun
medieval seller of church indulgences
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrdənər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdnə / (BrE)
(historical) A medieval cleric who raised money for the church by selling papal indulgences, often viewed with suspicion or criticism.

Examples

pardoner sells indulgences
In Chaucer's tale, the pardoner is depicted as a corrupt and greedy figure.
Many viewed the pardoner's practices as a form of church corruption.
The pardoner traveled from village to village selling indulgences to the faithful.

Related Terms

  • synonym – indulgence: a remission of punishment for sins, granted by the Catholic Church
