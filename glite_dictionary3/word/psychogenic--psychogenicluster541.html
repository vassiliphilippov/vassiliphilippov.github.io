psychogenic

adjective
originating from psychological causes
Pronunciation: / saɪ.kəˈdʒɛn.ɪk /; / ˌsaɪkəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / saɪ.kəˈdʒɛn.ɪk /; / ˌsaɪkəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a symptom, illness, or disorder) caused by mental or emotional factors, not by physical causes

Examples

psychogenic illness
Researchers studied cases of psychogenic blindness among trauma survivors.
The doctor explained that her symptoms were psychogenic rather than physical.
The patient's illness was determined to be psychogenic in nature.
Doctors distinguished between psychogenic and organic causes for her seizures.
Some forms of amnesia are considered psychogenic rather than resulting from brain injury.
The patient’s pain was believed to be psychogenic in origin, not the result of any physical disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym – psychosomatic: (of a condition) involving both mind and body, especially bodily symptoms caused by mental factors
