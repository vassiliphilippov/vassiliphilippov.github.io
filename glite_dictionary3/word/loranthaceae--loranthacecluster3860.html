Loranthaceae

noun
parasitic plant family (Loranthaceae)
Pronunciation: / lɔːˌrænˈθeɪsiˌi /; / lɔːˌrænˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / lɒːˌranˈθeɪsiː /; / lɒˌranˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of parasitic or hemiparasitic shrubs or small trees, mainly found in tropical and temperate regions, that often attach to host plants by specialized structures called haustoria
