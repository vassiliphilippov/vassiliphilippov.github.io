mass-produced

adjective
produced in large quantities
Pronunciation: / ˌmæs prəˈdust / (AmE), / ˌmæs prəˈdjuːst / (BrE)
(of goods) produced in large quantities, often using assembly-line methods, rather than by hand or in small amounts.

Examples

mass-produced cars
Many affordable cars today are mass-produced in giant factories.
She preferred unique, handmade items over mass-produced decorations.
The rise of mass-produced clothing transformed the fashion industry.

Related Terms

  • antonym – custom-made: made to order for a specific customer
  • antonym – handmade: made by hand, not by machine
