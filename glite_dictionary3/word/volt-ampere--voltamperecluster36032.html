volt-ampere

noun
unit of electrical power in AC circuits
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊlt ˈæmpɛr / (AmE), / ˈvəʊlt ˈæmpɛə / (BrE)
a unit of electrical power in an alternating-current circuit, equal to the power produced when one volt causes a current of one ampere; used to express apparent power

Examples

volt-ampere rating
Engineers often use volt-amperes to rate the capacity of transformers.
The generator has a maximum output of 5,000 volt-amperes.
To avoid overloading the circuit, calculate the total volt-ampere demand for all equipment.

  • synonym – watt: unit of electrical power equal to one joule per second
