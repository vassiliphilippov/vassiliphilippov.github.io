zymogen

noun
inactive enzyme precursor
Pronunciation: / ˈzaɪmoʊˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈzaɪmədʒən / (BrE)
an inactive substance that is converted into an active enzyme by a biochemical change

Examples

inactive zymogen enzyme
Activation of a zymogen occurs through cleavage of a specific peptide bond.
Pepsin is formed from its zymogen, pepsinogen, in the stomach.
The pancreas secretes digestive enzymes in zymogen form to prevent self-digestion.
