pantaloon

noun
old-fashioned close-fitting trousers
Pronunciation: / ˌpæn.t̬əˈlun / (AmE), / ˌpæn.təˈluːn / (BrE)
A man's close-fitting pair of trousers, especially those of a type worn in former times.

Examples

silk pantaloons costume
During the masquerade ball, the nobleman wore an elaborate mask and silk pantaloons.
He studied portraits from the 18th century that showed men dressed in pantaloons and waistcoats.
In some plays, the old clown character is depicted in baggy pantaloons.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trousers: an outer garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles, with a separate part for each leg
