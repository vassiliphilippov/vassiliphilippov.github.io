excision

noun
formal expulsion from church membership
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of formally expelling a member from a church, depriving them of membership and religious privileges

Examples

excision from the church
After repeated warnings, the church resorted to excision to uphold its doctrines.
Historically, excision was used as a disciplinary measure against heretics.
The process of excision was carried out with great solemnity in front of the congregation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – excommunication: official exclusion from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church
