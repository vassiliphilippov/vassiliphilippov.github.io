wasted

adjective
reduced in size or strength (body part)
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪd / (BrE)
(of a body or body part) very thin, weak, or reduced in size, usually because of illness, injury, or lack of use

Examples

wasted muscles
After months in a cast, her leg appeared wasted and weak.
Physical therapy can help restore strength to wasted limbs.
The patient's wasted muscles made movement difficult after the long illness.
Her wasted frame told the story of her long struggle against the disease.
The refugees arrived wasted and pale after weeks without food.
Years of illness had left his body wasted and frail.

Related Terms

  • synonym – atrophied: (of body tissue) gradually declined in effectiveness or vigor due to underuse or neglect
  • synonym – emaciated: abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food
  • synonym – gaunt: extremely thin, especially from suffering, hunger, or age
  • antonym – robust: strong and healthy; vigorous
