grading

noun
color and tonal adjustment in post-production
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
(in film, video, and photography) the post-production process of adjusting color, contrast, and tonal values of footage or images to achieve a desired look.

Examples

color grading footage
The editor completed the color grading to give the film its moody palette.
Proper grading can make daytime footage appear like a warm, cinematic evening scene.
She spent the afternoon learning LUTs and advanced grading techniques for her short film.
