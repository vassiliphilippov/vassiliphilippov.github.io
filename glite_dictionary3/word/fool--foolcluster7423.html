to fool

verb
act playfully or waste time
Pronunciation: / ful / (AmE), / fuːl / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or mischievous way, especially by indulging in horseplay or wasting time

Examples

to fool around playfully
He told his friends to stop fooling and pay attention to the instructions.
Sometimes it's nice just to fool about and not take life too seriously.
The students began to fool around after the teacher left the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mess around: to behave in a silly or playful way
  • synonym – play: to engage in activity for enjoyment and recreation
  • antonym – work: to engage in activity involving effort or energy; opposite of playing or fooling
