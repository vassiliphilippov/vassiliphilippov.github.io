Ash'arism

noun
school of Islamic theology
Pronunciation: / æʃˈɑːrɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / æʃˈɑːrɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A school of Islamic theology founded by al-Ash'ari, emphasizing a middle path between rationalism and traditionalism.

Examples

Ash'arism in Islamic theology
He studied Ash'arism to better understand classical debates in Islamic philosophy.
Many Sunni Muslims adhere to Ash'arism as the foundation of their theological beliefs.
The spread of Ash'arism influenced Islamic thought in the medieval period.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Mu'tazilism: A rationalist school of Islamic theology, contrasted with Ash'arism
