rigid

adjective
of an airship, with a stiff structure
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
(of an airship) having a shape maintained by a stiff, unyielding framework rather than by internal gas pressure alone.

Examples

rigid airship frame
The Hindenburg was a famous rigid airship powered by hydrogen gas.
The museum displays a model of a rigid dirigible from the early twentieth century.
Unlike blimps, rigid airships contain an internal framework to maintain their shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonrigid: an airship without a rigid internal structure
  • degree lower – semi-rigid: an airship with a partial rigid framework
