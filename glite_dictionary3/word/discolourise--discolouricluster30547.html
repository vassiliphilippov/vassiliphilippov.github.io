to discolourise

verb
make lighter or whiter in colour
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkʌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌl(ə)raɪz / (BrE)
to make something lose its original colour, especially by making it lighter or whiter

Examples

to discolourise white clothes
After being left outdoors, the paint began to discolourise from exposure to sunlight.
Chlorine can discolourise your clothes if used excessively in washing.
The sun gradually discolourised the curtains over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bleach: to remove color from something, often making it white or lighter
