Oceanus

proper noun
Greek god of the surrounding river
Pronunciation: / oʊˈsiː.ə.nəs / (AmE), / əʊˈsiː.ə.nəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god personifying the immense river believed to encircle the world.

Examples

Oceanus in mythology
Oceanus and his wife, Tethys, were believed to be the parents of all rivers and seas.
In the ancient myths, Oceanus was said to encircle the world with his endless stream.
Many artists depict Oceanus as an old man with a flowing beard, symbolizing the boundless river.
