self-insurance

noun
setting aside funds to cover losses
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
The practice of setting aside funds to cover potential losses instead of purchasing insurance from an external provider.

Examples

self-insurance fund setup
By choosing self-insurance, the family created a dedicated fund for unexpected expenses.
Some companies use self-insurance to handle minor claims instead of paying for commercial insurance.
The organization moved to a self-insurance model to have greater control over risk management.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insurance: a contract in which an individual receives financial protection against losses from an insurer
