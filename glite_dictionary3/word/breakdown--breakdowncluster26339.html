breakdown

noun
failure or collapse of a system or order
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.daʊn /; / ˈbreɪkdaʊn / (BrE)
a failure or collapse of a system, relationship, or machine so that it stops working properly

Examples

breakdown of communication
A breakdown in communication between management and staff caused widespread confusion.
Negotiations ended in a breakdown after neither side could reach a compromise.
The financial crisis led to the breakdown of several major banks across the country.
A sudden breakdown in the computer system delayed the project.
Engineers worked through the night to repair the electrical breakdown.
The factory was forced to shut down due to a breakdown in the main generator.
