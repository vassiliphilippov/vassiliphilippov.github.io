antherozoid

noun
motile male gamete in certain plants
Pronunciation: / ˌænθəroʊˈzɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌanθərəʊˈzɔɪd / (BrE)
a motile male gamete in plants such as algae, ferns, or gymnosperms, capable of movement towards the egg for fertilization

Examples

antherozoid moves to egg
Botanists observed the movement of the antherozoids under a microscope during the fern's reproductive cycle.
The antherozoid swims through a thin film of water to reach the egg cell for fertilization.
Unlike pollen grains, the antherozoid must actively move toward the female gamete in many non-flowering plants.
