hadron

noun
subatomic particle affected by strong nuclear force
Pronunciation: / ˈhæˌdrɑn / (AmE), / ˈhæd.rɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle that experiences the strong nuclear force, such as a proton or neutron, and is made up of quarks

Examples

a hadron particle
Physicists discovered a new type of hadron in the results from the particle accelerator.
Protons and neutrons are both examples of hadrons found in atomic nuclei.
The experiment aims to observe how a hadron behaves under extreme energy conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – baryon: a type of hadron such as a proton or neutron, made of three quarks
  • antonym – lepton: a subatomic particle not affected by the strong force (e.g. electron)
  • synonym – meson: a type of hadron made up of a quark and an antiquark
