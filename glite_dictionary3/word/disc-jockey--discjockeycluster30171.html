to disc-jockey

verb
play and comment on music as a DJ
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsk ˌdʒɑːki / (AmE), / ˈdɪsk ˌdʒɒki / (BrE)
to play recorded music and often comment on or introduce the music for an audience, typically as a DJ.

Examples

to disc-jockey a party
Last summer, he disc-jockeyed weddings and birthday parties for extra income.
She loves to disc-jockey at local radio stations during weekend nights.
They will disc-jockey the college event, mixing tracks and engaging the crowd.
