incommunicado

adjective
unable to communicate with others
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kəˌmjunɪˈkɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / ɪn.kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkɑː.dəʊ / (BrE)
not able to communicate with others; without the means or right to communicate, especially while being detained or isolated.

Examples

held incommunicado by police
Reporters complained that the embassy staff remained incommunicado after the incident.
She was held incommunicado abroad, with neither her friends nor family able to reach her.
The suspect was kept incommunicado for several days and had no contact with his lawyer.
