Nammu

proper noun
Sumerian mother goddess of the primeval sea
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːmuː / (AmE), / ˈnæmuː / (BrE)
A goddess in Sumerian mythology who personifies the primeval sea and is regarded as the mother of the gods, heaven, and earth.

Examples

Nammu the mother goddess
In ancient Sumerian texts, Nammu is revered as the creator goddess of the primeval sea.
Scholars believe that Nammu played a vital role in early Mesopotamian cosmogony.
The myth credits Nammu with giving birth to both the heavens and the earth.
