laniard

noun
nautical rope for rigging
Pronunciation: / ˈlæn.jɚd / (AmE), / ˈlæn.jəd / (BrE)
(nautical) A line or short rope used for extending, fastening, or securing rigging or other items on a ship.

Examples

laniard for rigging
Each sailor secured the mast with a laniard as the storm approached.
He pulled the laniard tight to prevent the sail from flapping in the wind.
The old ship’s rigging included a weathered laniard that held the blocks in place.
