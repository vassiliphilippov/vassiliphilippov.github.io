superscript

adjective
written above and to the side of another character
Pronunciation: / ˈsuː.pɚˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
(of a letter, symbol, or figure) written or printed above and to one side of another character.

Examples

superscript text style
Footnote numbers are often placed in superscript form next to the main text.
The exponent in a mathematical equation is usually written as a superscript.
To denote powers, type the number as a superscript after the variable.

Related Terms

  • antonym – subscript: (of a letter, symbol, or figure) written or printed below and to one side of another character.
