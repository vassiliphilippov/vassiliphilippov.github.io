dammar

noun
resin from certain trees used in varnish
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdæmə / (BrE)
A hard, translucent resin obtained from trees of the family Dipterocarpaceae and the genus Agathis, used in varnishes and other products.

Artists sometimes use dammar as a component in varnish for oil paintings.
