lanyard

noun
nautical line for rigging on ships
Pronunciation: / ˈlæn.jɚd / (AmE), / ˈlan.jəd / (BrE)
(nautical) A short rope or line used for fastening, securing, or extending rigging on a ship.

Examples

ship lanyard rope
Each mast was reinforced by several lanyards running from different points.
He checked that every lanyard was properly tied before setting sail.
The sailor secured the boom with a lanyard before the storm hit.
