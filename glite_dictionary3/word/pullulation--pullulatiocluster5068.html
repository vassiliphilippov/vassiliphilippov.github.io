pullulation

noun
asexual reproduction by budding/growth
Pronunciation: / ˌpʌl.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌl.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A type of asexual reproduction in which a new individual develops from a growth on the surface or inside the body of the parent organism.

Examples

pullulation in yeast
Certain yeast species reproduce by pullulation, forming new cells from the parent's surface.
In biology class, we studied pullulation as a common form of asexual reproduction in hydra.
The process of pullulation can result in rapid population growth among simple organisms.
