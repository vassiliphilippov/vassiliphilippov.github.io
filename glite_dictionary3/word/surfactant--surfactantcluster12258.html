surfactant

noun
substance that reduces surface tension
Pronunciation: / sərˈfæktənt / (AmE), / sɜːˈfæktənt / (BrE)
A chemical substance that reduces the surface tension of a liquid in which it is dissolved, often used to enhance mixing or wetting properties.

Examples

surfactant in water
Doctors administered a surfactant to the premature infant to help improve lung function.
The addition of a surfactant helps the cleaning solution spread more evenly on the surface.
Without a surfactant, oil and water do not mix easily.
