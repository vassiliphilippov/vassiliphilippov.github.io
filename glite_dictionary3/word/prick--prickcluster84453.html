to prick

verb
make a small hole with something sharp
Pronunciation: / prɪk / (AmE), / prɪk / (BrE)
to make a small hole in something with a sharp point, such as a needle, thorn, or insect's stinger

Examples

to prick a finger
She pricked the balloon accidentally with her pin.
The nurse pricked my finger to get a blood sample.
When walking barefoot, he pricked his foot on a thorn.
A mosquito will usually prick its victim before drawing blood.
The cactus spines can easily prick your skin if you're not careful.
The nurse used a needle to prick my finger for a blood sample.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – bite: to wound with the teeth; nip or gnaw
  • synonym – pierce: to make a hole through something, typically with a sharp object
  • synonym – sting: to wound or pierce with a sting (as by an insect)
