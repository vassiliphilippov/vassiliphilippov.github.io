reconsideration

noun
the act of considering again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən /; / ˌriːkənˌsɪdɚˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˌsɪdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of thinking again about a decision or opinion, especially with the possibility of changing it

Examples

reconsideration of a decision
After some debate, the committee agreed to a reconsideration of the proposal.
Her reconsideration of his application led to a different outcome.
The judge denied his request for reconsideration of the verdict.
After much debate, the panel decided that a reconsideration of the policy was necessary.
She asked for a reconsideration of her application after providing new information.
The judge granted a reconsideration of the case due to the emergence of new evidence.
