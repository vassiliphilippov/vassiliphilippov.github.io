association

noun
chemical combination by weak bonding
Pronunciation: / əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A process by which molecules combine and form complexes in solution through relatively weak chemical bonds.

Examples

molecular association
Certain compounds exhibit strong association behavior only in non-polar solvents.
Hydrogen bonding plays a key role in the association of biomolecules in the cell.
In aqueous solutions, the association of acetic acid molecules can affect its properties.
