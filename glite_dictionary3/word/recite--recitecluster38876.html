to recite

verb
repeat from memory aloud
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsaɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to say or repeat something aloud from memory, especially for an audience or in a formal setting

Examples

to recite a poem
At the assembly, she recited a classic Shakespearean sonnet from memory.
Children are often asked to recite poems or multiplication tables in school.
He stood confidently and recited the national anthem without hesitation.
At the competition, each participant was asked to recite a passage from Shakespeare.
He can recite all fifty states in alphabetical order.
She stood in front of the class and began to recite the poem.
Each student must recite a passage from Shakespeare by heart.
He practiced every day to recite his lines perfectly at the competition.
She was asked to recite the poem in front of the entire class.

Related Terms

  • synonym – declaim: to speak aloud in a formal, theatrical, or rhetorical way
  • antonym – improvise: to perform without preparation or memory
  • degree lower – memorize: to commit to memory; to learn by heart
  • synonym – read: to look at and comprehend the meaning of written or printed matter
