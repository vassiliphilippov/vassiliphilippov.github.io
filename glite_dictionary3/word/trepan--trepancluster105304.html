to trepan

verb
cut a hole with a surgical instrument
Pronunciation: / trɪˈpæn / (AmE), / trɪˈpan / (BrE)
to cut a hole in something, especially the skull, using a surgical instrument called a trepan

Examples

to trepan a skull
In ancient medicine, doctors would sometimes trepan to treat head injuries.
Modern neurosurgery rarely requires doctors to trepan the bone except in severe cases.
The surgeon had to trepan the patient's skull to relieve the pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trephine: to remove a circular section of tissue or bone, especially the skull, with a similar instrument
