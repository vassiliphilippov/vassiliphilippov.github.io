desalination

noun
removal of salt from water
Pronunciation: / diˌsælɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsælɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing salt and other minerals from seawater or brackish water to make it suitable for drinking or irrigation

Examples

desalination plant
Desalination plants provide fresh water to communities with limited access to natural sources.
Many desert countries rely on desalination to meet their growing water needs.
The cost of desalination has decreased with new advancements in technology.
