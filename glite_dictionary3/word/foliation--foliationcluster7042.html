foliation

noun
process of forming leaves
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Botany) The process by which leaves form and develop on a plant.

Examples

foliation of new leaves
Botanists study foliation patterns to understand the growth cycle of different species.
Early foliation can leave plants vulnerable to late frosts.
The foliation of the plant occurs rapidly in spring as the weather warms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – defoliation: The removal or loss of leaves from a plant
  • synonym – leafing: The production of leaves by a plant
