to binge

verb
watch large amounts of media at once
Pronunciation: / bɪndʒ / (AmE), / bɪndʒ / (BrE)
to consume something (such as food, drink, or media) excessively in a short period

Examples

to binge on snacks
She loves to binge on documentaries over the weekend.
They spent Saturday binging all the Star Wars movies back to back.
We decided to binge the entire season of the show in one night.
After a stressful week at work, he would sometimes binge on junk food.
During the holidays, people often binge more than they realize.
She promised herself not to binge on sweets this weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym – binge-watch: to watch multiple episodes of a TV show in succession
  • antonym – fast: to abstain from all or some kinds of food or drink
  • antonym – graze: to consume content slowly and sporadically
  • synonym – overeat: to eat more food than necessary or healthy
