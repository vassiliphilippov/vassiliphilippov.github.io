unheralded

adjective
without warning or prior notice
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhɛrəldɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɛr.əldɪd / (BrE)
not announced, predicted, or expected; happening without warning or prior notice.

Examples

unheralded arrival
An unheralded guest arrived at the party, catching everyone off guard.
News of the company's merger was completely unheralded by the media.
The team made an unheralded entrance, surprising even their supporters.
