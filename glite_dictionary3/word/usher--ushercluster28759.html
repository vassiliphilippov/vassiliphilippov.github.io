to usher

verb
escort or guide to seats
Pronunciation: / ˈʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə / (BrE)
to escort or guide someone to their seat or designated place, especially in a theater or public event

Examples

to usher guests inside
He smiled politely as he ushered the audience into the theater.
She ushered us quietly into the auditorium just as the performance began.
The attendant ushered the guests to their reserved seats in the front row.

Related Terms

  • synonym – escort: to accompany someone to a place
  • synonym – lead: to show someone the way
