crosstown

adjective
going across a town or city
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌtaʊn / (BrE)
(of a route or service) going from one side of a town or city to the other, rather than toward the center

Examples

crosstown bus route
A new crosstown bike path has made it easier for cyclists to avoid main streets.
She takes a crosstown subway every day to reach her office on the other side.
The crosstown bus was delayed due to heavy traffic this morning.
He waited at the corner for the crosstown bus to take him to the other side of the city.
Morning crosstown traffic can be much heavier than the traffic heading downtown.
She took a crosstown route to avoid the busy city center.

Related Terms

  • antonym – downtown: in or toward the central part of a city, especially its commercial area
  • antonym – uptown: toward or in a residential area of a city, especially away from the business district
