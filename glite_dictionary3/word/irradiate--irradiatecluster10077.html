to irradiate

verb
shine or cast light on
Pronunciation: / ɪˈreɪdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪdɪeɪt / (BrE)
to shine light on something; to cast rays of light upon

Examples

to irradiate a room
A single spotlight irradiated the painting, drawing everyone's attention to its vibrant colors.
Colored stage lights irradiated the dancers as they performed their final routine.
The morning sun began to irradiate the mountaintop, turning the snow a soft gold.
