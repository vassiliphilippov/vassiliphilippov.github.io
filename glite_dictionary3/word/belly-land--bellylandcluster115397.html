to belly-land

verb
land an aircraft without landing gear
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛli lænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛli lænd / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to land on the underside without using the landing gear, usually as an emergency measure.

Examples

to belly-land a plane
In rare emergencies, pilots may have to belly-land to avoid a more serious crash.
Rescue teams arrived quickly after the small aircraft belly-landed on the grass runway.
The pilot had no choice but to belly-land the plane after the landing gear failed.

  • synonym – crash-land: land forcibly due to emergency, not using standard methods
