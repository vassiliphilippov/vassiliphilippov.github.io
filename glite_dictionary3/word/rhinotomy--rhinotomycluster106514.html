rhinotomy

noun
surgical incision of the nose
Pronunciation: / raɪˈnɑːtəmi / (AmE), / raɪˈnɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an incision is made in the nose to drain accumulated pus

Examples

rhinotomy surgery procedure
Advancements in medical tools have made the rhinotomy procedure more precise and less invasive.
After the diagnosis of a nasal abscess, the surgeon performed a rhinotomy to relieve the pressure.
The patient's recovery after rhinotomy was swift due to early intervention and postoperative care.
