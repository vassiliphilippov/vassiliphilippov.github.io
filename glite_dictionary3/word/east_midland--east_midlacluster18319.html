East Midland

noun
historical Middle English dialect
Pronunciation: / ist ˈmɪdlənd / (AmE), / iːst ˈmɪdlənd / (BrE)
the dialect of Middle English, originating in the East Midlands of England, which replaced West Saxon as the literary language and later developed into Modern English.
