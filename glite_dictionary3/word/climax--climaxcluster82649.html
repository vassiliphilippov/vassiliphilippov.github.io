climax

noun
the highest point or peak of something
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪˌmæks /; / ˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.mæks /; / ˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
the most intense, exciting, or important point in a process, event, story, or career; the peak or culmination

Examples

climax of the story
Her performance at the finals was the climax of her gymnastics career.
The negotiations came to a climax late last night after hours of discussion.
The novel reaches its climax when the mystery is finally revealed.
After a long build-up, the climax of the movie was both surprising and satisfying.
As the play reached its climax, the audience sat in complete silence.
The thrilling climax of the novel left the readers breathless.
After years of hard work, winning the award was the climax of her career.
The climax of the film occurs when the hero finally confronts the villain.
The audience waited in suspense for the climax of the concert.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anticlimax: a disappointing or less dramatic conclusion following a build-up
  • synonym – culmination: the highest or climactic point of something
  • degree higher – denouement: the final resolution of the plot in a story or play
  • antonym – nadir: the lowest point or opposite of climax
  • synonym – peak: the highest or most important point
