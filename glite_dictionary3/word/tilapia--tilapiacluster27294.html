Tilapia

noun
a genus of freshwater fish; farmed food fish
Pronunciation: / tɪˈleɪpiə / (AmE), / tɪˈleɪpɪə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cichlidae, widely farmed and used as food, especially in Africa and Asia.

Examples

grilled tilapia fillet
Many fish farmers raise Tilapia because it grows quickly and is easy to rear in ponds.
Scientists are studying the genetics of Tilapia to improve disease resistance in aquaculture.
The diet of wild Tilapia typically consists of algae and plant matter.

Related Terms
