systematically

adverb
in a methodical or ordered way
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪstəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is done according to a fixed plan or system; methodically or consistently.

Examples

to systematically organize files
She systematically organized her files to make retrieval easier.
The company has been systematically updating its software to improve security.
The researchers systematically collected data from all participants over several months.

Related Terms

  • synonym – methodically: in an orderly or systematic way
