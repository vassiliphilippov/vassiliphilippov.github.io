Jeroboam I

proper noun
first king of northern Israel in the Old Testament
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛrəˈboʊəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛrəˈbəʊəm / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) the first king of the northern kingdom of Israel, known for leading the Israelites into sin; reigned in the 10th century BC.

Examples

Jeroboam I ruled Israel
Jeroboam I became the first king of the northern kingdom after the division of Israel.
According to the Bible, Jeroboam I established new religious practices at Bethel and Dan.
Historians debate the actual actions and motives of Jeroboam I as described in ancient texts.
