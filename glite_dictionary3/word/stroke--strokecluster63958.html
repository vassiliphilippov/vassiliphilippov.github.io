to stroke

verb
to row at a steady rate (rowing term)
Pronunciation: / stroʊk / (AmE), / strəʊk / (BrE)
to row a boat at a particular rate, especially as the person setting the pace for a crew

Examples

to stroke a boat
He has been stroking at a pace that challenges everyone else in the boat.
She stroked steadily, keeping the rhythm for the rest of the crew.
The experienced rower volunteered to stroke for the team during the regatta.
