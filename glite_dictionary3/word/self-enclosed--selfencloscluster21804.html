self-enclosed

adjective
enclosed or confined by oneself
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ɪnˈkloʊzd / (AmE), / sɛlf ɪnˈkləʊzd / (BrE)
Enclosed or confined by one's own action or nature; separated from outside influence by choice or internal characteristics.

Examples

self-enclosed world
His writing often explores self-enclosed worlds cut off from reality.
The artist created a self-enclosed space in her studio to focus on her work.
The monastery was a self-enclosed community, rarely interacting with the outside world.
