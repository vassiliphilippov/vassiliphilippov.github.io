to dramatise

verb
adapt for drama or performance
Pronunciation: / ˈdræməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdræmətaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt a story, event, or situation so that it can be presented as a play, film, or television drama

Examples

to dramatise a story
Many authors struggle to dramatise historical facts without distorting the truth.
She is planning to dramatise the events of the expedition as a stage play.
The director wanted to dramatise the novel for a television series.
