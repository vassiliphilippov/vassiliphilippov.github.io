fibrinogen

noun
blood protein involved in clotting
Pronunciation: / faɪˈbrɪnədʒən / (AmE), / fɪˈbrɪnədʒən / (BrE)
a soluble protein present in blood plasma that is converted into insoluble fibrin during blood clotting

Examples

fibrinogen blood test
During blood tests, fibrinogen is one of the key proteins examined to assess clotting ability.
The doctor explained that low fibrinogen levels could cause excessive bleeding.
When fibrinogen converts to fibrin, it helps stop bleeding by forming a stable clot.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fibrin: an insoluble protein formed from fibrinogen during blood clotting
