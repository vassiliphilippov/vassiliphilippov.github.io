stercobilinogen

noun
intestinal compound from bilirubin breakdown
Pronunciation: / stɝːkoʊbɪˈlɪnədʒən / (AmE), / stɜːkəʊbɪˈlɪnədʒən / (BrE)
a colorless compound formed in the intestine by the breakdown of bilirubin, which can be further converted into urobilins or excreted in feces, bile, or urine

Examples

stercobilinogen in feces
A deficiency in bile secretion may reduce the amount of stercobilinogen produced in the intestines.
Elevated levels of stercobilinogen in the urine can indicate liver dysfunction.
The presence of stercobilinogen is essential for the brown color of feces.
