kHz

noun
unit of frequency (kilohertz)
Pronunciation: / kiː eɪtʃ z / (AmE), / keɪ ʔ eɪtʃ zɛd / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one thousand hertz or cycles per second

Examples

signal at kHz
The crystal oscillator produces a stable signal at exactly 32 kHz.
The radio station broadcasts at 101.7 kHz.
Ultrasound machines often operate at frequencies above 20 kHz.
