to field

verb
select/send out team or player for a game
Pronunciation: / fild /; / fiːld / (AmE), / fiːld / (BrE)
to send out a team or player to take part in a game, or to play as a fielder in sports like cricket or baseball

Examples

to field a team
Despite several injuries, the club managed to field eleven players for the start of the season.
England will field a strong team in the upcoming World Cup.
The coach decided to field his best players for the championship match.
She loves to field in the outfield because of the excitement of catching fly balls.
The coach asked him to field at third base during today’s game.
Their team will field first and try to keep the opponents’ score low.
He enjoys fielding more than batting when playing with his friends.
She skillfully fielded the ball at shortstop and threw it to first base.
The cricketer fielded the shot near the boundary to prevent a four.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bat: to try to hit the ball with the bat in cricket or baseball
  • antonym – bowl: to deliver the ball in cricket
  • synonym – deploy: to send out people or equipment for a specific purpose
  • antonym – pitch: to throw the ball towards the batter in baseball or cricket
