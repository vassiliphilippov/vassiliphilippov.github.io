Pachyrhizus erosus

noun
Central American plant with edible roots (jícama)
Pronunciation: / ˌpækɪˈraɪzəs ˈɛroʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌpækɪˈraɪzəs ˈɛrəsəs / (BrE)
A tropical American climbing plant with large edible tuberous roots, commonly known as jícama; its roots are eaten raw or cooked, and its pods are a source of oils and rotenone.

Examples

Pachyrhizus erosus root salad
Chefs recommend peeling Pachyrhizus erosus before eating it raw in salads or cooking it in stir-fries.
Farmers often grow Pachyrhizus erosus both for its edible tubers and as a source of natural insecticides.
The large, crisp roots of Pachyrhizus erosus are a popular snack in Central America.
