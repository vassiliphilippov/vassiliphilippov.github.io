dehumanisation

noun
making people seem less human
Pronunciation: / diˌhjuːmənɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌhjuːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or act of depriving people of positive human qualities, making them seem less human.

Examples

dehumanisation of people
Many critics argue that technology can contribute to the dehumanisation of personal relationships.
The dehumanisation of prisoners during wartime has been widely condemned by human rights organizations.
The film powerfully explores the dehumanisation experienced by marginalized communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dehumanize: to deprive someone of human qualities
  • antonym – humanisation: the process of making something more humane or civilized
