hypospadias

noun
abnormal urethral opening in males
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˈspeɪ.di.əs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəˈspeɪ.dɪ.as / (BrE)
A congenital condition in males in which the opening of the urethra is located on the underside of the penis instead of at its tip.

Examples

hypospadias in boys
Parents were reassured that hypospadias is a treatable condition.
Surgical correction is often recommended for boys born with hypospadias.
The doctor diagnosed the infant with hypospadias during a routine examination.
