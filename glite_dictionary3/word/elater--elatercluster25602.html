elater

noun
a beetle of the Elateridae family, click beetle
Pronunciation: / ɪˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
any of various beetles of the family Elateridae, known for their clicking mechanism, commonly called click beetles.

Examples

elater beetle
In the woods, we found an elater flipping onto its feet using its unique mechanism.
Many crops are threatened by elater larvae, which are also known as wireworms.
The elater can right itself by snapping its body with an audible click.
