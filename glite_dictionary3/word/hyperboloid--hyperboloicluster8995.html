hyperboloid

noun
three-dimensional surface formed from a hyperbola
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɜːrbəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜːbəlɔɪd / (BrE)
A three-dimensional surface formed by rotating a hyperbola around its main axis, resulting in a shape that curves inward and outward.

Examples

hyperboloid shape in math
A hyperboloid surface appears when a hyperbola is rotated about its principal axis.
The hyperboloid can be found in many advanced architectural designs due to its unique properties.
The cooling towers of nuclear power plants are often shaped like a hyperboloid for structural efficiency.
