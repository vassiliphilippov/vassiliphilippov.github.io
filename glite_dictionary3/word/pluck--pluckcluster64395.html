pluck

noun
act of pulling and releasing a taut cord
Pronunciation: / plʌk / (AmE), / plʌk / (BrE)
the act of quickly pulling and releasing a stretched cord, string, or similar object, often to produce a sound

Examples

pluck of a string
Each pluck of the guitar string sent a clear note ringing through the room.
The harpist's gentle pluck of the strings produced a beautiful melody.
With a swift pluck, he released the arrow from the bowstring.
