upwards

adverb
towards a higher position or amount
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
towards a higher position, level, or amount; moving or directed from lower to higher, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

move upwards in price
Temperatures are expected to rise upwards of 30 degrees Celsius over the weekend.
The helium balloon drifted upwards into the sky until it disappeared from view.
With each promotion, she advanced upwards in the company hierarchy.
