thirdhand

adjective, adverb
by indirect means, twice removed
Pronunciation: / ˈθɝdˌhænd /; / ˈθɜrdˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈθɜːdˌhænd / (BrE)
describing information or knowledge that is received indirectly, through two other people or sources

Examples

thirdhand information source
Much of what we know about that time comes thirdhand, not from direct witnesses.
She only heard about the accident thirdhand through a friend of a friend.
The rumor was passed along thirdhand before it ever reached the manager.
His opinions were formed from thirdhand accounts rather than personal experience.
Much of the news we get about distant countries is thirdhand information.
She only heard the story thirdhand and wasn't sure how much of it was true.
