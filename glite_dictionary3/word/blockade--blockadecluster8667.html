blockade

noun
obstruction preventing access or progress
Pronunciation: / blɑˈkeɪd / (AmE), / blɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
an act of sealing off a place to stop people or goods from entering or leaving, often using force or barriers

Examples

blockade of a port
A blockade of the main road caused significant delays for commuters.
Protesters formed a human blockade in front of the entrance to the building.
The navy established a blockade to prevent supplies from reaching the enemy port.
During the war, a strict blockade was enforced to prevent trade with the outside world.
The government announced that the blockade would remain until all demands were met.
The navy established a blockade of the enemy port, cutting off supplies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embargo: official ban on trade or other commercial activity
  • synonym – siege: military operation to surround and cut off a place
