to face-harden

verb
to harden steel's surface by adding carbon
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪs ˌhɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs ˌhɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to harden the surface of steel by adding carbon, making it more resistant to wear while keeping the inner metal softer

Examples

to face-harden steel parts
Engineers often face-harden metal components that are exposed to significant friction.
The steel plates were face-hardened to increase their durability against heavy use.
They are considering face-hardening the gears to extend their operational life.
