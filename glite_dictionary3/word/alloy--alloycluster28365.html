to alloy

verb
reduce metal purity by mixing with base metal
Pronunciation: / əˈlɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɔɪ / (BrE)
to reduce the value or purity of (a precious metal) by mixing it with a less valuable metal; to debase.

Examples

to alloy pure gold
Dishonest merchants would sometimes alloy gold coins with copper to increase their profits.
The quality of the silver was alloyed by adding cheaper metals during production.
To reduce costs, the jeweler decided to alloy the platinum with a small amount of nickel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – debase: to reduce in quality or value
