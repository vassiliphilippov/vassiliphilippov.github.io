masochist

noun
person who enjoys pain or punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈmæ.sə.kɪst / (AmE), / ˈmas.ə.kɪst / (BrE)
a person who derives pleasure, especially sexual gratification, from their own pain or humiliation

Examples

self-described masochist
He admitted to being a masochist during an honest conversation with his therapist.
Some people mistakenly believe a masochist always enjoys any form of discomfort, which is not true.
The main character in the novel is portrayed as a masochist seeking punishment for his guilt.
