remission

noun
formal absolution or forgiveness (esp. religious)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of absolving or forgiving a sin, debt, or offense, especially as formally declared by a priest in a religious context

Examples

remission of sins
Many people sought remission through confession and prayer.
The doctrine teaches that remission of guilt is possible for all who repent.
The priest granted remission of sins during the sacrament of penance.
