epinephrine

noun
hormone and medication for stress response and allergies
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpəˈnɛfrɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈnɛfrɪn / (BrE)
a hormone and medication secreted by the adrenal glands that increases heart rate, blood pressure, and prepares muscles for exertion, especially during stress; also used medically to treat allergic reactions and cardiac arrest.

Examples

epinephrine injection
An epinephrine auto-injector can be lifesaving for people with serious allergies.
During moments of intense stress, the body releases epinephrine to prepare for action.
The doctor administered epinephrine to quickly treat the patient's severe allergic reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adrenaline: another name for epinephrine, chiefly British usage
