reagent

noun
substance used in chemical reactions
Pronunciation: / riˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / riˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a substance or compound that is added to a system to cause a chemical reaction or to see if a reaction occurs

Examples

reagent in a test
After mixing the sample with the reagent, a color change indicated the presence of protein.
The laboratory technician added the reagent to the solution to test for glucose.
This reagent is highly sensitive to light and should be stored in a dark place.

Related Terms

  • antonym – product: a substance produced during a chemical reaction
  • synonym – reactant: a substance that takes part in and undergoes change during a reaction
