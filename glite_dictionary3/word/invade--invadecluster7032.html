to invade

verb
spread rapidly in large numbers; infest
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪd / (BrE)
to enter and spread in a place or body in large numbers, often causing harm (used for animals, plants, or diseases)

Examples

to invade the garden
After a few years, non-native insects started to invade the garden and damage the crops.
The crabgrass began to invade the entire lawn by midsummer.
Unwanted bacteria can quickly invade the open wound if not properly cleaned.
As the virus invades the body, it begins to attack healthy cells.
Several invasive species invade local habitats, threatening native plants and animals.
The cancer had invaded her lungs, making it difficult for her to breathe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attack: to take aggressive action against; to assault
  • synonym – infest: to spread or be present in large numbers, typically causing damage
  • antonym – retreat: to move back or withdraw, especially from danger
