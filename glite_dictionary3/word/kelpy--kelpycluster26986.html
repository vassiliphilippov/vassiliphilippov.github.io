kelpy

noun
mythical water spirit (Scottish folklore)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛl.pi / (AmE), / ˈkɛl.pi / (BrE)
(Scottish folklore) a water spirit, typically taking the form of a horse, believed to drown people who ride it.

Examples

a kelpy in a river
In the old tales, a kelpy would appear near rivers to lure travelers onto its back.

The villagers whispered about a kelpy haunting the loch after dark.
