incoherency

noun
lack of clarity or logical connection
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkoʊˈhɪrənsi /; / ɪnˈkoʊhərəntsi / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈhɪərənsi /; / ɪnˈkəʊhərəntsi / (BrE)
the quality of lacking clarity, logical connection, or organization in thought, speech, or writing; or speech, writing, or ideas that are difficult to understand because they are not clear or logical

Examples

avoid incoherency in writing
A good essay should avoid incoherency and present its ideas logically.
Her argument was full of incoherency, making it hard to follow her point.
The incoherency in his explanation left everyone confused.
His drunken mumblings were pure incoherency by the end of the night.
The professor's lecture descended into incoherency, leaving the students confused and frustrated.
Trying to read his scribbled notes resulted in nothing but incoherency.

Related Terms

  • antonym – coherency: the quality of being logical, consistent, and orderly
  • synonym – nonsense: spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
