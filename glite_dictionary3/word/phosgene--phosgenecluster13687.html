phosgene

noun
a toxic, colorless gas used in industry and warfare
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊsˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈfɒsˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless gas with a smell like freshly cut hay, used in chemical manufacturing and formerly as a chemical weapon.

Examples

toxic phosgene gas
During World War I, phosgene was one of the most lethal chemical weapons used on the battlefield.
Industrial workers must take strict safety precautions when handling phosgene in chemical plants.
The accidental release of phosgene from the factory led to an evacuation of the surrounding area.
