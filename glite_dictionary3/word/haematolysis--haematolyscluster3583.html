haematolysis

noun
breakdown of red blood cells
Pronunciation: / hiːˌmætəˈlaɪsɪs / (AmE), / hɪˌmætəˈlɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
The destruction or breakdown of red blood cells, resulting in the release of hemoglobin into the surrounding fluid.

Examples

acute haematolysis episode
Acute haematolysis can lead to a sudden drop in red blood cell count.
Doctors closely monitored the patient for signs of haematolysis after the transfusion.
The laboratory test confirmed the presence of haematolysis in the blood sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hemolysis: the destruction of red blood cells with release of hemoglobin (US spelling)
