disgrace

noun
state of dishonor or shame
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡreɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡreɪs / (BrE)
The loss of respect, honor, or esteem; a state of shame or dishonor.

Examples

in disgrace after scandal
After the scandal, the politician lived in disgrace for years.
Cheating in the final exam brought disgrace upon the entire team.
He was dismissed from the company in utter disgrace.

Related Terms

  • antonym – honor: high respect or esteem
  • synonym – shame: a painful feeling of humiliation or distress caused by awareness of wrong or foolish behavior
