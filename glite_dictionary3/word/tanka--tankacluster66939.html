tanka

noun
form of Japanese five-line poetry
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈtæŋ.kə / (BrE)
a traditional Japanese poem consisting of five lines, with a syllable pattern of 5-7-5-7-7

Examples

write a tanka poem
In our workshop, we learned how to write a traditional tanka.
Many Japanese poets express their emotions through the brief form of the tanka.
She composed a tanka describing the peaceful morning by the lake.
