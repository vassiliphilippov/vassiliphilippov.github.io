saucepot

noun
deep pot for stewing or boiling
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔsˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsˌpɒt / (BrE)
A deep cooking pot with handles on either side and a tight-fitting lid, used for stewing or boiling food.

Examples

large saucepot lid
After washing the rice, he cooked it in a large saucepot with a tight-fitting lid.
She placed the vegetables into the saucepot to simmer for an hour.
The recipe recommends using a heavy-bottomed saucepot for best results.
