to dispel

verb
make something disappear or scatter
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɛl / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛl / (BrE)
to make something, such as a feeling, idea, or group, disappear or go away

Examples

to dispel a fear
She tried to dispel her worries before the important meeting.
The leader’s calm words began to dispel the fear among the crowd.
The morning sunlight helped to dispel the thick fog surrounding the city.
A good night's sleep can often dispel feelings of anxiety.
She tried to dispel the rumors with a public statement.
The new evidence helped to dispel many of their fears.

Related Terms

  • synonym – banish: send something or someone away, especially officially or forcefully
  • synonym – disperse: to scatter or spread things or people over a wide area
  • antonym – gather: to come together or collect into one group or place
  • synonym – scatter: to throw or separate things in different directions
  • antonym – summon: call for the presence of; to bring to mind or into being
