moralist

noun
person insisting on strict moral rules
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who demands strict conformity to established rules or moral standards

Examples

strict moralist critic
As a self-proclaimed moralist, he criticized anyone he saw breaking social conventions.
Many regarded her as a moralist because of her unwavering insistence on traditional values.
The new principal was a strict moralist who enforced every rule without exception.

Related Terms

  • antonym – libertine: a person who rejects accepted morals, especially in sexual matters
  • synonym – puritan: a person with strict moral beliefs, especially about pleasure
