stigmatism

noun
optical condition where rays converge at one point
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪɡ.məˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡ.mə.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
(Optics) A condition of an optical system, such as a lens, in which light rays from a single point converge at a single focal point, resulting in a clear image.

Examples

stigmatism in a lens
A high-quality camera lens is engineered to minimize distortion and maintain stigmatism.
Optical engineers work to ensure stigmatism so that all rays focus precisely on the sensor.
The new microscope design achieved perfect stigmatism for improved image clarity.

  • antonym – astigmatism: defect in optical systems where rays do not converge to a single focal point
