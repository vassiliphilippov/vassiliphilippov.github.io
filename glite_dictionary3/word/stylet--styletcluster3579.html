stylet

noun
small needlelike appendage (e.g., animal feeding organ)
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlət / (BrE)
a small, needlelike appendage, especially the feeding organ of certain animals such as tardigrades or insects

Examples

insect stylet
Some parasitic nematodes have a specialized stylet for feeding on host tissues.
The tardigrade uses its stylet to pierce plant cells and suck out their contents.
Under the microscope, the scientist observed the insect's hollow stylet penetrating a leaf.
