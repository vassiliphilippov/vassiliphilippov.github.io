fingerroot

noun
European plant; source of digitalis, poisonous to livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪŋɡɚˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌruːt / (BrE)
A tall, leafy European biennial or perennial plant with large pink-purple tubular flowers; its leaves are used to produce digitalis and are poisonous to livestock.

Examples

fingerroot plant leaves
Farmers need to keep livestock away from fingerroot because it is highly poisonous.
The leaves of fingerroot can be processed to obtain digitalis, a medication for heart conditions.
The spectacular pink-purple flowers of fingerroot bloom in late spring in woodlands.
