apoplexy

noun
sudden loss of consciousness from brain blood vessel issue
Pronunciation: / ˈæp.əˌplɛk.si / (AmE), / ˈap.ə.plek.si / (BrE)
A sudden loss of consciousness, often caused by the rupture or blockage of a blood vessel in the brain, leading to a lack of oxygen; an old term for stroke.

Examples

apoplexy attack
Her sudden collapse was first diagnosed as apoplexy by the attending physician.
In the nineteenth century, many sudden deaths were attributed to apoplexy.
The patient suffered apoplexy after the vessel in his brain ruptured.

  • synonym – stroke: sudden interruption of blood supply to the brain
