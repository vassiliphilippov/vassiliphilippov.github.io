transformation

noun
genetic modification by DNA uptake
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænsfəˈrmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) The modification of a cell or bacterium by the uptake and incorporation of external DNA.

Examples

gene transformation
Gene transformation is commonly used in biotechnology to produce genetically modified organisms.
Natural transformation allows bacteria to rapidly acquire new traits from their environment.
The scientist induced transformation in the bacterial culture by adding a plasmid containing an antibiotic resistance gene.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conjugation: direct transfer of DNA between bacteria
  • synonym – transduction: genetic transfer of DNA by a virus
