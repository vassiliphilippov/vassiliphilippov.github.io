disjoined

adjective
separated or disconnected
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈdʒɔɪnd / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪnd / (BrE)
separated or disconnected; having had a connection undone.

Examples

disjoined train cars
Following the accident, the train cars became disjoined from one another.
The committee was disjoined after several members resigned in protest.
The legal agreement is nullified if the assets are disjoined prior to completion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disjointed: lacking a coherent sequence or connection
  • antonym – joined: connected or combined
