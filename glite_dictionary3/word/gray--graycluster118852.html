gray

adjective
having gray or white hair from age
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a color between black and white; having an achromatic color of any lightness intermediate between black and white, or showing the effects of age by having gray or white hair

Examples

gray hair
His once dark beard had grown gray over the years.
She noticed her father was looking more gray than she remembered.
The old dog, gray around the muzzle, slept by the fire.
She chose a gray sweater because it matched almost everything in her wardrobe.
The sky was covered with gray clouds all afternoon.
Wisps of gray hair peeked out from under the old man's hat.
