sweeper

noun
person employed to sweep floors or streets
Pronunciation: / ˈswipər / (AmE), / ˈswiːpə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to sweep floors, streets, or similar areas to keep them clean

Examples

street sweeper

The sweeper started early in the morning to clear the leaves from the street.
They hired an extra sweeper to help maintain the cleanliness of the train station.
