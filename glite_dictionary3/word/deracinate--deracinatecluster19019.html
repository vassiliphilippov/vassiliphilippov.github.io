to deracinate

verb
forcibly remove from homeland
Pronunciation: / dɪˈræsəˌneɪt /; / dɪˈræsɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈræsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to uproot or remove completely from a native place, environment, or culture (often used about people or things being forced to leave their original home)

Examples

Generations have struggled to rebuild their identity after being deracinated by colonial expansion.
