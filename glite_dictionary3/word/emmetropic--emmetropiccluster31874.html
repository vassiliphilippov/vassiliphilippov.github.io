emmetropic

adjective
having normal vision (eye)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmɪˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛmɪˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of the eye) having normal refractive power so that visual images are focused clearly on the retina without the need for corrective lenses.

Examples

emmetropic eye vision
At her eye examination, the doctor confirmed that her vision was emmetropic and did not require correction.
Children are often emmetropic until their eyes begin to change in adolescence.
Unlike his myopic brother, John remained emmetropic throughout his adult life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hypermetropic: having defective vision so that close objects appear blurred; farsighted
  • antonym – myopic: having defective vision so that distant objects appear blurred; nearsighted
