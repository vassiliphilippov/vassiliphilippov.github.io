to betray

verb
deliver to an enemy by treachery
Pronunciation: / bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to deliver someone or something to an enemy by acting treacherously or disloyally

Examples

betray the country
During the war, several officers betrayed confidential information to the enemy.
He feared that someone in the group would betray them if they were captured.
She was shocked when her friend betrayed her to the occupying forces.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be loyal: to remain faithful and not betray someone
  • synonym – sell out: to betray someone or something for personal gain
