vulgarization

noun
making something accessible to the public
Pronunciation: / ˌvʌlɡərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more accessible or appealing to the general public, often by simplifying or popularizing it

Examples

vulgarization of science
Some critics argue that too much vulgarization reduces the accuracy of the original material.
The vulgarization of scientific ideas can help non-experts appreciate complex discoveries.
The museum's exhibits underwent vulgarization to attract a larger audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – popularization: the act of making something popular or easily understood by many people
