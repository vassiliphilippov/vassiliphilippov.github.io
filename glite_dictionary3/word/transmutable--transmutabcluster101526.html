transmutable

adjective
capable of being changed in substance
Pronunciation: / trænsˈmjutəbəl / (AmE), / tranzˈmjuːtəbl / (BrE)
capable of being changed in form, nature, or substance, especially in a way resembling alchemy.

Examples

transmutable metals
In science fiction, ordinary metals are often portrayed as transmutable into precious substances.
Some elements are theoretically transmutable under certain nuclear reactions.
The philosopher believed that negative emotions are transmutable into wisdom through experience.
