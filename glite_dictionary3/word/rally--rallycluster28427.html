to rally

verb
to recover after a decline
Pronunciation: / ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
to recover or improve after a decline or weakness; to return to a stronger or better condition

Examples

to rally after illness
After a brief illness, he began to rally and regain his strength.
The company's shares rallied after the announcement of a new product.
The team managed to rally in the second half and win the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deteriorate: to become progressively worse
  • synonym – recover: to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
