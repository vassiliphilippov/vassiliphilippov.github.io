beefwood

noun
reddish tropical hardwood (botany/timber)
Pronunciation: / ˈbifˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbiːfwʊd / (BrE)
A heavy, hard, reddish wood from certain tropical trees of the Casuarinaceae or Proteaceae families, sometimes used in cabinetwork.

Examples

beefwood cabinet wood
Because of its hardness, beefwood can be challenging to carve.
Several species known as beefwood are valued for their dense timber.
The furniture maker preferred beefwood for its durability and rich color.
