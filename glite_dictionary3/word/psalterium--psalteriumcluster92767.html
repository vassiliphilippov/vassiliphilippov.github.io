psalterium

noun
third stomach compartment of ruminant
Pronunciation: / sɔlˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / sɒlˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
the third compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals, located between the reticulum and the abomasum

Examples

cow's psalterium
A blockage in the psalterium can severely affect the cow's digestion.
During the dissection, we identified the reticulum, psalterium, and abomasum as distinct chambers.
The psalterium is responsible for further filtering ingested material before it enters the abomasum.
