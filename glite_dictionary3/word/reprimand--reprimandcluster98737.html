reprimand

noun
formal criticism or rebuke
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛprɪˌmænd / (AmE), / ˈrɛprɪˌmɑːnd / (BrE)
a formal expression of disapproval or criticism, especially by someone in authority

Examples

official reprimand
After the incident, the student received a formal reprimand from the principal.
Her mistake resulted in a written reprimand placed in her personnel file.
The manager gave him a strong reprimand for being late to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – commendation: praise or official approval
  • synonym – rebuke: an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
