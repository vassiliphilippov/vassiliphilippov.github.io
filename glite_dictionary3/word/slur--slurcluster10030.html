to slur

verb
play or sing notes smoothly (legato)
Pronunciation: / slɝː / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
(of a musician) to play or sing notes smoothly and connectedly, without a break between them.

Examples

to slur a melody
During the rehearsal, the conductor asked the flute section to slur the fast passages.
If you slur those notes, the melody will sound much more fluid and expressive.
The violinist was careful to slur two notes in each phrase for a smoother transition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – legato: In music, playing or singing notes smoothly and connectedly
  • antonym – staccato: In music, playing or singing notes sharply separated
