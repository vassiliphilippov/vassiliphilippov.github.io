depolarisation

noun
loss of electrical polarization
Pronunciation: / diˌpɔl.ə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / diˌpɔː.lə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a cell, tissue, or object loses its polarization, especially a reduction in the difference of electrical charge across the membrane of a cell

Examples

nerve cell depolarisation
During the action potential, depolarisation of the neuron membrane occurs rapidly.
The ECG showed evidence of abnormal depolarisation in the cardiac tissue.
Voltage-gated channels open in response to depolarisation, allowing ions to flow across the membrane.

Related Terms

  • antonym – polarisation: the process of causing something to acquire polarity
