cocuswood

noun
wood used for musical instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊkəsˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəsˌwʊd / (BrE)
The wood of the granadilla tree (Dalbergia spp.), used especially for making musical instruments such as clarinets and flutes.

Examples

cocuswood clarinet body
Many high-quality clarinets are made from cocuswood because of its density and rich tone.
Overharvesting of cocuswood has made it increasingly rare and valuable.
The craftsman selected a piece of cocuswood for the new flute body.
