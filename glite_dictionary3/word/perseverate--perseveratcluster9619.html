to perseverate

verb
repeat a response after stimulus stops
Pronunciation: / pərˈsɛvəˌreɪt / (AmE), / pəˈsɛvəreɪt / (BrE)
To repeat a particular response, word, or action insistently or redundantly, especially after the original stimulus has ceased, typically as a result of a psychological condition.

Examples

to perseverate the same word
After the distraction, the child began to perseverate the same word repeatedly.
Some patients with frontal lobe damage may perseverate on simple tasks during cognitive testing.
The subject continued to perseverate even though the initial instructions had changed.
