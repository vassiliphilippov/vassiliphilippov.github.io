flat-earther

noun
someone who believes the Earth is flat
Pronunciation: / flætˈɝːθɚ / (AmE), / flætˈɜːθə / (BrE)
a person who believes that the Earth is flat, in opposition to the scientific consensus that it is spherical

Examples

a flat-earther denies science
Calling someone a flat-earther is often a way to imply they reject widely accepted facts.
Many people dismissed him as a flat-earther after he argued against space exploration.
The documentary interviewed several flat-earthers about their beliefs and motivations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conspiracy theorist: a person who believes in conspiracy theories
  • antonym – scientist: a person engaged in and promotes scientific knowledge
