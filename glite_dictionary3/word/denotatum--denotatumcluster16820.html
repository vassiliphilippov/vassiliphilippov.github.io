denotatum

noun
object referred to by an expression
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːnoʊˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌdiːnəˈteɪtəm / (BrE)
the actual object or entity in the world to which a word, phrase, or linguistic expression refers

Examples

denotatum of a word
Different words may have the same denotatum but different connotations.
The denotatum of the word 'tree' is a physical tree in the real world.
When analyzing a sentence, it is important to distinguish between its sense and its denotatum.
