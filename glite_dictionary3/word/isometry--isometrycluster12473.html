isometry

noun
equal growth of body parts in organisms
Pronunciation: / aɪˈsɑmətri / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒmətri / (BrE)
equal growth rate in different parts of an organism so that proportions remain constant during development

Examples

isometry in growth patterns
Comparing isometry and allometry can help scientists understand evolutionary adaptations in morphology.
In some species, isometry is observed when their body parts grow at the same rate throughout development.
The concept of isometry explains why a young animal may look like a smaller version of the adult, with similar proportions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – allometry: growth of different parts of an organism at different rates
