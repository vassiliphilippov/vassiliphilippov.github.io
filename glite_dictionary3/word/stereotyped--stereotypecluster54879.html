stereotyped

adjective
lacking originality; overly conventional
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛriəˌtaɪpt / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəˌtaɪpt / (BrE)
lacking originality, spontaneity, or individuality; showing a fixed pattern or form that is repeated and unoriginal

Examples

stereotyped phrases
His speech was full of stereotyped expressions that failed to impress the audience.
She found the movie's plot too stereotyped to be engaging.
The novel avoided using stereotyped characters and instead created complex, unique personalities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – formulaic: made according to a fixed pattern, not original
  • antonym – original: new and not derived from something else; not copied
