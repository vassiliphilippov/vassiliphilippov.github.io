kinescope

noun
early TV cathode-ray tube display
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪnəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈkɪnɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A cathode-ray tube used in early television receivers to display pictures from broadcast signals onto a luminescent screen.

Examples

kinescope television screen
Before flat screens, every household television had a bulky kinescope inside.
Technicians replaced the faulty kinescope to restore picture quality to the old TV.
The vintage television set still used a kinescope to project black-and-white images.
