to crossbreed

verb
to breed from different varieties
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌbrid / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbriːd / (BrE)
to produce an animal or plant by mating individuals of different breeds, varieties, or species

Examples

to crossbreed plants
Scientists often crossbreed two plant species to create disease-resistant crops.
The farmer decided to crossbreed his sheep with another breed to improve wool quality.
To obtain unique flowers, gardeners frequently crossbreed different varieties of roses.

  • synonym – hybridize: to produce hybrids by crossbreeding
  • antonym – inbreed: to breed from closely related individuals
