to fidget

verb
move restlessly or nervously
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
to make small movements, especially of the hands and feet, usually because of nervousness or impatience

Examples

to fidget in a seat
After sitting still for an hour, the children began to fidget in their chairs.
She fidgeted nervously with her pen during the interview.
Stop fidgeting and try to concentrate on your homework.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freeze: to become motionless, especially due to fear or nervousness
  • synonym – squirm: to twist or move about uncomfortably, often due to nervousness
