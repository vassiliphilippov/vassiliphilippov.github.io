delirious

adjective
in a state of delirium or wild excitement
Pronunciation: / dəˈlɪriəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəs / (BrE)
in a disturbed state of mind, often with excitement, confusion, or hallucinations, usually as a result of illness or fever

Examples

delirious from fever
After three days of high fever, she became delirious and started mumbling incoherently.
He was so delirious with excitement that he could barely speak.
The delirious patient needed constant supervision for his own safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lucid: clear-minded, rational
