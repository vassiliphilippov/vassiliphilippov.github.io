infirm

adjective
lacking firmness of will or purpose
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɝm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
lacking strength or firmness of will, character, or purpose; weak or irresolute in spirit or determination.

Examples

infirm willpower
Despite good intentions, he proved too infirm to make the difficult decision.
Her infirm resolve made her easily swayed by others.
The leader's infirm character was apparent in times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – resolute: admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • synonym – weak-willed: lacking determination or resolve
