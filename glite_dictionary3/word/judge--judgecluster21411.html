to judge

verb
preside over a court case as a judge
Pronunciation: / dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
to preside over a court case and make legal decisions, especially as an official judge

Examples

to judge a trial
After reviewing the evidence, she began to judge the defendant’s case.
He has judged dozens of criminal trials during his career.
The court appointed her to judge the high-profile fraud trial.
