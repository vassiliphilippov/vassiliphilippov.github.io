haemoglobin

noun
oxygen-carrying blood protein
Pronunciation: / hiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
a red protein responsible for transporting oxygen in the blood of vertebrates, contained in red blood cells

Examples

low haemoglobin levels
Iron deficiency can cause low haemoglobin and lead to anaemia.
The haemoglobin in red blood cells binds to oxygen for transport around the body.
The doctor checked his haemoglobin levels during the blood test.
