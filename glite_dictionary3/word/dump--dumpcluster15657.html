dump

noun
computer memory/storage copy
Pronunciation: / dʌmp / (AmE), / dʌmp / (BrE)
(computing) a copy of the contents of a computer storage device, often used for debugging or analysis.

Examples

memory dump
After the crash, they examined the memory dump to diagnose the problem.
Please create a dump of the database before making any changes.
The engineer analyzed the dump to find the source of the error.

Related Terms

  • synonym – core dump: a recording of the contents of computer memory at a specific time, typically after a crash
