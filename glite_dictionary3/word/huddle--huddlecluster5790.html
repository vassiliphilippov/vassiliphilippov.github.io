huddle

noun
quick private meeting or discussion
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.əl / (BrE)
an informal or quick private meeting, especially to confer or discuss plans briefly

Examples

a quick huddle
After the announcement, the managers had a quick huddle to decide on the next steps.
She called everyone into a huddle so they could share important updates privately.
The team came together for a short huddle before the final play.
