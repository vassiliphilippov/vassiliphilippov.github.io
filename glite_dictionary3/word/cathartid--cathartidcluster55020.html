cathartid

noun
a large New World vulture or condor
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑrtɪd / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪd / (BrE)
A large bird of prey of the family Cathartidae, including New World vultures and condors, resembling Old World vultures.

Examples

cathartid vulture species
Researchers observed several cathartids circling the area in search of carrion.
The Andean condor is a magnificent cathartid found in South America.
Unlike Old World vultures, cathartids rely heavily on their sense of smell to locate food.
