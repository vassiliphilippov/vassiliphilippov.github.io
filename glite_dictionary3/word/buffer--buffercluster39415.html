buffer

noun
neutral zone between rival powers
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfər / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə / (BrE)
an area or zone of land that separates two rival powers or groups, acting to reduce the risk of conflict between them

Examples

buffer zone border
A demilitarized zone was established as a buffer along the border.
Switzerland was seen as a buffer between the warring nations of Europe.
The small kingdom acted as a buffer to separate the two empires.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buffer state: a country lying between two rival or potentially hostile greater powers, serving to prevent conflict
