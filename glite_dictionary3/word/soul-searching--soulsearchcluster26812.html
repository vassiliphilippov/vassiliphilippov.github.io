soul-searching

noun
deep self-examination; self-reflection
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlˌsɝrtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlˌsɜːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A deep and searching examination of one's own thoughts, feelings, or motives, typically in order to make a difficult decision or to understand oneself better.

Examples

serious soul-searching
After losing his job, he did a lot of soul-searching about his career path.
Her period of soul-searching led to a major change in her life.
The company’s leadership went through some soul-searching after the scandal was revealed.
