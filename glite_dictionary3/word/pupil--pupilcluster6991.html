pupil

noun
opening in the iris of the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪl / (BrE)
The circular opening in the center of the iris of the eye, which controls the amount of light that enters the eye.

Examples

pupil of the eye
A sudden flash caused her pupil to contract quickly.
In dim light, your pupil widens to let in more light.
The doctor shined a light into his eye to check his pupil reaction.
