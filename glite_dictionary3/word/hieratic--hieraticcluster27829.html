hieratic

adjective
highly formal or restrained in style
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪəˈrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəˈrætɪk / (BrE)
adhering to fixed types, methods, or forms; highly restrained and formal.

Examples

hieratic style speech
Her delivery was so hieratic that it seemed more like a ritual than a speech.
The ceremony had a hieratic structure that made participants feel both awe and distance.
The painter is known for his hieratic style, which gives his portraits a solemn air.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ceremonial: marked by or relating to a ceremony; formal and ritualistic
  • synonym – formal: done in accordance with established rules or customs; highly conventional
  • antonym – informal: having a relaxed, friendly, or unofficial style
