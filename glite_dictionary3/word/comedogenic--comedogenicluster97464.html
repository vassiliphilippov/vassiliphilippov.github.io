comedogenic

adjective
causing blocked pores that can lead to acne
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊmədoʊˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkəʊməˌdəʊˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) tending to cause or promote the formation of comedones (blackheads or whiteheads) by blocking pores on the skin.

Examples

highly comedogenic cream
Cocoa butter can be highly comedogenic, especially for people with oily skin.
Dermatologists often recommend using non-comedogenic products for acne-prone skin.
Some moisturizers are labeled as comedogenic, which means they may clog pores and cause breakouts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non-comedogenic: (of a substance) not likely to cause blocked pores or acne
