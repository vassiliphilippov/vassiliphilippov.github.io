rotation

noun
planned recurring sequence (of crops, personnel, etc.)
Pronunciation: / roʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a regular sequence or schedule in which things are replaced or occur in turn

Examples

crop rotation system
Farmers practice crop rotation to preserve soil nutrients and prevent disease.
The company implements a staff rotation to ensure fairness in weekend shifts.
The team's starting pitcher rotation changed after the star player's injury.
The company's security policy mandates routine rotation of all employee passwords every ninety days to reduce risks.
Cloud providers now offer automatic rotation of encryption keys to minimize the impact of a potential compromise.
After the breach, the IT team enforced immediate rotation of all API tokens and revoked old credentials.

Related Terms

  • synonym – credential management: the policies and tools used to create, store, rotate, and revoke authentication credentials
  • synonym – key rotation: the process of replacing cryptographic keys at regular intervals to limit the time a key is valid
  • synonym – schedule: a plan of activities or events and when they will happen
