anosmia

noun
loss of the sense of smell
Pronunciation: / əˈnɑːzmiə / (AmE), / əˈnɒzmiə / (BrE)
the loss or absence of the sense of smell, typically caused by injury, disease, or obstruction of the nasal passages

Examples

complete anosmia diagnosis
Complete anosmia can have a significant impact on quality of life, especially when it comes to enjoying food.
Some people with anosmia also experience changes in their sense of taste.
The patient was diagnosed with anosmia after she lost her sense of smell following a viral infection.
