anoxemia

noun
low oxygen in arterial blood (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌænɑkˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌænɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an abnormally low level of oxygen in arterial blood.

Examples

anoxemia in blood
After several minutes underwater, the diver experienced anoxemia due to lack of breathable air.
Doctors monitor newborns closely for signs of anoxemia in the delivery room.
Patients suffering from anoxemia should receive immediate medical attention to restore adequate oxygen levels.

  • synonym – hypoxemia: deficiency of oxygen in the blood
