to entitle

verb
to give a title of nobility
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪtəl / (BrE)
to give a rank or title to someone, especially by making them a member of the nobility

Examples

to entitle a person lord
For his bravery, the soldier was entitled to a position within the aristocracy.
The king decided to entitle his loyal knight, making him a duke.
Upon her marriage, she was entitled as the Countess of Warwick.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ennoble: to make someone a member of the nobility or to give a noble title
