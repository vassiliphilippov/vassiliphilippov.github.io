haemopoiesis

noun
formation of blood cells
Pronunciation: / hiːmoʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (AmE), / hiːməʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which blood cells are formed in the body, especially within the bone marrow

Examples

haemopoiesis in bone marrow
Chemotherapy can suppress haemopoiesis, leading to lower blood cell counts.
Healthy haemopoiesis is essential for producing adequate red and white blood cells.
The study focused on haemopoiesis in patients with bone marrow disorders.
