heliograph

noun
device sending signals by reflected sunlight
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːliəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhiːliəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an apparatus that uses a mirror to send telegraphic messages by reflecting flashes of sunlight

Examples

heliograph signal device
In clear weather, the heliograph could transmit signals over a hundred miles.
The army used a heliograph to send coded messages across the desert.
The museum's collection includes a 19th-century heliograph used by explorers.
