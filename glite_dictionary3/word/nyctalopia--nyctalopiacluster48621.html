nyctalopia

noun
difficulty seeing in low light; night blindness
Pronunciation: / ˌnɪktəˈloʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˈləʊpiə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person has difficulty seeing in dim or low light, commonly caused by a deficiency of vitamin A or retinal disease; night blindness.

Examples

nyctalopia in dim light
A hereditary form of nyctalopia is linked to certain retinal diseases.
People with nyctalopia may struggle to drive safely after sunset.
Vitamin A supplements can sometimes help improve nyctalopia caused by dietary deficiency.
