cryosurgery

noun
medical destruction of tissue by freezing
Pronunciation: / ˌkraɪoʊˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˌkraɪəʊˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which extreme cold, usually from liquid nitrogen, is used to destroy abnormal or unwanted tissue

Examples

cryosurgery for skin cancer
Cryosurgery is often used to treat certain types of skin cancer with minimal scarring.
Advances in cryosurgery have made it a safer option for removing abnormal tissue.
After consultation, the doctor recommended cryosurgery to remove the wart on her finger.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cryotherapy: the use of extreme cold in medical treatment, often for pain or inflammation
