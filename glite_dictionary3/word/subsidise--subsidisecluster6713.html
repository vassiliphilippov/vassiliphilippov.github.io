to subsidise

verb
provide financial support
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.sɪˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪz / (BrE)
to give money to help support an organization, activity, or country, usually from a government or authority

Examples

The government plans to subsidise local farmers to help increase food production.
The state agreed to subsidise the construction of new affordable housing units.
If we subsidise the industry, it could save thousands of jobs.
Many European countries subsidise public transportation to make it more affordable for citizens.
The government decided to subsidise local farmers to help them compete with imports.
