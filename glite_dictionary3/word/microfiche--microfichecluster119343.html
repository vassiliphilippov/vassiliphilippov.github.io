microfiche

noun
small sheet of microfilm for storing documents
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkɹəˌfiʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.krəˌfiːʃ / (BrE)
A small sheet of microfilm containing miniature photographs of documents, which can be viewed using a magnifying device.

Examples

microfiche records
Researchers accessed decades-old census records on microfiche for their study.
She learned to use the microfiche machine to find archival documents.
The library stores many old newspapers on microfiche to save space.

Related Terms

  • synonym – microfilm: film containing photographic reproductions of documents at reduced size
