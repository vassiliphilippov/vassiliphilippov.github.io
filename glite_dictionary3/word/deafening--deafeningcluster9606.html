deafening

adjective
extremely loud (sound)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛfənɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) extremely loud; so loud as to make hearing difficult or cause temporary loss of hearing.

Examples

deafening noise
The deafening roar of the thunder startled everyone in the room.
The applause was so deafening that the speaker had to wait before continuing.
We covered our ears as the deafening music blasted through the speakers at the concert.
