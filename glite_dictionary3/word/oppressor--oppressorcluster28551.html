oppressor

noun
a person who treats others cruelly or unjustly
Pronunciation: / əˈprɛsər / (AmE), / əˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a person in a position of power who treats people in a cruel or unfair way, especially by using force or authority

Examples

cruel oppressor
For years, the dictator acted as an oppressor to his own citizens, denying them basic rights.
She feared her boss, whom she saw not as a leader but as an oppressor in the workplace.
The people rose up against their oppressor to demand freedom and justice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – persecutor: person who subjects others to hostility or ill-treatment
  • synonym – tyrant: cruel and oppressive ruler
  • antonym – victim: person harmed or oppressed by others
