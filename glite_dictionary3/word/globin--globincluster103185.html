globin

noun
protein part of hemoglobin
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
A colorless protein component obtained by removing heme from hemoglobin, forming part of the oxygen-carrying compound in red blood cells.

Examples

globin protein chain
A deficiency in certain globin chains can lead to forms of anemia.
Researchers studied various globin types to understand genetic disorders affecting blood.
When the heme group is removed from hemoglobin, the remaining globin retains its protein structure.
