contracture

noun
permanent muscle or joint tightening
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræktʃɚ / (AmE), / kənˈtraktʃə / (BrE)
A condition in which a muscle or joint remains permanently tightened or shortened, often causing stiffness or deformity.

Examples

muscle contracture
After the accident, a severe contracture formed in his right hand, limiting its movement.
Physical therapy can sometimes help prevent a contracture in immobilized patients.
The surgeon corrected the joint contracture to restore mobility.
