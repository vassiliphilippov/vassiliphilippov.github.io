reducing

noun
chemical process adding electrons to an atom or ion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdusɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsɪŋ / (BrE)
In chemistry, any process in which electrons are added to an atom or ion, typically by removing oxygen or adding hydrogen; this always occurs together with oxidation of a reducing agent.

Examples

reducing agent reaction
Chemists study both oxidation and reducing reactions to understand how substances interact.
Fermentation is an example of a reducing process that occurs in many biological systems.
In the laboratory, reducing plays a vital role in the extraction of metals from ores.

Related Terms

  • antonym – oxidation: a chemical process in which electrons are removed from an atom or ion
