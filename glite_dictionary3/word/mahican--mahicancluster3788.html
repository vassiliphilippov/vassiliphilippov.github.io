Mahican

noun
a Native American people from the Hudson Valley
Pronunciation: / məˈhiːkən / (AmE), / məˈhiːkən / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian-speaking Native American people formerly living in the Hudson Valley and eastward to the Housatonic River

Examples

Mahican tribe member
A Mahican delegation met with colonial leaders to discuss land treaties.
Descendants of the Mahican people still preserve aspects of their language and culture today.
The Mahican were historically located along the Hudson River in what is now New York State.
