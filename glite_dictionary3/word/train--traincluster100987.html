to train

verb
cause a plant to grow in a controlled way
Pronunciation: / treɪn / (AmE), / treɪn / (BrE)
to encourage a plant to grow in a particular direction or shape by tying and pruning it

Examples

to train a vine
Gardeners often train climbing roses along fences to create beautiful displays.
It takes patience to train grapevines to grow neatly along the trellis.
You should train the young tree early to ensure it develops a strong shape.
