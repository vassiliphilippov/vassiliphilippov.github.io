clear

adjective
distinct and undimmed (sound or color)
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
(of a sound or color) free from anything that lessens purity, sharpness, or intensity; distinct and unmuddied.

Examples

clear sound
A clear ringing of bells echoed across the valley at dawn.
Her painting used only the most clear blues and reds to make the image striking.
The choir's clear voices resonated through the ancient cathedral.

Related Terms

  • antonym – muddy: (of sound or color) not clear; dull or indistinct
  • synonym – pure: (of sound or color) not mixed or adulterated; clean
