to degas

verb
remove gas from a substance
Pronunciation: / diˈɡæs / (AmE), / diˈɡæs / (BrE)
to remove gas or dissolved gases from a liquid or material, typically by physical or chemical means

Examples

to degas a liquid
Before filling the bottles, the company must degas the wine to ensure stability.
In the lab, we degas the solution to prevent unwanted reactions caused by dissolved oxygen.
The technician is degassing the resin before using it in the vacuum process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gas: to add gas to or treat with gas
