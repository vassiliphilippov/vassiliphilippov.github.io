radiopacity

noun
opacity to X-rays or radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈpæsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being opaque to X-rays or other forms of radiation; the degree to which a substance prevents the passage of X-rays

Examples

radiopacity of bone
Dental fillings are designed to have sufficient radiopacity to distinguish them from surrounding tooth structures.
Differences in radiopacity can help identify various tissues and foreign bodies in medical imaging.
The radiopacity of the material made it easily visible on the X-ray.

Related Terms

  • antonym – radiolucency: the property of being transparent to X-rays
