glow

noun
light emitted by a heated object
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊ / (AmE), / ɡləʊ / (BrE)
a steady, soft, or even light, especially one produced by heat, chemical reaction, or without flames

Examples

soft glow
As the temperature rose, a visible glow appeared around the filament.
The glow from the coals illuminated the small, dark room.
The iron rod began to emit a faint glow as it was heated in the forge.
A warm glow filled the room as the sun set behind the hills.
She admired the gentle glow of the city lights at dusk.
The glow from the lantern softly illuminated the entire campsite.
Astronomers study the glow of distant galaxies to learn about their properties.
The glow measured from the surface indicated a high level of radiation.
The infrared glow revealed regions of heat invisible to the naked eye.
