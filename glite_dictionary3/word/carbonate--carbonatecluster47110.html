carbonate

noun
a salt or ester of carbonic acid
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a salt or ester of carbonic acid containing the anion CO3, commonly found in minerals and used in various industrial processes

Examples

sodium carbonate
Sodium carbonate is commonly used in the production of glass and soap.
Some antacid tablets contain calcium carbonate to neutralize stomach acid.
The water in this region is rich in carbonate minerals, which makes it hard.
