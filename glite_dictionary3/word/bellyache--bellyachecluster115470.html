to bellyache

verb
to complain, often excessively
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛliˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌeɪk / (BrE)
to complain, especially in a way that is excessive or annoying

Examples

to bellyache about work
He likes to bellyache about his job even though he has it pretty easy.
She bellyached all afternoon about the long wait at the doctor's office.
Stop bellyaching and do something about the situation if it bothers you so much.
