weaverbird

noun
finch-like bird known for woven nests
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːvərˌbɝːd / (AmE), / ˈwiːvəbɜːd / (BrE)
A small African or Asian bird, resembling a finch, known for building intricately woven nests in colonies.

Examples

weaverbird nest building
A colony of weaverbirds built their nests together in the acacia tree.
During our safari, we watched as a weaverbird skillfully wove strips of grass into its nest.
The weaverbird is admired for its intricate and tightly constructed nests.
