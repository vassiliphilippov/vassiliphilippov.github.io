maidenliness

noun
quality of behaving like a young maiden
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdənli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdnlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving or appearing like a young maiden, often characterized by modesty, innocence, or purity

Examples

her maidenliness shone
Her maidenliness was evident in her gentle manner and reserved speech.
In that era, families placed great value on the maidenliness of their daughters.
The poet praised the maidenliness of his beloved, comparing her to a delicate rose.

Related Terms

  • antonym – coquetry: flirtatious behavior, often attributed to young women
  • synonym – modesty: the quality of being modest; reserve or propriety in speech, dress, or behavior
