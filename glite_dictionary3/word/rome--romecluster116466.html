Rome

proper noun
capital of Italy, historic city
Pronunciation: / roʊm / (AmE), / rəʊm / (BrE)
The capital and largest city of Italy, located on the Tiber River; historically significant as the center of the Roman Catholic Church and formerly the capital of the Roman Republic and Roman Empire.

In classical antiquity, Rome was the center of a vast empire stretching across Europe, Africa, and Asia.
