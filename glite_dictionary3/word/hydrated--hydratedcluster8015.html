hydrated

adjective
containing chemically combined water
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (BrE)
containing water that is chemically combined, especially as water of crystallization in a compound

Examples

hydrated salt crystals
Many salts are found in a hydrated form, such as copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate.
The mineral is hydrated, meaning it contains water molecules within its crystal structure.
When hydrated, the compound exhibits different color and solubility compared to its anhydrous state.
