scratchy

adjective
causing abrasion or irritation
Pronunciation: / ˈskrætʃi / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃi / (BrE)
(of a surface or material) causing slight abrasion or irritation when touched or rubbed against the skin.

Examples

scratchy wool sweater
After washing, the towels became noticeably scratchy to the touch.
She complained that the new labels inside her shirt were scratchy and uncomfortable.
The wool sweater felt scratchy against my skin, so I wore a T-shirt underneath.

Related Terms

  • antonym – smooth: having an even and regular surface; not rough or scratchy
