kibibyte

noun
unit of 1,024 bytes in computing
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪbiˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪbɪbʌɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,024 bytes, used in computing to measure data size.

Examples

one kibibyte of data
One kibibyte is technically equivalent to 1,024 bytes, not 1,000 bytes.
The file size is just 5 kibibytes, making it very easy to download.
When formatting old floppy disks, their capacity was often measured in kibibytes.
