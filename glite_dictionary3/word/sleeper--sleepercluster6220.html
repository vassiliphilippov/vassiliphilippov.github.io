sleeper

noun
unexpected achiever of success
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːpɚ /; / ˈsliːpər / (AmE), / ˈsliːpə / (BrE)
a person or thing (such as a film, book, or athlete) that becomes successful or popular unexpectedly

Examples

sleeper hit movie
He was drafted late but became a sleeper star in his rookie season.
That book was a sleeper hit, becoming popular months after its release.
The film turned out to be a sleeper, winning awards despite low expectations.
Her debut novel became a sleeper hit, surprising critics and readers alike.
Many experts dismissed the startup, but it quickly became a sleeper in the tech industry.
The low-budget film turned out to be a sleeper and grossed millions worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dark horse: a competitor about whom little is known who unexpectedly wins or succeeds
  • antonym – flop: something that fails or is very unsuccessful, especially in entertainment
  • synonym – hit: something very successful, especially in entertainment or business
