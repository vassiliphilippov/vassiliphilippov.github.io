word-splitting

noun
making overly fine distinctions
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːdˌsplɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdˌsplɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making excessively fine or trivial distinctions, often over matters of little importance

Examples

pointless word-splitting
His word-splitting was seen as unhelpful during the negotiations.
Some critics accuse the court of word-splitting rather than addressing the core issue.
The debate turned into endless word-splitting about the definition of a single term.
