to noose

verb
make a loop with a knot, esp. in rope
Pronunciation: / nus / (AmE), / nuːs / (BrE)
to make a loop with a running knot, especially in a rope, typically to catch or secure something

Examples

to noose a branch
Before starting the trick, she noosed the end of the cord just as she had practiced.
He carefully noosed the rope around the calf's neck to keep it from running away.
The fisherman expertly noosed the line to trap the eel.
