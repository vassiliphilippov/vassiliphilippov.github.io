dowery

noun
money or property brought at marriage
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊəri / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəri / (BrE)
An amount of money or property that a woman brings to her husband when they marry.

Examples

dowery for marriage
Her family provided a generous dowery when she married into the wealthy household.
Historical novels often discuss the importance of a young woman's dowery.
The absence of a substantial dowery sometimes hindered women's chances of marrying well in the past.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dowry: money or property brought by a bride to her husband at marriage
