distrust

noun
lack of trust or confidence
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtrʌst / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrʌst / (BrE)
a lack of trust or confidence in someone or something; a feeling of suspicion or doubt

Examples

public distrust
His growing distrust of the government made him question every official statement.
There is a deep distrust between the two rival companies, leading to constant suspicion.
Years of broken promises bred a sense of distrust among the employees.
Her deep distrust of politicians made her reluctant to vote in the election.
The company's secrecy fueled public distrust of its intentions.
Years of betrayal had bred a sense of distrust between the two neighbors.
