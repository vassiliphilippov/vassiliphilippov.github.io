Hungarumlaut

noun
diacritic mark (double acute) in Hungarian
Pronunciation: / ˌhʌŋɡərˈuːmlaʊt / (AmE), / ˌhʌŋɡərˈuːmlaʊt / (BrE)
A double acute accent ( ˝ ) used as a diacritic mark in the Hungarian alphabet to indicate long versions of the front rounded vowels.

Examples

Hungarumlaut mark in Hungarian
Linguists often refer to the double acute accent as the Hungarumlaut when describing Hungarian orthography.
The vowel 'ő' in Hungarian is marked with a Hungarumlaut to indicate it is a long front rounded vowel.
Unlike the German umlaut, the Hungarumlaut appears only in the Hungarian language.
