to flash

verb
appear briefly or suddenly
Pronunciation: / flæʃ / (AmE), / flæʃ / (BrE)
to move, run, or appear very quickly, often suddenly or for a short time

Examples

to flash by quickly
His name flashed in her mind for just a second before she refocused.
Images from the movie flashed quickly on the screen during the preview.
The warning message flashed across the monitor before disappearing.
He flashes from one task to another without pausing.
She flashed past the finish line to win the race.
The rabbit flashed across the road before the car could stop.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dart: to move suddenly and rapidly
  • synonym – dash: to move quickly and suddenly
  • synonym – flicker: to shine with a light that is sometimes bright and sometimes weak
  • antonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary
