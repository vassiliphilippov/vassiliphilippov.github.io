panegyric

noun
formal speech or text of praise
Pronunciation: / ˌpænəˈdʒɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpanɪˈdʒɪrɪk / (BrE)
A formal public speech or written text expressing high praise for someone or something.

Examples

panegyric speech
At the ceremony, she delivered a moving panegyric to celebrate her mentor's achievements.
The book closes with a panegyric on the importance of compassion and kindness.
The retiring professor was honored with a lengthy panegyric delivered by one of his former students.

Related Terms

  • antonym – criticism: the expression of disapproval of someone or something
  • synonym – eulogy: a speech or piece of writing that praises someone, especially after death
  • synonym – tribute: an act, statement, or gift that is intended to show gratitude, respect, or admiration
