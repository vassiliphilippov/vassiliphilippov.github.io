tritanopia

noun
color blindness affecting blue-yellow perception
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪtəˈnoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌtraɪtəˈnəʊpiə / (BrE)
A rare form of color blindness in which the affected person has difficulty distinguishing between blue and yellow due to decreased sensitivity to blue light.

Examples

tritanopia color blindness
A test for tritanopia often involves identifying colored patterns designed to reveal blue-yellow color vision deficiencies.
Because tritanopia is so rare, it is often misdiagnosed as another form of color blindness.
People with tritanopia may confuse blue with green and yellow with violet.
