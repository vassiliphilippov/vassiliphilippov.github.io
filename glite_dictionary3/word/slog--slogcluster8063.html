to slog

verb
walk with slow, heavy steps
Pronunciation: / slɑg / (AmE), / slɒg / (BrE)
to walk with slow, heavy steps, especially because of tiredness or through difficult conditions such as mud

Examples

to slog through mud
After hours of rain, we had to slog through the muddy field to reach the house.
Every morning he would slog to work through the crowded, noisy streets.
The exhausted hikers continued to slog up the steep, rocky path despite the downpour.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – march: to walk with regular, measured steps
  • synonym – plod: to walk heavily and slowly without much energy or excitement
  • synonym – trudge: to walk slowly with heavy steps, typically because of exhaustion
