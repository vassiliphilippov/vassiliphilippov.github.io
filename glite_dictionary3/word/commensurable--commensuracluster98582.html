commensurable

adjective
can be measured by a common standard
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛn.tʃɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɛn.tʃər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of quantities or values) capable of being measured by the same standard or by a common unit.

Examples

commensurable units of measurement
Minutes and seconds are commensurable units of time.
The committee sought commensurable data to compare the effectiveness of the projects.
The two lengths are commensurable because both can be measured in centimeters.

Related Terms

  • antonym – incommensurable: not able to be measured by the same standard; not comparable in measurement
