cold

adjective
having a low temperature; not warm
Pronunciation: / koʊld / (AmE), / kəʊld / (BrE)
having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body or to what is comfortable; feeling or causing a sensation of low temperature

Examples

cold winter night
Even though it was spring, the water in the lake was still cold.
She wrapped her hands around a mug of tea to soothe her cold fingers.
The salad was served cold, just as the recipe suggested.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cool: slightly cold, but not uncomfortably so
  • degree higher – freezing: extremely cold; at or below the freezing point
  • antonym – warm: having or producing a moderate degree of heat
