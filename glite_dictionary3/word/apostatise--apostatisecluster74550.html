to apostatise

verb
formally abandon one's faith or beliefs
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒstətaɪz / (BrE)
to formally renounce or abandon one's religious faith or previously held beliefs, especially in favor of another system

Examples

to apostatise from faith
After years of questioning, he finally chose to apostatise from the religion of his childhood.
She struggled with the choice to apostatise her long-held beliefs.
Some faced persecution simply because they refused to apostatise under pressure.
