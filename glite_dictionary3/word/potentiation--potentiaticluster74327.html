potentiation

noun
increased effect when drugs are combined
Pronunciation: / poʊˌtɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌtɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the increase in the effect of one drug by the simultaneous use of another drug, resulting in a greater combined effect than the sum of their individual effects

Examples

potentiation of drug effects
Doctors must be cautious of potentiation if patients are taking multiple central nervous system depressants.
In pharmacology, potentiation can lead to unexpected side effects when drugs interact.
The potentiation of pain relief by combining codeine and acetaminophen is well documented.

Related Terms

  • synonym – synergism: interaction of elements that produces a combined effect greater than the sum of their parts
