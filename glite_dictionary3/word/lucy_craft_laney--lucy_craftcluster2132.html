Lucy Craft Laney

proper noun
American educator and school founder
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsi kræft ˈleɪni / (AmE), / ˈluːsi krɑːft ˈleɪni / (BrE)
(1854–1933) An American educator who founded the first private school for Black students in Augusta, Georgia, and was a pioneering advocate for African-American education.
