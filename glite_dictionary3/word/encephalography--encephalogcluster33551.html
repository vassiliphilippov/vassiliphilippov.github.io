encephalography

noun
X-ray imaging method for the brain
Pronunciation: / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
an imaging procedure in which X-ray pictures of the brain are taken after replacing spinal fluid with a gas, producing an encephalogram

Examples

perform encephalography scan
Before the MRI era, encephalography was commonly used to visualize brain tumors and other disorders.
Modern imaging techniques have largely replaced encephalography in clinical practice.
The doctor recommended encephalography to diagnose abnormalities in the patient's brain structure.
