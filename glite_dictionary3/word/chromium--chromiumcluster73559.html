chromium

noun
a hard, silvery metallic element (Cr)
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊmiəm / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊmiəm / (BrE)
a hard, brittle, silvery metal (chemical element, symbol Cr) resistant to corrosion and tarnishing, used especially in stainless steel and other alloys.

Examples

chromium metal alloy
Scientists discovered that chromium is essential in trace amounts for human health.
