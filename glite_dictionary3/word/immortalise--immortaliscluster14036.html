to immortalise

verb
make remembered or famous forever
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɔrtəˌlaɪz /; / ɪˈmɔːrdəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something famous or remembered forever, especially by creating something that lasts (like a story, song, or picture)

Examples

to immortalise a hero
His heroic deeds were immortalised in song and story for generations to come.
Many artists hope their work will immortalise their names long after they are gone.
The monument was erected to immortalise the achievements of the city's founder.
That portrait painted by his friend immortalised the poet's gentle expression.
The statue in the town square will immortalise the hero's sacrifice for generations.
With her book, she hoped to immortalise her family's incredible story.

Related Terms

  • synonym – commemorate: to honor the memory of someone or something with a ceremony or object
  • synonym – immortalize: make famous forever (American spelling)
  • synonym – memorialize: to do or create something that keeps a person or event in people's memory
