Koasati

noun
member of a Native American people of Alabama
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɑːˌsɑːti / (AmE), / kəʊˈɑːˌsɑːti / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in northern Alabama and a part of the Creek Confederacy.

Examples

Koasati tribe member
A proud heritage is celebrated every year by descendants of the Koasati.
Researchers have documented the traditions and language of the Koasati people.
The Koasati were once part of the powerful Creek Confederacy in the southeastern United States.
