armoured

adjective
having a protective covering (zoological)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːr.mɚd / (AmE), / ˈɑː.məd / (BrE)
(of animals) having a protective covering, such as a shell, scales, or thick skin, that offers defense against predators or environmental hazards.

Examples

armoured dinosaur
Many dinosaurs, such as the ankylosaurus, were armoured for protection.
The armoured armadillo rolls into a ball to defend itself from predators.
The armoured scales of the pangolin make it difficult for most animals to harm it.
