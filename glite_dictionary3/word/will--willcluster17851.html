to will

verb
to leave property in a will
Pronunciation: / wɪl / (AmE), / wɪl / (BrE)
to declare in a legal will that someone is to receive one's property after death

Examples

to will property
According to the document, she willed her jewelry to her niece.
He decided to will his beloved books to his only grandson.
Her father willed the family home equally to all four children.
