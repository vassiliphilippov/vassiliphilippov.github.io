aperea

noun
South American rodent, possible guinea pig ancestor
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈreɪə / (AmE), / ˌapəˈreɪə / (BrE)
A South American cavy (rodent), possibly ancestral to the domestic guinea pig.

Examples

Scientists believe the domestic guinea pig was bred from the wild aperea.
The aperea is found throughout grasslands in South America.
Unlike the domestic guinea pig, the aperea has a coarser coat and lives in the wild.
