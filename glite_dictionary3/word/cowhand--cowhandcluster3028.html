cowhand

noun
person who tends cattle on a ranch
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌhand / (BrE)
a person employed to take care of cattle and perform ranch duties, often on horseback

Examples

cowhand herds cattle
After sunset, each cowhand returned to the bunkhouse to rest.
She worked for years as a cowhand before becoming a ranch manager.
The cowhand rode out early in the morning to check on the herd.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cowboy: person who herds cattle on horseback
  • antonym – rancher: someone who owns or manages a ranch
