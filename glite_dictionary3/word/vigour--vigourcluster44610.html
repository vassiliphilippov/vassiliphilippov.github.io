vigour

noun
physical or mental energy and strength
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
physical or mental strength, energy, or enthusiasm; the force or intensity with which something is done

Examples

with vigour and energy
After his illness, he regained his vigour and returned to work with renewed enthusiasm.
She attacked the problem with her usual vigour and determination.
The children played in the garden with remarkable vigour even in the heat.
Despite his age, the athlete performed with remarkable vigour throughout the competition.
She tackled her responsibilities each day with great vigour and enthusiasm.
The new policy was enforced with renewed vigour by the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym – energy: strength and vitality required for sustained activity
  • antonym – languor: lack of physical or mental energy
  • antonym – lethargy: lack of energy or enthusiasm
  • synonym – liveliness: the quality of being lively or animated
  • synonym – vigor: American spelling of vigour
