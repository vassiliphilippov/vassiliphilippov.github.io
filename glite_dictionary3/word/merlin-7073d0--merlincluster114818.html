merlin

noun
small falcon species used in falconry
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜrlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɜːlɪn / (BrE)
a small species of falcon (Falco columbarius) native to Europe and North America, with dark plumage and a banded tail, often used in falconry.

Examples

wild merlin falcon
The merlin is admired by falconers for its speed and agility.
Unlike larger birds of prey, the merlin can chase small songbirds with remarkable skill.
We spotted a merlin perched on the fence post during our birdwatching trip.
