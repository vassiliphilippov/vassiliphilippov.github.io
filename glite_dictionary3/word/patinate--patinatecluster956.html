to patinate

verb
apply a patina to a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈpætɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpætɪneɪt / (BrE)
to coat (a surface) with a patina, especially to color or oxidize the surface of metal to produce a thin film that gives an aged or decorative appearance

Examples

to patinate metal surfaces
Over time, exposure to air and moisture can naturally patinate copper roofs.
Special chemicals are often used to patinate metal surfaces in restoration work.
The sculptor decided to patinate the bronze statue for a rich green finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrode: to destroy or damage slowly by chemical action
  • synonym – oxidize: to undergo or cause to undergo a reaction with oxygen
