sepal

noun
green outer part of a flower
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.pəl / (AmE), / ˈsiː.pəl / (BrE)
each of the individual, usually green, leaf-like parts that form the outermost ring of a flower, collectively enclosing and protecting the petals when in bud.

Examples

sepal of a flower
In some plants, the sepal and petals are hard to distinguish.
The rosebud was tightly wrapped by each sepal, protecting its delicate petals.
When the flower opened, the green sepals were visible beneath the blossom.

Related Terms
