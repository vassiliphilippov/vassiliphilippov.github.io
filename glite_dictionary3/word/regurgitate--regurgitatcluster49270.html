to regurgitate

verb
to flow or rush back (medical/scientific)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡɝːdʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɜːdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, especially in a medical or scientific context) to flow or rush back into a place, especially in the wrong direction.

Examples

liquid regurgitates backward
If the structure is damaged, some contents might regurgitate unexpectedly.
In certain heart conditions, blood may regurgitate into the chamber it just left.
Valve defects can cause fluid to regurgitate rather than flow in the proper direction.
