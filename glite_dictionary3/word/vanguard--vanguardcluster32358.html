vanguard

noun
the foremost part of an army or movement
Pronunciation: / ˈvænˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈvanˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
the leading part of an army or force, or the foremost position in a movement or field

Examples

the vanguard of an army
During the march, the vanguard encountered resistance first.
Innovative scientists are at the vanguard of medical research.
The general positioned his best troops at the vanguard of the advancing army.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rear guard: troops positioned at the back of an army, protecting it from attack from behind
