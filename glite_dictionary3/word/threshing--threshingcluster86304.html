threshing

noun
separating grain from husks and straw
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of separating grain or seeds from the husks and straw, typically by beating or mechanical means

Examples

threshing wheat
Before modern equipment, threshing was done manually with a flail.
During harvest season, farmers work long hours threshing the wheat in the fields.
The invention of the threshing machine revolutionized the threshing process for agricultural communities.
