slippery

adjective
likely to cause slipping
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪpəri / (AmE), / ˈslɪpəri / (BrE)
likely to cause someone or something to slip or slide because of being smooth, wet, or icy

Examples

slippery floor surface
Be careful walking on the slippery sidewalk after the rain.
She lost her grip on the slippery bar of soap in the shower.
The kitchen floor became slippery when oil spilled on it.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rough: having an uneven or coarse surface; not smooth or slippery
  • synonym – slick: smooth and slippery, usually due to being wet or oily
