to destabilise

verb
become unstable; lose stability
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsteɪbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈsteɪ.bɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to become unstable or less secure; to lose stability

Examples

markets destabilise suddenly
After the reforms, the market began to destabilise and investors grew wary.
If environmental conditions suddenly change, the system may destabilise.
Their interference only served to destabilise the already fragile political situation.
