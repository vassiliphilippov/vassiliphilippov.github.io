dissuasive

adjective
discouraging or deterrent
Pronunciation: / dɪˈswæsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsweɪsɪv / (BrE)
Tending to discourage someone from taking a particular action; serving to deter.

Examples

dissuasive arguments
Her dissuasive arguments convinced him not to invest in the risky business.
Strict laws have a dissuasive effect on potential offenders.
The teacher's dissuasive tone made the students reconsider skipping class.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deterrent: something that discourages an action
  • antonym – persuasive: tending to convince someone to do something
