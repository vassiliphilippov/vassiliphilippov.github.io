anaplasmosis

noun
tick-borne disease affecting cattle
Pronunciation: / ˌænəplæzˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəplæzˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease of cattle and other animals caused by infection with Anaplasma bacteria, transmitted chiefly by ticks, leading to anemia and related symptoms.

Examples

anaplasmosis in cattle
Effective tick control can help prevent anaplasmosis outbreaks on livestock farms.
The spread of anaplasmosis is closely linked to the presence of cattle ticks in the area.
Veterinarians diagnosed anaplasmosis in several cows after they showed signs of severe anemia.

Related Terms

  • synonym – babesiosis: A similar tick-borne disease in cattle, also called Texas fever
