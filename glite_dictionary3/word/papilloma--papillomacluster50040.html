papilloma

noun
benign epithelial tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌpæpɪˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌpæpɪˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor of epithelial tissue forming a rounded mass, often on the skin or mucous membranes.

Examples

papilloma on skin
A papilloma can sometimes be mistaken for a wart due to its appearance.
Most papillomas are not cancerous but can cause discomfort depending on their location.
The doctor removed a small papilloma from the patient's vocal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym – carcinoma: a malignant tumor of epithelial origin
  • synonym – tumor: an abnormal mass of tissue
