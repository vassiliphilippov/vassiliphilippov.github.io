lubrication

noun
application of a lubricant
Pronunciation: / ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of applying a lubricant to reduce friction or make something smooth or slippery

Examples

lubrication of moving parts
A lack of proper lubrication can lead to equipment failure and costly repairs.
After several hours of use, the gears required additional lubrication to function smoothly.
Regular lubrication of the machine parts can prevent excessive wear and tear.
Insufficient lubrication can lead to increased friction and damage in moving components.
Proper lubrication of the engine parts ensures smooth operation and reduces wear.
The machine required regular lubrication to prevent overheating and mechanical failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lubricant: a substance, such as oil or grease, used to reduce friction
