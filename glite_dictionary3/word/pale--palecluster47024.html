pale

adjective
lacking vitality or interest
Pronunciation: / peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
(of something such as art, writing, or performance) lacking in vitality, interest, or effectiveness; feeble or unimpressive.

Examples

pale performance
Critics described her latest novel as a pale effort compared to her earlier, more dynamic work.
His speech was a pale shadow of the passionate address he gave last year.
The movie was a pale imitation of the classic original and failed to impress audiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dull: lacking interest or excitement
  • synonym – insipid: lacking flavor or interest
  • antonym – vivid: producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind
