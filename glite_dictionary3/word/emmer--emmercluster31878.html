emmer

noun
a type of wheat grown in Europe
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛmə / (BrE)
A type of hard, red wheat historically grown in Europe and used primarily for animal feed in the United States.

Examples

emmer wheat grain
Farmers in Germany have cultivated emmer for centuries due to its resilience to harsh climates.
In the United States, emmer is often grown as feed for livestock rather than for human consumption.
Some artisan bakers use emmer flour to produce breads with a unique flavor and texture.
