rip

noun
turbulent stretch of water due to crossing currents
Pronunciation: / rɪp / (AmE), / rɪp / (BrE)
a stretch of turbulent water in a river or the sea caused by opposing or crossing currents

Examples

rip current warning
Fishermen warned us about the dangerous rip that forms at the river mouth.
Swimmers are advised to avoid entering the water near a visible rip.
The boat struggled to cross the rip as the currents clashed fiercely.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rip current: a strong, narrow current of water flowing outward from the beach
