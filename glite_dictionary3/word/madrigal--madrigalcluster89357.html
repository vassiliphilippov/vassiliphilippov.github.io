madrigal

noun
unaccompanied partsong for several voices
Pronunciation: / ˈmædrɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmædrɪɡəl / (BrE)
a song for several voices, usually unaccompanied, that follows a strict poetic form, especially from the Renaissance period

Examples

sing a madrigal
She specialized in singing madrigals and early music pieces at the festival.
The choir performed a beautiful madrigal from the 16th century.
Their music class learned about the structure of the madrigal and practiced harmonizing.
