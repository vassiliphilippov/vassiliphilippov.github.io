to claim

verb
demand or assert ownership or right
Pronunciation: / kleɪm / (AmE), / kleɪm / (BrE)
to demand or assert a right to something, such as property, money, or privilege, especially legally

Examples

to claim lost baggage
After landing, he hurried to the carousel to claim his baggage.
She claimed a refund, arguing the item was defective.
You must claim your prize in person before the deadline.
He decided to claim his inheritance through the court system.
If your luggage is lost, you should claim compensation from the airline.
She plans to claim damages for the injuries she suffered in the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assert: to state or declare firmly
  • synonym – demand: to ask for forcefully or as a right
  • antonym – forsake: to give up, renounce, or abandon a claim
  • antonym – relinquish: to give up or surrender a claim or right
