retake

noun
a repeated shot or scene in film or photography
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈriːˌteɪk / (BrE)
an instance of shooting a scene, photograph, or shot again in film or photography, usually to correct an error or improve quality

Examples

retake of a scene
Due to equipment failure, the actors had to do several retakes.
Everyone was relieved when the scene was perfect and no retake was needed.
The director called for a retake after noticing a mistake in the background.
