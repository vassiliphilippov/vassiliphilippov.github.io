logogram

noun
symbol representing a word or phrase
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑɡəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡəˌɡræm / (BrE)
a written symbol that represents a whole word or phrase, rather than a single sound; used especially in some writing systems

Examples

logogram in Chinese writing
A logogram conveys meaning directly, unlike an alphabetic letter which represents a sound.
In ancient Egypt, many hieroglyphs functioned as logograms for entire words.
The Chinese character for 'water' is a classic logogram used in the language.

  • synonym – ideogram: a written symbol representing a concept or idea
