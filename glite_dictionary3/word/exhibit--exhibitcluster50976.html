to exhibit

verb
show or display publicly
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (BrE)
to show or display something publicly, including objects, qualities, or feelings

Examples

to exhibit strong feelings
He began to exhibit signs of nervousness during the interview.
Several local artists exhibited their work at the gallery opening.
The museum will exhibit ancient pottery from Greece next summer.
She plans to exhibit her photography at the local gallery.
The museum will exhibit several rare paintings this summer.
They exhibited their new product at the technology expo.
During the interview, he exhibited impressive knowledge of the subject.
She exhibits great patience when dealing with children.
The athlete exhibited remarkable determination throughout the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conceal: to hide something from view or keep it secret
  • synonym – display: to show something so it can be seen by others
  • antonym – hide: to conceal (not show) a feeling, ability, or attribute
  • synonym – show: to present something so that people can see it
