streptodornase

noun
enzyme dissolving fibrinous secretions, used medically
Pronunciation: / ˌstrɛptoʊˈdɔːrneɪs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəʊˈdɔːneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme produced by certain hemolytic streptococci that dissolves fibrinous secretions, used medicinally to aid in the removal of pus and debris from infected tissues, often together with streptokinase

Examples

streptodornase treatment wounds
In combination with streptokinase, streptodornase is effective at breaking down pus and dead tissue.
Researchers are studying new uses for streptodornase in the treatment of chronic infections.
The doctor prescribed streptodornase to help clear the patient's infected wounds.
