malicious

adjective
intending to harm or upset
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪʃəs / (AmE), / məˈlɪʃəs / (BrE)
Intended to harm someone or cause them pain or distress; showing a desire to cause harm.

Examples

malicious attack
Hackers launched a malicious attack on the company’s computer system.
She made a malicious remark about her colleague's appearance.
The newspaper published a malicious story that damaged his reputation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – benevolent: well meaning and kindly
  • synonym – spiteful: showing or caused by malice; wanting to hurt or upset
