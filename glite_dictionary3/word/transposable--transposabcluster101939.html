transposable

adjective
able to be transposed or changed in order
Pronunciation: / trænzˈpoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / trænzˈpəʊzəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being transformed or rearranged from one position, order, or sequence to another; especially used to describe genetic elements or data that can change position within a system.

Examples

transposable genetic elements
Some genes are transposable and can move to different locations within the genome.
Tables in this software are fully transposable for flexible data arrangement.
The melody of this song is easily transposable to other keys, making it adaptable for different instruments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – invariable: not able to be changed; remaining constant
