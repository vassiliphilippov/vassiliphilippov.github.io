nonterritorial

adjective
not displaying territorial behavior
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌtɛr.əˈtɔr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌtɛr.ɪˈtɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
Not displaying territorial behavior; not defending or claiming a particular area as territory.

Examples

nonterritorial animals
In a nonterritorial society, individuals do not defend specific areas against others.
Some animals become nonterritorial during the winter when food is scarce and competition is unwise.
Unlike its relatives, the robin is a nonterritorial species and shares feeding grounds peacefully.
