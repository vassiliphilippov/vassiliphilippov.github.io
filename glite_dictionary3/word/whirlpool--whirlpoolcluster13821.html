whirlpool

noun
powerful circular water current
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜrˌpul / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlpuːl / (BrE)
a powerful circular current of water, especially formed by opposing tides or currents, drawing objects towards its center

Examples

dangerous whirlpool
A sudden whirlpool formed where the two rivers met, creating a dangerous situation for kayakers.
Swimmers need to beware of the natural whirlpool near the base of the falls.
The boat was pulled into the whirlpool and spun around rapidly by the current.

Related Terms

  • synonym – eddy: a circular movement of water causing a small whirlpool
  • synonym – vortex: a mass of whirling fluid or air, especially a whirlpool or whirlwind
