moult

noun
periodic shedding of skin or covering
Pronunciation: / moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
the process by which certain animals periodically shed their outer covering, such as feathers, skin, or exoskeleton

Examples

annual moult
After its annual moult, the crab emerges with a larger, softer shell.
Many birds look scruffy during their moult in late summer.
The snake undergoes a moult several times a year as it grows.

Related Terms

  • synonym – molt: alternative spelling (American English) for the shedding process in animals
