tambour

noun
embroidery frame with hoops
Pronunciation: / ˈtæm.bʊr / (AmE), / ˈtæm.bʊə / (BrE)
A circular frame, often consisting of two hoops, used to hold fabric taut while embroidering.

Examples

embroidery tambour frame
A wooden tambour makes embroidery easier by holding the material taut.
She placed the cloth in the tambour to keep it steady while she embroidered.
The intricate design was stitched carefully onto the fabric stretched across the tambour.
