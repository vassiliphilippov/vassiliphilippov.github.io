to freak

verb
to lose control emotionally (informal)
Pronunciation: / friːk / (AmE), / friːk / (BrE)
(informal) To react very strongly or lose control of one's emotions, especially because of fear, shock, or anxiety.

Examples

to freak out suddenly
Don’t freak; we’ll find a solution if we work together.
He started to freak when he realized he had lost his passport at the airport.
She tried to stay calm but began to freak as the deadline approached.

  • synonym – panic: to suddenly feel intense fear or anxiety
