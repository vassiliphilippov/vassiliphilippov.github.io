clivers

noun
an annual plant with prickly stems
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪvərz / (AmE), / ˈklɪvə(z) / (BrE)
an annual plant with rough stems covered in curved prickles, found in North America, Europe, and Asia; also known as goosegrass or cleavers

Examples

clivers sticking to clothes
Children sometimes play by sticking clivers to their sleeves, amused by the plant's tiny hooks.
Gardeners often remove clivers because they spread rapidly and can choke other plants.
The hedgerow was thick with clivers, their stems clinging to my clothes as I walked past.
