apophatic

adjective
relating to describing God by negation
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈfætɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈfatɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the theological approach that describes God by stating what cannot be said about the divine nature, rather than by making positive assertions.

Examples

apophatic theology
Mystical writings often employ apophatic language to express the transcendence of the divine.
Some theologians prefer an apophatic approach to discussions of God's essence.
The apophatic tradition emphasizes what cannot be known or said about the divine.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cataphatic: relating to the approach of describing God by positive affirmations
