dystrophy

noun
degenerative disorder from poor nutrition
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrəfi / (BrE)
A disorder in which an organ or tissue degenerates because of inadequate or defective nutrition.

Examples

tissue dystrophy
Doctors are researching new treatments for various forms of dystrophy.
Muscular dystrophy causes progressive weakness in the muscles.
Nutritional dystrophy can develop if a child does not receive essential vitamins and minerals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – atrophy: the wasting away or decrease in size of an organ or tissue
  • antonym – hypertrophy: an abnormal enlargement of an organ or tissue
