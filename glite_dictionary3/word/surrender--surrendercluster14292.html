surrender

noun
admitting defeat; giving up
Pronunciation: / səˈrɛndɚ / (AmE), / səˈrɛndə / (BrE)
the act of giving up, admitting defeat, or handing over control or possession to someone else

Examples

surrender after defeat
Her speech was an open surrender to her opponent's superior argument.
Negotiations followed the army's surrender after months of conflict.
The enemy signaled their surrender by raising a white flag.
After weeks of negotiations, the company agreed to the surrender of its assets.
The surrender was formalized in a treaty signed by both sides.
The general announced the surrender of his troops after a long and costly siege.
After months of struggling with her illness, she felt a profound surrender to despair.
His face showed no anger, only a quiet surrender to the harsh reality of defeat.
The novel explores the surrender that comes when all hope is lost.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capitulation: an act of surrendering or ceasing to resist; act of giving up resistance
  • antonym – defiance: open or bold resistance; refusal to yield
  • synonym – resignation: acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable
  • antonym – resistance: refusal to accept or comply; attempt to prevent something by action or argument
