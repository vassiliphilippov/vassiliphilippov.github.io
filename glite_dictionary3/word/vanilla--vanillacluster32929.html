vanilla

noun
a tropical climbing plant used for flavoring
Pronunciation: / vəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the genus Vanilla, with fleshy leaves and clusters of large, fragrant white, green, or yellowish flowers, cultivated for its pods used in flavoring.

Examples

vanilla plant
Botanists classify vanilla among orchids because of its distinctive flowers.
Farmers carefully pollinate the flowers of the vanilla to ensure a good harvest of pods.
The vanilla plant thrives in the humid climates of Madagascar and Mexico.
