urea

noun
nitrogen compound found in urine, used in fertilizer
Pronunciation: / jʊˈriːə / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːə / (BrE)
A colorless, crystalline compound formed in the liver from ammonia and carbon dioxide, present in urine as the main nitrogenous waste, and widely used as a fertilizer and in manufacturing plastics and animal feeds.

Examples

urea fertilizer
Farmers often use urea as a nitrogen-rich fertilizer to promote healthy crop growth.
Synthetic urea is also a basic ingredient in the production of certain plastics.
The concentration of urea in the patient's blood was higher than normal, indicating possible kidney issues.
