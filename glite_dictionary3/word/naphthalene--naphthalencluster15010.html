naphthalene

noun
white crystalline hydrocarbon, used in mothballs
Pronunciation: / næfˈθælin / (AmE), / næfˈθæliːn / (BrE)
a white crystalline hydrocarbon with a strong odor, derived from coal tar or petroleum, used in organic synthesis and as a fumigant, especially in mothballs

Examples

naphthalene mothballs
Naphthalene is commonly found in mothballs used to protect clothing from moths.
In organic chemistry, naphthalene serves as a building block for more complex compounds.
The laboratory stored the naphthalene crystals in tightly sealed containers due to their pungent smell.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mothball: a small ball containing chemicals to repel moths and insects, often made of naphthalene
