to mastermind

verb
plan and direct a complex undertaking
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstərˌmaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəmaɪnd / (BrE)
to plan and direct a complex or difficult project or activity, especially one that is secret or illegal

Examples

to mastermind a scheme
Detectives believe he masterminded the entire operation from his prison cell.
She masterminded the fundraising campaign that saved the school from closure.
They accused him of masterminding a sophisticated fraud scheme.

Related Terms

  • antonym – foil: to prevent the success of (something planned)
  • synonym – orchestrate: to organize or arrange something carefully, especially a complex operation
  • synonym – plan: to decide on and arrange in advance
