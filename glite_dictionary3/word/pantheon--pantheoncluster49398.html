pantheon

noun
all the gods of a religion
Pronunciation: / ˈpænθiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈpanθɪən / (BrE)
all the gods of a particular religion or tradition regarded collectively.

Examples

pantheon of gods
Every religion has its own pantheon of figures, both benevolent and malevolent.
Greek mythology features a complex pantheon that has influenced literature and art for centuries.
In ancient Roman culture, the pantheon included Jupiter, Mars, Venus, and many other deities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deity: a god or goddess
  • synonym – polytheism: belief in or worship of more than one god
