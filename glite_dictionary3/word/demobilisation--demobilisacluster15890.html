demobilisation

noun
process of ending military mobilization
Pronunciation: / dɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of changing from a wartime footing to a peacetime footing, especially by discharging troops and reducing military operations

Examples

demobilisation of soldiers
After the war ended, the country began the long process of demobilisation.
Effective demobilisation requires careful planning to reintegrate troops into civilian life.
The sudden demobilisation of thousands of soldiers caused economic challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym – demobilization: American spelling: the process of disbanding armed forces and transitioning to peace
  • antonym – mobilisation: the process of preparing troops or resources for war
