to robe

verb
to dress in formal robes
Pronunciation: / roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
to dress or cover (someone or something) with a robe or in a formal or ceremonial way

Examples

to robe an actor
Before the play, the actors robe themselves in elaborate costumes backstage.
She robed her students in academic gowns for the graduation.
The bishop was robed in ornate vestments for the ceremony.
The ancient statue is robed in legends and stories from the past.
The mountain was robed in a thick blanket of mist by early morning.
The trees were robed in vibrant autumn colors each October.
