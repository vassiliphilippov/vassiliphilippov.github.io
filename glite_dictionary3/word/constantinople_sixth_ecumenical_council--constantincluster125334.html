Constantinople (Sixth Ecumenical Council)

noun
sixth ecumenical council, 680–681
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.stæn.tɪˈnoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stæn.tɪˈnəʊ.pəl / (BrE)
the sixth ecumenical council held in 680–681 in Constantinople, which condemned Monothelitism and defined that Christ has two wills, divine and human.
