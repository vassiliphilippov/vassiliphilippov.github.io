popular

adjective
of or by the people at large
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.pjələr / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
(of actions, institutions, etc.) carried on by or representing the people as a whole rather than a privileged group; of or relating to the populace at large.

Examples

popular vote
Constitutions of popular government aim to give power to ordinary citizens.
Many reforms were achieved through popular movements and widespread public participation.
The presidential election was decided by the popular vote rather than the electoral college.

  • synonym – democratic: relating to, or supporting democracy or its principles
