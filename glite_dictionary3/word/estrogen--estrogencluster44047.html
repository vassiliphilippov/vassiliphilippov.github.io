estrogen

noun
female steroid sex hormone
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstrədʒən / (AmE), / ˈiːstrədʒən / (BrE)
A group of steroid hormones produced mainly by the ovaries that promote the development and maintenance of female sexual characteristics.

Examples

estrogen levels in women
Certain cancers can be influenced by levels of estrogen in the body.
Hormone replacement therapy may include estrogen to alleviate menopausal symptoms.
Scientists are studying how estrogen affects bone density in postmenopausal women.
