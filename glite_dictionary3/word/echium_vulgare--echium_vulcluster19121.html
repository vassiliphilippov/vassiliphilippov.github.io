Echium vulgare

noun
a European plant with blue flowers (viper's bugloss)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛki.əm vʊlˈgɛəɹi / (AmE), / ˈɛki.əm vʊlˈgɛəri / (BrE)
A coarse, prickly European plant of the borage family with spikes of blue flowers, commonly known as viper's bugloss; it has become naturalized in North America.

Examples

Echium vulgare blue flowers
Echium vulgare is considered a weed in some regions, but valued as a wildflower in others.
Gardeners sometimes plant Echium vulgare for its striking spikes of color and drought tolerance.
The meadow was covered with Echium vulgare, its blue blossoms attracting bees all summer.
