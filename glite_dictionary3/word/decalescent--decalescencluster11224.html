decalescent

adjective
absorbing heat without temperature rise beyond a point
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəˈlɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəˈlɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) absorbing heat without an increase in temperature when heated beyond a certain point, typically used in metallurgy and physics.

Examples

decalescent steel reaction
At the decalescent point, steel absorbs heat but its temperature does not increase.
Some metals exhibit a decalescent property during certain phase changes.
The engineer monitored the decalescent behavior of the alloy during heating.
