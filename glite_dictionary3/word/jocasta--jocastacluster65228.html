Jocasta

proper noun
queen of Thebes in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / dʒoʊˈkæstə / (AmE), / dʒəʊˈkæstə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the queen of Thebes who, unknowingly, became the wife of her own son Oedipus.

Examples

Jocasta in Greek tragedy
In Sophocles' tragedy, Jocasta tries to soothe Oedipus's fears about prophecy.
Many modern plays reference Jocasta as a symbol of tragic fate.
The story of Jocasta and Oedipus is one of the most famous in Greek mythology.
