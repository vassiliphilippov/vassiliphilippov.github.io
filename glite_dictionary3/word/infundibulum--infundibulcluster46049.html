infundibulum

noun
funnel-shaped anatomical structure
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfənˈdɪbjələm / (AmE), / ˌɪnfʌnˈdɪbjʊləm / (BrE)
a funnel-shaped cavity or structure in the body, especially the stalk connecting the hypothalamus to the pituitary gland

Examples

the infundibulum of the brain
During surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the infundibulum of the fallopian tube.
The infundibulum connects the hypothalamus to the pituitary gland in the brain.
The respiratory system includes an infundibulum, which serves as a passageway for air in some animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – funnel: a tube or pipe that is wide at the top and narrow at the bottom, used for guiding liquid or powder into a small opening
