to befoul

verb
make dirty or pollute
Pronunciation: / bɪˈfaʊl / (AmE), / bɪˈfaʊl / (BrE)
to make dirty, impure, or polluted; to spoil by contamination

Examples

to befoul clean water
He accused them of trying to befoul his reputation with false rumors.
If you befoul this place again, you will not be welcome back.
The oil spill will befoul the pristine coastline.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cleanse: make something clean or pure
  • synonym – contaminate: make something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • synonym – pollute: make water, air, or soil dirty or harmful to people, animals, and plants
