dipstick

noun
rod for measuring liquid level
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪp.stɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɪp.stɪk / (BrE)
A rod marked with measurements, used by inserting it into a container to determine the level of liquid inside, especially in engines or machinery.

Examples

dipstick oil level
Check the oil level by pulling out the dipstick and wiping it clean.
He inserted the dipstick into the tank to see if more coolant was needed.
The mechanic showed me how to read the dipstick during my car’s service.
