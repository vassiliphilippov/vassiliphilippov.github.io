trenail

noun
wooden peg used to fasten timbers
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛneɪl / (BrE)
a wooden peg used to fasten timbers, especially in shipbuilding, which swells when wet to hold the timbers tightly together

Examples

trenail in shipbuilding
The shipwright hammered a trenail into place to secure the deck planks.
Traditional wooden ships often relied on trenails rather than metal fastenings.
Water would cause the trenail to swell, creating a tight and sturdy joint.

  • synonym – dowel: a pin, usually wooden, used to hold parts of a structure together
