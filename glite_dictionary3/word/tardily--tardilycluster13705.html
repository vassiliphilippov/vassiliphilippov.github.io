tardily

adverb
in a slow or delayed manner
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrdɪli /; / ˈtɑːrdɪli / (AmE), / ˈtɑːdɪli / (BrE)
in a slow, delayed, or late manner; not done quickly or on time

Examples

reply tardily to invitations
He grudgingly replied tardily to the invitations, well after the deadline had passed.
Spring came tardily that year, with the trees remaining bare into late April.
The group arrived tardily, missing the important announcement at the beginning of the event.
Awards were bestowed tardily, long after the achievements had taken place.
She responded tardily to the invitation, after all the seats were taken.
The report was submitted tardily, missing the official deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – late: at or after the expected or usual time
  • antonym – promptly: at the scheduled or correct time, without delay
  • antonym – quickly: at a fast speed; rapidly
  • synonym – slowly: at a slow speed; not quickly
