Athabaskan

noun
group of Native American languages
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈbæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈbæskən / (BrE)
a group of related Native American languages spoken in western North America, especially in Alaska, northwestern Canada, and the southwestern United States.

Examples

Athabaskan language family
Linguists have studied the similarities among Athabaskan languages across North America.
Many indigenous people of Alaska speak languages belonging to the Athabaskan family.
Navajo is one of the best-known Athabaskan languages.
