tune

noun
adjustment to a required frequency
Pronunciation: / tuːn /; / tun / (AmE), / tjuːn / (BrE)
the process or state of being properly adjusted for correct operation or sound (such as in music or electronics)

Examples

tune of the radio
For better reception, you need to check the tune of your device.
He carefully adjusted the tune on his receiver to listen to his favorite station.
The tune of the radio was slightly off, making the signal unclear.
After tuning her violin, she played beautifully and every note was perfectly in tune.
If a guitar string is loose, it won't produce a sound that is in tune.
The choir struggled to stay in tune during the rehearsal.
