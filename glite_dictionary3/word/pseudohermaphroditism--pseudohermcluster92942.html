pseudohermaphroditism

noun
congenital mismatch of external and internal sex organs
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˌhɜːrməˈfrɒdəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˌhɜːməˈfrɒdɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
A congenital medical condition in which a person's external genitalia do not match their internal sex organs.

Examples

congenital pseudohermaphroditism condition
Medical literature from the early 20th century often classified certain intersex conditions as pseudohermaphroditism.
The child was diagnosed with pseudohermaphroditism after doctors noticed a discrepancy between the internal and external reproductive organs.
Understanding pseudohermaphroditism requires expertise in both endocrinology and genetics.
