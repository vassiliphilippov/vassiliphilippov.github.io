contingent

noun
a representative group of people
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪndʒənt / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənt / (BrE)
a group of people representing a larger organization or country, especially at a meeting, event, or assembly

Examples

contingent from Brazil
A French contingent arrived at the summit to participate in the climate discussions.
Each company contributed a small contingent of experts to the industry taskforce.
The university sent a large contingent to compete in the championships.
