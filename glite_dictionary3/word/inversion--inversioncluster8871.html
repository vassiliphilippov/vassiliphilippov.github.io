inversion

noun
genetic mutation reversing gene order in a chromosome
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) a type of mutation where a section of a chromosome breaks off, reverses its orientation, and reattaches, resulting in the reversal of the gene order.

Examples

chromosome inversion
Genetic counselors explained how an inversion could lead to inherited disorders within the family.
Researchers detected a rare inversion in the patient's chromosome 9 using advanced sequencing techniques.
The scientist discovered a chromosomal inversion that affected the development of the fruit fly.
