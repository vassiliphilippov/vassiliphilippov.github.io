panegyric

adjective
formally expressing praise
Pronunciation: / ˌpænəˈdʒɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpanɪˈdʒɪrɪk / (BrE)
(of language or writing) formally expressing praise; highly laudatory.

Examples

panegyric language
His panegyric remarks about the retiring professor were met with a standing ovation.
Many reviewers found the book's panegyric tone excessive and insincere.
The letter was written in a highly panegyric style, extolling the achievements of the organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym – critical: expressing adverse or disapproving comments
  • synonym – laudatory: expressing praise or commendation
