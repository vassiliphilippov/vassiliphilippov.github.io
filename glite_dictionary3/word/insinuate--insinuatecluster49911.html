to insinuate

verb
suggest or hint indirectly
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɪn.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪn.jʊ.eɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or hint at something in an indirect or unpleasant way

Examples

to insinuate a hidden meaning
Are you trying to insinuate that I was at fault for the mistake?
He kept insinuating that my report was inaccurate, though he never said so directly.
She insinuated her doubts about his loyalty without saying it outright.

Related Terms

  • synonym – imply: to suggest something without stating it directly
  • antonym – state: to express something directly or clearly
  • synonym – suggest: to mention an idea, possible plan, or action for other people to consider
