webworm

noun
caterpillar that spins webs on foliage
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛbˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈwɛbˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A caterpillar of various moth species that spins webs over the leaves of trees or plants while feeding on them, often causing damage to foliage.

Examples

webworm infestation
During summer months, large clusters of webworms can be seen on the leaves of fruit trees.
Farmers worry that webworm infestations can cause serious damage to crops and ornamental plants.
The oak tree in the backyard was covered with webworms feeding between layers of silk.
