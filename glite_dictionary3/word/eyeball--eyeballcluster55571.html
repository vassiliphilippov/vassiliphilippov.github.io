to eyeball

verb
look at closely or directly
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to look at or examine closely or with interest, often in a direct or noticeable way

Examples

to eyeball a distance
Before buying the house, they eyeballed every inch of it.
She eyeballed the new student as he walked into the classroom.
The coach eyeballed the players during warm-ups to see who was ready to start.

Related Terms

  • antonym – glance: to look quickly or briefly
  • synonym – stare: to look at someone or something fixedly
