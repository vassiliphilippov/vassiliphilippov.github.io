to bastardise

verb
to spoil or corrupt by reducing quality
Pronunciation: / ˈbæstɚˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədaɪz / (BrE)
to spoil or corrupt something, especially by reducing its quality, value, or purity

Examples

to bastardise a tradition
Adding too many modern elements can bastardise a classic recipe.
Some critics argue that mass production has bastardised traditional craftsmanship.
The translation was so loose that it bastardised the original meaning of the poem.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrupt: to make something less pure or honest
  • synonym – debase: to reduce something in quality or value
