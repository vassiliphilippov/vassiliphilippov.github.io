carinate

noun
bird with a keeled breastbone
Pronunciation: / ˈkær.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkær.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
A bird having a keeled breastbone, allowing attachment of flight muscles; commonly refers to birds in the subclass Carinatae.

Examples

carinate bird species
The fossil record shows a clear distinction between carinates and flightless birds.
The majority of living birds are classified as carinates due to their keeled breastbones.
Unlike ratites, carinates possess a breastbone that supports powerful flight muscles.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ratite: flightless bird lacking a keeled breastbone
