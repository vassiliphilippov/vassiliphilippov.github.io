to bootlick

verb
to flatter excessively for favor
Pronunciation: / ˈbuˌtlɪk / (AmE), / ˈbuːtlɪk / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone in authority by giving them excessive praise or being overly submissive

Examples

to bootlick the boss
He refused to bootlick his superiors, even if it meant missing out on a promotion.
Instead of standing up for herself, she chose to bootlick the manager at every meeting.
Some politicians will bootlick influential donors to secure support for their campaigns.

Related Terms

  • antonym – defy: openly resist or refuse to obey
  • synonym – flatter: to give excessive or insincere praise
  • synonym – kowtow: to act in an excessively subservient manner
