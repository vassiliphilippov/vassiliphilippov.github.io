to shirr

verb
to bake eggs until set
Pronunciation: / ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
To bake eggs, typically without their shells, in a shallow dish until the whites are set and the yolks remain soft.

Examples

shirr eggs in oven
He decided to shirr some eggs for brunch instead of scrambling them.
If you shirr the eggs just right, the yolks will be perfectly runny.
She likes to shirr eggs with a dash of cream for a special breakfast.

  • synonym – bake: to cook food by dry heat in an oven or on a heated surface
