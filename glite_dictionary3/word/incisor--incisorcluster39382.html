incisor

noun
front tooth for cutting or gnawing
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪzə / (BrE)
a tooth at the front of the mouth, adapted for cutting or gnawing, found in both the upper and lower jaws of mammals

Examples

front incisor tooth
A child may lose an incisor around the age of six or seven.
Rabbits use their sharp incisors to gnaw through tough vegetation.
The dentist explained that my incisor was chipped and would need repair.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – canine: a pointed tooth next to the incisors
  • degree higher – molar: a large, flat tooth at the back of the mouth used for grinding food
