to thump

verb
make a heavy, dull sound
Pronunciation: / θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
to hit something or someone heavily, making a dull sound

Examples

to thump the table
Heavy rain thumped against the window all night.
The book thumped onto the floor when it slipped from her hands.
We heard his heart thump loudly in the quiet room.
He could hear someone thumping on the wall next door.
She thumped the table in frustration, making the glasses rattle.
The boxer thumped his opponent with a powerful right hook.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – tap: to strike lightly and quickly
  • synonym – thud: to make a dull, heavy sound
