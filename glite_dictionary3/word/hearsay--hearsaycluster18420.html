hearsay

noun
unverified rumor or information
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪrˌseɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɪəseɪ / (BrE)
information received from other people that one cannot adequately verify; rumor or unverified report passed from person to person.

Examples

hearsay and rumors
Much of the news about the celebrity's private life is based on hearsay rather than direct evidence.
The court ruled the statement was inadmissible because it was considered hearsay.
Try not to trust hearsay; always check the facts before spreading information.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fact: a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym – gossip: casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about people, typically unverified
  • synonym – rumor: a currently circulating story or report of uncertain or doubtful truth
