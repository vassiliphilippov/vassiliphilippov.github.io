barrel

noun
a unit of liquid capacity
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
a standard unit of measurement for liquids or other substances, based on the amount a barrel can hold (varies by substance and country)

Examples

barrel of oil
A standard American beer barrel holds 31 gallons.
Last year, the company produced 10,000 barrels of wine.
The price of oil is often quoted per barrel on international markets.
A barrel of beer contains more than thirty gallons.
Oil prices are usually quoted in dollars per barrel.
The tanker carried 500,000 barrels of crude oil across the ocean.
