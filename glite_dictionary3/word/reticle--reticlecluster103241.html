reticle

noun
markings in the eyepiece of an optical instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
a network of fine lines, dots, or crosshairs placed in the eyepiece of an optical instrument to help with alignment or measurement

Examples

reticle in a microscope
Modern microscopes often include a reticle to help users make precise measurements.
She adjusted the reticle to improve the rifle's accuracy during the competition.
Through the scope, the target lined up perfectly with the reticle.
