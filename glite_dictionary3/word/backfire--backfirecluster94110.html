to backfire

verb
set a fire to stop another fire
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfaɪə / (BrE)
to set a controlled fire in order to stop the spread of an advancing forest or prairie fire

Examples

to backfire forest
Before the wind shifted, they managed to backfire a protective line through the dry grass.
In some cases, teams will backfire large areas ahead of an advancing blaze.
The firefighters decided to backfire in order to prevent the wildfire from reaching the nearby town.

  • synonym – firebreak: an obstacle cleared to stop a fire spreading
