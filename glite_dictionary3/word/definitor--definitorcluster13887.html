definitor

noun
council member in religious order
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɪnɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪnɪtə / (BrE)
A member of a council in a religious order who assists and advises the superiors general or provincial superior in making decisions.

Examples

definitor of a monastery
All new appointments were discussed by the definitors before final approval was granted.
In the election of a new abbot, the definitors played a crucial consultative role.
The definitors met twice a year to advise the provincial superior on matters of governance.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – superior general: the chief official in a religious order
