V

noun
unit of electric potential (volt)
Pronunciation: / viː / (AmE), / viː / (BrE)
the symbol for volt, a unit of electric potential equal to the potential difference across a conductor when a current of one ampere dissipates one watt of power

Examples

V on a battery
Batteries are often labeled with their output in V, such as 1.5 V or 9 V.
The resistance of the wire was 20 ohms, so at 2 amps the voltage was 40 V.
To power the device, you need a power supply that provides at least 12 V.
