fat

adjective
thick or wide in diameter
Pronunciation: / fæt / (AmE), / fæt / (BrE)
(of an object) having a relatively large diameter; thick or wide in shape.

Examples

fat cable
A fat cable ran across the floor, connecting the computers.
She tied the packages together with a fat rope that wouldn't break easily.
The old novel was bound with a fat leather spine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thick: having a large distance between opposite sides
  • antonym – thin: having a small distance between opposite sides; not thick
