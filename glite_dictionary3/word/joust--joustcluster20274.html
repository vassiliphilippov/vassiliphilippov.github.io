to joust

verb
compete verbally; debate
Pronunciation: / d\u0292a\u028ast / (AmE), / d\u0292a\u028ast / (BrE)
to take part in a competitive contest of ideas, arguments, or influence; to debate or spar verbally, often in a public or formal setting.

Examples

to joust in debate
In the annual debate, the candidates joust for support from swing voters.
Two columnists regularly joust in print over the government's economic policies.
Opposing lawyers jousted in court with sharp questions and confident claims.

Related Terms

  • synonym – debate: to discuss a subject formally, especially in public, with opposing arguments.
  • synonym – spar: to engage in a short, usually verbal, argument or exchange of ideas.
