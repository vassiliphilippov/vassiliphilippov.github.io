to palliate

verb
lessen the seriousness of something
Pronunciation: / ˈpælieɪt / (AmE), / ˈpalɪeɪt / (BrE)
to make the effects of something, especially something bad, seem less serious, often without removing the cause; to alleviate or moderate

Examples

to palliate guilt
Although nothing could change the outcome, his apology helped palliate her feelings of hurt.
Doctors may try to palliate the symptoms of a disease instead of curing it completely.
She attempted to palliate his anger by explaining the circumstances calmly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exacerbate: to make a problem, situation, or feeling worse
  • synonym – mitigate: to make something less severe, serious, or painful
