demineralization

noun
abnormal loss of mineral salts
Pronunciation: / diˌmɪnərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌmɪnəraɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the abnormal loss of mineral salts, especially from bone, often resulting in weakening or disease

Examples

bone demineralization
After prolonged immobilization, patients may experience demineralization due to lack of weight-bearing activity.
Fluoridated water can help prevent dental demineralization in children.
Osteoporosis is associated with significant demineralization of the bones, increasing the risk of fractures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – remineralization: the restoration of mineral content, especially in teeth or bones
