ciliated

adjective
having hairlike projections (cilia)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlieɪtɪd / (BrE)
having fine, hairlike projections (cilia) along the margin or surface, especially in cells or tissues

Examples

ciliated cells
Ciliated epithelium lines many parts of the human body for movement of substances.
Certain cells in the respiratory tract are ciliated, helping to move mucus out of the lungs.
The surface of the organism appeared ciliated under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unciliated: lacking hairlike projections (cilia)
