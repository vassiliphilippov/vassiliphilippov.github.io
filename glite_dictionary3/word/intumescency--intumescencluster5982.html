intumescency

noun
swelling caused by fluid or blood accumulation
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.ən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuˈmɛs.ən.si / (BrE)
The swelling of a body part caused by the accumulation of blood or other fluids, especially as a result of congestion.

Examples

intumescency of tissue from fluid
Chronic congestion can result in persistent intumescency of the affected tissues.

The presence of intumescency in the nasal membranes was noted during the medical examination.
