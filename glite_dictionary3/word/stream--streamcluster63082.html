to stream

verb
wave or flow outward (as in wind)
Pronunciation: / striːm / (AmE), / striːm / (BrE)
to extend, wave, or float outward in a long, flowing manner, as if blown by the wind

Examples

to stream in the wind
Golden hair streamed out behind the cyclist as he sped down the road.
Her long scarf streamed in the wind as she ran down the street.
The flag streamed behind the boat as it raced across the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flow: to move along smoothly and continuously
  • synonym – trail: to hang or drag loosely along a surface
