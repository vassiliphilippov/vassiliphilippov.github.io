extenuation

noun
making an offense seem less serious
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛnjʊˈeɪʃ(ə)n /; / ɪkˌstɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making a fault or wrongdoing seem less serious or more forgivable, often by giving excuses or explanations
