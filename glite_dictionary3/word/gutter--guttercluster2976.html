to gutter

verb
burn or flicker unsteadily (of flame)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
(of a flame or light) to burn or glow unsteadily, often flickering or wavering feebly.

Examples

a guttering candle
As the oil lamp guttered, the room was plunged into darkness.
The candle began to gutter as the wind crept in through the open window.
The dying embers guttered in the hearth until only a faint glow remained.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flicker: to burn or shine with a wavering light
