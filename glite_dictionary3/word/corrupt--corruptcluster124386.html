to corrupt

verb
to bribe or influence dishonestly
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to persuade someone to act dishonestly by giving them money or other incentives; to bribe.

Examples

to corrupt a judge
He was arrested after attempting to corrupt a police officer with a large sum of money.
The company tried to corrupt government officials to win the contract.
They were accused of trying to corrupt the judge to secure a favorable verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bribe: to dishonestly persuade someone to act in one's favor by a gift of money or other inducement
