epizoic

adjective
living on body of an animal (as parasite)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism) living or growing on the external surface of an animal, typically as a parasite.

Examples

epizoic algae on fish
An epizoic fungus can harm the health of its animal host.
Some barnacles are epizoic, attaching themselves to the shells of turtles.
The biologist studied an epizoic algae species growing on fish skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ectoparasitic: living on the outside of another organism as a parasite
  • antonym – endoparasitic: living inside the body of another organism as a parasite
