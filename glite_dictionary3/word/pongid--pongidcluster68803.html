pongid

noun
large anthropoid ape (former Pongidae family)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑn.dʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.dʒɪd / (BrE)
any of the large anthropoid apes formerly classified in the family Pongidae, including orangutans, gorillas, and chimpanzees.

Examples

In the 20th century, gorillas and chimpanzees were often classified as pongids.
Recent studies question the relevance of the taxonomic group pongid in modern primatology.
The museum exhibit featured fossils of several extinct pongids from Africa and Asia.
