bonduc

noun
tree with pods used as coffee substitute
Pronunciation: / bɒnˈdʌk / (AmE), / bɒnˈdʌk / (BrE)
A handsome tree native to central and eastern North America, notable for its large bipinnate leaves, green-white flowers, and large woody brown pods containing seeds sometimes used as a coffee substitute.

Examples

bonduc tree pods
Botanists admire the unusual pods and foliage of the bonduc tree.
Several bonducs grew along the riverbank, their woody pods dangling from the branches.
The seeds of the bonduc are sometimes roasted and used as a substitute for coffee.
