fuddy-duddy

noun
old-fashioned or overly conservative person
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi / (AmE), / ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi / (BrE)
a person, especially an older one, who is considered old-fashioned, fussy, or overly conservative in attitude or appearance

Examples

old fuddy-duddy
Don't be a fuddy-duddy; try joining us for salsa dancing tonight!
In her conservative clothes and stern attitude, she was sometimes called the office fuddy-duddy.
My uncle is such a fuddy-duddy when it comes to technology and prefers handwritten letters over emails.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modernist: a person who advocates new ideas or behaviors
  • synonym – square: a person considered conventional and old-fashioned
  • synonym – stick-in-the-mud: a person who is reluctant to try new things; very conservative
