judgement

noun
the act of forming an opinion or decision
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of forming an opinion, evaluation, or decision about someone or something

Examples

personal judgement
He trusted his friend's judgement when making important career decisions.
Her judgement of the situation proved to be accurate and insightful.
Many people questioned the coach's judgement after the team's unexpected loss.
After careful consideration, the committee reached a judgement on the winner of the prize.
Her judgement was influenced by her previous experiences in similar situations.
It takes experience and knowledge to make a fair judgement in difficult cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assessment: the act of evaluating or appraising a person or situation
  • synonym – decision: a conclusion or resolution reached after consideration
  • antonym – indecision: inability to decide; hesitation
  • synonym – opinion: a belief or conclusion held with confidence but not necessarily substantiated
  • antonym – prejudice: preconceived opinion not based on reason or actual experience
