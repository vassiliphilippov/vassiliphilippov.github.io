Apollo

proper noun
Greek god of light, arts, and prophecy
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːloʊ / (AmE), / əˈpɒləʊ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of light, prophecy, poetry, music, and healing; the son of Zeus and Leto, and twin brother of Artemis.

Examples

Apollo the Greek god
In ancient myths, Apollo was revered as the god of music and poetry.
Many Renaissance artists depicted Apollo holding a lyre and surrounded by muses.
The temple of Apollo at Delphi was famous for its oracle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Artemis: Greek goddess, twin sister of Apollo
  • synonym – Phoebus: another name for Apollo, often used in poetry
  • synonym – Zeus: the chief god in Greek mythology, father of Apollo
