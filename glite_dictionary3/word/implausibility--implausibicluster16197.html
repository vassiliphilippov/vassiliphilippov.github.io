implausibility

noun
state of being unlikely to believe
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌplɔːzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌplɔːzɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unlikely or difficult to believe; lack of credibility.

Examples

the implausibility of a story
Critics pointed out the implausibility of the film's plot twists.
She was struck by the implausibility of his latest excuse.
The implausibility of his alibi made the jury doubt his innocence.
