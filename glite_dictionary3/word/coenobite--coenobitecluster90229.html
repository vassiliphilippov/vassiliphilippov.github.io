coenobite

noun
member of a communal religious order
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːnəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsiːnəbaɪt / (BrE)
A member of a religious community who lives with others according to a common rule, especially a monk living in a monastery.

Examples

coenobite in a monastery
The ancient monastery is still inhabited by a single coenobite dedicated to prayer.
The strict schedule of the coenobite left little time for personal pursuits.
Unlike an anchorite, a coenobite prefers the company of fellow monks and communal living.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anchorite: a religious recluse who lives in solitude
  • synonym – monk: a male member of a monastic religious order
