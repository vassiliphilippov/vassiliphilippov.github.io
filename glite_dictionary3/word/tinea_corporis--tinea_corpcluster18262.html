tinea corporis

noun
fungal skin infection (not scalp/groin)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪniə ˈkɔrpərɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɪniə ˈkɒpərɪs / (BrE)
A fungal infection of the nonhairy areas of the skin, commonly known as ringworm of the body.

Examples

tinea corporis infection
Athletes are more likely to develop tinea corporis due to frequent skin contact and shared equipment.
Proper hygiene can help prevent the spread of tinea corporis in schools and locker rooms.
