Moschus

noun
genus of musk deer
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑskəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒskəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, hornless deer native to Asia, known as musk deer, whose males produce musk used in perfumery.

Examples

Moschus deer species
Scientists are working to protect Moschus populations from illegal hunting.
The Moschus species are prized for the musk obtained from the males.
Unlike other deer, Moschus lacks antlers but is distinguished by its long canine teeth.
