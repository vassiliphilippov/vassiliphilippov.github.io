iceberg

noun
large floating mass of ice at sea
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsˌbɝɡ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A large floating mass of ice, detached from a glacier, found in the sea.

Examples

a huge iceberg floats
Many icebergs break off from Antarctica each year and drift into shipping lanes.
Only a small part of an iceberg is visible above the water's surface.
The ship struck an iceberg and began to sink rapidly.

  • synonym – glacier: a large slow-moving mass of ice on land
