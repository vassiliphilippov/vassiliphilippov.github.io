supercharger

noun
device supplying extra air to an engine
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌtʃɑrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈsuːpətʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
A device that increases the pressure or amount of air supplied to an internal-combustion engine, boosting its power output.

Examples

supercharger boosts engine
Installing a supercharger can improve an engine's performance and efficiency.
Mechanics inspected the supercharger for signs of wear and tear after each race.
The sports car's supercharger gives it a significant boost in acceleration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – turbocharger: a device that increases engine power by using exhaust gases to drive a turbine
