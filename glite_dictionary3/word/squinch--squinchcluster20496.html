to squinch

verb
draw back in fear or pain
Pronunciation: / skwɪntʃ / (AmE), / skwɪntʃ / (BrE)
to flinch, crouch, or draw your body together, especially because of pain, fear, or discomfort

Examples

to squinch in pain
He would always squinch at the sound of thunder during storms.
She couldn't help but squinch when the doctor approached with the needle.
The little boy squinched as the cold water splashed over him.
He squinches up every time someone shouts near him.
She squinched down behind the couch to avoid being seen.
The child squinched in fear as the thunder crashed outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crouch: to bend your knees and lower your body close to the ground
  • synonym – flinch: to make a quick, nervous movement as an instinctive reaction to surprise, fear or pain
  • synonym – squat: to sit in a position with your knees bent and your bottom near the ground
  • synonym – wince: to give a slight, involuntary shrinking movement of the body out of pain or distress
