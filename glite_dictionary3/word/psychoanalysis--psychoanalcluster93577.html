psychoanalysis

noun
Freudian therapy method for mental disorders
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊəˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊəˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
A system of psychological theory and therapy that aims to treat mental disorders by investigating the interaction of conscious and unconscious elements in the mind, based on the theories of Sigmund Freud.

Examples

undergo psychoanalysis therapy
Her doctor suggested psychoanalysis as a way to address her persistent anxiety.
Sigmund Freud is widely regarded as the founder of psychoanalysis.
Some people spend years in psychoanalysis to better understand themselves.

Related Terms

  • antonym – behavior therapy: therapy focusing on changing unwanted behaviors
  • synonym – psychotherapy: treatment of mental disorders through psychological means
