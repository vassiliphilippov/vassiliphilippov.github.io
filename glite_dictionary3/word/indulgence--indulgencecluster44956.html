indulgence

noun
remission of punishment for sins in Catholicism
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a remission of the temporal punishment still due for sins after absolution, granted by the pope or the Church.

Examples

buy a church indulgence
Many people in the Middle Ages purchased indulgences in hopes of reducing their time in purgatory.
Martin Luther criticized the sale of indulgences in his famous Ninety-five Theses.
The church granted an indulgence to all who made the pilgrimage to Rome.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absolution: formal release from guilt, obligation, or punishment, especially in a religious context
