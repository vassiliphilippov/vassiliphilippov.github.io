beta-lipoprotein

noun
cholesterol-transporting lipoprotein in blood
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪtə ˌlaɪpəˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə ˌlɪpəˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a lipoprotein in the blood that transports cholesterol and has a high cholesterol content; high levels are associated with increased risk of heart disease and atherosclerosis

Examples

beta-lipoprotein in blood
Diet and exercise can help lower beta-lipoprotein and reduce heart disease risk.
Doctors often measure beta-lipoprotein concentration when assessing cardiovascular health.
High levels of beta-lipoprotein in the blood are linked to an increased risk of atherosclerosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – alpha-lipoprotein: a different type of blood lipoprotein, involved in cholesterol transport, known as HDL
  • synonym – low-density lipoprotein: a type of beta-lipoprotein involved in cholesterol transport, known as LDL
