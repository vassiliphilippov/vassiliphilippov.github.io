floodgate

noun
gate or valve controlling water flow
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌdˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɡeɪt / (BrE)
a gate or valve used to control the flow of water (such as in a dam or sluice); also, something that controls or releases a large outpouring of anything

Examples

open the floodgate
Authorities monitored the floodgate closely during the storm to manage potential flooding.
Heavy rainfall forced workers to open the floodgate to prevent the dam from overflowing.
The old mill was built next to a stream, with a floodgate to control the water supply.
Her resignation acted as a floodgate for further complaints against the management.
Lifting the restrictions opened the floodgates to a wave of new applications.
The collapse of the dam's floodgate caused water to pour into the valley.
