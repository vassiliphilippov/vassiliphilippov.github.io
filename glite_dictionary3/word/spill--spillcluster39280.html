to spill

verb
let wind out of a sail (nautical)
Pronunciation: / spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
(of a sailor) to reduce the pressure of wind on a sail, typically by adjusting it or letting out the sheet.

Examples

to spill the sail
By spilling the wind from the jib, she slowed the boat efficiently.
Skilled sailors can quickly spill the sails to prevent capsizing in a sudden gust.
The captain ordered the crew to spill the mainsail as the wind picked up.
