knock-kneed

adjective
having knees close and ankles apart
Pronunciation: / nɑk nid / (AmE), / nɒk niːd / (BrE)
having the knees bent inward so that they touch or are abnormally close together while the ankles remain apart; affected by knock-knee.

Examples

knock-kneed legs
As she walked down the aisle, her knock-kneed stance was noticeable to the observers.
Some children are slightly knock-kneed at a young age, but often outgrow it with time.
The medical examination revealed that he was knock-kneed, requiring special orthotic shoes.

  • antonym – bow-legged: having legs that curve outward at the knees
