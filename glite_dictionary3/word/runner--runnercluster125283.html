runner

noun
person who smuggles goods to avoid duties
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who imports or exports goods without paying customs duties; a smuggler

Examples

drug runner
During the prohibition era, a runner could make a lot of money dodging the law.
The authorities arrested the runner at the border with a trunk full of contraband.
The coast guard is constantly on the lookout for a runner trying to evade inspection.
