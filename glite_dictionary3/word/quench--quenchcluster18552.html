to quench

verb
cool hot metal rapidly (metallurgy)
Pronunciation: / kwɛntʃ / (AmE), / kwɛntʃ / (BrE)
to cool hot metal rapidly by immersing it in cold water or another liquid, especially to harden the metal

Examples

to quench hot metal
After heating the metal, they quenched it in oil to achieve the desired properties.
Properly quenching steel improves its toughness and durability.
The blacksmith will quench the red-hot blade in water to harden it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – temper: to heat and then cool metal to achieve desired hardness
