to act

verb
have an effect; produce a result
Pronunciation: / ækt / (AmE), / ækt / (BrE)
to do something, take action, or have an effect

Examples

to act quickly
The new cleaning solution can act very quickly on tough stains.
The new security system failed to act when the alarm was triggered.
This drug will act almost immediately once it enters your bloodstream.
The government needs to act quickly to address the crisis.
When the fire alarm sounded, everyone acted immediately to evacuate the building.
You should always think carefully before you act.

Related Terms

  • antonym – do nothing: to not do anything at all
  • synonym – move: to take action, to do something
  • degree lower – react: to act in response to something that happened
  • synonym – work: to be effective or successful in producing a result
