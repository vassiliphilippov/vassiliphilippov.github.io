Pamlico

noun
member of an Algonquian people of North Carolina
Pronunciation: / ˈpæm.lɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpæm.lɪ.kəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the Algonquian people who formerly lived in the valley of the Pamlico River in North Carolina, USA

Examples

the Pamlico people
Archaeologists have uncovered pottery made by the Pamlico in eastern North Carolina.
The Pamlico were known for their fishing and farming along the river valley.
Throughout the 18th century, the Pamlico faced displacement from their ancestral lands.
