to confirm

verb
establish with new evidence
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɜrm /; / kənˈfɝːm / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to show that something is true or correct, or to make a belief, feeling, or quality stronger or more certain

Examples

to confirm the facts
I need one more source to confirm this information.
New research confirms the effectiveness of the treatment.
The witness's testimony confirmed the suspect's alibi.
His calm response confirmed their faith in his leadership.
The coach's words confirmed the team's determination to win.
The experience helped to confirm her resolve to succeed.
Her success confirmed her belief that hard work pays off.
The new evidence confirmed the police's suspicions about the suspect.
The results of the experiment confirmed the scientist's initial hypothesis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contradict: to assert the opposite of a statement; to deny the truth
  • synonym – corroborate: to support or strengthen with additional evidence
  • antonym – refute: to prove that something is wrong or false
  • synonym – strengthen: to make something stronger or more forceful
