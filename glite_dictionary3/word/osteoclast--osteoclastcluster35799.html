osteoclast

noun
bone-resorbing cell
Pronunciation: / ˈɑstiəˌklæst / (AmE), / ˈɒstiəʊˌklæst / (BrE)
a type of cell that breaks down and absorbs bone tissue

Examples

osteoclast breaks bone
An osteoclast is responsible for breaking down old bone during bone remodeling.
Excessive activity of the osteoclast can lead to weakened bones and osteoporosis.
Research has shown that certain hormones can regulate osteoclast function.
