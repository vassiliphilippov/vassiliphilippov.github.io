Robespierre

proper noun
French revolutionary leader (1758–1794)
Pronunciation: / roʊ.bəsˈpjɛr / (AmE), / rəʊ.bɛsˈpjɛː / (BrE)
(1758–1794) French revolutionary leader, prominent member of the Jacobins and principal figure during the Reign of Terror, who was eventually executed in a coup d'état.

Examples

Robespierre led revolution
During the French Revolution, Robespierre played a central role in establishing the Committee of Public Safety.
Many historians debate whether Robespierre was a visionary reformer or a ruthless dictator.
The trial and execution of Robespierre marked the end of the Reign of Terror.
