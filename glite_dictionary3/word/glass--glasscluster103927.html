to glass

verb
become glassy or glass-like in appearance
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
(of eyes or a surface) to become glassy in appearance or to take on a glass-like sheen.

Examples

eyes glass over
As the medication took effect, his eyes began to glass over.
Her expression would glass when she was deep in thought, staring at nothing in particular.
The surface appeared to glass as the frost formed overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glaze: (of eyes) to become dull, fixed, or glassy
