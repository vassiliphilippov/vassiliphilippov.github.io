takeout

noun
in bridge, a bid prompting partner to change suit
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈteɪk.aʊt / (BrE)
(in bridge) a bid made with the intention of prompting one's partner to bid another suit.

Examples

takeout bid bridge
His takeout bid signaled that he wanted his partner to switch suits.
She explained that a takeout shows support for the unbid suits in bridge.
When you have a strong hand but no support for your partner's suit, a takeout is a good strategy.
