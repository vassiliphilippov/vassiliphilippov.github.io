pitcher

noun
specialized leaf of some carnivorous plants
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə / (BrE)
(Botany) A leaf modified to form a deep cavity or tube, often used by certain plants to trap insects.

Examples

pitcher plant leaf
Some carnivorous plants have evolved a pitcher to capture and digest prey.
The pitcher of the plant trapped several insects overnight.
The brightly colored pitcher attracts insects with the promise of nectar.
