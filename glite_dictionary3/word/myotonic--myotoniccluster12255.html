myotonic

adjective
relating to myotonia (muscle relaxation disorder)
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪəˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by myotonia, a condition in which muscles are slow to relax after contraction.

Examples

myotonic disorder symptoms
Myotonic discharges were observed during the electromyography exam.
Certain genetic conditions can cause a myotonic response in skeletal muscles.
The patient was diagnosed with myotonic dystrophy following a series of muscle tests.
