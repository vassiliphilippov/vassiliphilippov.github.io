depressant

noun
drug reducing nervous system activity
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsənt / (BrE)
A drug or substance that lowers the activity of the nervous system and reduces excitability or calms a person.

Examples

depressant drug
Alcohol acts as a depressant on the central nervous system.
Doctors warn that mixing different depressant drugs can be dangerous.
Some anxiety medications are classified as depressants because they slow brain activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stimulant: a drug that increases nervous system activity
