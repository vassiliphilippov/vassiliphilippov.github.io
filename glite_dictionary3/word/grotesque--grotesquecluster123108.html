grotesque

noun
decorative art with fantastic mixed forms
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
a style of decorative art featuring fantastical or mythical figures, often combining human, animal, and plant forms in incongruous ways

Examples

medieval grotesque art
He was fascinated by the intricate grotesque found on the ancient manuscript's border.
Renaissance artists often painted grotesques to decorate the walls and vaults of palaces.
The cathedral's ceiling was adorned with elaborate grotesques of mythical creatures and vines.
