thrip

noun
small plant-feeding insect; crop pest
Pronunciation: / θrɪp / (AmE), / θrɪp / (BrE)
A small, slender insect with narrow, fringed wings, many species of which feed on plant sap and can be destructive to crops.

Examples

thrip infestation
A severe infestation of thrip can cause significant damage to greenhouse plants.
The farmer sprayed his crops to protect them from thrip and other pests.
Under the microscope, the delicate wings of a thrip are clearly visible.
