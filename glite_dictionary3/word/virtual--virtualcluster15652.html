virtual

adjective
almost; in effect but not in fact
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝː.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
almost or nearly as described, but not completely or according to strict definition; existing or happening in effect, but not in fact or name

Examples

virtual certainty
Despite being separated by thousands of miles, they maintained a virtual friendship through online communication.
The new policy resulted in a virtual ban on smoking in public places.
The organization has a virtual monopoly on supplying water to the town.
After such a long drought, the crops were in virtual ruin.
Owing to the severe budget cuts, the project was in virtual collapse.
With their lead in the polls, the candidate was a virtual certainty to win the election.

Related Terms

  • antonym – actual: existing in fact; real; opposite of virtual
  • synonym – nominal: existing in name only; not real or actual
