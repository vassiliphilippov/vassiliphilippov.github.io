softness

noun
declining state of the economy
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔft.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒft.nəs / (BrE)
a period or condition of economic decline or weakness, marked by reduced demand or activity

Examples

softness in the market
Analysts blame the softness in consumer demand for the industry's slow growth this year.
Retail sales showed unexpected softness last quarter, worrying many investors.
The company struggled to maintain profits during a period of economic softness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – boom: a period of rapid economic growth
  • synonym – slump: a period when business or economic activity falls sharply
  • synonym – weakness: lack of strength, power, or prosperity in an economy
