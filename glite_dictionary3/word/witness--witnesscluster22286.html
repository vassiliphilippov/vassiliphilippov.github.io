witness

noun
person who signs to verify a document
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
A person who, in a legal context, attests to the authenticity of a document or signature by adding their own signature.

Examples

witness a signature
The attorney asked the witness to add his signature to the document.
The contract was valid because a witness had signed next to each party's signature.
You must have an independent witness sign your will for it to be legally binding.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attestor: a person who attests or bears witness to something, especially the signing of a document
