magisterial

adjective
overbearingly authoritative; domineering
Pronunciation: / ˌmædʒəˈstɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌmadʒɪˈstɪəriəl / (BrE)
having or showing great authority or a commanding, dignified, or overbearing manner

Examples

magisterial tone
He managed the discussion in a magisterial manner, interrupting and overruling others without hesitation.
Her magisterial attitude quickly alienated her colleagues at the new job.
The principal spoke to the students in a magisterial tone, leaving no room for debate.
He walked onto the stage with a magisterial air, confident and dignified.
Her magisterial presence at the ceremony gave the event a sense of gravity and importance.
The professor spoke in a magisterial tone that commanded the attention of everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authoritative: showing confidence expected to be obeyed; commanding
  • synonym – domineering: asserting one's will over another in an arrogant way
  • antonym – humble: having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
  • synonym – imperious: assuming power or authority without justification; arrogant and domineering
  • antonym – submissive: ready to conform to the authority or will of others
