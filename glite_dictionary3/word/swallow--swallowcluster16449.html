to swallow

verb
accept or tolerate something unpleasant
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
to accept, tolerate, or believe something unpleasant or hard to accept, often without protest

Examples

to swallow an insult
He found it difficult to swallow the criticism from his colleagues.
She had to swallow her disappointment and congratulate the winner.
Sometimes you just have to swallow your pride and ask for help.
He just smiled and swallowed the lie, though he knew it wasn't true.
I can't believe you actually swallowed his excuse for being late.
She tends to swallow everything the tabloids print without a second thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accept: to consent to receive or undertake; to admit or approve
  • synonym – buy: to believe or accept something, especially a doubtful story
  • antonym – doubt: to feel uncertain about the truth or reliability of something
  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently; to tolerate hardship or pain
  • synonym – fall for: to be deceived by something; to believe something that is not true
  • antonym – reject: to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with
