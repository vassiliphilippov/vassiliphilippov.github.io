forecarriage

noun
wheeled support at front of plow
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small wheeled carriage attached under the front end of a plow beam, used to support and guide the plow

Examples

forecarriage of a plow
A sturdy forecarriage makes the plow easier to maneuver through rough soil.
The farmer greased the wheels of the forecarriage to ensure smooth operation in the field.
The old plow's forecarriage was replaced after it broke during spring planting.
