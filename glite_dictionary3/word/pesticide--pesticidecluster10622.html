pesticide

noun
chemical used to kill pests
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛstəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪsaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to kill pests, especially insects and rodents, that damage crops or spread disease.

Examples

spray pesticide on crops
Farmers often spray pesticide on their crops to protect them from insect damage.
Many fruits and vegetables are treated with pesticide during the growing season.
Overuse of pesticide can harm beneficial insects and affect soil health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – herbicide: chemical that kills unwanted plants or weeds
  • synonym – insecticide: chemical that kills insects
  • synonym – rodenticide: chemical that kills rodents
