to queue

verb
to form or join a line while waiting
Pronunciation: / kju / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
(of people) to form or join a line while waiting for something.

Examples

to queue for tickets
Dozens of fans started queueing outside the stadium early in the morning.
People queued patiently at the bus stop despite the rain.
We had to queue for over an hour to get tickets for the concert.

Related Terms

  • synonym – line up: to stand in a line
  • degree lower – wait: to stay where one is or delay action
