to sap

verb
to undermine by digging beneath
Pronunciation: / sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
to excavate the earth beneath something in order to weaken or undermine it, especially in military contexts

Examples

to sap enemy walls
In medieval warfare, armies would sap enemy fortifications to breach the defenses.
Miners used to sap tunnels beneath the fortress to cause it to collapse.
The soldiers began to sap the enemy walls under the cover of darkness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – undermine: to weaken or damage by secret means, especially by digging underneath
