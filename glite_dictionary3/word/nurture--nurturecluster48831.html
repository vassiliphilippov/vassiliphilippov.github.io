nurture

noun
upbringing's influence on development
Pronunciation: / ˈnɝː.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.tʃə / (BrE)
the influence of upbringing and environment on a person's development, especially as distinguished from inherited traits

Examples

nurture versus nature
Her kindness is a testament to the power of nurture in shaping character.
Many psychologists believe that both nurture and nature are important in child development.
The debate over the influence of nurture versus nature has continued for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nature: inborn or inherited qualities as opposed to environmental ones
