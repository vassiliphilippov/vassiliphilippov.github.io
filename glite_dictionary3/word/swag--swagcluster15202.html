to swag

verb
sway or move unsteadily
Pronunciation: / swæɡ / (AmE), / swæɡ / (BrE)
to sway, move unsteadily, or droop/sag from weight or pressure

Examples

to swag under weight
He watched the lantern swag back and forth as the ship rocked.
The old bridge began to swag under the weight of the heavy truck.
With every step, the overloaded donkey seemed to swag side to side.
Her clothesline swagged in the middle from the heavy blankets.
Over time, the tent canvas may swag if it is not tightened properly.
The old wooden beams began to swag under the weight of the roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym – droop: to bend or hang downward limply
  • synonym – sag: to sink, droop, or hang downward
  • synonym – sway: to move slowly or rhythmically backward and forward or from side to side
