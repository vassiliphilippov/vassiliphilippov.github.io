to receive

verb
detect or register a signal or input
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsiv / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːv / (BrE)
to detect, register, or convert signals or messages (of electronic equipment or sense organs)

Examples

receive radio signals
Our television can receive over a hundred different channels.
Sensory nerves in the skin receive information about changes in temperature.
The radar was able to receive faint signals from the storm far away.
Old televisions could only receive analogue broadcasts.
Satellite dishes receive television signals and convert them into pictures.
The radio is able to receive signals from distant stations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – detect: to discover or identify the presence of something
  • antonym – transmit: to send out signals; to broadcast
