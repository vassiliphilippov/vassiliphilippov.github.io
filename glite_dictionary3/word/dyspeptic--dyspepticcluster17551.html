dyspeptic

adjective
irritable or bad-tempered
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛptɪk / (BrE)
Irritable or bad-tempered, as if suffering from indigestion.

Examples

dyspeptic mood
After missing breakfast, he grew increasingly dyspeptic as the morning wore on.
Her dyspeptic mood made conversation difficult during the meeting.
The critic’s dyspeptic remarks soured the atmosphere at the event.
