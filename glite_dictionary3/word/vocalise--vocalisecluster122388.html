to vocalise

verb
to pronounce as a vowel
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a consonant or sound) to pronounce or articulate as a vowel; to change into a vowel sound.

Examples

to vocalise a consonant
During speech development, some sounds naturally vocalise over time.
In some dialects, the letter 'l' may be vocalised at the end of a word.
Linguists have studied how certain consonants are vocalised between vowels.
