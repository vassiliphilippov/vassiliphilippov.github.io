mythologization

noun
the making of something into a myth
Pronunciation: / mɪˌθɑlədʒəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌθɒlədʒaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of turning a story, event, or idea into a myth or making it seem mythical, often by exaggerating or idealizing it

Examples

mythologization of history
Many critics have analyzed the mythologization of the American West in literature and film.
Some stories undergo a process of mythologization, gaining new symbolic meanings over time.
The mythologization of historical figures can obscure the facts of their real lives.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demythologization: removal of mythological elements
  • synonym – mythologize: to turn something into a myth
