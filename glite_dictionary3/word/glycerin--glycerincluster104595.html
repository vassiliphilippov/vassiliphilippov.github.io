glycerin

noun
sweet, syrupy alcohol used in food and medicine
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪsərɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɪn / (BrE)
A sweet, syrupy, colorless liquid alcohol used in food, medicine, and industry, obtained by saponifying fats and oils.

Examples

glycerin in skin cream
Pharmaceutical syrups sometimes use glycerin as a sweetener and thickening agent.
The soap was made using glycerin to keep it gentle on the skin.
You often find glycerin listed as an ingredient in moisturizers and lotions.
