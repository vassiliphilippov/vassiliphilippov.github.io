stockjobber

noun
dealer on a stock exchange (not with public)
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑkˌdʒɑbər / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌdʒɒbə / (BrE)
a person or firm on a stock exchange who deals only with brokers or other jobbers and does not deal directly with the public

Examples

stockjobber on the exchange
Before Big Bang reforms, a stockjobber would handle trades between brokers on the London Stock Exchange.
In the 19th century, a stockjobber was a crucial part of the trading system, managing liquidity for securities.
The role of the stockjobber disappeared as electronic trading replaced traditional stock exchange practices.
