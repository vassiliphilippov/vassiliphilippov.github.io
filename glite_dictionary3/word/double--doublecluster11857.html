to double

verb
hit a two-base hit (baseball)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl / (BrE)
(in baseball) to hit the ball so that the batter reaches second base safely.

Examples

to double to second
If you double here, the tying run could be in scoring position.
She doubled to left field, giving her team a scoring opportunity.
The batter doubled in the seventh inning, sending a runner to third base.
