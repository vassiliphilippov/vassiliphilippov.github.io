to baptise

verb
perform baptism ceremony
Pronunciation: / ˈbæp.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈbæp.taɪz / (BrE)
to perform the Christian religious ceremony of baptism on someone by sprinkling or immersing them in water

Examples

to baptise a baby
Hundreds of converts were baptised during the annual ceremony at the river.
The priest agreed to baptise their newborn daughter next Sunday.
They watched as the minister baptised each child in turn, blessing them with holy water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – christen: to give a name to someone at baptism (especially in Christian tradition)
