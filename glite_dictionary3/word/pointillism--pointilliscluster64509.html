pointillism

noun
painting technique using dots of color
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪntɪˌlɪzəm /; / ˈpwɑɪn.tɪˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪlɪz(ə)m /; / ˈpɔɪn.tɪˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a painting technique in which small, distinct dots of color are applied in patterns to form an image, developed by Georges Seurat and his followers in late 19th-century France
