separationism

noun
advocacy of separating church and state
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The advocacy or support of maintaining a strict division between church and state.

Examples

separationism in government
Advocates for separationism argue that government should not fund religious education.
Her commitment to separationism shaped her views on public policy.
The court's decision was influenced by strong separationism in the country's legal tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – establishmentarianism: support for a close relationship between church and state
  • synonym – secularism: the principle of separation of government institutions from religious institutions
