integument

noun
outer protective covering (skin, shell, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
the outer protective covering of an organism or object, such as skin, a cuticle, seed coat, rind, or shell

Examples

integument of a seed
A scientist examined the insect's outer integument under a microscope.
Plant seeds are encased in an integument that shields the embryo.
The turtle's hard integument helps protect it from predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym – epidermis: the outer layer of skin in animals or the surface layer of a plant
