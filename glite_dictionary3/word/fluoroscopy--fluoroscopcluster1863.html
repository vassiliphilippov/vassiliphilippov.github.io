fluoroscopy

noun
medical imaging with a fluoroscope
Pronunciation: / flʊˈrɑːskəpi / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses a fluoroscope to obtain real-time moving images of internal body structures

Examples

fluoroscopy exam
During fluoroscopy, a contrast agent may be used to enhance the visibility of internal organs.
Radiologists use fluoroscopy to guide certain diagnostic and therapeutic procedures.
The doctor recommended fluoroscopy to evaluate the patient's digestive system.
