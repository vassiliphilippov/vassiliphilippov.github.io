to enact

verb
to act out or perform scenes
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnækt / (AmE), / ɪˈnakt / (BrE)
to perform or represent (something) as if in a play; to act out a role or scene

Examples

to enact a scene
At the festival, volunteers enacted a traditional wedding ceremony for the audience.
In therapy, clients sometimes enact situations from their past to gain insight.
The children decided to enact their favorite scene from the play for their parents.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – improvise: to perform without preparation or script
  • synonym – perform: to present a role or an action in front of an audience
