complexion

noun
general character or attitude
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (BrE)
the general character, quality, or attitude of something, especially a period, activity, or group

Examples

political complexion
The election results changed the political complexion of the country.
The new policy gave the project a very different complexion.
Their decision altered the entire complexion of the negotiation process.
