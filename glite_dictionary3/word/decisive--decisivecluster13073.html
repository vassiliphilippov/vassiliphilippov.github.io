decisive

adjective
clear and undeniable
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
so clear or obvious that it leaves no doubt or room for question; unmistakable or undeniable.

Examples

decisive answer
After years of uncertainty, there was finally a decisive move to resolve the issue.
Her victory in the debate was secured by a decisive argument that everyone agreed with.
The team held a decisive lead going into the final quarter, making their win almost certain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ambiguous: open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • synonym – unmistakable: impossible to mistake for anything else; very distinctive or obvious
