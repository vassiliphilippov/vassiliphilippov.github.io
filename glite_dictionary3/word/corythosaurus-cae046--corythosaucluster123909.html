corythosaurus

noun
duck-billed dinosaur with helmet-like crest
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrɪθəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪθəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A large herbivorous duck-billed dinosaur of the late Cretaceous period, noted for its tall, hollow crest on the top of its head.

Examples

a corythosaurus fossil
A corythosaurus could grow up to nine meters long and weighed several tons.
Scientists discovered a nearly complete corythosaurus skull in Alberta, Canada.
The distinctive crest of the corythosaurus may have helped it communicate with others of its kind.
