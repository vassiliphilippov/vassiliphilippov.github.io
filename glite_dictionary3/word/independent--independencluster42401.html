independent

adjective
not controlled by outside forces (political, organizational)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of countries or organizations) not governed or controlled by other countries, organizations, or outside authorities; self-governing.

Examples

an independent country
After years of colonial rule, the country became an independent nation in 1960.
Each branch of government must remain independent to preserve the separation of powers.
The university strives to maintain an independent board of trustees free from political influence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dependent: subject to the control of another; not autonomous
  • synonym – sovereign: having full right and power to govern itself
