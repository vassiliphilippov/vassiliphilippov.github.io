to cockle

verb
make ripples by stirring water
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəl /; / ˈkɒk(ə)l / (BrE)
to make small ripples in water or small wrinkles in a surface

Examples

to cockle a surface
A sudden breeze cockled the puddles left by the rain.
Children waded in the pond and their splashing cockled the still water.
The boat's movement cockled the surface of the calm lake.
After washing, the fabric began to cockle around the edges.
If you spill water on it, the cardboard will cockle and lose its smoothness.
The heat caused the paper to cockle as it dried.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pucker: to gather or contract into wrinkles or folds
  • antonym – smooth: to make the surface flat and without wrinkles
