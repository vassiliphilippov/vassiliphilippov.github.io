to redeploy

verb
assign troops or resources again
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.dɪˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
to move or assign (troops, workers, resources, etc.) to a new position or task after they have already been deployed; to deploy again or differently

Examples

to redeploy staff
After the peace agreement, the military decided to redeploy its forces to other regions.
The company plans to redeploy staff from the sales team to the customer support division.
With changing needs, the IT department will redeploy equipment to maximize efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deploy: arrange or position (troops, resources, etc.) strategically
