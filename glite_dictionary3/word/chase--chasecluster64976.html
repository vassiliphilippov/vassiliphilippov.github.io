to chase

verb
pursue in order to catch
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs / (AmE), / tʃeɪs / (BrE)
to run or move quickly after someone or something in order to catch them, including pursuing someone romantically

Examples

to chase a thief
Children laughed as they chased each other around the playground.
Police officers had to chase the suspect through several city blocks.
The dog loves to chase squirrels in the park every morning.
During college, she used to chase older men.
He's been chasing her for months, but she's not interested.
Some people enjoy the thrill of chasing someone who plays hard to get.

  • antonym – flee: to run away from danger or pursuit
  • synonym – pursue: to follow and try to catch or achieve
  • synonym – woo: try to gain the love of someone, especially with romantic intent
