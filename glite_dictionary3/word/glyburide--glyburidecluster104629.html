glyburide

noun
oral antidiabetic drug
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlaɪbjʊˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪbjʊˌraɪd / (BrE)
an oral drug used to treat type 2 diabetes by stimulating the release of insulin from the pancreas

Examples

glyburide tablets for diabetes
Glyburide is often used as part of a treatment plan for type 2 diabetes.
Patients taking glyburide should monitor for signs of low blood sugar.
