whiteness

noun
racial identity and privilege
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the social and political idea of white identity and the system of advantages, power, and norms associated with being white.

Examples

social whiteness
Scholars analyze whiteness as a social construct that shapes institutions and cultural norms.
Her research traced how whiteness created unearned advantages in housing, education, and hiring practices.
In class they discussed whiteness to understand historical power and ongoing racial inequalities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – white privilege: the unearned social and economic advantages historically associated with being white
