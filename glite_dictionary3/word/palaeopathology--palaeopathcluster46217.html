palaeopathology

noun
study of ancient diseases from fossils
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊpæˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊpæˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of diseases in ancient times, especially as revealed by fossil or skeletal remains

Examples

palaeopathology of ancient bones
Recent advances in palaeopathology have identified new diseases from ancient remains.

Thanks to palaeopathology, we can understand the prevalence of arthritis in prehistoric humans.
