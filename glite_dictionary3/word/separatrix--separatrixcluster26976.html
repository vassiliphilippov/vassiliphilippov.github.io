separatrix

noun
slash used to separate related items
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛpəˈreɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈrætrɪks / (BrE)
a punctuation mark (‘/’) used to separate related items of information, especially in mathematical or typographical contexts

Examples

separatrix in fractions
A separatrix can help clarify complex fractions when written horizontally.
In older mathematical texts, the separatrix was commonly used instead of a decimal point.
Typographers sometimes refer to the “slash” character as a separatrix.
