permeableness

noun
quality of being permeable
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.mi.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.ə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being permeable; the ability of a material or substance to be penetrated or passed through by a liquid, especially by osmosis or diffusion.

Examples

permeableness of fabric
Due to its low permeableness, the new fabric resists water effectively.
Scientists measured the permeableness of the membrane to analyze how substances passed through it.
The permeableness of the soil determines how quickly water will drain after a heavy rainfall.

Related Terms

  • antonym – impermeableness: the quality of not allowing fluids to pass through
  • synonym – permeability: the quality or state of being permeable
