Brahmin

noun
member of highest Hindu caste
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (BrE)
a member of the highest caste in the traditional Hindu social hierarchy, historically priests and scholars

Examples

Brahmin priest scholar
He was born into a respected Brahmin family in South India.
Historically, the role of a Brahmin was closely linked to religious duties and scholarship.
Many Brahmins today pursue a variety of professions beyond their traditional roles.
