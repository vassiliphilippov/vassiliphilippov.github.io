calculative

adjective
acting deliberately for a goal
Pronunciation: / ˈkælkjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
Acting with careful consideration and intent to achieve a specific goal, often with self-interest.

Examples

calculative approach to success
He was criticized for being too calculative in his relationships, always thinking of personal benefit.
Her calculative approach to negotiations often gave her an advantage in business deals.
Unlike his spontaneous friends, Martin was always calculative when making major life decisions.
