to whiz

verb
make a soft swishing sound
Pronunciation: / wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
(of an object) to make a soft or faint swishing or buzzing sound as it moves quickly through the air.

Examples

to whiz past someone
An arrow whizzed past his ear as he ducked behind the wall.
I heard a bee whiz by my head as I walked through the garden.
The bullet whizzed through the air, narrowly missing its target.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buzz: make a humming or buzzing sound
  • synonym – whir: make a low, continuous, regular sound
