Santa Gertrudis

noun
breed of red beef cattle
Pronunciation: / ˌsæn.tə ɡərˈtruːdɪs / (AmE), / ˌsæn.tə ɡəˈtruːdɪs / (BrE)
a breed of large red beef cattle developed in the United States by crossing Brahman and Shorthorn cattle, known for their hardiness in hot climates

Examples

Santa Gertrudis cattle breed
A herd of Santa Gertrudis grazed in the sun-drenched pastures.
Many ranchers in Texas raise Santa Gertrudis cattle for their quality beef.
The Santa Gertrudis breed is highly valued for its adaptability to hot environments.
