idealistic

adjective
aspiring to high moral or intellectual ideals
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdiəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing high moral or intellectual values; aspiring to noble, lofty, or elevated principles.

Examples

idealistic dreams
Her idealistic vision inspired others to strive for a better world.
The speech was filled with idealistic notions of justice and equality.
Young people are often more idealistic before they experience the realities of life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – realistic: accepting things as they are; practical and pragmatic
