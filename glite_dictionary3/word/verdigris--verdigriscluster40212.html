verdigris

noun
green patina on copper, brass, or bronze
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːdəˌɡris / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪˌɡriːs / (BrE)
a green or bluish deposit that forms on copper, brass, or bronze surfaces when they are exposed to air or moisture for extended periods

Examples

verdigris on copper
Over many years, a layer of verdigris developed on the old copper statue.
Rainwater exposure caused the brass handles to acquire a bright verdigris.
The artisan used chemicals to quickly produce a verdigris effect on the bronze sculpture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – patina: a green or brown film on the surface of metals from oxidation
