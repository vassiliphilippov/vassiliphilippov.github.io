double-tongued

adjective
deliberately deceitful or hypocritical
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌb.əlˈtʌŋd / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əlˈtʌŋd / (BrE)
deliberately deceitful, especially by pretending one set of feelings while acting on another; hypocritical or duplicitous.

Examples

double-tongued politician
Business rivals accused him of being double-tongued when he praised their work but secretly undermined them.
Many regarded the politician as double-tongued because he made promises he never intended to keep.
She was wary of his double-tongued reassurances, suspecting his true intentions were different.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deceitful: behaving by misleading others or lying
  • antonym – honest: always telling the truth, not lying or deceiving
