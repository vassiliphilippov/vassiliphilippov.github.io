to clone

verb
make an identical copy, often biotechnologically
Pronunciation: / kloʊn / (AmE), / kləʊn / (BrE)
to make an identical copy of a living organism or object, especially using biotechnology

Examples

to clone a plant
Scientists were able to clone the rare plant to prevent its extinction.
She is working to clone cells for medical research.
The team hopes to clone the software in order to create a backup of the system.
