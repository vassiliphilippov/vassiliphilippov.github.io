to postdate

verb
to date as later than something else
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to establish something, especially a document or event, as occurring at a date later than another specified date

Examples

postdate a document
Historians postdate the construction of the wall compared to the original settlement.
The letter appeared to postdate the official announcement by several days.
You should not postdate checks, as banks may cash them immediately.

Related Terms

  • antonym – predate: to date as earlier than something else
