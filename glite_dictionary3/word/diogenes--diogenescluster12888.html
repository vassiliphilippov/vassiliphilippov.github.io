Diogenes

proper noun
ancient Greek philosopher, founder of Cynicism
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɑːdʒəˌniz / (AmE), / daɪˈɒdʒɪniːz / (BrE)
(circa 400–325 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher and founder of Cynicism, known for rejecting social conventions and advocating a simple, ascetic lifestyle.
