judgmental

adjective
excessively critical; quick to judge
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒʌdʒˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌdʒˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
having or displaying an excessively critical point of view; tending to form opinions, often harshly or without sufficient evidence.

Examples

judgmental attitude
He apologized for his judgmental remarks during the meeting.
Her judgmental attitude made it difficult for her to make friends at work.
Try to be less judgmental when listening to other people's opinions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – critical: inclined to judge or find fault
  • antonym – tolerant: showing willingness to allow opinions or behaviors different from one's own
