to ravish

verb
enchant; fill with intense delight
Pronunciation: / ˈrævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈravɪʃ / (BrE)
to fill someone with intense delight or to enchant so greatly that they are held spellbound

Examples

to ravish the senses
He was ravished by the music, unable to think of anything else as it played.
Her performance on stage ravished the audience, leaving them breathless with admiration.
The beauty of the sunset seemed to ravish everyone on the beach into silence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bore: to make uninterested or weary
  • synonym – enchant: to delight greatly or cast a spell over
