ligature

noun
character made by joining letters
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪɡətʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡətjʊə / (BrE)
a character formed by joining two or more letters together as a single symbol, often to improve appearance or readability in printing or handwriting

Examples

letter ligature symbol
Older manuscripts often feature the ‘æ’ ligature in place of the separate letters.
Some typefaces use the ligature ‘ﬀ’ instead of two separate ‘f’ letters.
The designer included several elegant ligatures to improve the visual flow of the text.
