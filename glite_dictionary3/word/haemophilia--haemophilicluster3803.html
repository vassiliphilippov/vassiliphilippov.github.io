haemophilia

noun
genetic disorder causing excessive bleeding
Pronunciation: / hiːˌmoʊˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈfɪliə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder in which the blood does not clot properly, leading to excessive bleeding even from minor injuries; usually affects males and is passed from mother to son.

Examples

haemophilia causes bleeding
A simple cut can be dangerous for someone with haemophilia due to poor blood clotting.
His uncle suffers from haemophilia, which requires careful management and regular medical treatment.
She learned her son had haemophilia after he experienced severe bleeding from a minor injury.
