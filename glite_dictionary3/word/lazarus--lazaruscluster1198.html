Lazarus

proper noun
beggar in Jesus' parable (Bible)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæzərəs / (AmE), / ˈlazərəs / (BrE)
the poor beggar featured in Jesus' parable of the rich man and the beggar in the New Testament, representing suffering and reward in the afterlife

Examples

Lazarus the beggar
In theological discussions, Lazarus is contrasted with the wealthy man to illustrate moral lessons.
Many artists have depicted Lazarus as a symbol of hope and redemption.
The story of Lazarus and the rich man is often read in church services.
