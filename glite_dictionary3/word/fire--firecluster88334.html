to fire

verb
arouse emotion or feeling
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to cause a particular emotion, feeling, or response to be strongly felt in someone

Examples

to fire imagination
A well-told joke can easily fire a room full of laughter.
Her stories never fail to fire the imagination of young readers.
The leader's speech was designed to fire enthusiasm among the crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arouse: to evoke or awaken (a feeling, emotion, or response)
  • antonym – dampen: to make something less strong or intense
  • synonym – stimulate: to encourage interest or activity in something
