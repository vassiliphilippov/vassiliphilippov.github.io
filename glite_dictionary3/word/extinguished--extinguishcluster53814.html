extinguished

adjective
no longer present; faded response (psychology)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪksˈtɪŋɡwɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a conditioned response) having died out or disappeared due to the absence or removal of reinforcement.

Examples

extinguished response
After repeated trials without a reward, the dog's salivation response was extinguished.
In classical conditioning, a learned behavior can become extinguished over time if not maintained.
The psychologist explained that the fear had become extinguished because the trigger was no longer reinforced.
