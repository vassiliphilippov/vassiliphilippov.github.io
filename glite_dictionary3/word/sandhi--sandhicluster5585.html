sandhi

noun
phonological change at word boundaries
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑn.di / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.di / (BrE)
(in linguistics) the process by which the pronunciation of a word or morpheme changes when it occurs next to another, especially in fluent speech.

Examples

sandhi in connected speech
Many languages, including Sanskrit and English, exhibit sandhi in connected speech.
Phonological sandhi can make word boundaries harder to perceive for language learners.
The study of sandhi is important in understanding historical sound changes.
