border

noun
boundary line or adjacent area
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə / (BrE)
the line or area that separates two countries, states, or regions; a boundary

Examples

border between countries
Soldiers patrolled the border to prevent illegal crossings.
They live in a small village near the border between the two countries.
You must have your passport ready when you reach the border.
The river forms the natural border between the two countries.
There is a checkpoint at every border crossing in the region.
We crossed the border from France into Spain during our road trip.
