to band

verb
to attach an identifying ring to a bird's leg
Pronunciation: / bænd / (AmE), / bænd / (BrE)
to fasten a band or ring around something, or to tie things together with a band

Examples

to band a bird
Researchers band migratory birds in the spring to study their movements.
The conservation team carefully banded the rare chicks before releasing them into the wild.
Volunteers spent the morning banding geese along the riverbank for the annual survey.
He banded the scrolls together with a strip of cloth.
If you band the sticks tightly, they'll be easier to carry.
The workers banded the pipes so they wouldn't separate during transport.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bind: to tie or fasten tightly
  • synonym – ring: to fit a circular band for identification, usually to a bird's leg
  • antonym – untie: to release or loosen something that has been tied
