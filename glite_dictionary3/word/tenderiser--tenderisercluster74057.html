tenderiser

noun
substance that softens meat
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtɛndəˌraɪzə / (BrE)
A substance, often an enzyme, added to meat to make it softer and easier to eat.

Examples

tenderiser powder for meat
Many cooks add a tenderiser to steaks before grilling.
Some commercial marinades contain a natural tenderiser like papain.
Sprinkle the tenderiser evenly over the chicken and let it rest for thirty minutes.
