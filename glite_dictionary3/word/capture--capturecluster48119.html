capture

noun
retaining an object via gravity
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə / (BrE)
the process by which a star, planet, or atomic system acquires or retains another object or particle

Examples

capture of a comet
Planetary scientists study the capture of comets by Jupiter.
The gravitational capture of asteroids explains some planetary moons.
The theory describes how capture occurs when the object's velocity decreases below escape velocity.
Neutron capture is a key process in the formation of heavier elements inside stars.
Scientists studied muon capture on various atomic nuclei to better understand weak interactions.
The capture of an electron by a proton produces a neutron and a neutrino.

  • synonym – absorption: the process in which something is absorbed
  • antonym – release: the process of letting go or emitting a particle
  • synonym – seizure: the act of taking possession, also used in astronomy
