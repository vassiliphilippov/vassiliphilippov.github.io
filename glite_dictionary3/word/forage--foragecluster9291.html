forage

noun
food like grass or hay for livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
bulky food such as grass or hay, fed to horses and cattle for grazing or browsing

Examples

forage for cattle
Drought conditions made forage scarce, so ranchers had to buy hay from neighboring regions.
Horses require high-quality forage as the main part of their diets.
The farmer stored enough forage to feed the cattle throughout the winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fodder: food, especially dried hay or feed, for cattle and other livestock
