ethics

noun
moral principles or study thereof
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθɪks / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪks / (BrE)
Moral principles that govern a person's or group's behavior, or the study of such principles.

Examples

strong ethics
He is known for his strong ethics and fairness in business.
Medical professionals must adhere to strict ethics when treating patients.
The university offers a course on ethics and social responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym – immorality: behavior that goes against moral principles
  • synonym – morality: principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
