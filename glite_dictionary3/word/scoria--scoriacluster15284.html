scoria

noun
waste layer on molten metals
Pronunciation: / ˈskɔriə / (AmE), / ˈskɔːriə / (BrE)
A layer of slag or waste material formed by oxidation on the surface of molten metals.

Examples

molten metal scoria
After the metal was refined, the scoria was skimmed from the surface of the molten iron.
The black, glassy scoria cooled quickly after being separated from the metal.
Workers removed the scoria that had formed during the smelting process.
