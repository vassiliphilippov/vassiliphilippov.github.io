patter

noun
glib or persuasive talk
Pronunciation: / ˈpætər / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
quick, smooth, and often insincere talk, especially used by salespeople or entertainers to impress or persuade others.

Examples

sales patter skills
He practiced his comedy patter until the jokes sounded spontaneous.
She listened to the guide’s rapid patter about the history of the old building.
The salesman delivered his usual patter to try to convince the customers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – banter: playful and friendly exchange of teasing remarks
  • synonym – spiel: a long or fast speech intended to persuade
