absolute

noun
philosophical ultimate reality
Pronunciation: / ˈæbsəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːt / (BrE)
(philosophy) That which exists independently and is not relative to anything else; an ultimate reality or principle that is complete and beyond limitation.

Examples

the absolute truth
He devoted his life to seeking the absolute in both art and science.
Many philosophers have debated the existence of the absolute throughout history.
Some claim that moral laws are based on an absolute rather than human convention.

Related Terms

  • antonym – relative: that which is dependent on or related to something else
