choppiness

noun
jerky video playback
Pronunciation: / c8t8351p.i.n59s / (AmE), / c8t8352p.i.n6as / (BrE)
The quality of video or animation appearing jerky or uneven, typically caused by low frame rate, buffering, network issues, or heavy compression.

Examples

video choppiness
The streaming service reduced choppiness after upgrading its video encoding.
During the live call, the choppiness made it hard to follow the speaker's movements.
Gamers complain about choppiness when frame rates drop below sixty frames per second.

Related Terms

  • synonym – judder: a sudden, uneven movement or shaking in video caused by dropped frames or synchronization problems
  • antonym – smoothness: the quality of motion that is continuous and free of interruptions; opposite of choppiness in playback
