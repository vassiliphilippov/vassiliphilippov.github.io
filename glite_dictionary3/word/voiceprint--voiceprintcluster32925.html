voiceprint

noun
unique identification of a voice
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔɪsˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪs.prɪnt / (BrE)
A visual or electronic recording that uniquely represents a person's voice for the purpose of identification.

Examples

voiceprint identification system
Each individual has a unique voiceprint that cannot be exactly replicated by others.
The police compared the suspect's voiceprint to recordings from the crime scene.
The security system uses a voiceprint to verify the identity of each user.
