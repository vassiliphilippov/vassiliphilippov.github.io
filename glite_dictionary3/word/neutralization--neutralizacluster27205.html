neutralization

noun
military euphemism for killing or destroying a threat
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(euphemism) The removal of a threat by killing or destroying it, especially during a covert or military operation.

Examples

neutralization of a target
Intelligence agencies often use the term neutralization to refer to the elimination of high-level threats.
The military reported the neutralization of several enemy targets during the raid.
The operation's success hinged on the swift neutralization of the opposition's leadership.
