trichiniasis

noun
parasitic disease caused by trichina larvae
Pronunciation: / trɪˌkɪniˈeɪsɪs / (AmE), / trɪˌkɪnɪˈeɪsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation with trichina larvae, typically contracted by eating undercooked meat (especially pork), in which larvae migrate from the intestines to the muscles, where they become encysted.

Examples

trichiniasis from pork
Doctors diagnosed the patient with trichiniasis after detecting encysted larvae in his muscle tissue.
Eating undercooked pork can expose you to trichiniasis, a serious parasitic infection.
Strict meat inspection and proper cooking methods have greatly reduced cases of trichiniasis in many countries.
