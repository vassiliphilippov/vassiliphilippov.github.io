cross-classification

noun
classification by multiple attributes
Pronunciation: / krɔs ˌklæsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˌklɑːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of classifying items or cases according to two or more attributes or variables simultaneously

Examples

cross-classification of data
Data analysts often use cross-classification to examine the relationship between education and employment.
Our research includes a cross-classification by income bracket and geographic region.
The cross-classification of students was based on both their grade level and enrollment status.
