transformer

noun
electrical device that changes AC voltage
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfɔrmɚ / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a device used to transfer electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction, changing the voltage of an alternating current

Examples

power transformer
Many rural areas use a step-up transformer to transmit electricity over long distances.
The electrician installed a new transformer to supply power to the building.
You should never touch a transformer without proper training, as it can be very dangerous.

Related Terms

  • synonym – converter: a device that changes something from one form to another, especially voltage
