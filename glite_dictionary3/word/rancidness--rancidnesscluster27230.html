rancidness

noun
the state of being rancid
Pronunciation: / ˈræn.sɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈran.sɪd.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being rancid, especially having a foul or stale smell or taste due to decomposition.

Examples

rancidness of old food
He recoiled at the rancidness coming from the forgotten leftovers in the fridge.
Testing for rancidness is important to ensure the quality of cooking oils.
The rancidness of the old butter made it unfit to eat.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freshness: the state of being fresh, not stale or rancid
  • synonym – rancidity: the quality or state of being rancid
