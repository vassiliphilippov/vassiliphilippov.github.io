solubility

noun
ability of a problem to be solved
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑː.ljuˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɒl.juˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The property of a problem or difficulty that makes it possible to solve or resolve it.

Examples

solubility of a problem
Some legal disputes lack solubility without international cooperation.
The solubility of the dilemma was debated by the committee for hours.
The manager questioned the solubility of the technical issues facing the project.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insolubility: the property of being impossible to solve
