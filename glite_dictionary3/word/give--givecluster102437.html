to give

verb
convey or communicate a look, smile, or gesture
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv / (AmE), / ɡɪv / (BrE)
to express or communicate (a look, gesture, information, or nonverbal signal) to someone

Examples

give a look
He gave her a stern look when she interrupted the meeting.
She gave me a reassuring smile before the interview began.
The teacher gave a quick nod to signal that it was time to start.
He didn't give any compliments during the meeting.
Please give your full attention to the speaker.
She asked me to give her regards to your family.
If questioned by the police, he refused to give his full address.
Please give your details at the reception desk before entering.
She didn't want to give her reasons for leaving the job.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conceal: to keep something hidden; to not manifest
  • synonym – disclose: to make information known
  • synonym – offer: to present something for acceptance; sometimes used for looks or gestures
  • synonym – reveal: to make something known that was previously secret or hidden
  • synonym – send: to cause to go or be taken to a particular destination (figurative sense: send a glance)
  • synonym – show: to make visible or manifest
  • antonym – withhold: to refuse to give something to someone
