veto

noun
the power to block decisions or laws
Pronunciation: / ˈvitoʊ /; / ˈviːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈviːtəʊ / (BrE)
the official power or right to reject a decision or proposal and stop it from becoming effective

Examples

presidential veto
A single veto can overturn a majority decision in some organizations.
Any member of the Security Council can exercise a veto on major issues.
The president used his veto to block the proposed legislation.
A two-thirds majority is required in the assembly to override a veto.
The governor's veto prevented the bill from becoming law.
The president used his veto to block the proposed healthcare legislation.
