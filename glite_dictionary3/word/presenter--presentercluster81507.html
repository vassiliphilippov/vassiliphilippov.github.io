presenter

noun
person who presents something formally
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzɛntər /; / prəˈzɛntər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntə / (BrE)
a person who formally presents or introduces something or someone, such as an award, speech, or guest

Examples

presenter of an award
As the presenter of the annual award, she shared a brief speech before announcing the winner.
Each presenter must ensure their document is signed before submission.
The presenter handed the petition to the mayor during the ceremony.
As the presenter, she introduced the guest of honor to the audience.
He acted as the presenter during the graduation ceremony, recommending the candidates for their degrees.
The presenter gave a heartfelt speech before announcing the winner of the award.
