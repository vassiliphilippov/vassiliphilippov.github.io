to trawl

verb
fish using a trawl net from a boat
Pronunciation: / trɔl / (AmE), / trɔːl / (BrE)
to fish by dragging a large net (a trawl) through the water behind a boat or trawler.

Examples

to trawl for fish
Local fishermen are prohibited from trawling in protected marine areas.
The fleet will trawl off the coast for several months during the fishing season.
They trawled the deep waters hoping for a big catch of cod.

  • synonym – fish: to catch or try to catch fish
