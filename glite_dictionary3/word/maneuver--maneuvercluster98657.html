to maneuver

verb
act skillfully to achieve a goal
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvɚ / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
to move, act, or direct something or someone skillfully or strategically to achieve a goal

Examples

to maneuver a car
After months of planning, they finally maneuvered her into signing the agreement.
She skillfully maneuvered through the competition to win the promotion.
The politician maneuvered his way into the lead position within the party.
Enemy forces maneuvered skillfully during the simulated battle exercise.
The general decided to maneuver his troops to higher ground for a tactical advantage.
The navy maneuvered its fleet to create a blockade around the harbor.
He skillfully maneuvered the car through the narrow, winding streets.
She slowly maneuvered the boat away from the crowded dock.
The pilot had to maneuver the plane around a sudden storm.

  • synonym – scheme: to make secret or underhanded plans to achieve a goal
