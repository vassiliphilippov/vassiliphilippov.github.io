front

noun
meteorological boundary between air masses
Pronunciation: / frʌnt / (AmE), / frʌnt / (BrE)
(meteorology) the boundary between two different air masses, often associated with changes in weather.

Examples

a cold front
Meteorologists predicted that a warm front would arrive by morning.
Sudden changes in temperature often indicate the passage of a weather front.
The cold front brought heavy rain and thunderstorms to the region.
