mood

noun
state of mind or feeling
Pronunciation: / mud / (AmE), / muːd / (BrE)
a temporary state of mind or feeling, or the general emotional atmosphere of a person, group, or situation

Examples

in a mood today
He apologized for his bad mood yesterday and promised it wouldn't happen again.
She was in a cheerful mood after receiving the good news.
The rainy weather put everyone in a gloomy mood.
Her cheerful mood lifted the spirits of everyone in the office.
The country's political mood was tense following the sudden resignation of the prime minister.
There was a festive mood in the city as people celebrated the holiday.
Don't take it personally; he's just in a bad mood today.
His mood seemed darker than usual this morning.
She was in a terrible mood after failing her exam.

  • synonym – spirit: the prevailing or typical quality, mood, or attitude of a person, group, or period
