Lorenzo de' Medici

proper noun
Renaissance Florentine statesman and patron of the arts
Pronunciation: / loʊˈrɛnzoʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi /; / loʊˈrɛntsoʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ləˈrɛnzəʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi /; / lɒˈrɛntsəʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1449–1492) Italian statesman and scholar who was a major patron of the arts and humanism during the Renaissance in Florence, supporting figures such as Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Botticelli
