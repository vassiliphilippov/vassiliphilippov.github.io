rationalisation

noun
making something appear reasonable
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of explaining or justifying behavior or attitudes with logical reasons, sometimes to conceal true motives (can be a psychological defense mechanism)

Examples

rationalisation of actions
He offered a detailed rationalisation for his decision, though it seemed forced.
Her rationalisation of her lateness was that traffic had been unusually heavy.
Sometimes, people use rationalisation to defend actions they secretly regret.
After failing the exam, Mark used rationalisation to convince himself that he never wanted to pass in the first place.
Her rationalisation of her anger masked the deeper insecurities she was unwilling to admit.
Psychologists often encounter rationalisation when patients refuse to acknowledge their true feelings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – denial: a defense mechanism where unpleasant realities are ignored or refused to be accepted
  • synonym – justification: the act of showing something to be right or reasonable
  • synonym – rationalization: American spelling of rationalisation
