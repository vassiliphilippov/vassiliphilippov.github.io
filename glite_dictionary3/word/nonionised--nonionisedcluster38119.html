nonionised

adjective
not converted into ions
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːnˈaɪ.ə.naɪzd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈaɪ.ə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not converted into ions; existing in a molecular rather than ionic form.

Examples

nonionised molecules in water
At neutral pH, the compound remains largely nonionised in solution.
Only the nonionised form of the drug is readily absorbed by the body.
The nonionised molecules cannot pass through the charged membrane easily.
