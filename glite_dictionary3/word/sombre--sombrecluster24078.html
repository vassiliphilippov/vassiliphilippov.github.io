sombre

adjective
dull or dark in colour
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːmbər / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbə / (BrE)
Having a dull or dark colour; lacking brightness or vibrancy.

Examples

sombre colours
She wore a sombre brown dress that matched the mood of the funeral.
The overcast sky gave a sombre appearance to the entire city.
The room looked sombre with its faded curtains and shadowy corners.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drab: Lacking brightness or interest; dull or dreary.
  • synonym – dull: Lacking brightness, vividness, or sheen.
  • antonym – vivid: Producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind; bright and intense in colour.
