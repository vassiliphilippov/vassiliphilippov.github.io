to penetrate

verb
spread or diffuse through
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnɪˌtreɪt /; / ˈpɛnəˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpenɪtreɪt /; / ˈpɛnɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to spread, diffuse, or have an effect throughout something; (of an idea or feeling) to enter or affect deeply

Examples

penetrate the mind
A sense of hope penetrated the crowd despite the difficult circumstances.
The powerful aroma of freshly baked bread penetrated the entire house.
The sound of the alarm penetrated every corner of the building.
A sudden realization penetrated his mind as he listened to the news.
He was so overwhelmed that grief penetrated every part of his being.
The teacher's words slowly penetrated her consciousness, and she began to understand the concept.
