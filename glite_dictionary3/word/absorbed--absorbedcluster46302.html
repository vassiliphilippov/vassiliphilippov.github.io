absorbed

adjective
taken in without reflection
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɚbd / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbd / (BrE)
(of energy or a substance, especially light, heat, or liquid) taken in or retained without being reflected or transmitted.

Examples

absorbed light energy
Certain materials are designed to maximize absorbed energy and minimize reflection.
Most of the absorbed light is converted to heat within the material.
The sunscreen reduces the amount of UV rays absorbed by the skin.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reflected: (of light, heat, or energy) sent back from a surface, not absorbed
