uninominal

adjective
having one representative per district (voting system)
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː.nəˈnɑː.mə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈnɒ.mɪ.nəl / (BrE)
(of a voting system) having only one representative elected from each district.

Examples

uninominal voting system
Many democracies operate under a uninominal electoral system, where voters choose only one candidate per district.
Some experts argue that uninominal voting systems strengthen the link between representatives and their constituents.
The country switched from a plurinominal to a uninominal method to simplify elections.

Related Terms

  • antonym – plurinominal: having more than one representative elected from each district (voting system)
