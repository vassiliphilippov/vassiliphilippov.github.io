to expose

verb
put in danger or at risk
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a situation where they are affected by something, such as danger, harm, or a new experience

Examples

to expose skin to sun
Leaving your skin unprotected will expose you to sunburn.
She didn't want to expose her children to unnecessary danger.
The investigation could expose the company to legal risks.
It is important to expose children to different cultures at an early age.
The doctor advised not to expose the wound to dirt and dust.
The fabric should not be exposed to direct sunlight for long periods.
