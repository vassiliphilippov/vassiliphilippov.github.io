to abort

verb
terminate before completion
Pronunciation: / əˈbɔrt / (AmE), / əˈbɔːt / (BrE)
to stop a process, plan, or development before it is completed, often because of problems or natural causes

Examples

to abort a plan
He had to abort the experiment after unexpected results appeared.
The engineers decided to abort the launch due to a technical malfunction.
You can abort the download if it is taking too long.
If the embryo does not implant properly, it may abort early in pregnancy.
Some species will abort their offspring in response to environmental stress.
The experiment had to abort when the main system failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cancel: to decide not to carry out an activity
  • antonym – continue: to keep going without stopping
  • synonym – miscarry: (of a pregnancy) to end before the fetus is viable
