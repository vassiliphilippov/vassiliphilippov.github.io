to anchor

verb
fix securely in place
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to fix or secure something firmly in place, especially with an anchor

Examples

to anchor a ship
A heavy statue was anchored to the ground to prevent theft.
She used large stones to anchor the tent in place during the storm.
They anchored the fence posts in concrete to withstand strong winds.
Before the storm hit, the captain decided to anchor the vessel securely.
The ship anchored just outside the harbor for the night.
We anchored off the coast and took a small boat to shore.
