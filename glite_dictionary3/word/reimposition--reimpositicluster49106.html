reimposition

noun
the act of imposing again
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.ɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of imposing something again, especially a law, rule, or restriction that had previously been lifted or removed.

Examples

the reimposition of sanctions
Following a brief period of freedom, society faced the reimposition of old regulations.
Many critics spoke out against the reimposition of strict border controls.
The reimposition of sanctions caused concern among international businesses.
