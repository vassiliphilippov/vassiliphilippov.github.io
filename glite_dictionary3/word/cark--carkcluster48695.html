to cark

verb
disturb or worry (archaic)
Pronunciation: / kɑrk / (AmE), / kɑːk / (BrE)
(archaic) To disturb, worry, or cause unease in someone's mind.

Examples

to cark the mind
Financial troubles cark him night and day, leaving him restless.
It did cark her heart to hear such dreadful news.
The burden of secrecy began to cark the young squire's conscience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – perturb: to disturb or trouble mentally
  • antonym – reassure: to say or do something to remove the doubts or fears of someone
  • synonym – worry: to cause someone to feel anxious or concerned
