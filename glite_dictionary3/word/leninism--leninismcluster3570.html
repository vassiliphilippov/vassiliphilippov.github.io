Leninism

noun
theories of Lenin guiding the Soviet Union
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛnɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɛnɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
The political and economic theories developed by Vladimir Lenin, which guided the Soviet Union; a modification of Marxism emphasizing imperialism as the highest stage of capitalism and the necessity of a vanguard party.

Examples

Leninism in Soviet politics
He argued that Leninism placed greater emphasis on revolutionary leadership than Marxism did.
Many historians debate to what extent Leninism diverged from classical Marxist doctrine.
The policies of the early Soviet Union were deeply influenced by Leninism.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Marxism: the political and economic theories of Karl Marx
  • degree higher – Stalinism: the political and economic policies developed by Joseph Stalin
  • synonym – socialism: a political and economic theory advocating collective or governmental ownership
