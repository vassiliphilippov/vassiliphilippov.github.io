cursorial

adjective
adapted for running (biology)
Pronunciation: / kɜrˈsɔriəl / (AmE), / kɜːˈsɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of animals or their limbs and feet) adapted for running.

Examples

cursorial animals
Birds such as ostriches have cursorial legs that allow rapid movement across open ground.
The cheetah is a classic example of a cursorial mammal, built for tremendous speed.
The evolution of cursorial limbs helped some dinosaurs escape from predators.
