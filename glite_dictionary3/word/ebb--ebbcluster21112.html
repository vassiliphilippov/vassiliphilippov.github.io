to ebb

verb
to recede, decline, or diminish
Pronunciation: / ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
to gradually decrease, recede, or move away (such as water, feelings, or strength)

Examples

to ebb away
After the excitement of the festival, everyone's energy started to ebb.
Her confidence began to ebb as the interview progressed.
The strength of the tide will ebb by late afternoon.
As the storm passed, the floodwaters slowly ebbed away from the streets.
Her enthusiasm seemed to ebb as the meeting dragged on.
The tide began to ebb after noon, revealing the rocks along the shore.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flow: to move steadily and continuously in a current or stream (opposite sense of ebb)
  • synonym – wane: (of a feeling, power, or quality) to decrease in vigor or extent; become weaker
