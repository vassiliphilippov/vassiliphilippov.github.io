bauxite

noun
main ore of aluminum
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːksaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːksaɪt / (BrE)
A clay-like mineral that is the main ore of aluminum, consisting chiefly of aluminum oxides and hydroxides; used in the production of aluminum and occasionally as an abrasive or catalyst.

Examples

bauxite mining industry
Australia is one of the world's largest producers of bauxite.
Huge mines extract bauxite for the global metals industry.
The process of refining bauxite into aluminum requires high amounts of energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aluminum: a silvery-white metal extracted from bauxite
