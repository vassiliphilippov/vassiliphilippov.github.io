heavily

adverb
excessively or immoderately
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvəli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvɪli / (BrE)
To an excessive or immoderate degree, especially in relation to eating, drinking, or other indulgent actions.

Examples

drank heavily at parties
After his promotion, he began to drink heavily every weekend.
She often ate heavily when she was stressed.
The patient had been smoking heavily for over twenty years before seeking help.

Related Terms

  • synonym – excessively: to a degree exceeding what is normal or appropriate
  • antonym – lightly: to a small or minimal degree (opposite in some contexts)
