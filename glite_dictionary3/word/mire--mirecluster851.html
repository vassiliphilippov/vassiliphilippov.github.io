to mire

verb
to entrap or become stuck
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪə / (BrE)
to become stuck or cause someone or something to become stuck, especially in mud or a difficult situation

Examples

to mire a car
Heavy rains mired the vehicles in the field for hours.
She found herself mired in debt after losing her job.
The negotiations became mired in disagreements over policy details.
Her lack of preparation mired her in difficulties during the exam.
Political scandals often mire officials in endless investigations and controversy.
The heavy rains mired the trucks on the rural road for hours.

If you drive off-road without caution, you might mire your vehicle in the mud.
