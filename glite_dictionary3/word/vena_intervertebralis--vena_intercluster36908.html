vena intervertebralis

noun
vein accompanying spinal nerves
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪnə ˌɪntərˌvɜrtəˈbreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌveːnə ˌɪntəˌvɜːtəˈbreɪlɪs / (BrE)
Any of several veins located alongside the spinal nerves, accompanying them through the intervertebral foramina.

Examples

vena intervertebralis near spine
Doctors identified swelling near the vena intervertebralis following the patient's injury.
Many spinal surgeries must account for the position of the vena intervertebralis to prevent excessive bleeding.
The vena intervertebralis plays a role in draining blood from the spinal cord region.
