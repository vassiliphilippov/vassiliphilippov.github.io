to jawbone

verb
talk idly or casually; chat
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːbəʊn / (BrE)
to talk idly or casually and in a friendly way; to chat or gossip informally

Examples

to jawbone with friends
It's common for coworkers to jawbone around the water cooler during breaks.
They sat on the porch and jawboned for hours about old times.
We spent the afternoon jawboning instead of finishing our work.
