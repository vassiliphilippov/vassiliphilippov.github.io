enclosure

noun
naturally enclosed space
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkloʊʒɚ /; / ɪnˈkloʊʒər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
a space or area that is surrounded by boundaries, such as fences, walls, or natural features

Examples

animal enclosure
A natural enclosure of trees provided a safe retreat for the deer.
The horses wandered freely within the sheltered enclosure formed by the surrounding cliffs.
They set up camp in a quiet enclosure at the base of the mountains.
Children are not allowed inside the construction enclosure without supervision.
The fragile plants were kept safe in a glass enclosure during the cold months.
The zoo built a new enclosure for the polar bears to give them more space.
