elastance

noun
ability of a dielectric to oppose electric charge
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæstəns / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstəns / (BrE)
The property of a dielectric material that enables it to oppose the accumulation of an electric charge; the reciprocal of capacitance.

Examples

high elastance material
An increase in elastance corresponds to a decrease in the capacitance of a capacitor.
Engineers consider elastance when designing insulation for electrical circuits.
The elastance of a material determines how much it resists the storage of electric charge.

Related Terms

  • antonym – capacitance: the ability of a body to store an electric charge
