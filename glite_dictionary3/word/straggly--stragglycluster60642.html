straggly

adjective
growing or spread out untidily
Pronunciation: / ˈstræɡli / (AmE), / ˈstræɡli / (BrE)
growing or spreading in an untidy, irregular, or sparse way; scattered or tangled in appearance

Examples

straggly hair
After months without a haircut, her hair became long and straggly.
The straggly bushes needed pruning to look more presentable.
We could see his straggly beard from across the room.
A few straggly bushes dotted the edge of the deserted path.
He ran his fingers through his straggly beard, deep in thought.
The old fence was covered in straggly ivy that barely hid the wood.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dense: growing closely together, thick
  • synonym – scraggly: growing in a thin, uneven, or ragged way
  • synonym – sprawling: spreading out irregularly, usually in an ungainly manner
  • antonym – tidy: arranged neatly and in order
