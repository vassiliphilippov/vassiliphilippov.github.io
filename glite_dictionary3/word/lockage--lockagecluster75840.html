lockage

noun
system of canal locks
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a series or system of locks in a canal or other waterway, used to raise or lower boats between stretches of water of different levels

Examples

lockage system inspection
Engineers inspected the lockage for signs of wear after the heavy storms.
Improvements to the lockage increased canal traffic and boosted the local economy.
The construction of the lockage allowed ships to bypass the natural waterfalls on the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lock: a section of a canal or river enclosed by gates, used for raising and lowering boats
