ruggedization

noun
making equipment more robust/durable
Pronunciation: / ˌrʌɡɪdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌɡɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making equipment stronger and more durable, especially to withstand harsh use or conditions

Examples

ruggedization of equipment
After ruggedization, the tablets could be safely used in construction sites.
Consumer devices rarely undergo ruggedization unless intended for outdoor or industrial use.
The ruggedization of military equipment ensures it can survive extreme conditions.
