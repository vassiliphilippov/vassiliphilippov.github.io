St. Peter the Apostle

proper noun
leader of the Apostles; first Pope
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈpiːtɚ ði əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtə ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
One of the twelve disciples of Jesus, recognized as the leader of the Apostles and, by the Catholic Church, as the first Pope.

Examples

St. Peter the Apostle leads the disciples
According to tradition, St. Peter the Apostle was crucified upside down.
Many Christians consider St. Peter the Apostle to be the first leader of their church.
The Basilica of St. Peter the Apostle in Rome is a major pilgrimage site.
