to relax

verb
become less tight or loose
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlæks / (AmE), / rɪˈlaks /; / rɪˈlæks / (BrE)
to become or make something less tight, taut, or tense (such as muscles or physical objects)

Examples

relax tight muscles
As the muscles relax, the pain should diminish.

When metal heats up, it can relax and lose its rigidity.
As the brakes relax, the tension in the cable decreases.
He relaxed his grip on the hose, allowing water to flow freely.
Try to relax the fabric before sewing to avoid wrinkles.
