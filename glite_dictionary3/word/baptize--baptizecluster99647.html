to baptize

verb
administer baptism; perform a Christian initiation rite
Pronunciation: / bæpˈtaɪz / (AmE), / bæpˈtaɪz / (BrE)
to administer baptism to someone, especially as a religious rite signifying spiritual cleansing and admission to a Christian church

Examples

to baptize a baby
Many Christians baptize their children as an important tradition.
She decided to baptize her son despite her family's objections.
The church will baptize the newborn baby on Sunday.

  • synonym – christen: to give a name to at baptism; often used interchangeably with baptize
