mediate

adjective
acting through an intervening agency
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
acting or occurring through an intervening agent or means rather than directly

Examples

mediate effect process
Some chemicals exert their effects in a mediate way, involving several biological steps.
The virus can be transmitted by mediate contact, such as through contaminated objects.
Unlike direct communication, mediate communication often relies on third-party technologies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – immediate: acting or occurring directly, without any intervening agency
