antilepton

noun
antiparticle of a lepton
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈlɛptɑn / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈlɛptɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic antiparticle that corresponds to and has the opposite charge of a lepton

Examples

an antilepton in physics
In high-energy physics experiments, the creation of an antilepton can be observed alongside its corresponding lepton.
The positron is an example of an antilepton, being the antiparticle of the electron.
When a lepton collides with its matching antilepton, they can annihilate each other, producing photons.
