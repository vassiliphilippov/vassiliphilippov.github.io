Weizenbier

noun
German wheat beer
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪtsənˌbiːr / (AmE), / ˈvaɪts(ə)nˌbiə / (BrE)
A wheat beer of German origin, particularly in southern Germany, typically pale and cloudy, made with a significant proportion of wheat malt.

Examples

cold Weizenbier glass
He ordered a tall glass of Weizenbier to go with his Bavarian meal.
On summer evenings, we like to sip chilled Weizenbier in the garden.
The brewery specializes in traditional Weizenbier with notes of banana and clove.
