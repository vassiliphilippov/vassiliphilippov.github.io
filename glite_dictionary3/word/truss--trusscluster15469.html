truss

noun
bandage holding a hernia in place
Pronunciation: / trʌs / (AmE), / trʌs / (BrE)
A medical device consisting of a pad and belt, worn to apply pressure and keep a hernia in place.

Examples

wear a truss for hernia
He wore a truss daily to prevent the hernia from protruding.
Modern surgical techniques have made the truss less common for treating hernias.
The doctor recommended a truss to help manage his inguinal hernia.
