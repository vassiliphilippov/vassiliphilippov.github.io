to grovel

verb
act in an excessively submissive or fearful way
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɑːvəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒvəl / (BrE)
to behave in a way that shows excessive submission or fear, often by lying or crawling on the ground in order to gain favor or forgiveness

Examples

to grovel for forgiveness
After being caught, she began to grovel and beg for forgiveness.
He refused to grovel before his boss, even when he made a mistake.
You don't need to grovel to get their attention; just be yourself.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cringe: to behave in a servile or overly submissive way
  • antonym – defy: to openly resist or disobey
