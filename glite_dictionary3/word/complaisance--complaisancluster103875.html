complaisance

noun
willingness to please or yield to others
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpleɪsəns / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪzəns / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to please others or to accept their wishes readily

Examples

complaisance toward guests
He greeted every guest with remarkable complaisance, ensuring no one felt unwelcome.
Her complaisance made her popular among her colleagues, who appreciated her willingness to help.
The prince's complaisance towards his advisors was sometimes mistaken for weakness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compliance: the act of conforming to a wish or command
  • antonym – obstinacy: stubborn refusal to change one's opinion or course of action
