erythema

noun
redness of the skin (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛrəˈθimə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈθiːmə / (BrE)
Abnormal redness of the skin caused by dilation of blood vessels, typically as a result of inflammation or sunburn.

Examples

skin erythema
A rash with pronounced erythema can be an early indicator of an allergic reaction.
Persistent erythema may require further evaluation by a dermatologist.
The patient showed signs of erythema on her arms after spending too long in the sun.
