reproducible

adjective
able to be reproduced or duplicated
Pronunciation: / ˌriːprəˈduːsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdjuːsəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be reproduced or duplicated, especially so that the results are consistent when repeated.

Examples

reproducible results
In medicine, a reproducible procedure ensures consistent patient outcomes.
She values reproducible methods in her research to verify the findings.
The experiment gave highly reproducible results each time it was performed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – irreproducible: not able to be reproduced or repeated
