primitive

noun
root word for derivatives
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪm.ɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A word that serves as the basis for the formation of inflected or derived forms.

Examples

primitive word
In language studies, linguists often identify the primitive of a word family to trace its origins.
The dictionary lists 'act' as the primitive for words like 'action', 'active', and 'actor'.
The verb 'pick' is considered the primitive from which the noun 'picket' is derived.

Related Terms

  • antonym – derivative: a word formed from another by derivation, such as by adding a prefix or suffix
  • synonym – root: the basic part of a word, to which affixes can be added in derivation
