to surfeit

verb
to feed or indulge to excess
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪt / (BrE)
to supply or consume too much of something, especially food, so that it becomes unpleasant

Examples

to surfeit yourself
Eating too many sweets can easily surfeit even the most ardent chocolate lover.
He surfeited himself on rich foods during the holidays and felt unwell afterwards.
If you surfeit on distractions, productivity inevitably suffers.
After being surfeited with rich foods all week, she craved something plain.
He warned that the children might be surfeited with too much television.
The guests were surfeited with countless desserts at the banquet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – satiate: to satisfy fully, especially with food or pleasure
  • antonym – starve: to suffer or die from lack of food
