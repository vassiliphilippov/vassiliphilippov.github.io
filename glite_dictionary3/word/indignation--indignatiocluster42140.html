indignation

noun
righteous anger; anger at injustice
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
anger or annoyance provoked by what is perceived as unfair treatment or injustice

Examples

public indignation
He could not hide his indignation at the way he was treated by the officials.
Her voice quivered with indignation as she spoke about the unfair decision.
There was a sense of indignation among the employees after the announcement of wage cuts.
