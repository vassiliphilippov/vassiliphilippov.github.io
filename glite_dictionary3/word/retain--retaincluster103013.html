to retain

verb
to continue to keep or hold
Pronunciation: / rɪˈteɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪn / (BrE)
to continue to have or keep something, such as a position, possession, or quality

Examples

to retain a position
Despite criticism, she chose to retain her position as manager.
The law firm was retained to represent the company in court.
This fabric retains its color even after multiple washes.
She managed to retain her composure during the stressful interview.
The company hopes to retain its best employees by offering competitive salaries.
You should retain all your receipts for tax purposes.
