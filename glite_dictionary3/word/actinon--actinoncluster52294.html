actinon

noun
radioactive elements, atomic numbers 89–103
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tɪˌnɑn / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.nɒn / (BrE)
any of a series of radioactive elements with atomic numbers 89 through 103, known as the actinides.

Examples

radioactive actinon element
Scientists study the properties of each actinon to better understand radioactive decay.
The actinon elements play a crucial role in nuclear energy and weaponry.
Uranium and plutonium are both members of the actinon series of elements.
