knock-knee

noun
condition where knees angle inward
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑkˌni / (AmE), / ˈnɒkˌniː / (BrE)
a condition in which the knees angle inward and touch one another when the legs are straightened

Examples

knock-knee condition
Children with knock-knee often grow out of it as they age.
Special braces may be used to help correct severe knock-knee in young patients.
The doctor diagnosed him with knock-knee and recommended physical therapy.
