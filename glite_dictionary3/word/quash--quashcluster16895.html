to quash

verb
suppress or put down by force
Pronunciation: / kwɑʃ / (AmE), / kwɒʃ / (BrE)
to suppress something forcibly or put an end to it, especially by the use of authority or force

Examples

to quash a protest
Protesters feared the government would quash their movement with strict new laws.
The company tried to quash rumors about the new product's failure.
The military was ordered to quash the rebellion before it spread to other regions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – encourage: to give support, confidence, or hope to; to foster
  • synonym – subdue: to overcome or bring under control, especially by force
  • synonym – suppress: to put an end to something by force
