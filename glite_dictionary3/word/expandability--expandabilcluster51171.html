expandability

noun
capacity to expand (esp. of gases)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌspændəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌspændəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to expand, especially the property of gases to increase in volume and fill their container.

Examples

expandability of gases
Engineers must consider the expandability of materials when designing pressure vessels.
Studying the expandability of heated air is important in understanding weather patterns.
The expandability of gases makes them different from solids and liquids.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compressibility: ability of a substance to be compressed
  • synonym – expansion: process of increasing in size, volume, or amount
