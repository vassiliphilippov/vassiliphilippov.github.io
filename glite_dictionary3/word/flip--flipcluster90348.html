flip

noun
sudden, quick movement
Pronunciation: / flɪp / (AmE), / flɪp / (BrE)
a quick, light movement or action, especially tossing, turning, or flicking something (including passing a ball)

Examples

quick flip of thumb
He caught the coin with a quick flip of his thumb.
The gymnast executed a perfect flip during her routine.
With a deft flip, she turned the pancake in the air.
She called heads before the flip, hoping luck would be on her side.
They resolved the dispute by a flip of a coin.
With a single flip, the referee decided which team would start the game.
His unexpected flip caught the defense off guard during the basketball game.
She moved up the court and finished the play with a slick flip to her teammate.
The quarterback made a quick flip to the running back, who gained ten yards.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flick: a sudden quick movement or light blow
  • synonym – jerk: a quick, sharp movement
  • synonym – pass: an act of moving the ball to a teammate in a game
  • synonym – toss: the act of throwing something lightly or casually, especially a coin
