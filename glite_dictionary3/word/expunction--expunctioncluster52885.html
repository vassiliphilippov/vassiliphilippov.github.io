expunction

noun
deletion or erasure, esp. of a record
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act of erasing, deleting, or removing something, especially from a record or document

Examples

expunction of records
After the successful court petition, the expunction of her criminal record allowed her to apply for new jobs.
His attorney filed for the expunction of all charges, citing his innocence.
Many states permit the expunction of certain juvenile offenses after a set number of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expungement: the process of officially removing a record, especially in legal contexts
