to rick

verb
to pile into a rick (hay, etc.)
Pronunciation: / rɪk / (AmE), / rɪk / (BrE)
to pile (hay or similar material) into a rick for storage, especially outdoors

Examples

to rick the straw
Farmers used to rick hay in the fields before it was common to have barns.
For generations, workers would rick the harvested crops after the autumn season.
They ricked the straw to keep it dry over the winter.
