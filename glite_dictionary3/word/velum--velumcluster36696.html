velum

noun
muscular flap at the back of the mouth
Pronunciation: / ˈviːləm / (AmE), / ˈviːləm / (BrE)
a muscular flap at the back of the mouth that closes off the nasopharynx during swallowing or speaking

Examples

velum in the mouth
A cleft velum can lead to speech difficulties in children.
During swallowing, the velum lifts to prevent food from entering the nasal cavity.
The doctor explained how the velum functions in separating the oral and nasal passages.

Related Terms

  • synonym – soft palate: the soft part at the back of the roof of the mouth, synonymous with velum
