tonic

noun
first note of a diatonic scale (music)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(music) The first and main note of a diatonic scale, on which a piece of music is often based.

Examples

tonic note C
In C major, the note C is the tonic.
The melody begins on the tonic and rises to the dominant.
The symphony returns to the tonic at the end of the movement.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – dominant: the fifth note of the diatonic scale
  • degree lower – subdominant: the fourth note of the diatonic scale
  • synonym – tonality: the key or tonal system based on the tonic
