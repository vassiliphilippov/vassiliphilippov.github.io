thermography

noun
medical imaging by recording body heat
Pronunciation: / θɚˈmɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / θəˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A diagnostic technique that records and produces images showing the heat emitted by different parts of the body, often used to study blood flow or detect tumors.

Examples

medical thermography scan
Thermography is often used in medicine to help detect abnormalities in blood flow.
Advances in thermography have improved the accuracy of early cancer detection.
The doctor recommended thermography as a non-invasive way to check for possible tumors.
