to snap

verb
speak angrily or abruptly
Pronunciation: / snæp / (AmE), / snæp / (BrE)
to speak or act suddenly and sharply, especially in anger or after losing control

Examples

to snap at someone
"Don’t talk to me like that!" he snapped angrily.
She snapped at him when he asked another question.
Tired and frustrated, Julia snapped back a harsh reply.
After hours of criticism, he finally snapped and started yelling at his boss.
She tried to stay calm, but she snapped when the children wouldn't stop fighting.
Under pressure from the demanding schedule, the young actor almost snapped during rehearsals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bark: to speak abruptly or sharply
  • synonym – break down: lose control of emotions, typically by crying or becoming extremely upset
  • antonym – keep it together: maintain emotional composure under stress
  • synonym – snarl: to say something angrily or threateningly
