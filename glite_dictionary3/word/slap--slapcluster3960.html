to slap

verb
strike with the flat hand or object
Pronunciation: / slæp / (AmE), / slæp / (BrE)
to hit someone or something quickly with the flat part of the hand or a flat object, sometimes as a form of punishment

Examples

to slap a face
He slapped the book down on the table, startling everyone.
She was so angry that she slapped his face in front of everyone.
The water slapped against the side of the boat during the storm.
In some cultures, parents may slap their children as a form of discipline.
She slapped him for making a rude comment.
The teacher threatened to slap anyone who spoke out of turn.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hit: to strike someone or something with force
  • antonym – punch: to hit with a closed fist
  • synonym – smack: to strike with a sharp blow, especially with the hand
