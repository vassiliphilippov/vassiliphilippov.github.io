indeed

adverb
used to emphasize or confirm a statement
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdid / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːd / (BrE)
used to emphasize a statement or response, or to confirm and stress the truth of a preceding statement

Examples

very cold indeed
It was cold indeed when we arrived at the mountain cabin.
She is indeed one of the most talented artists of her generation.
The new policy is, indeed, a significant improvement over the old one.

Related Terms

  • synonym – certainly: used to show that something is definitely true or will definitely happen
  • synonym – really: used to emphasize the truth or reality of something
