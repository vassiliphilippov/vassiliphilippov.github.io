to prostrate

verb
lay or throw oneself flat on the ground
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː streɪt /; / ˈprɑː.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs treɪt /; / ˈprɒs.treɪt / (BrE)
to lie or throw yourself flat on the ground, especially as a sign of humility, submission, or adoration

Examples

to prostrate yourself
At the emperor’s feet, the envoy prostrated himself in a gesture of utter submission.
She prostrated herself on the ground to beg forgiveness.
The monk prostrated himself before the altar in silent prayer.
As a sign of repentance, she prostrated herself on the cold stone floor.
He prostrated himself at the king's feet as a sign of loyalty.
The monks would prostrate themselves before the altar during prayer.

  • degree lower – kneel: to get down on one's knees, usually as a sign of respect or worship
