to sour

verb
make or become sour
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊər /; / ˈsaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə / (BrE)
to make or become sour in taste, or to turn bad or spoiled

Examples

to sour quickly
His mood seemed to sour after hearing the bad news.
If you leave milk out overnight, it will sour quickly.
Lemon juice can sour the flavor of the stew if used excessively.
If you leave juice out in the heat, it will sour within a day.
Sometimes cream sours quickly if the lid isn't sealed tightly.
The milk soured when the refrigerator broke down last week.
