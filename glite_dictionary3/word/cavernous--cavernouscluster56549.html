cavernous

adjective
like a cavern; vast and deep
Pronunciation: / ˈkævərnəs / (AmE), / ˈkævənəs / (BrE)
(of a space or opening) very large, vast, and resembling or suggestive of a cavern, especially in being dark, deep, or hollow.

Examples

cavernous room space
His eyes looked sunken and cavernous after weeks of illness.
She spoke in a cavernous hall, her voice fading into the darkness above.
The abandoned warehouse had a cavernous interior that echoed with every step.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cramped: uncomfortably small or restricted
  • synonym – spacious: large and having ample space
