organza

noun
a crisp, sheer fabric used in clothing
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈɡænzə / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænzə / (BrE)
a thin, plain weave fabric, traditionally of silk, but also made of synthetic fibers, that is crisp and sheer, resembling organdy

Examples

organza dress fabric
A light organza scarf can be a perfect accessory for summer evenings.
Designers often use organza to add volume and structure to formal gowns.
The bride chose a dress made of white organza for its delicate appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – organdy: a fine, thin cotton fabric, usually stiffened, used in women's clothing
