to freeze

verb
turn to solid due to cold
Pronunciation: / friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
to become or cause to become solid or hard because of cold; to change from a liquid to a solid due to low temperature

Examples

to freeze water
If you leave the juice outside overnight in winter, it will freeze by morning.
The lake begins to freeze when the temperature drops below zero.
When water freezes, it expands and can crack pipes.
Be sure to cover the plants at night or they might freeze in the cold weather.
If the pipes freeze during winter, they can burst and cause damage.
She poured water into a tray and left it outside to freeze overnight.
I froze the berries so they would last all winter.
Please freeze the soup if you aren't eating it right away.
The bank decided to freeze his assets during the investigation.
