lifeblood

noun
essential or vital force
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪfˌblʌd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌblʌd / (BrE)
the thing that is most important for the continued success or existence of something

Examples

lifeblood of business
Agriculture has always been the lifeblood of the region's economy.
Innovation is considered the lifeblood of the tech industry.
Tourism serves as the lifeblood for many small island communities.
