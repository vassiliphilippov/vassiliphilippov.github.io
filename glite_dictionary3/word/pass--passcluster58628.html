to pass

verb
expel from the body
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
to expel something from the body, especially waste or a foreign object

Examples

to pass a stone
He passed a gallstone during the night and felt immediate relief.
If you cannot pass urine for several hours, contact your doctor immediately.
The patient was finally able to pass the kidney stone after several days of pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – excrete: to expel waste from the body
  • antonym – retain: to keep within the body, not expel
