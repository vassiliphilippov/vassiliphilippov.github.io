presbyope

noun
person with age-related farsightedness
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛz.biˌoʊp / (AmE), / ˈprɛzbɪəʊp / (BrE)
a person who has presbyopia, typically experiencing farsightedness due to aging and loss of lens elasticity

Examples

aging presbyope
After the age of forty, it's common to become a presbyope and require vision correction for close work.
As a lifelong presbyope, she needed reading glasses for small print.
Many presbyopes find that bifocals help them adapt to their changing vision.
