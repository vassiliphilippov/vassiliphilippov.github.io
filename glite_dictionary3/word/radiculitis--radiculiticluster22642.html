radiculitis

noun
inflammation of a nerve root
Pronunciation: / rəˌdɪkjəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / rəˌdɪkjʊˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the root of a nerve, causing pain and other symptoms along the affected nerve

Examples

radiculitis with nerve pain
Certain infections can lead to radiculitis by inflaming the nerve roots in the spine.
The patient was diagnosed with radiculitis after complaining of sharp pain radiating along her arm.
Treatment for radiculitis typically includes anti-inflammatory medications and physical therapy.
