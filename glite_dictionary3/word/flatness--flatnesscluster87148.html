flatness

noun
digital-design aesthetic of two-dimensional simplicity
Pronunciation: / ˈflætnəs / (AmE), / ˈflætnəs / (BrE)
(in digital design) the quality or aesthetic of a user interface that uses flat, two-dimensional elements, solid colours, minimal shading, and few depth cues; a design style contrasted with skeuomorphism.

Examples

flatness in design
Many apps now embrace flatness, favoring solid colors and minimal shading over skeuomorphic depth.
Designers praised the flatness of the new site because it improved clarity and loading speed.
Critics argued that excessive flatness can harm usability by removing clear visual affordances for users.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flat design: a design movement that favors simple, two-dimensional elements and minimal ornamentation
  • synonym – minimalism (design): a design approach that reduces elements to the essentials to improve clarity and usability
  • antonym – skeuomorphism: a design approach that mimics real-world textures and objects to suggest function; often contrasted with flatness
