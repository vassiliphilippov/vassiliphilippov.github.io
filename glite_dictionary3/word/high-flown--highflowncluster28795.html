high-flown

adjective
using pretentious or grand language
Pronunciation: / haɪ floʊn /; / ˈhaɪ floʊn / (AmE), / haɪ fləʊn /; / ˈhaɪ fləʊn / (BrE)
using very elaborate, grand, or elevated language or ideas, often in a way that seems exaggerated, unrealistic, or pretentious

Examples

high-flown language
Critics dismissed the politician's high-flown promises as unrealistic and empty.
She preferred simple explanations instead of high-flown philosophical theories.
The professor's high-flown rhetoric failed to convince the skeptical audience.
Critics described the film's message as high-flown, arguing it was idealistic but lacked practical relevance.
She tends to express her ideas in a rather high-flown manner, making them sound more important than they are.
The professor's high-flown speeches often left students feeling disconnected from practical realities.
