sensitising

noun
making sensitive to a serum via injections
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of making an organism sensitive to a substance, especially by repeated injections of a serum, so that it reacts to it upon subsequent exposure.

Examples

sensitising injection
Repeated sensitising injections were given before testing the immune response.
The sensitising of laboratory animals is an essential step in some allergy experiments.
The process of sensitising can help researchers study allergic reactions in controlled settings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitising: the process of making less sensitive, especially to an allergen or stimulus
