immanency

noun
the state of being inherent or within
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmənənsi / (AmE), / ˈɪmənənsi / (BrE)
The state or condition of being inherent or existing within something; not transcending a particular domain.

Examples

immanency of ideas
He questioned whether creativity possesses a sense of immanency or comes from external inspiration.
Many religious traditions emphasize the immanency of the divine in the natural world.
The philosopher discussed the immanency of ethical values within human society.

Related Terms

  • antonym – transcendence: the state of being beyond or above the range of normal experience
