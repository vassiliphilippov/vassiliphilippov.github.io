staminate

adjective
having stamens; male (in botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈstæm.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈstæm.ɪ.nət / (BrE)
(of a flower or plant) having stamens and capable of producing pollen, but lacking functional pistils; male in reproductive function.

Examples

staminate flower
Biologists examined the staminate structures to study the plant's reproductive cycle.
Some trees produce separate staminate and pistillate blossoms on the same individual.
The staminate flowers release pollen early in the season to fertilize nearby plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pistillate: (of a flower) having pistils but no functional stamens; female (in botany)
