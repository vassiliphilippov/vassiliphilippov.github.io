to plaster

verb
cover thickly with a substance
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstɚ /; / ˈplæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə /; / ˈplɑː.stə / (BrE)
to cover a surface thickly or conspicuously with a substance or by sticking things all over it

Examples

to plaster a wall
He plastered his bread with butter, hardly leaving any room for the jam.
She plastered her face with sunscreen before going out.
The walls were plastered with posters for the concert.
After the concert, fans plastered the city with photos of the band.
She plastered warning posters all over the school entrance.
The protesters plastered the walls with slogans and flyers.
After the repairs, they plastered the wall to create a smooth finish.
She carefully plasters the drywall to prepare it for wallpaper.
The workers plastered the ceiling before painting it.
