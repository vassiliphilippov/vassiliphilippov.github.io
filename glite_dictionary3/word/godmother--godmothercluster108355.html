godmother

noun
female sponsor at baptism
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑdˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who acts as a sponsor at a child's baptism, often taking an interest in the child's upbringing and religious education

Examples

godmother at baptism
My aunt was honored to be chosen as my godmother.
She invited her godmother to her graduation ceremony.
The godmother promised to guide the child in moral and spiritual matters.
