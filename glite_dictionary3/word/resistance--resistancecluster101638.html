resistance

noun
unwillingness to face repressed feelings (psychiatry)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
(Psychiatry) An unwillingness or inability to bring repressed feelings or memories into conscious awareness, often regarded as a defense mechanism during therapy.

Examples

resistance in therapy
Analysts are trained to recognize signs of resistance during psychoanalytic therapy.
Overcoming resistance is often a key part of effective psychotherapy.
The patient showed significant resistance when asked to discuss early childhood experiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defense mechanism: an unconscious psychological strategy used to cope with reality and maintain self-image
