to confuse

verb
mix up elements without order
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuz / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to mix up elements so that their order is not clear or logical; to assemble things without order or sense

Examples

confuse the files
If you confuse the files, it will be impossible to find anything.
Please don't confuse the puzzle pieces by mixing them all together.
She accidentally confused the ingredients while preparing the recipe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jumble: to mix things together without order
  • antonym – order: to arrange things neatly or methodically; opposite of confuse
