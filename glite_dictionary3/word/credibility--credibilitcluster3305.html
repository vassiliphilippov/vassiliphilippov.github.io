credibility

noun
quality of being believable or trustworthy
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɛdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌkrɛdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being trusted and believed in; the degree to which something is believable or trustworthy.

Examples

gain credibility
Building credibility takes time and consistent honesty.
The company's financial scandal damaged its credibility with investors.
The witness's story has little credibility because it keeps changing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trustworthiness: the quality of being reliable and deserving of trust
  • antonym – unreliability: the quality of not being reliable or trustworthy
