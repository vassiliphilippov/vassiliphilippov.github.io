scruples

noun
moral or ethical doubts or reservations
Pronunciation: / ˈskruːpəlz / (AmE), / ˈskruːplz / (BrE)
feelings of doubt or hesitation that arise from moral or ethical considerations

Examples

moral scruples

He had no scruples about lying to get what he wanted.
Her scruples prevented her from accepting the bribe.
