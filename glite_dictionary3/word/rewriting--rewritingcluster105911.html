rewriting

noun
act of revising by writing again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of revising a written text by writing it again, often to improve clarity or style

Examples

rewriting a draft
After receiving feedback from her editor, his novel required significant rewriting.
She spent the weekend rewriting her essay to make her arguments clearer.
The rewriting of the script took several months before it was ready for filming.
