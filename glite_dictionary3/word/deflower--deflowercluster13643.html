to deflower

verb
deprive of virginity
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflaʊər / (AmE), / dɪˈflaʊə / (BrE)
to deprive (a woman or girl) of her virginity, typically in a way that suggests loss of innocence

Examples

to deflower a young woman
He swore he would never deflower a woman without her consent.
Many old novels use the word deflower to refer to loss of virginity.
The story suggests that the villain planned to deflower the innocent princess.
