antitauon

noun
antiparticle of the tau lepton
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈtaʊ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈtaʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
A subatomic antiparticle, the antiparticle counterpart of the tauon, carrying positive charge and having very great mass among antileptons.

Examples

an antitauon particle
Scientists observed the decay of an antitauon in the particle accelerator experiment.
The antitauon is produced only in high-energy collisions due to its large mass.
Unlike an electron or a positron, an antitauon exists for an extremely short duration.
