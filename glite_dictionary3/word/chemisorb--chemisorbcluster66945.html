to chemisorb

verb
adsorb by chemical bonding
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmɪsɔːrb / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪsɔːb / (BrE)
(of a solid, especially a catalyst) to take up and hold another substance by means of chemical adsorption, forming a chemical bond between the surfaces.

Examples

to chemisorb gas molecules
Catalysts often chemisorb reactant molecules on their surfaces to increase reaction rates.
Certain metals can chemisorb hydrogen, making them useful in fuel cells.
The material was found to chemisorb oxygen at elevated temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – physisorb: adsorb a substance by physical, not chemical, means
