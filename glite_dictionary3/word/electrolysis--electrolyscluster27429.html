electrolysis

noun
decomposition by electric current (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
The chemical decomposition of a substance by passing an electric current through a solution containing ions.

Examples

electrolysis of water
Copper can be purified using electrolysis in an electrolytic cell.
During the science experiment, students observed electrolysis splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen gas.
The industrial production of chlorine involves electrolysis of brine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electrolyte: a substance that conducts electricity when dissolved in water
  • synonym – electroplating: process of using electrolysis to deposit a metal coating
