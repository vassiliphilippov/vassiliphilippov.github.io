to blunder

verb
move clumsily or blindly
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
to move or progress in a clumsy or confused way, often without clear direction

Examples

to blunder through darkness
In the pitch-black hallway, he blundered into the furniture.
She blundered through the crowd, looking for the exit.
The lost hiker blundered onward, unsure of which path to take.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stumble: to trip or lose balance while moving
