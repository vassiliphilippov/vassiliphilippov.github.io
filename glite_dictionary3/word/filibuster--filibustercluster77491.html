filibuster

noun
legislator who delays by long speeches
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a legislator who gives long speeches to delay or obstruct legislative action, especially in a parliamentary context

Examples

filibuster senator
As a dedicated filibuster, she often delayed crucial votes in the legislature.
Some critics argue that the role of a filibuster undermines the efficiency of parliamentary proceedings.
The senator became a famous filibuster, known for his lengthy speeches against proposed laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym – obstructionist: a person who deliberately obstructs progress, especially in a legislative context
