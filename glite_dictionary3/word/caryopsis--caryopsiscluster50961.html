caryopsis

noun
type of dry fruit in cereal grasses
Pronunciation: / ˌkæriˈɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkærɪˈɒpsɪs / (BrE)
a dry, one-seeded fruit characteristic of the cereal grasses, in which the seed coat is fused with the fruit wall, as in wheat, barley, and corn.

Examples

wheat caryopsis
Barley stores nutrients in its caryopsis for the developing seedling.
Each kernel of corn is actually a caryopsis, unique to grasses.
The wheat caryopsis is the primary source of flour worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grain: a small, hard seed, especially the edible seed of a cereal grass
