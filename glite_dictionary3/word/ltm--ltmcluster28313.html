LTM

noun
abbreviation: last twelve months (finance)
Pronunciation: / ˌel ˌti ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌel ˌtiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
abbreviation for 'last twelve months' (finance): the most recently completed 12-month period used when reporting or analysing financial results.

Examples

LTM financial results
The company's LTM revenue grew by 6% compared with the prior year.
Analysts prefer using LTM margins when comparing performance across firms with different fiscal years.
When valuing startups, investors often look at LTM figures rather than only quarterly results.

Related Terms

  • synonym – TTM: trailing twelve months; an equivalent financial measure that sums the most recent 12 months of results
