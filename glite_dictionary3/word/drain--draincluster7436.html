drain

noun
gradual depletion of resources or energy
Pronunciation: / dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
a continuous or gradual depletion of energy, resources, or talent

Examples

energy drain
Caring for her sick father was a constant emotional drain.
The new project has become a drain on the company's finances.
There has been a significant drain of skilled workers to larger cities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expenditure: the act of spending resources
  • antonym – replenishment: the act of refilling or restoring something that has been depleted
