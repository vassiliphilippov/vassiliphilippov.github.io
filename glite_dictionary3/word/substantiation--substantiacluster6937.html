substantiation

noun
proof supporting a claim or theory
Pronunciation: / səbˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən /; / sʌbˌstan.tiˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
evidence or proof that supports a statement, or the act of providing such evidence

Examples

substantiation of a claim
Recent studies offer strong substantiation of the connection between diet and mental health.
The lawyer demanded substantiation for the witness's claims before proceeding further.
Without proper substantiation, his theory was quickly dismissed by the scientific community.
His argument lacked sufficient substantiation to convince the committee.
The scientist's claim awaits substantiation through further experiments.
Without clear substantiation, her allegations remain unproven.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confirmation: the act of making something more certain
  • synonym – corroboration: evidence which confirms or supports a statement, theory, or finding
  • antonym – refutation: the act of proving something to be false
