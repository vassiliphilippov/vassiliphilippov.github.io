Anton van Leuwenhoek

proper noun
Dutch pioneer microscopist (1632–1723)
Pronunciation: / ˈænˌtɑn væn ˈleɪvənˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈantɒn van ˈleɪvənˌhʊk / (BrE)
(1632–1723) Dutch scientist known as the pioneer of microscopy, who was among the first to observe animal cells and described microbes, spermatozoa, and blood corpuscles.

Examples

Anton van Leuwenhoek discovered microbes
Anton van Leuwenhoek is often called the 'father of microbiology' due to his pioneering observations.
Many modern biological terms originated from the work conducted by Anton van Leuwenhoek in the 17th century.
The discoveries made by Anton van Leuwenhoek transformed our understanding of the microscopic world.
