phytophagous

adjective
feeding on plants (of animals)
Pronunciation: / faɪˈtɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / faɪˈtɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) feeding on plants; herbivorous.

Examples

phytophagous insects
Because it is phytophagous, the caterpillar feeds exclusively on the host plant’s foliage.
Many insects are phytophagous, relying on leaves, stems, and fruits for their diet.
The phytophagous habits of certain beetles make them serious agricultural pests.
