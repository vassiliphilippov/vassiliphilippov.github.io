foram

noun
marine microorganism with a perforated shell
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːrˌæm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːrəm / (BrE)
A marine microorganism with a calcareous shell that has openings through which pseudopods extend.

Examples

marine foram
Each foram has a unique shell structure that helps scientists identify different species.
Researchers collected forams from seabed samples to study oceanic changes over time.
The microscopic foram fossils provided valuable clues about ancient climates.
