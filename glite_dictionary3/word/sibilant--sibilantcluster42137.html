sibilant

adjective
speech sound with hissing or hushing quality
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪbɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced by forcing air through a narrow channel, resulting in a hissing or hushing sound, as in 's', 'z', or 'sh'.

Examples

sibilant speech sound
Her accent was characterized by a strongly sibilant pronunciation of certain consonants.
The teacher demonstrated how the sibilant 's' sound can be confusing for language learners.
The word 'hiss' contains a highly sibilant sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fricative: speech sound produced by forcing air through a narrow passage
