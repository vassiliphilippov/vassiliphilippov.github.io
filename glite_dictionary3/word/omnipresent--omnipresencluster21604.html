omnipresent

adjective
present everywhere at once
Pronunciation: / ɑm.nɪˈprɛz.ənt / (AmE), / ɒm.nɪˈprez.ənt / (BrE)
present everywhere at the same time; widespread or constantly encountered.

Examples

omnipresent influence
She felt an omnipresent sense of dread throughout the dark house.
Surveillance cameras are becoming omnipresent in urban areas.
The influence of technology is omnipresent in modern society.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rare: not occurring often; not common or frequent
  • synonym – ubiquitous: present, appearing, or found everywhere
