Obadiah

proper noun
a Hebrew prophet and biblical book
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊbəˈdaɪə / (AmE), / ˌəʊbəˈdaɪə / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet in the Old Testament who prophesied against Edom; also, the name of the biblical book attributed to him.

Examples

prophet Obadiah
Obadiah is one of the twelve minor prophets whose book appears in the Old Testament.
Many scholars have debated the historical context in which Obadiah delivered his message.
The prophecy of Obadiah focuses on the judgment of Edom for its actions against Israel.
