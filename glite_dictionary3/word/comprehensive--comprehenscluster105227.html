comprehensive

adjective
broad in scope; inclusive
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something; complete, thorough, or broad in scope

Examples

comprehensive review
She conducted a comprehensive review before making her final decision.
The report offered a comprehensive overview of the country's healthcare system.
We need a comprehensive plan to address all aspects of the project.
She gave a comprehensive list of all the materials required for the project.
The report provided a comprehensive analysis of the company's financial performance.
We need a comprehensive approach to address the challenges of climate change.

  • antonym – partial: existing only in part; not complete
