fissionable

adjective
capable of undergoing nuclear fission
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃənəbl / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of undergoing nuclear fission when hit by a neutron, especially used of atoms or materials.

Examples

fissionable material
Plutonium-239 is a highly fissionable isotope used in nuclear reactors.
The scientists carefully measured the amount of fissionable material present in the sample.
Uranium-235 is classified as a fissionable material due to its ability to sustain a chain reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fissile: capable of being split or divided, especially in nuclear physics
