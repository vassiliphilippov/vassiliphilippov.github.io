bearskin

noun
pelt of a bear
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɛəˌskɪn / (BrE)
the skin and fur of a bear, especially when used as a material

Examples

a bearskin rug
A thick bearskin rug covered the cabin floor, adding both warmth and decoration.
Early settlers often used bearskin to make coats and hats for winter.
The hunter proudly displayed the bearskin he had brought back from his trip.
