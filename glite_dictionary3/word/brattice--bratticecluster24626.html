brattice

noun
temporary mine partition for ventilation
Pronunciation: / ˈbrætɪs / (AmE), / ˈbrætɪs / (BrE)
A temporary partition of planks or cloth used to control ventilation in a mine.

Examples

wooden brattice wall
A wooden brattice separated the main shaft from the working faces.
After the explosion, the brattice was quickly replaced to restore proper ventilation.
The miners installed a brattice to improve airflow in the lower tunnel.
