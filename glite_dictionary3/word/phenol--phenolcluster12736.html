phenol

noun
weakly acidic organic compound (aromatic with hydroxyl group)
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːnɔl, ˈfiːnɑl / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɒl / (BrE)
a weakly acidic organic compound consisting of a hydroxyl group attached directly to an aromatic hydrocarbon ring, or any compound in a class of substances with this structure

Examples

phenol organic structure
Phenol is commonly used as a disinfectant in hospitals due to its antibacterial properties.
Chemists often study the reactivity of substituted phenols in organic synthesis.
Direct exposure to concentrated phenol can cause burns on the skin.
