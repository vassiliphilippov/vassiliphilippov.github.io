topical

adjective
applied to the surface of the body
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑː.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(of a medicine or treatment) applied directly to a particular part of the surface of the body.

Examples

topical cream
Some painkillers are available as topical gels for local relief.
The topical treatment should not be swallowed or applied to broken skin.
The dermatologist prescribed a topical cream to be applied to the rash twice daily.

Related Terms

  • antonym – systemic: (of a drug or treatment) affecting the entire body rather than a single part
