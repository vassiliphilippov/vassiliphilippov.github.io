strap

noun
long narrow strip for fastening
Pronunciation: / stræp / (AmE), / stræp / (BrE)
a long, narrow strip or loop of leather, cloth, or similar material used for fastening, holding, supporting, or for holding onto (such as on a bag, garment, or public transport)

Examples

strap of a bag
He secured the package with a strong strap to prevent it from opening.
She adjusted the strap on her backpack for a better fit.
The old suitcase had a leather strap to keep the lid closed.
He grabbed the strap as the bus lurched forward.
Many commuters rely on the strap for balance during rush hour.
The old train cars still had leather straps instead of metal bars.
He replaced the broken strap on his favorite camera bag.
She adjusted the strap of her backpack before leaving for school.
The dress has a delicate strap that sits lightly on the shoulder.

  • synonym – handgrip: a handle or grip for holding, especially on vehicles
