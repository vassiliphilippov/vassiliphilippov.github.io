Pontius Pilate

proper noun
Roman governor who ordered Jesus' crucifixion
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑn(t)ʃəs ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpɒn(t)ʃɪəs ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
(c. AD 20–36) The Roman governor of Judea who, according to the New Testament, presided over the trial of Jesus and authorized his crucifixion.

Examples

Pontius Pilate at Jesus' trial
Many historians debate the motivations of Pontius Pilate during Jesus' trial.
Some paintings depict Pontius Pilate washing his hands to show innocence.
The story of Pontius Pilate is often referenced in Christian teachings.
