strobilus

noun
cone-shaped plant structure with ovules or spores
Pronunciation: / stroʊˈbaɪləs / (AmE), / strəʊˈbɪləs / (BrE)
A cone-shaped structure in certain plants, such as conifers and horsetails, consisting of a cluster of scales or bracts that bear ovules or spores.

Examples

strobilus of a pine
Scientists examined the structure of the strobilus under a microscope to study spore development.
The horsetail plant develops a distinct strobilus at the tip of its stem in spring.
The pine tree produces a strobilus containing the seeds necessary for reproduction.
