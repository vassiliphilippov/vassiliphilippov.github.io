acathexis

noun
absence of emotional response (psychoanalysis)
Pronunciation: / ˌækəˈθɛksɪs / (AmE), / ˌækəˈθɛksɪs / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) A lack of emotional investment in significant objects, people, or memories; a condition where they arouse no feeling.

Examples

acathexis toward memories
His acathexis complicated therapy, as he showed no attachment to significant relationships.
Symptoms such as acathexis can indicate certain psychological conditions requiring further evaluation.
The psychologist observed acathexis in patients who failed to react emotionally to traumatic memories.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cathexis: concentration of emotional energy on an object or idea (psychoanalysis)
