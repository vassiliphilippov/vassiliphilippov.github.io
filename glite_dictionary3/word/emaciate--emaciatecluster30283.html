to emaciate

verb
cause to become abnormally thin or weak
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmeɪʃieɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmasɪeɪt / (BrE)
to make abnormally thin or weak, especially as a result of illness or lack of food

Examples

to emaciate a patient
Illness can emaciate even the strongest individuals if left untreated.
The disease quickly emaciated the once robust stray dog.
The harsh diet began to emaciate him after several weeks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – strengthen: to make or become stronger
  • synonym – weaken: to make or become weaker
