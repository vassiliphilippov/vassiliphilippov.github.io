bodega

noun
small grocery store, esp. in Hispanic neighborhoods
Pronunciation: / boʊˈdeɪɡə / (AmE), / bəʊˈdeɪɡə / (BrE)
a small grocery store, especially one in a Spanish-speaking neighborhood or urban area, often selling wine and everyday items

Examples

corner bodega

Many New Yorkers rely on their local bodega for quick snacks and groceries.
The bodega on the corner is always open late and sells fresh sandwiches.
