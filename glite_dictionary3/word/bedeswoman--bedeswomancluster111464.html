bedeswoman

noun
female paid to pray for another's soul (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːdzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbiːdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who was paid or supported to pray for the soul of another, especially in medieval Christian practice.

Examples

medieval bedeswoman prayers
In the medieval period, a bedeswoman often lived at the expense of a monastery or parish.
Records show that several bedeswomen were supported by the local church community.
The wealthy merchant arranged for a bedeswoman to pray for his soul after his death.
