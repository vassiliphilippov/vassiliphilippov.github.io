theory

noun
tentative explanation of facts or phenomena
Pronunciation: / ˈθiəri / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
an idea or set of ideas intended to explain facts or events, especially one not yet proven but that could account for them

Examples

theory of evolution
Before it gained widespread support, the germ theory of disease faced significant skepticism.
Einstein's theory of relativity transformed our understanding of time and space.
The scientist presented a new theory that could explain the unusual results.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fact: something that is known or proven to be true
  • degree lower – hypothesis: a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
  • degree higher – law: a statement based on repeated experimental observations that describes some aspect of the world
