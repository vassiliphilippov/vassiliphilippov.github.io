autotelism

noun
belief that art is an end in itself
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtoʊˈtɛlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈtɛlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that a work of art is an end in itself and does not require external justification

Examples

autotelism in art theory
Modernist poetry often embraces autotelism, focusing solely on artistic form and internal purpose.
Some critics believe in autotelism, defending the autonomy of artistic expression.
The concept of autotelism argues that art requires no external justification or function beyond itself.

Related Terms

  • synonym – art for art's sake: the idea that art should be valued for its own sake, not for any moral or political purpose
