buzzword

noun
fashionable or overused word or phrase
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌzˌwɜrd / (AmE), / ˈbʌzwɜːd / (BrE)
a word or phrase, often from jargon, that is fashionable or popular, especially when used so often that it loses its original meaning or becomes trivial

Examples

trendy buzzword
Every year, a new buzzword seems to sweep through the technology industry.
Many politicians rely on buzzwords to make their messages sound important without saying much.
The term 'synergy' became a tired buzzword in corporate meetings.

  • synonym – cliché: an overused phrase or idea
  • synonym – jargon: specialized language used by a particular group
