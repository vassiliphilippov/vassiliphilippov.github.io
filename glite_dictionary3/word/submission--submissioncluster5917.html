submission

noun
legal document agreeing to arbitration
Pronunciation: / səbˈmɪʃən /; / səbˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən /; / səbˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a legal document or agreement in which parties to a dispute agree to accept the decision of an arbitrator

Examples

arbitration submission
A judge reviewed the submission before issuing their final ruling.
The submission was filed with the court to formalize the arbitration agreement.
The parties signed a submission agreeing to resolve the dispute through arbitration.
A formal submission ensures that both sides abide by the outcome of the arbitration process.
Before proceeding with litigation, the parties signed a submission to be bound by the arbitrator's decision.
The two companies reached a submission and agreed to let an independent arbiter resolve the dispute.
