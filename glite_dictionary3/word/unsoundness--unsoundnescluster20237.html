unsoundness

noun
state of being not valid or logically sound
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsaʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not valid, true, or logically sound; the presence of a mistaken or fallacious basis

Examples

unsoundness of argument
Many were convinced by the proposal, despite its obvious unsoundness.
She criticized the unsoundness in their reasoning during the debate.
The unsoundness of his argument was quickly exposed by the experts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – error: a mistake or incorrect belief
  • synonym – fallacy: a mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument
  • antonym – validity: the quality of being logically or factually sound
