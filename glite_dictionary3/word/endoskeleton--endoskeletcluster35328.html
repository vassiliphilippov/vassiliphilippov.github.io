endoskeleton

noun
internal skeleton of an animal
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈskɛlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈskɛlɪtən / (BrE)
the internal skeleton of an animal, consisting of bone or cartilage, especially in vertebrates

Examples

endoskeleton of a shark
Sharks have an endoskeleton composed mainly of cartilage instead of bone.
The human body has an endoskeleton made primarily of bones and cartilage.
Unlike insects, which have exoskeletons, mammals are supported by an endoskeleton.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exoskeleton: external skeleton supporting and protecting an animal's body
