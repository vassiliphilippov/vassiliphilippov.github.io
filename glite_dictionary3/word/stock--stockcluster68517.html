stock

noun
supply kept for future use
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɒk / (BrE)
the goods or merchandise that a shop or business has for sale or in storage; a supply of something kept for future use or sale

Examples

stock the shelves
The manager checked the stock to make sure there were enough supplies for the week.
They ran out of printer paper because their stock was not replenished in time.
We need to reduce old stock before ordering new products.
Many items in the stock were on sale at the end of the month.
The bookstore ordered new stock to prepare for the holiday season.
The store manager checked the stock before placing another order with suppliers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inventory: a complete list or supply of items kept in stock
  • synonym – reserve: an extra supply set aside for future use
  • antonym – shortage: a state when the stock or supply is insufficient
  • synonym – supply: an amount of something available for use
