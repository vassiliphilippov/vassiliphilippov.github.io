Lordship

noun
title for British peer, bishop, or judge
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdʃɪp / (BrE)
a title or form of address used for certain British peers (except dukes), as well as bishops and judges

Examples

His Lordship arrives
Her property is under the supervision of His Lordship.
The solicitor addressed the judge as Lordship throughout the proceedings.
Your Lordship is requested to review the document at your convenience.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ladyship: female equivalent of lordship, used for certain female peers or judges
