backlog

noun
accumulated uncompleted work or orders
Pronunciation: / ˈbæk.lɑːɡ /; / ˈbæk.lɔg / (AmE), / ˈbæk.lɒɡ /; / ˈbæk.lɒg / (BrE)
a quantity of work, tasks, or orders that have built up and need to be completed

Examples

a backlog of work
She was hired to help manage the growing backlog of legal cases in the office.
The factory worked overtime to clear the production backlog caused by last month's strike.
We have a significant backlog of customer support requests to address this week.
After her vacation, she returned to a backlog of emails that needed a response.
The company is working through a backlog of customer orders that built up last month.
There is a significant backlog in the court system, causing long delays for cases.
