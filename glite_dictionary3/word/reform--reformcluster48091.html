to reform

verb
make someone abandon immoral conduct
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to cause a person or their behavior to abandon wrong or immoral ways and adopt better or more acceptable conduct

Examples

to reform a criminal
He struggled for years before finally being reformed by a supportive community.
It is never too late to reform your habits and choose a better path.
The organization aims to reform young offenders through education and counseling.

Related Terms

  • antonym – corrupt: to make someone or something morally bad; to ruin
  • synonym – improve: to make or become better; to enhance
