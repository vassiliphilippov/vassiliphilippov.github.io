late

adjective
of a later historical stage (of language/literature)
Pronunciation: / leɪt / (AmE), / leɪt / (BrE)
of or relating to a later stage in the historical development of a language or literature, especially one that is no longer in use; used chiefly in reference to dead languages or periods (e.g., Late Greek).

Examples

Late Greek period
Scholars often distinguish between Early and Late Egyptian in their research.
She is an expert in Late Latin vocabulary and its evolution.
The museum's exhibit focuses on ceramics from the Late Bronze Age.
