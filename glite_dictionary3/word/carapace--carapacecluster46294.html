carapace

noun
hard outer shell of some animals
Pronunciation: / ˈkær.ə.peɪs / (AmE), / ˈkær.ə.peɪs / (BrE)
the hard outer shell or covering of certain animals, such as crustaceans, arachnids, or turtles, that protects their body

Examples

carapace of a turtle
Scientists measured the length of the lobster's carapace to determine its age.
The crab's carapace is especially hard, making it difficult for predators to attack.
The turtle retracted its head inside its carapace for protection.
