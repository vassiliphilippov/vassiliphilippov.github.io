endergonic

adjective
requiring energy absorption (biochemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.dərˈɡɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌendəˈɡɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a reaction or process, especially in biochemistry) requiring the absorption of energy, with the products having greater free energy than the reactants.

Examples

endergonic chemical reaction
An endergonic change cannot proceed without a continuous input of energy.
Photosynthesis is an endergonic process because it requires energy from sunlight.
The synthesis of ATP from ADP and phosphate is an endergonic reaction in cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exergonic: (biochemistry) of a process that releases energy
