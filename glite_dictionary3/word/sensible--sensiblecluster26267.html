sensible

adjective
showing good judgment or reason
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəbəl / (BrE)
Having or showing good sense or judgment; practical and reasonable rather than foolish.

Examples

sensible decision
He offered some sensible advice that helped the team solve its problems.
It's always sensible to bring an umbrella when there is a chance of rain.
She made a sensible decision to save her money rather than spend it all at once.

Related Terms

  • antonym – foolish: lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym – rational: based on or in accordance with reason or logic
  • synonym – reasonable: having sound judgment; fair and sensible
