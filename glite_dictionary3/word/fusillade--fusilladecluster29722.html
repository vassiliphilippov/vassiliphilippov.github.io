to fusillade

verb
attack with a rapid series of shots
Pronunciation: / ˌfjuːsəˈleɪd / (AmE), / ˌfjuːsɪˈlɑːd / (BrE)
to attack or bombard with a rapid series of shots or missiles

Examples

to fusillade a position
As the troops advanced, they fusilladed the building with automatic gunfire.
Protesters were fusilladed with rubber bullets as they tried to cross the barricade.
The enemy position was fusilladed at dawn, forcing the soldiers to take cover.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bombard: to attack continuously with bombs, shells, or other missiles
