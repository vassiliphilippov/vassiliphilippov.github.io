header

noun
structural beam supporting ends of joists, etc.
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
a horizontal structural beam placed over a door, window, or opening to support the weight above

Examples

doorway header beam
A header ensures the weight from the upper floor is distributed to the adjoining wall studs.
The carpenter installed a header above the window frame to support the roof load.
When remodeling, it's important not to remove a header without proper structural reinforcement.
If the header is not strong enough, the wall could collapse under the load.
Replacing a damaged header requires careful removal of the bricks above the door.
The carpenter installed a sturdy header above the new window opening.
