progesterone

noun
female reproductive hormone
Pronunciation: / proʊˈdʒɛstəˌroʊn / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛstərəʊn / (BrE)
A steroid hormone produced mainly in the ovaries that prepares and maintains the uterus for pregnancy.

Examples

progesterone levels rise
During pregnancy, progesterone helps maintain the lining of the uterus.
Some hormone therapies include both estrogen and progesterone.
The doctor explained that low progesterone levels could affect fertility.
