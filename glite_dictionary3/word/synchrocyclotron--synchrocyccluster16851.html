synchrocyclotron

noun
type of particle accelerator
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪŋkroʊsaɪˈklɑːtrɑːn / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋkrəʊsaɪˈklɒtrɒn / (BrE)
a type of cyclotron that accelerates charged particles to relativistic speeds by varying the frequency of the accelerating electric field to account for relativistic effects

Examples

synchrocyclotron accelerates particles
A synchrocyclotron uses a variable frequency to accelerate protons to very high energies.
Early research in nuclear physics relied on the power of the synchrocyclotron to explore atomic structure.
The laboratory installed a state-of-the-art synchrocyclotron for medical isotope production.
