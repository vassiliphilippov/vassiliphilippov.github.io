rehearsal

noun
repetition in memory retention (psychology)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈhɜrsəl / (AmE), / rɪˈhɜːsəl / (BrE)
(psychology) The process of repeating information, either silently or aloud, to keep it in short-term memory.

Examples

mental rehearsal technique
Mental rehearsal can help you retain information for short quizzes and tests.
Students often rely on rehearsal when trying to remember a new phone number.
The psychologist explained that rehearsal is essential for transferring details into long-term memory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgetting: the loss of information from memory
  • synonym – memorization: the process of committing something to memory
