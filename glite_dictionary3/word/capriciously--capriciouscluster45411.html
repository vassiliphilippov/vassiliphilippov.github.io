capriciously

adverb
in an unpredictable, impulsive way
Pronunciation: / kəˈprɪʃəsli / (AmE), / kəˈprɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is unpredictable, impulsive, or governed by sudden changes or whims

Examples

to act capriciously
Public opinion can shift capriciously in response to unexpected events.
The CEO acted capriciously, making major decisions without warning or explanation.
The artist was known for changing her style capriciously from one project to the next.
Resources were allocated capriciously, leaving some teams with much more support than others.
She decided capriciously to redecorate her entire house.
The weather changed capriciously from sunshine to storm in a matter of minutes.

  • synonym – impulsively: in a way that is driven by sudden urges or desires
