jejune

adjective
naive or immature; superficial
Pronunciation: / dʒɪˈdʒun / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːn / (BrE)
naive, simplistic, or displaying a lack of maturity; superficial or uninteresting, especially in ideas or remarks

Examples

jejune comments
His jejune comments revealed a fundamental misunderstanding of the issue.
Many critics dismissed the novel's themes as jejune and lacking depth.
She found their jejune humor more irritating than amusing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – juvenile: childish or immature; related to young people
  • antonym – mature: showing the mental and emotional qualities of an adult
  • synonym – puerile: childishly silly and immature
