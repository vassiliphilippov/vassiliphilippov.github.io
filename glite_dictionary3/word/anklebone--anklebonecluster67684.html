anklebone

noun
bone forming the ankle joint
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋkəlˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈæŋkəlˌbəʊn / (BrE)
the bone of the ankle that connects the foot with the leg, forming part of the ankle joint

Examples

broken anklebone
Archaeologists discovered an ancient human skeleton with a fractured anklebone.
He twisted his anklebone during the soccer match and had to leave the field.
The doctor explained that the anklebone connects the leg to the foot, allowing for movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – talus: the large bone in the ankle that articulates with the tibia and fibula
