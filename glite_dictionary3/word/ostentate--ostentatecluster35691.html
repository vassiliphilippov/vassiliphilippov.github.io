to ostentate

verb
display showily or pretentiously
Pronunciation: / ˌɑː.stənˈteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɒs.tɛnˈteɪt / (BrE)
to display something in a showy or pretentious way, often to attract attention or admiration

Examples

to ostentate expensive clothes
He loved to ostentate his designer clothes at every opportunity.
The wealthy businessman would often ostentate his cars and jewelry in public.
There is no need to ostentate your achievements; let your work speak for itself.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hide: keep something out of sight; conceal
  • synonym – show off: display something to attract attention or admiration
