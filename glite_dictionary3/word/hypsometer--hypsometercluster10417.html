hypsometer

noun
instrument measuring elevation by boiling point of water
Pronunciation: / hɪpˈsɑmətər / (AmE), / hɪpˈsɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument that determines land elevation by measuring the boiling point of water at a given location

Examples

hypsometer measures elevation
A hypsometer operates on the principle that water boils at a lower temperature at higher altitudes.
Older explorers often relied on a hypsometer when surveying remote regions.
The scientist used a hypsometer to determine the elevation of the mountain camp.

  • synonym – altimeter: an instrument used to measure altitude
