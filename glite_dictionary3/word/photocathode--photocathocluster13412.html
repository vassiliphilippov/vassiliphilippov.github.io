photocathode

noun
cathode emitting electrons under light
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˈkæθoʊd / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈkæθəʊd / (BrE)
a cathode that emits electrons when exposed to light, used in devices like photomultiplier tubes and television cameras.

Examples

photocathode in a tube
Modern night vision goggles rely on a photocathode to convert light into an electrical signal.
The photocathode is a crucial part of the photomultiplier tube's ability to detect faint light signals.
When light strikes the photocathode, it releases electrons that are then amplified for measurement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anode: the positively charged electrode by which electrons leave a device
