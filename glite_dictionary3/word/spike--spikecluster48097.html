spike

noun
sharp points or shoes with points for grip in sports
Pronunciation: / spaɪk / (AmE), / spaɪk / (BrE)
a sharp metal or plastic point on the sole of an athletic shoe, or the shoe fitted with such points

Examples

running spikes
He had to replace a broken spike on his running shoes before the race.
Many field athletes wear spikes for better traction on grass or track.
The rules prohibit the use of metal spikes on artificial turf.
His golf shoes had metal spikes for extra grip on the course.
Soccer shoes often have rubber spikes to prevent slipping on wet grass.
The athlete replaced the worn spikes on her running shoes before the race.
