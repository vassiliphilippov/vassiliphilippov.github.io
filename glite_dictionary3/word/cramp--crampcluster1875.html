to cramp

verb
to restrict or hinder
Pronunciation: / kræmp / (AmE), / kræmp / (BrE)
to restrict or prevent the development, action, or movement of something or someone

Examples

to cramp growth
New regulations may cramp the growth of small businesses.
Strict rules can cramp a student's creativity and confidence.
The small room cramped our ability to move around during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – facilitate: to make an action or process easier
  • synonym – hamper: to hold back, impede, or interfere with
  • synonym – hinder: to make it difficult for someone or something to progress
