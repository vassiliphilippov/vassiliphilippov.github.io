elimination

noun
process of rejecting alternatives to reach a conclusion
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of systematically considering alternative possibilities and rejecting those that are unacceptable to reach a conclusion

Examples

elimination of options
In competitive quizzes, success often depends on logical elimination rather than memorization.
The detectives used the process of elimination to narrow down the list of suspects.
Through careful elimination of every other option, she arrived at the correct answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deduction: the process of reaching a conclusion based on available facts and logical reasoning
  • synonym – exclusion: the act of keeping something out or the state of being excluded
