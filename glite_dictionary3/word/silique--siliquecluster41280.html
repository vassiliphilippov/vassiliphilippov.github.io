silique

noun
narrow seed capsule in cruciferous plants
Pronunciation: / sɪˈlik / (AmE), / sɪˈliːk / (BrE)
a long, narrow seed capsule characteristic of plants in the mustard family (Cruciferae), which splits open when mature

Examples

silique of mustard plant
Many members of the mustard family produce a distinctive silique after flowering.
The silique splits lengthwise to release the seeds when the plant is mature.
The botany student carefully examined the silique under a magnifying glass.
