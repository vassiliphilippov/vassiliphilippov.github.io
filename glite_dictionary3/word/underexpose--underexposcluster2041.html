to underexpose

verb
expose insufficiently
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌʌndərəksˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to expose something to less light or influence than necessary; to expose insufficiently

Examples

to underexpose a child
Children who are underexposed to social interaction may struggle with communication skills.
If you underexpose the film, the pictures will not develop properly.
The photographer accidentally underexposed the image, making it look too dark.

Related Terms

  • antonym – overexpose: to expose something to too much light or influence
