to cumulate

verb
to gather or build up over time
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to increase or gather together in quantity over time; to accumulate

Examples

to cumulate results
If errors cumulate, the results of the experiment may be flawed.
Over the years, dust would cumulate in hard-to-reach corners of the attic.
Rainwater tends to cumulate in low-lying areas after a heavy storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accumulate: to gather or collect, especially over time
  • antonym – decrease: to become or make less
