to murk

verb
to make dark or gloomy
Pronunciation: / mɜːrk / (AmE), / mɜːk / (BrE)
to make dark, dim, or gloomy; to cloud or obscure with darkness or murkiness

Examples

to murk the sky
As the evening wore on, heavy clouds murked the sky, blocking out the stars.
Low fog had begun to murk the city streets, making the lamps glow dimly against the gloom.
The smoke from the campfire slowly murked the forest, creating an eerie atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – darken: to make or become dark or darker
