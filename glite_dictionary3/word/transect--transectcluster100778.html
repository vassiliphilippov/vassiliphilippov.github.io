to transect

verb
cut across or divide transversely
Pronunciation: / trænˈsɛkt / (AmE), / trænˈsɛkt / (BrE)
to cut across something or divide it by passing through it, especially along a straight line

Examples

to transect a road
A canal transects the city, dividing it into two distinct areas.
Scientists transected the rainforest with marked paths for ecological studies.
The new railway line will transect the farmland from north to south.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cross: to go or extend across something
  • synonym – intersect: to divide by passing through or across; to cross at a point
