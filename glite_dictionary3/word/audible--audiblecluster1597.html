audible

noun
American football play change spoken at the line
Pronunciation: / ˈɑdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪbəl / (BrE)
In American football, a play that is changed verbally by the quarterback after both teams are set at the line of scrimmage.

Examples

call an audible play
Manning is famous for his ability to recognize coverage and use an audible to exploit weaknesses.
Sometimes, the coach allows the quarterback to call an audible at the line of scrimmage.
The quarterback called an audible when he saw the defense shift.
