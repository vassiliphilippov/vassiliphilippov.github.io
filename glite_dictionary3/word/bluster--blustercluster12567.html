to bluster

verb
act in a loud or arrogant way
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌstər /; / ˈblʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
to behave or speak in a loud, aggressive, or boastful way, often to impress or intimidate others

Examples

to bluster about success
He often blusters about his achievements, but few take him seriously.
She tried to bluster her way through the meeting, but no one was convinced.
The manager tended to bluster when his authority was challenged, making everyone uncomfortable.
He tends to bluster when he feels threatened, but rarely takes real action.
Instead of calmly discussing the issues, he began to bluster about his achievements.
The manager tried to bluster his way through the criticism, but nobody was convinced.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bully: to act in a threatening way towards others, especially weaker people
  • antonym – cower: to crouch down in fear, often the opposite of arrogant behavior
  • synonym – swagger: to walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive way
