to captivate

verb
to attract and charm completely
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.təˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
to attract and hold the interest and attention of someone completely; to charm or enchant someone

Examples

to captivate an audience
Her smile and wit never failed to captivate those around her.
I was so captivated by the story that I couldn't put the book down.
The magician's performance captivated the audience from start to finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bore: to make someone feel uninterested or weary; the opposite of captivate
  • synonym – enchant: to delight greatly or put under a spell; to captivate
  • synonym – fascinate: to attract the strong attention and interest of someone
