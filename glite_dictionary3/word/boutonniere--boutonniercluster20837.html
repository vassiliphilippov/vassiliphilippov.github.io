boutonniere

noun
flower worn in a buttonhole
Pronunciation: / buːˌtɑːnˈjɛr / (AmE), / buːˌtɒnˈjɛə / (BrE)
A flower or small bunch of flowers worn in a buttonhole, typically on the lapel of a jacket.

Examples

boutonniere on a jacket
Each usher was given a small boutonniere to pin to his lapel.
He wore a red rose boutonniere on his wedding day.
The groom’s tuxedo was complemented by a white boutonniere.
