to turtle

verb
adopt a defensive strategy
Pronunciation: / c8t5dd0.t59l / (AmE), / c8t5cd0.tl29 / (BrE)
(informal) to adopt a very defensive or withdrawn strategy, especially in games or contests, protecting oneself or a position and waiting for the opponent to make a mistake.

Examples

to turtle in defense
In the final minutes they decided to turtle near the base and wait for the other team to overextend.
After losing a key player, the side began turtling and focused on protecting their remaining goals.
When the attackers broke through the line, the defenders turtled inside the fortifications until reinforcements arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym – play defensively: to adopt a cautious strategy that prioritizes defence over attack
  • antonym – rush: to attack quickly and aggressively, often the opposite of playing defensively
