to thread

verb
move in a winding or meandering course
Pronunciation: / θrɛd / (AmE), / θrɛd / (BrE)
to move in a winding, sinuous, or meandering course, especially through a place or among obstacles

Examples

to thread a path
A small stream threaded through the dense underbrush, barely visible from the hiking trail.
He threaded carefully through the crowd, making his way to the exit.
The mountain road threaded its way between ancient pine trees and rocky cliffs.
