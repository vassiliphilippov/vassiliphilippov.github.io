recess

noun
small inlet of water
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌsɛs, rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / ˈriːsɛs, rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
a small, sheltered part of a sea, lake, or other body of water, often set back between headlands or cliffs

Examples

sea recess
At low tide, the sandy recess was exposed, forming a secluded beach.
Seals are often found resting in the recesses of hidden coastal waters.
The boat drifted quietly into a narrow recess between the rocky cliffs.
