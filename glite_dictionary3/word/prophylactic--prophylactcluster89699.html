prophylactic

noun
contraceptive sheath (condom)
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.fɪˈlæk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.fɪˈlæk.tɪk / (BrE)
A contraceptive device, especially a thin sheath of rubber or latex worn over the penis during intercourse to prevent pregnancy or the transmission of diseases; a condom.

Examples

prophylactic condom
Doctors recommend carrying a prophylactic when engaging in sexual activity with new partners.
He discreetly took a prophylactic from his wallet before the encounter.
The use of a prophylactic can greatly reduce the risk of sexually transmitted infections.

Related Terms

  • synonym – condom: A sheath worn during intercourse as a contraceptive or as protection against infection.
