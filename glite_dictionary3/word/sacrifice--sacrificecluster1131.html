sacrifice

noun
personnel/assets given up for a goal
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs /; / ˈsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsak.rɪ.faɪs /; / ˈsækrɪfaɪs / (BrE)
the act of giving up or losing something important or valuable for someone else, for a goal, or as a penalty

Examples

personal sacrifice made
In the risky maneuver, the rear guard was left as a sacrifice to slow the enemy.
She refused to regard her fallen team as mere sacrifice for political gain.
The commander considered those soldiers a necessary sacrifice to secure the main objective.
Selling his house for such a low price was a major sacrifice.
She regarded moving to a smaller apartment as a necessary sacrifice after losing her job.
The company made a sacrifice by accepting a smaller profit to keep its employees.
As a sacrifice for his error, the athlete was benched during the next game.
She accepted the sacrifice of her bonus after failing to meet the sales target.
Sometimes, a minor sacrifice is necessary to correct a mistake in business.

  • synonym – loss: something or someone that has been lost
  • synonym – penalty: a punishment or disadvantage suffered for breaking a rule or making a mistake
