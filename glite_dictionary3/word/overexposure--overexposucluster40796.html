overexposure

noun
excessive light exposure (photography)
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
Exposure of photographic film or a sensor to too much light, resulting in a loss of image detail.

Examples

overexposure in a photo
He checked the camera settings to avoid overexposure on the next shot.
Improper lighting can easily result in overexposure when using sensitive film.
The overexposure of the photograph made the details in the sky completely disappear.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – exposure: the process of exposing film to light
  • antonym – underexposure: exposure of film to too little light
