intumescence

noun
swelling caused by accumulation of fluids
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuˈmɛs.əns / (BrE)
A swelling or enlargement of a part, especially due to accumulation of blood or other fluids.

Examples

intumescence after insect bite
After the allergic reaction, a visible intumescence developed at the site of the insect bite.
Excessive fluid retention can lead to the intumescence of internal organs.
The doctor noted an intumescence on the patient's ankle after the injury.

  • synonym – swelling: an abnormal enlargement, especially from injury or inflammation
