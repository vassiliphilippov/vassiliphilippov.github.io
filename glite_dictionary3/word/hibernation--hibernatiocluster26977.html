hibernation

noun
animal state of winter inactivity
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪbərˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪbəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which an animal (or plant) becomes inactive and slows its bodily functions to survive the winter

Examples

winter hibernation
Bears enter hibernation to survive the harsh winter months without food.
Many reptiles use hibernation as a strategy against cold temperatures and scarce resources.
The sudden end of hibernation marks the start of spring activity for these mammals.
Bears enter hibernation during the coldest months to survive when food is scarce.
Some reptiles rely on hibernation to avoid freezing temperatures in winter.
The hedgehog wakes occasionally from hibernation to eat stored food.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dormancy: a period in which an organism's growth or physical activity stops
  • antonym – estivation: a state of inactivity similar to hibernation, but during hot or dry periods instead of winter
  • degree lower – torpor: a state of mental or physical inactivity, sometimes used for less extreme forms than hibernation
