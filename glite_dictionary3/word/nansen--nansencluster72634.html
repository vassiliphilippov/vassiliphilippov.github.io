Nansen

proper noun
Norwegian Arctic explorer and humanitarian
Pronunciation: / ˈnæn.sən / (AmE), / ˈnæn.sən / (BrE)
Fridtjof Nansen (1861–1930), Norwegian explorer noted for Arctic expeditions and for his humanitarian work as director of refugee relief programs after World War I.

Examples

Nansen Arctic explorer
Nansen led the first crossing of Greenland on skis in 1888, a feat that brought him international acclaim.
Many of Nansen's scientific observations contributed to our understanding of polar regions.
The League of Nations awarded Nansen the Nobel Peace Prize in 1922 for his work with refugees.
