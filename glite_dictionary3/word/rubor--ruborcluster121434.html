rubor

noun
redness of skin/tissue (inflammation sign)
Pronunciation: / ˈruː.bɔr / (AmE), / ˈruː.bɔː / (BrE)
Redness of the skin or tissues, especially as one of the four classic signs of inflammation in response to injury or irritation.

Examples

rubor of the skin
Rubor around the wound indicated the body's response to infection.
Classic symptoms of inflammation include rubor, calor, tumor, and dolor.
The physician observed rubor at the site of the patient's injury.
