to combat

verb
to fight or struggle against
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbæt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.bæt / (BrE)
to fight or try to stop something harmful or dangerous; to battle against someone or something

Examples

to combat a problem
Doctors around the world are working together to combat the spread of infectious diseases.
She made it her life's mission to combat injustice and discrimination.
The government launched a campaign to combat drug abuse among teenagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – battle: to fight against; contend in battle
  • synonym – confront: to face up to and deal with a problem
  • antonym – yield: to give way to pressure or force; to submit
