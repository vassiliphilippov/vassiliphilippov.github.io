monkfish

noun
flesh of a North American anglerfish (food)
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of a large-headed anglerfish, especially as used for food, found in the Atlantic waters of North America.

Examples

grilled monkfish fillet
Fresh monkfish is often compared to lobster in both flavor and consistency.
Many chefs praise the taste and texture of monkfish in seafood dishes.
The restaurant served grilled monkfish with lemon and herbs.
