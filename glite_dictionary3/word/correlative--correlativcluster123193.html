correlative

adjective
expressing reciprocal or complementary relationship
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛlətɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
expressing or involving a reciprocal or mutual relationship, especially when things correspond or are used in pairs (such as paired conjunctions in grammar)

Examples

correlative relationship
Her interest in mathematics was correlative with her aptitude for logical reasoning.
In English grammar, 'both...and' are correlative conjunctions.
The two statements have a correlative relationship and support each other's validity.
Economic growth and environmental protection are often seen as correlative goals in sustainable development.
Mental and physical health can have correlative effects on each other.
The witness's testimony was correlative with the evidence presented by the prosecution.

Related Terms

  • antonym – independent: not influenced by others; not mutually related
  • synonym – reciprocal: mutually corresponding or complementary
