foetoscopy

noun
prenatal diagnostic procedure
Pronunciation: / fiˈɑːtəˌskəpi / (AmE), / fiːˈɒtəˌskəpi / (BrE)
A medical procedure for prenatal diagnosis in which a fetus is directly observed in the uterus, often allowing for the withdrawal of fetal blood.

Examples

foetoscopy procedure
Foetoscopy is often used in high-risk pregnancies to monitor fetal development and collect blood samples.
Recent advances in foetoscopy have made it safer for both mother and child.
The doctor recommended foetoscopy to get a clearer diagnosis of the fetal condition.
