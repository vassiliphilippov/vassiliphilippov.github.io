slip

noun
plant part used for propagation
Pronunciation: / slɪp / (AmE), / slɪp / (BrE)
a shoot, root, leaf, or bud removed from a plant and used to produce a new plant by rooting or grafting

Examples

a plant slip
Gardeners often use a slip to propagate their favorite varieties of geraniums.
He planted a slip from his grandmother's rose bush and watched it grow into a new plant.
The slip was carefully cut and placed in water to encourage root growth.
