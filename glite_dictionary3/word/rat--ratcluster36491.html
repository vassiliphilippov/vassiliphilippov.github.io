rat

noun
despicable or contemptible person
Pronunciation: / ræt / (AmE), / ræt / (BrE)
(informal) a person who betrays, informs on others, or is considered untrustworthy, including a strikebreaker

Examples

a rat betraying friends
Everyone turned on the rat who betrayed the team.
He acted like a rat and stole from his friends.
I can't believe you lied to me, you rat!
He was called a rat for taking over another worker's shift during the strike.
Some workers are accused of being a rat if they don't join the protest.
The union members were angry when a rat crossed the picket line.
After years in the gang, he became a rat and told the police everything.
No one in the group wanted to be labeled as a rat after the incident.
They discovered the cause of the arrests was a rat in their organization.
