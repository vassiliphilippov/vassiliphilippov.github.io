mat

noun
foundation of reinforced concrete
Pronunciation: / mæt / (AmE), / mat / (BrE)
a foundation made up of a thick, extended layer of reinforced concrete, usually laid on soft ground to support a building or structure

Examples

a mat foundation
A reinforced concrete mat distributes the load of heavy structures over a wide area.
During construction, the workers poured concrete to form the mat that would support the new factory.
The engineers decided to use a mat for the building because of the unstable soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – raft foundation: a type of foundation using a large concrete slab covering a wide area
