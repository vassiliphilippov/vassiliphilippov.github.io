malignant

adjective
dangerously spreading disease or tumor
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt / (BrE)
(of a disease or tumor) very dangerous and likely to grow and spread in a way that threatens life; having a tendency to become progressively worse.

Examples

malignant tumor
A malignant form of cancer can spread quickly to other organs.
Tests revealed that the growth was malignant, not benign, which concerned the patient.
The doctor explained that the malignant tumor would require immediate surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym – benign: (of a disease or tumor) not harmful in effect; not malignant
