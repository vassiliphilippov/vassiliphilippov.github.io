Samson

proper noun
biblical hero renowned for strength
Pronunciation: / ˈsæm.sən / (AmE), / ˈsæm.sən / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a judge of Israel celebrated for his great strength, who was betrayed by Delilah and captured by the Philistines.

Examples

Samson strength
He showed a Samson-like strength, lifting the heavy gate single-handedly.
Her betrayal reminded him of the tale of Samson losing his power.
The story of Samson and Delilah is often quoted to illustrate the dangers of misplaced trust.
