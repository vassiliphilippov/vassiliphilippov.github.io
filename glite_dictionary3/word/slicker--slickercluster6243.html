slicker

noun
stylish, suave person
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɪkə / (BrE)
a person who is smooth, suave, and stylish, especially in behavior and dress

Examples

city slicker
Everyone knew him as the office slicker, always impeccably dressed and polite.
She mistrusted the new manager, thinking he was just another slicker with a charming smile.
The party was full of slickers who seemed to care more about style than substance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – boor: a rude or unmannerly person
  • synonym – dandy: a man who places particular importance upon physical appearance and style, often in a showy way
  • synonym – fop: a man overly concerned with his appearance and clothes
