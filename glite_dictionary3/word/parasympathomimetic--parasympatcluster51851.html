parasympathomimetic

adjective
mimicking parasympathetic nervous system actions
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əˌsɪm.pə.θoʊ.mɪˈmɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˌsɪm.pə.θəʊ.mɪˈmɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or other substance) having an effect similar to stimulation of the parasympathetic nervous system; promoting the actions regulated by this part of the nervous system.

Examples

parasympathomimetic drug effect
Certain parasympathomimetic agents are used to treat glaucoma by reducing intraocular pressure.
Researchers are studying new parasympathomimetic compounds for their potential in treating digestive disorders.
The physician prescribed a parasympathomimetic drug to help slow the patient's heart rate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anticholinergic: blocking the action of acetylcholine in the nervous system
  • synonym – cholinergic: producing, resembling, or having the same effect as acetylcholine
