to tutor

verb
to act as a legal guardian
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
to act as a legal guardian of a child or minor, especially supervising their welfare and interests

Examples

to tutor a minor
After her parents died, a relative was chosen to tutor the young heiress and oversee her estate.
The court may tutor a minor to ensure his or her rights are protected.
Under the law, his uncle was appointed to tutor him until he reached adulthood.
