to fend

verb
withstand or defend against something
Pronunciation: / fɛnd / (AmE), / fɛnd / (BrE)
to withstand or defend oneself against something, especially an attack or difficulty

Examples

to fend off danger
The hikers had to fend for themselves during the severe storm.
The old fortress managed to fend off multiple attacks from invaders.
You need to fend against criticism if you want to succeed as a politician.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resist: to withstand the action or effect of something
  • antonym – succumb: to fail to resist pressure or an attack
