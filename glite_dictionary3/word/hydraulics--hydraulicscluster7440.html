hydraulics

noun
study and application of fluid mechanics
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɔlɪks / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɔːlɪks / (BrE)
the branch of science and engineering concerned with the mechanical properties and practical applications of fluids, especially liquids, in motion

Examples

hydraulics in engineering
He is taking a class on hydraulics as part of his engineering degree.
Modern irrigation systems rely heavily on the principles of hydraulics.
The bridge's movement is controlled by a complex hydraulics system.
