AFP

noun
abbreviation for alpha-fetoprotein (fetal blood protein)
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˌɛf ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌef ˈpiː / (BrE)
abbreviation for alpha-fetoprotein, a protein produced by the fetus, elevated levels of which may indicate fetal abnormalities such as neural tube defects.

Examples

AFP blood test
Doctors may request an AFP test to check for possible neural tube defects during pregnancy.
High AFP measurements in maternal blood can be an early indicator of congenital abnormalities.
The laboratory reported elevated AFP levels, suggesting the need for further prenatal testing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alpha-fetoprotein: a protein produced by the fetus; high levels may signal birth defects
