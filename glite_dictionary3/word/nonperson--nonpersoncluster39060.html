nonperson

noun
someone regarded as nonexistent, often politically
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as nonexistent and denied rights, often ignored or erased from recognition, especially for political or ideological reasons

Examples

a nonperson in society
After the scandal, he became a nonperson in the corporate world.
In authoritarian regimes, dissidents are often made into nonpersons and erased from records.
The novel explores the psychological impact of being declared a nonperson by society.
