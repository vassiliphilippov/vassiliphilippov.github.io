expelling

noun
bodily process of forcing out substances
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of forcing a substance out of the body

Examples

expelling pus from wounds
Severe infections may result in the expelling of pus from a wound.
Sneezing is one way that the body is expelling irritants from the nasal passages.
Some digestive conditions involve the expelling of waste more frequently than normal.
