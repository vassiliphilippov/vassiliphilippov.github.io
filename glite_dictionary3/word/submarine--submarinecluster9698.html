submarine

noun
underwater warship
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.məˌrin / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.məˈriːn / (BrE)
A warship designed to operate underwater, usually armed with torpedoes.

Examples

military submarine
During the war, a submarine sank several enemy ships in the Atlantic.
Modern submarines can stay submerged for weeks without resurfacing.
The submarine surfaced quietly in the harbor under cover of darkness.
