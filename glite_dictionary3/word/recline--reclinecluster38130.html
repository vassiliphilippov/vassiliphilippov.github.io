to recline

verb
cause to lean or lie back
Pronunciation: / rɪˈklaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to cause something, such as a part of the body, to lean or lie back in a resting position

Examples

to recline the seat
He reclined the seat so his daughter could sleep comfortably during the journey.
She gently reclined her head on the soft pillow.
The dentist reclined the patient’s chair before starting the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lean back: to move or rest in a backward position
