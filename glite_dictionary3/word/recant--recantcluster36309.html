to recant

verb
formally withdraw a belief or statement
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkænt / (AmE), / rɪˈkant / (BrE)
to formally withdraw or renounce a previously held belief or statement, especially publicly or under pressure

Examples

to recant a statement
After realizing his error, he decided to recant his earlier statement.
The scientist refused to recant his controversial findings despite public criticism.
Under pressure from authorities, she was forced to recant her testimony in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym – affirm: to state or assert positively; to confirm
  • synonym – retract: to withdraw a statement or accusation
