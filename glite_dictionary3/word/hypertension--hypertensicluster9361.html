hypertension

noun
abnormally high blood pressure
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pərˈtɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈtɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which blood pressure is consistently abnormally high, typically defined as 140/90 mm Hg or greater.

Examples

hypertension diagnosis
Lifestyle changes such as diet and exercise can help manage hypertension.
Regular check-ups are important for those at risk of hypertension.
Uncontrolled hypertension can lead to stroke or heart disease.
