dissolver

noun
liquid that dissolves other substances
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɑlvər / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlvə / (BrE)
a liquid substance that has the ability to dissolve another substance, often forming a solution

Examples

dissolver for paint
A suitable dissolver is required to prepare the chemical solution for the experiment.
Alcohol can act as a dissolver for certain oils and compounds.
Water is considered a universal dissolver because it dissolves so many substances.
