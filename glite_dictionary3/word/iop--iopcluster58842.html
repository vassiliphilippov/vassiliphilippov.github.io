IOP

noun
pressure inside the eyeball (intraocular pressure)
Pronunciation: / aɪ oʊ pi / (AmE), / aɪ əʊ piː / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by the fluids inside the eyeball, regulated by the resistance to the outward flow of aqueous humor

Examples

IOP measurement
Daily monitoring of IOP is important for patients at risk of eye diseases.
Elevated IOP may damage the optic nerve if left untreated.
The doctor measured her IOP during the eye exam to check for signs of glaucoma.
