to catenulate

verb
arrange in a linked chain, esp. in biology
Pronunciation: / kəˈtɛnjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈtiːnjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of biological structures) to arrange in a linked series or chain, especially of spores or cells.

Examples

to catenulate fungal spores
Some fungal spores catenulate during reproduction, creating characteristic linked patterns.
The cells catenulated as they divided, making their arrangement easy to observe under the microscope.
Under certain conditions, bacteria catenulate to form long chains.
