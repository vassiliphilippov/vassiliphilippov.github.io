long-windedness

noun
tedious verbosity; wordiness
Pronunciation: / lɔŋˈwɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / lɒŋˈwɪndɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of using too many words in speaking or writing, making something tedious or boring.

Examples

his long-windedness in speeches
His long-windedness during the meeting made it difficult for others to stay engaged.
If you avoid long-windedness, your writing will be clearer and more effective.
Many students complained about the professor's long-windedness in lectures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conciseness: the quality of being brief and to the point
  • synonym – verbosity: the quality of using more words than needed; wordiness
  • synonym – wordiness: the state of using too many words; verbosity
