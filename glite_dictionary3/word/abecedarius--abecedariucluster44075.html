abecedarius

noun
poem with lines beginning A, B, C, etc.
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪbiːsiːˈdɛriəs / (AmE), / ˌeɪbɪsiːˈdɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A poem in which each line or stanza begins with successive letters of the alphabet in order.

Examples

an abecedarius poem
Each stanza in the abecedarius started with the next letter of the alphabet, from A to Z.
The medieval monk composed an abecedarius to aid in the memorization of important virtues.
Writing an abecedarius can be a fun challenge for poets interested in form and constraint.
