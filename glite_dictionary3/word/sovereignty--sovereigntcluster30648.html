sovereignty

noun
a state's authority to govern another
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑvrənti / (AmE), / ˈsɒvərənti / (BrE)
The authority of a state to govern another state or territory; supreme control exercised by one political entity over another.

Examples

foreign sovereignty
International law often debates issues of sovereignty when one state interferes in another's affairs.
The colonial power exercised sovereignty over its overseas territories for centuries.
The treaty transferred sovereignty of the region from one country to another.

Related Terms

  • antonym – autonomy: the right or condition of self-government
