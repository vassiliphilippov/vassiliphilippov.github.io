to rejuvenate

verb
make landforms youthful again; renew land features
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒuːvənˌeɪt /; / rɪˈdʒuːvəˌneɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒuːvɪneɪt /; / rɪˈdʒuːvəneɪt / (BrE)
(of land or rivers) to develop more youthful features or renewed activity, often due to natural changes like uplift or erosion

Examples

to rejuvenate a river
Geologists study the ways in which rivers rejuvenate their courses after changes in elevation.
Glacial melting helped to rejuvenate the terrain, making old hills appear young and sharp again.
Over millions of years, tectonic uplift can rejuvenate a region's landscape, creating new valleys and steeper slopes.
Geologists observed that the area was rejuvenated during the last period of uplift.
Glacial retreat can also rejuvenate streams, triggering renewed erosion.
Tectonic uplift can rejuvenate a river, causing it to carve a deeper valley.

Related Terms

  • antonym – degrade: to reduce the erosive power or elevation of a river
  • synonym – revitalize: to give new life or vigor to something
