to slough

verb
shed or cast off skin, hair, or feathers
Pronunciation: / slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
(of an animal) to shed or cast off a layer of skin, hair, horn, or feathers, especially as part of a natural process of renewal or growth.

Examples

to slough off skin
As the wound began to heal, dead tissue started to slough off gradually.
During molting, birds slough their feathers to make way for fresh growth.
Every spring, the snake will slough its old skin in one continuous piece.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shed: to lose or cast off hair, skin, leaves, etc., naturally
