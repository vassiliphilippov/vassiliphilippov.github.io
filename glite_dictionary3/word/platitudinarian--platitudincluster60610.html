platitudinarian

noun
person who overuses platitudes
Pronunciation: / ˌplætɪˌtuːdɪˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˌtjuːdɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who habitually uses or utters platitudes, especially to a tedious or boring extent

Examples

a platitudinarian at meetings
Her colleagues began to avoid her after she developed into a relentless platitudinarian.
The committee was dominated by a platitudinarian who repeated the same clichés at every meeting.
Tired of his reputation as a platitudinarian, Michael tried to make his speeches more original.
