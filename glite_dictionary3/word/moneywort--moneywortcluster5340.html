moneywort

noun
creeping plant with round leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌniˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a creeping perennial plant of the loosestrife family, with round, coin-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, commonly found in damp places in Europe and Asia

Examples

moneywort plant leaves
After a few weeks, the yellow flowers of the moneywort brightened the wet meadow.
Many gardeners use moneywort as a ground cover because of its attractive foliage.
The garden pond was edged with clusters of moneywort creeping along the stones.
