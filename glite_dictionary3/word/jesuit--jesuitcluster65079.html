Jesuit

adjective
relating to Jesuits or their qualities
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛzjuɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjʊɪt / (BrE)
Having qualities characteristic of Jesuits or their doctrines, often associated with subtle reasoning or casuistry.

Examples

Jesuit reasoning
His argument was criticized for its Jesuit subtlety and complexity.
The Jesuit approach to teaching encourages moral reflection and discipline.
The school provided a Jesuit education that emphasized rigorous intellectual training.
