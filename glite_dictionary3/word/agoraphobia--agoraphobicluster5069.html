agoraphobia

noun
fear of open or public spaces
Pronunciation: / ˌæɡərəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌæɡərəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
An extreme or irrational fear of open or public spaces, often resulting in avoidance of such situations.

Examples

severe agoraphobia symptoms
After years of therapy, he finally overcame his agoraphobia and traveled alone.
Her severe agoraphobia made it difficult for her to leave her apartment.
People suffering from agoraphobia often avoid crowded places like shopping malls or train stations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – claustrophobia: fear of confined spaces
