evoked

adjective
called forth by stimulation (science/medicine)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvoʊkt / (AmE), / ɪˈvəʊkt / (BrE)
(of a response or potential) called forth from a latent or potential state by stimulation, especially in a scientific or medical context.

Examples

evoked potential test
Researchers studied evoked potentials to understand brain activity patterns.
The doctor measured the patient's evoked response after applying the stimulus.
Visual evoked responses are often used to test the function of the optic nerve.
