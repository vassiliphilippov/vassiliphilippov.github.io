aberrant

noun
person deviating from group norms
Pronunciation: / əˈbɛr.ənt / (AmE), / əˈbɛr.ənt / (BrE)
a person whose behavior significantly departs from what is considered normal or acceptable within a group

Examples

an aberrant individual
Fear of being seen as an aberrant kept many from expressing their true opinions.
Some labeled him an aberrant for refusing to follow the community’s strict religious customs.
The study focused on an aberrant who consistently challenged the school’s rules and traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conformist: a person who conforms to accepted behavior or established practices
  • synonym – deviant: a person who differs markedly from a group norm
