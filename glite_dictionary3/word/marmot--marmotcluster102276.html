marmot

noun
large burrowing rodent of northern regions
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːr.mət / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.mət / (BrE)
a large, burrowing rodent with coarse fur and a short bushy tail, found in mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere, and known for hibernating in winter.

Examples

wild marmot
Marmots live in colonies and communicate with each other using loud whistles.
The marmot hibernates through the cold winter months to survive until spring.
We spotted a marmot sunning itself on the rocks during our hike in the Alps.
