treacherously

adverb
in a disloyal or betraying way
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛtʃ.ɚ.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃ.ər.əs.li / (BrE)
In a manner that betrays trust or is disloyal and unfaithful.

Examples

to act treacherously
He acted treacherously when he revealed the secret to their rivals.
She treacherously befriended him only to betray his trust.
The treaty was broken treacherously by the invading army.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disloyally: in a way that is not loyal; betraying trust
  • antonym – faithfully: in a loyal and devoted manner
