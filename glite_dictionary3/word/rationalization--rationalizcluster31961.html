rationalization

noun
mathematical simplification by removing radicals
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) The process of simplifying an expression or equation by removing radicals, without altering its value or the roots of the equation.

Examples

rationalization of a fraction
After the rationalization of the expression, the result became much easier to interpret.
Students are often asked to perform rationalization when dealing with fractions that contain square roots.
The rationalization of the denominator simplifies calculations in many algebraic problems.
