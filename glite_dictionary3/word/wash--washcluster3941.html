wash

noun
airflow behind a moving aircraft part
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ / (AmE), / wɒʃ / (BrE)
the flow of air that is driven backward by an aircraft's propeller or wing

Examples

aircraft wash effect
Engineers studied the effect of wing wash on nearby structures during takeoff.
The pilot warned the ground crew to keep clear of the propeller wash.
The strong wash from the helicopter disturbed debris on the runway.
