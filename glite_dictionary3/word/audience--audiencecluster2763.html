audience

noun
formal meeting to present one's case
Pronunciation: / ˈɔdiəns /; / ˈɑdiəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəns / (BrE)
a formal meeting with an important person or group, especially to present your case or be heard

Examples

audience with the king
Despite many attempts, he was denied an audience with the board of directors.
She requested an audience before the committee to state her case.
The defendant was granted an audience with the magistrate to explain his situation.
During the medieval period, commoners rarely obtained an audience with the king.
She requested a private audience with the President to present her proposal.
The ambassador was granted an audience with the Pope to discuss international relations.
