armor

noun
protective outer covering of animals or plants
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a tough, usually rigid protective covering found on some animals or plants, such as shells, scales, or thick skin, that protects against injury or predators

Examples

animal armor protects
Certain beetles have a thick armor to shield them from attack.
The armor of the turtle protects it from predators in the wild.
The cactus developed a natural armor of spines and tough skin to survive in harsh climates.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exoskeleton: external skeleton that supports and protects an animal's body
  • synonym – shell: hard outer covering of certain animals, such as snails or turtles
