housemother

noun
woman supervising residents in dormitory
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman employed to supervise and care for young people living in a dormitory, boarding house, or similar residence

Examples

housemother in a dormitory
As the housemother, Mrs. Green offered guidance and support to the university freshmen.
The housemother checked that all the girls were in their rooms before lights out.
The boarding school appointed a new housemother for the junior dormitory this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – housefather: a man employed as a chaperon in a residence for young people
