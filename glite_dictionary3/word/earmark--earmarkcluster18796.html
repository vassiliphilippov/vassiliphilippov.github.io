to earmark

verb
assign for a specific use
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to designate or set aside a resource for a particular purpose or recipient

Examples

to earmark funds
Each year, the charity earmarks a portion of donations to support education programs.
She will earmark some of her savings for a trip next year.
The government decided to earmark additional funds for disaster relief efforts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allocate: to distribute resources for a specific purpose
  • synonym – reserve: to save something for a particular purpose or person
