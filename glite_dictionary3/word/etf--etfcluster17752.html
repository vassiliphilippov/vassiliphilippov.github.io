ETF

noun
exchange-traded investment fund
Pronunciation: / i ti ɛf / (AmE), / iː tiː ɛf / (BrE)
an investment fund traded on stock exchanges, similar to a mutual fund but with shares that can be bought and sold throughout the day

Examples

buy an ETF
Many investors choose ETFs for their low fees and liquidity.
The manager recommended diversifying my portfolio with an emerging markets ETF.
You can buy and sell ETFs on the stock market just like regular shares.
