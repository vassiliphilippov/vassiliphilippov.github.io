to carve

verb
form by cutting into shape
Pronunciation: / kɑrv / (AmE), / kɑːv / (BrE)
to shape, form, or engrave something by cutting into a material (such as wood, stone, or other surfaces)

Examples

to carve a statue
Each student tried to carve a pumpkin with a different face for the festival.
He spent hours trying to carve a statue that resembled an ancient warrior.
The artist will carve a delicate bird from a single block of wood.
He decided to carve their initials into the wet cement.
Some people like to carve their names on tree trunks to leave a mark.
The artist skillfully carved a lion's head from the block of marble.
