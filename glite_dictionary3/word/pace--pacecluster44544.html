pace

noun
unit of length, 3 feet, stride
Pronunciation: / peɪs / (AmE), / peɪs / (BrE)
a single step or stride, or the distance covered by one step (sometimes used as a unit of measurement)

Examples

a single pace
A single pace equals exactly 3 feet in traditional English units.
He calculated the distance by counting his paces from the gate to the river.
The old Roman road was measured in paces rather than in modern meters.
He measured the distance to the fence by taking careful paces.
The archaeologist marked out a trench using twenty paces as the standard length.
The old map instructs you to walk exactly ten paces north from the big oak tree.
Each pace brought her closer to the finish line.
He measured the length of the hallway in paces.
She paused after a few paces to catch her breath.

Related Terms

  • synonym – step: a single movement of raising one foot and putting it down in another spot
  • synonym – stride: a long, decisive step
