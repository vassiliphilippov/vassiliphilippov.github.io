redneck

noun
poor rural White person in the southern US
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.nɛk / (BrE)
(often offensive) A poor White person from the rural southern United States, especially one regarded as unsophisticated or having rural, working-class values.

Examples

a redneck from the South
He often jokes that he’s just a simple redneck from a small town in Georgia.
Some city folks assume every redneck drives a pickup truck and listens to country music.
The term redneck can be considered offensive when used to stereotype rural Americans.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hillbilly: a person from a remote rural area, especially in the southern US
