rhythm

noun
pattern of stresses in speech or poetry
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
the pattern formed by the arrangement of stressed and unstressed syllables in spoken words, especially in poetry or verse

Examples

poem rhythm
A clear rhythm can help listeners appreciate the musical quality of spoken words.
She struggled to identify the rhythm in the poem's first stanza.
The rhythm of Shakespeare's sonnets is carefully crafted to evoke emotion.
