to criminalise

verb
make (an act) illegal
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪm.ə.nəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make an activity or act illegal by making it a criminal offense under the law

Examples

to criminalise an activity
Activists argue that it is wrong to criminalise homelessness.
Some countries have recently criminalised smoking in public places.
The government decided to criminalise the possession of certain weapons.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decriminalise: to make legal or no longer a criminal offense
  • synonym – outlaw: to make illegal; ban under law
