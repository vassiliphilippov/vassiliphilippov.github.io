unexchangeability

noun
incapability of being exchanged
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.ɪkˌstʃeɪndʒ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being incapable of exchange or being interchanged with something else.

Examples

unexchangeability of currency
A currency's unexchangeability can severely limit its usefulness in international trade.
The unexchangeability of personal experiences means they cannot be truly shared with others.
They discussed the unexchangeability of certain rare artifacts, which cannot be replaced or swapped.
