lactase

noun
enzyme that breaks down lactose
Pronunciation: / ˈlækˌteɪs / (AmE), / ˈlæk.teɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of lactose into glucose and galactose

Examples

lactase enzyme activity
Lactase breaks down lactose into simple sugars that can be absorbed by the body.
People who are lactose intolerant may need to take lactase supplements with dairy foods.
The activity of lactase decreases as some people age, leading to digestive issues.
