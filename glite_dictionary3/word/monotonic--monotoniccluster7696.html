monotonic

adjective
consistently increasing or decreasing (math)
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.nəˈtɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈtɒ.nɪk / (BrE)
(of a sequence or function) Consistently increasing and never decreasing, or consistently decreasing and never increasing, in value.

Examples

monotonic sequence
Sorting algorithms may rely on input data being monotonic for optimization.
The company's revenue showed a monotonic increase throughout the year.
The function is monotonic over the interval, never decreasing at any point.

  • antonym – non-monotonic: not consistently increasing or decreasing
