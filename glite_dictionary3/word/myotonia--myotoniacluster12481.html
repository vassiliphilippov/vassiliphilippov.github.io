myotonia

noun
slow muscle relaxation disorder
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.əˈtoʊ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈtəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which muscles have abnormally prolonged contractions and relax slowly after use.

Examples

myotonia causes stiffness
Certain forms of myotonia can cause difficulty in climbing stairs or rising from a chair.
People with myotonia may have trouble relaxing their grip after shaking hands.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with myotonia due to his prolonged muscle stiffness.
