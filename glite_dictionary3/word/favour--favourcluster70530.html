to favour

verb
regard as most likely to win
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to regard or treat as the most likely to win or succeed; to consider as the favourite

Examples

to favour a winner
Critics favour her to take the main acting award this year.
The betting agency favoured the home team to win the championship.
The committee has always favoured innovative proposals over conventional ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disfavour: to regard with disapproval or as less likely to succeed
  • synonym – prefer: to like or choose one thing over another
