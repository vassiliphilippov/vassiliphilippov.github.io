to tessellate

verb
subdivide polygonal mesh for rendering
Pronunciation: / c8t5bs.59ccle6at / (AmE), / c8t5bs.6a.le6at / (BrE)
(in computer graphics) to subdivide a polygonal mesh or surface into smaller polygons (triangles or quads) to increase geometric detail or allow smoother rendering, often via dedicated algorithms or hardware tessellation.

Examples

to tessellate a mesh
The renderer will tessellate the character's mesh to add fine detail during animation.
By enabling hardware tessellation, the game engine tessellates terrain meshes for higher-resolution geometry.
Artists often use displacement maps while tessellating models to create realistic surface wrinkles and folds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – subdivide: to divide something (such as a surface) into smaller parts
  • synonym – tessellation: the process or result of covering a surface with shapes without gaps or overlaps; in graphics, also the subdivision of geometry
