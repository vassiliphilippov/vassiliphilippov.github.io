parlourmaid

noun
female domestic servant for parlour and table duties
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrlərˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːləmeɪd / (BrE)
a female domestic servant in a private home whose duties include caring for the parlour, tending the table, and answering the door

Examples

a parlourmaid serves tea
In Edwardian England, a parlourmaid was responsible for setting the table and serving afternoon tea.
The duties of a parlourmaid included dusting the sitting room and answering the bell.
The wealthy family employed a parlourmaid to greet guests at the front door.
