absorbance

noun
measure of absorbed light
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔɹ.bəns / (AmE), / əbˈzɔː.bəns / (BrE)
in physics and chemistry, a measure of the amount of light or electromagnetic radiation absorbed by a substance, typically expressed as a logarithmic ratio

Examples

absorbance of light
High absorbance readings indicate a large concentration of the solute.
The absorbance of the solution was measured using a spectrophotometer.
The graph shows how absorbance changes with different wavelengths of light.
