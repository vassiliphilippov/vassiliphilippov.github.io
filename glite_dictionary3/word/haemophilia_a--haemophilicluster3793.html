haemophilia A

noun
hereditary bleeding disorder (factor VIII deficiency)
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈfɪliə eɪ / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˈfɪliə eɪ / (BrE)
A hereditary disorder caused by a deficiency of clotting factor VIII, resulting in excessive bleeding; occurs almost exclusively in males.

Examples

haemophilia A diagnosis
Genetic testing confirmed that the child had haemophilia A, requiring lifelong treatment with clotting factor injections.
Most cases of haemophilia A are inherited and identified in early childhood after unusual bruising or bleeding.
The patient's diagnosis of haemophilia A explained his frequent episodes of severe bleeding after minor injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hemophilia: general hereditary bleeding disorder due to various clotting factor deficiencies
