to cook

verb
falsify or tamper with records
Pronunciation: / kʊk / (AmE), / kʊk / (BrE)
to falsify or tamper with something, especially records or figures, usually with the intention to deceive

Examples

to cook the books
He was fired after management discovered he had cooked the sales figures.
She refused to cook the data, insisting on reporting the results honestly.
The accountant tried to cook the books to hide the company's losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – doctor: to change something in order to deceive people
  • synonym – falsify: to change information or evidence so that it is not true
