to deactivate

verb
to remove from active service
Pronunciation: / diˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / diːˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to remove from active service, especially in the military or other official capacity

Examples

to deactivate a military unit
After the war, many units were deactivated and their members returned to civilian life.
He was officially deactivated from the military after serving for seven years.
The organization decided to deactivate its emergency response team once the crisis ended.

  • antonym – activate: to make active or put into operation
