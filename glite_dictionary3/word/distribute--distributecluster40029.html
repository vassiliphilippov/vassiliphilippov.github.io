to distribute

verb
to be mathematically distributive
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɪbjut / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
(in mathematics or statistics) to be spread or applied in a particular way, such as values or operations

Examples

distribute numbers equally
In Boolean algebra, 'and' distributes over 'or' in logical expressions.
In arithmetic, multiplication distributes over addition, making equations easier to solve.
When the function distributes over the sum, you can expand the expression accordingly.
The exam scores distribute normally, with most students scoring near the average.
The weights distribute unevenly along the beam, creating points of stress.
We analyzed how patient ages distribute within each trial group.
