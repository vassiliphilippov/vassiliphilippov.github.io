decentralisation

noun
transfer of authority from central to local bodies
Pronunciation: / diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring power or authority from a central organization or government to local branches or authorities

Examples

decentralisation of government power
Many countries have implemented decentralisation to give local governments more autonomy.
Opponents argue that decentralisation may lead to inconsistencies in policy across regions.
The decentralisation of power can improve efficiency and accountability in public administration.

Related Terms

  • antonym – centralisation: the concentration of control under a single authority
