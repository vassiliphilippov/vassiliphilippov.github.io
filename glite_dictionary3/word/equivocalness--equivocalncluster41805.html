equivocalness

noun
state of being ambiguous or unclear
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkwɪvəkəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvəkəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having more than one possible meaning; ambiguity or uncertainty in interpretation

Examples

equivocalness of answer
Her poem's equivocalness invites multiple readings and interpretations.
Legal documents must avoid equivocalness to ensure clear interpretation.
The equivocalness of his answer left the audience confused about his true intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ambiguity: the quality of being open to more than one interpretation
  • antonym – clarity: the quality of being clear and easy to understand
