to foliate

verb
to hammer metal into thin sheets
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.li.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɒl.i.eɪt /; / ˈfəʊ.li.eɪt / (BrE)
to hammer or shape metal into thin flat sheets, or to cover something with a thin layer of metal foil

Examples

to foliate metal sheets
During the process, workers foliate the metal to achieve the desired thinness.
Skilled artisans can foliate gold into nearly transparent leaves for decorative purposes.
The craftsman patiently foliated the silver, preparing it for use in ancient manuscripts.
High-quality picture frames are often foliated with gold or silver to increase their value.
The craftsman carefully foliated the mirror to achieve a brilliant, gilded effect.
They decided to foliate the glass panels to add a touch of elegance to the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hammer: to shape something with repeated blows, typically metal
  • synonym – laminate: to split or form into thin layers; to make into a thin sheet
