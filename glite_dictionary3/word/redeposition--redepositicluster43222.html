redeposition

noun
the act of depositing again in a new place
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.dɪˌpɑː.zɪˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˌpɒ.zɪˈʃən / (BrE)
the process of depositing material again in a new location after it has been moved from its original deposit, especially by natural forces such as water or wind

Examples

soil redeposition after erosion
Erosion and redeposition play a key role in shaping coastal landscapes.
Scientists studied the redeposition of volcanic ash after the eruption.
The heavy rains led to the redeposition of sediment along the riverbank.
