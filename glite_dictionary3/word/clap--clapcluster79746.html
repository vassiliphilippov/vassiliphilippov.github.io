to clap

verb
applaud by striking palms together
Pronunciation: / klæp / (AmE), / klæp / (BrE)
to strike your hands together to make a sharp noise, usually to show approval, enjoyment, or keep time to music

Examples

to clap for performers
People in the theater stood up and clapped for the actors during the curtain call.
She clapped her hands after the inspiring speech.
The audience began to clap enthusiastically as the band finished their song.
He likes to clap his hands to the beat during songs.
The excited audience began to clap loudly at the end of the concert.
When the teacher finished her story, the children started to clap.

Related Terms

  • synonym – applaud: to show approval by clapping hands or shouting
  • antonym – boo: to show disapproval by shouting 'boo'
