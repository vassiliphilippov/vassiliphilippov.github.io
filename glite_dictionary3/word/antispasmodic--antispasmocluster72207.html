antispasmodic

noun
drug to relieve muscle spasms
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.spæzˈmɑː.dɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.spæzˈmɒ.dɪk / (BrE)
a drug or substance used to relieve or prevent muscle spasms, especially in the smooth muscles of the body

Examples

take an antispasmodic medicine
After her diagnosis, the doctor prescribed an antispasmodic to ease her stomach cramps.
Some antispasmodics are available over the counter for the treatment of mild digestive discomfort.
The patient responded well to the antispasmodic and reported fewer muscle spasms after treatment.
