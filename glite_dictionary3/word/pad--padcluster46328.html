pad

noun
floating leaf of an aquatic plant
Pronunciation: / pæd / (AmE), / pad / (BrE)
a flat, cushion-like part of a plant or animal, such as a water lily leaf or the underside of a paw or fingertip

Examples

paw pad
Many fish hide beneath the pads during the hottest part of the day.
The frog sat perfectly still on a pad in the middle of the pond.
We saw a dragonfly resting on a lily pad while kayaking.
The cat silently walked across the room on its soft pads.
The dog injured the pad of its paw during the hike.
You can feel the pad of your finger when you press it against a surface.

  • synonym – leaf: the flat, typically green part of a plant that is attached to a stem
