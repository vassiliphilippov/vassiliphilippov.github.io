meniscus

noun
lens curved concave on one side, convex on the other
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
a lens that is concave on one side and convex on the other, used to focus or disperse light in optical instruments

Examples

meniscus lens in glasses
A simple telescope may contain only a single meniscus lens.
He replaced the broken lens with a new meniscus to restore clarity.
The camera uses a meniscus to correct for image distortion.
