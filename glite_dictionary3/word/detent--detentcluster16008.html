detent

noun
mechanical catch for positioning or locking
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnt / (BrE)
A device, often a hinged catch, that fits into a notch of a ratchet to move a wheel forward or prevent it from moving backward.

Examples

detent mechanism
A spring-loaded detent holds the lever securely in place until it's released.
The clock's escapement uses a detent to regulate the movement of the gears.
When you turn the knob, you'll feel a slight click as the detent engages.
