unicameral

adjective
having a single legislative chamber
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː.nɪˈkæm.ə.rəl / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈkæm.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a legislative body) having a single chamber or house, as opposed to two (bicameral).

Examples

unicameral parliament
Debate continues over whether a unicameral parliament is more efficient than a bicameral one.
Nebraska is unique among U.S. states in having a unicameral legislature.
The country adopted a unicameral system to simplify its legislative process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bicameral: having two legislative chambers
