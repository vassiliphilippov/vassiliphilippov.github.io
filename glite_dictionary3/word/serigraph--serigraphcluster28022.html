serigraph

noun
print made by screen printing
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛrɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsɛrɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A print produced by forcing ink onto paper or another surface through a screen with areas blocked off by a stencil, commonly known as screen printing or silkscreen printing.

Examples

serigraph artwork
Creating a serigraph requires skill in both design and technique.
