oviduct

noun
tube carrying egg from ovary
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.vɪˌdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɪˌdʌkt / (BrE)
either of a pair of tubes in female animals through which eggs pass from the ovary to the uterus or outside the body

Examples

female oviduct tube
Damage to the oviduct can prevent successful reproduction in birds.
Scientists studied the role of the oviduct in transporting eggs in various animals.
The fertilized egg travels through the oviduct before implanting in the uterus.
