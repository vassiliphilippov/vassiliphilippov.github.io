Crazy Horse

proper noun
Sioux chief who resisted U.S. forces (1849–1877)
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪzi hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi hɔːs / (BrE)
(1849–1877) A chief of the Oglala Lakota (Sioux) who led resistance against the U.S. government and played a key role in the defeat of General Custer at the Battle of the Little Bighorn.

Examples

Crazy Horse led resistance
Crazy Horse and Sitting Bull united their forces to defend their land at the Battle of the Little Bighorn.
Many historians consider Crazy Horse one of the most influential Native American leaders of the 19th century.
The bravery of Crazy Horse is commemorated by a massive mountain monument in South Dakota.

Related Terms

  • antonym – General Custer: U.S. general defeated at the Battle of the Little Bighorn
  • synonym – Sitting Bull: another Sioux leader involved in the Battle of the Little Bighorn
