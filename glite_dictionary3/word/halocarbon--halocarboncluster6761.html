halocarbon

noun
compound of carbon and halogen(s)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæl.oʊˌkɑr.bən / (AmE), / ˈhæl.əʊˌkɑː.bən / (BrE)
any of a class of chemical compounds consisting of carbon bonded to one or more halogen elements (such as fluorine, chlorine, bromine, or iodine)

Examples

industrial halocarbon compound
Chlorofluorocarbons are a type of halocarbon that have been widely used as refrigerants.
Scientists study the breakdown of halocarbon molecules in the atmosphere to understand ozone depletion.
The environmental impact of halocarbons has led to stricter regulations on their use.
