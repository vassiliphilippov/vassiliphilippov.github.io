to stitch

verb
fasten or sew with needle and thread
Pronunciation: / stɪtʃ / (AmE), / stɪtʃ / (BrE)
to fasten, join, or repair using a needle and thread; to sew sections of cloth or material together

Examples

to stitch a tear
He stitch patches onto his old jeans to give them new life.
She carefully stitched the torn seam on her dress before the party.
The doctor stitched the wound after the surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sew: to join with needle and thread
  • antonym – unstitch: to undo stitches
