jeer

noun
a mocking or scornful remark
Pronunciation: / dʒɪr / (AmE), / dʒɪə / (BrE)
a mocking or derisive remark or shout; an expression of scorn or contempt

Examples

a loud jeer from crowd
His speech was interrupted by a jeer from the back of the auditorium.
She ignored the jeer and continued her performance with confidence.
The crowd responded with a loud jeer when the referee made an unpopular call.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cheer: a shout of encouragement or praise
  • synonym – taunt: a remark made to provoke or mock someone
