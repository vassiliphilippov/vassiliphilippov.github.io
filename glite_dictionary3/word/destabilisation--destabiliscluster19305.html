destabilisation

noun
the act of making something less stable
Pronunciation: / diːˌsteɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌsteɪbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of making something less stable, especially a government, country, or economy.

Examples

destabilisation of a government
Continued unrest brought about the destabilisation of the government and widespread uncertainty.
Economic sanctions are sometimes used as tools of destabilisation against rival countries.
The sudden political crisis led to the destabilisation of the entire region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – destabilization: Alternative spelling, chiefly American English
  • antonym – stabilisation: The process of making something more stable
