lagomorph

noun
a mammal such as a rabbit or hare
Pronunciation: / ˈlæɡəˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈlæɡəmɔːf / (BrE)
a mammal of the order Lagomorpha, which includes hares, rabbits, and pikas, distinguished from rodents by having two pairs of upper incisors.

Examples

lagomorph family animals
Rabbits and hares are both examples of lagomorphs found in many habitats around the world.
The pika, a small mountain-dwelling lagomorph, is known for its high-pitched calls.
Unlike rodents, lagomorphs possess two pairs of upper incisors specialized for gnawing.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rodent: a mammal of the order Rodentia, similar in appearance but with one pair of upper incisors
