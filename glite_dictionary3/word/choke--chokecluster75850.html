to choke

verb
be too tight and press (so as to cause discomfort)
Pronunciation: / tʃoʊk / (AmE), / tʃəʊk / (BrE)
to be unable to breathe or to make someone unable to breathe, usually because the airway is blocked, squeezed, or something is too tight

Examples

to choke on food
His tie seemed to choke him as he adjusted it nervously.
Make sure the baby’s bib is not too tight or it will choke her.
The collar was so tight it began to choke the dog.
He started to choke after swallowing a piece of food too quickly.
Smoke filled the room, causing everyone to choke and gasp for air.
The child began to choke on a small toy until her mother intervened.
In a fit of rage, he tried to choke the snake to death.
She watched in horror as the wrestler attempted to choke his opponent during the match.
The villain threatened to choke the captive unless his demands were met.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gag: to experience a reflex or sensation of throat blockage when something is caught or touched
  • antonym – release: to set free from restraint, the opposite of choking
  • synonym – strangle: to squeeze or constrict the neck, causing difficulty in breathing
  • synonym – suffocate: to die or struggle for breath due to lack of air
  • synonym – throttle: to kill or injure by compressing the throat
