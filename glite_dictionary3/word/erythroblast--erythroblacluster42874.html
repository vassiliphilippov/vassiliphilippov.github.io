erythroblast

noun
cell in bone marrow developing into red blood cell
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɪθroʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪθrəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a nucleated cell found in bone marrow that develops into a red blood cell

Examples

erythroblast in bone marrow
An erythroblast contains a nucleus, unlike the mature red blood cell it will become.
In some blood disorders, the number of erythroblasts in circulation increases abnormally.
Under the microscope, the scientist identified an erythroblast among the bone marrow cells.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – erythrocyte: mature red blood cell
  • synonym – normoblast: a type of erythroblast with a normal nucleus
  • degree higher – reticulocyte: an immature red blood cell
