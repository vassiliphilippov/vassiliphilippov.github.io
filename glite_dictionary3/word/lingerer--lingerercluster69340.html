lingerer

noun
person who stays somewhere unnecessarily long
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋɡərər / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡərə / (BrE)
a person who stays in a place longer than necessary, often without purpose

Examples

a lingerer at the door
A lingerer wandered through the museum, reluctant to leave even as the lights dimmed.
She was a habitual lingerer, always the last to leave any gathering.
The security guard politely asked the last lingerers to make their way to the exit.
