foreperson

noun
presiding member of a jury
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔɹˌpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
the presiding member of a jury who speaks for the jury and represents it in court

Examples

foreperson of the jury
After discussing the case, the foreperson communicated the jury's final decision to the judge.
The foreperson is responsible for ensuring that all jurors have a chance to express their views.
The foreperson read the jury's verdict aloud in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foreman: the traditional term for the presiding member of a jury, often replaced by 'foreperson' for gender neutrality
