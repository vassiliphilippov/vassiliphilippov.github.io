to convoy

verb
to escort for protection in transit
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
to accompany and protect vehicles, ships, or people as they travel, especially during times of danger

Examples

to convoy supply ships
Allied ships often convoyed vital resources across the Atlantic during World War II.
During the evacuation, naval vessels were assigned to convoy the passenger liners.
The army decided to convoy the supply trucks through the dangerous region.

  • synonym – escort: to accompany and protect someone or something, especially in transit
