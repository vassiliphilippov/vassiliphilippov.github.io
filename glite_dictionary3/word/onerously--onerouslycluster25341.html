onerously

adverb
in a burdensome or difficult way
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.nɚ.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈɒn.ər.əs.li / (BrE)
In a manner that involves a great deal of effort, difficulty, or burden.

Examples

onerously complete tasks
She found herself onerously burdened with tasks no one else would take.
The contract terms were onerously restrictive, discouraging potential partners.
The new regulations are imposed onerously on small businesses, making compliance difficult for many.
