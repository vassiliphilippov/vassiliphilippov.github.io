restiveness

noun
state of being difficult to control
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being difficult to control or unwilling to be guided; stubbornness or resistance.

Examples

restiveness of a crowd
After hours of waiting, the children's restiveness became difficult for the teachers to handle.
Political restiveness spread throughout the country as protests increased.
The manager was frustrated by the restiveness of the team during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – obedience: the act or quality of obeying or being willing to comply
  • synonym – restive: difficult to control; stubborn or restless
