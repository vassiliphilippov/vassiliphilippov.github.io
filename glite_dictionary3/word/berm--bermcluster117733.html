berm

noun
narrow strip of land at roadside
Pronunciation: / bɝm / (AmE), / bɜːm / (BrE)
a narrow strip of land or unpaved shoulder along the edge of a road, often used for stopping vehicles or providing drainage

Examples

roadside berm
Cyclists often rest on the grassy berm at the side of the country road.
The driver pulled over onto the berm to check his tires.
Wildflowers were growing all along the berm beside the highway.
