deathtrap

noun
extremely unsafe structure
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛθˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ.træp / (BrE)
A building, vehicle, or other structure that is extremely unsafe and likely to cause death or serious injury.

Examples

old deathtrap building
Many people refused to enter the old warehouse, calling it a deathtrap due to its crumbling walls.
Safety inspectors warned that the abandoned mine was a potential deathtrap for explorers.
The authorities closed the nightclub after declaring it a deathtrap because of fire hazards.
