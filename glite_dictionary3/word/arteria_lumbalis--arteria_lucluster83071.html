arteria lumbalis

noun
artery supplying lumbar vertebrae and back muscles
Pronunciation: / ɑrˌtɪriə lʌmˈbeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriə lʌmˈbɑːlɪs / (BrE)
one of several paired arteries arising from the abdominal aorta and supplying blood to the lumbar vertebrae, back muscles, and abdominal wall

Examples

arteria lumbalis supplies vertebrae
A blockage in the arteria lumbalis may cause pain or weakness in the lumbar region.
Damage to the arteria lumbalis during surgery can lead to bleeding complications.
The arteria lumbalis supplies essential blood flow to the lower back muscles.
