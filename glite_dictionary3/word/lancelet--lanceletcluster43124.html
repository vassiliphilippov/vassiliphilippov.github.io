lancelet

noun
small primitive marine animal
Pronunciation: / ˈlænslət / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnslɪt / (BrE)
a small, translucent marine animal with a slender, lance-shaped body, regarded as a primitive relative of vertebrates

Examples

a lancelet under microscope
Because the lancelet is transparent, its internal structures are easily visible under the microscope.
Scientists found a population of lancelets burrowed in the sandy seabed of the bay.
The lancelet is often studied in biology classes due to its significance in vertebrate evolution.
