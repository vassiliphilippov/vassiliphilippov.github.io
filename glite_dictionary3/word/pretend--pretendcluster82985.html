to pretend

verb
put forward a claim or guess
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to claim or assert something, such as an opinion, right, or title, often without certainty or formally

Examples

to pretend knowledge

She would not pretend to understand quantum physics, but she listened carefully.
He attempted to pretend a right to the old family estate.
Several nobles sought to pretend the crown after the king's death.
She does not pretend any connection to the royal bloodline.
