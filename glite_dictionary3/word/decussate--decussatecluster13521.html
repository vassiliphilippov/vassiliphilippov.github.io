to decussate

verb
to cross or intersect as a cross
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkʌs.eɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌs.eɪt / (BrE)
(especially of nerves, fibers, or structures) to cross or intersect so as to form a cross or X shape.

Examples

to decussate nerve fibers
During neuroanatomy class, we learned how nerve pathways decussate at specific points in the body.
In some plant species, the leaves decussate along the stem for optimal sunlight exposure.
The optic nerve fibers decussate in the brain, forming a characteristic X-shaped structure.
