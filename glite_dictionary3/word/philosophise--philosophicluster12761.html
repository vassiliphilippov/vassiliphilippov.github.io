to philosophise

verb
reason or discuss philosophically
Pronunciation: / fəˈlɑːsəˌfaɪz / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒsəfaɪz / (BrE)
to discuss, consider, or reason about matters in a philosophical way; to reflect or speculate in a theoretical manner

Examples

to philosophise about life
Rather than act immediately, he sat back and began to philosophise about the problem.
She tends to philosophise whenever faced with a difficult decision, considering every possible angle.
They would often philosophise late into the night about the meaning of life.
