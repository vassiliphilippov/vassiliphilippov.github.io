hotspot

noun
place of intense heat or radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑtˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌspɒt / (BrE)
a place where heat or radiation is especially intense, often compared to its surroundings

Examples

volcanic hotspot
Scientists measured unusually high temperatures near the hotspot on the ocean floor.
The Chernobyl reactor's core became a hotspot for radioactive contamination.
Volcanoes often form above a hotspot deep within the Earth's mantle.
