hand

noun
side or direction of something
Pronunciation: / hænd / (AmE), / hænd / (BrE)
one of two or more sides or perspectives in a discussion or argument

Examples

on every hand
Help was offered on every hand when disaster struck the town.
Objections were raised on every hand during the meeting.
Rumors spread on all hands after the announcement.
Hand can mean a particular perspective, as in the phrase 'on the one hand, on the other hand.'
On the one hand, the proposal has many benefits; on the other hand, it could be risky.
She weighed each hand of the argument before making her decision.

  • synonym – side: a position to the left or right of something
