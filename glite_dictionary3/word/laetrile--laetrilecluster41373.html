laetrile

noun
substance once claimed to treat cancer
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪəˌtraɪl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ə.trɪl / (BrE)
a substance derived from amygdalin, formerly publicized as an anticancer drug despite a lack of scientific evidence for its effectiveness

Examples

laetrile cancer treatment
After reading about the risks, she decided against using laetrile as a therapy.
Some alternative clinics continue to offer laetrile as part of their treatments, despite warnings from medical authorities.
There is no credible scientific evidence supporting the effectiveness of laetrile in treating cancer.
