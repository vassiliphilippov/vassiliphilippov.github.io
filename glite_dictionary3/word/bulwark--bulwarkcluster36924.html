bulwark

noun
protective ship railing or wall
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊl.wɚk / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.wək / (BrE)
A strong wall or barrier built on the sides of a ship's deck to protect those on board from the sea or falling overboard.

Examples

ship's bulwark barrier
Crew members leaned against the bulwark as they looked out at the horizon.
Salt from the sea collected on the wooden bulwark over time.
The ship's bulwark offered protection from the crashing waves during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parapet: a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony
  • synonym – rampart: a broad embankment or defensive wall
