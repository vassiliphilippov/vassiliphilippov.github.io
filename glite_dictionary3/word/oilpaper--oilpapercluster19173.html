oilpaper

noun
translucent, oil-treated paper
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪlˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
Paper that has been made translucent and waterproof by soaking in oil.

Examples

oilpaper umbrella
The lantern's shade was made of delicate oilpaper that glowed softly in the darkness.
To protect the manuscript from moisture, it was wrapped in a sheet of oilpaper.
Traditional Chinese umbrellas are often crafted using bamboo and oilpaper.
