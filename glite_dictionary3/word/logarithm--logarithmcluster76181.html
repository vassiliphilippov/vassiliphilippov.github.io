logarithm

noun
mathematical exponent needed for a base to reach a number
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːɡəˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
a mathematical quantity representing the exponent to which a fixed base must be raised to produce a given number

Examples
