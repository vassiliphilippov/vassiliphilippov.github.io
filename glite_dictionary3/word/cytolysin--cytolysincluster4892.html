cytolysin

noun
substance that destroys animal cells
Pronunciation: / saɪˈtoʊlɪsɪn / (AmE), / saɪˈtɒlɪsɪn / (BrE)
a substance, often a protein, that causes the destruction or lysis of animal cells by disrupting their membranes
