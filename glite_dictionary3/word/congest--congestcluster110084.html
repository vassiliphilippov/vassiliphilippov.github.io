to congest

verb
to become or cause to become blocked
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
to cause (a passage or vessel) to become blocked or filled so that movement is difficult or impossible

Examples

to congest a road
Heavy rainfall may congest the gutter system, causing flooding.
If too much hair goes down the drain, it can congest the pipes.
Traffic tends to congest the city center during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear: to remove an obstruction from something so it is no longer blocked
  • synonym – clog: to block or become blocked so as to prevent movement
