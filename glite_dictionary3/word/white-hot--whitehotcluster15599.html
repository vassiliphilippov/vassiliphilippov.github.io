white-hot

adjective
glowing white with intense heat
Pronunciation: / waɪt hɑt / (AmE), / waɪt hɒt / (BrE)
Heated to such a high temperature that it glows with a white light.

Examples

white-hot metal rod
After hours in the forge, the metal rod was white-hot and dangerous to touch.
Molten steel glowed white-hot as it poured from the furnace.
The argument in the room reached a white-hot intensity.
