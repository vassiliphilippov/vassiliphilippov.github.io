to graft

verb
join parts of plants to grow together
Pronunciation: / ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to join a shoot, twig, or bud from one plant to another so they grow together as a single plant

Examples

to graft a rose
Gardeners often graft roses to create hardier varieties.
It takes skill to successfully graft a branch from one tree onto another.
The horticulturist decided to graft a new species onto the old apple tree.
By learning to graft, she could combine the strengths of two rose varieties.
Gardeners often graft fruit tree branches to improve their yield.
They hope to graft a new apple variety onto the old orchard trees.
