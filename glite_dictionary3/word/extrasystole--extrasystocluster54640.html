extrasystole

noun
premature heartbeat; cardiac arrhythmia
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.strəˈsɪs.toʊl / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈsɪs.təʊl / (BrE)
a premature heartbeat, occurring earlier than the next expected regular heart contraction, resulting in a brief cardiac arrhythmia

Examples

an extrasystole heartbeat
After experiencing palpitations, he was diagnosed with occasional extrasystoles.
Electrocardiograms are used to detect irregularities, including extrasystole, during routine checkups.
The cardiologist explained that an extrasystole is generally harmless but can be bothersome to patients.
