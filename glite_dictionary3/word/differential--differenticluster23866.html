differential

noun
distinguishing quality or factor
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a quality or factor that distinguishes one thing from another in a group of similar things

Examples

price differential
Price is often the key differential in customers’ final decisions.
Subtle differentials in taste set these two wines apart.
The main differential between the candidates is their approach to foreign policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – difference: the way in which things or people are not the same
  • antonym – similarity: the state of being similar; likeness
