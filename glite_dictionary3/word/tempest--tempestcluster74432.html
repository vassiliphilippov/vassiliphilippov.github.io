tempest

noun
violent windstorm (literary)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛm.pəst / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.pɪst / (BrE)
A violent and windy storm, especially used in literary contexts.

Examples

raging tempest
During the tempest, trees were uprooted and power lines were knocked down.
Shakespeare's play 'The Tempest' begins with a ship caught in a violent storm.
The tempest left the coastline battered and the ships severely damaged.
