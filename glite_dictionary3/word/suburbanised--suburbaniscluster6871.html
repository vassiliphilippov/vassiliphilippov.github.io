suburbanised

adjective
developed with or surrounded by suburbs
Pronunciation: / səˈbɝː.bəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / səˈbɜː.bə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of an area or city) having been developed with or surrounded by suburbs; characterized by a prevalence of suburban features.

Examples

suburbanised city area
As the population grew, the small town was gradually suburbanised and lost its rural charm.
Many critics argue that a suburbanised landscape lacks architectural diversity.
The city has become increasingly suburbanised over the past few decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suburban: relating to or characteristic of a suburb
  • antonym – urban: relating to a city or town rather than a suburb or rural area
