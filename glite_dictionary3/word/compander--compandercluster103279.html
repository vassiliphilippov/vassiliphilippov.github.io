compander

noun
device for signal compression and expansion
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpændər / (AmE), / kəmˈpandə / (BrE)
a device or system that improves a signal's quality by compressing its dynamic range before transmission and then expanding it back to its original form on reception

Examples

compander in audio system
By using a compander, engineers can achieve clearer sound during long-distance radio transmissions.
Most modern wireless microphones include a built-in compander for optimal audio fidelity.
The recording studio installed a new compander to reduce background noise in audio tracks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compressor: device that reduces the dynamic range of an audio signal
  • synonym – expander: device that increases the dynamic range of an audio signal
