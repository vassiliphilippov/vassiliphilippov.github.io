logbook

noun
official record book for journeys/events
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔɡˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌbʊk / (BrE)
an official book in which records of events, journeys, or activities are regularly written, especially on a ship or aircraft

Examples

ship's logbook
Engineers must sign the logbook whenever they inspect the equipment.
Our sailing club keeps a logbook to record every voyage we make.
The captain carefully entered each day's details into the logbook.
