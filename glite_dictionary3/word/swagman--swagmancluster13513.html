swagman

noun
itinerant Australian laborer (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈswæɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈswæɡmən / (BrE)
(in Australia, historical) a man who traveled from place to place in search of work, carrying his personal belongings in a bundle.

Examples

an old swagman walking
During the Great Depression, many men became swagmen as jobs dried up in the cities.
He wandered the outback as a swagman, carrying all he owned on his back.
The legendary swagman is immortalized in the Australian song 'Waltzing Matilda'.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hobo: a homeless person, typically one who travels for work
  • synonym – tramp: a person who travels from place to place, typically in search of work
