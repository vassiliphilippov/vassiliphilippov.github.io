trance

noun
semi-conscious spellbound state
Pronunciation: / træns / (AmE), / trɑːns / (BrE)
a state in which a person is not fully conscious and may not be aware of what is happening around them, often as if hypnotized or under a spell

Examples

in a deep trance
During the ceremony, the priest fell into a deep trance and spoke in strange tongues.
Lost in thought, she moved about the room as if in a trance.
The magician claimed he could put audience members into a trance with just a few words.
He seemed to be in a trance, staring blankly at the wall.
The music put her into a trance that lasted for hours.
The shaman entered a deep trance during the ritual.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – coma: a state of deep unconsciousness
  • synonym – ecstasy: an intense state of emotion or trance-like absorption
  • synonym – hypnosis: an artificially induced state of consciousness, similar to a trance
