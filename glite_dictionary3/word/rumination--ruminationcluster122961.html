rumination

noun
chewing of cud by ruminants
Pronunciation: / ˌruːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌruːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which ruminant animals chew previously swallowed food (cud) to aid digestion

Examples

cow rumination process
Cows spend several hours each day in rumination to properly digest their food.
Decreased rumination in cattle can be an early indicator of digestive problems.
The veterinarian observed the sheep’s rumination for any signs of illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chewing: the act of using the teeth to break up food
