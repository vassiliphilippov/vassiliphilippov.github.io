neoevolutionism

noun
anthropological theory of cultural evolution
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˌɛvəˈluʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˌiːvəˈluːʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
a school of anthropology concerned with long-term cultural change and with similar patterns of development observed in different, unrelated societies

Examples

neoevolutionism in anthropology
Debates about neoevolutionism often focus on the universality of cultural development.
Many anthropologists of the 20th century were influenced by neoevolutionism in their analyses of cultures.
The idea of multilinear evolution is a central concept in neoevolutionism.

  • synonym – evolutionism: the theory that societies develop through stages over time
