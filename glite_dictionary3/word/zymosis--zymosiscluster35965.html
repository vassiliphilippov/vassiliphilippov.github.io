zymosis

noun
biological breakdown process, especially fermentation
Pronunciation: / zaɪˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / zɪˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
a process in which an agent causes an organic substance to break down into simpler substances, especially the anaerobic breakdown of sugar into alcohol (fermentation)

Examples

zymosis of sugar
Pasteur's research clarified the role of yeast in zymosis.

Without oxygen, the fruits undergo zymosis and produce alcohol.
