allotropical

adjective
relating to allotropism in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈtrɑpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌaləˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting allotropism—the existence of a chemical element in two or more different forms in the same physical state.

Examples

allotropical forms of carbon
Carbon is an allotropical element with several crystalline forms, including diamond and graphite.
Phosphorus is commonly known for its allotropical modifications, such as white and red phosphorus.
Scientists study the allotropical behaviors of elements like sulfur to understand their structural differences.
