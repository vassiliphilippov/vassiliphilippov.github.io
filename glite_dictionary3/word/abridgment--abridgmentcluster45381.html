abridgment

noun
shortened version of a written work
Pronunciation: / əˈbrɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əˈbrɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
a shortened version of a written work, retaining the essential elements and meaning of the original

Examples

abridgment of a novel
The editor published an abridgment of the novel to make it accessible to younger readers.
This abridgment covers the main arguments but omits the detailed analysis.
We listened to an abridgment of the original lectures on the radio.

Related Terms

  • synonym – summary: a brief statement of the main points of something
  • antonym – unabridged: not shortened; complete, especially a book or text
