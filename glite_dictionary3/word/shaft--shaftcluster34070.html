shaft

noun
a pointed or critical remark
Pronunciation: / ʃæft / (AmE), / ʃɑːft / (BrE)
an indirect, sharply critical remark aimed at someone; a cutting or pointed verbal attack

Examples

a shaft at his rival
He couldn't resist launching one more shaft at his rival during the debate.
Her shaft of sarcasm left everyone in stunned silence.
The article was full of subtle shafts directed at the opposing party.
