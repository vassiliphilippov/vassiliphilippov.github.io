allograph

noun
variant form of a letter or symbol
Pronunciation: / ˈæləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈaləɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a variant form of a letter or other grapheme, such as a different style or case of the same written character

Examples

allograph of a letter
Both print and cursive letterforms are considered allographs of the same grapheme.
Handwritten versions of a letter can show many different allographs in personal styles.
The capital 'A' and the lowercase 'a' are allographs representing the same letter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grapheme: the smallest unit of a writing system
