to indict

verb
formally accuse of a crime
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaɪt / (BrE)
to formally accuse someone of a serious crime, especially by the decision of a jury or legal authority

Examples

to indict a suspect
After a lengthy investigation, the grand jury decided to indict the company’s executives.
He was indicted on counts of fraud and embezzlement.
Prosecutors hope to indict her by the end of the month.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acquit: to formally declare someone not guilty of a charge in a court of law
  • synonym – charge: to formally accuse someone of an offence in law
