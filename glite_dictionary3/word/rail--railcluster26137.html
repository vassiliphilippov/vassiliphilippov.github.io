rail

noun
steel bar for a railway track
Pronunciation: / reɪl / (AmE), / reɪl / (BrE)
a bar, typically made of rolled steel, forming part of the track that guides and supports trains or other vehicles on a railway

Examples

train rail
A loose rail can be a serious safety hazard on busy train lines.
Steel rails are laid on wooden or concrete sleepers to form a railway track.
The damaged rail had to be replaced before trains could resume service.

Related Terms

  • synonym – railroad tie: a beam supporting and fixing the rails on a railway track
  • synonym – track: the pair of rails forming a railway line
