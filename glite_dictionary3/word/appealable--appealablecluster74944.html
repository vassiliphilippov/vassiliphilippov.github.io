appealable

adjective
able to be appealed in court
Pronunciation: / əˈpiːləbəl / (AmE), / əˈpiːləbəl / (BrE)
(of a decision or case) able to be formally challenged or reviewed by a higher court or authority.

Examples

appealable court decision
Certain administrative rulings are not appealable under the current law.
Only judgments that violate procedure are appealable in this jurisdiction.
The verdict is appealable, so the defendant can take the case to a higher court.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unappealable: not able to be appealed; final
