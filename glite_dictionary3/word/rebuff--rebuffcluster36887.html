to rebuff

verb
reject abruptly or ungraciously
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
to reject someone or something in an abrupt or ungracious manner

Examples

to rebuff a request
He rebuffs any criticism, insisting his decision was correct.
She tried to help, but he rebuffed her offers politely.
The new proposal was rebuffed by the board without discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accept: to consent to receive something offered
  • synonym – reject: to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
