khat

noun
stimulant leaf chewed or brewed as tea
Pronunciation: / kɑt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
the leaves of a shrub (Catha edulis), chewed or brewed as a tea for their stimulating, euphoric effect, especially in parts of East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula

Examples

khat leaves chewed
After brewing a strong khat tea, he felt energized and more talkative.
Many people in Ethiopia and Yemen regularly chew khat as part of their social gatherings.
The import and sale of khat are banned in several countries due to its stimulant effects.
