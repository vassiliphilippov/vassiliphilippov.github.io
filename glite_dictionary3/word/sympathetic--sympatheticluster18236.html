sympathetic

adjective
caused by nearby vibrations (in physics, music)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a vibration or oscillation) occurring as a result of vibrations in a nearby object, rather than from direct contact or application of force.

Examples

sympathetic vibration
Certain musical instruments use sympathetic strings to enrich the sound.
The strings of the guitar began to move due to sympathetic vibrations from the speaker.
When struck, the bell produced a sympathetic resonance in the nearby glass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resonant: (of a sound or vibration) increasing amplitude due to matching frequency
