hunger

noun
physiological need for food
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
the feeling of discomfort or weakness caused by the need for food

Examples

intense hunger
After working all day without a break, he felt intense hunger.
Children suffering from chronic hunger are at risk of developmental problems.
The charity aims to reduce hunger in impoverished communities worldwide.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – appetite: desire to eat food
  • antonym – satiety: the feeling of being full or satisfied after eating
  • degree higher – starvation: suffering or death caused by prolonged lack of food
