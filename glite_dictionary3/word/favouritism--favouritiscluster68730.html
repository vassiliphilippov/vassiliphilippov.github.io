favouritism

noun
unfair preferential treatment
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪvərɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪtɪz(ə)m /; / ˈfeɪvərɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of giving unfair or preferential treatment to one person or group over others

Examples

favouritism towards children
Her constant favouritism created tension and resentment within the team.
In schools, teachers must avoid any appearance of favouritism towards particular students.
The manager was accused of favouritism when he promoted his friend over more qualified colleagues.
Accusations of favouritism have arisen within the selection committee.
Parents should avoid favouritism when dealing with their children.
The manager was criticized for showing favouritism towards certain employees.

Related Terms

  • antonym – impartiality: the state of treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • synonym – nepotism: the practice of giving jobs to relatives or friends, especially in politics or business
  • synonym – partiality: bias in favor of one thing, person, or group compared with another
