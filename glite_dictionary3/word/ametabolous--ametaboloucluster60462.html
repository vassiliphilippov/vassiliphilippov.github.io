ametabolous

adjective
not undergoing metamorphosis
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪməˈtæbələs / (AmE), / ˌeɪməˈtabələs / (BrE)
(of an insect or other arthropod) undergoing little or no metamorphosis during development.

Examples

ametabolous insect species
In entomology, ametabolous species are considered more primitive than those that undergo metamorphosis.
The silverfish is an example of an ametabolous insect, as it shows little change from nymph to adult.
Unlike butterflies, which are holometabolous, springtails develop through an ametabolous process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hemimetabolous: incompletely metamorphosing (insects with partial metamorphosis)
  • antonym – holometabolous: completely metamorphosing (insects with complete metamorphosis)
