satellite

noun
man-made object in orbit
Pronunciation: / ˈsæt̬.əl.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
An artificial object placed in orbit around the Earth, the moon, or another celestial body, especially for communication or research purposes.

Examples

communication satellite
A television signal is transmitted via satellite to homes around the world.
Scientists use a weather satellite to monitor changes in the Earth's atmosphere.
The communication satellite was launched successfully into orbit last night.
