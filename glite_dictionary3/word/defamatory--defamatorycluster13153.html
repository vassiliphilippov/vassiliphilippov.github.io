defamatory

adjective
damaging to someone's reputation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfæmətɔːri / (AmE), / dɪˈfæmətəri / (BrE)
(of statements or writing) damaging the reputation of a person or group; containing false or unjust accusations that discredit or malign.

Examples

defamatory statements
His lawyer argued that the comments were defamatory and caused irreparable harm to her reputation.
Making defamatory statements without evidence can lead to legal consequences.
The magazine was sued for publishing a defamatory article about the actor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – libelous: containing written statements that unjustly harm someone's reputation
  • synonym – slanderous: containing spoken statements that unjustly harm someone's reputation
