pluviometer

noun
rain-measuring instrument
Pronunciation: / pluːviˈɑːmətər / (AmE), / pluːviˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the amount of precipitation, such as rain, that falls in a specific period

Examples

pluviometer measures rainfall
After the monsoon season, the pluviometer indicated a significant increase in precipitation.
Modern weather stations are often equipped with an electronic pluviometer to monitor daily rainfall.
The meteorologist used a pluviometer to record the heavy rainfall during the storm.
