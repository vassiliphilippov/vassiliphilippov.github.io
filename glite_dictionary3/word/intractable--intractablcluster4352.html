intractable

adjective
hard to manage or control
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtræktəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtræktəbl / (BrE)
(of a person, problem, or material) difficult or impossible to manage, control, or shape.

Examples

intractable problem
His intractable attitude made collaboration almost impossible.
Negotiations stalled over the intractable differences between the two sides.
The doctors found her illness to be intractable despite trying several treatments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – manageable: able to be managed or controlled
  • synonym – unmanageable: impossible to control or manage
