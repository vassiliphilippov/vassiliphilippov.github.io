bad

adjective
serious or severe (situation, condition)
Pronunciation: / bæd / (AmE), / bad /; / bæd / (BrE)
serious, severe, harmful, risky, or likely to cause injury, damage, or loss; not working properly or defective; physically unsound or diseased

Examples

bad accident
He was in a bad car accident and needed surgery.

The city was hit by a bad storm last night.
Breathing in bad air can cause serious respiratory problems.
Eating food that has gone off is bad for your stomach.
It is widely known that smoking is bad for your health.
Banks often set aside reserves to cover bad loans that are unlikely to be repaid.
That account was declared a bad debt after repeated attempts to collect payment failed.
The company had to write off several bad debts at the end of the year.
