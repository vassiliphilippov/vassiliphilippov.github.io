Eos

proper noun
Greek goddess of the dawn
Pronunciation: / ˈiːɒs / (AmE), / ˈiːɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the winged goddess of the dawn; daughter of Hyperion and sometimes identified with Roman Aurora.

Examples

Eos goddess of dawn
A painting depicted Eos with wings, rising to announce the dawn.
In Greek mythology, Eos was said to bring the first light of day to the world.
Many poets have been inspired by the story of Eos and her golden chariot.
