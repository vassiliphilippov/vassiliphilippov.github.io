to re-arm

verb
to arm again with weapons or equipment
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈɑrm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɑːm / (BrE)
to equip again with weapons or military equipment; to arm again after disarmament or reduction of arms.

Examples

to re-arm the army
After the treaty was signed, the country was forbidden to re-arm its military.
The government decided it was necessary to re-arm the police force in response to rising crime.
There was international concern when the regime began to secretly re-arm despite sanctions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disarm: to take away weapons or make powerless
