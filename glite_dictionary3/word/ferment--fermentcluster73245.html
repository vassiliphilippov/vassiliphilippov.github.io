ferment

noun
anaerobic breakdown of organic substances, esp. sugar to alcohol
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a process in which an agent, such as yeast or bacteria, causes an organic substance to break down into simpler substances, especially the anaerobic conversion of sugar into alcohol.

Examples

ferment of sugar
During the ferment, sugars are converted into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
The natural ferment of dough gives sourdough bread its distinctive flavor.
Yeast initiates the ferment that transforms grape juice into wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fermentation: the chemical process of breaking down substances by bacteria, yeasts, or other microorganisms
