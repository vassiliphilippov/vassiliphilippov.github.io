nationalisation

noun
making national in character
Pronunciation: / ˌnæʃ.ə.nəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something national in character; rendering it characteristic of or pertaining to a nation as a whole

Examples

nationalisation of culture
Many language reforms involved the nationalisation of vocabulary to reflect the country's heritage.
The nationalisation of traditional festivals was a deliberate move to foster national identity.
The committee supported the nationalisation of education policies to ensure uniformity across all regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nationalization: the process of a government taking control of private industry (economic sense)
