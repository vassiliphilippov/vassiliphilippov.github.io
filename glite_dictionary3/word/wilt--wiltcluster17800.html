to wilt

verb
become limp or droop (of plants)
Pronunciation: / wɪlt / (AmE), / wɪlt / (BrE)
(of a plant, flower, or leaf) to become limp through heat, loss of water, or disease; to droop and lose freshness.

Examples

to wilt in heat
After being left out all day, the lettuce began to wilt and lose its crispness.
If you don't water the plants, they will wilt in the afternoon sun.
The intense heat caused the flowers to wilt by evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wither: (of a plant) to become dry and shriveled
