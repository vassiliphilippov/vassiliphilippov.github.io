flimsily

adverb
in a weak or insubstantial way
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmzəli / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzɪli / (BrE)
In a weak, insubstantial, or easily damaged manner.

Examples

built flimsily
He constructed the shelter flimsily, so it didn't survive the storm.
The curtain was so flimsily made that it tore at the slightest touch.
The evidence was flimsily presented and quickly dismissed by the jury.
