overhand

adjective
sewn with overhand stitches
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəhænd / (BrE)
(of sewing) sewn together with overhand stitches, which are close vertical stitches that pass over and draw two edges together.

Examples

overhand stitch sewing
Each patch on the blanket was overhand, making the pieces tightly joined.
She carefully finished the quilt with overhand seams along each border.
The fabric edges were overhand for extra durability.
