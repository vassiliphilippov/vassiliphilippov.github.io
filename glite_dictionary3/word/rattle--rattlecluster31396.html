to rattle

verb
shake and make a rattling noise
Pronunciation: / ˈræt̬l̩ /; / ˈrætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈræt.əl /; / ˈrætəl / (BrE)
to shake or make something shake, causing a series of short, sharp noises

Examples

to rattle a door
He began to rattle the coins in his pocket nervously.
Please don't rattle the cage; it frightens the bird.
The wind would rattle the windows during every storm.
She could hear the coins rattling in his pocket as he walked.
The train rattled over the bridge, making a loud noise.
The wind made the old windows rattle all night long.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bang: to make a sudden loud noise
  • synonym – clatter: to make a loud, rattling sound, often by hard objects striking each other
  • degree lower – rumble: to make a deep, continuous, resonant sound
  • synonym – shake: to move or make something move up and down or from side to side with small, quick movements
