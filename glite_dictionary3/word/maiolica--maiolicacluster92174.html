maiolica

noun
decorated tin-glazed earthenware pottery
Pronunciation: / maɪˈɑːlɪkə / (AmE), / maɪˈɒlɪkə / (BrE)
A type of highly decorated earthenware pottery, characterized by an opaque white glaze of tin oxide and often featuring colored patterns.

Examples

painted maiolica plate
Several pieces of maiolica were displayed prominently in the antique shop window.
She admired the vibrant colors and intricate designs on the maiolica plates.
The museum has an impressive collection of Italian maiolica dating from the Renaissance period.
