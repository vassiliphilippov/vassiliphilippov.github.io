saurian

noun
reptile of suborder Sauria, such as a lizard
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔriən / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪən / (BrE)
Any reptile of the suborder Sauria, especially lizards, and formerly including crocodiles and dinosaurs.

Examples

giant saurian fossils
In Victorian times, the term saurian was also used to describe dinosaurs.
Some scientists believe that modern birds evolved from a group of small, feathered saurians.
The museum featured a spectacular fossil of a prehistoric saurian.
