haemosiderin

noun
iron-storage pigment from hemoglobin breakdown
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊˈsɪdərɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˈsɪdərɪn / (BrE)
a granular brown protein containing iron, formed by the breakdown of hemoglobin, often found in body tissues as a sign of disturbed iron metabolism

Examples

haemosiderin deposits in tissue
After multiple blood transfusions, haemosiderin may accumulate in various organs.
Doctors found excessive haemosiderin deposits in the patient's liver tissue.
The presence of haemosiderin in urine can indicate bleeding in the urinary tract.
