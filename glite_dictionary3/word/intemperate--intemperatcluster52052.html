intemperate

adjective
(of weather) severe; not mild
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
(of weather or climate) severe or extreme; not mild.

Examples

intemperate climate
Certain plants cannot survive in an intemperate environment.
The area is known for its intemperate weather, with harsh winters and scorching summers.
The explorers struggled to adapt to the intemperate climate in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym – temperate: (of climate) mild; moderate in temperature or weather
