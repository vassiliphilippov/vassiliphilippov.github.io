factoid

noun
unverified or invented information appearing factual
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tɔɪd / (BrE)
an item of information that appears to be factual but is unverified, inaccurate, or invented, especially when accepted as true because it has been published or repeated widely

Examples

urban factoid
Despite being repeated in several articles, the supposed quote turned out to be just a factoid.
He loves sharing strange factoids he finds on the internet, but many are actually unverified.
The book is full of factoids that sound believable but lack any supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fact: a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym – myth: a widely held but false belief or idea
  • synonym – rumor: unverified information spread informally
