superposition

noun
principle that lower strata are oldest
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəzɪˈʃən /; / ˌsuːpəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle or process in geology where layers of rock or sediment are placed on top of each other, with the oldest at the bottom and the youngest at the top
