peddler

noun
itinerant street vendor
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛdlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛdlə / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place selling goods, typically in the streets or at markets

Examples

street peddler
A peddler knocked on our door yesterday, hoping to sell cleaning supplies.
Street peddlers often gather at the festival, selling food, toys, and souvenirs.
The peddler wandered through the village, offering colorful scarves and trinkets to passersby.
