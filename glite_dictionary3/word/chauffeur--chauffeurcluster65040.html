to chauffeur

verb
to drive someone as a chauffeur
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊˈfɝː / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊfə / (BrE)
to drive someone, particularly in a car, as a paid driver or in a manner similar to a chauffeur

Examples

to chauffeur a client
For the weekend, she chauffeured her friends around the city in her new car.
He offered to chauffeur his guests to the airport early in the morning.
I spent my summer chauffeuring tourists from the hotel to major attractions.
