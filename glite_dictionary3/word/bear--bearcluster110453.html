bear

noun
large, heavy mammal with thick fur and strong claws
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / beə / (BrE)
a large, heavy mammal with thick fur and strong claws, belonging to the family Ursidae, found in North America, Europe, and Asia, and typically omnivorous or carnivorous.

Examples

wild bear
A bear emerged from the forest and ambled across the clearing.
During the winter, a bear hibernates in its den to conserve energy.
The bear used its sharp claws to catch fish from the river.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – cub: the young of a bear
  • degree higher – grizzly bear: a large subspecies of brown bear found in North America
  • degree lower – panda: bear-like mammal with characteristic black and white markings
  • degree higher – polar bear: large white-furred bear native to the Arctic region
