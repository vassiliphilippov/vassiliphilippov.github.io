effect

noun
an outward appearance or impression
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an impression or appearance created deliberately to influence others, sometimes artificial or for show

Examples

effect on appearance
She wore a striking red dress for maximum effect at the party.
The artist layered colors to give the painting a three-dimensional effect.
The director used lighting to achieve a dramatic effect on the stage.
He paused dramatically, just for effect, before answering the question.
She wore bright makeup more for effect than for style.
The lighting change was included for theatrical effect in the play.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
