protest

noun
public demonstration of dissent
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊ.tɛst / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tɛst /; / ˈprəʊ.test / (BrE)
a public demonstration or act expressing strong objection or disapproval, often organized to show dissent or demand change

Examples

protest march
Hundreds of people attended the protest against the new policy in the city square.
The protest turned peaceful after organizers spoke with the police.
Thousands marched in a protest demanding action on climate change.
Her protest against the decision was clear and vocal during the meeting.
Hundreds of people held a protest outside the government building demanding justice.
The company ignored the workers' protest about poor working conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – approval: the act of agreeing with or supporting something
  • synonym – demonstration: a public gathering to express views, often political
  • synonym – rally: a mass meeting of people showing support or protest
  • antonym – support: agreement or approval, opposite of protest
