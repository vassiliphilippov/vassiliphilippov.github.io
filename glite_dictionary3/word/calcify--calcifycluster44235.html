to calcify

verb
become rigid or unchanging
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to become rigid or inflexible in attitude, ideas, or structure; to stop changing or adapting

Examples

to calcify opinions
If leaders become too comfortable, their thinking may calcify and hinder innovation.
Over time, organizational procedures can calcify and resist necessary reforms.
When debate is discouraged, opinions tend to calcify into dogma.

Related Terms

  • synonym – harden: to become or make less flexible or more inflexible
  • synonym – ossify: become rigid or fixed in attitude or position
  • antonym – soften: to become less rigid or more adaptable
