to palpitate

verb
make (the heart) beat rapidly
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.pəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈpal.pɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to cause (something, especially the heart) to throb or beat rapidly and strongly

Examples

to palpitate the heart
Excitement and anxiety palpitated her chest all at once.
The horror movie palpitated the audience's hearts from start to finish.
The shocking news palpitated his heart with fear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flutter: to move quickly in a way that is not smooth or regular, especially for the heart
  • synonym – throb: to beat or sound with a strong, regular rhythm
