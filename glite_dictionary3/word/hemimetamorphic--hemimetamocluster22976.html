hemimetamorphic

adjective
undergoing incomplete insect metamorphosis
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˌmɛtəˈmɔrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˌmɛtəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of an insect with aquatic young) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis in which the young does not resemble the adult.

Examples

hemimetamorphic insect young
Dragonflies exhibit a hemimetamorphic life cycle, with young forms called naiads that live in water.
Mayflies are hemimetamorphic insects because their nymphs are aquatic and look very different from the adults.
Unlike butterflies, hemimetamorphic insects do not have a pupal stage during development.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hemimetabolous: undergoing incomplete metamorphosis (synonym of hemimetamorphic)
  • antonym – holometabolous: (of insects) undergoing complete metamorphosis
