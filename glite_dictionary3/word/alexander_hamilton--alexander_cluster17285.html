Alexander Hamilton

proper noun
(1755–1804) U.S. statesman and Treasury Secretary
Pronunciation: / ˌæləgˈzændər ˈhæməltən / (AmE), / ˌalɪgˈzandə ˈhamɪltən / (BrE)
(1755–1804) United States statesman and first Secretary of the Treasury, founder of the Federalist Party, and principal author of the economic policies of George Washington’s administration.
