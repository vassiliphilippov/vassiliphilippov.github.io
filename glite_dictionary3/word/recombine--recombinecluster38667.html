to recombine

verb
to combine again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (BrE)
to combine or mix things together again, especially after they have been separated

Examples

to recombine the ingredients
After separating the chemicals, the scientist decided to recombine them to observe any new reactions.
Genes can recombine during reproduction, resulting in genetic variation.
You should only recombine the puzzle pieces once all other options have been explored.
