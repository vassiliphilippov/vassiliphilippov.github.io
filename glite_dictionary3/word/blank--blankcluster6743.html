blank

noun
cartridge with no bullet
Pronunciation: / blæŋk / (AmE), / blaŋk / (BrE)
a cartridge for a firearm that contains an explosive charge but no bullet, used to produce a gunshot sound without firing a projectile

Examples

blank in a gun
He mistakenly believed the gun was loaded with a blank, but proper precautions were still taken.
Police sometimes use blanks in training exercises for realism without the danger of live ammunition.
The actor fired a blank during the rehearsal to simulate the sound of gunfire.

Related Terms

  • antonym – live round: a cartridge containing both a bullet and an explosive charge
