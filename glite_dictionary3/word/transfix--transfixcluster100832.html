to transfix

verb
pierce with a sharp point or stake
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfɪks / (AmE), / trænsˈfɪks / (BrE)
to pierce something or someone with a sharp stake or point so as to impale or hold fast

Examples

to transfix a body
Lightning seemed to transfix the tree during the storm, splitting it in two.
She watched as the chef transfixed each vegetable on a wooden skewer for the grill.
The hunter used a spear to transfix the fish as it swam by.
