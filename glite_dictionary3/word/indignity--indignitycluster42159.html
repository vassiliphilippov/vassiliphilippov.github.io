indignity

noun
an act that offends someone's dignity
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪɡnəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪɡnəti / (BrE)
an act or circumstance that causes one to feel shame, loss of dignity, or insult to one's pride or self-respect

Examples

the indignity of rejection
He suffered the indignity of being excluded from the meeting.
Losing his job in such a public way was a major indignity.
The prisoners were subjected to countless indignities during their captivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – honor: high respect or esteem
  • synonym – humiliation: the state or an instance of losing self-respect or dignity
  • synonym – insult: a remark or action that is rude or offensive
