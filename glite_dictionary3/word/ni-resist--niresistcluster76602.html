Ni-resist

noun
corrosion-resistant cast iron alloy
Pronunciation: / naɪ rɪˈzɪst / (AmE), / naɪ rɪˈzɪst / (BrE)
A type of cast iron alloy characterized by graphite in a matrix of austenite, notable for its resistance to corrosion and heat, often used in industrial and automotive applications.

Examples

Ni-resist engine parts
Engineers selected Ni-resist for the valves because of its excellent corrosion resistance.
Pipes made from Ni-resist are commonly used in chemical plants due to their durability.
The automotive industry relies on Ni-resist components for parts exposed to high temperatures.
