bract

noun
specialized leaf below a flower cluster
Pronunciation: / brækt / (AmE), / brækt / (BrE)
a specialized leaf or leaflike structure that is located just below and protects a flower cluster or inflorescence

Examples

bract under flower
Some plants produce a large bract that surrounds and protects their buds.
The botanist explained the difference between a bract and a true leaf.
