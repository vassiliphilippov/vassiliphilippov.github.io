Sarcoptidae

noun
family of parasitic mites
Pronunciation: / sɑrˈkɑptɪˌdi / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒptɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, whitish parasitic mites, some species of which cause mange in animals and skin conditions in humans.

Examples

Sarcoptidae cause mange
Sarcoptidae include the mites responsible for sarcoptic mange in animals.
Research on Sarcoptidae has contributed to our understanding of skin diseases caused by mites.
Veterinarians often treat animals infested with Sarcoptidae.
