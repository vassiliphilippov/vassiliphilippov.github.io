to expunge

verb
remove or erase completely
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspʌndʒ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌndʒ / (BrE)
to remove or erase completely, especially something unwanted or objectionable

Examples

to expunge a record
He hoped to expunge his past mistakes from his memory.
She tried to expunge all traces of her old social media accounts.
The judge ordered the clerk to expunge the statement from the official record.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delete: remove or obliterate text, data, or record
  • synonym – erase: remove written or recorded marks
  • antonym – retain: continue to have or keep something
