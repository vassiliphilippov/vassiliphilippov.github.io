to duck

verb
avoid or evade questions or duties
Pronunciation: / dʌk / (AmE), / dʌk / (BrE)
to avoid or try to avoid fulfilling, answering, or performing (duties, questions, or issues), often by being evasive or clever

Examples

to duck a question
Don't duck your responsibilities—just deal with them directly.
He always seems to duck his share of the household chores.
The politician tried to duck the tough questions during the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dodge: to avoid something by a sudden quick movement or by being clever
  • synonym – evade: to avoid dealing with or fulfilling something directly
