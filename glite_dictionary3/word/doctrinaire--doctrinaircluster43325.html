doctrinaire

noun
an unyieldingly dogmatic person
Pronunciation: / ˌdɑːktrəˈner / (AmE), / ˌdɒktrəˈneə / (BrE)
A person who stubbornly adheres to a fixed theory or set of beliefs, often without regard to practical considerations.

Examples

a doctrinaire thinker
Despite mounting evidence, the doctrinaire refused to alter his position.
She was known as a doctrinaire, unwilling to accept any views but her own.
The committee was composed of both pragmatists and a single doctrinaire who refused to compromise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dogmatist: a person who lays down principles as incontrovertibly true
