barren

adjective
unable to support life or growth
Pronunciation: / ˈbærən / (AmE), / ˈbær.ən / (BrE)
(of land or a place) unable to support life, growth, or habitation; providing no shelter or sustenance.

Examples

barren land
After months without rain, the fields became completely barren and lifeless.
Satellite images revealed a barren stretch of land with no vegetation or wildlife.
The hikers trekked across miles of barren desert before reaching the next village.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fertile: (of land) capable of producing abundant vegetation or crops
