afterwards

adverb
at a later time
Pronunciation: / ˈæftɚwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəwədz / (BrE)
At a later time; after something else that has happened.

Examples

relaxed afterwards
She was nervous before the exam, but felt relieved afterwards.
We had lunch, and afterwards we went for a walk in the park.
You must submit your application first; decisions will be made afterwards.

Related Terms

  • antonym – before: at an earlier time
  • synonym – later: at a time after the present or after the time previously referred to
