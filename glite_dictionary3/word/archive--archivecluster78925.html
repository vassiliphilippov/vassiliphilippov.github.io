to archive

verb
store in an archive
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrˌkaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːˌkaɪv / (BrE)
to place or store documents, data, or materials in an archive for long-term preservation or reference

Examples

to archive important files
After finishing the project, all related documents were archived in the company database.
The company regularly archives its financial reports for future reference.
We decided to archive the old emails instead of deleting them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – delete: to remove or erase, especially from a record
  • synonym – store: to put something away for future use
