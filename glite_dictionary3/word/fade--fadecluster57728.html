to fade

verb
become weak or feeble
Pronunciation: / feɪd / (AmE), / feɪd / (BrE)
to gradually lose strength, power, or freshness; to become weak or less lively

Examples

fade in strength
His energy seemed to fade as the illness progressed.
She slowly faded away after years of poor health.
The old traditions have started to fade in this modern society.
As his energy started to fade, he found it hard to finish the race.
Over time, the brilliant color of the painting began to fade.
The enthusiasm for the new policy didn't fade as quickly as expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym – revive: to become strong or lively again after fading or declining
  • antonym – strengthen: to make or become stronger
  • synonym – weaken: to become or make weaker
  • synonym – wilt: to become limp or droop, usually due to lack of water or vitality
  • synonym – wither: to dry up and lose freshness, often referring to plants
