to dike

verb
enclose or protect with a dike
Pronunciation: / daɪk / (AmE), / daɪk / (BrE)
to surround, protect, or enclose an area with a dike, especially to control the flow of water or prevent flooding.

Examples

to dike a field
After heavy rains, the community quickly started diking the vulnerable areas.
Engineers worked to dike the coastline before the storm arrived.
Farmers diked the low-lying fields to prevent the river from overflowing.

  • synonym – dam: barrier constructed to hold back water
