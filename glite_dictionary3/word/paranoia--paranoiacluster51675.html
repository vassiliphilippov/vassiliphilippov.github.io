paranoia

noun
mental disorder with delusions of persecution or grandeur
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˈnɔɪə / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈnɔɪə / (BrE)
A mental disorder characterized by persistent delusions of persecution, unwarranted jealousy, or exaggerated self-importance, typically without hallucinations.

Examples

suffer from paranoia
After months of isolation, his paranoia grew worse, and he trusted no one.
Hollywood thrillers often feature characters whose paranoia leads them to suspect elaborate conspiracies.
The patient suffered from severe paranoia, convinced that everyone in the office was plotting against him.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delusion: a false belief held despite strong evidence against it
