Synoptics

noun
the first three Gospels of the New Testament
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɑptɪks / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒptɪks / (BrE)
the first three books of the New Testament—Matthew, Mark, and Luke—considered together because of their similar accounts of the life of Christ

Examples

the Synoptics in the Bible
Scholars often compare the Synoptics to identify common sources and traditions.
The Synoptics offer similar narratives of Jesus's teachings and miracles.
The Gospel of John differs significantly in style and content from the Synoptics.
