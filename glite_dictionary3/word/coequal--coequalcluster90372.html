coequal

adjective
equal in rank, status, or authority
Pronunciation: / koʊˈiːkwəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈiːkwəl / (BrE)
having the same rank, status, or authority as another; equal in all respects, especially before the law or in rights

Examples

coequal partners
Each team member is considered a coequal in the decision-making process.
Laws ensure that all citizens are treated as coequal before the court.
The three coequal branches of government provide a system of checks and balances.
