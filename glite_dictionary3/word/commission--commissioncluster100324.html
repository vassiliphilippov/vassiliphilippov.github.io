commission

noun
fee based on percentage of sales or services
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a fee paid to an employee or agent for services, calculated as a percentage of the amount involved in a transaction

Examples

earn commission sales
Because the company relies on commission, employees are motivated to close more deals.
Real estate agents typically earn their income through commission on property sales.
She was happy to receive a generous commission for securing the new client.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fee: a payment made for professional advice or services
  • antonym – salary: fixed regular payment for employment, typically monthly or biweekly
