mesh

noun
network fabric with open spaces
Pronunciation: / mɛʃ / (AmE), / mɛʃ / (BrE)
A material made of a network of wire, thread, or rope woven together with open spaces between the strands.

Examples

wire mesh fence
He caught the fish in a net made from sturdy mesh.
The fencing is constructed from steel mesh for extra strength.
The window was covered with a fine mesh to keep out insects.

Related Terms

  • synonym – netting: open-mesh fabric, usually for fishing or screens
