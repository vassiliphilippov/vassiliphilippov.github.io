confirming

adjective
indicating a suspected condition is present
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɜrmɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a test, symptom, or sign) indicating the existence or presence of a suspected condition or pathogen; serving to confirm a diagnosis.

Examples

confirming test result
A confirming biopsy revealed the presence of cancer cells.
The doctor ordered a confirming test to ensure the diagnosis was accurate.
This blood sample serves as a confirming indicator of the infection.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – definitive: providing a final, conclusive diagnosis
  • synonym – diagnostic: serving to identify a disease or condition
  • degree lower – suggestive: indicating but not confirming a possible condition
