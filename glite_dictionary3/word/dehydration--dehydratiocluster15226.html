dehydration

noun
excessive loss of body water
Pronunciation: / diˌhaɪˈdreɪʃən / (AmE), / diːhaɪˈdreɪʃən / (BrE)
an excessive loss of water from the body, which can cause symptoms such as thirst, dizziness, and confusion

Examples

dehydration in athletes
Athletes must be careful to avoid dehydration by drinking water before and after exercise.
Children and the elderly are particularly vulnerable to dehydration during a heatwave.
Prolonged exposure to heat can lead to dehydration if you don't drink enough fluids.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rehydration: the process of restoring lost water to the body
