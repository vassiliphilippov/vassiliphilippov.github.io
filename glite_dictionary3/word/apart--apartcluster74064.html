apart

adverb
into pieces or parts
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
Into pieces or separate parts; separated so as to be in pieces.

Examples

take apart a machine
She took the clock apart to see how it worked.
The old barn began to fall apart after years of neglect.
The teacher asked the students to pull the puzzle pieces apart.

Related Terms

  • antonym – together: in one place or piece; not separated
