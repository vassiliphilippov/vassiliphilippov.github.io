to disesteem

verb
regard with little or no respect
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstiːm / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstiːm / (BrE)
to have little or no respect for; to hold in low esteem or contempt

Examples

to disesteem a colleague
He openly disesteemed his rival's accomplishments in the meeting.
She tends to disesteem any suggestion that challenges her ideas.
Some critics disesteem popular literature as lacking artistic value.

Related Terms

  • synonym – despise: to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for
  • antonym – esteem: to respect or admire greatly
