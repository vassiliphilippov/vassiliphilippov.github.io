take-up

noun
device reducing slack in machinery
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈteɪk ʌp / (BrE)
a device that removes slack or takes up motion, especially in machinery such as sewing machines or looms

Examples

take-up spool
Film cameras rely on a take-up spool to wind exposed film securely.
The sewing machine's take-up ensures the thread is kept taut during stitching.
The technician adjusted the loom's take-up to improve the tension of the woven fabric.
