to aggrandize

verb
make greater; exaggerate importance
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrænˌdaɪz / (AmE), / əˈɡræn.daɪz / (BrE)
To make something appear greater or more important, especially by adding details or exaggerating.

Examples

to aggrandize achievements
Historians sometimes aggrandize the influence of certain individuals in their accounts.
She tends to aggrandize her role in the project when talking to others.
The company tried to aggrandize its reputation by mentioning minor achievements as major milestones.

Related Terms

  • antonym – diminish: to make something seem less important
  • synonym – exaggerate: to represent something as greater than it is
