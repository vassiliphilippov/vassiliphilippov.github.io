piracy

noun
using or copying someone else's ideas without permission
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪrəsi / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərəsi /; / ˈpaɪrəsi / (BrE)
the unauthorized copying, use, or distribution of someone else's work or ideas, such as music, films, software, or writing

Examples

piracy of music
Academic piracy undermines trust in research and the value of original scholarship.
He was accused of piracy when he submitted a paper using someone else's ideas.
The publishing company sued for piracy after their novel was copied without consent.
Online piracy has cost the music industry billions in lost revenue over the past decade.
The company filed a lawsuit against several websites accused of facilitating piracy of its movies.
He admitted downloading games illegally and said he understood that was piracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bootlegging: the unauthorized production, distribution, or sale of copyrighted or trademarked goods
  • synonym – copyright infringement: the violation of a copyright holder's exclusive rights by copying or distributing protected material without permission
  • synonym – plagiarism: the practice of taking someone else's work or ideas and passing them off as one's own
