flogger

noun
person who administers floggings
Pronunciation: / ˈflɑgər / (AmE), / ˈflɒɡə / (BrE)
a person whose job or role is to whip or scourge others as a form of punishment, especially officially; a person who flogs.

Examples

flogger punishes prisoners
During the 18th century, a notorious flogger enforced discipline among the crew.
The accused feared the harsh reputation of the town's flogger.
The prison's official flogger carried out the sentences in front of the assembled crowd.
