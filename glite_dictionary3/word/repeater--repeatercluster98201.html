repeater

noun
person repeatedly committing the same crime
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpiːtər / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a person who does something again and again, especially attending or attempting something multiple times

Examples

school repeater
As a known repeater, he was under close surveillance by the police.
The judge showed little leniency for repeaters who committed the same offense again.
The new law aims to impose harsher penalties on repeaters to reduce crime.

Many repeaters attend the morning yoga class every week.
The film festival was full of repeaters who had already seen the director's previous work.
