Malay

noun
Austronesian language family of Maritime Southeast Asia
Pronunciation: / məˈleɪ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ / (BrE)
a group of closely related Austronesian languages spoken in Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, and Singapore, forming a western subfamily of the Western Malayo-Polynesian languages.

Examples

the Malay language
Linguists classify Malay as part of the Austronesian language family.
She is fluent in Malay as well as English and Mandarin.
The Malay language has many regional dialects across Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Indonesian: the official language of Indonesia, a standardized register of Malay
  • antonym – Javanese: an unrelated major language of Indonesia
