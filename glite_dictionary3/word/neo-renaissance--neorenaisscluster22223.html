neo-Renaissance

noun
19th-century revival of Renaissance style
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊ rɪˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ rɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
A style of architecture and design from the 19th century that revived and adapted elements of Italian Renaissance styles, distinct from Grecian or Gothic revivals.

Examples

neo-Renaissance architecture style
Her thesis focused on the spread of neo-Renaissance design throughout central Europe.
Many public buildings in the 19th century were constructed in the neo-Renaissance style.
The city's museum is a beautiful example of neo-Renaissance architecture.
