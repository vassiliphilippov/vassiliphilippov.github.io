disproof

noun
evidence or act of proving false
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsˈpruf /; / dɪsˈpruf / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˈpruːf /; / dɪsˈpruːf / (BrE)
the act, process, or evidence that shows something is false or incorrect

Examples

No disproof of the hypothesis could be found, so it remained credible.
