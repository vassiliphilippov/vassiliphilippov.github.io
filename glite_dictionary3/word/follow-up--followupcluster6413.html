follow-up

noun
continued action after an initial event
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
an action or activity that continues, monitors, or checks progress after an initial action (such as a follow-up email or call)

Examples

a follow-up email
After the meeting, he sent a follow-up email to summarize the discussion and next steps.
The doctor scheduled a follow-up appointment to check her progress after the surgery.
The study included a six-month follow-up to assess long-term effects.
After the interview, she sent a follow-up email thanking the manager for his time.
The doctor scheduled a follow-up appointment to see how I was recovering.
Their recent study is a follow-up to research published last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – checkup: a medical examination or review of progress
  • antonym – initial: being the first or beginning; occurring at the start
  • synonym – review: an evaluation or assessment of something after completion
