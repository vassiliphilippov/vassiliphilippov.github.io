arteria labialis

noun
artery supplying blood to the lips
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪriə læˈbeɪliəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə læˈbeɪlɪɪs / (BrE)
An artery that branches from the facial artery to supply blood to the lips.

Examples

arteria labialis supplies lips
Damage to the arteria labialis can result in hematoma of the lips.
During facial surgery, careful consideration is given to the arteria labialis to avoid bleeding.
The arteria labialis is crucial in supplying oxygenated blood to the upper and lower lips.
