cupule

noun
sucker on a fly's foot
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuː.pjuːl / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.pjuːl / (BrE)
a small sucker-like structure on the feet of certain flies, used for attachment or locomotion

Examples

cupule on fly foot
Scientists observed that the cupule allows stable attachment even on glass.
The housefly uses its cupules to cling to smooth vertical surfaces.
The structure of the cupule is critical for the insect's movement and grip.
