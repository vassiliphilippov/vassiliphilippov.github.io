cathode

noun
negatively charged electrode
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθoʊd / (AmE), / ˈkæθəʊd / (BrE)
the negatively charged electrode in an electrical device from which electrons flow into the external circuit

Examples

cathode emits electrons
Electrons travel from the cathode to the anode in a typical galvanic cell.
Replacing the worn-out cathode improved the device's performance significantly.
The television's old picture tube contains a heated cathode that emits electrons.
