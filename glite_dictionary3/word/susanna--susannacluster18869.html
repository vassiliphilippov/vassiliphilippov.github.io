Susanna

proper noun
Apocryphal book and biblical story
Pronunciation: / suˈzænə / (AmE), / suˈzænə / (BrE)
An Apocryphal book consisting of text added to the Book of Daniel, recounting the story of Susanna, a Hebrew woman falsely accused of adultery by two elders.

Examples

Susanna and the Elders
Many artists have depicted the biblical scene of Susanna and the Elders in their paintings.
Some Bibles include the Book of Susanna as an addition to Daniel.
The story of Susanna is often cited as an example of unjust accusation and virtue.
