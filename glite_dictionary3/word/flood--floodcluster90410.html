flood

noun
broad-beam artificial light
Pronunciation: / flʌd / (AmE), / flʌd / (BrE)
a source of artificial light with a broad beam, used especially in photography, film, or stage lighting

Examples

a flood light
Modern LED floods are energy efficient and widely used in studios.
The photographer adjusted the flood to evenly illuminate the entire set.
We used a flood instead of a spotlight for softer, wider coverage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – spotlight: a lamp projecting a narrow, bright beam of light
