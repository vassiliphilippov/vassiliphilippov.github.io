capillarity

noun
movement of liquids in narrow tubes by surface tension
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpɪˈlærɪti / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪˈlarɪti / (BrE)
The ability of a liquid to rise or fall in a narrow tube due to surface tension, which results in the elevation or depression of the liquid in capillaries.

Examples

capillarity in thin tubes
Engineers must consider capillarity when designing microfluidic devices.
Plant roots absorb water from the soil partly due to capillarity in the tiny spaces within the soil.
The capillarity of the glass tube caused the water to rise above the surrounding liquid level.

Related Terms

  • synonym – surface tension: the elastic tendency of liquids that makes them acquire the least surface area possible
