to hatch

verb
devise or invent (a plan)
Pronunciation: / hætʃ / (AmE), / hætʃ / (BrE)
to devise or invent (a plan, idea, scheme, etc.), often in secret

Examples

to hatch a plan
It took weeks for him to hatch the perfect solution to the problem.
She secretly hatched a plan to surprise her friends on their birthday.
The team managed to hatch a strategy to outsmart their competitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conceive: to form an idea or plan in the mind
  • synonym – plan: to think about or make arrangements for something in advance
  • synonym – plot: to make plans, typically in secret, to do something
