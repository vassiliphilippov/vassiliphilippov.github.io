frostwort

noun
perennial US plant with frost-associated ice crystals
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɔstˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the eastern United States, known for its early solitary yellow flowers and late petalless flowers, named for the ice crystals that form on it during the first frosts.

Examples

frostwort yellow flowers
After the first frost, delicate ice crystals appeared on the stems of the frostwort.
Some botanists study frostwort to understand how plants survive cold snaps.
The meadow was dotted with frostwort blooming in the chilly early spring.
