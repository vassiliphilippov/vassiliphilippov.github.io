to conserve

verb
keep constant (physics, chemistry)
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːv / (BrE)
to keep a physical quantity, such as energy or mass, constant during a process, especially in physics or chemistry

Examples

to conserve energy in physics
During a chemical reaction, total mass is conserved despite any changes in state or form.
Genetic information can be conserved across species through evolutionary processes.
The law of thermodynamics states that energy must be conserved in any closed system.

Related Terms

  • antonym – destroy: to put an end to the existence of something; to ruin completely
  • synonym – preserve: to keep something in its original state or prevent it from changing
