to strangulate

verb
to constrict a vessel or organ tightly
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡjəˌleɪt /; / ˈstræŋgjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡjʊleɪt /; / ˈstræŋgjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to constrict or squeeze a body part, organ, or vessel so tightly that blood, air, or another flow is stopped or blocked (medical/biological use)

Examples

to strangulate a blood vessel
A hernia may strangulate the intestine, cutting off blood supply.
Left untreated, a twisted bowel can strangulate and cause tissue death.
The surgeon was careful not to strangulate any blood vessels during the procedure.
Doctors acted quickly because the intestine began to strangulate due to the blockage.
If the blood supply to the tissue is cut off, the organ may strangulate and become necrotic.
In rare cases, an umbilical hernia can strangulate and cause severe complications.
