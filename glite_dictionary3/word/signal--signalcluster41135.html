signal

noun
electrical quantity carrying information
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
an electrical quantity, such as voltage or current, that conveys information when modulated, especially in communication or electronic systems

Examples

phone signal strength
A weak signal can result in poor sound quality on your phone call.
Electronic engineers analyzed the signal for interference from other devices.
The radio receiver amplifies the signal to make it easier to process.

Related Terms

  • antonym – noise: unwanted disturbances that interfere with a signal
  • synonym – transmission: the act or process of sending information, especially electronically
