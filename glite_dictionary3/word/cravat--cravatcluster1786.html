cravat

noun
a type of neckwear tied at the front
Pronunciation: / krəˈvæt / (AmE), / krəˈvæt / (BrE)
A strip of fabric worn around the neck and tied in a slipknot at the front, with the ends overlapping, often as a formal accessory.

Examples

silk cravat
He wore a silk cravat at the wedding, which gave him a distinguished look.
In the nineteenth century, wearing a cravat was a sign of sophistication.
The gentleman adjusted his cravat before stepping onto the stage.
