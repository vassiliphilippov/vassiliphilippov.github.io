to gratify

verb
to please or satisfy
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræt̬.ə.faɪ /; / ˈɡræt̬.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to give pleasure or satisfaction to someone; to please or satisfy

Examples

to gratify a wish
He was gratified by the public's enthusiastic response to his work.
It would gratify me if you accepted my invitation.
The gift seemed to gratify her more than I expected.
Her success clearly gratified her parents, who had supported her from the beginning.
It would gratify me to know that my efforts made a difference in your life.
The company tried to gratify customer demands by improving its services.

  • antonym – dissatisfy: to fail to satisfy or please
  • synonym – please: to make someone feel happy or satisfied
