entozoic

adjective
living inside an animal (esp. as a parasite)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛntəˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛntəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism) living inside a living animal, usually as a parasite.

Examples

entozoic parasite worm
Certain worms have developed an entozoic lifestyle to evade external defenses.
Some entozoic parasites can seriously harm their animal hosts if left untreated.
The biologist studied entozoic organisms found in marine fish.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ectozoic: living on the outside of an animal, usually as a parasite
  • synonym – endoparasitic: living as a parasite inside the host's body
