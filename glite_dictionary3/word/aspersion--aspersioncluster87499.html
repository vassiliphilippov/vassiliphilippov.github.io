aspersion

noun
insult to someone's character
Pronunciation: / əˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / əˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
an insult or damaging remark about someone's character or reputation

Examples

cast aspersions on character
It is unfair to throw aspersions at someone without evidence.
Politicians often endure aspersions from their opponents during campaigns.
She cast an aspersion on his honesty during the heated debate.
Despite the aspersions against him, he maintained his composure and dignity.
He cast an aspersion on her honesty during the meeting.
Throwing aspersions without evidence can seriously damage someone's reputation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – calumny: the making of false and defamatory statements to damage someone's reputation
  • antonym – praise: expression of approval; opposite of insult or defamation
  • synonym – slander: spoken false statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • synonym – slur: an insulting or damaging remark about someone
