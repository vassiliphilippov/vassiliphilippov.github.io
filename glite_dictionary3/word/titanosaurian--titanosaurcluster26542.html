titanosaurian

noun
large Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaur group
Pronunciation: / taɪˌtænəˈsɔriən / (AmE), / tɪˌtanəˈsɔːrɪən / (BrE)
A large, herbivorous, quadrupedal dinosaur with a long neck and tail, belonging to the group Titanosauria and living during the Cretaceous period, mostly in the Southern Hemisphere.

Examples

giant titanosaurian dinosaur
Many Cretaceous-age rocks in southern continents have yielded the remains of gigantic titanosaurians.
Paleontologists discovered new fossils belonging to a previously unknown species of titanosaurian in Argentina.
The titanosaurian had a long, slender neck and a massive, whiplike tail.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sauropod: large, long-necked, herbivorous dinosaur
  • antonym – theropod: primarily carnivorous bipedal dinosaur
