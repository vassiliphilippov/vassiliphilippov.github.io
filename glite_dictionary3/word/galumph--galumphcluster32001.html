to galumph

verb
move clumsily or heavily
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈlʌmf / (AmE), / ɡəˈlʌmf / (BrE)
to move around in a heavy, clumsy, or noisy way

Examples

to galumph through mud
Children in muddy boots galumphed through the kitchen, leaving a trail of footprints.
The dog galumphed across the living room, knocking over a lamp in the process.
The tired hiker galumphed up the stairs, eager to collapse into bed.
