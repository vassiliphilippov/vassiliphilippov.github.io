allele

noun
alternative form of a gene
Pronunciation: / əˈliːl / (AmE), / əˈliːl / (BrE)
One of two or more alternative forms of a gene that occupy the same position (locus) on a chromosome and determine specific characteristics.

Examples

allele for eye color
Each parent contributes an allele to their child for every trait.
Scientists discovered a new allele that affects eye color.
Some alleles are dominant while others are recessive.
