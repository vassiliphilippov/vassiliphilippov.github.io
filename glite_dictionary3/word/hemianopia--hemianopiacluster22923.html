hemianopia

noun
blindness in half of the visual field
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmi.əˈnoʊpi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhiːmi.əˈnəʊpi.ə / (BrE)
blindness in one half of the visual field in one or both eyes, often caused by damage to the brain or optic pathways

Examples

hemianopia after stroke
Damage to the optic tract can lead to hemianopia and severely affect daily activities.
The patient developed hemianopia after suffering a stroke affecting the right side of his brain.
Visual field tests confirmed a left-sided hemianopia in both eyes.
