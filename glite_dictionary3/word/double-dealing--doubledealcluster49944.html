double-dealing

adjective
dishonest or deceitful behaviour
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl ˌdiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb(ə)l ˌdiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
deceitful or dishonest behaviour, especially the practice of pretending to act one way while behaving differently for personal advantage

Examples

double-dealing business partner
Her double-dealing nature made it hard for anyone to trust her promises.
In politics, accusations of double-dealing can ruin a person's reputation.
The investigation revealed his double-dealing and betrayal of his business partners.
