to slouch

verb
walk or move with poor posture
Pronunciation: / slaʊtʃ / (AmE), / slaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to stand, sit, or move with your shoulders and head bent forward in a lazy or drooping way

Examples

to slouch in a chair
He always slouches when he walks down the hall, making him look uninterested.
The students slouched into the classroom after a long day.
Try not to slouch as you walk; it makes you look tired and unconfident.
Don't slouch while you are standing; it looks unprofessional.
He tends to slouch in his chair when he gets tired.
She realized she was slouching and quickly straightened her back.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slump: to walk or sit with a bent posture
  • antonym – stand tall: stand or walk upright with good posture
  • antonym – straighten: to make or become erect or upright
