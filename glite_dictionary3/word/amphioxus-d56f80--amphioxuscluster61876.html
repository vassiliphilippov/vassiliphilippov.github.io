amphioxus

noun
primitive marine animal, vertebrate forerunner
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.fiˈɑk.səs / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈɒk.səs / (BrE)
A small, translucent, lancet-shaped marine animal that burrows in sand; regarded as a primitive ancestor of vertebrates.

Examples

a amphioxus in sand
Scientists collected several amphioxi from the tidal sand beds for observation.
The amphioxus plays an important role in studies of vertebrate evolution.
Unlike fish, the amphioxus lacks a true backbone but shares many features with early vertebrates.
