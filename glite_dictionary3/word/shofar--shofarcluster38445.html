shofar

noun
ram's horn used in Jewish ceremonies
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊˈfɑr / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊfɑː / (BrE)
an ancient musical instrument made from a ram's horn, used in Jewish religious ceremonies and historically as a signal or summons

Examples

blow the shofar in synagogue
During Rosh Hashanah, the rabbi blew the shofar as part of the service.
In ancient times, the Israelites used the shofar to warn of approaching danger.
The haunting sound of the shofar echoed through the synagogue, signaling the beginning of Yom Kippur.
