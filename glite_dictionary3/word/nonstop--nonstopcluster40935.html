nonstop

adverb
without stopping or interruption
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌstɒp / (BrE)
without stopping; continuously or without interruption, especially relating to travel routes.

Examples

run nonstop
Buses now run nonstop between the two cities during rush hour.
The flight from San Francisco to Paris goes nonstop, which saves travelers several hours.
They drove nonstop through the night to reach their destination by morning.
