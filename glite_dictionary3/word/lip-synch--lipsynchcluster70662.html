to lip-synch

verb
move lips in sync with recorded sound
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪp sɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈlɪp sɪŋk / (BrE)
to move the lips in synchronization with recorded speech or singing, so as to mimic speaking or singing without actually making sound

Examples

to lip-synch a song
He practiced for hours so he could perfectly lip-synch the song at the party.
Some artists are criticized when they lip-synch instead of singing live during concerts.
The singer chose to lip-synch her performance due to technical issues with the microphone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mime: to imitate someone or something in movement or action, often without speaking
