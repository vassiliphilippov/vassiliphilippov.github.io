staysail

noun
fore-and-aft sail set on a stay
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪseɪl / (BrE)
A fore-and-aft sail set on a stay, typically between two masts.

Examples

staysail on a yacht
He expertly trimmed the staysail to balance the schooner during the gusts.
The crew raised the staysail to help the yacht achieve greater speed.
When the main sail was too large for the wind, they used only the staysail.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foresail: the sail set on the foremost mast of a ship
