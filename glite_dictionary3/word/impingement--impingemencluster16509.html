impingement

noun
a forceful collision or contact
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a sharp collision or forceful contact resulting from striking against something

Examples

impingement of branches
Repeated impingement of the branches can cause damage to the tree bark over time.
The impingement of the ball against the window startled everyone in the room.
Turbine blades are designed to withstand the impingement of high-velocity gas streams.

Related Terms

  • synonym – impact: the action of one object coming forcibly into contact with another
