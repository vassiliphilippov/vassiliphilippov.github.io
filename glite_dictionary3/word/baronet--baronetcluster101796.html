baronet

noun
hereditary title below baron
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛɹ.ə.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈbær.ə.nɪt / (BrE)
A man holding a hereditary title of honor, ranking below a baron and above a knight, in the British order of honor.

Examples

British baronet title
As a baronet, he was addressed as 'Sir' followed by his name.
He was made a baronet in recognition of his public service.
The estate was passed down from one baronet to the next over generations.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – knight: a man awarded a non-hereditary title of honor for service
