orthopter

noun
aircraft propelled by flapping wings
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrθɑptər / (AmE), / ˈɔːθɒptə / (BrE)
A heavier-than-air aircraft that is propelled by the flapping of wings, imitating the motion of birds or insects.

Examples

experimental orthopter aircraft
Early aviation pioneers often experimented with orthopters inspired by the flight of birds.
The inventor designed an orthopter capable of lifting a single passenger.
Unlike traditional airplanes, an orthopter generates lift and thrust through wing motion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ornithopter: an aircraft that flies by flapping wings like a bird
