to repose

verb
to lie at rest (when dead)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpoʊz /; / riˈpoʊz / (AmE), / rɪˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to lie or rest in a place, especially in a relaxed or ceremonial way

Examples

to repose quietly
After his death, the great leader will repose in the national monument.
The king's body reposed in the cathedral for several days before the funeral.
Visitors quietly filed past as the hero reposed in his casket.
After a long walk, she reposed in the shade of an old oak tree.
He enjoyed reposing on the couch with a good book after work.
The cat reposed on the windowsill, basking in the afternoon sun.
