to shake

verb
move or vibrate rapidly
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ʃeɪk / (BrE)
to move or cause to move quickly back and forth or up and down, often with small, rapid, or forceful motions (including trembling, shaking off something, or making something come out by shaking)

Examples

to shake a bottle
My hands would shake whenever I had to give a speech.
The ground shook violently during the earthquake.
The table began to shake as the train passed by.
She tried to shake the dust off the rug by hitting it against the wall.
The earthquake caused the entire house to shake violently.
The wind made the branches shake back and forth.
He was so nervous that his hands began to shake during the interview.
The ground will shake if there is an earthquake nearby.
When she heard the news, she couldn't help but shake with emotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jolt: to move with sudden, rough movements
  • synonym – quiver: to shake with slight, rapid motions
  • synonym – rock: to move gently or rhythmically back and forth or from side to side
  • antonym – steady: to make or become firmly fixed, not shake
  • antonym – still: to make or become motionless or free from movement
  • synonym – sway: to move slowly or rhythmically backward and forward or from side to side
  • synonym – tremble: to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety, excitement, or weakness
  • synonym – vibrate: to move or cause to move back and forth rapidly
