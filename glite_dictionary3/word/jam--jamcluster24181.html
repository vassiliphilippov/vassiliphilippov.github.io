to jam

verb
become stuck and unable to move
Pronunciation: / dʒæm / (AmE), / dʒam / (BrE)
to block or obstruct something so that it cannot move or work properly, or to become stuck

Examples

to jam the door
Be careful not to let your fingers get jammed in the door.
Her zipper jammed just before she was about to leave the house.
The printer will jam if you load too much paper at once.
A fallen tree jammed the road, making it impassable for several hours.
She accidentally jammed the printer by feeding too much paper at once.
The suitcase was so full that I had to jam the zipper closed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – block: to obstruct movement or passage
  • antonym – free: to release from being stuck or immobilized
  • synonym – stick: to become fixed in one position and unable to move
  • antonym – unblock: to remove an obstruction or blockage
