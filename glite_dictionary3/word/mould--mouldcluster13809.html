to mould

verb
fit the shape of; conform closely
Pronunciation: / moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
to shape or form something by hand or by using a mold

Examples

to mould wet clay
Her hand gently moulded the wet clay as it spun on the wheel.
The plastic was heated so it could mould to the precise contours of the part.
This new jacket moulds perfectly to your body.
Candle makers often mould wax using decorative forms.
Plastic toys are usually moulded from heated material in a factory.
The artist moulded the molten bronze into an intricate statue.
Children love to mould animals from colorful modeling wax.
The artisan carefully moulded the figurine out of clay.
The sculptor moulded her masterpiece by hand, adding fine details to its surface.

  • synonym – shape: give a particular form to
