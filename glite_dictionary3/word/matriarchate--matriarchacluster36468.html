matriarchate

noun
matrilineal system with female headship
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.tri.ɑːr.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.tri.ɑː.keɪt / (BrE)
a social system in which a woman is the head of the family or group, and descent or inheritance is traced through the female line

Examples

matriarchate family system
Debates continue among historians about whether any full matriarchate has existed in world history.
Some indigenous cultures were structured as a matriarchate, with women inheriting property and leadership roles.
The Spartans are sometimes cited as having elements of a matriarchate in their society.

Related Terms

  • antonym – patriarchate: a social system in which a man is the head of the family or group
