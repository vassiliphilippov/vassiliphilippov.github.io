Thomas Young

proper noun
British physicist and Egyptologist (1773–1829)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑməs jʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs jʌŋ / (BrE)
(1773–1829) British physicist and Egyptologist noted for advancing the wave theory of light, proposing a three-component theory of colour vision, and aiding in the decipherment of Egyptian hieroglyphics.

Thomas Young double-slit experiment
Thomas Young was instrumental in proving the wave nature of light through his famous double-slit experiment.

Many physicists regard Thomas Young's work on color vision as a major milestone in the field.
