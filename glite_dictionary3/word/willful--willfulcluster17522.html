willful

adjective
habitually disobedient or stubborn
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪl.fəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.fəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) habitually inclined to disobey or resist authority, often stubbornly or deliberately.

Examples

willful child
Despite repeated warnings, he remained willful in his opposition to authority.
Her willful disregard for the rules often got her into trouble at school.
The willful child refused to follow any instructions, no matter how much his parents pleaded.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compliant: willing to comply or obey
  • synonym – headstrong: energetic and determined to do as one pleases, even if advised otherwise
  • synonym – obstinate: stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
