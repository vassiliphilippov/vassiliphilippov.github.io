impracticableness

noun
quality of being impracticable
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpræktɪkəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpræktɪkəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being practicable or capable of being put into practice.

Examples

the impracticableness of a plan
Despite its initial appeal, the proposal was ultimately rejected for its sheer impracticableness.
He argued at length about the impracticableness of setting up a colony on such an isolated island.
The impracticableness of the plan became obvious once we considered the available resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym – impracticability: the quality of being impracticable; incapability of being put into practice
  • antonym – practicableness: the quality of being practicable
