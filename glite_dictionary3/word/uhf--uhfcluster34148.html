UHF

noun
ultra high frequency radio range
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌjuː ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (BrE)
the range of radio frequencies from 300 to 3000 megahertz, used in television broadcasting, mobile phones, and other communications

Examples

UHF television channels
Digital TV channels are often transmitted over UHF to avoid interference.
The new television broadcast uses the UHF band for improved picture quality.
Two-way radios operating on UHF frequencies are common in urban environments.
