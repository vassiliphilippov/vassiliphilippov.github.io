heap

noun
a large amount or number of things
Pronunciation: / hip / (AmE), / hiːp / (BrE)
a large number or quantity of things, often forming a disorderly pile or collection; commonly used in informal speech with 'of' to emphasize abundance

Examples

a heap of praise
She received a heap of praise for her outstanding performance.
There was a heap of dirty laundry on the floor after the trip.
We’ve got a heap of work to do before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mass: a large amount or number of something, without order
  • synonym – pile: a quantity of things heaped together
