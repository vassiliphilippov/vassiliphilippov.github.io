to bridge

verb
connect or reduce a gap
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ / (BrE)
to connect or reduce the distance between two things, often to overcome a gap or difference

Examples

to bridge differences
Technology can help bridge the distance between family members living in different countries.
The new policy aims to bridge the gap between the wealthy and the poor.
We need someone who can bridge differences and bring the team together.

Related Terms

  • synonym – link: to make a connection between two or more things
  • antonym – separate: to set or keep apart; not connected
