Ninigi-no-Mikoto

proper noun
Japanese mythological figure; first ruler of Japan
Pronunciation: / nɪˌniːɡi noʊ miˈkoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / nɪˌniːɡi nəʊ mɪˈkəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
In Japanese mythology, the grandson of the sun goddess Amaterasu and the first ruler sent from heaven to govern Japan.

Examples

Ninigi-no-Mikoto descends from heaven
According to legend, Ninigi-no-Mikoto descended from heaven to rule over Japan.
Many shrines in Japan are dedicated to Ninigi-no-Mikoto and his divine lineage.
Shinto mythology credits Ninigi-no-Mikoto as the ancestor of Japan's imperial family.
