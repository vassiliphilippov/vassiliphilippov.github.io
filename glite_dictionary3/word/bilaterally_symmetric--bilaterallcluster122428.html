bilaterally symmetric

adjective
having matching parts on both sides of an axis
Pronunciation: / bɪˌlæt̬.ɚ.əli sɪˈmɛt.rɪk / (AmE), / bɪˌlat.ər.əli sɪˈmɛt.rɪk / (BrE)
having identical or nearly identical parts on both sides of a central axis; exhibiting bilateral symmetry

Examples

bilaterally symmetric animal body
A butterfly's wings are a classic example of a bilaterally symmetric structure.
In the study of zoology, many animals are described as bilaterally symmetric creatures.
Most mammals are bilaterally symmetric, meaning the left and right sides of their bodies mirror each other.
