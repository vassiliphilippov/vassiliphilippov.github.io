to infract

verb
break or violate (a law, rule, or promise)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfrækt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrækt / (BrE)
To act in violation of a law, rule, contract, or promise; to break or disregard.

Examples

to infract a rule
Anyone who infracts the company's code of conduct may face disciplinary action.
Repeatedly infracting the community guidelines could result in a ban from the forum.
The student was warned not to infract the school's strict uniform policy again.

Related Terms

  • antonym – comply: to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • synonym – violate: to break or fail to comply with (a rule, formal agreement, etc.)
