catchword

noun
word at top of reference book page
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌwɜːd / (BrE)
a word printed at the top of a page in a dictionary or reference book to indicate the first or last entry on that page

Examples

Each page of the index features a catchword for easy navigation.
Older books often included a catchword to guide readers through lengthy lists.
