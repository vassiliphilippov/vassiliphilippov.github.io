to demagnetise

verb
remove magnetism from a device (erase data)
Pronunciation: / diˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz /; / diˈmæɡnɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmæɡnɪtaɪz /; / diˈmaɡnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to remove magnetic properties from something, making it nonmagnetic

Examples

to demagnetise a metal object
He used a special tool to demagnetise the floppy disks for reuse.
Old videotapes are often demagnetised to permanently erase their contents.
The technician had to demagnetise the hard drive before disposing of it.
Some credit cards stop working if you accidentally demagnetise the strip.
The technician had to demagnetise the steel rod before using it in the experiment.
You should regularly demagnetise old cassette player heads to maintain sound quality.
