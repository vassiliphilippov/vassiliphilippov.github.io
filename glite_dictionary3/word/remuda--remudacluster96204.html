remuda

noun
herd of horses for selection by cowboys
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmuːdə / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːdə / (BrE)
a herd of horses from which those to be used for work, especially by cowboys or ranch hands, are selected

Examples

a remuda of horses
After lunch, they brought the remuda close to the camp so the horses could rest.
Each evening, the wrangler would round up the remuda and check the horses for injuries.
The cowboys gathered around the remuda at dawn to choose their mounts for the day's cattle drive.
