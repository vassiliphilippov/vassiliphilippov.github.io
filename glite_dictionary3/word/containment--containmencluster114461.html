containment

noun
system to prevent radioactive leaks from a reactor
Pronunciation: / kənˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
A system designed to prevent the accidental release of radioactive material from a reactor.

Examples

nuclear containment building
Engineers inspected the containment for any signs of leakage after the reactor shut down.
Safety drills regularly include scenarios in which the containment must prevent the escape of radioactive materials.
The containment was tested to ensure it could withstand extreme conditions during an emergency.
