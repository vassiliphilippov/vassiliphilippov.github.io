dot-com

noun
company operating mainly on the internet
Pronunciation: / dɑt ˈkɑm / (AmE), / dɒt ˈkɒm / (BrE)
A company that conducts its main business activities on the internet, usually with a website whose address ends in '.com'.

Examples

successful dot-com
During the late 1990s, many dot-com startups attracted significant investment.
Several dot-com companies went bankrupt after the bubble burst.
The dot-com era saw rapid growth in online retail businesses.
