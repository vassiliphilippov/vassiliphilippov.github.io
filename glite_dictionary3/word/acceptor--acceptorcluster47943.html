acceptor

noun
person or institution accepting responsibility for payment of a draft
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛptɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a person or institution that accepts a bill of exchange, check, or draft and assumes responsibility for paying the amount specified when it becomes due

Examples

bill acceptor
In international trade, the bank often acts as the acceptor to guarantee the transaction.
Once the check has been endorsed, the acceptor becomes legally obliged to pay the sum.
The acceptor of the draft is required to make the payment when it is due.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drawee: the party on whom a bill of exchange is drawn and who is expected to pay it
  • antonym – drawer: the person who writes a bill of exchange, check, or draft
