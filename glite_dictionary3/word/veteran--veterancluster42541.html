veteran

adjective
experienced; skilled through practice
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛt̬.ɚ.ən / (AmE), / ˈvɛt.ər.ən / (BrE)
Having long experience or practice in a particular activity and therefore competent or skilled.

Examples

veteran journalist
After years working in the field, she became a veteran journalist respected by all her peers.
Despite his young age, his veteran leadership was evident on the basketball court.
The company hired a veteran engineer to oversee the complex project.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inexperienced: Lacking experience or skill
  • synonym – seasoned: Having a lot of experience of doing something and therefore knowing how to do it well
