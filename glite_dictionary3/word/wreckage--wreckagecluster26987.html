wreckage

noun
remains after destruction
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the parts that remain after something has been badly damaged or destroyed

Examples

the wreckage of a car
After the storm, the street was littered with the wreckage of overturned cars and fallen trees.
Photographers captured images of the plane wreckage scattered across the field.
The rescue team combed through the wreckage looking for any survivors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – debris: scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked or destroyed
  • synonym – ruins: the remains of a building, typically one that has suffered destruction
  • antonym – whole: something complete, with all its parts intact, in contrast to wreckage
