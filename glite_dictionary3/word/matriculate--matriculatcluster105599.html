to matriculate

verb
enroll as a student at a university or college
Pronunciation: / məˈtrɪk.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / məˈtrɪk.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to officially enroll as a student at a college or university, especially at the beginning of a degree program

Examples

to matriculate at university
After being accepted, she was thrilled to finally matriculate as an undergraduate.
He plans to matriculate at Harvard this autumn.
Many students matriculate at prestigious universities every year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enroll: to officially join or register as a member or student
  • antonym – graduate: to successfully complete a course of study, typically at a university or college
