deliberation

noun
slow and careful thought or action
Pronunciation: / dɪˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən /; / dəˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
careful thought or discussion before making a decision; the quality of being slow and careful in making choices

Examples

careful deliberation before decisions
His words were chosen with deliberation, avoiding any possibility of misunderstanding.
She moved across the stage with great deliberation, making each gesture carefully and thoughtfully.
The panel reached its decision after much deliberation and calm discussion.
His deliberation in making choices earned him a reputation for wisdom.
She spoke with deliberation, weighing each word before she uttered it.
The committee reached its decision with great deliberation and care.
After much deliberation, the committee decided to approve the proposal.
She paused in deep deliberation before answering the difficult question.
The jury began its deliberation after hearing all the evidence.
