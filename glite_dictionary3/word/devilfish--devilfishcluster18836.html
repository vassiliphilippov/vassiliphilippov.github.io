devilfish

noun
medium-sized whale of the northern Pacific
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛvəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A medium-sized greyish-black whale of the northern Pacific, also known as the killer whale or orca.

Examples

killer devilfish whale
Early sailors called the orca a devilfish because of its fearsome reputation.
The devilfish is often spotted hunting in pods along the Alaskan coast.
The documentary featured rare footage of a devilfish breaching the water's surface.
