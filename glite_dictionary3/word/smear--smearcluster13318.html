smear

noun
mark made by spreading dirt or a substance
Pronunciation: / smɪr / (AmE), / smɪə / (BrE)
a mark or blemish left by dirt or another substance that has been spread across a surface

Examples

dirty smear
A muddy smear was visible on the edge of the table after the children played.
She wiped the smear of chocolate from the boy's face with a tissue.
There was a dark smear on the window where someone had touched it with dirty hands.

Related Terms

  • synonym – smudge: a dirty mark or smear
  • antonym – spotless: without any marks or stains
  • synonym – stain: a discolored spot that is difficult to remove
