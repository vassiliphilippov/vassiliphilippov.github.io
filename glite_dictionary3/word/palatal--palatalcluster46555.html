palatal

noun
speech sound made near the hard palate
Pronunciation: / ˈpælətl / (AmE), / ˈpalətl / (BrE)
A speech sound, such as a semivowel or consonant, articulated with the body of the tongue near or touching the hard palate, as in the initial sound of 'yeast.'

Examples

palatal consonant in language
Some languages have more than one palatal consonant in their phonetic inventory.
The English 'y' in 'yeast' is a palatal semivowel.
The linguist demonstrated how to pronounce a palatal by raising his tongue toward the hard palate.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – velar: a sound produced with the back of the tongue against the soft palate
