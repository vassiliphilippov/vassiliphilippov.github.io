deflection

noun
indicator movement on measuring instrument
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the movement of the pointer or pen of a measuring instrument away from its zero position, indicating a measurement.

Examples

deflection on the meter
A large deflection on the galvanometer indicated a strong current.
Even a tiny current can produce a measurable deflection in sensitive equipment.
The voltmeter showed a slight deflection when the circuit was completed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – displacement: the amount of movement from a reference position
