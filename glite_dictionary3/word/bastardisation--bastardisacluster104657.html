bastardisation

noun
corruption or debasement of something
Pronunciation: / ˌbæstɚˌdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɑː.stə.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of corrupting or debasing something so that it loses its original value, quality, or character

Examples

bastardisation of culture
Many critics argued that the new translation was a bastardisation of the original text.
Some purists see the modern version of the recipe as a bastardisation of authentic regional cuisine.
The city's rapid development led to the bastardisation of its traditional architecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corruption: the process of making someone or something morally depraved or impure
  • antonym – purification: the process of making something pure
