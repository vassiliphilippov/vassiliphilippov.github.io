decalescence

noun
slow temperature rise in heated metal
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəˈlɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈlɛsəns / (BrE)
a phenomenon in metallurgy where a metal, when heated, exhibits a sudden slowing in temperature rise due to an internal change in its crystal structure

Examples

decalescence in steel heating
During the heat treatment, the blacksmith observed decalescence as the metal's temperature stopped rising momentarily.
If not accounted for, decalescence can lead to inaccurate temperature readings during forging.
The phenomenon of decalescence is important for understanding phase changes in steel.
