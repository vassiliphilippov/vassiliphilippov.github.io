refill

noun
an additional supply of a prescription drug
Pronunciation: / ˈriˌfɪl /; / ˈriːˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈriːfɪl / (BrE)
an amount or product used to fill something again after it has been used up (such as a prescription, ink, or paper)

Examples

refill for a pen

Your doctor can authorize another refill if needed.
After using up the paper, she purchased a notebook refill to continue her notes.
I need to buy a refill for my water filter before it stops working.
The store sells ink refills for all major ballpoint pen brands.
