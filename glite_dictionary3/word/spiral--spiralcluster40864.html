spiral

noun
an accelerating process of change (esp. negative)
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪrəl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəl / (BrE)
a process or situation in which something increases, decreases, or deteriorates continuously and more and more rapidly, especially in economics or social contexts

Examples

inflation spiral
Rising energy costs created an inflationary spiral that affected the entire economy.
The country struggled to escape a downward spiral of unemployment and recession.
Without intervention, the crisis could trigger a spiral of worsening debt.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cycle: a regularly repeating sequence of events
