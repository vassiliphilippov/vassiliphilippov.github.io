disinformation

noun
deliberately false information
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪn.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
False information that is deliberately spread, especially to deceive or mislead people.

Examples

spread disinformation online
It can be difficult to distinguish between genuine news and disinformation online.
Social media platforms have become a major battleground for disinformation campaigns.
The government was accused of spreading disinformation to confuse the public during the scandal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – misinformation: false or incorrect information, especially when spread unintentionally
