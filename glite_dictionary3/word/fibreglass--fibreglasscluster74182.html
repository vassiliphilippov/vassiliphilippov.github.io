fibreglass

noun
material made from glass fibers in resin
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbərˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪbəɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A strong, lightweight material consisting of glass fibers embedded in resin, used as a covering or construction material.

Examples

fibreglass boat hull
Many car bodies are now constructed from fibreglass for improved durability.
The boat's hull was made entirely of fibreglass to keep it lightweight.
They repaired the roof using sheets of fibreglass.
