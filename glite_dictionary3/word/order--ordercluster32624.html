order

noun
state where rules are obeyed, especially in society
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a state in which rules are obeyed and people behave correctly, especially in society

Examples

restore order
After the riots subsided, order was restored to the city.
Teachers work hard to keep order in the classroom during lessons.
The new regulations helped maintain order in the busy marketplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disorder: a lack of order; chaos or confusion, especially in society
  • synonym – law and order: a situation characterized by respect for legal authority and peaceful conduct
