antimetabolite

noun
drug that inhibits cell metabolism, used in cancer treatment
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.məˈtæb.əˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.məˈtæb.ə.laɪt / (BrE)
A substance or drug that interferes with the normal metabolic processes of cells by inhibiting the use of a metabolite, often used in cancer treatment.

Examples

antimetabolite cancer drug
Doctors may prescribe an antimetabolite to treat certain types of cancer.
Researchers are developing new antimetabolites to improve cancer therapies.
The chemotherapy regimen included an antimetabolite to inhibit tumor growth.
