Rosa Parks

proper noun
American civil rights activist
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊzə pɑrks / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə pɑːks / (BrE)
(1913–2005) An American civil rights activist best known for her pivotal role in the Montgomery Bus Boycott after refusing to give up her seat to a white passenger in 1955.

Examples

Rosa Parks refused seat
A statue of Rosa Parks stands in the United States Capitol as a tribute to her legacy.
Many schoolchildren learn about Rosa Parks as a key figure in the civil rights movement.
The actions of Rosa Parks in Montgomery inspired nationwide efforts to end segregation.
