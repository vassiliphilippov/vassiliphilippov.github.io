to chafe

verb
to feel irritation or impatience
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪf / (AmE), / tʃeɪf / (BrE)
to feel or show irritation, impatience, or annoyance, especially because of restrictions or repeated discomforts

Examples

to chafe at rules
He began to chafe at the endless restrictions placed upon him.
Many workers chafe when management ignores their concerns.
She would often chafe under his criticism, despite her best efforts to hide it.
He chafes at the strict rules imposed by his new job.
It really chafes me when people are late for meetings.
She was chafed by his constant criticism of her work.
