plantation

noun
large estate growing cash crops
Pronunciation: / plænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / plænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A large estate on which crops such as coffee, sugar, or rubber are grown, especially in tropical or subtropical regions, often for commercial purposes.

Examples

coffee plantation estate
Many workers were employed to harvest timber on the tea plantation.
The plantation economy shaped much of the region's history and culture.
The old cotton plantation stretched for miles across the countryside.
