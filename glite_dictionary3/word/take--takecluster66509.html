to take

verb
experience or undergo something
Pronunciation: / teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
to experience, undergo, or be affected by something (such as an event, illness, or feeling)

Examples

to take an exam
Most students will take the exam at the end of the semester.
She decided to take the plunge and start her own business.
You must take the necessary steps to complete the process.
After hiking in the rain for hours, he was suddenly taken sick.
Several villagers were taken drunk after the festival celebrations.
She feared she might be taken ill during the long train ride.
After getting drenched, the child took a fever and had to rest.
He feared he might take cold after being caught in the storm.
She was out all night in the rain and took a chill.

Related Terms

  • antonym – avoid: to keep away from or prevent oneself from experiencing something
  • synonym – become: to begin to be or come to be something
  • synonym – catch: to contract or become affected by an illness
  • antonym – recover: to return to a normal state of health; antonym in the context of illness
  • synonym – undergo: to experience or be subjected to (something, typically something unpleasant or arduous)
