N

noun
concentration unit (normality) in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrməˌlɪti (for normality); ɛn (for letter N) / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməˌlɪti (for normality); ɛn (for letter N) / (BrE)
(in chemistry) a measure of the concentration of a solution, expressed as the number of gram equivalents of solute per liter of solution; normality.

Examples

N solution strength
The chemist prepared a 1N solution of hydrochloric acid for the experiment.
