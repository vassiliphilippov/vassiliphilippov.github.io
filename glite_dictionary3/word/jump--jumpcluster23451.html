to jump

verb
leave the rails (train, tram)
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
(of a train or tram) to move off or leave the rails, typically as a result of an accident.

Examples

jump the tracks
Investigators believe the train may have jumped after hitting debris on the tracks.
The old freight engine jumped the rails during the storm.
The tram jumped at the sharp curve, causing a major disruption to service.

Related Terms

  • synonym – derail: (of a train, tram, etc.) to come off the tracks
