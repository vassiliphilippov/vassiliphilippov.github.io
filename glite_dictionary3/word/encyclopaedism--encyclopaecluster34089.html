encyclopaedism

noun
profound scholarly knowledge
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpidɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌsʌɪklɪəˈpiːdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
extensive and profound scholarly knowledge, especially of a wide range of subjects in the manner of an encyclopedia

Examples

broad encyclopaedism
Her encyclopaedism was evident in every conversation, touching on art, science, and history with equal authority.
Striving for encyclopaedism, he spent years absorbing information from countless books and sources.
The scholar's encyclopaedism impressed the academic community during his keynote address.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erudition: deep, extensive learning; scholarly knowledge
  • antonym – ignorance: lack of knowledge or information
