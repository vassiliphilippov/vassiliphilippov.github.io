to confound

verb
to confuse or perplex
Pronunciation: / kənˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / kənˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
to confuse or perplex someone, making them unable to think clearly

Examples

to confound everyone
Her unexpected reaction confounded everyone in the room.
The complex instructions confounded even the most experienced technicians.
These contradictory findings continue to confound researchers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confuse: to make someone unable to think clearly or understand something
  • antonym – enlighten: to give someone greater knowledge or understanding
  • synonym – perplex: to make someone feel completely baffled
