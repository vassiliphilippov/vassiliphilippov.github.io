downdraft

noun
a strong downward air current
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌdræft / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌdrɑːft / (BrE)
a strong downward current of air, especially one occurring in the atmosphere or within a building

Examples

strong downdraft of air
A sudden downdraft through the chimney caused smoke to fill the living room.
Pilots are trained to watch for sudden downdraft conditions when flying near thunderstorms.
The wildfire was intensified by a powerful downdraft that pushed smoke and flames closer to the ground.
