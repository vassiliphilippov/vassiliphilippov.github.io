to gather

verb
bring or collect together
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæðɚ /; / \u02c8\u0261\u00e6\u00f0\u025a / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə /; / \u02c8\u0261\u00e6\u00f0\u0259 / (BrE)
to bring or collect people, things, or information together in one place or group

Examples

to gather information
A crowd began to gather in the park to listen to the concert.
Families usually gather around the table for Sunday dinner.
We should gather all the documents before the meeting starts.

When food was scarce, early humans had to gather edible roots and berries to survive.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assemble: to come or bring together in a group
  • synonym – collect: to bring things together into one place, especially things that are scattered
  • antonym – disperse: to spread or move apart in different directions
  • synonym – forage: to search for food, especially in the wild
  • antonym – scatter: in parallel computing, to distribute data from one source to many destinations; often the opposite of a gather operation.
