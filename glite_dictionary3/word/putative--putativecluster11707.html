putative

adjective
generally regarded as such, unconfirmed
Pronunciation: / ˈpjuːt̬ətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpjuːtətɪv / (BrE)
generally regarded or reputed to be the case, though not proven; commonly supposed or assumed to be such

Examples

putative father
Many historians doubt that the putative author of the letter actually existed.
She met with her putative cousin for the first time last summer.
The court is still trying to determine the putative owner of the disputed land.

Related Terms

  • antonym – actual: real and existing, as opposed to supposed or assumed
  • synonym – reputed: generally believed or claimed to be a particular thing or person
