to inveigh

verb
to speak passionately against
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪ / (BrE)
to speak or write about something with strong hostility or criticism

Examples

to inveigh against injustice
During the debate, he inveighed against the proposal with fervor.
She often inveighs against unfair policies in her editorial columns.
The senator inveighed passionately against corruption in government.
Many critics inveighed against the proposed changes in the law.
She inveighed bitterly against the new tax policy during the meeting.
The editor often inveighs against corruption in his weekly column.

Related Terms

  • synonym – declaim: to speak passionately against something, often formally or rhetorically
  • synonym – rail: to complain or protest strongly and persistently
