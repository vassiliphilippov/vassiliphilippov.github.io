to whipsaw

verb
to subject to two opposing adverse actions
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈwɪpˌsɔː / (BrE)
to subject someone to two adverse situations or actions at once, especially to victimise or defeat alternately in gambling, negotiations, or competing pressures

Examples

to whipsaw a person
He felt whipsawed by the aggressive tactics used in the negotiation.
Small investors were whipsawed by the rapid swings in the stock market.
The company was whipsawed between rising costs and falling revenues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – benefit: to receive an advantage; profit
  • synonym – defraud: to illegally obtain money or something else from someone by deception
  • synonym – victimize: to single someone out for cruel or unjust treatment
