to professionalise

verb
make more professional
Pronunciation: / prəˈfɛʃənəˌlaɪz /; / prəˈfəʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone more professional by improving standards, methods, or by turning an activity into a paid profession

Examples

to professionalise a sport
Efforts to professionalise sports coaching have led to higher qualifications and salaries.
Many companies aim to professionalise their staff by offering ongoing training and certification.
The association was set up to professionalise the standards of journalism in the region.
Education reforms aim to professionalise teaching and raise standards in schools.
Several amateur teams plan to professionalise in order to compete at a higher level.
The club decided to professionalise its management structure to attract top talent.
