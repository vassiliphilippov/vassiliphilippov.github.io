Purim

proper noun
Jewish festival commemorating deliverance from Haman
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊr ɪm, puːˈriːm / (AmE), / ˈpʊər ɪm, ˈpjʊər ɪm / (BrE)
A Jewish festival commemorating the deliverance of the Jews from a plot to massacre them in ancient Persia, as recorded in the book of Esther.

Examples

celebrate Purim festival
Children look forward to Purim because of the festive atmosphere and traditional pastries called hamantaschen.
During Purim, it is customary to exchange gifts of food and give to charity.
Every year, Jewish communities around the world celebrate Purim with costumes and music.
