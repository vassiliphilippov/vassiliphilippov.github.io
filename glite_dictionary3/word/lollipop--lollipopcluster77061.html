lollipop

noun
hard candy on a stick
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːliˌpɑp / (AmE), / ˈlɒliˌpɒp /; / ˈlɒlɪpɒp / (BrE)
a sweet treat, such as hard candy or ice cream, on a small stick and eaten by licking or sucking

Examples

colorful lollipop stick
He unwrapped his favorite grape lollipop and started to suck on it slowly.
She gave her son a cherry lollipop as a reward for good behavior.
The children lined up to get a free lollipop from the booth at the fair.
After school, the children bought a lollipop from the ice cream cart.
In England, what Americans call a popsicle is usually called an ice lollipop.
On hot days, I love sucking on a cold lollipop in the park.
