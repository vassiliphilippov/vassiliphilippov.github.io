feathering

noun
turning an oar parallel to the water between strokes
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəɹɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of turning an oar blade parallel to the water between strokes while rowing, to reduce wind resistance and improve efficiency

Examples

feathering an oar blade
During the race, their perfect feathering gave them a competitive advantage.
He struggled with the feathering technique during his first rowing lesson.
Proper feathering helps rowers move their oars smoothly through the air.
