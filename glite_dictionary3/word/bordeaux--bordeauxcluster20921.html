Bordeaux

noun
type of French wine
Pronunciation: / bɔrˈdoʊ / (AmE), / bɔːˈdəʊ / (BrE)
A red or white wine produced in or around the Bordeaux region of France, or a wine of similar style.

Examples

glass of Bordeaux
A fine Bordeaux can age for decades and improve in flavor.
He ordered a bottle of Bordeaux to go with the steak.
Some Californian wineries try to create wines in the style of Bordeaux.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Burgundy: a type of wine from the Burgundy region of France, often contrasted with Bordeaux
  • synonym – claret: a type of red Bordeaux wine, particularly in British usage
