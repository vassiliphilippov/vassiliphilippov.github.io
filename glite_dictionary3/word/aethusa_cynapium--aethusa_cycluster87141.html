Aethusa cynapium

noun
poisonous plant resembling parsley
Pronunciation: / iˈθuːsə sɪˈneɪpiəm / (AmE), / iːˈθuːsə sɪˈneɪpiəm / (BrE)
A poisonous European plant of the parsley family, naturalized in America, that resembles parsley but causes nausea and poisoning if eaten.

Examples

Aethusa cynapium poisonous plant
Children have mistaken Aethusa cynapium for parsley, with dangerous consequences.
It is important to remove Aethusa cynapium from gardens to prevent accidental poisoning.
The leaves of Aethusa cynapium are similar in appearance to edible parsley.
