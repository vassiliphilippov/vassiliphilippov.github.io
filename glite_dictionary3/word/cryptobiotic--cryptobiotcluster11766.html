cryptobiotic

adjective
relating to cryptobiosis (suspended animation in organisms)
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪptoʊbaɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊbaɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of cryptobiosis, a state in which metabolic activity is reduced to an undetectable level, as seen in certain organisms under extreme conditions.

Examples

cryptobiotic state in moss
Researchers observed that under drought, the moss became cryptobiotic.
Some tardigrades can enter a cryptobiotic state to survive harsh environmental conditions.
The cryptobiotic ability of these nematodes helps them withstand extreme desiccation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cryptobiosis: a state in which an organism's metabolic activities come to a reversible standstill
