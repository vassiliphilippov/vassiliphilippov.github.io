atheroma

noun
fatty deposit inside artery wall
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈroʊmə / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
A deposit of fatty material forming within the inner lining of an artery, which can restrict blood flow.

Examples

atheroma in artery
A build-up of atheroma can greatly increase the risk of heart attack or stroke.
Doctors monitor cholesterol levels to reduce the formation of atheroma in major blood vessels.
The surgeon removed an atheroma that was blocking the patient's carotid artery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – plaque: fatty deposit in an artery's inner wall
  • antonym – thrombus: a blood clot within a blood vessel
