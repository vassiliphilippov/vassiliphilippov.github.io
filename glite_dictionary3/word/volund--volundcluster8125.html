Volund

proper noun
legendary smith in Norse mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊ.lʊnd / (AmE), / ˈvɒ.lʊnd / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, a legendary and highly skilled smith, also known as Wayland in Anglo-Saxon tradition and Wieland in Teutonic lore.

Examples

Volund the smith
In many tales, Volund's skills as a smith were unmatched by any other mortal or god.
The legend tells how Volund was captured by an evil king but managed to escape using his craft.
The sword forged by Volund was said to be both unbreakable and exceedingly sharp.
