bronze

noun
alloy of copper and tin
Pronunciation: / brɑnz / (AmE), / brɒnz / (BrE)
a yellowish-brown alloy of copper and tin, and sometimes with other elements, widely used for making coins, medals, and sculptures

Examples

bronze medal
Many church bells are made from a special bronze alloy.
The ancient statue was cast in pure bronze.
They awarded him a bronze medal for third place in the race.
