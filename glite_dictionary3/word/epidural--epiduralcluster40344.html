epidural

noun
regional anesthesia injected near the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdʊrəl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdjʊərəl / (BrE)
A type of regional anesthesia in which an anesthetic is injected into the epidural space of the spinal cord, causing loss of sensation in the lower body; commonly used to relieve pain during childbirth or certain surgeries.

Examples

get an epidural injection
Many women choose an epidural for pain relief during childbirth.
She decided to have an epidural to help manage the pain during labor.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits of receiving an epidural before the surgery.
