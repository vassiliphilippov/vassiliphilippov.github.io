alopecia

noun
hair loss condition
Pronunciation: / ˌæloʊˈpiʃiə / (AmE), / ˌaləʊˈpiːsɪə / (BrE)
the loss of hair from the head or body, often due to heredity, hormonal changes, disease, or medical treatments

Examples

alopecia after chemotherapy
After her chemotherapy treatment, she experienced significant alopecia and began wearing a wig.
Certain autoimmune conditions can cause alopecia even at a young age.
The patient discussed treatments for alopecia with his dermatologist.
