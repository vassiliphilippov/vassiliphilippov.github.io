thallus

noun
simple plant body without true organs
Pronunciation: / ˈθæləs / (AmE), / ˈθal.əs / (BrE)
A plant body that lacks true stems, roots, leaves, or a vascular system, typical of algae, fungi, and some liverworts.

Examples

thallus of algae
In fungi, the thallus can be a single cell or a complex network of filaments.
The seaweed's thallus attaches to rocks with a simple holdfast.
Unlike higher plants, mosses and lichens have a thallus rather than true stems and leaves.
