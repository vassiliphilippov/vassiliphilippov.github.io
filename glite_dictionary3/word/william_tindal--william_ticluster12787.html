William Tindal

proper noun
English Bible translator and Protestant martyr (1494–1536)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm ˈtɪndəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈtɪndəl / (BrE)
(1494–1536) English scholar and translator whose English translation of the Bible had a profound influence on later versions and who was executed for heresy for his Protestant beliefs.

Examples

William Tindal translated Bible
William Tindal was executed in 1536 for his role in translating the Bible into English.
Many scholars credit William Tindal as a pioneer of the Protestant Reformation in England.
The King James Bible drew heavily on the translation work of William Tindal.
