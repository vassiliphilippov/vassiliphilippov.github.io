eosin

noun
red dye used chiefly as a biological stain
Pronunciation: / ˈi.oʊ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ə.sɪn / (BrE)
a red fluorescent dye, obtained by the action of bromine on fluorescein, used chiefly as a biological stain and in cosmetics

Examples

eosin stain slides
Eosin is a commonly used dye in histology laboratories around the world.
Some cosmetics contain eosin for its distinctive red or pink tint.
The tissue sample was stained with eosin to better observe the cellular structures under the microscope.
