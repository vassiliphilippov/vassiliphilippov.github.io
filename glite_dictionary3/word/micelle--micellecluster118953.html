micelle

noun
particle formed by aggregated molecules in solution
Pronunciation: / maɪˈsɛl, ˈmaɪˌsɛl / (AmE), / mɪˈsɛl, ˈmɪˌsiːl / (BrE)
a particle formed by the aggregation of molecules, such as in soaps and detergents, with a charged surface that allows it to remain dispersed in solution

Examples

soap micelle formation
Researchers studied the behavior of micelles in different concentrations of detergent.
Soaps form micelles in water, which help capture grease and dirt.
The structure of a micelle allows it to dissolve both in water and in oil.

Related Terms

  • synonym – colloid: a mixture in which one substance is dispersed evenly throughout another
