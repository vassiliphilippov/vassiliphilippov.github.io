incus

noun
anvil-shaped middle ear bone
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkəs / (BrE)
a small, anvil-shaped bone in the middle ear that transmits sound vibrations from the malleus to the stapes.

Examples

incus bone in ear
Damage to the incus can result in significant hearing loss.
In medical school, students learn that the incus is also known as the anvil bone.
The incus plays a critical role in transmitting sound vibrations within the ear.
