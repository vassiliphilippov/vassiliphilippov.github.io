to overexpose

verb
expose too much to something
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose someone or something to something, such as light or an experience, to an excessive degree

Examples

to overexpose a person
Be careful not to overexpose the film to sunlight or your photos will be ruined.
Some psychologists believe that children can become numb to violence if they are overexposed to it at a young age.
The celebrity worried that being in too many commercials would overexpose her and make the public lose interest.

Related Terms

  • antonym – underexpose: expose too little (especially of photographic film)
