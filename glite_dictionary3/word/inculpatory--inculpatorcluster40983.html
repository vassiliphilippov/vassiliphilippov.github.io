inculpatory

adjective
suggesting guilt or blame
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌlpəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌlpətəri / (BrE)
(of evidence or statements) tending to show or suggest that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing.

Examples

inculpatory evidence
His confession was considered highly inculpatory by the jury.
The prosecutor presented several inculpatory pieces of evidence during the trial.
There were no inculpatory fingerprints found at the crime scene.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exculpatory: (of evidence) tending to clear from alleged fault or guilt
