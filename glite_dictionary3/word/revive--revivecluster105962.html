to revive

verb
bring back to life or restore strength
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvaɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪv / (BrE)
to bring something back into use, activity, popularity, or a former state; to make something active, successful, or strong again

Examples

to revive a business
A new marketing campaign helped revive sales for the struggling company.
After a long break, she tried to revive her passion for painting.
Paramedics managed to revive the unconscious man after several minutes of CPR.
Efforts to revive the old railway line have been supported by the local community.
Recent interest in analog photography has helped revive the use of traditional film cameras.
The theater group plans to revive a classic play that has not been performed for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abandon: to give up or desert completely
  • synonym – restore: to bring back to a previous condition; to reinstate
  • synonym – resurrect: to bring back to active existence or use
  • synonym – resuscitate: to revive someone from unconsciousness or apparent death
