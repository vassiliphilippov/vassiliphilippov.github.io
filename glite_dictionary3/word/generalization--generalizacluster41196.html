generalization

noun
a broad statement or conclusion
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a statement or conclusion that applies broadly rather than to a specific case or individual.

Examples

broad generalization statement
He tends to rely on generalizations rather than looking at specific details.
People often make sweeping generalizations about cultures they know little about.
The study warns against drawing generalizations from a small sample size.

Related Terms

  • antonym – particularization: the process of making something specific
  • synonym – stereotype: a widely held but oversimplified idea of a particular type of person or thing
