aridity

noun
extreme dryness or lack of moisture
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈrɪdəti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being very dry, especially because of a lack of rainfall.

Examples

desert aridity
Long periods of aridity can lead to devastating droughts and crop failures.
Scientists study aridity to understand changes in global climate patterns.
The aridity of the desert makes it difficult for most plants to survive.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dryness: the state of being dry
  • antonym – humidity: the amount of moisture in the air; opposite of dryness
