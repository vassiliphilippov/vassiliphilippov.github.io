metacentric

adjective
(of a chromosome) with the centromere in the middle
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of a chromosome) having two arms of equal length because the centromere is located in the middle.

Examples

metacentric chromosome arms
A metacentric chromosome has two arms of approximately equal length.
Human chromosome 1 is metacentric, with its centromere positioned near the middle.
The researchers compared metacentric chromosomes to those that were acrocentric in structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acrocentric: (of a chromosome) having the centromere near one end, resulting in one short arm and one long arm
  • antonym – submetacentric: (of a chromosome) having the centromere slightly off-center, creating arms of unequal length
