to admit

verb
acknowledge or confess to something
Pronunciation: / ədˈmɪt / (AmE), / ədˈmɪt / (BrE)
to acknowledge the truth or existence of something, often reluctantly

Examples

to admit a mistake
After a long discussion, they admitted the problem was bigger than they thought.
He finally admitted that he had made a mistake during the meeting.
She refused to admit her involvement in the project, even when confronted with evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confess: to admit or state that one has committed a crime or is at fault
  • antonym – deny: to state that something is not true
