epicalyx

noun
whorl of bracts outside the true calyx in some flowers
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkeɪlɪks / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkeɪlɪks / (BrE)
A whorl of bracts resembling and lying outside the true calyx of a flower, as seen in carnations or hibiscus.

Examples

epicalyx of a hibiscus
Botanists often study the structure of the epicalyx to classify plant species.
Each bract in the epicalyx can resemble a miniature leaf.
The epicalyx is a key identifying feature of the hibiscus flower.
