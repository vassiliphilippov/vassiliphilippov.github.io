invasive

adjective
tending to spread undesirably
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
(of a disease or organism) tending to spread rapidly and undesirably, especially into healthy tissue or environments.

Examples

invasive species spread
Scientists study how invasive cells bypass the body's natural defenses.
Some procedures are classified as minimally invasive to reduce risk and speed up recovery.
The doctor explained that invasive tumors can quickly affect nearby organs if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – noninvasive: not tending to spread into healthy tissue
