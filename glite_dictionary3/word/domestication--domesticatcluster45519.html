domestication

noun
process of taming animals or plants
Pronunciation: / dəˌmɛstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dəˌmɛstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of taming animals or cultivating plants so they can live with people and be useful to humans

Examples

domestication of animals
Selective breeding is a key aspect of animal domestication.
The domestication of dogs is believed to have occurred over 15,000 years ago.
Through domestication, many wild plants have been transformed into crops.
Scientists study the process of domestication to understand how crops like wheat were first cultivated.
The domestication of animals has had a profound impact on agriculture and transportation.
The domestication of dogs began thousands of years ago and changed the nature of human societies.
