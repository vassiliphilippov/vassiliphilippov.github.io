chuck

noun
cut of beef from the forequarter
Pronunciation: / tʃʌk / (AmE), / tʃʌk / (BrE)
the cut of beef from the forequarter, situated between the neck and the ribs and including the shoulder blade

Examples

ground chuck beef
Ground chuck is often used in burgers because of its fat content.
The butcher recommended using chuck for making flavorful stews.
We bought a large chuck roast for Sunday dinner.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – rib: the cut of beef from the rib section
  • degree lower – shank: the cut of beef from the leg below the chuck
