chaperone

noun
person who accompanies and supervises young people
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæpəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃapərəʊn / (BrE)
a person, often an older woman, who accompanies and supervises a young woman or a group of young people to ensure proper behavior, especially in social situations

Examples

chaperone at a dance
As a chaperone, she made sure the students behaved during the field trip.
Her mother acted as a chaperone when she attended the party.
The dance committee requires each group to have a chaperone present during the event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – escort: a person who accompanies someone for protection or as a companion
  • synonym – guardian: someone who protects or looks after another, especially a minor
