Seleucus I Nicator

proper noun
Macedonian general; founder of the Seleucid dynasty
Pronunciation: / sɪˈluːkəs ˌaɪ nɪˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / sɪˈljuːkəs ˌaɪ nɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
(c. 358–281 BC) Macedonian general who served under Alexander the Great and founded the Seleucid dynasty in Asia Minor.

Examples

Seleucus I Nicator founded an empire
Seleucus I Nicator played a crucial role in the division of Alexander the Great's empire.
Historians often credit Seleucus I Nicator with founding one of the major successor states after Alexander's death.
The Seleucid dynasty was established by Seleucus I Nicator in the early Hellenistic period.
