incurrence

noun
the act of becoming subject to something
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɜːrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːrəns / (BrE)
the act of becoming liable or subject to something, especially something undesirable, such as a debt or penalty.

Examples

incurrence of debt
Late filing will result in the incurrence of penalty fees.
The incurrence of additional debt could impact the company's credit rating.
The policy covers any incurrence of unexpected medical expenses during travel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – incur: become subject to something as a result of one's actions
