to pull

verb
attract or draw toward oneself
Pronunciation: / pʊl / (AmE), / pʊl / (BrE)
to attract or draw toward oneself, often by means of influence, appeal, or power

Examples

to pull attention
Her inspiring speech pulled many people toward her cause.
The exciting sale pulled in customers from all over the city.
The new restaurant managed to pull large crowds during its first week.

  • synonym – draw: to cause to come toward, as by magnetism or appeal
