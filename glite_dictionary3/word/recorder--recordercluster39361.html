recorder

noun
part-time judge in the UK
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
in the UK, a barrister or solicitor who serves as a part-time judge in certain towns or boroughs

Examples

recorder of the court
After years as a barrister, she was appointed recorder of the borough.
He served as a recorder before becoming a full-time judge.
The recorder presided over the magistrates' court on Thursday.
