oligochaete

noun
a class of bristle-bearing worms (annelids)
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪɡəˌkit / (AmE), / ɒˈlɪɡəˌkiːt / (BrE)
A worm belonging to a class of annelids with hermaphroditic, terrestrial or aquatic members, each having bristles arranged singly along the body.

Each oligochaete possesses bristles called setae that help with movement.
