shadow

noun
place of protection or refuge (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
a place or condition providing protection or refuge from harm or observation, often used figuratively (as in 'in someone's shadow')

Examples

shadow of protection
He grew up in the shadow of his mentor's influence and guidance.
She found comfort living in the shadow of her older sister, who always looked out for her.
The rebels hid in the shadow of the mountains, safe from their pursuers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exposure: the state of being unprotected and open to danger
  • synonym – shelter: a place giving protection from danger or bad weather
