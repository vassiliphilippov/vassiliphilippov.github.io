daikon

noun
large Japanese white radish
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪkɒn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪkɒn / (BrE)
A large, white radish of Japanese origin with a long, crisp root, eaten raw or cooked.

Examples

sliced daikon salad
Pickled daikon is a common accompaniment in many Japanese dishes.
She sliced a fresh daikon into thin rounds for the salad.
