bollworm

noun
moth larva that damages cotton bolls
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊlˌwɜːrm / (AmE), / ˈbɒlˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A caterpillar of various moths that feeds on and destroys cotton bolls and other crops.

Examples

bollworm infestation
Farmers used biological controls to reduce bollworm populations in their fields.
Research focuses on developing resistant cotton varieties to combat bollworm damage.
The cotton crop suffered heavy losses due to a severe bollworm infestation this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – armyworm: caterpillar pest that feeds in large groups on crops and grasses
  • synonym – cutworm: larvae of some moths that cut down young plants at soil level
