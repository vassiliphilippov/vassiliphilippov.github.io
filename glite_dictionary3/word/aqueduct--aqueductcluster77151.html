aqueduct

noun
bridge-like water-carrying structure
Pronunciation: / ˈækwəˌdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈakwɪdʌkt / (BrE)
a structure built to carry water over a valley, often resembling a bridge

Examples

ancient aqueduct
A stone aqueduct spans the valley just outside the town.
Engineers designed a modern aqueduct to meet the city's growing water needs.
The ancient Romans built impressive aqueducts to supply their cities with water.
