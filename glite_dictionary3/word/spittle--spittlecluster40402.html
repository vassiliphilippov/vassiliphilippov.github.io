spittle

noun
saliva secreted in the mouth
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪtəl / (BrE)
Saliva, especially as ejected from the mouth; the liquid secreted by glands in the mouth that moistens it and initiates digestion.

Examples

spittle on the lips
An old man stood on the steps, a thread of spittle clinging to his lip.
He wiped the spittle from his chin with a handkerchief.
The child burst out laughing, sending spittle across the table.

  • synonym – saliva: the watery liquid secreted into the mouth by salivary glands
