spiritualism

noun
belief in or concern with spiritual matters
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪtʃuˌælɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that spiritual matters, especially communication with the spirits of the dead, are real and important; or a concern with things of the spirit rather than the material world

Examples

spiritualism in daily life
His interest in spiritualism shaped his personal philosophy and daily practices.
She devoted her life to spiritualism and sought meaning beyond the material world.
The rise of spiritualism in the 19th century led to widespread interest in séances and the afterlife.

Related Terms

  • antonym – materialism: belief that physical matter is the only reality
  • synonym – spirituality: the quality or state of being concerned with religion or the human spirit
