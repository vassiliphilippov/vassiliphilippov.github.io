interlock

noun
the act of joining or meshing tightly
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪntəlɒk / (BrE)
the act or process of interlocking or becoming joined together so that movement is restricted or coordinated

Examples

interlock between pieces
A strong interlock between the puzzle pieces made the structure stable.
The gears rely on a precise interlock to function smoothly within the machine.
The police maintained an interlock of their shields to control the protesters.
