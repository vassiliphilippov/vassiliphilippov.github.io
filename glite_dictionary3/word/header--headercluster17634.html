header

noun
brick laid sideways at wall top
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
A brick laid sideways at the top of a wall, so that its end is visible on the face of the wall.

Examples

brick header row
Each row of headers gives the wall a distinctive pattern and improved stability.
He explained the difference between a header and a stretcher to the apprentices.
The mason carefully placed a header at the corner to strengthen the wall.
