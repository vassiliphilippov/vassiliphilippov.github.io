pharmacologist

noun
expert in the science of drugs
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːrməˈkɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməkəˈlɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who is trained in or specializes in the science of drugs, including their composition, uses, and effects

Examples

pharmacologist studies drugs
Advances in medicine owe much to the work of dedicated pharmacologists.
As a pharmacologist, she often collaborates with medical doctors and chemists.
The pharmacologist conducted research on the side effects of the new medication.
