to intransitivise

verb
make or become intransitive (of verbs)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrænsətɪˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænsɪtɪvaɪz / (BrE)
To make (a verb) intransitive, or to become intransitive.

Examples

to intransitivise a verb
Linguists often study how objects are dropped to intransitivise verbs.
Some grammatical rules can intransitivise certain English verbs.
The verb 'break' can intransitivise in certain contexts, as in 'The glass broke.'
