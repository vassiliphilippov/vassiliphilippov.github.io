exhibitionistic

adjective
seeking attention in a conspicuous way
Pronunciation: / ˌɛɡˌzɪbɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˌbɪʃɪˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Displaying behavior intended to attract attention to oneself in an exaggerated or conspicuous manner, often by public exposure or dramatic actions.

Examples

exhibitionistic behavior
Her exhibitionistic behavior at the party made everyone uncomfortable.
Some celebrities thrive on being exhibitionistic in public to maintain their fame.
The artist's performances were often criticized as overly exhibitionistic.
