Pitocin

noun
synthetic hormone used in childbirth
Pronunciation: / pɪˈtoʊsɪn / (AmE), / pɪˈtəʊsɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic hormone used to induce or strengthen contractions during labor or to control bleeding after childbirth

Examples

Pitocin to start labor
After receiving Pitocin, her contractions became more frequent and intense.
Nurses often monitor patients closely when Pitocin is given during delivery.
The doctor administered Pitocin to help start the patient's labor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oxytocin: a hormone produced by the pituitary gland that causes uterine contractions and milk ejection
