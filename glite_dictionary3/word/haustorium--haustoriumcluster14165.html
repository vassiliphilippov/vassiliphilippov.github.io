haustorium

noun
root-like organ in parasitic plants
Pronunciation: / hɔːˈstɔːriəm / (AmE), / hɔːˈstɔːriəm / (BrE)
A specialized structure in parasitic plants that attaches to and penetrates the host's tissues to draw nutrients.

Examples

haustorium of a parasitic plant
Researchers studied the formation of the haustorium in various species of parasitic plants.
The mistletoe sends a haustorium into the branch of its host tree to extract water and nutrients.
Without a functioning haustorium, the parasite would not be able to survive on its host.
