teleological

adjective
relating to purpose or design (teleology)
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛliəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛliəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving the explanation of phenomena in terms of the purpose they serve rather than the cause by which they arise; pertaining to teleology.

Examples

teleological explanation of life
Some biologists criticize teleological explanations of evolution, preferring to focus on natural selection.
The legal decision was based on a teleological interpretation of the statute, considering its overall purpose.
The philosopher presented a teleological argument for the existence of the universe.

  • synonym – teleology: the philosophical study of design or purpose in natural phenomena
