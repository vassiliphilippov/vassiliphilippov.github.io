unique

adjective
existing only in a particular place or group
Pronunciation: / juˈnik / (AmE), / juːˈniːk / (BrE)
(followed by 'to') existing only in a particular place, group, or situation; found nowhere else.

Examples

unique to this place
Several customs are unique to the region and are not practiced elsewhere.
The dialect spoken here is unique to this remote mountain village.
This type of bird is unique to the Galapagos Islands and lives nowhere else in the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym – common: found or occurring often; not unique or exclusive
  • synonym – exclusive: restricted or limited to the person, group, or area concerned
