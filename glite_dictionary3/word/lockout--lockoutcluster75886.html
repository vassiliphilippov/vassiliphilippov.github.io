lockout

noun
temporary account access block
Pronunciation: / \u02c8l\u0251\u02d0k\u02cca\u028at / (AmE), / \u02c8l\u0252ka\u028at / (BrE)
a temporary security measure that blocks a user account or access to a system after too many failed login or authentication attempts, usually enforced automatically for a set time or until unlocked by an administrator.

Examples

account lockout
After five incorrect passwords the website enforced a thirty-minute lockout on her account.
IT recommended enabling automatic lockout policies to reduce the risk of brute-force attacks on servers.
He waited ten minutes after the lockout before calling support to regain access to his profile.

Related Terms

  • synonym – account lock: a restriction placed on a user account that prevents signing in or using the account
  • synonym – suspension: a temporary removal of access or privileges, often used for accounts or services
