to cast

verb
form by pouring into a mold
Pronunciation: / kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
to shape a substance by pouring it into a mold, especially metal or wax

Examples

to cast metal
Industrial workers cast engine parts from molten aluminum in the factory.
The artist cast a sculpture in bronze using a detailed mold.
Wax was cast into the shape of a candle before being colored and packaged.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forge: to shape (metal) by heating and hammering
  • synonym – mold: a hollow container used to give shape to molten or hot liquid material
