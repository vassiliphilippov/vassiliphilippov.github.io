unconstitutional

adjective
not allowed by a constitution
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌkɑːnstəˈtuːʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənl̩ / (BrE)
(of a law, action, or practice) not allowed by a country's constitution; contrary to the principles set out in the constitution.

Examples

unconstitutional law
Passing such a ban would be unconstitutional according to the Supreme Court's decision.
The court ruled that the law was unconstitutional and must be revoked immediately.
The senator argued that censorship of the press is unconstitutional.

  • antonym – constitutional: allowed by or consistent with the constitution
