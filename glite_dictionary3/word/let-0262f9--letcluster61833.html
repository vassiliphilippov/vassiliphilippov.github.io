to let

verb
allow to remain unchanged
Pronunciation: / lɛt / (AmE), / lɛt / (BrE)
to leave something as it is or to allow something or someone to remain in a particular state or condition

Examples

to let things be
She told him to let the situation go rather than try to fix it.
Sometimes it's best to let things be and not interfere.
The artist chose to let the paint dry naturally, without touching up any flaws.
Don't let the dog out into the street.
I let her finish her story before interrupting.
The news let him down and he became anxious.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allow: to permit or give permission
  • antonym – change: to make or become different
  • antonym – prevent: to stop something from happening or someone from doing something
