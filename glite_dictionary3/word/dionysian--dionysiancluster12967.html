Dionysian

adjective
relating to Dionysus or his qualities
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.əˈnɪʃ.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈnɪz.i.ən / (BrE)
Relating to Dionysus, the Greek god of wine, revelry, and ecstatic celebration, or to the qualities associated with him; characterized by excess, sensuality, and unrestrained emotion.

Examples

Dionysian celebration
Her paintings capture a Dionysian energy, overflowing with color and emotion.
Nietzsche contrasted the Dionysian spirit of ecstasy and chaos with Apollonian order in his philosophy.
The festival was a Dionysian celebration, filled with music, dance, and wild abandon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Apollonian: resembling Apollo, emphasizing order and reason in contrast to Dionysian qualities
