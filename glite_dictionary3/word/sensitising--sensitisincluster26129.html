sensitising

adjective
making more sensitive to stimuli
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.səˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone or something to become more sensitive to physical or emotional stimuli.

Examples

sensitising effect
Exposure to certain allergens has a sensitising impact on young children.
The sensitising effect of the drug made patients more aware of pain.
The training had a sensitising influence, making staff more responsive to cultural differences.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitising: making less sensitive to stimuli
