to chirr

verb
make a vibrant buzzing sound (insect)
Pronunciation: / tʃɪr / (AmE), / tʃɜː / (BrE)
of an insect such as a grasshopper or cicada: to make a continuous, vibrant, buzzing or trilling sound.

Examples

to chirr like grasshoppers
At dusk, grasshoppers began to chirr loudly all around us.
Cicadas chirr in the summer heat, filling the garden with their persistent song.
The night air was alive with insects that chirred from the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chirp: to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound (insects or birds)
