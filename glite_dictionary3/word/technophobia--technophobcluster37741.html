technophobia

noun
fear or dislike of new technology
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛk.nəˈfoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / ˌtɛk.nəˈfəʊ.bɪ.ə / (BrE)
A strong dislike of or fear of new technology, especially technological devices or complex systems.

Examples

overcome technophobia
Her technophobia made her reluctant to try online banking or social media.
Some older adults experience technophobia and avoid using smartphones or computers.
The company launched workshops to help employees overcome their technophobia.

Related Terms

  • synonym – luddite: a person opposed to new technology or ways of working
  • antonym – technophilia: love or enthusiasm for technology
