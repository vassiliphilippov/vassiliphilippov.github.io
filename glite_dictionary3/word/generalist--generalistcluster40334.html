generalist

noun
person with broad knowledge or skills
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnərəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlɪst / (BrE)
a person whose knowledge or skills cover a wide range of subjects, rather than specializing in one area

Examples

successful generalist
A good manager often needs to be a generalist rather than an expert in one field.
As a generalist, she is comfortable handling many diverse projects at work.
Some companies prefer to hire a generalist who can adapt to different roles.

Related Terms

  • antonym – specialist: a person who concentrates primarily on a particular subject or activity
