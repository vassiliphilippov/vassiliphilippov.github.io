oxidizer

noun
substance that causes oxidation
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪˌdaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance that causes another substance to undergo oxidation, typically by accepting electrons from it

Examples

powerful oxidizer chemical
A strong oxidizer can react violently with certain fuels.
Liquid oxygen is commonly used as an oxidizer in rocket engines.
The oxidizer must be handled carefully because it can accelerate combustion.
