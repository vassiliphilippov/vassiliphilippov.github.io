Bhumi Devi

proper noun
Hindu earth goddess; wife of Vishnu
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːmi ˌdeɪvi / (AmE), / ˈbuːmi ˌdeɪvi / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the earth goddess and one of the two wives of Vishnu, regarded as the personification of the Earth.

Examples

Bhumi Devi as earth goddess
According to legend, Bhumi Devi was rescued by Vishnu during his Varaha incarnation.
Devotees often pray to Bhumi Devi for a good harvest and protection of the earth.
Many temples in India are dedicated to Bhumi Devi as a symbol of fertility and abundance.
