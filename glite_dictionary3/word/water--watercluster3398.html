to water

verb
to produce saliva or tears
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ /; / ˈwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
(of the eyes or mouth) to produce or secrete liquid, such as tears or saliva

Examples

eyes water
His eyes began to water from the strong onions he was chopping.
The cold wind made her eyes water as she walked outside.
The delicious aroma made my mouth water.
Strong winds made my eyes water as I walked along the street.
The cold air can often cause your eyes to water unexpectedly.
Whenever she chops onions, her eyes immediately start to water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drool: to let saliva run from the mouth
  • antonym – dry: to become free of liquid or moisture (of eyes: to stop watering)
  • synonym – tear: to produce tears from the eyes
  • synonym – weep: to shed tears; to cry
