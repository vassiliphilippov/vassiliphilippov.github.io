red-blindness

noun
inability to distinguish red shades
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
A form of color blindness in which a person has difficulty distinguishing red and purplish-blue shades due to reduced sensitivity to long wavelengths of light.

Examples

red-blindness in vision
Because of his red-blindness, he avoids certain professions that require accurate color vision.
People with red-blindness often have trouble recognizing traffic signals correctly.
Testing for red-blindness is common during routine eye examinations.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – color blindness: inability to distinguish one or more types of color
  • synonym – protanopia: absence of red retinal photoreceptors, leading to red-blindness
