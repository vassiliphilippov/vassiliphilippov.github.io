Fahrenheit

adjective
relating to the Fahrenheit temperature scale
Pronunciation: / ˈfærənˌhaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɑːrənhaɪt / (BrE)
Of or relating to the temperature scale on which water freezes at 32 degrees and boils at 212 degrees, commonly used in the United States.

Examples

Fahrenheit temperature scale
She preferred to record the results in Fahrenheit rather than Celsius.
The oven should be set to 350 degrees Fahrenheit for this recipe.
Yesterday, the temperature reached 100 degrees Fahrenheit in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Celsius: relating to the temperature scale on which water freezes at 0 degrees and boils at 100 degrees
