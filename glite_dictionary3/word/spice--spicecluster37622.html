spice

noun
synthetic cannabinoid smoking product
Pronunciation: / spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
a synthetic cannabinoid product sold as herbal incense or a smoking blend and used recreationally for psychoactive effects; often unpredictable and potentially dangerous.

Examples

spice drug use
Police seized dozens of packets labeled as herbal incense, but tests showed the spice contained synthetic cannabinoids.
Teenagers were hospitalized after smoking a bought packet of spice that caused severe hallucinations.
Health warnings advise avoiding spice because its composition is unpredictable and can be very dangerous.

Related Terms

  • synonym – K2: another common name for some synthetic cannabinoid products sold like herbal incense
  • synonym – synthetic cannabinoid: a laboratory-made chemical that binds to cannabinoid receptors and can produce psychoactive effects
