freeman

noun
a man who is not a slave or serf
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːmən / (AmE), / ˈfriːmən / (BrE)
A man who is not a slave or serf; a person who has personal freedom and full legal rights, especially in historical contexts.

Examples

a proud freeman
After gaining his freedom, he lived proudly as a freeman among his fellow citizens.
He dreamed not only of being a freeman himself, but of securing freedom for others.
The rights of a freeman were protected by law in medieval England.

Related Terms

  • antonym – serf: an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system
  • antonym – slave: a person who is legally owned by another and forced to work
