poetiser

noun
writer of inferior verse
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊ.əˌtaɪz.ər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ɪˌtaɪ.zə / (BrE)
A writer who composes verse, especially one regarded as producing inferior or trivial poetry; often used with contempt.

Examples

amateur poetiser
In the 19th century, literary circles often labeled lesser talents as mere poetisers.
Many critics dismissed him as nothing more than a poetiser with little real talent.
Though she dreamed of acclaim, most regarded her as a mediocre poetiser rather than a true poet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – poetaster: an inferior poet; a writer of trivial or poor-quality verse
