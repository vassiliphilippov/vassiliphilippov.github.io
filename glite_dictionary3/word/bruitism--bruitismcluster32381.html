bruitism

noun
use of non-musical sounds in music
Pronunciation: / ˈbruːɪtˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbruːɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
The use in music of noises or sounds taken from non-musical sources, especially as a compositional element.

Examples

bruitism in music
Many avant-garde artists embraced bruitism, using typewriters and machinery as part of their sound palette.
The composer's latest piece is a fascinating exploration of bruitism in modern orchestral music.
The influence of bruitism can be heard in much of twentieth-century experimental electronic music.
