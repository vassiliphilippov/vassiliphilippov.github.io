M1

noun
measure of money supply (currency and demand deposits)
Pronunciation: / ɛm wʌn / (AmE), / ɛm wʌn / (BrE)
a measure of the money supply that includes currency in circulation and demand deposits (checking account balances)

Examples

M1 money supply
Changes in M1 can indicate shifts in consumer spending and saving habits.
Economists often use M1 to gauge the amount of liquid money in an economy.
The central bank's report showed that M1 grew rapidly in the last quarter.

Related Terms
