peridotite

noun
igneous rock rich in olivine
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɪdəˌtaɪt / (AmE), / pəˈrɪdəʊˌtaɪt / (BrE)
A dark, coarse-grained igneous rock composed mainly of olivine, often found in the Earth's mantle.

Examples

mantle peridotite
Geologists collected samples of peridotite from the volcanic region for analysis.
The Earth's mantle is primarily composed of peridotite beneath its crust.
The presence of olivine gives peridotite its characteristic greenish hue.
