viscus

noun
main internal body organ
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪs.kəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A main internal organ of the body, especially in the chest or abdomen, such as the heart, lungs, liver, or intestines.

Examples

ruptured viscus
A ruptured viscus often requires immediate surgical intervention.
Damage to any viscus in the abdominal cavity can be life-threatening.
