Frank

noun
member of ancient Germanic people
Pronunciation: / fræŋk / (AmE), / fræŋk / (BrE)
a member of a West Germanic people who invaded and settled in parts of the Roman Empire during the 4th century, giving their name to France.

Examples

a Frank warrior
Charlemagne was perhaps the most famous ruler among the Franks.
Historians study the migration of the Franks into the western regions of the Roman Empire.
The Franks established a powerful kingdom that later became the foundation of modern France.
