functionalism

noun
psychological theory: mental processes serve adaptation
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌŋkʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪzm / (BrE)
a theory in psychology that considers mental processes as serving adaptive purposes, helping individuals adjust to their environment

Examples

functionalism in psychology
Functionalism emphasizes how mental activities help an organism adapt to its environment.
In discussing psychological theories, she presented functionalism as the foundation for modern applied psychology.
Many early American psychologists were strong advocates of functionalism in opposition to structuralism.

  • antonym – structuralism: theory in psychology focusing on the structure of the mind
