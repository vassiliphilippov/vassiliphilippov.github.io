impromptu

adjective, adverb
without advance preparation
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɑmptu / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒmptjuː / (BrE)
done or said without preparation or planning; spontaneous or improvised (used as an adjective or adverb)

Examples

impromptu speech
He delivered his remarks impromptu, with no notes or rehearsal.
She decided to sing impromptu at the wedding, surprising everyone.
The manager called an impromptu meeting to address the issue.
His impromptu performance impressed everyone despite the lack of rehearsal.
She delivered an impromptu speech at the ceremony when the original speaker failed to arrive.
They held an impromptu meeting to discuss the unexpected changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ad lib: Without previous preparation, spontaneously
  • synonym – extemporaneous: spoken or done without preparation
  • antonym – rehearsed: practiced or planned in advance
  • synonym – spontaneous: performed or occurring as a result of a sudden impulse, without premeditation
  • synonym – spontaneously: In a spontaneous manner; without planning
