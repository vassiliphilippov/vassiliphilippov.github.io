liability

noun
state of being a disadvantage
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being a disadvantage or hindrance; something that causes difficulties or puts one at a disadvantage

Examples

liability at work
His poor communication skills have become a real liability at work.
In the fast-paced world of finance, hesitation can be a serious liability.
The company's outdated computers were a liability during the software upgrade.
