mutilation

noun
the act of damaging or disfiguring
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of damaging or disfiguring the appearance or surface of something, often making it imperfect or unsightly

Examples

mutilation of artwork
Covering graffiti with thick paint resulted in the mutilation of the building's historic façade.
Environmentalists criticized the mutilation of the forest for commercial development.
The ancient statue showed signs of mutilation, likely caused by vandals centuries ago.
