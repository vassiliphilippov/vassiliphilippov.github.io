novel

noun
long fictional prose story
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːvəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəl / (BrE)
a long written story about imaginary events and characters, usually in book form

Examples

read a novel
His first novel quickly became a bestseller and won several literary awards.
Many classic novels explore timeless themes such as love, ambition, and betrayal.
She spent the summer reading a fascinating novel about World War II.
He spent the weekend engrossed in a mystery novel.
Hundreds of new novels are published every year.
She has read every novel written by that author.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonfiction: writing that is about real events and facts
  • degree lower – novella: a short novel or long short story
  • degree lower – short story: a brief work of fiction, shorter than a novel
