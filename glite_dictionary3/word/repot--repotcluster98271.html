to repot

verb
transfer a plant to a new pot
Pronunciation: / riˈpɑt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpɒt / (BrE)
to transfer a plant into a new, usually larger, pot.

Examples

to repot a plant
I need to repot this orchid before it becomes root-bound.
It is best to repot houseplants in the spring when they start to outgrow their pots.
She carefully repotted the tomato seedlings into larger containers.
