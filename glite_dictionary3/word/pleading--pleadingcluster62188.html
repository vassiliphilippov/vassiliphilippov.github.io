pleading

noun
legal statement in a court case
Pronunciation: / ˈpliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(Law) a formal written statement submitted to a court by a party in a legal case, outlining the party's claims, defenses, or other matters.

Examples

court pleading
All pleadings must be filed within 30 days of the initial lawsuit according to court rules.
The attorney submitted a pleading to the court, outlining her client's defense against the charges.
The judge reviewed the pleading to determine if there was sufficient evidence to proceed with the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – motion: a formal request made to a court during a case
  • antonym – verdict: the decision made by a jury or judge in a trial
