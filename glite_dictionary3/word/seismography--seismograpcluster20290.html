seismography

noun
measurement of earthquake vibrations
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪzˈmɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪzˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the scientific process of measuring and recording the vibrations and movements of earthquakes

Examples

modern seismography equipment
Advances in seismography have enabled scientists to detect even the slightest tremors.
Before the development of seismography, earthquakes were difficult to study scientifically.
Modern seismography can distinguish between natural earthquakes and underground explosions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – seismograph: instrument that detects and records earthquakes
  • synonym – seismology: the scientific study of earthquakes and seismic waves
