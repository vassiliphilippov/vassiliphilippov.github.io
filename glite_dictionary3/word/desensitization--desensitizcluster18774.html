desensitization

noun
process of reducing sensitivity
Pronunciation: / diˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making someone or something less sensitive to physical or emotional stimuli, often through repeated exposure.

Examples

gradual desensitization process
Doctors use gradual desensitization to help patients overcome their allergies.
Repeated exposure to violent images can lead to desensitization over time.
The film explores society’s desensitization to suffering in the modern world.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sensitization: the process of becoming more sensitive to a stimulus
