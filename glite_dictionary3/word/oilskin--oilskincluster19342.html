oilskin

noun
waterproof coat made from oiled fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪlˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈɔɪl.skɪn / (BrE)
A waterproof coat or garment made from cotton fabric treated with oil and pigment, traditionally used especially by sailors.

Examples

oilskin raincoat
Old photographs show sailors wearing battered oilskins to protect themselves from the spray.
She hung her dripping oilskin by the door after walking home in the rain.
The fisherman pulled on his oilskin before heading out into the stormy sea.
