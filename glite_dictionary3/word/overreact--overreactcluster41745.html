to overreact

verb
respond excessively to a situation
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvər.iˈækt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəriˈækt / (BrE)
to respond to something with more emotion or force than is appropriate or reasonable

Examples

to overreact to bad news
He admitted that he had overreacted during the meeting.
She tends to overreact when things don't go as planned.
Try not to overreact to minor problems at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exaggerate: represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • antonym – underreact: respond with less emotion or force than is appropriate
