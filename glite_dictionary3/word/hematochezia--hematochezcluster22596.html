hematochezia

noun
passing blood in stool (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmətoʊˈkiːziə / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəˈkiːziə / (BrE)
The passage of stools containing fresh blood, typically caused by bleeding in the lower digestive tract.

Examples

hematochezia with fresh blood
Patients presenting with hematochezia are often tested for colon polyps or cancer.
Sudden hematochezia can be a sign of a serious gastrointestinal condition.
The doctor suspected hematochezia after noticing blood in the patient's stool.

Related Terms

  • antonym – melena: the passage of black, tarry stools containing digested blood
