to encroach

verb
gradually intrude or infringe
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkroʊt͡ʃ /; / ɪnˈkroʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to gradually go into someone else's space, rights, or limits in a way that is unwanted or not allowed

Examples

to encroach on territory
He felt that the company was starting to encroach upon his personal time.
The new building project will encroach on the public park.
The weeds quickly encroached into the garden, taking over the flower beds.
Developers must be careful not to encroach on protected wildlife habitats.
She felt her colleague was starting to encroach on her responsibilities at work.
The city's suburbs continue to encroach upon farmland each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – infringe: to act so as to limit or undermine (something); to encroach
  • synonym – intrude: to put oneself deliberately into a place or situation where one is unwelcome
  • synonym – invade: to enter forcefully or as an unwelcome intruder
  • antonym – withdraw: to move back or away, the opposite of encroach
