bluff

noun
high steep bank or cliff
Pronunciation: / blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
A high, steep bank or cliff, especially one formed by river erosion.

Examples

steep bluff
Erosion gradually carved the bluff into its present dramatic shape.
The boat drifted past a towering bluff that overlooked the river.
They set up camp at the foot of the bluff for the night.
