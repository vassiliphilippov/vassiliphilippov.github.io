sinking

noun
descent through liquid, especially water
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of something going down below the surface of liquid, especially water

Examples

sinking of a ship
After the sinking of the fishing boat, the crew was rescued by a nearby vessel.
The sinking of Atlantis is a legend that has fascinated people for centuries.
The news reported the sinking of a cargo ship during the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – floating: the act of remaining on the surface of a liquid
  • synonym – submersion: the act of being submerged or put under liquid
