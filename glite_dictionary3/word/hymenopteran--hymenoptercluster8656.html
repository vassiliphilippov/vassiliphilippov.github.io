hymenopteran

noun
an insect of the order Hymenoptera
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪməˈnɑptərən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪmɪˈnɒptərən / (BrE)
an insect of the order Hymenoptera, characterized by two pairs of membranous wings and, in many species, a stinger or piercing ovipositor; includes bees, wasps, and ants

Examples

social hymenopteran insects
Many scientists study hymenopteran behavior to understand the complex societies of bees and ants.
Parasitic wasps, a type of hymenopteran, are used in biological pest control.
The entomologist identified the insect as a hymenopteran because of its distinctive wings and stinger.
