major

noun
student specializing in a particular subject
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a university or college student who is specializing in a particular subject as their principal field of study

Examples

psychology major
As a major in economics, he often attended additional seminars.
Many English majors enjoy analyzing literature and writing essays.
She is a biology major at Stanford University.
