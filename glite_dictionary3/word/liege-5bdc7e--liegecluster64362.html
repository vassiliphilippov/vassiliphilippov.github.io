liege

noun
feudal lord entitled to service
Pronunciation: / lidʒ / (AmE), / liːdʒ / (BrE)
a feudal lord to whom allegiance and service are owed, especially in the context of the medieval feudal system

Examples

liege lord
He served his liege faithfully throughout the long and turbulent wars.
In the castle hall, the knights pledged their loyalty to their liege.
The liege summoned all his vassals for a council of war.

Related Terms

  • antonym – vassal: a person who held land from a feudal lord and owed him allegiance
