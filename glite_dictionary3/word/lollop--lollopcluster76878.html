to lollop

verb
walk with a relaxed, bouncing motion
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑː.ləp / (AmE), / ˈlɒl.əp / (BrE)
to move or walk with an awkward, relaxed, and slightly bouncing motion

Examples

to lollop down the street
After a long day, he could only lollop lazily around the house, barely lifting his feet.
She watched the bear lollop down the hill with a carefree swagger.
The puppy would lollop happily through the grass, its ears flopping from side to side.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amble: to walk at a slow, relaxed pace
  • antonym – stride: to walk with long, decisive steps
