antinomianism

noun
belief that faith frees from moral law
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tɪˈnoʊ.mi.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈnəʊ.mi.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
the theological doctrine that faith and divine grace free a Christian from obligation to obey any moral law

Examples

religious antinomianism debate
Debates about antinomianism shaped the development of early Protestant theology.
His interpretation of grace led some to accuse him of antinomianism.
The doctrine of antinomianism was considered heretical by many religious authorities.
