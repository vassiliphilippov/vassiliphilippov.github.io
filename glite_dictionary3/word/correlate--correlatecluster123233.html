correlate

noun
thing closely related to another; complementary variable
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒrɪleɪt / (BrE)
either of two or more things that are closely related or complementary, especially as variables whose values change together

Examples

a correlate of stress
Geneticists identified several correlates that may influence the development of the disease.
In their experiment, pain intensity and stress level acted as significant correlates.
Temperature and humidity are often considered correlates in climate research.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – dependent variable: a variable whose value depends on another
  • degree lower – independent variable: a variable whose variation does not depend on another
  • synonym – variable: an element, feature, or factor that is liable to vary or change
