scholia

noun
ancient explanatory notes in classical works
Pronunciation: / ˈskoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈskəʊlɪə / (BrE)
Ancient marginal notes or explanatory comments written by scholars or commentators on classical texts, often found in manuscripts.

Examples

scholia on ancient texts
He devoted years to studying the scholia on Homer to deepen his knowledge of Greek epic poetry.
Many ancient manuscripts contain scholia in the margins elucidating difficult passages.
The scholia were essential for understanding how ancient readers interpreted the text.
