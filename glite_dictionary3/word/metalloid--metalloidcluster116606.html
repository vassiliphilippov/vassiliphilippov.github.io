metalloid

adjective
element with both metallic and nonmetallic properties
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtəlˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəlɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a chemical element) having some properties of metals and some of nonmetals.

Examples

metalloid element type
Arsenic is considered a metalloid because it shows characteristics of both metals and nonmetals.
Certain metalloid elements can conduct electricity, but not as well as true metals.
Pure silicon is a classic example of a metalloid used in the electronics industry.
