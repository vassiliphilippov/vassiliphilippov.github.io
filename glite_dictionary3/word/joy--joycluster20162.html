to joy

verb
(archaic) make glad or happy
Pronunciation: / dʒɔɪ / (AmE), / dʒɔɪ / (BrE)
(archaic) to make someone happy or to feel great happiness

Examples

to joy the heart
Let music joy your spirit in times of sorrow.
She sought to joy her friends with a kind gesture.
The news did much to joy the hearts of the people.
In the company of friends, he joyed as he had not in years.
She joyed in the success of her children, her face alight with pride.
They joyed at the arrival of spring after a long, harsh winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gladden: to make someone glad or happy
  • synonym – rejoice: to feel or show great joy or delight
  • antonym – sorrow: to feel deep distress or sadness
