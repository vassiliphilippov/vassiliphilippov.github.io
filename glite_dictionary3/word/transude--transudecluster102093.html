to transude

verb
release liquid in drops or small amounts
Pronunciation: / trænˈsuːd / (AmE), / trænˈsjuːd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to pass through a surface or membrane and be released in drops or small quantities.

Examples

to transude through walls
If excess moisture transudes through the wall, it may indicate poor waterproofing.
The wound began to transude a clear serum as it healed.
Tiny droplets of fluid transude from the surface of the plant's leaves in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exude: to discharge (liquid or moisture) slowly through pores or small openings
