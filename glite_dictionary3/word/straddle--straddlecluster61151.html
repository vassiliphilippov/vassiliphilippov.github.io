to straddle

verb
be noncommittal or avoid taking sides
Pronunciation: / ˈstræd.əl / (AmE), / ˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
to avoid committing to one side in a dispute or issue; to be noncommittal

Examples

straddle the fence
Politicians often straddle the fence on divisive topics to appeal to a broader base.
The company tends to straddle controversial issues to avoid upsetting customers.
When asked about the merger, she chose to straddle rather than give a direct answer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – side: to support a particular person or opinion
  • synonym – waffle: to avoid giving a clear answer or position
