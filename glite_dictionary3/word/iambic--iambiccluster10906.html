iambic

noun
line of verse made of iambs
Pronunciation: / aɪˈæmbɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈæmbɪk / (BrE)
A line of verse composed of iambs (metrical feet with an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable).

Examples

iambic line
Many English poems are written in iambic to achieve a natural speaking cadence.
Shakespeare’s plays often use iambic for both dialogue and verse.
The poet composed the sonnet in perfect iambic, giving the lines a rhythmic flow.
