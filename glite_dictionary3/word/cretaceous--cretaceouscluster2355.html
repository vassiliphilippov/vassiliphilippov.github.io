Cretaceous

noun
geological period from 145 to 66 million years ago
Pronunciation: / krəˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / krɪˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
the geological period lasting from about 145 to 66 million years ago, marking the end of the age of dinosaurs and the emergence of flowering plants and modern insects

Examples

the Cretaceous period
Flowering plants first appeared during the Cretaceous, changing Earth's ecosystems dramatically.
Many of the best-known dinosaur fossils date from the Cretaceous period.
The mass extinction at the end of the Cretaceous wiped out most dinosaurs.
