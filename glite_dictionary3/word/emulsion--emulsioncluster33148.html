emulsion

noun
photographic coating of silver bromide in gelatin
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmʌlʃən / (BrE)
a light-sensitive coating on photographic paper or film, consisting of fine grains of silver bromide suspended in gelatin, used to capture images

Examples

emulsion layer on film
Handle negatives carefully to avoid damaging the emulsion.
The photographic film has a thin layer of emulsion on its surface.
The quality of the emulsion determines the sharpness of the final image.
