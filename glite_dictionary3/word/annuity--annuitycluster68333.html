annuity

noun
fixed yearly payment, often from investment
Pronunciation: / əˈnuːəti / (AmE), / əˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
A fixed sum of money paid to someone each year, typically for the rest of their life or for a set period, often as a result of a capital investment.

Examples

buy an annuity for retirement
After decades of saving, she decided to purchase an annuity to provide steady income during retirement.
He relied on the annuity from his investments to support his lifestyle after retiring.
Many life insurance policies can be converted into an annuity with regular monthly payments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lump sum: a single payment made at one time, as contrasted with regular payments
  • synonym – pension: regular payment made during retirement
