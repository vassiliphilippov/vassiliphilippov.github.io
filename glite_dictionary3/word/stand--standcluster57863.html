to stand

verb
withstand or endure something
Pronunciation: / stænd / (AmE), / stænd / (BrE)
to withstand, endure, resist, or tolerate something (such as pressure, criticism, or something unpleasant); to not yield or give in

Examples

to stand the pressure
Her reputation has stood the test of time despite many challenges.
The bridge was built to stand the force of strong river currents.
These ancient ruins have stood the harsh weather for centuries.
Despite the criticism, she decided to stand by her convictions.
He knew the consequences but chose to stand firm and not apologize.
The company will stand its ground against unfair competition.
How do you stand working in such a noisy environment?
I can't stand the loud music next door every night.
She couldn't stand his rude behavior any longer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cannot bear: unable to accept or endure something
  • synonym – endure: to continue despite difficulty or suffering
  • synonym – resist: to strive against or oppose something
  • synonym – tolerate: to accept or bear something unpleasant
  • synonym – withstand: to resist or hold out against something
  • antonym – yield: to give way under pressure; to surrender
