Aesculapius

proper noun
Roman god of medicine and healing
Pronunciation: / ˌɛskjəˈleɪpiəs / (AmE), / ˌiːskjʊˈleɪpɪəs / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of medicine and healing, son of Apollo, regarded as a hero and often identified with the Greek Asclepius.

Aesculapius statue
A statue of Aesculapius stands at the entrance of the old medical academy.
Legends say that Aesculapius could cure any illness with his divine touch.
The ancient Romans worshipped Aesculapius as the god of medicine and healing.
