whiptail

noun
a slender, active New World lizard
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.teɪl / (BrE)
any of various slender, active New World lizards with long, whip-like tails, belonging to the family Teiidae.

Examples

whiptail lizard
A whiptail lizard darted out from under a rock to catch an insect.
Several species of whiptail are found throughout the southwestern United States.
The desert whiptail can move exceptionally quickly across sandy terrain.
