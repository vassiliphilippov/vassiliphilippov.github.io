to force-land

verb
to make an emergency landing
Pronunciation: / fɔːrs ˈlænd / (AmE), / fɔːs ˈlænd / (BrE)
to make (an aircraft) land unexpectedly or under emergency conditions, typically due to technical failure or bad weather

Examples

to force-land a plane
Emergency responders arrived quickly after the helicopter was force-landed on a highway.
In severe weather, the crew decided it was safest to force-land rather than risk continuing the flight.
The pilot had to force-land the plane in a field after both engines failed.
