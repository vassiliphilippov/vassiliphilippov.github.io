carbonation

noun
process of dissolving CO₂ in liquid
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrbəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of dissolving carbon dioxide in a liquid, especially to make it fizzy, as in soda water or soft drinks

Examples

carbonation in soda
During the manufacturing process, carbonation is added to soft drinks to make them fizzy.
Some people prefer drinks with less carbonation to avoid bloating.
The carbonation in sparkling water gives it a refreshing fizz.
