absolutization

noun
making something absolute; treating as absolute
Pronunciation: / ˌæb.səˌluː.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˌljuː.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something absolute or treating it as absolute, especially in a philosophical or theoretical context.

Examples

absolutization of values
Her argument was weakened by the absolutization of her personal beliefs.
Philosophers often criticize the absolutization of concepts that are inherently relative.
The absolutization of certain values can lead to dogmatism in a society.
