to electroplate

verb
coat with metal by electrolysis
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktroʊˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to cover the surface of an object with a thin layer of metal using electrolysis

Examples

to electroplate jewelry
A jeweler can electroplate a ring with gold to improve its appearance.
Some companies electroplate silverware to prevent it from tarnishing.
The manufacturer electroplated the parts to add a protective layer against corrosion.
