caecilian

noun
limbless burrowing amphibian (order Gymnophiona)
Pronunciation: / siˈsɪliən / (AmE), / siːˈsɪliən / (BrE)
a small, slender, limbless burrowing amphibian of the order Gymnophiona, typically inhabiting moist tropical soil

Examples

burrowing caecilian animal
Researchers discovered a new species of caecilian in the rainforest of South America.
The caecilian is rarely seen above ground because it spends most of its life burrowing in moist soil.
Unlike frogs and salamanders, caecilians have no limbs and resemble large worms or snakes.
