to edit

verb
revise written work for publication
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪt / (BrE)
to prepare, revise, correct, or supervise written material or other content for publication or presentation, including removing or changing parts as needed

Examples

to edit an article
Journalists often edit their articles several times to ensure accuracy and clarity.
She was hired to edit the manuscript before it went to print.
The publisher asked him to edit the novel for length and style.
For over a decade, Mr. Robinson has edited the prestigious science magazine.
He was hired to edit the national daily newspaper, bringing a fresh perspective to its coverage.
She will edit the new anthology of short stories compiled from emerging writers.
She had to edit out unnecessary paragraphs to meet the word limit.
The director decided to edit several scenes to make the movie shorter.
They plan to edit the interview before it airs.

  • antonym – add: to include or insert material into a text or work
  • synonym – cut: to remove or delete parts of a text or work
  • synonym – delete: to remove or erase written or recorded material
  • synonym – proofread: to read text in order to detect and correct errors
  • synonym – publish: to prepare and issue printed or digital material for public distribution
  • synonym – revise: to reconsider and alter text in order to improve it
