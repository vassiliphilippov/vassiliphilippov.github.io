agnostic

noun
person who is doubtful or noncommittal
Pronunciation: / æɡˈnɑstɪk /; / æɡˈnɑːstɪk / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
a person who is noncommittal or doubtful, especially about the existence of God or the truth of something

Examples

an agnostic person
As an agnostic, he prefers to keep an open mind about complex scientific debates.
Many agnostics demand stronger evidence before accepting new claims.
She considers herself an agnostic when it comes to political ideologies.
Many agnostics argue that religious truth is unknowable and prefer to suspend judgment.
She considers herself an agnostic because she feels there's no way to know if God exists.
The debate included both a passionate agnostic and a convinced believer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – atheist: a person who does not believe in the existence of God or gods
  • antonym – believer: a person who accepts something as true or real, often without proof
  • synonym – skeptic: a person inclined to question or doubt accepted opinions
  • antonym – theist: a person who believes in the existence of a god or gods
