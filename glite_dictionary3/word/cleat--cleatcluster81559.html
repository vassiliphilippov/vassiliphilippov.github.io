to cleat

verb
fit or supply with cleats
Pronunciation: / klit / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
to provide with cleats or a cleated surface, especially to improve grip, hold, or traction

Examples

to cleat football boots
Before the race, the coach instructed us to cleat our shoes for better traction on the muddy field.
The carpenter will cleat the boards to prevent them from slipping during installation.
They decided to cleat the new soccer shoes for extra grip on wet grass.

  • synonym – stud: projecting part on the sole of a shoe for grip
