strawman

noun
effigy of a man made of straw to scare birds
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɔˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːmæn / (BrE)
An effigy, often roughly in the shape of a human, made of straw and set up to scare birds away from crops.

Examples

field strawman
As we walked by the cornfield, a tattered strawman stood guarding the rows.
The children dressed the strawman in old clothes and a floppy hat.
The farmer placed a strawman in the middle of the field to protect his crops.
