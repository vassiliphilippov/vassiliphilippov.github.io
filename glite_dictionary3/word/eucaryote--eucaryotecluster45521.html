eucaryote

noun
organism with membrane-bound nucleus
Pronunciation: / juːˈkæriˌoʊt / (AmE), / juːˈkærɪəʊt / (BrE)
an organism whose cells have a nucleus enclosed within a membrane, characteristic of all life forms except certain primitive microorganisms such as bacteria

Examples

eucaryote cell structure
All plants and animals are classified as eucaryotes because their cells contain nuclei.
Fungi are eucaryotes and play important roles in ecosystems as decomposers.
Unlike bacteria, eucaryotes have membrane-bound organelles within their cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym – prokaryote: an organism whose cells lack a membrane-bound nucleus
