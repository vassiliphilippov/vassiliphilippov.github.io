pen-tail

noun
Malaysian tree shrew with a fringed tail
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛn ˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɛn ˌteɪl / (BrE)
A small brown tree shrew of Malaysia, notable for its naked tail, which is fringed on both sides with long stiff hairs at the tip.

Examples

a pen-tail climbing trees
Researchers in Malaysia have been studying the nocturnal habits of the pen-tail.
The pen-tail is easily identified by the unique fringe of stiff hairs on its tail tip.
The forest reserve protects rare species, including the elusive pen-tail.
