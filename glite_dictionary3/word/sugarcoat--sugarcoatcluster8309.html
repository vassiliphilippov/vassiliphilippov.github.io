to sugarcoat

verb
make something seem more pleasant than it is
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuɡɚˌkoʊt /; / ˈʃʊɡɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəkəʊt /; / ˈʃʊɡəˌkəʊt / (BrE)
to make something seem better, more pleasant, or less harsh than it really is, especially by softening the truth or omitting negative details

Examples

to sugarcoat bad news
She tried to sugarcoat the bad news so it wouldn't upset her friend.
The report does not sugarcoat the seriousness of the environmental damage.
There is no need to sugarcoat the feedback; honesty is important.
He tends to sugarcoat bad news to avoid upsetting people.
It's important not to sugarcoat anything when giving performance feedback.
Please don't sugarcoat the situation—I need to know the real facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – downplay: to make something appear less important or serious than it really is
  • antonym – exaggerate: to represent something as greater or more serious than it really is
  • synonym – soften the blow: to make something unpleasant seem less severe
  • synonym – sweeten: make something more agreeable; to improve the appeal of something
