to scurry

verb
move quickly with short steps
Pronunciation: / ˈskɜːri / (AmE), / ˈskʌri / (BrE)
to move quickly and with short, hurried steps, often because you are frightened or in a hurry

Examples

to scurry away
She was late, so she scurried down the hallway to her meeting.
Shoppers scurried through the rain, eager to get home before the storm worsened.
The mice scurried across the kitchen floor when the lights were turned on.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dawdle: to move slowly, take one's time
  • synonym – scamper: to run quickly and lightly
