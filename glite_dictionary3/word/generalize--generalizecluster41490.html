to generalize

verb
draw broad conclusions from specific cases
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəˌlaɪz /; / ˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz /; / \u02c8d\u0292\u025bn(\u0259)r\u0259\u02ccla\u026az / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪz /; / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz /; / \u02c8d\u0292\u025bn(\u0259)r\u0259la\u026az / (BrE)
to form a general principle, make a broad statement, or apply learned patterns from specific cases to new situations

Examples

to generalize from facts
It is not always accurate to generalize from a small sample of data.
Scientists must avoid generalizing too quickly based on limited evidence.

  • antonym – overfit: to fit a model too closely to the training data so it performs poorly on new data; opposite of generalize in ML.
