to privilege

verb
grant a special right or advantage
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to grant someone a special right, advantage, or immunity not enjoyed by others

Examples

to privilege certain people
As a student, she was privileged to attend exclusive seminars with the visiting professor.
The committee privileged certain applications over others based on need.
The regulations privilege domestic producers by limiting imports.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discriminate: to treat someone differently, usually in a negative sense
  • synonym – favor: to show approval or preference by treating specially
