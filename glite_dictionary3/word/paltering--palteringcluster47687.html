paltering

noun
deception by vagueness or ambiguity
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.tɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːl.tər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of deceiving by being deliberately vague or using ambiguous language rather than telling an outright lie.

Examples

politician's paltering
His subtle paltering was almost as dishonest as a direct falsehood.
She accused management of paltering rather than giving straightforward answers.
The politician's paltering left the audience uncertain about his true intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – equivocation: the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth
  • synonym – lying: the act of saying something untrue with intent to deceive
