to deflate

verb
reduce value or prices by lessening supply
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to reduce prices, value, or economic activity, especially in an economy

Examples

to deflate prices
A sudden increase in interest rates can deflate asset prices in the housing market.
Central banks sometimes deflate the economy by tightening the money supply.
The government decided to deflate the currency to control runaway inflation.
A sudden drop in consumer demand can deflate an entire market sector.
Rising interest rates are often used to deflate inflation and stabilize prices.
The government hoped the new policies would deflate the overheated economy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inflate: increase the amount or value, especially of money or prices
