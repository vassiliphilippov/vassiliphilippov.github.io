bucksaw

noun
a hand saw in an H-shaped frame for cutting wood
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌsɔː / (BrE)
a hand saw mounted in an H-shaped frame, designed to be used with both hands for cutting wood, typically with the wood held in a sawbuck.

Examples

bucksaw cutting logs
Before chainsaws were common, people relied on a bucksaw for most of their woodcutting.
He used a bucksaw to cut logs into firewood for the winter.
The old bucksaw hung on the wall of the workshop, still sharp despite its age.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bow saw: a type of saw with a thin, narrow blade held in tension by a metal frame
