cross-question

noun
question asked in cross-examination
Pronunciation: / krɔs ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
a question asked to a witness during cross-examination, typically to test the accuracy or credibility of their previous statements

Examples

cross-question in court
Every cross-question was designed to highlight inconsistencies in his story.
She struggled to answer the cross-question posed by the defense attorney.
The lawyer asked a sharp cross-question to challenge the witness's testimony.
