cassie

noun
tropical shrub with fragrant flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsi / (AmE), / ˈkæsi / (BrE)
a tropical American thorny shrub or small tree, particularly Acacia farnesiana, with fragrant yellow flowers used in perfumery.

Examples

cassie flowers perfume
A hedge of cassie lined the garden, blooming with bright yellow flowers in spring.
Locals collect the blossoms of the cassie tree to sell to perfumeries.
The fragrance industry often uses cassie as a key ingredient in luxury perfumes.
