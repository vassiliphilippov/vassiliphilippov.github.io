disconnect

noun
lack of understanding or agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪskəˌnɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəˌnɛkt / (BrE)
a lack of understanding or agreement between people or groups; a significant difference or gap between two things

Examples

disconnect between ideas
Many analysts see a disconnect between stock prices and the real economy.
The disconnect between parents and teenagers can lead to frequent misunderstandings.
There is a clear disconnect between what the company promises and what it delivers.
