pyemia

noun
septicemia from pus-forming bacteria
Pronunciation: / paɪˈiːmiə / (AmE), / paɪˈiːmɪə / (BrE)
A type of septicemia caused by pus-forming bacteria released from an abscess, often leading to the spread of pus through the bloodstream.

Examples

pyemia after infection
Pyemia can result in abscesses forming in other organs if not treated promptly.
In the nineteenth century, pyemia was a common and often fatal postoperative complication.
The patient was diagnosed with pyemia after bacteria from the abscess entered his bloodstream.
