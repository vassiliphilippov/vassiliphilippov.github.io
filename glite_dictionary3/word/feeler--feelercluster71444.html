feeler

noun
sensitivity to subtle signals or information
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːlər / (AmE), / ˈfiːlə / (BrE)
a person’s ability to sense or detect subtle signals or information, similar to a sensory organ; a sensitivity to particular situations or influences

Examples

political feeler
His political feeler tells him that public opinion is shifting.
She seems to have a remarkable feeler for people’s true intentions.
Writers often rely on a well-developed feeler for cultural trends.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insensitivity: lack of sensitivity or awareness to subtle cues
  • synonym – intuition: the ability to understand something instinctively, without conscious reasoning
