to perennate

verb
survive from season to season (of plants)
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛneɪt / (AmE), / pəˈrɛneɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant) to survive from one growing season to the next, usually by means of underground parts such as roots, bulbs, or tubers.

Examples

to perennate underground
After a harsh winter, only the bulbs that perennated successfully will produce new shoots.
Certain cacti can perennate during drought by storing water in their tissues.
Some herbaceous plants perennate by means of underground rhizomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overwinter: (of a plant or animal) live through the winter season
