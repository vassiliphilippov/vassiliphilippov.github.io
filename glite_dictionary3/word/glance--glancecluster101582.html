to glance

verb
to hit at an angle and deflect
Pronunciation: / ɡlæns / (AmE), / ɡlɑːns / (BrE)
(of an object or blow) to strike or hit something at an angle, causing it to deflect or change direction slightly.

Examples

to glance off a wall
Her car glanced a tree but continued down the embankment.
The ball glanced off the edge of the table and rolled onto the floor.
The bullet glanced off his jacket, leaving only a small mark.
