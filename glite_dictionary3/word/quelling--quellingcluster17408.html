quelling

noun
forceful suppression or putting down
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of forcefully putting an end to something, especially by the use of authority or force.

Examples

quelling unrest
Rapid quelling of rumors prevented widespread panic among the public.
The quelling of the uprising required both negotiation and military intervention.
The government ordered the quelling of the protest to restore order in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stifling: the act of restraining or stopping something
  • synonym – suppression: the act of stopping something by force
