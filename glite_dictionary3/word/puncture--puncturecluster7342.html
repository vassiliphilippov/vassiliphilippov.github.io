to puncture

verb
reduce someone's confidence or egotism
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌŋktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
to reduce or weaken someone’s confidence, ego, or sense of importance, especially suddenly or effectively

Examples

to puncture his pride
Her sarcastic remark punctured his sense of self-importance.
The controversy quickly punctured the company's reputation for transparency.
The harsh critique punctured his confidence and made him reconsider his abilities.
