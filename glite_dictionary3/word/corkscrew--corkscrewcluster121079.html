to corkscrew

verb
move in a spiral or zigzag course
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrkˌskru / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkˌskruː / (BrE)
to move or twist in a spiral or zigzag course, like the motion of a corkscrew

Examples

to corkscrew through air
A trail of smoke corkscrewed skyward from the burning building.
She watched as the river corkscrewed its way through the valley below.
The fighter jets corkscrewed through the clouds during their complex maneuver.

Related Terms

  • antonym – straighten: to make or become straight, lacking curves or bends
  • synonym – twist: to move in a winding or spiraling course
