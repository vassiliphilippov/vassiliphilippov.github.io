refractory

adjective
temporarily unresponsive to stimulus
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktəri / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktəri / (BrE)
(of a body, tissue, or organism) temporarily unresponsive or not fully responsive to a stimulus or process, especially after being activated.

Examples

refractory period after stimulus
After firing, a neuron enters a refractory state in which it cannot immediately fire again.
Some cardiac cells are refractory and do not respond to another stimulus until recovery.
The refractory period prevents muscles from being restimulated too soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – responsive: reacting quickly and positively
