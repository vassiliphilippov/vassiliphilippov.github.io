pestis

noun
infectious disease caused by Yersinia pestis
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛstɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪs / (BrE)
A serious (sometimes fatal) infectious disease of rodents caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis, which can be transmitted to humans by the bite of an infected flea.

Examples

bubonic pestis
Doctors identified the disease as pestis after laboratory analysis of blood samples.
Fleas are the main vectors carrying pestis from infected animals to people.
The outbreak of pestis among wild rodents raised concerns about potential transmission to humans.
