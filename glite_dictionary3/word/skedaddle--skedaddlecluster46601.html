skedaddle

noun
a hasty or disorderly escape
Pronunciation: / skəˈdæd.əl / (AmE), / skɪˈdæd.əl / (BrE)
a hurried or disorderly departure or escape, usually in order to avoid trouble or danger

Examples

a quick skedaddle escape
After the alarm sounded, there was a mad skedaddle from the building.
At the first sign of trouble, the crowd made a skedaddle for the exit.
The thief beat a quick skedaddle when the police arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advance: moving forward towards a place, opposite of retreat or skedaddle
  • synonym – flight: the act of fleeing or running away
  • synonym – retreat: an act of withdrawing or escaping suddenly
