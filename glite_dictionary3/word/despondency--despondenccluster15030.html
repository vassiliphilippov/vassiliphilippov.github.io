despondency

noun
a feeling of hopelessness or dejection
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɑndənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒndənsi / (BrE)
a state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage

Examples

deep despondency
A sense of despondency spread throughout the team after their defeat.
He sank into a deep despondency after losing his job.
She struggled to overcome the despondency that followed the end of her relationship.
