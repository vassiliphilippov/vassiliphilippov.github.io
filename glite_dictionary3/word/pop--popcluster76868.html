to pop

verb
make a quick, sharp, explosive noise
Pronunciation: / pɑp / (AmE), / pɒp / (BrE)
to make or cause a short, sharp, explosive sound, to burst, or to open suddenly with such a sound

Examples

to pop a balloon

She loves hearing the corn pop in the microwave when making popcorn.

The hunter popped his rifle twice before the birds scattered.
Bubbles can pop as they bulge under pressure.
Her veins would pop from her forehead whenever she got angry.
When he heard the news, his eyes seemed to pop out of his head.
