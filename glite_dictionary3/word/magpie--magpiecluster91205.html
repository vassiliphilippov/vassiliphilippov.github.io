magpie

noun
person who talks a lot foolishly
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.pʌɪ / (BrE)
a person who talks excessively and foolishly, often in an annoying or trivial way

Examples

talkative magpie person
He’s a real magpie—you can never get a word in edgewise when he’s around.
She tried to focus, but the magpie in the next cubicle kept chattering away.
The meeting was delayed by a magpie who wouldn’t stop telling irrelevant stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blabbermouth: a person who talks excessively or indiscreetly
  • synonym – chatterbox: a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial matters
  • antonym – taciturn: a person who is reserved or says very little
