teaspoon

noun
unit of measure for small amounts
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːˌspun / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌspuːn / (BrE)
A unit of measure equal to the amount that a teaspoon can hold, used especially in cooking or to describe a small quantity of something.

Examples

teaspoon of sugar
Add a teaspoon of sugar to your tea for extra sweetness.
He measured out a teaspoon of salt for the soup.
The recipe calls for one teaspoon of cinnamon.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – pinch: a very small amount, especially of powder or spice
  • degree higher – tablespoon: a larger spoon and unit of measure used in cooking
