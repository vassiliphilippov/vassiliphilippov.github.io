threat

noun
a warning of possible harm or danger
Pronunciation: / θrɛt / (AmE), / θrɛt / (BrE)
a statement or warning that someone may cause harm or something bad may happen

Examples

make a threat
A bomb threat caused the evacuation of the building.
She made a veiled threat to expose his secrets unless he cooperated.
The employees ignored the threat of a pay cut and continued their protest.
He dismissed the threat as an empty gesture, but the police took it seriously.
Making a threat is a serious offense that could lead to legal consequences.
The senator received a threat after announcing her controversial decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym – menace: a person or thing that is likely to cause harm; a threat or danger
  • antonym – promise: an assurance that one will do something or that something will happen (opposite of threat)
  • degree lower – warning: an indication or statement of something impending, often as a caution
