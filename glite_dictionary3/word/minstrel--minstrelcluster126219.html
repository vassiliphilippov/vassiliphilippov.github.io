minstrel

noun
performer in a minstrel show
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnstrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəl / (BrE)
a performer in a traditional minstrel show, typically involving music, comedy, and variety acts, historically associated with blackface performance in 19th- and early 20th-century America.

Examples

minstrel show performer
Historians study the costumes and music used by minstrels on stage.
Many minstrels in the 19th century performed in blackface, a legacy now widely condemned.
The traveling minstrel entertained crowds with comedy and music during the show.
