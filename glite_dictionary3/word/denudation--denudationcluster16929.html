denudation

noun
removal of a covering, especially by erosion
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛn(j)uˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːnjuːˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing a covering, especially the wearing away of the earth's surface by natural agents

Examples

denudation of land
In some diseases, the denudation of the skin leaves it vulnerable to infection.
Rapid denudation by wind and water can dramatically change the landscape over time.
The denudation of the hillside exposed ancient rock layers to view.
