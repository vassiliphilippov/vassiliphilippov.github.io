defence

noun
protection by military action or resources
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
military action, resources, organizations, or structures used to protect a country or area from attack

Examples

national defence system
Modern defence systems are designed to intercept potential threats before they reach the target.
The government increased defence spending to strengthen the country's security.
Thousands volunteered to join the defence forces during the conflict.
After years of neglect, the ancient city's defences had fallen into disrepair.
Modern military bases use both technological and physical defences to ensure security.
The castle's defences were strong enough to withstand the siege for months.
After joining the army, he was assigned to the defence of the northern border.
During the crisis, the defence presented a united front against any incursion.
The country invested heavily in its national defence after recent threats.

Related Terms

  • antonym – attack: an aggressive military action against a place or enemy force
  • synonym – barricade: a temporary structure for blocking passage or defending an area
  • synonym – defense: military action or resources protecting a country (US spelling)
  • synonym – fortification: a defensive wall or reinforcement built to strengthen a place against attack
  • antonym – offense: organization or means of attack rather than defense
