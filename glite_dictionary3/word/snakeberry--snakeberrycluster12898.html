snakeberry

noun
a North American herb with poisonous red berries
Pronunciation: / ˈsneɪkˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkˌbɛri / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb (commonly Actaea rubra) with compound leaves, small white flowers in racemes, and bright red poisonous berries.

Examples

snakeberry plant berries
Children were taught to recognize snakeberry to avoid accidental poisoning.
Hikers are warned not to eat the bright red snakeberries found along the trail, as they are toxic.
The woodland garden was dotted with the white flowers and glossy fruits of the snakeberry plant.
