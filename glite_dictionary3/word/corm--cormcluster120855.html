corm

noun
bulb-like underground plant stem
Pronunciation: / kɔrm / (AmE), / kɔːm / (BrE)
a short, thick, bulb-like underground stem base that stores nutrients and serves as a reproductive structure in certain plants

Examples

plant corm underground
After the plant flowers, the old corm may wither and be replaced by new ones.
Each spring, shoots emerge from the swollen corm just below the soil surface.
The gladiolus reproduces by means of a corm rather than a true bulb.
