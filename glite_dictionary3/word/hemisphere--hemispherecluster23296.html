hemisphere

noun
half of a sphere; often refers to halves of the earth
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
a half of a sphere, especially one of the two halves of the Earth (northern/southern or eastern/western)

Examples

northern hemisphere of Earth
The brain is divided into the left and right hemisphere by a deep groove.
The northern hemisphere experiences winter while the southern hemisphere has summer.
We studied maps showing each hemisphere of the globe separately.
Australia is located in the Southern hemisphere.
Many migratory birds cross from one hemisphere to the other each year.
The equator divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern hemispheres.

Related Terms

  • synonym – equator: the imaginary line dividing the northern and southern hemispheres
  • synonym – globe: the earth as a whole; a spherical planet
