optative

noun
grammatical mood expressing wish or hope
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːp.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(in grammar) a mood, as in Greek or Sanskrit, used to express a wish or hope, often represented in English by modal verbs such as 'may' or 'might'.

Examples

optative mood marker
English does not have a true optative mood, but uses verbs like 'may' or 'might' to express similar meanings.
In Ancient Greek, the optative mood was often used to express hopes or potential actions.
Sanskrit makes use of the optative to convey wishes and possibilities.
