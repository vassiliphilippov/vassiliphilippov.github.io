versicle

noun
short liturgical verse in worship
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːsɪkəl / (BrE)
a short verse recited or sung by a priest, minister, or leader in public worship, usually followed by a congregational response

Examples

versicle in church service
At each versicle, the congregation responded in unison.
During the morning prayer, the minister recited a versicle from the Psalms.
The choir sang the versicle while the congregation joined in the response.

Related Terms

  • synonym – response: a reply or answer, especially the answer given by the congregation in worship after a versicle
