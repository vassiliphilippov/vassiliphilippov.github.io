to agree

verb
be agreeable or suitable (often of food or conditions)
Pronunciation: / əˈɡri / (AmE), / əˈɡriː / (BrE)
(of food, drink, or conditions) to be agreeable, suitable, or not cause adverse effects, especially to someone's health or well-being.

Examples

agree with my stomach
Red wine doesn't agree with him, so he always chooses white.
Spicy food just doesn't agree with me, so I try to avoid it.
This humid weather doesn't agree with people who have asthma.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disagree: to be unsuitable, unpleasant, or cause discomfort
