prophylaxis

noun
prevention of disease
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.fəˈlæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌprɒf.ɪˈlæk.sɪs / (BrE)
action taken to prevent disease, especially by specified means or against a specified disease.

Examples

prophylaxis against infection
Dentists often recommend fluoride treatments as a prophylaxis for tooth decay.
The doctor explained the importance of mosquito nets as malaria prophylaxis in tropical regions.
Vaccination is a common form of prophylaxis against infectious diseases.
