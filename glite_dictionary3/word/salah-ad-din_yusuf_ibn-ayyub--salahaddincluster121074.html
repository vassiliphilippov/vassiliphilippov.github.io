Salah-ad-Din Yusuf ibn-Ayyub

proper noun
sultan of Syria and Egypt, opponent of Crusaders
Pronunciation: / sæˌlɑː əˈdiːn ˈjuːsəf ˌɪbn ɑːˈjuːb / (AmE), / sæˌlæd əˈdiːn ˈjuːsɪf ˌɪbn aɪˈjuːb / (BrE)
(1137–1193) Sultan of Syria and Egypt who recaptured Jerusalem from the Crusaders in 1187 and later opposed Richard the Lionheart during the Third Crusade.

Examples

Salah-ad-Din Yusuf ibn-Ayyub recaptured Jerusalem
Salah-ad-Din Yusuf ibn-Ayyub is remembered as one of the greatest Muslim military leaders of the medieval period.
Many history books highlight the chivalry shown by Salah-ad-Din Yusuf ibn-Ayyub towards his enemies.
The recapture of Jerusalem in 1187 was a major victory for Salah-ad-Din Yusuf ibn-Ayyub during the Crusades.
