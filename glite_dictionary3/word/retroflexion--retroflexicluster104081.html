retroflexion

noun
articulation with tongue turned back
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the action of turning the tip of the tongue back against the roof of the mouth, especially in producing certain speech sounds

Examples

retroflexion of the tongue
Many languages, such as Hindi, include sounds produced by retroflexion of the tongue.
Students of phonetics practice retroflexion to master unfamiliar speech sounds.
The teacher demonstrated retroflexion to help the class understand the difference between 't' and 'ṭ'.

Related Terms

  • synonym – retroflex: (of a sound) articulated with the tongue curled back against the palate
