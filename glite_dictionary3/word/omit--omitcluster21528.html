to omit

verb
to leave out; not include
Pronunciation: / oʊˈmɪt /; / əˈmɪt / (AmE), / əʊˈmɪt /; / əˈmɪt / (BrE)
to leave out or not include someone or something, either intentionally or by accident

Examples

to omit a detail
If you omit any required fields on the form, it will not be processed.
She accidentally omitted his name from the guest list.
The editor decided to omit the final chapter from the published version.
Certain sensitive data was omitted from the official report.
If you omit any details, your application might be rejected.
She decided to omit the final paragraph from her essay.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exclude: to deliberately leave something out
  • antonym – include: to contain as part of the whole
