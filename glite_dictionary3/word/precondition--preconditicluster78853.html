to precondition

verb
prepare in advance; set necessary condition
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to prepare something in advance by putting it into a required condition

Examples

to precondition a system
The software installation script will precondition the environment to meet all requirements.
They preconditioned the building to ensure it was safe for occupancy.
You should precondition the laboratory equipment before starting the experiment.

  • synonym – prepare: to make ready beforehand for a specific purpose
