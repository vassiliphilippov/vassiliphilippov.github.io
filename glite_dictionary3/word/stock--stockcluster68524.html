stock

noun
thickened perennial plant stem
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɒk / (BrE)
the persistent thickened stem of a plant, or a plant/stem onto which a graft is made, providing the root part for grafted plants

Examples

Botanists studied the old stock to learn more about the plant's life cycle.
Many perennial flowers will regrow each year from the same stock.
The gardener carefully trimmed the stock to encourage healthy growth next spring.
Before grafting, it's important to ensure the stock is free from disease.
The stock determines the vigor and hardiness of the entire grafted plant.
The gardener selected a healthy stock to graft the new rose variety onto.
