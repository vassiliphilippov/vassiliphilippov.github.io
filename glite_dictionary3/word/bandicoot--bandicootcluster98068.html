bandicoot

noun
a small Australian marsupial
Pronunciation: / ˈbændɪˌkut / (AmE), / ˈbændɪˌkuːt / (BrE)
a small, agile, ratlike marsupial native to Australia and nearby islands, typically insectivorous or herbivorous

Examples

wild bandicoot
A bandicoot darted across the garden searching for insects under the leaves.
Researchers are working to protect the endangered eastern barred bandicoot from extinction.
Unlike rabbits, bandicoots dig cone-shaped holes to find food.

Related Terms

  • synonym – marsupial: a mammal of the order Marsupialia, most of which carry and suckle their young in a pouch
  • degree lower – rat: a rodent, similar in appearance to which the bandicoot is often compared for its ratlike look
