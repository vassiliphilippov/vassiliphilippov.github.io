blennioid

noun
elongated marine fish (Blennioidei)
Pronunciation: / blɛˈniː.ɔɪd / (AmE), / blɛnˈiː.ɔɪd / (BrE)
a marine fish of the group Blennioidei, typically elongated and mostly scaleless, with large pectoral fins and reduced pelvic fins

Examples

a blennioid fish
Researchers observed that the blennioid used its large pectoral fins to stabilize itself among rocks.
Some blennioids are known for their ability to rapidly dart into crevices when threatened by predators.
The coral reef was home to a variety of species, including the distinctive blennioid with its elongated body.
