pastille

noun
medicated throat lozenge
Pronunciation: / pæˈstɪl / (AmE), / ˈpæstɪl / (BrE)
A small, medicated lozenge dissolved in the mouth to soothe the throat or relieve cough.

Examples

pastille for sore throat
He always keeps a pastille in his pocket in case his throat gets dry.
She sucked on a pastille to ease her sore throat during the meeting.
The pharmacist recommended honey-flavored pastilles for his persistent cough.
