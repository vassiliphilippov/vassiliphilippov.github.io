diploid

noun
organism or cell with two sets of chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of an organism or cell) having two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent.

Examples

a diploid cell
After fertilization, the zygote becomes diploid, containing two sets of genetic material.
Humans are diploid organisms, inheriting a set of chromosomes from each parent.
The scientist examined the diploid cells under a microscope to study their chromosome structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – haploid: having a single set of unpaired chromosomes
