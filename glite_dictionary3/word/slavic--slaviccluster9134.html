Slavic

noun
branch of Indo-European languages
Pronunciation: / ˈslævɪk / (AmE), / ˈslaːvɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Russian, Polish, Czech, Serbian, and related languages

Examples

Slavic languages
Linguists study the evolution of Slavic from Proto-Indo-European roots.
Russian and Polish are both classified as Slavic languages.
The Slavic languages are spoken by millions of people across Eastern Europe.
