Oglala

noun
member of a Teton Sioux Native American group
Pronunciation: / oʊˈɡlɑːlə / (AmE), / əʊˈɡlɑːlə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people who are a division of the Teton Sioux, traditionally inhabiting the Black Hills of western South Dakota.

Examples

an Oglala leader
Many Oglala leaders have contributed to efforts for Native sovereignty and cultural revitalization.
She visited the Oglala community on the Pine Ridge Reservation to learn about their history.
The Oglala played an important role in the preservation of the Lakota language and traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Sioux: a group of Native American peoples including the Oglala, Dakota, and Nakota
