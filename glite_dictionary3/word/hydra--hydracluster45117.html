Hydra

proper noun
mythical many-headed serpent, slain by Hercules
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrə / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a serpent-like monster with nine heads; when one head was cut off, two more grew in its place. It was ultimately slain by Hercules.

Examples

the Hydra monster
In classical art, the hero is often shown locked in fierce combat with the monstrous Hydra.
Many legends tell of Hercules battling the fearsome Hydra in the swamps of Lerna.
The Hydra grew two new heads each time one was cut off, making it a nearly invincible foe.
