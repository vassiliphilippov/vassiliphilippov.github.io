to scan

verb
look over quickly
Pronunciation: / skæn / (AmE), / skæn / (BrE)
to look at or examine something quickly or carefully, often to find information or check for something

Examples

to scan a room
He quickly scanned the room to see if his friends had arrived.
I scan my emails each morning before starting work.
She scanned the newspaper headlines while waiting for the taxi.
Engineers scanned the building's structure to identify potential weaknesses.
She scanned the room, searching for a familiar face in the crowd.
The doctor carefully scanned the patient's chart for any irregularities.
Powerful searchlights scanned the coastline as the fog began to roll in.
Standing on the hilltop, she scanned the crowd for her missing friend.
The security guard scanned the area with his flashlight during his nightly patrol.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inspect: to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it, find problems, etc.
  • degree higher – scrutinize: to examine or inspect closely and thoroughly
  • synonym – skim: to read or glance through quickly, especially in search of something specific
  • synonym – sweep: to move quickly and smoothly in a wide curve, often referring to a search or scan
