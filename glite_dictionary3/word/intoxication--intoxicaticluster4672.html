intoxication

noun
state caused by excessive alcohol consumption
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɑksɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɒksɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A temporary state in which a person's physical and mental control is diminished due to excessive consumption of alcohol.

Examples

alcohol intoxication
Her speech was slurred due to intoxication after the party.
Symptoms of alcohol intoxication include impaired judgment and loss of coordination.
The driver was arrested for operating a vehicle while in a state of intoxication.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inebriation: the state of being drunk; intoxication
  • antonym – sobriety: the state of being sober; not affected by alcohol
