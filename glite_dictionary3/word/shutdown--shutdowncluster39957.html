shutdown

noun
stoppage of business or operations
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌtˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt.daʊn / (BrE)
an occasion when a business, system, or service stops operating, either temporarily or permanently.

Examples

factory shutdown
After the power shutdown, the building was left in total darkness.
The factory shutdown resulted in hundreds of workers losing their jobs.
There was a government shutdown due to a disagreement over the budget.

Related Terms

  • synonym – closure: the act or process of closing something, especially permanently
  • antonym – startup: an instance of beginning operations, especially in business or machinery
