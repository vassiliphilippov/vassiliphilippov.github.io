vaginoscope

noun
medical instrument for examining the vagina and cervix
Pronunciation: / vəˈdʒaɪnəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / vəˈdʒaɪnəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument used for the direct visual examination of the vagina and cervix, typically by magnifying epithelial tissues in vivo.

Examples

use a vaginoscope in exam
New advances in vaginoscope technology have improved the detection of cervical abnormalities.
Proper sterilization of the vaginoscope is essential to prevent infection during examinations.
The gynecologist used a vaginoscope to examine the patient's cervix during the procedure.
