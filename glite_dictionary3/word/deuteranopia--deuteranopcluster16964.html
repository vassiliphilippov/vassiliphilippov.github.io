deuteranopia

noun
color blindness with difficulty distinguishing green and red
Pronunciation: / ˌduːtərəˈnoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtərəˈnəʊpiə / (BrE)
A form of color blindness in which a person has difficulty distinguishing between green and red colors due to lowered sensitivity to green light.

Examples

deuteranopia color blindness
After testing, he was diagnosed with deuteranopia, which affects approximately 1% of males.
People with deuteranopia may have trouble telling the difference between red and green traffic lights.
The genetic mutation responsible for deuteranopia is most commonly inherited from the mother.

Related Terms

  • synonym – color blindness: inability to see colors normally or distinguish between some colors
  • degree higher – protanopia: color blindness characterized by an inability to distinguish red and green, specifically due to deficiency in red receptors
