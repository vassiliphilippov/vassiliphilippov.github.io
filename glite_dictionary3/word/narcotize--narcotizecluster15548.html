to narcotize

verb
to administer narcotics
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːrkəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkəˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to administer a narcotic to someone; to make someone drowsy or insensible with narcotics

Examples

to narcotize a patient
Some substances can narcotize the central nervous system, leading to drowsiness.
Strong sedatives were used to narcotize the animals for transport.
The doctor had to narcotize the patient before performing the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedate: to administer a sedative to produce a calming effect
  • antonym – stimulate: to increase the activity or alertness of someone, opposite of making drowsy
