to grate

verb
to irritate or cause resentment
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant) to irritate or make someone feel resentful or angry over time.

Examples

to grate on nerves
Her colleague's rude remarks began to grate on her nerves.
It really grated on me that he never apologized for his mistake.
The memory of the unfair treatment still grates on him after all these years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fester: (of a feeling) to become worse or more intense, especially through long-term neglect
  • antonym – please: to make someone feel happy or satisfied
  • synonym – rankle: to cause persistent annoyance or resentment
