bowfin

noun
primitive North American freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊ.fɪn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.fɪn / (BrE)
a long-bodied freshwater fish (Amia calva) of North America with a long dorsal fin, regarded as a primitive type of bony fish

Examples

a bowfin fish
Anglers sometimes mistake the bowfin for more common fish like bass or pike.
Despite its fierce appearance, the bowfin poses no threat to humans.
The bowfin is often found in weedy backwaters and slow-moving streams.
