to sequestrate

verb
to isolate or set apart
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkwɛstreɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstreɪt / (BrE)
to set apart or isolate someone or something from others, often by official or formal means

Examples

to sequestrate dangerous materials
During the experiment, the scientist sequestrated the samples to prevent contamination.
In some medical procedures, it is necessary to sequestrate the affected tissue from the surrounding area.
The committee decided to sequestrate the confidential documents for security reasons.
After the chemical spill, authorities sequestrated the contaminated area to protect the public.
During the trial, the judge decided to sequestrate the jury to ensure their impartiality.
He often sequestrates himself in his office when he needs to concentrate on writing.
