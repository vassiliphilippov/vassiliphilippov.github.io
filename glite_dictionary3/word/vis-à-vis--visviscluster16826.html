vis-à-vis

adverb
face to face; opposite
Pronunciation: / ˌviːz.ɑːˈviː / (AmE), / ˌviː.zɑːˈviː / (BrE)
Facing or situated opposite; in a position face to face with.

Examples

chairs placed vis-a-vis
He placed the two chairs vis-à-vis so they could talk easily.
The witness found herself vis-à-vis the accused in the courtroom.
They sat vis-à-vis at the coffee shop, deep in conversation.

  • synonym – opposite: positioned across from something or someone; facing
