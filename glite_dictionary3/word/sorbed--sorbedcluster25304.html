sorbed

adjective
taken into or onto another substance
Pronunciation: / sɔːrbd / (AmE), / sɔːbd / (BrE)
(Of a substance) taken into or onto and retained in another substance, especially by absorption or adsorption.

Examples

sorbed water molecules
Large amounts of water can be sorbed by certain types of clay.
Once sorbed, the dye cannot be easily removed from the fabric.
The researchers measured how much gas was sorbed by the activated carbon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desorbed: released from another substance after being sorbed
