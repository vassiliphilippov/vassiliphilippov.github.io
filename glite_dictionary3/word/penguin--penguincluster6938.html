penguin

noun
flightless Antarctic seabird
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛŋɡwɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛŋɡwɪn / (BrE)
A large, short-legged, flightless seabird native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere, especially Antarctica, with webbed feet and flipper-like wings adapted for swimming.

Examples

penguin waddles on ice
A baby penguin huddled close to its parents to stay warm.
Many documentaries explore how penguins survive in the harsh conditions of Antarctica.
The penguin waddled across the ice towards the water.
