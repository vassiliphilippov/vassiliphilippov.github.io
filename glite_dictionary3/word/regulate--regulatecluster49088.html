to regulate

verb
control by rules or laws
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to control something by means of rules or laws; to make something operate according to a system or standard

Examples

to regulate business activity
It's important to regulate financial markets to prevent abuse and ensure stability.
Schools often regulate students' behavior through a strict code of conduct.
The government plans to regulate the use of pesticides to protect the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – control: to exercise power over something or someone
  • antonym – deregulate: to remove regulations or rules from something
