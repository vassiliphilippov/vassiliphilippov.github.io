to exteriorize

verb
make external or outwardly visible
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪriəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriəraɪz / (BrE)
to make something external, objective, or visible; to express or represent an idea, feeling, or quality in an outward or observable form

Examples

to exteriorize feelings
Language allows us to exteriorize our private thoughts and share them with others.
The artist sought to exteriorize her emotions through her paintings.
Therapists encourage patients to exteriorize their feelings rather than suppress them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – internalize: to make something internal or subjective
