larvacea

noun
small free-swimming tunicate
Pronunciation: / lɑːrˈveɪʃiə / (AmE), / lɑːˈveɪsɪə / (BrE)
A small, free-swimming marine animal belonging to a group of tunicates, sometimes classified as an order.

Examples

marine larvacea
Marine biologists study larvacea to understand how these tunicates filter food particles from seawater.
Some species of larvacea build mucous 'houses' that help them trap and eat plankton.
The transparent body of the larvacea makes it difficult to spot in open ocean research.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appendicularian: another name for larvacea, a free-swimming tunicate
  • degree higher – tunicate: a marine animal with a sac-like body, part of the same subphylum as larvacea
