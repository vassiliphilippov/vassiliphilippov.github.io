aphanitic

adjective
fine-grained igneous rock texture
Pronunciation: / ˌæfəˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌafəˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of an igneous rock) having a fine-grained texture where the individual mineral crystals are too small to be seen without a microscope.

Examples

aphanitic volcanic rock
Geologists classified the specimen as aphanitic, distinguishing it from coarse-grained granite.
Most volcanic rocks are aphanitic due to their rapid cooling at or near the Earth's surface.
The basalt sample is described as aphanitic because its crystals are too small to identify with the naked eye.

Related Terms

  • antonym – phaneritic: coarse-grained igneous rock texture, crystals visible to naked eye
