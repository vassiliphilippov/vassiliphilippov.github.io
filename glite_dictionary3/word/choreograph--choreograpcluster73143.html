to choreograph

verb
compose and arrange dance steps
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔriəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈkɒrɪəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to compose and arrange the movements and steps for a dance, especially for a performance or production

Examples

to choreograph a dance
He spent weeks trying to choreograph a waltz for the wedding scene.
The director asked her to choreograph the opening number for the show.
They plan to choreograph the dance routine to match the upbeat music.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compose: to create or put together music, poetry, or other works
  • antonym – improvise: to perform or create spontaneously without preparation
