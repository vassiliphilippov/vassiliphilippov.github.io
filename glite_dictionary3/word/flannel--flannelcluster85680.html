flannel

noun
trousers made of flannel fabric (British)
Pronunciation: / ˈflænəl / (AmE), / ˈflænəl / (BrE)
(British, usually plural) Trousers made of flannel or similar fabric, such as gabardine or tweed; often refers to light-colored formal or sports trousers.

Examples

grey flannels
College students once considered a blazer and flannels appropriate campus attire.
He wore a crisp white shirt with grey flannels to the garden party.
The golf club required all members to wear flannels on the course.
