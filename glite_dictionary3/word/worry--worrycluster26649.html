to worry

verb
bite and tear at persistently (of animal)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜri / (AmE), / ˈwʌri / (BrE)
(of an animal) to bite and tear at something repeatedly, especially with the teeth.

Examples

to worry a bone
Do not let the dog worry the fence, or it might get hurt.
The terrier began to worry the old shoe with its sharp teeth.
The wolf would worry its prey before dragging it away.
