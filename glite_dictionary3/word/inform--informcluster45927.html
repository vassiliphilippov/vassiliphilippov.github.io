to inform

verb
secretly give information about someone
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to secretly give information about someone, often to authorities, especially about wrongdoing or criminal activity

Examples

to inform the police
After being caught, he decided to inform on his accomplices to avoid a harsher sentence.
She was afraid that someone might inform on her to the police.
Their neighbor informed on them, leading to their arrest.

  • synonym – snitch: to inform on someone in a secretive or disloyal way
