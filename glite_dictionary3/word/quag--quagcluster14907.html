quag

noun
soft, wet low-lying land; bog
Pronunciation: / kwæɡ / (AmE), / kwæɡ / (BrE)
A soft, wet area of low-lying land that yields or sinks underfoot; a bog or swamp.

Examples

muddy quag
After the heavy rain, the trail turned into a slippery quag.
The cattle avoided the quag, knowing they could get stuck in the mud.
They used planks to cross the treacherous quag safely.
