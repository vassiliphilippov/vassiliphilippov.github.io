defence

noun
protection from harm or danger
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
the act of protecting someone or something from attack, danger, harm, or illness

Examples

defence against illness
Regular exercise is an important defence against illness.
She spoke in defence of her colleague during the meeting.
The walls were built as a defence against invaders.
The army organized a strong defence against the invading forces.
The city built flood barriers as a defence against rising water levels.
Vaccination provides an important defence against many diseases.

Related Terms

  • antonym – attack: an aggressive action against someone or something
  • synonym – defense: protection from harm (American spelling)
