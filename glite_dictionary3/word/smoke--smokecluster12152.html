to smoke

verb
emit a cloud of smoke
Pronunciation: / smoʊk / (AmE), / sməʊk / (BrE)
(of something such as a chimney, volcano, or engine) to emit a cloud of smoke or fine particles.

Examples

chimneys smoke daily
The factory chimneys smoke every morning at sunrise.
The old diesel bus smoked heavily as it climbed the hill.
You could see the volcano smoking in the distance after the eruption.

  • synonym – fume: emit gas, smoke, or vapor
