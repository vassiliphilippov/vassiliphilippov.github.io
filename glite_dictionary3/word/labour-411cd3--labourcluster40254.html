labour

noun
the working class; wage earners
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
Workers, especially those who do manual work or work for wages, regarded as a social class.

Examples

labour force
His book examines the history of labour movements in Europe.
The factory was forced to close due to a shortage of skilled labour.
There are ongoing debates about the rights of labour in many countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – working class: the class of people employed for wages, especially manual or industrial work
