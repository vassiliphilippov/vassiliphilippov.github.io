dybbuk

noun
possessing demon in Jewish folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪbək / (AmE), / ˈdɪbʊk / (BrE)
(in Jewish folklore) a malevolent spirit or demon that enters and possesses the body of a living person, controlling their behavior until exorcised.

Examples

possessed by a dybbuk
In the old story, a dybbuk haunted the village until it was exorcised by a rabbi.
Legends say that a dybbuk can only be removed through a complex ritual.
The film explores the terrifying idea of a dybbuk taking control of an unsuspecting victim.
