to cascade

verb
to flow or fall in large quantities
Pronunciation: / kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kæˈskeɪd / (BrE)
(of liquid, objects, or information) to flow or fall quickly and in large amounts, like a waterfall.

Examples

to cascade down stairs
As the fire alarm sounded, people cascaded down the stairwell to reach safety.
Her long hair cascaded down her back in waves.
The water began to cascade over the rocks after the heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pour: to flow rapidly in a steady stream
  • degree lower – trickle: to flow in a small stream or in drops
