orbiter

noun
spacecraft in orbit around a celestial body
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪtə / (BrE)
a spacecraft designed to orbit around a planet, moon, or other celestial body, especially one built by humans to study or explore it

Examples

space orbiter
NASA launched a new orbiter to study the atmosphere of Venus.
Scientists analyzed data collected by the lunar orbiter.
The Mars orbiter sent back detailed images of the planet's surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lander: a spacecraft designed to land on a planet or moon
