volcanic

adjective
formed from erupted magma (rock)
Pronunciation: / vəˈkænɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk / (BrE)
(of rock) relating to or formed from magma erupted onto or near the earth's surface, as in basalt, andesite, or rhyolite.

Examples

volcanic rock
Geologists collected samples of volcanic basalt from the mountain slope.
Obsidian is a type of volcanic glass often used for tools and ornaments.
The island is largely composed of volcanic rock formed by ancient eruptions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – igneous: (of rocks) formed by solidification of molten magma
  • antonym – plutonic: (of rock) formed by solidification of magma deep underground
