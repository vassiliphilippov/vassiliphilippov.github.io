testament

noun
strong evidence or proof
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
something that shows, proves, or is strong evidence of a particular fact, quality, or belief

Examples

testament to her skill
Her success is a testament to her dedication and hard work.
The museum itself stands as a testament to the city’s rich history.
The restored painting is a testament to the skill of the conservators.
His autobiography serves as a testament to his unwavering integrity.
The speech was a powerful testament of her commitment to social justice.
They published a testament outlining the group's founding principles.

  • synonym – manifesto: a public declaration of policy and aims
