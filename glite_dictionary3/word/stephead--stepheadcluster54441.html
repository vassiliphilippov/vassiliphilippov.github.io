stephead

noun
headline with lines increasingly indented
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛpˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈstɛpˌhɛd / (BrE)
A headline in which the first line is aligned on the left, and each succeeding line is indented further to the right.

Examples

stephead headline layout
Graphic designers often choose a stephead layout for complex headlines.
The stephead format helped readers follow the multi-part headline easily.
The magazine used a stephead to introduce the feature article, making it stand out on the page.
