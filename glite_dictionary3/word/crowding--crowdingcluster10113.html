crowding

noun
condition of being excessively crowded
Pronunciation: / ˈkraʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
A situation in which a large number of people or things are gathered together in a space, making it feel cramped or congested.

Examples

classroom crowding
Excessive crowding in classrooms can affect students' ability to learn.
She avoided shopping malls because the crowding made her feel uncomfortable.
The crowding on public transportation during rush hour can be overwhelming.
