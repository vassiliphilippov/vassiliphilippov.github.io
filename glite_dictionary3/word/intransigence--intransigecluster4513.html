intransigence

noun
refusal to compromise; stubbornness
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrænsədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænzɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unwilling to compromise or change one's views; stubborn refusal to agree

Examples

political intransigence
Despite growing pressure, his intransigence signaled that he was unwilling to compromise.
Her intransigence during the negotiations made it impossible to reach an agreement.
The committee's intransigence was criticized by both sides of the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stubbornness: the quality of being determined not to change one's attitude or position
