fundamentalism

noun
strict adherence to basic principles, esp. in religion
Pronunciation: / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
strict adherence to the basic principles or doctrines of a religion, ideology, or system, often characterized by a literal interpretation of foundational texts or beliefs.

Examples

religious fundamentalism
Not all religious groups agree with the principles of fundamentalism.
Some people fear that fundamentalism leads to intolerance of other beliefs.
The rise of fundamentalism has influenced politics in several countries.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – extremism: the holding of extreme political or religious views
  • synonym – orthodoxy: generally accepted or traditional beliefs, especially in religion
