musculus sphincter pupillae

noun
muscle that constricts the pupil
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs sfɪŋkˈtɚ pjuːˈpɪli.i / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs sfɪŋ(k)tə pjuːˈpɪli.iː / (BrE)
a ring of smooth muscle in the eye surrounding the iris, responsible for constricting the pupil

Examples

musculus sphincter pupillae controls pupil
Certain medications affect the musculus sphincter pupillae, leading to changes in pupil diameter.
Damage to the musculus sphincter pupillae can cause abnormal pupil responses.
The musculus sphincter pupillae contracts in bright light to reduce the size of the pupil.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dilator pupillae: muscle that dilates the pupil of the eye
