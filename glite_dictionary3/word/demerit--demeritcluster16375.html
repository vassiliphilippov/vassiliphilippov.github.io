demerit

noun
mark for misconduct or failure
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɛrɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛrɪt / (BrE)
a mark recorded against a person, especially in a school or the armed forces, for a fault or misconduct

Examples

receive a demerit
He was careful to follow the rules to avoid getting another demerit on his record.
If a student receives three demerits in one week, she must attend detention on Friday.
The soldier's repeated demerits eventually led to his suspension from duty.

Related Terms

  • antonym – merit: a mark given for achievement or a good action
