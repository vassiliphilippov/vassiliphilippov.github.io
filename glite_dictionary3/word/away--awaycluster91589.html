away

adverb
at a distance in space or time
Pronunciation: / əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
at or to a distance from a place, or at a time in the future or past

Examples

live away from home
She lives just a few blocks away from the school.
That memory feels like it happened ages away in my childhood.
The concert is still three days away, so we have plenty of time to prepare.
After graduation, she moved away to another city.
Please keep away from the construction site for your safety.
The dog ran away as soon as it saw the stranger.
