Parashurama

proper noun
a Hindu avatar of Vishnu
Pronunciation: / pɑːrəʃʊˈrɑːmə / (AmE), / pəːrəʃʊˈrɑːmə / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the sixth avatar of Vishnu, known for ridding the world of Kshatriyas.

Examples

Parashurama the warrior sage
Parashurama is revered in many Hindu traditions as the warrior sage who wielded an axe.
According to legend, Parashurama eradicated the Kshatriya class from the earth several times.
The temple dedicated to Parashurama attracts thousands of devotees during religious festivals.
