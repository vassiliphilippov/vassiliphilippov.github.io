slavey

noun
female domestic servant (dated)
Pronunciation: / ˈsleɪvi / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvi / (BrE)
(dated) A female domestic servant who does menial or drudging work, often for low pay or with little appreciation.

Examples

household slavey
In Victorian novels, a slavey was often portrayed as the lowest in the household hierarchy.
The hotel employed a slavey to clean the rooms and carry guests' luggage.
The young slavey worked from dawn till dusk with barely a moment's rest.
