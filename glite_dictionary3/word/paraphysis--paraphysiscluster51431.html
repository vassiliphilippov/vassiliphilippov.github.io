paraphysis

noun
sterile filament among sporangia (biology)
Pronunciation: / pəˈræfəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈræfɪsɪs / (BrE)
A sterile, simple or branched filament or hair occurring among sporangia, often pointed or club-shaped, found especially in fungi and certain algae.

Examples

paraphysis in fungi
A biologist counted numerous paraphyses extending from the surface of the ascoma.
In some algae, paraphyses help to support the developing reproductive organs.
The paraphyses are visible under the microscope as hair-like structures among the sporangia of the fungus.
