heritor

noun
person entitled to inherit property
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛr.ɪ.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.ɪ.tɔː / (BrE)
a person who is legally entitled to receive property or assets from another person, usually through a will or by law

Examples

heritor of an estate
After his uncle's passing, James became the sole heritor of the vast estate.
As the only child, Emma was the primary heritor under the terms of the will.
The will named several heritors who would receive portions of the family property.

Related Terms

  • synonym – heir: someone who inherits property or a title
  • synonym – legatee: a person who receives a legacy through a will
