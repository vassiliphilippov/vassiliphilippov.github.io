urobilinogen

noun
chemical from bilirubin breakdown in the intestines
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊroʊbɪˈlɪnoʊdʒən / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊbɪˈlɪnədʒən / (BrE)
a colorless compound formed in the intestine from the breakdown of bilirubin, some of which is excreted in feces while the rest is reabsorbed and excreted in bile or urine

Examples

urobilinogen in urine
A high level of urobilinogen may suggest hemolytic disease or liver disorders.
After the breakdown of bilirubin, urobilinogen is partly reabsorbed and excreted in urine.
The presence of urobilinogen in urine can be an indicator of liver function.

Related Terms

  • synonym – urobilin: a pigment formed by the oxidation of urobilinogen
