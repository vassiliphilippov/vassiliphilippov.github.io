to wreck

verb
to destroy or damage severely
Pronunciation: / rɛk / (AmE), / rɛk / (BrE)
to seriously damage or destroy something, especially by smashing or breaking it forcefully

Examples

to wreck a car
Careless driving can easily wreck a brand-new car.
He accidentally wrecked his bike by riding into a tree.
The storm wrecked hundreds of homes along the coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym – destroy: to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • antonym – repair: to fix or mend something that is damaged or broken
  • synonym – ruin: to harm something so badly that it loses all value, enjoyment, or usefulness
