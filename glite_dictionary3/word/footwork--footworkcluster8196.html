footwork

noun
skillful maneuvering or dealing
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈfʊtwɜːk / (BrE)
the ability to handle a situation or problem skillfully and with flexibility, especially in avoiding difficulty or achieving a goal

Examples

clever footwork
Her quick footwork in negotiations helped the company avoid a costly lawsuit.
Managing the different interests at the meeting required some agile footwork on his part.
To succeed in politics, you need excellent footwork when dealing with rivals and controversy.
