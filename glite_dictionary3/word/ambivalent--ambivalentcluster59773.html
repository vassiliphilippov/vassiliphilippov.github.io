ambivalent

adjective
having mixed or conflicting feelings
Pronunciation: / æmˈbɪvələnt / (AmE), / æmˈbɪvələnt / (BrE)
having mixed or contradictory feelings about someone or something; uncertain or unable to choose between two opposing courses of action

Examples

ambivalent feelings
His ambivalent attitude towards the project made decision-making difficult.
Many people have ambivalent feelings about technology and its impact on society.
She felt ambivalent about moving to a new city for her job.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decisive: able to make decisions quickly and confidently
  • synonym – uncertain: not sure or confident about something
