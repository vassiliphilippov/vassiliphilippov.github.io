lee

noun
sheltered side from the wind
Pronunciation: / liː / (AmE), / liː / (BrE)
the side or area of something that is sheltered from the wind

Examples

lee of a hill
Cows gathered in the lee of the barn during the storm.
The hikers set up their camp in the lee of a large rock.
We anchored the boat on the lee of the island to avoid the strong winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym – windward: the side from which the wind is blowing
