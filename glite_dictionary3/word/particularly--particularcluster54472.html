particularly

adverb
especially; more than others
Pronunciation: / pərˈtɪkjələrli / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊləli / (BrE)
especially; more than usual or more than others; in a way that emphasizes something as special or specific

Examples

particularly interested in music
I wasn't particularly interested in going out tonight, but I agreed anyway.
She enjoys classical music, particularly the works of Mozart and Beethoven.
This region has been particularly affected by the recent storms.
I am particularly interested in modern art and sculpture.
She was particularly proud of her latest achievement.
The weather in June is particularly unpredictable in this region.
