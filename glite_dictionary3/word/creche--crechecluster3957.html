creche

noun
institution for foundlings
Pronunciation: / kreɪʃ / (AmE), / kreɪʃ / (BrE)
a hospital or institution where foundlings (infant children of unknown parents) are taken in and cared for

Examples

creche for babies
Historically, a creche was established to provide shelter for foundlings in large cities.
The abandoned newborn was brought to the local creche where specialists could care for her.
The nuns worked tirelessly at the creche to ensure every child received food and comfort.
