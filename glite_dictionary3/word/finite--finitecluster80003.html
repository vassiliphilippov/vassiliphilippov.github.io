finite

adjective
marked by tense, person, and number (grammar)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪ.naɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.naɪt / (BrE)
(Of a verb form) marked by tense, person, and number; limited by features such as tense and agreement.

Examples

finite verb form
In the sentence 'She walks home,' 'walks' is a finite verb form.
Only finite verbs can function as the main verb of a sentence in English.
The distinction between finite and nonfinite verbs is important in syntactic analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonfinite: (of a verb) not limited by tense, person, or number
