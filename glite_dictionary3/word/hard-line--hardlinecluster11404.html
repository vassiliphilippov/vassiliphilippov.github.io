hard-line

adjective
firm and uncompromising
Pronunciation: / hɑrd laɪn / (AmE), / hɑːd laɪn / (BrE)
not willing to change one's opinions or policies; firm and uncompromising, especially in politics or negotiations.

Examples

hard-line stance on policy
She is known for her hard-line approach to discipline.
The government adopted a hard-line stance on immigration reform.
The party's hard-line members opposed any form of negotiation with the opposition.
