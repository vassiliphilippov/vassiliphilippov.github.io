nervus ulnaris

noun
ulnar nerve of the arm
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs ʌlˈnɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ʌlˈnɛərɪs / (BrE)
the nerve running along the inner side of the arm and passing near the elbow, supplying muscles and skin on the medial side of the hand; also called the ulnar nerve

Examples

nervus ulnaris injury
Compression of the nervus ulnaris is often responsible for the symptoms of cubital tunnel syndrome.
Injury to the nervus ulnaris can cause numbness in the ring and little fingers.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the nervus ulnaris during elbow operations.

  • synonym – ulnar nerve: the nerve that runs along the ulna, inner side of the arm
