to whip

verb
criticize harshly; scold severely
Pronunciation: / wɪp / (AmE), / wɪp / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something harshly and forcefully, often in public

Examples

to whip the team
After the poor performance, the coach publicly whipped the team during the press conference.
During the debate, she whipped her opponent's policies with sharp criticism.
The editor did not hesitate to whip the author for factual errors in the article.

Related Terms

  • antonym – praise: express approval or admiration
  • synonym – scold: reprimand or criticize angrily
