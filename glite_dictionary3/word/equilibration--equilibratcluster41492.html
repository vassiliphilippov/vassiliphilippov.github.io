equilibration

noun
process of reaching equilibrium
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkwɪləˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌkwɪlɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something or becoming stable by bringing it into a state of equilibrium

Examples

chemical equilibration
Proper equilibration is essential before conducting chemical experiments to ensure consistent results.
The equilibration of temperature between the two rooms occurred within an hour.
The scientist waited for equilibration before taking the final measurement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – balance: a state where different things are equal or in the correct proportions
  • antonym – disequilibrium: a loss or lack of equilibrium or stability
