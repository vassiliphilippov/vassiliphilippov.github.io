to overdrive

verb
to drive or work too hard
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈdraɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdraɪv / (BrE)
to make someone or something work too hard or use something too much; to push beyond normal limits

Examples

to overdrive a machine
If you overdrive your employees, their productivity may actually decrease.
She tends to overdrive herself and ends up exhausted at the end of the week.
The manager was accused of overdriving his team to meet unrealistic deadlines.
He tends to overdrive his point in arguments, repeating the same facts unnecessarily.
If you overdrive your employees, they may become fatigued and less productive.
The company overdrove its marketing strategy, irritating potential customers with too many advertisements.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overuse: to use something too much
  • synonym – overwork: to work too hard or cause someone to work too hard
  • antonym – underuse: to use something too little or infrequently
