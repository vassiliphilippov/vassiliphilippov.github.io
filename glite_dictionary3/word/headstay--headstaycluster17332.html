headstay

noun
rope or cable supporting a mast from the front
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdsteɪ / (BrE)
a strong rope or cable running from the bow or bowsprit of a vessel to the top of the mast, supporting the mast and often used to attach the foremost sail.

Examples

headstay supports mast
He checked the headstay before hoisting the jib.
Tensioning the headstay can improve the boat’s upwind performance.
The yacht's headstay needed to be replaced after the rough crossing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forestay: a stay leading forward and down to support the mast, similar to or including the headstay
