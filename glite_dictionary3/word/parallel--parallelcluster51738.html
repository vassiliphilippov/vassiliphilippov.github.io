to parallel

verb
place in same direction as something else
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl / (BrE)
to make or place something so that it runs in the same direction as, and is equally distant from, another line or object

Examples

to parallel a road
The design required the wires to be paralleled closely along the metal frame.
The engineers paralleled the new road to the old railway line to minimize disruptions.
The river parallels the highway for several miles before turning south.

Related Terms

  • synonym – align: to arrange in a straight line or in correct relative positions
