reconciliation

noun
bringing accounts or records into agreement
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛkənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkənsɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making two sets of figures, records, or accounts consistent or bringing them into agreement

Examples

reconciliation of accounts
After a mistake was found, the reconciliation between the invoices and payments took several days.
Bank reconciliation is a crucial step in ensuring accurate bookkeeping for any business.
The accountant spent the afternoon working on the reconciliation of the two financial reports.

Related Terms

  • synonym – balancing: the act of making sure financial records match
  • antonym – discrepancy: a lack of compatibility or similarity between two facts or figures
