arteria poplitea

noun
artery behind the knee supplying lower leg and foot
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə pɑːˈplɪtiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə pɒˈplɪtɪə / (BrE)
the continuation of the femoral artery located behind the knee, which branches to supply blood to the legs and feet

Examples

arteria poplitea behind knee
An injury to the arteria poplitea can compromise blood flow to the lower leg.
The surgeon carefully exposed the arteria poplitea during the bypass procedure.
Ultrasound imaging was used to assess the condition of the arteria poplitea.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – femoral artery: major artery supplying blood to the thigh and leg
  • degree lower – tibial artery: artery branching from the popliteal artery to supply the lower leg
