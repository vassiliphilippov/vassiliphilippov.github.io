to outflank

verb
go around the side of an opponent (military)
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈflæŋk / (AmE), / aʊtˈflæŋk / (BrE)
to move around the side of an opposing force, especially to gain a tactical advantage in battle

Examples

to outflank enemy troops
In the last battle, their cavalry managed to outflank us and attack from behind.
Modern armies often seek to outflank their opponents to gain a strategic advantage.
The general tried to outflank the enemy forces by sending troops to the east.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encircle: to surround completely, often for tactical advantage
  • synonym – outmaneuver: to gain an advantage over an opponent by skillful maneuvering
