vesicula umbilicus

noun
embryonic structure for early circulation
Pronunciation: / vɛˈsɪkjələ ʌmˈbɪlɪkəs / (AmE), / vɛˈsɪkjʊlə ʌmˈbɪlɪkəs / (BrE)
A membranous structure in mammalian embryos that temporarily serves as the circulatory system until the heart becomes functional.

Examples

vesicula umbilicus in embryos
Defects in the vesicula umbilicus can disrupt embryonic blood circulation before the heart forms.
Researchers observed changes in the vesicula umbilicus as the embryo's heart began to function.
The vesicula umbilicus plays a vital role in the early development of mammalian embryos.
