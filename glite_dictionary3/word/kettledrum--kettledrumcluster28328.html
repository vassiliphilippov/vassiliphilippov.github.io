kettledrum

noun
large tunable orchestral drum
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛtlˌdrʌm / (AmE), / ˈkɛtldrʌm / (BrE)
A large hemispherical drum, usually made of brass or copper, with a tunable drumhead that produces a definite pitch, commonly used in orchestras.

Examples

orchestral kettledrum
Each kettledrum in the orchestra is tuned to a specific note for the performance.
He marveled at the rich, deep sound of the kettledrum during the concert.
The percussionist struck the kettledrum with precision during the symphony's dramatic crescendo.

  • synonym – timpani: a set of kettledrums played in an orchestra
