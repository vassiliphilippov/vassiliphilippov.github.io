small-capitalization

adjective
stocks of companies with small market cap
Pronunciation: / smɔl ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / smɔːl ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(of stocks) relating to companies with a market capitalization of less than one billion dollars.

Examples

small-capitalization stocks
During periods of economic recovery, small-capitalization shares often outperform larger firms.
Many investors are attracted to small-capitalization stocks for their growth potential.
This fund primarily invests in small-capitalization companies across various sectors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – large-capitalization: of stocks of companies with a large market capitalization, typically over ten billion dollars
  • degree higher – mid-capitalization: of stocks of companies with a market capitalization between small-cap and large-cap
