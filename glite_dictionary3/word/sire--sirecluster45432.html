sire

noun
formal address for a man of rank (archaic)
Pronunciation: / saɪr / (AmE), / saɪə / (BrE)
a formal title of address for a man of rank and authority, especially a king; now archaic

Examples

address the king as sire
In medieval times, people would address their monarch as sire.
“Sire, your loyal subjects await your command,” the advisor said to the king.
“Yes, sire, I shall do as you wish,” the knight replied respectfully.
