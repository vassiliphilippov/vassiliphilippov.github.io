to diverge

verb
separate and go in different directions
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɜrdʒ /; / daɪˈvɝrdʒ /; / daɪˈvɝːdʒ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to separate and go in a different direction from a common point, or to become different, especially in opinion, development, or approach

Examples

to diverge in opinion
As they grew older, their career paths gradually diverged.
At this junction, the two roads diverge and head in opposite directions.
The river diverges into several smaller streams just outside the town.
After years of similar research, their scientific approaches began to diverge significantly.
At the old oak tree, the two trails diverge and lead to different parts of the forest.
Tastes in music often diverge as people grow older.
As society changes, traditional values may diverge from modern expectations.
Our views on education policy diverge significantly from theirs.
The two scientists began to diverge in their interpretation of the data.
