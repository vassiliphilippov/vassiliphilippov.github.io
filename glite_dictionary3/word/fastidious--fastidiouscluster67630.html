fastidious

adjective
requiring special nutritional conditions (biology)
Pronunciation: / fæˈstɪdiəs / (AmE), / fæˈstɪdɪəs / (BrE)
(of a microorganism or plant) having complex and specific nutritional requirements, especially growing only under special conditions in artificial cultures.

Examples

fastidious bacteria growth
Because the pathogen is fastidious, only specialized lab techniques can successfully culture it.
Some bacteria are fastidious and require enriched media to grow in the laboratory.
The fastidious nature of these plants makes them challenging to cultivate outside their native habitat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exacting: making great demands on skill, attention, or resources
