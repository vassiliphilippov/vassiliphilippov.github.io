combination

noun
alliance formed for a specific purpose
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmbəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an alliance of people, corporations, or countries formed for a particular purpose, especially political or economic objectives

Examples

business combination
Historically, some combinations were created to limit competition in certain industries.
Several companies formed a combination to lobby for new trade regulations.
The European nations entered into a combination to promote economic stability.
