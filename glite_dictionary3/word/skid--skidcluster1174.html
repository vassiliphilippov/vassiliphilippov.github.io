skid

noun
an unintentional sliding movement (esp. of vehicles)
Pronunciation: / skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
an instance of sliding unintentionally, typically by a vehicle losing traction on a slippery surface

Examples

a skid on a road
A sudden skid caused the cyclist to fall off his bike.
Experienced drivers know how to react if their vehicle goes into a skid during rain.
The car went into a skid on the icy road, but the driver regained control.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slide: an act of moving smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it
