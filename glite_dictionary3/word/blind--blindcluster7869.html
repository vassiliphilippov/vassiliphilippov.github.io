blind

adjective
not based on reason or evidence
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd / (AmE), / blaɪnd / (BrE)
not noticing, understanding, or willing to see something; lacking awareness or acting without reason (figurative)

Examples

blind faith
Despite all the evidence, he had blind faith in his leader.
Her blind hatred prevented her from seeing the truth.
The crowd's blind panic led to chaos and confusion.
Despite repeated warnings, he remained blind to the company’s financial problems.
Many people are blind to their own biases, even when they affect important decisions.
She was blind to the risks involved in the plan because of her enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – aware: having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • synonym – ignorant: lacking knowledge or awareness in general
  • synonym – irrational: not logical or reasonable
