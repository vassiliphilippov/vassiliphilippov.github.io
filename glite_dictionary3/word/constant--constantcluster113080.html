constant

adjective
unchanging; remaining the same
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
remaining the same over a period of time; unchanging or unvarying in nature, quality, or value.

Examples

constant temperature
Her dedication to her job remained constant despite changing circumstances.
The machine operates at a constant speed for hours on end.
We need to keep the temperature constant throughout the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – steady: firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • antonym – variable: likely to change or vary; not constant
