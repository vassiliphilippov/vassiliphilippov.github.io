to lyophilise

verb
to dry by freeze-drying (usually in science/medicine)
Pronunciation: / laɪˈɑːfəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / laɪˈɒfɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to dry (a substance such as blood, serum, or tissue) by freezing it and then reducing the surrounding pressure to remove the frozen water by sublimation; to freeze-dry.

Examples

to lyophilise a vaccine
After collection, the vaccine was lyophilised to extend its shelf life and ease transportation.
Researchers often lyophilise tissue specimens to ensure long-term stability and prevent degradation.
The laboratory staff carefully lyophilised the blood samples to preserve them for analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – freeze-dry: to dry by freezing and then reducing pressure to remove moisture
