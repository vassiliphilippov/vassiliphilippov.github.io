vena gastrica-dextra

noun
vein of the stomach draining to the portal vein
Pronunciation: / ˈviː.nə ɡæˈstrɪ.kə ˈdɛk.strə / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ɡæˈstrɪ.kə ˈdɛk.strə / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the upper surfaces of the stomach and empties into the portal vein.

Examples

vena gastrica-dextra drains stomach
Blockage in the vena gastrica-dextra can contribute to complications in the portal system.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully ligated the vena gastrica-dextra to prevent bleeding.
The vena gastrica-dextra plays a crucial role in draining blood from the upper stomach.
