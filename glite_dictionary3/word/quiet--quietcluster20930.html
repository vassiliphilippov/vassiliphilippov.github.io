to quiet

verb
make calm or still
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪət / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət / (BrE)
to make or become calm, still, or less noisy

Examples

to quiet a child
He tried to quiet the crying baby by singing a lullaby.
She took a deep breath to quiet her anxious thoughts before the speech.
The teacher raised her hand, hoping to quiet the noisy classroom.
As the night progressed, the noisy party finally quieted.
The engine gradually quieted after the repairs were done.
When the teacher walked in, the students quickly quieted down.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agitate: to make troubled or nervous
  • synonym – calm: to make quiet or tranquil
  • antonym – noisy: making a lot of noise
  • synonym – silence: to make or become silent
  • synonym – soothe: to gently calm or relieve agitation
