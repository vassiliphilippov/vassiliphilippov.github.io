mediateness

noun
quality of being mediate
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiətnəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiətnɪs / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being mediate; the state of being in an intermediate position or acting as a means.

Examples

the mediateness of communication
In theology, the mediateness of revelation is a significant topic of debate.
Some critics argue about the mediateness inherent in all forms of communication.
The philosopher discussed the mediateness of human perception in her lecture.
