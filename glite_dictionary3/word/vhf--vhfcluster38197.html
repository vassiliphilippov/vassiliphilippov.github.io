VHF

noun
viral hemorrhagic fever disease
Pronunciation: / viː ɛtʃ ɛf / (AmE), / viː ɛtʃ ɛf / (BrE)
An infectious disease syndrome caused by certain viruses, characterized by fever, bleeding, and often affecting specific regions.

Examples

VHF viral infection
Certain rodents can transmit viruses responsible for VHF to humans.
Healthcare workers take special precautions to prevent the spread of VHF.
Many outbreaks of VHF have occurred in tropical regions with inadequate healthcare.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Ebola: A severe viral hemorrhagic fever caused by the Ebola virus
  • synonym – Lassa fever: A viral hemorrhagic fever endemic to West Africa
