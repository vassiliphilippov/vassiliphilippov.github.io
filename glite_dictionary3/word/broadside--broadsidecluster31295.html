to broadside

verb
collide with the side of a vehicle
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔːd.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.saɪd / (BrE)
to collide violently with the side of (another vehicle, especially a car or ship)

Examples

to broadside a car
An inattentive driver broadsided the police cruiser during the chase.
Our car was broadsided by another vehicle when we were crossing the street.
The truck broadsided the van at the intersection, causing significant damage.
