to plunk

verb
pull or pluck sharply (musical context)
Pronunciation: / plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
to pull or pluck lightly but sharply, especially a string on a musical instrument

Examples

to plunk piano keys
Each child took turns plunking a single note on the old piano.
He absentmindedly plunked on his ukulele while thinking of a new melody.
She plunked the bass guitar strings, letting the deep notes ring out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pluck: to pull something quickly and sharply, especially a string on an instrument
