tangent

noun
trigonometric ratio; opposite/adjacent in a right triangle
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒənt / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The trigonometric function that gives the ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the adjacent side in a right-angled triangle.

Examples

tangent of an angle
She explained how the tangent function is used in right triangle trigonometry.
The tangent of a 45-degree angle is exactly one.
To solve the problem, you need to calculate the tangent of the given angle.
