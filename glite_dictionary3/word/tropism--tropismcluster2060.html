tropism

noun
involuntary response to a stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊ.pɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊ.pɪz.əm / (BrE)
an involuntary orientation or movement of an organism or part toward or away from an external stimulus

Examples

tropism toward light
Many plants display tropism by growing towards light sources.
Scientists study viral tropism to understand how viruses infect specific cell types.
The tropism of roots toward moisture helps plants survive in dry conditions.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – phototropism: movement or growth in response to light
  • synonym – taxism: orientation or movement of an organism in response to a stimulus
