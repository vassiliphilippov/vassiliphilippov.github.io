presentism

noun
belief that biblical prophecies are now being fulfilled
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛzəntɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɛzəntɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that biblical prophecies, especially those concerning the Apocalypse in the Book of Revelation, are currently being fulfilled.

Examples

biblical presentism doctrine
His book defends presentism by citing passages from the Book of Revelation.
Many theologians who subscribe to presentism interpret current world events as signs of the Apocalypse.
The conference debated whether presentism or futurism offered a more accurate reading of biblical prophecy.
