base

noun
part of a transistor in electronics
Pronunciation: / beɪs / (AmE), / beɪs / (BrE)
(electronics) The part of a transistor that separates the emitter from the collector and controls the flow of current between them.

Examples

base of a transistor
By applying a voltage to the base, you can control the transistor's output.
Damage to the base can disrupt the flow of current between the emitter and collector.
The base of the transistor must be connected properly for the device to function.
