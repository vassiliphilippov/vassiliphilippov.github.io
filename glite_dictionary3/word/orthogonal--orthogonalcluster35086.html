orthogonal

adjective
statistically unrelated; independent
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈθɑɡənəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒɡənəl / (BrE)
(of statistical variables, functions, or factors) independent or uncorrelated; having no statistical relationship.

Examples

orthogonal variables
In the analysis, the two variables are considered orthogonal because their correlation is nearly zero.
Principal component analysis seeks orthogonal axes to reduce the dimensionality of the data.
The researchers designed the experiment so that the factors were statistically orthogonal to avoid confounding effects.

Related Terms

  • antonym – correlated: having a mutual relationship or connection; statistically related
  • synonym – independent: not influenced or affected by others; statistically uncorrelated
