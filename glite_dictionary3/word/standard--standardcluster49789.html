standard

noun
basis of value in a monetary system
Pronunciation: / ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / ˈstændəd / (BrE)
the commodity or measure (such as gold or silver) on which the value of a country's currency is based in a monetary system

Examples

gold standard
During the gold standard era, each dollar represented a fixed quantity of gold.
Many economists debate the benefits of returning to a commodity-based standard for currency.
The country abandoned the standard of silver in favor of gold in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gold standard: a monetary system where the value of currency is defined in terms of gold
