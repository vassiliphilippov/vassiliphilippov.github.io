RBC

noun
abbreviation for red blood cell
Pronunciation: / ɑr bi si / (AmE), / ɑː biː siː / (BrE)
An abbreviation for red blood cell; a mature blood cell containing hemoglobin that carries oxygen to tissues and lacks a nucleus in mammals.

Examples

RBC count test
A low RBC count can indicate anemia or other health problems.
Medical students learn how RBCs transport oxygen to body tissues.
