verger

noun
church caretaker and ceremonial official
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒə / (BrE)
A person in a church who looks after the church building and assists in ceremonies, often carrying a rod or staff.

Examples

verger prepares church
As a dedicated verger, she ensured the altar was always perfectly prepared for services.
During the procession, the verger led the clergy down the aisle, carrying a long staff.
The verger unlocked the church doors early every morning before the congregation arrived.
