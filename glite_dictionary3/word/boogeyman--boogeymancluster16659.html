boogeyman

noun
imaginary monster used to frighten children
Pronunciation: / ˈbuɡiˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈbʊɡimæn / (BrE)
An imaginary monster said to scare children, often used by adults to encourage good behavior.

Examples

the boogeyman under the bed
As a child, he checked under his bed every night for the boogeyman.
In movies, the boogeyman is often depicted as a shadowy figure hiding in the dark.
Some parents warned their kids that the boogeyman would get them if they didn’t behave.
