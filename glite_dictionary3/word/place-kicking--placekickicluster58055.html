place-kicking

noun
kicking a ball placed on the ground (sports)
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪsˌkɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsˌkɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
(In sports) the act of kicking a ball after it has been placed on the ground, commonly used in football and rugby for field goals, extra points, or kickoffs.

Examples

place-kicking a football
He perfected his place-kicking technique through countless hours of practice.
In rugby, place-kicking is essential for converting tries into additional points.
The team's success depended heavily on accurate place-kicking during crucial games.
