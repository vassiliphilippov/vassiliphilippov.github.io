angular

adjective
measured by angle or rate of angle change
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a measurement or quantity) measured by an angle or by the rate of change of an angle, as in physics or mathematics.

Examples

angular velocity calculation
Engineers must calculate the angular acceleration to design stable spinning machines.
In astronomy, angular distance helps to describe the position of stars in the sky.
The scientist explained how angular velocity differs from linear velocity in rotating objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym – linear: measured along a straight line, as opposed to angular
