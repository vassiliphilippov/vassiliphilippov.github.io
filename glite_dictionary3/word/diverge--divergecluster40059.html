to diverge

verb
(mathematics) to have no finite limit
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɝdʒ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(of a mathematical series) to increase without bound or have no finite limit.

Examples

series diverges mathematically
A harmonic series will diverge, as its partial sums do not approach a finite value.
If the sum of the terms grows indefinitely, the series is said to diverge.
In calculus, it's important to determine whether a series will diverge or converge.

Related Terms

  • antonym – converge: (of a mathematical series) to approach a finite limit
