to appraise

verb
assess or consider carefully
Pronunciation: / əˈpreɪz / (AmE), / əˈpreɪz / (BrE)
to assess or evaluate something carefully, especially to judge its value, quality, or importance

Examples

to appraise a situation
Before making a decision, he appraised all possible outcomes.
She appraised the evidence thoroughly before giving her opinion.
The panel appraises each proposal based on its merit and feasibility.
Before purchasing the house, she had an expert appraise its condition.
The company's assets are appraised each year for tax purposes.
We asked a specialist to appraise the value of the antique vase.
