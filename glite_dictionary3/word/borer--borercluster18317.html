borer

noun
wood-boring insect or larva
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrə / (BrE)
an insect, larva, or mollusk that bores into wood, often causing damage to trees and timber

Examples

wood borer
An infestation of borer larvae can weaken wooden beams in old houses.
Farmers often worry about crop losses due to borers infesting their fields.
The fallen tree was riddled with holes made by a borer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – termite: a social insect whose colonies feed on wood
  • synonym – woodworm: a larva that bores into wood, especially in furniture
