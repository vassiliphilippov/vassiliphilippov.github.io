to collide

verb
crash together forcefully
Pronunciation: / kəˈlaɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd / (BrE)
to crash into or strike against something with force (used for objects or people)

Examples

to collide with a car
If two opinions collide, it can lead to a heated debate.
Police reported that two vehicles collided at the busy intersection last night.
The football players collided midair as they both jumped for the ball.
In particle accelerators, scientists collide protons at near light speed.
The laboratory experiment requires us to collide these two types of molecules.
To test the safety features, engineers collide cars under controlled conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – avoid: to keep away from or prevent contact with
  • synonym – clash: to come together forcefully, often with a loud noise
  • synonym – crash: to hit something noisily and violently
