tebibyte

noun
unit of digital information (2^40 bytes)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛbɪˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛbɪˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 gibibytes, or 1,099,511,627,776 bytes (2^40 bytes).

Examples

one tebibyte of storage
A single tebibyte can store over a thousand gigabytes of data.
Modern servers are often equipped with several tebibytes of memory.
The backup file size exceeded one tebibyte, so it took longer to transfer.

  • degree higher – pebibyte: A unit of digital information equal to 2^50 bytes.
  • synonym – terabyte: A unit of digital information equal to 10^12 bytes.
