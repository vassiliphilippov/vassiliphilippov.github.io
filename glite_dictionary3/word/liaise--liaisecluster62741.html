to liaise

verb
act as a link between groups or people
Pronunciation: / liˈeɪz / (AmE), / liˈeɪz / (BrE)
to act as a link between people or groups in order to help them work together effectively or to reconcile differences

Examples

to liaise with colleagues
As the project manager, she often needs to liaise between the marketing and design teams.
He was asked to liaise with the client to resolve the outstanding issues.
The embassy staff will liaise with local authorities to ensure the event runs smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intercede: to intervene on behalf of another
  • synonym – mediate: to try to end a disagreement between two parties
