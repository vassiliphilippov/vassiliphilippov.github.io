indicator

noun
chemical that shows presence of substance
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.dəˌkeɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪ.tə / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A substance used to show, usually by a color change, the presence, absence, or concentration of a chemical, or to test for a reaction.

Examples

chemical indicator paper
Doctors may use a special chemical indicator to detect glucose in urine samples.
Phenolphthalein is a common indicator used in acid-base experiments.
The solution turned pink when the indicator was added, signaling the end of the titration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reagent: substance used to cause a chemical reaction to detect or measure other substances
