to full

verb
to treat cloth to clean and thicken it
Pronunciation: / fʊl / (AmE), / fʊl / (BrE)
to beat or treat cloth, especially wool, in order to clean and thicken it during textile manufacturing.

Examples

to full wool cloth
After weaving, the wool is often fulled to improve its texture and warmth.
The workers would full the cloth by hand in the olden days.
Traditional mills still demonstrate how to full fabric using old techniques.
