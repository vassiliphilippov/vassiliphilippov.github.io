jamais vu

noun
feeling unfamiliar with the familiar; opposite of déjà vu
Pronunciation: / ʒæˌmeɪ ˈvu / (AmE), / ʒaˌmeɪ ˈvuː / (BrE)
A phenomenon in which a person feels unfamiliarity or strangeness with a situation or person that should be familiar, sometimes linked to certain types of epilepsy.

Examples

experience jamais vu suddenly
After years at the job, he was struck by jamais vu as his office suddenly seemed strange and foreign.
Some people experience jamais vu as a symptom during epileptic episodes.
The phenomenon of jamais vu can be unsettling, making the familiar world feel oddly unknown.

Related Terms

  • antonym – déjà vu: the feeling of having already experienced the present situation
