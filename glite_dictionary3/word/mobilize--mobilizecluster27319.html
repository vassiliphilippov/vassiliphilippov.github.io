to mobilize

verb
prepare troops for military service
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to prepare and organize troops or resources for military action or war

Examples

to mobilize the army
After the declaration of war, all able-bodied men were quickly mobilized.
Nations may mobilize their armies when faced with an imminent threat.
The government decided to mobilize additional forces in response to the crisis.
Countries often mobilize quickly in response to international crises.
If tensions escalate, the army may be mobilized within hours.
The government decided to mobilize its forces after the threat became clear.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demobilize: to release troops from military service
  • synonym – deploy: to move troops or resources into position for action
