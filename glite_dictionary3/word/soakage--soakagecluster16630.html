soakage

noun
process of soaking or saturation
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process of becoming softened and saturated by being immersed in water or another liquid

Examples

soakage of the soil
Continuous soakage of the soil helped the plants survive the drought.
Overnight soakage made the beans perfect for cooking.
The old ship's wood needed soakage before it could be restored.
