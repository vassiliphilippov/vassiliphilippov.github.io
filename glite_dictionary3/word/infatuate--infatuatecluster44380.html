to infatuate

verb
to inspire irrational passion or admiration
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfætʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfatjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to be filled with intense but short-lived or irrational passion or admiration, often leading them to act foolishly

Examples

to infatuate a young fan
He became so infatuated with the actress that he followed all her work obsessively.
Her charming personality managed to infatuate most of the people she met at the event.
The city's vibrant rhythm can easily infatuate anyone who visits for the first time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enamor: to cause someone to be filled with love or admiration
