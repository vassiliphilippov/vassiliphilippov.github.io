seizure

noun
sudden attack of illness
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
A sudden attack or recurrence of an illness, especially a sudden episode of abnormal electrical activity in the brain, such as in epilepsy.

Examples

seizure during epilepsy
During the test, she suddenly had a seizure and fell to the floor.
People with epilepsy are at risk of repeated seizures.
The patient experienced a seizure after missing his medication.

  • synonym – convulsion: a violent, involuntary contraction of the muscles
