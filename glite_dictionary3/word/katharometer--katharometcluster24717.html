katharometer

noun
device for measuring thermal conductivity
Pronunciation: / kəˌθærəˈmɑːtər / (AmE), / kəˌθærəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the thermal conductivity of gases, often employed in chemical analysis

Examples

katharometer measures gases
A katharometer is often used in gas chromatography to detect and quantify gases.
Researchers chose a katharometer because of its sensitivity to changes in thermal conductivity.
The technician calibrated the katharometer before analyzing the gas sample.
