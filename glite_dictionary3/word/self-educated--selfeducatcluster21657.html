self-educated

adjective
educated by one's own efforts
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈɛdʒəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈɛdjuːˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having acquired knowledge or skills through one's own efforts rather than through formal instruction or education.

Examples

self-educated expert
Despite never attending university, she is entirely self-educated and widely respected in her field.
He became a self-educated expert in botany by reading every book he could find.
Many great writers of history were largely self-educated.
