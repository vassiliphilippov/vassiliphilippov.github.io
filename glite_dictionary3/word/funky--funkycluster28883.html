funky

adjective
soulful and rhythmic (jazz)
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋki / (BrE)
(of jazz music) having a distinctive soulful or bluesy feeling, often with strong rhythms and improvisation.

Examples

funky jazz music
He loves listening to funky jazz records from the 1960s.
Her saxophone solo had a funky groove reminiscent of old-school blues.
The band played a funky jazz set that energized the crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bluesy: resembling or suggestive of blues music
  • synonym – soulful: expressing deep emotion, especially in music
