Sauk

noun
a member of a Native American people
Pronunciation: / sɔk / (AmE), / sɔːk / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting the Fox River Valley and Green Bay area of Wisconsin.

Examples

a Sauk person
A small group of Sauk later migrated to Iowa and Kansas.
The Sauk once lived along the Fox River valley in present-day Wisconsin.
Today, many descendants of the Sauk are part of the Sac and Fox Tribe.
