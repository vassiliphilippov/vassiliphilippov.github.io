peculation

noun
fraudulent misuse of funds or property entrusted to one's care
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛkjʊˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of fraudulently taking money or property that has been entrusted to one's care, especially in a public or official capacity

Examples

peculation of public funds
He was convicted of peculation after diverting public funds for personal use.
Strict laws are in place to prevent peculation by government officials.
The audit revealed widespread peculation within the finance department.
