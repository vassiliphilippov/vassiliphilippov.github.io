Descartes

proper noun
French philosopher and mathematician
Pronunciation: / deɪˈkɑrt / (AmE), / deɪˈkɑːt / (BrE)
(1596–1650) French philosopher and mathematician noted for his theory of mind-body dualism and the development of Cartesian coordinates.

Descartes and philosophy
In mathematics, the Cartesian coordinate system was named after Descartes.
Many consider Descartes the father of modern philosophy for his emphasis on rational thought.
