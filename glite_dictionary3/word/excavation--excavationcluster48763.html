excavation

noun
the act of digging
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.səˈveɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.skəˈveɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of digging or removing earth or other materials, especially for archaeology, construction, or mining

Examples

archaeological excavation
Archaeologists spent weeks carefully conducting the excavation of the burial mound.
Due to the depth of the excavation, safety measures had to be implemented.
The excavation at the construction site uncovered several ancient artifacts.
Heavy machinery is required for the excavation of valuable minerals from deep underground.
Recent excavation at the site has revealed large deposits of iron ore.
The excavation of the coal mine took several years to complete.

  • synonym – mining: the process of extracting minerals from the earth
