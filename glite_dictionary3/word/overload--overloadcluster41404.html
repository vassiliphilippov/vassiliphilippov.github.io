to overload

verb
fill too much; impair function
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈloʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈləʊd / (BrE)
to put too much of something into or on something, so that it cannot function properly

Examples

to overload a machine
Please do not overload the washing machine as it may stop working.
She overloaded her mind with information and ended up feeling confused.
The website crashed because too many users overloaded the server at once.
The teacher tried not to overload students with homework during exam week.
When trucks are overloaded, they become dangerous on the road.
You should not overload the washing machine with clothes, or it could break down.
During peak hours, the server may overload and become unresponsive.
If you try to run too many appliances at once, the circuit will overload.
The washing machine started to smoke because it began to overload.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overburden: give (someone) too much work or responsibility
  • antonym – underload: not making full use of capacity or ability
