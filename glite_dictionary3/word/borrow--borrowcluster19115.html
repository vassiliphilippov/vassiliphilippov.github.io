to borrow

verb
adopt and use from another source
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɒrəʊ / (BrE)
to take and use (an idea, method, or custom) from another source as if it were one's own

Examples

borrow an idea
His speech borrowed heavily from the language of past leaders.
Many modern technologies borrow concepts from nature to solve engineering problems.
The artist decided to borrow elements of cubism for her new work.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adopt: to take and use an idea or method as one's own
