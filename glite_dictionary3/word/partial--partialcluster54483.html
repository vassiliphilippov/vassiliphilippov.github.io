partial

adjective
showing favoritism or bias
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəl / (BrE)
favoring one side or person over others; showing bias or preference

Examples

partial judge
Parents should strive not to be partial when settling disputes between their children.
The committee was criticized for being partial towards certain candidates.
The judge cannot be partial to either side in the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym – biased: showing unfair preference for or against something or someone
  • antonym – neutral: taking no sides or showing no bias
