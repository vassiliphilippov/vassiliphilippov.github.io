opiate

noun
narcotic drug from opium or its derivatives
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.pi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pi.eɪt / (BrE)
A narcotic drug containing opium or a derivative of opium, used to relieve pain or induce sleep.

Examples

opiate pain relief
Doctors sometimes prescribe opiates to manage severe pain after surgery.
Morphine is a well-known opiate used in hospital settings.
The misuse of opiates has led to a serious public health crisis in many countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – opioid: a synthetic or semi-synthetic drug that has effects similar to opium
  • antonym – stimulant: a drug that increases alertness or energy
