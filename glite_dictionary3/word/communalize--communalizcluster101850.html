to communalize

verb
make communal property
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjunəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something the property of a commune or community, rather than of private individuals

Examples

to communalize land
After the revolution, many farms were rapidly communalized under the new policies.
Some activists argued that housing should be communalized to solve the affordability crisis.
The government decided to communalize the land to ensure equal access for all villagers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – privatize: to transfer ownership from the public to private individuals or organizations
