androgeny

noun
embryo development from only paternal chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ænˈdrɑdʒəni / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒdʒəni / (BrE)
A form of male parthenogenesis in which the embryo develops with only paternal chromosomes because the egg nucleus does not contribute to fertilization.

Examples

androgeny in reproduction
Cases of androgeny provide insight into genetic inheritance patterns in certain species.
