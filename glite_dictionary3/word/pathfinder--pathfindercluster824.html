pathfinder

noun
person who finds routes through unknown places
Pronunciation: / ˈpæθˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
a person who finds a way through unexplored or difficult territory; a pioneer or trailblazer

Examples

famous pathfinder
His reputation as a pathfinder grew after he mapped several unknown mountain passes.
In ancient times, every tribe depended on a skilled pathfinder during migrations.
The pathfinder led the expedition safely through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trailblazer: a person who is the first to do something or go somewhere, showing others the way
