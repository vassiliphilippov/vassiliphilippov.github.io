moorage

noun
securing a vessel with ropes
Pronunciation: / ˈmʊrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of securing a vessel with ropes, especially when it arrives at a dock or port

Examples

safe moorage procedure
Improper moorage can lead to damage during rough weather conditions.
The moorage of the ship was completed just before the storm began.
Vessels require secure moorage to prevent drifting overnight at the marina.
