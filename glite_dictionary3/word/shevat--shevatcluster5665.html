Shevat

noun
month in the Jewish calendar
Pronunciation: / ʃɛˈvɑt / (AmE), / ʃɛˈvæt / (BrE)
the fifth month of the civil year and the eleventh month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in January and February

Examples

Shevat in the Jewish calendar
The Jewish calendar's month of Shevat often marks the beginning of spring in Israel.
This year, Shevat coincides with the latter part of January and early February.
Tu Bishvat, a minor Jewish holiday, occurs in the month of Shevat.
