to seek

verb
move toward a place or state (of a thing)
Pronunciation: / siːk / (AmE), / siːk / (BrE)
(of a thing) to move towards or go to a particular place or state, often as a natural tendency.

Examples

needle seeks north
A compass needle seeks north due to Earth's magnetic field.
Heat energy seeks areas of lower temperature to establish equilibrium.
Water always seeks the lowest point in the landscape.
