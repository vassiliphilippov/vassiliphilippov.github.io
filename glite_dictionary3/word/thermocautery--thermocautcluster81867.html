thermocautery

noun
destruction of tissue by heat (surgery)
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜːrmoʊˈkɔːtəri / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈkɔːtəri / (BrE)
the destruction of tissue by the application of heat, especially as a surgical technique

Examples

thermocautery in surgery
Thermocautery is required to precisely remove abnormal growths without excessive bleeding.
Advances in thermocautery have improved patient recovery times for minor surgeries.
The surgeon used thermocautery to stop the bleeding during the procedure.
