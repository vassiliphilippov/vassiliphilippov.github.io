carburetor

noun
device that mixes fuel and air in engines
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrbjəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / kɑːˈbjʊərətə / (BrE)
A device in an internal combustion engine that mixes air with a fine spray of liquid fuel for combustion.

Examples

carburetor adjustment
In classic cars, the carburetor is often adjusted to achieve the correct fuel-air mixture.
Modern vehicles usually use fuel injection systems instead of a carburetor.
The mechanic replaced the old carburetor to improve the engine's performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fuel injector: device that injects fuel directly into an engine; modern alternative to carburetors
