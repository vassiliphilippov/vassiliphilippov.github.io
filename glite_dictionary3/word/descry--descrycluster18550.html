to descry

verb
catch sight of with difficulty
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskraɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈskraɪ / (BrE)
to catch sight of (something difficult to see)

Examples

to descry a distant ship
At last, the lookout descried a ship on the horizon.
From the hilltop, they could descry the distant lights of the village.
She strained her eyes to descry a figure moving in the fog.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discern: to perceive or recognize (something)
  • synonym – spot: to see or notice someone or something, especially suddenly or when it is not easy to do so
