uproar

noun
loud confused noise or commotion
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌrɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌprɔː / (BrE)
a situation with loud, confused noise and activity, usually caused by a group of people

Examples

loud uproar
Children's laughter and shouting filled the playground with uproar.
The decision to raise taxes caused a public uproar.
There was an uproar in the theater when the fire alarm sounded.
The controversial announcement caused an immediate uproar among the students.
The meeting descended into uproar as tempers flared over the new policy.
There was an uproar in the audience when the results were announced.
