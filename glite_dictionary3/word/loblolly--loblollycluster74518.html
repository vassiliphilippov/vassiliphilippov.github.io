loblolly

noun
thick porridge, gruel
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑː.bˌlɑː.li / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.ləli / (BrE)
A thick, often coarse porridge or gruel, especially one made with meal and water; formerly used as food for sailors or the sick.

Examples

hot loblolly porridge
He remembered eating a bland loblolly at his grandmother’s house when he was a child.
Sailors were often served loblolly during long ocean voyages in the 18th century.
The nurse prepared a bowl of loblolly for the patients in the infirmary.
