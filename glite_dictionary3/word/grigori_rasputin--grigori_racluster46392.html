Grigori Rasputin

proper noun
Russian mystic and royal advisor
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪˈɡɔri ræˈspuːtɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːri rɑːsˈpjuːtɪn / (BrE)
(1872–1916) A Siberian mystic and self-proclaimed holy man who became a close advisor to the Russian royal family of Nicholas II, noted for his influence and controversial lifestyle.
