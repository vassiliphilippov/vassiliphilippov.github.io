monetization

noun
making something legal tender
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of establishing something, such as gold or silver, as the legal tender or official currency of a country

Examples

monetization of gold
Debate continues over the monetization of digital currencies as official legal tender.
Government policies regarding monetization can have far-reaching effects on financial stability.
The monetization of silver in the nineteenth century transformed international trade and economies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demonetization: the act of removing the status of legal tender from a currency
