furfural

noun
liquid aldehyde used as a solvent
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝː.fjɚˌæl / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.fjʊˌrɑːl / (BrE)
A colorless or yellowish liquid aldehyde with a strong odor, obtained from plant materials and used in making furan and as a solvent.

Examples

furfural solvent production
Corncobs and oat hulls are major raw materials used to produce furfural.
Industrial processes often use furfural as a solvent for refining lubricating oils.
The strong, almond-like odor of furfural makes it distinctive in chemical laboratories.
