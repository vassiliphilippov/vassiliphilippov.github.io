call

noun
right to buy a stock at a set price before a date
Pronunciation: / kɔl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
an option that gives the holder the right to buy a particular stock or commodity at a specified price within a certain time period, or a demand by a broker for additional funds

Examples

a call option
If the price goes above the strike price, the call option becomes profitable for the holder.
Many investors hedge their portfolios using both call and put options.
She purchased a call on the stock, anticipating that the price would rise.
After the market dropped sharply, he received a call from his broker to add more funds.
Failure to respond to a margin call can result in the sale of your securities.
The sudden call forced several investors to liquidate their positions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – margin call: a formal request for more margin in a brokerage account
  • antonym – put: an option to sell a stock at a specified price before a set date
