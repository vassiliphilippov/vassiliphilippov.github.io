to set

verb
cause to attack (urge attack)
Pronunciation: / sɛt / (AmE), / sɛt / (BrE)
to cause something to begin, or to encourage someone or something to attack

Examples

to set dogs on intruders
In old stories, villains would often set their henchmen on the hero.
She threatened to set her angry cat on anyone who disturbed her.
The guard set his dog on the trespasser at the first sign of trouble.
Careless campers accidentally set the forest ablaze by leaving a smoldering campfire.
He was arrested after attempting to set a chain reaction that would cause the alarms to go off.
The protestors threatened to set fire to the abandoned building if their demands were not met.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignite: to set on fire or start burning
  • antonym – restrain: to prevent (someone or something) from attacking or acting violently
  • synonym – sic: to urge (especially a dog) to attack
  • synonym – start: to cause to begin or initiate
