to incline

verb
tend or be likely to do something
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈklaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to have or show a tendency, or to feel or cause someone to feel willing or favorably disposed toward something

Examples

to incline toward helping
Even as a child, he would incline toward helping others in need.
Given his background, he naturally inclined to support the proposal.
She inclines to pessimism when facing new challenges.
I incline to agree with your suggestion, as it seems reasonable.
She often inclines toward a more optimistic view of the situation.
When faced with conflicting evidence, he inclined to trust his instincts.
Her gentle words inclined him to trust her intentions.
His upbringing inclined him toward generosity.
The evidence inclines me to believe that the theory is sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disincline: to make or become unwilling or reluctant
  • antonym – dissuade: to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • synonym – lean: to have a tendency toward (a particular opinion, action, etc.)
  • synonym – lean toward: to be likely to prefer or support a particular idea or opinion
  • synonym – persuade: to cause someone to do or believe something by reasoning or urging
  • antonym – resist: to oppose or have a tendency against doing something
  • synonym – tend: to regularly or frequently behave in a particular way; be likely
