eleven-plus

noun
British school selection exam (historical)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛvən ˌplʌs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛv.ən ˌplʌs / (BrE)
(formerly in Britain) an exam taken at age 11 or 12 to determine placement into grammar or secondary schools.

Examples

pass the eleven-plus exam
After passing the eleven-plus, he gained admission to a prestigious grammar school.
Her parents were anxious about her performance in the eleven-plus tests.
In the 1950s, every child had to sit the eleven-plus exam to determine their secondary education.
