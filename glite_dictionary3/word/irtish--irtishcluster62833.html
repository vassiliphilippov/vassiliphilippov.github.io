Irtish

proper noun
river in northern Asia, Ob tributary
Pronunciation: / ɪrˈtɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪəˈtɪʃ / (BrE)
A major river in Asia that originates in the Altai Mountains in northern China and flows northwest to join the Ob River.

Examples

the Irtish River
Many towns and cities have developed along the banks of the Irtish.
The Irtish River is vital for irrigation and hydroelectric power in the regions it passes through.
The Irtish joins the Ob River, forming one of the world's largest river systems.
