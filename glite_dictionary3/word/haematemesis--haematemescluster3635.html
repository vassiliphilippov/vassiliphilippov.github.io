haematemesis

noun
vomiting of blood (medical)
Pronunciation: / hiːˌmætəˈmisɪs / (AmE), / hiːˌmatɪˈmiːsɪs / (BrE)
The vomiting of blood, usually as a result of internal bleeding in the stomach or esophagus.

Examples

haematemesis after ulcers
Severe peptic ulcers can sometimes cause haematemesis in affected individuals.
The doctor ordered urgent tests due to the presence of haematemesis and abdominal pain.
The patient was admitted to the hospital after experiencing haematemesis during the night.
