to pith

verb
remove spongy tissue from a plant
Pronunciation: / pɪθ / (AmE), / pɪθ / (BrE)
to remove the spongy tissue (pith) from the inside of a plant, especially from fruits or stems

Examples

to pith a fruit
After harvesting the stems, the botanist proceeded to pith them for further study.
To make the marmalade less bitter, carefully pith the oranges before adding them to the pot.
You need to pith the stalks to ensure the recipe turns out smooth.
