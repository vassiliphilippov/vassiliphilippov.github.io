to demur

verb
raise a formal legal objection
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɝː /; / dɪˈmɝ / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊə / (BrE)
to raise objections or show reluctance, especially for legal, moral, or personal reasons

Examples

to demur politely

Several board members demurred when the proposal was put to a vote.
She demurred politely when asked to lead the meeting.
