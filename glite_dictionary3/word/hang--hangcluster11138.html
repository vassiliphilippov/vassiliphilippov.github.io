to hang

verb
hold on tightly; cling
Pronunciation: / hæŋ / (AmE), / hæŋ / (BrE)
to hold on tightly or tenaciously to something or someone, especially so as not to let go

Examples

to hang on to a hand
Children always hang on to their parents in crowded places.
The climber managed to hang from the ledge until help arrived.
You need to hang on when the bus goes around sharp corners.

  • antonym – let go: to stop holding or release one's grip
