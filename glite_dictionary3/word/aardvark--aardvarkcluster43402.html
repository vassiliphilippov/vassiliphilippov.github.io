aardvark

noun
nocturnal African burrowing mammal
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrdˌvɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɑːd.vɑːk / (BrE)
A nocturnal mammal native to Africa, known for burrowing and feeding mainly on termites and ants, and recognized as the only living species in the order Tubulidentata.

Examples

wild aardvark
Although rarely seen, an aardvark left distinctive tracks near the termite mounds.
At the zoo, visitors learned about the unique adaptations of the aardvark.
The aardvark uses its long tongue to eat termites in the African savanna.
