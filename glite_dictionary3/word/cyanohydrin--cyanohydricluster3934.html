cyanohydrin

noun
organic compound with –CN and –OH on same carbon
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪənoʊˈhaɪdrɪn / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənəʊˈhaɪdrɪn / (BrE)
an organic compound in which a cyano group (–CN) and a hydroxyl group (–OH) are attached to the same carbon atom

Examples

acetone cyanohydrin compound
Acetone cyanohydrin is used in the production of methyl methacrylate.
Adding hydrogen cyanide to an aldehyde produces a cyanohydrin.
The formation of a cyanohydrin is an important step in some organic synthesis reactions.
