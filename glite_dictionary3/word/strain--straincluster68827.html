to strain

verb
to exert great effort or energy
Pronunciation: / streɪn / (AmE), / streɪn / (BrE)
to make a great physical or mental effort or to use something to its limits

Examples

to strain your muscles
He strained to lift the heavy box without any help.
She strained her eyes trying to read the fine print on the contract.
They strained to hear what was being said on the other side of the wall.
Her patience strained as the meeting dragged on for hours.
The old bridge cables strained under the weight of the passing truck.
The rope strained but managed to hold the boat in place during the storm.
Don't strain my patience with more excuses.
Her constant demands began to strain their friendship.
The last-minute changes really strained the entire team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exert: to apply or use force or effort
  • antonym – relax: to make or become less tense or anxious
  • antonym – relieve: to ease or reduce pressure or strain
  • antonym – slacken: to become loose or less tense
  • synonym – stretch: to extend or exert to the full length or capacity
  • synonym – tax: to place a burden on; to strain resources or patience
  • synonym – tense: to become tight or stretched
