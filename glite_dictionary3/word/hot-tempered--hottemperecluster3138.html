hot-tempered

adjective
easily angered
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑt ˌtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌtɛmpəd / (BrE)
easily and quickly angered; having a tendency to lose one's temper readily.

Examples

a hot-tempered person
Although he was a brilliant chef, his hot-tempered personality often led to arguments in the kitchen.
Her hot-tempered reactions made it difficult for her to keep long-term friendships.
The hot-tempered coach shouted at the referee after the controversial call.

Related Terms

  • antonym – even-tempered: habitually calm and not easily angered
  • synonym – irritable: easily annoyed or angered
