South

noun
the Global South; developing regions
Pronunciation: / saʊθ / (AmE), / saʊθ / (BrE)
(the Global South) the countries and regions, especially in Africa, Latin America, and parts of Asia and Oceania, that are often described as less economically developed and contrasted with the Global North.

Examples

the South countries
Many development programs focus on the needs of the South rather than the wealthy North.
Countries in the South often face higher debt and lower access to healthcare services.
A recent report compared education outcomes between the South and the Global North.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Global North: the economically developed countries that are often contrasted with the Global South
  • synonym – developing countries: countries with lower levels of industrialization, income, and human development indicators
