Ali

proper noun
the fourth caliph of Islam; cousin and son-in-law of Muhammad
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈliː / (AmE), / ˈɑːli / (BrE)
(c. 600–661) The fourth caliph of Islam and the first caliph recognized by Shiites; cousin and son-in-law of Muhammad, whose assassination led to the division of Islam into Shiite and Sunni sects.

Examples

Ali the fourth caliph
After the assassination of Ali, the Muslim community split into Sunni and Shiite branches.
Many historical accounts discuss Ali's role during the early years of Islam.
Shiites regard Ali as the rightful successor to Muhammad.
