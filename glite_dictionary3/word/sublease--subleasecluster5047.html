to sublease

verb
lease property to another tenant
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌlis / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌliːs / (BrE)
to lease or rent all or part of a leased or rented property to another person

Examples

to sublease an apartment
After moving abroad, they decided to sublease their apartment to a friend for a year.
She is currently subleasing her studio while traveling for work.
Tenants must get written permission from their landlord before they sublease any part of the property.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sublet: to lease or rent property to another where the original party is still a tenant
