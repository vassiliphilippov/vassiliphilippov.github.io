Keynesian

adjective
relating to Keynes's economic theories
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪnziən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnzɪən / (BrE)
Relating to the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes, particularly those advocating government intervention to stabilize the economy.

Examples

Keynesian policy ideas
Her research focuses on the development of Keynesian thought in modern macroeconomics.
Many economists debate the effectiveness of Keynesian versus monetarist approaches.
The government introduced Keynesian policies during the recession to boost demand.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Keynesianism: the economic theories and policies based on Keynes's ideas
  • antonym – monetarist: relating to the economic theories emphasizing control of a country's money supply
