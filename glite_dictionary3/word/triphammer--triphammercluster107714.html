triphammer

noun
mechanical power hammer used in industry
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪpˌhæmər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpˌhamə / (BrE)
a large, heavy power hammer operated by a mechanical cam and allowed to fall by gravity, used in forging and other industrial processes

Examples

industrial triphammer
A massive triphammer stood at the center of the old workshop, testament to a bygone era of industry.
Early factories often used a triphammer to forge large iron parts quickly and efficiently.
The blacksmith operated the triphammer to shape the red-hot metal into a horseshoe.
