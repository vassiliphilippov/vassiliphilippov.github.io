Mother Teresa

proper noun
Albanian-born Indian nun and missionary, Nobel laureate
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌðɚ təˈreɪsə / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə təˈreɪzə / (BrE)
(1910–1997) An Albanian-born Indian Roman Catholic nun and missionary renowned for her lifelong dedication to helping the poor in India, particularly in Kolkata.

Examples

Mother Teresa helped the poor
Mother Teresa devoted her life to caring for the sick and destitute in Kolkata.
