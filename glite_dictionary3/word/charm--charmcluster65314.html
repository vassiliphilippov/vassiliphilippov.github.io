charm

noun
object believed to bring good luck
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrm / (AmE), / tʃɑːm / (BrE)
an object or a word/phrase believed to have magical power or bring good luck

Examples

charm bracelet
He wore a silver charm on his bracelet, believing it kept him safe.
Many cultures use a charm to ward off bad luck.
She carried a lucky charm in her pocket for the exam.
He believed the ancient charm could heal the sick if spoken at midnight.
Inscribed on the old amulet was a mysterious charm in Latin.
The witch recited a charm to protect the village from harm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amulet: an ornament or small piece of jewelry thought to give protection against evil, danger, or disease
  • synonym – incantation: a series of words said as a magic spell
  • synonym – spell: a set of words with magical power
  • synonym – talisman: an object thought to have magical powers and bring good luck
