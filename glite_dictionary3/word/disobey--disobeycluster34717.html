to disobey

verb
refuse to follow orders or rules
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbeɪ / (BrE)
to refuse or fail to follow rules, orders, or instructions from someone in authority

Examples

to disobey the rules
Children sometimes disobey their parents when they want to assert their independence.
If you disobey the traffic laws, you could be fined or arrested.
She disobeyed her doctor's advice and continued working despite being ill.
