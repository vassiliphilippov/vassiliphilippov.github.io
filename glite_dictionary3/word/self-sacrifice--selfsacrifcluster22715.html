self-sacrifice

noun
giving up one's own interests for others
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˌselfˈsækrɪfaɪs / (BrE)
the act of giving up one's own interests, comfort, or well-being for the benefit of others or for a cause.

Examples

self-sacrifice for family
He showed great self-sacrifice by working overtime without seeking recognition or extra pay.
Her self-sacrifice during the crisis inspired everyone in the team to do their best.
True self-sacrifice often means putting the needs of others ahead of your own desires.

Related Terms

  • synonym – altruism: selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • antonym – selfishness: concern chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure
