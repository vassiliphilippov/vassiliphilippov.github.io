yodeling

noun
singing technique with voice breaks
Pronunciation: / ˈjoʊdəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjəʊdəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a form of singing in which the performer rapidly switches between the chest voice and falsetto, producing distinct breaks in tone

Examples

yodeling in the mountains
She learned the art of yodeling during her travels in Austria.
Swiss mountain singers are famous for their skillful yodeling across valleys.
The concert featured traditional yodeling as part of the folk music performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – yodel: to sing by rapidly changing between chest and falsetto registers
