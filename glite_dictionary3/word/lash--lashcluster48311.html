to lash

verb
tie or secure with rope, chain, or cord
Pronunciation: / læʃ / (AmE), / læʃ / (BrE)
to tie or fasten something securely with a rope, chain, or cord, often by wrapping it tightly around

Examples

to lash a box
Before traveling, he lashed his suitcase to the roof rack of the car.
She carefully lashed the poles together to build a sturdy tent frame.
The sailors lashed the cargo to the deck to prevent it from shifting during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bind: to tie or fasten tightly
  • antonym – unfasten: to open or undo something that is fastened
