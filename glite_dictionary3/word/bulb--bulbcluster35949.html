bulb

noun
(archaic) old term for medulla oblongata
Pronunciation: / bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
(archaic) The lower or hindmost part of the brain, continuous with the spinal cord; an old term for the medulla oblongata.

Examples

brain bulb
In outdated medical texts, the term bulb is sometimes used instead of medulla oblongata.
Some early anatomists referred to the medulla as the bulb of the brain due to its shape.
The vital functions regulated by the bulb include breathing and heart rate.
