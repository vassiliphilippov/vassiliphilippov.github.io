to cross-question

verb
to closely question, esp. in court
Pronunciation: / krɔs ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈkwes.tʃən / (BrE)
to question a witness closely, especially after they have already been questioned by the other side in a legal proceeding; to cross-examine

Examples

to cross-question a witness
During the trial, each side had an opportunity to cross-question the other's witnesses.
She felt anxious when the defense counsel started to cross-question her on the stand.
The lawyer began to cross-question the witness after the prosecution finished their examination.

  • synonym – cross-examine: to question a witness closely after direct examination, especially in court
