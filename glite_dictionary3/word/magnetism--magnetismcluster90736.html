magnetism

noun
the property of magnetic attraction or field
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡ.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The physical phenomenon by which materials such as iron are attracted by a magnet, or by which electric currents produce magnetic fields.

Examples

strong magnetism
He studied magnetism in his physics class at university.
Some animals can sense magnetism and use it to navigate long distances.
The earth's magnetism causes compasses to point north.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electricity: the phenomenon associated with electric charge
  • synonym – magnet: an object that attracts iron and produces a magnetic field
