requital

noun
deserved punishment or consequence
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkwaɪtəl / (AmE), / rɪˈkwʌɪtəl /; / rɪˈkwaɪtəl / (BrE)
the act of giving something (such as repayment, reward, or punishment) in return for what someone has done

Examples

requital for kindness
Fearing requital, the traitor fled into exile.
His cruel actions eventually led to his requital by the court.
Some believe every crime demands a fitting requital.
His generous gift was a requital for years of kindness.
She acted out of pure requital after being insulted by her colleague.
The novel explores the theme of requital and forgiveness after betrayal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgiveness: the act of stopping blaming or being angry with someone for something
  • synonym – reciprocity: the practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit
  • synonym – retaliation: the act of returning an injury or wrong
  • antonym – reward: something given in return for service or merit; the opposite sense of requital
