Gothic

noun
extinct East Germanic language of the Goths
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
an extinct East Germanic language spoken by the ancient Goths, known mainly from a 4th-century Bible translation by Bishop Ulfilas

Examples

Gothic language study
Linguists struggle to reconstruct Gothic pronunciations due to the language’s extinction.
Several biblical passages in Gothic have been preserved thanks to Bishop Ulfilas’s translation.
The Gothic language provides valuable insight into early Germanic tongue structure and vocabulary.
