semiautobiographical

adjective
combining autobiography and fiction
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌɔtoʊˌbaɪəˈɡræfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌɔːtəʊbaɪəˈɡræfɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a work of literature or art) combining autobiography with fiction; based partly on the author's life but also containing fictional elements.

Examples

semiautobiographical novel
He wrote a semiautobiographical novel inspired by his years in the army.
Her first book is a semiautobiographical account of her childhood experiences.
The film is acclaimed for its semiautobiographical portrayal of small-town life in the 1960s.

  • degree lower – autobiographical: relating to a person's own life or autobiography
