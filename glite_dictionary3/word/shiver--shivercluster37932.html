to shiver

verb
tremble from cold, fear, or excitement
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪvɚ /; / ˈʃɪvər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
to shake or tremble slightly and uncontrollably, usually because of cold, fear, or excitement

Examples

to shiver from cold
I shivered with excitement before going on stage for the first time.
She began to shiver uncontrollably in the icy wind.
The child would shiver at the sound of thunder during storms.
He began to shiver as the cold wind blew through the open window.
She tried to hide her fear, but she could not help but shiver.
The puppy would shiver every time it was left alone outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freeze: become hardened into ice or be very cold (opposite effect; not shaking but being still from cold)
  • synonym – quiver: to tremble or shake with a slight rapid motion
  • synonym – shake: to move up and down or from side to side with small, quick movements
  • synonym – tremble: to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety, excitement, or frailty
