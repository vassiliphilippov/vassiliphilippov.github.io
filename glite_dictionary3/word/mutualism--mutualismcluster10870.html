mutualism

noun
beneficial relationship between species
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuː.tʃu.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tjuː.əˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a relationship between two different species in which both benefit from their association

Examples

mutualism between species
Many flowering plants and their insect pollinators demonstrate mutualism in ecosystems around the world.
Research into mutualism has revealed its importance for species survival and ecosystem stability.
The cleaner fish and its host engage in mutualism, with both gaining advantages from their interaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym – commensalism: relationship in which one species benefits and the other is unaffected
  • antonym – parasitism: relationship in which one organism benefits at the expense of another
  • synonym – symbiosis: close and long-term biological interaction between two different organisms
