to twiddle

verb
to move or fiddle nervously with fingers
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪd.əl / (BrE)
to turn or move something repeatedly with your fingers, usually in a nervous or unconscious way

Examples

to twiddle a pen
He would often twiddle his pen during long meetings.
Nervously, Anna twiddled with her ring as she listened.
She began to twiddle her hair while she waited for a response.
He absentmindedly twiddled his pen during the meeting.
She likes to twiddle her hair when she's deep in thought.
The boy twiddled the dial until the radio found a clear signal.
