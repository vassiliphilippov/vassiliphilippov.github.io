acid-fast

adjective
not easily decolorized by acid (microbiology)
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd fæst / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd fɑːst / (BrE)
(of microorganisms) not easily decolorized by acid solutions, especially as applies to certain bacteria such as the tubercle bacillus.

Examples

acid-fast bacteria stain
A positive result in the acid-fast stain test can indicate tuberculosis infection.
Some acid-fast organisms are significant in diagnosing respiratory diseases.
The acid-fast bacteria retained the dye even after being washed with acid alcohol.
