amenable

adjective
answerable to authority
Pronunciation: / əˈminəbəl, əˈmɛnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəbl, əˈmɛnəbl / (BrE)
liable or accountable to a higher authority or jurisdiction; answerable.

Examples

amenable to law
As a government official, she is amenable to oversight by the parliament.
Companies operating in this country are amenable to local regulations and laws.
The president is amenable to the constitutional court and must follow its rulings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accountable: required or expected to justify actions or decisions; responsible
  • antonym – unaccountable: not required to justify actions or not subject to authority
