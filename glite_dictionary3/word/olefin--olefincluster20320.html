olefin

noun
unsaturated aliphatic hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊləˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊlɪfɪn / (BrE)
an unsaturated aliphatic hydrocarbon containing at least one double bond, such as ethylene or propylene

Examples

olefin chemical compound
Ethylene is the simplest olefin used in the chemical industry.
Manufacturers often extract olefins from crude oil for plastic production.
The double bond in an olefin makes it highly reactive in certain reactions.
