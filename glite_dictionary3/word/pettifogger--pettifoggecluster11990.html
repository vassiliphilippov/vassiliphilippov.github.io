pettifogger

noun
person who makes petty objections
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛtiˌfɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈpɛtifɒɡə / (BrE)
a lawyer or politician who is petty, dishonest, or uses unethical methods, especially about small or trivial matters

Examples

dishonest pettifogger
He had a reputation as a notorious pettifogger, always finding faults in every proposal.

Critics accused the politician of behaving like a pettifogger, twisting the facts for personal gain.
He refused to be represented by a pettifogger who would stoop to any tactic to win.
The court was fed up with the pettifogger who constantly found loopholes and delayed proceedings.
