to submarine

verb
throw underhanded, as in baseball
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.məˌrin / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.məˌriːn / (BrE)
To throw or pitch (a ball) with an underhand motion, especially so that the hand is below the waist level at the point of release.

Examples

to submarine a pitch
He often submarines his throws to add a surprise element.
Her unique ability to submarine kept the batters guessing.
The pitcher decided to submarine the ball for the final strike.
