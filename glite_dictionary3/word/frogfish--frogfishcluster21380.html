frogfish

noun
marine fish with a lure on the snout
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A marine fish with a frog-like mouth and a lure on its snout, used to attract prey.

Examples

colorful frogfish underwater
Divers were thrilled to spot a rare painted frogfish during their expedition.
The frogfish is known for its extraordinary camouflage, blending perfectly with sponges and corals.
The frogfish uses its lure to attract smaller fish close enough to catch.
