apocrine

adjective
relating to a type of glandular secretion
Pronunciation: / ˈæpəˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈapəkrɪn / (BrE)
(of a gland) relating to a type of exocrine gland that releases part of its own cell along with its secretion.

Examples

apocrine sweat gland
Some mammals produce apocrine secretions as part of their scent-marking behavior.
Sweat glands in the armpit are classified as apocrine glands.
The pathologist identified an apocrine pattern of secretion in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym – eccrine: (of glands) secreting substances without loss of cellular material
