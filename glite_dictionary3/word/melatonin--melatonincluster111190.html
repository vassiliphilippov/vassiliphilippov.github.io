melatonin

noun
hormone regulating sleep
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛləˈtoʊnɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈtəʊnɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the pineal gland in the brain that regulates sleep and wake cycles

Examples

melatonin levels in blood
Changes in light exposure can affect how much melatonin your body makes.
Some people take melatonin supplements to help them fall asleep faster.
The body's natural production of melatonin increases in the evening as it gets dark.
