arthroscope

noun
instrument for examining joints
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrθrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɑːθrəskəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument, a type of endoscope, that is inserted into a joint to examine its interior visually.

Examples

use an arthroscope in surgery
An arthroscope allows doctors to view and treat problems inside a joint without making large incisions.
The surgeon inserted an arthroscope into the patient's knee to assess the damage.
Use of an arthroscope can greatly speed up recovery times for patients undergoing joint surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endoscope: an instrument used to examine the interior of a body cavity
