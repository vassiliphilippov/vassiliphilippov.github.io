mouthwash

noun
medicated mouth-rinsing solution
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊθˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
A liquid solution, often medicated, used for rinsing and cleansing the mouth to maintain oral hygiene and freshen breath.

Examples

use mouthwash after brushing
After brushing, I swished some mouthwash around my mouth for extra cleanliness.
She uses mouthwash every morning to keep her breath fresh.
The dentist recommended a medicated mouthwash for healthier gums.
