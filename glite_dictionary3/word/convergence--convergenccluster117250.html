convergence

noun
becoming more similar; finding common ground
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝːdʒəns /; / kənˈvɝrdʒəns / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the process or act of coming together, becoming more similar, or uniting (such as ideas, strategies, or technologies)

Examples

convergence of ideas
Recent scientific discoveries show a remarkable convergence between genetics and environmental research.
The convergence of digital and traditional media is changing the way information is consumed.
There is increasing convergence in the approaches used by economists and sociologists.
Over time, the team's strategies saw a convergence that led to a unified approach.
The convergence of the two rivers created a powerful current.
We witnessed a convergence of ideas from different disciplines at the conference.
Recent decades have seen a convergence of technologies that once seemed unrelated.
The convergence of the rivers creates a fertile valley.
There has been a convergence of interests between the two companies.
