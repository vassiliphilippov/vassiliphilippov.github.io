to discharge

verb
become empty or release contents
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to release, emit, or let out a substance (such as a liquid, gas, or energy), or to become empty by releasing contents

Examples

to discharge waste water
If you forget to use the water, the reservoir could slowly discharge on its own.
The tank began to discharge after the valve was opened.
When the battery is used too long, it will gradually discharge.
After the heavy rain, water began to discharge from the drainage pipes.
The battery can discharge electricity when needed.
The factory was fined for allowing waste to discharge into the river.
Some plants discharge oxygen as a byproduct of photosynthesis.
The engine will discharge exhaust gases once it has started.
The factory is required by law not to discharge any toxic chemicals into the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym – absorb: to take in or soak up a substance instead of releasing it
  • synonym – emit: to send out a substance, such as gas or light
  • synonym – empty: to remove all contents from; to become empty
  • synonym – release: to let go or set free something held back
