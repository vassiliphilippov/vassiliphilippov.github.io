paramnesia

noun
memory disorder: confusion of fantasy and reality
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəmˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˌparəmˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
(psychiatry) a disorder of memory in which dreams, fantasies, or imagined events are confused with reality.

Examples

paramnesia affects memory
Cases of paramnesia can make it difficult for individuals to distinguish fact from fiction.
She was diagnosed with paramnesia after repeatedly confusing her fantasies with true memories.
The patient exhibited signs of paramnesia, often believing that his dreams were real events.
