to miss

verb
fail to have; lack
Pronunciation: / mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
to fail to have or lack something that is needed or expected

Examples

miss real flavor
My coffee seems to miss any real sweetness.
The plan will miss key details required for approval.
This recipe would miss something important without a pinch of spice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lack: to be without or not have enough of something
  • antonym – possess: to have or own something
