unrest

noun
restless agitation or discontent
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈrɛst / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛst / (BrE)
a state of restlessness or agitation, especially caused by worry or dissatisfaction

Examples

nighttime unrest
His constant unrest kept him awake through the night, worrying about the future.
Periods of political unrest often coincide with widespread public dissatisfaction.
There was a deep sense of unrest in the community after the announcement.
