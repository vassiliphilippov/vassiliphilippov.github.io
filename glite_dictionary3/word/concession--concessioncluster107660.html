concession

noun
right to run a business in a specific place
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a contract or official right to operate a business, especially within another organization's property or venue

Examples

win a concession contract
His company lost the parking concession after the contract expired.
The stadium awarded the food and beverage concession to a local company.
We secured the snack concession for the summer festival.
