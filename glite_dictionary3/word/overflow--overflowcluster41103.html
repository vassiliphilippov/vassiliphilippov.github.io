to overflow

verb
be extremely full of emotion or quality
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚˈfloʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈfləʊ / (BrE)
(of a person or their feelings) to be so full of a particular quality or emotion that it is obvious to others.

Examples

to overflow with joy
He is always overflowing with new ideas and enthusiasm.
Her heart seemed to overflow with gratitude after hearing the news.
The room overflowed with excitement as the band took the stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brim: to be full to the point of spilling over, often with emotion
  • synonym – burst: to be filled with emotion so that it is very obvious
