Ixodidae

noun
family of hard ticks
Pronunciation: / aɪkˈsɑdəˌdi / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of ticks known as hard ticks, having a hard outer shield and capable of transmitting disease.

Examples

Ixodidae family ticks
Researchers found that Ixodidae are the primary vectors for several tick-borne illnesses.

Unlike soft ticks, Ixodidae possess a tough protective shield called a scutum.
