theory

noun
belief or principle guiding actions
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪri / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
a belief or principle that influences or directs behaviour or decisions, especially one not yet proven or generally accepted

Examples

theory of motivation
His reluctance was based on a theory that the risks outweighed the benefits.
She worked on the theory that honesty was the best policy.
They acted on the theory that avoiding the area would keep them safe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assumption: something that is accepted as true without proof
  • antonym – fact: a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym – hypothesis: a suggested explanation or idea that is not yet proven
