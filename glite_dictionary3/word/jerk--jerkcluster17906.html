jerk

noun
sudden quick pull or movement
Pronunciation: / dʒɝk / (AmE), / dʒɜːk / (BrE)
a sudden, quick, or sharp movement or pull

Examples

jerk of the arm

The rope broke with a violent jerk as they tried to pull the crate up.
With a sudden jerk, the train started moving again.
He felt a sudden jerk as the car came to a stop.
The elevator gave a jerk before moving upward again.
With a quick jerk of her head, she signaled him to follow.
