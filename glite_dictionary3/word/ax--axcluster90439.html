to ax

verb
end or cancel suddenly (informal)
Pronunciation: / æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
(informal) to put an end to suddenly; to terminate or cancel something, especially a program, project, or job.

Examples

to ax a project
After months of uncertainty, the city council finally axed the expansion project.
The company decided to ax 200 jobs due to budget cuts.
With disappointing ratings, the network chose to ax the new TV series.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cancel: to decide that something planned will not happen
  • antonym – keep: to continue without ending or terminating
  • synonym – sack: to dismiss someone from a job
