Passover

noun
Jewish festival commemorating the Exodus
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A major Jewish festival commemorating the exodus of the Israelites from Egypt, traditionally observed for eight days beginning on the 15th of Nissan.

Examples

Passover holiday meal
During Passover, leavened bread is removed from Jewish homes in remembrance of the Exodus.
Families gather each year to celebrate Passover with a special meal called the Seder.
The traditions and stories of Passover have been passed down through generations.
