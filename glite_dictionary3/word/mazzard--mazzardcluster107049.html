mazzard

noun
wild sweet cherry used as grafting stock
Pronunciation: / ˈmæzərd / (AmE), / ˈmazəd / (BrE)
a wild or seedling sweet cherry tree, especially one used as rootstock for grafting cultivated cherries

Examples

mazzard cherry tree
A mature mazzard can grow taller than most cultivated cherry trees.
Many commercial cherry varieties are grafted onto mazzard rootstocks to improve disease resistance.
The orchard was planted with mazzard trees, prized for their hardy roots.
