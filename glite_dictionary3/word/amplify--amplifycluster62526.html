to amplify

verb
exaggerate or make something bigger
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.pləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈam.plɪ.faɪ /; / ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to exaggerate or make something seem larger, greater, or more important than it really is

Examples

to amplify a story
He amplified his achievements when talking to the interviewer.
Her report tends to amplify the problems the city faces.
Politicians sometimes amplify small issues to attract more attention.
Politicians may amplify their achievements to impress voters.
Stories about the haunted house tend to amplify the truth.
The legend was amplified with each retelling, making the hero seem larger-than-life.

  • antonym – minimize: to make something seem less important or smaller than it is
