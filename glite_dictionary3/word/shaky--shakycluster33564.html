shaky

adjective
trembling or unsteady
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪki / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪki / (BrE)
trembling, quivering, or not physically stable; likely to shake or move unsteadily

Examples

shaky hands
After the marathon, he stood on shaky legs, barely able to keep his balance.
Her shaky hands made it hard to pour the tea.
The little dog's shaky body gave away how frightened it was.
After the accident, she felt a bit shaky on her feet.
He gave a shaky laugh, betraying his nervousness.
The old bridge looked shaky, swaying slightly as the wind blew.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rickety: poorly made and likely to collapse
  • antonym – stable: firmly fixed and not likely to give way
  • antonym – steady: firm, not shaking or changing
  • synonym – trembling: shaking involuntarily, typically from anxiety, excitement, or frailty
  • synonym – wobbly: liable to move unsteadily or lose balance
