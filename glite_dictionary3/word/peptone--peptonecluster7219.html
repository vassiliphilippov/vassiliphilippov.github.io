peptone

noun
soluble protein hydrolysate used in laboratories
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛpˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɛptəʊn / (BrE)
a water-soluble compound formed by the partial hydrolysis of proteins, used in biological research and as a nutrient in culture media

Examples

peptone solution for bacteria
Peptone solutions are commonly used for cultivating microorganisms in research settings.
Biologists added peptone to the culture medium as a source of nitrogen and carbon.
The nutrient broth contained peptone to support bacterial growth in the laboratory.
