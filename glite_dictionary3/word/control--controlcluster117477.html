to control

verb
hold back or restrain
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to hold back, restrain, or keep something within limits; to lessen the intensity of something

Examples

to control anger
He struggled to control his anger during the heated discussion.
She tried hard to control her laughter during the serious meeting.
You must control your emotions if you want to be a good leader.

Related Terms

  • synonym – moderate: make less intense or extreme; restrain
  • antonym – release: allow to act freely; set loose; opposite of restrain or control
  • synonym – restrain: hold back or keep under control
