to haul

verb
transport or carry (usually heavy)
Pronunciation: / hɔl / (AmE), / hɔːl / (BrE)
to pull, drag, or transport something heavy, especially with effort or using a vehicle

Examples

to haul a heavy load
Every autumn, trucks haul pumpkins from the farm to the city markets.
The workers hauled sandbags to the riverbank to prevent flooding.
They hauled the furniture across town in a rented van.
A truck hauled the broken-down car to the nearest garage.
The sailors hauled the heavy nets out of the water early each morning.
Workers haul logs out of the forest using powerful machines.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drag: to pull something along forcefully
  • antonym – push: to move something away from oneself by applying force
  • synonym – transport: to carry something from one place to another
  • synonym – tug: to pull something suddenly or with force
