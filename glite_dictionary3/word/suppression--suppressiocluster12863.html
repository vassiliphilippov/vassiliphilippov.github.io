suppression

noun
conscious exclusion of unwanted thoughts (psychology)
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / səˈprɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
(psychology) The conscious act of excluding unacceptable thoughts, feelings, or desires from awareness.

Examples

suppression of feelings
Some psychologists believe that suppression can temporarily reduce anxiety but may cause other issues.
Therapists often debate the long-term effects of suppression on a patient's mental health.
Through conscious suppression, he tried not to think about his embarrassing mistake.
