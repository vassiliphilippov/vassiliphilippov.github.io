didacticism

noun
the practice of being instructive, especially in teaching or literature
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdæktɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / daɪˈdaktɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the practice or philosophy of being instructive, especially in a moral or educational way, often with an emphasis on teaching or conveying a lesson

Examples

moral didacticism
A strong sense of didacticism can sometimes make a work feel preachy.
Some critics argue that the novel's didacticism overshadows its storytelling.
The didacticism of the exhibit made it both informative and engaging for visitors.
