to crawl

verb
move slowly close to the ground
Pronunciation: / krɔl / (AmE), / krɔːl / (BrE)
to move forward slowly with the body close to the ground, especially on hands and knees or with the entire body in contact with the surface

Examples

to crawl on hands
Soldiers had to crawl under the barbed wire to cross the field safely.
The baby began to crawl across the living room carpet.
The lizard crawled slowly along the sun-warmed rock.

Related Terms

  • synonym – creep: to move slowly and carefully, especially to avoid being noticed
