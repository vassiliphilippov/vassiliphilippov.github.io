to elect

verb
choose or decide to do
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to choose someone for a position or office, usually by voting or making a formal decision

Examples

to elect a president
After much consideration, she elected to pursue a career in medicine.
They elected not to participate in the final round of interviews.
You can elect to receive your statements electronically or by mail.
She was elected president of the student council after a close race.
The shareholders will elect a new board of directors next month.
They elected him as their leader by a unanimous vote.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appoint: to assign a job or role to someone without an election
  • synonym – choose: to select from a number of possibilities
  • antonym – decline: to refuse or choose not to do
  • antonym – dismiss: to remove someone from a position or office
