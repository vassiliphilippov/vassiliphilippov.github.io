midget

noun
(offensive) very small person
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
(offensive) A person of unusually short stature, especially one who is much smaller than average. The term is now considered offensive and should be avoided in reference to people.

Examples

a midget performer
Books from the past sometimes refer to very short people as midgets, but language has changed since then.
He disliked being called a midget, as the term felt disrespectful and outdated.
The circus once featured a midget as part of its main attractions, though this is now seen as inappropriate.
