to evoke

verb
to bring to mind or cause to happen
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to cause a feeling, memory, image, or response to come to mind

Examples

evoke strong memories
His speech evoked a strong emotional response from the audience.
The scent of pine trees evokes images of winter holidays for me.
The song managed to evoke memories of her childhood summers.
Her speech evoked sympathy from the entire audience.
The film's soundtrack was designed to evoke strong emotions.
The old photograph managed to evoke memories of his childhood.
Her performance managed to evoke a strong emotional response from the audience.
The scent of pine trees can evoke childhood memories of camping trips.
This photograph evokes a sense of nostalgia for the old city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – elicit: to draw out a response or reaction
  • synonym – provoke: to stimulate a reaction or feeling, often a strong one
  • synonym – summon: to call forth or bring to the surface (a feeling, thought, etc.)
  • antonym – suppress: to prevent the expression of feelings or responses
