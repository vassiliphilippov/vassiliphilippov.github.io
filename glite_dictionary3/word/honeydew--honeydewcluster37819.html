honeydew

noun
sweet substance secreted by insects
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌniˌdu / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪˌdjuː / (BrE)
a sweet, sticky substance excreted by aphids and some scale insects as they feed on plant sap

Examples

honeydew on leaves
After noticing a sticky coating on her plants, she realized it was caused by honeydew produced by scale insects.
Bees collect honeydew from infested trees to make a unique type of honey.
Several ants were attracted to the honeydew left on the leaves by aphids.
