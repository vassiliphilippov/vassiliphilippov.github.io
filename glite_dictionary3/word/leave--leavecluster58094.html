to leave

verb
transmit knowledge, skills, or instructions
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liːv / (BrE)
to cause knowledge, skills, or information to remain with someone after you have gone; to pass on knowledge or instructions to others

Examples

to leave advice for students
He intends to leave his knowledge of the craft to his apprentices.
She hopes to leave her expertise to future generations through her writings.
The retiring teacher wanted to leave valuable lessons behind for her students.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bequeath: to leave or give by will (especially property or knowledge)
  • synonym – impart: to make information or knowledge known
