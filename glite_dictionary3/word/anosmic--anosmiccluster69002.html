anosmic

adjective
lacking sense of smell
Pronunciation: / əˈnɑzmɪk / (AmE), / əˈnɒzmɪk / (BrE)
having little or no sense of smell; unable to perceive odors

Examples

anosmic patient
After a severe cold, she became anosmic and could not enjoy the aroma of her morning coffee.
Some COVID-19 patients report feeling anosmic for weeks after their other symptoms have resolved.
The chef worried that being anosmic would compromise his ability to create flavorful dishes.
After recovering from the illness, she remained anosmic for several months.
Doctors often test whether a patient is anosmic as part of a neurological exam.
Some people are born anosmic and never know what scents smell like.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anosmia: loss or absence of the sense of smell
  • degree lower – hyposmic: having a reduced sense of smell
  • antonym – normosmic: having a normal sense of smell
