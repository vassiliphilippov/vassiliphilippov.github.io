Tojo Hideki

proper noun
Japanese WWII leader and war criminal
Pronunciation: / toʊˈdʒoʊ hɪˈdeɪki / (AmE), / təʊˈdʒəʊ hɪˈdeɪki / (BrE)
(1884–1948) Japanese army officer and Prime Minister during World War II, who authorized the attack on Pearl Harbor and exercised dictatorial control over Japan; executed as a war criminal after the war.
