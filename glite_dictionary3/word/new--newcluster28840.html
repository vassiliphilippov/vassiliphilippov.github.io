new

adjective
current stage of a language
Pronunciation: / nu / (AmE), / njuː / (BrE)
(of a language) in its current or modern stage of development, especially in contrast to an older or earlier form.

Examples

New English
New English includes words and usages that did not exist in Old English.
Linguists study the grammar changes between New Hebrew and its ancient predecessor.
Some dialects of new Japanese differ significantly from classical forms of the language.

Related Terms

  • synonym – modern: relating to the present or most recent period of a language or culture
  • antonym – old: denoting earlier or former stages of a language
