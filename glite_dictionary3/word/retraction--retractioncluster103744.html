retraction

noun
withdrawal of a previous statement
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
an official statement saying that something you previously said or wrote is not true or correct; the act of withdrawing a previous statement or claim

Examples

issue a retraction statement
After his mistake was exposed, the scientist published a formal retraction in the journal.
The company demanded a public retraction of the false allegations.
The journalist issued a retraction after realizing her report contained several errors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – assertion: a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • synonym – denial: the act of declaring something to be untrue
  • synonym – recantation: the public withdrawal of a former opinion or belief
