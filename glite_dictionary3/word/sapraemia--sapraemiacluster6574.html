sapraemia

noun
blood poisoning by putrefactive bacteria
Pronunciation: / sæˈpriːmiə / (AmE), / sæˈpriːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which blood poisoning is caused by putrefactive bacteria, typically resulting from the absorption of toxins produced by decaying organic matter

Examples

sapraemia from infection
Early detection of sapraemia is essential to prevent septic shock.
The patient developed sapraemia after consuming spoiled food.
Without prompt treatment, sapraemia can rapidly become life-threatening.
