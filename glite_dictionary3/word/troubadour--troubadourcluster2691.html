troubadour

noun
medieval poet-singer of romantic songs
Pronunciation: / ˈtruːbəˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈtruːbədɔː / (BrE)
A poet and singer, especially in medieval France, who composed and performed songs of chivalry and courtly love.

Examples

medieval troubadour sings
During the festival, a modern troubadour performed songs about love and history.
In medieval courts, a troubadour was highly regarded for their talent in poetry and music.
The troubadour captivated the audience with his soulful ballads and gentle guitar.
