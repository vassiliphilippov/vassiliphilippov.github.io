exothermic

adjective
releasing heat (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.səˈθɝː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊˈθɜː.mɪk / (BrE)
(Of a chemical reaction or process) accompanied by the release of heat.

Examples

exothermic chemical reaction
Combustion is an exothermic reaction that releases a significant amount of energy as heat.
Many industrial processes rely on exothermic reactions to provide necessary heat.
The reaction between sodium and water is highly exothermic, producing both heat and hydrogen gas.

Related Terms

  • antonym – endothermic: (of a chemical reaction) requiring the absorption of heat
