to condemn

verb
to officially take property for public use
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
(of an authority) to officially take private property for public use, typically by means of legal proceedings.

Examples

city condemns property
After months of negotiation, the city condemned the building to expand the public park.
If authorities condemn your property, you may be entitled to compensation.
The government decided to condemn the land in order to build a new school.
