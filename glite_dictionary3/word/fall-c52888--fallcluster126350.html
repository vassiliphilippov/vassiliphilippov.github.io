to fall

verb
(of speech) to be spoken or uttered
Pronunciation: / fɔl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
(of words, sounds, etc.) to come out or be produced, especially spontaneously or unexpectedly, from someone's mouth or lips.

Examples

fall from her lips
A quiet apology fell from her lips after the argument.
Laughter fell from the children's mouths as they played in the park.
Not a single kind word fell from his lips during the meeting.
