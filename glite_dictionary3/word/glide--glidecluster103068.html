to glide

verb
move something smoothly or quietly
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪd / (AmE), / ɡlaɪd / (BrE)
to move or make something move smoothly and effortlessly, often through the air or along a surface

Examples

to glide across ice
He glided his hand over the smooth surface of the piano.
She gently glided the boat across the calm lake.
The waiter glided the dish onto the table without a sound.
Clouds glide slowly across the bright blue sky.
She watched the skater glide gracefully over the ice.
The swan seemed to glide effortlessly across the lake.
After shutting off the engine, the pilot managed to glide the plane safely to the field.
She loved to watch the birds glide on air currents above the cliffs.
The eagle began to glide effortlessly over the valley, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym – skim: move lightly and quickly over a surface
  • synonym – slide: to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact
  • synonym – soar: to fly or rise high in the air
