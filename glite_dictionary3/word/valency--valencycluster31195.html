valency

noun
number of antigens in a vaccine
Pronunciation: / c8ve6al59nsi / (AmE), / c8ve6al59nsi / (BrE)
(in immunology and vaccinology) the number of distinct antigen types or strains included in a multivalent vaccine.

Examples

valency of a vaccine
The tetravalent flu shot's valency means it protects against four influenza strains this season.
Researchers increased the vaccine's valency to provide broader immunity across expected variants.
Public health agencies recommend higher valencies for vaccines when multiple strains circulate in a region.

Related Terms

  • antonym – monovalent: containing a single antigen or strain, opposite of multivalent
  • synonym – multivalent: containing multiple different antigens or strains; often used to describe vaccines covering several variants
