to chide

verb
scold or censure; reprimand
Pronunciation: / tʃaɪd / (AmE), / tʃaɪd / (BrE)
to speak to someone severely or angrily because you disapprove of their behavior

Examples

to chide a child
He chided himself for not being more careful with his words.
She gently chided her friend for forgetting the meeting.
The teacher chided the students for talking during the exam.

Related Terms

  • antonym – praise: to express approval for someone or their actions
  • synonym – scold: to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
