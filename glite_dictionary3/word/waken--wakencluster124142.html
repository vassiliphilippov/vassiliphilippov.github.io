to waken

verb
stop sleeping; cause to stop sleeping
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪkən / (AmE), / ˈweɪkən / (BrE)
to stop sleeping or to make someone stop sleeping; to wake up

Examples

to waken a child
A loud crash wakened her from a deep sleep.
He tries not to waken his roommate when he leaves early in the morning.
I did not waken until the sun was high in the sky.
Could you waken me before sunrise tomorrow?
I tried not to waken anyone when I left early this morning.
The loud thunder wakened the children in the middle of the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – awaken: to cause to stop sleeping; to rouse
  • antonym – sleep: to rest with eyes closed and mind and body inactive
  • synonym – wake: to emerge or cause to emerge from sleep; become awake
