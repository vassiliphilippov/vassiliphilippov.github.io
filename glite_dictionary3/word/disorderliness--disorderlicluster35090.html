disorderliness

noun
state of being untidy or disordered
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrdɚliˌnəs /; / dɪsˈɔrdərlɪnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəlɪnɪs /; / dɪsˈɔːdəlɪnəs / (BrE)
the state of being untidy or not arranged in order (messiness in appearance, clothing, or environment)

Examples

disorderliness in the room
His disorderliness was evident from his wrinkled shirt and messy hair.
Teachers often complain about the disorderliness of students' desks.
The disorderliness of the room made it hard to find anything.
After the party, the room was left in complete disorderliness.
She was criticized for the disorderliness of her classroom.
The disorderliness of his desk made it hard to find anything quickly.
