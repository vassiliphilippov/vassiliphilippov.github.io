to labour

verb
work hard; toil
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbɚ /; / ˈleɪbər / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
to work hard or make a great effort

Examples

to labour all day
She had to labour for hours on the challenging project.
The workers continued to labour in the fields despite the heat.
Volunteers laboured tirelessly to help rebuild the community after the storm.
After losing his job, he continued to labour night and day to support his family.
She is labouring to finish her novel before the end of the year.
The researchers laboured over their experiments for months before making a breakthrough.

Related Terms

  • antonym – relax: to rest and stop working hard
  • synonym – strive: to make great efforts to achieve or obtain something
  • synonym – toil: to work extremely hard or incessantly
