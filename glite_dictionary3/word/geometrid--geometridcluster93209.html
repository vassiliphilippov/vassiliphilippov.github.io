geometrid

noun
slender-bodied moth; larvae called measuring worms
Pronunciation: / dʒiˈɑːmətrɪd / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɒmɪtrɪd / (BrE)
A slender-bodied, broad-winged moth of the family Geometridae, whose larvae are known as measuring worms or inchworms.

Examples

geometrid moth species
A biologist identified the caterpillar as the larvae of a geometrid.
Many gardens attract geometrids, whose larvae are often seen inching along branches.
The geometrid moth is easily recognized by its slender body and wide wings.
