blether

noun
idle or foolish talk; chatter
Pronunciation: / ˈblɛðər / (AmE), / ˈblɛðə / (BrE)
idle, foolish, or irrelevant talk; chatter or gossip, especially of little importance

Examples

pointless blether
Instead of working, they wasted time in endless blether.
Please stop your blether and get to the point.
The room was full of blether about the latest office gossip.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chatter: talk rapidly or casually in a trivial manner
  • synonym – prattle: to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • antonym – silence: the absence of sound or talk
