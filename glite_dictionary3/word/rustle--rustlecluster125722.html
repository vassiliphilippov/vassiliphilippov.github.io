to rustle

verb
search for food, forage
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌsəl / (BrE)
to search for and gather food, especially by animals foraging in the wild

Examples

to rustle for food
Every morning, the deer could be seen rustling for berries near the stream.
In autumn, wild pigs rustle through the forest, searching for acorns.
The cattle rustle for food in the open fields from dawn until dusk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forage: to search widely for food or provisions
