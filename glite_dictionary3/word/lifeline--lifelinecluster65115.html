lifeline

noun
essential support or connection for survival
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.laɪn / (BrE)
a thing on which someone or something depends or which provides a means of escape from a difficult situation, especially one that is crucial for survival or continued existence.

Examples

lifeline for survival
After the natural disaster, international aid became a lifeline for the affected region.
Her weekly phone calls were a lifeline during his difficult recovery.
The hotline serves as a lifeline for people struggling with mental health issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aid: help that is given to someone, especially in an emergency
  • antonym – obstacle: a thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress
  • synonym – support: provision of help or assistance to someone or something
