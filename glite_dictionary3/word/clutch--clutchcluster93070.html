to clutch

verb
affect suddenly and powerfully (emotion, pain, etc.)
Pronunciation: / klʌtʃ / (AmE), / klʌtʃ / (BrE)
(of an emotion or sensation) to affect someone suddenly and strongly, often with a feeling of fear, panic, or pain.

Examples

fear clutched her
A sudden panic clutched at her as she realized the door was locked.
Cold dread clutched his heart before the speech began.
The terrible news clutched him with a wave of despair.

Related Terms

  • synonym – seize: to take hold of suddenly or forcibly; to affect someone suddenly (as in fear seizing someone)
