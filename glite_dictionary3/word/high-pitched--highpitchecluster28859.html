high-pitched

adjective
steeply sloping (esp. a roof)
Pronunciation: / haɪ pɪtʃt / (AmE), / haɪ pɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a roof or surface) set at a steep or sharp angle; sloping steeply.

Examples

high-pitched roof
Many Victorian houses feature high-pitched gables above the main entrance.
The architect recommended installing a high-pitched structure to improve drainage.
The old church had a high-pitched roof that shed snow easily in winter.
