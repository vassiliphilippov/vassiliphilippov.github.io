naif

adjective
innocent or unsophisticated
Pronunciation: / naɪˈiːf / (AmE), / naɪˈiːf / (BrE)
Showing unaffected simplicity, innocence, or lack of worldly experience; unsophisticated.

Examples

naif style painting
As a naif artist, she approached her work with a fresh and untrained perspective.
He was a naif in the business world, trusting everyone he met.
Her naif charm made her instantly likable to the group.

Related Terms

  • synonym – naive: showing lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • antonym – sophisticated: having a great deal of worldly experience and knowledge of fashion and culture
