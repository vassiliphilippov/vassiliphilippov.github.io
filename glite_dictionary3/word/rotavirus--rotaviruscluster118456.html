rotavirus

noun
virus causing infant diarrhea
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊtəˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
A virus that causes severe diarrhea and vomiting, especially in infants and young children.

Examples

rotavirus infection in children
Outbreaks of rotavirus are common in daycare centers during winter and spring.
The pediatrician diagnosed her baby with a rotavirus infection after several days of vomiting and diarrhea.
Vaccination against rotavirus can greatly reduce severe childhood illnesses worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reovirus: a group of RNA viruses, including rotavirus
