scale

noun
measuring instrument with marked divisions
Pronunciation: / skeɪl / (AmE), / skeɪl / (BrE)
an instrument, device, or system with marks or levels used for measuring, indicating, or evaluating quantities, including weight, size, or value (such as a ruler, bathroom scale, or rating scale)

Examples

scale for weight
A new color-coded scale was added to improve accuracy in reading measurements.
Adjust the dial until the pointer is on the correct number on the scale.
The thermometer has a scale marked in both Celsius and Fahrenheit.
He stepped on the bathroom scale to check his weight this morning.
The grocery store uses a digital scale to weigh fresh produce.
The laboratory scale must be calibrated before each experiment.
On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate your experience?
Pain was assessed using a visual analog scale.
The earthquake measured 6.0 on the Richter scale.
