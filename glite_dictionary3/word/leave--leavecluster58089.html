to leave

verb
to go away from or stop being involved with
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liːv / (BrE)
to go away from a place, person, or organization; to depart or quit

Examples

to leave a place
After five years with the company, Maria decided to leave and pursue a new opportunity.
Nathan will leave the committee next month after completing his term as chair.
When tensions grew at home, he chose to leave and find his own place to live.
The last bus leaves the city center at midnight.
We had to leave the party early because our child was sick.
What time does your train leave tomorrow morning?
Once the meeting ended, everyone left the conference room quickly.
She was sad to leave her friends at the end of the summer.
We should leave the house by seven o'clock to avoid traffic.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abandon: to leave someone or something behind, especially permanently or irresponsibly
  • antonym – arrive: to reach a place; to come to a place
  • synonym – depart: to go away, especially in order to start a journey
  • antonym – join: to become a member or part of a group or organization
