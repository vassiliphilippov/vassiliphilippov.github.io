swoon

noun
a brief fainting or loss of consciousness
Pronunciation: / swun / (AmE), / swuːn / (BrE)
a temporary loss of consciousness; a fainting spell, often caused by insufficient blood to the brain

Examples

a sudden swoon
A sudden swoon overtook her during the hot afternoon.
She fell into a swoon after hearing the shocking news.
The sight of blood made him collapse in a swoon on the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collapse: to fall down or suddenly lose strength or consciousness
  • synonym – faint: a sudden, brief loss of consciousness
