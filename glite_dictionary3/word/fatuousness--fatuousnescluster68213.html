fatuousness

noun
foolishness; silliness
Pronunciation: / ˈfætʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈfatjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being foolish or silly in an unintelligent or empty-headed way

Examples

his fatuousness in meetings
Her constant fatuousness made it hard to take her suggestions seriously in the meeting.
People were astonished by the sheer fatuousness of the plan he proposed.
The fatuousness of his remarks left everyone speechless.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absurdity: the quality of being ridiculous or wildly unreasonable
  • antonym – wisdom: the quality of being wise or making sensible decisions
