coherent

adjective
clear and logical in speech or thought
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
clear, logical, and consistent; easy to understand because all parts are connected in an orderly way (used for people, speech, writing, arguments, or systems)

Examples

coherent argument
After a good night's sleep, he was much more coherent during the meeting.
Despite the shock, she managed to give a coherent account of what happened.
The medication helped him become coherent enough to answer the doctor's questions.
He struggled to present a coherent argument during the debate.
Her essay was well-organized and coherent, making it easy to follow her reasoning.
The new policy lacks a coherent structure, which leads to confusion among employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym – consistent: acting or happening in the same way; not self-contradictory
  • antonym – incoherent: not able to express yourself clearly; lacking logical connection
  • synonym – logical: using clear, sound reasoning
  • synonym – lucid: expressed clearly; easy to understand (applied to a person or their expression)
