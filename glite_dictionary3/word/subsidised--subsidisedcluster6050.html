subsidised

adjective
receiving government financial support
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd / (BrE)
Receiving partial financial support from public funds, typically to reduce the cost to users.

Examples

subsidised housing
Many families rely on subsidised housing to afford a place to live.
She attended a subsidised preschool program designed to help low-income families.
The city offers subsidised public transport for students.
