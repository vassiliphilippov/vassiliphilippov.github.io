centimo

noun
fractional monetary unit (100th of base) in some countries
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɪˌmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪ.məʊ / (BrE)
a fractional monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic unit of currency in Venezuela, Costa Rica, Equatorial Guinea, Paraguay, and formerly Spain.

Examples

one centimo coin
During the currency change, he exchanged his remaining centimos for euros.
Prices in Costa Rica are sometimes quoted down to the last centimo.
The coin was worth only one centimo, but it was a precious memento from his trip to Spain.
