monopolisation

noun
exclusive control of a market or commodity
Pronunciation: / məˌnɑːpələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌnɒpəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of gaining exclusive control over a market or commodity, excluding others from competing

Examples

monopolisation of the market
Critics argue that monopolisation stifles innovation and limits consumer choice.
The government's new policies aim to prevent the monopolisation of essential services by a single company.
There were allegations of monopolisation in the telecommunications industry after several mergers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – competition: rivalry among businesses to attract customers
  • synonym – monopoly: exclusive control by one company over a commodity or service
