callow

adjective
inexperienced or immature (young person)
Pronunciation: / ˈkæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈkaləʊ / (BrE)
(of a young person) inexperienced and immature; lacking adult sophistication or experience.

Examples

callow young man
He was just a callow youth, eager to prove himself in the new job.
Her callow optimism faded as she faced the harsh realities of adulthood.
The callow recruits made several mistakes on their first day of training.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inexperienced: lacking in experience or skill
  • antonym – mature: having reached full development or experience
