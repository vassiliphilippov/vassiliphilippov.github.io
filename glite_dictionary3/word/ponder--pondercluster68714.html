to ponder

verb
think deeply about something
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːndər / (AmE), / ˈpɒndə / (BrE)
to think about something carefully and deeply, especially for a prolonged time

Examples

to ponder an idea
After the meeting, I pondered his words for hours before responding.
He sat by the window, quietly pondering the decision he had to make.
She likes to ponder big questions about life and existence late at night.
