Loki

proper noun
Norse god of mischief and discord
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ki / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ki / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the trickster god of mischief and discord, known for causing the death of Balder and being opposed by Thor.

Examples

Loki tricks the gods
In Norse mythology, Loki is often portrayed as a cunning and unpredictable trickster.
Many stories depict Loki as both friend and foe to the other gods.
The legend says that Loki played a central role in Balder's tragic death.
