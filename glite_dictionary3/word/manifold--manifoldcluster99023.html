manifold

noun
lightweight paper for making copies
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
A lightweight paper used with carbon paper to make multiple copies of a document.

Examples

manifold carbon paper
Before the advent of photocopying, documents were often written with manifold paper and carbon sheets.
He filled out the form on manifold so every party received a duplicate copy.
The secretary placed an original and two manifolds in the typewriter to produce three copies at once.
