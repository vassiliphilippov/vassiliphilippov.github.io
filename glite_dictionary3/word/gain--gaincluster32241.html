gain

noun
increase in signal power or amplitude
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪn / (AmE), / ɡeɪn / (BrE)
an increase in signal power, voltage, or current, typically measured as the ratio of output to input

Examples

gain in signal
If the gain is set too high, distortion may occur in the output.
Measuring the gain of this circuit is essential for precise signal processing.
The amplifier provides a gain of 20 decibels for the input signal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amplification: the process of increasing the strength of an electrical signal
  • antonym – attenuation: the reduction of signal strength
