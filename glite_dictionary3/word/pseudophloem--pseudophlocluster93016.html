pseudophloem

noun
false plant phloem tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˈfloʊɛm / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈfləʊɛm / (BrE)
Plant tissue resembling phloem but not functionally or structurally true phloem, found in some abnormal or secondary growth.

Examples

abnormal pseudophloem tissue
In some dicots, pseudophloem formation is associated with abnormal growth patterns.
Researchers identified strands of pseudophloem in the cross-section of the tree.
The abnormal stem contains pseudophloem instead of regular vascular tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – phloem: vascular plant tissue that conducts food from the leaves
