to off

verb
slang: kill deliberately
Pronunciation: / ɑf / (AmE), / ɒf / (BrE)
(slang) To kill (someone) intentionally and with premeditation.

Examples

to off a rival
He was worried someone might try to off him for what he knew.
In the movie, the hitman offed three people in one night.
The gang decided to off the witness before the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – murder: to kill someone unlawfully and with intent
  • antonym – spare: to refrain from killing or injuring
