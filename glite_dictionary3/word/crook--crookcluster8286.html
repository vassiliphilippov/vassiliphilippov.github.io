crook

noun
dishonest person; criminal
Pronunciation: / krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a person who is dishonest or a criminal, especially one who cheats or steals

Examples

a crook steals money
After years of fraud, the police finally arrested the notorious crook.
The company lost thousands of dollars because of a crook in the accounting department.
You can't trust that crook; he's lied about everything.
