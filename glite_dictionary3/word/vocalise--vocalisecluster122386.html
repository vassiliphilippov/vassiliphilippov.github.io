to vocalise

verb
express or state clearly in words
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to express or state something clearly in words or speech, especially aloud

Examples

to vocalise your thoughts
It's important to vocalise your feelings instead of keeping them hidden.
She struggled at first, but soon learned how to vocalise her thoughts in meetings.
The teacher encouraged students to vocalise their concerns during class discussions.
He tried to vocalise his thoughts clearly during the meeting.
Infants begin to vocalise long before they utter real words.
Some birds can vocalise complex sounds to communicate with their flock.

  • antonym – mumble: speak in a quiet and indistinct way, making it hard to hear clearly
  • degree higher – sing: to produce musical tones with the voice
