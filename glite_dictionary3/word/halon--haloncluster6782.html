halon

noun
brominated hydrocarbon used in fire extinguishers
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈheɪlɒn / (BrE)
any of several chemically stable compounds in which hydrogen atoms of a hydrocarbon are replaced by bromine and other halogen atoms, used especially in fire extinguishers and known to contribute to ozone depletion.

Examples

halon fire extinguisher
Concerns about ozone depletion have led to restrictions on halon production and usage worldwide.
Technicians must handle halon with care due to its environmental impact.
The fire suppression system uses halon to extinguish electrical fires quickly and effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym – halogen: any element of group 17 of the periodic table (fluorine, chlorine, bromine, etc.)
