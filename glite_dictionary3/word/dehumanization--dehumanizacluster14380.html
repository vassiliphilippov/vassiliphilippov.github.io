dehumanization

noun
the act of making people seem less human
Pronunciation: / diˌhjuːmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌhjuːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of depriving people of human qualities, dignity, or individuality, often by treating them in a less human or degrading way

Examples

the dehumanization of people
Industrialization has sometimes led to the dehumanization of workers.
Many activists protest against the dehumanization of refugees in political rhetoric.
The film explores the dehumanization of prisoners in the justice system.

Related Terms

  • synonym – depersonalization: the act of making someone feel or appear less like a person
  • antonym – humanization: the process of making something more humane or giving it human qualities
