IRA

noun
Irish nationalist militant organization
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪˌɑrˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪˌɑːˈreɪ / (BrE)
a militant organization of Irish nationalists, active mainly in the 20th century, that used terrorism and guerrilla tactics to seek the removal of British authority from Northern Ireland and achieve Irish unification.

Examples

the IRA attacks
Membership in the IRA was illegal and carried severe penalties under British law.
Several films have depicted the actions and motivations of the IRA during the Troubles.
The British government negotiated a peace agreement with the IRA after decades of violence.
