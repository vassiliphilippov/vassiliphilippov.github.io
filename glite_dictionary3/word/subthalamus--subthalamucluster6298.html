subthalamus

noun
part of the brain beneath the thalamus
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈθæləməs / (AmE), / sʌbˈθæləməs / (BrE)
the ventral (lower) part of the thalamus in the brain, located beneath the thalamus and involved in regulating movements

Examples

the subthalamus controls movement
Damage to the subthalamus can result in motor disorders such as hemiballismus.
Researchers are studying how the subthalamus interacts with other parts of the basal ganglia.
The subthalamus plays a key role in controlling movement in the brain.
