meniscus

noun
cartilage disk in a joint
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
Anatomy: a crescent-shaped cartilage disk that acts as a cushion between the bones in a joint, especially in the knee.

Examples

knee meniscus injury
Arthroscopic surgery was required to repair the damaged meniscus in his knee.
Physical therapy can help strengthen the joint after a meniscus injury.
The doctor said I had torn my meniscus during the basketball game.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cartilage: firm, flexible connective tissue found in various forms in the body
