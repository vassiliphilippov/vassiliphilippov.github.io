reprisal

noun
retaliatory military action
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpraɪzəl / (AmE), / rɪˈpraɪzəl / (BrE)
an act of retaliation, especially a military action taken in response to an enemy's attack

Examples

reprisal attack
In wartime, reprisal actions are sometimes used to deter further aggression.
The bombing was carried out as a reprisal for the earlier attack on their base.
The country threatened military reprisals if its demands were not met.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgiveness: the act of pardoning or letting go of resentment
  • synonym – retaliation: the act of returning an attack; revenge
