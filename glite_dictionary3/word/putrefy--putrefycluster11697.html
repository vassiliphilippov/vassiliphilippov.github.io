to putrefy

verb
to decay and become foul-smelling
Pronunciation: / ˈpjuːtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjuːtrɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decay and produce a foul-smelling substance; to become putrid.

Examples

to putrefy in the sun
After several days, the animal remains had started to putrefy, attracting flies.
If meat is left unrefrigerated in hot weather, it will quickly putrefy.
The discarded fruit began to putrefy in the compost pile.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decompose: to break down into simpler substances, often by bacteria or fungi
  • antonym – preserve: to keep something from decaying or being damaged
  • synonym – rot: to decay or decompose with an unpleasant smell
