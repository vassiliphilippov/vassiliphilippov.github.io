to reverse

verb
change to the opposite direction or order
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrs / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs / (BrE)
to change the position, order, direction, or effect of something to its opposite; to turn something around or move it backward

Examples

to reverse direction
If you reverse the order of the list, the youngest will come first.
It can be hard to reverse the effects of such a decision once it's made.
They decided to reverse the arrangement of the chairs for the ceremony.
If you reverse the image, you'll see the text is backwards.
Please reverse the shirt before washing it to protect the print.
The chef reversed the cake onto a plate, revealing the caramelized top.
New evidence could reverse the outcome of the trial.
Technological advances may reverse some of the damage caused by pollution.
The government decided to reverse its previous policy after strong public opposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – invert: to turn something upside down or reverse the order
  • antonym – maintain: to keep something in the same position or order
  • antonym – reinforce: to strengthen or support (the opposite of reversing an effect)
  • degree lower – restore: to bring back to a former condition
  • synonym – undo: to cancel or annul an action or effect
  • synonym – upend: to set or turn something on its end or upside down
