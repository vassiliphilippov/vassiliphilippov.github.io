Gemini

proper noun
lightweight text-first internet protocol
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛmɪnaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmɪni / (BrE)
A lightweight, text-first internet protocol and ecosystem used for publishing and reading simple documents, typically accessed via gemini:// URLs.

Examples

Gemini protocol website
Researchers tested the Gemini protocol for lightweight, text-based publishing across private networks.
I prefer using Gemini when I want a distraction-free reading experience on slow connections.
Many hobbyists set up a Gemini server to publish short essays and maintain simple archives.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Gopher: an earlier internet protocol and system for distributed text documents, conceptually similar in simplicity
  • synonym – HTTP: the standard protocol for transferring web pages and resources on the internet; a more feature-rich alternative
