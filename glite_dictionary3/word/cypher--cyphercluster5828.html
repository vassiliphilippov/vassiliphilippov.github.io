to cypher

verb
convert into a secret code
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪfər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪfə / (BrE)
to convert (a message) into a secret code; to encode

Examples

to cypher a message
He decided to cypher the contents of the email before sending it to his colleague.
Intelligence agencies often cypher sensitive messages to prevent interception.
The secret instructions were fully cyphered prior to being transmitted overseas.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decipher: to convert code into ordinary language
  • synonym – encode: to convert information into a different form, especially for secrecy
  • synonym – encrypt: to put information into code, especially for secrecy
