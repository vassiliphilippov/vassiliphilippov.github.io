prohibitory

adjective
discouraging or preventing, especially of prices or rules
Pronunciation: / prəˈhɪbɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / prəˈhɪbɪtəri / (BrE)
(of prices or rules) so high or strict as to discourage or prevent something from happening.

Examples

prohibitory price increase
A prohibitory tax on imported goods can effectively discourage trade.
She found the application fee to be so prohibitory that she chose not to apply.
The restaurant's prohibitory prices deter many potential diners.

Related Terms

  • antonym – permissive: allowing or not preventing something
  • synonym – prohibitive: (of prices or rules) so high or strict as to prevent something
