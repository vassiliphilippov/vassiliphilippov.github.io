flap

noun
state of nervous excitement or agitation
Pronunciation: / flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
a state of nervous excitement or agitation, often in reaction to an unexpected event or problem

Examples

in a flap before exams
She was in a real flap before her big presentation at work.
The lost ticket caused a flap among the passengers.
There was quite a flap at home when nobody could find the cat.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: a state of peace or tranquillity, absence of agitation
  • synonym – dither: a state of agitation or indecision
