syntagmatic

adjective
relating to elements in a sequence
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪn.təɡˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪn.təɡˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the arrangement or relationship of elements in a sequence, especially in linguistics or semiotics.

Examples

syntagmatic relationship in language
His research explored the syntagmatic structure of narrative texts.
In linguistics, a syntagmatic analysis examines how words combine in a sentence.
The study focused on syntagmatic relationships between words in spoken language.
