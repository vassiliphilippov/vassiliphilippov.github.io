bps

noun
bits per second (data transfer rate)
Pronunciation: / biː piː ɛs / (AmE), / biː piː ɛs / (BrE)
A unit for measuring the rate at which data is transmitted, equal to bits per second.

Examples

bps data speed
Modems in the 1990s often worked at rates no higher than 56,000 bps.
Please specify the required bps for optimal data transmission in your application.
The internet connection has a speed of 1000 bps, which is considered very slow by modern standards.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – Mbps: megabits per second; much higher rate of data transfer
  • degree higher – kbps: kilobits per second; a higher rate of data transfer
