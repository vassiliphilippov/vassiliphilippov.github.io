to murmur

verb
speak softly or indistinctly
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.mə / (BrE)
to speak or say something in a soft, low, and often indistinct voice, sometimes as a quiet complaint

Examples

to murmur a reply
He murmured something under his breath and quickly walked away.
Lying in bed, she murmured a quiet goodnight before drifting off to sleep.
She murmured a few soothing words to calm the frightened child.
He murmured a complaint, hoping no one would hear.
She started to murmur her frustrations as soon as her boss left the room.
The students would murmur about the difficult homework during class breaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – complain: to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym – grumble: to complain in a quiet or muted way
  • synonym – whisper: to speak very quietly so that only someone close can hear you
