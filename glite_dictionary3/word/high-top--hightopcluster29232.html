high-top

adjective
shoe or boot with high uppers
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ tɑp / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ tɒp / (BrE)
(of shoes or boots) having uppers that extend higher up the ankle than standard styles, often providing extra support.

Examples

high-top sneakers
He prefers to wear high-top sneakers when playing basketball for the extra ankle support.
Her old high-top trainers were finally starting to wear out.
The store has several styles of high-top boots in stock for winter.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low-top: (of shoes) with uppers that do not cover the ankle
