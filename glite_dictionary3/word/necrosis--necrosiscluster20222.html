necrosis

noun
the localized death of body cells
Pronunciation: / nəˈkroʊsɪs / (AmE), / nɛˈkrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the death of cells or tissues in a localized part of the body, typically due to injury, infection, or loss of blood supply

Examples

necrosis of tissue
Early treatment is essential to avoid necrosis following a serious injury.
If blood flow is interrupted for too long, necrosis can occur in the surrounding tissues.
The surgeon removed the tissue affected by necrosis to prevent the spread of infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gangrene: death of body tissue due to loss of blood supply or infection
