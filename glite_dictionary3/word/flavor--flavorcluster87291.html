flavor

noun
type of quark in physics
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
In physics, any of the six types of quarks distinguished by unique properties: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom.

Examples

flavor of quark
In particle physics, each flavor of quark has a unique set of properties.
Scientists discovered that there are six distinct flavors of quarks.
The 'strange' and 'charm' flavor quarks play crucial roles in certain particle interactions.
