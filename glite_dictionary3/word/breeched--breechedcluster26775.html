breeched

adjective
dressed in trousers
Pronunciation: / briːtʃt / (AmE), / briːtʃt / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a boy) dressed in trousers, rather than the more childish clothes previously worn.

Examples

breeched boy
He wore his first pair of trousers and proudly declared himself breeched.
In Victorian times, young boys were only breeched when they reached a certain age.
Once breeched, a boy was considered to have taken a step toward manhood.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unbreeched: not yet dressed in trousers
