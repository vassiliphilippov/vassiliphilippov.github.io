bitter

adjective
having a sharp, unpleasant taste
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə / (BrE)
having a sharp, acrid, and often unpleasant taste; not sweet

Examples

bitter dark chocolate
Dark chocolate can be bitter if it contains a high percentage of cocoa.
She grimaced after tasting the bitter herbs in her salad.
The medicine had a bitter taste that lingered in his mouth for hours.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – acrid: having an irritatingly strong and unpleasant taste or smell
  • degree lower – sour: having an acidic taste like lemon or vinegar
  • antonym – sweet: having a pleasant taste like sugar or honey
