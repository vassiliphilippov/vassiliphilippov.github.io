to chug

verb
make dull, rhythmic, explosive noises
Pronunciation: / tʃʌɡ / (AmE), / tʃʌɡ / (BrE)
(of an engine or machine) to make a series of dull, explosive, or rhythmic noises, especially while moving

Examples

to chug along the road
As the boat chugged along the river, we admired the peaceful scenery.
In the early morning, the delivery truck chugged up the steep hill toward the bakery.
The old tractor chugged across the field, leaving a trail of smoke behind.

  • synonym – hum: produce a continuous low sound
  • synonym – throb: make a regular pulsating sound
