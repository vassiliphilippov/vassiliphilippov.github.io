Kuan Yin

proper noun
Buddhist Goddess of Mercy
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑn ˈjin / (AmE), / ˌkwɑːn ˈjin / (BrE)
In Chinese Buddhism, a female Bodhisattva venerated as the Goddess of Mercy, often regarded as an incarnation of compassion and identified with Avalokiteśvara.

Examples

Kuan Yin statue
A statue of Kuan Yin stood at the entrance of the Buddhist temple.
In Chinese folklore, Kuan Yin is revered as a protector of women and children.
Many people pray to Kuan Yin for compassion and mercy in times of trouble.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Avalokiteśvara: a Bodhisattva who embodies the compassion of all Buddhas in Mahayana Buddhism
  • synonym – Kwannon: Japanese name for Kuan Yin, the Goddess of Mercy
