to spoil

verb
become unfit for use; go bad
Pronunciation: / spɔɪl / (AmE), / spɔɪl / (BrE)
(of food or other perishable items) to become unfit for consumption or use, especially as a result of decomposition or decay.

Examples

milk spoils quickly
Fish spoils faster than most other meats, so it should be cooked the same day.
If not refrigerated, milk will quickly spoil in warm weather.
Leftover food can spoil after a few days without proper storage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decay: to decompose, usually as a result of natural processes
  • antonym – preserve: to keep (food) from spoiling
  • synonym – rot: to decay and break down due to bacteria or fungi
