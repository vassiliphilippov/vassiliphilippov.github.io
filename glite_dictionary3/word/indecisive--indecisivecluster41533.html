indecisive

adjective
not settling a decision or issue
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv /; / ˌɪn.dɪˈsaɪ.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪv /; / ˌɪn.dɪˈsaɪ.sɪv / (BrE)
not able to make decisions quickly or confidently; lacking firmness or resolve

Examples

an indecisive person
His indecisive answer failed to resolve the dispute.
The meeting was long and indecisive, leaving everyone frustrated.
Voters grew tired of the government's indecisive response to the crisis.
His indecisive nature makes planning anything a real challenge.
She is often indecisive when choosing where to eat.
The team's progress stalled because their leader was so indecisive.
