to manage

verb
succeed using skillful or devious methods
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to succeed in doing something difficult or to cope in a difficult situation, especially with limited resources

Examples

to manage with little
Despite strict supervision, they managed to sneak out of the facility.
He managed to secure the deal by bending the rules a little.
She somehow managed to get backstage without a ticket.
Despite the heavy rain, he managed to finish the race.
She managed to solve the puzzle on her own.
They managed to get tickets for the sold-out show.
Even during the drought, the small village managed with limited water supplies.
Somehow, we managed on just two hours of sleep each night.
With such a tight budget, it's amazing how they still manage to pay the rent.

  • synonym – finagle: to obtain or achieve something by trickery

  • synonym – make do: to manage with what is available, even if it is not enough or not perfect
  • degree lower – struggle: to strive to achieve or cope with difficulty
