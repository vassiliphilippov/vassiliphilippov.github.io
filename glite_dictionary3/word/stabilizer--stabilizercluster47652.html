stabilizer

noun
chemical that maintains stability in mixtures
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a chemical substance added to a solution, mixture, or suspension to maintain stability or prevent unwanted change

Examples

food stabilizer added
A stabilizer was added to the solution to keep it from spoiling.
Many processed foods contain a chemical stabilizer to maintain freshness.
The stabilizer in the paint prevents it from separating over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catalyst: a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction
  • synonym – preservative: a substance used to preserve food or materials from decay
