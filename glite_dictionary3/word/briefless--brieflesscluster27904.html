briefless

adjective
(of lawyers) without clients
Pronunciation: / ˈbriːfləs / (AmE), / ˈbriːflɪs / (BrE)
(of a lawyer or barrister) without clients or cases to represent.

Examples

briefless barrister
After graduating, he spent several months as a briefless barrister in London.
Many aspiring advocates remain briefless until they have built a reputation.
The young lawyer lamented his briefless status at the firm party.

Related Terms

  • antonym – briefed: (of a lawyer) having been given a client or case to represent
