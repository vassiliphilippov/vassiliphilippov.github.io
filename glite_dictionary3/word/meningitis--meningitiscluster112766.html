meningitis

noun
inflammation of brain/spinal cord membranes
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease in which the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord become inflamed, usually caused by bacteria or viruses, often resulting in headache, fever, and stiff neck.

Examples

bacterial meningitis
After recovering from meningitis, she continued to experience headaches for several weeks.
Doctors quickly recognized the symptoms of meningitis and began treatment.
Vaccination has helped reduce outbreaks of meningitis in many countries.
