intima

noun
innermost membrane of a vessel or organ
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.tɪ.mə / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.mə / (BrE)
the innermost membrane or lining of an organ, especially the inner layer of an artery, vein, or lymphatic vessel

Examples

intima of an artery
Atherosclerosis is characterized by the thickening of the intima within blood vessels.
Damage to the intima can lead to the formation of blood clots in arteries.
The surgeon carefully examined the intima for any signs of plaque buildup.
