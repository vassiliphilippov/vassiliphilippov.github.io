hydroelectricity

noun
electricity produced by water power
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
Electricity generated by the energy of moving water, typically from a dam or flowing river.

Examples

hydroelectricity from a dam
Engineers are designing new dams to maximize hydroelectricity production while protecting local ecosystems.
Much of Norway's energy comes from hydroelectricity generated by its rivers and waterfalls.
The shift to hydroelectricity has significantly reduced the country's carbon emissions.
