fluster

noun
state of confused agitation
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌstə / (BrE)
a state of nervous confusion or agitation

Examples

a fluster of activity
His arrival caused a slight fluster among the staff, who were not expecting him.
She was in such a fluster that she forgot her keys at home.
Trying to answer all the questions at once put him into a fluster.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confusion: a state of being unclear or disordered
