doyley

noun
small decorative mat for dishes or bowls
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɪli / (AmE), / ˈdɔɪli / (BrE)
A small, often decorative, mat of lace or linen placed under a dish, bowl, or plate to protect the surface or for decoration.

Examples

doyley under plate
Please put the hot bowl on a doyley to protect the wooden table.
She placed a delicate doyley under each dessert plate for the tea party.
The antique silver tray was set on a lace doyley to prevent scratches.
