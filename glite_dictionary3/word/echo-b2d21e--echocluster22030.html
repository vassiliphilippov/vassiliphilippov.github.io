echo

noun
repeated sound caused by reflection
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
a sound that is repeated after being reflected off a surface, such as a wall or mountain

Examples

a loud echo
As she yelled into the canyon, her voice created an echo that bounced off the cliffs.
The empty hall amplified the echo of their laughter.
We heard the distant echo of thunder rumbling across the valley.
Her song produced a beautiful echo against the stone walls.
The echo of footsteps could be heard in the empty corridor.
The mountains sent back a clear echo of his call.

  • synonym – reverberation: prolongation of a sound; continued reflection of sound waves
