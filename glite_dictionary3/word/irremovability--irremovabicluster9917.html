irremovability

noun
quality of not being removable
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to remove from an office, position, or place

The irremovability of judges is a key principle in many legal systems.
