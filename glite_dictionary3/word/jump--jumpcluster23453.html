to jump

verb
switch rapidly between states
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to switch rapidly back and forth between two states or conditions

Examples

jump between moods
Her mood would often jump from cheerful to irritable in minutes.
Stock prices continue to jump up and down in response to the market news.
The conversation jumped between serious topics and lighthearted jokes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oscillate: to move or swing back and forth in a regular rhythm
  • synonym – toggle: to switch between two options repeatedly
