regular

adjective
scheduled for fixed times
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
happening or done at fixed times, intervals, or following a usual pattern; frequent or scheduled

Examples

regular check-ups
Please schedule regular check-ups with your doctor to monitor your health.
The committee holds regular meetings on the first Monday of every month.
There is a regular bus service between the airport and the city center.
Buses arrive at regular intervals along this route.
She tried to keep her breathing regular during meditation.
The heart monitor showed a regular rhythm throughout the night.
A regular attendee of the conference, Mark knew everyone by name.
She became a regular at the local yoga class over the years.
The café gives discounts to regular customers.

  • antonym – irregular: not happening at fixed times or intervals
  • antonym – occasional: happening or done irregularly or infrequently
  • synonym – routine: something done as a usual practice; habitual
