plutonic

adjective
of igneous rock formed underground
Pronunciation: / pluˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / pluˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of igneous rock) formed by the solidification of magma beneath the Earth's surface.

Examples

plutonic rock formation
Granite is a common example of a plutonic rock.
The region's bedrock is predominantly plutonic in origin, indicating deep magmatic activity.
Unlike volcanic rocks, plutonic rocks cool slowly, allowing larger crystals to form.

Related Terms

  • antonym – volcanic: (of igneous rock) formed from magma erupted onto the surface
