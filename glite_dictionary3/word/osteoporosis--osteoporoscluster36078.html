osteoporosis

noun
loss of bone mass causing fragile bones
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːstiəpəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊpəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormal loss of bone tissue, leading to fragile and porous bones, commonly due to lack of calcium, especially in postmenopausal women.

Examples

osteoporosis in older women
After being diagnosed with osteoporosis, she began taking a daily calcium supplement.
The doctor explained that osteoporosis increases the risk of bone fractures in elderly patients.
Weight-bearing exercise can help prevent osteoporosis and strengthen your bones.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – osteopenia: a condition of lower bone density, less severe than osteoporosis
