to control

verb
check or regulate an experiment
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to check, regulate, or validate an experiment or records by comparison with a standard

Examples

to control for errors
It is important to control for confounding variables in clinical trials.
Researchers controlled the experiment by using a placebo group.
You must control the sample conditions to ensure accurate results.
Auditors regularly control inventory records to ensure accuracy.
The accountant will control the account by comparing it with the duplicate register.
You must control the company's financial entries at the end of every month.
