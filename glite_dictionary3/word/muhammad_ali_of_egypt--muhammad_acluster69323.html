Muhammad Ali of Egypt

proper noun
19th-century viceroy and reformer of Egypt
Pronunciation: / muːˈhɑːməd ˈɑːli əv iˈdʒɪpt / (AmE), / mʊˈhæməd ˈɑːli əv ˈiːdʒɪpt / (BrE)
(1769–1849) Albanian-Ottoman soldier and statesman who became the ruler of Egypt, initiating many reforms and effectively establishing Egypt as a modern, semi-independent state.

Examples

Muhammad Ali of Egypt ruled Egypt
Muhammad Ali of Egypt implemented sweeping military and economic reforms during his rule in the 19th century.
Historians often credit Muhammad Ali of Egypt with laying the foundation for the modern Egyptian state.
The rise of Muhammad Ali of Egypt marked a turning point in the country's independence from Ottoman control.
