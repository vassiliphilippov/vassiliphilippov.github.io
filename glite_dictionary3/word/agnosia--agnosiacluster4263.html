agnosia

noun
inability to recognize objects or people (medical)
Pronunciation: / ægˈnoʊziə / (AmE), / ægˈnəʊziə / (BrE)
A loss or impairment of the ability to recognize familiar objects or people through the senses, even though the sensory organs are functioning normally.

Examples

agnosia after brain injury
After the accident, his agnosia made it impossible for him to identify everyday items by sight.
Her doctor explained that her symptoms pointed to a rare form of agnosia.
People with visual agnosia may see objects clearly but cannot name or describe them.
