to poise

verb
hold or balance steadily
Pronunciation: / pɔɪz / (AmE), / pɔɪz / (BrE)
to hold or be in a balanced, steady, or ready position, or to prepare oneself to act

Examples

to poise a tray
He poised the tray carefully on one hand as he opened the door.
The bird poised on the branch, ready to swoop down on its prey.
The gymnast managed to poise herself on the narrow beam for several seconds.
A hawk poised in the air, ready to dive onto its prey.
He poised the plate on the edge of the table, careful not to let it fall.
The gymnast poised herself gracefully on the balance beam before beginning her routine.
He took a deep breath and poised himself to deliver the bad news.
She poised herself at the starting line, ready for the race to begin.
The soldiers poised themselves for the difficult journey ahead.
