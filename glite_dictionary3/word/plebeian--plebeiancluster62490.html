plebeian

noun
common person; ordinary citizen
Pronunciation: / pləˈbiːən / (AmE), / plɪˈbiːən / (BrE)
A member of the common people, especially in ancient Rome; an ordinary person with no special rank or title.

Examples

Roman plebeian
During the Roman Republic, a plebeian could not hold public office.
In his novel, the hero was a humble plebeian from the countryside.
Many reforms in Rome aimed to increase the rights of the plebeians.

Related Terms

  • synonym – commoner: an ordinary person without rank or title
  • antonym – patrician: a member of the aristocracy, especially in ancient Rome
