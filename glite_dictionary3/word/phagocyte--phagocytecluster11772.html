phagocyte

noun
cell that engulfs harmful particles
Pronunciation: / ˈfæɡəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæɡəsaɪt / (BrE)
a type of cell that engulfs and digests foreign particles, bacteria, and cellular debris as part of the immune system

Examples

white phagocyte cell
Reduced activity of phagocytes can increase susceptibility to infection.
Scientists observed how phagocytes engulfed the invading microorganisms.
The body's immune system uses phagocytes to destroy bacteria and worn-out cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lymphocyte: type of white blood cell involved in the immune response
  • synonym – macrophage: large white blood cell that ingests foreign particles
