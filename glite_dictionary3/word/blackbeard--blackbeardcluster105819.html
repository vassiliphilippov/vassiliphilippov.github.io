Blackbeard

proper noun
notorious English pirate (c. 1680–1718)
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbɪəd / (BrE)
(c. 1680–1718) The nickname of Edward Teach (or Thatch), a notorious English pirate who raided ships in the Caribbean and along the American Atlantic coast.

Examples

the pirate Blackbeard
Blackbeard was known for tying slow-burning fuses into his beard to intimidate his enemies.
Many legends surround the life and exploits of Blackbeard.
Tourists visit the coast of North Carolina to learn about Blackbeard and his final battle.
