homeothermic

adjective
maintaining constant high body temperature
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊ.mi.oʊˈθɜr.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊ.mi.əʊˈθɜː.mɪk / (BrE)
(of birds and mammals) having a constant and relatively high body temperature, maintained independently of the environment.

Examples

homeothermic animals
Birds remain homeothermic even in very cold climates due to their metabolic processes.
Most mammals are homeothermic and maintain their body temperature regardless of external conditions.
Unlike reptiles, homeothermic creatures can stay active in a wide range of environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endothermic: (of an organism) capable of generating heat internally to maintain body temperature
  • antonym – poikilothermic: (of an organism) having a body temperature that varies with the environment
