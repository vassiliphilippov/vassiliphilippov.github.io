A. testudineus

noun
air-breathing climbing perch fish
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˌtɛs.tuˈdɪn.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌtɛs.tjʊˈdɪn.i.əs / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish native to India and Southeast Asia, known as the climbing perch, whose gills are adapted for breathing air and which can travel short distances on land using its spiny pectoral fins
