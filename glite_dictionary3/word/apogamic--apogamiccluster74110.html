apogamic

adjective
development without fertilization (plants, embryos)
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈɡæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈɡamɪk / (BrE)
(of plants or embryos) relating to or resulting from the development of an embryo without fertilization.

Examples

apogamic reproduction process
Researchers observed apogamic development in several rare flowering plants in the study.
Some fern species reproduce through an apogamic process, bypassing the need for fertilization.
The apogamic nature of this species allows it to produce offspring clones without seeds.
