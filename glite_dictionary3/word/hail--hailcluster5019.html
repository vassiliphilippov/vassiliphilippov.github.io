to hail

verb
call or signal to attract attention
Pronunciation: / heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
to call or signal to a person, vehicle, or vessel, especially to attract attention or request a service

Examples

to hail a taxi
People stood in the rain, waving to hail the approaching bus.
She quickly stepped to the curb to hail a taxi during the morning rush hour.
Unable to find parking, he decided to hail a cab instead.
