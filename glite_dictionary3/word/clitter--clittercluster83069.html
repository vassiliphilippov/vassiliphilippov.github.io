to clitter

verb
make a shrill, creaking noise by rubbing body parts together
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪtər / (AmE), / ˈklɪtə / (BrE)
(of certain animals, especially insects) to make a shrill creaking or rattling noise by rubbing particular body parts together.

Examples

clitter like crickets
At dusk, the crickets clitter loudly in the long grass.
Some beetles clitter their wings in warning when threatened by predators.
The grasshoppers clittered throughout the night, filling the air with their distinctive noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stridulate: to produce a shrill sound by rubbing body parts together, as some insects do
