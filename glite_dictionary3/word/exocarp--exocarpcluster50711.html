exocarp

noun
outer skin of a fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.soʊˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈɛk.səʊˌkɑːp / (BrE)
the outermost layer of the pericarp (fruit wall) of a fruit, such as the skin of a peach or grape

Examples

exocarp of a fruit
In citrus fruits, the brightly colored outer peel is the exocarp.
Scientists analyze the exocarp to determine how fruits resist pests.
The exocarp of a peach is soft and slightly fuzzy to the touch.
