to wound

verb
to hurt someone's feelings deeply
Pronunciation: / wund / (AmE), / wuːnd / (BrE)
to cause someone emotional pain or distress; to hurt someone's feelings deeply, especially in a serious or lasting way

Examples

to wound feelings
He was deeply wounded by the unfair criticism from his colleagues.
Her harsh words wounded him more than she realized.
Try not to wound someone with careless remarks.

Related Terms
