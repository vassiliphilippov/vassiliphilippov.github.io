moot

noun
legal exercise; mock case argued by students
Pronunciation: / mut / (AmE), / muːt / (BrE)
a hypothetical legal case that students argue as an exercise, especially in law schools.

Examples

moot court
Each law school team prepares arguments for the moot before a panel of judges.
She participated in the university's annual moot to improve her advocacy skills.
The final round of the moot was held in the main lecture hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mock trial: a simulated court trial, often used for teaching
