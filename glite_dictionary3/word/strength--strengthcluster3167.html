strength

noun
persuasiveness or force of an argument
Pronunciation: / strɛŋkθ / (AmE), / strɛŋθ / (BrE)
the degree to which an argument, idea, or viewpoint is compelling or persuasive

Examples

strength of argument
He doubted the strength of their arguments in court.
The strength of her reasoning convinced everyone at the meeting.
The committee was swayed by the strength of public opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – force: strength or power to influence (of arguments or opinions)
  • antonym – weakness: lack of persuasiveness or effectiveness (of an argument)
