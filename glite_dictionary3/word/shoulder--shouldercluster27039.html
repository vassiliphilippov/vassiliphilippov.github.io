to shoulder

verb
to carry or take on a burden or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
to carry or accept a burden, responsibility, or task, either physically or figuratively

Examples

to shoulder responsibility
After his father's death, he had to shoulder the responsibility of supporting the family.
She willingly shouldered the task of organizing the event despite her busy schedule.
The team must shoulder the blame for failing to meet their goals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bear: to accept or deal with something difficult or unpleasant
  • antonym – evade: to avoid accepting or dealing with something
  • synonym – undertake: to take upon oneself, as a responsibility or task
