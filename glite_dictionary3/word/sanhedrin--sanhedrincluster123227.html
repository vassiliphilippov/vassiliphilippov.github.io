Sanhedrin

noun
supreme council of ancient Jerusalem
Pronunciation: / sænˈhiːdrɪn, sænˈhɛdrɪn / (AmE), / sanˈhiːdrɪn, sanˈhɛdrɪn / (BrE)
the supreme judicial and religious council in ancient Jerusalem, particularly during the time of the Second Temple.

Examples

the Sanhedrin council
According to some accounts, Jesus was brought before the Sanhedrin for questioning.
The Sanhedrin played a central role in the religious affairs of ancient Judaism.
The decisions of the Sanhedrin were regarded as final in legal and religious matters.
