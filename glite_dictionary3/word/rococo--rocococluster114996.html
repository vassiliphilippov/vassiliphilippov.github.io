rococo

noun
style of ornate art and architecture
Pronunciation: / rəˈkoʊˌkoʊ / (AmE), / rəˈkəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
an ornate, decorative style of art, architecture, and design characterized by elaborate, graceful, and asymmetrical ornamentation, especially popular in 18th-century France

Examples

rococo furniture style
Collectors admired the delicate carvings and gilded details of the rococo furniture.
The museum's new exhibit features paintings from the rococo period.
The palace was decorated in the rococo style, with elaborate gold trim and pastel colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – baroque: an earlier, similarly ornate style in art and architecture
