to smudge

verb
make a dirty or blurred mark
Pronunciation: / smʌdʒ / (AmE), / smʌdʒ / (BrE)
to make a dirty or blurred mark on something; to soil or blur by touching or rubbing

Examples

to smudge ink
If you smudge the text, it might be hard to read.
Please be careful not to smudge the freshly painted surface with your fingers.
She accidentally smudged her makeup while putting on her coat.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clean: remove dirt or marks; make clean
  • synonym – stain: mark or discolor with something that is not easily removed
