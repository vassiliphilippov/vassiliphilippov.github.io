population

noun
entire group studied (statistics)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Statistics) The complete set of individuals or items from which samples may be drawn for study.

Examples

study population
A statistical inference can be made about the population based on data from a sample.
The researchers randomly selected 500 people from the population for their study.
When calculating the mean, it's important to distinguish between a sample and the entire population.
