canalisation

noun
the construction or conversion to canals
Pronunciation: / kəˌnæl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌnal.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of constructing canals or converting an area so that it is served by canals, especially for irrigation or navigation

Examples

canalisation of rivers
Government investment in canalisation aimed to enhance inland transportation in the 19th century.
The canalisation of the river transformed the region’s agricultural potential.
The canalisation project was completed after five years of extensive engineering work.
