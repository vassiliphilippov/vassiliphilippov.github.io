proof

noun
measure of alcohol strength
Pronunciation: / pruːf / (AmE), / pruːf / (BrE)
a measure of the strength of alcoholic beverages, equal to twice the percentage of alcohol by volume

Examples

proof alcohol level
Beverages with a higher proof are typically stronger and more potent.
The proof of rum sold in the U.S. is often different from that in other countries.
This whiskey is labeled as 80 proof, meaning it contains 40% alcohol by volume.

Related Terms

  • synonym – alcohol by volume: the percentage of ethanol present in an alcoholic beverage
