to outbrave

verb
to surpass in bravery or courage
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈbreɪv / (AmE), / aʊtˈbreɪv / (BrE)
to be braver or show more courage than someone else

Examples

Despite being outnumbered, she outbraved her opponents during the debate.
He outbraved his fears and stepped onto the stage to perform.
The small dog outbraved the larger animals to defend its owner.
No one could outbrave the old captain in the face of danger.
She managed to outbrave all her competitors during the difficult climb.
The soldiers tried to outbrave each other on the battlefield.
