to exaggerate

verb
do something excessively
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzadʒəreɪt / (BrE)
to make something seem larger, better, worse, or more extreme than it really is, either by action or description

Examples

to exaggerate a story
Don't exaggerate the decorations; sometimes less is more.
He tends to exaggerate his workout routine and ends up feeling exhausted.
If you exaggerate your efforts, you might injure yourself.
He tends to exaggerate his skills on his resume.
Reporters sometimes exaggerate the importance of minor events.
She admitted she had exaggerated the size of the crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overdo: to do something to an excessive degree; to do too much
  • synonym – overstate: to state something in too strong terms; exaggerate
  • antonym – understate: to describe something as less important than it really is
