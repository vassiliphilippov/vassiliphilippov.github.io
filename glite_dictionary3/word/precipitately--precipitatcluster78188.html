precipitately

adverb
with excessive haste or speed
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪpɪtətli / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtətli / (BrE)
with excessive haste or at great speed, often without careful thought.

Examples

leave precipitately after news
He exited the room precipitately, clearly upset by the news.
She acted precipitately, agreeing to the plan before considering all the details.
The executives left the meeting precipitately when the fire alarm sounded.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deliberately: in a careful or unhurried way, intentionally
  • synonym – hurriedly: in a hurried or rushed manner
  • synonym – recklessly: without regard to danger or consequences
