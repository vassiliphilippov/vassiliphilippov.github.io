Tunker

noun
member of a religious sect; Christian baptizer by immersion
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋkə / (BrE)
A member of a Christian group, particularly the German Baptist Brethren, known for practicing baptism by immersion.

Examples

Tunker religious group
As a Tunker, he followed the Brethren practice of nonviolence and communal worship.
Many Tunker communities settled in Pennsylvania during the 18th century.
The Tunker tradition emphasizes baptism by completely immersing the believer in water.
