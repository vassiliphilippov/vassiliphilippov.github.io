paraleipsis

noun
rhetorical device: professed omission to draw attention
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈlaɪpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈlaɪpsɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which attention is called to something by professing to omit or ignore it.

Examples

paraleipsis in a speech
The senator used paraleipsis to bring up her opponent’s scandals, saying she wouldn’t dignify them with discussion.
Writers sometimes use paraleipsis to imply something without stating it outright.
‘I won’t mention’ is a classic phrase employed to signal paraleipsis in speeches and writing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – apophasis: rhetorical device of mentioning something by stating that one will not mention it
