resuspension

noun
act of particles being stirred up again
Pronunciation: / ˌri.səˈspɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.sʌsˈpɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which previously settled or precipitated particles are reintroduced into suspension in a fluid

Examples

resuspension of particles
Heavy rainfall can trigger the resuspension of pollutants in river water.
Laboratory tests measured the resuspension of dust when the air was agitated.
Proper handling helps prevent the resuspension of hazardous particles during cleanup.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suspension: the state in which particles are dispersed throughout a fluid
