to occult

verb
to hide from view
Pronunciation: / əˈkʌlt /; / əˈkʌlt, ɑːˈkʌlt / (AmE), / əˈkʌlt /; / əˈkʌlt, ɒˈkʌlt / (BrE)
to hide or block from view, especially by passing in front of something

Examples

to occult the sun
During the solar eclipse, the moon occulted the sun, casting a shadow over the Earth.
The leaves of the plant occult the nest from predators.
The thick clouds occulted the moon, making the night especially dark.
Astronomers observed as the moon occulted the bright star during last night's event.
Careful timing is required to photograph a star as it is occulted by an asteroid.
Jupiter's moon is expected to occult the distant planet, making it invisible for several minutes.
As the planet transits the sun, it momentarily occults a distant star from astronomers' telescopes.
During the lunar eclipse, the shadow of the Earth occulted the Moon completely.
The passing clouds briefly occult the stars, making them disappear from view.
