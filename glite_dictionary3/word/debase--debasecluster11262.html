to debase

verb
to corrupt morally or lower in character
Pronunciation: / dɪˈbeɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪs / (BrE)
to lower in moral quality or corrupt someone's character, especially through indulgence or sensuality

Examples

to debase a person
Exposure to greed and dishonesty can debase even the most honorable individuals.
He feared that luxury and excess might debase the character of the nation.
Some critics argued that the new media would debase the morals of young people.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrupt: cause to become morally depraved
  • synonym – debauch: lead into moral corruption or excessive indulgence
  • antonym – uplift: improve the moral quality or status of someone
