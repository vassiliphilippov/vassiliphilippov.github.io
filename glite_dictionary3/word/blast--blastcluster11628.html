to blast

verb
produce a loud, strident sound
Pronunciation: / blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
to produce a loud, harsh, or strident sound; to emit or cause to emit such a noise, often suddenly or forcefully

Examples

to blast loud music
She tends to blast her voice in crowded rooms, overpowering the conversation.
The alarm suddenly blasted through the quiet building, making everyone jump.
The horn blasted so loudly that it startled the entire street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blare: to make a loud, harsh sound
  • synonym – shriek: to make a high-pitched, piercing sound
