yarmulka

noun
Jewish skullcap
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑrməlkə / (AmE), / ˈjɑːmʊlkə / (BrE)
A small, round skullcap worn by religious Jewish men, especially during prayer, as a sign of reverence.

Examples

wear a yarmulka at synagogue
At the synagogue, David placed his yarmulka on his head before entering the prayer hall.
Some Jewish men wear a yarmulka at all times, while others only during religious services.
The rabbi adjusted his yarmulka as he greeted the congregation.
