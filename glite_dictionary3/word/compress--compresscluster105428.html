to compress

verb
make more compact by pressing
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛs / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to press or squeeze something so that it becomes smaller or more compact

Examples

to compress a file
He used his hands to compress the packing material into the suitcase.
Modern algorithms allow us to compress video without losing much quality.
The software can compress large files into a smaller size for easy storage.
She compressed her lips in anger, refusing to speak.
The technician used a special machine to compress the material into thin sheets.
When you compress the files, they take up less disk space.
