void

adjective
having no legal effect
Pronunciation: / vɔɪd / (AmE), / vɔɪd / (BrE)
empty or having no legal effect; not valid

Examples

void contract
Any agreement signed under duress is usually void in the eyes of the law.
If the terms are illegal, that part of the contract is considered void.
The contract was declared void because it lacked a necessary signature.
After the fire, the house stood void and silent, stripped of all life and possessions.
His words echoed through the void hall, emphasizing the emptiness within.
The laboratory was left void of any equipment or furniture after the move.
