caesarian

noun
surgical delivery of a baby
Pronunciation: / siˈzɛriən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːriən / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which a baby is delivered through an incision in the mother's abdomen and uterus

Examples

caesarian operation
After complications during labor, the doctors performed a caesarian to safely deliver the baby.
Her recovery from the caesarian took a few weeks, but both mother and child were healthy.
In some cases, a planned caesarian is recommended for medical reasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym – caesarean section: the surgical operation to deliver a baby through the abdomen and uterus
  • antonym – natural birth: childbirth through the vaginal canal without surgery
