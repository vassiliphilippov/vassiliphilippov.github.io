crystallite

noun
minute, rudimentary crystal in glassy rock
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪt / (BrE)
A minute, rudimentary crystal found in glassy igneous rocks, whose composition and structure are often not fully determined.

Examples

tiny crystallite in rock
Scientists study crystallite formation to understand the cooling history of igneous rocks.
The microscope revealed numerous crystallites embedded in the volcanic glass.
Tiny crystallites are formed as molten rock cools rapidly, preventing the development of larger crystals.
