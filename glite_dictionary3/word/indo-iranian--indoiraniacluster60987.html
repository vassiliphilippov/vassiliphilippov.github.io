Indo-Iranian

noun
branch of the Indo-European languages
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndoʊ ɪˈræniən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ ɪˈrɑːnɪən / (BrE)
the branch of the Indo-European language family that includes the Indic and Iranian language groups

Examples

Indo-Iranian language family
Sanskrit is considered part of the Indo-Iranian language family.
Scholars study the origins of the Indo-Iranian branch to trace ancient migrations.
The Indo-Iranian languages include Hindi, Urdu, Farsi, and Kurdish.

  • degree higher – Indo-European: the large language family that includes most languages of Europe and many in Asia
