dockage

noun
securing a vessel at a dock
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of securing an arriving vessel at a dock with ropes or other moorings

Examples

dockage of a ship
After arrival, the port workers handled the dockage with precision and care.
Proper dockage is crucial during rough weather to prevent damage to the vessel.
The crew completed the dockage of the ship in record time.
