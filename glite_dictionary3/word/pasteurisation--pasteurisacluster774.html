pasteurisation

noun
partial sterilization of food or drink by heat
Pronunciation: / ˌpæs.tʃə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɑːs.tjʊ.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of partially sterilizing food or drink by heating it to a specific temperature to destroy harmful microorganisms without significantly altering its chemical composition

Examples

pasteurisation of milk
All milk sold in stores is pasteurisation is carefully monitored to guarantee safety.
Modern pasteurisation techniques help extend the shelf life of fruit juices.
Without pasteurisation, the risk of contamination in dairy products would be much higher.
