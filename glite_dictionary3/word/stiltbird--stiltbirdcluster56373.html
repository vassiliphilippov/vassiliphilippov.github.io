stiltbird

noun
long-legged wading bird found in ponds and marshes
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪltˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈstɪltˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A long-legged wading bird with three toes, typically found in inland ponds, marshes, or brackish lagoons; especially refers to birds of the genus Himantopus, commonly known as stilts.

Examples

a stiltbird in marshes
Many nature enthusiasts visit the marshes during migration season to see the stiltbird.
The stiltbird is known for its exceptionally long legs and slender build.
We spotted a rare stiltbird wading through the shallow waters of the lagoon.
