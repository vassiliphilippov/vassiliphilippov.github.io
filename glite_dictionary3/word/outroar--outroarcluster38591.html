to outroar

verb
roar louder than
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈrɔr / (AmE), / aʊtˈrɔː / (BrE)
to roar louder than (someone or something); to produce a greater roar than another

Examples

to outroar the crowd
Despite the storm, the cheering crowd managed to outroar the thunder.
Even with their loud voices, the children couldn't outroar the stadium announcer.
No one could outroar the lion in the zoo during feeding time.
