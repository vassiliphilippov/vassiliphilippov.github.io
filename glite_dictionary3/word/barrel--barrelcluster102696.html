barrel

noun
cylindrical liquid container
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
a large, usually cylindrical container, typically made of wood or metal, used for storing or transporting liquids such as wine or oil

Examples

wooden barrel
Rainwater collected in an old barrel beside the shed.
The winery stores its best wine in oak barrels for several years.
They rolled a heavy barrel of oil across the dock.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cask: a large container for storing alcoholic drinks, especially wine or beer
