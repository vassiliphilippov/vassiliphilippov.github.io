amora

noun
Talmudic rabbi (AD 250–500)
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈmoʊrə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɔːrə / (BrE)
a rabbi of a group active in AD 250–500 who discussed and explained Mishnaic law, with their teachings recorded in the Talmud.

Examples

a amora explains law
Each amora was known for interpreting Mishnaic teachings in his own way.
Many Jewish legal traditions derive from debates among the amoraim.
The discussions of the amora shaped much of the content of the Talmud.
