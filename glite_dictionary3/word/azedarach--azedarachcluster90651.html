azedarach

noun
tree with purple blossoms, cultivated for shade
Pronunciation: / ˌæzəˈdæræk / (AmE), / ˌazɛˈdarak / (BrE)
A tree native to northern India and China, known for its purple blossoms and inedible yellow fruit; widely cultivated as a shade tree in warmer regions.

Examples

azedarach tree shade
An azedarach was planted by the old well to provide shade during the hot summer months.
Birds rarely eat the fruit of the azedarach, as it is considered inedible and mildly toxic.
The azedarach produces clusters of purple flowers in the early spring.
