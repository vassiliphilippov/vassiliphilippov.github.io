to foam

verb
produce or become covered with bubbles
Pronunciation: / foʊm / (AmE), / fəʊm / (BrE)
to produce or become covered with a mass of small bubbles; to become frothy or bubbly

Examples

to foam at mouth
He watched the milk foam as he steamed it for his coffee.
The chemicals reacted, causing the liquid to foam rapidly.
The waves began to foam as they crashed against the rocks.
