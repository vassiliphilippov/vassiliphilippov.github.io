to hover

verb
remain in a place longer than necessary
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
to remain in a place longer than necessary, often because of uncertainty or indecision

Examples

hover near the door
He continued to hover awkwardly near the exit even after the meeting had ended.
Reporters hovered around the actress, waiting for her to make a statement.
She tends to hover by the doorway at parties before joining the crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym – depart: to go away or leave a place
  • synonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary
