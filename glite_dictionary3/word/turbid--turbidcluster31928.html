turbid

adjective
cloudy or opaque liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.bɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) cloudy or opaque, especially due to stirred-up sediment or suspended particles.

Examples

turbid water
Her tea turned turbid when she accidentally dropped loose leaves into the cup.
Scientists measured the turbid water to assess pollution levels in the lake.
The river became turbid after the heavy rainfall stirred up the mud at the bottom.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear: free from cloudiness or particles (of liquid)
  • synonym – cloudy: not clear; full of suspended particles (of liquid)
  • synonym – murky: dark or cloudy, especially of liquids
