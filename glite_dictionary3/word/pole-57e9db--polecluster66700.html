pole

noun
either end of a magnet
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
either of the two ends of a magnet or the contact points on an electrical device where current enters or leaves

Examples

magnetic pole end
A compass needle aligns itself with the Earth's magnetic poles.
The north pole of the magnet attracted the iron filings.
When you split a magnet in half, each piece still has a north and south pole.
Each cell in the battery has a positive and a negative pole.
Make sure you connect the positive pole of the battery to the correct terminal.
The device will not work if the wires are attached to the wrong pole.
