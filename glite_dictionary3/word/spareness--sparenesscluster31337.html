spareness

noun
quality of being scanty or sparse
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspɛə.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being scanty, bare, or lacking in density or fullness.

Examples

literary spareness
Many admired the spareness of the poet’s writing style, which left nothing unnecessary.
The spareness of the furniture accentuated the room’s quiet elegance.
The artist’s work is known for its spareness and subtle use of space.

Related Terms

  • antonym – density: the degree of compactness, opposite of spareness
  • synonym – sparsity: the state of being sparse or in short supply
