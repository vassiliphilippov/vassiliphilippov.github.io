pons

noun
part of the brainstem connecting brain regions
Pronunciation: / pɑnz / (AmE), / pɒnz / (BrE)
a part of the brainstem that links the medulla oblongata and the cerebellum with the midbrain, consisting of a band of nerve fibers

Examples

the pons in the brain
Damage to the pons can affect vital functions such as breathing and sleep.
The pons plays a crucial role in relaying signals between different regions of the brain.
The doctor explained that the stroke had affected the patient's pons, leading to difficulty in movement.
