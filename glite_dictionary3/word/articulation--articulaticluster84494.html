articulation

noun
shaping speech sounds with speech organs
Pronunciation: / ɑrˌtɪk.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪk.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the physical process of bringing together speech organs, such as the tongue and lips, to shape sounds during pronunciation

Examples

articulation of sounds
Accent differences are often the result of variations in articulation and intonation.
Her precise articulation made every word in the speech easy to understand.
Speech therapists often focus on improving a child's articulation of difficult sounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enunciation: clear pronunciation of words
  • antonym – mumbling: speaking indistinctly and quietly
  • synonym – pronunciation: the manner in which a word is spoken
