ossicle

noun
small bone, especially in the middle ear
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪkəl / (BrE)
a very small bone, especially one of the three bones in the middle ear (the malleus, incus, and stapes)

Examples

middle ear ossicle
Damage to an ossicle can result in hearing loss.
The surgeon carefully examined the ossicle for signs of damage.
The three ossicles of the middle ear transmit sound vibrations to the inner ear.
