adjusted

adjective
well adapted to environment or circumstances
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) emotionally or socially stable; having adapted well to circumstances

Examples

adjusted to new life
After moving to the city, she quickly became an adjusted member of the community.
He is a well-adjusted adult despite his difficult childhood.
The program helps children become better adjusted to school life.
Although he faced many challenges as a child, he has become a well adjusted adult.
Children who feel secure at home tend to be better adjusted at school.
The counselor praised the students for being socially adjusted and emotionally balanced.

Related Terms

  • antonym – maladjusted: unable to adapt to the environment or circumstances
  • synonym – well-adjusted: (of a person) mentally healthy and able to deal with life well
