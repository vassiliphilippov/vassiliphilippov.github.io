gulfweed

noun
floating brown seaweed of the Atlantic
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌlfˌwid / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlfˌwiːd / (BrE)
A type of brown seaweed (Sargassum) with rounded, air-filled bladders, forming dense floating masses in tropical Atlantic waters such as the Sargasso Sea.

Examples

gulfweed floating at sea
Many marine creatures find food and shelter among the drifting gulfweed in tropical waters.
Massive patches of gulfweed floated on the ocean's surface near the Sargasso Sea.
Sailors reported that their ship became entangled in thick gulfweed during the crossing.
