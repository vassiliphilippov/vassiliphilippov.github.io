laxative

noun
substance that relieves constipation
Pronunciation: / ˈlæk.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈlæk.sə.tɪv / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that helps stimulate or facilitate bowel movements, typically used to relieve constipation.

Examples

take a laxative
After eating too much cheese, he took a laxative as a precaution.
Some herbal teas contain natural laxative ingredients.
The doctor recommended a gentle laxative to relieve her discomfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cathartic: a substance that accelerates defecation
  • antonym – constipating: causing or tending to cause constipation
