resilience

noun
ability to return to original shape
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪliəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪliəns / (BrE)
the ability to return to an original shape after being bent or stretched, or to recover quickly from difficulties or setbacks

Examples

material resilience
Engineers tested the resilience of the new polymer under repeated stress.
Rubber's natural resilience allows it to be stretched and then return to its original shape.
The resilience of steel makes it ideal for use in construction.
After the flood, the community showed remarkable resilience in rebuilding their homes.
The resilience of the rubber made it perfect for use in shock absorbers.
The trampoline's resilience allows it to return to its original shape after each jump.

Related Terms

  • synonym – elasticity: the ability of a material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed
  • antonym – rigidity: the quality of being stiff and not bending or springing back
