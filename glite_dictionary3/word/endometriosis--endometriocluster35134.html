endometriosis

noun
growth of uterine tissue outside the uterus
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˌmiːtriˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˌmiːtrɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside the uterus, often causing pain and sometimes infertility.

Examples

endometriosis causes pain
Many women with endometriosis suffer from chronic pelvic discomfort.
She was diagnosed with endometriosis after experiencing severe abdominal pain.
The doctor discussed various treatment options for patients living with endometriosis.
