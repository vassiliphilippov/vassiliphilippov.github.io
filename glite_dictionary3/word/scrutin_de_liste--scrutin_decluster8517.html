scrutin de liste

noun
proportional party-list electoral system
Pronunciation: / skruːˌtæ̃ də ˈlist / (AmE), / skruːˌtæ̃ də ˈliːst / (BrE)
an electoral system in which voters choose between party lists, with seats allocated to each list according to the proportion of votes received

Examples

scrutin de liste voting system
Political analysts debated the effectiveness of scrutin de liste systems in coalition governments.
Several European countries use the scrutin de liste to ensure fair representation of minority parties.
The election adopted a scrutin de liste, allowing smaller parties to gain parliamentary seats.

Related Terms

  • synonym – proportional representation: an electoral system in which parties gain seats in proportion to the number of votes cast for them
