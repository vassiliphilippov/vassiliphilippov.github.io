unliveried

adjective
not wearing a livery or uniform
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɪvərid / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪvərid / (BrE)
Not wearing a livery or uniform, especially one that is required for a particular role or position.

Examples

unliveried servant
Guests were surprised to see an unliveried footman at the formal event.
She preferred her staff unliveried to create a more relaxed atmosphere.
The estate's new driver was unliveried, unlike the others who wore crisp uniforms.
