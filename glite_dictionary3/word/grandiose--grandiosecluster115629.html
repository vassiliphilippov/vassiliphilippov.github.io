grandiose

adjective
pretentiously grand or elaborate
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræn.diˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.di.əʊs /; / ˈɡræn.dɪ.əʊs / (BrE)
having an excessively grand, elaborate, or impressive style, often in a way that is meant to impress or seems pretentious or exaggerated

Examples

grandiose plans
His grandiose plans for the new building were far beyond their actual needs.
She spoke in a grandiose tone, as if she were addressing royalty.
They dismissed the proposal as too grandiose for the available budget.
He often makes grandiose promises that he fails to keep.
Her grandiose plans for the party ended up being impractical and costly.
The architect designed a grandiose building that was criticized for being overly extravagant.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modest: having a humble or moderate opinion of oneself; not grand or boastful
  • synonym – pretentious: attempting to impress by affecting greater importance than is actually possessed
