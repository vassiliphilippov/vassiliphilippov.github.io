metic

noun
resident alien in ancient Greece
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
In ancient Greece, a foreign resident in a city-state, especially Athens, who paid a tax or fee for the right to live there without full citizenship.

Examples

Athenian metic resident
Although a metic could live and work in the city, he was denied the rights of citizenship.
In ancient Greece, metics were often involved in trade and crafts.
The city of Athens had a large population of metics who contributed to its economy.
