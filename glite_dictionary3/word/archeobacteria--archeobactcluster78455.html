archeobacteria

noun
ancient group of microorganisms (Archaea)
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrkiˌoʊbækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiəʊbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a group of ancient microorganisms that evolved separately from true bacteria and cyanobacteria, now generally referred to as Archaea

Examples

archeobacteria in hot springs
Scientists study archeobacteria to understand the origins of life on Earth.
The reclassification of archeobacteria has led to them being placed in their own domain, Archaea.
Unlike typical bacteria, archeobacteria can survive in extreme environments such as hot springs and salt lakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – archaea: a domain of single-celled microorganisms, genetically distinct from bacteria
  • antonym – bacteria: a large group of single-celled microorganisms distinct from archaea
