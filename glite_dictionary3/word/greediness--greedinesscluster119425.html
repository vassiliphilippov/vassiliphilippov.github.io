greediness

noun
preference for local choices in algorithms
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːdinəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdinəs / (BrE)
(in computer science) the tendency of an algorithm or heuristic to choose the locally best option at each step, possibly causing it to miss a globally optimal solution.

Examples

greediness in algorithms
The algorithm's greediness caused it to accept a quick solution instead of searching for a better one.
Researchers measured the greediness of several heuristics to compare their performance on large graphs.
Reducing the algorithm's greediness by adding lookahead improved results on many benchmark problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exhaustive search: a search that examines all possible solutions (contrasts with a greedy approach)
  • synonym – greedy algorithm: an algorithmic approach that makes a locally optimal choice at each step
