paramedic

noun
emergency medical care provider
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əˈmɛd.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈmed.ɪk / (BrE)
a person who is trained to provide emergency medical care and assist medical professionals, especially at the scene of an accident or emergency

Examples

paramedic treats injuries
A team of paramedics arrived within minutes to provide emergency care.
She decided to become a paramedic after witnessing a car crash.
The paramedic quickly assessed the patient's injuries at the scene of the accident.
