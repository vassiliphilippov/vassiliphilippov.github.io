embargo

noun
government trade restriction
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
an official government order that restricts or prohibits trade with a particular country or the exchange of specific goods

Examples

an embargo on goods
Due to the embargo, many factories faced shortages of essential materials.
The United Nations voted to lift the oil embargo after the peace agreement was signed.
The government imposed an embargo on all arms shipments to the country.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ban: an official or legal prohibition
  • synonym – boycott: an act of refusing to do business with a country, organization, or person
