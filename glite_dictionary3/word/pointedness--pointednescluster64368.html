pointedness

noun
quality of being intentionally directed or pointed
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪntɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of a remark, look, or action being obviously directed at a particular person or thing, often in a critical or significant way

Examples

pointedness in a comment
Critics noted the pointedness with which the author addressed social issues in her novel.
The pointedness of her comment made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
You could not ignore the pointedness in his tone during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bluntness: the quality of being uncompromisingly forthright or direct, often lacking subtlety or tact
