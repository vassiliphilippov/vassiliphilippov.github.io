shred

noun
tiny amount; small piece
Pronunciation: / ʃrɛd / (AmE), / ʃrɛd / (BrE)
a very small piece or amount of something, either physical (like paper or food) or abstract (like hope)

Examples

a shred of hope
Even a shred of hope kept her going during tough times.
He didn't show a shred of remorse for his actions.
There wasn't a shred of evidence to support the rumor.
After tearing the letter, he threw every shred away.
Not a shred of evidence was left behind at the scene.
She kept a shred of cloth from her childhood blanket as a keepsake.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bit: a small amount or piece
  • synonym – fragment: a small part broken or separated off something
  • synonym – scrap: a small piece or amount of something, especially one that is left over after the greater part has been used
