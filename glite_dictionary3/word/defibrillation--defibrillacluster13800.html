defibrillation

noun
medical treatment to stop abnormal heart rhythm
Pronunciation: / diˌfɪbrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌfɪbrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical treatment that stops fibrillation of the heart muscles, usually by delivering an electric shock with a defibrillator

Examples

defibrillation after cardiac arrest
Paramedics performed defibrillation when the patient was found unresponsive and without a pulse.
Prompt defibrillation can increase the chances of survival after a cardiac arrest.
Thanks to early defibrillation, she made a full recovery from her sudden heart emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cardioversion: restoring normal heart rhythm by electrical shock
  • antonym – fibrillation: rapid and irregular contraction of heart muscles
