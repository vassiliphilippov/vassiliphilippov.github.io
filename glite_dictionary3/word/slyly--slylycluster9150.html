slyly

adverb
in a cunning or secretive manner
Pronunciation: / ˈslaɪli / (AmE), / ˈslaɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that is cunning or clever, often with an intent to deceive or achieve something secretly.

Examples

to slyly avoid questions
He slyly changed the topic to avoid answering the question.
She slyly smiled before revealing her surprise for the team.
The child slyly hid the cookie behind his back so nobody would see it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – craftily: in a crafty, cunning manner
  • antonym – innocently: in an innocent, harmless manner
