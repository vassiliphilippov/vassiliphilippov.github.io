nervus spinalis

noun
nerve from the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs spaɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs spaɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
any of the 31 pairs of nerves that originate from each side of the spinal cord, each connected by ventral and dorsal roots.

Examples

nervus spinalis injury
An infection may affect a nervus spinalis, leading to numbness or pain along the nerve.
Damage to a nervus spinalis can result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Each nervus spinalis carries both sensory and motor information to and from the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spinal nerve: any of the paired nerves that emerge from the spinal cord
