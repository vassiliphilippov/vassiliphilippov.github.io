to eject

verb
leave an aircraft by ejection seat
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to leave an aircraft rapidly by means of an ejection seat or capsule, usually in an emergency

Examples

to eject from aircraft
She was trained to eject in emergencies during her years as a test pilot.
The pilot had to eject when the engine failed over the mountains.
Two crew members ejected safely moments before the fighter jet crashed.
