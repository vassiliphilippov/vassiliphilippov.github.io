to offset

verb
to print using offset printing
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)
to produce (printed material) by means of offset printing, a technique in which ink is transferred from a plate to a rubber blanket and then to the printing surface

Examples

to offset a magazine
Many scientific journals are still offset rather than printed digitally.
The publisher decided to offset the annual report for higher quality.
These brochures were offset last week at a local print shop.
