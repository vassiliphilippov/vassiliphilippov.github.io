stool

noun
tree stump for regrowth (forestry)
Pronunciation: / stuːl / (AmE), / stuːl / (BrE)
(Forestry) The stump of a tree that has been cut down or cut back, from which new shoots or saplings grow.

Examples

tree stool
After felling, new shoots quickly sprouted from the old stool to form a dense thicket.
Experienced woodcutters know which stool will produce the straightest saplings.
The forester carefully marked each stool to encourage healthy coppicing in spring.
