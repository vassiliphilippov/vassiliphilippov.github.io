stoup

noun
basin for holy water in a church
Pronunciation: / stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
A basin, usually at the entrance of a church, containing holy water for worshippers to dip their fingers in.

Examples

church stoup of water
During renovations, the ancient stoup was carefully restored to its original condition.
He dipped his fingers into the stoup and crossed himself before entering the sanctuary.
The old church had a marble stoup by the entrance, filled with holy water.
