Tabernacles

proper noun
Jewish festival commemorating the Israelites' shelter in the wilderness
Pronunciation: / ˈtæbərˌnækəlz / (AmE), / ˈtabənakəlz / (BrE)
A major Jewish festival, also called Sukkot, commemorating the shelter of the Israelites during their 40 years in the wilderness, celebrated beginning on the 15th of Tishri.

Examples

Tabernacles festival celebration
During Tabernacles, it is customary to build and dwell in temporary shelters called sukkahs.
Every autumn, Jews around the world celebrate Tabernacles with family gatherings and special meals.
Schools in Israel often close for the week of Tabernacles to allow families to observe the festival.
