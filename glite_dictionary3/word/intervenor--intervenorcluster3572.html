intervenor

noun
party joining a legal proceeding
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈviːnər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈviːnə / (BrE)
(law) a person or entity who enters an ongoing legal proceeding to protect an interest or present a viewpoint.

Examples

intervenor joins lawsuit
A trade association filed as an intervenor in the environmental lawsuit.
The court allowed an intervenor to participate in the hearing to represent local residents' concerns.
Without the intervenor, important evidence might not have been considered in the final judgment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defendant: a person against whom a legal action is brought
  • synonym – litigant: a party engaged in a lawsuit
  • synonym – plaintiff: a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
