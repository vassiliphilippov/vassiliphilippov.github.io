to euphemise

verb
use a euphemism for something
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːfəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːfɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to refer to something by using a mild or indirect word or phrase instead of one considered harsh or blunt

Examples

to euphemise bad news
Journalists sometimes euphemise unpleasant facts to avoid causing alarm.
Rather than say the patient died, the doctor euphemised it as 'passed away.'
The report euphemised the layoffs as 'workforce optimization.'
