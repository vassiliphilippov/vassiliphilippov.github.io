Hesperides

proper noun
nymphs guarding Hera's golden apples (mythology)
Pronunciation: / hɛˈspɛrɪˌdiz / (AmE), / hɛˈspɛrɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a group of nymphs who guarded the golden apples in a garden at the western edge of the world, given to Hera as a wedding gift by Gaea.

Examples

the Hesperides guard apples
Artists often depict the Hesperides with the dragon Ladon guarding the sacred fruit.
Hercules was tasked with stealing apples from the garden of the Hesperides as one of his labors.
The legend says that the Hesperides tended a beautiful garden full of golden apples.
