bitt

noun
post for securing mooring lines (nautical)
Pronunciation: / bɪt / (AmE), / bɪt / (BrE)
a strong post on a ship or quay for securing mooring lines

Examples

bitt on deck
A heavy chain was fastened to the bitt at the edge of the pier.
The crew tied the rope securely around the bitt on the deck.
When docking the vessel, the captain instructed the sailors to use the bitt for the mooring lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bollard: a short post used to prevent vehicles from entering an area or for mooring boats
