to squint

verb
to cross the eyes
Pronunciation: / skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
to have or make your eyes look in different directions (deliberately or because of a medical condition)

Examples

to squint both eyes
He squinted on purpose to amuse his little sister.
If you squint like that for too long, your eyes might hurt.
The children made faces and squinted at each other for fun.
Her daughter used to squint before she had corrective surgery.
People who squint may have trouble focusing on distant objects.
The doctor noticed the child began to squint during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cross one's eyes: to turn one's eyes inward so they point toward the nose
  • synonym – cross-eyed: having eyes that turn inwards or outwards rather than looking straight ahead
  • synonym – strabismus: a condition in which the eyes do not properly align with each other
