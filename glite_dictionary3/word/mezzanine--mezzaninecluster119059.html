mezzanine

noun
intermediate floor above ground level
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛz.əˌnin / (AmE), / ˈmɛz.əniːn / (BrE)
an intermediate floor between main floors of a building, typically just above the ground floor

Examples

mezzanine level
A spiral staircase connects the ground floor with the mezzanine above.
Our new office occupies the mezzanine overlooking the lobby.
The restaurant added extra seating on a mezzanine level.
