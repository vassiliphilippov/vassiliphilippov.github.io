RFD

noun
request to delete online content
Pronunciation: / ɑr ɛf di / (AmE), / ɑː ɛf diː / (BrE)
(in online communities) a formal request to remove or delete a post, comment, or other content, typically submitted to moderators or administrators.

Examples

submit RFD request
The moderator filed an RFD to delete the outdated question after community discussion.
Several users supported the RFD and added comments explaining why the post violated policy.
Before approving, the admin reviewed the RFD thread and checked the reported evidence carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym – take-down request: a request to remove content from a website or platform
