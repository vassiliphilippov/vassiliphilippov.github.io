to unsanctify

verb
remove sacred or holy status
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsæŋktɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaŋktɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove the sacred or holy status from something; to deprive of sanctity

Examples

to unsanctify a church
After years of misuse, the site was officially unsanctified by the clergy.
Rituals are sometimes held to unsanctify objects before they can be used for secular purposes.
The council decided to unsanctify the old chapel before its demolition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sanctify: to make holy or sacred
