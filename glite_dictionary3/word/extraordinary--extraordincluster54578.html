extraordinary

adjective
very unusual or remarkable
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstrɔːrdəˌneri /; / ɪkˈstrɔr.dəˌner.i / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrɔːdnri /; / ɪkˈstrɔː.dɪ.nər.i / (BrE)
very unusual, remarkable, or much greater than what is normal or expected

Examples

extraordinary courage
She showed extraordinary courage during the rescue.
The chef prepared an extraordinary meal that impressed all the guests.
Winning three gold medals in one day is an extraordinary achievement.
Her extraordinary talent for music was obvious to everyone who heard her play.
It was an extraordinary coincidence that they met in such a remote place.
The scientists made an extraordinary discovery that changed our understanding of the disease.
