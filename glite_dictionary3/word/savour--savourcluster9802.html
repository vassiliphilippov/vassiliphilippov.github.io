to savour

verb
taste or enjoy something fully
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪvər / (AmE), / ˈseɪvə / (BrE)
to enjoy or appreciate something fully, especially by taking time to notice its taste, quality, or experience

Examples

to savour every moment
He likes to savour every spoonful of soup slowly.
She paused to savour the taste of freshly baked bread.
Take your time and savour the moment.
He paused to savour the victory after months of hard training.
She wanted to savour every moment of her holiday before returning to work.
You should savour this meal; it was prepared with great care.

Related Terms

  • synonym – relish: to enjoy greatly; to take pleasure in
