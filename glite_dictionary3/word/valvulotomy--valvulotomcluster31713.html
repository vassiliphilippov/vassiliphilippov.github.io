valvulotomy

noun
surgical incision into a heart valve
Pronunciation: / ˌvælvjəˈlɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌvalvjʊˈlɒtəmi / (BrE)
A surgical incision made into a stenosed heart valve to relieve obstruction to blood flow.

Examples

valvulotomy for heart valve
Advancements in minimally invasive techniques have made valvulotomy safer for elderly patients.
Following the valvulotomy, the patient's symptoms of heart failure significantly improved.
The surgeon performed a valvulotomy to treat the patient's narrowed mitral valve.
