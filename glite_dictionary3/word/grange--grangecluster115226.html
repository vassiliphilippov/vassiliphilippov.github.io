grange

noun
country farm or farmhouse
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɡreɪndʒ / (BrE)
a farm, especially a large farmhouse with its associated buildings, typically situated in the countryside and often historically associated with a manor or religious establishment

Examples

old grange building
Medieval monasteries often managed a grange several miles from the main abbey.
She inherited a small grange that had once belonged to her great-grandfather.
The old grange stood alone at the edge of the fields, surrounded by haystacks and livestock.
