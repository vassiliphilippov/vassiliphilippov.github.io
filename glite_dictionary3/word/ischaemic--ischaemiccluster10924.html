ischaemic

adjective
relating to inadequate blood supply
Pronunciation: / ɪsˈkiːmɪk / (AmE), / ɪsˈkiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by ischemia (an inadequate blood supply to an organ or part of the body).

Examples

ischaemic heart disease
Chronic ischaemic heart disease is a major cause of mortality worldwide.
Doctors identified the tissue as ischaemic due to the sudden loss of blood flow.
The patient suffered an ischaemic stroke that required immediate treatment.
