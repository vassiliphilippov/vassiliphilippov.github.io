osteoclasis

noun
intentional surgical bone fracture
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːstiˈɑːkləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈɒkləsɪs / (BrE)
the medical treatment of a skeletal deformity by intentionally fracturing a bone to allow for realignment or correction

Examples

osteoclasis procedure correction
Osteoclasis is sometimes considered when less invasive treatments fail to address bone malformations.
Early methods of osteoclasis have been refined with advances in orthopedic surgery.
