transsexualism

noun
condition of identifying with a gender different from assigned sex
Pronunciation: / trænˈsɛksjuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trænˈseksjʊəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a condition in which a person experiences a persistent identification with a gender different from their assigned physical sex, often seeking to transition to the identified gender

Examples

transsexualism diagnosis
She read extensively about transsexualism before coming out as transgender.
Some medical professionals historically used transsexualism as a diagnosis for people seeking gender transition.
The understanding and treatment of transsexualism have evolved significantly over the past decades.
