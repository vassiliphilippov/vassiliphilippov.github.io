to shoot

verb
interweave with different colored threads (textiles)
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt / (AmE), / ʃuːt / (BrE)
(textiles) To variegate by interweaving weft threads of different colors.

Examples

to shoot colored threads
The artisan decided to shoot the fabric with threads of gold and silver for a shimmering effect.
The tapestry was shot through with blue and red, giving it a vibrant appearance.
They would often shoot the cloth in bold colors to create eye-catching patterns.
