counterfoil

noun
retained part of a document for record
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
the part of a check, ticket, or other document that is kept as a record after the main section is given away or used

Examples

counterfoil of a ticket
Each ticket's counterfoil is numbered for tracking purposes.
Please keep the counterfoil as proof of your payment.
The bank teller asked to see the counterfoil from my checkbook.
