troubler

noun
person who causes trouble
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌblɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌblə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes trouble or disturbance

Examples

a troubler at work
Her reputation as a troubler made team meetings tense and unpredictable.
Some saw him as a troubler, while others admired his courage to challenge the rules.
The troubler in the group was always finding ways to start arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – peacemaker: a person who tries to create harmony or stop conflict
  • synonym – troublemaker: a person who causes difficulties or problems, often deliberately
