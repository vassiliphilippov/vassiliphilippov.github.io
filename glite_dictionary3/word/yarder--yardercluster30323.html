yarder

noun
something of a specified yard length (in combinations)
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdə / (BrE)
(chiefly in combination) something that measures a specified number of yards in length, height, or distance, especially a shot, object, or event related to that measure.

Examples

a 40-yarder pass
He completed a 40-yarder pass to score a touchdown.
That field goal was a tough 50-yarder in windy conditions.
The golfer hit a massive 300-yarder down the fairway.
