whale

noun
large marine mammal with a blowhole
Pronunciation: / weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
a very large marine mammal with a streamlined body and a blowhole on the head for breathing, belonging to the order Cetacea

Examples

blue whale
A blue whale can weigh more than 150 tons and is the largest animal on Earth.
Scientists are tracking the migration patterns of whales across the Pacific Ocean.
We spotted a pod of whales breaching off the coast during our boat trip.
