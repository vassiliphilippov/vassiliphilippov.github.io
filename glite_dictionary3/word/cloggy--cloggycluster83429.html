cloggy

adjective
compact, heavy soil texture
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑɡi / (AmE), / ˈklɒɡi / (BrE)
(of soil) compact and fine-grained, often heavy and tending to retain water.

Examples

cloggy soil
After the rain, the cloggy field made walking difficult for the players.
Some crops struggle to grow in cloggy soils that do not drain well.
Tom found it hard to dig through the cloggy earth in the new garden bed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clayey: consisting of or resembling clay, often heavy or sticky soil
  • antonym – sandy: (of soil) composed mostly of sand, loose and coarse
