undercurrent

noun
hidden or subtle underlying emotion or tendency
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌkɝːrənt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌrənt / (BrE)
a hidden or subdued feeling or tendency existing beneath the surface of what is openly shown or said

Examples

undercurrent of emotion
Despite her cheerful words, I sensed an undercurrent of sadness in her tone.
The speech contained an undercurrent of warning, even if it sounded optimistic on the surface.
There was an undercurrent of hostility in their conversation, though they remained polite.
