beak

noun
hard pointed mouthpart of a bird
Pronunciation: / bik / (AmE), / biːk / (BrE)
the hard, pointed mouthpart of a bird or similar animals (such as turtles or squids), used for eating and other functions

Examples

bird's beak
Parrots have a curved beak which helps them crack nuts and seeds.
The eagle used its sharp beak to tear the meat from its prey.
The robin wiped its beak on a branch after catching an insect.
A squid's beak is strong enough to break through shells.
Some mollusks use their sharp beak to tear food apart.
The snapping turtle has a powerful beak to catch its prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bill: the beak of a bird, especially a web-footed one
