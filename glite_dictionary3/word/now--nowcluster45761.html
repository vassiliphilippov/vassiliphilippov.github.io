now

adverb
used narratively for past events (historical present)
Pronunciation: / naʊ / (AmE), / naʊ / (BrE)
Used with the present tense to narrate a series of past events, especially in historical or literary recounting (the historical present).

Examples

he now says
In her memoir, she writes: He now stands and addresses the crowd, his voice trembling.
The explorer now faces the river, unsure whether to cross in the darkness.
The general now orders his troops forward, completely changing the momentum of the battle.

  • antonym – then: at that time (in the past or future)
