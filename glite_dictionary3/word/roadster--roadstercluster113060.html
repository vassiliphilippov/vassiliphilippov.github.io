roadster

noun
open two-seater automobile
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊd.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd.stə / (BrE)
an open car with two seats, often with an additional folding seat behind, typically designed for driving pleasure rather than practicality.

Examples

classic roadster car
He restored a vintage roadster from the 1930s and takes it to classic car shows.
She always dreamed of driving down the coast in a red roadster with the top down.
The roadster provided little protection from the rain, but its style was unmatched.

Related Terms

  • synonym – convertible: a car with a folding or detachable roof
  • antonym – coupe: a car with a fixed roof and two doors
