one-party

adjective
controlled by a single political party
Pronunciation: / wʌn ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / wʌn ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
(of a system, event, or decision) controlled or determined by a single political party.

Examples

one-party system
A one-party election lacks real political competition.
He criticized the one-party rule for stifling dissent and debate.
The country became a one-party state after banning all opposition groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym – multi-party: involving or controlled by more than one political party
  • synonym – one-party state: a state controlled by a single political party
