Phidias

proper noun
famous ancient Greek sculptor
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪdɪəs / (BrE)
(c. 500–432 BC) An ancient Greek sculptor, painter, and architect, renowned for his large-scale statues and artistic contributions to ancient Athens.

Examples

Phidias created statues
Phidias designed the massive statue of Zeus at Olympia, considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Many art historians regard Phidias as the greatest sculptor of classical antiquity.
The Parthenon's elaborate sculptures were overseen by Phidias, whose influence is still evident today.
