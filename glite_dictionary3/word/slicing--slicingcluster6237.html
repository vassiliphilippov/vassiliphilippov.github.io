slicing

noun
3D printing layer-preparation process
Pronunciation: / ˈslaɪ.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslaɪ.sɪŋ / (BrE)
(in 3D printing and additive manufacturing) the process of converting a 3D digital model into a series of thin layers and printer instructions (often G-code) so a printer can build the object.

Examples

slicing a 3D model
The engineer adjusted the slicing settings to improve print strength before sending the file to the printer.
After slicing, the software exported G-code that told the printer where to deposit each layer precisely.
Hobbyists often share recommended slicing profiles to help others avoid failed prints and save time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – layering: dividing a model into successive thin layers for additive manufacturing
  • synonym – slicer: software that performs slicing by converting 3D models into layers and printer instructions
