challah

noun
sweet, braided Jewish bread
Pronunciation: / ˈxɑːlə, ˈhɑːlə / (AmE), / ˈxɑːlə, ˈhɑːlə / (BrE)
A type of sweet, white bread made with eggs and yeast, usually braided and glazed with egg before baking, traditionally eaten in Jewish culture.

Examples

fresh challah bread
At the bakery, I bought a fresh challah with a golden crust.
She baked a beautiful challah for the Sabbath dinner.
We shared slices of challah dipped in honey during the holiday celebration.
