flit

noun
secret departure to evade debts
Pronunciation: / flɪt / (AmE), / flɪt / (BrE)
an act of secretly leaving one's home or lodgings to avoid paying debts or being detected

Examples

make a flit at night
Faced with mounting debts, he considered making a flit to start anew elsewhere.
Rumors spread quickly after the shopkeeper's sudden flit from town.
The tenants did a flit in the middle of the night, leaving the rent unpaid.

Related Terms

  • antonym – eviction: the action of expelling someone from a property
  • synonym – runaway: a person who has run away, especially to avoid trouble or payment
