back-formation

noun
word formed by removing an affix
Pronunciation: / ˌbæk fɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbæk fəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a word that is formed by removing a real or supposed affix from another word, often on the mistaken assumption that the longer word is derived from it

Examples

back-formation from a noun
He explained that 'burgle' is a back-formation from 'burglar'.
Many English verbs are created by back-formation from existing nouns.
The verb edit is a back-formation from the noun 'editor'.
