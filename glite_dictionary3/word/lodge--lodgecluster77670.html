to lodge

verb
to make something stuck or embedded
Pronunciation: / lɑdʒ / (AmE), / lɒdʒ / (BrE)
to become stuck or embed something firmly in a particular place

Examples

to lodge a bullet
A piece of glass became lodged in his foot after the accident.
He managed to lodge the axe head deep into the log with a single swing.
The bone was lodged in her throat, making it difficult to breathe.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dislodge: to remove (something) from a fixed position
  • synonym – embed: to fix firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
