separatism

noun
the policy or practice of separation for minority groups
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛp.ə.rəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.ər.ə.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
The policy or practice of maintaining separate institutions or facilities for different groups, especially for minority groups within a society.

Examples

institutional separatism
Civil rights activists campaigned against institutionalized separatism in public facilities.
Modern societies generally reject separatism and seek greater social integration.
The government was criticized for its support of separatism in the education system.

Related Terms

  • antonym – integration: the process of combining or incorporating different groups into a unified whole
