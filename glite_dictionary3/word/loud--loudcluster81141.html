loud

adjective, adverb
high volume in music
Pronunciation: / laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
(of sound, music, or manner) making a lot of noise or strong in volume; noisily or with high intensity

Examples

loud music
During the crescendo, the choir sang loud to fill the entire hall with sound.
The composer marked the passage loud to emphasize the power of the melody.
The score instructs the orchestra to play this section loud for dramatic effect.
He called loud for help when he saw the fire.
Please don't play your music so loud at night.

Please keep your voices down; don't be loud in the library.
The music at the concert was so loud that I could feel the floor vibrating.
The thunder was so loud last night that it woke everyone in the house.
