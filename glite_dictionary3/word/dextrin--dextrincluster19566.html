dextrin

noun
substance from starch used as thickener/adhesive
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkstrɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɛkstrɪn / (BrE)
a group of tasteless, odorless, gummy substances produced by the breakdown of starch, used as thickeners, adhesives, and in dietary supplements

Examples

dextrin powder
Dextrin is sometimes added to nutritional supplements for extra fiber.
Because of its adhesive properties, dextrin is used in the production of envelopes and stamps.
The manufacturer uses dextrin as a thickening agent in several processed foods.
