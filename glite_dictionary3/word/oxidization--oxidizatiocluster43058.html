oxidization

noun
process of combining with oxygen or losing electrons
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːk.sɪ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒk.sɪ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a substance combines with oxygen or loses electrons, often resulting in a chemical change

Examples

oxidization of metal
During respiration, energy is released in the body through the oxidization of glucose.
Food must be stored properly to prevent oxidization and spoilage.
The oxidization of iron causes rust to form on metal surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oxidation: chemical process involving loss of electrons or addition of oxygen
  • antonym – reduction: chemical process involving gain of electrons, opposite of oxidation
