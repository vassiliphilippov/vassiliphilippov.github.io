graft

noun
joining of plant parts or tissues
Pronunciation: / ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
the act or process of joining one plant or piece of tissue to another so that they grow together as a single organism.

Examples

graft onto a tree
A well-performed graft can produce hardier fruit trees.
She studied the technique of graft in her botany class.
The graft of the rose cutting onto the rootstock was successful.
