Marsupialia

noun
order of mammals including marsupials
Pronunciation: / mɑːrˌsuːpiˈeɪliə / (AmE), / mɑːˌsjuːpɪˈeɪlɪə / (BrE)
an order of mammals (marsupials) whose young are born at an early stage of development and are typically carried and suckled in a pouch

Examples

Marsupialia order animals
Koalas, wombats, and opossums are all members of the Marsupialia order.
Scientists study the evolutionary adaptations of Marsupialia to understand their diversity.
The kangaroo belongs to the Marsupialia, a group native to Australia and nearby islands.
In contrast to placental mammals, animals in the Marsupialia give birth to much less developed young.
The Marsupialia include kangaroos, koalas, and opossums among many other species.
The fossil record shows that Marsupialia diversified in South America and Australia.
