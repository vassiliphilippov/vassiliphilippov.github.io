slick

adjective
superficially impressive or smooth
Pronunciation: / slɪk / (AmE), / slɪk / (BrE)
impressively smooth, clever, or attractive in a way that is superficial, insincere, or intended to deceive

Examples

slick salesman
Despite their slick exterior, the new advertisements failed to address fundamental customer concerns.
Her report was slick and well-presented, but lacked original insight into the problem.
The politician's slick response gave voters little real information about his policies.
He gave a slick presentation that covered up the lack of real progress.
The politician's slick answers impressed the audience, but some doubted his sincerity.
The website has a slick design but the content is rather shallow.
Mark gave a slick explanation to avoid answering the tough questions.
She came up with a slick way to talk herself out of trouble.
The politician's slick promises won over many voters, but few believed him.
