to segregate

verb
separate people by race, religion, etc.
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡ.rɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to separate people, especially by race, religion, or gender, and treat them differently; to enforce or practice segregation

Examples

to segregate students by race
It is wrong to segregate people in the workplace based on their religion.
Several states continued to segregate public facilities well into the 20th century.
The government used to segregate schools by race, but this practice is now illegal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – integrate: to bring people or groups with different characteristics together; to end segregation
  • synonym – separate: to set or keep apart; not together or touching
