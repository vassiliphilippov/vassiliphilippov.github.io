plausible

adjective
seemingly reasonable but misleading
Pronunciation: / ˈplɔːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or argument) given to presenting seemingly reasonable or valid arguments that are actually misleading or false.

Examples

plausible liar
His plausible explanation cleverly disguised the true facts of the case.
She was a plausible liar who could charm anyone into believing her stories.
The con artist's plausible manner made people trust him too easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym – specious: superficially plausible, but actually wrong or misleading
