stock

noun
cooking liquid used in soups or sauces
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɒk / (BrE)
a clear or flavored liquid made by simmering meat, bones, and vegetables, used as a base for soups or sauces

Examples

chicken stock soup
She simmered the bones for hours to make a rich beef stock.
The recipe calls for two cups of chicken stock to enhance the flavor.
Vegetable stock is a great vegetarian alternative in many soups and stews.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bouillon: a clear, seasoned soup made from boiled meat or vegetables
  • synonym – broth: liquid made by simmering meat or vegetables, often lighter than stock
  • synonym – consommé: a clear, refined meat or fish soup, often served as a starter
