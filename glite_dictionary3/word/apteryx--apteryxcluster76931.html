apteryx

noun
flightless bird of New Zealand (kiwi genus)
Pronunciation: / ˈæptəˌrɪks / (AmE), / ˈæptərɪks / (BrE)
A nocturnal, flightless bird native to New Zealand, known for its long neck, stout legs, and distinctive appearance; the only surviving member of the order Apterygiformes.

Examples

the apteryx bird
Conservation efforts aim to protect the dwindling population of the apteryx.
The apteryx is better known as the kiwi, New Zealand's national bird.
Unlike most birds, the apteryx lays one of the largest eggs in proportion to its body size.
