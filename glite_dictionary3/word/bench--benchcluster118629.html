to bench

verb
remove a player from a game (sports)
Pronunciation: / bɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɛntʃ / (BrE)
(in sports) to remove (a player) from a game, usually by having them sit on the bench and not participate.

Examples

to bench a player
If you keep arguing with the referee, I'll have to bench you for the rest of the match.
She was benched for most of the second half due to her injury.
The coach decided to bench the striker after his third missed shot.

Related Terms

  • synonym – substitute: replace a player on the field with another
