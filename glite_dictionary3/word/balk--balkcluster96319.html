balk

noun
illegal baseball pitching motion
Pronunciation: / bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
In baseball, an illegal motion made by the pitcher while runners are on base, which allows all runners to advance one base.

Examples

balk by pitcher
After the pitcher's balk, the crowd erupted into boos from the stands.
The manager argued with the umpire about whether it was really a balk or just a hesitation.
The umpire called a balk, allowing the runner on third base to score.
