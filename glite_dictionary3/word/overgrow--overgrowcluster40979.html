to overgrow

verb
to grow too large or excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
To grow too large or excessively, often beyond what is normal or desired.

Examples

to overgrow its pot
If left unchecked, tree roots can quickly overgrow their containers.
The city pond was overgrown with algae after the warm spell.
The weeds overgrew the entire garden by the end of summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – outgrow: to grow too large for something
  • antonym – wither: to shrivel or cease growing
