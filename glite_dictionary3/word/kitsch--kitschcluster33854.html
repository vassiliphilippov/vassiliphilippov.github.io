kitsch

noun
gaudy art or design in poor taste
Pronunciation: / kɪtʃ / (AmE), / kɪtʃ / (BrE)
Art, objects, or design considered to be in poor taste because of excessive garishness or sentimentality, but sometimes appreciated in a knowing or ironic way.

Examples

collect kitsch decorations
Many people decorate their homes with kitsch figurines and bright plastic flowers.
Some artists embrace kitsch to make a statement about popular culture.
The exhibition was full of kitsch, from velvet Elvis paintings to novelty lamps.

Related Terms

  • synonym – camp: Art or style deliberately exaggerated and theatrical, often appreciated ironically
  • antonym – tasteful: Showing good aesthetic judgment; the opposite of being in bad taste
