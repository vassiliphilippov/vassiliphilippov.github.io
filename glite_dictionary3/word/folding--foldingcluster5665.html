folding

noun
geological process bending rock layers
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which rock layers are bent or curved due to stress in the Earth's crust

Examples

rock folding formation
Geologists study folding to understand the history of Earth's crustal movements.
Mountain ranges are often formed by the folding of rock layers over millions of years.
The area exhibits evidence of severe folding due to tectonic activity.
