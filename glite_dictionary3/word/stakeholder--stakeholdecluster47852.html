stakeholder

noun
person holding stakes in a bet
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪkˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪkˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
A person entrusted to hold money or stakes wagered by others, especially in a bet, until the outcome is determined and the stakes are paid to the winner.

Examples

stakeholder holds money
As the official stakeholder, he was responsible for delivering the winnings to the victorious player.
Both parties agreed to use a neutral stakeholder for their high-stakes wager.
During the card game, Julia acted as the stakeholder to ensure everyone’s money was safe.
