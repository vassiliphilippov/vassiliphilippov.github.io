karyotype

noun
chromosome makeup of a cell/organism
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈkærɪəʊˌtaɪp / (BrE)
the number and visual appearance of the chromosomes in the cell nuclei of an organism or species

Examples

human karyotype analysis
A human karyotype typically contains 46 chromosomes, arranged in 23 pairs.
Certain genetic disorders can often be identified by an abnormal karyotype in the affected individual.
The scientist analyzed the karyotype of the patient to check for chromosomal abnormalities.
