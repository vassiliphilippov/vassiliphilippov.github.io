iconoclastic

adjective
attacking established beliefs or traditions
Pronunciation: / aɪˌkɑnəˈklæstɪk / (AmE), / aɪˌkɒnəˈklæstɪk / (BrE)
Characterized by attacking or strongly rejecting established beliefs, traditions, or institutions.

Examples

iconoclastic ideas challenge
His iconoclastic essays provoked heated debates among traditional scholars.
She developed an iconoclastic approach to education, questioning accepted methods and policies.
The artist is known for her iconoclastic views that challenge the conventions of modern art.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conventional: following traditional beliefs or practices
  • synonym – heretical: holding opinions contrary to established beliefs
