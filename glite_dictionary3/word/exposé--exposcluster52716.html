exposé

noun
report exposing wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈspoʊzeɪ / (AmE), / ɛkˈspəʊzeɪ / (BrE)
A published report that reveals the truth about something, especially something dishonest or illegal.

Examples

shocking expose
After reading the exposé, the public demanded a government investigation.
Her bestselling book is an exposé of corruption in the fashion industry.
The journalist wrote an exposé of the company's illegal financial practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cover-up: an attempt to prevent people from discovering the truth about something
  • synonym – revelation: the act of revealing something previously unknown, especially of a surprising or shocking kind
