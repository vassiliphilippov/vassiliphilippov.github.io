meritocratic

adjective
relating to meritocracy
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛrɪtəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛrɪtəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a system in which people are chosen or moved into positions of success, power, or influence on the basis of their abilities and merits rather than other factors such as wealth or social class.

Examples

meritocratic system
He criticized the university's admissions process for not being truly meritocratic.
Many people aspire to live in a meritocratic society where effort and talent are valued above all else.
The company adopted a meritocratic approach to promotions, rewarding employees based on their performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meritocracy: a system in which advancement is based on individual ability or achievement
