shoe

noun
part of a brake system
Pronunciation: / ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
a part of a brake system consisting of a curved piece that presses against a drum to slow or stop a vehicle's wheel

Examples

brake shoe
He was surprised by how quickly the brake shoe needed to be replaced after heavy use.
The mechanic inspected the worn shoe and recommended replacing it to ensure safe braking.
When the shoe presses against the drum, it creates friction to slow the car.
