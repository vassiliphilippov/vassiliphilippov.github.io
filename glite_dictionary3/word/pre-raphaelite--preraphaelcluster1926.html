Pre-Raphaelite

noun
member of 19th-century art/literary movement
Pronunciation: / priˈræfəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / priːˈræfəlaɪt / (BrE)
A painter or writer belonging to or influenced by a 19th-century movement dedicated to reviving the detail, vivid colors, and complex compositions of early Renaissance art.

Examples

famous Pre-Raphaelite artist
As a writer, he was heavily influenced by the ideals of the Pre-Raphaelite movement.
Many Pre-Raphaelite paintings feature medieval or literary themes and exquisite attention to detail.
She studied the techniques of the Pre-Raphaelites for her dissertation on Victorian art.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – Impressionist: member of an artistic movement after the Pre-Raphaelites, focusing on light and color
  • degree higher – Romantic: related artistic and literary movement emphasizing emotion and individualism
  • degree lower – realist: artist or writer depicting subjects truthfully, linked via stylistic differences with Pre-Raphaelites
