deplorable

noun
person regarded as morally despicable
Pronunciation: / dɪˈplɔrəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔːrəbəl / (BrE)
a person regarded as morally despicable or deserving strong condemnation; often used as a pejorative label in political or public debate.

Examples

a deplorable person
After the scandal, many voters labeled him a deplorable and vowed never to support him again.
During the debate she refused to meet a single deplorable from the opposing party in private.
Social media filled with insults after the columnist called his critics deplorable for spreading falsehoods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reprehensible: deserving censure or condemnation
  • synonym – reprobate: a morally unprincipled person
