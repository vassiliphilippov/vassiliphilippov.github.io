parallel

noun
something similar or comparable
Pronunciation: / ˈpæɹəˌlɛl / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl / (BrE)
a person or thing that is similar or analogous to another; a comparison showing such similarity

Examples

draw a parallel
Historians often draw parallels between current events and those of the past.
She pointed out the parallel with another case that happened last year.
There is a clear parallel between the rise of technology and the changes in communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym – analogy: a similarity between like features of two things
  • antonym – contrast: a person or thing strikingly dissimilar from another
