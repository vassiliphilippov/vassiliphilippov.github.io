binomial

adjective
having two names, especially in taxonomy
Pronunciation: / baɪˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
having or characterized by two names, especially referring to the two-term naming system in biological classification (genus and species).

Examples

binomial name
In binomial nomenclature, each species is given a two-part Latin name.
The scientist used the binomial system to classify the newly discovered plant.
The textbook explained the importance of binomial names in biological classification.
