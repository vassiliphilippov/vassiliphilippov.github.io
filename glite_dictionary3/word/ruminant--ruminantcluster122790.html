ruminant

noun
cud-chewing hoofed mammal with multi-chambered stomach
Pronunciation: / ˈruː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈruː.mɪ.nənt / (BrE)
any hoofed mammal that chews cud and has a stomach divided into four (occasionally three) compartments, such as cattle, sheep, and deer

Examples

ruminant animal group
Cows, sheep, and deer are all examples of ruminant animals.
The digestive system of a ruminant is specially adapted for fermenting plant material.
Unlike humans, a ruminant regurgitates and rechews its food.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonruminant: an animal that does not chew cud or have a multi-chambered stomach
