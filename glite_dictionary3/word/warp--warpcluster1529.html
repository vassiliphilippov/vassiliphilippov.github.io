to warp

verb
distort or twist the truth
Pronunciation: / wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
to distort or change the truth, character, or meaning of something, especially by making it false or misleading

Examples

to warp the truth
He tried to warp the facts to suit his own agenda.
Over time, repeated retelling can warp the original story.
Social media sometimes warps public perception of the truth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – distort: to change the shape or meaning of something so that it is no longer true or accurate
  • synonym – falsify: to change or alter something in order to deceive
