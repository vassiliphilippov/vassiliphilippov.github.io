to bloat

verb
become swollen or puffed up
Pronunciation: / bloʊt / (AmE), / bləʊt / (BrE)
to become swollen or to make something swollen, especially with gas, air, or fluid

Examples

to bloat after eating
After eating too quickly, his stomach began to bloat painfully.
If you let the dough sit too long, it will bloat and lose its shape.
The dead fish started to bloat after several days in the sun.
Certain medical conditions may bloat your abdomen uncomfortably.
Heavy rainfall can bloat the river until it floods the fields.
Too much salt in the diet can bloat the body and cause discomfort.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deflate: to release air or gas from something swollen or inflated
  • synonym – swell: to become larger or rounder in size, typically as a result of an accumulation of fluid
