reinforcer

noun
stimulus increasing behavioral occurrence
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːrsər / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːsə / (BrE)
(psychology) A stimulus or event that increases the likelihood that a particular behavior will occur again by being presented after that behavior.

Examples

reinforcer like food
Food was used as a reinforcer during the animal training session.
Praise can serve as a powerful reinforcer for good behavior in children.
The experiment tested whether money would act as an effective reinforcer for completing the task.

Related Terms

  • antonym – punisher: stimulus that decreases the likelihood of a behavior
