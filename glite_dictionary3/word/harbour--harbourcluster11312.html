to harbour

verb
secretly shelter (esp. fugitives or criminals)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
to secretly provide shelter or refuge to someone, especially someone who is wanted by the authorities or is in trouble with the law

Examples

to harbour a criminal
Anyone found to harbour a fugitive will be prosecuted under the law.
It's a crime to harbour anyone who is running from the police.
She was accused of having harboured the escaped prisoner in her basement for weeks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – betray: to reveal or expose information about someone, often to authorities
  • synonym – conceal: to keep something hidden or secret
