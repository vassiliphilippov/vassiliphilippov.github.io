treadwheel

noun
wheel powered by walking, used for work
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛdˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛdˌwiːl / (BrE)
a large wheel or circular device powered by people or animals walking or climbing on it, used to drive machinery or raise water

Examples

medieval treadwheel crane
A medieval treadwheel crane can still be seen in the old cathedral tower.
In rural mills, donkeys or oxen would power the treadwheel to grind grain.
The prisoners were put to work turning the treadwheel for hours each day.
