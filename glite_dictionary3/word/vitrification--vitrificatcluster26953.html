vitrification

noun
a glassy substance formed by heating
Pronunciation: / ˌvɪtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of turning something into glass or a glass-like substance, usually by heating

Examples

vitrification of minerals
The vitrification of certain minerals leads to a shiny, glass-like coating.
The nuclear waste underwent vitrification to make it stable for long-term storage.
When heated to a high temperature, clay can experience vitrification and become waterproof.
Nuclear waste can be stabilized through vitrification, turning it into solid glass for long-term storage.
Scientists observed vitrification in the lava as it cooled and hardened into a shiny, brittle crust.
The vitrification of the pottery created a smooth, glassy finish on its surface.
