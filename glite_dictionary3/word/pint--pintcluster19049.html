pint

noun
unit of capacity (British, 568 ml)
Pronunciation: / paɪnt / (AmE), / paɪnt / (BrE)
a unit for measuring liquid or dry capacity in the British imperial system, equal to about 568 milliliters

Examples

a pint of beer
A British pint is larger than the American pint.
I ordered a pint of ale at the pub.
The recipe asks for one pint of milk.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – gill: a unit of liquid measure equal to a quarter of a pint
  • synonym – liter: metric unit of capacity, about 1.76 British pints
  • degree higher – quart: a unit of capacity equal to two pints
