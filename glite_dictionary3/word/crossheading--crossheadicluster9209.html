crossheading

noun
heading for a subsection within text
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
a heading printed within the body of a text to introduce or highlight a subsection

Examples

crossheading in a chapter
A bold crossheading helped readers quickly locate the information they needed.
She used a crossheading to clarify the transition between two major topics in her essay.
The chapter was divided into several sections, each introduced by a crossheading.

Related Terms

  • synonym – subheading: a heading given to a subsection of a piece of writing
