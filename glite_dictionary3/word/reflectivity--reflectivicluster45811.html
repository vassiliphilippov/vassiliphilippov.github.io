reflectivity

noun
measure of how much energy a surface reflects
Pronunciation: / rɪˌflɛkˈtɪvəti /; / ˌrɛflɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / rɪˌflɛkˈtɪvɪti /; / ˌrɪflɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the ability of a surface or material to reflect light or energy; a measure of how much light or energy is reflected

Examples

high reflectivity of mirrors
A mirror has a high reflectivity, returning almost all the light that strikes its surface.
Scientists measured the reflectivity of the new coating to assess its efficiency.
The reflectivity of snow can make it difficult to see without sunglasses.
Scientists measured the reflectivity of different materials to find the best heat shield.
Snow has a greater reflectivity than grass, which helps keep temperatures lower.
The shiny metal surface has high reflectivity, making it suitable for mirrors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – absorption: the process of taking in energy instead of reflecting it
  • synonym – albedo: proportion of the incident light or radiation that is reflected by a surface
  • synonym – reflection: the process of reflecting light, sound, or heat
