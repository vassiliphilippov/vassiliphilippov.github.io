psychopathy

noun
personality disorder marked by antisocial behavior
Pronunciation: / saɪˈkɑːpəθi / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒpəθi / (BrE)
A personality disorder characterized by persistent antisocial behavior, impaired empathy and remorse, and bold, disinhibited, and egotistical traits.

Examples

diagnose psychopathy
He learned about psychopathy while taking a psychology course at university.
Researchers are studying the causes and treatment of psychopathy in adults.
