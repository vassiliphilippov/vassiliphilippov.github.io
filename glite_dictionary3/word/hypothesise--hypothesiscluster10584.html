to hypothesise

verb
form a theory based on limited evidence
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑːθəsaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒθɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to form an idea or theory about something based on limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation

Examples

to hypothesise a result
Before conducting her experiment, Maria decided to hypothesise about the possible outcomes.
It is important for scientists to hypothesise and then test their ideas rigorously.
Some researchers hypothesise that climate change could increase the frequency of certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • antonym – know: to be aware of something as a fact
  • synonym – presume: to take for granted or accept as true without proof
  • synonym – suppose: to assume something is true without certain proof
