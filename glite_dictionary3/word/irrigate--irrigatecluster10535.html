to irrigate

verb
to rinse or cleanse with liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to supply a wound or body part with a continuous flow or spray of liquid, typically to cool, cleanse, or disinfect it

Examples

to irrigate a wound
Doctors sometimes irrigate the ear canal to clear out excess wax.
The nurse will irrigate the wound to remove all debris before bandaging it.
To prevent infection, you should irrigate the area thoroughly with sterile saline solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cleanse: to make something thoroughly clean
  • synonym – rinse: to wash lightly with water or another liquid
