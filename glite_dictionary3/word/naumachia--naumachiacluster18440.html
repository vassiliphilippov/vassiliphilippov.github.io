naumachia

noun
mock sea battle in ancient Rome
Pronunciation: / naʊˈmeɪkiə / (AmE), / naʊˈmakɪə / (BrE)
In ancient Rome, a spectacle consisting of a mock sea battle, sometimes staged in an arena flooded with water.

Examples

Roman naumachia show
The Colosseum was sometimes flooded to stage an elaborate naumachia for the public.
The emperor ordered a grand naumachia to celebrate his victory.
Thousands of spectators gathered to watch the dramatic naumachia unfold in the arena.
