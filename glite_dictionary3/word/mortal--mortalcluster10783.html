mortal

adjective
deadly; showing absolute enmity
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtəl /; / ˈmɔrt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtəl / (BrE)
causing or likely to cause death; deadly or fatal (including deadly hostility or enmity)

Examples

mortal enemy
He considered his rival a mortal enemy and would never forgive him.
She vowed mortal vengeance after the betrayal.
Their feud had become a mortal conflict, with neither side willing to compromise.
Doctors warned that without treatment, the infection could prove mortal.
The wound he suffered in battle was mortal and could not be treated.
They faced mortal danger as the building began to collapse around them.
