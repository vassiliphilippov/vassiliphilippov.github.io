to purify

verb
become clean or free from sin
Pronunciation: / ˈpjʊr.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊə.rɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to become clean, pure, or free from guilt, sin, or contamination; to undergo cleansing physically or spiritually

Examples

purify through meditation
Some people believe that meditation can purify the soul of negative thoughts.
The monks fasted to purify their minds before the ceremony.
The river water began to purify after months of environmental efforts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cleanse: to clean or rid of impurities or toxins; often used for physical or spiritual cleaning
  • antonym – contaminate: to make something impure or dirty by contact or mixture
