cowpox

noun
a mild viral disease of cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌpɒks / (BrE)
a mild viral disease of cattle that causes pustules on the udders, formerly used to inoculate humans against smallpox.

Examples

cowpox infection in cattle
Cases of cowpox in humans are now extremely rare due to vaccinations and modern hygiene.
Edward Jenner used cowpox virus to create the first successful smallpox vaccine.
The outbreak of cowpox among the herd caused concern for the local dairy farmers.
