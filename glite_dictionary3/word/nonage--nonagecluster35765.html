nonage

noun
period before legal adulthood
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑn.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɒn.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period of life before reaching the legal age of adulthood; the state of being underage

Examples

nonage of a child
During his nonage, his father managed his inheritance until he came of age.
In medieval times, guardians were appointed for nobles during their nonage.
The contract was declared void because he was still in his nonage.
