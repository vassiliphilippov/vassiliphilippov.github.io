to spike

verb
experience a sharp increase
Pronunciation: / spaɪk / (AmE), / spaɪk / (BrE)
to increase suddenly and sharply, often to a much higher level

Examples

to spike sharply
After the announcement, there was a sudden spike in internet traffic.
Temperatures are expected to spike during the heatwave next week.
The company's sales spiked after the new product launched.
Internet usage spiked yesterday due to the live-streamed event.
Reports show that crime rates spike after midnight in certain areas.
The price of gasoline spiked during the summer holiday season.

Related Terms

  • antonym – plummet: to fall or drop suddenly in amount or value
  • antonym – plunge: to decrease sharply and suddenly
  • synonym – soar: to rise or increase quickly to a high level
  • synonym – surge: to increase suddenly and powerfully
