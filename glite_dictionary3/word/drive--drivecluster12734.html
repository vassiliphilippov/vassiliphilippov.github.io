to drive

verb
propel or press with force
Pronunciation: / draɪv / (AmE), / draɪv / (BrE)
to supply force or power to make something move or work; or to be moved by a force (including machines, objects, or computers)

Examples

to drive a nail
He used a hammer to drive the nail into the wood.
With one strong blow, she drove the stake into the ground.
You need to apply enough pressure to drive the peg into place.
Specialized machines are used to drive shafts in mining operations.
The engineers plan to drive a new tunnel through the mountain.
They drove a passage beneath the old city to lay new cables.
A small motor drives the moving parts of the machine.
Solar panels can drive many household appliances during the day.
The wind turbines drive the electric generators efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bore: to make a hole or tunnel by drilling or digging
  • synonym – hammer: to strike something repeatedly with a tool
  • synonym – operate: to control the functioning of something
  • synonym – power: to supply with energy or power, especially for functioning
  • synonym – propel: to drive or push something forwards
  • antonym – pull: to move something toward oneself, often by force
  • degree lower – tap: to strike lightly
