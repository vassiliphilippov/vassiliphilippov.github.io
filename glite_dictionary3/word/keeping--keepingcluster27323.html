keeping

noun
state of being in agreement or harmony
Pronunciation: / ˈkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the state of being in agreement, harmony, or conformity with something; often used in the phrase 'in keeping with'.

Examples

in keeping with tradition
His response was perfectly keeping with company policy.
The new decorations are not in keeping with the building’s historic character.
Their actions were in keeping with the club’s long-standing traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accordance: agreement or conformity with a rule or request
  • antonym – conflict: lack of agreement or harmony
