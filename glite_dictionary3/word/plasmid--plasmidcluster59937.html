plasmid

noun
small circular DNA molecule in cells
Pronunciation: / ˈplæz.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæz.mɪd / (BrE)
A small, typically circular DNA molecule in the cell that is separate from the chromosomal DNA and can replicate independently.

Examples

bacterial plasmid
A plasmid can carry antibiotic resistance genes, making infections harder to treat.
Researchers often use a plasmid as a vector to transfer DNA into cells.
The scientist inserted the gene into a plasmid to study its effects in bacteria.
