to amortise

verb
gradually pay off a debt
Pronunciation: / əˈmɔːrtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈmɔːtʌɪz / (BrE)
To gradually reduce or pay off a debt or an asset through regular payments over a set period.

Examples

to amortise a loan
By the end of the term, you will have fully amortised your mortgage.
Many businesses amortise expensive equipment to spread the cost over its useful life.
The company plans to amortise the loan over ten years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amortize: pay off a debt gradually (American spelling)
  • synonym – liquidate: settle a debt or obligation by payment
