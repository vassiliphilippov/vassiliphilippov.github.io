allegedly

adverb
claimed, but not proven
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛdʒɪdli / (AmE), / əˈlɛdʒɪdli / (BrE)
Used to convey that something is claimed to be the case or have taken place, although there is no proof.

Examples

allegedly stolen paintings
Several paintings were allegedly stolen from the museum last night.
The company is allegedly under investigation for tax evasion.
The politician was allegedly involved in the scandal, though no evidence was presented.

Related Terms

  • antonym – certainly: without any doubt; definitely true
  • synonym – supposedly: used to indicate something is claimed or believed to be true, often with doubt
