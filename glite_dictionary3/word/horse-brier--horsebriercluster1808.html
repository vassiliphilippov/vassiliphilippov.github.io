horse-brier

noun
prickly woody vine of eastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsˌbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌbraɪə / (BrE)
A very prickly, woody vine native to the eastern United States, with tough round stems, shiny leathery leaves, small greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible shiny black berries.

Examples

horse-brier vine thorns
Gardeners often struggle to remove horse-brier due to its tough stems and tangled growth.
She recognized the distinctive shiny leaves of the horse-brier along the forest edge.
The thicket was full of horse-brier, making it nearly impossible to walk through.
