sensitivity

noun
degree of susceptibility to pathogens
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which an organism or cell is susceptible to infection or harm by a pathogen or substance

Examples

allergy sensitivity
Scientists test the sensitivity of crops to various plant viruses in the laboratory.
Some bacteria have developed a low sensitivity to antibiotics, making infections harder to treat.
The patient's high sensitivity to pollen led to severe allergic reactions during spring.

Related Terms

  • antonym – resistance: the ability of an organism to withstand or fend off a pathogen
