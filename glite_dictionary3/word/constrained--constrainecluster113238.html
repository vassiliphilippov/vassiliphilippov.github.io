constrained

adjective
not natural or spontaneous
Pronunciation: / kənˈstreɪnd / (AmE), / kənˈstreɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person, expression, or action) appearing forced, artificial, or inhibited; not natural or spontaneous.

Examples

constrained smile
He gave a constrained smile when he met his former boss at the party.
Her laughter sounded constrained, as if she didn't really find the joke amusing.
The atmosphere at the meeting was constrained and tense, making open discussion difficult.
