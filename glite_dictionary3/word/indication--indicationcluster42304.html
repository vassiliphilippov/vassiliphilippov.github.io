indication

noun
reason to use a medical treatment or procedure
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Medicine) A valid reason for using a particular treatment, performing a procedure, or prescribing a drug.

Examples

indication for surgery
Chronic pain is an indication for the use of certain medications.
Surgery is only considered when there is a clear indication for intervention.
The presence of a heart murmur was an indication for further tests.

Related Terms

  • antonym – contraindication: a reason for not using a particular treatment or procedure
