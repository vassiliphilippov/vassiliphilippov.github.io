angiography

noun
medical imaging of blood vessels
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.dʒiˈɑːɡ.rə.fi / (AmE), / ˌæn.dʒiˈɒɡ.rə.fi / (BrE)
A medical imaging technique for visualizing the inside of blood vessels after injecting a radiopaque contrast medium.

Examples

angiography of blood vessels
Following chest pain, he underwent an angiography to examine his coronary vessels.
Modern angiography allows physicians to diagnose vascular diseases with improved accuracy.
The doctor recommended an angiography to check for blockages in her arteries.

  • synonym – angiogram: an image produced by angiography
