wing

noun
side area of a stage, out of audience view
Pronunciation: / wɪŋ / (AmE), / wɪŋ / (BrE)
the side area of a stage, out of sight of the audience, used by performers and stage crew awaiting their cue or managing scenery

Examples

actor waits in wing
After her performance, she quickly exited into the wing to catch her breath.
Stagehands stood in the wings, ready to change the scenery between acts.
The actor waited in the wing for his entrance onto the stage.
