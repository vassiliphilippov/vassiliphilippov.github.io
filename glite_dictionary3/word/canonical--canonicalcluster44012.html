canonical

adjective
reduced to simplest or standard form
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑnɪkəl / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
reduced to the simplest or most standard form without loss of generality, often used in academic, mathematical, or scientific contexts.

Examples

canonical form
By transforming the matrix into its canonical form, the problem became much easier to solve.
In linguistics, each word is broken down into its canonical syllable pattern.
The database requires that all entries follow a canonical format for consistency.

  • synonym – standard: conforming to a set or established form
