to summate

verb
form a cumulative effect
Pronunciation: / sʌˈmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmeɪt / (BrE)
to form or constitute a cumulative effect by adding together parts or elements

Examples

In neurons, electrical signals summate to trigger an action potential.
Small errors in calculation can summate into a major discrepancy.
The minor incidents summate to create significant stress over time.
