editor

noun
person deciding final text content
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for the final content and editorial aspects of a publication, such as a newspaper, magazine, or book

Examples

editor of a newspaper
An experienced editor ensures that the book is free from errors and inconsistencies.
She was promoted to chief editor after years of hard work at the newspaper.
The editor carefully reviewed every article before the magazine went to print.

Related Terms

  • synonym – publisher: person or company responsible for issuing books or periodicals
