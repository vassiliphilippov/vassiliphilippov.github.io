canal

noun
tubular bodily passage
Pronunciation: / kəˈnæl / (AmE), / kəˈnæl / (BrE)
a tubular passage in the body, lined with epithelial cells, through which fluid or other substances are conveyed

Examples

ear canal pain
Damage to the ear canal can affect hearing significantly.
The canal of the spinal cord is known as the central canal.
The dentist explained that the infection had spread into the root canal.
