gameboard

noun
flat surface used in board games
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪmˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪmˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A flat, typically rectangular surface designed for playing board games on, often marked with spaces, tracks, or designs specific to different games.

Examples

gameboard for chess
After dinner, the family gathered around the gameboard for an evening of fun.
He unfolded the gameboard and arranged the chess pieces carefully.
The colorful gameboard included spaces for each player's character to move along.
