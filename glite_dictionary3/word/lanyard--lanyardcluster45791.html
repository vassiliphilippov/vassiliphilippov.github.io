lanyard

noun
cord for firing cannons
Pronunciation: / ˈlænjərd / (AmE), / ˈlanjəd / (BrE)
A cord with an attached hook used to fire certain types of cannon.

Examples

cannon lanyard hook
Early artillery relied on a simple lanyard mechanism for ignition.
The gunner pulled the lanyard to fire the cannon during the demonstration.
With a swift tug on the lanyard, the shell was launched toward the target.
