to blurt

verb
say suddenly and impulsively
Pronunciation: / blɝːt / (AmE), / blɜːt / (BrE)
to say something suddenly and without careful thought, often because of strong emotion or nervousness

Examples

to blurt out a secret
He couldn't help but blurt the news to everyone at the party.
If you blurt out the answer, you might spoil the surprise.
Sometimes she would blurt out comments without thinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spill: to reveal (information) unintentionally or too soon
  • antonym – suppress: to keep something from being revealed or expressed
