riotous

adjective
noisy and disorderly; wild
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪ.ə.təs / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ət.əs / (BrE)
marked by noisy disorder, wildness, or unrest; involving violent or uncontrolled behavior

Examples

riotous crowd
During the revolution, there were riotous scenes outside the parliament building.
The city streets became riotous after the championship game ended.
The party grew increasingly riotous as midnight approached.

Related Terms

  • antonym – orderly: characterized by organization and neatness, not disorderly
  • synonym – tumultuous: full of confusion, commotion, or disorder
  • synonym – turbulent: characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
