labiodental

noun
consonant articulated with lips and teeth
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪbioʊˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌleɪbiəʊˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
a consonant produced by touching the bottom lip to the upper teeth, as in the sounds 'f' and 'v' in English

Examples

a labiodental sound
In phonetics, a labiodental is produced by bringing the lower lip into contact with the upper teeth.
She struggled to pronounce the French labiodental sounds correctly when learning the language.
The English 'f' and 'v' sounds are both classified as labiodentals by phoneticians.
