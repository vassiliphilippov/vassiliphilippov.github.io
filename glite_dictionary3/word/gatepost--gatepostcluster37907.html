gatepost

noun
upright post at side of a gate
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪtˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪt.pəʊst / (BrE)
either of the upright posts on each side of a gate, supporting it and marking its boundary

Examples

gatepost at entrance
He leaned against the gatepost while waiting for his friend to arrive.
She tied the bicycle securely to the gatepost before entering the house.
The old wooden gatepost was rotting at the base and needed replacing.
