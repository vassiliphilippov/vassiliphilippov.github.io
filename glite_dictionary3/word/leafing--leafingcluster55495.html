leafing

noun
formation of leaves on a plant
Pronunciation: / ˈliːfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːfɪŋ / (BrE)
(botany) The process by which a plant forms or produces new leaves.

Examples

spring leafing
Gardeners monitor the leafing of shrubs to decide the best times for planting.
Heavy frost can delay the leafing process in some plant species.
The leafing of trees in early spring is a sign that warmer weather is approaching.

Related Terms

  • antonym – defoliation: loss or removal of leaves from a plant
