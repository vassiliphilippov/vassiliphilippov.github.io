vesication

noun
formation of blisters in the skin
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛsɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛsɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming blisters (vesicles) in or beneath the skin, especially as a result of disease or certain chemicals

Examples

vesication from chemicals
Blister agents were used in warfare for their ability to induce rapid vesication on contact with skin.
Prolonged exposure to the chemical caused vesication on the laboratory worker’s hands.
Some skin diseases are characterized by vesication and severe itching.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blistering: the formation of blisters on the skin
  • antonym – ulceration: formation of an ulcer as a skin lesion
