headstall

noun
part of a bridle fitting around a horse's head
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌstɑl / (AmE), / ˈhɛdstɔːl / (BrE)
The part of a bridle that fits around a horse's head and to which the bit and reins are attached.

Examples

headstall of a bridle
A broken headstall can make the bridle unusable until it is repaired.
She cleaned the headstall and reins after returning from a long ride.
The leather headstall was carefully adjusted to ensure the horse was comfortable.
