varlet

noun
knight's attendant in training (medieval)
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːr.lət / (AmE), / ˈvɑː.lɪt / (BrE)
In medieval times, a youth who served as a knight's attendant as the first stage of training for knighthood.

Examples

varlet serving knight
In his youth, the prince worked as a varlet to learn the art of chivalry.
Stories of a faithful varlet often appear in medieval literature.
The varlet cleaned his master's armor carefully before every battle.
