reovirus

noun
type of RNA virus; includes rotavirus
Pronunciation: / ˈrioʊˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈriːəʊˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of RNA viruses, including rotavirus, that can cause gastrointestinal and other infections in humans and animals.

Examples

reovirus infection
Scientists identified the reovirus as the source of the outbreak in the nursery ward.
Some reoviruses, like rotavirus, can cause severe diarrhea in infants and young children.
The study focused on how the reovirus infects the digestive tract of its host.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rotavirus: a group of viruses causing gastroenteritis, especially in infants and young children
