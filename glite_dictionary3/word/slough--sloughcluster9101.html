slough

noun
outer layer that is shed (e.g. snake skin)
Pronunciation: / slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
an outer layer or covering of an animal, such as skin or shell, that is shed or cast off

Examples

slough of old skin
Finding the slough of a lizard is a sign that it lives nearby.
Some amphibians periodically shed their slough as they grow.
The snake left its slough behind on the forest floor.
