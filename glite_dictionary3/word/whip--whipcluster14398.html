to whip

verb
move or thrash about rapidly
Pronunciation: / wɪp / (AmE), / wɪp / (BrE)
to move or thrash about quickly and flexibly, often with a sudden motion resembling the action of a whip

Examples

to whip around
His hair whipped around his face as he ran down the hill.
Long branches whipped against the window during the gale.
The flags whipped violently as the storm intensified.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lash: to move suddenly or strike like a whip
