to postdate

verb
to happen later than something else
Pronunciation: / poʊsˈdeɪt / (AmE), / pəʊsˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to occur, happen, or exist later than something else in time

Examples

postdate an event
Her findings postdate the initial experiment, providing new insight into the results.
Technological advances often postdate important scientific discoveries.
The renovations postdate the original construction by several decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym – antedate: to occur earlier in time than something else
  • synonym – follow: to come after something in time or sequence
