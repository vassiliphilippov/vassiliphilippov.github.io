pars intermedia

noun
thin tissue layer in pituitary gland
Pronunciation: / pɑːrz ˌɪntərˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / pɑːz ˌɪntəˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
the thin layer of tissue in the pituitary gland located between the anterior and posterior lobes, which in humans is typically merged with the posterior pituitary

Examples

pars intermedia of pituitary
During embryonic development, the pars intermedia can be more prominent than in adult anatomy.
Pathologies affecting the pars intermedia are rare in humans, but can occur in other species.
The pars intermedia produces certain hormones in some animals, but is largely vestigial in adult humans.
