roughcast

noun
coarse wall plaster
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌfˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈrʌfˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A coarse plaster made of lime, cement, gravel, and small stones, applied to the surface of external walls.

Examples

roughcast finish
Builders applied a layer of roughcast to protect the exterior from harsh weather.
Some homeowners choose roughcast as a decorative finish for garden walls.
The old cottage was covered in roughcast, giving it a rustic appearance.
