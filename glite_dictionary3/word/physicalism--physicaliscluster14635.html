physicalism

noun
view that only the physical exists
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪzɪkəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(philosophy) the theory that everything that exists is physical in nature, and that only matter and its properties are fundamental.

Examples

modern physicalism theory
Debates about consciousness frequently center around the limits of physicalism.
His rejection of physicalism led him to explore non-materialist philosophies of mind.
Modern science is often associated with physicalism, the idea that only physical entities exist.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dualism: the belief that reality consists of two distinct principles, such as mind and matter
  • synonym – materialism: the doctrine that only material things exist
