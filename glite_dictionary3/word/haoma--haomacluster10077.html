haoma

noun
leafless vine, source of intoxicating drink
Pronunciation: / haʊˈoʊmə / (AmE), / haʊˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A leafless East Indian vine whose sour milky juice was formerly used to make an intoxicating drink, especially in ancient religious rituals.

Examples

haoma plant ritual
Researchers have studied haoma for its historical role in religious ceremonies.
Some legends describe the effects of drinking haoma as both sacred and intoxicating.
