Pope Paul VI

proper noun
Pope of the Catholic Church, 1963–1978
Pronunciation: / poʊp pɔl sɪksθ / (AmE), / pəʊp pɔːl sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1897–1978) The head of the Roman Catholic Church from 1963 to 1978, known for implementing significant reforms and promoting ecumenical relations.

Examples

Pope Paul VI led reforms
Pope Paul VI led the Catholic Church through a period of great change during the 1960s and 1970s.
Many remember Pope Paul VI for his efforts to promote dialogue between different Christian denominations.
The encyclical Humanae Vitae, issued by Pope Paul VI, sparked widespread debate within the Church.
