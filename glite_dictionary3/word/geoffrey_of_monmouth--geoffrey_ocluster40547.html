Geoffrey of Monmouth

proper noun
Welsh chronicler and author of Arthurian legends
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛfɹi əv ˈmɒnməθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛfɹi əv ˈmɒnməθ / (BrE)
(circa 1100–1154) A Welsh chronicler known for writing an account of the kings of Britain that contributed to Arthurian legend, though it contains little historical fact.

Examples

Geoffrey of Monmouth wrote legends
Geoffrey of Monmouth played a pivotal role in shaping the modern Arthurian legend.
Many historians debate the accuracy of Geoffrey of Monmouth's account of early British kings.
The tales of King Arthur would not be the same without Geoffrey of Monmouth's influential writings.
