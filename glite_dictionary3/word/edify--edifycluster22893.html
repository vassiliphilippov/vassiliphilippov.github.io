to edify

verb
to instruct or enlighten
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to instruct or improve someone intellectually or morally; to enlighten

Examples

to edify young minds
A good book can edify as well as entertain its reader.
He spoke with the hope that his words would edify those who listened.
The teacher sought to edify her students by sharing inspiring stories from history.

Related Terms

  • antonym – confuse: to make someone unable to think clearly or understand
  • synonym – enlighten: to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject
  • synonym – teach: to impart knowledge or instruct someone
