Harold Godwinson

proper noun
last Anglo-Saxon King of England, died 1066
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrəld ˈɡɒdwɪnsən / (AmE), / ˈhærəld ˈɡɒdwɪnsən / (BrE)
(c. 1022–1066) King of England who succeeded Edward the Confessor in 1066 and was the last Anglo-Saxon monarch; he died at the Battle of Hastings fighting William the Conqueror.

Examples

Harold Godwinson lost Hastings
Harold Godwinson became king of England after the death of Edward the Confessor in 1066.
Many historians consider Harold Godwinson a pivotal figure in English history.
The Battle of Hastings resulted in the death of Harold Godwinson and the end of Anglo-Saxon rule.
