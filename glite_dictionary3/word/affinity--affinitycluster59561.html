affinity

noun
attraction; especially the force binding atoms or molecules
Pronunciation: / əˈfɪnəti /; / əˈfɪn.ə.ti / (AmE), / əˈfɪnɪti /; / əˈfɪn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a similarity, relationship, or force of attraction between things in science, such as in chemistry or biology

Examples

affinity for electrons
Chlorine has a high affinity for electrons, making it a reactive element.
The dye's affinity for cotton fabric allowed it to bond firmly and produce vivid colors.
Water molecules exhibit a strong affinity for one another due to hydrogen bonding.
Fossil evidence suggests an affinity between extinct and modern birds.
Genetic studies have revealed a deep affinity between certain primates and humans.
The two species share a remarkable affinity in their bone structure.
