quitclaim

noun
legal document transferring property claim
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm / (AmE), / ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm / (BrE)
a legal document or act in which someone gives up their rights or claims to property and transfers them to another person, without any guarantee of validity

Examples

quitclaim deed form
He signed a quitclaim to transfer his rights to the property.
The lawyer prepared a quitclaim for the disputed land.
Without a quitclaim, there could be issues with the title transfer.
A quitclaim is often used when property is transferred without warranties.
After the divorce, he signed a quitclaim so that his ex-wife would have the house.
The property was transferred to her by a quitclaim from her mother.

  • synonym – release: the act of giving up a claim, right, or title
