thalamus

noun
brain structure; sensory relay center
Pronunciation: / ˈθæl.ə.məs / (AmE), / ˈθal.ə.məs / (BrE)
a large, egg-shaped mass of grey matter located in the brain that forms the dorsal part of the diencephalon and serves as a relay center for sensory and motor signals

Examples

the thalamus relays signals
Damage to the thalamus can result in sensory loss or motor impairment.
During deep sleep, activity in the thalamus decreases significantly.
The thalamus acts as a relay station, directing sensory information to the appropriate areas of the brain.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cerebrum: the principal and most anterior part of the brain
  • degree lower – hypothalamus: region of the brain located below the thalamus, controlling autonomic functions
