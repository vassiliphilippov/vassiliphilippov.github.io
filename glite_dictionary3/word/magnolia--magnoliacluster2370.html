Magnolia

noun
genus of flowering trees or shrubs
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs or trees native to North America and Asia, known for their large, fragrant flowers and broad evergreen or deciduous leaves; among the oldest angiosperm genera.
