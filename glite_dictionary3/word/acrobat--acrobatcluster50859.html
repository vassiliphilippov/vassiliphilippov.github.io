acrobat

noun
skilled performer of gymnastic feats
Pronunciation: / ˈækrəˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈækrəˌbæt / (BrE)
a person who performs physical acts that require skill, agility, and coordination, especially as part of a circus or public entertainment

Examples

circus acrobat
After years of practice, the young acrobat finally mastered the tightrope act.
She trained as an acrobat before joining the world-famous circus troupe.
The acrobat amazed the audience with a series of daring flips and somersaults.
