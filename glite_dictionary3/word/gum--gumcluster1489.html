gum

noun
plant substance that hardens on drying
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
a natural or synthetic sticky substance from plants, used as an adhesive or for making things stick together

Examples

gum as glue
Many traditional adhesives were made from natural gum.
The tree began to ooze a sticky gum after it was injured.
Tragacanth is a gum obtained from the sap of certain shrubs.
Artists sometimes use gum to affix paper to boards.
She applied a thin layer of gum to seal the envelope.
The label peeled off because the gum had dried out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glue: An adhesive substance used for sticking objects or materials together
  • synonym – paste: A soft, wet adhesive used for sticking paper and other materials
  • synonym – sealant: A substance used to block the passage of fluids through the surface or joints or openings in materials
