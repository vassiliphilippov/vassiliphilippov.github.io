Veda

noun
ancient Hindu sacred scriptures
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪdə / (AmE), / ˈveːdə / (BrE)
any of the most ancient and sacred Hindu scriptures, composed in early Sanskrit and traditionally comprising the Samhitas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, and Upanishads.

Examples

the Veda scriptures
He devoted years to translating the Veda into English for Western readers.
Many scholars have studied the Veda to understand the origins of Hindu philosophy.
The Rig Veda is one of the oldest known religious texts in the world.

  • synonym – Upanishad: philosophical texts forming part of the Vedas
