pure

adjective
theoretical, not applied; academic
Pronunciation: / pjʊr / (AmE), / pjʊə / (BrE)
(of a subject or discipline) concerned with theory and data rather than practical application; theoretical rather than applied.

Examples

pure mathematics
She prefers to conduct pure research rather than seek immediate technological applications.
The university offers both pure and applied science degrees.
Unlike engineering, pure mathematics focuses on abstract concepts rather than practical uses.

Related Terms

  • antonym – applied: concerned with practical applications rather than theory
