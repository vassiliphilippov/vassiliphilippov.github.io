to plot

verb
plan the sequence of a story
Pronunciation: / plɑt / (AmE), / plɒt / (BrE)
to plan or devise the sequence of events in a literary work, play, film, or similar narrative

Examples

to plot a novel
He plotted the entire ballet around a single dramatic event.
Screenwriters must carefully plot each twist in a television series.
The author spent months plotting her crime novel before writing a single chapter.

Related Terms

  • antonym – improvise: to create or perform spontaneously, without preparation
  • synonym – plan: to decide on and arrange in advance
