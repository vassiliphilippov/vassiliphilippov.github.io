late-ripening

adjective
ripening late in the season (plants)
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪtˈraɪpənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌleɪtˈrʌɪp(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant or fruit) ripening later in the growing season, especially in the fall.

Examples

late-ripening apples
Gardeners prefer late-ripening grapes for making wine after the summer heat has passed.
Peaches that are late-ripening often have a deeper flavor due to a longer growing period.
This variety of apple is known for being late-ripening and is harvested in October.
