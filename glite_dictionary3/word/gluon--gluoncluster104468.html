gluon

noun
particle mediating strong force
Pronunciation: / ˈɡluˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡluːɒn / (BrE)
A subatomic particle that acts as an exchange particle for the strong force between quarks.

Examples

a gluon particle
In quantum chromodynamics, the gluon is the mediator of the strong nuclear force.
Researchers are studying how gluons contribute to the mass of atomic nuclei.
The gluon is responsible for holding quarks together inside protons and neutrons.
