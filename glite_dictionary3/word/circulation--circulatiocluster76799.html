circulation

noun
movement of air, fluid, or sap through a system
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝː.kjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the movement or flow of a substance, such as air, blood, or fluid, within a system or organism

Examples

circulation of blood
Opening the windows improves the circulation of fresh air in the room.
Plants depend on the circulation of water and nutrients to survive.
Scientists study ocean circulation to understand climate changes.
Cold weather can affect your body's circulation, making your hands and feet feel numb.
Regular exercise can help improve circulation and overall heart health.
The doctor checked his circulation by measuring his pulse and observing blood flow to the extremities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blood flow: movement of blood through the circulatory system
  • synonym – flow: continuous movement in a current, especially of liquids, gases, or electricity
