to mortify

verb
(of tissue) undergo necrosis or gangrene
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːr.t̬əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(of tissue) to undergo necrosis; to become dead or gangrenous as a result of injury or disease.

Examples

mortify injured tissue
Doctors examined the wound and saw that some cells had already begun to mortify.
If the blood supply is not restored, the tissue may mortify and require amputation.
Without prompt treatment, infected tissue can rapidly mortify and spread infection.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – gangrene: localized tissue death due to lack of blood supply
  • synonym – necrose: (of tissue) to undergo necrosis
