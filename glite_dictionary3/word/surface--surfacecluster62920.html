to surface

verb
rise or come to the top; become visible
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪs / (BrE)
to rise or come to the top, or to become visible or known after being hidden

Examples

to surface after diving
After several minutes, the divers finally surfaced near the boat.

She tends to surface at family gatherings after months of no contact.
The missing documents did not surface until years after the investigation had closed.
