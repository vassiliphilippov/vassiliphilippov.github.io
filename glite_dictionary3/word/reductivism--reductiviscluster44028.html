reductivism

noun
art movement emphasizing simplicity
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌktɪvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an art movement in sculpture and painting, originating in the 1950s, characterized by extreme simplification of form and color

Examples

reductivism in art
His approach to design borrows heavily from reductivism, favoring only essential forms and colors.
Many artists in the 1960s were influenced by reductivism and strove for purity in their work.
The gallery's new exhibition showcases the principles of reductivism in both sculpture and painting.

  • synonym – minimalism: art movement focused on simple, geometric forms and limited color palette
