silage

noun
fermented animal feed from green crops
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
fodder such as grass or corn that is harvested when green and preserved by fermentation in a silo to be used as animal feed

Examples

silage for cows
Cows at the dairy farm are fed silage to provide them with essential nutrients year-round.
Preparing high-quality silage requires careful management of moisture levels and fermentation.
The farmer stored the freshly cut grass as silage for the winter months.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hay: dried grass used as animal feed
