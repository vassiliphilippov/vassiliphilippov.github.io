bourn

noun
archaic word for boundary or limit
Pronunciation: / bɜrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
(Archaic) A boundary or limit, especially of land; a dividing line.

Examples

bourn between fields
He journeyed beyond the known bourns of his land.
In literature, death is sometimes called the undiscovered bourn from which no traveler returns.
The river served as the bourn between the two ancient kingdoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boundary: a line that marks the limits of an area; a dividing line
