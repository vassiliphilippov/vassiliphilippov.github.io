fragile

adjective
delicate; easily damaged or harmed
Pronunciation: / ˈfrædʒ.əl / (AmE), / ˈfrædʒ.aɪl / (BrE)
delicate and easily damaged, both physically or emotionally; vulnerable to harm or injury.

Examples

fragile health condition
After her illness, she seemed fragile and in need of rest.
The negotiations are in a fragile state and could collapse at any moment.
Their fragile peace was broken by a single misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delicate: easily damaged or requiring careful handling
  • antonym – robust: strong and not easily damaged or affected
