callosectomy

noun
surgical cutting of corpus callosum (brain)
Pronunciation: / ˌkæloʊˈsɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌkæləʊˈsɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the severing of the corpus callosum to interrupt communication between the cerebral hemispheres, usually to treat severe epilepsy

Examples

callosectomy for epilepsy
A callosectomy is generally considered when medications fail to control epilepsy.
After undergoing a callosectomy, the patient's epileptic episodes decreased significantly.
The neurosurgeon recommended a callosectomy for the patient with severe uncontrolled seizures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corpus callosotomy: a similar procedure severing the corpus callosum to treat epilepsy
