tissue

noun
aggregate of similar cells in an organism
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈtɪs.juː / (BrE)
A group of cells in an organism that have a similar structure and function.

Examples

muscle tissue
Human muscle tissue is responsible for movement throughout the body.
Scar tissue can develop after an injury heals.
The scientist examined the tissue sample under a microscope for signs of disease.
