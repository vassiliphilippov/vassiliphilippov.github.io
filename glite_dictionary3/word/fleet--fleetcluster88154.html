to fleet

verb
move rapidly and lightly
Pronunciation: / fliːt / (AmE), / fliːt / (BrE)
to move quickly and lightly over a surface; to skim or dart

Examples

to fleet across water
Clouds fleeted across the sky as the wind began to rise.
Her shadow fleeting through the hallway reminded me of a wisp of smoke.
The dragonflies fleet over the still surface of the pond each morning.
