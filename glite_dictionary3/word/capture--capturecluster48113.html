to capture

verb
to catch or seize after a chase
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.tʃɚ /; / ˈkæptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə /; / ˈkæptʃə / (BrE)
to catch, seize, or take control of a person, animal, place, or thing, often by force or after a chase

Examples

to capture a wild animal
It took hours to capture the escaped dog.
The police managed to capture the burglar after a long chase.
They finally captured the flag and won the game.
The army finally captured the city after a long siege.
The rebels planned to capture the capital during the night offensive.
Troops moved quickly to capture enemy positions along the border.
The documentary showed how difficult it is to capture wildlife on camera.
The researchers managed to capture a rare butterfly in the forest reserve.
They set a trap overnight to capture the troublesome raccoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – catch: to take hold of someone or something, especially after trying to do so
  • synonym – conquer: take control of a place or people by military force
  • antonym – release: to allow someone or something to go free
  • synonym – seize: to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
  • antonym – surrender: to give up control, especially after defeat
