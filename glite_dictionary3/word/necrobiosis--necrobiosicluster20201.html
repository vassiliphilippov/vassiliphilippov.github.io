necrobiosis

noun
normal cell death and degeneration
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛk.roʊ.baɪˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛk.rəʊ.baɪˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
the normal degeneration and death of living cells in tissues, especially in epithelial tissues

Examples

necrobiosis of skin cells
Certain skin disorders involve abnormal necrobiosis of connective tissue cells.
The process of necrobiosis ensures that old skin cells are regularly replaced by new ones.
Under the microscope, the pathologist noted signs of necrobiosis in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym – apoptosis: programmed cell death in organisms
  • antonym – necrosis: death of body tissue due to disease or injury
