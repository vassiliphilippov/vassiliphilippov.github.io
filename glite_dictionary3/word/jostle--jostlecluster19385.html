to jostle

verb
push or shove to move or compete
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒsəl / (BrE)
to push, elbow, or bump against someone, especially in a crowd, to make your way or compete for space

Examples

to jostle through crowds
Commuters often jostle for space on the busy subway trains during rush hour.
She skillfully jostled her way to the front of the line without causing offense.
We had to jostle through the crowd to catch a glimpse of the performers.
He tried not to jostle anyone as he made his way through the busy hallway.
Shoppers began to jostle for position as the store doors opened.
The runners jostled for the lead during the chaotic start of the race.

  • synonym – push: move someone or something away by pressing against it
