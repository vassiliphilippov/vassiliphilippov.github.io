Corinthian

noun
pleasure-seeking man (historical)
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɪnθiən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪnθɪən / (BrE)
A man devoted to the pursuit of pleasure and luxurious living, often used of wealthy or fashionable men in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Examples

wealthy Corinthian gentleman
He fancied himself a Corinthian, frequenting the most fashionable clubs and races in the city.
In Regency England, a true Corinthian was renowned for his lavish lifestyle and love of entertainment.
The novel's protagonist, a notorious Corinthian, squandered his fortune on gambling and parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hedonist: a person who pursues pleasure as a goal
