margay

noun
spotted wildcat of Central and South America
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrˌɡeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːɡeɪ / (BrE)
a small, spotted wildcat (Leopardus wiedii) native to Central and South America, known for its agility and tree-dwelling habits.

Examples

wild margay cat
Researchers spotted a margay prowling through the undergrowth in Brazil's Atlantic Forest.
The margay is an expert climber, rarely coming down from the treetops in the rainforest.
Unlike the ocelot, the margay has ankles that can turn to help it descend trees headfirst.
