beneficed

adjective
holding a church benefice
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛnəfɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪst / (BrE)
(of a member of the clergy) having been granted a benefice, typically an ecclesiastical office that provides an income or property.

Examples

beneficed priest
He aspired to be beneficed so he could support his growing family.
Only beneficed clergy could vote in certain church matters at the time.
The village church was served by a beneficed priest for over fifty years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unbeneficed: not holding a benefice (without a church office or income)
