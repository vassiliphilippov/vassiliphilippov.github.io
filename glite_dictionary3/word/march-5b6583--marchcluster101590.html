march

noun
public procession or demonstration
Pronunciation: / mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a public procession or the act of walking with regular, organized steps, especially as part of a group (such as in a protest, demonstration, or military event)

Examples

march for peace
Police blocked traffic to allow the peaceful march to proceed along Main Street.
The annual pride march celebrates diversity and inclusion.
Thousands gathered to join the march for climate action in the city center.
After hours on the march, the soldiers finally reached their resting place.
The protestors began their march through the city streets at noon.
We watched the annual march honoring veterans on television.

Related Terms

  • antonym – amble: to walk at a slow, relaxed pace
  • synonym – demonstration: a public meeting or march protesting against or supporting something
  • synonym – parade: a public procession, usually celebratory
