tablespoon

noun
unit of measurement (about 15 ml)
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪ.bəlˌspoʊn / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.bəl.spuːn / (BrE)
an amount of substance equal to what a tablespoon can hold (approximately 15 milliliters); used as a unit of measurement in cooking and medicine

Examples

tablespoon of sugar
She added a tablespoon of sugar to her tea.
Take one tablespoon of medicine after each meal.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of olive oil.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cup: a larger unit of measure, usually about 240 ml
  • degree lower – teaspoon: a smaller unit of measure, about 5 ml
