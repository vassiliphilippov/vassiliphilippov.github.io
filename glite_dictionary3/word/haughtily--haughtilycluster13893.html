haughtily

adverb
in an arrogant or superior way
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔːtɪli / (AmE), / ˈhɔːtɪli / (BrE)
In a proud, unfriendly, or arrogantly superior manner.

Examples

speak haughtily to others
He haughtily dismissed the suggestion, convinced it was beneath him.
She spoke haughtily to the staff, making everyone feel uncomfortable.
The princess nodded haughtily, barely acknowledging the crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modestly: in a humble or unassuming manner
  • synonym – proudly: in a way that shows satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements
