cinchona

noun
tree genus; source of quinine
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈtʃoʊnə / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə / (BrE)
Any of several South American trees of the genus Cinchona, whose bark is used to produce quinine and other medicinal alkaloids.

Examples

cinchona tree Andes
Early explorers in South America collected cinchona bark to export to Europe.
Quinine, derived from cinchona, revolutionized the treatment of tropical diseases.
The bark of the cinchona tree is famous for its use in treating malaria.
