to push

verb
advocate or campaign vigorously for something
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ / (AmE), / pʊʃ / (BrE)
to make a determined effort to achieve something or to advocate strongly for a goal or cause

Examples

to push for change
After years of effort, the group continued to push for stronger environmental laws.
Community leaders pushed for better healthcare services in the rural areas.
He is pushing his agenda despite significant resistance from colleagues.
He continues to push for better results, even when others give up.
If we push hard this month, we can finish the entire project on time.
She had to push herself to complete the marathon despite the pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – advocate: to publicly support or recommend a particular cause or policy
  • synonym – campaign: to actively work toward a particular goal, especially in politics or social causes
  • synonym – drive: to work hard and with determination to achieve a goal
  • antonym – relax: to make less effort or take things easy
  • synonym – strive: to make great efforts to achieve or obtain something
