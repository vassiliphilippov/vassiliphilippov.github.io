variable

noun
something subject to change or variation
Pronunciation: / ˈver.i.ə.bəl /; / ˈvɛriəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈveəri.ə.bəl /; / ˈveəriəbl̩ / (BrE)
something that can change or be changed, such as a factor or quantity in science or research

Examples

key variable in research
Economic growth is influenced by a number of variables, including interest rates and consumer confidence.
In any experiment, temperature is an important variable that must be controlled.
The weather is one variable that can affect the outcome of a sporting event.
In the experiment, temperature was a variable that was carefully measured.
Researches often control for one variable to isolate the effect of another.
The value of the variable x increases as you move along the graph.

Related Terms

  • antonym – constant: something that does not change
  • synonym – parameter: a quantity that influences the output or behavior but is held constant in an experiment
