to typecast

verb
to classify as belonging to a type
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪpˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpˌkɑːst /; / ˈtaɪpkɑːst / (BrE)
to always see or treat someone as belonging to a particular type or group, especially by repeatedly giving them the same kind of role or job

Examples

typecast an actor
He was often typecast as the villain because of his appearance.
It is unfair to typecast children based on their family backgrounds.
Some critics typecast the novel as just another detective story, ignoring its deeper themes.
He was typecast as a villain after his breakout role in the movie.
Many actors fear being typecast and missing out on a variety of roles.
The director decided not to typecast her, offering her a part unlike any she had played before.
