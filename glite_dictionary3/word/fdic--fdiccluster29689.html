FDIC

noun
US federal bank deposit insurer
Pronunciation: / ˌɛf di aɪ ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌɛf diː aɪ ˈsiː / (BrE)
A United States government agency that insures deposits in banks and savings institutions to protect depositors against bank failures.

Examples

FDIC insures deposits
After the bank failed, the FDIC stepped in to protect the customers' savings.
Many customers feel more secure knowing their bank is insured by the FDIC.
The FDIC guarantees deposits up to $250,000 per account holder.
