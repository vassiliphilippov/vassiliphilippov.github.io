quassia

noun
bitter compound from tropical trees, used medicinally
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːsiə / (AmE), / ˈkwæsɪə / (BrE)
a bitter substance obtained from the wood and bark of certain tropical trees, used as an insecticide, tonic, and vermifuge

Examples

quassia extract medicine
Quassia chips are steeped in water to produce a solution used for treating intestinal worms.
Gardeners sometimes use a quassia extract as a natural insecticide to protect plants from pests.
The herbal tonic was flavored with quassia for its bitter taste and medicinal benefits.
