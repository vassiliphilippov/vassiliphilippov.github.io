to trap

verb
to hold fast; prevent from escaping
Pronunciation: / træp / (AmE), / træp / (BrE)
to catch, hold, or prevent someone or something from escaping, either literally or figuratively

Examples

to trap a person
Rising water quickly trapped the car on the flooded street.
She accidentally trapped her finger in the door.
The miners were trapped underground for several days after the explosion.
The new regulations trap many people in awkward legal situations.
The small cracks in the wall trap dust and dirt over time.
They managed to trap the butterfly safely under a glass jar.
During the meeting, he was trapped by an unexpected question.
She felt trapped in a job she no longer enjoyed.
The rebels trapped the soldiers inside the fortress for days.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capture: to catch or seize, especially after a chase
  • synonym – catch: to capture something so it cannot escape
  • synonym – corner: to force someone into a situation where they have no escape
  • antonym – free: to release or allow something to escape
  • antonym – release: to set free or allow to escape
