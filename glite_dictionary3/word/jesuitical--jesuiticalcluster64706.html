Jesuitical

adjective
of or relating to Jesuits; sometimes cunning
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛzjuˈɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛz.juˈɪt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Characteristic of, or relating to, Jesuits or Jesuitism; sometimes used to mean cunning, crafty, or subtle in argument.

Examples

Jesuitical argument
His Jesuitical reasoning made it difficult to know what he truly believed.
Some critics described the politician's evasive answers as Jesuitical in nature.
The school's Jesuitical traditions influence its approach to education and discipline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Jesuit: a member of the Society of Jesus, a Catholic religious order
