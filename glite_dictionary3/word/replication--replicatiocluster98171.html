replication

noun
plaintiff's reply to a defendant's plea (law)
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) A formal reply made by a plaintiff to the defendant's plea or answer in a legal proceeding.

Examples

legal replication statement
In his replication, the plaintiff addressed all points raised in the defense's plea.
The court allowed the submission of a second replication due to new evidence.
The plaintiff filed a replication challenging the facts stated in the defendant's answer.
