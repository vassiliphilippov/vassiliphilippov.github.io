TDT

noun
uniform astronomical time standard
Pronunciation: / ti di ti / (AmE), / tiː diː tiː / (BrE)
an astronomical time standard based on Earth's orbital motion, used as a uniform measure of time; terrestrial time defined to correct for variations in mean solar time.

Examples

TDT time standard
Astronomers use TDT to ensure their measurements are consistent around the world.
Spacecraft navigation relies on precise TDT values to predict trajectories.
The difference between TDT and Universal Time can be important for certain calculations.
