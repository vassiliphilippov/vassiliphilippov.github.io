antiproton

noun
antiparticle of a proton
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈproʊ.tɑn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈprəʊ.tɒn / (BrE)
A subatomic particle that is the antiparticle of a proton, having the same mass as a proton but a negative electric charge and instability.

Examples

an antiproton particle
Physicists at CERN successfully created and trapped several antiproton particles for study.
The experiment measured the properties of the antiproton with unprecedented accuracy.
When a antiproton meets a proton, they annihilate each other, releasing energy.
