titanium

noun
strong, light metallic element (symbol Ti)
Pronunciation: / taɪˈteɪniəm / (AmE), / tɪˈteɪniəm / (BrE)
a strong, light, corrosion-resistant metallic chemical element with the symbol Ti, used especially in alloys for aircraft and other high-performance applications

Examples

titanium alloy parts
Modern jet engines make extensive use of titanium to reduce weight and withstand high temperatures.
Rutile and ilmenite are two minerals from which titanium is commonly extracted.
The frame of the bicycle is made entirely from titanium for strength and durability.
