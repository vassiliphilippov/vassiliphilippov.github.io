to malinger

verb
pretend illness to avoid work
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪŋɚ / (AmE), / məˈlɪŋɡə / (BrE)
to pretend to be ill or injured in order to avoid work or duty

Examples

to malinger at work
Every Monday, Tom tries to malinger so he can miss the team meeting.
Soldiers who malinger during training face serious consequences.
The supervisor suspected one of the workers was malingering to get out of difficult tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dodge: to avoid doing something, especially by trickery
  • synonym – shirk: to avoid or neglect a duty or responsibility
