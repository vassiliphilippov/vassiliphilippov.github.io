admonition

noun
firm warning or reprimand
Pronunciation: / ˌæd.məˈnɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌæd.məˈnɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a warning or piece of advice, especially to correct someone or to caution about possible danger or problems

Examples

a gentle admonition
Despite her mother's admonition, she stayed out past midnight.
He received a gentle admonition rather than a severe punishment for his mistake.
The teacher's admonition left the class silent and attentive for the rest of the period.
Despite his teacher's admonition, he continued to procrastinate on his assignments.
In her speech, she gave an admonition about the consequences of reckless driving.
The hikers ignored the ranger's admonition and ventured into the dangerous area.
