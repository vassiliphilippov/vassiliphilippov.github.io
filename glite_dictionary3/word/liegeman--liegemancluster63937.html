liegeman

noun
man owing service to a feudal lord
Pronunciation: / ˈliːdʒˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈliːdʒmən / (BrE)
a man who owes allegiance and service to a feudal lord, especially during the Middle Ages

Examples

liegeman serves a lord
Every liegeman was expected to defend his lord against all adversaries.
He pledged to remain a loyal liegeman until the end of his days.
The liegeman knelt and swore his oath of fealty before the king.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lord: a person who has power or authority, especially a landowner in feudal times
  • synonym – vassal: a person in a subordinate position who owes allegiance and service to a feudal lord
