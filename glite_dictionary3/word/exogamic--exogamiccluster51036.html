exogamic

adjective
relating to cross-fertilization in plants
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksəˈɡæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛksəˈɡamɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) suitable for fertilization by another not closely related individual; promoting cross-fertilization.

Examples

exogamic plant species
Researchers studied the exogamic mechanisms that prevent self-fertilization in the species.
Some plant species have evolved exogamic flowers to promote genetic diversity.
The exogamic tendency in these wildflowers ensures pollen comes from distant populations.
