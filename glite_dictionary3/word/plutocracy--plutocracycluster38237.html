plutocracy

noun
government by the wealthy
Pronunciation: / pluˈtɑkrəsi / (AmE), / pluːˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A political system or society in which the wealthy class rules or has control over government and decision-making.

Examples

wealthy plutocracy rules
Critics argue that campaign finance laws have turned the democracy into a plutocracy.
The country was accused of becoming a plutocracy where only the rich had any political influence.
Throughout history, several societies have drifted toward plutocracy during periods of great wealth inequality.
