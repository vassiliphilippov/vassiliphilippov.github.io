Tay-Sachs

noun
hereditary metabolic disorder causing nerve damage
Pronunciation: / teɪ ˈsæks / (AmE), / teɪ ˈsæks / (BrE)
A rare hereditary disorder of lipid metabolism, mainly affecting people of Eastern European Jewish descent, characterized by the accumulation of lipids in nerve cells and leading to early childhood death.

Carriers of the Tay-Sachs gene can be identified through genetic testing.
