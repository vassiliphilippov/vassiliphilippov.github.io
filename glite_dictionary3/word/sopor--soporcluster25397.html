sopor

noun
deep, unnatural sleep or stupor
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.pɔɚ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.pɔː / (BrE)
A state of deep, unnatural sleep or stupor, often caused by illness or drugs.

Examples

deep sopor state
After the accident, he fell into a sopor from which it was hard to rouse him.
The heat of the afternoon induced a sopor that made thinking difficult.
The patient's sopor worried the medical staff, who monitored her closely.
