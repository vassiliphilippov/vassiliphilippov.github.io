immemorial

adjective
originating beyond memory or recorded history
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈmɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈmɔːriəl / (BrE)
existing or originating in the distant past, so old that no one remembers or records it

Examples

from time immemorial
The oak tree seems as if it has stood there from time immemorial.
Their dispute dates back to an immemorial era.
These traditions have been followed since immemorial times.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ancient: belonging to the very distant past
  • antonym – modern: relating to the present or recent times
