presumptuously

adverb
in an overconfident or bold manner
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzʌmp.tʃu.əs.li / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmp.tjʊ.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows too much confidence or boldness, especially by overstepping appropriate boundaries.

Examples

to speak presumptuously
He presumptuously assumed he could speak on behalf of the entire group.
The young intern presumptuously challenged the manager's decision during the meeting.
You shouldn't presumptuously judge others without knowing their situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modestly: in a humble or unassuming manner
  • synonym – presumptuous: showing too much confidence or boldness
