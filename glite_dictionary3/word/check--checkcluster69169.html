to check

verb
to mark with a check mark
Pronunciation: / tʃɛk / (AmE), / tʃɛk / (BrE)
to mark something with a check mark to indicate it has been chosen, verified, or completed, or to create a pattern of squares or crossed lines

Examples

to check boxes
He checked the completed items on his to-do list.
Please check each box that applies to you.
Remember to check your answers before handing in the test.
Artists sometimes check their canvases to help with proportion and placement.
He checked the paper with a pencil so students could solve the puzzle easily.
The tablecloth was checked with red and white squares.
