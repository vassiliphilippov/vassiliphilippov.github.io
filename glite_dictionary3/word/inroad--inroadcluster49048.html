inroad

noun
encroachment or intrusion
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪnrəʊd / (BrE)
an advance into an area that affects it; a significant encroachment or intrusion, often into a market, field, or sphere

Examples

make inroads into business
Education reforms have started to create inroads against illiteracy in remote areas.
The new technology is beginning to make inroads into traditional manufacturing methods.
With improved advertising, the company made significant inroads into the Asian market.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encroachment: the act of gradually taking over someone else's rights or property
  • synonym – intrusion: the act of entering a place or situation without permission
