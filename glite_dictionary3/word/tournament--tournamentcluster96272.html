tournament

noun
medieval series of jousts
Pronunciation: / ˈtʊrnəmənt, ˈtɜrnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtʊənəmənt, ˈtɔːnəmənt / (BrE)
In the Middle Ages, a series of jousts or contests between knights competing for a prize.

Examples

medieval tournament
Knights from all over Europe gathered for the annual tournament in the royal city.
The tournament provided an opportunity for young noblemen to demonstrate their skill and bravery.
The king sponsored a grand tournament to celebrate the princess's birthday.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – duel: A fight between two people, usually with weapons, to settle a matter of honor.
  • synonym – joust: A combat between two knights on horseback with lances.
