to overtax

verb
tax excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtæks / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtæks / (BrE)
to tax someone or something too heavily; to require payment of taxes beyond what is reasonable or fair

Examples

to overtax the people
If you overtax the citizens, it could lead to public unrest.
The government decided not to overtax small businesses to encourage growth.
They warned that new policies might overtax already struggling families.

Related Terms

  • antonym – undertax: to tax less than is necessary or fair
