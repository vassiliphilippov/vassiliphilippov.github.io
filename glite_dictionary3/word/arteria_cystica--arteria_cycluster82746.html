arteria cystica

noun
artery supplying the gall bladder
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə sɪsˈtiːkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə sɪsˈtiːkə / (BrE)
a branch of the hepatic artery that supplies blood to the gall bladder and the surface of the liver

Examples

arteria cystica supplies gallbladder
Injury to the arteria cystica can lead to significant bleeding during a cholecystectomy.
The arteria cystica is commonly found as a branch of the right hepatic artery during surgery.
