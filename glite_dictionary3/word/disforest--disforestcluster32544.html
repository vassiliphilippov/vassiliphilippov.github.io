to disforest

verb
remove all trees from land
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfɔɹɪst / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɒrɪst / (BrE)
to remove all the trees from (an area of land); to deforest.

Examples

to disforest a hillside
It took decades to recover the land after it was disforested by loggers.
Many species lost their habitats when the hills were disforested for agriculture.
The government decided to disforest the area to make way for new roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deforest: to clear an area of trees
