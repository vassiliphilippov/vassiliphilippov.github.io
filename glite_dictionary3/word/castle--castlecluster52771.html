castle

noun
chess move: king and rook switched
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːsəl / (BrE)
(in chess) a move in which the king and a rook are moved at the same time, switching their positions according to specific rules.

Examples

a castle move
After he decided to castle, his defense became much stronger.
It's often a good idea to castle early in the game to protect your king.
You cannot castle if your king or rook has already moved.

Related Terms

  • synonym – castling: the process or act of performing the castle move in chess
