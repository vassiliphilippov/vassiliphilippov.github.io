segregation

noun
the act of separating or setting apart
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action or state of setting someone or something apart from others or the act of separating groups, often by law or policy

Examples

jury segregation
Jury segregation was ordered to prevent outside influence during the trial.
Racial segregation was enforced in some schools during the 20th century.
The segregation of waste materials helps improve recycling efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym – integration: the process of combining or mixing different groups or things
  • synonym – separation: the action or state of moving or being moved apart
