to supplant

verb
replace or take the place of
Pronunciation: / səˈplænt / (AmE), / səˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to take the place of someone or something, especially by force or as something more suitable

Examples

to supplant a rival
He hoped to supplant his rival as chairperson of the committee.
New technologies often supplant older methods of communication.
The smartphone has supplanted traditional cameras for many casual photographers.
