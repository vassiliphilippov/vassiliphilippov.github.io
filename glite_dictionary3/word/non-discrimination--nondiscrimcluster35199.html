non-discrimination

noun
the practice of treating people without prejudice or bias
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪˌskrɪm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˌskrɪm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the practice of treating people equally and fairly, without prejudice or bias based on personal characteristics such as race, gender, or religion

Examples

non-discrimination policy
Laws promoting non-discrimination help protect minority groups from unfair treatment.
The company is committed to non-discrimination in its hiring policies.
The principle of non-discrimination is fundamental to human rights.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discrimination: unfair treatment of people based on personal characteristics
  • synonym – equality: the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
