snatch

noun
brief fragment; small part of something
Pronunciation: / snætʃ / (AmE), / snætʃ / (BrE)
a small part or brief fragment of something, especially of sound, conversation, or music.

Examples

snatch of music
He remembered a snatch of her laughter from the crowded room.
Only a snatch of the melody reached his ears before the band stopped playing.
She caught a snatch of their conversation as she walked by the office door.
