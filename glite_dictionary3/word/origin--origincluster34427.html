origin

noun
intersection point of coordinate axes
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrədʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
the fixed point where the axes of a coordinate system intersect, usually where all coordinates are zero

Examples

origin of coordinates
In the Cartesian plane, the origin is where the x-axis and y-axis meet.
The coordinates of the origin are always (0, 0) in two-dimensional space.
The graph is centered at the origin to simplify calculations.
