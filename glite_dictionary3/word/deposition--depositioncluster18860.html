deposition

noun
legal pretrial witness statement
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) a formal statement made by a witness under oath, taken outside of court before a trial.

Examples

give a deposition
During her deposition, the witness provided crucial information about the case.
The judge reviewed the deposition transcripts before the trial began.
The lawyer scheduled the deposition for next Friday afternoon.
