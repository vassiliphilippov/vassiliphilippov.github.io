Qin Shi Huang Ti

proper noun
first emperor of unified China, died 210 BC
Pronunciation: / tʃɪn ʃi hwɑŋ ˈti / (AmE), / tʃɪn ʃi hwæŋ ˈtiː / (BrE)
the first emperor of the Qin dynasty, who unified China, initiated construction of the Great Wall, and instituted major reforms in law, currency, and measurement; died 210 BC

Examples

Qin Shi Huang Ti unified China
Qin Shi Huang Ti introduced sweeping reforms that standardized currency and measurement across China.

The terracotta army was built to guard the tomb of Qin Shi Huang Ti.
