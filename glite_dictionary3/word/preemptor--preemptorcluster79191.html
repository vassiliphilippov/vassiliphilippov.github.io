preemptor

noun
bridge player making a preemptive bid
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priːˈɛmptə / (BrE)
A bridge player who makes a preemptive bid, attempting to disrupt opponents' communication by opening the bidding at a high level with a weak hand.

Examples

skilled preemptor in bridge
A good preemptor knows when to take calculated risks to put pressure on the other side.
As a skilled preemptor, she frequently disrupted the opponents' bidding strategy.
The team's success often depended on having an effective preemptor at the table.
