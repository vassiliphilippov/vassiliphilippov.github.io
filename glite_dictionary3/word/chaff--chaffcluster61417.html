chaff

noun
husks or seed coverings from grain
Pronunciation: / tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
the husks of grains and grasses separated during threshing, or the seed coverings and small, dry pieces left after seeds are processed

Examples

chaff and grain
After harvesting, the seeds were cleaned of all chaff and debris.
The farmer used a machine to separate the chaff from the wheat.
Wind scattered the chaff across the barn floor during threshing.
