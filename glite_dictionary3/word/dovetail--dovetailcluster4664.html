to dovetail

verb
fit together perfectly
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊvˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌv.teɪl / (BrE)
to fit or combine things together so that they work well and harmoniously

Examples

to dovetail plans together
She searched for a solution that would dovetail her career goals with her family life.
The new evidence dovetailed with the suspect's alibi, supporting his account of the events.
Their two proposals dovetail nicely, so implementing both should be straightforward.
Her research findings dovetail nicely with existing theories in the field.
The interior designer was able to dovetail traditional and modern elements beautifully.
We should try to dovetail our schedules to avoid any overlap during the renovations.
