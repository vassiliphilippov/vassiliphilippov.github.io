Ojibway

noun
Algonquian language of the Ojibwa people
Pronunciation: / oʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Ojibwa people, chiefly in the regions of southern Canada and the northern United States.

Examples

Ojibway language
Many traditional stories are still told in Ojibway among elders in the community.
She has studied the Ojibway language to understand indigenous Canadian cultures.
The dictionary contains over 6,000 words from Ojibway and their English translations.
