Capsicum frutescens

noun
species of hot pepper plant
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpsɪkəm fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkapsɪkəm fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (BrE)
a tropical plant species bearing very hot, medium-sized oblong red peppers, cultivated mainly for making hot sauces

Examples

Capsicum frutescens hot peppers
Farmers in Louisiana grow Capsicum frutescens for the production of Tabasco sauce.
Many varieties of Capsicum frutescens are used to make spicy hot sauces.
The fruit of Capsicum frutescens is small, bright red, and extremely hot.
