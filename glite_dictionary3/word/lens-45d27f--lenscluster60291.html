lens

noun
transparent structure in the eye focusing light
Pronunciation: / lɛnz / (AmE), / lɛnz / (BrE)
a transparent object (such as in the eye, a camera, or glasses) that focuses or disperses light to form an image

Examples

lens of a camera
Changes in the shape of the lens allow us to focus on objects at various distances.
During the surgery, the damaged lens was replaced with an artificial one.
The lens of the eye becomes cloudy in cases of cataracts.
A magnifying glass uses a convex lens to enlarge small objects.
She cleaned her glasses to remove fingerprints from the lens.
The photographer changed the lens to capture a wider angle for the landscape shot.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cornea: the transparent front part of the eye covering the iris and pupil
  • synonym – objective: main lens or lens system in a microscope or telescope
