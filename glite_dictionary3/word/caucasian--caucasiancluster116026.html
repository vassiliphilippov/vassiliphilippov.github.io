Caucasian

noun
languages spoken in the Caucasus region
Pronunciation: / kɔˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / kɔːˈkeɪzɪən / (BrE)
any of a group of languages spoken in the Caucasus region, which are not related to languages spoken elsewhere.

Examples

Caucasian language family
Linguists study Caucasian languages to understand their unique grammar systems.
Some Caucasian languages have sounds that are rare in other parts of the world.
The Caucasian languages include Georgian, Chechen, and Abkhaz.
