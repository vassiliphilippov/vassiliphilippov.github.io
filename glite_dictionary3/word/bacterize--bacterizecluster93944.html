to bacterize

verb
treat with bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˈbæk.təˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbæk.tə.raɪz / (BrE)
to subject (a substance or material) to the action or effect of bacteria, often in order to induce a chemical or biological change

Examples

to bacterize milk
Cheese can be bacterized intentionally to develop specific flavors during production.
The laboratory aimed to bacterize the nutrient solution to promote fermentation.
To improve soil fertility, farmers sometimes bacterize compost before spreading it on the fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inoculate: to introduce microorganisms, such as bacteria, into a medium
  • antonym – sterilize: to make free from bacteria or other living microorganisms
