prismoid

noun
geometric solid with similar polygonal bases
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪz.mɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈprɪz.mɔɪd / (BrE)
A solid geometric figure whose two parallel bases are polygons with the same number of sides and whose other faces are either trapezoids or parallelograms.

Examples

a prismoid shape
The engineer modeled the bridge support as a prismoid to simplify the calculations.
The shape of the building resembled a prismoid with its parallel polygonal bases.
To find the volume of a prismoid, use the prismoidal formula.
