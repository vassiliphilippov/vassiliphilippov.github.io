sensitization

noun
becoming more sensitive to a stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛnsətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person or organism becomes highly sensitive to specific events, situations, or stimuli, especially emotionally or psychologically

Examples

sensitization to stress
After repeated exposure to stress, sensitization to minor triggers can lead to anxiety attacks.
Some therapies aim to reduce sensitization to traumatic memories in patients with PTSD.
The experiment measured the sensitization of rats to a specific sound after several pairings with a shock.

Related Terms

  • antonym – desensitization: the process of making someone less sensitive, especially to stimuli or triggers
