abbey-lubber

noun
historically, an idle resident of an abbey
Pronunciation: / ˈæbi ˌlʌb.ər / (AmE), / ˈæbi ˌlʌb.ə / (BrE)
an idle or lazy person, especially one who avoids work or outdoor activities; historically, a monk who lived in an abbey without fulfilling the associated duties

Examples

lazy abbey-lubber
He was called an abbey-lubber because he shirked all communal responsibilities.
In old tales, the abbey-lubber is often depicted dozing by the hearth rather than helping with chores.
The friar warned against becoming an abbey-lubber and neglecting honest labor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – drudge: a person made to do hard, menial, or dull work
  • synonym – idler: a person who avoids work or activity
