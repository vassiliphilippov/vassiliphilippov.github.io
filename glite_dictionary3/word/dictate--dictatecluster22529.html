to dictate

verb
to rule as a dictator
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪkˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪkteɪt /; / dɪkˈteɪt / (BrE)
to give orders or commands with authority; to control or rule

Examples

to dictate rules
For decades, the king dictated every aspect of daily life in his nation.
She fears her new boss will try to dictate the team's every move.
The general attempted to dictate the country's laws without consulting anyone.
She resented having her actions dictated by someone else.
The manager tried to dictate every aspect of the project to his team.
The new policy dictates that all employees must wear identification badges.

  • synonym – order: to direct or instruct authoritatively
  • antonym – request: to ask for something politely or formally
  • antonym – submit: to yield or surrender to authority
