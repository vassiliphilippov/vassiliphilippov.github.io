to surcharge

verb
charge an extra fee
Pronunciation: / sərˈtʃɑrdʒ /; / ˈsɜrˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / səˈtʃɑːdʒ /; / ˈsɜː.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to charge an extra or additional fee, especially for a special service or in addition to the usual cost

Examples

to surcharge a bill
If you pay by credit card, they may surcharge an extra 3% fee.
Some airlines surcharge customers for checked baggage.
The hotel surcharged us for early check-in and late checkout.
Be wary of restaurants that surcharge diners for splitting the check.
Many companies surcharge customers for paying their bills late.
The taxi driver tried to surcharge tourists by doubling the normal fare.

  • antonym – undercharge: to charge less than the correct or appropriate amount; antonym for 'surcharge' (verb)
