stachyose

noun
a plant-derived tetrasaccharide sugar
Pronunciation: / ˈstækiˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈstækɪəʊz / (BrE)
a tetrasaccharide sugar found in certain plants, especially the tubers of the Chinese artichoke and other vegetables

Examples

stachyose in beans
Stachyose is commonly found in beans and legumes, contributing to their carbohydrate content.
Consuming foods rich in stachyose may promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.
Researchers isolated stachyose from the roots of the Chinese artichoke for use in nutritional studies.
