timpani

noun
large tunable orchestral drums
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪmpəˌnaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpəni / (BrE)
A set of large hemispherical drums, usually made of brass or copper, with tunable drumheads, played in orchestras or bands.

Examples

timpani in orchestra
During the symphony, the timpani provided powerful rhythmic support to the string section.
He specialized in percussion and was particularly skilled at playing the timpani.
The orchestra's performance was heightened by the dramatic roll of the timpani.
