kakemono

noun
Japanese hanging scroll with art or writing
Pronunciation: / kɑː.keɪˈmoʊ.noʊ / (AmE), / kæ.keˈməʊ.nəʊ / (BrE)
A Japanese wall hanging, usually made of paper or silk, that is narrow and features a picture or writing, with a roller at the bottom.

Examples

Japanese kakemono painting
Experts carefully restored the faded silk of the centuries-old kakemono.
She purchased a kakemono as a souvenir from her trip to Kyoto.
The museum displayed a priceless kakemono depicting a tranquil mountain scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scroll: a roll of parchment or paper for writing or art, often used for comparison with the kakemono
