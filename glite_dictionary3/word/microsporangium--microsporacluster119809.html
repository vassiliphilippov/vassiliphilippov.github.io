microsporangium

noun
plant structure producing microspores
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊspəˈrændʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊspəˈrandʒiəm / (BrE)
A plant structure that produces microspores, typically found in the male reproductive organs of seed plants.

Examples

microsporangium on stamen
Each microsporangium develops on the stamens, producing numerous microspores.
In flowering plants, the anther serves as the microsporangium.
The microsporangium of a pine tree releases pollen grains into the air.
