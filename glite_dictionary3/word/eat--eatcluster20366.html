to eat

verb
use up resources or materials
Pronunciation: / it / (AmE), / iːt / (BrE)
(of resources or materials) to use up or consume gradually, often wastefully; to deplete.

Examples

eat up resources
Running the generators all night will eat through our fuel reserves quickly.
The new appliances eat a lot of electricity, so our bills went up.
This project is really starting to eat into our budget.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conserve: to use sparingly or save resources
  • synonym – consume: to use up, especially resources or energy
  • synonym – expend: to use up, as in spending materials or energy
