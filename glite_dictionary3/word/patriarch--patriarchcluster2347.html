patriarch

noun
early biblical founding figure
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtriˌɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑːk / (BrE)
Any of the early male figures in the Bible, such as Abraham, Isaac, or Jacob, regarded as founding fathers of the human race or of the Hebrew people.

Examples

Biblical patriarch
Abraham is often considered the most prominent patriarch in the Old Testament.
Isaac and Jacob are among the patriarchs referenced throughout the Book of Genesis.
The stories of the biblical patriarchs have shaped Western religious traditions.
