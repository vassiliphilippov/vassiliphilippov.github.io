to conscript

verb
compulsorily enroll into military service
Pronunciation: / kənˈskrɪpt / (AmE), / kənˈskrɪpt / (BrE)
to force someone by law to serve in the armed forces

Examples

to conscript young men
During the war, thousands of young men were conscripted into the army against their will.
He feared that his sons would be conscripted if a conflict broke out.
Many countries no longer conscript citizens, relying instead on volunteers for their armed forces.

Related Terms

  • synonym – draft: to select people for compulsory military service
  • antonym – enlist: to enroll in the armed forces, often voluntarily
