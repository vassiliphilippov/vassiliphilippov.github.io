indigenousness

noun
the quality of being native to a place
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪdʒənəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪdʒɪnəsnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of originating or occurring naturally in a particular place; nativeness.

Examples

cultural indigenousness
Much research has focused on the indigenousness of various communities around the world.
Preserving the indigenousness of traditional art forms is vital for maintaining cultural heritage.
The indigenousness of these plants is evident in the way they thrive without human intervention.

Related Terms

  • antonym – foreignness: the state of being foreign or not native to a place
  • synonym – indigeneity: the fact of originating in a particular place; used especially in cultural or political contexts
  • antonym – nonnativeness: the quality of not being native to a place
