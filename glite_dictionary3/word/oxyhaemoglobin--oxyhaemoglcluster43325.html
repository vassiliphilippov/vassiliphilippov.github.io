oxyhaemoglobin

noun
hemoglobin combined with oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksiˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
the bright red substance formed when hemoglobin combines with oxygen in the lungs, responsible for transporting oxygen in the blood

Examples

oxyhaemoglobin in blood
Oxyhaemoglobin is responsible for the red color of oxygenated blood.
