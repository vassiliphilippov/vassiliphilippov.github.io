countermand

noun
order cancelling a previous command
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌmænd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmænd / (BrE)
an order that cancels or reverses a previous command

Examples

countermand from headquarters
A sudden countermand from headquarters halted the deployment of troops.
The manager sent a countermand to reverse the earlier directive about overtime work.
When the general issued a countermand, all previous instructions were immediately revoked.

Related Terms

  • antonym – command: an authoritative order
