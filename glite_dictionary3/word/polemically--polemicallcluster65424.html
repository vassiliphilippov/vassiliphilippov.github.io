polemically

adverb
in a controversial or argumentative manner
Pronunciation: / poʊˈlɛmɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that involves strong criticism, controversy, or disputation.

Examples

polemically attack ideas
At the debate, she responded polemically to her opponent's arguments.
Critics claimed that the article was polemically biased and inflammatory.
The columnist wrote polemically about the government's new policy.
