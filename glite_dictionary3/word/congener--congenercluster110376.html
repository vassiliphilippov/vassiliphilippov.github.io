congener

noun
minor chemical affecting liquor character
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.dʒə.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒɪ.nə / (BrE)
a minor chemical substance in wine or liquor that contributes to its distinctive character, often affecting flavor or aroma

Examples

alcohol congener
Higher levels of congeners in whiskey are believed to contribute to its complex flavor profile.
Some people find that drinks with fewer congeners result in milder hangovers.
The distillation process can influence the variety and concentration of congeners in spirits.
