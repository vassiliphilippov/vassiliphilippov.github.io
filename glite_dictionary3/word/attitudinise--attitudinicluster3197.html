to attitudinise

verb
adopt a pretentious attitude
Pronunciation: / əˈtɪtʃəˌduːnaɪz / (AmE), / əˈtɪtjuːdɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to adopt or display an affected or pretentious attitude, especially to impress or influence others

Examples

to attitudinise for the camera
He was clearly attitudinising for the camera, trying to look intellectual.
She disapproved of people who attitudinise in social situations instead of being sincere.
The actor began to attitudinise during the interview to appear more interesting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be genuine: to behave sincerely, without affectation or pretense
  • synonym – pose: to pretend to be something one is not; to assume a particular stance or attitude
  • synonym – posture: to adopt a particular attitude or pose for effect
