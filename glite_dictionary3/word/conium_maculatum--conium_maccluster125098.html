Conium maculatum

noun
poisonous plant; hemlock
Pronunciation: / koʊˈnaɪəm ˌmækjʊˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / kəʊˈnʌɪ.əm ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A highly poisonous Eurasian and African biennial herb, commonly known as hemlock, with fernlike leaves and clusters of small white flowers, often growing in damp areas.

Conium maculatum plant identification
Botanists warn that Conium maculatum can easily be mistaken for harmless wild parsley.
