neo-Kantianism

noun
late 19th-century philosophical movement based on Kant
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊ ˈkæn.ti.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ ˈkæn.ti.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A philosophical movement originating in the late 19th century that revived and developed the ideas of Immanuel Kant, especially in epistemology, ethics, and jurisprudence, often in opposition to materialism and idealism.

Examples

rise of neo-Kantianism
Her dissertation analyzed how neo-Kantianism contributed to modern discussions in ethics and law.
Many scholars in the early twentieth century were influenced by neo-Kantianism in their approach to philosophy.
The rise of neo-Kantianism marked a shift from materialist philosophies dominating Europe at the time.
