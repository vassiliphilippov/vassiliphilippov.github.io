vacuity

noun
emptiness of meaning or ideas
Pronunciation: / vəˈkjuːɪti / (AmE), / vəˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the state of being empty of meaning, ideas, or content; emptiness or lack of substance

Examples

vacuity of ideas
His eyes betrayed a certain vacuity as he listened without understanding.
Many critics have commented on the vacuity of the politician's speech.
She was frustrated by the vacuity of popular television programs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – depth: profoundness, richness in ideas or meaning (opposite)
  • synonym – emptiness: the state of containing nothing
