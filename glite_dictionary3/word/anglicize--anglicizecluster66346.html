to anglicize

verb
make or become English in form or character
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡləˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make something English in form, character, or appearance; especially to adapt (a word, name, or custom) to English usage.

Examples

to anglicize a foreign name
After moving to England, she decided to anglicize her French surname.
Many foreign place names are anglicized for use in English publications.
Some traditional foods are anglicized to suit the local palate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – foreignize: to make or keep something foreign in character
  • synonym – naturalize: to make something conform to the nature of a country or language
