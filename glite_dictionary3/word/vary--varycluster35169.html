to vary

verb
to change or differ based on variables
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈveəri / (BrE)
to be different, to differ, or to not conform depending on circumstances

Examples

vary in size
His interests vary depending on his mood.
Temperatures vary greatly between summer and winter in this region.
The amount of rainfall can vary from year to year.
His actions vary from what was expected by the team.
The latest figures vary with the official estimates provided earlier.
These results vary significantly from those in previous studies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – change: to become different; to undergo alteration
  • antonym – conform: to comply with rules, standards, or laws; to be in line with
  • synonym – differ: to be unlike or distinct in nature, form, or characteristics
  • antonym – remain: to stay the same, to not change
