to blindside

verb
attack or surprise from an unprotected side
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndˌsaɪd / (BrE)
to attack, hit, or surprise someone from the side where their view is obstructed or they are unprepared

Examples

to blindside a defender
During the match, one player tried to blindside his opponent, but the referee spotted the foul.
She was completely blindsided by the sudden announcement of layoffs.
The defender managed to blindside the quarterback, causing a fumble.
