Ea

proper noun
Babylonian god of wisdom and water
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈiː.ə / (BrE)
In Babylonian mythology, the god of wisdom, water, and creation; son of Apsu and father of Marduk, often identified with the Sumerian Enki.

Examples

Ea in mythology
In ancient texts, Ea is often depicted as a protector of humanity.
Priests in Babylon would pray to Ea for wisdom and guidance.
The myth describes how Ea defeated Apsu and established order among the gods.
