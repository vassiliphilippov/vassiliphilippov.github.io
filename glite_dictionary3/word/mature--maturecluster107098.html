mature

adjective
showing adult qualities or full development
Pronunciation: / məˈtʃʊr, məˈtʊr / (AmE), / məˈtjʊə, məˈtʃʊə / (BrE)
behaving in a sensible, adult way; having reached a stage of full development or showing the qualities expected in an adult

Examples

mature attitude
Although Emma is only twelve, she is very mature for her age.
He handled the criticism in a very mature and thoughtful way.
The company is looking for mature individuals who can work independently.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grown-up: like or characteristic of an adult
  • antonym – immature: not fully developed or lacking adult qualities
