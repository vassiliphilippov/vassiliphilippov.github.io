to localize

verb
restrict to a particular area
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to restrict or confine something to a particular area or spot

Examples

to localize a problem
Firefighters managed to localize the fire to a single building.
Strict measures were taken to localize the contamination in the laboratory.
The doctors were able to localize the infection and prevent it from spreading.
During the inspection, the engineers managed to localize the source of the leak.
The doctors were relieved when the tumor localized and did not spread further.
The pain has localized mainly in her lower back over the past week.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confine: to keep something within limits; restrict
  • antonym – disperse: to scatter or spread over a wide area
  • antonym – spread: to become distributed over a larger area or among a greater number of people or things
