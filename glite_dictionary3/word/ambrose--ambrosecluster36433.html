Ambrose

proper noun
bishop of Milan and Church Father, c. 340–397
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.broʊz / (AmE), / ˈæm.brəʊz / (BrE)
(c. 340–397) A Roman priest and bishop of Milan, recognized as a major Church Father, saint, and Doctor of the Church who made significant contributions to Christian doctrine and hymnody.

Examples

Ambrose of Milan
Ambrose is widely credited with shaping early Christian theology in the Western Roman Empire.
As bishop of Milan, Ambrose played a key role in establishing the Church's authority.
The hymns composed by Ambrose continue to be sung in churches today.
