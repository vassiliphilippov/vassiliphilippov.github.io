penetrability

noun
quality of being penetrable
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛn.ə.trəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.ɪ.trəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be penetrated, such as by people, light, or other substances.

Examples

high penetrability of glass
Because of its high penetrability, the armor was deemed unsuitable for modern combat vehicles.
Engineers study the penetrability of soil layers before designing building foundations.
The penetrability of the material was tested to determine if light could pass through it.

Related Terms

  • antonym – impermeability: the quality of not allowing substances to pass through
  • synonym – penetrable: able to be penetrated
  • synonym – permeability: the ability to be permeated (especially by liquids or gases)
