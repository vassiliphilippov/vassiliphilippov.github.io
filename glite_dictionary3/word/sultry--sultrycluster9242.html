sultry

adjective
oppressively hot and humid (weather)
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌltri / (AmE), / ˈsʌltri / (BrE)
(of weather or air) oppressively hot and humid; uncomfortably warm and sticky.

Examples

sultry weather
A sultry breeze wafted through the room, making everyone sweat in silence.
August afternoons in the city were unbearably sultry and made walking outside exhausting.
The sultry weather finally broke with an evening thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – muggy: (weather) warm and damp in an unpleasant way
  • synonym – stifling: very hot and uncomfortable, making it difficult to breathe
