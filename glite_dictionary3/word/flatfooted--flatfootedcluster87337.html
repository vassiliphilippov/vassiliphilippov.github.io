flatfooted

adjective
unprepared; unable to react quickly
Pronunciation: / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
surprised and unprepared, unable to react quickly or effectively

Examples

caught flatfooted
The politician was caught flatfooted by the reporter's unexpected question.
The sudden announcement left the management team flatfooted and searching for answers.
We can't afford to be flatfooted when competitors launch new features.
