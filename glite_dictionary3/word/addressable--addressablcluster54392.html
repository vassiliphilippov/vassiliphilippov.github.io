addressable

adjective
capable of being addressed
Pronunciation: / əˈdrɛsəbəl / (AmE), / əˈdrɛsəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being addressed, especially identified and reached by a specific address (such as in memory or computing systems)

Examples

addressable memory space
All sectors of the addressable memory can be accessed directly by the processor.
In modern network architectures, every node must be addressable by a unique identifier.
The digital system consists of many addressable devices.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unaddressable: not capable of being addressed
