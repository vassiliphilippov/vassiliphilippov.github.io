to pace

verb
move at a steady speed (animal, esp. horse)
Pronunciation: / peɪs / (AmE), / peɪs / (BrE)
(of a horse or other animal) to move at a steady or characteristic speed, often with a specific gait.

Examples

a horse paces
Every morning, he watched the deer pace silently along the tree line.
The mare paced gracefully around the ring, impressing the judges with her smooth gait.
The trainers taught the horse to pace instead of galloping during the parade.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – trot: (of a horse) to move at a pace faster than a walk but slower than a gallop
  • synonym – walk: to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
