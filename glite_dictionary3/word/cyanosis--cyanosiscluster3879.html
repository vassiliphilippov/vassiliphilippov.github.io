cyanosis

noun
bluish discoloration of skin from lack of oxygen
Pronunciation: / saɪəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / saɪəˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A bluish discoloration of the skin and mucous membranes caused by insufficient oxygen in the blood.

Examples

cyanosis of the lips
During the examination, the doctor noticed cyanosis on the patient's lips and fingertips.
Extreme cold can sometimes cause temporary cyanosis in the extremities.
If left untreated, severe heart conditions in infants may lead to visible cyanosis.
