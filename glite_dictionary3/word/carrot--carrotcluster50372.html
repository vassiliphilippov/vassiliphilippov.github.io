carrot

noun
incentive or reward offered to encourage certain behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈkærət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
an incentive or reward promised to encourage someone to act in a desired way, often contrasted with punishment ('stick')

Examples

the carrot of a reward
He dangled the carrot of a promotion to persuade her to take on extra work.
Sometimes a leader must use both a carrot and a stick to motivate their team.
The government offered the carrot of tax cuts to businesses to encourage investment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stick: punishment or threat used as a means of persuasion (as contrasted with 'carrot')
