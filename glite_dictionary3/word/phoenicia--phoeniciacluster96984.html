Phoenicia

proper noun
ancient maritime civilization on the eastern Mediterranean
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɪʃə / (AmE), / fiˈniːʃə / (BrE)
an ancient region and civilization comprising a group of city-states located along the eastern Mediterranean coast, notably in present-day Lebanon and parts of Syria and Israel, renowned for maritime trade and the development of the alphabet.

Examples

ancient Phoenicia civilization
Historians credit Phoenicia with significant contributions to the development of the alphabet.
Many ancient texts describe the seafaring prowess of Phoenicia.
The merchants of Phoenicia were famous for trading purple dye throughout the ancient world.
