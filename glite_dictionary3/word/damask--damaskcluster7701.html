damask

adjective
having a woven patterned fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈdæm.æsk / (AmE), / ˈdam.əsk / (BrE)
(of fabric) having a woven pattern, typically with a subtle sheen, often used for tablecloths or upholstery.

Examples

damask fabric
His jacket was lined with a luxurious, red damask fabric.
She admired the intricate damask curtains hanging in the old manor house.
The table was elegantly set with damask napkins and polished silverware.
