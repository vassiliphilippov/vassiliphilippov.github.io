to verbalize

verb
to be verbose or overly wordy
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to speak or write at length, often in a way that is unnecessarily wordy or verbose

Examples

to verbalize for hours
The professor tends to verbalize during lectures, making it difficult for students to stay focused.
Try not to verbalize your thoughts unnecessarily when writing essays.
When asked a simple question, she would often verbalize for several minutes before giving an answer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: using few words; not wordy
  • synonym – ramble: to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
