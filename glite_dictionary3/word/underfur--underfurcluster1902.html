underfur

noun
soft, insulating layer of animal fur
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌfɝː / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌfəː / (BrE)
The dense, soft layer of fur that lies beneath the longer, coarser guard hairs on certain animals, providing insulation.

Examples

thick underfur layer
Beavers have a thick layer of underfur that helps them stay dry in the water.
The rabbit's underfur keeps it warm during cold winter nights.
When grooming her cat, she noticed a lot of underfur coming off with the brush.

Related Terms

  • antonym – guard hair: the longer, coarser outer hairs of animal fur
