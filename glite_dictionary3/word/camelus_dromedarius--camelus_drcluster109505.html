Camelus dromedarius

noun
one-humped desert camel
Pronunciation: / kəˈmiːləs ˌdroʊməˈdɛəriəs / (AmE), / kəˈmiːləs ˌdrɒmɪˈdɛəriəs / (BrE)
A species of camel with a single hump, native to the deserts of northern Africa and southwestern Asia; commonly known as the dromedary.

Examples

Camelus dromedarius desert animal

Unlike the Bactrian camel, the Camelus dromedarius has only one hump on its back.
