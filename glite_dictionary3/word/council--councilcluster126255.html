council

noun
assembly of church leaders to decide doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
an assembly of theologians, bishops, and other representatives of various Christian churches or dioceses, convened to regulate matters of doctrine or discipline

Examples

council of bishops
Roman Catholic doctrine was clarified at the council of Trent.
The Nicene council was convened to address theological disputes in early Christianity.
The church historian wrote extensively about the outcomes of each major council.

Related Terms

  • synonym – synod: an assembly of church officials for discussion and decision-making
