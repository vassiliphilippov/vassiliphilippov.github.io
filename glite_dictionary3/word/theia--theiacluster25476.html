Theia

proper noun
hypothetical proto-planet that formed the Moon
Pronunciation: / ˈθiːə / (AmE), / ˈθiːə / (BrE)
A hypothetical ancient, Mars-sized planet in the early solar system that is believed to have collided with the early Earth and contributed material that formed the Moon.

Examples

Theia collided with Earth
Current models propose that Theia collided with early Earth, helping form the Moon about 4.5 billion years ago.
Scientists use simulations to study whether Theia's collision explains the Moon's composition and angular momentum.
Evidence for Theia comes from isotopic similarities between Earth and Moon rocks collected during missions.
