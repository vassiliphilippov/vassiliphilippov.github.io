polarisation

noun
restriction of wave vibrations to a direction
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or phenomenon in which the vibrations of light or other electromagnetic waves are restricted to a particular direction.

Examples

light polarisation filter
Certain sunglasses use polarisation to reduce glare from surfaces.
Scientists study the polarisation of radio waves to learn about space.
The polarisation of light can be observed using special filters.

Related Terms

  • synonym – polarization: the same phenomenon—restriction of wave vibrations, American spelling
