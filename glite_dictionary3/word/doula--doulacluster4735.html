doula

noun
end-of-life support person
Pronunciation: / ˈduːlə / (AmE), / ˈduːlə / (BrE)
(in end-of-life care) a non-medical professional who provides emotional, practical and advocacy support to a dying person and their family.

Examples

doula for dying person
Hospice programs sometimes hire a doula to support patients and their families during final days.
After her mother fell ill, she arranged for two experienced doulas to help with comfort and practical tasks.
The volunteer doula gently guided the family through conversations about wishes and funeral planning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – death doula: a person who provides emotional and practical support to a dying person and their family; synonymous with end-of-life doula
