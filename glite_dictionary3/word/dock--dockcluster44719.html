dock

noun
enclosure for the defendant in court
Pronunciation: / dɑk / (AmE), / dɒk / (BrE)
an enclosed area in a courtroom where the defendant sits during a trial

Examples

the dock in court
She watched her son nervously from the public gallery while he sat in the dock.
The accused entered the dock as the trial commenced.
The judge addressed the defendant directly from the bench to the dock.
