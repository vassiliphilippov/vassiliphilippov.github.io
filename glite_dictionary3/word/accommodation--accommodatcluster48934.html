accommodation

noun
adjustment of eye lens to focus
Pronunciation: / əˌkɑməˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
(physiology) The automatic adjustment of the focal length of the eye's natural lens to focus on objects at different distances.

Examples

eye accommodation focus
Age can affect the eye's ability to perform accommodation efficiently.
Problems with accommodation may cause difficulties in focusing on nearby objects.
The optometrist explained how accommodation allows the eye to shift focus from distant to near objects.
