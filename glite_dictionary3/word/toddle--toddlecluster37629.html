to toddle

verb
walk with short, unsteady steps
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒdəl / (BrE)
(of a young child) to walk with short, unsteady steps, typically in a manner characteristic of someone just learning to walk.

Examples

to toddle across the room
After taking her first steps, Emily managed to toddle over to her mother.
Some elderly people toddle slowly down the garden path for their morning walk.
The little boy loves to toddle around the living room, giggling as he goes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stagger: walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • antonym – stride: walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction
