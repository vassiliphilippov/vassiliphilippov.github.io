handoff

noun
transfer of football by hand to a teammate
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈhændɒf / (BrE)
(in American football) a play in which one player hands the ball to a teammate, typically to start a running play.

Examples

handoff to the running back
A fumble occurred after the handoff was mishandled by the fullback.
During the final play, a clever handoff caught the defense off guard.
The quarterback made a quick handoff to the running back for a short gain.

  • antonym – pass: a throw of the football to a teammate
