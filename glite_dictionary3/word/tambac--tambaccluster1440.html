tambac

noun
copper-zinc alloy for gold imitation
Pronunciation: / ˈtæmbæk / (AmE), / ˈtæmbæk / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and zinc (sometimes also arsenic) used to imitate gold in inexpensive jewelry and for gilding

Examples

tambac jewelry
She bought a set of tambac bangles from the street market.
Some antique coins are composed of tambac rather than precious metals.
The necklace is made of tambac to resemble real gold.
