to pass

verb
be allowed to go without comment or criticism
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
(of something spoken or done) be allowed to go unnoticed or unchallenged, especially without comment or criticism.

Examples

pass without comment
His sarcastic comment passed without anyone reacting to it.
The teacher decided to pass the obvious mistake in the assignment.
We shouldn't let his rude remarks pass without addressing them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym – overlook: to fail to notice or consider something
