two-step

adjective
requiring two authentication steps
Pronunciation: / c8tuccst5bp / (AmE), / c8tud0ccst5bp / (BrE)
requiring two separate actions or pieces of evidence to confirm a user's identity, especially for online accounts or transactions (often in the phrase 'two-step verification' or 'two-step authentication').

Examples

two-step verification process
The company enabled two-step verification to protect employee accounts from unauthorized access.
Before approving withdrawals the bank required two-step authentication using a password and a text code.
Many apps now offer two-step options, letting users add an extra security layer to their accounts.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – multi-factor authentication: a security approach that requires two or more separate forms of verification
  • synonym – two-factor authentication: a security method requiring two different forms of identification to access an account
