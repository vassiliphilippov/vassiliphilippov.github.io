sidetrack

noun
railroad track branch for storage or passing
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪˌtræk / (BrE)
a short stretch of railroad track connected to the main line and used for storing trains or allowing others to pass

Examples

train on a sidetrack
A train pulled into the sidetrack to allow an express service to pass.
Maintenance crews used the sidetrack to repair damaged railcars out of the way of traffic.
The freight cars were left on the sidetrack overnight until the main line was clear.

  • synonym – siding: a short railway track connected to the main track, used for storage or passing
