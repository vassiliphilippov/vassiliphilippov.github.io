vasodilator

noun
drug that widens blood vessels
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪsoʊˈdaɪleɪtər / (AmE), / ˌveɪsəʊˈdaɪleɪtə / (BrE)
a drug or agent that causes blood vessels to widen by relaxing their muscular walls, thereby increasing blood flow

Examples

vasodilator drug
Certain natural substances can also act as vasodilators in the human body.
Nitroglycerin acts as a vasodilator to relieve chest pain in some heart patients.
The doctor prescribed a vasodilator to help lower his blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – antihypertensive: a drug used to lower blood pressure
  • antonym – vasoconstrictor: a drug causing narrowing of blood vessels
