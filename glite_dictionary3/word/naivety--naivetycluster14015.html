naivety

noun
lack of experience or sophistication
Pronunciation: / naɪˈivəti / (AmE), / naɪˈiːvəti / (BrE)
lack of experience, wisdom, or sophistication; the state of being naive.

Examples

childish naivety
He paid a high price for his naivety in trusting strangers too easily.
Her naivety about business made her vulnerable to scams.
Many admired the child's naivety and genuine belief in the goodness of people.

Related Terms

  • synonym – innocence: lack of worldly experience or sophistication
  • antonym – sophistication: the quality of having experience, knowledge, and refinement
