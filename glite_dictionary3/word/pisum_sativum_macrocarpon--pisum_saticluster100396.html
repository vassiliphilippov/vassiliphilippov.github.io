Pisum sativum macrocarpon

noun
variety of pea plant with edible pods (snow pea)
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪzəm səˈtɪvəm ˌmækroʊˈkɑrpən / (AmE), / ˈpiːzəm səˈtɪvəm ˌmakrəʊˈkɑːpɒn / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of pea plant that produces soft, thick, edible pods without a fibrous inner lining, commonly known as the snow pea

Examples

Pisum sativum macrocarpon edible pods
Pisum sativum macrocarpon is widely cultivated in East Asia for its crisp, edible pods.
Chefs often use Pisum sativum macrocarpon in stir-fry dishes due to its tender texture.
The pods of Pisum sativum macrocarpon lack the tough inner lining found in common peas.
