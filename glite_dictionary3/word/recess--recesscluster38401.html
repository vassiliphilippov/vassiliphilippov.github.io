to recess

verb
create an indentation or hollow space
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
to make or fit something into a hollow or indented space so it is set back from the surface

Examples

to recess a shelf
After measuring carefully, they recessed the shelf into the wall.
The carpenter will recess the door hinges into the frame for a flush fit.
You need to recess the light fixture into the ceiling so it doesn't stick out.
Please recess the cabinet so it sits flush with the wall.
The electrician will recess the lights into the ceiling for a modern look.
They recessed the shelves into the alcove to save space.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embed: to fix an object firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • synonym – indent: to make a dent or notch in something
  • antonym – protrude: to stick out from a surface, the opposite of making an indentation
