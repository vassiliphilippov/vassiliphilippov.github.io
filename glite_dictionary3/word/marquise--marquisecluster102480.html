marquise

noun
permanent canopy over a building entrance
Pronunciation: / mɑrˈkiz / (AmE), / mɑːˈkiːz / (BrE)
a permanent canopy or structure projecting over the entrance of a building, especially a hotel or theater, providing shelter and often bearing signage

Examples

hotel marquise entrance
Guests gathered under the marquise to escape the sudden rain before entering the theater.
The hotel's grand entrance was sheltered by a glass marquise that sparkled at night.
The vintage cinema still has the original marquise displaying movie titles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – marquee: a large tent or a projecting sign over a theater entrance
