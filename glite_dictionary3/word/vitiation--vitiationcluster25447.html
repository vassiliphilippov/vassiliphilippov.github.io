vitiation

noun
nullification of legal force
Pronunciation: / ˌvɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪtɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of impairing or destroying the legal validity or effectiveness of something, especially a contract or agreement

Examples

vitiation of a contract
Any coercion during signing could cause the vitiation of the legal document.
Evidence of fraud resulted in the vitiation of the agreement.
The court ruled that the misrepresentation led to the vitiation of the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym – invalidation: the action of making something invalid or ineffective, especially legally
  • synonym – nullification: the act of making something legally null and void
  • antonym – validation: the act of making something legally valid or effective
