rector

noun
clergyman in charge of a parish or church
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktər / (AmE), / ˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a clergyman in certain Christian churches who is authorized to conduct religious worship and is often in charge of a parish or church

Examples

rector of a church
After the ceremony, the rector greeted everyone at the church entrance.
The rector delivered a powerful sermon on Sunday morning.
The parishioners held a celebration to honor their retiring rector.

Related Terms

  • synonym – minister: a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches
  • synonym – vicar: a representative or deputy of a bishop or a parish priest in the Church of England
