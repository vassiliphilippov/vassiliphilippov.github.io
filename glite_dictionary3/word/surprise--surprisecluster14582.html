to surprise

verb
attack suddenly and unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / sərˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to attack suddenly and unexpectedly, often catching the opponent off guard

Examples

to surprise an enemy
Pirates would often surprise merchant ships by approaching under a false flag.
The rebels hoped to surprise the outpost before the guards were fully awake.
The soldiers planned to surprise the enemy at dawn with a swift attack.
