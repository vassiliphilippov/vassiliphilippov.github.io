peripatetic

noun
a person who travels frequently
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place, especially for work or duty

Examples

a peripatetic worker
As a peripatetic, he rarely stayed in one town for more than a month.
Her career as a peripatetic took her to several countries every year.
The ancient philosophers were often peripatetics, sharing their ideas as they traveled.

Related Terms

  • synonym – itinerant: a person who travels from place to place, often for work
  • antonym – settler: a person who moves to a new area and stays there permanently
  • synonym – wanderer: a person who moves about without a fixed home or goal
