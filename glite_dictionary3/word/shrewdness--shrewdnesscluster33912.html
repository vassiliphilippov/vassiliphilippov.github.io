shrewdness

noun
keen practical judgment; astuteness
Pronunciation: / ˈʃrudnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃruːdnɪs / (BrE)
The ability to judge situations or people accurately and turn this to one's advantage, especially in business.

Examples

business shrewdness
Her shrewdness in negotiating contracts earned her respect in the industry.
The company’s success was largely due to the CEO’s financial shrewdness.
They admired his shrewdness when it came to investing money wisely.

Related Terms

  • synonym – astuteness: the ability to accurately assess situations or people
  • antonym – naivety: lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
