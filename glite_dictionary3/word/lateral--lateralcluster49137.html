lateral

noun
a sideways or backward pass (sports)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(in American football) a pass to a teammate that travels sideways or backward, rather than forward.

Examples

lateral pass in football
A perfectly timed lateral helped the player avoid being tackled.
The quarterback threw a risky lateral to his running back.
The team lost possession after the lateral was fumbled.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forward pass: an American football pass thrown forward toward the opponent's goal line
