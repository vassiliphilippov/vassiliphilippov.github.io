to drone

verb
make a dull monotonous sound
Pronunciation: / droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
to make a continuous, low, dull, monotonous sound (including speaking in a dull, unvaried way)

Examples

to drone in class
A distant airplane droned overhead as we sat in the garden.
His voice continued to drone on, putting half the audience to sleep.
The air conditioner began to drone softly in the background.
As the meeting dragged on, she found herself tuning out her boss, who continued to drone about company policy.
He droned through the entire speech, barely changing his tone.
The professor tended to drone on during his lectures, making it hard for students to stay awake.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buzz: make a continuous, vibrating sound
  • synonym – hum: make a low, continuous sound
  • synonym – intone: to say or recite with little rise and fall of the pitch of the voice
  • synonym – monotone: a continuing sound, especially of a person's voice, that is unchanging in pitch
  • degree higher – ring: produce a clear resonant or vibrating sound
