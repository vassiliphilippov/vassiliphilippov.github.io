to pitch

verb
move abruptly or lurch
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
to move or tilt suddenly, or to cause something to do so (such as a boat, vehicle, or person); to slope or incline at an angle

Examples

As the storm intensified, the boat began to pitch wildly in the heavy waves.
Suddenly, the truck pitched to one side when it hit the pothole.
The airplane would pitch up and down during turbulence, making some passengers feel uneasy.
The airplane pitched forward, sending the passengers into their seatbelts.
The boat began to pitch as the storm grew stronger.
Without warning, the floor seemed to pitch beneath their feet.
After the earthquake, the floor pitched noticeably to one side.
The road pitched downward as we drove into the valley.
The roof pitches steeply to improve water drainage.
