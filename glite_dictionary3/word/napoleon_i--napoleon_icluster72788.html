Napoleon I

proper noun
French emperor and military leader (1769–1821)
Pronunciation: / nəˈpoʊliən ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / nəˈpəʊliən ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1769–1821) French general and emperor who ruled France from 1804 to 1814 and again in 1815, noted for his military campaigns across Europe.

Examples

Napoleon I crowned emperor
Napoleon I crowned himself Emperor of the French in 1804.
After his defeat at Waterloo, Napoleon I was exiled to Saint Helena.
Many historians consider Napoleon I one of the greatest military leaders in history.
