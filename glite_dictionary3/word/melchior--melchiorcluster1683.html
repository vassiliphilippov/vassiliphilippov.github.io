Melchior

proper noun
one of the three wise men in Christian tradition
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛlkiˌɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɛlkɪˌɔː / (BrE)
In Christian tradition, one of the three wise men who visited the infant Jesus, often depicted as a king from Nubia.

Examples

Melchior the wise man
Every Christmas, the story of Melchior and the other wise men is retold in churches.
In the nativity play, Daniel played Melchior, bringing gold as a gift to Jesus.
Paintings often show Melchior wearing elaborate robes and a crown.
