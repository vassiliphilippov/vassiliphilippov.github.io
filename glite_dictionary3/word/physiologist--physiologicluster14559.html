physiologist

noun
scientist specializing in physiology
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪziˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌfɪziˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies or specializes in physiology, the branch of biology that deals with the normal functions of living organisms and their parts

Examples

physiologist researches health
As a sports physiologist, he researches the effects of exercise on muscle tissue.
Marie Curie’s daughter, Irène, became a renowned physiologist and studied cellular function.
The physiologist explained how nerves transmit signals throughout the human body.
