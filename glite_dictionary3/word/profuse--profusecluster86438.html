profuse

adjective
abundant; in great quantity
Pronunciation: / prəˈfjus / (AmE), / prəˈfjuːs / (BrE)
(especially of something offered or produced) existing or occurring in large amounts; abundant or excessive.

Examples

profuse apologies
He apologized with profuse gratitude after making the mistake.
The accident caused a profuse bleeding that needed immediate attention.
The garden was filled with profuse blooms in every color imaginable.
