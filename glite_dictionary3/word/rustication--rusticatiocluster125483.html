rustication

noun
rough-textured masonry finish
Pronunciation: / ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of decorating masonry or brickwork by giving the surface a rough, textured, or projecting appearance, often with deep grooves or recessed joints

Examples

rustication stone walls
Many Renaissance palaces feature rustication on their ground floors to suggest strength and solidity.
The rustication of the stone façade gave the building a rugged, classical appearance.
The architect chose rustication for the entrance to highlight contrast with the smoother upper levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ashlar: smooth-surfaced, finely dressed masonry
