forced

adjective
compelled; not voluntary
Pronunciation: / fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
done because of compulsion, necessity, or lack of willingness; not voluntary or natural

Examples

forced smile
She gave a forced smile, trying to hide her disappointment.
The company made a forced decision to lay off several workers due to financial trouble.
The prisoners were subjected to forced labor for many years.
Due to engine failure, the pilot executed a forced landing in a nearby field.
Her early retirement was a forced decision following the unexpected restructuring of the department.
The company was put up for a forced sale after declaring bankruptcy.
Despite her forced enthusiasm, everyone sensed her lack of true interest.
Her forced smile made it clear she was uncomfortable at the party.
His laughter sounded forced and did not convince anyone in the room.
