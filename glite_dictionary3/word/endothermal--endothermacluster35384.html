endothermal

adjective
chemical reaction absorbing heat
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈθɜrməl / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈθɜːməl / (BrE)
(of a chemical reaction or compound) occurring or formed with absorption of heat.

Examples

endothermal reaction
An endothermal compound absorbs heat from its surroundings during formation.
Photosynthesis is an endothermal process because it requires sunlight to provide energy.
The researchers studied several endothermal reactions in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exothermic: (of a chemical reaction) giving off heat
