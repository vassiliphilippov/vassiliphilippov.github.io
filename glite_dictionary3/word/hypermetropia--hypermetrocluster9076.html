hypermetropia

noun
farsightedness; better distance than near vision
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərməˈtroʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəməˈtrəʊpiə / (BrE)
A vision condition in which distant objects can be seen more clearly than near objects, due to the focusing of images behind the retina; farsightedness.

Examples

hypermetropia correction glasses
Prescription glasses can correct hypermetropia, making reading and close work easier.
Regular eye exams can help detect hypermetropia in children at an early stage.
The optometrist explained that my headaches were likely caused by hypermetropia.

Related Terms

  • synonym – farsightedness: The condition of seeing distant objects better than near ones
  • antonym – myopia: A vision condition where near objects are seen more clearly than distant ones (nearsightedness)
