classic

adjective
well-known and traditional in style
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪk / (BrE)
recognized as a model or standard of its kind; of the highest quality and lasting worth

Examples

a classic style
Her black dress was a classic choice for the elegant dinner.
That building is a classic example of Victorian architecture.
The film has become a classic of the horror genre.
Her little black dress is considered a classic in the world of fashion.
Many believe that Shakespeare's works are the classic examples of English literature.
The museum displayed a classic painting from the Renaissance period.

  • synonym – iconic: widely recognized and well-established; classic
  • antonym – modern: relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past; opposite of traditional
  • synonym – timeless: not affected by the passage of time or changes in fashion; classic
