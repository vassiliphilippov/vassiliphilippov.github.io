metastatic

adjective
relating to spread of disease, especially cancer
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈstætɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease, especially cancer) relating to or involving metastasis, the spread of disease from one part of the body to another.

Examples

metastatic cancer cells
Advanced-stage cancer is often characterized by metastatic disease.
The doctors discovered a metastatic tumor in her lungs.
The research focused on the mechanisms behind metastatic spread in melanoma patients.
