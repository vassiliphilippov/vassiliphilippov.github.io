slack

adjective
loose; not tight or taut
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
not tight, firm, or stretched; loose or not held tightly

Examples

slack shoelaces
After the wind died down, the sails became slack and hung loosely from the mast.
He noticed his grip was slack, so he held the handle more firmly.
The clothesline was slack and needed tightening before use.
