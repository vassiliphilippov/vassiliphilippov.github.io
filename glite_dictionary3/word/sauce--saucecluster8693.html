to sauce

verb
speak or behave impudently
Pronunciation: / sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
to speak or behave toward someone in a rude or cheeky way; to show impudence or disrespect.

Examples

to sauce your teacher
Don’t you sauce your elders, young man!
She was never one to sauce authority, always responding politely instead.
The student sauced the teacher and was promptly sent out of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cheek: impertinent talk or behavior
  • antonym – respect: showing polite regard or deference
