concentrated

adjective
having a high proportion of solute (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution) made stronger by reducing the amount of solvent; having a high proportion of solute compared to solvent.

Examples

concentrated solution
Please wear gloves when handling concentrated cleaning products, as they can be corrosive.
The chemist used a concentrated acid to speed up the reaction.
This orange juice is made from concentrated syrup and needs to be diluted with water before drinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dilute: (of a liquid) made weaker by adding more solvent; opposite of concentrated
