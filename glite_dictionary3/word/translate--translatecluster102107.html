to translate

verb
move an object in space without rotation
Pronunciation: / trænzˈleɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈleɪt /; / trænsˈleɪt / (BrE)
to move an object or body in space from one position to another without rotating it (technical/physics)

Examples

to translate an object
Computer graphics often translate images across the screen along a straight path.
If an object is translated five units to the right, every point moves the same distance.
In geometry, to translate a shape, you slide it to a new position without turning it.
In physics, a rigid body can translate without rotating along a straight path.
The shape will translate along the x-axis by five units in this transformation.
When you translate the object, every point moves the same distance and direction.

  • antonym – rotate: to turn around a central point or axis
