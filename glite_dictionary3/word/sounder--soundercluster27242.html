sounder

noun
device for measuring depth underwater
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndə / (BrE)
a device used to measure the depth of water or the presence of objects underwater by determining how long it takes for a sound to be reflected back.

Examples

sounder measures depth
Early navigators relied on a lead line or a sounder to avoid running aground.
The ship lowered a sounder to determine the depth of the channel before proceeding.
With its modern sounder, the vessel could easily map the seafloor contours.

Related Terms

  • synonym – echo sounder: a device that uses sonar to measure water depth
