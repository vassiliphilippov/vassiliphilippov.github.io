amphidiploidy

noun
condition of having two sets of chromosomes from different species
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.fɪˈdɪp.lɔɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈdɪp.lɔɪ.di / (BrE)
the condition in which an organism has two complete sets of chromosomes from each of two different species, forming an amphidiploid.

Examples

amphidiploidy in hybrid plants
Amphidiploidy often results from hybridization between two distinct species, followed by chromosome doubling.
Scientists created an artificial amphidiploidy to increase the plant's genetic diversity.
The study focused on the effects of amphidiploidy in wheat breeding programs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allopolyploidy: having two or more sets of chromosomes derived from different species
  • degree lower – diploidy: condition of having two complete sets of chromosomes
  • degree higher – polyploidy: condition of having more than two complete sets of chromosomes
