Secession

noun
Austrian art and architecture movement (1890s)
Pronunciation: / sɪˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / sɪˈsɛs.ən / (BrE)
An Austrian movement in art and architecture, active in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, aligned with the aesthetics of Art Nouveau.

Examples

the Secession movement
She studied the influence of the Secession movement on early 20th-century design.
The Secession artists rejected traditional styles in favor of innovative forms and decorative motifs.
The building’s ornate façade is a hallmark of Vienna Secession architecture.
