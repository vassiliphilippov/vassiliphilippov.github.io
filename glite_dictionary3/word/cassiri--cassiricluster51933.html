cassiri

noun
fermented cassava-based drink
Pronunciation: / kəˈsɪri / (AmE), / kəˈsɪri / (BrE)
A drink resembling beer, made from fermented cassava juice, traditionally prepared and consumed in parts of South America and the Caribbean.

Examples

drink cassiri at a festival
Anthropologists studied the process of making cassiri in the remote village.
In the Amazon, locals often brew cassiri for festivals and special gatherings.
