transmutation

noun
change of one element into another (physics)
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænsˌmjuːˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtranz.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
in physics, the process by which one chemical element is changed into another, typically by nuclear reactions such as radioactive decay or bombardment

Examples

nuclear transmutation of elements
Modern physics has made transmutation a routine process in particle accelerators.
Nuclear reactors can achieve the transmutation of uranium into plutonium.
The ancient dream of transmutation drove many early chemists to search for a way to create gold.
