cheesing

noun
using easy, low-skill tactics in games
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of playing a game or sport in a way that is effective but requires little skill, often by exploiting easy or unbalanced tactics.

Examples

cheesing a video game
He won by cheesing the boss fight, not by using real strategy.
Some players were accused of cheesing by using the same simple move repeatedly.
The gaming community often debates whether cheesing ruins the competitive spirit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exploiting: Making use of a situation or resource in an unfair or selfish way, especially in games
