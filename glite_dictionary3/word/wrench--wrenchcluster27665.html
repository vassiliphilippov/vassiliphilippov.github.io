to wrench

verb
twist or contort in pain
Pronunciation: / rɛntʃ / (AmE), / rɛntʃ / (BrE)
to twist or contort suddenly and forcibly, especially as a result of pain or anguish

Examples

to wrench hands in pain
He wrenched his hands in agony as he received the terrible news.
Her face wrenched with pain when she heard the diagnosis.
The child wrenched his fingers together anxiously while waiting for the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym – twist: to turn something, especially repeatedly, or to bend out of shape
  • synonym – wring: to twist and squeeze something
