Brahmanism

noun
modern caste-based ideology favoring Brahmin dominance
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑːmənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A modern socio-political ideology or attitude that promotes or preserves Brahmin (upper-caste) dominance in cultural, political, or institutional life; used critically to describe caste-based privilege and exclusion.

Examples

Brahmanism in politics
Many activists described the minister's decision as evidence of Brahmanism influencing policy.
Scholars debated whether contemporary Brahmanism reflects ancient religious ideas or modern political power.
Opposition leaders argued that the reforms reinforced Brahmanism, privileging upper castes over others.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Brahminical: relating to or supporting Brahmin dominance or authority
  • synonym – casteism: social or political discrimination and hierarchy based on caste
