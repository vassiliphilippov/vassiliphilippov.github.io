to follow

verb
perform accompaniment in music
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ / (BrE)
(of a musician or group) to perform in accompaniment to another performer, matching their timing or changes.

Examples

to follow a soloist
During the concert, the pianist skillfully followed the soloist's improvisation.
It's challenging for an orchestra to perfectly follow a singer with unpredictable timing.
The band struggled to follow the conductor's shifting tempo.
