divergence

noun
the act of moving apart from a common point
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɝːdʒəns / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the act or process of moving or spreading apart from a common point or standard

Examples

divergence of a river
A river's divergence can create smaller branches that flow away from the main channel.
Over time, there was increasing divergence between the two scientific theories.
The divergence of the two roads led travelers in opposite directions.
