sentimental

adjective
displaying or affected by tender emotion
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.t̬əl /; / ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmɛn.təl /; / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmɛn.tl̩ / (BrE)
showing or feeling tender emotions, such as love or sadness, in an exaggerated or overly emotional way

Examples

sentimental value
He became sentimental when he spoke about his childhood memories.
She kept every letter as a sentimental reminder of their friendship.
The movie was too sentimental for my taste, full of tearful reunions.
His sentimental speech at the award show felt insincere and overly dramatic to many in the audience.
Many critics dismissed the novel as overly sentimental and emotionally manipulative.
The movie was too sentimental for my taste, with exaggerated scenes of crying and reconciliation.

  • synonym – maudlin: overly sentimental, especially in a tearful or weakly emotional way
  • antonym – realistic: showing things as they are, not sentimental
  • synonym – schmaltzy: excessively sentimental, especially in art or music
  • antonym – unsentimental: not influenced by emotions or sentimentality
