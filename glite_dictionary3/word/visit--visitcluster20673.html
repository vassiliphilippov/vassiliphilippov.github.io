to visit

verb
stay as a guest
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪzɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪt / (BrE)
to go to see someone or a place, either socially, as a guest, for sightseeing, or as part of one's duties

Examples

to visit a museum
He visited his cousin in New York and stayed for the entire summer.
We visited my grandparents for two weeks during the holidays.
Whenever she travels abroad, she always visits friends who live there.
Many tourists visit the Grand Canyon every year to admire its beauty.
Next summer, I want to visit all the famous landmarks in Rome.
We decided to visit the national museum during our city trip.
The health inspector will visit the restaurant tomorrow to ensure standards are met.
The principal often visits classrooms to observe teachers and speak with students.
The senator visited the community center as part of her official duties.
