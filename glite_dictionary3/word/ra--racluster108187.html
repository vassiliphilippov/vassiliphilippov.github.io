RA

noun
astronomy: right ascension (celestial coordinate)
Pronunciation: / ɑr eɪ / (AmE), / ɑː ɒɹ eɪ / (BrE)
(astronomy) The abbreviation for right ascension: the angular distance of a celestial object eastward along the celestial equator from the vernal equinox, usually measured in hours, minutes, and seconds.

Examples

RA and declination
Astronomers use RA and declination to identify precise locations in the night sky.
If the RA of a galaxy is 5 hours, it lies 75 degrees east of the vernal equinox.
The star's RA is listed as 3 hours, 15 minutes, and 42 seconds.
