promise

noun
a commitment to do (or not do) something
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪs / (BrE)
a statement or assurance by someone that they will definitely do or not do something in the future

Examples

keep a promise
A promise is only as strong as the person who makes it.
He broke his promise and arrived late to the meeting.
She made a solemn promise to never reveal their secret.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oath: a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
  • synonym – pledge: a serious or formal promise or undertaking
  • antonym – threat: a statement of intention to cause harm or punishment if a condition is not met
