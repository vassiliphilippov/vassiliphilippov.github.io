one-dimensional

adjective
having only one dimension
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌn dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌwʌn dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
having or involving a single dimension or relating to something that extends in only one direction, such as a line.

Examples

one-dimensional line
In physics, movement along a rail is often modeled as one-dimensional motion.
The mathematician spoke about a one-dimensional line extending infinitely in both directions.
Their analysis was too one-dimensional and did not consider the problem's complexity.

  • degree higher – two-dimensional: having or relating to two dimensions, such as width and height
