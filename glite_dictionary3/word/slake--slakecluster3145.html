to slake

verb
satisfy or quench, especially thirst
Pronunciation: / sleɪk / (AmE), / sleɪk / (BrE)
to satisfy or quench thirst, desire, or a strong feeling

Examples

to slake thirst
After hours in the sun, nothing could slake her craving for water.
He drank deeply, hoping to slake his parched throat.
The hikers stopped by a stream to slake their thirst.
After running the marathon, he needed water to slake his thirst.
Her curiosity was slaked by the detailed explanation provided by the guide.
The rain helped to slake the heat of the summer afternoon.
