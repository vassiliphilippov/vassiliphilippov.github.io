to release

verb
to set free from confinement or restraint
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlis / (AmE), / rɪˈliːs / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to go free, especially after being kept somewhere; to set free from confinement or restraint

Examples

to release a prisoner
She was released from jail after evidence proved her innocence.
The authorities decided to release the prisoners after the peace agreement was signed.
The wildlife center will release the rehabilitated owl back into the wild next week.

Related Terms

  • antonym – detain: to keep someone in official custody, typically for questioning or as a punishment
  • synonym – free: to set someone or something free
