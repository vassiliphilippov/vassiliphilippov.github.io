filament

noun
thin wire in a light bulb
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪləmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪləmənt / (BrE)
a thin wire, often made of tungsten, that is heated to produce light in electric bulbs and similar devices

Examples

light bulb filament
Early light bulbs used a carbon filament instead of tungsten.
If the filament breaks, the light bulb will no longer work.
The tungsten filament inside the bulb glowed brightly when the lamp was switched on.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bulb: a glass enclosure containing a filament emitting light
  • synonym – wire: a thin, flexible strand of metal
