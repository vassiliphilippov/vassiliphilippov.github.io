minicomputer

noun
medium-sized digital computer
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪni kəmˌpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈmɪni kəmˌpjuːtə / (BrE)
A medium-sized digital computer, larger than a microcomputer but smaller and less powerful than a mainframe, typically used in business or scientific applications.

Examples

minicomputer in business
During the 1970s, many businesses relied on minicomputers for data processing tasks.
He wrote his first software program on a minicomputer in the university computing center.
The laboratory upgraded its old minicomputer to a faster, more modern system.
