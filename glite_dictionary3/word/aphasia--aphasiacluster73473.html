aphasia

noun
loss of language ability due to brain injury
Pronunciation: / əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person is unable to use or understand spoken or written language because of brain damage.

Examples

speech aphasia
After the accident, he developed aphasia and could no longer express his thoughts verbally.
The stroke left her with severe aphasia, making communication challenging.
Therapists worked with the patient to improve his aphasia through language exercises.
