to raven

verb
to seize or plunder violently (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪvən / (AmE), / ˈreɪvən / (BrE)
(archaic) to plunder, seize, or search for prey or loot

Examples

to raven a village
Armies used to raven villages, taking whatever they could by force.
The marauders would raven the countryside, leaving destruction in their wake.
The pirates ravened the coastal towns, looting and burning as they went.
In winter, desperate animals raven for whatever food they can find in the bleak landscape.
Pirates ravened along the coast, searching for merchant ships to plunder.
Wolves once ravened across these forests, preying relentlessly on deer and elk.

  • synonym – plunder: to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
