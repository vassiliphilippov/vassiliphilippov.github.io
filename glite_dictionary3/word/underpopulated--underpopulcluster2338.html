underpopulated

adjective
having too few inhabitants
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpɑːpjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpɒpjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having fewer inhabitants than is typical, normal, or desirable for a particular area.

Examples

underpopulated rural area
Many rural regions remain underpopulated despite government efforts to attract new residents.
Some countries encourage immigration to boost their underpopulated areas and stimulate economic growth.
The school closed because the village became increasingly underpopulated over the decades.
