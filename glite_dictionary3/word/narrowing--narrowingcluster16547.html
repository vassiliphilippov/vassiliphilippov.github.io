narrowing

adjective
restricting or limiting freedom
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of circumstances or conditions) restricting freedom or becoming more limited; making things less open or flexible.

Examples

narrowing options
Many workers fear narrowing choices as companies reduce benefits and flexibility.
The government's narrowing policies have reduced opportunities for free expression.
With each new law, citizens noticed a narrowing of their personal freedoms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – liberating: making someone feel free and able to behave as they like
  • synonym – limiting: reducing or controlling the possible range or extent
  • synonym – restrictive: serving to limit or control something
