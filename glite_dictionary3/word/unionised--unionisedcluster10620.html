unionised

adjective
not converted into ions
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not converted into ions; remaining in a neutral or non-ionic form.

Examples

unionised form of a drug
At lower pH levels, the drug remains largely unionised and can diffuse across cell membranes.
Researchers measured the unionised fraction of the compound in the bloodstream.
The solubility of unionised ammonia is higher in water than its ionised form.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ionised: converted into ions; has undergone ionization
