to compound

verb
to calculate interest on principal and accrued interest
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
(of interest or a sum of money) to calculate on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods.

Examples

to compound interest
Banks often compound interest quarterly to help savings grow faster.
If your account compounds annually, interest is added once per year.
She chose a loan that compounds daily for maximum return.

Related Terms

  • antonym – simple interest: interest calculated only on the principal
