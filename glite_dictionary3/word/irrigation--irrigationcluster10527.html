irrigation

noun
medical flushing or washing out of a body part
Pronunciation: / ˌɪr.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of cleaning a wound or body organ by flushing it with water or a medicated solution

Examples

irrigation of wounds
Proper irrigation of the eye was necessary after exposure to the chemical.
Saline irrigation is often used to prevent infection after dental surgery.
The surgeon performed irrigation of the wound to remove any debris before closing it.
