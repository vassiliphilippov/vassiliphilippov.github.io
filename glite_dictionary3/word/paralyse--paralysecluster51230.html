to paralyse

verb
make unable to move or function
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something unable to move or function, either physically or by stopping activity

Examples

to paralyse a city
A massive power outage can paralyse a city for hours.
She was so shocked by the news that it seemed to paralyse her.
The venom of the snake can paralyse its prey within seconds.
Heavy snow can paralyse an entire city, bringing transport to a standstill.
News of the sudden strike seemed to paralyse everyone in the office momentarily.
The new regulations could paralyse small businesses if not carefully implemented.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cripple: to cause serious impairment or disable completely
  • antonym – enable: to make able to do or be something
  • synonym – immobilize: to prevent from moving
  • synonym – paralyze: to make unable to move or act (American spelling)
