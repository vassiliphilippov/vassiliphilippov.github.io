consecrate

adjective
made or declared sacred
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnsɪˌkreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪˌkreɪtɪd / (BrE)
officially declared or made sacred, especially by a formal ceremony or by being set apart for a holy or important purpose

Examples

consecrated ground
She devoted her consecrated life to serving the greater good.
The consecrated grounds are only used for sacred rituals.
The ancient altar was consecrated during a special religious ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dedicated: devoted or set aside for a particular purpose
  • antonym – profane: not sacred or religious; secular
