to concur

verb
to be in agreement
Pronunciation: / kənˈkɝː / (AmE), / kənˈkɜː / (BrE)
to agree with someone or have the same opinion or decision as someone else

Examples

concur with a decision
All the members of the committee concurred with the recommendation.
Historians generally concur that the discovery was groundbreaking.
I do not concur with your interpretation of the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym – agree: to have the same opinion as someone else
  • antonym – disagree: to have a different opinion; not to agree
