hardened

adjective
made tough by experience or exposure
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdnd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdnd / (BrE)
made tough, resistant, or emotionally unfeeling, especially as a result of difficult experience

Examples

hardened criminal mind
His face had become hardened after years of working at sea.
Many hardened soldiers find it difficult to express their emotions.
She was a hardened skeptic, unwilling to believe anything without proof.
After years in law enforcement, she became a hardened officer who rarely showed her feelings.
He was a hardened skeptic, unwilling to believe anyone's promises.
The hardened criminal showed no remorse for his actions in court.
