skiffle

noun
1950s music genre using folk and improvised instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈskɪf.əl / (BrE)
a style of popular music, especially in Britain in the 1950s, based on American folk, blues, and jazz, typically played using guitars and improvised percussion instruments

Examples

skiffle band performance
He learned to play guitar while performing in a local skiffle group as a teenager.
Many British bands from the 1950s started out playing skiffle before exploring other genres.
The lively rhythms and homemade instruments make skiffle a unique style of music.
