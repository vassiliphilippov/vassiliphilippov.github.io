electrodynamometer

noun
instrument for measuring electric power, current, or voltage
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˌdaɪnəˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌdaɪnəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A device for measuring electric current, voltage, or power by using the interaction of magnetic fields generated by two coils.

Examples

precision electrodynamometer
An electrodynamometer is preferred for measuring alternating currents in certain tests because of its accuracy.
Modern physics students learn about the electrodynamometer when studying how to measure electrical power.
The laboratory technician used an electrodynamometer to check the current flowing through the circuit.
