to infuse

verb
instill by repeated teaching
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to fill or instill someone or something with a particular quality, idea, or feeling

Examples

to infuse energy
She worked hard to infuse confidence in her team through constant encouragement and positive feedback.
Teachers try to infuse a sense of responsibility in their students through daily reminders and guidance.
The school aims to infuse respect and empathy by modeling these values every day.
A spirit of generosity infused every aspect of the celebration.
Her words infuse the team with a sense of hope and determination.
The light infused the room with warmth and comfort.

Related Terms

  • antonym – drain: to cause a quality or feeling to leave or diminish
  • synonym – fill: to make full or occupy completely
  • synonym – imbue: to inspire or permeate with a feeling or quality
  • synonym – inculcate: to instill by persistent instruction
  • synonym – indoctrinate: to teach someone to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
