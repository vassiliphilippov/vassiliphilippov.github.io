subshrub

noun
low-growing woody perennial plant
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌʃrʌb / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.ʃrʌb / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant with a woody base, often shorter and less woody than typical shrubs.

Examples

woody subshrub plant
Many Mediterranean gardens feature subshrubs such as lavender and rosemary.
Sage is often classified as a subshrub due to its partially woody stems.
The low, woody subshrub spread quickly across the rocky hillside.
