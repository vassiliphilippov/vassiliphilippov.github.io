salientian

noun
tailless leaping amphibian (frog or toad)
Pronunciation: / seɪˈliːn.ti.ən / (AmE), / seɪˈliːn.ti.ən / (BrE)
Any member of a group of tailless, stout-bodied amphibians with long hind limbs for leaping, including frogs and toads.

Examples

salientian with long legs
Biologists studied the evolution of salientians to understand how frogs and toads diversified.
Many salientian species have adapted to both aquatic and terrestrial habitats.
The museum's collection includes several rare salientian specimens from South America.

Related Terms

  • synonym – toad: a tailless amphibian with dry, warty skin
