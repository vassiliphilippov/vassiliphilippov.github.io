grebe

noun
aquatic diving bird with lobed feet
Pronunciation: / ɡrib / (AmE), / ɡriːb / (BrE)
a small, aquatic diving bird with a compact body and lobed toes, often building floating nests; similar to but smaller than loons

Examples

grebe swimming underwater
A flock of grebes could be seen diving for fish near the shore.
During migration season, several species of grebes stop by the local lake.
The grebe builds a floating nest anchored among reeds.
