collectible

adjective
subject to payment
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktəbəl / (BrE)
(of money, a bill, etc.) subject to or requiring payment, especially as specified in a contract or agreement.

Examples

collectible debt
Only legally collectible accounts should be transferred to the agency.
The company wrote off its non-collectible receivables at the end of the year.
The invoice is a collectible debt that must be settled within thirty days.
