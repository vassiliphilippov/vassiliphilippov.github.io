to house

verb
provide accommodation or shelter
Pronunciation: / haʊs / (AmE), / haʊs / (BrE)
to provide someone with accommodation or a place to live

Examples

to house refugees
After the flood, families were temporarily housed in local schools.
The charity houses hundreds of people who would otherwise be homeless.
The dormitory can house up to fifty students at a time.
