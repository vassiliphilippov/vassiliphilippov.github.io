pyrostat

noun
high-temperature thermostat
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪroʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪərəʊstat / (BrE)
A thermostat designed to operate at very high temperatures, used especially in industrial or laboratory settings.

Examples

pyrostat in a furnace
Engineers installed a pyrostat to automatically shut off the reactor at critical temperatures.
In high-temperature kilns, a pyrostat ensures the environment does not exceed specified heat limits.
The furnace is equipped with a pyrostat to maintain safe operating temperatures.

  • synonym – thermostat: a device that regulates temperature automatically
