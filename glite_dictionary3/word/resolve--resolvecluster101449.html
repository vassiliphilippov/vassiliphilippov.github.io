to resolve

verb
make an image or detail clearly visible
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
(of an optical instrument or process) to make the separate parts of something visible or distinguishable, especially in images; to show clearly.

Examples

to resolve an image
On the computer, I adjusted the settings to resolve the blurry image.
The telescope was powerful enough to resolve the two stars as separate points of light.
With this microscope, individual cells can now be resolved in fine detail.

Related Terms

  • antonym – blur: to make or become unclear or less distinct
