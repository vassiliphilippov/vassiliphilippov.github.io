palpitation

noun
rapid or irregular heartbeat
Pronunciation: / ˌpælpəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpalpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a noticeably rapid, strong, or irregular heartbeat, often felt by a person

Examples

heart palpitation
After drinking too much coffee, he noticed a strange palpitation when he tried to relax.
Anxiety attacks can sometimes cause a sensation of palpitation in the chest.
She went to the doctor because she was experiencing frequent palpitations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arrhythmia: an irregular heart rhythm
  • degree higher – tachycardia: abnormally rapid heart rate
