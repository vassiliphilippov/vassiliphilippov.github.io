to disorganize

verb
cause to lose order or structure
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to lose its order, structure, or system; to make disordered or chaotic

Examples

to disorganize a schedule
A sudden change in routine can disorganize even the most efficient office.
He tends to disorganize his desk with piles of unread documents.
Heavy rain and flooding disorganized the delivery schedule for days.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disorder: to make untidy or disarranged
  • antonym – organize: to arrange or order systematically
