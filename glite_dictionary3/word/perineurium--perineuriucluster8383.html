perineurium

noun
connective tissue sheath of nerve fascicle
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈnʊriəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈnjʊəriəm / (BrE)
the sheath of connective tissue that surrounds and protects a bundle (fascicle) of nerve fibers within a nerve

Examples

perineurium of a nerve bundle
Damage to the perineurium can lead to impaired nerve function and increased susceptibility to infection.
During surgery, care must be taken not to disrupt the perineurium to prevent nerve damage.
The perineurium provides both protection and support to the nerve fascicles within a peripheral nerve.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – endoneurium: connective tissue surrounding individual nerve fibers
  • degree higher – epineurium: outermost connective tissue sheath of a nerve
