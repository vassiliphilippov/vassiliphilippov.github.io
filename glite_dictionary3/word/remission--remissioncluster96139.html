remission

noun
temporary reduction or disappearance of symptoms
Pronunciation: / rəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a period during which the symptoms of a disease decrease or disappear; a reduction in intensity or severity

Examples

cancer remission period
After six months of treatment, the patient's leukemia was in remission.
Periods of remission can give patients hope for a better quality of life.
She hopes her migraines will go into remission after the new medication.

Related Terms

  • antonym – relapse: a return of symptoms after a period of improvement
