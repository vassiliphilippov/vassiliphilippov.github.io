tupelo

noun
pale soft wood of a tupelo tree
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː.pəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.pɪˌləʊ / (BrE)
A pale, soft wood from a tupelo tree, especially the water gum.

Examples

tupelo wood
Because tupelo is pale and easy to work with, it's favored by woodcarvers.
Swamps in the southeastern United States are rich with tupelo wood.
The craftsman used tupelo to carve delicate fishing lures because of its softness.
