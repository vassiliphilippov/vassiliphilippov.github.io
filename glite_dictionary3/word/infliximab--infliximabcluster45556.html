infliximab

noun
monoclonal antibody medication for autoimmune diseases
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈflɪksɪmæb / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪksɪmæb / (BrE)
a monoclonal antibody drug used to treat autoimmune diseases (such as Crohn's disease and rheumatoid arthritis) by inhibiting tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNFα)
