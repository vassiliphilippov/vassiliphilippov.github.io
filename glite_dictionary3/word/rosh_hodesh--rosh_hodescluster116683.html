Rosh Hodesh

noun
start of month in Jewish calendar
Pronunciation: / roʊʃ hoʊˈdeɪʃ / (AmE), / rəʊʃ həʊˈdeɪʃ / (BrE)
(in Judaism) the beginning of each month in the Jewish calendar, observed with a special liturgy and some religious customs.

Examples

Rosh Hodesh prayers in synagogue
Rosh Hodesh is celebrated with special prayers and readings in synagogues.
Many families gather for a festive meal on Rosh Hodesh.
