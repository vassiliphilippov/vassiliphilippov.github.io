ratch

noun
mechanical device with a toothed wheel and pawl
Pronunciation: / rætʃ / (AmE), / rætʃ / (BrE)
a mechanical device consisting of a toothed wheel or rack and a pawl, allowing movement in only one direction

Examples

ratch mechanism
A ratch allows the wheel to turn in one direction while preventing backward motion.
In old clocks, a ratch helped control the movement of the gears.
The engineer adjusted the ratch mechanism to ensure the gate locked properly.
