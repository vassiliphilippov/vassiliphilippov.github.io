self-mortification

noun
voluntary self-punishment, often for religious reasons
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ˌmɔrtəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌmɔːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the practice of deliberately inflicting pain or discomfort on oneself, often as an act of self-discipline or to atone for wrongdoing, especially for religious reasons

Examples

self-mortification for forgiveness
His act of self-mortification was seen as a sign of sincere repentance by the community.
Some monks practice self-mortification as a way to deepen their spiritual discipline.
The discussion turned to the role of self-mortification in various religious traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – asceticism: severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence, often for religious reasons
  • synonym – penance: a voluntary act of self-punishment as an outward expression of repentance
  • antonym – self-indulgence: excessive gratification of one's own desires
