Gymnocladus dioica

noun
species of tree (Kentucky coffee tree)
Pronunciation: / dʒɪm.nəˌklæd.əs daɪˈoʊ.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˌklæd.əs dɪˈɒɪ.kə / (BrE)
A species of tree native to central and eastern North America, known for its large bipinnate leaves, greenish-white flowers, and brown woody pods whose seeds have been used as a coffee substitute; commonly called the Kentucky coffee tree.
