neutral

adjective
not taking sides in a conflict
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəl /; / ˈnutrəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəl / (BrE)
not supporting or favoring either side in a conflict or situation; impartial

Examples

stay neutral in arguments
During the heated debate, she remained strictly neutral and did not express her personal views.
It's difficult to stay neutral when both sides have strong arguments.
Switzerland is known for being neutral in international conflicts.
As a judge, she must remain neutral throughout the proceedings.
The news reporter tried to provide a neutral account of the events.
We need a neutral party to mediate the dispute.

Related Terms

  • antonym – biased: unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • synonym – impartial: treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just
