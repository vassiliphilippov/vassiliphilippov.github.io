scampo

noun
slender European lobster (culinary term)
Pronunciation: / ˈskɑːm.poʊ / (AmE), / ˈskam.pəʊ / (BrE)
A slender lobster native to European waters, closely related to the Norway lobster and commonly used in Mediterranean cooking.

Examples

scampo pasta dish
Many Italian seafood dishes feature scampo as the main ingredient.
The chef prepared grilled scampo served with lemon and herbs.
Unlike the larger American lobster, the scampo has a more delicate flavor.
