contrite

adjective
feeling remorse or guilt
Pronunciation: / kənˈtraɪt / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪt / (BrE)
feeling or expressing sorrow and remorse for having done something wrong; deeply repentant.

Examples

contrite apology
After realizing the harm he had caused, he wrote a contrite letter seeking forgiveness.
She sounded genuinely contrite when she apologized for her mistake.
The contrite student promised never to cheat on a test again.

Related Terms

  • synonym – remorseful: feeling deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • antonym – unapologetic: not acknowledging or expressing regret for a wrong or offense
