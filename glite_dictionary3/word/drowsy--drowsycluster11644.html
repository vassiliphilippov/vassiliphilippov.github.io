drowsy

adjective
feeling sleepy or half awake
Pronunciation: / ˈdraʊzi / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzi / (BrE)
feeling or appearing sleepy, tired, or only half awake; likely to fall asleep soon or not fully alert

Examples

feel drowsy after lunch
Driving late at night can make even the most alert driver dangerously drowsy.
He grew drowsy while reading and nearly fell asleep with the book in his hands.
The warm afternoon sun made me feel drowsy during the meeting.
After lunch, I often feel drowsy and find it hard to focus on work.
She became drowsy after listening to the professor's monotone voice.
The room felt warm and everyone looked drowsy during the lecture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – alert: fully awake and attentive
  • synonym – sleepy: ready or needing to sleep; feeling inclined to sleep
