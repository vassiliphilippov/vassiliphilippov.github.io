grubstake

noun
funds supplied to a prospector or entrepreneur for a share of profits
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌbˌsteɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbsteɪk / (BrE)
an amount of money or supplies given to a prospector or an entrepreneur in exchange for a share of the profits

Examples

grubstake for mining
He promised her half the profits as a grubstake for her investment in his startup.
The miner received a grubstake from a local businessman before heading into the mountains.
Without a grubstake, many prospectors would never have been able to begin their journeys.
