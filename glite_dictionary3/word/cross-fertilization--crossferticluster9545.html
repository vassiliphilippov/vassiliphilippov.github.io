cross-fertilization

noun
productive exchange between different areas or cultures
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɔsˌfɜrt̬əlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɒsˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the exchange of ideas, influences, or methods between different subjects, groups, or cultures that results in productive and beneficial outcomes

Examples

cross-fertilization of ideas
Many of the company's breakthroughs resulted from cross-fertilization between different departments and teams.
The conference encouraged cross-fertilization between scientists and artists from around the world.
The university values cross-fertilization of ideas among students from diverse backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collaboration: the action of working with someone to produce or create something
  • antonym – isolation: the state of being separate from others, with little influence or interaction
