to capsulate

verb
enclose in a capsule
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpsjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæpsjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to enclose or surround something in a capsule or similar protective covering, especially in scientific or medical contexts

Examples

to capsulate a drug
Scientists capsulate the medication to ensure it dissolves only in the intestine.
Technicians will capsulate the sample before sending it to the laboratory.
The seeds are capsulated to protect them from harsh environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encapsulate: to enclose in or as if in a capsule
