to elute

verb
remove by washing with solvent (chromatography)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːt / (AmE), / ɪˈluːt / (BrE)
to remove or extract (one substance from another) by washing with a solvent, especially in chromatography

Examples

to elute a sample
After binding to the resin, the antibody was eluted and collected for further analysis.
The technician used a buffer to elute the protein from the chromatography column.
To isolate the compound, you must carefully elute it with the appropriate solvent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – extract: to remove or take out, especially by effort or force
