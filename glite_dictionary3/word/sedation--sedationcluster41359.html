sedation

noun
administration of a sedative drug
Pronunciation: / səˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the administration of a drug to produce a state of calm or sleep, especially before or during a medical procedure

Examples

sedation before surgery
After receiving sedation, the child was relaxed and ready for the minor surgery.
Patients often require sedation before undergoing an endoscopy.
The dentist recommended sedation to help the anxious patient remain calm during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anesthesia: loss of sensation induced by drugs, often to allow medical procedures
  • antonym – stimulation: encouragement of activity or increased alertness, often in contrast to calming or sedating
