to recognize

verb
identify and respond to antigen or substrate
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛkəɡnʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a cell, molecule, etc.) to identify and respond specifically to a particular antigen or substrate.

Examples

recognize a virus
Certain antibodies are able to recognize foreign antigens with high specificity.
Enzymes recognize their specific substrates and facilitate chemical reactions.
The immune cells recognize the invading virus and mount a defense against it.
