upstairs

noun
upper floor(s) of a building
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈstɛrz / (AmE), / ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
the part of a building that is on or to an upper floor, above the ground level

Examples

children upstairs
Children are not allowed upstairs without an adult.
He heard strange noises coming from upstairs while he was in the kitchen.
The guests stayed upstairs during the party.

Related Terms

  • antonym – downstairs: the part of a building below or on the ground floor
