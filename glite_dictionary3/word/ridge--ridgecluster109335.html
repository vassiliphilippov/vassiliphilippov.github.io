ridge

noun
highest horizontal beam of a roof
Pronunciation: / rɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɪdʒ / (BrE)
the horizontal beam or line at the highest point of a roof where two sloping sides meet, providing support for the upper ends of rafters

Examples

roof ridge
A decorative tile was placed along the ridge of the roof for added strength.
Rainwater flows down from the ridge to the eaves on both sides of the house.
The ridge beam supports the structure where the roof's two slopes meet.

Related Terms

  • antonym – eaves: the part of a roof that meets or overhangs the walls of a building
