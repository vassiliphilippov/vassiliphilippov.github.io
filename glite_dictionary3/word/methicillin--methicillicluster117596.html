methicillin

noun
antibiotic for certain staph infections
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛθəˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌmiːθɪˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic of the penicillin group used in the treatment of bacterial infections, especially those caused by staphylococci

Examples

methicillin resistant bacteria
Because of the rise of MRSA, the use of methicillin has declined in recent years.
Some strains of bacteria are no longer susceptible to methicillin.
The doctors prescribed methicillin to combat the resistant staph infection.
