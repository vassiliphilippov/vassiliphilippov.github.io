verdolagas

noun
edible herb also known as purslane
Pronunciation: / vɛrˈdoʊlɑɡəz / (AmE), / vɜːˈdɒlɑːɡəz / (BrE)
A weedy trailing herb (Portulaca oleracea), with bright yellow flowers, cultivated for its edible, mildly acid leaves, especially used in Indian, Greek, and Middle Eastern cuisines.

Examples

verdolagas in salads
In Mexico, people often add verdolagas to stews for added flavor and nutrition.
The salad was topped with fresh verdolagas picked from the garden.
You can eat verdolagas both raw in salads or cooked in soups.
