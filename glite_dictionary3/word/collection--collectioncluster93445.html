collection

noun
group of gathered things
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a group of things that have been gathered together or are considered as a whole, often because they are related or of interest

Examples

collection of stamps
His stamp collection has grown steadily since he was a child.
She keeps a large collection of rare coins in a locked cabinet.
The museum's latest collection features modern art from around the world.

  • antonym – dispersal: the act of distributing things over a wide area
  • synonym – hoard: a hidden or carefully guarded collection of valuables
