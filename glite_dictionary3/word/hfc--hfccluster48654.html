HFC

noun
hydrofluorocarbon compound (industrial/greenhouse gas)
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ ɛf ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ɛf ˈsiː / (BrE)
a hydrofluorocarbon; a compound containing hydrogen, fluorine, and carbon, used mainly as refrigerants and emitted as greenhouse gases during industrial processes

Examples

HFC emissions
Many refrigerators now use HFC instead of older, ozone-depleting chemicals.
Scientists are seeking alternatives to HFC due to their impact on global warming.
The production of HFC is now strictly regulated under international agreements.
