endothermic

adjective
absorbing heat (chemical/physical process)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈθɝːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈθɜːmɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical reaction or process) absorbing heat from its surroundings.

Examples

endothermic reaction
Melting ice is an endothermic process that absorbs heat from its surroundings.
Photosynthesis is an endothermic reaction because it requires energy from sunlight to proceed.
The teacher explained that dissolving ammonium nitrate in water is endothermic as it cools the mixture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exothermic: (of a chemical reaction or process) releasing heat
