cardboard

adjective
insubstantial; lacking depth or realism
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdbɔːd / (BrE)
(of a person, character, or thing) lacking realism, depth, or substance; insubstantial or unconvincing.

Examples

cardboard character
Critics complained that the film's villain was a cardboard character with no real motivation.
She found the politician's promises to be cardboard and meaningless.
The novel’s hero felt like a cardboard figure, flat and unconvincing.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lifelike: having a realistic or natural appearance or quality
  • synonym – superficial: not thorough or deep; lacking depth
