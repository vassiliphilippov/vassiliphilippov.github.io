Cornish

noun
breed of domestic chicken for meat
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪʃ / (BrE)
a compact British breed of domestic chicken, primarily raised for meat production and for crossbreeding to produce roasters

Examples

Cornish chicken breed
A purebred Cornish has a broad, muscular body ideal for roasting.
Many commercial broilers are the result of crossing Cornish with other breeds.
The farm specializes in raising Cornish for their high-quality meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Plymouth Rock: another breed of domestic chicken, often used in crossbreeding
