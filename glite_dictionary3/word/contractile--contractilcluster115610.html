contractile

adjective
capable of contracting or shrinking
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræk.taɪl / (AmE), / kənˈtræk.taɪl / (BrE)
able to contract or shrink; having the quality or function of becoming smaller or shorter.

Examples

contractile muscle fibers
A jellyfish moves by pulsing its contractile bell, propelling itself through the water.
Specialized contractile cells in the heart help it pump blood throughout the body.
The nematocyst has a contractile thread that can rapidly shoot out in response to stimuli.
