to document

verb
to support with references or evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑː.kjə.mənt /; / ˈdɑːkjəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.jʊ.mənt /; / ˈdɒkjʊmənt / (BrE)
to record or provide evidence of something, especially in writing or with supporting materials

Examples

to document a process
Investigators were careful to document every step of their process.
The professor asked if you could document your research sources with published studies.
You need to document all expenditures in your report to the board.
All incidents must be documented thoroughly for the official report.
She documents the lives of endangered animals through her photography.
The researcher carefully documented each phase of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chronicle: to record events or information in chronological order
  • antonym – contradict: to say the opposite of a statement, or to deny the truth of it
  • synonym – prove: to demonstrate the truth or existence of something by evidence or argument
  • synonym – record: to set down information for preservation in writing or other form
  • synonym – substantiate: to provide evidence to support or prove the truth of something
