to begin

verb
to do in the slightest degree (usually in the negative)
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn / (BrE)
to do, achieve, or deal with something in any degree, usually used in the negative to emphasize inadequacy or impossibility

Examples

begin to understand
Most students cannot begin to grasp the complexity of quantum physics.
The new security policies don't begin to address the site's vulnerabilities.
You can't begin to imagine how much work went into this event.
