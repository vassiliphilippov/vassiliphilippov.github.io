scattering

noun
deflection of particles or waves by collisions
Pronunciation: / ˈskæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process in which particles or waves are deflected in various directions as a result of collisions with other particles or obstacles

Examples

scattering of particles
Physicists studied the scattering of neutrons as they passed through the crystal.
Rayleigh scattering explains why sunsets are red and skies are blue.
The scattering of light by the atmosphere causes the sky to appear blue.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reflection: return of light, heat, or sound from a surface
