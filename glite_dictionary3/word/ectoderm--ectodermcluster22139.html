ectoderm

noun
outer embryonic layer
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.toʊˌdɝm / (AmE), / ˈɛk.təʊ.dɜːm / (BrE)
the outermost layer of cells in the embryo, which develops into the skin and nervous system

Examples

ectoderm forms skin
Damage to the ectoderm can lead to defects in the nervous system.
During development, the ectoderm gives rise to the brain and spinal cord.
Skin cells are derived from the ectoderm of the embryo.

Related Terms

  • antonym – endoderm: the innermost germ layer, forming the gut lining and associated structures
  • antonym – mesoderm: the middle germ layer, developing into muscles, bones, and the circulatory system
