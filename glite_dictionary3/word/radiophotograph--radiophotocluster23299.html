radiophotograph

noun
photo sent by radio transmission
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈfoʊtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈfəʊtəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A photograph that is transmitted by radio waves, especially for news or scientific purposes.

Examples

radiophotograph of news event
In the early days of remote journalism, a radiophotograph was the fastest way to share images across continents.
Technicians checked the quality of the radiophotograph before publishing it in the magazine.
The breaking news was accompanied by a radiophotograph of the disaster site, sent directly to newspapers.
