ray

noun
a kind of flat, winged cartilaginous fish
Pronunciation: / reɪ / (AmE), / reɪ / (BrE)
a type of cartilaginous fish with a flattened body, winglike pectoral fins, and gills on the underside, closely related to sharks

Examples

a ray fish
A stingray is a type of ray known for the barbed spine on its tail.
Many species of ray use their wide fins to 'fly' through the water.
The aquarium has a large tank where you can see rays gliding gracefully above the sandy floor.
