to prologise

verb
write or deliver a prologue
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊ.ləˌɡaɪz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ləˌɡaɪz / (BrE)
To write or deliver a prologue, especially to a play, book, or other work.

Examples

to prologise a play
Before the play began, the director prologised to the audience, sharing insights about its creation.
He would often prologise before his lectures, setting the scene for his main argument.
The author chose to prologise her novel with a heartfelt explanation to the readers.
