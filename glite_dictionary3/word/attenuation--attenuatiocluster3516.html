attenuation

noun
weakening in force or intensity
Pronunciation: / əˌtɛnjuˈeɪʃən /; / əˌtɛn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌtɛnjuˈeɪʃən /; / əˌtɛnjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of making something weaker, less intense, or less dense (such as sound, signal, or force)

Examples

attenuation of sound
Soundproofing materials are used for the attenuation of unwanted noise.
The attenuation of the signal was caused by the long cable run.
There was noticeable attenuation in his voice over the phone.
Modern fiber optic cables minimize the attenuation of data signals over long distances.
Researchers measured the attenuation of sound as it passed through the dense forest.
The attenuation of the medication's effect means it must be administered more frequently.

Related Terms

  • antonym – amplification: the process of making something stronger, especially in signal strength
  • synonym – weakening: the process of becoming weaker
