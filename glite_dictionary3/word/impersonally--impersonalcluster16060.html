impersonally

adverb
in an impersonal manner
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɜrsənəli /; / ɪmˈpɝːsənəli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
in a way that is not personal, warm, or emotional; objectively or without showing personal feelings

Examples

to speak impersonally
Feedback was given impersonally, without regard for anyone's feelings.
He always responds impersonally to emails, never adding any personal touch.
The manager addressed the concerns impersonally, focusing only on the facts.
News anchors are trained to report impersonally to maintain objectivity.
She always responds impersonally to criticism, never letting it affect her mood.
The manager addressed the staff impersonally, avoiding any mention of individual concerns.
