discharge

noun
the act of releasing or venting
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the act of releasing or venting something, such as a substance, emotion, or force

Examples

emotional discharge after argument
After the heated argument, she felt a discharge of pent-up frustration.
Safety procedures were put in place to control the discharge of pressure from the equipment.
The discharge of toxic fumes from the factory was quickly detected by nearby residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – release: the action of letting something out
  • antonym – restraint: the act of holding something back or controlling it
