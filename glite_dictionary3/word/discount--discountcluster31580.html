to discount

verb
disregard or ignore as unimportant
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
to regard something as unworthy of consideration or ignore as unimportant

Examples

to discount an idea
His expertise shouldn't be discounted when making the final decision.
It's unfair to discount her achievements just because she's young.
The board chose to discount the rumors due to lack of evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – consider: to think about carefully
  • synonym – dismiss: to decide not to think about or consider
