vena epigastrica superficialis

noun
superficial abdominal wall vein
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.nə ˌɛp.ɪˈɡæs.trɪ.kə ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.ə.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˌɛp.ɪˈɡæs.trɪ.kə ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.ə.lɪs / (BrE)
a superficial vein in the lower abdominal wall that drains into the great saphenous vein.

Examples

vena epigastrica superficialis drains blood
During surgery, the vena epigastrica superficialis must be carefully identified to prevent excessive bleeding.
The vena epigastrica superficialis drains blood from the lower anterior abdominal wall.
The vena epigastrica superficialis is sometimes visible in patients with thin abdominal walls.
