diuresis

noun
increased urine production
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪjʊˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪjʊəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
increased production or excretion of urine, especially not due to increased fluid intake or diuretic drugs

Examples

excessive diuresis after illness
Patients with diabetes may experience diuresis as a symptom of elevated blood sugar.
Persistent diuresis can lead to dehydration if not properly managed.
The doctor noted a marked diuresis after administering the intravenous fluids.

Related Terms

  • synonym – polyuria: excessive or abnormally large production of urine
