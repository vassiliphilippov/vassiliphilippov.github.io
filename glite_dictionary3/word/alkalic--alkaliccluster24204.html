alkalic

adjective
containing or relating to alkali; pH above 7
Pronunciation: / ælˈkælɪk / (AmE), / ælˈkælɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) relating to or containing an alkali; having a pH greater than 7; alkaline.

Examples

alkalic soil
Certain plants thrive in alkalic conditions, while others prefer more acidic environments.
Gardeners often test soil to see if it is alkalic or acidic.
The alkalic rocks in the region are rich in sodium and potassium.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acidic: containing acid or having a pH below 7
  • synonym – alkaline: having the properties of an alkali; pH greater than 7
