to attend

verb
to deal with; take care of
Pronunciation: / əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to deal with or take charge of something; to give one's attention to something that needs action

Examples

to attend urgent matters
I have to attend to some urgent emails before I can leave.
She asked her assistant to attend to the arrangements for the conference.
The doctor will attend to you in just a moment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to something; not deal with it
  • antonym – neglect: to fail to care for or deal with something properly
