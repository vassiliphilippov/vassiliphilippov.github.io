apostle

noun
ardent early supporter of a cause
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
A passionate early supporter and promoter of a particular cause or reform.

Examples

apostle of change
He is widely recognized as an apostle of free-market capitalism in the early twentieth century.
She became an apostle of environmental awareness, speaking at every possible event.
The scientist was an apostle of vaccination when few others supported the idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym – advocate: a person who supports or recommends a particular cause
  • antonym – opponent: someone who is against or resists a cause or proposal
