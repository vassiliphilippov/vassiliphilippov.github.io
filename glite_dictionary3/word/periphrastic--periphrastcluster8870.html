periphrastic

adjective
using unnecessarily many words
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˈfræstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈfrastɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) using more words than necessary; roundabout or indirect in expression.

Examples

periphrastic explanation
His explanation was unnecessarily periphrastic, making a simple topic sound confusing.
Instead of being direct, she gave a periphrastic account of the events.
The report was criticized for its periphrastic style and lack of clarity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circumlocutory: using too many words to express an idea
  • antonym – concise: expressing much in few words
