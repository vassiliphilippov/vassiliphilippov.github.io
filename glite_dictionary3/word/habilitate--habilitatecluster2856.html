to habilitate

verb
qualify for university teaching (Europe)
Pronunciation: / həˈbɪlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / həˈbɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
to achieve the qualifications required to teach at a university, especially in some European countries, typically above the level of a doctoral degree

Examples

to habilitate as professor
After several years of research, she finally habilitated and began to supervise her own doctoral students.
He habilitated after his sabbatical at a prestigious American university.
In some European countries, professors must habilitate before leading independent research groups.

  • synonym – qualify: to achieve a position or meet standards, especially for a job or privilege
