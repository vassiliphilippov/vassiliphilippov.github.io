to buck

verb
strive with determination
Pronunciation: / bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
(of a person) to make a determined effort to achieve or obtain something, especially to advance in position or rank.

Examples

to buck for promotion
He bucked for more responsibility within the team.
She has been bucking for a management position all year.
The interns are bucking to get noticed by the new supervisor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – slack: to be careless or uninvolved; the opposite of striving
  • synonym – strive: to make a great effort to achieve something
  • synonym – vie: to compete eagerly with someone for something
