to divagate

verb
to stray from the subject or path
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪvəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪvəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to wander or stray, especially from the main subject in speech, writing, or thought

Examples

to divagate from topic
Her essay divagates into personal anecdotes that distract from her main argument.
The lecturer began to divagate from the topic, discussing unrelated historical events at length.
Whenever he is nervous, he tends to divagate during meetings and loses track of the main agenda.

Related Terms

  • synonym – digress: to leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing
