Lactuca sativa crispa

noun
lettuce variety with loose, curled leaves
Pronunciation: / lækˈtuːkə səˈtaɪvə ˈkrɪspə / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə səˈtaɪvə ˈkrɪspə / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with curled or incised leaves forming a loose rosette, not developing a compact head; also known as loose-leaf lettuce.

Examples

Gardeners often prefer growing Lactuca sativa crispa because of its tender, flavorful leaves.
Salads made with Lactuca sativa crispa have a lighter texture than those with head lettuce.
