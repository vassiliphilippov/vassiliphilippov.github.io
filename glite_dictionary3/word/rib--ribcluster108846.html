to rib

verb
tease or joke with someone
Pronunciation: / rɪb / (AmE), / rɪb / (BrE)
to tease or joke with someone in a good-natured or playful way

Examples

to rib a friend
Don't take it personally if Alan ribs you—it's just his way of being friendly.
He likes to rib his coworkers about their coffee choices.
They were ribbing her for her unusual hat, but she took it well.

  • antonym – praise: to express approval or admiration
