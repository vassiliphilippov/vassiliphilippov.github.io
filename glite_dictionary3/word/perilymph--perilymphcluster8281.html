perilymph

noun
fluid in the space of the inner ear
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrɪˌlɪmf / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪlɪmf / (BrE)
the clear bodily fluid that occupies the space between the bony labyrinth and the membranous labyrinth in the inner ear

Examples

inner ear perilymph
Any disruption to the composition of perilymph can result in balance and hearing disorders.
The hair cells in the cochlea are surrounded by perilymph and play a crucial role in hearing.
The surgeon carefully avoided spilling the perilymph during the inner ear procedure.
