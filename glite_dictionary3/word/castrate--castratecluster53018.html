to castrate

verb
edit by omitting indelicate parts
Pronunciation: / ˈkæstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæstreɪt / (BrE)
To edit a text by removing or altering parts considered indelicate or offensive.

Examples

to castrate a book
Many classic novels have been castrated for younger audiences over the years.
Some critics claim the movie was castrated by censors before its release.
The publisher decided to castrate the memoir to make it suitable for schools.
