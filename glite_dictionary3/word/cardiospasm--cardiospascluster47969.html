cardiospasm

noun
spasm of the cardiac sphincter
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdioʊˌspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪəʊˌspazəm / (BrE)
a spasm of the cardiac sphincter, located between the esophagus and the stomach, which can obstruct the passage of food into the stomach

Examples

cardiospasm blocks swallowing
Chronic cardiospasm may require surgical intervention to allow the passage of food into the stomach.
Doctors use specialized tests to distinguish cardiospasm from other conditions affecting the esophagus.
The patient was diagnosed with cardiospasm after experiencing difficulty swallowing solid foods.
