to stomach

verb
to tolerate or bear (food, situation)
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
to be able to tolerate or endure something (such as food, a situation, or a person), especially when it is unpleasant

Examples

to stomach bad news
He can't stomach the thought of eating oysters.
Many people find it hard to stomach spicy foods.
She could barely stomach his arrogant attitude during the meeting.
He managed to stomach the harsh criticism without complaining.
I can't stomach the idea of injustice in the workplace.
She couldn't stomach his arrogant attitude any longer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abide: to accept or tolerate something unpleasant
  • synonym – bear: accept or tolerate (something painful or difficult)
  • synonym – endure: to continue to exist or deal with something unpleasant
  • synonym – tolerate: to allow or endure something unpleasant
