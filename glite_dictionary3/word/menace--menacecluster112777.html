to menace

verb
to threaten; pose a danger
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnəs /; / ˈmɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛnɪs / (BrE)
to threaten or pose a danger to someone or something

Examples

to menace the public
Industrial waste continues to menace the health of local residents.
Rising sea levels menace coastal towns across the region.
The expansion of the invasive species could menace the native wildlife.
Rising floodwaters menaced the small village all night.
The angry dog menaced anyone who tried to approach the yard.
The tornado continued to menace the coastline as it moved closer to shore.
She menaced me with a raised fist when she got angry.
The dog menaced the mail carrier by barking and baring its teeth.
The gang menaced everyone in the neighborhood for weeks before being arrested.
