to depolarise

verb
reduce political or social polarization
Pronunciation: / did0c8po8al59ccra6az / (AmE), / did0c8p598al59ra6az / (BrE)
(of politics, a debate, or a society) to reduce polarization or strong opposing divisions between groups or viewpoints.

Examples

to depolarise a debate
The new mayor promised to depolarise city politics by forming a cross-party advisory council.
Several community groups worked together to depolarise local debate around the school expansion plan.
Efforts to depolarise the national conversation were undermined by inflammatory social media posts.

Related Terms
