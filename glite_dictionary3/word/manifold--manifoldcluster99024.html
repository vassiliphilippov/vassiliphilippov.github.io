to manifold

verb
make multiple copies (especially of documents)
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.əˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ɪ.fəʊld / (BrE)
to make multiple copies of something, especially a document; to duplicate.

Examples

to manifold a document
Before photocopiers existed, offices often manifolded business correspondence to distribute to several departments.
In the early 20th century, secretaries would manifold important letters using carbon paper.
She manifolds each invoice to ensure both the customer and accounting receive a copy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – copy: to reproduce text or images
  • synonym – duplicate: to make an exact copy of something
