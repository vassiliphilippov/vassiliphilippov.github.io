parrotfish

noun
brightly colored tropical fish with beak-like mouth
Pronunciation: / ˈpærətˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpærətˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A brightly colored tropical fish with a beak-like mouth formed by fused teeth, commonly found in coral reefs.

Examples

colorful parrotfish swimming
A parrotfish can often be seen using its strong beak to scrape algae from rocks.
Many species of parrotfish contribute to the production of sand on tropical beaches by grinding up coral.
The dazzling colors of the parrotfish make it a favorite among divers exploring coral reefs.
