sheet

noun
single piece of paper for writing or printing
Pronunciation: / ʃit / (AmE), / ʃiːt / (BrE)
a flat, thin piece of material, such as paper, metal, glass, or plastic, that is much wider and longer than it is thick

Examples

sheet of paper
Each student was given a sheet to complete during the exam.
She scribbled her notes on a sheet of lined paper.
The printer jammed because too many sheets loaded at once.
She bought a sheet every morning to catch up on the neighborhood news.
The latest gossip was splashed across the front page of the evening sheet.
Unlike the broadsheets, the local sheet focused on sensational stories and photographs.
A thin sheet of plastic covered the table to protect it from paint.
After the storm, a sheet of water covered the entire road.
In the winter, a sheet of ice forms over the lake.

  • synonym – tabloid: a newspaper with a compact page size, typically half of a broadsheet
