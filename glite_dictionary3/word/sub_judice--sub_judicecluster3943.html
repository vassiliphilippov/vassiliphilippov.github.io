sub judice

adjective
under judicial consideration
Pronunciation: / sʌb ˈdʒuːdɪsi / (AmE), / sʌb ˈdʒuːdɪsi / (BrE)
(of a case or matter) under consideration by a judge or court and therefore prohibited from public discussion elsewhere.

Examples

sub judice case
Because the matter is sub judice, journalists are restricted in what they may report.
Discussion of the incident is sub judice until the trial has concluded.
The issue remains sub judice, so officials refused to comment on its details.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pendente lite: during litigation; while a lawsuit is pending
