fellowship

noun
grant for study or research
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛloʊˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊʃɪp / (BrE)
A grant of money awarded by a university, foundation, or other agency for advanced study or research.

Examples

win a fellowship grant
Many postdoctoral researchers rely on a fellowship to fund their studies.
She received a fellowship to conduct research at Oxford University.
The university offers a prestigious fellowship for graduate students each year.
