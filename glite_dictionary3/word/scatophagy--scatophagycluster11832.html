scatophagy

noun
eating of excrement or filth
Pronunciation: / skæˈtɑfədʒi / (AmE), / skəˈtɒfəˌdʒi / (BrE)
the practice of eating excrement or other filth, often observed in some animals and rarely in humans

Examples

scatophagy in animals
Rare cases of scatophagy have been documented in humans, usually linked to psychiatric disorders.
Some insects, such as dung beetles, exhibit scatophagy as a regular part of their life cycle.
The study focused on the effects of scatophagy among certain mammal species in the wild.
