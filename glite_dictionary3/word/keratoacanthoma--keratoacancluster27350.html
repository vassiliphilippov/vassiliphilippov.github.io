keratoacanthoma

noun
rapidly growing skin tumor, often self-resolving
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrətoʊˌækænˈθoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌkɛrətəʊˌækænˈθəʊmə / (BrE)
A rapidly growing skin tumor, especially found in older adults, that resembles a carcinoma but typically does not spread and often resolves on its own, sometimes leaving a scar.

Examples

a keratoacanthoma tumor
After a few weeks, the keratoacanthoma began to shrink and eventually disappeared, leaving only a small scar.
The dermatologist diagnosed the lesion as a keratoacanthoma, reassuring the patient that it would likely resolve on its own.
While keratoacanthoma can look alarming, it's important to distinguish it from more dangerous skin cancers.
