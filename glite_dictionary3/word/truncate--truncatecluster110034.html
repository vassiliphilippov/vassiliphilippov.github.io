to truncate

verb
shorten by cutting off a part
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌŋ.keɪt /; / ˈtrʌŋkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋ.keɪt /; / ˈtrʌŋkeɪt / (BrE)
to shorten something by cutting off a part, especially the end (including omitting parts in mathematics or data)

Examples

to truncate a story
As the trail was blocked, we were forced to truncate our hike.
The professor decided to truncate his lecture due to time constraints.
The publisher had to truncate the article to fit it on one page.
Engineers sometimes truncate the data set to improve processing efficiency.
The value was truncated to three decimal places for simplicity.
To speed up calculations, scientists often truncate the infinite series after a few terms.
