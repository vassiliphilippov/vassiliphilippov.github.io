dissimilation

noun
a linguistic process where similar sounds become less alike
Pronunciation: / dɪˌsɪməˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in language by which similar sounds in a word become less alike, often to make pronunciation easier

Examples

sound dissimilation process
In some dialects, the dissimilation of similar consonants creates new word forms.
Linguists study dissimilation to understand how languages change over time.
The word 'colonel' in English is a result of historical dissimilation.
