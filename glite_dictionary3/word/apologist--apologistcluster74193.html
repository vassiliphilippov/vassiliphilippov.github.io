apologist

noun
person who defends a belief or policy
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who defends or justifies a particular policy, institution, or belief, often by argument

Examples

apologist for government actions
Historians sometimes act as apologists when evaluating past regimes.
She became known as an apologist for government surveillance programs.
The senator was labeled an apologist for the controversial new law.
