to clobber

verb
defeat thoroughly (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑːbər / (AmE), / ˈklɒbə / (BrE)
to defeat thoroughly and decisively in a competition or fight

Examples

to clobber a team
If we train hard enough, we can clobber them in the finals next week.
She absolutely clobbered her opponent in the chess tournament.
The home team managed to clobber their rivals with a final score of 8–1.

  • synonym – thrash: defeat soundly in a contest
