to anticipate

verb
to precede or foreshadow
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to be a forerunner of or occur earlier than; to foreshadow or prefigure something before it happens

Examples

anticipate future trends
Her writing style anticipates the themes found in modernist literature.
The artist’s early work clearly anticipates the development of cubism.
This discovery anticipated later theories in quantum mechanics by decades.
