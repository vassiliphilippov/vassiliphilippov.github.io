deflector

noun
device that redirects flow
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛktə / (BrE)
a device that changes the direction of a flow, such as of air, water, or smoke

Examples

deflector plate
A smoke deflector was installed to prevent fumes from entering the room.
The car was fitted with a deflector to reduce wind noise inside the cabin.
The engineer recommended a special deflector to redirect the airflow around the building.
