fanatism

noun
intolerant zeal or excessive devotion
Pronunciation: / fəˈnætɪzəm / (AmE), / fəˈnætɪzəm / (BrE)
excessive intolerance or zeal in support of one's own views, often accompanied by hostility toward opposing opinions

Examples

religious fanatism
His fanatism left no room for compromise or understanding of others' perspectives.
Religious fanatism often fuels conflict and intolerance across the world.
The rise of political fanatism led to increasing social divisions within the country.

  • synonym – fanaticism: excessive zeal or obsessive enthusiasm, especially for a cause or belief
