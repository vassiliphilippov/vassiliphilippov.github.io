congealed

adjective
hardened or solidified (by cooling)
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒild / (AmE), / kənˈdʒiːld / (BrE)
(of a liquid or soft substance) having become solid or thickened, usually by cooling.

Examples

congealed soup
After being left out overnight, the soup had congealed into a thick mass.
Fat congealed on the surface of the stew as it cooled.
He grimaced at the sight of congealed gravy on his plate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – liquefied: having become liquid
  • synonym – solidified: having become solid or hard
