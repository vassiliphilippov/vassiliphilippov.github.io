phosphor

noun
light-emitting substance in screens and displays
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑs.fɔr / (AmE), / ˈfɒs.fɔː / (BrE)
a synthetic or natural substance that emits light when exposed to radiation, commonly used to coat the screens of cathode ray tubes and other display devices.

Examples

phosphor coating screens
Scientists developed a new phosphor that enhances the brightness of LED lights.
The phosphor coating inside the television tube emits light when struck by electrons.
The display's vibrant colors are produced by different types of phosphor materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fluorescent: emitting visible light when exposed to radiation
  • synonym – phosphorescent: emitting light without noticeable heat
