to bate

verb
to restrain or lessen; moderate
Pronunciation: / beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
to restrain, moderate, or lessen the force, intensity, or amount of something (such as emotions or enthusiasm)

Examples

to bate excitement
He bated his enthusiasm to avoid overwhelming his friends.
She could not bate her anger, even in front of the audience.
You should bate your excitement until the results are announced.

She spoke with bated enthusiasm, unwilling to get her hopes up too high.
They decided to bate their expectations after hearing about the new changes.
