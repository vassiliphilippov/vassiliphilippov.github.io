muckraking

noun
exposure of scandal, especially about public figures
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌkˌreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌkˌreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or practice of searching out and publicizing scandal, especially about public figures

Examples

muckraking journalism
She made her career through persistent muckraking that exposed corporate wrongdoing.
The newspaper's muckraking led to several politicians being investigated for corruption.
While some saw his reporting as responsible journalism, others accused him of sensationalist muckraking.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cover-up: the act or an instance of concealing or covering up a scandal
  • synonym – investigative journalism: journalism that investigates and exposes issues or scandals
