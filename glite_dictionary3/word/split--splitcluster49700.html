split

adjective
divided; unity destroyed
Pronunciation: / splɪt / (AmE), / splɪt / (BrE)
(of a group or organization) having been divided into separate parts, often with a loss of unity or agreement.

Examples

split group opinion
A split party is unlikely to succeed in the upcoming elections.
After the heated argument, the group remained deeply split over the issue.
The split committee struggled to reach any agreement on the proposal.

  • antonym – united: joined or combined as a single entity
