archeologist

noun
person who studies ancient cultures through artifacts
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrkɪˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation and analysis of artifacts and other physical remains

Examples

archeologist uncovers ruins
As an archeologist, she often traveled to remote sites in search of ancient ruins.
Many archeologists specialize in the study of prehistoric tools and artifacts.
The archeologist carefully brushed dirt away from the newly discovered pottery shard.
