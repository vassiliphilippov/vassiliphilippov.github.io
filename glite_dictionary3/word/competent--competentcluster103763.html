competent

adjective
having the required skill or ability
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmpətənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpɪtənt / (BrE)
having the necessary ability, knowledge, or skill to do something well enough; able or good enough for the purpose

Examples

competent worker
Although he was new to the role, Jack proved himself to be competent.
Only competent drivers are allowed to operate the vehicle on this site.
She is a competent engineer, capable of solving complex problems quickly.
His work is competent, but lacks the creativity needed to stand out.
She is a competent pianist, though not considered particularly gifted.
The actor gave a competent performance that met expectations but did not impress the critics.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adequate: satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity
  • synonym – capable: having the ability or qualities necessary for something
  • antonym – exceptional: unusually good; outstanding
  • antonym – incompetent: not having the necessary abilities or skills
