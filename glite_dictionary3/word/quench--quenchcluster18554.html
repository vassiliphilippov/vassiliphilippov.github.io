to quench

verb
to suppress a spark or discharge in a circuit
Pronunciation: / kwɛntʃ / (AmE), / kwɛntʃ / (BrE)
(of an electric circuit or component) to suppress or stop a spark, oscillation, or electrical discharge, especially when a current is interrupted.

Examples

to quench a spark
An additional circuit was added to quench the unwanted oscillations in the amplifier.
Proper design helps quench electrical discharges before they can damage sensitive components.
The capacitor is designed to quench the spark created when the switch is opened.

Related Terms

  • synonym – extinguish: to cause something to stop burning or shining
  • synonym – suppress: to put an end to something, especially forcibly
