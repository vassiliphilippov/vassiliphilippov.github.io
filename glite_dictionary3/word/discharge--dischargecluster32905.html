discharge

noun
substance released from the body
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a substance that comes out from the body, often as the result of a natural process or disease

Examples

body discharge after surgery
After the surgery, she noticed a small amount of clear discharge near the incision.
Excessive vaginal discharge can be a sign of infection.
The doctor examined the unusual discharge from the patient's ear.
