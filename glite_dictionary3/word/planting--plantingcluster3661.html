planting

noun
act of fixing or placing firmly in place
Pronunciation: / ˈplæn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing something or someone firmly in position, especially secretly or with intent to cause an effect

Examples

the planting of evidence
She was accused of the planting of false information to mislead the public.
The planting of microphones in the room was discovered by investigators.
There was evidence of the planting of security guards at all entrances before the event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – placement: the act of putting something in a specific place
  • antonym – removal: the act of taking something away from a place
