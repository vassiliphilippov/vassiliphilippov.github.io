workday

noun
required daily working time
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːkˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.deɪ / (BrE)
the amount of time in a day that an employee is required or expected to work, usually for a set wage

Examples

eight-hour workday
Due to new regulations, the standard workday has been reduced to seven hours.
During the busy season, his workday often extends into the evening.
Her workday typically starts at 9 a.m. and ends at 5 p.m.

  • synonym – shift: a scheduled period of work within a workday
