blusterous

adjective
stormy, violently blowing (weather)
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌstɚəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌstərəs / (BrE)
(of wind or weather) blowing in violent and abrupt bursts; stormy and noisy.

Examples

blusterous wind
Sailing became dangerous as the weather turned suddenly blusterous.
The blusterous wind rattled the windows all night, making it impossible to sleep.
We postponed our hike due to the blusterous conditions on the mountain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blustery: (of weather) characterized by strong winds; stormy
