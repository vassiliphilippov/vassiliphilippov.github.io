Bombax malabarica

noun
East Indian silk cotton tree
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑmbæks ˌmælɑːˈbɑrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈbɒmbaks ˌmæl.əˈbɑːr.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
An East Indian silk cotton tree whose fibers are used for stuffing but are considered inferior to kapok.

Examples

Bombax malabarica tree fibers
Farmers in India cultivate Bombax malabarica for both its wood and its cottony fruit.
The silk-like fibers from the Bombax malabarica are often used for pillow stuffing.
Unlike kapok, the fibers of Bombax malabarica are coarser and less buoyant.
