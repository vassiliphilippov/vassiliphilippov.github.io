waters

noun
amniotic fluid released before childbirth
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚz / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the serous fluid in which the embryo is suspended inside the amnion; especially as released when a woman's amniotic sac breaks before childbirth

Examples

waters broke suddenly
She realized she was about to go into labor when her waters broke.
The doctor explained that when the waters break, delivery is usually imminent.
When a woman's waters rupture early, it can lead to complications.
