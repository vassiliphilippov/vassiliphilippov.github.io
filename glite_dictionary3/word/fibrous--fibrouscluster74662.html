fibrous

adjective
meat with tough, stringy fibers
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɹəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbɹəs / (BrE)
(of meat or other food) containing tough fibers, making it difficult to chew.

Examples

fibrous meat texture
Older cuts of beef tend to become more fibrous and require longer cooking times.
She found the roast chicken too fibrous to enjoy and left most of it uneaten.
The steak was so fibrous that I could barely cut through it with my knife.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tough: (of food, especially meat) difficult to chew or cut
