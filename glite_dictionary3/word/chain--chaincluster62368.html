chain

noun
series of connected metal rings or links
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn / (AmE), / tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a series of metal links connected together, used for fastening, pulling, supporting, or as ornamentation

Examples

metal chain
She wore a delicate gold chain around her neck.
The dog was kept outside with a heavy chain attached to its collar.
The old gate was secured with a rusty iron chain.
The old castle's dungeon still held rusted chains attached to the stone walls.
The prisoner struggled against the chains that bound his ankles.
They removed the chains from her wrists after the trial was over.
