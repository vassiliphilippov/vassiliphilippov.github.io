to wander

verb
move aimlessly without destination
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːndɚ /; / ˈwɑːndər / (AmE), / ˈwɒndə / (BrE)
to move from place to place without a fixed course or destination, often in a relaxed or aimless way

Examples

to wander the streets
During the recession, many workers were forced to wander from town to town looking for jobs.
She wandered the city streets with no particular destination in mind.
The dog likes to wander through the fields on sunny afternoons.
After dinner, we wandered into town to explore the streets.
Rather than following a map, he just wandered around the city all afternoon.
She likes to wander through the forest, enjoying the sights and sounds of nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drift: to move passively, aimlessly, or involuntarily into a certain situation or condition
  • antonym – march: to walk with regular steps in a specified direction
  • synonym – roam: to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically, especially over a wide area
  • synonym – stray: to move away aimlessly from a group or from the right course or place
