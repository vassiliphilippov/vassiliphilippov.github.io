to unyoke

verb
remove a yoke from an animal
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈjoʊk / (AmE), / ʌnˈjəʊk / (BrE)
to remove a yoke from an animal, especially one used to pull a plow or cart

Examples

to unyoke the oxen
After a long day in the fields, the farmer carefully unyoked the oxen.
The workers paused to unyoke the horses at the end of the journey.
You must unyoke the cows before letting them rest for the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym – yoke: a wooden crosspiece fastened over the necks of animals to link them together for pulling a load
