chatelaine

noun
waist chain for carrying keys or small items
Pronunciation: / ˌʃætəˈleɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃatəleɪn / (BrE)
A chain worn at a woman's waist, used for carrying keys, a purse, or other small items, especially popular in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Examples

chatelaine with keys
In the 19th century, a chatelaine was a practical accessory for keeping important items close at hand.
Museum displays often feature a Victorian chatelaine complete with scissors and thimble.
The antique chatelaine she wore was decorated with tiny silver charms.
