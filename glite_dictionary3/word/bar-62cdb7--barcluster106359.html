bar

noun
unit of pressure
Pronunciation: / bɑr / (AmE), / bɑː / (BrE)
a unit of pressure, equal to 100,000 pascals or one million dynes per square centimeter, used mainly in meteorology and engineering

Examples

bar of pressure
Many pressure gauges in Europe are calibrated in bars rather than in psi.
The atmospheric pressure at sea level is approximately one bar.
The weather forecast predicted a drop of half a bar overnight.

  • synonym – pascal: the SI unit of pressure equal to one newton per square meter
