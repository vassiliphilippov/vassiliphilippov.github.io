to grubstake

verb
to fund someone for a share of profits
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌbˌsteɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbsteɪk / (BrE)
to supply someone with funds or equipment in exchange for a promised share of any future profits or returns, especially in mining or prospecting.

Examples

to grubstake a prospector
He agreed to grubstake the prospector in exchange for a share of the gold found.
Many early miners were grubstaked by investors hoping for big returns.
The wealthy merchant grubstaked several expeditions during the gold rush.

  • synonym – finance: to provide funding for something or someone
