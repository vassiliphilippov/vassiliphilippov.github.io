mordant

noun
substance aiding dyeing process
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrdənt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdənt / (BrE)
a substance used to treat materials such as textiles or leather before dyeing, to help fix the dye or make it more intense

Examples

apply mordant before dyeing
A mordant must be applied to the wool before immersing it in the dye bath.
Alum is a common mordant used in natural dyeing to help colors set on fabric.
They experimented with different mordants to achieve brighter and longer-lasting colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fixative: a substance that fixes colors in dyeing or photography
