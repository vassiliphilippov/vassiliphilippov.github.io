anthropocentrism

noun
belief that humans are central or most important
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpoʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or practice of regarding humans and human experience as the central or most significant entities in the universe.

Many environmentalists criticize anthropocentrism for placing human interests above those of other living beings.
