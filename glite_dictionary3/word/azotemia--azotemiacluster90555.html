azotemia

noun
elevated blood nitrogen waste (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌæz.oʊˈtiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌaz.əʊˈtiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
an accumulation of nitrogen-containing waste products, such as urea, in the blood, typically due to impaired kidney function

Examples

azotemia in kidney disease
Chronic kidney disease often leads to azotemia if left untreated.
Doctors monitor azotemia in patients to assess the severity of renal impairment.
The patient's blood tests showed an increase in azotemia after kidney function worsened.
