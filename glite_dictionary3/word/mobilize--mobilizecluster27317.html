to mobilize

verb
cause to move or become active
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to move or become active; to put into movement or motion

Examples

to mobilize body cells
Researchers hope to mobilize cells in the body to fight infection more effectively.
The coach will mobilize the players as soon as practice starts.
The leader worked to mobilize the entire community for the clean-up effort.

Related Terms

  • synonym – activate: to make active; put into operation
  • antonym – immobilize: to prevent from moving
