to transit

verb
to pass across (in astronomy)
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
(of a celestial body) to pass across the face of another body or the meridian of a place, as observed from a particular point.

Examples

to transit a planet
Astronomers observed the satellite transit Jupiter's disk through their telescope.
The comet is predicted to transit the meridian at midnight, making it visible to the naked eye.
The planet Venus will transit the sun next year, offering a rare astronomical event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cross: to move or extend across something
