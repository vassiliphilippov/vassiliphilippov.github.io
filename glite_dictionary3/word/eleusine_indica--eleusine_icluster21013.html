Eleusine indica

noun
annual grass; widespread weed species
Pronunciation: / iˌluːˈsiːni ˈɪndɪkə / (AmE), / ɛˌljuːˈsiːni ˈɪndɪkə / (BrE)
A coarse annual grass with fingerlike spikes of flowers, native to the Old World tropics and widely naturalized as a weed in other regions.

Examples

Eleusine indica grass weed
Eleusine indica is a common weed found in lawns, golf courses, and gardens across many continents.
Farmers often struggle to control Eleusine indica because it is resistant to several herbicides.
The fingerlike seed heads of Eleusine indica make it easy to identify among other grasses.
