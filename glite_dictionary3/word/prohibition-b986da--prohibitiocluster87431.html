prohibition

noun
law or period forbidding sale of alcohol
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.ɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the period during which the manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages was legally prohibited, especially in the United States from 1920 to 1933; or a law forbidding such practices

During prohibition, many illegal bars known as speakeasies were established across the United States.
