flamboyant

adjective
elaborately showy or decorated
Pronunciation: / flæmˈbɔɪənt / (AmE), / flamˈbɔɪənt / (BrE)
very showy, elaborate, or decorated in a way that attracts attention

Examples

flamboyant style
Critics described the architect's new building as flamboyant and out of place among the older, simpler structures.
His living room was decorated in a flamboyant style, with gold curtains and bright patterns everywhere.
The actress wore a flamboyant gown covered in sequins to the premiere.
Her flamboyant dress drew stares from everyone at the party.
The actor was known for his flamboyant gestures and dramatic speeches.
They found the decorations a bit too flamboyant for such a formal event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ornate: elaborately decorated or adorned
  • synonym – ostentatious: intended to attract notice and impress others
  • synonym – showy: making an impressive display
  • antonym – subdued: not showy or flamboyant, restrained in style
  • antonym – understated: presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way, not showy
