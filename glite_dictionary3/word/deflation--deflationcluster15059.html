deflation

noun
fall in general price levels (economics)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
a decrease in the general level of prices in an economy, usually caused by a reduction in the supply of money or credit

Examples

economic deflation period
Central banks often lower interest rates to combat the effects of deflation in the economy.
During the Great Depression, the United States experienced severe deflation across multiple industries.
Many economists worry that prolonged deflation can lead to higher unemployment and economic stagnation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inflation: general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money
