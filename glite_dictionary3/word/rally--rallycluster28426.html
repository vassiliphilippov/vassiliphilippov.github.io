rally

noun
continuous exchange of strokes in sports
Pronunciation: / ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
A continuous sequence of successful returns in sports such as tennis, badminton, or table tennis, during which the ball is not allowed to hit the ground or rules are not violated, until one player wins the point.

Examples

tennis rally
During the match, the longest rally lasted over twenty shots.
She won the point after an intense rally that thrilled the audience.
The players engaged in a long rally before one finally scored.

Related Terms

  • synonym – volley: a series of rapid returns made before the ball touches the ground (especially in tennis or volleyball)
