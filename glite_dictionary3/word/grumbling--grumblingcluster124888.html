grumbling

noun
low, indistinct complaint
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
a complaint expressed in a low, indistinct, or muttered tone, often showing discontent

Examples

office grumbling
Despite some grumbling, the new policy was eventually accepted by most employees.
His constant grumbling about the weather annoyed everyone around him.
There was a quiet grumbling in the audience as the speaker continued past his allotted time.
