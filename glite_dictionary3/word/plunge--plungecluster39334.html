to plunge

verb
begin something suddenly or energetically
Pronunciation: / plʌndʒ / (AmE), / plʌndʒ / (BrE)
to begin or get involved in an activity suddenly and energetically

Examples

to plunge into work
After months of planning, they plunged into the project with great enthusiasm.
She decided to plunge into her new job without any hesitation.
Sometimes it's best to plunge into a challenge rather than overthink your approach.
After graduating, she decided to plunge into a career in medical research.
He plunged into learning French, spending hours each day studying.
The author plunged himself into his latest novel, working day and night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – engage: to participate or become involved fully
  • antonym – hesitate: to pause before beginning something, often due to uncertainty
  • synonym – immerse: to become completely involved in something
  • synonym – launch: to start or set in motion with force or enthusiasm
