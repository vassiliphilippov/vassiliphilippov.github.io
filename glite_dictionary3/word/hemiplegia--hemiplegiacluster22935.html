hemiplegia

noun
paralysis of one side of the body
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛməˈpliːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˈpliːdʒɪə / (BrE)
Paralysis of one side of the body, typically resulting from stroke or brain injury.

Examples

hemiplegia after stroke
As a result of her accident, she suffered from left-sided hemiplegia and needed physical therapy.
Early rehabilitation can improve outcomes for those diagnosed with hemiplegia.
The patient's sudden hemiplegia indicated a possible stroke and required immediate medical attention.
