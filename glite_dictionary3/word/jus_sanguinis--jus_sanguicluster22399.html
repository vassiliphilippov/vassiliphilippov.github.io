jus sanguinis

noun
citizenship determined by parents' nationality
Pronunciation: / juːs sæŋˈɡwaɪnɪs / (AmE), / juːs sæŋˈɡwɪnɪs / (BrE)
the principle by which a person's nationality or citizenship is determined by the nationality or ethnicity of their parents, rather than by the place of birth.

Examples

jus sanguinis citizenship law
Because of jus sanguinis, children born abroad to Italian parents are automatically Italian citizens.
Many countries in Europe still use jus sanguinis as the basis for granting citizenship.
The debate about jus sanguinis versus jus soli is ongoing in several nations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – jus soli: the principle that a person's nationality or citizenship is determined by place of birth
