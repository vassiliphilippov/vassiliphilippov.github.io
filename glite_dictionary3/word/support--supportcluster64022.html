to support

verb
endure or tolerate something unpleasant
Pronunciation: / səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to tolerate or endure something unpleasant or difficult, often without complaint

Examples

to support bad behavior
He finds it hard to support his boss's rude behavior.
I don't know how much longer I can support this constant noise in the office.
She supported years of criticism without ever complaining.
