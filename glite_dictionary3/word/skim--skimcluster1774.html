to skim

verb
cover a liquid with a layer
Pronunciation: / skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to cover or coat (a liquid) with a thin layer of another substance

Examples

to skim soup with wax
A light wax was skimmed over the soup to prevent it from cooling too quickly.
After pouring the cream, she skimmed the surface with a thin layer of oil to seal it.
He carefully skimmed the paint over the liquid to form a protective film.
