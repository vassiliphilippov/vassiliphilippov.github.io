impeded

adjective
made difficult or slowed down
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpidɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːdɪd / (BrE)
Made difficult or slowed down; unable to progress freely.

Examples

impeded progress
Heavy traffic meant our journey was impeded by frequent stops and delays.
Her recovery was impeded by a lack of proper nutrition and rest.
The construction work left the walkway impeded by barriers and equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hindered: kept back or held up; delayed
  • antonym – unimpeded: not slowed or blocked; free to move or progress
