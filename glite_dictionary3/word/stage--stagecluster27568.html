to stage

verb
perform (a play) on stage
Pronunciation: / steɪdʒ / (AmE), / steɪdʒ / (BrE)
to present, perform, organize, or carry out a play, event, or activity, especially in public

Examples

to stage a play
A local theater group staged a modern adaptation of Hamlet.
The school plans to stage a new musical next semester.
They are staging several performances throughout the summer.
Protesters staged a demonstration outside the city hall.
The company will stage a grand opening ceremony next week.
They are staging a charity concert for the local hospital.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cancel: to decide or announce that an event will not take place
  • synonym – conduct: to lead or carry out an organized activity
  • synonym – organize: to arrange or coordinate the elements of an event
  • synonym – produce: to supervise and finance the staging of a play or show
