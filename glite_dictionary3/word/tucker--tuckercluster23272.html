tucker

noun
detachable garment worn over breast
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌkər / (AmE), / ˈtʌkə / (BrE)
a detachable yoke of linen or lace worn over the breast of a low-cut dress, especially in the 17th to 19th centuries

Examples

tucker lace collar
Fashion in the 18th century often required a tucker as part of women’s formal attire.
She wore a silk tucker to add modesty to her evening gown.
The museum displayed a delicate tucker from the Victorian era, trimmed with fine lace.
