fossilisation

noun
process of becoming a fossil
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːsələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɒsɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which the remains of a plant or animal become preserved as a fossil, typically turning into stone over a long period

Examples

fossilisation of bones
Rapid burial increases the chances of fossilisation in sedimentary environments.
Scientists study the fossilisation process to understand ancient ecosystems.
The fossilisation of dinosaur bones can take millions of years.
