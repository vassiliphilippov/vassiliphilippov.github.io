outcry

noun
loud protest or public anger
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.kraɪ / (BrE)
a strong and public expression of protest, disapproval, or anger, often by a group of people

Examples

public outcry
Public outcry forced the council to reconsider its plans to cut funding for the arts.
The government's decision sparked a nationwide outcry from citizens and activists alike.
There was an immediate outcry when the company announced the closure of the local factory.
After the verdict was announced, there was a national outcry against the decision.
Many voices joined the outcry for better working conditions.
The new policy sparked an immediate outcry from local residents.

  • synonym – clamor: a loud and confused noise, especially from a crowd
  • synonym – protest: statement or action expressing disapproval or dissent
  • synonym – uproar: a state of noisy confusion or protest
