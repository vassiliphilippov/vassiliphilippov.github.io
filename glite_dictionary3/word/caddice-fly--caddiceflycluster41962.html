caddice-fly

noun
aquatic moth-like insect
Pronunciation: / ˈkædɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkadɪs flaɪ / (BrE)
A small, moth-like insect with two pairs of hairy membranous wings; its larvae are aquatic and build protective cases from debris.

Examples

caddice-fly larva case
Larvae of the caddice-fly construct their cases with small pebbles and plant material.
Many freshwater fish feed on caddice-fly larvae during their development.
The caddice-fly is often found near streams and ponds during the summer months.
