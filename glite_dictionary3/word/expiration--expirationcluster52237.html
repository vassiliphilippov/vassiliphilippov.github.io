expiration

noun
end of validity period (contract, document)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the ending of a period for which something, such as a contract or document, is valid

Examples

expiration date
Make sure you check the expiration date on all your food products.
The expiration of the lease means we have to move out next month.
You need to renew your passport before its expiration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expiry: the deadline or point when something legally ends or ceases to be valid
  • antonym – renewal: the process of extending the validity of a contract or document
