to sublimate

verb
redirect impulses into acceptable activities
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌblɪˌmeɪt /; / ˈsʌbləˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌblɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to redirect strong emotions, impulses, or desires into socially acceptable, creative, or refined activities or qualities

Examples

to sublimate anger
He sublimated his frustration into writing and soon published his first novel.
Many people sublimate their aggressive impulses through competitive sports.
She tried to sublimate her anger by channeling it into her artwork.
Artists often sublimate personal experiences into their creative work.
She sought to sublimate her anger into careful, constructive criticism.

Therapists may encourage patients to sublimate negative emotions through creative hobbies or volunteering.

  • synonym – refine: to improve something by making small changes, especially to make it more subtle or precise
  • antonym – repress: to hold back or suppress impulses or emotions
