settling

noun
gradual sinking to a lower level
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which something gradually sinks to a lower level, especially through natural or physical causes

Examples

settling of the ground
Engineers monitored the settling of the building's foundation over time.
The settling of dust on the furniture happens quickly after cleaning.
There was visible settling of the soil after the heavy rainfall.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedimentation: the process of depositing particles in a liquid
  • synonym – subsidence: the gradual caving in or sinking of an area of land
