to cog

verb
join wood pieces using cogs
Pronunciation: / kɑɡ / (AmE), / kɒɡ / (BrE)
to join pieces of wood together using cogs or projections that fit into corresponding sockets

Examples

to cog wooden boards
He spent the afternoon carefully cogging each piece to ensure a tight fit.
The carpenter decided to cog the beams together for extra strength.
They cogged the boards so that the floor would be more stable.
