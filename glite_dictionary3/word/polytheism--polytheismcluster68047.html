polytheism

noun
belief in multiple gods
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌθiɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌθiːɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or worship of more than one god.

Examples

polytheism in ancient religions
Ancient Greeks practiced polytheism, worshipping a pantheon of gods and goddesses.
Some cultures today still adhere to forms of polytheism in their spiritual traditions.
The shift from polytheism to monotheism marked a major change in religious history.
