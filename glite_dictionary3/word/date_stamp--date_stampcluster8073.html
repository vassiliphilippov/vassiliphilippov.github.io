to date stamp

verb
mark with a date
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪt stæmp / (AmE), / ˈdeɪt stæmp / (BrE)
to mark or stamp something with the current date or a specific date, typically to record when it was received, sent, or processed

Examples

to date stamp a letter
All incoming mail is date stamped upon receipt.
The librarian will date stamp your book when you check it out.
They date stamped the invoice before filing it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – timestamp: to record the time and/or date of an event digitally or physically
