circumstantial

adjective
detailed and specific; thorough
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝːkəmˈstænʃəl / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstænʃl̩ / (BrE)
Giving full details and including specific information about particulars; thorough and minute in description.

Examples

circumstantial account
His circumstantial description left no detail of the event unexplored.
She submitted a circumstantial report of her findings to the committee.
The journalist wrote a circumstantial account of the trial, including quotes from several witnesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concise: giving much information clearly and in a few words
  • synonym – detailed: including many details, thorough
