perversely

adverb
in a deliberately unreasonable or contrary manner
Pronunciation: / pərˈvɜrsli / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsli / (BrE)
in a way that is deliberately unreasonable, contrary, or goes against what is expected or accepted

Examples

perversely ignore advice
Perversely, she enjoyed doing the exact opposite of what she was told.
He perversely refused to help, even though everyone expected his support.
They acted perversely, challenging every rule simply for the sake of rebellion.
Even when proven wrong, he perversely insisted he was right.
She perversely refused to follow the advice, knowing it was in her best interest.
Some people perversely enjoy doing the opposite of what is expected.
