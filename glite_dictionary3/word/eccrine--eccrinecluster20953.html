eccrine

adjective
producing watery secretion (esp. sweat glands)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkrin / (AmE), / ˈɛkrʌɪn / (BrE)
(of glands) producing a watery secretion without loss of cytoplasm, especially in reference to sweat glands that help regulate body temperature.

Examples

eccrine sweat glands
Eccrine sweat glands are distributed all over the body and play a crucial role in thermoregulation.
Excessive eccrine activity can lead to conditions such as hyperhidrosis.
The clear fluid produced by eccrine glands differs from the thicker secretion of apocrine glands.

Related Terms

  • antonym – apocrine: glands that release secretions by losing part of the secreting cell
