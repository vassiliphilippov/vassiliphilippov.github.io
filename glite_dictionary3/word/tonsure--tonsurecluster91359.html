to tonsure

verb
shave head as religious rite
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːnʃər / (AmE), / ˈtɒnʃə / (BrE)
to shave all or part of the hair on the scalp, especially as a religious rite for monks or priests at induction

Examples

to tonsure a monk
At the ceremony, the abbot will tonsure the new monks as a sign of their devotion.
It is customary to tonsure candidates during their induction into the religious order.
The young novice was tonsured before taking his vows at the monastery.
