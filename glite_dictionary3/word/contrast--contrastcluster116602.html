contrast

noun
visual effect from juxtaposed colors
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnˌtræst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːst / (BrE)
the degree of visual difference between colors or between the lightest and darkest parts of an image, affecting how clear or striking it looks

Examples

high contrast image
High contrast between the background and text improves readability on screens.
Photographers often adjust the contrast in images to make colors more vivid.
The artist used a strong contrast of red and green to catch the viewer's attention.
If the contrast on your television is too high, the colors may appear unnatural.
Increasing the contrast helped reveal the details in the shadows of the old photograph.
Photographers often adjust the contrast to make black-and-white images look more dramatic.

  • degree higher – contrast ratio: the ratio of the luminance of the brightest color to that of the darkest color displayed on a screen
  • antonym – similarity: the quality of being similar; resemblance
