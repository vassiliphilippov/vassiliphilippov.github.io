Job

proper noun
biblical figure of patience and suffering
Pronunciation: / dʒoʊb / (AmE), / dʒəʊb / (BrE)
a figure in the Old Testament who remained faithful to God despite severe suffering and trials, often cited as a symbol of patience and endurance.

Her suffering made her feel like Job, enduring one hardship after another.
Many people admire Job for his unwavering belief during difficult times.
The story of Job is often referenced as a lesson in patience and faith.
