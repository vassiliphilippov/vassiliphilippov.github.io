cuttle

noun
a type of marine cephalopod
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtəl / (BrE)
A ten-armed, oval-bodied marine cephalopod with narrow fins running along the body and a large internal calcareous shell.

Examples

cuttle swimming underwater
Divers were excited to spot a rare cuttle in the shallow waters.
Shell collectors often seek the calcareous bone of the cuttle.
The cuttle uses its ink to escape from predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym – octopus: a marine cephalopod with eight arms
  • synonym – squid: a cephalopod with a long, slender body and ten arms
