bully

noun
hired thug or ruffian (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
a person who intimidates, threatens, or harms others, especially those who are weaker (sometimes used for a hired thug in the past)

Examples

school bully
In the 18th century, it was not uncommon for a bully to be paid handsomely for his services.
Local politicians sometimes employed a bully to silence their critics in the streets.
The wealthy merchant kept a bully nearby to discourage competitors from interfering with his business.
Many people remember being targeted by a bully during their childhood.
She stood up to the bully and refused to let him intimidate her.
The bully at school made several students feel unsafe in the hallway.

Related Terms

  • synonym – persecutor: one who harasses or mistreats others persistently
  • antonym – protector: person who defends others from harm
  • synonym – thug: a violent person, especially a criminal hired to intimidate or attack others
  • synonym – tormentor: someone who inflicts pain or suffering on others
  • antonym – victim: person harmed or bullied by others
