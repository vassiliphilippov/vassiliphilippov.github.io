convertible

noun
security exchangeable for another, esp. in finance
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːtɪbəl / (BrE)
a corporate security, such as a bond or preferred stock, that can be exchanged for another form of security, typically common stock

Examples

convertible bond investment
Many investors prefer convertibles because they offer the potential for conversion to common stock if the company's fortunes improve.
The company issued a new series of convertibles to attract investment from shareholders.
The value of the convertible increased after the corporation's stock price surged.
