to dimple

verb
make a small indentation or depression
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪmpəl / (BrE)
to make a small depression or indentation on the surface of something, or cause it to be marked as if with dimples

Examples

to dimple metal
Her laughter dimpled her cheeks, making her smile even more charming.
Raindrops dimple the surface of the lake, creating tiny circular ripples.
The artist's brush lightly dimpled the canvas to add texture to the painting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – indent: to form a deep recess or notch in a surface
  • antonym – smooth: having an even and regular surface; opposite of being marked with dimples
