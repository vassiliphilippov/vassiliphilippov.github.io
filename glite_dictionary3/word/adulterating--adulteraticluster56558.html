adulterating

adjective
making impure by adding other substances
Pronunciation: / əˈdʌltɚˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈdʌltəˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making something impure or less valuable by adding foreign or inferior substances

Examples

adulterating food products
Strict regulations prevent adulterating practices in the pharmaceutical industry.
The adulterating chemicals reduced the quality of the food product.
There was concern about the adulterating influence of synthetic additives in natural foods.

  • antonym – purifying: removing impurities and making pure
