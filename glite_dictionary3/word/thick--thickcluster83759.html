thick

adjective
solid and broad in form (person/body)
Pronunciation: / θɪk / (AmE), / θɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or a part of the body) having a short, solid, and broad form; stocky or heavily built.

Examples

thick arms
He was shorter than his friends but more thick in build.
She admired the puppy’s thick legs, which made it look sturdy and strong.
The weightlifter had thick arms and a powerful chest.

Related Terms

  • antonym – slender: (of a person or part of the body) gracefully thin
  • synonym – stocky: (of a person) broad and sturdily built
