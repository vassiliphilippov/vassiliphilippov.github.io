to hightail

verb
leave in a hurry; run away quickly
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.teɪl / (BrE)
to leave or move away very quickly, especially to escape or retreat

Examples

to hightail it home
After the embarrassing mistake, she hightailed out of the meeting room.
As soon as the alarm went off, the thieves hightailed out of the building.
We'd better hightail it if we want to catch the last bus.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flee: to run away from danger or pursuit
  • antonym – linger: to stay longer; to be slow in leaving
  • synonym – retreat: to withdraw quickly from a position or situation
