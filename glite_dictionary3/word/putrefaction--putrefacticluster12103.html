putrefaction

noun
moral decay or corruption
Pronunciation: / ˌpjuːtrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
(formal) The state of moral corruption or perversion; moral decay and loss of virtue.

Examples

moral putrefaction
Many critics believe that the novel is a reflection on the putrefaction of society's values.
Some historians argue that the putrefaction of ethical standards led to the downfall of the empire.
The dictator's regime brought about the political and moral putrefaction of the nation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corruption: dishonest or immoral behavior
  • synonym – depravity: moral corruption; wickedness
  • antonym – virtue: moral excellence
