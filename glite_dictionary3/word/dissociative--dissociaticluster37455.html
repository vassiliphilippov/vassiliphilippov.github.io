dissociative

noun
drug causing dissociation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsoʊʃiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsiətɪv / (BrE)
a psychoactive drug (or class of drugs) that produces dissociation — a sense of detachment from reality, self, or surroundings (for example, ketamine or PCP).

Examples

dissociative drug
At the festival, several attendees admitted trying a dissociative that left them feeling unreal and detached from their bodies.
Paramedics recognized signs of overdose after the patient had taken a dissociative and lost motor control.
Researchers studied how chronic use of dissociatives affects memory and perception in long-term users.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dissociative anesthetic: a type of drug that produces dissociation and is used medically as an anaesthetic (often cited as a subtype of dissociatives).
