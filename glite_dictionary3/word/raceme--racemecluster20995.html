raceme

noun
elongate flower cluster with flowers opening from base
Pronunciation: / rəˈsiːm / (AmE), / rəˈsiːm / (BrE)
a type of flower cluster with an elongated main stem, in which the flowers at the base open first and each flower is attached by a short stalk

Examples

flower raceme
Botanists use the term raceme to distinguish one type of inflorescence from others.
The cherry tree's raceme bursts into bloom in early spring, creating a beautiful display.
The wisteria's raceme can grow up to a foot in length, producing dozens of purple flowers.
