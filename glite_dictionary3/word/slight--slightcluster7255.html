to slight

verb
to treat with disrespect or indifference
Pronunciation: / slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
to treat someone with disrespect or as if they are unimportant; to ignore or snub.

Examples

to slight a friend
After the argument, she slighted him by refusing to speak to him at the party.
He was careful not to slight anyone during the meeting, greeting each guest warmly.
She felt hurt when her coworkers slighted her contribution to the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignore: to refuse to take notice of
  • synonym – snub: to ignore or spurn disdainfully
