to dissuade

verb
persuade not to do something
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsweɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsweɪd / (BrE)
to persuade someone not to do something; to advise against a course of action

Examples

to dissuade a friend
He tried to dissuade her from quitting her job, but she had made up her mind.
I was unable to dissuade him despite presenting all the risks involved.
Rising costs may dissuade people from buying new cars this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – persuade: to convince someone to do something
