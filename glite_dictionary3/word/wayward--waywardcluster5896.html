wayward

adjective
difficult to control or guide
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪwɚd / (AmE), / ˈweɪwəd / (BrE)
Difficult to control or predict because of unusual or perverse behavior; resistant to guidance or discipline.

Examples

wayward child
Astrid's wayward hair refused to stay in place no matter what products she used.
Despite his wayward tendencies, his friends knew he had a good heart.
The principal found the student's wayward behavior challenging to manage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contrary: opposing or stubbornly uncooperative
  • antonym – obedient: willing to comply with rules or authority
