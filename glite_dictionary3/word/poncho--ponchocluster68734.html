poncho

noun
blanket-like cloak with a head hole
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːn(t)ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.tʃəʊ / (BrE)
A garment made of a large piece of cloth with a hole in the middle for the head, worn as a cloak, especially in South America.

Examples

colorful poncho
She wore a colorful poncho to keep warm on the chilly evening.
The hikers put on their ponchos when it started to rain.
Traditional ponchos are often made of wool and feature bright patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cape: a sleeveless cloak, typically fastening at the neck
  • synonym – cloak: a loose outer garment, often sleeveless
