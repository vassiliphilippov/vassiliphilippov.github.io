synchromesh

noun
system for smooth gear shifting in cars
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkroʊˌmɛʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrəʊˌmɛʃ / (BrE)
an automotive transmission system that allows gears to bring themselves to the same speed before engaging, enabling smooth gear shifts

Examples

synchromesh gearbox
Modern vehicles almost always use synchromesh in their manual transmissions for easier driving.
Older cars without synchromesh required double-clutching to change gears smoothly.
The mechanic explained that the synchromesh was worn out, causing grinding noises during shifts.
