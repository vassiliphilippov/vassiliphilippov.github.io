tarnish

noun
discoloration of a metal surface
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
A dull or discolored film that forms on the surface of a metal caused by oxidation or other chemical reactions, often resulting in loss of luster.

Examples

tarnish on silver
Silver jewelry often develops a tarnish if it is not stored properly.
The antique candlesticks had a layer of tarnish that dulled their shine.
You can remove the tarnish from copper pots with a mixture of lemon juice and salt.

  • synonym – rust: reddish-brown coating on iron or steel from oxidation
