tough

adjective
resistant to cutting or chewing
Pronunciation: / tʌf / (AmE), / tʌf / (BrE)
(of food or material) difficult to cut or chew; not tender.

Examples

tough meat
After cooking too long, the meat turned tough and unappetizing.
The steak was so tough I could hardly chew it.
This bread is tough and stale—let's bake a fresh loaf.

Related Terms

  • antonym – tender: (of food or material) easy to cut or chew; not tough
