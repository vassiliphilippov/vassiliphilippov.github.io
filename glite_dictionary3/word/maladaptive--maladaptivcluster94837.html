maladaptive

adjective
not adapting in a healthy or helpful way
Pronunciation: / ˌmæl.əˈdæp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈdap.tɪv / (BrE)
not adjusting or adapting in a healthy or appropriate way; tending to be harmful rather than helpful in response to the environment or circumstances.

Examples

maladaptive behavior patterns
Avoidance is often seen as a maladaptive response to anxiety-provoking situations.
Her maladaptive coping mechanisms made it hard to deal with stress effectively.
Some behaviors that are helpful in the short term can become maladaptive over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – adaptive: able to adjust or modify to suit new conditions
