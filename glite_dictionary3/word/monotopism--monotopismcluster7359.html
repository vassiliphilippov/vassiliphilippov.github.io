monotopism

noun
single-point origin in biology
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈtoʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈtəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
The origin of a systematic group in biology occurring only once, or at a single geographic location, often due to mutation.
