narrow

adjective
limited in extent or scope
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ /; / ˈnɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
limited or restricted in extent, amount, scope, or outlook; involving only a small difference or margin

Examples

narrow view
He has only a narrow understanding of the problem.
In the narrow sense, the law applies only to these specific cases.
The committee took a narrow approach to the issue, missing key factors.
He won the election by a narrow margin of only two votes.
It was a narrow escape—she nearly missed the falling branch.
The team lost after a narrow defeat in the finals.
A narrow perspective can limit opportunities for growth and understanding.
His narrow attitude made it difficult for him to accept new ideas.
She became frustrated with the narrow views expressed during the debate.
