verb
being forced out in a narrow stream
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɝːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) being forced out suddenly in a narrow stream, often with some force.

Examples

squirting water out

Oil was squirting from the broken pipe, making a mess on the ground.
The bottle was squirting water all over the kitchen floor when I dropped it.
