cofactor

noun
substance essential for enzyme activity
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌfæktə / (BrE)
A substance, such as a coenzyme, whose presence is essential for the activity of an enzyme.

Examples

cofactor for an enzyme
Most enzymes require a cofactor to become active and catalyze their specific reactions.
The removal of the cofactor can render the enzyme inactive.
Vitamin B6 acts as an important cofactor in many metabolic processes.

  • synonym – coenzyme: an organic molecule that acts as a cofactor
