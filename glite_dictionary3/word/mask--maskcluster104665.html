mask

noun
binary or image selection pattern
Pronunciation: / mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
a pattern of bits or pixels used to select, hide, or protect parts of data (for example a bitmask or image alpha mask).

Examples

apply a mask to data
The programmer applied a mask to ignore the alpha channel during the compositing step.
To extract the lowest byte, set a mask of ones for the lower eight bits of the value.
The photo editor painted a mask to protect the subject while adjusting the background color.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bitmask: a binary pattern used to select or modify specific bits within a data value
  • synonym – filter: a process or pattern that selects or removes parts of data
