to change

verb
put on different clothes
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to put on different clothes or to remove and replace coverings such as clothes, diapers, or bed linen

Examples

to change clothes
He usually changes clothes as soon as he gets home from the gym.
She quickly changed into jeans after school.
You should change before dinner if you've just come from work.
After the spill, they had to change the tablecloth before dinner.
I always change the bed sheets every week for a fresh feeling.
She asked her husband to change the baby's diaper during the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dress: to put clothes on oneself
  • synonym – replace: to put something new in the place of something old
  • synonym – strip: to remove all covering from something
  • antonym – undress: to take one's clothes off
