ineffaceability

noun
quality of being impossible to erase or remove
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪˌfeɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˌfeɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible or extremely difficult to erase, remove, or wipe away.

Examples

ineffaceability of memories
She was haunted by the ineffaceability of a mistake she could never fully erase from her past.
Some artists prize the ineffaceability of certain pigments due to their lasting impact on a canvas.
The ineffaceability of those childhood memories means they continue to shape his outlook on life.
