to hanker

verb
desire strongly or persistently
Pronunciation: / ˈhæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈhæŋkə / (BrE)
to have a strong or persistent desire for something; to long for

Examples

to hanker for chocolate
Even after years abroad, she still hankers for the taste of home cooking.
He hankered for adventure, dreaming of traveling around the world.
Many city dwellers hanker after a quieter, simpler life in the countryside.

  • synonym – yearn: to have a strong desire or longing for something
