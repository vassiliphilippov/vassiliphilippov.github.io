glomerule

noun
a compact cluster of flowers (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɑːmɚˌjuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒm.əˌjuːl / (BrE)
A compact, generally rounded cluster of flowers in which the stalks are short or almost absent, forming a sessile cyme.

Examples

glomerule of flowers
Several species in the genus produce a distinctive glomerule of tiny white flowers.
The botany textbook illustrated the development of a glomerule as a form of inflorescence.
Within the glomerule, new blossoms continue to emerge in a tight cluster.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cyme: a type of flower cluster with a central stem bearing a single terminal flower that develops first
