scyphozoan

noun
marine animal; true jellyfish class
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪfəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌsɪfəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
a marine coelenterate of the class Scyphozoa, typically having a jellylike, free-swimming medusa as the dominant stage in its life cycle; a true jellyfish.

Examples

scyphozoan jellyfish
Marine biologists studied several species of scyphozoans collected from the Mediterranean Sea.
The scyphozoan drifts gracefully through the ocean currents, its body translucent and bell-shaped.
Unlike some other jellyfish, a scyphozoan spends most of its life in the medusa stage.
