to elegize

verb
compose an elegy; mourn in verse
Pronunciation: / ˈɛlɪˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to compose or write an elegy, especially as an act of mourning or lamentation

Examples

to elegize a lost friend
He chose to elegize his late friend in a moving poem published in the local paper.
Instead of celebrating the heroes, the speech seemed to elegize their sacrifices with solemn words.
The poet would often elegize lost loved ones through her evocative verses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – eulogize: to praise highly in speech or writing, especially after death
  • synonym – lament: to express grief or sorrow, especially in words or song
