tokenish

adjective
insignificant or purely symbolic
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊkənɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkənɪʃ / (BrE)
done as a mere formality or gesture, and therefore of little real significance; having only symbolic value

Examples

tokenish gesture
His contribution to the project was merely tokenish and had little real impact.
The government's reforms were criticized as tokenish efforts rather than substantive change.
They made a tokenish apology that failed to satisfy anyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nominal: existing in name only; very small or symbolic in amount
  • synonym – token: done as a sign or symbol rather than as something important
