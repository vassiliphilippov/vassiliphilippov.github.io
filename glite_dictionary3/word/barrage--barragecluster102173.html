barrage

noun
an overwhelming outpouring (e.g., of questions)
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
a rapid and overwhelming outpouring or stream of something, especially questions, comments, or criticism

Examples

barrage of questions
He received a barrage of emails following the announcement.
Reporters launched a barrage of questions at the politician after the press conference.
The teacher was met with a barrage of complaints from concerned parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bombardment: a continuous attack or outpouring, especially of questions or complaints
