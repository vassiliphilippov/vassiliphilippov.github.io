to unscramble

verb
make clear or intelligible again
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈskræmbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈskræmbəl / (BrE)
to make something that is confusing, disordered, or mixed up clear or understandable again

Examples

to unscramble a message
After the storm, engineers had to unscramble the network signals for TV broadcasts.
It took her several minutes to unscramble the encrypted text file.
The detective worked hard to unscramble the clues and solve the mystery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decode: to convert a coded message into ordinary language
  • antonym – scramble: to mix up or make difficult to understand
