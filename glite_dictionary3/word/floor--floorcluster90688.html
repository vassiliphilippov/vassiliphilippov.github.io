floor

noun
minimum allowable level or value
Pronunciation: / flɔr / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
the minimum level or value that something can reach, especially an officially set lower limit

Examples

price floor
Many countries have established a floor on minimum wage to protect workers.
The agreement includes a floor price, ensuring that the commodity cannot be sold below a certain amount.
The central bank set a floor for interest rates to prevent them from falling too low.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ceiling: the maximum level or value that something can reach, especially an officially set upper limit
