brainwashing

noun
forcible indoctrination of beliefs or attitudes
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪnwɑʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɒʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of forcibly changing a person's beliefs or attitudes, often through pressure, manipulation, or indoctrination

Examples

brainwashing of prisoners
He claimed he was a victim of brainwashing during his captivity.
Some cults use brainwashing techniques to control their members' thoughts and behaviors.
The documentary explores the role of brainwashing in totalitarian regimes.
