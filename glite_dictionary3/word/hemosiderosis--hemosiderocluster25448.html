hemosiderosis

noun
excessive tissue iron deposition
Pronunciation: / ˌhimoʊˌsɪdəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˌsɪdəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the abnormal accumulation of hemosiderin (an iron-storage complex) in tissues, often as a result of bleeding or disorders such as thalassemia or hemochromatosis.

Examples

hemosiderosis in organs
Chronic bleeding disorders can lead to organ damage through hemosiderosis.
Doctors monitored him closely for signs of hemosiderosis after diagnosing thalassemia.
The patient's liver biopsy revealed extensive hemosiderosis due to repeated blood transfusions.
