OPV

noun
oral polio vaccine
Pronunciation: / oʊ pi ˈvi / (AmE), / əʊ piː ˈviː / (BrE)
an oral vaccine containing live but weakened poliovirus, given to prevent poliomyelitis (polio)

Examples

OPV drops for children
A bottle of OPV must be kept refrigerated to maintain its effectiveness.
Doctors recommend that every child receive multiple doses of the OPV for full protection.
The government conducted an OPV campaign to eradicate polio in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • antonym – IPV: inactivated polio vaccine, given by injection
  • synonym – vaccine: a substance that provides immunity to a specific disease
