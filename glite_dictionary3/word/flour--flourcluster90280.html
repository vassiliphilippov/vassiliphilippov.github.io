flour

noun
powdered grain used in cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
A fine powder made by grinding grain, especially wheat, and used to make bread, cakes, and other foods.

Examples

white flour bag
A dusting of flour covered the kitchen counter after baking.
Many breads are made from wheat flour.
She sifted the flour before adding it to the cake mixture.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – meal: coarse ground grain, less finely ground than flour
  • degree lower – wholemeal flour: flour made from the whole grain, less refined
