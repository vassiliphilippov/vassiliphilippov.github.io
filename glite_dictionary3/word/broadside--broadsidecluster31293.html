broadside

noun
large printed advertisement or announcement
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔd.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.saɪd / (BrE)
A large sheet of paper printed on one side, often used for advertisements, announcements, or proclamations, and distributed widely.

Examples

broadside poster printed
A vintage broadside advertising a circus was found among her grandfather's belongings.
The town crier posted a broadside announcing the new tax law in the village square.
They distributed a broadside to every household to promote the upcoming festival.
