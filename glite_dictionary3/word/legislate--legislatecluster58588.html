to legislate

verb
make or enact laws
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪt / (BrE)
to make or enact laws, or to bring something into effect by making laws

Examples

to legislate new laws
Congress may legislate stricter regulations on environmental pollution.
It can be difficult to legislate changes in social behavior.
The government plans to legislate new safety standards for all workplaces.
