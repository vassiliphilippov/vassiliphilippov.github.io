to extend

verb
offer or express formally
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnd / (BrE)
to offer or make something available, such as greetings, thanks, services, or opportunities

Examples

to extend an invitation
I would like to extend my congratulations to all the competition winners.
Please extend our best wishes to your family.
The president extended his apologies for not attending the meeting.
The company decided to extend free health insurance to part-time employees.
The library will extend its opening hours during exam week.
We are pleased to extend this invitation to all our valued clients.

Related Terms

  • synonym – offer: to present or express something for acceptance
  • antonym – withhold: to refuse to give something that is wanted or needed
