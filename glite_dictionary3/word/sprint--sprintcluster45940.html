sprint

noun
time-boxed work period (Agile)
Pronunciation: / sprɪnt / (AmE), / sprɪnt / (BrE)
(in Agile software development and project management) a short, fixed period of time (usually one to four weeks) during which a team works to complete a set of planned tasks or backlog items.

Examples

a sprint planning meeting
During the two-week sprint, developers focused on fixing bugs and improving performance.
The team completed the feature during the next sprint in our agile cycle.
Product managers plan which backlog items to include in the next sprint.

Related Terms

  • synonym – iteration: a repeated development cycle in Agile; often used interchangeably with 'sprint'.
  • synonym – time-box: a fixed period during which a particular task or set of tasks must be completed.
