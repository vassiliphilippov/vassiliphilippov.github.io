collaborationism

noun
cooperation with an occupying enemy
Pronunciation: / kəˌlæbəˈreɪʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of cooperating with an enemy occupying one's country, typically viewed as traitorous

Examples

collaborationism with invaders
He was tried for collaborationism, having openly aided the enemy.
Many in postwar France were accused of collaborationism during the German occupation.
The government's official policy condemned all forms of collaborationism during wartime.
