piping

adverb
extremely (of heat); very hot
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(adverb, usually in 'piping hot') to a very great or extreme degree (of heat); extremely.

Examples

piping hot soup
Be careful—the soup is piping hot and might burn your mouth.
She served the coffee piping hot, just the way he liked it.
The bread came out of the oven piping hot and smelled delicious.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lukewarm: moderately warm; not hot
  • synonym – scalding: extremely hot; able to burn
