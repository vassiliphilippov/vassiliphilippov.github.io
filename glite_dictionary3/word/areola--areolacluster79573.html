areola

noun
small space in tissue or body part
Pronunciation: / əˈriːələ / (AmE), / əˈriːələ / (BrE)
a small space or area in a tissue or body part, such as the region between veins on a leaf or on an insect's wing

Examples

areola between veins
Each leaf showed a distinct areola bordered by its veins.
Insects such as dragonflies have a transparent areola on their wings.
The areola between two veins often contains specialized cells.
