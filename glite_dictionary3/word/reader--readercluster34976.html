reader

noun
person who assesses manuscripts for publishing
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdər /; / ˈriːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to read and assess or correct written material, such as manuscripts or proofs

Examples

reader checks manuscripts
Before the book reached the editor, it was first assessed by an in-house reader.
She worked as a reader for a major literary agency, reviewing dozens of submissions each month.
The publishing house hired an experienced reader to evaluate unsolicited manuscripts.
His job as a reader required exceptional attention to detail and a strong command of the language.
She worked as a reader at the publishing house, reviewing manuscripts for spelling and grammar mistakes.
The reader found several typographical errors that the author had missed.
