stem-winder

noun
watch wound by turning a knob
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛmˌwaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈstɛmˌwaɪndə / (BrE)
A watch that is wound by turning a knob at the end of the stem, rather than using a key.

Examples

old stem-winder watch
Before wristwatches became popular, a stem-winder was considered a modern invention.
His grandfather's old stem-winder still keeps perfect time after all these years.
The museum displayed a collection of antique stem-winders alongside pocket watches.
