to beat

verb
to sail a boat by tacking against the wind
Pronunciation: / bit / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
(of a boat) to sail with repeated tacking, especially against the wind or with difficulty.

Examples

to beat against wind
The yacht beat for hours before reaching calm waters.
They beat slowly up the coast, making progress with every tack.
We had to beat all afternoon to make any headway in the gale.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tack: to change a sailing boat's course by turning its bow through the wind
