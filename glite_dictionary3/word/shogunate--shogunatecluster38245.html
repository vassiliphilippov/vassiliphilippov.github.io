shogunate

noun
government ruled by a shogun
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊɡəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈʃɒɡjʊnət / (BrE)
A government or period in Japanese history ruled by a shogun, typically characterized by military dictatorship.

Examples

Tokugawa shogunate period
During the Edo period, the Tokugawa shogunate maintained strict control over Japan.
Historians often compare the shogunate to European feudal systems.
The rise of the shogunate marked a shift from imperial to military rule in Japan.
