surplus

adjective
more than needed; extra; excess
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.pləs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.pləs / (BrE)
more than what is needed, desired, or required; extra or excess

Examples

surplus goods available
After the harvest, there was a surplus of wheat in the market.
She donated the surplus food to a local charity.
The company sold off its surplus inventory at a discount.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deficit: the amount by which something is too small or less than needed
  • synonym – excess: an amount that is more than necessary, permitted, or desired
