catena

noun
series or chain of connected items or comments
Pronunciation: / kəˈteɪnə / (AmE), / kəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A connected series or chain, especially a series of commentary or explanation in which each part is closely linked to the next, often used of patristic commentaries on Christian doctrine.

Examples

a catena of commentaries
A catena of biblical commentaries provided insight into how early Christians understood the text.
She traced the catena of arguments back to the original Greek philosophers.
The manuscript contains a catena of theological interpretations spanning several centuries.
