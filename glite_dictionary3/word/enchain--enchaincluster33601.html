to enchain

verb
restrain or bind with chains
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtʃeɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈtʃeɪn / (BrE)
to restrain or bind with chains, either literally or figuratively

Examples

to enchain a prisoner
Centuries of tradition can enchain the minds of a people.
He sought to enchain the prisoner to prevent escape.
Her heart seemed to enchain him with its mysterious power.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shackle: to restrain with shackles or chains
  • antonym – unchain: to release from chains
