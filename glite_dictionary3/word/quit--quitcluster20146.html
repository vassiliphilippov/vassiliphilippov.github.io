to quit

verb
to stop trying or give up
Pronunciation: / kwɪt / (AmE), / kwɪt / (BrE)
to stop trying or accept defeat, especially when faced with difficulty or hopelessness

Examples

to quit a game
After losing the first set badly, he refused to quit and fought back.
Many people quit when faced with failure, but she kept going.
Though the task was tough, Sam would not quit until he succeeded.

Related Terms

  • antonym – persevere: to continue attempting despite difficulties
