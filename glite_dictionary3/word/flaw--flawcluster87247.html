to flaw

verb
make imperfect by adding a defect
Pronunciation: / flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
to make imperfect or less effective by introducing a fault or defect

Examples

to flaw a plan
One careless remark can flaw an otherwise perfect presentation.
Rough handling during delivery flawed the surface of the table.
The discovery of a calculation error flawed the entire experiment.
