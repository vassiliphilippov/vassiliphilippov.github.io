vanguard

noun
the leading position in a movement or field
Pronunciation: / ˈvænˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈvænɡɑːd /; / ˈvanˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
the people or group at the forefront of new developments or ideas, especially in the arts or a movement

Examples

the vanguard of change
Artists at the vanguard of the movement challenged traditional forms of expression.
Her innovative research placed her at the vanguard of cancer treatment.
The company has always been in the vanguard of technological advancements.
As part of the vanguard, she introduced experimental techniques to the mainstream art world.
That theatre company has always been in the vanguard of political drama.
The gallery showcased the latest works by artists at the vanguard of contemporary sculpture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – avant-garde: new and experimental ideas and methods in art, music, or literature
  • synonym – forefront: the leading or most important position or place
  • antonym – rearguard: the soldiers positioned at the back of an army or movement; the opposite of vanguard
