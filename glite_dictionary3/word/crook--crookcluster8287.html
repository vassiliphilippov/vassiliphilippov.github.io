crook

noun
shepherd's staff with a hooked end
Pronunciation: / krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a long staff with a hooked end, typically used by shepherds to manage sheep

Examples

a shepherd's crook
She carried a tall crook as part of her costume in the Christmas play.
The old crook had been passed down through generations of herders.
The shepherd leaned on his crook while watching over the flock.
