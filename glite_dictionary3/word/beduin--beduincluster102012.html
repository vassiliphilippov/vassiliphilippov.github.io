Beduin

noun
member of a nomadic Arab tribe
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛduˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɛdjuːɪn / (BrE)
a member of a traditionally nomadic Arab tribe, especially from the deserts of North Africa, Arabia, or the Middle East

Examples

a Beduin tribe
A group of Beduin set up their tents near the oasis for the night.
Many Beduin have adapted to modern life, though some still follow traditional nomadic ways.
The Beduin are known for their rich oral poetry and hospitality traditions.
