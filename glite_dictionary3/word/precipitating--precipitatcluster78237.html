precipitating

adjective
causing something sudden to happen
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪpɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing something to happen suddenly or unexpectedly, especially an event, action, or change.

Examples

precipitating cause
A sudden drop in temperature acted as the precipitating cause of the thunderstorm.
Financial instability was the precipitating factor in the company’s collapse.
The assassination was the precipitating event that led to the war.
