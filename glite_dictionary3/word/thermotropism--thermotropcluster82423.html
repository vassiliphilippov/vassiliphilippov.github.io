thermotropism

noun
movement/growth in response to temperature
Pronunciation: / ˌθɝːmoʊˈtroʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈtrəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
the movement or growth of an organism in response to a change in temperature, especially warmth

Examples

thermotropism in plants
Scientists observed thermotropism in the plant's roots, which grew away from the heated soil.
Some plants exhibit thermotropism, bending towards a source of warmth during colder months.
The study focused on the thermotropism of certain bacteria in response to temperature gradients.
