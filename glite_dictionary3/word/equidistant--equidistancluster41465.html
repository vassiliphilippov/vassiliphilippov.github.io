equidistant

adjective
at equal distances from a point or between objects
Pronunciation: / ˌikwɪˈdɪstənt / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈdɪstənt / (BrE)
(of two or more things) at the same distance from a particular point or from each other at every point.

Examples

equidistant from the center
All points on a circle are equidistant from the center.
The hotel is equidistant from the airport and the city center, making it a convenient location for travelers.
We chose a meeting spot that was equidistant from both our houses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parallel: (of lines) being always the same distance apart; never meeting
