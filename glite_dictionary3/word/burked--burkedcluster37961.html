burked

adjective
suppressed or stifled quietly
Pronunciation: / bɜrkt / (AmE), / bɜːkt / (BrE)
Suppressed or prevented quietly or indirectly; stifled without open action.

Examples

burked criticism
Any criticism of the policy was swiftly burked by management.
Concerns about the project were quietly burked by the administration.
The committee burked the proposal before it could be discussed publicly.
