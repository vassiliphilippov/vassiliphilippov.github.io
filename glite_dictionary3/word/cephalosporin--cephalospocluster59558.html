cephalosporin

noun
class of broad-spectrum antibiotics
Pronunciation: / sɛfələˈspɔrɪn / (AmE), / sɛfələˈspɔːrɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of broad-spectrum antibiotics derived from fungi, structurally related to penicillin, and used to treat bacterial infections

Examples

cephalosporin antibiotic treatment
Allergic reactions to cephalosporin antibiotics are rare, but they can occur in some individuals.
Later generations of cephalosporin antibiotics are effective against a wider range of bacteria.
The doctor prescribed a cephalosporin to treat the patient's bacterial infection.
