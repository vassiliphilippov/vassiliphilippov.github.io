vestrywoman

noun
female member of a church's vestry
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛstriˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈvɛstriˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is a member of the governing body (vestry) of a church, especially in the Anglican tradition.

Examples

vestrywoman on church council
As the first vestrywoman in her parish, she brought new perspectives to the church council.
The vestrywoman suggested organizing a fundraiser for the local food bank.
Three vestrywomen were elected to oversee the refurbishment of the church building.
