to scuttle

verb
move quickly in a hurried manner
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈskʌt.l̩ / (BrE)
to move quickly with short, hurried steps, often with the suggestion of being unnoticed or afraid

Examples

to scuttle away
A mouse scuttled along the baseboard and disappeared into a hole.
As soon as the lights came on, the insects scuttled into the cracks.
The children scuttled across the playground when the bell rang.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dawdle: to move or act too slowly
  • synonym – scurry: to move quickly and with short steps, especially because you are frightened or trying to get away
