to cause

verb
make someone do something
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔz /; / kɔz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːz /; / kɔːz / (BrE)
to make something happen or bring about an effect

Examples

to cause a problem
Her advice caused me to reconsider my decision.
Stress at work caused him to seek professional help last year.
The new security policy caused employees to change their passwords regularly.
Her sudden outburst caused everyone in the room to fall silent.
The leaking pipe caused serious water damage in the basement.
Traffic delays were caused by heavy snowfall this morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bring about: to make something happen
  • synonym – force: to make someone do something against their will
  • synonym – induce: to persuade or influence someone to do something
  • antonym – prevent: to stop something from happening
  • synonym – result in: to lead to a particular result
