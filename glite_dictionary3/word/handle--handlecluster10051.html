to handle

verb
deal with or manage (something)
Pronunciation: / ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhændl /; / ˈhændl̩ / (BrE)
to be responsible for, manage, or deal with a task, situation, or person

Examples

to handle a problem
Let me handle the arrangements for the meeting tomorrow.
She was promoted because she could handle difficult clients with professionalism.
The new software can't handle large amounts of data efficiently.
She wasn't sure how to handle her new responsibilities at work.
Teachers must handle disruptive students with patience and care.
The manager tried to handle the situation diplomatically to avoid conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deal with: to take action in order to solve a problem or task
  • synonym – manage: to be in charge of or direct
  • antonym – mishandle: to deal with something or someone badly or ineffectively
  • antonym – neglect: to fail to care for or attend to something
  • synonym – treat: to behave towards or deal with someone in a particular way
