to review

verb
go over material to refresh memory
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvju / (AmE), / rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
to go over or study material again in order to refresh one's memory, or to look at something again to check details or form an opinion

Examples

to review your notes
Before the big exam, I always review my notes to make sure I remember everything.
She reviewed the key points of the lecture to prepare for the quiz.
You should review the safety procedures regularly to keep them fresh in your mind.
He will review the security footage to check for any suspicious activity.
Let's review the agenda before starting the meeting.
The teacher asked the students to review their essays for any errors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to pay no attention to something
  • synonym – reexamine: to examine something again
  • synonym – revise: to read or go over material again, especially for examination purposes (British English)
