restrictiveness

noun
quality of being restrictive
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪktɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of limiting freedom or preventing certain actions or behaviors

Examples

parental restrictiveness
Some parents worry that too much restrictiveness can stifle their children's creativity.
Teachers debated the restrictiveness of the school dress code in a faculty meeting.
The restrictiveness of the new company policies frustrated many employees.

Related Terms

  • antonym – permissiveness: the quality of allowing behavior with few restrictions
  • synonym – strictness: the quality of being very demanding or rigid in rules or requirements
