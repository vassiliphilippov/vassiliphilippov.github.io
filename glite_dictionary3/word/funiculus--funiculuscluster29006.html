funiculus

noun
stalk attaching ovule/seed in plants
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɪkjələs / (AmE), / fjʊˈnɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
the slender stalk attaching an ovule or seed to the ovary wall in plants

Examples

seed funiculus
Botanists observed that the funiculus was unusually thick in this species of flower.
If the funiculus is damaged, the seed may not receive enough nourishment to grow.
The funiculus supplies the developing seed with nutrients from the parent plant.
