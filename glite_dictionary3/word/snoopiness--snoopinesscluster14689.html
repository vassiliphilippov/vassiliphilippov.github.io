snoopiness

noun
intrusive inquisitiveness
Pronunciation: / ˈsnupɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈsnuːpɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being offensively or intrusively inquisitive; a tendency to pry into other people's affairs

Examples

snoopiness about secrets
He was criticized for the snoopiness with which he questioned his neighbors about their private lives.
Her constant snoopiness made everyone in the office uncomfortable.
People tend to resent snoopiness in their colleagues or friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym – curiosity: a desire to know or learn; inquisitiveness
  • antonym – discretion: the quality of behaving in a way that avoids causing offense or revealing private information
