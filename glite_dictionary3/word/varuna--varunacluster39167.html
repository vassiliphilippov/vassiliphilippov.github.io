Varuna

proper noun
Kuiper belt object (TNO)
Pronunciation: / vəˈruːnə / (AmE), / vəˈruːnə / (BrE)
(astronomy) a trans‑Neptunian/Kuiper belt object discovered in 2000, named after the Vedic god Varuna.

Examples

Varuna distant object
Astronomers imaged Varuna in 2000 when mapping distant objects beyond Neptune in the Kuiper belt.

In the survey, astronomers tentatively classified Varuna as a trans‑Neptunian object requiring further observation.
