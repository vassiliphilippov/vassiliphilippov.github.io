aphasic

noun
person with aphasia
Pronunciation: / əˈfeɪsɪk / (AmE), / əˈfeɪsɪk / (BrE)
a person affected by aphasia, the impairment or loss of the ability to understand or use language

Examples

an aphasic patient
After her stroke, she became aphasic, unable to express her thoughts clearly.
Many aphasics experience frustration because they struggle to find the right words.
The therapist worked closely with the aphasic to improve his communication skills.
