injector

noun
device that uses fluid to move another fluid
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɛktər /; / ɪnˈdʒɛktɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
a device that forces a fluid or particles into another substance or system (such as in engines, boilers, or laboratory equipment)

Examples

injector nozzle
A vacuum injector can be used to remove air from laboratory setups.
Modern jet engines use a fuel injector to deliver fuel to the combustion chamber.
The steam injector was invented to efficiently feed water into boilers.
A malfunctioning fuel injector can cause a car engine to run poorly.
Scientists calibrated the injector to introduce particles into the accelerator at precise speeds.
The injector on the steam engine needed to be replaced after years of heavy use.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aspirator: device for creating suction, often by jet action
  • synonym – ejector: device that expels a substance using a jet of fluid
  • synonym – pump: a machine for moving fluids, similar in function to some injectors
  • synonym – syringe: a device used for injecting fluids, especially in medical contexts
