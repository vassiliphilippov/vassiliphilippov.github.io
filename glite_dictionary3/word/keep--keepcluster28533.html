to keep

verb
prevent (from doing something)
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiːp / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from doing something or entering a particular state; to restrain

Examples

to keep from crying
He tried hard to keep his voice from trembling during the speech.
Please keep your dog from jumping on the furniture.
The locked gate keeps children from running into the road.
Security will keep everyone in the building until it is safe.
She kept him in her office for further questioning.
The teacher decided to keep the class inside during the storm.
Despite the bad news, he managed to keep his emotions under control.
He tried to keep his anger from showing during the meeting.
She could barely keep her excitement hidden.
