to waste

verb
(of liquid) to flow away or be lost
Pronunciation: / weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
(of liquid or substance) to flow away or be lost, especially as something no longer useful or needed.

Examples

waste water flows
During the storm, much of the water would waste back into the sea.
If the pipe is not fixed, the coolant will continue to waste onto the ground.
Without proper containment, the chemicals could waste into the surrounding environment.
