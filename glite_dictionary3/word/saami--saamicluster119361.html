Saami

noun
member of a northern Scandinavian indigenous people
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmi / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous nomadic people living in the northern parts of Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia, traditionally known for reindeer herding.

Examples

Saami people
A Saami elder spoke about the importance of preserving their language and culture.
She learned about the customs of the Saami during her trip to Lapland.
The Saami have a long tradition of reindeer herding in the Arctic regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Lapp: an alternative (now often considered offensive) name for a Saami person
