hypocalcaemia

noun
low calcium level in the blood
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˌkælsiˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəʊˌkalsɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which there is an abnormally low level of calcium in the blood, often associated with disorders such as hypoparathyroidism, kidney malfunction, or vitamin D deficiency

Examples

severe hypocalcaemia symptoms
If left untreated, hypocalcaemia can lead to serious complications involving the nerves and heart.
Muscle cramps are a common symptom of hypocalcaemia in individuals with chronic kidney disease.
The patient was diagnosed with hypocalcaemia after blood tests revealed unusually low calcium levels.
