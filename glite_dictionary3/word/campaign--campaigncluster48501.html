to campaign

verb
to take part in military action or war
Pronunciation: / kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
(of soldiers or armies) to take part in a series of military actions or go off to war.

Examples

to campaign in battle
After several months, the general decided not to campaign through the harsh winter.
He campaigned with Napoleon across Europe in the early 19th century.
The army will campaign in the frontier region during the summer.
