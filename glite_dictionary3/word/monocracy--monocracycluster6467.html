monocracy

noun
absolute rule by one person
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / məˈnɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A form of government in which a single person holds absolute power, unrestricted by laws, constitution, or opposition.

Examples

absolute monocracy
Debates raged over whether monocracy could ever ensure fair governance for its citizens.
The country descended into monocracy when the general seized power and dissolved the parliament.
Throughout history, monocracy has often led to abuse of authority and lack of political freedoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – autocracy: government by one person with absolute power
  • antonym – democracy: government by the people or their elected representatives
