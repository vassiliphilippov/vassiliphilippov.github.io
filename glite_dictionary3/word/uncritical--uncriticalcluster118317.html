uncritical

adjective
lacking critical judgment
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkrɪtɪkəl /; / ʌnˈkrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
not judging, questioning, or thinking carefully; accepting things without criticism or analysis

Examples

uncritical acceptance of ideas
His uncritical reading of the report led to several mistakes.
She tends to be uncritical when reviewing her friends' ideas.
The manager gave an uncritical acceptance of the proposal, ignoring potential risks.
He accepted the report in an uncritical manner, without asking any questions.
Many readers gave the book their uncritical praise, overlooking its flaws.
She was an uncritical supporter of the policy, never challenging its assumptions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – critical: judging with careful analysis
  • antonym – discerning: able to judge well
