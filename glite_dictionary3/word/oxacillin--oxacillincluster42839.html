oxacillin

noun
penicillinase-resistant antibiotic
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksəˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌɒksəˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
A semisynthetic form of penicillin that is resistant to penicillinase and used to treat infections caused by penicillin-resistant staphylococci.
