to defang

verb
make harmless or less severe
Pronunciation: / diˈfæŋ /; / dɪˈfæŋ / (AmE), / diˈfæŋ /; / dɪˈfaŋ / (BrE)
to make something (such as a threat, criticism, or opposition) harmless or less powerful; to reduce its impact or effectiveness

Examples

to defang a threat
A carefully worded statement helped to defang the scandal before it could do serious damage.
The company attempted to defang the scandal by releasing all relevant documents.
The politician's team worked quickly to defang the scandal through transparency and accountability.
Quick action by the leadership helped to defang much of the opposition's outrage.
The company attempted to defang the criticism by being more transparent with the public.
They revised the proposal in order to defang any negative feedback from stakeholders.

  • synonym – neutralize: to make something ineffective or harmless
  • antonym – strengthen: to make something stronger or more powerful
  • synonym – weaken: to make something less strong
