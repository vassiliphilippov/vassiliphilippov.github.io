gibbsite

noun
white crystalline aluminum hydroxide mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪbˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪbsaɪt / (BrE)
A white crystalline mineral consisting of aluminum hydroxide, forming a major component of bauxite and used as a source of alumina.

Examples

gibbsite mineral sample
Gibbsite is one of the main minerals extracted from bauxite during aluminum processing.
The refinery uses gibbsite as a raw material to produce high-purity alumina for industry.
Under the microscope, gibbsite crystals often appear as thin plates or flakes.
