idealization

noun
psychological defense mechanism of splitting into good/bad
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdiəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) A defense mechanism in which a person splits an ambivalent object into two representations — one wholly good and one wholly bad.

Examples

idealization in therapy
Frequent idealization and subsequent disappointment often characterize borderline personality disorder.
In therapy, his pattern of idealization and devaluation became a focus of discussion.
The patient’s idealization of her parents made it difficult for her to acknowledge their faults.

Related Terms

  • synonym – splitting: A psychological defense mechanism involving division of an object or person into all good or all bad, often seen in certain personality disorders.
