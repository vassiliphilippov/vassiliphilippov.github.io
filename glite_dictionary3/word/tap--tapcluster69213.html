to tap

verb
walk making a tapping sound
Pronunciation: / tæp / (AmE), / tæp / (BrE)
to dance by making rhythmic tapping sounds with special shoes, or to walk making such sounds

Examples

to tap dance
He tapped into the room, the sound of his heels echoing on the wooden floor.
She tapped lightly across the stage during her dance performance.
The children tapped down the hallway, their shoes making gentle noises on the tiles.
He loves to tap on stage because the rhythms excite the audience.
She learned to tap as a child and soon became an accomplished dancer.
The performers tapped in perfect unison during the grand finale.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clog: to dance by striking the heels, toes, or sides of the shoes
  • synonym – step: to move by lifting your foot and putting it down in another place
  • antonym – stomp: to walk with heavy steps, often making a loud noise
  • synonym – tap dance: a dance in which rhythmic clicking sounds are made with the feet
