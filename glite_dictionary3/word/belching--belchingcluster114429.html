belching

noun
forceful expulsion of something, like gas or smoke
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛltʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛltʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of forcefully expelling something, especially gas or smoke, from inside

Examples

belching smoke clouds
The belching of smoke from the chimneys covered the town in a thick haze.
There was a loud belching of steam as the train pulled into the station.
With every belching of gas, the old engine rattled and coughed.
