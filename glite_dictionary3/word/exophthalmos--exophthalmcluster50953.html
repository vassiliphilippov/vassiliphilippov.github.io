exophthalmos

noun
protrusion of the eyeball
Pronunciation: / ˌekˌsɑfˈθælməs / (AmE), / ˌɛksɒfˈθalmɒs / (BrE)
abnormal protrusion of the eyeball from the socket, often due to disease

Examples

exophthalmos in Graves disease
Exophthalmos is often seen in individuals with Graves' disease.
Early treatment of the underlying cause can sometimes reverse exophthalmos.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with exophthalmos caused by a thyroid disorder.
