shin

noun
cut of meat from the lower leg
Pronunciation: / ʃɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪn / (BrE)
A cut of meat taken from the lower part of the leg of an animal, especially a cow or calf, often used for making stews or soups.

Examples

beef shin
She simmered the shin for hours to make a hearty broth.
The butcher recommended using shin because it becomes tender when slow-cooked.
The recipe calls for two pounds of shin to add rich flavor to the stew.
