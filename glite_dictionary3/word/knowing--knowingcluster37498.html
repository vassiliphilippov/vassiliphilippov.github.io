knowing

adjective
intentional; done deliberately
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
done with full awareness or intention; having or showing understanding, insight, or cleverness

Examples

knowing smile
He gave a knowing nod, fully aware of the consequences.
Her knowing actions showed clear intent to break the rules.
The company made a knowing decision to ignore the complaint.
As a knowing collector of rare books, she easily spotted the forgeries.
She gave a knowing smile as though she understood more than she let on.
The detective's knowing glance revealed he had already solved half the puzzle.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accidental: happening by chance, not planned or intended
  • antonym – ignorant: lacking knowledge, awareness, or information
  • synonym – intentional: done on purpose or with intent
  • synonym – knowledgeable: well informed; having much knowledge
