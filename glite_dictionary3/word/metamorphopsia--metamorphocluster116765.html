metamorphopsia

noun
distorted vision due to retinal defect
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛt̬.ə.mɔːrˈɑp.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌmɛt.ə.mɔːˈfɒp.si.ə / (BrE)
A defect of vision in which objects appear distorted, typically caused by a problem in the retina.

Examples

metamorphopsia after surgery
After surgery, his metamorphopsia gradually improved as the retina healed.
Conditions like macular degeneration can lead to symptoms of metamorphopsia.
The patient reported experiencing metamorphopsia, making straight lines appear wavy.
