SOD

noun
enzyme converting superoxide to hydrogen peroxide and oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˌɛsˌoʊˈdi / (AmE), / ˌɛsˌəʊˈdiː / (BrE)
an enzyme, also known as superoxide dismutase, that catalyzes the conversion of superoxide radicals into hydrogen peroxide and oxygen

Examples

SOD enzyme activity
Mutations affecting SOD levels can be linked to certain neurological disorders.
Researchers studied the activity of SOD in patients with oxidative stress-related diseases.
The presence of SOD helps protect human cells from damage caused by free radicals.
