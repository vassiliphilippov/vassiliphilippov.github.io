autoecious

adjective
completing life cycle on one host
Pronunciation: / ɔˈtoʊʃəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtiːʃəs / (BrE)
(of a parasite, especially a rust fungus) completing its entire life cycle on a single host species.

Examples

autoecious rust fungus
Researchers studied several autoecious species to understand how host specificity affects parasite evolution.
Some autoecious fungi can severely damage their host plants during every stage of their life cycle.
The autoecious nature of this rust fungus means it does not need an alternate host to reproduce.

Related Terms

  • antonym – heteroecious: (of a parasite) requiring two different host species to complete its life cycle
