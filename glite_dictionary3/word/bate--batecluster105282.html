to bate

verb
(of falcon) beat wings wildly
Pronunciation: / beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
(of a falcon) to beat the wings wildly or frantically, especially when restrained.

Examples

bate wings wildly
During training, young birds will often bate before learning to settle.
The falcon began to bate its wings as soon as it felt the leather jesses tighten.
When startled, a hawk may bate frantically, struggling to fly from the handler's glove.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flutter: (of a bird) to flap or vibrate the wings quickly and lightly
