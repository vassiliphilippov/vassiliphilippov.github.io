caul

noun
membrane covering intestines
Pronunciation: / kɔl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
a thin membrane, especially the part of the peritoneum that attaches to the stomach and colon and covers the intestines

Examples

caul covering intestines
Damage to the caul can lead to complications in abdominal surgery.
In rare cases, a baby may be born with a caul covering its head or face.
The surgeon carefully lifted the caul to access the underlying organs.
