to discompose

verb
to disturb composure; unsettle
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to disturb or upset the composure of someone; to make someone feel uneasy or unsettled

Examples

to discompose a calm person
Even the loud crash failed to discompose the experienced surgeon during the operation.
She tried not to let the rude comment discompose her in front of the audience.
The sudden news seemed to discompose him, and he sat down in silence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compose: to calm or settle (oneself or someone)
  • synonym – unsettle: to make someone feel anxious or uneasy
