to scuff

verb
poke or scrape with the foot
Pronunciation: / skʌf / (AmE), / skʌf / (BrE)
to move, poke, or drag something with your foot, or to walk by sliding or dragging your feet

Examples

to scuff your foot
He scuffed the loose stone with his shoe as he waited nervously.
Please don’t scuff the door with your boots when you come in.
She absentmindedly scuffed at the dirt while talking on the phone.
He tends to scuff when he is tired, making a soft shuffling noise across the carpet.
She scuffed through the fallen leaves, enjoying the crisp sound as she dragged her feet.
The children scuffed along the sidewalk, not eager to get to school.

Related Terms

  • synonym – kick: to strike something with the foot
  • synonym – scrape: to rub an object with something rough or sharp
  • synonym – shuffle: to walk by dragging the feet along the ground without lifting them fully
