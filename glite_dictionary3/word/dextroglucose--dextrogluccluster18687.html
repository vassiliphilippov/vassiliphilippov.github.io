dextroglucose

noun
D-isomer of glucose; a sugar found in fruits and honey
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkstɹoʊˈɡluːkoʊs / (AmE), / ˌdɛkstrəʊˈɡluːkəʊs / (BrE)
An isomer of glucose, specifically the D-form, commonly found in honey and sweet fruits.

Examples

dextroglucose in honey
Dextroglucose is often used in medical settings as an immediate energy source for patients.
Honey contains a significant amount of dextroglucose, contributing to its sweetness.
Many sports drinks are supplemented with dextroglucose to aid in quick energy recovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dextrose: another name for dextroglucose, the D-form of glucose
  • antonym – levoglucose: L-isomer of glucose (the opposite isomer to dextroglucose)
