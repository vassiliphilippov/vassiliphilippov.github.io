skewness

noun
asymmetry or slant; statistical measure of asymmetry
Pronunciation: / ˈskjuːnəs / (AmE), / ˈskjuːnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being asymmetrical or slanted, especially in terms of a lack of balance or evenness; in statistics, a measure of the asymmetry of a probability distribution.

Examples

statistical skewness
In architecture, skewness in a structure can indicate foundational problems.
Researchers measured the skewness of the income distribution to assess inequality.
The skewness of the data suggests that most students scored above the average.
