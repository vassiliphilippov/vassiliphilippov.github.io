to clear

verb
remove impurities or pollution
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
to become or make something free from impurities, cloudiness, or pollution; to make transparent or pure

Examples

to clear water
Technicians worked all night to clear the river of toxins.
The filtration system will clear the water before it reaches your tap.
We need to clear the air of smoke after the fire.
After days of rain, the sky finally cleared and the sun came out.
Once the fog cleared, we could see the mountains in the distance.
The weather is expected to clear by the afternoon, allowing for outdoor activities.
After filtering, the technician managed to clear the solution so it became fully transparent.
The fog began to clear as the sun rose higher in the sky.
You can clear the stain from the glass by using vinegar and water.
