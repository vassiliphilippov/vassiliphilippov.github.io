St. Olav

proper noun
king and patron saint of Norway
Pronunciation: / seɪnt oʊˈlɑːv / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈɒlæv / (BrE)
(c. 995–1030) King of Norway and its patron saint, credited with the Christianization of Norway.

Examples

St. Olav of Norway
Legends say that St. Olav played a key role in converting Norway to Christianity.
Many Norwegian towns celebrate the feast day of St. Olav each July.
The Nidaros Cathedral in Trondheim is dedicated to St. Olav.

Related Terms

  • synonym – king: male ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth
  • synonym – saint: a person acknowledged as holy or virtuous and regarded in Christian faith as being in heaven after death
