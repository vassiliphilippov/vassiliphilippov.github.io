quadrant

noun
instrument for measuring celestial altitude
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑː.drənt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.rənt / (BrE)
an instrument, typically shaped as a quarter-circle, used for measuring the altitude of celestial objects above the horizon

Examples

astronomical quadrant
Before telescopes, astronomers relied on the quadrant for measuring the altitude of stars.
He proudly displayed an antique quadrant in his study, a relic of early navigation.
The sailor used a quadrant to determine the ship's position at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sextant: an instrument for measuring angles in navigation, similar to a quadrant
