spiv

noun
a slick, dubious street dealer (Britain)
Pronunciation: / spɪv / (AmE), / spɪv / (BrE)
(in British English) a man, typically one who is smartly dressed, who makes a living by dubious or illegal dealings, often without regular employment.

Examples

flashy spiv in a suit
Dressed in a flashy suit, the spiv tried to sell fake watches to passers-by.
During the war, black market goods were often sold by a spiv lurking on street corners.
He was a known spiv who always seemed to have a dodgy deal on the go.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gentleman: a man of good, courteous conduct and respectability
  • synonym – hustler: a person who makes money by sly or dishonest means
  • synonym – swindler: a person who cheats others out of money
