indulgence

noun
the act of gratifying a desire
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns /; / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns /; / ɪnˈdʌldʒəns / (BrE)
the act of allowing oneself to enjoy something, especially as a special pleasure or treat, sometimes with little self-restraint

Examples

a sweet indulgence
He regretted his indulgence in late-night snacks when he stepped on the scale.
Her weekend spa visit was a rare indulgence she allowed herself after a stressful week.
Sometimes a little indulgence in chocolate is all you need to lift your mood.
A little indulgence now and then is good for the soul.
His indulgence in desserts made it difficult to lose weight.
She allowed herself the indulgence of sleeping in on Sundays.

Related Terms

  • antonym – restraint: the act of holding back or limiting oneself
  • antonym – self-control: the ability to restrain one's impulses or desires
  • degree higher – self-indulgence: excessive or unrestrained gratification of one's desires
  • antonym – self-restraint: the act of holding back or controlling one's desires
