flaky

adjective
having the texture or appearance of flakes
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪki / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki / (BrE)
consisting of or breaking into small, thin pieces or flakes; easily coming apart in thin layers

Examples

flaky pastry crust
After drying out, the paint became rough and flaky.
She loves the flaky crust on fresh croissants.
Some types of fish have a particularly flaky texture when cooked.
After a week of sunburn, his skin became dry and flaky.
Some types of fish have a mild, flaky texture when cooked.
The croissant was perfectly flaky and buttery, just like the ones in Paris.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crumbly: breaking easily into small fragments
  • antonym – smooth: having an even and regular surface or texture
