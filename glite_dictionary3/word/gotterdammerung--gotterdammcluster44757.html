Gotterdammerung

noun
mythic destruction of the gods; catastrophic downfall
Pronunciation: / ˌgɒtərˈdæmərʊŋ / (AmE), / ˌɡɒtədæməˈrʊŋ / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the prophesied destruction of the gods and the end of the world in a final battle with evil forces; also used figuratively to denote a cataclysmic downfall or collapse.

Examples

the Gotterdammerung of the gods
Many interpreted the collapse of the empire as a modern Gotterdammerung—an epic struggle ending in ruin.
Some historians refer to the fall of Rome as the West’s Gotterdammerung.
Wagner’s opera Gotterdammerung dramatizes the final destruction of the gods.
