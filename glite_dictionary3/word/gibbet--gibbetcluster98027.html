to gibbet

verb
expose to public ridicule or scorn
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪbət / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪbɪt / (BrE)
to expose someone or something to ridicule or public scorn, often in a way that humiliates or shames them

Examples

to gibbet someone publicly
She feared he would be gibbeted by colleagues for his unconventional ideas.
The politician was gibbeted in the press for his controversial remarks.
Writers throughout history have gibbeted hypocrisy and corruption in their works.

Related Terms

  • synonym – humiliate: to make someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • antonym – praise: to express approval or admiration of someone or something
