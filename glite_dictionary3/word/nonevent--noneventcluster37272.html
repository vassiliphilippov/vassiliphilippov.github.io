nonevent

noun
disappointing anticipated event
Pronunciation: / ˌnʌnɪˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌnʌnɪˈvent / (BrE)
An event that was expected to be important but turns out to be insignificant or disappointing.

Examples

a nonevent after hype
After all the hype, the much-advertised concert turned out to be a nonevent.
Many expected the meeting to bring change, but it was basically a nonevent.
The predicted storm was a nonevent, bringing only a light drizzle.
