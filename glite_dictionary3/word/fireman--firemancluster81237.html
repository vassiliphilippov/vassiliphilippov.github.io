fireman

noun
worker tending fires on steam machinery
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəmən / (BrE)
A man employed to tend and maintain fires, especially one who manages the boiler fire on a steam locomotive, ship, or other steam-powered machine.

Examples

fireman on a steam train
Each fireman on the steamship worked long shifts in the engine room.
In the age of steam engines, being a fireman was a hot and demanding job.
The fireman shoveled coal into the locomotive's furnace to keep the train running.
