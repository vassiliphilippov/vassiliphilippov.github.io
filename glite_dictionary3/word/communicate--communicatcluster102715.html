to communicate

verb
transmit (disease or feeling) to another
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to transmit (especially a disease or feeling) from one person or organism to another

Examples

to communicate a disease
HIV cannot communicate by casual contact such as hugging or sharing food.
If the proper hygiene is not maintained, influenza can easily communicate within crowded environments.
In rare cases, rabies can communicate from animals to humans through a bite.
