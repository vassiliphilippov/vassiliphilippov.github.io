hyperpigmentation

noun
abnormal darkening of the skin
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌpɪɡmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˌpɪɡmənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an unusual darkening of the skin caused by excess production of melanin or other pigments, often due to disease, injury, or sun exposure

Examples

hyperpigmentation after acne
After her acne cleared, she was left with hyperpigmentation on her cheeks.
Dermatologists often recommend treatments to reduce hyperpigmentation and even out the complexion.
Too much sun exposure can result in hyperpigmentation in sensitive skin areas.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hypopigmentation: abnormal lightening of the skin
