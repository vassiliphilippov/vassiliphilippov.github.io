Scarabaeus

noun
a genus of scarab beetles
Pronunciation: / skəˈræbiˌus / (AmE), / skəˈræbɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Scarabaeidae, comprising the typical scarab beetles, many of which are dung beetles.

Examples

Scarabaeus beetle species
Researchers discovered a new species of Scarabaeus in the arid regions of North Africa.
Several species within the genus Scarabaeus are known for rolling dung into balls as a food source.
The ancient Egyptians considered the Scarabaeus sacred and often used its likeness in jewelry.
