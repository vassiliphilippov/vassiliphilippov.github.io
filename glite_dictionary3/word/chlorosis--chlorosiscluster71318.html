chlorosis

noun
anemia with greenish skin tint
Pronunciation: / kləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / klɔːˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A form of iron deficiency anemia, typically found in young women, characterized by weakness, menstrual disturbances, and a greenish tint to the skin.

Examples

chlorosis in young women
In the nineteenth century, chlorosis was commonly reported in young women with poor diets.
Medical textbooks describe chlorosis as an anemia marked by a green tint to the skin.
The physician diagnosed the patient with chlorosis after noticing her pale, greenish complexion.
