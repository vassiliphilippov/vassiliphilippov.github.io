ideologue

noun
strong advocate of an ideology
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪdiəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəlɒg / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports and promotes a particular ideology, especially in a way that ignores practical considerations or opposing views

Examples

political ideologue
As an ideologue, she puts her political beliefs above all practical concerns.
Some accuse the judge of being an ideologue for consistently supporting one side of the argument.
The senator was criticized as an ideologue who refused to compromise on any issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym – partisan: a strong supporter of a party, cause, or person
  • antonym – pragmatist: someone who deals with things sensibly and realistically, contrasting with an ideologue
