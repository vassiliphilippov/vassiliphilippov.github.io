hypotenuse

noun
side opposite right angle in a triangle
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑtənus / (AmE), / hʌɪˈpɒtɪnjuːz / (BrE)
the side of a right-angled triangle that is opposite the right angle

Examples

hypotenuse of a triangle
In a 3-4-5 triangle, the hypotenuse is the side with a length of 5 units.
She measured the hypotenuse to calculate the diagonal of the triangle.
The length of the hypotenuse can be found using the Pythagorean theorem.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cathetus: either of the other two sides of a right-angled triangle
