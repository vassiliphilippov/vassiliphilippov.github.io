limb

noun
animal appendage (arm, leg, wing, etc.)
Pronunciation: / lɪm / (AmE), / lɪm / (BrE)
a jointed appendage of an animal, such as an arm, leg, wing, or flipper, used for movement or grasping

Examples

broken limb
After the accident, she lost use of her left limb for several months.
Birds use each wing as a specialized limb for flight.
The frog extended its powerful limb to leap across the pond.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appendage: a body part that projects from the main body
  • antonym – trunk: the main part of the body excluding limbs and head
