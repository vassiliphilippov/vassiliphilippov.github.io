ghoul

noun
person who steals corpses from graves
Pronunciation: / ɡuːl / (AmE), / ɡuːl / (BrE)
a person who exhumes bodies from graves, especially to sell them for anatomical dissection

Examples

ghoul digs graves
In the 19th century, a ghoul could earn a living by supplying medical schools with cadavers.
Rumors spread that a ghoul was stealing bodies from the churchyard at night.
The police caught the notorious ghoul as he was leaving a freshly disturbed grave.
