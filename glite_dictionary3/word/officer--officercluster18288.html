officer

noun
person with authority on a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːfəsər / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a person holding a position of authority and responsibility on a ship, often in charge of specific operations or departments

Examples

ship officer
Each officer on the vessel has specific duties during an emergency.
She was promoted to second officer after only three years at sea.
The chief officer checked the navigation equipment before departure.
