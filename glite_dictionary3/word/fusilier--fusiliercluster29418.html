fusilier

noun
a historical British infantry soldier
Pronunciation: / ˌfjuːzəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌfjuːzɪˈlɪə / (BrE)
(historically) a British infantry soldier armed with a light flintlock musket, especially one belonging to a regiment originally equipped in this way.

Examples

British fusilier regiment
During the ceremony, the veteran proudly wore his old fusilier cap badge.
Her ancestor was a fusilier who fought at the Battle of Waterloo.
The museum displayed the uniform of a British fusilier from the eighteenth century.
