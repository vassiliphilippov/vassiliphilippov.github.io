pelisse

noun
long, sleeveless fur-trimmed cloak
Pronunciation: / pəˈlis / (AmE), / pəˈliːs / (BrE)
A woman's long cloak or outer garment, often sleeveless and typically lined or trimmed with fur, worn especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Examples

elegant pelisse coat
During the Regency era, a fashionable pelisse was an essential part of a woman's wardrobe.
She arrived at the ball draped in an elegant pelisse lined with sable fur.
The novel describes a young lady donning her blue pelisse before venturing into the snow.
