saddleback

noun
double sloping roof with gables at each end
Pronunciation: / ˈsædəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl.bæk / (BrE)
A roof having two slopes that meet at a central ridge, with gables at each end.

Examples

saddleback roof shape
Many historic churches feature a saddleback tower, distinct from flat-roofed designs.
Rainwater runs off easily from a saddleback because of its double slopes and central ridge.
The old barn was topped with a prominent saddleback roof, giving it a traditional appearance.
