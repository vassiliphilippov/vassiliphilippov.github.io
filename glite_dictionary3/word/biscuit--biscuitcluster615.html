biscuit

noun
small, sweet, flat baked cake (British)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪs.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪs.kɪt / (BrE)
A small, flat, sweet baked cake, typically crisp and eaten as a snack, especially in British English.

Examples

biscuit with tea
Could you please pass me a biscuit to have with my tea?
He offered his guests a plate of homemade biscuits.
The chocolate-covered biscuit was her favorite afternoon treat.
