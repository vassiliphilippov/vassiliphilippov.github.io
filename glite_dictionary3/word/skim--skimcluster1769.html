to skim

verb
move quickly across water's surface
Pronunciation: / skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to move lightly and quickly over or across the surface of something, especially water

Examples

to skim across water
The flat stone began to skim across the lake after he threw it.
The small boat seemed to skim over the gentle waves, barely making a sound.
Water birds are known to skim the surface in search of food.
He tried to skim a stone across the surface of the lake.
She watched him expertly skim the pebble over the still water.
The boys loved to skim flat rocks on the river during summer.
A bird skimmed the surface of the lake at sunrise.
Clouds skimming the mountain peaks created a beautiful view.
The stone skimmed across the water before sinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glide: to move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort
  • antonym – plunge: to move or fall suddenly and quickly in a different direction (opposite of 'skim' in terms of movement quality)
  • synonym – skip: to move lightly and quickly across a surface, especially water
