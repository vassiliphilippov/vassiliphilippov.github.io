to aestivate

verb
enter summer dormancy (animal biology)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛs.tɪ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈiː.stɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to spend a hot or dry period in a dormant state, typically during the summer.

Examples

to aestivate underground
In order to survive drought, the African lungfish will aestivate for several months.
Many desert snails aestivate in shallow burrows during the hottest months.
Some amphibians aestivate when water becomes scarce in the summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hibernate: to spend the winter in a dormant state (biology)
