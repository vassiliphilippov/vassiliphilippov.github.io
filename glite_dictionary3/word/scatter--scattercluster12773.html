scatter

noun
the act of spreading things about
Pronunciation: / ˈskæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈskætə / (BrE)
an act or result of things or people being spread out or distributed in a disorderly way

Examples

a scatter of papers
After the fireworks, there was a scatter of people heading home through the park.
The scatter of papers on his desk made it hard to find anything.
There was a sudden scatter of birds when the dog ran into the park.
A scatter of coins lay across the floor after the box tipped over.
She observed a scatter of wildflowers blooming in the meadow.
There was only a scatter of applause at the end of the speech.
