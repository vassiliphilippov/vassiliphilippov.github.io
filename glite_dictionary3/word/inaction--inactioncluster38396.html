inaction

noun
lack of action; doing nothing
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈækʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈækʃən / (BrE)
lack of action; the state of doing nothing, especially when action is expected or required.

Examples

government inaction
Her inaction during the crisis led to further problems.
Sometimes, inaction can be more damaging than making the wrong decision.
The committee's inaction on climate change has been widely criticized.

Related Terms

  • antonym – action: the process of doing something to achieve an aim
  • synonym – passivity: acceptance of what happens without active response
