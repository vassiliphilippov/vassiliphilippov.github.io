to bar

verb
obstruct or block passage
Pronunciation: / bɑr / (AmE), / bɑː / (BrE)
to block, obstruct, or officially prevent someone from entering, participating, or doing something

Examples

to bar entry

Several journalists were barred from entering the press briefing.
The club decided to bar anyone who broke the rules.
Remember to bar the door when you leave for vacation.
She barred the window to keep out the wind.
The villagers hurried to bar the gates before nightfall.
