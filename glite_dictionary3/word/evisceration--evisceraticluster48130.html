evisceration

noun
weakening or stripping of essential value
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of weakening or stripping an idea, law, or statement of its essential value or force, often by alteration

Examples

evisceration of a law
Critics argued that the policy underwent evisceration during committee discussions.
Some see the court's decision as an evisceration of civil rights protections.
The amendments resulted in the evisceration of the original bill's intent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dilution: the process of making something weaker or less effective
  • antonym – strengthening: the act of making something stronger or more effective
