association

noun
ecological community of organisms
Pronunciation: / əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Ecology) A group of plants and animals living together in a specific area and forming a community with a few dominant species.

Examples

plant association
Each association is characterized by a specific set of species that thrive together in that region.
Researchers studied the plant association found in the river valley to understand local biodiversity.
The forest association is dominated by oak and pine trees, with a variety of understory shrubs.
