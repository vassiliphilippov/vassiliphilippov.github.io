to aggrade

verb
raise a riverbed by sediment deposition
Pronunciation: / əˈɡreɪd / (AmE), / əˈɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of a river or stream) to raise the level of its bed by depositing sediment.

Examples

to aggrade a riverbed
Engineers took measures to prevent the stream from continuing to aggrade and causing overflow.
Over time, the river began to aggrade its channel, raising the bed above its original level.
Sediment carried by floods can aggrade the floodplain, altering the landscape significantly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – degrade: to lower the level of a riverbed by removing material
