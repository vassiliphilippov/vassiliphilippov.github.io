A

noun
blood group with A antigen
Pronunciation: / eɪ / (AmE), / eɪ / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells carry the A antigen and whose plasma contains anti-B antibodies

Examples

A blood type
His medical chart indicates that his blood group is A.
People with blood type A can safely receive blood from donors with type A or O.
When donating blood, it is important to know whether you are A, B, AB, or O.
