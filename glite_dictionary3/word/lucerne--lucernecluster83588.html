lucerne

noun
European forage plant; also called alfalfa
Pronunciation: / luˈsɜrn / (AmE), / ljuːˈsɜːn / (BrE)
A European legume with trifoliate leaves and blue-violet flowers, widely grown as a pasture and hay crop.

Examples

lucerne hay crop
After harvesting the lucerne, they stored it as hay for the winter months.
In many regions, lucerne is valued for its ability to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
The farmer planted lucerne to improve the quality of his cattle feed.
