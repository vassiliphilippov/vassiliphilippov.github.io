Capparis spinosa

noun
Mediterranean shrub; source of capers
Pronunciation: / kəˈpærɪs spaɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / kəˈpærɪs spaɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
a low, spiny Mediterranean shrub grown for its green flower buds, which are pickled and used as capers in cooking

Examples

Capparis spinosa shrub
Capparis spinosa thrives in dry, rocky soils of the Mediterranean region.
Botanists often study Capparis spinosa for its unique adaptation to arid climates.
The flower buds of Capparis spinosa are harvested and pickled to become the familiar capers.
