unstable

adjective
highly reactive (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a substance or compound) highly or violently reactive and likely to undergo chemical change, often dangerously so.

Examples

unstable chemical
Handling unstable compounds requires special laboratory equipment and strict safety procedures.
Mixing the chemicals created an unstable solution that needed to be handled with extreme care.
Some unstable isotopes can decay suddenly, releasing dangerous levels of radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reactive: readily responsive to a stimulus, especially a chemical one
  • antonym – stable: not likely to change, react, or deteriorate; steady
