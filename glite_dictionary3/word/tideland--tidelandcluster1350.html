tideland

noun
coastal land covered by tides
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪdˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of land near the sea that is regularly covered and uncovered by the tide

Examples

coastal tideland area
Birds nest in the tidelands where the water retreats each day.
Fishermen gathered along the tideland at low tide to collect shellfish.
The new nature reserve protects a stretch of coastal tideland rich in marine life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – highland: an area of land that is at a high elevation, especially compared to surroundings
  • synonym – tidal flat: a flat area of muddy land that is covered and uncovered by the tide
