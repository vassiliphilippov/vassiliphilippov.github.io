toxoid

noun
inactivated toxin used for immunization
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑk.sɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɒk.sɔɪd / (BrE)
a bacterial toxin modified so it is no longer harmful, but still able to trigger an immune response and provide immunity

Examples

toxoid vaccine injection
After exposure to the toxoid, the immune system produces protective antibodies.
Diphtheria toxoid is commonly included in childhood immunization schedules.
The toxoid in the vaccine helps the body build immunity to tetanus.
