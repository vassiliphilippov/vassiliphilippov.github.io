oxidised

adjective
combined with or changed by oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːksɪˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) combined with or changed by a chemical reaction with oxygen.

Examples

oxidised metal surface
An oxidised layer forms on aluminum, protecting it from further corrosion.
The once shiny copper roof had become oxidised and turned green over the years.
Vitamin C is less effective when it is oxidised by exposure to air.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oxidized: having undergone a chemical reaction with oxygen (American spelling)
  • antonym – reduced: having gained electrons, opposite process to oxidized in chemistry
