unchangeably

adverb
in an unalterable way
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be changed or altered.

Examples

unchangeably fixed
His beliefs remained unchangeably set despite years of debate.
She holds unchangeably to her principles, no matter the circumstances.
The rules were unchangeably established by tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – changeably: in a way that is able to be changed; opposite of 'unchangeably'
  • synonym – immutably: in a way that cannot be changed; synonym of 'unchangeably'
