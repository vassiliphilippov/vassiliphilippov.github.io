to acidulate

verb
to make slightly acidic
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪdʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪdjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to make something slightly acidic or more sour in taste

Examples

to acidulate a solution
Chemists sometimes acidulate a solution to adjust its pH during an experiment.
Many recipes call for chefs to acidulate water with lemon juice before adding certain vegetables.
To keep the apples from browning, she decided to acidulate the water with a splash of vinegar.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acidify: to make or become acid; to turn acidic
