coloured

adjective
artificially produced color
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(of a color) artificially produced; not natural; dyed or altered from its original state.

Examples

coloured hair
She wore coloured contact lenses for the party.
The fabric had been coloured rather than left in its natural state.
The wood was coloured to look like mahogany.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dyed: having had color added by the process of dyeing
  • antonym – natural: existing in nature; not man-made or artificial (of color)
