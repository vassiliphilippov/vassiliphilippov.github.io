to mortise

verb
cut a hole for a tenon (woodworking)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrtɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪs / (BrE)
to cut a hole (mortise) in wood or other material, or to join materials using a mortise and tenon joint

Examples

to mortise a wooden board
She carefully mortised the door for the new lock plate.
The carpenter had to mortise the beams before assembling the frame.
You can mortise the ends of the board to fit tightly with the adjoining piece.
Experts often mortise the beams before fitting the tenons.
If you mortise the shelves properly, the bookcase will last for years.
The cabinetmaker decided to mortise the joints for added strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tenon: a projecting piece prepared for insertion into a mortise
