reification

noun
treating people as things; loss of individuality
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of regarding or treating a person as a thing, especially by stripping away individuality or personal qualities.

Examples

reification of people
Critics argued that the film's portrayal of its characters was an example of reification.
Marxist thinkers highlight the dangers of reification in capitalist economies.
The reification of workers in industrial society can lead to widespread alienation and loss of purpose.

Related Terms

  • synonym – objectification: regarding a person as an object, often used in feminist or sociological contexts
