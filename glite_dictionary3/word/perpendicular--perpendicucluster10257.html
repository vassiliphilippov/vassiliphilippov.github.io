perpendicular

noun
English Gothic architectural style, 14th–15th centuries
Pronunciation: / ˌpɝː.pənˈdɪk.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.pənˈdɪk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
a style of English Gothic architecture from the 14th and 15th centuries, characterized by strong vertical lines, four-centered arches, and elaborate fan vaulting

Examples

Perpendicular style church
Many English cathedrals display elements of Perpendicular architecture, especially in their towers and nave arcades.
The choir was rebuilt in the Perpendicular style, featuring soaring windows and intricate tracery.
The use of four-centered arches distinguishes the Perpendicular period from earlier phases of Gothic design.
