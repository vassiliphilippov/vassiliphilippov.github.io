to gip

verb
cheat or swindle (offensive)
Pronunciation: / dʒɪp / (AmE), / dʒɪp / (BrE)
(sometimes offensive) To cheat or swindle someone out of money or possessions.

Examples

to gip a customer
I realized too late that the ticket seller had gipped us.
It’s wrong to gip customers, no matter how much you dislike your job.
The mechanic tried to gip me by charging for work he never did.

  • synonym – swindle: to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions
