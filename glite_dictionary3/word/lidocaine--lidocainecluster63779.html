lidocaine

noun
local anesthetic medication
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪdəˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪdəʊˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A local anesthetic used to numb tissue in a specific area, often applied topically to the skin or mucous membranes.

Examples

lidocaine injection
Dentists often use lidocaine as a local anesthetic for procedures.
Some over-the-counter creams contain lidocaine to relieve itching and minor burns.
The doctor applied lidocaine before stitching the wound to prevent pain.
