Savonarola

proper noun
Italian Dominican friar and reformer (1452–1498)
Pronunciation: / sævəˈnɑroʊlə / (AmE), / savəˈnɑːrələ / (BrE)
(1452–1498) An Italian Dominican friar and reformer in Florence who preached against sin and corruption, expelled the Medici dynasty, and was executed for criticizing the Pope.

Examples

Savonarola preached reform
After Savonarola's downfall, the Medici family regained control of Florence.
Many historians study Savonarola's influence on Florence during the Renaissance.
The sermons of Savonarola inspired both admiration and fierce opposition.
