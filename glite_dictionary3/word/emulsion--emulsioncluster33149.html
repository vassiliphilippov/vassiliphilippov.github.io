emulsion

noun
mixture of two unmixable liquids
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmʌlʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A mixture of two liquids that do not normally mix, where one liquid contains tiny droplets dispersed in the other.

Examples

milk emulsion example
Milk is a natural emulsion of fat in water.
Pharmaceutical creams often rely on the stability of their emulsion for effectiveness.
The salad dressing separated because the emulsion broke down.

Related Terms

  • antonym – suspension: mixture in which particles are dispersed throughout a fluid but are large enough to settle out
