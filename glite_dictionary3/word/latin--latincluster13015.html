Latin

adjective
relating to Romance-language-speaking people or countries
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ən / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
relating to people, countries, or cultures that speak Romance languages, especially those derived from Latin, such as Spanish, Portuguese, French, Italian, and Romanian

Examples

Latin culture influence
Brazil is the largest Latin country in South America.
Many Caribbean nations are considered Latin because their official languages derive from Latin.
The university offered courses in Latin literature and history.
Many Latin words can be found in English via French and other Romance languages.
Spanish is a prime example of a Latin language spoken by millions worldwide.
The French and Italian languages are considered Latin languages because of their origins.
