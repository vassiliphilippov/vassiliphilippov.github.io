to snag

verb
obtain quickly or cleverly
Pronunciation: / snæɡ / (AmE), / snæɡ / (BrE)
to obtain or achieve something quickly or cleverly, especially when it is difficult to get

Examples

to snag a bargain
I managed to snag the last pair of shoes in my size during the sale.
She snagged a front-row seat at the concert by arriving early.
We were lucky to snag tickets for the game before they sold out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grab: to take or seize suddenly; similar meaning
