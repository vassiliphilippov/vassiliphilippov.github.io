flash

adjective
tastelessly showy; ostentatious
Pronunciation: / flæʃ / (AmE), / flæʃ / (BrE)
(especially British) Showy in a way that is meant to attract attention but is often considered tasteless or lacking in style; ostentatious.

Examples

flash car
He drove a flash car that turned heads wherever he went.
She wore a flash outfit to the party, hoping to stand out from the crowd.
The restaurant's flash décor was more impressive than the food itself.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flashy: showy in a way that attracts attention; ostentatious
  • synonym – gaudy: extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • antonym – modest: not showy or ostentatious
