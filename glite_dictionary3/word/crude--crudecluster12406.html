crude

adjective
in an early, unrefined stage
Pronunciation: / kruːd / (AmE), / kruːd / (BrE)
simple, rough, or lacking sophistication; made in a basic or unskilled way

Examples

crude tools
At the archaeological site, they found crude tools made from stone and bone.
Early filmmakers had to work with crude equipment that could barely capture moving images.
The first computers were crude by modern standards but marked a major technological leap.
He fashioned a crude tool from a piece of stone and some twine.
The shelter they built was a crude structure of branches and leaves.
They used a crude bench made from a wooden plank balanced on rocks.

  • antonym – refined: improved or made more sophisticated; opposite of crude
  • synonym – rough: not finished with care or precision
