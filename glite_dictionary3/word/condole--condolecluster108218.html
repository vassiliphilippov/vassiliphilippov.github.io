to condole

verb
express sympathy for someone's loss
Pronunciation: / kənˈdoʊl / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊl / (BrE)
to express sympathy or sorrow, especially on the occasion of someone's death

Examples

to condole with a family
After hearing the sad news, neighbors came to condole with the grieving family.
He wrote a heartfelt letter to condole with his friend after her father's passing.
Many people gathered at the memorial service to condole with the widow.

Related Terms

  • synonym – commiserate: to express sympathy or sorrow for another's misfortune
