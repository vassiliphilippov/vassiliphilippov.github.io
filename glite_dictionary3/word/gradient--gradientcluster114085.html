gradient

noun
a gradual change in value or amount
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪdiənt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdiənt / (BrE)
a gradual change in the value or amount of something, especially the rate of such change in physical quantity or dimension

Examples

gradient of temperature
After the rain, the gradient of the river's flow became more pronounced.
The color gradient on the map shows elevation changes in the landscape.
The temperature gradient across the metal plate indicates how heat is distributed.
