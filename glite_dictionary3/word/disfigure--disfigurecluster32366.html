to disfigure

verb
spoil the appearance of
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
to spoil or mar the appearance of someone or something, often by damaging, deforming, or marking it

Examples

to disfigure a face
Graffiti can disfigure public buildings and monuments.
She was worried that the surgery might disfigure her face permanently.
The fire badly disfigured the old painting in the hallway.

Related Terms

  • antonym – beautify: to make something more beautiful or attractive
  • synonym – damage: to physically harm or spoil something so that it is less attractive or of less value
  • synonym – deface: to damage the surface or appearance of something, especially by writing or drawing on it
