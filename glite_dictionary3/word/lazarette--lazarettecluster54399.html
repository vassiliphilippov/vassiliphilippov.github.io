lazarette

noun
hospital for people with infectious diseases
Pronunciation: / ˌlæzəˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌlæzəˈrɛt / (BrE)
An establishment, especially a hospital, used for isolating people with infectious diseases, especially leprosy.

Examples

lazarette for patients
During the epidemic, a lazarette was established on the outskirts of the city to contain the disease.
In the 19th century, ships suspected of carrying infection were held at a lazarette before passengers could disembark.
The old lazarette remains as a historic reminder of the fight against leprosy.
