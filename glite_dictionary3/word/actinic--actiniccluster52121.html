actinic

adjective
relating to light that causes chemical changes
Pronunciation: / ækˈtɪnɪk / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the property of radiation, especially ultraviolet light, to produce photochemical effects, such as causing reactions or changes in photographic film or other substances.

Examples

actinic light exposure
Special glass blocks actinic light to prevent unwanted chemical reactions in laboratories.
Sunlight contains actinic rays that can damage photographic materials if they are not properly protected.
The actinic quality of ultraviolet radiation is used for sterilization and phototherapy.
