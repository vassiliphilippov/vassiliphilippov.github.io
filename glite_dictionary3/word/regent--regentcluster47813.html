regent

noun
member of a governing board
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənt / (BrE)
A member of a governing board, particularly in certain universities or institutions, who is responsible for overseeing policy and administration.

Examples

university regent
Serving as a regent requires a commitment to the institution's long-term goals.
She was elected as a regent of the university to help guide its future direction.
The board of regents met to discuss changes to the college curriculum.
