to press

verb
urge or force to act
Pronunciation: / prɛs / (AmE), / prɛs / (BrE)
to urge, insist, or advocate for something or someone with persistence

Examples

to press for change
His parents constantly pressed him to study harder for his exams.
Reporters pressed her to comment on the recent controversy.
The manager pressed for a decision at the meeting.
Activists press for improvements in education funding every year.
She intends to press her case until the committee gives her an answer.
The senator continued to press for stricter environmental regulations despite opposition.
He presses his students to pursue their goals relentlessly.
She pressed her friend to reconsider the decision to move away.
The citizens pressed the mayor for answers at the town hall meeting.
