sex linkage

noun
gene association with sex chromosomes
Pronunciation: / sɛks ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɛks ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the association of certain genes with sex chromosomes, resulting in specific traits appearing more often in one sex than the other

Examples

sex linkage in inheritance
Mendelian genetics did not fully account for the effects of sex linkage on certain inherited traits.
Red-green color blindness is a classic example of a trait determined by sex linkage.
The inheritance of hemophilia is explained by sex linkage, as the gene is found on the X chromosome.

Related Terms

  • antonym – autosomal inheritance: inheritance of traits determined by genes not on sex chromosomes
