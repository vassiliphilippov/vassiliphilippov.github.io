naturalisation

noun
establishment of non-native species in new regions
Pronunciation: / ˌnætʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃ(ə)rələˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process of introducing animals or plants to regions where they are not native but become able to thrive as if they were.

Examples

naturalisation of plants
Efforts to control the naturalisation of invasive species are ongoing.
Many plants have undergone naturalisation after being introduced by humans to new continents.
The naturalisation of rabbits in Australia has had significant ecological impacts.

  • synonym – introduction: the act of bringing a species into a new environment
