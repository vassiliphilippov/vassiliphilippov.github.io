incline

noun
a sloping surface between levels
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌklaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪn.klaɪn / (BrE)
a sloping surface or stretch of land, such as a ramp or hill

Examples

steep incline
The incline up to the bridge was steep and challenging for cyclists.
They built a wooden incline to make the entrance accessible for wheelchairs.
Walkers paused to catch their breath halfway up the incline.
Heavy rains caused the incline to become slippery and difficult to climb.
The hikers stopped to rest on the incline before reaching the top of the hill.
The train tracks curve sharply as they ascend the steep incline through the mountains.
