eyelet

noun
small ring reinforcing a hole for cords/laces
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪlət /; / ˈaɪ.lət / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɪt /; / ˈaɪ.lɪt / (BrE)
a small reinforced hole (often with a metal ring or stitching) in fabric, leather, or similar material, used for threading a cord, lace, or hook

Examples

eyelet for laces
She laced her boots, threading the cords through each eyelet securely.
The curtain was hung using metal eyelets that allowed the rod to pass through easily.
The sail had reinforced eyelets at the corners for attaching ropes.
She threaded the ribbon through each eyelet on the corset.
The shoes have metal eyelets for the laces to pass through.
To reinforce the fabric, a brass eyelet was installed at each corner.
