to personate

verb
to attribute human qualities (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜːrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsəneɪt / (BrE)
(archaic) To attribute human qualities to something; to personify.

Examples

to personate the sun
In ancient myths, poets would often personate the sun as a mighty chariot driver.
Many cultures personate abstract concepts such as Justice or Death in art and storytelling.
The novelist chose to personate nature, giving the forest desires and moods.
