stasis

noun
abnormal slowing or stoppage of flow
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪsɪs, ˈstæ- / (AmE), / ˈsteɪsɪs, ˈstæ- / (BrE)
A condition in which the normal flow of a liquid, especially blood or other bodily fluids, is slowed or stopped.

Examples

blood stasis
Chronic heart failure can result in pulmonary stasis due to poor circulation.
Doctors monitored for signs of stasis after the operation to prevent complications.
The patient developed stasis in the leg veins, increasing the risk of a blood clot.

  • synonym – congestion: accumulation of fluid or blood causing swelling or blockage
