Carl Linnaeus

proper noun
Swedish botanist; creator of modern taxonomy
Pronunciation: / kɑrl lɪˈniəs / (AmE), / kɑːl lɪˈniːəs / (BrE)
(1707–1778) A Swedish botanist, physician, and zoologist, renowned for developing the modern system of naming and classifying living organisms.

Examples

Carl Linnaeus created taxonomy
Carl Linnaeus revolutionized biology by creating a standardized system for classifying plants and animals.
Many consider Carl Linnaeus the father of taxonomy due to his influential work in the eighteenth century.
The binomial nomenclature used in science today was first introduced by Carl Linnaeus.
