vascularisation

noun
formation of blood vessels in tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌvæskjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvæskjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
The process by which body tissue develops blood vessels, especially capillaries.

Examples

vascularisation of tissue
Delayed vascularisation can lead to poor wound healing.
Research focuses on improving vascularisation in engineered tissues for transplantation.
The tumor showed increased vascularisation after several weeks of treatment.
