plebeianism

noun
quality of being vulgar or common
Pronunciation: / pləˈbiː.i.əˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / plɪˈbiː.i.ə.nɪzəm / (BrE)
The quality or state of being associated with the common people, regarded as coarse, unrefined, or vulgar.

Examples

rise from plebeianism
He viewed his rise from plebeianism to aristocracy as a mark of personal achievement.
Some critics accused the novel of excessive plebeianism in both language and themes.
Their disdain for what they saw as plebeianism was evident in their choice of art and literature.

Related Terms

  • antonym – refinement: cultivated elegance or taste
  • synonym – vulgarity: the quality of being vulgar or coarse
