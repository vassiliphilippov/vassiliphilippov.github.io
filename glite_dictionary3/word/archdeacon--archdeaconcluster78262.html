archdeacon

noun
senior church official below a bishop
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrtʃˌdiːkən / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈdiːkən / (BrE)
a senior clergy official in certain Christian churches, ranking immediately below a bishop and having administrative authority over a diocese or part of a diocese

Examples

archdeacon of the diocese
After many years of service as a priest, he was appointed archdeacon of the area.
The archdeacon delivered a thoughtful sermon during the Sunday service.
The responsibilities of an archdeacon include overseeing the clergy within his jurisdiction.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – deacon: an ordained minister of an order ranking below that of priest
