scoop

noun
exclusive news report published first
Pronunciation: / skuːp / (AmE), / skuːp / (BrE)
an exclusive news story or report that is published or broadcast by one organization before others

Examples

breaking scoop
Everyone was talking about the reporter's latest scoop on the election scandal.
Getting a scoop is highly valued in the world of competitive journalism.
The newspaper landed a scoop by publishing the investigation results before anyone else.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exclusive: a story or report published by only one news outlet ahead of others
