gelatine

noun
protein used as gelling agent
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛlətin / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlətɪn / (BrE)
A clear, tasteless, water-soluble protein obtained from animal bones and skin, used as a gelling agent in food and pharmaceuticals.

Examples

gelatine powder
She dissolved the gelatine in hot water before adding the fruit puree.
The recipe calls for two teaspoons of gelatine to set the dessert.
Vegetarians often avoid products containing gelatine because it comes from animals.
