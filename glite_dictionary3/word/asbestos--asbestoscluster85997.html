asbestos

noun
fire-resistant fibrous mineral
Pronunciation: / æsˈbɛstoʊs / (AmE), / æsˈbɛstɒs / (BrE)
a group of naturally occurring fibrous minerals that are resistant to heat and fire, formerly used in building materials and other products, but hazardous to health when inhaled

Examples

asbestos insulation material
Inhaling asbestos fibers can lead to serious respiratory illnesses.
The use of asbestos in construction has been banned in many countries due to health risks.
Workers must wear protective gear when removing asbestos from old buildings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – asbestosis: lung disease caused by inhaling asbestos fibers
  • synonym – lung cancer: a malignant tumor in the lungs, sometimes caused by asbestos exposure
