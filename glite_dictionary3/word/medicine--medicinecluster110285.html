medicine

noun
punishment; necessary consequence
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈmɛdsɪn / (BrE)
An unpleasant but necessary experience or punishment that one must accept as a consequence of their actions.

Examples

get your medicine
After years of cheating, the company finally got its medicine when it was fined heavily.
If you break the rules, you have to take your medicine, no matter how unpleasant it is.
The coach told the team they had to accept their medicine after a poor performance.

  • synonym – face the music: to accept consequences or punishment for one's actions
