to expand

verb
to exaggerate or make seem bigger
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspænd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænd / (BrE)
to make something seem larger or more important than it really is; to exaggerate

Examples

to expand the truth
He tends to expand the truth when telling stories about his travels.
She often expands her achievements in interviews to impress potential employers.
The company tried to expand the value of its assets for the report.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exaggerate: to represent as greater than is actually the case
  • antonym – minimize: to make something seem less important or smaller than it is
