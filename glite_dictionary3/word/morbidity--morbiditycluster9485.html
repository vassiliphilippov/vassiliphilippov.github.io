morbidity

noun
the state of being unhealthy or harmful
Pronunciation: / mɔrˈbɪdəti / (AmE), / mɔːˈbɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being unhealthy or harmful to health.

Examples

physical morbidity
High rates of morbidity were observed in polluted urban areas.
Smoking is a major cause of increased morbidity worldwide.
The report highlights the morbidity associated with poor diet and lack of exercise.

Related Terms

  • antonym – health: the state of being free from illness or injury
  • synonym – mortality: the state of being subject to death
