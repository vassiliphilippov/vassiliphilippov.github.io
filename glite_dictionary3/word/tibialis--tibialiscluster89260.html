tibialis

noun
muscle in the lower leg beside the tibia
Pronunciation: / ˌtɪbiˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɪbɪˈælɪs / (BrE)
either of two muscles in the lower leg (anterior and posterior) located beside the tibia, responsible for movement of the foot

Examples

tibialis muscle injury
Injury to the tibialis can cause pain when flexing or extending the foot.
Physical therapists often recommend exercises to strengthen the tibialis muscles after a leg injury.
The tibialis is essential for stabilizing the ankle during walking and running.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – tibialis anterior: the muscle in the front part of the lower leg responsible for dorsiflexing the foot
  • degree higher – tibialis posterior: the muscle in the back part of the lower leg responsible for inverting the foot
