to unseat

verb
cause to fall from a seat, especially a horse
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsit / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːt / (BrE)
to cause someone to fall from their seat, especially from a horse or vehicle

Examples

to unseat a rider
A sudden bump in the road nearly unseated the cyclist.
The rough waters threatened to unseat passengers from the small boat.
The wild horse unseated its rider within seconds of leaving the stable.
