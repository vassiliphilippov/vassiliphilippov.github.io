qualified

adjective
limited or restricted; not absolute
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
limited, restricted, or not complete; not absolute or unconditional

Examples

qualified approval
He expressed qualified support for the proposal, noting some areas of concern.
Her acceptance of the offer was qualified by certain conditions she wanted to discuss.
The board gave only a qualified endorsement to the new policy.
His approval was a qualified endorsement, as he still had some reservations about the proposal.
She gave a qualified yes, agreeing only if certain requirements were fulfilled.
The offer comes with several qualified conditions that must be met before it is finalized.
