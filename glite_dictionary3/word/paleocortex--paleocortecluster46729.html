paleocortex

noun
part of the brain's olfactory cortex
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪli.oʊˈkɔːrtɛks / (AmE), / ˌpæli.əʊˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
a region of the cerebral cortex associated with olfaction, considered evolutionarily older than most other cortical areas

Examples

the paleocortex processes smell
Damage to the paleocortex can impair an organism's sense of smell.
In evolutionary terms, the paleocortex is considered older than the neocortex.
The paleocortex is primarily responsible for processing olfactory information in some animals.
