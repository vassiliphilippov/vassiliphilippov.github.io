antibody

noun
protein that neutralizes foreign substances
Pronunciation: / ˈæntɪˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈæntɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
a protein produced by the body’s immune system in response to an antigen, helping to neutralize or destroy foreign substances such as bacteria and viruses

Examples

antibody against virus
An antibody binds specifically to an antigen to protect the body from infection.
Scientists are developing a new antibody treatment for autoimmune diseases.
The doctor explained that the antibody test would show whether I had been exposed to the virus.

Related Terms

  • antonym – antigen: a substance that provokes an immune response, often by triggering antibodies
  • synonym – immunoglobulin: another term for antibody, referring to the class of proteins involved
