to honour

verb
accept and pay a cheque or draft
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
(of a bank or other institution) to accept and pay (a cheque, bill, or draft) when it is presented.

Examples

to honour a cheque
Her payment was delayed because the company refused to honour the check.
Not all institutions are willing to honour foreign drafts due to security concerns.
The bank will honour your cheque as long as there are sufficient funds in your account.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dishonour: to refuse to accept or pay a cheque or bill
