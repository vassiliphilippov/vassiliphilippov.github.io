fuel

noun
substance used to produce energy
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːəl / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəl / (BrE)
A substance burned or otherwise consumed to produce energy, especially for heating, lighting, or powering engines.

Examples

car fuel
Bioethanol is a renewable fuel used in some environmentally friendly vehicles.
Ships often carry enough fuel for long journeys across the ocean.
The generator ran out of fuel after several hours of use.

Related Terms

  • synonym – energy: the ability to do work or produce change, typically used with reference to fuel
