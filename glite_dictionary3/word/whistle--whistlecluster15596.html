to whistle

verb
send or move as if with a whistle (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
to cause something to move away, be sent, or brought as if propelled or accompanied by the sound of whistling, often used figuratively

Examples

to whistle away worries
His carefree attitude quickly whistled away any doubts about the plan.
She tried to whistle her fears away with a cheerful tune.
The coach's sharp words whistled the tension out of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blow away: to drive away or remove with a sudden current of air (or figuratively)
