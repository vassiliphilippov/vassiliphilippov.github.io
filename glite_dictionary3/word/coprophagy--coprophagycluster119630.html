coprophagy

noun
eating of feces (zoology, medicine)
Pronunciation: / kəˈprɑːfədʒi / (AmE), / kəˈprɒfədʒi / (BrE)
the eating of feces, especially as a behavior observed in some animals or as a symptom of certain mental disorders in humans

Examples

coprophagy in animals
Coprophagy is common among certain rodents as a means of obtaining nutrients produced by bacteria.
In some psychiatric conditions, coprophagy may emerge as a distressing symptom.
Veterinarians may be concerned if a household dog frequently engages in coprophagy.
