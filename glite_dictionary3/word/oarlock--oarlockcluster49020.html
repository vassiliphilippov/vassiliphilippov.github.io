oarlock

noun
device that holds an oar on a boat
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɔːlɒk / (BrE)
A device fixed to the side of a boat to hold an oar in place, acting as a pivot for rowing.

Examples

oarlock on a boat
As he rowed across the lake, the right oarlock began to squeak loudly.
She slid the oar into the oarlock and prepared to push off from the dock.
The boat cannot be used until the broken oarlock is repaired.
