to boycott

verb
refuse to do business with
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪˌkɒt / (BrE)
to refuse to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting or forcing change

Examples

to boycott a company
Many countries chose to boycott the Olympics as a form of political protest.
Several groups agreed to boycott the company's products until their demands were met.
The students decided to boycott the lecture in protest against the new policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – support: to give assistance or approval to something
