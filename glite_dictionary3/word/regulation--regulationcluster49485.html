regulation

noun
embryo's ability to recover from damage
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Embryology) The ability of an early embryo to continue normal development after its structure has been damaged or altered.

Examples

embryo regulation
Research in embryology has shown that regulation is a crucial factor for normal growth after minor injuries.
Thanks to regulation, some mammalian embryos can still develop normally even after experimental manipulation.
The concept of regulation helps explain how an embryo can compensate for the loss of some cells early in development.
