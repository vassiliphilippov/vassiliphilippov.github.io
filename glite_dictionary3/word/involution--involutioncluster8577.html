involution

noun
social/economic stagnation with internal intensification
Pronunciation: / cc6an.v59c8lud0.8359n / (AmE), / cc6an.v59c8lud0.8359n / (BrE)
A social or economic process in which increasing internal complexity or competition leads to little or no overall growth or improvement; intensification without productive development.

Examples

economic involution
Economists warned that the country's job market had entered involution, with fierce competition but no wage growth.
After years of internal competition, the tech sector fell into involution, innovating more but growing less.
Many regional involutions of agriculture were described in the anthropologist's detailed field study.

Related Terms

  • synonym – diminishing returns: a situation in which each additional input produces progressively smaller increases in output
  • antonym – evolution: gradual outward development or progressive change; often contrasted with inward intensification
  • synonym – stagnation: lack of growth, development, or progress in an economy or sector
