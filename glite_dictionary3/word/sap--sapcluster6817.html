sap

noun
fluid in plants transporting nutrients
Pronunciation: / sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
the watery fluid containing dissolved sugars, salts, and minerals that circulates through the vascular system of a plant

Examples

sap from trees
In spring, maple trees are tapped so their sap can be collected and turned into syrup.
Scientists studied the sap flow to understand how nutrients move within the plant.
When a leaf is damaged, the sap sometimes leaks out and dries along the edges.
