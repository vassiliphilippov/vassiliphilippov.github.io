miosis

noun
constriction of the pupil
Pronunciation: / maɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the reflex contraction of the pupil of the eye, typically in response to bright light or certain drugs

Examples

pupil miosis
Certain medications may induce miosis, making it harder to see in low light.
Prolonged exposure to bright sunlight can cause miosis as the eyes adjust to the intensity.
The doctor observed miosis in response to the flashlight during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mydriasis: dilation of the pupil of the eye
