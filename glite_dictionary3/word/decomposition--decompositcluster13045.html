decomposition

noun
breaking down a mathematical entity into components
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking down a vector field or mathematical entity into simpler or distinct components for analysis

Examples

mathematical decomposition method
Fourier decomposition is used to analyze periodic functions in terms of sines and cosines.
In fluid dynamics, decomposition of the velocity field helps to understand the flow's underlying structures.
The Helmholtz decomposition allows any vector field to be written as a sum of an irrotational and a solenoidal part.

Related Terms

  • synonym – analysis: detailed examination of something complex
  • antonym – composition: the act of putting parts together to form a whole
