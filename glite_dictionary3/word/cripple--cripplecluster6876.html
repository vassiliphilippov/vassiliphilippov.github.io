to cripple

verb
deprive of the use of a limb
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpəl / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose the use of a limb, especially a leg, often resulting in permanent disability

Examples

to cripple a leg
A severe stroke can cripple a person's ability to speak clearly or move their limbs.
His right arm was crippled in the crash and never fully recovered.
The accident crippled her for life, leaving her unable to walk without assistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disable: to make unable to use part of the body; to incapacitate
  • antonym – heal: to restore to health or normal function
  • synonym – paralyze: to make unable to move or feel part or all of the body
