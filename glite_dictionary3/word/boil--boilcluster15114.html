boil

noun
the boiling temperature of a liquid
Pronunciation: / bɔɪl / (AmE), / bɔɪl / (BrE)
the temperature at which a liquid changes to a vapor, especially at sea level; the point at which a liquid boils

Examples

bring to a boil
Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for ten minutes.
Milk will rise quickly once it reaches a boil, so watch carefully.
The soup started to bubble as it neared a boil.
