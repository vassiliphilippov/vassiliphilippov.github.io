scalawag

noun
white Southerner supporting Reconstruction post–Civil War
Pronunciation: / ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (AmE), / ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (BrE)
(historically, US) A white Southerner who supported Reconstruction policies after the American Civil War, often perceived as acting out of self-interest.

Examples

Southern scalawag politician
Historians now debate whether the actions of scalawags were more pragmatic than opportunistic.
Many scalawags were accused of exploiting new political opportunities after the war.
Some considered him a scalawag for backing the Northern Republicans during Reconstruction.
