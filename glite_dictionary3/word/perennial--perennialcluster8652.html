perennial

adjective
lasting several years (of plants)
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛniəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəl / (BrE)
(of a plant) living for several years, typically more than two, and flowering each year.

Examples

perennial flowering herb
Lavender is a perennial herb that can thrive for many seasons in your garden.
Most gardeners prefer perennial flowers because they do not need to be replanted each year.
The perennial shrubs produce blossoms every spring without fail.

Related Terms

  • antonym – annual: plant that completes its life cycle in one year
  • degree lower – biennial: plant that takes two years to complete its life cycle
