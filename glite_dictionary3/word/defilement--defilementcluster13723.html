defilement

noun
condition of being polluted or impure
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfaɪlmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪlmənt / (BrE)
The state of being made unclean, impure, or polluted, especially by contact with something considered contaminating.

Examples

defilement of nature
He viewed the destruction of the ancient forest as a profound defilement of nature.
Many cultures have strict taboos against the defilement of holy places.
The sacred river suffered defilement after the factories discharged their waste.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pollution: the process or act of polluting or the state of being polluted
  • antonym – purity: the state of being pure or untainted
