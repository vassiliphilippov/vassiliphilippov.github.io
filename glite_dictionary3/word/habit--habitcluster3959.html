habit

noun
regular or automatic behavioral pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
a regular or automatic pattern of behavior or practice, especially one acquired by frequent repetition and often hard to give up

Examples

form a daily habit
After years of waking at dawn, it became a habit he couldn’t break.
Biting his nails was a nervous habit he wanted to stop.
She developed the habit of checking her phone before getting out of bed.
Changing an old habit can be difficult but rewarding.
He has developed the habit of taking a walk after dinner every day.
It is my habit to read before going to bed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – custom: a traditional or habitual practice commonly observed by people
  • synonym – routine: a sequence of actions regularly followed; a fixed program
  • degree lower – tendency: an inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior
