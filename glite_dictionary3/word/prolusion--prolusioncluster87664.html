prolusion

noun
short introduction to a book
Pronunciation: / proʊˈluːʒən / (AmE), / prəˈluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a short introductory essay or preface that comes before the main text of a book

Examples

prolusion before chapters
A brief prolusion sets the tone for the themes explored in the following chapters.
The author's prolusion provides helpful background information for the reader.
Without the prolusion, some passages in the book might be difficult to interpret.
