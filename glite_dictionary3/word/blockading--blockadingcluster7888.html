blockading

adjective
imposing a blockade, especially at sea
Pronunciation: / blɑːˈkeɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / blɒˈkeɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
Imposing a blockade by preventing access to or from a port, harbor, or region, especially by surrounding it with ships.

Examples

blockading ships surround
A line of blockading ships was stationed outside the harbor throughout the conflict.
The blockading fleet made it impossible for merchants to leave port.
The navy sent several blockading vessels to cut off the enemy's supplies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blockade: the act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving
  • synonym – siege: a military operation in which enemy forces surround a town or building
