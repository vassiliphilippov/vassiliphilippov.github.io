outcast

adjective
person excluded from society
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A person who has been rejected or excluded from a society or group.

Examples

outcast child
After his controversial opinion, he felt like an outcast among his former friends.
She was treated as an outcast because she refused to follow the community's strict rules.
The novel tells the story of an outcast struggling to find acceptance in a new city.
