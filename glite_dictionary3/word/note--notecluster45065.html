note

noun
written promise to pay money
Pronunciation: / noʊt / (AmE), / nəʊt / (BrE)
a written promise to pay a specified sum of money either on demand or at a fixed time

Examples

sign a note
He defaulted on his note and faced legal action from the lender.
The bank required her to sign a note before approving the loan.
They agreed to extend the maturity date of the note by one year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bond: a debt security in which the issuer owes the holders a debt and is obliged to pay interest and/or repay the principal
  • synonym – promissory note: a written, signed promise to pay a certain sum of money at a future date or on demand
