jointworm

noun
larva of chalcid flies harmful to grain straw
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔɪntˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪntˌwɜːm / (BrE)
The larva of certain chalcid flies that damages the straw of wheat and other grains.

Examples

jointworm damages wheat
Farmers use specific methods to control the spread of jointworm in their fields.
The presence of jointworm can significantly reduce both the quality and yield of harvested grain.
