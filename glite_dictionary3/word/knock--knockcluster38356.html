to knock

verb
(of engine) make metallic sound from improper ignition
Pronunciation: / nɑk / (AmE), / nɒk / (BrE)
(of an internal combustion engine) to make a sharp, metallic sound because the fuel is igniting too early or irregularly in the cylinder.

Examples

the engine knocks loudly
High-performance engines may knock under heavy acceleration if the ignition timing is incorrect.
If you hear your engine knocking, you should have it checked by a mechanic.
Older cars are more likely to knock when running on low-octane fuel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ping: (of an engine) to make a metallic knocking sound
  • synonym – pinking: engine knocking due to pre-ignition
