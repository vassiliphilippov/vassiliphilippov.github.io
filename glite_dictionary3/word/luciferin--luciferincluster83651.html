luciferin

noun
bioluminescent pigment in some organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌluːsəˈfɪrɪn / (AmE), / ˌluːsɪˈfɪərɪn / (BrE)
a pigment found in luminescent organisms, such as fireflies, which emits light without heat when it undergoes oxidation

Examples

luciferin in fireflies
Fireflies produce light through a chemical reaction involving luciferin and an enzyme called luciferase.
Scientists extracted luciferin from jellyfish to study its properties in the laboratory.
The presence of luciferin is what enables some marine organisms to glow in the dark.
