scarp

noun
steep slope in front of a fortification
Pronunciation: / skɑrp / (AmE), / skɑːp / (BrE)
a steep artificial slope or wall in front of a fortification, serving as a defensive barrier

Examples

scarp wall of fortress
Engineers constructed a new scarp to strengthen the old fortress.
The scarp made direct attacks on the walls nearly impossible.
The defenders took position behind the scarp to repel the assault.

Related Terms

  • synonym – escarpment: a long, steep slope at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights
