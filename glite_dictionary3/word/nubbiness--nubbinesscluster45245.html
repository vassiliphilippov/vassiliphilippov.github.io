nubbiness

noun
rough or uneven texture in fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌbinəs / (AmE), / ˈnʌbinɪs / (BrE)
The quality of having a rough or uneven texture, especially in fabrics, due to small lumps or bumps.

Examples

nubbiness of fabric
She ran her fingers over the fabric, noticing the pronounced nubbiness in its weave.
Some people prefer towels with a bit of nubbiness for a gentle exfoliating effect.
The nubbiness of the wool gave the sweater a unique appearance and feel.

Related Terms

  • antonym – smoothness: the quality of being even or free from roughness
