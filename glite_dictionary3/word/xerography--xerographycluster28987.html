xerography

noun
a dry photocopying process
Pronunciation: / zɪˈrɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / zɪəˈrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a dry photocopying process in which images are formed by the action of light on a specially charged plate and developed with powder that adheres to charged areas

Examples

xerography machine copy
Most modern photocopiers are based on the principle of xerography.
The invention of xerography revolutionized the office environment by making paper copying fast and convenient.
Unlike traditional photography, xerography does not require any liquid chemicals for development.
