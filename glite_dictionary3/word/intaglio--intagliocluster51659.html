intaglio

noun
sunken engraving or carving on a surface
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtælioʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtalioʊ / (BrE)
an engraving or carving in which the design is formed by cutting into a surface so that it is sunken below the level of the surrounding material, especially on a stone or gem

Examples

intaglio gem engraving
He admired the delicate intaglio work carved into the sapphire.
Many collectors seek rare gemstones with fine intaglio engravings.
The museum displayed a Roman intaglio set in an ancient ring.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cameo: a piece of jewelry with a raised (positive) relief image, especially one carved on a gemstone
