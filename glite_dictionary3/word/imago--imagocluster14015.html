imago

noun
adult insect after metamorphosis
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
The adult stage of an insect following metamorphosis, in which it is sexually mature.

Examples

adult imago stage
After pupating, the insect reached its final imago stage and was ready to reproduce.
Entomologists carefully examined the imago to identify the species.
The butterfly's imago emerges from the chrysalis with fully developed wings.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – larva: the immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect
  • degree lower – pupa: the non-feeding, usually inactive stage between larva and imago
