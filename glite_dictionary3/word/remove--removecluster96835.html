to remove

verb
dispose of; get rid of
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmuːv /; / ɹɪˈmuv / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːv /; / ɹɪˈmuːv / (BrE)
to get rid of, eliminate, or take away something or someone, including abstract things like problems or requirements

Examples

to remove a stain
It's important to remove expired items from the refrigerator regularly.
She decided to remove all the clutter from her office before the new year.
The editor suggested we remove unnecessary paragraphs to make the article clearer.
The committee decided to remove that requirement from the contract.
The new policy will remove barriers to entry for small businesses.
Therapy helped him remove his feelings of guilt.
She was removed from the committee due to her absence.
The principal can remove a disruptive student from class if necessary.
The security guard had to remove several people from the building during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • antonym – add: to put something in; opposite of remove
  • synonym – discard: to throw something away or get rid of it
  • synonym – dismiss: to remove someone from a position or place
  • synonym – eliminate: to remove something completely
  • synonym – evict: to force someone to leave a place or property
