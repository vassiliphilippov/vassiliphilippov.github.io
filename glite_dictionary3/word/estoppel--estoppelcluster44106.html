estoppel

noun
legal rule preventing contradictory claims
Pronunciation: / ɪˈstɑːpəl / (AmE), / ɪˈstɒpəl / (BrE)
A legal rule that prevents a person from denying or asserting something contrary to what has been previously established as fact, especially in a court of law.

Examples

legal estoppel applies
A finding of estoppel can have serious legal consequences in civil cases.
Because of estoppel, she could not deny that the contract was valid.
The court applied estoppel to prevent the defendant from contradicting his earlier statement.
