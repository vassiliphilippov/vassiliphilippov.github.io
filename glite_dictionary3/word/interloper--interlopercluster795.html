interloper

noun
a person who intrudes or meddles
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntɚˌloʊpɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪntələʊpə / (BrE)
a person who becomes involved in a place or situation where they are not wanted or are considered not to belong

Examples

unwelcome interloper
Some saw her as an interloper in their ongoing debate.
The local community regarded the reporter as an unwelcome interloper.
Without an invitation, any guest would be considered an interloper at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intruder: a person who enters a place without permission
  • synonym – meddler: a person who interferes unnecessarily
