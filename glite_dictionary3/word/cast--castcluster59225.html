to cast

verb
choose or decide by random method
Pronunciation: / kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
to select or decide something by a random method, such as drawing lots

Examples

to cast lots
If the vote was tied, the group would cast dice to break the deadlock.
Instead of arguing, they cast lots for the last slice of cake.
Traditionally, they would cast lots to decide who would go first.
