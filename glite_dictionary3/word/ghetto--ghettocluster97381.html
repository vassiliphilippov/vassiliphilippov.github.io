ghetto

noun
historical Jewish quarter in European cities
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtəʊ / (BrE)
A part of a city, especially in Europe from the 16th to the 20th centuries, where Jews were required by law to live.

Examples

historic ghetto area
Historians have studied daily life in the Prague ghetto before emancipation.
Many Jewish families struggled to survive in the ghetto of Warsaw during the Second World War.
The Venetian ghetto was one of the first areas in Europe where Jews were segregated.
