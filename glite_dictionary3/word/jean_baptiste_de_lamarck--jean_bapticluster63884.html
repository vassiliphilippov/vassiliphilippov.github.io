Jean Baptiste de Lamarck

proper noun
French naturalist; early evolution theorist
Pronunciation: / ʒɑn bæpˈtiːst də ləˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ bapˈtist də laˈmaʁk / (BrE)
A French naturalist (1744–1829) known for proposing that evolution occurred through the inheritance of acquired characteristics.

Examples

Jean Baptiste de Lamarck proposed evolution
Jean Baptiste de Lamarck was among the first to propose a comprehensive theory of evolution.
Many biology textbooks discuss the ideas of Jean Baptiste de Lamarck alongside those of Darwin.
Though later disproven, Jean Baptiste de Lamarck's theory influenced future research in evolutionary biology.
