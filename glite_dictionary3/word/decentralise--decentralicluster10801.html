to decentralise

verb
transfer authority from central to local levels
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈsɛntrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to transfer authority, power, or responsibility from a central organization or government to local or regional branches

Examples

to decentralise power
After the revolution, food distribution was decentralised across all regions.
Many governments are working to decentralise power to better serve local communities.
The company decided to decentralise its management structure to improve efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym – centralise: to concentrate control or authority in a central organization
