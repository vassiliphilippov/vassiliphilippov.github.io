derelict

adjective
neglectful of duty or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛr.ə.lɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛr.ɪ.lɪkt / (BrE)
failing to do what duty or responsibility requires; neglectful or remiss.

Examples

derelict in duty
It's derelict of him not to respond to urgent emails.
She was found to be derelict in her duties as a supervisor.
The teacher was accused of being derelict in caring for her students' safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conscientious: wishing to do one's work or duty well and thoroughly
  • synonym – neglectful: failing to give proper care or attention
  • synonym – remiss: lacking care or attention to duty
