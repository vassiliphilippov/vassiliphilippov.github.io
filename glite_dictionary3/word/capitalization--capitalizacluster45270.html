capitalization

noun
estimation of a business's value
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpɪtl̩əˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkapɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an estimation of the total value of a business, especially as determined by the market value of its outstanding shares

Examples

company capitalization
A sudden change in stock price can significantly affect a business's capitalization.
Analysts use capitalization to compare the size of different firms.
The company's capitalization has doubled in the past five years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – market capitalization: the total market value of a company's outstanding shares
