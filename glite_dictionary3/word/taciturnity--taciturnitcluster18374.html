taciturnity

noun
quality of being reserved or uncommunicative
Pronunciation: / ˌtæsɪˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / ˌtæsɪˈtɜːnɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being reserved and uncommunicative; reluctance to speak

Examples

his taciturnity at meetings
Despite his taciturnity, people respected his thoughtful opinions when he spoke.
His taciturnity at the meeting was noted by everyone present.
She was known for her taciturnity, rarely joining in the lively conversations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loquacity: the quality of talking a great deal; talkativeness
  • synonym – reticence: the quality of being reserved, especially with regard to speaking freely
