Leishmania

noun
protozoan parasite causing leishmaniasis
Pronunciation: / liʃˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / liˈʃmɑːnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of flagellate protozoan parasites that cause leishmaniasis in humans and animals

Examples

Leishmania parasite infection
Leishmania is transmitted to humans through the bite of infected sandflies.
Scientists are developing new treatments to combat Leishmania infection.
The presence of Leishmania in tissue samples confirms the diagnosis of leishmaniasis.
