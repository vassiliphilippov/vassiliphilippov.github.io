divide

noun
a ridge separating river systems
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvaɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪd / (BrE)
a ridge of land that separates two adjoining drainage basins or river systems

Examples

mountain divide
Several rivers originate near the divide, then flow in opposite directions toward different seas.
The Continental divide plays a crucial role in determining the flow of major North American rivers.
We hiked all day to reach the divide between the two valleys.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ridge: a long, narrow elevation of land
  • synonym – watershed: an area of land separating waters flowing to different rivers, basins, or seas
