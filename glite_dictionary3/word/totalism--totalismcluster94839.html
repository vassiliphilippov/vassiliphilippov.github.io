totalism

noun
belief in absolute government control
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊt̬əˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or support for the complete and unrestricted power of government over all aspects of life.

Examples

political totalism
Opponents of totalism advocate for checks and balances within the government.
Some political scientists argue that totalism can lead to the suppression of individual freedoms.
