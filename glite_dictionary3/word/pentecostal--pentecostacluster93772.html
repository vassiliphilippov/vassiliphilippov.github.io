Pentecostal

noun
member of a Pentecostal Christian group
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɑː.stəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɒs.təl / (BrE)
a member of a Christian religious group that emphasizes the direct experience of the Holy Spirit, especially through speaking in tongues, healing, and prophecy

Examples

devout Pentecostal believer
As a Pentecostal, she emphasized personal experiences of faith over ritual.
He was raised as a Pentecostal and attended church several times a week.
Many Pentecostals believe in the power of divine healing and speaking in tongues.

  • synonym – Pentecostalism: the beliefs and practices of Pentecostal religious groups
