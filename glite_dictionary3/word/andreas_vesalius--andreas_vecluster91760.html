Andreas Vesalius

proper noun
Flemish anatomist, pioneer of modern anatomy
Pronunciation: / ænˌdreɪəs vəˈseɪliəs / (AmE), / ænˌdreɪəs vɛˈseɪliəs / (BrE)
(1514–1564) A Flemish surgeon and anatomist regarded as the father of modern anatomy for his groundbreaking work in the study of the human body.

Examples

Andreas Vesalius studied anatomy
Andreas Vesalius revolutionized the study of human anatomy through his meticulous dissections and detailed drawings.
Many historians credit Andreas Vesalius as the founder of modern anatomy due to his influential publications.
Medical students often study the work of Andreas Vesalius to understand the development of anatomical science.
