failure

noun
inability to pay debts (insolvency)
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪljɚ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪliə / (BrE)
the inability to pay all debts when they are due, often resulting in insolvency or bankruptcy

Examples

failure of bank
After years of mounting losses, the bank's failure became inevitable.
He studied the causes of failure in major corporations during the economic crisis.
The law requires companies to report their failure to meet financial obligations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bankruptcy: the legal state of being unable to pay debts
  • synonym – insolvency: the condition of being unable to pay debts
