royalty

noun
payment for use of intellectual property or resources
Pronunciation: / ˈrɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈrɔːɪəlti / (BrE)
a sum of money paid to the holder of a patent, copyright, or resource for the right to use, publish, or extract their property

Examples

earn royalties from books
She signed a contract to receive royalties from her patented invention.
The artist was paid a royalty for each time his song was streamed online.
The author earns a royalty on every copy of the novel sold.

Related Terms

  • synonym – license fee: a fee paid for the authorization to use something, especially intellectual property
