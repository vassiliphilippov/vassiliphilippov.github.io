flimflam

noun
deceptive scheme or swindle
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmˌflæm / (AmE), / ˈflɪm.flæm / (BrE)
A swindle or deceptive scheme, especially one involving cheating at gambling or persuading someone to buy something worthless.

Examples

a flimflam scheme
He lost all his savings in a flimflam orchestrated by professional con artists.
That so-called investment opportunity turned out to be nothing but a flimflam.
The police warned citizens to beware of flimflam schemes targeting the elderly.

  • synonym – fraud: deceit or trickery for personal gain
  • antonym – honesty: the quality of being truthful or genuine
  • synonym – scam: a dishonest scheme or fraud
