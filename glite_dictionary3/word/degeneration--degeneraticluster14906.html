degeneration

noun
reversion to a simpler biological form
Pronunciation: / dɪˌdʒɛnɚˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something, especially a biological structure, becomes less complex or returns to a simpler form

Examples

degeneration of tissue
Over time, some species show degeneration in features that are no longer necessary for survival.
The scientist studied the degeneration of certain organs in cave-dwelling creatures.
The theory suggests degeneration is a natural consequence in isolated populations with limited resources.

Related Terms

  • antonym – evolution: process of gradual development from a simpler to a more complex form
  • synonym – regression: return to an earlier or less advanced state
