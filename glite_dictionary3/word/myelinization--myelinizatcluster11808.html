myelinization

noun
development of a myelin sheath around nerves
Pronunciation: / maɪˌɛlɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˌɛlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a myelin sheath forms around a nerve fiber, enabling faster transmission of nerve impulses

Examples

myelinization of nerve fibers
Certain diseases can disrupt normal myelinization and affect nervous system function.
Research shows that myelinization continues well into adolescence in some parts of the brain.
The rapid myelinization of nerve fibers occurs during early childhood development.
