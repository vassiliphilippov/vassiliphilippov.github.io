foreground

noun
part of a scene nearest to the viewer
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the part of a scene or picture that is closest to the viewer

Examples

the foreground of a picture
Bright flowers dominated the foreground of the painting.
Several children played in the foreground while the mountains loomed in the background.
The tree in the foreground gives the photo a sense of depth.

Related Terms

  • antonym – background: the part of a scene or picture that is farthest from the viewer
