throw-weight

noun
missile payload weight (excluding rocket)
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊ ˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ ˌweɪt / (BrE)
the weight of the payload that a missile can deliver, excluding the weight of the rocket itself

Examples

throw-weight of a missile
Improvements in propulsion did not significantly increase the throw-weight of the missile.
Nuclear arms negotiations often focus on the throw-weight limitations of each country's missile systems.
The new missile has a greater throw-weight than its predecessor, allowing it to carry more warheads.
