hilum

noun
mark on seed showing point of attachment
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪləm / (BrE)
the scar or mark on a seed that shows where it was attached to the ovary wall or funicle

Examples

hilum on a seed
Botanists use the hilum to determine the seed's orientation during development.
The hilum is often visible as a small spot on a bean seed.
When examining the seed under a microscope, the hilum can be clearly identified.
