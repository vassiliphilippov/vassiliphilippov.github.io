to bail

verb
deliver in trust for a special purpose
Pronunciation: / beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
To deliver something into the custody of another for a special purpose and for a limited period, especially under legal agreement.

Examples

to bail property
She agreed to bail her jewelry to the loan agency as collateral.
The bank will bail the documents to the court for review next week.
Under the agreement, he must bail the equipment to the company for six months.
