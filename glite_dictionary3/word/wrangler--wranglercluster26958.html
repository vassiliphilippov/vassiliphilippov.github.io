wrangler

noun
someone who argues loudly
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person who argues noisily or angrily, especially about petty issues

Examples

argument wrangler
Jake's reputation as a wrangler made people avoid debates with him at meetings.
She disliked being called a wrangler, even though she enjoyed lively discussions.
The two wranglers kept interrupting each other, making it impossible to reach a decision.
