to jampack

verb
fill or crowd to full capacity
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæm.pæk / (AmE), / ˈdʒæm.pæk / (BrE)
to fill or crowd something completely, especially so there is no room left

Examples

to jampack a stadium
Fans began to jampack the stadium hours before the concert started.
The bus was jampacked during the morning rush hour.
They managed to jampack the hall with over two hundred guests.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overfill: to fill something beyond its capacity
  • synonym – pack: to fill a container or space tightly
