to jog

verb
continue aimlessly in speech or writing
Pronunciation: / dʒɑg / (AmE), / dʒɒg / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) to continue in a dull, aimless, or monotonous manner without clear direction or purpose.

Examples

to jog along aimlessly
His speech began to jog, losing focus as he moved from one unrelated topic to another.
The author tends to jog through lengthy descriptions that add little to the plot.
The letter jogged on for several pages without making any clear point.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ramble: to talk or write aimlessly, often with many digressions
