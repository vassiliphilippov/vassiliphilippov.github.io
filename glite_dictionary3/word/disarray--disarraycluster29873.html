to disarray

verb
to bring disorder to
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈreɪ / (BrE)
to bring disorder or confusion to something; to make untidy or disorganized.

Examples

to disarray a room
A wild wind had disarrayed the papers scattered across the desk.
Her unexpected arrival completely disarrayed his schedule for the day.
The sudden storm disarrayed the carefully planned outdoor event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disorder: to make untidy or chaotic; to disturb order
  • antonym – organize: to arrange or put in order
