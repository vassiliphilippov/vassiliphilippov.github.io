Yahveh

noun
name for God in the Old Testament
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːveɪ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːveɪ / (BrE)
a name for God in the Old Testament, transliterated from the Hebrew YHVH

Examples

Yahveh in the Bible
Jewish tradition avoids pronouncing Yahveh, substituting titles like 'Adonai' instead.
Many biblical scholars use Yahveh to refer to the God of Israel as described in the Hebrew Scriptures.
The spelling Yahveh appears in some early English translations of the Bible.
