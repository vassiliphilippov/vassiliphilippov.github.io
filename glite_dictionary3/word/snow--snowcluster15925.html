snow

noun
frozen white layer covering the ground
Pronunciation: / snoʊ / (AmE), / snəʊ / (BrE)
soft, white frozen water crystals that fall from the sky and cover the ground in cold weather

Examples

fresh snow on ground
After a night of heavy snow, the roads were closed until midday.
Fresh snow blanketed the entire landscape this morning.
The children built a snowman out of the fallen snow in the yard.
Heavy snow fell overnight, covering the roads and trees in white.
The children ran outside to play in the fresh snow after school.
Travel delays are common when a lot of snow accumulates quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hail: precipitation in the form of hard pellets of ice
  • synonym – ice: frozen water, solid state of water
  • antonym – rain: precipitation in the form of water drops from clouds
  • synonym – sleet: a form of precipitation consisting of ice pellets, often mixed with rain or snow
