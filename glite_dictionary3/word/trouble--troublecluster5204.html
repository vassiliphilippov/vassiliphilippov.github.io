trouble

noun
inconvenient or bothersome effort
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
Effort or inconvenience taken to achieve something, often causing difficulty or bother.

Examples

go to trouble
He went to great trouble to make sure everything was perfect for the party.
Please don't go to any trouble on my account.
She finished the paperwork without much trouble.

Related Terms

  • synonym – difficulty: the state or fact of being hard to do, deal with, or understand
  • antonym – ease: absence of difficulty or effort
  • synonym – effort: a vigorous or determined attempt to accomplish something
