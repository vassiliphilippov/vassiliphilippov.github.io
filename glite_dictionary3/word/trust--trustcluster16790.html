to trust

verb
(archaic) extend credit
Pronunciation: / trʌst / (AmE), / trʌst / (BrE)
(archaic) To extend credit to someone; to allow someone to owe money with the expectation of future payment.

Examples

to trust a customer
In earlier times, shopkeepers would often trust customers who promised to pay later.
The merchant refused to trust him after his previous unpaid debts.
You should not trust every traveler who asks for goods on credit.
