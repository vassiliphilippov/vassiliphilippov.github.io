claystone

noun
fine-grained sedimentary rock
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkleɪstəʊn / (BrE)
A fine-grained sedimentary rock composed mainly of compacted clay particles.

Examples

hard claystone layer
In some regions, claystone forms steep cliffs due to its compact nature.
Layers of claystone indicate ancient environments with slow-moving water.
The geologist collected samples of claystone to analyze their mineral composition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shale: a fine-grained sedimentary rock that can split into thin layers
  • degree higher – siltstone: a sedimentary rock composed mainly of silt-sized particles
