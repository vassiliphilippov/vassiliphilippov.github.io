bastardised

adjective
mixed or impure in origin or style
Pronunciation: / ˈbæstɚˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.stə.daɪzd / (BrE)
(of something) made impure or less valuable by being mixed with inferior or unrelated elements; deriving from more than one source or style, often in a way considered debased.

Examples

bastardised version
Some critics argue that modern architecture is a bastardised version of classical style.
The novel was criticized for being a bastardised blend of genres that failed to capture the essence of either.
The restaurant offered a bastardised version of traditional cuisine, adding unnecessary ingredients.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hybrid: something made by combining different elements
  • antonym – pure: not mixed with anything else; without adulteration
