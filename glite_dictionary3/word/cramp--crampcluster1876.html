to cramp

verb
to suffer a sudden muscle contraction
Pronunciation: / kræmp / (AmE), / kramp / (BrE)
to experience or cause a sudden, painful muscle contraction

Examples

to cramp painfully
After sitting for too long, his back muscles started to cramp painfully.

Swimmers often cramp if they don't stretch properly before getting into the pool.

Her fingers would cramp whenever she wrote for more than an hour.
If you take on too much work at once, it may cramp your creativity.
