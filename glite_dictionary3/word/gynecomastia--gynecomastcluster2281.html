gynecomastia

noun
enlarged male breast tissue (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌɡaɪnəkəˈmæstiə / (AmE), / ˌɡɪnəkəˈmæstɪə / (BrE)
abnormal enlargement of breast tissue in males, often caused by hormonal imbalance or certain medications

Examples

gynecomastia in men
Certain medications are known to cause gynecomastia as a side effect.
The patient was diagnosed with gynecomastia after noticing swelling in his chest.
Treatments for gynecomastia may include hormone therapy or surgery.

  • antonym – mastectomy: surgical removal of breast tissue
