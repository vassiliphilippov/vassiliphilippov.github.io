plop

adverb
with a short hollow thud
Pronunciation: / plɑp / (AmE), / plɒp / (BrE)
With a short, hollow, and often soft thudding sound, as of something falling or landing heavily yet softly.

Examples

plop into a bowl
Plop went the cherry as it dropped into the bowl.
He sat down plop onto the grass, exhausted from the run.
The pebble landed in the pond with a gentle plop.
