high-energy

adjective
producing large amounts of energy (chemically)
Pronunciation: / haɪˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / haɪˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
(of a substance or reaction) providing or producing a large amount of energy when undergoing a chemical change.

Examples

high-energy material
Explosives are considered high-energy materials due to their rapid release of energy.
Researchers developed a high-energy battery to improve electric vehicle performance.
The fuel used in rockets is a high-energy compound that releases a substantial amount of heat.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low-energy: producing or providing a small amount of energy when undergoing a chemical reaction
