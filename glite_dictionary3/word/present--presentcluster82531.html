to present

verb
bring something to someone's attention or awareness
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to show, introduce, or display something or someone to others (such as ideas, people, performances, or evidence)

Examples

to present information
It's important to present all available evidence to the committee.
She presented her concerns in a way that made everyone reconsider their opinion.
The lawyer presented several key points during his closing statement.
After the audit, the manager was presented with a series of serious allegations.
She presented him with all the facts, leaving him unable to deny the accusation.
The lawyer presented the suspect with new evidence during the interrogation.
The artist chose to present the city as a place of peace and beauty in her mural.
The documentary presents poverty through a series of haunting images and personal stories.
This sculpture presents the mythological figure in a completely new and unexpected way.
