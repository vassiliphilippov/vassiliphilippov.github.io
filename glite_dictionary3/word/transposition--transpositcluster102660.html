transposition

noun
abnormal organ position
Pronunciation: / trænspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / trænspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition in which an organ or organs are in an abnormal position within the body, especially one that deviates from the usual arrangement.

Examples

transposition of organs
Careful imaging is required to detect a transposition of the kidneys.
The surgeon diagnosed transposition of the great arteries in the newborn.
This rare condition, called intestinal transposition, can lead to digestive problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym – malposition: incorrect or abnormal position of a body part
