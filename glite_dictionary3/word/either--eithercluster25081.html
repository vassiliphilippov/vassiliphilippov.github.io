either

adverb
used after a negative statement meaning 'also' or 'likewise'
Pronunciation: / ˈiðər / (AmE), / ˈaɪðə / (BrE)
used after a negative statement to indicate that a similar negative statement applies to another person or thing; also, likewise (in the negative)

Examples

not happy either
He didn't call her yesterday, and she didn't call him either.
She doesn't like coffee, and I don't either.
They haven't finished the project yet, and we haven't either.

Related Terms

  • synonym – too: used to mean also or in addition (mainly in positive statements)
