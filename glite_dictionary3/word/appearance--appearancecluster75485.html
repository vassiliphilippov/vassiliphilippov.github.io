appearance

noun
outward show or pretense
Pronunciation: / əˈpɪrəns / (AmE), / əˈpɪərəns / (BrE)
the act of pretending or presenting a false or outward show to create a favorable impression

Examples

appearance of happiness
Despite his cheerful appearance, he was feeling deeply unhappy inside.
Sometimes a formal apology is made just for the sake of appearance.
They hosted a grand party to keep up appearances, even though they were having financial troubles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – façade: a false, superficial, or artificial appearance or effect
  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • antonym – reality: the state of things as they actually exist
