episodic

adjective
relating to a single episode or event
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
made up of separate episodes or relating to a single episode, especially in writing, TV, or storytelling

Examples

episodic TV series
Some TV shows are highly episodic, allowing viewers to enjoy episodes out of order.
The historian preferred an episodic account, focusing on individual reigns rather than a continuous narrative.
Their podcast takes an episodic approach, with each episode telling a self-contained story.
Her memoirs are episodic, recounting events from different periods of her life without a strict chronological order.
The novel has an episodic structure, with each chapter focusing on a different character.
The show's episodic format allows viewers to enjoy individual stories without needing to follow every episode.
