demyelination

noun
loss of myelin from nerve fibers
Pronunciation: / diˌmaɪəˌlɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌmaɪəˌlɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
loss or destruction of the myelin sheath that surrounds nerve fibers, leading to impaired nerve function

Examples

demyelination of nerve fibers
Multiple sclerosis is a disease characterized by demyelination of nerve fibers in the central nervous system.
Researchers are investigating therapies that could prevent or reverse demyelination in patients with neurological conditions.
The process of demyelination can result in symptoms such as weakness, numbness, and loss of coordination.
