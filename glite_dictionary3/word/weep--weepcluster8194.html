to weep

verb
to shed tears; cry from emotion
Pronunciation: / wiːp / (AmE), / wiːp / (BrE)
to shed tears, usually because of sadness, pain, or strong emotion

Examples

to weep with sadness
After hearing the tragic news, she began to weep uncontrollably.
Children sometimes weep when they feel overwhelmed by their emotions.
He tried to hide his face as he wept during the heartfelt speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cry: to shed tears, typically accompanied by sobs or loud vocalizations
  • antonym – laugh: to show emotion with vocal sounds, particularly when amused
