to dispatch

verb
send quickly to a destination
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)
to send someone or something quickly to a place for a particular purpose (including in computing, to send a task or message to be handled)

Examples

to dispatch emergency help
Paramedics were to the accident scene within minutes of receiving the call.
The company decided to dispatch a team of engineers to the construction site immediately.
The general ordered his troops to dispatch supplies to the front lines as quickly as possible.
The runtime system will dispatch the incoming request to the appropriate worker.
After validation, the server dispatched the job to a background queue for later processing.
When overloaded, the application starts dispatching tasks across multiple threads to improve throughput.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enqueue: to add a task or message to a queue for later processing
  • antonym – recall: to call back to the place of origin
  • synonym – route: to send something along a particular path to a destination or handler
  • synonym – schedule: to arrange for a task or job to run at a particular time or by a particular worker
  • synonym – send: cause to go or be taken to a destination
