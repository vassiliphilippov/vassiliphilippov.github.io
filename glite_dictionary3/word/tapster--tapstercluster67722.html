tapster

noun
person who serves drinks in a tavern
Pronunciation: / ˈtæp.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈtæp.stə / (BrE)
A person who serves drinks, especially beer, in a tavern or inn by drawing them from casks or kegs.

Examples

tapster pours beer
In the bustling inn, the tapster kept the kegs flowing for thirsty patrons.
Shakespeare's plays sometimes mention a tapster among the tavern folk.
The old tapster filled our tankards with ale as we gathered around the fire.
