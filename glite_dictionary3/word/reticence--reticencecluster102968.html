reticence

noun
reserve or restraint in communication
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛt̬.ə.səns / (AmE), / ˈrɛt.ɪ.səns / (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to speak freely or reveal one's thoughts; reserved or restrained in communication

Examples

her reticence speaking
Despite her reticence, she eventually shared her opinion with the group.
He showed reticence in discussing his personal life with reporters.
Her natural reticence made her a keen listener during meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – outspokenness: the quality of speaking out freely and openly
  • synonym – reserve: the quality of keeping one's thoughts or feelings private; restraint in speech
