serail

noun
women's quarters in a Muslim household
Pronunciation: / səˈraɪl / (AmE), / səˈraɪl / (BrE)
The section of a Muslim household, especially in former times, reserved for wives, concubines, and female relatives.

In the Ottoman Empire, the serail was strictly off-limits to men who were not family members.
