to bother

verb
make the effort to do something
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːðər / (AmE), / ˈbɒðə / (BrE)
to make the effort to do something, especially when it is not strictly necessary or when it involves inconvenience

Examples

to bother to help
Don't bother making dinner; I've already eaten.
He didn't even bother to say goodbye before leaving the party.
She rarely bothers to check her emails on weekends.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mind: to care or make an effort about something
  • antonym – neglect: to fail to give attention or effort to something
  • synonym – trouble: to take care or make the effort to do something
