Henry VI of England

proper noun
King of England, 1422–1461, 1470–1471
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛnri sɪksθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri sɪksθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
(1421–1471) King of England from 1422 to 1461 and again from 1470 to 1471, remembered for his turbulent reign during the Wars of the Roses and eventual murder in the Tower of London.

Examples

Henry VI of England ruled England
Henry VI of England became king as an infant after the death of his father, Henry V.
During the Wars of the Roses, Henry VI of England was deposed and later restored to the throne.
The tragic fate of Henry VI of England is often recounted in English history books.
