unwillingness

noun
reluctance; lack of willingness
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being willing to do something; reluctance or lack of readiness to act

Examples

unwillingness to help
Despite his unwillingness to participate, he was eventually persuaded by his friends.
Her unwillingness to apologize made it difficult to resolve the conflict.
The company's unwillingness to change its policies led to protests from employees.
