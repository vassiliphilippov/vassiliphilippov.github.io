Eleocharis acicularis

noun
fine-leaved aquatic plant for aquariums
Pronunciation: / ˌɛliːoʊˈkɛərɪs ˌæsɪˈkjʊlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛliəˈkæːrɪs ˌæsɪˈkjʊlɛərɪs / (BrE)
A fine-leaved aquatic plant (spike rush), often used as an aerator in aquariums.

Examples

Eleocharis acicularis in aquariums
Eleocharis acicularis is often chosen by aquarists for its fine, grass-like appearance in planted tanks.
Because Eleocharis acicularis spreads quickly, it can form a lush carpet at the bottom of an aquarium.
The delicate blades of Eleocharis acicularis provide excellent shelter for small fish and shrimp.
