peptisation

noun
conversion to a colloidal solution
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛptəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛptɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of converting a precipitate into a colloidal solution by dispersing its particles, usually with the help of an electrolyte.

Examples

peptisation of particles
Many industrial processes rely on peptisation to produce uniform dispersions of materials.
The peptisation of the precipitate resulted in a stable colloidal solution.
Without proper peptisation, the particles quickly aggregated and settled out.
