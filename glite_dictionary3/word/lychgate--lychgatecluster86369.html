lychgate

noun
roofed churchyard gateway for funerals
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtʃ.ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtʃ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
A roofed gateway at the entrance to a churchyard, formerly used to shelter a coffin before a funeral service.

Examples

lychgate at a churchyard
Many old English churches still have a lychgate at their entrance.
Mourners gathered at the lychgate to await the arrival of the funeral party.
The procession paused beneath the lychgate before entering the churchyard.
