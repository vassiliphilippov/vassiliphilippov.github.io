Cerberus

proper noun
three-headed dog of Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrbərəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːbərəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a three-headed dog that guards the entrance to Hades, preventing the dead from leaving and the living from entering.

Examples

Cerberus guards Hades
According to the myth, Cerberus prevented anyone from escaping the underworld.
Artists often depict Cerberus as a fierce, three-headed guardian with a serpent's tail.
Hercules was tasked with capturing Cerberus as one of his twelve labors.
