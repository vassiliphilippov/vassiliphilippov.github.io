novena

noun
devotional prayers over nine days (Catholic)
Pronunciation: / noʊˈveɪnə / (AmE), / nəʊˈviːnə / (BrE)
In the Roman Catholic tradition, a devotion consisting of prayers repeated for nine consecutive days for a special intention.

Examples

attend a novena service
Attending the evening novena became part of his daily routine for over a week.
Many Catholics prayed a novena for peace during the troubled times.
She began a novena to Saint Jude in hopes of finding employment.
