to localise

verb
restrict to a particular area
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz /; / ˈloʊ.kə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz /; / ˈləʊ.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to restrict or confine something to a particular area or place

Examples

to localise an infection
Doctors were able to localise the infection to a single organ.
Security measures were introduced to localise the disturbance and prevent it from spreading.
The company hopes to localise production in order to better serve regional markets.
New safety measures helped localise the fire before it spread to other buildings.
Pain from the injury tends to localise in the lower back region.
The doctors hope the treatment will localise the infection to only one area of the body.
