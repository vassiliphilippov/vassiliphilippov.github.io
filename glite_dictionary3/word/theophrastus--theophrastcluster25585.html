Theophrastus

proper noun
Greek philosopher, successor of Aristotle
Pronunciation: / θiˈɑːfrəstəs / (AmE), / θiˈɒfrəstəs / (BrE)
(c. 371–287 BC) A Greek philosopher, student of Aristotle, and successor as leader of the Peripatetic school.

Examples

Theophrastus the philosopher
Theophrastus is known for his foundational work in botany and for succeeding Aristotle as head of the Peripatetic school.
Aristotle entrusted his school to Theophrastus upon his death, recognizing his intellectual leadership.
Many ancient texts cite Theophrastus as an authority on plants and nature.
