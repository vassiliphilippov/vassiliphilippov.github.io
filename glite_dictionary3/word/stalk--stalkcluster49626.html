to stalk

verb
follow secretly or persistently
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
to follow or pursue someone stealthily and often with malicious intent, or to recur persistently in someone's thoughts or life

Examples

to stalk a person
He was arrested after he stalked his former colleague to her home.
Terrifying memories of the accident continued to stalk her long after it happened.
The private investigator stalked the suspect for days without being noticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – haunt: to come back repeatedly to the mind of someone
  • synonym – pursue: to follow someone in order to catch or attack them
