preempt

noun
high bid to disrupt opponents (bridge)
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmpt / (AmE), / priːˈɛmpt / (BrE)
(in bridge and similar card games) a high bid made to prevent opponents from bidding or to disrupt their strategy.

Examples

a preempt bid
He made a preempt with seven clubs, cutting off any opposing bids.
Their team's aggressive preempt forced us to pass early.
Using a preempt can be risky if your partner is not prepared.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preemptive bid: a high, blocking bid in bridge
