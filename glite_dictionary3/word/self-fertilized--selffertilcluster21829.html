self-fertilized

adjective
fertilized by own pollen or sperm
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈfəːtɪˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) fertilized by its own pollen or sperm, rather than from another individual.

Examples

self-fertilized flower
Certain flowers are capable of becoming self-fertilized without the help of insects.
The pea plant produced seeds because it was self-fertilized.
These self-fertilized plants maintain genetic uniformity across generations.
