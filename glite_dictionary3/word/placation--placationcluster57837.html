placation

noun
the act of calming or appeasing someone
Pronunciation: / pleɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pləˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making someone less angry or hostile, especially by yielding to their demands or calming them

Examples

offer placation gifts
After the argument, he brought flowers as an act of placation.
Despite his efforts at placation, the angry crowd refused to disperse.
Her gentle words of placation helped ease the tension in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appeasement: the act of making peace by giving in to demands
  • antonym – provocation: action or speech that makes someone angry or provokes a response
