conditionally

adverb
subject to a condition
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənəli / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəli / (BrE)
With the condition that certain requirements are met; subject to, or dependent on, a condition.

Examples

conditionally accept an offer
Her release from jail was granted conditionally, requiring her to report to a parole officer weekly.
The company agreed to hire him conditionally, pending the results of his background check.
We accepted the proposal conditionally, waiting for further clarification on several terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unconditionally: without conditions; not subject to any condition
