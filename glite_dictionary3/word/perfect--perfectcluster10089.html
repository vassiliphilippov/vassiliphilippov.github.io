perfect

adjective
absolute or complete (esp. of something negative)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrfɪkt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
(informal, often pejorative) Used to emphasize that someone or something is a complete or absolute example of a particular type, especially a negative one.

Examples

perfect fool
After that mistake, he felt like a perfect fool in front of his colleagues.
He’s a perfect idiot if he thinks that plan will actually work.
She made a perfect mess of the situation by arriving late and unprepared.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absolute: total or complete, with no limits or restrictions
  • antonym – partial: not complete or total; limited
