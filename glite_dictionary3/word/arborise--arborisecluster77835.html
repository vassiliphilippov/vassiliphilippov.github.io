to arborise

verb
branch out like a tree
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɑːbərʌɪz / (BrE)
To branch out or spread in the shape or manner of a tree; to form a branching pattern, especially as seen in nerve fibers or vessels.

Examples

to arborise like branches
Some blood vessels arborise extensively as they reach muscle tissues.
The neurons begin to arborise during early development, creating complex networks.
Under the microscope, the dendrites were seen to arborise beautifully throughout the sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym – branch: to separate into subdivisions or offshoots, like the limbs of a tree
