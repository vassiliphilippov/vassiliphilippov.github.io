creole

noun
language developed from mixed origins
Pronunciation: / ˈkriː.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈkriː.əʊl / (BrE)
a stable natural language developed from the mixing of two or more languages, often as a mother tongue in a community

Examples

creole language community
After centuries of use, the creole has become the community's primary language.
Many people in the Caribbean speak a creole that combines African and European language elements.
Researchers studied how the creole developed from a Portuguese-based pidgin.
