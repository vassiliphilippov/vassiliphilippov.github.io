to strike

verb
pull sharply on a line to hook a fish
Pronunciation: / straɪk / (AmE), / straɪk / (BrE)
(in fishing) to pull sharply on the fishing line with the intent to hook a fish.

Examples

to strike a fish
Experienced anglers know just when to strike after feeling a nibble on the line.
If you strike too early, you might miss hooking the fish entirely.
When the float disappeared, he quickly struck and landed a large carp.
