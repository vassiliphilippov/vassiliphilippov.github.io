sphagnum

noun
a type of moss that forms peat
Pronunciation: / ˈsfæɡ.nəm / (AmE), / ˈsfæɡ.nəm / (BrE)
any of various mosses of the genus Sphagnum, typically pale in color, whose decomposed remains accumulate to form peat used in gardening and as a soil improver

Examples

sphagnum moss
Gardeners often use sphagnum moss for its excellent water-retaining properties.
Scientists study sphagnum to understand peat bog ecosystems and carbon storage.
The wetland was covered in thick layers of sphagnum that made the ground spongy.
