gambrel

noun
roof with two slopes per side
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæm.brəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.brəl / (BrE)
A roof design with two slopes on each side, where the lower slope is steeper than the upper one, commonly seen in barns.

Examples

gambrel roof design
Many old barns in the countryside feature a gambrel to create extra storage space in the loft.
Restoring the farmhouse, they decided to keep the original gambrel for its historical character.
The architect recommended a gambrel roof for its aesthetic appeal and practicality.
