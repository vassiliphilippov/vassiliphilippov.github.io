swash

noun
the movement or sound of water, especially waves
Pronunciation: / swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
the movement or sound made by water, especially as waves break on a shore

Examples

swash of waves
Seagulls called above the rhythmic swash on the sandy beach.
She listened to the gentle swash as the waves lapped the boat's hull.
The swash of the sea against the rocks was soothing after a long day.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – crash: a loud sound of something breaking or hitting, often related to waves
  • synonym – splash: the sound or effect of something wet hitting a surface
