blurb

noun
promotional book statement
Pronunciation: / blɝb / (AmE), / blɜːb / (BrE)
A short promotional statement, typically found on the back cover or dust jacket of a book, used to advertise its contents.

Examples

blurb on a book
Many readers rely on the blurbs to decide whether to buy a book.
She was asked to write a blurb for her colleague's upcoming book.
The new novel featured a glowing blurb from a famous author on its cover.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endorsement: an act of giving public approval or support
  • synonym – review: a critical assessment of a book, movie, or other work
