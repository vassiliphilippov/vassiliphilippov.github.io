tefillin

noun
Jewish prayer boxes containing scripture
Pronunciation: / təˈfɪlɪn / (AmE), / təˈfɪlɪn / (BrE)
(In Judaism) either of two small black leather boxes containing scrolls of parchment inscribed with verses from the Hebrew Scriptures, worn by Jewish men during weekday morning prayers on the forehead and left arm.

Examples

wear tefillin praying
He carefully wrapped the leather straps of his tefillin around his arm before beginning the service.
Orthodox Jewish men are seen putting on tefillin each morning as part of their prayers.
Visitors to the synagogue watched as the rabbi explained the significance of the tefillin.
