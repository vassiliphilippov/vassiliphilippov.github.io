vagile

adjective
able to move freely; not fixed
Pronunciation: / ˈvædʒaɪl / (AmE), / ˈvædʒaɪl / (BrE)
(of an organism) able to move freely from place to place, not fixed in one location.

Examples

vagile animal species
In contrast to plants, most mammals are considered highly vagile organisms.
Many fish species are vagile, enabling them to adapt quickly to changing environments.
The vagile nature of these insects allows them to escape unfavorable conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sessile: (of an organism) fixed in one place; immobile
