expurgation

noun
removal of offensive content from a work
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.spɚˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the removal of objectionable or offensive parts from a literary or artistic work

Examples

The new edition of the novel underwent significant expurgation to remove controversial passages.
