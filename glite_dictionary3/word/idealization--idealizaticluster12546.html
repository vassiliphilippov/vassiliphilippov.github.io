idealization

noun
portrayal as perfect or ideal
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdiələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of representing or imagining something as perfect or better than it really is

Examples

idealization of beauty
Many artists engage in the idealization of beauty in their work.
She criticized the idealization of historical figures, arguing it ignores their flaws.
The idealization of childhood in literature often overlooks the difficulties that children actually face.
Her description of the city was an idealization, ignoring its many practical problems.
Philosophers often discuss the idealization of justice rather than its imperfect real-world forms.
The hero in the story is simply an idealization of what people wish to be.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ideal: a person or thing regarded as perfect
  • antonym – realism: the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly
  • synonym – romanticization: the act of making something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
