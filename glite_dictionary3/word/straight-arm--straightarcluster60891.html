straight-arm

noun
warding off a tackler with an outstretched arm
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪt ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt ɑːm / (BrE)
(in American football) the act of pushing away a tackler by fully extending the arm with the hand against the opponent.

Examples

straight-arm a defender
Coaches often teach young players how to execute a proper straight-arm in practice.
His impressive straight-arm kept the tackler at bay and allowed him to score.
The running back used a powerful straight-arm to fend off the defender.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stiff-arm: the act of fending off a tackler by holding out one arm
