derivable

adjective
able to be derived
Pronunciation: / dɪˈraɪvəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be obtained or deduced from something else, especially by reasoning or calculation.

Examples

derivable from data
All necessary values are derivable from the information provided in the report.
In logic, the conclusion must be derivable from the given premises.
The formula is derivable from basic mathematical principles.
