thrombosed

adjective
affected by a blood clot
Pronunciation: / θrəmˈboʊzd / (AmE), / θrɒmˈbəʊzd / (BrE)
(of a blood vessel or tissue) affected with or obstructed by a clot of coagulated blood.

Examples

thrombosed vein
A thrombosed artery can lead to serious complications such as tissue death.
The surgeon discovered that the patient's vein was thrombosed and required immediate intervention.
They treated the thrombosed hemorrhoid to relieve the patient's discomfort.
