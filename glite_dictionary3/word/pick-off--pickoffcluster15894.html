pick-off

noun
baseball play catching a runner off base
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪk ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpɪk ˌɒf / (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which a base runner is caught off base and tagged out by a fielder, usually due to a swift throw from the pitcher or catcher.

Examples

a pick-off play
After the pick-off, the team celebrated their quick defensive play.
He practiced his pick-off move daily to keep runners alert on the bases.
The pitcher executed a perfect pick-off at first base, stunning the runner and the crowd.
