to approach

verb
move closer to something/someone
Pronunciation: / əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to move closer to someone or something; to come nearer in distance or time

Examples

to approach a building
As the storm clouds approached, people rushed inside for shelter.
The deadline is approaching faster than I expected.
We could see the train approaching the station from a distance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – advance: to move forward, often toward a place
  • antonym – depart: to go away from a place
