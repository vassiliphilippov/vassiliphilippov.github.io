potato

noun
edible starchy tuber vegetable
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪdoʊ / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A round or oval edible tuber with brown, yellow, or red skin and white, yellow, or purple flesh, originally from South America, commonly used as a staple food.

Examples

baked potato
He prefers his potatoes mashed with a little butter and cream.
She bought a bag of russet potatoes at the market for dinner.
The Irish famine in the 1840s was partly caused by a potato blight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sweet potato: a different edible tuber with orange flesh
