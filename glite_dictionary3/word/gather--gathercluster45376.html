to gather

verb
conclude or infer from evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to conclude or understand something based on evidence or information

Examples

to gather from clues
From your tone, I gather you aren't happy with the results.
I gather that you haven't seen the latest report.
She gathered from their conversation that the event had been cancelled.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deduce: to reach a conclusion based on available information or evidence
  • synonym – infer: to deduce or conclude from evidence and reasoning rather than from explicit statements
