positivist

noun
adherent of the philosophy of positivism
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːzətəvɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪtɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who emphasizes observable facts and excludes metaphysical speculation, especially as an adherent of the philosophy of positivism

Examples

a positivist thinker
As a committed positivist, he argued that scientific knowledge must be based solely on observable evidence.
Many critics accused the positivist school of ignoring deeper philosophical questions.
The positivist rejected any explanation that could not be verified through empirical observation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – empiricist: someone who believes knowledge comes only from experience or evidence
  • antonym – metaphysician: someone who studies metaphysics; opposite to empirical approach
