adsorbable

adjective
able to be adsorbed (onto a surface)
Pronunciation: / ædˈsɔrbəbəl / (AmE), / adˈsɔːbəbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being adsorbed or accumulated on the surface of a solid.

Examples

adsorbable chemicals
Certain pollutants are highly adsorbable, allowing them to be removed from water using filtration systems.
Researchers studied which gases were most adsorbable by the new material.
The drug is adsorbable onto activated charcoal, making it effective for detoxification.

Related Terms

  • antonym – absorbable: able to be absorbed (taken up internally, not just on the surface)
  • synonym – adsorption: the process by which atoms, ions, or molecules adhere to a surface
