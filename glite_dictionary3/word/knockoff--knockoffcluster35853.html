knockoff

noun
unauthorized copy or imitation
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːkˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈnɒkˌɒf / (BrE)
An unauthorized copy or imitation of something, especially a product or item of popular design.

Examples

knockoff designer bag
He prefers the real thing and would never buy a knockoff jersey.
She realized her new handbag was a knockoff after noticing the misspelled brand name.
The market was full of knockoff watches that looked like expensive brands.

Related Terms

  • synonym – counterfeit: a fraudulent imitation of something valuable
  • antonym – original: the genuine or first version, not a copy
