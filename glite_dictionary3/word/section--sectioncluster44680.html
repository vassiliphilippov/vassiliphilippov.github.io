section

noun
group of similar instruments in an orchestra
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃən /; / ˈsekʃən / (BrE)
a group or unit of people within a larger group, such as in the military, police, orchestra, or a class

Examples

section of an orchestra
Each section in the orchestra brings a unique texture to the overall sound.
The conductor asked the brass section to play more softly during the second movement.
The violin section rehearsed separately before joining the full orchestra.
Each section of the unit was responsible for a different area of the operation.
The radio operator stayed close to his section during the mission.
The sergeant led his section through a field training exercise early in the morning.
Each section meets with a different teaching assistant to review the material.
Graduate students are often responsible for leading discussion sections in large introductory courses.
This semester, I'm enrolled in the afternoon section of biology.
