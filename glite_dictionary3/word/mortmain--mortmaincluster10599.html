mortmain

noun
perpetual ownership of property by a corporation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtˌmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɔːt.meɪn / (BrE)
(historical, legal) The perpetual, inalienable ownership of real property, especially by a corporation such as an ecclesiastical institution.

Examples

mortmain property ownership
During the Middle Ages, lands held in mortmain could not be inherited or sold.
Many monarchs passed laws to limit the spread of mortmain by religious orders.
The church accumulated vast wealth through mortmain before reforms were introduced.

Related Terms
