to straggle

verb
wander away from a direct course
Pronunciation: / ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡl /; / ˈstræɡəl / (BrE)
to move, grow, or spread in an irregular, untidy, or scattered way, often away from a group

Examples

to straggle behind others
As the parade ended, some participants continued to straggle down the empty street.

Some students would straggle in late after the bell had rung.
The town began to straggle across the plains as more people settled.
Vines straggle up the old stone wall in every direction.
