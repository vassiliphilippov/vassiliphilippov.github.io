moon

noun
period between new moons; lunar month
Pronunciation: / mun / (AmE), / muːn / (BrE)
A period of about 29.5 days between successive new moons, used in lunar calendars.

Examples

a moon cycle
Many festivals occur once every moon in some cultures.
The ancient calendar was based on the moon rather than the solar year.
The tides were measured from one moon to the next.

Related Terms

  • synonym – month: period of about four weeks, especially as based on the lunar cycle
