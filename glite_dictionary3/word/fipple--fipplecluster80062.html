fipple

noun
wooden mouthpiece plug in wind instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪp.əl / (AmE), / ˈfɪp.əl / (BrE)
A wooden plug that forms the mouthpiece of certain wind instruments, such as a recorder, creating a channel for the airflow.

Examples

fipple mouthpiece recorder
Dust can accumulate in the fipple, affecting the performance of the instrument.
Early flute makers experimented with different fipple designs for clearer tones.
The fipple directs the flow of air in a recorder to produce its sound.
