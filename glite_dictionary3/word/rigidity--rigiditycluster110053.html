rigidity

noun
strictness; inflexibility
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɪdəti / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality of being strict, uncompromising, or inflexible in rules or behavior.

Examples

rigidity in rules
His rigidity about deadlines frustrated the more creative members of the team.
Some critics point out the rigidity of the school's disciplinary approach.
The manager's rigidity in enforcing the new policies left employees no room for flexibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flexibility: the quality of being adaptable or variable
  • synonym – severity: the quality of being very harsh or strict
