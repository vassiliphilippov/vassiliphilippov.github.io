cornflour

noun
fine starch from maize, thickener
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A fine, powdery starch made from maize, used in cooking to thicken sauces and soups.

Examples

cornflour thickens sauce
Bakers often use cornflour to give cakes a lighter texture.
She mixed the cornflour with a little cold water before adding it to the soup.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of cornflour to thicken the sauce.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cornstarch: starch powder obtained from maize, primarily in American English
