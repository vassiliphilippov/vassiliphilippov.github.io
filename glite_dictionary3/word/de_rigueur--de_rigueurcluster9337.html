de rigueur

adjective
required by etiquette or fashion
Pronunciation: / də ɹiˈɡɜr / (AmE), / də ɹiˈɡɜː / (BrE)
required by etiquette, custom, or current fashion; considered necessary or expected in a particular situation.

Examples

de rigueur dress code
At formal dinners, wearing a black tie is de rigueur for all male guests.
In many upscale restaurants, a suit jacket used to be de rigueur for men.
Using environmentally friendly packaging is now de rigueur for modern businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mandatory: required by rule or law
  • antonym – optional: not required; left to personal choice
