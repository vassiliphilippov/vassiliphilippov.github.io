inanimate

adjective
denoting nonliving things
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnænɪmət / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪmət / (BrE)
(of a noun) denoting a nonliving thing rather than a person or animal.

Examples

inanimate noun
Grammatical rules may differ for inanimate objects in certain languages.
In some languages, nouns are classified as inanimate or animate.
The word ‘rock’ is considered inanimate in English grammar.
