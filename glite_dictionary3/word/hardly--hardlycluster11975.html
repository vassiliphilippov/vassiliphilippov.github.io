hardly

adverb
only a very short time before
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdli / (BrE)
Only a very short time before a particular moment or event.

Examples

hardly finished work
She had hardly stepped outside when it started to rain.
The movie had hardly begun when his phone rang.
We hardly finished dinner before the guests arrived.
