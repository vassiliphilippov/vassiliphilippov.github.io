portwatcher

noun
security guard at a wharf or port
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔrtˌwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtˌwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person assigned to guard or monitor a wharf or port area, often to ensure security or prevent theft

Examples

portwatcher guards docks
As a portwatcher, she was responsible for monitoring activity on the dock at night.
In the 19th century, a portwatcher helped prevent cargo theft at busy wharves.
The portwatcher made his rounds every hour to ensure no one entered the restricted area.
