lymphedema

noun
swelling caused by lymph fluid accumulation
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪmfəˈdimə / (AmE), / ˌlɪmfɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
A chronic swelling, usually in the legs or arms, caused by an accumulation of lymph fluid in the tissues due to a blockage or malfunction of the lymphatic system.

Examples

chronic lymphedema in legs
After her surgery, she developed lymphedema in her left arm, causing persistent swelling and discomfort.
Doctors recommend exercise and massage to reduce lymphedema in patients experiencing lymphatic blockage.
Wearing compression garments can help manage the symptoms of lymphedema.
