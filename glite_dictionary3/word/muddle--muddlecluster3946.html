to muddle

verb
to mix up or confuse
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌdəl / (BrE)
to mix up or confuse things, making them unclear or difficult to understand

Examples

to muddle the facts
He managed to muddle the instructions so no one knew what to do.
If you muddle your facts, the argument becomes much weaker.
Try not to muddle the details in your presentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clarify: to make something easier to understand
  • synonym – confuse: to make something unclear by mixing things up
