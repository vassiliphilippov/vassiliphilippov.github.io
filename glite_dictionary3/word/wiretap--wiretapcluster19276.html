to wiretap

verb
secretly listen to phone calls
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪərˌtæp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəˌtæp / (BrE)
to secretly listen to people's telephone conversations by connecting a device to the telephone line, especially as part of an investigation

Examples

to wiretap phone calls
It is illegal to wiretap someone’s calls without a court order.
The police had to wiretap his home phone to gather enough evidence.
They discovered that the calls were being wiretapped by authorities.
