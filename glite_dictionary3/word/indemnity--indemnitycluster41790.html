indemnity

noun
protection against loss or damage (often financial)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛm.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
protection or exemption from having to pay for loss, damage, or liability, especially through insurance or law

Examples

indemnity insurance policy
She took out indemnity insurance to protect her business from potential lawsuits.
The company sought indemnity for the damages caused by the defective product.
The contract included a clause providing indemnity against any claims arising from the project.
The government offered indemnity to healthcare workers during the emergency.
The insurance policy provided full indemnity against natural disasters.
Under the contract, the company received indemnity for any losses arising from lawsuits.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compensation: something, typically money, awarded to someone in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury
  • synonym – insurance: arrangement providing compensation for loss or damage
  • antonym – liability: the state of being legally responsible for something
