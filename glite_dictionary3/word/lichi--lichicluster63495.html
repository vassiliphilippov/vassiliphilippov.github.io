lichi

noun
a sweet Chinese fruit with a brittle shell
Pronunciation: / ˈliːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈliːtʃi / (BrE)
A small, round, sweet fruit with a thin, brittle shell, translucent white pulp, and a single seed, native to China and often dried for use.

Examples

fresh lichi fruit
Dried lichis are popular in Asian markets, especially as a sweet snack.

The dessert was garnished with fresh lichis and mango slices.
