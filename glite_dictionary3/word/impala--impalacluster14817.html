impala

noun
African antelope known for its leaping ability
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɑːlə / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːlə / (BrE)
a medium-sized African antelope with slender legs and curved, ridged horns (in males), noted for its ability to leap great distances

Examples

a wild impala
A male impala can leap over nine meters in a single bound.
Herds of impalas are a common sight in many African game reserves.
The impala sprang gracefully across the savannah as the lions gave chase.
