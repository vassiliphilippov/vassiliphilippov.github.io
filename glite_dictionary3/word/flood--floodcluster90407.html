flood

noun
a large, overwhelming quantity
Pronunciation: / flʌd / (AmE), / flʌd / (BrE)
a very large number or amount of something that arrives at the same time or within a short period

Examples

a flood of messages
After the product launch, there was a flood of customer inquiries.
She was met with a flood of support from her friends during the difficult time.
The company received a flood of applications after advertising the job opening.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deluge: a great quantity of something, especially arriving suddenly
  • synonym – influx: a large arrival or entry of people or things
  • antonym – trickle: a small, slow quantity or flow
