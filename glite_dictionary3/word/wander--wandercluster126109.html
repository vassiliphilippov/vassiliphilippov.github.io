to wander

verb
move in a winding course
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑndɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndə / (BrE)
(of a path, stream, or similar) to move in a winding, indirect, or meandering course.

Examples

wander through the forest
Our hike followed a trail that wandered through the ancient forest.
The little stream wandered across the meadow, twisting between tufts of grass.
The old stone wall wandered along the hillside, curving around boulders and trees.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meander: to follow a winding, turning course, especially of a river or path
  • synonym – wind: (of a road, river, etc.) to follow a curving or twisting course
