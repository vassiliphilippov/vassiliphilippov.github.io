sensitive

adjective
aware of others' feelings or needs
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsətɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtɪv / (BrE)
aware of and responsive to the feelings, needs, or circumstances of others; easily affected by others' attitudes or emotions.

Examples

sensitive to feelings
He tried to be sensitive when discussing the difficult topic with his friend.
It is important for teachers to be sensitive to students' diverse backgrounds.
Julia is very sensitive to the concerns of her coworkers and always listens carefully.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insensitive: not aware of or affected by others' feelings or circumstances
