to prepossess

verb
influence opinion in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːpəˈzɛs / (AmE), / ˌpriːpəˈzɛs / (BrE)
to influence or affect someone's opinion or feelings beforehand, often before they have direct experience

Examples

to prepossess the audience
Careful advertising was used to prepossess consumers with positive expectations about the new product.
Her charming manners prepossessed the jury in her favor early on.
The article aimed to prepossess readers against the candidate before the debate began.
Anxiety about the results can prepossess students long before the exams start.
She was so prepossessed by worry that she could not concentrate on her work.
The prospect of failure seemed to prepossess his mind before the interview even began.

Related Terms

  • synonym – prejudice: to cause someone to have an unjustified opinion
  • synonym – preoccupy: to dominate or engross the mind of someone to the exclusion of other thoughts
