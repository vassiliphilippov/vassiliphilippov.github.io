katharsis

noun
medical purging of the bowels
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑrsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːsɪs / (BrE)
(medical) The process of purging the body, especially the bowels, by using a cathartic.

Examples

medical katharsis
Ancient medical practices often relied on katharsis to treat a variety of ailments.

The use of herbal remedies for katharsis dates back centuries in traditional medicine.
