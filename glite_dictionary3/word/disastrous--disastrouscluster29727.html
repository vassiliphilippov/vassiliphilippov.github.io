disastrous

adjective
causing great damage or failure
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzæstrəs / (AmE), / dɪˈzɑːstrəs / (BrE)
(of an event or action) causing great damage, suffering, or failure; extremely unfortunate.

Examples

disastrous consequences
His decision to ignore safety warnings proved to be disastrous for the project.
The company suffered disastrous losses after the sudden market downturn.
The flood had a disastrous effect on the local community, leaving hundreds homeless.

Related Terms

  • synonym – calamitous: causing great harm or suffering, especially through a disaster
  • synonym – catastrophic: involving or causing sudden great damage or suffering
  • antonym – fortunate: having good fortune; lucky
