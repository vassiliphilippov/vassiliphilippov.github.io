woolgathering

noun
idle daydreaming or fantasy
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊlˌɡæðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌɡæðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of indulging in idle daydreaming or fanciful thoughts, often with a lack of practical purpose

Examples

lost in woolgathering
During the long meeting, his woolgathering made him miss several important points.
Instead of working on his assignment, he spent hours in woolgathering.
She was caught woolgathering and didn't hear the teacher call her name.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concentration: focus of mental effort on a particular task or idea
  • synonym – daydreaming: the activity of being absorbed in pleasant thoughts that distract from the present
