to counter

verb
respond with an opposing argument or statement
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚ /; / ˈkaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
to respond to something with an opposing action or argument; to act to prevent or neutralize something

Examples

to counter a claim
She quickly countered his argument by citing recent statistics.
The lawyer countered the accusation with evidence of his client's innocence.
Whenever he is criticized, he tends to counter with a joke.
Doctors recommend vaccines to counter the spread of infectious diseases.
New security measures were introduced to counter the rising threat of cyber attacks.
The company launched an advertising campaign to counter negative publicity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concur: to agree with a statement or opinion
  • synonym – oppose: to act against; resist
  • synonym – rebut: to argue against or refute a statement
  • antonym – support: to give assistance to; help
