triolein

noun
a type of fat found in oils and fats
Pronunciation: / traɪˈoʊliːɪn / (AmE), / traɪˈəʊliːɪn / (BrE)
A naturally occurring fat consisting of three molecules of oleic acid joined to glycerol, found in many fats and oils.

Examples

triolein in vegetable oil
Triolein is commonly used in laboratory studies to simulate natural fats.
Olive oil contains a high proportion of triolein.
The breakdown of triolein yields glycerol and oleic acid.
