burlesque

adjective
relating to comic or exaggerated imitation (especially theater)
Pronunciation: / bɜrˈlɛsk / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the style of burlesque, typically involving comic or exaggerated imitation, especially in theatrical performances.

Examples

burlesque style costume
Her costume was inspired by traditional burlesque performances from the early twentieth century.
Several posters advertised the upcoming burlesque revue at the downtown theater.
The show had a distinct burlesque style, filled with exaggerated characters and comedic routines.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parodic: imitating in an exaggerated or humorous way
  • antonym – serious: having a solemn or earnest character, not intended to amuse
