to spurt

verb
come out suddenly in a stream
Pronunciation: / spɝt / (AmE), / spɜːt / (BrE)
(of liquid, gas, etc.) to come out suddenly and forcefully in a stream or jet.

Examples

to spurt blood
Blood spurted from the small cut on his finger.
The faucet suddenly began to spurt water all over the counter.
The whale spurted a jet of water from its blowhole into the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gush: to flow out suddenly and in large amounts
  • antonym – trickle: to flow or fall in drops or a thin stream
