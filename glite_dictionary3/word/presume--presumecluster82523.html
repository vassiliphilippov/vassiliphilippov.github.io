to presume

verb
suppose something without proof
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzum / (AmE), / prɪˈzjuːm / (BrE)
to suppose or assume that something is true, often without proof or based on evidence

Examples

to presume the truth
It would be unwise to presume that the weather will stay nice all week.
She presumed the meeting had been canceled because nobody showed up.
We cannot presume innocence or guilt until more evidence is presented.
A signed contract presumes full understanding of the agreement.
Her silence presumes consent to the proposed changes.
The evidence presumes the defendant's involvement in the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym – assume: to suppose something to be true without proof
  • degree lower – guess: to estimate or suppose without sufficient information
  • synonym – imply: to suggest something is true without stating it directly
  • antonym – prove: to establish the truth or validity of something
  • synonym – suggest: to cause one to think that something exists or is the case
