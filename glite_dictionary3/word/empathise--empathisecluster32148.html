to empathise

verb
show understanding of others' feelings
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ɪmˈpæθaɪz / (BrE)
to understand and share the feelings of another person

Examples

to empathise with others
If you want to help effectively, you must first empathise with their situation.
It's important for managers to empathise with their employees during difficult times.
She found it easy to empathise with the characters in the novel.

Related Terms

  • antonym – antipathy: a strong feeling of dislike
  • synonym – empathize: to understand and share the feelings of another person (American spelling)
  • synonym – sympathise: to feel or express sympathy or compassion for someone
