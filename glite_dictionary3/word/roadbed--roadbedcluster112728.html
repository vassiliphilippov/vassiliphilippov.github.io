roadbed

noun
foundation supporting a road or track
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊdˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌbɛd / (BrE)
the layer of material that forms the foundation supporting a road or railway track.

Examples

railway roadbed
After the heavy rain, the roadbed was inspected for signs of erosion or instability.
Before laying new asphalt, workers must ensure that the roadbed is level and compacted.
The engineers reinforced the roadbed to prevent the highway from sinking in the future.
