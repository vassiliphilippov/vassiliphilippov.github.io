exponent

noun
symbol indicating repeated multiplication
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊnənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊnənt / (BrE)
a symbol or number placed above and to the right of another number to show how many times the number is to be multiplied by itself

Examples

exponent of a number
In scientific notation, the exponent shows the number of decimal places moved.
Raising a number to a negative exponent gives its reciprocal.
The exponent in 3² tells you to multiply 3 by itself once.

Related Terms

  • synonym – power: the product of multiplying a number by itself a certain number of times
