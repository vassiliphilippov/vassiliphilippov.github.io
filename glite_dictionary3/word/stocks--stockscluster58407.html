stocks

noun
frame for restraining animals during care
Pronunciation: / stɑks / (AmE), / stɒks / (BrE)
a strong wooden frame with movable barriers, used to restrain an animal, especially a horse, during veterinary procedures or while being shod

Examples

stocks for holding horses
The blacksmith used stocks to safely shoe the restless animal.
The veterinarian secured the horse in the stocks before starting the examination.
Without stocks, treating large animals would be dangerous for both the animal and the handler.
