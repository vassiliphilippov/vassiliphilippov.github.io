to owe

verb
be obliged to pay or repay
Pronunciation: / oʊ / (AmE), / əʊ / (BrE)
to have an obligation to pay, return, or give something (such as money, a favor, or an apology) to someone

Examples

owe money to a friend
She owed both her parents an apology for her behavior.
The team owes its success to careful planning and hard work.
You still owe me fifty dollars from last week.
I still owe him fifty dollars from last week.
She owes her success to her supportive family.
They owed a large sum of money to the bank.

Related Terms

  • antonym – borrow: to take and use something belonging to someone else with the intention of returning it
  • antonym – lend: to give someone money or something temporarily with the expectation of return
  • synonym – repay: to pay back money that is owed
