Shem

proper noun
eldest son of Noah in the Bible
Pronunciation: / ʃɛm / (AmE), / ʃɛm / (BrE)
In the Old Testament, the eldest son of Noah, traditionally considered an ancestor of the Semitic peoples.

Examples

Shem son of Noah
According to Genesis, Shem survived the flood with his father Noah and brothers Ham and Japheth.
Many genealogies in the Bible trace the origins of nations back to Shem.
The descendants of Shem are considered the forebears of various Semitic peoples.
