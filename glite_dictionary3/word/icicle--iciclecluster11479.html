icicle

noun
pointed hanging piece of ice
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A long, thin, pointed piece of ice that hangs from an edge, such as a roof, formed by the freezing of dripping water.

Examples

icicle hanging from roof
A long icicle hung from the edge of the roof, glittering in the morning sun.
Children watched as icicles formed outside their classroom after the snowstorm.
He accidentally broke an icicle when he closed the window with too much force.

  • synonym – stalactite: a pointed structure hanging from the ceiling of a cave, often made from mineral deposits
