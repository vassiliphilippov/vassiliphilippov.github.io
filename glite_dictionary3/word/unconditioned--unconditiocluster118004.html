unconditioned

adjective
not learned or conditioned
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
(of a response or reflex) not acquired or modified by conditioning or learning; innate or natural.

Examples

unconditioned reflex action
Infants display several unconditioned responses to stimuli, such as blinking or grasping.
The doctor explained that a sneeze is an unconditioned reflex rather than a learned behavior.
Unlike trained behaviors, unconditioned responses do not require previous experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conditioned: acquired or modified by conditioning or learning
