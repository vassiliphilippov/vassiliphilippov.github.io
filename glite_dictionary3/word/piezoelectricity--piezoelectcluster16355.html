piezoelectricity

noun
electricity from pressure on crystals
Pronunciation: / piˌeɪzoʊˌɛlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (AmE), / piˌɛtsəʊˌɪlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (BrE)
Electricity produced by applying mechanical pressure to certain crystals, such as quartz, or the reverse effect, where an electric field causes the crystal to change shape.

Examples

piezoelectricity in quartz crystals
Applying pressure to certain materials, such as quartz, generates piezoelectricity that can power small circuits.
Many electronic devices utilize piezoelectricity for precise frequency control.
The microphone works by converting sound waves into piezoelectricity using a quartz crystal.
