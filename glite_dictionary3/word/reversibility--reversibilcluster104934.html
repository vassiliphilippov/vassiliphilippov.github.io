reversibility

noun
the ability to be reversed
Pronunciation: / rɪˌvɜrsəˈbɪlət̬i / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be reversed, so that the original state can be restored.

Examples

reversibility of a process
One important feature of this chemical reaction is its reversibility, allowing products to convert back into reactants.
Some medical treatments are valued for their reversibility, so patients can discontinue them if necessary.
The reversibility of the decision means it can be changed if new information arises.

Related Terms

  • antonym – irreversibility: the quality of not being reversible
  • synonym – reversible: able to be turned the other way or undone
