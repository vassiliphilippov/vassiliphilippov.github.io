to match

verb
set in competition or opposition
Pronunciation: / mætʃ / (AmE), / matʃ / (BrE)
to set (people or things) in opposition or competition with each other

Examples

match teams in competition
The coach decided to match their top sprinter against the reigning champion in the finals.
The game show often matches contestants with similar skills to make the competition fair.
We should match our best debater against their team leader to increase our chances of winning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compete: to strive against another or others to win something
  • synonym – oppose: to set against, often in competition or disagreement
  • antonym – unite: to bring together for a common purpose
