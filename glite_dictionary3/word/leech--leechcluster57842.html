leech

noun
parasitic bloodsucking worm
Pronunciation: / liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
An aquatic or terrestrial annelid worm with suckers at both ends, many species of which are parasitic and feed on the blood of other animals.

Examples

leech in water
A leech attached itself to my leg while I was swimming in the lake.
Fish and frogs often fall victim to the bite of a leech in shallow ponds.
Some doctors once used a leech to draw blood as part of medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parasite: organism that lives on or in another and benefits by deriving nutrients at the other's expense
