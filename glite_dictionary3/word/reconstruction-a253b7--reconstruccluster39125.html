reconstruction

noun
the act of rebuilding
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of building something again after it has been damaged or destroyed

Examples

reconstruction after disaster
After the earthquake, reconstruction efforts focused on rebuilding homes and schools.
The reconstruction of the museum took nearly two years to complete.
The city began the reconstruction of the historic bridge after the flood.
