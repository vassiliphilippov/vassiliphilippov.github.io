palisade

noun
a defensive fence of stakes
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪˈseɪd / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈseɪd / (BrE)
a fence of strong wooden or metal stakes fixed deeply in the ground, used as a defensive barrier or enclosure

Examples

palisade fence
A sturdy palisade surrounded the fortress, making it difficult to attack.
Ancient villages often relied on a wooden palisade for protection.
The soldiers built a palisade around the camp to ward off intruders.
