to unbalance

verb
to throw out of balance
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to make something or someone unstable or lose balance, physically or mentally

Examples

to unbalance a system
Adding so many new features at once could unbalance the entire software system.
Her harsh criticism began to unbalance the team's morale.
High winds can easily unbalance a cyclist on the road.
Constant stress can unbalance even the strongest minds.
It was feared that the shock might unbalance him permanently.
The tragic accident seemed to unbalance her for years afterward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – balance: to keep or put in a steady position so that it does not fall
  • synonym – derange: to disturb the order or arrangement of; to make insane
  • synonym – destabilize: to make unstable or less steady
  • antonym – stabilize: to make stable or restore to normal mental balance
  • synonym – unhinge: to make mentally unbalanced or unstable
