wardership

noun
position or office of a warder
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrdərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or term of being a warder, especially one who guards or supervises a place such as a prison, castle, or institution

Examples

wardership of a prison
During her wardership, several reforms were introduced to improve prison conditions.
He was appointed to the wardership of the Tower of London in 1660.
The wardership came with significant responsibilities and privileges.

Related Terms

  • synonym – warder: a guard or watchman, especially in a prison
