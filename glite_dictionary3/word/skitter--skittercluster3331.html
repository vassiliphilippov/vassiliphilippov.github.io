to skitter

verb
move lightly and quickly over a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtə / (BrE)
to move lightly and quickly, often with a skipping, gliding, or jittery motion (used for people, animals, or things)

Examples

leaves skitter on pavement
Autumn leaves skitter along the pavement when the wind picks up.
The insect skittered across the surface of the pond, barely making a ripple.
The mouse skittered under the furniture as soon as the lights turned on.
He expertly skittered the lure across the surface to attract the fish.
The angler skittered his bait along the water, hoping for a strike.
Try to skitter the hook gently so it looks like an insect.
