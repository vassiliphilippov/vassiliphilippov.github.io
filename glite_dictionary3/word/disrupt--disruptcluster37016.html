to disrupt

verb
cause radical industry change
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌpt / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(in business and technology) to cause a radical and lasting change in an industry or market by introducing a new product, service, or business model that displaces established competitors.

Examples

disrupt an industry
The small startup hoped to disrupt the taxi industry with an app that matched drivers and riders.
When streaming services disrupted video rental stores, many local chains went out of business.
Their low-cost plan is disrupting traditional banks by offering faster, cheaper international transfers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – transform: to make a thorough or dramatic change in form, appearance, or character
  • synonym – upend: to overturn or reverse the position or order of something; often used for dramatic change
