Silenus

proper noun
chief satyr of Bacchus in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / saɪˈliːnəs / (AmE), / saɪˈliːnəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the chief satyr accompanying Bacchus, often depicted as an old, jolly, drunken man riding a donkey; sometimes considered the father of Dionysus.

Examples

Silenus rides a donkey
Artists often depict Silenus with a jolly expression and surrounded by satyrs.
In ancient art, Silenus is frequently shown riding a donkey beside Bacchus.
The stories describe Silenus as a wise but perpetually drunken companion to Dionysus.
