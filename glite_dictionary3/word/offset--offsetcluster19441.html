offset

noun
ledge where wall narrows abruptly
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)
A part of a wall or building where its width is suddenly reduced, creating a ledge or step-like feature.

Examples

offset in a wall
At the offset, the thickness of the wall decreases to support the upper floors.
Restoration revealed an original offset buried beneath later additions to the building.
The architect designed an offset to provide visual interest along the exterior wall.
