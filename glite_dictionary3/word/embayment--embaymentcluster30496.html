embayment

noun
indentation of a shoreline
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈbeɪmənt / (AmE), / ɛmˈbeɪm(ə)nt / (BrE)
An indentation of a shoreline that is larger than a cove but smaller than a gulf.

Examples

coastal embayment
A series of small islands lies just off the embayment to the south.
Geologists studied the embayment to understand past changes in sea level.
The coastline features a broad embayment that provides shelter to the harbor.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – cove: a small, sheltered bay
  • degree higher – gulf: a large inlet from the ocean into the landmass
