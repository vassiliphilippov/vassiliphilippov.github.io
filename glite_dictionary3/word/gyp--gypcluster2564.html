to gyp

verb
cheat or swindle (offensive)
Pronunciation: / dʒɪp / (AmE), / dʒɪp / (BrE)
(sometimes offensive) To cheat or swindle someone out of money or goods.

Examples

to gyp a customer
She felt angry when she realized the seller had gypped her with a fake watch.
The mechanic tried to gyp me on the repair cost, but I noticed right away.
Tourists are often warned not to let vendors gyp them at the market.
