biochip

noun
microchip used in biological analysis
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.oʊˌtʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊˌtʃɪp / (BrE)
A microchip designed for biological use, often containing DNA or other molecules, used to rapidly analyze or identify thousands of genes or other biological elements at once.

Examples

biochip analyzes DNA
Modern biochips can analyze thousands of genes in a single experiment.
Scientists used a biochip to screen for genetic mutations in the patient's DNA sample.
The development of the biochip greatly accelerated research in molecular biology.
