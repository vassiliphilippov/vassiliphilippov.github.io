amphibia

noun
class of land and water-dwelling vertebrates
Pronunciation: / æmˈfɪbiə / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbɪə / (BrE)
A class of vertebrate animals that typically live on land but breed in water, including frogs, toads, newts, salamanders, and caecilians.

Examples

amphibia include frogs
Most amphibia undergo metamorphosis from a larval stage to an adult form.
Scientists are studying the impact of pollution on native amphibia populations.
The biology textbook includes a section about the characteristics of amphibia.
