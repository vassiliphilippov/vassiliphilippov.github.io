radiographer

noun
medical imaging technician
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiəˈɡræfər / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
A person trained to operate equipment used to produce X-ray images or other diagnostic medical images.

Examples

radiographer takes X-rays
After completing her studies, she began working as a radiographer at a large city clinic.
If you have concerns about exposure, the radiographer can explain the safety measures used during the scan.
The radiographer prepared the patient for a chest X-ray in the hospital's imaging department.
