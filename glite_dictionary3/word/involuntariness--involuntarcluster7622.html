involuntariness

noun
lack of willingness or intentional control
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvɑːlənˈtɛrɪnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɒlənˈtɛərɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not done by choice or intentional will; lack of willingness or control over one's actions

Examples

involuntariness of actions
Her involuntariness in revealing the secret was apparent to everyone in the room.
Legal systems often distinguish between crimes committed with intention and those marked by involuntariness.
The involuntariness of his response made it clear he had no control over it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – unwillingness: the state of being not willing
  • antonym – voluntariness: the quality of being voluntary or done by choice
