Pyrethrum

noun
a genus of plants in the daisy family
Pronunciation: / paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / paɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, formerly used in classifications for plants now often included in Chrysanthemum or Tanacetum.

Examples

Pyrethrum plant species
Botanists once classified several species now in Tanacetum under the genus Pyrethrum.
The dried flowers of Pyrethrum are commonly used to produce a natural insecticide.
The historical use of Pyrethrum in medicine dates back many centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Chrysanthemum: a genus of flowering plants, including the popular garden chrysanthemum, sometimes including plants from Pyrethrum
  • synonym – Tanacetum: a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, now often including species formerly in Pyrethrum
