sphinx

noun
Egyptian statue with lion's body and human head
Pronunciation: / sfɪŋks / (AmE), / sfɪŋks / (BrE)
A large stone statue with the body of a lion and the head of a man, built by the ancient Egyptians as a symbol of strength and wisdom.

Examples

the Great sphinx
Archaeologists are still uncovering the mysteries behind the sphinx and its origins.
The Great sphinx at Giza is one of Egypt's most famous monuments.
Tourists from around the world visit the ancient sphinx every year.
