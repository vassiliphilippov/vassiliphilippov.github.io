ordinary

noun
clergyman attending condemned prisoners
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɹ.dəˌnɛɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dɪ.n(ə).ɹi / (BrE)
(historical) A clergyman appointed to minister to condemned prisoners before execution.

Examples

ordinary prison chaplain
Historically, the ordinary would record confessions of those about to be executed.
Many accounts of the gallows were written by the ordinary of Newgate Prison.
The ordinary visited the condemned cell each evening to offer comfort to the prisoner.
