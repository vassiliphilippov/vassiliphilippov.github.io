mitigation

noun
lessening the seriousness of an offense
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪt̬ɪˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌmɪt̬.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌmɪt.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making an offense or its consequences seem less serious, often by giving excuses or explanations

Examples

mitigation of blame
In mitigation, she explained that she was unaware of the new policy.
The court considered several factors in mitigation before passing sentence.
The defendant's lawyer presented evidence in mitigation of his client's actions.
In mitigation, he explained that he had acted under extreme stress.
The defendant's lawyer argued for mitigation because of her difficult childhood.
The judge considered the circumstances put forward in mitigation before passing sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – aggravation: the act of making an offense or situation worse
  • synonym – alleviation: the act of making something less severe
  • synonym – justification: a reason or explanation that justifies an action
