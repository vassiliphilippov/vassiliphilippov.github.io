Aleut

noun
member of Aleutian indigenous people
Pronunciation: / əˈljuːt /; / əˈluːt / (AmE), / ˈæliːuːt /; / əˈluːt / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous people inhabiting the Aleutian Islands and southwestern Alaska

Examples

Aleut people community
Belonging to both Russian and American history, the Aleut community maintains a rich cultural heritage.
Many Aleut still practice traditional fishing and hunting methods handed down through generations.
The Aleut were among the first indigenous peoples encountered by Russian explorers in Alaska.
Anthropologists study the culture and language of the Aleut people.
Many Aleut now live both in traditional villages and in larger Alaskan cities.
The Aleut have a rich tradition of sea mammal hunting and fishing.

  • synonym – Inuit: a member of the indigenous people of northern Canada, Alaska, and Greenland
