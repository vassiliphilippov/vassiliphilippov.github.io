decidable

adjective
able to be resolved in formal logic
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsaɪdəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪdəbəl / (BrE)
(of a statement or problem in logic or mathematics) capable of being resolved as true or false by a defined procedure within a formal system.

Examples

decidable problem in logic
A decidable set is one for which a definite yes-or-no answer can be calculated.
Gödel's incompleteness theorem shows that some mathematical propositions are not decidable within certain systems.
It is important to determine whether a given problem is decidable or not when developing algorithms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – undecidable: not capable of being resolved as true or false
