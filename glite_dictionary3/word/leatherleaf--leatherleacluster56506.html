leatherleaf

noun
bog shrub with leathery evergreen leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛðərˌlif / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌliːf / (BrE)
A low-growing evergreen shrub of boggy areas in northern temperate regions, having leathery leaves and small white cylindrical flowers, especially Chamaedaphne calyculata.

Examples

leatherleaf shrub in bog
Botanists often study leatherleaf to understand adaptations to bog environments.
In early spring, the leatherleaf produces small clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers.
The leatherleaf thrives in wetlands and provides ground cover for many species.
