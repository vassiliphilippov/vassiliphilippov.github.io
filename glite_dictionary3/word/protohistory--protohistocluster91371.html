protohistory

noun
study of humans before writing
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtoʊˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
the study of human societies in the period before written records but after the earliest indirect historical sources, such as archaeological findings or accounts from literate neighbors

Examples

protohistory of Europe
Courses on protohistory often cover both archaeological methods and comparative studies with early written cultures.
Her research focuses on the protohistory of ancient Europe, just before the adoption of writing systems.
The discovery of new artifacts has expanded our understanding of protohistory in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – prehistory: the period of human history before the existence of written records
