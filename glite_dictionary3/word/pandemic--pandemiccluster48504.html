pandemic

adjective
widespread epidemic disease (adj.)
Pronunciation: / pænˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / panˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease) occurring over a wide geographical area and affecting an exceptionally high proportion of the population.

Examples

pandemic disease spread
Public health organizations monitor the risk of a pandemic disease spreading rapidly worldwide.
The authorities declared COVID-19 a pandemic health emergency in 2020.
There is growing concern about a pandemic flu outbreak in several countries.

  • degree lower – epidemic: (of a disease or occurrence) affecting many individuals within a community or region at the same time
