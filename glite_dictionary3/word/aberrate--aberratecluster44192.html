to aberrate

verb
deviate from a straight path (technical)
Pronunciation: / ˈæbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈæbəˌreɪt / (BrE)
(technical) To deviate from a straight path or from the normal form; to cause or produce aberration, especially in optics.

Examples

to aberrate a light beam
Engineers worked to correct the way the device would aberrate under certain light conditions.
The telescope's mirror began to aberrate after years of exposure to extreme temperature changes.
When a lens is not properly shaped, it may aberrate and distort the image.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aberration: a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected
  • synonym – deviate: to depart from an established course or norm
