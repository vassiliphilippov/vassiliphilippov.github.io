fret

noun
raised bar on instrument fingerboard
Pronunciation: / frɛt / (AmE), / frɛt / (BrE)
a thin metal strip set across the fingerboard of a stringed musical instrument, used to produce notes of different pitches when the string is pressed against it

Examples

guitar fret
Mandolins typically have twenty-one frets on their necks.
The guitarist pressed the string firmly behind the third fret to play a C note.
The luthier replaced the worn frets on the old guitar to improve its sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fingerboard: the part of a stringed instrument over which the strings are stretched
