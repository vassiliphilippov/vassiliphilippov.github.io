to slack

verb
make less tight or loose
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
to make something less tight or release tension on it, especially a rope or similar object

Examples

to slack a rope
If you slack the tension on the cable, it will be easier to adjust.
Please slack the rope a little so the boat can move freely.
The sailor was told to slack the line before securing the sail.

  • antonym – tighten: to make something tighter or more tense
