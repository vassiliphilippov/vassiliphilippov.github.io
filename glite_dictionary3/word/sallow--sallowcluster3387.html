sallow

noun
type of shrubby broad-leaved willow
Pronunciation: / ˈsæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaləʊ / (BrE)
a shrubby or small tree of the willow genus (Salix), native to Europe and Asia, with broad leaves and large catkins, some species of which are valued for their bark and as sources of charcoal.

Examples

broad-leaved sallow tree
Some species of sallow are traditionally harvested for their tanbark.
The sallow thrives in moist soils along riverbanks and wetlands.
The broad leaves of the sallow distinguish it from other types of willows.

  • synonym – willow: a tree or shrub of the genus Salix with slender branches and narrow leaves
