corrupt

adjective
containing errors or alterations (text, data, etc.)
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of something, especially text, data, or language) containing errors, alterations, or degeneration from the original form.

Examples

corrupt file
A corrupt version of the software caused unexpected errors during installation.
Scholars struggled to interpret the corrupt manuscript found in the ruins.
The document was unreadable because the file was corrupt.

Related Terms

  • synonym – damaged: impaired or harmed in function or condition
  • antonym – intact: remaining whole or untouched
