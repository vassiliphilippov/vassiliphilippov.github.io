anticholinesterase

noun
drug inhibiting cholinesterase enzyme
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˌkoʊ.lɪˈnɛs.təˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌkəʊ.lɪˈnɛs.təˌreɪs / (BrE)
a drug or substance that inhibits the action of cholinesterase, thereby enhancing the action of acetylcholine in the body

Examples

anticholinesterase drug treatment
Overdose of an anticholinesterase can result in toxic symptoms due to excess acetylcholine.
Physostigmine is an anticholinesterase used in the treatment of glaucoma.
The doctor prescribed an anticholinesterase to help manage the patient's muscle weakness.
