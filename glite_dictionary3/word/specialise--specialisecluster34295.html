to specialise

verb
evolve or adapt to a specific environment or role
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of an organism) to evolve or adapt so as to become particularly suited to a specific environment, role, or function.

Examples

animals specialise for survival
As the climate changed, the mammal population gradually specialised to survive in the new environment.
Over many generations, the finches specialised for different food sources on the islands.
Some insects specialise in feeding on a single type of plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adapt: to change so as to become suited to new conditions
