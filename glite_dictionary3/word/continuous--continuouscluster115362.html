continuous

adjective
extending without break or irregularity
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪn.ju.əs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əs / (BrE)
forming an unbroken whole; continuing without interruption in time or space (including in mathematics: without breaks or jumps)

Examples

continuous line
A continuous curve has no gaps or jumps.
Mathematicians proved the solution was continuous over the entire interval.
The graph of the function is continuous at every point in its domain.
A continuous line of cars stretched for miles along the highway.
She managed to work for five continuous hours without taking a break.
The rain was continuous throughout the night, making it impossible to sleep soundly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discontinuous: not continuous; having breaks or interruptions
  • synonym – incessant: continuing without pause or interruption; never ceasing
  • antonym – intermittent: occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady
