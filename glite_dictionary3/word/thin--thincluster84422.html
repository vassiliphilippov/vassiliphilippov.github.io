thin

adjective
not dense; sparse
Pronunciation: / θɪn / (AmE), / θɪn / (BrE)
having a low density or containing few elements, parts, or members over an area or in a substance

Examples

thin layer
A thin layer of fog hovered over the field in the early morning.
He grew a thin patchy mustache during the winter.
The forest was so thin that sunlight reached the ground easily.
At high elevations, the thin air can make breathing more difficult.
Skimmed milk is much thinner than cream.
We made a thin vegetable soup to start the meal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dense: closely packed together; opposite of thin (not dense)
  • synonym – sparse: thinly dispersed or scattered; synonym for thin (not dense)
  • antonym – thick: having a relatively high density or viscosity; opposite of thin in this context
