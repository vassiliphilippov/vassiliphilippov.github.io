to capsule

verb
enclose in a capsule
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.sul / (AmE), / ˈkæp.sjuːl / (BrE)
to enclose something in a small container or capsule, typically for protection or administration

Examples

to capsule medicine
Researchers are working to capsule the vaccine for easier distribution.
Scientists often capsule medication to make it easier to swallow.
The tiny robot was capsuled before being injected into the patient's bloodstream.
