inhibition

noun
physiological prevention of organ activity
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The process by which nerve signals slow down or prevent the activity of an organ or part of the body.

Examples

nerve inhibition
Research shows that inhibition of certain enzymes can reduce inflammation in tissues.
Some medications work by increasing neuronal inhibition within the brain.
The inhibition of the heart rate by the vagus nerve is well documented.

Related Terms

  • antonym – excitation: (Physiology) stimulation that increases the activity of an organ or part
