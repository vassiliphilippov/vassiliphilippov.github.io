to bore

verb
make a hole with a tool
Pronunciation: / bɔr / (AmE), / bɔː / (BrE)
to make a hole in something, especially with a pointed power or hand tool

Examples

to bore a hole
Beavers often bore holes into tree trunks to build their homes.
Engineers will bore a tunnel through the mountain for the new railway line.
The carpenter bored a hole into the wooden plank for the screw.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drill: to make a hole in something with a rotating tool
  • synonym – pierce: to make a hole by pushing through something sharply
