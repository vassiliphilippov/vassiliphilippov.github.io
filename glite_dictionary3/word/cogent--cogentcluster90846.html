cogent

adjective
clear, convincing argument
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdʒənt / (BrE)
(of an argument or case) clear, logical, and convincing; powerfully persuasive.

Examples

cogent argument
His essay was so cogent that it persuaded even the most skeptical readers.
She presented several cogent reasons for changing the policy.
The lawyer made a cogent case that convinced everyone in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym – convincing: causing someone to believe that something is true or real
  • antonym – unconvincing: not seeming convincing or persuasive
