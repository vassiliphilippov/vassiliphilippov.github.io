mulch

noun
protective garden covering material
Pronunciation: / mʌltʃ / (AmE), / mʌltʃ / (BrE)
Material such as decaying leaves, bark, or compost spread around or over a plant to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and improve soil quality.

Examples

garden mulch
After planting the shrubs, she spread mulch around their bases to prevent weeds.
Gardeners often add a layer of mulch to keep the soil moist during summer.
Wood chips make an effective and attractive mulch for flower beds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compost: decayed organic material used as plant fertilizer
