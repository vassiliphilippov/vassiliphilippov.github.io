to bustle

verb
move energetically or busily
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌsl̩ / (BrE)
to move energetically and busily; to be full of activity or movement

Examples

to bustle around the kitchen
She bustled into the office, balancing a stack of papers.
The kitchen staff bustled around, preparing dinner for the guests.
The market square bustled with shoppers and street vendors on Saturday mornings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dawdle: to move slowly and waste time
  • synonym – scurry: to move quickly and with short steps
