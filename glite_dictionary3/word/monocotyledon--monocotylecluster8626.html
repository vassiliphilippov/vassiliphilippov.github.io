monocotyledon

noun
a flowering plant with one seed leaf
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˌkɑːtəˈlidn̩ / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəkɒtɪˈliːdən / (BrE)
A plant whose seed has a single embryonic leaf (cotyledon), belonging to a large group of flowering plants that includes grasses, lilies, and palms.

Examples

monocotyledon plant group
Banana plants are a well-known example of a monocotyledon found in tropical regions.
Most grasses are classified as monocotyledons, having only one seed leaf when they germinate.
The monocotyledon structure can be observed in plants such as lilies and orchids.
