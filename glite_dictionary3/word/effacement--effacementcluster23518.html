effacement

noun
thinning of cervix during labor
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the process by which the cervix becomes shorter and thinner during labor, preparing for childbirth

Examples

cervical effacement
During active labor, cervical effacement must reach nearly 100% before delivery is possible.
Early effacement of the cervix can be an indicator that labor is progressing.
The doctor monitored the patient's effacement and dilation during her contractions.
