to mark

verb
to brand or stigmatize as disgraceful
Pronunciation: / mɑrk / (AmE), / mɑːk / (BrE)
to be or to make someone or something a distinctive feature, attribute, or characteristic, often in a notable or negative way (including branding, stigma, or notable traits)

Examples

to mark someone
Once accused, he was publicly marked and could never regain his former reputation.
She was unfairly marked by rumors and lost many friends.
The scandal marked him as an outcast in the community.
Curiosity marked the early years of her scientific research.
Her attention to detail marks her as an exceptional manager.
Integrity and dedication marked his entire career in public service.
