to nullify

verb
make legally invalid
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪ /; / ˈnʌl.ɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈnʌlɪfʌɪ /; / ˈnʌl.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something legally invalid or without effect; to cancel officially

Examples

to nullify a contract
A single mistake can nullify months of careful preparation.
If any part of the contract is found to be illegal, it could nullify the entire agreement.
The court's decision will nullify the previous ruling if it finds the evidence insufficient.
A new law could nullify previous agreements between the two companies.
If one party breaches the agreement, it may nullify the contract entirely.
The court decided to nullify the contract due to fraudulent terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym – annul: to declare something invalid, especially in a legal context
  • synonym – cancel: to make something no longer valid or effective
  • synonym – invalidate: to make something not valid
  • antonym – validate: to make something valid or confirm its validity
  • synonym – void: to declare something to be without legal force
