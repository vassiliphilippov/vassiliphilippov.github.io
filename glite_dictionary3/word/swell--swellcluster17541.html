swell

noun
rounded elevation, esp. on ocean floor
Pronunciation: / swɛl / (AmE), / swɛl / (BrE)
a rounded or raised part of a surface, or a long, smooth wave or movement on the sea

Examples

ocean swell
A series of underwater swells created unique habitats for marine life.
Researchers mapped a swell that rose sharply from the ocean floor.
The submarine passed over a broad swell on the sea bed.
After the storm had passed, a steady swell continued to roll toward the shore.
Surfers waited patiently for the next big swell to arrive.
The boat rocked gently over the swell of the open ocean.
