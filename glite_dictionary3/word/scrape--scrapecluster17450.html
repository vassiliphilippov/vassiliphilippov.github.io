scrape

noun
a deep formal bow indicating humility
Pronunciation: / skreɪp / (AmE), / skreɪp / (BrE)
a deep formal bow made with the foot drawn backwards, traditionally to show exaggerated humility or deference

Examples

scrape before someone
His constant scrapes before his employer bordered on absurdity.
In old novels, characters sometimes greet nobility with a formal scrape.
The butler gave a low scrape as the guests entered the grand hall.
