mephaquine

noun
an antimalarial drug, also called Lariam
Pronunciation: / məˈfæk.wɪn / (AmE), / məˈfækwɪn / (BrE)
An antimalarial drug also known by the trade names Lariam and Mephaquine, effective in cases resistant to chloroquine but sometimes associated with harmful neuropsychiatric effects.

Examples

mephaquine malaria treatment
Due to potential side effects, some travelers avoid taking mephaquine for malaria prevention.
Severe neuropsychiatric reactions have occasionally been reported after using mephaquine.
The doctor prescribed mephaquine for her upcoming trip to a malaria-endemic region.
