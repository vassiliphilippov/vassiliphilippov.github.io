trophotropism

noun
an organism's orienting response to food
Pronunciation: / ˌtroʊfəˈtroʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtrɒfəˈtrəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
a physiological response in which an organism or part of an organism orients or adjusts itself towards a source of food.

Examples

trophotropism toward nutrients
Certain protozoa exhibit trophotropism by moving towards nutrients in their environment.
