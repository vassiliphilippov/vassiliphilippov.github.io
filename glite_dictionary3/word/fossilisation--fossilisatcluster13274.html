fossilisation

noun
process of becoming rigid or outdated
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːsəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɒsɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming rigid, fixed, or outdated, especially in ideas, systems, or practices

Examples

fossilisation of ideas
Language fossilisation can result in learners retaining persistent errors despite continued practice.
Some critics argue that fossilisation in government bureaucracy prevents effective reform.
The fossilisation of old traditions can make it hard for organizations to adapt to change.

Related Terms

  • antonym – innovation: the introduction of new ideas or methods
  • synonym – stagnation: lack of movement or progress; inactivity
