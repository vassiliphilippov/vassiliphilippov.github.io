Tamandua

noun
a genus of lesser anteaters
Pronunciation: / təˈmændu.ə / (AmE), / təˈmændjʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of anteaters native to Central and South America, known as the lesser anteaters, characterized by their long snouts and prehensile tails.

Examples

Tamandua climbing trees
Researchers observed a Tamandua climbing trees in search of ants and termites for food.
The Tamandua is often called the lesser anteater due to its smaller size compared to its relatives.
Unlike the giant anteater, the Tamandua spends much of its time in trees.
