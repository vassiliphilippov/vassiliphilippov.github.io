laryngitis

noun
inflammation of the larynx
Pronunciation: / ˌlærɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌlarɪnˈdʒʌɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the larynx, typically resulting in hoarseness or loss of voice and a cough.

Examples

laryngitis after shouting
After shouting at the concert, he developed laryngitis and could barely speak.
Teachers are often at risk of laryngitis due to frequent speaking in loud environments.
The doctor diagnosed her with laryngitis after she complained of hoarseness and a sore throat.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – larynx: the voice box; a part of the respiratory tract
  • synonym – pharyngitis: inflammation of the pharynx
