prolixity

noun
tedious verbosity or wordiness
Pronunciation: / proʊˈlɪksəti / (AmE), / prəˈlɪksəti / (BrE)
the use of too many words in speech or writing, making it tedious or boring

Examples

avoid prolixity in writing
Editors often cut prolixity from manuscripts to improve their clarity and readability.
His tendency toward prolixity overshadowed the main point of his argument.
The professor's prolixity made it difficult for students to stay engaged during the lecture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conciseness: the use of as few words as possible
  • synonym – verbosity: the quality of using more words than needed
