holey

adjective
full of holes; perforated
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli / (BrE)
having many holes; allowing passage through because of being perforated or not solid.

Examples

holey socks
After years of wear, his favorite socks were so holey that his toes stuck out.
The cheese was famously holey, with large gaps formed during fermentation.
The old roof had become holey, leaking every time it rained.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compact: closely and firmly packed together, not allowing passage
  • synonym – porous: having minute spaces or holes that allow liquid or air to pass through
