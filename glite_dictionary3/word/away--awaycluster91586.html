away

adverb
from one's possession or place
Pronunciation: / əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to or from another place, position, or possession; to remove, take, or move something so it is no longer present (physically or metaphorically)

Examples

throw away trash
After cleaning the attic, she threw away many old magazines.
He gave away all his childhood toys to charity.
They handed away the prizes at the end of the contest.
Please put your toys away when you're finished playing.
She swept the crumbs away after dinner.
The old sign was taken away and replaced with a new one.
Please turn your head away while I change the bandage.
She quickly looked away when their eyes met.
The dog runs away whenever it hears loud noises.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aside: so as to be put or kept out of the way, specifically out of one's thoughts; synonym in this figurative sense
  • synonym – gone: no longer present or existing
  • antonym – remain: to continue to exist, especially after other parts have disappeared or been removed
  • antonym – toward: in the direction of something
  • synonym – vanish: to disappear suddenly and completely
