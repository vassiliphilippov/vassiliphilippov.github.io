telegraph

noun
device for sending messages over wire
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a system or device for sending messages over long distances by wire, especially using Morse code

Examples

old telegraph machine
Before the telephone, messages were sent using the telegraph and Morse code.
She received news from her family via telegraph when she lived abroad.
The telegraph revolutionized long-distance communication in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym – telegram: a message sent by telegraph
  • antonym – telephone: device for transmitting speech over distance by wire or radio
