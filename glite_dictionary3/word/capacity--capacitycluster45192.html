capacity

noun
alcohol tolerance; limit before intoxication
Pronunciation: / kəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪti / (BrE)
the ability or power to do, experience, or understand something (such as mental ability or tolerance for alcohol)

Examples

mental capacity
He quickly found his capacity when he tried to keep up with the locals.
Her capacity for wine surprised everyone at the party.
I admire his capacity for drinking without showing any signs of being tipsy.
His mental capacity was assessed by a team of doctors during the trial.
She has an incredible capacity for remembering facts and details from her studies.
The judge questioned whether the defendant had the capacity to understand the charges against him.

Related Terms

  • synonym – competence: the ability to do something successfully or efficiently; in law, legal ability
  • antonym – incapacity: the lack of ability to do something, especially due to mental or physical condition
  • synonym – tolerance: ability to endure effects of a substance, especially alcohol or drugs
