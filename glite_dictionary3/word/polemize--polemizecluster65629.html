to polemize

verb
engage in controversial debate
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɛməˌzaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈliːməˌzaɪz / (BrE)
to engage in or conduct a contentious argument or debate, especially in writing or speech

Examples

to polemize in debate
Historians frequently polemize over the interpretation of key historical events.
She tends to polemize on social media, often stirring heated arguments.
The two professors continued to polemize about the origins of the theory at every conference.

  • antonym – agree: to have the same opinion

  • synonym – debate: to discuss reasons for or against something
