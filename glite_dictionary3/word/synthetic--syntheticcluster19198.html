synthetic

adjective
(logic) empirical proposition; not true by definition
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
involving synthesis; combining separate elements to form a whole, especially in science or philosophy

Examples

synthetic reasoning process
Logical positivists distinguished between analytic and synthetic propositions in their philosophy.
The statement 'all bachelors are unmarried' is analytic, while 'all bachelors are unhappy' is a synthetic claim.
To verify a synthetic statement, one must consult empirical facts, not just meanings.
Her argument was highly synthetic, drawing together theories from philosophy, psychology, and sociology.
Modern linguistics often uses both synthetic and analytic approaches when studying language evolution.
The report presented a synthetic summary of data from multiple disciplines.
