corruptible

adjective
easily influenced to become dishonest
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptəbl̩ / (BrE)
easily influenced to do something dishonest or immoral; susceptible to corruption.

Examples

corruptible government official
A corruptible official could undermine the public's trust in the entire system.
As the years passed, she proved herself to be anything but corruptible in her dealings.
The committee ensured that only those with impeccable reputations, and not corruptible individuals, were appointed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – incorruptible: not susceptible to corruption or bribery
