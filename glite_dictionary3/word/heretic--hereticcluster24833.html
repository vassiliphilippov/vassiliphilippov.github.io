heretic

noun
person with unorthodox religious beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrətɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛrətɪk / (BrE)
a person who holds beliefs or opinions that are against the official or accepted teachings, especially in religion

Examples

religious heretic
Galileo was once accused of being a heretic for supporting the heliocentric model.
In the Middle Ages, the church sometimes executed a heretic for challenging its authority.
