to proscribe

verb
forbid or ban by authority
Pronunciation: / proʊˈskraɪb / (AmE), / prəʊˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to forbid or prohibit something, especially by law or authority

Examples

to proscribe certain activities
Certain books were proscribed by the government for their controversial content.
The new policy proscribes the use of mobile phones during class hours.
The organization's rules proscribe any form of discrimination among its members.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ban: to officially or legally prohibit
  • synonym – forbid: to order someone not to do something; refuse to allow
  • antonym – permit: to allow something to happen
