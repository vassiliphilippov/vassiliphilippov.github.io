cutlassfish

noun
long-bodied marine fish with sharp teeth
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌt.ləsˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.ləsˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A long-bodied marine fish with a whiplike scaleless body and sharp teeth, closely related to the snake mackerel.

Examples

silver cutlassfish
A school of cutlassfish was seen swimming near the surface just before sunset.
Fishermen off the coast of Japan often catch cutlassfish due to their abundance in local waters.
The cutlassfish is easily recognized by its shiny, elongated body and formidable teeth.
