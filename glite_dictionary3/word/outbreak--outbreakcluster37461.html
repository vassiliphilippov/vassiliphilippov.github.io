outbreak

noun
sudden beginning of something unwelcome
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbreɪk / (BrE)
a sudden start or occurrence of something unwelcome, such as war, disease, or violence

Examples

an outbreak of disease
Officials tried to contain the outbreak before it could spread further.
The sudden outbreak of violence caught everyone by surprise.
There was an outbreak of influenza in the city last winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – epidemic: a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease
  • antonym – peace: a state of tranquility or the absence of war or violence
