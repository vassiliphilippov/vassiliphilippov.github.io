galaxy

noun
large system of stars in space
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæl.əksi / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.əksi / (BrE)
A system of millions or billions of stars, together with gas and dust, held together by gravitational attraction.

Examples

spiral galaxy
Astronomers discovered a new galaxy over ten billion light-years away.
Each galaxy can contain billions of stars and massive clouds of gas.
The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our solar system.
