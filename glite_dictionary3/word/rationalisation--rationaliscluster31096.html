rationalisation

noun
simplification by removing radicals (maths)
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The process of simplifying an expression or equation by removing radicals, without altering its value or the roots of the equation.

Examples

rationalisation of equations
Students are often asked to practise rationalisation when simplifying mathematical expressions.
The rationalisation of the denominator is a common technique in algebra.
Through rationalisation, the square root was removed from the fraction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – simplification: the process of making something simpler or easier to do or understand
