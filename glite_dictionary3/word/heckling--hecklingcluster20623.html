heckling

noun
disrupting a speaker with interruptions
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛk.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shouting comments or interruptions to disrupt a speaker, especially in disagreement

Examples

audience heckling a speaker
Loud heckling made it difficult for the comedian to finish his routine.
Security removed an audience member for repeated heckling during the presentation.
The politician was frustrated by constant heckling from the crowd during her speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym – applauding: showing approval by clapping or cheering
  • synonym – interrupting: speaking or acting to stop someone temporarily
