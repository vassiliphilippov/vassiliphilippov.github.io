sensation

noun
a feeling of excitement or heightened interest
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a general feeling of excitement and heightened interest, often caused by anticipation or something remarkable

Examples

exciting sensation
She felt a strange sensation of excitement every time she walked on stage.
The news of the discovery created a sensation in the scientific community.
Winning the competition gave him a sensation unlike anything he had experienced before.

Related Terms

  • antonym – boredom: a state of being weary and uninterested due to lack of excitement
  • synonym – thrill: a sudden feeling of excitement and pleasure
