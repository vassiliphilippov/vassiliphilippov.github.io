Slavophilia

noun
Russian movement favoring traditional Slavic values
Pronunciation: / ˌslævəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌslɑːvəˈfɪliə / (BrE)
A 19th-century intellectual movement in Russia that advocated developing the Russian Empire based on traditional Slavic values and institutions.

His interest in Slavophilia led him to study Slavic folklore and Orthodox traditions.
