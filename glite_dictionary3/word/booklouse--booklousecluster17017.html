booklouse

noun
a tiny insect found in books and papers
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkˌlaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbʊk.laʊs / (BrE)
A very small, wingless insect that is often found among old books and papers and can cause damage to them.

Examples

booklouse in old books
Conservators carefully inspected the archives for signs of booklouse activity.
If your books develop a musty odor, check for booklouse infestations in the pages.
The old library was infested with booklice that damaged many rare volumes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – psocid: a small, soft-bodied insect of the order Psocoptera
