scab

noun
person working during a strike (derogatory)
Pronunciation: / skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
a person who works or provides workers during a strike, especially as a replacement for striking workers; considered derogatory by many

Examples

scab during strike
He lost the support of his colleagues after being labeled a scab during the factory strike.
The company hired several scabs to keep production going while the employees were on strike.
Union members shouted at the scab who crossed the picket line this morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – strikebreaker: a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike
