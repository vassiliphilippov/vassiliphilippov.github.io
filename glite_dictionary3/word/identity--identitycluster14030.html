identity

noun
math operator leaving elements unchanged
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)
in mathematics, an element or property that leaves something unchanged, or the state of being exactly the same

Examples

identity element
In group theory, the identity element plays a crucial role in defining the structure.
Multiplying any number by the identity, which is 1, does not change its value.
Zero acts as the identity element in addition, as adding zero leaves numbers unchanged.
In mathematics, the identity of two expressions means they are exactly equal for all values.
The two samples shared an identity in chemical composition.
There was complete identity of opinion among the committee members.
