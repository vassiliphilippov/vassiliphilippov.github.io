duskiness

noun
state of being dimly lit
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌskinəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌskinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dimly lit or lacking bright illumination

Examples

evening duskiness outdoors
As night fell, the duskiness in the garden grew deeper and shadows lengthened.
Her eyes adjusted slowly to the duskiness inside the abandoned house.
The duskiness of the old library added to its mysterious charm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – brightness: the state of being very well lit or luminous
  • synonym – dimness: the state of being somewhat dark or lacking light
