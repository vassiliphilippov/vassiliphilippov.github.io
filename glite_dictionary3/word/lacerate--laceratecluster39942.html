to lacerate

verb
hurt or distress deeply (emotionally)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæsəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlasəreɪt / (BrE)
to deeply hurt or distress someone emotionally; to wound emotionally

Examples

to lacerate her feelings
He lacerated her feelings with his cruel criticism.
Her harsh words lacerated his confidence, leaving him feeling utterly defeated.
The novel's ending lacerates readers with unexpected tragedy and loss.

Related Terms

  • antonym – comfort: to soothe or ease emotional pain
  • synonym – injure: to cause hurt or harm, including emotionally
  • synonym – wound: to cause emotional pain or distress
