perineotomy

noun
surgical incision of the perineum
Pronunciation: / pɛrəˌniːˈɑtəmi / (AmE), / pɛrɪˌniːˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision made into the perineum, often to facilitate childbirth or surgery

Examples

perineotomy during childbirth
A perineotomy should only be done when medically indicated to avoid unnecessary trauma.
Recent studies have examined the benefits and risks of performing a perineotomy.
The doctor decided a perineotomy was necessary during the complicated delivery.
