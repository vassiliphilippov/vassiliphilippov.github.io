Guru Nanak

proper noun
founder of Sikhism; Indian religious leader
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʊru nɑːˈnɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡʊruː ˈnʌnək / (BrE)
(1469–1539) The Indian religious leader who founded Sikhism and taught the equality of all people and the importance of meditation on God's name.

Examples

Guru Nanak founded Sikhism
Guru Nanak traveled extensively to spread his message of spiritual liberation and social justice.
Many Sikhs make a pilgrimage to places associated with Guru Nanak every year.
The teachings of Guru Nanak emphasize kindness, equality, and devotion to God.
