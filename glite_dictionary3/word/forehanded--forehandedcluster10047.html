forehanded

adjective
prepared for the future
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈhændɪd / (BrE)
having made provision for the future; prepared or prudent, especially in matters of finance or planning

Examples

forehanded financial planning
Farmers who are forehanded plant extra crops in case of a poor harvest.
He was praised for being forehanded and never leaving things to chance.
Her forehanded approach to saving ensured a comfortable retirement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – improvident: not providing for the future; lacking foresight
  • synonym – prudent: acting with care and thought for the future
