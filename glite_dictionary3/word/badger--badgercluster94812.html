to badger

verb
to pester or nag persistently
Pronunciation: / ˈbædʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈbadʒə / (BrE)
to repeatedly pester, annoy, or pressure someone with persistent requests or questions

Examples

to badger a friend
His little sister would badger him until he agreed to play the game.
I felt annoyed when my coworkers badgered me about joining their project.
The reporter continued to badger the politician with tough questions.
She felt badgered by her parents to study harder for the exam.
Stop badgering me about my decision—I've already made up my mind.
The reporters continued to badger the politician with the same questions all morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – harass: to bother or pressure persistently
  • antonym – leave alone: to refrain from bothering or disturbing someone
  • synonym – pester: to trouble or annoy someone with frequent requests
