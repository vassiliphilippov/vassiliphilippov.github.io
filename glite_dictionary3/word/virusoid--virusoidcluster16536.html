virusoid

noun
smallest type of plant virus (circular RNA)
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪrəsˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪərəsɔɪd / (BrE)
a subviral agent, smaller than a virus, found in plants, consisting of circular RNA without a protein coat

Examples

plant virusoid infection
A virusoid can only replicate in the presence of a helper virus.
Because a virusoid lacks a protein coat, it relies entirely on its RNA for infection.
Researchers discovered a new virusoid affecting certain crop species.
