to administer

verb
direct or oversee the giving/taking of something formal
Pronunciation: / ədˈmɪnəstɚ /; / ədˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
to formally oversee, direct, or perform the giving or taking of something, such as an exam, oath, or religious ceremony

Examples

to administer a test
A proctor was assigned to administer the standardized tests to the students.
Only a judge can administer the oath of office to the new mayor.
The teacher will administer the final exam this Friday.
During the ceremony, the bishop will administer confirmation to the candidates.
Only ordained clergy are allowed to administer the sacrament of communion in this church.
The priest was called to administer the last rites to the patient.
