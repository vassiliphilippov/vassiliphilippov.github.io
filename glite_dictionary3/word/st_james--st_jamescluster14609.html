St. James

proper noun
apostle of Jesus in the New Testament
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, the brother of John, and traditionally credited as the author of the Epistle of James.
