moiety

noun
one of two subdivisions of a tribe
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔɪ.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪ.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
either of two social or kinship groups into which a tribe is divided, especially in some Indigenous cultures.

Examples

the Eagle moiety
Anthropologists studied how each moiety played a distinct role in ceremonial life.
Members of the Eagle moiety were required to marry members of the Wolf moiety.
The tribe was traditionally divided into two moieties, each with its own totems and customs.
