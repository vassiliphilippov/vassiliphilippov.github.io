to prepare

verb
arrange by systematic planning
Pronunciation: / prɪˈpɛr / (AmE), / prɪˈpeə / (BrE)
to arrange, organize, or make something ready for use, an event, or a particular purpose (including making plans or documents)

Examples

to prepare a plan
It took months to prepare the logistics for the festival.
The committee will prepare a detailed proposal before the end of the month.
They worked together to prepare the plan for the school fundraiser.
She always prepares a detailed plan before starting a new project.
The teacher helped the students prepare for their final exams.
We need to prepare the room for the meeting this afternoon.
He prepared detailed notes to accompany his presentation.
I need to prepare a report for tomorrow's meeting.
She spent all night preparing her speech for the conference.

  • antonym – neglect: to fail to prepare or care for properly
  • synonym – organize: to arrange or structure systematically
