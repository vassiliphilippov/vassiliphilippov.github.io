fly

noun
two-winged flying insect
Pronunciation: / flaɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ / (BrE)
a small insect with two wings, known for its active flight and ability to move quickly, especially of the order Diptera

Examples

a buzzing fly
A fly landed on my sandwich at the picnic.
Some species of fly can transmit diseases to humans.
There is a fly buzzing around the kitchen window.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mosquito: a slender, long-legged fly known for biting and sucking blood
  • antonym – wasp: an insect similar in appearance but not a true fly, with a narrow waist and sting
