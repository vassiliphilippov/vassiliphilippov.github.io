postganglionic

adjective
beyond or distal to a ganglion (of nerves)
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstɡæŋɡliˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstɡæŋɡlɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of nerves or fibers) situated beyond or distal to a ganglion, especially those that originate from cells in autonomic ganglia.

Examples

postganglionic nerve fibers
A physician studied the difference between preganglionic and postganglionic axons.
Damage to postganglionic neurons can affect the function of internal organs.
The postganglionic fibers of the sympathetic nervous system are typically unmyelinated.

Related Terms

  • antonym – preganglionic: situated before or proximal to a ganglion (in neuroanatomy)
