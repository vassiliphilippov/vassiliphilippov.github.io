stridulation

noun
chirping noise made by insects rubbing body parts
Pronunciation: / ˌstrɪdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstrɪdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of producing a shrill, grating, or chirping noise by rubbing certain body parts together, especially as done by insects or arthropods

Examples

insect stridulation sound
Katydids communicate with each other through stridulation in the dense forest foliage.
Scientists recorded the stridulation produced by beetles during mating rituals.
The stridulation of crickets became louder as night fell over the field.
