offstage

adjective
out of view of the audience (theatre)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
Situated or taking place in the area of a stage not visible to the audience.

Examples

offstage entrance
The actor waited offstage for her cue.
The director signaled for the lights to dim offstage.
We could hear mysterious sounds coming from offstage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – onstage: situated or taking place on the part of the stage visible to the audience
