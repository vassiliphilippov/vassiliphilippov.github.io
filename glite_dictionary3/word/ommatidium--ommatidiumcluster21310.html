ommatidium

noun
unit of an arthropod's compound eye
Pronunciation: / ˌɑməˈtɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌɒməˈtɪdiəm / (BrE)
any of the small, cone-shaped units that collectively form the compound eye of arthropods such as insects and crustaceans.

Examples

compound eye ommatidium
A dragonfly has thousands of ommatidia in each compound eye, allowing it to track fast-moving prey.
Damage to even a single ommatidium can affect the overall vision of a honeybee.
Each ommatidium functions as a separate visual receptor, contributing to the insect's wide field of vision.
