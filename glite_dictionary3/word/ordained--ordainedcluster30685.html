ordained

adjective
fixed or established by order
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈdeɪnd / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪnd / (BrE)
(of a rule, condition, or period) fixed or established, especially by order or authority.

Examples

ordained period
Plans unfolded as the ordained sequence of events was carried out.
She followed the ordained procedures set out by the committee.
The meeting will take place at the ordained hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appointed: fixed or set officially; assigned for a purpose or time
  • antonym – arbitrary: based on random choice or personal whim, not fixed by rule
  • synonym – prescribed: set down as a rule or guide; established authoritatively
