to expose

verb
abandon by leaving unprotected in the open
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to abandon someone, especially a young child or infant, by leaving them in a place where they are unprotected or at risk, typically outdoors

Examples

to expose a baby outdoors
In ancient times, unwanted infants were sometimes exposed on mountainsides.
It was illegal to expose a child, but such cases were hard to prevent.
Stories tell of a king who exposed his heir to eliminate the threat to his throne.
