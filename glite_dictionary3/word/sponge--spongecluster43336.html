to sponge

verb
obtain things for free from others
Pronunciation: / spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
to obtain money, food, or other benefits from someone by continually taking advantage of their generosity; to live at another's expense.

Examples

to sponge money
After losing his job, he started sponging money from his relatives to get by.
He always tries to sponge off his friends instead of paying for meals himself.
She didn't want to sponge on anyone's hospitality during her visit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mooch: to obtain or try to obtain something without paying for it
  • synonym – scrounge: to obtain something by seeking it from others, often without paying
