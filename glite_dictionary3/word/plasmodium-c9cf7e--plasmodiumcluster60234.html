plasmodium

noun
multinucleate mass in slime molds
Pronunciation: / plæzˈmoʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / plæzˈməʊ.dɪ.əm / (BrE)
A mass of cytoplasm containing many nuclei, characteristic of certain life stages of slime molds and some other organisms, that moves and feeds as a single body.

Examples

plasmodium in slime mold
In favorable conditions, the plasmodium spreads out in search of food particles.
Scientists observed the movement of the plasmodium under a microscope.
The yellowish plasmodium slowly crept over the decaying leaf.
