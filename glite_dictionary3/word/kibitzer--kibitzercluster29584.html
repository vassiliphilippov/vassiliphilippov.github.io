kibitzer

noun
person who gives unsolicited advice
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪbɪtsər / (AmE), / ˈkɪbɪtsə / (BrE)
a person who offers unwanted or meddlesome advice, especially as a spectator; an onlooker who interrupts or comments without being asked

Examples

noisy kibitzer at games
Don’t be such a kibitzer—let me handle this myself!
During our chess match, a kibitzer kept pointing out possible moves to both players.
Every time we hold a meeting, that same kibitzer chimes in with advice nobody wants.

Related Terms

  • synonym – busybody: a person who meddles in others' affairs
  • synonym – meddler: a person who interferes in matters that do not concern them
