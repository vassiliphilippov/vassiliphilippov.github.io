direfully

adverb
in a direful or dreadful manner
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪərfəli / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəfəli / (BrE)
In a manner characterized by great fear, dread, or seriousness; extremely or terribly.

Examples

direfully warn of danger
He direfully predicted disaster if the warnings were ignored.
She spoke direfully of the dangers that lay ahead, warning everyone to be cautious.
The winds howled direfully through the abandoned house, unsettling all who passed by.
