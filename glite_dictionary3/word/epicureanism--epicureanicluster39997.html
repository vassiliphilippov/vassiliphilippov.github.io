epicureanism

noun
philosophy prioritizing pleasure, by Epicurus
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkjʊriːəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkjʊəriːəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophy that pleasure, especially the enjoyment of simple physical pleasures, is the highest good, as taught by Epicurus and his followers in ancient Greece

Examples

ancient epicureanism philosophy
Many people misunderstand epicureanism as a pursuit of luxury rather than simple pleasures.
She studied epicureanism as part of her course on ancient Greek philosophy.
The principles of epicureanism emphasize moderation and freedom from fear.
