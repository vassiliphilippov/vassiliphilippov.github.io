exfoliation

noun
shedding of skin or bark in flakes
Pronunciation: / ɛkˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛksˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of shedding or peeling off in thin flakes or scales, especially of the outer layer of skin or bark

Examples

regular exfoliation routine
Regular exfoliation helps remove dead skin cells and keep your complexion smooth.
Some trees experience natural exfoliation of their bark, revealing colorful layers underneath.
Sun damage can lead to increased exfoliation of the skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym – desquamation: the shedding of the outer layers of the skin
