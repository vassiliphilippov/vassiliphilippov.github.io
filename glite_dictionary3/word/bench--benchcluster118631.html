to bench

verb
temporarily suspend a proposal
Pronunciation: / bɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɛntʃ / (BrE)
to temporarily suspend or set aside consideration of a proposal or issue, especially in a meeting or committee setting.

Examples

to bench a proposal
After much debate, the board voted to bench the proposal rather than reject it outright.
Management chose to bench the proposal so they could review alternative options.
The committee decided to bench the proposal until more data became available.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delay: to put off until a later time
  • synonym – table: to postpone consideration of a proposal (especially US English)
