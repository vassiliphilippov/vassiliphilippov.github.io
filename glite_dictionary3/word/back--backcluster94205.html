to back

verb
support or confirm (a claim, statement, etc.)
Pronunciation: / bæk / (AmE), / bæk / (BrE)
to support, endorse, or give approval to someone or something (including financially or by betting)

Examples

to back a candidate
I can't back your story unless you provide more details.
The scientist was unable to back her theory when questioned by peers.
You must be able to back your arguments with reliable evidence.
Many people decided to back the underdog in the championship game.
She always backs horses with long odds at the races.
They backed the right candidate and won a lot of money.
If you don't back this project, it may never get off the ground.
Several leading scientists back the proposal for increased funding in renewable energy research.
The senator publicly backed the new healthcare bill during yesterday's press conference.

  • synonym – bet: to risk money on the outcome of an event

  • antonym – oppose: to bet against (in gambling contexts)
  • antonym – refute: to prove that something is false or incorrect
  • synonym – support: to give assistance or approval to someone or something
