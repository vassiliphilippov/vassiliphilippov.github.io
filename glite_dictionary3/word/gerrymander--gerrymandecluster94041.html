gerrymander

noun
manipulation of voting district boundaries for advantage
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛriˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrɪˌmɑːndə / (BrE)
an instance of manipulating the boundaries of electoral districts to favor one political party or group

Examples

a political gerrymander
Critics argue that every gerrymander undermines the fairness of elections.
The court overturned the gerrymander for violating voters' rights.
The new district map is widely condemned as a blatant gerrymander.
