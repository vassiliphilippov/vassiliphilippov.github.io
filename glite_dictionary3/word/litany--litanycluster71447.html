litany

noun
tedious list or recital
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtni / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəni / (BrE)
a long, repetitive, and often tedious list or account, especially of complaints or problems

Examples

litany of complaints
At the meeting, the manager presented a litany of customer complaints gathered over the month.
The report outlined a litany of problems facing the company.
Tired of listening to his endless litany of grievances, she eventually walked away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – catalogue: a list of items, especially one that is tedious or exhaustive
  • synonym – enumeration: the act of mentioning things one by one, especially in a tedious way
  • antonym – summary: a brief statement of main points, opposite in length and tedium
