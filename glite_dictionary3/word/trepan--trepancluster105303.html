trepan

noun
circular hole-cutting drill
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛpæn / (AmE), / ˈtrɛpan / (BrE)
a tool or drill used for cutting circular holes around a center, especially in surgery or engineering

Examples

engineer trepan drill
In archaeology, a trepan may be used to take core samples from stone.
The mechanic selected a trepan to drill a perfect circle in the metal plate.
The surgeon used a trepan to remove a small disc of bone from the patient's skull.
