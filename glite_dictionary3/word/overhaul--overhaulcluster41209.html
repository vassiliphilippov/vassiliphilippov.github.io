to overhaul

verb
to repair or renovate thoroughly
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈhɔl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɔːl / (BrE)
to examine thoroughly and make necessary repairs, renovations, or adjustments; to renovate or update comprehensively

Examples

to overhaul an engine
Before selling his boat, he spent weeks overhauling the engine and fixing minor issues.
It was necessary to overhaul the entire curriculum to keep up with new educational standards.
The company decided to overhaul its outdated computer systems to improve efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym – destroy: to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • synonym – renovate: to restore or improve something, especially a building
  • synonym – repair: to restore to good condition after damage or wear
