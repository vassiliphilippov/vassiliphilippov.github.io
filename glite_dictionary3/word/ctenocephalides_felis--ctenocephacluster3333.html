Ctenocephalides felis

noun
species of flea found on cats and dogs
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛn.oʊˌsɛfəˈlaɪdiːz ˈfiːlɪs / (AmE), / ˌtiːnəʊˌkɛfəˈlaɪdiːz ˈfiːlɪs / (BrE)
A species of flea that primarily infests cats, dogs, and rats, known as the cat flea.

Examples

Ctenocephalides felis bites cats
Ctenocephalides felis is responsible for much of the itching seen in cats and dogs.
Recent studies show that Ctenocephalides felis can also carry certain diseases transmissible to humans.
Veterinarians often prescribe treatments to eliminate Ctenocephalides felis infestations in household pets.
