exorcist

noun
minor clerical order in the historical Western Church
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkˌsɔrsɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛksəsɪst / (BrE)
in the unreformed Western Church, a member of a minor clerical order whose duty was to exorcise evil spirits; this order is now suppressed in the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

exorcist in church order
He studied to become an exorcist in the early years of the seminary.
Historically, an exorcist would assist priests in ceremonies of exorcism.
The role of exorcist is no longer part of the current Roman Catholic minor orders.
