to supervene

verb
occur as an unexpected development
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈviːn / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈviːn / (BrE)
to occur as something additional or unexpected, especially as a new development in a sequence of events

Examples

to supervene suddenly
As the evening progressed, a sense of unease supervened among the guests.
The patient's sudden decline supervened after a week of apparent improvement.
Unexpected legal issues supervened just as the negotiations were nearing completion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ensue: to happen or occur afterward or as a result
  • antonym – precede: to come before something in time or order
