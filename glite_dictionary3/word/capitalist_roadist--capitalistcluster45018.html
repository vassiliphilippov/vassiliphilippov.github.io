capitalist roadist

noun
advocate of capitalism (esp. in political context)
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəlɪst ˈroʊdɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst ˈrəʊdɪst / (BrE)
A person who advocates capitalism, particularly as an ideological perspective or as a supporter of policies favoring privatization and market-based approaches; originally used in a derogatory sense within Chinese political discourse to refer to those seen as straying from socialist principles.

Examples

capitalist roadist in politics
During the debate, she was labeled a capitalist roadist for promoting free-market policies.

The concept of the capitalist roadist emerged during ideological struggles in China.
