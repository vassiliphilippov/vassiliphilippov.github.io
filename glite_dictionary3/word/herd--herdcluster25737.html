herd

noun
large group of ordinary people or things
Pronunciation: / hɝd / (AmE), / hɜːd / (BrE)
a large group of people or things, especially considered as ordinary, undistinguished, or lacking individuality

Examples

herd of people
He prided himself on standing out from the herd in his thinking.
Politicians sometimes address the herd rather than focusing on individual needs.
The artist refused to follow the herd and developed her own unique style.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crowd: a large number of people gathered together
  • antonym – elite: a group of people considered to be the best or most distinguished
