to produce

verb
grow or cultivate agricultural products
Pronunciation: / prəˈdus / (AmE), / prəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to make, grow, or create something; to cause something to exist or happen

Examples

to produce goods
Local farmers produce most of the organic vegetables sold at the market.
Modern vineyards use innovative methods to produce superior wines.
The region is famous for the high-quality apples it produces every fall.
Modern farms produce more food than ever before thanks to new technology.
The factory produces thousands of televisions every month for the international market.
This artist produced a series of sculptures that gained national attention.
Certain medications can produce unwanted skin rashes as a side effect.
The caterpillar will produce beautiful wings during metamorphosis.
With age, some men produce grey hair as a natural change.

  • antonym – consume: to use up something, especially resources or products
  • synonym – create: to bring something into existence
  • antonym – destroy: to put an end to the existence of something, the opposite of producing
  • synonym – develop: to grow or cause to grow and become more mature or advanced
  • synonym – generate: to produce or create something, especially energy or power
  • synonym – grow: to cultivate plants or crops
  • synonym – manufacture: to make something on a large scale using machinery
  • synonym – raise: to breed and care for animals or plants for agricultural production
