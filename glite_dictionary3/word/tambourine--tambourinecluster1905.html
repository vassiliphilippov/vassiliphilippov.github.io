tambourine

noun
musical instrument with drumhead and jingles
Pronunciation: / ˌtæm.bəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌtæm.bəˈriːn / (BrE)
A shallow handheld drum with a single drumhead and pairs of small metal disks set into the frame, played by shaking or striking.

Examples

shake a tambourine
A musician passed out small tambourines for the children to shake along with the beat.
She played the tambourine enthusiastically during the chorus of the song.
The tambourine added a lively jingle to the folk band's performance.

  • synonym – drum: a percussion instrument sounded by being struck with sticks or hands
