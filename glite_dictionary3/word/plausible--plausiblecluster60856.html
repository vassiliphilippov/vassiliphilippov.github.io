plausible

adjective
seeming reasonable or believable
Pronunciation: / ˈplɔːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzəbl / (BrE)
Seeming reasonable or probable; appearing worthy of belief, especially when open to doubt or suspicion.

Examples

plausible excuse
Although his story sounded plausible, the detective remained suspicious.
She gave a plausible explanation for why she was late to the meeting.
The alibi was certainly plausible, but further investigation was needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – credible: able to be believed; convincing
  • antonym – improbable: not likely to be true or to happen
