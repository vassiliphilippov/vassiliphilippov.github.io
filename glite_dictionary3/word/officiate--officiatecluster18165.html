to officiate

verb
perform a formal or religious ceremony
Pronunciation: / əˈfɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to perform an official duty at a ceremony, especially as a religious or formal authority

Examples

to officiate a wedding
She was asked to officiate at her niece’s baptism.
The judge will officiate over the signing of the marriage certificate.
The local pastor will officiate at their wedding ceremony next month.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preside: to be in charge of a formal event or meeting
