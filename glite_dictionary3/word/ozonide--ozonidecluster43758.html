ozonide

noun
unstable compound formed by addition of ozone
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊzəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊzənaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed by the reaction of ozone with an unsaturated compound, typically unstable and containing the O3 group

Examples

unstable ozonide compound
Most ozonide compounds are too unstable to be stored for long periods.
