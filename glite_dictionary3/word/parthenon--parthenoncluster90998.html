Parthenon

proper noun
main temple of Athena in Athens
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrθəˌnɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθənɒn / (BrE)
the main temple of the goddess Athena, built on the Acropolis in Athens in the 5th century B.C., and considered a masterpiece of Doric architecture.

Examples

the Parthenon in Athens
Scholars have studied the sculptures and reliefs of the Parthenon for insights into Greek art and religion.
The Parthenon stands as a symbol of ancient Greek civilization in the heart of Athens.
Thousands of tourists visit the Parthenon every year to admire its classical architecture.
