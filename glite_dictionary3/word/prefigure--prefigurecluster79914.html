to prefigure

verb
to foreshadow; to suggest in advance
Pronunciation: / priˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / priˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
to represent, indicate, or suggest something in advance, especially as a sign or omen

Examples

prefigure a coming change
Dark clouds on the horizon prefigure an approaching storm.
The artist’s early work prefigures the style that would make him famous later in his career.
The collapse of the bridges prefigured the disaster that followed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foreshadow: to be a warning or indication of a future event
  • synonym – predict: to say or estimate that something will happen in the future
  • antonym – reveal: to make known something previously hidden or unknown
