to elevate

verb
promote to higher position
Pronunciation: / ˈɛləˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪveɪt / (BrE)
to give someone a higher or more important position, rank, or status

Examples

to elevate a leader
After years of hard work, the company decided to elevate her to the position of manager.
She was elevated to a senior role within the organization last year.
The board chose to elevate him above his peers due to his outstanding leadership.
He was elevated to the position of director after only five years at the company.
The charity aims to elevate women in rural communities by providing educational opportunities.
The policy helped elevate thousands out of poverty.
