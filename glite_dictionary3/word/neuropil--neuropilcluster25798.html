neuropil

noun
network of nerve fibers in grey matter
Pronunciation: / ˈnʊr.oʊˌpɪl / (AmE), / ˈnjʊə.rə(ʊ)ˌpɪl / (BrE)
A dense network of unmyelinated nerve fibers and branches of glial cells in the central nervous system, forming much of the grey matter.

Examples

brain neuropil tissue
Damage to the neuropil can disrupt communication between neurons.
Researchers studied changes in the neuropil during brain development.
The cerebral cortex contains a dense neuropil packed with synaptic connections.
