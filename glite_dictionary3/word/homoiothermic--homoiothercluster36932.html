homoiothermic

adjective
maintaining a constant, high body temperature
Pronunciation: / hoʊˌmɔɪ.əˈθɜrmɪk / (AmE), / həʊˌmɔɪ.əˈθɜːmɪk / (BrE)
(of birds and mammals) having a constant and relatively high body temperature, independent of the environment.

Examples

homoiothermic animals
Most birds are homoiothermic, allowing them to remain active in cold climates.
The homoiothermic nature of mammals is crucial for their survival in diverse habitats.
Unlike reptiles, homoiothermic animals can thrive in a wide range of environmental temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – poikilothermic: (of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the environment
