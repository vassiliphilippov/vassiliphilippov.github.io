Taraxacum officinale

noun
Common dandelion plant
Pronunciation: / təˌræksəkəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑli / (AmE), / təˌræksəkəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑːli / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant commonly known as the dandelion, widely naturalized as a weed in North America, with yellow flowers, toothed leaves, and fluffy seed heads; its leaves are used in salads and its roots and flowers can be used to make wine.
