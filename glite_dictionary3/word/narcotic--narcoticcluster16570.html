narcotic

noun
drug causing numbness or stupor
Pronunciation: / nɑrˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / nɑːˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
a drug that produces numbness, stupor, or insensibility, often used for pleasure or pain relief and liable to cause addiction

Examples

prescribe narcotic drugs
Doctors prescribe certain narcotic painkillers to patients after surgery.
Police seized a shipment of illegal narcotic substances at the border.
The misuse of narcotic drugs can have serious health consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedative: a substance that induces sedation by reducing irritability or excitement
  • antonym – stimulant: a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
