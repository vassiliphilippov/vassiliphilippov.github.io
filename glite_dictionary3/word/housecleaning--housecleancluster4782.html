housecleaning

noun
removal of unwanted elements for reform
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌkliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(figurative) the process of removing unwanted people, practices, or conditions in order to reform an organization or system.

Examples

housecleaning in management
After the merger, the new management began a thorough housecleaning of the staff and procedures.
Many outdated practices were swept away during the recent housecleaning at city hall.
The scandal prompted a much-needed housecleaning throughout the entire department.

Related Terms

  • synonym – purge: an abrupt or systematic removal of people, often for political reasons
