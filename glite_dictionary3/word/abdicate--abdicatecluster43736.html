to abdicate

verb
formally give up a position of power
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.dɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈab.dɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to formally give up a position of power or responsibility, especially that of a monarch or leader

Examples

to abdicate the throne
After months of political pressure, the president decided to abdicate his office and retire from public life.
He refused to abdicate his responsibilities despite the mounting challenges.
Rumors spread quickly that the queen might abdicate due to ill health.

Related Terms

  • antonym – assume: to take on a position of power or responsibility
  • synonym – relinquish: to give up or let go of something, especially power or responsibility
  • synonym – resign: to voluntarily leave a job or position
