Arnold of Brescia

proper noun
Italian theologian and church reformer (c. 1090–1155)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrnəld əv brɛsˈtʃiə / (AmE), / ˈɑːnəld əv brɛsˈtʃiːə / (BrE)
(c. 1090–1155) An Italian theologian and reformer known for criticizing the wealth of the clergy and the political power of the Church, who was condemned for heresy by the Second Lateran Council.

Examples

Arnold of Brescia opposed clergy
Arnold of Brescia was executed in 1155 for his outspoken criticism of the Church's temporal power.
Historians often cite Arnold of Brescia as an early advocate for church reform.
The movement led by Arnold of Brescia challenged the accumulation of wealth by clergy in medieval Italy.
