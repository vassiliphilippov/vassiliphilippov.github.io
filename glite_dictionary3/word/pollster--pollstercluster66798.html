pollster

noun
person conducting public opinion surveys
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊl.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊl.stə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to conduct surveys to gauge public opinion on various topics, especially political or social issues

Examples

pollster conducts surveys
Many pollsters conduct interviews by phone to collect their data.
The pollster predicted that the candidate would win based on the latest survey results.
The accuracy of a pollster's findings depends on the quality of the sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym – surveyor: a person who measures or examines something, especially land or public opinion
