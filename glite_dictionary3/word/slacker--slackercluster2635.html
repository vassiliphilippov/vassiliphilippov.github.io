slacker

noun
a person who avoids work or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈslækər / (AmE), / ˈslækə / (BrE)
a person who avoids work, responsibilities, or duty, especially one who is habitually lazy or tries to evade obligations

Examples

lazy slacker
During the team meeting, he was called out as a slacker for missing several deadlines.
In college, she overcame her reputation as a slacker and became a top student.
My boss thinks I'm a slacker because I always finish my work at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • synonym – idler: a person who avoids work or is prone to being lazy
  • antonym – workaholic: a person who compulsively works hard and long hours
