sectarianism

noun
rigid loyalty to a sect or party
Pronunciation: / sɛkˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / sɛkˈtɛːrɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
Strong and often narrow-minded support or loyalty to a particular religious sect, party, or denomination, often leading to conflict with others.

Examples

religious sectarianism conflict
Efforts to promote unity focused on reducing sectarianism in local schools.
Her speech addressed the dangers of political sectarianism in dividing the community.
The country has struggled for decades with sectarianism between different religious groups.
