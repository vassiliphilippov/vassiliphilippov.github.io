katabolism

noun
biological breakdown of substances with energy release
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈtæbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the process in living organisms by which complex substances are broken down into simpler ones with the release of energy

Examples

katabolism of proteins
During fasting, katabolism helps provide the body with energy by breaking down stored molecules.
