steatorrhea

noun
excess fat in feces (medical symptom)
Pronunciation: / stiːˌætəˈriːə / (AmE), / stiːˌatəˈrɪə / (BrE)
the presence of abnormally high amounts of fat in the feces, causing them to be frothy, foul-smelling, and to float, typically as a symptom of disorders affecting fat metabolism or absorption

Examples

steatorrhea in stool
Chronic pancreatitis can often result in steatorrhea as a symptom.
The doctor suspected steatorrhea due to the patient's greasy, floating stools.
Treatment of steatorrhea usually focuses on addressing the underlying digestive disorder.
