scanty

adjective
insufficient; barely enough
Pronunciation: / ˈskænti / (AmE), / ˈskænti / (BrE)
insufficient in quantity or amount; barely enough

Examples

scanty food supply
After a long, hot summer, the farmers were disappointed by the scanty harvest.
Her scanty savings were barely enough to pay the bills.
The room was furnished in a scanty manner, with only a bed and a chair.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abundant: present in large quantities; more than enough
  • synonym – meager: lacking in quantity or quality
  • synonym – sparse: thinly dispersed or scattered; not dense
