smooth

adjective
calm, undisturbed water
Pronunciation: / smuð / (AmE), / smuːð / (BrE)
(of water) calm and not disturbed by strong waves or wind; not rough.

Examples

smooth sea
The ferry enjoyed a smooth crossing because the sea was unusually calm.
The lake was perfectly smooth at dawn, reflecting the mountains like a mirror.
We waited until the water was smooth before launching the small boat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – calm: (of water) peaceful and still, not rough
  • antonym – rough: (of water) having strong waves or turbulence
