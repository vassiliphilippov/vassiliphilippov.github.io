speculation

noun
idea or theory without firm evidence
Pronunciation: / ˌspɛkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an idea, theory, or opinion formed without firm evidence; a guess or conjecture

Examples

wild speculation
His theory remains pure speculation until more evidence can be found.
The company's silence led to wild speculation in the media.
There was much speculation about who would win the award this year.
His theory is based on speculation, not on solid scientific evidence.
Rumors and speculation quickly spread through the media after the announcement.
There has been much speculation about who will win the election.
