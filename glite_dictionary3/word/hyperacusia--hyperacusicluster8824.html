hyperacusia

noun
abnormal sensitivity to sound
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərəˈkuːziə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərəˈkjuːziə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by extreme sensitivity and intolerance to ordinary levels of sound.

Examples

hyperacusia to loud sounds
Because of his hyperacusia, he wears earplugs even in quiet environments.
People with hyperacusia often find everyday noises unbearably loud or painful.
The doctor diagnosed her with hyperacusia after she complained about discomfort from normal conversation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hyperacusis: Increased sensitivity to normal environmental sounds; intolerance to ordinary sound levels
