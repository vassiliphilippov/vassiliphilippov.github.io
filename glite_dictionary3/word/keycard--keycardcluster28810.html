keycard

noun
magnetically coded card for locks
Pronunciation: / ˈkiː.kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkiː.kɑːd / (BrE)
A plastic card with a magnetically coded strip used to operate a lock or other mechanism.

Examples

swipe a keycard
Some offices require employees to swipe a keycard to enter secure areas.
The hotel receptionist handed me a new keycard because I lost my old one.
You will need your keycard to access the gym after hours.
