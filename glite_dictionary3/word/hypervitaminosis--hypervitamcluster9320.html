hypervitaminosis

noun
condition from excessive vitamin intake
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌvaɪtəˌmɪˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˌvɪtəmɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal condition caused by the excessive intake of vitamins, especially vitamins A, D, or K, which can lead to serious health problems

Examples

hypervitaminosis from supplements
Chronic use of high-dose supplements can result in hypervitaminosis, particularly with fat-soluble vitamins.
Doctors warn that hypervitaminosis is a risk when people take more vitamins than recommended.
Patients suffering from hypervitaminosis A may experience symptoms such as nausea and dizziness.
