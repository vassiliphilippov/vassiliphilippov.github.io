fuzzy

adjective
indistinct or blurry in outline
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌzi / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi / (BrE)
not clear or sharp; indistinct or hard to understand (can refer to images, memories, ideas, or information)

Examples

fuzzy memory
Her memory of the incident became increasingly fuzzy over time.
The photograph was fuzzy and it was difficult to recognize the faces.
Through the mist, the mountains looked fuzzy and unreal.
His explanation of the plan was too fuzzy to follow.
I'm still feeling fuzzy about the details of the project.
She has only a fuzzy memory of what happened that day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blurry: lacking clear definition; not sharp
  • antonym – clear: easy to understand; not confused
  • antonym – sharp: clear and distinct in outline
  • synonym – vague: not clearly expressed, known, described, or decided
