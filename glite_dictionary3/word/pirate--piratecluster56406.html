pirate

noun
someone who robs ships at sea
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
a person who attacks and robs ships at sea, especially without the authority of any country or government

Examples

a pirate ship
A pirate ship was spotted on the horizon near the old port.
Stories about buried treasure and pirates have fascinated children for generations.
The famous pirate Blackbeard terrorized the Atlantic coast in the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buccaneer: a pirate, especially one who attacked Spanish ships in the Caribbean during the 17th century
  • synonym – corsair: a pirate, especially along the Barbary Coast of North Africa
  • antonym – naval officer: a commissioned officer in a navy, opposite of pirate in official status
  • degree lower – privateer: a privately owned ship or its captain allowed by a government to attack enemy ships
