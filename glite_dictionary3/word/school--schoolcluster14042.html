school

noun
group of artists or thinkers with shared style
Pronunciation: / skuːl / (AmE), / skuːl / (BrE)
A group of artists, writers, or thinkers who are united by a shared style, methods, or by having studied under the same teacher, often associated with a place or period in history.

Examples

the Impressionist school
She studied the works of composers from the Second Viennese school.
The Bloomsbury school greatly influenced British literature and thought.
The impressionist school revolutionized art in the 19th century.

  • synonym – movement: A group of people with a shared aim or style in art or ideas
