jealously

adverb
in a jealous or envious manner
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛləsli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛləsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows jealousy or envy, by being protective, possessive, or envious

Examples

jealously guard secrets
He jealously watched as his colleague received all the praise.
Parents sometimes jealously protect their children from outside influences.
She jealously guarded her secret recipe from other chefs.
He jealously guarded his private collection from curious visitors.
She watched her sister open presents jealously, wishing she had received more gifts herself.
The cat glanced jealously at the dog playing with its favorite toy.
