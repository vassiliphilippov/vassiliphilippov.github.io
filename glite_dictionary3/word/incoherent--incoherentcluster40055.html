incoherent

adjective
unable to speak clearly or fluently
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkoʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
not clear, logical, or easy to understand; unable to express ideas in a clear way

Examples

incoherent speech
After hearing the shocking news, she became incoherent with shock and couldn't say a word.
Grief can make a person incoherent, struggling to find the right words.
His incoherent explanation only added to the confusion in the meeting.
Her speech became increasingly incoherent as she grew more nervous.
His explanation was incoherent, leaving everyone confused about what actually happened.
The essay was so incoherent that the main argument was difficult to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – articulate: able to express thoughts and feelings clearly and easily
  • antonym – coherent: logical and consistent, clear and understandable
  • synonym – illogical: lacking sense or clear, sound reasoning
  • synonym – tongue-tied: unable to speak easily because of shyness or embarrassment
