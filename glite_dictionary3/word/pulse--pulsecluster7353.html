to pulse

verb
to produce or modulate in pulses
Pronunciation: / pʌls / (AmE), / pʌls / (BrE)
to produce or modulate (something, especially electromagnetic waves) in the form of short bursts or pulses, or to cause a device to emit such pulses

Examples

to pulse a laser
Engineers adjusted the system to pulse electrical signals during the test.
Lasers are often designed to pulse light for precise measurements.
The radio transmitter can pulse waves at regular intervals.
