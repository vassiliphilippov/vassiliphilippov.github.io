field

noun
algebraic set with two operations
Pronunciation: / fiːld / (AmE), / fiːld / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A set of elements in which addition and multiplication are commutative and associative, multiplication is distributive over addition, and which contains two distinct elements (0 and 1); commonly, both addition and multiplication are invertible except for zero in multiplication.

Examples

number field
In linear algebra, every vector space is defined over a field.
Students must understand the properties of a field to study abstract algebra.
The real numbers form a field under usual addition and multiplication.
