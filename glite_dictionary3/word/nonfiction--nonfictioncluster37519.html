nonfiction

noun
factual prose writing
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / nɒnˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
prose writing that is based on facts, real events, and real people, rather than fiction.

Examples

nonfiction book
His latest nonfiction work explores environmental issues.
She prefers reading nonfiction books about history and science.
The library has a large nonfiction section for biographies and memoirs.

  • antonym – fiction: prose writing that describes imaginary events and people
