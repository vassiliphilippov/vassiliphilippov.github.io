pyridoxine

noun
a form of vitamin B6
Pronunciation: / pɪrəˈdɑksiːn / (AmE), / pɪrɪˈdɒksiːn / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B6 essential for the metabolism of amino acids and starch, important for various bodily functions

Examples

pyridoxine supplement
A lack of pyridoxine in the diet can lead to neurological symptoms.
Doctors often recommend pyridoxine supplements to treat vitamin B6 deficiency.
Foods such as fish, poultry, and bananas are good sources of pyridoxine.
