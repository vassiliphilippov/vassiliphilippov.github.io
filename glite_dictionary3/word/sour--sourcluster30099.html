sour

adjective
unpleasantly turned or spoiled (taste)
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə / (BrE)
(of food, drink, or smell) having turned bad or spoiled, often with an unpleasant, sharp, or fermented odor or taste

Examples

sour milk smell
After a few days in the heat, the juice turned sour.
The cream had gone sour and was no longer safe to eat.
You should throw out any sour milk from the fridge immediately.
After a week of rain, the basement developed a sour smell that lingered for days.
His clothes had become sour after being left damp in the washing machine.
The milk in the fridge had a distinctive sour odor that warned me not to drink it.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fresh: recently made or obtained; not spoiled or stale
  • synonym – putrid: decaying and emitting a foul smell
  • synonym – spoiled: (of food) unfit to eat due to decay or decomposition
