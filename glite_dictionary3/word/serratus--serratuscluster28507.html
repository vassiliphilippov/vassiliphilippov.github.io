serratus

noun
trunk muscle with saw-toothed edge
Pronunciation: / sɛˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / səˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
Any of several thin muscles of the trunk with a saw-toothed edge, especially connecting the scapula with the ribs or vertebrae.

Examples

serratus muscle pain
After his injury, he experienced pain in the serratus muscles when lifting his arm.
Strengthening the serratus can improve shoulder stability in athletes.
The serratus anterior aids in the upward rotation of the scapula.
