scholiast

noun
a commentator on classical texts
Pronunciation: / ˈskoʊliˌæst / (AmE), / ˈskəʊlɪast / (BrE)
A scholar who writes explanatory notes or comments on an author, especially an ancient commentator on classical authors.

Examples

scholiast on Homer
A famous scholiast on Homer helped modern scholars understand obscure passages.
Ancient manuscripts often include notes by a scholiast in the margins.
The scholiast provided valuable insights into the original Greek text.
