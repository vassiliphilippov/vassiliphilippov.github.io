to usurp

verb
take power or control illegally
Pronunciation: / juˈsɝːp /; / juːˈsɜrp / (AmE), / juːˈzɜːp / (BrE)
to take power, a position, or someone else's place by force or without the right to do so

Examples

to usurp the throne
If you try to usurp authority, you might face serious consequences.
Several rebels plotted to usurp control of the region from the ruling party.
The general attempted to usurp the throne after the king's sudden death.
After the accident, anxiety usurped her usual sense of security.
Doubt usurped his confidence as the deadline drew nearer.
With technology evolving rapidly, new devices often usurp outdated ones in the market.

  • antonym – relinquish: to voluntarily cease to keep or claim; give up
  • synonym – replace: to take the place of (someone or something)
  • synonym – seize: to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
  • synonym – supplant: to take the place of, especially by force or strategy
