to plasticise

verb
make a material flexible with a plasticizer
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstəˌsaɪz /; / ˈplæstɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstɪsaɪz /; / ˈplɑːstɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to make or become flexible or moldable by adding a plasticizer (especially for materials like polymers)

Examples

to plasticise a material
Manufacturers often plasticise PVC to create flexible tubing for medical use.
The addition of certain chemicals can plasticise otherwise rigid polymers.
You need to plasticise the material before it can be molded into shape.
Certain additives cause PVC to plasticise and become more flexible.
If the mixture does not plasticise properly, the final product may crack.
The material will plasticise when heated, making it easy to shape.
