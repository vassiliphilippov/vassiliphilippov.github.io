to simulate

verb
create an imitation or model
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to create an imitation or representation of something, especially to study, test, or make it seem real

Examples

to simulate real conditions
The experiment was designed to simulate conditions found on Mars.
The software can simulate complex weather patterns for research purposes.
Video games often simulate real-world physics to enhance realism.
Some children like to simulate their teachers’ voices when playing school at home.
The actor was able to simulate the famous politician’s gestures perfectly on stage.
With lots of practice, you can simulate a convincing British accent.
