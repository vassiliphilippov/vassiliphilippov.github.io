unsuitableness

noun
quality of being unsuitable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsuːtəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsjuːtəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being suitable for a particular purpose or situation.

Examples

unsuitableness for the job
Due to its unsuitableness, the proposal was rejected by the committee.
He argued against the policy, highlighting its unsuitableness to the current economic situation.
The unsuitableness of this material for outdoor use became clear after a week of rain.
