wader

noun
long-legged water bird
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪdər / (AmE), / ˈweɪdə / (BrE)
a long-legged bird that wades in shallow water in search of food, such as a heron or stilt

Examples

wader bird species
Many waders migrate long distances between their breeding and wintering grounds.
Species such as herons and stilts are considered waders due to their long legs and feeding habits.
While walking along the mudflats, we spotted several waders searching for insects and small fish.
