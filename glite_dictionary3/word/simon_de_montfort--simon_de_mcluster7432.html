Simon de Montfort

proper noun
English nobleman; led rebellion against Henry III
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪmən də ˈmɑnt.fɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmən dɪ ˈmɒnfət / (BrE)
(1208–1265) An English nobleman who led the baronial rebellion against King Henry III and played a major role in the development of early parliamentary government.

Examples

Simon de Montfort led rebellion
Simon de Montfort is often credited with calling the first English parliament that included commoners.
Many historians consider Simon de Montfort a pioneer of democratic government in England.
The leadership of Simon de Montfort changed the course of English constitutional history.
