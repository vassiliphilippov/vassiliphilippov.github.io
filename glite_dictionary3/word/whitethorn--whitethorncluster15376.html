whitethorn

noun
thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌθɔːn / (BrE)
A thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree, typically with clusters of white to scarlet flowers and deep red berries, sometimes naturalized in North America.

Examples

flowering whitethorn bush
Birds love to feed on the red berries produced by the whitethorn.
In spring, the whitethorn blooms with clusters of delicate white flowers.
The garden hedge was made up of dense whitethorn bushes.
