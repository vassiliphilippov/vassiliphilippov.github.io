bottle-grass

noun
a European foxtail grass weed
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːtlˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɒtlˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European foxtail grass (Alopecurus myosuroides) naturalized in North America, often considered a troublesome weed in cultivated fields.

Examples

bottle-grass in wheat fields
Farmers often struggle to control bottle-grass in their wheat fields.
Many herbicides are used in attempts to eradicate bottle-grass from cropland.
The rapid spread of bottle-grass has caused problems for native plants.
