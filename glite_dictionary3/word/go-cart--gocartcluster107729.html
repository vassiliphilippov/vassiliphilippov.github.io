go-cart

noun
framework on wheels for babies learning to walk
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊ kɑrt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ kɑːt / (BrE)
a small wheeled vehicle for babies or children, either to help them learn to walk or to be pushed around

Examples

child in a go-cart
Modern parents often choose a go-cart with safety features for their children.
She learned to walk quickly thanks to a sturdy go-cart her parents bought.
The baby pushed the go-cart across the living room, giggling with excitement.
He pushed his baby sister in a go-cart through the park.
She carefully buckled her infant into the go-cart before heading out for a walk.
The old-fashioned go-cart had large, spoked wheels and a cloth canopy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stroller: a similar four-wheeled vehicle for babies or young children
  • synonym – walker: a frame used by babies or elderly people to assist with walking
