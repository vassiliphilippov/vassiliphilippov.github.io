to nationalise

verb
make national in character or scope
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make national in character, scope, or importance; to give a national focus or significance to something

Examples

to nationalise a campaign
The government aimed to nationalise the campaign to increase public awareness across the country.
The politician tried to nationalise the festival, turning it into a celebration of cultural unity.
Writers often nationalise folk tales by emphasizing their country's values and traditions.
