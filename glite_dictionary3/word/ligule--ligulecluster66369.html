ligule

noun
strap-shaped plant appendage (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪɡjul / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡjuːl / (BrE)
(Botany) A thin, strap-shaped appendage or structure in some plants, often found at the junction of leaf and stem.

Examples

ligule on a grass leaf
A membranous ligule is present where the leaf blade meets the sheath in many cereals.
Botanists examined the ligule to help classify the unknown plant specimen.
The grass species can be identified by the shape and size of its ligule.
