overspill

noun
excess liquid exceeding a boundary
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌspɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌspɪl / (BrE)
the situation in which liquid, especially water, goes beyond the limits of a container or place because there is too much of it

Examples

overspill of water
A sudden overspill of water flooded the basement after the tank was filled too quickly.
Engineers installed barriers to control the overspill from the reservoir during storms.
The heavy rain caused an overspill from the river onto the nearby fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overflow: the flow of liquid over the limits of a container
