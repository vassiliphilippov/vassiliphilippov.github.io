to read

verb
to hear and understand (in communications)
Pronunciation: / ɹid / (AmE), / ɹiːd / (BrE)
(used in communications) to hear and understand spoken or transmitted information clearly.

Examples

to read a signal
"Do you read me?" asked the pilot over the radio.
I read you loud and clear; go ahead with your message.
It's important that the operator can read instructions during emergencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – comprehend: to understand the meaning of something
  • antonym – mishear: to hear incorrectly or fail to understand
