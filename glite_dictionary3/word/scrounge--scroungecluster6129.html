to scrounge

verb
to obtain by cadging or wheedling
Pronunciation: / skraʊndʒ / (AmE), / skraʊndʒ / (BrE)
to obtain or try to obtain something, usually by asking or persuading without offering anything in return; to cadge or wheedle

Examples

to scrounge a free meal
He always tries to scrounge free meals from his friends.
It's amazing how much food you can scrounge from the office kitchen.
She managed to scrounge enough money for a train ticket home.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – borrow: to take and use something belonging to someone else with the intention of returning it
  • synonym – cadge: to ask for or obtain something to which one is not strictly entitled
