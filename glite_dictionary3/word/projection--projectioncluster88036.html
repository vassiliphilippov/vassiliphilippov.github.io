projection

noun
attributing one's own feelings to others (psychology)
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Psychology) The unconscious act of attributing one's own feelings or traits to another person, especially as a defense mechanism.

Examples

psychological projection behavior
Her accusation of dishonesty was a clear case of projection, since she herself had been lying.
Many therapists observe projection when patients attribute their own anxieties to others.
Understanding projection can help people recognize their own hidden emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – transference: Shift of feelings from one person to another, especially in therapy
