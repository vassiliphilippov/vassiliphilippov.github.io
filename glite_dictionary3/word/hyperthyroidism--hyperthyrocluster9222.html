hyperthyroidism

noun
overactivity of the thyroid gland
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pərˈθaɪ.rɔɪdˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈθaɪ.rɔɪd.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the thyroid gland is overactive, producing excessive amounts of thyroid hormones.

Examples

hyperthyroidism symptoms appear
The doctor diagnosed her with hyperthyroidism after noticing her rapid heartbeat and weight loss.
Treatment for hyperthyroidism often includes medication to reduce thyroid hormone levels.
Weight loss and anxiety are common symptoms of hyperthyroidism.
