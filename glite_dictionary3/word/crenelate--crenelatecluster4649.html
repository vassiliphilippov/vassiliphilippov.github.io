to crenelate

verb
to provide with battlements
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛnəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɛnɪleɪt / (BrE)
to provide (a wall or building) with battlements (notched defensive parapets), as for military defense or decoration

Examples

to crenelate a castle wall
During the restoration, craftsmen crenelated the top of the tower for an authentic medieval appearance.
Many Victorian mansions were crenelated to give them a romantic, fortress-like look.
The castle walls were crenelated to allow archers to defend the fortress effectively.
