crusade

noun
vigorous campaign for a cause
Pronunciation: / kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
A vigorous campaign for social, political, or religious change, typically involving passionate and sustained effort.

Examples

a crusade for justice
He became known for his lifelong crusade to promote equality and social justice.
She led a crusade to improve literacy rates in her community.
The organization launched a crusade against plastic pollution in the oceans.
