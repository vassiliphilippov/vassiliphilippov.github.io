Citrus aurantium

noun
bitter/seville orange tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtɹəs ɔːˈɹænʃəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɹəs ɔːˈɹæntiəm / (BrE)
A species of orange tree, also known as bitter orange or Seville orange, with sour or bitter fruit used in marmalade, flavorings, and as grafting stock.

Examples

Citrus aurantium fruit marmalade
Gardeners frequently use Citrus aurantium as a grafting stock for sweet orange trees.
Spanish marmalade is traditionally made from the bitter fruit of Citrus aurantium.
The extract from Citrus aurantium is often used as a natural flavoring in liqueurs.
