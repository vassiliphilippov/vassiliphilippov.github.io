mahagua

noun
tropical tree used for wood and fiber
Pronunciation: / məˈhɑːɡwə / (AmE), / məˈhɑːɡwə / (BrE)
A tropical shrubby tree widely found along shores, known for its light, tough wood used in canoe outriggers and its fiber used for making cordage and caulking; often grown as an ornamental plant.

Examples

mahagua canoe wood
Because of its attractive foliage, mahagua is often planted as an ornamental tree.
Ropes and caulking were traditionally made from the fiber of the mahagua tree.
The villagers used mahagua wood to build sturdy outriggers for their canoes.
