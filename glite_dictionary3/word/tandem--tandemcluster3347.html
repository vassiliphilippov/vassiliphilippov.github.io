tandem

noun
objects or people in a row, one behind another
Pronunciation: / ˈtændəm / (AmE), / ˈtændəm / (BrE)
an arrangement in which two or more objects or people are placed one behind the other

Examples

chairs in tandem row
Our office desks are arranged in tandem to maximize floor space.
The dogs were harnessed in tandem for the sledding race.
The two cyclists rode the steep hill on a tandem to save energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – side by side: arrangement where items are placed next to each other
