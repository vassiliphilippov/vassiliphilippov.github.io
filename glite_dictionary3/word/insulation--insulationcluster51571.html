insulation

noun
material or process that blocks heat, sound, or electricity
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of protecting something by surrounding it with a material that reduces or prevents the passage of heat, sound, or electricity.

Examples

add insulation to walls
Adding foam insulation will help reduce noise from the street.
Proper insulation is essential for energy-efficient buildings.
The attic needed better insulation to keep the house warm in winter.
