undercarriage

noun
framework supporting the underside of a vehicle
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
the supporting framework underneath a vehicle, such as an aircraft or car, that supports the main structure and often includes the wheels

Examples

undercarriage of a car
Before servicing the car, the mechanic inspected its undercarriage for rust.
Heavy mud had collected on the vehicle's undercarriage after driving off-road.
The plane's undercarriage was damaged during the rough landing.
