yam

noun
a sweet potato with orange flesh, moist when baked
Pronunciation: / jæm / (AmE), / jæm / (BrE)
A type of sweet potato with deep orange flesh that remains moist when baked, commonly found in North American supermarkets.

Examples

baked yam dish
At the grocery store, I can never tell the difference between a yam and a sweet potato.
She baked a yam for dinner and served it with butter and cinnamon.
The recipe calls for one large yam, peeled and cut into cubes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sweet potato: a root vegetable with orange flesh
  • antonym – yam (true yam): the starchy tuber from plants of the genus Dioscorea
