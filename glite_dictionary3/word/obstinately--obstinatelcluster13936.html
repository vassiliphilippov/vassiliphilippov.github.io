obstinately

adverb
in a stubborn or unyielding way
Pronunciation: / ˈɑb.stə.nət.li / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stɪ.nət.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows unwillingness to change one's opinion or action; stubbornly

Examples

obstinately refuse help
He obstinately held on to his beliefs despite criticism from others.
She obstinately refused to admit she was wrong, even when faced with clear evidence.
The child obstinately ignored his parents' instructions and continued to play outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flexibly: in a way that adapts easily to change; opposite of stubbornly
  • synonym – stubbornly: in a way that shows dogged determination not to change one's attitude or position
