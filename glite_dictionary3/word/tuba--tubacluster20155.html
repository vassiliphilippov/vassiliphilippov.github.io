tuba

noun
large low-pitched brass instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːbə / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbə / (BrE)
a large brass wind instrument with a deep, low sound, forming the bass of the brass section in an orchestra or band

Examples

play the tuba
It takes a lot of breath control to master the tuba.
The musician played the tuba during the school concert.
The orchestra's bass line was provided by the tuba.

Related Terms

  • synonym – euphonium: a brass instrument similar to a small tuba, with a higher pitch
  • antonym – trumpet: a brass instrument producing a higher, brighter sound than a tuba
