megalomania

noun
delusions of grandeur
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəloʊˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
A psychological condition characterized by delusional fantasies of power, relevance, or omnipotence, often involving inflated self-esteem.

Examples

dangerous megalomania
Her megalomania led her to make reckless decisions as CEO.
Historians often discuss the megalomania of certain dictators.
The prince showed signs of megalomania, believing himself destined to rule the world.
