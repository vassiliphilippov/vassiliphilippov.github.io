to plunge

verb
to dash or move suddenly and forcefully
Pronunciation: / plʌndʒ / (AmE), / plʌndʒ / (BrE)
to fall, move, or drop suddenly and quickly from a higher to a lower position (literally or figuratively)

Examples

to plunge downwards
As soon as the doors opened, the shoppers plunged inside.
She plunged through the crowd toward the exit.
The dog plunged into the river to fetch the stick.
Stock prices plunged after the disappointing earnings report was released.
The car skidded on the icy road and plunged into a ditch.
The diver took a deep breath and plunged into the cold water.
After the announcement, the company's shares plunged to their lowest level in years.
Temperatures can plunge overnight during the winter months.
The boulder plunged off the cliff and crashed into the river below.

  • synonym – dive: to jump or move quickly downward or forward
  • synonym – drop: to fall or let something fall
  • antonym – rise: to move upward to a higher position
