omnivorousness

noun
consuming diverse cultural goods
Pronunciation: / \u0251m\u02c8n\u026av\u025a\u0259sn\u0259s / (AmE), / \u0252m\u02c8n\u026av\u0259r\u0259sn\u0259s / (BrE)
The tendency or habit of consuming a very wide and diverse range of cultural goods and activities (books, music, film, art), without regard to traditional high/low distinctions.

Examples

cultural omnivorousness
Sociologists argued that rising omnivorousness among young people reduces cultural boundaries and increases varied tastes.
Her omnivorousness meant she read thrillers, attended operas, and streamed indie films every week.
Music festivals prize omnivorousness in audiences, programming both classical ensembles and electronic DJs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cultural omnivore: a person who enjoys both popular and elite cultural forms
  • antonym – specialization: focusing on a narrow range of cultural forms; opposite tendency
