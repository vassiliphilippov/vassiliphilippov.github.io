candle

noun
former unit of luminous intensity
Pronunciation: / ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkændl̩ / (BrE)
a former unit of luminous intensity equal to the luminous intensity emitted by a standard candle; replaced by the candela in the International System of Units (SI)

Examples

candle power unit
Old photometric tables often list values in candle units, which can be confusing to readers.
Scientists now prefer the candela instead of the candle for measuring luminous intensity.
The lamp's brightness was once measured in candles before the adoption of the modern SI unit.
