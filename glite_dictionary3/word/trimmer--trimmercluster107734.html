trimmer

noun
joist supporting an opening in construction
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmə / (BrE)
a joist in floor or roof framing that supports the ends of a header, creating an opening for structures such as staircases or chimneys

Examples

floor trimmer
During renovations, the carpenter replaced the damaged trimmer near the chimney flue.
Proper installation of a trimmer is essential to maintain structural integrity around floor openings.
The architect specified a heavy-duty trimmer to support the new staircase opening.
