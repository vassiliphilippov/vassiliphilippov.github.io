electrosurgery

noun
surgery using electric currents
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a surgical technique in which tissue is cut or cauterized using high-frequency electric currents

Examples

electrosurgery for cutting tissue
Modern electrosurgery allows for precise cutting and coagulation of tissue.
The surgeon chose electrosurgery to minimize bleeding during the procedure.
Training in electrosurgery is essential for many medical professionals.
