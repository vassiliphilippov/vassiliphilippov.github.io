to motley

verb
to make something multicolored or varied
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑt.li / (AmE), / ˈmɒt.li / (BrE)
to make something varied in color or appearance by combining elements of different colors or types

Examples

to motley a landscape
Springtime has motleyed the once-barren landscape with bright wildflowers and green shoots.
The artist motleyed the canvas with splashes of red, yellow, and blue.
The festival parade motleyed the streets with performers in every shade imaginable.

Related Terms

  • synonym – variegate: to make varied in appearance, especially with different colors
