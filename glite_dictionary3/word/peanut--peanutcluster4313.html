peanut

noun
edible underground seed/pod
Pronunciation: / ˈpiː.nʌt /; / ˈpiˌnʌt /; / ˈpiːnʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nʌt /; / ˈpiːnʌt / (BrE)
a small, oval, edible seed that grows in a pod underground and is commonly eaten as a snack or used to make peanut butter; also, the plant that produces these seeds

Examples

roasted peanut snack

She packed a bag of peanuts to take with her on the hike.
The recipe calls for roasted peanut halves sprinkled on top.

Related Terms

  • synonym – groundnut: another name for the peanut, especially outside North America
  • synonym – monkey nut: British term for a peanut in its shell
  • antonym – tree nut: edible seeds from trees, as contrasted with peanuts which are legumes
