neoconservatism

noun
return to traditional policies or values
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊkənˈsɝːvətɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊkənˈsɜːvətɪzəm / (BrE)
A political ideology advocating a return to traditional policies and values, often in reaction against liberal or radical ideas from the 1960s and beyond.

Examples

rise of neoconservatism
Critics of neoconservatism claim that it led to more interventionist government policies.
Many scholars argue that neoconservatism emerged as a response to the social upheavals of the 1960s.
The influence of neoconservatism was particularly strong in American foreign policy during the early 2000s.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conservatism: commitment to traditional values and ideas in politics
  • antonym – liberalism: willingness to embrace progressive reforms and ideas
