dudgeon

noun
a state of strong indignation
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈdʌdʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a feeling of intense indignation or resentment, used especially in the phrase 'in high dudgeon'

Examples

in high dudgeon
He strode out in dudgeon, refusing to listen to anyone else's opinion.
She left the meeting in high dudgeon after her proposal was rejected.
The official resigned in high dudgeon over the recent policy changes.
