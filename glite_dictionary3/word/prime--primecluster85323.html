prime

adjective
of a number: divisible only by itself and one
Pronunciation: / praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
(of a number) divisible only by itself and one; not composite.

Examples

prime factor
A prime factor is a factor of a number that is itself prime.
Mathematicians have proven that there are infinitely many prime numbers.
The number 7 is a prime number because it cannot be divided evenly by any other integers except 1 and itself.

Related Terms

  • antonym – composite: (of a number) having factors other than itself and one
