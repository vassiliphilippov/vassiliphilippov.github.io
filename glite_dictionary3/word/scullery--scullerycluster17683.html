scullery

noun
small room by kitchen for cleaning and storage
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌləri / (AmE), / ˈskʌləri / (BrE)
a small room next to the kitchen, especially in large old houses, where kitchen utensils are cleaned and stored and other household chores are done

Examples

old house scullery
She found the cat curled up in the scullery among the pots and pans.
The scullery was filled with the sound of running water and clattering plates.
The old manor house still had a scullery where the servants used to wash the dishes.
