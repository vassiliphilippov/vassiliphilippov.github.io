fissiparous

adjective
reproducing by fission
Pronunciation: / fɪˈsɪpəˌrəs / (AmE), / fɪˈsɪpərəs / (BrE)
Reproducing or dividing by fission; capable of reproduction by splitting into two or more parts.

Examples

fissiparous bacteria
Many single-celled organisms are fissiparous, multiplying simply by splitting in two.
Some bacteria exhibit fissiparous reproduction under favorable environmental conditions.
The fissiparous nature of certain protozoans allows for rapid population growth.
