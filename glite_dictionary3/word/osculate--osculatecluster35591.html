to osculate

verb
be intermediate between taxonomic groups
Pronunciation: / ˈɑskjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒskjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of a species or taxonomic group) to be intermediate between two groups; to have characteristics of both.

Examples

osculate two species
In evolutionary biology, some fish lineages osculate, making classification challenging.
Several subspecies osculate between the two genera, sharing features of both.
The fossil record suggests that the ancient mammal osculated between primates and rodents.
