to truss

verb
tie a bird's wings and legs before cooking
Pronunciation: / trʌs / (AmE), / trʌs / (BrE)
to tie the wings and legs of a bird closely to its body before cooking it, usually with string, to keep its shape

Examples

to truss a chicken
Before roasting the chicken, the chef carefully trussed it to ensure even cooking.
It is important to truss the bird so the stuffing does not fall out during cooking.
She watched a video to learn how to truss a turkey for Thanksgiving dinner.

  • synonym – bind: to tie or secure tightly
