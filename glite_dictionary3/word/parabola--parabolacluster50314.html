parabola

noun
a symmetrical curved shape in mathematics
Pronunciation: / pəˈræbələ / (AmE), / pəˈrabələ / (BrE)
a symmetrical open plane curve formed by the intersection of a cone with a plane parallel to its side; every point is equally distant from a fixed point (the focus) and a fixed straight line (the directrix)

Examples

mathematical parabola
A satellite dish is shaped like a parabola to focus signals at a single point.
The graph of a quadratic function is always a parabola that opens either upward or downward.
When you throw a ball, its path through the air is approximately a parabola.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ellipse: a closed curve on a plane, the sum of whose distances from two fixed points is constant
  • synonym – hyperbola: an open curve formed by a plane that cuts the base of a cone
