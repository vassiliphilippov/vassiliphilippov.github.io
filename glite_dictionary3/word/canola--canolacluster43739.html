canola

noun
vegetable oil from rapeseed
Pronunciation: / kəˈnoʊlə / (AmE), / kəˈnəʊlə / (BrE)
A type of vegetable oil made from specially bred rapeseed, valued for being high in monounsaturated fatty acids and low in saturated fat.

Examples

canola oil cooking
Many health experts recommend canola for its low saturated fat content.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of canola oil instead of butter.
We use canola oil for frying because it's lighter and healthier than other options.
