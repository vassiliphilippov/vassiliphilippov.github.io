control

noun
standard for comparison in experiments
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a standard used in a scientific experiment for comparison with other conditions to determine the effect of an experimental variable

Examples

control group
Every experiment should include a control to ensure the results are valid.
The researchers compared the results of the new drug to those of the control.
Without a proper control, it is difficult to interpret the data from the study.

Related Terms

  • antonym – experimental group: the group in an experiment that receives the treatment or variable being tested
  • antonym – variable: a factor or condition that can change in an experiment
