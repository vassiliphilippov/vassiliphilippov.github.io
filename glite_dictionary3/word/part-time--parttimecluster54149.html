part-time

adverb
for fewer than standard hours; not full-time
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrtˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtˌtaɪm / (BrE)
for less than the usual or full number of hours; not full-time (especially for work or study)

Examples

work part-time
He was employed part-time at the supermarket last summer.
Many students prefer part-time jobs to have more free time.
She decided to work part-time while finishing her degree.
He teaches part-time at the university during the evenings.
Many parents choose flexible jobs so they can be part-time employees.
She decided to work part-time while completing her studies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – full-time: working for the whole of the usual or standard working hours
  • synonym – part-time: working or done for only part of the usual time or hours (adj.)
