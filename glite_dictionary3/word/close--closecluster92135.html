to close

verb
become shut or not open
Pronunciation: / kloʊz / (AmE), / kləʊz / (BrE)
to shut, block, or make something inaccessible (such as a door, window, gap, or computer window)

Examples

to close the door
As the night grew colder, all the windows closed automatically.
Once the store closes, no customers are allowed inside.
The door closed silently behind me as I entered the room.
Before painting, make sure to close any holes or cracks in the wall.
The engineers worked to close the leak in the pipe as quickly as possible.
We need to close all gaps around the windows to prevent drafts.
Could you please close the window?
She always remembers to close the gate after leaving.
The shop will close its doors at 6 p.m. today.
