nail

noun
old unit of length (1/16 yard)
Pronunciation: / neɪl / (AmE), / neɪl / (BrE)
a former unit of length for measuring cloth, equal to 1/16 of a yard (about 2 1/4 inches or 5.7 centimeters)

Examples

measure by nail
He sold fabric by the nail before switching to metric units.
In the past, a nail was commonly used to measure small amounts of cloth.
The tailor requested two nails of velvet for the cuffs.
