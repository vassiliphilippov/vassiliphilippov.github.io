turnpike

noun
historical road gate for toll collection
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːnˌpaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌpaɪk / (BrE)
(historical) A gate or barrier set across a road, especially from the 16th to 19th centuries, to prevent passage until a toll had been paid.

Examples

old turnpike gate
A guard stood by the turnpike, collecting coins from weary travelers.
Several turnpikes once controlled entry to the city after dark.
The old coach was delayed at the turnpike while the driver paid the toll.
