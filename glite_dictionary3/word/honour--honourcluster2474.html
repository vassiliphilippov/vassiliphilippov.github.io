to honour

verb
to show great respect or give an award
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
to show great respect or reverence for someone or something, often by giving a special title, award, or public acknowledgment

Examples

to honour a hero
Each year, we honour those who served in the armed forces.
He was honoured for his outstanding contributions to science.
The city decided to honour her achievements with a special ceremony.
Many cultures honour their ancestors through annual ceremonies and rituals.
She was honoured for her dedication to the community.
We should always honour our promises if we want to be trusted.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disgrace: to bring shame or dishonor upon
  • antonym – dishonour: to show a lack of respect or bring shame upon
  • synonym – respect: to have a deep admiration for someone or something
  • synonym – reward: give something in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
