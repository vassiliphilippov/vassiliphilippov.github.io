Gerrididae

noun
family of insects (water striders)
Pronunciation: / dʒɛˈrɪdəˌdi / (AmE), / dʒɛˈrɪdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Hemiptera, commonly known as water striders, characterized by their ability to walk on water surfaces.

Examples

Gerrididae water striders
Members of the Gerrididae are also called water striders due to their unique locomotion on water.
Scientists have discovered over 750 species within the Gerrididae family worldwide.
The Gerrididae are well known for their ability to skate across the surface of ponds and streams.
