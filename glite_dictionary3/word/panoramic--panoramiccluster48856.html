panoramic

adjective
giving an extensive view
Pronunciation: / ˌpænəˈræmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpænəˈræmɪk / (BrE)
Offering a wide, unbroken view of an area, often seen from a high place or distance.

Examples

panoramic view
She admired the panoramic scenery from the observation deck.
The photograph captures a panoramic landscape stretching from the mountains to the sea.
Visitors climbed the hill to enjoy a panoramic view of the city and its surroundings.

  • antonym – narrow: restricted in extent or scope
  • synonym – sweeping: covering a wide area or range
