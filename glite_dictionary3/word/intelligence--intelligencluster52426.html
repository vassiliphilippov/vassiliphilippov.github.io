intelligence

noun
information-gathering unit or agency
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛl.ə.dʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛl.ɪ.dʒəns / (BrE)
an organization or unit responsible for collecting and analyzing information, especially regarding enemy activities and strategies.

Examples

intelligence agency
After a security breach, the intelligence unit reviewed all classified reports.
She works in military intelligence and specializes in data analysis.
The army's intelligence provided critical information about the enemy's plans.

Related Terms

  • antonym – counterintelligence: activities or departments aiming to prevent spying by enemies
  • synonym – intelligence agency: an organization responsible for spying and gathering confidential information, often for national security
