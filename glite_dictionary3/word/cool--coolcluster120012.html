to cool

verb
become less intense (emotion, enthusiasm)
Pronunciation: / kul / (AmE), / kuːl / (BrE)
(of an emotion or enthusiasm) to become less intense or passionate; to diminish in strength or fervor.

Examples

to cool excitement
After a few months, her excitement about the new job began to cool.
Interest in the project tends to cool when funding runs out.
Their friendship gradually cooled after the argument.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intensify: to become greater in strength or intensity
  • synonym – wane: to decrease in strength, intensity, or power
