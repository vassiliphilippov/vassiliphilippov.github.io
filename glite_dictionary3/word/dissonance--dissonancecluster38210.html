dissonance

noun
harsh or discordant sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.ə.nəns /; / ˈdɪsənəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.ə.nəns /; / ˈdɪsənəns / (BrE)
a combination of sounds that are harsh, jarring, or unpleasant to hear; a lack of harmony in music or noise

Examples

musical dissonance
He winced at the sudden dissonance coming from the orchestra's rehearsal.
Many modern composers make use of dissonance to create tension in their music.
The piece ends with a burst of dissonance that surprises the audience.
His music is full of dissonance and unexpected chord changes.
The dissonance in the experimental piece made some listeners cover their ears.
To emphasize the story’s tension, the composer used sudden dissonance in the background score.
