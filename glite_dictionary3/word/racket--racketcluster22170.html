racket

noun
sports implement with strings for hitting a ball or shuttlecock
Pronunciation: / ˈrækɪt / (AmE), / ˈrækɪt / (BrE)
a sports implement with a handle and an oval frame strung with tightly interlaced strings, used to strike a ball or shuttlecock in games such as tennis or badminton

Examples

tennis racket handle
A good badminton racket should feel light and well-balanced.
She bought a new racket for her tennis lessons.
The strings on his racket snapped during the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bat: a solid implement used to hit a ball in games such as baseball or cricket
  • synonym – racquet: alternate spelling of racket, used especially in sports contexts
