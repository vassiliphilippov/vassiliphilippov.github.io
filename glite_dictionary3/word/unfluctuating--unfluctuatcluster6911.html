unfluctuating

adjective
not changing; steady
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈflʌktʃuˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈflʌktjʊeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
remaining steady and not changing or varying, especially in value or level.

Examples

unfluctuating support
Her support was unfluctuating throughout the project, never wavering even once.
The exchange rate remained unfluctuating despite economic uncertainty.
We prefer investments with a more unfluctuating value over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fluctuating: rising and falling irregularly in number or amount
  • synonym – steady: firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
