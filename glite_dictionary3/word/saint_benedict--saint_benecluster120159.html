Saint Benedict

proper noun
Italian monk; founder of Benedictine order
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈbɛnədɪkt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbɛnədɪkt / (BrE)
(c. 480–547) An Italian Christian monk who founded the Benedictine order of monks and established the Rule of St. Benedict, which became the standard for Western monasticism.

Examples

Saint Benedict founded monasteries
Saint Benedict is often depicted in art holding a book and a cross.
Many monasteries across Europe were established by followers of Saint Benedict.
The Rule of Saint Benedict has influenced Christian monastic life for centuries.
