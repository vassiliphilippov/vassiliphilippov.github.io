to retrograde

verb
to worsen; revert to a previous state
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of a process, condition, or action) to become worse or revert to an earlier, less developed state.

Examples

to retrograde in health
If left untreated, the disease may retrograde and become more difficult to manage.
Sometimes, social progress can retrograde during periods of political instability.
The company started to retrograde after a series of poor management decisions.
