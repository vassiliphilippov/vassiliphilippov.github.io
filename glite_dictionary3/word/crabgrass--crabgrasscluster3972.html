crabgrass

noun
invasive lawn weed grass
Pronunciation: / ˈkræbˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkræbˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass with creeping stems that root easily and is considered a common weed in lawns.

Examples

crabgrass in lawns
Gardeners often struggle to eliminate crabgrass from their flower beds every summer.
If left unchecked, crabgrass can quickly overtake healthy turf grasses.
The crabgrass has spread across the entire backyard, making it difficult to keep the lawn neat.
