antimycotic

noun
fungus-destroying or inhibiting agent
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.maɪˈkɑː.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.maɪˈkɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
a substance that destroys or prevents the growth of fungi, especially used in medicine to treat fungal infections

Examples

antimycotic medicine
Research into new antimycotic agents is crucial due to growing resistance among fungi.
The doctor prescribed an antimycotic to treat the persistent fungal infection.
Topical antimycotic creams are commonly used for athlete's foot.
