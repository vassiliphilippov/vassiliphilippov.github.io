collectivisation

noun
policy of organizing an economy by collectivism
Pronunciation: / kəˌlɛktəvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌlɛktɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the organization of an economy or nation according to the principles of collectivism, especially by replacing private ownership with collective ownership of property and resources

Examples

collectivisation of farms
Debates about collectivisation continue to influence modern economic policy discussions.
Many farmers suffered as a result of forced collectivisation.
The policy of collectivisation transformed agriculture in the Soviet Union during the 1930s.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collectivism: the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual
  • antonym – privatization: the transfer of business, industry, or service from public to private ownership and control
