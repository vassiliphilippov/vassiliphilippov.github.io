shunning

noun
the act of deliberately avoiding
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of deliberately avoiding or ostracizing someone or something.

Examples

shunning a former friend
After her controversial remarks, she faced shunning by members of her community.
Some religious groups practice shunning as a form of discipline for breaking rules.
The fear of shunning keeps many people from expressing unpopular opinions.
