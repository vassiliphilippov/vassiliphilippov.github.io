to stew

verb
remain upset or resentful
Pronunciation: / stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
to remain silently angry or upset, letting negative feelings build up

Examples

to stew over problems
He stewed in silence, refusing to talk to anyone for hours.
If you keep stewing about the mistake, you’ll never move forward.
She continued to stew over his rude comment long after the conversation had ended.
After the argument, he would just stew in his room for hours, refusing to speak to anyone.
Don’t stew all day—talk to me about what's bothering you.
She tends to stew over minor mistakes instead of moving on quickly.

  • synonym – fume: to be visibly angry or irritated
