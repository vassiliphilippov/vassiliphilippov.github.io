ctenoid

adjective
having comb-like projections or edges
Pronunciation: / ˈtiː.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtiː.nɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a structure) having projections or edges resembling the teeth of a comb; especially used to describe certain types of fish scales.

Examples

ctenoid fish scales
The fish is covered in ctenoid scales, which feel rough to the touch.
The scientist explained that bass have ctenoid scales, unlike the smooth cycloid scales of trout.
Under the microscope, the scale’s ctenoid edge was clearly visible, lined with tiny comb-like projections.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cycloid: (of a scale) smooth-edged, in contrast to ctenoid
