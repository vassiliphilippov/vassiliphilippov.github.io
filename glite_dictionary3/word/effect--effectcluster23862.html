effect

noun
symptom or result of illness/drug
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a result or change that is caused by something (such as an action, event, illness, or drug)

Examples

effect of a drug
Doctors are studying the long-term effects of the virus on patients.
Many medications can have unexpected effects on the body.

She noticed the effect of the medicine within an hour.
The new law had a significant effect on local businesses.
The sound effect made the movie much more exciting.
