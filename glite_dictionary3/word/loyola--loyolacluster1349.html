Loyola

proper noun
Spanish founder of the Jesuits
Pronunciation: / loʊˈjoɪlə / (AmE), / lɔɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
Ignatius of Loyola (1491–1556), Spanish theologian and founder of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits), and a leading figure in the Catholic Counter-Reformation.

Examples

Loyola founded Jesuits
Loyola founded the Society of Jesus to promote Catholic education and missionary work.
Many schools and universities around the world are named after Loyola.
The teachings of Loyola have had a significant influence on the Catholic Church.
