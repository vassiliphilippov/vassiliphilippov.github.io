to transform

verb
alter a bacterial cell by DNA introduction
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfɔrm / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to change a bacterial cell into a genetically distinct cell by introducing DNA from another cell of the same or a closely related species

Examples

to transform bacteria
Researchers successfully transformed the bacterial strain by inserting a gene from another species.
Scientists can transform bacteria in the laboratory to carry new genetic traits.
The experiment transforms the cells by introducing foreign DNA into their structure.
