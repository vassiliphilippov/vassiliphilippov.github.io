alloy

noun
mixture of metals
Pronunciation: / ˈælˌɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɔɪ / (BrE)
A substance made by mixing two or more metals, or a metal and another element, usually by melting them together.

Examples

metal alloy
Brass is an alloy of copper and zinc, commonly used for making musical instruments.
Many coins are made from an alloy rather than pure metal to increase durability.
Stainless steel is an alloy containing iron, chromium, and often nickel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – metal: a solid material, typically hard, shiny, malleable, fusible, and ductile, with good electrical and thermal conductivity
