to temporise

verb
delay to gain time or avoid action
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəraɪz / (BrE)
to delay making a decision or taking action, especially in order to gain time or avoid conflict

Examples

to temporise during talks
She tried to temporise by raising minor objections during the meeting.
The committee continued to temporise despite growing pressure to make a decision.
The manager chose to temporise rather than commit to a clear plan immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dawdle: to waste time and move slowly
  • antonym – decide: to come to a resolution or make a final choice
