paralanguage

noun
non-verbal elements of spoken communication
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the aspects of spoken communication other than the words themselves, such as intonation, pitch, and speed, that express meaning or emotion

Examples

paralanguage in speech
Effective communication relies not only on words but also on paralanguage such as tone and pitch.
His paralanguage conveyed more about his feelings than the actual words he used.
Training in paralanguage can help improve public speaking skills.
