to step

verb
enter or move into a new situation
Pronunciation: / stɛp / (AmE), / stɛp / (BrE)
to move or proceed into a new situation or role, often implying a change or transition

Examples

to step into a role
After years of hard work, she finally stepped into a leadership position at the company.
He is about to step into a new role as a parent for the first time.
The artist stepped into the world of filmmaking with her latest project.

  • antonym – leave: to go out of or depart from a place or situation
