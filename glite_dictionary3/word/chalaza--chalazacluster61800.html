chalaza

noun
base of a plant ovule opposite the micropyle
Pronunciation: / kəˈleɪzə / (AmE), / kəˈleɪzə / (BrE)
The part of a plant ovule at the base, opposite the micropyle, where the integument and the nucellus are joined.

Examples

chalaza in plant ovule
In botanical diagrams, the chalaza is always marked at the base of the ovule.
Nutrients are transported from the chalaza to the developing embryo sac in flowering plants.
The fusion of integument and nucellus at the chalaza is vital for seed development.
