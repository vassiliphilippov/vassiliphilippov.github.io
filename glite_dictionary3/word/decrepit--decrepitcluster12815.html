decrepit

adjective
old and in poor condition
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkrɛpɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɛpɪt / (BrE)
Worn out or ruined because of age or neglect; in poor condition due to long use or lack of maintenance.

Examples

decrepit building
He drove a decrepit car that barely made it up the hill.
Over the years, the factory became increasingly decrepit until it was eventually demolished.
The abandoned house at the end of the street looked decrepit and haunted.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dilapidated: in a state of ruin or disrepair, especially as a result of age or neglect
  • antonym – new: recently made or in good condition; not old or worn
  • synonym – run-down: in bad condition due to lack of care or attention
