to idealise

verb
see or present as perfect
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdiəlaɪz /; / aɪˈdiəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to see or show something or someone as perfect or better than they really are, often ignoring faults

Examples

to idealise a person
After moving away, she tended to idealise her hometown and remember only the good times.
It is common for young people to idealise celebrities and overlook their flaws.
The director tried not to idealise the historical figures, portraying them with all their strengths and weaknesses.
Her biography clearly idealises the achievements of its subject.
It can be dangerous to idealise someone you have just met.
Many people tend to idealise their childhood memories, forgetting the difficult moments.
