to page

verb
number the pages of a book or document
Pronunciation: / peɪdʒ / (AmE), / peɪdʒ / (BrE)
to number the pages of (a book or manuscript) in sequence

Examples

to page a manuscript
After editing the manuscript, she began to page it carefully to avoid mistakes.
Each chapter must be paged consecutively for the book to meet the publisher’s requirements.
The printer forgot to page the document before binding it.
