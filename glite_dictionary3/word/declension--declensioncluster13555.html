declension

noun
a process of decline or deterioration
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈklɛnʃən / (BrE)
a process or period of decline, deterioration, or moving to a worse or less prosperous state

Examples

declension of morals
Some critics attribute the empire's fall to a gradual declension in moral values.
The report highlights the declension of standards in public education.
There was a noticeable declension in the quality of the neighborhood over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • synonym – decline: a gradual and continuous loss of strength or value
  • antonym – improvement: the process of becoming better
