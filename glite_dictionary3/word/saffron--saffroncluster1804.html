saffron

noun
a crocus whose stigmas are used as a spice
Pronunciation: / ˈsæfrən / (AmE), / ˈsæfrən / (BrE)
A crocus plant native to southwest Asia, with purple or white flowers and bright orange stigmas, which are dried to make the spice used in cooking and as coloring.

Examples

saffron crocus flower
Farmers carefully harvested the precious stigmas from the saffron flowers at dawn.
The fields of saffron blossomed in autumn, their purple petals carpeting the landscape.
This region is famous for its cultivation of saffron, which is prized around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym – saffron (spice): the spice derived from the dried stigmas of the saffron crocus
