suppressed

adjective
kept from public knowledge
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛst / (AmE), / səˈprɛst / (BrE)
kept from being known, revealed, or expressed; prevented from being made public

Examples

suppressed information
Details of the investigation were suppressed to protect the victim's identity.
Evidence that contradicted their claims was suppressed during the trial.
The government suppressed reports of the incident to avoid public panic.
His suppressed laughter threatened to escape during the serious meeting.
Many artists worked under a suppressed culture where creative freedom was not permitted.
The suppressed report contained valuable information that the public never saw.
