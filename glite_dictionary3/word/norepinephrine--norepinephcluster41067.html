norepinephrine

noun
hormone and neurotransmitter
Pronunciation: / ˌnɔrəˌɛpɪˈnɛfrɪn / (AmE), / ˌnɒrɪˌɛpɪˈnɛfrɪn / (BrE)
A hormone and neurotransmitter, produced by the adrenal glands and some nerve endings, that helps regulate blood pressure and mood.

Examples

release norepinephrine quickly
During stressful situations, the body releases norepinephrine to increase heart rate and alertness.
Low levels of norepinephrine may contribute to depression.
The doctor explained that norepinephrine acts on both the brain and the body.
