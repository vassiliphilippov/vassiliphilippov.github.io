Pteridospermae

noun
extinct group of fossil seed ferns
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrɪdoʊˈspɜːrmi / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪdəʊˈspɜːmiː / (BrE)
a group of extinct seed-bearing plants (fossil gymnosperms), sometimes called seed ferns, known mainly from the Paleozoic era and classified under the order Cycadofilicales

Examples

fossil Pteridospermae plants
Fossils of Pteridospermae play a vital role in understanding plant evolution in the Paleozoic era.
Several species within the Pteridospermae group exhibited both seed- and fern-like characteristics.
The Pteridospermae are often referred to as seed ferns due to their fern-like appearance.
