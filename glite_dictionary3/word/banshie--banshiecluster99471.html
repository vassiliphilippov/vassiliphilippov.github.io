banshie

noun
female spirit in Irish folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈbænʃi / (AmE), / ˈbanʃi / (BrE)
(In Irish folklore) a female spirit whose wailing warns of an impending death in a family.

Examples

wailing banshie spirit
In ancient tales, a family's fate was sealed by the mournful cry of a banshie at midnight.
She swore she heard the wail of a banshie the night before her uncle passed away.
The legend of the banshie remains one of the most haunting in Irish folklore.
