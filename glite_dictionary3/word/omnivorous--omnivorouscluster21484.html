omnivorous

adjective
eating both plants and animals
Pronunciation: / ɑmˈnɪvərəs / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪvərəs / (BrE)
eating both plant and animal matter.

Examples

omnivorous animals eat meat
Humans are considered omnivorous because they eat both meat and vegetables.
Some bears are omnivorous, eating both berries and small animals.
The omnivorous diet of raccoons allows them to survive in many environments.

  • antonym – herbivorous: eating only plants
