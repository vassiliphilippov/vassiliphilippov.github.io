to cripple

verb
render ineffective or powerless
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪp.əl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪp.əl / (BrE)
to cause serious damage to someone or something, making them weak or unable to function effectively

Examples

to cripple the economy
Fear of failure can sometimes cripple a person’s ability to try new things.
New regulations could cripple small businesses if they are not carefully implemented.
The severe storm crippled the city’s power supply for days.

Related Terms

  • antonym – enable: to provide with the means or ability to do something
  • synonym – handicap: to put at a disadvantage or render less efficient
  • synonym – paralyze: to make unable to move or function
