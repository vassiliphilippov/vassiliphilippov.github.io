croaker

noun
lean flesh of a saltwater fish (food)
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkə / (BrE)
the lean flesh of a saltwater fish, especially any of several species of the drum family found along the Atlantic coast of the southern United States, used as food

Examples

fresh croaker fillet
Fishermen along the coast often sell fresh croaker at the market each morning.
Grilled croaker is a favorite dish in some southern seafood restaurants.
The chef prepared the croaker fillets with a spicy southern seasoning.
