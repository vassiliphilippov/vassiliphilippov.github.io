Chalcedon

noun
the fourth ecumenical council of 451 defining Christ's two natures
Pronunciation: / kælˈsiːdən / (AmE), / kælˈsiːdən / (BrE)
the fourth ecumenical council held in AD 451 that defined the doctrine of the two natures (human and divine) in Christ

Examples

Chalcedon council 451
Debates at Chalcedon centered on the nature of Christ and his divinity.
Many historical theologians reference the outcomes of Chalcedon in their writings.
The Council of Chalcedon played a crucial role in shaping Christian doctrine.
