Hallel

noun
Jewish chant of praise recited on festivals
Pronunciation: / hɑˈlɛl / (AmE), / haˈlɛl / (BrE)
A Jewish chant of praise consisting of Psalms 113 to 118, recited on religious festivals such as Passover, Shavuot, Sukkot, Hanukkah, and Rosh Chodesh.

Examples

recite the Hallel prayers
During Passover, families gather to recite the Hallel as part of the celebration.
Reciting the Hallel is an important tradition during Jewish festivals.
The congregation sang the Hallel with great enthusiasm on Hanukkah.
