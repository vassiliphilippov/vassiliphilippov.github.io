to seep into

verb
to slowly pass or leak through
Pronunciation: / sip ˈɪntu / (AmE), / siːp ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to slowly move or leak through a surface or into something.

Examples

to seep into the ground
Darkness seemed to seep into every corner of the abandoned house.
Over time, the smell of smoke would seep into the old furniture.
The rainwater began to seep into the basement after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leak: to allow liquid or gas to escape slowly
