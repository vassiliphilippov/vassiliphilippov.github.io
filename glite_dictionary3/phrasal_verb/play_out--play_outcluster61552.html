to play out

verb
continue playing until finished
Pronunciation: / pleɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pleɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to continue playing a game, match, or contest until it finishes, especially to the end regardless of the outcome

Examples

play out the match
Despite knowing they would lose, the team decided to play out the match to the very end.
The chess players agreed to play out the game even though it seemed obvious who would win.
We have to play out the last few minutes of the match, regardless of the score.

Related Terms

  • synonym – finish: to bring something to an end or completion
  • antonym – quit: to stop or give up before something is finished
