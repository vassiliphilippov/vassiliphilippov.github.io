to spit out

verb
eject suddenly from the mouth
Pronunciation: / spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to force something out of your mouth suddenly and forcefully (such as food, gum, or phlegm)

Examples

to spit out food

He coughed violently and spit out a thick glob of sputum into the tissue.
Please do not spit out gum on the sidewalk.
The patient was instructed to spit out any phlegm after coughing.
Please spit out your gum before entering the classroom.
She quickly spit out the sour candy when she realized how bad it tasted.
The child spat out the medicine as soon as she tasted it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expectorate: to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs
  • synonym – spew: to eject forcefully, often refers to liquid or vomit
  • synonym – spit up: to bring up something from the stomach or mouth, often for babies
