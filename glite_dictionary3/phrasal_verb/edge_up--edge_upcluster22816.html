to edge up

verb
increase slightly
Pronunciation: / ɛdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / ɛdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to increase slowly or slightly, especially used for prices, amounts, or rates.

Examples

edge up in price
The stock index continued to edge up gradually after the market rally.
Temperatures are expected to edge up a few degrees by the weekend.
Sales began to edge up slowly after the new advertising campaign launched.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fall: to decrease in amount or level; the opposite of increasing
  • synonym – rise: to move or go upward; to increase in level or amount
  • synonym – tick up: to move slightly upward (often used about small changes in numbers or prices)
