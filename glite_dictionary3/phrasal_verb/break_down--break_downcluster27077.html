to break down

verb
make ineffective; remove obstacles
Pronunciation: / breɪk daʊn / (AmE), / breɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to make something ineffective or to remove obstacles, especially in a system or attitude

Examples

break down barriers
Activists hope to break down outdated social norms with their campaign.
His speech helped break down resistance to the new reforms.
The government is working to break down barriers to equal education.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abolish: to formally put an end to a system, practice, or institution
  • synonym – overcome: to succeed in dealing with or controlling a problem or difficulty
