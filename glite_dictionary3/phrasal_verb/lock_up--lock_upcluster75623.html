to lock up

verb
to confine securely
Pronunciation: / lɑk ʌp / (AmE), / lɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a place that cannot be left or removed, usually to keep them safe or to prevent escape

Examples

to lock up a prisoner
Make sure you lock up your valuables before you leave the hotel room.
The police decided to lock up the suspect overnight for questioning.
They threatened to lock up anyone found trespassing after dark.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confine: to keep someone or something within limits
  • antonym – release: to set someone or something free
