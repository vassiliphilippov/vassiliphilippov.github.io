to step up

verb
take responsibility; become more involved
Pronunciation: / stɛp ʌp / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp / (BrE)
to take action or become more actively involved, especially by accepting responsibility or making oneself visible

Examples

to step up as leader
It's time for someone to step up and lead this project.
She decided to step up and address the issue directly.
When the team needed help, he didn't hesitate to step up.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hold back: to refrain from taking action or becoming involved
  • synonym – step forward: to volunteer or come forward to help
