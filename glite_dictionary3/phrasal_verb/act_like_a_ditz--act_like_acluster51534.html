to act like a ditz

verb
behave in a silly or foolish way
Pronunciation: / ækt laɪk ə dɪts / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə dɪts / (BrE)
to behave in a silly or foolish way, especially in a manner that appears scatterbrained or superficial

Examples

to act like a ditz on purpose
During the interview, she deliberately acted like a ditz to appear less intimidating.
He tends to act like a ditz whenever he wants to avoid serious questions.
Please don't act like a ditz; I know you're actually quite clever.

Related Terms

  • antonym – act sensibly: to behave in a reasonable or wise manner
  • synonym – act silly: to behave in a playful or foolish way
