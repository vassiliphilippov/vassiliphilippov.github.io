to run for

verb
last for a certain period
Pronunciation: / rʌn fɔr / (AmE), / rʌn fə / (BrE)
(of a program, meeting, performance, etc.) to last or continue for a particular period of time.

Examples

run for one year
My subscription runs for one year from the date of purchase.
The lecture ran for nearly two hours, but the audience remained attentive.
The play will run for three weeks at the downtown theater.
