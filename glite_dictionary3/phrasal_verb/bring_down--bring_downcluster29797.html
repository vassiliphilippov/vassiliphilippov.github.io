to bring down

verb
cause to fall or land (esp. aircraft)
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to move something or someone from a higher to a lower position, or to land an aircraft

Examples

to bring down a plane
After hours of negotiation, the pilot finally brought down the plane without incident.
The firefighters managed to bring down the damaged helicopter safely despite the storm.
The hunter tried to bring down the bird with a single shot.
Could you bring down that box from the top shelf for me?
She brings down the curtains every spring to wash them.
The fireman brought down the child safely from the ladder.

Related Terms

  • synonym – land: to bring an aircraft or a person safely to the ground
  • antonym – lift: to move or raise to a higher position
  • synonym – take down: to remove from a higher to a lower place
