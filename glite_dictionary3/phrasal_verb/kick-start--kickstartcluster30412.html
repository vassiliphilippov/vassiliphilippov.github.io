to kick-start

verb
to stimulate or initiate something quickly
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪk stɑrt / (AmE), / ˈkɪk stɑːt / (BrE)
to provide a quick start or stimulus to something, especially to begin a process or activity or to revive it.

Examples

to kick-start a project
A strong marketing campaign can kick-start new product sales.
Her passionate speech helped kick-start the community project.
The government plans to kick-start the economy with tax cuts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jump-start: to start or stimulate something quickly, especially a process or project
