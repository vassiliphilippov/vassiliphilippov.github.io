to slack up

verb
slow down or reduce activity
Pronunciation: / slæk ʌp / (AmE), / slæk ʌp / (BrE)
to slow down, become less active, or reduce the intensity of something

Examples

to slack up the pace
As the team grew tired, they began to slack up during the final minutes of the game.
Traffic didn't slack up until well after midnight last night.
You can't afford to slack up on your studies right before the exams.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slacken: to make or become slower or less intense
  • antonym – speed up: to make or become faster; increase speed
