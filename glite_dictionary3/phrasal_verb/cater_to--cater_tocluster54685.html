to cater to

verb
to provide for specific needs or demands
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪtər tu / (AmE), / ˈkeɪtə tə / (BrE)
to provide services or things that a particular person or group wants or needs, often with the implication of trying to please them

Examples

to cater to customers
Some politicians cater to public opinion in order to gain more votes.
The hotel staff always cater to their guests' preferences, no matter how unusual.
The new smartphone is designed to cater to tech-savvy users with advanced features.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cater for: to provide what is needed for someone or something
