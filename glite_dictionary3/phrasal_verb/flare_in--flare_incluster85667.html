to flare in

verb
suddenly become more intense or noticeable
Pronunciation: / flɛr ɪn / (AmE), / fleə ɪn / (BrE)
to become suddenly more intense or noticeable, especially after a period of calm or lower activity

Examples

flare in talent
Her artistic talents really flared in during her final year at university.
Public interest in the case started to flare in after new evidence was revealed.
Tensions between the two teams began to flare in the second half of the match.
