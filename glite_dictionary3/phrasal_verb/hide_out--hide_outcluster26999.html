to hide out

verb
stay secretly to avoid danger
Pronunciation: / haɪd aʊt / (AmE), / haɪd aʊt / (BrE)
to stay in a secret place, especially to avoid trouble or danger

Examples

to hide out in a safe place
After the robbery, the suspects decided to hide out in an abandoned warehouse.
She had to hide out for weeks until it was safe to return home.
The witness chose to hide out from the media until the trial was over.

Related Terms

  • antonym – come out: to leave a place of hiding; opposite
  • synonym – lay low: to keep oneself out of sight to avoid trouble; similar meaning
