to boil down

verb
have as the main or essential aspect
Pronunciation: / bɔɪl daʊn / (AmE), / bɔɪl daʊn / (BrE)
(of an idea, situation, or statement) to have as its main or most important aspect.

Examples

boil down to one point
Her explanation boiled down to a simple request for help.
It all boils down to making the right decision at the right time.
The issue boils down to whether we can afford it or not.
