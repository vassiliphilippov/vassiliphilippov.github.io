to dangle from

verb
hang loosely from something
Pronunciation: / ˈdæŋɡəl frəm / (AmE), / ˈdaŋɡl̩ frəm / (BrE)
to hang or swing loosely from a support or attachment point

Examples

dangle from the ceiling
A single bulb dangled from the ceiling, barely lighting the room.
Several keys dangled from his belt as he walked down the corridor.
The child's feet dangled from the edge of the dock, just above the water.

  • synonym – hang from: to be attached and suspended by something
