to take on

verb
admit to a group or organization
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ɒn / (BrE)
to allow someone to join a group, organization, or community

Examples

take on new members
Each year, the university takes on dozens of new students from around the world.
Our club will take on new members at the start of the season.
The company decided not to take on any new employees this quarter.
