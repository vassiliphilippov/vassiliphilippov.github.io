to flow away

verb
to move away in a stream or current
Pronunciation: / floʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / fləʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to move or be carried away from a place, typically gradually, in a stream or current.

Examples

to flow away slowly
After the dam burst, most of the water flowed away down the valley.
The dye started to flow away from the fabric when it got wet.
The rainwater will flow away if the gutters are kept clean.
