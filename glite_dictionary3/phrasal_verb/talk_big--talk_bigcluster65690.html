to talk big

verb
to boast or brag
Pronunciation: / tɔk bɪɡ / (AmE), / tɔːk bɪɡ / (BrE)
to boast or brag, often making exaggerated claims about oneself

Examples

to talk big about success
He loves to talk big about his business deals, but most of them never happen.
People who talk big usually have little to show for it.
She doesn't talk big; she prefers to let her actions speak for themselves.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boast: to talk with excessive pride about achievements or possessions
  • synonym – brag: to speak too proudly about what one has done or owns
  • antonym – downplay: to make something appear less important or impressive
