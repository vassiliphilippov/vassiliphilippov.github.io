to bail out

verb
provide financial or emergency help
Pronunciation: / beɪl aʊt / (AmE), / beɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to give financial help or other assistance to prevent a person, company, or organization from failing or getting into serious trouble

Examples

to bail out a company
After the storm damaged their business, the owners hoped that someone would bail out their company.
She asked her parents to bail out the family restaurant when revenue dropped suddenly.
The government had to bail out the airline to prevent thousands of layoffs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bail out (literal): to remove water from something (like a boat) with a container
  • synonym – rescue: to save someone or something from danger or harm
