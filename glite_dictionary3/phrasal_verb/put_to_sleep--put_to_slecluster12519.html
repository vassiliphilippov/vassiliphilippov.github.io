to put to sleep

verb
make unconscious, euthanize, or cause to forget
Pronunciation: / pʊt tə sliːp / (AmE), / pʊt tə sliːp / (BrE)
to cause to become unconscious, especially by anesthesia; to euthanize an animal humanely; or to cause someone to forget or stop thinking about something.

Examples

to put to sleep a patient
After the accident, the doctor decided to put her to sleep before operating.
He tries to put to sleep the painful memories of his childhood.
It was heartbreaking when we had to put our cat to sleep last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anesthetize: to make someone unconscious for surgery with anesthesia
  • synonym – euthanize: to humanely end the life of an animal or person
