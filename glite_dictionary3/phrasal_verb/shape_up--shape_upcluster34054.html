to shape up

verb
improve behavior or performance
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪp ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to improve one's behavior, performance, or physical fitness, especially after a period of neglect or poor conduct

Examples

to shape up at work
After a warning from her boss, she began to shape up at work.
If you don't shape up, you'll be in danger of failing the class.
Many people resolve to shape up at the start of the new year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deteriorate: to become worse
  • synonym – improve: to become better or make something better
