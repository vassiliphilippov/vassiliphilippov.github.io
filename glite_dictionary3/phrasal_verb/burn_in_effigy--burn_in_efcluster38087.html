to burn in effigy

verb
publicly protest by burning a figure
Pronunciation: / bɜrn ɪn ˈɛfɪdʒi / (AmE), / bɜːn ɪn ˈɛfɪdʒi / (BrE)
to publicly express strong disapproval or criticism of someone by burning a representation of them, usually a dummy or image, in protest.

Examples

to burn in effigy a leader
During the demonstration, an effigy of the CEO was burned in effigy to show the workers' anger.
He was shocked to learn that a group had burned him in effigy after his controversial speech.
The protesters decided to burn in effigy the unpopular politician outside city hall.

  • synonym – effigy: a sculpture or model of a person
