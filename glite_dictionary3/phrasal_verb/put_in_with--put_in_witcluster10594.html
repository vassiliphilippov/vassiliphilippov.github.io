to put in with

verb
to become part of a group or activity
Pronunciation: / pʊt ɪn wɪθ / (AmE), / pʊt ɪn wɪð / (BrE)
to join or become associated with a group of people or others in an activity

Examples

put in with a group
After moving to the city, he decided to put in with a group of musicians to find friends.
Many students put in with campus clubs to meet people with similar interests.
She hopes to put in with the new marketing team at her company.
