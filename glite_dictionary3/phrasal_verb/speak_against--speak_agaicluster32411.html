to speak against

verb
express opposition or criticism publicly
Pronunciation: / spiːk əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / spiːk əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to express opposition to or criticize something publicly

Examples

to speak against new rules
During the meeting, several members spoke against the changes suggested by management.
Many leaders chose to speak against the proposed law during the public forum.
She was not afraid to speak against injustice, even when others remained silent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oppose: to actively resist or refuse to comply with
  • antonym – speak for: to express support for something or someone publicly
