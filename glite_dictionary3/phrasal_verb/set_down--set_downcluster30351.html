to set down

verb
remove and leave cargo or people from transport
Pronunciation: / sɛt daʊn / (AmE), / sɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to remove (cargo, passengers, etc.) from a vehicle and leave them at a particular place

Examples

to set down passengers
Please set down the boxes by the loading dock when you arrive.
The bus set down its passengers in front of the hotel.
The driver set down the tourists at the entrance to the museum.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drop off: to deliver someone or something to a place
  • antonym – pick up: to collect or take on passengers or cargo
