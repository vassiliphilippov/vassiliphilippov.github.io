to lead up

verb
to prepare or set in motion before an event
Pronunciation: / liːd ʌp / (AmE), / liːd ʌp / (BrE)
to cause or prepare something to happen, especially by creating the necessary conditions or events beforehand

Examples

events lead up to a result
Her months of training led up to her winning the marathon.
Many small changes led up to the company's major restructuring.
The tense negotiations led up to the signing of the peace treaty.

Related Terms

  • synonym – precede: to come before something in time or order
