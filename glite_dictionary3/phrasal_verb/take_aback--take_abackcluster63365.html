to take aback

verb
to surprise or astonish
Pronunciation: / teɪk əˈbæk / (AmE), / teɪk əˈbak / (BrE)
to surprise or shock someone greatly; to astonish

Examples

take aback with news
I was truly taken aback by the amount of support I received.
She was taken aback when she saw the beautiful surprise party.
The news of his resignation took aback everyone in the office.
