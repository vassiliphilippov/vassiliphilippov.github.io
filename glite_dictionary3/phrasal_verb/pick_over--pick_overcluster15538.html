to pick over

verb
examine items to select or remove
Pronunciation: / pɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / pɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to examine a group of things carefully in order to choose the best or to remove the unwanted ones

Examples

pick over the beans
Before cooking, it's important to pick over the beans to remove any stones.
She always picks over the strawberries before buying them.
The volunteers picked over the donated clothes for anything that could be salvaged.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reject: to refuse to accept or choose something
  • synonym – sort through: to examine a group of items to find something or put them in order
