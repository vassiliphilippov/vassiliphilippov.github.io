to contract out

verb
arrange work to be done externally
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrækt aʊt / (AmE), / kənˈtrɑːkt aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange for work to be done by a person or company outside your own organization, rather than employing staff to do it

Examples

to contract out work
Many organizations contract out IT support instead of hiring full-time employees.
The company decided to contract out its cleaning services to a local firm.
They have contracted out the catering for the annual conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym – outsource: to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier
