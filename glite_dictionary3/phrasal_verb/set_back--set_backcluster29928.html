to set back

verb
to cost (money; informal)
Pronunciation: / sɛt bæk / (AmE), / sɛt bæk / (BrE)
(informal) To cost someone a specified amount of money, often more than expected.

Examples

set back your wallet
A trip to Paris will set back most travelers at least a thousand dollars.
That new car set back my parents nearly $30,000.
The repairs to the roof will set back the company a considerable sum.
