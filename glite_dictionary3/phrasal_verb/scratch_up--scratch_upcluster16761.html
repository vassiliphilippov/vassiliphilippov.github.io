to scratch up

verb
gather with difficulty
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / skrætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to obtain or gather (money, supplies, or similar resources) with difficulty, by scraping together small amounts, borrowing, or making extra effort.

Examples

to scratch up money
We managed to scratch up enough money to cover the car repair.
She is trying to scratch up some spare parts from friends to fix the bike.
If we hurry, maybe we can scratch up the remaining fees before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scrape together: to collect a small amount of something, usually money, by making an effort or using small sources
  • synonym – scrounge up: to find or get something, especially money or supplies, often by asking others or searching
