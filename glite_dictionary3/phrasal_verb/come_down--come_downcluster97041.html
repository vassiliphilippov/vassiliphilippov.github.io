to come down

verb
move downward; become lower
Pronunciation: / kʌm daʊn / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn / (BrE)
to move downward, become lower, or decrease in value, amount, or mood

Examples

come down in price
As the sun set, the temperature began to come down quickly.
The prices of houses finally came down after years of steady increase.
You could hear the excitement in the stadium come down after the game ended.
If property values come down any further, it could be a good time to buy.
She was excited at first, but her mood really came down after the argument.
The cost of gasoline has finally come down after months of high prices.

Related Terms

  • synonym – descend: move or fall downward
  • synonym – fall: to decrease, drop, or come down from a higher position
  • antonym – go up: to increase in amount, level, or price
  • antonym – rise: move upward or increase in level
