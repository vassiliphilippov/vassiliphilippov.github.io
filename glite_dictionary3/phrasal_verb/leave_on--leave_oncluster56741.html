to leave on

verb
not turn off or remove
Pronunciation: / liv ɑn / (AmE), / liːv ɒn / (BrE)
to allow something to remain operating, activated, or in place; not to remove or switch off something

Examples

leave on the lights
Don't leave on your computer all night to save energy.
Please leave on the lights until I get back.
She prefers to leave on her jewelry while sleeping.
