to take off

verb
to begin or progress rapidly (figurative)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to become successful or popular, often suddenly

Examples

take off in popularity
After months of planning, our new marketing campaign finally took off last week.
Once the new smartphone app was released, downloads really took off.
Online shopping only took off after fast delivery became available.
After winning the award, his business really started to take off.
He decided to take off from work without telling anyone.
She hurried to take off her coat as she entered the warm house.
