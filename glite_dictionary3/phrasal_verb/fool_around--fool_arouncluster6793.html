to fool around

verb
have an affair or commit adultery (informal)
Pronunciation: / ful əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(informal) To have a sexual relationship with someone who is not your spouse or regular partner; to commit adultery.

Examples

fool around with another person
It's heartbreaking when someone finds out their spouse has been fooling around.
Rumors spread that he had fooled around with one of his colleagues.
She divorced him after learning he had fooled around during their marriage.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be faithful: to remain loyal and not commit adultery
  • synonym – cheat: to have a sexual relationship with someone other than your partner
