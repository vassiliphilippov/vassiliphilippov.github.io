to stamp out

verb
eliminate or end by force
Pronunciation: / stæmp aʊt / (AmE), / stæmp aʊt / (BrE)
to eliminate or put an end to something, especially by using strong or forceful action

Examples

to stamp out a problem
Health officials are working hard to stamp out the spread of the disease.
The government hopes to stamp out corruption through strict new laws.
They launched a campaign to stamp out smoking in public places.

Related Terms

  • antonym – encourage: to give support, confidence, or hope to someone (opposite of suppress)
  • synonym – eradicate: to completely destroy or eliminate something harmful
  • synonym – suppress: to forcibly put an end to something
