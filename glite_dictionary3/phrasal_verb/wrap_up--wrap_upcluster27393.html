to wrap up

verb
cover or protect by folding around
Pronunciation: / ræp ʌp / (AmE), / ræp ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange or cover something, or someone, completely with paper, cloth, or another material to protect or conceal it

Examples

to wrap up leftovers
Let’s wrap up the leftovers so we can eat them tomorrow.
She remembered to wrap up the fragile vase in bubble wrap before packing it.
You should wrap up warmly if you're going outside in the snow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unwrap: to remove a covering or wrapper
