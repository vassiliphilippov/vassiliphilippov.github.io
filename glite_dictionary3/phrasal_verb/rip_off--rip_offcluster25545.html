to rip off

verb
steal; take without consent
Pronunciation: / rɪp ɔf / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to steal something, especially by taking it quickly or without the owner's knowledge

Examples

to rip off a wallet
He was caught ripping off expensive electronics from the store.
Someone tried to rip off my bag while I was shopping in the market.
The thieves ripped off several laptops from the office last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pilfer: to steal, especially small amounts or things of little value
  • antonym – return: to give back something that was taken
  • synonym – steal: to take another person's property without permission
