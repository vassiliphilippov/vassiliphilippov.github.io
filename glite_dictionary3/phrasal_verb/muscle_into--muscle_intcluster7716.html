to muscle into

verb
force one's way in; intrude aggressively
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌsəl ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl̩ ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to force one's way into an organization, situation, or place, often using strength, influence, or aggressive tactics

Examples

to muscle into a market
A new company is attempting to muscle into the industry, causing concern among existing players.
He managed to muscle into the conversation despite not being invited to join.
Several competitors tried to muscle into the rapidly growing market.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barge in: to enter or interrupt rudely or forcefully
  • synonym – intrude: to enter or become involved without invitation or permission
