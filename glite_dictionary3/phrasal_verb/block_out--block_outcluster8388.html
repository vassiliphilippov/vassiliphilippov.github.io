to block out

verb
prevent light or noise from entering
Pronunciation: / blɑk aʊt / (AmE), / blɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to stop something such as light, noise, or an image from coming in or being noticed

Examples

to block out memories
He tried to block out the memory of the accident.
Heavy curtains help block out sunlight in the early morning.
She wore earplugs to block out the city noise while sleeping.
