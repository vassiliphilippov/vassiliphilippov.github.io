to suck up

verb
try to gain favor by flattering
Pronunciation: / sʌk ʌp / (AmE), / sʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone by flattering them or acting in a servile or insincere way

Examples

to suck up to boss
Everyone knows she's just trying to suck up to the manager for a promotion.
He spent the whole meeting sucking up to the boss, and it was embarrassing to watch.
You don't have to suck up to be appreciated for your hard work.
He always tries to suck up to his boss whenever a promotion is mentioned.
She blatantly sucked up to her manager in hopes of getting a raise.
Stop sucking up to the teacher and just do your work like everyone else.

Related Terms

  • synonym – butter up: to be extra nice to someone to gain favor (informal)
  • synonym – flatter: to praise in an insincere way, often to gain favor
  • antonym – insult: to speak to or treat with disrespect or scornful abuse
  • synonym – kowtow: to act in an excessively subservient manner
  • antonym – snub: to treat someone with disdain or ignore them deliberately
