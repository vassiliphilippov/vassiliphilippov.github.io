to play it straight

verb
pretend to be heterosexual
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ɪt streɪt / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪt streɪt / (BrE)
to present oneself as heterosexual, especially by concealing or downplaying one's actual sexual orientation in social, public, or media situations.

Examples

to play it straight in public
To avoid scandal, the actor decided to play it straight during interviews about his private life.
Under pressure, she played it straight on the talk show and avoided discussing her relationships.
Some contestants choose to play it straight despite their true orientation to protect their careers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – come out: to publicly reveal one's sexual orientation or gender identity
  • synonym – pretend: to behave as if something is true when it is not; to give a false appearance
