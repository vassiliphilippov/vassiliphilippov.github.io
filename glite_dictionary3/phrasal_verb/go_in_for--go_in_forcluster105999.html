to go in for

verb
have a liking or take part in
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɪn fər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɪn fə / (BrE)
to have a liking or preference for something, or to regularly take part in an activity

Examples

to go in for sports
I don't really go in for spicy food, but my brother loves it.
Many students go in for sports and clubs in their free time.
She never went in for fancy clothes when she was younger.

  • synonym – enjoy: to take pleasure in something
