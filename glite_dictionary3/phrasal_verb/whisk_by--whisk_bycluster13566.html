to whisk by

verb
move past quickly and smoothly
Pronunciation: / wɪsk baɪ / (AmE), / wɪsk baɪ / (BrE)
to move past someone or something very quickly and smoothly

Examples

to whisk by the window
Clouds whisked by the moon, covering it in moments.
She barely noticed him as he whisked by on his skateboard.
The express train whisked by the station without stopping.
