to bear the thought

verb
tolerate or accept an idea
Pronunciation: / bɛr ðə θɔt / (AmE), / beə ðə θɔːt / (BrE)
to tolerate or accept the idea or possibility of something, especially something unpleasant or distressing

Examples

bear the thought of failure
He doesn’t want to bear the thought of moving to another city.
I can't bear the thought of losing my childhood home.
She could hardly bear the thought of disappointing her parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accept: to come to terms with something
  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently; to tolerate
