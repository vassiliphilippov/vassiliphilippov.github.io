to walk in on

verb
enter unexpectedly and witness something private
Pronunciation: / wɑk ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / wɔːk ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to enter a room or place suddenly or unexpectedly and see something private or secret, often by accident

Examples

to walk in on a private moment
He walked in on the meeting without realizing it had already started.
I didn't mean to walk in on you while you were changing clothes.
She was horrified to walk in on an argument between her friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym – interrupt: to stop someone from continuing what they are doing
