to take occasion

verb
seize the opportunity to act
Pronunciation: / teɪk əˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / teɪk əˈkeɪʒən / (BrE)
to seize or use the opportunity to do something, often at an appropriate or suitable time

Examples

to take occasion to thank
She always takes occasion to thank her supporters in her speeches.
They took occasion to discuss the project during the lunch break.
You should take occasion to meet the new neighbors while they are here.

Related Terms

  • antonym – miss the chance: to fail to act when given the opportunity
  • synonym – take the opportunity: to make use of a chance to do something
