to sit out

verb
not participate in an activity
Pronunciation: / sɪt aʊt / (AmE), / sɪt aʊt / (BrE)
To not take part in an activity, such as a dance, game, or event, often by choice or necessity.

Examples

sit out a game
After his injury, Mark had to sit out the entire basketball season.
Several team members chose to sit out the protest for personal reasons.
She decided to sit out the last few dances because she was tired.

Related Terms

  • antonym – participate: to take part in an activity or event
