to hunt down

verb
search for and find after effort
Pronunciation: / hʌnt daʊn / (AmE), / hʌnt daʊn / (BrE)
to search for and successfully find someone or something, especially after a thorough or determined effort

Examples

to hunt down lost items
After hours of searching, she finally hunted down the recipe her grandmother used to make.
Detectives worked tirelessly to hunt down the suspect responsible for the theft.
Journalists tried to hunt down more information about the anonymous whistleblower.

Related Terms

  • synonym – track down: to search for and find someone or something after difficulty
