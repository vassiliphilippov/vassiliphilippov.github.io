to bear down

verb
move forcefully toward; attack
Pronunciation: / bɛr daʊn / (AmE), / beə daʊn / (BrE)
to move quickly and forcefully toward someone or something, especially in order to attack or confront

Examples

bear down on the enemy
As the storm bore down on the coast, residents hurried to find shelter.
The cavalry began to bear down on the enemy lines at full speed.
The linebacker bears down on the quarterback, determined to make a tackle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – charge: to rush forward or attack
  • antonym – retreat: to withdraw from enemy forces as a result of their superior power or after a defeat
