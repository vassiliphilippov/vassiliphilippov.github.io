to get misty

verb
become emotional or teary-eyed
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ˈmɪsti / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈmɪsti / (BrE)
to begin to feel emotional or to have tears come to one's eyes, especially due to sentiment or nostalgia

Examples

get misty hearing music
He couldn't help but get misty when he heard his favorite childhood song.
Many people get misty while looking through old photo albums.
She tends to get misty at weddings, especially during the vows.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – break down: to lose control of one's emotions and begin to cry
  • synonym – tear up: to become teary-eyed or start to cry
