to catch off guard

verb
to surprise someone unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / kætʃ ɑf ɡɑrd / (AmE), / kætʃ ɒf ɡɑːd / (BrE)
to surprise someone so that they are unprepared or unable to react immediately

Examples

catch off guard with news
New regulations often catch businesses off guard if they're not paying attention.
She was caught off guard by his unexpected visit.
The sudden announcement caught me off guard and I didn't know what to say.
