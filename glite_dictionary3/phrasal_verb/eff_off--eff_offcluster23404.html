to eff off

verb
tell someone rudely to go away (euphemism)
Pronunciation: / ɛf ˈɑf / (AmE), / ɛf ˈɒf / (BrE)
(informal, euphemistic, rude) Used to tell someone forcefully to go away or stop bothering you, without using the explicit vulgarity.

Examples

to eff off angrily
I was so annoyed at his comments that I told him to eff off.
If they're bothering you, just tell them to eff off and leave you alone.
She didn't want to be rude, but eventually she had to say eff off to the persistent salesman.
