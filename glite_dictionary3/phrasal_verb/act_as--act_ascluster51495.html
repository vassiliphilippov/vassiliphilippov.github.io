to act as

verb
pretend to have qualities or state of mind
Pronunciation: / ækt æz / (AmE), / ækt æz / (BrE)
to pretend to have particular qualities or to be in a particular state of mind

Examples

act as an expert
During the meeting, she acted as the expert even though she was new to the field.
The boy acted as if he were asleep to avoid doing his chores.
When questioned about the mistake, he chose to act as though he knew nothing about it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pretend: to behave as if something is true when it is not
