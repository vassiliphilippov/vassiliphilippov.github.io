to hold over

verb
postpone to a later time
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to postpone or extend something so it happens or continues at a later time

Examples

to hold over a meeting
Due to the storm, the committee decided to hold over the meeting until next week.
The theater will hold over the film's release because the renovations aren't finished.
We agreed to hold over discussion of the new policy to the next session.
Due to the popularity of the play, the theater decided to hold over its run for another week.
Some members of the committee were held over from last year to help with the transition.
The law was held over from colonial times despite changes in government.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bring forward: to change something so it happens earlier
  • synonym – carry over: to transfer or extend to another time or situation
  • synonym – postpone: to delay something until a later time
  • synonym – retain: to continue to have or keep something
