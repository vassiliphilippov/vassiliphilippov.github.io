to go off

verb
stop running or functioning (machine or device)
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
(of a machine, light, or device) to stop running, functioning, or operating; to become disconnected or cease activity.

Examples

go off during work
Her computer went off in the middle of her work, causing her to lose important files.
If the alarm goes off, it means there's a power failure in the system.
The lights suddenly went off during the storm, plunging the house into darkness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – break down: to stop working due to a fault or malfunction (of machines)
  • synonym – shut down: to stop operating, especially in a controlled or deliberate way
