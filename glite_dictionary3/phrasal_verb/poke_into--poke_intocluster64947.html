to poke into

verb
physically or closely investigate (often by prodding)
Pronunciation: / poʊk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pəʊk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to examine physically or investigate something, especially by prodding or probing.

Examples

to poke into private matters
She didn’t want anyone to poke into her personal affairs.
The scientist used a stick to poke into the abandoned burrow.
They warned the children not to poke into unknown holes in the ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inspect: to examine closely or officially
  • synonym – probe: to physically examine or explore with a tool or device
