to creep up

verb
move stealthily and gradually
Pronunciation: / krip ʌp / (AmE), / kriːp ʌp / (BrE)
to move slowly, quietly, and gradually so as to not be noticed; to approach stealthily

Examples

to creep up behind someone
A feeling of unease began to creep up inside her as the night grew darker.
He didn't notice the deadline creeping up until it was almost too late.
The cat managed to creep up on the bird without making a sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sneak up: to approach quietly and secretly, especially so as to surprise someone
