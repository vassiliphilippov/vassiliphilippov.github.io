to bear down

verb
focus attention or effort
Pronunciation: / bɛr daʊn / (AmE), / beə daʊn / (BrE)
to use focused effort, strength, or concentration to achieve something difficult

Examples

to bear down on studies
During the final exam period, students tend to bear down on their studies.
She advised the team to bear down on meeting the project's deadlines.
The investigation will bear down on the causes of the accident.
As the storm worsened, the crew bore down to keep the ship steady.
She knew she had to bear down and focus during the final minutes of the exam.
With two outs and the bases loaded, the pitcher really had to bear down to win the game.
