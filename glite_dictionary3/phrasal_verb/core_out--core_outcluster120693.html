to core out

verb
remove the central part
Pronunciation: / kɔr aʊt / (AmE), / kɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to remove the central or innermost part of something, especially the core of a fruit or vegetable

Examples

to core out an apple
A drill was used to core out a section of the old tree trunk.
He used a small knife to core out the apples before baking the pie.
You need to core out the peppers before you stuff them.
