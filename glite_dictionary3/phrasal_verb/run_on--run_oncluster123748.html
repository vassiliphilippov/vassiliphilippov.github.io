to run on

verb
talk at unnecessary length
Pronunciation: / rʌn ɑn / (AmE), / rʌn ɒn / (BrE)
to talk or narrate at excessive or unnecessary length, often without interruption or relevance

Examples

run on about stories
He ran on about his travels for almost an hour without taking a breath.
If you run on like this in your essay, you'll lose your reader's interest.
She tends to run on whenever she gets nervous during a presentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be concise: to express oneself clearly and with brevity
  • synonym – digress: to stray from the main subject in speech or writing
  • synonym – ramble: to talk or write in a confused or overly lengthy way
