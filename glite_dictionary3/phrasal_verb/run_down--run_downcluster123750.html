to run down

verb
pursue until caught
Pronunciation: / rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to pursue someone or something until they are caught or stopped

Examples

to run down a suspect
After hours of searching, they finally managed to run down the missing dog.
It took the authorities weeks to run down the fraudster hiding overseas.
The police worked tirelessly to run down the escaped prisoner.

Related Terms

  • synonym – catch: to capture or seize, especially after a chase
  • antonym – let go: to release or stop pursuing someone or something
