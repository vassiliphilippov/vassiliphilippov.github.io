to whack up

verb
break or divide into portions
Pronunciation: / wæk ʌp / (AmE), / wæk ʌp / (BrE)
to break or damage something into pieces, or to cut or divide into portions

Examples

whack up the bill
At the end of the night, we decided to whack up the bill evenly.
He suggested we whack up the profits among all team members.
They had to whack up the fallen branches after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – break up: to separate into pieces; to break apart
  • synonym – divide up: to separate something into parts or portions
