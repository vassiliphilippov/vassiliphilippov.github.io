to walk away

verb
leave or depart in a deliberate way
Pronunciation: / wɑk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place, situation, or person, often in a deliberate or detached way

Examples

to walk away from trouble
After the argument, she decided to simply walk away and not respond further.
He walked away before the meeting ended, clearly frustrated.
Sometimes the smartest thing to do is to walk away from a bad situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leave: to go away from a place or person
  • synonym – walk off: to leave a place, often abruptly or without warning
