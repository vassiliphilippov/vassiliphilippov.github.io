to break down

verb
collapse due to stress, illness, or attack
Pronunciation: / breɪk daʊn / (AmE), / breɪk daʊn / (BrE)
(of a person) to lose control emotionally or physically, often by crying or collapsing

Examples

break down in tears
After running the marathon without enough training, she broke down from sheer exhaustion.
He broke down during the meeting, overwhelmed by stress and lack of sleep.
If you push yourself too hard, your body might eventually break down.
After hearing the tragic news, she broke down in tears at the hospital.
During the interview, the actor broke down and shared personal details about his struggle.
He tried to stay strong at the funeral, but eventually broke down.
After hearing the bad news, she began to break down and cry uncontrollably.
If you don't maintain your computer, it may break down unexpectedly.
We had to wait on the roadside when our van broke down in the middle of nowhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collapse: to fall down suddenly, especially as a result of illness or exhaustion
  • antonym – compose oneself: to regain or maintain control over one's emotions
  • synonym – fail: to stop working or performing successfully
  • synonym – lose it: to lose control of one's emotions
  • antonym – recover: to return to a normal state after malfunctioning or being emotionally upset
