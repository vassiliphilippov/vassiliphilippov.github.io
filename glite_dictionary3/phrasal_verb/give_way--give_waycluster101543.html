to give way

verb
move aside or be replaced
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
to move aside, make space, or be replaced by something or someone else

Examples

give way to new ideas
The crowd gave way as the parade approached.
The old building will give way to a modern shopping center next year.
Tradition sometimes has to give way to innovation in fast-changing industries.
As the city expanded, farmland gave way to new housing developments.
The crowd gave way so the ambulance could pass through.
The old bridge finally gave way under the weight of the truck.

  • synonym – yield: to give up or surrender; to allow someone else to go first
