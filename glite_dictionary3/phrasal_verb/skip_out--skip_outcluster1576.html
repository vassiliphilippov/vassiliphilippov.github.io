to skip out

verb
leave suddenly, often to avoid obligations
Pronunciation: / skɪp aʊt / (AmE), / skɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave unexpectedly or without giving notice, especially to avoid a duty or responsibility

Examples

to skip out on work
He decided to skip out of the agreement at the last minute.
If you skip out on your rent, the landlord will be very upset.
Some students tried to skip out before the teacher took attendance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bail: slang term for leaving abruptly, especially without fulfilling obligations
  • synonym – duck out: leave quickly or secretly to avoid something
