to chimp out

verb
lose control or panic suddenly (informal)
Pronunciation: / tʃɪmp aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɪmp aʊt / (BrE)
To panic, lose control, or act irrationally, especially in a sudden or exaggerated manner.

Examples

to chimp out suddenly
She chimped out when she saw the giant spider on her desk.
Try not to chimp out if things get stressful at work today.
You can't just chimp out every time plans change unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm down: to become quiet and controlled after being upset
  • synonym – freak out: to become very anxious, upset, or lose control suddenly
