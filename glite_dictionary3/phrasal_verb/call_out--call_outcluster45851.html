to call out

verb
publicly criticize or accuse
Pronunciation: / kɔl aət / (AmE), / kɔːl aət / (BrE)
to publicly criticize, accuse, or demand accountability from someone, especially in public discourse or on social media.

Examples

call out bad behavior
Activists often call out corporations for greenwashing on social platforms to demand accountability.
She publicly called out her manager at the meeting for making discriminatory remarks.
Journalists are increasingly calling out public figures who spread false information online.

Related Terms

  • synonym – criticize: to express disapproval of someone or something
  • antonym – praise: to express warm approval or admiration (opposite of publicly condemning)
