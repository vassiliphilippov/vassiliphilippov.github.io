to munch on

verb
eat noisily or with enjoyment
Pronunciation: / mʌntʃ ɑn / (AmE), / mʌntʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to eat something with noisy, enthusiastic, or continuous chewing, often enjoying the process

Examples

to munch on chips
During the meeting, she discreetly munched on a granola bar.
I like to munch on carrots when I'm watching TV.
The children munch on apples as they walk home from school.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – devour: to eat something eagerly and in large amounts
  • degree lower – nibble: to eat small bites of something, often slowly
