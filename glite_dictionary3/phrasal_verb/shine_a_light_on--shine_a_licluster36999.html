to shine a light on

verb
make something clearer or better known
Pronunciation: / ʃaɪn ə laɪt ɑn / (AmE), / ʃaɪn ə laɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to clarify, reveal, or bring public attention to an issue or topic

Examples

shine a light on a problem
Several journalists worked to shine a light on the environmental disaster.
The investigation helped shine a light on corruption within the local government.
The new policy hopes to shine a light on the struggles of low-income families.

Related Terms

  • synonym – highlight: to emphasize or draw special attention to something
  • antonym – obscure: to make unclear or difficult to understand
