to bum around

verb
spend time idly or lazily
Pronunciation: / bʌm əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / bʌm əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time doing very little or nothing in particular, often in an idle or lazy way

Examples

to bum around all day
After college, he just bummed around before starting his first job.
He spent the summer bumming around Europe with his friends.
Instead of working, she prefers to bum around the house all weekend.
