to scare up

verb
obtain or gather with effort
Pronunciation: / skɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skeə ʌp / (BrE)
to find, obtain, or gather something, often with some difficulty or effort

Examples

to scare up some help
Can you scare up some volunteers for tomorrow's cleanup?
Despite the short notice, she managed to scare up a few last-minute gifts.
They tried to scare up enough chairs for everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rustle up: to prepare or obtain something quickly, especially food
