to bullrush through

verb
push or rush forcefully through
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ θru / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ θruː / (BrE)
to move quickly and forcefully through a crowd or obstacle, pushing others aside if necessary

Examples

to bullrush through a crowd
The fans had to bullrush through the security gate to catch the beginning of the concert.
We watched as the protesters tried to bullrush through the police barricade.
When the doors finally opened, excited customers bullrushed through to claim the best seats.
