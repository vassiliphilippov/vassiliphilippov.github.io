to feel sus

verb
seem suspicious or untrustworthy (slang)
Pronunciation: / fil sʌs / (AmE), / fiːl sʌs / (BrE)
to give the impression of being suspicious or untrustworthy

Examples

feel sus about actions
His sudden silence during the interview felt sus to everyone in the room.
I thought his explanation felt sus, so I decided to double-check his story.
The deal feels sus because the seller refuses to meet in person.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suspicious: having the belief or impression that someone is involved in wrongdoing
  • antonym – trustworthy: able to be relied on as honest or truthful
