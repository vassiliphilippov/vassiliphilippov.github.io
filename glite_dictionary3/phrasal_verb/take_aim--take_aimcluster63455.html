to take aim

verb
point a weapon or object at a target
Pronunciation: / teɪk eɪm / (AmE), / teɪk eɪm / (BrE)
to point a weapon, camera, or object at a target or goal in preparation for action

Examples

take aim at the target
Before throwing the dart, he paused to take aim at the bullseye.
She took aim with her camera and captured the perfect shot.
The sniper carefully took aim before pulling the trigger.
