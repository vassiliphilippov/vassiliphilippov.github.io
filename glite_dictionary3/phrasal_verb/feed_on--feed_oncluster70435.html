to feed on

verb
be sustained or nourished by
Pronunciation: / fid ɑn / (AmE), / fiːd ɒn / (BrE)
to eat or be nourished by something, either literally (as food) or figuratively (as support, inspiration, etc.)

Examples

to feed on fresh ideas
As a writer, she feeds on the experiences she gathers from her travels.
He fed on the support of his family during difficult times.
Some people feed on the excitement of competition and always strive for more.
Many caterpillars feed on the leaves of specific plants.
News organizations sometimes feed on public fear to attract more viewers.
The wolves in this region feed on deer and other small animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – depend on: to rely on for support or sustenance
  • synonym – prey on: to hunt and eat as food; to take advantage of
  • synonym – subsist on: to maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level, by using a particular food or resource
  • synonym – thrive on: to flourish because of something
