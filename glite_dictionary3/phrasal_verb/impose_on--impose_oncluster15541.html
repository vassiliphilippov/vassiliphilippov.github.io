to impose on

verb
force something on someone
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpoʊz ɑn / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊz ɒn / (BrE)
to force something to be accepted or put in place by someone, often unwillingly

Examples

to impose on your time
I don't want to impose on your time, but could you help me with this project?
She hated to impose on her friends for a place to stay.
The new rules were imposed on all employees without consultation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – force upon: to make someone accept something against their will
