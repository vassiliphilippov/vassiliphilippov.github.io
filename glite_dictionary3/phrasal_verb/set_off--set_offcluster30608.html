to set off

verb
to counterbalance or compensate for
Pronunciation: / sɛt ɔf / (AmE), / sɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to counterbalance or compensate for something; to make up for a disadvantage or loss

Examples

set off a weakness
Her quick thinking set off the lack of experience in her team.
His generosity helped set off some of his previous mistakes.
The beauty of the garden set off the unattractive building behind it.
