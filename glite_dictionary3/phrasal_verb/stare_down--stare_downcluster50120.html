to stare down

verb
overcome by unwavering stare
Pronunciation: / stɛr daʊn / (AmE), / steə daʊn / (BrE)
to overcome or cause someone to yield by looking at them in a steady, unwavering way

Examples

stare down an opponent
No one could stare down the principal during his lectures.
She tried to stare down her fears, refusing to look away from what scared her.
The champion managed to stare down his rival before the match even started.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intimidate: to frighten or overawe, especially in order to make them do what one wants
