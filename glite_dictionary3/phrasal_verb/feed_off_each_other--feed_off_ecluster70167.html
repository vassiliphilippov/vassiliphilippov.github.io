to feed off each other

verb
draw energy or motivation from each other
Pronunciation: / fid ɔf iʧ ˈʌðɚ / (AmE), / fiːd ɒf iːʧ ˈʌðə / (BrE)
(of two or more people, groups, etc.) to gain energy, motivation, or inspiration from the actions or emotions of one another, often intensifying each other's feelings or behavior.

Examples

feed off each other's energy
Creative teams sometimes feed off each other's ideas to develop better solutions.
During heated debates, politicians often feed off each other and become more passionate.
The two comedians feed off each other, making their performances even funnier.
