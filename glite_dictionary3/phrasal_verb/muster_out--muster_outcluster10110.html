to muster out

verb
be officially released from military service
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌstɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈmʌstə aʊt / (BrE)
to officially release someone from military service, usually after the completion of their term of service

Examples

muster out of the army
After serving two tours overseas, he was mustered out of the army last summer.
Many soldiers looked forward to being mustered out once the war ended.
She mustered out in 1945 and returned to civilian life.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discharge: to officially release someone from duty or employment
  • antonym – enlist: to join or enroll in military service
