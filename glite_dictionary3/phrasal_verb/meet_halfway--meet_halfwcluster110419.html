to meet halfway

verb
to compromise or find a middle ground
Pronunciation: / mit ˈhævˌweɪ / (AmE), / miːt ˌhɑːfˈweɪ / (BrE)
to compromise with someone by each giving up part of what they want in order to reach an agreement

Examples

to meet halfway on a plan
If you can meet halfway, I'm sure we'll find a solution that works for both of us.
Negotiations took weeks, but the two sides finally met halfway and signed the contract.
To resolve the disagreement, they agreed to meet halfway on the terms.

  • synonym – compromise: to settle a dispute by mutual concession
