to begin to throb

verb
start to feel rhythmic pain
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɪn tə θrɑb / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə θrɒb / (BrE)
to start experiencing a rhythmic or pulsating pain, especially in part of the body such as the head

Examples

begin to throb with pain
After running for an hour, his feet began to throb painfully.
As the music got louder, the old wound in his leg began to throb again.
She knew her migraine was coming when her temples began to throb.
