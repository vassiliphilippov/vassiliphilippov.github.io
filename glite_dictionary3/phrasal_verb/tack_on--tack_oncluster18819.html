to tack on

verb
add or attach as extra
Pronunciation: / tæk ɑn / (AmE), / tæk ɒn / (BrE)
to add or attach something extra, especially as an afterthought or something not originally planned

Examples

to tack on a comment
She tacked on a brief disclaimer to her statement.
The teacher decided to tack on an extra assignment at the end of the semester.
You can't just tack on fees without telling customers first.
The teacher agreed to tack on ten more minutes for questions.
They decided to tack on extra time at the end of the meeting.
We had to tack on another hour to finish the presentation.

  • synonym – add: to put something together with something else
  • synonym – append: to add something as an attachment or supplement
  • synonym – attach: to fasten, join, or connect something
  • antonym – remove: to take something away
