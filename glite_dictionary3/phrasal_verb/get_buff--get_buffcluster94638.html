to get buff

verb
receive a temporary in-game stat boost
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt bʌf / (AmE), / ɡɛt bʌf / (BrE)
(in gaming) to receive a temporary increase to a character's abilities, attributes, or performance from an in-game effect, item, or spell.

Examples

to get buff from a potion
In the raid, the healer used a potion so the tank could get buff before the boss fight.
After casting the spell, the warrior got buff and dealt much higher damage for thirty seconds.
Players avoid traps while getting buff in the safe room to prepare for tougher encounters.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buff: a temporary in-game effect that increases a character's stats or abilities
  • antonym – debuff: an in-game effect that temporarily reduces a character's stats or abilities
