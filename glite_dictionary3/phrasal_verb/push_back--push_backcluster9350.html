to push back

verb
force back or repel
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ bæk / (AmE), / pʊʃ bak / (BrE)
to force someone or something to move backward, either physically or by resisting or repressing an influence or feeling

Examples

to push back a crowd
Protesters were pushed back by the police as they advanced toward the building.
She tried to push back her growing sense of panic during the interview.
The soldiers managed to push back the enemy forces after a fierce battle.
