to beat about

verb
search anxiously or in a confused manner
Pronunciation: / biːt əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / biːt əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to search anxiously and with some confusion, often in several places; to move around looking for something in a worried or disorganized way

Examples

to beat about in the dark
He beat about in the dark, trying to find the light switch.
The police beat about the whole area in search of the lost child.
We beat about the house looking for the car keys, but couldn't find them anywhere.
