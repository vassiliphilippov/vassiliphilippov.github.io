to age out

verb
become ineligible due to age
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒ aʊt / (AmE), / eɪdʒ aʊt / (BrE)
to reach an age at which one is no longer eligible for a service or program, especially because of age limits

Examples

age out of a program
Athletes often age out of junior competitions by their early twenties.
Many foster children age out of the system when they turn eighteen.
Once you age out of the youth program, you must apply for adult services.
