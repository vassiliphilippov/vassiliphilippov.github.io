to take over

verb
assume control or responsibility
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈoʊvɚ /; / teɪk ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to begin to have control, responsibility, or leadership of something from someone else

Examples

to take over a project
She is expected to take over the project by the end of the month.
The vice president will take over after the current president retires.
When the new manager took over, several changes were implemented immediately.
Can you take over for me while I run to the store?
I'll take over your shift tonight so you can attend the meeting.
When John got sick, Sarah took over his project responsibilities for a week.
After the coach retired, his assistant decided to take over his training methods.
Many young chefs are eager to take over traditional recipes and make them their own.
The artist hoped her daughter would take over her studio one day.

  • antonym – relinquish: to give up control, responsibility, or leadership
