to scrub up

verb
wash thoroughly before medical procedures
Pronunciation: / skrʌb ʌp / (AmE), / skrʌb ʌp / (BrE)
to wash the hands and forearms very thoroughly, especially in preparation for surgery or other medical procedures

Examples

to scrub up before surgery
After gardening, I need to scrub up to get rid of the dirt.
All staff must scrub up before entering the operating theatre.
She quickly scrubbed up before treating the patient.
