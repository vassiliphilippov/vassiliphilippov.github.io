to ask for

verb
act in a way that makes something likely to happen
Pronunciation: / æsk fɔr / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə / (BrE)
to behave in a way that makes something, especially trouble or criticism, likely to happen

Examples

ask for trouble
By ignoring safety warnings, the company was clearly asking for a disaster.
Challenging the boss in front of everyone is really asking for criticism.
If you drive that fast on icy roads, you're asking for trouble.
