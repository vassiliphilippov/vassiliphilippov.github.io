to knock down

verb
strike so as to make fall
Pronunciation: / nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)
to hit or push someone or something so that they fall to the ground

Examples

to knock down a person
A sudden gust of wind could easily knock down the fence.
She was accidentally knocked down by a player during the game.
The boxer managed to knock down his opponent in the third round.
Security quickly knocked down the intruder when he tried to run.
She accidentally knocked down the vase while cleaning the shelf.
The cyclist was knocked down by a car on the busy street.
