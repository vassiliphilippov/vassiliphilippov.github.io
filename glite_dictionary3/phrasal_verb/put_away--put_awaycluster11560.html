to put away

verb
confine or imprison someone
Pronunciation: / pʊt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to send someone to prison or a secure institution; to confine someone or something so they cannot leave

Examples

to put away a criminal
After his third offense, the judge decided to put away the thief for five years.
The most dangerous criminals were put away in a high-security facility.
They finally put away the notorious gangster after years of investigation.
After the trial, he was put away for ten years in a high-security prison.
She put away the documents in the safe so no one could access them.
The jewelry was put away in a locked drawer for safekeeping.

  • antonym – release: to set someone free from confinement
