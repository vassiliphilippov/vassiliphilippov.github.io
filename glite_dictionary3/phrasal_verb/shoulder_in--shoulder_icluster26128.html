to shoulder in

verb
push one's way in forcefully
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊldər ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊldə ɪn / (BrE)
to push one's way into a place, group, or situation using one's shoulders, especially in a forceful or determined manner

Examples

to shoulder in through a crowd
Crowds gathered at the entrance, each person trying to shoulder in ahead of the others.
She shouldered in to get a better view of the performance.
When the elevator doors opened, a man shouldered in without waiting for others to exit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barge in: to enter a place or join a situation rudely or abruptly
  • antonym – ease in: to enter gradually and with little force
