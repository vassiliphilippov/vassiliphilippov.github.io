to choose up teams

verb
divide people into teams by choosing
Pronunciation: / tʃuz ʌp timz / (AmE), / tʃuːz ʌp tiːmz / (BrE)
to divide a group of people into two sides or teams, especially for a game or activity, by taking turns picking members.

Examples

to choose up teams for a game
As always, the captains took turns to choose up teams so everyone got to play.
Before we started the kickball game, we had to choose up teams on the playground.
The students gathered in the gym to choose up teams for basketball.
