to push through

verb
break out or emerge through a surface
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ θru / (AmE), / pʊʃ θruː / (BrE)
(of new growth, such as a tooth or plant) to break through a surface, especially as it emerges for the first time.

Examples

to push through hard ground
A cactus will push through even the hardest, driest ground to reach the sun.
New shoots began to push through the soil in early spring.
The baby's first tooth finally pushed through after weeks of teething.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erupt: to break out or burst forth, especially through a surface (as of a tooth or volcano)
