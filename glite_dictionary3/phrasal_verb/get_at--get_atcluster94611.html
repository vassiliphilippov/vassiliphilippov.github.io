to get at

verb
annoy or disturb repeatedly
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt æt / (BrE)
To annoy or disturb someone, especially through repeated minor irritations.

Examples

get at his nerves
Don't let his teasing get at you; he's only joking.
Little noises like the ticking clock can get at me when I'm trying to concentrate.
The constant criticism from her boss really gets at her.
