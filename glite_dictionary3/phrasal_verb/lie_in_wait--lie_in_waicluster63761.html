to lie in wait

verb
hide and wait to ambush
Pronunciation: / laɪ ɪn weɪt / (AmE), / laɪ ɪn weɪt / (BrE)
to hide and wait in order to make a surprise attack on someone or something

Examples

to lie in wait for prey
For hours, the tiger would lie in wait before finally pouncing on its prey.
Police believe the suspect lay in wait outside the victim's home.
The thief decided to lie in wait for the unsuspecting traveler near the crossroads.
