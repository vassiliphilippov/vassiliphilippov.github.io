to break out

verb
start suddenly (war, fighting, disease)
Pronunciation: / breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to start suddenly, especially something unpleasant or dangerous like fire, war, or disease

Examples

break out a fire
A fire broke out in the factory, causing widespread panic among workers.
If the disease breaks out again, the authorities are prepared to respond quickly.
Riots could break out at any moment if the situation doesn't improve.
A fire can break out in a crowded building with little warning.
Violence broke out after the match ended.
When the news was announced, panic broke out across the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cease: to stop happening
  • synonym – erupt: to start suddenly and violently
  • antonym – subside: to become less intense, violent, or severe
