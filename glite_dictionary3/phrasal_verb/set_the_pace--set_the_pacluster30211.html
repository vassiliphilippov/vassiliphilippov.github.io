to set the pace

verb
establish a speed or standard for others
Pronunciation: / sɛt ðə peɪs / (AmE), / sɛt ðə peɪs / (BrE)
to establish the speed, level, or standard at which something is done, often serving as an example or benchmark for others to follow

Examples

set the pace for others
Innovative companies often set the pace in their industries by launching new products first.
She worked quickly and efficiently, setting the pace for her colleagues in the busy office.
The captain will set the pace for the rest of the team during the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym – follow: to come after or take direction from someone
  • synonym – lead: to be in front or act as a guide
