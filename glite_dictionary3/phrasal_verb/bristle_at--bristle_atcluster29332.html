to bristle at

verb
react with anger or indignation
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪsəl æt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl at /; / ˈbrɪsəl æt / (BrE)
to react suddenly with anger, irritation, or indignation to something said or done

Examples

bristle at a comment
He bristles at any criticism of his leadership style.
Many readers bristled at the tone of the editorial.
She bristled at the suggestion that she was unprepared for the meeting.
It's important not to bristle at honest feedback from your colleagues.
Many people bristle at unfair criticism, even if it's meant to be constructive.
She tends to bristle at any suggestion that she is not doing her job well.

Related Terms

  • antonym – shrug off: to dismiss or ignore something, often negative
  • synonym – take offense: to feel hurt or insulted by something
