to latch on

verb
come to understand something
Pronunciation: / lætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / lætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to understand, realize, or adopt an idea, especially after not immediately grasping it

Examples

to latch on to an idea
After a few hints, she finally latched on to what he was talking about.
He was slow to latch on, but eventually he understood the instructions.
It took me a while to latch on to the new concept, but now it makes sense.
After some hesitation, the team finally latched on to the new marketing strategy.
Children can quickly latch on to new trends at school.
It took a while for the public to latch on to the importance of recycling.

Related Terms

  • synonym – catch on: to understand or comprehend something, especially after some time
  • synonym – get it: to understand or grasp something
  • synonym – take up: to adopt or accept an idea, suggestion, or course of action
