to get the goods on

verb
obtain incriminating information
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ðə ɡʊdz ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ɡʊdz ɒn / (BrE)
to obtain incriminating or revealing information about someone or something, typically with the aim of using it against them

Examples

to get the goods on a rival
If you want to win the case, you need to get the goods on the defendant.
She claimed she could get the goods on anyone in the company if asked.
The journalist worked hard to get the goods on the corrupt official.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dig up dirt (on): to search for and find damaging information about someone
  • antonym – have nothing on: to have no incriminating evidence against someone
