to throw out

verb
remove from a position or office
Pronunciation: / θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or expel someone from a place, position, or institution

Examples

to throw out a guest
After the scandal, the board decided to throw out the CEO from his job.
She was thrown out of the organization after repeatedly violating their rules.
The committee voted to throw out the treasurer due to financial mismanagement.
If you don't pay your rent, the landlord might throw out your belongings.
She was thrown out of the meeting for continuously interrupting the speaker.
The security guards threw out the unruly customer after the argument escalated.
