to trump out

verb
announce loudly or with fanfare
Pronunciation: / trʌmp aʊt / (AmE), / trʌmp aʊt / (BrE)
to proclaim or announce (something), especially in a showy or boastful manner, as if with a fanfare

Examples

to trump out big news
Every achievement was trumped out by the marketing team as a major breakthrough.
The company trumped out its new product with much publicity and excitement.
They trump out their awards every chance they get, seeking attention and praise.
