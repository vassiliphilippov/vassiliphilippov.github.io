to skimp over

verb
deal with superficially or insufficiently
Pronunciation: / skɪmp ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / skɪmp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to deal with something superficially or insufficiently, especially in a hurried or careless way

Examples

to skimp over details
He tends to skimp over details when he's in a hurry.
The report skimped over the most critical issues, leaving many questions unanswered.
You shouldn't skimp over the safety instructions if you want to avoid accidents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gloss over: to treat or describe something as less important or serious than it really is
