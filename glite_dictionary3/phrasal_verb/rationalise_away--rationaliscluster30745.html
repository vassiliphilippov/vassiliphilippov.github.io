to rationalise away

verb
dismiss by logical explanation
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dismiss something, especially something unusual or supernatural, by offering a logical or natural explanation

Examples

to rationalise away strange events
He attempted to rationalise away his failure with elaborate excuses, refusing to accept responsibility.
Many people try to rationalise away their fears of the dark by blaming creaking floors or the wind.
The scientist did her best to rationalise away the unexplained phenomena, insisting there was always a logical cause.

Related Terms

  • synonym – explain away: to attempt to dismiss or minimize something by offering an explanation
