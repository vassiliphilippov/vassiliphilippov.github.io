to charge up

verb
make excited or emotionally stirred
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to cause (someone or something) to become highly excited, agitated, or emotionally roused

Examples

charge up a crowd
An energetic song can really charge up a crowd at a concert.
Her passionate speech charged up the audience and inspired them to take action.
The coach managed to charge up the team just before the final match.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm down: to become or make less agitated or excited
  • synonym – excite: to cause strong feelings of enthusiasm or eagerness
