to lay on the line

verb
risk something important
Pronunciation: / leɪ ɑn ðə laɪn / (AmE), / leɪ ɒn ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to risk losing something important, such as money, job, or reputation

Examples

to lay on the line your job
He was willing to lay on the line his reputation for what he believed in.
She decided to lay on the line everything she owned to support her new business.
They laid on the line their jobs when they reported the financial misconduct.

  • synonym – jeopardize: to put in danger or at risk
  • antonym – protect: to keep safe from harm or risk
  • synonym – risk: to expose to a chance of loss or damage
