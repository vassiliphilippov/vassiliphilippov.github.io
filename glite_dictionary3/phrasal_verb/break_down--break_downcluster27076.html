to break down

verb
cause to collapse or fall down
Pronunciation: / breɪk daʊn / (AmE), / breɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to cause something to fall or collapse, especially by force or pressure

Examples

break down a wall
She asked the engineer to break down the machinery so it could be moved easily.
The firefighters used an axe to break down the door and rescue the people inside.
The old bridge finally broke down after years of neglect and heavy traffic.

  • synonym – demolish: pull or knock down; destroy completely
