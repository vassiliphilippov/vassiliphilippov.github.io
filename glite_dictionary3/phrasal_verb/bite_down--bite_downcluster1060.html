to bite down

verb
press the teeth firmly together
Pronunciation: / baɪt daʊn / (AmE), / baɪt daʊn / (BrE)
To press or close the teeth firmly together, often in response to pain, concentration, or in order to endure something difficult.

Examples

to bite down on a stick
During the dental procedure, she had to bite down on a cotton roll.
He tried to bite down and ignore the sharp pain in his ankle.
Sometimes you just have to bite down and keep moving forward, no matter how hard it gets.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clench: to close tightly, especially with the teeth or fist
  • synonym – grit one's teeth: to press the teeth tightly together and endure something difficult
