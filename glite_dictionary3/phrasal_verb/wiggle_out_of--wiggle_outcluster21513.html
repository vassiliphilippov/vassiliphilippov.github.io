to wiggle out of

verb
avoid a duty or responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪɡəl aʊt əv / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡl aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to succeed in avoiding or escaping a duty, responsibility, or difficult situation, often by using excuses or clever arguments

Examples

to wiggle out of a promise
He managed to wiggle out of attending the meeting by claiming he was sick.
She always tries to wiggle out of taking the blame when something goes wrong.
The politician attempted to wiggle out of answering the tough questions during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – evade: to avoid doing something, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • antonym – fulfill: to do or carry out as required, counter to avoiding
