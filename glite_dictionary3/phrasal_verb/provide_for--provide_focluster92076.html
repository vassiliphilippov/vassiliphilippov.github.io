to provide for

verb
make possible or arrange for
Pronunciation: / prəˈvaɪd fər / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd fə / (BrE)
to make arrangements or give the means so that something is possible or will happen; to allow or plan for something to occur

Examples

provide for emergency funding
The law provides for emergency funding in case of natural disasters.
The new contract provides for annual salary increases based on performance.
The schedule provides for several breaks throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allow for: to make allowances or plans for something
  • synonym – leave room for: to permit the possibility or opportunity for something
