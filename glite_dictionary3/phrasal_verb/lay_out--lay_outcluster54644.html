to lay out

verb
explain or present a detailed plan
Pronunciation: / leɪ aʊt / (AmE), / leɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to present or explain something clearly and in detail

Examples

to lay out a plan
He will lay out all the steps required to complete the project.
In her report, she lays out the company's new strategy in detail.
The architect laid out the changes on the blueprints for us to review.
He carefully laid out his argument, making it easy for everyone to follow.
In her speech, she will lay out the company's vision for the next decade.
The instructions lay out every step needed to complete the process.

Related Terms

  • synonym – explain: to make something clear or easy to understand
  • synonym – outline: to give the main ideas or facts about something, without all the details
