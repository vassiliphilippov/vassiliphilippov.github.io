to settle down

verb
come to rest on a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛtl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl daʊn / (BrE)
(of a substance) to rest, come to rest, or accumulate in a position, typically on a surface or the ground.

Examples

settle down on the ground
After the storm, a fine layer of dust settled down on the parked cars.
The snow settled down quietly over the streets during the night.
Tiny particles from the construction site eventually settled down on every surface in the house.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sink: to move downward, especially slowly or by gravity
