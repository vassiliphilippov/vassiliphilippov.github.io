to buckle to

verb
to yield to pressure or criticism
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkəl tu / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl tuː / (BrE)
to yield or give in to pressure or criticism

Examples

to buckle to pressure
Despite mounting criticism, she refused to buckle to public pressure.
He finally buckled to his parents' wishes after months of arguing.
The company will not buckle to threats from competitors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – resist: to withstand the force of something; to not give in
