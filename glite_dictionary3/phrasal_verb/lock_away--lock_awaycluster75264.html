to lock away

verb
put out of reach or prevent escape
Pronunciation: / lɑk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / lɒk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a place where they cannot leave or be accessed, usually to keep them safe or prevent escape

Examples

to lock away a prisoner
After his conviction, he was locked away in prison for ten years.
He tends to lock away his feelings instead of sharing them with others.
She decided to lock away her valuables in a safe before leaving town.
