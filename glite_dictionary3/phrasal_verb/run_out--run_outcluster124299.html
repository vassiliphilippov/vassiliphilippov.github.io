to run out

verb
leave suddenly and in a hurry
Pronunciation: / rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and as if in a hurry, often to avoid something or someone

Examples

to run out the door
As soon as she saw her ex enter the room, she ran out without saying goodbye.
He ran out before anyone could ask him a question.
The students ran out of the classroom when the fire alarm went off.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bolt: to leave suddenly and quickly, especially to escape or avoid something
  • synonym – flee: to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary
