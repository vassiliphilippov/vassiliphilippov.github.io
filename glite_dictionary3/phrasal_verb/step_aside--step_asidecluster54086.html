to step aside

verb
move out of the way; withdraw from position
Pronunciation: / stɛp əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / stɛp əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to move out of the way, or to withdraw from a position of responsibility to allow someone else to take over

Examples

to step aside for someone
During the ceremony, everyone was instructed to step aside so the procession could proceed.
He decided to step aside and allow a younger manager to take charge of the team.
When the police asked, she quickly stepped aside to let the ambulance pass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resign: to give up a job or position formally
  • synonym – stand aside: to move out of someone's way; yield position
