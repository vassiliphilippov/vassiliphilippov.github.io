to scratch out

verb
remove or cancel by marking or rubbing
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ aʊt / (AmE), / skrætʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or cancel something, especially by drawing lines through it or rubbing it so that it can no longer be seen

Examples

scratch out a mistake
He scratched out the old price and wrote a new one on the sticker.
Please scratch out any incorrect answers before handing in your test.
She quickly scratched out his phone number from her address book.

  • synonym – cross out: to draw a line through text to indicate removal or cancellation
