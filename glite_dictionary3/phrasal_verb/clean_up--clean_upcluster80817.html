to clean up

verb
finish dealing with or straightening up
Pronunciation: / klin ˈʌp / (AmE), / kliːn ˈʌp / (BrE)
to finish dealing with or attending to something, often by putting things in order or clearing away what is no longer needed

Examples

to clean up paperwork
It took hours to clean up all the paperwork at the end of the fiscal year.
Once the meeting was over, we stayed to clean up the conference room.
She offered to clean up after the party was finished.
