to chock up

verb
to pack or crowd to capacity
Pronunciation: / tʃɑk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to fill or pack something so full that no more can be added; to crowd to capacity

Examples

chock up a room
The stadium was chocked up with fans for the championship game.
Vendors chock up their shelves with goods before the holiday season.
We managed to chock up the entire trunk with luggage for our trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jam-pack: to fill completely; to crowd tightly
