to kick in

verb
begin to take effect
Pronunciation: / kɪk ɪn / (AmE), / kɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to start to have an effect or begin working, often after a delay

Examples

pain relief kicks in
After a few minutes, the pain medication finally kicked in and she began to feel better.
When the effects of caffeine kick in, he usually feels more alert and focused.
You might not notice a difference at first, but the new policy will kick in next month.
It took a moment for her confidence to kick in before she began her speech.
The painkillers usually kick in about twenty minutes after you take them.
When the alarm system kicked in, the lights started flashing and a siren sounded.

Related Terms

  • synonym – set in: to begin and seem likely to continue (e.g. a period or feeling)
  • synonym – take effect: to start to produce results
  • antonym – wear off: to diminish gradually so as to have less effect
