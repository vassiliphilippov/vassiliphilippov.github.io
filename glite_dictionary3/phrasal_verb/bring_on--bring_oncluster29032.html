to bring on

verb
cause to appear or happen
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ ɑn / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɒn / (BrE)
to cause something to happen or appear, especially an event or condition (often something unpleasant)

Examples

to bring on a headache
Allergies can bring on a headache if you're not careful.
It's time to bring on the next performer for the show.
They will bring on dessert after everyone finishes their main course.
Exposure to cold weather can bring on a sore throat or even a cold.
Her comments in the meeting brought on a heated debate among the staff.
The new management policies brought on several unexpected problems for the team.
