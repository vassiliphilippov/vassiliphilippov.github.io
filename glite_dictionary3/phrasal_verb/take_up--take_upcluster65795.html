to take up

verb
occupy or assume a position
Pronunciation: / teɪk ʌp / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to occupy or assume (a position, place, or posture)

Examples

take up a position
She decided to take up her usual spot by the window during the meeting.
The guard will take up his post at the entrance at dawn.
When the music began, the dancers took up their positions on the stage.

Related Terms
