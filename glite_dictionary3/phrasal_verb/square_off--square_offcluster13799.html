to square off

verb
settle an argument or dispute
Pronunciation: / skwɛr ɔf / (AmE), / skwɛə ɒf / (BrE)
to settle an argument or dispute conclusively; to come to an agreement or resolution

Examples

to square off in a debate
After hours of heated discussion, the two parties decided to square off and finally reached a compromise.
It took years for the families to square off their differences once and for all.
The union and the company are expected to square off their contract negotiations by next week.

  • synonym – settle: to resolve or come to an agreement about something
