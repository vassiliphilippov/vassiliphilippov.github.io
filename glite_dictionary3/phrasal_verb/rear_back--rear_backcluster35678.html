to rear back

verb
(of animals) rise on hind legs
Pronunciation: / rɪr ˈbæk / (AmE), / rɪə ˈbæk / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a horse) to suddenly raise the front part of the body off the ground, standing on the hind legs.

Examples

horse rears back
The horse reared back suddenly, startled by the noise in the stable.
The rider lost control as the stallion reared back on the trail.
When threatened, a snake may rear back before striking.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rear: (of a horse or animal) to rise up on hind legs
