to die down

verb
gradually weaken or subside
Pronunciation: / daɪ daʊn / (AmE), / daɪ daʊn / (BrE)
to become gradually less strong, loud, or intense; to decrease in force or activity.

Examples

die down after noise
After the storm passed, the winds finally died down, allowing the crew to leave the shelter.
Her excitement didn't die down even hours after hearing the news.
The noise from the crowd slowly died down as the speaker took the stage.
