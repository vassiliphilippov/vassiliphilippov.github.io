to spread out

verb
move apart from each other
Pronunciation: / sprɛd aʊt / (AmE), / sprɛd aʊt / (BrE)
(of people or things) to move apart from each other over a wider area; to disperse.

Examples

to spread out in a field
As soon as the students entered the park, they spread out in all directions.
Make sure everyone spreads out so you don't block the entrance.
The search team spread out across the field to look for clues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disperse: to move or be spread out over a wide area; to scatter
  • antonym – gather: to come together in a group; to assemble
