to take measures against

verb
act to prevent or combat something
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈmɛʒɚz əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / teɪk ˈmɛʒəz əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to do something in order to prevent or deal with a problem or undesirable situation

Examples

take measures against a threat
After the data breach, the company decided to take measures against future cyberattacks.
Citizens urged the council to take measures against increasing air pollution in the city.
The government must take measures against the effects of climate change before it’s too late.

Related Terms

  • synonym – take action against: to act to oppose or stop something
  • degree lower – take precautions against: to act in advance to avoid danger or harm
