to run down

verb
flow downward along a surface
Pronunciation: / rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to move or flow downward, especially along a surface.

Examples

run down the wall
After the heavy rain, muddy water ran down the hillside into the valley below.
Condensation runs down the window on cold mornings.
Sweat ran down his face as he finished the marathon.
