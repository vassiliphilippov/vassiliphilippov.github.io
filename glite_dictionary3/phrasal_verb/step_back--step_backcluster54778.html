to step back

verb
pause to reconsider a situation
Pronunciation: / stɛp ˈbæk / (AmE), / stɛp ˈbak / (BrE)
to pause in order to consider a situation more carefully or from a different perspective

Examples

to step back and reflect
Before making the final decision, it's wise to step back and consider all possible outcomes.
She decided to step back from the project to gain new insights.
Sometimes, when emotions run high, we need to step back and look at the bigger picture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reflect: to think carefully about something
  • antonym – rush in: to act hastily without consideration
