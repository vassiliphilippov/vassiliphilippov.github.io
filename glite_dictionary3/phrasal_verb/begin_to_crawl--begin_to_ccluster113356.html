to begin to crawl

verb
to start moving very slowly
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɪn tə krɔl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə krɔːl / (BrE)
to start moving or making progress very slowly, especially after being faster or more active before

Examples

begin to crawl after excitement
After a lively start, the meeting began to crawl as discussions dragged on.
As the rain intensified, the traffic began to crawl on the highway.
The economy began to crawl following the sudden financial crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – creep: to move slowly and carefully, especially in order to avoid being heard or noticed
