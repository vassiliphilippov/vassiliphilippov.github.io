to dig in

verb
prepare a defensive position (military)
Pronunciation: / dɪɡ ɪn / (AmE), / dɪɡ ɪn / (BrE)
(of soldiers or other groups) to prepare a defensive position by occupying a trench or fortified area, often for protection during combat or threat.

Examples

dig in for protection
Knowing an attack was coming, the unit prepared to dig in for the night.
The army dug in along the riverbank, reinforcing their defensive line.
The soldiers dug in on the hillside to wait for reinforcements.

  • synonym – entrench: to establish a defensive military position in a trench
