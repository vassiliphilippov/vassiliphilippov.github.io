to quail at

verb
anticipate with fear or anxiety
Pronunciation: / ˈkweɪl æt / (AmE), / ˈkweɪl æt / (BrE)
to feel or show fear or anxiety in anticipation of something; to shrink with dread at the thought of something

Examples

to quail at danger
He did not quail at the prospect of speaking before a large audience.
Many residents quailed at the idea of another harsh winter.
She quailed at the thought of facing her demanding supervisor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shrink from: to avoid doing something because of fear or dislike
