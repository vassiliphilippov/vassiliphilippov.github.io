to loom large

verb
be prominent or significant
Pronunciation: / lum lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / luːm lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
To appear especially important or threatening, or to have a great influence on someone’s thoughts or emotions.

Examples

loom large in memory
As the election approached, the question of leadership loomed large in voters' minds.
Concerns about the environment loom large in public debates these days.
The fear of failure looms large for many aspiring entrepreneurs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dominate: to have a commanding influence over; overshadow
  • antonym – fade: to become less distinct or significant
