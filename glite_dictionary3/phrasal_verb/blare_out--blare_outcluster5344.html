to blare out

verb
announce or broadcast loudly
Pronunciation: / blɛr aʊt / (AmE), / blɛə aʊt / (BrE)
to announce or broadcast something very loudly or forcefully, often to attract attention

Examples

blare out loud music
As we walked past the shop, music blared out from the open doors.
Headlines blared out the news of the Prime Minister's resignation.
The loudspeaker blared out the emergency announcement across the station platform.

Related Terms

  • synonym – broadcast: to transmit information or programmes by radio or television
  • antonym – whisper: to speak very softly or quietly
