to glass over

verb
become dull or glassy (e.g., of eyes)
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of eyes or expressions) to become dull, blank, or without expression, often appearing glassy.

Examples

eyes glass over
As the medication took effect, his gaze started to glass over.
He noticed her expression glass over as the conversation grew dull.
Her eyes would sometimes glass over when she listened to the long lecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glaze over: (of the eyes or face) to become dull and lose expression
