to head up

verb
lead or be in charge
Pronunciation: / hɛd ʌp / (AmE), / hɛd ʌp / (BrE)
to lead or be in charge of a group, organization, or activity

Examples

to head up a team
After years of hard work, Sara now heads up her own company.
He will head up the panel investigating environmental issues.
She was chosen to head up the marketing team for the new project.

Related Terms

  • antonym – follow: to go after or come behind someone; the opposite of lead
  • synonym – lead: to guide or be in charge of a group or organization
