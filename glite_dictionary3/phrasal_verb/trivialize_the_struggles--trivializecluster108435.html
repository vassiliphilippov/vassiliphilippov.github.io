to trivialize the struggles

verb
make struggles seem unimportant
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪviəˌlaɪz ðə ˈstrʌɡəlz / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪəlaɪz ðə ˈstrʌɡlz / (BrE)
to make someone's hardships or challenges seem less important or significant than they actually are

Examples

to trivialize the struggles of others
It's unfair to trivialize the struggles of people living with chronic illness.
Politicians sometimes trivialize the struggles of marginalized communities in their speeches.
We shouldn't trivialize the struggles that students face while adapting to new environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – belittle: to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • synonym – downplay: to make something appear less important than it really is
  • antonym – exaggerate: to represent something as being larger or greater than it really is
