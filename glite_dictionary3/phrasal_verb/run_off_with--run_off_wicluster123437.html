to run off with

verb
escape with stolen or taken things
Pronunciation: / rʌn ɔf wɪð / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf wɪð / (BrE)
to escape with something that has been stolen, won, or taken, often quickly and secretly.

Examples

run off with the money
After winning the jackpot, he ran off with the prize money.
Someone ran off with my bike from outside the store.
The thief ran off with her handbag while she was distracted.

Related Terms

  • synonym – make off with: to steal something and leave quickly
