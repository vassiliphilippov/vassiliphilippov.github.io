to break away

verb
leave an organization or group
Pronunciation: / breɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / breɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave or withdraw from an organization, group, or association, especially to form a separate group

Examples

to break away from a group
After months of tension, the region finally broke away and declared its independence.
Several members decided to break away and form their own political party.
The club threatened to break away from the association over disagreements with leadership.

Related Terms

  • antonym – join: to become a member or part of a group or organization
  • synonym – secede: to formally withdraw from an alliance, federation, or association
