to swallow up

verb
enclose or envelop completely
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑːloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
To enclose or envelop completely, often so that something disappears or is no longer visible.

Examples

swallow up all the money
Rising rents have swallowed up most of their monthly income.
The dark clouds swallowed up the sun, casting the town into shadow.
Within minutes, the dense fog swallowed up the entire coastline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – consume: to completely destroy or use up
  • synonym – engulf: to sweep over and cover completely
  • antonym – release: to set free or let go (opposite of enclosing or enveloping)
