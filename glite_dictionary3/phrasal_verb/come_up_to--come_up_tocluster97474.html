to come up to

verb
meet a standard or expectation
Pronunciation: / k\u028cm \u028cp tu / (AmE), / k\u028cm \u028cp t\u028a / (BrE)
to reach, meet, or be equal to a required standard, expectation, or level; to satisfy requirements.

Examples

come up to expectations
The new product didn't come up to customer expectations and received poor reviews.
Her final project finally came up to the professor's standards after several revisions.
If the next build doesn't come up to security requirements, we cannot deploy it.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fall short: to fail to reach a required standard
  • synonym – live up to: to fulfil expectations or standards
  • synonym – measure up: to be as good as expected or required
