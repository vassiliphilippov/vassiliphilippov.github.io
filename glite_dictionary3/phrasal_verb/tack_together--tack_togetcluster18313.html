to tack together

verb
join parts together quickly or loosely
Pronunciation: / tæk təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / tæk təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to join or assemble parts or elements loosely or hastily, often in a temporary or makeshift way

Examples

to tack together a quick plan
She managed to tack together a last-minute costume for the school play.
The report was tacked together from several unrelated sources and lacked coherence.
They quickly tacked together a shelter using some old boards and a tarp.
