to keep back

verb
to reserve or retain for later use
Pronunciation: / kip ˈbæk / (AmE), / kiːp ˈbak / (BrE)
to reserve or retain something for future use or application, not using or giving all of it at once.

Examples

keep back some money
Each year, the company keeps back some profits as an emergency fund.
Please keep back enough cake for the guests who arrive late.
They kept back several tickets for VIPs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – reserve: to keep something for a particular use or future time
  • synonym – set aside: to keep something separate for a special purpose
