to stand up

verb
remain firm against criticism or attack
Pronunciation: / stænd ʌp / (AmE), / stænd ʌp / (BrE)
to refuse to back down or remain firm when facing criticism or attack

Examples

stand up to a bully
He refused to back down and decided to stand up to the unfair accusations.
It's important to stand up when you know you're right, no matter what others say.
She learned to stand up for herself despite heavy criticism from her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give in: to finally agree to something after initially resisting; to yield
