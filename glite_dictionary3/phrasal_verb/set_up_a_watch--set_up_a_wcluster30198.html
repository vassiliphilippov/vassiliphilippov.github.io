to set up a watch

verb
establish surveillance or observation
Pronunciation: / sɛt ʌp ə wɑtʃ / (AmE), / sɛt ʌp ə wɒtʃ / (BrE)
to establish surveillance or observation, often to monitor for suspicious activity or potential threat

Examples

to set up a watch for danger
After receiving threats, the company set up a watch to protect its employees.
During the festival, volunteers set up a watch to monitor crowd safety.
The guards set up a watch outside the building during the night.
