to jump on

verb
respond or act quickly to take advantage
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp ɑn / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn / (BrE)
to take advantage of or respond to something very quickly

Examples

to jump on an opportunity
Investors were quick to jump on the company’s new stock offering.
Mark didn't jump on the job offer, preferring to think about it first.
She tends to jump on every new trend that appears online.

Related Terms

  • antonym – miss: to fail to take advantage of an opportunity
  • synonym – pounce on: to immediately take advantage of something
  • synonym – seize on: to take quick advantage of an opportunity or remark
