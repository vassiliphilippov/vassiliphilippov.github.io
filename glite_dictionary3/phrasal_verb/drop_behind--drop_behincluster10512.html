to drop behind

verb
fall behind; lag behind others
Pronunciation: / drɑp bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / drɒp bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to move more slowly than others and fall to a position behind them; to fail to keep up

Examples

to drop behind the group
During the marathon, several runners started to drop behind as the pace increased.
If you drop behind on your assignments, it will be hard to catch up later.
It's important not to drop behind in your studies if you want to graduate on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catch up: to reach the same level as others
  • synonym – fall behind: to fail to keep up with others
