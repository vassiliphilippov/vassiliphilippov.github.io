to skip the detail

verb
omit unnecessary information
Pronunciation: / skɪp ðə dɪˈteɪl / (AmE), / skɪp ðə dɪˈteɪl / (BrE)
to omit unnecessary or less important information when explaining or describing something.

Examples

to skip the detail in a story
He tends to skip the detail in his reports, focusing only on the big picture.
If you're in a hurry, you can skip the detail and just give me the main points.
When explaining the process, I'll skip the detail and keep it simple for everyone.
