to pull out

verb
remove with force or effort
Pronunciation: / pʊl aʊt / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or take something out of a place, container, or position, often by pulling (can be physical or abstract, like information)

Examples

to pull out a tooth
It's amazing what secrets journalists can pull out of public records.
She had to pull out a stubborn nail from the wall.
The dentist will pull out your wisdom tooth if it's causing pain.
He quickly pulled out his phone to check the time.
She pulled out a notebook from her backpack and started writing.
The magician pulled out a rabbit from his hat, astonishing the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – extract: to remove or take out, often with effort
  • antonym – insert: to put something into another thing
