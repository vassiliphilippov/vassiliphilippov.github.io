to run past

verb
move quickly by; let pass without action
Pronunciation: / rʌn pæst / (AmE), / rʌn pɑːst / (BrE)
to move quickly past someone or something, or to allow an opportunity or moment to pass without taking action

Examples

to run past a person
A cyclist ran past us on the narrow sidewalk.
Don't let this chance run past without trying your best.
I watched the children run past my window on their way to school.

Related Terms

  • antonym – grab: to take quickly, often used for opportunities (opposite sense)
  • synonym – miss: to fail to take advantage of or notice something
  • synonym – pass by: to go past without stopping or taking action
