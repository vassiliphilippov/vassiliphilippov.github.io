to put away

verb
dismiss or set aside (a feeling or thought)
Pronunciation: / pʊt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to stop thinking about or dealing with (something, such as a feeling, thought, or worry); to dismiss or set aside

Examples

to put away worries
Before starting the meeting, she asked everyone to put away their personal concerns.
He struggled to put away the memory of the accident.
It's time to put away your doubts and move forward with confidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – banish: to drive something away, especially thoughts or feelings
  • synonym – dismiss: to deliberately stop thinking about something
