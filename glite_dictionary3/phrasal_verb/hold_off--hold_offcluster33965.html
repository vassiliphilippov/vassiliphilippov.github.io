to hold off

verb
resist and prevent an opponent's success
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ɔf / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf / (BrE)
to resist and prevent an opponent from succeeding or advancing, especially by maintaining one's position until the end

Examples

to hold off attackers
Despite heavy pressure, the team held off their opponents and won the match.
She tried to hold off her rivals in the final lap of the race.
The defense managed to hold off the attackers until reinforcements arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give in: to stop resisting and yield to someone
  • synonym – hold back: to restrain or prevent progress
