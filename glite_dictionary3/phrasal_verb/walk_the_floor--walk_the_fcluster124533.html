to walk the floor

verb
to supervise by moving around an area
Pronunciation: / wɔk ðə flɔr / (AmE), / wɔːk ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to move through an area, especially in a workplace or public space, to supervise, check, or interact with people

Examples

to walk the floor checking staff
During the conference, he would walk the floor to engage with attendees and answer questions.
Each morning, the supervisor makes it a point to walk the floor and greet her team.
Retail managers often walk the floor to monitor customer satisfaction and staff performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circulate: to move around within a group or area
