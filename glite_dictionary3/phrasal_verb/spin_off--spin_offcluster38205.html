to spin off

verb
create as a byproduct or consequence
Pronunciation: / spɪn ɔf / (AmE), / spɪn ɒf / (BrE)
to create something new as a byproduct or consequence of a larger process or entity, often forming a separate business or product.

Examples

to spin off a company
Several new products were spun off from the research project.
The popular television series was so successful that it spun off two movies.
The tech company plans to spin off its mobile division into a separate entity next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – create: to bring something into existence
  • antonym – merge: to combine two or more things, opposite of creating a separate entity
  • synonym – result: to occur as a consequence
