to light up

verb
to become happy; to illuminate; to scold (informal)
Pronunciation: / laɪt ˈʌp /; / laɪt ʌp / (AmE), / laɪt ˈʌp /; / laɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to become suddenly happy or excited, or to make a place lively and cheerful

Examples

light up her face
Her eyes lit up when she saw the birthday cake.
If you keep talking back, the coach will light up at you during practice.
The city skyline lights up at night for the festival.
His jokes made the room light up and everyone started to relax.
Whenever she enters, the entire room lights up with laughter and joy.
You could feel the room light up the moment the band started playing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brighten up: to become brighter or more cheerful
  • antonym – darken: to make or become less bright
  • synonym – illuminate: to light up or make brighter
  • synonym – scold: to reprimand somebody angrily
