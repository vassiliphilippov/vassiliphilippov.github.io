to refinance a mortgage

verb
obtain a new home loan to improve terms
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfaɪnæns ə ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfaɪnæns ə ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
to replace an existing home loan with a new one, typically to secure better terms such as a lower interest rate or reduced monthly payments

Examples

to refinance a mortgage for lower payments
After interest rates dropped, they decided to refinance a mortgage to lower their monthly bills.
Many homeowners choose to refinance a mortgage to shorten their loan term and pay less interest.
She was able to refinance a mortgage with a much better rate after improving her credit score.
