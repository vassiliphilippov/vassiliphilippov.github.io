to kick around

verb
discuss informally or casually
Pronunciation: / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to discuss an idea, suggestion, or subject informally or casually, often without reaching a decision

Examples

to kick around ideas
Let's kick around some possible solutions during the meeting.
The team will kick around a few ideas before making a final decision.
We kicked around the proposal over coffee yesterday afternoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bandy about: to discuss or mention something frequently, often carelessly
