to keep up

verb
maintain or preserve in good condition
Pronunciation: / kip ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp / (BrE)
to maintain something in good condition or protect it from harm, decay, or loss

Examples

to keep up a building
It's important to keep up historic monuments for future generations.
The landlord struggled to keep up the old house during the winter.
Without regular repairs, it became impossible to keep up the building.
