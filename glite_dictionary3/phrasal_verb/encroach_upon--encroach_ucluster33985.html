to encroach upon

verb
gradually intrude or violate (space, rights, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkroʊtʃ əˌpɒn / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃ əˌpɒn / (BrE)
to gradually intrude on or violate someone's space, rights, or privacy

Examples

encroach upon private land
Many fear the new development will encroach upon the wildlife habitat.
Some believe that these laws encroach upon individual freedoms.
The reporter's persistent questions began to encroach upon her personal life.
