to tattle on

verb
report someone's wrongdoing to an authority
Pronunciation: / ˈtæt̬l ɑn / (AmE), / ˈtætl ɒn / (BrE)
to report someone’s misbehavior or wrongdoing to a person in authority, often in a way that is seen as disloyal or petty.

Examples

to tattle on a classmate
Children sometimes tattle on each other over the smallest things.
If you tattle on your classmate, the teacher might give them detention.
She promised not to tattle on her younger brother for stealing cookies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – keep quiet: to not report or reveal someone's wrongdoing
  • synonym – snitch: to secretly tell someone in authority about someone else's wrong or criminal activity
  • synonym – tell on: to inform someone in authority about someone's wrongdoing
