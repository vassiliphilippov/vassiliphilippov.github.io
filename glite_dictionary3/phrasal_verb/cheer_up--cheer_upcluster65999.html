to cheer up

verb
to become or make happier
Pronunciation: / tʃɪr ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɪə ʌp / (BrE)
to become happier or to make someone feel happier, especially after feeling sad or disappointed

Examples

to cheer up a friend
A funny movie always helps me cheer up when I'm feeling sad.
He tried to cheer up after hearing the good news.
They brought her flowers to cheer up her spirits.
After hearing the good news, she began to cheer up quickly.
He brought flowers to cheer up his sick friend.
Let's watch a funny movie tonight to cheer up everyone.
A good laugh always helps me cheer up when I'm feeling down.
She brought him flowers to cheer up after his surgery.
They played her favorite song to cheer up the mood in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brighten up: to become or make more cheerful or lively
  • antonym – calm down: to become or make less excited or agitated
  • synonym – console: to comfort someone who is unhappy
  • antonym – depress: to make someone feel sad or hopeless
  • synonym – encourage: to give someone support and confidence
  • synonym – lift (someone's) spirits: to make someone feel happier
