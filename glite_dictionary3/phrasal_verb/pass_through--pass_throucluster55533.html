to pass through

verb
move across enemy lines (military)
Pronunciation: / pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
(of troops or individuals) to move across or penetrate (an enemy line or area), typically during a military engagement.

Examples

to pass through enemy lines
During the night, our battalion attempted to pass through the enemy line undetected.
Several units tried to pass through, but only a few were successful in reaching the other side.
The soldiers managed to pass through enemy territory under the cover of darkness.

  • antonym – retreat: withdraw from enemy forces or lines
