to close out

verb
prevent or make impossible beforehand
Pronunciation: / kloʊs aʊt / (AmE), / kləʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to make something impossible, especially by taking anticipatory action; to prevent something from happening or being considered

Examples

close out options
New evidence may close out the possibility of a retrial.
She tried to close out all doubts before making her decision.
The new contract terms close out any further negotiations on the subject.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preclude: to make impossible, especially beforehand
