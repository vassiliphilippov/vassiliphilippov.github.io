to wash out

verb
destroy/erode by water
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ aʊt / (AmE), / wɒʃ aʊt / (BrE)
(of water) to destroy, remove, or erode something by flowing over it with force.

Examples

wash out a road
Heavy rains can wash out roads and paths in low-lying areas.
That section of the trail was washed out after last winter's floods.
The river gradually washes out the soil at its banks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – erode: to gradually wear away or be worn away by natural forces
