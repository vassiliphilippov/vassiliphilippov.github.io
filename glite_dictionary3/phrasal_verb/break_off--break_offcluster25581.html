to break off

verb
interrupt or stop early
Pronunciation: / breɪk ɔf / (AmE), / breɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to suddenly stop or interrupt an activity, conversation, or relationship before it is finished

Examples

to break off a conversation
He had to break off the conversation to answer a call.
Negotiations were broken off after new issues arose.
She broke off her speech when she heard the alarm.
He broke off in the middle of his sentence when he saw her enter the room.
Negotiators had to break off talks when an agreement could not be reached.
She decided to break off the engagement after much thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cease: to stop doing something; come to an end
  • antonym – continue: to keep going or doing something
  • synonym – cut short: to end something earlier than expected
