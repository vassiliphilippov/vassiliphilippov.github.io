to dress up

verb
make something seem more attractive than it is
Pronunciation: / drɛs ʌp / (AmE), / drɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to make something seem more attractive, impressive, or appealing than it really is, often in a superficial or misleading way

Examples

to dress up the facts
It's not wise to dress up the facts just to please the audience.
She attempted to dress up her résumé with exaggerated achievements.
The company tried to dress up its quarterly report to hide the losses.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bare: to reveal something truthfully, without hiding its negative aspects
  • synonym – embellish: to make something more attractive by adding extra details, often false
  • synonym – sugarcoat: to make something seem more pleasant or acceptable than it really is
