to pooch out

verb
protrude or round one's lips
Pronunciation: / putʃ aʊt / (AmE), / puːtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
(of lips) to round or protrude outward, especially as if about to kiss.

Examples

pooch out lips
She tends to pooch out her lips whenever she's thinking deeply.
The baby pooched out his lips in a cute imitation of a kiss.
Try not to pooch out your mouth so much when blowing bubbles.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pout: to push out the lips in annoyance or to look attractive
  • synonym – purse: to pucker or contract the lips
