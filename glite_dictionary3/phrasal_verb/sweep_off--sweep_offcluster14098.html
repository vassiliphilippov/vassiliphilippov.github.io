to sweep off

verb
overwhelm someone emotionally
Pronunciation: / swip ɑf / (AmE), / swiːp ɒf / (BrE)
(of a person or emotion) to overwhelm someone suddenly and strongly, often in an emotional sense.

Examples

sweep off your feet
He found himself swept off by a wave of nostalgia during the ceremony.
She was completely swept off her feet by his romantic gesture.
The beauty of the music swept off the audience, leaving many in tears.
