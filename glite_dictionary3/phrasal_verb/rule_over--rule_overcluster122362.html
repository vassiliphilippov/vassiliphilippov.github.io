to rule over

verb
control or have authority over
Pronunciation: / ruːl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ruːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to have control or authority over a person, group, or place

Examples

to rule over a country
After the revolution, a new government ruled over the region.
For decades, the dictator ruled over the country with an iron fist.
The queen ruled over a vast empire during her reign.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dominate: to have commanding influence on; exercise control over
  • synonym – govern: to officially control and direct the affairs of a state, city, or organization
  • antonym – submit: to accept or yield to the authority of another; antonym to 'rule over' in control relationship
