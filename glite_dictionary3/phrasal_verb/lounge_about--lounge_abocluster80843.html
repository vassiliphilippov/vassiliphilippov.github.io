to lounge about

verb
be lazy or idle
Pronunciation: / laʊndʒ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / laʊndʒ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to spend time lazily or without doing much; to be idle

Examples

lounge about the house
He spent the weekend lounging about the house, doing absolutely nothing.
Instead of working on his project, he just lounged about all afternoon.
She prefers to lounge about at the pool rather than go sightseeing on vacation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – laze around: to spend time doing very little, being lazy
  • antonym – work: to engage in physical or mental activity to achieve a result
