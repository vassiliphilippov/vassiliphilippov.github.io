to bring in

verb
announce or deliver a legal verdict
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)
(of a jury or judge) to officially announce or deliver a verdict or decision in court.

Examples

bring in a verdict
After reviewing the evidence, the judge brought in a decision in favor of the plaintiff.
It took several days for the panel to bring in their final judgment.
The jury brought in a verdict of not guilty after only three hours of deliberation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deliver: to formally give or announce (especially a judgment or verdict)
  • synonym – return: to pronounce or render (a verdict or decision, especially by a jury)
