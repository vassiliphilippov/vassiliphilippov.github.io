to patch up

verb
reconcile after a disagreement
Pronunciation: / pætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / pætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to resolve, improve, or restore a relationship or problem, especially after a disagreement

Examples

to patch up a friendship
After the heated argument, it took weeks for them to patch up.
They finally decided to patch up their differences and move forward.
We should try to patch up things before the family meeting.
After their heated disagreement, the two colleagues tried to patch up their working relationship.
She quickly patched up her torn jacket before heading out to the meeting.
The mechanic managed to patch up the leak just enough to get us home.
