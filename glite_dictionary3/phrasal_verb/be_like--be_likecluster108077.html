to be like

verb
say or think (informal reporting)
Pronunciation: / bi ˈlaɪk / (AmE), / bi ˈlaɪk / (BrE)
(informal) Used to introduce what someone says or thinks, especially in casual storytelling or dialogue.

Examples

was like 'No way!'
He was like, 'I can't believe you did that!'
She was like, 'No way am I going to that party.'
When I saw the mess, I was like, 'Who did this?'
