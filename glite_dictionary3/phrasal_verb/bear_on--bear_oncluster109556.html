to bear on

verb
be relevant to; affect
Pronunciation: / bɛr ɑn / (AmE), / beə ɒn / (BrE)
to be relevant to, connected with, or have an effect on something

Examples

bear on the issue
His financial situation does not bear on our decision.
The new evidence may bear on the outcome of the investigation.
These regulations bear on every aspect of the industry.
Her research findings bear on the environmental policies being considered.
Several new studies bear on our understanding of climate change.
The evidence does not bear on the central issue of the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym – affect: to have an influence on something
  • synonym – pertain to: to relate to or be about something
  • synonym – refer to: to mention or have relation to something
  • synonym – relate to: to be connected to; be relevant to
