to come down

verb
stop functioning; become unavailable (systems)
Pronunciation: / kəm daʊn / (AmE), / kəm daʊn / (BrE)
(of a computer system, website, service, or network) to stop functioning or become unavailable, often suddenly, because of an error, overload, or outage.

Examples

come down during outage
During the outage, the website can come down for several hours.
Last night the server came down after too many users tried to log in.
If the database keeps coming down, customers will lose trust in the service.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crash: to fail suddenly, especially a computer program or system
  • synonym – go down: to become unavailable or stop working (often used of websites or services)
