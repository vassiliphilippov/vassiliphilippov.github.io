to canvass opinion

verb
to survey people's opinions
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.vəs əˈpɪn.jən / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs əˈpɪn.jən / (BrE)
to ask a group of people for their views or opinions about something, especially to collect a range of responses

Examples

to canvass opinion before a decision
Before writing the report, she canvassed opinion from all departments.
The committee decided to canvass opinion before making any major changes.
We will canvass opinion among staff members on the proposed relocation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – poll: to collect opinions or votes by asking questions
  • synonym – survey: to collect data or opinions from a group
