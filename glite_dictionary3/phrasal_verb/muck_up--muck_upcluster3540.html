to muck up

verb
spoil or ruin something
Pronunciation: / mʌk ʌp / (AmE), / mʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, ruin, or make a mess of something, especially by making mistakes

Examples

to muck up a report
If you muck up this report, we’ll have to start all over again.
She accidentally mucked up the cake recipe and it didn’t rise.
Try not to muck up your room before your friends arrive.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fix: to repair or correct something
  • synonym – mess up: to do something badly or spoil something
  • synonym – screw up: to make a serious mistake or mess something up
