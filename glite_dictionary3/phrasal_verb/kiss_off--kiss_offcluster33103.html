to kiss off

verb
dismiss someone or something contemptuously
Pronunciation: / kɪs ɔf / (AmE), / kɪs ɒf / (BrE)
To dismiss someone or something contemptuously or tell someone to go away, often in a brusque or angry manner.

Examples

kiss off an idea
After hearing his excuses, she decided to kiss off the whole project and start anew.
If you don't like my idea, you can just kiss off and leave us alone.
Tired of the criticism, he told them all to kiss off and walked away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brush off: to ignore or dismiss someone or something lightly
  • synonym – dismiss: to send away or remove from consideration
