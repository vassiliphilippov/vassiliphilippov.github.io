to move in on

verb
make intrusive advances towards
Pronunciation: / muːv ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / muːv ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to make unwelcome or intrusive advances towards someone, often in a romantic or competitive way

Examples

to move in on a person
After I expressed my idea, my colleague began to move in on the project.
He tried to move in on her at the party, but she wasn't interested.
She was annoyed when he attempted to move in on her conversation with the director.

Related Terms

  • antonym – back off: to withdraw or stop making advances
  • synonym – hit on: to make sexual advances towards someone
