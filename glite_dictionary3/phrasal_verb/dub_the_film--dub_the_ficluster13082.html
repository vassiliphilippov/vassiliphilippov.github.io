to dub the film

verb
replace original film dialogue with a foreign language
Pronunciation: / dʌb ðə fɪlm / (AmE), / dʌb ðə fɪlm / (BrE)
to replace the original spoken dialogue of a film with a translated version in another language

Examples

to dub the film into Spanish
Before international release, they had to dub the film into several different languages.
Many European countries prefer to dub the film rather than show subtitles.
The company will dub the film into Spanish for Latin American audiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym – subtitle the film: to provide a film with translated written text on screen instead of replacing dialogue
