to do with

verb
to need or benefit from something
Pronunciation: / du ˈwɪð / (AmE), / dʊ ˈwɪð / (BrE)
to need, benefit from, or find useful; to be helped by having or receiving something

Examples

could do with a break
After a long day, I could do with a nice cup of tea.
He said he could do with some extra help organizing the event.
The garden is looking dry; it could do with some water.
