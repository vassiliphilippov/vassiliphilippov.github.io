to pick at

verb
pluck or pull at with the fingers
Pronunciation: / pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to pluck or pull at something with the fingers, especially repeatedly or nervously

Examples

to pick at a label
He absentmindedly picked at the label on his water bottle while he listened.
She nervously began to pick at the hem of her skirt during the interview.
Stop picking at that scab or it might get infected.
