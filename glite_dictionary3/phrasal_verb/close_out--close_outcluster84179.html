to close out

verb
sell off remaining stock; end an operation
Pronunciation: / kloʊz aʊt / (AmE), / kləʊz aʊt / (BrE)
to bring something to an end, finish, or terminate it (such as a business, project, or account), often by selling off remaining assets

Examples

close out a business
After 30 years, the store will close out its operations at the end of the month.
The company decided to close out its remaining merchandise at huge discounts.
They had to close out the warehouse inventory before relocating to a new facility.
After many years, the team finally closed out the project with a successful launch.
The store will close out its inventory during the final sale next month.
We decided to close out our savings account before moving to a new city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – complete: to make something whole or finished
  • synonym – finish: to bring something to completion
  • synonym – liquidate: to sell assets to settle a debt or end a business
  • synonym – wind up: to bring a business to an end
