to take charge

verb
assume control or responsibility
Pronunciation: / teɪk tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / teɪk tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to assume control or responsibility for something or someone; to take the lead

Examples

to take charge of a project
After months of indecision, he finally chose to take charge and solve the problem himself.
During emergencies, it's important for someone to quickly take charge and organize everyone.
When the manager was absent, Sarah decided to take charge of the meeting.
After the manager resigned, someone had to take charge of the project.
If you don't take charge of your finances, you might run into trouble.
Whenever the team faces a crisis, Julia is always ready to take charge.
