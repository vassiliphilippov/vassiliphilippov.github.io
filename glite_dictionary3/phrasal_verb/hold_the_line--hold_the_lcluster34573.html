to hold the line

verb
keep prices or rates unchanged
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ðə laɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to keep prices, spending, or another variable at the current level without allowing an increase

Examples

hold the line on prices
Despite rising costs, the company promised to hold the line on prices for the coming year.
The government is trying to hold the line on public spending during the economic downturn.
They have held the line on tuition fees for the past five years.

Related Terms

  • antonym – raise prices: to increase the amount charged for goods or services
