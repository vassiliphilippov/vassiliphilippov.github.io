to sweep away

verb
eliminate or remove completely
Pronunciation: / swip əˈweɪ / (AmE), / swiːp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate something completely, so that nothing remains

Examples

sweep away all traces
Heavy rains can sweep away bridges and roads in a matter of hours.
The new government vowed to sweep away corruption and inefficiency.
Years of tradition were suddenly swept away by the reforms.
A sense of relief swept away her anxiety after hearing the good news.
Many traditions were swept away by the rapid modernization of the city.
The heavy rains threatened to sweep away the bridge during the flood.
