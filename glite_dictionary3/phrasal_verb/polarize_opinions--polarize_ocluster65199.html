to polarize opinions

verb
divide people into opposing viewpoints
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊləˌraɪz əˈpɪnjənz / (AmE), / ˈpəʊləˌraɪz əˈpɪnjənz / (BrE)
to cause people to divide into groups with opposing views or opinions, often leading to strong disagreement

Examples

polarize opinions on politics
Controversial topics often polarize opinions and spark heated arguments among colleagues.
Social media has the power to polarize opinions on political issues very quickly.
The proposed law polarized opinions within the community, dividing residents into two strong camps.

Related Terms

  • synonym – divide opinion: to cause different people to have different views about something
  • antonym – unify opinions: to bring people to agreement or the same view
