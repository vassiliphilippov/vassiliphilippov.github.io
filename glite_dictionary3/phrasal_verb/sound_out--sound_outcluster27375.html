to sound out

verb
discreetly learn opinions or intentions
Pronunciation: / saʊnd aʊt / (AmE), / saʊnd aʊt / (BrE)
to try to discover someone's opinions or intentions, often by asking indirect questions

Examples

to sound out a colleague
He spent weeks sounding out voters about their views on the policy.
She decided to sound out her colleagues before proposing any changes.
The manager tried to sound out the new team member’s attitude towards remote work.

Related Terms

  • synonym – probe: to investigate or explore, especially by asking questions
