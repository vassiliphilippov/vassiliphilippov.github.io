to come down

verb
to be reduced to the essential point
Pronunciation: / kʌm ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / kʌm ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to be reduced to the most important point or essential factor

Examples

come down to one thing
His decision came down to what was best for his family.
It all comes down to whether we trust each other.
The argument comes down to a question of priorities.
