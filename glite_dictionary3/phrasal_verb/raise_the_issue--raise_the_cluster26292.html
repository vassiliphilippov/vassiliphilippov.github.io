to raise the issue

verb
bring up a topic for discussion
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪz ði ˈɪʃuː / (AmE), / ˈreɪz ði ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
to bring up a topic, question, or problem for discussion or so it can be addressed (including in meetings, conversations, or issue-tracking systems)

Examples

raise the issue in a meeting
During the interview, she decided to raise the issue of pay equity.
He was hesitant about raising the issue in front of the entire team.
If no one else will raise the issue, I suppose I must.
When a bug blocks testing, the developer needs to raise the issue in the tracker immediately.
She raised the issue on the project's board so the team could assign someone to fix it.
During the sprint review, they began raising the issue of recurring login failures and tracking them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bring up: to mention or introduce a topic
  • synonym – log (an issue): to record a problem or event in a system or register for future action
  • synonym – report (a bug): to formally notify others about a software fault so it can be investigated
