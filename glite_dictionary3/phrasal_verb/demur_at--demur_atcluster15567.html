to demur at

verb
express objection or hesitation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɜr æt / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊə æt / (BrE)
to express hesitation or raise objections concerning something

Examples

to demur at a proposal
Several members demurred at the manager's plan to reduce staff hours.
She politely demurred at attending the late-night meeting, citing other commitments.
When asked to share personal details, he demurred at first but eventually agreed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accept: to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • synonym – object to: to express or feel disapproval or opposition
