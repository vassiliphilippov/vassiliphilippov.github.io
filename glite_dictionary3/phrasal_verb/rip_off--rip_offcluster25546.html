to rip off

verb
deceive or cheat someone, especially financially
Pronunciation: / rɪp ɔf / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to cheat someone, especially by charging too much money or by deceiving them in a transaction

Examples

to rip off a customer
Don't let that contractor rip off your elderly parents by charging for unnecessary repairs.
That electronics store always tries to rip off unsuspecting tourists with high prices.
We realized too late that the tickets were fake and we'd been ripped off.
