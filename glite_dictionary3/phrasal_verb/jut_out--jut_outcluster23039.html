to jut out

verb
extend or project beyond a surface
Pronunciation: / dʒʌt aʊt / (AmE), / dʒʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to extend outward or project beyond the surrounding surface or line

Examples

jut out from a wall
A massive boulder jutted out from the hillside, creating a small overhang.
At the corner, the ledge juts out, making it easy for pigeons to perch.
The balcony seemed to jut out dangerously over the street below.

Related Terms

  • synonym – protrude: to stick out from a surface
  • antonym – recede: to move back or retreat from a position
