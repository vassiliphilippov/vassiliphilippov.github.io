to close in on

verb
approach or surround closely
Pronunciation: / kloʊz ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / kləʊz ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to move nearer to someone or something, especially in order to surround or attack; to approach closely

Examples

to close in on the target
As night fell, the fog seemed to close in on the small village.
The competitive teams are starting to close in on the league leaders in the standings.
The police began to close in on the fugitives after receiving new information.

Related Terms

  • antonym – retreat: to move away from or withdraw from someone or something
