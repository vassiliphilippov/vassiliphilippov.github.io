to break open

verb
erupt or intensify suddenly
Pronunciation: / breɪk ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
(of an emotion, conflict, or situation) to erupt or intensify suddenly and forcefully.

Examples

a conflict breaks open suddenly
A long-standing family feud broke open at the holiday gathering, surprising everyone.
Tensions in the city broke open after the controversial decision was announced.
Violence broke open between the rival groups, despite attempts at negotiation.

  • synonym – flare up: to intensify or suddenly become more severe
