to step down

verb
reduce intensity or level
Pronunciation: / stɛp daʊn / (AmE), / stɛp daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the level, intensity, size, or scope of something.

Examples

to step down production
The government will step down restrictions now that the infection rate has fallen.
To prevent overheating, they decided to step down the power output.
You need to step down the voltage before plugging in that device.

Related Terms

  • synonym – de-escalate: to make (something) less intense or serious; to reduce the intensity of a conflict or situation
  • antonym – step up: to increase the amount, level, or intensity of something
