to put down

verb
cause an aircraft to land
Pronunciation: / pʊt daʊn / (AmE), / pʊt daʊn / (BrE)
(of an aircraft or its pilot) to cause an aircraft to land on the ground or water, especially in an emergency or unusual situation.

Examples

to put down an aircraft
Despite the poor weather, the captain managed to put down the helicopter safely.
The pilot was forced to put down the plane in a field when the engine failed.
They had to put down on a remote island after losing radio contact.

Related Terms

  • synonym – land: (of an aircraft) come down through the air onto the ground
