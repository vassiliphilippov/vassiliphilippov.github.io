to get hold of

verb
to suddenly affect or take control of someone
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt hoʊld əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
(of a feeling, emotion, or condition) to take control of or strongly affect someone suddenly.

Examples

fear gets hold of her
A fit of laughter got hold of her during the meeting.
A sense of dread got hold of everyone as the storm approached.
Panic got hold of him as he realized the door was locked.

  • synonym – overcome: to be strongly affected by a feeling or emotion
  • synonym – seize: to take control of suddenly or forcibly (emotion or condition)
