to luck through

verb
succeed by chance, not effort
Pronunciation: / lʌk θru / (AmE), / lʌk θruː / (BrE)
to succeed at something by chance rather than by skill or effort

Examples

to luck through an exam
He didn't study for the test but managed to luck through anyway.
I often luck through interviews despite feeling unprepared.
Some teams just luck through the early rounds of the tournament without real strategy.
