to lock in

verb
secure or make definite a commitment or arrangement
Pronunciation: / lɑk ɪn / (AmE), / lɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to make something definite or secured, such as an investment, arrangement, or decision, so that it cannot easily be changed

Examples

lock in a price
Once you've locked in your plans, it can be hard to make changes.
She wants to lock in the date for her wedding as soon as possible.
We need to lock in these interest rates before they go up again.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cancel: to decide not to proceed with a previously arranged plan or commitment
  • synonym – finalize: to complete all the details and make definite
