to get around

verb
(of news) become widely known
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of news, information, etc.) to become widely known or spread among people.

Examples

get around the news
It didn't take long for the news of their engagement to get around.
The rumor about his resignation quickly got around the office.
Word got around that the restaurant was under new management.

Related Terms

  • synonym – break: (of news) to become known or published for the first time
  • synonym – leak out: (of information) to become known to the public
