to reel off

verb
recite or list quickly and easily
Pronunciation: / ril ɔf / (AmE), / riːl ɒf / (BrE)
to recite or list a series of things quickly and easily, often without pause or much thought

Examples

to reel off facts
At the meeting, she could reel off statistics about the company's performance without hesitation.
He likes to reel off football scores to impress his friends.
When asked about the capitals of Europe, she reeled off the answers effortlessly.
