to look across

verb
have a view toward something
Pronunciation: / lʊk əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / lʊk əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to have a view in a particular direction or toward something, especially from a distance

Examples

to look across a valley
From the window, you can look across the valley to the distant mountains.
Her office looks across the river, offering a beautiful view in the morning.
They built a house that looks across the lake toward the old city.
