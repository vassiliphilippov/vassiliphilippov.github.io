to flow out

verb
to move or spill outward continuously
Pronunciation: / floʊ aʊt / (AmE), / fləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to move or spill outward in a continuous stream, typically of a liquid or other substance

Examples

lava flow out
Bright red lava began to flow out of the crater after the eruption.
Tears flowed out as she listened to the moving speech.
The river flows out into the open sea at this point.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pour out: to flow rapidly and in large amounts
  • degree lower – trickle out: to flow slowly out in drops
