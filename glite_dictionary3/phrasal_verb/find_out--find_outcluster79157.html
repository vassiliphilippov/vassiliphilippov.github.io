to find out

verb
expose someone in a wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / faɪnd aʊt / (AmE), / faɪnd aʊt / (BrE)
to discover or expose someone, especially when they have done something wrong or dishonest

Examples

to find out a liar
After months of lying to his boss, he was finally found out by a coworker.
If you cheat on the exam, you will eventually be found out.
She tried to cover up her mistake, but the auditors found out what happened.
