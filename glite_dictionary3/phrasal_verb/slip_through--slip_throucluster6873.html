to slip through

verb
pass through or be overlooked
Pronunciation: / slɪp θru / (AmE), / slɪp θruː / (BrE)
to pass easily or quickly through something; to escape notice or control; to be forgotten or overlooked

Examples

slip through the cracks
Don't let any important details slip through when you fill out the application.
Several items managed to slip through the security checks unnoticed.
She let the opportunity to win the prize slip through her fingers.
