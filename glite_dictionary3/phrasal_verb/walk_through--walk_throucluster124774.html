to walk through

verb
perform casually, as a first rehearsal
Pronunciation: / wɔk ˈθru / (AmE), / wɔːk ˈθruː / (BrE)
to perform or practice something, such as a play or a procedure, in a casual or simplified manner, often as a first rehearsal without full effort or detail

Examples

to walk through a rehearsal
The director asked the cast to walk through the script to identify any problem areas.
The team will walk through their presentation once before the actual meeting.
We did a simple walk through of the scene before the full dress rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rehearse: to practise a play, piece of music, etc. in preparation for a public performance
