to choke on

verb
have breathing blocked by swallowing something
Pronunciation: / tʃoʊk ɑn / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to have one's breathing obstructed by something blocking the throat, usually food or drink

Examples

to choke on food
He began to choke on a large piece of steak during dinner.
Laughing too hard, she almost choked on her coffee.
The child choked on a small toy and had to be rushed to the hospital.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gag on: to experience difficulty swallowing because something is blocking the throat
  • synonym – suffocate: to die or have serious difficulty breathing due to lack of air
