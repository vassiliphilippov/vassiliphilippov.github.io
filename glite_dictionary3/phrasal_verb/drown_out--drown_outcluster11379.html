to drown out

verb
make imperceptible by louder noise
Pronunciation: / draʊn aʊt / (AmE), / draʊn aʊt / (BrE)
to make a sound impossible to hear by being louder than it

Examples

music drowns out voices
Applause from the audience drowned out the end of his speech.
She turned up the music to drown out the noise from the street.
The sound of the construction work drowned out our conversation.
He raised his voice to drown out the cheers from the crowd.
The roaring of the engine drowned out the conversation inside the car.
Thunderstorms sometimes drown out the sound of birds in the morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cover: to hide or obscure so that it cannot be seen or heard
  • synonym – mask: to conceal or disguise a sound
  • synonym – muffle: to reduce the sound or loudness of something
