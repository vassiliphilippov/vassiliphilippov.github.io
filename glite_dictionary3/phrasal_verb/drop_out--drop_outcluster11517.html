to drop out

verb
stop participating (esp. after difficulty)
Pronunciation: / drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a competition, activity, or educational program before finishing, usually because of difficulty or other reasons

Examples

to drop out of a race
After injuring his leg, he had to drop out of the race.
Many students drop out when the course becomes too challenging.
She didn't want to drop out, but her health gave her no choice.
After his father lost his job, Tom had to drop out of university to help support the family.
Several athletes were forced to drop out of the race because of injuries.
She regretted her decision to drop out before completing her final semester.

Related Terms

  • antonym – continue: to keep doing something without stopping
  • antonym – enroll: to sign up for a course or institution (opposite sense)
  • synonym – give up: to stop trying to do something because it is too difficult
  • synonym – withdraw: to remove oneself from participation in something
