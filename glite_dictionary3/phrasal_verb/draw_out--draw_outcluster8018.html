to draw out

verb
make something last longer
Pronunciation: / drɔː aʊt / (AmE), / drɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to make something last longer than usual or necessary; to prolong in time

Examples

to draw out a meeting
She tried to draw out the conversation because she enjoyed his company.
The negotiations were drawn out over several weeks due to disagreements.
Their departure was drawn out by lengthy goodbyes and last-minute packing.

  • antonym – shorten: to make something shorter or briefer
