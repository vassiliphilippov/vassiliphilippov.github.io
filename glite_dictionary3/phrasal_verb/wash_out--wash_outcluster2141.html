to wash out

verb
make extremely weak or tired
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ aʊt / (AmE), / wɒʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone very weak or tired; to drain someone of energy or vitality

Examples

feel washed out after work
After working all night, I felt completely washed out and needed to rest.
Flu can wash out even the healthiest people, leaving them bedridden for days.
The long hike really wash out the entire team by the end of the day.
