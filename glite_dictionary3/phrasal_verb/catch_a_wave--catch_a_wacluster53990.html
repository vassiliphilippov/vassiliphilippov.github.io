to catch a wave

verb
ride a trend or opportunity
Pronunciation: / kætʃ ə weɪv / (AmE), / katʃ ə weɪv / (BrE)
(informal) to take advantage of a rising trend, surge of interest, or new opportunity in order to gain rapid benefit or growth.

Examples

to catch a wave of success
The startup tried to catch a wave of interest after launching its new AI chatbot last month.
They caught a wave of consumer enthusiasm, and sales doubled within six weeks.
By catching a wave of remote-work interest, the consultancy quickly expanded its client base.

Related Terms

  • synonym – capitalize on: to use a situation or opportunity to gain an advantage
  • synonym – ride a trend: to follow and benefit from a popular trend or movement
  • synonym – seize an opportunity: to take immediate action to benefit from a favourable chance
