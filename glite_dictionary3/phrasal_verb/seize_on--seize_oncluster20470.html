to seize on

verb
quickly take and use an idea or opportunity
Pronunciation: / siz ɑn / (AmE), / siːz ɒn / (BrE)
to quickly and eagerly take advantage of something such as an idea, opportunity, or remark

Examples

to seize on an idea
Critics seized on his comments as evidence of incompetence.
Politicians often seize on popular issues to win public support.
She was quick to seize on the idea and turn it into a successful project.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adopt: to take up or accept an idea or plan
  • antonym – reject: to refuse to accept, use, or believe something
