to die hard

verb
persist despite attempts to remove
Pronunciation: / daɪ hɑrd / (AmE), / daɪ hɑːd / (BrE)
to continue to exist or be believed for a long time, despite opposition or attempts to change or eliminate it

Superstitions sometimes die hard, lasting for generations.
