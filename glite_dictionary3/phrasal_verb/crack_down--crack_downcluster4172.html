to crack down

verb
take strong action to suppress something
Pronunciation: / kræk daʊn / (AmE), / kræk daʊn / (BrE)
to take strong or severe action to stop or control illegal or unacceptable behavior

Examples

to crack down on crime
After several complaints, the school began to crack down on bullying.
Authorities will crack down on illegal parking in the downtown area starting next week.
The government has promised to crack down on tax evasion this year.
Police have started to crack down on illegal street racing in the city.
The government plans to crack down on tax evasion by introducing stricter penalties.
The school will crack down on cheating during exams to ensure fairness.

  • antonym – let up: to become less strict or severe
