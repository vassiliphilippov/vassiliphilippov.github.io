to tumble on

verb
continue haphazardly despite obstacles
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌmbəl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəl ɒn / (BrE)
to continue or proceed despite difficulties or obstacles, often in a disorganized or haphazard manner

Examples

to tumble on through trouble
Even when plans fall apart, people often tumble on and find a way through.
She tumbled on through her speech, undeterred by interruptions from the audience.
The festival tumbled on despite the rain and unplanned mishaps.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give up: to stop trying or abandon effort
  • synonym – press on: to continue with determination or effort
  • synonym – struggle on: to keep going despite difficulty
