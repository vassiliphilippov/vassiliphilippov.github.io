to muck around

verb
spend time idly or doing unplanned activities
Pronunciation: / mʌk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / mʌk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time doing unimportant or unplanned things, often in a relaxed or idle way

Examples

muck around at home
Stop mucking around and help me with this project, please.
The kids love to muck around at the park after school.
We spent the afternoon mucking around in the backyard, playing and chatting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mess about: to behave in a silly or aimless way; to waste time
