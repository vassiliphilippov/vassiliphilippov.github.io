to take kindly to

verb
be inclined to accept or approve
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈkaɪndli tu / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkaɪndli tʊ /; / teɪk ˈkaɪndli tə / (BrE)
to be willing to accept or approve of something (often used in the negative to mean to dislike or resist something)

Examples

to take kindly to criticism
Our dog doesn't take kindly to strangers entering the garden.
She did not take kindly to criticism from her colleagues.
The committee will not take kindly to any last-minute changes to the proposal.
He doesn't take kindly to being interrupted during meetings.
Most cats do not take kindly to being bathed.
The manager did not take kindly to suggestions that his methods were outdated.
