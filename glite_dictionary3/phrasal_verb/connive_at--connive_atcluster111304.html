to connive at

verb
give silent approval to wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / kəˈnaɪv æt / (AmE), / kəˈnaɪv æt / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or give silent approval to something wrong or illegal

Examples

to connive at bad behavior
It is wrong to connive at such unethical business practices.
She refused to connive at any attempts to cover up the incident.
The government appeared to connive at the widespread corruption within its ranks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – condone: to accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong or offensive
