to bring to bear

verb
apply or use to achieve an effect
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ tə bɛr /; / bɹɪŋ tə bɛr / (AmE), / brɪŋ tə beə /; / bɹɪŋ tə bəː / (BrE)
to use or apply something (such as influence, effort, knowledge, or resources) to achieve a result or solve a problem

Examples

to bring to bear new ideas

The committee will bring to bear all available evidence in its investigation.

The committee will bring to bear all its expertise to solve the issue.
