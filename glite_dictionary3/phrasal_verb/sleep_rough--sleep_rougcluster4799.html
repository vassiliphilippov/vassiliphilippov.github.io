to sleep rough

verb
sleep without shelter outdoors
Pronunciation: / sliːp rʌf / (AmE), / sliːp rʌf / (BrE)
to sleep outdoors without shelter or proper protection, often because of homelessness or lack of accommodation

Examples

to sleep rough on streets
After losing his job, he slept rough for several months before getting help.
Many homeless people have no choice but to sleep rough on cold nights.
When their hostel closed down, they started sleeping rough in a city park.
