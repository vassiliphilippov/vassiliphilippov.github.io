to eat away

verb
wear away or erode gradually
Pronunciation: / it əˈweɪ / (AmE), / iːt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually wear away or erode something, often by chemical action or persistent force

Examples

eat away at confidence
Over time, doubt started to eat away at his confidence.
Rust can eat away at metal if it is left unprotected.
The constant dripping of water began to eat away the stone foundation over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrode: to destroy or damage (metal, stone, etc.) slowly by chemical action
  • synonym – erode: to gradually wear away, especially by natural forces
