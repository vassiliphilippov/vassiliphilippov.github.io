to lock out

verb
prevent employees entering as a protest
Pronunciation: / lɑk aʊt / (AmE), / lɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent someone from entering or accessing a place, account, or system

Examples

lock out workers
After several days of negotiations failed, the employer threatened to lock out all union members.
The company decided to lock out the workers after they voted to strike.
Union leaders criticized management's decision to lock out employees during the dispute.
After three wrong passwords, the system locked out her account until she reset her password.
To protect data, administrators sometimes lock out users showing suspicious login activity immediately.
If you forget your password, the website may lock out your profile for a short time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deny access: to prevent someone from using or reaching a resource, system, or data
  • synonym – lockout: the noun form referring to an event where employees are prevented from working
  • antonym – strike: a refusal to work as a form of protest
  • antonym – unlock: to restore or allow access to a locked account, system, or resource
