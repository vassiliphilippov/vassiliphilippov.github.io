to hold to

verb
remain committed to (a belief, plan, etc.)
Pronunciation: / hoʊld tu / (AmE), / həʊld tuː / (BrE)
to remain committed or loyal to something such as a principle, belief, or plan

Examples

to hold to a belief
Despite the criticism, she continued to hold to her initial proposal.
He will always hold to his promise, no matter the circumstances.
They held to their beliefs, even during difficult times.
