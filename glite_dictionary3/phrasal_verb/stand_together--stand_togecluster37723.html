to stand together

verb
unite in support or solidarity
Pronunciation: / stænd təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / stænd təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to unite with others in support of a shared cause or in solidarity, especially during difficult times

Examples

to stand together against trouble
In times of adversity, we must stand together to overcome our challenges.
The communities decided to stand together in protest against the unfair policy.
They stood together and supported each other after the disaster.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stand apart: to remain separate or uninvolved
  • synonym – stand by: to support or remain loyal to someone
