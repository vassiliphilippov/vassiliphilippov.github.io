to run out

verb
exhaust the supply of something
Pronunciation: / rʌn aʊt / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt / (BrE)
to use up or exhaust something so that none remains (such as supplies, energy, or patience)

Examples

run out of time
If we run out of milk, I'll go to the store and buy more.
The team ran out of energy in the final minutes of the game.
We must leave now before we run out of time.
The printer ran out of paper during my presentation.
They were worried they might run out of time to finish the project.
We need to hurry before the water runs out.
After several hours of hiking, I ran out and had to take a rest.
She runs out quickly after intense exercise.
The old battery will run out and stop powering your device.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deplete: to use up the supply or resources of something
  • synonym – exhaust: to use up completely
  • synonym – expire: to come to an end; cease to be valid or in force
  • synonym – poop out: to run out of energy or strength and stop functioning
  • antonym – replenish: to fill something up again
  • degree lower – run short: to have nearly no more of something
  • synonym – tire: to lose energy and become less able to continue
