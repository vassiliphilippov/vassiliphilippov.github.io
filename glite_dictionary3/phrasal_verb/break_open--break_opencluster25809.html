to break open

verb
to open by force
Pronunciation: / breɪk ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / breɪk ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to open something by using force, especially something that is shut, locked, or sealed

Examples

to break open a locked box
He tried to break open the stubborn jar lid with a knife.
Someone had to break open the locked door to rescue the puppy.
The police decided to break open the safe during the investigation.
Excitement filled the room as the contestants broke open the sealed envelopes.
She accidentally dropped the jar and it broke open, spilling jam everywhere.
The firefighters had to break open the door to rescue the family inside.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burst: to break open suddenly, often with force from inside
  • degree higher – explode: to burst or cause to burst violently and noisily
  • synonym – force open: to open something by applying force
