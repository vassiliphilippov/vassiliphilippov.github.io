to come out

verb
to become visible or to appear
Pronunciation: / kʌm aʊt / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt / (BrE)
to appear, become visible, or emerge from somewhere (such as flowers, people, or objects)

Examples

flowers come out
Many flowers come out in early spring, adding color to the garden.
The sun started to come out after a long morning of rain.
When the truth finally came out, everyone was surprised.
The milk will come out if you squeeze the carton gently.
When his name was called, he did not come out of the classroom.
When the sun began to come out, the whole room brightened.
After years of working behind the scenes, he decided to come out and take charge of the project.
She was too shy to come out and introduce herself to the group.
When the team needed a leader, Sarah chose to come out and guide them.
