to pop off

verb
speak angrily or abruptly
Pronunciation: / pɑp ɔf / (AmE), / pɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to suddenly become angry or speak out abruptly, especially without warning

Examples

to pop off at someone
He apologized for popping off at his colleague yesterday.
Try not to pop off during the meeting, even if you're upset.
Whenever the topic comes up, she tends to pop off without thinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hold back: to refrain from expressing anger or speaking abruptly
  • synonym – snap: to speak sharply or suddenly, especially in anger
