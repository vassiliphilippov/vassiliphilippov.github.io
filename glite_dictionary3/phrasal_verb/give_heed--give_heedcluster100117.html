to give heed

verb
pay attention or take notice
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv hid / (AmE), / ɡɪv hiːd / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something or someone; to take notice

Examples

to give heed to advice
He refused to give heed to the repeated warnings and acted recklessly.
It is wise to give heed to expert advice when making big decisions.
We must give heed to the needs of the community if we want to succeed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention
