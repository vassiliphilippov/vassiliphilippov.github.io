to pull back

verb
move or draw something backward
Pronunciation: / pʊl bæk / (AmE), / pʊl bæk / (BrE)
to move or draw something backward or away from a forward position (including physically pulling something back, stretching a bowstring, or holding tissue back in surgery)

Examples

to pull back a hand

The troops received orders to pull back from the front line.
A retractor can be used to pull back the muscle away from the bone.
The surgeon used forceps to pull back the skin and expose the underlying tissue.
We need to pull back the wound edges to gain better access for the procedure.
Before releasing the arrow, she pulled back her bow with steady hands.
He skillfully pulled back the bowstring and aimed at the target.
The scene showed the hunter pulling back his bow before firing.
