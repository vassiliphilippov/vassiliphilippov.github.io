to put on

verb
perform/show; dress oneself; prepare for fun
Pronunciation: / pʊt ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn / (BrE)
to perform or present (a show, event, or performance); to dress oneself in a particular item of clothing; to prepare to dance or enjoy oneself

Examples

to put on music
Let’s put on some music and dance.
She always puts on her favorite dress for special dinners.
The school will put on a play for parents next month.

Related Terms

  • antonym – take off: to remove an item of clothing from one's body
  • synonym – wear: to have clothing on one's body
