to vote out

verb
reject by vote
Pronunciation: / voʊt aʊt / (AmE), / vəʊt aʊt / (BrE)
to reject or defeat a proposal, measure, or motion by voting against it

Examples

vote out a proposal
If enough members disagree, they can vote out the motion during the meeting.
Several senators worked together to vote out the controversial proposal.
The committee decided to vote out the amendment after much discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – approve: to accept or agree to something by voting
  • synonym – defeat: to win against something in a contest or vote; to prevent passage
