to set out

verb
arrange or lay out in order
Pronunciation: / sɛt aʊt / (AmE), / sɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or lay out objects, ideas, or information in an organized or logical manner

Examples

to set out the tools
He set out the tools on the table so they were easy to find.
She set out the plates and cutlery before the guests arrived.
The report sets out the main points clearly and logically.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disorganize: to make something unorganized or disorderly
  • synonym – lay out: to arrange or spread something out
