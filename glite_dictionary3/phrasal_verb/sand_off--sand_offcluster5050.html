to sand off

verb
remove a surface layer by sanding
Pronunciation: / sænd ɑf / (AmE), / sænd ɒf / (BrE)
to remove the surface layer of something by rubbing it with sandpaper or a similar abrasive material

Examples

to sand off old paint
After stripping the wallpaper, she had to sand off the remaining glue before painting the walls.
He sanded off the rough edges to make the table child-friendly.
Make sure to sand off any rust before applying the new coat of paint.
