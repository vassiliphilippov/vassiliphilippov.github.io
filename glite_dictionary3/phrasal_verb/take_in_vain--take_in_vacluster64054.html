to take in vain

verb
use a name without due respect
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn veɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn veɪn / (BrE)
to use a name or phrase, especially 'God', without proper respect or reverence, often in a casual or disrespectful way

Examples

take in vain God's name
Many people believe you should never take in vain the name of God.
Religious texts warn against taking in vain words that deserve reverence.
She accidentally took in vain a sacred phrase during the heated argument.
