to follow out

verb
pursue or carry through to completion
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to pursue (an idea, plan, or course of action) to its conclusion or to carry through to completion

Examples

to follow out a plan
If you follow out this approach, you should be able to solve the problem.
She is determined to follow out her vision for the new project despite the challenges.
The committee promised to follow out all the recommendations made in the report.

Related Terms
