to break out

verb
escape suddenly from confinement
Pronunciation: / breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to escape from a place or situation suddenly, especially from captivity or confinement

Examples

to break out of prison
Several dangerous animals broke out of the zoo after the storm.
She tried to break out of her routine by traveling to a new country.
The prisoners managed to break out during the night and were not found until morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – break in: to enter a place by force or illegally
  • synonym – escape: to get free from somewhere or something
