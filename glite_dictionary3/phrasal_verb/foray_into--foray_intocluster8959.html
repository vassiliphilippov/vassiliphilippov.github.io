to foray into

verb
enter new area/activity, often to take spoils
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːreɪ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈfɒreɪ ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to enter an area or activity, especially one that is new or belongs to someone else, often to take something or achieve a goal

Examples

to foray into a new field
After years in finance, she decided to foray into the world of art galleries.
During the war, soldiers would often foray into enemy territory at dawn.
The tech company plans to foray into the healthcare sector next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – raid: a sudden attack, often to steal or seize
  • antonym – retreat from: to withdraw from a place or activity
