to cut back

verb
return to an earlier scene/time (film/TV)
Pronunciation: / kʌt bæk / (AmE), / kʌt bæk / (BrE)
(of film, television, etc.) to return to an earlier scene or time in the narrative.

Examples

cut back to a scene
Suddenly, the episode cut back to last year’s events to explain her actions.
The director chose to cut back to a childhood memory during the movie.
The documentary often cuts back to historic footage from the 1950s.
