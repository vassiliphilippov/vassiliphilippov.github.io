to come up

verb
to arise or be mentioned
Pronunciation: / kʌm ʌp / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to be mentioned, arise, or occur (such as a topic, issue, problem, or opportunity) unexpectedly or in conversation

Examples

a topic comes up
Her name always seems to come up whenever we talk about local artists.
If any new questions come up during the meeting, please let me know.
That issue didn't come up in our previous discussions.
Her name didn't come up during the meeting, so I didn't mention her achievements.
If any important issues come up in the interview, please let me know.
Questions about funding may come up later in the discussion.
If any problems come up during the process, let me know immediately.
Something important has come up, so I need to leave early.
The question of funding came up at the last meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arise: to emerge or become apparent
  • degree higher – come up against: to be faced with or to encounter (a difficulty or problem)
  • synonym – crop up: (of something) to appear or occur unexpectedly, especially in conversation
  • synonym – occur: to happen or take place
