to fudge the details

verb
to alter or distort facts
Pronunciation: / fʌdʒ ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (AmE), / fʌdʒ ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (BrE)
to deliberately alter or distort facts, especially to make a situation seem better or less clear than it really is

Examples

fudge the details in a report
Please don't fudge the details when writing your report for the committee.
She was accused of fudging the details to make the results look more impressive.
While telling the story, he tended to fudge the details to keep things interesting.

  • synonym – distort: to change the shape or meaning of something so that it is no longer accurate
  • synonym – fabricate: to invent or concoct (something), typically with deceitful intent
