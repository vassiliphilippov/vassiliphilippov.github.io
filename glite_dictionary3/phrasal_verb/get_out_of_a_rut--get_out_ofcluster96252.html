to get out of a rut

verb
escape a period of stagnation
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt aʊt əv ə ˈrʌt / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt əv ə ˈrʌt / (BrE)
to escape from a situation or habit in which nothing changes and you feel bored or stuck

Examples

to get out of a rut at work
After years in the same job, Helen decided to get out of a rut and travel the world.
Sometimes you need to try something new to get out of a rut in your daily life.
Taking up painting helped Mark get out of a rut and rediscover his creativity.
