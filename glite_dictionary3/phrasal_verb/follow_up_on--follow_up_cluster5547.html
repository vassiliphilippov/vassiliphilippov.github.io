to follow up on

verb
take further action on something
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊ ʌp ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ʌp ɒn / (BrE)
to take further action concerning something; to check, continue, or pursue after an initial contact or effort

Examples

to follow up on an application
After sending my application, I decided to follow up on it with a phone call.
It's important to follow up on customer complaints to ensure satisfaction.
The detective promised to follow up on every lead.
