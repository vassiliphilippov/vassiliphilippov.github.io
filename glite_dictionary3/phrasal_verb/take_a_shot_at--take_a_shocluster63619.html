to take a shot at

verb
make an attempt at something
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə ʃɑt æt / (AmE), / teɪk ə ʃɒt æt / (BrE)
to attempt to do or achieve something, especially when success is uncertain

Examples

to take a shot at a new job
He nervously took a shot at asking her out for coffee.
She decided to take a shot at writing her own novel.
Why not take a shot at the exam, even if you think it's hard?
