to take the lead

verb
assume control or initiative
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə lid / (AmE), / teɪk ðə liːd / (BrE)
to assume control, responsibility, or initiative in a situation or activity

Examples

to take the lead in a project
During the emergency, Sarah decided to take the lead and organize the evacuation.
If this team wants to win, someone needs to take the lead and make tough decisions.
When the discussion stalled, John stepped in to take the lead and move things forward.
