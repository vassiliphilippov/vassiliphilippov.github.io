to slack off

verb
become less intense or severe
Pronunciation: / slæk ɑf / (AmE), / slæk ɒf / (BrE)
(of something) to decrease in amount, intensity, or activity; to become less severe or forceful.

Examples

slack off after a storm
If the winds slack off soon, the planes will be able to land safely.
The heavy rain finally began to slack off by mid-afternoon.
Traffic tends to slack off late at night in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abate: to become less strong or intense
  • synonym – let up: to become less strong or intense, especially about something unpleasant
