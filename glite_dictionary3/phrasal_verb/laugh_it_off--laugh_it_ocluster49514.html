to laugh it off

verb
dismiss something as unimportant by laughing
Pronunciation: / læf ɪt ɔf / (AmE), / lɑːf ɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to dismiss something embarrassing, upsetting, or problematic as unimportant by making a joke or laughing about it

Examples

laugh it off after a mistake
After slipping on stage, he tried to laugh it off and keep performing.
Despite the awkward situation at the meeting, she laughed it off with a clever joke.
When his coworkers teased him about the mistake, he simply laughed it off and continued working.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shrug off: to treat something as unimportant and not let it affect you
