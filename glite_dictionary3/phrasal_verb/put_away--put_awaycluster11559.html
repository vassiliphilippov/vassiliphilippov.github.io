to put away

verb
store or set aside; discard temporarily
Pronunciation: / pʊt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put something in its usual or proper place for storage or safekeeping, so it is out of sight or not in use

Examples

to put away toys
After the holidays, we put away all the decorations until next year.
He decided to put away his anger and try to forgive.
She told the children to put away their toys before dinner.
After you finish reading, please put away the books on the shelf.
Every evening, the children put away their toys before dinner.
She reminded him to put away his phone during meetings.
After dinner, please put away the dishes in the cupboard.
He put away his winter clothes when the weather got warmer.
The teacher asked the students to put away their textbooks before leaving the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym – leave out: to not put something away; to keep something in use or in sight
  • synonym – put aside: to save or reserve for later use
  • synonym – store: to put something away for future use
  • antonym – take out: to remove something from its storage place
