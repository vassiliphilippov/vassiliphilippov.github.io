to hit the dirt

verb
drop suddenly to avoid danger
Pronunciation: / hɪt ðə dɝt / (AmE), / hɪt ðə dɜːt / (BrE)
to drop to the ground quickly, especially to avoid danger or harm

Examples

to hit the dirt for cover
As the helicopter passed overhead, the children hit the dirt until it was safe.
At the sound of the explosion, everyone hit the dirt behind the nearest cover.
The hikers were told to hit the dirt as soon as they heard gunshots.
