to wind up

verb
finally arrive in a situation
Pronunciation: / waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to finally arrive in a particular situation, place, or state, especially despite initial intentions or plans

Examples

to wind up in trouble
He studied engineering but wound up working as a chef.
If you keep taking risks, you might wind up in trouble.
We didn't plan to stay so late, but we wound up talking for hours.

Related Terms

  • synonym – end up: to reach or come to a particular situation at the end
