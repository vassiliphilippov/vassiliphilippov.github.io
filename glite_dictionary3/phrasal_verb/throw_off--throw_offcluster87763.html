to throw off

verb
remove quickly (esp. clothing or quality)
Pronunciation: / θroʊ ˈɔf / (AmE), / θrəʊ ˈɒf / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of something quickly, such as clothing, a feeling, or something affecting you

Examples

to throw off a jacket
As soon as the race was over, the athlete threw off his running shoes.
He struggled to throw off his old reputation as unreliable.
She rushed in and threw off her wet jacket by the door.
Despite her efforts, she couldn't throw off the flu for weeks.
He accelerated suddenly to throw off the cyclists who were following closely behind.
The suspect tried to throw off the police by taking several turns through back alleys.
