to blend in

verb
become harmonious or unobtrusive
Pronunciation: / blɛnd ɪn / (AmE), / blend ɪn /; / blɛnd ɪn / (BrE)
to appear similar to the people or things around you so that you are not easily noticed; to fit in naturally

Examples

to blend in with people
He hoped his quiet behavior would help him blend in at his new school.
She tried to blend in with the locals by wearing traditional clothes.
The new curtains blend in perfectly with the rest of the décor.
Chameleons can blend in with their surroundings to avoid predators.
He tried to blend in at the party by wearing similar clothes to everyone else.
The spy used a disguise to blend in with the crowd.
