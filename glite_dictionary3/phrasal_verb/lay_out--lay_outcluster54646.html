to lay out

verb
spend or invest (money)
Pronunciation: / leɪ aʊt / (AmE), / leɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to spend or invest (a sum of money), often a large or specific amount

Examples

to lay out money
He decided to lay out a substantial sum for his new business venture.
They laid out more than $10,000 renovating their old house.
You shouldn't lay out so much money on something you don't really need.

Related Terms

  • synonym – expend: to spend or use up (resources, money, time)
  • antonym – save: to keep or store up (money) rather than spend it, especially for a particular purpose
