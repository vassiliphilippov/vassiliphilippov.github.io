to pass through

verb
to travel through a place
Pronunciation: / pæs ˈθru / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈθruː / (BrE)
to travel through a place without staying for long or making it your main destination

Examples

to pass through a city
As the parade passed through the city, the streets filled with laughter and music.
Many migratory birds pass through this region each spring.
We didn't stay in the village; we just passed through on our way to the coast.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stop over: to stay briefly at a place during a longer journey
  • synonym – travel through: to travel through a place
