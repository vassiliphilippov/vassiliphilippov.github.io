to crack up

verb
suffer a mental breakdown
Pronunciation: / kræk ʌp / (AmE), / kræk ʌp / (BrE)
to suffer a mental or emotional breakdown due to stress or pressure

Examples

crack up under pressure
After months of relentless pressure at work, he finally cracked up and had to take a leave of absence.
Many soldiers cracked up during the intense battles of the war.
She was afraid she might crack up if things got any worse at home.
