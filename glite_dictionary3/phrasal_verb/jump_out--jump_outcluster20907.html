to jump out

verb
be highly noticeable or striking
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp aʊt / (AmE), / dʒʌmp aʊt / (BrE)
to be immediately or highly noticeable, often because of being very different or striking

Examples

jump out in a crowd
Her talent for design jumps out in her portfolio.
One word jumped out at me as I read the letter.
The bright red cover really jumps out on the shelf.
