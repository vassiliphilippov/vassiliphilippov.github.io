to hold up

verb
delay or slow down
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ʌp / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to be delayed or slowed down

Examples

hold up the traffic
Construction on the bridge is holding up the entire project.
Sorry I'm late, something at work held up the meeting.
The accident held up traffic for several hours this morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – delay: to make something happen later than planned or expected
  • antonym – speed up: to make something happen faster
