to play up

verb
behave insincerely to gain favor
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ʌp / (AmE), / pleɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone, often in an insincere or flattering way

Examples

play up to authority
Don't play up to the teacher just to get a better grade.
He always tries to play up to his boss in meetings to get promoted.
She was playing up to the new manager, laughing at all his jokes.
