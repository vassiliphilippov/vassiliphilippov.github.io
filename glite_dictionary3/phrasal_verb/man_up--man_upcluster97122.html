to man up

verb
behave courageously or responsibly
Pronunciation: / mæn ʌp / (AmE), / mæn ʌp / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is considered strong, responsible, or courageous, especially in the face of difficulty or challenge

Examples

to man up under pressure
She told her brother to man up and admit his mistake to their parents.
Sometimes you just need to man up and face your fears head-on.
When things got tough at work, he decided to man up and handle the situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – coward out: to avoid a difficult situation due to fear or lack of courage
  • synonym – toughen up: to become stronger and more able to deal with difficult situations
