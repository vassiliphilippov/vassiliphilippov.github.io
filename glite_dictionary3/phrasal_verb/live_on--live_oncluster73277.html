to live on

verb
survive using something as a resource
Pronunciation: / lɪv ɑn / (AmE), / lɪv ɒn / (BrE)
to survive or continue to exist using or relying on a particular resource or income

Examples

to live on little money
During college, she had to live on instant noodles and soup.
Many retired people live on a fixed income.
The panda lives on a diet of bamboo.
