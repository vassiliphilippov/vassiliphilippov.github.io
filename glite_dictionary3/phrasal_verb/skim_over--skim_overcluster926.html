to skim over

verb
move swiftly over a surface
Pronunciation: / skɪm oʊvɚ / (AmE), / skɪm əʊvə / (BrE)
to move or pass swiftly and lightly just above or across the surface of something

Examples

skim over the water
A flock of birds skimmed over the lake at sunrise.
She watched the dragonfly skimming over the surface of the pond.
The stone skimmed over the water before finally sinking.

  • synonym – glide: move smoothly and continuously along a surface
