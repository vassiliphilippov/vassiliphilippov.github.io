to bar off

verb
to block or restrict access to an area
Pronunciation: / bɑr ɔf / (AmE), / bɑː ɒf / (BrE)
to block or restrict access to a place, especially by putting up a physical barrier

Examples

to bar off an area
After the accident, authorities decided to bar off the damaged section of the road.
During the protest, police were ordered to bar off key government buildings.
The workers had to bar off the construction site for public safety.
