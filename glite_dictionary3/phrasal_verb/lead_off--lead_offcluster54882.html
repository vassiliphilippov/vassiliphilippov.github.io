to lead off

verb
to corrupt or teach immoral behavior
Pronunciation: / liːd ɔf / (AmE), / liːd ɒf / (BrE)
to cause someone to adopt immoral or improper behavior; to corrupt the morals of someone, especially a younger or inexperienced person.

Examples

to lead off a child
It is wrong to lead off innocent children by exposing them to harmful habits.
She worried that bad company might lead off her son into a life of crime.
The older boys tried to lead off their younger siblings by encouraging them to skip school.

Related Terms

  • synonym – corrupt: cause someone to become immoral or dishonest
  • antonym – reform: to improve someone's behavior or morals
