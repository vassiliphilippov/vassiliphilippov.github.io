to fall back

verb
move or tumble backwards
Pronunciation: / fɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move backwards suddenly, often losing balance, so that your body comes down toward the ground.

Examples

fall back onto a chair
He lost his balance and suddenly fell back onto the sofa.
The kids laughed when one of them pretended to fall back in surprise.
When the train jerked, she almost fell back against the door.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fall over: to lose one's balance and collapse to the ground
  • antonym – stand up: to rise to a standing position
