to settle for

verb
accept less than desired
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬l̩ fɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl fə / (BrE)
to accept or agree to something that is less than what was wanted or hoped for

Examples

to settle for second best
After weeks of searching, they finally settled for a less expensive hotel.
He didn't want to settle for an ordinary life and dreamed of doing something extraordinary.
She felt she shouldn't settle for less than she deserved in her career.
