to spirit up

verb
to encourage or boost morale
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrət ʌp / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to encourage or enliven someone; to boost someone's spirits or morale

Examples

to spirit up the team
A pep talk helped spirit up the volunteers for the tough day ahead.
The good news seemed to spirit up everyone in the office.
The team manager tried to spirit up the players before the final match.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discourage: to make (someone) less confident or enthusiastic
