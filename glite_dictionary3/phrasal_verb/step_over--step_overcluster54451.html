to step over

verb
debugger command: execute next line without entering functions
Pronunciation: / stɛp ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / stɛp ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(in computing, especially debugging) to execute the next line or statement but run any called functions as a single step, so the debugger does not enter those functions.

Examples

to step over a function
When the debugger paused, she clicked step over to run past the library function without entering it.
He usually uses step over during testing so his team can inspect the results after each line.
To avoid stepping into complicated calls, type step over at the debugger prompt and press Enter.

Related Terms

  • antonym – step into: a debugger command that executes the next statement and enters any called functions so you can inspect their execution
