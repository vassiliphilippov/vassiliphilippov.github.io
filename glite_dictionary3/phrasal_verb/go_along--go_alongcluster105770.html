to go along

verb
continue; carry on doing something
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to continue in a particular state, activity, or condition

Examples

go along smoothly
As the meeting went along, people became more comfortable sharing their ideas.
She decided to go along with her studies, even when it got challenging.
The day went along quietly as everyone worked on their own projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym – continue: to keep happening, existing, or doing something without stopping
  • antonym – stop: to cease from doing something; to come to an end
