to feel around

verb
search by touching or groping
Pronunciation: / fil əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fiːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to search for something by touching or groping, especially in the dark or when unable to see clearly

Examples

to feel around in the dark
He feels around under the sofa cushions, hoping to find the lost remote.
I had to feel around for the light switch in the dark room.
She felt around in her bag until she found her phone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grope: to search blindly or uncertainly by feeling with the hands
