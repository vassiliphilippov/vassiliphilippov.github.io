to shoot for

verb
aim for an ambitious goal
Pronunciation: / ʃut fɔr / (AmE), / ʃuːt fɔː / (BrE)
to aim to achieve something ambitious or difficult; to set one's sights on a challenging goal

Examples

to shoot for the stars
If you shoot for the stars, you might reach the sky.
They decided to shoot for first place in the national competition.
When planning your career, it's important to shoot for something that excites you.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aim for: to direct efforts towards achieving something
  • antonym – settle for: to accept something less than what was hoped for
