to bolt down

verb
run away quickly or suddenly
Pronunciation: / boʊlt daʊn / (AmE), / bəʊlt daʊn / (BrE)
to run away quickly or suddenly, usually in a hurry or because of fear

Examples

to bolt down the hallway
As soon as the bell rang, the children bolted down the hallway to the playground.
He saw the police coming and bolted down the alley without looking back.
Startled by the loud noise, the cat bolted down the stairs and hid under the bed.

  • synonym – dash: move quickly or suddenly
  • synonym – flee: run away from danger or pursuers
  • antonym – linger: stay in a place longer than necessary; move slowly
