to take away

verb
diminish; detract from something
Pronunciation: / teɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / teɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to reduce the value, effectiveness, or impact of something; to diminish or detract from.

Examples

to take away the fun
A chipped plate can take away from the enjoyment of a nice meal.
His lack of enthusiasm really takes away from the team's overall spirit.
The rude comments take away from her otherwise excellent presentation.
