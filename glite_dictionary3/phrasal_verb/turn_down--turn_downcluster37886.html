to turn down

verb
begin to decline or decrease
Pronunciation: / tɝn daʊn / (AmE), / tɜːn daʊn / (BrE)
(of a process, activity, or trend) to begin to decline or decrease after rising or remaining steady.

Examples

turn down after growth
After years of growth, the housing market finally turned down in 2008.
Although sales were rising last year, they have turned down sharply in recent months.
If consumer spending continues to drop, the economy may turn down next quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fall: to move downward, typically rapidly and freely without control
  • antonym – improve: to become better; to increase in value, quality, or amount
