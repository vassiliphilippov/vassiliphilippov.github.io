to flood in

verb
arrive in large numbers
Pronunciation: / flʌd ɪn / (AmE), / flʌd ɪn / (BrE)
(of people or things) to arrive somewhere in very large numbers, often suddenly or continuously.

Examples

flood in with people
Applications for the new position are flooding in from all over the country.
As soon as the store opened, customers began to flood in.
Donations flooded in after the disaster was reported on television.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pour in: arrive continuously or in large amounts
  • antonym – trickle in: arrive in small numbers or slowly
