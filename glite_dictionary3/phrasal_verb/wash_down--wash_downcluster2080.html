to wash down

verb
be carried along by water
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ daʊn / (AmE), / wɒʃ daʊn / (BrE)
(of something) to be carried along or to flow freely with a current of water, especially downstream.

Examples

wash down the river
After the dam broke, entire trees washed down the river for miles.
During the heavy rains, debris washed down the mountain into the valley below.
When the tide came in, abandoned boats washed down the coastline.

  • synonym – float: to rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid
