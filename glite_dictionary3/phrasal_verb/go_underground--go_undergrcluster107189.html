to go underground

verb
become secretive or operate covertly
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˌʌndərˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˌʌndəˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to become secretive or hidden, especially in order to avoid attention or operate covertly, often for political or illegal reasons

Examples

go underground to avoid police
After the scandal was revealed, the organization chose to go underground for several years.
During the crackdown, many leaders went underground to avoid arrest.
Some activists had to go underground to continue their protests safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym – become secretive: to start keeping actions or information hidden
  • antonym – go public: to stop hiding and make something known openly
