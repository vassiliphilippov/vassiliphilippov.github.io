to break the buck

verb
lose standard value, esp. financially
Pronunciation: / breɪk ðə bʌk / (AmE), / breɪk ðə bʌk / (BrE)
(of a financial fund or currency) to fall below its standard value, especially when a money market fund's share price drops below one dollar.

Examples

break the buck in finance
During the financial crisis, several funds were at risk of breaking the buck.
If the reserve isn't maintained, the fund might break the buck and lose investor confidence.
Tighter regulations were introduced to prevent money market funds from breaking the buck.
