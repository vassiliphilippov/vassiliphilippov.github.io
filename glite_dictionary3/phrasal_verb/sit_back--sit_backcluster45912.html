to sit back

verb
remain inactive or uninvolved
Pronunciation: / sɪt bæk / (AmE), / sɪt bæk / (BrE)
to remain inactive or not get involved while something is happening, especially when action is expected

Examples

to sit back during trouble
Instead of helping, he chose to sit back and do nothing.
Many people sit back expecting someone else to solve the problem.
You can't just sit back and watch while injustice occurs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intervene: to become involved and take action in a situation
