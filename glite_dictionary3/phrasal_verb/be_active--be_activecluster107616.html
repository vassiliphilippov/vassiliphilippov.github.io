to be active

verb
be in a state of action or movement
Pronunciation: / bi ˈæktɪv / (AmE), / bi ˈæktɪv / (BrE)
to be engaged in physical movement or activity; to not be idle or inactive

Examples

be active every day
Children should be active for at least an hour every day.
It is important to be active in order to maintain good health.
She tries to be active even on weekends by going for long walks.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – be energetic: to be full of energy and activity
  • antonym – be idle: to not be active; to be inactive or not doing anything
