to draw back

verb
move away or backwards
Pronunciation: / drɔː ˈbæk / (AmE), / drɔː ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move away or backwards, especially because of fear, surprise, or a change of mind

Examples

to draw back in fear
As the curtain drew back, the actors took their places on stage.
He drew back his hand before touching the hot surface.
She instinctively drew back when the dog barked loudly.
