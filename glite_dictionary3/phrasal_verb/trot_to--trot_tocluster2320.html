to trot to

verb
go briskly to a place
Pronunciation: / trɑt tu / (AmE), / trɒt tʊ / (BrE)
to go briskly to a place, typically at a pace faster than walking but slower than running

Examples

to trot to the park
Every morning, she trots to the park to get some fresh air before work.
He trotted to his friend's house in just a few minutes.
Realizing she was late, Emily quickly trotted to the bus stop.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – run to: to go to a place by running
  • degree lower – walk to: to go to a place by walking
