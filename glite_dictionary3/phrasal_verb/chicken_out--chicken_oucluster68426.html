to chicken out

verb
withdraw due to fear or lack of courage
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn aʊt / (BrE)
to decide not to do something difficult or dangerous because of fear or lack of courage

Examples

to chicken out of a challenge
He almost went skydiving, but he chickened out at the last minute.
Many contestants chicken out when they see how tough the challenge really is.
She didn't want to chicken out in front of her friends when it was her turn to sing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – back out: to withdraw from an agreement or position
  • antonym – face up to: to confront or deal bravely with a difficult situation
