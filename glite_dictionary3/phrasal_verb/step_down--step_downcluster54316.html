to step down

verb
resign from a position
Pronunciation: / stɛp daʊn / (AmE), / stɛp daʊn / (BrE)
to resign from or leave an important job or position, especially so someone else can take over

Examples

to step down as leader
After years as CEO, she decided to step down and let someone else lead the company.
Facing mounting criticism, the official finally chose to step down from his post.
The coach announced he would step down at the end of the season.
After years of dedicated service, she decided to step down as chairperson.
Amid controversy, the official was pressured to step down from his post.
The company announced that their CEO would step down next month.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resign: to formally leave a job or position
  • degree lower – retire: to leave one's job and stop working, often due to age
  • synonym – stand down: to leave a position or post, especially an official one
