to lay up

verb
be confined by illness or injury
Pronunciation: / leɪ ʌp / (AmE), / leɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to confine someone to bed or incapacitate them, especially because of illness or injury

Examples

lay up with illness
A broken leg laid him up for most of the summer.
Many workers were laid up by the outbreak of food poisoning.
She was laid up with a bad case of flu for a week.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bedridden: unable to get out of bed due to illness or weakness
  • antonym – recover: to return to normal health after illness or injury
