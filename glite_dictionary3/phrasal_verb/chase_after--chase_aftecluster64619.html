to chase after

verb
to pursue or run after
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to run or hurry to catch up with someone or something; to pursue or actively seek something

Examples

to chase after dreams
He decided to chase after his childhood dreams and enrolled in art school.
I had to chase after the bus this morning because I was running late.
She realized it was pointless to chase after people who didn't value her.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pursue: to follow with the intent to catch or achieve
