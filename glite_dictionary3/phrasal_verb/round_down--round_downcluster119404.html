to round down

verb
reduce to nearest lower value (math)
Pronunciation: / raʊnd daʊn / (AmE), / raʊnd daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce a number to the nearest whole number or specified place value that is less than or equal to the original number

Examples

to round down a number
He rounded down the number to make calculations easier.
If the total comes to $12.99, we round down to $12.
The cash register automatically rounds down to the nearest dollar.

Related Terms

  • antonym – round up: to increase a number to the nearest whole or specified higher value
