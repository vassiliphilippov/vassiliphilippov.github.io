to break out

verb
release or launch something
Pronunciation: / breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to introduce, launch, or present something, especially a product or service, for use or public release

Examples

to break out a product
The company will break out its updated line of smartphones at the tech conference.
They're planning to break out the improved version of the app this quarter.
We decided to break out our new menu items for the grand reopening.

Related Terms

  • synonym – launch: to start or introduce something new, especially a product
  • synonym – roll out: to introduce something, such as a new product, gradually or to a wider market
