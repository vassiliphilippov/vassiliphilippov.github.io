to grow on someone

verb
become more liked by someone over time
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊ ɑn ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ ɒn ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
(of something) to gradually become more liked or appreciated by someone over time.

Examples

grow on you over time
I didn't enjoy classical music at first, but it started to grow on me after a few weeks.
The comedian's humor may seem strange, but it will grow on you with time.
This little town has really grown on me since I moved here.
