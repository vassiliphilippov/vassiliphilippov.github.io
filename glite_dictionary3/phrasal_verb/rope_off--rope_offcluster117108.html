to rope off

verb
separate with a rope barrier
Pronunciation: / roʊp ɔf / (AmE), / rəʊp ɒf / (BrE)
to separate or restrict access to an area by putting a rope or ropes around it

Examples

rope off a dangerous area
After the spill, workers quickly roped off the contaminated area to keep people away.
The construction crew had to rope off the entire street to ensure safety.
They roped off the section of the beach where turtles were nesting.
