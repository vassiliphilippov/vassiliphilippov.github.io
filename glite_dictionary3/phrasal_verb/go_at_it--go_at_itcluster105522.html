to go at it

verb
engage energetically in conflict or activity
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈæt ɪt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈæt ɪt / (BrE)
to engage energetically and often aggressively, especially in a fight, argument, or other activity involving intense effort

Examples

go at it fiercely
The players really went at it during last night's game.
They started to go at it before the referee could even blow the whistle.
Whenever they go at it, everyone else in the office tries to stay away.
