to give away

verb
reveal secret information about someone
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to reveal or make known secret or confidential information, whether intentionally or by accident

Examples

give away a secret
Don't give away my secret to anyone.
She accidentally gave away his surprise party plans.
Under pressure, he gave away the names of his accomplices.
He accidentally gave away the surprise party plans to his wife.
Please don't give away any details about the movie's ending.
The journalist refused to give away her sources.
Don't let your face give away the surprise party we're planning.
Her nervous laughter always gives away how anxious she is.
The spy's hesitation gave away his true identity to the enemy agents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – betray: to reveal information that should be kept secret, especially to an enemy
  • antonym – conceal: to keep something secret or hidden
  • synonym – disclose: to make secret information known
  • antonym – keep quiet: to not say anything about something, especially a secret
  • synonym – reveal: to make known something that was secret
