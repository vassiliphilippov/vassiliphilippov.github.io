to make good

verb
fulfill a promise or commitment
Pronunciation: / meɪk ɡʊd / (AmE), / meɪk ɡʊd / (BrE)
to do what one has promised or expected to do; to fulfill a commitment or obligation

Examples

make good on a promise
After months of delay, the company finally made good on its commitment to improve working conditions.
He promised to help us move, and he always makes good on his word.
The government must make good on its pledge to reduce carbon emissions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – break a promise: to fail to do what one said one would do
  • synonym – fulfill: to carry out or bring to completion (a promise, duty, etc.)
