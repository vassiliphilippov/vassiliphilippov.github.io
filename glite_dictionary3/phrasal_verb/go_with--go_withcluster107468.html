to go with

verb
to accompany or be associated with
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð / (BrE)
to be present with, accompany, or occur together with something else

Examples

go with a feeling
A sense of nostalgia can go with certain pieces of music.
French fries often go with hamburgers as a popular meal choice.
White wine typically goes with seafood dishes at fancy restaurants.
Economic downturns frequently go with rising unemployment rates.
Spicy food often goes with a glass of cold milk.
The color blue goes with almost everything in her wardrobe.

  • synonym – coexist: to exist together or at the same time
