to strike out

verb
begin a new course of action
Pronunciation: / straɪk aʊt / (AmE), / straɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to begin a new course of action, especially independently or with determination

Examples

strike out on your own
After graduation, she decided to strike out on her own and start a business.
Many young people strike out from home in search of independence and adventure.
When his ideas were dismissed, he chose to strike out in a new direction.
