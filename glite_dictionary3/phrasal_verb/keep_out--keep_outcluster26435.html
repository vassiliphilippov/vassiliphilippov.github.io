to keep out

verb
remain outside; not enter
Pronunciation: / kip aʊt / (AmE), / kiːp aʊt / (BrE)
to remain outside a particular place or not enter it

Examples

to keep out of trouble
A sign on the fence warned people to keep out.
She told the children to keep out while she was cleaning the kitchen.
Visitors are required to keep out of restricted areas at all times.
