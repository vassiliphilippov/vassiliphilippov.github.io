to warm to

verb
become enthusiastic about
Pronunciation: / wɔrm tu / (AmE), / wɔːm tʊ / (BrE)
to begin to like or become enthusiastic about someone or something

Examples

to warm to new people
At first, she was hesitant, but she gradually warmed to the new team members.
During the meeting, the director warmed to the innovative suggestions from her staff.
He didn't like the proposal initially, but soon warmed to the idea after some thought.
