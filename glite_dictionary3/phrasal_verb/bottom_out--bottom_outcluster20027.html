to bottom out

verb
reach lowest point before improving
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːtəm aʊt /; / ˈbɑtəm aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm aʊt / (BrE)
to reach the lowest or worst point before beginning to improve or rise again (used for moods, prices, activity, etc.)

Examples

bottom out in price
His mood bottomed out in late winter but soon improved with the arrival of spring.
Many experts believe the economy will bottom out before the end of the year.
Shares finally bottomed out after months of decline and began to recover.
After months of declining sales, the company finally bottomed out in March.
Experts predict that unemployment will bottom out in the second quarter of this year.
Housing prices continued to fall, but many analysts believe they will soon bottom out.

Related Terms

  • antonym – peak: to reach the highest point (opposite meaning)
  • degree higher – plateau: to reach and stay at a steady level
