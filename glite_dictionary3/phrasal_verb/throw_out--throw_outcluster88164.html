to throw out

verb
dismiss, especially legally or formally
Pronunciation: / θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject something, especially in a legal or formal context; to cease to consider a case, proposal, or request.

Examples

to throw out a case
If the motion is irrelevant, the court may throw out the entire case.
Several complaints were thrown out before the hearing even began.
The judge decided to throw out the lawsuit for lack of evidence.
