to well over

verb
to overflow or pour out
Pronunciation: / wɛl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / wɛl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of liquid, emotion, etc.) to flow or spill over the edge of a container, or to be expressed with great force or emotion.

Examples

well over with emotion
Pride would sometimes well over in his heart when he thought of his achievements.
Tears began to well over her eyelids as she listened to the sad news.
The river started to well over its banks after days of heavy rain.
