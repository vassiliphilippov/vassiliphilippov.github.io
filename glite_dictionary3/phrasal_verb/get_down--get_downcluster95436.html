to get down

verb
make someone feel sad or demoralized
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt daʊn / (AmE), / ɡɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to lower someone’s spirits; to make someone feel sad or demoralized

Examples

to get down your mood
Don't let the harsh comments get down your enthusiasm for the project.
Rainy weather can sometimes get down even the most optimistic people.
The constant criticism at work really gets down a lot of employees.
