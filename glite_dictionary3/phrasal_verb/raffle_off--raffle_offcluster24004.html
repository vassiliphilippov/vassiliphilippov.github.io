to raffle off

verb
give away or sell by raffle
Pronunciation: / ˈræfəl ɔf / (AmE), / ˈræfəl ɒf / (BrE)
to offer or dispose of something as a prize by means of a raffle, usually to raise money

Examples

raffle off a prize
The school decided to raffle off a new bicycle to raise funds for charity.
They raffled off a signed football at the company event last year.
We are raffling off several gift baskets at the holiday party.

  • synonym – auction off: to sell something by auction
