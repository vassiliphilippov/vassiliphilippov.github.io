to bump around

verb
move around roughly or unsteadily
Pronunciation: / bʌmp əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / bʌmp əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move about in a rough or unsteady way, often being knocked or jostled repeatedly by physical forces or obstacles

Examples

bump around in a vehicle
During the turbulence, the plane bumped around and made everyone nervous.
The old bus bumped around on the rocky road for hours.
They spent the afternoon bumping around the crowded market, constantly jostled by other shoppers.
