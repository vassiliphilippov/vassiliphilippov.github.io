to get around

verb
avoid something difficult or unpleasant
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with something difficult, unpleasant, or required, often by finding an indirect or clever way

Examples

to get around a rule
He always finds a way to get around doing his chores.
They tried to get around the new regulations with a loophole.
You can't get around the fact that honesty is expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym – confront: to face up to or deal with a problem or situation directly
  • synonym – evade: to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
