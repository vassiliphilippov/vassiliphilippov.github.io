to sally forth

verb
set out quickly or energetically
Pronunciation: / ˈsæli fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈsæli fɔːθ / (BrE)
To leave a place in a sudden, energetic, or determined manner, often with a sense of adventure or purpose.

Examples

to sally forth on an adventure
After gathering their supplies, the team sallied forth into the unknown wilderness.
Despite the storm, the hikers decided to sally forth in search of adventure.
Every morning, the baker would sally forth to deliver fresh bread to the village.

Related Terms

  • antonym – retreat: to move back or withdraw, especially from danger
  • synonym – venture out: to go somewhere new, especially when it involves risk or uncertainty
