to get someone started

verb
provoke someone to talk at length
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌn ˈstɑrdəd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈsʌmwən ˈstɑːtɪd / (BrE)
to provoke or encourage someone to begin speaking at length, especially about something they feel strongly about or are likely to complain about

Examples

to get him started talking
As soon as you brought up cars, you got him started on his favorite subject.
Don't get her started about her neighbor, or you'll be here all night!
If you mention taxes, it will really get him started on a long rant.

Related Terms

  • synonym – set someone off: to cause someone to become angry or excited, often leading them to talk at length
