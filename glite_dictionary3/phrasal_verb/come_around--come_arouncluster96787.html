to come around

verb
change opinion or position
Pronunciation: / kʌm əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kʌm əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to change your opinion or attitude, especially after disagreeing, or to visit or return after an absence

Examples

to come around to an idea
After much discussion, she finally came around and agreed to the proposal.
It took him months to come around to the idea of moving abroad.
They didn’t agree at first, but they came around in the end.
After much debate, John finally came around to our proposal and agreed to the plan.
It took some time, but she eventually came around and apologized.
They came around to visit us last weekend after being away for months.
