to get on

verb
board a vehicle (train, bus, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to board or enter a train, bus, ship, aircraft, or similar form of transport

Examples

to get on a bus
Passengers are advised not to get on the aircraft until their row is called.
She quickly got on the bus as soon as it arrived at the stop.
We need to get on the next train to Manchester before it leaves.

  • antonym – get off: to leave or disembark from a form of transport
