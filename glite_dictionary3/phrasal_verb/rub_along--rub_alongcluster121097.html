to rub along

verb
manage to get by with difficulty
Pronunciation: / rʌb əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / rʌb əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to manage to live or continue despite difficulties, often with limited means; to get by.

Examples

to rub along with little money
After the company downsized, many employees had to rub along with lower wages.
Even though resources were limited, the community continued to rub along year after year.
We didn’t have much money growing up, but we managed to rub along just fine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – get by: manage with difficulty or with limited resources
  • synonym – scrape by: survive with just enough resources
  • antonym – thrive: to prosper or do very well
