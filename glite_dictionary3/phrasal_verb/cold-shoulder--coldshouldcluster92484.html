to cold-shoulder

verb
deliberately ignore or snub
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊld ˌʃoʊldɚ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld ˌʃəʊldə / (BrE)
To deliberately ignore or show indifference toward someone, typically as a form of rebuff or slight.

Examples

cold-shoulder a friend
Despite his efforts, the entire group cold-shouldered the newcomer for months.
He realized he was being cold-shouldered when no one replied to his emails.
She decided to cold-shoulder him after their argument at work.

  • synonym – snub: to ignore or treat with disdain
