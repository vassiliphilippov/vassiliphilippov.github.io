to stop up

verb
to block or fill completely
Pronunciation: / stɑp ʌp / (AmE), / stɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to fill or block something so that nothing can pass through

Examples

to stop up a hole
During the storm, we had to stop up the cracks in the windows to keep out the rain.
He used some old rags to stop up the leak in the pipe temporarily.
If you stop up the sink, the water won't drain properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – plug: to block or fill a hole tightly
  • antonym – unblock: to remove a blockage; to make open again
