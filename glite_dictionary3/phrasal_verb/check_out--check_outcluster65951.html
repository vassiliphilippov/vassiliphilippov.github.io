to check out

verb
be found correct after verification
Pronunciation: / tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
(of a claim, fact, or situation) to be found correct, accurate, or as expected after investigation or verification.

Examples

check out the story
Her story didn't check out when we looked at the evidence.
If these numbers check out, the company is in serious trouble.
We'll have to see if his alibi checks out with the security footage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – check: to verify or investigate for accuracy
  • antonym – prove false: to be shown to be untrue or inaccurate
