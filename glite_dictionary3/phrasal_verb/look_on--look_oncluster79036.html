to look on

verb
observe without taking part
Pronunciation: / lʊk ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to watch something happen without getting involved or taking part

Examples

look on at an event
Crowds gathered to look on as the firefighters battled the blaze.
He often looked on during family arguments but rarely got involved.
I couldn't just look on while my friend was being treated unfairly.
Many people just look on when they see an accident instead of helping.
She prefers to look on during meetings rather than join the discussions.
The children gathered to look on as the magician performed his tricks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – observe: to watch carefully, especially in order to learn something
  • antonym – participate: to take part in an activity or event
  • synonym – watch: to observe attentively
