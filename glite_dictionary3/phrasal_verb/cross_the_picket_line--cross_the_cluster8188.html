to cross the picket line

verb
work during a strike; defy a strike
Pronunciation: / krɑs ðə ˈpɪkɪt laɪn / (AmE), / krɒs ðə ˈpɪkɪt laɪn / (BrE)
to go to work or enter a workplace during a strike, especially in defiance of a group of striking workers

Examples

to cross the picket line during a strike
He faced criticism from his colleagues after he decided to cross the picket line.
Management offered bonuses to those willing to cross the picket line during the strike.
Some workers refused to cross the picket line out of solidarity with the strikers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scab: a person who works or takes the place of others during a strike
