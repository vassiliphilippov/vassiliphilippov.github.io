to charge down the field

verb
move swiftly and forcefully on a field
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ daʊn ðə fiːld / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ daʊn ðə fiːld / (BrE)
to move quickly and aggressively forward across a playing field, especially in sports to advance towards a goal or objective.

Examples

to charge down the field quickly
Every time they win possession, they charge down the field with impressive speed.
The home team began to charge down the field, hoping to score before halftime.
With only seconds left, the players charged down the field in a last desperate attempt.
