to create friction

verb
cause disagreement or tension
Pronunciation: / kriˈeɪt ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / kriːˈeɪt ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)
to cause disagreement, tension, or conflict between people or groups

Examples

create friction between people
Different management styles can create friction between departments.
Her critical remarks often create friction within the team.
Unclear expectations sometimes create friction among colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – smooth things over: to make a situation less tense or serious
