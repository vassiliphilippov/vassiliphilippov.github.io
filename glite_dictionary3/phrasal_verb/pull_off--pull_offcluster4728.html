to pull off

verb
cause withdrawal from activity
Pronunciation: / pʊl ɔf / (AmE), / pʊl ɒf / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to withdraw from an activity or position

Examples

to pull off a team
After reviewing the project's progress, the manager chose to pull off her team early.
The company decided to pull off their sponsor from the event after the controversy.
They had to pull off several workers due to budget cuts.
