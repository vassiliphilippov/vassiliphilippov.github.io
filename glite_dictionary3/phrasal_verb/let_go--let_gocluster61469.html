to let go

verb
release from one's grasp
Pronunciation: / lɛt ˈgoʊ / (AmE), / lɛt ˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
to release something or someone from one's hold or grasp

Examples

let go of the rope
Please let go of my arm, you're hurting me.
When the train stopped, she let go of the handrail and stepped off.
You need to let go if you want to move forward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hold on: to keep a grip on something
  • synonym – release: to let go of something that is held
