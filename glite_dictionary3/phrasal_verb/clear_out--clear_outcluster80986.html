to clear out

verb
remove mucus from chest or lungs
Pronunciation: / klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to make the chest or lungs free of mucus or other unwanted substances, usually by expectoration

Examples

to clear out the chest
Doctors sometimes prescribe inhalants to clear out the chest of patients with respiratory illnesses.
The coughing fit finally managed to clear out all the mucus from his chest.
The new medicine helped to clear out my lungs after I got the flu.
