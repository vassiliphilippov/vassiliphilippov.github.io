to wind up

verb
bring to an end; conclude
Pronunciation: / waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to bring something to an end; to finish or conclude an activity, event, or process

Examples

to wind up a meeting
Let's wind up the meeting so everyone can go home.
They needed to wind up the project by the end of the month.
We should wind up this discussion before it gets too late.
