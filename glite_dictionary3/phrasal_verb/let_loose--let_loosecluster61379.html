to let loose

verb
emit or utter a sound suddenly
Pronunciation: / lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to make a sound or utterance freely or suddenly; to express something audibly, especially a sound or exclamation

Examples

let loose a loud scream
During the performance, the singer suddenly let loose a wild, joyful laugh.
He let loose a loud sigh after hearing the disappointing news.
The children let loose a cheer when their team scored the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – let out: to allow something, such as a sound, to escape or be expressed
  • synonym – utter: to say or make a sound with your voice
