to branch off

verb
diverge from a main route or topic
Pronunciation: / bræntʃ ˈɔf / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ ˈɒf / (BrE)
(of a road, path, or subject) to separate from a main route, area, or topic and go in a different direction.

Examples

branch off a main road
During our meeting, the discussion branched off into completely unrelated issues.
The cyclist watched as the trail branched off to the left and right.
Their research began to branch off into several specialized areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – diverge: to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • synonym – split off: to separate from a main body or group
