to listen in

verb
listen quietly without participating
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪs.ən ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪs.ən ɪn / (BrE)
to listen secretly or quietly to a conversation or broadcast without being noticed or without permission

Examples

to listen in on a conversation
Curious children sometimes listen in on adult conversations without being noticed.

She likes to listen in on her neighbors when they are arguing next door.

It’s rude to listen in when others are having a private conversation.
She suspected someone might be listening in on their conversation through the wall.
