to bottom out

verb
(vehicle) to hit the ground underneath
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːtəm aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm aʊt / (BrE)
(of a vehicle) to reach such a low point that the underside touches the ground or another surface.

Examples

bottom out on a bump
After hitting the pothole, the truck bottomed out and scraped its chassis.
If you drive too fast over the dip, your car might bottom out.
The old sedan bottomed out as it drove over the speed bump.
