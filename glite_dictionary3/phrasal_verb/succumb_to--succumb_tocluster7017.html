to succumb to

verb
to yield or give in to
Pronunciation: / səˈkʌm tu / (AmE), / səˈkʌm tə / (BrE)
to fail to resist pressure, temptation, or some other negative force; to give in or yield to something

Examples

to succumb to pressure
Despite his illness, he refused to succumb to despair.
If you succumb to temptation, it can be hard to break bad habits.
Many people succumb to peer pressure in high school.

Related Terms

  • antonym – resist: to withstand the action or effect of something
