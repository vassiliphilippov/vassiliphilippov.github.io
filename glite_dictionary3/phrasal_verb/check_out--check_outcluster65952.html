to check out

verb
examine or inspect something
Pronunciation: / tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to examine, inspect, or look at something to learn more about it or see if it is suitable or interesting

Examples

check out a restaurant
I want to check out that restaurant everyone keeps talking about.
Please check out the new software updates before installing them.
The mechanic will check out your car to see what’s wrong with it.
Before making a decision, she wanted to check out all the available options.
Have you checked out the song everyone is talking about this week?
You should check out the new science museum; it's really interactive and fun.
