to snuff out

verb
put an end to; kill or extinguish
Pronunciation: / snʌf aʊt / (AmE), / snʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to put an end to something; to kill a person or destroy something so that it no longer exists

Examples

to snuff out a life
A sudden heart attack snuffed out his promising career.
She devoted her life to organizations that seek to snuff out hunger and poverty.
The dictator tried to snuff out any sign of opposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – extinguish: to put out a fire, light, or flame
  • synonym – wipe out: to destroy something completely
