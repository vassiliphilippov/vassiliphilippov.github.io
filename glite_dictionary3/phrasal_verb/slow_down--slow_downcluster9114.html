to slow down

verb
become less tense or rest
Pronunciation: / sloʊ daʊn / (AmE), / sləʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to move, act, or progress more slowly, or to make someone or something do this; to relax or take things easier

Examples

to slow down after work
After working nonstop for weeks, I finally decided to slow down and take a vacation.
She realized she hadn't slowed down in months and was feeling exhausted.
You need to slow down and enjoy life instead of rushing through everything.
As we approach retirement, many people choose to slow down and enjoy life more.
During heavy traffic, all vehicles must slow down to avoid accidents.
The economy tends to slow down after periods of rapid growth.
After the accident, traffic began to slow down considerably.
He asked her to slow down so he could understand the instructions.
You should slow down when approaching a school zone.

  • synonym – decelerate: to reduce the speed of something
  • synonym – relax: to become less tense or anxious; to rest
  • antonym – speed up: to increase speed or progress
