to go under

verb
sink below the surface
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈʌndər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to sink below the surface or to fail completely (such as a business failing or something being submerged)

Examples

to go under the water
After the storm, several small dinghies went under near the dock.
If you don't keep swimming, you might go under in the deep lake.
The old fishing boat began to go under as water filled the hull.
If the lifeboats don't arrive soon, the boat will go under.
Several restaurants in the area had to go under during the pandemic.
The company began to go under after the recession hit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collapse: to fall down or give way suddenly, often used for financial failure
  • antonym – float: to remain on the surface of a liquid and not go under
  • synonym – sink: to go down below the surface of water or another liquid
