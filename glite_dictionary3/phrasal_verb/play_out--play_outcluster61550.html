to play out

verb
develop or be performed to conclusion
Pronunciation: / pleɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pleɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to develop or happen until a conclusion is reached, or to be performed until the end

Examples

to play out a situation
No one knows how this complex situation will play out.
The story played out over several dramatic weeks.
We watched the tense negotiations play out on the news.
