to put to sleep

verb
place a device in low-power/standby mode
Pronunciation: / p8at t59 slid0p / (AmE), / p8at t59 slid0p / (BrE)
To place an electronic device or system into a low-power (sleep or standby) state so it uses less energy but can quickly resume normal operation.

Examples

to put to sleep a computer
Before the meeting, she put to sleep her laptop to save battery during the presentation.
The technician usually puts to sleep servers overnight to reduce energy costs and wear.
Restarting the phone fixed the problem after accidentally putting to sleep its wireless adapter.

Related Terms

  • antonym – shut down: to turn a device off completely so it stops operating until restarted
  • synonym – sleep mode: a low-power state in electronic devices that allows quick resumption of activity
