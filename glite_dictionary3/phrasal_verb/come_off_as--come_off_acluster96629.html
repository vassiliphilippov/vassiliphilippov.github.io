to come off as

verb
be perceived as; give an impression
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɔf æz / (AmE), / kʌm ɒf æz / (BrE)
to give a particular impression or be perceived in a specific way by others

Examples

come off as rude
His joke came off as insensitive to many in the audience.
If you speak too formally, you might come off as pretentious.
She doesn't want to come off as rude during the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym – seem: to appear to be; to give the impression of being
