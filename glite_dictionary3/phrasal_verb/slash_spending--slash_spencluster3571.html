to slash spending

verb
drastically reduce expenses
Pronunciation: / slæʃ ˈspɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / slæʃ ˈspɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
to drastically reduce the amount of money that is being spent, especially as a deliberate policy or strategy.

Examples

to slash spending on programs
In an effort to reduce debt, the government plans to slash spending across all departments.
Many families have had to slash spending due to the rising cost of living.
The company was forced to slash spending after profits dropped last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cut costs: to reduce expenses
  • antonym – spend freely: to use money without restraint
