to run out of

verb
have no more left of something
Pronunciation: / rʌn ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / rʌn ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to have used all of something so that none is left

Examples

run out of money
If you run out of ideas, try taking a short break to clear your mind.
The printer ran out of ink right in the middle of my project.
We ran out of milk this morning, so I couldn't have cereal for breakfast.
