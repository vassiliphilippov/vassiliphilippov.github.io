to sally into

verb
make a sudden start into an activity
Pronunciation: / ˈsæli ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈsæli ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to make a sudden or energetic start into an activity, often one that is new or unfamiliar

Examples

to sally into a new field
After years in accounting, Mark made a bold sally into entrepreneurship and opened his own café.
She decided to sally into the world of painting, even though she had never picked up a brush before.
Their sally into political activism was brief but made a noticeable impact on the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foray into: a sudden or brief attempt to become involved in a new activity
  • synonym – venture into: to undertake a risky or daring journey or course of action
