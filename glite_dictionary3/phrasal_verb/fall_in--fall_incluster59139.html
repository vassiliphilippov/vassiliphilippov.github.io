to fall in

verb
join a line or formation (military)
Pronunciation: / fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of soldiers) to form a line or take one's position in a military formation as ordered.

Examples

fall in for inspection
As the bugle sounded, the soldiers quickly fell in along the parade ground.
Following the command, the entire platoon began falling in by the barracks.
The sergeant ordered the recruits to fall in for inspection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fall out: to leave a military formation
  • synonym – form up: to arrange in a military formation
