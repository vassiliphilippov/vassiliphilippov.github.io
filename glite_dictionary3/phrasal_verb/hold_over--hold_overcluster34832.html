to hold over

verb
continue term of office beyond usual period
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to continue to serve in an official position after the normal term has expired, often temporarily until a successor is appointed

Examples

to hold over in office
He was asked to hold over in office for an additional three months.
Sometimes, officials hold over if there is a delay in confirming their successors.
The governor will hold over until a replacement is appointed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – remain: to continue to exist or stay in a position
