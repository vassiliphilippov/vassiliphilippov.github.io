to play off

verb
respond creatively to something in performance
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ɔf / (AmE), / pleɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to use something as a basis for humor, performance, or action, especially by reacting to it creatively or cleverly

Examples

to play off a joke
During the interview, he cleverly played off the reporter's remarks to lighten the mood.
She plays off the audience's reactions to make her comedy routines more engaging.
The actor skillfully played off his co-star's unexpected ad-libs in the scene.

  • synonym – riff on: to speak or perform at length on a theme in a creative way
