to move around

verb
go to the other side of something
Pronunciation: / muːv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / muːv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to pass to the other side of an object or obstacle by changing direction

Examples

to move around an obstacle
She had to move around the fallen tree to reach the gate.
The hikers decided to move around the large boulder blocking their path.
We watched the cat carefully move around the sleeping dog.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circumnavigate: to go completely around something, especially in a purposeful way
  • synonym – go around: to avoid or pass by something by changing direction
