to call on

verb
challenge someone to justify a statement
Pronunciation: / kɔl ɑn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒn / (BrE)
to challenge someone to justify a statement or take responsibility for an action, especially by pointing out an inconsistency or offense

Examples

to call on his mistake
He was called on his inappropriate comment by several members of the audience.
The journalist called on the politician about the inconsistency in his story.
When he lied during the meeting, she called on him to provide evidence for his claim.
