to keep on ice

verb
postpone or reserve for future use
Pronunciation: / kip ɑn aɪs / (AmE), / kiːp ɒn aɪs / (BrE)
to postpone something or keep someone or something in reserve for later use

Examples

keep on ice a plan
His application was kept on ice while the committee reviewed other candidates.
She suggested that we keep on ice our plans for expansion until next year.
The company decided to keep on ice the new project until market conditions improve.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defer: to put off to a later time
  • synonym – put on hold: to postpone or suspend temporarily
