to hold onto

verb
keep or maintain a grip on something
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈɑnˌtu / (AmE), / həʊld ˈɒnˌtu / (BrE)
to keep or not get rid of something; to grasp or keep one's grip on something

Examples

hold onto the railing
He tried to hold onto the slippery pole but lost his grip.
It's wise to hold onto important documents for future reference.
You should hold onto your ticket in case you need it later.

Related Terms

  • antonym – let go of: to release one's hold on something; to stop keeping or possessing
