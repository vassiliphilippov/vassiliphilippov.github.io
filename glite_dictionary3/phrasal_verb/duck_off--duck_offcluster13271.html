to duck off

verb
leave quickly; euphemistic command to go away
Pronunciation: / dʌk ɑf / (AmE), / dʌk ɒf / (BrE)
(slang) To leave quickly or discreetly; also used as a euphemism for telling someone to go away in a rude manner.

Examples

to duck off quickly
After the fight, he decided to duck off before things got worse.
Let's duck off before anyone notices we're gone.
She told her annoying coworker to duck off and mind his own business.
