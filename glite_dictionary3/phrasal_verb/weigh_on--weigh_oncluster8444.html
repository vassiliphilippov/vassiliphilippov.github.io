to weigh on

verb
to cause worry or distress
Pronunciation: / weɪ ɑn / (AmE), / weɪ ɒn / (BrE)
To cause feelings of worry, sadness, or pressure; to be oppressive or troubling to someone.

Examples

weigh on your mind
His conscience has been weighing on him for weeks.
The decision to move far from home really weighed on him.
The responsibility of caring for her family continued to weigh on her mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burden: to cause someone worry or difficulty; to load heavily
  • synonym – oppress: to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
