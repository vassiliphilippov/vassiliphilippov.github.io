stuck with

adjective
burdened with something unwanted
Pronunciation: / stʌk wɪð / (AmE), / stʌk wɪð / (BrE)
having to deal with or accept something unpleasant or unwanted, often because there is no choice.

Examples

stuck with extra work
After the meeting was canceled, I was stuck with all the leftover food.
He's always stuck with extra work when someone calls in sick.
She found herself stuck with a huge bill after her friends left the restaurant.
