to doctor up

verb
alter or embellish deceitfully
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə ʌp / (BrE)
to change or add to something, usually dishonestly or in order to deceive

Examples

to doctor up a report
He attempted to doctor up the report to hide his mistakes.
The chef tried to doctor up the soup by adding extra spices.
They doctored up the evidence, hoping no one would notice the changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adulterate: to make something poorer in quality by adding another substance
  • synonym – falsify: to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • antonym – purify: to make something pure by removing unwanted substances
