to suck up

verb
absorb, take in (liquid or metaphorically)
Pronunciation: / sʌk ʌp / (AmE), / sʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to take in or absorb a liquid, substance, or influence, both literally and figuratively

Examples

to suck up water
He tried to suck up as much information as possible before the meeting.
The plant quickly sucked up all the nutrients from the fertilizer.
The roots suck up moisture from the soil after it rains.
