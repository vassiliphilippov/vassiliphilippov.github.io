to lard down

verb
make richer or heavier by adding fat or extras
Pronunciation: / lɑrd daʊn / (AmE), / lɑːd daʊn / (BrE)
to make something richer, heavier, or more indulgent by adding fat, extra ingredients, or by smothering it

Examples

lard down a meal
She tends to lard down her meals with cheese and rich toppings.
The chef chose to lard down the sauce with extra butter and cream.
Too many authors lard down their books with unnecessary details.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enrich: to make richer or better by adding something
  • synonym – smother: to cover thickly or excessively
