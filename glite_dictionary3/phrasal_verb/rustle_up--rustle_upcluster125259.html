to rustle up

verb
prepare or gather quickly, often with little notice
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌsəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrʌsəl ʌp /; / ˈrʌsl ʌp / (BrE)
to quickly prepare or find something, especially food, often with little planning or by improvising

Examples

to rustle up a meal
If you give me five minutes, I'll rustle up some extra chairs for everyone.
Let me rustle up some coffee for our guests while you take a seat.
She can rustle up a meal from almost nothing in the fridge.
With half an hour left, she rustled up a simple pasta and salad for the guests.
He rustled up a short presentation from old slides before the unexpected meeting started.
During the storm, volunteers rustled up blankets and warm drinks for the stranded travelers.

  • synonym – whip up: to prepare or produce something quickly
