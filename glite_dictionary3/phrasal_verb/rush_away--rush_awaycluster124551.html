to rush away

verb
depart quickly and suddenly
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / rʌʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly and suddenly, usually because you are in a hurry

Examples

to rush away from work
As soon as the bell rang, the students began to rush away from the classroom.
I’m sorry to rush away, but I have another appointment.
She had to rush away from the meeting to catch her train.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hurry off: to leave quickly or in haste
  • antonym – linger: to stay in a place longer than necessary
