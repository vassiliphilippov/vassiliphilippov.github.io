to toughen up

verb
become more resilient or strong
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌf.ən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtʌf.ən ʌp / (BrE)
to become stronger or more resilient, especially emotionally or mentally; to make oneself or someone else less sensitive to hardship or criticism

Examples

to toughen up under pressure
After hearing the criticism, she knew she had to toughen up if she wanted to succeed.
Parents sometimes encourage their children to toughen up when facing challenges at school.
The marathon was hard, but it really helped him toughen up both physically and mentally.

Related Terms

  • synonym – harden: to become or make more severe or less sympathetic
  • antonym – soften: to become less severe or strong; opposite of toughen up
