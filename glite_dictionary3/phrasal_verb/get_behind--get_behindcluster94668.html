to get behind

verb
fall behind or lag behind
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to fail to keep up with others or with a schedule; to lag or fall behind

Examples

get behind on work
Don't get behind on your bills, or you may face late fees.
If you get behind on your assignments, it's hard to catch up.
She tends to get behind in her work when she's distracted.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catch up: to reach the same level as others after falling behind
  • synonym – fall behind: to fail to maintain the same progress as others
