to factor out

verb
resolve into factors (math)
Pronunciation: / ˈfæktɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfæktə aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve (a mathematical expression or number) into its factors; to express as a product of factors.

Examples

to factor out x
If you factor out x from 3x + 6x^2, you get x(3 + 6x).
Modern algorithms can factor out very large numbers much more efficiently than before.
The teacher showed us how to factor out common terms in the equation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – expand: to multiply out an expression (opposite process in algebra)
  • synonym – factor: to resolve (a number or expression) into factors (math)
