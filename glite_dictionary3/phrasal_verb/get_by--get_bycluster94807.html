to get by

verb
pass or move in front of
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to pass or move in front of someone or something, especially in a competitive or physical context.

Examples

to get by another runner
During the marathon, she got by several runners in the final mile.
Excuse me, could I get by you to reach the exit?
He quickly got by the defenders to score the winning goal.
