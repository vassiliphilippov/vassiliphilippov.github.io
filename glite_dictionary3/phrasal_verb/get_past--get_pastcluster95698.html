to get past

verb
overcome or move beyond something
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt pæst / (AmE), / ɡɛt pɑːst / (BrE)
To succeed in overcoming or moving beyond an obstacle, problem, or negative feeling.

Examples

to get past a problem
He struggled for months to get past the loss of his job, but eventually found a new opportunity.
It's never easy to get past a major disappointment, but support from friends helps a lot.
Once you get past the initial fear, public speaking becomes much more manageable.
