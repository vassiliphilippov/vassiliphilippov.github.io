to take aim at

verb
focus effort or criticism toward a target
Pronunciation: / teɪk eɪm æt / (AmE), / teɪk eɪm æt / (BrE)
to direct attention, effort, or criticism toward achieving or targeting something or someone

Examples

take aim at a target
At the meeting, she took aim at the company's outdated policies.
The marketing campaign takes aim at younger consumers.
The senator took aim at the new tax proposal during the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – overlook: to ignore or fail to address something; the opposite of focusing on it
  • synonym – target: to direct action or criticism toward a particular objective
