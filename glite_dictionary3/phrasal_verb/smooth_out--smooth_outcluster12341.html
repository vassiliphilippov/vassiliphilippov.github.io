to smooth out

verb
make a process or situation easier
Pronunciation: / smuð aʊt / (AmE), / smuːð aʊt / (BrE)
to make a process, situation, or problem easier or less difficult by removing obstacles or resolving issues

Examples

to smooth out problems
Extra funding helped smooth out the transition to the new system.
Good communication can smooth out misunderstandings in a team.
Negotiators managed to smooth out the conflicts and finalize the agreement.
The manager worked hard to smooth out any conflicts within the team.
They spent the evening trying to smooth out all the last-minute details.
We need to smooth out the issues before the presentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hinder: to make it difficult for something to happen or progress
  • synonym – iron out: to resolve problems or difficulties
  • synonym – remove: to take something away or get rid of an obstacle
