to gouge on

verb
charge excessively or unfairly
Pronunciation: / ɡaʊdʒ ɑn / (AmE), / ɡaʊdʒ ɒn / (BrE)
to charge someone an excessively high price for a service or product, often unfairly or dishonestly

Examples

to gouge on prices
Be careful or they might gouge on the price just because you're a tourist.
Some airlines try to gouge on baggage fees during busy travel seasons.
We felt the company was gouging on repair costs after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overcharge: to charge someone too much money
  • antonym – undercharge: to charge less than the correct or normal price
