to come on

verb
develop or progress positively
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɑn / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn / (BrE)
to develop, improve, or progress in a positive way

Examples

her skills come on
Her cooking skills have really come on in the past year.
My son’s reading abilities have come on faster than I expected.
The project is coming on nicely despite the initial difficulties.

Related Terms

  • synonym – come along: to make progress; to improve
  • antonym – fall behind: to fail to make progress or keep pace
  • synonym – progress: to move forward or improve towards a better state
