to bull through

verb
force through despite opposition
Pronunciation: / bʊl θru / (AmE), / bʊl θruː / (BrE)
to force or push something through despite opposition; to insist on or accomplish something by sheer determination or strength

Examples

to bull through a plan
Despite objections, he managed to bull through the proposal at the last meeting.
She wasn't willing to listen to criticism and simply bulled through her own plan.
The manager tried to bull through changes without consulting the team.
