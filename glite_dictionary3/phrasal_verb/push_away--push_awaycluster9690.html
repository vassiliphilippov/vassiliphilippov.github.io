to push away

verb
move away by pushing
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊʃ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move someone or something away from oneself by pushing, usually with the hands or arms

Examples

push away the plate
He pushed away the plate because he wasn't hungry anymore.
She tried to push away the thought, but it kept returning.
When the dog jumped up, she gently pushed away its paws.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pull towards: to move something nearer by pulling
  • synonym – push aside: to move something out of the way by pushing
