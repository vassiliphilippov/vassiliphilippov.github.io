to stack up

verb
accumulate or save (resources or debts)
Pronunciation: / stæk ʌp / (AmE), / stæk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or save things, especially resources or debts, usually so that they form a larger amount over time

Examples

stack up emails
If you don't clear your emails, they will stack up quickly.
She managed to stack up enough vacation days for a long trip.
Unpaid invoices can stack up and cause financial stress.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accumulate: to gather or collect, especially over time
  • synonym – pile up: to become larger in quantity; to accumulate
