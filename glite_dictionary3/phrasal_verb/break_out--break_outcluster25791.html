to break out

verb
remove from storage for use
Pronunciation: / breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from storage or stowage in order to prepare it for use

Examples

to break out supplies
The crew was ordered to break out emergency supplies during the storm.
They will break out the tents as soon as we reach the campsite.
We had to break out the life jackets when the alarm sounded.
