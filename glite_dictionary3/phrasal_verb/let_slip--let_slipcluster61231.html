to let slip

verb
unintentionally reveal something
Pronunciation: / lɛt slɪp / (AmE), / lɛt slɪp / (BrE)
to reveal something, especially information or a secret, unintentionally or without meaning to

Examples

let slip a secret
During the interview, she let slip that a new product was coming soon.
He accidentally let slip the details of her surprise party.
I didn't mean to let slip the secret; it just happened.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blurt out: to say something suddenly and without thinking, especially information
  • antonym – keep quiet: to not say something or not reveal information
  • synonym – spill the beans: to reveal secret information unintentionally or prematurely
