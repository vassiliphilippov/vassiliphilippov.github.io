shut up

adjective
closely confined; shut in
Pronunciation: / ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) closely confined, especially so as to be unable to leave; shut in.

Examples

shut up inside a room
During the storm, we were shut up inside the house all day.
She felt lonely and anxious after being shut up in bed for so long.
The prisoner was shut up in a tiny cell for weeks on end.
