to knock out

verb
eliminate someone or something
Pronunciation: / nɑk aʊt / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to eliminate or remove someone or something from a competition, situation, or activity, especially by defeating them

Examples

knock out a team
A single mistake could knock out even the strongest teams in the early rounds.
If they win this game, they will knock out the defending champions from the tournament.
The virus was engineered to knock out a specific gene in the mice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advance: to move on to the next stage, often by not being eliminated
  • synonym – eliminate: to completely remove or get rid of something or someone
