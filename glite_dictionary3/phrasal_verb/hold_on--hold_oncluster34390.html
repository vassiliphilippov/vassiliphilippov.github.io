to hold on

verb
grasp or grip something firmly
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to grasp or grip something firmly to avoid letting go

Examples

hold on the rail
He held on tightly as the boat rocked in the waves.
Please hold on to the rope so you don't fall.
She had to hold on to the railing while climbing the slippery steps.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grip: to take and keep a firm hold of something
  • antonym – let go: to release one's hold on something
