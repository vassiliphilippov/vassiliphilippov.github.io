to hold open

verb
keep something available or possible
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to keep something available or ensure the possibility remains, especially by retaining rights or not making a final decision

Examples

hold open a seat
Could you hold open my seat while I step out for a moment?
The company agreed to hold open her position while she was on maternity leave.
We need to hold open the possibility of renegotiating the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hold: to keep or maintain
  • synonym – keep open: to not close something; to make available
