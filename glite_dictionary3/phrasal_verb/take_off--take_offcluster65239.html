to take off

verb
depart or leave for somewhere
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk ɒf / (BrE)
(of a person or group) to leave a place, especially suddenly or to travel somewhere.

Examples

take off after lunch
After the meeting ended, the team took off for lunch.
She took off for Paris without telling anyone.
We should take off early if we want to avoid the traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – arrive: to reach a place, especially at the end of a journey
  • synonym – leave: to go away from a place
