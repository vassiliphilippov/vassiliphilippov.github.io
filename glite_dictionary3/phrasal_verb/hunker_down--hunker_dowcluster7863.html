to hunker down

verb
refuse to change position; resist
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌŋkɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə daʊn / (BrE)
to refuse to change one's position or opinion, especially by becoming more determined or resistant

Examples

hunker down on an issue
Despite mounting pressure from her colleagues, she hunkered down and refused to compromise on her beliefs.
During the negotiations, the management hunkered down and would not accept any of the workers' demands.
He knew he was right, so he hunkered down and stuck by his decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dig in: to firmly resist or become entrenched in a position
  • antonym – give in: to yield or surrender one's position
