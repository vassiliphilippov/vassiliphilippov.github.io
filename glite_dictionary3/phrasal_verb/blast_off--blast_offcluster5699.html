to blast off

verb
to launch with great force (rocket, spacecraft)
Pronunciation: / blæst ɑf /; / blæst ˈɔf / (AmE), / blɑːst ɒf /; / blɑːst ˈɒf / (BrE)
(of a rocket or spacecraft) to launch or take off from the ground into the air or space

Examples

blast off into space
At exactly 9 a.m., the shuttle blasted off from the launch pad.
Scientists cheered when their experiment successfully blasted off aboard the spacecraft.
The crowd watched as the rocket blasted off into the clear morning sky.

Crowds gathered to watch as the rocket prepared to blast off into orbit.
The countdown reached zero, and the spacecraft blasted off smoothly from the launch pad.
