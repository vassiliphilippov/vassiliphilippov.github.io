to black out

verb
to censor or suppress information
Pronunciation: / blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent information from being published or visible, especially by censoring text or images

Examples

to black out names
During wartime, authorities would often black out names to protect identities.
Sensitive information in the document was blacked out before release to the public.
The government ordered the media to black out certain controversial details of the report.

Related Terms

  • synonym – censor: to examine and remove content considered unacceptable
  • antonym – reveal: to make information known or visible
