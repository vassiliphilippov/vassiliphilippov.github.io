to hold back

verb
restrain progress, expansion, or influence
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈbæk /; / hoʊld bæk / (AmE), / həʊld ˈbæk /; / həʊld bæk / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from moving forward, making progress, or developing

Examples

to hold back progress
New regulations were imposed to hold back the negative effects of pollution.
The government took steps to hold back the spread of the epidemic.
Troops were sent in to hold back the advance of enemy forces.
Lack of confidence can hold back even the most talented students.
She tried to hold back her tears during the emotional speech.
The firefighters built a barrier to hold back the advancing flames.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contain: to keep something harmful within limits and not allow it to spread
  • antonym – encourage: to give support, confidence, or hope to (the opposite of holding back progress)
  • synonym – impede: to slow down or block progress
  • antonym – release: to let go or allow to move forward
  • synonym – restrain: to prevent something from growing, increasing, or developing
