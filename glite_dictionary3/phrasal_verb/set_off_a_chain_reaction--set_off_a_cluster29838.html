to set off a chain reaction

verb
cause a series of related events
Pronunciation: / sɛt ɔf ə tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (AmE), / sɛt ɒf ə tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (BrE)
to cause a series of related events or actions to happen, each triggered by the previous one

Examples

to set off a chain reaction of events
His resignation set off a chain reaction that changed the entire company.
One careless remark can sometimes set off a chain reaction of misunderstandings.
The discovery of a small leak set off a chain reaction of repairs throughout the building.

  • synonym – trigger: to cause something to happen, often suddenly
