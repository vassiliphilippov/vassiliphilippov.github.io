to gummy up

verb
become sticky or clump together
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌmi ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmi ʌp / (BrE)
(of a substance) to become sticky or clump together, often making it difficult to work with or use.

Examples

gummy up a machine
Hair products can gummy up your brush if not cleaned regularly.
If you leave the glue open, it will gummy up and become hard to use.
The sugar began to gummy up when exposed to moisture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clump together: to form into clusters or lumps
  • antonym – harden: to become firm or solid
