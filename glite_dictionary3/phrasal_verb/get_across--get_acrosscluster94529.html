to get across

verb
to cross from one side to another
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to cross from one side of something to the other, such as a street, river, or area.

Examples

to get across a river
He couldn't get across the border without the right documents.
It's not easy to get across busy highways on foot.
We managed to get across the river before it got dark.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cross: to go across from one side to the other
