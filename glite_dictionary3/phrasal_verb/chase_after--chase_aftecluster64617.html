to chase after

verb
pursue with intent to catch
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to run or go after someone or something in order to catch them

Examples

to chase after a thief
Police had to chase after the suspect when he ran from the scene.
The children began to chase after the stray puppy in the park.
Tourists sometimes chase after famous actors hoping to get a photograph.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flee: to run away from someone or something, typically to escape
  • synonym – pursue: to follow or chase in order to catch or attain
