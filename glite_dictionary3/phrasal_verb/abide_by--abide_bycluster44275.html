to abide by

verb
accept and follow a rule or decision
Pronunciation: / əˈbaɪd baɪ / (AmE), / əˈbaɪd baɪ / (BrE)
to accept and act according to a rule, decision, or agreement

Examples

to abide by the rules
All employees must abide by the company's code of conduct at all times.
If you want to stay here, you will have to abide by our house rules.
The two nations agreed to abide by the terms of the peace treaty.

Related Terms

  • synonym – comply with: to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • antonym – disobey: to refuse to follow rules or instructions
