to keep in sight

verb
maintain focus or awareness of something
Pronunciation: / kip ɪn saɪt / (AmE), / kiːp ɪn saɪt / (BrE)
To continue to be aware of or focused on something, especially as a goal or priority.

Examples

keep in sight your goal
During the project, we must keep in sight our original objectives.
It's easy to get distracted, but try to keep in sight what matters most.
You should always keep in sight the values that are most important to you.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose sight of: to stop considering or focusing on something important
