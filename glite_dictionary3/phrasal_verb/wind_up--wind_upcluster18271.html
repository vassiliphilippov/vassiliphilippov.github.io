to wind up

verb
prepare to throw by swinging the arm
Pronunciation: / waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
(in baseball, cricket, etc.) to move the throwing arm in a circular motion before pitching or throwing the ball.

Examples

to wind up for a pitch
She carefully wound up and released a perfect pitch.
The pitcher began to wind up before delivering the fastball.
You must wind up properly to get enough power behind the throw.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pitch: to throw (a baseball or similar ball) toward a batter
