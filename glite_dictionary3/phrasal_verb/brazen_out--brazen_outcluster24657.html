to brazen out

verb
face a challenge boldly
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪzən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪzən aʊt / (BrE)
to face or deal with a difficult or embarrassing situation in a bold and unashamed way

Examples

to brazen out a scandal
Despite the criticism, she decided to brazen out the controversy and stand by her decision.
He hoped he could brazen out the accusations without confessing to any wrongdoing.
They tried to brazen out the scandal, acting as though nothing had happened.

Related Terms

  • synonym – face down: to confront someone or something and force them to back down
