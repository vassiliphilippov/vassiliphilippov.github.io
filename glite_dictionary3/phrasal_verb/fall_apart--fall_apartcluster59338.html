to fall apart

verb
lose emotional or mental control
Pronunciation: / fɔl əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to lose emotional or mental control; to become unable to cope

Examples

fall apart emotionally
Despite her strong appearance, she sometimes falls apart in private.
She completely fell apart after hearing the tragic news about her friend.
When the pressure became too much, he started to fall apart at work.
After the divorce, his life seemed to fall apart completely.
If you keep moving that box, it's going to fall apart soon.
When the funding was cut, their entire project fell apart overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collapse: to fall down suddenly, often due to structural failure or defeat
  • synonym – disintegrate: to break into small parts or pieces, or lose cohesion
