to sit out

verb
endure until something is over
Pronunciation: / sɪt aʊt / (AmE), / sɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to remain in a place or situation until it is finished, especially something unpleasant or boring; to endure until the end.

Examples

sit out a boring meeting
Even though the meeting was tedious, he managed to sit out the entire discussion.
Many fans chose to sit out the match due to the heavy rain.
She decided to sit out the storm in her small cottage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stick it out: to endure a difficult situation until it ends
