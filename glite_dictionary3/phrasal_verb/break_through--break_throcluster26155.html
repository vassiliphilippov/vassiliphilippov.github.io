to break through

verb
to penetrate an obstacle
Pronunciation: / breɪk θruː /; / breɪk θru / (AmE), / breɪk θruː / (BrE)
to force a way through or emerge from a barrier or obstacle, often suddenly or after effort

Examples

to break through a wall
A shaft of light broke through the darkness in the abandoned tunnel.
After hours of digging, the miners finally broke through to the trapped workers.
Scientists are hoping to break through the barriers to a cure for the disease.
After weeks of trying, the plant broke through the soil.
Sunlight broke through the clouds after a long storm.
The baby's first tooth finally broke through yesterday, much to her parents' delight.
After hours of digging, the rescue team finally broke through to the trapped miners.
Suddenly, a ray of sunlight broke through the dense forest canopy.
The crowd cheered as the athlete broke through the finish line tape.

  • antonym – give way: to collapse, especially after being obstructive
  • synonym – penetrate: to make a way into or through something
