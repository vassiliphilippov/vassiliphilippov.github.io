to simmer up

verb
to gently bring to a simmer
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪm.ɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.ə ʌp / (BrE)
to reach a gentle boiling point or cook a liquid gently at a low temperature, just below boiling

Examples

to simmer up a sauce
Let the sauce simmer up on low heat until it thickens.
She waited for the soup to simmer up before adding the spices.
The chef told us to simmer up the broth slowly for the best flavor.
