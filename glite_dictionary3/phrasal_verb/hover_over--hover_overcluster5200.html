to hover over

verb
stay in one place in the air above
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌvɚ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an object or person) to stay in one place in the air above something or someone, often by moving only slightly.

Examples

hover over the city
A helicopter began to hover over the accident site as rescuers arrived.
I saw a butterfly hover over the blooming flowers in the garden.
The magician made the ball hover over the table, amazing the audience.
