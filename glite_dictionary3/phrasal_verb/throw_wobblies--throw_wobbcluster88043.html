to throw wobblies

verb
have a tantrum; throw a fit (Brit.)
Pronunciation: / θɹoʊ ˈwɑbliz / (AmE), / θrəʊ ˈwɒbliz / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) To have a sudden outburst of anger or a tantrum, often in a childish or exaggerated manner.

Examples

throw wobblies at work
She started to throw wobblies over the smallest inconveniences after a long day at work.
The coach was known to throw wobblies when the team wasn't performing well.
Whenever he can't find his favorite toy, he tends to throw wobblies until someone helps him.

Related Terms

  • synonym – throw a fit: to have an angry outburst; to lose one's temper suddenly
