to see through

verb
persist until completion
Pronunciation: / si ˈθru / (AmE), / siː ˈθruː / (BrE)
to remain with someone or something until it is finished or completed, especially despite difficulties

Examples

to see through a project
He knew it would be difficult, but he was determined to see through his plan to the end.
It's important to see through your commitments and not give up halfway.
She promised to see through the project, no matter how challenging it became.

Related Terms

  • synonym – complete: to finish making or doing something
  • antonym – give up: to stop doing something before it is finished
