to dwindle down

verb
gradually decrease in size or amount
Pronunciation: / ˈdwɪndəl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈdwɪndəl daʊn / (BrE)
to gradually become smaller, fewer, or less important until almost nothing remains

Examples

to dwindle down in size
After months without rain, the river's water level began to dwindle down until it was just a trickle.
Her enthusiasm for the project started to dwindle down as time went by.
My savings have dwindled down to almost nothing since I lost my job.

  • synonym – decrease: to become smaller or make less
  • synonym – dwindle: to diminish gradually in size, amount, or strength
  • antonym – expand: to become larger or more extensive
