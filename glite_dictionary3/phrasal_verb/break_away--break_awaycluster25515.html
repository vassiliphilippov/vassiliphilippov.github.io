to break away

verb
to detach a piece from the whole
Pronunciation: / breɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / breɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to separate or detach a piece from a whole, often by force or accident

Examples

break away a piece
A piece of chocolate will easily break away if you snap it in half.
Part of the cliff began to break away after the heavy rain.
When the statue fell, fragments started to break away from the base.

  • synonym – break off: to remove or become removed by breaking
