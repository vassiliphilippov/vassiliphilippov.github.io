to follow through

verb
complete a swing or motion after hitting a ball
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊ θru / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ θruː / (BrE)
to carry a swinging or striking motion to its natural completion after hitting or releasing a ball, especially in sports

Examples

to follow through a swing
A good golfer always remembers to follow through on their swing.
If you don't follow through, the shot won't have enough power or accuracy.
The coach stressed that every player must follow through after releasing the basketball.
