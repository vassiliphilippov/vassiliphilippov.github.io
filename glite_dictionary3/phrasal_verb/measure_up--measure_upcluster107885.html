to measure up

verb
be good enough; meet a standard
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛʒər ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒə ʌp / (BrE)
to be good enough or as good as expected or required; to meet a standard or expectation

Examples

to measure up to expectations
It's hard for new employees to measure up to the company's high standards.
She wondered if she would ever measure up to her older sister's achievements.
This new product doesn't quite measure up to what we expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fall short: to fail to reach the required standard
  • synonym – live up to: to fulfill expectations or standards
