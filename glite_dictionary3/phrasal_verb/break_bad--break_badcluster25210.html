to break bad

verb
to turn to a life of crime
Pronunciation: / breɪk bæd / (AmE), / breɪk bæd / (BrE)
to become involved in criminal activity or morally questionable behavior, especially after previously leading a law-abiding life

Examples

to break bad after losing job
After losing his job, he started to break bad and became involved with illegal activities.
Many crime dramas depict ordinary people who break bad under pressure.
She never imagined her brother would break bad and get arrested for theft.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reform: to stop engaging in criminal or immoral behavior
