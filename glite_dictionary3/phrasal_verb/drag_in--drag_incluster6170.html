to drag in

verb
involve someone unwillingly
Pronunciation: / dræɡ ɪn / (AmE), / dræɡ ɪn / (BrE)
to involve someone in a situation, argument, or activity when they do not want to be involved or it is not their concern.

Examples

to drag in an outsider
He felt angry that his family had been dragged in to the dispute.
Please don't drag in your personal problems when we're discussing work.
Why must she always drag in irrelevant details during the meeting?
