to close down

verb
stop operating permanently (business, etc.)
Pronunciation: / kloʊz daʊn / (AmE), / kləʊz daʊn / (BrE)
to stop operating permanently, especially for a business or organization

Examples

to close down a store
After twenty years in business, the bookstore will close down next month due to low sales.
The government ordered all non-essential shops to close down during the crisis.
They had to close down their restaurant after failing to renew the lease.
Many small shops were forced to close down after the new mall opened.
The factory will close down at the end of the year due to falling profits.
They had to close down their restaurant when they could no longer afford the rent.

  • antonym – open: to begin or resume operation (of a business, etc.)
