to set in

verb
to begin and become established
Pronunciation: / sɛt ɪn / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn / (BrE)
to begin and become established, especially of something unpleasant or likely to continue

Examples

set in cold weather
As soon as the rain set in, the streets quickly emptied.
Panic began to set in as the deadline approached.
Winter usually sets in around November in this region.
After a few hours of waiting, boredom set in among the children.
As the evening grew colder, the fog began to set in across the fields.
When the reality of her new job set in, she started to feel nervous.

Related Terms

  • synonym – begin: to start or commence
  • antonym – end: to finish or come to a close
  • synonym – kick in: to begin to take effect or operate
