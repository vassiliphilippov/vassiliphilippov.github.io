to fudge together

verb
make or put together carelessly or quickly
Pronunciation: / fʌdʒ təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / fʌdʒ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to produce or assemble something hastily and carelessly, without much attention to detail

Examples

to fudge together a report
The report was clearly fudged together to meet the deadline, with many errors left uncorrected.
They fudged together a plan at the last minute without considering the consequences.
We had to fudge together a meal from whatever we found in the fridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cobble together: to put together roughly or hastily
  • synonym – piece together: to assemble from available parts
