to slop out

verb
empty or clean out dirty liquid or waste
Pronunciation: / slɑp aʊt / (AmE), / slɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to empty a container of dirty liquid or waste, or to clean it by removing such contents

Examples

to slop out a bucket
Please slop out the tray before washing it in the sink.
Prisoners were required to slop out their cell buckets every morning.
The janitor had to slop out the bucket at the end of his shift.

Related Terms

  • synonym – empty out: to remove all contents from a container or place
