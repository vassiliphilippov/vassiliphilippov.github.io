to prepare for backlash

verb
get ready for negative reactions
Pronunciation: / prɪˈpɛr fər ˈbækˌlæʃ / (AmE), / prɪˈpeə fə ˈbæklæʃ / (BrE)
to get ready to face strong negative reactions or criticism, especially from the public or a group

Examples

to prepare for backlash after changes
After announcing the new policy, the company had to prepare for backlash from customers.
Politicians often prepare for backlash when making controversial reforms.
The director prepared for backlash by holding a press briefing before releasing the film.
