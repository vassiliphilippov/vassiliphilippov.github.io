to bolt out

verb
leave suddenly and quickly
Pronunciation: / boʊlt aʊt / (AmE), / bəʊlt aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and quickly, often in a rush or out of surprise or fear

Examples

to bolt out the door
As soon as the alarm rang, everyone bolted out of the building.
He bolted out of the meeting room without saying a word to anyone.
The cat bolted out the open door when it heard the loud noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dash out: to leave quickly or suddenly
  • degree higher – flee: run away quickly from a place or situation of danger
  • degree lower – slip away: leave quietly or unobtrusively
