to clean up

verb
make oneself clean and presentable
Pronunciation: / klin ʌp / (AmE), / kliːn ʌp / (BrE)
to make oneself clean, neat, and presentable, especially by washing or grooming

Examples

to clean up after work
After working in the garden, he took a shower to clean up.
She needed to clean up before the important interview.
You should clean up a bit before dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym – freshen up: to quickly wash or groom oneself to feel clean and refreshed
  • antonym – mess up: to become or make someone dirty or disheveled
  • synonym – tidy up: to make something neat or organized, often used for spaces or objects rather than people
