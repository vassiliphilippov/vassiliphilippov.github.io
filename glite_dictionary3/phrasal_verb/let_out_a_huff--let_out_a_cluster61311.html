to let out a huff

verb
audibly express annoyance with a breath
Pronunciation: / lɛt aʊt ə hʌf / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə hʌf / (BrE)
to audibly express frustration, annoyance, or impatience, typically by emitting a forceful breath

Examples

let out a huff in anger
After hearing the news, she let out a huff and crossed her arms.
Ben let out a huff when he realized he forgot his keys again.
The teacher smiled as the students let out a huff at the surprise assignment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sigh: to emit a long, deep breath as an audible sign of tiredness, frustration, or relief
