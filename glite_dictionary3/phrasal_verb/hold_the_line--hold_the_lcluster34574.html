to hold the line

verb
maintain a position without yielding
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ðə laɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to refuse to change a position, stance, or limit, especially in the face of pressure

Examples

hold the line against pressure
Despite mounting pressure, the negotiators managed to hold the line during all discussions.
During the crisis, leaders asked everyone to hold the line and remain calm.
The government pledged to hold the line on spending despite rising costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give ground: yield or compromise on a position
  • synonym – stand firm: refuse to change one's decision or position
