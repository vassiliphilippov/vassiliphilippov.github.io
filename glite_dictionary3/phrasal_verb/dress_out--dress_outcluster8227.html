to dress out

verb
kill and prepare an animal for eating
Pronunciation: / drɛs aʊt / (AmE), / drɛs aʊt / (BrE)
to kill and prepare (an animal) for market or for eating by removing the entrails, cleaning, and sometimes cutting up the carcass

Examples

to dress out a deer
After hunting season, many farmers dress out deer to supply meat for the winter.
The chef will dress out the chicken before preparing the dish.
They learned how to dress out a turkey as part of the survival course.

  • synonym – butcher: to kill and cut up an animal for food
