to drop off

verb
to fall from a place or become detached
Pronunciation: / drɑp ɑf / (AmE), / drɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to fall from a position or place; to become detached from where something was held or placed.

Examples

drop off the wall
A picture frame dropped off the wall and broke during the storm.
Books sometimes drop off the shelf if it's packed too tightly.
The sign kept dropping off the door whenever the wind blew.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fall off: to become detached and fall from a higher place
