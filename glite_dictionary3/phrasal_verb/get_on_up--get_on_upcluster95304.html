to get on up

verb
rise to feet; begin activity or celebration
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɑn ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ʌp / (BrE)
to rise to one's feet or begin to be active, especially in order to celebrate or participate

Examples

to get on up and dance
Every morning my grandmother would get on up and make breakfast for the whole family.
If you want to win, you need to get on up and give it your best shot.
When the band started to play, the crowd couldn't help but get on up and dance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stand up: to rise from a sitting or lying position to a standing position
