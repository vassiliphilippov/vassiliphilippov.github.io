to flip out

verb
lose control emotionally
Pronunciation: / flɪp aʊt / (AmE), / flɪp aʊt / (BrE)
To lose control of one's emotions; to become extremely angry, upset, or excited.

Examples

flip out in anger
He completely flipped out when he saw the mess in the kitchen.
I almost flipped out when I realized I had lost my passport.
Try not to flip out if they arrive late—they probably got stuck in traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm down: to become calmer and less agitated
  • synonym – freak out: to become very anxious, upset, or angry
