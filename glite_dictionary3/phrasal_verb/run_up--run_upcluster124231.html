to run up

verb
raise with ropes and pulleys
Pronunciation: / rʌn ʌp / (AmE), / rʌn ʌp / (BrE)
to raise (something) to a higher position by using ropes and pulleys, typically a flag or sail

Examples

to run up a flag
At sunrise, the sailors ran up the national flag on the main mast.
During the ceremony, the scout leader ran up the banner while everyone saluted.
They quickly run up the sails to catch the morning breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hoist: to raise something, especially by means of ropes and pulleys
