to make out

verb
manage or succeed with limited means
Pronunciation: / meɪk aʊt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to manage, succeed, or get by, especially in difficult circumstances

Examples

to make out in life
Even though times were tough, they managed to make out with what little they had.

We only had a few supplies left, but we tried to make out as best we could.
Despite the challenges, he managed to make out during the tough times.
How are you making out with your new responsibilities at work?
She hasn't been here long, but she's making out just fine in her new role.
