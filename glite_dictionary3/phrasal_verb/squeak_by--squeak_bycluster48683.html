to squeak by

verb
manage to survive or succeed by a narrow margin
Pronunciation: / skwik baɪ / (AmE), / skwiːk baɪ / (BrE)
to barely succeed, survive, or achieve something; to manage with only just enough

Examples

to squeak by with little money
I didn’t have much money in college, but I managed to squeak by.
They're just squeaking by each month on his salary.
With only one vote to spare, the bill squeaked by in the Senate.
I didn't have enough money for rent last month, but I somehow squeaked by.
She was worried about the exam but managed to squeak by with a passing grade.
The underdog team squeaked by their rivals in the final minutes of the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fail: to not succeed in achieving a goal or passing a standard
  • synonym – get by: to manage to cope or survive with what one has
  • synonym – scrape by: to manage to survive or succeed with minimal resources
