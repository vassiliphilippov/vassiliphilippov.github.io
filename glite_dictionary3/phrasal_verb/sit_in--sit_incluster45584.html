to sit in

verb
participate in a protest by occupying a place
Pronunciation: / sɪt ɪn / (AmE), / sɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to take part in a form of protest in which people occupy a place as an act of civil disobedience, usually to demand change

Examples

to sit in during protest
Activists sat in overnight to protest racial segregation at local restaurants.
Several students decided to sit in at the university administration office to demand tuition reform.
Workers will sit in at the factory until management addresses their concerns.

  • synonym – protest: an organized public demonstration expressing objection
