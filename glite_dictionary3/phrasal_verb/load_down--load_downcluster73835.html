to load down

verb
to burden with a heavy load
Pronunciation: / loʊd daʊn / (AmE), / ləʊd daʊn / (BrE)
to burden or weigh someone or something with a heavy load or excessive items, often making movement more difficult

Examples

to load down a truck
Moving day was exhausting; we were loaded down with boxes and furniture.
She didn't want to load down her backpack with unnecessary items for the trip.
The hikers were loaded down with camping equipment as they made their way up the mountain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burden: to load heavily or weigh down
  • antonym – unburden: to relieve of a load or burden
