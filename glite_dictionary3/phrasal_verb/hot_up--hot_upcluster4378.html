to hot up

verb
to become or make more intense
Pronunciation: / hɑt ʌp / (AmE), / hɒt ʌp / (BrE)
(chiefly British) To become or make more intense, active, or exciting.

Examples

hot up negotiations
Negotiations have hotted up in the last few days with both sides eager to reach a deal.
The debate began to hot up as the election drew closer.
Things really hot up in the kitchen during the dinner rush.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cool off: to become less intense or heated
  • synonym – intensify: to make or become more intense
