to resonate through

verb
to fill a space with sound
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛzəˌneɪt θru / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪt θruː / (BrE)
(of a sound) to fill a space and be clearly heard throughout it, often with strength or a lasting effect.

Examples

resonate through the hall
Her laughter would resonate through the house, making everyone smile.
Music from the cathedral bells resonated through the quiet streets on Sunday morning.
The thunder seemed to resonate through the valley, echoing off the mountainsides.
