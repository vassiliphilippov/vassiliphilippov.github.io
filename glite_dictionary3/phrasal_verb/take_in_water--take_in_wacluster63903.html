to take in water

verb
(of a vessel) to begin leaking water
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
(of a boat or ship) to leak or admit water, especially through the hull or bilge.

Examples

take in water during a storm
During the storm, the old fishing boat began to take in water rapidly.
If the hull is damaged, the ship could take in water and eventually sink.
The crew worked quickly to plug the hole when they realized the yacht was taking in water.
