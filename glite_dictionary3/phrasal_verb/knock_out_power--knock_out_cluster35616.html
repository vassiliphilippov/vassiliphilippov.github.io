to knock out power

verb
to cause a power outage
Pronunciation: / nɑk aʊt ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / nɒk aʊt ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to cause a loss of electricity supply, typically by damaging electrical lines or equipment

Examples

knock out power during a storm
A fallen tree knocked out power to hundreds of homes last night.
Contractors accidentally knocked out power while working on the main line.
Heavy winds can easily knock out power in rural areas.
