to fall in line

verb
conform or agree with a position or standard
Pronunciation: / fɔl ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
to conform to rules, standards, or expectations, especially after initial reluctance

Examples

to fall in line with rules
After much debate, the committee members finally fell in line with the proposal.
It took some convincing, but he agreed to fall in line with the new regulations.
Most employees eventually fall in line with the company’s policies.
After some initial hesitation, the new employees fell in line with the company's dress code.
If you don't fall in line soon, you may face disciplinary action.
The government expects citizens to fall in line with its new regulations.
