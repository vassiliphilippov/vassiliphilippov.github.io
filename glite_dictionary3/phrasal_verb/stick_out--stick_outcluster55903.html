to stick out

verb
extend or project outward
Pronunciation: / stɪk aʊt / (AmE), / stɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to extend outward or project beyond a surface or boundary

Examples

stick out from a wall
A broken branch was sticking out from the trunk of the tree.
A piece of wire began to stick out from the fence.
Her elbows stuck out awkwardly as she squeezed through the gap.

Related Terms

  • synonym – protrude: to extend beyond or above a surface
  • antonym – recede: to move back or withdraw
