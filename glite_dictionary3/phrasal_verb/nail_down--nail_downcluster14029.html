to nail down

verb
secure a position or opportunity
Pronunciation: / neɪl daʊn / (AmE), / neɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to succeed in obtaining or securing a position, deal, or opportunity firmly

Examples

to nail down a job
After several interviews, she finally nailed down the job she wanted.
He worked hard to nail down a spot on the national soccer team.
It took weeks of negotiations before the company nailed down the lucrative contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose: to fail to obtain or secure something
  • synonym – secure: to obtain or achieve something, especially after effort
