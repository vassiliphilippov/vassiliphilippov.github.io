to chew up

verb
use up resources quickly
Pronunciation: / t\u0283u \u028cp / (AmE), / t\u0283u\u02d0 \u028cp / (BrE)
to use up or consume a resource (such as time, data, or computer memory) quickly or wastefully, often causing delays or problems.

Examples

chew up data
The streaming app will chew up your data plan if you watch in HD.
Running that script constantly began to chew up the server's memory, causing slowdowns for users.
The new graphics driver chewed up hours of debugging before we found the real issue.
