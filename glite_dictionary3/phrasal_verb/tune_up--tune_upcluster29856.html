to tune up

verb
adjust for better functioning
Pronunciation: / tun ʌp /; / tuːn ʌp / (AmE), / tjuːn ʌp / (BrE)
to adjust or service a machine or instrument so that it works better or more efficiently

Examples

to tune up a car
Before the road trip, I need to tune up my car to make sure it's running smoothly.
She always tunes up her guitar before a performance to get the best sound.
The technician will tune up the air conditioner before the summer heat sets in.
It's important to tune up your bike before a long ride.
The mechanic will tune up your car to ensure it runs smoothly.
The orchestra gathered on stage to tune up their instruments before the concert.

  • synonym – adjust: to change something slightly to improve its function or fit
  • synonym – calibrate: to set or correct the settings of an instrument
  • antonym – tune down: to lower the pitch or intensity, especially in music
