to snack on

verb
eat small amounts of food
Pronunciation: / snæk ɑn / (AmE), / snæk ɒn / (BrE)
to eat small amounts of a particular food, often between regular meals

Examples

to snack on chips
Children sometimes snack on chips after school instead of eating a healthy meal.
I often snack on nuts when I'm working late at night.
She tries to snack on vegetables to maintain her diet.
