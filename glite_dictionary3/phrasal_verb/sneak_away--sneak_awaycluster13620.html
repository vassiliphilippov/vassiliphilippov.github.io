to sneak away

verb
leave secretly and quietly
Pronunciation: / sniːk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sniːk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly and secretly in order to avoid being noticed

Examples

sneak away from a party
As the meeting dragged on, Sarah managed to sneak away for a coffee break.
He sneaked away before his parents noticed he was gone.
I tried to sneak away from the noisy party without attracting anyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slip away: to leave quietly and without being noticed
  • antonym – sneak in: to enter a place quietly and secretly
