to take hold

verb
assume control; become dominant
Pronunciation: / teɪk hoʊld / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld / (BrE)
to assume control or to become dominant in a situation or process

Examples

take hold of a situation
A sense of calm took hold as she entered the room.
As soon as the new manager arrived, her leadership style took hold in the office.
When fear began to take hold, the team lost its focus.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose control: to cease having control over something
  • synonym – seize control: to take control quickly or forcefully
