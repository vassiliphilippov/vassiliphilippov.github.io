to leg it

verb
run or walk quickly (informal, British)
Pronunciation: / lɛɡ ɪt / (AmE), / lɛɡ ɪt / (BrE)
(informal, British) to walk or run quickly, especially in order to escape or get somewhere fast.

Examples

to leg it down the street
If we leg it now, we’ll make it before the rain starts.
The kids saw the police and decided to leg it down the alley.
We had to leg it the last mile to catch the train.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hoof it: to walk, especially briskly or over a distance
  • degree higher – run: to move rapidly on foot
