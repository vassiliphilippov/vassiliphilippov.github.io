to take account

verb
be fully aware; give due consideration
Pronunciation: / teɪk əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / teɪk əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
to be fully aware of something or to realize it completely; to give due consideration to something

Examples

take account of differences
Good teachers always take account of their students' different learning styles.
She failed to take account of the new regulations, and her proposal was rejected.
We must take account of all possible risks before proceeding with the plan.

Related Terms

  • synonym – consider: think carefully about something
  • antonym – ignore: fail to consider or pay attention to
