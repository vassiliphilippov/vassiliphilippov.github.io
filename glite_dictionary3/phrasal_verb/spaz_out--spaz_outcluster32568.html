to spaz out

verb
lose control emotionally
Pronunciation: / spæz aʊt / (AmE), / spæz aʊt / (BrE)
to become overly emotional or lose control, often in an exaggerated or frantic way; to react with excessive excitement, anger, fear, or anxiety

Examples

to spaz out over a mistake
He spazzed out when he discovered the mistake in his project.
She tends to spaz out whenever she gets nervous before a big performance.
Try not to spaz out if things don't go as planned.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm down: to become less agitated or emotional; to regain composure
  • synonym – freak out: to become very agitated or lose control emotionally
