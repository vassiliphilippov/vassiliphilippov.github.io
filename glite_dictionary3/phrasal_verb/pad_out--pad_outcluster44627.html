to pad out

verb
add extra or unnecessary material
Pronunciation: / pæd aʊt / (AmE), / pæd aʊt / (BrE)
to add unnecessary or extra material to something in order to make it longer or fuller

Examples

to pad out an essay
He realized he shouldn't pad out his essays with irrelevant information.
Some speakers pad out their presentations with anecdotes to fill the allotted time.
The novelist had to pad out the story to meet the publisher's minimum word count.

Related Terms

  • synonym – embellish: to make something more interesting by adding extra details, sometimes unnecessary
  • synonym – inflate: to increase something, often artificially, by adding extra material
