to sell out

verb
abandon principles for gain
Pronunciation: / sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to abandon or betray your principles, ideals, or integrity for personal gain or advantage

Examples

to sell out for money
Many fans accused the band of having sold out when they switched to a more commercial sound.
Politicians often face criticism if people feel they have sold out to powerful lobbyists.
She vowed never to sell out her beliefs for money.
He promised never to sell out his beliefs, no matter how tempting the offer.
Many fans accused the artist of selling out after she signed a major label deal.
Politicians are often criticized for selling out when they put personal ambition above public interest.

  • synonym – compromise: to accept standards that are lower than is desirable
