to stand for

verb
tolerate or accept (something)
Pronunciation: / stænd fər / (AmE), / stænd fə / (BrE)
to tolerate or accept something unpleasant or wrong without objection

Examples

stand for bad behavior
I refuse to stand for such unfair treatment in the workplace.
She won't stand for anyone lying to her.
The teacher made it clear that she would not stand for bullying of any kind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accept: to receive or agree to something willingly
  • antonym – oppose: to actively resist something; not tolerate
  • synonym – put up with: to tolerate or accept something you do not like
  • synonym – tolerate: to allow something to continue even if you do not agree with it
