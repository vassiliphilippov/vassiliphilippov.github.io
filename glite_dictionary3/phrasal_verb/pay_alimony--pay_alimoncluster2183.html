to pay alimony

verb
make spousal support payments after divorce
Pronunciation: / peɪ ˈælɪˌmoʊni / (AmE), / peɪ ˈælɪməni / (BrE)
to make regular payments to a former spouse for their financial support after a divorce or separation, as required by law or agreement.

Examples

to pay alimony after divorce
After their divorce, she refused to pay alimony until the settlement was finalized.
He is required by the court to pay alimony every month.
Many people struggle financially when they have to pay alimony for several years.
