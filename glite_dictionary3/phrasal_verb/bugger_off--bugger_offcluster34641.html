to bugger off

verb
tell someone rudely to go away
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌɡər ˈɔf / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡə ˈɒf / (BrE)
(informal, British) used to tell someone to go away immediately, often in a rude or annoyed way.

Examples

to bugger off quickly
He told me to bugger off when I asked him to pay his share.
If you don't like it here, just bugger off and stop complaining.
She shouted at the reporters to bugger off and let her be.

Related Terms

  • synonym – go away: to leave a place
  • synonym – scram: to leave quickly, especially when told to do so
