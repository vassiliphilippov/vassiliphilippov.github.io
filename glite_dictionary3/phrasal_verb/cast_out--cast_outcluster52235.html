to cast out

verb
to expel or banish
Pronunciation: / kæst aʊt / (AmE), / kɑːst aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place or group, often because they are not wanted

Examples

cast out a member
After years of tension, he was finally cast out of the community.
The corrupt official was cast out from public office following the scandal.
The tribe decided to cast out those who broke their laws.
Anyone who broke the rules would be cast out of the village.
He felt like an outsider, cast out by his former friends after the argument.
The group cast out members who do not adhere to their strict code.

Related Terms

  • antonym – accept: to receive or welcome someone into a group
  • synonym – banish: to send someone away as punishment
  • synonym – exclude: to prevent someone from taking part in something
  • synonym – expel: to force someone to leave a place
