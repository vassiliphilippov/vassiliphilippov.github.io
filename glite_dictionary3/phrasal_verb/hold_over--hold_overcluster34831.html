to hold over

verb
intimidate or control by a threat
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to use a threat or information to intimidate or have power over someone

Examples

to hold over a secret
He can't hold over that mistake forever; it's time to move on.
She threatened to hold over the secret unless he complied.
The manager tried to hold over the staff by hinting at possible layoffs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intimidate: to frighten or threaten someone in order to control them
