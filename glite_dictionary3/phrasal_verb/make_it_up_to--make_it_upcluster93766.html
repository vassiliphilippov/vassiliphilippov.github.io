to make it up to

verb
compensate for a mistake or wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / meɪk ɪt ʌp tu / (AmE), / meɪk ɪt ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
to do something good for someone in order to show that you are sorry for something you have done or failed to do

Examples

make it up to a friend
After forgetting our anniversary, he tried to make it up to me with flowers and dinner.
I know I hurt your feelings, but I promise I'll make it up to you.
She offered to babysit my kids to make it up to me for being late.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compensate: to make amends for loss or damage
  • antonym – forgive: to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense
