to fend off

verb
to defend against or prevent something undesirable
Pronunciation: / fɛnd ɑf / (AmE), / fɛnd ɒf / (BrE)
to defend oneself against or prevent someone or something undesirable from affecting or harming you

Examples

to fend off an attack
He managed to fend off criticism by clearly explaining his decision.
She tried to fend off awkward questions from the reporters.
The thick sweater helped him fend off the cold during the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – avert: to prevent something bad from happening
  • antonym – invite: to cause or bring on something undesired
  • synonym – ward off: to prevent something unpleasant from harming or affecting you
