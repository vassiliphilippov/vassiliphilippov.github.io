to fall behind

verb
move back; retreat
Pronunciation: / fɔl bəˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to move back or retreat, especially in a situation where others are advancing or progressing

Examples

to fall behind the group
After hours of hiking, several members had to fall behind and rest.
The troops were forced to fall behind as the enemy advanced.
When the competition intensified, he decided to fall behind rather than take unnecessary risks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advance: to move forward, especially towards the enemy or a goal
  • synonym – retreat: to move away from the enemy or from a position, especially to avoid danger
