to eat up

verb
finish all the food
Pronunciation: / it ʌp / (AmE), / iːt ʌp / (BrE)
to eat all the food on your plate or finish everything that is served

Examples

to eat up your dinner
He quickly ate up his breakfast so he wouldn't be late for work.
Please eat up your vegetables before you leave the table.
The children ate up every last crumb of cake at the party.
Before we leave, make sure you eat up your breakfast.
Please eat up your dinner so we can have dessert.
The children quickly ate up all the cookies on the plate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – devour: to eat something quickly and eagerly
  • antonym – pick at: to eat food slowly or without enthusiasm
  • synonym – polish off: to finish or consume something completely, especially food
