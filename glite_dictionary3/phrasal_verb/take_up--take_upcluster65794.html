to take up

verb
occupy time or space
Pronunciation: / teɪk ʌp / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to use or occupy a particular amount of time, space, or attention

Examples

take up time
Her hobbies take up a large part of her free time.
The boxes take up too much space in the garage.
This project will take up most of my weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fill: to make or become full; to occupy space
  • synonym – occupy: to take up or fill (space, time, etc.)
  • antonym – save: to avoid using time or space, the opposite of take up
