to black out

verb
lose consciousness suddenly
Pronunciation: / blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness, especially suddenly and temporarily, often as a result of trauma or shock.

Examples

to black out suddenly
He was so dehydrated after the race that he suddenly blacked out on the pavement.
If you stand up too quickly, you might black out for a moment.
She blacked out for a few seconds after hitting her head on the door frame.

Related Terms

  • antonym – come to: to regain consciousness
  • synonym – faint: to lose consciousness for a short time
  • synonym – pass out: to become unconscious unexpectedly
