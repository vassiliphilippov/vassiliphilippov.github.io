to be salty

verb
be angry or resentful (informal)
Pronunciation: / bi ˈsɔlti / (AmE), / bi ˈsɒlti / (BrE)
To be angry, upset, or resentful, especially in a petty or grudging way.

Examples

be salty after losing
Don't be salty just because you lost the game.
He was clearly being salty after his idea was rejected by the team.
She tends to be salty whenever someone corrects her mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be bitter: To be resentful or cynical, especially due to disappointment or defeat
  • antonym – be calm: To remain peaceful and not show anger or resentment
