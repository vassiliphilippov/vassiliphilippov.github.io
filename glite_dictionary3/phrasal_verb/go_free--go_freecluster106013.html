to go free

verb
be released from confinement or obligation
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ friː / (AmE), / ɡəʊ friː / (BrE)
to be released from confinement or control; to be allowed to leave or be at liberty, especially after being detained or accused

Examples

go free after prison
After serving five years in prison, the judge decided to let him go free.
If the prisoners are found innocent, they will go free immediately.
The hostages were finally allowed to go free after negotiations.
