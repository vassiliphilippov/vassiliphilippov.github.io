to march on

verb
to move or progress forward, often steadily
Pronunciation: / mɑrtʃ ɑn / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue to move forward, especially in a steady or determined way; to progress, often used metaphorically for the passage of time or inevitable progress.

Examples

to march on the city
Despite their losses, the soldiers continued to march on towards the city.
No matter what happens in our lives, time will always march on.
The project must march on despite the recent setbacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – advance: to move forward, especially in a purposeful way
  • synonym – proceed: to move forward or continue
