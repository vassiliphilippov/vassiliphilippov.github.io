to jack in

verb
connect to a computer system or network
Pronunciation: / dʒæk ɪn / (AmE), / dʒæk ɪn / (BrE)
to connect to a computer system or network, especially by plugging in or logging on

Examples

to jack in to a network
Before you can access your files remotely, you need to jack in to the company server.
She jacked in just before the training session started, ensuring her updates were complete.
Technicians routinely jack in to monitor system performance and troubleshoot issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – jack out: to disconnect from a computer system or network
  • synonym – log on: to enter a computer system or network by providing credentials
