to bring on

verb
release or launch something
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ ɑn / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɒn / (BrE)
to introduce or release (a product, book, play, etc.) to the public

Examples

to bring on a new product
After months of work, the author was ready to bring on her debut novel.
The company plans to bring on a new smartphone model next year.
The theater will bring on a fresh production to attract new audiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bring out: to produce and launch something (e.g., a book, product)
