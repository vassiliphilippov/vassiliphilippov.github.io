to break out

verb
develop skin spots or rash
Pronunciation: / breɪk aʊt / (AmE), / breɪk aʊt / (BrE)
(of skin) to develop spots, a rash, or other visible skin problems, often suddenly or unexpectedly.

Examples

break out in spots
After running, he suddenly broke out in hives all over his arms.
Her face breaks out every spring due to allergies.
I often break out in a rash after eating shellfish.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear up: (of skin problems) to disappear or heal
  • synonym – erupt: (of the skin) to burst out or break through suddenly
