to sit in for

verb
temporarily substitute for someone
Pronunciation: / sɪt ɪn fɔr / (AmE), / sɪt ɪn fə / (BrE)
to take the place of someone temporarily, especially by performing their duties or role

Examples

to sit in for a colleague
Could you sit in for me at tomorrow’s team meeting?
Karen will sit in for Tom while he recovers from surgery.
The producer asked if I could sit in for the late-night show host.
