to chalk up to

verb
attribute something to a cause
Pronunciation: / tʃɑk ʌp tu / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to consider or regard something as being caused by a particular reason

Examples

chalk up to bad luck
Many people chalk their success up to a mixture of talent and luck.
She wasn't angry; she simply chalked his mistake up to inexperience.
The coach chalked the team's loss up to a lack of preparation.
