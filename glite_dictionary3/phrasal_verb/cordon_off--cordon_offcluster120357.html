to cordon off

verb
close off an area with barriers or tape
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdən ɔf / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdən ɒf / (BrE)
to prevent people from entering or leaving an area by placing a line of police, barriers, or tape around it

Examples

police cordon off a street
Firefighters cordoned off the building to keep bystanders at a safe distance.
Officials cordoned off the park during the investigation.
The police quickly cordoned off the street after the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym – block off: to close something so that people or things cannot pass through
  • synonym – seal off: to prevent people from entering or leaving an area, especially to stop the spread of something dangerous
