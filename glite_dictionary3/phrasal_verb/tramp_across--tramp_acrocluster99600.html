to tramp across

verb
walk heavily or purposefully across
Pronunciation: / træmp əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / træmp əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to walk heavily or purposefully across a place, especially over a large area or distance

Examples

to tramp across a field
The soldiers tramped across the bridge in time with the beat of the drum.
Tourists love to tramp across the ancient ruins, exploring every corner with interest.
We tramped across the muddy fields, our boots sticking with every step.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stride: to walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction
  • synonym – trudge: to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically because of exhaustion
