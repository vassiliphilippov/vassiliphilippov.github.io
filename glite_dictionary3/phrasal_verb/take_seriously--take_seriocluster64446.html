to take seriously

verb
to treat as important
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈsɪriəsli / (AmE), / teɪk ˈsɪəriəsli / (BrE)
to consider something important or worthy of attention; to treat with due respect or concern

Examples

take seriously a warning
It's important to take seriously the responsibilities of your new job.
Some people find it hard to take seriously politicians' promises.
They didn't take seriously the warnings about the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disregard: to pay no attention to; to ignore or treat as unimportant
