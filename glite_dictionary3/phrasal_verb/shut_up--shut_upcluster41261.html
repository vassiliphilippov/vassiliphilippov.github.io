to shut up

verb
command to stop speaking
Pronunciation: / ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to tell someone to stop talking or to be quiet, often abruptly or rudely

Examples

to shut up someone
He couldn't help but shut up when his boss walked into the room.
She told her noisy brother to shut up during the movie.
The teacher asked the class to shut up so the lesson could begin.
Could you please shut up for a moment so I can concentrate?
Even after being asked to shut up, he continued to complain.
She told her little brother to shut up during the movie.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be quiet: to stop making noise or talking
  • synonym – silence: to make someone or something quiet
  • antonym – speak: to say words; to talk
