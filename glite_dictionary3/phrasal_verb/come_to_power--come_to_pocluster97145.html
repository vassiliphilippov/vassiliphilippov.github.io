to come to power

verb
to assume authority or control
Pronunciation: / kʌm tə ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / kʌm tə ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to assume control or authority, especially as a new government or leader

Examples

come to power after election
After years of struggle, the opposition finally came to power in the last election.
Many citizens worry about what policies the party will implement when they come to power.
Reforms were promised by the leaders as soon as they came to power.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose power: to cease to have authority or control, especially in politics
  • synonym – take office: to begin a formal position of authority, especially in government
