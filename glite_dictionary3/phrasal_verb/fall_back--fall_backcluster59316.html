to fall back

verb
move or lag behind in progress or movement
Pronunciation: / fɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move or lag behind others in movement, progress, or development; to lose ground relative to others.

Examples

to fall back in a race
As the runners approached the final lap, two competitors began to fall back.
Technological advances mean that companies can fall back quickly if they do not innovate.
You must not fall back in your studies if you want to graduate on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – catch up: to reach or come level with someone who is ahead
  • synonym – fall behind: to fail to keep pace with others or a schedule
