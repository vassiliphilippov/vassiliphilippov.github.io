force out

noun
baseball play where runner is put out by force
Pronunciation: / fɔrs aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːs aʊt / (BrE)
An act in baseball where a base runner is put out by a fielder holding the ball and touching the base the runner is forced to advance to before the runner arrives.

Examples

force out at second base
He was disappointed to be retired on a force out at third.
The inning ended with a close force out at second base.
The shortstop made a quick throw to second base for the force out.
