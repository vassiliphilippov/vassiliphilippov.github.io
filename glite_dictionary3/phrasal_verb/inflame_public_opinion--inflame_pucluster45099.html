to inflame public opinion

verb
provoke strong public feelings
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪm ˌpʌblɪk əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪm ˌpʌblɪk əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
to provoke or intensify strong feelings and reactions among the general public, especially anger or outrage

Examples

to inflame public opinion against leaders
The documentary inflamed public opinion by exposing government corruption.
The politician's speech was designed to inflame public opinion against the new tax bill.
Unverified rumors on social media can easily inflame public opinion and lead to unrest.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm public opinion: to reduce public anger or anxiety
  • synonym – sway public opinion: to influence the views or attitudes of the public
