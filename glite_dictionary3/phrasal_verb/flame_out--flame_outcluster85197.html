to flame out

verb
to fail or lose momentum suddenly
Pronunciation: / fleɪm aʊt / (AmE), / fleɪm aʊt / (BrE)
to fail or come to an end suddenly and dramatically, especially after a promising start; to lose energy or interest suddenly

Examples

to flame out after success
If you push yourself too hard, you might flame out before reaching your goals.
Many athletes flame out early in their careers due to injuries or burnout.
The promising new tech company flamed out after just one year in business.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burn out: to lose effectiveness, energy, or enthusiasm due to stress or overwork
