to skate over

verb
address something superficially or briefly
Pronunciation: / skeɪt ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / skeɪt ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to deal with something quickly and superficially, without examining or addressing it in detail

Examples

skate over a problem
During the meeting, he skated over the budget cuts, hoping no one would notice.
She tends to skate over her mistakes instead of admitting them fully.
The report skated over the most controversial issues, leaving important questions unanswered.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gloss over: to treat or describe something as less serious or important than it really is
