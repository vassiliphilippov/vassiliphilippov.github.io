to turn out

verb
expel someone from a place
Pronunciation: / tɝn aʊt / (AmE), / tɜːn aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place or to expel someone from somewhere

Examples

turn out unwanted guests
At closing time, the security staff began to turn out the remaining patrons.
During the protest, police were called to turn out those who refused to leave the building.
The landlord had to turn out the tenants for not paying rent.

  • synonym – exclude: deny (someone) access to a place, group, or privilege
