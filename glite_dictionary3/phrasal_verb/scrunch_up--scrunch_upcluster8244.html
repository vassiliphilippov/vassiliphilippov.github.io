to scrunch up

verb
to squeeze or wrinkle up
Pronunciation: / skrʌntʃ ʌp / (AmE), / skrʌntʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to squeeze, fold, or contract something so that it becomes wrinkled or compacted

Examples

to scrunch up paper
He scrunched up the piece of paper before throwing it away.
She scrunched up the letter and tossed it in the bin.
The children scrunched up their faces when they tasted the sour candy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crumple: to crush something so that it becomes wrinkled or creased
  • antonym – smooth out: to make a surface flat or free from wrinkles
