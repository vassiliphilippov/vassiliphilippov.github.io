to take the point

verb
understand and accept someone's argument
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to understand and accept the significance of someone's argument or remark, even if you may not agree

Examples

to take the point in a discussion
He paused during the debate to acknowledge that he took the point raised by his opponent.
While I disagree, I do take the point you are making about cost.
You don't have to accept my decision, but I hope you take the point.
