to neutralize the taste

verb
make a taste less strong by balancing
Pronunciation: / ˈn(j)uːtrəˌlaɪz ðə teɪst / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz ðə teɪst / (BrE)
to make a flavor less strong or unpleasant by adding another ingredient that balances or masks it

Examples

to neutralize the taste with lemon
A squeeze of lemon can neutralize the taste of fish in many recipes.
He added a bit of honey to neutralize the taste of the sour medicine.
Sometimes, a pinch of salt is enough to neutralize the taste of bitterness in coffee.
