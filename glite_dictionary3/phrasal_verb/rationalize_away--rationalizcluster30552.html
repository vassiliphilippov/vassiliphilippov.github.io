to rationalize away

verb
explain away, dismiss with logic
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃ.ə.nəl.aɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ə.nə.laɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to attempt to explain or justify something, especially supernatural or unusual events, with logical or natural reasons, often to dismiss other explanations

Examples

to rationalize away a problem
He tried to rationalize away her sudden recovery as coincidence rather than a miracle.
It's easy to rationalize away mysterious noises in the house as just the pipes creaking.
Skeptics often rationalize away reports of ghosts with explanations based on physics or psychology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – explain away: to dismiss something by giving an explanation that minimizes its importance or plausibility
