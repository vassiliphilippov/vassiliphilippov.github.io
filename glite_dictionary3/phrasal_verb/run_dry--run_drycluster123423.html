to run dry

verb
become empty of water or liquid
Pronunciation: / rʌn ˈdraɪ / (AmE), / rʌn ˈdraɪ / (BrE)
(of a river, well, etc.) to become empty of water or liquid; to stop having any more water or liquid.

Examples

run dry in a river
If your inspiration has run dry, take a break and return later.
In the hottest months, the small creek would often run dry by August.
We must conserve water or our reservoirs will run dry in a drought.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dry up: to become completely dry or to make something dry by removing its water
