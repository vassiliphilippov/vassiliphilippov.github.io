to break down the stovepipe

verb
remove barriers to communication
Pronunciation: / breɪk daʊn ðə ˈstoʊvpaɪp / (AmE), / breɪk daʊn ðə ˈstəʊvpaɪp / (BrE)
to eliminate barriers to communication, especially between departments or groups within an organization

Examples

to break down the stovepipe between teams
After hiring a new director, the organization started to break down the stovepipe between development and marketing.
The management team hopes to break down the stovepipe and encourage collaboration across departments.
To innovate successfully, companies must break down the stovepipe mentality that keeps teams isolated.
