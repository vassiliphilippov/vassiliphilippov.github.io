to rush out

verb
quickly release or publish
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ aʊt / (AmE), / rʌʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something available quickly, especially a product, update, or statement, often in response to an event or problem.

Examples

rush out a product
The tech firm promised to rush out a security patch within hours of the breach.
They rushed out a brief press release before competitors announced similar features.
Every time a problem appears, the company rushes out fixes to reassure customers quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – issue: to formally distribute or publish something, such as a statement or update
  • synonym – release: to make something available to the public or users
