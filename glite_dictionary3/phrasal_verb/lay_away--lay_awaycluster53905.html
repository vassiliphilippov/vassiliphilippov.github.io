to lay away

verb
to save or put aside for future use
Pronunciation: / leɪ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / leɪ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to save or set aside something, especially money or goods, for use or purchase at a future time

Examples

to lay away money
Every month, she tries to lay away a portion of her salary for emergencies.
It's wise to lay away funds for your children's education.
The store will lay away the coat until you are ready to pay the full amount.
