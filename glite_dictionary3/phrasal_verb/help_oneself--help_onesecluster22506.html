to help oneself

verb
be unable to stop oneself
Pronunciation: / hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hɛlp wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to be unable to stop oneself from doing something, especially something undesirable; usually used in negative or interrogative contexts.

Examples

cannot help oneself laughing
He tried not to laugh during the meeting, but he couldn't help himself.
I know I shouldn't eat more cake, but I just can't help myself.
Whenever she hears good news, she can't help herself from sharing it with everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym – restrain oneself: to stop oneself from acting on an impulse or desire
