to take on

verb
compete against; face as an opponent
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ɒn / (BrE)
to compete against or face someone as an opponent, especially in a sport, game, or battle

Examples

to take on a rival team
She decided to take on the defending chess grandmaster.
The boxer was eager to take on any challenger who came his way.
This Saturday, our team will take on last year’s champions.

  • synonym – take up arms against: to go to battle or oppose someone
