to sidle up

verb
approach in a quiet or ingratiating way
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl ʌp / (BrE)
to approach someone in a quiet, cautious, or ingratiating manner, often with insincere intentions

Examples

to sidle up to a person
He tried to sidle up to his boss during the office party.
She would often sidle up to new students to make them feel welcome, though sometimes it seemed forced.
Whenever there was a discussion about promotions, he would sidle up to the manager with compliments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ingratiate oneself: to try to win favor with someone by flattery or insincere behavior
