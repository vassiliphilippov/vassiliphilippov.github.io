to stay at

verb
reside somewhere temporarily
Pronunciation: / steɪ æt / (AmE), / steɪ æt / (BrE)
to reside or live somewhere on a temporary basis, especially in a hotel, guesthouse, or at someone’s home

Examples

to stay at a hotel
Many business travelers stay at the conference hotel for convenience.
She will stay at her cousin’s house while she looks for an apartment.
We decided to stay at a small inn near the beach during our vacation.
