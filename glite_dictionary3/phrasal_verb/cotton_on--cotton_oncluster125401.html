to cotton on

verb
begin to understand; realize
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtən ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something, often after some initial confusion or delay

Examples

to cotton on to an idea
After a few minutes, the children cottoned on to the rules of the new game.
It took me a while to cotton on to what they were talking about.
She finally cottoned on to the reason why everyone was acting so strangely.
