to provide for

verb
make arrangements for future needs
Pronunciation: / prəˈvaɪd fɔr / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd fə / (BrE)
to make arrangements or take necessary measures in anticipation of something, especially to ensure that needs are met or future events are addressed

Examples

to provide for a family
It is important to provide for your children's education by saving early.
The new policy was designed to provide for those who are unable to work.
They have carefully provided for any unexpected emergencies during the trip.

Related Terms

  • antonym – neglect: to fail to care for or attend to
  • synonym – prepare: to make ready or get ready for something
