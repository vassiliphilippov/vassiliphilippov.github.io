to jack it

verb
abandon something suddenly (slang)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæk ɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk ɪt / (BrE)
(informal, slang) To abandon or give up on something suddenly, often out of frustration or lack of interest.

Examples

to jack it after problems
After three hours of trying to fix the old computer, he finally decided to jack it.
If the plan doesn't work this time, I'm just going to jack it and move on.
She realized the project wasn't worth her time anymore and chose to jack it.
