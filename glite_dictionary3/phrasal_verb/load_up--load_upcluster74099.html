to load up

verb
to fill or put a load onto
Pronunciation: / loʊd ʌp / (AmE), / ləʊd ʌp / (BrE)
to fill or put a lot of things into or onto something (such as a vehicle, container, or plate), often until it is full

Examples

to load up the car
Before leaving for the trip, we loaded up the car with supplies.
She loads up her shopping cart every time there's a sale.
The workers loaded up the truck with boxes all morning.
At parties, he likes to load up his plate with food from the buffet.
Be sure to load up on snacks for the road trip.
We need to load up the truck with boxes before it starts raining.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fill up: to make full; occupy completely
  • antonym – unload: to remove a load from something
