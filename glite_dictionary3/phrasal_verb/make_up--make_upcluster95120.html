to make up

verb
devise or compose; invent
Pronunciation: / meɪk ʌp / (AmE), / meɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to invent or create something, such as a story, excuse, or idea, often not true

Examples

to make up a story
She had to make up an excuse to explain her absence.
The chef loves to make up creative dishes using unusual ingredients.
The team worked late to make up a new advertising slogan for the campaign.
He likes to make up fantastic stories to entertain the children.
If you don’t know the answer, don’t just make up something.
She tried to make up an excuse for being late, but nobody believed her.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compose: to create by putting together elements
  • synonym – devise: to plan or invent by careful thought
  • synonym – fabricate: to invent or concoct, typically with deceitful intent
  • synonym – lie: to say something that is not true
  • antonym – tell the truth: to state what is true, not invent or falsify
