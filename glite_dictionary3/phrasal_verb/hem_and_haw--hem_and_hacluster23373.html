to hem and haw

verb
to hesitate or be indecisive in speech or action
Pronunciation: / hɛm ənd hɑ / (AmE), / hɛm ənd hɔː / (BrE)
to speak or act with hesitation, often making pauses or unintelligible noises instead of responding clearly

Examples

to hem and haw before answering
He hemmed and hawed so much during the interview that he seemed unprepared.
Stop hemming and hawing and just tell us what you want.
Whenever she was asked about the project, she would hem and haw for several minutes before answering.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hesitate: to pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • synonym – stall: to delay taking action or avoid giving a clear answer
