to blow over

verb
to disappear or be forgotten gradually
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a problem, argument, or difficult situation) to gradually go away, become less serious, or be forgotten

Examples

blow over a problem
Don't worry about the argument; it will blow over eventually.
Everyone hopes the controversy will blow over soon.
Tensions can be high at first, but they usually blow over with time.
I'm hoping this controversy will blow over before the end of the month.
Once the excitement had blown over, things returned to normal.
She waited for the argument to blow over before speaking to her friend again.

Related Terms

  • antonym – escalate: to become more intense or serious
  • synonym – pass: to come to an end or stop happening
  • synonym – subside: to become less intense or severe
