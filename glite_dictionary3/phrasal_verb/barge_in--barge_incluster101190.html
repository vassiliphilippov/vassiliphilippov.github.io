to barge in

verb
rudely interrupt (conversation or place)
Pronunciation: / bɑrdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / bɑːdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place or interrupt a conversation suddenly and rudely, without being invited

Examples

to barge in on a meeting
He tends to barge in during meetings, which annoys his colleagues.
Please don't barge in when others are speaking; wait for your turn.
They were having a private conversation when someone suddenly barged in and changed the topic.
I'm sorry to barge in like this, but I have urgent news.
She always tends to barge in when we're in the middle of a meeting.
The kids barge in without knocking, startling everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cut in: to interrupt someone who is speaking
  • synonym – interrupt: to stop someone from speaking by suddenly speaking oneself
  • synonym – intrude: to go into a place or situation where you are not wanted or invited
