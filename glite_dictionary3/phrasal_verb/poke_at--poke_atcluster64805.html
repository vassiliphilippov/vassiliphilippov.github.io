to poke at

verb
prod or push gently
Pronunciation: / poʊk æt / (AmE), / pəʊk æt / (BrE)
to push or prod something or someone gently, usually with a finger, stick, or similar object

Examples

to poke at food
He poked at his food absentmindedly, not feeling very hungry.
She reached out and poked at the fire to keep it burning.
The child couldn’t resist poking at the jelly with his spoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – poke: to jab or prod, especially with a finger or stick
