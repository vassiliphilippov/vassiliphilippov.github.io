to drop out

verb
withdraw from conventional life or society
Pronunciation: / drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to withdraw from society or stop participating in conventional life, especially because of disillusion with its values

Examples

to drop out of society
After years of stress, he decided to drop out and live a simpler life in the countryside.
Many young people chose to drop out of mainstream society in the 1960s.
She felt disconnected from conventional values, so she eventually dropped out.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conform: to behave in accordance with convention or expectations
  • synonym – opt out: to choose not to participate in something
