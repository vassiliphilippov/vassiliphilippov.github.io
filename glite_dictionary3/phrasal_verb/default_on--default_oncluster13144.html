to default on

verb
fail to pay a debt or obligation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔlt ɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt ɒn / (BrE)
to fail to make a required payment on a loan, debt, or other financial obligation

Examples

to default on a loan
If the company defaults on its loans, it could face bankruptcy.
Many homeowners lost their properties after they defaulted on their mortgages.
She is worried about defaulting on her student loan repayments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pay off: to pay the entirety of a debt
  • antonym – repay: to pay back money that is owed
