to bog down

verb
to become stuck or unable to progress
Pronunciation: / bɑɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to become stuck or unable to make progress, either literally (in mud) or figuratively (in a task or situation)

Examples

bog down in work
Negotiations bog down when participants can't agree on key points.
Progress on the project bogged down after several technical issues arose.
The truck bogged down in the thick mud, unable to move forward.
Don't let yourself bog down with minor problems—stay focused on the main goal.
She often bogged down in the details and missed her deadlines.
The project started well but soon bogged down in bureaucracy.
He tends to bog down in details and loses sight of the bigger picture.
Our plans were bogged down by unexpected complications.
The negotiations bogged down after several days with no agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advance: to move forward or make progress
  • synonym – impede: to slow down or prevent progress
  • antonym – move forward: to make progress; to proceed
  • synonym – stall: to stop running or moving, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
