to bridle up

verb
show anger or indignation
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪdəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbraɪd(ə)l ʌp / (BrE)
to show anger or indignation, often by drawing oneself up or reacting sharply

Examples

bridle up at criticism
She bridled up in response to the rude comments from her colleague.
She couldn’t help but bridle up at the unfair accusation.
When criticized in public, he tended to bridle up and defend his work.

  • synonym – bristle: to react angrily or defensively
