to wear upon

verb
cause tiredness or exhaustion through strain
Pronunciation: / wɛr əˈpɑn / (AmE), / wɛə əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to cause someone to become exhausted or tired due to overuse, strain, or stress

Examples

wear upon your nerves
His constant negativity began to wear upon everyone in the office.
The pressure of meeting daily deadlines can wear upon even the most dedicated journalists.
Years of hard labor finally wore upon his health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wear out: to exhaust or tire completely; to damage through use
