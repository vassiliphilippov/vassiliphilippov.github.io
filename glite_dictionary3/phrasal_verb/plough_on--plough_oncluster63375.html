to plough on

verb
continue resolutely despite difficulty
Pronunciation: / plaʊ ɑn / (AmE), / plaʊ ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something determinedly, especially when it is difficult or when others want you to stop

Examples

to plough on through work
Despite the harsh weather, the hikers decided to plough on toward the summit.
She felt exhausted but continued to plough on with her research late into the night.
The team faced technical issues but chose to plough on until the project's completion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give up: to stop trying or abandon effort
  • synonym – persevere: to persist in doing something despite difficulty or delay
