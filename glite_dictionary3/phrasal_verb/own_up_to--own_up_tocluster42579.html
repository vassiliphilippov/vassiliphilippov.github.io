to own up to

verb
admit responsibility or fault for something
Pronunciation: / oʊn ʌp tu / (AmE), / əʊn ʌp tʊ / (BrE)
to admit or confess that you are responsible for something wrong, dishonest, or embarrassing

Examples

to own up to a mistake
After weeks of denial, he finally owned up to breaking the vase.
It's better to own up to your mistakes than to cover them up.
She refused to own up to her role in the misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confess: admit to having done something wrong or illegal
  • antonym – deny: refuse to admit the truth or existence of something
