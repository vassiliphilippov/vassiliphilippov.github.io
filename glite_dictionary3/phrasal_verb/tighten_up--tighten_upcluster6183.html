to tighten up

verb
make rules or controls stricter
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtən ʌp / (BrE)
to make rules, controls, or restrictions more severe or strict

Examples

to tighten up security
After the incident, the school decided to tighten up security at all entrances.
The government plans to tighten up regulations on advertising unhealthy foods.
They had to tighten up the club's membership rules to prevent misuse.

Related Terms

  • antonym – loosen up: to become less strict or severe
  • antonym – relax: to make rules or restrictions less strict
