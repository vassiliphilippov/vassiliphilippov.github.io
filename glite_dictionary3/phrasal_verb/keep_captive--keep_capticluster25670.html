to keep captive

verb
hold someone's full attention
Pronunciation: / kip ˈkæptɪv / (AmE), / kiːp ˈkæptɪv / (BrE)
to hold someone's complete attention or interest so that they are unable to focus on anything else

Examples

keep captive an audience
As the thunderstorm raged outside, the old novel kept captive my imagination.
Her haunting voice kept captive everyone in the room until the last note faded away.
The speaker's passion and storytelling kept captive the entire audience for over an hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absorb: to take up all the attention or interest of someone
  • synonym – captivate: to attract and hold the interest and attention of someone
