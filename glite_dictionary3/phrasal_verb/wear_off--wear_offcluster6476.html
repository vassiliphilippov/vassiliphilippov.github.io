to wear off

verb
become less effective or deteriorate through use
Pronunciation: / wɛr ɔf / (AmE), / wɛə ɒf / (BrE)
to become gradually damaged, weakened, or less effective through continued use or stress

Examples

wear off paint
The color on the old sign had started to wear off from years in the sun.
The painkiller's effect began to wear off after a few hours.
With repeated washing, the print on the shirt will eventually wear off.
