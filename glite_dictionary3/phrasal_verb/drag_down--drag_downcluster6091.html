to drag down

verb
pull or force downward
Pronunciation: / dræɡ daʊn / (AmE), / dræɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to exert a force that causes something to move downward, especially by pulling with weight or pressure

Examples

drag down heavy branches
Heavy rain can drag down tree branches and cause them to break.
The extra equipment started to drag down the balloon, lowering it to the ground.
The weight of the wet clothes began to drag down the washing line.

Related Terms

  • synonym – weigh down: to make heavier or press down by weight
