to pry into someone's business

verb
intrude into another's private affairs
Pronunciation: / praɪ ˈɪntu ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈbɪznɪs / (AmE), / praɪ ˈɪntjuː ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
to inquire too closely into someone else's private affairs or matters that do not concern you

Examples

to pry into someone's business rudely
He always tries to pry into my business, but I never tell him anything private.
It's rude to pry into someone's business without their permission.
She told her neighbor not to pry into her business because it was personal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meddle: to interfere in or busy oneself unduly with something that is not one's concern
  • antonym – mind one's own business: to avoid interfering in other people's affairs
