to hole up

verb
hibernate or shelter for winter
Pronunciation: / hoʊl ʌp / (AmE), / həʊl ʌp / (BrE)
(Of an animal) to take shelter in a safe place and spend the winter dormant or hibernating.

Examples

bears hole up for winter
Bears often hole up in caves or dens during the cold winter months.
Many small creatures hole up underground to survive the harsh weather.
The hedgehogs will hole up for the winter as temperatures continue to drop.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hibernate: to spend the winter in a dormant condition
