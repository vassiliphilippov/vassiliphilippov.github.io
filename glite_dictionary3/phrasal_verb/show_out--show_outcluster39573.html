to show out

verb
perform or behave flamboyantly to impress
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ʃəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to perform or behave in a way intended to attract notice or impress others, often in an extravagant or flamboyant manner

Examples

to show out on stage
The band really showed out during their encore performance.
The dance team wanted to show out at the finals and drew cheers from the crowd.
Whenever she gets a chance, she loves to show out in front of her friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym – show off: to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration, often boastfully
