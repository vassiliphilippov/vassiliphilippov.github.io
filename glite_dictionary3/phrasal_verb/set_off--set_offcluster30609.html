to set off

verb
to begin a journey
Pronunciation: / sɛt ɔf / (AmE), / sɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to begin a journey; to start traveling toward a place

Examples

to set off on a trip
As soon as the rain stopped, the hikers set off up the mountain trail.
They set off on their road trip just after sunrise.
We decided to set off early in the morning to avoid traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – arrive: to reach a place, especially at the end of a journey
  • synonym – depart: to leave a place, especially to start a journey
