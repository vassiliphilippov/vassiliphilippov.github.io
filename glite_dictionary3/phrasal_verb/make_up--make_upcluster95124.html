to make up

verb
form or constitute a whole
Pronunciation: / meɪk ʌp / (AmE), / meɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to form or constitute; to combine together as parts to create a whole

Examples

make up a team
Students from different countries make up half the university's population.
Twelve months make up a calendar year.
Volunteers make up the majority of the staff at the event.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compose: to form or make up by combining parts
  • synonym – comprise: to consist of; be made up of
  • synonym – constitute: to be the parts that together form something
