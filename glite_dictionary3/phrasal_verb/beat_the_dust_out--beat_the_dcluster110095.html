to beat the dust out

verb
remove dust by beating or shaking
Pronunciation: / bit ðə dʌst aʊt / (AmE), / biːt ðə dʌst aʊt / (BrE)
to remove dust or dirt from something, especially fabric or carpets, by hitting or shaking it repeatedly

Examples

to beat the dust out of a rug
Every spring, my grandmother would beat the dust out of the old rugs in the backyard.
He spent half an hour beating the dust out of the heavy woolen blanket.
You should beat the dust out of those cushions before bringing them inside.
