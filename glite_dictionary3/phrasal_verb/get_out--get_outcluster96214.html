to get out

verb
escape responsibility or consequences
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to escape responsibility or consequences for something, especially by avoiding blame or punishment

Examples

to get out of trouble
He always tries to get out of doing the dishes after dinner.
She managed to get out of taking the blame for the accident.
They found a way to get out of their contractual obligations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – face up to: to accept and deal with responsibility or consequences
  • synonym – get away with: to avoid being punished for doing something wrong
