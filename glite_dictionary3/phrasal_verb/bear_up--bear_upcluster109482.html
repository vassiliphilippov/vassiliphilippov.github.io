to bear up

verb
remain cheerful in adversity
Pronunciation: / bɛr ʌp / (AmE), / beə ʌp /; / bɛə ʌp / (BrE)
to stay strong, positive, or courageous when facing difficulties or adversity

Examples

to bear up under stress
Even after losing his job, he tried to bear up for the sake of his family.
It's hard to bear up under so much pressure, but he remained optimistic.
She managed to bear up despite the bad news she received.
Despite all the setbacks, she continued to bear up and kept a positive attitude.
It was difficult for him to bear up after the loss of his friend.
The community tried to bear up during the long period of uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • antonym – collapse: to give way completely under pressure or stress
  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently; to bear something difficult
  • antonym – give in: to yield or stop resisting
