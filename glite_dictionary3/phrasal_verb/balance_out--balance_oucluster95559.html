to balance out

verb
make equal or compensate
Pronunciation: / ˈbæləns aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns aʊt / (BrE)
to make something equal or compensate for something so that there is no overall excess or deficiency

Examples

to balance out the flavors
The chef added lemon juice to balance out the sweetness of the dessert.
To maintain harmony, they tried to balance out their differences through open communication.
You should balance out your studies with some leisure activities to avoid burnout.

Related Terms

  • synonym – counterbalance: to offset or balance the effect of something
  • synonym – offset: to counteract or compensate for something
