to rule out

verb
eliminate as a possibility
Pronunciation: / ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
to decide that something is not possible or not suitable, and eliminate it as an option

Examples

to rule out a possibility
Her alibi helped rule out her involvement in the crime.
Technical issues have been ruled out as the cause of the problem.
We cannot rule out the possibility of rain tomorrow.
The doctor was able to rule out meningitis after reviewing the test results.
The evidence presented will help the court rule out any suspicion of fraud.
We can't rule out the possibility of rain during the event.
After several interviews, we decided to rule out Tom for the position.
The detective ruled out theft as a motive in this case.
The doctor was able to rule out any serious illness after reviewing the test results.
