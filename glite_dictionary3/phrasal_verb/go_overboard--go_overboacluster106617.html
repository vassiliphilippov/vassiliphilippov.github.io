to go overboard

verb
do something excessively or to extremes
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈoʊvərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvəbɔːd / (BrE)
to do something to an excessive or extreme degree, beyond what is reasonable or necessary

Examples

to go overboard with decorations
Don't go overboard with the decorations; a few balloons are enough.
I think you went overboard with the spices in this dish—it's a bit too hot for me.
She tends to go overboard when buying gifts for her friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exaggerate: to represent something as better or worse than it really is; act excessively
  • synonym – go too far: to go beyond the limits of what is acceptable
