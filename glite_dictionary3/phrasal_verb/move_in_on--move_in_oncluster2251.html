to move in on

verb
to intervene in order to control or dominate
Pronunciation: / muːv ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / muːv ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation, often in order to take control of it, usually without being invited

Examples

to move in on a company
After the founder retired, new management quickly moved in on the company.
Big corporations often move in on small markets once they've proven successful.
The rival gang tried to move in on their territory after the leader was arrested.

Related Terms

  • synonym – take over: to assume control of something, especially by force or without invitation
  • antonym – withdraw: to remove oneself or pull back from involvement or control
