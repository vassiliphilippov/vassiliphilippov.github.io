to come on

verb
move closer or approach
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɑn / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn / (BrE)
to move closer or towards a place, person, or event, often gradually or steadily

Examples

the storm comes on
As the storm began to come on, the sky turned dark and the wind picked up.
Night had come on by the time we reached the campsite.
The train could be seen coming on in the distance as we waited at the station.

Related Terms

  • synonym – approach: to come nearer in space, time, or quality
  • antonym – retreat: to move away from a place or person
