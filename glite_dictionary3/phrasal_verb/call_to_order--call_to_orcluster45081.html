to call to order

verb
formally begin a meeting
Pronunciation: / kɔl tə ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / kɔːl tə ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to formally announce the beginning of a meeting or session, typically to signal that proceedings should commence

Examples

call to order a meeting
After some informal conversation, the committee was called to order by the vice-chair.
Please remain quiet while the president calls to order so we can begin.
The chairman called to order at exactly 10 a.m. to start the conference.
