to get a grip on

verb
gain control over something
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ə ɡɹɪp ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə ɡɹɪp ɒn / (BrE)
to start to understand and control something, especially your feelings or a difficult situation

Examples

to get a grip on your emotions
It took me some time to get a grip on my finances after losing my job.
She struggled to get a grip on her nerves before stepping on stage.
You need to get a grip on your anger if you want to resolve this problem.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose control: fail to maintain power or authority over something
