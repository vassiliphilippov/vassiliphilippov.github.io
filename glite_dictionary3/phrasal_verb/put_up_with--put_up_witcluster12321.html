to put up with

verb
tolerate something unpleasant
Pronunciation: / pʊt ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or tolerate something or someone unpleasant or difficult without complaining

Examples

put up with bad behavior
How do you put up with his messy habits?
I can't put up with this noise any longer.
She had to put up with rude customers all day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bear: to be able to accept and deal with something unpleasant
  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently or without giving up
  • synonym – tolerate: to allow or accept something unpleasant or annoying
