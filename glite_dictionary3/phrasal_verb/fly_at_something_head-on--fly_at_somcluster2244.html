to fly at something head-on

verb
confront directly and decisively
Pronunciation: / flaɪ æt ˈsʌmθɪŋ hɛd ˈɑn / (AmE), / flaɪ æt ˈsʌmθɪŋ hɛd ˈɒn / (BrE)
to confront a challenge, problem, or difficulty directly and decisively without avoiding it

Examples

to fly at a problem head-on

He prefers to fly at challenges head-on rather than procrastinate or avoid them.
The team flew at the competition head-on and emerged victorious despite tough odds.
