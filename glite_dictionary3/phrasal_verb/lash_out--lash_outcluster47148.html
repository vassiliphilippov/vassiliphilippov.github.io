to lash out

verb
criticize or attack angrily (verbally)
Pronunciation: / læʃ aʊt / (AmE), / læʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to suddenly criticize or attack someone or something angrily, especially in speech or writing

Examples

to lash out at critics
After reading the unfair review, the author lashed out in a public statement.
The politician lashed out at journalists for misrepresenting his views.
Upset by the criticism, she lashed out at her coworkers during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – attack: to criticize or speak out against someone or something forcefully
  • antonym – praise: to express approval or admiration for someone or something
