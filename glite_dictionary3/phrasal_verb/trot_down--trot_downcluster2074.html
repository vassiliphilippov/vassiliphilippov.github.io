to trot down

verb
walk quickly or casually to a place
Pronunciation: / trɑt daʊn / (AmE), / trɒt daʊn / (BrE)
to walk quickly or casually, often for a short distance or toward a nearby place

Examples

to trot down the street
After lunch, we decided to trot down to the river for a walk.
Every morning, I trot down to the corner shop for fresh bread.
She trotted down the stairs, eager to share her news.

Related Terms

  • synonym – walk: to move at a regular, fairly slow pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
