to remain firm

verb
refuse to change position or opinion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmeɪn fɝːm / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn fɜːm / (BrE)
to refuse to change your decision, position, or attitude, especially in the face of opposition

Examples

remain firm in your beliefs
Despite the criticism, she remained firm in her beliefs.
He remains firm in his decision not to participate.
The company remained firm on its policy despite public pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stand firm: to refuse to change your decision or opinion
  • antonym – yield: to give in or change your position
