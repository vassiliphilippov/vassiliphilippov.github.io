to duck out

verb
leave quickly or avoid responsibility
Pronunciation: / dʌk aʊt / (AmE), / dʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly and secretly, or to avoid doing something you should do

Examples

to duck out of work
Many students duck out early on Fridays to start their weekends.
She tried to duck out of doing her share of the housework.
We saw him duck out right before the speeches began.

Related Terms

  • antonym – face up to: to confront a problem or responsibility directly
  • synonym – slip away: to leave quietly without being noticed
  • synonym – sneak out: to leave a place quietly and secretly
