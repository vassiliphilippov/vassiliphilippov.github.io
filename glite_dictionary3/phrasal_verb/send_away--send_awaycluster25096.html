to send away

verb
dismiss or end association with someone
Pronunciation: / sɛnd əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɛnd əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to tell someone to leave or to dismiss someone, especially from a job or position

Examples

to send away an employee
After the scandal, the company decided to send away the manager responsible.
She was heartbroken when her friends sent away her from their group.
The club had to send away several members for violating the code of conduct.
After the scandal, the company decided to send away several senior executives.
He was heartbroken when they sent away his closest colleague without warning.
Management threatened to send away any employee caught violating company policy.
