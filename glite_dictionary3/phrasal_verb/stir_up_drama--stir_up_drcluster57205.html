to stir up drama

verb
to create or provoke conflict or trouble
Pronunciation: / stɪr ʌp ˈdrɑmə / (AmE), / stɪə ʌp ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
to deliberately create or provoke conflict, tension, or emotional disturbance, often unnecessarily

Examples

stir up drama at work
He always manages to stir up drama at family gatherings by bringing up sensitive topics.
Instead of addressing the problem calmly, she chose to stir up drama amongst her friends.
Social media can give people a platform to stir up drama over minor issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm down: to bring a situation to a peaceful state or quiet someone's anger
  • synonym – cause trouble: to create difficulties or problems
