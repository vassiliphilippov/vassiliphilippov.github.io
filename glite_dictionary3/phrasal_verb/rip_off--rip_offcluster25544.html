to rip off

verb
remove by violent tearing
Pronunciation: / rɪp ɔf / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling or tearing it away violently and forcefully

Examples

to rip off the wrapping
She ripped off the wrapping paper from her birthday present in excitement.
The impatient driver ripped off the side mirror when squeezing through the narrow street.
The old poster was so stuck that I had to rip off a few layers of paint with it.
