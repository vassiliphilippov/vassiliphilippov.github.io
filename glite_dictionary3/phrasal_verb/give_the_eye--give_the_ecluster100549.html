to give the eye

verb
look at critically or appraisingly
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ði aɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ði ˈaɪ / (BrE)
to look at someone or something in a critical, appraising, or often admiring manner

Examples

give the eye to a stranger
Every time she walked past the group, they would give the eye and whisper to each other.
He knew the interviewer was giving the eye to his shabby shoes.
Susie caught Mark giving the eye to the expensive cars in the showroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym – give the once-over: to examine or look at someone or something quickly but carefully
