to take off

verb
subtract an amount
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk ɒf / (BrE)
to subtract an amount from a total or from another amount

Examples

take off the price
After taxes are taken off, your net salary will be lower.
Could you take off the cost of lunch from this bill?
You should take off any discounts before calculating the final total.
