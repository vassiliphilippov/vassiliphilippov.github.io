to uphold the tradition

verb
maintain and respect customs or practices
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈhoʊld ðə trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ʌpˈhəʊld ðə trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to maintain, continue, and show respect for established customs or practices

Examples

uphold the tradition of family
As the oldest in the family, she felt it was her duty to uphold the tradition.
Many organizations work hard to uphold the tradition while adapting to modern times.
The younger generation is determined to uphold the tradition of the annual festival.
