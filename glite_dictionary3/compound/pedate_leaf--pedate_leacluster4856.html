pedate leaf

noun
leaf with radiating, divided lobes
Pronunciation: / ˈpiː.deɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈpiː.deɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf structure in which the radiating lobes are each deeply cleft or divided, resembling a bird's foot.

Examples

pedate leaf shape
Botanists identified the plant by its characteristic pedate leaf structure.
Each pedate leaf on the vine spread out like a fan from the central stem.
The horse chestnut tree is known for its pedate leaf pattern.
