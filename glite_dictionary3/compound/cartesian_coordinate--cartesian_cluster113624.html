Cartesian coordinate

noun
position value in Cartesian coordinates
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈtiʒən koʊˈɔrdɪnət / (AmE), / kɑːˈtiːzɪən kəʊˈɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
one of the values that define a point's position in a Cartesian coordinate system, giving its distance from the axes or planes.

Examples

Cartesian coordinate of a point
In a two-dimensional graph, each point is described by two Cartesian coordinates.
The robot's movement was programmed according to its Cartesian coordinates in the workspace.
You can convert a point from polar to Cartesian coordinate form using trigonometric functions.
