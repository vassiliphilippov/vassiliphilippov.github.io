hard steel

noun
steel with high carbon content (>0.3%)
Pronunciation: / hɑrd stil / (AmE), / hɑːd stiːl / (BrE)
Steel containing more than 0.3% carbon, making it harder and less ductile than mild steel.

Examples

hard steel tools
Proper heat treatment can further improve the strength of hard steel.
The blade is forged from hard steel to ensure durability and sharpness.
Tools made with hard steel are often used in industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mild steel: steel with a low carbon content, typically less than 0.3%
