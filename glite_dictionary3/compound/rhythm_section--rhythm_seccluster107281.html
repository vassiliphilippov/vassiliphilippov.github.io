rhythm section

noun
group of musicians providing rhythm and harmony
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪðəm ˌsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɪðəm ˌsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the group of musicians in a band or orchestra who play percussion, bass, and often chordal instruments to provide the underlying rhythm and harmony

Examples

rhythm section in a band
A strong rhythm section is essential for any successful jazz ensemble.

The band's rhythm section kept the beat steady throughout the concert.
