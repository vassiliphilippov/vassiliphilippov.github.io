xiphoid process

noun
small cartilage at lower end of breastbone
Pronunciation: / ˈzaɪ.fɔɪd ˌprɑː.sɛs / (AmE), / ˈzɪf.ɔɪd ˌprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
the small, cartilaginous lower part of the breastbone that articulates with the body of the sternum and the seventh rib; often ossifies in adulthood

Examples

the xiphoid process of the sternum
During CPR, hands should be placed above the xiphoid process to avoid injury.
The surgeon made a small incision just below the xiphoid process to access the abdominal cavity.
You can sometimes feel the xiphoid process as a small, hard lump at the bottom of the breastbone.
