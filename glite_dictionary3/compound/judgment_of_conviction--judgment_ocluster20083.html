judgment of conviction

noun
criminal law: formal finding of guilt and punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt əv kənˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt əv kənˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
(criminal law) the official decision in a criminal case declaring a defendant guilty and specifying the punishment imposed.

Examples

court judgment of conviction
A judgment of conviction carries serious consequences, including a criminal record and potential imprisonment.
After a long trial, the judge pronounced the judgment of conviction against the defendant.
The defense attorney filed an appeal immediately after the judgment of conviction was entered.
