mirror dweller

noun
mythical being in a mirror; introspective person
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪrər ˌdwɛlər / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə ˌdwɛlə / (BrE)
A mythical being believed to inhabit a mirror, or figuratively, a person who is deeply introspective or self-reflective.

Examples

a mirror dweller artist
As an artist, she often felt like a mirror dweller, observing and analyzing her own emotions.
In old folktales, the mirror dweller might emerge at midnight to communicate with the living.
The villagers whispered about a mirror dweller who haunted the abandoned mansion.
