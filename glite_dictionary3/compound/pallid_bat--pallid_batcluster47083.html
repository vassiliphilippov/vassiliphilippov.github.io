pallid bat

noun
a pale yellowish bat with large ears
Pronunciation: / ˈpælɪd bæt / (AmE), / ˈpælɪd bæt / (BrE)
A species of bat with large ears and pale yellowish fur, found in caves in western North America.

Examples

a pallid bat in a cave
Researchers observed a pallid bat roosting in the mouth of a cavern.
The pallid bat hunts insects by listening for their movements on the ground.
Unlike other species, the pallid bat is known for its unusually large ears and pale coloring.
