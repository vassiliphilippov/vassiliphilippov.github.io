central vision

noun
the sharp vision at the center of the visual field
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
vision that uses the fovea and parafovea, responsible for seeing objects in the middle part of the visual field with greatest clarity.

Examples

central vision loss
Damage to the macula can severely affect a person's central vision.
Many eye diseases primarily impact central vision, making daily tasks difficult.
Reading small print relies on your central vision for detail and clarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym – peripheral vision: ability to see objects outside the direct line of sight
