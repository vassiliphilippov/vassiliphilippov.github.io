ground stroke

noun
tennis stroke made after ball bounces
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd ˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
(in tennis) a stroke made by hitting the ball after it has bounced once on the court.

Examples

powerful ground stroke
A well-executed ground stroke can keep your opponent on the defensive.
He practiced his ground stroke every day to improve his consistency.
Her powerful ground stroke helped her win the final set.
