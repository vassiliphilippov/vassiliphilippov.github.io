legal jointure

noun
estate settled on a wife in place of dower
Pronunciation: / ˈliɡəl ˈdʒɔɪn.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈdʒɔɪn.tʃə / (BrE)
(Law) An estate settled on a prospective wife as a marriage arrangement, in place of a dower, to secure her financial provision if widowed.

Examples

legal jointure for a widow
In historical England, women often relied on a legal jointure rather than a dower for security.
The legal jointure ensured that the widow would have sufficient income after her husband's death.
Under the marriage contract, a legal jointure was settled upon her for life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dower: the portion of a deceased husband's estate given by law to his widow
  • synonym – dowry: property or money brought by a bride to her husband on marriage
