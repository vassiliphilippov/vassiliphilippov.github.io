black-headed snake

noun
small ground snake with a black head
Pronunciation: / ˌblækˈhɛdəd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˌblækˈhɛdɪd sneɪk / (BrE)
a small, secretive, ground-dwelling snake, typically found from the central United States to Argentina, known for its black-colored head

Examples

a black-headed snake hiding
Herpetologists were excited to discover a colony of black-headed snakes in the region.
The black-headed snake is rarely seen because it spends much of its time under rocks or leaf litter.
Unlike venomous species, the black-headed snake poses no threat to humans.
