crime wave

noun
sudden rise in crime
Pronunciation: / ˈkraɪm weɪv / (AmE), / ˈkraɪm weɪv / (BrE)
A sudden and notable increase in criminal activity in a particular area over a period of time.

Examples

a crime wave hits city
After the economic downturn, the neighborhood suffered from a crime wave that alarmed residents.
Officials launched a new initiative to curb the recent crime wave that had gripped the downtown area.
The city experienced a crime wave last summer, with burglaries and thefts on the rise.
