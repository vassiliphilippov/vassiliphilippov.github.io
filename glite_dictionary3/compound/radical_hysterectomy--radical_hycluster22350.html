radical hysterectomy

noun
major surgery to remove uterus and related tissues
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəl ˌhɪstɚˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl ˌhɪstəˈrɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the uterus, ovaries, oviducts, cervix, and related lymph nodes are removed, typically to treat cancer

Examples

radical hysterectomy for cancer
A radical hysterectomy is often recommended when cervical cancer has spread to nearby lymph nodes.
After her cancer diagnosis, she underwent a radical hysterectomy to remove all affected tissues.
Recovery from a radical hysterectomy can be longer than from less extensive procedures.
