Gram's procedure

noun
bacterial staining technique (Gram method)
Pronunciation: / ɡræmz prəˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / ɡræmz prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a laboratory staining technique used to classify bacteria as Gram-positive or Gram-negative based on their cell wall properties

Examples

Gram's procedure for bacteria
A positive reaction in Gram's procedure indicates that the bacteria have a thick peptidoglycan cell wall.
Medical students must learn Gram's procedure during their microbiology training.
The microbiologist performed Gram's procedure to identify the type of bacteria present in the sample.
