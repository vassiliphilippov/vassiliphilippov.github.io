at the same time

adverb
nevertheless; despite this
Pronunciation: / ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (BrE)
used to indicate a contrast or reservation; nevertheless; despite what has just been said.

Examples

at the same time show contrast
I understand your position, at the same time I hope you'll consider mine.
It's expensive; at the same time, it's the best option available.
She wanted to go out, but at the same time, she knew she needed to rest.
