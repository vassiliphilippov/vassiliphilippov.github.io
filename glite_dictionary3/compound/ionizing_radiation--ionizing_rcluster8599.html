ionizing radiation

noun
high-energy radiation that creates ions
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪzɪŋ reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪzɪŋ reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
High-energy radiation that carries enough energy to remove tightly bound electrons from atoms, thus creating ions as it passes through substances.

Examples

ionizing radiation from X-rays
Medical X-rays are a form of ionizing radiation that can penetrate the body to create images of internal structures.
Unlike ionizing radiation, visible light does not have enough energy to alter atoms or molecules.
Workers in nuclear power plants are regularly monitored for exposure to ionizing radiation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non-ionizing radiation: radiation that does not produce ionization in matter
