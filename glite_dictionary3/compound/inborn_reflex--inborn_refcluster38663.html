inborn reflex

noun
instinctive response present at birth
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˈbɔrn ˈriˌflɛks / (AmE), / ɪnˈbɔːn ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
an automatic, unlearned response to a stimulus that is present at birth

Examples

baby inborn reflex
Babies are born with several inborn reflexes, such as the sucking reflex when something touches their lips.
The doctor tested the infant's inborn reflexes to assess the health of the nervous system.
The grasping of a finger by a newborn is a classic example of an inborn reflex.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conditioned reflex: a learned response to a stimulus
  • synonym – instinct: an innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in animals in response to certain stimuli
