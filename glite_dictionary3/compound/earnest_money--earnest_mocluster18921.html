earnest money

noun
deposit binding a contract
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrnɪst ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪst ˈmʌni / (BrE)
Money paid by a buyer to a seller as a pledge to complete a contract, often in real estate purchases.

Examples

earnest money deposit
If the contract falls through due to the buyer's fault, the seller may keep the earnest money.
The buyer provided earnest money to demonstrate commitment to purchasing the house.
Typically, earnest money is credited toward the purchase at closing.
