shore duty

noun
naval service at a land base
Pronunciation: / ʃɔr ˈduːti / (AmE), / ʃɔː ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
naval service performed at a land base rather than aboard a ship

Examples

shore duty at a naval base
After three years at sea, he was finally assigned to shore duty at the naval base.
His responsibilities during shore duty included training new recruits and maintaining equipment.
Many sailors look forward to shore duty because it allows them to spend more time with family.
