psychic energy

noun
hypothetical force driving mental processes
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkɪk ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪkɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
a hypothetical force believed to underlie and drive mental processes and activities, especially in psychoanalytic theory

Examples

psychic energy drives behavior
According to Freud, psychic energy is the driving force behind our desires and actions.
He felt his psychic energy was depleted after a long day of emotional work.
The concept of psychic energy is central to many theories in depth psychology.
