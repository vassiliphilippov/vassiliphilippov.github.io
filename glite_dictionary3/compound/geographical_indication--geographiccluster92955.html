geographical indication

noun
product name signifying place of origin
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː.əˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl ˌɪn.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl ˌɪn.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A name or sign used on products that identifies them as originating from a specific place, which gives them certain qualities, reputation, or characteristics.

Examples

geographical indication protects products
Products such as Champagne and Roquefort cheese are protected by geographical indications.
Registering a geographical indication can help local producers prevent imitation of their goods.
The European Union strictly regulates geographical indications to preserve regional food identities.
