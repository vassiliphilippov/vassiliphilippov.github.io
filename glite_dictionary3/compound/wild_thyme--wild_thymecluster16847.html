wild thyme

noun
aromatic shrub, Thymus serpyllum
Pronunciation: / waɪld θaɪm / (AmE), / waɪld taɪm / (BrE)
a small aromatic shrub (Thymus serpyllum) native to Europe and Asia, with small purple flowers, often found on banks and hillsides; also naturalized in North America

Examples

wild thyme plant
Bees are often attracted to wild thyme because of its plentiful flowers.
She picked some wild thyme to add a fresh, earthy flavor to her dish.
The hillsides were covered in blooming wild thyme, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.
