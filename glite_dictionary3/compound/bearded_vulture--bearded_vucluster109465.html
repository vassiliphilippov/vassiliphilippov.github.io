bearded vulture

noun
large vulture with black beard-like feathers
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪrdəd ˈvʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪədɪd ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large Old World vulture (Gypaetus barbatus) with a distinctive black beard of feathers below the beak, found in mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa

Examples

wild bearded vulture
Conservation efforts have helped increase the bearded vulture population in the Alps.

The bearded vulture is known for its habit of dropping bones from great heights to crack them open.
