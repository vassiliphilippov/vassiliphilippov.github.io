federal case

noun
legal matter under federal law
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəl keɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl keɪs / (BrE)
a legal matter that is investigated or prosecuted under federal law, rather than state or local law

Examples

a federal case in court
After the bank robbery, it quickly became a federal case.
The company was involved in a federal case over environmental violations.
The judge explained the difference between a state case and a federal case.
