Bartholin's gland

noun
mucus-secreting gland near vaginal opening
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrθəlɪnz ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈbɑːθəlɪnz ɡlænd / (BrE)
either of a pair of small glands located on each side of the vaginal opening that secrete lubricating mucus

Examples

Bartholin's gland blockage
Doctors examined the patient for blockage of the Bartholin's gland.
Lubricating mucus is produced by the Bartholin's gland during sexual arousal.
The Bartholin's gland can become infected, leading to a swelling known as a Bartholin's cyst.
