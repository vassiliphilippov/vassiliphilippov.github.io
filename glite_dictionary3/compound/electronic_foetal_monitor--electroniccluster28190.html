electronic foetal monitor

noun
device monitoring fetal heartbeat and contractions
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈfiːtəl ˈmɑnɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈfiːtəl ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
A medical device that continuously monitors a fetus's heartbeat and the mother's uterine contractions during childbirth using electronic sensors.

Examples

check with electronic foetal monitor
An electronic foetal monitor provides real-time data about the fetus and the mother's contractions.
Many hospitals rely on an electronic foetal monitor to ensure safer deliveries for both mother and child.
The nurse attached the electronic foetal monitor to check the baby's heart rate during labor.
