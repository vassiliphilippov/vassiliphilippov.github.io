cubic meter

noun
metric unit of volume (1,000 liters)
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːbɪk ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪk ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 liters, used for measuring large amounts of liquid or space

Examples

one cubic meter of water
A shipping container might have a capacity of thirty cubic meters.
One cubic meter of concrete weighs more than two tons.
The pool holds nearly fifty cubic meters of water when it is full.
