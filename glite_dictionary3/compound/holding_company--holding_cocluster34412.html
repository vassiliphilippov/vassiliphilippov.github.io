holding company

noun
company controlling others through share ownership
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
A company that owns enough voting stock in other companies to control their policies and management.

Examples

holding company owns shares
A holding company differs from an operating company, as it primarily owns assets rather than producing goods or services.
By forming a holding company, the family gained control over several major businesses.
The multinational created a holding company to manage its various subsidiaries.
