dead reckoning

noun
navigation by estimated position
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛd ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɛd ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of determining one's position by estimating the direction and distance traveled from a known point, without using celestial observations or external references.

Examples

dead reckoning navigation method
Early pilots used dead reckoning to navigate across long stretches of ocean.
Sailors relied on dead reckoning when clouds obscured the stars for several days.
Without GPS, hikers sometimes use dead reckoning to retrace their path in the wilderness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – celestial navigation: navigation using observations of the sun, moon, stars, or planets
