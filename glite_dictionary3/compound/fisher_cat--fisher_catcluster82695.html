fisher cat

noun
North American carnivorous mammal (Pekania pennanti)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃər kæt / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə kæt / (BrE)
A large, dark brown, carnivorous mammal native to North America, related to the marten and known for its arboreal habits.

Examples

wild fisher cat
A fisher cat was spotted prowling near the forest edge at dusk.
Despite its name, the fisher cat does not typically eat fish.
The fisher cat is known for its ability to climb trees and hunt small mammals.
