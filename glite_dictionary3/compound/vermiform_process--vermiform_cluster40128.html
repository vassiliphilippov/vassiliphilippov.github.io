vermiform process

noun
the appendix; worm-shaped pouch in the intestine
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝː.mɪ.fɔrm ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / ˈvəː.mɪ.fɔːm ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
A narrow, worm-shaped pouch extending from the lower end of the cecum, commonly referred to as the appendix.

Examples

vermiform process removal surgery
Doctors removed the patient's vermiform process during an emergency appendectomy.
In anatomical diagrams, the vermiform process is illustrated as a narrow tube attached to the cecum.
Inflammation of the vermiform process can lead to a condition known as appendicitis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – appendix: a small sac at the beginning of the large intestine
