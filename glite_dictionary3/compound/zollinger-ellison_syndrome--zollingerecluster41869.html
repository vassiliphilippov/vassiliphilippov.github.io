Zollinger-Ellison syndrome

noun
rare disorder with severe ulcers and excess stomach acid
Pronunciation: / ˈzoʊ.lɪŋ.ɚ ˈɛl.ɪ.sən ˌsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˈzɒ.lɪŋ.ə ˈɛl.ɪ.sən ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A rare medical disorder characterized by severe peptic ulcers, excessive gastric acid secretion, and tumors in the pancreas or duodenum.

Examples

Zollinger-Ellison syndrome causes ulcers
Doctors considered Zollinger-Ellison syndrome due to the unusually high levels of gastric acid found.
The patient was diagnosed with Zollinger-Ellison syndrome after presenting with multiple peptic ulcers.
Treatment for Zollinger-Ellison syndrome often requires medications to reduce stomach acid and sometimes surgery.
