dark-field microscope

noun
microscope using scattered light for tiny particles
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑrk.fild ˈmaɪ.kɹəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈdɑːk.fiːld ˈmaɪ.krə.skəʊp / (BrE)
a light microscope designed to illuminate specimens with scattered light, allowing observation of particles too small to be seen by ordinary microscopes

Examples

dark-field microscope shows bacteria
A dark-field microscope enables scientists to detect organisms that would be hidden in a regular bright-field setup.
The biologist used a dark-field microscope to examine the bacteria invisible under normal illumination.
Tiny particles suspended in liquid became visible when viewed through a dark-field microscope.
