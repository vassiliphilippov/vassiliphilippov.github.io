whispering campaign

noun
the organized spread of damaging rumors
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪspərɪŋ kəmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɪspərɪŋ kəmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
an organized effort to spread derogatory or damaging rumors in order to discredit someone, especially in politics

Examples

whispering campaign against a politician
A whispering campaign can be effective in spreading distrust without any direct accusations.
Rumors about her past began to circulate as part of a whispering campaign within the company.
The politician was the target of a whispering campaign orchestrated by his opponents before the election.

Related Terms

  • synonym – smear campaign: an attempt to damage someone's reputation by making false accusations or spreading rumors
