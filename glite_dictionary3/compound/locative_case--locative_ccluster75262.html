locative case

noun
grammatical case denoting location
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈləʊkətɪv keɪs / (BrE)
A grammatical case used in some languages to indicate location or the place where something happens.

Examples

locative case in Russian
Russian uses the locative case to express places where actions occur.
The locative case is rare in modern Indo-European languages but remains in use in some Slavic languages.
When learning Latin, students are often surprised by the existence of the locative case.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ablative case: case indicating movement away from something
  • synonym – dative case: case indicating recipient, indirect object, or benefactive role
  • synonym – genitive case: case indicating possession or close association
