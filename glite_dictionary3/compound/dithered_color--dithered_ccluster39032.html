dithered color

noun
digitally simulated color effect
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪðɚd ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪðəd ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A color effect in digital images created by arranging patterns of differently colored dots to simulate another color.

Examples

dithered color effect
Early video games relied on dithered color to make graphics appear more vibrant.
The old computer used dithered color to display images with a limited palette.
When zoomed in, you can see the dithered color pattern of tiny dots on the screen.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dither: to apply a pattern of dots to reduce visual artifacts or create new colors in digital images
