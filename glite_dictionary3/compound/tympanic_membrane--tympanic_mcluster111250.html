tympanic membrane

noun
membrane in the ear that vibrates with sound
Pronunciation: / tɪmˈpænɪk ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
a thin membrane separating the outer ear from the middle ear, which vibrates in response to sound waves

Examples

A ruptured tympanic membrane can cause temporary hearing loss.
