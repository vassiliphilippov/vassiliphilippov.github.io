experimenter bias

noun
bias from experimenter's expectations in research
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntər ˈbaɪəs / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntə ˈbaɪəs / (BrE)
bias introduced by an experimenter's expectations, which can influence participants and affect the outcome of an experiment, especially in psychology and the sciences.

Examples

experimenter bias affects results
Double-blind procedures are often used to control for experimenter bias in scientific studies.
The researchers took special precautions to minimize experimenter bias during the clinical trials.
Unconscious experimenter bias can lead to skewed or unreliable results in psychological research.
