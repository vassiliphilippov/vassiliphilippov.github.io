Kipp's apparatus

noun
laboratory device for generating gas
Pronunciation: / kɪps əˈpærətəs / (AmE), / kɪps əˈparətəs / (BrE)
a laboratory device used to generate a gas by the reaction of a liquid with a solid, typically without heating

Examples

Kipp's apparatus produces gas
Many old laboratories still have a Kipp's apparatus on display, although it's rarely used today.
The chemist used a Kipp's apparatus to obtain hydrogen sulfide for the experiment.
When properly set up, the Kipp's apparatus produces a steady stream of gas as needed.
