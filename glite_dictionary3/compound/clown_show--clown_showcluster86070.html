clown show

noun
chaotic or farcical situation
Pronunciation: / klaʊn ʃoʊ / (AmE), / klaʊn ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a situation that is chaotic, disorganized, or farcical, often due to poor management or execution

Examples

total clown show
After the changes in management, the entire department turned into a clown show.
Don't invite them—they turned last year's event into a clown show.
The press conference was such a clown show that reporters struggled to take it seriously.
