solid angle

noun
three-dimensional geometric angle
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːlɪd ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
an angle formed in three-dimensional space by three or more planes intersecting at a common point, measured in steradians.

Examples

solid angle in space
A detector's sensitivity depends on the solid angle it covers relative to the source.
The solid angle subtended by a sphere at its center is 4π steradians.
The concept of a solid angle is essential in understanding how light radiates from a point source.

Related Terms

  • synonym – angle: the space between two intersecting lines or surfaces at or close to the point where they meet
