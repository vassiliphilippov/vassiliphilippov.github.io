Clifford trust

noun
type of income-shifting trust (legal/financial)
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪfərd trʌst / (AmE), / ˈklɪfəd trʌst / (BrE)
A trust established to transfer income to someone taxed at a lower rate than the grantor for a period of 10 years or more.

Examples

Clifford trust for tax savings
Before 1986, many wealthy individuals made use of a Clifford trust in estate planning.
Changes in tax law have largely eliminated the benefits of setting up a Clifford trust.
The family created a Clifford trust to reduce their overall tax burden.
