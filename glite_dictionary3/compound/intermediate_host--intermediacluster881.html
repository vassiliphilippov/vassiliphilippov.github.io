intermediate host

noun
organism hosting a parasite mid-life cycle
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmid.i.ət hoʊst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət həʊst / (BrE)
an organism that temporarily harbors a parasite during some stage of its development before it reaches its definitive host

Examples

intermediate host snail
Schistosomes require a snail as an intermediate host during their life cycle.
Scientists studied the role of the intermediate host in the spread of the disease.
The mosquito acts as an intermediate host for the malaria parasite.

Related Terms

  • antonym – definitive host: organism where a parasite reaches maturity and reproduces sexually
