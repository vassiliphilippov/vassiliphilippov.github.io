penetrating injury

noun
injury where an object enters the body
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnəˌtreɪtɪŋ ˈɪndʒəri / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˌtreɪtɪŋ ˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
an injury in which an object, such as a knife or bullet, enters the body through the skin or other tissue

Examples

penetrating injury from a knife
A penetrating injury can lead to serious internal damage that is not always visible on the surface.
Doctors treated multiple penetrating injuries caused by flying glass from the explosion.
The patient suffered a penetrating injury to his abdomen after falling onto a metal rod.

  • antonym – blunt injury: injury caused by impact without penetration
