velvet grass

noun
tall grass with velvety stems, used for forage
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛlvət ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A tall perennial grass with soft, velvety stems, originally from Europe, now naturalized in North America and used for forage.

Examples

velvet grass in fields
Farmers often use velvet grass as forage for their livestock.
