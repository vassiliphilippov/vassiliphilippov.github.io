Burmese rosewood

noun
tree yielding mahogany-like wood
Pronunciation: / bɝːˈmiːz ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / bɜːˈmiːz ˈrəʊzˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tree native to India and Myanmar (Burma) that yields a hard, reddish wood resembling mahogany, valued for furniture and decorative uses

Examples

Burmese rosewood furniture
Artisans prize Burmese rosewood for its deep color and durability in fine woodworking.
Due to overharvesting, some species of Burmese rosewood are now protected.
The cabinet was made of beautifully polished Burmese rosewood that glowed in the sunlight.
