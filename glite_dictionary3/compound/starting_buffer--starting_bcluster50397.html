starting buffer

noun
buffer solution used at start of a reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrtɪŋ ˈbʌfər / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ ˈbʌfə / (BrE)
a buffer solution prepared and used at the beginning of a chemical or biochemical reaction to maintain desired pH conditions

Examples

starting buffer for reactions
Changing the starting buffer can significantly affect the outcome of the enzymatic reaction.
It is important to use a fresh starting buffer to avoid contamination in sensitive assays.
The starting buffer was carefully prepared to achieve the desired pH for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buffer solution: a solution that resists changes in pH when acid or alkali is added
