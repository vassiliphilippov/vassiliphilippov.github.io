sticker shock

noun
surprise at very high prices
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪkər ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈstɪkə ʃɒk / (BrE)
A feeling of surprise or dismay at learning that something costs much more than expected.

Examples

sticker shock at prices
After seeing the rent for downtown apartments, his sticker shock was obvious.
Buyers often get sticker shock when shopping for new cars these days.
When she opened the utility bill this month, the sticker shock left her speechless.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bargain: something bought for less than the usual price
  • synonym – price gouging: unfairly high pricing by sellers
