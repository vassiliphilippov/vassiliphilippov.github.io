war of words

noun
bitter verbal dispute
Pronunciation: / wɔr əv wɝdz / (AmE), / wɔːr əv wɜːdz / (BrE)
a prolonged and bitter exchange of verbal attacks or arguments, especially in public or between opposing sides

Examples

war of words between rivals
A bitter war of words erupted between the rival companies over the disputed patent.
After the match, the managers got into a war of words during the press conference.
The online war of words between fans of the two teams showed no sign of ending.

  • synonym – debate: a formal discussion on a particular topic in a public meeting
