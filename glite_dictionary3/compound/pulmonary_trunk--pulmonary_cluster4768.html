pulmonary trunk

noun
major artery from the right ventricle to the lungs
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌlməˌnɛri trʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri trʌŋk / (BrE)
the large artery that carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle of the heart to the lungs, where it divides into the right and left pulmonary arteries

Examples

A blockage in the pulmonary trunk can lead to life-threatening complications.
