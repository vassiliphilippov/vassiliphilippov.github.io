cavity wall

noun
double-layer wall with a gap between
Pronunciation: / ˈkævəti wɔl / (AmE), / ˈkævəti wɔːl / (BrE)
a wall composed of two masonry layers separated by a space, usually for insulation or moisture prevention

Examples

cavity wall insulation
He hired a specialist to inject foam into the cavity wall for better heat retention.
Many modern houses are built with a cavity wall to improve insulation.
The cavity wall helped prevent moisture from seeping into the home.
