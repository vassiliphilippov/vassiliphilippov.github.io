strong headwind

noun
powerful wind against movement
Pronunciation: / strɔŋ ˈhɛdˌwɪnd / (AmE), / strɒŋ ˈhɛdˌwɪnd / (BrE)
a wind of considerable force blowing directly against the direction of movement, making progress more difficult

Examples

face a strong headwind
Despite the strong headwind, the runner managed to finish the race in record time.
Pilots must account for a strong headwind when calculating flight times and fuel needs.
The hikers made slow progress due to a strong headwind on the mountain trail.
