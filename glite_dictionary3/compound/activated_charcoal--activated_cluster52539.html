activated charcoal

noun
form of carbon used for purification and poisoning treatment
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd ˈtʃɑr.koʊl / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd ˈtʃɑː.kəʊl / (BrE)
A form of carbon processed to have a large surface area, used to purify substances by adsorption and as an antidote for some poisons.

Examples

activated charcoal for poisoning
In cases of drug overdose, doctors may administer activated charcoal to absorb the toxins.
The pharmacist explained how activated charcoal can bind to certain poisons and prevent their absorption.
Water filters often contain activated charcoal to remove impurities.
