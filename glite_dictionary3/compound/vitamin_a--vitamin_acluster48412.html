vitamin A

noun
fat-soluble vitamin important for vision
Pronunciation: / ˌvaɪtəmɪn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A fat-soluble vitamin essential for normal vision, immune system function, and healthy skin; prevents night blindness and eye disorders.

Examples

vitamin A for eyesight
Carrots are rich in vitamin A, which is essential for healthy eyesight.
Doctors recommend a diet containing sufficient vitamin A to prevent night blindness.
Excessive intake of vitamin A can be harmful to your health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beta-carotene: precursor of vitamin A found in plants
  • synonym – retinol: the main active form of vitamin A
