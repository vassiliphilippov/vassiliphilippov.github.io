octal digit

noun
a digit from 0 to 7 in base-8 (octal) notation
Pronunciation: / ˈɑktəl ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒktəl ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
A single digit from 0 to 7 used in octal (base-8) numeral systems.

Examples

octal digit 7
The file permissions in Unix are often represented by a three-digit octal digit code.
To convert a binary number to octal, group the bits in sets of three to get each octal digit.
When working with old computer systems, every octal digit must be between 0 and 7.
