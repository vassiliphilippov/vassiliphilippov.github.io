metacarpal vein

noun
vein of the hand's metacarpal area
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈkɑrpəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈkɑːpəl veɪn / (BrE)
Any of the veins located on the back (dorsal) or palm (palmar) side of the hand that drain blood from the metacarpal region.

Examples

metacarpal vein in hand
Bruising appeared near her metacarpal vein after the intravenous injection.
Swelling can sometimes occur along a metacarpal vein following injury to the hand.
The surgeon carefully avoided the metacarpal vein during the procedure.
