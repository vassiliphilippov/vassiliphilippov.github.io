Lewis and Clark Expedition

proper noun
historic U.S. exploration mission (1804–1806) led by Lewis and Clark
Pronunciation: / ˌluːɪs ən ˈklɑrk ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌluːɪs ən ˈklɑːk ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
the expedition (1804–1806) led by Meriwether Lewis and William Clark, sent by President Thomas Jefferson to explore the newly acquired northwestern territories of the United States, traveling from St. Louis to the Pacific Ocean

Examples

the Lewis and Clark Expedition to the Pacific
Artifacts from the Lewis and Clark Expedition are on display at the local history museum.
Many books and documentaries have examined the challenges faced by the Lewis and Clark Expedition.
Teachers often use the Lewis and Clark Expedition to illustrate early American exploration and westward expansion.
