Order of Saint Benedict

noun
Roman Catholic monastic order
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər əv ˌseɪnt bəˈnɛdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ˌseɪnt bəˈnɛdɪkt / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic monastic order founded in the 6th century, known for its liturgical worship and scholarly activities.

Examples

the Order of Saint Benedict monastery
He joined the Order of Saint Benedict to dedicate his life to prayer and study.
Many monasteries affiliated with the Order of Saint Benedict are renowned for their beautiful architecture.
The Order of Saint Benedict played a major role in preserving knowledge during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – Cistercian: member of another Roman Catholic monastic order inspired by the Benedictines
  • degree higher – Trappist: member of a more ascetic offshoot of the Benedictine tradition
