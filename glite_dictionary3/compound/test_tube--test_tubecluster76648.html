test tube

noun
glass laboratory tube closed at one end
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛst tuːb / (AmE), / ˈtɛst tjuːb / (BrE)
a small glass tube that is closed at one end, used in laboratories for holding, heating, or mixing liquids and chemicals

Examples

test tube experiment
Several test tubes were lined up on the laboratory bench, each containing a different chemical solution.
She heated the mixture in a test tube until it began to bubble.
The scientist carefully poured the liquid into the test tube for analysis.
