Hirschsprung's disease

noun
congenital colon disorder lacking nerve cells
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrʃˌsprʊŋz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːʃˌsprʊŋz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which part of the colon lacks nerve cells, leading to difficulty passing stool and enlargement of the bowel (megacolon).

Examples

Hirschsprung's disease in newborns
Early detection of Hirschsprung's disease can greatly improve outcomes for affected children.
Surgery is often required to treat Hirschsprung's disease and restore normal bowel function.
The newborn was diagnosed with Hirschsprung's disease after experiencing severe constipation and abdominal swelling.
