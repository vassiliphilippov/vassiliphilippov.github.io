guard's van

noun
railway car for the guard (British)
Pronunciation: / ɡɑrdz væn / (AmE), / ɡɑːdz væn / (BrE)
a railway carriage at the end of a train, used by the guard and for storing luggage or parcels

Examples

train guard's van
During the journey, the conductor checked the cargo in the guard's van.
Older trains often had a guard's van equipped with basic safety equipment and seating for the guard.
The guard's van was located at the rear of the train, just behind the last passenger carriage.
