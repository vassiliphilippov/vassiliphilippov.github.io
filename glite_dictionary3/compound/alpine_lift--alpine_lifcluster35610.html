Alpine lift

noun
surface ski lift with bar for pulling riders uphill
Pronunciation: / ˈælpaɪn lɪft / (AmE), / ˈælpaɪn lɪft / (BrE)
A type of surface ski lift in which riders hold a bar and are pulled up a slope while remaining on their skis or snowboard.

Examples

ride the Alpine lift up
After a long day skiing, we took the Alpine lift one last time before heading to the lodge.
Children enjoy using the Alpine lift because it's easy to hold onto the bar as they go up.
The resort installed a new Alpine lift to help beginners reach the top of the gentle slope.
