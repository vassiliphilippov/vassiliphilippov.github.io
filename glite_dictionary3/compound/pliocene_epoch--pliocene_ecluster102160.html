Pliocene epoch

noun
geological epoch about 5.3–2.6 million years ago
Pronunciation: / ˈplaɪ.əˌsin ˈɛp.ək / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.əˌsiːn ˈiː.pɒk / (BrE)
The geological epoch from about 5.3 to 2.6 million years ago, marked by significant mountain formation, cooling climates, and the expansion of mammals.

Examples

the Pliocene epoch period
During the Pliocene epoch, large mammals such as elephants and horses diversified across continents.
Fossil records from the Pliocene epoch provide crucial evidence for the evolution of many modern animal groups.
The Pliocene epoch was a time of significant global cooling and glaciation.
