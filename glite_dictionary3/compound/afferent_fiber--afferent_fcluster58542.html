afferent fiber

noun
nerve fiber carrying signals to CNS
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.ɚ.ənt ˈfaɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt ˈfaɪ.bə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that carries impulses toward the central nervous system

Examples

afferent fiber transmits signals
An afferent fiber transmits sensory information from the skin to the spinal cord.
Damage to an afferent fiber can disrupt the body's ability to sense touch or pain.
Signals entering the brain typically travel along an afferent fiber.

Related Terms

  • antonym – efferent fiber: a nerve fiber that carries impulses away from the central nervous system
