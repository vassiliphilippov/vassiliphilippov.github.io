Mull of Kintyre rule

noun
informal guideline about image censorship
Pronunciation: / mʌl əv kɪnˈtaɪɚ ruːl / (AmE), / mʌl əv kɪnˈtaɪə ruːl / (BrE)
an informal guideline stating that certain features or depictions should not extend above a diagonal line drawn from left to right on an image, originally referring to the censorship of images in media

Examples

Mull of Kintyre rule for images
Designers had to adjust the artwork to comply with the Mull of Kintyre rule.
Some experts question whether the Mull of Kintyre rule is still relevant in modern classification standards.
The Mull of Kintyre rule is often cited when discussing censorship policies in the UK.
