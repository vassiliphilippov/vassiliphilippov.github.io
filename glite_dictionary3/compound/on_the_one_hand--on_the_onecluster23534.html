on the one hand

adverb
introducing one side of an argument
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə wʌn hænd / (AmE), / ɒn ðə wʌn hænd / (BrE)
used to introduce one of two or more contrasting points of view, facts, or options in a discussion or argument.

Examples

on the one hand he stays
On the one hand, he wants to study abroad; on the other, he doesn't want to leave his family.
On the one hand, living in the city is convenient, but it can also be noisy.
On the one hand, the job pays well, but on the other, it requires long hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym – on the other hand: used to introduce an opposing or contrasting point
