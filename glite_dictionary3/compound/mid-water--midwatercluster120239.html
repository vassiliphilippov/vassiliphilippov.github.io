mid-water

noun
middle zone of water (between surface and bottom)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
the part of a body of water that is well below the surface but still well above the bottom; the middle vertical zone of water

Examples

mid-water zone
Certain species of squid are commonly found in mid-water habitats.
Researchers lowered cameras to observe life in the mid-water zone.
The fish migrated from the ocean floor to mid-water each night.
