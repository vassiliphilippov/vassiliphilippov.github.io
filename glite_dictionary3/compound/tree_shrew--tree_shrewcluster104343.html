tree shrew

noun
small arboreal mammal of Southeast Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈtri ʃru / (AmE), / ˈtriː ʃruː / (BrE)
a small, insectivorous, arboreal mammal native to Southeast Asia, resembling a squirrel, with large eyes and a long, pointed snout

Examples

a tree shrew climbs trees
Although called a shrew, the tree shrew is not closely related to true shrews.
Researchers observed a tree shrew using its sharp snout to search for insects under the bark.
The tree shrew is known for its agility as it moves among the branches in the rainforest.
