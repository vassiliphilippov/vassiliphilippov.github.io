rail fence

noun
type of fence made from split rails
Pronunciation: / reɪl fɛns / (AmE), / reɪl fɛns / (BrE)
A fence typically constructed from split logs or rails laid across each other, often at an angle, to form a zigzag or straight pattern, used especially in rural areas.

Examples

old rail fence
After the storm, he repaired the broken section of the rail fence by replacing several logs.
The old rail fence zigzagged through the meadow, blending into the rural landscape.
They built a rail fence around the property to keep the livestock in.
