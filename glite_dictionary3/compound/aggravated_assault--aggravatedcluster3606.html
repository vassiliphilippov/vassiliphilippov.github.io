aggravated assault

noun
serious attack with intent to injure
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtɪd əˈsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəveɪtɪd əˈsɔːlt / (BrE)
A serious attack on someone, often involving a weapon, with the intent to cause significant bodily harm.

Examples

charged with aggravated assault
He was arrested and charged with aggravated assault after the violent incident.
The jury found the defendant guilty of aggravated assault with a deadly weapon.
Victims of aggravated assault often require hospitalization due to the severity of their injuries.
