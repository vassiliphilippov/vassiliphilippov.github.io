balsam woolly aphid

noun
insect pest of fir trees
Pronunciation: / ˈbælsəm ˈwʊli ˈeɪfɪd / (AmE), / ˈbalsəm ˈwʊli ˈeɪfɪd / (BrE)
a small, wingless insect (Adelges piceae) that infests and kills fir trees, especially Balsam Fir and Fraser Fir

Examples

balsam woolly aphid infestation
Foresters are concerned about the spread of the balsam woolly aphid in local forests.
Infestation by the balsam woolly aphid can cause extensive dieback in mature balsam firs.
The balsam woolly aphid has devastated large areas of Fraser fir in the Appalachian Mountains.

  • synonym – adelgid: a group of small sap-sucking insects that infest conifers
