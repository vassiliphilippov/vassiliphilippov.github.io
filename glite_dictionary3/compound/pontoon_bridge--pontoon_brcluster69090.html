pontoon bridge

noun
temporary floating bridge
Pronunciation: / pɑnˈtun brɪdʒ / (AmE), / pɒnˈtuːn brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A temporary bridge supported by floating pontoons, often used by military or engineering teams to cross rivers or other bodies of water.

Examples

pontoon bridge over river
After the flood washed away the old bridge, a pontoon bridge was set up as a temporary solution.
During the training exercise, soldiers practiced building a pontoon bridge in just under two hours.
The army engineers quickly constructed a pontoon bridge across the river to transport vehicles.
