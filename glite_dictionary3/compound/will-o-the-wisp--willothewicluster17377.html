will-o'-the-wisp

noun
ghostly marsh light; folklore phenomenon
Pronunciation: / wɪl əðə ˈwɪsp / (AmE), / wɪl əv ðə ˈwɪsp / (BrE)
A pale, flickering light sometimes seen at night over marshy ground, caused by the combustion of gases, and often associated with folklore.

Examples

see a will-o'-the-wisp light
As we walked by the marsh, a faint will-o'-the-wisp flickered in the distance.
Legend says the will-o'-the-wisp lured travelers off the safe path in the swamp.
Some believe that a will-o'-the-wisp is nothing more than gases igniting in the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignis fatuus: A phosphorescent light seen over marshy ground, same as 'will-o'-the-wisp'.
