fifty-first state

noun
hypothetical or symbolic next US state
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪfti ˈfɜrst steɪt / (AmE), / ˌfɪfti ˈfɜːst steɪt / (BrE)
a colloquial term referring to a US territory or foreign nation that is considered—often humorously or speculatively—as likely to become the next US state, or as closely associated with the United States as if it were a state

Examples

fifty-first state of America
At one time, some people referred to Hawaii as the fifty-first state before it officially joined the Union.
Many Canadians joke about their country's close ties by calling it the fifty-first state.
Politicians have debated whether Puerto Rico should become the fifty-first state in the US.
