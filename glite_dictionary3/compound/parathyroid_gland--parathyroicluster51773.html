parathyroid gland

noun
small endocrine gland near the thyroid
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əˈθaɪ.rɔɪd ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈθaɪ.rɔɪd ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
any of typically four small endocrine glands located near or within the thyroid gland, producing hormones that regulate calcium levels in the blood

Examples

overactive parathyroid gland
A disorder of the parathyroid gland can lead to abnormal calcium levels.
Doctors monitor patients with parathyroid gland removal for changes in blood calcium.
The surgeon carefully located each parathyroid gland during the procedure.
