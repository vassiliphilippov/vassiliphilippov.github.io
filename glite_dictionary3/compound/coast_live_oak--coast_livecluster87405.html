coast live oak

noun
evergreen oak tree native to western North America
Pronunciation: / koʊst laɪv oʊk / (AmE), / kəʊst laɪv əʊk / (BrE)
a highly variable, often shrubby evergreen oak tree of the coastal zone of western North America, with small, thick, usually spiny-toothed dark-green leaves

Examples

ancient coast live oak
Beneath the spreading branches of the coast live oak, hikers often pause to rest in the shade.

The coast live oak provides valuable habitat for wildlife along California's coastal hills.
