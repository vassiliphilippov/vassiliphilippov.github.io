anterior cerebral artery

noun
artery supplying parts of the brain
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪriɚ səˈribrəl ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə səˈriːbrəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of a pair of arteries branching from the internal carotid artery, supplying blood to the thalamus and parts of the frontal and parietal lobes of the brain

Examples

anterior cerebral artery supplies brain
A blockage in the anterior cerebral artery may lead to a specific type of stroke.
Damage to the anterior cerebral artery can affect movement and sensation in the legs.
Neurosurgeons must carefully navigate around the anterior cerebral artery during brain surgery.
