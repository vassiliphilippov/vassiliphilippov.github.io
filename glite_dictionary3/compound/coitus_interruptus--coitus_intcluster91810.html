coitus interruptus

noun
birth control method involving withdrawal before ejaculation
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊɪtəs ˌɪntəˈrʌptəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊɪtəs ˌɪntəˈrʌptəs / (BrE)
a method of birth control in which the penis is withdrawn from the vagina before ejaculation to prevent pregnancy

Examples

coitus interruptus as birth control
He explained that coitus interruptus requires careful self-control and is prone to failure.
Medical professionals often advise against coitus interruptus due to the high risk of unintended pregnancy.
Some couples rely on coitus interruptus as a form of contraception, though it is not completely reliable.
