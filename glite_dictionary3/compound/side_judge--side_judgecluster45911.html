side judge

noun
American football official positioned downfield
Pronunciation: / saɪd dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / saɪd dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
an official in American football who works downfield behind the defensive secondary on the same sideline as the head linesman

Examples

the side judge signals touchdown
During the game, the side judge kept a close eye on the receivers.
The side judge signaled that the pass was incomplete.
The referee consulted the side judge after the controversial play.
