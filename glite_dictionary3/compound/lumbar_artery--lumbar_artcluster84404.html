lumbar artery

noun
artery supplying lumbar region of the back
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌm.bɚ ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈlʌm.bɑː ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
any of several paired arteries branching from the abdominal aorta to supply blood to the lumbar vertebrae, back muscles, and abdominal wall

Examples

lumbar artery supplies spine
Anatomists studied the branching patterns of the lumbar arteries in detail.
Injury to a lumbar artery can result in significant bleeding in the abdominal cavity.
The surgeon carefully isolated the lumbar artery before proceeding with the operation.
