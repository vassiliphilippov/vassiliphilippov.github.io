jazz-rock

noun
music style blending jazz and rock
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæz ˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈdʒæz ˌrɒk / (BrE)
A style of music developed in the 1970s that combines jazz improvisation and harmony with the rhythms and instruments of rock music, often featuring electric guitars and synthesizers.

Examples

jazz-rock band
Her expertise in both jazz and rock allowed her to excel in the jazz-rock genre.
Many musicians in the 1970s experimented with jazz-rock as a way to push musical boundaries.
The band's latest album features a jazz-rock sound with complex rhythms and extended solos.
