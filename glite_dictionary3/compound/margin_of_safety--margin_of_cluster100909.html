margin of safety

noun
amount exceeding minimum for safety
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪn əv ˈseɪfti / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn əv ˈseɪfti / (BrE)
an amount by which something exceeds the minimum required to ensure safety or prevent failure, allowing for uncertainty or error

Examples

wide margin of safety
Always allow for a margin of safety when making critical calculations in engineering.
Investors look for a margin of safety before purchasing undervalued stocks.
The bridge was built with a generous margin of safety to handle unexpected loads.

Related Terms

  • synonym – safety factor: ratio indicating how much stronger a system is than required
