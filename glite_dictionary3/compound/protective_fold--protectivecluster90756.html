protective fold

noun
tissue flap that shields or covers
Pronunciation: / prəˈtɛktɪv foʊld / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv fəʊld / (BrE)
a flap or layer of tissue that serves to shield or cover a part it overlaps, especially in biological or anatomical contexts

Examples

protective fold of tissue
During surgery, the doctor lifted a protective fold of tissue to access the underlying organ.
Some aquatic animals have a protective fold of skin covering their gills.
The eyelid acts as a protective fold for the eye, keeping out dust and debris.
