acoustic nerve

noun
sensory nerve of hearing and balance
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk nɜːrv / (AmE), / əˈkjuːstɪk nɜːv / (BrE)
the sensory nerve that transmits sound and balance information from the inner ear to the brain; also known as the auditory nerve

Examples

acoustic nerve damage
An MRI scan was ordered to evaluate the integrity of the acoustic nerve.
Damage to the acoustic nerve can result in hearing loss or balance problems.
The doctor explained that the tumor was pressing on her acoustic nerve.
