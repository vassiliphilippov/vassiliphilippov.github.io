public square

noun
an open meeting area in a town or city
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk skwɛr / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk skweə / (BrE)
an open area in a town or city where streets meet, often used for gatherings or public events

Examples

busy public square
Artists gathered in the public square to display their work during the festival.
The mayor held a rally in the public square to address the citizens.
Vendors set up their stalls in the public square every Saturday morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – plaza: a public square in a city, especially in Spanish-speaking regions
