Chinese goose

noun
large domestic goose breed from China
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪ.niz ɡus / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ɡuːs / (BrE)
a large breed of domestic goose originating in China, closely related to the wild swan goose and known for its distinctive knob at the base of the bill.

Examples

Chinese goose with large knob
A flock of Chinese geese walked gracefully across the meadow.
Many farmers raise the Chinese goose for both meat and ornamental purposes.
The Chinese goose is easily recognized by the prominent knob on its bill.
