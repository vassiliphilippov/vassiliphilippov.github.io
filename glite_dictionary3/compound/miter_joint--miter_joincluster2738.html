miter joint

noun
joint forming a right-angled corner with bevelled ends
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtɚ dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a joint forming a corner, where both surfaces are bevelled, typically at a 45-degree angle, so they fit together to form a 90-degree corner

Examples

miter joint in woodwork
A miter joint is commonly used in carpentry to create neatly joined edges on moldings.
Properly cutting a miter joint requires precise measurement and a sharp saw.
The picture frame was assembled using a miter joint at each corner for a clean look.
