Iceland lichen

noun
lichen used as food or medicine in Nordic regions
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪslənd ˌlaɪkən / (AmE), / ˈaɪslənd ˌlaɪkən / (BrE)
a type of lichen with a branched, flattened, partly upright thallus found in mountainous and Arctic regions, used as medicine or food, and as a source of glycerol

Examples

Iceland lichen in bread
Dried Iceland lichen was added to bread by people living in mountainous regions.
The reindeer feed on Iceland lichen during the harsh Arctic winters.
Traditional remedies in Scandinavia have often included Iceland lichen for coughs and chest ailments.
