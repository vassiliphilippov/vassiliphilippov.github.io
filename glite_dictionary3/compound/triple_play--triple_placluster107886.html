triple play

noun
three outs on one play in baseball/softball
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪpl̩ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpl̩ pleɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball or softball) a play in which three players are put out as a result of continuous action.

Examples

triple play ends inning
The crowd erupted when the shortstop started an incredible triple play to end the inning.
They lost their chance to score after the defense executed a flawless triple play.
Turning a triple play is one of the rarest feats in professional baseball.
