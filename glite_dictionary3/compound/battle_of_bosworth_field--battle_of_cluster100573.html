Battle of Bosworth Field

proper noun
battle ending the Wars of the Roses (1485)
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬əl əv ˈbɑzwɜːrθ fiːld / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈbɒzwəθ fiːld / (BrE)
the decisive 1485 battle that ended the Wars of the Roses, in which Richard III was killed and Henry Tudor became King Henry VII

Examples

the Battle of Bosworth Field ended the war
Historians often cite the Battle of Bosworth Field as a turning point in English history.
Richard III famously lost his life at the Battle of Bosworth Field.
The Battle of Bosworth Field marked the end of Plantagenet rule in England.
