Canadian fleabane

noun
a North American weed with small yellowish flowers
Pronunciation: / kəˈneɪdiən ˈfliˌbeɪn / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (BrE)
A common North American weed with slender leaves and small yellowish flower heads, widely naturalized in temperate regions; sometimes classified as Erigeron canadensis.

Examples

Canadian fleabane plant
Canadian fleabane is commonly seen growing along roadsides throughout the summer.
Some gardeners consider Canadian fleabane to be an invasive species in their flower beds.
The yellowish flower heads of Canadian fleabane appear in late spring and attract various pollinators.
