throttle valve

noun
valve controlling engine fuel/air flow
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɑt̬l vælv / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtl vælv / (BrE)
A valve that regulates the supply of fuel or air to an engine, controlling its speed or power output.

Examples

throttle valve controls engine
Dust buildup can cause the throttle valve to stick, affecting acceleration.
Modern vehicles often use electronic sensors to operate the throttle valve precisely.
The mechanic inspected the throttle valve to diagnose the engine's performance issues.
