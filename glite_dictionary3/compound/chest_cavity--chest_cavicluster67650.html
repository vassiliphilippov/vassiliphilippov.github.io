chest cavity

noun
the area in the chest containing the heart and lungs
Pronunciation: / tʃɛst ˈkævəti / (AmE), / tʃɛst ˈkævəti / (BrE)
the space inside the chest, enclosed by the ribs, between the diaphragm and the neck, that contains the heart and lungs

Examples

fluid in the chest cavity
During the operation, fluid was drained from the patient's chest cavity.
Injuries to the chest cavity can be life-threatening and require immediate attention.
The surgeon carefully examined the chest cavity for any signs of damage.
