vertical integration

noun
combination of all production stages in one company
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtɪkəl ˌɪntəˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the combination in one company of two or more stages of production normally operated by separate companies, from raw materials to final product distribution

Examples

vertical integration strategy
Investors praised the manufacturer for its successful vertical integration strategy.
Recent vertical integration has led to greater efficiency and reduced costs.
The company's vertical integration allowed it to control every stage of the supply chain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – horizontal integration: combining firms at the same stage of production
