forcing out

noun
the act of expelling or ejecting
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrsɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
the act of expelling, projecting, or ejecting someone or something, often by applying force or pressure

Examples

forcing out a manager
After months of disputes, the union demanded the forcing out of the new management.
The board's forcing out of the CEO shocked many investors.
The company faced criticism for its forcing out of long-term employees.
