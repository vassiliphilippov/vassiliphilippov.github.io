register language

noun
type of tone language with pitch-based contrast
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdʒ.ɪ.stɚ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒ.ɪ.stə ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A tone language in which meaning differences are distinguished by different pitch registers or voice qualities rather than by tone contours.

Examples

register language distinguishes pitch
A register language distinguishes words by the relative height of the voice rather than by pitch movement.
Many Southeast Asian languages are considered examples of register languages.
Unlike contour tone languages, a register language uses level pitches to mark differences in meaning.
