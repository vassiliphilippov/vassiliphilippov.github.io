rolled biscuit

noun
biscuit made from rolled and cut dough
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊld ˈbɪskɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊld ˈbɪskɪt / (BrE)
A biscuit made from dough that is rolled out and then cut into shape before baking.

Examples

fresh rolled biscuit
Before baking, you should cut the rolled biscuit dough with a round cutter.
Many traditional Southern recipes call for rolled biscuits instead of drop biscuits.
She preferred to make rolled biscuits for breakfast because of their flaky texture.
