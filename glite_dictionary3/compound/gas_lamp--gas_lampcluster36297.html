gas lamp

noun
lamp that burns gas to provide light
Pronunciation: / ɡæs læmp / (AmE), / ɡæs læmp / (BrE)
A lamp that produces light by burning gas, commonly used before the widespread adoption of electric lighting.

Examples

old gas lamp
Before electricity, streets were often illuminated with gas lamps.
He admired the soft glow of the gas lamp in the study.
The old mansion was lit by a single gas lamp in each hallway.
