Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease

noun
rare fatal brain disorder causing dementia
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔɪtsfɛlt ˈjɑkɒb dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈkrɔɪtsfɛlt ˈjækɒb dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare, usually fatal brain disorder characterized by progressive dementia and loss of muscle control, thought to be caused by abnormal prion proteins.

Examples

Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease causes dementia
Researchers are still searching for an effective treatment for Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease.
Symptoms of Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease often include memory loss and difficulty with coordination.
The patient was diagnosed with Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease after showing rapid cognitive decline.
