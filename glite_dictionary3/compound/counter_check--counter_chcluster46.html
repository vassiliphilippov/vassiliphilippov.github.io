counter check

noun
temporary blank check from a bank
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.tɚ ˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə ˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
a blank check provided by a bank to customers, usually for immediate withdrawals when personalized checks are unavailable

Examples

counter check for cash withdrawal
She used a counter check to withdraw money before her regular checks arrived.
The bank only issues a counter check if you present valid identification.
When he forgot his checkbook, he requested a counter check from the bank teller.
