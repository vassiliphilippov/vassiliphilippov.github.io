constructed script

noun
a deliberately invented writing system
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌktɪd skrɪpt / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪd skrɪpt / (BrE)
a writing system intentionally invented by an individual or group, rather than one that evolved naturally within a language or culture

Examples

invent a constructed script
Creating a constructed script adds depth and realism to fictional worlds.
The linguist designed a constructed script to accompany her newly invented language.
Tolkien's Elvish languages each have their own unique constructed script.

Related Terms

  • synonym – constructed language: an artificially created language, not arising naturally
  • antonym – natural script: a writing system that evolved organically within a culture or language
