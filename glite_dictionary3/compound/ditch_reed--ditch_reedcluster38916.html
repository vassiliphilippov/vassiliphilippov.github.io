ditch reed

noun
North American reed used for mats and screens
Pronunciation: / dɪtʃ ˈrid / (AmE), / dɪtʃ ˈriːd / (BrE)
A tall North American reed with broad leaves and large, feathery flower clusters, found in moist areas and used for making mats, screens, and arrow shafts.

Examples

tall ditch reed plants
After the flood, the banks were filled with tall ditch reed swaying gently in the breeze.
Native Americans often fashioned arrow shafts from ditch reed because of its strength and straightness.
The local artisans wove baskets using ditch reed collected from the wetland.
