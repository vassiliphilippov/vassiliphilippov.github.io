licensing fee

noun
fee paid for an official license
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsənsɪŋ fi / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsənsɪŋ fiː / (BrE)
a sum of money paid to an authority, typically a government, for permission to carry out a particular activity, such as selling certain goods or practicing a regulated profession

Examples

annual licensing fee
Doctors must submit their licensing fee each year to maintain their right to practice.
Streaming companies face a licensing fee to use copyrighted music in their services.
The bar owner paid a substantial licensing fee to sell alcoholic beverages legally.

  • synonym – license fee: an amount to be paid for a license; alternate form
