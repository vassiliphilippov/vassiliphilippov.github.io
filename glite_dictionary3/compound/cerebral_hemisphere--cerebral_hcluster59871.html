cerebral hemisphere

noun
either half of the brain's cerebrum
Pronunciation: / səˈriː.brəl ˈhɛm.əˌsfɪr / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl ˈhɛm.ɪˌsfɪə / (BrE)
Either of the two symmetrical halves of the cerebrum, the largest part of the brain, responsible for different functions such as movement, sensation, and cognition.

Examples

left cerebral hemisphere
An MRI scan revealed an abnormality in the patient's right cerebral hemisphere.
Both the right and left cerebral hemispheres are involved in different aspects of memory and learning.
Damage to the left cerebral hemisphere can result in language and speech difficulties.
