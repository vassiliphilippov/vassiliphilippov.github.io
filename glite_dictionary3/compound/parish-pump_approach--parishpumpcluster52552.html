parish-pump approach

noun
narrow, local perspective in governance
Pronunciation: / ˈpærɪʃ pʌmp əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈpærɪʃ pʌmp əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
An approach to governance or decision-making that is limited to local or narrow interests, often disregarding broader or national considerations.

Examples

a parish-pump approach to politics
Critics accused the committee of taking a parish-pump approach instead of considering impacts on the whole country.
Her speech condemned the parish-pump approach that often hindered meaningful reform.
The new mayor rejected the parish-pump approach and promoted policies that benefited the entire city.

Related Terms

  • antonym – broad-minded: willing to accept wider opinions and ideas
  • synonym – parochial: having a limited or narrow outlook
  • synonym – provincial: having a narrow or limited perspective, especially outside major urban centers
