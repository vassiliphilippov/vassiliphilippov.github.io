demand feeding

noun
feeding as needed, not by schedule
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmænd ˈfiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːnd ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of feeding a baby or animal whenever it shows signs of hunger, rather than on a fixed schedule

Examples

demand feeding for babies
Breastfeeding experts often recommend demand feeding over strict feeding schedules.
Many parents choose demand feeding because it allows babies to eat whenever they are hungry.
With demand feeding, newborns can nurse more frequently during growth spurts.
