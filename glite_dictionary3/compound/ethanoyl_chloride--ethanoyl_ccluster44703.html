ethanoyl chloride

noun
a colorless liquid chemical, CH3COCl
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθəˈnɔɪl klaɪˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌiːθəˈnɔɪl ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless liquid acyl chloride (CH3COCl) with a pungent odor, used in organic synthesis

Examples

ethanoyl chloride in chemical reactions
Ethanoyl chloride reacts violently with water, releasing fumes of hydrogen chloride.
Because of its reactivity, ethanoyl chloride must be handled with care and under anhydrous conditions.
In the laboratory, ethanoyl chloride is used to introduce acyl groups into organic molecules.
