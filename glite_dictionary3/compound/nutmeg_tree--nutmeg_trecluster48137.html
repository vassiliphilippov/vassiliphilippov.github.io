nutmeg tree

noun
tropical tree producing nutmeg and mace
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌmɛɡ ˌtriː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to the East Indies and widely cultivated in tropical regions for its aromatic seeds, which are the source of the spices nutmeg and mace.

Examples

tall nutmeg tree
Farmers harvest both nutmeg and mace from the same nutmeg tree.
In colonial times, the nutmeg tree was a highly valuable commodity for spice traders.
The nutmeg tree thrives in humid tropical climates and can live for several decades.
