glenoid cavity

noun
depression in the scapula for shoulder joint
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɛnɔɪd ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈɡliːnɔɪd ˈkævɪti / (BrE)
the shallow depression in the scapula that articulates with the head of the humerus to form the shoulder joint

Examples

shoulder glenoid cavity
A dislocated shoulder occurs when the head of the humerus slips out of the glenoid cavity.
Arthritis in the glenoid cavity can significantly reduce shoulder mobility.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient’s glenoid cavity for signs of fracture.
