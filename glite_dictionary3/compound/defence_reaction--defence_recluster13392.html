defence reaction

noun
unconscious psychological process reducing anxiety
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns riˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns riˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
An unconscious psychological process that acts to reduce anxiety caused by instinctive desires; a type of defense mechanism.

Examples

a defence reaction to stress
A common defence reaction is to deny any feelings of jealousy.
Her therapist helped her become aware of her automatic defence reaction when discussing personal issues.
Projection can be considered a type of defence reaction in psychoanalytic theory.
