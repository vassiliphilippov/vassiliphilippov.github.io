alveolar arch

noun
curved jaw ridge holding teeth
Pronunciation: / ælˈvioʊlər ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ælˈviːələ ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the part of the upper or lower jaw where the teeth are anchored in a curved bony ridge

Examples

alveolar arch of the jaw
In children, the growth of the alveolar arch affects how their teeth are aligned.
Surgery may be needed if the alveolar arch is malformed.
The dentist examined the patient's alveolar arch for signs of gum disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jawbone: the bone forming the framework of the mouth, containing the teeth
