heaving line

noun
lightweight rope with weighted end for throwing
Pronunciation: / ˈhivɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈhiːvɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
A lightweight rope with a weighted end, thrown to transfer a heavier line between ships or to the shore.

Examples

heaving line to throw ashore
A heaving line is essential for passing a tow rope between two boats.
The sailor tossed the heaving line to the dockworker on shore.
They practiced throwing the heaving line accurately during the safety drill.
