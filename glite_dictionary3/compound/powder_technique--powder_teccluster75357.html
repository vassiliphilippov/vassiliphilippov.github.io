powder technique

noun
X-ray method for identifying substances
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊdɚ tɛkˈniːk / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə tɛkˈniːk / (BrE)
A method for identifying minerals or crystals by analyzing the X-ray diffraction pattern produced by a powdered sample.

Examples

powder technique for crystals
Researchers rely on the powder technique to accurately identify crystalline substances in mixtures.
The powder technique was used to determine the composition of the mineral sample.
X-ray analysis with the powder technique yields distinctive ring patterns for each compound.
