ripping bar

noun
steel tool for prying and pulling nails
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪp.ɪŋ bɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈrɪp.ɪŋ bɑː / (BrE)
a heavy steel tool resembling a crowbar, with one end shaped as a chisel for prying or splitting and the other end curved with a claw for pulling nails

Examples

use a ripping bar for demolition
A ripping bar is essential for demolition work.
He used a ripping bar to remove the old floorboards.
The carpenter reached for his ripping bar to pry open the crate.
