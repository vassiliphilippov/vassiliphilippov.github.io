skillet fish

noun
a small disc-shaped clingfish
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪlɪt ˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɪlɪt ˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small clingfish with a flattened, disc-like body resembling a skillet, found in shallow waters along the Atlantic coast of North America

Examples

skillet fish in shallow water
Scientists studied the habitat of the skillet fish along the Atlantic coastline.
The skillet fish uses its modified fins to cling tightly to rocks and shells in the shallows.
While snorkeling, we spotted a small skillet fish attached to an oyster shell.

  • synonym – clingfish: a small fish of the family Gobiesocidae, able to cling to surfaces
