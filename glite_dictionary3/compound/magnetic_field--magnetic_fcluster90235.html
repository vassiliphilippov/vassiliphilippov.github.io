magnetic field

noun
region influenced by magnetic force
Pronunciation: / mægˈnɛtɪk fiːld / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk fiːld / (BrE)
The region around a magnet, electric current, or moving charged particle within which the force of magnetism acts.

Examples

Earth's magnetic field
A compass needle aligns itself with the magnetic field of the planet.
Scientists study the magnetic field generated by electric currents.
The Earth's magnetic field protects us from harmful solar radiation.
