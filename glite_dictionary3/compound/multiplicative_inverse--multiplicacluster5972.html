multiplicative inverse

noun
a number which, when multiplied by another, gives 1
Pronunciation: / mʌltəpləˌkeɪtɪv ɪnˈvɜrs / (AmE), / mʌltɪplɪkətɪv ɪnˈvɜːs / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A number which, when multiplied by a given number, results in 1; also called the reciprocal.

Examples

multiplicative inverse of 5
Every nonzero real number has a multiplicative inverse.
