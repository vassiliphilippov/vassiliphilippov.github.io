dust bowl

noun
region affected by severe dust storms
Pronunciation: / dʌst boʊl / (AmE), / dʌst bəʊl / (BrE)
A region suffering severe dust storms, especially referring to areas in the central United States during the 1930s that experienced extreme drought and soil erosion.

Examples

the dust bowl disaster
The 1930s dust bowl devastated countless farms across the Midwest, driving families to migrate westward.
The novel vividly describes the hardship faced by those living in the dust bowl region.
Years after the dust bowl, many communities were still struggling to recover from its effects.
