central sulcus

noun
major groove separating frontal and parietal lobes of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl ˈsʌlkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈsʌlkəs / (BrE)
a prominent groove on the outer surface of the brain that separates the frontal lobe from the parietal lobe in each cerebral hemisphere

Examples

central sulcus separates lobes
Damage near the central sulcus can affect both movement and sensation in the body.
During brain surgery, surgeons carefully avoid the central sulcus to prevent impairing motor function.
The central sulcus is easily identified in brain scans as a deep groove running from the top of the head downward.
