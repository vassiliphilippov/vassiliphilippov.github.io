Nyquist frequency

noun
maximum frequency representable by sampling
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪkwɪst ˌfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˈnaɪkwɪst ˌfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
The highest frequency that can be accurately represented when a signal is sampled, equal to half the sampling rate; twice the maximum frequency present in the transmitted signal.

Examples

Nyquist frequency limit
If the signal exceeds the Nyquist frequency, aliasing can occur in digital recordings.
The Nyquist frequency determines the highest frequency that can be reconstructed from sampled data.
To avoid distortion, the sampling rate must be at least twice the Nyquist frequency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sampling rate: the number of samples of audio carried per second
