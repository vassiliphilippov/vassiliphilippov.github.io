lemon law

noun
consumer protection law for defective products
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛmən lɔ / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən lɔː / (BrE)
a law providing consumers with a remedy for vehicles or other products that repeatedly fail to meet standards of quality and performance

Examples

lemon law protection
After his new truck broke down twice in a month, he considered filing a claim under the lemon law.
It's important to read about the lemon law before making a major vehicle purchase.
Many states have enacted a lemon law to protect buyers from defective automobiles.
