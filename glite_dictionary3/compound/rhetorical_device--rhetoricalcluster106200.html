rhetorical device

noun
language technique for effect or emphasis
Pronunciation: / rəˈtɔrɪkəl dɪˈvaɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl dɪˈvaɪs / (BrE)
a technique in language used to produce a particular effect, often to persuade or create emphasis, rather than to convey literal meaning

Examples

use a rhetorical device in speech
Metaphor is a common rhetorical device used to make comparisons between unrelated things.
The politician's speech was full of rhetorical devices designed to evoke an emotional response.
When analyzing literature, students often identify various rhetorical devices to understand the author's intent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – figure of speech: an expression that uses words in a non-literal way for effect
