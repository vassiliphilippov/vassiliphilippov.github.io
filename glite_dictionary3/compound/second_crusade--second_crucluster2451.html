Second Crusade

proper noun
failed 12th-century Christian crusade to the Holy Land
Pronunciation: / ˈsek.ənd kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / ˈsek.ənd kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
the crusade led by European Christians from 1147 to 1149, aimed at reclaiming territory in the Holy Land but ultimately unsuccessful due to internal conflicts among leaders

Examples

the Second Crusade failed
During the Second Crusade, many European nobles marched to the Middle East but failed to achieve their goals.
Historians often debate the reasons why the Second Crusade was less successful than the First.
The failure of the Second Crusade had significant consequences for Christian control in the Holy Land.
