silk vine

noun
ornamental climbing plant with shiny leaves and poisonous fruit
Pronunciation: / sɪlk vaɪn / (AmE), / sɪlk vaɪn / (BrE)
a deciduous climbing plant with dark, shiny leaves, ill-scented flowers, and poisonous yellow fruits, native to southeastern Europe and Asia Minor, often grown for ornamental purposes

Examples

silk vine climbing fence
Despite its attractive look, the silk vine produces yellow fruits that are toxic if ingested.
Many gardeners train silk vine to climb arbors and fences for a dramatic effect.
The silk vine is prized for its glossy foliage and unique appearance in garden landscapes.
