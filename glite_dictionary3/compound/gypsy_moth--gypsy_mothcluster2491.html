gypsy moth

noun
destructive moth pest (Lymantria dispar)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪpsi mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsi mɒθ / (BrE)
A European moth (Lymantria dispar) that has been introduced to North America and is a major pest of shade trees, causing significant defoliation.

Examples

gypsy moth infestation
A severe gypsy moth infestation can leave trees bare and vulnerable to disease.
Officials set out traps each spring to monitor gypsy moth populations in local woodlands.
The gypsy moth has devastated large areas of oak forest throughout the northeastern United States.
