agent-based model

noun
computational model simulating autonomous agents
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒənt beɪst ˈmɑdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt beɪst ˈmɒdəl / (BrE)
a computational model that simulates the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the entire system

Examples

agent-based model simulates behavior
An agent-based model revealed how traffic congestion emerges from the behavior of individual drivers.
In economics, an agent-based model can analyze market dynamics among independent buyers and sellers.
The researchers developed an agent-based model to simulate disease spread within the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – multi-agent system: system comprising multiple interacting agents, often used in computer science
  • synonym – simulation: the imitation of the operation of a real-world process or system over time
