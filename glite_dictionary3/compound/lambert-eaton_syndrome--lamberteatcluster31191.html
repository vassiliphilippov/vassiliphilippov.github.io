Lambert-Eaton syndrome

noun
rare muscle-weakening disorder, often cancer-related
Pronunciation: / ˈlæm.bɚt ˈiː.tn̩ ˈsɪn.droʊm / (AmE), / ˈlæm.bət ˈiː.tən ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a rare disorder often associated with cancer, in which antibodies interfere with nerve signals to muscles, leading to muscle weakness and fatigue, especially in the hips, thighs, and back

Examples

Lambert-Eaton syndrome muscle weakness
Patients with Lambert-Eaton syndrome often present with fatigue and muscle weakness.
The diagnosis of Lambert-Eaton syndrome was confirmed after muscle strength testing.
The neurologist explained that Lambert-Eaton syndrome is sometimes linked to certain types of lung cancer.
