Lake Aral

proper noun
a lake between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan
Pronunciation: / leɪk əˈrɑl / (AmE), / leɪk əˈrɑːl / (BrE)
a large lake located to the east of the Caspian Sea, between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan, once one of the world's largest lakes but now greatly reduced in size due to water diversion.

Examples

Lake Aral disaster
Fisheries around Lake Aral collapsed after the lake dramatically receded.
Researchers studied the shrinking water levels of Lake Aral over several decades.
The environmental disaster at the Lake Aral has been called one of the worst in history.
