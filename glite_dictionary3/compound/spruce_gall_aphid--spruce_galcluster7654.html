spruce gall aphid

noun
insect pest of spruce trees
Pronunciation: / spruːs ɡɔl ˈæfɪd / (AmE), / spruːs ɡɔːl ˈæfɪd / (BrE)
an insect pest (Adelges abietis) that infests Norway spruce and several North American spruces, causing galls to form on the trees

Examples

spruce gall aphid damages trees
Foresters monitor the presence of spruce gall aphid to prevent damage to valuable timber resources.
In some regions, infestations of spruce gall aphid can weaken entire stands of spruce trees.
The spruce gall aphid causes distinctive pineapple-shaped galls to form on young spruce shoots.
