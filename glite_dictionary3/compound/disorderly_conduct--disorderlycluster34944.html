disorderly conduct

noun
offensive or disruptive behavior in public
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrdɚli ˈkɑndʌkt / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəli ˈkɒndʌkt / (BrE)
behavior that disturbs public order or peace, often considered an offense under law

Examples

disorderly conduct in public
Repeated disorderly conduct can result in more serious charges and penalties.
She was fined for disorderly conduct during the protest outside the city hall.
The police arrested him for disorderly conduct after he shouted and caused a scene in the street.
