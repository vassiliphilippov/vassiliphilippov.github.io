pericardial cavity

noun
fluid-filled space around the heart
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑrdiəl ˈkævɪti / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑːdiəl ˈkævɪti / (BrE)
the fluid-filled space between the layers of the pericardium that surrounds the heart, allowing smooth heart movement

Examples

the pericardial cavity around heart
During heart surgery, doctors take care not to damage the pericardial cavity.
Inflammation of the pericardial cavity can lead to serious cardiac complications.
The pericardial cavity contains a small amount of fluid that reduces friction as the heart beats.
