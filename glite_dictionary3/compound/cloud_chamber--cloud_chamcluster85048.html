cloud chamber

noun
particle detector using vapor tracks
Pronunciation: / klaʊd ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / klaʊd ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a device used for detecting the paths of high-energy particles, where the particles leave visible tracks in a supersaturated vapor

Examples

cloud chamber reveals tracks
A cloud chamber can reveal the invisible paths of radiation as thin, white streaks.
Early studies of atomic particles often relied on the use of a cloud chamber for detection.
The scientist observed the movement of charged particles in the cloud chamber during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bubble chamber: a device similar to a cloud chamber but using liquid to detect particles
