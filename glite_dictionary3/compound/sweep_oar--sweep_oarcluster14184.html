sweep oar

noun
long oar for rowing boats
Pronunciation: / swip ɔr / (AmE), / swiːp ɔː / (BrE)
a long oar used to row an open boat, typically with both hands on a single oar per rower

Examples

sweep oar rowing boat
Each rower in the competition used a single sweep oar to propel the boat forward.
Proper technique is crucial for handling a sweep oar during a race.
The university's rowing club ordered new sweep oars for their novice team.
