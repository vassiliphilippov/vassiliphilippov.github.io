malignant hyperthermia

noun
rare inherited reaction to some anesthetics
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt ˌhaɪpərˈθɜrmiə / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt ˌhʌɪpəˈθɜːmɪə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder in which exposure to certain anesthetics causes dangerously high body temperature and severe muscle rigidity.

Examples

malignant hyperthermia after anesthesia
Because her brother had experienced malignant hyperthermia, extra precautions were taken for her operation.
Prompt treatment with dantrolene is essential if malignant hyperthermia develops during surgery.
The anesthesiologist carefully screened patients for a history of malignant hyperthermia before administering anesthetics.
