precentral gyrus

noun
fold in the frontal brain lobe containing motor cortex
Pronunciation: / priˈsɛntrəl ˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / priːˈsɛntrəl ˈdʒaɪrəs / (BrE)
the fold of the frontal lobe in the brain, located in front of the central sulcus, which contains the primary motor cortex

Examples

the precentral gyrus controls movement
Damage to the precentral gyrus can result in loss of voluntary muscle movement.
Electrical stimulation of the precentral gyrus may produce muscle contractions.
The precentral gyrus is commonly referred to as the primary motor cortex.
