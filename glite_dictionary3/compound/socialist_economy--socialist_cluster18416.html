socialist economy

noun
economy based on state or collective ownership
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəlɪst ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪst ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
An economic system in which capital, resources, and means of production are owned and controlled by the state or the community as a whole.

Examples

socialist economy controls industries
After the revolution, the country adopted a socialist economy and nationalized major industries.
Some economists argue that a socialist economy provides more equality among citizens.
The socialist economy faced challenges adapting to global market competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym – capitalist economy: an economic system based on private ownership of capital
