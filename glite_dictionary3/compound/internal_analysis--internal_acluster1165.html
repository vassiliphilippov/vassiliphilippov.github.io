internal analysis

noun
examination of an organization's internal strengths and weaknesses
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrnəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnl əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
A systematic examination of an organization's internal strengths and weaknesses, focusing on factors within its own control or domain.

Examples

internal analysis of company strengths
An internal analysis revealed several areas where the company could improve efficiency and cut costs.
Most business plans begin with both an internal analysis and an external analysis to identify opportunities and threats.
The first step in our strategy was conducting an internal analysis to evaluate our resources and capabilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – external analysis: evaluation of factors outside an organization that affect its performance
