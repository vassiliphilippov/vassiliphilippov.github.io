past progressive tense

noun
verb tense for ongoing past action
Pronunciation: / pæst prəˈɡrɛsɪv tɛns / (AmE), / pɑːst prəˈɡrɛsɪv tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to indicate that an action was ongoing at a specific time in the past

Examples

past progressive tense in stories
Grammar books often explain the past progressive tense as describing background actions in stories.
In the sentence 'She was reading', the past progressive tense is used to show the action was ongoing.
To form the past progressive tense, use 'was' or 'were' followed by a present participle verb.
