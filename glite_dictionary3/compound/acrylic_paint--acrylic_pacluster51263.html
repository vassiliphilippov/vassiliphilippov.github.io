acrylic paint

noun
paint with acrylic polymer base
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɪlɪk peɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk peɪnt / (BrE)
A fast-drying paint made of pigment suspended in an acrylic polymer emulsion, used especially by artists.

Examples

acrylic paint on canvas
Many artists prefer acrylic paint because it dries quickly and is easy to clean up with water.
She used acrylic paint to create vibrant colors on her canvas.
Unlike oil paints, acrylic paint does not require any special solvents for thinning or cleaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – oil paint: paint in which pigment is suspended in oil, used by artists
  • antonym – watercolor: paint in which pigment is suspended in a water-based solution
