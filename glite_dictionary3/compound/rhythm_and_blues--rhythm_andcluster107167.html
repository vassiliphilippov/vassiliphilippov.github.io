rhythm and blues

noun
music genre blending blues and jazz
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪðəm ənd ˈbluːz / (AmE), / ˌrɪð.əm ən ˈbluːz / (BrE)
A type of popular music that combines elements of blues and jazz, originating in the United States among Black musicians and an important influence on early rock and roll.

Examples

classic rhythm and blues music
I grew up listening to classic rhythm and blues on the radio every Sunday.
Many legendary artists in the 1950s helped shape the sound of rhythm and blues.
Their new album has a strong rhythm and blues influence in both the vocals and guitar arrangements.
