white lily

noun
Egyptian water lily; sacred lotus
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈlɪli / (AmE), / waɪt ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A type of water lily, native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, especially the Egyptian lotus, which was held sacred by the ancient Egyptians.

Examples

white lily of Egypt
Botanists studied the unique features of the white lily native to the Nile region.
The ancient Egyptians often depicted the white lily in their art and hieroglyphs.
The pond was covered with the elegant blooms of the white lily.
