Coosa River

proper noun
river in Georgia and Alabama, USA
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːsə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkuːsə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States that rises in northwestern Georgia, flows southwest through eastern Alabama, and joins the Tallapoosa River near Montgomery to form the Alabama River.

the Coosa River in Alabama
Fishermen often visit the Coosa River for its abundance of bass and catfish.

The Coosa River provides important water resources for several Alabama cities.
