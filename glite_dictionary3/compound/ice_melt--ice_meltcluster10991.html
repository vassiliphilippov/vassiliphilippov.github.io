ice melt

noun
melting of ice (process/rate)
Pronunciation: / aɪs mɛlt / (AmE), / aɪs mɛlt / (BrE)
the process or amount of ice turning into water, especially referring to melting of glaciers, ice sheets, or sea ice; also used for the measured rate or extent of that melting.

Examples

Arctic ice melt increases
Satellite data show accelerating ice melt across Greenland as temperatures rise.
After the warm summer, local communities worried about rapid ice melt that threatened freshwater supplies.
Researchers measure annual ice melt to estimate sea-level rise and predict coastal flooding risks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ice growth: the increase or formation of ice; the opposite of melting
  • synonym – melting: the process of changing from a solid to a liquid because of heat
  • synonym – thaw: the process of ice or frozen ground becoming softer or liquid as it warms
