firing squad

noun
group assigned to military execution or salute
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrɪŋ skwɑd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ skwɒd / (BrE)
a group of soldiers chosen to shoot and kill someone as a form of military execution, or to fire volleys as a salute at a military funeral

Examples

executed by a firing squad
A firing squad rendered military honors during the funeral ceremony.
He faced the firing squad with remarkable calm.
The prisoner was executed by a firing squad at dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym – execution: the carrying out of a death sentence
  • synonym – salute: a firing of guns or rifles as a mark of respect
