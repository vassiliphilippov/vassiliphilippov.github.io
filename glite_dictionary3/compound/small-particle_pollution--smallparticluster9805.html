small-particle pollution

noun
air pollution from fine particles
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɔl ˈpɑɹtɪkəl pəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːl ˈpɑːtɪkəl pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
air pollution consisting of fine particles, such as soot, often produced by power plants or diesel engines, which can harm health and the environment.
