plantigrade mammal

noun
mammal walking with whole foot on ground
Pronunciation: / ˈplæn.tɪˌɡreɪd ˈmæm.əl / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tɪˌɡreɪd ˈmam.əl / (BrE)
A mammal that walks with the entire sole of the foot touching the ground, such as bears or humans.

Examples

plantigrade mammal like a bear
Humans are considered plantigrade mammals, unlike cats and dogs, which walk on their toes.
In evolutionary biology, the distinction between digitigrade and plantigrade mammals is important for understanding locomotion.
The bear is a classic example of a plantigrade mammal because it walks with its entire foot on the ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym – digitigrade: an animal that walks on its toes with the heel off the ground
