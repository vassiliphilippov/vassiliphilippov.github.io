iron pyrite

noun
a pale yellow mineral (iron disulfide)
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪərn paɪˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌaɪən paɪˈraɪt / (BrE)
A common mineral composed of iron disulfide, known for its pale yellow, metallic appearance and often mistaken for gold.

Geologists often refer to iron pyrite as 'fool's gold' due to its resemblance to real gold.
Many prospectors mistook iron pyrite for gold during the gold rush.
