to send a message

verb
to give a clear signal or indication
Pronunciation: / sɛnd ə ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɛnd ə ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
to communicate an idea, attitude, or intention indirectly, often through actions or signals rather than direct words

Examples

send a message through actions
By refusing to participate, she hoped to send a message about the need for reform.
The manager's strict policy changes send a message that lateness will not be tolerated.
The new safety measures send a message to employees that their well-being is taken seriously.
Her resignation was meant to send a message to the company's management.
The government’s new policy is designed to send a message to potential offenders.
Wearing casual clothes to the interview might send a message that you’re not serious about the job.

Related Terms

  • synonym – communicate: to impart or convey information or ideas
  • synonym – imply: to strongly suggest the truth or existence of something not explicitly stated
  • synonym – signal: to convey information or instructions by means of a gesture, action, or sound
