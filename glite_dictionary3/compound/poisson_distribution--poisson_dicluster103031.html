Poisson distribution

noun
probability distribution for rare events
Pronunciation: / ˌpwɑːsɑ̃ dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌpwɑːsɒ̃ dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən / (BrE)
a probability distribution that gives the likelihood of a number of rare events occurring within a fixed interval of time or space

Examples

Poisson distribution of rare events
If accidents occur randomly, a Poisson distribution can be used to predict their frequency each day.
The Poisson distribution is often used to model the number of phone calls received by a call center in an hour.
Understanding the Poisson distribution is essential for analyzing rare event probabilities in statistics.
