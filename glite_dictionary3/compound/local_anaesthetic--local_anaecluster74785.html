local anaesthetic

noun
drug that numbs a specific area
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəl ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl ˌanəsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or agent that numbs sensation in a specific area of the body, typically to allow minor surgery or procedures without pain.

Examples

injected local anaesthetic
A local anaesthetic is often used for minor skin surgery.
The dentist injected a local anaesthetic before filling the cavity.
You may feel some pressure, but the local anaesthetic will prevent any pain during the procedure.
