thyroid vein

noun
vein draining blood from thyroid gland
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
any of several small veins that drain blood from the thyroid gland and its surrounding area

Examples

thyroid vein drains blood
Blood from the thyroid gland is carried away through the thyroid veins into larger vessels.
Damage to a thyroid vein can cause significant internal bleeding in the neck region.
The surgeon carefully ligated the thyroid vein during the procedure to prevent bleeding.
