subalpine larch

noun
mountain-dwelling larch tree species
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈælpaɪn lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈalpʌɪn lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a medium-sized species of larch tree native to the Rocky Mountains, closely related to the western larch (Larix occidentalis)

Examples

subalpine larch forest
Hikers admired the scattered subalpine larch trees on the high-elevation slopes.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the range of the subalpine larch.
The subalpine larch turns a brilliant golden-yellow in autumn before shedding its needles.
