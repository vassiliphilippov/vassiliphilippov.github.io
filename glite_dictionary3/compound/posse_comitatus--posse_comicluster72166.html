posse comitatus

noun
temporary police force called by authority
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑsi ˌkɑməˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒsi ˌkɒmɪˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A group of people mobilized by the authorities, especially a sheriff, to assist in maintaining law and order or pursuing lawbreakers; a temporary police force.

Examples

a posse comitatus chasing criminals
Historically, a posse comitatus could be assembled to enforce court orders or capture criminals.
The law allowed him to summon a posse comitatus in times of emergency.
When the sheriff formed a posse comitatus, local citizens volunteered to join the search for the fugitives.
