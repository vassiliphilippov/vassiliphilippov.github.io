anti-war movement

noun
campaign against war
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈwɔr ˈmuːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈwɔː ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
A social or political campaign opposing the initiation or ongoing involvement in a particular war or wars in general.

Examples

the anti-war movement protests
During the Vietnam War, the anti-war movement gained momentum across many college campuses.
Members of the anti-war movement organized rallies to protest military intervention.
The anti-war movement often uses peaceful demonstrations to voice its opposition.
