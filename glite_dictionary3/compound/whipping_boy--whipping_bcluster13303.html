whipping boy

noun
one punished for others' faults
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpɪŋ bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪpɪŋ bɔɪ / (BrE)
A person who is blamed or punished for the mistakes or faults of others.

Examples

whipping boy for mistakes
For years, he was the whipping boy in the office, blamed for errors he didn't commit.
Politicians often look for a whipping boy to distract from their own mistakes.
She refused to be the whipping boy for problems caused by poor management.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scapegoat: a person who is blamed for the wrongdoings of others
