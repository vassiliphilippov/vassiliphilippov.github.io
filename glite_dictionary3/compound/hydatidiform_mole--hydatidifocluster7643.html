hydatidiform mole

noun
abnormal pregnancy growth forming cysts
Pronunciation: / haɪˌdætɪdɪˌfɔrm ˈmoʊl / (AmE), / haɪˌdætɪdɪˌfɔːm ˈməʊl / (BrE)
a rare abnormal growth of tissue in the uterus at the start of pregnancy, in which the chorionic villi develop into fluid-filled cysts rather than a normal placenta, usually resulting in loss of the fetus

Examples

hydatidiform mole in pregnancy
Patients with a hydatidiform mole may experience abnormal bleeding and elevated hormone levels.
The doctor diagnosed her pregnancy complication as a hydatidiform mole after an ultrasound examination.
Treatment of a hydatidiform mole often requires removing the abnormal tissue from the uterus.
