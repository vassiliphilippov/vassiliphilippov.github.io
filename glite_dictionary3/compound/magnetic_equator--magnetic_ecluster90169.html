magnetic equator

noun
imaginary line of zero magnetic dip near equator
Pronunciation: / məɡˈnɛtɪk ɪˈkweɪtər / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ɪˈkweɪtə / (BrE)
An imaginary line around the Earth, roughly parallel to the geographic equator, where a magnetic needle shows no vertical dip.

Examples

the magnetic equator crosses Earth
Near the magnetic equator, compasses point horizontally without any tilt upward or downward.
Scientists use the magnetic equator to study variations in Earth's magnetism across different regions.
The magnetic equator shifts slightly from year to year due to changes in the Earth's magnetic field.
