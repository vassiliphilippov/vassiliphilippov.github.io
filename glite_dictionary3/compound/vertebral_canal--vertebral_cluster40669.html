vertebral canal

noun
passage in the spine for the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / vərˈtiː.brəl kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.brəl kəˈnæl / (BrE)
the canal formed by the successive vertebrae of the spine through which the spinal cord passes

Examples

vertebral canal protects cord
In a healthy adult, the vertebral canal provides crucial protection for the spinal cord.
Injury to the vertebral canal can lead to serious neurological problems.
