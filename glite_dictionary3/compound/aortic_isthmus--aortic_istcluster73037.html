aortic isthmus

noun
narrowed part of the aortic arch
Pronunciation: / eɪˈɔrtɪk ˈɪsθməs / (AmE), / eɪˈɔːtɪk ˈɪsθməs / (BrE)
a narrow section of the aortic arch between the origin of the left subclavian artery and the descending aorta, often referring to a natural constriction present in fetal circulation.

Examples

aortic isthmus narrowing
A narrowing at the aortic isthmus can sometimes be detected in prenatal ultrasounds.
In fetal development, the aortic isthmus plays a significant role in directing blood flow.
The surgeon examined the aortic isthmus for evidence of obstruction in the newborn.
