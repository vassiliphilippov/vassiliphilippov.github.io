post-rotational nystagmus

noun
eye movement disorder after spinning stops
Pronunciation: / poʊst roʊˈteɪʃənl nɪsˈtæɡməs / (AmE), / pəʊst rəʊˈteɪʃənəl nɪsˈtæɡməs / (BrE)
a form of nystagmus occurring after rapid spinning stops, characterized by slow eye movements opposite to the direction of previous rotation

Examples

experience post-rotational nystagmus after spinning
After spinning in the chair, the child experienced post-rotational nystagmus and his eyes moved slowly in the opposite direction.
Doctors observed post-rotational nystagmus during the neurological examination after stopping the rotation test.
Pilots may report temporary post-rotational nystagmus after rapid changes in direction during flight training.
