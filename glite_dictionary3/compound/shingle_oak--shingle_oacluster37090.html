shingle oak

noun
species of oak tree with shiny leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪŋ.ɡəl oʊk / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋ.ɡl̩ əʊk / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to eastern and central United States, with shiny laurel-like leaves; its wood is used to make shingles, especially in western regions.

Builders in the Midwest once used shingle oak wood to make durable roofing shingles.
