law of reciprocal proportions

noun
chemistry law about combining ratios
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv rɪˌsɪprəkl prəˈpɔrʃənz / (AmE), / lɔː əv rɪˌsɪprɪkəl prəˈpɔːʃənz / (BrE)
A principle in chemistry stating that if two elements separately combine with a fixed mass of a third element, the ratio in which they do so will be the same or a simple multiple of the ratio in which they combine with each other.

law of reciprocal proportions in chemistry
