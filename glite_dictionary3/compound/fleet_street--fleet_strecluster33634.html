Fleet Street

noun
the British newspaper industry
Pronunciation: / flit strit / (AmE), / fliːt striːt / (BrE)
The British national press and journalists collectively, especially as a powerful or influential group; named after the London street formerly home to many newspaper offices.

Examples

Fleet Street journalists
Fleet Street has long been a symbol of British journalism.
Many scandals were exposed by Fleet Street reporters during the 1990s.
She always dreamed of working at one of the big Fleet Street newspapers.
