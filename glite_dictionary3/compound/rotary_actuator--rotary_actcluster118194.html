rotary actuator

noun
device that produces rotational motion (esp. in computer drives)
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊˌtɛri ˈæk.tʃuˌeɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəɹi ˈaktjueɪtə / (BrE)
A device that produces rotational motion, especially one used in computer drives to move a read/write head to the correct data track.

Examples

rotary actuator moves head
A malfunctioning rotary actuator can result in data read errors on the disk.
Modern hard drives use a rotary actuator to access information quickly and accurately.
The rotary actuator precisely positions the read/write head over the correct data track.

Related Terms

  • antonym – linear actuator: a device that produces straight-line motion
