self-punishment

noun
punishing oneself
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of inflicting punishment on oneself, usually as a response to guilt or perceived wrongdoing.

Examples

self-punishment for mistakes
Feelings of guilt often lead people to engage in self-punishment.
His constant criticism of himself was a form of self-punishment.
She turned to rigorous exercise as self-punishment after failing her exam.

Related Terms

  • synonym – punishment: the act of inflicting a penalty for an offence
  • antonym – self-forgiveness: the act of forgiving oneself for wrongdoing
