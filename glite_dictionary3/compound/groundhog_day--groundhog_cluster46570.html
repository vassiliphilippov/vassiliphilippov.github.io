Groundhog Day

noun
North American holiday predicting winter's length
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌhɑɡ ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndhɒɡ ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
February 2nd, a traditional North American holiday when, according to folklore, a groundhog predicts the length of the remaining winter based on whether it sees its shadow upon emerging from its burrow.

Examples

Groundhog Day celebration
Every year, people in Pennsylvania celebrate Groundhog Day with a big festival.
Some people consider Groundhog Day just a bit of fun, while others follow its predictions closely.
The local news covered the results of Groundhog Day to see if spring would come early.
