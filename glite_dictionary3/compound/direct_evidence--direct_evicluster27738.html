direct evidence

noun
evidence that directly proves a fact
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ˈɛv.ə.dəns / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈɛv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
Evidence that directly proves a fact, typically through witness testimony, without inference or presumption.

Examples

direct evidence from a witness
In this trial, there was no direct evidence linking the defendant to the crime scene.
Legal scholars often distinguish between direct evidence and circumstantial evidence in court proceedings.
The prosecutor relied on direct evidence provided by an eyewitness to build the case.

Related Terms

  • antonym – circumstantial evidence: evidence that suggests a fact indirectly, by inference
