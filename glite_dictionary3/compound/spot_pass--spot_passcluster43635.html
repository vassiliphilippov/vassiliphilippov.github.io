spot pass

noun
pass thrown to a fixed spot for the receiver
Pronunciation: / spɑt pæs / (AmE), / spɒt pɑːs / (BrE)
a pass in which the ball is thrown to a predetermined spot on the field for the receiver to catch upon arrival

Examples

spot pass to receiver
A well-timed spot pass can be difficult for defenders to intercept.
The quarterback threw a perfect spot pass to the corner of the end zone.
The receiver sprinted toward the sideline to meet the spot pass just as the ball arrived.
