podsol soil

noun
soil type with leached, grey layer
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑdˌsoʊl sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɒdsɒl sɔɪl / (BrE)
A type of soil found in temperate to cold, moist climates, characterized by a surface organic mat and a leached, grey subsurface layer, often occurring under coniferous or heath vegetation.

Examples

podsol soil under pine trees
Podsol soils are commonly found beneath pine forests in northern Europe.
