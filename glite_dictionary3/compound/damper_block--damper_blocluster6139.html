damper block

noun
felt block stopping piano string vibration
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmpər blɑk / (AmE), / ˈdæmpə blɒk / (BrE)
a small block, typically made of felt, that drops onto a piano string to stop its vibration and silence the note

Examples

damper block on piano string
A faulty damper block can cause certain piano notes to keep ringing longer than intended.
During the repair, the technician replaced a worn damper block on the upper register strings.
Each key on a piano controls a damper block to mute the corresponding string when released.

  • synonym – damper: device that deadens sound in a piano or similar instrument
