to pour a libation

verb
make a ceremonial drink offering
Pronunciation: / pɔr ə laɪˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / pɔːr ə laɪˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
to make a ceremonial offering of a drink, usually by pouring liquid on the ground or an altar as an act of reverence or worship

Examples

to pour a libation for ancestors
Before the feast, the priest poured a libation to honor the gods.
In their tradition, they pour a libation to remember loved ones who have passed away.
She poured a libation of wine on the earth as part of the ancient ceremony.
