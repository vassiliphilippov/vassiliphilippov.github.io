foul line

noun
boundary line in baseball field
Pronunciation: / faʊl laɪn / (AmE), / faʊl laɪn / (BrE)
either of the lines extending past first and third base that mark the boundaries of the playing field in baseball

Examples

foul line in baseball field
If a batter hits the ball beyond the foul line, it's considered a foul ball.
The ball landed just inside the foul line, so it was counted as fair.
White chalk marks are used to draw the foul lines before each baseball game.
