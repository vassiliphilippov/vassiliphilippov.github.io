old style font

noun
18th-century style serif typeface
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊld staɪl fɑnt / (AmE), / ˈəʊld staɪl fɒnt / (BrE)
A typeface style, originating in the 18th century, characterized by irregularity, slanted ascender serifs, and minimal contrast between light and heavy strokes.

Examples

old style font for books
Designers often choose an old style font for a classic or traditional look.
He preferred to print wedding invitations using an old style font for added elegance.
Many books use an old style font for their main text because it's easy to read.
