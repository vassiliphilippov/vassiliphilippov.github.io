service break

noun
a tennis game won on opponent's serve
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrvɪs breɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs breɪk / (BrE)
a game in tennis won by the receiving player or team when the opponent is serving

Examples

service break in tennis
He fought hard to avoid a service break but lost the game after a double fault.
The match was close until he managed a crucial service break in the final set.
Winning a service break in the first set gave her the momentum she needed.
