propulsion system

noun
system that creates propelling force
Pronunciation: / prəˈpʌlʃən ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / prəˈpʌlʃən ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system designed to provide the force or thrust that moves a vehicle or object forward.

Examples

propulsion system for spacecraft
Engineers are developing a new propulsion system that uses solar energy for deep-space missions.
The malfunction in the propulsion system forced the ship to return to port early.
The spacecraft's propulsion system was tested before launch to ensure it could travel to Mars.
