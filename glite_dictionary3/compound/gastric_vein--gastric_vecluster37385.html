gastric vein

noun
vein draining the stomach walls
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the stomach walls and empty into the portal venous system

Examples

gastric vein blockage
An enlarged gastric vein may be observed in patients with portal hypertension.
Damage to a gastric vein can lead to internal bleeding in the stomach area.
The surgeon carefully tied off the gastric vein during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – artery: a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • synonym – vein: a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
