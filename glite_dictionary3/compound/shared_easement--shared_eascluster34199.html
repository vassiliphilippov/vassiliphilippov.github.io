shared easement

noun
joint legal right to use another's property
Pronunciation: / ʃɛrd ˈizmənt / (AmE), / ʃeəd ˈiːzmənt / (BrE)
a legal right allowing more than one party to use a portion of another person's property, typically for access or utilities

Examples

shared easement for driveway
Before purchasing the land, she checked whether there was a shared easement for the neighboring property.
Due to the shared easement, both businesses can use the alley for deliveries.
The two homeowners have a shared easement to access the driveway behind their houses.
