coefficient of self induction

noun
ratio in electromagnetism; measured in henries
Pronunciation: / koʊˌɪfɪʃənt ʌv sɛlf ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt ɒv sɛlf ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the ratio of the electromotive force produced in a circuit by self-induction to the rate of change of current causing it, measured in henries

Examples

coefficient of self induction in a coil
The coefficient of self induction determines how effectively a coil opposes changes in current flowing through it.
The engineer calculated the coefficient of self induction of the solenoid as part of the circuit design.
When the coefficient of self induction is high, the induced emf is greater for a given rate of current change.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mutual inductance: inductance between two circuits when current in one induces emf in another
  • synonym – self-inductance: property of a circuit whereby a change in current induces an emf in itself
