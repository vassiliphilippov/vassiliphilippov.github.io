spastic bladder

noun
disorder causing loss of bladder control
Pronunciation: / ˈspæstɪk ˈblædər / (AmE), / ˈspæstɪk ˈblædə / (BrE)
A disorder of the urinary bladder characterized by loss of sensation, incontinence, and interrupted urination, typically resulting from nerve injury, multiple sclerosis, or trauma.

Examples

spastic bladder causes incontinence
People with spastic bladder may experience frequent and involuntary urination.
The patient was diagnosed with spastic bladder following her spinal cord injury.
Treatment options for spastic bladder include medications and physical therapy.
