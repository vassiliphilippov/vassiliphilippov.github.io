extension cord

noun
electric cable for extending power reach
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛn.ʃən kɔrd / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛn.ʃən kɔːd / (BrE)
A length of electric cable with a plug at one end and one or more sockets at the other, used to extend the reach of an electrical power source.

Examples

plug devices into extension cord
Please don't overload the extension cord with too many devices.
The extension cord made it possible to use the vacuum cleaner in the hallway.
We used an extension cord to connect the lamp to a distant outlet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – power strip: a block of electrical sockets attached to the end of a flexible cable, allowing multiple devices to be powered
