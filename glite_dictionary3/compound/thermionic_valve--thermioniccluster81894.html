thermionic valve

noun
vacuum tube electronic device
Pronunciation: / θɜrˈmaɪ.ɑnɪk vælv / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.mʌɪˈɒn.ɪk vælv / (BrE)
an electronic device consisting of electrodes in a vacuum-sealed glass or metal envelope, used to control electric current, especially in older radios and amplifiers

Examples

thermionic valve in a radio
Early radios often used a thermionic valve to amplify weak signals.
The engineer replaced the faulty thermionic valve to restore audio clarity.
With the arrival of transistors, the thermionic valve became largely obsolete in modern electronics.
