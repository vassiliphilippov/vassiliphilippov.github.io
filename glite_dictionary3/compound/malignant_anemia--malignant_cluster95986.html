malignant anemia

noun
chronic anemia caused by vitamin B12 deficiency
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A chronic and progressive type of anemia, typically occurring in older adults, caused by a lack of intrinsic factor necessary for vitamin B12 absorption.

Examples

malignant anemia vitamin B12
Doctors often prescribe vitamin B12 injections for individuals with malignant anemia.
The patient was diagnosed with malignant anemia after complaining of persistent fatigue and memory problems.
Untreated malignant anemia can lead to nerve damage and serious health complications.

Related Terms
