Widal test

noun
test to detect typhoid and salmonella infections
Pronunciation: / waɪˈdɑl tɛst / (AmE), / ˈwiːdɑːl tɛst / (BrE)
a test for detecting typhoid fever and other salmonella infections by identifying specific antibodies in a patient's blood

Examples

Widal test for typhoid
A positive Widal test result may indicate an infection with Salmonella bacteria.
Although the Widal test is still used, newer blood culture methods are often more accurate.
The doctor ordered a Widal test to confirm the diagnosis of typhoid fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym – serological test: a diagnostic test based on serum analysis
  • synonym – typhoid fever: infectious disease caused by Salmonella typhi
