survivorship annuity

noun
annuity paid if another can't receive it
Pronunciation: / sərˈvaɪvɚʃɪp əˈnuːəti / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvəʃɪp əˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
an annuity that is payable to one person if another designated person is unable or no longer qualifies to receive it, often used as a financial arrangement for spouses or partners.

Examples

survivorship annuity for a spouse
A survivorship annuity can provide financial security if one partner outlives the other.
Many retirees consider a survivorship annuity as part of their estate planning strategy.
The couple chose a survivorship annuity to ensure ongoing income for the surviving spouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym – joint-life annuity: an insurance annuity paid as long as at least one of two or more people is alive
