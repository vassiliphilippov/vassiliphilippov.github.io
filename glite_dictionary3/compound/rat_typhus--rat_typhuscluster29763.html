rat typhus

noun
infectious disease from flea bites
Pronunciation: / ræt ˈtaɪ.fəs / (AmE), / ræt ˈtɪ.fəs / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease caused by rickettsia bacteria, typically transmitted by the bite of an infected flea, marked by fever, chills, muscle aches, and a rash

Examples

rat typhus infection
After being bitten by a flea, she developed symptoms consistent with rat typhus.
Effective pest control can significantly reduce the risk of rat typhus in densely populated areas.
The outbreak of rat typhus in the city led to widespread health warnings.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – epidemic typhus: a more severe form of typhus transmitted by lice
  • synonym – murine typhus: a form of typhus transmitted from rats to humans by fleas
  • synonym – scrub typhus: a related rickettsial disease transmitted by mites
