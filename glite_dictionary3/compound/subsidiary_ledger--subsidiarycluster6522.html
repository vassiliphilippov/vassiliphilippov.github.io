subsidiary ledger

noun
detailed record supporting a general ledger account
Pronunciation: / səbˈsɪdiˌɛri ˈlɛdʒər / (AmE), / səbˈsɪdiəɹi ˈlɛdʒə / (BrE)
A book or electronic record that contains detailed information supporting an account in the general ledger, typically listing individual transactions or balances for specific items.

Examples

subsidiary ledger for accounts
Each subsidiary ledger provides supporting details for its related control account.
In the audit, discrepancies were found between the subsidiary ledger and the general ledger.
The accounts receivable subsidiary ledger lists the amounts owed by each customer.
