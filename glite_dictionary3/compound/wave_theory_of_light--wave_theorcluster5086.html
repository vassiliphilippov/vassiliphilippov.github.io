wave theory of light

noun
theory that light travels as waves
Pronunciation: / weɪv ˈθɪri əv laɪt / (AmE), / weɪv ˈθɪəri əv laɪt / (BrE)
the theory in physics that light travels as a wave, explaining phenomena such as interference and diffraction

Examples

wave theory of light explains interference
According to the wave theory of light, interference occurs when light waves overlap.
The wave theory of light was first proposed to explain the patterns seen in experiments with prisms and lenses.
The discovery of diffraction was strong evidence supporting the wave theory of light.
