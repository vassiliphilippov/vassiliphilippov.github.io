epidemic disease

noun
rapidly spreading infectious disease
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
an infectious disease that spreads rapidly to affect a large number of people within a community, region, or population

Examples

deadly epidemic disease
Influenza has historically been a common epidemic disease affecting large populations.
The city imposed strict quarantine measures to contain the epidemic disease.
Vaccination campaigns quickly halted the spread of the epidemic disease.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – endemic disease: a disease regularly found among particular people or in a certain area
  • degree higher – pandemic: a disease outbreak affecting an even larger geographic area, often globally
