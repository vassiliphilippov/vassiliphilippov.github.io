bomb shelter

noun
reinforced shelter for protection from bombs
Pronunciation: / bɑm ˈʃɛltɚ / (AmE), / bɒm ˈʃɛltə / (BrE)
a structure, often underground, reinforced to protect people from bombs during attacks, especially air raids

Examples

underground bomb shelter
During the war, families spent nights in the bomb shelter to stay safe from aerial attacks.
Emergency supplies are always kept stocked in the bomb shelter just in case.
The old bomb shelter in the basement is now used for storage.
