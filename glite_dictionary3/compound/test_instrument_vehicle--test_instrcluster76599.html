test instrument vehicle

noun
a rocket launched for testing purposes
Pronunciation: / tɛst ˈɪnstrəmənt vəˈhɪkl̩ / (AmE), / tɛst ˈɪnstrəmənt vɪˈhiːkl̩ / (BrE)
a rocket launched for the purpose of conducting tests or experiments, often used to trial equipment, systems, or scientific instruments in flight conditions.

Examples

a test instrument vehicle launches
A test instrument vehicle successfully carried the payload to the edge of the atmosphere for data collection.
NASA used a test instrument vehicle to trial the performance of experimental navigation systems.
The engineers prepared the test instrument vehicle for its launch to evaluate new sensor equipment.
