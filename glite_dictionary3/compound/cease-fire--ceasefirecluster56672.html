cease-fire

noun
temporary suspension of fighting
Pronunciation: / ˈsisˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsiːsˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an agreement between opposing sides to stop fighting for a certain period, usually to allow peace talks

Examples

declare a cease-fire
After months of conflict, a fragile cease-fire was finally put in place.
The soldiers remained alert even during the cease-fire, unsure if the peace would last.
The warring nations agreed to a cease-fire so that negotiations could begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym – armistice: a formal agreement to stop fighting a war
  • antonym – hostilities: acts of fighting in a war
  • synonym – truce: an agreement between enemies to stop fighting for a time
