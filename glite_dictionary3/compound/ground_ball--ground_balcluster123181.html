ground ball

noun
baseball hit rolling on the ground
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd bɔl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd bɔːl / (BrE)
(in baseball) a batted ball that rolls or bounces along the ground instead of being hit into the air

Examples

hit a ground ball
He specializes in inducing ground balls to avoid extra-base hits from batters.
The batter hit a sharp ground ball down the third-base line.
The shortstop easily fielded the ground ball and threw to first for the out.
A slow ground ball down the third-base line allowed the runner to reach first base safely.
He was known for his ability to force hitters into hitting ground balls rather than fly balls.
The batter hit a sharp ground ball to the shortstop, who made an easy play for the out.
