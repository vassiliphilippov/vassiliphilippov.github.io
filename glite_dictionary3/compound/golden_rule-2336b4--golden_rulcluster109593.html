golden rule

noun
ethical principle of treating others as oneself
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ruːl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ruːl / (BrE)
the principle that one should treat others as one would like others to treat oneself; often formulated as 'do unto others as you would have them do unto you'

Examples

the golden rule of kindness
Applying the golden rule can help create a more respectful workplace environment.
Many religions and philosophies teach the golden rule as a guide to ethical behavior.
Parents often emphasize the golden rule when teaching children about kindness and fairness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ethic of reciprocity: the principle of treating others as one is treated, another name for the golden rule
