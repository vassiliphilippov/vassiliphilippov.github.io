firehosing style

noun
overwhelming communication method
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌhoʊzɪŋ staɪl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəhəʊzɪŋ staɪl / (BrE)
a method of communication characterized by overwhelming the audience with a rapid, large volume of information, often to confuse or manipulate

Examples

firehosing style of communication
Her manager's firehosing style left the team overloaded with information and unsure of priorities.
In online debates, a firehosing style can overwhelm opponents and derail meaningful discussion.
The politician's firehosing style during the press conference made it hard to separate facts from exaggeration.

Related Terms

  • synonym – firehosing: the practice of spreading massive amounts of information to overwhelm or confuse
