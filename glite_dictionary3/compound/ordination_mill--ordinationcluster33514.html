ordination mill

noun
organization granting insincere religious ordinations
Pronunciation: / ɔːrˈdeɪʃən mɪl / (AmE), / ɔːˈdɪəɪʃən mɪl / (BrE)
an organization or institution that rapidly grants or sells religious ordinations, often with little or no genuine spiritual or educational requirements, and is regarded as lacking authenticity

Examples

online ordination mill
He received his certificate from an ordination mill, raising doubts about his legitimacy.
Some religious leaders have spoken out against the rise of the ordination mill industry.
The small website was exposed as an ordination mill that granted clergy titles to anyone who paid a fee.
