tuberculin skin test

noun
medical test for tuberculosis exposure
Pronunciation: / tuˈbɝːkjəlɪn skɪn tɛst / (AmE), / tjuːˈbɜːkjʊlɪn skɪn tɛst / (BrE)
a medical test in which tuberculin is injected under the skin to determine if a person has been exposed to the tuberculosis bacterium, based on the skin's reaction

Examples

tuberculin skin test result
A positive tuberculin skin test usually indicates past exposure to tuberculosis bacteria.
Healthcare workers often receive a tuberculin skin test during routine screening for tuberculosis.
The doctor administered a tuberculin skin test to check for latent tuberculosis infection.
