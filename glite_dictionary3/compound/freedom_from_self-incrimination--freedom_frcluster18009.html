freedom from self-incrimination

noun
right to refuse self-incriminating testimony
Pronunciation: / ˈfri.dəm frəm ˌsɛlf ɪnˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfriː.dəm frəm ˌself ɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the legal right to refuse to answer questions or give testimony that could incriminate oneself, as protected under the Fifth Amendment in the United States
