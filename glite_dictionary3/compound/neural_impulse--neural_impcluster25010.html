neural impulse

noun
electrical signal in a nerve fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈnʊrəl ɪmˌpʌls / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl ɪmˌpʌls / (BrE)
an electrical signal that travels along a nerve fiber, transmitting information within the nervous system

Examples

a neural impulse travels
A neural impulse is generated when a neuron is activated by a stimulus.
Pain is sensed when a neural impulse travels from the injury site to the brain.
The speed of a neural impulse can vary depending on the type of nerve fiber.
