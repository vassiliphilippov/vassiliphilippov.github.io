head register

noun
the higher vocal range felt in the head
Pronunciation: / hɛd ˈrɛdʒɪstər / (AmE), / hɛd ˈrɛdʒɪstə / (BrE)
the higher ranges of the human voice, especially in singing, where the vibrations are felt in the head rather than the chest

Examples

sing in head register
He struggled to control his head register during the complex aria.
Her teacher encouraged her to switch to her head register for the higher notes.
The head register gives a lighter, more resonant quality to the singer’s voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – chest register: the lower ranges of the voice with vibrations felt in the chest
