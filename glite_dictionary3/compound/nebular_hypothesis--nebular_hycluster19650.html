nebular hypothesis

noun
theory of solar system formation from a nebula
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛbjələr haɪˈpɑθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊlə hʌɪˈpɒθɪsɪs / (BrE)
the theory that the solar system originated from a hot, rotating cloud of gas and dust (nebula) which gradually condensed to form the sun and planets

Examples

nebular hypothesis of solar system
Many astronomy textbooks explain the nebular hypothesis when discussing the origin of our solar system.
The nebular hypothesis has been revised over time as new astronomical discoveries have been made.
The nebular hypothesis suggests that the sun and planets formed from a rotating cloud of gas.
