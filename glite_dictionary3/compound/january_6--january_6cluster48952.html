January 6

noun
6 January; Christian feast day (Epiphany)
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒænjuˈɛri sɪks / (AmE), / ˌdʒan.juˈɛː.ri sɪks / (BrE)
The sixth day of January, observed in many Christian traditions as Epiphany, celebrating the visit of the three wise men to the infant Jesus.

Examples

January 6 as Epiphany
Church services are held on January 6 to commemorate the Magi's visit to Jesus.
In Spain, children receive gifts on January 6 to honor the arrival of the three wise men.
Many countries celebrate January 6 as Epiphany, marking the end of the Christmas season.
