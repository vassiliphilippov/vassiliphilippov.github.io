Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

noun
ancient world's most famous monuments
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛvən ˈwʌndɚz əv ði ˈeɪnʃənt wɝld / (AmE), / ˈsɛvən ˈwʌndəz əv ði ˈeɪnʃənt wɜːld / (BrE)
the seven remarkable constructions of classical antiquity admired for their extraordinary architecture and engineering, traditionally listed as the Great Pyramid of Giza, Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Statue of Zeus at Olympia, Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, Colossus of Rhodes, and Lighthouse of Alexandria.
