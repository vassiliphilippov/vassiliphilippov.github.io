pericardial sac

noun
membrane surrounding the heart
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛr.ɪˈkɑːr.di.əl sæk / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˈkɑː.di.əl sæk / (BrE)
the double-walled membranous sac that surrounds and protects the heart in vertebrates

Examples

the pericardial sac around the heart
During open-heart surgery, the surgeon carefully opened the pericardial sac.
Fluid can sometimes build up in the pericardial sac, leading to a condition called pericardial effusion.
The pericardial sac provides a protective barrier for the heart against infections.
