cadmium cell

noun
standard voltaic cell using cadmium electrode
Pronunciation: / ˈkædmiəm sɛl / (AmE), / ˈkædmiəm sɛl / (BrE)
a standard electrical cell that uses cadmium as one of its electrodes, formerly used as a voltage standard in laboratories

Examples

cadmium cell voltage standard
Early electrical experiments often relied on the cadmium cell to ensure consistent measurements.
Technicians measured the voltage deviation by comparing it with a standard cadmium cell.
The laboratory maintains its reference voltage with a cadmium cell for calibration purposes.
