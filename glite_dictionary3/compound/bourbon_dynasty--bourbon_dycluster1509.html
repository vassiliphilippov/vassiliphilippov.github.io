Bourbon dynasty

proper noun
European royal family ruling France and Spain
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜr.bən ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈbʊə.bɒn ˈdɪn.ə.sti / (BrE)
a European royal family that ruled France from 1589 to 1793, and later Spain, Naples, and Sicily.

Examples

the Bourbon dynasty ruled France
After the French Revolution, members of the Bourbon dynasty regained the throne for a brief period.
The Bourbon dynasty brought relative stability to France after years of civil strife.
The influence of the Bourbon dynasty extended beyond France, reaching into Spain and southern Italy.

  • antonym – Habsburg dynasty: another major European royal family
