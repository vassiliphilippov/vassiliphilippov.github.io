coefficient of drag

noun
measure of aerodynamic drag resistance
Pronunciation: / koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv dræɡ / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv dræɡ / (BrE)
a dimensionless quantity representing the ratio of the drag force on a body moving through a fluid to the product of the fluid's density, the body's velocity squared, and a reference area

Examples

low coefficient of drag
A lower coefficient of drag means less energy is needed to move the object through air.
Engineers worked to reduce the coefficient of drag on the airplane's wings for better performance.
The new car model has a remarkably low coefficient of drag, improving its fuel efficiency.
