global attribution

noun
belief that a cause applies across contexts
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbəl ˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəl ˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the belief that the cause of a negative event is consistent across various situations or contexts

Examples

global attribution after failure
In cases of failure, global attribution can lead to feelings of helplessness.
Therapists help clients avoid global attribution to encourage healthier thinking patterns.
When someone makes a global attribution, they might think, 'I always mess things up.'

Related Terms

  • antonym – specific attribution: the belief that a cause is limited to a particular situation or context
