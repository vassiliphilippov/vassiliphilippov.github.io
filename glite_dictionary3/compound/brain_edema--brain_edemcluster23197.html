brain edema

noun
swelling of the brain from excess water
Pronunciation: / breɪn ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / breɪn ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the brain swells due to the accumulation of excess water in the neuropile and white matter

Examples

brain edema after injury
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of brain edema following surgery.
The CT scan revealed significant brain edema after the head injury.
Treating brain edema promptly is crucial to reduce the risk of permanent damage.
