intermittent tetanus

noun
muscle twitching syndrome; medical
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmɪtənt ˈtɛtənəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪt(ə)nt ˈtɛtənəs / (BrE)
A clinical neurological syndrome marked by muscle twitching, cramps, and sometimes seizures, often caused by calcium or vitamin D deficiency or alkalosis.

Examples

intermittent tetanus from low calcium
A deficiency in calcium can lead to intermittent tetanus in some individuals.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with intermittent tetanus after observing recurrent spasmodic contractions.
Patients suffering from intermittent tetanus may experience episodes of muscle cramps and twitching.
