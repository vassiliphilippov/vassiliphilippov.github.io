sugar syrup

noun
liquid sweetener made from sugar and water
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːɡər ˈsɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈsʊɡə ˈsɪr.əp / (BrE)
A liquid sweetener made by boiling sugar and water together, sometimes with the addition of corn syrup; commonly used in drinks and desserts.

Examples

sugar syrup in cocktails
She added a tablespoon of sugar syrup to her cocktail for extra sweetness.
The recipe calls for equal parts water and sugar syrup.
To make candied fruit, first prepare a sugar syrup and let the fruit soak in it.
