optical fiber

noun
thin glass or plastic fiber for transmitting light signals
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A very thin strand of glass or plastic that transmits light signals, used for high-speed data or image transmission.

Examples

optical fiber cable
Modern telecommunications depend increasingly on optical fiber cables for reliable, high-capacity connections.
The surgeon used a bundle of optical fibers to illuminate and visualize the tissue during the procedure.
Transmitting data through an optical fiber network allows for much higher speeds than traditional copper wires.

  • synonym – fiber optics: the technology based on transmitting light through optical fibers
