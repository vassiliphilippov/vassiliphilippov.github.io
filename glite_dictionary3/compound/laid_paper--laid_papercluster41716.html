laid paper

noun
writing paper with textured fine lines
Pronunciation: / leɪd ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / leɪd ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
writing paper characterized by a textured pattern of fine lines, formed by the wires of the papermaking mould, running across the grain of the paper.

Examples

laid paper texture
Many artists prefer laid paper for drawing because of its unique texture.

Traditional printing techniques often use laid paper to achieve an authentic historical look.
