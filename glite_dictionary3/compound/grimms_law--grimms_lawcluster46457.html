Grimm's law

noun
historical linguistics principle about Germanic consonant shifts
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪmz lɔ / (AmE), / ɡrɪmz lɔː / (BrE)
a principle in historical linguistics stating the regular shift of consonant sounds in Germanic languages as compared to other Indo-European languages

Examples

Grimm's law describes shifts
According to Grimm's law, English 'father' is related to Latin 'pater' and Greek 'pater'.
Many linguistics students study Grimm's law to understand the consonant shifts in Germanic languages.
The discovery of Grimm's law was a significant milestone in the field of comparative linguistics.
