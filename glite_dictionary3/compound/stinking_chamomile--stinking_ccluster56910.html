stinking chamomile

noun
a weed with strong smell and white-yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ kəˈmɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ kəˈmɔɪl / (BrE)
a widespread weed with a strong, unpleasant smell, having white-rayed flowers with yellow centers; also known as Anthemis cotula

Examples

wild stinking chamomile
Children sometimes mistake stinking chamomile for regular chamomile, but the smell quickly reveals the difference.
Farmers often try to remove stinking chamomile because of its strong odor and rapid spread.
The field was overrun with stinking chamomile, making the air almost unbearable.
