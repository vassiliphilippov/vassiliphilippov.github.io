sinus transversus

noun
paired venous channel in the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪnəs trænzˈvɜrsəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnəs transˈvɜːsəs / (BrE)
either of two paired channels of venous blood within the dura mater of the brain that drain blood from the brain to the sigmoid sinus

Examples

sinus transversus of brain
During brain surgery, care must be taken not to damage the sinus transversus.
Imaging revealed a thrombosis in the patient's right sinus transversus.
The sinus transversus collects blood from the brain and drains it toward the sigmoid sinus.
