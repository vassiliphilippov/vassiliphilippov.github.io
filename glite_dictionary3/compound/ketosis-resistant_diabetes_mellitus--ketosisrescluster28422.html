ketosis-resistant diabetes mellitus

noun
mild adult-onset diabetes managed without insulin
Pronunciation: / kɪˌtoʊsɪs rɪˈzɪstənt daɪəˈbiːtiz məˈlaɪtəs / (AmE), / kɪˌtəʊsɪs rɪˈzɪstənt daɪəˈbiːtiːz mɛˈlaɪtəs / (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus that develops gradually in adults, often triggered by obesity, stress, menopause, or other factors, and can usually be managed with diet and oral hypoglycemic agents rather than insulin injections.

Examples

ketosis-resistant diabetes mellitus in adults
Many cases of ketosis-resistant diabetes mellitus can be managed successfully with lifestyle modifications and oral medications.
Obesity and menopause are both risk factors for developing ketosis-resistant diabetes mellitus.
Unlike type 1 diabetes, ketosis-resistant diabetes mellitus rarely requires insulin injections for control.

Related Terms

  • antonym – type 1 diabetes: an autoimmune form of diabetes requiring insulin therapy
  • synonym – type 2 diabetes: a common form of diabetes associated with insulin resistance and adult onset
