affine geometry

noun
geometry of affine transformations
Pronunciation: / əˈfaɪn dʒiˈɑːmətri / (AmE), / əˈfaɪn dʒiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
A branch of geometry that studies the properties of figures that remain unchanged under affine transformations, such as translation, scaling, and shear.

Examples

affine geometry studies transformations
In affine geometry, parallel lines remain parallel after any affine transformation.
Many algorithms in computer graphics are founded on the principles of affine geometry.
Unlike Euclidean geometry, affine geometry does not preserve angles or distances.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Euclidean geometry: geometry based on Euclid's axioms
  • synonym – projective geometry: geometry dealing with properties unchanged by projection
