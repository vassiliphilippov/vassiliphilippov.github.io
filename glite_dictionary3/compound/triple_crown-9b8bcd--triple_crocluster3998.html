triple crown

noun
baseball title for batting, RBIs, and home runs
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪpəl kraʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpl kraʊn / (BrE)
(Baseball) An unofficial title awarded to a batter who leads the league in batting average, runs batted in, and home runs in a single season.

Examples

win the triple crown in baseball
Miguel Cabrera achieved the triple crown in 2012, breaking a decades-long drought.
Only a few players in history have won the triple crown in Major League Baseball.
Winning the triple crown is considered one of the greatest achievements for a hitter.
