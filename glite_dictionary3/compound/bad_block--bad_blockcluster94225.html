bad block

noun
unusable storage section (computing)
Pronunciation: / bæd blɑk / (AmE), / bad blɒk / (BrE)
(computing) a section of storage, usually one sector, that cannot reliably store or retrieve data due to physical damage or corruption.

Examples

disk bad block
Data loss occurred because the disk contained several bad blocks that could not be repaired.
If a file is stored on a bad block, it may become unreadable.
The utility will scan your hard drive for any bad blocks and attempt to isolate them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bad sector: an area on a hard drive or storage device that is unusable
