heteroclitic antibody

noun
antibody with higher affinity for a different antigen
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˌklɪtɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɑː.di / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˌklɪtɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɒd.i / (BrE)
an antibody produced in response to immunization with one antigen but having a higher affinity for a different antigen not present during immunization

Examples

heteroclitic antibody binds antigen
Researchers observed that the heteroclitic antibody in the serum bound more strongly to a different antigen than the one used for immunization.
Some vaccines can induce heteroclitic antibody responses that cross-react with related pathogens.
The discovery of a heteroclitic antibody helped explain the unexpected immune protection in the experiment.
