brain mushroom

noun
poisonous wrinkled mushroom (Gyromitra)
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪn ˌmʌʃruːm / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌmʌʃrʊm / (BrE)
A poisonous mushroom of the genus Gyromitra, with a cap that is initially smooth and later becomes convoluted and wrinkled, resembling a brain; color ranges from dull yellow to brown.

Examples

poisonous brain mushroom
Foragers sometimes mistake the brain mushroom for edible varieties, leading to accidental poisoning.
The brain mushroom should never be eaten because it contains dangerous toxins.
The cap of the brain mushroom has a distinctive wrinkled appearance that resembles the folds of a brain.
