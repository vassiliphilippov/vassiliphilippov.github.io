Second Vatican Council

noun
1962–1965 Catholic Church council of reforms
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkənd ˈvætɪkən ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / ˈsekənd ˈvætɪkən ˈkaʊnsl / (BrE)
the ecumenical council of the Roman Catholic Church held from 1962 to 1965, which reformed church practices, promoted ecumenism, and replaced the universal use of Latin in liturgy

Examples

the Second Vatican Council reforms
His research focuses on the impact of the Second Vatican Council on modern theology.
Many changes in Catholic worship practices were introduced by the Second Vatican Council.
The Second Vatican Council encouraged greater dialogue with other Christian denominations.
