joint snake

noun
legless lizard with regenerative tail
Pronunciation: / dʒɔɪnt sneɪk / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt sneɪk / (BrE)
A legless lizard found in Europe, Asia, and North America, known for its fragile tail that can detach and regenerate; often referred to in folklore for its perceived ability to join itself after being cut.

Examples

a joint snake in folklore
In some rural legends, people believed that a joint snake could come back to life if its severed pieces were placed together.
The joint snake is actually a harmless legless lizard, not a true snake at all.
When threatened, a joint snake can shed its tail to escape predators, later growing it back.
