Star of David

noun
six-pointed Jewish religious symbol
Pronunciation: / stɑr əv ˈdeɪvɪd / (AmE), / stɑːr əv ˈdeɪvɪd / (BrE)
a six-pointed star formed from two overlapping equilateral triangles, used as a symbol of Judaism and Jewish identity

Examples

Star of David symbol
He wore a necklace with a small Star of David pendant.
Many synagogues display a large Star of David above the entrance.
The Star of David appears on the flag of Israel as a symbol of Jewish identity.
