secured bond

noun
bond backed by collateral
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkjʊrd bɑnd / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊəd bɒnd / (BrE)
A bond that is backed by specific assets or collateral, providing security to the lender if the issuer defaults.

Examples

secured bond backed by assets
Because investors have a claim on specific assets, secured bonds are generally considered less risky than unsecured ones.
If the issuer defaults, holders of the secured bond may be entitled to the underlying collateral.
The company issued a secured bond to finance its new factory, using its real estate as collateral.

  • antonym – unsecured bond: a bond not backed by collateral
