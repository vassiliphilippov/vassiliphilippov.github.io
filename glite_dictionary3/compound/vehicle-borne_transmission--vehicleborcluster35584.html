vehicle-borne transmission

noun
indirect infection via contaminated objects or substances
Pronunciation: / ˈviː.ɪ.kəl bɔrn trænzˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈviː.ɪ.kəl bɔːn trænzˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
indirect transmission of an infectious agent that occurs when a contaminated object or substance (vehicle) touches a person's body or is ingested, leading to infection

Examples

vehicle-borne transmission of disease
Health professionals educate the public about the risks of vehicle-borne transmission during disease outbreaks.
Proper sterilization of medical equipment helps prevent vehicle-borne transmission of infections in hospitals.
Salmonella outbreaks are often linked to vehicle-borne transmission through contaminated food or water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vector-borne transmission: transmission of disease via living organisms like insects
