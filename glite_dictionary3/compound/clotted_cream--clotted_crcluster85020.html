clotted cream

noun
thick cream from scalded milk
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑːt̬ɪd ˈkriːm / (AmE), / ˈklɒtɪd ˈkriːm / (BrE)
A thick, rich cream made by heating unpasteurized milk and allowing it to cool so the cream rises and thickens on the surface, especially common in British cuisine.

Examples

clotted cream on scones
Devon is famous for its traditional clotted cream, often enjoyed with afternoon tea.
The scones were served with a generous dollop of clotted cream and strawberry jam.
You can buy clotted cream at specialty stores, but it's best when freshly made.
