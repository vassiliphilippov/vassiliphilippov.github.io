probate court

noun
court for wills and estates
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌbeɪt kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌbeɪt kɔːt / (BrE)
a court that has the authority to validate wills and supervise the administration of estates after death

Examples

probate court handles wills
If there is a dispute over the will, the case goes to probate court.
She appeared before the probate court to be appointed as executor.
The probate court will decide who inherits the property.
