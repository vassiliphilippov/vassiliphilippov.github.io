seed plant

noun
plant that reproduces by seeds
Pronunciation: / sid plænt / (AmE), / siːd plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that reproduces by producing seeds, rather than spores

Examples

seed plant produces seeds
Most flowering plants are considered seed plants because they reproduce by forming seeds.
The pine tree is a classic example of a seed plant found in northern forests.
Unlike ferns, a seed plant does not rely on spores for reproduction.
