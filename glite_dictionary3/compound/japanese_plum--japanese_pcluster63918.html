Japanese plum

noun
a semitropical fruit with a large stone
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz plʌm / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn.iːz plʌm / (BrE)
A yellow, olive-sized semitropical fruit with a large central stone and little flesh, used for making jellies.

Examples

ripe Japanese plum fruit
She made a delicious jam from fresh Japanese plum fruit picked from her garden.
The Japanese plum has a unique flavor that is both sweet and tart, perfect for desserts.
We planted a Japanese plum tree in our backyard last spring.
