boot camp

noun
intensive training program (often military or fitness)
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːt kæmp / (AmE), / ˈbuːt kæmp / (BrE)
a short, intensive training program designed to improve fitness, skills, or discipline, often in a military or fitness setting

Examples

fitness boot camp
After college graduation, he attended a coding boot camp to jumpstart his career in tech.
Many people join a fitness boot camp to quickly improve their stamina and lose weight.
The boot camp lasted six weeks and included rigorous daily workouts and strict discipline.

  • synonym – training camp: place for organized instruction or practice
