jet lag

noun
fatigue after flying across time zones
Pronunciation: / dʒɛt læɡ / (AmE), / dʒɛt læɡ / (BrE)
A feeling of tiredness and temporary sleep disturbance experienced after long flights across different time zones, caused by disruption of the body's internal clock.

Examples

suffer from jet lag
Adjusting your schedule before your flight can help reduce jet lag.
Frequent travelers often develop strategies to minimize jet lag.
She struggled with jet lag for days after arriving in Australia.

Related Terms

  • antonym – alertness: the state of being awake and attentive
  • synonym – fatigue: extreme tiredness from physical or mental exertion
