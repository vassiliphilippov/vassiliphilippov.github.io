sealing wax

noun
resin for sealing letters or documents
Pronunciation: / ˈsilɪŋ ˌwæks / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɪŋ ˌwæks / (BrE)
A substance made from resin and other materials, which becomes soft when heated and is used to seal letters, documents, or packages so they cannot be opened without breaking the seal.

Examples

melt sealing wax on envelope
He pressed the signet ring into the warm sealing wax to secure the document.
In historical dramas, letters are often closed with red sealing wax.
She enjoyed using sealing wax and a stamped emblem on her invitations.

  • synonym – seal: a device or substance used to close something securely
