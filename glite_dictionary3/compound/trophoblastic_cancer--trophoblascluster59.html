trophoblastic cancer

noun
malignant uterine tumor from chorionic epithelium
Pronunciation: / ˌtroʊfəˈblæstɪk ˈkænsər / (AmE), / ˌtrɒfəˈblæstɪk ˈkɑːnsə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of the uterus originating from the chorionic epithelium, often occurring during or after pregnancy.

Examples

trophoblastic cancer after pregnancy
After her miscarriage, the doctors discovered a rare form of trophoblastic cancer.
Regular follow-ups are essential after pregnancy to detect any signs of trophoblastic cancer early.
Treatment for trophoblastic cancer may include surgery and chemotherapy.
