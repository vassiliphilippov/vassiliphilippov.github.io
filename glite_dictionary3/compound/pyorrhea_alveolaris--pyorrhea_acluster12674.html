pyorrhea alveolaris

noun
chronic gum inflammation with pus
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪəˈriːə ˌælviəˈleɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈriːə ˌalvɪəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A severe, chronic inflammation of the sockets of the teeth involving pus formation, often resulting in the loosening or loss of teeth.

Examples

pyorrhea alveolaris damages gums
Poor dental hygiene can contribute to the development of pyorrhea alveolaris, which may require intensive treatment.
Symptoms of pyorrhea alveolaris include persistent bad breath, bleeding gums, and loosening of the teeth.
The dentist diagnosed him with pyorrhea alveolaris after noticing severe gum inflammation and pus.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – gingivitis: inflammation of the gums; less severe than periodontitis
  • synonym – periodontitis: inflammation of the tissues surrounding the teeth
