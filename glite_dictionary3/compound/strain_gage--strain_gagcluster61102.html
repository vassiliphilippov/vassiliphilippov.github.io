strain gage

noun
device for measuring surface strain
Pronunciation: / streɪn ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / streɪn ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a device used to measure strain (deformation) in a surface or material

Examples

strain gage sensor
Modern bridges often use strain gages to ensure structural safety over time.
The engineer attached a strain gage to the beam to monitor its deformation during the test.
When the metal bar was bent, the strain gage provided precise measurements of the strain.
