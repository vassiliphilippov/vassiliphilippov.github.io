sustainable agriculture

noun
environmentally friendly farming method
Pronunciation: / səˈsteɪnəbl ˌæɡrɪˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / səˈsteɪnəbl ˌæɡrɪˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
a method of farming that meets current needs without depleting natural resources or harming the environment, ensuring long-term agricultural productivity

Examples

sustainable agriculture practices
By practicing sustainable agriculture, the farmers ensured healthy soil for future generations.
Many universities now offer courses in sustainable agriculture to promote environmentally responsible farming practices.
The government provided grants to encourage sustainable agriculture in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – conventional agriculture: traditional farming methods not focused on environmental sustainability
  • synonym – organic farming: agriculture without synthetic chemicals
