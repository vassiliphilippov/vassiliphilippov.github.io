designated hitter

noun
baseball player who bats instead of pitcher
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzɪɡˌneɪtɪd ˈhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛzɪɡneɪtɪd ˈhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a player chosen to bat in place of the pitcher, especially in the American League.

Examples

designated hitter bats instead
As a designated hitter, she didn’t have to play the field but focused solely on batting.
David Ortiz was one of the greatest designated hitters in baseball history.
The team's designated hitter homered in the first inning.
