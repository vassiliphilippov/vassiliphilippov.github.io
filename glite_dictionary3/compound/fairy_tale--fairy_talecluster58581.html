fairy tale

noun
an implausible or untrue story
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛri teɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeə.ri teɪl / (BrE)
A story that is highly implausible or untrue, often used as an excuse or to deceive.

Examples

made-up fairy tale
Her explanation for being late was just another fairy tale to avoid getting into trouble.
The politician's promises turned out to be nothing but a fairy tale.
When asked about the missing funds, he spun a fairy tale that nobody believed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fabrication: a story or statement that is invented and not true
  • antonym – truth: what is true or in accordance with fact or reality
