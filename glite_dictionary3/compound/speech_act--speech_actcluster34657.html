speech act

noun
linguistic action performed via speech
Pronunciation: / spiːtʃ ækt / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ækt / (BrE)
an utterance considered as an action, particularly in terms of its intention, effect, or performative function, rather than merely its content

Examples

make a speech act
Making a request is one kind of speech act in everyday conversation.
The promise he made was analyzed as a speech act in the linguistics seminar.
When you apologize to someone, you are performing a speech act rather than just stating a fact.

  • synonym – utterance: a spoken word, statement, or vocal sound
