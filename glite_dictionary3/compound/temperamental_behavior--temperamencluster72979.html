temperamental behavior

noun
unpredictable or changeable behavior
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛmpəɹəˈmɛnɾəl bəˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntl bəˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
behavior marked by unpredictable or sudden changes in mood or attitude

Examples

unpredictable temperamental behavior
Her temperamental behavior made it difficult to anticipate how she would react in new situations.
His temperamental behavior often frustrated colleagues who preferred a more predictable work environment.
Many directors have tolerated actors' temperamental behavior because of their undeniable talent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – moody: frequently changing mood, often without apparent reason
  • antonym – stable: not likely to change suddenly or unexpectedly
