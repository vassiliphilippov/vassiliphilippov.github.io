regulatory gene

noun
gene controlling other genes' activity
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələˌtɔri ˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlətəri ˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that controls the expression of one or more other genes by producing a substance, often a repressor, that influences their activity

Examples

regulatory gene controls expression
A mutation in the regulatory gene caused the downstream genes to be expressed uncontrollably.
Researchers discovered that the regulatory gene codes for a protein that binds to the operator region.
