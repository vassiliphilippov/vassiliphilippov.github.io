spasmodic laryngitis

noun
disease causing sudden coughing in children
Pronunciation: / spæzˈmɑdɪk ˌlærɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / spæzˈmɒdɪk ˌlærɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A disease of infants and young children characterized by sudden attacks of harsh coughing, hoarseness, fever, and difficulty breathing.

Examples

spasmodic laryngitis in children
Doctors diagnosed him with spasmodic laryngitis when he developed a harsh, barking cough during the night.
The child was rushed to the hospital after a severe episode of spasmodic laryngitis caused breathing difficulties.
Tim's parents were worried when his spasmodic laryngitis symptoms worsened suddenly in the early morning.
