chills and fever

noun
alternating chills and fever, common in infections
Pronunciation: / tʃɪlz ənd ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / tʃɪlz ənd ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A condition involving alternating episodes of shivering (chills) and high body temperature (fever), often associated with infections such as malaria.

Examples

suffer chills and fever
Repeated episodes of chills and fever led the doctor to suspect a malarial infection.
The patient complained of chills and fever that began two days after travel to a tropical region.
When you have malaria, the chills and fever can make you feel extremely weak.

Related Terms

  • synonym – malaria: A serious disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing chills and fever
