sample distribution

noun
statistical set of randomly selected items
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmpəl dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmpəl dɪsˌtrɪbjuːˈʃən / (BrE)
a set of values or items selected at random from a larger population, used to test hypotheses or draw inferences about that population

Examples

sample distribution of test scores
If the sample distribution is not representative, the results may be misleading.
The researcher analyzed the sample distribution to estimate the average income of the city.
Understanding the sample distribution is essential when performing statistical tests in science.

Related Terms

  • antonym – population: the complete set of items or individuals relevant to a statistical study
  • synonym – sampling distribution: the probability distribution of a given statistic based on a random sample
