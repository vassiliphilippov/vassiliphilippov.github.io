three-point turn

noun
driving maneuver for turning in limited space
Pronunciation: / ˈθri pɔɪnt tɝn / (AmE), / ˈθri pɔɪnt tɜːn / (BrE)
a driving maneuver for turning a vehicle around in a limited space by moving forward, in reverse, and then forward again to face the opposite direction

Examples

make a three-point turn
It took her just a minute to complete a perfect three-point turn and head back the way she came.
Learning how to do a three-point turn is essential for passing your driving test.
The instructor asked me to perform a three-point turn on the narrow street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – K-turn: another name for a three-point turn, especially in American English
  • synonym – U-turn: a turn made to go in the opposite direction, usually in one continuous movement
