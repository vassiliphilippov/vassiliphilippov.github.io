Strait of Hormuz

proper noun
narrow strait between Persian Gulf and Gulf of Oman
Pronunciation: / streɪt əv hɔrˈmuz / (AmE), / streɪt əv hɔːˈmuːz / (BrE)
a narrow, strategically significant strait between the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman, through which a large portion of the world's oil passes

Examples

the Strait of Hormuz oil route
International naval forces regularly patrol the Strait of Hormuz to ensure the safe passage of ships.
Much of the world's oil supply travels through the Strait of Hormuz every day.
Tensions in the Middle East often focus on the Strait of Hormuz due to its strategic location.
