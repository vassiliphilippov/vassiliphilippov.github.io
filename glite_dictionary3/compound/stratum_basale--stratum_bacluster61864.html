stratum basale

noun
deepest layer of the epidermis
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪ.təm bəˈseɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈstrɑː.təm bəˈseɪ.li / (BrE)
the deepest layer of the epidermis, made up of basal cells that continuously divide to form new skin cells

Examples

the stratum basale of skin
Damage to the stratum basale can lead to impaired wound healing.
Melanocytes, which give skin its color, are located within the stratum basale.
The stratum basale plays a crucial role in generating new skin cells.
