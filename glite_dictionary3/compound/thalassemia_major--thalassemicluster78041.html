thalassemia major

noun
severe inherited blood disorder
Pronunciation: / θəˌlæsəˈmiːə ˈmeɪdʒɚ / (AmE), / θəˌlæsɪˈmiːə ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
A severe inherited blood disorder, caused by receiving thalassemia genes from both parents, resulting in absent or severely reduced hemoglobin, skeletal abnormalities, and enlargement of the heart, spleen, and liver.

Examples

thalassemia major diagnosis in children
Advances in treatment have improved the prognosis for those born with thalassemia major.
Bone changes can occur in individuals suffering from thalassemia major due to the overactive bone marrow.
Children diagnosed with thalassemia major require regular blood transfusions throughout their lives.
