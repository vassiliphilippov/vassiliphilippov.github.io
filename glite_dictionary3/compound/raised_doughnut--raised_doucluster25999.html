raised doughnut

noun
yeast-raised leavened doughnut
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪzd ˈdoʊˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈreɪzd ˈdəʊ.nʌt / (BrE)
A doughnut made light and fluffy with yeast as a leavening agent, rather than baking powder.

Examples

fresh raised doughnut
Many people prefer a raised doughnut because of its airy texture and lighter taste.
The bakery specializes in raised doughnuts topped with chocolate glaze and sprinkles.
Unlike a cake doughnut, a raised doughnut is made with yeast and fried until golden brown.
