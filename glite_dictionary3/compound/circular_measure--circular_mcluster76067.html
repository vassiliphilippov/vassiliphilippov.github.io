circular measure

noun
angle measurement in radians
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrkjələr ˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊlə ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
A method of measuring angles by expressing them in radians, where one full revolution equals 2π radians.

Examples

circular measure in radians
In mathematics, angles are often given in circular measure rather than degrees.
Scientists prefer circular measure when working with trigonometric functions.
The formula for arc length uses the circular measure of the angle.

Related Terms

  • antonym – degree: unit for measuring angles, with one revolution equal to 360 degrees
  • synonym – radian: unit of angular measure based on the radius of a circle
