liquid assets

noun
assets readily convertible to cash
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈæsɛts / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈæsɛts / (BrE)
assets in the form of cash or those that can be quickly and easily converted into cash without significant loss of value

Examples

liquid assets in cash
Banks are required to maintain a certain level of liquid assets for regulatory purposes.
Selling investments can help free up liquid assets during a financial emergency.
The company increased its liquid assets to cover unexpected expenses.

  • antonym – illiquid assets: assets not easily convertible into cash
