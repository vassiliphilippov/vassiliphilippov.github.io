podzol soil

noun
acidic soil with leached layer under conifers
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑdzɔl sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɒdzɒl sɔɪl / (BrE)
A type of soil found in temperate to cold, moist climates, typically under coniferous or heath vegetation, consisting of an organic mat over a leached, grey layer.

Examples

podzol soil in pine forests
In northern forests, podzol soil develops beneath layers of pine needles and moss.
Many crops struggle to grow in podzol soil due to its low nutrient content.
Researchers analyze podzol soil to understand boreal forest ecosystems.
