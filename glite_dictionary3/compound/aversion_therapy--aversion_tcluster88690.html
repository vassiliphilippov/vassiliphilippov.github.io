aversion therapy

noun
behavioral therapy using unpleasant stimuli
Pronunciation: / əˈvɝʒən ˌθɛrəpi / (AmE), / əˈvɜːʃən ˌθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a psychological treatment using unpleasant stimuli to reduce or eliminate undesirable behaviors such as addiction

Examples

aversion therapy for addiction
During aversion therapy, a mild electric shock may be used to discourage unwanted habits.
Some psychologists question the long-term effectiveness of aversion therapy.
The clinic recommended aversion therapy for patients struggling with substance abuse.
