old guard

noun
conservative, long-serving group resisting change
Pronunciation: / oʊld ɡɑrd / (AmE), / əʊld ɡɑːd / (BrE)
A group of established or long-serving members of an organization or movement who are resistant to new ideas or changes.

Examples

the old guard resists change
As new technologies emerged, the old guard struggled to maintain its influence.
Many young members wanted reforms, but the old guard insisted on following tradition.
The old guard in the company opposed the new management's innovative strategies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – progressive: person favoring reform and new ideas
  • synonym – traditionalist: person favoring tradition and resisting change
