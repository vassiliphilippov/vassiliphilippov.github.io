out of context

adverb
without considering full circumstances
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ˈkɑntɛkst / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈkɒntɛkst / (BrE)
without considering the full circumstances or surrounding information, especially in a way that distorts the intended meaning

Examples

out of context quote
His statement was quoted out of context, making it sound much more serious than he intended.
The article took her remarks out of context to support its argument.
When you read the sentence out of context, its meaning completely changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – in context: with full consideration of the surrounding circumstances or original situation
