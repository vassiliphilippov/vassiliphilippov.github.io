heat of formation

noun
heat change when forming one mole of a compound
Pronunciation: / hit əv fɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv fɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount of heat released or absorbed when one mole of a compound is formed from its constituent elements under standard conditions

Examples

heat of formation of water
A negative heat of formation indicates that energy is released during the formation of a compound.
Calculating the heat of formation helps chemists predict the energy changes in reactions.
The heat of formation of water is commonly measured in thermochemistry experiments.
