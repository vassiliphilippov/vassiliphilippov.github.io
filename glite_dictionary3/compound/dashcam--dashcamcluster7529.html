dashcam

noun
dashboard-mounted video camera for vehicles
Pronunciation: / ˈdæʃˌkæm / (AmE), / ˈdæʃˌkam / (BrE)
A small camera mounted on the dashboard or windscreen of a vehicle, used to record the view through the front or rear windows while driving.

Examples

dashcam footage
After reviewing the dashcam footage, it was clear that the other car had run the red light.
Many taxi drivers install a dashcam to record their journeys for safety and insurance purposes.
The driver's dashcam captured the accident clearly, helping police determine what happened.
