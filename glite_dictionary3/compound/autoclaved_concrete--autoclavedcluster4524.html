autoclaved concrete

noun
lightweight, precast concrete cured in an autoclave
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd kɑnˈkrit / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd ˈkɒnkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast concrete building material made by curing concrete under high pressure and temperature in an autoclave, offering structural strength as well as insulation and resistance to fire and mold
