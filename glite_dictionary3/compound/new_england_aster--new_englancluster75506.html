New England aster

noun
perennial with showy purple or pink flowers (plant)
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈɪŋɡlənd ˈæstər / (AmE), / njuː ˈɪŋɡlənd ˈæstə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of eastern North America with showy purple or pink flower heads, known scientifically as Symphyotrichum novae-angliae, and cultivated as an ornamental; a parent of the Michaelmas daisy.
