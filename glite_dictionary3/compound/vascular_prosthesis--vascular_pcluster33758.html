vascular prosthesis

noun
artificial blood vessel (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈvæskjələr prɑsˈθisɪs / (AmE), / ˈvaskjʊlə prɒsˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
an artificial blood vessel implanted to replace or bypass damaged vessels during surgery

Examples

implant a vascular prosthesis
Advances in materials have improved the longevity of the modern vascular prosthesis.
Patients with a vascular prosthesis must be monitored regularly for potential complications.
The surgeon implanted a vascular prosthesis to restore blood flow to the patient's leg.
