tank locomotive

noun
steam locomotive carrying fuel and water on itself
Pronunciation: / tæŋk ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.tɪv / (AmE), / tæŋk ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
a steam locomotive that carries its own fuel and water in tanks mounted on the engine, so it does not require a separate tender

Examples

old tank locomotive train
Because a tank locomotive does not require a tender, it is ideal for short journeys and branch lines.
Engineers praised the tank locomotive for its ability to easily reverse direction on tight tracks.
The old railway museum displays a beautifully restored tank locomotive from the late nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym – tender locomotive: a locomotive that pulls a separate vehicle (tender) for fuel and water
