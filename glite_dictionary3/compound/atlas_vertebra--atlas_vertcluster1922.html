atlas vertebra

noun
first cervical vertebra supporting the skull
Pronunciation: / ˈætləs vərˈtebrə / (AmE), / ˈætləs ˈvɜːtɪbrə / (BrE)
the first cervical vertebra of the spine, supporting the skull and allowing nodding movement of the head

Examples

fractured atlas vertebra
A fracture of the atlas vertebra can result in serious spinal cord injuries.
Doctors examined the patient's atlas vertebra on the X-ray to check for abnormalities.
The atlas vertebra supports the skull and allows the head to nod up and down.
