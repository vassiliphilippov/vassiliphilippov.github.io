carbon cycle

noun
process of carbon exchange in nature
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
the continuous process by which carbon is exchanged between the atmosphere, living organisms, and the environment

Examples

global carbon cycle
Deforestation can disrupt the carbon cycle by reducing the number of trees that absorb carbon dioxide.
Scientists study how the carbon cycle responds to rising global temperatures.
The carbon cycle is crucial for maintaining the Earth's climate balance.
