fault line

noun
geological line showing a fault
Pronunciation: / fɔlt laɪn / (AmE), / fɔːlt laɪn / (BrE)
(Geology) a line on the Earth's surface where a fault has caused a break or displacement of the ground.

Examples

major fault line
Scientists study fault lines to assess the risk of seismic activity in the region.
The San Andreas fault line is notorious for earthquakes in California.
The city was built dangerously close to a major fault line.
