fine-textured

adjective
having a smooth, fine-grained structure
Pronunciation: / faɪn ˈtɛkstʃɚd / (AmE), / faɪn ˈtɛkstʃəd / (BrE)
Having a smooth or fine-grained structure; composed of small, delicate, or closely packed parts.

Examples

fine-textured hair
Her hair was remarkably fine-textured, soft and silky to the touch.
The pastry chef preferred a fine-textured flour for making delicate cakes.
This painting is done on a fine-textured canvas that helps with intricate details.

  • antonym – coarse-textured: having a rough or large-grained structure
