ball bearing

noun
bearing with small metal balls
Pronunciation: / bɔl ˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / bɔːl ˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
A bearing that contains small metal balls to reduce friction between moving parts.

Examples

ball bearing in a machine
A damaged ball bearing can cause noise and vibration in machinery.
Smooth movement is ensured by well-lubricated ball bearings.
The mechanic replaced the worn ball bearing in the wheel hub.
