stock-taker

noun
person doing inventory counts
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑk ˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstɒk ˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to count and record the goods or materials held in stock at a business

Examples

stock-taker checks inventory
An external stock-taker was brought in to ensure accuracy in the warehouse records.
As a stock-taker, she carefully checked every item in the store's back room.
The manager hired an experienced stock-taker to conduct the annual inventory.
