title deed

noun
legal document proving ownership of property
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtl̩ did / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtl̩ diːd / (BrE)
An official legal document showing a person's ownership of property, especially land or buildings.

Examples

title deed for a house
She found the title deed to the house in her grandfather's desk.
When they purchased the property, the title deed was transferred to their names.
Without the title deed, it was difficult to prove ownership of the land.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deed: a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially one regarding ownership of property or legal rights
