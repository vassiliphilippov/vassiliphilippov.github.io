twill weave

noun
weaving pattern with diagonal ribs
Pronunciation: / twɪl wiːv / (AmE), / twɪl wiːv / (BrE)
A method of weaving in which the threads are arranged to produce a pattern of parallel diagonal ribs.

Examples

twill weave pattern
Denim is typically made using a twill weave, giving it a distinct diagonal pattern.
The fabric's twill weave makes it durable and resistant to tearing.
You can recognize a twill weave by its characteristic diagonal ribs.
