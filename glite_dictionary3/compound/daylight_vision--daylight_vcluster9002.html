daylight vision

noun
vision under daylight or bright conditions
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌlaɪt ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪlaɪt ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the mode of vision under normal daylight or bright conditions, in which the eye's cones are active and colors are perceived

Examples

daylight vision sees colors
Humans rely on daylight vision to distinguish colors and fine details in their environment.
The retinas' cones are responsible for daylight vision and allow us to see a vivid spectrum of colors.
With age, some people experience a decline in daylight vision, making reading outside more difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym – scotopic vision: vision in low light involving the rods and no color perception
