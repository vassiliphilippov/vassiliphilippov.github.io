railroad train

noun
series of railway cars pulled by a locomotive
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌrɔd treɪn / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊd treɪn / (BrE)
A connected series of railway cars that transport people or goods, pulled by a locomotive on railway tracks.

Examples

a railroad train arrives
He watched a long railroad train thunder past the crossing, its cars loaded with containers.
Local commuters rely on the railroad train for their daily trip into the city.
The railroad train arrived exactly on schedule, impressing all the passengers on the platform.
