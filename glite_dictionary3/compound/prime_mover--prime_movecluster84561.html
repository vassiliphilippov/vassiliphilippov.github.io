prime mover

noun
first cause; uncaused cause (philosophy, theology)
Pronunciation: / praɪm ˈmuvɚ / (AmE), / praɪm ˈmuːvə / (BrE)
(in philosophy) an agent that is the initial cause of all things, itself uncaused, especially in reference to the first cause or God in philosophical and theological contexts.

Examples

the prime mover in philosophy
Aristotle described the prime mover as something that causes all motion but is itself unmoved.
Many philosophers have debated the existence of a prime mover who set the universe in motion.
The concept of a prime mover is central to certain arguments for the existence of God.
