true lobster

noun
clawed edible marine crustacean
Pronunciation: / tru ˈlɑb.stɚ / (AmE), / tru ˈlɒb.stə / (BrE)
A large edible marine crustacean of the family Nephropidae, distinguished by the presence of large pincers (claws) on its first pair of legs.

Examples

fresh true lobster claws
Freshly caught true lobsters are often featured in fine dining restaurants.
The true lobster is highly prized for its sweet, tender meat.
Unlike the spiny lobster, the true lobster has large, powerful claws.

Related Terms

  • antonym – spiny lobster: a lobster-like crustacean without large claws
