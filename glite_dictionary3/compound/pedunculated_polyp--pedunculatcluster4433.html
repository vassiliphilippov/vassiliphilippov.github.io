pedunculated polyp

noun
polyp with a stalk
Pronunciation: / pəˈdʌŋkjəˌleɪtɪd ˈpɑːlɪp / (AmE), / pɪˈdʌŋkjʊˌleɪtɪd ˈpɒlɪp / (BrE)
a polyp that is attached to the surrounding tissue by a stalk or peduncle

Examples

pedunculated polyp with stalk
A pedunculated polyp can often be distinguished from a sessile polyp by its stalk.
Biopsy confirmed that the growth was a benign pedunculated polyp.
The doctor removed a pedunculated polyp during the colonoscopy.
