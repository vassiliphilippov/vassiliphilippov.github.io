sedative-hypnotic drug

noun
drug inducing calm and sleep
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛdəˌtɪv hɪpˈnɑtɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌsɛdətɪv hɪpˈnɒtɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that depresses activity of the central nervous system, reduces anxiety, and induces sleep; used as a sedative and hypnotic

Examples

a sedative-hypnotic drug for sleep
Misuse of a sedative-hypnotic drug can lead to dependence and withdrawal symptoms.
Researchers are studying the effects of a new sedative-hypnotic drug on anxiety disorders.
The doctor prescribed a sedative-hypnotic drug to help the patient sleep through the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stimulant: a drug that increases activity of the central nervous system
  • synonym – tranquilizer: a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety
