edge of the cliff

noun
outer part of a steep drop-off
Pronunciation: / ɛdʒ əv ðə klɪf / (AmE), / ɛdʒ əv ðə klɪf / (BrE)
the outermost part or boundary of a steep, vertical or near-vertical rock face, beyond which lies a sharp drop

Examples

Birds often nest in ledges just below the edge of the cliff.

The wind was strong near the edge of the cliff, making it dangerous to stand too close.
