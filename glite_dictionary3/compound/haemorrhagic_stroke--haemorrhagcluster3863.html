haemorrhagic stroke

noun
stroke caused by bleeding in the brain
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmɚˈrædʒɪk stroʊk / (AmE), / ˌhɛmərˈrædʒɪk strəʊk / (BrE)
A type of stroke caused by bleeding from a ruptured blood vessel in the brain, leading to damage to surrounding brain tissue.

Examples

suffer a haemorrhagic stroke
A haemorrhagic stroke requires immediate medical attention to minimize brain damage.
She was diagnosed with a haemorrhagic stroke after collapsing at home.
The patient survived a haemorrhagic stroke but needed extensive rehabilitation afterward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ischemic stroke: stroke caused by blockage of a blood vessel in the brain
