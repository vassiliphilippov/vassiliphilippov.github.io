foreign draft

noun
bill of exchange payable in another country
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrən dræft / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən drɑːft / (BrE)
a bill of exchange drawn in one country and made payable in another country

Examples

foreign draft payment overseas
Many banks charge a fee for processing a foreign draft due to international transaction costs.
The company paid its overseas supplier by issuing a foreign draft.
To pay tuition at the university abroad, she obtained a foreign draft from her bank.
