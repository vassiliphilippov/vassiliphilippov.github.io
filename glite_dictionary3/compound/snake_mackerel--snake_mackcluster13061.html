snake mackerel

noun
predatory tropical fish with long jaws and sharp teeth
Pronunciation: / sneɪk ˈmækəɹəl / (AmE), / sneɪk ˈmækərəl / (BrE)
A predatory tropical fish with long, slender body, jutting jaws, and strong teeth, typically found in deep ocean waters.

Examples

deep-sea snake mackerel
Because of its long, slender body, the snake mackerel can move quickly to catch its prey.
Fishermen often encounter snake mackerel in warm, deep ocean waters.
The snake mackerel is known for its sharp teeth and aggressive hunting behavior.
