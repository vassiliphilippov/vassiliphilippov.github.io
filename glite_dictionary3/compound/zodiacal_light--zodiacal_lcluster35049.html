zodiacal light

noun
faint light seen near the horizon at dawn or dusk
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈdaɪ.ək.əl laɪt / (AmE), / zəʊˈdaɪ.ək.əl laɪt / (BrE)
A faint, diffuse, cone-shaped band of light seen in the night sky along the ecliptic just before sunrise or just after sunset, caused by sunlight reflecting off interplanetary dust.

Examples

see zodiacal light at dawn
Astronomers often study the zodiacal light to learn more about dust in the solar system.
The zodiacal light is best seen far from city lights on clear, dark nights.
We woke before dawn to catch a glimpse of the zodiacal light stretching across the eastern sky.
