oil cartel

noun
group controlling oil production and prices
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl kɑrˈtɛl / (AmE), / ɔɪl kɑːˈtɛl / (BrE)
a group of companies or countries that collaborate to control the production, pricing, and distribution of oil

Examples

oil cartel controls prices
OPEC is the most influential oil cartel in the world today.
Some economists argue that an oil cartel can artificially inflate global prices.
The formation of an oil cartel has significant implications for international relations.
