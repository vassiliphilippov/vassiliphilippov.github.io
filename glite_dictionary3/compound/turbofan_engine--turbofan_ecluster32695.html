turbofan engine

noun
jet engine with turbine-driven fan
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.boʊ.fæn ˈɛn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bə.fæn ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A type of jet engine in which a turbine-driven fan forces additional air into the engine to provide extra thrust and improve efficiency.

Examples

modern turbofan engine
Engineers tested the latest turbofan engine in a wind tunnel to analyze its thrust output.
Most commercial airliners today are powered by turbofan engines due to their efficiency and reliability.
The new fighter jet features a high-bypass turbofan engine for improved performance and reduced noise.
