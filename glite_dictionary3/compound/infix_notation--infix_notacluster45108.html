infix notation

noun
mathematical expression format with operators between operands
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfɪks noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌfɪks nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A way of writing mathematical expressions in which operators are placed between operands and parentheses may be used to indicate order of operations.

Examples

infix notation math expression
In infix notation, multiplication is written as 3 × 4 rather than placing the operator at the end or beginning.
Most calculators use infix notation for performing arithmetic operations.
The equation in infix notation can be converted to postfix notation by following certain rules.

Related Terms

  • antonym – postfix notation: notation where operators follow their operands
  • antonym – prefix notation: notation where operators precede their operands
