Jehovah's Witnesses

noun
Protestant Christian denomination; evangelistic group
Pronunciation: / dʒɪˈhoʊvə(z) ˈwɪtnəsɪz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈhəʊvə(z) ˈwɪtnəsɪz / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination, founded in the United States in 1884 by Charles Taze Russell, known for distinct beliefs and active evangelism.

Examples

Jehovah's Witnesses knock doors
Jehovah's Witnesses are known for refusing blood transfusions on religious grounds.
