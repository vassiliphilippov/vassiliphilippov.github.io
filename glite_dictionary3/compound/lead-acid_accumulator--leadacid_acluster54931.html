lead-acid accumulator

noun
rechargeable battery with lead and acid
Pronunciation: / lɛd ˈæsɪd əˌkjumjəˈleɪtɚ / (AmE), / lɛd ˈæsɪd əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a type of rechargeable battery that uses lead electrodes and dilute sulfuric acid as the electrolyte, commonly used in vehicles and backup power systems

Examples

lead-acid accumulator in a car
A lead-acid accumulator is often used in solar power systems to store energy.
The car wouldn't start because its lead-acid accumulator was dead.
You must handle a lead-acid accumulator carefully due to the corrosive acid inside.
