dry gangrene

noun
type of gangrene with dry, dark tissue
Pronunciation: / draɪ ˈɡæŋˌɡrin / (AmE), / draɪ ˈɡæŋɡriːn / (BrE)
A form of gangrene caused by arterial obstruction, resulting in dry, dark brown dead tissue.

Examples

dry gangrene in toes
If untreated, dry gangrene can lead to the loss of affected limbs.
Older adults with poor blood circulation are at higher risk of developing dry gangrene.
The surgeon diagnosed the patient with dry gangrene after noticing the dry, blackened appearance of the foot.
