governing body

noun
decentralized/algorithmic governance system

(in computing and digital platforms) a decentralized or algorithmic system—often implemented with smart contracts, token-weighted voting, or protocol rules—that has the authority to make and enforce decisions for a platform or protocol.

Examples

governing body for a platform
In the blockchain project, the governing body is defined by smart contracts and token-holder voting rules.
Rather than a central committee, the platform's governing body consists of automated protocols and stakeholder votes.
Developers proposed changing the protocol, but the governing body required a two-thirds token consensus first.
