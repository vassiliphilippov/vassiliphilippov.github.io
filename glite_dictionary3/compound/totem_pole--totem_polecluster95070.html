totem pole

noun
carved wooden post with totemic figures
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊ.təm poʊl / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təm pəʊl / (BrE)
A tall, carved and painted wooden post featuring totemic figures, traditionally created by Indigenous peoples of the northwest Pacific coast as an emblem or record of ancestry.

Examples

totem pole with carved animals
Each figure on the totem pole represents a different ancestor or legend.
The museum displays a beautiful totem pole carved by local Indigenous artists.
Visitors gathered around the massive totem pole to admire its intricate designs.
