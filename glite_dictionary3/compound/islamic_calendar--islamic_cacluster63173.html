Islamic calendar

noun
the Muslim lunar calendar beginning from 622 AD
Pronunciation: / ɪˈslɑːmɪk ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈslæmɪk ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the lunar calendar used by Muslims, starting from 622 AD (the year of the Hijra), used to determine the dates of Islamic holidays and rituals

Examples

Islamic calendar for Ramadan dates
Muslims use the Islamic calendar to determine the dates of Ramadan and other religious observances.
The Islamic calendar is based on lunar cycles instead of the solar year.
Unlike the Gregorian calendar, the Islamic calendar completes a cycle relative to the solar year every 32 years.
