arrest warrant

noun
official order to detain a suspect
Pronunciation: / əˈrɛst ˌwɔɹənt / (AmE), / əˈrɛst ˌwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official legal document authorizing law enforcement officials to detain a person suspected of a crime and bring them before a court

Examples

issue an arrest warrant
The judge signed the arrest warrant late last night to ensure immediate action.
The police obtained an arrest warrant for the suspect after gathering sufficient evidence.
Without an arrest warrant, officers cannot legally detain the individual.
