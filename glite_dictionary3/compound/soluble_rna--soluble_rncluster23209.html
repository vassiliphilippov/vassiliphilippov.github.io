soluble RNA

noun
type of RNA involved in protein synthesis
Pronunciation: / ˈsɒl.jə.bəl ˌɑːr.enˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.jʊ.bəl ˌɑːr.enˈeɪ / (BrE)
transfer RNA; a type of RNA molecule in cells that attaches specific amino acids to a growing protein chain during synthesis, according to genetic directions coded in messenger RNA.
