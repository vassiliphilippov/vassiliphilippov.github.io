petty juror

noun
member of a trial jury
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛti ˈdʒɝɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛti ˈdʒʊə.rə / (BrE)
A member of a petit jury; one of the ordinary jurors summoned to try a case in a court of law.

Examples

petty juror in court
Each petty juror listened carefully to the evidence throughout the trial.
The court called twelve petty jurors to decide on the defendant’s innocence or guilt.
The duty of a petty juror is to impartially consider the facts of the case.
