loco disease

noun
livestock disease from locoweed poisoning
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkoʊ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəʊ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A disease of livestock caused by eating locoweed, marked by weakness, lack of coordination, trembling, and partial paralysis.

Examples

loco disease in cattle
Animals suffering from loco disease may display erratic behavior and have difficulty standing.
Ranchers worry about loco disease during the spring, when locoweed grows fastest in the pastures.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cows with loco disease after several showed signs of trembling and weakness.
