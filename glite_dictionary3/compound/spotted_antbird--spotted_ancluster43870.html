spotted antbird

noun
a small Central and South American antbird
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑt̬ɪd ˈæntˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˈæntˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small antbird species (Hylophylax naevioides) native to Central America and northwestern South America, noted for its spotted plumage and association with army ants

Examples

the spotted antbird in rainforest
Birdwatchers travel to Costa Rica hoping to catch a glimpse of the elusive spotted antbird.
Researchers tracked the movements of a male spotted antbird in Panama's rainforest.
The spotted antbird is often seen flitting through the understory near swarms of army ants.
