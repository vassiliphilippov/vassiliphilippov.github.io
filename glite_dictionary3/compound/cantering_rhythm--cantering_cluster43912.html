cantering rhythm

noun
galloping musical or poetic rhythm
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.tə.rɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tər.ɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
A hoofbeat-like, galloping rhythmic pattern used in music, dance, or poetry, producing a steady three-beat or gallop-like pulse.

cantering rhythm in music
The band's drummer created a cantering rhythm that pushed the song forward with relentless energy.

In the poem, the repeated syllables formed a cantering rhythm that suggested long horse strides.
