bell book

noun
a ship's engine order logbook
Pronunciation: / bɛl bʊk / (AmE), / bɛl bʊk / (BrE)
a logbook used on a ship to record all orders related to the operation of the main engines

Examples

bell book entry record
Every shift, a new entry was made in the bell book by the engine officer.
Regulations require the bell book to be up-to-date and available for inspection at all times.
The chief engineer entered the speed change command into the bell book.
