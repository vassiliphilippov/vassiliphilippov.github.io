whole milk

noun
milk with all its natural fat
Pronunciation: / hoʊl mɪlk / (AmE), / həʊl mɪlk / (BrE)
Milk from which no fat or other constituents have been removed; typically contains about 3.25% milk fat.

Examples

glass of whole milk
Many people prefer whole milk because of its rich taste and higher fat content.
She used whole milk instead of cream in the recipe for a lighter texture.
The health guidelines suggest that children under two should drink whole milk rather than skim milk.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low-fat milk: milk with some fat removed, less than whole milk
  • antonym – skim milk: milk with most of the fat removed
