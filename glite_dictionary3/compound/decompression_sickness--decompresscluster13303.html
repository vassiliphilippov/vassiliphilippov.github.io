decompression sickness

noun
painful condition from rapid pressure decrease
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛʃən ˌsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛʃən ˌsɪknəs / (BrE)
A condition caused by a rapid decrease in atmospheric pressure, resulting in pain and other symptoms due to the formation of gas bubbles in the body.

Examples

decompression sickness after diving
After the submarine accident, three crew members showed signs of decompression sickness.
Divers who ascend too quickly are at risk of decompression sickness.
Special chambers are used to treat patients suffering from decompression sickness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – caisson disease: another term for decompression sickness, especially in construction
  • synonym – the bends: informal term for decompression sickness
