disjointed conversation

noun
conversation lacking coherence or continuity
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd ˌkɑːnvərˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd ˌkɒnvəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a conversation that lacks coherence or logical continuity, often jumping between unrelated topics

Examples

a disjointed conversation jumps topics
After the interruption, the group struggled to return from their disjointed conversation to the original topic.
The meeting turned into a disjointed conversation as everyone spoke over each other about unrelated topics.
Their late-night phone call was more of a disjointed conversation than a productive discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym – coherent conversation: a conversation that flows logically and smoothly
