heart valve

noun
valve controlling blood flow in the heart
Pronunciation: / hɑrt vælv / (AmE), / hɑːt vælv / (BrE)
a structure in the heart that allows blood to flow in one direction and prevents it from flowing backward

Examples

damaged heart valve
An artificial heart valve is sometimes used for patients with congenital defects.
Diseases affecting the heart valve can lead to serious health problems if untreated.
The surgeon carefully replaced the damaged heart valve during the procedure.
