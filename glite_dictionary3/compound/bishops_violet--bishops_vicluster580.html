bishop's violet

noun
moderate reddish-purple color
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪʃəps ˌvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps ˌvaɪələt / (BrE)
A moderate reddish-purple color that is bluer, stronger, and slightly lighter than heliotrope, and bluer and duller than eupatorium purple.

Examples

bishop's violet dress
She chose a dress in bishop's violet for the evening gala, which made her stand out beautifully.
The artist mixed several shades to achieve the perfect bishop's violet for the portrait background.
The flowers in the garden ranged in color from pale pink to a deep bishop's violet.
