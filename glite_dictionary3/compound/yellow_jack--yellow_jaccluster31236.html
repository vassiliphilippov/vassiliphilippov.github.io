yellow jack

noun
yellow fever; mosquito-borne viral disease
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˌdʒæk / (BrE)
another name for yellow fever, a serious infectious disease caused by a flavivirus transmitted by mosquitoes, leading to fever, jaundice, and hemorrhage

Examples

catch yellow jack disease
In the 19th century, outbreaks of yellow jack caused widespread fear and quarantines in southern cities.
Some ports flew the yellow jack to signal an outbreak of disease on board.
The doctor suspected the patient had yellow jack after observing symptoms of jaundice and fever.
