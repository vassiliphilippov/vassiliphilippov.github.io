substantia alba

noun
white nervous tissue in the CNS
Pronunciation: / səbˈstænʃiə ˈæl.bə / (AmE), / sʌbˈstan.tɪ.ə ˈæl.bə / (BrE)
The white nervous tissue of the central nervous system, composed mainly of nerve fibers with their myelin sheaths; also known as white matter.

Examples

substantia alba in the brain
Damage to the substantia alba can affect the transmission of nerve signals in the spinal cord.
Researchers are studying diseases that primarily impact the substantia alba of the brain.
The substantia alba appears lighter in color compared to surrounding gray matter under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym – substantia grisea: gray nervous tissue of the central nervous system; gray matter
  • synonym – white matter: white tissue in the brain and spinal cord consisting mostly of myelinated nerve fibers
