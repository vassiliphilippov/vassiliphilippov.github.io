soil bank

noun
land retired from farming with government subsidy
Pronunciation: / sɔɪl bæŋk / (AmE), / sɔɪl bæŋk / (BrE)
a government program in which farmland is retired from crop production and planted with soil-building crops, with farmers receiving subsidies for the retired land

Examples

enroll in the soil bank
By enrolling in the soil bank, he helped improve the land’s fertility over time.
Many farmers placed their marginal fields into the soil bank to take advantage of the subsidies.
The soil bank program aimed to reduce surpluses while preventing soil erosion.
