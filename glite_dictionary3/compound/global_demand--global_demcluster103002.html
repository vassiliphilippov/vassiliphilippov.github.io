global demand

noun
total demand in an economy
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbəl dɪˈmænd / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəl dɪˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
The total demand for final goods and services within an entire economy at a given time and price level.

Examples

rising global demand
A slowdown in global demand has affected many export-driven economies around the world.
Analysts predict that global demand for consumer electronics will rebound next year.
The rise in oil prices can be attributed to an increase in global demand for energy resources.

  • synonym – aggregate demand: the total demand for goods and services in an economy
