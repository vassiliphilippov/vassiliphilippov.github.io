spring chicken

noun
young person (often used negatively about age)
Pronunciation: / sprɪŋ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
a young person, especially one who is unusually youthful or inexperienced; often used in the phrase 'no spring chicken' to indicate someone is not young anymore.

Examples

no spring chicken anymore
Although he still plays soccer every weekend, he's no spring chicken anymore.
Don't expect a spring chicken to have the same experience as a seasoned expert.
She may be a spring chicken compared to her colleagues, but she's very talented.

Related Terms

  • antonym – elder: an older person, especially one with greater age or experience
  • synonym – youth: a young person, especially a young man
