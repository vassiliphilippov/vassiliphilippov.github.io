hemal arch

noun
vertebral structure enclosing tail blood vessels
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːməl ɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhiːməl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an anatomical structure arising from the underside of a vertebral centrum and enclosing the blood vessels in the tail region

Examples

hemal arch of a vertebra
Damage to the hemal arch can result in impaired blood flow in the caudal region.
Researchers examined the shape of each hemal arch to study its evolutionary significance.
The hemal arch protects the major blood vessels running beneath the tail vertebrae of many fish and reptiles.
