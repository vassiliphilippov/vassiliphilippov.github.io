accommodation reflex

noun
eye's automatic focusing response
Pronunciation: / əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃən ˈriːflɛks / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃən ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
the automatic adjustment of the eye that changes the shape of the lens to focus on near or distant objects

Examples

accommodation reflex focuses eyes
A defect in the accommodation reflex can lead to difficulty seeing nearby objects clearly.
During the exam, the doctor checked the patient's accommodation reflex by having her focus on a moving finger.
The accommodation reflex allows us to quickly refocus our eyes on objects at different distances.
