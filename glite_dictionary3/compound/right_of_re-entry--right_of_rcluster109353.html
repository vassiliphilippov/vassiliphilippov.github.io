right of re-entry

noun
legal right to retake possession of property
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪt əv ˌriːˈɛntri / (AmE), / ˌraɪt əv ˌriːˈɛntri / (BrE)
(law) The legal right, reserved upon transferring property or possession, to retake or resume possession at a future time under certain conditions.

Examples

right of re-entry after breach
If the building is not used as agreed, the owner retains a right of re-entry.
The landlord exercised his right of re-entry when the tenant failed to pay rent.
The lease contract clearly states the conditions under which the right of re-entry may be invoked.
