scale of measurement

noun
system for assigning or ranking values
Pronunciation: / skeɪl əv ˌmɛʒərmənt / (AmE), / skeɪl əv ˈmɛʒəmənt / (BrE)
a standard system for assigning values to represent the properties or characteristics of something, often in an ordered manner

Examples

scale of measurement from 1 to 5
On a scale of measurement from 1 to 5, the product received the highest rating.
Psychologists often use a scale of measurement to quantify responses in surveys.
Their progress was assessed using a standardized scale of measurement developed for the study.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rating scale: a set range of numbers or points for expressing judgments or measurements
