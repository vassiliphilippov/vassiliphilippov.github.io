foster brother

noun
boy/man raised by same parents, not biological brother
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑstər ˈbrʌðər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
A boy or man who is not your biological or adopted brother, but who is raised as a member of your family by your parents.

Examples

foster brother joins family
After his parents welcomed Daniel into their home, he became Michael's foster brother.
Even though Sam is my foster brother, we are as close as real siblings.
When my mother became a foster parent, I gained a foster brother named Alex.

Related Terms

  • antonym – biological brother: a brother related by birth
  • synonym – foster sister: a girl or woman raised by your parents but not biologically related
  • antonym – stepbrother: a male child of your stepparent
