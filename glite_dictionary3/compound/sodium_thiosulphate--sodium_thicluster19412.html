sodium thiosulphate

noun
compound used as photographic fixer
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm θaɪˈɒsəlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm θaɪˈɒsɒlˌfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, water-soluble compound (Na2S2O3), used chiefly as a photographic fixer and in chemical analysis

Examples

sodium thiosulphate solution
Sodium thiosulphate is commonly used in photography to fix developed film and make the image permanent.
In chemistry labs, sodium thiosulphate can neutralize chlorine solutions during water treatment experiments.
The stain on the fabric was removed after soaking it in a sodium thiosulphate solution.
