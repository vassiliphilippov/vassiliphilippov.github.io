resonating chamber

noun
hollow space for resonance of waves
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛzəˌneɪtɪŋ ˈtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪtɪŋ ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a hollow space whose size and shape allow it to amplify or sustain sound or electromagnetic waves by resonance

Examples

resonating chamber in a guitar
A microwave oven contains a resonating chamber to direct electromagnetic waves.
The guitar's sound depends greatly on the design of its resonating chamber.
The shape of the vocal tract acts as a resonating chamber to modify the sound of your voice.

  • synonym – resonator: an object or chamber that naturally vibrates at certain frequencies
