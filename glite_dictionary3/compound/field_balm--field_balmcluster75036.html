field balm

noun
aromatic European plant, mint family
Pronunciation: / ˈfild bælm / (AmE), / ˈfiːld bɑːm / (BrE)
a trailing European aromatic plant of the mint family, with rounded leaves and small purplish flowers, often grown in hanging baskets; sometimes known as ground ivy and naturalized in North America

Examples

field balm hanging baskets
In some herbal remedies, field balm is used for its mild medicinal properties.
The gardener recommended planting field balm near the patio to attract bees and provide a pleasant scent.
The hanging basket was overflowing with lush green leaves and delicate purple flowers of field balm.
