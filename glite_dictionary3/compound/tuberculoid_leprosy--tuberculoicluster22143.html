tuberculoid leprosy

noun
form of leprosy with skin and nerve tumors
Pronunciation: / təˌbɜrkjəˌlɔɪd ˈlɛprəsi / (AmE), / tjuːˌbɜːkjʊlɔɪd ˈlɛprəsi / (BrE)
a form of leprosy characterized by nodular lesions on the skin and involvement of cutaneous nerves, usually showing a strong immune response to the bacterium

Examples

tuberculoid leprosy skin lesions
Early treatment improved nerve function in cases of tuberculoid leprosy.
The patient was diagnosed with tuberculoid leprosy after developing several numb, nodular lesions.
Unlike lepromatous forms, tuberculoid leprosy often has few skin patches and a strong immune response.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lepromatous leprosy: a more severe form of leprosy with widespread skin bumps and nerve damage
