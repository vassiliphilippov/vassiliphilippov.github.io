angiotensin II inhibitor

noun
drug blocking angiotensin II
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒioʊtɛnˌsɪn tu ɪnˈhɪbɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˌandʒiəʊˌtɛnsɪn tuː ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a drug that reduces or blocks the effects of angiotensin II, used especially to treat high blood pressure and heart failure

Examples

angiotensin II inhibitor treats hypertension
Long-term use of an angiotensin II inhibitor may improve heart function in patients with heart failure.
Many patients tolerate an angiotensin II inhibitor better than other blood pressure medications.
The doctor prescribed an angiotensin II inhibitor to help control her hypertension.
