matter of fact

noun
legal: factual issue for jury to decide
Pronunciation: / ˈmæt̬ɚ əv ˈfækt /; / ˈmædər əv ˈfækt / (AmE), / ˈmætə əv ˈfækt /; / ˈmætə əv ˈfakt / (BrE)
a factual issue or statement that can be proven true, not an opinion or a matter of law (especially in legal contexts, a disputed fact decided by a jury)

Examples

a matter of fact in court
In civil trials, the jury serves as the trier of matter of fact.
The question of whether the defendant was present is a matter of fact for the jury to determine.
Whether the contract was actually signed is a matter of fact, not a matter of law.
In a trial, the jury determines the matter of fact, while the judge rules on legal issues.
The contract dispute hinged on a matter of fact about delivery dates.
Whether he was present at the scene is a matter of fact to be decided by the evidence.
