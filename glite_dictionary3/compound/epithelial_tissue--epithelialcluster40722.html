epithelial tissue

noun
tissue covering surfaces or lining organs
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpəˈθiːli.əl ˈtɪʃuː / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈθiːlɪəl ˈtɪʃuː / (BrE)
Membranous tissue that forms the outer layer of the body's surfaces and lines internal organs, cavities, and other internal structures.

Examples

epithelial tissue covers organs
Damage to the epithelial tissue can lead to a variety of health problems.
Scientists studied the regeneration of epithelial tissue after injury.
The lungs are lined with epithelial tissue that helps protect against pathogens.
