Berlin airlift

noun
1948–49 operation supplying West Berlin by air
Pronunciation: / bərˈlɪn ˈɛrˌlɪft / (AmE), / bəˈlɪn ˈeəˌlɪft / (BrE)
An operation in 1948–49 in which Western Allies supplied food and fuel to West Berlin by air after Soviet forces blocked land routes into the city.

Examples

the Berlin airlift supplies food
Historians regard the Berlin airlift as a major humanitarian effort in postwar Europe.
My grandfather told me stories about witnessing the planes overhead during the Berlin airlift.
The Berlin airlift demonstrated the resolve of Western allies during the early years of the Cold War.
