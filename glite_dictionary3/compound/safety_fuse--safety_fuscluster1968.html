safety fuse

noun
slow-burning cord for igniting explosives
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti fjuːz / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti fjuːz / (BrE)
a slow-burning cord or tube filled with combustible material, used for safely igniting detonators from a distance

Examples

safety fuse for explosives
Before using explosives, always check that the safety fuse is properly installed.
Miners rely on the safety fuse to ensure detonations occur at a safe distance.
The safety fuse was lit, giving the workers time to retreat before the blast.
