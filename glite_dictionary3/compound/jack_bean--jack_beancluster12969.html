jack bean

noun
tropical forage plant with white seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæk ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈdʒak ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an annual bushy plant of tropical South America with long pods containing white seeds, grown especially as forage or green manure

Examples

jack bean plant
Cattle were fed jack bean during the dry season when other forage was scarce.
Farmers planted jack bean as a cover crop to improve the soil's fertility.
The jack bean thrives in warm climates and is valued for its robustness.
