leaded bronze

noun
bronze alloy containing lead
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdɪd ˈbrɑnz / (AmE), / ˈlɛdɪd ˈbrɒnz / (BrE)
a type of bronze alloy in which 1-4% lead is added to improve its machinability and other properties

Examples

leaded bronze alloy
Archaeologists discovered ancient tools composed of leaded bronze at the site.
Engine bearings are often made from leaded bronze because of its lubricating properties.
The addition of lead to leaded bronze makes it easier to machine than pure bronze.
