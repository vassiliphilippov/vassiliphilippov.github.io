intake valve

noun
valve controlling entry of fluid or gas
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌteɪk vælv / (AmE), / ˈɪnteɪk vælv / (BrE)
A valve that regulates the entry of fluid or gas into a chamber, such as an engine cylinder.

Examples

intake valve controls air
A faulty intake valve can cause poor engine performance and reduced fuel efficiency.
Mechanics inspected the intake valve for signs of wear and deposits.
The intake valve opens to allow the air-fuel mixture to enter the engine cylinder.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exhaust valve: a valve that controls the release of gases from an engine cylinder
