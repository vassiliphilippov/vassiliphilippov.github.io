dead centre

noun
crank position in line with connecting rod
Pronunciation: / dɛd ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / dɛd ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
The position of a crank when it is exactly in line with the connecting rod and does not produce torque.

Examples

dead centre of a crank
A crank stalled at dead centre makes it hard to operate the mechanism.
Engineers must avoid stopping machines at dead centre to prevent starting difficulties.
When the piston reaches dead centre, the engine cannot generate any turning force.
