Hinayana Buddhism

noun
offensive term for early conservative schools of Buddhism
Pronunciation: / ˌhiː.nəˈjɑː.nə ˈbʊd.ɪz.m̩ / (AmE), / ˌhiː.nəˈjɑː.nə ˈbʊd.ɪz.m̩ / (BrE)
A dated and offensive term used to refer to the early, conservative schools of Buddhism, especially Theravada, considered pejorative by many Buddhists.

Examples

Hinayana Buddhism label controversy
Scholars discourage the use of Hinayana Buddhism in academic discourse due to its pejorative origin.
Some older Western texts refer to Theravada Buddhism as Hinayana Buddhism, though this is now considered disrespectful.
The term Hinayana Buddhism is rarely used today because of its derogatory connotations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Theravada: the main surviving school related to what's called Hinayana; early conservative Buddhism
