brachial plexus

noun
network of nerves supplying the arm
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkiəl ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / ˈbrækiəl ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of nerves originating from the lower cervical and upper thoracic spinal nerves, supplying the arm and parts of the shoulder

Examples

brachial plexus injury
A severe fall caused stretching of his brachial plexus, resulting in nerve pain.
Injury to the brachial plexus can lead to weakness or loss of movement in the arm.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage the brachial plexus during shoulder operations.
