fish bowl

noun
situation with no privacy
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃ boʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃ bəʊl / (BrE)
A situation in which a person or group has very little or no privacy, being observed by many people.

Examples

live in a fish bowl
After becoming the principal, she found it hard to adjust to life in a fish bowl.
Being a celebrity means living in a fish bowl where everyone knows your business.
Reporters made the politician feel like he was trapped in a fish bowl.
