ivory palm

noun
South American palm tree producing ivory nuts
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪvəri pɑm / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri pɑːm / (BrE)
a stemless palm tree native to Brazil and Peru that produces ivory nuts, used as a source of vegetable ivory

Examples

tall ivory palm
Artisans use the nuts from the ivory palm to make buttons and jewelry.
Conservationists promote the cultivation of the ivory palm as an alternative to elephant ivory.
The ivory palm is best known for its hard seeds, often carved to imitate animal ivory.
