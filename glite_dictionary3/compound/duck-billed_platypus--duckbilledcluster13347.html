duck-billed platypus

noun
Australian aquatic mammal with a bill and webbed feet
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌk bɪld ˈplætɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌk bɪld ˈplætɪpəs / (BrE)
A small aquatic mammal of Australia and Tasmania with dense fur, a broad bill, webbed feet, and a flat tail; the only species in the family Ornithorhynchidae.

Examples

a duck-billed platypus swimming
Children were fascinated by the unusual appearance of the duck-billed platypus at the zoo.
Scientists studied the habitat of the duck-billed platypus in the rivers of Tasmania.
The duck-billed platypus is one of the few mammals that lay eggs.
