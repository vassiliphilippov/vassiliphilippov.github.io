streamer fly

noun
artificial fishing fly with long wings
Pronunciation: / ˈstriːmər flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmə flaɪ / (BrE)
an artificial fishing fly with wings that extend back beyond the crook of the hook, used to imitate small fish or other aquatic creatures

Examples

streamer fly pattern
He learned how to tie his own streamer flies for the upcoming fishing season.
Many fishermen prefer using a streamer fly in fast-moving waters to mimic small baitfish.
The angler tied a bright streamer fly to his line, hoping to attract a large trout.
