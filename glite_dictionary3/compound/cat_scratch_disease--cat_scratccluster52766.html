cat scratch disease

noun
bacterial infection from a cat scratch
Pronunciation: / kæt skrætʃ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / kæt skrætʃ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a bacterial disease in humans, usually caused by a scratch or bite from a cat, resulting in swollen lymph nodes and mild infection

Examples

cat scratch disease after kitten bite
Children are most commonly affected by cat scratch disease when playing with young kittens.
Doctors diagnosed her with cat scratch disease after she developed swollen lymph nodes.
If you develop a fever after being scratched by a cat, you should ask your doctor about cat scratch disease.
