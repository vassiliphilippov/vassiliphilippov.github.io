sour mash whiskey

noun
whiskey made with sour mash process
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊɚ mæʃ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə mæʃ ˈwɪski / (BrE)
whiskey made using a fermentation process in which some of the mash from a previous batch is included to ensure consistency and control acidity.

Examples

Kentucky sour mash whiskey
Distilleries often promote their sour mash whiskey as a mark of quality and tradition.
He prefers sour mash whiskey over other types for its smoothness and hint of sweetness.
Many American bourbons are produced as sour mash whiskey to retain a consistent flavor.
