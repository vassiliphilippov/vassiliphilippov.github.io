box seat

noun
special seat in theater or stadium
Pronunciation: / bɑks sit / (AmE), / bɒks siːt / (BrE)
a premium or private seat in a separate, often elevated, area of a theater, stadium, or venue, usually offering a good view and extra comfort or privacy

Examples

box seat at the theater
From his box seat, he had an excellent view of the entire stage.
The royal family watched the game from a private box seat above the field.
We were lucky to get a box seat for the opening night performance.
From our box seat, we had a perfect view of the stage and the orchestra.
He saved up all year to buy box seat tickets for the championship game.
She felt special sitting in the box seat during the opera performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – balcony: a platform projecting from the wall of a theater, with seats above the main floor
  • synonym – stall: the main floor seating area in a theater
  • synonym – stalls: main floor seats in a theater, especially in British English
