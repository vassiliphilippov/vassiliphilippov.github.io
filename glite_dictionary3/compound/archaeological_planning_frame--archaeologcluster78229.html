archaeological planning frame

noun
square frame used in archaeological mapping
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrkiəˌlɑːdʒɪkəl ˈplænɪŋ freɪm / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒɪkəl ˈplænɪŋ freɪm / (BrE)
A square wooden frame used by archaeologists to help map and record the precise location of objects or features at a site.

Examples

use an archaeological planning frame at a site
Students learned how to use an archaeological planning frame to record the site’s stratigraphy accurately.
The excavator carefully placed the archaeological planning frame over the trench to map the artifacts.
Without an archaeological planning frame, it would have been much harder to draw precise field sketches.
