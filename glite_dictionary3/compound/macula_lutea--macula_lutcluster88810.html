macula lutea

noun
yellowish retinal region for sharp vision
Pronunciation: / ˈmækjələ ˈluːtiə / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə ˈluːtɪə / (BrE)
a small yellowish area near the center of the retina, rich in cones, responsible for sharp, detailed central vision

Examples

macula lutea damage
Damage to the macula lutea can result in loss of central vision.
Doctors examined the patient's macula lutea for signs of degeneration.
The macula lutea contains a high concentration of cone cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fovea: the central pit of the macula lutea responsible for the sharpest vision
