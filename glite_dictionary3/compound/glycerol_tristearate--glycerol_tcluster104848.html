glycerol tristearate

noun
triglyceride of stearic acid
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl traɪˈstɪəreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɒl traɪˈstɪəreɪt / (BrE)
A chemical compound formed by the esterification of glycerol with three molecules of stearic acid; used as an emulsifier and stabilizer in foods and cosmetics.

Examples

glycerol tristearate in food products
Glycerol tristearate is commonly used as an emulsifier in processed foods to improve texture.
Many skin creams contain glycerol tristearate as a stabilizer to maintain consistency.
The laboratory synthesized pure glycerol tristearate for use in cosmetic formulations.
