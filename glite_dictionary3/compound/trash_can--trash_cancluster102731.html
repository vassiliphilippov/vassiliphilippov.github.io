trash can

noun
virtual folder for deleted files
Pronunciation: / træʃ ˈkæn / (AmE), / træʃ ˈkan / (BrE)
(in computing) a virtual folder or location in an operating system where deleted files or items are stored temporarily until they are permanently removed or restored.

Examples

delete files to trash can
After realizing her mistake, she opened the trash can and restored the file to Documents.
Emptying the trash can on my laptop permanently removed dozens of old photos and downloads.
The instructor told us to check the trash can before submitting the final project to avoid losing attachments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – recycle bin: the Windows name for a virtual folder storing deleted files before permanent deletion; similar functionally to a trash can.
  • synonym – trash: a general term for deleted files or the macOS folder named 'Trash' where deleted items are kept temporarily.
