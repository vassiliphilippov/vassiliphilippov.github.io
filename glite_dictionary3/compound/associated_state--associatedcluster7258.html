associated state

noun
territory partly controlled but internally autonomous
Pronunciation: / əˈsoʊsiˌeɪtɪd steɪt / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsiˌeɪtɪd steɪt / (BrE)
A territory or state that is partly controlled by a stronger state but is autonomous in its internal affairs, usually established by treaty; often referred to as a protectorate.

Examples

an associated state treaty
Several countries have entered into associated state relationships to retain internal autonomy while receiving foreign protection.
The Pacific island became an associated state of the larger country in 1965, maintaining its own government.
The constitution of the associated state guarantees its right to local self-government.

  • synonym – protectorate: a state controlled and protected by another
