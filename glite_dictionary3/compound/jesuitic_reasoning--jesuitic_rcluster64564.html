Jesuitic reasoning

noun
overly complex or convoluted reasoning
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛzjuˈɪtɪk ˈriːzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛzjuːˈɪtɪk ˈriːzənɪŋ / (BrE)
reasoning that is excessively intricate, subtle, or convoluted, often intended to evade or obscure the truth

Examples

complex Jesuitic reasoning
Her Jesuitic reasoning left everyone bewildered and unable to follow her argument.
The judge criticized the lawyer's Jesuitic reasoning as an attempt to confuse the jury.
The philosopher employed Jesuitic reasoning to justify his controversial conclusions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sophistry: use of clever but false arguments
  • antonym – straightforward reasoning: simple, direct reasoning, not convoluted
