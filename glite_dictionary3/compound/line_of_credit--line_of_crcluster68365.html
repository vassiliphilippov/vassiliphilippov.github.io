line of credit

noun
maximum credit allowed by a bank
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / laɪn əv ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
an arrangement with a bank or other financial institution that allows a customer to borrow up to a specified maximum amount

Examples

open a line of credit
A line of credit can be a flexible way to access funds when needed.
She used her line of credit to pay for emergency home repairs.
The bank approved a line of credit to help the company manage its cash flow.
