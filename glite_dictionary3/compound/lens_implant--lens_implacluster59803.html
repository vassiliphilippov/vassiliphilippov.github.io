lens implant

noun
artificial lens implanted in the eye
Pronunciation: / lɛnz ˈɪmplænt / (AmE), / lɛnz ˈɪmplɑːnt / (BrE)
a clear artificial lens surgically implanted in the eye, usually to replace a natural lens removed during a cataract operation

Examples

lens implant after cataract
After her cataract surgery, she received a lens implant in her left eye.
Modern lens implants can often correct vision problems such as nearsightedness or farsightedness.
The surgeon explained the different types of lens implants available before the procedure.

  • synonym – intraocular lens: an artificial lens implanted to replace the eye's natural lens
