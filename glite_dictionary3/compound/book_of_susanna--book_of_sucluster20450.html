Book of Susanna

proper noun
biblical apocryphal story of Susanna
Pronunciation: / bʊk əv suˈzænə / (AmE), / bʊk əv suˈzænə / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book consisting of text added to the Book of Daniel, recounting the story of Susanna, a Hebrew woman falsely accused of promiscuity by two elders

In the Book of Susanna, two elders falsely accuse Susanna after she rejects their advances.
