penetrating trauma

noun
injury from object entering the body
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnəˈtreɪtɪŋ ˈtrɔmə / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪtɪŋ ˈtrɔːmə / (BrE)
a bodily injury caused by an object entering the body, such as a knife, bullet, or shrapnel

Examples

penetrating trauma from a knife
Emergency surgeons are trained to assess and manage cases of penetrating trauma quickly.
Gunshot wounds are a common cause of penetrating trauma in urban hospitals.
The patient suffered penetrating trauma after being stabbed in the abdomen.
