rampant arch

noun
arch with supports at different heights
Pronunciation: / ˈræmpənt ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈræmpənt ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch whose support is higher on one side than the other, creating an inclined span.

Examples

rampant arch in a bridge
A rampant arch is often used where one side of a structure must be higher than the other.
The cathedral's rampant arch elegantly bridged the uneven ground between the two towers.
The engineer explained how a rampant arch distributes load differently from a regular arch.
