dwarf pocket rat

noun
a small rodent, smaller than a kangaroo rat
Pronunciation: / dwɔrf ˈpɑkɪt ræt / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈpɒkɪt ræt / (BrE)
A small, silky-haired rodent with external cheek pouches, similar to but smaller than kangaroo rats.

Examples

a dwarf pocket rat burrow
Researchers observed the dwarf pocket rat feeding at night to avoid predators.
The dwarf pocket rat is native to arid regions and avoids the heat of the day.
Unlike its larger relatives, the dwarf pocket rat often buries its seeds close to its burrow.
