trial balloon

noun
balloon released to test air currents
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (BrE)
a balloon released to test air currents, often used to gather information before making a decision

Examples

release a trial balloon
Before launching the new aircraft, engineers released a trial balloon to study the wind direction.
The weather team sent up a trial balloon early in the morning to gather data on the upper atmosphere.
Years ago, explorers often used a trial balloon before attempting a journey by air.
