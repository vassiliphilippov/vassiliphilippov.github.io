single-entry bookkeeping

noun
simple bookkeeping system with one entry per transaction
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈɛntri ˈbʊkˌkiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈɛntri ˈbʊkˌkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
a bookkeeping system in which each transaction is recorded in only one account, typically used for small or simple businesses

Examples

single-entry bookkeeping for small business
Many small businesses still use single-entry bookkeeping for its simplicity.
She learned single-entry bookkeeping before managing the shop's finances.
Unlike double-entry methods, single-entry bookkeeping is easier but less accurate.

Related Terms

  • antonym – double-entry bookkeeping: an accounting system where every transaction is recorded in at least two accounts
