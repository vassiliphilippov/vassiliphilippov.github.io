rose beetle

noun
European beetle, pest of roses
Pronunciation: / roʊz ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / rəʊz ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a common European beetle with a metallic green appearance, whose larvae feed on plant roots and adults feed on the leaves and flowers of plants such as roses

Examples

rose beetle damage roses
Both larvae and adults of the rose beetle can cause significant harm to ornamental plants.
Gardeners often struggle with rose beetle infestations that damage their prized rose bushes.
The metallic green color of the rose beetle makes it easy to spot among the leaves.
