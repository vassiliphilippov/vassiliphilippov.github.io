revolving door syndrome

noun
frequent staff turnover in an organization
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɑlvɪŋ dɔr ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlvɪŋ dɔː ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a situation in which employees or staff frequently leave and are replaced, resulting in continual turnover within an organization

Examples

revolving door syndrome in companies
Several nonprofit organizations struggle with revolving door syndrome, making it difficult to maintain experienced teams.
The company's revolving door syndrome was blamed on poor leadership and lack of job satisfaction.
To prevent revolving door syndrome, the new manager implemented better training and staff engagement policies.
