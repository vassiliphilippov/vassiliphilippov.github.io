Low Latin

noun
Medieval or non-classical Latin
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈlætɪn / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈlatɪn / (BrE)
any form of Latin used after the classical period, especially in the Middle Ages; non-classical Latin.

Examples

Low Latin texts
Medieval scholars composed their treatises in Low Latin, which often varied from ancient forms.
The influence of local languages contributed to the development of Low Latin.
Understanding documents written in Low Latin requires familiarity with its unique vocabulary and grammar.
