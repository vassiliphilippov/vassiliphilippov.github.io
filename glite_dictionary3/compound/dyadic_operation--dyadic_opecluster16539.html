dyadic operation

noun
operation on two operands
Pronunciation: / daɪˈædɪk ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈadɪk ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an operation that takes exactly two operands or arguments, commonly used in mathematics and logic

Examples

dyadic operation on numbers
Addition is a common dyadic operation because it combines two numbers to produce a sum.
In group theory, the group operation is a type of dyadic operation defined on the set.
Multiplication of two matrices is another example of a dyadic operation in mathematics.
