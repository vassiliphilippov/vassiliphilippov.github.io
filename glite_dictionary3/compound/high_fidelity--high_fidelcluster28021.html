high fidelity

noun
accurate representation (models, simulations, prototypes)
Pronunciation: / haɪ fəˈdɛləti / (AmE), / haɪ fɪˈdɛləti / (BrE)
a very accurate reproduction or representation of something (for example a model, prototype, simulation, or digital media) that preserves the original's important details.

Examples

high fidelity simulation model
The simulation was designed for high fidelity to real-world behavior.
We created a high fidelity prototype to test the user interface before development.
Researchers used a high fidelity model to predict how the system would respond under stress.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fidelity: faithfulness to or accuracy in representing an original
  • synonym – hi-fi: short for high fidelity; often used for accurate reproduction or equipment, sometimes applied to prototypes informally
