whole-word method

noun
reading instruction method using whole words
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊl wɝd ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl wɜːd ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of teaching reading by training learners to recognize and associate entire printed words with their spoken forms, rather than decoding them letter by letter

Examples

the whole-word method of reading
Many educators debate the effectiveness of the whole-word method in early literacy programs.
Some schools combine phonics and the whole-word method to teach children how to read.
The whole-word method emphasizes memorizing entire words rather than sounding them out.
