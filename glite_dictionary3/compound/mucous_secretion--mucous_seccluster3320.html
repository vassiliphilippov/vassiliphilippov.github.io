mucous secretion

noun
protective fluid from mucous membranes
Pronunciation: / ˈmjukəs sɪˈkriʃən / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkəs sɪˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
A protective fluid produced by mucous membranes, serving to lubricate, moisten, and protect tissues in the body.

Examples

excess mucous secretion
Excessive mucous secretion can lead to nasal congestion during allergies.
Healthy mucous secretion in the gut aids in protecting the lining of the digestive system.
The mucous secretion in the respiratory tract helps trap dust and microbes.
