public school

noun
British private independent secondary school
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk skuːl / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk skuːl / (BrE)
In Britain, a private independent secondary school, often supported by endowment and tuition, typically attended by pupils from wealthy or influential families.

Examples

elite public school
He attended a renowned public school before enrolling at university.
Many British politicians are former public school students.
The traditions of the public school have influenced much of the country's elite.

  • synonym – private school: A school supported by private funds rather than by the government
