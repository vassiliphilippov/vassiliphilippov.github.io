debris field

noun
area with scattered wreckage or debris
Pronunciation: / dəˈbri fild / (AmE), / dəˈbriː fiːld / (BrE)
An area in which scattered fragments or wreckage from a destructive event, such as an explosion, crash, or storm, are spread over the ground.

Examples

wide debris field
Hurricane winds left a vast debris field along the coast.
Satellite images showed the debris field stretching for several kilometers.
The investigators combed the debris field for clues about the cause of the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wreckage: remains or fragments of something that has been destroyed
