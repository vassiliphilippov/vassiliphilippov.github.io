underwater archeology

noun
archaeology of underwater sites
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈwɔtər ˌɑːrkiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈwɔːtə ˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The study of human activity through the recovery and analysis of material remains from underwater sites such as shipwrecks, submerged settlements, and sunken structures.

Examples

underwater archeology of shipwrecks
Advances in technology have greatly expanded the possibilities of underwater archeology in recent decades.
Many fascinating discoveries, such as ancient shipwrecks, have been made through underwater archeology.
She is pursuing a degree in underwater archeology to study submerged ruins and lost cities.
