driven well

noun
well formed by driving pipe into ground for water
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪvən wɛl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪv.ən wɛl / (BrE)
A well made by driving a pipe or tube into the ground to reach water-bearing strata.

Examples

driven well water supply
The farm relied on a driven well for its water supply during the dry seasons.
To access groundwater, they installed a new driven well in their backyard.
Unlike a traditional dug well, a driven well can be completed with less excavation.
