heat unit

noun
unit for measuring heat energy
Pronunciation: / hit ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / hiːt ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for heat energy, such as the calorie or the British thermal unit (BTU)

Examples

heat unit in calories
One heat unit can represent the energy needed to raise water temperature by a specific amount.
Scientists often express energy in joules instead of a heat unit.
The heat unit commonly used in the United States is the BTU.

Related Terms

  • synonym – British thermal unit: a unit of heat equal to the amount required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit
  • synonym – calorie: a traditional heat unit; the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1°C
