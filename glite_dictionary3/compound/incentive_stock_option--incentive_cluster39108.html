incentive stock option

noun
stock option tied to company performance
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɛntɪv stɑk ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛntɪv stɒk ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
a stock option granted to employees, especially executives, as part of their compensation, usually conditioned on the achievement of specific company performance goals

Examples

incentive stock option grant
As part of her compensation, the executive received an incentive stock option package that would vest after three years.
Employees can benefit significantly from incentive stock options if the company's share price rises.
The company's financial growth triggered the release of incentive stock options to its senior managers.
