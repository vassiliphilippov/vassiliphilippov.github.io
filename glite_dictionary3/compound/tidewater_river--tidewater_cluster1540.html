tidewater river

noun
a river influenced by tides far inland
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪdˌwɔtər ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdˌwɔːtə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river or stream where the effects of ocean tides are perceptible far upstream

Examples

tidewater river with ocean tides
Fish populations in a tidewater river are influenced by changes in salinity due to the tides.
Settlements often grew along a tidewater river because ships could travel inland with the tides.
The Potomac is a well-known tidewater river where tidal effects can be seen far upstream.
