chronic pyelonephritis

noun
long-term kidney inflammation
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑː.nɪk paɪˌɛlənoʊˈfraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪk paɪˌɛl.ə.nɪˈfraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A form of pyelonephritis that develops gradually over a long period, potentially leading to kidney failure if untreated, often associated with kidney stones or narrowed urinary passages.

Examples

chronic pyelonephritis damages kidneys
Doctors suspect chronic pyelonephritis in patients with a history of kidney stones and persistent kidney inflammation.
If left untreated, chronic pyelonephritis can cause irreversible kidney damage and eventual renal failure.
The patient was diagnosed with chronic pyelonephritis after months of recurring urinary tract infections.

  • antonym – acute pyelonephritis: a sudden severe kidney infection
