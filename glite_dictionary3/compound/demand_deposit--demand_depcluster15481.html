demand deposit

noun
bank account funds available for immediate withdrawal
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmænd dɪˌpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːnd dɪˌpɒzɪt / (BrE)
A sum of money placed into a bank account from which withdrawals can be made at any time without advance notice.

Examples

demand deposit account
A checking account is a typical example of a demand deposit in modern banking.
Many people keep their salaries in a demand deposit because the funds are readily accessible.
The bank allows you to withdraw money from your demand deposit at any time without penalty.

Related Terms

  • antonym – savings account: a bank account that earns interest and often has restrictions on withdrawal
