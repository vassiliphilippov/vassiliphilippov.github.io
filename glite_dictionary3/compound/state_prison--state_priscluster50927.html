state prison

noun
US prison operated by a state
Pronunciation: / steɪt ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / steɪt ˈprɪzən / (BrE)
A prison that is operated and maintained by a state government in the United States, typically for felony offenders.

Examples

sent to state prison
After his conviction, he was sent to a state prison to serve his sentence.
Many state prisons are overcrowded due to rising incarceration rates.
