house of detention

noun
institution for temporary detention, often of juveniles
Pronunciation: / ˌhaʊs əv dɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaʊs əv dɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
an institution where people, especially juvenile offenders, are held in custody, usually under court supervision, for a temporary period

juvenile house of detention
