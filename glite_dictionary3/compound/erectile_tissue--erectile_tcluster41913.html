erectile tissue

noun
tissue that becomes rigid with blood
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛk.taɪl ˈtɪʃ.uː / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛk.taɪl ˈtɪs.juː / (BrE)
Tissue in the body that becomes rigid and enlarged when filled with blood, especially found in the penis, clitoris, and other organs.

Examples

erectile tissue in the penis
Damage to erectile tissue can affect normal sexual function.
The clitoris is made up of the same type of erectile tissue found in the male genitalia.
The penis contains a large amount of erectile tissue that fills with blood during arousal.
