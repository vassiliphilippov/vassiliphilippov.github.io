littoral zone

noun
region of shore near water
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬.ɚ.əl zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əl zəʊn / (BrE)
the part of the shore of a sea, lake, or ocean that is close to the water and influenced by the presence of water

Examples

the littoral zone of a lake
Birds often hunt for food in the littoral zone during low tide.
Many aquatic plants thrive in the littoral zone where sunlight can reach the lakebed.
The littoral zone is home to a diverse range of species due to its unique environment.
