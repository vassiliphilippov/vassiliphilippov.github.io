loaded language

noun
emotionally charged, manipulative wording
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.dɪd ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.dɪd ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
words or expressions with strong emotional connotations used to influence or manipulate opinions

Examples

avoid loaded language
Academic writing tries to avoid loaded language in order to maintain objectivity.
Debates can quickly become heated when participants use loaded language instead of neutral terms.
The journalist was accused of using loaded language to appeal to readers' emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – emotive language: language intended to evoke an emotional response
  • antonym – neutral language: unbiased or unemotional wording
