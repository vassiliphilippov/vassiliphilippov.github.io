global aphasia

noun
severe loss of all language abilities
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbl əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbl əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A severe form of aphasia in which a person loses nearly all ability to understand or express language, both spoken and written.

Examples

global aphasia after stroke
After his stroke, the patient was diagnosed with global aphasia and could no longer speak or understand language.
Doctors explained that global aphasia affects both the expressive and receptive aspects of language.
Rehabilitation for global aphasia focuses on improving communication through alternative means.
