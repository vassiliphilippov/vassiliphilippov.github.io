sphenoid fontanel

noun
soft spot where skull bones meet in infants
Pronunciation: / ˈsfiːnɔɪd fɒnˈtænəl / (AmE), / ˈsfiːnɔɪd fɒnˈtænəl / (BrE)
the irregularly shaped soft area on each side of an infant's skull where the frontal, parietal, temporal, and sphenoid bones meet; later becomes the pterion after ossification

Examples

infant sphenoid fontanel
Abnormal closure of the sphenoid fontanel can sometimes indicate underlying developmental conditions.
During a newborn examination, the doctor carefully palpated the sphenoid fontanel to check for normal skull development.
The sphenoid fontanel usually closes within the first six months of life as the skull bones fuse together.

Related Terms
