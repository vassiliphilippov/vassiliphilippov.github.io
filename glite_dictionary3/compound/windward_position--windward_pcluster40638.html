windward position

noun
side facing the wind
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndwɚd pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɪndwəd pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the side or location that faces toward the wind; the direction from which the wind is blowing

Examples

windward position in sailing
Sailing instructors emphasize the importance of staying in a windward position during a race.
The rescue boat remained in a windward position to better approach the stranded vessel.
When setting up camp, always choose a windward position to avoid smoke blowing into your tent.

Related Terms

  • antonym – leeward: the side sheltered from the wind; opposite of windward
