bass clef

noun
musical symbol for lower-pitched notes
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪs klɛf / (AmE), / ˈbæs klɛf / (BrE)
the clef marking that assigns the F below middle C to the fourth line of the musical staff, used for lower-pitched instruments and voices

Examples

bass clef symbol
Learning to read the bass clef can be challenging for beginner pianists.
Most trombone parts are written in the bass clef rather than the treble clef.
The cellist read the music using the bass clef at the start of each line.
