stock issue

noun
authorization and distribution of company shares
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / stɒk ˈɪʃu / (BrE)
(in corporation law) The authorization and distribution of shares of stock for sale to the public, or the shares thus offered at a particular time.

Examples

stock issue for investors
During the stock issue, thousands of new shares became available to investors.
Each stock issue must be approved by the board before sale to the public.
The company's first stock issue helped finance its rapid expansion.
