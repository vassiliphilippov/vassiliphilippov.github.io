leading edge

noun
forward edge of an airfoil or wing
Pronunciation: / ˈliːdɪŋ ˌɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪŋ ˌɛdʒ / (BrE)
the forward edge of an airfoil or wing, which first meets the airflow

Examples

leading edge of a wing
Engineers examined the leading edge of the wing for signs of damage after the storm.
Ice often builds up on the leading edge of airplane wings, affecting performance.
The leading edge is designed to minimize air resistance during flight.
