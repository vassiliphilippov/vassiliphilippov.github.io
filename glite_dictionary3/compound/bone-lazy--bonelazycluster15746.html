bone-lazy

adjective
extremely lazy; utterly idle
Pronunciation: / boʊn ˈleɪzi / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈleɪzi / (BrE)
Extremely lazy by nature; utterly idle or unwilling to make any effort.

Examples

a bone-lazy person
After lunch, he felt bone-lazy and decided to nap on the sofa.
She's so bone-lazy that even walking to the mailbox seems like a chore.
You can't expect bone-lazy students to turn in their homework on time.
