Caucasoid race

noun
term for a 'white' racial classification (outdated)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔː.kə.sɔɪd reɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.kə.sɔɪd reɪs / (BrE)
A term formerly used in physical anthropology to refer to people with light or white skin, mainly of European, North African, or Southwest Asian descent; now considered outdated and potentially offensive.

Examples

the Caucasoid race classification
In nineteenth-century anthropology, the Caucasoid race was proposed as one of several main racial groups.
Modern science has discredited the idea of a Caucasoid race as a valid biological concept.
Some old textbooks classify Europeans, North Africans, and West Asians as part of the Caucasoid race.

  • antonym – Mongoloid race: outdated anthropological classification for people of East Asian and related ancestry
  • antonym – Negroid race: outdated anthropological classification for people of sub-Saharan African ancestry
