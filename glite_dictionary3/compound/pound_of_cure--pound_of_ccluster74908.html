pound of cure

noun
large effort to fix a problem
Pronunciation: / paʊnd əv kjʊr / (AmE), / paʊnd əv kjʊə / (BrE)
A large amount of effort or resources required to fix or remedy problems, especially compared to preventing them in the first place.

Examples

spend a pound of cure
Remember: maintenance now prevents having to spend a pound of cure later.
They ignored the early warning signs and now require a pound of cure to address the situation.
Trying to repair the old bridge after years of neglect is truly a case of needing a pound of cure.
