serjeant-at-law

noun
English barrister of the highest rank (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrdʒənt æt ˈlɔ / (AmE), / ˌsɜːdʒənt ət ˈlɔː / (BrE)
a member of an order of barristers of the highest rank in England, formerly having exclusive rights of pleading in the Court of Common Pleas

Examples

a serjeant-at-law in court

The appointment of a serjeant-at-law was considered a high honor in medieval England.
