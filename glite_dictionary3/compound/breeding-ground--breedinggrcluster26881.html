breeding-ground

noun
place where something develops or thrives
Pronunciation: / ˈbriːdɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbriːdɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A place or environment where something is likely to develop or thrive, especially something undesirable or notable.

Examples

a breeding-ground for trouble
The abandoned warehouse was a breeding-ground for criminal activities.
The startup scene in the city has become a breeding-ground for tech innovation.
Unchecked online forums can be a breeding-ground for misinformation.
