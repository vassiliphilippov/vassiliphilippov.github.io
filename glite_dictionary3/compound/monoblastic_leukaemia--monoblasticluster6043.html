monoblastic leukaemia

noun
leukemia with proliferation of monocytes and monoblasts
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊnəˈblæstɪk luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈblæstɪk luːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A type of leukemia marked by the excessive production of monocytes and monoblasts in the blood.

Examples

monoblastic leukaemia diagnosis
Doctors monitor blood counts closely in patients with monoblastic leukaemia.
The patient was diagnosed with monoblastic leukaemia after laboratory tests revealed high numbers of monoblasts.
Treatment for monoblastic leukaemia often includes chemotherapy and supportive care.
