shale oil

noun
crude oil from shale formations (by fracking)
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ʃeɪl ɔɪl / (BrE)
(in petroleum industry and energy reporting) crude oil produced from shale formations by horizontal drilling and hydraulic fracturing; often referred to as 'tight oil'.

Examples

shale oil from drilling
The 2010s shale oil boom transformed energy markets and increased domestic crude production significantly.
Investors rushed into regions where shale oil reserves could be produced with horizontal drilling and fracking.
Environmentalists warned that expanding shale oil extraction could increase water use and local pollution risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – petroleum: a naturally occurring, flammable liquid consisting of hydrocarbons used as fuel and feedstock
  • synonym – tight oil: crude oil produced from low-permeability rock (including many shale formations) using fracking and horizontal drilling
