clapped out

adjective
worn out from use (informal)
Pronunciation: / klæpt aʊt / (AmE), / klæpt aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) Worn out from age or heavy use and no longer able to function properly, especially of vehicles, machines, or people.

Examples

clapped out old car
After working twelve hours straight, I felt absolutely clapped out.
The old van was completely clapped out by the time we finally sold it.
They replaced the clapped out generator with a more reliable model.

Related Terms

  • antonym – brand new: completely new; not used or worn at all
  • synonym – run-down: in poor physical condition; worn and tired
  • synonym – worn out: damaged or impaired from long use; exhausted
