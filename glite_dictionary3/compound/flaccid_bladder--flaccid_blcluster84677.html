flaccid bladder

noun
urinary disorder with loss of bladder control
Pronunciation: / ˈflæsɪd ˈblædər / (AmE), / ˈflæsɪd ˈblædə / (BrE)
a condition in which the bladder becomes distended and cannot contract properly to expel urine, often due to nerve damage, resulting in loss of bladder sensation and an inability to urinate voluntarily

Examples

flaccid bladder after nerve injury
A neurologist explained that a flaccid bladder can result from disruption of nerve pathways controlling urination.
In cases of flaccid bladder, individuals may not feel the urge to urinate even when the bladder is full.
The patient developed a flaccid bladder after spinal cord injury, requiring catheterization to empty his bladder.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neurogenic bladder: bladder dysfunction caused by neurologic damage
  • antonym – spastic bladder: bladder disorder involving involuntary contractions and difficulty holding urine
