coattails effect

noun
influence of a popular candidate on others' electoral success
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊt.teɪlz ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊt.teɪlz ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
the tendency for a popular candidate in an election to attract votes for other candidates of the same political party

Examples

coattails effect helps party
Analysts debated whether the coattails effect would boost down-ballot candidates this year.
The party relied on the coattails effect to win several closely contested districts.
The senator's popularity created a significant coattails effect in the state election.
