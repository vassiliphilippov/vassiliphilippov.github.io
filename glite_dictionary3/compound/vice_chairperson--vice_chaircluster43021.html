vice chairperson

noun
person second to a chairperson
Pronunciation: / vaɪs ˈtʃɛrˌpɜrsən / (AmE), / vaɪs ˈtʃeəˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
A person who ranks immediately below the chairperson and may act in their place when necessary.

Examples

vice chairperson leads meeting
After the chairperson resigned, the vice chairperson took over the meeting.
If the chairperson is absent, the vice chairperson will preside over the session.
The board elected a new vice chairperson to assist with leadership duties.
