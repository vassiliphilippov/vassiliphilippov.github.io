safety rail

noun
barrier for safety alongside paths or stairs
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti reɪl / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti reɪl / (BrE)
a strong bar or barrier, often made of metal, placed alongside stairways, roads, or platforms to prevent falls and provide protection

Examples

safety rail on stairs
He gripped the safety rail tightly as the train approached the platform.
New regulations require a safety rail on all elevated walkways.
The stairs to the basement have a safety rail installed for extra protection.

Related Terms

  • synonym – guardrail: a strong bar or barrier at the edge of something to prevent people from falling off
