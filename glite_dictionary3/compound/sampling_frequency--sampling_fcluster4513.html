sampling frequency

noun
rate of taking signal samples
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmpəlɪŋ friˈkwɛnsi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplɪŋ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
the rate at which samples of a continuously varying signal are taken, usually measured in hertz (Hz)

Examples

sampling frequency in audio recording
A higher sampling frequency allows for more accurate representation of the original signal.
The sampling frequency determines the quality of digital audio recordings.
When designing a system, engineers must choose an appropriate sampling frequency to avoid data loss.
