service ceiling

noun
maximum altitude for effective climb (aircraft)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːvɪs ˌsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs ˌsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the maximum altitude at which an aircraft can maintain a specified rate of climb under standard conditions

Examples

service ceiling of an aircraft
Pilots must be aware of the service ceiling when planning flight paths over mountains.
That aircraft approached its service ceiling during the test, limiting its climb rate significantly.
The service ceiling of this jet is 45,000 feet, making it suitable for high-altitude flights.
