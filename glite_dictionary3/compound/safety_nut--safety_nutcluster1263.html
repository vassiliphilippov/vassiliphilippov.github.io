safety nut

noun
supplementary nut for securing another nut
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti nʌt / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti nʌt / (BrE)
a supplementary nut screwed onto a primary nut to prevent it from loosening

Examples

safety nut on a bolt
Always use a safety nut on critical joints to prevent fasteners from loosening due to vibration.
She replaced the missing safety nut to make the assembly more secure.
The mechanic tightened the safety nut to ensure the wheel didn't come loose.
