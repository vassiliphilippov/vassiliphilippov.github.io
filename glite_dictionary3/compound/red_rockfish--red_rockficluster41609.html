red rockfish

noun
a large Pacific coast fish; red color
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈrɑkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈrɒkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large reddish fish found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for inhabiting rocky areas

Examples

catch a red rockfish
Many anglers along the Pacific coast hope to catch a red rockfish during the summer fishing season.
Some species of red rockfish can live for more than a hundred years.
The red rockfish is often cooked in stews or grilled because of its firm texture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rockfish: a general term for any fish of the genus Sebastes or related genera, found among rocks
