voltage divider

noun
circuit that splits voltage using resistors
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊltɪdʒ dɪˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈvəʊltɪdʒ dɪˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
a circuit of resistors connected in series across a voltage source, used to produce a desired fraction of the total voltage

Examples

voltage divider circuit
By adjusting the resistor values, the voltage divider can produce almost any desired output voltage.
The engineer designed a voltage divider to supply the correct voltage to the sensor.
To measure a lower voltage from a high voltage source, you can use a voltage divider.
