cross wire

noun
fine lines for aiming in optics
Pronunciation: / krɔs waɪr / (AmE), / krɒs waɪə / (BrE)
either of two fine mutually perpendicular wires or lines, used in the focus plane of an optical instrument for aiming or calibration

Examples

cross wire in a scope
He adjusted the cross wire so that it perfectly aligned with the reference point.
The telescope's cross wire helped the astronomer center the star in her field of view.
Through the microscope, the specimen was carefully positioned at the intersection of the cross wire.
