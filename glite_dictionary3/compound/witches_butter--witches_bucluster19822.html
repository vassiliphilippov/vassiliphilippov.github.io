witches' butter

noun
yellow jelly fungus
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtʃɪz ˌbʌtər / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪz ˌbʌtə / (BrE)
a yellow, jelly-like fungus found on decaying wood, often resembling lumps of butter

Examples

witches' butter on wood
After the rain, witches' butter appeared on the fallen branches in the forest.
Some people mistakenly think witches' butter is edible, but it is not commonly consumed.
The bright yellow color of witches' butter makes it easy to spot on decaying logs.
