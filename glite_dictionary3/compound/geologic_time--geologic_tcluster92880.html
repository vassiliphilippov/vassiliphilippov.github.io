geologic time

noun
the period of Earth's physical formation and development
Pronunciation: / dʒiˈɑː.lə.dʒɪk taɪm / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɒl.ə.dʒɪk taɪm / (BrE)
the vast period of time covering the physical formation and development of the Earth, especially before human history

Examples

geologic time scale
By studying rock layers, scientists reconstruct the events that occurred throughout geologic time.
Dinosaurs roamed the Earth during geologic time long before human beings appeared.
