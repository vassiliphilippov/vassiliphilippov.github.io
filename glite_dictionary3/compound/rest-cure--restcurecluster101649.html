rest-cure

noun
medical treatment involving enforced rest
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛstˌkjʊr / (AmE), / ˈrɛstˌkjʊə / (BrE)
a period of enforced rest prescribed as a medical treatment, especially for stress, anxiety, or nervous disorders

Examples

a rest-cure for recovery
After suffering from extreme exhaustion, her doctor recommended a rest-cure to help her recover.
During his rest-cure, he was confined to bed and forbidden from any stimulating activity.
The rest-cure was a common prescription in the nineteenth century for women diagnosed with hysteria.
