fuel-air bomb

noun
bomb using fuel-air explosive
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːl ɛr bɑm / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəl eə bɒm / (BrE)
A bomb that uses a fuel-air explosive, dispersing fuel and igniting it to create a powerful blast.

Examples

drop a fuel-air bomb
A fuel-air bomb can create a massive shockwave, making it effective against large area targets.
The international community has debated the use of fuel-air bombs due to their destructive power.
The military deployed a fuel-air bomb to clear the heavily fortified bunker.
