proportional tax

noun
tax with constant rate regardless of amount
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɔrʃənl tæks / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənl tæks / (BrE)
A tax system in which the tax rate remains the same, regardless of the amount of income or value subject to tax.

Examples

proportional tax system applies
A proportional tax ensures that everyone pays the same percentage of their income, no matter how much they earn.
Many flat tax proposals are based on the concept of a proportional tax structure.
Unlike a progressive tax, a proportional tax does not increase rates for higher income brackets.

Related Terms

  • antonym – progressive tax: a tax in which the rate increases as the taxable amount increases
