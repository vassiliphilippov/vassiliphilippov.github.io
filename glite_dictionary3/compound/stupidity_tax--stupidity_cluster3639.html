stupidity tax

noun
consequence of foolish decisions
Pronunciation: / stuˈpɪdəti tæks / (AmE), / stjuːˈpɪdəti tæks / (BrE)
a figurative penalty or loss incurred as a result of making foolish decisions or ignoring obvious warnings

Examples

pay a stupidity tax
After investing without research, she soon realized she'd paid a stupidity tax.
Forgetting to set an alarm and missing the flight felt like a stupidity tax.
He called the late fee on his credit card a self-imposed stupidity tax.
