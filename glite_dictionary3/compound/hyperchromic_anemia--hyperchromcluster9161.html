hyperchromic anemia

noun
anemia with excess hemoglobin in red cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈkroʊmɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrəʊmɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
a type of anemia in which red blood cells contain more hemoglobin than normal

Examples

hyperchromic anemia diagnosis
Patients with hyperchromic anemia often present with fatigue and shortness of breath.
The laboratory results indicated that she was suffering from hyperchromic anemia due to increased hemoglobin concentration.
Treatment of hyperchromic anemia requires careful identification of the underlying cause.
