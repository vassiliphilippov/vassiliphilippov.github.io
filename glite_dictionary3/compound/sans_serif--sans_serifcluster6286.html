sans serif

noun
typeface without serifs
Pronunciation: / sænz ˈsɛrɪf / (AmE), / sænz ˈsɛrɪf / (BrE)
a style of typeface in which the letters do not have small projecting features called serifs at the ends of strokes

Examples

sans serif font style
Helvetica is a well-known example of a sans serif font.
Many modern websites use sans serif fonts for a clean and minimalist appearance.
The report was formatted entirely in a sans serif typeface to improve readability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – serif: a small line or stroke attached to the end of a larger stroke in a letter or symbol
