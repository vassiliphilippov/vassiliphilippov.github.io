Archean aeon

noun
geological eon when Earth's crust formed
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈkiːən ˈiː.ɑːn / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiː.ən ˈiː.ən / (BrE)
The geological eon from about 3,800 million to 2,500 million years ago, during which Earth's crust formed and the earliest known life appeared.

Examples

the Archean aeon period
During the Archean aeon, the atmosphere was very different from that of today.
Scientists believe the Archean aeon was a pivotal period for the formation of the Earth’s crust.
The earliest evidence of life comes from rocks that formed in the Archean aeon.
