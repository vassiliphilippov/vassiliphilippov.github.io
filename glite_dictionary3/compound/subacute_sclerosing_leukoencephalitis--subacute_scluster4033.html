subacute sclerosing leukoencephalitis

noun
rare brain disease from persistent measles infection
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.əˈkjuːt ˌsklɛr.əˈsoʊ.zɪŋ ˌluː.koʊˌɛn.sə.fəˈlaɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.əˈkjuːt ˌsklɛr.əˈsəʊ.zɪŋ ˌluː.kəʊˌen.sə.fəˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A rare, chronic, and progressive brain inflammation caused by a persistent infection with the measles virus, primarily affecting children and young adults, and usually leading to death within a few years.

Examples

subacute sclerosing leukoencephalitis after measles
Children who contract measles very young are at a higher risk for subacute sclerosing leukoencephalitis.
The diagnosis of subacute sclerosing leukoencephalitis was confirmed after a series of neurological tests.
There is currently no cure for subacute sclerosing leukoencephalitis, making prevention through vaccination vital.
