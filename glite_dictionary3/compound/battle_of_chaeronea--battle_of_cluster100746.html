Battle of Chaeronea

proper noun
historic battles near Chaeronea in 338 BC and 86 BC
Pronunciation: / ˌbætəl əv kɛrəˈniːə / (AmE), / ˌbætl əv kiːrəˈniːə / (BrE)
A major battle fought in 338 BC near Chaeronea, where Philip II of Macedon defeated the allied Greek forces of Athens and Thebes; also refers to a later battle in 86 BC where Sulla defeated Mithridates' forces.

Examples

the Battle of Chaeronea in Greece
Historians study the Battle of Chaeronea to understand Philip II's military tactics.
The Battle of Chaeronea marked the end of Greek city-state independence.
The monument at the site of the Battle of Chaeronea attracts many visitors each year.
