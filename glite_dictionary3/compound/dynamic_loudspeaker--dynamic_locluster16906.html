dynamic loudspeaker

noun
type of loudspeaker using a moving coil
Pronunciation: / daɪˈnæmɪk ˈlaʊdˌspiːkər / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪk ˈlaʊdˌspiːkə / (BrE)
A type of loudspeaker that produces sound by moving a lightweight diaphragm, which is attached to a voice coil that moves within a magnetic field.

Examples

dynamic loudspeaker produces sound
A dynamic loudspeaker converts electrical signals into audible sound by moving a diaphragm and voice coil.
Most modern audio systems use a dynamic loudspeaker to reproduce music and speech clearly.
The engineer replaced the old tweeter with a new dynamic loudspeaker for better sound quality.
