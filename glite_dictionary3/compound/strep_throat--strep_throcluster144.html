strep throat

noun
sore throat caused by strep infection
Pronunciation: / stɹɛp θɹoʊt / (AmE), / stɹɛp θɹəʊt / (BrE)
A sore throat caused by infection with streptococcus bacteria, often accompanied by fever and swollen tonsils.

Examples

strep throat infection
Children are especially likely to get strep throat during the winter months.
