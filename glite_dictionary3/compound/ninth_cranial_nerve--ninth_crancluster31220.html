ninth cranial nerve

noun
cranial nerve involved in throat and tongue function
Pronunciation: / naɪnθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜːrv / (AmE), / naɪnθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the cranial nerve that supplies sensory fibers to the pharynx and back of the tongue, and motor fibers to muscles that elevate the pharynx and larynx; also includes parasympathetic fibers to the otic ganglion

Examples

the ninth cranial nerve controls swallowing
Damage to the ninth cranial nerve can result in difficulty eating and speaking.
Medical students must learn the function of the ninth cranial nerve during their anatomy courses.
The ninth cranial nerve plays a crucial role in swallowing and taste sensation.
