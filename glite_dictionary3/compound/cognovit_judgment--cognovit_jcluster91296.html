cognovit judgment

noun
court judgment by written debtor confession
Pronunciation: / kɑɡˈnoʊvɪt ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / kɒɡˈnəʊvɪt ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
A judgment entered by a court against a debtor based on the debtor's written confession, without the need for a trial or ordinary legal proceedings.

Examples

cognovit judgment for debt
A cognovit judgment can expedite debt collection for the creditor.
By signing a cognovit judgment, the defendant allowed the court to enter judgment without further litigation.
The creditor obtained a cognovit judgment after the debtor signed a written confession of debt.
