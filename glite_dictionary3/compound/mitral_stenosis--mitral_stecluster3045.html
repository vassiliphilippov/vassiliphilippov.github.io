mitral stenosis

noun
narrowing of the heart's mitral valve
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.trəl stəˈnoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.trəl stəˈnəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a narrowing or obstruction of the mitral valve in the heart, usually caused by scarring, often from rheumatic fever

Examples

mitral stenosis causes breathlessness
Rheumatic fever in childhood can sometimes lead to mitral stenosis later in life.
Surgery may be required if mitral stenosis becomes severe and interferes with normal heart function.
The patient was diagnosed with mitral stenosis after experiencing shortness of breath and fatigue.
