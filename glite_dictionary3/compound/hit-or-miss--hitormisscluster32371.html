hit-or-miss

adjective
unpredictable; depending on chance
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪt ər ˈmɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪt ɔː ˈmɪs / (BrE)
(of an action, result, or outcome) unpredictable and dependent on luck or chance, rather than careful planning or consistency.

Examples

hit-or-miss results
His attempts to solve the puzzle were completely hit-or-miss.
Quality control in the factory used to be hit-or-miss until they improved their procedures.
The reviews of the new restaurant have been rather hit-or-miss so far.
