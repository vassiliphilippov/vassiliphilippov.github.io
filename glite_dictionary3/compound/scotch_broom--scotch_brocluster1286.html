Scotch broom

noun
shrub with yellow flowers, Cytisus scoparius
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ bruːm / (AmE), / skɒtʃ bruːm / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub (Cytisus scoparius) native to western Europe, with slender green branches and bright yellow flowers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant

Examples

Scotch broom yellow flowers
Gardeners often plant Scotch broom for its vibrant spring flowers and hardy nature.
The hillside was covered in Scotch broom, its yellow blossoms brightening the landscape.
While lovely, Scotch broom can become invasive in some regions if not managed carefully.
