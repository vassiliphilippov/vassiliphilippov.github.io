straw man

noun
misrepresented argument for easy refutation
Pronunciation: / strɔ mæn /; / strɔː mæn / (AmE), / strɔː mæn / (BrE)
an argument or position that is misrepresented to make it easier to attack or criticize (a logical fallacy)

Examples

straw man argument
By creating a straw man, he avoided dealing with the real issue in the debate.
Critics accused her of knocking down a straw man argument rather than responding seriously.
The politician attacked a straw man instead of addressing her opponent's actual points.
Debaters often attack a straw man instead of addressing their opponent's actual position.
It's unfair to misrepresent my argument as a straw man just to dismiss it.
The politician set up a straw man by exaggerating the proposal before tearing it down.

Related Terms

  • synonym – argumentum ad hominem: an argument attacking the person rather than the position
  • synonym – red herring: a misleading clue or argument that diverts attention
