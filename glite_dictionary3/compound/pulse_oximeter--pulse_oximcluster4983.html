pulse oximeter

noun
device for measuring blood oxygen saturation
Pronunciation: / pʌls ɑkˈsɪmɪtɚ / (AmE), / pʌls ɒkˈsɪmɪtə / (BrE)
A small medical device that non-invasively measures the oxygen saturation of someone's blood, usually by attaching to a fingertip or earlobe.

Examples

use a pulse oximeter on finger
Many athletes use a pulse oximeter during training at high altitudes.
The nurse clipped a pulse oximeter to my finger to check my oxygen levels.
Your doctor may recommend using a pulse oximeter at home if you have a respiratory condition.
