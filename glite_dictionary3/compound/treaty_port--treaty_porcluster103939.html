treaty port

noun
historical East Asian port opened by treaty
Pronunciation: / ˈtriti pɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtriːti pɔːt / (BrE)
a port in China, Korea, or Japan that was historically opened to foreign trade by treaty, particularly in the 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

treaty port in China
Foreign businesses flourished in the treaty port districts of 19th-century Japan.
Many treaty ports were established in China after the Opium Wars.
Shanghai was once a major treaty port where foreign powers held extraterritorial rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym – free port: a port open to all traders without customs duties
