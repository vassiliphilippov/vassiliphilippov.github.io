idler pulley

noun
pulley guiding or tightening a belt
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪdlɚ ˌpʊli / (AmE), / ˈaɪdlə ˌpʊli / (BrE)
a pulley mounted on a shaft that presses against a guide belt to guide or maintain tension in the belt

Examples

idler pulley guides belt
A faulty idler pulley can cause the serpentine belt to slip or break unexpectedly.
Routine inspections often include checking the tension of the idler pulley in the drive system.
The mechanic replaced the worn idler pulley to stop the squeaking noise from the engine belt.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pulley: a wheel on an axle or shaft designed to support movement and change of direction of a taut cable or belt
