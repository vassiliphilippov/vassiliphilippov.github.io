Marshall Plan

proper noun
U.S. program for European recovery (1948–52)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrʃəl plæn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl plæn / (BrE)
A United States program of economic aid for the reconstruction of Western Europe after World War II, active from 1948 to 1952.

Examples

the Marshall Plan for Europe
Many historians consider the Marshall Plan one of the most successful foreign aid initiatives ever undertaken.
The Marshall Plan was instrumental in the economic recovery of postwar Europe.
Without the Marshall Plan, Europe's postwar reconstruction would have been much slower and more difficult.
