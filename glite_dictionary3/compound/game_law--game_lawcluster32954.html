game law

noun
law managing hunting or game animals
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪm lɔ / (AmE), / ɡeɪm lɔː / (BrE)
a law or regulation intended to manage, conserve, or protect game animals, especially by controlling hunting and trapping

Examples

game law protects animals
Hunters must be familiar with local game laws before going out in the field.
The new game law has helped increase the deer population in the region.
Violating a game law can result in hefty fines or even jail time.

Related Terms

  • antonym – poaching: illegal hunting or capturing of wild animals
  • synonym – wildlife law: the body of law that governs the conservation and use of wildlife
