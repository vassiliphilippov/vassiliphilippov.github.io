hot flash

noun
sudden sensation of heat (often in menopause)
Pronunciation: / hɑt flæʃ / (AmE), / hɒt flæʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, brief sensation of intense heat, typically experienced by women during menopause or as a symptom of certain medical conditions

Examples

experience a hot flash
During menopause, many women experience hot flashes that can be quite uncomfortable.
She woke up in the night drenched in sweat from a particularly severe hot flash.
Some medications can cause side effects like dizziness, nausea, or hot flashes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hot flush: a sudden feeling of warmth, especially as a symptom of menopause (British English term)
