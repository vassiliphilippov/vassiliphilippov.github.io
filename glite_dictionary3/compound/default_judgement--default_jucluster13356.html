default judgement

noun
court decision due to party's failure to respond
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔlt ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a legal decision made by a court in favor of one party when the other party fails to appear or take necessary action

Examples

receive a default judgement
If you ignore the lawsuit, you could end up with a default judgement against you.
The court issued a default judgement after the defendant failed to show up for the hearing.
The plaintiff won a default judgement when the company did not respond to the complaint.

  • synonym – summary judgement: legal ruling made without a full trial
