wide stance

noun
position with feet far apart
Pronunciation: / waɪd stæns / (AmE), / waɪd stɑːns / (BrE)
A position in which the feet are placed far apart, often to improve stability or balance.

Examples

wide stance for balance
She adjusted to a wide stance before swinging the golf club to increase her power.
The coach instructed the players to adopt a wide stance for better balance during the drill.
With a wide stance, the weightlifter felt more secure lifting heavy weights above his head.
