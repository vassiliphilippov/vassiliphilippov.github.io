special contract

noun
sealed, formal contract
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl kənˈtrɑːkt / (BrE)
a formal contract signed and sealed by the parties, typically distinguished by the affixing of a (wax) seal

Examples

special contract with a seal
The court ruled that the special contract was binding because it bore the official seal.

Until recently, few businesses still used a special contract with a physical wax seal.
