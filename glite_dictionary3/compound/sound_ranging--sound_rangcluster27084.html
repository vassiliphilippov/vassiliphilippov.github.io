sound ranging

noun
technique to locate sound source using microphones
Pronunciation: / saʊnd ˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of locating the source of a sound, especially an enemy gun, by measuring the time it takes for the sound to reach microphones in known positions

Examples

sound ranging enemy guns
During World War I, armies frequently used sound ranging to pinpoint the location of enemy artillery.
Modern engineers have improved sound ranging techniques for use in urban environments.
The accuracy of sound ranging depends on the precise placement of microphones and timing devices.
