polyhedral angle

noun
angle formed by intersecting planes at a vertex
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈhɪdrəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈhiːdrəl ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the space formed at the intersection of three or more planes that meet at a single point (vertex) in a solid figure

Examples

polyhedral angle at a vertex
A cube has a right polyhedral angle at each corner, where three faces come together.
