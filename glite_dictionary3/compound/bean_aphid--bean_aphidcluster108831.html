bean aphid

noun
a blackish aphid pest on beans and crops
Pronunciation: / bin ˈæfɪd / (AmE), / biːn ˈæfɪd / (BrE)
a small blackish aphid (Aphis fabae) that infests beans, sugar beets, and other plants, often causing damage

Examples

bean aphid infestation
Farmers often lose bean crops to infestations of the bean aphid, which feeds on plant sap.
Researchers are developing eco-friendly ways to control the bean aphid population.
The bean aphid spreads viruses among sugar beets and other vegetables.
