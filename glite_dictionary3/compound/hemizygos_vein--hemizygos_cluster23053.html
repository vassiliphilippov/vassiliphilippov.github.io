hemizygos vein

noun
vein on the left side of the vertebral column
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmɪˈzaɪɡəs veɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˈzɪɡɒs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein on the left side of the vertebral column, continuing from the left ascending lumbar vein, crossing the middle of the body near the 8th vertebra, and emptying into the azygos vein.

Examples

hemizygos vein joins azygos
During surgery, the hemizygos vein can be identified as it joins the azygos vein.
Radiologists observed an enlargement of the hemizygos vein in the patient's CT scan.
The hemizygos vein crosses the midline of the body at the eighth thoracic vertebra.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – azygos vein: the vein into which the hemizygos vein empties, located on the right side of the vertebral column
