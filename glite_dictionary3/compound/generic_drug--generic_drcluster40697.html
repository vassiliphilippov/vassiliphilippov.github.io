generic drug

noun
pharmaceutical drug not protected by patent
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛrɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that is equivalent to a brand-name drug in dosage, strength, route of administration, quality, and intended use, but is marketed under its chemical name after the original product's patent expires.

Examples

buy a generic drug
After the patent expired, several generic drugs became widely available for treating the condition.
Doctors often prescribe generic drugs when they are confident in their effectiveness and safety.
Many patients choose generic drugs because they offer the same benefits at a lower cost.
