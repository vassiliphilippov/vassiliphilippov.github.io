cationic detergent

noun
synthetic detergent with a cationic surfactant
Pronunciation: / ˌkætaɪˈɑnɪk dɪˈtɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌkætaɪˈɒnɪk dɪˈtɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
a synthetic detergent whose surface-active component is a positively charged ion (cation), used especially as a disinfectant or fabric softener

Examples

cationic detergent disinfects surfaces
Hospitals often use cationic detergent solutions to disinfect surfaces.
Many fabric softeners are cationic detergents that coat fibers to reduce static.
Unlike soaps, cationic detergents are effective in hard water conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anionic detergent: a synthetic detergent in which the active part of the molecule is an anion
