lumbar nerve

noun
spinal nerve emerging from the lumbar region
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌm.bɑr nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈlʌm.bɑː nɜːv / (BrE)
any of the five pairs of spinal nerves that emerge from the lumbar section of the spinal cord and supply the lower back, abdomen, and legs

Examples

lumbar nerve injury
Injuries to the lumbar nerves often result from car accidents or heavy lifting.
Pain radiating down the leg can sometimes be traced to a compressed lumbar nerve.
The surgeon was careful not to damage the lumbar nerve during the procedure.
