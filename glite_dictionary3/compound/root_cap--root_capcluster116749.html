root cap

noun
protective cell mass at root tip
Pronunciation: / ruːt kæp / (AmE), / ruːt kap / (BrE)
a small, thimble-shaped mass of cells that covers and protects the growing tip of a plant root

Examples

root cap protects tip
A damaged root cap can make the root more vulnerable to injury and disease.
Scientists studied how the root cap responds to different soil conditions.
The root cap protects the delicate cells at the tip of the root as it pushes through the soil.
