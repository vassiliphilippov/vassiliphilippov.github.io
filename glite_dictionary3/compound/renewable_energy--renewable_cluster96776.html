renewable energy

noun
energy from naturally replenished sources
Pronunciation: / rɪˈnuː.ə.bəl ˈen.ɚ.dʒi / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuː.ə.bəl ˈen.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
energy that is generated from natural sources which are constantly replenished, such as sunlight, wind, or water

Examples

renewable energy sources
Many countries are investing heavily in renewable energy to combat climate change.
The new legislation encourages businesses to switch to renewable energy sources like wind and solar.
Universities are conducting research on improving the efficiency of renewable energy technologies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fossil fuel: energy source formed from ancient organic matter, not replenished
