atrioventricular trunk

noun
heart muscle bundle transmitting impulses to ventricles
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtrioʊvɛnˈtrɪkjələr trʌŋk / (AmE), / ˌeɪtrɪəʊvɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə trʌŋk / (BrE)
a bundle of specialized heart muscle fibers that transmits the electrical impulse from the atrioventricular node to the ventricles, causing them to contract

Examples

Damage to the atrioventricular trunk can disrupt the normal rhythm of the heartbeat.
Doctors studied the function of the atrioventricular trunk in patients with cardiac arrhythmias.
The atrioventricular trunk is essential for coordinating the contraction of the heart's ventricles.
