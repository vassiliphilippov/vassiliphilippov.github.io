cheek pouch

noun
membranous sac in animal's mouth for storing food
Pronunciation: / tʃiːk paʊt͡ʃ / (AmE), / tʃiːk paʊtʃ / (BrE)
a membranous sac inside the mouth of certain animals, especially rodents, used for storing food temporarily

Examples

hamster cheek pouch
Hamsters use their cheek pouch to carry food back to their nests.
Many rodents have a cheek pouch that allows them to store large amounts of seeds.
The scientist examined the gopher's cheek pouch under a microscope.
