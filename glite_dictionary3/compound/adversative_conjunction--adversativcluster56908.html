adversative conjunction

noun
a conjunction expressing contrast or opposition
Pronunciation: / ædˈvɝːsətɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːsətɪv kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction that expresses contrast or opposition between the meanings of the clauses or phrases it connects, such as 'but', 'however', or 'yet'

Examples

adversative conjunction like but
In the sentence 'She tried hard, but she failed,' the adversative conjunction links contrasting ideas.
The word 'but' is an example of an adversative conjunction in English grammar.
Writers often use an adversative conjunction to highlight differences between two statements.

  • antonym – subordinating conjunction: a conjunction that introduces a subordinate clause
