bulgur wheat

noun
parboiled, crushed wheat used in cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌlɡər wiːt / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɡə wiːt / (BrE)
a cereal food made from wheat kernels that have been parboiled, dried, and crushed, used especially in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines

Examples

cooked bulgur wheat salad
Many vegetarian recipes call for bulgur wheat as a base ingredient instead of rice or pasta.
Tabbouleh is a classic salad that features bulgur wheat, parsley, tomatoes, and lemon juice.
You can find bulgur wheat in the grains aisle of most supermarkets.
