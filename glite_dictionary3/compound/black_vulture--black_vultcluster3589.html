black vulture

noun
large Eurasian-African vulture species
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈvʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large dark-plumaged vulture (Aegypius monachus) native to southern Eurasia and northern Africa, noted for its robust build and scavenging habits.

Examples

Eurasian black vulture
Researchers observed a black vulture circling over the savanna in northern Africa.
The black vulture is one of the largest birds of prey found in Eurasia.
Unlike the New World species, the black vulture of Eurasia has a distinctive mane of feathers around its neck.
