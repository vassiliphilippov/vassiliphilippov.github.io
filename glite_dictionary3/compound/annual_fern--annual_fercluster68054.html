annual fern

noun
small, short-lived tropical fern
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ju.əl fɝn / (AmE), / ˈæn.ju.əl fɜːn / (BrE)
A small, short-lived fern native to Central and South America, completing its life cycle within a year.

Examples

small annual fern
Because it completes its life cycle in a year, the annual fern must grow and reproduce quickly.
Gardeners sometimes plant annual ferns for their delicate foliage and fast growth.
The annual fern thrives in the humid climates of Central and South America.
