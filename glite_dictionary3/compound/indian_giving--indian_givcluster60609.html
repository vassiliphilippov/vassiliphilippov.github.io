Indian giving

noun
taking back a previously given gift (offensive idiom)
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˌɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of taking back a gift after having given it, or expecting a gift to be returned; considered offensive due to its negative stereotypical connotations.

Examples

Indian giving after a present
Many found his Indian giving behavior rude and inconsiderate at the birthday party.
Please avoid Indian giving; if you give something, let it stay given.
She accused him of Indian giving after he asked for the gift back.
