right coronary artery

noun
artery supplying right side of the heart
Pronunciation: / raɪt ˈkɔːrənɛri ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / raɪt ˈkɒr.ə.nər.i ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
the artery that arises from the right aortic sinus and supplies blood to the right side of the heart

Examples

blocked right coronary artery
A blockage in the right coronary artery can lead to a heart attack affecting the right side of the heart.
Surgeons worked to restore blood flow through the right coronary artery during the bypass operation.
The right coronary artery is often examined during cardiac catheterization procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym – left coronary artery: the artery arising from the left aortic sinus, supplying the left side of the heart
