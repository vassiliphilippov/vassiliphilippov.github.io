third dimension

noun
depth dimension in space
Pronunciation: / θɝd dɪˈmɛnʃən / (AmE), / θɜːd dɪˈmɛnʃən / (BrE)
the property or aspect of space that adds depth to length and width, distinguishing three-dimensional objects from two-dimensional ones

Examples

third dimension of space
A cube demonstrates the concept of the third dimension compared to a square.
The artist used shading to give the illusion of the third dimension on a flat canvas.
Virtual reality graphics aim to immerse users in the third dimension of digital environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – three-dimensional: having or appearing to have three dimensions (length, width, and depth)
  • antonym – two-dimensional: having only length and width, lacking depth
