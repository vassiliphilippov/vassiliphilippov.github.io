delayed action

noun
self-timer mechanism on a camera
Pronunciation: / dɪˈleɪd ˈækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪd ˈakʃən / (BrE)
a mechanism in a camera that automatically delays the release of the shutter for a set period, allowing the photographer to appear in the picture

Examples

delayed action camera timer
He set the delayed action on the camera so he could run into the group photo.
Many digital cameras are equipped with a delayed action function for self-portraits.
Using the delayed action helps reduce camera shake when taking long exposure shots.
