Indian tick fever

noun
tick-borne infectious disease in India and Mediterranean
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.di.ən tɪk ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.di.ən tɪk ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
A disease, common in India and around the Mediterranean, caused by a rickettsia and transmitted to humans by a reddish-brown tick that lives on dogs and other mammals.

Examples

Indian tick fever infection
Indian tick fever most commonly affects people who are exposed to ticks on dogs and livestock.
Doctors in rural clinics often screen patients for Indian tick fever during the summer.
Preventing Indian tick fever requires controlling tick populations on domestic animals.
