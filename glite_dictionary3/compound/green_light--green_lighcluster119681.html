green light

noun
signal or permission to proceed
Pronunciation: / ɡrin laɪt / (AmE), / ɡriːn laɪt / (BrE)
official permission or a signal to begin or continue something (such as a green traffic light or approval to start a project)

Examples

get the green light to start
After getting the green light from the authorities, construction began immediately.
The manager gave the project a green light to move forward next month.
We had to wait for the green light before crossing the intersection.
Once the project received the green light, the team began work immediately.
The city council finally gave the green light to the new sports complex.
We're waiting for management to give us the green light before we start the campaign.
