Proterozoic eon

noun
geological eon (2,500–544 million years ago)
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtəroʊˈzoʊɪk ˈiːɒn / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtərəʊˈzəʊɪk ˈiːən / (BrE)
the geological eon lasting from about 2,500 to 544 million years ago, marked by the appearance of bacteria, fungi, and the earliest multicellular organisms

Examples

the Proterozoic eon period
Scientists study rocks from the Proterozoic eon to learn about the planet's early atmosphere.
The Proterozoic eon saw the emergence of simple multicellular life forms in Earth's oceans.
The end of the Proterozoic eon is marked by the rise of more complex life during the Cambrian period.

  • synonym – Precambrian: the eon before the Cambrian period, including the Proterozoic
