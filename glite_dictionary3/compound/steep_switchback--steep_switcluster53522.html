steep switchback

noun
sharp turn on a steep slope
Pronunciation: / stiːp ˈswɪtʃˌbæk / (AmE), / stiːp ˈswɪtʃˌbak / (BrE)
a sharp and abrupt bend in a road or path on a steep slope, usually intended to help vehicles or hikers ascend or descend more safely

Examples

dangerous steep switchback
A sudden rainstorm made the steep switchback especially slippery and hazardous.
Driving up the steep switchback required careful steering and low gear.
The hikers slowed down as they encountered a steep switchback on the mountain trail.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hairpin bend: a very sharp turn in a road, resembling a hairpin shape
