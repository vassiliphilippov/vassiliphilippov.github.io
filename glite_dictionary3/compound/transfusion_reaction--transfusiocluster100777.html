transfusion reaction

noun
adverse reaction to a blood transfusion
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfjuːʒən riˈækʃən / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːʒən rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
a harmful response by the body to receiving incompatible blood during a transfusion, which can cause symptoms from mild allergic reactions to serious complications such as kidney failure or death

Examples

severe transfusion reaction
Doctors closely monitor for any signs of transfusion reaction during and after the procedure.
Preventing a transfusion reaction requires careful matching of blood types.
The patient suffered a severe transfusion reaction shortly after receiving the wrong blood type.

Related Terms

  • synonym – allergic reaction: an immune system response to a substance that is normally harmless
