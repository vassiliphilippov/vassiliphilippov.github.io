sorrel tree

noun
deciduous tree of eastern North America
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrəl tri / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of eastern North America with deeply fissured bark, sprays of small fragrant white flowers, and sour-tasting leaves.

Examples

sorrel tree with white flowers
Hikers in the Appalachian region often encounter the sorrel tree growing along wooded slopes.
Leaves from the sorrel tree have a distinctly sour taste that gives the tree its common name.
The sorrel tree is known for its clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer.
