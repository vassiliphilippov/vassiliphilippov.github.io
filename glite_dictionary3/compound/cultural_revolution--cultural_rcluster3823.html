Cultural Revolution

noun
radical Chinese political movement (1966–1976) led by Mao Zedong
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltʃərəl ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˈkʌltʃərəl ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the radical political movement in China (1966–1976), led by Mao Zedong, aiming to enforce communism by removing capitalist, traditional, and cultural elements from society, resulting in widespread upheaval and persecution, especially of intellectuals.

Examples

the Cultural Revolution in China
Her family suffered greatly during the Cultural Revolution, losing their home and livelihood.
Historians continue to debate the long-term effects of the Cultural Revolution on Chinese society.
The Cultural Revolution caused immense disruption to China's education system and intellectual life.
