branchial cleft

noun
slit opening in fish/amphibian pharynx for water passage
Pronunciation: / ˈbrænkiəl klɛft / (AmE), / ˈbraŋkɪəl klɛft / (BrE)
one of a series of slit-like openings in the pharynx of fishes and some amphibians, through which water passes for respiration

Examples

branchial cleft in fish
Some congenital disorders in humans are related to improper closure of the branchial clefts during development.
The embryonic development of the fish revealed the formation of several branchial clefts.
Water passes through the branchial cleft, allowing the fish to extract oxygen efficiently.
