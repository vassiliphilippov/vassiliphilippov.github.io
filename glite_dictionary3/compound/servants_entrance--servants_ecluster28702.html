servant's entrance

noun
entrance for servants or deliveries
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːvənts ˈɛntrəns / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvənts ˈɛntrəns / (BrE)
a separate entrance to a building, typically at the rear or side, intended for use by servants or for the delivery of goods and removal of refuse

Examples

the servant's entrance at the back
During the tour, guests were shown the old servant's entrance that led directly to the kitchen.
Groceries and supplies were always brought in through the servant's entrance to avoid disturbing the family.
The staff were instructed to use the servant's entrance at the back of the manor.
