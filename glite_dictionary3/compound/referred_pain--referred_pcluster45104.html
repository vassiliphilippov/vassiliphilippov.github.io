referred pain

noun
pain perceived away from its actual source
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɜrd peɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfɜːd peɪn / (BrE)
pain that is perceived in a part of the body other than its actual source due to nerve pathways

Examples

referred pain in the arm
A heart attack can produce referred pain in the jaw or left arm.
Doctors often consider referred pain when diagnosing chest or abdominal complaints.
The patient’s backache turned out to be referred pain from a kidney infection.
