probiotic flora

noun
beneficial gut bacteria
Pronunciation: / proʊˈbaɪɑtɪk ˈflɔrə / (AmE), / prəʊˈbaɪ.ət.ɪk ˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
the community of beneficial bacteria that live in the intestines and help maintain digestive health

Examples

healthy probiotic flora
A diet rich in fiber supports the growth of probiotic flora in the gut.
Some yogurts contain live cultures that help maintain a healthy balance of probiotic flora.
Taking antibiotics can disrupt your natural probiotic flora and lead to digestive issues.
