Spanish oyster plant

noun
thistle with edible roots and leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈspænɪʃ ˈɔɪstər plænt / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ ˈɔɪstə plɑːnt / (BrE)
A golden thistle native to southwestern Europe, cultivated for its edible sweet roots, leaves, and stalks; its yellow flowers are sometimes used as a substitute for saffron.

Examples

Spanish oyster plant sweet roots
Farmers in Spain often cultivate Spanish oyster plant both for food and for use as a saffron substitute.

The Spanish oyster plant is prized in Mediterranean cuisine for its sweet, nutty roots.
