concrete noun

noun
noun referring to tangible object
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.krit naʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kriːt naʊn / (BrE)
A noun that denotes a physical object, place, or substance that can be perceived by the senses.

Examples

concrete noun example word
A 'table' is a concrete noun because it refers to something that can be seen and touched.
When teaching grammar, it helps to distinguish between a concrete noun and an abstract noun.
Words like 'dog,' 'apple,' and 'river' are all examples of a concrete noun.

Related Terms

  • antonym – abstract noun: noun denoting an idea, quality, or state rather than a concrete object
