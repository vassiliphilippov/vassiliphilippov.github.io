spruce grouse

noun
a North American grouse species
Pronunciation: / spruːs ɡraʊs / (AmE), / spruːs ɡraʊs / (BrE)
A North American grouse (Falcipennis canadensis) that inhabits coniferous forests and feeds mainly on evergreen buds and needles.

Examples

spruce grouse in forest
Spruce grouse are well camouflaged against the forest floor, making them hard to spot.
The diet of the spruce grouse consists mainly of spruce needles in the winter months.
We caught a glimpse of a spruce grouse as we hiked through the pine forest.
