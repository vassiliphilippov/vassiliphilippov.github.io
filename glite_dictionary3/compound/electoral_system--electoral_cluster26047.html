electoral system

noun
system for conducting elections
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktərəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktərəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The set of rules and procedures by which votes are cast and counted to elect representatives in a democracy.

Examples

proportional electoral system
A new electoral system was introduced after widespread criticism of the previous one.
Debates about the advantages of each electoral system are common during election season.
The country decided to reform its electoral system to ensure fairer representation.
