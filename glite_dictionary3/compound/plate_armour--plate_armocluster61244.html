plate armour

noun
historical body armor made from metal plates
Pronunciation: / pleɪt ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / pleɪt ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A type of personal armor made from large iron or steel plates, used historically to protect the body, especially in the late Middle Ages.

Examples

medieval plate armour
By the fifteenth century, plate armour had largely replaced mail among European cavalry.
Craftsmen spent months forging plate armour to fit each individual warrior perfectly.
The museum’s medieval collection features a stunning suit of plate armour once worn by a famous knight.
