rickettsial disease

noun
disease caused by rickettsial bacteria
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɛtsiəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / rɪˈkɛtsiəl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by rickettsial bacteria, typically transmitted by ticks, mites, or body lice

Examples

serious rickettsial disease
Early treatment with antibiotics is crucial for patients diagnosed with a rickettsial disease.
Rocky Mountain spotted fever is a well-known rickettsial disease transmitted by ticks in North America.
Typhus is another type of rickettsial disease caused by lice and associated with crowded living conditions.
