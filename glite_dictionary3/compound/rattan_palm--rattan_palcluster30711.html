rattan palm

noun
tropical climbing palm with long, flexible stems
Pronunciation: / rəˈtæn pɑm / (AmE), / rəˈtæn pɑːm / (BrE)
A climbing palm native to Sri Lanka and southern India, notable for its long stems used in making items such as Malacca canes and furniture.

Examples

rattan palm furniture
Harvesting the rattan palm requires skill due to the plant's spiny stems and great length.
Local artisans in Sri Lanka have long relied on rattan palm for weaving baskets and chairs.
The furniture was crafted from rattan palm, making it both lightweight and durable.
