rectifying valve

noun
thermionic tube used as a rectifier
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪɪŋ vælv / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪɪŋ vælv / (BrE)
an electronic device consisting of a thermionic tube with two electrodes, used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) as a rectifier.

Examples

rectifying valve in a radio
Early radios often used a rectifying valve to convert AC power to DC.
Replacing the old rectifying valve improved the radio's performance.
The rectifying valve failed, causing the device to malfunction.
