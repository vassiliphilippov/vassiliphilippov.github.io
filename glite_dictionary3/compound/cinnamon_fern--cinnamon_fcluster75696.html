cinnamon fern

noun
a North American fern with cinnamon-colored fronds
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnəmən fɝn / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən fɜːn / (BrE)
A large North American fern with woolly, cinnamon-colored spore-bearing fronds appearing in early spring, later surrounded by green fronds; the young fronds are edible.

Examples

cinnamon fern fronds uncurling
In early spring, the uncurling fronds of the cinnamon fern are sometimes harvested as food.
The distinctive cinnamon-colored stalks make the cinnamon fern easy to identify in the forest.
We spotted a beautiful cinnamon fern growing by the stream during our hike.
