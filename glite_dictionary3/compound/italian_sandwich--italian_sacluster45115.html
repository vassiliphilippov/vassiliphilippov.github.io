Italian sandwich

noun
large sandwich with meats, cheese, and vegetables
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæliən ˈsændwɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈtæliən ˈsændwɪtʃ / (BrE)
A large sandwich made with a long crusty roll split lengthwise and filled with various meats, cheese, and often vegetables such as tomato, onion, and lettuce, typically accompanied by condiments. Known by different names in various regions of the United States.

Examples

a big Italian sandwich
He brought a homemade Italian sandwich to the picnic, complete with olives and spicy peppers.
I ordered an Italian sandwich for lunch, piled high with salami, ham, and provolone cheese.
The deli on Main Street makes the best Italian sandwich in town, loaded with fresh vegetables and meats.
