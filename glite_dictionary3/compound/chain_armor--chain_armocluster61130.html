chain armor

noun
flexible armor of linked metal rings
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
Flexible armor made by linking small metal rings together, commonly used during the Middle Ages.

Examples

chain armor protects knights
Chain armor offered more flexibility than solid plate armor, but less defense against heavy weapons.
Medieval knights often wore chain armor for protection in battle.
The museum displayed a suit of chain armor from the twelfth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mail: armor made of interlinked metal rings; another term for chain armor
  • antonym – plate armor: armor made from large solid metal plates
