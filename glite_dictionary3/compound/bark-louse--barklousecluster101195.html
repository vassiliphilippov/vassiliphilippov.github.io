bark-louse

noun
small insect living on tree bark
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrkˌlaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɑːkˌlaʊs / (BrE)
A small insect that lives on the bark of trees and shrubs, feeding on fungi, algae, or plant material.

Examples

bark-louse on tree
A bark-louse infestation often resembles fine dust on the surface of tree bark.
Several species of bark-louse can be found in temperate forests around the world.
We found a bark-louse colony clinging to the rough trunk of the maple tree.
