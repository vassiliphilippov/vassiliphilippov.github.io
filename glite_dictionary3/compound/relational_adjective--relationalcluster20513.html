relational adjective

noun
grammatical term for a classifying adjective
Pronunciation: / rɪˈleɪʃənəl ˈædʒɪktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃənəl ˈadʒɪktɪv / (BrE)
An adjective that classifies or restricts its noun by relation rather than describing its qualities, as in 'a musical instrument' or 'nervous disease'.

Examples

relational adjective in grammar
'Musical' as in 'a musical instrument' is a classic example of a relational adjective.
Linguists sometimes distinguish between descriptive and relational adjectives when analyzing noun phrases.
Unlike 'beautiful', which is descriptive, 'chemical' in 'chemical reaction' is a relational adjective.

Related Terms

  • antonym – descriptive adjective: an adjective that describes a property or quality of the noun
