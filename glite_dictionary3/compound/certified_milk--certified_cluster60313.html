certified milk

noun
milk supervised by a medical commission
Pronunciation: / sɝːˈtɪfaɪd mɪlk / (AmE), / səːˈtɪfʌɪd mɪlk / (BrE)
milk produced and distributed under supervision of an authorized medical commission to ensure sanitary conditions and safety

Examples

certified milk for babies
In the early 20th century, certified milk was recommended for infants due to its strict hygiene standards.
The hospital only used certified milk for patients with compromised immune systems.
Today, certified milk is rarely found, as modern pasteurization has made most milk products safe.
