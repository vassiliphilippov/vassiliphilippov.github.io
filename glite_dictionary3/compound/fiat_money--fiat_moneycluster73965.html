fiat money

noun
government-issued paper currency not backed by commodity
Pronunciation: / ˈfi.æt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈfiː.æt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
Money that has value because a government maintains it as legal tender, but is not backed by a physical commodity such as gold or silver.

Examples

fiat money replaces gold
After the government introduced fiat money, faith in the currency became crucial to its stability.
The U.S. dollar is a classic example of fiat money since it is not backed by gold or silver.
Unlike gold coins, fiat money relies on the trust in the issuing authority.

Related Terms

  • antonym – commodity money: money backed by a physical commodity such as gold or silver
