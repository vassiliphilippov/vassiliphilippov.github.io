willful ignorance

noun
deliberate disregard for information
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlfəl ˈɪɡnərəns / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfʊl ˈɪɡnərəns / (BrE)
the deliberate and conscious decision to ignore information or facts, especially those that are unpleasant or inconvenient

Examples

willful ignorance of the truth
Her willful ignorance of the rules led to repeated mistakes.
Some politicians display willful ignorance when confronted with scientific evidence.
The company's willful ignorance of safety standards endangered its workers.
