Industrial Revolution

noun
period of major industrialization and societal change
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌdʌstriəl ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdʌstri.əl ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the period of major industrialization that transformed predominantly agrarian societies into industrial ones, beginning in Britain in the late 18th century and spreading globally

Examples

the Industrial Revolution in Britain
Many historians debate the causes and effects of the Industrial Revolution in Europe.
The Industrial Revolution dramatically increased productivity and changed the lives of millions of people worldwide.
The steam engine became one of the most important inventions of the Industrial Revolution.
