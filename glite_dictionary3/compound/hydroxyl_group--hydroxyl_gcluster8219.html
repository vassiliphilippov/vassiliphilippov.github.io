hydroxyl group

noun
the -OH chemical group in compounds
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑksəl ɡruːp / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
A chemical group consisting of one oxygen and one hydrogen atom (-OH), present in alcohols and some acids and bases.

Examples

hydroxyl group in alcohol
Many organic compounds owe their solubility in water to the presence of the hydroxyl group.
Replacing the hydroxyl group with a different functional group changes the substance's properties.
The alcohol contained a hydroxyl group attached to its carbon atom.
