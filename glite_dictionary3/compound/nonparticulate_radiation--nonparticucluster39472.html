nonparticulate radiation

noun
energy radiation not involving particles
Pronunciation: / nɑnˌpɑrtɪˈkjuːlət ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɒnˌpɑːtɪˈkjuːlət ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
radiation consisting of energy waves, such as electromagnetic waves, rather than particles; commonly associated with electric and magnetic fields produced by accelerating electric charges.

Examples

nonparticulate radiation like ultraviolet
Ultraviolet light is a form of nonparticulate radiation that can harm living tissues.
Unlike alpha particles, nonparticulate radiation does not consist of matter but of pure energy.
X-rays and gamma rays are types of nonparticulate radiation used in medical imaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electromagnetic radiation: radiation consisting of electric and magnetic waves, such as light, radio, X-rays
  • antonym – particulate radiation: radiation consisting of particles, such as alpha or beta particles
