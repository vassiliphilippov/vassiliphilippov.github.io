universal metre

noun
historical unit of length based on pendulum
Pronunciation: / juːˈnɪvərsəl ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəsəl ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a former unit of length equal to the length of a free seconds pendulum, introduced by Tito Livio Burattini in 1675; differs from the modern metre by about half a centimetre

Examples

universal metre standard length
Before the adoption of the metric system, the universal metre was suggested as a standard measure.
The length of a universal metre was based on the swing of a pendulum.
Tito Livio Burattini proposed the universal metre in the 17th century as a universal unit of length.
