to choose violence

verb
opt for aggression or confrontation
Pronunciation: / tʃuz ˈvaɪə.ləns / (AmE), / tʃuːz ˈvaɪə.ləns / (BrE)
to decide to respond with aggressive or confrontational actions rather than peaceful or conciliatory ones

Examples

to choose violence in an argument
In the heated debate, she finally chose violence and confronted her opponent directly.
Rather than ignore the insult, he chose violence and started arguing loudly.
Tired of being pushed around, they chose violence and stood up to their rivals.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resort to violence: to use physical force as a means of conflict
  • antonym – turn the other cheek: to respond to aggression without retaliating
