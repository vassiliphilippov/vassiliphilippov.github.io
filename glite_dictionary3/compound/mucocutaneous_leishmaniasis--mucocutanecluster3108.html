mucocutaneous leishmaniasis

noun
leishmaniasis affecting skin and mucous membranes
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːkoʊkjuːˈteɪniəs ˌliːʃməˈnaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkəʊkjuːˈteɪniəs ˌliːʃməˈnaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A form of leishmaniasis prevalent in Mexico and Central and South America, characterized by sores that are limited to the skin and mucous membranes.

Examples

mucocutaneous leishmaniasis affects skin
Cases of mucocutaneous leishmaniasis can cause severe disfigurement if left untreated.
The patient was diagnosed with mucocutaneous leishmaniasis after returning from a trip to Central America.
Treatment for mucocutaneous leishmaniasis often requires prolonged medical therapy.
