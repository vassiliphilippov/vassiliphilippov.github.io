conservation of mass

noun
principle that mass is neither created nor destroyed
Pronunciation: / kənˌsɜrvəˈʃən əv mæs / (AmE), / kɒnˌsɜːvəˈveɪʃən əv mæs / (BrE)
the principle that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system; the total mass remains constant over time
