superficial temporal vein

noun
vein in the side of the head
Pronunciation: / səˌpɝː.fɪ.ʃəl ˈtɛm.pɚ.əl veɪn / (AmE), / sjuːˌpɪ.fɪ.ʃəl ˈtɛmp.ər.əl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein running along the side of the head that drains blood from the scalp and empties into the retromandibular vein

Examples

superficial temporal vein in the scalp
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the superficial temporal vein.
Inflammation in the area may affect the superficial temporal vein and cause localized swelling.
The superficial temporal vein can be seen running just above the ear during certain medical examinations.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – retromandibular vein: a vein formed by the union of superficial temporal and maxillary veins
