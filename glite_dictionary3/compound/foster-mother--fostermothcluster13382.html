foster-mother

noun
a woman who raises another's child
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑstər ˌmʌðər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˌmʌðə / (BrE)
A woman who raises and cares for a child who is not her biological offspring, usually by arrangement with social services or legal authorities.

Examples

caring foster-mother
After her parents died, Julia was cared for by a loving foster-mother.
The child's foster-mother attended every school event and provided a supportive home environment.
The court appointed a foster-mother to look after the young boy until he could be reunited with his family.

Related Terms

  • antonym – biological mother: the woman who gave birth to a child
  • synonym – foster-father: a man who raises another's child
