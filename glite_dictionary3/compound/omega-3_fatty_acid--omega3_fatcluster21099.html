omega-3 fatty acid

noun
a type of polyunsaturated fatty acid essential in diet
Pronunciation: / oʊˈmeɪɡə θri ˈfæti ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / əʊˈmiːɡə θriː ˈfæti ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a polyunsaturated fatty acid in which the first double bond in the carbon chain is located three atoms from the methyl end of the molecule, important in human nutrition

Examples

omega-3 fatty acid supplement
Doctors recommend including foods high in omega-3 fatty acids for heart health.
Fish like salmon are a rich source of omega-3 fatty acid.
Some supplements provide a concentrated form of omega-3 fatty acid extracted from marine oils.

  • degree higher – omega-6 fatty acid: a polyunsaturated fatty acid with the first double bond at the sixth carbon
  • antonym – saturated fatty acid: a fatty acid without double bonds between carbon atoms
