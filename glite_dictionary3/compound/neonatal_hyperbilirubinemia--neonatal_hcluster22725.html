neonatal hyperbilirubinemia

noun
high bilirubin in newborns
Pronunciation: / niːoʊˈneɪtəl haɪpərˌbɪlɪruːbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / niːəʊˈneɪtəl haɪpəbɪlɪruːbɪˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A common disorder in newborn infants characterized by high levels of bilirubin in the blood, often caused by an immature liver, and usually resolving on its own.

Examples

neonatal hyperbilirubinemia in newborns
Most cases of neonatal hyperbilirubinemia resolve on their own without the need for medical intervention.
The pediatrician explained that neonatal hyperbilirubinemia is quite common and usually harmless in full-term infants.
Treatment for neonatal hyperbilirubinemia may include phototherapy to lower the baby's bilirubin levels.
