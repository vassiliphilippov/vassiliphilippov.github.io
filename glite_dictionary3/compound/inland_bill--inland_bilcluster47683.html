inland bill

noun
bill of exchange payable in the same country
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌlænd bɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪnlənd bɪl / (BrE)
A bill of exchange drawn and payable within the same country.

Examples

inland bill of exchange
An inland bill must be drawn and paid within the borders of the same country.
Banks often handle inland bills differently from foreign bills due to the lower risk.
The company paid the supplier using an inland bill to simplify the transaction.
