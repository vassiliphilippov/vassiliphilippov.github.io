roentgen ray

noun
type of electromagnetic radiation (X-ray)
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛntɡən ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛntɡən ˌreɪ / (BrE)
a form of electromagnetic radiation with very short wavelength, produced when high-speed electrons strike a solid target, commonly used in medical imaging
