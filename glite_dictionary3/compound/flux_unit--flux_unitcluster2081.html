flux unit

noun
unit of magnetic field strength
Pronunciation: / flʌks ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / flʌks ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit for measuring the strength of a magnetic field per unit area

Examples

flux unit of magnetism
One flux unit can represent the magnetic field strength passing through a specific area.
Scientists measure the intensity of magnetic fields using the flux unit as a standard.
The magnetic flux unit in the CGS system is called the gauss.
