Aswan High Dam

proper noun
major dam on the Nile River in Egypt
Pronunciation: / æzˈwɑn haɪ dæm / (AmE), / æzˈwæn haɪ dæm / (BrE)
a large embankment dam on the Nile River in southern Egypt, notable as one of the world's largest dams; built to control flooding, provide water storage, and generate hydroelectric power

Examples

the Aswan High Dam on the Nile
The Aswan High Dam was completed in 1970 and transformed agriculture along the Nile.
The construction of the Aswan High Dam created Lake Nasser, one of the world's largest artificial lakes.
Tourists visiting Egypt often take guided tours of the impressive Aswan High Dam.
