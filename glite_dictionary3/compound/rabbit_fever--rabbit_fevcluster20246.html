rabbit fever

noun
infectious disease transmitted from rabbits and rodents
Pronunciation: / ˈræb.ɪt ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈræb.ɪt ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
A highly infectious disease of rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, which can be transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or by handling infected animals.

Examples

rabbit fever from wild animals
Rabbit fever can cause serious illness in people if left untreated.
Several hunters contracted rabbit fever after handling wild hares last season.
Ticks are known to transmit rabbit fever to both animals and humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tularemia: A bacterial disease affecting animals and humans, also called rabbit fever.
