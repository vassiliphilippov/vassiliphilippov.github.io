essential amino acid

noun
amino acid required in diet, not synthesized by body
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɛnʃəl əˈminoʊ ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃl əˈmiːnəʊ ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an amino acid that is required for normal body functioning but cannot be synthesized by the organism and must be obtained from the diet

Examples

an essential amino acid in food
A deficiency in any essential amino acid can lead to health problems.
Protein sources like meat and eggs contain all essential amino acids needed for human health.
Vegetarians must plan their diets carefully to ensure they get enough essential amino acids.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonessential amino acid: an amino acid that can be synthesized by the body
