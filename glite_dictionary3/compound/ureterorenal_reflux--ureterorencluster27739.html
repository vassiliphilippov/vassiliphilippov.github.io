ureterorenal reflux

noun
backflow of urine from ureter to kidney
Pronunciation: / jʊˌriːt̬əroʊˈriːnəl ˈriːflʌks / (AmE), / jʊəˌriːtərəʊˈriːnəl ˈriːflʌks / (BrE)
the backflow of urine from the ureter into the renal pelvis, which can lead to kidney dysfunction or infection

Examples

ureterorenal reflux damages kidneys
Chronic ureterorenal reflux can cause scarring of the kidney tissue over time.
Doctors detected ureterorenal reflux during tests for recurrent kidney infections.
Treatment for ureterorenal reflux often involves surgery to prevent further urinary backflow.
