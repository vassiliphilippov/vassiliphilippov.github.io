cyma recta

noun
architectural molding with concave upper and convex lower
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪmə ˈrɛktə / (AmE), / ˌsɪmə ˈrɛktə / (BrE)
A type of molding or architectural ornament in which the upper section is concave and the lower section is convex.

Examples

cyma recta molding profile
Architects often choose between cyma recta and cyma reversa profiles for decorative edging.
The cyma recta provided a graceful transition between the wall and the roof.
The classical temple featured an elaborate cornice decorated with a cyma recta molding.
