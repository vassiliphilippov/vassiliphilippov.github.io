field-emission microscope

noun
electron microscope for surface analysis
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ɪˈmɪʃən ˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɪˈmɪʃən ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
An electron microscope that uses field emission to observe the surface structure of a solid at high resolution.

Examples

A field-emission microscope can reveal atomic arrangements on a metal surface.
