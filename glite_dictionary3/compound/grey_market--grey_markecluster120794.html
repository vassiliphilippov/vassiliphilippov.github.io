grey market

noun
unofficial market for goods at unofficial prices
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
An unofficial market in which goods are bought and sold, often at lower prices than those set by manufacturers or regulatory agencies, but without breaking the law.

Examples

buy from the grey market
Car dealers sometimes obtain vehicles from the grey market to meet demand.
Many luxury watches are sold through the grey market at significant discounts.
The regulations aim to prevent pharmaceuticals from entering the grey market.

Related Terms

  • antonym – black market: illegal market for goods or services
