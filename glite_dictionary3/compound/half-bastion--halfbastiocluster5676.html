half-bastion

noun
bastion with one face and one flank
Pronunciation: / ˈhæf ˌbæstʃən / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌbæstɪən / (BrE)
In military architecture, a bastion constructed of one face and one flank instead of two.

Examples

fortress half-bastion
Engineers designed a half-bastion to fit the irregular layout of the terrain.
Historical diagrams revealed the presence of a half-bastion at the fortress’s main entrance.
The fort's half-bastion provided some defense on the weaker side of the wall.
