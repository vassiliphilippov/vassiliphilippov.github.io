face-to-face

adjective
in each other's presence; directly meeting
Pronunciation: / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
(of people) in each other's physical presence; meeting or interacting with someone directly rather than remotely or indirectly.

Examples

face-to-face meeting
She prefers face-to-face discussions to written communication.
The interview was conducted face-to-face rather than over the phone.
We finally had a face-to-face meeting after months of video calls.

Related Terms

  • synonym – in-person: taking place with people physically present
  • antonym – remote: taking place at a distance, not physically present
