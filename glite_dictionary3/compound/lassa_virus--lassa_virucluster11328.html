Lassa virus

noun
virus causing Lassa fever
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːsə ˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈlæsə ˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a single-stranded RNA virus that causes Lassa fever, an acute viral disease found in West Africa

Examples

Lassa virus infection
Outbreaks of Lassa virus have been reported in several West African countries.
Researchers are studying new vaccines to prevent infections caused by the Lassa virus.
The Lassa virus is primarily transmitted to humans through contact with food or household items contaminated by rodents.
