four-footed butterfly

noun
butterfly with reduced forelegs
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔːr ˈfʊtɪd ˈbʌtərˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˌfɔː ˈfʊtɪd ˈbʌtəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A butterfly with brightly colored wings whose front pair of legs are greatly reduced and held folded against the body, making it appear to have only four functional legs.

Examples

colorful four-footed butterfly
Researchers studied the feeding habits of several four-footed butterflies in tropical forests.
The four-footed butterfly is characterized by its reduced forelegs, which are not used for walking.
Unlike most butterflies, the four-footed butterfly carries its front legs folded against its chest.
