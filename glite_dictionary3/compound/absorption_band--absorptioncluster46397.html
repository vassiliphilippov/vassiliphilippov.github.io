absorption band

noun
dark band in a spectrum due to selective absorption
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔɹpʃən bænd / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən band / (BrE)
A dark band in the spectrum of white light that appears after the light has passed through a substance which absorbs at certain wavelengths.

Examples

absorption band in spectrum
A strong absorption band at 600 nm indicated the presence of copper ions.
Infrared spectroscopy reveals each molecule's unique absorption band pattern.
The chemist analyzed the absorption band to identify the compound present in the solution.
