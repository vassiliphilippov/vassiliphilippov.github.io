anthrax pneumonia

noun
severe pneumonia from inhaled anthrax spores
Pronunciation: / ˈænˌθræks pnuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈænˌθræks njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A severe form of pneumonia caused by inhaling Bacillus anthracis spores, leading to acute respiratory symptoms and often rapid cardiovascular collapse.

Examples

severe anthrax pneumonia case
Early detection of anthrax pneumonia is critical due to its rapid and often fatal progression.
She was admitted to the hospital with severe symptoms of anthrax pneumonia following exposure to animal hides.
The outbreak was traced to factory workers who developed anthrax pneumonia after inhaling contaminated dust.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inhalational anthrax: anthrax infection acquired by inhaling spores
  • synonym – pneumonia: lung infection causing inflammation and fluid in the lungs
