end-to-end

adjective
in a line, ends touching
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnd tə ˈɛnd / (AmE), / ˌɛnd tʊ ˈɛnd / (BrE)
with one object placed so that its end is in contact lengthwise with the end of another object; in a continuous line with no overlap or gap

Examples

end-to-end connection
She connected the train cars end-to-end until none were left.
The desks were arranged end-to-end along the center of the room.
The pipes were laid end-to-end to form a continuous stretch.

Related Terms

  • antonym – side-by-side: with objects placed so their sides are touching, not their ends
