gumbo soil

noun
heavy, sticky silty soil type
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌm.boʊ sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌm.bəʊ sɔɪl / (BrE)
A type of fine-grained, silty soil that becomes extremely sticky and heavy when wet, making it difficult to work with.

Examples

wet gumbo soil
After the rain, the gumbo soil turned into a slippery, sticky mess that was hard to walk through.
Farmers often struggle to plant crops on gumbo soil because it holds water and becomes very heavy.
The construction crew complained that the gumbo soil kept clogging their machinery after a storm.
