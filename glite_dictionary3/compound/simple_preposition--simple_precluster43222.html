simple preposition

noun
single-word preposition
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəl ˌprɛpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl ˌprɛpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A preposition that consists of a single word, rather than two or more words combined.

Examples

simple preposition in grammar
English students often confuse simple prepositions with compound ones when learning grammar.
The word 'in' is a simple preposition in the phrase 'in the house'.
Words like 'by', 'at', and 'from' are all examples of simple prepositions.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compound preposition: A preposition consisting of two or more words, often derived from a noun or adjective with a prepositional suffix.
