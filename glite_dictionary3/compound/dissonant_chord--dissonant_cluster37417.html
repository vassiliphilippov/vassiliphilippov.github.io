dissonant chord

noun
music: chord producing tension or discord
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsənənt kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɪsənənt kɔːd / (BrE)
a group of musical notes played together that create a sense of tension or lack of harmony

Examples

play a dissonant chord
Modern music often features a dissonant chord to challenge traditional harmonies.
She winced as the pianist struck a particularly harsh dissonant chord.
The composer used a dissonant chord to build suspense in the symphony.
