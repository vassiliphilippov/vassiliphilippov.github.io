point of apoapsis

noun
farthest orbital point from primary body
Pronunciation: / pɔɪnt əv ˌæpoʊˈæpsɪs / (AmE), / pɔɪnt əv ˌapəʊˈapsɪs / (BrE)
(astronomy) The point in an object's orbit where it is farthest from the body it is orbiting.

Examples

point of apoapsis in orbit
At the point of apoapsis, the spacecraft is moving at its slowest speed along its orbit.
Calculations must account for the distance at the point of apoapsis to ensure a stable orbit.
The satellite reached its point of apoapsis before beginning its descent toward Earth again.

Related Terms

  • antonym – periapsis: the point in an orbit closest to the body being orbited
