bottom of the ninth

noun
final opportunity in baseball or metaphorically
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːt̬əm əv ðə naɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ɒv ðə naɪnθ / (BrE)
(in baseball) the second half of the ninth and final inning, regarded as the last opportunity to win or change the outcome of the game; also used metaphorically for a final chance.

Examples

in the bottom of the ninth inning
She delivered her presentation in the bottom of the ninth, hoping for a last-minute victory.
They knew it was the bottom of the ninth, but they still believed a comeback was possible.
With only one run needed in the bottom of the ninth, the crowd held its breath.
