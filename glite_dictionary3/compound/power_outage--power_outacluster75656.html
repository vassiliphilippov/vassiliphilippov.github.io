power outage

noun
interruption of electricity supply
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌaʊ.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌaʊ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
A period of time when the supply of electricity is interrupted, usually due to equipment failure or external events.

Examples

power outage during storm
Frequent power outages have affected businesses in the area.
The recent storm caused a power outage that lasted several hours in our neighborhood.
We had to use candles during the power outage last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blackout: a period when all lights or power are turned off or lost
