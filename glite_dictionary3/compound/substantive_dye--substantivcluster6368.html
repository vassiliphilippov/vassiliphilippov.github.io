substantive dye

noun
dye with high affinity for cellulose fibers
Pronunciation: / səbˈstæn.tɪv daɪ / (AmE), / səbˈstæn.tɪv daɪ / (BrE)
a dye that has a strong natural affinity for cellulose fibers, such as cotton or rayon, allowing it to attach without a mordant

Examples

substantive dye for cotton
Cotton fabrics are often colored with substantive dye because of its strong attraction to cellulose.
The textile industry values substantive dye for its simplicity of application to rayon.
Unlike acid dyes, a substantive dye can dye cotton directly without needing a mordant.

Related Terms

  • synonym – direct dye: dye that can be applied directly to cellulose fibers without a mordant
