to stifle innovation

verb
to hinder creative ideas or progress
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪfəl ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈstaɪfəl ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
to suppress or prevent the development of new and creative ideas or methods

Examples

to stifle innovation in business
Excessive regulations sometimes stifle innovation instead of protecting consumers.
Fear of failure may stifle innovation among talented employees.
Rigid corporate policies can stifle innovation in rapidly changing industries.
