Black Panthers

proper noun
militant Black political organization (1960s-70s US)
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈpænθɚz / (AmE), / blæk ˈpænθəz / (BrE)
A militant African-American political organization founded in 1966 in the United States to challenge police brutality and promote Black rights.

Examples

the Black Panthers movement
Many regarded the Black Panthers as both a civil rights group and a symbol of Black empowerment.
The Black Panthers organized community programs such as free breakfasts for children in Oakland.
The actions of the Black Panthers sparked debates about race relations in the United States during the 1960s.
