soil pipe

noun
pipe for carrying waste from toilets
Pronunciation: / sɔɪl paɪp / (AmE), / sɔɪl paɪp / (BrE)
A pipe that carries liquid waste away from toilets and other sanitary fixtures to a drainage system.

Examples

soil pipe installation
Building codes now require that a soil pipe be properly vented to avoid blockages.
He traced the unpleasant smell back to a crack in the house's main soil pipe.
The plumber replaced the old soil pipe to prevent leaks and odors in the bathroom.
