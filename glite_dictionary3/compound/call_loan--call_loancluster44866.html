call loan

noun
loan repayable on demand
Pronunciation: / kɔl loʊn / (AmE), / kɔːl ləʊn / (BrE)
a loan that must be repaid whenever the lender demands, rather than on a fixed schedule

Examples

call loan repayment demanded
A call loan typically carries a lower interest rate because of its flexible repayment terms.
During financial uncertainty, many investors worry about their call loans being recalled unexpectedly.
The bank issued a call loan to the brokerage, which could be recalled at any time.
