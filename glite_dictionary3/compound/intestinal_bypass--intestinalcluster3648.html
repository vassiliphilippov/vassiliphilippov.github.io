intestinal bypass

noun
surgical procedure reducing small intestine absorption
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈbaɪpæs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛstɪnəl ˈbaɪpɑːs / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which part of the small intestine is bypassed to reduce nutrient absorption, primarily used to treat obesity

Examples

intestinal bypass surgery
After undergoing an intestinal bypass, she noticed significant weight loss within a few months.
Complications from an intestinal bypass can include nutritional deficiencies and digestive issues.
The surgeon recommended an intestinal bypass to help manage the patient's obesity.
