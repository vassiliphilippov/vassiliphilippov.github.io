southern cypress

noun
wood from a southeastern U.S. cypress tree
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌð.ɚn ˈsaɪ.prəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌð.ən ˈsaɪ.prəs / (BrE)
the wood of a cypress tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its expanded trunk base and found in coastal swamps or river bottoms

Examples

southern cypress wood furniture
Furniture made of southern cypress can withstand high humidity and is popular in the South.
The deck was constructed from southern cypress because of its durability in wet climates.
They harvested southern cypress from the swamps to use in boat building.
