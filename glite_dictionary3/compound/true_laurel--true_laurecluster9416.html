true laurel

noun
Mediterranean evergreen tree, used in cooking and crown-making
Pronunciation: / tru ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / truː ˈlɔːrəl / (BrE)
A small Mediterranean evergreen tree (Laurus nobilis) with glossy, aromatic leaves used for flavoring in cooking, and whose branches were used by the ancient Greeks to crown victors.

Examples

true laurel leaves in cooking
Fresh true laurel leaves are often used to flavor soups and stews in Mediterranean cuisine.
In ancient Greece, athletes were crowned with a wreath made from true laurel branches.
The garden boasts a mature true laurel tree, prized for both its beauty and culinary uses.
