outboard motor

noun
detachable boat engine
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtbɔrd ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbɔːd ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A detachable engine mounted on the outside at the stern of a small boat, used for propulsion and steering.

Examples

outboard motor on a boat
He lifted the outboard motor out of the water after docking the boat.
Many fishermen prefer boats powered by an outboard motor for easy maneuvering.
We replaced the old engine with a new outboard motor for better performance.

  • antonym – inboard motor: engine mounted inside a boat's hull
