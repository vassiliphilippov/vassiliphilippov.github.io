pair formation

noun
creation of an electron-positron pair from a photon
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛr fɔrˌmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpeə fəːˌmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a gamma-ray photon is converted into an electron and a positron when passing near an atomic nucleus

Examples

pair formation by gamma rays
Researchers observed pair formation as part of their experiments with gamma radiation.
The phenomenon of pair formation provides evidence for the existence of antimatter.
When a high-energy photon passes near a nucleus, pair formation can occur, resulting in an electron and a positron.
