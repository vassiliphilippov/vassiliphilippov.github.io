small calorie

noun
unit of heat (1g water, 1°C)
Pronunciation: / smɔl ˈkæləri / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈkæləri / (BrE)
a unit of heat equal to the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1 degree Celsius at atmospheric pressure

Examples

one small calorie of heat
Chemistry textbooks sometimes refer to the small calorie instead of the kilocalorie.
Scientists measured the amount of heat released in small calories.
The energy needed to raise 1 gram of water by 1°C is defined as one small calorie.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – kilocalorie: a unit of energy equal to 1,000 small calories, often used in food energy measurements
