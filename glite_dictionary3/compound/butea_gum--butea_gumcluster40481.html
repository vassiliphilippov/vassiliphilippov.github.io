butea gum

noun
resin from the dhak tree, used as an astringent
Pronunciation: / buˈtiːə ɡʌm / (AmE), / bjuːˈtiːə ɡʌm / (BrE)
A resinous substance obtained from the dried juice of the dhak tree (Butea monosperma), used as an astringent.

Examples

butea gum extract
Extraction of butea gum from the dhak tree has been practiced for centuries in South Asia.
The resin known as butea gum is prized for its astringent properties in herbal medicine.
Traditional healers often use butea gum to treat minor wounds and ulcers.
