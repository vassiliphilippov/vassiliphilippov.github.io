methyl phenol

noun
a type of poisonous phenolic compound
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːθəl ˈfiːˌnoʊl / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəl ˈfiːnɒl / (BrE)
Any of three poisonous, colorless isomeric phenols derived from coal or wood tar, used especially as a disinfectant.

Examples

methyl phenol disinfectant use
Exposure to methyl phenol can cause irritation of the skin and respiratory tract.
Methylated spirits sometimes contain small amounts of methyl phenol as a disinfectant.
The laboratory stored solutions of methyl phenol in tightly sealed containers due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cresol: an alternative name for methyl phenol isomers, used especially in chemistry and medicine
