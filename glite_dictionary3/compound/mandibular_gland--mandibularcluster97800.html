mandibular gland

noun
salivary gland inside the lower jaw
Pronunciation: / mænˈdɪbjələr ɡlænd / (AmE), / mænˈdɪbjʊlə ɡlænd / (BrE)
a salivary gland located inside the lower jaw that produces saliva, especially during the night, and releases it under the tongue

Examples

mandibular gland produces saliva
Saliva from the mandibular gland helps keep the mouth moist while sleeping.
Swelling in the mandibular gland can indicate an infection or blockage.
The mandibular gland produces more saliva at night than during the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – salivary gland: any of the glands in the mouth that secrete saliva
