kinetic theory of heat

noun
theory linking heat to kinetic energy of particles
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛtɪk ˈθɪəri ʌv hit / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈθɪəri ɒv hiːt / (BrE)
the scientific theory proposing that the temperature of a body rises when the kinetic energy of its particles increases

Examples

kinetic theory of heat explains temperature
According to the kinetic theory of heat, heating a substance increases the speed of its particles.
Many physics teachers use simple experiments to demonstrate the kinetic theory of heat.
The kinetic theory of heat explains how molecules in motion cause different temperatures.
