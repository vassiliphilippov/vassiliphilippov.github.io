ignition coil

noun
device converting battery current for spark plugs
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈnɪʃən kɔɪl / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɪʃən kɔɪl / (BrE)
A device in an internal combustion engine that transforms low-voltage current from the battery into the high-voltage current needed to fire the spark plugs.

Examples

ignition coil in a car engine
Modern cars usually have one ignition coil per cylinder for greater efficiency.
The mechanic checked the ignition coil to diagnose the starting problem.
When the ignition coil fails, the engine may misfire and lose power.
