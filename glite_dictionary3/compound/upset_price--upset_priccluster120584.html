upset price

noun
auction minimum sale price
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsɛt praɪs / (AmE), / ˈʌpsɛt praɪs / (BrE)
the minimum price at which a seller will accept bids at an auction

Examples

upset price at auction
Bidders were hesitant because the upset price was set quite high.
If the upset price is not met, the property may remain unsold.
The auctioneer announced the upset price before accepting any bids.
