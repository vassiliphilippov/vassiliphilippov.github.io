fat-soluble vitamin

noun
a vitamin soluble in fats or oils
Pronunciation: / fæt ˈsɑl.jə.bəl ˈvaɪ.tə.mɪn / (AmE), / fæt ˈsɒl.juː.bəl ˈvɪ.tə.mɪn / (BrE)
a vitamin that dissolves in fats or oils and can be stored in the body's fatty tissue

Examples

fat-soluble vitamin absorption
A balanced diet should include sources of fat-soluble vitamins to maintain good health.
Taking too many fat-soluble vitamins can lead to their buildup in the body.
Vitamins A, D, E, and K are considered fat-soluble vitamins because they dissolve in fats.

Related Terms

  • antonym – water-soluble vitamin: vitamin that dissolves in water, not stored in body fat
