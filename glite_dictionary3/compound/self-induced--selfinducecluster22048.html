self-induced

adjective
caused or brought about by oneself
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ɪnˈdust / (AmE), / sɛlf ɪnˈdjuːst / (BrE)
(of a condition or action) caused or brought about by oneself, especially intentionally.

Examples

self-induced vomiting
He was hospitalized after experiencing self-induced vomiting.
Many cases of self-induced illness are related to stress and lifestyle choices.
The author describes a period of self-induced isolation to focus on writing.
