to blanket jam

verb
block wide-range radio signals
Pronunciation: / ˈblæŋkɪt dʒæm / (AmE), / ˈblaŋkɪt dʒam / (BrE)
to jam a wide range of radio frequencies simultaneously, interfering with all communications in an area except those using directional antennas

Examples

to blanket jam enemy signals
Military forces may blanket jam enemy communications during an operation to reduce coordination.
The device is capable of blanket jamming all civilian radio frequencies within a mile radius.
To prevent interference with their own transmissions, they avoided blanket jamming the area.
