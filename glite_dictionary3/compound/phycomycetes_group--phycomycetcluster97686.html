Phycomycetes group

noun
old fungal group; outdated taxonomy
Pronunciation: / faɪ.kəˈmaɪ.siːts ɡruːp / (AmE), / fʌɪ.kəˈmiː.siːts ɡruːp / (BrE)
a historical classification of fungi and funguslike organisms that included Mastigomycota (such as Oomycetes) and Zygomycota, no longer widely used in modern taxonomy

Examples

the Phycomycetes group classification
Older textbooks reference the Phycomycetes group, although this classification is rarely used today.
Recent studies have shown that the Phycomycetes group is polyphyletic and obsolete in modern taxonomy.
The Phycomycetes group once included many water molds now classified elsewhere.

  • synonym – Zygomycota: a division of fungi formerly included in the Phycomycetes
