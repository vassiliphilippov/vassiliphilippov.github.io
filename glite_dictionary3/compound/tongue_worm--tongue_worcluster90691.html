tongue worm

noun
parasitic arthropod in mammals' nasal passages
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌŋ ˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ ˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A wormlike parasitic arthropod with two pairs of hooks at the sides of the mouth, typically found in the nasal sinuses of mammals.

Examples

tongue worm infection
Farmers are advised to check livestock for signs of tongue worm infestation.
Researchers studied how the tongue worm attaches itself using its hooks.
The veterinarian diagnosed the dog with a tongue worm infection in its nasal cavity.
