to calcine limestone

verb
heat limestone to produce lime
Pronunciation: / kælˈsaɪn ˈlaɪmˌstoʊn / (AmE), / kælˈsaɪn ˈlaɪm.stəʊn / (BrE)
to heat limestone at a high temperature to produce lime by removing carbon dioxide

Examples

to calcine limestone in a kiln
In chemistry class, we learned how to calcine limestone to obtain quicklime.
Industries often calcine limestone as a crucial step in producing materials for construction.
To make cement, manufacturers first calcine limestone in large rotary kilns.

Related Terms

  • synonym – calcine: to heat a substance to a high temperature but below its melting point to effect thermal decomposition
