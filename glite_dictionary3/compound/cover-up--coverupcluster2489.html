cover-up

noun
concealment of a scandal or crime
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvərˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkʌvərəp / (BrE)
an attempt to prevent the public from discovering information about a scandal or crime

Examples

cover-up of a scandal
Journalists exposed the cover-up within the corporation after months of investigation.
She resigned after allegations surfaced about her involvement in the cover-up.
The government was accused of orchestrating a massive cover-up to hide the truth from the public.
