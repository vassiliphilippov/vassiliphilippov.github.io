social liberalism

noun
political ideology balancing liberty with social justice
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ˈlɪbərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈlɪbərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political ideology that advocates individual liberty within the framework of social justice, supporting both civil rights and government action to address economic and social issues

Examples

social liberalism in politics
Debates on healthcare often highlight the differences between social liberalism and more conservative policies.
His views on economic reform are influenced by the principles of social liberalism.
Many European countries have adopted social liberalism as a guiding philosophy for their welfare states.
