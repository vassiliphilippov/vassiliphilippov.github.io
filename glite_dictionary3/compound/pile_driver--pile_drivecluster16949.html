pile driver

noun
machine for driving piles into the ground
Pronunciation: / paɪl ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / paɪl ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
a heavy machine used for driving piles into the ground, especially in the construction of foundations for buildings and other structures

Examples

pile driver machine
A modern hydraulic pile driver can set hundreds of piles in a single day.
The construction crew used a pile driver to install the building’s deep foundation.
You could hear the rhythmic thudding of the pile driver from across the river.
