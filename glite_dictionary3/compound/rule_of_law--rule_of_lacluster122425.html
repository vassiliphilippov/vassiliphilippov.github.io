rule of law

noun
principle that law governs a state
Pronunciation: / ɹuːl əv lɔ / (AmE), / ɹuːl əv lɔː / (BrE)
the principle that all individuals and institutions are subject to and accountable to law that is fairly applied and enforced; the administration of law in a state.

Examples

strong rule of law
A strong rule of law is essential for a stable and just society.
Corruption often flourishes where the rule of law is weak or absent.
International organizations promote the rule of law to help ensure human rights and fair governance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anarchy: absence of government or laws
  • synonym – law and order: the condition of a society in which laws are obeyed, and people behave safely and peacefully
