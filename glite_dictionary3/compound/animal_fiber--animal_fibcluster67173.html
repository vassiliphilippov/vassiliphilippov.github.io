animal fiber

noun
fiber derived from animals
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪməl ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A natural fiber obtained from animals, such as wool, silk, or cashmere, used especially in textiles.

Examples

animal fiber sweater
Cashmere sweaters are prized for being soft, lightweight, and made from a luxurious animal fiber.
Unlike synthetic fabrics, animal fiber breathes well and naturally regulates temperature.
Wool is a type of animal fiber that is often used to make warm clothing.

  • antonym – plant fiber: fiber obtained from plants, such as cotton or flax
