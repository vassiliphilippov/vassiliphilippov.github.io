costal cartilage

noun
cartilage connecting ribs to sternum
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑstl ˈkɑrtɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒstl ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the cartilages that connect the ribs to the sternum, allowing for flexibility and movement of the chest during breathing.

Examples

costal cartilage injury
An injury to the costal cartilage can cause persistent chest pain and discomfort.
In children, the costal cartilage is more pliable than in adults, making the chest wall softer.
The flexibility of the costal cartilage is essential for normal breathing movements.
