life tenant

noun
person with right to property for life
Pronunciation: / laɪf ˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / laɪf ˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
A person who has the legal right to occupy or use land or buildings for the duration of their own life or the life of another specified person.

Examples

life tenant holds property
The life tenant is responsible for maintaining the property during her lifetime.
The rights of a life tenant differ significantly from those of a full property owner.
Upon the death of the life tenant, the ownership of the estate passes to the named heirs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freeholder: person owning property outright, not limited to a lifetime
  • antonym – remainder man: person entitled to inherit property after the life tenant's interest ends
