Benford's law

noun
statistical law about digit frequency in data
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛn.fɚdz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.fədz lɔː / (BrE)
a statistical principle stating that in many naturally occurring sets of numbers, the leading digit is more likely to be small, with 1 appearing as the first digit more frequently than larger digits; often used in forensic accounting and fraud detection

Examples

Benford's law in accounting
Benford's law is often used to detect fraudulent financial data by examining how often each leading digit appears.
Many datasets, such as electricity bills and addresses, naturally follow Benford's law.
The accountant applied Benford's law to the company's expense records to check for irregularities.
