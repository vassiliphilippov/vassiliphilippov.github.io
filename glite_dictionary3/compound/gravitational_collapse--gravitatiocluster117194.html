gravitational collapse

noun
collapse of a star due to gravity
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl kəˈlæps / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl kəˈlaps / (BrE)
the process in which a star or other celestial body collapses under its own gravity, leading to a smaller and denser object
