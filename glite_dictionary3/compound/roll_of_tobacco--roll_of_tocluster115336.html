roll of tobacco

noun
tobacco formed into a cylinder
Pronunciation: / roʊl əv təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / rəʊl əv təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
A quantity of tobacco leaves that have been wrapped or rolled into a cylindrical shape, typically for smoking or storage.

Examples

hand-rolled roll of tobacco
He carried a roll of tobacco in his pocket for hand-rolling cigarettes.
She offered him a roll of tobacco as a gesture of hospitality.
The farmer dried the leaves before forming them into a roll of tobacco.
