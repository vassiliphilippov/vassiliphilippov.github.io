squelch circuit

noun
electronic circuit for muting weak signals
Pronunciation: / skwɛltʃ ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electronic circuit that automatically mutes or disables a receiver when the received signal is weaker than the background noise

Examples

squelch circuit in radios
A well-designed squelch circuit is essential for clear communication in two-way radios.
Technicians tested the squelch circuit to ensure it cut off noise below a certain threshold.
The radio's squelch circuit prevents annoying static when no station is being received.
