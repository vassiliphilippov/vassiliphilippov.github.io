naive Bayes classifier

noun
simple probabilistic classification model
Pronunciation: / naɪˈiv beɪz kləˈsɪfaɪər / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv beɪz klɑːˈsɪfʌɪə / (BrE)
A simple probabilistic model for classification tasks, based on Bayes' theorem, which assumes that features are independent of each other.

Examples

train a naive Bayes classifier
Many introductory machine learning courses teach the naive Bayes classifier due to its simplicity and effectiveness.
The naive Bayes classifier performed surprisingly well on the spam detection task.
Using a naive Bayes classifier requires the assumption that the features are independent given the class label.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Bayes' theorem: mathematical theorem relating conditional probabilities
