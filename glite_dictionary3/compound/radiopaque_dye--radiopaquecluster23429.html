radiopaque dye

noun
dye visible on X-rays for imaging
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈpeɪk daɪ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈpeɪk daɪ / (BrE)
a dye that blocks X-rays or other radiation, used to outline organs during X-ray examinations

Examples

radiopaque dye in X-ray test
Accurate diagnosis often depends on the use of radiopaque dye during imaging procedures.
Before the scan, the patient was given a radiopaque dye to highlight her urinary tract.
The doctor injected a radiopaque dye to help visualize the blood vessels on the X-ray.
