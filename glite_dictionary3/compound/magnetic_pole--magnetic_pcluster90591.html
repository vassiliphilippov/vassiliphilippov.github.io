magnetic pole

noun
end of a magnet where force is strongest
Pronunciation: / məɡˈnɛtɪk poʊl / (AmE), / maɡˈnɛtɪk pəʊl / (BrE)
either of the two ends of a magnet at which the magnetic force is strongest.

Examples

bar magnet magnetic pole
Each bar magnet has a north and a south magnetic pole.
Iron filings align themselves along the lines between the two magnetic poles.
The compass always points towards the north magnetic pole.

Related Terms

  • synonym – magnet: an object that produces a magnetic field
  • synonym – magnetic field: the area around a magnet where magnetic force is felt
