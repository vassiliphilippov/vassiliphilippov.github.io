Congo gum

noun
a resin from African trees, used in varnish
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːŋɡoʊ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ ɡʌm / (BrE)
A hard, yellowish or brown resin, usually fossilized, obtained from trees in Africa and used in varnishes and adhesives.

Examples

Congo gum in varnish
Congo gum is valued for its use in making adhesives and fine polishes.
High-quality varnish is sometimes made using Congo gum because of its excellent hardening qualities.
The shipment included several crates of Congo gum for export to Europe.
