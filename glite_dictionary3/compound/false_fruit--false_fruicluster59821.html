false fruit

noun
fruit formed from tissues beyond the ovary
Pronunciation: / fɔls frut / (AmE), / fɔːls fruːt / (BrE)
A fruit composed of tissues other than the ovary, such as the apple or pear, where the fleshy part develops from surrounding flower tissues.

Examples

false fruit like apple
Pears and quinces are also considered false fruits in botanical terms.
The apple is a classic example of a false fruit because its edible part is derived mainly from the receptacle.
Unlike a berry, a false fruit includes tissues that are not part of the ovary.
