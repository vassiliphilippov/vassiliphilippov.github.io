time-ball

noun
ball dropped to signal exact time
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪm ˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm ˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a ball that slides down a pole or staff at a set time, especially at an observatory, to signal a precise time to observers

Examples

time-ball drops at noon
The time-ball was an important method of time signaling in the 19th century.
The observatory drops the time-ball at noon every day for ships in the harbor.
Tourists gathered to watch the time-ball descend atop the old clock tower.
