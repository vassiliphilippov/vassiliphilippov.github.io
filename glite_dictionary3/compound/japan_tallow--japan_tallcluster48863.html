Japan tallow

noun
yellow wax from sumac berries used in polishes
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæn ˈtæloʊ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpan ˈtaləʊ / (BrE)
a yellowish wax obtained from the berries of certain sumac trees, used in polishes and as a substitute for animal tallow

Examples

Japan tallow polish finish
The craftsman applied a thin layer of Japan tallow to the wood to give it a polished finish.
The formula for the floor polish listed Japan tallow among its main ingredients.
Traditional candle makers sometimes use Japan tallow as an alternative to beeswax.
