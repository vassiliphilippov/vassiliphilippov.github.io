kabuki dance

noun
elaborate, deceptive act or performance
Pronunciation: / kəˈbuːki dæns / (AmE), / kəˈbuːki dɑːns / (BrE)
a complex and elaborate performance or act, typically one intended to deceive or give a false impression of activity or progress

Examples

kabuki dance in politics
Critics described the negotiations as a political kabuki dance designed to delay real action.
The entire budget process became a kabuki dance with little chance of meaningful results.
Their public debate was just a kabuki dance, hiding what had already been agreed upon privately.

Related Terms

  • synonym – charade: an act or event that is obviously false, meant to create a deceptive appearance
  • synonym – pretense: an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • antonym – straightforwardness: the quality of being direct, honest, and clear; opposite of being elaborate or deceptive
