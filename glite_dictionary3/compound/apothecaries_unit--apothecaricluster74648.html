apothecaries' unit

noun
traditional pharmacy weight unit
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑθəkɛriz ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɒθəkərɪz ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A unit of weight formerly used in pharmacy, in which an ounce equals 480 grains and a pound equals 12 ounces.

Examples

apothecaries' unit of weight
Few modern pharmacies use the apothecaries' unit system, as metric units have become the standard.
Historical recipes for medicines often list ingredients in apothecaries' units such as grains and drams.
The doctor instructed the pharmacist to use the apothecaries' unit when preparing the prescription.
