lachrymal secretion

noun
fluid secreted by lacrimal glands (tears)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæk.rɪ.məl sɪˈkri.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈlæk.rɪ.məl sɪˈkriː.ʃən / (BrE)
the saline fluid secreted by the lacrimal glands, serving to lubricate and protect the surface of the eyeball

Examples

lachrymal secretion in the eye
A dysfunction in lachrymal secretion may lead to dry eye syndrome.
During strong emotions, lachrymal secretion increases, resulting in visible tears.
Excessive lachrymal secretion can cause watery eyes and blurred vision.
