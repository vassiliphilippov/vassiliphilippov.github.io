ground ice

noun
ice formed in soil or rock (permafrost)
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd aɪs / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd aɪs / (BrE)
ice that forms within soil or rock, typically in permafrost regions, as a result of the freezing of subsurface water.

Examples

ground ice in permafrost
Much of the visible damage to northern roads is a result of melting ground ice.
Scientists drilled into the tundra to study the ground ice beneath the surface.
Thawing ground ice can cause the land above to sink and create uneven terrain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – permafrost: permanently frozen ground found in polar regions
