shaft of light

noun
narrow column or beam of light
Pronunciation: / ʃæft əv laɪt / (AmE), / ʃɑːft əv laɪt / (BrE)
a narrow column or beam of light, especially one shining through an opening or illuminating a particular area

Examples

a shaft of light through clouds
A single shaft of light pierced the darkness through the crack in the door.
Dust danced in the shaft of light that streamed through the window.
The photographer waited until a shaft of light illuminated the subject perfectly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beam of light: a ray or narrow stream of light
