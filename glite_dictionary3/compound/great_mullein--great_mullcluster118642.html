great mullein

noun
tall woolly plant with yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt ˈmʌlən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈmʌlɪn / (BrE)
A tall plant (Verbascum thapsus) with woolly leaves and spikes of yellow flowers, traditionally used for torches by the ancient Greeks and Romans.

Examples

great mullein plant
During our hike, we spotted several great mulleins growing on the hillside, their woolly leaves soft to the touch.
In ancient times, people used great mullein stalks dipped in fat as torches for ceremonies.
The great mullein stood nearly six feet tall in the garden, its yellow flowers bright against the stone wall.
