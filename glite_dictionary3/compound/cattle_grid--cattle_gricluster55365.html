cattle grid

noun
bars over a ditch to stop cattle crossing
Pronunciation: / ˈkætl ˌɡrɪd / (AmE), / ˈkatl ˌɡrɪd / (BrE)
A set of parallel metal bars placed over a ditch at the entrance to a field or road to prevent cattle from passing while allowing vehicles and pedestrians to cross.

Examples

farm cattle grid
A cattle grid was installed at the farm entrance to keep livestock from wandering onto the road.
Farm vehicles crossed the cattle grid without stopping, but the cows stayed safely in their field.
Pedestrians and cars can cross a cattle grid easily, but animals like sheep and cattle cannot.
