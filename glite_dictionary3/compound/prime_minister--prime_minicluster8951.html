Prime Minister

noun
head of government (UK and other countries)
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
the head of the government in the United Kingdom or other parliamentary systems, typically responsible for leading the cabinet and directing government policy

Examples

the Prime Minister of a country
Boris Johnson served as the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 2019 to 2022.
The Prime Minister addressed the nation during the live television broadcast.
The decision was reviewed by the Prime Minister and her cabinet.
