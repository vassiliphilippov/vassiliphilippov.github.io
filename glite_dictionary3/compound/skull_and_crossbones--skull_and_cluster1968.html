skull and crossbones

noun
danger or poison warning symbol
Pronunciation: / skʌl ənd ˈkrɔsˌboʊnz / (AmE), / skʌl ən ˈkrɒsˌbəʊnz / (BrE)
a symbol consisting of a human skull above two crossed bones, used as a warning of danger or death, especially poisonous substances

Examples

skull and crossbones on poison
Pirates often used the skull and crossbones flag to strike fear into their enemies.
She drew a skull and crossbones on the sign to warn people to keep out.
The bottle had a skull and crossbones on the label to indicate it contained poison.
