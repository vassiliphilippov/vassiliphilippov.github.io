dual scan display

noun
passive matrix display with simultaneous top/bottom refresh
Pronunciation: / ˈduəl skæn dɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / 'djuːəl skæn dɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
a type of passive matrix display in which the screen is divided into two sections, with the top and bottom halves refreshed simultaneously to improve performance

Examples

dual scan display refreshes faster
A dual scan display generally has slower response times compared to active matrix displays.
Early color notebooks often used a dual scan display to reduce production costs.
The laptop featured a dual scan display that was brighter than many other screens at the time.

  • synonym – passive matrix display: a type of flat-panel display technology using a grid to control pixels
