rotational latency

noun
data access delay on rotating disks
Pronunciation: / roʊˈteɪʃənl ˈleɪtənsi / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃənl ˈleɪtənsi / (BrE)
(computer science) the time delay for a specific block of data on a disk to rotate to the position of the read/write head.

Examples

reduce rotational latency
Hard drives with a higher speed have lower rotational latency and typically perform better.
Improvements in storage technology aim to reduce rotational latency for faster data retrieval.
The total time to access data on a disk includes both rotational latency and seek time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – seek time: the time it takes for a disk drive's read/write head to move to the track where data is stored
