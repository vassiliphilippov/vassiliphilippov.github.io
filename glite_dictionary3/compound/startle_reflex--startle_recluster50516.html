startle reflex

noun
infant reflex to loud noise
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrtl̩ rɪˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtl̩ rɪˌflɛks / (BrE)
a normal reflex seen in young infants in which a sudden loud noise causes the arms to flail out and the legs to flex

Examples

infant startle reflex
A loud bang triggered the infant's startle reflex, causing her arms to shoot out suddenly.
Doctors monitor the startle reflex as a sign of healthy neurological development in newborns.
The pediatrician checked the baby's startle reflex during the examination.
