central bank

noun
national institution managing currency and interest rates
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl baŋk / (BrE)
A national institution that manages a country's currency, money supply, and interest rates, and acts as the bank for the government and other banks.

Examples

central bank controls money
All commercial banks are required to hold a percentage of their reserves at the central bank.
The central bank plays a crucial role in maintaining financial stability.
The central bank raised interest rates to control inflation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – commercial bank: a bank that offers services to the general public and businesses
