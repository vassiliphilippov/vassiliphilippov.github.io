vaporous thought

noun
unclear or insubstantial idea
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpɚəs θɔt / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəs θɔːt / (BrE)
an idea or impression that is unclear, insubstantial, or lacking definite form

Examples

unclear vaporous thought
During the lecture, his vaporous thoughts kept him from focusing on the main points.
She tried to grasp the vaporous thought that flickered at the edge of her mind.
Writers often struggle to turn vaporous thoughts into concrete ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concrete thought: an idea that is definite and clear
  • synonym – fleeting thought: an idea that comes and goes quickly, without lasting
