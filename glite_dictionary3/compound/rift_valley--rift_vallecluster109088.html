rift valley

noun
long, steep-sided geological valley
Pronunciation: / rɪft ˈvæli / (AmE), / rɪft ˈval.i / (BrE)
A long, deep valley with steep sides formed by the subsidence of a block of the earth's crust between faults or fractures.

Examples

the rift valley floor
A rift valley often has lakes and rivers running along its floor.
Geologists studied the formation of the rift valley to understand tectonic plate movements.
The Great East African rift valley is one of the most prominent geological features in Africa.
