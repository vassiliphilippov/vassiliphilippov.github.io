abstract entity

noun
non-physical concept or idea
Pronunciation: / ˈæbˌstrækt ˈɛn.tə.ti / (AmE), / ˈab.strækt ˈɛn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
A general concept or idea that exists only in thought or as an idea, not as a physical object.

Examples

an abstract entity like justice
Justice is an example of an abstract entity that cannot be touched or seen.
Love and freedom are abstract entities that can be difficult to define.
Numbers are often considered as abstract entities in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concrete entity: a physical object or thing that exists in reality
