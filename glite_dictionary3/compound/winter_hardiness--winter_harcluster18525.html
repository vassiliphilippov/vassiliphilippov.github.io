winter hardiness

noun
ability of plants to survive winter conditions
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪntər ˈhɑrdiˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈhɑːdinɪs / (BrE)
The ability of a plant to survive or remain healthy during winter, often by remaining dormant through periods of cold.

Examples

winter hardiness of plants
Gardeners often select perennials for their winter hardiness to ensure healthy regrowth each spring.
Improved winter hardiness allows these plants to thrive in colder climates than before.
Researchers are breeding wheat varieties with greater winter hardiness for agricultural sustainability.
