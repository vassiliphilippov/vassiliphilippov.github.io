alpha blocker

noun
drug blocking alpha-adrenergic receptors
Pronunciation: / ˈælfə ˌblɑkər / (AmE), / ˈælfə ˌblɒkə / (BrE)
a drug that blocks alpha-adrenergic receptors, used especially to treat high blood pressure and benign prostatic hyperplasia by relaxing the muscles of blood vessels, prostate, or bladder

Examples

take an alpha blocker pill
Many men with enlarged prostates benefit from taking an alpha blocker.
Side effects of an alpha blocker can include dizziness and fatigue.
The doctor prescribed an alpha blocker to help reduce his high blood pressure.
