system clock

noun
computer component that synchronizes operations
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstəm klɑk / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm klɒk / (BrE)
an electronic component in a computer that generates a regular high-frequency signal to synchronize the operations of internal components

Examples

system clock controls speed
The system clock sends signals that regulate how quickly instructions are executed by the CPU.
Upgrading the system clock can sometimes increase the overall performance of your device.
Without a functioning system clock, your computer will not be able to coordinate its processes efficiently.
