act of faith

noun
action based on trust or belief
Pronunciation: / ækt əv feɪθ / (AmE), / ækt əv feɪθ / (BrE)
an action or decision taken based on trust or belief without proof or certainty

Examples

an act of faith in someone
Investing in a new and untested technology can often be an act of faith.
Starting her own business was an act of faith that required courage and optimism.
Trusting that everything would work out, he took an act of faith and moved abroad.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leap of faith: an act of believing in or attempting something whose existence or outcome cannot be proved
