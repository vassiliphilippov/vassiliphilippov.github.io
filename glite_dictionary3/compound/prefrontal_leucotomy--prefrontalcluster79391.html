prefrontal leucotomy

noun
surgical procedure on frontal brain lobe
Pronunciation: / priˈfrʌntəl luˈkɑtəmi / (AmE), / priˈfrʌntəl ljuːˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the cutting of nerve fibers to and from the frontal lobe of the brain, historically used to treat mental disorders but often resulting in significant cognitive and personality changes

Examples

prefrontal leucotomy for mental illness
Advances in psychiatric medicine have made prefrontal leucotomy largely obsolete today.
After the prefrontal leucotomy, the patient experienced significant personality changes.
The controversial practice of prefrontal leucotomy was once used to treat severe mental illnesses.
