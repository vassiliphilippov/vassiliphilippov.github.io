block anesthesia

noun
anesthesia by blocking a nerve
Pronunciation: / blɑk əˌnɛsθiˈziə / (AmE), / blɒk əˌnɛsθɪˈziːə / (BrE)
a type of anesthesia in which an anesthetic agent is applied to a nerve to numb the area it supplies

Examples

block anesthesia for surgery
Block anesthesia is often chosen for minor surgeries in the hand or arm.
Complications from block anesthesia are rare but can include nerve damage.
The dentist administered block anesthesia to numb the patient's lower jaw before extracting the tooth.
