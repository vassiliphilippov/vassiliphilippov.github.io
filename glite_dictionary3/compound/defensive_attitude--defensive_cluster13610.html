defensive attitude

noun
mindset of self-protection or resistance
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛnsɪv ˈætɪˌtjud / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪv ˈætɪtjuːd / (BrE)
a state of mind in which a person feels the need to protect themselves from criticism or perceived threats, often responding with justification or resistance

Examples

show a defensive attitude
A defensive attitude can prevent you from learning from your mistakes.
Her defensive attitude was obvious when she quickly denied any wrongdoing.
When given feedback, his defensive attitude made it difficult to have a productive discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defensiveness: readiness to defend oneself from criticism
  • antonym – open-mindedness: willingness to consider new ideas
