honoris causa

noun
degree awarded as an honor
Pronunciation: / ɑːˌnorɪs ˈkaʊsə / (AmE), / hɒˌnɔːrɪs ˈkaʊzə / (BrE)
(in full: degree honoris causa) A degree awarded as an honor, without the recipient completing the usual requirements.

Examples

doctorate honoris causa
Dr. Lee received a doctorate honoris causa in recognition of her humanitarian work.
It was a great honor for him to accept the honoris causa from such a prestigious institution.
The university awarded an honoris causa to the renowned author at the commencement ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – honorary degree: an academic degree given as an honor, not for completing requirements
