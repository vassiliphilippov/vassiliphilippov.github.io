Second Lateran Council

proper noun
church council held in 1139 in Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈsekənd ˌlætəˌræn ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / ˈsekənd ˌlatərəːn ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
the ecumenical council of the Western Church held in 1139 in Rome, which addressed church reforms and condemned the teachings of Arnold of Brescia

Examples

the Second Lateran Council in Rome
At the Second Lateran Council in 1139, the Church outlawed certain practices among the clergy.
Historians often cite the decisions of the Second Lateran Council as pivotal in addressing heresies of the time.
The Second Lateran Council enacted reforms that significantly influenced the structure of the medieval Church.
