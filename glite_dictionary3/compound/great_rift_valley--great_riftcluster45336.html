Great Rift Valley

proper noun
large geological depression in Asia and Africa
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt rɪft ˈvæli / (AmE), / ɡreɪt rɪft ˈvæli / (BrE)
a major geological depression extending from the valley of the Jordan River in southwestern Asia through eastern Africa to Mozambique, marked by significant faults and tectonic activity

Examples

the Great Rift Valley in Africa
Geologists study the Great Rift Valley to understand the movement of tectonic plates in the region.
Many species of wildlife thrive in the Great Rift Valley due to its varied landscapes and ecosystems.
The Great Rift Valley is home to some of the world's earliest human fossils and artifacts.
