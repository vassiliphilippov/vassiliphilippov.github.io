bass guitar

noun
low-pitched guitar with four or six strings
Pronunciation: / beɪs ɡɪˈtɑr / (AmE), / beɪs ɡɪˈtɑː / (BrE)
A guitar with a longer neck and scale length, typically having four or six strings, designed to produce low-pitched tones in a band or ensemble.

Examples

play the bass guitar
He learned to play the bass guitar to join the school band.
Her collection includes a vintage bass guitar from the 1960s.
The bass guitar provides the rhythmic foundation in the song.
