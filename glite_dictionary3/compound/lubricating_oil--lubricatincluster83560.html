lubricating oil

noun
oil used to reduce friction in machinery
Pronunciation: / ˈluːbrɪˌkeɪtɪŋ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈluːbrɪkeɪtɪŋ ɔɪl / (BrE)
a thick, oily liquid used to reduce friction between moving parts in machinery.

Examples

lubricating oil for engines
Heavy machinery requires regular application of lubricating oil to function smoothly.
The mechanic checked the level of lubricating oil before starting the engine.
Without enough lubricating oil, engine parts can overheat and become damaged.
