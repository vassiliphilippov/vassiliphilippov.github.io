Portuguese man-of-war

noun
marine animal with stinging tentacles
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔrtʃəˌɡiz ˈmæn əv ˌwɔr / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃuˌɡiːz ˈmæn əv ˌwɔː / (BrE)
a large marine animal with a bladder-like float and long stinging tentacles, belonging to the siphonophore group, often mistaken for a jellyfish

Examples

a Portuguese man-of-war sting
During our boat tour, we spotted a Portuguese man-of-war floating on the surface of the water.
Lifeguards closed the shore after several Portuguese men-of-war washed up overnight.
Swimmers should be cautious, as a Portuguese man-of-war can deliver a painful sting even on the beach.
