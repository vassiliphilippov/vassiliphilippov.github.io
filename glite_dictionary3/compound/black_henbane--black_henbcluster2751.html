black henbane

noun
a poisonous Eurasian plant with hairy leaves and yellow-brown flowers
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈhɛnˌbeɪn / (AmE), / blak ˈhɛnbeɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous plant of the nightshade family, with sticky, hairy leaves and yellow-brown flowers, native to Eurasia and North Africa, containing toxic alkaloids such as hyoscyamine and scopolamine

Examples

black henbane plant
In medieval Europe, black henbane was sometimes used in potions and remedies, despite its risks.
The herbalist warned that black henbane is extremely poisonous and should not be ingested.
The sticky leaves and unpleasant odor make black henbane easy to identify among wild plants.
