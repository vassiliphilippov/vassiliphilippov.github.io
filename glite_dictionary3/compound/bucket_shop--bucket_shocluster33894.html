bucket shop

noun
cheap bar selling liquor by the bucket (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkət ˌʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪt ˌʃɒp / (BrE)
(historical) A cheap saloon or bar where liquor was sold by the bucket, often of questionable quality.

Examples

old bucket shop bar
In the 19th century, a bucket shop was a common sight in some city neighborhoods.
Old newspaper articles detailed raids on bucket shops for selling alcohol illegally.
She avoided entering the bucket shop on the corner, knowing its reputation for watered-down whiskey.
