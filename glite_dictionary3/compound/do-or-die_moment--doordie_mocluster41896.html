do-or-die moment

noun
critical and decisive moment
Pronunciation: / ˌduː ɔr ˈdaɪ ˌmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˌduː ɔː ˈdaɪ ˌməʊmənt / (BrE)
a time or situation in which someone must succeed or risk failure, often under great pressure

Examples

a do-or-die moment in sports
It’s a do-or-die moment for the company as they try to secure funding.
Reaching the summit before sunset was a do-or-die moment for the climbers.
The final debate was a do-or-die moment for the candidate’s campaign.
