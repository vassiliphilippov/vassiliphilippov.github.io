case law

noun
law based on judicial decisions
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪs lɔ /; / keɪs lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs lɔː /; / keɪs lɔː / (BrE)
the body of law based on judicial decisions and precedents, rather than on statutes

Examples

Related Terms

  • synonym – common law: law developed through court decisions, often used interchangeably with case law
  • antonym – statute law: law made by legislative bodies, in contrast to case law
  • antonym – statutory law: law created by legislation, in contrast to case law
