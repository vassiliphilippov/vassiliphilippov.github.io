double entendre

noun
expression with two meanings, one usually risqué
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəl ɑnˈtɑndrə / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl ɒnˈtɒ̃drə / (BrE)
a phrase or expression open to two interpretations, one of which is usually risqué or indecent

Examples

a double entendre joke
Her comment about the banana was a classic double entendre that didn't go unnoticed.
The comedian's routine was full of double entendre that made the audience laugh and blush.
Writers often use double entendre to add layers of humor or suggestiveness to their work.

Related Terms

  • synonym – innuendo: an indirect or subtle, often derogatory implication
  • synonym – pun: a humorous play on words exploiting multiple meanings
