vena vertebralis

noun
vein passing through cervical vertebrae
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnə vɜɹtəˈbɹælɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːnə vɜːtəˈbrælɪs / (BrE)
a vein that passes through the cervical vertebrae and forms a network around the vertebral artery, draining into the brachiocephalic vein

Examples

vena vertebralis injury
Damage to the vena vertebralis during surgery can lead to significant bleeding.
Radiologists carefully examine the vena vertebralis when assessing spinal circulation.
The vena vertebralis plays an important role in draining blood from the cervical spine.

  • synonym – vertebral vein: vein running alongside the cervical vertebrae
