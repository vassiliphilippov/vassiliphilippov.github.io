man-made fiber

noun
fiber produced by human processes
Pronunciation: / ˌmæn ˈmeɪd ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˌmæn ˈmeɪd ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A fiber produced from natural materials or synthesized by chemical processes, rather than occurring naturally in its final form.

Examples

man-made fiber clothing
Many carpets are now woven using man-made fiber for greater durability.
The shirt is made from a man-made fiber that dries quickly after washing.
Unlike wool, man-made fiber resists shrinking and wrinkling.
