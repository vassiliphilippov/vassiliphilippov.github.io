grey birch

noun
birch tree with pale bark of eastern North America
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A medium-sized birch tree native to eastern North America, characterized by white or pale grey bark and typically found in second-growth forests.
