shadow show

noun
theatrical performance using shadows
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊ ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a theatrical performance in which figures are used to cast shadows onto a screen or wall to tell a story

Examples

shadow show performance
A traditional shadow show uses figures cut from leather or paper to create the characters.
The children were delighted by the shadow show performed at the festival.
They attended a fascinating shadow show that depicted ancient legends using lights and silhouettes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shadow play: a performance art using shadows of objects or people to narrate a story
