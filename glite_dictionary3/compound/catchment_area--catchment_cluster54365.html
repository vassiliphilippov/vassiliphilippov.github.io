catchment area

noun
land area draining into a river or body of water
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃmənt ˌɛriə / (AmE), / ˈkætʃmənt ˌeəriə / (BrE)
an area of land from which all precipitation collects and drains off into a common outlet, such as a river, lake, or reservoir

Examples

catchment area of a river
Flooding often occurs when heavy rainfall overwhelms the river's catchment area.
Strict regulations protect the catchment area of the city's main reservoir.
The Nile's catchment area covers several countries in northeastern Africa.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drainage basin: an area of land where all surface water converges to a single point
  • synonym – watershed: an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers or basins
