rugby football

noun
form of football played with an oval ball
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌɡbi ˌfʊtbɔl / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡbi ˌfʊtbɔːl / (BrE)
A form of football played with an oval ball, in which players may carry, kick, and pass the ball with their hands, and points are scored by grounding the ball beyond the opponents' goal line or by kicking it between goalposts.

Examples

rugby football match
Rugby football is especially popular in countries like England, New Zealand, and South Africa.
He played rugby football at university and represented his college in several matches.
The rules of rugby football differ significantly from those of American football.

Related Terms

  • antonym – American football: A North American form of football with different rules and a pointed ball
  • synonym – rugby: A team sport derived from rugby football
  • antonym – soccer: A form of football played mainly with the feet (association football)
