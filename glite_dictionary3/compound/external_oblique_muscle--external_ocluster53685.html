external oblique muscle

noun
broad abdominal muscle on the side of the torso
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɝnəl oʊˈblik ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl əʊˈbliːk ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a broad, flat muscle on each side of the abdomen, running diagonally from the lower ribs to the pelvis, that helps compress the abdominal wall and rotate the trunk

Examples

external oblique muscle injury
After the accident, she experienced pain in her external oblique muscle while coughing.
Proper stretching of the external oblique muscle can help improve flexibility and prevent injury.
The external oblique muscle is engaged when you twist your torso during exercise.

Related Terms

  • antonym – internal oblique muscle: muscle lying underneath the external oblique, also involved in abdominal movement
