folk writer

noun
writer of folktales or traditional stories
Pronunciation: / foʊk ˈraɪtər / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈraɪtə / (BrE)
A writer who creates or records folktales or traditional stories belonging to a community or culture.

Examples

folk writer collects stories
As a prominent folk writer, she helped preserve many oral traditions from her region.
Many folk writers draw inspiration from ancient cultural stories handed down through generations.
The folk writer traveled from village to village collecting local legends and myths.
