full metal jacket

noun
bullet with a hard metal casing
Pronunciation: / fʊl ˈmɛt̬l ˈdʒækɪt / (AmE), / fʊl ˈmetl ˈdʒækɪt / (BrE)
A bullet consisting of a lead core encased in a harder metal, typically copper, forming a shell around the projectile.

Examples

full metal jacket bullet
A full metal jacket helps prevent fragmentation of the bullet upon impact.
He collected several spent full metal jacket bullets from the shooting range for analysis.
The army uses full metal jacket rounds for their superior penetration and reduced lead exposure.
