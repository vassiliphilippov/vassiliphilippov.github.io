National Liberation Army

proper noun
Bolivian Marxist-Leninist guerrilla organization
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəl lɪˌbɚˈreɪʃən ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl lɪb.əˈreɪ.ʃən ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
a Marxist-Leninist guerrilla organization in Bolivia, originally established in the 1960s by Che Guevara and later revived, acting as an umbrella for various indigenous subversive groups

Examples

Bolivian National Liberation Army
Che Guevara played a major role in establishing the National Liberation Army during the 1960s.
Reports indicated that the National Liberation Army was responsible for several attacks on police stations.
The Bolivian government increased its military presence in areas controlled by the National Liberation Army.
