Abney level

noun
surveying instrument for measuring inclination
Pronunciation: / ˈæbni ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈabni ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
a surveying instrument with a spirit level and sighting tube, used to measure the angle of inclination between the observer and a target

Examples

measure slope with an Abney level
By using an Abney level, engineers can determine the grade of a road.
She demonstrated how to calibrate the Abney level before taking any measurements.
The Abney level allowed the surveyor to efficiently measure the slope of the hill.

Related Terms

  • synonym – clinometer: an instrument used for measuring angles of slope, elevation, or depression
