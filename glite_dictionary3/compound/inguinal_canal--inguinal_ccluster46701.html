inguinal canal

noun
passage in lower abdominal wall (anatomy)
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡwɪnəl kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡwɪnəl kəˈnal / (BrE)
an oblique passage through the lower abdominal wall which, in males, allows the descent of the testes and transmits the spermatic cord, and in females transmits the round ligament of the uterus

Examples

the inguinal canal hernia
A hernia may occur when tissue pushes through the inguinal canal.
During fetal development, the testes descend through the inguinal canal into the scrotum.
The surgeon examined the patient's inguinal canal for any signs of abnormal swelling.
