Kawasaki disease

noun
acute illness in children with fever and rash
Pronunciation: / kɑːwəˈsɑːki dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / kawəˈsɑːki dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
an acute illness in young children marked by fever, rash, and swollen lymph nodes, sometimes leading to heart complications

Examples

Kawasaki disease in children
Prompt diagnosis of Kawasaki disease is important to prevent complications affecting the heart.
Some children with Kawasaki disease develop a characteristic red rash and peeling skin on their hands and feet.
The doctor suspected Kawasaki disease because the child's fever had lasted several days and his lymph nodes were swollen.
