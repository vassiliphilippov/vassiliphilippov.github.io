regional anaesthesia

noun
loss of sensation in part of the body via local anesthetic
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdʒənəl əˌniːsθiˈeɪʒə / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəl əˌnɪəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
loss of sensation in a specific region of the body caused by administering an anesthetic agent to all the nerves in that area, often used during procedures such as childbirth or surgery

Examples

regional anaesthesia for surgery
During childbirth, regional anaesthesia such as an epidural can help manage pain effectively.
He remained awake throughout the operation because regional anaesthesia was used on his lower body.
The doctor recommended regional anaesthesia for her knee surgery to minimize recovery time.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – general anaesthesia: loss of sensation and consciousness throughout the entire body
  • degree lower – local anaesthesia: loss of sensation in a small, specific area of the body
