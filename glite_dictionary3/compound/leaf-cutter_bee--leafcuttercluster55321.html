leaf-cutter bee

noun
bee that cuts leaves for its nest
Pronunciation: / ˈliːf ˌkʌtɚ biː / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌkʌtə biː / (BrE)
a bee that cuts pieces from leaves or flowers to construct or line its nest

Examples

leaf-cutter bee cutting leaves
A leaf-cutter bee was busy collecting pieces of rose leaves for its nest.
Gardeners sometimes notice smooth, circular cuts in foliage made by a leaf-cutter bee.
Unlike honeybees, the leaf-cutter bee builds nests with carefully cut leaf fragments.

Related Terms
