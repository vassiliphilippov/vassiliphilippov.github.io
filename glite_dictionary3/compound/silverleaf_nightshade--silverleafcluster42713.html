silverleaf nightshade

noun
a weedy plant with silvery leaves and violet flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvɚˌlif ˈnaɪtˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌliːf ˈnaɪtʃeɪd / (BrE)
A perennial weed with silvery foliage and violet, blue, or white flowers, native to central United States and South America; its berries are sometimes used to curdle milk.

Examples

silverleaf nightshade plant
Farmers often remove silverleaf nightshade because it can be toxic to livestock.
The berries of silverleaf nightshade are sometimes used in traditional cheesemaking.
The fields were overrun with silverleaf nightshade after the heavy rains.
