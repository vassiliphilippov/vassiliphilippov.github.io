manic-depressive psychosis

noun
mental disorder with mania and depression
Pronunciation: / ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv saɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv saɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a mental disorder characterized by alternating episodes of mania and depression; more commonly referred to today as bipolar disorder

Examples

manic-depressive psychosis diagnosis
Many historical figures are now believed to have suffered from manic-depressive psychosis.
She was diagnosed with manic-depressive psychosis after experiencing several periods of extreme highs and lows.
The term manic-depressive psychosis has largely been replaced by 'bipolar disorder' in modern psychiatry.
