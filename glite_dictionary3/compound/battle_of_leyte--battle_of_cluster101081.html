Battle of Leyte

noun
World War II battle in the Philippines, 1944
Pronunciation: / ˈbætəl əv ˈleɪti / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈleɪti / (BrE)
a major World War II battle in October 1944, marking the return of United States troops to the Philippines and the first use of Japanese kamikaze aircraft.

Examples

the Battle of Leyte in 1944
Historians often emphasize the strategic importance of the Battle of Leyte in liberating the Philippines.
The Battle of Leyte was a turning point in the Pacific theater of World War II.
The first use of kamikaze tactics occurred during the Battle of Leyte in 1944.
