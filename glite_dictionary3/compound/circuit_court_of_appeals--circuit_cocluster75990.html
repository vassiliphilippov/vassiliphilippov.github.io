circuit court of appeals

noun
federal appellate court in the U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːkɪt ˌkɔrt əv əˈpilz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkɪt ˌkɔːt əv əˈpiːlz / (BrE)
one of the twelve federal courts in the United States that reviews cases from district courts within a particular group of states, known as a circuit
