enabling legislation

noun
law granting power to enforce or implement laws
Pronunciation: / ɪˈneɪ.blɪŋ ˌlɛdʒ.ɪˈsleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪ.blɪŋ ˌlɛdʒ.ɪˈsleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A law or group of laws that grants authorities the power to enforce existing laws or implement particular policies.

Examples

enabling legislation grants power
Congress passed enabling legislation to give the department authority to administer the new tax program.
The new enabling legislation allows local governments to regulate short-term rentals in their districts.
Without enabling legislation, the agency cannot legally enforce the new environmental standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ordinance: a piece of legislation enacted by a municipal authority
  • synonym – statute: a written law enacted by a legislative body
