Strait of Calais

proper noun
strait between England and continental Europe
Pronunciation: / streɪt əv kəˈleɪ / (AmE), / streɪt əv kaˈleɪ / (BrE)
the narrow strait between the English Channel and the North Sea, marking the shortest distance between England and the European continent

Examples

the Strait of Calais crossing
Crossing the Strait of Calais has been significant in both trade and military history.
Many ships pass through the Strait of Calais each day, making it one of the busiest waterways in the world.
The Strait of Calais is just 21 miles wide at its narrowest point between Dover and Calais.
