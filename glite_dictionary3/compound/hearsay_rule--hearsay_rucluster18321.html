hearsay rule

noun
legal rule on admissibility of evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪrˌseɪ ruːl / (AmE), / ˈhɪəseɪ ruːl / (BrE)
A legal principle stating that statements made outside of court are generally not admissible as evidence during a trial, unless they fall under certain exceptions.

Examples

hearsay rule in court
Because of the hearsay rule, the letter was not admitted into evidence during the trial.
Lawyers must often argue whether an exception to the hearsay rule applies to a particular statement.
The judge excluded the testimony under the hearsay rule, since the witness was repeating someone else's comments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hearsay: information received from others that cannot be substantiated; rumor or report, or out-of-court statement
