field judge

noun
American football official downfield
Pronunciation: / ˈfild ˌdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
an official in American football who is positioned downfield behind the defensive secondary, on the same sideline as the line judge, responsible for enforcing game rules and observing specific aspects of play

Examples

the field judge throws flag
A penalty flag was thrown by the field judge for pass interference.
During the game, the field judge monitored the back end of the defensive formation.
The field judge signaled that the pass was incomplete.
