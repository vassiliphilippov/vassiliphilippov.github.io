Sacred College

noun
body of Roman Catholic cardinals
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪkrəd ˈkɑlədʒ / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrɪd ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the body of all cardinals in the Roman Catholic Church, responsible for electing and advising the Pope

Examples

the Sacred College of cardinals
Debate within the Sacred College often shapes important decisions in the Catholic Church.
Membership in the Sacred College is regarded as one of the highest honors for a Catholic cleric.
The Sacred College convenes in a conclave to elect a new pope when the papal seat is vacant.
