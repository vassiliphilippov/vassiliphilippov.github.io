Last Judgement

noun
final divine judgement at the end of the world
Pronunciation: / læst ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / lɑːst ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
(In Christian theology) the final judgement by God of all people at the end of the world, determining their eternal fate based on their deeds.

Examples

the Last Judgement day
Christian doctrine holds that the Last Judgement will determine the eternal fate of every soul.
Many works of art depict the Last Judgement as a dramatic and awe-inspiring event.
The preacher spoke about the consequences our actions might have at the Last Judgement.
