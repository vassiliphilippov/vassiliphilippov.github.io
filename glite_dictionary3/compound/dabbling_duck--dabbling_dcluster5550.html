dabbling duck

noun
shallow-water duck that feeds by dabbling
Pronunciation: / ˈdæblɪŋ ˌdʌk / (AmE), / ˈdæblɪŋ ˌdʌk / (BrE)
a duck that feeds primarily by upending and dabbling in shallow water rather than diving

Examples

dabbling duck feeding in water
Many species of dabbling duck can be found in marshes and wetlands across North America.
The Mallard is a well-known dabbling duck commonly seen in city parks and ponds.
Unlike diving ducks, a dabbling duck feeds by tipping its head underwater while its tail remains above the surface.
