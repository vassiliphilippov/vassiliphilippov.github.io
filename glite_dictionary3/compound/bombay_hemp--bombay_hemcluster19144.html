Bombay hemp

noun
fiber plant native to East Indies
Pronunciation: / bɑmˈbeɪ hɛmp / (AmE), / bɒmˈbeɪ hɛmp / (BrE)
a valuable fiber-yielding plant, native to the East Indies and now widely cultivated, used for making rope and other products

Examples

Bombay hemp rope making
Farmers in Southeast Asia have cultivated Bombay hemp for centuries to produce fishing nets and rope.
Some traditional crafts use Bombay hemp as an environmentally friendly alternative to synthetic fibers.
The strong fibers of Bombay hemp make it an important material in the textile industry.
