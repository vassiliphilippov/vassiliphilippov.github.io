bond certificate

noun
official document representing a loan or bond
Pronunciation: / bɑnd səˈtɪfɪkət / (AmE), / bɒnd səˈtɪfɪkət / (BrE)
an official document issued by a government or corporation that represents a loan made by an investor, specifying the amount borrowed, interest rate, and repayment terms

Examples

bond certificate for investment
He keeps his grandfather's bond certificate stored in a safety deposit box.
Investors received a bond certificate as proof of their loan to the city.
The bond certificate details the principal, interest rate, and maturity date.
