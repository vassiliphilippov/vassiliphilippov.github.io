semipermeable membrane

noun
membrane permitting selective passage of molecules
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌpɜːrmiˈeɪbəl ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌpɜːmɪˈeɪbəl ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (BrE)
a membrane that allows certain molecules or ions to pass through it by diffusion but not others

Examples
