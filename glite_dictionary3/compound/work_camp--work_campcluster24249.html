work camp

noun
camp where prisoners do manual labor
Pronunciation: / wɝk kæmp / (AmE), / wɜːk kæmp / (BrE)
a camp in which prisoners or detainees are required to do manual labor, especially as part of government projects or as a form of punishment.

Examples

prison work camp
During the 1930s, many unemployed men were sent to a work camp to help build public roads.
Historians have studied conditions in the work camp to better understand the era's penal practices.
The prisoners in the work camp were required to complete eight hours of labor every day.

Related Terms

  • antonym – concentration camp: a place where large numbers of people are detained or confined under armed guard, often forcibly
