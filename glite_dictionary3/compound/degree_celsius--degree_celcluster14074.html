degree Celsius

noun
unit of Celsius temperature
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡri ˈsɛlsiəs / (AmE), / dɪˈɡri ˈsɛlsiəs / (BrE)
a unit of temperature on the Celsius scale, equal to one one-hundredth of the interval between the freezing and boiling points of water

Examples

30 degrees Celsius outside
Body temperature is about 37 degrees Celsius in a healthy adult.
The forecast predicts temperatures to reach 30 degrees Celsius this afternoon.
Water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius and boils at 100 degrees Celsius under standard conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Celsius: the temperature scale in which 0° is the freezing point and 100° is the boiling point of water
  • antonym – degree Fahrenheit: a unit of temperature on the Fahrenheit scale
