electrodermal response

noun
change in skin's electrical properties due to stress or arousal
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈdɜrməl rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈdɜːməl rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a change in the electrical properties of the skin in response to stress, anxiety, or arousal, often measured for psychological or medical purposes

Examples

measure electrodermal response
An increase in electrodermal response is often correlated with feelings of anxiety and excitement.
Researchers use electrodermal response to study emotional reactions in clinical trials.
The psychologist measured the patient's electrodermal response during the stress test.
