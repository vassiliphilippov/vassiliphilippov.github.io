zero coupon security

noun
security sold at discount with no periodic interest
Pronunciation: / ˈzɪəroʊ ˈkuːpɑn sɪˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ ˈkuːpɒn sɪˈkjʊərɪti / (BrE)
A security that does not pay periodic interest but is sold at a substantial discount and redeemed at face value on maturity.

Examples

zero coupon security investment return
A zero coupon security may appeal to those who do not need regular income from their investment.
At maturity, a zero coupon security pays the investor its full face value.
Investors often choose zero coupon security for long-term goals like funding college expenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym – coupon bond: a bond that pays interest periodically
  • synonym – zero coupon bond: a bond sold at a discount that pays no interest and is repaid at face value
