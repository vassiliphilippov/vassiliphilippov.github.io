beta iron

noun
nonmagnetic allotrope of iron
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪtə ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə ˈaɪən / (BrE)
an allotrope of iron that is identical to alpha iron but is nonmagnetic and is stable between 768 and 906°C.

Examples

beta iron loses magnetism
Above the Curie temperature, beta iron loses its magnetic properties.
Materials scientists study beta iron to understand its structural changes at high temperatures.
The transition from beta iron to gamma iron occurs at 906°C.
