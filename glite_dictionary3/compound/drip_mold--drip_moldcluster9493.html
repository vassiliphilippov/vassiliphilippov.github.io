drip mold

noun
projecting molding diverting rainwater
Pronunciation: / drɪp moʊld / (AmE), / drɪp məʊld / (BrE)
An architectural projection from a cornice, sill, or ledge designed to divert rainwater away from the surface below, such as above a window or doorway.

Examples

drip mold above window
Careful restoration of the Victorian drip molds preserved the original character of the building.
Rainwater was safely deflected by the drip mold over the doorway, keeping the entrance dry.
The architect included a drip mold above each window to protect the stonework from water damage.
