retained object

noun
object remaining in passive construction
Pronunciation: / rɪˈteɪnd ˈɑbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnd ˈɒbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
an object in a passive sentence that remains after the verb's transformation from active voice, often corresponding to the indirect object of the active construction.

Examples

retained object in a sentence
In the sentence 'She was given a gift by her friend', 'a gift' is the retained object.
Teachers often ask students to identify the retained object in passive sentences.
When converting to the passive, the recipient becomes the subject, and the original direct object may become a retained object.

Related Terms

  • antonym – direct object: the entity acted upon by the verb
  • synonym – indirect object: the recipient or beneficiary of the action in a clause
