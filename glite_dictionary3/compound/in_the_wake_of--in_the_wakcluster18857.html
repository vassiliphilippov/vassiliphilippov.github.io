in the wake of

preposition
following as a result of something
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə weɪk ʌv / (AmE), / ɪn ðə weɪk ɒv / (BrE)
occurring as a result of or following closely after (an event or situation)

Examples

in the wake of a disaster
People remained cautious in the wake of last year's financial crisis.
Several new policies were introduced in the wake of the scandal.
There was increased security in the wake of the recent attacks.
