cut-rate sale

noun
temporary event with reduced prices
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌreɪt seɪl / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌreɪt seɪl / (BrE)
an occasion when goods are offered for sale at specially reduced prices, often for a brief period

Examples

cut-rate sale on clothes
Many shops hold a cut-rate sale after the holidays to clear out unsold merchandise.
She waited for the cut-rate sale before buying her new television.
The department store attracted hundreds of customers with its cut-rate sale last weekend.
