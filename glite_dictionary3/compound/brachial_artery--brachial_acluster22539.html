brachial artery

noun
main artery of the upper arm
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkiəl ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈbræk.i.əl ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
the main artery of the upper arm, continuing from the axillary artery and dividing into the radial and ulnar arteries at the elbow

Examples

brachial artery injury
After the accident, surgeons had to repair the torn brachial artery.
The brachial artery is often used to measure blood pressure with a cuff.
