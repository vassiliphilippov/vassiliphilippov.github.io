complex conjugate

noun
the paired number of a complex number with the sign of the imaginary part reversed
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛks ˈkɑn.dʒə.ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks ˈkɒn.dʒʊ.ˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
either of a pair of complex numbers having identical real parts and imaginary parts that differ only in sign; for example, for a + bi, its complex conjugate is a - bi.
