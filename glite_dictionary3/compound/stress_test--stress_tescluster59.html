to stress test

verb
subject to heavy load for testing
Pronunciation: / strɛs tɛst / (AmE), / strɛs tɛst / (BrE)
to subject a system, device, or process to heavy or extreme load or adverse conditions to check its performance, stability, or resilience.

Examples

to stress test equipment
The QA team will stress test the software before releasing the update to customers.
Last week our engineers stress-tested the servers to reproduce the outage under heavy load.
Before deployment, remember to stress test the new feature with simulated user traffic.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stress: to place demanding conditions on something to see how it performs under pressure.
  • synonym – test: to evaluate the performance, quality, or reliability of something by examination or trial.
