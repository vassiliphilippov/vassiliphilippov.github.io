visible spectrum

noun
range of visible light wavelengths
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪzəbəl ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪbəl ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the range of wavelengths of light that are visible to the human eye, appearing as a distribution of colors when light is dispersed, such as by a prism

Examples

visible spectrum of light
A rainbow is a natural display of the visible spectrum in the sky.
The visible spectrum includes all the colors that our eyes can perceive, from red to violet.
When white light passes through a prism, the visible spectrum fans out into distinct colors.
