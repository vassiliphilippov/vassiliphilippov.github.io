collective bargaining

noun
negotiation between employer and trade union
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪv ˈbɑrɡənɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv ˈbɑːɡənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of negotiation between an employer and a group of employees, usually represented by a trade union, concerning working conditions, wages, and other employment terms.

Examples

collective bargaining agreement
Successful collective bargaining often requires compromise from both employers and employees.
The union leaders are preparing for collective bargaining with the company next month.
Through collective bargaining, workers were able to secure higher wages and better benefits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – arbitration: settlement of a dispute by an independent person or body
  • synonym – negotiation: discussion aimed at reaching an agreement
