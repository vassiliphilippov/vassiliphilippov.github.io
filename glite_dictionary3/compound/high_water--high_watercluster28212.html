high water

noun
the highest point of the tide
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈwɑtɚ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
the state of the tide when the water reaches its highest level

Examples

high water at noon
Due to the storm, today's high water was higher than usual.
Fishermen wait for high water to bring in their boats closer to shore.
The harbor was accessible only at high water when the tide was in.
