latchkey child

noun
unsupervised child after school
Pronunciation: / ˈlætʃki tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈlatʃki tʃʌɪld / (BrE)
a school-age child who returns to an empty home after school and remains without adult supervision until the parents return from work

Examples

latchkey child returns alone
As a latchkey child, she learned to be independent from a young age.
Teachers are sometimes concerned about the safety of latchkey children after school.
The rise in dual-income households has led to an increase in latchkey children in many urban areas.
