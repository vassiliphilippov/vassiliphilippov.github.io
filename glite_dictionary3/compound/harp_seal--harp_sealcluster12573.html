harp seal

noun
a species of Arctic seal with young that are all white
Pronunciation: / hɑrp sil / (AmE), / hɑːp siːl / (BrE)
A large, Arctic seal (Pagophilus groenlandicus) with dark, harp-shaped markings on its back; the young are born with a white coat.

Examples

young harp seal
Climate change is threatening the icy habitats where harp seals give birth to their young.
Many people are familiar with the fluffy white pups of the harp seal.
The harp seal is easily recognized by the distinctive harp-shaped pattern on its back.
