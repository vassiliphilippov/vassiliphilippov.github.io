subsidiary company

noun
company controlled by another company
Pronunciation: / səbˈsɪdiˌɛri ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / səbˈsɪdiəri ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a company that is wholly or partly owned and controlled by another company, known as the parent company

Examples

subsidiary company of a group
As a subsidiary company, it must follow the directives of its parent organization.
The financial statements of a subsidiary company are often consolidated with those of the parent company.
The multinational corporation owns several subsidiary companies in Europe and Asia.
