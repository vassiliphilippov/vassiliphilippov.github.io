burning bush

noun
plant with flammable white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːnɪŋ bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ bʊʃ / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Dictamnus albus) with white flowers that give off a flammable vapor in hot weather.

Examples

burning bush white flowers
Botanists studied the burning bush to understand its unusual ability to emit flammable gases.
On a warm afternoon, the vapor from the burning bush can actually ignite if exposed to a flame.
The burning bush in our garden released a strange scent during the hottest part of summer.
