anemic hypoxia

noun
hypoxia due to low hemoglobin levels
Pronunciation: / əˈniːmɪk haɪˈpɑksiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪk hɪˈpɒksɪə / (BrE)
A form of hypoxia caused by a decreased concentration of hemoglobin in the blood, resulting in reduced oxygen transport to tissues.

Examples

anemic hypoxia after blood loss
Doctors treated her anemic hypoxia with a blood transfusion to restore oxygen-carrying capacity.
In cases of severe anemia, anemic hypoxia can lead to fatigue and shortness of breath.
The patient was diagnosed with anemic hypoxia due to chronic blood loss and low hemoglobin levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anemia: a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • synonym – hypoxia: deficiency in the amount of oxygen reaching the tissues
