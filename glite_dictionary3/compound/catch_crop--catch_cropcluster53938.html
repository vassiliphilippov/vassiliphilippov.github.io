catch crop

noun
quick-growing crop grown between regular crops
Pronunciation: / kætʃ krɑp / (AmE), / katʃ krɒp / (BrE)
A quick-growing crop planted between two main crops or between rows of crops to make efficient use of land and growing season.

Examples

catch crop between harvests
Farmers often plant a catch crop like lettuce to utilize the space between main crop cycles.
Radishes are sometimes used as a catch crop between longer-maturing vegetables.
The use of a catch crop can help prevent soil erosion and increase land productivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cover crop: a crop grown for the protection and enrichment of the soil
