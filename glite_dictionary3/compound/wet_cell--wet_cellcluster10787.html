wet cell

noun
voltaic cell with liquid electrolyte
Pronunciation: / wɛt sɛl / (AmE), / wɛt sɛl / (BrE)
A primary voltaic cell in which the electrolyte is in liquid form.

Examples

wet cell battery
A car battery is an example of a wet cell used to start the engine.
Early experiments in electricity often relied on the wet cell for power.
Unlike a dry cell, a wet cell requires careful handling to prevent spills.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dry cell: a voltaic cell with a paste electrolyte instead of a liquid
