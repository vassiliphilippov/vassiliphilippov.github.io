point-contact diode

noun
type of semiconductor rectifier
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪnt kɒnˌtækt ˈdaɪˌoʊd / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt ˌkɒn.tækt ˈdaɪ.əʊd / (BrE)
A type of semiconductor rectifier that uses a barrier between a specially prepared semiconductor surface and a metal point to allow current to pass in one direction only.

Examples

point-contact diode circuit board
Repairing old equipment sometimes requires sourcing a replacement point-contact diode.
The point-contact diode was one of the earliest types used in radio receivers.
Unlike modern diodes, a point-contact diode has a metal wire pressed onto a semiconductor crystal.
