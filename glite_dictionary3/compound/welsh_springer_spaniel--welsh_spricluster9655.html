Welsh springer spaniel

noun
a red-and-white spaniel breed from Wales
Pronunciation: / wɛlʃ ˈsprɪŋər spænˈjɛl / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈsprɪŋə ˈspæn.jəl / (BrE)
A breed of dog with a red-and-white coat, slightly smaller than the English springer spaniel, originally bred in Wales for hunting.

Examples

friendly Welsh springer spaniel
The Welsh springer spaniel is known for its friendly temperament and striking red-and-white coat.
Their family adopted a Welsh springer spaniel because of its affectionate nature and manageable size.
Unlike its English cousin, the Welsh springer spaniel is slightly smaller and has a distinct coloration.
