Gunter's chain

noun
unit of length (22 yards)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌntərz tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌntəz tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a historical unit of length used in land surveying, equal to 22 yards (66 feet or about 20.12 metres)

Examples

Gunter's chain measures land
A distance of one Gunter's chain was marked out by placing brass links end to end.
Early surveyors often measured land using a Gunter's chain and not modern tape measures.
The field was recorded as four Gunter's chains long by two chains wide.
