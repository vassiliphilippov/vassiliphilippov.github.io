mercury cell

noun
type of primary electric cell
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝːkjəri sɛl / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊəri sɛl / (BrE)
A type of primary electric cell with a zinc anode, a mercury oxide cathode, and potassium hydroxide as the electrolyte, formerly used in batteries.

Examples

mercury cell battery
A mercury cell provides a steady voltage output, making it useful for some precision instruments.
Due to environmental concerns, the use of mercury cells in batteries has been discontinued in many countries.
The old hearing aid was powered by a small mercury cell that needed regular replacement.
