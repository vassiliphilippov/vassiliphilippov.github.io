Secretary of the Navy

noun
former head of the U.S. Navy Department
Pronunciation: / səˈkrɛt̬.ɚ.i əv ðə nəˈvi / (AmE), / sɪˈkrɛt.ər.i əv ðə nəˈvi / (BrE)
the head of the United States Department of the Navy, an executive position that oversaw Navy operations before being merged under the Secretary of Defense in 1947

Examples

The Secretary of the Navy played a key role in shaping naval policy during World War II.
