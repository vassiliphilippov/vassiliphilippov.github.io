appropriation bill

noun
legislative proposal for public fund expenditure
Pronunciation: / əˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən bɪl / (AmE), / əˌprəʊpriˈeɪʃən bɪl / (BrE)
a legislative proposal authorizing the allocation and expenditure of public funds for a specified purpose

Examples

pass an appropriation bill
Each appropriation bill must be carefully reviewed before receiving final approval.
The Senate passed an appropriation bill to fund national infrastructure projects.
Without an appropriation bill, government agencies may face a shutdown.
