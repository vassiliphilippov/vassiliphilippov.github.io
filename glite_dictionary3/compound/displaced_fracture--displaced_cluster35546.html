displaced fracture

noun
bone fracture with separated ends
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪst ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪst ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
a fracture in which the two ends of the broken bone are separated from each other and no longer aligned

Examples

severe displaced fracture
A displaced fracture often causes more swelling and pain than a simple fracture.
After the accident, he suffered a displaced fracture in his arm that needed realignment.
The X-ray revealed a displaced fracture of her left femur, requiring surgical intervention.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non-displaced fracture: fracture where the bone fragments remain in alignment
