positive feedback

noun
feedback that amplifies changes in a system
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzɪtɪv ˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
a process in which the effects of a small disturbance on a system include an increase in the magnitude of the disturbance; feedback that augments or amplifies changes in a system

Examples

positive feedback loop
In climate science, positive feedback loops can accelerate global warming.
The concept of positive feedback is essential for understanding some biological control mechanisms.
The microphone picked up positive feedback, causing an increasingly loud screeching noise.

Related Terms

  • antonym – negative feedback: feedback that reduces or counteracts changes in a system
