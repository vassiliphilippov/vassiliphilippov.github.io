two-man saw

noun
large two-person hand saw
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː mæn sɔ / (AmE), / ˈtuː mæn sɔː / (BrE)
a large saw with handles at both ends, designed to be operated by two people, typically for cutting lumber or logs

Examples

use a two-man saw on logs
The lumberjacks used a two-man saw to cut through the thick tree trunk.
This old two-man saw was passed down from my grandfather, who worked in the forests.
With a steady rhythm, they pulled the two-man saw back and forth to fell the giant oak.
