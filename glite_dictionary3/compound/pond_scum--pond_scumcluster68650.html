pond scum

noun
layer of green algae on pond surface
Pronunciation: / pɑnd skʌm / (AmE), / pɒnd skʌm / (BrE)
A layer of free-floating green algae that forms on the surface of still freshwater ponds.

Examples

pond scum covers water
A thick layer of pond scum had formed on the water after several hot days.
Children poked sticks into the pond scum to see what lived underneath.
The lake looked unpleasant because pond scum covered much of its surface.
