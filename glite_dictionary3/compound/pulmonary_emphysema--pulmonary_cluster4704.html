pulmonary emphysema

noun
chronic lung disease causing shortness of breath
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌlməˌneri ˌɛmfəˈsiːmə / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri ˌɛmfɪˈsiːmə / (BrE)
A chronic lung condition characterized by damage to the air sacs, causing shortness of breath and reduced respiratory function.

Examples

pulmonary emphysema from smoking
Long-term smoking is a major risk factor for developing pulmonary emphysema.
Medical research shows that pulmonary emphysema often occurs alongside chronic bronchitis.
The patient was diagnosed with pulmonary emphysema after experiencing difficulty breathing for several months.
