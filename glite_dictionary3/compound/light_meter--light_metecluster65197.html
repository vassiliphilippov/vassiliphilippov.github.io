light meter

noun
device for measuring light intensity
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌmiːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a device used in photography to measure the intensity of light, helping to determine the correct exposure settings

Examples

use a light meter for photos
A handheld light meter can be more accurate than relying on your camera's automatic settings.
Older cameras often require a separate light meter because they don't have one built in.
The photographer used a light meter to ensure the picture would be properly exposed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exposure meter: device that measures the amount of light to determine correct photographic exposure
