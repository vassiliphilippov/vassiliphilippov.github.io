dental plaque

noun
film of bacteria on teeth
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛntəl ˌplæk / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl ˌplɑːk / (BrE)
a soft, sticky film made up of bacteria and mucus that forms on the surface of teeth and can lead to tooth decay

Examples

remove dental plaque
Good brushing and flossing help remove dental plaque and keep your mouth healthy.
Regular visits to the dentist can help control the buildup of dental plaque.
The main cause of gum disease is the accumulation of dental plaque along the gum line.

Related Terms

  • synonym – plaque: a sticky deposit on teeth or other surfaces
  • degree higher – tartar: hard calcified deposit that forms from dental plaque on teeth
