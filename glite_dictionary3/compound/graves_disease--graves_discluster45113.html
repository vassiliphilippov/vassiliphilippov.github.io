Graves' disease

noun
thyroid disorder causing eye protrusion and swelling
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪvz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A medical condition in which an overactive thyroid gland causes symptoms such as swelling of the neck and protrusion of the eyes.

Examples

Graves' disease bulging eyes
After months of fatigue and weight loss, doctors diagnosed her with Graves' disease.
Effective treatments for Graves' disease can help manage the symptoms and prevent complications.
The classic symptom of Graves' disease is bulging eyes, known as exophthalmos.

  • synonym – hyperthyroidism: excessive activity of the thyroid gland
