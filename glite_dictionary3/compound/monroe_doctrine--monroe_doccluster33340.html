Monroe Doctrine

noun
U.S. policy against European intervention in Americas
Pronunciation: / məˈnoʊ ˈdɑktrɪn / (AmE), / məˈnɒ ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
An American foreign policy announced in 1823, opposing European colonialism and interference in the affairs of countries in the Western Hemisphere.

Examples

the Monroe Doctrine warns Europe
President Monroe's message established the Monroe Doctrine as a warning to European powers.
The Monroe Doctrine shaped American foreign policy throughout the nineteenth century.
Throughout history, the United States has invoked the Monroe Doctrine to justify its actions in Latin America.
