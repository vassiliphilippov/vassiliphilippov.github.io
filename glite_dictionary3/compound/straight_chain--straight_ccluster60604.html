straight chain

noun
unbranched chain of atoms in a molecule
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪt tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a chain of atoms in a molecule arranged in a continuous sequence without any side branches

Examples

straight chain molecule
Hexane can exist in both branched and straight chain forms, each with different properties.
In alkanes, a straight chain refers to molecules with carbon atoms connected in a single, unbranched row.
The simplest straight chain hydrocarbon is called n-butane.

Related Terms

  • antonym – branched chain: a chain of atoms with one or more side chains
