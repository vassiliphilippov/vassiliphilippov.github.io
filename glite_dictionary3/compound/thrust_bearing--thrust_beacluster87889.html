thrust bearing

noun
bearing for axial or thrust loads
Pronunciation: / θrʌst ˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / θrʌst ˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
A bearing designed to support thrust or axial loads parallel to the axis of rotation in a machine or mechanism.

Examples

thrust bearing in machinery
A properly lubricated thrust bearing ensures smooth axial movement in rotating machinery.
The thrust bearing in the engine needed to be replaced after extensive wear.
The technician inspected the thrust bearing for signs of overheating and misalignment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bearing: a machine part that supports and reduces friction in a rotating shaft or element
