economic infrastructure

noun
facilities and systems enabling an economy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkɑnəmɪk ˌɪnfrəˈstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmɪk ˌɪnfrəˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
The physical facilities and organizational systems, such as transportation, communication, power, and water supply, that are necessary for an economy to function and grow.

Examples

economic infrastructure projects
Governments often allocate funds to improve economic infrastructure such as roads, ports, and power grids.
Investment in economic infrastructure has boosted industrial growth in the region.
Poor economic infrastructure can hinder a country's development and its ability to attract foreign investment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – infrastructure: the basic physical and organizational structures needed for the operation of a society or enterprise
