real socialism

noun
practical form of socialism in Soviet bloc
Pronunciation: / ˈriəl ˌsoʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˌsəʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
A form of socialism that prioritizes practical and attainable objectives over the ultimate theoretical goals of Marxism-Leninism, especially as implemented in the Soviet Union and Eastern Europe.

Examples

real socialism in Eastern Europe
Real socialism was implemented in several Eastern European countries during the Cold War era.
Many historians analyze the differences between real socialism and Marxist theory.
The term real socialism was used to distinguish the Soviet system from theoretical models of socialism.
