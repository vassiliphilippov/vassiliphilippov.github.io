Battle of Flodden Field

proper noun
1513 battle between England and Scotland
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ˈflɑd(ə)n ˈfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈflɒdən ˈfiːld / (BrE)
a battle fought in 1513 near Branxton, Northumberland, in which the English army defeated the invading Scots and King James IV of Scotland was killed
