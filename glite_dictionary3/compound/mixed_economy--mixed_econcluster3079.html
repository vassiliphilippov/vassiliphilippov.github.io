mixed economy

noun
economic system with both private and state sectors
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪkst ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˌmɪkst ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
an economic system in which both private individuals and the state own and control means of production and distribution of goods

Examples

a mixed economy system
After a period of central planning, the country transitioned to a mixed economy to encourage private enterprise.
Many countries have adopted a mixed economy to balance the benefits of free markets with social welfare programs.
The United Kingdom is often cited as an example of a mixed economy due to its combination of private and public sectors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – market economy: an economic system in which production and prices are determined by unrestricted competition between privately owned businesses
  • antonym – planned economy: an economic system in which the government controls production and distribution of goods
