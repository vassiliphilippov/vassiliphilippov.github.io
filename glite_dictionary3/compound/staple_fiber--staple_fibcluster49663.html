staple fiber

noun
natural fiber suitable for spinning
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪpəl ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪpəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A natural fiber, such as cotton, wool, hemp, or flax, that is of limited length and can be twisted together to form yarn.

Examples

cotton staple fiber
Cotton is classified as a staple fiber because it is composed of short, twistable lengths.
Manufacturers blend different staple fibers to produce textiles with varied textures and strengths.
Wool, as a staple fiber, is commonly spun into yarn for knitting and weaving.

Related Terms

  • antonym – filament fiber: a fiber of indefinite or extreme length, such as silk
