blue goose

noun
dark-plumed North American wild goose
Pronunciation: / blu ˈɡus / (AmE), / blu ˈɡuːs / (BrE)
a North American wild goose with dark plumage in summer and white plumage in winter, a color variant of the snow goose.

Examples

a blue goose flying
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare blue goose among the flock.
The blue goose migrates annually between the Arctic and southern United States.
Unlike the white morph, the blue goose has distinctive slate-colored feathers.
