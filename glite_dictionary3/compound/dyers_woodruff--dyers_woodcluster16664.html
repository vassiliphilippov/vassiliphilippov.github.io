dyer's woodruff

noun
European plant used in dyeing fabrics
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪərz ˈwʊdrəf / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz ˈwʊdrəf / (BrE)
a creeping European perennial plant (Asperula tinctoria) with red or pinkish-white flowers and red roots, sometimes used as a substitute for madder in dyeing fabrics
