petty jury

noun
jury deciding facts in a trial
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛti ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ˈpɛti ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A jury of twelve people who decide the facts and verdict in civil or criminal legal proceedings.

Examples

petty jury decides verdict
A petty jury is responsible for determining the facts in a criminal trial.
Both the prosecution and the defense questioned prospective members of the petty jury.
The petty jury deliberated for hours before reaching a verdict.

Related Terms

  • antonym – grand jury: a jury that examines accusations and issues indictments, but does not determine guilt
