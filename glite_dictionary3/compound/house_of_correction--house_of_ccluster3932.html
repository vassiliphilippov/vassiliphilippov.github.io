house of correction

noun
(historical) minor-offence detention facility
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊs əv kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs əv kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
A place of detention for persons convicted of minor offences, especially as used formerly in England and the United States.

Examples

sent to a house of correction
Before the rise of modern prisons, a house of correction held those convicted of minor crimes.
Many petty criminals were sent to the house of correction for their offences in the 18th century.
The old house of correction has now been converted into a museum.
