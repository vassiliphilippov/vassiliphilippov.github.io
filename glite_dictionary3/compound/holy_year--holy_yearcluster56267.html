Holy Year

noun
a special Roman Catholic year granting remission of sins
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊli jɪr / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli jɪə / (BrE)
In the Roman Catholic Church, a special year, usually every 25 years, during which remission from sins is granted and special indulgences may be obtained.

Examples

celebrate a Holy Year
During the Holy Year, special ceremonies and events are held in Vatican City.
Pilgrims from around the world traveled to Rome to celebrate the Holy Year.
Pope Francis declared 2016 as a Holy Year of Mercy for the Catholic Church.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Jubilee: a special anniversary, especially one of 25 or 50 years, and in the Catholic Church a year of remission of sins
