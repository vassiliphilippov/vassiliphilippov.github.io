pericardial space

noun
space between pericardium layers
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑrdiəl speɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkɑːdiəl speɪs / (BrE)
the space between the layers of the pericardium that contains fluid, enabling smooth movement of the heart

Examples

fluid in the pericardial space
Doctors examined the pericardial space for signs of inflammation after the surgery.
Excess fluid in the pericardial space can compress the heart and impair its function.
