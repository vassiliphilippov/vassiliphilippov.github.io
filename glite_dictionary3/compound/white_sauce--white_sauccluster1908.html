white sauce

noun
sauce of milk thickened with butter and flour
Pronunciation: / waɪt sɔs / (AmE), / waɪt sɔːs / (BrE)
A sauce made by thickening milk with a butter and flour roux, commonly used in cooking.

Examples

white sauce for pasta
He made a creamy white sauce for the lasagna from scratch.
Pour the white sauce over the cooked vegetables before baking.
The recipe calls for a basic white sauce as the foundation.
