heat sink

noun
device for dissipating heat
Pronunciation: / hit sɪŋk / (AmE), / hiːt sɪŋk / (BrE)
A device, typically a metal component, designed to absorb and dissipate heat from another object, especially electronic components, to prevent overheating.

Examples

heat sink for a computer
Aluminum is commonly used to manufacture heat sinks for computers and other electronics.
Proper installation of a heat sink can significantly improve a system's cooling efficiency.
The heat sink ensures that the processor does not overheat during heavy use.

Related Terms

  • synonym – radiator: a device used to transfer heat from one medium to another for heating or cooling
