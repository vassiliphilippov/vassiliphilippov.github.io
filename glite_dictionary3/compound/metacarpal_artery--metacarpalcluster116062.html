metacarpal artery

noun
artery in the hand along metacarpal bones
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈkɑrpəl ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈkɑːpəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
Any of several arteries in the hand that run along the metacarpal bones, including both dorsal and palmar branches.

Examples

metacarpal artery supplies hand
An injury to the metacarpal artery can lead to significant bleeding in the hand.
Blood flow in the metacarpal artery is essential for proper healing after a fracture.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the metacarpal artery during the procedure.
