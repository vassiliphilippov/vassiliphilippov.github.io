vicious cycle

noun
sequence where problems worsen each other
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃ.əs ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A sequence of events in which one problem causes another problem, making the original problem worse and continuing the cycle.

Examples

break the vicious cycle
Being in debt often leads to a vicious cycle of borrowing more to pay off existing loans.
The company was caught in a vicious cycle of declining sales and cost-cutting measures that hurt morale.
Without proper intervention, poverty and illiteracy can form a vicious cycle that's difficult to break.

  • synonym – vicious circle: a sequence where problems worsen each other in a self-perpetuating loop
