indirect expression

noun
a not-direct way of expressing something
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndəˈrɛkt ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkt ɪkˈsprɛʃən / (BrE)
a way of conveying an idea or message without stating it directly

Examples

use indirect expression
Her preference for indirect expression sometimes made her comments difficult to interpret.
Instead of saying she was fired, he used an indirect expression to say she was let go.
Often, indirect expressions are used to avoid offending someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circumlocution: the use of many words to say something that could be said more directly
  • antonym – direct expression: a straightforward or explicit way of expressing something
  • synonym – euphemism: a mild or indirect word used in place of one considered harsh
