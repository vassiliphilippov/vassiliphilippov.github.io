Irish pound

noun
historical Irish currency unit
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrɪʃ paʊnd / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ paʊnd / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Ireland, equal to 100 pence and replaced by the euro in 2002

Examples

the Irish pound coin
Before 2002, Ireland used the Irish pound as its official currency.
He exchanged his Irish pounds for euros during his trip to Dublin.
The Irish pound featured unique symbols and imagery distinct from its British counterpart.
