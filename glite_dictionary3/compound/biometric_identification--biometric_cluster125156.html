biometric identification

noun
automatic identification using biological traits
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛtrɪk aɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əˈmɛtrɪk aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of automatically identifying individuals using unique physiological or behavioral characteristics, such as fingerprints or facial features

Examples

biometric identification at airports
Many airports now use biometric identification systems to speed up security checks.
The company upgraded its security by introducing biometric identification for all employees.
Unlike passwords, biometric identification relies on a person's unique physical traits.
