value-added tax

noun
tax on value added to goods and services
Pronunciation: / ˈvælju ˌædɪd ˈtæks / (AmE), / ˈvalju ˌadɪd ˈtaks / (BrE)
A tax levied on the value added to goods and services at each stage of production or distribution, usually collected by the final retailer.

Examples

value-added tax on goods
A recent debate questioned whether to raise the value-added tax to increase government revenue.
Many European countries charge a value-added tax on most consumer products.
The value-added tax is often included in the price displayed on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sales tax: a tax on sales or on the receipts from sales
