bellwether state

noun
US state indicating election trends
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛlˌwɛðər steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌwɛðə steɪt / (BrE)
(in US politics) a state whose election results are seen as indicative of wider national trends or outcomes.

Examples

key bellwether state in elections
Because of its consistent voting record, the media often refers to Ohio as a bellwether state.
Political strategists pay close attention to the bellwether state during presidential elections.
Winning the bellwether state is often seen as a key sign of national victory.

Related Terms

  • synonym – swing state: a state in which both major political parties have similar levels of support among voters
