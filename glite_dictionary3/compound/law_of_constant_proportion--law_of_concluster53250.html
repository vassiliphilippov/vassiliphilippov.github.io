law of constant proportion

noun
chemical law about fixed element ratios
Pronunciation: / lɔ ʌv ˈkɑnstənt prəˈpɔrʃən / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈkɒnstənt prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
A scientific principle stating that every pure substance always contains the same elements combined in the same proportions by weight.

Examples

law of constant proportion in chemistry
In chemistry class, we learned how the law of constant proportion applies to the formation of compounds like carbon dioxide.
Thanks to the law of constant proportion, scientists can predict the composition of pure substances.
The law of constant proportion explains why water always contains hydrogen and oxygen in a fixed ratio by mass.

Related Terms

  • synonym – law of definite proportions: the principle that a chemical compound always contains the same elements in exactly the same proportions by weight or mass
