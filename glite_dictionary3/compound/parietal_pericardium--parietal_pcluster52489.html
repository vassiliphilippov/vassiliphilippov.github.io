parietal pericardium

noun
outer layer of the pericardium
Pronunciation: / pəˈraɪ.ɪ.t̬l ˌpɛr.iˈkɑːr.di.əm / (AmE), / pəˈraɪ.ɪ.təl ˌpɛr.ɪˈkɑːd.i.əm / (BrE)
the tough outer membrane of the pericardium that is attached to the diaphragm and the sternum, surrounding the heart

Examples

parietal pericardium covers heart
During surgery, the parietal pericardium was carefully separated from the diaphragm.
Inflammation of the parietal pericardium can cause significant chest pain.
The parietal pericardium protects the heart by providing a tough outer layer.
