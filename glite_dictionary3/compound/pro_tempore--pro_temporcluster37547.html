pro tempore

adverb
for the time being; temporarily
Pronunciation: / proʊ ˈtɛmpəˌreɪ / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈtɛmpəri /; / prəʊ ˈtɛmpəriː / (BrE)
for the time being; temporarily (especially when someone is acting in a position until a permanent person is chosen)

Examples

pro tempore chairman
He was appointed as the head of the committee pro tempore until a permanent leader was chosen.
The arrangement is pro tempore and subject to review next month.
The vice president served pro tempore during the chairman's absence.
He served as acting governor pro tempore during the absence of the elected official.
She was appointed chairperson pro tempore until a permanent replacement was found.
The board elected a secretary pro tempore for the emergency meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – permanent: lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • antonym – permanently: in a way that lasts or remains forever; not temporarily
  • synonym – temporarily: for a limited period of time; not permanently
  • synonym – temporary: lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent
