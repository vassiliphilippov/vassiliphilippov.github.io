covering fire

noun
protective gunfire in battle
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvərɪŋ ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌvərɪŋ ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
intense gunfire directed at or near an enemy to prevent them from returning fire, allowing friendly troops to advance or withdraw safely

Examples

covering fire protects soldiers
During the retreat, tanks provided covering fire to shield the infantry.
The soldiers waited for covering fire before making their move toward the enemy position.
Without adequate covering fire, the advance could have ended in disaster.

Related Terms

  • synonym – suppressive fire: gunfire intended to suppress enemy action
