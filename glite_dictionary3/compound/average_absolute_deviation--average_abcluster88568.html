average absolute deviation

noun
mean of absolute differences from a central value
Pronunciation: / ˈævərɪdʒ ˌæbsəluːt ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈævərɪdʒ ˌæbsəluːt ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the average of the absolute differences between each data point in a set and a central value (such as the mean or median), used as a summary statistic of variability.

Examples

average absolute deviation of scores
The average absolute deviation is less sensitive to extreme values than the standard deviation.
To estimate the variability of the scores, we calculated the average absolute deviation.
Using the average absolute deviation provides a straightforward way to describe the spread of the data.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mean absolute deviation: the average of the absolute differences between values and the mean
  • degree higher – standard deviation: a measure of the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of values
