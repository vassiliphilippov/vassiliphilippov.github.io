closing price

noun
last trade price of a session
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊzɪŋ praɪs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzɪŋ praɪs / (BrE)
the price at which a stock or other security last trades during a regular trading session, typically used as a daily benchmark

Examples

closing price of a stock
Analysts pay close attention to the closing price when evaluating market trends.
The closing price for the stock was reported at $150 per share.
The company's closing price was higher than expected after a day of intense trading.

Related Terms

  • antonym – opening price: the price at which a security first trades during a trading session
