sellers' market

noun
market with more buyers than sellers
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlɚz ˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛləz ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
an economic situation in which demand for a product is greater than the supply, giving sellers an advantage over buyers

Examples

a sellers' market for houses
During a sellers' market, prices tend to rise due to increased competition among buyers.
It's tough to find bargains in a sellers' market because demand is so high.
The housing sector is currently a sellers' market, with more buyers than available homes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – buyers' market: a market in which supply exceeds demand, favoring buyers
