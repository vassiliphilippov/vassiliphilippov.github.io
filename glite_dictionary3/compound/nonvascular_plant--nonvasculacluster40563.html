nonvascular plant

noun
plant without vascular tissue (e.g. moss)
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈvæskjələr plænt / (AmE), / nɒnˈvæskjʊlə plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant, such as a moss or liverwort, that lacks specialized vascular tissue for conducting water and nutrients

Examples

nonvascular plant like moss
Because it lacks true roots and stems, a nonvascular plant absorbs water directly through its surfaces.
Mosses are a common type of nonvascular plant found in damp, shady areas.
Unlike ferns, a nonvascular plant does not have xylem or phloem to transport water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – vascular plant: plant with specialized vascular tissue; opposite of nonvascular plant
