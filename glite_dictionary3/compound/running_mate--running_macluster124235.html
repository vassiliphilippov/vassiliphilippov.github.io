running mate

noun
candidate for secondary political office
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnɪŋ ˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ ˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a candidate who is nominated for the subordinate position in a pair of closely linked political offices, typically as the vice-presidential nominee on a presidential ticket

Examples

running mate for president
Each candidate chooses a running mate who shares similar political views.
Selecting the right running mate can influence voters' decisions in a close election.
The presidential nominee announced her running mate during the party's national convention.
