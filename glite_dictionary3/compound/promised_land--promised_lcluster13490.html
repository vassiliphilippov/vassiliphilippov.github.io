Promised Land

proper noun
the land promised to Abraham's descendants in the Bible
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑmɪst ˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈprɒmɪst ˌlænd / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the land promised by God to Abraham and his descendants; traditionally identified as the region of ancient Palestine on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean Sea.

Examples

the Promised Land of Israel
For centuries, the Promised Land has been a site of historical and spiritual significance.
Many religious pilgrims visit the Promised Land every year to connect with their faith.
Stories of the Israelites journeying to the Promised Land are central to the Bible.
