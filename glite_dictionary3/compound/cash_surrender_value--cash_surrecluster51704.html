cash surrender value

noun
value paid when insurance is cancelled
Pronunciation: / kæʃ səˈrɛndɚ ˈvælju / (AmE), / kæʃ səˈrɛndə ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
the amount an insurance company pays to the policyholder if a life insurance policy is cancelled before the insured's death

Examples

cash surrender value of a policy
After reviewing the policy, she decided to claim the cash surrender value and cancel her insurance.
Some people use the cash surrender value of their life insurance as an emergency fund.
The cash surrender value increases gradually as the policy matures over time.
