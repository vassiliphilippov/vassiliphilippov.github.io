bank line

noun
maximum credit limit from a bank
Pronunciation: / ˈbæŋk laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbæŋk laɪn / (BrE)
the maximum amount of credit that a bank authorizes for a customer, typically for use in borrowings such as loans or overdrafts

Examples

bank line increase request
During the financial review, the manager increased the firm's bank line to meet future needs.
If you exceed your bank line, the bank may charge additional fees.
She negotiated a bank line to support her company's expansion.
