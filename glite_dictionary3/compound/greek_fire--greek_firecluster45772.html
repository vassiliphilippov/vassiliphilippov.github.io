Greek fire

noun
incendiary weapon used by Byzantines
Pronunciation: / ɡrik faɪɚ / (AmE), / ɡriːk faɪə / (BrE)
a flammable liquid weapon used by the Byzantine Greeks, notable for catching fire when wetted and often deployed against enemy ships or troops

Examples

Greek fire destroys ships
Enemy ships were quickly consumed when struck by Greek fire during the siege.
The Byzantines famously used Greek fire to defend Constantinople from naval attacks.
The secret formula for Greek fire has been lost to history, intriguing scholars for centuries.
