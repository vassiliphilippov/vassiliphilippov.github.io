polyphonic prose

noun
prose using poetic devices for rhythm and sound
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈfɑnɪk proʊz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfɒnɪk prəʊz / (BrE)
A type of rhythmical prose that employs poetic devices such as alliteration and assonance to achieve a musical effect.

Examples

polyphonic prose style
Modernist writers often employed polyphonic prose in their attempts to blur the boundaries between poetry and prose.
The author experimented with polyphonic prose to give her narrative a lyrical, musical quality.
The use of alliteration and assonance in polyphonic prose creates a rich auditory experience for the reader.
