gas embolism

noun
painful condition from gas bubbles in the blood
Pronunciation: / ɡæs ˈɛm.boʊˌlɪz.əm /; / ɡæs ˈɛm.bə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈɛm.bəʊˌlɪz.əm /; / ɡæs ˈɛm.bə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
a medical condition where gas or air bubbles block a blood vessel, often caused by diving or medical procedures

Examples

danger of gas embolism
Divers are at risk of a gas embolism if they ascend too quickly from deep water.
Symptoms of a gas embolism can include severe joint pain and difficulty breathing.
The patient suffered a gas embolism following an accident involving compressed air.
A gas embolism can be life-threatening if not treated promptly by medical professionals.
Divers are trained to surface slowly to reduce the risk of developing a gas embolism.
The patient suffered a gas embolism during a routine surgical procedure.
