marsh milkweed

noun
a North American herbaceous plant of wet areas
Pronunciation: / mɑrʃ ˈmɪlkwid / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ˈmɪlkwiːd / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb (Asclepias incarnata) with whorled leaves and terminal clusters of pink flowers often spotted with purple, typically found in wet or marshy areas.

Examples

marsh milkweed flowers bloom
Bees and monarch caterpillars are often seen feeding on the flowers of marsh milkweed.
She identified the tall stems by the riverbank as marsh milkweed during our botany walk.
The wetland restoration project included the planting of marsh milkweed to attract native butterflies.
