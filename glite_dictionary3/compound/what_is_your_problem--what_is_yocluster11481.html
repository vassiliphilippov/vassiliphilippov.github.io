asking about someone's issue or concern
Pronunciation: / wʌt ɪz jʊr ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / wɒt ɪz jɔː ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
used to ask someone why they are upset, troubled, or behaving in a difficult way; sometimes used as a challenge or expression of annoyance.

Examples

what is your problem with me
He snapped, 'what is your problem?' when she criticized his work.
I noticed you were sighing a lot—what is your problem today?
If you have an issue, just tell me what is your problem.
