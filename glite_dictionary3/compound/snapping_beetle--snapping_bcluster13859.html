snapping beetle

noun
beetle that rights itself by snapping
Pronunciation: / ˈsnæp.ɪŋ ˌbiː.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈsnæp.ɪŋ ˌbiː.təl / (BrE)
A beetle that can flip itself into the air with a clicking sound in order to right itself when turned onto its back; also called a click beetle.

Examples

snapping beetle jumps upright
A snapping beetle can startle you if you accidentally turn one over and watch it snap upright.
Researchers studied how the snapping beetle uses its unusual jumping mechanism to avoid predators.
The snapping beetle made a sharp clicking noise as it flipped off its back.
