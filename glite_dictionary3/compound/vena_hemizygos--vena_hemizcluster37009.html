vena hemizygos

noun
vein on the left of the vertebral column
Pronunciation: / ˌviːnə hɛˌmɪˈzaɪgəs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə hɛmɪˈzɪɡɒs / (BrE)
a vein running up the left side of the vertebral column, which crosses over at the level of the 8th thoracic vertebra to drain into the azygos vein

Examples

vena hemizygos drains blood
During surgery, careful identification of the vena hemizygos helps prevent accidental injury.
If the vena hemizygos is blocked, collateral circulation through the azygos vein may increase.
The vena hemizygos plays a crucial role in draining blood from the left posterior thoracic wall.
