second-in-command

noun
person next in authority after a leader
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkənd ɪn kəˈmænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ɪn kəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
the person in an organization or group who has authority immediately below the leader or commander and takes over in their absence

Examples

the second-in-command officer
After the general fell ill, the second-in-command immediately assumed control of the operation.
Every team should have a second-in-command in case the leader is unavailable.
She served as second-in-command for three years before being promoted to director.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – commander: person in overall control or command
  • synonym – deputy: person appointed to assist or act as a substitute for another
