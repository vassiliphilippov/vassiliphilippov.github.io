elongate leaf

noun
long, slender leaf
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɔŋɡeɪt liːf / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋɡeɪt liːf / (BrE)
A long, slender leaf, typically much longer than it is wide.

Examples

an elongate leaf shape
During the plant identification workshop, we learned to distinguish an elongate leaf from an oval one.
Many grasses have an elongate leaf structure, allowing them to sway in the wind without breaking.
The willow tree is easily recognized by its elongate leaves that hang gracefully from the branches.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ovate leaf: a leaf shaped like an egg, with a broad base
