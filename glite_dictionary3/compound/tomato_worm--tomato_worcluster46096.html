tomato worm

noun
large green caterpillar that feeds on tomato and tobacco plants
Pronunciation: / təˈmeɪɾoʊ wɜrm / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ wɜːm / (BrE)
a large green larva with white stripes, the caterpillar of a hawkmoth, that feeds on tomato, tobacco, and related plants

Examples

tomato worm on plants
Farmers often check their crops for signs of the tomato worm to prevent damage to their tomato plants.
She found a tomato worm crawling on the underside of a tobacco leaf.
The large green tomato worm can quickly consume the leaves of several plants in a garden.
