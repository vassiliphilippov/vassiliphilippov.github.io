Ergotrate Maleate

noun
drug inducing uterine contractions
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrɡətreɪt məˈleɪət / (AmE), / ˈɜːɡətreɪt məˈliːeɪt / (BrE)
a trade name for an ergot-derived alkaloid used to induce contraction of the uterus, typically after childbirth or abortion

Examples

Ergotrate Maleate tablets after childbirth
Ergotrate Maleate is considered less toxic than some other ergot alkaloids.
After delivery, the doctor prescribed Ergotrate Maleate to help the uterus contract.
The nurse administered Ergotrate Maleate following the procedure to minimize bleeding.
