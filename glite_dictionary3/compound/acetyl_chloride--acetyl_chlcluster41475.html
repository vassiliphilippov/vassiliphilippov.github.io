acetyl chloride

noun
colorless liquid chemical compound
Pronunciation: / əˈsiːtəl ˈklaɪərd / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪl ˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, volatile liquid with a pungent odor, used in organic synthesis; the acyl chloride CH3COCl.

Examples

acetyl chloride reaction
Acetyl chloride is widely used in laboratories for the synthesis of pharmaceuticals and perfumes.
Always handle acetyl chloride under a fume hood due to its pungent and irritating vapors.
When acetyl chloride reacts with water, it produces acetic acid and hydrogen chloride gas.
