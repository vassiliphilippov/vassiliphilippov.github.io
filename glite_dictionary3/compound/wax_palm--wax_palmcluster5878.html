wax palm

noun
Brazilian palm yielding fiber and wax
Pronunciation: / wæks pɑm / (AmE), / wæks pɑːm / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree with edible roots, used as a source of leaf fiber and a brittle yellowish wax.

Examples

Brazilian wax palm roots
Local farmers harvest the yellowish wax produced by the wax palm for industrial use.
The wax palm is prized for the valuable fiber extracted from its leaves.
The wax palm thrives in certain regions of Brazil and supports several small economies.
