Meniere's disease

noun
inner ear disease causing vertigo and hearing loss
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪərz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / məˈnjeəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A disorder of the inner ear causing episodes of vertigo, tinnitus, and gradually increasing hearing loss, typically affecting one ear.

Examples

Meniere's disease causes vertigo
People with Meniere's disease may suffer from sudden attacks of vertigo that can last for hours.
She was diagnosed with Meniere's disease after experiencing repeated bouts of dizziness and ringing in her ears.
Treatment for Meniere's disease often focuses on managing the symptoms and reducing the risk of future episodes.
