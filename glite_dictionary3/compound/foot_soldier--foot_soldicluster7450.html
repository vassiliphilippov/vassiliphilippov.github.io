foot soldier

noun
routine or subordinate worker
Pronunciation: / fʊt ˈsoʊldʒɚ / (AmE), / fʊt ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
A person who does routine or subordinate work for an organization, often under the authority of others.

Examples

company foot soldier
Most of the research was done by graduate students, the foot soldiers of the project.
She started as a foot soldier in the company before working her way up to management.
The campaign relied on hundreds of foot soldiers to distribute flyers to every house.

Related Terms

  • antonym – superior: someone in a position of higher authority
  • synonym – underling: a subordinate or junior worker
