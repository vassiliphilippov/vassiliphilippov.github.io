rotational nystagmus

noun
eye movement caused by body rotation
Pronunciation: / roʊˈteɪʃənəl naɪsˈtæɡməs / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃənəl nɪsˈtæɡməs / (BrE)
a type of involuntary eye movement occurring when the body rotates rapidly, characterized by slow, large movements of the eyeballs in the direction of rotation

Examples

rotational nystagmus after spinning
After spinning on the playground, the child temporarily experienced rotational nystagmus and saw the world spinning.
Researchers observed rotational nystagmus in subjects who underwent rapid rotational movements.
The doctor explained that the dizziness was related to rotational nystagmus after spinning quickly in the chair.
