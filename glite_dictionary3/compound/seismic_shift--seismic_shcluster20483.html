seismic shift

noun
dramatic or fundamental change
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪz.mɪk ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈsaɪz.mɪk ʃɪft / (BrE)
A sudden, significant, or dramatic change, especially in ideas, beliefs, policies, or circumstances.

Examples

a seismic shift in opinion
The election represented a seismic shift in the country's political landscape.
The merger brought a seismic shift in the structure of the entire industry.
There has been a seismic shift in public opinion on climate change over the last decade.
