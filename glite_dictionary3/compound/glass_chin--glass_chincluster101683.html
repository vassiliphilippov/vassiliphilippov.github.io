glass chin

noun
vulnerability to being knocked out (sports)
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs tʃɪn / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs tʃɪn / (BrE)
a tendency to be easily knocked out, especially because of a weak jaw; vulnerability to being struck unconscious, often in boxing or combat sports

Examples

a glass chin in boxing
Commentators agreed that his glass chin cost him the match.
Despite his talent, his glass chin was his greatest weakness in the ring.
Many boxers try to hide their glass chin by relying on defensive skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym – weak chin: chin lacking physical strength, especially in boxing context
