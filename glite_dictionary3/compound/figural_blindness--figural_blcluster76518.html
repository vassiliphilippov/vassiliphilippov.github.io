figural blindness

noun
inability to see shapes and contours
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪɡjɚəl ˈblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡjərəl ˈblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
A condition in which a person is unable to perceive shapes and contours visually.

Examples

figural blindness to shapes
After his stroke, the neurologist diagnosed him with figural blindness when he was unable to identify simple geometric patterns.
Patients with figural blindness may see colors or lights but struggle to recognize objects by their outlines.
The study explored rehabilitation methods for those experiencing figural blindness following traumatic brain injury.
