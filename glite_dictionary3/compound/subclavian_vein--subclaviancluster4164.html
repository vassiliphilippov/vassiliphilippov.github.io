subclavian vein

noun
major vein beneath the collarbone
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈkleɪviən veɪn / (AmE), / sʌbˈkleɪvɪən veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein that continues from the axillary vein and joins with the internal jugular vein to form the brachiocephalic vein, located beneath the clavicle.

Examples

subclavian vein injury
An injury to the subclavian vein can lead to serious bleeding and requires immediate attention.
During the procedure, the doctor carefully avoided puncturing the subclavian vein.
The catheter was inserted into the subclavian vein for intravenous access.
