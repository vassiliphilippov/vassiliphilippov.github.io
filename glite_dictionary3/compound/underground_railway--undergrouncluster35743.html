Underground Railway

noun
secret network aiding escaped slaves (US history)
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌɡraʊnd ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌɡraʊnd ˈreɪlweɪ / (BrE)
a secret network organized to help enslaved people escape to free states or Canada, chiefly in the United States before the Civil War

Examples

the Underground Railway escape route
Harriet Tubman was a famous conductor on the Underground Railway.
Many abolitionists risked their lives to participate in the Underground Railway.
