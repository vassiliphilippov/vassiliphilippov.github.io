Proto-Indo-European language

noun
reconstructed ancestor of Indo-European languages
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtoʊˌɪndoʊ ɪˈrɔpiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˌɪndəʊ ɪˈrɔːpiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the hypothetical prehistoric language reconstructed as the common ancestor of all Indo-European languages, spoken before written records existed

Examples

ancient Proto-Indo-European language
Linguists have reconstructed many words from the Proto-Indo-European language using comparative methods.
No written evidence of the Proto-Indo-European language exists, but its features are widely studied.
The Proto-Indo-European language is the theoretical ancestor of most languages in Europe and parts of Asia.
