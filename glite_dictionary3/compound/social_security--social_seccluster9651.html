Social Security

noun
US government welfare program
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊʃəl sɪˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəl sɪˈkjʊərəti / (BrE)
In the United States, a government program providing financial support to people who are retired, unemployed, or disabled, including old-age, survivors, and unemployment insurance.

Examples

Social Security benefits
After turning 65, my grandmother began receiving Social Security benefits every month.
He applied for Social Security disability benefits after his injury.
Many Americans rely on Social Security for their retirement income.
