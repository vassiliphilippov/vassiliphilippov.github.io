chemical agent

noun
substance causing chemical reactions
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance that produces or promotes chemical reactions, especially one deliberately used for its chemical effect.

Examples

toxic chemical agent
Exposure to a toxic chemical agent can cause severe health problems if proper precautions are not taken.
The laboratory stores each chemical agent in labeled containers to prevent accidental mixing.
They used a special chemical agent to accelerate the cleaning process.
