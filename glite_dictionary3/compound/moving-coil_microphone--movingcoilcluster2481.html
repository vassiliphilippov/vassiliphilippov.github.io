moving-coil microphone

noun
type of dynamic microphone
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːvɪŋ kɔɪl ˈmaɪkrəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmuːvɪŋ kɔɪl ˈmaɪkrəfəʊn / (BrE)
A type of dynamic microphone in which sound waves move a diaphragm attached to a coil of wire, generating an electric current within a magnetic field.

Examples

moving-coil microphone for live music
Because of its rugged design, a moving-coil microphone is a staple of stage productions.
Engineers recommended a moving-coil microphone for recording loud instruments like drums or electric guitars.
The moving-coil microphone is widely used in live performances due to its durability.
