to count to five

verb
pause to calm down before reacting
Pronunciation: / kaʊnt tə faɪv / (AmE), / kaʊnt tʊ faɪv / (BrE)
to briefly pause and compose oneself before reacting, often by mentally counting from one to five

Examples

to count to five calmly
Before replying to the harsh email, she took a moment to count to five.
He taught his son to count to five whenever he was upset to avoid arguments.
Whenever I feel frustrated at work, I try to count to five before saying anything.
