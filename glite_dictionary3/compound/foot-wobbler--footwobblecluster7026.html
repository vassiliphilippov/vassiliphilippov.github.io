foot-wobbler

noun
person who moves unsteadily
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊt ˌwɑb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˌwɒb.lə / (BrE)
a person, especially a child or someone in high heels, who walks unsteadily or struggles to keep balance

Examples

a foot-wobbler in high heels
After twisting her ankle, she became a foot-wobbler for a few days.
At the wedding, she was clearly a foot-wobbler in her new high heels.
The toddler was a real foot-wobbler, always stumbling around the living room.
