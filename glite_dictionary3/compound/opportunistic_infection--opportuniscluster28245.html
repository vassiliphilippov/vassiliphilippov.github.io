opportunistic infection

noun
infection affecting those with weak immune systems
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpərˈtunɪstɪk ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpətjuːˈnɪstɪk ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection caused by microorganisms that usually do not cause disease but can do so when the immune system is weakened or compromised

Examples

opportunistic infection after illness
Doctors monitor transplant recipients closely for any signs of opportunistic infection.
HIV/AIDS can leave individuals vulnerable to opportunistic infections that are rare in healthy people.
The patient developed an opportunistic infection due to his compromised immune system.

  • synonym – infection: the invasion and multiplication of microorganisms in body tissues
