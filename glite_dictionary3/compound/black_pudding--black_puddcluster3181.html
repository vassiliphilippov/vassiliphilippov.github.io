black pudding

noun
dark sausage made from pig's blood and fillers
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / blak ˈpʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of dark sausage made from pig's blood, fat, and various fillers such as oatmeal, commonly eaten cooked or fried.

Examples

fried black pudding
At breakfast, she ordered fried black pudding alongside her eggs and toast.
Many people are surprised to learn that black pudding is made with blood.
Traditional black pudding is often served as part of a full English breakfast.
