jack ladder

noun
nautical rope or chain ladder with rungs
Pronunciation: / dʒæk ˈlædər / (AmE), / dʒæk ˈladə / (BrE)
(nautical) a rope or chain ladder with wooden or metal rungs, typically used on ships or for boarding.

Examples

climb a jack ladder
A jack ladder was secured to the side of the ship for boarding small boats.
Emergency drills required everyone to descend the jack ladder safely.
The sailors quickly climbed the jack ladder to reach the deck.
