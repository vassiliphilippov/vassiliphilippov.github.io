phototropic bacteria

noun
bacteria using sunlight for energy
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtəˈtroʊpɪk bəˈktɪriə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈtrɒpɪk bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Bacteria that obtain energy from sunlight and derive their carbon from carbon dioxide or organic compounds, including green and purple bacteria.

Examples

phototropic bacteria in sunlight
Green and purple phototropic bacteria are often found in aquatic environments exposed to sunlight.
Researchers study phototropic bacteria to better understand alternative photosynthetic pathways.
Some phototropic bacteria can perform photosynthesis in the absence of oxygen.
