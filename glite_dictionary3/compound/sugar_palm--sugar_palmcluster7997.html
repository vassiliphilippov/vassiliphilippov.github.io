sugar palm

noun
tropical palm tree yielding sap and sago
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuːɡɚ pɑːm / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə pɑːm / (BrE)
A large tropical palm tree native to Southeast Asia, especially Malaysia, whose sap is used to make sugar and wine, and whose trunk pith yields sago.

Examples

tall sugar palm tree
Farmers climb the tall sugar palm to collect sap for making palm wine.
Sago is extracted from the trunk pith of the sugar palm.
The sugar palm is an important source of sweet sap in many Southeast Asian countries.
