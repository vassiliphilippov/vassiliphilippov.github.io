buck-passing culture

noun
environment of shirking responsibility
Pronunciation: / bʌk ˈpæsɪŋ ˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / bʌk ˈpɑːsɪŋ ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
A culture or environment within an organization or group where individuals regularly avoid responsibility by shifting blame to others.

Examples

toxic buck-passing culture
A buck-passing culture often leads to low morale and inefficiency.
Efforts to improve accountability failed because the buck-passing culture was deeply entrenched.
The new manager is trying to change the buck-passing culture that has developed in the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buck-passing: the act of shifting responsibility to someone else
