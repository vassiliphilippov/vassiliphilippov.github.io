common louse

noun
parasitic insect found on humans
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən laʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən laʊs / (BrE)
A small parasitic insect (Pediculus humanus) that infests the head or body of humans, feeding on blood and causing irritation.

Examples

a common louse infestation
A common louse can live up to a month on the human body if not treated.
Infestations of the common louse are more frequent among school-aged children.
The school nurse checked all the children for common louse infestations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – body louse: a type of common louse that infests clothing and skin
  • synonym – head louse: a type of common louse that infests the scalp
