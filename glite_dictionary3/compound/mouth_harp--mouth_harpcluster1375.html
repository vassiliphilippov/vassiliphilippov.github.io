mouth harp

noun
free-reed wind musical instrument; harmonica
Pronunciation: / maʊθ hɑrp / (AmE), / maʊθ hɑːp / (BrE)
A small rectangular free-reed musical instrument with a row of reeds set in air holes, played by blowing into the desired hole; also called a harmonica.

Examples

play a mouth harp
He carried his mouth harp everywhere, playing cheerful tunes on street corners.
Learning to play the mouth harp takes practice, but it is a rewarding instrument.
The blues musician captivated the crowd with his skillful mouth harp performance.
