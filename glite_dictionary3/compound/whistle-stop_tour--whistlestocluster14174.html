whistle-stop tour

noun
brief visits to many places, often for campaigning
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪsəl stɑp tʊr / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl stɒp tʊə / (BrE)
A series of brief visits to various places, especially small towns, made by a political leader or candidate during a campaign.

Examples

whistle-stop tour across towns
During her whistle-stop tour, she delivered speeches at every train station along the route.
The candidate embarked on a whistle-stop tour of rural communities to meet local voters.
The senator's whistle-stop tour helped boost his popularity in towns often overlooked during campaigns.

Related Terms

  • synonym – campaign trail: route taken by a political candidate during a campaign
  • synonym – roadshow: a series of public performances or appearances in different locations
