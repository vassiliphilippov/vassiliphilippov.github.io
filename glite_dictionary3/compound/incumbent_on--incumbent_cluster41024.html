incumbent on

adjective
morally binding, required as a duty
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌmbənt ɑn / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmbənt ɒn / (BrE)
necessary as a duty or responsibility; morally binding or required for someone to do

Examples

incumbent on managers
As a manager, it is incumbent on you to lead by example.
He felt it was incumbent on him to support the project, despite his reservations.
It is incumbent on all employees to follow the safety rules at all times.
