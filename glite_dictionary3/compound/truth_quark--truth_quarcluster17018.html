truth quark

noun
a type of elementary particle (top quark)
Pronunciation: / truθ kwɑrk / (AmE), / truːθ kwɑːk / (BrE)
a hypothetical or previously hypothesized quark with a charge of +2/3 and a mass more than 100,000 times that of an electron; also known as the top quark in particle physics

Examples

the truth quark in physics
Some textbooks still refer to the truth quark instead of the more modern term, top quark.
The truth quark has a mass much greater than any other quark known to scientists.
The discovery of the truth quark was one of the major milestones in particle physics.
