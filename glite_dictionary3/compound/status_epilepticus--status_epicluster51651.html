status epilepticus

noun
prolonged epileptic seizures without recovery
Pronunciation: / ˌsteɪtəs ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌstɑːtəs ˌɛpɪˈlɛptɪkəs / (BrE)
A serious condition in which a person experiences continuous or repeated epileptic seizures without regaining consciousness between them, potentially causing brain damage or death.

Examples

status epilepticus emergency treatment
Doctors monitored her closely because status epilepticus is a life-threatening condition if left untreated.
Immediate medical treatment is critical in cases of status epilepticus to prevent permanent brain damage.
The patient was admitted to the emergency room after developing status epilepticus.
