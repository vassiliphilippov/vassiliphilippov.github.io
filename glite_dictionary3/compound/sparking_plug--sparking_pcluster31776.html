sparking plug

noun
engine device that ignites fuel-air mixture
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑrkɪŋ ˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkɪŋ ˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
An electrical device that fits into the cylinder head of an internal-combustion engine and ignites the fuel-air mixture by means of an electric spark; also known as a spark plug.

Examples

sparking plug in engine
Old cars often needed their sparking plugs cleaned or replaced to start efficiently.
The mechanic checked the sparking plug to diagnose the engine trouble.
Without a functioning sparking plug, the engine would not be able to run.
