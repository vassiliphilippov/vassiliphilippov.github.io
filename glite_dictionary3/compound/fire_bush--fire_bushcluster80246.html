fire bush

noun
ornamental shrub; foliage turns red in autumn
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
A densely branched Eurasian shrub known for its foliage that turns purple-red in autumn.

Examples

fire bush autumn leaves
Gardeners often plant fire bushes to add color to their landscapes in the fall.
In the park, a row of fire bush shrubs brightened the walkway with their brilliant foliage.
The leaves of the fire bush turn a vibrant red when autumn arrives.
