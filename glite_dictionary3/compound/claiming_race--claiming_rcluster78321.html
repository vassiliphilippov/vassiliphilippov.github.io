claiming race

noun
horse race where horses may be bought after the race
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪmɪŋ reɪs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪmɪŋ reɪs / (BrE)
A horse race in which each owner sets a price for which their horse may be purchased by others after the race.

Examples

a claiming race entry
In a claiming race, any horse can be purchased for the price set before the competition begins.
Many trainers use a claiming race to give their horses a better chance at winning.
She entered her horse in a claiming race in hopes of attracting buyers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – handicap race: a horse race in which different horses carry different weights to even the chances of winning
