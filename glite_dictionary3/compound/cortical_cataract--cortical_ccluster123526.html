cortical cataract

noun
cataract in the lens cortex
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtɪkəl ˈkætəˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkəl ˈkætərækt / (BrE)
a cataract characterized by clouding or opacity in the lens cortex of the eye

Examples

cortical cataract blurs vision
A cortical cataract often causes glare and problems with night driving before other symptoms appear.
Researchers are studying the risk factors that contribute to the development of a cortical cataract.
The surgeon explained that my mother had a cortical cataract affecting her vision in the left eye.
