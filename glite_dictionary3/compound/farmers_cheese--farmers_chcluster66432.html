farmer's cheese

noun
mild white cheese from soured skim milk
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑrmərz ˌtʃiz / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməz ˌtʃiːz / (BrE)
A mild white cheese made from the curds of soured skim milk, usually pressed to remove most of the whey.

Examples

slice of farmer's cheese
She spread farmer's cheese on her toast for a light breakfast.
The recipe calls for a cup of farmer's cheese to be mixed into the batter.
Traditionally, farmer's cheese is used in Eastern European dumplings.

  • synonym – cottage cheese: soft, lumpy cheese made from curds
