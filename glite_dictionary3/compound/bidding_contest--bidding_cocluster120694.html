bidding contest

noun
competition involving competing bids
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪdɪŋ ˌkɑntɛst / (AmE), / ˈbɪdɪŋ ˌkɒntɛst / (BrE)
a competition in which two or more parties make successive and higher monetary offers to win something, typically at an auction or for a contract

Examples

bidding contest for a contract
A bidding contest erupted as several companies vied for the construction contract.
Online platforms often spark intense bidding contests among interested buyers.
The painting was sold after a fierce bidding contest between two wealthy collectors.

  • synonym – auction: a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
