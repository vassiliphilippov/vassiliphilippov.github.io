parietal lobe

noun
area of the brain processing sensory input
Pronunciation: / pəˈraɪət̬l̩ loʊb / (AmE), / pəˈraɪətl̩ ləʊb / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex located near the upper sides of the brain, responsible for processing sensory information such as touch, temperature, and spatial awareness

Examples

the parietal lobe processes touch
Damage to the parietal lobe can affect a person's sense of touch and spatial awareness.
Researchers are studying how the parietal lobe contributes to complex tasks like reading maps.
The parietal lobe helps the brain interpret pain, pressure, and temperature.
