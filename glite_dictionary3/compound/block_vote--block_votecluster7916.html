block vote

noun
vote representing a group, not just an individual
Pronunciation: / blɑk voʊt / (AmE), / blɒk vəʊt / (BrE)
a vote that counts for the number of individuals represented by a delegate, used especially in assemblies or unions

Examples

block vote in a union
Delegates were given a block vote proportional to the number of members they represent.
Each union leader casts a block vote representing thousands of workers.
The committee decided to use a block vote system for allocating resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym – proxy vote: vote cast on behalf of another person
