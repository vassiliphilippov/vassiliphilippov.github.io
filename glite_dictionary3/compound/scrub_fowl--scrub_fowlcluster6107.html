scrub fowl

noun
Australasian mound-building bird
Pronunciation: / ˈskrʌb faʊl / (AmE), / ˈskrʌb faʊl / (BrE)
Any of several large-footed, short-winged birds of Australasia that build mounds of decaying vegetation to incubate their eggs.

Examples

scrub fowl nest mound
Naturalists studied how the scrub fowl maintains the temperature of its nesting site.
Several species of scrub fowl can be found in the rainforests of New Guinea.
The scrub fowl builds an elaborate mound to provide warmth for its eggs.
