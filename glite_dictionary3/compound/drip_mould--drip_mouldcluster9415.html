drip mould

noun
projection to deflect rainwater
Pronunciation: / drɪp moʊld / (AmE), / drɪp məʊld / (BrE)
An architectural projection from a cornice or sill designed to deflect rainwater away from an area below, such as above a window or doorway.

Examples

drip mould above window
Restoration of the historic building included repairing the original stone drip moulds over the doorway.
The mason installed a drip mould above each window to protect against rainwater seeping into the frame.
You can see the decorative drip mould running along the top of the facade.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dripstone: a stone projection that diverts rainwater from a building
