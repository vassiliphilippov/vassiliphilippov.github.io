smoke bomb

noun
device emitting thick smoke upon explosion
Pronunciation: / smoʊk bɑm / (AmE), / sməʊk bɒm / (BrE)
a device that releases a dense cloud of smoke when it explodes, typically used to obscure vision or mark a location

Examples

throw a smoke bomb
A colorful smoke bomb marked the start of the race.
During the demonstration, the protestors set off a smoke bomb to create a distraction.
The soldiers used a smoke bomb to conceal their movements from the enemy.
