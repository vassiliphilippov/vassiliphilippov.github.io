riddle canon

noun
type of cryptic musical canon
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdəl ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˈrɪdəl ˈkanən / (BrE)
A musical canon in which performers must solve cryptic symbols or clues to determine the timing of entries, popular in the 15th and 16th centuries.

Examples

riddle canon with hidden clues
One of the most famous Renaissance riddle canons uses a rebus to hide its solution.
Performers often enjoyed unraveling the mysteries hidden in a riddle canon during rehearsals.
The composer's riddle canon challenged musicians to decipher when to enter using only visual clues.
