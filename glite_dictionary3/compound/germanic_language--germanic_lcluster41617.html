Germanic language

noun
branch of Indo-European languages
Pronunciation: / dʒərˈmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒəˈmænɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family that includes languages such as English, German, Dutch, and the Scandinavian languages.

Examples

Germanic language family
English is a Germanic language, sharing roots with German and Dutch.
