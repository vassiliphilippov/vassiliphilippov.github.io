peripheral nervous system

noun
nerves outside the brain and spinal cord
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɪfɚəl ˈnɝvəs ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəl ˈnɜːvəs ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the part of the nervous system that consists of all the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord, responsible for transmitting signals between the central nervous system and the rest of the body

Examples

peripheral nervous system controls limbs
Certain viruses can attack the peripheral nervous system, leading to pain and numbness.
Damage to the peripheral nervous system can affect muscle control and sensation in the limbs.
Unlike the brain and spinal cord, the peripheral nervous system is not protected by bones.

Related Terms

  • antonym – central nervous system: the part of the nervous system consisting of the brain and spinal cord
