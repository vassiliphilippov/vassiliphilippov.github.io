order Galliformes

noun
order of ground-feeding birds
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ɡælɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɡælɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of heavy-bodied ground-feeding birds, including pheasants, turkeys, quails, chickens, and related species.

Examples

order Galliformes includes chickens
Scientists study the order Galliformes to better understand the evolution of ground-dwelling birds.
The order Galliformes includes domesticated species such as chickens and turkeys.
Wild members of the order Galliformes, like grouse and quail, are found across many continents.
