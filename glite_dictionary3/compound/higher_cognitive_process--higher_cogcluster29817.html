higher cognitive process

noun
advanced mental process using knowledge
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪər ˈkɑɡnɪtɪv ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
an advanced mental process that relies on acquired knowledge and uses it to reason, plan, solve problems, or understand complex ideas

Examples

complex higher cognitive process
Memory recall can sometimes be a higher cognitive process when it involves interpretation or analysis.
Problem-solving is considered a higher cognitive process that often requires combining different types of information.
Teaching critical thinking skills involves encouraging students to engage in higher cognitive processes.
