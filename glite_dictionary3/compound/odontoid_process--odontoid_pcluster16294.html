odontoid process

noun
toothlike projection on the second neck vertebra
Pronunciation: / oʊˈdɑːn.tɔɪd ˈprɑː.ses / (AmE), / əʊˈdɒn.tɔɪd ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a toothlike projection on the second cervical vertebra (axis) that serves as a pivot for the rotation of the first cervical vertebra (atlas) and the skull

Examples

odontoid process fracture
A fracture of the odontoid process can be severe and requires immediate medical attention.
Movement of the head from side to side depends on the odontoid process of the axis vertebra.
The X-ray revealed that the odontoid process was intact after the accident.
