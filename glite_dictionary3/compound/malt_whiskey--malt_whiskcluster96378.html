malt whiskey

noun
whiskey made from malted barley
Pronunciation: / mɔlt ˈwɪski / (AmE), / mɔːlt ˈwɪski / (BrE)
Whiskey distilled primarily from malted barley, especially as produced in Scotland using traditional pot stills.

Examples

a glass of malt whiskey
He prefers to drink malt whiskey because of its rich and complex flavors.
She toured the Highlands and sampled various types of malt whiskey along the way.
The distillery specializes in producing single malt whiskey using traditional Scottish methods.

Related Terms

  • antonym – blended whiskey: whiskey made by blending different types or batches
  • synonym – scotch whisky: whisky distilled in Scotland, traditionally from malted barley
