cy pres doctrine

noun
legal rule guiding intent in gifts or wills
Pronunciation: / siː preɪ ˈdɑːktrɪn / (AmE), / siː preɪ ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
a legal rule stating that when literal fulfillment of a gift or will is impossible, the donor's or testator's intent should be carried out as closely as possible

Examples

cy pres doctrine in wills
Lawyers often invoke the cy pres doctrine when interpreting a will that cannot be carried out as written.
The cy pres doctrine aims to honor a donor's intentions even when circumstances have changed.
When the original charitable purpose became impossible, the court applied the cy pres doctrine to redirect the funds.
