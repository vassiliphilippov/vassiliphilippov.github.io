negative correlation

noun
inverse relationship between variables
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˌkɒrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A relationship between two variables in which an increase in one variable is associated with a decrease in the other.

Examples

negative correlation between variables
In their data, a negative correlation indicated that as one variable rose, the other fell.
Scientists observed a negative correlation between exercise frequency and body fat percentage.
There was a negative correlation between hours of TV watched and students' exam scores.

Related Terms

  • antonym – positive correlation: a relationship in which both variables increase or decrease together
