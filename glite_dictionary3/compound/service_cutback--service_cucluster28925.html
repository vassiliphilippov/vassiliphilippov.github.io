service cutback

noun
a reduction in service provided
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːvɪs ˈkʌtbæk / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs ˈkʌtbæk / (BrE)
a reduction in the amount, frequency, or extent of a service provided, especially by an organization or public authority

Examples

service cutback on buses
Due to budget constraints, the city announced a service cutback on all bus routes.
Passengers complained when the airline implemented a service cutback during the winter months.
The hospital had to make a service cutback by closing its maternity ward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – service increase: the process of increasing the level of service provided
  • synonym – service reduction: the process of decreasing the level of service provided
