comb jelly

noun
marine animal with rows of cilia
Pronunciation: / koʊm ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / kəʊm ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
a small, transparent marine animal resembling a jellyfish, with eight rows of cilia for movement

Examples

glowing comb jelly
Children at the aquarium marveled at the shimmering comb jelly as it pulsed its cilia.
The biologist pointed out a comb jelly gliding gracefully through the water.
Unlike true jellyfish, a comb jelly moves by beating rows of tiny hair-like cilia.
