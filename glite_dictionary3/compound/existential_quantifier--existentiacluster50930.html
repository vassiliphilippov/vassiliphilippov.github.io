existential quantifier

noun
logic symbol asserting existence
Pronunciation: / ɛɡˈzɪstʃən(t)ʃəl ˈkwɑːntəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ɛɡˈzɪstənʃ(ə)l ˈkwɒntɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a symbol or phrase in logic that states there exists at least one thing for which a given proposition is true

Examples

existential quantifier in logic
A statement containing an existential quantifier claims the existence of at least one object meeting certain conditions.
In mathematics, the existential quantifier is used to assert that at least one solution exists to an equation.
The symbol ∃ is commonly used as the existential quantifier in formal logic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – universal quantifier: a logical quantifier asserting the truth of a proposition for all things concerned
