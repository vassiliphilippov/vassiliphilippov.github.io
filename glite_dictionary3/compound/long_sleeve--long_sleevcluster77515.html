long sleeve

noun
sleeve extending from shoulder to wrist
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ sliːv / (AmE), / lɒŋ sliːv / (BrE)
A sleeve that extends from the shoulder down to the wrist, as found in many shirts and dresses.

Examples

long sleeve shirt
He wore a long sleeve shirt to protect his arms from the sun.
Some people prefer long sleeves even in summer for added coverage.
The dress features elegant long sleeves perfect for colder weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym – short sleeve: a sleeve that does not cover the full length of the arm, stopping above the elbow or mid-arm
