nuclear cataract

noun
cataract affecting the center of the lens
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈkæt̬.əˌrækt / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈkæt.ərækt / (BrE)
a type of cataract that affects the central (nuclear) region of the eye's lens, causing it to become cloudy and impair vision

Examples

nuclear cataract affects vision
A nuclear cataract typically develops slowly and can significantly affect reading and night driving.
Surgery is often required to restore sight lost to a nuclear cataract.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed her with a nuclear cataract after she reported blurry vision.
