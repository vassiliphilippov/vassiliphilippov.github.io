lateral pass

noun
sideways or backward football pass
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl pæs / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl pɑːs / (BrE)
(in American football) a pass thrown by a player parallel to or behind the line of scrimmage, rather than forward.

Examples

lateral pass in football
A well-executed lateral pass caught the defense off guard and led to a touchdown.
Players practiced the lateral pass repeatedly to ensure their timing was perfect.
The quarterback made a risky lateral pass to the running back near the sideline.
