galvanic battery

noun
battery of voltaic cells
Pronunciation: / ɡælˈvænɪk ˈbætəri / (AmE), / ɡælˈvænɪk ˈbætəri / (BrE)
a battery made up of multiple voltaic cells connected in series or parallel to produce electricity from chemical reactions

Examples

galvanic battery produces electricity
A galvanic battery produces electricity through a chemical reaction between its electrodes and electrolyte.
Early telegraph systems relied on galvanic batteries for a consistent supply of electrical current.
The scientist connected several galvanic batteries to power the device during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – dry cell: a battery in which the electrolyte is in the form of a paste, not a liquid
  • synonym – voltaic pile: an early type of battery invented by Alessandro Volta
