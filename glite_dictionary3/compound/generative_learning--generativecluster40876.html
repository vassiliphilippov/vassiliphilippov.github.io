generative learning

noun
machine-learning paradigm that generates data
Pronunciation: / c8d925bn5a59t6av c8l5dd0n6a4b / (AmE), / c8d925bn59r59t6av c8l5cd0n6a4b / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a paradigm in which a model learns to represent or generate data (often by modelling the joint distribution), enabling tasks such as data synthesis, semi-supervised learning, or sample generation.

Examples

generative learning for data
Researchers used generative learning models to create realistic images for training autonomous vehicles.
By applying generative learning, the team augmented a small dataset with synthetic examples for testing.
Students compared discriminative methods with generative learning approaches for semi-supervised classification tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discriminative learning: a learning approach that focuses on predicting labels from inputs rather than modelling the full data distribution; often contrasted with generative learning
  • synonym – generative model: a model that learns the distribution of data and can produce new samples; often used interchangeably with generative learning in ML contexts
