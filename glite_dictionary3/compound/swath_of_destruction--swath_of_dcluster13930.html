swath of destruction

noun
area of widespread damage
Pronunciation: / swɑθ əv dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / swɒθ əv dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
a wide area where there has been serious damage or devastation, typically caused by a natural disaster, war, or other force

Examples

leave a swath of destruction
The invading army marched on, leaving a swath of destruction wherever they went.
The tornado carved a swath of destruction through several neighborhoods, flattening homes and uprooting trees.
Wildfires left a swath of destruction across the countryside, destroying everything in their path.
