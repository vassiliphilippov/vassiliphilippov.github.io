accessory vertebral vein

noun
a vein near the vertebral vein in the neck
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛsəri vɚˈtɛbrəl veɪn / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri vəˈtɛbrəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that runs alongside the vertebral vein, passing through the foramen of the transverse process of the seventh cervical vertebra and emptying into the brachiocephalic vein

Examples

accessory vertebral vein variation
Anatomical variations can cause the accessory vertebral vein to appear larger than usual.
The accessory vertebral vein is sometimes encountered during neck surgery.
The radiologist noted that the accessory vertebral vein drained directly into the brachiocephalic vein.
