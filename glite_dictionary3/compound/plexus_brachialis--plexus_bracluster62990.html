plexus brachialis

noun
network of nerves in the shoulder and arm
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛksəs brəˈkeɪli.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs brəˈkeɪlɪ.əs / (BrE)
a complex network of nerves formed by the lower cervical and first thoracic spinal nerves, supplying the shoulder, arm, and hand

Examples

plexus brachialis nerve injury
Injury to the plexus brachialis can result in weakness or paralysis of the arm.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage the plexus brachialis during operations near the shoulder.
