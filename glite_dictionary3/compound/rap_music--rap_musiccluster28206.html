rap music

noun
genre featuring rhythmic spoken lyrics
Pronunciation: / ræp ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ræp ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A style of popular music, originating among African-American communities in the 1980s and 1990s, in which rhythmic and rhyming speech (rapping) is spoken or chanted over a musical backing.

Examples

listen to rap music
Many young artists find inspiration in rap music and its use of poetic lyrics.
Some critics argue that rap music is a powerful form of social commentary.
Throughout the 1990s, rap music gained mainstream popularity around the world.
