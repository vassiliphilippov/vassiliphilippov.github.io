division Eubacteria

noun
major bacterial division, simple one-celled organisms
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən juːˈbæktɪriə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən juːˈbæktɪərɪə / (BrE)
A group of unicellular organisms classified as monerans, characterized by simple cells with rigid walls and, in motile types, flagella; considered a major division of bacteria in older taxonomies.
