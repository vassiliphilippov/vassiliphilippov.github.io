metacentric chromosome

noun
chromosome with equal arms (central centromere)
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk ˈkroʊməˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk ˈkrəʊməsəʊm / (BrE)
a chromosome in which the centromere is located near the center, resulting in two arms of approximately equal length

Examples

metacentric chromosome with equal arms
A metacentric chromosome has arms of almost the same length due to the central position of the centromere.
Scientists compared the behavior of metacentric chromosomes with acrocentric chromosomes during cell division.
The karyotype analysis revealed that the species possesses several metacentric chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acrocentric chromosome: chromosome with centromere near one end, producing one long and one short arm
  • degree lower – submetacentric chromosome: chromosome with centromere slightly off center, arms of unequal length
