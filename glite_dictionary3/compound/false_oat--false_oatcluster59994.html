false oat

noun
a grass resembling oat, used as forage
Pronunciation: / fɑls oʊt / (AmE), / fɔːls əʊt / (BrE)
a coarse perennial grass of Eurasian origin, resembling oat, commonly found on roadsides, rough grasslands, and in hay meadows; introduced in North America as forage

Examples

false oat grassland
Agriculturalists sometimes use false oat as a forage crop for livestock.

The field was dominated by false oat and other tall grasses.
