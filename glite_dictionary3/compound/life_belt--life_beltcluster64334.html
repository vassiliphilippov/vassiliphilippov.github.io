life belt

noun
safety belt for restraint
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪf bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf belt / (BrE)
A belt designed to attach a person to an object as a restraint, to prevent injury, especially during activities such as climbing, construction, or rescue operations.

Examples

life belt for climbing
In hazardous environments, a life belt can make the difference between life and death.
The climber secured his life belt before starting the ascent.
Workers must wear a life belt when operating at heights on the construction site.
