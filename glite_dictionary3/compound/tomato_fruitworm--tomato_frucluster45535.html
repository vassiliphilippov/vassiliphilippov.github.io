tomato fruitworm

noun
destructive moth larva damaging crops
Pronunciation: / təˈmeɪtoʊ ˈfruːtˌwɜːrm / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ ˈfruːtˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of a noctuid moth (Helicoverpa zea), highly destructive to crops such as tomatoes, corn, and cotton

Examples

tomato fruitworm damages crops
Many pesticides are developed to control the spread of the tomato fruitworm in agricultural fields.
Regular inspection can help detect tomato fruitworm infestations early.
The tomato fruitworm caused significant damage to the farmer's tomato crop this season.
