suppresser gene

noun
gene that inhibits another gene's expression
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛsər dʒin / (AmE), / səˈprɛsə dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that reduces or prevents the expression of another gene, especially a mutant gene.

Examples

suppresser gene mutation
A mutation in a suppresser gene can lead to unchecked cell growth and possibly cancer.
Researchers are studying how the suppresser gene interacts with oncogenes in tumor formation.
The presence of a suppresser gene can sometimes neutralize the effects of a harmful mutation.
