bid price

noun
price offered by buyer (finance)
Pronunciation: / bɪd praɪs / (AmE), / bɪd prʌɪs / (BrE)
(in finance) the price at which a buyer, especially a broker, is willing to purchase a particular security or asset.

Examples

bid price of a stock
Brokers update the bid price in real-time during volatile market sessions.
She placed her order at the current bid price to ensure a quick sale.
The bid price for the stock was significantly lower than the ask price.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ask price: the price at which a seller is willing to sell a security
