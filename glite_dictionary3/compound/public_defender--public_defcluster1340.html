public defender

noun
lawyer for people who can't afford one
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk dɪˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk dɪˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a lawyer appointed to represent people who cannot afford to hire a private attorney, especially in criminal cases, at public expense

Examples

public defender in court
After being arrested, she was assigned a public defender to handle her case.
Many public defenders work long hours to ensure everyone receives legal representation.
The public defender argued that his client deserved a fair trial despite limited resources.
