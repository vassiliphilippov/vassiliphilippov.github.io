railroad siding

noun
a rail track for storage or passing
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌrɑd ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrəʊd ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a short length of railway track connected to the main track, used for storing trains or letting other trains pass
