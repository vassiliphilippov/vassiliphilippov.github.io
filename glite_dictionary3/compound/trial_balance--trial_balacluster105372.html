trial balance

noun
accounting statement of debits and credits
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪ.əl ˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
a statement showing the equality of debits and credits in double-entry bookkeeping, prepared to check the accuracy of accounting records

Examples

trial balance report
A trial balance is an essential part of the double-entry bookkeeping process.
If the totals in the trial balance do not agree, it indicates an error in the ledger.
The accountant prepared a trial balance at the end of the quarter to ensure the books were accurate.
