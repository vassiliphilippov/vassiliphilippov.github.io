hepatitis delta

noun
severe type of hepatitis infection
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs ˈdɛltə / (AmE), / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs ˈdɛltə / (BrE)
A severe form of hepatitis caused by infection with the hepatitis D virus, usually occurring in people already infected with hepatitis B.

Examples

hepatitis delta infection
Chronic infection with hepatitis delta can lead to liver failure more rapidly than other forms of hepatitis.
The patient was diagnosed with hepatitis delta after testing positive for both hepatitis B and D viruses.
Treatment options for hepatitis delta are limited, making prevention especially important.
