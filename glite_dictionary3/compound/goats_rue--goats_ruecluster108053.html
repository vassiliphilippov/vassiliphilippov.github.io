goat's rue

noun
North American subshrub; source of rotenone
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊts ru / (AmE), / ɡəʊts ruː / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to eastern North America, with downy leaves and yellowish or rose flowers, known as a source of rotenone.

Examples

goat's rue natural insecticide
Farmers once used goat's rue as a natural insecticide due to its rotenone content.

The forest floor was dotted with patches of goat's rue in full bloom.
