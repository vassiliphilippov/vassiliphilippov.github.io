enteric bacteria

noun
bacteria living in animal intestines
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtɛrɪk bəˈktɪriə / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛrɪk bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
rod-shaped Gram-negative bacteria that typically live in the intestines of humans and other animals, either harmlessly or as pathogens

Examples

enteric bacteria in the gut
Certain enteric bacteria play a vital role in digestion and nutrient absorption.
Doctors are concerned about antibiotic resistance among enteric bacteria in hospitals.
When enteric bacteria escape the intestines, they can cause serious infections elsewhere in the body.

  • antonym – pathogen: organism causing disease
