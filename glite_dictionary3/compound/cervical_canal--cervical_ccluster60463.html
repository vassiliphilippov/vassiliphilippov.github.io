cervical canal

noun
passage in the cervix connecting uterus and vagina
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrvɪkəl kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl kəˈnal / (BrE)
the narrow, spindle-shaped passage within the cervix that connects the uterus to the vagina

Examples

the cervical canal during childbirth

Blockage of the cervical canal can affect a woman's fertility.
