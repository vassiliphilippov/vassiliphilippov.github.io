reflexive verb

noun
grammatical term; verb acting on itself
Pronunciation: / rəˈflɛksɪv vɝb /; / rəˈflɛksɪv vɜrb / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb whose subject and object are the same person or thing, usually used with a reflexive pronoun (for example, 'He prides himself')

Examples

'He prides himself on his work'—here 'prides' is used as a reflexive verb.
In some languages, every reflexive verb uses a special pronoun.
