enabling clause

noun
provision authorizing law's enforcement
Pronunciation: / ɪˈneɪblɪŋ klɔz / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪblɪŋ klɔːz / (BrE)
a provision in a law that gives officials the authority to implement or enforce the law

Examples

enabling clause in legislation
The court debated whether the enabling clause covered the new regulatory measures.
The legislation included an enabling clause that allowed the agency to issue penalties for violations.
Without an enabling clause, the law would lack the authority for officials to enforce its provisions.
