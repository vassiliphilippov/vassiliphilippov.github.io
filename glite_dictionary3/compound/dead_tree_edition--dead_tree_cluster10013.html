dead tree edition

noun
printed version of a book or magazine
Pronunciation: / dɛd tri ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / dɛd triː ɪˈdɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an informal term for the printed (paper) version of a book, magazine, or newspaper, as opposed to a digital or online version

Examples

dead tree edition of a book
Although he owns an e-reader, he prefers to buy the dead tree edition for his favorite authors.
Many readers still enjoy the feel and smell of a dead tree edition in their hands.
The school library carries both the online and dead tree edition textbooks for students.

Related Terms

  • antonym – electronic edition: a digital version of a publication
  • synonym – hard copy: a physical printed version of a document or publication
