paralinguistic communication

noun
meaning communicated by tone or manner of speaking
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.ə.lɪŋˈɡwɪs.tɪk kəˌmjuː.nəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpær.ə.lɪŋˈɡwɪs.tɪk kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Communication that conveys meaning through features of speech such as tone, pitch, and pace, rather than through words themselves.

Examples

paralinguistic communication in speech
Her paralinguistic communication made it clear she was upset, even though she said nothing directly.
In cross-cultural situations, paralinguistic communication can easily be misunderstood.
Teachers pay attention to both verbal and paralinguistic communication to understand their students' feelings.
