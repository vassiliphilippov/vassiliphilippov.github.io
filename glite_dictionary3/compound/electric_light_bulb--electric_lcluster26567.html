electric light bulb

noun
device producing light from electricity
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk laɪt bʌlb / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk laɪt bʌlb / (BrE)
An electric lamp with a transparent or translucent glass housing containing a wire filament that emits light when heated by electricity.

Examples

change an electric light bulb
He replaced the old electric light bulb with a more energy-efficient model.
The electric light bulb revolutionized how people illuminate their homes and workplaces.
Without an electric light bulb, the room was left in near total darkness.
