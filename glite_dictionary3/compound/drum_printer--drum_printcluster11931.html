drum printer

noun
line printer using a rotating drum
Pronunciation: / drʌm ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / drʌm ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a type of line printer in which characters for printing are mounted on a rotating drum, allowing rapid printing of full lines at a time

Examples

drum printer for reports
Early computer centers often used a drum printer to produce large volumes of reports.
Technicians had to replace the drum printer when it became obsolete in the 1980s.
The drum printer was known for its speed but also for being very noisy during operation.

  • synonym – line printer: a printer that prints an entire line of text at a time
