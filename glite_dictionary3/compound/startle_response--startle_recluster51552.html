startle response

noun
involuntary reflex to sudden stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑːrtl rɪˈspɑːns / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtl rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
an involuntary reflex reaction to a sudden unexpected stimulus, such as a loud noise, typically involving muscle movement and other bodily responses

Examples

strong startle response
Her startle response was unusually strong compared to most people.
Infants demonstrate a natural startle response when exposed to sudden noises.
The loud bang triggered his startle response, causing him to jump in his seat.
