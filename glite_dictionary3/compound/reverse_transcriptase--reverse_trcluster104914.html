reverse transcriptase

noun
enzyme making DNA from RNA, esp. in retroviruses
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝːs trænˈskrɪpteɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs trænˈskrɪpteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that synthesizes DNA from an RNA template, chiefly found in retroviruses

Examples

reverse transcriptase enzyme action
Development of drugs that inhibit reverse transcriptase has been crucial in the treatment of AIDS.
Scientists often use reverse transcriptase in the laboratory to synthesize complementary DNA from RNA samples.
The HIV virus uses reverse transcriptase to copy its RNA into DNA within human cells.
