fish ladder

noun
structure helping fish bypass dams
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ ˈlædɚ / (AmE), / fɪʃ ˈlædə / (BrE)
A structure consisting of a series of ascending pools or steps, designed to enable fish such as salmon to swim upstream past obstacles like dams.

Examples

fish ladder at a dam
A fish ladder was installed at the dam to allow salmon to reach their spawning grounds.
Engineers designed a new fish ladder to improve migration routes for trout upstream.
The construction of the fish ladder helped restore the local fish population.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fishway: an alternative term for fish ladder, passage for fish around obstacles
