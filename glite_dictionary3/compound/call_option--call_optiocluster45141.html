call option

noun
right to buy an asset at a set price
Pronunciation: / kɑl ˈɑpʃən /; / kɔl ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
a financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy an asset at a set price within a certain period

Examples
