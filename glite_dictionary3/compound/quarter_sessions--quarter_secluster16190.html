quarter sessions

noun
historical local criminal court
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrdər ˌsɛʃ.ənz / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə ˌsɛʃ.ənz / (BrE)
A local court in England and Wales that met four times a year to deal with criminal cases and some administrative matters, before being abolished in 1972.

Examples

quarter sessions court hearing
Before their abolition, quarter sessions handled a wide variety of local cases.

The thief was brought before the quarter sessions for trial.
