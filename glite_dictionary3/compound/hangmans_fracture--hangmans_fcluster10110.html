hangman's fracture

noun
fracture of the second cervical vertebra (C2)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæŋmænz ˌfræktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋmænz ˌfræktʃə / (BrE)
a fracture of the second cervical vertebra (C2) in the neck, caused by forceful hyperextension, often resulting from trauma such as a fall or accident

Emergency physicians are trained to recognize the symptoms of a hangman's fracture.
