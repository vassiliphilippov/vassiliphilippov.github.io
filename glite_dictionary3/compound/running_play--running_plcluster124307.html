running play

noun
ball-carrying play in American football
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnɪŋ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ pleɪ / (BrE)
(in American football) a play in which a player carries the ball forward by running rather than passing it.

Examples

American football running play
After the failed pass attempt, they switched to a running play for better control of the ball.
The coach called for a running play on third down to surprise the defense.
The team relied heavily on a running play to gain short but crucial yards.
