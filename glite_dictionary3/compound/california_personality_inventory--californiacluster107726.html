California Personality Inventory

noun
personality assessment test
Pronunciation: / kæ.lɪˈfɔr.ni.ə pərˌsɒ.nəˈlɛt̬.i ɪnˌvɛn.təˌri / (AmE), / kæl.ɪˈfɔː.ni.ə pɜː.səˈnæl.ə.ti ɪnˈvɛn.tər.i / (BrE)
a self-report psychological test consisting of yes-no questions, designed to assess personality traits such as dominance, self-acceptance, self-control, socialization, and achievement

Examples

take the California Personality Inventory test
Many psychologists use the California Personality Inventory to assess social and behavioral traits in research participants.
Scores from the California Personality Inventory provide insights into an individual's interpersonal style and self-control.
She completed the California Personality Inventory as part of her psychological evaluation for the study.
