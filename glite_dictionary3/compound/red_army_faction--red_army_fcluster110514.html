Red Army Faction

noun
German left-wing terrorist group (1970–1998)
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛd ˈɑrmi ˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈɑːmi ˈfækʃən / (BrE)
a left-wing terrorist organization active in Germany from the 1970s to the 1990s, known for violent acts and underground operations in pursuit of Marxist and Maoist goals; also called the Baader-Meinhof Group

Examples

the Red Army Faction attacked banks
After years of underground activity, the Red Army Faction officially disbanded in 1998.
Many books and films have explored the motives and actions of the Red Army Faction.
The Red Army Faction was responsible for a series of high-profile attacks in West Germany during the 1970s.
