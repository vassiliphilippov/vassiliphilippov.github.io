apparent horizon

noun
where sky and Earth appear to meet
Pronunciation: / əˈpɛrənt həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / əˈpærənt həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
the line in the distance where the sky and the Earth's surface seem to meet, as observed from a particular point

Examples

the apparent horizon at sea
Clouds gathered along the apparent horizon, hinting at a coming storm.
From the mountain peak, the apparent horizon stretched endlessly in all directions.
The ship disappeared below the apparent horizon as it sailed away.
