buffered aspirin

noun
aspirin combined with acid-neutralizing agent
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfərd ˈæsprɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌfəd ˈæsprɪn / (BrE)
A type of aspirin that is coated or combined with a substance that neutralizes stomach acid, reducing irritation when taken orally.

Examples

buffered aspirin for stomach comfort
Because her stomach was sensitive, she chose buffered aspirin instead of the regular kind.
Doctors sometimes recommend buffered aspirin to reduce stomach upset in patients needing frequent pain relief.
The pharmacist suggested taking buffered aspirin to help prevent gastric discomfort.
