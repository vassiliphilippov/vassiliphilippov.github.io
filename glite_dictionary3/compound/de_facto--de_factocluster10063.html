de facto

adjective, adverb
existing in fact, not officially
Pronunciation: / di ˈfæktoʊ / (AmE), / deɪ ˈfæktəʊ / (BrE)
existing or happening in fact, even if not officially recognized or established by law (in reality, actually)

Examples

de facto leader
Even though she held no official title, she acted as the de facto manager of the team.
The company was run by the CEO, but the de facto leader was the founder.
There is a de facto ban on smoking in most public places, even if it's not written in law.
Although he was never formally appointed, he acted as the de facto leader of the group.
English is the de facto language of business in many countries.
Segregation in schools was banned, but de facto segregation continued for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – actually: in fact; really
  • antonym – de jure: existing by law or by right; lawful
  • antonym – officially: formally or according to official rules
