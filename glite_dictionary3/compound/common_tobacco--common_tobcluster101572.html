common tobacco

noun
plant cultivated for tobacco production
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑmən təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmən təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
A tall South American plant (Nicotiana tabacum) cultivated for its large leaves, which are processed to make tobacco products.

Examples

common tobacco plant leaves
Common tobacco is grown extensively in many countries for the production of cigarettes and cigars.
Farmers prepared the soil to plant a new crop of common tobacco in the spring.
The broad leaves of common tobacco are harvested, cured, and later used in various tobacco products.
