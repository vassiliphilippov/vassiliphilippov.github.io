work study

noun
analysis of job methods for efficiency
Pronunciation: / wɝːk ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / wɜːk ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
the systematic analysis of a specific job to determine the most efficient method in terms of time and effort

Examples

work study analysis
A thorough work study can help identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies in the process.
Improvements suggested by the work study reduced production costs significantly.
The company hired a consultant to conduct a work study on the assembly line.

Related Terms

  • synonym – time and motion study: the analysis of the time and movements required to perform a task
