fire tower

noun
tower for spotting fires
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ˌtaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌtaʊə / (BrE)
a tall structure, often in a forest or wilderness area, where a person can observe the surroundings to watch for signs of fire

Examples

forest fire tower
From the top of the fire tower, she could see for miles across the forest.
Many fire towers have been replaced by aerial surveillance but some are still in use.
The ranger climbed the fire tower every morning to scan for smoke on the horizon.
