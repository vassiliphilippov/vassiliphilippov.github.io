imperative form

noun
verb form expressing commands or requests
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv fɔrm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv fɔːm / (BrE)
the verb form or mood used to give commands, requests, or instructions

Examples

imperative form of a verb
In the sentence "Please close the door," "close" is in the imperative form.
Teachers often use the imperative form when instructing students to do homework.
The imperative form is used in English to issue direct commands such as "Sit down!".

Related Terms

  • synonym – imperative mood: the mood expressing commands, requests, or instructions
  • antonym – indicative form: verb form or mood for stating facts
  • antonym – subjunctive form: verb form or mood expressing wishes, doubts, or hypotheticals
