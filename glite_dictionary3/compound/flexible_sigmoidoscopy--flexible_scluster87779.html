flexible sigmoidoscopy

noun
examination of the lower colon using a flexible scope
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛksəbəl sɪɡˌmɔɪdəˌskɑpi / (AmE), / ˈflɛksəbl sɪɡˌmɔɪdəˌskɒpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a flexible tube with a camera is inserted into the lower part of the colon to examine for abnormalities such as polyps

Examples

flexible sigmoidoscopy procedure
A flexible sigmoidoscopy can help detect early signs of bowel disease.
Preparation is required before undergoing a flexible sigmoidoscopy to ensure clear visualization.
The doctor recommended a flexible sigmoidoscopy to screen for colorectal cancer.
