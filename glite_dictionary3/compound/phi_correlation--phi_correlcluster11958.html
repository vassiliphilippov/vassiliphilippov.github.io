phi correlation

noun
statistical measure of binary association
Pronunciation: / faɪ ˌkɔːr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / faɪ ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to determine the strength and direction of association between two binary variables

Examples

phi correlation between variables
If both variables are truly dichotomous, the phi correlation can provide meaningful insights into their relationship.
Researchers calculated the phi correlation between smoking status and the presence of a specific disease.
The phi correlation is particularly useful when analyzing two binary variables, such as pass/fail or yes/no outcomes.
