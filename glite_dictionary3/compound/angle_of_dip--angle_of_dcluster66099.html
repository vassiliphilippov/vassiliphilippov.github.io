angle of dip

noun
angle of a magnetic needle from horizontal
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡəl əv dɪp / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl̩ əv dɪp / (BrE)
the angle that a magnetic needle makes with the plane of the horizon at a specific location, indicating the inclination of Earth's magnetic field

Examples

angle of dip of a compass
At the magnetic equator, the angle of dip is zero, indicating a horizontal field.
Geologists use the angle of dip to study variations in Earth's magnetic field.
He measured the angle of dip with a special instrument called a dip circle.
