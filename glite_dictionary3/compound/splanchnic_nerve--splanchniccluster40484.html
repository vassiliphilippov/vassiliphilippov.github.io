splanchnic nerve

noun
a nerve supplying internal organs
Pronunciation: / ˈsplæŋk.nɪk nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈsplæŋk.nɪk nɜːv / (BrE)
any of several nerves of the sympathetic nervous system that supply the internal organs and blood vessels.

Examples

splanchnic nerve damage
Damage to the splanchnic nerve can result in impaired function of several abdominal organs.
Stimulation of a splanchnic nerve can influence blood flow within the digestive tract.
The surgeon carefully avoided the splanchnic nerves during the procedure to prevent autonomic complications.
