star fort

noun
fort with angular projecting bastions
Pronunciation: / stɑr fɔrt / (AmE), / stɑː fɔːt / (BrE)
a fortification with projecting triangular bastions, usually five to eight, designed so that the sides cover each other, first developed in 15th-century Italy

Examples

old star fort walls
Historians studied the layout of the old star fort to understand Renaissance military architecture.
The museum exhibit included a scale model of a 16th-century star fort.
The town was once protected by a massive star fort with seven angular bastions.
