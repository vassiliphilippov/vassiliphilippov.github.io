group B

noun
a blood group with B antigen
Pronunciation: / ɡruːp biː / (AmE), / ɡruːp biː / (BrE)
the blood group whose red blood cells have the B antigen and whose plasma contains anti-A antibodies

Examples

group B blood type
Her blood type is group B, so she can donate to people with B or AB blood.
Only group B red cells can be used for this particular transfusion.
The doctor asked if anyone in the family had group B blood.

Related Terms

  • synonym – group A: the blood group whose red cells carry the A antigen
  • degree higher – group AB: the blood group whose red cells carry both the A and B antigens
  • degree lower – group O: the blood group whose red cells carry neither the A nor B antigens
