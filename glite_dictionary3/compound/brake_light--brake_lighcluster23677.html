brake light

noun
red rear light indicating braking on a vehicle
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪk ˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A red light at the rear of a motor vehicle that illuminates to signal when the brakes are applied.

Examples

car brake light
He was pulled over because his brake light wasn't working properly.
The mechanic replaced both brake lights during the inspection.
When you press the pedal, the brake light comes on to warn drivers behind you.
