ammonium salt

noun
a salt containing the ammonium ion NH₄⁺
Pronunciation: / əˈmoʊniəm sɔlt / (AmE), / əˈməʊniəm sɒlt / (BrE)
a salt containing the NH₄⁺ ion, typically formed by the reaction of ammonium hydroxide with an acid

Examples

ammonium salt fertilizer
Fertilizers often contain ammonium salt compounds to supply nitrogen to plants.
The chemist added acid to the solution to produce an ammonium salt as a precipitate.
When ammonium hydroxide reacts with hydrochloric acid, it forms the ammonium salt called ammonium chloride.
