sea lamprey

noun
a parasitic jawless fish, invasive in Great Lakes
Pronunciation: / si ˈlæmpri / (AmE), / si ˈlampri / (BrE)
a large parasitic jawless fish (Petromyzon marinus) native to the North Atlantic, known for attaching to and feeding on other fish; considered invasive in the Great Lakes where it harms native fish populations

Examples

sea lamprey attacks fish
A scientist examined a sea lamprey to study its impact on freshwater ecosystems.
In some countries, people eat sea lamprey as a delicacy despite its reputation in North America.
The invasive sea lamprey has caused significant declines in native fish populations in the Great Lakes.
