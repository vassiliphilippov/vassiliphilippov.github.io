press gang

noun
group forcibly recruiting for military service
Pronunciation: / prɛs ɡæŋ / (AmE), / prɛs ɡaŋ / (BrE)
a group of people with official authority to force civilians into service in the army or navy, especially in the past

Examples

press gang forced sailors
Many sailors were unwilling recruits, forced into service by the notorious press gang.
Stories about the press gang terrorizing coastal towns are common in British history.
The press gang would roam the streets looking for able-bodied men to conscript.
