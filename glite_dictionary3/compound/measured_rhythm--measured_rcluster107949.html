measured rhythm

noun
musical rhythm with structured time values
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛʒɚd ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒəd ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
A type of musical rhythm in which each note's duration is a multiple or fraction of a set time unit, without recurring regular accents.

Examples

measured rhythm in music
In Gregorian chant, singers often move between free and measured rhythm depending on the section.
The composer specified a measured rhythm to ensure every note receives its precise duration.
The piece opens with a measured rhythm that guides the performers through the unfamiliar melody.

  • antonym – free rhythm: rhythm with no strict time values or regular meter
