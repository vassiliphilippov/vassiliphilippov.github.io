voice of conscience

noun
inner moral guide
Pronunciation: / vɔɪs əv ˈkɑːn.ʃəns / (AmE), / vɔɪs əv ˈkɒn.ʃəns / (BrE)
an inner sense or feeling regarded as a guide to the rightness or wrongness of one's behavior; the moral sense within a person.

Examples

inner voice of conscience
He tried to ignore the voice of conscience that told him he had made a mistake.
In moments of temptation, his voice of conscience was louder than ever.
Whenever she faced a difficult choice, the voice of conscience urged her to do what was right.
