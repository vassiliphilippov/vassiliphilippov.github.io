calcined lime

noun
calcium oxide produced by heating limestone
Pronunciation: / kælˈsaɪnd laɪm / (AmE), / kælˈsaɪnd laɪm / (BrE)
a white caustic crystalline substance (calcium oxide) obtained by heating limestone, used chiefly in the production of calcium hydroxide and in various industrial processes

Examples

calcined lime in cement
After the limestone is heated, it is transformed into calcined lime for use in construction.
The chemical reaction requires calcined lime as a catalyst to proceed efficiently.
The production process involves adding water to calcined lime to create slaked lime.

Related Terms

  • synonym – quicklime: calcium oxide used industrially, also known as burnt lime
