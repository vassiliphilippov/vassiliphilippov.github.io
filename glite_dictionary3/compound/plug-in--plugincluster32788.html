plug-in

noun
circuit board for computer expansion
Pronunciation: / ˈplʌɡˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈplʌɡ.ɪn / (BrE)
a circuit board designed to be inserted into an expansion slot of a computer to add extra capabilities or features

Examples

plug-in card
I installed a new plug-in to add extra USB ports to my desktop computer.
If you want better sound quality, you can buy an audio plug-in for your PC.
The graphics card is a type of plug-in that enhances video performance.
