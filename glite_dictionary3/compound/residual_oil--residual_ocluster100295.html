residual oil

noun
oil left after petroleum distillation
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪdʒuəl ɔɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjuːəl ɔɪl / (BrE)
the oil that remains after the distillation process of petroleum, often used as fuel or for further processing

Examples

residual oil after refining
After processing crude oil, the refinery was left with large quantities of residual oil.
Environmental regulations now require better handling of residual oil to prevent pollution.
The ship's engine runs on heavy residual oil due to its high energy density.
