piano reduction

noun
music arranged for piano from ensemble
Pronunciation: / piˈænoʊ rɪˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / piˈanəʊ rɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
an arrangement of music originally written for an ensemble, condensed for performance on the piano, typically with the main elements reduced to a two-line staff

Examples

piano reduction of an orchestra piece
Many composers create a piano reduction to make their works accessible for solo practice.
She played from the piano reduction during the opera rehearsal.
The piano reduction of the symphony helped the choir rehearse without a full orchestra.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arrangement: a musical adaptation of a composition for a different instrument or ensemble
  • antonym – orchestration: the arrangement or scoring of music for orchestral performance
