skin patch

noun
adhesive wearable sensor for monitoring vitals
Pronunciation: / skɪn pætʃ / (AmE), / skɪn pætʃ / (BrE)
A small adhesive wearable device applied to the skin that contains sensors or electronics for monitoring physiological signals (for example, heart rate, temperature, or glucose) and recording or transmitting data; does not deliver medication.

Examples

monitoring skin patch
Hospitals use a skin patch that continuously monitors patients' vital signs.
She wore a small skin patch on her chest to measure heart rate during the test.
The research team developed disposable skin patches to monitor glucose without finger-prick blood tests.

Related Terms

  • synonym – biosensor patch: a skin-applied sensor patch that detects and reports biological signals such as glucose or ECG
  • synonym – wearable sensor: a device worn on the body that measures and records physiological or movement data
