recessive gene

noun
gene expressed only when inherited from both parents
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛsɪv dʒin / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛsɪv dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene whose effect is not shown unless two copies are present, since it is masked by a dominant gene

Examples

inherit a recessive gene
Both parents must carry the recessive gene for their child to inherit the trait.
Some inherited disorders result from a single recessive gene.
Two copies of the recessive gene are needed for blue eyes to appear.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dominant gene: a gene that expresses its effect even if only one copy is present
