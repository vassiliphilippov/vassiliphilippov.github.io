fire trench

noun
military trench for delivering fire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ˌtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
a trench, especially constructed in warfare, designed to allow soldiers to deliver small-arms fire while providing protection from enemy fire

Examples

soldiers in a fire trench
During the assault, each fire trench was carefully manned by a squad of riflemen.
The engineers spent the night digging a new fire trench along the front line.
The soldiers took cover in the fire trench as bullets whistled overhead.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foxhole: a small pit used for cover from enemy fire
  • synonym – trench: a long, narrow ditch, especially in a military context
