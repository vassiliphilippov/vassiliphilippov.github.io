February 2

proper noun
the second day of February; Groundhog Day
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛb.ɹuˌɛɹ.i ˈsɛk.ənd / (AmE), / ˌfɛb.jʊə.ˌri ˈsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
The second day of February; in North America, traditionally observed as Groundhog Day, when folklore says that a groundhog's behavior predicts the length of winter.

Examples

February 2 Groundhog Day
On February 2, many Americans celebrate Groundhog Day by watching the groundhog emerge from its burrow.
Our school hosts an annual assembly on February 2 to explain the history of Groundhog Day.
The weather on February 2 is said to predict the length of the remaining winter.
