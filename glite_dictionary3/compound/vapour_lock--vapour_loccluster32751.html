vapour lock

noun
fuel flow stoppage caused by gas bubbles
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpər lɑk / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə lɒk / (BrE)
A condition in an engine's fuel system in which gas bubbles form in the fuel line, blocking the flow of fuel and causing engine stoppage, especially in hot weather.

Examples

vapour lock in engine
Drivers often experience vapour lock when operating vehicles in high temperatures.
Mechanics recommend insulating fuel lines to prevent vapour lock during hot weather.
The car engine stalled on the highway because of vapour lock in the fuel line.

Related Terms

  • synonym – airlock: a stoppage of liquid flow caused by trapped air in a pipe or tube
