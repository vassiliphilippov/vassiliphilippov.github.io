parallel processing

noun
simultaneous computing by multiple processors
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl ˈproʊsɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpærəlɛl ˈprəʊsɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
The simultaneous use of two or more processors or processing units to perform tasks more quickly.

Examples

parallel processing speeds tasks
By using parallel processing, the software can analyze massive datasets in much less time.
Modern graphics cards are designed for parallel processing to render images efficiently.
The supercomputer relies on parallel processing to solve complex scientific problems quickly.
