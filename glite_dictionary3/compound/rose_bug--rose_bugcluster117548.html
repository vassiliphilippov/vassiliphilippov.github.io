rose bug

noun
North American beetle pest of garden plants
Pronunciation: / roʊz bʌɡ / (AmE), / rəʊz bʌɡ / (BrE)
a common North American beetle whose larvae feed on roots and whose adults feed on the leaves and flowers of rose bushes, apple trees, grape vines, and similar plants

Examples

rose bug on flowers
Many gardeners handpick rose bugs from their plants to protect their flowers.
The garden was infested with rose bugs that were eating the leaves off the rose bushes.
The larvae of the rose bug can cause serious damage to the roots of fruit trees.
