tug-of-war

noun
struggle between evenly matched groups
Pronunciation: / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔɹ / (AmE), / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a hard struggle between two evenly matched people or groups, often over power, control, or an important outcome

Examples

political tug-of-war
Negotiations became a mental tug-of-war as neither side wanted to compromise.
The debate turned into a political tug-of-war that lasted for months.
There was a constant tug-of-war between management and staff over working hours.
