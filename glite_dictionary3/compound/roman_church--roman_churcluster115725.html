Roman Church

noun
the Christian Church led by the Pope in Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊmən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the Christian Church based in the Vatican, presided over by the Pope and a hierarchy of bishops; also known as the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

the Roman Church in Rome
He converted to the Roman Church after years of spiritual searching.
Many global traditions can trace their roots back to the Roman Church.
The Roman Church played a significant role in shaping European history.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Eastern Orthodox Church: the main Christian Church in Eastern Europe and parts of the Middle East, not under the Pope
  • synonym – Roman Catholic Church: the main Christian Church with the Pope as leader, based in the Vatican
