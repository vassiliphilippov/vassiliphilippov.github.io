headlong into

adverb
rushing eagerly and recklessly into
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlɒŋ ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
rushing into something eagerly and without considering the risks or consequences

Examples

rush headlong into trouble
He jumped headlong into the debate without knowing all the facts.
She often throws herself headlong into projects, regardless of the possible difficulties.
The company dived headlong into new markets, hoping to outpace its competitors.
