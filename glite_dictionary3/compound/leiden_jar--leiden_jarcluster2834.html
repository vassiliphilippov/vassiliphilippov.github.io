Leiden jar

noun
historical electrostatic capacitor
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪdən dʒɑr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪdən dʒɑː / (BrE)
A device used to store static electricity, consisting of a glass jar with metal foil on the inside and outside, used historically as a capacitor.

Examples

Leiden jar stores electricity
A demonstration of a charged Leiden jar can create impressive sparks when discharged.
Early scientists used the Leiden jar to conduct experiments with static electricity.
The Leiden jar played a key role in the discovery of electricity in the 18th century.
