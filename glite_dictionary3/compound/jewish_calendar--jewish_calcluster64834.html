Jewish calendar

noun
calendar used in Judaism, lunar-solar system
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuɪʃ ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːɪʃ ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the calendar system traditionally used in Judaism, with years counted from 3761 BC and months based on lunar cycles, incorporating periodic leap years to align with the solar year

Examples

Jewish calendar holidays
Dates on the Jewish calendar do not correspond exactly with those on the Gregorian calendar.
Rosh Hashanah marks the beginning of the new year according to the Jewish calendar.
The holidays of Passover and Hanukkah are determined by the Jewish calendar.
