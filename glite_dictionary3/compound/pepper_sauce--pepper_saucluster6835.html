pepper sauce

noun
sauce made with black pepper, often served with meat
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛpər sɔs / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə sɔːs / (BrE)
A sauce made with an abundance of black pepper, often served with meat such as venison, and typically containing brown sauce, sautéed vegetables, trimmings, and marinade.

Examples

pepper sauce with steak
A rich pepper sauce can enhance the taste of roasted venison or beef.
He poured a generous amount of pepper sauce over his steak for extra flavor.
The restaurant is famous for its wild game dishes served with homemade pepper sauce.
