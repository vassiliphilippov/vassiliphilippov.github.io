protective covering

noun
tough natural outer layer of an organism
Pronunciation: / prəˈtɛktɪv ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktɪv ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the tough natural outer layer that shields or protects an organism from injury, infection, or adverse conditions

Examples

animal protective covering
Cacti have a thick protective covering to reduce water loss in desert climates.
The beetle's shell acts as a protective covering against damage and dehydration.
The turtle's protective covering shields it from predators and harsh environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym – membrane: a thin, flexible layer acting as a boundary, lining, or protection in organisms
  • synonym – shell: the hard outer covering of some animals, such as mollusks or eggs
  • synonym – skin: the outer protective layer of a living organism, especially a vertebrate
