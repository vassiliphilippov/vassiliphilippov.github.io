tract house

noun
house in a group of similar suburban houses
Pronunciation: / trækt haʊs / (AmE), / trækt haʊs / (BrE)
a house that is one of many with a similar design built together on a large area of land, especially in suburban developments

Examples

new tract house development
After the war, thousands of tract houses were built to accommodate the growing population.
Rows of identical tract houses lined the quiet streets.
They moved into a new tract house in the expanding suburb.
