fetal circulation

noun
blood flow system in a fetus
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːtəl sɜrkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəl sɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the system of blood vessels and structures that direct blood flow in a developing fetus, differing from postnatal circulation

Examples

fetal circulation in the womb
Doctors can assess fetal circulation using advanced ultrasound techniques to check for abnormalities.
During pregnancy, fetal circulation ensures that oxygen and nutrients reach the developing organs.
Unlike adults, fetal circulation bypasses the lungs via the ductus arteriosus and foramen ovale.
