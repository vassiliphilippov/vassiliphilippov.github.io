epicyclic gear train

noun
gear system with rotating axes
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈsaɪklɪk ɡɪr treɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsɪklɪk ɡɪə treɪn / (BrE)
a system of gears in which at least one gear rotates around a central gear while simultaneously spinning on its own axis; commonly used in machinery and vehicles for compact power transmission.

Examples

epicyclic gear train in a car
An epicyclic gear train allows for more compact and efficient designs in modern robotics.
Maintenance of an epicyclic gear train requires precise alignment to prevent mechanical failures.
The automatic transmission relies on an epicyclic gear train to change gear ratios smoothly.
