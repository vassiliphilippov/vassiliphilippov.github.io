correctional rehabilitation

noun
treatment and reform of convicted offenders
Pronunciation: / kəˌrɛkʃənl ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌrɛkʃənəl ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of providing treatment, education, or supervision as part of penal custody, probation, or parole intended to reform convicted offenders

Examples

correctional rehabilitation program
Many experts believe that correctional rehabilitation programs are more effective than punitive measures alone.
The prison system focuses on correctional rehabilitation to reduce reoffending rates.
Through correctional rehabilitation, offenders are given a chance to reintegrate into society.
