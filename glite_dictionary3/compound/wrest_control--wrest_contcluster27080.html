to wrest control

verb
seize control by force or effort
Pronunciation: / rɛst kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / rɛst kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to seize control from someone else by force or determined effort

Examples

to wrest control from rivals
After months of negotiation, she finally wrested control away from her rivals.
The new CEO plans to wrest control of the company from the current board of directors.
The rebels are attempting to wrest control of the city from government forces.

Related Terms

  • antonym – relinquish control: to give up control willingly
  • synonym – seize control: to take control suddenly or by force
