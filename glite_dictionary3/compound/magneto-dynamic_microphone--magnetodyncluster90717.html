magneto-dynamic microphone

noun
microphone using electromagnetic induction
Pronunciation: / mægˌniːtoʊ daɪˈnæmɪk ˌmaɪkrəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / mægˌniːtəʊ daɪˈnæmɪk ˌmaɪkrəˌfəʊn / (BrE)
A microphone that converts sound into an electrical signal using electromagnetic induction, commonly known as a dynamic microphone.

Examples

magneto-dynamic microphone for live music
A magneto-dynamic microphone is often favored for its durability and ability to handle high sound pressure levels.
Many vocalists prefer using a magneto-dynamic microphone because it performs well on stage.
The sound engineer set up a magneto-dynamic microphone for the live recording.
