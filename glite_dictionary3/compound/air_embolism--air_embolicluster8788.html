air embolism

noun
air bubbles in blood causing pain, often from pressure change
Pronunciation: / ɛr ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / eə ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a serious medical condition where air bubbles enter the bloodstream and block blood vessels, often caused by surgery, injections, or rapid changes in pressure (such as during diving)

Examples

air embolism after surgery
Divers are particularly at risk of developing an air embolism during rapid ascent.
Proper training is essential to prevent air embolism when working in pressurized environments.
The patient experienced severe chest pain due to an air embolism after the surgical procedure.
An air embolism can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
During surgery, care is taken to avoid the introduction of an air embolism into the bloodstream.
The diver was rushed to the hospital after developing an air embolism during a rapid ascent.
