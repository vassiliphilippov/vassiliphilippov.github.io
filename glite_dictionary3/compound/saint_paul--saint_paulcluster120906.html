Saint Paul

proper noun
Christian apostle and missionary
Pronunciation: / seɪnt pɔl / (AmE), / seɪnt pɔːl / (BrE)
A Christian missionary and apostle, originally known as Saul, who spread Christianity among the Gentiles and authored several Epistles in the New Testament.

Examples

Saint Paul the Apostle
Saint Paul traveled extensively to spread the teachings of Christianity throughout the Roman Empire.
Before his conversion, Saint Paul was known as Saul and persecuted Christians.
Many of the Epistles in the New Testament are attributed to Saint Paul.
