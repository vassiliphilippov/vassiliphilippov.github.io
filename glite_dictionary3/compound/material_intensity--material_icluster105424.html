material intensity

noun
amount of material resources used per production unit
Pronunciation: / məˈtɪriəl ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəl ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (BrE)
the quantity of material resources consumed per unit of production in a manufacturing or economic process

Examples

material intensity in production
By increasing recycling rates, industries can decrease their material intensity and environmental impact.
Government policies are aimed at lowering the material intensity of the national economy.
The company reduced its material intensity by adopting more efficient manufacturing processes.
