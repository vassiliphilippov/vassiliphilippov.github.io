absorption indicator

noun
chemical indicator in precipitation reactions
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔːrpʃən ˌɪndəˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a chemical indicator used to signal the end point in reactions involving precipitation, especially by changing color when adsorption occurs on the precipitate's surface

Examples

absorption indicator changes color
In analytical chemistry, an absorption indicator is often used in titrations involving silver halides.
The chemist added an absorption indicator to the solution to determine the point at which precipitation occurred.
Without an absorption indicator, it would be difficult to visually identify the endpoint of the reaction.
