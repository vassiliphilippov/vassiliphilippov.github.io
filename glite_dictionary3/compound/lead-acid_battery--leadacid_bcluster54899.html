lead-acid battery

noun
type of rechargeable battery with lead and acid
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛd ˌæsɪd ˈbætəri / (AmE), / ˈlɛd ˌæsɪd ˈbatəri / (BrE)
A type of rechargeable battery with lead electrodes and dilute sulphuric acid as the electrolyte; commonly used in vehicles and for backup power.

Examples

lead-acid battery in a car
Many solar energy systems use a lead-acid battery bank for energy storage.
Proper maintenance can extend the life of a lead-acid battery in backup power supplies.
