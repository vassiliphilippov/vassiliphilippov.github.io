in the short run

adverb
for a brief or limited time
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə ʃɔrt rʌn / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ʃɔːt rʌn / (BrE)
for a limited or brief period of time in the future; not considering long-term effects or consequences

Examples

in the short run prices change
In the short run, rising gas prices may reduce car travel.
In the short run, the company expects lower profits due to higher costs.
These changes might help in the short run, but a permanent solution is needed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – in the long run: over a long period of time; considering long-term effects
