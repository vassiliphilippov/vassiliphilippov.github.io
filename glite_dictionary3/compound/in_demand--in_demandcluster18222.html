in demand

adjective
highly wanted or sought after
Pronunciation: / ɪn dɪˈmænd / (AmE), / ɪn dɪˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
wanted by many people; greatly sought after or popular at a given time.

Examples

in demand for jobs
After the movie's release, the actor was suddenly in demand for major film roles.
Qualified nurses are always in demand in hospitals around the world.
This smartphone is in demand because of its cutting-edge features and stylish design.

Related Terms

  • antonym – out of demand: not wanted or sought after, the opposite of 'in demand'
  • synonym – popular: liked or enjoyed by many people
