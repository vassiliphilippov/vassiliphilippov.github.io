Divine Office

noun
set daily prayers in Christian tradition
Pronunciation: / dəˈvaɪn ˈɑːfɪs / (AmE), / dɪˌvaɪn ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
the set of prescribed prayers recited daily at fixed hours by clergy and some laypersons in the Christian tradition, especially in the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

pray the Divine Office
A priest is required to pray the Divine Office each day as part of his duties.
Laypeople are increasingly participating in the Divine Office, especially during Lent.
Many monasteries observe the Divine Office at regular intervals throughout the day and night.
