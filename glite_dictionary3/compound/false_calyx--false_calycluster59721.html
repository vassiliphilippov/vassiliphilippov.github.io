false calyx

noun
a group of bracts resembling a calyx
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈkeɪlɪks / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈkeɪlɪks / (BrE)
A group of bracts resembling a calyx, found in some flowers such as carnations or hibiscus.

Examples

false calyx in hibiscus
Botanists can distinguish the false calyx from the actual calyx by examining the flower's structure.
In carnations, the false calyx is formed by bracts that enclose the true calyx.
The hibiscus flower displays a distinctive false calyx beneath its petals.

  • synonym – calyx: the sepals of a flower, collectively forming the outer floral envelope
