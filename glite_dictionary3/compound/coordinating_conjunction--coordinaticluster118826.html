coordinating conjunction

noun
a conjunction joining equal grammatical elements
Pronunciation: / koʊˌɔrdɪˈneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən /; / koʊˌɔːrdəˌneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən /; / kəʊˌɔːdɪˌneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a word, such as 'and', 'but', or 'or', that joins words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance or rank

Examples

coordinating conjunction like 'and'
'Or' is often used as a coordinating conjunction to present alternatives.
In the sentence 'I wanted to go, but it was raining,' 'but' is a coordinating conjunction.
You should use a coordinating conjunction to join two independent clauses.
He brought snacks and drinks, linking the items with a coordinating conjunction.
Use a coordinating conjunction like 'and' to connect the two clauses.
When joining main clauses, a coordinating conjunction such as 'but' is used.
