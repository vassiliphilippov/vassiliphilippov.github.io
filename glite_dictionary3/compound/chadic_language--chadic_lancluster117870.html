Chadic language

noun
a family of Afroasiatic languages spoken around Lake Chad
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃædɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃadɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of a family of Afroasiatic tonal languages, mostly with two tones, spoken in regions west and south of Lake Chad in north-central Africa

Examples

Chadic language spoken in Nigeria
Hausa is the most widely spoken Chadic language in West Africa.
Linguists have documented hundreds of Chadic languages in Cameroon and Nigeria.
The Chadic language family is known for its distinctive use of tones.
