antagonistic muscle

noun
a muscle that opposes another's action
Pronunciation: / ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle that acts in opposition to another muscle, usually to control or moderate movement at a joint

Examples

antagonistic muscle controls movement
Flexing your arm involves the cooperation of the agonist and the antagonistic muscle.
Physical therapy often targets both the primary mover and its antagonistic muscle for balanced rehabilitation.
When the biceps contracts, the triceps, as an antagonistic muscle, relaxes to allow smooth movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agonist muscle: a muscle that causes movement by contracting, opposed by the antagonist
