even-pinnate leaf

noun
pinnate leaf with paired leaflets at apex
Pronunciation: / ˈiːvən pɪˈneɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈiːvən pɪˈneɪt liːf / (BrE)
A pinnate leaf ending with a pair of leaflets at the tip rather than a single terminal leaflet.

Examples

an even-pinnate leaf pair
Identifying an even-pinnate leaf can help distinguish between similar tree species.
The compound leaves of the honey locust are of the even-pinnate leaf type.
Unlike the odd-pinnate forms, an even-pinnate leaf has no terminal leaflet.
