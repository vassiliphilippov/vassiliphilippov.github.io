sexual reproduction

noun
biological reproduction by gamete fusion
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃuəl ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the process of reproduction involving the union or fusion of male and female gametes, resulting in offspring with genetic material from both parents

Examples

sexual reproduction in animals
Most animals rely on sexual reproduction to create genetic diversity in their populations.
Plants such as flowering species often use sexual reproduction to produce seeds.
Unlike bacteria, which typically reproduce asexually, humans require sexual reproduction to have offspring.

Related Terms

  • antonym – asexual reproduction: reproduction without the fusion of gametes
