physiological reaction

noun
body's automatic response to a stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkəl riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl riˈækʃən / (BrE)
an automatic, instinctive, and unlearned response of the body to a stimulus

Examples

a physiological reaction to stress
A sudden loud noise can trigger a physiological reaction such as increased heart rate.
Sweating is a common physiological reaction when the body is overheated.
The doctor explained that the rash was a physiological reaction to the medication.
