bullock block

noun
pulley-block at a topmast head (nautical)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlək blɑk / (AmE), / ˈbʊlək blɒk / (BrE)
a type of pulley-block fitted at the head of a ship's topmast, used for handling lines or rigging

Examples

bullock block on mast
The crew attached the halyard to the bullock block at the top of the mast.
The old bullock block needed to be replaced before raising the new sails.
With a creak, the rope ran smoothly through the bullock block as the sailors worked.
