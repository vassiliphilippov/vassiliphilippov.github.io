mountain fever

noun
disease transmitted by ticks
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊntn ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
a disease caused by rickettsial bacteria and transmitted by the bite of wood ticks, also known as Rocky Mountain spotted fever

Examples

mountain fever symptoms appear
After days of fever and rash, she was diagnosed with mountain fever by her physician.
Doctors warned campers to protect themselves from ticks to avoid mountain fever.
Several cases of mountain fever were reported in the region during the summer.
