reentrant polygon

noun
polygon with at least one reentrant angle
Pronunciation: / riˈɛn.trənt ˈpɑː.liˌgɑn / (AmE), / riˈɛn.trənt ˈpɒl.ɪˌɡɒn / (BrE)
A polygon that has one or more interior angles greater than 180 degrees (reentrant angles), resulting in at least one indentation in its shape.

Examples

reentrant polygon with indentation
A reentrant polygon always has at least one angle that is greater than 180 degrees.
A star-shaped figure is an example of a reentrant polygon because it has inward-pointing angles.
Designers must account for stress concentrations in any reentrant polygon used in engineering structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – concave polygon: a polygon with at least one interior angle greater than 180 degrees
  • antonym – convex polygon: a polygon in which all interior angles are less than 180 degrees
