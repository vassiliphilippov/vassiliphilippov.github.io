late-blooming

adjective
maturing or succeeding late
Pronunciation: / c8leeat ccblud0m6a4b / (AmE), / c8leeat ccblud0m6a4b / (BrE)
(figurative) developing, maturing, or achieving success later in life or later than is typical.

Examples

late-blooming artist
After decades of small exhibitions, the painter became a late-blooming star at fifty.
Their project was late-blooming, finally attracting users and funding five years after launch.
He was a late-blooming athlete who reached his peak performance in his thirties.

Related Terms
