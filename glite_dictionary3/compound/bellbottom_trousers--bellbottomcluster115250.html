bellbottom trousers

noun
flared-leg trousers; 1960s/70s fashion
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛlˌbɑtəm ˈtraʊzɚz / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌbɒtəm ˈtraʊzəz / (BrE)
Trousers with legs that flare widely below the knee, especially as worn by sailors and popularized as a fashion trend in the 1960s and 1970s.

Examples

bellbottom trousers with flared legs
In the 1970s, bellbottom trousers became a symbol of youthful rebellion and disco fashion.

The navy issued bellbottom trousers as part of the sailors' uniforms for decades.
