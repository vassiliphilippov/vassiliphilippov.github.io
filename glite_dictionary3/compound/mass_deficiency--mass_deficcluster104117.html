mass deficiency

noun
difference between nucleus mass and its particles' total mass
Pronunciation: / mæs dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / mɑːs dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
the difference between the mass of an atomic nucleus and the sum of the masses of its separate protons and neutrons, a result of binding energy.

Examples

mass deficiency in nucleus
In nuclear reactions, the mass deficiency corresponds to the energy released according to Einstein's equation E=mc².
Scientists calculate the mass deficiency to determine the nuclear binding energy of isotopes.
The concept of mass deficiency is essential for understanding why nuclei are more stable than their individual protons and neutrons.

Related Terms

  • synonym – binding energy: energy that holds the nucleus together, related to the mass deficiency
