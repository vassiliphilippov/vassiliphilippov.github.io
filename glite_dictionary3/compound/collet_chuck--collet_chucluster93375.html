collet chuck

noun
cone-shaped lathe clamp
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlət ˌtʃʌk / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪt ˌtʃʌk / (BrE)
A cone-shaped clamp used to securely hold cylindrical workpieces in a lathe or similar machine.

Examples

collet chuck holds rod
A collet chuck provides greater precision than a standard three-jaw chuck.
He replaced the old chuck with a new collet chuck for improved accuracy during milling.
The machinist tightened the collet chuck to hold the metal rod firmly in place.
