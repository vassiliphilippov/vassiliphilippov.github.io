pocket veto

noun
indirect veto by retaining a bill unsigned
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt ˈviːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˈviːtəʊ / (BrE)
an indirect veto of a legislative bill by the president or a governor by retaining the bill unsigned until it is too late for it to be dealt with during the legislative session

Examples

pocket veto a bill
Because the legislature was out of session, the governor was able to exercise a pocket veto.
Legal scholars debated whether the pocket veto applied when Congress had scheduled a brief recess.
The president used a pocket veto to block the bill from becoming law after Congress adjourned.
