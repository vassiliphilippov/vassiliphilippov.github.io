batter's box

noun
marked area where batter stands in baseball
Pronunciation: / ˈbædərz ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈbætəz ˌbɒks / (BrE)
the marked area on either side of home plate on a baseball field in which the batter stands while batting

Examples

in the batter's box waiting
He stepped confidently into the batter's box to face the next pitch.
Rain had washed away part of the chalk outline for the batter's box.
The umpire checked to see if the player was still in the batter's box during the swing.
