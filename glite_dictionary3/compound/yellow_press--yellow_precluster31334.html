yellow press

noun
sensationalist journalism
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ prɛs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ prɛs / (BrE)
Journalism that presents sensationalized or exaggerated stories, often for profit and increased readership, typically with little regard for accuracy.

Examples

yellow press stories
Many historians blame the yellow press for stirring up public emotions before the conflict.
She accused the yellow press of invasion of privacy in their latest coverage.
The yellow press often prioritizes shocking headlines over factual accuracy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – quality press: reputable newspapers committed to factual reporting and in-depth analysis
  • synonym – tabloid: popular newspaper focusing on sensationalism and entertainment
