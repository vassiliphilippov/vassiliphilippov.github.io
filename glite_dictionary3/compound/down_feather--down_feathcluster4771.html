down feather

noun
soft, fine feather of a bird
Pronunciation: / daʊn ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / daʊn ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
A very soft, fine feather found under the outer feathers of birds, used especially for insulation and padding.

Examples

down feather pillow
Manufacturers often use down feathers for high-quality pillows and bedding.
The jacket is filled with down feathers to keep you warm during winter.
Young ducklings are covered in down feathers before their adult plumage grows in.
