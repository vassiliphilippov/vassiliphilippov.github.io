poison ash

noun
American swamp shrub with irritating oil
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzən æʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən æʃ / (BrE)
A smooth American swamp shrub with pinnate leaves, greenish flowers, and greenish-white berries, which produces an irritating oil that can cause skin reactions.

Examples

poison ash shrub leaves
Botanists warn that poison ash can be mistaken for other harmless swamp plants.
The hiker unwittingly brushed against poison ash and developed a rash the next day.
You should learn to identify poison ash before walking in wetland areas.
