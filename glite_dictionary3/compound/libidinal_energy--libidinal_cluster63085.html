libidinal energy

noun
psychic energy generated by the libido
Pronunciation: / lɪˈbɪdənəl ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / lɪˈbɪdɪnəl ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the psychic energy generated by the libido that drives mental processes and behavior, especially those related to desire and motivation.

Examples

libidinal energy drives behavior
According to Freud, much unconscious behavior is powered by libidinal energy.
Jung expanded the meaning of libidinal energy to include all creative and psychic forces.
The therapist explained that blocking libidinal energy can lead to psychological distress.
