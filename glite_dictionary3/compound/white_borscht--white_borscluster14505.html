white borscht

noun
Polish sour soup made with wheat flour
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈbɔrʒt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈbɔːʃt / (BrE)
A traditional Polish soup made with wheat flour and fermented grains, typically served sour and often containing potatoes and sausage.

Examples

white borscht with sausage
Every Easter, my grandmother prepares a big pot of white borscht for the family meal.
Restaurants in Kraków often serve white borscht with a hard-boiled egg and sausage.
The flavor of white borscht is pleasantly sour due to the fermented wheat starter.
