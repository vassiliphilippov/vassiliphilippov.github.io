magnetic tape

noun
plastic strip for recording data
Pronunciation: / məgˈnɛtɪk teɪp / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk teɪp / (BrE)
A long, thin plastic strip coated with magnetic material, used to record, store, and play back audio, video, or computer data.

Examples

magnetic tape recorder
Data was once archived on magnetic tape before the widespread use of hard drives and cloud storage.
He found a box of magnetic tapes in the attic, each labeled with the year it was recorded.
The old recordings were stored on magnetic tape and had to be digitized for preservation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cassette: compact case containing magnetic tape for recording and playback
  • synonym – reel-to-reel: type of audio recorder using open spools of magnetic tape
