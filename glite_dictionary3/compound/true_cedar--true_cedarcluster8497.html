true cedar

noun
any tree of the genus Cedrus
Pronunciation: / tru ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / truː ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
any tree of the genus Cedrus, native to mountainous regions of the western Himalayas and the Mediterranean, as distinguished from other trees called cedars.

Examples

true cedar tree species
Himalayan true cedar is prized for its fragrant, decay-resistant wood.
Many garden centers mislabel other conifers as true cedar when they are not in the genus Cedrus.
The forest was dominated by a stand of ancient true cedar trees.
