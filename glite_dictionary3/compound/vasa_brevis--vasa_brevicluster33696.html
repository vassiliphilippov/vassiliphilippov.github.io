vasa brevis

noun
small arteries supplying part of the stomach
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪsə ˈbreɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˌveɪsə ˈbriːvɪs / (BrE)
several small arteries that branch from the splenic artery and supply blood to the greater curvature of the stomach

Examples

vasa brevis arteries stomach
Damage to the vasa brevis can lead to complications in gastric procedures.
During surgery, the vasa brevis must be carefully preserved to maintain blood flow to the stomach.
The surgeon explained that the vasa brevis are often divided when removing the spleen.
