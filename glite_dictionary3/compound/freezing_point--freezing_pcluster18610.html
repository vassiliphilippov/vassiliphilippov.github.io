freezing point

noun
temperature at which liquid solidifies
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːzɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfriːzɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
The temperature at which a liquid changes to a solid state under standard atmospheric pressure.

Examples

freezing point of water
Most liquids have a unique freezing point depending on their chemical composition.
The freezing point of water is 0 degrees Celsius under normal atmospheric pressure.
When the temperature dropped below the freezing point, a thin layer of ice formed on the pond.
