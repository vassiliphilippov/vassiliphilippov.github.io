malignant neuroma

noun
malignant tumor of nerve and connective tissue
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt nuˈroʊmə / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt njʊəˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor composed of nerve, fibrous, and connective tissue.

Examples

malignant neuroma causes pain
A malignant neuroma can cause severe pain and neurological symptoms depending on its location.
The doctor explained that the patient was diagnosed with a malignant neuroma after several imaging tests.
Treatment options for a malignant neuroma often include surgery and chemotherapy.
