rectus inferior

noun
eye muscle that turns eyeball down and medially
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktəs ɪnˈfɪriər / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəs ɪnˈfɪərɪə / (BrE)
the extraocular muscle of the eye responsible for turning the eyeball downward and toward the midline

Examples

rectus inferior muscle movement
Damage to the rectus inferior can result in double vision and difficulty looking downward.
Surgeons must carefully avoid the rectus inferior during certain orbital procedures.
The ophthalmologist examined the rectus inferior to check for any weakness in eye movement.
