heat content

noun
thermodynamic quantity; enthalpy
Pronunciation: / hit ˈkɑntɛnt / (AmE), / hiːt ˈkɒntɛnt / (BrE)
(thermodynamics) The total energy of a thermodynamic system, equal to its internal energy plus the product of its pressure and volume; also called enthalpy.

Examples

heat content of a system
During the reaction, the heat content of the mixture changed, indicating energy transfer.
Engineers calculate the heat content to understand how much energy a system can deliver.
The heat content of a substance increases as it absorbs energy from its surroundings.

Related Terms

  • synonym – enthalpy: a measure of the total heat content of a system in thermodynamics
