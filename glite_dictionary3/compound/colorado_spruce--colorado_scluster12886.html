Colorado spruce

noun
a species of spruce tree with blue-green needles
Pronunciation: / kəˌlɑːrədoʊ ˈsprus / (AmE), / kəˌlɒrədəʊ ˈspruːs / (BrE)
a species of tall spruce tree (Picea pungens), native to the Rocky Mountains, with blue-green needles and a dense conic crown that becomes columnar and sweeping with age

Examples

Colorado spruce tree
A mature Colorado spruce can reach heights of over 75 feet in the right conditions.
Gardeners often choose the Colorado spruce for its striking blue-green foliage and symmetrical shape.
The lower branches of the Colorado spruce tend to sweep downward as the tree ages.
