Salton Sea

proper noun
saltwater lake in California
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔltən ˌsi / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltən ˌsiː / (BrE)
a large, shallow, saline lake located in southeastern California, formed by accidental flooding and now maintained by agricultural runoff

Examples

the Salton Sea in California
In the early twentieth century, an unexpected flood created what is now the Salton Sea.
Scientists are studying the wildlife along the shores of the Salton Sea.
The Salton Sea is shrinking rapidly due to water diversion and drought.
