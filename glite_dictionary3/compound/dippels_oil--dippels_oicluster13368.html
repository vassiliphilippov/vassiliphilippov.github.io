Dippel's oil

noun
foul-smelling oil from bones, used in industry
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpəlz ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɪpəlz ɔɪl / (BrE)
A dark, foul-smelling oil obtained by heating animal bones in the absence of air, used especially in sheep dips and to denature alcohol.

Examples

Dippel's oil in sheep dip
Sheep farmers once relied on Dippel's oil for protecting their flocks from parasites.
The unpleasant odor of Dippel's oil made it easy to identify during chemical production.
The use of Dippel's oil in denaturing alcohol dates back to the 19th century.
