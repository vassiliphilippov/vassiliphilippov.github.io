sweat equity

noun
ownership value earned through labor
Pronunciation: / swɛt ˈɛkwəti /; / swɛt ˈɛkwɪti / (AmE), / swɛt ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the value or ownership in a property, project, or business that someone earns by working on it themselves, instead of investing money

Examples

sweat equity in a business
After months of painting and repairing the house themselves, they built up considerable sweat equity.
Many startups reward early employees with sweat equity in place of high salaries.
The renovation added value to the home, thanks to the family’s sweat equity.
By doing most of the repairs himself, Daniel built up a lot of sweat equity in the property.
Many startups reward founders with sweat equity for their hard work and commitment.
She gained a significant share in the company through sweat equity rather than cash investment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – capital: money or assets invested in a business
  • synonym – equity: the value of ownership in a property or business
