pressure dome

noun
dome-shaped pressurized building
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər doʊm / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə dəʊm / (BrE)
a dome-shaped building or enclosure that is maintained at a higher or controlled internal air pressure than the surrounding environment

Examples

pressure dome protects equipment
After the storm, the pressure dome remained intact, safely protecting the equipment and personnel inside.
Building a pressure dome is essential for habitats planned on the Moon or Mars.
The research team conducted their experiments inside a pressure dome to simulate Martian conditions.
