trespass on the case

noun
indirect injury legal action (historical law)
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛspæs ɑn ðə keɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspas ɒn ðə keɪs / (BrE)
a legal action brought to recover damages for indirect injury or loss caused by another's actions, as distinguished from direct trespass

Examples

trespass on the case lawsuit
Before statutory reforms, victims often relied on trespass on the case rather than direct trespass for compensation.
In early English law, a plaintiff could bring a trespass on the case for injury indirectly caused by another's negligence.
The doctrine of trespass on the case influenced the modern development of negligence claims.
