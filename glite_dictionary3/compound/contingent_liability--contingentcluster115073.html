contingent liability

noun
potential liability dependent on future events
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪndʒənt laɪˈæbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənt laɪˈabɪlɪti / (BrE)
A potential financial obligation that may arise depending on the outcome of a future uncertain event, such as a lawsuit.

Examples

contingent liability from lawsuit
If the court rules against them, their contingent liability could become a substantial actual debt.
The company disclosed a contingent liability in its financial statements due to an unresolved lawsuit.
Warranties provided to customers often create a contingent liability that must be reported by businesses.
