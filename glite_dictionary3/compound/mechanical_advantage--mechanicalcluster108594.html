mechanical advantage

noun
the efficiency ratio of a machine's output to input force
Pronunciation: / məˌkænɪkəl ədˈvæntɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪˌkanɪkəl ədˈvɑːntɪdʒ / (BrE)
the ratio of the force produced by a machine to the force applied to it, indicating how much a machine amplifies input force

Examples

mechanical advantage of a lever
A lever can provide a mechanical advantage, making it easier to lift heavy objects.
The mechanical advantage of a pulley system allows workers to lift loads with less effort.
Understanding mechanical advantage is essential for designing efficient simple machines.
