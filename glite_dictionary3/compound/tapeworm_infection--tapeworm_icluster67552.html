tapeworm infection

noun
intestinal infection by a tapeworm
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪpˌwɝm ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈteɪpˌwɜːm ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
An intestinal infection caused by a parasitic tapeworm, typically contracted from eating undercooked meat or fish.

Examples

tapeworm infection from pork
Improperly cooked pork can sometimes be the source of a tapeworm infection.
The doctor diagnosed her with a tapeworm infection after she experienced ongoing stomach pain and weight loss.
Travelers are advised to avoid raw fish to reduce the risk of tapeworm infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym – taeniasis: infection with tapeworms, especially of the genus Taenia
