mass number

noun
sum of protons and neutrons in an atom
Pronunciation: / ˈmæs ˌnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæs ˌnʌmbə / (BrE)
the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom

Examples

mass number of an atom
An isotope of carbon with a mass number of 14 contains six protons and eight neutrons.
The mass number helps scientists identify different isotopes of the same element.
Uranium-238 is named for its mass number, indicating the total particles in its nucleus.
