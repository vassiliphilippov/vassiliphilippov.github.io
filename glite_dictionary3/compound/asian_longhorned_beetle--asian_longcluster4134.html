Asian longhorned beetle

noun
an invasive beetle harmful to hardwood trees
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪʒən ˈlɔːŋhɔːrnd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃ(ə)n ˈlɒŋhɔːnd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a large, invasive beetle native to eastern Asia that attacks and kills hardwood trees, especially in North America and Europe, where it has no natural predators

Examples

Asian longhorned beetle damages trees
Inspectors found evidence of Asian longhorned beetles inside the trunks of several infected hardwood trees.
Scientists warn that the Asian longhorned beetle poses a serious threat to North American forests.
The discovery of an Asian longhorned beetle infestation prompted city officials to cut down several maple trees.
