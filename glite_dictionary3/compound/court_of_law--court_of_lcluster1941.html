court of law

noun
tribunal for legal proceedings
Pronunciation: / kɔrt əv lɔ / (AmE), / kɔːt əv lɔː / (BrE)
A tribunal presided over by a judge or judges, where legal cases are heard and decided according to the law.

Examples

court of law hears cases
A court of law is responsible for ensuring that justice is administered fairly.
Matters of inheritance must often be settled in a court of law.
