caveat emptor

noun
principle: buyer assumes risk of quality
Pronunciation: / ˌkæviˌɑt ˈɛmptɔr / (AmE), / ˌkæviːˌæt ˈɛmptɔː / (BrE)
The principle that a buyer alone is responsible for checking the quality and suitability of goods before making a purchase; let the buyer beware.

Examples

caveat emptor in shopping
In some markets, caveat emptor still applies, so buyers must inspect items carefully.
The contract made no guarantees, operating under the doctrine of caveat emptor.
When shopping for antiques at flea markets, remember the rule of caveat emptor.
