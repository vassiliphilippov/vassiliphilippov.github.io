suicide squeeze play

noun
baseball play where runner sprints home on pitch
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːɪˌsaɪd skwiz pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈsuːɪsʌɪd skwiːz pleɪ / (BrE)
A baseball play in which the runner on third base breaks for home plate as the pitcher delivers the ball, relying on the batter to bunt and allow the runner to score.

Examples

suicide squeeze play at home plate
Executing the suicide squeeze play perfectly, the runner slid home safely.

Their coach is known for calling a suicide squeeze play when the pressure is on.
