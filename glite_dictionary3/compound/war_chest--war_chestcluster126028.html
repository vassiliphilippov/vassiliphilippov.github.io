war chest

noun
reserve fund for campaigns or conflict
Pronunciation: / wɔr tʃɛst / (AmE), / wɔː tʃɛst / (BrE)
a reserve of funds set aside to finance a particular activity, especially a military conflict or a political campaign

Examples

war chest for a campaign
The company built up a war chest to prepare for a potential takeover battle.
The senator used his substantial war chest to outspend his opponents during the election.
With a large war chest, the charity was ready to launch its national campaign.
