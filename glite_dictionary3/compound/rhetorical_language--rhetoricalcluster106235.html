rhetorical language

noun
language used to persuade or influence
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɔrɪkəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
Language intended to persuade or influence others, often using stylistic or expressive devices.

Examples

use rhetorical language in speeches
Her speech was filled with rhetorical language that made her message more compelling.
In essays, students are often encouraged to avoid excessive rhetorical language in favor of clear arguments.
