to conduct electricity

verb
allow electric current to flow
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌkt ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (AmE), / kənˈdʌkt ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (BrE)
to allow electric current to flow through; to transmit electricity

Examples

conduct electricity well
Copper wires are commonly used in electrical wiring because they conduct electricity efficiently.
Materials like wood and plastic do not conduct electricity well, making them good insulators.
Seawater can conduct electricity due to the presence of dissolved salts.
