half-and-half

noun
dairy mixture of milk and cream
Pronunciation: / ˈhæf ən ˌhæf / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ən ˈhɑːf / (BrE)
A mixture of equal parts milk and light cream, typically containing 10% to 18% butterfat, used in coffee, recipes, and cooking.

Examples

half-and-half in coffee
He always adds half-and-half to his morning coffee instead of milk.
The recipe calls for a cup of half-and-half to make the soup creamier.
You can substitute half-and-half for heavy cream to reduce the fat content in desserts.
