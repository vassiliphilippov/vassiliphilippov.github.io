tidal river

noun
a river affected by tides upstream
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.dl ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dl ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in which the rise and fall of the tide can be observed far upstream from the river's mouth

Examples

tidal river flows upstream
During high tide, saltwater flows far upriver in the tidal river system.
Navigation on a tidal river can be tricky due to fluctuations in water level.
The Thames is a famous tidal river in England, with tidal effects visible in central London.
