good luck charm

noun
person believed to bring luck
Pronunciation: / ɡʊd lʌk tʃɑrm / (AmE), / ɡʊd lʌk tʃɑːm / (BrE)
a person whose presence is believed to bring good luck to a group, team, or event.

Examples

call someone a good luck charm
Before every match, the team invited their veteran goalie, the good luck charm, to sit with them.
Whenever sales fell, managers hoped the visiting client would act as their good luck charm.
The small pub considered the old pianist its good luck charm after business boomed on his nights.

Related Terms

  • antonym – jinx: a person or thing believed to bring bad luck
  • synonym – lucky charm: an object or person thought to bring good luck
