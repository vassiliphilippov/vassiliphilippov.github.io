pollen tube

noun
slender tube conveying pollen in plants
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlən tuːb / (AmE), / ˈpɒlən tjuːb / (BrE)
(botany) a slender tubular structure developed from a pollen grain after landing on the stigma, through which male gametes travel to reach the ovule for fertilization.

Examples

pollen tube growth
Microscopic examination revealed a healthy pollen tube emerging from the pollen grain.
Successful fertilization in flowering plants depends on the formation of the pollen tube.
The pollen tube grows down through the style to deliver sperm cells to the ovule.
