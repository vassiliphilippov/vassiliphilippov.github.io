twilight sleep

noun
partial anesthesia with some consciousness
Pronunciation: / ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt sliːp / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪ.laɪt sliːp / (BrE)
a state of partial anesthesia in which a person remains semiconscious, often induced by drugs such as scopolamine or morphine, historically used especially during childbirth

Examples

twilight sleep during childbirth
In the early 20th century, women giving birth were sometimes placed in twilight sleep to ease the pain.
Medical textbooks now discuss twilight sleep mainly as a historical practice in obstetrics.
The use of twilight sleep has largely been replaced by safer methods of anesthesia.
