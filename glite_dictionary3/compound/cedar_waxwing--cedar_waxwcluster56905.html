cedar waxwing

noun
North American songbird with waxy wing tips
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.dɚ ˈwæks.wɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiː.də ˈwæks.wɪŋ / (BrE)
a North American songbird (Bombycilla cedrorum) with sleek brown plumage, a crest, a black eye mask, and waxy red tips on some wing feathers, often found in flocks and known for eating fruit and berries

Examples

flock of cedar waxwings
A flock of cedar waxwings landed in the garden to feast on the berries.
During migration, cedar waxwings are often seen in large, noisy groups.
The cedar waxwing is easily recognized by its sleek crest and bright yellow tail tip.
