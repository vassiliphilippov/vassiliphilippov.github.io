foster-brother

noun
male child raised by your parents, not biological
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑstər ˌbrʌðər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˌbrʌðə / (BrE)
A boy or man who is not the biological son of your parents but who is raised as part of your family.

Examples

my foster-brother at home
After his parents adopted Sam, he became my foster-brother.
Although we are not related by blood, my foster-brother and I grew up together.
She considers her foster-brother to be one of her closest friends.

  • synonym – foster-sister: a female who is not a daughter of your parents but is raised by your parents
  • antonym – stepbrother: a son of one's stepparent by a previous marriage
