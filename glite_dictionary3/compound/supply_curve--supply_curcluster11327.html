supply curve

noun
graph showing producers' quantity supplied at different prices
Pronunciation: / səˈplaɪ kɝv / (AmE), / səˈplaɪ kɜːv / (BrE)
A graph showing how the quantity of a product that producers are willing to sell varies with its price.

Examples

supply curve shifts right
If production costs fall, the supply curve shifts to the right, indicating a higher quantity supplied at every price.
In microeconomics, the supply curve typically slopes upward from left to right.
The intersection of the demand curve and the supply curve determines the market equilibrium price.
