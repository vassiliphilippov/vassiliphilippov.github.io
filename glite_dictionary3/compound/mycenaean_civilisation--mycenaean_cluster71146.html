Mycenaean civilisation

noun
late Bronze Age Greek culture 1400-1100 BC
Pronunciation: / maɪˌsɪniˈən ˌsɪvələˌzeɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˌsiːniˈiːən ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the advanced Bronze Age Greek culture centered in Mycenae that flourished from about 1400 to 1100 BC, known for its palatial states, art, and writing system

Examples

ancient Mycenaean civilisation ruins
Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts from the Mycenaean civilisation in mainland Greece.
Scholars believe the collapse of the Mycenaean civilisation led to the Greek Dark Ages.
The Mycenaean civilisation is often credited with influencing later Greek culture, including myth and architecture.
