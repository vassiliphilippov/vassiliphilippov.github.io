fusible metal

noun
alloy with a low melting point for safety uses
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːzəbəl ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːzɪbəl ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
an alloy with a low melting point, used for soldering, and in safety devices such as plugs and sprinkler fuses.

Examples

fusible metal for soldering
Manufacturers select fusible metal for applications where precise melting points are required.
Sprinkler systems often contain a fusible metal that melts in response to high temperatures, activating the system.
The plumber used a fusible metal to join the pipes securely.
