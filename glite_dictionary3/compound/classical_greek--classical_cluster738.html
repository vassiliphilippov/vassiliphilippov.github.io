Classical Greek

noun
Attic dialect of Ancient Greek, 5th–4th century BC
Pronunciation: / ˌklæsɪkəl ˈɡrik / (AmE), / ˌklæsɪkəl ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the form of Ancient Greek language used chiefly in Athens and the surrounding regions during the 5th and 4th centuries BC, renowned for its literary and philosophical works

Examples

read Classical Greek texts
Many modern scholars study Classical Greek to read ancient texts in their original form.
Plato and Aristotle wrote their works in Classical Greek.
The influence of Classical Greek is evident in the vocabulary of scientific terms.
