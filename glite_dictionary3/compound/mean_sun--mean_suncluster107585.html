mean sun

noun
theoretical sun for measuring mean solar time
Pronunciation: / min sʌn / (AmE), / miːn sʌn / (BrE)
an imaginary sun that moves at a uniform speed along the celestial equator, whose motion is used as the basis for measuring mean solar time.

Examples

mean sun moves evenly
The concept of the mean sun simplifies timekeeping by assuming a uniform movement along the celestial equator.
To calculate mean solar time, astronomers refer to the position of the mean sun rather than the actual sun.
Unlike the mean sun, the real sun's speed varies due to the elliptical shape of Earth's orbit.

  • antonym – apparent sun: the actual sun as observed from Earth
