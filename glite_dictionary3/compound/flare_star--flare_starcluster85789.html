flare star

noun
a red dwarf star with sudden brightness surges
Pronunciation: / flɛr stɑr / (AmE), / fleə stɑː / (BrE)
a faint red dwarf star that can suddenly increase in brightness for a few minutes due to eruptions on its surface

Examples

a flare star brightens suddenly
Astronomers observed a flare star brighten unexpectedly during the night.
Many flare stars are found in our galaxy, exhibiting unpredictable eruptions.
The sudden light from the flare star was detected by sensitive telescopes.
