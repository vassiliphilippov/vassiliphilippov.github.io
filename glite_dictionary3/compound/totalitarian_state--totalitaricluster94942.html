totalitarian state

noun
state with absolute government control
Pronunciation: / toʊˌtæləˈtɛəriən steɪt / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriən steɪt / (BrE)
A government in which the individual is entirely subordinate to the authority of the state, which strictly controls all aspects of life through coercive measures.

Examples

a totalitarian state controls citizens
He fled his country to escape life in a totalitarian state where dissent was harshly punished.
In literature, dystopian societies are often modeled after a totalitarian state.
The citizens of the totalitarian state lived under constant surveillance by the government.

Related Terms

  • synonym – authoritarian state: a government characterized by strong central power and limited political freedoms
