energy state

noun
stable level of energy a system can have
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnɚdʒi steɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi steɪt / (BrE)
a definite and stable level of energy that a physical system, such as an atom or molecule, can have, especially in quantum theory

Examples

quantum energy state
In quantum mechanics, an electron can occupy only certain energy states within an atom.
The transition from one energy state to another often results in the emission or absorption of light.
When an atom absorbs energy, its electrons may move to a higher energy state.

  • synonym – energy level: one of the possible stable energy values in a physical system
