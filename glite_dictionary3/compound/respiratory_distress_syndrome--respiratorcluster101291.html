respiratory distress syndrome

noun
acute lung disease of newborns
Pronunciation: / rɪˌspɪrəˌtɔri dɪˈstrɛs ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / rɪˌspɪrətəri dɪˈstrɛs ˌsɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
A serious acute lung condition in newborns, especially in premature infants, where the lungs cannot expand properly due to a lack of surfactant, leading to rapid, shallow breathing and bluish skin color.

Examples

newborn respiratory distress syndrome
Advances in neonatal care have greatly improved outcomes for infants with respiratory distress syndrome.
Doctors recognized the symptoms of respiratory distress syndrome shortly after birth.
The premature baby developed respiratory distress syndrome and needed assistance to breathe.
