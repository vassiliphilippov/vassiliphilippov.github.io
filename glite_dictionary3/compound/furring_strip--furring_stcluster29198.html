furring strip

noun
thin strip used in construction
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrɪŋ strɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɜːrɪŋ strɪp / (BrE)
a thin length of wood or metal used to create a level surface for attaching wallboard, paneling, or plasterwork

Examples

furring strip for walls
Metal furring strips are often used in basement remodeling to support wallboard.
The carpenter nailed a furring strip to the wall before installing the drywall.
Use a furring strip to ensure the panels are mounted evenly.
