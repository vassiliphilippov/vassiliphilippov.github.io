Presidents' Day

noun
U.S. holiday honoring Lincoln and Washington
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛzɪdənts deɪ / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənts deɪ / (BrE)
A public holiday in the United States observed on the third Monday in February, honoring Presidents Abraham Lincoln and George Washington.

Examples

Presidents' Day holiday sales
Many businesses offer sales on Presidents' Day to attract holiday shoppers.
Schools across the country are closed for Presidents' Day every February.
Some families take advantage of the long Presidents' Day weekend to go on a short trip.
