olfactory impairment

noun
disorder of the sense of smell
Pronunciation: / ɑlˈfæktɚi ɪmˈpɛr.mənt / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæktəri ɪmˈpeə.mənt / (BrE)
a disorder or loss in the sense of smell, typically due to illness, injury, or age

Examples

olfactory impairment after illness
A head injury can cause olfactory impairment that may be temporary or permanent.
Early diagnosis of olfactory impairment can help identify underlying health issues.
People with olfactory impairment often have difficulty detecting everyday odors such as smoke or perfume.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – anosmia: complete loss of sense of smell
  • degree lower – hyposmia: reduced sense of smell
