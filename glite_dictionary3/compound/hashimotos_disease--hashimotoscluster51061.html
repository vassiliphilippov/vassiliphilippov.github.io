Hashimoto's disease

noun
autoimmune thyroid disorder
Pronunciation: / ˌhæʃɪˈmoʊtoʊz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌhæʃɪˈməʊtəʊz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
An autoimmune disorder in which the thyroid gland is gradually destroyed, often leading to hypothyroidism; most common in middle-aged women.

Examples

Hashimoto's disease causes tiredness
Her symptoms of weight gain and depression were eventually linked to Hashimoto's disease.
People with Hashimoto's disease often require lifelong thyroid hormone replacement therapy.
The doctor diagnosed her with Hashimoto's disease after several months of unexplained fatigue.
