phase I

noun
first stage of a clinical trial
Pronunciation: / feɪz wʌn / (AmE), / feɪz wʌn / (BrE)
the first stage of a clinical trial, involving a small number of participants, mainly to assess the safety, tolerability, and dosage limits of a new drug or invasive medical device

Examples

phase I clinical trial
Researchers reported that the phase I results showed the drug was generally well tolerated.
She was enrolled in a phase I clinical trial to test the safety of the new medication.
The company announced the start of a phase I study for its experimental cancer therapy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – phase II: the second stage of a clinical trial, studying effectiveness and side effects
