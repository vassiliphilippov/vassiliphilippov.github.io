Confederate flag

noun
flag of the Confederate States of America
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛdɚət flæɡ / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət flaɡ / (BrE)
the flag used to represent the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War, most commonly referring to the battle flag design

Examples

the Confederate flag flies
He wore a shirt with the Confederate flag printed on it, sparking a heated debate.
Many people view the Confederate flag as a symbol of Southern heritage, while others see it as representing racism and division.
The museum displayed an original Confederate flag from the Civil War era.
