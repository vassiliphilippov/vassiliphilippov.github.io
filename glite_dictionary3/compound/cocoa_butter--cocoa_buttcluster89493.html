cocoa butter

noun
fat from cocoa beans
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊkoʊ ˌbʌtər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəʊ ˌbʌtə / (BrE)
a pale yellow, edible fat from cocoa beans, used in making chocolate, cosmetics, and some medicines

Examples

cocoa butter lotion

The chocolate contains a high proportion of cocoa butter for a smooth texture.
White chocolate is made from cocoa butter, sugar, and milk solids.
Many moisturizing creams contain cocoa butter as a key ingredient.
The smooth texture of cocoa butter makes it ideal for making chocolate bars.
White chocolate is primarily made with cocoa butter instead of cocoa solids.
