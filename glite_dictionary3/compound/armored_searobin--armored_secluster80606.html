armored searobin

noun
a sea robin fish with bony scutes and barbels
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrmərd ˈsiˌroʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˈɑːməd ˈsiːˌrəʊbɪn / (BrE)
A type of sea robin fish characterized by bony scutes on its body and barbels on its chin, usually found on the continental slope.

Examples

armored searobin with bony plates
Divers often spot the armored searobin near the slopes of the continental shelf.
The armored searobin can be identified by the bony plates along its sides.
The armored searobin uses its chin barbels to search for food on the ocean floor.
