vitreous humour

noun
clear gel filling the eyeball
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪtriəs ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɪtrɪəs ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
the clear, colorless, transparent jelly-like substance that fills the space between the lens and the retina of the eyeball

Examples

the vitreous humour of the eye
As we age, the vitreous humour can shrink and pull away from the retina, sometimes causing floaters.
In certain eye surgeries, part of the vitreous humour may be removed and replaced.
The vitreous humour helps maintain the shape of the eye and allows light to pass through to the retina.
