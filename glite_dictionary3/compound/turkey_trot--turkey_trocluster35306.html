turkey trot

noun
early ragtime dance style
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːki trɑt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki trɒt / (BrE)
an early ragtime dance popular in the early 20th century, characterized by a jerky, trotting motion

Examples

to dance the turkey trot
Historians noted that the turkey trot influenced later social dances.
In the ballroom, couples performed the turkey trot to lively ragtime music.
The turkey trot was considered quite scandalous when it first became popular in the 1910s.
