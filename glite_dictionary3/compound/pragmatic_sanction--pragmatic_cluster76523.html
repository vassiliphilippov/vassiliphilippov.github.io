pragmatic sanction

noun
royal decree with force of fundamental law
Pronunciation: / præɡˈmætɪk ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪk ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
A royal or imperial decree that becomes a fundamental law, especially one issued by a sovereign and incorporated into the legal basis of a state.

Examples

pragmatic sanction issued by emperor
Historians debate the long-term effects of the pragmatic sanction on European succession laws.
The pragmatic sanction of 1713 ensured that Maria Theresa could inherit the Habsburg throne.
The emperor issued a pragmatic sanction to formally amend the laws of succession.
