beta ray

noun
radiation of beta particles
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪtə reɪ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə reɪ / (BrE)
Radiation consisting of streams of beta particles emitted during radioactive decay of certain nuclei.

Examples

beta ray emission
During radioactive decay, some isotopes emit beta rays along with other particles.
Exposure to beta rays can be hazardous and requires proper shielding.
The Geiger counter detected the presence of beta rays in the laboratory sample.
