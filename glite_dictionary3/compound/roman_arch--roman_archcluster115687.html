Roman arch

noun
a semi-circular architectural arch
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mən ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch with a semi-circular shape, formed by drawing a curve from a single center point, characteristic of Roman architecture.

Examples

a Roman arch structure
The Roman arch revolutionized architecture by allowing stronger and more durable constructions.
The aqueduct was supported by a series of impressive Roman arches spanning the valley.
You can identify the building’s era by the large Roman arch above the main entrance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Gothic arch: a pointed arch found in Gothic architecture
  • synonym – round arch: an arch formed in a continuous curve; especially a semicircular arch
