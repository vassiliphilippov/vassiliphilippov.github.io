stretch receptor

noun
sensory receptor detecting muscle stretch
Pronunciation: / strɛtʃ rɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / strɛtʃ rɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a receptor in muscle tissue that detects and responds to stretching or changes in muscle length

Examples

muscle stretch receptor
A stretch receptor sends signals to the nervous system when a muscle is lengthened.
Damage to a stretch receptor can lead to problems with muscle coordination and reflexes.
The stretch receptor in your muscles helps your body sense changes in position and maintain balance.
