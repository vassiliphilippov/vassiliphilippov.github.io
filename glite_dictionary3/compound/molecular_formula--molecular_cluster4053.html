molecular formula

noun
actual composition of a molecule
Pronunciation: / məˈlɛkjələr ˈfɔrmjələ / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə ˈfɔːmjʊlə / (BrE)
a chemical formula that shows the actual number and kinds of atoms in a molecule of a compound

Examples

molecular formula of glucose
Glucose has a molecular formula of C₆H₁₂O₆, representing the exact number of atoms in each molecule.
Knowing the molecular formula helps chemists understand the composition and properties of a compound.
The molecular formula of water is H₂O, indicating two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
