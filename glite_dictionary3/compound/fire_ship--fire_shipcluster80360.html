fire ship

noun
ship used as explosive weapon
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ʃɪp / (BrE)
A ship filled with explosives, set adrift and directed towards enemy vessels with the intent to destroy them by fire or explosion.

Examples

enemy fleet with a fire ship
During the naval battle, the attackers launched a fire ship toward the enemy fleet.
Historical accounts describe how a single fire ship could turn the tide of a sea battle.
The ingenious use of a fire ship caused panic and chaos among the defenders.
