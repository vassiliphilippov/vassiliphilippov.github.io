landing place

noun
structure where boats land
Pronunciation: / ˈlændɪŋ pleɪs / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ pleɪs / (BrE)
a structure or area where boats can come ashore to load or unload people or goods

Examples

busy landing place
A new landing place was built to accommodate larger cargo boats.
Fishermen unloaded their catch at the small landing place near the village.
The ferry approached the landing place as passengers prepared to disembark.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dock: a structure for the mooring of boats and ships
  • synonym – pier: a platform extending from shore over water for boats to dock
  • synonym – wharf: a level area to which a ship may be moored to load and unload
