batting average

noun
baseball hitting performance statistic
Pronunciation: / ˈbætɪŋ ˌævərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ ˌævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a measure of a batter's performance in baseball, calculated as the number of base hits divided by the number of official at-bats

Examples

batting average in baseball
A player's batting average often determines how valuable they are to the team.
His batting average dropped after the injury, but he worked hard to recover.
Ted Williams finished the season with a remarkably high batting average.
