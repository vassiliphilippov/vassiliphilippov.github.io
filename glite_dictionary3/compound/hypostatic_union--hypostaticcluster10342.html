hypostatic union

noun
union of Christ's divine and human natures
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pəˈstæt.ɪk ˈjuː.njən / (AmE), / ˌhɪ.pəˈstæt.ɪk ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the union of Christ's divine and human natures in a single individual existence.

Examples

the hypostatic union of Christ
Discussions on the hypostatic union often explore how Christ could be fully human and fully divine.
Many theologians consider the hypostatic union a central mystery of Christian faith.
The doctrine of the hypostatic union was formally affirmed at the Council of Chalcedon in 451.
