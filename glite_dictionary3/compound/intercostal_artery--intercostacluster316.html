intercostal artery

noun
artery between the ribs
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkɑstl ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkɒstl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
any of several arteries that run between the ribs and supply blood to the muscles and skin of the chest wall

Examples

intercostal artery injury
A ruptured intercostal artery can cause bleeding into the chest cavity.
Each rib is supplied with blood by an intercostal artery running along its lower edge.
The surgeon carefully avoided cutting the intercostal artery during the procedure.

Related Terms
