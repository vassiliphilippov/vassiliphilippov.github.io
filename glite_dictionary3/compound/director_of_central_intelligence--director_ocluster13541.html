Director of Central Intelligence

noun
US intelligence service director (1946–2005)
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛktər əv ˌsɛntrəl ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / dəˈrɛktə əv ˌsɛntrəl ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the official who served as head of the United States Intelligence Community and as director of the Central Intelligence Agency from 1946 to 2005

Examples

the Director of Central Intelligence appointment
In 1977, Stansfield Turner was appointed as Director of Central Intelligence by President Carter.
The Director of Central Intelligence oversaw all major U.S. intelligence agencies during the Cold War.
The office of Director of Central Intelligence was replaced by the Director of National Intelligence in 2005.
