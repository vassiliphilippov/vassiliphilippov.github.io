leaf node

noun
part of stem where leaves emerge (botany)
Pronunciation: / lif noʊd / (AmE), / liːf nəʊd / (BrE)
(Botany) A small swelling on a plant stem from which one or more leaves emerge.

Examples

leaf node on a stem
Each leaf node produces one or more leaves, helping the plant expand outward.
It's common for roots to grow from a leaf node when the stem is placed in water.
