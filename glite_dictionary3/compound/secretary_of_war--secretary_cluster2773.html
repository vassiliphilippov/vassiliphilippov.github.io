Secretary of War

noun
former U.S. cabinet official overseeing the Army
Pronunciation: / sɛk.rəˌtɛɹ.i əv wɔɹ / (AmE), / sɛk.rəˌtɛə.ɹi əv wɔː / (BrE)
the head of a former executive department of the United States responsible for the administration of the Army, a position that existed until 1947 when it was merged into the role of Secretary of Defense.

Examples

the Secretary of War position
After 1947, the position of Secretary of War was replaced by the Secretary of Defense.
Henry L. Stimson served as Secretary of War under President Franklin D. Roosevelt.
The Secretary of War played a pivotal role in shaping U.S. military policy during the Civil War.
