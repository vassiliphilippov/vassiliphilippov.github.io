three-quarter binding

noun
bookbinding style with partial contrasting cover
Pronunciation: / ˌθriː ˈkwɔrtər ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌθriː ˈkwɔːtə ˈbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of bookbinding in which the spine and a large part of the sides are covered with a different material from the rest of the cover, typically leather or cloth.

Examples

three-quarter binding with leather
Collectors often prefer books with three-quarter binding for their decorative contrast and durability.
The library commissioned several volumes in three-quarter binding to display classic craftsmanship.
The rare edition was preserved in a three-quarter binding of calf and marbled paper.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – full binding: bookbinding with the entire cover in a single material
  • degree lower – half binding: bookbinding where only the spine and corners are in a different material
