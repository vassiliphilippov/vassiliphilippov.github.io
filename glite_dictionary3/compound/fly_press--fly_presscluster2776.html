fly press

noun
screw press with flywheel for shaping materials
Pronunciation: / flaɪ prɛs / (AmE), / flaɪ prɛs / (BrE)
a screw press operated by a flywheel or fly weights attached to a horizontal bar, used for pressing or shaping materials, especially in metalworking.

Examples

fly press shapes metal
A fly press can be found in many traditional metalworking workshops.
The company invested in a new motor-driven fly press for increased production efficiency.
The machinist used the fly press to form precise holes in the metal sheet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – screw press: a press operated by turning a screw to exert pressure
