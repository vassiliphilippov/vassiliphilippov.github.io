neural tube

noun
embryonic precursor to brain and spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈnʊrəl tuːb / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəl tjuːb / (BrE)
A tube of embryonic ectodermal tissue that develops into the brain and spinal cord in vertebrates.

Examples

developing neural tube
Certain birth defects, like spina bifida, occur when the neural tube does not close properly.
Folic acid supplementation helps prevent neural tube defects in newborns.
The neural tube closes during the early stages of embryonic development.

Related Terms

  • synonym – brain: the organ of the body in the head coordinating sensations and intellect
  • synonym – spinal cord: the column of nerve tissue within the spine
