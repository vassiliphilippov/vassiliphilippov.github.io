scalar product

noun
product of two vectors yielding a real number
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪ.lɚ ˈprɑː.dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈskeɪ.lə ˈprɒd.ʌkt / (BrE)
the product of two vectors that results in a single real number, calculated as the sum of the products of their corresponding components; also known as the dot product.

Examples

scalar product of two vectors
In physics, the work done is found by taking the scalar product of force and displacement vectors.
The scalar product is useful in determining if two vectors are perpendicular, as their product will be zero.
To calculate the scalar product of two vectors, multiply their corresponding components and sum the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dot product: the scalar product of two vectors; another common term for this concept
  • antonym – vector product: the cross product of two vectors resulting in a vector, not a scalar
