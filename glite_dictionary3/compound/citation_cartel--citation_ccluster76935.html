citation cartel

noun
group manipulating academic citations for advantage
Pronunciation: / saɪˈteɪʃən kɑːrˈtɛl / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən kɑːˈtɛl / (BrE)
a group of individuals, journals, or institutions that manipulate or exploit academic citations to artificially increase impact or gain advantage.

Examples

academic citation cartel
Awareness of citation cartels has prompted reforms in academic publishing.
