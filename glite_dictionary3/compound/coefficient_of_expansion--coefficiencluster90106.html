coefficient of expansion

noun
ratio measuring thermal expansion
Pronunciation: / koʊ.ɪˈfɪʃ.ənt əv ɪkˈspæn.ʃən / (AmE), / kəʊ.ɪˈfɪʃ.ənt əv ɪkˈspæn.ʃən / (BrE)
A ratio that measures the fractional change in length, area, or volume of a material per unit change in temperature at constant pressure.

Examples

coefficient of expansion of metal
Engineers measured the coefficient of expansion before selecting a material for the bridge.
Steel structures must account for the coefficient of expansion to prevent damage from temperature changes.
The glass has a low coefficient of expansion, making it suitable for laboratory equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thermal expansion: increase in size of a material as it is heated
