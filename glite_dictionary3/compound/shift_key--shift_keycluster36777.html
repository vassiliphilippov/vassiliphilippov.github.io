shift key

noun
key for changing case or accessing symbols
Pronunciation: / ʃɪft ki / (AmE), / ʃɪft kiː / (BrE)
A key on a computer or typewriter keyboard that, when pressed, changes lowercase letters to uppercase and allows access to alternate characters.

Examples

press the shift key
Always hold down the shift key to type a capital letter.
If your shift key is stuck, you might not be able to type normally.
To type an exclamation mark, press the shift key and the number 1 together.

Related Terms

  • synonym – caps lock: keyboard key that switches letters to uppercase without holding it down
