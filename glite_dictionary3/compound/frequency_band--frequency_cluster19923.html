frequency band

noun
range of radio frequencies for specific use
Pronunciation: / ˈfriː.kwən.si bænd / (AmE), / ˈfriː.kwən.si bænd / (BrE)
a range of adjacent radio frequencies allocated for a particular use, such as transmitting radio or television signals

Examples

frequency band allocation
Military communications often use a dedicated frequency band to avoid interference.
Television broadcasters must operate within an assigned frequency band to prevent overlap.
The FM radio frequency band covers frequencies from 88 to 108 megahertz.

Related Terms

  • synonym – frequency range: the extent of frequencies between two limits, often used interchangeably with 'frequency band'
