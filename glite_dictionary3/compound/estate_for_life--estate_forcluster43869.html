estate for life

noun
legal interest lasting for a holder's lifetime
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsteɪt fər laɪf / (AmE), / ɪˈsteɪt fə laɪf / (BrE)
(law) An interest in property that lasts for the lifetime of the person holding it, ceasing at their death.

Examples

estate for life ownership
He inherited only an estate for life and could not sell the property permanently.
In legal disputes, an estate for life can affect the transfer of ownership.
The widow occupied the house under her estate for life until her death.
