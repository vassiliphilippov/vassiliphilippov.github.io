unleavened bread

noun
bread made without leavening agents
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɛvənd brɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɛvənd brɛd / (BrE)
Bread made without leavening agents such as yeast, resulting in a flat texture; commonly eaten during Passover and in various cultural traditions.

Examples

unleavened bread at Passover
During Passover, Jewish families eat unleavened bread to commemorate the Exodus from Egypt.
Some religious ceremonies feature unleavened bread instead of regular loaves.
The recipe calls for unleavened bread made only with flour and water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – matzo: thin, crisp unleavened bread traditionally eaten during Passover
