law of proximity

noun
Gestalt principle of perceiving nearby objects as a group
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv prɑːkˈsɪmɪti / (AmE), / lɔː əv prɒkˈsɪmɪti / (BrE)
(in psychology) the principle that objects or events near to each other in space or time are perceived as belonging together as a unit.

Examples

law of proximity in perception
According to the law of proximity, people tend to see the three clusters of dots as separate groups.
Children often organize objects based on the law of proximity, arranging items that are close together as a single collection.
The designer grouped buttons together on the screen, using the law of proximity to guide the user’s eye.
