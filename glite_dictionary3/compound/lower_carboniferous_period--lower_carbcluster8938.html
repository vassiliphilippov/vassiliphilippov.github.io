Lower Carboniferous period

noun
early subdivision of the Carboniferous period
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊər ˌkɑːrbəˈnɪfərəs ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈləʊə ˌkɑːbɒˈnɪfərəs ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the earlier subdivision of the Carboniferous period, lasting from about 345 million to 310 million years ago, marked by increased land areas and development of primitive ammonites and winged insects

Examples

the Lower Carboniferous period fossils
Coal deposits dating back to the Lower Carboniferous period are important resources in several countries.
Geologists found fossils from the Lower Carboniferous period in the limestone cliffs.
The Lower Carboniferous period saw the rise of primitive winged insects and early amphibians.
