ballistic pendulum

noun
device for measuring projectile velocity
Pronunciation: / bəˈlɪstɪk ˈpɛndjələm / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪk ˈpɛndjʊləm / (BrE)
a device consisting of a large mass suspended from a rod, used to measure the velocity of a projectile by observing the resulting displacement when struck

Examples

a ballistic pendulum measures speed
Before the invention of modern chronographs, the ballistic pendulum was an essential tool for measuring firearm performance.
Researchers used a ballistic pendulum to determine the velocity of small projectiles in their experiment.
The physics instructor demonstrated how a ballistic pendulum could be used to calculate bullet speed.
