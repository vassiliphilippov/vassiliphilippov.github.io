elastic modulus

noun
ratio of stress to strain in a material
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlæstɪk ˈmɑːdʒələs / (AmE), / ɪˌlæstɪk ˈmɒdjʊləs / (BrE)
A quantity expressing a material's ability to resist deformation, defined as the ratio of applied stress to resulting strain.

Examples

elastic modulus of steel
An engineer measures the elastic modulus to evaluate how a material will deform under stress.
Materials with a low elastic modulus are more flexible and easier to stretch.
The elastic modulus of steel is much higher than that of rubber.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Young's modulus: the elastic modulus for stretching or compression in a particular direction
