combination lock

noun
lock opened with a sequence of numbers or symbols
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːm.bɪˈneɪ.ʃən lɑk / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.bɪˈneɪ.ʃən lɒk / (BrE)
A lock that opens only when its dials or buttons are turned or pressed in a specific sequence, rather than with a key.

Examples

combination lock on a locker
He forgot the code to his combination lock and couldn't open his gym locker.
Many school lockers use a combination lock instead of a traditional padlock.
