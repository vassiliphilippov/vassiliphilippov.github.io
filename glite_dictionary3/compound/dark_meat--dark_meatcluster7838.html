dark meat

noun
meat from bird's legs and thighs
Pronunciation: / dɑrk miːt / (AmE), / dɑːk miːt / (BrE)
the meat from the legs and thighs of a bird such as a chicken or turkey, which is darker in color and has a slightly stronger flavor than meat from the breast or wings

Examples

dark meat from chicken
At Thanksgiving, my uncle always requests dark meat instead of breast meat.
Some people prefer dark meat because it is juicier and more flavorful than white meat.
The recipe calls for two pounds of dark meat from chicken legs and thighs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – white meat: the lighter-colored meat from the breast or wings of poultry
