planktonic algae

noun
unicellular algae floating in water
Pronunciation: / plæŋkˈtɑːnɪk ˈældʒi / (AmE), / plæŋkˈtɒnɪk ˈældʒi / (BrE)
Tiny unicellular algae that float freely in water as part of the plankton community.

Examples

planktonic algae bloom
A sudden increase in planktonic algae can cause harmful algal blooms in freshwater lakes.
Scientists study planktonic algae to monitor the health and productivity of aquatic ecosystems.
The fish in this pond feed primarily on planktonic algae and small insects.
