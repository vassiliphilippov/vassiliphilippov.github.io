defense reaction

noun
unconscious psychological process reducing anxiety
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns riˈækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns riˈækʃən / (BrE)
(Psychiatry) an unconscious psychological process that reduces anxiety arising from instinctive drives or unacceptable thoughts.

Examples

a defense reaction to stress
Fear of intimacy can sometimes trigger a defense reaction such as withdrawal or hostility.
Intellectualization is one type of defense reaction people use to avoid uncomfortable feelings.
The therapist explained that her constant laughter might be a defense reaction against emotional pain.
