extrauterine pregnancy

noun
pregnancy outside the uterus
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrəˈjutərɪn ˈprɛɡnənsi / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈjuːtərɪn ˈprɛɡnənsi / (BrE)
A pregnancy in which the embryo implants and develops outside the uterus, most commonly in a fallopian tube.

Examples

extrauterine pregnancy in a fallopian tube
After severe abdominal pain, she was diagnosed with an extrauterine pregnancy requiring immediate medical attention.
An extrauterine pregnancy can be life-threatening if not treated promptly by healthcare professionals.
Doctors must consider the possibility of an extrauterine pregnancy in women with certain symptoms early in pregnancy.
