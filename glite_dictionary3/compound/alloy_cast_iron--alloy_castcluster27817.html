alloy cast iron

noun
cast iron with added alloying elements
Pronunciation: / əˈlɔɪ kæst ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / əˈlɔɪ kɑːst ˈʌɪən / (BrE)
cast iron that contains additional alloying elements, such as nickel, chromium, copper, or molybdenum, to improve strength or aid in heat treatment

Examples

alloy cast iron engine block
Engine blocks are often made from alloy cast iron to enhance durability and performance.
The addition of chromium or nickel to alloy cast iron improves its mechanical properties.
Using alloy cast iron allows manufacturers to achieve higher resistance to wear and corrosion.
