foot brake

noun
vehicle brake operated by foot pedal
Pronunciation: / fʊt breɪk / (AmE), / fʊt breɪk / (BrE)
A brake on a vehicle that is operated by pressing a pedal with the foot, usually part of the main braking system.

Examples

press the foot brake pedal
Most cars have a foot brake and a separate handbrake for parking.
Sudden use of the foot brake can cause the vehicle to skid on wet roads.
The driver pressed the foot brake to slow down as he approached the intersection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – handbrake: a brake operated by hand, usually to keep a vehicle stationary
