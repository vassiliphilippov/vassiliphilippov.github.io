sweet jazz

noun
a jazz style with a regular beat and limited improvisation
Pronunciation: / swit dʒæz / (AmE), / swiːt dzæz / (BrE)
a style of jazz characterized by a regular beat, moderate tempo, limited improvisation, and an emphasis on a warm tone and clear melody

Examples

classic sweet jazz band
He prefers sweet jazz to the more improvisational hot jazz of the same era.
Many dance halls in the 1930s featured sweet jazz as the preferred music style for social gatherings.
The orchestra specialized in sweet jazz, delighting audiences with its smooth performances.
