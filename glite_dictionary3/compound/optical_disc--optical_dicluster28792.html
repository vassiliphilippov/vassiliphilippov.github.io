optical disc

noun
disk for storing digital data, read by laser
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl dɪsk / (BrE)
A flat, round disk coated with plastic and used to store digital data, which is read using a laser.

Examples

optical disc stores data
He backed up his computer files onto an optical disc for safekeeping.
Many movies are still sold on optical discs like DVDs and Blu-ray discs.
The information on the optical disc can be accessed only with a compatible player.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compact disc: a small optical disc used to store digital audio or data
  • antonym – magnetic disk: a data storage device that uses a magnetic coating to store data
