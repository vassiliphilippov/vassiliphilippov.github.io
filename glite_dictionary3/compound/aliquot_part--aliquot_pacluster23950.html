aliquot part

noun
exact divisor of a number
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌkwɑt pɑrt / (AmE), / ˈælɪkwət pɑːt / (BrE)
an integer that divides another number exactly without leaving a remainder; an exact divisor of a given quantity.

Examples

aliquot part of 12
All the aliquot parts of 10 are 1, 2, and 5.
Mathematicians use the term aliquot part when discussing factors of a number.
Since 6 divides 18 exactly, 6 is an aliquot part of 18.
