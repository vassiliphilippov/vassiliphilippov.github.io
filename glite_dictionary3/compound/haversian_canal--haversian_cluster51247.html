Haversian canal

noun
small channel in bone containing blood vessels
Pronunciation: / həˈvɜr.ʒən kəˈnæl / (AmE), / həˈvɜː.ʃən kəˈnal / (BrE)
any of the numerous small channels in bone that contain blood vessels and connective tissue, forming a network within compact bone

Examples

Haversian canal in bone
Blood vessels travel through each Haversian canal to supply nutrients to bone cells.
Damage to a Haversian canal can interfere with bone health and repair.
The Haversian canal runs longitudinally through the center of each osteon in compact bone.
