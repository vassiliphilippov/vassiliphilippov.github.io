croton oil

noun
strong purgative oil from Croton seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊtən ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊtən ɔɪl / (BrE)
a thick, acrid, brownish-yellow oil obtained from the seeds of Croton tiglium, formerly used as a powerful purgative

Examples

croton oil as medicine
Because of its potency, croton oil is no longer commonly used in modern medicine.
Early physicians often administered croton oil as a powerful laxative, despite its harsh side effects.
The chemist handled the croton oil with extreme caution due to its toxic properties.
