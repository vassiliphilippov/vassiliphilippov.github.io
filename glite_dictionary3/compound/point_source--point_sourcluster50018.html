point source

noun
single, concentrated source of emission
Pronunciation: / pɔɪnt sɔrs / (AmE), / pɔɪnt sɔːs / (BrE)
a concentrated and identifiable source from which radiation, pollution, or other substances are emitted; typically located at a single, defined spot.

Examples

point source pollution
Scientists measured radiation levels directly at the point source to determine potential hazards.
The factory was identified as a point source of air pollution in the region.
Unlike nonpoint pollution, a point source can be traced to a specific discharge location.

Related Terms

  • antonym – nonpoint source: a source of pollution that is diffuse and not from a single, identifiable origin
