radiation pyrometer

noun
device for measuring temperature remotely via radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən paɪˈrɑmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən paɪˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used to measure the temperature of distant heat sources by focusing radiation onto a sensor.

Examples

radiation pyrometer measures temperature
Because the object was too hot to approach, a radiation pyrometer was employed for a safe temperature reading.
Modern steel plants often rely on a radiation pyrometer for continuous monitoring of molten metal.
The engineer used a radiation pyrometer to determine the furnace temperature without making contact.
