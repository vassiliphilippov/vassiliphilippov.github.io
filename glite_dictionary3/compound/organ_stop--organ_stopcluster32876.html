organ stop

noun
set of organ pipes producing a particular tone
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.ɡən stɑp / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡən stɒp / (BrE)
a set of organ pipes of similar tone quality that produces a particular sound when selected on a pipe organ

Examples

organ stop controls sound
Each organ stop has its own unique timbre and is used for different musical effects.
He adjusted the organ stop to achieve a brighter tone for the hymn.
The cathedral's new organ stop gives a rich, mellow sound to the music.
