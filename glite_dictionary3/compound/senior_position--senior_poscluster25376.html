senior position

noun

(finance, finance-law) a claim, debt, or interest that has priority over other creditors for repayment in bankruptcy, liquidation, or restructuring.

Examples

senior position in bankruptcy
The bank treated the loan as a senior position when organizing claims in the bankruptcy.
Investors prefer bonds that hold a senior position because they are repaid before others.
During negotiations, creditors argued for a senior position in the company's capital structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – senior debt: debt that has priority over other debt in repayment schedules and insolvency.
  • synonym – seniority: the quality or status of having priority in the order of repayment or claims.
  • antonym – subordinated debt: debt that ranks below other debts for repayment and is paid after senior claims.
