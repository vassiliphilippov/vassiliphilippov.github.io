dew point

noun
temperature where dew forms
Pronunciation: / ˈduː ˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdjuː ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
the temperature at which air becomes saturated with water vapor and dew begins to form

Examples

dew point temperature
Meteorologists often mention the dew point in weather forecasts to indicate moisture levels.
On humid summer days, the dew point can make the weather feel even more uncomfortable.
The dew point dropped overnight, making the air feel much cooler in the morning.
