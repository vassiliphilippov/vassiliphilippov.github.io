hyacinth bean

noun
tropical climbing plant with edible pods and seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪəsɪnθ ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəsɪnθ ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a tropical perennial vine with clusters of fragrant purple flowers and maroon pods containing edible seeds; grown ornamentally and as a vegetable, especially in South Asia.

Examples

hyacinth bean vine flowers
A single hyacinth bean plant can quickly cover a trellis with its lush, leafy vines.
The young pods of the hyacinth bean are often cooked and eaten as a vegetable in Indian cuisine.
They planted hyacinth bean vines along the garden fence for their beautiful flowers and decorative pods.
