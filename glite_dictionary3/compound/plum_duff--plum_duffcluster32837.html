plum duff

noun
steamed or boiled dried-fruit pudding
Pronunciation: / plʌm dʌf / (AmE), / plʌm dʌf / (BrE)
a traditional steamed or boiled pudding made with flour, dried fruit such as currants and raisins, and often citron

Examples

slice of plum duff
Her grandmother used to make a rich plum duff every winter holiday.
The sailors enjoyed a serving of plum duff after their dinner on Christmas Day.
Traditional recipes for plum duff often include a generous helping of raisins and currants.
