Rejoicing in the Law

noun
Jewish festival marking end of Torah reading cycle
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ ɪn ðə lɔ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪsɪŋ ɪn ðə lɔː / (BrE)
(Judaism) A Jewish holy day celebrated on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri, marking the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings.

Examples

Rejoicing in the Law festival celebration
As part of Rejoicing in the Law, children often receive sweets and join in the festivities.
