wage concession

noun
agreement to raise employees' wages
Pronunciation: / weɪdʒ kənˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / weɪdʒ kənˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
an agreement by an employer to increase employees' wages, typically as a result of negotiations with a union or workers

Examples

After weeks of negotiation, the workers finally secured a wage concession from the company.
Management announced a wage concession in response to rising inflation and cost of living.
The union is pushing for a significant wage concession in the next contract.
