O-ring

noun
circular gasket for sealing joints
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ rɪŋ / (BrE)
A circular gasket made of rubber or plastic, used to seal joints against high pressure.

Examples

O-ring seal failure
A damaged O-ring can cause a loss of pressure in the hydraulic system.
Engineers chose a high-temperature O-ring for use in the industrial valve.
The mechanic replaced the worn O-ring to stop the oil from leaking.
