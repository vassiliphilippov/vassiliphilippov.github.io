wrist pin

noun
pin joining piston to connecting rod
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪst pɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪst pɪn / (BrE)
A pin that connects a piston to a connecting rod in an internal combustion engine or similar mechanism.

Examples

wrist pin in engine
A broken wrist pin can lead to severe engine damage if not replaced promptly.
During the rebuild, they inspected each wrist pin for signs of scoring or bending.
The mechanic discovered that the wrist pin had worn out, causing a knocking noise in the engine.
