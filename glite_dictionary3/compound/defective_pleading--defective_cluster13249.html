defective pleading

noun
legal pleading with errors or deficiencies
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛktɪv ˈpliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktɪv ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A legal pleading that does not meet the required standards of accuracy or sufficiency in form or substance.

Examples

file a defective pleading
If a defective pleading is not corrected promptly, it may result in adverse consequences for the party at fault.
The judge dismissed the case due to a defective pleading filed by the defendant's lawyer.
The plaintiff was given an opportunity to amend the defective pleading before the court proceeded further.
