new penny

noun
British coin worth one hundredth of a pound (since 1971)
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈpɛni / (AmE), / njuː ˈpɛni / (BrE)
A coin introduced in Great Britain in 1971, worth one hundredth of a pound, and formerly termed to distinguish it from the pre-decimal penny.

Examples

Collectors often seek the first issue of the new penny from the early 1970s.
