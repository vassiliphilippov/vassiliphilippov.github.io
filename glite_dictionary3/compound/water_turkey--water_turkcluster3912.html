water turkey

noun
large blackish water bird (anhinga)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ ˌtɝki / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌtɜːki / (BrE)
A large, blackish water bird of swampy regions in the Americas, also known as the anhinga, with a long neck and pointed bill.

Examples

water turkey swimming
Photographers are drawn to the unique silhouette of the water turkey as it glides through the wetlands.
The water turkey gets its name from its turkey-like tail and habit of swimming with only its neck above water.
We spotted a water turkey drying its wings on a branch above the swamp.
