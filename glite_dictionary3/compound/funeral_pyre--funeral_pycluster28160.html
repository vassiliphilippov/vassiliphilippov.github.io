funeral pyre

noun
wood pile for burning a dead body in a funeral rite
Pronunciation: / ˈfjunərəl paɪr / (AmE), / ˈfjuːnərəl paɪə / (BrE)
A pile of wood on which a dead body is placed and burned as part of a funeral ceremony, especially in some Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

Examples

burn on a funeral pyre
In ancient times, warriors were often cremated on a funeral pyre with their possessions.
Smoke rose slowly from the funeral pyre on the riverbank at dawn.
The body was placed on the funeral pyre as mourners gathered to pay their last respects.
