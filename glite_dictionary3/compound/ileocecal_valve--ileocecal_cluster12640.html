ileocecal valve

noun
valve between small and large intestines
Pronunciation: / ˌɪlioʊˈsiːkəl vælv / (AmE), / ˌɪliəʊˈsiːkəl vælv / (BrE)
a valve between the ileum of the small intestine and the cecum of the large intestine, preventing material from flowing back into the small intestine

Examples

ileocecal valve controls flow
A malfunctioning ileocecal valve can cause discomfort or digestive problems.
During the examination, the doctor checked the functioning of the ileocecal valve.
The ileocecal valve plays a crucial role in regulating the flow of digestive material.
