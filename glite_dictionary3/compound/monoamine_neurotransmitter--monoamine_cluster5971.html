monoamine neurotransmitter

noun
type of neurotransmitter with a monoamine structure
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊ.noʊ.əˈmin ˌnju.roʊˈtræn.smɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əʊ.əˈmiːn ˌnjuː.rəʊˈtræn.smɪ.tə / (BrE)
A neurotransmitter that contains one amino group connected to an aromatic ring by a two-carbon chain, important in the transmission of nerve impulses.

Dopamine, a monoamine neurotransmitter, is involved in movement, motivation, and reward pathways.
Serotonin is a well-known monoamine neurotransmitter that plays a role in regulating mood and sleep.
