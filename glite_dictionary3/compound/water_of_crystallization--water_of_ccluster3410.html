water of crystallization

noun
water bound within crystals of a compound
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔɾər əv ˌkrɪstələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə r əv ˌkrɪstələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
water molecules that are chemically bound within the crystal structure of a compound, typically present in hydrates

Examples

water of crystallization in a crystal
Copper sulfate crystals appear blue due to the water of crystallization contained within their structure.
The loss of water of crystallization can change the color and properties of the salt.
When heated, a hydrate loses its water of crystallization and turns into an anhydrous compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hydrate: a compound containing water molecules bound to its structure
