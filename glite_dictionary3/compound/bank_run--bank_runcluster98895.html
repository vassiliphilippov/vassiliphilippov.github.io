bank run

noun
mass withdrawal of deposits from a bank
Pronunciation: / bæŋk rʌn / (AmE), / bæŋk rʌn / (BrE)
a situation in which a large number of bank customers withdraw their deposits because they believe the bank may fail

Examples

bank run causes panic
A bank run can quickly deplete a bank's reserves, leading to its collapse.
During the economic crisis, several local banks experienced a bank run as panicked customers rushed to withdraw their funds.
The government stepped in to reassure the public and prevent a bank run.
