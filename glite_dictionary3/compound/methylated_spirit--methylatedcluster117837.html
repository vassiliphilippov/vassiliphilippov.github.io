methylated spirit

noun
denatured alcohol used as solvent or fuel
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛθəˌleɪtɪd ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪleɪtɪd ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
ethyl alcohol that has been made undrinkable by adding methyl alcohol or other chemicals, used chiefly as a solvent or fuel
