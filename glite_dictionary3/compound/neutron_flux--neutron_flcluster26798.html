neutron flux

noun
rate of neutron flow through a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.trɑn flʌks / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.trɒn flʌks / (BrE)
the rate at which neutrons pass through a given area, typically measured as the number of neutrons per unit area per unit time

Examples

high neutron flux
An abnormal drop in neutron flux may indicate a problem within the core.
Scientists measured the neutron flux to ensure consistent experimental results.
The reactor's safety depends on the careful monitoring of the neutron flux.
