deadly sin

noun
a grave sin in Christian theology
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdli sɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli sɪn / (BrE)
A grave sin regarded in Christian theology as causing the loss of grace and spiritual death unless repented.

Examples

commit a deadly sin
According to doctrine, a deadly sin leads to spiritual death unless forgiven.
Confession is necessary to absolve one from a deadly sin.
Pride is often considered the most serious deadly sin in Christian teaching.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mortal sin: a sin regarded as leading to damnation unless repented
  • antonym – venial sin: a lesser sin that does not result in the complete loss of grace
