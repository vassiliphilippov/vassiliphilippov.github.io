real number

noun
a number that is rational or irrational
Pronunciation: / ˈriəl ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
any of the numbers, rational or irrational, that can be represented on the number line, including all positive and negative integers, fractions, and decimals, but not imaginary numbers.

Examples

real number on the number line
Every point on the number line corresponds to a unique real number.
Pi is a famous example of an irrational real number.
While 2 and -3 are both real numbers, the square root of -1 is not.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – complex number: a number of the form a + bi, where a and b are real and i is the imaginary unit
  • antonym – imaginary number: a number that has a negative square root, represented as a multiple of the imaginary unit i
  • degree lower – integer: a whole number, positive, negative, or zero, without fractions or decimals
