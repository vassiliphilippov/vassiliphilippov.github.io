geological era

noun
major division of geological time
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈɪərə / (BrE)
A major division of geological time, typically spanning hundreds of millions of years, and usually subdivided into periods.

Examples

the geological era of dinosaurs
Scientists believe the dinosaurs lived during a different geological era from humans.
The Paleozoic is a well-known geological era that ended over 250 million years ago.
The transition from one geological era to another can be marked by mass extinction events.
