homogeneous polynomial

noun
polynomial with terms of the same degree
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈməʊdʒəˌniəs pəˈlɪn.i.əl / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒɪnɪəs pəˈlɪn.i.əl / (BrE)
A polynomial in which every term has the same total degree.

Examples

homogeneous polynomial of degree 3
Every term in a homogeneous polynomial must have the same total degree.
In advanced algebra, homogeneous polynomials are often used to simplify problems involving symmetry.
The equation x² + y² is a homogeneous polynomial of degree 2.
