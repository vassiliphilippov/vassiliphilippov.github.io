to stalk its prey

verb
approach or pursue prey stealthily
Pronunciation: / stɔk ɪts preɪ / (AmE), / stɔːk ɪts preɪ / (BrE)
to approach or pursue prey quietly and carefully, often in order to catch it by surprise

Examples

to stalk its prey silently
At dusk, the owl stalks its prey before swooping in for the catch.
The leopard silently stalked its prey through the tall grass.
You could see the tiger stalking its prey with careful, measured steps.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ambush: to lie in wait in order to attack by surprise
  • synonym – hunt: to chase or search for wild animals as prey
