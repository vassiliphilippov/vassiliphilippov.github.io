rain tree

noun
large tropical American ornamental tree
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪn ˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈreɪn ˌtriː / (BrE)
A large ornamental tropical American tree (Samanea saman) with bipinnate leaves, spherical clusters of flowers with crimson stamens, and seed pods eaten by cattle.

Examples

giant rain tree shade
During flowering season, the rain tree attracts a variety of pollinators with its striking crimson stamens.
Many cattle graze beneath the rain tree, feeding on its nutritious seed pods.
The rain tree provides dense shade and is often planted along roadsides in tropical cities.
