solid bastion

noun
bastion with a completely filled interior
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːlɪd ˈbæstiən / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd ˈbæstɪən / (BrE)
A bastion constructed so that its interior is completely filled, with the ground level even with the top of the rampart and no hollow space in the center.

Examples

massive solid bastion
The castle's defenses included a solid bastion on its northern wall, providing significant strength against attacks.
The engineers recommended constructing a solid bastion to withstand potential siege artillery.
Unlike a hollow bastion, a solid bastion offers greater durability since there are no empty spaces inside.
