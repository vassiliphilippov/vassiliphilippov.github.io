deed poll

noun
legal document signed by one person
Pronunciation: / did poʊl / (AmE), / diːd pəʊl / (BrE)
A legal document executed and signed by one person, especially to officially change their name.

Examples

change name by deed poll
He changed his surname by deed poll after getting married.
Many people in the UK use a deed poll when they wish to change their legal name.
To update your passport, you must provide a certified copy of your deed poll.
