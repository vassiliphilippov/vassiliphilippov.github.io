petty morel

noun
a woody perennial plant of North America with aromatic roots
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛti məˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈpɛti mɒˈrɛl / (BrE)
An unarmed woody perennial plant, found in southeastern North America to Mexico, distinguished by its aromatic roots and panicled umbels, different from wild sarsaparilla.

Examples

wild petty morel plant
Botanists found a rare petty morel during their survey in southern Georgia.
The petty morel is valued for its aromatic roots in traditional medicine.
Unlike wild sarsaparilla, petty morel produces panicled umbels of flowers.
