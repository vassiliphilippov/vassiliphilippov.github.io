junction rectifier

noun
semiconductor with a p-n junction for rectification
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən ˈrɛktəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a semiconductor device with a single p-n junction, used to convert alternating current to direct current

Examples

The junction rectifier is widely used in power supply circuits to convert AC to DC.
