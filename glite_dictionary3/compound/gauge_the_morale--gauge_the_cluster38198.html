to gauge the morale

verb
assess a group's emotional state
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪdʒ ðə məˈræl / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ ðə məˈrɑːl / (BrE)
to assess or estimate the emotional or mental state of a group, especially regarding their attitude and enthusiasm

Examples

to gauge the morale of staff
Before announcing the new policy, the manager attempted to gauge the morale of the staff.
It's important to regularly gauge the morale in any large organization.
Leaders often try to gauge the morale of their teams during times of change.
