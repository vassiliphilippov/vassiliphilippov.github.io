invisible lat syndrome

noun
pretending to have large lat muscles, seen in posture
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɪzəbəl læt ˈsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪzəbəl læt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
an informal term for a condition where a person postures as if they have developed latissimus dorsi muscles (lats), often leading to exaggerated poor posture, despite lacking visible muscle development

Examples

invisible lat syndrome at the gym
He realized his exaggerated posture was due to invisible lat syndrome and corrected it during training.

Videos mocking invisible lat syndrome have become popular among fitness influencers online.
