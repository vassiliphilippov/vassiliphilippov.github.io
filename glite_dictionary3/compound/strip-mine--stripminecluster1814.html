to strip-mine

verb
harvest large amounts of data
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
(figuratively) to extract or harvest very large quantities of data or information from a source, often rapidly or indiscriminately, usually for commercial or analytic use.

Examples

to strip-mine data
The startup plans to strip-mine user data to build detailed consumer profiles for advertisers.
Regulators accused the company of strip-mining public records to sell targeted political advertising lists.
After the merger they aggressively strip-mined server logs to monetize behavioral patterns and preferences.

Related Terms

  • synonym – extract: to remove or obtain data or information from a larger set or source
  • synonym – harvest: to collect large amounts of data or resources for use or analysis
