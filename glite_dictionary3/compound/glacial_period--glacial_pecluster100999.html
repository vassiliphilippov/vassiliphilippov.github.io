glacial period

noun
time when glaciers covered much of Earth
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃəl ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsɪəl ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
a time in Earth's history when large areas were covered by glaciers, especially during the Pleistocene epoch

Examples

the glacial period in history
A drop in global temperatures can trigger a new glacial period.
Scientists study fossils to learn about life during a glacial period.
The most recent glacial period ended around 11,700 years ago.
