Indian hemp

noun
fiber plant cultivated for strong fibers
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən hɛmp / (BrE)
a plant grown for its strong, tough fibers, used to make rope, cloth, or cord (can refer to several species, including those from the East Indies and North America)

Examples

Indian hemp rope making
Indian hemp is cultivated in many countries for its strong, durable fibers.
Some people confuse Indian hemp with true hemp, but they are different species.
The ropes were made from the fibers of Indian hemp.
Botanists often study Indian hemp for its similarities to true hemp species.
Some Indigenous communities valued Indian hemp for its medicinal properties against rheumatism.
The tough fibers of Indian hemp were traditionally used for making fishing lines and nets.
