Kallman's syndrome

noun
congenital disorder with hypogonadism and anosmia
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl mənz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈkæl mənz ˌsɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
A congenital disorder characterized by hypogonadism and an impaired sense of smell, leading to failure of the testicles to mature at puberty.

Examples

Kallman's syndrome affects puberty
Genetic counseling is recommended for families affected by Kallman's syndrome.
Individuals with Kallman's syndrome often fail to develop secondary sexual characteristics without hormone therapy.
The patient was diagnosed with Kallman's syndrome after showing delayed puberty and anosmia.
