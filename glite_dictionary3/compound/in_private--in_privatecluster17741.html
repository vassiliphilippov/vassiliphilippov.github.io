in private

adverb
not in public; privately
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈpraɪvət / (AmE), / ɪn ˈpraɪvət / (BrE)
without other people being present; not in public; in a situation where only those intimately concerned are present.

Examples

talk in private
All sensitive negotiations will be handled in private rather than in front of the entire group.
She told him the news in private so that no one else would hear.
The CEO prefers to give critical feedback in private instead of during meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – in public: with other people present; openly or publicly
  • synonym – privately: without other people being present; not publicly
