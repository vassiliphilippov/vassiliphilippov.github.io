majority rule

noun
governing principle where majority decides
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒɔɹəti ruːl / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒrəti ruːl / (BrE)
the principle or doctrine that the decisions of a group are determined by the greater number of its members

Examples

majority rule in elections
In a democracy, majority rule is often balanced with protections for minority opinions.
Some critics argue that majority rule can lead to the oppression of smaller groups.
The club made its decision based on majority rule, accepting the option favored by most members.
