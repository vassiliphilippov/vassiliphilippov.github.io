business interruption insurance

noun
insurance for business income lost due to interruption
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪznəs ˌɪn.t̬əˈrʌp.ʃən ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
insurance coverage that compensates a business for loss of income and continuing fixed expenses when operations are disrupted by an insured event

Examples

business interruption insurance pays losses
After the fire, their business interruption insurance helped pay for lost income and ongoing rent.
Many companies purchase business interruption insurance to protect themselves against unforeseen disasters.
Without business interruption insurance, the firm struggled to recover financially after the flood.
