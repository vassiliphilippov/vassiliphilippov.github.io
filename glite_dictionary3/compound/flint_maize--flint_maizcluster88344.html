flint maize

noun
maize with hard outer kernels
Pronunciation: / flɪnt meɪz / (AmE), / flɪnt meɪz / (BrE)
A type of maize with kernels that have a hard outer layer enclosing a soft endosperm.

Examples

flint maize kernels
Farmers in the Midwest often grow flint maize for its resistance to pests and drought.
Researchers compared the nutritional content of flint maize with that of other maize varieties.
The hard kernels of flint maize make it ideal for grinding into cornmeal.
