goofing off

noun
avoiding work or duty
Pronunciation: / ˈɡuːfɪŋ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈɡuːfɪŋ ˌɒf / (BrE)
the act of avoiding work or duty, usually by engaging in aimless or idle activities

Examples

goofing off at work
He spent most of the afternoon goofing off at the park with his friends.
The manager noticed that several employees were goofing off instead of finishing their assignments.
Too much goofing off can lead to missed deadlines and unfinished work.
