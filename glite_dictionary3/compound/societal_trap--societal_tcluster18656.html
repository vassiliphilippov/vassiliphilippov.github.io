societal trap

noun
social limitations restricting freedom
Pronunciation: / səˈsaɪətl træp / (AmE), / səˈsaɪətl træp / (BrE)
a situation in which social expectations restrict an individual's freedom or choices

Examples

escape a societal trap
Breaking free from a societal trap often requires challenging long-standing norms and expectations.
Many people find themselves stuck in a societal trap that prevents them from pursuing their true passions.
She realized the career path she chose was a societal trap shaped more by others than by her own desires.
