uterine cervix

noun
lower part of the uterus
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːtərɪn ˈsɜːrvɪks / (AmE), / ˈjuːtərɪn ˈsɜːvɪks / (BrE)
the narrow, lower end of the uterus that forms a canal between the uterus and the vagina

Examples

cancer of the uterine cervix
Certain types of cancer, such as cervical cancer, originate in the uterine cervix.
During a routine examination, the doctor checked the uterine cervix for any abnormalities.
The uterine cervix plays a crucial role during childbirth, helping to guide the baby out of the uterus.
