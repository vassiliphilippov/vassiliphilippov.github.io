acute anterior poliomyelitis

noun
a form of polio affecting nerve cells in the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / əˈkjuːt ænˈtɪriər ˌpoʊlioʊmaɪˈɛlɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ænˈtɪəriə ˌpəʊlɪəʊmaɪˈɛlɪtɪs / (BrE)
an acute viral disease characterized by inflammation of nerve cells in the brain stem and spinal cord, leading to rapid onset of paralysis; commonly known as a form of polio
