malignant hepatoma

noun
cancerous liver tumor
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt hɛˈpætoʊmə / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt hɛˈpætəmə / (BrE)
A cancerous tumor of the liver, also known as hepatocellular carcinoma.

Examples

diagnose malignant hepatoma
A family history of hepatitis B can increase the risk of developing malignant hepatoma.
The patient was diagnosed with a malignant hepatoma after imaging tests identified a large tumor in his liver.
Treatment options for malignant hepatoma include surgery, chemotherapy, and sometimes liver transplantation.
