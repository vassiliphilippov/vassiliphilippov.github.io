gang plow

noun
plow with multiple working shares
Pronunciation: / ɡæŋ plaʊ / (AmE), / ɡæŋ plaʊ / (BrE)
A plow with two or more shares, coulters, and moldboards, designed to work the soil simultaneously.

Examples

use a gang plow in fields
Early settlers improved efficiency by switching from a single plow to a gang plow.
Modern gang plows can have many more shares than those from a century ago.
The farmer used a gang plow to turn over several rows of soil at once.
