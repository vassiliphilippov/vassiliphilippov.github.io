Shi'ite Muslim

noun
member of a branch of Islam
Pronunciation: / ʃiːˈaɪt ˈmʊzləm / (AmE), / ʃiːˈaɪt ˈmʊzlɪm / (BrE)
A member of the branch of Islam that regards Ali as the legitimate successor to Muhammad and rejects the first three caliphs.

Examples

Shi'ite Muslim community leader
As a Shi'ite Muslim, he observes religious practices that differ from those of his Sunni neighbors.
She studied the beliefs of Shi'ite Muslim scholars for her thesis.
The city has a large Shi'ite Muslim population who celebrate unique holidays.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Sunni Muslim: a member of the main branch of Islam, which accepts the first four caliphs
