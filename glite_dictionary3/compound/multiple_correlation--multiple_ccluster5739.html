multiple correlation

noun
statistical predictor using several variables
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪpəl ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical method used to predict the value of one variable by considering two or more other variables together.

Examples

multiple correlation analysis
In the report, multiple correlation helped identify which factors most strongly predict academic performance.
Researchers used multiple correlation to examine how age, diet, and exercise together affect blood pressure.
Statisticians often calculate multiple correlation coefficients to analyze complex datasets with several influencing variables.
