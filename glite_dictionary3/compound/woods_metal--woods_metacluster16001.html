Wood's metal

noun
fusible low-melting-point alloy
Pronunciation: / wʊdz ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / wʊdz ˈmɛt.əl / (BrE)
a fusible alloy of about half bismuth, with lead, tin, and cadmium, which melts at a low temperature (about 160°F or 71°C)

Examples

Wood's metal melts easily
Because Wood's metal is fusible, it is useful for making safety devices.
Fire sprinklers are sometimes equipped with Wood's metal triggers that melt in the heat.
The scientist used Wood's metal because it melts at a relatively low temperature.
