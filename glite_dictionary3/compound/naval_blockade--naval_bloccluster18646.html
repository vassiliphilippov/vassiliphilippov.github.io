naval blockade

noun
military interdiction of sea traffic using ships
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪvəl blɑːˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl blɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
An act of using naval forces to prevent ships from entering or leaving enemy ports, usually to cut off supplies during conflict.

Examples

naval blockade of a port
A naval blockade was declared to restrict all shipping routes in and out of the conflict zone.
During the war, the country suffered severe shortages due to the naval blockade imposed by its enemies.
The naval blockade prevented humanitarian aid from reaching the besieged city for weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – siege: the surrounding of a place to cut off supplies, often used on land
