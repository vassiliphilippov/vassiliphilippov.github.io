parental leave

noun
time off work to care for a child
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɛntəl liv / (AmE), / pəˈrɛntəl liːv / (BrE)
Paid or unpaid time off work granted to an employee to care for a child or make arrangements for the child's welfare, including after childbirth or adoption.

Examples

take parental leave after birth
Employees can take parental leave after the birth or adoption of a child.
She used her parental leave to bond with her newborn daughter.
The company offers generous parental leave for both mothers and fathers.
