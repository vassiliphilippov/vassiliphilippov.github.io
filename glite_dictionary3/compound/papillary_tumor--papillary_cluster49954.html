papillary tumor

noun
benign epithelial tumor forming a rounded mass
Pronunciation: / ˈpæpɪˌleri ˈtuːmər / (AmE), / ˈpapɪləri ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
A benign growth of epithelial tissue forming a rounded mass resembling a small, nipple-like projection.

Examples

papillary tumor in bladder
A papillary tumor is typically benign but may require monitoring for changes.
The biopsy revealed that the mass was actually a papillary tumor rather than a malignant growth.
The surgeon removed a papillary tumor from the patient's bladder.

Related Terms

  • synonym – polyp: a growth projecting from a mucous membrane, often benign
