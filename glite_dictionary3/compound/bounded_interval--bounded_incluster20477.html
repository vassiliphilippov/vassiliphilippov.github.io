bounded interval

noun
interval including its endpoints
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊndɪd ˈɪn.tɚ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈbaʊn.dɪd ˈɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
an interval in mathematics that includes its endpoints

Examples

bounded interval on a line
A bounded interval on the real line includes both the lower and upper endpoints.
In calculus, a function is integrable over a bounded interval.
The set [0,1] is an example of a bounded interval.

Related Terms

  • synonym – closed interval: an interval that includes both endpoints
  • antonym – open interval: an interval that does not include its endpoints
