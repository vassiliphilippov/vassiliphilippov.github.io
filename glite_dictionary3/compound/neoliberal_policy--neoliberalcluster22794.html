neoliberal policy

noun
policy favoring free-market economics
Pronunciation: / ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəl ˈpɑːlɪsi / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈlɪbərəl ˈpɒlɪsi / (BrE)
An economic policy that advocates free markets, privatization, deregulation, and limited government intervention in the economy.

Examples

neoliberal policy supports privatization
Critics argue that neoliberal policy increases inequality and undermines public services.
Many countries adopted neoliberal policy during the 1980s in hopes of boosting economic growth.
The candidate opposed further neoliberal policy, advocating more government regulation instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym – laissez-faire: policy of minimal government intervention in the economy
  • antonym – protectionist policy: policy favoring trade barriers and economic regulation
