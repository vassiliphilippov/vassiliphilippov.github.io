patent ductus arteriosus

noun
congenital heart defect involving an open ductus arteriosus
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtənt ˈdʌktəs ɑːrˌtɪriˈoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt ˈdʌktəs ɑːˌtɪəriˈəʊsəs / (BrE)
a congenital heart defect in which the ductus arteriosus fails to close after birth, allowing abnormal blood flow between the aorta and the pulmonary artery

Examples

patent ductus arteriosus in newborns
After birth, the patent ductus arteriosus often requires medication or surgery to close.
An untreated patent ductus arteriosus can lead to serious complications if not managed properly.
Doctors detected a heart murmur caused by a patent ductus arteriosus during the newborn’s examination.
