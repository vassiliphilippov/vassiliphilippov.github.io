kilogram calorie

noun
unit of heat/energy (food, science)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪl.əˌɡræm ˈkæl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈkɪl.əˌɡræm ˈkæl.ə.ri / (BrE)
A unit of heat equal to the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius at one atmosphere pressure; used especially to measure the energy value of food.

Examples

kilogram calorie in food energy
A single kilogram calorie is often referred to as a 'large calorie' in food science.
Many nutrition labels use the kilogram calorie to indicate the energy content of food.
When discussing diet plans, experts may talk about daily intake in terms of kilogram calories.
