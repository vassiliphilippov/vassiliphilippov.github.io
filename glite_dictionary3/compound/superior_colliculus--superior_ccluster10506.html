superior colliculus

noun
a visual center in the midbrain
Pronunciation: / suˈpɪriɚ kəˈlɪkjʊləs / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə kɒˈlɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
a paired structure on the dorsal midbrain that acts as a principal visual center, connecting signals from the retina to the striate cortex and coordinating visual processing and reflexes

Examples

superior colliculus processes vision
Damage to the superior colliculus can impair an organism's ability to respond to visual stimuli.
Researchers observed neuronal activity in the superior colliculus during the experiment.
The superior colliculus plays a key role in initiating rapid eye movements.
