acoustic delay line

noun
device for delaying signals via sound waves
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (BrE)
a device that delays an electrical signal by converting it into sound waves, which travel through a medium before being converted back to electrical form
