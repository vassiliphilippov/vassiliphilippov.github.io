dry spell

noun
period of inactivity or no rain
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ ˌspɛl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌspɛl / (BrE)
A period during which there is little or no rain, or by extension, a period without success or activity.

Examples

long dry spell
After weeks of little progress, the researchers broke their dry spell with a major discovery.
She was relieved when her inspiration returned after a long dry spell in her art.
The region is suffering through a serious dry spell that threatens local crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drought: a long period of abnormally low rainfall
  • synonym – streak: a continuous period of a particular event or activity
  • antonym – winning streak: a period marked by consecutive wins or successes
