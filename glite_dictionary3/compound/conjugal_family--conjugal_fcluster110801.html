conjugal family

noun
family unit of parents, children, and some grandparents
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.dʒə.ɡəl ˈfæm.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒʊ.ɡəl ˈfæm.ɪ.li / (BrE)
a family unit composed of a married couple, their children, and sometimes the grandparents of one marital partner

Examples

traditional conjugal family
In a traditional conjugal family, the household may include grandparents on one parent's side.
Many sociologists distinguish the conjugal family from the extended family structure.
The conjugal family is more common in urban settings where smaller households prevail.

Related Terms

  • antonym – extended family: a family that extends beyond the nuclear family, including grandparents, aunts, uncles, etc.
  • synonym – nuclear family: a family group consisting of parents and their children
