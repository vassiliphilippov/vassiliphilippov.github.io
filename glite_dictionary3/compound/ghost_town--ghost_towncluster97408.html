ghost town

noun
deserted or abandoned town
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊst taʊn / (AmE), / ɡəʊst taʊn / (BrE)
a deserted or abandoned town, especially one that was once populated or thriving

Examples

an old ghost town
After the mine closed, the settlement became a ghost town almost overnight.
The film was set in a ghost town where tumbleweeds rolled down empty streets.
We visited a ghost town in Nevada that still had old wooden buildings standing.
After the gold mine shut down, the village slowly turned into a ghost town.
By the time we arrived, it was clear the area had become a ghost town.
Walking through the empty streets, he felt as if he were in a ghost town.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abandoned town: a town that has been left by all its population
  • antonym – boom town: a town undergoing rapid growth or expansion, often the opposite of a ghost town
  • antonym – boomtown: town that experiences rapid growth due to sudden prosperity
