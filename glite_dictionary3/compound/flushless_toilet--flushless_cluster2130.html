flushless toilet

noun
toilet using bacteria instead of water
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌʃləs ˈtɔɪlət / (AmE), / ˈflʌʃləs ˈtɔɪlɪt / (BrE)
a type of toilet that does not require water for flushing, relying on bacteria to break down waste instead

Examples

flushless toilet with bacteria
A flushless toilet requires periodic maintenance to keep the bacteria working efficiently.
After installing a flushless toilet, their household water usage dropped significantly.
Many remote cabins use a flushless toilet to conserve water and protect the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – composting toilet: a toilet that treats human waste by composting it with aerobic bacteria
