bone marrow

noun
soft tissue inside bones; blood cell producer
Pronunciation: / boʊn ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
the soft, fatty tissue inside bones that produces blood cells and fills the cavities of most bones in humans and animals

Examples

bone marrow transplant
A transplant of bone marrow was necessary to treat her leukemia.
Chemotherapy can damage the bone marrow and reduce blood cell production.
Doctors extracted bone marrow from his hip for analysis.
