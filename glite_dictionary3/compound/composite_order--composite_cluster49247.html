Composite order

noun
classical Roman architectural style combining Corinthian and Ionic elements
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑz.ɪt ˈɔr.dɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒz.ɪt ˈɔː.də / (BrE)
a classical Roman architectural order that combines the decorative acanthus leaves of the Corinthian order with the volute scrolls of the Ionic order
