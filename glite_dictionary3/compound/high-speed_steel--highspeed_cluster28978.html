high-speed steel

noun
a heat-resistant alloy steel used for cutting tools
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ spiːd ˈstiːl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ spiːd ˈstiːl / (BrE)
an alloy steel that retains its hardness at high temperatures, used mainly for making cutting tools for metalworking

Examples

high-speed steel cutting tools
High-speed steel remains sharp even after prolonged use on tough metals.
She ordered a new set of high-speed steel drill bits for her workshop.
The blades are made from high-speed steel to withstand the heat generated during cutting.
