Friedreich's ataxia

noun
inherited neurological disorder causing muscle weakness
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdraɪks əˈtæksiə / (AmE), / ˈfriːdraɪks əˈtæksiə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disease characterized by progressive damage to the nervous system, causing muscle weakness, loss of coordination, and gait abnormalities, typically starting in childhood.

Examples

Friedreich's ataxia diagnosis
Advances in genetic testing have made it easier to confirm a diagnosis of Friedreich's ataxia.
Children diagnosed with Friedreich's ataxia often show signs of unsteady walking by their early teens.
Early intervention and supportive therapy can help manage the symptoms of Friedreich's ataxia.
