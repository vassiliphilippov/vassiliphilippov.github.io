pigeon hawk

noun
a small falcon of Europe and America
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdʒən hɔk / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn hɔːk / (BrE)
a small falcon (merlin) found in Europe and America, with dark plumage and a black-barred tail, sometimes used in falconry

Examples

wild pigeon hawk
Falconers sometimes train the pigeon hawk due to its quick reflexes and hunting skill.
In North America, the pigeon hawk is commonly seen during migration season.
The pigeon hawk is known for its agility and speed in flight.
