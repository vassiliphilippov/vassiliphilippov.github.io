swine influenza

noun
human illness from swine-origin influenza
Pronunciation: / swa\u026an \u026an\u02c8flu\u02d0\u0259nz\u0259 / (AmE), / swa\u026an \u026an\u02c8flu\u02d0\u0259nt\u0361s\u0259 / (BrE)
a human respiratory illness caused by swine-origin influenza A viruses, especially the H1N1 pdm09 strain responsible for the 2009–2010 pandemic.

Examples

human swine influenza outbreak
During 2009 officials worked to monitor outbreaks of swine influenza and advise the public on prevention.
Researchers sequenced the virus responsible for the swine influenza cases to track how it spread globally.
Vaccination campaigns during the pandemic reduced severe outcomes from swine influenza among high-risk populations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – H1N1 influenza: a subtype of influenza A viruses, including the 2009 pandemic strain associated with swine-origin infections in humans
  • synonym – influenza: an infectious viral disease causing fever and respiratory symptoms
