arteria mesenterica

noun
artery supplying blood to the intestines
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə mɛsˌɛnˈtɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə mɛsˌɛnˈtɛrɪkə / (BrE)
either of the two arteries branching from the aorta and supplying blood to the intestines through the mesentery

Examples

arteria mesenterica supplies intestines
Blockage in the arteria mesenterica can lead to serious intestinal complications.
During the surgery, the arteria mesenterica was carefully preserved.
The arteria mesenterica supplies blood to significant portions of the digestive tract.
