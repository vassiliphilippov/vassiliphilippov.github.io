net income

noun
profit after all expenses and taxes
Pronunciation: / nɛt ˈɪn.kəm / (AmE), / nɛt ˈɪn.kəm / (BrE)
the total profit of a person or business after all expenses, taxes, and deductions have been subtracted from total revenue in a given period

Examples

net income after taxes
After calculating expenses and taxes, their net income was significantly higher than expected.
It's important to distinguish between gross income and net income when reviewing financial statements.
The company reported a record-high net income in the last quarter of the fiscal year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gross income: total income before expenses and taxes
  • synonym – net profit: total earnings after all expenses and taxes
