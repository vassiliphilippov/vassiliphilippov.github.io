primary colour

noun
one of three basic colours for mixing others
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
any of a group of three colours from which all other colours can be made by mixing; typically red, yellow, and blue in painting, or red, green, and blue in light.

Examples

primary colour mixing
Children learn to mix primary colours to create new shades in art class.
Red, blue, and yellow are commonly taught as the primary colours.
The primary colours of light are different from those of paint.
