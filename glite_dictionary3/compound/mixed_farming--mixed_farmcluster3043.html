mixed farming

noun
raising crops and livestock together
Pronunciation: / mɪkst ˈfɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪkst ˈfɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
a system of farming in which crops and livestock are raised together on the same farm

Examples

mixed farming with animals
Mixed farming allows farmers to diversify their income and make better use of resources.
She studied the benefits of mixed farming during her agricultural science degree.
The region is known for its mixed farming, with fields of wheat and herds of cattle side by side.
