financial obligation

noun
requirement to pay money owed
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəl ˌɑːbləˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
an amount of money that a person or organization is legally required to pay to another

Examples

repay a financial obligation
After buying the house, she took on a large financial obligation that would last for many years.
Businesses must meet all financial obligations to avoid legal issues and maintain their reputation.
Students should consider their future financial obligations before taking out large loans for college.
