Geiger tube

noun
ionization tube in a Geiger counter
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪɡər tuːb / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪɡə tjuːb / (BrE)
a small tube serving as the ionization chamber in a Geiger counter, used to detect ionizing radiation

Examples

Geiger tube detects radiation
Older models used a fragile Geiger tube that was easily damaged during fieldwork.
Radiation is detected when ionizing particles pass through the Geiger tube and create an electrical pulse.
The Geiger tube inside the counter must be replaced periodically for accurate radiation measurement.
