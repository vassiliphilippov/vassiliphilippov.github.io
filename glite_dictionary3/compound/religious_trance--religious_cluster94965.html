religious trance

noun
trance from intense religious devotion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs træns / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs trɑːns / (BrE)
a state of altered consciousness brought about by intense religious devotion, characterized by heightened focus and emotion without reduction in bodily functions typical of other trances

Examples

deep religious trance
He entered a religious trance during the prayer ceremony, remaining motionless and deeply absorbed for hours.
Some mystics are believed to have received visions while in a religious trance.
The anthropologist studied the practices that could induce a religious trance in followers.
