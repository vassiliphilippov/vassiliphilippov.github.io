sinus venosus sclerae

noun
canal in the eye draining aqueous humor
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪnəs vɪˈnoʊsəs skˈlɪəˌri / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.nəs vɪˈnəʊ.səs ˈsklɪə.ri / (BrE)
a circular channel in the eye that drains aqueous humor from the anterior chamber into the anterior ciliary veins

Examples

sinus venosus sclerae drains fluid
A blockage in the sinus venosus sclerae can lead to increased intraocular pressure and glaucoma.
Ophthalmologists study the sinus venosus sclerae when diagnosing certain eye diseases.
The sinus venosus sclerae is essential for maintaining the fluid balance within the eye.
