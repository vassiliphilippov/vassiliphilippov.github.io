legal fraud

noun
fraud involving breach of legal duty or trust
Pronunciation: / ˈliɡəl frɔd / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl frɔːd / (BrE)
Fraud that consists of acts, omissions, or concealments breaching a legal or equitable duty, trust, or confidence.

Examples

legal fraud by a trustee
His actions constituted legal fraud because he breached the trust placed in him by the client.
The company was accused of legal fraud after concealing vital information from its investors.
Under the law, legal fraud can lead to both civil and criminal penalties.

  • synonym – fraud: wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
