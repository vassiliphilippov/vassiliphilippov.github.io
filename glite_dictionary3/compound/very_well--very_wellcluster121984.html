very well

adverb
expression of agreement or acceptance
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (AmE), / ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (BrE)
used to express agreement or acceptance, especially to concede a point or to signify readiness to comply, often at the beginning of a sentence.

Examples

very well then
'You may go.' 'Very well,' she replied, gathering her things.
Very well, I will accompany you to the station.
Very well, if you insist, we will start the meeting now.
