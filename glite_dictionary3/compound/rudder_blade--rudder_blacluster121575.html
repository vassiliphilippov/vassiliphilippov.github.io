rudder blade

noun
vertical steering part of a rudder
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdər bleɪd / (AmE), / ˈrʌdə bleɪd / (BrE)
the flat, vertical part of a rudder that moves through the water to steer a boat, ship, or aircraft

Examples

rudder blade of a boat
A damaged rudder blade can significantly affect the steering performance of a sailboat.
During the inspection, the technician noticed a crack in the rudder blade of the yacht.
The pilot adjusted the rudder blade to correct the aircraft's direction in strong winds.
