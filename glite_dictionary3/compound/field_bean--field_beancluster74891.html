field bean

noun
upright plant grown for edible seeds and fodder
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an upright Old World plant (Vicia faba) grown for its large, flat edible seeds and as fodder for livestock

Examples

field bean stew
Some vegetarian recipes use field beans as a protein-rich meat substitute.
The farmers decided to plant field beans this year to enrich the soil and feed their cattle.
The large seeds of the field bean are often used in Mediterranean cuisine.
