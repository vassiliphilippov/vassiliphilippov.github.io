lubricating substance

noun
substance that reduces friction
Pronunciation: / ˈluː.brɪ.keɪ.t̬ɪŋ ˈsʌb.stəns / (AmE), / ˈluː.brɪ.keɪ.tɪŋ ˈsʌb.stəns / (BrE)
A substance that reduces friction between surfaces by making them smooth or slippery.

Examples

apply lubricating substance to metal
Applying a thin layer of lubricating substance can prevent metal parts from wearing out quickly.
Oil is a common lubricating substance used in engines to ensure smooth operation.
The use of a synthetic lubricating substance extends the lifespan of machinery.
