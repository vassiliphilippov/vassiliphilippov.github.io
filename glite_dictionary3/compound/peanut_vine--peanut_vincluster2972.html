peanut vine

noun
plant producing edible peanuts
Pronunciation: / ˈpiː.nʌt vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nət vaɪn / (BrE)
a widely cultivated plant of warm climates, with yellow flowers and pods that develop underground, producing edible peanuts

Examples

peanut vine grows peanuts
After flowering, the stems of the peanut vine bend so the pods can develop below the soil.
Farmers harvest the peanut vines when the pods have fully ripened underground.
The peanut vine thrives in sandy soils and warm climates.
