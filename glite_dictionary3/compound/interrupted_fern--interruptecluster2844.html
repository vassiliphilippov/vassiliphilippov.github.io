interrupted fern

noun
North American fern with band of spore-bearing fronds
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd fɝn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd fɜːn / (BrE)
A North American fern with tall upright divided fronds and a distinctive band of spore-bearing leaflets near the centre of the fertile fronds.

Examples

interrupted fern in forests
Botanists easily recognize the interrupted fern by the gap in its frond where the fertile leaflets appear.
During spring hikes, we spotted several interrupted ferns growing along the stream bank.
The interrupted fern thrives in moist, shady woodlands across eastern North America.
