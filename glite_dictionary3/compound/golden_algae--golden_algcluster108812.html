golden algae

noun
microscopic algae with golden pigments
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ˈældʒi / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈældʒi / (BrE)
a group of microscopic algae characterized by the presence of chlorophyll, carotene, and xanthophyll pigments, giving them a golden color

Examples

golden algae bloom
Blooms of golden algae have caused significant fish kills in some freshwater ecosystems.

The distinctive pigmentation of golden algae makes them easily identifiable under a microscope.
