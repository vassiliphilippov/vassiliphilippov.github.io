negative muon

noun
elementary particle with a negative charge
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈmjuːˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈmjuːɒn / (BrE)
an elementary particle with a negative charge and a half-life of about 2 microseconds, which decays into an electron, a neutrino, and an antineutrino

Examples

a negative muon decays quickly
A negative muon has a much shorter lifespan compared to an electron.
Scientists detected a negative muon as it passed through the particle detector.
The decay of a negative muon produces an electron and two types of neutrinos.

Related Terms

  • synonym – muon: an elementary particle similar to the electron but with greater mass
  • antonym – positive muon: elementary particle similar to the negative muon but with a positive charge
