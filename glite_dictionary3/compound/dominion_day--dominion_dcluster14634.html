Dominion Day

noun
former name for Canada Day, Canadian holiday
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɪnjən deɪ / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjən deɪ / (BrE)
(historical) The former name for Canada Day, a legal holiday in Canada commemorating the confederation and granting of Dominion status in 1867.

Examples

Dominion Day celebrations
Many Canadians used to celebrate Dominion Day with parades, fireworks, and community events.
Some people still refer to July 1st as Dominion Day out of tradition.
The official name Dominion Day was changed to Canada Day in 1982.
