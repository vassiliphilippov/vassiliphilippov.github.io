Invasion Day

noun
commemoration of European settlement in Australia, often controversial
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪʒən deɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪʒən deɪ / (BrE)
A day marking the anniversary of the arrival of European settlers in Australia, observed especially by Indigenous Australians and their supporters as a reminder of colonization and its ongoing impact.

Examples

Invasion Day protests
Debate over whether to change the date of Invasion Day has grown in recent years.
Every year, protests and rallies take place on Invasion Day across Australian cities.
Some people prefer to call the national holiday Invasion Day rather than Australia Day.
