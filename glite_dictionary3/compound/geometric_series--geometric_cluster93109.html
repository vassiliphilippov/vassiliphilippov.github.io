geometric series

noun
mathematical series with constant ratio
Pronunciation: / dʒiˈoʊmətrɪk ˈsɪriz / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒmɪtrɪk ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
A mathematical series in which each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous term by a constant ratio.

Examples

geometric series formula
In financial mathematics, a geometric series is often used to model compound interest.
She explained how a geometric series with a ratio less than one converges to a finite value.
The sum of a geometric series can be calculated using a specific formula.
