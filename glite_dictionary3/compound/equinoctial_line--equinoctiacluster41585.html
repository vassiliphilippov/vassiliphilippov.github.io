equinoctial line

noun
celestial equator; great circle on celestial sphere
Pronunciation: / ˌekwəˈnɒkʃəl laɪn / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈnɒkʃəl laɪn / (BrE)
the imaginary great circle on the celestial sphere, equidistant from the celestial poles, corresponding to the celestial equator

Examples

equinoctial line on the sky
Ancient astronomers used the equinoctial line to track the apparent motion of the sun across the sky.
Stars near the equinoctial line are visible from both the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.
The equinoctial line divides the celestial sphere into northern and southern hemispheres.
