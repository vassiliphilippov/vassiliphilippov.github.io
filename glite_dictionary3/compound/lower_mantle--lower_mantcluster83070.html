lower mantle

noun
deeper part of Earth's mantle
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ɚ ˈmæn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə ˈmæn.tl̩ / (BrE)
the part of the Earth's mantle that lies between the transition zone and the outer core, extending from about 660 to 2,900 kilometers below the Earth's surface

Examples

Earth's lower mantle
Geologists study the lower mantle to understand its role in plate tectonics and Earth's structure.
Seismic waves travel differently through the lower mantle compared to the upper mantle.
The lower mantle extends to a depth where temperatures and pressures are extremely high.

Related Terms

  • antonym – upper mantle: the part of the Earth's mantle above the lower mantle
