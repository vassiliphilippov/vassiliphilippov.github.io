cranial index

noun
ratio of skull width to length (anthropology)
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪniəl ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˈkreɪnɪəl ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
the ratio, expressed as a percentage, of the maximum width to the maximum length of a skull, used to classify skull shapes

Examples

cranial index measurement
A high cranial index suggests that the skull is relatively broad compared to its length.
Anthropologists often use the cranial index when studying human evolution and variation.
The scientist calculated the cranial index of the fossil to determine its classification.
