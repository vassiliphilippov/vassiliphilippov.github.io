malathion poisoning

noun
toxicity from exposure to malathion
Pronunciation: / ˌmæləˈθaɪən ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈθʌɪɒn ˈpɔɪz(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
A toxic condition resulting from inhaling or ingesting the insecticide malathion, characterized by symptoms such as nausea, dizziness, difficulty breathing, and muscle weakness.

Examples

malathion poisoning in workers
Proper protective equipment can help prevent malathion poisoning among agricultural workers.
Symptoms of malathion poisoning include nausea, sweating, and muscle twitching.
The patient was hospitalized due to severe malathion poisoning after an accidental spill in the warehouse.
