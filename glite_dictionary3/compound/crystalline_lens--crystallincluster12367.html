crystalline lens

noun
transparent lens in the eye focusing light on the retina
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪn lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlɪn lɛnz / (BrE)
the transparent, biconvex structure behind the iris in the eye that, together with the cornea, focuses light onto the retina

Examples

the crystalline lens of the eye
Cataracts cause clouding of the crystalline lens, leading to blurred vision.
The crystalline lens helps the eye adjust focus to see objects at various distances.
The surgeon carefully removed the damaged crystalline lens during the procedure.
