sea anchor

noun
device to limit a vessel's drift
Pronunciation: / si ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / siː ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
A device, usually a canvas-covered frame, towed behind a vessel to reduce drift or keep the bow facing into the wind.

Examples

sea anchor in rough water
The sailor deployed the sea anchor to prevent the boat from drifting during the storm.
We used a sea anchor to keep our bow pointed into the oncoming waves.
Without a sea anchor, the yacht would have been pushed far off course by the wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anchor: a heavy object used to moor a vessel to the sea bottom
