peer group

noun
group of people of similar status or age
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪr ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈpɪə ɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of people who are of similar age, background, or social status, considered as influencing each other's attitudes and behavior

Examples

teen peer group
Adolescents are often strongly influenced by their peer group when making decisions.
Her peer group provided valuable support during times of change.
The school encourages students to interact with their peer group in a positive way.
