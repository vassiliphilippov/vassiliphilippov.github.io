easy money

noun
a period of readily available credit
Pronunciation: / ˈizi ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈiːzi ˈmʌni / (BrE)
A situation where credit or loans are readily available and borrowing is simple, often due to low interest rates or relaxed lending standards.

Examples

easy money policy
During the era of easy money, many people bought houses they could not afford.
Experts warned that easy money policies could eventually inflate asset bubbles.
The central bank's decision led to a period of easy money for borrowers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – tight money: an economic condition where credit is difficult to obtain
