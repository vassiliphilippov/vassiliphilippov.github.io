rough bindweed

noun
a species of climbing evergreen plant
Pronunciation: / rʌf ˈbaɪndˌwid / (AmE), / rʌf ˈbaɪndwiːd / (BrE)
a creeping or climbing evergreen plant with spiny, zigzag stems, shiny leaves, and pale-green flowers, native to the Canary Islands, southern Europe, Ethiopia, and India

Examples

rough bindweed climbing fence
Botanists studied the pale-green flowers characteristic of rough bindweed found on the island.
In certain climates, rough bindweed can grow vigorously and cover large areas quickly.
The gardener noticed the rough bindweed climbing over the old stone wall.
