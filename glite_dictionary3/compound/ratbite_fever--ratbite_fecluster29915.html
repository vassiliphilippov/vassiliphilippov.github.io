ratbite fever

noun
disease from a rat or mouse bite
Pronunciation: / ˈrætˌbaɪt ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈrætˌbaɪt ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
either of two infectious diseases transmitted to humans by the bite of a rat or mouse, characterized by fever, headache, nausea, and skin eruptions.

Examples

ratbite fever after a rat bite
After being bitten by a rodent, she was diagnosed with ratbite fever and required immediate treatment.
Cases of ratbite fever are rare but can cause serious illness if not recognized quickly.
The doctor explained that ratbite fever may present with symptoms like fever, chills, and skin rash.
