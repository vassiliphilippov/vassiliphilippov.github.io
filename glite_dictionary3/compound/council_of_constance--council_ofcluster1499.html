Council of Constance

proper noun
historic church council (1414–1418) ending the Great Schism
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈkɑn.stəns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl əv ˈkɒn.stəns / (BrE)
the ecumenical council held from 1414 to 1418 that ended the Great Schism in the Roman Catholic Church by resolving disputes over papal succession

Examples

the Council of Constance ended schism
Many important church reforms were initiated at the Council of Constance in the early fifteenth century.
The Council of Constance played a pivotal role in reuniting the Roman Catholic Church after years of division.
The execution of Jan Hus was a controversial decision made during the Council of Constance.
