control surface

noun
movable device for steering aircraft
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl ˌsɝː.fɪs / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl ˌsɜː.fɪs / (BrE)
a movable device on an aircraft, such as a rudder, aileron, or elevator, used to steer or stabilize the aircraft by altering its aerodynamic forces

Examples

control surface of an airplane
Damaged control surfaces can compromise the safety of an aircraft.
Proper maintenance of each control surface is crucial for the plane's maneuverability.
The pilot adjusted the control surface to maintain level flight during turbulence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aileron: a hinged flight control surface on the wing of an aircraft
  • synonym – elevator: a control surface that controls pitch of an aircraft
  • synonym – rudder: a primary control surface used to steer an aircraft
