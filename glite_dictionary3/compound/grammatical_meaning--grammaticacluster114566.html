grammatical meaning

noun
meaning based on grammatical role
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈmæt̬ɪkəl ˈmiːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmætɪkəl ˈmiːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the aspect of meaning in a word or phrase that is determined by its grammatical role in a sentence, such as tense, number, or case

Examples

grammatical meaning of a word
In the verb 'walked', the '-ed' ending gives the word its grammatical meaning of past tense.
Some words have little lexical meaning and exist primarily to convey grammatical meaning.
The grammatical meaning of a noun can indicate singular or plural form through its ending.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lexical meaning: the meaning of a word independent of its grammatical role
