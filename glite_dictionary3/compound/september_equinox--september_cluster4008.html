September equinox

noun
the autumnal equinox in September
Pronunciation: / sɛpˈtɛmbɚ ˈiːkwɪˌnɑks / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛmbə ˈiːkwɪnɒks / (BrE)
The time at which the sun crosses the celestial equator, occurring around September 22, marking the beginning of autumn in the Northern Hemisphere and spring in the Southern Hemisphere.

Examples

the September equinox marks autumn
Day and night are approximately equal in length at the September equinox.
Many cultures celebrate holidays around the time of the September equinox.
The September equinox marks the start of autumn in the Northern Hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – autumnal equinox: another term for September equinox, marking the start of autumn in the Northern Hemisphere
  • antonym – vernal equinox: the equinox occurring in March, marking the start of spring in the Northern Hemisphere
