ramjet engine

noun
a jet engine requiring high-speed launch
Pronunciation: / ˈræmˌdʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈræm.dʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A type of jet engine that has no moving parts and relies on high-speed forward motion to compress incoming air, which is then mixed with fuel and ignited; it must be launched at high speed to operate.

Examples

ramjet engine powers aircraft
A ramjet engine is efficient at supersonic speeds but cannot produce thrust from a standstill.
Scientists are developing a new ramjet engine for high-speed aircraft.
The missile is powered by a ramjet engine that needs to be accelerated to supersonic speed before ignition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jet engine: an engine using jet propulsion to generate thrust
