derivative instrument

noun
security with value based on another asset
Pronunciation: / dɪˈrɪvətɪv ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪvətɪv ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
a financial security whose value is derived from the value of another asset, such as stocks, bonds, or market indexes

Examples

derivative instrument trading
Financial regulators closely monitor the trading of derivative instruments to ensure stability.
Many investors use derivative instruments to hedge against price fluctuations in the stock market.
The value of a derivative instrument depends on the performance of its underlying asset.
