mortise joint

noun
joint in woodworking using tenon and mortise
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtəs dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪs dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A strong joint made by inserting a projecting tenon on one piece into a corresponding mortise hole in another piece, commonly used in woodworking and carpentry.

Examples

wooden mortise joint
Learning how to make a mortise joint is an essential skill in woodworking.
Many traditional doors are constructed with mortise joints for extra durability.
The craftsman used a mortise joint to connect the table leg securely to the frame.
