fiduciary relation

noun
legal relationship of trust
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃiˌɛri rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəri rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A legal relationship in which one party places trust and reliance on another, who is obligated to act in their best interest.

Examples

fiduciary relation with client
Attorneys and clients share a fiduciary relation that requires honesty and integrity.
In a fiduciary relation, the trustee must always act in the best interests of the beneficiary.
The court examined whether a fiduciary relation existed between the financial advisor and the investor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fiduciary duty: the legal obligation to act in another's best interest
