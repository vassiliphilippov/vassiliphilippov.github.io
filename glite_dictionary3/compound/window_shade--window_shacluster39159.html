window shade

noun
opaque covering for a window
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndoʊ ʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊ ʃeɪd / (BrE)
A piece of opaque material fitted on a roller or frame used to cover or uncover a window to block out light or provide privacy.

Examples

pull down the window shade
He replaced the old window shade with a new, patterned one.
She pulled down the window shade to shut out the bright afternoon sun.
The room was dark because every window shade was drawn.

  • synonym – blind: a device to cover a window for blocking light or privacy
