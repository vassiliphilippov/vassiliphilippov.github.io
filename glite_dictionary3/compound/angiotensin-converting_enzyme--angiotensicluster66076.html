angiotensin-converting enzyme

noun
enzyme that converts angiotensin I to angiotensin II
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒioʊˈtɛn.sɪn kənˈvɝː.t̬ɪŋ ˈɛn.zaɪm / (AmE), / ˌandʒi.əʊˈtɛn.sɪn kənˈvɜː.tɪŋ ˈɛn.zaɪm / (BrE)
a proteolytic enzyme that catalyzes the conversion of angiotensin I into angiotensin II, playing a crucial role in blood pressure regulation

Examples

angiotensin-converting enzyme regulates blood
A deficiency in angiotensin-converting enzyme activity can lead to medical complications.
Researchers measured the levels of angiotensin-converting enzyme to assess its impact on cardiovascular health.
Some blood pressure medications work by blocking the action of the angiotensin-converting enzyme.

Related Terms
