freedom of the seas

noun
principle allowing free travel in international waters
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdəm əv ðə ˈsiz / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm ɒv ðə ˈsiːz / (BrE)
the principle that merchant ships have the right to travel unfettered in international waters, outside any nation's territorial jurisdiction

Examples

freedom of the seas principle
Conflicts can arise when nations challenge the principle of freedom of the seas near their coasts.
Many countries support freedom of the seas to protect global shipping routes.
