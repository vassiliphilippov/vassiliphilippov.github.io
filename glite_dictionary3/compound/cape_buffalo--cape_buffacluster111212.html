Cape buffalo

noun
large African buffalo with curved horns
Pronunciation: / keɪp ˈbʌfəloʊ / (AmE), / keɪp ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
a large, typically aggressive buffalo of southern Africa, with distinctive upward-curving horns, found mainly in game reserves

Examples

wild Cape buffalo
The Cape buffalo is considered one of the most dangerous animals in Africa due to its unpredictable nature.
Unlike the Asian water buffalo, the Cape buffalo has thick, curved horns and a robust build.
We spotted a herd of Cape buffaloes grazing near the river during our safari.
