kidney failure

noun
loss of kidney function
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪdni ˈfeɪljər / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the kidneys lose their ability to excrete waste products and help maintain the body's electrolyte balance.

Examples

chronic kidney failure
Managing chronic conditions like diabetes can help prevent kidney failure.
She was diagnosed with kidney failure and began dialysis shortly thereafter.
The patient's worsening fatigue and swelling were signs of kidney failure.
