prelude to

noun
introduction to something to come
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛlˌjud tu / (AmE), / ˈprɛljuːd tʊ / (BrE)
an event or action that serves as an introduction or precursor to something more important

Examples

prelude to disaster
A sudden drop in temperature can be a prelude to snowfall in the region.
The negotiations were merely a prelude to the much larger agreement signed later.
The rain was a prelude to the storm, signaling what was about to come.
