over-the-counter

adjective
(of securities) not traded on a stock exchange
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
(of securities) not listed or traded on a stock exchange but bought and sold through a network of dealers.

Examples

over-the-counter stocks
Many small technology firms are traded over-the-counter due to their size and limited reporting requirements.
Some investors prefer buying over-the-counter stocks because of the potential for higher returns, despite the risks.
The company raised capital by issuing over-the-counter shares instead of listing on a major exchange.
