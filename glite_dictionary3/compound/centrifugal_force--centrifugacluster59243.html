centrifugal force

noun
apparent outward force on bodies in curved motion
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtrɪfjəɡəl fɔrs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪfjʊɡəl fɔːs / (BrE)
an apparent force that acts outward on a body moving in a curved path around a center, caused by the body's inertia

Examples

centrifugal force in a turn
Engineers must calculate centrifugal force when designing vehicles that turn at high speeds.
The ride at the amusement park creates a strong centrifugal force that presses you against the wall.
When you spin a bucket of water quickly, centrifugal force seems to push the water against the bottom.

Related Terms

  • antonym – centripetal force: the inward force that keeps a body moving in a curved path
