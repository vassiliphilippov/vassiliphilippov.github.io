vapour density

noun
density of a gas relative to hydrogen
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpər dɛnˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə dɛnˈsɪti / (BrE)
the density of a gas or vapour compared to the density of hydrogen under equal conditions

Examples

vapour density of hydrogen
Because hydrogen is the reference, vapour density is always a ratio with no units.
Scientists measured the vapour density of the unknown gas to identify its composition.
The vapour density of oxygen is used to determine its molecular weight in laboratory experiments.
