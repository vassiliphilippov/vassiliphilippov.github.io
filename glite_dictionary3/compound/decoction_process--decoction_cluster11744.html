decoction process

noun
brewing method involving boiling part of the mash
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkɑkʃən ˈproʊˌsɛs / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒkʃən ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a brewing method in which a portion of the mash is removed, boiled, and then returned to the main mash to raise its temperature

Examples

decoction process in brewing
Many homebrewers skip the decoction process in favor of simpler infusion mashing techniques.
The decoction process involves removing some mash, boiling it, and returning it to the mash tun.
Traditional German breweries often use the decoction process to enhance malt flavors in lagers.
