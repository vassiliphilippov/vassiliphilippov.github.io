fake news

noun
dismissive label attacking a report
Pronunciation: / fe6ak nud0z / (AmE), / fe6ak njud0z / (BrE)
a dismissive or rhetorical label used to attack or delegitimize a news item, report, or source—often for political reasons—regardless of whether the information is true.

Examples

call it fake news
The candidate called the opposition's report fake news, refusing to engage with its evidence.
After the study was criticised on social media, pundits labeled it as fake news without checking facts.
She accused the newspaper of publishing fake news to undermine her campaign, though the facts stood.

Related Terms

  • synonym – disinformation: false information deliberately spread to deceive people; often discussed alongside 'fake news'.
  • antonym – news: newly received or noteworthy information, especially about recent events; often contrasted with 'fake news'.
