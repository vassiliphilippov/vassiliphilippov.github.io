go-slow

noun
industrial action where work is intentionally slowed
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊ ˌsloʊ /; / ˈɡoʊ sloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ ˌsləʊ /; / ˈɡəʊ sləʊ / (BrE)
a form of protest in which workers deliberately work more slowly to pressure their employer during a dispute

Examples

stage a go-slow
After talks broke down, the workers began a go-slow to put pressure on the company.
Management warned that a go-slow would seriously disrupt production schedules.
The union threatened a go-slow if their demands were not met.
Management threatened disciplinary action in response to the prolonged go-slow.
Negotiations continued while the staff maintained their go-slow.
The workers began a go-slow that drastically affected production rates.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slowdown: a general reduction in activity or speed, often in the context of labor protest
  • synonym – strike: a refusal to work as a form of protest
  • synonym – work-to-rule: industrial action where workers follow rules strictly to reduce output
