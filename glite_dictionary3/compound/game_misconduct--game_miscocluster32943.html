game misconduct

noun
ice hockey penalty removing a player from the game
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪm mɪsˈkɑndʌkt / (AmE), / ɡeɪm mɪsˈkɒndʌkt / (BrE)
(Ice hockey) a penalty that removes a player from the game for the remainder of play, while allowing the team to substitute another player.

Examples

game misconduct penalty
Receiving a game misconduct meant he could not return for the rest of the match.
Several fans protested when their captain was given a game misconduct and forced to leave the ice.
The referee assessed a game misconduct after the dangerous hit in the second period.

  • synonym – misconduct: improper or unacceptable behavior, especially by a professional person
