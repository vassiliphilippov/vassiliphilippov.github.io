Father of the Church

noun
early influential Christian theologian
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑðər əv ðə tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə əv ðə tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A prominent theologian and writer from the early Christian period (2nd to 7th century) whose teachings helped to define and establish official church doctrine.
