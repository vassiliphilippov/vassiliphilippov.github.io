concurring opinion

noun
judge's supporting legal opinion
Pronunciation: / kənˈkɝːɪŋ əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrɪŋ əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
an opinion written by a judge who agrees with the outcome of a court's decision but wishes to express different or additional reasoning

Examples

judge's concurring opinion
Although he agreed with the decision, he chose to issue a separate concurring opinion.
The Supreme Court's ruling included a lengthy concurring opinion from Justice Jackson.
The judge wrote a concurring opinion to clarify her views on the constitutional issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dissenting opinion: an opinion written by a judge who disagrees with the majority decision of the court
