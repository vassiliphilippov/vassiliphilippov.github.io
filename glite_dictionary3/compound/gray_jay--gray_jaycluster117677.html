gray jay

noun
a northern North American jay bird
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ dʒeɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ dʒeɪ / (BrE)
a jay of northern North America with a black-capped head and no crest, known for its boldness in stealing food

Examples

a gray jay steals food
I had never seen a gray jay before visiting the northern forests of Canada.
The gray jay is known for approaching hikers boldly in search of food.
We spotted a gray jay swooping down to snatch a sandwich from our picnic table.
