straight pin

noun
a type of pin for fastening cloth or paper
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪt pɪn / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt pɪn / (BrE)
A pin made of a short, straight, stiff piece of wire with a pointed end, used for fastening cloth or paper.

Examples

straight pin holds fabric
A straight pin can temporarily hold your measurements in place while you cut the pattern.
Be careful not to prick your finger with a straight pin when working with delicate material.
She secured the fabric with a straight pin before sewing the seam.
