jaboticaba tree

noun
tropical fruit tree native to Brazil
Pronunciation: / ˌʒɑːbəˈtiːkəˌbɑː triː / (AmE), / ˌʒabəˈtiːkəbə triː / (BrE)
A small evergreen tropical tree native to Brazil and the West Indies, grown in Brazil for its edible, tough-skinned, purple, grapelike fruit that grows directly on the trunk and branches; introduced to the southern United States.
