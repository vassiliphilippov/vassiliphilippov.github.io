oil oligarchy

noun
powerful group controlling oil interests
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl əˈlɪɡɑːrki / (AmE), / ɔɪl əˈlɪɡɑːki / (BrE)
A small group of wealthy and powerful people or organizations that control and influence the oil industry in a country or globally.

Examples

the oil oligarchy controls prices
Activists called for more transparency to limit the power of the oil oligarchy.
Many blame the oil oligarchy for stalling renewable energy progress around the world.
The country's oil oligarchy wields enormous influence over economic and foreign policy decisions.
