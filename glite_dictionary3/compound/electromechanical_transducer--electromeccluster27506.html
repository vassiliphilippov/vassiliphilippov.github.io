electromechanical transducer

noun
device converting between electrical and mechanical energy
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊməˈkænɪkəl trænzˈduːsər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊməˈkænɪkəl trænzˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
A device that converts energy between electrical and mechanical forms, either by receiving electrical signals and producing mechanical movement or vice versa.

Examples

electromechanical transducer converts signals
Engineers selected an electromechanical transducer to convert electrical signals into physical vibrations.
Regular maintenance of the electromechanical transducer ensures precise operation in the automated system.
The microphone is an example of an electromechanical transducer commonly used in sound recording.
