meridian line

noun
imaginary line marking longitude
Pronunciation: / məˈrɪdiən laɪn / (AmE), / məˈrɪdiən laɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary line on the Earth's surface connecting the North and South Poles, used to indicate longitude

Examples

meridian line on a map
Ancient astronomers used a meridian line to track the movement of the sun.
His navigation chart showed every meridian line along with degrees of longitude.
The Prime Meridian is the best-known meridian line and passes through Greenwich, England.
