calcium hypochlorite

noun
chemical bleaching agent and disinfectant
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.si.əm haɪ.poʊˈklɔːr.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm hʌɪpəʊˈklɔːrʌɪt / (BrE)
a white chemical compound (Ca(ClO)₂) used as a bleaching agent and disinfectant, especially for water treatment and sanitation.

Examples

calcium hypochlorite disinfects water
Because of its strong bleaching properties, calcium hypochlorite is widely used in laundry and sanitation industries.
Disinfecting drinking water often involves using calcium hypochlorite as a powerful oxidizing agent.
The pool is treated with calcium hypochlorite to keep the water clean and free of bacteria.
