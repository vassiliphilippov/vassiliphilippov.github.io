bridged-T

noun
type of electronic circuit with a bridged T-network
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪdʒd ti / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdʒd tiː / (BrE)
an electronic circuit consisting of a T-network with an added connection (shunt) bridging the two series branches, used especially in filter and oscillator designs

Examples

bridged-T filter circuit
Engineers selected a bridged-T arrangement to achieve the desired frequency response in the design.
The bridged-T is often used in audio filter circuits for precise frequency control.
You can build a simple oscillator by configuring a bridged-T using basic resistors and capacitors.
