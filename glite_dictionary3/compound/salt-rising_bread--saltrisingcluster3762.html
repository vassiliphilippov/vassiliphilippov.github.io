salt-rising bread

noun
white bread leavened by salt-tolerant bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔlt ˌraɪzɪŋ brɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlt ˌraɪzɪŋ brɛd / (BrE)
A type of white wheat bread leavened by salt-tolerant bacteria, typically made with a mixture containing salt and cornmeal or potato pulp.
