mind game

noun
psychological manipulation for advantage
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnd ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a deliberate psychological tactic or manipulation intended to confuse, intimidate, or gain an advantage over someone, especially in a competitive context

Examples

play mind games
During the chess match, he tried to throw his opponent off balance with subtle mind games.
She quickly recognized the mind game he was playing and refused to react.
Some managers use mind games to motivate their employees or keep them alert.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bluff: an attempt to deceive or intimidate an opponent by pretending to have a stronger position
  • synonym – psychological warfare: the use of psychological tactics to undermine an opponent's morale
