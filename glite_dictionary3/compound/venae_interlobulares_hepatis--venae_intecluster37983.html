venae interlobulares hepatis

noun
small veins between liver lobules
Pronunciation: / ˌviː.naɪ ˌɪn.tər.loʊˈbjul.jʊˌlɛərɪz hɛˈpætɪs / (AmE), / ˌviː.naɪ ˌɪn.tə.ləʊˈbjuː.ljʊˌlɛərɪz hɛˈpætɪs / (BrE)
the small veins located between the lobules of the liver, responsible for draining blood from the portal tracts into the liver sinusoids

Examples

The venae interlobulares hepatis play a crucial role in distributing blood through the liver.
