phrenic nerve

noun
nerve that controls the diaphragm
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɛnɪk nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈfrɛnɪk nɜːv / (BrE)
either of a pair of nerves arising from the cervical spinal cord that pass down through the thorax to innervate the diaphragm and control breathing

Examples

phrenic nerve controls breathing
A problem with the phrenic nerve can cause difficulty in breathing.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the phrenic nerve.
The phrenic nerve plays a crucial role in controlling the movement of the diaphragm.
