deflection of light

noun
bending of light rays
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflɛk.ʃən əv laɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛk.ʃən əv laɪt / (BrE)
the bending of light rays as they pass through different media or around objects, often resulting in phenomena like rainbows

Examples

deflection of light by gravity
Einstein's theory predicted the deflection of light by gravity near massive objects like the sun.
Scientists study the deflection of light to understand the properties of different materials.
The deflection of light through water droplets can create a spectacular rainbow after a storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reflection: the return of light rays from a surface
  • synonym – refraction: the change in direction of light as it passes from one medium to another
