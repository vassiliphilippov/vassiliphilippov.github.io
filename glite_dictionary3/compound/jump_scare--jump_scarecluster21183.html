jump scare

noun
sudden scary moment in media
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp skɛr / (AmE), / dʒʌmp skeə / (BrE)
a sudden, unexpected moment in a film, video game, or other media intended to frighten the audience or players, usually accompanied by a loud sound or abrupt visual

Examples

sudden jump scare
Even though I was expecting a jump scare, it still made me flinch.
Many video games use jump scares to increase tension and surprise players.
The horror film used a jump scare to make the entire audience scream.

  • synonym – shock: a sudden upsetting or surprising event or experience
