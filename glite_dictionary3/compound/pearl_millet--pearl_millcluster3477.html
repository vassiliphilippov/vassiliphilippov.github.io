pearl millet

noun
a grain-producing tall grass
Pronunciation: / pɜrl ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / pɜːl ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
a tall grass cultivated for its edible grain, especially in Africa and Asia, and for forage; also used in brewing beer

Examples

pearl millet grain harvest
A traditional African beer is sometimes brewed using fermented pearl millet.
Farmers in northern India grow pearl millet as a staple crop due to its drought resistance.
The nutritional value of pearl millet makes it popular in health foods and livestock feed.
