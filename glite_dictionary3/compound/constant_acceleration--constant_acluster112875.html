constant acceleration

noun
uniform change in velocity over time
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnstənt əkˌsɛləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt əkˌsɛləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a steady increase or decrease in the velocity of an object by equal amounts in equal periods of time

Examples

constant acceleration in motion
In physics class, we studied how constant acceleration affects objects in motion.
The car moved with constant acceleration after the driver pressed the gas pedal steadily.
When an object falls freely near Earth, it experiences constant acceleration due to gravity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – uniform acceleration: acceleration that does not change in magnitude or direction
  • antonym – variable acceleration: acceleration that changes in magnitude or direction over time
