interquartile range

noun
difference between upper and lower quartiles
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkwɔrtaɪl reɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkwɔːtaɪl reɪndʒ / (BrE)
a measure of statistical dispersion, equal to the difference between the upper and lower quartiles of a data set

Examples

interquartile range of scores
The interquartile range gives a sense of how spread out the central 50% of the data are.
To reduce the impact of extreme values, analysts frequently report the interquartile range alongside the median.
When outliers affect the standard deviation, the interquartile range is often more informative.

Related Terms

  • synonym – range: difference between the highest and lowest values in a set
  • synonym – standard deviation: a measure of statistical dispersion indicating how data deviates from the mean
