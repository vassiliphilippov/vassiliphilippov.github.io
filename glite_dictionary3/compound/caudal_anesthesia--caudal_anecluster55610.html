caudal anesthesia

noun
regional anesthesia via injection into the lower spine
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔdəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl ænɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia produced by injecting an anesthetic into the caudal (lower) end of the spinal canal, typically used for surgical procedures involving the lower part of the body

Examples

caudal anesthesia for surgery
Compared to epidural anesthesia, caudal anesthesia is now less commonly performed.
Some pediatric surgeries use caudal anesthesia because it is both effective and safe for children.
The patient received caudal anesthesia before the lower limb surgery.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – epidural anesthesia: regional anesthesia injected into the epidural space of the spinal cord
  • synonym – spinal anesthesia: regional anesthesia injected into the spinal canal but above the caudal region
