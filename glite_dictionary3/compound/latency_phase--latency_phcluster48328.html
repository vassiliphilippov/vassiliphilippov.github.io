latency phase

noun
psychoanalytic stage from age 5-6 to puberty
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪtənsi feɪz / (AmE), / ˈleɪtənsi feɪz / (BrE)
In psychoanalysis, the fourth stage of psychosexual development, occurring roughly from age 5 or 6 until puberty, when sexual impulses are redirected toward other activities.

Examples

Freud's latency phase in childhood
According to Freud, children enter the latency phase before reaching puberty.
During the latency phase, social and intellectual skills often become more prominent.
Teachers may notice that students in the latency phase are less concerned with gender differences.
