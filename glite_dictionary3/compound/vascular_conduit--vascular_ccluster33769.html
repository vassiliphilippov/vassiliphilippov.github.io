vascular conduit

noun
artificial blood vessel for redirecting flow
Pronunciation: / ˈvæskjələr ˈkɑn.du.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈvæskjʊlə ˈkɒn.djuː.ɪt / (BrE)
an artificial blood vessel used in surgery to redirect blood flow to or from a specific area of the body

Examples

implant a vascular conduit
During bypass surgery, a vascular conduit is often used to create an alternative pathway for circulation.
Synthetic vascular conduits have improved outcomes in complex cardiac procedures.
The surgeon implanted a vascular conduit to restore blood flow to the patient's leg.

  • synonym – graft: a piece of living tissue transplanted surgically
