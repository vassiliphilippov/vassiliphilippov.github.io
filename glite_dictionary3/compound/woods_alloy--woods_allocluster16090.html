Wood's alloy

noun
a low-melting-point alloy of bismuth, lead, tin, and cadmium
Pronunciation: / wʊdz ˈælɔɪ / (AmE), / wʊdz ˈælɔɪ / (BrE)
a fusible alloy consisting chiefly of bismuth, lead, tin, and cadmium, which melts at a low temperature (about 160°F or 71°C)
