tomato streak

noun
disease caused by mixed mosaic virus infection in plants
Pronunciation: / təˈmeɪɾoʊ strik / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ striːk / (BrE)
A disease affecting various plants such as tomatoes, potatoes, and peas, caused by a mixed infection of potato and tomato mosaic viruses, resulting in streaks or mottling on the plants.
