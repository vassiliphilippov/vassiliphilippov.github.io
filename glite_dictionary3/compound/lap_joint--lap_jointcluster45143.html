lap joint

noun
joint formed by overlapping materials
Pronunciation: / læp dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / læp dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a joint made by overlapping two pieces of material and fastening them together

Examples

lap joint in woodwork
A properly glued lap joint can be nearly as strong as the original material.
Metal frames are often assembled using a lap joint for added strength.
The carpenter used a lap joint to connect the two wooden boards securely.
