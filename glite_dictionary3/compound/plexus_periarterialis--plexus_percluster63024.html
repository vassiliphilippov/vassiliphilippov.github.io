plexus periarterialis

noun
nerve network accompanying an artery
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛksəs ˌpɛriɑːrˈtɛriəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs ˌpɛrɪɑːˈtɪərɪælɪs / (BrE)
An autonomic network of nerves that runs alongside an artery.

Examples

plexus periarterialis around artery
Damage to the plexus periarterialis can affect the regulation of blood flow.
In surgical anatomy, the plexus periarterialis must be preserved to maintain vascular function.
The plexus periarterialis provides autonomic nerve supply to the walls of major arteries.
