tuberous plant

noun
plant that grows from a tuber
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːbərəs plænt / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbərəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that grows from an underground storage organ known as a tuber, such as a potato or dahlia.

Examples

tuberous plant like potato
Gardeners often grow tuberous plants like begonias for their beauty and resilience.

The potato is perhaps the most widely cultivated tuberous plant in the world.
