blank verse

noun
unrhymed verse, often iambic pentameter
Pronunciation: / blæŋk vɝs / (AmE), / blæŋk vɜːs / (BrE)
unrhymed verse, especially in iambic pentameter, commonly used in English dramatic and narrative poetry

Examples

blank verse in poetry
Many of Shakespeare’s plays are written in blank verse to create a natural flow of speech.
Milton’s 'Paradise Lost' is a famous example of an epic poem composed in blank verse.
Modern poets sometimes choose blank verse over rhymed forms for greater expressive freedom.
