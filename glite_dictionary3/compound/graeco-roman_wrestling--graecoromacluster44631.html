Graeco-Roman wrestling

noun
wrestling style prohibiting holds below waist
Pronunciation: / ˌɡreɪkoʊ ˈroʊmən ˈrɛslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɡriːkəʊ ˈrəʊmən ˈrɛslɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of wrestling in which the use of legs to trip or hold the opponent below the waist is prohibited

Examples

Olympic Graeco-Roman wrestling match
Graeco-Roman wrestling requires great upper body strength since attacks below the waist are forbidden.
He won a gold medal in Graeco-Roman wrestling at the Olympics.
Unlike freestyle wrestling, Graeco-Roman wrestling has strict rules about the use of the legs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – freestyle wrestling: a type of wrestling allowing attacks on the opponent's legs
