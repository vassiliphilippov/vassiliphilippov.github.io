bulk modulus

noun
measure of resistance to compression
Pronunciation: / bʌlk ˈmoʊdʒələs / (AmE), / bʌlk ˈməʊdjʊləs / (BrE)
A measure of a material's resistance to uniform compression, calculated as the ratio of the change in pressure to the resulting fractional change in volume.

Examples

bulk modulus of steel
An increase in temperature can affect the bulk modulus of many substances.
Scientists measured the bulk modulus of the new material to determine its suitability for deep-sea applications.
The bulk modulus of steel is significantly higher than that of rubber, indicating greater resistance to compression.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compressibility: measure of how much a material decreases in volume under pressure
