Tower of Babel

proper noun
biblical tower whose builders' languages were confused by God
Pronunciation: / ˈtaʊ.ər əv ˈbeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə əv ˈbæb.l̩ / (BrE)
In the Bible (Genesis 11:1–9), a tower built by the descendants of Noah in Babylon, whose construction was stopped by God by causing the builders to speak different languages.

Examples

the Tower of Babel story
Many writers use the Tower of Babel as a metaphor for failed communication.
The ancient ruins were once thought to be the site of the Tower of Babel.
The story of the Tower of Babel explains the origin of different languages.
