hard beech

noun
New Zealand tree with hard wood
Pronunciation: / hɑrd bitʃ / (AmE), / hɑːd biːtʃ / (BrE)
A tall New Zealand tree (Nothofagus truncata) known for producing very hard wood used in furniture and construction.

Examples

hard beech wood
Carpenters often choose hard beech when they need a strong, long-lasting material for their projects.
Many forests in New Zealand are dominated by hard beech trees, which are valued for their resilience.
The wood of the hard beech is highly prized for making durable flooring and cabinetry.
