spinal canal

noun
passage in the spine for the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪnəl kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl kəˈnæl / (BrE)
the passage formed by the vertebrae of the spine through which the spinal cord runs

Examples

narrow spinal canal
A narrowing of the spinal canal can lead to a condition known as spinal stenosis.
An injury to the spinal canal can have serious consequences for mobility and sensation.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully examined the spinal canal to avoid damaging the spinal cord.
