blank canvas

noun
an empty space for new ideas or creation
Pronunciation: / blæŋk ˈkæn.vəs / (AmE), / blæŋk ˈkæn.vəs / (BrE)
a surface or situation that is empty and ready to be filled with ideas, creativity, or possibilities

Examples

a blank canvas for ideas
Every new project offers a blank canvas for innovation.
Standing before the blank canvas, the painter felt a rush of inspiration.
With the renovation complete, the living room became a blank canvas for her interior design ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tabula rasa: an absence of preconceived ideas; a clean slate
