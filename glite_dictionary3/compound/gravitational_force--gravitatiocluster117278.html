gravitational force

noun
force of attraction between masses
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl fɔrs / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl fɔːs / (BrE)
the force of attraction between all masses in the universe, especially the pull of the earth on objects near its surface

Examples

Earth's gravitational force
Isaac Newton formulated the law of universal gravitational force in the 17th century.
Objects fall to the ground because of the gravitational force exerted by the Earth.
The gravitational force between Earth and the Moon influences ocean tides.

Related Terms

  • antonym – electromagnetic force: the force responsible for interactions between charged particles
  • synonym – gravity: the natural force that attracts things toward the center of the earth
