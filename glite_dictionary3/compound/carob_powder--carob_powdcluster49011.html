carob powder

noun
powdered substitute for cocoa
Pronunciation: / ˈkær.əb ˌpaʊ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈkær.əb ˌpaʊ.də / (BrE)
A fine powder made from the ground seeds and pods of the carob tree, often used as a substitute for cocoa powder in cooking and baking.

Examples

carob powder in baking
She bought carob powder to bake a chocolate-free cake for her friend with allergies.
Some people use carob powder instead of cocoa to make healthier desserts.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of carob powder as a cocoa substitute.

  • antonym – cocoa powder: powdered substance made from roasted cocoa beans, used in chocolate products
