harsh winter

noun
very cold, severe winter
Pronunciation: / hɑrʃ ˈwɪntɚ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ ˈwɪntə / (BrE)
A winter that is very cold and severe, often causing hardship or damage.

Examples

survive a harsh winter
After a harsh winter, many roads were damaged and needed repairs.
Farmers struggled with the loss of crops due to the harsh winter.
The flowers did not survive the harsh winter last year.
