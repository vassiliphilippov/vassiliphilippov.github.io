Cox-2 inhibitor

noun
anti-inflammatory drug targeting COX-2 enzyme
Pronunciation: / koʊ ɛks tu ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / kəʊ ɛks tuː ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a drug that reduces inflammation and pain by specifically blocking the enzyme cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) without interfering with COX-1 activity, often used to treat arthritis and similar conditions

Examples

Cox-2 inhibitor for arthritis
Long-term use of Cox-2 inhibitors may increase the risk of cardiovascular events, according to recent studies.
Some doctors prescribe Cox-2 inhibitors to patients with chronic arthritis to minimize gastrointestinal side effects.
The development of Cox-2 inhibitors marked a significant advance in pain management for people with joint problems.
