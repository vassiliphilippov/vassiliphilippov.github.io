gymnospermous tree

noun
tree of the gymnosperms division
Pronunciation: / dʒɪmˈnɑːspɜːrməs triː / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒspɜːməs triː / (BrE)
a tree belonging to the division Gymnospermophyta, which produces seeds that are not enclosed in an ovary.

Examples

gymnospermous tree forest
Many gymnospermous trees are evergreens, retaining their needles throughout the year.
The pine is a well-known example of a gymnospermous tree found in temperate forests.
Unlike flowering plants, a gymnospermous tree produces seeds in cones rather than fruits.
