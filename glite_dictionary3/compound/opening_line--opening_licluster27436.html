(in sports betting) the initial odds or point spread posted by bookmakers for a game or event, before betting causes the market to change.

Examples

opening line for betting
The opening line had the visitors favored by two points, surprising many bettors.
Bookmakers adjusted the opening line after early bets showed heavy support for the underdog.
Sports analysts compared the opening line to last week's closing numbers to predict market movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – opening odds: the first set of odds offered on an event before market movement.
  • synonym – point spread: the number of points by which a bookmaker expects one team to beat another; used to balance betting.
