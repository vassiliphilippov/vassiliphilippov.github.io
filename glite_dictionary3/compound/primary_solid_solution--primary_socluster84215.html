primary solid solution

noun
homogeneous solid phase in alloys
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˌsɑlɪd səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˌsɒlɪd səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
A homogeneous solid phase in which atoms of one or more elements are dissolved in the crystal structure of a metal, forming part of an alloy.

Examples

primary solid solution in alloys
A primary solid solution is essential for achieving uniform material properties in many engineering applications.
Copper and nickel form a primary solid solution over a wide range of compositions.
The strength of the alloy increased due to the formation of a primary solid solution.
