stock index

noun
measure of relative stock market performance
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / stɒk ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
An index showing the aggregate value of a selected group of stocks, used to track the performance of a part of the stock market.

Examples

stock index rises sharply
A stock index can help you compare the returns of different financial investments.
Many investors track the performance of a stock index to gauge market trends.
The S&P 500 is a well-known stock index in the United States.
