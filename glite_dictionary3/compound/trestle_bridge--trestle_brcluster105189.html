trestle bridge

noun
bridge supported by trestlework
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛsəl brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛsəl brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bridge composed of a number of short spans supported by closely spaced transverse frames, known as trestles.

Examples

trestle bridge over river
Engineers inspected the wooden trestle bridge after the recent flood to ensure its safety.
Many trestle bridges were constructed in the 19th century for expanding railway lines.
The old railroad trestle bridge is now a popular site for hikers and photographers.
