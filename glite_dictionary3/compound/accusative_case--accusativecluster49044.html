accusative case

noun
grammatical case for direct objects
Pronunciation: / əˈkjuːzətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːzətɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case of a noun or pronoun that serves as the direct object of a verb

Examples

accusative case marks object
In Latin, the accusative case indicates the direct object of a sentence.
Some languages no longer clearly mark the accusative case in their grammar.
Students often confuse the accusative case with the nominative case when learning German.
