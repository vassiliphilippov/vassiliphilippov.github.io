barium enema

noun
X-ray exam using barium contrast in colon
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛr.i.əm ˈɛn.ə.mə / (AmE), / ˈbeə.ri.əm ˈen.ɪ.mə / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a barium sulfate solution is injected into the rectum as a contrast medium and X-ray images are taken to detect abnormalities in the colon

Examples

barium enema procedure
Before the barium enema, patients are usually asked to follow a special diet.
The doctor ordered a barium enema to investigate the cause of her abdominal pain.
