branched chain

noun
chain with side branches (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈbræntʃt tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːntʃt tʃeɪn / (BrE)
A chemical structure consisting of a linear sequence of atoms with one or more side chains attached.

Examples

branched chain molecule
Isomers can be classified as either straight chain or branched chain based on their structure.
Many amino acids have a branched chain structure that affects their properties.
The branched chain hydrocarbons are generally more stable than their straight-chain counterparts.
