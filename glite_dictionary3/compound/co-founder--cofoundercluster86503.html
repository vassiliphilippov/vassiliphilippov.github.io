co-founder

noun
person who helps found something
Pronunciation: / koʊˈfaʊndər / (AmE), / kəʊˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
A person who, together with one or more others, establishes an organization, business, or institution.

Examples

company co-founder
After disagreements with her co-founder, she decided to leave the organization.
Each co-founder brought unique skills to the company, making the partnership strong.
She is the co-founder of a successful tech startup in Silicon Valley.
