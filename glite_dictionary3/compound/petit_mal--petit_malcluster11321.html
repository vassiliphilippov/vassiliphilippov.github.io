petit mal

noun
mild epilepsy with brief loss of awareness
Pronunciation: / pəˌti ˈmɑl /; / ˌpɛti ˈmæl / (AmE), / ˌpeti ˈmɑːl /; / ˌpɛti ˈmæl / (BrE)
a type of epilepsy or seizure where a person briefly loses awareness, usually without convulsions (also called an absence seizure)

Examples

petit mal seizure episode
Children with petit mal may seem to be daydreaming when a seizure occurs.
Her petit mal attacks went unnoticed for years because they were so brief.
The doctor diagnosed his sudden gaps in attention as episodes of petit mal.
After experiencing a petit mal, he often felt confused but quickly recovered.
During her petit mal, she stared blankly for a few seconds before continuing her sentence.
The doctor explained that a petit mal is less dramatic than other types of seizures.

Related Terms

  • synonym – absence seizure: Another term for brief lapses in consciousness, often used instead of 'petit mal' in modern medicine
  • synonym – epilepsy: A neurological disorder marked by recurrent seizures
  • degree higher – grand mal: A severe type of epileptic seizure involving convulsions and loss of consciousness
