Elizabethan collar

noun
cone-shaped protective pet collar
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən ˈkɑːlər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
a protective medical device in the form of a broad, cone-shaped collar placed around the neck of a dog or cat to prevent it from licking or scratching wounds or surgical sites

Examples

Elizabethan collar for a dog
After his operation, the vet gave Max an Elizabethan collar to stop him from licking his stitches.
The sight of her cat in an Elizabethan collar made her smile despite the situation.
Vets often recommend using an Elizabethan collar after surgery to protect healing wounds.
