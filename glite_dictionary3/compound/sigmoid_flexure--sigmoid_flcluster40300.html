sigmoid flexure

noun
S-shaped colon segment before rectum
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡˌmɔɪd ˈflɛkʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡmɔɪd ˈflɛkʃə / (BrE)
the S-shaped curve of the colon that connects the descending colon to the rectum

Examples

the sigmoid flexure of colon
A blockage in the sigmoid flexure may cause abdominal pain and constipation.
During surgery, the doctor examined the patient's sigmoid flexure for signs of disease.
The sigmoid flexure can sometimes be affected by certain digestive disorders.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – descending colon: the part of the colon that passes downward on the left side of the abdomen toward the rectum
  • degree higher – rectum: the final section of the large intestine, terminating at the anus
  • synonym – sigmoid colon: the part of the large intestine closest to the rectum and anus
