abuse of distress

noun
exploitation of emotional distress
Pronunciation: / əˈbjus ʌv dɪˈstrɛs / (AmE), / əˈbjuːs əv dɪˈstrɛs / (BrE)
the act of exploiting or manipulating emotional distress to gain sympathy or personal advantage

Examples

abuse of distress for sympathy
Constant abuse of distress can damage trust in relationships.
He accused her of abuse of distress when she cried to avoid blame.
Some people recognize abuse of distress as an attempt to manipulate others.

Related Terms

  • antonym – compassion fatigue: reduced capacity to empathize, often due to overexposure to others' distress
  • synonym – emotional manipulation: the act of influencing someone's emotions for personal benefit
