threshing floor

noun
area for threshing grain
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛʃɪŋ flɔr / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃɪŋ flɔː / (BrE)
an area, usually a flat piece of ground or a prepared surface, where grain is separated from the husks by threshing or treading

Examples

wheat on the threshing floor
Farmers used animals to trample the wheat on the threshing floor.
In ancient villages, the threshing floor was often a central gathering place after harvest.
The grain was spread out on the threshing floor to be separated from the chaff.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thresh: to separate grain from its husks by beating or treading
