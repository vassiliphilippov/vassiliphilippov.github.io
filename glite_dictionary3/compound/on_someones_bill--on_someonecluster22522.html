on someone's bill

preposition
charged to someone's account
Pronunciation: / ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz bɪl / (AmE), / ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz bɪl / (BrE)
to be charged to a particular person's account; paid for by someone else rather than directly at the time of purchase

Examples

dinner on his bill
Put the meal on my bill and I'll settle everything at checkout.
She insisted that the flowers should be on her bill as a gift to the couple.
The company agreed to put all expenses on his bill for the business trip.
