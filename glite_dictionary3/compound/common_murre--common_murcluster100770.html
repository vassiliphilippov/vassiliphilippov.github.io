common murre

noun
a black-and-white northern seabird
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈmɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmɜː / (BrE)
a large black-and-white seabird of the auk family (Uria aalge), found across the northern Atlantic and Pacific oceans

Examples

common murre colony
The common murre nests in large colonies on coastal cliffs and islands.
Unlike other auks, the common murre lays its egg directly on the bare rock.
