birch rod

noun
bundle of birch twigs used for punishment
Pronunciation: / bɝːtʃ rɑd / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ rɒd / (BrE)
a bundle of twigs from a birch tree, traditionally used for corporal punishment by striking

Examples

birch rod for punishment
Displays in the museum include a birch rod used in 19th-century classrooms.
In Victorian schools, the birch rod was a common tool for disciplining students.
The old judge ordered the offender to be punished with a birch rod.
