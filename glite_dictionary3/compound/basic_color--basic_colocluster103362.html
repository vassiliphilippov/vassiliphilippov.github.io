basic color

noun
a dye with a chromophore in a positive ion
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsɪk ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A dye in which the chromophore is part of a positive ion, often used in textile dyeing and histology.

Examples

basic color dye
Because it binds to acidic components, a basic color is ideal for staining cell nuclei.
Methylene blue is a commonly used basic color in histology.
Some basic colors are employed to dye wool and silk fabrics.
