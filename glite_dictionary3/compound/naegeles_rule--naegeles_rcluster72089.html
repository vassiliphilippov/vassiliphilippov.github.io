Naegele's rule

noun
method for calculating expected delivery date
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːɡɛlz ruːl / (AmE), / ˈneɪɡəlz ruːl / (BrE)
a rule used in obstetrics to estimate a pregnant woman's expected delivery date by subtracting three months from the first day of her last menstrual period and adding seven days

Examples

Naegele's rule for due date
According to Naegele's rule, her expected delivery date is November 18th.
Many pregnancy tracking apps include Naegele's rule as part of their calculation tools.
Obstetricians frequently use Naegele's rule to estimate when a baby is due.
