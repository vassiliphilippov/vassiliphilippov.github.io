sense of balance

noun
bodily ability to maintain equilibrium
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛns əv ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈsɛns əv ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
the ability to maintain bodily equilibrium, regulated by the sensory system in the inner ear that detects head position and movement

Examples

good sense of balance
After his illness, his sense of balance was affected and he found it hard to walk straight.
Gymnasts rely heavily on a strong sense of balance to perform their routines.
The medication restored her sense of balance and reduced her dizziness.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sense of equilibrium: the physiological and perceptual ability to remain upright and steady
