gang plough

noun
plough with multiple blades
Pronunciation: / ɡæŋ plaʊ / (AmE), / ɡæŋ plaʊ / (BrE)
a plough equipped with two or more blades or shares and designed to turn over multiple furrows at once

Examples

large gang plough field
Early agricultural machinery included the gang plough for efficient tillage of expansive farmland.
In some regions, the gang plough revolutionized the way fields were cultivated by allowing several furrows at once.
The farmer used a gang plough to prepare the large field much faster than with a single plough.
