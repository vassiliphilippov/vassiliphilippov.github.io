Georgia pine

noun
a timber pine tree from the southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔrdʒə paɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒə paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree of the southeastern United States, noted for its long needles, twisted branches, and reddish-brown deeply ridged bark; valued for timber

Examples

tall Georgia pine tree
The house was built with beams made of sturdy Georgia pine.
Towering Georgia pine trees lined both sides of the winding country road.
Wildlife found shelter among the twisted branches of the ancient Georgia pine in the forest.
