holy water

noun
water blessed for religious ritual
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊli ˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water that has been blessed by a priest or religious figure, used in ceremonies for purification or protection.

Examples

holy water in church
Many people believe holy water offers protection from evil.
She dipped her fingers in the holy water before entering the church.
The priest sprinkled holy water on the congregation during the ceremony.
