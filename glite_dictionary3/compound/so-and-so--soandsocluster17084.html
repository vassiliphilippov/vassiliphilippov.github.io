so-and-so

noun
a mildly disparaging term for an unpleasant person
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ ən ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ ənd ˌsəʊ / (BrE)
an unpleasant, annoying, or contemptible person; used to refer to someone in a mildly disparaging way when one does not wish to use a stronger word or name

Examples

that so-and-so at work
Everyone avoids that so-and-so because he's always so rude at meetings.
I can't believe that so-and-so took my parking spot again.
Sometimes I wish that so-and-so would mind his own business.
