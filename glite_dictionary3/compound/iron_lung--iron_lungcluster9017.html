iron lung

noun
mechanical respirator for artificial breathing
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪɚn lʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən lʌŋ / (BrE)
A mechanical respirator that surrounds a patient's body and uses changes in air pressure to stimulate breathing, historically used to treat polio.

Examples

an iron lung machine
After contracting polio, the patient had to be placed in an iron lung for several months.
Few hospitals today still have a functioning iron lung on display as a historical artifact.
The invention of the iron lung revolutionized the treatment of respiratory paralysis in the 20th century.
