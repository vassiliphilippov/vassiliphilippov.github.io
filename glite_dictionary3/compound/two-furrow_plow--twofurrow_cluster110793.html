two-furrow plow

noun
plow with two moldboards
Pronunciation: / tu ˈfɜroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / tuː ˈfʌrəʊ plaʊ / (BrE)
A plow designed with two moldboards, allowing it to turn over two furrows of soil simultaneously when drawn by a tractor or horses.

Examples

use a two-furrow plow in field
For smaller operations, a two-furrow plow offers a practical compromise between efficiency and maneuverability.
Modern tractors can easily handle a two-furrow plow even in heavy soils.
The farmer used a two-furrow plow to speed up cultivation of his large field.
