Janus perspective

noun
considering two opposing viewpoints
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒænəs pɚˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˈdʒænəs pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
the practice of viewing a situation from two opposing or contrasting viewpoints

Examples

adopt a Janus perspective
Debate participants were encouraged to adopt a Janus perspective to better understand the complexity of the issue.
Her Janus perspective helped the team appreciate both the risks and opportunities in the proposal.
Many historians apply a Janus perspective when analyzing events from both the winners' and losers' points of view.
