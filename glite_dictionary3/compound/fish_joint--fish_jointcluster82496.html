fish joint

noun
butt joint using fish plates (rails/beams)
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / fɪʃ dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a butt joint formed by bolting fish plates to the sides of two rails or beams to connect them end to end

Examples

fish joint connects beams
Engineers inspected every fish joint to ensure the beams were securely connected.
Proper alignment of the fish joint is essential for safe train operation.
The old railway line was repaired by installing a new fish joint between the rails.
