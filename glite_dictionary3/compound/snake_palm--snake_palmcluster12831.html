snake palm

noun
tropical plant with showy spathe, edible corms
Pronunciation: / sneɪk pɑm / (AmE), / sneɪk pɑːm / (BrE)
A foul-smelling, somewhat fleshy tropical plant of southeastern Asia, cultivated for its edible corms or as an ornamental for its large leaves and showy dark red spathe enclosing a large spadix.

Examples

snake palm edible corms
Some farmers in Asia cultivate the snake palm for its starchy, edible corms.
The snake palm is often grown in greenhouses for its striking foliage.
The dark red spathe of the snake palm stands out among tropical plants.
