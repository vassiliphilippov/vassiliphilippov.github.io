chorionic villus biopsy

noun
prenatal test involving sampling of chorionic villi
Pronunciation: / kɔriˈɑnɪk ˈvɪləs baɪˈɑpsi / (AmE), / kɒriˈɒnɪk ˈvɪləs baɪˈɒpsi / (BrE)
a prenatal medical procedure in which a small sample of tissue is taken from the chorionic villi to test for genetic or chromosomal abnormalities in the fetus

Examples

chorionic villus biopsy for genetic testing
A chorionic villus biopsy can provide important information about the baby's health before birth.
After the chorionic villus biopsy, she had to rest for several hours at the clinic.
The doctor recommended a chorionic villus biopsy to check for genetic disorders early in the pregnancy.
