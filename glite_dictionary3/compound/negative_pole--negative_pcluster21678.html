negative pole

noun
pole of a magnet pointing south
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv poʊl / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv pəʊl / (BrE)
the end of a magnet that points toward the Earth's south when the magnet is freely suspended; also called the south-seeking pole.

Examples

negative pole of a magnet
If you bring the negative pole of one magnet close to the positive pole of another, they attract.
The negative pole is often marked with a minus sign on batteries and electrical diagrams.
When using a compass, the negative pole of the needle aligns with the Earth's south magnetic pole.

Related Terms

  • antonym – positive pole: the end of a magnet pointing north when suspended freely
