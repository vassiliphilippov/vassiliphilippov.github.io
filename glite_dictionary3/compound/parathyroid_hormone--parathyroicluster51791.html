parathyroid hormone

noun
hormone from parathyroid glands controlling blood calcium
Pronunciation: / ˌpæɹ.əˈθaɪ.ˌɹɔɪd ˈhɔːr.moʊn / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈθaɪ.rɔɪd ˈhɔː.məʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the parathyroid glands that regulates levels of calcium and phosphorus in the blood

Examples

parathyroid hormone controls calcium
Abnormal parathyroid hormone production may result in bone disorders.
Low parathyroid hormone can lead to muscle cramps and weakness.
The doctor checked her parathyroid hormone levels to assess her calcium balance.
