charge unit

noun
standard unit for measuring electric charge
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a standard measure of the quantity of electric charge, such as the coulomb.

Examples

charge unit of electricity
Each electron carries a charge unit of negative elementary charge.
The charge unit commonly used in physics is the coulomb.
To calculate total charge, multiply the current by the time in the appropriate charge unit.
