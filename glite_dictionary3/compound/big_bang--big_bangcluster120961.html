big bang

noun
theory of the origin of the universe
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ bæŋ / (AmE), / bɪɡ bæŋ / (BrE)
the theorized cosmic explosion that marked the origin of the universe and the beginning of its expansion, according to cosmology.

Examples

the big bang theory
Before the big bang, time and space as we know them did not exist.
Many scientists study cosmic background radiation to learn more about the events following the big bang.
The big bang theory is widely accepted as the explanation for the origin of the universe.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – cosmic inflation: the rapid expansion of the universe immediately following the Big Bang
  • antonym – steady state theory: alternative theory to the Big Bang, proposing the universe has no beginning or end and remains essentially unchanged
