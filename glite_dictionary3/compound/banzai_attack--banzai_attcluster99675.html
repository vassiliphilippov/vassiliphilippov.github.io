banzai attack

noun
mass reckless military attack
Pronunciation: / ˈbæn.zaɪ əˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈban.zʌɪ əˌtak / (BrE)
A mass, often reckless, attack by troops with little concern for casualties, originally associated with Japanese soldiers who shouted 'banzai' during the charge.

Examples

banzai attack by soldiers
Historians still debate the effectiveness of the banzai attack during the Pacific War.
The Japanese forces launched a banzai attack in a desperate attempt to break through enemy lines.
The soldiers braced themselves as word of a possible banzai attack spread through the ranks.

Related Terms

  • synonym – charge: a sudden, forceful attack, especially by soldiers
  • antonym – retreat: the act of withdrawing troops, the opposite of an attack
