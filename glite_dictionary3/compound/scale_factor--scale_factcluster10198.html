scale factor

noun
number used to multiply dimensions in scaling
Pronunciation: / skeɪl ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / skeɪl ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a number by which all dimensions of an object or figure are multiplied to achieve a proportional scaling

Examples

scale factor of a map
The map has a scale factor of 1:100,000, meaning one unit on the map equals 100,000 units in reality.
To enlarge the drawing, use a scale factor of 2 for all dimensions.
When reducing a model in size, apply a scale factor less than one.

  • synonym – ratio: the relationship in quantity, amount, or size between two or more things
