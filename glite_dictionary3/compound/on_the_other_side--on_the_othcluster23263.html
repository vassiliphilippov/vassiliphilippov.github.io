on the other side

adverb
introducing a contrasting viewpoint
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə ˈʌðɚ saɪd / (AmE), / ɒn ði ˈʌðə saɪd / (BrE)
used to introduce a contrast or an opposing point of view in a discussion or argument

Examples

on the other side of the argument
Many people fear change, but on the other side, it often brings improvement.
She thought the new policy might be unpopular, but on the other side, it could save money.
The city felt crowded and noisy; on the other side, it was lively and full of opportunities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – on the one hand: used to introduce the first of two contrasting points
