epicanthic fold

noun
vertical fold of skin at eye's inner corner
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkænθɪk foʊld / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkanθɪk fəʊld / (BrE)
A vertical fold of skin that covers the inner corner of the eye, common among many East Asian peoples and sometimes seen in certain medical conditions.

Examples

an epicanthic fold of the eye
She explained the genetics behind the epicanthic fold during her anthropology lecture.
Some individuals with Down's syndrome have a prominent epicanthic fold.
The presence of an epicanthic fold is a normal anatomical feature in many East Asian populations.
