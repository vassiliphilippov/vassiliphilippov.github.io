guide fossil

noun
fossil used to date rock layers
Pronunciation: / ɡaɪd ˈfɑːsəl / (AmE), / ɡaɪd ˈfɒsəl / (BrE)
a fossil of an organism that was widespread but lived during a relatively short geological time span, used to identify and date the strata in which it occurs

Examples

guide fossil in rock layers
Scientists use guide fossils to correlate the ages of rocks in different locations around the world.
The discovery of a guide fossil helped geologists accurately date the sedimentary rock layer.
Trilobites are often cited as a classic example of a guide fossil in Cambrian rocks.
