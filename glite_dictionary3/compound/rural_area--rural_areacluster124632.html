rural area

noun
countryside outside towns or cities
Pronunciation: / ˈrʊrəl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈrʊərəl ˈeəriə / (BrE)
an area outside cities and towns, with low population density and open land, often used for farming or as natural habitats

Examples

rural area with farms
Access to public transportation is limited in most rural areas.
Many families in the rural area rely on agriculture as their main source of income.
The school district serves both urban and rural area communities.
Access to healthcare can be limited for people who reside in a rural area.
Many families in this country live in a rural area and make a living through farming.
She moved from a busy city to a peaceful rural area to enjoy a slower pace of life.

Related Terms

  • antonym – urban area: an area within a city or town, characterized by higher population density and developed infrastructure
