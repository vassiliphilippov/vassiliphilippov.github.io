wage slavery

noun
exploitation through very low-paid work
Pronunciation: / weɪdʒ ˈsleɪvəri / (AmE), / weɪdʒ ˈsleɪvəri / (BrE)
A situation in which a person is compelled to work for very low pay under exploitative conditions, often with little control or alternative options.

Examples

trapped in wage slavery
Many workers feel trapped in wage slavery with no hope for advancement.
Some activists argue that wage slavery persists even in modern economies.
The novel explores the psychological effects of wage slavery in industrial societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exploitation: the action of treating someone unfairly in order to benefit from their work
  • synonym – serfdom: the status of being a serf or laborer bound under feudal system
