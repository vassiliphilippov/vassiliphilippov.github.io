ear trumpet

noun
old device to aid hearing
Pronunciation: / ɪr ˈtrʌmpɪt / (AmE), / ɪə ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
a conical device used in the past to collect and direct sound into the ear, aiding people with hearing loss

Examples

old ear trumpet
In the Victorian era, people who were hard of hearing often used an ear trumpet.
She examined the antique ear trumpet displayed in the museum's medical collection.
The old man carried his ear trumpet to church every Sunday to better hear the sermon.
