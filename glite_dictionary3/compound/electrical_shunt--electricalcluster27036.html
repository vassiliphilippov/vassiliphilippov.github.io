electrical shunt

noun
parallel conductor to divert electrical current
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.trɪ.kəl ʃʌnt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɪ.kəl ʃʌnt / (BrE)
a conductor with low resistance connected in parallel with another device to divert some of the electric current

Examples

electrical shunt in circuit
An electrical shunt is commonly used in automotive battery monitoring systems.
