forked tongue

noun
deceitful or duplicitous speech
Pronunciation: / fɔrkt tʌŋ / (AmE), / fɔːkt tʌŋ / (BrE)
deceptive or treacherous speech; the act of saying one thing while meaning another, especially to deceive someone.

Examples

speak with a forked tongue
Promises made with a forked tongue are rarely meant to be kept.
She realized too late that he was speaking with a forked tongue.
The politician's forked tongue eventually led to the collapse of his career.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deceit: the act of deceiving or misleading
  • antonym – honest speech: speech that is truthful and sincere
