gaff topsail

noun
triangular sail above the gaff
Pronunciation: / ɡæf ˈtɑpˌseɪl / (AmE), / ɡaf ˈtɒpseɪl / (BrE)
A triangular fore-and-aft sail set above the gaff, with its foot along the gaff and its luff along the topmast.

Examples

gaff topsail rigged high
Adding a gaff topsail improves a vessel's speed in light winds.
Sailors quickly climbed the rigging to adjust the gaff topsail before the storm hit.
The schooner unfurled her gaff topsail as the wind picked up in the afternoon.
