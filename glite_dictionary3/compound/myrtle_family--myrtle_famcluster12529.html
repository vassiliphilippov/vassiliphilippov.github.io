myrtle family

noun
family of fragrant trees and shrubs (Myrtaceae)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜrtəl ˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtəl ˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of trees and shrubs (Myrtaceae) that typically yield fragrant oils and include myrtle, eucalyptus, and clove.

Examples

myrtle family plants
Cloves, which come from the myrtle family, are commonly used as a spice in cooking.
Eucalyptus is a well-known member of the myrtle family and is valued for its aromatic oils.
Many species in the myrtle family are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
