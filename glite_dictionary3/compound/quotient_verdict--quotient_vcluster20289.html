quotient verdict

noun
improper compromise verdict by averaging jury awards
Pronunciation: / ˈkwoʊʃənt ˈvɝːdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊʃənt ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
A verdict in which jurors agree in advance to individually state the damages they think should be awarded, sum these figures, divide by the number of jurors, and make the result the verdict, regardless of individual opinion; considered improper and unacceptable in law.

Examples

quotient verdict in court
A quotient verdict is not permissible because it undermines individual juror responsibility.
Attorneys sometimes question juries to ensure their decision was not a quotient verdict.
The judge declared a mistrial after discovering the jury had reached a quotient verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compromise verdict: a jury verdict arrived at by each juror conceding part of their opinion to reach a decision collectively
