merit badge

noun
award for scouts' achievement or skill
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛrɪt ˌbædʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪt ˌbadʒ / (BrE)
a badge awarded to members of scouting organizations, especially Boy Scouts, in recognition of achievement or skill in a particular area

Examples

earn a merit badge
He earned his first merit badge for camping last summer.
The scout proudly sewed the new merit badge onto his sash.
To get the merit badge, she completed several challenging community service projects.
