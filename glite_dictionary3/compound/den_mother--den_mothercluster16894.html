den mother

noun
female supervisor of Cub Scouts den
Pronunciation: / dɛn ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / dɛn ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who supervises, cares for, or mentors a group, especially of children or young people (originally in Cub Scouts, but also in other groups)

Examples

den mother leads scouts
As a den mother, she helped young scouts earn their badges and learn new skills.
My mother volunteered to be the den mother for my younger brother's Cub Scout group.
The den mother organized a fun camping trip for her den this summer.
In the start-up office, Jane took on the role of den mother, making sure everyone felt welcome and supported.
Sarah was the den mother for the local Cub Scout troop, organizing activities and caring for the children.
Since their parents worked late, Mrs. Lee became the unofficial den mother for the neighborhood kids after school.
