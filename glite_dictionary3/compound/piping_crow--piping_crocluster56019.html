piping crow

noun
black-and-white mimicking bird
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪpɪŋ kroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ krəʊ / (BrE)
A crow-sized black-and-white bird, known for its ability to mimic sounds and often kept in cages.

Examples

piping crow mimics sounds
I saw a beautiful piping crow perched on the garden fence, repeating the noise of a whistle.
Many bird enthusiasts keep a piping crow for its ability to imitate household sounds.
The piping crow is admired for its impressive mimicry of other birds' calls.
