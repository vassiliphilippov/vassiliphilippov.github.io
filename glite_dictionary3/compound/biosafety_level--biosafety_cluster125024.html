biosafety level

noun
degree of lab safety against harmful biological agents
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈseɪf.ti ˌlɛv.əl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈseɪf.ti ˌlɛv.əl / (BrE)
a designated level of safety regarding exposure to infectious agents, based on standardized laboratory practices, safety equipment, and facility design

Examples

biosafety level 3 laboratory
Different microorganisms require different biosafety level precautions to ensure safety.
The laboratory was upgraded to biosafety level 3 to work with more dangerous pathogens.
To handle Ebola virus, strict biosafety level protocols must be followed.
