to blow the coals

verb
fan or ignite a fire by blowing on coals
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ðə koʊlz / (AmE), / bləʊ ðə kəʊlz / (BrE)
to make a fire burn hotter or ignite by blowing air onto the coals

Examples

to blow the coals for fire
Before adding more wood, remember to blow the coals so the fire stays hot.
He crouched by the hearth and began to blow the coals with careful breaths.
Old campfire manuals often instruct you to blow the coals gently to revive a dying flame.
