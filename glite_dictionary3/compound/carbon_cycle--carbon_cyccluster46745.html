carbon cycle

noun
thermonuclear reaction in stars
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a thermonuclear process occurring in the cores of some stars, in which carbon acts as a catalyst to fuse hydrogen into helium

Examples

stellar carbon cycle
Astrophysicists study the carbon cycle to understand how stars produce energy.
The carbon cycle in massive stars enables the fusion of hydrogen at higher temperatures.
Unlike the proton-proton chain, the carbon cycle dominates in hotter, heavier stars.

Related Terms

  • synonym – CNO cycle: a process in stars where carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen act as catalysts for hydrogen fusion
