prisoner of war censorship

noun
military censorship of POW communications
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzənər əv wɔr sɛnˈsɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈprɪzənə əv wɔː sɛnˈsɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
The official monitoring and restriction of communication to and from prisoners of war and civilian internees by military authorities.

Examples

prisoner of war censorship on letters
During World War II, all letters sent by prisoners were subject to prisoner of war censorship before reaching their families.
Historians often study prisoner of war censorship to understand how sensitive information was controlled during conflicts.
The Red Cross monitored prisoner of war censorship to ensure compliance with international conventions.
