pulmonic plague

noun
airborne form of the plague affecting the lungs
Pronunciation: / pʌlˈmɑnɪk pleɪɡ / (AmE), / pʌlˈmɒnɪk pleɪɡ / (BrE)
A rapidly progressing and often fatal form of plague that affects the lungs and spreads through the air from person to person.

Examples

deadly pulmonic plague outbreak
Historically, outbreaks of pulmonic plague have devastated entire communities within days.
Patients with pulmonic plague develop severe lung symptoms and can transmit the infection by coughing.
Rapid medical intervention is crucial in cases of suspected pulmonic plague to prevent further spread.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bubonic plague: the most common form of plague affecting lymph nodes
  • synonym – pneumonic plague: an alternate term often used for pulmonic plague
