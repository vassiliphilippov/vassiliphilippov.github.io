Manila hemp

noun
fiber from the abaca plant of the Philippines
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (BrE)
a strong, flexible fiber obtained from the abaca plant of the Philippines, used for making ropes, textiles, and other products

Examples

Manila hemp fiber rope
Many specialty papers use Manila hemp fibers because of their strength.
She bought a basket woven from Manila hemp at the local market.
The ropes on the old sailing vessel were made of Manila hemp, prized for its durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abaca: a species of banana native to the Philippines, source of Manila hemp
