dauphin of France

noun
heir apparent to the French throne
Pronunciation: / ˌdoʊˈfæ̃n əv fræns / (AmE), / ˈdəʊfæ̃ əv ˈfrɑːns / (BrE)
the title given to the heir apparent to the throne of France, especially during the period from 1350 to 1830

Examples

the dauphin of France becomes king
In many plays, the dauphin of France is portrayed as both ambitious and youthful.
Louis became the dauphin of France after his father's coronation as king.
The dauphin of France was educated extensively in preparation for his future role as king.

Related Terms

  • synonym – prince: a male royal rank below king, often but not always the heir
