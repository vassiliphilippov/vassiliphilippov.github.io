record book

noun
official compilation of records or data
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkərd ˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌbʊk / (BrE)
a book or compilation in which official facts, data, or achievements are systematically recorded, especially notable accomplishments.

Examples

record book of achievements
He set a new high score that will go down in the record book.
The athlete's achievements are prominently listed in the record book.
Whenever there is a question about policy, we check the record book for clarification.

  • synonym – record: an official written account of facts or events
