Cape of Good Hope

proper noun
former province of South Africa
Pronunciation: / keɪp əv ɡʊd hoʊp / (AmE), / keɪp əv ɡʊd həʊp / (BrE)
a former province in western South Africa, now divided into several modern provinces, originally named after the nearby Cape of Good Hope.

Examples

the Cape of Good Hope province
During the colonial era, the Cape of Good Hope played a significant role in trade between Europe and Asia.
He was born in the Cape of Good Hope when it was still a unified province.
The history of the Cape of Good Hope is closely tied to Dutch and British colonization in southern Africa.
