fraternal twin

noun
twin from a separate egg; non-identical twin
Pronunciation: / frəˈtɜrnəl twɪn / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəl twɪn / (BrE)
either of two twins who are born at the same time but develop from two separate fertilized eggs and may not be identical in appearance.

Examples

fraternal twin siblings
Doctors explained that fraternal twins can be of different sexes and have unique physical traits.
Even though they’re fraternal twins, they share a very close bond.
My brother and I are fraternal twins, so we don’t look exactly alike.

Related Terms

  • antonym – identical twin: either of two twins developed from a single fertilized egg, genetically identical
