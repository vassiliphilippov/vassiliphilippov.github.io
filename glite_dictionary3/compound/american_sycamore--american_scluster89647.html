American sycamore

noun
large North American plane tree
Pronunciation: / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈsɪk.əˌmɔr / (AmE), / əˌmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈsɪk.ə.mɔː / (BrE)
a very large deciduous tree of eastern and central North America, with broad leaves and distinctive mottled bark, also known as the plane tree (Platanus occidentalis)

Examples

majestic American sycamore tree
Many city parks in the Midwest are lined with American sycamore trees, admired for their impressive size and shade.
The American sycamore is easily recognized by its peeling, patchy bark and massive trunk.
The leaves of the American sycamore turn yellow and brown in the autumn before falling.
