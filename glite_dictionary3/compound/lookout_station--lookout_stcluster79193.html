lookout station

noun
structure for wide surveillance view
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊkˌaʊt ˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlʊk.aʊt ˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A structure built in a high place to allow someone to observe a wide surrounding area, usually for surveillance or safety purposes.

Examples

mountain lookout station
From the lookout station on the hill, you could see ships approaching from miles away.
Storm warnings were communicated quickly from the lookout station to the nearby villages.
The forest rangers manned the lookout station during fire season to spot any signs of danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym – observation post: a position or structure used for observing and reporting activity
  • synonym – watchtower: a tall structure designed to observe or guard a place
