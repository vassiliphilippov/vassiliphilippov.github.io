laryngeal vein

noun
vein draining the larynx
Pronunciation: / lɛˈrɪndʒiəl veɪn / (AmE), / ləˈrɪndʒɪəl veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that drain blood from the larynx.

Examples

laryngeal vein drains blood
An obstruction in the laryngeal vein can lead to swelling of the vocal cords.
During surgery, care was taken not to damage the laryngeal vein.
The laryngeal vein carries deoxygenated blood away from the larynx.

Related Terms

  • antonym – laryngeal artery: artery supplying blood to the larynx
