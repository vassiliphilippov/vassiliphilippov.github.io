Lot's wife

noun
woman from the Bible turned into a pillar of salt
Pronunciation: / lɑts waɪf / (AmE), / lɒts waɪf / (BrE)
a woman in the Old Testament who was turned into a pillar of salt after looking back at the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah, contrary to God's command

Examples

Lot's wife turned to salt
In literature, 'turning into Lot's wife' is sometimes used as a metaphor for being unable to let go of the past.
Some sculptures depict Lot's wife as a solitary figure gazing backward.
The story of Lot's wife is a warning against disobedience in religious teachings.
