stomach pump

noun
device for removing stomach contents
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌmək pʌmp / (AmE), / ˈstʌmək pʌmp / (BrE)
a medical device used to remove the contents of the stomach by suction, typically in cases of poisoning or overdose

Examples

use a stomach pump in hospital
Advances in medicine have reduced the need for the traditional stomach pump in many cases.
After ingesting the poison, he was rushed to the hospital and treated with a stomach pump.
The doctor used a stomach pump to clear the patient's digestive tract quickly.
