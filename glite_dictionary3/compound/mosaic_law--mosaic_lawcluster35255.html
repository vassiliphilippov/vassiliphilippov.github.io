Mosaic law

noun
biblical laws given through Moses
Pronunciation: / moʊˈzeɪɪk lɔ / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk lɔː / (BrE)
the body of laws given by God to the Israelites through Moses, chiefly detailed in the first five books of the Old Testament, including the Ten Commandments and other rules of religious observance

Examples

Mosaic law in the Bible
Debates about the relevance of Mosaic law continue in religious and legal scholarship today.
Many aspects of traditional Jewish practice are based on the Mosaic law as outlined in the Torah.
The Mosaic law set specific rules regarding diet, worship, and social conduct for the Israelites.
