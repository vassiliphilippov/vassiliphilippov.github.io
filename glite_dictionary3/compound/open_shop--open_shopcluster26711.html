open shop

noun
workplace with no union membership requirement
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ʃɒp / (BrE)
a place of employment where workers are not required to join a labor union or pay union dues as a condition of employment

Examples

an open shop policy
Labor disputes arose when the factory changed from a closed shop to an open shop.
Some workers prefer an open shop because they do not want to pay union dues.
The company operates as an open shop, so employees can choose whether to join the union.
