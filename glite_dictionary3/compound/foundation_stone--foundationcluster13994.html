foundation stone

noun
ceremonial stone marking start of building
Pronunciation: / faʊnˈdeɪʃən stoʊn / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃən stəʊn / (BrE)
A stone laid with ceremony to mark the beginning of construction of a building, often inscribed with details of the date and founder.

Examples

lay a foundation stone
An inscription on the foundation stone commemorates the building's original architects.
Every year, the city celebrates the day the foundation stone was laid.
The mayor placed the foundation stone of the new hospital during a special ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cornerstone: a stone at the corner of a building, often ceremonial or symbolic
