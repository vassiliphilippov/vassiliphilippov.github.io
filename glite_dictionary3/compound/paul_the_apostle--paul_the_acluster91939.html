Paul the Apostle

proper noun
early Christian missionary and biblical writer
Pronunciation: / pɔl ði əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / pɔːl ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
An early Christian missionary and writer of several New Testament Epistles, originally named Saul, regarded as an Apostle despite not being present at the Last Supper.

Examples

Paul the Apostle writes letters
Paul the Apostle is credited with writing many of the letters found in the New Testament.
Before his conversion, Paul the Apostle was known as Saul of Tarsus.
Many churches are named after Paul the Apostle due to his influence on Christianity.
