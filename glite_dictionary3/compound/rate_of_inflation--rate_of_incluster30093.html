rate of inflation

noun
percentage increase in prices over time
Pronunciation: / reɪt əv ɪnˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / reɪt əv ɪnˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
the percentage at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises over a specific period, usually annually or monthly

Examples

high rate of inflation
A sudden increase in the rate of inflation can erode people's purchasing power.
Economists often analyze trends in the rate of inflation to predict future economic conditions.
The rate of inflation reached its highest point in a decade last year.
