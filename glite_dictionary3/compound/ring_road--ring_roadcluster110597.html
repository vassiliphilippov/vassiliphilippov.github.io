ring road

noun
road encircling a city or town
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ roʊd / (AmE), / rɪŋ rəʊd / (BrE)
A main road or highway that circles around a city or town, allowing traffic to avoid passing through the urban center.

Examples

busy ring road
Drivers are advised to use the ring road to avoid roadworks in the central district.
Many European cities have a ring road to help divert heavy traffic away from the city center.
The new ring road has greatly reduced downtown congestion during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beltway: a highway passing around an urban area
  • synonym – bypass: a road passing around a town rather than through it
