spinal anesthesia

noun
anesthesia by injection near the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪnəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl ˌanɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
a type of anesthesia in which an anesthetic is injected into the area around the spinal cord to numb the lower half of the body, typically for surgery

Examples

spinal anesthesia for surgery
Complications from spinal anesthesia are rare but can include headaches and low blood pressure.
Some women choose spinal anesthesia during childbirth to manage pain.
The surgeon recommended spinal anesthesia for the procedure to reduce recovery time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – epidural anesthesia: anesthesia produced by injection into the epidural space around the spinal cord
