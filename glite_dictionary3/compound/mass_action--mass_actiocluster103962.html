mass action

noun
neurological principle of cortical unity
Pronunciation: / mæs ˈækʃən / (AmE), / mæs ˈakʃən / (BrE)
(in neurology) the principle that the cerebral cortex functions as a unified system, with large regions of neural tissue participating in all complex brain activities.

Examples

principle of mass action
Lashley's work supported the idea of mass action, challenging the notion of strict localization of brain functions.
Researchers studying memory loss often discuss the implications of mass action in brain function.
The theory of mass action suggests that damage to one part of the cortex can be partially compensated by other areas.
