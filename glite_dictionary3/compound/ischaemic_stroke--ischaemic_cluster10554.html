ischaemic stroke

noun
stroke caused by blocked blood flow to the brain
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskiəmɪk ˈstroʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈsiːəmɪk ˈstrəʊk / (BrE)
a type of stroke caused by an interruption in the blood supply to the brain, usually due to a clot blocking a blood vessel

Examples

suffer an ischaemic stroke
Quick treatment is crucial in cases of ischaemic stroke to restore blood flow and prevent brain damage.
The patient was diagnosed with an ischaemic stroke after doctors found a blood clot blocking an artery in the brain.
Unlike a hemorrhagic stroke, an ischaemic stroke is typically caused by a blockage rather than bleeding.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hemorrhagic stroke: stroke caused by bleeding in the brain
