sand cherry

noun
a small shrub with purplish-black fruit
Pronunciation: / sænd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / sand ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a small North American shrub growing in sandy soil, with small purplish-black fruit that is scarcely edible

Examples

sand cherry bush
Although the sand cherry produces fruit, it is generally not considered suitable for eating.
Some gardeners plant sand cherry as an ornamental shrub because of its beautiful foliage.
The sand cherry thrives in well-drained, sandy soils along the edge of forests.
