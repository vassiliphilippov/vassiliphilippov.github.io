sleeping partner

noun
partner who invests but remains inactive or anonymous
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
A partner in a business, especially one who provides capital but does not take part in its management or is not publicly associated with it.

Examples

sleeping partner invests money
As a sleeping partner, she avoids publicity and lets others handle the business affairs.
He invested in the company as a sleeping partner, preferring to remain behind the scenes.
The sleeping partner contributed significant funds but had little say in daily operations.
