screw thread

noun
helical ridge on a screw or bolt
Pronunciation: / skru ˈθrɛd / (AmE), / skruː ˈθrɛd / (BrE)
a raised helical ridge running around the shaft of a screw, bolt, or similar fastener, used to convert rotational motion to linear motion or to fasten objects together

Examples

screw thread on a bolt
A screw thread allows a screw to grip wood or metal securely as it turns.
The screw thread on this bolt is damaged and won't hold tightly.
You should measure the diameter and pitch of the screw thread before buying a replacement.
