quick-eared

adjective
having keen hearing
Pronunciation: / kwɪk ɪrd / (AmE), / kwɪk ɪəd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having keen or acute hearing.

Examples

quick-eared owl
Owls are known for being quick-eared hunters, able to locate prey by sound alone.
Thanks to her quick-eared nature, she noticed the faintest cry for help in the distance.
The quick-eared detective overheard the suspect's whispered confession from across the room.
