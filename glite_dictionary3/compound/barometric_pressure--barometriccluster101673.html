barometric pressure

noun
atmospheric pressure measured by a barometer
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛrəˈmɛtrɪk ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˌbærəˈmɛtrɪk ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by the atmosphere, measured using a barometer

Examples

barometric pressure drops suddenly
Changes in barometric pressure can affect how some people feel physically.
Pilots monitor barometric pressure to ensure accurate altitude readings.
The barometric pressure dropped suddenly before the storm arrived.
