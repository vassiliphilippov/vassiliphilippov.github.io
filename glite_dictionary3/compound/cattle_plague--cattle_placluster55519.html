cattle plague

noun
a severe viral disease of cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt̬l̩ pleɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈkætl̩ pleɪɡ / (BrE)
an acute, highly infectious viral disease of cattle, usually fatal, marked by fever, diarrhea, and inflammation of mucous membranes; also known as rinderpest.

Examples

cattle plague outbreak
Scientists worked for decades to develop a vaccine against cattle plague.
The outbreak of cattle plague devastated herds across the region in the nineteenth century.
The symptoms of cattle plague include high fever and severe diarrhea in infected animals.
