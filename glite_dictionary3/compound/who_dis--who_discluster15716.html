informal inquiry about someone's identity
Pronunciation: / huː dɪs / (AmE), / huː dɪs / (BrE)
(informal) Used to ask who is speaking or texting, especially when the sender's identity is unknown or not saved.

Examples

who dis in a text
I got a new phone, who dis? Let me save your number.
Someone messaged the group chat and just wrote 'who dis?'
You texted me last night, but I didn't recognize the number—who dis?
