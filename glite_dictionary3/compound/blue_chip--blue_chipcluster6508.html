blue chip

noun
stock of a stable, reputable company
Pronunciation: / bluː tʃɪp / (AmE), / bluː tʃɪp / (BrE)
a nationally well-known company stock considered a safe investment due to its stable value and reliable dividends

Examples

blue chip stock investment
He diversified his portfolio by purchasing several blue chip companies' shares.
Investors often regard blue chip stocks as safer during economic downturns.
The financial advisor recommended blue chip investments for long-term stability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – penny stock: a stock trading at a relatively low price, typically with higher risk
