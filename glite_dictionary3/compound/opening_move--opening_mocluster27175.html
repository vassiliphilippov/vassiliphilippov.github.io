opening move

noun
first action in a series
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpənɪŋ muːv / (AmE), / ˈəʊp(ə)nɪŋ muːv / (BrE)
the first action in a series of planned steps, especially at the start of a game, competition, or negotiation

Examples

opening move in chess
He considered his opening move carefully before making the first chess play.
Her opening move in the negotiations was to set out her terms clearly.
The candidate's opening move was to address the biggest issue facing the voters.
