to command respect

verb
earn admiration and authority
Pronunciation: / kəˈmænd rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːnd rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to earn admiration and authority from others through one's behavior, qualities, or position

Examples

command respect from others
Her honesty and dedication commanded respect from everyone on the team.
It was clear that the judge commanded respect in the courtroom.
The old professor commands respect with his deep knowledge and calm demeanor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demand respect: to insist that others show respect, often without necessarily deserving it
  • synonym – earn respect: to gain respect through one's actions or qualities
