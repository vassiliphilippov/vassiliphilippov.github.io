Russian dandelion

noun
perennial dandelion grown for rubber-rich roots
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌʃ.ən ˈdæn.dəˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃ.ən ˈdæn.dɪˌlʌɪ.ən / (BrE)
a perennial dandelion (Taraxacum kok-saghyz) native to Kazakhstan, cultivated for its fleshy roots that contain high levels of natural rubber

Examples

Russian dandelion rubber plant
Farmers in Central Asia are growing Russian dandelion for the sustainable production of natural rubber.
Researchers are developing new methods to increase the latex yield in Russian dandelion roots.
The Russian dandelion was once used as an alternative source of rubber during wartime shortages.
