O level

noun
basic exam formerly taken in UK secondary schools
Pronunciation: / oʊ ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / əʊ ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
(in the UK) a now mostly discontinued public examination formerly taken by secondary school students, representing the basic level of a subject; replaced by GCSEs.

Examples

O level exam results
Many older employees took O level exams, which are no longer available today.
She passed her O levels in mathematics and English before leaving school.
To enroll in the advanced course, you must have at least a C in your O level science.
