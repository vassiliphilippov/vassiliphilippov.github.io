index case

noun
first identified case in disease study
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌdɛks keɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɛks keɪs / (BrE)
the earliest documented case of a disease that is included in an epidemiological study, often used to help trace the source and spread of infection

Examples

first index case reported
Researchers traced the outbreak back to the index case in the small village.
The index case was identified as a traveler returning from abroad.
The hospital staff worked to isolate contacts of the index case to prevent further spread.

Related Terms

  • synonym – patient zero: the first person to be infected in an outbreak
