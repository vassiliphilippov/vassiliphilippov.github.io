infantile fixation

noun
arrested development at an infantile stage
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnfənˌtaɪl fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪnfəntaɪl fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a psychological condition in which emotional or psychological development is arrested at an early, infantile stage, often leading to immature behaviors or responses

Examples

infantile fixation on parents
Freud described infantile fixation as remaining stuck at a particular early stage of development.
Some adults exhibit infantile fixation, behaving in childlike ways when under stress.
Therapists may address infantile fixation to help patients progress emotionally.
