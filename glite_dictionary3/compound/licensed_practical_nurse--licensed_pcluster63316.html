licensed practical nurse

noun
nurse licensed to give basic care
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsənst ˈpræktɪkəl nɝːs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsənst ˈpræktɪkəl nɜːs / (BrE)
A nurse who has completed the required training and is licensed by a state to provide basic medical care to patients under the supervision of registered nurses and doctors.

Examples

After her surgery, a licensed practical nurse visited her at home to help with medication and wound care.
Many hospitals depend on licensed practical nurses to provide routine patient care and support.
To become a licensed practical nurse, you must pass an approved training program and a licensing exam.
