Saint Lawrence Seaway

proper noun
waterway linking Atlantic Ocean to Great Lakes
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈlɔrəns ˈsiˌweɪ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns ˈsiːweɪ / (BrE)
a system of locks, canals, and channels in Canada and the United States that allows ships to travel between the Atlantic Ocean and the Great Lakes via the Saint Lawrence River

Examples

ships use the Saint Lawrence Seaway
Many cities along the Great Lakes benefitted from the construction of the Saint Lawrence Seaway.
Ships from Europe can enter the Great Lakes through the Saint Lawrence Seaway.
The completion of the Saint Lawrence Seaway revolutionized shipping in North America.
