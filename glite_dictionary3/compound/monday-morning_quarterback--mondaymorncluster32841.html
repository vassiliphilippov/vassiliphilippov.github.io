Monday-morning quarterback

noun
someone who critiques events after the fact
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌn.deɪ ˌmɔːr.nɪŋ ˈkwɔːr.t̬ɚ.bæk / (AmE), / ˈmʌn.deɪ ˌmɔː.nɪŋ ˈkwɔː.tə.bæk / (BrE)
a person who criticizes or offers advice about an event after it has already happened, especially with the benefit of hindsight

Examples

a Monday-morning quarterback after failure
After the project failed, everyone became a Monday-morning quarterback with their own opinions.
Don't be a Monday-morning quarterback; try supporting the team instead.
It's easy to be a Monday-morning quarterback when you weren't involved in the decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backseat driver: a person who gives unwanted advice or criticism to someone doing something
