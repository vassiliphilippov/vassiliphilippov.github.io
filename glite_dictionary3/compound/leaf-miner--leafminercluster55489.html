leaf-miner

noun
insect larva that feeds within leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈlif ˌmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌmaɪnə / (BrE)
an insect whose larva burrows into and feeds on leaf tissue, especially a small moth or fly of the family Gracilariidae

Examples

leaf-miner damage on leaves
Gardeners often struggle to control leaf-miners in their vegetable patches.
Some leaf-miner species can severely damage crops if left untreated.
The leaf-miner causes visible trails on the upper surfaces of the leaves.
