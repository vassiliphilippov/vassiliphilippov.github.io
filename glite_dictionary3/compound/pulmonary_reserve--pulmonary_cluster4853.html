pulmonary reserve

noun
additional breathing capacity of the lungs
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊlməˌnɛri rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
the additional volume of air the lungs can inhale and exhale when breathing at maximum capacity, especially during physical stress.

Examples

pulmonary reserve during exercise
After years of smoking, his pulmonary reserve was greatly reduced, making even mild exercise difficult.
Athletes often have a higher pulmonary reserve compared to non-athletes, allowing them to perform intense physical activities.
Doctors measure a patient's pulmonary reserve before major surgery to assess how well their lungs can cope with stress.
