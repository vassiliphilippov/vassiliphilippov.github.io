beacon light

noun
warning light tower for ships
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːkən laɪt / (AmE), / ˈbiːkən laɪt / (BrE)
a tower equipped with a light that serves as a warning to passing ships about dangerous areas such as shoals

Examples

beacon light warns ships
A storm damaged the offshore beacon light, making it harder for sailors to avoid the reefs.
Mariners relied on the beacon light to warn them of treacherous shoals during nighttime voyages.
The old beacon light stood on the rocky shore, guiding ships safely through the fog.
