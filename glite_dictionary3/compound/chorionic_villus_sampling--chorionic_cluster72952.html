chorionic villus sampling

noun
prenatal test sampling placental tissue
Pronunciation: / kɔriˈɑnɪk ˈvɪləs ˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / kɒriˈɒnɪk ˈvɪləs ˈsæmplɪŋ / (BrE)
a prenatal test in which a small sample of tissue is taken from the chorionic villi to test for birth defects early in pregnancy

Examples

chorionic villus sampling for genetic testing
Because she was over thirty-five, she was offered chorionic villus sampling to check for chromosomal abnormalities.
Some parents choose chorionic villus sampling over amniocentesis for earlier diagnosis of possible conditions.
The doctor explained that chorionic villus sampling could detect genetic disorders early in pregnancy.
