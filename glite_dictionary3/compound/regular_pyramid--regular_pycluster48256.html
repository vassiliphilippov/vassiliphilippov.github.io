regular pyramid

noun
pyramid with a regular polygon base and symmetric apex
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr ˈpɪrəˌmɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə ˈpɪrəmɪd / (BrE)
a pyramid with a base that is a regular polygon and a vertex positioned directly above the center of the base, so all lateral faces are congruent isosceles triangles

Examples

a regular pyramid in geometry
A regular pyramid has congruent triangular faces that meet at a single apex.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a regular pyramid.
The ancient Egyptians did not build regular pyramids because their bases were not always perfect polygons.
