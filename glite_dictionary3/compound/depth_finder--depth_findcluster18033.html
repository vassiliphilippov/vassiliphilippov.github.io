depth finder

noun
instrument measuring water depth
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛpθ ˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈdɛpθ ˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
a navigational instrument used for measuring the depth of water, typically by means of ultrasound or radar

Examples

depth finder on a boat
He relied on the depth finder to avoid running aground in shallow waters.
Our boat is equipped with a modern depth finder that shows the underwater terrain.
The captain checked the depth finder before navigating the narrow channel.
