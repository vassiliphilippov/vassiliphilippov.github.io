equinoctial circle

noun
celestial equator; great circle between poles
Pronunciation: / ˌikwəˈnɑkʃəl ˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈnɒkʃəl ˈsɜːkl̩ / (BrE)
the great circle on the celestial sphere that lies midway between the celestial poles, coinciding with the celestial equator.

Examples

equinoctial circle on the sky
Ancient astronomers used the equinoctial circle as a reference when charting the stars.
The equinoctial circle divides the sky into northern and southern hemispheres.
The path of the Sun crosses the equinoctial circle at the equinoxes.
