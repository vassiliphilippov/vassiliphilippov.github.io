nitric bacteria

noun
bacteria converting nitrites to nitrates
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtrɪk bækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrɪk bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
soil bacteria that convert nitrites into nitrates, playing a key role in the nitrogen cycle

Examples

nitric bacteria enrich soil
Nitric bacteria play an essential role in enriching soil fertility by converting nitrites into nitrates.
Farmers rely on nitric bacteria in the soil to enhance crop growth naturally.
Without nitric bacteria, the crucial step of turning nitrites into nitrates would not occur efficiently in the nitrogen cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym – denitrifying bacteria: bacteria that convert nitrates into nitrogen gas or nitrogen compounds
  • synonym – nitrifying bacteria: bacteria that convert ammonia to nitrites or nitrites to nitrates
