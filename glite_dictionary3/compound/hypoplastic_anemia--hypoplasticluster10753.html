hypoplastic anemia

noun
anemia from underactive bone marrow
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pəˈplæs.tɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈplæs.tɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of anemia caused by the bone marrow not producing enough blood cells; can progress to aplastic anemia.

Examples

hypoplastic anemia diagnosis
Early treatment is important to prevent hypoplastic anemia from progressing to a more severe form.
Patients with hypoplastic anemia may experience fatigue, weakness, and increased risk of infection.
The doctor diagnosed her with hypoplastic anemia after a series of bone marrow tests.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – aplastic anemia: anemia caused by the body's inability to produce blood cells
