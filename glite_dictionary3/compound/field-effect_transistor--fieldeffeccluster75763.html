field-effect transistor

noun
type of transistor controlled by an electric field
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ɪˌfɛkt trænˈzɪstər / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɪˌfɛkt trænˈzɪstə / (BrE)
a transistor in which most current flows through a channel whose resistance is controlled by an electric field applied perpendicular to the channel

Examples

field-effect transistor circuit design
A field-effect transistor is commonly used in integrated circuits for its high input impedance.
Designers selected a field-effect transistor because it consumes less power than other types.
The performance of your circuit can often be improved by replacing a bipolar transistor with a field-effect transistor.
