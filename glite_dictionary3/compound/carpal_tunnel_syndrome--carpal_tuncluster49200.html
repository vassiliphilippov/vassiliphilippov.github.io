carpal tunnel syndrome

noun
nerve disorder of the wrist causing hand symptoms
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑr.pəl ˈtʌn.əl sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pəl ˈtʌn.əl ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A painful condition caused by compression of a nerve in the wrist, leading to numbness, tingling, or weakness in the hand and fingers.

Examples

carpal tunnel syndrome from typing
Office workers are at higher risk for developing carpal tunnel syndrome due to repetitive typing tasks.
Prolonged use of vibrating tools can increase the likelihood of carpal tunnel syndrome.
She underwent surgery to relieve the symptoms of carpal tunnel syndrome.
