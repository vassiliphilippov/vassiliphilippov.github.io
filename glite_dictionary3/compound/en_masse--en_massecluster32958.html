en masse

adverb
all together; as a group
Pronunciation: / ɑn ˈmæs / (AmE), / ɒ̃ ˈmæs / (BrE)
All together as a group; in a body or collectively.

Examples

en masse leave the room
Shareholders voted en masse to reject the proposal.
The children left the classroom en masse as soon as the bell rang.
The demonstrators marched en masse through the city center.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collectively: as a group; together
  • antonym – individually: one at a time, separately
