dithered colour

noun
colour created by mixing dots in digital graphics
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪðɚd ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪðəd ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A colour produced by closely arranged dots of different colours, giving the visual effect of a new colour, commonly used in digital graphics to simulate more colours than a display can physically show.

Examples

dithered colour in images
Modern monitors rarely need dithered colour because they can display millions of shades directly.
The dithered colour made the old computer game graphics look smoother.
You can achieve subtle shading in pixel art by using dithered colour techniques.
