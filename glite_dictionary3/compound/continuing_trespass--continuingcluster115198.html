continuing trespass

noun
persistent legal trespass
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪn.ju.ɪŋ ˈtrɛs.pæs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.ɪŋ ˈtrɛs.pæs / (BrE)
A legal violation in which an act of trespass persists as long as the offensive object or condition remains on another’s property.

Examples

continuing trespass of land
Allowing polluted water to flow onto someone's property is an example of continuing trespass.
The court found that the company's parked trucks amounted to a continuing trespass on private land.
The illegal structure left on the neighbor's land constituted a continuing trespass until it was removed.

  • synonym – trespass: the unlawful entry onto another's property
