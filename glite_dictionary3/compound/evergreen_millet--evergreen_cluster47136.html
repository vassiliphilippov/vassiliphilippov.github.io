evergreen millet

noun
perennial grass used as fodder; invasive pest
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvərˌɡrin ˈmɪlət / (AmE), / ˈɛvəˌɡriːn ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
A tall perennial grass that spreads by creeping rhizomes, cultivated for fodder and considered an invasive pest in some regions.

Examples

evergreen millet fodder grass
Evergreen millet is widely grown as fodder for livestock in tropical climates.
Farmers often struggle to control the spread of evergreen millet in their cultivated fields.
The rapid growth of evergreen millet makes it both valuable for forage and challenging as a weed.
