merino sheep

noun
breed of sheep valued for fine wool
Pronunciation: / məˈriːnoʊ ʃiːp / (AmE), / məˈriːnəʊ ʃiːp / (BrE)
a breed of white sheep originally from Spain, known for producing a heavy fleece of exceptionally fine and high-quality wool

Examples

merino sheep wool quality
Australian merino sheep are famous for the softness and quality of their wool.
He purchased a flock of merino sheep to improve his wool production.
The farm specializes in breeding merino sheep for the textile industry.
