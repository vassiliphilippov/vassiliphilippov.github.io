radiant energy

noun
energy in the form of electromagnetic radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiənt ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiənt ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
energy transmitted in the form of electromagnetic radiation, such as light, radio waves, or X-rays, and capable of traveling through a vacuum

Examples

radiant energy from sunlight
Infrared lamps use radiant energy to keep food warm in restaurants.
Scientists measure radiant energy to study the effects of ultraviolet light on plants.
Solar panels convert radiant energy from the sun into electricity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – electromagnetic radiation: radiation consisting of waves of energy, such as light or X-rays
  • antonym – thermal energy: energy related to the temperature of matter
