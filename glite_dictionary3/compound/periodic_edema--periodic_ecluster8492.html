periodic edema

noun
recurrent, sudden skin swelling (medical condition)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪriˈɑdɪk ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪk ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by recurrent, sudden swelling of areas under the skin, usually resolving within 24 hours, most often seen in young women and commonly triggered by allergic reactions to certain foods or drugs

Examples

periodic edema after eating seafood
Periodic edema often disappears within 24 hours without leaving any permanent damage.
Patients with periodic edema are commonly advised to avoid foods or medications that might trigger the reaction.
The physician diagnosed her with periodic edema after observing the recurrent swelling episodes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – angioedema: sudden swelling of skin or mucous membranes, usually due to allergy
