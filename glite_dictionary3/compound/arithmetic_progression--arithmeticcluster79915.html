arithmetic progression

noun
sequence of numbers with a constant difference
Pronunciation: / əˌrɪθmətɪk prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌærɪθˈmɛtɪk prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) a sequence of numbers in which the difference between consecutive terms is constant.

Examples

arithmetic progression of numbers
Each row in the table increases by five, creating an arithmetic progression.
The sequence 3, 6, 9, 12 forms an arithmetic progression with a common difference of 3.
To solve the problem, identify the first term and common difference of the arithmetic progression.
