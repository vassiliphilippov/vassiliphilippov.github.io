elevated railroad

noun
a railway on a raised track above streets
Pronunciation: / ˌɛləˌveɪtəd ˈreɪlˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˌveɪtɪd ˈreɪlˌrɒd / (BrE)
a railway powered by electricity that runs on a track raised above street level, typically supported by columns or framework

Examples

city elevated railroad
Many commuters rely on the elevated railroad for their daily travel across town.
The city expanded its elevated railroad to serve more neighborhoods.
The noise from the passing elevated railroad can be heard throughout the day.
