flare path

noun
airstrip outline marked with guiding lights
Pronunciation: / flɛr pæθ / (AmE), / fleə pɑːθ / (BrE)
a line or arrangement of lights marking the edge of an airstrip to guide pilots in landing or taking off at night or in poor visibility

Examples

flare path lights runway
During the blackout, the flare path was lit only moments before the plane landed.
Engineers carefully inspected each light along the flare path before the night’s first flight.
The pilot searched for the flare path to align his final approach in the heavy fog.
