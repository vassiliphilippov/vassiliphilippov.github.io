private treaty

noun
direct sale agreement without auction
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvət ˈtriti / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət ˈtriːti / (BrE)
a sale of property by direct agreement between the seller and the buyer, without the involvement of a public auction or intermediary

Examples

private treaty sale
Many rural properties change hands by private treaty rather than by auction.
The house was sold through private treaty after negotiations with a single buyer.
They decided to offer the business for sale via private treaty instead of going to public tender.

Related Terms

  • antonym – auction: public sale where goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
