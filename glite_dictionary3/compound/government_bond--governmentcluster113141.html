government bond

noun
security issued by a government
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌvɚnmənt bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənmənt bɒnd / (BrE)
a security issued by a government, especially the United States Treasury, representing a loan made by investors to the government, typically considered a very safe investment

Examples

buy a government bond
He diversified his portfolio by purchasing several government bonds from different countries.
Many investors view government bonds as a safe haven during periods of economic uncertainty.
The yield on a 10-year government bond dropped to a new low yesterday.

Related Terms

  • antonym – municipal bond: a bond issued by a local government or its agencies
  • synonym – treasury bond: a US government bond with a maturity of more than ten years
