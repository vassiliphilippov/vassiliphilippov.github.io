fixed star

noun
star thought to be stationary in ancient astronomy
Pronunciation: / fɪkst stɑr / (AmE), / fɪkst stɑː / (BrE)
a star that, in ancient and medieval astronomy, was believed to remain stationary in the sky relative to other stars, as opposed to planets which were thought to move

Examples

a fixed star in the sky
Early astronomers distinguished between a planet and a fixed star based on observed movement.
Galileo's observations challenged the traditional idea of the fixed star.
In ancient astronomy, a fixed star was thought to remain motionless in the sky.
