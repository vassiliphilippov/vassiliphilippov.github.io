greenhouse gas

noun
gas that traps heat in Earth's atmosphere
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriˌnhaʊs ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhaʊs ˌɡas / (BrE)
a gas, such as carbon dioxide or methane, that contributes to the greenhouse effect by absorbing infrared radiation, trapping heat in the Earth's atmosphere

Examples

carbon dioxide greenhouse gas
Carbon dioxide is the most prominent greenhouse gas released by human activity.
Methane, another potent greenhouse gas, is released from livestock and landfills.
Reducing greenhouse gas emissions is crucial for tackling climate change.

Related Terms

  • synonym – methane: a greenhouse gas produced by natural and human activities
