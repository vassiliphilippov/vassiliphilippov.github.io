ionization tube

noun
instrument for measuring ionizing radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən tuːb / (AmE), / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən tjuːb / (BrE)
a device used to measure the amount of ionizing radiation by detecting ions produced within a gas-filled tube

Examples

Accurate readings from an ionization tube are crucial for nuclear facility monitoring.
