fool's cap

noun
cone-shaped punitive paper hat
Pronunciation: / ˈfulz kæp / (AmE), / ˈfuːlz kap / (BrE)
a cone-shaped paper hat formerly placed on the head of slow or lazy pupils as a form of punishment or ridicule

Examples

wear a fool's cap
A fool's cap was placed on his head to ridicule his laziness in front of the class.
He remembered reading about the days when wearing a fool's cap was a common punishment.
In old classrooms, a fool's cap was sometimes used to shame underperforming students.
