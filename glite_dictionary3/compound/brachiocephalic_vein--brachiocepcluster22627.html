brachiocephalic vein

noun
major vein joining jugular and subclavian veins
Pronunciation: / ˌbreɪki.oʊsəˈfælɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌbræki.əʊsɪˈfælɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein formed by the joining of the internal jugular and subclavian veins on each side of the neck, which merges to form the superior vena cava

Examples

brachiocephalic vein blockage
A blood clot in the brachiocephalic vein can lead to serious complications.
During surgery, the brachiocephalic vein was carefully exposed to prevent accidental injury.
The right and left brachiocephalic veins join to form the superior vena cava.

Related Terms

  • synonym – internal jugular vein: major vein in the neck draining blood from the brain and neck
  • synonym – subclavian vein: vein that drains blood from upper limb into the brachiocephalic vein
