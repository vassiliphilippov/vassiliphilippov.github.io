facile argument

noun
superficial or simplistic argument
Pronunciation: / ˈfæsl ˈɑːrgjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈfæsaɪl ˈɑːgjʊmənt / (BrE)
an argument that appears neat and simple but is superficial, lacking depth or careful thought

Examples

a facile argument sounds convincing
His facile argument might sound convincing at first, but it quickly falls apart under scrutiny.
Rather than relying on a facile argument, she thoroughly examined the data to support her claims.
The author criticized the pundit's facile argument as failing to address the complex realities of the issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rigorous argument: an argument developed with thoroughness and depth
  • synonym – superficial argument: argument lacking depth or thoroughness
