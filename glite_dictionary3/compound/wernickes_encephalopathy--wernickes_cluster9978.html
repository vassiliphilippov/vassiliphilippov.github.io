Wernicke's encephalopathy

noun
brain disease from thiamine deficiency, often with alcoholism
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrnɪkəz ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪkəz ɪnˌkefəˈlɒpəθi / (BrE)
An inflammatory degenerative disease of the brain caused by thiamine deficiency, most commonly associated with chronic alcoholism.

Examples

Wernicke's encephalopathy from alcohol abuse
Chronic alcohol abuse can lead to Wernicke's encephalopathy if not treated with thiamine supplements.
Early recognition of Wernicke's encephalopathy is critical for preventing irreversible brain damage.
The patient exhibited confusion and ataxia, classic symptoms of Wernicke's encephalopathy.
