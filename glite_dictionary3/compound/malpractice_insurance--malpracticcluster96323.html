malpractice insurance

noun
insurance covering professional negligence lawsuits
Pronunciation: / mælˈpræktɪs ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / mælˈpræktɪs ɪnˈʃɔːrəns / (BrE)
insurance purchased by professionals, especially physicians and hospitals, to cover the costs of lawsuits arising from claims of professional negligence or malpractice

Examples

malpractice insurance for doctors
Before starting her practice, she ensured that she had adequate malpractice insurance.
Hospitals require all their staff to carry malpractice insurance as a safeguard against legal claims.
Many doctors find the cost of malpractice insurance to be a significant burden.

Related Terms

  • synonym – liability insurance: insurance protecting against claims resulting from injuries and damage to people or property
