normal fault

noun
a type of geological fault with downward movement of the hanging wall
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrməl fɔlt / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl fɔːlt / (BrE)
an inclined fault in the earth's crust where the upper side (hanging wall) has moved downward in relation to the lower side (footwall)

Examples

a normal fault in rock
A normal fault forms when the crust is extended by tectonic forces.
Geologists identified a normal fault running through the valley.
The earthquake was caused by movement along a normal fault.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reverse fault: an inclined fault where the hanging wall moves upward relative to the footwall
