minstrel show

noun
historical comedic stage show with blackface performers
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnstrəl ˌʃoʊ /; / ˈmɪnstrəl ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəl ˌʃəʊ /; / ˈmɪnstrəl ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a type of entertainment popular in the 19th and early 20th centuries, where performers (often white) in blackface presented comic sketches, songs, and dances that caricatured African Americans
