varnish tree

noun
large Asian tree; source of varnish oil
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑrnɪʃ tri / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ triː / (BrE)
a large tree native to southeastern Asia, whose nuts yield an oil used in varnishes and whose kernels are sometimes used as candles

Examples

varnish tree oil nuts
In some villages, the kernels of the varnish tree are strung together and used as candles.
Local artisans extract oil from the varnish tree to make traditional paints and lacquers.
The varnish tree produces nuts that are highly valued for their oil.
