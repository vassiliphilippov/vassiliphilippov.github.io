linguistic geography

noun
study of language distribution by region
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk dʒiˌɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk dʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study of how language features are distributed geographically and the mapping of language differences across regions

Examples

linguistic geography maps dialects
Linguistic geography reveals how certain words and pronunciations vary from one region to another.
Courses on linguistic geography often include the creation of dialect maps.
Her research in linguistic geography focused on the spread of dialects across northern England.
