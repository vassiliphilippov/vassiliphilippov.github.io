mail-order buying

noun
buying goods to have them shipped by mail
Pronunciation: / ˌmeɪl ˈɔrdɚ ˈbaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmeɪl ˈɔːdə ˈbaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of purchasing goods that are delivered by mail, typically using a catalog or online service rather than buying in person

Examples

mail-order buying from a catalog
Before online shopping, many people relied on mail-order buying for items not available locally.
The company specializes in mail-order buying and delivers products to customers nationwide.
