immune carrier

noun
person or animal immune but able to transmit disease
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmjun ˈkæriər / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːn ˈkæriə / (BrE)
(medicine) a person or animal who harbors a pathogen without showing symptoms because of immunity, but can transmit it to others.

Examples

immune carrier of disease
After the outbreak, scientists discovered that one dog was an immune carrier of the virus.
An immune carrier may feel healthy but can still infect others with the disease.
Typhoid Mary became famous as an immune carrier who spread typhoid without being sick herself.
