smart money

noun
money invested by experts or insiders
Pronunciation: / smɑrt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / smɑːt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money that is invested or bet by people with expert knowledge, experience, or insider information, and is therefore expected to make profitable choices

Examples

smart money bets
If you want to know which horse will win, watch where the smart money goes.
Most analysts agree that the smart money has already moved into the technology sector.
The smart money is betting on the company’s stock to rise after the merger.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dumb money: money invested by inexperienced or uninformed people
