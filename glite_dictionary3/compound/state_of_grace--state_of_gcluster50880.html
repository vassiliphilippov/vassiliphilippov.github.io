state of grace

noun
condition of being free from sin by God's favor
Pronunciation: / steɪt əv ɡreɪs / (AmE), / steɪt əv ɡreɪs / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) a condition in which a person is free from sin and is sanctified by God's favor.

Examples

live in a state of grace
According to church teachings, those who confess sincerely may return to a state of grace.
Many paintings depict saints in a state of grace, surrounded by radiant light.
She prayed daily, striving to remain in a state of grace throughout her life.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grace: unmerited favor or mercy from God
  • antonym – original sin: the innate sinful state according to Christian doctrine
