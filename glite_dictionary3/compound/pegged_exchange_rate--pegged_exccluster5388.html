pegged exchange rate

noun
currency value fixed to another or a basket of currencies
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛɡd ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ reɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛɡd ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ reɪt / (BrE)
A system in which a country's currency value is fixed or linked to the value of another currency or a basket of currencies, often within a specified range, and adjusted as needed by the government.

Examples

pegged exchange rate system
During the crisis, the government abandoned its pegged exchange rate in favor of a floating system.
Some developing countries prefer a pegged exchange rate to minimize currency fluctuations.
The central bank maintained a pegged exchange rate to ensure stability in international trade.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fixed exchange rate: An exchange rate system where a currency's value is fixed with respect to another currency or a commodity such as gold.
  • antonym – floating exchange rate: An exchange rate system in which the currency's value is allowed to fluctuate according to the foreign exchange market.
