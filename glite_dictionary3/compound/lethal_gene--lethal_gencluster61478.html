lethal gene

noun
gene causing death of organism
Pronunciation: / ˈliːθəl dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈliːθəl dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that causes the death of the organism carrying it at any stage of its life

Examples

carry a lethal gene
A single copy of the lethal gene can be harmless, but two copies can cause early death.
Researchers screened the population for carriers of the lethal gene responsible for the disorder.
The discovery of a lethal gene in fruit flies advanced our understanding of genetics.
