Bronze Star

noun
U.S. military honor for combat service
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑnz stɑr / (AmE), / ˈbrɒnz stɑː / (BrE)
A United States military decoration awarded for heroic or meritorious service or achievement in a combat zone, not involving aerial flight.

Examples

receive the Bronze Star medal
He was awarded the Bronze Star for his bravery during the mission.
Her father received the Bronze Star after serving in the Korean War.
The Bronze Star is one of the highest honors in the U.S. Army.
