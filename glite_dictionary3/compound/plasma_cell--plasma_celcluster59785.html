plasma cell

noun
antibody-producing immune cell
Pronunciation: / ˈplæz.mə sɛl / (AmE), / ˈplaz.mə sɛl / (BrE)
A type of white blood cell that develops from a B lymphocyte and produces antibodies in response to specific antigens; found in bone marrow and sometimes in the blood.

Examples

plasma cell produces antibodies
A plasma cell produces large quantities of antibodies to help fight infections.
Doctors observed an abnormal increase in plasma cells in the patient’s bone marrow.
When stimulated by an antigen, a B lymphocyte develops into a plasma cell.

Related Terms

  • synonym – B cell: A type of lymphocyte that can differentiate into a plasma cell
