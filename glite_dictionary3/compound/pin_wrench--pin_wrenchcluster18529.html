pin wrench

noun
a wrench with a pin for specific sockets
Pronunciation: / pɪn rɛntʃ / (AmE), / pɪn rɛntʃ / (BrE)
A wrench with a projecting pin designed to fit into a corresponding socket or hole on the object to be turned.

Examples

pin wrench for round nuts
The mechanic used a pin wrench to unscrew the unusual fastener on the machine casing.
This tool kit comes with a pin wrench for handling specialized bicycle components.
You may need a pin wrench to open the grinder's disc lock.
