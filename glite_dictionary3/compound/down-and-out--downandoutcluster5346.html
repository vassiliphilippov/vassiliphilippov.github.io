down-and-out

noun
person who is destitute or homeless
Pronunciation: / ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (BrE)
a person who has no money, no job, and no home; someone who is destitute

Examples

help a down-and-out
After losing his job, he ended up living on the streets as a down-and-out.
Despite being a down-and-out, he remained hopeful for a better future.
The shelter offers warm meals and beds for every down-and-out who walks through its doors.
