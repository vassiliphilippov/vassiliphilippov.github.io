subliminal perception

noun
perception without conscious awareness
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈlɪmɪnəl pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɪmɪnəl pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process of perceiving or reacting to stimuli without conscious awareness, typically below the threshold of conscious perception

Examples

subliminal perception in advertising
Advertising often tries to use subliminal perception to influence consumers without their direct awareness.
Research has shown that subliminal perception can cause people to react to images they do not consciously see.
Some psychologists debate whether subliminal perception can truly affect a person's decisions.
