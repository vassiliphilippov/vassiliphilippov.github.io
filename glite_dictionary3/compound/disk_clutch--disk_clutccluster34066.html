disk clutch

noun
friction clutch with disk surfaces
Pronunciation: / dɪsk klʌtʃ / (AmE), / dɪsk klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a type of friction clutch in which the frictional surfaces are flat disks pressed together to transmit power

Examples

disk clutch in a car
A disk clutch is commonly used in automotive manual transmissions.
Technicians replaced the old disk clutch with a new, more durable model.
When the disk clutch wears out, it can slip and fail to transfer power efficiently.
