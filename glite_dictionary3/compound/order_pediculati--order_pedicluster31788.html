order Pediculati

noun
former order of spiny-finned marine fishes
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ˌpɛdɪˈkjuːlətaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpɛdɪˈkjuːlətaɪ / (BrE)
a former scientific order of spiny-finned marine fishes, including anglers and batfishes, characterized by pectoral fins at the ends of armlike processes and a long movable spine on the dorsal fin used to lure prey.

Examples

order Pediculati anglerfish group
Anglerfish, which belong to the order Pediculati, use their dorsal spine as a lure to attract prey.
Batfishes, another member of the order Pediculati, are noted for their unusual pectoral fins.
Marine biologists have long studied the unique adaptations of the order Pediculati.
