molar concentration

noun
concentration in moles per liter of solution
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊlər ˌkɑnsənˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈməʊlə ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
The amount of a substance (solute) present in one liter of solution, measured in moles per liter.

Examples

molar concentration of salt
As the reaction proceeded, the molar concentration of the reactants decreased significantly.
The molar concentration of the salt solution was determined to be 0.5 M.
To ensure accuracy, the chemist carefully measured the molar concentration of each sample.
