inductive reasoning

noun
reasoning from facts to principles
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌktɪv ˈrizənɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌktɪv ˈriːzənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of deriving general principles from specific observations or facts

Examples

inductive reasoning from facts
Inductive reasoning allows scientists to form hypotheses based on patterns they observe in their data.
