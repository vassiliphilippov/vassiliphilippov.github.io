screw pine

noun
tropical tree of the genus Pandanus
Pronunciation: / skruː paɪn / (AmE), / skruː paɪn / (BrE)
Any of various tropical Old World trees of the genus Pandanus, having large prop roots, straplike leaves, and conelike edible fruits; not a true pine.

Examples

screw pine tree leaves
Her recipe included slices of the ripe fruit from a screw pine gathered in the tropics.
In coastal gardens, the screw pine is valued for its drought tolerance and striking prop roots.
The screw pine produces long, narrow leaves that are often woven into mats and baskets.
