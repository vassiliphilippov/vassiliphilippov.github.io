senior pilot

noun
pilot in command, especially of an airship
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːniɚ ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈsiːnɪə ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
the pilot who has command responsibility for an airship or aircraft, usually the most experienced or highest-ranking among the pilots on board

Examples

senior pilot in command
During emergencies, the crew always defers to the senior pilot's instructions.
Only the senior pilot is authorized to land the airship in difficult weather conditions.
The senior pilot made the final decision to change the airship's course.
