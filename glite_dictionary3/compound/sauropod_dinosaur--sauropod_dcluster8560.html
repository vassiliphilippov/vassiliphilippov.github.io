sauropod dinosaur

noun
large herbivorous dinosaur with long neck and tail
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrəˌpɑd ˈdaɪnəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrəpɒd ˈdaɪnəsɔː / (BrE)
a very large herbivorous dinosaur of the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, with a small head, long neck and tail, and five-toed limbs; among the largest known land animals

Examples

giant sauropod dinosaur fossil
Fossils of the sauropod dinosaur have been discovered on every continent.
Scientists believe that the sauropod dinosaur used its long neck to reach high vegetation.
The museum's new exhibit features a life-size sauropod dinosaur skeleton towering over visitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sauropod: a member of the group of large, long-necked dinosaurs
  • antonym – theropod: a group of bipedal dinosaurs, mostly carnivorous
