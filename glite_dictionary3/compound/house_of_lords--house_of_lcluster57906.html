House of Lords

proper noun
upper chamber of UK Parliament
Pronunciation: / haʊs əv lɔrdz / (AmE), / haʊs əv lɔːdz / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the Parliament of the United Kingdom, consisting of appointed and hereditary members

Examples

the House of Lords appointment
Appointment to the House of Lords is often considered a great honor in British society.
The House of Lords debated the new legislation for several hours before reaching a decision.
Unlike the elected House of Commons, the House of Lords is composed mainly of appointed members.

Related Terms

  • antonym – House of Commons: the lower chamber of the UK Parliament, composed of elected representatives
