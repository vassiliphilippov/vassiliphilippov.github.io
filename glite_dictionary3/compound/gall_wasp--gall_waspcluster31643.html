gall wasp

noun
small wasp that produces plant galls
Pronunciation: / ɡɔl wɑsp / (AmE), / ɡɔːl wɒsp / (BrE)
a small wasp that induces the formation of galls on oaks and other plants, in which its larvae develop

Examples

gall wasp on oak tree
Many gardeners have learned to recognize the distinctive galls created by the gall wasp.
Scientists study how the gall wasp manipulates plant tissues to form protective habitats for its larvae.
The gall wasp lays its eggs on oak leaves, causing the tree to develop abnormal growths.
