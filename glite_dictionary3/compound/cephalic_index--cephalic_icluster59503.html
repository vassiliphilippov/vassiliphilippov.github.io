cephalic index

noun
ratio of skull width to length
Pronunciation: / səˈfælɪk ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / sɪˈfælɪk ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
the ratio, expressed as a percentage, of the maximum breadth to the maximum length of a skull, used in anthropology and medicine to classify cranial shapes

Examples

cephalic index measurement
A cephalic index above 80 is classified as brachycephalic.
Researchers calculated the cephalic index to compare skull shapes among ancient populations.
The measurement of the cephalic index can provide insight into evolutionary adaptations.
