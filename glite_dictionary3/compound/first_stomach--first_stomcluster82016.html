first stomach

noun
first compartment of a ruminant's stomach
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
the first compartment of the stomach in a ruminant animal, where food is initially collected and later returned to the mouth for chewing as cud

Examples

cow's first stomach
During digestion, the first stomach acts as a large fermentation chamber.
Food is initially stored in the first stomach before being regurgitated as cud.
The cow's first stomach plays a vital role in breaking down tough plant materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rumen: the first stomach of a ruminant where fermentation of plant material occurs
