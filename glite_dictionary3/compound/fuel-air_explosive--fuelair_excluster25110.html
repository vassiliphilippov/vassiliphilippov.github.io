fuel-air explosive

noun
powerful weapon using dispersed fuel detonated in air
Pronunciation: / ˌfjuːəl ˈɛr ɪkˌsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ˌfjʊəl ˈeə ɪkˌspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
A weapon consisting of a device that disperses fuel into the air and then detonates it, producing a powerful blast wave.

Examples

fuel-air explosive bomb detonation
A fuel-air explosive creates a devastating shock wave when the fuel cloud ignites.
Concerns over civilian casualties have limited the use of fuel-air explosives in urban areas.
The military deployed a fuel-air explosive to destroy the enemy bunker.
