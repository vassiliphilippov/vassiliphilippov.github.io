popliteal vein

noun
vein behind the knee
Pronunciation: / pɑːpˈlɪtiəl veɪn / (AmE), / pɒpˈlɪtɪəl veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein located behind the knee that carries blood from the lower leg to the femoral vein

Examples

the popliteal vein behind knee
A blood clot in the popliteal vein can lead to deep vein thrombosis.
The surgeon carefully clamped the popliteal vein during the procedure to prevent excessive bleeding.
Ultrasound imaging revealed a blockage in the patient's popliteal vein.
