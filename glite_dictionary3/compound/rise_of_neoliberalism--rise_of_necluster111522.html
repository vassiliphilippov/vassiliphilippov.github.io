rise of neoliberalism

noun
increase in neoliberal policies and ideas
Pronunciation: / raɪz əv ˌnioʊˈlɪbərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / raɪz ɒv ˌniːəʊˈlɪbərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the period or process in which neoliberal policies and ideologies became increasingly prevalent, especially from the late 20th century onward

Examples

the rise of neoliberalism in politics
Debates about the rise of neoliberalism are common in political science and economics.
Many scholars attribute growing economic inequality to the rise of neoliberalism in the 1980s.
The rise of neoliberalism influenced government policy across much of the Western world.
