broom palm

noun
a small palm tree of Florida and the West Indies
Pronunciation: / bruːm pɑːm / (AmE), / bruːm pɑːm / (BrE)
a small palm tree native to southern Florida and the West Indies, closely resembling the silvertop palmetto

Examples

broom palm tree
Botanists often compare the broom palm to the silvertop palmetto because of their similar appearance.
Local communities use leaves from the broom palm to make traditional brooms and other crafts.
The broom palm grows abundantly in the wetlands of southern Florida.
