empirical formula

noun
simplest ratio of elements in a compound
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈpɪrɪkəl ˈfɔrmjələ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪərɪkəl ˈfɔːmjʊlə / (BrE)
a chemical formula that shows the simplest whole-number ratio of the elements in a compound, rather than the actual number of atoms

Examples

empirical formula of glucose
By analyzing the compound, scientists determined its empirical formula as CH2O.
The empirical formula for hydrogen peroxide is HO, showing the simplest ratio of elements.
Unlike the molecular formula, the empirical formula does not indicate the exact number of atoms in the molecule.
