free trader

noun
advocate of unrestricted international trade
Pronunciation: / ˌfriˈtreɪdər / (AmE), / ˌfriːˈtreɪdə / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates for unrestricted trade between nations, without tariffs or other barriers

Examples

committed free trader
As a committed free trader, she opposed tariffs on imported goods.
Many economists argue from a free trader perspective when discussing global markets.
The senator has long been known as a free trader in economic debates.
