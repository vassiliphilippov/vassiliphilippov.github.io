order Gruiformes

noun
a bird order including cranes, rails, and bustards
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ ɡruːɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ɡruːɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A group of inland marsh-dwelling birds, such as cranes, rails, and bustards, characterized by long legs, necks, and bills for wading in water to search for food.
