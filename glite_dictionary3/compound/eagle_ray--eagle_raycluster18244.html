eagle ray

noun
tropical marine ray with winglike fins
Pronunciation: / ˈiɡəl ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl ˌreɪ / (BrE)
A large, tropical marine ray with broad, winglike fins, known for its graceful swimming and usually harmless nature though it may have venomous tissue at the base of the tail.

Examples

spotted eagle ray
A group of eagle rays was swimming together, their spotted bodies easy to recognize.
Divers often spot eagle rays gliding effortlessly through warm coastal waters.
The eagle ray flapped its wide fins as it soared gracefully above the coral reef.
