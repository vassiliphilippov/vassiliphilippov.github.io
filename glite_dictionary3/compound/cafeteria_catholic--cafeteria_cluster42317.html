cafeteria Catholic

noun
a Catholic who selectively follows Church teachings
Pronunciation: / ˌkæfəˈtɪriə ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / ˌkæfɪˈtɪəriə ˈkæθlɪk / (BrE)
a Catholic who selects which Church doctrines to accept and which to disregard, rather than adhering to all official teachings

Examples

a cafeteria Catholic ignores rules
His views on contraception led some parishioners to label him a cafeteria Catholic.
She considers herself a cafeteria Catholic, supporting some Church positions while rejecting others.
Some critics accuse politicians of being cafeteria Catholics when their policies contradict Church doctrine.

Related Terms

  • antonym – orthodox Catholic: a Catholic who strictly adheres to all official doctrines
