drift ice

noun
floating sea ice masses
Pronunciation: / drɪft aɪs / (AmE), / drɪft aɪs / (BrE)
Large fragments or masses of ice floating freely on the open sea, not attached to the shoreline or other fixed features.

Examples

drift ice blocks ships
During summer, much of the drift ice melts, opening sea routes for navigation.
Polar bears are often seen traveling across drift ice in search of food.
The ship had to slow down as it encountered thick drift ice in the Arctic waters.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pack ice: a mass of closely packed pieces of floating ice
