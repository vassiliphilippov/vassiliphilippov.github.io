wrought iron

noun
tough, malleable form of iron used in metalwork
Pronunciation: / rɑt ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈrɔːt ˌaɪən / (BrE)
A tough, malleable form of iron with a very low carbon content, suitable for forging and welding and commonly used for decorative ironwork and construction.

Examples

wrought iron gate
Artisans used wrought iron to create durable railings for the historic building.
She admired the antique wrought iron bed frame in the guest room.
The ornate gates were made of wrought iron and featured intricate floral designs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cast iron: iron with a relatively high carbon content that is hard but brittle
