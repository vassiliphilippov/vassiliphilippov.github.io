judgment by default

noun
legal decision for nonappearance in court
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt baɪ dɪˈfɔlt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt baɪ dɪˈfɒlt / (BrE)
A legal decision made in favor of one party when the other party fails to appear in court or respond to a legal claim.

Examples

judgment by default against a defendant
He lost his case by not showing up, resulting in a judgment by default.
If you do not file an answer, the plaintiff may obtain a judgment by default against you.
The court awarded a judgment by default after the defendant failed to respond to the lawsuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – default judgment: a judgment rendered because of the defendant's failure to answer or appear
