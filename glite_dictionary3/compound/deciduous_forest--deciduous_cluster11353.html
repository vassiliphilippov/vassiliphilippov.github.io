deciduous forest

noun
forest of trees that shed leaves annually
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪdʒuəs ˈfɔrɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjʊəs ˈfɒrɪst / (BrE)
A forest consisting predominantly of broad-leaved trees that lose their leaves annually, typically in response to seasonal changes.

Examples

colorful deciduous forest
In autumn, the deciduous forest is ablaze with vibrant reds and yellows as the trees prepare for winter.
Many animals in the deciduous forest adapt their behavior to the changing seasons.
The explorers camped on the edge of a dense deciduous forest teeming with wildlife.

Related Terms
