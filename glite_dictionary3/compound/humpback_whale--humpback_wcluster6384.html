humpback whale

noun
large whale with long flippers
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmpˌbæk weɪl / (AmE), / ˈhʌmpbæk weɪl / (BrE)
A large baleen whale (Megaptera novaeangliae) noted for its long flippers and for arching its back when diving.

Examples

humpback whale migration
Each year, humpback whales migrate thousands of miles from their feeding to breeding grounds.
The humpback whale is known for its distinctive songs.
We spotted a humpback whale breaching near the coast during our boat tour.

  • synonym – orca: a type of toothed whale, also known as killer whale
