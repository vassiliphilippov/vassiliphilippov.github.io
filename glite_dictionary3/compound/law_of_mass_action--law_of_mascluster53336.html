law of mass action

noun
principle relating reaction rate to reactant concentrations
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv mæs ˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / lɔː əv mæs ˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
In chemistry, the principle that the rate of a chemical reaction is directly proportional to the concentrations of the reactants.

Examples

law of mass action in chemistry
According to the law of mass action, increasing the concentration of a reactant speeds up the reaction.
In high school chemistry, students learn how the law of mass action explains reaction rates.
The law of mass action underlies many calculations in chemical equilibrium.
