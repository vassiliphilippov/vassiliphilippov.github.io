salt lick

noun
place where animals lick salt deposits
Pronunciation: / sɔlt lɪk / (AmE), / sɒlt lɪk / (BrE)
a place where natural or artificial salt deposits are found and animals regularly lick them to obtain minerals

Examples

cows at the salt lick
Cattle gathered around the salt lick to satisfy their need for minerals.
Deer often visit the salt lick in the evenings, especially during the summer months.
The farmer placed a new salt lick near the pond to attract his livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mineral lick: a place where animals go to lick essential mineral nutrients
