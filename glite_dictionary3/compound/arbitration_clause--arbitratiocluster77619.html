arbitration clause

noun
contract term requiring arbitration of disputes
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrbəˈtreɪʃn̩ klɔːz / (AmE), / ˌɑːbɪˈtreɪʃn̩ klɔːz / (BrE)
a section in a contract stating that disputes will be resolved by arbitration rather than by court litigation

Examples

standard arbitration clause
An arbitration clause may stipulate how arbitrators are chosen in the event of a disagreement.
Many businesses use an arbitration clause to avoid lengthy litigation.
The contract contains an arbitration clause requiring both parties to resolve disputes out of court.

Related Terms

  • synonym – arbitration: the process of settling a dispute by an impartial third party
