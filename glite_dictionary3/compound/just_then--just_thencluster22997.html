just then

adverb
at that exact past moment
Pronunciation: / dʒəst ðɛn / (AmE), / dʒʌst ðɛn / (BrE)
at that precise moment in the past; used to refer to an event that happened exactly at a particular time

Examples

the bell rang just then
I was about to leave when the phone rang just then.
She finished her story just then, as the bell rang.
The door opened just then, and everyone turned to look.
