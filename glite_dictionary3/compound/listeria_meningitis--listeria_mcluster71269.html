listeria meningitis

noun
meningitis caused by Listeria monocytogenes
Pronunciation: / lɪˈstɪriə ˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈstɪəriə ˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of meningitis in humans and animals caused by infection with the bacterium Listeria monocytogenes, often affecting newborns or immunosuppressed individuals and sometimes livestock.

Examples

listeria meningitis in newborns
Newborns are particularly vulnerable to listeria meningitis due to their developing immune systems.
Prompt treatment is crucial for patients with listeria meningitis to minimize complications.
The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with listeria meningitis after observing neurological symptoms.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – encephalitis: inflammation of the brain, often due to infection
  • synonym – meningitis: inflammation of the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord
