secret police

noun
covert police force, often oppressive
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːkrət pəˈliːs / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrɪt pəˈliːs / (BrE)
a police organization that works covertly, especially to suppress dissent and monitor or punish political opponents, often using unlawful or oppressive methods

Examples

secret police arrest dissidents
Many oppressive regimes have relied on the secret police to maintain control.
People lived in constant fear of being watched or reported by the secret police.
The new government disbanded the notorious secret police after coming to power.
