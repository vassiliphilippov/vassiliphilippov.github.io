tetrabasic acid

noun
acid with four replaceable hydrogens
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈbeɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈbeɪsɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an acid containing four replaceable hydrogen atoms in each molecule, often able to form four series of salts
