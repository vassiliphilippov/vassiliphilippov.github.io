communicable disease

noun
disease spread between individuals
Pronunciation: / kəˌmjunɪkəbəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪkəbl̩ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a disease that can be passed from one person or animal to another, typically through direct or indirect contact.

Examples

catch a communicable disease
Infectious agents such as bacteria and viruses are responsible for most communicable diseases.
Proper handwashing can reduce the risk of many communicable diseases in schools and workplaces.
Public health officials work to prevent the spread of communicable diseases like measles and tuberculosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym – infectious disease: disease caused by pathogenic microorganisms that can be spread
  • antonym – noncommunicable disease: disease not transmissible from person to person
