Old Style calendar

noun
Julian calendar (pre-Gregorian system)
Pronunciation: / oʊld staɪl ˈkæləndɚ / (AmE), / əʊld staɪl ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
the calendar system introduced by Julius Caesar in 46 b.c., establishing a 12-month year of 365 days with a leap year of 366 days every four years; also known as the Julian calendar

Examples

Old Style calendar date
Many historical documents use dates from the Old Style calendar, which can differ from modern dates.
Russia officially switched from the Old Style calendar to the Gregorian calendar in 1918.
When comparing events across countries, scholars often clarify whether they are referring to the Old Style calendar.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Gregorian calendar: calendar system introduced in 1582, now internationally used; replaced Julian calendar
  • synonym – Julian calendar: calendar introduced by Julius Caesar, predecessor to the Gregorian calendar
  • antonym – New Style calendar: another term for the Gregorian calendar, which replaced the Old Style (Julian) calendar
