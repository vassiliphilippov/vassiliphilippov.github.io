speed of light

noun
the speed at which light travels in a vacuum
Pronunciation: / spid əv laɪt / (AmE), / spiːd əv laɪt / (BrE)
the constant speed at which light travels in a vacuum, exactly 299,792,458 meters per second

Examples

the speed of light in a vacuum
Nothing in the universe can travel faster than the speed of light.
Scientists have measured the speed of light with incredible precision.
The theory of relativity is based on the constancy of the speed of light.
