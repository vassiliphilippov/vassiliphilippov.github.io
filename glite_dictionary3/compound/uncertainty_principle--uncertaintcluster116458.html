uncertainty principle

noun
quantum mechanics: simultaneous measurement limit
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɝː.tən.ti ˈprɪn.sə.pəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜː.tənti ˈprɪn.sɪ.pl̩ / (BrE)
the theory in quantum mechanics stating that certain pairs of physical properties, such as position and momentum, cannot both be known to arbitrary precision at the same time

Examples

the uncertainty principle in physics
Because of the uncertainty principle, complete accuracy in quantum measurement is impossible.
Many physicists discuss the uncertainty principle when explaining the strange behavior of subatomic particles.
The uncertainty principle fundamentally limits how precisely we can measure a particle's position and momentum.
