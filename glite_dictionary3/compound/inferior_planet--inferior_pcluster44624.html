inferior planet

noun
planet inside Earth's orbit (Mercury or Venus)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪriər ˈplænɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
a planet whose orbit is inside the Earth's orbit, specifically Mercury or Venus

Examples

inferior planet like Mercury
Mercury is an inferior planet, as its orbit is closer to the Sun than Earth's.
Observations of an inferior planet can only be made during certain times, such as at dawn or dusk.
Venus, being an inferior planet, exhibits phases similar to the Moon when viewed from Earth.
