informer’s privilege

noun
government right to keep informer’s identity secret
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔrmɚz ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːməz ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the right of the government to refuse to disclose the identity of a confidential informant in legal proceedings
