anti-G suit

noun
suit used to resist high gravity forces
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti ˈdʒi suːt / (AmE), / ˌænti ˈdʒiː suːt / (BrE)
a special suit worn by pilots and astronauts to counteract the effects of increased gravity and acceleration during flight

Examples

wear an anti-G suit in flight
Astronauts often rely on an anti-G suit to help withstand the intense forces experienced at launch.
Modern anti-G suits are tailored to fit comfortably while maintaining maximum effectiveness.
The pilot wore an anti-G suit to prevent blackouts during high-speed maneuvers.
