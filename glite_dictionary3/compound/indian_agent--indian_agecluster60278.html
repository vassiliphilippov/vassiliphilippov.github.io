Indian agent

noun
government official managing American Indian affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A representative of the United States federal government assigned to liaise with and oversee the affairs of American Indian tribes, especially on reservations.
