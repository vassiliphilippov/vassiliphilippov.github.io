beef cattle

noun
cattle bred for meat
Pronunciation: / bif ˈkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / biːf ˈkætl̩ / (BrE)
Cattle that are raised primarily for meat production rather than for milk or labor.

Examples

beef cattle farm
After years of dairy farming, they switched to rearing beef cattle instead.
Many breeds of beef cattle are valued for their high-quality meat.
The farm specializes in raising beef cattle for local markets.
