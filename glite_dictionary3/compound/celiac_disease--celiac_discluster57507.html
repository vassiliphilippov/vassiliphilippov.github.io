celiac disease

noun
immune reaction to gluten
Pronunciation: / siˈliæk dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈsiːliæk dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the immune system reacts to gluten, leading to damage in the small intestine and difficulty absorbing nutrients.

Examples

celiac disease diagnosis
Her doctor diagnosed her with celiac disease after she experienced chronic digestive problems.
People with celiac disease must avoid foods containing wheat, barley, and rye.
Symptoms of celiac disease can improve significantly with a strict gluten-free diet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gluten intolerance: inability to digest or tolerate gluten
