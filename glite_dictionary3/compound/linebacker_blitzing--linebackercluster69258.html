linebacker blitzing

noun
linebacker attacking offensive play (football)
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪnˌbækər ˈblɪtsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnbækə ˈblɪtsɪŋ / (BrE)
(in American football) the action of a linebacker aggressively charging toward the offensive line to try to disrupt a play, often by pressuring the quarterback or tackling the ball carrier.

Examples

linebacker blitzing the quarterback
During the game, several successful linebacker blitzing plays resulted in tackles behind the line of scrimmage.
Effective linebacker blitzing can force turnovers and disrupt the timing of the offense.
The coach called for a linebacker blitzing strategy to put extra pressure on the opposing quarterback.
