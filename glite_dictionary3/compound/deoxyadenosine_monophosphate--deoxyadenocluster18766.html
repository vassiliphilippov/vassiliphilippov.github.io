deoxyadenosine monophosphate

noun
a DNA nucleotide containing adenine
Pronunciation: / diˌɑksiəˌdinˌoʊˌsiːn ˌmɑnəˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / diˌɒksiəˌdiːnˌəʊˌsiːn ˌmɒnəˈfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
A nucleotide that is one of the four building blocks of DNA, consisting of a phosphate group, the sugar deoxyribose, and the base adenine; also known as deoxyadenylate or dAMP.

Examples

deoxyadenosine monophosphate in DNA
During DNA replication, deoxyadenosine monophosphate pairs with thymine on the complementary strand.
Scientists often abbreviate deoxyadenosine monophosphate as dAMP in genetic studies.
The presence of deoxyadenosine monophosphate is essential for the formation of the DNA double helix.
