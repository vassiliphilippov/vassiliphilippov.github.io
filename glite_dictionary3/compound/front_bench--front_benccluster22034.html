front bench

noun
the seats for ministers or opposition leaders in parliament
Pronunciation: / frʌnt bɛntʃ / (AmE), / frʌnt bɛntʃ / (BrE)
(in the British House of Commons and some other legislatures) the seats reserved for government ministers or leading members of the opposition.

Examples

the front bench ministers
Debates on the front bench were lively during the parliamentary session.
He moved from the front bench to a less prominent role in the party.
She aspired to one day sit on the front bench as a cabinet minister.

Related Terms

  • antonym – back bench: the seats in parliament occupied by members who are not ministers or leading opposition members
