binary star

noun
pair of stars orbiting each other
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪnɛri stɑr / (AmE), / ˈbaɪnəri stɑː / (BrE)
A system of two stars that orbit around a common center of mass due to their mutual gravitational attraction.

Examples

binary star system
A binary star can sometimes be seen with a telescope as two distinct points of light.
Astronomers recently discovered a binary star system only 30 light-years away from Earth.
The movement of a binary star system helps scientists calculate the masses of the stars involved.
