liliopsid family

noun
family of monocotyledonous flowering plants
Pronunciation: / lɪliːˌɑpsɪd ˈfæmɪli / (AmE), / lɪlɪˌɒpsɪd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants distinguished by having a single embryonic leaf (cotyledon) in the seed, commonly known as monocots.

Examples

liliopsid family of orchids
Orchids are part of the liliopsid family, featuring just one seed leaf at germination.
Scientists use the term liliopsid family to classify plants like palms and irises.
The liliopsid family includes grasses and lilies among many other flowering plants.
