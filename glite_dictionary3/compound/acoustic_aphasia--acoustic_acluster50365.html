acoustic aphasia

noun
impaired comprehension of spoken language
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk əˈfeɪʒə / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person is unable to understand spoken language, despite not having any hearing loss.

Examples

acoustic aphasia diagnosis
Despite having normal hearing, he struggled with acoustic aphasia and needed support to communicate.
Research into acoustic aphasia has helped doctors better understand language processing in the brain.
The neurologist diagnosed the patient with acoustic aphasia after tests showed she could not understand speech.
