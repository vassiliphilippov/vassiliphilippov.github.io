wire-guided torpedo

noun
torpedo guided via wires from a vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪɚ ˌɡaɪdɪd tɔrˈpiːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə ˌɡaɪdɪd tɔːˈpiːdəʊ / (BrE)
a torpedo steered from its launching vessel by electrical signals sent through wires that remain connected during its approach to the target

Examples

wire-guided torpedo tracks target
A wire-guided torpedo can be accurately steered toward its target even if the enemy tries to evade.
The new submarine is equipped with the latest wire-guided torpedoes for underwater defense.
Training exercises demonstrated the effectiveness of the wire-guided torpedo when attacking moving ships.
