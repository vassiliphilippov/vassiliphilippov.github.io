drepanocytic anaemia

noun
anemia with crescent-shaped red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌdrɛpəˈnɑsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌdrɛpənəˈsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A congenital form of anemia, mainly affecting people of African descent, characterized by red blood cells that have a crescent or sickle shape instead of the normal disc shape.

Drepanocytic anaemia is particularly prevalent in regions where malaria is or was common.
