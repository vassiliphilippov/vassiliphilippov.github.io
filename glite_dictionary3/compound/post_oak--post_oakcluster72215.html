post oak

noun
a North American oak tree with durable wood
Pronunciation: / poʊst oʊk / (AmE), / pəʊst əʊk / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree (Quercus stellata) native to eastern and central United States, with tough, moisture-resistant wood commonly used for fence posts

Examples

post oak tree wood
Many old farm fences in Texas were built using post oak posts.
The wood of the post oak is valued for its strength and resistance to moisture.
We hiked through a forest filled with post oak and hickory trees.
