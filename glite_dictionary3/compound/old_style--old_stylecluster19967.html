old style

noun
a style of typeface with classic features
Pronunciation: / oʊld staɪl / (AmE), / əʊld staɪl / (BrE)
a style of typeface, originating in the 18th century, characterized by irregularity, slanted ascender serifs, and little contrast between thick and thin strokes.

Examples

old style font design
Designers often choose old style fonts when they want a traditional and readable look.
Many books from the 18th century were printed in old style typefaces for a classic appearance.
The text in this document uses an old style that is easy on the eyes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – modern: a later style of typeface distinguished by high contrast between thick and thin strokes
