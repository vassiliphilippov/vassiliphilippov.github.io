conservation of matter

noun
principle that matter is neither created nor destroyed
Pronunciation: / kənˌsɜːrveɪʃən əv ˈmætər / (AmE), / kənˌsɜːvˈeɪʃən əv ˈmætə / (BrE)
the scientific principle that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system; it can only change forms

Examples

conservation of matter in science
According to the conservation of matter, mass remains constant in a closed system.
Her science project demonstrated the conservation of matter by capturing all reactants and products during an experiment.
The principle of conservation of matter is fundamental to understanding chemical reactions.
