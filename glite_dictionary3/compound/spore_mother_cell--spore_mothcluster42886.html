spore mother cell

noun
cell giving rise to spores
Pronunciation: / spɔr ˈmʌðɚ sɛl / (AmE), / spɔː ˈmʌðə sɛl / (BrE)
a diploid cell in plants, fungi, or algae from which spores are produced through meiosis

Examples

spore mother cell produces spores
Each spore mother cell gives rise to a tetrad of spores in flowering plants.
In mosses, the spore mother cell is found within the capsule.
