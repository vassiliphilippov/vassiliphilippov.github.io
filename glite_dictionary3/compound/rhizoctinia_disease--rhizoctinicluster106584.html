rhizoctinia disease

noun
plant disease caused by specific fungi
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪzoʊkˈtɪniə dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˌraɪzɒkˈtɪniə dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A plant disease caused by fungi of the genera Rhizoctinia, Pellicularia, or Corticium, affecting the roots and sometimes other parts of the plant.

Examples

rhizoctinia disease damages roots
Farmers must monitor for rhizoctinia disease, especially in damp, poorly drained soils.
Many crops can be affected by rhizoctinia disease, leading to significant yield losses.
Symptoms of rhizoctinia disease usually appear as root rot and wilting in young plants.
