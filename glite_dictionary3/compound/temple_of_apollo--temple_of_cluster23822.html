Temple of Apollo

noun
the oracular temple at Delphi in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpəl əv əˈpɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl ɒv əˈpɒləʊ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the temple at Delphi where a priestess, known as the Pythia, delivered oracular messages from the god Apollo, often in obscure or ambiguous language.

Examples

the Temple of Apollo at Delphi
Legends claim that the Temple of Apollo was built on the site where Zeus's eagle landed.
Many ancient Greeks traveled to the Temple of Apollo at Delphi seeking guidance from the oracle.
The ruins of the Temple of Apollo are still a popular tourist destination today.
