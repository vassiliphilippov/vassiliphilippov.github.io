blight canker

noun
fire blight stage marked by cankers
Pronunciation: / blaɪt ˈkæŋkɚ / (AmE), / blaɪt ˈkæŋkə / (BrE)
a stage of fire blight disease in plants, marked by the appearance of lesions or cankers on branches or stems

Examples

blight canker on tree branch
Careful inspection revealed a blight canker near the base of the apple tree.
Orchard owners are advised to prune all visible blight cankers to help control the spread of fire blight.
The tree's branches showed signs of blight canker after a severe outbreak last spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym – canker: an open sore or lesion on plant tissue caused by disease
  • synonym – fire blight: a contagious disease attacking apples, pears, and other plants, caused by the bacterium Erwinia amylovora
