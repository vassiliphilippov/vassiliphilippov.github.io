transfer RNA

noun
RNA molecule that carries amino acids for protein synthesis
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænsfɚ ˌɑr ɛn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈtrɑːnsfə ˌɑːr ɛn ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A type of RNA molecule that helps decode a messenger RNA (mRNA) sequence into a protein by carrying specific amino acids to the ribosome during protein synthesis.

Examples

transfer RNA carries amino
transfer RNA plays a critical role in translating genetic information into functional proteins.
Scientists discovered that each type of transfer RNA is specific to one amino acid.
Without transfer RNA, cells would not be able to build proteins from genetic instructions.
