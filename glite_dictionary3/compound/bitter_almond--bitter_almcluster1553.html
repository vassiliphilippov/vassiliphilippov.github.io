bitter almond

noun
almond variety with poisonous nuts used for oil
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˈɑːmənd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈɑːmənd / (BrE)
a variety of almond that has white blossoms and produces poisonous nuts containing amygdalin, used for making oil for flavoring and medicinal purposes

Examples

bitter almond oil flavoring
Bitter almond oil is sometimes used as a flavoring in small quantities.
Extracts from the bitter almond are also used in some traditional medicines.
You should not eat raw bitter almonds because they contain toxic substances.
