transverse flute

noun
side-blown woodwind instrument (concert flute)
Pronunciation: / trænˈsvɜrs fluːt /; / trænzˌvɜrs ˈfluːt / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːs fluːt /; / trɑːnzˌvɜːs ˈfluːt / (BrE)
a side-blown woodwind instrument, usually held horizontally and played by blowing across a hole; commonly known as the Western concert flute
