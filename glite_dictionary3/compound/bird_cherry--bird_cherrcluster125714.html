bird cherry

noun
a wild cherry tree with small fruit eaten by birds
Pronunciation: / bɝd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / bɜːd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a small-fruited wild cherry tree with black or red fruit, often eaten by birds, especially Prunus padus or related species

Examples

bird cherry tree blossoms
Many gardens feature the bird cherry because of its ornamental flowers and wildlife appeal.
Ripe fruits from the bird cherry provide an important food source for migratory birds.
The bird cherry blooms in late spring, attracting many species of birds to the garden.
