liquidation process

noun
selling assets to pay debts
Pronunciation: / lɪkwɪˈdeɪʃən ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / lɪkwɪˈdeɪʃən ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
the process of selling a company's assets in order to pay off its debts, often before closing the business

Examples

company liquidation process
After the court ruling, the liquidation process began immediately to satisfy all outstanding creditors.
Shareholders received their payouts only after the liquidation process was fully completed.
The company's assets were sold as part of the liquidation process to cover its financial obligations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – liquidation: the act of converting assets into cash, especially to pay debts
