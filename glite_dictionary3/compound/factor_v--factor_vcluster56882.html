factor V

noun
blood clotting protein (coagulation factor)
Pronunciation: / ˈfæktɚ ˈfaɪv / (AmE), / ˈfæktə ˈfaɪv / (BrE)
a protein in blood plasma that is essential for blood clotting, also known as coagulation factor V

Examples

factor V deficiency
A deficiency of factor V can lead to excessive bleeding after injury.
Doctors measured her factor V levels to assess her risk of clotting disorders.
The discovery of factor V mutations has improved our understanding of thrombosis.
