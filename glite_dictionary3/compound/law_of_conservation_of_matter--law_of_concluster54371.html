law of conservation of matter

noun
principle that matter cannot be created or destroyed
Pronunciation: / lɔː əv ˌkɑːnsərˈveɪʃən əv ˈmætər / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən əv ˈmætə / (BrE)
A fundamental principle of classical physics stating that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system; the total amount of matter remains constant over time.

law of conservation of matter in science
In science class, we learned that the law of conservation of matter applies to all physical changes.
The law of conservation of matter explains why the total mass remains unchanged in a chemical reaction.
The law of conservation of matter is central to both chemistry and physics curricula.
