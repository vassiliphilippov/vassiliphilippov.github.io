cerebral aqueduct

noun
canal connecting third and fourth brain ventricles
Pronunciation: / səˈribɹəl ˈækwəˌdʌkt / (AmE), / sɪˈriːbrəl ˈakwɪdʌkt / (BrE)
a narrow canal in the brain that connects the third and fourth ventricles, allowing cerebrospinal fluid to flow between them

Examples

the cerebral aqueduct connects ventricles
MRI scans help doctors examine the cerebral aqueduct for any abnormalities.
Proper cerebrospinal fluid flow relies on an open and functioning cerebral aqueduct.
The cerebral aqueduct can sometimes become blocked, leading to hydrocephalus.
