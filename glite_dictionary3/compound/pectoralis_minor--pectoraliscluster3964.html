pectoralis minor

noun
a chest muscle beneath the pectoralis major
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛktəˈreɪlɪs ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˌpɛktəˈreɪlɪs ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a thin, flat skeletal muscle of the upper chest that draws down the scapula and helps raise the ribs during deep inhalation

Examples

pectoralis minor muscle pain
During deep breaths, the pectoralis minor helps lift the ribs to expand the chest cavity.
Injuries to the pectoralis minor can cause shoulder pain and limited mobility.
The pectoralis minor plays an essential role in stabilizing the scapula during movement.
