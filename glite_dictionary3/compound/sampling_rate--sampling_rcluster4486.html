sampling rate

noun
number of samples per second in a system
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmplɪŋ reɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplɪŋ reɪt / (BrE)
The number of samples taken per second in a system that converts a continuous signal, such as sound, into discrete digital data.

Examples

high sampling rate
A higher sampling rate allows for more accurate digital representations of analog sounds.
Engineers increased the sampling rate to capture more detail in the signal.
The sampling rate of the audio recording affects its quality and file size.
