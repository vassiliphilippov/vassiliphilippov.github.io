hexanoic acid

noun
a fatty acid with a six-carbon chain
Pronunciation: / hɛkˈsænoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / hɛkˈsænəʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid with six carbon atoms, found in animal oils and fats or produced synthetically, known for its pungent odor similar to that of goats

Examples

hexanoic acid goat smell
Hexanoic acid is commonly found in the fat of goat's milk, contributing to its distinctive smell.
Chemists can synthesize hexanoic acid in the laboratory for use in various industries.
The strong odor of hexanoic acid is often described as similar to that of goats.
