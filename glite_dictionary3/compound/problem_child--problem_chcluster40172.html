problem child

noun
troublesome child or difficult issue
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːbləm tʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈprɒbləm tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a child who causes trouble or difficulty; also, something (such as a project or product) that regularly causes problems or requires special attention

Examples

difficult problem child
After several incidents at school, James was seen as a problem child by his teachers.
She worried that her son would be labeled a problem child without understanding his needs.
The company’s overseas branch turned into a problem child due to ongoing losses.
