batter bread

noun
soft, spoonable cornmeal bread, Southern US
Pronunciation: / ˈbædɚ brɛd / (AmE), / ˈbætə brɛd / (BrE)
A soft type of bread made from cornmeal, and sometimes rice or hominy, with a batter-like consistency that must be served with a spoon; chiefly found in Southern United States cuisine.

Examples

spoonful of batter bread
At the family reunion, a large bowl of batter bread sat at the center of the table.

This recipe for batter bread is one my grandmother used to make every Sunday.
