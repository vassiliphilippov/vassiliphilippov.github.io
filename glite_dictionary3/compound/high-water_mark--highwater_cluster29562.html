high-water mark

noun
peak resource usage (computing)
Pronunciation: / ccha6a 8w54t5a m51rk / (AmE), / ccha6a 8w54d0t59 m51d0k / (BrE)
(in computing) the maximum level of a resource (for example, memory or disk) reached by a process or system, often recorded to trigger cleanup, growth, or scaling decisions.

Examples

system high-water mark
The allocator uses the high-water mark to decide when to allocate more memory.
During stress tests the server's high-water mark reached 95% of available disk capacity.
Developers monitor the application's high-water mark to schedule garbage collection and scaling events.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low-water mark: the lowest level reached (opposite of a peak or high-water mark)
  • synonym – maximum: the greatest amount, value, or degree observed
  • synonym – peak: the highest point or level, especially of a measurement or activity
