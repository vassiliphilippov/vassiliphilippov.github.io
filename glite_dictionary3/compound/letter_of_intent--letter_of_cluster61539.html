letter of intent

noun
formal letter stating intended action
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtər əv ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə əv ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
a formal letter expressing an intention to take or refrain from some action, often preceding a binding agreement

Examples

sign a letter of intent
A letter of intent can demonstrate a party's seriousness before a full agreement is signed.
Before starting negotiations, they required a letter of intent from the applicant.
The company submitted a letter of intent outlining its plans to acquire the startup.
