hard-cooked egg

noun
egg cooked until yolk and white are solid
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑrd ˈkʊkt ɛg / (AmE), / ˌhɑːd ˈkʊkt ɛg / (BrE)
An egg that has been boiled gently until both the white and the yolk are completely solidified.

Examples

peel a hard-cooked egg
She packed a hard-cooked egg in her lunch for extra protein.
Sliced hard-cooked egg is often added to salads for flavor and texture.
To make a hard-cooked egg, simmer it for about ten minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hard-boiled egg: an egg boiled until both the white and the yolk solidify
  • antonym – soft-cooked egg: an egg boiled so the white is set but the yolk remains runny
