hydrokinetic engine

noun
engine converting water energy to shaft power
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊkɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊkɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an engine that converts the energy of falling or moving water into mechanical shaft power

Examples

hydrokinetic engine generates power
A hydrokinetic engine harnesses river currents to generate electricity in remote areas.
Innovations in hydrokinetic engine technology have improved renewable energy production.
The ancient mill used a hydrokinetic engine to power its grinding stones.
