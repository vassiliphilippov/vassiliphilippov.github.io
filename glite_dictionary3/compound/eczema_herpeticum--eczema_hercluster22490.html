eczema herpeticum

noun
severe herpes-infected eczema skin condition
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksɪmə hərˌpɛtɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪmə hɜːˌpɛtɪkəm / (BrE)
A serious skin condition, usually in children, in which areas of eczema become infected with the herpes simplex virus, causing fever and widespread blisters or vesicles.

Examples

eczema herpeticum outbreak in children
Doctors diagnosed the sudden outbreak of blisters as eczema herpeticum.
Prompt treatment of eczema herpeticum is critical to prevent serious complications.
The child was hospitalized after developing eczema herpeticum on top of her existing eczema.
