trolling line

noun
fishing line trailed behind a moving vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊlɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊlɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
a fishing line, baited with natural or artificial hooks, that is trailed behind a moving vessel near the surface or at a specific depth to catch fish.

Examples

trolling line catches fish
After hours without a bite, they finally caught a salmon on the trolling line.
She adjusted the depth of her trolling line to target larger fish near the surface.
The fisherman let out the trolling line as the boat picked up speed across the lake.
