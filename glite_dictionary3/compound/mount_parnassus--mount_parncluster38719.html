Mount Parnassus

proper noun
mythological mountain in Greece; home of the Muses
Pronunciation: / maʊnt pɑrˈnæsəs / (AmE), / maʊnt pɑːˈnæsəs / (BrE)
A mountain in central Greece, regarded in Greek mythology as the home of the Muses and a symbol of artistic inspiration, especially in music and poetry.

Examples

Mount Parnassus inspires poets
According to legend, Mount Parnassus was sacred to Apollo and the source of music and poetry.
She referred to Mount Parnassus as the symbol of artistic achievement in her essay.
The poets climbed Mount Parnassus in their imagination, seeking inspiration from the Muses.
