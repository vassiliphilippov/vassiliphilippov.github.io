East Turkistan Islamic Movement

proper noun
Uighur separatist organization in Xinjiang
Pronunciation: / ist tɜrˈkɪstɑːn aɪˈslɑːmɪk muˈvmənt / (AmE), / iːst tɜːˈkɪstæn ɪˈslæmɪk ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
an Islamist separatist organization primarily composed of Uighur Muslims that seeks independence for Xinjiang (East Turkestan) from China; designated a terrorist group by several countries.

Examples

the East Turkistan Islamic Movement seeks independence
International organizations have debated the extent of the East Turkistan Islamic Movement's activities and influence.
Reports suggest the East Turkistan Islamic Movement operates in exile, recruiting members from Uighur communities abroad.
The Chinese government has accused the East Turkistan Islamic Movement of plotting attacks in Xinjiang.
