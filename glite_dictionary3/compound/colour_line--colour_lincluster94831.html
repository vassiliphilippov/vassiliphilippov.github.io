colour line

noun
barrier enforcing racial segregation
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə laɪn / (BrE)
a barrier, especially in the past, preventing Black people from participating in activities or institutions with white people, especially because of racial segregation

Examples

break the colour line
Efforts to break down the colour line changed the course of civil rights history.
Many writers spoke out against the injustices of the colour line in their works.
The colour line was strictly enforced in public schools during that era.

Related Terms

  • synonym – color bar: a social or legal barrier based on race, especially against Black people
  • synonym – segregation: the enforced separation of different racial groups
