field chickweed

noun
a tufted perennial chickweed plant
Pronunciation: / fiːld ˈtʃɪkˌwid / (AmE), / fiːld ˈtʃɪkwiːd / (BrE)
A densely tufted perennial plant of the chickweed family, found in the north temperate zone.

Examples

field chickweed plant
Field chickweed is commonly found in meadows and grasslands across Europe and North America.
Gardeners often remove field chickweed to prevent it from spreading among their flowers.
The densely tufted leaves of field chickweed provide ground cover in temperate climates.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chickweed: a small, low-growing plant in the carnation family, often considered a weed
