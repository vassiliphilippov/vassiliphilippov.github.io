melanin-challenged

adjective
humorous term for light-skinned person
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛlənɪn ˈtʃælɪndʒd / (AmE), / ˈmɛlənɪn ˈtʃælɪndʒd / (BrE)
(humorous) Having little or no melanin in the skin; having light or pale skin.

Examples

melanin-challenged person
He often jokes about being melanin-challenged during the summer because he can't tan.
She laughed and called herself melanin-challenged after getting sunburned at the beach.
Some people use the phrase melanin-challenged as a lighthearted way to refer to fair skin.
