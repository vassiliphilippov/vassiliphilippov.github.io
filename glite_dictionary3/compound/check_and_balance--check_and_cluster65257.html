check and balance

noun
system preventing abuse of power
Pronunciation: / tʃɛk ənd ˈbæləns / (AmE), / tʃɛk ənd ˈbæləns / (BrE)
a system or arrangement designed to prevent any one person or group from gaining too much power, especially in government, by distributing authority and providing oversight mechanisms

Examples

system of check and balance
Effective check and balance mechanisms improve trust in democratic institutions.
The constitution establishes a system of check and balance to keep the government accountable.
Without proper check and balance, corruption can easily go unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – separation of powers: division of government responsibilities into distinct branches
