crop failure

noun
failure of crops to yield a sufficient harvest
Pronunciation: / krɑp ˈfeɪljɚ / (AmE), / krɒp ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
A situation in which crops do not yield enough produce to be sold or to meet expected needs, often due to adverse conditions.

Examples

crop failure after drought
A single season of crop failure can devastate rural communities dependent on agriculture.
Farmers are hoping to avoid another crop failure this year by planting new seed varieties.
The country faced severe famine after the crop failure caused by prolonged drought.

  • synonym – harvest: the process or period of gathering in crops
