deer tick

noun
North American tick species; disease vector
Pronunciation: / dɪr tɪk / (AmE), / dɪə tɪk / (BrE)
a species of tick (Ixodes scapularis) found in North America, especially in the northeast, known as a carrier of Lyme disease

Examples

deer tick bite
Bite prevention is important in areas where deer ticks are common.
The deer tick is notorious for transmitting Lyme disease to humans.
We found a deer tick attached to our dog's ear after a walk in the woods.
