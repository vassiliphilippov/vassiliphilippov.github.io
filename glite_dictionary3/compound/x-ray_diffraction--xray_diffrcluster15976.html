X-ray diffraction

noun
scattering of X-rays by a crystal's atoms
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks ˌreɪ dɪˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈɛks ˌreɪ dɪˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
the scattering of X-rays by the atoms in a crystal, used to determine the crystal's structure through analysis of the resulting pattern

Examples

X-ray diffraction pattern analysis
Rosalind Franklin's X-ray diffraction images were crucial in discovering the double helix structure of DNA.
Scientists used X-ray diffraction to reveal the detailed structure of the mineral sample.
The laboratory specializes in analyzing materials using X-ray diffraction techniques.
