Doric order

noun
classical Greek architectural order with plain columns
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔrɪk ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈdɒrɪk ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
the oldest and simplest of the classical Greek architectural orders, characterized by sturdy columns without bases and plain capitals

Examples

ancient Doric order columns
Architecture students studied the Doric order to understand the principles of classical design.
The Parthenon in Athens is a remarkable example of the Doric order.
Unlike the Ionic and Corinthian styles, the Doric order is notable for its simplicity.
