electrical elastance

noun
property of opposing electric charge in a dielectric
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl ɪˈlæstəns / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl ɪˈlæstəns / (BrE)
The ability of a dielectric material to oppose an electric charge; the reciprocal of electrical capacitance.

Examples

electrical elastance of a material
In circuit analysis, electrical elastance is measured as the inverse of capacitance in farads.
Materials with high electrical elastance are poor at storing electrical energy.
The electrical elastance of the material determines its ability to resist the accumulation of electrical charge.

  • antonym – capacitance: the ability of a system to store electrical charge
