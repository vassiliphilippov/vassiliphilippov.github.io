mushroom poisoning

noun
illness from eating toxic mushrooms
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌʃˌruːm ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃˌrʊm ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
A toxic condition caused by eating certain poisonous mushroom species, especially those of the genus Amanita.

Examples

mushroom poisoning from wild mushrooms
Doctors warned hikers about the dangers of mushroom poisoning in the region.
He was hospitalized after suffering mushroom poisoning from eating wild fungi.
The symptoms of mushroom poisoning can include nausea, vomiting, and confusion.
