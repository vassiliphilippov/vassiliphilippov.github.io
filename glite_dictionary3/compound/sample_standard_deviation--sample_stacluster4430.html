sample standard deviation

noun
standard deviation calculated from a sample
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmpəl ˌstændərd ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmpəl ˌstændəd ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the standard deviation calculated from data in a sample, used as an estimate of the population standard deviation in statistics.

Examples

sample standard deviation of test scores

When comparing groups, a lower sample standard deviation suggests more consistent results.
