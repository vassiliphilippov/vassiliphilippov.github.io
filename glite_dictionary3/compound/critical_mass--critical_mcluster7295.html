critical mass

noun
minimum mass for nuclear chain reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪdɪkəl mæs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl mɑːs / (BrE)
the minimum amount of fissile material needed to sustain a nuclear chain reaction

Examples

nuclear critical mass
Below critical mass, a fission chain reaction cannot sustain itself.
Scientists calculated the critical mass required for a controlled nuclear reaction.
The reactor must be loaded with enough uranium to reach critical mass and start the process.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – subcritical mass: amount of fissile material below the level needed for a chain reaction
  • degree higher – supercritical mass: amount of fissile material exceeding the minimum needed for a chain reaction
