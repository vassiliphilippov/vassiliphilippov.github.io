white coat hypertension

noun
elevated clinic blood pressure due to anxiety
Pronunciation: / waɪt koʊt haɪpərˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / waɪt kəʊt haɪpəˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a condition in which a person’s blood pressure rises above normal levels in clinical settings, typically as a reaction to seeing medical staff or being in a doctor's office

Examples

white coat hypertension at the doctor
Monitoring blood pressure at home can help distinguish true hypertension from white coat hypertension.
Patients with white coat hypertension may have normal blood pressure at home but elevated readings at the doctor’s office.
The doctor explained that white coat hypertension can cause misleadingly high blood pressure readings in the clinic.
