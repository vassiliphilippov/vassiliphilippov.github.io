pole position

noun
first starting position in a race
Pronunciation: / poʊl pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / pəʊl pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the most favorable starting position in a race, usually at the front of the grid, typically awarded to the fastest qualifier

Examples

pole position in a race
Hamilton has claimed the pole position for tomorrow's Grand Prix.
He secured pole position after a flawless qualifying lap.
Starting from pole position gave her a clear advantage over the other racers.
