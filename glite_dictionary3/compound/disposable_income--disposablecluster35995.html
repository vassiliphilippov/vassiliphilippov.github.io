disposable income

noun
income left after taxes for spending or saving
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspoʊzəbəl ˈɪŋkʌm / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəbəl ˈɪnkʌm / (BrE)
the amount of money a person or household has available to spend or save after taxes have been deducted from their total income

Examples

disposable income after taxes
After paying all his taxes, Mark calculated how much disposable income he had left each month.
Many families use their disposable income to save for vacations or unexpected expenses.
The rise in salaries increased the average household's disposable income significantly.
