compos mentis

adjective
of sound mind; mentally competent
Pronunciation: / ˌkɒmpəs ˈmɛntɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpɒs ˈmɛntɪs / (BrE)
having full use of one's mental faculties; of sound mind and able to make rational decisions, especially in a legal context.

Examples

compos mentis in court
Although elderly, she remained fully compos mentis until her passing.
Only those who are clearly compos mentis can sign legal documents without a guardian.
The court needed to determine if the defendant was compos mentis at the time of the incident.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non compos mentis: not of sound mind; mentally incompetent
