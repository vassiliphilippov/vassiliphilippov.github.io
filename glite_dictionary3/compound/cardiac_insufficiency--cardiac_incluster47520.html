cardiac insufficiency

noun
condition where heart fails to pump enough blood
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdiæk ɪnˌsəfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiæk ɪnˌsʌfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
A condition in which the heart is unable to pump sufficient blood to meet the body's needs, often leading to symptoms such as chest pain or fatigue.

Examples

A diet low in sodium is often recommended for those with cardiac insufficiency to help manage their condition.
Left untreated, cardiac insufficiency can progress to severe heart failure.
The patient was diagnosed with cardiac insufficiency after experiencing frequent shortness of breath and fatigue.
