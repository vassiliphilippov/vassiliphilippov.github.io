disturbance of the peace

noun
interruption of public order or tranquility
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɝbəns əv ðə pis / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbəns əv ðə piːs / (BrE)
any act that interrupts public order or tranquility, often considered a minor legal offense

Examples

disturbance of the peace charge
Loud parties late at night can lead to a citation for disturbance of the peace.
Residents complained about the ongoing disturbance of the peace caused by street racing.
The police arrested him for disturbance of the peace after a noisy argument outside the restaurant.
