water vapour

noun
gaseous form of water in air
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ˈveɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈveɪpə / (BrE)
water in the form of a gas, especially in the air and below its boiling point

Examples

water vapour in the air
Clouds form when water vapour in the atmosphere cools and condenses into droplets.
In hot weather, the water vapour in the air increases, making it feel humid.
The greenhouse effect is partly caused by water vapour trapping heat in the earth's atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – humidity: the amount of water vapor in the air
  • degree higher – steam: water in a vaporous state, usually at or above boiling point
