Old World

noun
Europe, Asia, and Africa as known before the Americas
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊld ˈwɝːld / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
the regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa as known to Europeans before the discovery of the Americas

Examples

the Old World civilizations
Many domesticated plants and animals originated in the Old World before being introduced elsewhere.
The explorers of the 15th century set out from the Old World in search of new lands.
Trade between the Old World and the Americas transformed global economies.
