estimated tax

noun
tax paid on non-withheld income by estimate
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstɪˌmeɪtɪd tæks / (AmE), / ˈɛstɪˌmeɪtɪd tæks / (BrE)
income tax paid at regular intervals on income not subject to withholding, based on the taxpayer's projection of their tax liability

Examples

pay estimated tax quarterly
Freelancers often have to pay estimated tax each quarter to avoid penalties.
If you receive significant income outside of your regular job, you may owe estimated tax.
Missing a payment on your estimated tax can result in interest charges from the IRS.

Related Terms

  • antonym – withholding tax: tax withheld from wages and paid directly to the government by an employer
