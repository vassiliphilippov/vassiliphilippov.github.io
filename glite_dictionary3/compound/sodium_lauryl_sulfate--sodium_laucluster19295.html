sodium lauryl sulfate

noun
detergent found in shampoos and soaps
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm ˈlɔriəl ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈlɔːriəl ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic detergent and surfactant used in many cleaning and personal care products, known for its effectiveness at removing grease but may cause skin irritation

Examples

sodium lauryl sulfate in shampoo
Many shampoos contain sodium lauryl sulfate for its foaming and cleaning properties.
Some people experience skin irritation when exposed to sodium lauryl sulfate in soaps and toothpastes.
The label warns that sodium lauryl sulfate should not be ingested.
