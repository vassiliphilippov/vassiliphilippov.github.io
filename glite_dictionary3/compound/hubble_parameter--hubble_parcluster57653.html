Hubble parameter

noun
measure of universe's expansion rate
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌbəl pəˈræmɪtər / (AmE), / ˈhʌb(ə)l pəˈramɪtə / (BrE)
(cosmology) the ratio of the speed at which a galaxy recedes due to the expansion of the universe to its distance from the observer; a measure of the current rate of cosmic expansion, often referred to as the Hubble constant when considering its present-day value.

Examples

the Hubble parameter value
Astronomers use the Hubble parameter to estimate the age and size of the universe.
Recent observations suggest that the value of the Hubble parameter may differ depending on the measurement method.
The Hubble parameter helps scientists understand how quickly galaxies are moving apart due to cosmic expansion.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Hubble constant: the present value of the Hubble parameter, often considered a constant
