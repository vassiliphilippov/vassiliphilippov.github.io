strong yer

noun
historical Slavic non-reduced yer vowel
Pronunciation: / strɔŋ jɪr / (AmE), / strɒŋ jɜː / (BrE)
In historical Slavic linguistics, a 'yer' vowel that did not reduce to an ultrashort vowel but developed into a different sound in a modern Slavic language.

In Old Church Slavonic, a strong yer was retained and developed into a full vowel in many daughter languages.
