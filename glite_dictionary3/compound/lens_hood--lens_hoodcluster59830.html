lens hood

noun
camera lens attachment to block stray light
Pronunciation: / lɛnz hʊd / (AmE), / lɛnz hʊd / (BrE)
a tubular or conical attachment fitted to the front of a camera lens to prevent stray light from causing flare or reducing contrast in photographs

Examples

lens hood blocks sunlight
A lens hood can help prevent glare when shooting in bright sunlight.
Photographers often attach a lens hood to improve image contrast.
The lens hood protected the lens from accidental bumps as well as stray light.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lens cap: a protective cover for a camera lens
  • synonym – lens shade: another term for lens hood; used to block stray light
