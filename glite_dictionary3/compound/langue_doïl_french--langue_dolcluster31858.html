Langue d'oïl French

noun
medieval French dialects of northern/central France
Pronunciation: / lɑ̃ɡ dɔɪl ˈfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / lɑ̃ɡ dɔɪl ˈfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
The group of medieval dialects of French that were spoken mainly in northern and central France, distinguished by the use of 'oïl' for 'yes'.

Examples

Langue doil French dialects
Many medieval texts from northern France are written in Langue d'oïl French.
Scholars study Langue d'oïl French to understand the evolution of modern French.
The word 'oui' in modern French comes from 'oïl' in Langue d'oïl French.
