axillary artery

noun
artery in the armpit region
Pronunciation: / ˈæksəˌlɛri ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈæksɪləri ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
the part of the main artery supplying the arm that passes through the armpit, connecting the subclavian and brachial arteries

Examples

axillary artery injury
Damage to the axillary artery can lead to serious complications in blood supply to the arm.
During the anatomy lecture, we traced the path of the axillary artery from the shoulder to the upper arm.
The surgeon carefully identified the axillary artery before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – brachial artery: the main artery of the upper arm
  • degree higher – subclavian artery: artery located below the clavicle, supplying the arms and chest
