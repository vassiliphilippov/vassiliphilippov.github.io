end of the world

noun
final judgment and end of earthly life
Pronunciation: / ɛnd əv ðə wɝːld / (AmE), / ɛnd əv ðə wɜːld / (BrE)
(in Christian eschatology) the final event in the world’s history, when God is believed to judge all human beings, determining their eternal fate.

Examples

the end of the world prophecy
Many people throughout history have predicted the end of the world would occur in their lifetime.
Religious texts often describe the end of the world as a time of judgment.
Some interpret natural disasters as signs pointing toward the end of the world.
