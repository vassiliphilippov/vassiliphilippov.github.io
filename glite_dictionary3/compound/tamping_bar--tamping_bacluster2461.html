tamping bar

noun
tool for packing or compressing material
Pronunciation: / ˈtæmpɪŋ bɑr / (AmE), / ˈtæmpɪŋ bɑː / (BrE)
a long, heavy tool used for packing or compressing material into a space, such as tobacco in a pipe or explosives in a drill hole

Examples

tamping bar compresses soil
A small tamping bar is often used to press tobacco evenly into a pipe bowl.
He carried a tamping bar to pack the charge firmly into the drill hole at the quarry.
The worker used a tamping bar to compress the soil before laying the pavement.
