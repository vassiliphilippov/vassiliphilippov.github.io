light-emitting diode

noun
an electronic component that emits light
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ɪˌmɪtɪŋ daɪˌoʊd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ɪˌmɪtɪŋ dʌɪˌəʊd / (BrE)
an electronic device consisting of a diode that emits light when a current flows through it, used especially as an indicator or for illumination

Examples

light-emitting diode indicator light
A red light-emitting diode on the device indicates when it is powered on.
Many traffic lights now use light-emitting diodes instead of incandescent bulbs.
The new television uses hundreds of light-emitting diodes for brighter and more efficient lighting.
