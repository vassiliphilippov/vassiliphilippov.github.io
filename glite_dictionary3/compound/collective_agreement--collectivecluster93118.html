collective agreement

noun
labor contract between employers and employees
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪv əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
A formal contract between an employer and a group of employees, usually represented by a union, that sets out wages, benefits, and working conditions.

Examples

sign a collective agreement
After months of talks, both sides finally signed the collective agreement.
All employees are bound by the terms of the collective agreement.
The union successfully negotiated a new collective agreement covering pay raises and parental leave.

Related Terms

  • antonym – individual contract: a contract between an employer and a single employee
  • synonym – labor contract: an agreement outlining terms between an employee and employer
