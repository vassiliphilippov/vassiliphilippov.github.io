parallel operation

noun
simultaneous execution of operations
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the simultaneous execution of two or more operations, especially in computing, engineering, or electronics

Examples

parallel operation of machines
In electrical engineering, parallel operation of generators ensures a steady supply of power.
Modern computers rely on parallel operation to handle multiple instructions efficiently.
Running tasks in parallel operation can significantly reduce overall processing time.
