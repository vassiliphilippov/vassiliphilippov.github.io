character witness

noun
person testifying to another’s good character in court
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrɪktɚ ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈkærəktə ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who testifies in court about the good reputation and moral character of someone involved in a case

Examples

character witness in court
A character witness can sometimes help a defendant receive a more lenient sentence.
She served as a character witness at her colleague's trial, vouching for his integrity.
The defense called a character witness to speak about the accused's honesty.
