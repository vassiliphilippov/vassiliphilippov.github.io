general assembly

noun
official legislative body
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛnɚl əˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəl əˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
an official legislative body composed of representatives, typically responsible for making, amending, or repealing laws in a government or organization

Examples

general assembly meets annually
Every year, representatives gather at the general assembly to debate new legislation.
The constitution requires the general assembly to meet at least twice annually.
The state general assembly passed a bill to improve access to healthcare.
