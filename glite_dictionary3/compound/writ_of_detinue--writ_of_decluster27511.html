writ of detinue

noun
legal order to return unlawfully detained goods
Pronunciation: / rɪt əv ˈdɛtɪˌnuː / (AmE), / rɪt əv ˈdɛtɪnjuː / (BrE)
A legal order commanding the return of goods unlawfully withheld from their rightful owner.

Examples

writ of detinue for property return
After months of waiting, the museum obtained a writ of detinue to recover their stolen artifacts.
His lawyer advised him to apply for a writ of detinue to get his property back from the landlord.
The court issued a writ of detinue to ensure the company returned the disputed equipment.
