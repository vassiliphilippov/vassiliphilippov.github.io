homocercal fin

noun
symmetrical tail fin of most bony fishes
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːsɚkəl fɪn / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒsəkəl fɪn / (BrE)
a symmetrical tail fin that extends beyond the end of the vertebral column, as found in most bony fishes

Examples

homocercal fin in fish
Many modern fish species possess a homocercal fin that aids in efficient swimming.
The evolutionary shift to a homocercal fin allowed bony fishes to maneuver with greater agility.
Unlike sharks, goldfish have a homocercal fin that is nearly symmetrical.
