jump ball

noun
basketball play to start or resume game
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp bɔl / (AmE), / dʒʌmp bɔːl / (BrE)
(in basketball) a method of starting or resuming play by tossing the ball into the air between two opposing players, who jump to tap it to a teammate.

Examples

basketball jump ball
After a tie-up under the basket, the official called for a jump ball.
Both centers reached for the jump ball, but only one managed to tip it to a teammate.
The referee tossed up the jump ball to start the basketball game.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tip-off: the initial jump ball that starts a basketball game
