interocular lens implant

noun
artificial lens implanted in the eye
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈɑːkjələr lɛnz ɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈɒkjʊlə lɛnz ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
a clear artificial lens surgically implanted in the eye to replace the natural lens, typically after cataract removal

Examples

receive an interocular lens implant
After his cataract was removed, an interocular lens implant was necessary for clear eyesight.
Modern interocular lens implants are designed to last a lifetime without replacement.
The surgeon carefully inserted the interocular lens implant to restore the patient's vision.
