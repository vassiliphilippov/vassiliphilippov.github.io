anti-takeover defense

noun
measure to prevent hostile takeover
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈteɪˌkoʊ.vɚ dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈteɪ.kəʊ.və dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
A strategy or measure adopted by a company to prevent or discourage an unwanted takeover attempt by another company.

Examples

anti-takeover defense strategy
Legal teams often advise on the best anti-takeover defense strategies for vulnerable firms.
Shareholders debated the merits of implementing an anti-takeover defense during the annual meeting.
The company adopted an anti-takeover defense to prevent unwanted bids from competitors.

  • synonym – poison pill: A specific anti-takeover defense that makes a takeover prohibitively expensive or unattractive.
