eldest hand

noun
first player to act after dealer (cards)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛldəst hænd / (AmE), / ˈɛldɪst hand / (BrE)
the player sitting to the immediate left of the dealer in a card game; usually the first to act or play.

Examples

eldest hand acts first
After the cards were dealt, the eldest hand placed a bet to start the round.
In bridge, the eldest hand is responsible for making the opening lead.
The eldest hand often sets the tone for the rest of the game with their first move.
