basting stitch

noun
temporary sewing stitch
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪstɪŋ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪstɪŋ stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a long, loose, temporary sewing stitch used to hold layers of fabric together before permanent stitching

Examples

basting stitch holds fabric
A basting stitch makes it easier to adjust the layers during fitting.
Before sewing the final seam, she secured the fabric with a basting stitch.
You should remove the basting stitch after the permanent seams are finished.

Related Terms

  • antonym – permanent stitch: a stitch intended to permanently join fabrics
  • synonym – tacking stitch: another term for a temporary sewing stitch
