soft soap

noun
insincere flattery to gain favor
Pronunciation: / sɔft soʊp / (AmE), / sɒft səʊp / (BrE)
exaggerated praise or insincere flattery used to persuade or gain favor; persuasive talk intended to influence someone

Examples

give soft soap compliments
Don't fall for her soft soap; she's only being nice because she wants something.
He tried to win their approval with plenty of soft soap but failed to convince them.
The politician's speech was filled with soft soap, but his promises rang hollow.

  • antonym – straight talk: honest and direct speech, without flattery
