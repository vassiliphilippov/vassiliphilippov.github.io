wood warbler

noun
European woodland songbird
Pronunciation: / wʊd ˈwɔːrlbər / (AmE), / wʊd ˈwɔːləbə / (BrE)
A small European woodland songbird (Phylloscopus sibilatrix) with dull yellow plumage, known for its distinctive trilling song.

Examples

European wood warbler
Conservation efforts have helped increase the wood warbler population in several protected forests.
The wood warbler is noted for its sweet, trilling song heard in European woodlands.
We spotted a wood warbler flitting among the branches during our walk in the forest.
