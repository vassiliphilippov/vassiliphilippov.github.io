autoclaved aerated concrete

noun
lightweight, steam-cured concrete building material
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtoʊˌkleɪvd ˈɛreɪtɪd kɑnˈkrit / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd ˈeəreɪtɪd ˈkɒnkriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast building material made from concrete that is cured with steam under pressure, providing structural strength, thermal insulation, and resistance to fire and mold
