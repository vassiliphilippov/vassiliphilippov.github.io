auxiliary equipment

noun
supporting electronic devices, independent of the CPU
Pronunciation: / ɔɡˈzɪljəˌri ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈzɪliəri ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (BrE)
electronic devices that operate in support of the main system but are not directly controlled by the central processing unit

Examples

auxiliary equipment for computers
The laboratory uses several pieces of auxiliary equipment to monitor environmental conditions.
The technician checked the auxiliary equipment for any malfunction before starting the experiment.
When the main system failed, the auxiliary equipment continued to operate independently.
