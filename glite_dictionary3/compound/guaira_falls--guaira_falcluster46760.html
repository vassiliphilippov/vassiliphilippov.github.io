Guaira Falls

proper noun
former major waterfall on Brazil–Paraguay border
Pronunciation: / ˌgwaɪˈɾɑː fɔlz / (AmE), / ˌɡwaɪˈrɑː fɔːlz / (BrE)
a large former waterfall on the border between Brazil and Paraguay, once one of the world's largest by volume before being submerged by the Itaipu Dam

Examples

Guaira Falls on the border
Many people still remember the roar of Guaira Falls, even decades after it was lost.
The powerful Guaira Falls once attracted visitors from all over the world before its disappearance.
With the construction of the Itaipu Dam, Guaira Falls was submerged beneath the new reservoir.
