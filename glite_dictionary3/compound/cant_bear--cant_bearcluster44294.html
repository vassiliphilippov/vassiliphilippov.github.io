to can’t bear

verb
unable to tolerate or endure
Pronunciation: / kænt bɛr / (AmE), / kɑːnt beə / (BrE)
to be unable to tolerate or endure something; to find something so unpleasant, upsetting, or difficult that one cannot stand it

Examples

to can't bear bad news
He couldn’t bear to tell her the truth and decided to wait.
I can’t bear listening to that song anymore; it reminds me of sad memories.
She can’t bear the thought of leaving her hometown.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endure: to suffer patiently; to tolerate
  • synonym – stand: to tolerate or endure something
  • synonym – tolerate: to allow or endure something unpleasant
