silky oak

noun
Australian tree with feathery leaves and orange flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlki ˌoʊk / (AmE), / ˈsɪlki ˌəʊk / (BrE)
a medium to tall fast-growing tree of eastern Australia, Grevillea robusta, with feathery bipinnate leaves that are silky beneath and bright orange flowers; often planted as an ornamental or for timber.

Examples

tall silky oak tree
A mature silky oak can provide excellent shade in large gardens.
The avenue was lined with magnificent silky oaks that bloomed in the spring.
The timber from silky oak is valued for its attractive grain and durability.
