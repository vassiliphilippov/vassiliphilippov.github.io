manganese steel

noun
type of wear-resistant steel alloy
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡəniːz stil / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəniːz stiːl / (BrE)
a type of steel containing 10–14% manganese, known for its exceptional resistance to wear and shock

Examples

manganese steel railway tracks
Many railway tracks use manganese steel for its remarkable durability.
Replacing the old parts with manganese steel components greatly increased the machine's lifespan.
