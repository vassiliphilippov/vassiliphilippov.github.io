Kolmogorov's strong law of large numbers

noun
probability theorem on sample average convergence
Pronunciation: / koʊlˈmoʊɡərɒvz strɔːŋ lɔː əv lɑːrdʒ ˈnʌmbərz / (AmE), / kɒlˈmɒɡərɒvz strɒŋ lɔː əv lɑːdʒ ˈnʌmbəz / (BrE)
a fundamental theorem in probability theory stating that, under certain conditions, the sample averages of independent random variables converge almost surely to the expected value as the number of samples increases indefinitely
