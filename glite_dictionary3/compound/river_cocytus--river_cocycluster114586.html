River Cocytus

proper noun
mythical river in Hades (Greek mythology)
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪvər koʊˈsaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌrɪvə kəʊˈsaɪtəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a river in Hades believed to be a tributary of the Acheron and associated with lamentation.

Examples

the River Cocytus in Hades
According to legend, the souls of the dead crossed the River Cocytus on their journey to the afterlife.
In classical literature, the River Cocytus symbolizes sorrow and unending lamentation.
The poet described the mournful waters of the River Cocytus in vivid detail.
