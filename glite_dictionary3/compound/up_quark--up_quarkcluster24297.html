up quark

noun
type of elementary particle (quark)
Pronunciation: / ʌp kwɑrk / (AmE), / ʌp kwɑːk / (BrE)
A stable elementary particle with an electric charge of +2⁄3, one of the fundamental constituents of matter in the Standard Model of particle physics.

Examples

an up quark particle
An up quark is much lighter than most other fundamental particles.
Both the up quark and the down quark play vital roles in forming atomic nuclei.
The proton is composed of two up quarks and one down quark.
