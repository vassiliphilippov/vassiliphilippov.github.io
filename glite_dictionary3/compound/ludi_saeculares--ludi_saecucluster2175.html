Ludi Saeculares

proper noun
ancient Roman centennial games and rituals
Pronunciation: / ˌluːdi saɪˈkjuːləriːz / (AmE), / ˌluːdi saiˈkjuːləriːz / (BrE)
the centennial religious rites and games held in ancient Rome to mark the beginning of a new 'saeculum' or generation, celebrated roughly every hundred years

Ludi Saeculares celebrated in Rome

Scholars study the inscriptions detailing how the Ludi Saeculares evolved from the Republic into the Empire.
The Ludi Saeculares were grand public celebrations intended to honor the gods and mark the passage of a new generation in Rome.
