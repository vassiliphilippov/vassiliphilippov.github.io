emotional intelligence

noun
ability to manage and understand emotions
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmoʊʃənl ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊʃənl ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the ability to recognize, understand, and manage one's own emotions as well as those of others, and to use this awareness to guide thinking and behavior

Examples

high emotional intelligence
Companies now seek leaders with high emotional intelligence to handle workplace conflicts more effectively.
Developing emotional intelligence can significantly improve personal and professional relationships.
Her strong emotional intelligence enables her to connect deeply with her team members.

Related Terms

  • synonym – empathy: the ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • antonym – intelligence quotient: a measure of a person's intellectual abilities
  • synonym – self-awareness: conscious knowledge of one's own character and feelings
