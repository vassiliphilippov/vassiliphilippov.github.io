permanent injunction

noun
court order prohibiting an act permanently
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.mə.nənt ɪnˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mə.nənt ɪnˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
a court order that permanently prohibits a party from performing a specific act, typically issued as a final judgment after a trial

Examples

grant a permanent injunction
A permanent injunction was issued, barring the defendant from contacting the plaintiff.
After a lengthy trial, the court imposed a permanent injunction against further construction on the site.
The judge granted a permanent injunction to prevent the company from polluting the river.
