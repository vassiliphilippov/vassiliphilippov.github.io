force-feed lubricating system

noun
engine lubrication system using a pump
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrs fiːd ləˈbrɪkeɪtɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːs fiːd lʊˈbrɪkeɪtɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A mechanical system for lubricating internal combustion engines in which a pump forces oil under pressure into the engine bearings.

Examples

force-feed lubricating system in engines
Modern engines typically use a force-feed lubricating system to ensure adequate oil reaches all moving parts.
Regular maintenance of the force-feed lubricating system can prevent engine failure and costly repairs.
Unlike the older splash type, a force-feed lubricating system employs a pump to circulate oil under pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym – splash lubricating system: engine lubrication system that relies on splashing oil rather than pumping it
