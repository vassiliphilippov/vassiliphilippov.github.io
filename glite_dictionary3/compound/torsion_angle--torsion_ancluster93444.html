torsion angle

noun
angle between two planes
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔrʒən ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɔːʃən ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
the angle formed between two intersecting planes, commonly used in geometry and chemistry to describe molecular structures.

Examples

torsion angle in a molecule
Small changes in the torsion angle can alter the chemical properties of a compound.
The torsion angle between the two bonds significantly affects the molecule's shape.
X-ray crystallography is often used to measure the torsion angle in complex organic molecules.
