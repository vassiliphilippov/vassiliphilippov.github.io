dog whistle

noun
coded message for a select group
Pronunciation: / dɔɡ ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
a statement or message that conveys a secondary, often political, meaning understood only by a specific group of people

Examples

dog whistle message
Many criticized the speech for being a thinly veiled dog whistle aimed at certain voters.
Some phrases in political campaigns function as dog whistles that only insiders fully comprehend.
The candidate's remarks about 'traditional values' acted as a dog whistle to his base.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coded language: language with a hidden or secondary meaning
  • antonym – plain speech: direct, clear language without hidden meanings
  • synonym – signal: a gesture or action used to convey a message or instruction, often for a particular group
