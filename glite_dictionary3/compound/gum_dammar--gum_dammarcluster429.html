gum dammar

noun
hard resin from certain tropical trees
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm ˈdæmɑr / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈdæmə / (BrE)
a hard, pale resin obtained from certain trees, especially the amboyna pine, used in varnishes, incense, and as a glazing agent

Examples

gum dammar varnish finish
Restorers often use gum dammar in traditional picture varnishes.
