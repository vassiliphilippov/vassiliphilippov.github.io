high frequency

noun
a pitch higher than others
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
a pitch or sound with a greater number of vibrations per second, perceived as higher than other pitches

Examples

high frequency sound
Dogs can hear high frequencies that are inaudible to humans.
Some people find high frequency sounds irritating or uncomfortable.
The whistle emitted a high frequency tone to call the dog.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low frequency: a pitch or sound perceived as lower than others
