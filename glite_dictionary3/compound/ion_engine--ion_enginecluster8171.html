ion engine

noun
spacecraft propulsion engine using ions
Pronunciation: / aɪɑn ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / aɪən ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A type of propulsion engine for spacecraft, in which a stream of positive ions is accelerated to high velocity by an electric field to produce thrust.

Examples

ion engine spacecraft propulsion
Future deep space missions may rely heavily on ion engines for efficient propulsion.
The spacecraft was equipped with an advanced ion engine for its journey to Mars.
Unlike chemical rockets, an ion engine provides continuous but gentle thrust over long periods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rocket engine: an engine that produces thrust by expelling exhaust gases; used to propel rockets
