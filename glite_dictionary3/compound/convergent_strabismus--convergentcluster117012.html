convergent strabismus

noun
strabismus with inward-turning eyes
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝːdʒənt strəˈbɪzməs / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒənt strəˈbɪzməs / (BrE)
A type of strabismus in which one or both eyes turn inward toward the nose.

Examples

convergent strabismus in children
A specialist explained that convergent strabismus can sometimes require surgical intervention.
Children with convergent strabismus may experience difficulties with depth perception if left untreated.
