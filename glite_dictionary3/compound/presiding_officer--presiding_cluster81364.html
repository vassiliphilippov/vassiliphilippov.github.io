presiding officer

noun
person in charge of a meeting or assembly
Pronunciation: / prɪˈzaɪdɪŋ ˈɑːfəsər / (AmE), / prɪˈzaɪdɪŋ ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the person who leads or is in charge of a meeting, organization, or assembly, ensuring that it is conducted properly

Examples

presiding officer leads meeting
If there is any dispute, it is the presiding officer who makes the final decision.
She was elected as the presiding officer for the annual shareholders’ meeting.
