multiple-furrow plough

noun
plough with more than one share
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪpəl ˈfɜroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl ˈfʌrə plʌf / (BrE)
A plough equipped with two or more shares, coulters, and mouldboards so that several furrows can be turned at the same time.

Examples

use a multiple-furrow plough in fields
A multiple-furrow plough increases efficiency by tilling several rows at once.
Modern tractors can easily pull a multiple-furrow plough through heavy soil.
The farmer used a multiple-furrow plough to prepare the field much faster than before.
