bile acid

noun
a steroid acid in bile aiding digestion
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪl ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪl ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
any of a group of steroid acids formed in the liver, which aid in the digestion and absorption of fats, and are present in bile

Examples

bile acid helps digestion
A deficiency in bile acids can result in poor fat absorption and digestive issues.
Doctors measured the patient’s bile acid levels to assess liver function.
The liver synthesizes bile acids to facilitate the breakdown of dietary fats.
