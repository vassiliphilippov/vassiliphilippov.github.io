half hitch

noun
a simple knot for fastening a rope
Pronunciation: / hæf hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɑːf hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a simple knot made by passing the end of a rope around an object and through the loop formed, used to fasten a rope temporarily

Examples

half hitch knot
He secured the tent rope with a half hitch around the stake.
The sailor quickly tied a half hitch to keep the boat in place.
To finish the job, wrap the cord around the hook and use a half hitch.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – clove hitch: a knot formed from two successive half hitches around an object
