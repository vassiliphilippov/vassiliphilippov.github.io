extended family

noun
wider family including relatives beyond parents and children
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛndɪd ˈfæmɪli / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛndɪd ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family group consisting of the nuclear family together with other relatives such as grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins.

Examples

extended family gathering
Many cultures place great importance on the extended family and its role in daily life.
She grew up with her extended family living nearby, including her grandparents and several cousins.
Their extended family gathers for a big meal every holiday season.
