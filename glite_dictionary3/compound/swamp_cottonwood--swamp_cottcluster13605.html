swamp cottonwood

noun
North American poplar tree found in wetlands
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈkɑt̬nˌwʊd / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈkɒtnˌwʊd / (BrE)
A North American poplar tree (Populus heterophylla) with large, rounded, scalloped leaves and brownish bark and wood, typically found in wetland areas.

Examples

tall swamp cottonwood tree
The swamp cottonwood thrives in the wet lowlands along the riverbanks.

We identified several swamp cottonwood trees during our ecological survey of the marsh.
