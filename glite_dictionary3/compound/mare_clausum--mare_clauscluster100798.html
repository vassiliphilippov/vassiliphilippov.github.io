mare clausum

noun
body of water under exclusive national jurisdiction
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.reɪ ˈklɔː.səm / (AmE), / ˌmɑː.reɪ ˈklɔː.səm / (BrE)
A navigable body of water, such as a sea or ocean, that is under the exclusive jurisdiction of a single nation and closed to other states.

Examples

mare clausum policy debate
Debates over the South China Sea often reference the concept of mare clausum.
During the 17th century, some European powers asserted the right to treat certain seas as a mare clausum.
The doctrine of mare clausum contrasts with the idea of open seas or 'mare liberum'.

Related Terms

  • antonym – mare liberum: a sea open to navigation by all nations
