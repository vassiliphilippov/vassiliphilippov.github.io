electromagnetic spectrum

noun
range of electromagnetic wave frequencies
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the complete range of all frequencies of electromagnetic radiation, including radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays

Examples

the electromagnetic spectrum of light
Astronomers study different parts of the electromagnetic spectrum to learn about celestial objects.
Medical imaging often uses X-rays, which are located at the high-frequency end of the electromagnetic spectrum.
Visible light is only a small part of the electromagnetic spectrum that our eyes can perceive.
