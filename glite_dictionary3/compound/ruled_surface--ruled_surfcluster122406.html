ruled surface

noun
surface formed by moving straight line
Pronunciation: / ˈruːld ˌsɜːrfɪs / (AmE), / ˈruːld ˌsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
A surface formed by a straight line moving through space, so that at every point on the surface, a straight line lies entirely within the surface.

Examples

mathematical ruled surface
Artists sometimes experiment with shapes inspired by the mathematical concept of the ruled surface.
In differential geometry, a hyperboloid is a classic example of a ruled surface.
The architect used a ruled surface to design the curved yet straight-lined roof of the stadium.
