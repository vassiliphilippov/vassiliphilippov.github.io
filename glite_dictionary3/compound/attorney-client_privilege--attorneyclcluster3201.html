attorney-client privilege

noun
legal right to lawyer-client confidentiality
Pronunciation: / əˌtɜrni ˈklaɪənt ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˌtɜːni ˈklaɪənt ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the legal right that ensures communications between a lawyer and their client are kept confidential and cannot be disclosed without the client's consent

Examples

attorney-client privilege protects conversations
Discussing your case openly with your lawyer is protected by attorney-client privilege.
The attorney-client privilege protects clients from having their confidential communications disclosed in court.
The judge ruled that the documents were covered by attorney-client privilege and could not be admitted as evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confidentiality: state of keeping something secret, especially information
  • antonym – waiver: voluntary relinquishment of a right (in law, including privilege)
