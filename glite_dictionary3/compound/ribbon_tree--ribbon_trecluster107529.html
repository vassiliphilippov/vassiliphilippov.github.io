ribbon tree

noun
deciduous NZ tree with fiber-like bark
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪb.ən ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən ˌtriː / (BrE)
A deciduous New Zealand tree whose inner bark produces a strong fiber resembling flax, known as New Zealand cotton.

Examples

ribbon tree fiber use
During our visit to New Zealand, we learned how the ribbon tree is harvested for its so-called New Zealand cotton.
Local craftspeople use the bark of the ribbon tree to make traditional items.
The ribbon tree is valued for the strong fibers obtained from its inner bark.
