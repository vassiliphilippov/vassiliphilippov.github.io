ring vaccination

noun
targeted vaccination around infected individuals
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ ˌvæksɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪŋ ˌvaksɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the practice of vaccinating only those people who are in close contact with an infected person in order to contain the spread of a highly infectious disease

Examples

ring vaccination strategy
Effective ring vaccination requires rapid identification of cases and their close contacts.
Health authorities implemented ring vaccination to contain the outbreak of the virus.
The success of ring vaccination was a key factor in eradicating smallpox.
