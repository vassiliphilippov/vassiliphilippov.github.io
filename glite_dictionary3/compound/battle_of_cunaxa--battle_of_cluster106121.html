battle of Cunaxa

noun
a battle in 401 BC between Artaxerxes II and Cyrus the Younger
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv kuːˈnæksə / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv kjuːˈnæksə / (BrE)
a battle fought in 401 BC near Babylon, in which Artaxerxes II defeated his younger brother Cyrus the Younger, who had attempted to seize the Persian throne

Examples

the battle of Cunaxa in Persia
After the battle of Cunaxa, the Greek mercenaries were stranded deep inside Persia.
The battle of Cunaxa was a decisive moment in Persian history.
Xenophon chronicled the battle of Cunaxa in his work Anabasis.
