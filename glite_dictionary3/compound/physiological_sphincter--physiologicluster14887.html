physiological sphincter

noun
functionally defined sphincter (not anatomically visible)
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈsfɪŋktər / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈsfɪŋktə / (BrE)
A sphincter in the body whose presence is defined by its function rather than any visible anatomical structure, and which cannot be easily identified during an autopsy.

Examples

the physiological sphincter controls flow
Doctors rely on manometry tests to assess the function of a physiological sphincter.
The lower esophageal physiological sphincter prevents acid reflux into the esophagus.
Unlike an anatomical sphincter, a physiological sphincter is not visible as a distinct muscle band at autopsy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anatomical sphincter: a sphincter with a distinct anatomical structure visible in the body
