ischemic anoxia

noun
oxygen deficiency from poor blood circulation
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskiːmɪk əˈnɑksiə / (AmE), / ɪˈskiːmɪk əˈnɒksiə / (BrE)
A deficiency of oxygen in body tissues due to restricted or slow blood circulation, often associated with heart failure or poor peripheral blood flow.

Examples

ischemic anoxia after heart attack
Ischemic anoxia can lead to cell death if not promptly addressed by restoring circulation.
After a heart attack, patients may experience ischemic anoxia in certain tissues due to impaired blood flow.
Doctors monitor patients with congestive heart failure closely for signs of ischemic anoxia.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anoxia: absence or deficiency of oxygen reaching the tissues
  • degree lower – hypoxia: condition in which the body or a region of the body is deprived of adequate oxygen supply
