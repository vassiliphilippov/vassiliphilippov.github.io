brown ash

noun
North American tree with dark brown wood
Pronunciation: / braʊn æʃ / (AmE), / braʊn æʃ / (BrE)
A vigorous North American tree (Fraxinus nigra) with dark brown, heavy wood and leaves that turn gold in autumn.

Examples

a brown ash tree
In autumn, the leaves of the brown ash turn a beautiful gold color.
Many wetlands in North America are home to the brown ash tree.
The dense wood of the brown ash is valued for making baskets and furniture.
