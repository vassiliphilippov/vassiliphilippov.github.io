soft glass

noun
glass with a low softening point
Pronunciation: / sɑft ɡlæs / (AmE), / sɒft ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
Glass with a relatively low softening point, making it easier to work with at lower temperatures.

Examples

soft glass for lampworking
Many artists prefer soft glass for lampworking because it melts at a lower temperature.
Soda-lime glass is a common type of soft glass used in windows and bottles.
The laboratory switched from hard glass to soft glass tubing for certain experiments.
