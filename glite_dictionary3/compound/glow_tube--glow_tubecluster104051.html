glow tube

noun
gas-filled tube producing colored glow by electrical discharge
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊ tuːb / (AmE), / ɡləʊ tjuːb / (BrE)
a glass or metal tube filled with gas and fitted with electrodes, producing light by electrical discharge; the color of the glow depends on the gas used

Examples

glow tube experiment setup
Engineers use a glow tube to demonstrate the properties of ionized gases.
The laboratory displayed several glow tubes filled with different gases showing a range of colors.
When connected to a high voltage, the glow tube emitted a soft orange light.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neon lamp: a miniature gas-discharge lamp containing neon or other gases
