tapering off

adjective
gradually diminishing or decreasing
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪpɚɪŋ ɒf / (AmE), / ˈteɪpərɪŋ ɒf / (BrE)
becoming gradually smaller, weaker, or less in amount until almost nothing remains.

Examples

tapering off after rain
After weeks of heavy rain, the storms are finally tapering off.
Attendance at the event started strong but began tapering off after lunchtime.
The medication should be tapering off before you stop taking it completely.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dwindling: gradually diminishing in size, amount, or strength
  • antonym – increasing: becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
