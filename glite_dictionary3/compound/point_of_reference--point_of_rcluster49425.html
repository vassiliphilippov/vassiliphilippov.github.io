point of reference

noun
basis for comparison or orientation
Pronunciation: / pɔɪnt əv ˈrɛf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ɒv ˈrɛf.ərəns / (BrE)
something that is used as a basis for comparison or as a guide for orientation in understanding or evaluating other things

Examples

a point of reference for comparison
Historical events often become a point of reference for later generations.
In scientific experiments, the control group serves as a point of reference for interpreting results.
Without a point of reference, it is difficult to assess progress or change.
