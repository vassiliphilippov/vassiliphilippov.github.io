circumflex vein

noun
curved vein adjacent to circumflex artery
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrˌkʌmˌflɛks veɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəmˌflɛks veɪn / (BrE)
any of several curved veins running parallel to arteries with the same name, especially in the body and limbs

Examples

circumflex vein injury
An injury to the circumflex vein can cause significant bleeding in the upper arm.
Several circumflex veins accompany the arteries supplying the hip joint.
The surgeon carefully identified the circumflex vein before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circumflex artery: an artery that runs in a curved path around a structure, usually accompanied by a circumflex vein
