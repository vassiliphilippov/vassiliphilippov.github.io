landed gentry

noun
land-owning social class
Pronunciation: / ˈlændɪd ˈdʒɛntri / (AmE), / ˈlændɪd ˈdʒɛntri / (BrE)
A social class consisting of gentry who own land, especially those who live off rental income from their estates rather than practicing a profession.

Examples

English landed gentry
Many members of the landed gentry derived their wealth from vast agricultural estates.
She came from a family of the landed gentry and inherited a large manor house.
The landed gentry played a significant role in rural British society throughout the nineteenth century.
