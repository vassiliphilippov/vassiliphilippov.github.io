close to the wind

adverb
sailing nearly into the wind
Pronunciation: / kloʊs tə ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / kləʊs tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
(of a sailing vessel) with its head pointed nearly towards the direction from which the wind is blowing, so as to sail as directly into the wind as possible without losing speed.

Examples

sail close to the wind
Navigating close to the wind requires skill and careful adjustment of the sails.
The yacht was sailing close to the wind to get the best speed up the coast.
They managed to keep close to the wind even as the breeze shifted direction.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – leeward: the direction away from the wind
  • degree higher – windward: the direction from which the wind is coming
