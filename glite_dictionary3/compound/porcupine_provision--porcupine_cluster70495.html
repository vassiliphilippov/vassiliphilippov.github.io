porcupine provision

noun
corporate tactic to prevent takeovers
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔrk.jəˌpaɪn prəˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.kjʊˌpaɪn prəˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a defensive measure taken by a corporation to deter or prevent unwanted takeover attempts, typically by making itself less attractive to potential acquirers

Examples

porcupine provision blocks takeover
Many corporations use a porcupine provision as a legal shield against aggressive mergers.
Shareholders debated whether the porcupine provision would truly protect their interests in the long run.
The company adopted a porcupine provision to fend off the hostile takeover bid.
