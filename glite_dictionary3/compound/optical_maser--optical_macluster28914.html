optical maser

noun
early term for a laser
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl ˈmeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈmeɪsə / (BrE)
an optical device that produces an intense, monochromatic, coherent beam of light by stimulated emission of radiation; an early term for 'laser'

Examples

build an optical maser
Scientists built an optical maser to study the behavior of coherent light.
The first optical maser was developed in the early 1960s, paving the way for modern laser technology.
Though the term optical maser is rarely used today, it marked the beginning of laser research.
