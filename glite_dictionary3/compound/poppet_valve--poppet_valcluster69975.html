poppet valve

noun
mushroom-shaped engine valve
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːpɪt ˌvælv / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪt ˌvɑːlv / (BrE)
A valve consisting of a disc or mushroom-shaped component that moves perpendicularly from its seat to open or close a passage, commonly used in internal combustion engines.

Examples

poppet valve in an engine
A faulty poppet valve can greatly reduce engine performance.
Automotive engineers often prefer the poppet valve for its efficient sealing capabilities.
The engine relies on a poppet valve system to control intake and exhaust gases.
