chance-half correlation

noun
test reliability correlation method
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæns hæf kəˌrleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːns hɑːf kəˌlɜːɹeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a correlation coefficient measured between scores on two randomly divided halves of a test, used as an estimate of the test's reliability

Examples

chance-half correlation in test scores
A high chance-half correlation indicates the test has good internal reliability.
Researchers often use chance-half correlation to validate new assessment tools.
The psychologist calculated the chance-half correlation to estimate the consistency of the test results.
