labial palpus

noun
jointed appendage on insect's mouthpart
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.bi.əl ˈpæl.pəs / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bɪ.əl ˈpal.pəs / (BrE)
Either of the jointed appendages located on the front of the mentum in an insect, typically involved in sensory functions related to feeding.

Examples

labial palpus of an insect
Damage to the labial palpus can affect the feeding behavior of certain beetles.
The labial palpus of the moth helps it to taste and manipulate food.
Under the microscope, the labial palpus is clearly visible extending from the insect's head.
