treading water

noun
staying afloat by moving arms and legs
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛdɪŋ ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛdɪŋ ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
a method of staying afloat in water by moving the arms and legs to keep the head above the surface

Examples

treading water in the pool
Children learn treading water to increase their confidence in the pool.
Lifeguards are trained in treading water as part of their certification.
She became tired after treading water in the deep end for several minutes.
