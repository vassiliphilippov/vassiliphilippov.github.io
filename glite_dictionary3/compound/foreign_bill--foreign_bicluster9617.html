foreign bill

noun
bill of exchange payable in a different country
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrən bɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪn bɪl / (BrE)
A bill of exchange drawn in one country and made payable in another.

Examples

foreign bill for payment abroad
A foreign bill can facilitate transactions between buyers and sellers in different countries.
Bankers often verify the authenticity of a foreign bill before processing international payments.
The company received a foreign bill that was payable in London.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inland bill: a bill of exchange drawn and payable within the same country
