crash dive

noun
rapid emergency submarine descent
Pronunciation: / kræʃ daɪv / (AmE), / kræʃ daɪv / (BrE)
a rapid, steep descent by a submarine or aircraft, usually as an emergency maneuver to avoid danger or detection

Examples

crash dive to safety
During the drill, the crew practiced a crash dive in under a minute.
The order for a crash dive was given when the periscope spotted a hostile plane overhead.
The submarine executed a crash dive to avoid the approaching enemy ship.
After a warning of incoming aircraft, the commander ordered an immediate crash dive.
The submarine's crash dive drills were essential for their survival in hostile waters.
When the enemy ship appeared on sonar, the crew prepared for a crash dive.
