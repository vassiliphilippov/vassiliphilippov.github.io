trichloroacetic acid

noun
a strong, chlorinated acid used in science and medicine
Pronunciation: / traɪˌklɔroʊəˈsitɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / traɪˌklɔːrəʊəˈsɪtɪk ˌasɪd / (BrE)
a strong acid obtained by replacing three hydrogen atoms in acetic acid with chlorine, used in biochemistry and medicine

Examples

trichloroacetic acid solution
Trichloroacetic acid is frequently used to precipitate proteins in biochemical experiments.
In the laboratory, samples are often deproteinized with trichloroacetic acid before analysis.
