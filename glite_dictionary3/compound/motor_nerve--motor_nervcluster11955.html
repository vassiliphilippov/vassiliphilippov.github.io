motor nerve

noun
nerve carrying signals to muscles or glands
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊtər nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə nɜːv / (BrE)
a nerve that carries impulses from the central nervous system to muscles or glands, causing them to act

Examples

motor nerve controls muscles
A motor nerve transmits signals from the brain to your hand, allowing you to write.
Injury to a motor nerve may prevent the affected muscle from moving properly.
The doctor explained that a damaged motor nerve can cause muscle weakness or paralysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sensory nerve: a nerve that carries impulses from sensory receptors toward the central nervous system
