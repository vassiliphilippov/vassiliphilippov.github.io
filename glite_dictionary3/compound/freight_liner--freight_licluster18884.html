freight liner

noun
express freight train for rapid cargo transfer
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪt ˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈfreɪt ˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
a long-distance express freight train connecting industrial centers and seaports, equipped for rapid loading and unloading of goods

Examples

a freight liner carries goods
A freight liner arrived at the yard, ready to be loaded with containers heading overseas.
Freight companies rely on the efficiency of a freight liner for timely goods transportation.
The new freight liner made it possible to deliver products from the factory to the port overnight.
