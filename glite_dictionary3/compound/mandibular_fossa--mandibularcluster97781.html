mandibular fossa

noun
depression in the skull for the lower jaw
Pronunciation: / mænˈdɪbjələr ˈfɑsə / (AmE), / mænˈdɪbjʊlə ˈfɒsə / (BrE)
the deep concavity in the temporal bone at the base of the skull where the condyle of the mandible fits

Examples

mandibular fossa of skull
Damage to the mandibular fossa can lead to issues with jaw movement and pain.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the structures near the mandibular fossa.
The temporomandibular joint is formed where the mandible meets the mandibular fossa of the temporal bone.
