working party

noun
temporary group organized for a specific task
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːkɪŋ ˌpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a group of people appointed to work together temporarily to achieve a specific goal, often within an organization, company, or government

Examples

working party on safety rules
A working party was set up to draft the new environmental guidelines.
The working party will present its final report to the board next month.
The committee formed a working party to investigate the issue and make recommendations.
