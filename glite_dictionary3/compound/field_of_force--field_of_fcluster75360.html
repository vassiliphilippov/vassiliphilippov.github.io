field of force

noun
space influenced by a force (physics)
Pronunciation: / fiːld əv fɔːrs / (AmE), / fiːld əv fɔːs / (BrE)
The region surrounding a radiating body within which its electromagnetic or other physical forces can affect another body not in contact with it.

Examples

electric field of force
Electric charges create a field of force that influences nearby particles.
Scientists study how a field of force interacts with matter at the atomic level.
The Earth's gravitational field of force extends far into space.
