economic mobilization

noun
organizing economy for a specific purpose
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkɑnəmɪk ˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / iːˌkɒnəmɪk ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of organizing and directing a country's economic resources towards a specific goal, especially in preparation for war or national emergency

Examples

economic mobilization for war
Government leaders discussed strategies for economic mobilization in times of crisis.
Rapid economic mobilization helped the nation recover after the natural disaster.
The country began economic mobilization to support its military efforts during the conflict.
