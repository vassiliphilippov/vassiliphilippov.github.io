urinary tract

noun
organs and tubes for urine production/excretion
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrɪˌnɛri trækt / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri trakt / (BrE)
the system of organs and tubes in the body responsible for the production, storage, and removal of urine.

Examples

the urinary tract infection
A bacterial infection of the urinary tract can cause pain and discomfort.
Doctors often test urine samples to check for urinary tract problems.
The kidneys, bladder, and urethra are all part of the urinary tract.
