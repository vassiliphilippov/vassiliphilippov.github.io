wire matrix printer

noun
impact printer using wires to form dots
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪr ˌmætrɪks ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə ˌmætrɪks ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
an impact printer in which characters are produced as patterns of dots formed by thin wires or styli striking an inked ribbon

Examples

wire matrix printer prints invoices
The office still uses a wire matrix printer to generate carbon copies of invoices.
Unlike modern inkjet models, a wire matrix printer forms each character with tiny dots.
You can hear the distinctive buzzing sound when a wire matrix printer is working.
