removable disk

noun
storage device that can be removed from a drive
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmuvəbəl dɪsk / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːvəbəl dɪsk / (BrE)
a hard disk or storage device that can be physically removed from a disk drive, allowing for transport or enhanced security

Examples

insert removable disk into drive
He inserted the removable disk into the drive to transfer his files.
Many companies use removable disks to safely store backup data offsite.
The security policy requires all removable disks to be locked away after use.
