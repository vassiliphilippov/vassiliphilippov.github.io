vertebral column

noun
the spine or backbone
Pronunciation: / vɝˈtibrəl ˈkɑləm / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrəl ˈkɒləm / (BrE)
the series of bones (vertebrae) extending from the base of the skull to the tailbone, forming the main axis of the skeleton and enclosing the spinal cord

Examples

Damage to the vertebral column can lead to serious health problems.

The doctor explained that the vertebral column protects the spinal cord from injury.
