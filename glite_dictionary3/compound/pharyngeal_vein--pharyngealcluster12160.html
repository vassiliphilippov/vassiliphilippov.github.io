pharyngeal vein

noun
vein draining the pharynx
Pronunciation: / fəˈrɪndʒiəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌfærɪnˈdʒiːəl veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins from the pharyngeal plexus that drain the pharynx and empty into the internal jugular vein

Examples

pharyngeal vein injury
Damage to a pharyngeal vein can lead to internal bleeding during throat surgeries.
The pharyngeal vein helps drain blood from the areas surrounding the pharynx.
The doctor explained how the pharyngeal vein connects to the internal jugular vein.
