conduction anaesthesia

noun
anesthesia by blocking a nerve's area
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌkʃən ˌænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / kənˈdʌkʃən ˌanɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Anesthesia of a part of the body produced by applying an anesthetic to a nerve, blocking sensation in the area it supplies.

Examples

conduction anaesthesia for dental work
Conduction anaesthesia is commonly used during minor surgical procedures to avoid general anesthesia.
Complications from conduction anaesthesia are rare when performed by experienced practitioners.
The dental surgeon used conduction anaesthesia to numb the patient's jaw before the extraction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – local anaesthesia: loss of sensation in a small area of the body, typically by injection
