empty nester

noun
parent whose children have left home
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmpti ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
A parent whose children have grown up and left home, especially regarded as entering a new life stage.

Examples

become an empty nester
After their daughter went to college, Jane found it hard adjusting to life as an empty nester.
Becoming an empty nester gave him the opportunity to focus more on his hobbies.
Many empty nesters choose to downsize and move to a smaller home.
