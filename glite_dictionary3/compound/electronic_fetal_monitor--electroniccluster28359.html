electronic fetal monitor

noun
device monitoring fetal heartbeat and contractions
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈfiːtəl ˈmɑnɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈfiːtəl ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
a medical device that uses electronic sensors to track a fetus's heartbeat and the mother's uterine contractions during childbirth

Examples

electronic fetal monitor tracks heartbeat
During labor, the nurse checked the electronic fetal monitor readings every few minutes.
Hospitals typically use an electronic fetal monitor to ensure the baby's safety during delivery.
The doctor placed an electronic fetal monitor on the patient's abdomen to track the baby's heart rate.
