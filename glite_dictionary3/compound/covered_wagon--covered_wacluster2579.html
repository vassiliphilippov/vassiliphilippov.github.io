covered wagon

noun
wagon with canvas top used by US pioneers
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvɚd ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈkʌvəd ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
a large wagon with broad wheels and an arched canvas top, used especially by United States pioneers to cross the prairies in the 19th century

Examples

a covered wagon crossing prairies
Children learned about the hardships of travel by covered wagon during their history lesson.
Many museums display a restored covered wagon as an example of frontier transportation.
The pioneers packed all their belongings into a covered wagon for the long journey west.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Conestoga wagon: a type of large, covered wagon used for transporting freight
  • synonym – prairie schooner: a light covered wagon used by pioneers crossing the North American prairies
