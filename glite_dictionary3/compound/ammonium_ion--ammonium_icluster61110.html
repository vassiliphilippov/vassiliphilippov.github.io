ammonium ion

noun
the NH4+ ion derived from ammonia
Pronunciation: / əˈmoʊniəm aɪˌɑn / (AmE), / əˈməʊniəm aɪˌɒn / (BrE)
a positively charged ion (NH4+) formed from ammonia, common in chemical compounds and behaving similarly to alkali metals

Examples

ammonium ion in solution
Biologists often measure the ammonium ion concentration in soil to assess nutrient content.
The ammonium ion is commonly found in many fertilizers.
When dissolved in water, ammonium ion forms a slightly acidic solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ammonia: a colorless gas with a strong smell, NH3, from which ammonium ion is derived
