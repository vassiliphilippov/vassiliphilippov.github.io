raffia palm

noun
tropical palm yielding raffia fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈræfi.ə pɑm / (AmE), / ˈræfɪə pɑːm / (BrE)
a large tropical palm of Africa and Madagascar with very long feather-like fronds, whose leafstalks provide a strong commercial fiber

Examples

raffia palm leaves
The raffia palm is widely cultivated for its strong fiber used in basket weaving.
The dense fronds of the raffia palm offer shelter for many rainforest animals.
Villagers harvested leaves from the raffia palm for traditional crafts and rope-making.
