exchange of contracts

noun
formal swapping of signed contracts (esp. in property sales)
Pronunciation: / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ əv ˈkɑntrækts / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ əv ˈkɒntrækts / (BrE)
the formal process in which two parties each sign and swap contracts, making an agreement, especially in property sales, legally binding

Examples

exchange of contracts in house sale
After the exchange of contracts, both parties arranged for the final payment and moving date.
Delays in the exchange of contracts can cause uncertainty in property transactions.
The exchange of contracts marks the point when both buyer and seller are legally committed to the sale.
