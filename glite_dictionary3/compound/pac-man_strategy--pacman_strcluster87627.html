Pac-Man strategy

noun
corporate takeover defense tactic
Pronunciation: / ˈpæk mæn ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˈpak man ˈstratədʒi / (BrE)
A defensive tactic used by a company being targeted for a takeover, in which the target company attempts to take over the acquirer instead.

Examples

Pac-Man strategy in business
Analysts described the Pac-Man strategy as risky but potentially effective in defending against unwanted takeovers.
In a surprising move, the small tech firm used a Pac-Man strategy and tried to acquire its much larger adversary.
The board adopted a Pac-Man strategy to fight off the hostile bid from their rival.
