straight ticket

noun
voting for one party's candidates only
Pronunciation: / streɪt ˈtɪkɪt / (AmE), / streɪt ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
a ballot on which a voter selects all candidates from the same political party, rather than choosing candidates from different parties

Examples

vote a straight ticket
Casting a straight ticket can simplify the voting process for those who are loyal to one party.
He voted a straight ticket in every election for the past twenty years.
Some voters prefer a straight ticket to show strong support for their party.

Related Terms

  • antonym – split ticket: a ballot on which a voter selects candidates from more than one party
