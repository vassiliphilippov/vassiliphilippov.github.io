gambrel roof

noun
roof with two slopes per side, lower steeper
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæmbrəl ruːf / (AmE), / ˈɡæmbrəl ruːf / (BrE)
A roof with two slopes on each side, the lower slope being steeper than the upper, commonly seen on barns and some houses.

Examples

classic gambrel roof barn
Many colonial homes feature a classic gambrel roof design.
The barn's gambrel roof allowed for extra storage space in the loft.
They decided on a gambrel roof for the new shed to give it a traditional look.
