indefinite numeral

noun
numeral expressing an inexact quantity
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət ˈnuːmərəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət ˈnjuːmərəl / (BrE)
a numeral that refers to a non-specific or approximate quantity rather than an exact number, such as 'many', 'few', or 'most'.

Examples

indefinite numeral like 'many'
The teacher used an indefinite numeral when she said, "Several students have completed the assignment."
Unlike exact numbers, an indefinite numeral gives a general sense of quantity without specifying the count.
Words like most and many are considered indefinite numerals because they do not state a precise amount.

Related Terms

  • antonym – definite numeral: a numeral specifying an exact quantity (e.g., 'three', 'twenty')
