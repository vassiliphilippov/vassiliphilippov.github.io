vanity fair

noun
scene of futile or frivolous pursuits
Pronunciation: / ˈvænəti fɛr / (AmE), / ˈvænəti feə / (BrE)
A life or scene characterized by vain, frivolous pursuits and materialistic pleasures, especially in a large city.

Examples

the vanity fair of city life
He soon tired of the endless vanity fair that seemed to define city life.
Her memoir exposes the hollowness of the vanity fair she once enjoyed.
The novel portrays London as a modern vanity fair, filled with parties and superficial ambitions.
