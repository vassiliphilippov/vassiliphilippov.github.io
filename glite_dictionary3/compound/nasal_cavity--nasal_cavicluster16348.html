nasal cavity

noun
hollow space inside the nose
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪzəl ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈneɪzl ˈkævəti / (BrE)
either of the two hollow spaces inside the nose that connect the nostrils to the throat, involved in breathing and smelling

Examples

the nasal cavity passage
Air enters the body through the nasal cavity during normal breathing.
Doctors examined the patient's nasal cavity for signs of infection after the accident.
The nasal cavity is lined with mucous membranes to trap dust and microbes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nostril: either of the two external openings of the nose
  • degree higher – pharynx: the membrane-lined cavity behind the nose and mouth
