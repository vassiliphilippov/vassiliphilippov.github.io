antler moth

noun
European moth with antler-like markings
Pronunciation: / ˈæntlər mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈæntlɚ mɒθ / (BrE)
A European moth (Cerapteryx graminis) with white, antler-like markings on its forewings; its larvae can cause damage to pastures and grasslands.

Examples

the antler moth with white markings
A distinctive feature of the antler moth is the white, branching markings on its forewings.
Farmers are often concerned when large numbers of antler moth caterpillars appear in their grasslands.
The fields were infested with antler moth larvae, which destroyed much of the grass.
