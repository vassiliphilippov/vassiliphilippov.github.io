biotic community

noun
group of interacting organisms in an area
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɑːtɪk kəˈmjunəti / (AmE), / baɪˈɒtɪk kəˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
(Ecology) a group of interdependent organisms living together in the same area and interacting with each other.

Examples

biotic community in a forest
Human activities can disrupt the balance of a lake's biotic community.
Researchers study changes in the biotic community to understand the impact of environmental stress.
The forest's biotic community includes plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms that interact constantly.
