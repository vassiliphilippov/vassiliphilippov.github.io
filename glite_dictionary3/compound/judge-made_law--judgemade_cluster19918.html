judge-made law

noun
law created by court decisions
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒ meɪd lɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ meɪd lɔː / (BrE)
Law that is established by judicial decisions and precedents rather than by statutes or formal legislation.

Examples

judge-made law shapes decisions
In the absence of statutes, courts often apply judge-made law to resolve disputes.
Some legal systems rely heavily on judge-made law to fill gaps in legislation.
