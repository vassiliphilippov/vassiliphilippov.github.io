tax write-off

noun
deductible amount for tax reduction
Pronunciation: / tæks ˈraɪt ɑf / (AmE), / tæks ˈraɪt ɒf / (BrE)
An amount that a taxpayer is allowed to deduct from their total taxable income, thereby reducing the overall tax liability.

Examples

tax write-off for donations
Donating old clothes to charity can sometimes qualify as a tax write-off.
Many small business owners track expenses carefully to maximize their tax write-offs each year.
The company claimed a large tax write-off for the purchase of new equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deduction: an amount that is subtracted from gross income for tax purposes
