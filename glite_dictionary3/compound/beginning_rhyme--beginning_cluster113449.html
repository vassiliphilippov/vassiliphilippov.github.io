beginning rhyme

noun
repetition of initial consonant sounds in poetry
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɪnɪn raɪm / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪnɪn raɪm / (BrE)
the repetition of the same consonant sound at the beginning of each stressed syllable in a line of verse; also known as alliteration.

Examples

beginning rhyme in poetry
Classic tongue-twisters often rely on beginning rhyme to create a playful effect.
In the phrase 'Peter Piper picked a peck,' the beginning rhyme makes the line memorable.
The poet used beginning rhyme in every line to enhance the musical quality of her verse.
