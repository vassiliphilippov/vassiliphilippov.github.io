mitre box

noun
tool for guiding saw in angle cuts
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtər bɑks / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə bɒks / (BrE)
A hand tool with slots for guiding a saw to make precise angle cuts in wood, especially for miter joints.

Examples

cut wood in a mitre box
A mitre box helps carpenters achieve precise angles when making picture frames.
He used a mitre box to make sure each piece was cut at exactly 45 degrees.
Without a mitre box, it's hard to get clean, accurate miter joints by hand.

Related Terms

  • synonym – miter joint: an angled joint, usually at 45 degrees, formed by two pieces of wood
