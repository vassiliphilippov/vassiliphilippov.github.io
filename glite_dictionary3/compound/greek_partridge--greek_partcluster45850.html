Greek partridge

noun
a partridge found in southern Europe
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrik ˈpɑr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːk ˈpɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
a species of partridge (Alectoris graeca) found in mountainous regions of southern Europe, especially Greece and surrounding countries

Examples

wild Greek partridge
The Greek partridge inhabits rocky hillsides and is well adapted to the mountainous landscape.
Unlike the red-legged partridge, the Greek partridge is native to southeastern Europe.
