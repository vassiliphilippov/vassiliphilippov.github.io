hard-pressed

adjective
in difficulty, especially financially
Pronunciation: / hɑrdˈprɛst / (AmE), / hɑːdˈprɛst / (BrE)
Experiencing difficulty, especially financial or due to a lack of resources.

Examples

hard-pressed for money
Many families are hard-pressed to pay their bills during economic downturns.
She was hard-pressed to find a solution to their financial problems.
The company is hard-pressed for cash after several months of poor sales.
