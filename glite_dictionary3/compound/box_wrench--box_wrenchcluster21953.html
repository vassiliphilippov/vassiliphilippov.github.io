box wrench

noun
wrench with a closed loop end
Pronunciation: / bɑks rɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɒks rɛntʃ / (BrE)
A wrench with a closed, typically circular end (a socket) that fits over the entire head of a nut or bolt for tightening or loosening.

Examples

tighten bolts with a box wrench
A box wrench can reach nuts in tight spaces where other tools might slip off.
He used a box wrench to tighten the bolts on the car battery.
The mechanic prefers a box wrench for jobs requiring extra grip and torque.

Related Terms

  • antonym – open-end wrench: a wrench with a U-shaped opening at each end for gripping nuts and bolts
  • synonym – socket wrench: a wrench with a removable socket to fit different-sized fasteners
