complete fracture

noun
bone break through entire width
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplit ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
a bone fracture in which the break goes through the entire width of the bone, separating it into two or more pieces

Examples

a complete fracture of bone
Surgery was required due to the complete fracture and displacement of the bone.
The X-ray revealed a complete fracture of his femur after the accident.
Unlike an incomplete break, a complete fracture means the bone is entirely separated.

Related Terms

  • antonym – incomplete fracture: a bone fracture in which the break does not extend through the entire width of the bone
