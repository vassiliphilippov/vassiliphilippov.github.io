fumaric acid

noun
a colorless organic acid used in food and industry
Pronunciation: / fjuˈmærɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / fjuːˈmɑːrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline organic acid with a fruity taste, used in food and in making polyester resins and other industrial products

Examples

fumaric acid in food products
Fumaric acid is commonly added to food products as an acidity regulator.
Because of its fruity taste, fumaric acid is sometimes used in beverage powders.
The laboratory synthesized fumaric acid for use in polyester resin production.
