wood louse

noun
small terrestrial crustacean; lives in damp places
Pronunciation: / wʊd laʊs / (AmE), / wʊd laʊs / (BrE)
A small terrestrial crustacean with a flattish, segmented body, found in damp habitats such as under logs or stones.

Examples

wood louse under a log
Children often discover a wood louse while lifting up old logs or stones outside.
We found a wood louse hiding beneath the damp flower pot in the garden.
When disturbed, the wood louse scurried away into a crack in the wall.

Related Terms

  • antonym – insect: six-legged arthropod, distinct from wood louse, which is a crustacean
  • synonym – pill bug: a woodlouse that can roll into a ball; Armadillidiidae family
  • synonym – sow bug: a common term for wood lice, especially those that do not roll into a ball
