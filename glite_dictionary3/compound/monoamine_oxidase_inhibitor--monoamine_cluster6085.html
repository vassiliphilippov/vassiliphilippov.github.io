monoamine oxidase inhibitor

noun
antidepressant drug inhibiting monoamine oxidase
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊnoʊˌæmin ˈɑksɪˌdeɪs ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˌæmiːn ˈɒksɪˌdeɪz ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a drug or substance that blocks the activity of monoamine oxidase, often used to treat depression by increasing levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain
