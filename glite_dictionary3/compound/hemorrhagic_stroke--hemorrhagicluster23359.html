hemorrhagic stroke

noun
stroke caused by brain bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk stroʊk / (AmE), / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk strəʊk / (BrE)
A type of stroke caused by bleeding from a ruptured blood vessel in the brain.

Examples

hemorrhagic stroke in brain
A hemorrhagic stroke can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.
Her grandfather suffered a hemorrhagic stroke after a blood vessel burst in his brain.
Recovery from a hemorrhagic stroke often involves extensive rehabilitation and therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ischemic stroke: stroke caused by blockage of blood supply to the brain
