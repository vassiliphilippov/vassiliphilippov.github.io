ethical motive

noun
motivation rooted in moral principles
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθɪkəl ˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪkəl ˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
a reason for acting that is based on ideas of what is morally right or wrong

Examples

ethical motive for action
Her decision to speak out against injustice was driven primarily by an ethical motive.
Some argue that charity should have an ethical motive rather than a desire for recognition.
The lawyer’s ethical motive was clear in the way he defended his client.
