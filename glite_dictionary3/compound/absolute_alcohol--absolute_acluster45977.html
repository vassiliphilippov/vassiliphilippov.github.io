absolute alcohol

noun
pure ethyl alcohol with little to no water
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.səˌlut ˈæl.kəˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈab.səˌluːt ˈæl.kəˌhɒl / (BrE)
pure ethyl alcohol (ethanol) containing no more than 1% water, used especially for laboratory or industrial purposes.

Examples

absolute alcohol for experiments
Absolute alcohol is often used as a solvent in chemical analyses.
She stored the absolute alcohol in a tightly sealed container to prevent it from absorbing moisture.
The laboratory required absolute alcohol for the experiment to avoid any contamination from water.

Related Terms

  • antonym – denatured alcohol: ethyl alcohol with additives to make it unfit for drinking
  • synonym – ethanol: the type of alcohol found in alcoholic drinks; ethyl alcohol
