pressure point

noun
place where problems may arise
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place, situation, or aspect that is likely to cause problems or difficulties, especially in a process or system

Examples

critical pressure point
Negotiations stalled at a pressure point when both sides refused to compromise.
The company's finances have reached a pressure point that demands immediate attention.
The introduction of new technology became a major pressure point for the management team.

Related Terms

  • synonym – flashpoint: a place, event, or time at which trouble, such as violence or anger, flares up
