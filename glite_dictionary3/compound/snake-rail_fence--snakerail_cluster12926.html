snake-rail fence

noun
zigzag wooden rail fence
Pronunciation: / ˈsneɪk reɪl fɛns / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk reɪl fɛns / (BrE)
A type of fence made from interlocking rails arranged in a zigzag pattern, commonly used in North America for marking boundaries or containing livestock.

Examples

old snake-rail fence
Settlers built snake-rail fences because they required no nails and could be erected quickly.
The snake-rail fence added historic charm to the countryside landscape.
The old farm was bordered by a traditional snake-rail fence that meandered through the fields.
