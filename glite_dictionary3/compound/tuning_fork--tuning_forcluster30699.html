tuning fork

noun
metal device for tuning instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈtu.nɪŋ ˌfɔrk / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.nɪŋ ˌfɔːk / (BrE)
A metal instrument with two prongs that produces a fixed pitch when struck, used for tuning musical instruments.

Examples

tuning fork for music
Doctors sometimes use a tuning fork to test hearing or bone conduction in medical exams.
The pianist used a tuning fork to ensure her instrument was perfectly in tune.
When the tuning fork vibrates, it emits a clear tone that helps musicians tune by ear.
