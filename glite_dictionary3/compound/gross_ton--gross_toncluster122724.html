gross ton

noun
British unit of weight, 2,240 pounds
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊs tʌn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs tʌn / (BrE)
a British unit of weight equal to 2,240 pounds (about 1,016 kilograms), mainly used in shipping and freight

Examples

one gross ton of cargo
Freight rates are often calculated by the gross ton in British shipping.
One gross ton is equivalent to 2,240 pounds, which differs from the American short ton.
The ship has a capacity of 10,000 gross tons.
