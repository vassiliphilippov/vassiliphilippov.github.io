poison sumac

noun
North American swamp shrub causing skin rash
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzən suˈmæk / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən ˈsuːmæk / (BrE)
A North American shrub or small tree, Toxicodendron vernix, found in swamps, with pinnate leaves, clusters of greenish-white berries, and an oil that causes skin irritation.

poison sumac causes rash
Contact with poison sumac can cause a severe skin rash in many people.

The berries of poison sumac turned white in late summer, standing out against the dark green leaves.
