physics envy

noun
admiration or jealousy of physics' rigor or prestige
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪzɪks ˌɛnvi / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪks ˌenvi / (BrE)
A feeling of jealousy or admiration for the perceived accuracy, prestige, or scientific rigor of physics, especially as compared to other academic fields.

Examples

physics envy in research
Her research proposal was criticized as an example of physics envy, striving for unnecessary exactness.
Many social scientists have been accused of physics envy when they try to model human behavior with mathematical precision.
Some economists suffer from physics envy, aspiring to achieve the same status as natural scientists.
