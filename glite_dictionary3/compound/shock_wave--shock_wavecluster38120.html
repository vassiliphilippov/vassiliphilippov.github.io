shock wave

noun
sudden pressure wave from explosion or impact
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk weɪv / (AmE), / ʃɒk weɪv / (BrE)
a sudden, powerful wave of energy or pressure moving through air, water, or another material, usually caused by an explosion, impact, or other strong force; also, a strong reaction or disturbance caused by a surprising event

Examples

a shock wave from explosion

The scientists measured the shock wave created by the supersonic jet.
A shock wave travels faster than the speed of sound in air.
The explosion produced a massive shock wave that shattered nearby windows.
The scandal sent a shock wave through the entire organization.

  • synonym – pressure wave: a wave in which the disturbance is a variation in pressure
