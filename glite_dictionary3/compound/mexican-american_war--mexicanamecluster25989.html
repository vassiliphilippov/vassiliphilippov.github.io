Mexican-American War

noun
war between the U.S. and Mexico, 1846–1848
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛksɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən wɔr / (AmE), / ˌmɛksɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən wɔː / (BrE)
the war fought between the United States and Mexico from 1846 to 1848, resulting in the U.S. acquisition of large territories in the modern American Southwest

Examples

the Mexican-American War began
After the Mexican-American War, the United States acquired present-day California and New Mexico.
Many historians study the causes and consequences of the Mexican-American War in their research.
The Mexican-American War greatly expanded the territory of the United States in the nineteenth century.
