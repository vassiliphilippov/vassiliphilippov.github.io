zinc white

noun
white pigment made of zinc oxide
Pronunciation: / zɪŋk waɪt / (AmE), / zɪŋk waɪt / (BrE)
a white pigment made of zinc oxide, commonly used in paints and art materials as an alternative to lead white

Examples

zinc white paint pigment
A touch of zinc white brightened the entire painting without altering its texture.
House paints containing zinc white are often chosen for their durability and brightness.
The artist preferred using zinc white instead of lead-based pigments for safety reasons.
