subcutaneous injection

noun
injection administered under the skin
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbkjuˈteɪniəs ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbkjuːˈteɪnɪəs ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
an injection of medication under the skin, usually into the layer of fat and tissue just beneath the surface

Examples

subcutaneous injection of insulin
Some vaccines are administered by subcutaneous injection rather than by mouth.
The nurse demonstrated how to give a subcutaneous injection for insulin.
You may experience mild pain or redness at the site of the subcutaneous injection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intramuscular injection: an injection given directly into a muscle
  • antonym – intravenous injection: an injection given directly into a vein
