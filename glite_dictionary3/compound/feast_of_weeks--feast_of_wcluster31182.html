Feast of Weeks

noun
Jewish holy day commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments
Pronunciation: / fiːst əv wiːks / (AmE), / fiːst əv wiːks / (BrE)
A Jewish holy day celebrated on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating Moses receiving the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai; also called Shavuot.

Examples

Feast of Weeks celebration
Every year, Jewish families gather to celebrate the Feast of Weeks with prayers and festive meals.
Many people bring offerings of the first fruits to the synagogue during the Feast of Weeks.
The Feast of Weeks marks the anniversary of the giving of the Ten Commandments at Mount Sinai.
