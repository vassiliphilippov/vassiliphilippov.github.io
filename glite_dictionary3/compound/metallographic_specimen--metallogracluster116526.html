metallographic specimen

noun
prepared metal sample for structural analysis
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtələˈɡræfɪk ˈspɛsəmən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtələˈɡræfɪk ˈspɛsɪmɪn / (BrE)
a sample of metal that has been carefully prepared, usually by grinding, polishing, and etching, for microscopic analysis of its structure

Examples

metallographic specimen for analysis
Each metallographic specimen must be etched before microscopic examination to reveal its grain structure.
Researchers compared several metallographic specimens to assess the impact of different heat treatments.
The lab technician prepared the metallographic specimen by grinding and polishing the steel sample.
