radial nerve

noun
major nerve in the arm
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the largest branch of the brachial plexus, running down the arm and supplying the extensor muscles and skin on the back of the hand

Examples

radial nerve injury
Doctors examined the patient's radial nerve function after the surgery.
The radial nerve can be injured during a fracture of the humerus.
Weakness in the wrist and fingers may result from radial nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – median nerve: major nerve of the upper limb running down the center of the arm
