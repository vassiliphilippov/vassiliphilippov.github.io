terrestrial planet

noun
planet with a rocky surface (e.g. Earth, Mars)
Pronunciation: / təˈrɛstriəl ˈplænɪt / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəl ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
A planet with a solid, rocky surface, similar to Earth, such as Mercury, Venus, Earth, or Mars.

Examples

rocky terrestrial planet
Mercury is the smallest terrestrial planet in our solar system.
Scientists believe that water once flowed on the surface of Mars, another terrestrial planet.
The Earth is classified as a terrestrial planet because of its rocky surface.
