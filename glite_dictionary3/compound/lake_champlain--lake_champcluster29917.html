Lake Champlain

proper noun
lake between New York, Vermont, and Quebec
Pronunciation: / leɪk tʃæmˈpleɪn / (AmE), / leɪk tʃæmˈpleɪn / (BrE)
a large lake situated between northeastern New York, northwestern Vermont, and southern Quebec, known for its historical significance in several major wars

Examples

Lake Champlain borders Vermont
During the War of 1812, several key naval battles were fought on Lake Champlain.
Many communities along the shores of Lake Champlain depend on the lake for tourism and recreation.
The scenic beauty of Lake Champlain attracts thousands of visitors each year.
