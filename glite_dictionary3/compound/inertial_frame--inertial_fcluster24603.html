inertial frame

noun
reference frame where Newton's first law holds
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɝːʃəl freɪm / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːʃəl freɪm / (BrE)
a frame of reference in which an object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by a force, consistent with Newton's first law of motion

Examples

inertial frame of reference
An accelerating train is not considered an inertial frame because objects appear to move without a visible force.
Observers in an inertial frame see a ball in motion continue unless acted upon by another force.
The laws of physics take a simpler form in an inertial frame.

Related Terms

  • antonym – non-inertial frame: a reference frame in which Newton's first law does not hold, typically accelerating
