tied up

adjective
busy or engaged; unavailable
Pronunciation: / taɪd ʌp / (AmE), / taɪd ʌp / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) occupied or engaged and therefore unable to do something else.

Examples

tied up with work
I'm sorry, but I'm tied up with meetings all afternoon.
She asked to reschedule because she would be tied up with other commitments.
The manager was tied up and couldn't take my call right away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – busy: having a lot to do; occupied
  • antonym – free: not occupied; available
