top-down

adjective
approach starting from general to detail
Pronunciation: / tɑp ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / tɒp ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
(of an approach) starting from a general overview or the highest conceptual level and moving towards detailed parts.

Examples

top-down approach
A top-down approach can help clarify overall objectives before addressing specifics.
Research in cognitive science often contrasts top-down and bottom-up processing.
The company implemented a top-down strategy to restructure its departments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bottom-up: (of an approach) starting at the detail level and working towards a general overview
