good ole boy

noun
Southern US white male loyal to his peers
Pronunciation: / ˌɡʊd oʊl ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌɡʊd əʊl ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
(US, informal, sometimes derogatory) A white male Southerner who is sociable and unpretentious but often holds conservative or intolerant views, showing strong loyalty to his peers.

Examples

a good ole boy politician
Despite his friendly persona, the senator's reputation as a good ole boy made some voters uneasy.
The club was run by a group of good ole boys who had been friends since childhood.
The new sheriff was just another good ole boy who grew up in the county.

Related Terms

  • antonym – outsider: a person who does not belong to a particular group
  • synonym – redneck: an uneducated white farmer or laborer in the southern US, often regarded as being unsophisticated or narrow-minded
