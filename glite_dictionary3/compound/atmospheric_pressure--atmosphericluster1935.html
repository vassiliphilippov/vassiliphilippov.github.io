atmospheric pressure

noun
pressure exerted by the atmosphere
Pronunciation: / ˌætməsˈfɛrɪk ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˌatməsˈfɛrɪk ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by the weight of the atmosphere on the Earth's surface or on objects within it

Examples

atmospheric pressure drops
At high altitudes, atmospheric pressure is significantly lower than at sea level.
Changes in atmospheric pressure can affect how you feel during the day.
The barometer showed a drop in atmospheric pressure before the storm arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym – barometric pressure: pressure measured by a barometer, often equivalent to atmospheric pressure
