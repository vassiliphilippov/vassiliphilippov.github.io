Darwinical approach

noun
strategy based on survival of the fittest
Pronunciation: / dɑrˈwɪnɪkəl əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / dɑːˈwɪnɪkəl əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a method or strategy that emphasizes competition and the survival of the fittest, often inspired by Darwin's theory of natural selection

Examples

a Darwinical approach to business
In politics, a Darwinical approach often results in harsh competition and little cooperation.
Some critics argue that a Darwinical approach to business can undermine teamwork and morale.
The company adopted a Darwinical approach to management, promoting only those who outperform their peers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Darwinism: the theory of evolution by natural selection as proposed by Charles Darwin
