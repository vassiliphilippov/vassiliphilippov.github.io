anterior horn

noun
front section of spinal cord gray matter
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪriər hɔrn / (AmE), / anˈtɪəriə hɔːn / (BrE)
the front section of the gray matter in the spinal cord, containing motor neurons whose axons form the ventral roots of spinal nerves

Examples

anterior horn of the spinal cord
Damage to the anterior horn of the spinal cord can result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Poliomyelitis primarily affects the nerve cells in the anterior horn.
The doctor explained that the anterior horn contains motor neurons responsible for voluntary muscle movement.
