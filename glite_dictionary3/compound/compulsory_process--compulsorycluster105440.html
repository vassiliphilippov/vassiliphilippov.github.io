compulsory process

noun
legal right to compel witness attendance in court
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpʌlsəri ˈprɑːsɛs / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌlsəri ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
the legal right of a defendant to require the presence of witnesses in court, typically by having the court issue subpoenas

Examples

compulsory process for witnesses
Access to compulsory process is fundamental to a fair trial in many legal systems.
The defendant exercised his right to compulsory process to ensure key witnesses appeared in court.
Without compulsory process, the defense would have been unable to present critical testimony.
