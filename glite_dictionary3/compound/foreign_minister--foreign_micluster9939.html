foreign minister

noun
government official for foreign affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrən ˈmɪnɪstər / (AmE), / ˈfɒrən ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
the government official in charge of a country's relations with other nations, typically responsible for diplomatic policy and international affairs

Examples

foreign minister of France
After years as ambassador, she was appointed foreign minister by the new administration.
The foreign minister stressed the importance of international cooperation during the conference.
The country's foreign minister met with diplomats to discuss the ongoing peace process.
