magnetic ink

noun
ink with magnetic particles for sensing
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ɪŋk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ɪŋk / (BrE)
ink containing particles of a magnetic substance, allowing its presence to be detected by magnetic sensors, especially in security printing and banking applications.

Examples

magnetic ink on checks
Banks often use magnetic ink to print account numbers on checks for automated processing.
Special printers are required to produce documents with magnetic ink.
The presence of magnetic ink makes it easier for machines to read security documents.
