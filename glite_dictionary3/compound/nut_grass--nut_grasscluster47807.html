nut grass

noun
a perennial sedge with edible tubers
Pronunciation: / nʌt ɡræs / (AmE), / nʌt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a widely distributed perennial sedge with slender grasslike leaves and small edible nutlike tubers, often considered a weed

Examples

nut grass spreads quickly
Nut grass quickly spreads in lawns and is notoriously difficult to control.
Some traditional dishes in Asia use the tubers of nut grass as an ingredient.
The gardener struggled to remove nut grass from the vegetable patch every spring.
