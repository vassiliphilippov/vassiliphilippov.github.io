bill of exchange

noun
written payment order (finance, legal)
Pronunciation: / bɪl əv ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / bɪl əv ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
A written order from one person or bank directing another to pay a specified sum of money to a third party at a set time.

Examples

international bill of exchange
A bill of exchange must be accepted by the drawee before it becomes legally binding.
She presented the bill of exchange at the bank to collect the funds owed to her client.
The company used a bill of exchange to guarantee payment to its overseas supplier.

Related Terms

  • synonym – promissory note: written promise to pay a certain amount of money to a specific person
