common carrier

noun
firm or person transporting people or goods for the public
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmən ˈkæriər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkæriə / (BrE)
a person or company that transports goods or passengers for the general public and is legally responsible for any loss or damage

Examples

common carrier transports goods
Airlines acting as common carriers must accept all reasonable requests to transport passengers.
The railway company operated as a common carrier, offering services to any member of the public.
Under the law, a common carrier has a duty to deliver goods safely to their destination.

Related Terms

  • antonym – private carrier: a carrier that transports under individual contracts, not for the public
