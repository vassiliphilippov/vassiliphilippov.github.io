phase change

noun
change of state of matter
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪz tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪz tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a change from one physical state (solid, liquid, or gas) to another without a change in chemical composition

Examples

phase change from solid to liquid
Some materials can store energy during a phase change, making them useful in thermal storage systems.
The melting of ice into water is a classic example of a phase change.
When water boils, it undergoes a phase change from liquid to gas.
