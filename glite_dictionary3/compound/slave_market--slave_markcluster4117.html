slave market

noun
marketplace for buying and selling slaves
Pronunciation: / sleɪv ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / sleɪv ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a place where enslaved people were bought and sold, particularly in the southern United States before the American Civil War

Examples

the slave market in town
Enslaved people were separated from their families at the slave market.
Historians have discovered records of transactions from the old slave market downtown.
The tragic history of the slave market still haunts the city today.
