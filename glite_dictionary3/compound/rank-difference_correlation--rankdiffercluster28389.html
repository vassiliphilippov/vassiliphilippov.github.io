rank-difference correlation

noun
statistical method for correlating ranks
Pronunciation: / ræŋk ˈdɪf(ə)r(ə)ns ˌkɔr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ræŋk ˈdɪf(ə)r(ə)ns ˌkɒr.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the statistical method used to compute a correlation coefficient between the ranks of scores on two variables, often to assess the relationship between them without assuming a linear relationship or interval-scaled data.

Examples

rank-difference correlation between scores
A strong rank-difference correlation was found between job performance and years of experience.
Researchers used a rank-difference correlation to analyze the association between students' test rankings in math and science.
The rank-difference correlation is useful when the data are ordinal rather than interval.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Spearman's rank correlation: a specific type of rank-difference correlation coefficient
