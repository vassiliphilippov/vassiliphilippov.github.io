dividend warrant

noun
order to pay a shareholder a dividend
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd ˈwɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official order or document instructing a payment of a dividend to a shareholder, typically in the form of a check or written order

Examples

dividend warrant payment received
If a dividend warrant is lost, the shareholder must request a replacement from the issuing company.
She received a dividend warrant in the mail, which she deposited into her bank account.
The company sent a dividend warrant to every eligible shareholder at the end of the quarter.
