mechanical press

noun
machine applying pressure for shaping or extracting
Pronunciation: / məˈkænɪkəl prɛs / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl prɛs / (BrE)
a machine that applies pressure to shape, cut, compress, or extract substances from materials, often using mechanical force

Examples

mechanical press shapes metal
The factory relies on a mechanical press to shape metal parts for cars.
The new mechanical press can handle larger loads than the previous model.
Workers used a mechanical press to extract juice from the fruit efficiently.
