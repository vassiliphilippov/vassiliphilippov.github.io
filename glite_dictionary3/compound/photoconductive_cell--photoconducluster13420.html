photoconductive cell

noun
light-sensitive electronic device
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtəʊˈkɑndʌktɪv sɛl / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊkənˈdʌktɪv sɪl / (BrE)
An electronic component that acts as a transducer, detecting and measuring light or other radiation by changing its electrical resistance.

Examples

photoconductive cell in street lights
A photoconductive cell is often used in street lights to automatically turn them on at dusk.
The camera's light meter relies on a photoconductive cell to measure ambient light accurately.
When exposed to sunlight, the resistance of the photoconductive cell drops significantly, triggering the control system.

Related Terms

  • synonym – photocell: a device that detects light and converts it to an electrical signal
