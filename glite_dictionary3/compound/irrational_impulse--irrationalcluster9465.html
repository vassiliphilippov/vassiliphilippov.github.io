irrational impulse

noun
sudden illogical motivation
Pronunciation: / ɪˈræʃənl ɪmˈpʌls / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənl ɪmˈpʌls / (BrE)
a sudden and strong motivation to act without logical reasoning or clear justification

Examples

sudden irrational impulse
Advertising agencies are skilled at triggering our irrational impulses as consumers.
He often acted on an irrational impulse, buying things he didn't need.
The sudden urge to shout was clearly an irrational impulse she struggled to control.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compulsion: an irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, often against one's wishes
  • antonym – rational impulse: a motivation to act based on logical reasoning
