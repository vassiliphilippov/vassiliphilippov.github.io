sand bar

noun
narrow ridge of sand in water
Pronunciation: / ˈsænd ˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsænd ˌbɑː / (BrE)
A long, narrow deposit of sand formed by currents in a river or sea, often visible above the surface of the water.

Examples

walk on the sand bar
A colony of birds nested on the sand bar that formed at low tide.
Boats cannot pass through that channel because a sand bar blocks the way.
Waves crashed against the sand bar just beyond the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sandbank: a raised area of sand in a river or sea, similar to a sand bar
