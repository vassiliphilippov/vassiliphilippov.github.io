night-line

noun
fishing line left overnight with hooks
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A fishing line set with baited hooks and left in the water overnight to catch fish.

Examples

set a night-line
Local laws require you to mark your night-line with a visible flag.
The fishermen set a night-line each evening and checked it early in the morning.
Using a night-line allowed them to catch more fish while they slept.
