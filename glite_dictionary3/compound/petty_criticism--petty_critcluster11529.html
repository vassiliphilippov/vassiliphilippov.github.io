petty criticism

noun
trivial or unimportant criticism
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛti ˈkrɪtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɛti ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
criticism that is trivial or concerned with unimportant matters, often regarded as insignificant or unnecessarily negative.

Examples

petty criticism of mistakes
Her report was full of petty criticism that added no real value to the discussion.
Instead of offering solutions, he focused on petty criticism of his coworker's presentation style.
Tired of the constant petty criticism, she decided to ignore minor complaints and move forward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – constructive criticism: criticism intended to help improve
  • synonym – nitpicking: the act of paying too much attention to insignificant details
