rhomboid minor muscle

noun
upper back muscle connecting scapula and spine
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːm.bɔɪd ˈmaɪ.nɚ ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈrɒm.bɔɪd ˈmaɪ.nə ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a muscle of the upper back that connects the scapula with the spine, responsible for drawing the scapula toward the vertebral column and slightly upward

Examples

rhomboid minor muscle injury
An injury to the rhomboid minor muscle can cause pain between the spine and the shoulder blade.
Physical therapists often focus on strengthening the rhomboid minor muscle to improve upper back posture.
The rhomboid minor muscle plays a key role in retracting the shoulder blades during rowing exercises.
