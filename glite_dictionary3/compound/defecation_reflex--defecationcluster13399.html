defecation reflex

noun
involuntary urge to expel feces
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛfəˈkeɪʃən ˈriːflɛks / (AmE), / ˌdɛfɪˈkeɪʃən ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
the normal involuntary response that stimulates the urge to expel feces when they are present in the rectum

Examples

defecation reflex triggers urge
In some medical conditions, the defecation reflex may be weakened or absent.
The defecation reflex is triggered when the rectum becomes distended with feces.
Training young children often involves teaching them to recognize the defecation reflex.
