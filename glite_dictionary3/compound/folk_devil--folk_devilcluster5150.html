folk devil

noun
a group or person blamed for social problems
Pronunciation: / foʊk ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
a person or group that is portrayed as a threat to societal values and blamed for social problems, often in an exaggerated or unjust manner

Examples

media folk devil
Moral panics often result in the creation of a folk devil to focus public anger.
Social media can quickly turn someone into a folk devil in the eyes of the public.
The press made him out to be a folk devil responsible for the town's troubles.
