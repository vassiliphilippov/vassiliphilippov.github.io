thymus gland

noun
gland in neck crucial for immunity
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪ.məs ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.məs ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
A gland located at the base of the neck that produces lymphocytes and is important in developing the body's immune system, especially during childhood.

Examples

the thymus gland produces cells
As people age, the thymus gland gradually shrinks and becomes less active.
Doctors studied the effects of disease on the thymus gland in laboratory animals.
The thymus gland plays a crucial role in developing a child's immune system.
