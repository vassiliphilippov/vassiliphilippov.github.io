malignant anaemia

noun
anemia caused by vitamin B12 absorption failure
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A chronic, progressive form of anemia in adults caused by the inability to absorb vitamin B12 due to lack of intrinsic factor in the stomach.

Examples

malignant anaemia diagnosis
Early detection of malignant anaemia can help prevent serious neurological complications.
Her doctor diagnosed her with malignant anaemia after noticing persistent fatigue and paleness.
The treatment for malignant anaemia typically involves regular vitamin B12 injections.
