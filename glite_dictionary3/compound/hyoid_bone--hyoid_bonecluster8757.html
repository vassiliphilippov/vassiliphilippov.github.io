hyoid bone

noun
U-shaped bone in the neck supporting the tongue
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌɔɪd ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪɔɪd bəʊn / (BrE)
a U-shaped bone in the neck at the base of the tongue that supports the tongue muscles

Examples

broken hyoid bone
Because the hyoid bone supports the tongue, injury to it may affect speech and swallowing.
In forensic science, a fractured hyoid bone can be an indicator of strangulation.
The hyoid bone is the only bone in the body not directly connected to any other bone.
