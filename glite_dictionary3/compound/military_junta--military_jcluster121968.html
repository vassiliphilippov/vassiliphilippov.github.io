military junta

noun
government ruled by military officers
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˈhʊntə / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ˈhʌntə / (BrE)
A group of military officers who seize control of a country and rule it, often after overthrowing the previous government.

Examples

military junta seizes power
Following the coup, a military junta suspended the constitution and dissolved parliament.
International sanctions were imposed on the military junta for human rights abuses.
The people protested against the military junta that had taken control of the country.
