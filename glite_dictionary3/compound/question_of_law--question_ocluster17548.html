question of law

noun
legal issue decided by a judge
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɛs.tʃən əv lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən əv lɔː / (BrE)
an issue concerning the interpretation or application of laws, typically decided by a judge rather than a jury

Examples

important question of law
The appeal was dismissed because it did not raise a substantial question of law.
The jury determines the facts, but the judge rules on any question of law.
Whether the contract is enforceable is a question of law for the court to decide.
