bacterial toxin

noun
poison produced by bacteria
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɪri.əl ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəri.əl ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous substance produced by bacteria, either released from the bacteria or contained within their cells

Examples

dangerous bacterial toxin
A new vaccine was developed to neutralize the bacterial toxin produced by the pathogen.
Some diseases are caused by a bacterial toxin rather than the bacteria themselves.
The scientists studied the effects of the bacterial toxin on human tissue.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – endotoxin: toxin present inside bacterial cells, released when cells disintegrate
  • degree higher – exotoxin: toxin secreted by bacteria into their environment
