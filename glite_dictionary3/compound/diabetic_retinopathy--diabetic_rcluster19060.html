diabetic retinopathy

noun
retinal damage caused by diabetes
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈbɛtɪk ˌrɛtɪˈnɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbiːtɪk ˌrɛtɪˈnɒpəθi / (BrE)
A medical condition in which chronic high blood sugar damages the small blood vessels of the retina, often causing vision problems or blindness in people with diabetes.

Examples

diabetic retinopathy causes blindness
Early stages of diabetic retinopathy may show no symptoms, so regular eye exams are important for diabetic patients.
His vision deteriorated due to diabetic retinopathy after years of poorly controlled diabetes.
Laser treatment can help slow the progression of diabetic retinopathy in many patients.
