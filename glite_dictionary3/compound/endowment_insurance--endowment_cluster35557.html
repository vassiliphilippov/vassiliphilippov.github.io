endowment insurance

noun
type of life insurance policy
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdaʊmənt ɪnˌʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt ɪnˌʃɔːrəns / (BrE)
Life insurance that pays a specified sum either to the insured if they survive a set period or to a beneficiary upon their death within that period.

Examples

endowment insurance policy matures
At the end of the policy term, the endowment insurance matured and paid out a lump sum.
Many parents buy endowment insurance as a way to save for their children's education.
She decided to purchase endowment insurance to provide financial security for her family.
