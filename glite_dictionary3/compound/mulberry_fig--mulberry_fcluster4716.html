mulberry fig

noun
African tree producing cluster of edible inferior figs
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌlˌbɛri fɪg / (AmE), / ˈmʌlbəri fɪg / (BrE)
A large, wide-spreading tree (Ficus sycomorus) native to Africa and southwestern Asia, often with buttressed trunks and clusters of edible but inferior figs; also known as the biblical sycamore.

Examples

mulberry fig tree shade
Ancient Egyptians sometimes planted the mulberry fig along irrigation canals for shade and food.
Clusters of mulberry figs can be seen growing directly from the branches near the trunk.
The mulberry fig is mentioned several times in biblical texts as the sycamore tree.
