optic disk

noun
point on retina where optic nerve enters
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪk dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk dɪsk / (BrE)
the point on the retina where the optic nerve enters the eye and which lacks light-sensitive cells

Examples

optic disk examination
During the eye exam, the doctor closely inspected the optic disk for signs of disease.
Swelling of the optic disk can indicate increased pressure inside the skull.
The optic disk is also known as the blind spot because it lacks photoreceptor cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blind spot: the area of the retina without photoreceptors, causing a gap in the visual field
