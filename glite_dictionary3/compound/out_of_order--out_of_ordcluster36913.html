out of order

adjective
not working; unavailable for use
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
(of a machine, device, or facility) not working; broken or temporarily unavailable for use.

Examples

out of order sign
A sign on the restroom door said it was out of order.
Please do not use the coffee machine; it is currently out of order.
The elevator was out of order for several hours yesterday.

Related Terms

  • synonym – broken: not functioning; damaged
  • antonym – in service: working and available for use
