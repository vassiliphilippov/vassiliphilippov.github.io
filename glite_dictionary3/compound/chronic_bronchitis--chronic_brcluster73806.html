chronic bronchitis

noun
long-term bronchitis with persistent cough
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑnɪk brɑŋˈkaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnɪk brɒŋˈkaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A long-term inflammation of the bronchi characterized by persistent cough with excessive mucus and airflow obstruction.

Examples

chronic bronchitis causes coughing
Effective treatment for chronic bronchitis often involves quitting smoking and using inhaled medications.
Long-term smokers are at high risk of developing chronic bronchitis due to prolonged irritation of their airways.
Symptoms of chronic bronchitis include a persistent cough that produces a lot of mucus.
