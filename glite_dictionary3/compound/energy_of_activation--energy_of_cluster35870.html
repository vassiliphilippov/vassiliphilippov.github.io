energy of activation

noun
minimum energy needed for a reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnɚdʒi əv ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi əv ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the minimum amount of energy required for atoms or molecules to undergo a specific process, especially a chemical reaction.

Examples

energy of activation for a reaction
Catalysts help lower the energy of activation in many chemical reactions.
The energy of activation for this process is relatively high, making the reaction slow at room temperature.
Understanding the energy of activation allows scientists to design more efficient industrial processes.
