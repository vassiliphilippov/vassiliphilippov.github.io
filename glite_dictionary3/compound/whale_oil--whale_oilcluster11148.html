whale oil

noun
oil obtained from whale blubber, used historically
Pronunciation: / weɪl ɔɪl / (AmE), / weɪl ɔɪl / (BrE)
a pale yellow to brown oil extracted from the blubber of whales, once commonly used as fuel for lamps and in various industries

Examples

whale oil lamp fuel
In the nineteenth century, whale oil was widely used to light lamps in homes and streets.
Old lanterns often burned whale oil before kerosene became popular.
The decline in whale oil demand helped reduce commercial whaling.

  • synonym – blubber: the thick layer of fat beneath whales' skin
