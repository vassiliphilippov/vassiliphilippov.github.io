time series

noun
a sequence of data points over time
Pronunciation: / taɪm ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / taɪm ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
A series of values or data points of a variable, recorded or measured at successive points in time, often at uniform intervals.

Examples

time series data analysis
A time series can reveal trends and seasonal patterns in a dataset.
Researchers compiled a long time series from historical temperature records.
The economist analyzed the time series data to forecast next year's growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – data series: a set of related data points collected for analysis
