reed stop

noun
organ stop imitating reed instruments
Pronunciation: / rid stɑp / (AmE), / ɹiːd stɒp / (BrE)
an organ stop designed to imitate the sound of a reed instrument, such as an oboe or clarinet, using vibrating brass reeds.

Examples

reed stop on organ
A skilled organ builder can carefully voice each reed stop to match the acoustics of the church.
Many composers specify the use of the reed stop in their organ pieces for its distinctive sound.
The cathedral organist added a reed stop to give the music a brighter timbre.

Related Terms

  • antonym – flue stop: an organ stop using air columns, not reeds, to produce sound
  • synonym – organ stop: a component on an organ controlling a set of pipes for a specific timbre
