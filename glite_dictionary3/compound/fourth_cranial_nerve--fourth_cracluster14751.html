fourth cranial nerve

noun
cranial nerve controlling eye muscle
Pronunciation: / fɔrθ ˈkreɪniəl nɝv / (AmE), / fɔːθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
either of the pair of cranial nerves that control the superior oblique muscles of the eyes, allowing for certain eye movements

Examples

fourth cranial nerve injury
Because the fourth cranial nerve is so slender, it is particularly susceptible to trauma.
Damage to the fourth cranial nerve can cause double vision and difficulty looking downward.
The ophthalmologist suspected an issue with the patient's fourth cranial nerve after the eye exam.
