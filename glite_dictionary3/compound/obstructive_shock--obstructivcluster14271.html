obstructive shock

noun
shock caused by blocked blood flow
Pronunciation: / əbˈstrʌktɪv ʃɑk / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktɪv ʃɒk / (BrE)
a form of shock resulting from physical obstruction of blood flow in the circulatory system, leading to inadequate tissue perfusion

Examples

severe obstructive shock
Doctors must act quickly to identify the cause of obstructive shock to save the patient's life.
Pulmonary embolism can lead to obstructive shock if not treated promptly.
Tension pneumothorax is a medical emergency that may result in obstructive shock.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cardiogenic shock: shock caused by failure of the heart to pump blood effectively
  • antonym – hypovolemic shock: shock resulting from low blood volume
