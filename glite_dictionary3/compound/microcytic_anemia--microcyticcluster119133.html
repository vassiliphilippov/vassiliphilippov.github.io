microcytic anemia

noun
anemia with abnormally small red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈsɪt.ɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəʊˈsɪt.ɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A form of anemia in which the red blood cells (erythrocytes) are smaller than normal.

Examples

microcytic anemia from iron deficiency
Iron deficiency is the most common cause of microcytic anemia worldwide.
Patients with microcytic anemia may experience fatigue and weakness due to reduced oxygen delivery.
The doctor diagnosed her with microcytic anemia after reviewing the blood test results.
