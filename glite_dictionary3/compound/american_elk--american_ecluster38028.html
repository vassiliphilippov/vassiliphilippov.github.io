American elk

noun
large North American deer with antlers
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ɛlk / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ɛlk / (BrE)
A large North American deer (Cervus canadensis), the male of which has large, branching antlers.

Examples

American elk herd
During our hike in the Rockies, we saw a herd of American elk grazing in a clearing.
Photographers visit the national park each autumn to capture images of American elk during the rut.
The male American elk sheds its antlers every winter and regrows them in the spring.
