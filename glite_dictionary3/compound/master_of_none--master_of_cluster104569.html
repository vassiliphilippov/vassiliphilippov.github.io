master of none

noun
person not expert in any skill
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstɚ əv nʌn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə əv nʌn / (BrE)
a person who is competent in many areas but does not excel in any particular one

Examples

master of none at work
Despite knowing a bit about everything at the company, he was a master of none and struggled with specialist tasks.
She describes herself as a master of none, always dabbling in new hobbies but never perfecting one.
The phrase 'jack of all trades, master of none' applies well to generalists in many fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jack of all trades: a person who can do many different jobs
