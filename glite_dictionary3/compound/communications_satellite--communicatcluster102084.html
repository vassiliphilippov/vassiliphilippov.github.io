communications satellite

noun
artificial satellite relaying signals
Pronunciation: / kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃənz ˈsæt̬əlˌaɪt / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃənz ˈsætəlaɪt / (BrE)
an artificial satellite in geostationary orbit used to relay signals for television, radio, telephone, or internet communication back to Earth

Examples

launch a communications satellite
A communications satellite was launched to improve internet access across rural regions.
Maintenance crews tracked the movement of every communications satellite to prevent signal interference.
Signals bounced off a communications satellite allow television broadcasts to reach remote areas.
