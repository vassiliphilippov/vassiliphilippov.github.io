white dead nettle

noun
European plant with white flowers, mint family
Pronunciation: / waɪt dɛd ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / waɪt dɛd ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
A European plant of the mint family with white flowers, resembling true nettles but lacking stinging hairs.

Examples

flowering white dead nettle
Bees are attracted to the white dead nettle because of its nectar-rich flowers.
The white dead nettle is often found growing along hedgerows and roadsides in Europe.
Unlike true nettles, the white dead nettle does not sting when touched.
