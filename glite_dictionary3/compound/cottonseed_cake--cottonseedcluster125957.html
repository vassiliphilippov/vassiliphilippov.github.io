cottonseed cake

noun
residue after extracting cottonseed oil
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtnˌsid keɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌsiːd keɪk / (BrE)
the solid residue remaining after oil has been extracted from cottonseeds, used mainly as animal feed or fertilizer

Examples

cottonseed cake for cattle
After harvesting cotton, the cottonseed cake is sold to agricultural suppliers.
Farmers often use cottonseed cake as a high-protein supplement in cattle feed.
The factory produced tons of cottonseed cake after the oil extraction process.
