Battle of the Boyne

proper noun
1690 battle in Ireland between William III and James II
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ðə ˈbɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ðə ˈbɔɪn / (BrE)
a decisive battle fought in Ireland in 1690, where the forces of William III defeated those of the deposed King James II, ending attempts to restore Catholicism in England by the Stuarts

Examples

the Battle of the Boyne in 1690
Historians often discuss the political consequences of the Battle of the Boyne for Irish society.
In 1690, William III's victory at the Battle of the Boyne marked a turning point in British and Irish history.
The anniversary of the Battle of the Boyne is still celebrated by some in Northern Ireland.
