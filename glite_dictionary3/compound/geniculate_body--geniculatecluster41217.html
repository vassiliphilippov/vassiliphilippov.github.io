geniculate body

noun
part of the thalamus relaying sensory signals
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɪkjələt ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪkjʊlət ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
each of a pair of small oval masses on the underside of the thalamus that serve as synaptic centers relaying sensory signals to the cerebral cortex

Examples

the geniculate body in the brain
Damage to the geniculate body can affect sensory perception in the brain.
