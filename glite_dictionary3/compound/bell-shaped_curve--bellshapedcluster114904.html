bell-shaped curve

noun
normal distribution curve (statistics)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛl ʃeɪpt kɜrv / (AmE), / ˈbɛl ʃeɪpt kɜːv / (BrE)
A symmetrical, bell-like curve representing the normal distribution in statistics, with most values clustering around a central mean.

Examples

bell-shaped curve in statistics
Exam scores are often distributed in a bell-shaped curve around the average.
If the points form a bell-shaped curve, the results can be considered statistically normal.
The data followed a classic bell-shaped curve, indicating a normal distribution.
