to hear from the other side

verb
receive input from an opposing viewpoint
Pronunciation: / hɪr frəm ði ˈʌðɚ saɪd / (AmE), / hɪə frəm ði ˈʌðə saɪd / (BrE)
to receive input or opinions from an opposing or alternate viewpoint, especially before making a decision

Examples

to hear from the other side before deciding
As a fair judge, it's important to hear from the other side before making any decisions.
In the meeting, each group was allowed to hear from the other side to ensure balance.
Let's not argue any further until we hear from the other side about their perspective.
