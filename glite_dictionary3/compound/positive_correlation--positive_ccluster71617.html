positive correlation

noun
direct statistical relationship between two variables
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzɪtɪv ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪtɪv ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between two variables in which they both increase or decrease together, so that high values of one tend to be associated with high values of the other

Examples

positive correlation between variables
A strong positive correlation was observed between temperature and ice cream sales.
Researchers found a positive correlation between years of education and income levels.
There is a positive correlation between exercise frequency and overall health.

Related Terms

  • synonym – correlation: a mutual relationship between two or more things
  • antonym – negative correlation: a relationship where one variable increases as the other decreases
