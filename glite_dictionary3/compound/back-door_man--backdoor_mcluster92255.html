back-door man

noun
person acting secretly or unofficially
Pronunciation: / ˌbækˈdɔr ˌmæn / (AmE), / ˌbækˈdɔː ˌmæn / (BrE)
a person who operates secretly or unofficially, often managing or influencing activities from behind the scenes

Examples

a back-door man in business
He acted as the back-door man, arranging deals that were never made public.
Rumors suggested that a back-door man had influenced the board's surprising decision.
The company's success was partly due to a back-door man who kept things moving smoothly.
