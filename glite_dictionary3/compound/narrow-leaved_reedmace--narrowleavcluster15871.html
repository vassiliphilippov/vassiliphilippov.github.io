narrow-leaved reedmace

noun
a wetland plant with narrow leaves and brown spike
Pronunciation: / ˌnæroʊ livd ˈridˌmæs / (AmE), / ˌnærəʊ liːvd ˈriːdmæs / (BrE)
a tall water plant with narrow leaves and a characteristic cylindrical brown flower spike, found in wetlands of America, Europe, North Africa, and Asia; also known as Typha angustifolia.
