judicial torture

noun
state-sanctioned torture by officials
Pronunciation: / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈtɔrtʃər / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəl ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
torture that is officially authorized by the state and carried out by accredited officials, especially as part of judicial proceedings or legal investigations, historically used to extract confessions or information.

Examples

use judicial torture in trials
Documentation reveals that judicial torture was sometimes used to extract confessions during witch trials.
Historians debate when judicial torture was first officially abolished in France.
The use of judicial torture was once common in European legal systems.
