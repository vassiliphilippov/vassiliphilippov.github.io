Malay Peninsula

proper noun
peninsula in Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Thailand, Myanmar)
Pronunciation: / məˈleɪ pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ pəˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
a long peninsula in Southeast Asia, occupied by parts of Malaysia, Thailand, and Myanmar, forming a natural boundary between the South China Sea and the Andaman Sea

Examples

the Malay Peninsula in Southeast Asia
Singapore is located at the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula.
The Malay Peninsula has been a vital trade route for centuries due to its strategic location.
The Malay Peninsula is known for its tropical rainforests and diverse wildlife.
