Dominican order

noun
Roman Catholic religious order of preachers
Pronunciation: / dəˌmɪnɪkən ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dəˌmɪnɪkən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order of mendicant preachers founded by Saint Dominic in the early 13th century, noted for scholarship and missionary work.

Examples

the Dominican order of friars
Members of the Dominican order are often recognized by their distinctive white habits.
She studied the contributions of the Dominican order to European philosophy and education.
The Dominican order played a crucial role in the spread of Christianity during the Middle Ages.
