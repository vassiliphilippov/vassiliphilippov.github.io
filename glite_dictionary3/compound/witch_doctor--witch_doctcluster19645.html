witch doctor

noun
traditional healer using magic and rituals
Pronunciation: / wɪtʃ ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
a person in some societies who is believed to have magical powers of healing and uses traditional remedies and rituals to cure illness

Examples

witch doctor heals villagers
Anthropologists studied the role of the witch doctor in indigenous communities.
In some cultures, a witch doctor is trusted to heal both the body and the spirit.
The villagers consulted a witch doctor when modern medicine failed to cure their illnesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shaman: a person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of spirits, often a traditional healer
