retail price index

noun
measure of changes in consumer prices
Pronunciation: / ˈriːteɪl ˈpraɪs ˌɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl ˈpraɪs ˌɪndɛks / (BrE)
an official measure of the average change in prices of goods and services bought by consumers over time

Examples

If the retail price index rises sharply, the cost of living typically increases.
