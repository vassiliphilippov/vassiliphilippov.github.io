nickel-cadmium accumulator

noun
rechargeable battery of nickel and cadmium
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪkəl ˈkædmiəm əˌkjuːmjəˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ˈkædmiəm əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A rechargeable battery with a nickel cathode and a cadmium anode, commonly used in emergency systems for its low self-discharge rate.

Examples

nickel-cadmium accumulator in emergency lighting
Due to environmental concerns, disposing of a nickel-cadmium accumulator must follow special procedures.
Many older cordless tools use a nickel-cadmium accumulator because of its durability.
The emergency lighting system relies on a nickel-cadmium accumulator for backup power.
