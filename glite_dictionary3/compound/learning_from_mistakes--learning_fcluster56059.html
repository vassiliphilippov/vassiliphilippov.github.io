learning from mistakes

noun
acquiring knowledge through correcting errors
Pronunciation: / ˈlɝːnɪŋ frəm mɪˈsteɪks / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ frəm mɪˈsteɪks / (BrE)
the process of acquiring knowledge, skills, or understanding by reflecting on and correcting one's errors

Examples

learning from mistakes after failure
Many great inventions were achieved by learning from mistakes instead of giving up after failure.
She believes that learning from mistakes is an important part of education and personal growth.
Successful athletes often attribute their achievements to learning from mistakes made in previous competitions.
