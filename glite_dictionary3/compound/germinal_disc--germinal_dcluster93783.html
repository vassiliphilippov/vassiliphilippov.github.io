germinal disc

noun
region of cells giving rise to the embryo
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɝː.mə.nəl dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.mɪ.nəl dɪsk / (BrE)
a small region of cells on the surface of an ovum or blastula from which the embryo develops

Examples

germinal disc in an egg
If the germinal disc is fertilized, it will give rise to a chick embryo.
Scientists studied changes in the germinal disc during early embryonic development.
The germinal disc can be seen as a small white spot on the surface of a bird's egg yolk.
