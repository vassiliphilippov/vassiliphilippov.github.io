national insurance

noun
British state-run social insurance system
Pronunciation: / ˌnæʃənl ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌnæʃnəl ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
A system of compulsory payments made by employees and employers in Britain to provide state benefits for people who are unemployed, sick, retired, or in need of medical services.

Examples

national insurance contributions
All employees in the UK are required to pay national insurance contributions from their earnings.
Retirement pensions in Britain are partly funded through national insurance payments.
She checked her national insurance number before filling out the benefits application form.
