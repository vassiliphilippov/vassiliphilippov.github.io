rank-order correlation

noun
statistical measure for comparing ranked lists
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋk ˌɔrdər kəˌrɛˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈræŋk ˌɔːdə kɒˌrɛˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical method for measuring how closely the orderings of two sets of ranked data are related

Examples

rank-order correlation between scores
A strong rank-order correlation suggests that higher values in one list correspond to higher values in another.
Researchers used a rank-order correlation to determine whether the two rankings matched.
The rank-order correlation between test scores and final grades was remarkably high.
