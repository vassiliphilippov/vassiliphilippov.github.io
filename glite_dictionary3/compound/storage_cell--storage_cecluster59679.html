storage cell

noun
a rechargeable electrical cell
Pronunciation: / ˈstɔrɪdʒ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪdʒ sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that can be recharged by passing an electric current through it, used to store electrical energy

Examples

storage cell in a battery
Nickel-cadmium is a common type of storage cell found in portable electronics.
The solar panel charges the storage cell during the day for use at night.
When the storage cell is depleted, you can simply recharge it.

  • antonym – primary cell: an electrical cell that cannot be recharged
