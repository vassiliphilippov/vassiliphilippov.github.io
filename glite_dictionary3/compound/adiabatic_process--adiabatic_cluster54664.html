adiabatic process

noun
thermodynamic process without heat transfer
Pronunciation: / ˌædiəˈbætɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌadɪəˈbatɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
(Physics) A thermodynamic process in which no heat is transferred to or from the system by its surroundings.

Examples

adiabatic process in gases
In an adiabatic process, a gas expands without gaining or losing heat from its environment.
Rapid compression of air, such as in a bicycle pump, can be approximated as an adiabatic process.
The concept of the adiabatic process is important in the study of thermodynamics.

Related Terms

  • antonym – isothermal process: thermodynamic process occurring at constant temperature
