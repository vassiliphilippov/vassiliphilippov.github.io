endothermic reaction

noun
a reaction that absorbs heat
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.doʊˈθɜːr.mɪk riˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəˈθɜː.mɪk rɪˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction that absorbs heat from its surroundings

Examples

an endothermic reaction absorbs heat
If the temperature decreases during a chemical change, the process is likely an endothermic reaction.
Mixing barium hydroxide with ammonium chloride is a classic laboratory demonstration of an endothermic reaction.
Photosynthesis in plants is an example of an endothermic reaction because it requires energy from sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – exothermic reaction: a reaction that releases heat
