double saucepan

noun
two saucepans fitted for gentle heating
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl ˈsɔːs.pæn / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈsɔːs.pæn / (BrE)
A set of two saucepans, one fitting inside the other, used to heat food gently by placing it over boiling water in the lower pan.

Examples

double saucepan for melting chocolate
A double saucepan is ideal for making delicate sauces like hollandaise.
Melt the chocolate in a double saucepan to prevent it from burning.
She placed the pudding bowl over simmering water in a double saucepan.
