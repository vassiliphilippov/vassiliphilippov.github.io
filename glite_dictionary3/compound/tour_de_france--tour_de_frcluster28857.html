Tour de France

proper noun
annual French professional cycling race
Pronunciation: / tuːr də ˈfræns / (AmE), / tuə də ˈfrɑːns / (BrE)
An annual multi-stage bicycle race primarily held in France, lasting three weeks and covering around 3,000 miles, and considered the most prestigious event in professional cycling.

Examples

win the Tour de France
He trained for years before qualifying for the Tour de France in 2022.
The Tour de France attracts millions of spectators from around the world every summer.
Winning the Tour de France is the ultimate goal for many professional cyclists.
