stem cell

noun
undifferentiated cell with self-renewal ability
Pronunciation: / stɛm sɛl / (AmE), / stɛm sɛl / (BrE)
An undifferentiated cell that can develop into different types of specialized cells and has the ability to self-renew.

Examples

human stem cell research
A single stem cell has the potential to give rise to many different cell types in the body.
Scientists are researching how stem cells could be used to treat diseases like Parkinson's.
The bone marrow transplant involved harvesting stem cells from the patient.
