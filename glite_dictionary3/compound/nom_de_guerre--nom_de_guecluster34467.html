nom de guerre

noun
assumed name used in a specific role
Pronunciation: / nɑm də ˈɡɛr / (AmE), / nɒm də ˈɡɛə / (BrE)
A fictitious name used by someone, especially to conceal their identity when performing a particular social role or activity.

Examples

nom de guerre for a writer
He wrote under the nom de guerre 'Martin Gray' during the resistance.
Many revolutionaries adopted a nom de guerre to protect their real identities.
The artist became famous for his provocative works, all signed with a mysterious nom de guerre.
