aplastic anemia

noun
failure of bone marrow to produce blood cells
Pronunciation: / əˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A serious condition in which the bone marrow fails to produce enough blood cells, leading to a deficiency of red cells, white cells, and platelets.

Examples

aplastic anemia diagnosis
Exposure to certain chemicals and radiation has been linked to cases of aplastic anemia.
People with aplastic anemia often require frequent blood transfusions to manage their condition.
The doctor diagnosed her with aplastic anemia after extensive blood tests and a bone marrow biopsy.
