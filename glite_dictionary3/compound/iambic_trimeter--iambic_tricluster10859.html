iambic trimeter

noun
three iambs per poetic line
Pronunciation: / aɪˌæmbɪk ˈtrɪˌmitər / (AmE), / aɪˌæmbɪk ˈtrɪmɪtə / (BrE)
A poetic meter in which each line consists of three iambic feet (an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable).

Examples

iambic trimeter in Greek plays
Many Greek tragedies are written in iambic trimeter for the spoken dialogue.
Students analyzed the structure of iambic trimeter during the literature class.
The poet experimented with iambic trimeter to create a brisk, rhythmic effect in his verses.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – iambic dimeter: a meter with two iambic feet per line
  • degree higher – iambic pentameter: a meter with five iambic feet per line
