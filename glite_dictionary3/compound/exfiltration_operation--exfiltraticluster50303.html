exfiltration operation

noun
covert rescue mission to remove someone from danger
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksˌfɪlˈtreɪʃən ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksˌfɪlˈtreɪʃən ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a covert action to secretly remove a defector, refugee, operative, or their family from a dangerous or hostile area

Examples

exfiltration operation saves people
After receiving credible threats, the diplomat and her family were saved by a swift exfiltration operation.
During the Cold War, numerous exfiltration operations were carried out to rescue defectors from behind enemy lines.
The agency launched an exfiltration operation to safely extract the undercover agent from hostile territory.
