nasolacrimal duct

noun
duct carrying tears into nose
Pronunciation: / ˌneɪzoʊˈlækrɪməl dʌkt / (AmE), / ˌneɪzəʊˈlækrɪməl dʌkt / (BrE)
a tube that carries tears from the lacrimal sac into the nasal cavity

Examples

nasolacrimal duct blockage
A blockage in the nasolacrimal duct can cause excessive tearing or recurrent eye infections.
Doctors sometimes irrigate the nasolacrimal duct to clear obstructions in infants.
Tears normally drain into the nose through the nasolacrimal duct.
