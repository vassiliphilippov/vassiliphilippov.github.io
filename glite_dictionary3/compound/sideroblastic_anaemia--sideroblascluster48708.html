sideroblastic anaemia

noun
type of anaemia with iron-laden cells
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪ.dɚ.əˈblæs.tɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɪd.ər.əˈblæs.tɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A refractory type of anaemia characterized by the presence of sideroblasts—abnormal red blood cell precursors containing excess iron—in the bone marrow.

Examples

sideroblastic anaemia diagnosis
Doctors often test for vitamin B6 deficiency when investigating sideroblastic anaemia.
The patient was diagnosed with sideroblastic anaemia after bone marrow tests revealed abnormal cells.
Treatment options for sideroblastic anaemia may depend on whether it is hereditary or acquired.
