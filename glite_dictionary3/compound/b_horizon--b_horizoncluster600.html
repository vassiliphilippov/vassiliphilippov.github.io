B horizon

noun
layer of soil below the A horizon
Pronunciation: / bi hʌˈraɪzən / (AmE), / bi həˈraɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
the soil layer lying beneath the A horizon, characterized by the accumulation of minerals and organic matter leached from the layers above

Examples

B horizon soil layer
Farmers study the B horizon to understand drainage and soil fertility.
Organic matter is sometimes transported from the topsoil down into the B horizon.
The B horizon often contains more clay than the layers above it.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – A horizon: the uppermost layer of soil, rich in organic material
  • degree lower – C horizon: the layer of soil beneath the B horizon, consisting mainly of parent material
