fiber optics

noun
light signal transmission via glass fibers
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɚ ˈɑptɪks / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə ˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
The technology or process of transmitting light signals through thin flexible glass or plastic fibers, used especially in telecommunications and medical instruments.

Examples

fiber optics internet cables
Many modern internet connections rely on fiber optics for their high-speed data transmission.
Thanks to fiber optics, international phone calls have become clearer and more reliable.
The hospital upgraded their equipment to use fiber optics for more precise imaging.
