to sidestep the controversy

verb
avoid addressing a controversial issue
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌstɛp ðə kənˈtrɒvɝsi / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌstɛp ðə kənˈtrɒvəsi / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid discussing or becoming involved in a controversial issue or topic

Examples

to sidestep the controversy in debate
Instead of providing a direct answer, the speaker sidestepped the controversy with a vague response.
Many politicians choose to sidestep the controversy during election campaigns to avoid alienating voters.
The CEO tried to sidestep the controversy by focusing on the company's future plans in his statement.
