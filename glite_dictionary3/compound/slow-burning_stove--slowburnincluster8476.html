slow-burning stove

noun
stove with controlled, slow fuel combustion
Pronunciation: / sloʊ ˈbɝː.nɪŋ stoʊv / (AmE), / sləʊ ˈbɜː.nɪŋ stəʊv / (BrE)
a stove designed to burn fuel slowly by controlling the air inlet, allowing for prolonged heat output and efficiency

Examples

slow-burning stove keeps heat
By adjusting the air inlet, the slow-burning stove can maintain a steady temperature for hours.
He bought a slow-burning stove to save on firewood and reduce trips outside in winter.
The old cabin was heated by a slow-burning stove that kept the rooms warm all night.
