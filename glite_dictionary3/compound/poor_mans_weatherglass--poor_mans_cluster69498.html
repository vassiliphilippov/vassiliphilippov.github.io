poor man's weatherglass

noun
herb whose flowers close before rain
Pronunciation: / pʊr mænz ˈwɛðɚˌɡlæs / (AmE), / pɔː mænz ˈwɛðəˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a herb (Anagallis arvensis) with scarlet, white, or purple flowers that close at the approach of rainy weather

Examples

poor man's weatherglass flowers close
Farmers used the poor man's weatherglass to predict rain before modern forecasts existed.
If the flowers of the poor man's weatherglass are closed, it means wet weather is coming.
The poor man's weatherglass is often found growing wild in country gardens.
