lobe-finned fish

noun
fish with fleshy, lobed fins
Pronunciation: / loʊb fɪnd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ləʊb fɪnd fɪʃ / (BrE)
any fish of the group Sarcopterygii, characterized by fleshy, lobed paired fins, including both living and extinct species, most known only from fossils.

Examples

fossil lobe-finned fish
Fossils of lobe-finned fish provide crucial evidence for understanding the evolution of land vertebrates.
Scientists were surprised to discover a lobe-finned fish alive off the coast of South Africa.
The coelacanth is a rare living example of a lobe-finned fish.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ray-finned fish: fish with fins supported by rays rather than fleshy lobes
