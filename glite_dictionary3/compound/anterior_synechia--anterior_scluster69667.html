anterior synechia

noun
adhesion of iris to cornea in the eye
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪriɚ sɪˈnikiə / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə sɪˈniːkɪə / (BrE)
an adhesion between the iris and the cornea of the eye, often caused by injury or inflammation

Examples

anterior synechia blocks vision
If left untreated, anterior synechia can lead to an increase in intraocular pressure.
Surgical intervention was required to correct the anterior synechia in her right eye.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed the patient with anterior synechia following a severe eye infection.
