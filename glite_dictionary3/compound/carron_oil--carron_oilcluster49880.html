carron oil

noun
former burn-treatment ointment
Pronunciation: / ˈkærən ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈkærən ɔɪl / (BrE)
an ointment, formerly used to treat burns, made from a mixture of linseed oil and limewater.

Examples

carron oil for burns
Although largely replaced today, carron oil was once stocked in every medical kit on ships.
In the nineteenth century, carron oil was a common remedy for treating severe scalds and burns.
The nurse quickly applied carron oil to the burn on his arm to soothe the pain.
