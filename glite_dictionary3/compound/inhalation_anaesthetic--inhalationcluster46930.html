inhalation anaesthetic

noun
gas that produces anesthesia when inhaled
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌheɪləˈʃən ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˌheɪləˈʃən ˌanɪsˈθiːtɪk / (BrE)
A substance in the form of a gas or vapor that produces general anesthesia when inhaled.

Examples

to give inhalation anaesthetic
Children undergoing surgery are often given an inhalation anaesthetic because it works quickly and is easy to control.
Most modern inhalation anaesthetics are administered through a mask or breathing tube.
The surgeon used an inhalation anaesthetic to ensure the patient felt no pain during the operation.

  • synonym – general anaesthetic: Drug that causes loss of consciousness and sensation
