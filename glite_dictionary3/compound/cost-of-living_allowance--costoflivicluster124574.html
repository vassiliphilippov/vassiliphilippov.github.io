cost-of-living allowance

noun
compensation for cost of living changes
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑst əv ˈlɪvɪŋ əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / ˌkɒst əv ˈlɪvɪŋ əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an allowance paid to employees to compensate for changes in the cost of living, as reflected by the consumer price index.

Examples

cost-of-living allowance for employees
Many expatriates are given a cost-of-living allowance when working abroad.
Some employees receive a cost-of-living allowance to help offset inflation in their city.
The contract includes a cost-of-living allowance that is adjusted annually.
