anaphoric relation

noun
relation between an anaphor and its antecedent
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈfɔrɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænəˈfɒrɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the relationship between an anaphor (such as a pronoun) and its antecedent in a sentence or discourse

Examples

anaphoric relation between words
In the sentence 'John lost his wallet; he is upset,' there is an anaphoric relation between 'he' and 'John'.
Linguists often study the anaphoric relation between pronouns and the nouns they refer to.
Understanding the anaphoric relation helps clarify ambiguous references in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anaphor: a word or phrase that refers back to another word or phrase
  • synonym – antecedent: the word, phrase, or clause to which an anaphor refers
