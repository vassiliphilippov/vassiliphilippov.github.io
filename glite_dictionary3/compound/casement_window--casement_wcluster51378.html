casement window

noun
window that opens on side hinges
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsmənt ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsmənt ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A window that is attached to its frame by one or more hinges at the side, typically opening outward like a door.

Examples

casement window opens outward
Because casement windows open outward, they are easy to clean from the inside.
The architect recommended casement windows to give the room a classic look.
We replaced our old sliding windows with modern casement windows for better ventilation.
