pressure suit

noun
inflatable protective suit for high altitude or space
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər suːt / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə suːt / (BrE)
a protective garment that can be inflated to maintain normal air pressure around the body, used by pilots and astronauts in high-altitude or space environments.

Examples

wear a pressure suit
Astronauts rely on their pressure suit to survive in the vacuum of space.
The development of the pressure suit was crucial for missions above the Earth's atmosphere.
The pilot wore a pressure suit during the high-altitude test flight.
