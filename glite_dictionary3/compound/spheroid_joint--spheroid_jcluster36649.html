spheroid joint

noun
type of freely moving anatomical joint
Pronunciation: / ˈsfiːrɔɪd ˌdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsfiːrɔɪd ˌdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
an anatomical joint in which a spherical end of one bone fits into a rounded cavity of another, allowing free movement in all directions

Examples

spheroid joint in the hip
Damage to the hip's spheroid joint can cause significant loss of mobility.
Surgeons studied the movement of the spheroid joint to improve prosthetic designs.
The shoulder is a classic example of a spheroid joint in the human body.
