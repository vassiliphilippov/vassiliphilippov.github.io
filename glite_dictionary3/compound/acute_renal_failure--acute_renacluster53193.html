acute renal failure

noun
sudden severe loss of kidney function
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ˈriː.nəl ˈfeɪ.ljɚ / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˈriː.nəl ˈfeɪ.li.ə / (BrE)
A sudden and severe loss of kidney function, often due to injury, infection, or blockage in the urinary tract.

Examples

acute renal failure after surgery
Doctors treated her acute renal failure aggressively to restore kidney function as quickly as possible.
Severe dehydration can sometimes trigger acute renal failure in vulnerable individuals.
The patient was diagnosed with acute renal failure after a severe infection led to kidney complications.

Related Terms

  • antonym – chronic renal failure: long-term, gradual loss of kidney function
  • synonym – kidney failure: inability of the kidneys to function properly
