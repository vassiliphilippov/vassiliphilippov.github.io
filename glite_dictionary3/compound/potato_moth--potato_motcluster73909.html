potato moth

noun
moth whose larva is a potato crop pest
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪɾoʊ mɔθ / (AmE), / pəˈtəʊtəʊ mɒθ / (BrE)
A greyish-brown moth whose larva is known as the potato tuberworm, a pest of potato crops.

Examples

potato moth infestation
Farmers struggled to control the potato moth infestation in their fields last year.
Researchers are developing new methods to prevent potato moth outbreaks.
The larvae of the potato moth cause significant damage to stored potatoes.
