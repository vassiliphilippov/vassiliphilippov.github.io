vocal fold

noun
fold of mucous membrane in the larynx; produces sound
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊkəl foʊld / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəl fəʊld / (BrE)
either of the two pairs of folds of mucous membrane in the larynx, involved in producing sound by vibrating as air passes through them

Examples

vocal fold vibration
Damage to a vocal fold can affect a person’s ability to speak clearly.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient’s vocal folds during the procedure.
When you scream, your vocal folds vibrate rapidly to produce the sound.
