baby boomer

noun
member of the postwar baby boom generation
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbuː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbuː.mə / (BrE)
a person born during the post-World War II baby boom, typically between 1946 and 1964, especially in the United States and other Western countries

Examples

older baby boomer
As a baby boomer, she remembers life before the internet and smartphones.
Many baby boomers are now reaching retirement age and changing the workforce demographic.
The concert was packed with baby boomers reliving the music of their youth.
