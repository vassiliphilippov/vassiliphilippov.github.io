groundwater level

noun
level where ground is saturated with water
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌwɔt̬ɚ ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwɔːtə ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
the level below the earth's surface at which the ground is fully saturated with water; also called the water table

Examples

high groundwater level
Construction was delayed because the groundwater level was higher than expected.
Farmers monitor the groundwater level to ensure there is enough water for irrigation.
The groundwater level in the region dropped significantly after months of drought.

Related Terms

  • synonym – water table: the upper level of an underground surface in which the soil or rocks are permanently saturated with water
