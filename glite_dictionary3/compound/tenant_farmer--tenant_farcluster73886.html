tenant farmer

noun
farmer who works rented land
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnənt ˈfɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənt ˈfɑːmə / (BrE)
A farmer who rents land from a landlord and farms it, instead of owning the land.

Examples

tenant farmer grows crops
Her grandfather was a tenant farmer in rural England during the early twentieth century.
Many tenant farmers struggled to make ends meet after paying rent for the land.
The tenant farmer carefully tended the fields he had leased from the wealthy estate owner.

Related Terms

  • antonym – landlord: a person who owns and rents out land or property
  • synonym – sharecropper: a farmer who works land for a share of the crops
