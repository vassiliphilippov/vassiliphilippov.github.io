Iron Lady

noun
epithet for a tough female leader
Pronunciation: / c8aarn c8leadi / (AmE), / c8aa59n c8leadi / (BrE)
A (sometimes journalistic) epithet for a woman political leader who is perceived as strong, strict, or uncompromising in style or policy; used of leaders other than Margaret Thatcher.

Examples

Iron Lady woman leader
Many commentators called Angela Merkel the Iron Lady of Europe after her strong economic decisions.
The mayor earned a reputation as an Iron Lady for refusing to negotiate on city budget cuts.
Reporters dubbed the new party leader an Iron Lady because she demanded strict discipline from members.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Margaret Thatcher: British Prime Minister (1979–1990) originally called the Iron Lady
  • synonym – strong leader: a leader known for decisiveness and firm control
