silicone polymer

noun
synthetic material used for lubricants, adhesives, etc.
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlɪkoʊn ˈpɑːlɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkəʊn ˈpɒlɪmə / (BrE)
A synthetic material composed of repeating siloxane units, known for its stability over wide temperature ranges and used in products like lubricants, adhesives, coatings, synthetic rubbers, and electrical insulators.

Examples

silicone polymer insulation material
A silicone polymer can withstand extreme temperatures, making it ideal for electrical insulation.
Medical implants are often made of silicone polymer because of its stability and biocompatibility.
The surface was coated with a silicone polymer to provide water resistance and durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym – silicone: a synthetic material made from silicon, oxygen, carbon, and hydrogen
