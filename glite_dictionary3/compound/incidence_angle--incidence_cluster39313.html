incidence angle

noun
angle between incoming line and perpendicular to a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnsɪdəns ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɪdəns ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
the angle formed between an incoming line (such as a ray of light) and a line perpendicular to the surface at the point where it meets the surface

Examples

incidence angle of light
At a steep incidence angle, more light is reflected from the surface of the water.
The incidence angle of sunlight affects the amount of energy absorbed by a solar panel.
The experiment measured how changing the incidence angle impacted the brightness of the reflected beam.
