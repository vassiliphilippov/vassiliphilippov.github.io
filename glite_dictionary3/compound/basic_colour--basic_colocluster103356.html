basic colour

noun
a type of dye with chromophore as a positive ion
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsɪk ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A dye that is a base in which the chromophore forms part of a positive ion, used especially in textile and biological staining.

Examples

basic colour dye
Many basic colours are used in the dyeing of cotton and wool fabrics.
The textile was stained using a basic colour for better visibility under the microscope.
This laboratory procedure requires both basic colours and acid dyes for differential staining.
