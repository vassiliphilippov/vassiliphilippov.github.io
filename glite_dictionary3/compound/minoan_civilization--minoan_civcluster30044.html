Minoan civilization

noun
ancient Bronze Age culture of Crete
Pronunciation: / mɪˈnoʊ.ən ˌsɪ.və.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / mɪˈnəʊ.ən ˌsɪv.ɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the Bronze Age culture that developed on the island of Crete and flourished from about 3000 to 1100 BC, known for its advanced art, architecture, and writing.

Examples

ancient Minoan civilization
Archaeologists have uncovered frescoes and pottery from the Minoan civilization at the site of Knossos.
Many scholars believe that the Minoan civilization profoundly influenced later Greek art and religion.
The decline of the Minoan civilization is often attributed to natural disasters and invasions.
