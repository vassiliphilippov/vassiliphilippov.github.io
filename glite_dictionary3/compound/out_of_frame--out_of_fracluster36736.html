out of frame

adjective
not in a picture or not appropriate
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv freɪm / (AmE), / aʊt əv freɪm / (BrE)
not visible within the borders of a picture, or not appropriate, suitable, or relevant to the context

Examples

out of frame comment
Her joke was out of frame for such a serious meeting, so nobody laughed.
The actor’s awkward gesture was out of frame and did not distract the audience.
The mountain peak was so tall, it was out of frame in every photo I took.

Related Terms

  • antonym – in frame: visible within the borders of a picture or appropriate to the context
