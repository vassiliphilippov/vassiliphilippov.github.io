molecular pump

noun
device for creating high vacuum
Pronunciation: / məˈlɛkjələr pʌmp / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə pʌmp / (BrE)
a device that maintains a vacuum by removing gas molecules from a chamber using high-speed jets or rotating blades

Examples

molecular pump creates vacuum
A molecular pump is crucial for experiments that require ultra-high vacuum conditions.
Engineers selected a molecular pump because it achieves lower pressures than most mechanical pumps.
The laboratory upgraded its vacuum system by installing a new molecular pump for greater efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vacuum pump: a device for removing gas molecules from a sealed volume to leave a partial vacuum
