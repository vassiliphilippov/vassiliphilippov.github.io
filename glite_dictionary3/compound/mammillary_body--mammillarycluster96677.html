mammillary body

noun
brain structure involved in memory processing
Pronunciation: / ˈmæməˌlɛri ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈmamɪləri ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
either of a pair of small, round structures on the underside of the brain that form part of the limbic system and are involved in memory processing

Examples

the mammillary body in the brain
Damage to the mammillary body can cause memory disorders such as amnesia.
Neurologists examined the mammillary body for signs of disease in the MRI scan.
The mammillary body is an essential structure in the brain's limbic system.
