electrostatic precipitation

noun
removal of particles from gas using electric charge
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˌstætɪk prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌstætɪk prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a process of removing suspended dust or particles from a gas by applying a high-voltage electrostatic charge, causing the particles to collect on charged plates.

Examples

electrostatic precipitation in power plants
Electrostatic precipitation is commonly used in power plants to control emissions of particulate matter.
Thanks to electrostatic precipitation, the exhaust gases released are much cleaner.
The factory installed an electrostatic precipitation system to reduce air pollution.

Related Terms

  • synonym – filtration: process of removing particles from a fluid by passing it through a filter
