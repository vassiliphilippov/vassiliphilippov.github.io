cumulative vote

noun
a voting system with flexible vote allocation
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːmjəˌleɪtɪv voʊt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv vəʊt / (BrE)
a system of voting in which each voter has as many votes as there are positions to be filled and may allocate them all to one candidate or distribute them among several candidates

Examples

cumulative vote for directors
Shareholders have the option to cast all their cumulative votes for a single candidate if they choose.
The company's board of directors was elected using the cumulative vote system to ensure minority representation.
Under cumulative vote rules, you can divide your votes among several nominees as you prefer.

Related Terms

  • antonym – majority vote: a system where a candidate needs more than half the votes to win
  • antonym – plurality vote: a voting system where the candidate with the most votes wins
