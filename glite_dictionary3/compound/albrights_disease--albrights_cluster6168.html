Albright's disease

noun
rare bone disorder affecting multiple bones
Pronunciation: / ˈɔlˌbraɪts dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈɔːlbraɪts dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare disorder characterized by fibrous dysplasia affecting multiple bones, causing bone deformities and fractures.

Examples

People with Albright's disease often experience recurrent fractures during childhood.
Researchers are studying new treatments for Albright's disease to improve bone strength.
