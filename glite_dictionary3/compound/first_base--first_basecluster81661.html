first base

noun
baseball fielding position at first base
Pronunciation: / ˌfɝst ˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈbeɪs / (BrE)
the fielding position of the player on a baseball team who is stationed at the first of the four bases in the infield, located counterclockwise from home plate

Examples

play first base
During practice, she learned how to play first base effectively.
He scooped up the ball and stepped on first base for the out.
The team's new player will be covering first base for the rest of the season.
