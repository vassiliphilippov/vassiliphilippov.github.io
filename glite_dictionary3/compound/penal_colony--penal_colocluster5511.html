penal colony

noun
prison colony for exiled convicts
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːnəl ˌkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəl ˌkɒləni / (BrE)
A place, often an island, used to imprison and exile people convicted of crimes, where escape is difficult or impossible.

Examples

penal colony for prisoners
Britain once used Australia as a penal colony for convicts.
The memoir describes the harsh conditions faced by prisoners in the penal colony.
The notorious French penal colony on Devil’s Island has a dark and tragic history.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exile: the state of being barred from one's native country, typically for punitive reasons
  • synonym – prison: a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime or while waiting for trial
