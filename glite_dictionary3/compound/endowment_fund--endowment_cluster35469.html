endowment fund

noun
fund providing income to an institution
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdaʊmənt fʌnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt fʌnd / (BrE)
A fund consisting of financial assets donated to an institution, the income from which supports its ongoing operations.

Examples

endowment fund supports education
A strong endowment fund can ensure an organization's long-term financial stability.
Over the years, the museum's endowment fund has grown substantially thanks to generous donors.
The university's endowment fund helps finance scholarships and research grants.
