grape arbour

noun
framework for growing grapevines
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪp ˈɑrbər / (AmE), / ɡreɪp ˈɑːbə / (BrE)
a wooden framework or shelter, often in a garden, on which grapevines are trained to grow

Examples

shady grape arbour
He built a new grape arbour to provide both shade and fresh grapes for his family.
The old grape arbour was heavy with clusters of ripe fruit by late summer.
We enjoyed a picnic lunch under the shady grape arbour in her backyard.
