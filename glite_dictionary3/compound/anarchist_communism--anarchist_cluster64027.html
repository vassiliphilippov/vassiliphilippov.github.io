anarchist communism

noun
theory advocating stateless, communal ownership
Pronunciation: / ˈænərkɪst kəˈmjunɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈænəkɪst kəˈmjuːnɪzəm / (BrE)
A political theory advocating the abolition of the state, capitalism, wages, and private property (while retaining personal property), and supporting common ownership of the means of production, direct democracy, and organization through voluntary associations and workers' councils.

Examples

anarchist communism theory
Kropotkin was a major theorist of anarchist communism in the late nineteenth century.
Some activists see anarchist communism as the logical extension of both anarchist and communist ideas.
The novel explores a society based on anarchist communism rather than hierarchical government.

Related Terms

  • synonym – communism: a political theory advocating class war and leading to a society in which all property is publicly owned
