valuation account

noun
reserve fund for asset value changes
Pronunciation: / ˌvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account in which a company sets aside funds, typically by charging profits, to cover changes in the value of its assets

Examples

valuation account for assets
A well-managed valuation account helps companies report accurate profits and prevent financial surprises.
Auditors reviewed the valuation account to ensure it reflected the changes in asset worth.
The company created a valuation account to prepare for possible decreases in inventory value.
