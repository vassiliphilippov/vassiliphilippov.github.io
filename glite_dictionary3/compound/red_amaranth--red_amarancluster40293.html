red amaranth

noun
a tropical plant with red flowers and edible seeds
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈæm.əˌrænθ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈam.ər.anθ / (BrE)
a tall, showy tropical American annual plant with hairy stems and long spikes of usually red flowers, often grown as an ornamental or harvested for its edible seeds and leaves

Examples

red amaranth plant
Gardeners often choose red amaranth for its striking appearance and easy growth.
In many cuisines, red amaranth is prized for its vibrant leaves and nutritious seeds.
The seeds of red amaranth can be ground into flour or cooked as a cereal.
