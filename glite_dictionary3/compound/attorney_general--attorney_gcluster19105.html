Attorney General

noun
principal legal officer of a government
Pronunciation: / əˌtɝːni dʒəˈnɝːəl /; / əˈtɝni dʒəˈnɪrəl / (AmE), / əˌtɜːni dʒəˈnɜːrəl /; / əˈtɜːni dʒəˈnɛrəl / (BrE)
the chief legal officer who represents a government (country or state) in legal matters and is usually the head of the Department of Justice
