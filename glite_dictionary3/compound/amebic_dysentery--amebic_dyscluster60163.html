amebic dysentery

noun
dysentery caused by ameba infection
Pronunciation: / əˈmibɪk dɪˈsɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɪk dɪˈsɛn.təri / (BrE)
A form of dysentery caused by infection of the intestines with the parasite Entamoeba histolytica, resulting in severe diarrhea and abdominal pain, typically spread through contaminated food or water.

Examples

amebic dysentery infection
He was hospitalized after symptoms of amebic dysentery appeared, including persistent diarrhea and fever.
Many travelers to tropical countries are advised on how to avoid contracting amebic dysentery.
Untreated amebic dysentery can lead to severe dehydration and other complications.
