blood platelet

noun
cell fragment for blood clotting
Pronunciation: / blʌd ˈpleɪtlət / (AmE), / blʌd ˈpleɪtlət / (BrE)
a small cell fragment in the blood that is essential for clotting and helps stop bleeding by forming plugs in blood vessel injuries

Examples

blood platelet count
A low blood platelet count can make it difficult for wounds to heal properly.
After surgery, her blood platelet levels were closely monitored.
The doctor explained that blood platelets are crucial for preventing excessive bleeding.
