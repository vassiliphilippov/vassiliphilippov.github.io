hawk moth

noun
large moth known for hovering flight
Pronunciation: / hɔk mɔθ / (AmE), / hɔːk mɒθ / (BrE)
a moth with long, narrow forewings capable of powerful flight and hovering over flowers to feed, belonging to the family Sphingidae

Examples

hawk moth hovering over flowers
Gardeners often spot hawk moths in the evening as they are attracted to fragrant blooms.
The hawk moth hovered expertly over the flower, feeding on its nectar.
Unlike most moths, the hawk moth is known for its ability to hover while feeding.
