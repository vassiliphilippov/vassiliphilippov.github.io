hot medium

noun
medium conveying high-definition information (media theory)
Pronunciation: / hɑt ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / hɒt ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A means of communication that transmits information with high definition, providing a large amount of detail and requiring little audience participation, such as radio, film, or photography.

Examples

hot medium like radio
According to McLuhan, radio is a hot medium because it gives listeners detailed content with little involvement required.
Compared to television, film is a hot medium, engaging viewers with its high level of detail.
Photography is considered a hot medium, as the viewer receives a complete image with little missing information.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cool medium: a medium that provides less detail and requires more audience participation (media theory)
