European curlew

noun
large Eurasian wading bird with curved bill
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈkɜrlju / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpi.ən ˈkɜːljuː / (BrE)
A large wading bird (Numenius arquata) native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its long curved bill and distinctive call.

European curlew calls loudly
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the European curlew in several countries.
