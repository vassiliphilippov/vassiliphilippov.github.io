leaf-foot bug

noun
sap-sucking insect with leaflike legs
Pronunciation: / liːf fʊt bʌɡ / (AmE), / liːf fʊt bʌɡ / (BrE)
a large sap-sucking insect with leaflike expansions on its legs, belonging to the family Coreidae.

Examples

leaf-foot bug on a plant
Leaf-foot bugs often feed on the sap of various plants, sometimes damaging crops.
Gardeners sometimes mistake the leaf-foot bug for other types of plant pests.
The leaf-foot bug can be easily identified by the flat, leaflike structures on its hind legs.
