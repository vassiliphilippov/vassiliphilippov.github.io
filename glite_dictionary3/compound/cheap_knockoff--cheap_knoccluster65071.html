cheap knockoff

noun
inexpensive imitation product
Pronunciation: / tʃip ˈnɑːkˌɔf / (AmE), / tʃiːp ˈnɒkɒf / (BrE)
An imitation product made to resemble a more expensive or well-known original, typically sold at a much lower price and of inferior quality.

Examples

buy a cheap knockoff
She was disappointed when she realized the watch was just a cheap knockoff and not the real thing.
That bag looks expensive, but it's actually a cheap knockoff bought at the market.
The store was full of cheap knockoffs trying to mimic designer brands.
