cyclic disorder

noun
mild chronic bipolar disorder
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A mild form of bipolar disorder characterized by frequent and chronic fluctuations between mild depression and hypomania over a long period.

Examples

cyclic disorder with mood swings
A cyclic disorder can significantly affect daily life, even though its symptoms may seem mild compared to classic bipolar disorder.
She was diagnosed with cyclic disorder after experiencing years of mood swings.
Unlike major depression, cyclic disorder involves periods of both low and elevated mood.
