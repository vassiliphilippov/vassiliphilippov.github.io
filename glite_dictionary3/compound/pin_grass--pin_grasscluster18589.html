pin grass

noun
a weed with upright slender fruits, often grown for forage
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪn ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈpɪn ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European weed, naturalized in southwestern United States and Mexico, with reddish stems, fernlike leaves, reddish-lavender flowers, and upright slender fruits, often grown for forage

Examples

pin grass for cattle
Cattle in the region often graze on pin grass during the dry season.
Gardeners sometimes plant pin grass as a forage crop for livestock.
The slender fruits of pin grass stick straight up after the plant flowers.
