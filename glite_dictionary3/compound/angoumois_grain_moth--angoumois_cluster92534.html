Angoumois grain moth

noun
pest moth larvae feed on stored grain
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋɡuːˈmwɑː ˈɡreɪn ˌmɑθ / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡuːˈmwɑː ˈɡreɪn ˌmɒθ / (BrE)
A small moth (Sitotroga cerealella) whose larvae feed on the kernels of stored grains, often considered a pest in granaries.

Examples

Angoumois grain moth infests grain
Farmers regularly check their silos for signs of the Angoumois grain moth to protect their grain stocks.
Infestation by the Angoumois grain moth often goes unnoticed until damaged kernels are discovered.
The Angoumois grain moth can cause significant losses in stored wheat and corn.
