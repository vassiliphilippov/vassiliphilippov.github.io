Irish punt

noun
former currency of Ireland
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrɪʃ pʌnt / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪʃ pʊnt / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Ireland, equal to 100 pence, replaced by the euro in 2002

Examples

exchange Irish punt for euros
Before Ireland adopted the euro, people used the Irish punt for all financial transactions.
Collectors sometimes seek out rare Irish punt notes and coins at auctions.
He exchanged his old Irish punts for euros at the bank in Dublin.

Related Terms

  • antonym – euro: the official currency of the Eurozone, which replaced the Irish punt
  • synonym – pound sterling: the basic monetary unit of the United Kingdom, similar in structure to the Irish punt
