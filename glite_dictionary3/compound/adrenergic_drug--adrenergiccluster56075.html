adrenergic drug

noun
drug with effects like epinephrine
Pronunciation: / ˌædrəˈnɜrdʒɪk drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌadrəˈnɜːdʒɪk drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that mimics or stimulates the effects of epinephrine (adrenaline) in the body

Examples

adrenergic drug for asthma
Some asthma inhalers contain an adrenergic drug to relax the airway muscles.
The doctor prescribed an adrenergic drug to help increase the patient’s heart rate.
Use of an adrenergic drug may result in side effects such as increased blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sympathomimetic: drug that mimics the effects of the sympathetic nervous system
