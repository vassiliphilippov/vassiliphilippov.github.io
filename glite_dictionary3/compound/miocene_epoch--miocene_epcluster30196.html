Miocene epoch

noun
epoch in geologic timescale, 23–5 million years ago
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.əˌsiːn ˈɛp.ək / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.əʊˌsiːn ˈɛp.ɒk / (BrE)
a geological epoch of the Neogene period, lasting from about 23 to 5 million years ago, marked by the appearance and diversification of grazing mammals

Examples

the Miocene epoch period
During the Miocene epoch, the Earth's climate was warmer and many modern mammal families evolved.
Geologists study fossils from the Miocene epoch to understand environmental changes and animal adaptation.
Grasslands expanded significantly during the Miocene epoch, supporting new species of grazing mammals.
