relative humidity

noun
percentage of water vapor in air
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪv hjuːˈmɪdəti / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv hjuːˈmɪdəti / (BrE)
The amount of water vapor present in the air, expressed as a percentage of the maximum amount the air could hold at the same temperature.

Examples

relative humidity rises quickly
A drop in relative humidity makes it easier for sweat to evaporate from your skin.
Meteorologists monitor relative humidity to predict the likelihood of rain or fog.
The relative humidity was so high that the air felt muggy and uncomfortable.
