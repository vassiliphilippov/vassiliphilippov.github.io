ghost gum

noun
Australian tree with white or light-grey bark
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊst ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡəʊst ɡʌm / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized Australian tree with smooth white or light-grey bark that sheds in patches or strips, commonly found in Australia and Tasmania

Examples

tall ghost gum tree
During our hike, we rested under a tall ghost gum that provided welcome shade.
Many artists are inspired by the unique beauty of the ghost gum in the Australian landscape.
The ghost gum stood out in the moonlight, its pale bark almost glowing beside the riverbank.
