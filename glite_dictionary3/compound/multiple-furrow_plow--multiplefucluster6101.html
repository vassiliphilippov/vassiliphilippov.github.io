multiple-furrow plow

noun
plough with two or more shares
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltəpəl ˈfɝːroʊ plaʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl ˈfʌrəʊ plaʊ / (BrE)
A plough equipped with two or more shares, coulters, and mouldboards, allowing it to turn over several furrows of soil simultaneously.

Examples

modern multiple-furrow plow
A multiple-furrow plow can significantly reduce the time required for tilling compared to a traditional single-furrow model.
Advancements in machinery led to the widespread adoption of the multiple-furrow plow on large farms.
The farmer used a multiple-furrow plow to prepare the fields more efficiently before planting season.

Related Terms

  • antonym – single-furrow plow: a plough with only one share, working one furrow at a time
