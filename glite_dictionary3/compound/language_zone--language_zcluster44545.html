language zone

noun
area of the brain responsible for language
Pronunciation: / ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ zəʊn / (BrE)
a large area of the cerebral cortex, typically in the left hemisphere, that contains all the centers responsible for language processing

Examples

the language zone of the brain
A stroke affecting the language zone often results in various types of aphasia.
Damage to the language zone can lead to severe difficulties with speaking and understanding speech.
Researchers have identified the language zone as critical for both comprehension and production of language.
