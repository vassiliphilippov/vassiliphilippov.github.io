paraumbilical vein

noun
small vein near the navel connecting to the portal system
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəʌmˈbɪlɪkəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌparəʌmˈbɪlɪkəl veɪn / (BrE)
a small vein located near the navel that drains blood from the skin around the navel and connects to the portal vein system

Examples

paraumbilical vein ultrasound finding
An ultrasound can sometimes detect a prominent paraumbilical vein as a sign of increased portal pressure.
In patients with liver cirrhosis, the paraumbilical vein may become dilated visible under the skin.
The surgeon identified the paraumbilical vein during the abdominal procedure to prevent accidental bleeding.
