Socratic method

noun
method of teaching by question and answer
Pronunciation: / soʊˈkrætɪk ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / səˈkrætɪk ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of teaching or discussion characterized by asking and answering questions to stimulate critical thinking and to draw out ideas and underlying assumptions

Examples

use the Socratic method in class
Many law schools use the Socratic method to encourage critical thinking and active debate.
She found that the Socratic method improved students' engagement and reasoning skills.
The teacher employed the Socratic method to help students discover the solution on their own.
