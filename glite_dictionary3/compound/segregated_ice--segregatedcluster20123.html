segregated ice

noun
ice layers formed in freezing ground
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡrəˌɡeɪtɪd aɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡrɪɡeɪtɪd aɪs / (BrE)
Ice formed in permafrost as films, lenses, or layers by accumulation of water locally drawn into the freezing ground.

Examples

segregated ice forms layers
As the ground freezes, segregated ice forms by drawing in groundwater, creating distinct lenses within the soil.
Engineers must account for the potential thaw of segregated ice when designing foundations in Arctic environments.
The presence of segregated ice in the soil can significantly affect the stability of construction in permafrost regions.
