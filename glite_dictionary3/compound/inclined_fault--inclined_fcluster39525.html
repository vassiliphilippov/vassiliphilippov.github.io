inclined fault

noun
geological fault with one side above the other
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈklaɪnd fɔlt / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪnd fɔːlt / (BrE)
a geological fault in which one side has moved above the other, typically forming an angled or sloping fracture in the earth's crust

Examples

active inclined fault
Earthquakes are often associated with the movement along an inclined fault beneath the surface.
