aphthous ulcer

noun
small mouth sore; canker sore
Pronunciation: / ˈæfθəs ˈʌlsər / (AmE), / ˈafθəs ˈʌlsə / (BrE)
A small, painful blister or sore on the mucous membranes inside the mouth, lips, or gastrointestinal tract, often called a canker sore.

Examples

aphthous ulcer inside mouth
She visited the dentist because the aphthous ulcer on her lip was not healing.
Stress has been known to trigger aphthous ulcers in some individuals.
The doctor diagnosed the painful lesion as an aphthous ulcer and recommended a topical treatment.
