pharyngeal tonsil

noun
lymphatic tissue in upper throat (nasopharynx)
Pronunciation: / fəˈrɪn.dʒi.əl ˈtɑn.səl / (AmE), / fəˈrɪn.dʒɪ.əl ˈtɒn.səl / (BrE)
a collection of lymphatic tissue located on the posterior wall and roof of the nasopharynx, behind the uvula

Examples

swollen pharyngeal tonsil
Infections can cause the pharyngeal tonsil to swell, leading to discomfort and nasal blockage.
Surgical removal of an enlarged pharyngeal tonsil is sometimes necessary when breathing is obstructed.
The pharyngeal tonsil plays a role in the immune response during childhood.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adenoid: enlarged pharyngeal tonsil, especially in children
