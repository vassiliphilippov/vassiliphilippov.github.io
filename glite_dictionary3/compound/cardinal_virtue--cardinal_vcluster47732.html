cardinal virtue

noun
principal moral virtue in classical philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrdɪnl ˈvɝːtʃuː / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl ˈvɜː.tjuː / (BrE)
any of the four principal virtues in classical philosophy—prudence, justice, temperance, and fortitude—considered fundamental for good moral life.

Examples

cardinal virtue of justice
He admired her honesty and saw it as a reflection of the cardinal virtues.
Justice, as a cardinal virtue, guides ethical behavior in society.
Prudence is often regarded as the chief cardinal virtue in classical philosophy.
