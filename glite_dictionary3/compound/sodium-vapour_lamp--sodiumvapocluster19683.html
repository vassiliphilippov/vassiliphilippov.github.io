sodium-vapour lamp

noun
lamp producing yellow light from sodium vapor
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm ˈveɪpər læmp / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˈveɪpə læmp / (BrE)
A type of lamp in which an electric current passes through a tube filled with sodium vapor, producing a characteristic yellow light, commonly used in street lighting.

Examples

sodium-vapour lamp street lighting
A characteristic yellow glow on the highway is often produced by sodium-vapour lamps.
Many scientists study the spectrum of sodium-vapour lamps for use in physics experiments.
The city replaced all its old streetlights with new sodium-vapour lamps to improve visibility at night.
