wetting agent

noun
chemical that reduces liquid surface tension
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛtɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈwɛtɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance that lowers the surface tension of a liquid, allowing it to spread or penetrate more easily

Examples

add a wetting agent to water
Adding a wetting agent helps the pesticide solution spread uniformly on plant leaves.
Painters often use a wetting agent to ensure even absorption of water into paper.
To achieve better cleaning results, a wetting agent was included in the formula.
