f number

noun
photographic lens aperture setting
Pronunciation: / ɛf ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ɛf ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
the ratio of the focal length to the diameter of a camera lens, used to indicate the aperture setting

Examples

f number of a lens
A lower f number allows more light to reach the camera sensor, resulting in brighter photos.
She set the lens to an f number of 1.8 to create a shallow depth of field.
When shooting landscapes, photographers often choose a higher f number for greater sharpness throughout the image.

  • synonym – aperture: the opening in a lens through which light passes
