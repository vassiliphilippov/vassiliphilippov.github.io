fiscal year

noun
12-month accounting period
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪs.kəl jɪr / (AmE), / ˈfɪs.kəl jɪə / (BrE)
a period of twelve months used by governments, businesses, or organizations for accounting and financial purposes, which may or may not coincide with the calendar year

Examples

fiscal year budget planning
Many government agencies plan their budgets based on the fiscal year.
The company's fiscal year runs from April 1 to March 31.
The report compares sales figures from the previous fiscal year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calendar year: the period from January 1 to December 31; standard yearly period
