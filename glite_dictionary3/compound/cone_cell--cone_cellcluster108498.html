cone cell

noun
retinal cell sensitive to light and color
Pronunciation: / koʊn sɛl / (AmE), / kəʊn sɛl / (BrE)
a type of cell in the retina of the eye that is sensitive to bright light and enables color vision

Examples

cone cell in the eye
Damage to cone cells can result in color blindness or reduced visual clarity.
Human eyes rely on cone cells to perceive colors in bright environments.
Researchers are studying how cone cells respond to different wavelengths of light.
