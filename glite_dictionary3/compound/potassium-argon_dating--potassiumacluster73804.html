potassium-argon dating

noun
method for dating rocks using potassium and argon
Pronunciation: / pəˌtæsiəm ˈɑːrɡɑn ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˌtæsiəm ˈɑːɡən ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of determining the age of rocks by measuring the ratio of radioactive potassium to its decay product, argon.

Examples

potassium-argon dating ancient rocks
Many significant archaeological finds have been dated using potassium-argon dating techniques.
Scientists used potassium-argon dating to estimate the age of the ancient volcanic rock.
The accuracy of potassium-argon dating makes it valuable for studying early human fossils.
