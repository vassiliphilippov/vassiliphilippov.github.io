combined transport bill of lading

noun
shipping document for multimodal transport
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbaɪnd ˈtrænspɔrt bɪl əv ˈleɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪnd ˈtrænspɔːt bɪl əv ˈleɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
A document issued when goods are shipped using at least two different modes of transport, transferring responsibility to a single carrier for the entire journey.

Examples

issue a combined transport bill of lading
A combined transport bill of lading simplifies paperwork for shipments that require multiple carriers.
The shipping company issued a combined transport bill of lading for the container's journey by ship and truck.
When goods move internationally using different modes, a combined transport bill of lading ensures seamless responsibility throughout transit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bill of lading: a document acknowledging the receipt of cargo for shipment
