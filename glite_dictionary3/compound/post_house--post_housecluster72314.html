post house

noun
inn for changing horses and accommodating travelers
Pronunciation: / poʊst haʊs / (AmE), / pəʊst haʊs / (BrE)
A historic inn where post horses could be exchanged and travelers accommodated during long journeys.

Examples

old post house inn
In eighteenth-century Europe, the fastest mail traveled from post house to post house across the countryside.
The old post house at the edge of the village now serves as a museum.
Travelers often stopped at the post house to rest and exchange their tired horses for fresh ones.
