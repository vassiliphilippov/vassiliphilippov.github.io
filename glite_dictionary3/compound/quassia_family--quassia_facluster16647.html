quassia family

noun
family of tropical trees and shrubs (Simaroubaceae)
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːsiə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈkwæsɪə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs with bitter bark, usually producing dry, one-seeded winged fruit; the botanical family Simaroubaceae.
