bleeder's disease

noun
genetic disorder causing excessive bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˈbliːdərz dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / ˈbliːdəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A congenital disorder in which the blood does not clot normally, leading to excessive bleeding, usually affecting males and inherited from the mother.

Examples

bleeder's disease symptoms
Because bleeder's disease is inherited, it is important to inform your doctor if there is a family history.
Families with a history of bleeder's disease often seek genetic counseling before having children.
The young boy was diagnosed with bleeder's disease after experiencing prolonged bleeding from a minor cut.

  • synonym – hemophilia: a medical condition in which the blood's ability to clot is severely reduced
