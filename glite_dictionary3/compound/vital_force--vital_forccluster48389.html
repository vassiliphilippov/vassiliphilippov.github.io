vital force

noun
historical term for life-causing force
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪtəl fɔrs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəl fɔːs / (BrE)
(historical, biology) A hypothetical force once believed to cause the development and evolution of living organisms, distinct from physical or chemical processes.

Examples

the vital force of life
Henri Bergson wrote extensively about the concept of vital force in his philosophy of evolution.
Many 19th-century scientists believed a vital force separated living things from inanimate matter.
The idea of vital force has been largely rejected by modern biology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – vitalism: the doctrine that life is due to a vital force
  • synonym – élan vital: Bergson's term for the vital force driving evolution
