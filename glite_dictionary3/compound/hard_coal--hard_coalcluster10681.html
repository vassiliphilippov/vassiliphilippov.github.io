hard coal

noun
a dense coal that burns with intense heat
Pronunciation: / hɑrd koʊl / (AmE), / hɑːd kəʊl / (BrE)
a type of hard, natural coal that burns slowly and produces intense heat, typically used as fuel

Examples

burn hard coal for heat
Anthracite is commonly referred to as hard coal because of its high carbon content.
Because hard coal burns slowly, it is preferred for home heating in some regions.
The power plant was converted to burn hard coal instead of natural gas.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anthracite: a hard, shiny type of coal with a high carbon content
  • antonym – soft coal: coal that is softer and burns less intensely, also known as bituminous coal
