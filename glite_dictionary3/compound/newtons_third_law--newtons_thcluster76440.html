Newton's third law

noun
law: action and reaction are equal and opposite
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtənz θɜrd lɔ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtənz θɜːd lɔː / (BrE)
the scientific principle stating that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction

Examples

Newton's third law of motion
Engineers must consider Newton's third law when designing rockets and jet engines.
The recoil of a gun after firing is a classic demonstration of Newton's third law.
When a swimmer pushes the water backwards, Newton's third law explains why they move forward.
