red dwarf star

noun
a small, cool, long-lived star
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛd ˈdwɔrf ˌstɑr / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈdwɔːf ˌstɑː / (BrE)
A small, relatively cool, long-lived star of low mass, typically emitting a reddish light.

Examples

a red dwarf star shines dimly
Because a red dwarf star burns fuel slowly, it can shine for billions of years.
Many exoplanets have been discovered orbiting red dwarf stars in recent years.
The closest red dwarf star to Earth is Proxima Centauri.
