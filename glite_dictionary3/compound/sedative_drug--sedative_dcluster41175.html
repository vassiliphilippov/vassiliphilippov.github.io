sedative drug

noun
a drug that calms or soothes
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛdəˌtɪv drʌg / (AmE), / ˈsɛdətɪv drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that calms the central nervous system, reducing excitement or irritability

Examples

take a sedative drug before surgery
Because of her anxiety, she was given a mild sedative drug before the procedure.
Some sedative drugs can cause drowsiness and affect concentration.
The doctor prescribed a sedative drug to help the patient relax before surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stimulant: a drug that increases activity in the body or nervous system
  • synonym – tranquilizer: a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety
