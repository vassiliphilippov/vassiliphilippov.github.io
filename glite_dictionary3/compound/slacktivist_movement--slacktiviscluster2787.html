slacktivist movement

noun
minimal-effort activism, often online
Pronunciation: / ˈslæk.tɪ.vɪst ˈmuːv.mənt / (AmE), / ˈslæk.tɪ.vɪst ˈmuːv.mənt / (BrE)
A form of activism characterized by minimal effort, often limited to online actions such as sharing posts or signing petitions.

Examples

online slacktivist movement
After the campaign went viral, the slacktivist movement turned public attention to the issue.
Many critics argue that the slacktivist movement leads to awareness without real change.
The slacktivist movement gained momentum as more people supported causes online without significant commitment.
