hack writer

noun
mediocre, disdained writer
Pronunciation: / hæk ˈraɪtɚ / (AmE), / hæk ˈraɪtə / (BrE)
a writer who produces mediocre or low-quality work, often for payment and with little regard for originality or literary merit

Examples

a hack writer for magazines
Despite his reputation as a hack writer, his books continued to sell well.
She was dismissed as a hack writer cranking out romance novels for quick profit.
The magazine refused to hire just another hack writer with no passion for their craft.

Related Terms

  • antonym – author: a writer of books, articles, or other written works
  • synonym – scribbler: a person who writes in a hurried, careless, or unskilled way
