to wear ship

verb
turn a ship away from the wind (nautical)
Pronunciation: / wɛr ʃɪp / (AmE), / wɛə ʃɪp / (BrE)
(of a sailing vessel) to turn away from the wind by moving the stern through the wind, rather than the bow, as an alternative to tacking.

Examples

to wear ship in strong winds
During heavy weather, the crew had to wear ship to avoid capsizing.
Experienced sailors know when it is safer to wear ship rather than tack.
The captain ordered the crew to wear ship as the wind shifted unexpectedly.
