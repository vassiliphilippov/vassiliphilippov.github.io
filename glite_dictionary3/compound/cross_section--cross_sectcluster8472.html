cross section

noun
sample representing a whole population
Pronunciation: / krɑs ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
a group or sample chosen to represent the wider population or the range of something being studied

Examples

a cross section of people
Researchers selected a cross section of students from different grades for the study.
The exhibit attracted a cross section of art lovers, ranging from teenagers to retirees.
The survey interviewed a cross section of the local community to get diverse opinions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sample: a small part intended to show what the whole is like
