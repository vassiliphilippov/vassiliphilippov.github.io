somatic sensation

noun
perception of bodily sensations
Pronunciation: / səˈmætɪk sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the perception of bodily sensations such as touch, proprioception, or visceral feelings

Examples

somatic sensation of touch
A loss of somatic sensation can occur after certain injuries or medical conditions.
Doctors assess somatic sensation to check nerve function in patients with suspected neurological disorders.
The nervous system coordinates different types of somatic sensation to help us interact with the environment.
