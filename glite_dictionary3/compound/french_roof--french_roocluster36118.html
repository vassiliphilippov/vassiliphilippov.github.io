French roof

noun
type of steep-sided mansard roof
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ruːf / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ruːf / (BrE)
A type of mansard roof with sides that are nearly vertical or perpendicular to the ground, commonly used in French architecture.

Examples

French roof on a house
Adding a French roof gave the building an elegant and classic appearance.
Many 19th-century homes in the city feature a French roof for extra attic space.
The historic mansion was renovated to preserve its distinctive French roof.
