to linger too long

verb
stay longer than necessary
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋɚ tu lɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡə tuː lɒŋ / (BrE)
to remain in a place or situation for more time than is necessary or advisable

Examples

to linger too long at work
If we linger too long at the market, we might miss our appointment.
When you linger too long in the sun, you risk getting sunburned.
You shouldn't linger too long after the concert ends, as the buses stop running early.
