ecological niche

noun
the role of a species in its environment
Pronunciation: / ˌikəˈlɑdʒɪkəl nɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl nɪtʃ / (BrE)
the role or position of an organism or species within its environment, including how it obtains resources and interacts with other organisms

Examples

fill an ecological niche
Bees occupy a unique ecological niche as pollinators in many plant communities.
Scientists study how changes in climate affect the ecological niche of different animal populations.
When an invasive species enters a new environment, it may disrupt the existing ecological niche structure.
