open fracture

noun
bone fracture with open wound
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈfræktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈfræktʃə / (BrE)
A fracture in which a broken bone is exposed through an open wound in the skin, often associated with damaged soft tissue.

Examples

serious open fracture
An open fracture increases the risk of infection compared to a simple closed fracture.
Immediate treatment is critical when dealing with an open fracture to prevent complications.
The patient was rushed to the hospital after sustaining an open fracture of his tibia during the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym – closed fracture: a bone fracture in which the skin remains intact
