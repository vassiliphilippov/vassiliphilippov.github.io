line of longitude

noun
imaginary north–south line on the Earth
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv ˈlɑːndʒɪˌtud / (AmE), / laɪn əv ˈlɒndʒɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
An imaginary north–south line on the Earth's surface, passing through both poles and at right angles to the equator, used to indicate position east or west.

Examples

line of longitude on a map
Each city lies on a different line of longitude and therefore has a different local time.
Greenwich is famous for being the standard zero line of longitude in geographical measurements.
The explorer documented the line of longitude at which he landed on the remote island.
