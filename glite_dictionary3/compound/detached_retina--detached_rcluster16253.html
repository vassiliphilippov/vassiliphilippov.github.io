detached retina

noun
separation of the retina from the back of the eye
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtætʃt ˈrɛt.nə / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃt ˈret.ɪ.nə / (BrE)
a condition in which the retina separates from the underlying tissue at the back of the eye, often causing vision loss if untreated

Examples

detached retina surgery
If left untreated, a detached retina can result in permanent blindness.
Surgery is often required to repair a detached retina and restore vision.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed him with a detached retina after he experienced sudden vision loss.
