to spirits fall

verb
to become sad or discouraged
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪts fɔl / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪts fɔːl / (BrE)
(of a person's mood or morale) to become sad, depressed, or discouraged.

Examples

spirits fall after bad news
My spirits fell when I realized I had lost my wallet.
When he heard about the cancelled trip, his spirits fell immediately.
You could see her spirits fall as she read the disappointing letter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – spirits sink: to start feeling less happy or hopeful
