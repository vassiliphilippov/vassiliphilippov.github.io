hot spot

noun
place of intense heat or radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑt ˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌspɒt / (BrE)
a place or point where the temperature or level of radiation is significantly higher than its surroundings

Examples

thermal hot spot
Engineers use infrared cameras to detect hot spots in machinery.
Solar flares often originate from a hot spot on the sun's surface.
The faulty wire created a hot spot that led to the fire.
