logical topology

noun
virtual layout of a network's connections
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑdʒɪkəl təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəl təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the arrangement of network devices and connections as they function, regardless of the physical layout of the network

Examples

logical topology of a network
Even though the office used a ring wiring system, its logical topology operated like a star.
The company's new network diagram showed the logical topology necessary for efficient data sharing.
Understanding the logical topology is essential for diagnosing network communication issues.
