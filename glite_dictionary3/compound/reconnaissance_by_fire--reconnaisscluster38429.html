reconnaissance by fire

noun
military tactic to provoke enemy by firing
Pronunciation: / rɪˌkɑnəsəns baɪ faɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˌkɒnɪsəns baɪ faɪə / (BrE)
a military tactic in which gunfire is directed at a suspected enemy position to provoke a response and reveal the enemy's location

Examples

reconnaissance by fire against enemy
Commanders sometimes order reconnaissance by fire when there's uncertainty about the enemy's position.
The unit conducted reconnaissance by fire to determine if the enemy was hiding in the forest.
Using reconnaissance by fire carries the risk of revealing your own location to the opponent.
