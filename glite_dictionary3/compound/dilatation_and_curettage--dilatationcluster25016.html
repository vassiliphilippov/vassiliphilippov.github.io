dilatation and curettage

noun
surgical procedure to scrape uterine lining
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪləˈteɪʃən ənd kjʊˈrɛtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪləˈteɪʃən ənd kjʊˈrɛtɪdʒ / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the cervix is dilated and the uterine lining is scraped with a curette, often to obtain tissue samples, stop bleeding, or remove abnormal tissue

Examples

dilatation and curettage procedure
A dilatation and curettage is sometimes called a D&C in medical settings.
She underwent a dilatation and curettage following a miscarriage to ensure no tissue remained.
The doctor recommended a dilatation and curettage to diagnose the cause of her abnormal bleeding.
