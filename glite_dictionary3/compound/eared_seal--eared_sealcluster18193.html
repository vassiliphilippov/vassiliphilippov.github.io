eared seal

noun
pinniped mammal with ear flaps
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrd ˌsil / (AmE), / ˈɪəd ˌsiːl / (BrE)
A pinniped mammal distinguished by external ear flaps and hind limbs adapted for walking on land; includes sea lions and fur seals, valued for their soft underfur.

Examples

eared seal colony
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered eared seal.
Many species of eared seal are found along the coasts of the Pacific Ocean.
The eared seal is easily recognized by its visible external ears and agile movement on land.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sea lion: Type of eared seal with long foreflippers and external ears
  • antonym – true seal: Pinniped without external ear flaps
