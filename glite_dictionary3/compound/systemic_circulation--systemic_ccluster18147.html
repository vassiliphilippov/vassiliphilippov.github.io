systemic circulation

noun
blood circulation to and from the body (excluding lungs)
Pronunciation: / sɪˈstɛmɪk ˌsɝːkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈstɛmɪk ˌsɜːkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the part of the circulatory system that carries oxygenated blood from the heart to all parts of the body except the lungs and returns deoxygenated blood back to the heart

Examples

systemic circulation supplies body
A blockage in the systemic circulation can result in serious health complications.
During exercise, changes occur in both pulmonary and systemic circulation to meet increased oxygen demands.
The systemic circulation delivers oxygen-rich blood to tissues throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym – pulmonary circulation: the circulation of blood between the heart and the lungs
