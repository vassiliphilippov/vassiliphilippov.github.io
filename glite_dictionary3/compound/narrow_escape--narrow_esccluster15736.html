narrow escape

noun
barely avoiding danger or failure
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ ɪˈskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ ɪˈskeɪp / (BrE)
a situation in which someone only just avoids danger, failure, or an unpleasant outcome

Examples

a narrow escape from danger
It was a narrow escape when the car skidded on the icy road but didn't crash.
She had a narrow escape after accidentally touching the electric wire.
The hikers had a narrow escape from the falling rocks during their climb.
