maxillary artery

noun
artery supplying face and jaw
Pronunciation: / ˌmæksəˈlɛri ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ˌmæksɪˈlæri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of the two arteries branching from the external carotid artery that supply blood to parts of the face, jaw, and mouth

Examples

maxillary artery supplies blood
Damage to the maxillary artery can lead to significant bleeding in the facial region.
Several branches of the maxillary artery supply the upper jaw and teeth.
The surgeon carefully avoided the maxillary artery during the dental procedure.
