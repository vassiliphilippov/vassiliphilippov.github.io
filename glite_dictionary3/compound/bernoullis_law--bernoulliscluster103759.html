Bernoulli's law

noun
law about random sampling and statistical averages
Pronunciation: / bərˈnuːliz lɔ / (AmE), / bəːˈnuːliz lɔː / (BrE)
In statistics, the law stating that the average of a large number of randomly chosen items from a population will approximate the population statistics.

Examples

Bernoulli's law in statistics
According to Bernoulli's law, the average result becomes more predictable as the sample size increases.
In probability theory, Bernoulli's law underpins the idea of reliable long-term statistical trends.
Researchers referenced Bernoulli's law when analyzing data collected from a large, random population sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym – law of large numbers: the principle stating that as a sample size increases, its mean will get closer to the average of the whole population
