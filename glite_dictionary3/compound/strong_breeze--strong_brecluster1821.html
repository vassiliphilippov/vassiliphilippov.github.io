strong breeze

noun
wind of 25–31 knots; Beaufort scale 6
Pronunciation: / strɔŋ briːz / (AmE), / strɒŋ briːz / (BrE)
A wind with a speed of 25–31 knots (about 46–57 km/h), classified as number 6 on the Beaufort scale.

Examples

a strong breeze at sea
Sailing in a strong breeze can be both challenging and exhilarating for experienced sailors.
The forecast warns of a strong breeze developing along the coast tomorrow.
The picnic was cut short by a sudden strong breeze that scattered our belongings.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – gale: a very strong wind (Beaufort scale 7 or above)
  • degree lower – moderate breeze: wind of 11–16 knots (Beaufort scale 4)
