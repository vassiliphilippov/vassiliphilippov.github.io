learned reaction

noun
a response acquired by learning
Pronunciation: / ˈlɝːnɪd riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a reaction or response that is acquired through experience or training rather than being innate

Examples

a learned reaction to stress
Habits such as checking your phone constantly can become a learned reaction to boredom.
The dog's wagging tail when it hears the bell is a learned reaction from repeated training.
Unlike a reflex, a learned reaction requires practice and exposure to certain situations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conditioned response: a response elicited by a conditioned stimulus in classical conditioning
  • antonym – innate reaction: a reaction present from birth, not learned
