Dutch elm

noun
European hybrid shade tree
Pronunciation: / dʌtʃ ɛlm / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ɛlm / (BrE)
A hybrid ornamental elm tree of European origin, cultivated as a shade tree and varying in size from dwarf to tall.

Examples

Dutch elm tree planting
Due to disease resistance, the Dutch elm is a popular choice for urban landscaping.
Gardeners admire the graceful shape of the Dutch elm in both large parks and small yards.
The city planted several Dutch elm trees along the boulevard for their broad canopies.
