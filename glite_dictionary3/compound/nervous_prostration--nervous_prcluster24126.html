nervous prostration

noun
extreme exhaustion from emotional stress
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs prɑːˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs prɒˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
A condition characterized by extreme mental and physical exhaustion, often caused by emotional stress, resulting in an inability to work or function normally.

Examples

nervous prostration from stress
After a period of intense pressure at work, she collapsed with nervous prostration.
He took a six-month leave to recover from nervous prostration brought on by personal loss.
Victorian doctors often diagnosed nervous prostration when patients suffered from unexplained fatigue.

Related Terms

  • synonym – neurasthenia: a psychological disorder marked by chronic fatigue and weakness
