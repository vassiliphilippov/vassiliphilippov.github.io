celestial sphere

noun
imaginary sphere for celestial bodies
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛstʃəl sfɪr / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl sfɪə / (BrE)
An imaginary sphere surrounding the Earth, on which all celestial bodies appear to be projected.

Examples

the celestial sphere of stars
Astronomers use the concept of the celestial sphere to map the positions of stars.
The ancient Greeks imagined the stars fixed on a gigantic celestial sphere revolving around the Earth.
To locate a planet in the night sky, you can refer to coordinates on the celestial sphere.
