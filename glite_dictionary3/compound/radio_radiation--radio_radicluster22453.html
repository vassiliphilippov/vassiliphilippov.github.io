radio radiation

noun
electromagnetic waves used in radio communication
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
electromagnetic waves with wavelengths between about 0.5 centimeters and 30,000 meters, typically used for radio communication

Examples

radio radiation from space
Astronomers detect radio radiation from distant galaxies to study their structure and composition.
Natural sources such as the Sun generate significant amounts of radio radiation that can interfere with communications.
The experiment measured the radio radiation emitted by the device to ensure it complied with safety standards.
