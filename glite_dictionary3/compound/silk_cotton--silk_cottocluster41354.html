silk cotton

noun
fiber from kapok tree for stuffing/insulation
Pronunciation: / sɪlk ˈkɑː.tən / (AmE), / sɪlk ˈkɒt.ən / (BrE)
A soft, silky plant fiber obtained from the kapok tree, used especially for stuffing pillows and insulation.

Examples

silk cotton pillow filling
The fiber from the kapok tree, known as silk cotton, is lightweight and water-resistant.
They used silk cotton to fill the cushions for extra softness.
Traditional life jackets were often stuffed with silk cotton for buoyancy.
