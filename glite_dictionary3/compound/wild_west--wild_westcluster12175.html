Wild West

noun
western US frontier during expansion period
Pronunciation: / waɪld wɛst / (AmE), / waɪld west / (BrE)
the western United States during the period of frontier expansion, characterized by lawlessness, pioneering settlements, and conflicts between settlers and Native Americans

Examples

the Wild West era
During the Wild West era, settlements sprang up quickly along new railroad lines.
Many movies depict the Wild West as a place full of gunfights and rugged cowboys.
The legends of the Wild West continue to influence American culture today.
