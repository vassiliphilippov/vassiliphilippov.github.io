Shakespearean sonnet

noun
a sonnet form with specific rhyme scheme (abab cdcd efef gg)
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪkˈspɪriən ˈsɑnɪt / (AmE), / ʃeɪkˈspɪəriən ˈsɒnɪt / (BrE)
A sonnet composed of three quatrains and a final couplet, written in iambic pentameter with the rhyme scheme abab cdcd efef gg, as popularized by William Shakespeare.

Examples

classic Shakespearean sonnet
He wrote a Shakespearean sonnet for his beloved, following the traditional rhyme scheme.
Many poets have experimented with the Shakespearean sonnet form to express modern themes.
The student recited a Shakespearean sonnet as part of her English literature class.
