Hertzian wave

noun
former term for a radio wave
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜrtsiən weɪv / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtsɪən weɪv / (BrE)
a former name for a radio wave; an electromagnetic wave of radio frequency produced by oscillations in an electric circuit

Examples

Hertzian wave transmission
Early wireless communication relied on Hertzian waves to transmit signals through the air.
Physicists in the 19th century conducted experiments to prove the existence of Hertzian waves.
The term Hertzian wave has largely been replaced by 'radio wave' in modern science.
