war on expenses

noun
ongoing campaign to control costs
Pronunciation: / wɔr ɑn ɪkˈspɛnsɪz / (AmE), / wɔːr ɒn ɪkˈspensɪz / (BrE)
an ongoing campaign or determined effort to reduce or control costs, especially within an organization

Examples

war on expenses in business
After the merger, there was a renewed war on expenses with strict limits on travel and supplies.
Every department is expected to contribute to the war on expenses by identifying unnecessary spending.
The new financial manager intensified the war on expenses to help the company stay competitive.
