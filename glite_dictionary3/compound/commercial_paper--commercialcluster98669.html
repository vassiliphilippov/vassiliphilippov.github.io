commercial paper

noun
short-term unsecured corporate debt instrument
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɝːʃəl ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
an unsecured, short-term debt instrument issued by companies to finance their immediate monetary needs, typically not backed by collateral

Examples

commercial paper investment
Banks and large corporations commonly invest in commercial paper for a quick return on idle cash.
During a credit crunch, access to commercial paper can become restricted for many firms.
The company issued commercial paper to raise funds for its short-term expenditures.
