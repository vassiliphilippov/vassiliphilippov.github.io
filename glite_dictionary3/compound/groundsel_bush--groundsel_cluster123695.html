groundsel bush

noun
a salt-marsh shrub with hairy tufts on fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd.səl bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd.səl bʊʃ / (BrE)
a shrub native to salt marshes of eastern and south central North America and the West Indies, with fruit surrounded by white, plumelike, hairy tufts

Examples

A cluster of groundsel bush stood at the waterline, their white tufts catching the sunlight.
