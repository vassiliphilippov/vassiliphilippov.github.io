cranial nerve

noun
nerve originating from the brain, not the spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪniəl nɜːrv / (AmE), / ˈkreɪniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
any of the twelve pairs of nerves that emerge directly from the brain, rather than the spinal cord, and primarily serve the head and neck

Examples

facial cranial nerve
Damage to the facial cranial nerve can lead to paralysis on one side of the face.
Doctors tested the patient's cranial nerves to assess her neurological function.
The optic cranial nerve is responsible for transmitting visual information to the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – spinal nerve: any of the nerves that arise from the spinal cord
