pseudohypertrophic dystrophy

noun
a type of muscular dystrophy affecting mostly males
Pronunciation: / suːdoʊˌhaɪpərˈtroʊfɪk dɪsˈtrəfi / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˌhaɪpəˈtrəʊfɪk dɪsˈtrɒfi / (BrE)
A common form of muscular dystrophy in which muscles appear enlarged but are weak, inherited as an X-linked recessive trait mostly affecting males.

Examples

pseudohypertrophic dystrophy in boys
Boys affected by pseudohypertrophic dystrophy often show enlarged calf muscles despite significant muscle wasting.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of pseudohypertrophic dystrophy.
The child was diagnosed with pseudohypertrophic dystrophy after presenting progressive muscle weakness.
