flurry of emotions

noun
brief outburst of mixed feelings
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌri əv ɪˈmoʊʃənz / (AmE), / ˈflʌri əv ɪˈməʊʃənz / (BrE)
a sudden, brief period involving many emotions felt at the same time

Examples

a flurry of emotions after news
After watching the film, he was left with a flurry of emotions that lingered for hours.
Hearing her name called out brought a flurry of emotions she hadn't expected.
The announcement triggered a flurry of emotions among the participants, ranging from excitement to anxiety.
