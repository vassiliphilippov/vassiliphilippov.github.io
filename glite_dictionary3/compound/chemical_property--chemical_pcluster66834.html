chemical property

noun
property revealed by chemical change
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a characteristic of a substance that becomes evident during a chemical reaction which changes the substance's identity or composition

Examples

chemical property of water
Flammability is an important chemical property tested in laboratory safety experiments.
Reactivity with acids is a chemical property of many metals.
The chemical property of oxidation makes iron rust when exposed to moisture.

Related Terms

  • antonym – physical property: a characteristic that can be observed or measured without changing the identity of the substance
