Minoan culture

noun
Bronze Age civilization of ancient Crete
Pronunciation: / mɪˈnoʊ.ən ˈkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / mɪˈnəʊ.ən ˈkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
the Bronze Age civilization that existed on the island of Crete and flourished from about 3000 to 1100 BC, known for its advanced art, architecture, and trade.

Examples

ancient Minoan culture
Archaeologists have uncovered many artifacts from the Minoan culture during excavations on Crete.
Many scholars believe that the Minoan culture played a key role in the development of later Greek civilizations.
The artwork and palaces of the Minoan culture reveal a sophisticated society with rich traditions.
