horizontal stabiliser

noun
fixed tail surface on an aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntəl ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntəl ˈsteɪbɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
A fixed horizontal surface on an aircraft's tail assembly to which the elevator is attached, helping to maintain stability in flight.

Examples

horizontal stabiliser of an airplane
A damaged horizontal stabiliser can affect an airplane's control and safety during flight.
The aircraft's horizontal stabiliser ensures stable flight by resisting up-and-down pitching movements.
The mechanic inspected the horizontal stabiliser for signs of wear and corrosion.
