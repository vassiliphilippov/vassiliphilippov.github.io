short covering

noun
buying to close a short position in finance
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈkʌvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of buying back securities or commodities that were previously sold short, in order to close out a short position

Examples

short covering by investors
Analysts attributed the unusual trading volume to aggressive short covering by hedge funds.
The sudden rally in the market was partly driven by a wave of short covering.
When the stock price started rising rapidly, many investors engaged in short covering to limit their losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – short sale: the sale of a security not owned by the seller, with the intention of buying it back later at a lower price
