sum total

noun
the whole amount; final aggregate
Pronunciation: / sʌm ˈtoʊtəl / (AmE), / sʌm ˈtəʊtəl / (BrE)
the entirety of something regarded as an aggregate; the final total amount resulting from the addition of all elements

Examples

the sum total of knowledge
The sum total of my experience makes me confident in handling this project.
The sum total of their findings proved the theory beyond any doubt.
When you add up all expenses, the sum total exceeds our initial budget.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aggregate: a whole formed by combining several elements
  • antonym – partial: being only a part of the whole
