Dutch florin

noun
former Dutch monetary unit
Pronunciation: / dʌtʃ ˈflɔrɪn / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈflɒrɪn / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in the Netherlands, equal to 100 cents, replaced by the euro in 2002

Examples

old Dutch florin coin
Before the euro, the Dutch florin was used in shops across the Netherlands.
Collectors often seek rare coins such as the Dutch florin for their historical value.
In 1998, prices in Amsterdam were still listed in Dutch florins rather than euros.
