light reaction

noun
initial stage of photosynthesis using light energy
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt riˌækʃən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt riːˌækʃən / (BrE)
the first stage of photosynthesis in which light energy is used to produce ATP and other energy-rich compounds

Examples

light reaction in photosynthesis
During the light reaction, plants convert solar energy into chemical energy.
If the light reaction is inhibited, ATP and NADPH production will decrease.
The light reaction occurs in the thylakoid membranes of chloroplasts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dark reaction: the second stage of photosynthesis not requiring light
