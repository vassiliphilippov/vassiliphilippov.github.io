Korsakoff's psychosis

noun
memory disorder from chronic alcoholism
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːr.səˌkɑfz saɪˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.səˌkɒfs sʌɪˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A chronic memory disorder characterized by severe impairment of short-term memory, occurring in the later stages of chronic alcoholism, with long-term memory typically preserved.

Examples

Korsakoff's psychosis impairs memory
Short-term memory loss is the hallmark symptom of Korsakoff's psychosis.
The patient was diagnosed with Korsakoff's psychosis after a long history of alcohol abuse.
Without proper treatment, chronic alcoholics may develop Korsakoff's psychosis in addition to other neurological problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Wernicke's encephalopathy: acute neurological disorder often seen with Korsakoff's due to thiamine deficiency
  • synonym – amnesia: loss of memory, especially for past events
