lee side

noun
sheltered side, out of the wind
Pronunciation: / liː saɪd / (AmE), / liː saɪd / (BrE)
the side of something, such as a ship or hill, that is sheltered from the wind

Examples

the lee side of a hill
Sheltering ourselves on the lee side of the barn, we found relief from the biting cold.
The hikers set up their tent on the lee side of the hill to avoid the strong winds.
We anchored the boat on the lee side of the island for better protection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – windward: the side from which the wind blows
