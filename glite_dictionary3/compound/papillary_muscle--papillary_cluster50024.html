papillary muscle

noun
muscle in the heart attached to heart valves
Pronunciation: / ˈpæpəˌlɛri ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈpapɪləri ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
one of several muscles located within the heart that attach to the atrioventricular valves and contract to prevent backflow of blood into the atria during heart contraction

Examples

heart papillary muscle
Damage to the papillary muscle can lead to severe heart valve problems.
During a heart attack, the papillary muscles may be affected and impair valve function.
The surgeon repaired the torn papillary muscle to restore normal heart function.
