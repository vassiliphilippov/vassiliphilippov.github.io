petite bourgeoisie

noun
lower middle class (shopkeepers, clerks, etc.)
Pronunciation: / pəˌtit bʊrˈʒwɑzi / (AmE), / pəˌtiːt ˌbʊəˈʒwɑːzi / (BrE)
the lower middle class, especially made up of small shopkeepers, clerical staff, and similar groups, distinguished from the working class and higher bourgeoisie

Examples

the petite bourgeoisie class
During the economic crisis, members of the petite bourgeoisie feared losing their small businesses.
Many sociological studies examine the role of the petite bourgeoisie in urban development.
The petite bourgeoisie often found themselves squeezed between the interests of the wealthy and the working class.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – bourgeoisie: the middle class, typically with reference to its perceived materialistic values or conventional attitudes
  • degree lower – proletariat: the working class, especially those who do manual labor or work for wages
