computed axial tomography

noun
X-ray-based medical imaging technique
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpjuːt̬ɪd ˈæksi.əl təˈmɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtɪd ˈæksi.əl təˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses X-rays and a computer to create cross-sectional images of body organs along a single axis

Examples

computed axial tomography scan
After a car accident, the doctor ordered a computed axial tomography to check for internal injuries.
Modern computed axial tomography can detect tumors and other abnormalities with high precision.
The hospital now offers 24-hour computed axial tomography for emergency patients.
