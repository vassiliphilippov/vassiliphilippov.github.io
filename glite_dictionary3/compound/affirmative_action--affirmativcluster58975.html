affirmative action

noun
policy to redress discrimination and improve opportunities
Pronunciation: / əˈfɝː.mə.tɪv ˈæk.ʃən /; / əˈfɝːmətɪv ˈækʃən / (AmE), / əˈfɜː.mə.tɪv ˈæk.ʃən /; / əˈfɜːmətɪv ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a policy or set of policies that aim to increase opportunities for groups that have been historically disadvantaged or underrepresented, especially in education and employment

Examples

affirmative action policy
Debates over affirmative action policies have been ongoing in the United States for decades.
Many companies now have affirmative action programs to promote diversity in their workforce.
The university implemented affirmative action to increase the enrollment of underrepresented minorities.
Some people believe affirmative action is necessary to address historical injustices.
The new law requires companies to implement affirmative action in their hiring practices.
The university adopted affirmative action policies to increase diversity among its students.

Related Terms

  • antonym – discrimination: the unjust or prejudicial treatment of people based on group characteristics
  • synonym – equal opportunity: the principle of treating all people equally without discrimination
  • synonym – positive discrimination: the practice of favoring individuals belonging to groups known to have been discriminated against previously
