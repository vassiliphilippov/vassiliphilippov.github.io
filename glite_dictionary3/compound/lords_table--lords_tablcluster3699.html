Lord's table

noun
table for communion in Christian churches
Pronunciation: / lɔrdz ˈteɪ.bəl / (AmE), / lɔːdz ˈteɪ.bəl / (BrE)
the table in a Christian church on which the bread and wine are placed for the celebration of the Eucharist (Holy Communion)

Examples

the Lord's table in church
During the service, the priest placed the chalice and bread on the Lord's table.
Fresh flowers were arranged beside the Lord's table each Sunday.
Members of the congregation approached the Lord's table to receive communion.
