visual area

noun
cortical region processing visual information
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒu.əl ˈeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a region of the cerebral cortex that receives and processes visual information from the eyes, particularly from the lateral geniculate body of the thalamus

Examples

brain visual area
Damage to the visual area can result in partial or complete loss of vision.
Researchers mapped the activity of the visual area in response to light stimuli.
The primary visual area is crucial for interpreting images transmitted from the retina.
