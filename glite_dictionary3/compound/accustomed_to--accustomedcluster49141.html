accustomed to

adjective
familiar with; adapted to
Pronunciation: / əˈkʌstəmd tu / (AmE), / əˈkʌstəmd tʊ / (BrE)
familiar with something so that it seems normal or usual; in the habit of or adapted to.

Examples

accustomed to cold weather
After several weeks, she became accustomed to the cold weather.
I am accustomed to waking up early for work every day.
Travelers are often accustomed to different customs and foods in new countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unaccustomed to: not familiar with or not adapted to something
  • synonym – used to: familiar with or adapted to something through experience
