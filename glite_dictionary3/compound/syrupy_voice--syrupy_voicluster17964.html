syrupy voice

noun
excessively sweet or sentimental voice
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəpi vɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪrəpi vɔɪs / (BrE)
a voice that is excessively sweet, sentimental, or ingratiating

Examples

a syrupy voice charms listeners
Despite his syrupy voice, his words were surprisingly harsh.
She greeted us in such a syrupy voice that it sounded insincere.
The radio host's syrupy voice made every announcement sound overly sentimental.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gruff voice: a rough, low, and harsh-sounding voice
  • synonym – honeyed voice: a voice that sounds pleasingly sweet
