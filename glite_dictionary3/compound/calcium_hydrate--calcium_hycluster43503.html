calcium hydrate

noun
a white, caustic chemical compound (slaked lime)
Pronunciation: / ˈkælsiəm ˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælsiəm ˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
a white, caustic, crystalline substance (Ca(OH)₂) formed when calcium oxide (quicklime) reacts with water; used in building and agriculture

Examples

calcium hydrate in cement
Agricultural lime is often composed of calcium hydrate to help neutralize acidic soils.
Exposure to calcium hydrate can be dangerous, so proper protective gear is recommended.
The builder mixed calcium hydrate with sand and water to make mortar for the bricks.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – quicklime: calcium oxide, which reacts with water to form calcium hydrate
