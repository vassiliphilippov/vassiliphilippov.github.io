water bug

noun
aquatic true bug
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
A large true bug that lives in or on the surface of water, adapted to aquatic life.

Examples

pond water bug
At the pond, I noticed a water bug gliding across the surface with its long legs.
Some species of water bug can deliver a painful bite if handled carelessly.
The children were fascinated by the large water bug swimming just beneath the water.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – giant water bug: a very large aquatic bug of the family Belostomatidae
  • synonym – pond skater: an aquatic bug that skates on the surface of water (Gerridae)
