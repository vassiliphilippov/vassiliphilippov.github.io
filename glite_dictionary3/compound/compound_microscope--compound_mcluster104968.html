compound microscope

noun
microscope with two lens systems
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpaʊnd ˈmaɪ.krəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / kəmˈpaʊnd ˈmaɪ.krə.skəʊp / (BrE)
A microscope that uses two sets of lenses, the objective and the eyepiece, to magnify small objects with transmitted light.

Examples

compound microscope in a laboratory
A compound microscope can magnify objects up to 1,000 times, making it essential in laboratory research.
In biology class, each student received a compound microscope to study plant cell structures.
The scientist examined the blood sample under a compound microscope to observe the red blood cells in detail.
