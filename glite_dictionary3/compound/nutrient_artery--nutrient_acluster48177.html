nutrient artery

noun
artery supplying bone marrow
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtriənt ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.tri.ənt ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the medullary cavity of a long bone

Examples

nutrient artery of bone
Damage to the nutrient artery can impair bone healing after a fracture.
During surgery, care was taken not to injure the nutrient artery supplying the femur.
The nutrient artery enters the bone through a small opening called the nutrient foramen.
