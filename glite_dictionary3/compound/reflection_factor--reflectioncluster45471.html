reflection factor

noun
fraction of energy reflected from a surface
Pronunciation: / rəˈflɛk.ʃən ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛk.ʃən ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
The fraction of incoming radiant energy that is reflected from a surface.

Examples

reflection factor of a surface
Due to its low reflection factor, the material absorbed most of the sunlight.
Scientists calculated the reflection factor to determine how much energy was retained by the surface.
The reflection factor of the mirror was measured to ensure maximum brightness in the room.

  • synonym – reflectance: the proportion of light or other radiation striking a surface which is reflected off it
