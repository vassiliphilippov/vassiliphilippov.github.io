stable attribution

noun
psychological explanation with unchanging cause
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪbəl ˌætrəˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəl ˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
A psychological explanation in which the cause of an event or behavior is seen as consistent and unchanging over time or situations.

Examples

stable attribution of personality
People may make a stable attribution if they think someone's kindness is a lasting trait, not just a one-off act.
The teacher's stable attribution of Anna's success meant she believed Anna would always excel in math.
When failure is explained by a stable attribution, such as low ability, it can discourage future effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unstable attribution: an attribution that is seen as changeable or temporary
