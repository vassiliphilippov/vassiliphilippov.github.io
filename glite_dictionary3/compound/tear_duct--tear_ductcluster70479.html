tear duct

noun
duct carrying tears from eye gland
Pronunciation: / tɪr dʌkt / (AmE), / tɪə dʌkt / (BrE)
a small tube that carries tears from the lacrimal gland to the surface of the eye

Examples

blocked tear duct
Babies are sometimes born with a narrow tear duct that resolves on its own.
The doctor examined her tear duct for signs of infection.
When a tear duct is blocked, your eye may water excessively.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lacrimal duct: the duct that conveys tears from the lacrimal gland to the eye
  • synonym – tear gland: another term for lacrimal gland, which produces tears
