nervous impulse

noun
electrical signal in a nerve fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs ˈɪmpʌls / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ˈɪmpʌls / (BrE)
an electrical signal that travels along a nerve fiber, allowing the transmission of information within the nervous system

Examples

A nervous impulse travels rapidly along the axon to reach the next neuron.
Certain drugs can block the passage of a nervous impulse and prevent muscle contraction.
