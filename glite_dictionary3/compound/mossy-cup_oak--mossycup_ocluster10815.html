mossy-cup oak

noun
a North American oak tree with large fringed acorn cups
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑsi ˌkʌp ˌoʊk / (AmE), / ˈmɒsi ˌkʌp ˌəʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous oak tree of central and eastern North America, known for its ovoid acorns that are deeply set in large, fringed cups; its wood is tough and close-grained.

Examples

a mossy-cup oak tree
Carpenters value the wood of the mossy-cup oak for its strength and durability.
Many parks in the Midwest feature towering mossy-cup oak trees along their trails.
The mossy-cup oak is easily identified by its large fringed acorn cups.
