open-heart surgery

noun
surgical operation on the heart with the chest opened
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˌhɑrt ˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˌhɑːt ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which the chest is opened and surgery is performed on the heart, often involving stopping the heart and using a heart-lung machine.

Examples

open-heart surgery to repair heart
He underwent open-heart surgery last year to repair a defective valve.
Recovery from open-heart surgery can take several weeks, depending on the patient's condition.
The success rate for open-heart surgery has greatly improved in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bypass surgery: surgery to create a new route for blood flow to the heart
