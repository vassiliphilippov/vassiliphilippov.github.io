ichneumon fly

noun
a parasitic wasp-like insect
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈnuːmən flaɪ / (AmE), / ɪkˈnjuːmən flaɪ / (BrE)
A wasp-like insect of the family Ichneumonidae, whose larvae are parasitic on caterpillars and other insect larvae.

Examples

ichneumon fly lays eggs
An ichneumon fly was hovering over the rose bush, searching for a suitable host.
Gardeners appreciate the presence of the ichneumon fly because it helps control pest populations.
The ichneumon fly lays its eggs inside the caterpillar, where the larvae will develop.
