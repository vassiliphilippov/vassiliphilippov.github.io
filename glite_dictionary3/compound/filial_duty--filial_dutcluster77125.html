filial duty

noun
child's duty to parents
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪliəl ˈduːti / (AmE), / ˈfɪliəl ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
the duty or obligation that a child has towards their parents, especially in terms of respect, care, and support

Examples

filial duty to parents
He felt a strong sense of filial duty and cared for his mother in her old age.
Many cultures emphasize the importance of filial duty toward elderly parents.
Neglecting one's filial duty is often frowned upon in traditional societies.
