Vichy regime

noun
France's WWII collaborationist government
Pronunciation: / ˈviːʃi rəˈʒim / (AmE), / ˈviːʃi rɛˈʒiːm / (BrE)
the French government that ruled from 1940 to 1944 during World War II after France's defeat by Germany, collaborating with Nazi authorities

Examples

the Vichy regime in France
Many French citizens opposed the Vichy regime during its rule in World War II.
The Vichy regime collaborated with Nazi Germany, causing controversy for decades afterward.
The policies of the Vichy regime have been widely debated by historians.
