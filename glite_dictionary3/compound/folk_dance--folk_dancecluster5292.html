to folk dance

verb
to perform a traditional regional dance
Pronunciation: / foʊk dæns / (AmE), / fəʊk dɑːns / (BrE)
to perform a traditional dance originating from the customs of a particular region or community

Examples

to folk dance at festival
Every summer, villagers gather in the square to folk dance in celebration of the harvest.
She learned to folk dance while living in Greece for a year.
The children will folk dance during the cultural festival next week.
