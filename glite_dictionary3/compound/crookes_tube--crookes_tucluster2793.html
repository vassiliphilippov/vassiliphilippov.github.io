Crookes tube

noun
early gas-discharge cathode-ray tube
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʊks tuːb / (AmE), / ˈkrʊks tjuːb / (BrE)
an early type of gas-discharge tube, used to discover cathode rays, consisting of a partially evacuated glass tube through which an electric current is passed

Examples

a Crookes tube experiment
A physics museum displayed an original Crookes tube used in 19th-century research.
Early experiments with the Crookes tube led to breakthroughs in understanding electrical conduction through gases.
The Crookes tube played a crucial role in the discovery of the electron.
