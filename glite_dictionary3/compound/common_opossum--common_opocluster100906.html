common opossum

noun
a marsupial mammal known for playing dead
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən əˈpɑsəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən əˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
an omnivorous marsupial (Didelphis marsupialis) of the eastern United States and Central and South America, known for feigning death when threatened; sometimes used as food in local cuisines

Examples

common opossum climbs trees
Some communities in the South use common opossum as a traditional source of meat.
The common opossum is often seen foraging at night near rural homes.
When threatened, a common opossum can feign death as a defense mechanism.
