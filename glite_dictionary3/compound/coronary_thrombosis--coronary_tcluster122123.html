coronary thrombosis

noun
blockage of coronary artery by clot
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrəˌneri θrʌmˈboʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrənəri θrɒmˈbəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the blockage of a coronary artery by a blood clot, often leading to a heart attack

Examples

suffer a coronary thrombosis
A coronary thrombosis can quickly cut off blood supply to the heart muscle, causing damage.
After suffering a coronary thrombosis, she was placed on medication to prevent further clots.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with a coronary thrombosis after he experienced severe chest pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – myocardial infarction: a heart attack, often caused by coronary thrombosis
