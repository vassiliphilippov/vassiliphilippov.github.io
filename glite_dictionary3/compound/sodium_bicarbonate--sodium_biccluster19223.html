sodium bicarbonate

noun
white, soluble chemical used in baking and medicine
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm baɪˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm baɪˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white, soluble chemical compound (NaHCO₃) commonly used in baking, making effervescent drinks, and as an antacid to relieve heartburn or indigestion

Examples

sodium bicarbonate in baking
Sodium bicarbonate is often used in science experiments to produce carbon dioxide gas.
Adding sodium bicarbonate to water can relieve minor indigestion.
The recipe calls for a teaspoon of sodium bicarbonate to help the cake rise.
