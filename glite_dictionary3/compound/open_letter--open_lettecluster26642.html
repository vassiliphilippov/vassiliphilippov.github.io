open letter

noun
public letter intended for wide audience
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a letter addressed to a particular person but intended to be read by the general public, often published as a form of protest or to draw attention to an issue.

Examples

publish an open letter
An open letter published in the newspaper criticized the company's labor practices.
She posted an open letter online to address the misinformation being spread about her work.
The activist wrote an open letter to the mayor demanding urgent action on climate change.

Related Terms

  • synonym – letter: a written or printed message addressed to a person or organization
