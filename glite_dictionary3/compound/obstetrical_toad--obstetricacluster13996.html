obstetrical toad

noun
European toad with egg-carrying male
Pronunciation: / əbˈstɛtrɪkəl toʊd / (AmE), / əbˈstɛtrɪkəl təʊd / (BrE)
A small European toad (Alytes obstetricans) in which the male carries fertilized eggs wrapped around its hind legs until they hatch.

Examples

obstetrical toad carries eggs
Researchers observed a male obstetrical toad carefully guarding its developing eggs.
The obstetrical toad is known for its unusual breeding behavior, with males carrying eggs on their legs.
Unlike most amphibians, the obstetrical toad father's role involves protecting the offspring until they hatch.
