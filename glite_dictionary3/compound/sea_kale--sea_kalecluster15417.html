sea kale

noun
coastal plant with edible leaves
Pronunciation: / siː keɪl / (AmE), / siː keɪl / (BrE)
a perennial plant (Crambe maritima) native to coastal regions of northern Europe, with thick blue-green leaves and clusters of small white flowers, sometimes cultivated as a vegetable

Examples

sea kale leaves
Sea kale grows wild along many beaches in northern Europe and is valued for its edible shoots.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate sea kale for its decorative foliage and tasty roots.
The thick, fleshy leaves of sea kale can be cooked and eaten like spinach.
