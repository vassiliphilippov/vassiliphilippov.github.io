present progressive tense

noun
verb tense for ongoing actions
Pronunciation: / ˈprezənt prəˈɡrɛsɪv tɛns / (AmE), / ˈprezənt prəˈɡrɛsɪv tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that is ongoing at the time of speaking

Examples

present progressive tense in a sentence
'They are eating dinner' uses the present progressive tense to show the action is happening now.
In English, the present progressive tense is often formed with 'am', 'is', or 'are' plus a verb ending in -ing.
She is reading a book right now, which is an example of the present progressive tense.
