sudden amnesia

noun
unexpected loss of memory
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌdn æmˈniʒə / (AmE), / ˈsʌd(ə)n amˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person unexpectedly loses their memory, often for a short period and without warning.

Examples

experience sudden amnesia
After the accident, she experienced sudden amnesia and could not remember her name.
Doctors were puzzled by the patient's sudden amnesia following the stressful event.
The film's plot revolves around a detective who suffers from sudden amnesia.
