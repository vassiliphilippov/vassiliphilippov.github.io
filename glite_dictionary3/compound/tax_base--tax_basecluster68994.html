tax base

noun
total value of taxable assets or income
Pronunciation: / tæks beɪs / (AmE), / tæks beɪs / (BrE)
the collective value of assets, income, or transactions that are subject to taxation by a government.

Examples

broad tax base
A narrow tax base makes it difficult for governments to raise sufficient funds for public spending.
Efforts to formalize small businesses can help broaden the tax base and increase public revenue.
The country expanded its tax base by including digital services in its revenue regulations.
