Federal Reserve note

noun
U.S. paper currency issued by the central bank
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜrv noʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl rɪˈzɜːv nəʊt / (BrE)
A piece of paper currency issued by the United States Federal Reserve, serving as legal tender in the country.

Examples

Federal Reserve note ten dollars
Every Federal Reserve note carries a unique serial number and the signature of the Treasurer of the United States.
He paid for the meal with a crisp Federal Reserve note.
Tourists often exchange their home currency for Federal Reserve notes when visiting America.
