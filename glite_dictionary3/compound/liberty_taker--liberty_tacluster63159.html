liberty taker

noun
person who restricts others' freedoms
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbɚti ˌteɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti ˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person who infringes upon or restricts the freedoms of others, especially by imposing unjust rules or limitations

Examples

liberty taker imposes rules
Many citizens viewed the new leader as a liberty taker who cared little for personal rights.
She accused her critics of being liberty takers for their attempts to censor free speech.
The protesters rallied against any liberty taker who threatened their civil liberties.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dictator: a ruler who has total power, often taken and used in an oppressive way
  • antonym – liberator: a person who sets others free, in contrast to one who restricts their freedom
  • synonym – oppressor: a person who exercises unjust authority or power over others
