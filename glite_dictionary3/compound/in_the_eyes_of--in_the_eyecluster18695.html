in the eyes of

preposition
according to someone's opinion or view
Pronunciation: / ɪn ði ˈaɪz əv / (AmE), / ɪn ði ˈaɪz ɒv / (BrE)
according to the opinion, judgment, or perspective of someone; as viewed by someone

Examples

in the eyes of the law
In the eyes of the law, the defendant was guilty despite his protests.
She was a hero in the eyes of her children for her bravery.
The decision was justified in the eyes of many critics, though some strongly disagreed.
