Carnot's ideal cycle

noun
theoretical thermodynamic cycle
Pronunciation: / kɑrˌnoʊz aɪˈdiəl ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / kɑːˌnɒts aɪˈdiːəl ˈsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
A theoretical thermodynamic cycle consisting of a sequence of reversible processes that defines the maximum possible efficiency of a heat engine.

Examples

Carnot's ideal cycle in thermodynamics
In a Carnot's ideal cycle, all processes are reversible and no energy is lost as heat.
Students often study the Carnot's ideal cycle to understand the fundamental limits of heat engines.
The efficiency of any real engine can never exceed that of the Carnot's ideal cycle.
