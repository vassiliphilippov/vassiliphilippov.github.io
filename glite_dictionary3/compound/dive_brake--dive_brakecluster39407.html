dive brake

noun
aircraft device to reduce speed in descent
Pronunciation: / daɪv breɪk / (AmE), / daɪv breɪk / (BrE)
a hinged flap or small parachute used on an aircraft to increase drag and reduce speed, especially during steep descents.

Examples

dive brake on aircraft
Older bombers were often equipped with a special dive brake for steep attack maneuvers.
The pilot deployed the dive brake to control the jet's descent speed.
Without a functioning dive brake, the glider would descend too quickly.
