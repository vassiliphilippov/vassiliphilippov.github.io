amino acid

noun
organic compound with amino and carboxylic groups
Pronunciation: / əˈminoʊ ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəʊ ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
Any of a class of organic compounds containing both an amino group (–NH2) and a carboxylic acid group (–COOH); amino acids are the building blocks of proteins.

Examples

amino acid in proteins
Each amino acid in a protein sequence helps determine the protein’s function.
Scientists study how changes in amino acid composition affect enzyme activity.
The human body can synthesize some amino acids, but others must come from food.
