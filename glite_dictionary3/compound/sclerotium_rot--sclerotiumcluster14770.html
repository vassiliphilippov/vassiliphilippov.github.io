sclerotium rot

noun
fungal plant disease (Sclerotium genus)
Pronunciation: / skləˈroʊtiəm rɑt / (AmE), / skləˈrəʊtiəm rɒt / (BrE)
A plant disease caused by fungi of the genus Sclerotium, characterized by the formation of hard, resistant structures called sclerotia.

Examples

sclerotium rot damages crops
Farmers took extra precautions after a recent outbreak of sclerotium rot devastated the tomato fields.
Sclerotium rolfsii is a common pathogen responsible for sclerotium rot in a wide variety of crops.
The laboratory confirmed that the symptoms on the potatoes were caused by sclerotium rot.
