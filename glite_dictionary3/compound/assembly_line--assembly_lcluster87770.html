assembly line

noun
factory system for sequential production
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛmbli laɪn / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbli laɪn / (BrE)
a system in a factory where a product is progressively assembled as it moves between workstations, each performing a specific operation

Examples

car assembly line
The company's new assembly line allowed them to produce cars much more efficiently.
The invention of the assembly line revolutionized the manufacturing industry.
Workers on the assembly line perform the same task repeatedly as products move past them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – production line: a series of workers and machines in a factory, each doing a part of the manufacturing process
