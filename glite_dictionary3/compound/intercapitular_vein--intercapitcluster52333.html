intercapitular vein

noun
vein connecting veins in hand or foot
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkæpɪtjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈpɪtjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
A vein that connects the dorsal and palmar veins of the hand or the dorsal and plantar veins of the foot.

Examples

intercapitular vein joins veins
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the intercapitular vein.
Swelling in the foot may sometimes indicate an issue with the intercapitular vein.
The intercapitular vein plays a crucial role in venous drainage of the hand.
