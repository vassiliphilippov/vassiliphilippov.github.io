renal artery

noun
artery supplying the kidneys
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.nəl ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈriː.nəl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery that branches off the abdominal aorta and supplies blood to the kidneys, adrenal glands, and ureters

Examples

renal artery blockage
A blockage in the renal artery can lead to high blood pressure and kidney damage.
Each kidney is supplied by a separate renal artery branching from the abdominal aorta.
The surgeon carefully located the renal artery before proceeding with the kidney transplant.

Related Terms

  • antonym – renal vein: the vein draining blood from the kidney
