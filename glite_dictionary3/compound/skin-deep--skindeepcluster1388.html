skin-deep

adjective
superficial; shallow; only at surface
Pronunciation: / skɪnˈdip / (AmE), / skɪnˈdiːp / (BrE)
superficial; only on the surface and not deep or lasting

Examples

skin-deep beauty
He realized his understanding of the topic was merely skin-deep after failing the test.
Her beauty was only skin-deep, with little kindness behind her smile.
The cut was skin-deep and did not require stitches.
Her apparent confidence was only skin-deep and faded under pressure.
The politician's concern for the poor was merely skin-deep and lacked real action.
Their friendship proved to be skin-deep when they argued over money.

Related Terms

  • antonym – profound: very great or intense; going deep
  • synonym – superficial: not thorough or deep; existing on the surface
