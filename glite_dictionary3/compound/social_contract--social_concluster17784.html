social contract

noun
theory of agreement forming society
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ˈkɑnˌtrækt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃl ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
an implicit agreement among the members of a society to cooperate for social benefits, often involving the surrender of some individual freedoms in exchange for protection and order

Examples

the social contract theory
According to the social contract, people give up some freedoms to ensure security and political order.
Critics argue that the social contract is not always fair to all members of society.
Many philosophers, including Rousseau and Hobbes, have written about the concept of the social contract.

  • synonym – tacit agreement: implicit or unspoken agreement
