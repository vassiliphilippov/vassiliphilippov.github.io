American frontier

noun
the western U.S. during its frontier period
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən frənˈtɪr / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən frʌnˈtɪə / (BrE)
the western part of the United States during the period of westward expansion, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries, characterized by settlement and exploration.

Examples

life on the American frontier
Life on the American frontier was often harsh and unpredictable, requiring perseverance and courage.
Many pioneers traveled westward in search of opportunity on the American frontier.
The expansion of the American frontier played a significant role in shaping U.S. history.

  • antonym – settled territory: land in the U.S. that was organized and no longer considered frontier
