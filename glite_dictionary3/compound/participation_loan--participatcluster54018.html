participation loan

noun
loan shared by multiple banks
Pronunciation: / pɑːrtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən loʊn / (AmE), / pɑːtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən ləʊn / (BrE)
a loan that is provided by a group of banks that join together to fund an amount too large for any one of them alone

Examples

participation loan from several banks
A participation loan allowed the risk of lending to be spread among several financial institutions.
Due to the project's size, the company needed a participation loan involving multiple lenders.
The developer secured a participation loan from three regional banks to finance the new shopping center.
