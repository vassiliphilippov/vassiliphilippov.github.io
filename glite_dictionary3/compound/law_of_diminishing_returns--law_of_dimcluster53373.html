law of diminishing returns

noun
principle: smaller gains after certain point
Pronunciation: / lɔː əv dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋ rɪˈtɜrns / (AmE), / lɔː əv dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋ rɪˈtɜːnz / (BrE)
the principle stating that after a certain point, adding more input will result in smaller increases of output or effectiveness

Examples

the law of diminishing returns in work
After a few hours of studying, the law of diminishing returns set in and her progress slowed significantly.
Even in business, the law of diminishing returns applies if extra funding stops producing extra results.
Farmers experience the law of diminishing returns when adding more fertilizer doesn't yield better crops.
