siderochrestic anaemia

noun
type of refractory anemia with sideroblasts
Pronunciation: / saɪdɚ.oʊˈkrɛstɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / saɪdə.rəʊˈkrɛstɪk əˈniː.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
A refractory type of anemia marked by the presence of sideroblasts—abnormal red blood cell precursors containing iron—in the bone marrow.

Examples

siderochrestic anaemia diagnosis
Doctors found that his chronic fatigue was caused by siderochrestic anaemia, which did not respond to standard iron therapy.
In cases of siderochrestic anaemia, iron accumulates in red blood cell precursors instead of being properly incorporated into hemoglobin.
The patient was diagnosed with siderochrestic anaemia after bone marrow tests revealed abnormal sideroblasts.
