to had better

auxiliary verb
advice or recommendation with a warning
Pronunciation: / hæd ˈbɛtər / (AmE), / hæd ˈbetə / (BrE)
used to advise or recommend that someone should do something, often implying a warning or undesirable result if not followed

Examples

you had better hurry
Students had better finish their assignments before the deadline.
With the weather turning bad, you had better bring an umbrella.
You had better call your parents to let them know you arrived safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ought to: used to indicate duty or correctness
  • synonym – should: used to indicate obligation or a suggestion
