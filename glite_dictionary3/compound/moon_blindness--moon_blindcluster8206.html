moon blindness

noun
impaired night vision due to retinal disorder or vitamin A deficiency
Pronunciation: / mun ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / muːn ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
A condition involving partial or total loss of vision, especially in dim light, often caused by a deficiency of vitamin A or by a retinal disorder; observed in both humans and animals.

Examples

moon blindness in dim light
Farmers have long recognized moon blindness as a serious threat to livestock health.
The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with moon blindness after it started stumbling at dusk.
Vitamin A deficiency can sometimes lead to moon blindness in cattle or even in humans.
