Seward's Folly

noun
the U.S. purchase of Alaska from Russia in 1867
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːərdz ˈfɑːli / (AmE), / ˈsjuːədz ˈfɒli / (BrE)
the 1867 purchase of Alaska from Russia by the United States, negotiated by Secretary of State William H. Seward, originally considered foolish by many Americans
