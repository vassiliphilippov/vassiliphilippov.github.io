going ashore

noun
disembarking from a boat or ship
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊɪŋ əˈʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
the act of leaving a boat or ship and stepping onto land

Examples

going ashore after sailing
After a long journey, the crew looked forward to going ashore and exploring the town.
Passengers were required to assemble before going ashore at the next port.
Strict procedures are followed whenever tourists are going ashore from the cruise ship.
