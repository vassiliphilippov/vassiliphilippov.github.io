safety factor

noun
engineering ratio for structural safety
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti ˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌfæktə / (BrE)
The ratio of the maximum load a structure or material can withstand to the expected load it will face in normal use, used to ensure safety.

Examples

safety factor of a bridge
Engineers increased the safety factor of the bridge to ensure it could withstand heavy traffic loads.
It is standard practice to include a safety factor when designing pressure vessels for industrial use.
The building code requires a minimum safety factor for all support beams to prevent structural failure.
