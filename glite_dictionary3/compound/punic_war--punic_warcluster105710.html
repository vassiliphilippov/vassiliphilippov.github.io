Punic War

proper noun
wars between Rome and Carthage (264–146 BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈpjunɪk wɔr / (AmE), / ˈpjuːnɪk wɔː / (BrE)
any of the three wars fought between Rome and Carthage between 264 and 146 BC, resulting in the destruction of Carthage and Roman dominance over the western Mediterranean.

Examples

the Punic War shaped history
He wrote his thesis on the economic consequences of the Punic War for Carthage.
Many students find the Punic War a fascinating topic in ancient history.
The rivalry between Rome and Carthage during the Punic War shaped the future of the Mediterranean region.
