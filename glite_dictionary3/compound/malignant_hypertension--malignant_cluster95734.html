malignant hypertension

noun
severe, rapidly progressing high blood pressure
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡ.nənt haɪ.pərˈten.ʃən / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡ.nənt haɪ.pəˈten.ʃən / (BrE)
A severe and rapidly progressing form of high blood pressure that causes damage to the inner linings of blood vessels and various organs such as the heart, kidneys, and brain.

Examples

severe malignant hypertension
Doctors monitor blood pressure closely in patients at risk for malignant hypertension.
The patient was hospitalized urgently due to symptoms of malignant hypertension.
Untreated malignant hypertension can quickly result in organ failure and death.
