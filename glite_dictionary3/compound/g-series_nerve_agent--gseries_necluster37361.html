G-series nerve agent

noun
class of early nerve agents
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒi ˌsɪriːz ˈnɜrv ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː ˌsɪəriːz ˈnɜːv ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a group of toxic nerve agents first synthesized before or during World War II, including tabun, sarin, and soman, characterized by high toxicity and rapid action
