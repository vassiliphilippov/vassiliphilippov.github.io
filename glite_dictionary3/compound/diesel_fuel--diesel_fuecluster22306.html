diesel fuel

noun
fuel for diesel engines
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːzəl ˌfjul / (AmE), / ˈdiːzəl ˌfjuːl / (BrE)
A heavy mineral oil used as fuel in diesel engines, especially in vehicles and machinery.

Examples

diesel fuel for trucks
Due to rising prices, the cost of diesel fuel has become a major concern for shipping companies.
Modern trains often rely on diesel fuel to power their engines across the countryside.
The truck runs on diesel fuel instead of gasoline, making it more efficient for long hauls.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gasoline: a light fuel for spark-ignition engines, such as in most cars
  • antonym – petrol: British term for gasoline, a different (lighter) fuel for engines
