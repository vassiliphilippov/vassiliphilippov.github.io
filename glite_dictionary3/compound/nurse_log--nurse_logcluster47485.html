nurse log

noun
decaying tree trunk that nurtures new growth
Pronunciation: / nɝs lɑɡ / (AmE), / nɜːs lɒɡ / (BrE)
A fallen, decaying tree trunk that provides nutrients and support for new plants, fungi, and insects to grow, especially in forests.

Examples

seedlings on nurse log
Many seedlings germinated from the moist, decaying surface of the nurse log.
Moss and fungi thrived on the nurse log, creating a rich environment for new plants.
The ecologist explained how a nurse log supports biodiversity in old-growth forests.
