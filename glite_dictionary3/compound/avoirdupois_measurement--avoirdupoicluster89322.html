avoirdupois measurement

noun
system of weights based on a 16-ounce pound
Pronunciation: / ˌævərdəˈpɔɪz ˈmɛʒərmənt / (AmE), / ˌavədjuːˈpɔɪz ˈmɛʒəmənt / (BrE)
a system of weights based on a pound divided into 16 ounces, commonly used in the United States and formerly in Britain for most goods except precious metals, gemstones, and medicines

Examples

avoirdupois measurement in pounds
Chemists usually avoid avoirdupois measurement when working with medicines, preferring the metric system.
One pound in avoirdupois measurement is equal to 16 ounces.
The United States still relies on the avoirdupois measurement system for everyday goods.
