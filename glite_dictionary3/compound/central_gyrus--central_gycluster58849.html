central gyrus

noun
ridge of brain near central sulcus
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl ˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈdʒɪərəs / (BrE)
either of two ridges (gyri) of the brain located on either side of the central sulcus, separating the frontal and parietal lobes

Examples

the central gyrus of brain
Damage to the central gyrus can affect movement or sensation depending on the area involved.
Neurosurgeons carefully avoid the central gyrus when operating near the central sulcus.
The central gyrus plays a crucial role in processing motor and sensory information.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – postcentral gyrus: the gyrus located immediately behind the central sulcus, associated with sensory processing
  • degree lower – precentral gyrus: the gyrus located immediately in front of the central sulcus, involved in voluntary motor control
