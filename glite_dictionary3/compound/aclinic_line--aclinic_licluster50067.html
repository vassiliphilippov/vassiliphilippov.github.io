aclinic line

noun
line of zero magnetic dip near equator
Pronunciation: / əˈklɪnɪk laɪn / (AmE), / əˈklɪnɪk laɪn / (BrE)
An imaginary line around the Earth parallel to the equator, along which a magnetic needle remains horizontal, showing no inclination or dip.

Examples

aclinic line on a map
As you cross the aclinic line, the angle of dip in a magnetic needle changes direction.
Early geophysicists mapped the aclinic line to study Earth's magnetic field.
The aclinic line marks the part of the Earth where a compass needle shows no vertical inclination.
