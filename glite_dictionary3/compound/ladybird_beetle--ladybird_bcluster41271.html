ladybird beetle

noun
small, spotted insect; feeds on pests
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪdiˌbɝd ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪbɜːd ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
A small, round beetle with bright colors and spots, commonly known for feeding on aphids and other pest insects.

Examples

spotted ladybird beetle
A ladybird beetle can eat hundreds of aphids in a single day, making it a farmer’s friend.
Children happily watched a bright red ladybird beetle crawl across the leaf.
Gardeners often encourage ladybird beetles to control pests without using chemicals.
