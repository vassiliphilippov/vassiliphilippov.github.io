cartilaginous tube

noun
duct or channel with cartilaginous walls
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrtl̩ˈædʒɪnəs tuːb / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtɪˈlædʒɪnəs tjuːb / (BrE)
a duct or channel in the body with walls made of cartilage, such as the trachea or part of the ear canal

Examples

cartilaginous tube in the trachea
In fish, the gills are supported by a cartilaginous tube structure.
The external auditory canal is partially formed by a cartilaginous tube.
The trachea is a cartilaginous tube that connects the larynx to the lungs.
