to direct traffic

verb
manage the flow of vehicles
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
to manage or control the flow of vehicles, especially at intersections or during an emergency or disruption

Examples

to direct traffic at an intersection
After the power outage, a police officer stood in the intersection to direct traffic.
Construction workers had to direct traffic around the closed lane during rush hour.
When the lights malfunctioned, the teacher volunteered to direct traffic in the school parking lot.
