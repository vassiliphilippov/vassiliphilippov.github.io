knight banneret

noun
knight with special honors and rank
Pronunciation: / naɪt ˌbænəˈrɛt / (AmE), / naɪt ˌbænəˈrɛt / (BrE)
A knight who was honored for bravery and given the right to display a square banner, often holding a higher command than regular knights.

Examples

knight banneret leads troops
As a knight banneret, he had the privilege of leading his own squadron under his personal banner.
Many stories of the Middle Ages recount the exploits of the knight banneret in service to the crown.
The king bestowed the title of knight banneret upon him after his courageous deeds in battle.
