detention house

noun
juvenile offenders' temporary institution
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɛnʃən haʊs / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən haʊs / (BrE)
An institution where juvenile offenders are held temporarily, usually under the supervision of a juvenile court.

Examples

juvenile detention house
After his arrest, the boy was sent to a detention house until his court date.
Many detention houses offer educational programs to help rehabilitate young offenders.
