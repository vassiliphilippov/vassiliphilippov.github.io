early childhood

noun
period from birth to about age six
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrli ˈtʃaɪldhʊd / (AmE), / ˈɜːli ˈtʃaɪldhʊd / (BrE)
the period of a person's life from birth to around six or seven years old, marked by rapid physical, cognitive, and emotional development

Examples

early childhood education
Access to quality education in early childhood can improve a child's future opportunities.
In early childhood, children rapidly acquire language and social skills.
Many lifelong habits are formed during early childhood, making these years critical for development.
