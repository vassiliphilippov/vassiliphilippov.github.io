little-leaf fig

noun
Australian ornamental tree; like a banyan
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtəl ˌlif fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˌliːf fɪɡ / (BrE)
An Australian tree resembling the banyan, often grown for ornament or brushwood and introduced to South Africa.

Examples

little-leaf fig tree
Gardeners in South Africa plant the little-leaf fig as a fast-growing source of brushwood.
The little-leaf fig is commonly used in landscaping for its dense foliage.
Unlike the common fig, the little-leaf fig has smaller leaves and a more compact growth habit.
