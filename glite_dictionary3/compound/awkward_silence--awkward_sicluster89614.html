awkward silence

noun
an uncomfortable pause in conversation
Pronunciation: / ˈɔkwɚd ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwəd ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
an uncomfortable or tense pause in conversation, often leaving participants feeling uneasy

Examples

an awkward silence after a question
An awkward silence filled the room when no one knew how to respond.
She laughed nervously to break the awkward silence.
There was an awkward silence after he asked the personal question.

  • synonym – dead air: a period of unintended silence during a broadcast or conversation
