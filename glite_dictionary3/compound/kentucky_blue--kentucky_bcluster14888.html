Kentucky blue

noun
meadow and pasture grass species
Pronunciation: / kənˈtʌki bluː / (AmE), / kənˈtʌki bluː / (BrE)
A species of valuable meadow and pasture grass, known for its tall stalks and slender bright green leaves, widely used in lawns and pastures, especially in the central United States.

Examples

Kentucky blue grass lawns
Many golf courses across the Midwest are sown with Kentucky blue for its resilience and lush appearance.
Our backyard lawn, filled with Kentucky blue, stays green throughout the summer.
The farmer reseeded his pasture with Kentucky blue to improve grazing conditions for his cattle.
