trick question

noun
misleading or deceptive question
Pronunciation: / trɪk ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / trɪk ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
A question that is intended to mislead or deceive, often by having a confusing or unexpected answer.

Examples

a trick question in a test
Be careful—sometimes there’s a trick question in the survey to test your attention.
She saw right through the trick question and answered confidently.
That history quiz had a trick question that confused nearly everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gotcha question: a question designed to catch someone off guard or reveal a mistake
