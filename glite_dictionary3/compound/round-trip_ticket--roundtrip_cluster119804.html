round-trip ticket

noun
ticket for both outbound and return journeys
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊnd tɹɪp ˈtɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd trɪp ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
a ticket that allows a journey to a destination and a return to the starting point, usually using the same route

Examples

buy a round-trip ticket
A round-trip ticket usually costs less than two one-way tickets.
Do you prefer buying a round-trip ticket or booking each way separately?
She purchased a round-trip ticket to Paris for her vacation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – one-way ticket: a ticket for travel to a destination without return
