abductor muscle

noun
muscle that moves body part away from midline
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʌktɚ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / əbˈdʌktə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
A muscle that moves a limb or other body part away from the midline of the body or from another part.

Examples

abductor muscle injury
Injury to the abductor muscle can affect a person's ability to move their arm sideways.
Rehabilitation exercises often focus on strengthening the abductor muscle after hip surgery.
The gluteus medius is an important abductor muscle of the hip.
