aspiration pneumonia

noun
pneumonia from inhaling material into the lungs
Pronunciation: / ˌæspəˈreɪʃən njuːˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌæspɪˈreɪʃən njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A type of pneumonia caused by inhaling foreign material, such as vomit, into the lungs, often during unconsciousness.

Examples

aspiration pneumonia after vomiting
After the surgery, the patient developed aspiration pneumonia due to vomit entering his lungs.
Elderly individuals are at higher risk of aspiration pneumonia if they have swallowing difficulties.
The doctor ordered a chest X-ray to confirm the diagnosis of aspiration pneumonia.

  • synonym – pneumonia: infection of the lungs causing inflammation and fluid
