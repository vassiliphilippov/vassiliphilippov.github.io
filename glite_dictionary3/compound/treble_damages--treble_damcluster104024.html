treble damages

noun
damages tripled by court (law)
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛb.əl ˈdæm.ɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrɛb.əl ˈdam.ɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
(in law) damages awarded by a court that are three times the actual loss suffered by the injured party.

Examples

treble damages for fraud
If the defendant is found guilty of fraud, the court may impose treble damages as a penalty.
The jury awarded treble damages to the plaintiff for the company's willful violations.
Under certain statutes, claimants can recover treble damages to deter unlawful conduct.
