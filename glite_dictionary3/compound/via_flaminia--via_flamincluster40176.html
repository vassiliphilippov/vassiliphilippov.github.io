Via Flaminia

proper noun
an ancient Roman road in Italy
Pronunciation: / ˌviː.ə fləˈmɪni.ə / (AmE), / ˌviː.ə flaˈmɪni.ə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman road in Italy, built by Gaius Flaminius in 220 BC, stretching north from Rome to Cisalpine Gaul
