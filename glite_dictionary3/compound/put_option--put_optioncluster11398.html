put option

noun
option contract to sell an asset
Pronunciation: / pʊt ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / pʊt ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
a financial contract that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell a specific asset at a set price within a specified period

Examples

buy a put option
Investors use a put option to protect themselves from significant losses in volatile markets.
She decided to buy a put option as a hedge against falling stock prices.
The value of the put option rose as the company's stock price declined.
If the stock price falls below the strike price, the put option gains value.
Many traders use put options as a way to speculate on downward movements in the market.
The investor purchased a put option to protect his portfolio against market declines.
