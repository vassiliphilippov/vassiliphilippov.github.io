high treason

noun
the crime of betraying one's country
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈtriːzən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈtriːzən / (BrE)
the crime of betraying one's country, especially by attempting to overthrow the government or harm the sovereign

Examples

commit high treason
Committing high treason has historically been punishable by death in some countries.
He was found guilty of high treason and sentenced to life imprisonment.
