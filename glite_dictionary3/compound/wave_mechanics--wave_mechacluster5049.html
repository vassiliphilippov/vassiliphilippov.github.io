wave mechanics

noun
quantum theory based on particle waves
Pronunciation: / weɪv məˈkænɪks / (AmE), / weɪv mɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
A branch of quantum theory in which atomic and subatomic events are explained as the interactions of particle waves, based on Schrödinger's equation.

Examples

wave mechanics in quantum theory
His research focuses on the applications of wave mechanics in quantum computing.
Students learn about Schrödinger's equation as a fundamental part of wave mechanics in their physics courses.
The development of wave mechanics revolutionized our understanding of atomic structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – quantum mechanics: the branch of mechanics that deals with the mathematical description of the motion and interaction of subatomic particles
