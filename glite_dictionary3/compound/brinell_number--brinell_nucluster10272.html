Brinell number

noun
measure of material hardness
Pronunciation: / brɪˈnɛl ˌnʌmbər / (AmE), / brɪˈnɛl ˌnʌmbə / (BrE)
A numerical measure of the hardness of a material, determined by the Brinell hardness test.

Examples

Brinell number of steel
Engineers use the Brinell number as a standard metric when comparing different metal alloys.
Manufacturers often specify the Brinell number to ensure consistent quality of materials used in production.
The steel sample was found to have a Brinell number of 250, indicating moderate hardness.

  • synonym – Rockwell number: numerical measure of hardness using the Rockwell test
  • synonym – Vickers hardness: measure of hardness using the Vickers test
