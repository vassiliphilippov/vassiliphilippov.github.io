selective jamming

noun
targeted electronic interference
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛktɪv ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate interference with a specific channel or frequency in electronic communications, as opposed to disrupting all channels

Examples

selective jamming of signals
Engineers developed new methods of selective jamming to counter increasingly sophisticated wireless networks.
Modern equipment can carry out selective jamming to block only specific frequencies used by opponents.
The military employed selective jamming to disrupt enemy radar without affecting their own communications.
