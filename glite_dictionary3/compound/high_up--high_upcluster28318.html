high up

adverb
at a great altitude or height
Pronunciation: / haɪ ʌp / (AmE), / haɪ ʌp / (BrE)
At a great height or altitude; far above the ground or a reference point.

Examples

high up on a building
From the rooftop, he could see the entire city spread out high up above the streets.
The birds were nesting high up in the cliffs where humans could not reach.
The hikers paused to rest high up on the mountain, enjoying the spectacular view.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aloft: in the air; at or to a great height
  • antonym – low down: at or near the ground or a reference point
