to seethe with anger

verb
to feel intense, suppressed anger
Pronunciation: / sið wɪð ˈæŋɚ / (AmE), / siːð wɪð ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
to be extremely angry in a way that is barely controlled or visible; to feel intense, suppressed anger

Examples

seethe with anger inside
He seethed with anger when he heard about the decision, barely able to contain his frustration.
Many employees seethed with anger at the unfair treatment by management.
She tried to hide her feelings, but she began to seethe with anger after the insult.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fume: to show irritation or anger visibly but not necessarily express it aloud
  • synonym – simmer: to be filled with suppressed emotion, especially anger
