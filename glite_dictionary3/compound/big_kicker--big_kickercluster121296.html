big kicker

noun
main advantage or decisive factor
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ ˈkɪkɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈkɪkə / (BrE)
the main advantage or decisive factor in a situation, often one that has a significant impact on the outcome

Examples

the big kicker in negotiations
For me, the big kicker was the flexible work schedule offered by the new job.
The big kicker came when the company offered a year of free service with the device.
The big kicker in choosing this apartment was its proximity to my office.
