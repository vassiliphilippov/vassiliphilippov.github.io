organic disorder

noun
disease caused by physical organ changes
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈgænɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A disorder caused by identifiable physiological or structural changes in an organ or tissue.

Examples

diagnose organic disorder
Advances in imaging technology help detect early signs of organic disorder in patients.
The doctor diagnosed him with an organic disorder after detecting physical changes in his brain.
Unlike a functional disorder, an organic disorder has an identifiable physiological cause.

Related Terms

  • antonym – functional disorder: disorder with no detectable organic cause
