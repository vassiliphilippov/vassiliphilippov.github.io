gotcha journalism

noun
aggressive journalism seeking to expose mistakes
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑtʃə ˈdʒɜrnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒtʃə ˈdʒɜːnəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a style of journalism that seeks to catch public figures off guard or expose their mistakes, often in an aggressive or confrontational manner

Examples

gotcha journalism exposes mistakes
Candidates often prepare for interviews to avoid falling victim to gotcha journalism.
She accused the reporter of practicing gotcha journalism instead of focusing on the issues.
Some critics argue that gotcha journalism undermines serious political discussion.
