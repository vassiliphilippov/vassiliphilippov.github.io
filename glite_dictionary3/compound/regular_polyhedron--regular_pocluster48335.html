regular polyhedron

noun
a polyhedron with identical regular polygonal faces
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr pəˈliːhiˌdrɑn / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə pɒlɪˈhiːdrɒn / (BrE)
a polyhedron whose faces are congruent regular polygons and whose angles are all congruent, specifically, any of the five Platonic solids

Examples

a regular polyhedron cube
A cube is the most familiar type of regular polyhedron found in everyday objects.
Ancient Greek mathematicians classified each regular polyhedron based on its symmetry and geometry.
The dodecahedron is one example of a regular polyhedron with twelve pentagonal faces.
