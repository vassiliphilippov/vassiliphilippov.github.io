caustic lime

noun
calcium oxide from heated limestone
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔstɪk laɪm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːstɪk laɪm / (BrE)
a caustic white substance, calcium oxide, obtained by heating limestone, used in building, industry, and agriculture

Examples

caustic lime in mortar
Old buildings sometimes used caustic lime as a key ingredient in mortar.
The workers handled the caustic lime with gloves to avoid chemical burns.
When water was added to caustic lime, it produced heat and formed slaked lime.

Related Terms

  • synonym – quicklime: calcium oxide, especially as obtained from limestone
  • degree lower – slaked lime: calcium hydroxide, obtained by adding water to calcium oxide
