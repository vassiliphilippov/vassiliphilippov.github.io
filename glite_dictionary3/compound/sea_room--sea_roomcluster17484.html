sea room

noun
space for maneuver at sea
Pronunciation: / si ˈrum / (AmE), / si ˈruːm / (BrE)
enough open space at sea for a ship to maneuver safely without risk of running aground or hitting obstacles

Examples

enough sea room for safety
The captain ensured there was sufficient sea room before changing course.
The wide bay provided ample sea room for the ships to anchor safely.
Without enough sea room, the yacht risked hitting the rocks during the storm.
