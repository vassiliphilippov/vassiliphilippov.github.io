navigation light

noun
position-indicating light on aircraft or ship
Pronunciation: / ˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən laɪt / (AmE), / ˌnavɪˈɡeɪʃən laɪt / (BrE)
a light fixed on an aircraft or ship to indicate its position, direction, or status, especially at night or in poor visibility; on aircraft, typically a red light is placed on the left (port) wing tip and a green light on the right (starboard) wing tip.

Examples

navigation light on a ship
At night, the plane's red and green navigation lights were clearly visible from the ground.
Failure to operate navigation lights can result in a safety violation for both ships and aircraft.
The pilot checked the navigation lights before taking off at dusk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beacon: a visible signal or guiding light
  • synonym – running light: a light on a ship or vehicle to show position or movement
