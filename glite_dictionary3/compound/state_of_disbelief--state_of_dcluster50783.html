state of disbelief

noun
condition of being unable to believe
Pronunciation: / steɪt əv dɪsˈbɪlif / (AmE), / steɪt əv dɪsˈbiːliːf / (BrE)
a condition of being unable or unwilling to believe something, often due to shock or surprise

Examples

in a state of disbelief after news
After hearing the unexpected news, she remained in a state of disbelief for hours.
He walked around in a state of disbelief, unable to process what had happened.
The entire team was in a state of disbelief when they won the championship.
