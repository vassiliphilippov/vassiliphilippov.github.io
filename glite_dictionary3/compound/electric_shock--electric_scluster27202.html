electric shock

noun
injury from electrical current
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɑk /; / əˈlɛktrɪk ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ʃɒk / (BrE)
a sudden physical effect or injury caused by electricity passing through the body, often resulting in pain, burns, or muscle spasms

Examples

Some toys carry warnings about the risk of electric shock if misused near water.
The technician wore rubber gloves to avoid getting an electric shock while working with live wires.
