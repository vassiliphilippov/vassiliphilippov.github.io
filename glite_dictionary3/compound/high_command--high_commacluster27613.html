high command

noun
highest leaders in an organization, esp. military
Pronunciation: / haɪ kəˈmænd / (AmE), / haɪ kəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
the group of people with the highest authority and power in an organization, especially the military; senior leadership including the top officers or officials.

Examples

the high command of the army
He reported directly to the high command during the crisis.
Rumors of disagreement at the high command level led to uncertainty among the troops.
The high command decided to launch a counteroffensive early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – leadership: the people who lead a group or organization
  • antonym – rank and file: the ordinary members of an organization, as opposed to its leaders
