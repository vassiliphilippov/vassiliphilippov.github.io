imperial moth

noun
large North American moth with yellow and purple/brown wings
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɪriəl mɑθ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəl mɒθ / (BrE)
A large North American moth (Eacles imperialis) with yellow wings marked with purple or brown, whose larvae feed on trees such as maple and pine.

Examples

an imperial moth on a tree
The imperial moth is known for its striking yellow wings patterned with brownish markings.
The larvae of the imperial moth can be seen feeding on pine needles during late summer.
We found an imperial moth resting on the trunk of a maple tree in our backyard.
