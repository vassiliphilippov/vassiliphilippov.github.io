Whig Party

noun
US political party, 1834–1854
Pronunciation: / wɪɡ ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / wɪɡ ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a political party in the United States (1834–1854) that opposed the Democratic Party and supported a loose interpretation of the Constitution and high protective tariffs

Examples

the Whig Party opposed Democrats
Many former members of the Whig Party joined the newly formed Republican Party.
The Whig Party played a major role in American politics before the Civil War.
The Whig Party supported Henry Clay and his American System.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Democratic Party: a major contemporary political party in the United States, historically opposed by the Whigs
  • degree higher – Republican Party: the party that emerged after the Whig Party's decline, absorbing many of its members
