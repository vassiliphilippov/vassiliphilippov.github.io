malt sugar

noun
sugar formed during starch digestion
Pronunciation: / mɔlt ˈʃʊɡɚ / (AmE), / mɔːlt ˈʃʊɡə / (BrE)
a white, crystalline sugar (maltose) produced during the breakdown of starches, especially in the malting process

Examples

malt sugar in beer
Barley is rich in malt sugar, which is essential for brewing beer.
During digestion, starches break down into malt sugar and other simpler sugars.
The sweetness in some cereals comes from naturally occurring malt sugar.

Related Terms

  • synonym – maltose: a disaccharide sugar formed from two glucose units, also known as malt sugar
