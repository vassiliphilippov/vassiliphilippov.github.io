hypoplastic anaemia

noun
anaemia from underactive bone marrow
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpəˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpə(ʊ)ˈplastɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A type of anaemia caused by underactive bone marrow, resulting in a reduced production of blood cells.

Examples

hypoplastic anaemia diagnosis
If untreated, hypoplastic anaemia can progress to more severe forms of bone marrow failure.
Patients with hypoplastic anaemia may require regular blood transfusions as part of their treatment.
The doctor diagnosed her with hypoplastic anaemia after reviewing her blood test results.
