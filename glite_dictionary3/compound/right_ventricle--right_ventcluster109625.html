right ventricle

noun
chamber of the heart pumping blood to the lungs
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˈvɛntrɪkl̩ / (BrE)
the chamber on the right side of the heart that receives blood from the right atrium and pumps it into the pulmonary artery

Examples

right ventricle pumps blood
A defect in the right ventricle can affect the overall function of the heart.
Oxygen-poor blood is pumped from the right ventricle to the lungs.
The surgeon checked the right ventricle for any signs of abnormal blood flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym – left ventricle: chamber on the left side of the heart, pumps blood to the body
  • degree lower – right atrium: upper right chamber of the heart, receives blood from the body
