concentration camp

noun
penal camp for political prisoners or persecuted groups
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən kæmp / (BrE)
a place where large numbers of people, especially political prisoners or persecuted groups, are kept in harsh, crowded, and inadequate conditions, often without legal trial

Examples

concentration camp for prisoners
During the war, thousands were sent to a concentration camp for political reasons.
Many historians have documented the conditions inside the Nazi concentration camp at Auschwitz.
She lost several relatives who were held in a concentration camp during the conflict.
International organizations have condemned the creation of any new concentration camp for political prisoners.
Many innocent people suffered in the concentration camp during the war.
The museum displays photographs and artifacts from a former concentration camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym – internment camp: camp where people are confined, typically during wartime, without trial
