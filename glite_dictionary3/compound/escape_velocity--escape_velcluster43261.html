escape velocity

noun
self-sustaining growth threshold
Pronunciation: / \u026a\u02c8ske\u026ap v\u0259\u02c8l\u0251\u02d0s\u0259ti / (AmE), / \u026a\u02c8ske\u026ap v\u026a\u02c8l\u0252s\u0259ti / (BrE)
(business, tech) the growth point at which a company, product, or project becomes self-sustaining and no longer requires substantial external support or funding.

Examples

escape velocity for a startup
After the new funding round, the company reached escape velocity and began hiring aggressively.
Hitting escape velocity let the product spend marketing money on retention rather than acquisition costs.
Investors said the startup had finally found escape velocity after months of steady revenue growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – critical mass: the necessary size or amount at which something becomes sufficient to sustain itself
  • synonym – tipping point: the moment when a small change produces a large effect and a new trend or process becomes self-sustaining
