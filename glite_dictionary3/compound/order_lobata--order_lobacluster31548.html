order Lobata

noun
a taxonomic order of ctenophores
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər loʊˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ləʊˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of ctenophores characterized by vertically compressed bodies, two large oral lobes, and tentacles present only in the immature stage.

Examples

order Lobata ctenophores
Larvae of ctenophores in the order Lobata have tentacles, unlike adults.
Marine biologists discovered a new species belonging to the order Lobata near the ocean floor.
Members of the order Lobata are distinguished by their two large oral lobes and four pointed processes.
