milk whey

noun
liquid left after cheese-making
Pronunciation: / mɪlk weɪ / (AmE), / mɪlk weɪ / (BrE)
the watery liquid remaining after milk has been curdled and strained in the process of making cheese.

Examples

milk whey in cheese making
After the curds are removed, milk whey remains as a yellowish liquid.
Some athletes add milk whey to their diets for its nutritional value.
The cheese factory uses milk whey to produce protein supplements.
