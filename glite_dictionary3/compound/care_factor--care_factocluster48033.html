care factor

noun
degree of interest or concern
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛr ˌfæktɚ / (AmE), / ˈkeə ˌfæktə / (BrE)
The degree of interest or concern that a person has about something, often implying little or none.

Examples

care factor for rules
His care factor for company policy is almost zero.
Most teenagers have a low care factor for chores around the house.
