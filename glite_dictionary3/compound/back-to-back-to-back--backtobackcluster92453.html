back-to-back-to-back

adjective
three consecutive events; three in a row
Pronunciation: / ˌbæk tə ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (AmE), / ˌbæk tə ˌbæk tə ˈbæk / (BrE)
(of events) occurring three times in a row, one immediately after another, without interruption.

Examples

back-to-back-to-back wins
She received back-to-back-to-back promotions over three years at the company.
The team achieved a back-to-back-to-back victory, winning three games in a row.
They celebrated their back-to-back-to-back holiday parties, three nights in succession.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – back-to-back: two consecutive events or achievements, one immediately following the other
