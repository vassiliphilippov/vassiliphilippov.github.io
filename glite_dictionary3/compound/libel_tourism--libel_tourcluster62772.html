libel tourism

noun
suing for libel in favorable jurisdictions
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪbəl ˈtʊrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪbəl ˈtʊərɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of suing for libel in a jurisdiction perceived as more likely to provide a favorable judgment to the plaintiff, often due to more plaintiff-friendly libel laws.

Examples

libel tourism case abroad
Debate over libel tourism has prompted some countries to reform their defamation laws.
Journalists argue that libel tourism threatens the freedom of the press worldwide.
The author faced libel tourism after publishing his controversial memoir in multiple countries.

  • synonym – forum shopping: the practice of choosing the most favorable jurisdiction or court to bring a lawsuit
