port of entry

noun
place for customs and immigration checks
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔrt əv ˈɛntri / (AmE), / ˌpɔːt əv ˈɛntri / (BrE)
a place, typically an airport, seaport, or border crossing, where authorities check and authorize the entry or exit of people and goods into or out of a country

Examples

international port of entry
All commercial shipments must be inspected at a designated port of entry before entering the country.
The international airport serves as the main port of entry for foreign visitors.
When we arrived at the port of entry, we had to present our passports to the customs officers.

  • synonym – border crossing: location where people and goods pass between countries and are inspected by officials
