line of fire

noun
situation of danger or criticism
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv faɪər / (AmE), / laɪn əv faɪə / (BrE)
a position or situation where someone is at risk of being harmed, attacked, or criticized

Examples

in the line of fire from critics
As the company's spokesperson, she was constantly in the line of fire from reporters.
During the protest, several journalists were caught in the line of fire.
If we stay here, we'll be right in the line of fire when the debate gets heated.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hot seat: a position in which someone is under a lot of scrutiny or pressure
  • antonym – out of harm's way: protected from danger
