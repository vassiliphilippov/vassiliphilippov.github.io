oral contraceptive

noun
hormonal birth control pill
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəl kənˌtræpsɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl kənˌtræpsɪtɪv / (BrE)
A pill containing hormones, taken by mouth, that prevents pregnancy by inhibiting ovulation.

Examples

take an oral contraceptive pill
Many women rely on an oral contraceptive for effective birth control.
She began taking an oral contraceptive as recommended by her doctor.
The oral contraceptive is one of the most popular methods of preventing pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • synonym – birth control pill: a pill to prevent pregnancy, often containing hormones
  • antonym – condom: a sheath used during intercourse to prevent pregnancy
