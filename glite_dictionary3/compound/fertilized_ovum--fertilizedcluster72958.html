fertilized ovum

noun
cell formed by sperm and egg union
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzd ˈoʊvəm / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪˌlaɪzd ˈəʊvəm / (BrE)
a cell formed by the union of a sperm and an egg, from which a new organism develops; a zygote

Examples

human fertilized ovum
After fertilization, the fertilized ovum begins to divide rapidly as it implants in the uterus.
In mammals, the fertilized ovum develops into an embryo during early pregnancy.
The fertilized ovum contains genetic material from both parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym – zygote: a cell formed by the fusion of two gametes
