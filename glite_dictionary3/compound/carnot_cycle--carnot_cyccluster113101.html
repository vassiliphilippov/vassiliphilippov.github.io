Carnot cycle

noun
idealized reversible heat engine cycle
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrˌnoʊ ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnɒt ˈsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
an idealized cycle of processes in a reversible heat engine, consisting of alternating expansion and compression, used to determine the maximum possible efficiency of a heat engine.

Examples

Carnot cycle in a heat engine
Every process in the Carnot cycle is either isothermal or adiabatic, making it idealized.
In thermodynamics, the Carnot cycle provides a benchmark for reversible processes.
The Carnot cycle sets a theoretical upper limit on the efficiency of all heat engines.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thermodynamic cycle: a sequence of processes in a heat engine returning to its starting point
