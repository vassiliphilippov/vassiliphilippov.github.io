low-birth-weight infant

noun
an infant weighing under 2,500 grams at birth
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊ ˈbɝːθ weɪt ˈɪn.fənt / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈbɜːθ weɪt ˈɪn.fənt / (BrE)
an infant who is born weighing less than 5.5 pounds (2,500 grams), regardless of gestational age

Examples

low-birth-weight infant needs care
A low-birth-weight infant requires specialized care in the neonatal unit.
Babies born prematurely are often classified as low-birth-weight infants by medical staff.
Doctors monitor the growth of low-birth-weight infants to ensure they catch up developmentally.

  • synonym – preterm infant: an infant born before 37 weeks of gestation
