tuberculin test

noun
test for tuberculosis infection
Pronunciation: / tuˈbɝːkjəlɪn tɛst / (AmE), / tjuːˈbɜːkjʊlɪn tɛst / (BrE)
a medical test involving the injection of tuberculin beneath the skin to determine whether a person has been infected with tuberculosis

Examples

tuberculin test for tuberculosis
All healthcare workers are required to have a tuberculin test annually.
Her tuberculin test showed a positive reaction, indicating previous exposure to tuberculosis.
The doctor recommended a tuberculin test after noticing her persistent cough.
