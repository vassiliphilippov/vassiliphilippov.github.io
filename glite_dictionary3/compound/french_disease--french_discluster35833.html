French disease

noun
historical term for syphilis
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / frɛntʃ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
an old euphemism for syphilis, used historically, especially in Europe

Examples

the French disease epidemic
Doctors struggled to treat the French disease before the discovery of penicillin.
Syphilis was commonly referred to as the French disease in European writings of the 16th century.
The term French disease reflects the historic practice of blaming foreign nations for epidemics.
