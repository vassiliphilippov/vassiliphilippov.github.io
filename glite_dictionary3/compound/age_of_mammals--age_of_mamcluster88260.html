Age of Mammals

noun
geological era when mammals dominated; Cenozoic
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒ əv ˈmæməlz / (AmE), / eɪdʒ ɒv ˈmaməlz / (BrE)
the geological era covering the last 63 million years, during which mammals became the dominant land animals; also known as the Cenozoic Era

Examples

the Age of Mammals period
Many fossils from the Age of Mammals reveal how quickly mammals diversified after the Cretaceous.
The Cenozoic period is often called the Age of Mammals due to the rise of mammalian life forms.
The extinction of the dinosaurs paved the way for the Age of Mammals to begin.
