sandy soil

noun
soil made mostly of sand
Pronunciation: / ˈsæn.di ˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsæn.di ˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
Soil that consists largely of sand particles, drains water quickly, and tends to be loose and easy to work.

Examples

sandy soil drains quickly
After the rain, the sandy soil dried out much faster than clay soils.
Gardeners recommend mixing compost into sandy soil to improve its fertility.
Tomatoes often grow best in sandy soil with plenty of sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clay soil: soil with a high proportion of clay, slow-draining and dense
