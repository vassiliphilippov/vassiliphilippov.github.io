efferent nerve

noun
nerve carrying impulses to muscles or glands
Pronunciation: / ˈɛfərənt nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈɛfərənt nɜːv / (BrE)
a nerve that carries impulses away from the central nervous system to muscles or glands

Examples

efferent nerve controls muscles
Damage to an efferent nerve can result in the loss of motor function in a limb.
Researchers studied how efferent nerves control glandular secretion in response to stress.
The doctor explained how an efferent nerve sends signals from the brain to the muscles.
