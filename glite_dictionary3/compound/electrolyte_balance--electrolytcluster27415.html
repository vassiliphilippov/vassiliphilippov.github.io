electrolyte balance

noun
proper equilibrium of body electrolytes
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktɹoʊˌlaɪt ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktɹəˌlaɪt ˈbæləns / (BrE)
a state in which the concentrations of electrolytes, such as calcium, sodium, and potassium, in the body are kept at proper levels for normal health and functioning

Examples

normal electrolyte balance
An abnormal electrolyte balance can signal serious kidney or metabolic problems.
Athletes need to maintain their electrolyte balance to avoid cramps and dehydration during intense training.
Doctors monitor a patient's electrolyte balance closely when they are on intravenous fluids.
