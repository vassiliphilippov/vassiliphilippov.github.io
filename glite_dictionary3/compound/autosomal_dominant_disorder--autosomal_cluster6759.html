autosomal dominant disorder

noun
genetic disorder from dominant autosomal gene
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtəˌsoʊməl ˈdɒmɪnənt dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˌsəʊməl ˈdɒmɪnənt dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A disease caused by a dominant mutant gene located on a non-sex chromosome (autosome), where only one copy of the gene is necessary for the disorder to be expressed.

Examples

an autosomal dominant disorder runs in families
A child with one affected parent has a 50% chance of inheriting an autosomal dominant disorder.
Doctors often recommend genetic counseling for families with a history of autosomal dominant disorder.
Huntington's disease is an example of an autosomal dominant disorder that usually appears in adulthood.

Related Terms

  • antonym – autosomal recessive disorder: a disorder caused by two copies of a mutant gene on an autosome
  • synonym – dominant: a gene that expresses its effect even when only one copy is present
