carbonyl group

noun
chemical group: carbon double-bonded to oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbəˌnɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a divalent chemical group (CO) consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom, occurring in many organic compounds such as aldehydes and ketones.
