theta rhythm

noun
brainwave pattern in relaxed wakefulness
Pronunciation: / ˈθeɪtə ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈθiːtə ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
a type of brainwave pattern, characterized by low frequency and low amplitude, typically observed in a relaxed or drowsy wakeful state

Examples

brain theta rhythm
A clear theta rhythm can indicate that a person is awake but ready to fall asleep.
Researchers study the theta rhythm to understand the brain's activity during meditation.
When the patient entered a relaxed state, the theta rhythm became more pronounced on the EEG.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – alpha rhythm: brainwave pattern associated with relaxed, wakeful states, usually with higher frequency than theta
  • degree lower – delta rhythm: brainwave pattern with even lower frequency, typically occurring during deep sleep
