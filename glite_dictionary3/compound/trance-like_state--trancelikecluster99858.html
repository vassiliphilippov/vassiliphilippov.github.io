trance-like state

noun
condition of deep absorption or daze
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænsˌlaɪk steɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɑːnslʌɪk steɪt / (BrE)
a condition in which someone is deeply absorbed or appears to be in a daze, often unresponsive to the environment, similar to being in a trance

Examples

deep trance-like state
After hours of meditation, he entered a trance-like state and lost track of time.
She stared at the fire in a trance-like state, barely aware of her surroundings.
The hypnotist put the volunteer into a trance-like state during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trance: a half-conscious state characterized by lack of response
