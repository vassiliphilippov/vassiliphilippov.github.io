import duty

noun
tax on imported goods
Pronunciation: / ˈɪm.pɔrt ˌdu.ti / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pɔːt ˌdjuː.ti / (BrE)
a tax imposed by a government on goods brought into a country from abroad

Examples

import duty on cars
Companies must factor in the import duty when calculating the final price of imported goods.
Many luxury items are subject to a high import duty when brought into the country.
The government increased the import duty on foreign cars to protect local manufacturers.

Related Terms

  • antonym – export duty: a tax imposed on goods sent out of a country
  • synonym – tariff: a tax on imports or exports
