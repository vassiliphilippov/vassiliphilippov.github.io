thyroid-stimulating hormone

noun
hormone that stimulates the thyroid gland
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪˌrɔɪd ˈstɪmjəˌleɪtɪŋ ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd ˈstɪmjʊˌleɪtɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce its own hormones

Examples

thyroid-stimulating hormone blood test
The doctor ordered a blood test to check her thyroid-stimulating hormone levels.
Treatment decisions are often based on measurements of thyroid-stimulating hormone in the patient's blood.
When the thyroid-stimulating hormone is too high, it may indicate hypothyroidism.
