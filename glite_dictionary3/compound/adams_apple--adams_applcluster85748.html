Adam's apple

noun
prominent laryngeal cartilage in the neck
Pronunciation: / ˈædəmz ˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈædəmz ˌæp(ə)l / (BrE)
the prominent projection of the thyroid cartilage of the larynx, typically more visible in males, in the front of the neck

Examples

his Adam's apple moves
He nervously swallowed, and his Adam's apple moved up and down.
Some people are self-conscious about having a large Adam's apple.
The doctor examined his throat and pointed out the prominent Adam's apple.
