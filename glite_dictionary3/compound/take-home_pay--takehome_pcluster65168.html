take-home pay

noun
net amount of salary after deductions
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪk ˌhoʊm ˌpeɪ / (AmE), / ˈteɪk həʊm ˌpeɪ / (BrE)
the amount of money an employee receives after all deductions such as taxes, dues, and insurance have been taken from their gross salary

Examples

monthly take-home pay
After taxes and health insurance were deducted, my take-home pay was lower than I expected.
It is important to know your take-home pay when planning a monthly budget.
She calculated her take-home pay to make sure she could afford the apartment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gross pay: total salary before any deductions
  • synonym – net pay: amount received after all deductions from salary
