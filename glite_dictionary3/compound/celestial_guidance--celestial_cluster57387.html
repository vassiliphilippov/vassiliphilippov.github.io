celestial guidance

noun
navigation using positions of celestial bodies
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛstʃəl ˈɡaɪdəns / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl ˈɡaɪdəns / (BrE)
a method of navigating or controlling the path of a missile or spacecraft by using the positions of celestial bodies such as stars or planets

Examples

celestial guidance system
Improvements in celestial guidance technology enabled longer and more accurate space missions.
Older missile systems often used celestial guidance in combination with radio navigation.
The spacecraft relied on celestial guidance to maintain its course beyond Earth's atmosphere.
