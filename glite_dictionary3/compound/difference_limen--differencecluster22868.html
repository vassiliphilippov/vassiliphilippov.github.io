difference limen

noun
smallest detectable difference in stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪfɚəns ˈlaɪmən / (AmE), / ˈdɪfərəns ˈliːmən / (BrE)
the smallest detectable difference in the intensity or magnitude of a stimulus that can be sensed by an individual

Examples

difference limen for sound
A subject's difference limen tends to decrease with practice, allowing them to detect finer distinctions.
In hearing tests, researchers measure the difference limen for changes in sound intensity.
The difference limen for weight perception varies depending on the original mass being lifted.
