surplus to requirements

adjective
no longer needed or useful
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜr.pləs tu rɪˈkwaɪr.mənts / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.pləs tə rɪˈkwaɪə.mənts / (BrE)
more than what is needed or wanted; no longer necessary or useful

Examples

surplus to requirements after changes
After the restructuring, several employees were considered surplus to requirements and let go.
Once the new system was installed, the old equipment became surplus to requirements.
We decided to sell the furniture that was surplus to requirements.
