water mint

noun
a mint plant growing in wet places
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə mɪnt / (BrE)
a European species of mint (Mentha aquatica) that grows in wet places, with a fragrance similar to bergamot; naturalized in parts of North America

Examples

water mint leaves
Water mint grows wild in the marshy meadows along the riverbanks.
Gardeners often plant water mint around ponds for its attractive flowers and fragrant foliage.
The leaves of water mint have a refreshing, somewhat citrus-like aroma.
