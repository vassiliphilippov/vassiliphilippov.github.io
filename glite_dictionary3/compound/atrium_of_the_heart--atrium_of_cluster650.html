atrium (of the heart)

noun
upper chamber of the heart
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪtriəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪtriəm / (BrE)
Either of the two upper chambers of the heart that receive blood from the veins and pass it to the ventricles.

Examples

atrium of the heart
Damage to the atrium of the heart can disrupt normal blood flow.
Oxygen-poor blood returns to the right atrium of the heart before being pumped to the lungs.
The left atrium of the heart receives blood from the pulmonary veins.

  • antonym – ventricle (of the heart): lower chamber of the heart
