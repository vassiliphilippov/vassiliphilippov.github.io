lenticular nucleus

noun
part of basal ganglia in the brain
Pronunciation: / lɛnˈtɪkjələr ˈnuːkliəs / (AmE), / lɛnˈtɪkjʊlə ˈnjuːkliəs / (BrE)
a lens-shaped mass of gray matter in the brain, part of the basal ganglia, consisting of the putamen and the globus pallidus

Examples

the lenticular nucleus of brain
Damage to the lenticular nucleus can result in various movement disorders.
MRI scans showed changes in the size of the patient's lenticular nucleus.
The lenticular nucleus plays a key role in regulating voluntary motor movements.
