elective course

noun
a freely chosen academic course
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktɪv kɔrs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktɪv kɔːs / (BrE)
A course in a school or college curriculum that a student may choose from among alternatives, rather than one that is required.

Examples

choose an elective course
Enrollment in an elective course depends on the student's interests and future plans.
She chose photography as her elective course this semester.
Sophomores are allowed to take one elective course in addition to their core classes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – required course: a course that students must take as part of a curriculum
