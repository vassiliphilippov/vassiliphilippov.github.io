coronal suture

noun
joint between parietal and frontal skull bones
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊ.nəl ˈsuː.tʃɚ / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊ.nəl ˈsjuː.tʃə / (BrE)
the immovable joint (suture) between the parietal and frontal bones at the top of the skull

Examples

coronal suture of skull
During the procedure, the surgeon made an incision near the coronal suture.
Premature fusion of the coronal suture can lead to skull deformities in infants.
The coronal suture can be clearly seen in the top view of the human skull.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sagittal suture: the suture between the two parietal bones of the skull
