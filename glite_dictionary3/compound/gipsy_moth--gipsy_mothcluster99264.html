gipsy moth

noun
a moth pest of shade trees
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪpsi mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsi mɒθ / (BrE)
A large European moth (Lymantria dispar) introduced into North America, whose larvae are serious pests of shade trees and forests.

Examples

gipsy moth caterpillar infestation
Homeowners are advised to check their trees regularly for signs of gipsy moth caterpillars.
Scientists are developing new methods to control gipsy moth populations and protect native wildlife.
The spread of the gipsy moth has caused widespread defoliation of forests in the northeastern United States.
