topical anaesthetic

noun
local numbing medicine for body surface
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑpɪkəl əˈnɛsθətɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl əˈniːsθətɪk / (BrE)
A substance applied to a particular area of the body to cause numbness or loss of sensation in that area.

Examples

topical anaesthetic cream
A topical anaesthetic cream can reduce pain during minor skin procedures.
She requested a topical anaesthetic for her tattoo removal session to make it more comfortable.
The dentist applied a topical anaesthetic to numb the patient's gum before the injection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – general anaesthetic: an anaesthetic that affects the entire body and induces unconsciousness
  • synonym – local anaesthetic: an anaesthetic that produces loss of sensation in a limited area
