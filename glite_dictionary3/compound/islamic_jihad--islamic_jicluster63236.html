Islamic Jihad

noun
Egyptian Islamic extremist group
Pronunciation: / ɪˈslɑmɪk dʒɪˈhɑd / (AmE), / ɪˈslæmɪk dʒɪˈhɑːd / (BrE)
an Islamic extremist organization founded in the late 1970s in Egypt, aiming to overthrow the government and establish an Islamic state; known for operating in underground cells and for its involvement in political violence.

Examples

Islamic Jihad in Egypt
Islamic Jihad was implicated in the assassination of Egypt's President Anwar Sadat in 1981.
Egyptian authorities have targeted Islamic Jihad due to its efforts to destabilize the government.
Many underground cells associated with Islamic Jihad operated in secrecy during the 1980s.
