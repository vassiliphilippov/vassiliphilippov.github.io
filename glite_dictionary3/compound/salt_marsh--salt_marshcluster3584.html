salt marsh

noun
coastal wetland flooded by saltwater
Pronunciation: / sɔlt mɑrʃ / (AmE), / sɒlt mɑːʃ / (BrE)
an area of low-lying, coastal land that is frequently flooded by saltwater, supporting salt-tolerant plants

Examples

coastal salt marsh
At high tide, seawater covers much of the salt marsh, bringing nutrients to the ecosystem.
Birds nest in the salt marsh during the summer breeding season.
The salt marsh was teeming with wildlife and unusual plants.
