appeals board

noun
committee that hears administrative appeals
Pronunciation: / əˈpilz bɔrd / (AmE), / əˈpiːlz bɔːd / (BrE)
a committee of appointed officials, not part of the judicial system, that hears and decides on appeals, especially in administrative cases

Examples

appeals board decision
Applicants can submit their case to the appeals board if they disagree with the initial ruling.
The appeals board meets monthly to consider cases involving license suspensions.
The appeals board reviewed the employee's grievance and overturned the original decision.
