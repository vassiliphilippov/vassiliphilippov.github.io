sleeping pill

noun
tablet or capsule taken to induce sleep
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːpɪŋ pɪl / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ pɪl / (BrE)
a tablet or capsule containing a drug taken to help someone fall asleep

Examples

take a sleeping pill before bed
After struggling with insomnia, he finally tried a sleeping pill prescribed by his doctor.
It is important not to take a sleeping pill without consulting a healthcare professional.
She forgot she had taken a sleeping pill and felt groggy all morning.
