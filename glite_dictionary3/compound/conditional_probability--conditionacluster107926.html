conditional probability

noun
probability of an event given another event
Pronunciation: / kənˌdɪʃənəl prəˈbæbɪləti / (AmE), / kənˌdɪʃənəl prəˈbæbɪləti / (BrE)
The probability that a certain event will occur given that one or more other events have already occurred.

Examples

conditional probability of rain
In statistics, conditional probability helps us calculate the chance of an event based on related prior events.
The conditional probability of drawing an ace from a deck, given that a king has already been removed, is slightly higher.
Understanding conditional probability is essential for analyzing dependent events in probability theory.
