Colossus of Rhodes

proper noun
ancient bronze statue in Rhodes
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɑːsəs əv ˈroʊdz / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsəs ɒv ˈrəʊdz / (BrE)
A huge bronze statue of the sun god Helios, erected around 285 BC at the harbor entrance of Rhodes and toppled by an earthquake after about 50 years.

Examples

the Colossus of Rhodes statue
After standing for just over five decades, the Colossus of Rhodes was destroyed by an earthquake.
Many historians debate how the Colossus of Rhodes actually looked and where it stood.
The Colossus of Rhodes was considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
