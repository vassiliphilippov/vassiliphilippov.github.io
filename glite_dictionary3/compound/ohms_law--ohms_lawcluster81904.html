Ohm's law

noun
law relating voltage, current, and resistance
Pronunciation: / oʊmz lɔ / (AmE), / əʊmz lɔː / (BrE)
A law stating that the electric current passing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage across it and inversely proportional to the resistance (I = V/R).

Examples

Ohm's law in electricity
According to Ohm's law, increasing the resistance will decrease the current if the voltage stays the same.
If you know the voltage and resistance, you can use Ohm's law to calculate the current.
The circuit did not behave as predicted by Ohm's law due to non-ohmic components.
