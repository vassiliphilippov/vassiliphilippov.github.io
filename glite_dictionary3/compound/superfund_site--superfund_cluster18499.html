Superfund site

noun
hazardous waste cleanup site (U.S.)
Pronunciation: / ˈsuː.pɚ.fʌnd saɪt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.fʌnd saɪt / (BrE)
A site in the United States where hazardous waste has been dumped, and which has been identified by the Environmental Protection Agency as requiring cleanup under the Superfund program.

Examples

old Superfund site
Local residents are concerned about potential health risks near the old Superfund site.
The EPA has allocated millions of dollars to clean up the contaminated Superfund site.
The town became notorious after it was declared a Superfund site in the 1980s.
