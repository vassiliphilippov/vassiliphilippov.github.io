trade balance

noun
difference between value of imports and exports
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪd ˌbæl.əns / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd ˌbæl.əns / (BrE)
the difference in value between a country's imports and exports of goods over a period of time

Examples

trade balance improves exports
A positive trade balance means the nation exports more goods than it imports.
Changes in the trade balance can affect a country's economic stability.
The government reported a growing trade balance deficit last quarter.
