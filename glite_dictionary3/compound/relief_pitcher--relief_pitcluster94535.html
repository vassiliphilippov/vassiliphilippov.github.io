relief pitcher

noun
a baseball player who replaces the starting pitcher
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlif ˌpɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈliːf ˌpɪtʃə / (BrE)
a baseball pitcher who enters the game after the starting pitcher, usually to replace a tiring or ineffective player

Examples

relief pitcher enters game
Mariano Rivera is considered one of the best relief pitchers in baseball history.
Teams often rely on a strong relief pitcher to secure the final outs of a close game.
The manager called for a relief pitcher after the starter struggled in the sixth inning.
