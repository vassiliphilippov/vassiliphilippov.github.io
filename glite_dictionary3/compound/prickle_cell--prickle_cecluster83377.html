prickle cell

noun
a spiny cell in the skin's epidermis
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪkəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˈprɪkəl sɛl / (BrE)
a type of cell in the lower layers of the epidermis, characterized by spiny projections that connect with neighboring cells

Examples

prickle cell layer
Damage to the prickle cell layer can lead to compromised skin function.
In cases of certain skin conditions, the prickle cells may show abnormal growth patterns.
The prickle cell is easily identified under the microscope by its spiny extensions.
