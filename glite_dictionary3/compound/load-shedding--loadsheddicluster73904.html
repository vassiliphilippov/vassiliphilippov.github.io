load-shedding

noun
temporary power cut to manage supply shortage
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊd ˌʃɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊd ˌʃɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate and temporary reduction or discontinuation of electric power supply to certain areas when demand exceeds available supply

Examples

load-shedding schedule
Residents are unhappy about the frequent load-shedding that disrupts their daily routines.
The city implemented load-shedding to prevent the entire grid from failing during the heatwave.
The government announced a schedule for load-shedding to manage electricity shortages.
