Islamic Group of Uzbekistan

noun
Central Asian militant Islamist organization
Pronunciation: / ɪˈzlæmɪk ɡruːp ʌv ˌuːzbɛkɪˈstæn / (AmE), / ɪˈzlæmɪk ɡruːp əv ˌuːzbɛkɪˈstæn / (BrE)
a militant Islamist organization founded in 1996 that opposes Uzbekistan's secular government and seeks to establish an Islamic state in Central Asia; known for involvement in terrorism and drug trafficking.

Examples

the Islamic Group of Uzbekistan attacks
Analysts warn that the activities of the Islamic Group of Uzbekistan pose a continued threat to Central Asian stability.

The Islamic Group of Uzbekistan has been linked to several attacks in the region over the past decade.
