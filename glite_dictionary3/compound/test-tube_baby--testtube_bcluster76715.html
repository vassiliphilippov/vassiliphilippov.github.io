test-tube baby

noun
baby conceived via in vitro fertilization
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛst tuː ˈbeɪbi / (AmE), / ˈtɛst tjuː ˈbeɪbi / (BrE)
a baby conceived by in vitro fertilization, where fertilization of an egg occurs outside the mother's body and the embryo is later implanted in the uterus

Examples

test-tube baby born by IVF
Being a test-tube baby no longer carries the stigma it once did, as the procedure has become more common.
Many couples have turned to in vitro fertilization, resulting in the birth of a test-tube baby.
The world's first test-tube baby was born in 1978, marking a milestone in medical science.
