obligate anaerobe

noun
organism intolerant to oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˈɑblɪˌɡeɪt æˈnɛroʊb / (AmE), / ˈɒblɪɡət əˈnɪərəʊb / (BrE)
an organism that cannot survive or grow in the presence of oxygen, requiring an oxygen-free environment

Examples

obligate anaerobe bacteria
Certain bacteria are known as obligate anaerobes because they die in the presence of oxygen.
Clostridium botulinum is a well-known obligate anaerobe responsible for botulism poisoning.
The lab took special precautions to grow obligate anaerobes in a sealed, oxygen-free environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – aerobe: an organism that requires oxygen for growth
