Boston brown bread

noun
dark steamed bread with molasses and cornmeal
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑstən braʊn brɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɒstən braʊn brɛd / (BrE)
A dark, steamed bread made from a mixture of cornmeal, wheat flour, rye flour, molasses, and baking soda, often served sliced and warm.

Examples

slice of Boston brown bread
My grandmother used to serve Boston brown bread with baked beans every Sunday.
Steaming instead of baking gives Boston brown bread its distinctive moist texture.
You can find delicious Boston brown bread at many traditional New England bakeries.
