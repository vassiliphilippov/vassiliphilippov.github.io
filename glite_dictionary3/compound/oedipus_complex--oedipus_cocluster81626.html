Oedipus complex

noun
Freudian theory: male child's sexual desire toward mother
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɪpəs ˌkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪpəs ˌkɒmpleks / (BrE)
(in psychoanalytic theory) the unconscious desire of a boy to possess his mother sexually and to exclude or compete with his father, considered by Freud to be a crucial stage of psychosexual development.

Examples

Freud's Oedipus complex theory
Freud considered the Oedipus complex to be central to the formation of the human psyche.
He referenced the Oedipus complex when discussing childhood family dynamics in his lecture.
Some modern psychologists debate the existence and impact of the Oedipus complex.
