sea duck

noun
a large diving duck found along seacoasts
Pronunciation: / ˈsi dʌk / (AmE), / ˈsiː dʌk / (BrE)
any of various large diving ducks found along seacoasts, such as the eider, scoter, or merganser

Examples

a sea duck swims
Eiders, scoters, and mergansers are all classified as types of sea duck.
Many birdwatchers travel to the coast to spot rare species of sea duck.
The sea duck is adapted to diving for food in cold ocean waters.
