internal-combustion engine

noun
engine burning fuel inside for power
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɜrnəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜː.nəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an engine in which fuel is burned within the engine itself to generate power, with expanding gases either moving pistons or turning a turbine.

Examples

car with internal-combustion engine

Modern cars commonly rely on the internal-combustion engine for their main source of power.
The internal-combustion engine revolutionized transportation by powering automobiles and airplanes.
