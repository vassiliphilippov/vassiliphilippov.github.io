Miranda rule

noun
US legal rule on rights of arrested suspects
Pronunciation: / məˈrændə ruːl / (AmE), / məˈrændə ruːl / (BrE)
The rule that requires police to inform arrested individuals of their right to remain silent and to have legal counsel, with anything said potentially used as evidence.

Examples

Miranda rule in police questioning
Failure to follow the Miranda rule may result in statements being inadmissible in court.
Many television shows depict police officers reciting the Miranda rule during arrests.
The officer informed the suspect of the Miranda rule before beginning the interrogation.
