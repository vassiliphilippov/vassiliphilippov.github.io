freshmanic attitude

noun
naive behavior of a first-year student
Pronunciation: / ˌfrɛʃˈmænɪk ˈætɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˌfrɛʃˈmænɪk ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
naive or inexperienced behavior typical of a student in their first year, especially at university or high school

Examples

Even years later, he would laugh about his freshmanic attitude during orientation week.
Her freshmanic attitude made her both endearing and sometimes frustrating to her older classmates.
The coach warned the team not to let a freshmanic attitude affect their performance under pressure.
