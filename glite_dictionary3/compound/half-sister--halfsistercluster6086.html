half-sister

noun
sister sharing only one parent
Pronunciation: / ˈhæfˌsɪstər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌsɪstə / (BrE)
A sister who shares either the same mother or the same father with you, but not both.

Examples

older half-sister
After their father's remarriage, John gained a new half-sister.
His half-sister is five years older than him and visits every summer.
Maria discovered she had a half-sister living in another country.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stepsister: a female child of your stepparent, not biologically related
