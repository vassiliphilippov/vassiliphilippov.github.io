ruddy duck

noun
a small reddish-brown stiff-tailed duck
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdi dʌk / (AmE), / ˈrʌdi dʌk / (BrE)
a small duck with a reddish-brown body and a stiff tail, native to North America and northern South America

Examples

male ruddy duck display
The ruddy duck is known for its distinctive blue bill during the breeding season.
Unlike other waterfowl, the ruddy duck dives underwater to feed rather than dabbling at the surface.
We spotted several ruddy ducks swimming quietly on the lake this morning.
