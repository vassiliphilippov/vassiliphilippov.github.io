outside clinch

noun
a knot with the line outside the loop
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊt.saɪd ˈklɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.saɪd ˈklɪntʃ / (BrE)
A type of knot in which the end of the line passes outside the loop before being tightened, commonly used in fishing.

Examples

outside clinch knot tying
He demonstrated how to make an outside clinch with the fishing line.
The outside clinch is favored by many anglers for its strength.
To secure the hook, tie an outside clinch and pull it tight.
