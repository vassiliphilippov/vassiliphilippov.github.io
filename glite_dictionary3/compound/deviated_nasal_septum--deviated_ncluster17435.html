deviated nasal septum

noun
displaced nasal septum causing breathing issues
Pronunciation: / ˈdiː.vi.eɪ.tɪd ˈneɪ.zəl ˈsɛp.təm / (AmE), / ˈdiː.vi.eɪ.tɪd ˈneɪ.zəl ˈsɛp.təm / (BrE)
a condition in which the nasal septum is displaced from the center, resulting in obstruction of the nasal passages and difficulty in breathing

Examples

deviated nasal septum causes blockage
Many people do not realize they have a deviated nasal septum until symptoms like nosebleeds or sinus infections occur.
Surgery may be required to correct a deviated nasal septum that causes persistent breathing difficulties.
The doctor diagnosed her with a deviated nasal septum after she complained of constant nasal blockage.
