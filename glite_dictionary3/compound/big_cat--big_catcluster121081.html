big cat

noun
large wild feline, e.g. lion or tiger
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ kæt / (AmE), / bɪɡ kæt / (BrE)
any of several large wild members of the cat family, such as lions, tigers, leopards, and jaguars, typically able to roar

Examples

a big cat roars
Cheetahs are sometimes considered big cats, but they cannot roar.
Many big cats are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.
The zoo houses several big cats, including lions and tigers.

  • synonym – lion: large tawny wild cat native to Africa and India
  • antonym – small cat: small domestic or wild members of the cat family
  • synonym – tiger: large striped feline native to Asia
