remittance person

noun
an exile living on money from home
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪt.əns ˈpɝː.sən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪt.əns ˈpɜː.sən / (BrE)
An exile or emigrant living abroad who is financially supported by regular payments sent from their home country, often due to family arrangement or as a means of keeping them away.

Examples

remittance person living abroad
He became a remittance person in Australia after disgracing himself at home.
Many colonies had more than a few remittance persons living quietly on their allowance from England.
The wealthy family treated their troublesome son as a remittance person, sending him to Canada with a stipend.
