read/write head

noun
device in drives that reads and writes data
Pronunciation: / ˈrid ˌraɪt hɛd / (AmE), / ˈriːd ˌraɪt hɛd / (BrE)
a small electromagnetic device in a storage drive that reads and writes data by changing or detecting magnetic patterns on a disk

Examples

read/write head in a hard drive
As technology advances, read/write heads have become smaller and more accurate.
Damage to the read/write head can cause data loss and make the disk unusable.
The hard disk's read/write head moves rapidly across the platter to access stored information.

Related Terms

  • synonym – read head: device that reads data on a magnetic disk or tape
  • synonym – write head: device that writes data on a magnetic disk or tape
