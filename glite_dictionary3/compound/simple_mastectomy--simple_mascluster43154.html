simple mastectomy

noun
surgical removal of a breast, muscles and nodes intact
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəl məˈstɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl məˈstɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove a breast while leaving the underlying muscles and lymph nodes intact

Examples

simple mastectomy for breast cancer
A simple mastectomy is often performed for early-stage breast cancer as it is less invasive.
After undergoing a simple mastectomy, she recovered quickly and did not need further surgery.
The surgeon recommended a simple mastectomy because the cancer had not spread to the lymph nodes.
