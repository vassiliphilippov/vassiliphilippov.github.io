Roman Empire

proper noun
ancient empire centered on Rome
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mən ˈɛm.paɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
The ancient empire established by Augustus in 27 BC, which at its peak controlled territories in Europe, Africa, and Asia, and was later divided into the Western Roman Empire and the Eastern (Byzantine) Empire in AD 395.

Examples

the Roman Empire fell
After the fall of the Western Roman Empire, the Eastern Empire continued as Byzantium.
Many modern European cities were founded during the time of the Roman Empire.
The Roman Empire reached its greatest territorial extent under Emperor Trajan.
