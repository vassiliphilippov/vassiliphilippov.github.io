checkered whiptail

noun
a lizard with distinctive dark markings
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkərd ˈwɪptaɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəd ˈwɪptaɪl / (BrE)
A species of lizard (Aspidoscelis tesselatus) notable for its distinctive dark, marked pattern, found from southeastern Colorado to eastern Chihuahua.

Examples

checkered whiptail lizard
Biologists found several checkered whiptails basking in the sun near the desert edge.
The checkered whiptail is easily recognized by the distinct pattern on its back.
Unlike its relatives, the checkered whiptail has more pronounced dark markings along its body.
