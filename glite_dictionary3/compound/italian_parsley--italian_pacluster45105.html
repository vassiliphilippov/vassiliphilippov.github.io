Italian parsley

noun
flat-leaved culinary parsley
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæliən ˈpɑrsli / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪən ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
A variety of parsley with flat, dark green leaves, used as a culinary herb for its mild, fresh flavor.

Examples

chopped Italian parsley
Fresh Italian parsley adds a bright flavor to salads, soups, and sauces.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of chopped Italian parsley to garnish the dish.
Unlike curly parsley, Italian parsley has flat, broad leaves and a milder taste.
