time bill

noun
draft payable at a future date
Pronunciation: / taɪm bɪl / (AmE), / taɪm bɪl / (BrE)
A bill of exchange or draft that is payable at a fixed future date rather than on demand.

Examples

time bill matures later
A time bill matures ninety days after the shipping date.
Banks often discount a time bill before it reaches its due date.
The exporter agreed to accept a time bill rather than demand immediate payment.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sight bill: a bill payable on demand (at sight)
