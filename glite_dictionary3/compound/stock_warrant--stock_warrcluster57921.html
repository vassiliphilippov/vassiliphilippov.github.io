stock warrant

noun
certificate granting right to buy stock at set price
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈwɔɹənt / (AmE), / stɒk ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
a document or security that gives you the right to buy a company's shares at a specific price before a certain date

Examples

stock warrant gives right
Investors often use a stock warrant to speculate on the future price of a company's shares.
She invested in a stock warrant that allowed her to buy shares at a lower price if the market rose.
The company issued a stock warrant as an incentive for its employees.
He exercised his stock warrant to purchase 100 shares at the agreed price.
Stock analysts often discuss the potential value of a stock warrant in a company's capital structure.
The company issued stock warrants to attract more investors to their bond offering.
