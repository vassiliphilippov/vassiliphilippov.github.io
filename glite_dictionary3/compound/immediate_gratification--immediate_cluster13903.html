immediate gratification

noun
pleasure or satisfaction without delay
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmiːdiət ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the experience or act of seeking pleasure, satisfaction, or reward without delay or postponement

Examples

seek immediate gratification
Children often find it difficult to resist immediate gratification in favor of long-term rewards.
Many people struggle with immediate gratification when trying to stick to a diet.
The popularity of online shopping reflects our culture's preference for immediate gratification.

Related Terms

  • antonym – delayed gratification: the act of resisting an immediate reward in preference for a later reward
  • synonym – self-indulgence: the act of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasure freely
