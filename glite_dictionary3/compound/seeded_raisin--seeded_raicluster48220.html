seeded raisin

noun
raisin with seeds or formerly seeded
Pronunciation: / ˈsidɪd ˈreɪzən / (AmE), / ˈsiːdɪd ˈreɪzɪn / (BrE)
A raisin that has been dried with its seeds present or after the seeds have been removed, as opposed to a naturally seedless raisin.

Examples

seeded raisin in bread
She bit into a seeded raisin and found a hard seed inside.
The recipe calls for seeded raisins, but you can use seedless ones if you prefer.
Traditional fruitcakes are often made with seeded raisins for added texture.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – raisin: a dried grape
  • antonym – seedless raisin: a raisin from a grape variety that naturally lacks seeds
