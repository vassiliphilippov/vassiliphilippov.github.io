aqueous humor

noun
clear fluid in front part of the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪkwiəs ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈeɪkwɪəs ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
the clear, watery fluid in the space between the cornea and the lens of the eye

Examples

aqueous humor fills eye
An imbalance in the production and drainage of aqueous humor can lead to increased intraocular pressure.
Doctors measured the pressure of the aqueous humor during the eye examination.
The aqueous humor helps maintain the shape of the eyeball and provides nutrients to the cornea.
