Precambrian period

noun
eon before the Phanerozoic era
Pronunciation: / priˈkæmbriən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / priˈkæmbriən ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the eon in Earth's history between the Hadean and Phanerozoic eons, lasting from about 3,800 to 544 million years ago

Examples

the Precambrian period rocks
Geologists found evidence of microbial life from the Precambrian period in Australian rocks.
Many of the oldest minerals on Earth date back to the Precambrian period.
The majority of Earth's geological history falls within the Precambrian period.
