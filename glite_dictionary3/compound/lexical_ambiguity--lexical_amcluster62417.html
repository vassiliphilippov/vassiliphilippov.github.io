lexical ambiguity

noun
a word or phrase with multiple meanings
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛksɪkəl æmˈbɪgjuəti / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəl æmˈbɪɡjuːɪti / (BrE)
The quality of a word or phrase having more than one meaning, leading to uncertainty in interpretation.

Examples

lexical ambiguity in words
Automatic translation systems struggle with lexical ambiguity in languages with many homonyms.
Teachers often discuss lexical ambiguity to help students understand how context determines meaning.
The lexical ambiguity of the word 'bank' can cause confusion between a river bank and a financial bank.
