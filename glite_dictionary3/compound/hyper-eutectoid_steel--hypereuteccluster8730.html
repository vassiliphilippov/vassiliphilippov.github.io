hyper-eutectoid steel

noun
steel with over 0.9% carbon
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpər juːˈtɛktɔɪd stil / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpə juːˈtɛktɔɪd stiːl / (BrE)
Steel containing more than 0.9% carbon by weight, leading to an excess of cementite in its structure.

Examples

hyper-eutectoid steel with high carbon
Hyper-eutectoid steel is often used in applications requiring high hardness and strength.
The microstructure of hyper-eutectoid steel consists of pearlite and excess cementite.
Tool-making frequently utilizes hyper-eutectoid steel due to its wear resistance.
