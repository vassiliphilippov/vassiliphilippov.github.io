two-tier bid

noun
takeover offer with different share prices for control and remaining shares
Pronunciation: / tuː tɪr bɪd / (AmE), / tuː tɪə bɪd / (BrE)
a takeover offer in which the buyer offers a higher price for enough shares to gain control and a lower price for the remaining shares

Examples

two-tier bid offer price
Regulators are considering new rules to address the risks associated with two-tier bids.
Some shareholders felt the two-tier bid was unfair, as not everyone would receive the higher offer.
The company defended itself against a two-tier bid by seeking a friendly buyer.
