cell membrane

noun
biological membrane surrounding a cell
Pronunciation: / sɛl ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / sɛl ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
The thin, flexible outer layer enclosing the cytoplasm of a cell, composed mainly of lipids and proteins, that controls the movement of substances in and out of the cell.

Examples

cell membrane controls entry
Damage to the cell membrane can disrupt the normal function of the cell.
Scientists stained the cell membrane to observe its structure under a microscope.
The cell membrane regulates the transport of nutrients and waste products in and out of the cell.
