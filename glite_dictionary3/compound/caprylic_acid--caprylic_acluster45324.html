caprylic acid

noun
a fatty acid found in butter and oils
Pronunciation: / kəˈprɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / kəˈprɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid with a rancid taste, found in butter, coconut oil, and other fats and oils, used in the manufacture of esters, perfumes, and as a food additive

Examples

caprylic acid in coconut oil
Caprylic acid is often used in the production of esters for perfumes.
Goat milk contains a significant amount of caprylic acid, which contributes to its distinctive flavor.
Researchers studied the effects of caprylic acid on bacterial growth in dairy products.
