book scorpion

noun
minute arachnid found in books
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊk ˌskɔːrpiən / (AmE), / ˈbʊk ˌskɔːpiən / (BrE)
a very small arachnid, resembling a tiny scorpion, that is sometimes found among old books and papers

Examples

tiny book scorpion
A book scorpion scurried across the page as I opened the old novel.
Finding a book scorpion in the attic surprised her, but she learned it was harmless.
Librarians appreciate book scorpions because they feed on pests that damage books.
