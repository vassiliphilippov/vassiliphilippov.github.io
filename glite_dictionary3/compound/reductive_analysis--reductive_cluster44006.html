reductive analysis

noun
oversimplified examination or interpretation
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌktɪv əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌktɪv əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
an examination or interpretation that oversimplifies a subject by reducing its complexity to basic elements, often ignoring important details or nuances

Examples

reductive analysis of literature
A reductive analysis may provide clarity but can miss the nuance present in complex systems.
Critics accused the author of using reductive analysis when interpreting the novel's themes.
The report relied on a reductive analysis that failed to address underlying social factors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – oversimplification: the act of simplifying something so much that the meaning or truth is distorted
