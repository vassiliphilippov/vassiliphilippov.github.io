goblet cell

noun
mucus-secreting epithelial cell
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːblət sɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblɪt sɛl / (BrE)
an epithelial cell found in mucous membranes that secretes mucus to protect and lubricate body surfaces

Examples

goblet cell secretes mucus
Goblet cells are essential for maintaining a healthy barrier in the intestines.
Excessive production of mucus by goblet cells may occur in certain lung diseases.
Goblet cells in the respiratory tract help trap and clear dust particles.
