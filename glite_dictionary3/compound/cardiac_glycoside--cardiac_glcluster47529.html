cardiac glycoside

noun
heart-stimulating plant compound (medicine)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑr.di.æk ˈɡlaɪ.kəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.æk ˈɡlaɪ.kəˌsaɪd / (BrE)
a class of organic compounds obtained from certain plants, used in medicine to increase the force of contraction of the heart in heart failure

Examples

cardiac glycoside medicine
Many cardiac glycosides are derived from the foxglove plant.
Proper dosage of a cardiac glycoside is crucial due to its narrow therapeutic range.
The doctor prescribed a cardiac glycoside to help manage the patient's heart failure.
