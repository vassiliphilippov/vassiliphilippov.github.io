superior planet

noun
planet with orbit outside Earth's
Pronunciation: / səˈpɪriɚ ˈplænɪt / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
any planet in the Solar System whose orbit lies outside that of the Earth, such as Mars, Jupiter, or Saturn.

Examples

superior planet like Jupiter
Astronomers once used the terms 'inferior planet' and superior planet to describe planetary positions in the Solar System.
Mars is classified as a superior planet because its orbit lies outside Earth’s.
The motion of a superior planet appears retrograde when Earth passes it in its orbit.
