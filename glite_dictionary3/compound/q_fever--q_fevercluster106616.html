Q fever

noun
acute infectious disease; resembles influenza
Pronunciation: / kju ˈfi vɚ / (AmE), / kju ˈfiː və / (BrE)
an acute, infectious disease caused by the bacterium Coxiella burnetii, characterized by fever, chills, and headache, often resembling influenza

Examples

Q fever outbreak
Outbreaks of Q fever have occurred in rural areas where livestock are kept.
Proper hygiene and control measures can help prevent the spread of Q fever in agricultural settings.
The doctor suspected Q fever after the patient returned from working on a farm with flu-like symptoms.
