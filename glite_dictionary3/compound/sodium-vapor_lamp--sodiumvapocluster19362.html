sodium-vapor lamp

noun
lamp producing yellow light with sodium vapor
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm ˈveɪpər læmp / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˈveɪpə læmp / (BrE)
a lamp in which an electric current passes through a tube containing sodium vapor to produce a bright yellow light, commonly used in street lighting

Examples

sodium-vapor lamp street lighting
A sodium-vapor lamp emits a distinctive yellow glow that is easily recognizable at night.
Many highways are lit by sodium-vapor lamps because they are energy-efficient and long-lasting.
The city replaced all its old streetlights with new sodium-vapor lamps to improve nighttime visibility.
