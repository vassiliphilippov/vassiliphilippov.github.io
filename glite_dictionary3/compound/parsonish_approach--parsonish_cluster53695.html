parsonish approach

noun
moralistic, clergyman-like manner
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrsənɪʃ əˈproʊt͡ʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsənɪʃ əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a method or manner that is reminiscent of a clergyman, especially in being moralistic or preachy

Examples

parsonish approach to problems
Her parsonish approach to management often annoyed the staff.
His parsonish approach made the debate feel more like a sermon than a discussion.
The review criticized the film for its parsonish approach to social issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym – didactic: intended to teach, especially in a patronizing way
  • synonym – preachy: tending to give moral advice in an annoying or tedious way
