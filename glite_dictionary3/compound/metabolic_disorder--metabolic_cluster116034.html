metabolic disorder

noun
medical condition affecting metabolism
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by problems in the body's metabolism, such as the way the body processes food or energy.

Examples

rare metabolic disorder
A genetic mutation can sometimes cause a rare metabolic disorder in infants.
Diabetes is a common type of metabolic disorder affecting millions worldwide.
