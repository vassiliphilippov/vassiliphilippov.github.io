Old World leishmaniasis

noun
ulcerative skin disease in the Old World
Pronunciation: / oʊld wɜrld liːʃ.məˈnaɪ.əsɪs / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld liːʃ.məˈnaɪ.əsɪs / (BrE)
A form of leishmaniasis occurring in parts of the Old World (Europe, Asia, Africa), characterized by ulcerative skin lesions.

Examples

Old World leishmaniasis skin sores
Old World leishmaniasis is commonly transmitted by sandflies in certain regions of Africa and Asia.
A biopsy confirmed that the skin ulcers were caused by Old World leishmaniasis.
Some travelers contracted Old World leishmaniasis after spending time in rural areas of the Middle East.
