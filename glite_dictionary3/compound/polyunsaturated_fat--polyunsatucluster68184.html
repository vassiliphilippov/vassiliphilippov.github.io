polyunsaturated fat

noun
a fat with multiple double bonds, found in plant oils and fish
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd fæt / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd fæt / (BrE)
A type of fat containing long carbon chains with two or more double bonds, often considered healthier for the heart and commonly found in plant oils and fish.

Examples

healthy polyunsaturated fat
Doctors often recommend replacing saturated fats with polyunsaturated fat to promote heart health.
Many vegetable oils, such as sunflower and soybean oil, are good sources of polyunsaturated fat.
The nutrition label lists the amount of polyunsaturated fat per serving.

Related Terms

  • antonym – saturated fat: a fat with no double bonds between carbon atoms
