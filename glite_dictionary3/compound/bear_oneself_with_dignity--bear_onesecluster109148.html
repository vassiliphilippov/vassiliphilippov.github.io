to bear oneself with dignity

verb
conduct oneself with dignity
Pronunciation: / bɛr wʌnˈsɛlf wɪð ˈdɪɡnɪti / (AmE), / bɛə wʌnˈsɛlf wɪð ˈdɪɡnɪti / (BrE)
to conduct oneself in a composed and dignified manner, especially in challenging or formal situations

Examples

Despite the harsh criticism, she continued to bear herself with dignity at every meeting.
Even in defeat, the athlete tried to bear himself with dignity in front of his fans.
He managed to bear himself with dignity throughout the long and difficult trial.
