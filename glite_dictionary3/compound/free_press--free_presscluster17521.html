free press

noun
independent, uncensored news media
Pronunciation: / friː prɛs / (AmE), / friː prɛs / (BrE)
news media that operate independently and are not controlled or censored by the government, especially in matters of politics or ideology.

Examples

independent free press
Many countries restrict journalists, making a true free press difficult to achieve.
She believes in the importance of the free press to hold governments accountable.
The existence of a free press is a key indicator of a healthy democracy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – censored press: news media subject to government control or censorship
  • synonym – journalism: the activity or profession of reporting news
