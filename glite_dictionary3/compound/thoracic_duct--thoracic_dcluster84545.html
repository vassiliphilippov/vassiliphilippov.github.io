thoracic duct

noun
main lymphatic duct in the body
Pronunciation: / θəˈræsɪk dʌkt / (AmE), / θəˈræsɪk dʌkt / (BrE)
the main duct of the lymphatic system, carrying lymph from the lower body and left side of the upper body into the bloodstream

Examples

the thoracic duct carries lymph
Damage to the thoracic duct can result in serious lymphatic leakage.
Surgeons must take care not to injure the thoracic duct during procedures in the chest.
The thoracic duct drains lymph from most of the body into the left subclavian vein.
