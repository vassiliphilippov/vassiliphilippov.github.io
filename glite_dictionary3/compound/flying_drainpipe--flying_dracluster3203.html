flying drainpipe

noun
simple, primitive jet engine
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈdreɪnˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈdreɪnˌpʌɪp / (BrE)
A simple type of jet engine, especially a pulsejet, requiring high-speed launch and known for its rudimentary tube-like design.

Examples

a flying drainpipe engine
Although inefficient, the flying drainpipe allowed missiles to travel long distances during the war.
Engineers studied the flying drainpipe to understand pulsed propulsion systems in early aviation history.
The V-1 flying bomb was powered by a flying drainpipe, giving it its infamous buzzing sound.
