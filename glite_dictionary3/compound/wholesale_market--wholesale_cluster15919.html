wholesale market

noun
market for selling goods to retailers
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊlˌseɪl ˈmɑrkət / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a market where goods are sold in large quantities to retailers, rather than directly to consumers

Examples

wholesale market for vegetables
Fresh produce is distributed through the wholesale market before reaching local grocery stores.
Many small retailers rely on the wholesale market to stock their shelves affordably.
The city has a large wholesale market where shop owners buy their supplies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – retail market: a market where goods are sold directly to consumers
  • synonym – wholesaler: a person or business that sells goods in large quantities to retailers
