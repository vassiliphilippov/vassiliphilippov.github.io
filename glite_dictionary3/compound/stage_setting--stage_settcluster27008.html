stage setting

noun
arrangement of scenery and props for a play or movie
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪdʒ ˌsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒ ˌsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement of scenery and props on a stage or film set to represent the location where the action occurs

Examples

stage setting for a play
A realistic stage setting can greatly enhance a performance's impact.
She designed the stage setting to complement the play's dramatic themes.
The elaborate stage setting transported the audience to Victorian London.
