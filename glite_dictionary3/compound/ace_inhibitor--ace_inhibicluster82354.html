ACE inhibitor

noun
drug that inhibits angiotensin-converting enzyme
Pronunciation: / eɪ si ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / eɪ si ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a drug that lowers blood pressure by blocking the activity of angiotensin-converting enzyme, used to treat hypertension and heart failure

Examples

take an ACE inhibitor pill
Common side effects of an ACE inhibitor include a persistent cough and increased urination.
Many patients with heart failure benefit from taking an ACE inhibitor daily.
Your doctor may prescribe an ACE inhibitor to help control your high blood pressure.
