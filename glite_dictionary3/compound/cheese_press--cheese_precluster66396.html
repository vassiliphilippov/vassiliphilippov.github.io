cheese press

noun
device for pressing cheese curds
Pronunciation: / tʃiːz prɛs / (AmE), / tʃiːz pres / (BrE)
a device used for pressing and shaping curds into cheese by applying pressure

Examples

cheese press curds
After the curds are ready, they are placed in the cheese press for several hours.
Modern dairies often use a hydraulic cheese press to speed up production.
The traditional cheesemaker used a cheese press to give the cheese its firm texture.
