alpha-linolenic acid

noun
an essential omega-3 fatty acid
Pronunciation: / ˌælfə lɪˌnoʊlɛnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌælfə lɪˌnəʊliːnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a polyunsaturated omega-3 fatty acid essential for human nutrition, found chiefly in vegetable oils such as canola and flaxseed

Examples

alpha-linolenic acid in flaxseed oil
Canola oil contains high levels of alpha-linolenic acid, making it popular among health-conscious consumers.
Flaxseed is an excellent source of alpha-linolenic acid, an important nutrient for heart health.
