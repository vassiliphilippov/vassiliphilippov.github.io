ventricular septal defect

noun
congenital heart defect with hole between ventricles
Pronunciation: / vɛnˈtrɪkjələr ˈsɛptəl dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / vɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə ˈsɛptəl dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a congenital heart defect in which there is an abnormal opening in the wall (septum) dividing the left and right ventricles, allowing blood to flow between them

Examples

a ventricular septal defect in babies
Children born with a ventricular septal defect often require close medical monitoring.
Surgery was performed to repair the ventricular septal defect and improve the child's heart function.
The doctor diagnosed the infant with a ventricular septal defect after hearing a heart murmur.
