T cell

noun
immune system lymphocyte from the thymus
Pronunciation: / ti sɛl / (AmE), / ti sɛl / (BrE)
a type of small lymphocyte that develops in the thymus and plays a central role in the immune system, particularly in responding to infected or malignant cells

Examples

T cell attacks virus
An HIV infection targets and destroys T cells, weakening the body's ability to defend itself.
Scientists are researching how T cell therapies could help fight certain types of cancer.
The doctor explained that a healthy immune response relies on the activity of T cells.
