three-point switch

noun
electric switch with three terminals
Pronunciation: / ˈθri pɔɪnt swɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈθriː pɔɪnt swɪtʃ / (BrE)
An electric switch with three terminals, used to control a circuit from two different locations.

Examples

install a three-point switch
A three-point switch allows you to turn the lights on or off from two different spots in the hallway.
Electricians commonly install a three-point switch in staircases for convenience and safety.
She replaced the old switch with a modern three-point switch to control the lighting from both entrances.
