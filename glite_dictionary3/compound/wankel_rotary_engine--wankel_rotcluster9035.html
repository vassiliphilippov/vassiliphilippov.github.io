Wankel rotary engine

noun
rotary internal-combustion engine design
Pronunciation: / ˈwæŋkəl ˈroʊˌtɛri ˈɛnʤɪn / (AmE), / ˈwaŋkəl ˈrəʊtəri ˈɛnʤɪn / (BrE)
A type of four-stroke internal-combustion engine that uses a rotary design without reciprocating parts, invented by Felix Wankel.

Examples

Wankel rotary engine in a Mazda
Mazda popularized the Wankel rotary engine in many of its sports cars during the late twentieth century.
Some engineers admire the compact design of the Wankel rotary engine for specialized applications.
The Wankel rotary engine offers a smoother ride due to its lack of reciprocating parts.
