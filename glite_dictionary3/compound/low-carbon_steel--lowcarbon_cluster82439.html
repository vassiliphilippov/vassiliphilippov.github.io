low-carbon steel

noun
steel with very low carbon content
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊ ˈkɑrbən stil / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈkɑːbən stiːl / (BrE)
A type of steel containing less than 0.15% carbon, known for its softness, ductility, and ease of welding.

Examples

low-carbon steel construction beams
Construction beams are often made from low-carbon steel due to its strength and workability.
The welder prefers low-carbon steel because it is easy to join and form.
This pipe is manufactured using low-carbon steel to prevent brittleness.

Related Terms

  • antonym – high-carbon steel: steel with a high percentage of carbon
  • synonym – mild steel: steel with a low carbon content similar to low-carbon steel
