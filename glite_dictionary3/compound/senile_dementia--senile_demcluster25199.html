senile dementia

noun
dementia in the elderly
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːnaɪl dɪˈmɛnʃə / (AmE), / ˈsiːnaɪl dɪˈmɛnʃə / (BrE)
A form of dementia occurring in elderly people, caused by progressive degeneration of brain tissue not due to cerebrovascular disease.

Examples

senile dementia in elderly people
Advances in medicine have improved the understanding and treatment of senile dementia.
Her grandmother was diagnosed with senile dementia and required constant supervision.
Many families struggle to cope when a loved one develops senile dementia.
