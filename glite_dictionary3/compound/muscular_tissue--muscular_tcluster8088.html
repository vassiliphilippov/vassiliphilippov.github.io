muscular tissue

noun
tissue made of muscle cells
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskjələr ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊlə ˈtɪʃuː / (BrE)
Animal tissue made up mostly of contractile muscle cells that enables movement and force in the body.

Examples

muscular tissue damage
Injuries to muscular tissue can take a long time to heal because of the complex structure of muscle fibers.
Regular exercise strengthens muscular tissue throughout the body.
The heart is made up of a special type of muscular tissue that contracts rhythmically.
