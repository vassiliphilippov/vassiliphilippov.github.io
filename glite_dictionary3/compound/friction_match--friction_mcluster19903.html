friction match

noun
match that ignites by striking
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪkʃən ˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən ˌmatʃ / (BrE)
a match consisting of a thin strip of wood or cardboard tipped with a chemical that ignites when struck against a rough surface

Examples

light a friction match
He struck a friction match against the box to light the candle.
Modern lighters have largely replaced the friction match for most people.
The old factory once produced thousands of friction matches a day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – match: a small stick for making fire, ignited by friction
  • degree higher – safety match: a type of match that lights only by striking on a specially prepared surface
