streamline flow

noun
steady fluid flow with constant velocity at each point
Pronunciation: / ˈstriːmlaɪn floʊ / (AmE), / ˈstriːmlaɪn fləʊ / (BrE)
A steady flow of a fluid in which the velocity at every point remains constant over time.

Examples

streamline flow over wings
Aircraft are designed to encourage streamline flow over their wings to reduce drag.
Once the speed increases beyond a certain point, streamline flow transitions to turbulence.
The water in the pipe exhibits streamline flow at low velocities.

Related Terms

  • synonym – laminar flow: smooth, orderly flow in parallel layers with little mixing
  • antonym – turbulent flow: irregular fluid flow with mixing and eddies
