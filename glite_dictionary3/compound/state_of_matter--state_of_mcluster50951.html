state of matter

noun
distinct form of matter, such as solid, liquid, or gas
Pronunciation: / steɪt əv ˈmætər / (AmE), / steɪt əv ˈmætə / (BrE)
any of the distinct forms in which matter can exist, traditionally solid, liquid, or gas, each with specific physical properties.

Examples

state of matter solid form
At room temperature, oxygen's state of matter is gaseous.
Plasma is sometimes considered a fourth state of matter beyond solid, liquid, and gas.
Water exists as a solid, liquid, or gas, depending on the state of matter.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – gas: a state of matter with neither fixed shape nor fixed volume
  • degree higher – liquid: a state of matter with fixed volume but no fixed shape
  • synonym – phase: a distinct form of a substance, especially in physical science
  • degree lower – solid: a state of matter with fixed shape and volume
