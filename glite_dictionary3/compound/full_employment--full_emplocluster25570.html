full employment

noun
economic condition of universal employment
Pronunciation: / fʊl ɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (AmE), / fʊl ɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (BrE)
a situation in which everyone who is willing and able to work at prevailing wage rates is employed

Examples

achieve full employment
Government policies aim to achieve full employment by stimulating economic growth.
Many economists debate whether full employment is realistically attainable in modern economies.
The country has not seen full employment since the economic boom of the 1960s.
