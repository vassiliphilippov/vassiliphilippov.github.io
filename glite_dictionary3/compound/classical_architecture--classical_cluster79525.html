classical architecture

noun
architecture inspired by ancient Greeks and Romans
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪkəl ˌɑrkɪˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
A style of architecture influenced by the principles and designs of ancient Greek and Roman buildings, characterized by columns, symmetry, and proportion.

Examples

classical architecture with columns
Many government buildings in Washington, D.C., were inspired by classical architecture.
She studied classical architecture to better understand the origins of Western building techniques.
The museum's facade was designed in the classical architecture style, complete with tall marble columns.
