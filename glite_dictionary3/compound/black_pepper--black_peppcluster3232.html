black pepper

noun
spice made from whole peppercorns
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A spice made from whole dried peppercorns with their outer husks, commonly used to season food.

Examples

freshly ground black pepper
Freshly ground black pepper enhances the taste of any dish.
I prefer using black pepper over white pepper in my recipes.
She added a pinch of black pepper to the soup for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – peppercorn: the dried berry from which black pepper is made
  • antonym – white pepper: spice made from ripe peppercorns with the husk removed
