Laptev Sea

proper noun
sea in the Arctic Ocean north of Siberia
Pronunciation: / ˈlæp.tɛv ˌsi / (AmE), / ˈlæp.tɛv ˌsiː / (BrE)
a marginal sea of the Arctic Ocean north of Siberia, between the Taimyr Peninsula and the New Siberian Islands, usually covered with ice for most of the year

Examples

the Laptev Sea north of Siberia
Many rivers from Siberia flow into the Laptev Sea, influencing its salinity and ecosystem.
Scientists study the Laptev Sea to monitor changes in Arctic climate patterns.
The Laptev Sea is often covered by ice, making navigation challenging for much of the year.
