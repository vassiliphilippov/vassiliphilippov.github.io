chronostratigraphic unit

noun
division of rock by geologic time
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊnoʊstrəˌtaɪɡrəfɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌkrɒnəʊstrəˌtaɪɡrəfɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A division of rock layers that corresponds to a specific interval of geological time, used to classify rocks based on their age.

chronostratigraphic unit of rocks
Finding the precise chronostratigraphic unit helps paleontologists date the fossils they discover.
Scientists classify rock layers into chronostratigraphic units to study changes over geological time.
