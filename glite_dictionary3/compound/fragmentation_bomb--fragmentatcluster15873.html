fragmentation bomb

noun
bomb that scatters deadly fragments on explosion
Pronunciation: / ˌfræɡmənˈteɪʃən bɑm / (AmE), / ˌfræɡmənˈteɪʃən bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb designed so its casing breaks into numerous high-velocity fragments upon detonation, primarily to cause injury or damage over an area

Examples

explode a fragmentation bomb
After the explosion, investigators recovered pieces of the fragmentation bomb scattered throughout the area.
Many injuries were caused by shrapnel from the fragmentation bomb during the battle.
The military used a fragmentation bomb to disable enemy vehicles and infantry in the open field.

Related Terms

  • synonym – grenade: a small hand-thrown bomb that also uses fragmentation to cause injury
  • antonym – incendiary bomb: a bomb designed to start fires rather than produce fragments
