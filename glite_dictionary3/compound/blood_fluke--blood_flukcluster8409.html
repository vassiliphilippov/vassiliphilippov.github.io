blood fluke

noun
parasitic flatworm in blood vessels
Pronunciation: / blʌd fluːk / (AmE), / blʌd fluːk / (BrE)
a type of parasitic flatworm that lives in the blood vessels of mammals, especially causing diseases such as schistosomiasis

Examples

blood fluke infection
People living near certain freshwater sources are at higher risk of exposure to blood flukes.
Scientists studied the lifecycle of the blood fluke to develop better treatments for the infection.
The blood fluke is responsible for millions of cases of schistosomiasis worldwide each year.
