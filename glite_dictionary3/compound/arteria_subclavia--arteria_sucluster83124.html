arteria subclavia

noun
major artery to neck and arms
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪriə səbˈkleɪviə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriə səbˈkleɪvɪə / (BrE)
either of two major arteries that supply blood to the neck and arms, branching from the aorta or brachiocephalic artery

Examples

arteria subclavia supplies blood
During the operation, the surgeon carefully examined the arteria subclavia for any signs of blockage.
Injuries to the arteria subclavia can lead to serious complications due to its role in supplying blood to the arm.
The left arteria subclavia branches directly from the aortic arch.
