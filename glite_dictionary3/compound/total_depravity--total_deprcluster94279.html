total depravity

noun
doctrine that humans are inherently sinful (Calvinism)
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊtəl dɪˈprævəti / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəl dɪˈpravɪti / (BrE)
(in Calvinist doctrine) the belief that all humans are born in a state of moral corruption due to original sin and are incapable of achieving salvation without divine grace.

Examples

total depravity in Calvinism
Calvinists believe in total depravity, teaching that humans are incapable of choosing God without divine intervention.
In theological debates, he defended the concept of total depravity against its critics.
The doctrine of total depravity emphasizes the depth of humanity's fallen nature.
