glass fibre

noun
thin thread of glass for transmitting light or reinforcing materials
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A very thin thread made of glass, used especially in technology to transmit light or images, or to reinforce materials.

Examples

glass fibre cable
His research focuses on strengthening materials using glass fibre.
Modern endoscopes use bundles of glass fibre to transmit images inside the body.
The cables are made from glass fibre to allow fast data transmission.
