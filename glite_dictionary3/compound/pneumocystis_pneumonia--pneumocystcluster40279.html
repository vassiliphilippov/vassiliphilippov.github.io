pneumocystis pneumonia

noun
fungal pneumonia in immunocompromised patients
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːmoʊˈsɪstɪs nuːˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈsɪstɪs njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A form of pneumonia caused by the fungus Pneumocystis jirovecii, occurring mainly in infants or people with weakened immune systems, such as those with AIDS.

Examples

pneumocystis pneumonia in AIDS patients
Infants with compromised immune systems may contract pneumocystis pneumonia during their first year of life.
Many patients with HIV are at increased risk of developing pneumocystis pneumonia.
Prompt diagnosis and treatment of pneumocystis pneumonia can significantly improve patient outcomes.

  • synonym – pneumonia: inflammation of the lungs, typically due to infection
