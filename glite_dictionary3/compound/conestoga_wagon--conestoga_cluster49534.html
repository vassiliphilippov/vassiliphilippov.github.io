Conestoga wagon

noun
19th-century American pioneer wagon
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑnəˈstoʊɡə ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnɪˈstəʊɡə ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A large covered wagon with broad wheels and an arched canvas top, used by United States pioneers to cross the prairies in the 19th century.

Examples

a Conestoga wagon crossing prairies
A Conestoga wagon could carry several tons of cargo across rough terrain.
The museum displayed a restored Conestoga wagon from the 1800s.
The pioneers loaded their belongings into a Conestoga wagon before heading west.
