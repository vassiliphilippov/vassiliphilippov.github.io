puritan view

noun
strict moral or religious belief
Pronunciation: / ˈpjʊrətən vjuː / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪtən vjuː / (BrE)
a strict moral or religious attitude, especially one with an emphasis on self-discipline and suppression of pleasure, characteristic of Puritanism

Examples

strict puritan view
He was brought up with a puritan view that emphasized hard work and self-restraint.
Her puritan view of relationships led her to disapprove of casual dating.
The company's policies reflect a puritan view on workplace conduct.

Related Terms

  • antonym – permissive: allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior
  • synonym – strict: not allowing for relaxation or leniency; severe in discipline or judgment
