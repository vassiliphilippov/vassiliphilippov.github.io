ash-leaved maple

noun
a North American maple tree with ash-like leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈæʃ livd ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈæʃ liːvd ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
a maple tree (Acer negundo) native to eastern and central North America, known for its compound leaves resembling those of an ash tree

Examples

an ash-leaved maple tree
An ash-leaved maple can be easily recognized by its compound leaves, unlike most other maples.
The ash-leaved maple is often called box elder and grows well in urban environments.
They planted an ash-leaved maple in the backyard for shade and quick growth.
