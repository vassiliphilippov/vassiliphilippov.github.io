First Crusade

proper noun
Christian military campaign (1096-1099) to capture Jerusalem
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
a military expedition by Western European Christians from 1096 to 1099, aimed at recapturing Jerusalem from Muslim control, resulting in the establishment of several Crusader states

Examples

the First Crusade captures Jerusalem
Many knights joined the First Crusade seeking both religious salvation and adventure.
The First Crusade marked a turning point in the history of medieval Europe.
The capture of Jerusalem in the First Crusade had lasting repercussions for the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Crusades: series of religious wars between Christians and Muslims over control of holy sites
  • degree higher – Second Crusade: the second major crusade launched from Europe to the Holy Land (1147–1149)
