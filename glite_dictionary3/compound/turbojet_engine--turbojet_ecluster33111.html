turbojet engine

noun
jet engine using a turbine-driven fan
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːboʊˌdʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌdʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A jet engine in which air is drawn in by a turbine-driven fan, mixed with fuel and burned to produce a high-speed exhaust that creates thrust.

Examples

turbojet engine powers aircraft
Early turbojet engines were less efficient than modern turbofan engines.
Technicians spent weeks repairing the damaged turbojet engine before the aircraft could fly again.
The fighter plane was powered by a state-of-the-art turbojet engine capable of supersonic speeds.

Related Terms
