Neolithic Age

noun
last period of the Stone Age; new stone tools and farming
Pronunciation: / niˈɑː.ləˌθɪk eɪdʒ / (AmE), / niːˈɒl.ɪ.θɪk eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the last period of the Stone Age, marked by the development of agriculture and polished stone tools, beginning around 10,000 BC in the Middle East and later elsewhere

Examples

the Neolithic Age began
Many early human settlements were established during the Neolithic Age.
Stone tools from the Neolithic Age have been found in archaeological sites across Europe.
The Neolithic Age saw the domestication of plants and animals, changing human society forever.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – Bronze Age: age following the Neolithic Age, marked by use of bronze tools
  • degree lower – Paleolithic Age: earlier part of the Stone Age, before Neolithic
