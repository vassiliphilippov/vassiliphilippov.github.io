bandwagon jumper

noun
person who follows latest trends or popular choices
Pronunciation: / ˈbændˌwæɡən ˈdʒʌmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈbændˌwæɡwən ˈdʒʌmpə / (BrE)
a person who frequently switches allegiance or support to whatever is currently popular or trending

Examples

a bandwagon jumper in sports
As soon as the new technology became popular, the bandwagon jumper switched to using it.
Everyone accused him of being a bandwagon jumper when he started supporting the championship team.
True fans stayed loyal, but the bandwagon jumper quickly abandoned the team after one loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fair-weather fan: a supporter who is only loyal during successful times
  • antonym – trendsetter: a person who leads trends rather than follows them
