brushback pitch

noun
baseball pitch thrown to intimidate
Pronunciation: / ˈbrʌʃˌbæk pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃˌbæk pɪtʃ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitch thrown close to the batter to force them away from the plate or intimidate them.

Examples

throw a brushback pitch
After the umpire issued a warning for a brushback pitch, the game grew more intense.
Many batters are unsettled by a well-placed brushback pitch early in the count.
The pitcher threw a brushback pitch to warn the hitter against crowding the plate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beanball: a pitch deliberately thrown at a batter's head
  • degree lower – fastball: a pitch thrown at high speed
