evasive answer

noun
law: reply not admitting or denying allegations
Pronunciation: / ɪˈveɪsɪv ˈænsər / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪsɪv ˈɑːnsə / (BrE)
(law) an answer by a defendant that fails to clearly admit or deny the allegations made in a complaint.

Examples

evasive answer in court
An evasive answer can result in additional legal action or requests for clarification.
The court criticized the use of an evasive answer in response to clear questions.
The defendant gave an evasive answer instead of addressing the allegations directly.
