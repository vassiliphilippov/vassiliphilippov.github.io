great-leaved macrophylla

noun
large shrub or tree with huge leaves and creamy flowers
Pronunciation: / ˌɡreɪt ˈlivd ˌmækroʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪt ˈliːvd ˌmæk.rəʊˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
A large deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its huge leaves arranged in dense false whorls and large creamy flowers tinged with purple at the base.
