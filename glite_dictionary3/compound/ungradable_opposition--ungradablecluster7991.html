ungradable opposition

noun
opposition with no intermediate degree
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡreɪdəbəl ˌɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪdəbəl ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
an opposition between two terms with no intermediate degree; either one or the other applies, without gradation

Examples

ungradable opposition alive-dead
Alive and dead represent an ungradable opposition because there is no intermediate state.
In binary distinctions such as male and female, we see an example of ungradable opposition.
The concept of ungradable opposition is useful when analyzing antonyms like true/false.
