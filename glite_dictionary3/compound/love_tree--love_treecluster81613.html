love tree

noun
Mediterranean tree with purple-red flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌv ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈlʌv ˌtriː / (BrE)
A small tree of the eastern Mediterranean, known for its abundant purplish-red flowers that grow directly from the stems before the leaves appear; often cultivated in mild regions, and valued for its wood used in veneers.

Examples

flowering love tree
Gardeners prize the love tree for its striking appearance and easy maintenance.
The love tree blooms profusely in early spring, covering its branches with vivid flowers.
The wood of the love tree is often used to create decorative veneers for furniture.
