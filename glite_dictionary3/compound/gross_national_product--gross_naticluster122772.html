gross national product

noun
total value of goods/services by a nation's citizens
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊs ˈnæʃənl ˈprɑːdʌkt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs ˈnæʃənəl ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
the total market value of all goods and services produced by a country's citizens and capital, typically measured over one year; formerly a primary measure of a nation's economic activity.

Examples

gross national product growth
Analysts often compare gross national product with gross domestic product to assess economic performance.
Before being replaced by other measures, gross national product was the main indicator of economic health.
The country's gross national product grew by 3% last year despite economic challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gross domestic product: total market value of goods and services produced within a country's borders
