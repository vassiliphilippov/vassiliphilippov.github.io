catabatic wind

noun
wind caused by descending cold air
Pronunciation: / ˌkæt.əˈbæt.ɪk wɪnd / (AmE), / ˌkat.əˈbat.ɪk wɪnd / (BrE)
A wind that is produced when cold, dense air flows downward from a higher elevation, such as a mountain or plateau.

Examples

strong catabatic wind
Pilots must be cautious when flying near valleys prone to sudden catabatic wind.
Strong catabatic wind swept down the mountainside, chilling the village below.
The Antarctic coast experiences fierce catabatic wind that can last for days.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anabatic wind: a wind caused by the upward movement of warm air
