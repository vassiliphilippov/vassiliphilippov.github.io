literary argument

noun
summary of a literary work's plot or subject
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri ˈɑːrgjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈɑːgjumənt / (BrE)
a summary or outline of the subject or plot of a literary work, especially placed at the beginning of a poem, play, or book

Examples

literary argument before a poem
Scholars often analyze the literary argument to uncover the author's intentions.
The literary argument helped me understand the poem's main themes before I began reading.
The editor included a literary argument at the beginning of each chapter to aid the reader.
