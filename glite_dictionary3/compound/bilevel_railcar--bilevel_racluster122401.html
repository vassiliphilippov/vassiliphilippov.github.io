bilevel railcar

noun
rail car with two passenger levels
Pronunciation: / baɪˌlɛvəl ˈreɪlˌkɑr / (AmE), / baɪˌlɛvəl ˈreɪlˌkɑː / (BrE)
A rail car with two levels of passenger accommodation, designed to increase passenger capacity compared to single-level cars.

Examples

modern bilevel railcar
From the platform, you could see the distinctive tall profile of the bilevel railcar as it pulled into the station.
Many commuter lines have switched to bilevel railcars to handle increased ridership without adding extra trains.
The new train features a bilevel railcar design, allowing more passengers to be seated comfortably during rush hour.
