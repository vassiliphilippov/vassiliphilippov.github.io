drainage area

noun
land drained by a river system
Pronunciation: / ˈdreɪ.nɪdʒ ˌɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdreɪ.nɪdʒ ˌeə.ri.ə / (BrE)
the entire land area from which water drains into a particular river, stream, or body of water

Examples

drainage area of a river
Proper management of the drainage area is essential for preventing floods downstream.
The Mississippi River has the largest drainage area of any river in North America.
The scientists studied the drainage area to predict water flow patterns during heavy rainfall.

Related Terms

  • synonym – catchment area: the area from which rainfall flows into a river, lake, or reservoir
  • synonym – watershed: an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers, basins, or seas
