sea crawfish

noun
marine crustacean similar to spiny lobster
Pronunciation: / si ˈkrɔːˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / siː ˈkrɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large edible marine crustacean with a spiny shell and no large pincers, closely related to the spiny lobster

Examples

sea crawfish dish
Divers harvesting sea crawfish must be careful of the creature's sharp spines.
The Caribbean is known for its delicious sea crawfish, often served with spicy sauces.
Unlike the common lobster, the sea crawfish does not have large pincers.
