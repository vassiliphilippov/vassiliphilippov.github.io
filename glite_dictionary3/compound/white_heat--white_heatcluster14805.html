white heat

noun
extremely high temperature where something glows white
Pronunciation: / waɪt hiːt / (AmE), / waɪt hiːt / (BrE)
The extreme temperature at which an object becomes so hot that it glows white; intense heat.

Examples

white heat of metal
At white heat, even the toughest metals become malleable.
The metal rod was heated to white heat before being shaped by the blacksmith.
The process requires temperatures approaching white heat for the reaction to occur.
