time signature

noun
musical notation for beats in a measure
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪm ˌsɪɡ.nə.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm ˌsɪɡ.nə.tʃə / (BrE)
A sign at the beginning of a piece of music that shows the number of beats in each measure and which note value counts as one beat.

Examples

time signature in sheet music
Before you begin, check the time signature at the start of the music.
She struggled to play the piece correctly because the time signature kept changing.
The waltz is usually written with a time signature of 3/4.
