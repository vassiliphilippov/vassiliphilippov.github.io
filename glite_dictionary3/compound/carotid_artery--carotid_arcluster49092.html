carotid artery

noun
major artery in the neck supplying blood to the head
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑt̬.ɪd ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / kəˈrɒt.ɪd ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
either of the two main arteries in the neck that supply blood to the head and neck, branching from the aorta

Examples

carotid artery in the neck
A blockage in the carotid artery can lead to a stroke.
The doctor checked his pulse by feeling the carotid artery in his neck.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the carotid artery during the procedure.
