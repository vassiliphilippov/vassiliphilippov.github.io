green manure

noun
crop plowed in to fertilize soil
Pronunciation: / ɡrin məˈnʊr / (AmE), / ɡriːn məˈnjʊə / (BrE)
A crop that is grown and then plowed into the soil to improve its fertility by adding organic matter and nutrients.

Examples

green manure crop
By plowing green manure into the soil, organic farmers reduce the need for chemical fertilizers.
Farmers often use green manure like clover or alfalfa to enrich their fields naturally.
Leguminous plants serve as effective green manure to restore nutrients to depleted soils.
