glide slope

noun
final landing path of an aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlaɪd ˌsloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɡlaɪd sləʊp / (BrE)
the downward flight path that an aircraft follows as it approaches and lands on a runway, especially as indicated by electronic guidance systems

Examples

follow the glide slope
A malfunction in the airport's glide slope system required pilots to land manually.
Air traffic control reported that the glide slope was clear for the incoming flight.
The pilot adjusted the plane's angle to stay on the glide slope for a smooth landing.
