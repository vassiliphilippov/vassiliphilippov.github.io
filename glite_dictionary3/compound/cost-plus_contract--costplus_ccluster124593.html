cost-plus contract

noun
contract paying costs plus profit margin
Pronunciation: / kɑst plʌs ˈkɑntrækt / (AmE), / kɒst plʌs ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
a contract in which a contractor is paid for all costs incurred plus an agreed percentage or fixed fee as profit

Examples

cost-plus contract for construction
Government projects often use cost-plus contracts when the total scope of work is uncertain.
The company agreed to build the bridge under a cost-plus contract to ensure their expenses would be covered.
Under a cost-plus contract, the contractor has less incentive to minimize actual expenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fixed-price contract: a contract with a set price regardless of actual costs
