Cyrillic alphabet

noun
alphabet used for Slavic languages
Pronunciation: / səˈrɪlɪk ˈælfəbɛt / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪlɪk ˈalfəbɛt / (BrE)
an alphabet derived from the Greek script, used for writing many Slavic languages such as Russian, Bulgarian, Serbian, and others

Examples

Cyrillic alphabet in Russian
He studied the Cyrillic alphabet to read old Bulgarian texts.
The Russian language is written using the Cyrillic alphabet.
The Serbian language uses both the Latin and Cyrillic alphabet systems.
