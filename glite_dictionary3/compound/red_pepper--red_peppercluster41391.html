red pepper

noun
hot red chili pepper used in food
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌpɛpə / (BrE)
a type of hot, usually long and thin red chili pepper, either fresh or dried, used to flavor food

Examples

fresh red pepper slices
Avoid touching your eyes after handling red pepper, as it can cause irritation.
She added some chopped red pepper to give the soup a spicy kick.
The market had stalls piled high with dried red peppers of every size.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chili pepper: a hot-tasting red or green fruit of a variety of capsicum, used as a pungent spice in cooking
