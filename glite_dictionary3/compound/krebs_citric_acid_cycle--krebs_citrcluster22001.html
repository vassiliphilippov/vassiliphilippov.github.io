Krebs citric acid cycle

noun
cycle of reactions in cells producing energy
Pronunciation: / krɛbz ˌsɪtrɪk ˈæsɪd ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / krɛbz ˌsɪtrɪk ˈæsɪd ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
a series of enzymatic reactions in the mitochondria of cells, by which acetyl compounds are oxidized to produce energy in the form of high-energy phosphate compounds

Examples

During aerobic respiration, the Krebs citric acid cycle plays a crucial role in generating cellular energy.
Students often study the Krebs citric acid cycle in their high school biology classes.
The Krebs citric acid cycle occurs within the mitochondria of plant and animal cells.
