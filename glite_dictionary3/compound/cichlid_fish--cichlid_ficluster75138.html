cichlid fish

noun
freshwater fish of the cichlid family
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪklɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A freshwater fish of the family Cichlidae, native to tropical regions of America, Africa, and Asia, often kept in aquariums and sometimes used as food.

Examples

cichlid fish in aquarium
Many aquarium enthusiasts enjoy keeping cichlid fish because of their bright colors and unique behavior.
Some types of cichlid fish are cultivated as a food source in certain parts of Africa.
The waters of Lake Malawi are home to hundreds of species of cichlid fish.
