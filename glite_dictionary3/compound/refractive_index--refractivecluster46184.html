refractive index

noun
ratio of light speeds in vacuum and a medium
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktɪv ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktɪv ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
A number representing the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to its speed in a given medium, indicating how much the medium slows down light.

Examples

refractive index of glass
Different types of glass have different refractive indices, affecting how lenses bend light.
Scientists measured the refractive index of the new material to determine its optical properties.
The refractive index of water is about 1.33, which means light travels slower in water than in a vacuum.

Related Terms

  • synonym – optical density: measure of a medium’s ability to slow light, closely related to refractive index
