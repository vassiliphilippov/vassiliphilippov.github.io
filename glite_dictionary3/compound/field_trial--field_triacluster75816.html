field trial

noun
test of something in real-world conditions
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ˌtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌtraɪəl / (BrE)
a test of a new product, method, or system under actual conditions of use to evaluate its performance

Examples

field trial of new product
Before releasing the software, the company organized a field trial to see how it performed with real users.
The new vaccine underwent a field trial to determine its effectiveness in different communities.
The results of the field trial showed significant improvement over the previous product.
