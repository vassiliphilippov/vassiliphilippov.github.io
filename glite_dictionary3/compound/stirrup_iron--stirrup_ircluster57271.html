stirrup iron

noun
metal loop for rider's foot on saddle
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪr.əp ˌaɪ.ərn / (AmE), / ˈstɪr.əp ˌaɪən / (BrE)
a metal loop attached to a horse's saddle into which a rider places a foot for support while riding

Examples

stirrup iron on saddle
A broken stirrup iron can be dangerous during a jump.
She polished each stirrup iron on her saddle until it gleamed.
The rider adjusted his stirrup iron before mounting the horse.
