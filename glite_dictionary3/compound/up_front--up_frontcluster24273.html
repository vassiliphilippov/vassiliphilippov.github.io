up front

adverb
in advance; honest and direct
Pronunciation: / ʌp frʌnt / (AmE), / ʌp frʌnt / (BrE)
(of payment) made in advance; (of a person or behavior) honest and direct in communication.

Examples

be up front with feelings
If you have any concerns, it's best to be up front with your manager.
She was always up front about her plans for the project.
We require a deposit to be paid up front before starting the work.

  • synonym – honest: truthful and direct
  • synonym – in advance: before something happens; ahead of time
  • antonym – secretive: not open or honest about information or feelings
