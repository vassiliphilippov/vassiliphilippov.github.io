eastern grey squirrel

noun
a North American tree squirrel, now in Britain
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstɚn ɡreɪ ˈskwɝːrl / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ɡreɪ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a common greyish tree squirrel native to eastern North America, now also found in Britain, with a bushy tail and large body

Examples

a eastern grey squirrel climbing trees
The eastern grey squirrel has become common in many parts of England.
Unlike the red squirrel, the eastern grey squirrel can adapt to urban environments very well.
