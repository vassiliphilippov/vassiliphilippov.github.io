Wars of the Roses

proper noun
English civil wars (1455–1485) over the throne
Pronunciation: / wɔrz əv ðə ˈroʊzɪz / (AmE), / wɔːz əv ðə ˈrəʊzɪz / (BrE)
A series of civil wars in England (1455–1485) between the York and Lancaster families for control of the throne, ending with the rise of the Tudor dynasty.

Examples

the Wars of the Roses in England
Many novels and films have depicted the intrigue of the Wars of the Roses.
The Wars of the Roses dramatically shaped the course of English history.
The Wars of the Roses ended with Henry VII becoming king.
