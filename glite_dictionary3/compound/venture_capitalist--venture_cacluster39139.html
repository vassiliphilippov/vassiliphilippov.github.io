venture capitalist

noun
investor in high-risk, innovative businesses
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛntʃər ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈvɛntʃə ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
a person or organization that invests money in new or emerging businesses, especially those involving high risk and innovation, in exchange for equity or potential profit

Examples

venture capitalist funds startups
Many technology companies get their initial funding from a venture capitalist or investment firm.
She met with a venture capitalist to discuss potential backing for her project.
The venture capitalist decided to fund the startup after seeing its innovative business model.

Related Terms

  • synonym – angel investor: an individual who provides financial backing for small startups or entrepreneurs
  • degree lower – entrepreneur: a person who starts and runs a business, taking on financial risks
  • synonym – financier: a person concerned with the management of large amounts of money on behalf of governments or other large organizations
