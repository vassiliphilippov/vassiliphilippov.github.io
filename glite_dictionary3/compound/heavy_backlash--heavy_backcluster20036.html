heavy backlash

noun
strong negative public reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi ˈbækˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈbæk.læʃ / (BrE)
A strong and widespread negative reaction or criticism from the public or a group, often following a decision or event.

Examples

face heavy backlash
After her remarks on social media, she faced a heavy backlash from fans and critics alike.
The company's decision to lay off workers led to a heavy backlash from the public.
The law was quickly withdrawn due to the heavy backlash it received nationwide.
