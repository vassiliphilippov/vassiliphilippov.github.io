market fundamentalism

noun
belief in free market supremacy
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkət ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief that free, unregulated markets are the most efficient and fair way to organize economic and social life.

Examples

belief in market fundamentalism
Many economists have criticized market fundamentalism for ignoring issues of inequality and market failure.
Some policy decisions in the 1980s were influenced by market fundamentalism.
The risks of market fundamentalism became apparent during the financial crisis.

Related Terms
