wilt disease

noun
plant disease causing wilting
Pronunciation: / wɪlt dɪˌziz / (AmE), / wɪlt dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
any plant disease in which the leaves and stems droop and shrivel, typically caused by parasites attacking the roots.

Examples

wilt disease in tomatoes
Prevention of wilt disease involves rotating crops and ensuring healthy soil.
The farmer lost half his crop to a sudden outbreak of wilt disease last summer.
Tomato plants infected with wilt disease often display yellowing leaves and drooping stems.
