chalice cloth

noun
linen cloth used with Eucharist chalice
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃælɪs klɔθ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪs klɒθ / (BrE)
A piece of linen used to cover or handle the chalice during the celebration of the Eucharist in Christian liturgy.

Examples

chalice cloth on altar
During the ceremony, the deacon arranged the chalice cloth on the altar.
Each chalice cloth was washed and folded with great reverence by the sacristan.
The priest carefully placed the chalice cloth over the cup after communion.
