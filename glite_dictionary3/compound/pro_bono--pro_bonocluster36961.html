pro bono

adjective
done without charge for the public good
Pronunciation: / proʊ ˈboʊnoʊ / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈbəʊnəʊ / (BrE)
(of work, especially by a professional) undertaken for the public good and without charge.

Examples

pro bono legal work
Many doctors volunteer their time pro bono at community health clinics.
She offers pro bono accounting services to local nonprofits.
The law firm took on the case pro bono to help those who could not afford legal representation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – paid: work done for payment or compensation
  • synonym – voluntary: done or given because you want to and without being forced or paid
