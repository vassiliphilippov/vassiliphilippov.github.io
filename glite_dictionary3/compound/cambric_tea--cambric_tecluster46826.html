cambric tea

noun
weak tea with milk and sugar for children
Pronunciation: / ˈkæm.brɪk ti / (AmE), / ˈkæm.brɪk tiː / (BrE)
A weakly brewed tea mixed with hot water, milk, and sugar, served especially to children.

Examples

a cup of cambric tea
As a child, my grandmother always made me cambric tea on chilly mornings.
He remembered the taste of cambric tea from his early school days.
The hostess offered cambric tea to the children instead of regular tea.
