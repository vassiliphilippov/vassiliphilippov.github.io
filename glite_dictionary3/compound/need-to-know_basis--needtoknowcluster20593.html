need-to-know basis

noun
principle where information is restricted to those who need it
Pronunciation: / nid tə noʊ ˈbeɪsɪs / (AmE), / niːd tə nəʊ ˈbeɪsɪs / (BrE)
a principle or policy in which information is shared only with those who require it for their work or responsibilities

Examples

on a need-to-know basis only
All the financial records are accessible only on a need-to-know basis to protect client privacy.
She was frustrated by being kept in the dark due to the need-to-know basis policy.
The security protocols were explained to staff on a need-to-know basis only.

Related Terms

  • synonym – confidential: meant to be kept secret, not to be shared with everyone
  • antonym – open access: information available to everyone without restrictions
