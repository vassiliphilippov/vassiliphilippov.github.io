limited review

noun
less detailed financial inspection
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmɪtɪd rɪˈvju / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪtɪd rɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
An examination of financial data that is less comprehensive than an audit, providing a moderate level of assurance about its reliability.

Examples

limited review of accounts
A limited review can uncover significant errors without the expense of a full audit.
After conducting a limited review, the accountants reported no major discrepancies.
The company requested a limited review of its quarterly accounts to reassure investors.
