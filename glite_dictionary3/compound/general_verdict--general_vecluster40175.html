general verdict

noun
jury verdict stating which party wins
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈvɝːdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
a verdict in a trial in which the jury states simply which party wins, without making any special findings of fact

Examples

general verdict in court
A general verdict gives the jury the power to decide the outcome without detailing specific findings.
In some complex cases, the judge may require a special verdict instead of a general verdict.
The jury returned a general verdict in favor of the plaintiff after a short deliberation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – special verdict: a verdict in which the jury makes specific findings of fact rather than deciding which party wins directly
