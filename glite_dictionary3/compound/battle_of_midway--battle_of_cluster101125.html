Battle of Midway

proper noun
decisive World War II naval battle near Midway Islands
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ˈmɪdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈmɪdweɪ / (BrE)
A decisive naval battle of World War II, fought in June 1942 near the Midway Islands, in which American forces defeated a Japanese fleet attempting to invade the islands.

Examples

the Battle of Midway changed history
After the Battle of Midway, Japan lost its naval superiority in the Pacific.
Many historians consider the Battle of Midway the turning point in the Pacific War.
The anniversary of the Battle of Midway is commemorated every June by the U.S. Navy.
