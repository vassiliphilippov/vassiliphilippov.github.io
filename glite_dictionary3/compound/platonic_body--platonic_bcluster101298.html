Platonic body

noun
a regular convex polyhedron in geometry
Pronunciation: / pləˈtɑː.nɪk ˈbɑː.di / (AmE), / pləˈtɒn.ɪk ˈbɒd.i / (BrE)
any of five regular convex polyhedra in which the faces are congruent regular polygons and the angles at each vertex are equal; also known as a Platonic solid.

Examples

a Platonic body model
Sixty-four small marbles were placed inside a transparent Platonic body to demonstrate its symmetry to the class.
The ancient Greeks believed that each Platonic body was associated with one of the classical elements.
The cube is a classic example of a Platonic body because all its faces are squares and its angles are equal.
