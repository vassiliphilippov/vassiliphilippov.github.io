close supporting fire

noun
gunfire near friendly units for immediate protection
Pronunciation: / kloʊs səˈpɔrtɪŋ faɪɚ / (AmE), / kləʊs səˈpɔːtɪŋ faɪə / (BrE)
gunfire directed at enemy forces or positions near friendly units, intended to protect them from the most immediate threats.

Examples

close supporting fire protects troops
Air units provided close supporting fire to suppress hostile positions threatening the infantry.
