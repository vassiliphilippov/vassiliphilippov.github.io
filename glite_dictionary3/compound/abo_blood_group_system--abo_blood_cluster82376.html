ABO blood group system

noun
system classifying human blood groups
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.biːˈoʊ blʌd ɡruːp ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.biːˈəʊ blʌd ɡruːp ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system for classifying human blood based on the presence or absence of A and B antigens on red blood cells, resulting in four main blood groups: A, B, AB, and O
