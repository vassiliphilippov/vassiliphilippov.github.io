medial geniculate

noun
thalamic relay for auditory information
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiəl dʒəˈnɪkjələt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəl dʒɪˈnɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
a relay center in the thalamus of the brain that processes auditory signals before they reach the auditory cortex

Examples

the medial geniculate nucleus
Auditory information passes through the medial geniculate before reaching the auditory cortex.
Damage to the medial geniculate can result in significant hearing deficits.
Research shows that the medial geniculate is critical for processing sound frequencies in the brain.
