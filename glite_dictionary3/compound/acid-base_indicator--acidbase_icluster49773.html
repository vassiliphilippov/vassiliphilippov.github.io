acid-base indicator

noun
chemical that shows acid or base by color
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd beɪs ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd beɪs ˌɪndɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a chemical substance that changes color depending on whether a solution is acidic or basic, used to determine the pH of a solution

Examples

acid-base indicator changes color
An acid-base indicator is essential for performing accurate titrations in experiments.
Litmus is a common acid-base indicator used in chemistry laboratories.
You should add a few drops of acid-base indicator to see if the solution is acidic or basic.
