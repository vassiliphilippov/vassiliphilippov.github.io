herpes genitalis

noun
genital herpes infection by HSV-2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrpiːz ˌdʒɛnɪˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz ˌdʒɛnɪˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A sexually transmitted infection caused by herpes simplex virus type 2 (HSV-2), characterized by recurrent, painful sores on the skin and mucous membranes of the genital area.

Examples

herpes genitalis infection
Doctors recommend practicing safe sex to reduce the risk of transmitting herpes genitalis.
Many people with herpes genitalis experience only mild symptoms or none at all.
Outbreaks of herpes genitalis can be triggered by stress or a weakened immune system.
