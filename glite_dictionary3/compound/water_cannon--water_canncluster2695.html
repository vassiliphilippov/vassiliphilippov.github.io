water cannon

noun
device for crowd dispersal with high-pressure water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ˌkænən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌkænən / (BrE)
A machine that shoots water at high pressure, typically used by police to disperse crowds or riots.

Examples

police use a water cannon
A powerful water cannon can knock people off their feet.
Authorities used a water cannon when the crowd refused to disperse.
The police deployed a water cannon to break up the protest.
