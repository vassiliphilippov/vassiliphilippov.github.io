T lymphocyte

noun
immune system white blood cell
Pronunciation: / ti ˈlɪm.foʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ti ˈlɪm.fəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell developed in the thymus that plays a central role in the immune system's response to infected or malignant cells

Examples

T lymphocyte count
T lymphocyte counts are often measured to assess immune function in HIV patients.
Each T lymphocyte has a unique receptor that helps identify a specific pathogen.
The vaccine is designed to stimulate the production of T lymphocytes that can attack infected cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym – B lymphocyte: a type of lymphocyte producing antibodies
