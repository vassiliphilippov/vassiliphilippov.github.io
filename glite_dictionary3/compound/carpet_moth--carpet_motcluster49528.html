carpet moth

noun
moth whose larvae feed on carpets
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrpət mɑːθ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪt mɒθ / (BrE)
a small moth whose larvae feed on carpets and other woolen materials, often causing damage to textiles

Examples

carpet moth larvae damage
Homeowners are often advised to remove carpet moth larvae with regular vacuuming.
The presence of carpet moths can lead to unsightly holes in woolen carpets and upholstery.
We discovered carpet moths had been eating our living room rug over the winter.
