coronary bypass

noun
bypass surgery for blocked coronary arteries
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrənˌɛri ˈbaɪˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri ˈbaɪpɑːs / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a section of blood vessel is grafted to bypass a blocked coronary artery in order to improve blood supply to the heart

Examples

coronary bypass surgery
Advances in coronary bypass procedures have greatly improved patient outcomes over the past decade.
After his heart attack, the doctor recommended a coronary bypass to restore proper blood flow.
She recovered well following her coronary bypass surgery last year.
