flea beetle

noun
small jumping leaf beetle
Pronunciation: / ˈfli ˌbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˈfliː ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
Any of various small leaf beetles with enlarged hind legs, able to jump like fleas.

Examples

flea beetle jumps
Brightly colored flea beetles are often seen jumping away when disturbed.
Gardeners use row covers to protect their plants from flea beetle infestations.
The flea beetle can cause significant damage to vegetable crops by chewing small holes in leaves.
