blood extravasation

noun
leakage of blood into tissues
Pronunciation: / blʌd ɪkˌstrævəˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / blʌd ɪkˌstrævəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the leakage of blood from a blood vessel into the surrounding tissues, often occurring due to injury, burns, or allergic reactions

Examples

blood extravasation after injury
Blood extravasation can cause severe bruising and discomfort in the affected area.
Allergic reactions sometimes result in blood extravasation, leading to visible red patches on the skin.
The doctor diagnosed the swelling as blood extravasation following the patient’s fall.
