can buoy

noun
navigational buoy with conical top
Pronunciation: / kæn ˈbuː.i / (AmE), / kan ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
a floating navigational marker with a cylindrical or slightly conical body and a flat or conical top, used to mark channels or hazards at sea

Examples

can buoy marks channel
Green can buoys are used to indicate the left side of a channel when entering from the sea.
The chart clearly showed the position of every can buoy along the harbor approach.
The sailor spotted a can buoy marking the edge of the shipping channel.
