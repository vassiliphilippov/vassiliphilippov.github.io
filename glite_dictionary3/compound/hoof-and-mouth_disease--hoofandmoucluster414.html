hoof-and-mouth disease

noun
contagious animal disease causing mouth and hoof ulcers
Pronunciation: / hʊf ənd maʊθ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / hʊf ənd maʊθ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A highly contagious viral disease affecting cloven-footed animals, characterized by fever and ulcers in the mouth and around the hooves.

Examples

hoof-and-mouth disease outbreak
Farmers were required to quarantine their animals due to a case of hoof-and-mouth disease nearby.
The outbreak of hoof-and-mouth disease devastated livestock farms across the region.
Veterinarians implemented strict controls to prevent the spread of hoof-and-mouth disease among cattle.
