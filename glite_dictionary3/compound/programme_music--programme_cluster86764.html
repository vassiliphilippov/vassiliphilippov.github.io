programme music

noun
music that tells a story or paints a scene
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊɡræm ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡræm ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music composed to evoke images or suggest events, often telling a story or painting a scene through sound.

Examples

programme music tells a story
Debussy's 'La Mer' is a classic example of programme music, evoking the movement and moods of the sea.
Many Romantic composers were fascinated by programme music as a way to tell stories through sound.
The orchestra performed a piece of programme music that vividly described a thunderstorm.
