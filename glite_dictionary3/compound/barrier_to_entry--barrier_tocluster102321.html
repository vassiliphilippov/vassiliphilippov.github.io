barrier to entry

noun
obstacle preventing entering a market
Pronunciation: / ˈbæriər tə ˈɛntri / (AmE), / ˈbæriə tə ˈɛntri / (BrE)
a circumstance or obstacle that makes it difficult for newcomers to enter a particular market or industry

Examples

barrier to entry for new companies
Expensive technology requirements often create a barrier to entry in advanced industries.
Strict government regulations can serve as a barrier to entry for foreign companies.
The lack of experience in the field may act as a barrier to entry for aspiring entrepreneurs.
