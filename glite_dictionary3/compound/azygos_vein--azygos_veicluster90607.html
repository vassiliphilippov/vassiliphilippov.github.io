azygos vein

noun
major chest vein draining into superior vena cava
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪzɪˌɡɑs veɪn / (AmE), / ˈazɪɡɒs veɪn / (BrE)
a vein running up the right side of the thoracic vertebral column that drains blood from the chest and abdominal walls into the superior vena cava

Examples

azygos vein on X-ray
An enlarged azygos vein can be detected on a chest X-ray and may indicate circulatory problems.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the azygos vein.
The azygos vein provides an important route for blood to return to the heart if the inferior vena cava is blocked.
