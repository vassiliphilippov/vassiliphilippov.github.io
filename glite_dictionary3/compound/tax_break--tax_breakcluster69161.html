tax break

noun
deduction from taxes to encourage activities
Pronunciation: / tæks breɪk / (AmE), / tæks breɪk / (BrE)
an amount of money that you are allowed to deduct from your taxes in order to encourage certain activities or investments

Examples

tax break for homeowners
Many homeowners benefit from a tax break when they install solar panels on their roofs.
Small businesses are seeking a tax break to help them recover from last year's economic downturn.
The government offered a significant tax break to companies that invest in renewable energy.
