bone ash

noun
white ash from burned bones, used in industry
Pronunciation: / boʊn æʃ / (AmE), / bəʊn æʃ / (BrE)
the white ash remaining after bones are burned, rich in calcium phosphate, used in fertilizers and the manufacture of bone china

Examples

bone ash in fertilizer
Gardeners often add bone ash to the soil as a source of calcium.
The factory imported bone ash for the production of fine porcelain.
Traditional bone ash gives bone china its translucency and strength.
