law of conservation of energy

noun
principle that total energy remains constant
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˌkɑnsɚˈveɪʃən əv ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən əv ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the principle in physics stating that the total energy of an isolated system remains constant, regardless of internal changes within the system

Examples

the law of conservation of energy in physics
According to the law of conservation of energy, energy cannot be created or destroyed but only transformed.
Scientists rely on the law of conservation of energy in calculations involving chemical reactions and physical processes.
The law of conservation of energy explains why a pendulum eventually comes to rest only if energy is lost to the environment.
