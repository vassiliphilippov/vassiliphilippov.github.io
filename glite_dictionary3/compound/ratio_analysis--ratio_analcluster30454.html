ratio analysis

noun
evaluation of financial relationships using ratios
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪʃioʊ əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃiəʊ əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the examination of the relationships between different financial figures in order to assess an organization's performance, often by comparing them with past results or with other firms.

Examples

ratio analysis of company accounts
Bankers often request a ratio analysis before approving business loans.
Comparing last year's data through ratio analysis helped identify areas for operational improvement.
The company's financial strength was assessed using a thorough ratio analysis of its balance sheet and income statement.

Related Terms

  • synonym – financial analysis: the process of evaluating businesses, projects, budgets, and other finance-related entities
