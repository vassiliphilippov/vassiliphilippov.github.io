curb roof

noun
roof with multiple slopes on each side
Pronunciation: / kɝb ruːf / (AmE), / kɜːb ruːf / (BrE)
A roof with two or more slopes on each side of the ridge, typically featuring a steeper lower slope and a shallower upper slope.

Examples

curb roof design
Many colonial homes feature a curb roof for added attic space and aesthetic appeal.
The architect recommended a curb roof to improve both drainage and natural light in the building.
The old barn was restored with a traditional curb roof to preserve its historic charm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mansard roof: a type of curb roof with two slopes on each side, the lower steeper than the upper
