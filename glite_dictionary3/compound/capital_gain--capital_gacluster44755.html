capital gain

noun
profit from selling an asset above purchase price
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəl ɡeɪn / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl ɡeɪn / (BrE)
an increase in the value of an asset or investment, realized when it is sold for more than its purchase price

Examples

report a capital gain
If you sell property at a higher price than you paid, you must report the capital gain for tax purposes.
Selling her shares after five years resulted in a significant capital gain.
The capital gain from his investment in stocks helped him pay off his debts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – capital loss: a decrease in value of an asset when it is sold for less than its purchase price
