trial period

noun
period for testing someone or something
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪəl ˌpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl ˌpɪəriəd / (BrE)
a fixed length of time during which someone or something is tested to determine suitability or quality

Examples

trial period for new employees
During the trial period, the employee’s performance will be closely monitored.
She was delighted when she passed her trial period and got the permanent job.
The software is free to use for a 30-day trial period.

Related Terms

  • synonym – probation: a period of testing or trial, especially for a new employee or student
  • synonym – probationary period: a period during which someone's suitability for a job or task is tested
