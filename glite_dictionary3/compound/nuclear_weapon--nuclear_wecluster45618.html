nuclear weapon

noun
weapon using nuclear reactions for explosive power
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈwɛp.ən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈwɛp.ən / (BrE)
a weapon whose explosive power comes from nuclear reactions, especially fission or fusion

Examples

a nuclear weapon explosion
A single nuclear weapon can cause massive destruction in seconds.
Many countries have agreed to treaties limiting the spread of nuclear weapons.
The threat of nuclear weapon use has been a major concern since the end of World War II.
