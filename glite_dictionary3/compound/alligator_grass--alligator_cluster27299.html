alligator grass

noun
aquatic weed with grass-like leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtər ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A fast-growing aquatic plant of South American origin, with grass-like leaves and short spikes of white flowers, known for clogging waterways with dense floating mats.

Examples

alligator grass blocks rivers
Dense mats of alligator grass can block sunlight, harming aquatic life beneath.
Farmers are concerned about alligator grass spreading rapidly in irrigation canals.
The removal of alligator grass is essential to keep waterway traffic moving smoothly.
