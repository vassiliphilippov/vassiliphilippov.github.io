straw poll

noun
unofficial vote to gauge opinion
Pronunciation: / strɔ pɔl / (AmE), / strɔː pəʊl / (BrE)
an unofficial vote taken to measure the general opinion of people on a particular issue

Examples

straw poll among friends
A quick straw poll at the office showed that most employees preferred remote work.
Before the election, organizers conducted a straw poll among the attendees.
The committee held a straw poll to see which proposal had the most support.
