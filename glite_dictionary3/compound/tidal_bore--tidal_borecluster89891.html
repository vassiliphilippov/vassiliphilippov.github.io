tidal bore

noun
wave caused by incoming tide in river
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.dəl bɔr / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dəl bɔː / (BrE)
A high, often dangerous wave caused by the incoming tide moving against the direction of a river current, usually in a narrow estuary.

Examples

tidal bore rushes upriver
A tidal bore swept up the river, surprising the group of kayakers.
The local authorities issued a warning about the dangerous tidal bore expected at dawn.
Twice a year, thousands gather to watch the tidal bore along the Qiantang River.
