bass drum

noun
large, low-pitched drum
Pronunciation: / beɪs drʌm / (AmE), / beɪs drʌm / (BrE)
a large drum with two heads, producing a deep, low-pitched sound, commonly used in orchestras and bands

Examples

marching bass drum
A marching band usually includes at least one bass drum to keep the tempo.
He bought a new bass drum for his drum kit to enhance the band's sound.
The percussionist struck the bass drum to create a thunderous effect during the concert.
