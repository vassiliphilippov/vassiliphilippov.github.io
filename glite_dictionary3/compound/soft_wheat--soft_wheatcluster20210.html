soft wheat

noun
wheat with soft kernels for pastry or cereals
Pronunciation: / sɔft wit / (AmE), / sɒft wiːt / (BrE)
a variety of wheat with soft starch kernels, mainly used in pastry and breakfast cereals

Examples

soft wheat flour
Breakfast cereals often contain soft wheat because of its light texture and mild flavor.
Flour made from soft wheat is ideal for cookies and other delicate baked goods.
Many bakers prefer soft wheat for making tender pastries and cakes.
