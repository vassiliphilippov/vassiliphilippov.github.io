matron of honor

noun
chief female attendant at a wedding (married)
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪtrən əv ˈɑnɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtrən əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
A married woman who serves as the chief attendant to the bride at a wedding.

Examples

matron of honor at a wedding
Her sister was honored to be the matron of honor at the ceremony.
The matron of honor gave a heartfelt toast to the happy couple.
They chose a matron of honor and two bridesmaids for the bridal party.
