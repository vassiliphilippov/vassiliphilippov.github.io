marsh wren

noun
small marsh-dwelling songbird
Pronunciation: / mɑrʃ rɛn / (AmE), / mɑːʃ rɛn / (BrE)
a small North American songbird of the genus Cistothorus that inhabits marshes, known for its distinctive bubbling song

Examples

singing marsh wren
A marsh wren built its nest among the reeds at the edge of the pond.
Naturalists observed several marsh wrens darting between the cattails during their birdwatching trip.
The loud, bubbling song of the marsh wren can be heard throughout the wetlands in spring.
