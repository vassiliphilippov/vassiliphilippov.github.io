goosefoot maple

noun
North American maple with striped bark and two-lobed leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈɡuːsfʊt ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsfʊt ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A maple tree native to eastern North America, distinguished by its striped bark and large, two-lobed leaves that turn clear yellow in autumn.
