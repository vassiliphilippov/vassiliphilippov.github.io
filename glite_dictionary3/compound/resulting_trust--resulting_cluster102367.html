resulting trust

noun
court-imposed trust based on intention
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzʌltɪŋ trʌst / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌltɪŋ trʌst / (BrE)
a trust imposed by a court when it determines that the parties intended to create a trust, but did not formally do so

Examples

resulting trust in property law
Legal disputes often arise over whether a resulting trust should be recognized in property transactions.
The court declared a resulting trust in favor of the original owner after reviewing the evidence.
When the property was transferred without payment, a resulting trust arose by operation of law.

Related Terms

  • synonym – constructive trust: a trust imposed by law to prevent unjust enrichment
