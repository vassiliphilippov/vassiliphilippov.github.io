data encryption

noun
encoding data to protect it
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪtə ɪnˌkrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtə ɪnˌkrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting data into a coded form to prevent unauthorized access

Examples

data encryption protects information
Banks use data encryption to secure customers' personal information.
The company implemented strong data encryption policies to comply with regulations.
Without data encryption, sensitive files are vulnerable to cyberattacks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – decryption: the process of converting encoded data back into its original form
  • synonym – encryption: the process of converting information into code
