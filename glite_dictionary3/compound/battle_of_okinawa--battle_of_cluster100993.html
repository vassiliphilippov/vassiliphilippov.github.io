Battle of Okinawa

proper noun
World War II battle on Okinawa in 1945
Pronunciation: / ˈbæɾl əv ˌoʊkɪˈnɑwə / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌəʊkɪˈnɑːwə / (BrE)
a major battle fought on the island of Okinawa from April to June 1945, during World War II, in which United States forces captured the island from Japan in a decisive victory

Examples

the Battle of Okinawa in 1945
Historians often cite the Battle of Okinawa as a turning point that led to the end of the war.
The Battle of Okinawa was one of the bloodiest conflicts in the Pacific theater of World War II.
Thousands of soldiers and civilians lost their lives during the fierce Battle of Okinawa.
