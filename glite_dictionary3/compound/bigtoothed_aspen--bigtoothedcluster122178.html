bigtoothed aspen

noun
species of aspen tree with large-toothed leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪɡˌtuθt ˈæspən / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡˌtuːθt ˈaspɪn / (BrE)
A species of aspen (Populus grandidentata) native to eastern North America, characterized by its narrow crown and large, coarse-toothed leaves.

Examples

In eastern forests, you can find the bigtoothed aspen growing alongside pines and maples.
The bigtoothed aspen is known for its distinctive leaves with large, irregular teeth along the edges.
The wood from the bigtoothed aspen is valued for making pulp and paper products.
