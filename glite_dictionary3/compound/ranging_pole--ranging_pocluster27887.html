ranging pole

noun
surveying rod with colored bands
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪndʒɪŋ poʊl / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒɪŋ pəʊl / (BrE)
a long, straight rod marked with alternating red and white bands, used by surveyors to mark positions for measuring and sighting

Examples

ranging pole for surveying
Red and white bands on the ranging pole made it easy to spot from a distance.
The survey team placed a ranging pole at each end of the field to mark the boundary.
Using a ranging pole, the engineer aligned the instrument with the correct position.
