gross profit margin

noun
net sales minus cost of goods sold (finance)
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊs ˈprɑːfɪt ˈmɑːrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs ˈprɒfɪt ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
the difference between net sales and the cost of goods and services sold, often expressed as a percentage of net sales

Examples

gross profit margin percentage
An increase in raw material prices negatively impacted their gross profit margin.
Retailers with a high gross profit margin can better withstand changes in demand.
The company improved its gross profit margin by reducing manufacturing costs.
