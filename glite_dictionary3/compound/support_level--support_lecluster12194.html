support level

noun
lowest price attracting investors (finance)
Pronunciation: / səˈpɔrt ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / səˈpɔːt ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
in finance, the price point at which a falling stock or market is expected to stop declining because there is enough demand to prevent further drops

Examples

support level for a stock
Once the support level was breached, the price quickly dropped further.
The stock rebounded after hitting its support level at $50 per share.
Traders watch for a support level to identify possible buying opportunities.
Analysts often look for the support level to predict possible price movements.
Breaking below a key support level can signal further declines in the market.
The company's shares rebounded after hitting a support level at $50.
