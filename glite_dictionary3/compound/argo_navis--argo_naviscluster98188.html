Argo Navis

noun
historical constellation now divided into four
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrɡoʊ ˈneɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡəʊ ˈneɪvɪs / (BrE)
a former large constellation in the southern sky, now divided into Carina, Puppis, Vela, and Pyxis

Examples

Argo Navis constellation
Early star charts depicted Argo Navis before it was officially divided into smaller constellations.
Modern astronomy no longer uses Argo Navis, but refers to its parts as separate constellations.
The ancient Greeks recognized Argo Navis as a single impressive constellation representing Jason's ship.
