ghost word

noun
word created accidentally through error
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊst wɝd / (AmE), / ɡəʊst wɜːd / (BrE)
A word that has entered a language as the result of a mistake, such as a misreading or misprinting, and has no actual origin in usage.

Examples

a ghost word in a dictionary
'Phantomnation' was once thought to be a valid term, but it turned out to be a ghost word.
Linguists enjoy tracing the origins of every ghost word in historical dictionaries.
The dictionary entry for 'dord' was actually a ghost word resulting from a typographical error.
