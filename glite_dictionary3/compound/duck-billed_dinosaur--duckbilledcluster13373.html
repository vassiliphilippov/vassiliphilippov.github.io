duck-billed dinosaur

noun
plant-eating dinosaur with a duck-like bill
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌk ˌbɪld ˈdaɪ.nəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈdʌk ˌbɪld ˈdaɪ.nə.sɔː / (BrE)
any of a group of large plant-eating dinosaurs of the Cretaceous period, having a long, flat, duck-like bill; especially referring to hadrosaurids.

Examples

The fossilized skull of a duck-billed dinosaur was discovered in the Canadian badlands.
