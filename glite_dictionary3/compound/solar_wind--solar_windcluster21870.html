solar wind

noun
stream of charged particles from the sun
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlər wɪnd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə wɪnd / (BrE)
a continuous stream of charged particles, mainly protons and electrons, released from the upper atmosphere of the sun and flowing through the solar system

Examples

solar wind from the sun
Satellites must be designed to withstand the effects of the solar wind.
Scientists study the solar wind to better understand its impact on Earth's magnetic field.
The solar wind can cause beautiful auroras near the planet's poles.
