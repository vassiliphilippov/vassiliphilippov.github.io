trench fever

noun
infectious disease marked by pain and fever
Pronunciation: / trɛntʃ ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / trɛntʃ ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An infectious disease marked by pain in the muscles and joints, headache, and fever, transmitted by lice, especially common among soldiers in World War I.

Examples

trench fever outbreak
He was hospitalized after experiencing symptoms associated with trench fever.
Many soldiers in World War I suffered from trench fever due to unhygienic living conditions.
The outbreak of trench fever was traced to lice infestation in the camps.
