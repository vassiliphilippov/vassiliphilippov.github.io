economic mobilisation

noun
organizing economy for national goals
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkɑnəmɪk ˌmoʊbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / iˌkɒnəmɪk ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
The process of organizing and directing a country's economy to support national objectives, especially during war or crisis.

Examples

economic mobilisation for war
During World War II, the economic mobilisation of many countries led to rapid industrial growth.
Effective economic mobilisation requires cooperation between government, industries, and the workforce.
Governments may use economic mobilisation strategies in response to natural disasters or military threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mobilization: the act of assembling and preparing resources for use
