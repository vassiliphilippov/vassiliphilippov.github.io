fecal impaction

noun
hardened stool trapped in rectum (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːkəl ɪmˈpækʃən / (AmE), / ˈfiːkəl ɪmˈpækʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which hardened stool accumulates in the rectum or lower colon and cannot be passed naturally.

Examples

fecal impaction in elderly patients
Chronic constipation can sometimes lead to fecal impaction, especially in elderly patients.
Doctors often use manual removal techniques to treat fecal impaction when laxatives are ineffective.
The patient was diagnosed with fecal impaction after complaining of severe abdominal pain.
