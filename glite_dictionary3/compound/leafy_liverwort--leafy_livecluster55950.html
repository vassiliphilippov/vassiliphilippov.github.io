leafy liverwort

noun
moss-like liverwort with tiny leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈliːfi ˈlɪvərˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈliːfi ˈlɪvəˌwɔːt / (BrE)
A type of liverwort resembling moss, having tiny, scalelike leaves and typically growing on the surfaces of trees or rocks.

Examples

leafy liverwort on tree bark
Researchers discovered a new species of leafy liverwort high in the mountain cloud forests.
The humid rainforest supports a wide variety of leafy liverworts on its tree trunks and branches.
You can distinguish leafy liverworts from mosses by their flattened, scalelike leaves.
