leap year

noun
a year with an extra day in February
Pronunciation: / lip jɪr / (AmE), / liːp jɪə / (BrE)
A year, occurring every four years, that has 366 days instead of 365, with the extra day added to February.

Examples

leap year February 29
February has 29 days during a leap year instead of the usual 28.
People born on February 29 celebrate their birthday only in a leap year.
The next leap year will be in 2028, adding an extra day to the calendar.
