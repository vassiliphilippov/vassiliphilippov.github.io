round robin

noun
letter signed by multiple people
Pronunciation: / ˌraʊnd ˈrɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˌraʊnd ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A letter or petition that is signed by several people, often to express a collective opinion without singling out any one signatory.

Examples

round robin letter
A round robin was circulated among the students to protest the new policy.
The employees wrote a round robin to the manager requesting better working conditions.
They presented a round robin to the board, making it clear the concerns were shared by many.
