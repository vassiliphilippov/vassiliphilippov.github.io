colic vein

noun
vein draining the large intestine
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑlɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪk veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the large intestine (colon) and empty into the superior mesenteric vein or other abdominal veins.

Examples

colic vein drains colon
Surgeons must carefully identify the colic vein during procedures involving the large intestine.
The colic vein plays a crucial role in transporting blood away from the colon to the portal vein.
The middle colic vein is one of several veins that drain sections of the colon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mesenteric vein: one of the veins draining blood from the intestine
