cow cockle

noun
a European annual flowering plant, weed or ornamental
Pronunciation: / kaʊ ˈkɑkəl / (AmE), / kaʊ ˈkɒkəl / (BrE)
A European annual plant with pale rose-colored flowers, often found as a weed in grain fields or cultivated as an ornamental; sometimes considered a type of soapwort.

Examples

cow cockle flowers bloom
European settlers inadvertently spread cow cockle to North America, where it has naturalized in many regions.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate cow cockle for its attractive blossoms, despite its reputation as a weed.
The cow cockle brightened the edges of the wheat fields with its delicate pink flowers.
