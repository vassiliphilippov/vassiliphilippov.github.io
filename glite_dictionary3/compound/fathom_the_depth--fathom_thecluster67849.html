to fathom the depth

verb
to fully understand the extent of something
Pronunciation: / ˈfæθəm ðə dɛpθ / (AmE), / ˈfaðəm ðə dɛpθ / (BrE)
to understand or grasp the full extent or seriousness of something, often something abstract or profound

Examples

to fathom the depth of emotion
It’s difficult to fathom the depth of his disappointment after losing the championship.
Only after hearing her story did I truly fathom the depth of her courage.
Scientists are still trying to fathom the depth of the ocean’s mysteries.
