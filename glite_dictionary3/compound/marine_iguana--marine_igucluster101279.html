marine iguana

noun
Galapagos shore-dwelling seaweed-eating lizard
Pronunciation: / məˈrin ɪˈɡwɑnə / (AmE), / məˈriːn ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (BrE)
a large, dark-colored lizard native to the Galapagos Islands, known for living on the shore and feeding mainly on seaweed

Examples

marine iguana eats seaweed
Scientists traveled to the Galapagos Islands to study the marine iguana's algae-eating habits.
The marine iguana is unique for its ability to swim and feed in the ocean.
Unlike other lizards, the marine iguana can dive to great depths to search for food.
