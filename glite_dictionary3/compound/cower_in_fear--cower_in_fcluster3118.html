to cower in fear

verb
shrink or crouch due to fear
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊ.ər ɪn fɪr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ə ɪn fɪə / (BrE)
to shrink back or crouch down because of extreme fear or submission

Examples

cower in fear during danger
Prisoners sometimes cower in fear during harsh interrogations.
The child would cower in fear every time the thunder rumbled outside.
Wild animals may cower in fear when confronted by a predator.
