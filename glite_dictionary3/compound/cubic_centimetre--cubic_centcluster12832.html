cubic centimetre

noun
a metric unit of volume (1/1000 liter)
Pronunciation: / ˌkjuː.bɪk ˈsɛn.tɪˌmiː.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌkjuː.bɪk ˈsɛn.tɪ.miː.tə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter; commonly used to measure small quantities of liquid or gas.

Examples

one cubic centimetre of water
One cubic centimetre of water equals one millilitre.
The doctor measured the injection as exactly 5 cubic centimetres of liquid.
The engine's capacity is rated at 1600 cubic centimetres.
