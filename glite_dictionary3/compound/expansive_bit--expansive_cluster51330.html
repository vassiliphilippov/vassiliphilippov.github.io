expansive bit

noun
adjustable drill bit
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspænsɪv bɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɑːnsɪv bɪt / (BrE)
a drill bit with an adjustable cutting blade that allows holes of various diameters to be bored

Examples

expansive bit drills holes
An expansive bit is useful when you need multiple hole diameters but want to carry fewer tools.
Before power drills, an expansive bit was commonly used for adjustable wood boring.
The carpenter used an expansive bit to drill holes of different sizes in the wooden beam.
