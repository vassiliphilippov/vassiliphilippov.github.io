silver willow

noun
a willow tree with greyish, downy leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvər ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A North American willow tree with greyish, silky, downy leaves that usually turn black when dried.

Examples

old silver willow tree
A flock of birds landed on the branches of the old silver willow by the river.
The leaves of the silver willow shimmered in the afternoon sun.
When pressed for herbaria, the silver willow often develops darkened, distinctive leaves.
