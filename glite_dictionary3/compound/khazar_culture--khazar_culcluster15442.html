Khazar culture

noun
the culture of the Khazar people
Pronunciation: / kəˈzɑr ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / kəˈzɑː ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the distinctive social practices, beliefs, and traditions of the Khazar people, a semi-nomadic Turkic group who established a major empire in Eastern Europe between the 7th and 10th centuries
