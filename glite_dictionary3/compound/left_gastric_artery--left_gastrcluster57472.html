left gastric artery

noun
artery supplying part of the stomach and esophagus
Pronunciation: / lɛft ˈɡæstrɪk ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / lɛft ˈɡæstrɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a branch of the celiac artery that supplies blood to the lesser curvature of the stomach and the abdominal part of the esophagus

Examples

left gastric artery supplies stomach
A blockage in the left gastric artery may lead to compromised blood supply to the stomach wall.
Surgeons carefully ligate the left gastric artery to control bleeding during certain procedures.
The left gastric artery is often exposed during surgery on the upper stomach.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – celiac artery: the major artery from which the left gastric artery branches
  • antonym – right gastric artery: an artery that supplies the lesser curvature of the stomach, opposite the left gastric artery
