warehouseman's lien

noun
legal right to retain stored goods for unpaid charges
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛrˌhaʊsmənz ˌliːən / (AmE), / ˈwɛəˌhaʊsmənz ˌliːən / (BrE)
the legal right of a warehouseman to keep possession of stored goods until all storage charges due are paid by the owner

Examples

warehouseman's lien for unpaid storage
Because of the unpaid charges, the warehouseman's lien prevented the delivery of the goods to the customer.
He could not retrieve his equipment until the warehouseman's lien was satisfied.
The company exercised its warehouseman's lien to secure payment for the overdue storage fees.
