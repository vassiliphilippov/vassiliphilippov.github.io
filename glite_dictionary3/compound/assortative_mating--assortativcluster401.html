assortative mating

noun
mates choosing similar partners (biology/social science)
Pronunciation: / əˈsɔrt̬.ə.tɪv ˈmeɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈsɔː.tə.tɪv ˈmeɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the mating of individuals who are more similar to each other in certain traits than would be expected by chance

Examples

assortative mating by traits
In humans, assortative mating often occurs with respect to education and intelligence.
Many bird species practice assortative mating based on plumage coloration or song.
Research suggests that assortative mating can increase genetic similarity within pairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – random mating: mating without regard to similarity of traits
