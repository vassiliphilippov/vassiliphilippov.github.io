letters of administration

noun
court order appointing estate administrator
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛt̬ɚz əv ədˌmɪnəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz əv ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a legal document issued by a court appointing someone to administer the estate of a deceased person when there is no executor named in the will

Examples

letters of administration granted by court
Letters of administration are necessary for handling the assets of a person who dies intestate.
She obtained letters of administration to manage her uncle's estate after he died without a will.
Without a named executor, the court issued letters of administration to his spouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym – executor: a person appointed to carry out the terms of a will
  • synonym – probate: the legal process of proving the validity of a will
  • synonym – will: a legal document that sets forth how a person wishes their property to be distributed after death
