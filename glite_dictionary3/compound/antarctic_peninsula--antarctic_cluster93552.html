Antarctic Peninsula

proper noun
peninsula in Antarctica extending toward South America
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɑːrktɪk pəˈnɪnsələ / (AmE), / ænˌtɑːktɪk pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
a large peninsula in Antarctica extending about 1,200 miles north toward South America, separating the Weddell Sea from the South Pacific Ocean.

Examples

the Antarctic Peninsula glaciers
Glaciers on the Antarctic Peninsula have been retreating at an alarming rate.
Researchers set up a new base on the Antarctic Peninsula to study climate change.
The Antarctic Peninsula is one of the fastest-warming regions on Earth.
