horn fly

noun
bloodsucking cattle pest fly
Pronunciation: / hɔrn flaɪ / (AmE), / hɔːn flaɪ / (BrE)
a small black bloodsucking fly (Haematobia irritans), originally from Europe and now established in North America, that commonly infests cattle, especially near the base of their horns

Examples

horn fly on cattle
During the summer, large numbers of horn flies can be seen clustered around the heads of cows.
The veterinarian explained that the horn fly population was responsible for the recent drop in milk production.
To protect their cattle from horn flies, farmers often use insecticidal ear tags.
