intercellular substance

noun
material between tissue cells
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛljələr ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəsɛlˈjuːlə ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
the material found between cells in a tissue, in which the cells are embedded and supported

Examples

intercellular substance in tissue
Bone tissue is characterized by a hard, mineralized intercellular substance.
Changes in the intercellular substance can affect the function of connective tissues.
The cartilage contains an abundant intercellular substance that provides support and flexibility.
