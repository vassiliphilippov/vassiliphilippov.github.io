double take

noun
delayed reaction of surprise
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl teɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl teɪk / (BrE)
an act of suddenly noticing something and showing surprise after first appearing not to notice it

Examples

do a double take in surprise
His new haircut was so shocking that I had to do a double take.
She did a double take when she saw her old friend across the street.
The actor's comic double take made the whole audience burst out laughing.

  • synonym – startle: showing sudden surprise or shock
