finger-flower

noun
European plant with tubular flowers, source of digitalis
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪŋɡɚ ˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A tall, leafy European biennial or perennial plant with clusters of large pink-purple tubular flowers; its leaves yield the drug digitalis and are poisonous to livestock.

Examples

A field of finger-flowers adds a vibrant splash of color to the countryside in early summer.

The poisonous leaves of the finger-flower have been used to make heart medication.
