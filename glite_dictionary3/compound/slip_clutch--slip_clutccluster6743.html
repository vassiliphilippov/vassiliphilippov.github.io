slip clutch

noun
torque-limiting friction clutch
Pronunciation: / slɪp ˈklʌtʃ / (AmE), / slɪp ˈklʌtʃ / (BrE)
a mechanical friction clutch that automatically slips to limit torque when it exceeds a set value, protecting machinery from overload

Examples

slip clutch protects machinery
If you hear a ratcheting sound, the slip clutch may be engaging to protect the drive components.
Technicians adjusted the slip clutch to ensure the conveyor stops before excessive force is applied.
The machine is equipped with a slip clutch to prevent damage during an overload situation.

  • synonym – friction clutch: a clutch that transmits motion by friction
