dog collar

noun
clerical collar worn by clergy
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔg ˌkɑlər / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ ˌkɒlə / (BrE)
A stiff white collar with no opening in the front, worn as a distinctive symbol by some Christian clergy.

Examples

priest's dog collar
In some denominations, only ordained ministers wear the traditional dog collar.
She recognized the bishop immediately by the dog collar he wore.
The priest adjusted his dog collar before stepping into the pulpit.
