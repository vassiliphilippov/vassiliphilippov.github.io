vervain sage

noun
a Eurasian sage with blue flowers
Pronunciation: / vərˈveɪn seɪdʒ / (AmE), / vəˈveɪn seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of sage (Salvia verbenaca) with blue flowers and verbena-like foliage, sometimes naturalized in the United States.

Examples

vervain sage blue flowers
Vervain sage is often grown in gardens for its attractive blue flowers.
In some parts of the United States, vervain sage has become naturalized and grows wild.
The leaves of vervain sage resemble those of verbena, making it easy to identify.
