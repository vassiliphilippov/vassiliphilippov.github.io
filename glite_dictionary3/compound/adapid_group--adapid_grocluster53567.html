adapid group

noun
extinct group of early primates
Pronunciation: / ˈædəpɪd ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈadəpɪd ɡruːp / (BrE)
an extinct group of small, mostly diurnal primates that lived 30 to 50 million years ago, probably ancestral to lemurs and possibly related to anthropoids

Examples

fossil adapid group
Fossils from the adapid group offer clues about primate evolution.
Many paleontologists believe that the adapid group is closely related to modern lemurs.
The adapid group was abundant in Europe and North America during the Eocene epoch.
