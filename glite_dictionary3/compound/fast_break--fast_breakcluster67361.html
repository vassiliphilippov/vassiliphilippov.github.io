fast break

noun
quick offensive play in basketball
Pronunciation: / fæst breɪk / (AmE), / fɑːst breɪk / (BrE)
(in basketball) a quick offensive play in which a team moves rapidly down the court in an attempt to score before the opposing defense can set up.

Examples

fast break after steal
After stealing the ball, the players launched a swift fast break that ended with an easy basket.
Her ability to anticipate passes made the fast break especially effective for their team.
The team scored twelve points off the fast break in the first quarter alone.
