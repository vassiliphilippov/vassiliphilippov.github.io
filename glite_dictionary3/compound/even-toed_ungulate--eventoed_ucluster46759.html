even-toed ungulate

noun
hoofed mammal with an even number of toes
Pronunciation: / ˈivən toʊd ˈʌŋɡjələt / (AmE), / ˈiːvən təʊd ˈʌŋɡjʊlət / (BrE)
a placental mammal with hooves, having an even number of functional toes on each foot, such as cattle, pigs, and deer

Examples

even-toed ungulate like a pig
Many farm animals, including sheep and pigs, are even-toed ungulates.
Scientists classify deer as even-toed ungulates because they walk on two of their four toes.
The giraffe is an example of an even-toed ungulate found in African savannas.

  • antonym – odd-toed ungulate: hooved mammal with an odd number of toes, like horses and rhinos
