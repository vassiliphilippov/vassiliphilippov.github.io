great seal

noun
principal governmental seal of authority
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt siːl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt siːl / (BrE)
the principal official seal of a government, used to symbolize authority or sovereignty and to authenticate important state documents

Examples

the great seal of England
After the new monarch ascended the throne, the design of the great seal was updated.
Only certain officials are allowed to handle the great seal of the country.
The document was stamped with the great seal to confirm its authenticity.
