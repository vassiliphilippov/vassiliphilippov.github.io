African millet

noun
a cereal grass with edible seeds, staple food
Pronunciation: / ˈæfrɪkən ˈmɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈæfrɪkən ˈmɪlɪt / (BrE)
An East Indian cereal grass cultivated for its edible seeds, which yield a somewhat bitter flour used as a staple food in some regions.

Examples

African millet flour
Farmers in arid regions often rely on African millet as a drought-resistant crop.
In traditional recipes, flour made from African millet can be used to make flatbreads.
The somewhat bitter taste of African millet flour adds a unique flavor to many regional dishes.

Related Terms
