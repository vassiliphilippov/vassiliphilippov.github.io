electrical line of force

noun
imaginary line showing electric field direction
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl laɪn əv ˈfɔrs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl laɪn əv ˈfɔːs / (BrE)
an imaginary line in an electric field along which a positive test charge would move; used to represent the direction and strength of the field

Examples

curved electrical line of force
Diagrams often illustrate the direction of an electric field with curved electrical lines of force.
The pattern of electrical lines of force between two charges reveals the nature of their interaction.
When you place a conductor in the field, the electrical lines of force become distorted around it.
