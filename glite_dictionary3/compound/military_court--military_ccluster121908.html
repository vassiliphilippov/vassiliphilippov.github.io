military court

noun
court for military discipline and justice
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri kɔːt / (BrE)
a court made up of commissioned officers to try members of the armed forces for breaches of military law

Examples

military court trial
The military court found the officer guilty of misconduct during active duty.
The accused soldier was summoned before a military court to answer the charges of disobedience.
Unlike a civilian trial, a military court operates under a separate set of legal rules.
