sickle-cell anemia

noun
genetic disorder causing crescent-shaped red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪkəl sɛl əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl sɛl əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A genetic blood disorder, chiefly affecting people of African descent, in which red blood cells become crescent-shaped, causing anemia and other health problems.

Examples

sickle-cell anemia diagnosis
Children with sickle-cell anemia require regular medical check-ups.
Many people with sickle-cell anemia experience episodes of pain known as crises.
