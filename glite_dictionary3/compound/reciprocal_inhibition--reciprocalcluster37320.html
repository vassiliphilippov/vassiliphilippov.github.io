reciprocal inhibition

noun
behavior therapy technique inhibiting one response with another
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɪprəkəl ˌɪnhəˈbɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl ˌɪnhɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
a method in behavior therapy where one response is deliberately elicited to inhibit another, mutually incompatible response, such as inducing relaxation to counter anxiety

Examples

reciprocal inhibition in therapy
In behavior therapy, reciprocal inhibition helps clients manage phobias by pairing anxiety triggers with relaxation techniques.
Practicing reciprocal inhibition can aid in reducing stress responses to certain stimuli.
The therapist explained reciprocal inhibition as a process of countering unwanted behaviors through introducing incompatible actions.
