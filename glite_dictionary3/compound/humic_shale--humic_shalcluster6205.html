humic shale

noun
shale rich in humic acids
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmɪk ʃeɪl / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmɪk ʃeɪl / (BrE)
A type of shale that contains a high concentration of humic acids, usually formed from the remains of plant material.

Examples

humic shale layer
Layers of humic shale in the region suggest a history of abundant plant life.
Oil companies often analyze humic shale for its potential as a hydrocarbon source.
The geologists collected samples of humic shale to study its organic content.
