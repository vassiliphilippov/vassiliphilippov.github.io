Golden Age

noun
period of great prosperity or achievement
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ˌeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˌeɪdʒ / (BrE)
A period of great happiness, prosperity, or achievement; originally, in classical mythology, the first and best age of the world.

Examples

the Golden Age of art
Historians often refer to the 5th century BC in Greece as its Golden Age.
Many believe we are living through a Golden Age of television drama.
The painter's work reflected the ideals of a lost Golden Age of innocence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dark age: a period of decline or stagnation, often after a golden age
  • degree lower – silver age: a period of less prosperity or achievement following a golden age
