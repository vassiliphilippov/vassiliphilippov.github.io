electrical fuse

noun
safety device for breaking overloaded circuit
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl fjuz / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl fjuːz / (BrE)
a safety device consisting of a wire or strip that melts and breaks an electric circuit if the current exceeds a safe level

Examples

replace a blown electrical fuse
Always replace a blown electrical fuse with one of the correct rating for your device.
An electrical fuse is designed to protect wiring and appliances from overheating or catching fire.
He traced the fault back to a faulty electrical fuse in the main panel.
