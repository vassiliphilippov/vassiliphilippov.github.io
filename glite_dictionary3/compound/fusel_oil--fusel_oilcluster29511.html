fusel oil

noun
a by-product mixture from liquor distillation
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːzəl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːzəl ɔɪl / (BrE)
an oily mixture of higher alcohols (such as amyl alcohols, propanol, and butanol) produced as a by-product during the distillation of fermented liquors

Examples

fusel oil in whiskey
Distillers use special techniques to reduce fusel oil during the production of whiskey.
Excess fusel oil gives homemade spirits an unpleasant odor and harsh flavor.
The presence of fusel oil in moonshine can affect both its taste and safety.
