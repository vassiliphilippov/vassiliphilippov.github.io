auditory canal

noun
outer ear passageway
Pronunciation: / ˈɔdətɔri kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt(ə)ri kəˈnal / (BrE)
the passage in the outer ear that leads from the auricle to the eardrum (tympanic membrane)

Examples

A cotton swab should not be inserted deep into the auditory canal as it can cause damage.
Earwax can build up inside the auditory canal and affect hearing.
The doctor examined the patient's auditory canal for signs of infection.
