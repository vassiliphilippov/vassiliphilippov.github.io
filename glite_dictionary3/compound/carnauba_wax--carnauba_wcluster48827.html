carnauba wax

noun
a hard wax from the carnauba palm
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈnaʊbə wæks / (AmE), / kɑːˈnaʊbə wæks / (BrE)
a hard yellowish to brownish wax obtained from the leaves of the carnauba palm, used especially in floor waxes and polishes

Examples

carnauba wax polish shoes
Bakers often use carnauba wax to coat candies and give them a glossy finish.
Many car care products contain carnauba wax to protect and enhance vehicle paint.
The floor had a brilliant shine after being polished with carnauba wax.
