quick grass

noun
a rapidly spreading European grass weed
Pronunciation: / kwɪk ɡræs / (AmE), / kwɪk ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European grass that spreads quickly by creeping rhizomes and is naturalized in North America as a weed.

Examples

quick grass spreads fast
Quick grass is notoriously hard to remove from gardens because of its spreading roots.
Some herbicides are effective against quick grass, but manual removal is also necessary.
The farmer struggled to control the quick grass invading his cornfields.
