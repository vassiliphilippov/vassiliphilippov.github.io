anomalistic month

noun
lunar period from perigee to perigee
Pronunciation: / əˌnɑː.məˈlɪs.tɪk mʌnθ / (AmE), / əˌnɒ.məˈlɪs.tɪk mʌnθ / (BrE)
the period taken by the Moon to return to the same point of perigee in its orbit, about 27.5546 days

Examples

anomalistic month calculation
Astronomers use the anomalistic month to calculate certain lunar phenomena.
The anomalistic month is slightly longer than the sidereal month due to the Moon's elliptical orbit.
The difference between a anomalistic month and a synodic month can affect eclipse predictions.
