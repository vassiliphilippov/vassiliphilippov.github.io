true rib

noun
first seven ribs attached to the sternum
Pronunciation: / tru rɪb / (AmE), / truː rɪb / (BrE)
any of the first seven ribs in humans that are directly attached to the sternum by costal cartilage

Examples

true rib injury
Doctors examined his chest to check for any broken true ribs.
The first true rib is located just below the clavicle.
Unlike a false rib, a true rib is connected directly to the sternum.

Related Terms

  • antonym – false rib: any rib not directly attached to the sternum
