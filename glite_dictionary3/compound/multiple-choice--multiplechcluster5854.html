multiple-choice

adjective
having several possible answers to choose from
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltəpl ˈtʃɔɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪpl ˈtʃɔɪs / (BrE)
(of questions or tests) offering several possible answers from which the correct one must be chosen.

Examples

multiple-choice question
It’s important to read every answer option carefully when tackling multiple-choice quizzes.
She prefers multiple-choice tests to essay-based assessments.
The exam consisted entirely of multiple-choice questions.
