corn borer

noun
insect larva that infests maize
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrn ˌbɔrər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːn ˌbɔːrə / (BrE)
an insect (moth or its larva) that damages corn and other crops by boring into the stems, especially the European corn borer

Examples

corn borer infestation
Farmers often lose large portions of their crop to the destructive corn borer.
Pesticide-resistant strains of corn borer have become a significant problem for agriculture.
The corn borer is a major threat to maize production around the world.
Crop losses increased after the corn borer infestation spread across the region.
Farmers use biological controls to manage the corn borer population in their fields.
The larvae of the corn borer tunnel into the stalks, weakening the corn plants.
