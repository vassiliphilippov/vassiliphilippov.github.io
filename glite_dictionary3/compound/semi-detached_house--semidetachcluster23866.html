semi-detached house

noun
house joined to another on one side
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmi dɪˈtætʃt haʊs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi dɪˈtætʃt haʊs / (BrE)
A house that is joined to another house on only one side, sharing a wall with it.

Examples

modern semi-detached house
She grew up in a semi-detached house with her parents and younger brother.
The street was lined with rows of semi-detached houses built in the 1950s.
They decided to buy a semi-detached house in the suburbs to have more space.

Related Terms

  • antonym – detached house: a house not attached to any other house
  • antonym – terraced house: a house in a row of identical or mirror-image houses sharing side walls
