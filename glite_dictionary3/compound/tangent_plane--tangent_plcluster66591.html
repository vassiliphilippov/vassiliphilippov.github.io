tangent plane

noun
plane touching a surface at one point
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒənt pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈtændʒənt pleɪn / (BrE)
a plane that touches a surface at a single point and contains all the lines tangent to that point on the surface

Examples

tangent plane to a surface
Calculating the tangent plane is an important step in multivariable calculus.
The tangent plane at a given point on the sphere is perpendicular to the radius at that point.
To approximate the surface near a point, mathematicians often use the tangent plane.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tangent line: a straight line that touches a curve at a single point without crossing it
