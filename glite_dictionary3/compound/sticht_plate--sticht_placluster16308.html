Sticht plate

noun
climbing rope brake device
Pronunciation: / stɪkt pleɪt / (AmE), / stɪxt pleɪt / (BrE)
a small metal device used in climbing as a rope brake, consisting of a slotted plate through which a bight of rope passes to a locking carabiner, allowing for belaying or controlling descent

Examples

Sticht plate for belaying
Compared to modern devices, the Sticht plate can be less forgiving with thicker ropes.
Some instructors still use a Sticht plate for teaching basic belaying techniques.
The climber attached a Sticht plate to his harness before beginning the ascent.
