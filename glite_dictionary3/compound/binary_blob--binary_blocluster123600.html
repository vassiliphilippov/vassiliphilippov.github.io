binary blob

noun
block of unstructured binary data
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌnɛri ˌblɑb / (AmE), / ˈbɪnəri ˌblɒb / (BrE)
a file or block of data consisting of binary information stored as a single, often opaque, entity, typically without a defined structure or easily accessible content

Examples

download a binary blob
Developers often avoid using a binary blob when they need transparency in their code.
