objective case

noun
grammatical case for direct objects
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛktɪv keɪs / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case used for a noun or pronoun when it serves as the direct object of a verb or the object of a preposition

Examples

objective case for pronouns
Certain pronouns, like 'me' and 'us,' belong to the objective case rather than the subjective.
In the sentence 'She saw him,' the pronoun 'him' is in the objective case.
The objective case is used for the object of a verb or preposition in English.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accusative case: case marking the direct object; in some languages, similar to the objective case
  • antonym – nominative case: case for the subject of a verb
