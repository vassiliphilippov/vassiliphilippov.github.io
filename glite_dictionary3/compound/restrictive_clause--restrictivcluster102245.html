restrictive clause

noun
subordinate clause essential to noun meaning
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪktɪv klɑːz / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪv klɑːz / (BrE)
a subordinate clause that limits or defines the meaning of the noun phrase it modifies, essential to the sentence's meaning

Examples

restrictive clause gives details
A restrictive clause is not set off by commas in standard English.
Can you identify the restrictive clause in this sentence?
The sentence 'The book that I borrowed was excellent' contains a restrictive clause modifying 'book'.
