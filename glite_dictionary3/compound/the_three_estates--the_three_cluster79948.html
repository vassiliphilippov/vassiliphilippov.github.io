the three estates

noun
the three main social classes in historical societies
Pronunciation: / ði ˈθri ɪˈsteɪts / (AmE), / ði ˈθri ɪˈsteɪts / (BrE)
the three main social classes in certain societies, especially in medieval and early modern Europe, traditionally regarded as the clergy, the nobility, and the commons, each with distinct roles and privileges

the three estates of society
Debates about the privileges of the three estates contributed to the outbreak of revolution.
