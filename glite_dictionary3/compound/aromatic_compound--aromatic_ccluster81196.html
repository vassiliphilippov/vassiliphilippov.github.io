aromatic compound

noun
compound with benzene ring(s)
Pronunciation: / əˈroʊ.mə.tɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / əˈrɒ.mə.tɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
a compound, especially a hydrocarbon, containing one or more benzene rings or similar ring structures, notable for their stability and characteristic chemical properties

Examples

simple aromatic compound
Benzene is a simple aromatic compound commonly used in the chemical industry.
Many aromatic compounds have distinctive odors, but not all are actually fragrant.
Naphthalene, found in mothballs, is another example of an aromatic compound.
