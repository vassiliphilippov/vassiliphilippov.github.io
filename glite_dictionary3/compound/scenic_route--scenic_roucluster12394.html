scenic route

noun
indirect but enriching path (figurative)
Pronunciation: / c8sid0.n6ak rud0t / (AmE), / c8sid0.n6ak rud0t / (BrE)
a figurative indirect way of achieving something that is more enjoyable, instructive, or enriching than the fastest, most direct route.

Examples

choose the scenic route in life
After several failures, they chose the scenic route to build skills before scaling the product.
Parents sometimes take the scenic route to teach children patience instead of rushing lessons.
Rather than immediately expanding, the manager took the scenic route and tested new markets slowly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – detour: an indirect route or method that avoids the most direct path
  • antonym – shortcut: a quicker, more direct route or method; opposite of taking a longer, indirect approach
