fox squirrel

noun
large North American tree squirrel
Pronunciation: / fɑks ˈskwɝːl / (AmE), / fɒks ˈskwɪəl / (BrE)
an exceptionally large tree-dwelling squirrel native to the eastern United States, known for its bushy tail and often reddish-brown fur.

Examples

a fox squirrel climbing trees
The fox squirrel is larger than the more common gray squirrel and has a reddish coat.
Unlike other squirrels, the fox squirrel prefers open woodlands and savannas.
We spotted a fox squirrel gathering acorns in the park this morning.
