hereditary spinal ataxia

noun
genetic spinal cord disorder causing ataxia
Pronunciation: / həˌrɛdəˌtɛri ˈspaɪnl əˈtæksiə / (AmE), / hɛˌrɛdɪˈtɛəri ˈspaɪnl əˈtæksiə / (BrE)
A progressive disorder involving sclerosis of the posterior and lateral columns of the spinal cord, characterized by muscular weakness and abnormal gait, usually beginning in childhood.
