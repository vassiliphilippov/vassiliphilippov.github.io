temperature gradient

noun
rate of temperature change over distance
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚətʃɚ ˈɡreɪdiənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətʃə ˈɡreɪdiənt / (BrE)
the rate at which temperature changes over a certain distance, especially with changes in altitude or across a substance

Examples

steep temperature gradient
A steep temperature gradient can lead to strong thermal currents in the ocean.
Scientists measured the temperature gradient beneath the Earth's surface to study geothermal activity.
The temperature gradient in the atmosphere causes air to move and form winds.
