full page

noun
entire page covered (e.g., ad or photo)
Pronunciation: / fʊl peɪdʒ / (AmE), / fʊl peɪdʒ / (BrE)
an entire page of a publication covered by text, an image, or advertising, without sharing space with other items

Examples

full page newspaper ad
He purchased a full page in the newspaper to make the announcement.
Her photo was featured on a striking full page at the start of the article.
The magazine ran a full page ad for the new smartphone.
