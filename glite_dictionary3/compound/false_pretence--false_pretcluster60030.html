false pretence

noun
offense of obtaining property by deceit
Pronunciation: / fɔls prɪˈtɛns / (AmE), / fɔːls prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
(law) the offense of obtaining property or money by intentionally misrepresenting facts with the intent to defraud.

Examples

charged with false pretence
He was convicted of false pretence after tricking the victim into signing over her car.
Obtaining money under false pretence is a serious criminal offense in many countries.
The company was accused of selling products on the basis of false pretence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fraud: the crime of deceiving someone to gain money or property
  • synonym – larceny: theft of personal property
