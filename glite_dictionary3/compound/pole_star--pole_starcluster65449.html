pole star

noun
the star marking the North Pole direction
Pronunciation: / poʊl stɑr / (AmE), / pəʊl stɑː / (BrE)
the star situated nearly in direct line with the Earth's axis above the North Pole, currently Polaris, used for navigation; the brightest star in Ursa Minor.

Examples

the pole star in the sky
Many ancient cultures used the pole star for navigation and to align their monuments.
Sailors have long relied on the pole star to find their way at sea.
The pole star appears almost stationary in the night sky.
