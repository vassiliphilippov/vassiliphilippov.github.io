Iraqi National Congress

proper noun
Iraqi political opposition coalition (formed 1992)
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɑki ˈnæʃənəl ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɑːki ˈnæʃənəl ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
a coalition of Iraqi opposition groups, including Sunni and Shiite Arabs and Kurds, formed in 1992 to oppose Saddam Hussein's government and promote the establishment of a new government in Iraq.

Examples

the Iraqi National Congress formed
Following its formation in 1992, the Iraqi National Congress aimed to unite diverse ethnic and religious groups against the regime.
Several world leaders met with representatives of the Iraqi National Congress to discuss the future of Iraq.
The Iraqi National Congress played a crucial role in rallying international support against Saddam Hussein.
