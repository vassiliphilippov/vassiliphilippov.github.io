Dover's powder

noun
historical medicinal powder with opium and ipecac
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊvərz ˈpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvəz ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A medicinal powder composed mainly of ipecac and opium, formerly used to relieve pain and induce sweating.

Examples

Dover's powder for fever
Medical texts from the 19th century frequently mention the use of Dover's powder for relieving pain.
Physicians once prescribed Dover's powder to treat fevers and encourage sweating in patients.
The formula for Dover's powder included opium and ipecac, making it a potent remedy in its time.
