flour corn

noun
maize variety with soft, starchy kernels
Pronunciation: / ˈflaʊɚ kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə kɔːn / (BrE)
a variety of maize with kernels composed almost entirely of soft starch, suitable for making flour

Examples

flour corn for baking
The farmers planted flour corn because its soft kernels are ideal for grinding into meal.
Traditional tortillas in some regions are made using flour corn rather than dent or flint varieties.
Unlike other types, flour corn is prized for its ability to produce fine, white corn flour.
