globus pallidus

noun
pale inner part of brain's lenticular nucleus
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbəs pəˈlaɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəs pəˈleɪdəs / (BrE)
the pale, rounded mass of gray matter located within the basal ganglia of the brain, forming the inner part of the lenticular nucleus

Examples

the globus pallidus in the brain
Damage to the globus pallidus can result in movement disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
Doctors observed abnormal activity in the patient's globus pallidus using specialized brain imaging equipment.
The globus pallidus works closely with other basal ganglia structures to help control voluntary movement.
