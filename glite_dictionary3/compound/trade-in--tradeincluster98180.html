trade-in

noun
something given as partial payment for a new purchase
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪd ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd ɪn / (BrE)
an item offered as part of the payment when purchasing something new, especially a vehicle or appliance

Examples

trade-in car value
He used his old car as a trade-in to lower the price of the new model.
Many customers prefer to offer a trade-in rather than paying the full price upfront.
The dealer examined my phone before offering a trade-in value.
