preventive strike

noun
military attack to prevent expected aggression
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvɛntɪv straɪk / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv straɪk / (BrE)
a military attack launched to prevent or deter expected aggression from hostile forces

Examples

launch a preventive strike
International law often debates the justification for a preventive strike in situations of escalating tension.
Some analysts argue that a preventive strike could worsen the conflict rather than stop it.
The government authorized a preventive strike to deter possible attacks from the neighboring country.

Related Terms

  • synonym – preemptive strike: a military attack launched in anticipation of immediate aggression
