point duty

noun
traffic control by police at an intersection
Pronunciation: / pɔɪnt ˈduːti / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
the control of traffic by a police officer stationed at an intersection, especially by directing vehicles and pedestrians

Examples

on point duty at a crossing
After the traffic lights failed, an officer performed point duty to keep vehicles moving smoothly.
In the rain, she stood on point duty for hours, ensuring everyone crossed safely.
The police officer was assigned point duty at the busy city intersection during rush hour.
