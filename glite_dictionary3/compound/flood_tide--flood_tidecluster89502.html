flood tide

noun
rising period of the tide
Pronunciation: / flʌd taɪd / (AmE), / flʌd taɪd / (BrE)
the period during which the tide is rising and water flows towards the shore, between low tide and the next high tide

Examples

flood tide rises quickly
Several fishing boats set out to sea at flood tide to take advantage of the rising water.
Shellfish gatherers waited for the flood tide before beginning their work.
The riverbanks were barely visible during the flood tide.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ebb tide: the period when the tide is falling and water flows away from the shore
