wood tar

noun
tar distilled from wood
Pronunciation: / wʊd tɑr / (AmE), / wʊd tɑː / (BrE)
a dark, viscous liquid obtained by the destructive distillation of wood, used as a preservative or in making chemicals

Old medical remedies sometimes included small amounts of wood tar as an ingredient.
