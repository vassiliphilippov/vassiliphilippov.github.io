poor rates

noun
local tax for relief of the poor
Pronunciation: / pʊr reɪts / (AmE), / pɔː reɪts / (BrE)
A local tax levied for the support of the poor, especially as administered in Britain before the 20th century.

Examples

poor rates payment
Debates over the fairness of poor rates were common in 19th-century England.
Landowners were required to contribute to the poor rates each year.
The introduction of poor rates changed how parishes cared for their destitute residents.
