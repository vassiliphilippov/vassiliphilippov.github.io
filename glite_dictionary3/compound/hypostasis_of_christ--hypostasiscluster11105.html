hypostasis of Christ

noun
theological concept of Christ's united natures
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑː.stə.sɪs əv kraɪst / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒs.tə.sɪs əv kraɪst / (BrE)
In Christian theology, the union of divine and human natures in the single person of Christ, or more generally, any of the three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit) comprising the Trinity.

Examples

the hypostasis of Christ doctrine
Early church councils sought to clarify the nature of the hypostasis of Christ.
The doctrine of the hypostasis of Christ is central to many theological debates.
Understanding the hypostasis of Christ helps explain the relationship between Christ's divinity and humanity.
