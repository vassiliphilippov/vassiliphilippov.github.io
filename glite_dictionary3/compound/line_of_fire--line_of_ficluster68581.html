line of fire

noun
path of a discharged projectile
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv faɪr / (AmE), / laɪn ɒv faɪə / (BrE)
the path along which a bullet or other projectile travels after being fired from a weapon

Examples

in the line of fire during shooting
Emergency personnel should never enter the line of fire without proper protection.
Make sure you are not standing in the line of fire during shooting practice.
The soldier quickly moved out of the line of fire to avoid getting hit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crossfire: gunfire from two or more directions crossing each other's paths
  • synonym – direct fire: gunfire delivered directly at a visible target
