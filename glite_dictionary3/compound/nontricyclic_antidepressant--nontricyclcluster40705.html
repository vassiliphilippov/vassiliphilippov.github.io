nontricyclic antidepressant

noun
antidepressant drugs not tricyclic or MAO inhibitors
Pronunciation: / nɑnˌtraɪˈsɪklɪk ˌæn.ti.dɪˈprɛs.ənt / (AmE), / nɒnˌtraɪˈsɪklɪk ˌæn.ti.dɪˈprɛs.ənt / (BrE)
A class of antidepressant drugs that are not tricyclic antidepressants and do not act by inhibiting monoamine oxidase (MAO).

Examples

take a nontricyclic antidepressant pill
Some patients respond better to a nontricyclic antidepressant than to a traditional tricyclic medication.
Switching to a nontricyclic antidepressant can reduce drowsiness for some individuals.
Your doctor may prescribe a nontricyclic antidepressant if you experience side effects from other types of antidepressants.
