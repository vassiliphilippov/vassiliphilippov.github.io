mission creep

noun
gradual expansion of project scope
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪʃən kriːp / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃən kriːp / (BrE)
The gradual expansion of a project's original goals, often resulting in undesirable complexity or loss of focus.

Examples

mission creep in projects
Project managers warned about the risks of mission creep during their weekly meetings.
The team tried to prevent mission creep by sticking to the original plan.
Unchecked mission creep led to significant delays and extra costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – scope creep: uncontrolled changes or continuous growth in a project's scope
