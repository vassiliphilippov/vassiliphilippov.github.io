microcosmic salt

noun
white salt used in chemical analysis
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈkɑzmɪk sɔlt / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈkɒzmɪk sɒlt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt, sodium ammonium hydrogen phosphate, found in urine and used in analytical chemistry to test for metal oxides

Examples

microcosmic salt test metal
Early chemists frequently relied on microcosmic salt for conducting qualitative analysis in their laboratories.
The presence of microcosmic salt in the solution facilitated the identification of various metal oxides.
When heated, microcosmic salt forms a clear bead, useful for testing metal ions.
