supreme court

noun
the highest court in a country or state
Pronunciation: / suˈprim kɔrt / (AmE), / suˈpriːm kɔːt / (BrE)
The highest judicial court in a country or state, especially one that has authority to review decisions of lower courts.

Examples

the supreme court decision
She plans to appeal the decision to the state's supreme court.
The U.S. Supreme Court will hear several important cases this term.
The ruling was upheld by the supreme court after a lengthy legal battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – high court: a court with authority above lower courts; often synonymous with 'supreme court' outside the US
