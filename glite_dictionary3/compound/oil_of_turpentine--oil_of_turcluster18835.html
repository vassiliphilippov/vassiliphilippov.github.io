oil of turpentine

noun
volatile liquid used as paint thinner and solvent
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl əv tɜːrˈpɛntaɪn / (AmE), / ɔɪl əv tɜːˈpɛntaɪn / (BrE)
a volatile liquid distilled from turpentine oleoresin, used as a paint thinner, solvent, and sometimes in medicine

Examples

oil of turpentine as paint thinner
Artists traditionally use oil of turpentine to thin oil paints for smoother brushwork.
In the past, oil of turpentine was sometimes prescribed in small doses for medicinal purposes.
The smell of oil of turpentine filled the studio as she cleaned her brushes.
