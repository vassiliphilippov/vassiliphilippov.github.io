suckling reflex

noun
reflex causing newborns to suck for milk
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌk.lɪŋ ˈriː.flɛks / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.lɪŋ ˈriː.flɛks / (BrE)
a reflex in newborn mammals that causes them to find, grasp, and suck on the nipple to obtain milk

Examples

newborn suckling reflex
Doctors checked the baby's suckling reflex to ensure normal neurological development.
If the suckling reflex is weak, feeding difficulties may occur in infants.
The suckling reflex is present in newborns to help them feed immediately after birth.
