oil palm

noun
tropical palm yielding palm oil
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl pɑm / (AmE), / ɔɪl pɑːm / (BrE)
A tropical palm tree of the genus Elaeis, with pinnate leaves and clusters of reddish fruit, cultivated for its oil-rich fruit and seeds.

Examples

oil palm plantation
Many rainforest areas have been cleared to make way for oil palm plantations.
The oil palm is widely cultivated in Southeast Asia for its valuable oil.
The fruit of the oil palm is processed to produce both palm oil and palm kernel oil.
