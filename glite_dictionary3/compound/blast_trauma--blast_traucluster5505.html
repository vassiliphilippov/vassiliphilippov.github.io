blast trauma

noun
injury from an explosion, especially in enclosed spaces
Pronunciation: / blæst ˈtrɑmə / (AmE), / blɑːst ˈtrɑːmə / (BrE)
an injury caused by the explosion of a bomb or similar device, especially in enclosed spaces; commonly associated with physical damage from blast waves, flying debris, or pressure changes

Examples

blast trauma from explosion
Doctors trained in emergency services must be able to recognize signs of blast trauma.
The effects of blast trauma can include hearing loss, lung damage, and other internal injuries.
The patient suffered blast trauma after a bomb detonated in the building.
