white poplar

noun
a poplar tree with white bark and leaves underneath
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈpɑːplɚ / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a species of poplar tree (Populus alba) characterized by white bark and leaves with whitish undersides, widely cultivated for ornamental purposes

The white poplar is easily recognized by the silvery underside of its leaves.
