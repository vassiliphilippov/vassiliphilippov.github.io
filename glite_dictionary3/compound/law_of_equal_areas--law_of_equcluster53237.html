law of equal areas

noun
Kepler's second law in planetary motion
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˈikwəl ˈɛriəz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈiːkwəl ˈeəriəz / (BrE)
Kepler's second law stating that a line joining a planet and the sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time.

Examples

law of equal areas in planetary motion
According to the law of equal areas, planets move faster when they are closer to the sun.
Astronomers use the law of equal areas to predict a planet's changing speed along its orbit.
Kepler's law of equal areas was a breakthrough in understanding planetary motion.
