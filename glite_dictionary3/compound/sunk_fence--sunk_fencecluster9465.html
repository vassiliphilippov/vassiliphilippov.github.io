sunk fence

noun
land-dividing ditch with a retaining wall
Pronunciation: / sʌŋk fɛns / (AmE), / sʌŋk fɛns / (BrE)
a ditch with one side formed by a retaining wall, used as a barrier to divide lands without interrupting the natural landscape

Examples

sunk fence in a garden
A sunk fence separated the estate from the surrounding fields, maintaining an unbroken view of the landscape.
He admired how the sunk fence blended with the grounds, keeping livestock contained without spoiling the scenery.
The elegant garden design featured a sunk fence instead of a visible wall.
