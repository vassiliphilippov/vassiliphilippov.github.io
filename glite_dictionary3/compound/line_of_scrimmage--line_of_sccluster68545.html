line of scrimmage

noun
football: imaginary line teams face at play start
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / laɪn ɒv ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
an imaginary line on a football field parallel to the goal lines where the teams line up at the start of each play

Examples

line of scrimmage snap
After a penalty, the referee moved the line of scrimmage five yards forward.
Both teams lined up on the line of scrimmage before the play began.
The quarterback stepped back from the line of scrimmage to pass the ball.
