instrumental role

noun
semantic role of an agent's tool or means
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəl roʊl / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəl rəʊl / (BrE)
the semantic role of an entity used by an agent to carry out an action or initiate a process, typically in linguistic analysis

Examples

instrumental role in grammar
In many languages, the instrumental role is marked by a special case or preposition.
Linguists study how the instrumental role is expressed in different syntactic structures.
The word 'key' in 'She opened the door with a key' has an instrumental role.
