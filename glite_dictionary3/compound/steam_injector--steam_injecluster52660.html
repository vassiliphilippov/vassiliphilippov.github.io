steam injector

noun
device injecting liquid with steam pressure
Pronunciation: / stiːm ɪnˈdʒɛktɚ / (AmE), / stiːm ɪnˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
a device using steam pressure to inject liquid, especially water, into a boiler or closed system

Examples

steam injector in a boiler
A malfunctioning steam injector can cause dangerous drops in boiler water levels.
Modern steam engines still utilize a steam injector to ensure a continuous supply of water.
The engineer used a steam injector to feed water into the locomotive's boiler while it was under pressure.
