musicogenic epilepsy

noun
epilepsy triggered by music
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːzɪkoʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪkəʊˈdʒɛnɪk ˌɛpɪˈlepsi / (BrE)
A form of reflex epilepsy in which seizures are triggered by listening to music.

Examples

musicogenic epilepsy triggered by music
Cases of musicogenic epilepsy are rare, but they highlight the powerful effect music can have on the brain.
He avoided concerts and loud environments due to his musicogenic epilepsy.
The neurologist diagnosed her with musicogenic epilepsy after she experienced seizures while listening to certain songs.
