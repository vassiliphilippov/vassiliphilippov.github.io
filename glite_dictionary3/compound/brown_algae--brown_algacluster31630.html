brown algae

noun
group of brown-colored algae
Pronunciation: / braʊn ˈældʒi / (AmE), / braʊn ˈældʒiː / (BrE)
A large group of mostly marine algae that appear brownish or olive in color because their chlorophyll is masked by brown and yellow pigments.

Examples

brown algae in the ocean
Brown algae are an important source of food and habitat in many marine environments.
Kelp forests, made up of brown algae, provide shelter for numerous marine species.
Scientists are studying the compounds produced by brown algae for potential use in medicine.
