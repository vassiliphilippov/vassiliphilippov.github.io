gross sales

noun
total sales before deductions
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊs seɪlz / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs seɪlz / (BrE)
The total income from the sale of goods or services before any deductions, such as returns, allowances, or discounts, over a given period.

Examples

gross sales increased
Although gross sales increased, the business faced lower profits due to rising costs and higher returns.
Last year, the company reported gross sales of over two million dollars.
Retailers often track their gross sales to evaluate overall store performance before considering returns or discounts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – net sales: total sales after deductions such as returns and allowances
  • synonym – revenue: total income, especially of a company, before expenses are deducted
