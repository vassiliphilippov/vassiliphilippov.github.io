specialty crop

noun
crop grown for specific or niche markets
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəlti krɑp / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlti krɒp / (BrE)
a crop that is cultivated for a specific market or purpose, typically outside of major staple food production, such as fruits, vegetables, nuts, and ornamental plants

Examples

specialty crop production
Blueberries and cut flowers are major specialty crops in this area.
Grant programs are available to support specialty crop production in sustainable agriculture.
Many farmers in the region have switched to growing specialty crops to increase their profits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – staple crop: a main or important crop grown widely for food
