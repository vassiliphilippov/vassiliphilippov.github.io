eating away

noun
gradual wearing down or destruction
Pronunciation: / ˈitɪŋ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːtɪŋ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
The gradual wearing down or destruction of something by a continuous process, especially by erosion in geology or metaphorically by persistent adverse effects.

Examples

eating away at trust
Corruption can act as an eating away at public trust in government institutions.
The eating away of the stone monument was evident after centuries of wind and rain.
The constant eating away of the coastline by waves is a serious concern for local residents.
