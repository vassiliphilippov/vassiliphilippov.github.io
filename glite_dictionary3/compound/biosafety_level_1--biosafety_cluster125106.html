biosafety level 1

noun
lowest laboratory biosafety containment level
Pronunciation: / baɪ.oʊˌseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl wʌn / (AmE), / baɪ.əʊˌseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl wʌn / (BrE)
the lowest level of precautions required in laboratories, dealing with agents that pose minimal potential hazard to laboratory personnel and the environment and do not ordinarily cause human disease

Examples

biosafety level 1 laboratory
Basic microbiology teaching labs are generally classified as biosafety level 1.
The laboratory was certified as meeting biosafety level 1 standards.
Working with non-pathogenic strains of E. coli requires only biosafety level 1 precautions.
