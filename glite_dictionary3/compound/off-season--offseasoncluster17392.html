off-season

noun
period of low activity or demand
Pronunciation: / ˈɑf ˌsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈɒf ˌsiːzən / (BrE)
The period of the year when activity, demand, or business is at its lowest, often resulting in lower prices or reduced offerings, especially in tourism or sports.

Examples

off-season hotel rates
Many hotels offer discounts during the off-season to attract guests.
Most seaside shops close for the off-season and reopen in the spring.
We prefer to travel in the off-season when destinations are less crowded.

Related Terms

  • synonym – low season: another term for period of least demand, especially in tourism
  • antonym – peak season: the period when activity or demand is highest
