European blueberry

noun
a blue-black berry native to Europe
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈbluːˌbɛri / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small blue-black berry, similar to the American blueberry, native to Europe and commonly known as bilberry.

Examples

fresh European blueberry tart
She made a tart using freshly picked European blueberries from the forest.
The European blueberry thrives in acidic soils throughout Scandinavia and central Europe.
Unlike the American variety, the European blueberry is smaller and darker in color.
