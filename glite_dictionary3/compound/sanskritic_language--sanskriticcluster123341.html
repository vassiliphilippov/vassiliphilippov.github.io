Sanskritic language

noun
ancient classical language of India
Pronunciation: / ˈsæn skrɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsan skrɪtɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
An ancient Indic language of India, the classical language of Hinduism and the Vedas, now used mainly for religious and scholarly purposes; also, an official language of India.

Examples

ancient Sanskritic language
Although rarely spoken today, the Sanskritic language remains influential in Indian culture and literature.
Many Hindu religious ceremonies are conducted using the Sanskritic language.
The hymns of the Rigveda were composed in an early form of Sanskritic language.
