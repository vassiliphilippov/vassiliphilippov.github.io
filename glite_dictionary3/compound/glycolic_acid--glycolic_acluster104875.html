glycolic acid

noun
a crystalline acid used in skin care
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪˈkɑlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɡlʌɪˈkɒlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless or white crystalline acid found in sugar cane, sugar beets, and unripe grapes, used in skin care and chemical peels

Examples

glycolic acid skin treatment
Many skincare products contain glycolic acid to help exfoliate and brighten the skin.
Researchers extracted glycolic acid from sugar cane for use in laboratory experiments.
Use of glycolic acid can make your skin more sensitive to sunlight.
