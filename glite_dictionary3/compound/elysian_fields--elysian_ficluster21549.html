Elysian Fields

noun
paradise in Greek mythology for the blessed dead
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪʒən ˈfildz / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪz.i.ən ˈfiːldz / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the dwelling place of the blessed after death; a paradise reserved for heroes and the virtuous.

Examples

the Elysian Fields of heroes
In ancient stories, heroes who pleased the gods were granted eternal rest in the Elysian Fields.
Poets often describe the Elysian Fields as a realm of perfect happiness and peace.
The legend says only the most virtuous souls could enter the Elysian Fields after death.
