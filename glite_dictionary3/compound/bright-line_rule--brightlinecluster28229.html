bright-line rule

noun
clear, unambiguous rule or standard
Pronunciation: / braɪt laɪn ruːl / (AmE), / braɪt laɪn ruːl / (BrE)
a clearly defined and unambiguous rule or standard that provides clear guidance in decision-making, especially in legal or professional contexts

Examples

a bright-line rule in law
Courts often prefer a bright-line rule over subjective standards in contentious cases.
