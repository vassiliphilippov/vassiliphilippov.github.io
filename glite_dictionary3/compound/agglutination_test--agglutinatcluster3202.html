agglutination test

noun
blood test detecting antigens via agglutination
Pronunciation: / əˌɡluːtəˈneɪʃən tɛst / (AmE), / əˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən tɛst / (BrE)
a blood test in which blood is mixed with a known antibody to detect specific antigens by observing agglutination, commonly used in tissue matching, blood grouping, and diagnosing infections

Examples

agglutination test for blood type
An agglutination test can quickly reveal a person's blood type before a transfusion.
Researchers often use the agglutination test in the diagnosis of various infectious diseases.
The doctor ordered an agglutination test to help determine the cause of the patient's fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym – agglutination: the clumping of particles, especially as occurs in blood testing
  • synonym – blood test: a scientific test of a sample of blood
