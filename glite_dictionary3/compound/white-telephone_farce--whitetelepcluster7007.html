white-telephone farce

noun
light comedy genre in Italian film/theater
Pronunciation: / waɪt tɛləˌfoʊn fɑrs / (AmE), / waɪt tɛlɪˌfəʊn fɑːs / (BrE)
a genre of light, sophisticated comedy, especially in Italian film of the 1930s and 1940s, featuring upper-class settings and decor, often characterized by the presence of white telephones as a symbol of luxury
