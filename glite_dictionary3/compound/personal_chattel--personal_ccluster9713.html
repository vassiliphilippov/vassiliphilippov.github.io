personal chattel

noun
movable personal property
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrsənl ˈtʃætəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənl ˈtʃætəl / (BrE)
A piece of tangible movable property that is not fixed to land; personal property that can be physically moved, such as furniture, vehicles, or livestock.

Examples

personal chattel like furniture
A car is generally considered a personal chattel rather than real property.
Auction houses frequently deal with personal chattel such as antique furniture and jewelry.
Under the will, all personal chattel must be sold to pay the debts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – real property: land and anything attached to it; immovable property
