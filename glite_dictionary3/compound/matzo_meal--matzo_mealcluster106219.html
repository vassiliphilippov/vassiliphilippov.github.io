matzo meal

noun
coarse flour from ground matzos
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːtsoʊ ˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈmæt.səʊ ˌmiːl / (BrE)
a coarse flour made by grinding matzos, used especially in Jewish cooking for making dishes like matzo balls or as a coating

Examples

matzo meal for matzo balls
For Passover, we used matzo meal instead of regular breadcrumbs.
She mixed eggs with matzo meal to make traditional matzo balls.
The recipe calls for one cup of matzo meal as a thickener.
