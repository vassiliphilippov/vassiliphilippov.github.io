litany of reasons

noun
a lengthy list of reasons
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtəni əv ˈrizənz / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəni əv ˈriːzənz / (BrE)
a long and often repetitive list of reasons, especially given as justifications or complaints

Examples

litany of reasons for refusing
During the meeting, he read out a litany of reasons for declining the proposal.
She gave me a litany of reasons why she couldn't attend the party.
The manager offered a litany of reasons to explain the project's delay.
