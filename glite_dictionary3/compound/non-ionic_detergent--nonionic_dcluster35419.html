non-ionic detergent

noun
synthetic detergent not ionizing in water
Pronunciation: / nɑn ˈaɪ.ɑn.ɪk dɪˈtɝ.dʒənt / (AmE), / nɒn ˈaɪ.ɒn.ɪk dɪˈtɜː.dʒənt / (BrE)
a synthetic detergent whose molecules do not ionize when dissolved in water, used in various cleaning and industrial applications

Examples

mild non-ionic detergent
A non-ionic detergent is preferred in laboratories because it is less likely to interfere with biochemical assays.
Industrial cleaners often contain non-ionic detergent to avoid the formation of soap scum in hard water.
Many household products now use non-ionic detergents for their effective cleaning and low foaming properties.
