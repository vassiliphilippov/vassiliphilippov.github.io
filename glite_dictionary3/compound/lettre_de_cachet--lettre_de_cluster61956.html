lettre de cachet

noun
royal French warrant for imprisonment or punishment
Pronunciation: / lɛtr də kɑːˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / lɛtr də kaˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
(historical) A letter signed by the French king, authorizing imprisonment or punishment of an individual without trial.

Examples

a lettre de cachet from the king
Many revolutionaries protested against abuses of power like the infamous lettre de cachet.
The king issued a lettre de cachet ordering the noble’s immediate imprisonment.
Under the old regime, a lettre de cachet could ruin a family’s reputation overnight.
