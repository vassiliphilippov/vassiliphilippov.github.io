unsifted soil

noun
soil not screened for debris
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɪftɪd sɔɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɪftɪd sɔɪl / (BrE)
soil that has not been passed through a sieve to remove debris or large particles

Examples

unsifted soil for planting
Some seeds struggle to grow well when planted in unsifted soil due to poor aeration.
The gardener prefers to remove clumps and stones from unsifted soil before filling pots.
You shouldn't use unsifted soil for planting flowers because it may contain rocks or debris.
