invert soap

noun
synthetic detergent with cationic surfactant
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɝt soʊp / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːt səʊp / (BrE)
a class of synthetic detergents in which the surface-active part of the molecule is the cation, rather than the usual anion

Examples

invert soap detergent type
Cationic detergents, also known as invert soaps, are widely used as fabric softeners.
Technicians analyzed the cleaning efficiency of several invert soaps in laboratory conditions.
Unlike regular soaps, invert soap has a positively charged active group.
