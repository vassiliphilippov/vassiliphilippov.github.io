folk etymology

noun
popular but mistaken word origin
Pronunciation: / foʊk ˈɛtɪˌmɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈɛtɪˌmɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a popularly held but incorrect account of the origin of a word or phrase, especially when the word is modified to resemble more familiar words

Examples

classic folk etymology story
A classic folk etymology is the transformation of 'asparagus' into 'sparrow grass'.
Many words have changed form due to folk etymology over centuries of use.
The common belief that 'cockroach' comes from 'cock' and 'roach' is actually a folk etymology.
