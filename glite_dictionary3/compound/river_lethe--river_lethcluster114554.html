River Lethe

proper noun
mythological river of forgetfulness in Hades
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvər ˈliːθi / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə ˈliːθi / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a river in Hades whose waters caused those who drank from it to forget their earthly lives.

Examples

drink from the River Lethe
According to legend, the souls of the dead drank from the River Lethe to lose all memories of their previous lives.
In stories about the afterlife, the River Lethe is often depicted as a boundary between mortal life and oblivion.
The poet referenced the River Lethe to symbolize the act of forgetting painful memories.
