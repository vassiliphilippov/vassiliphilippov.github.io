over the counter security

noun
security not traded on a formal exchange
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚ ðə ˈkaʊntɚ sɪˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈkaʊntə sɪˈkjʊərɪti / (BrE)
A security, such as a stock or bond, that is traded directly between parties, typically via a dealer network, rather than on a formal exchange.

Examples

buy an over the counter security
Investors should research the risks before buying over the counter security offerings.
Many small companies raise capital by issuing over the counter securities rather than seeking a stock exchange listing.
Unlike listed shares, over the counter securities are traded through networks of dealers.
