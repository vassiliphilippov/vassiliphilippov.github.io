self-rising flour

noun
flour mixed with leavening and salt
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈraɪzɪŋ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌselfˈraɪzɪŋ ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
flour that has salt and a leavening agent, such as baking powder, already mixed in, used for baking to help dough rise without additional ingredients

Examples

self-rising flour for baking
If you don't have self-rising flour, you can make your own by adding baking powder and salt to plain flour.
Many American recipes call for self-rising flour instead of plain flour and baking powder.
You can use self-rising flour to save time when baking biscuits.

Related Terms

  • antonym – plain flour: flour that does not contain salt or leavening agents
