premature labor

noun
early labor before 37 weeks of pregnancy
Pronunciation: / ˌpriməˈtʃʊr ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈtjʊə ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the onset of labor before the 37th week of pregnancy, potentially leading to preterm birth

Examples

premature labor at 34 weeks
She was admitted to the hospital after going into premature labor at 34 weeks.
Signs of premature labor include regular contractions and lower back pain before the 37th week.
The doctor explained that premature labor could pose risks to both mother and baby.
