collective farm

noun
farm operated collectively, esp. in Communist countries
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪv fɑrm / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv fɑːm / (BrE)
A large farm or group of farms organized as a single unit and operated collectively by a group of workers under government direction or ownership, especially as practiced in former Communist countries.

Examples

collective farm workers
Life on the collective farm was strictly regulated by state officials.
She grew up on a collective farm in the Soviet Union.
The government forced many peasants to join a collective farm during the 1930s.

Related Terms

  • synonym – state farm: a government-owned farm run by salaried workers, especially in former Communist countries
