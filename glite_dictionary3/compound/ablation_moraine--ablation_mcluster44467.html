ablation moraine

noun
rock debris layer on melting glacier ice
Pronunciation: / əˈbleɪʃən məˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈbleɪʃ(ə)n məˈreɪn / (BrE)
A layer of rock debris resting on the surface of glacier ice, formed as the underlying ice melts or ablates away.

Examples

ablation moraine covers glacier
Scientists studied the ablation moraine to understand the retreat of the ice sheet.
The ablation moraine featured a thick cover of rocks that insulated the glacier below.
When the glacier melted, the ablation moraine was left as a distinctive ridge on the landscape.
