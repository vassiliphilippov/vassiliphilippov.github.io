grand larceny

noun
theft of property above a certain value
Pronunciation: / ɡrænd ˈlɑrsəni / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈlɑːsəni / (BrE)
the theft of property of a value greater than a legally specified amount, constituting a more serious crime than petty larceny

Examples

grand larceny charge
He was charged with grand larceny after stealing jewelry worth over $10,000.
The court found her guilty of grand larceny because the stolen items exceeded the minimum threshold.
Under state law, grand larceny is a felony and may result in a lengthy prison sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – petty larceny: theft of property of low monetary value
