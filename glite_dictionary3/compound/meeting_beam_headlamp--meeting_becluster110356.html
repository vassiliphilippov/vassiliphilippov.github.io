meeting beam headlamp

noun
vehicle headlamp setting for low-beam use
Pronunciation: / ˈmitɪŋ biːm ˈhɛdˌlæmp / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪŋ biːm ˈhɛdˌlamp / (BrE)
A car headlamp setting that provides sufficient lighting without dazzling other drivers, used when other vehicles are present.

Examples

meeting beam headlamp on a car
European cars are required to have a properly aligned meeting beam headlamp for safety.
The meeting beam headlamp must be used whenever there is oncoming traffic.
When approaching another car at night, switch your headlights to the meeting beam headlamp setting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dipped-beam headlamp: a headlamp setting that directs light downward to avoid glare for other road users
  • antonym – high-beam headlamp: a headlamp setting with a higher and more forward-directed beam, used without oncoming traffic
