eclectic method

noun
method choosing best from various sources
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklɛktɪk ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪk ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a way of making decisions or choosing styles by selecting what seems best from various sources, rather than following one doctrine or style

Examples

an eclectic method of teaching
By adopting an eclectic method, the designer incorporated elements from both modern and traditional art.
Many therapists now favor an eclectic method rather than sticking to a single therapeutic approach.
The teacher used an eclectic method to blend different teaching styles for her diverse classroom.
