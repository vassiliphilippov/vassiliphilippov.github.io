profit margin

noun
percentage measuring profitability
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑfɪt ˈmɑrdʒɪn /; / ˈprɑː.fɪt ˌmɑːr.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt ˈmɑːdʒɪn /; / ˈprɒf.ɪt ˌmɑː.dʒɪn / (BrE)
the difference between the cost to produce or buy something and its selling price, usually shown as a percentage, used to measure how profitable a product or business is

Examples

profit margin increases
Even small changes in production costs can significantly affect the profit margin of a product.
Retailers must closely monitor their profit margin to ensure long-term viability.
The company's profit margin increased last quarter due to higher sales and reduced costs.
Rising raw material prices can narrow a manufacturer's profit margin significantly.
Small businesses often struggle to maintain a healthy profit margin in competitive markets.
The company's profit margin improved after they reduced operational costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gross margin: the difference between sales and cost of goods sold, not accounting for other expenses
  • synonym – net margin: the percentage of revenue left after all expenses have been deducted
  • synonym – net profit: actual profit after all expenses have been deducted from revenue
