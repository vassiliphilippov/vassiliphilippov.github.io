stinking yew

noun
evergreen shrub with foul-smelling leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ juː / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ juː / (BrE)
a rare small evergreen shrub of northern Florida, known for its glossy green leaves that emit a foul smell when crushed

Examples

a stinking yew shrub

Gardeners warn that the stinking yew can produce an unpleasant odor if its leaves are touched.
The stinking yew is found mainly in the shaded swamps of northern Florida.
