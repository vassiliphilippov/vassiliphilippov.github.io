ketamine hydrochloride

noun
anesthetic and tranquilizer drug (medical, sometimes illicit use)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛtəˌmin haɪˌdrɑːkləˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɛtəmiːn haɪˌdrɒkləˈraɪd / (BrE)
a general anesthetic and tranquilizer used in medicine and veterinary practice, sometimes misused as a hallucinogenic drug

Examples

ketamine hydrochloride for anesthesia
Illicit use of ketamine hydrochloride can lead to severe dissociation and hallucinations.
In pediatric medicine, ketamine hydrochloride is sometimes used for minor surgical procedures due to its safety profile.
The veterinarian administered ketamine hydrochloride to sedate the animal before surgery.
