rima glottidis

noun
space between true vocal folds
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪmə ɡlɒˈtaɪdɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːmə ɡlɒˈtɪdɪs / (BrE)
the space between the two true vocal folds in the larynx, through which air passes during breathing and speech

Examples

open rima glottidis
Anesthesia can sometimes affect the muscles controlling the rima glottidis.
In cases of laryngeal edema, the rima glottidis may become dangerously constricted.
The rima glottidis narrows during phonation to help produce sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glottis: the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them
