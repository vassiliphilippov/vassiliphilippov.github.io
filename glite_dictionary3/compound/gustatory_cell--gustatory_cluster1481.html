gustatory cell

noun
cell in a taste bud that senses taste
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌstəˌtɔri sɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌstət(ə)ri sɛl / (BrE)
An epithelial cell in a taste bud that detects chemicals dissolved in saliva and stimulates sensory nerve fibers involved in the sense of taste.

Examples

gustatory cell in taste bud
Damage to the gustatory cells can result in a diminished ability to detect certain tastes.
Scientists studied the structure of gustatory cells to better understand how taste is perceived.
The tongue is covered in taste buds, each containing several gustatory cells that respond to different flavors.
