High Renaissance

noun
Italian art style, early 16th century
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈrɛnəˌsɑns / (AmE), / haɪ ˈrɛnɪˌsɑːns / (BrE)
the style and period of art, especially painting and sculpture, in early 16th-century Italy, marked by technical mastery, balanced composition, and humanistic subject matter, chiefly associated with artists like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael

Examples

the High Renaissance style
Art historians often cite the High Renaissance as a period of exceptional creativity and innovation.
Leonardo da Vinci's "The Last Supper" is a quintessential example of High Renaissance painting.
The High Renaissance produced some of the most celebrated masterpieces in Western art.
