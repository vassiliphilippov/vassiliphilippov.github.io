freedom of assembly

noun
the right to gather peacefully
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdəm əv əˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm əv əˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
the right of people to gather together peacefully and express their views, especially to protest or petition the government

Examples

freedom of assembly protest rights
Restrictions on the freedom of assembly sparked criticism from international human rights groups.
The constitution guarantees the freedom of assembly as a basic civil right.
The protestors exercised their freedom of assembly by gathering peacefully in the city square.
