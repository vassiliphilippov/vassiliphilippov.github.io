investment trust

noun
company investing pooled funds in securities
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt trʌst / (BrE)
a company that raises money by selling shares to investors and invests the pooled funds in a diversified portfolio of securities issued by other companies

Examples

investment trust shares
Many people choose an investment trust to gain access to a broad range of global markets.
She invested her savings in an investment trust for long-term growth and diversification.
The investment trust reported strong returns this year due to its technology holdings.
