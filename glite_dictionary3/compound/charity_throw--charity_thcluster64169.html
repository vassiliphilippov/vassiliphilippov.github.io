charity throw

noun
unhindered basketball shot from the foul line
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃærəti θroʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃærəti θrəʊ / (BrE)
an unhindered shot made from the foul line in basketball, awarded to a player after a foul by the opposing team; also called 'free throw'.

Examples

charity throw in basketball
After being fouled, he calmly sank both charity throws to tie the game.
Missing a charity throw in the final seconds cost them the championship.
She practiced her charity throw routine until she could make ten in a row.
