navigational system

noun
system for determining position and course
Pronunciation: / næˌvɪɡeɪʃənəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / nævɪˌɡeɪʃənəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system that supplies information needed to determine the position and course of a ship or aircraft.

Examples

navigational system in an airplane
The aircraft relies on a sophisticated navigational system to ensure it stays on course.
The pilot checked the navigational system before taking off in poor weather conditions.
Upgrading the ship's navigational system improved safety during long ocean crossings.
