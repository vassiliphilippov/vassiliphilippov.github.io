fagot stitch

noun
needlework stitch joining parallel threads
Pronunciation: / ˈfæɡət stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfæɡət stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A decorative sewing stitch used to join two pieces of fabric with openwork by tying together groups of parallel threads, commonly used in embroidery and fagoting.

Examples

fagot stitch embroidery border
Learning the fagot stitch is essential for anyone interested in traditional embroidery techniques.
The seamstress used a fagot stitch to create a delicate border on the dress sleeves.
You can enhance table linens by joining panels with a fagot stitch for an elegant, lacy effect.
