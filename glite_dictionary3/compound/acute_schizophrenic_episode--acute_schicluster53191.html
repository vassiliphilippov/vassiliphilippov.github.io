acute schizophrenic episode

noun
brief, sudden episode of schizophrenia
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut skɪtsoʊˈfrɛnɪk ɛˈpoʊd / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt skɪtsəʊˈfrɛnɪk ɪˈpəʊd / (BrE)
a period of schizophrenia that begins abruptly and lasts for a relatively short time, typically a few weeks or months

Examples

an acute schizophrenic episode begins suddenly
Her symptoms improved considerably after her first acute schizophrenic episode.
Rapid intervention is crucial during an acute schizophrenic episode to ensure patient safety.
The patient was hospitalized following an acute schizophrenic episode that lasted three weeks.
