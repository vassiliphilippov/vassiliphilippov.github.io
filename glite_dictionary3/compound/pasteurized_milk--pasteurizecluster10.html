pasteurized milk

noun
milk treated by heating to kill germs
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪstʃəˌraɪzd mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstʃəraɪzd mɪlk / (BrE)
Milk that has been heated to a specific temperature for a set period to kill harmful microorganisms and prevent spoilage.

Examples

pasteurized milk in bottles
Many consumers prefer pasteurized milk for its safety and longer shelf life.
The law requires that all milk sold in stores must be pasteurized milk.
Unlike raw milk, pasteurized milk has been treated to kill bacteria.
