political liberty

noun
freedom to exercise one's legal rights
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
the freedom of individuals to exercise their rights and participate in the governance of their country as guaranteed by law

Examples

enjoy political liberty
Education is essential for people to fully enjoy their political liberty.
Many activists fought for political liberty during the period of authoritarian rule.
The constitution was written to protect the political liberty of every citizen.

Related Terms

  • synonym – civil liberty: the state of being subject only to laws established for the good of the community, especially with regard to freedom of action and speech
  • antonym – oppression: prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or exercise of authority
