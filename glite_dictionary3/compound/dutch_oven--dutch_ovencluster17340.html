Dutch oven

noun
heavy lidded cooking pot
Pronunciation: / dʌtʃ ˈʌvən / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈʌvən /; / dʌtʃ ˈʌv.ən / (BrE)
a heavy, thick-walled cooking pot with a tight-fitting lid, usually made of cast iron or earthenware, used for slow-cooked dishes

Examples

cast-iron Dutch oven
My grandmother passed down her cast-iron Dutch oven to me.
The Dutch oven is perfect for stews that require long, slow cooking.
We baked fresh bread in a Dutch oven over the campfire.
After hours of slow roasting, the chicken in the Dutch oven was perfectly tender and juicy.
Many campers use a cast-iron Dutch oven for outdoor cooking because it heats evenly.
The chef prepared a delicious beef stew in the Dutch oven over a low fire.
