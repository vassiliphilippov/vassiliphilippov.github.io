tower block

noun
tall apartment or office building (British)
Pronunciation: / ˈtaʊ.ɚ ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə ˌblɒk / (BrE)
A tall building divided into separate apartments or offices on different floors; a high-rise building, chiefly used in British English.

Examples

high-rise tower block
Several tower blocks were built in the city center during the 1960s.
The tower block offers impressive views over the river from the upper floors.
Their family moved into a modern tower block on the outskirts of London.

Related Terms

  • synonym – high-rise: a tall building with many stories, especially used for apartments or offices
