colour bar

noun
social system enforcing racial segregation
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər bɑr / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə bɑː / (BrE)
a social system or set of practices that prevents people of different races, especially black people, from participating equally in activities or opportunities, particularly with white people

Examples

end the colour bar
After decades of struggle, the colour bar in employment was finally dismantled.
Many sports clubs maintained a colour bar until the mid-twentieth century.
The government strictly enforced the colour bar in schools and public places.
