coral root

noun
a leafless wildflower of the genus Corallorhiza
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.əl ˌrut / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əl ˌruːt / (BrE)
Any of various leafless wildflowers of the genus Corallorhiza that grow from a hard mass of rhizomes, often dependent on fungi for nutrient absorption.

Examples

wild coral root flower
The rare coral root was discovered growing under the pine trees in the forest preserve.
Unlike most plants, the coral root does not rely on photosynthesis but instead absorbs nutrients with the help of fungi.
Wildlife photographers are often pleased to find a patch of blooming coral roots in the woods.
