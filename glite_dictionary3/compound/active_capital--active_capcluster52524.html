active capital

noun
readily convertible money or property
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv ˈkapɪtəl / (BrE)
money or property that can readily be converted into cash or used in transactions, as opposed to fixed or illiquid assets.

Examples

active capital for investment
Banks prefer to hold more active capital to ensure liquidity during uncertain times.
Much of the firm’s active capital was deployed in short-term market instruments.
The company increased its active capital to invest in new opportunities.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fixed capital: capital invested in long-term assets not easily converted to cash
