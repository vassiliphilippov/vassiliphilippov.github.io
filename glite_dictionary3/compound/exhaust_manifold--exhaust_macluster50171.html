exhaust manifold

noun
engine part for collecting exhaust gases
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɔst ˈmænɪˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːst ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
a pipe or chamber that collects exhaust gases from an engine's cylinders and channels them to the exhaust pipe

Examples

exhaust manifold leak repair
A damaged exhaust manifold can severely reduce an engine's performance.
The mechanic replaced the cracked exhaust manifold to stop fumes from leaking into the engine compartment.
Upgrading to a custom exhaust manifold can improve your car's horsepower.
