family Labiatae

noun
botanical family including mint, thyme, sage, rosemary
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli læˈbaɪəˌtiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli læˈbɪətiː / (BrE)
a large family of aromatic herbs and shrubs, including mint, thyme, sage, and rosemary, with characteristic two-lipped flowers and four-lobed ovaries, botanically known as Lamiaceae

Examples

family Labiatae includes mint

Many culinary herbs, such as mint and basil, belong to the family Labiatae.
The characteristic flowers of the family Labiatae are two-lipped and highly aromatic.
