projective test

noun
personality test using ambiguous stimuli
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛktɪv tɛst / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪv tɛst / (BrE)
a personality assessment in which a person responds freely to ambiguous stimuli, revealing hidden emotions and motives

Examples

projective test with inkblots
Many therapists use the Rorschach inkblot as a classic projective test.
Some experts question the reliability of a projective test compared to more structured assessments.
The psychologist administered a projective test to better understand the child's emotional state.
