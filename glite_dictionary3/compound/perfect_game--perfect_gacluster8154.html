perfect game

noun
baseball: no batter reaches base in a game
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.fɪkt ˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt ˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher does not allow any opposing player to reach base for the entire game.

Examples

pitch a perfect game
Achieving a perfect game is one of the rarest feats in professional baseball.
Fans will remember his perfect game as the highlight of the season.
