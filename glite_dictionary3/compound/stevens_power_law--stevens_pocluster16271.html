Stevens' power law

noun
principle on perceived stimulus intensity
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːvənz ˈpaʊ.ɚ lɔ / (AmE), / ˈstiːvənz ˈpaʊ.ə lɔː / (BrE)
a principle in psychophysics stating that the perceived intensity of a stimulus grows as a power function of the actual stimulus intensity

Examples

Stevens' power law in perception studies
In research on pain perception, Stevens' power law often provides a better fit than alternative models.
