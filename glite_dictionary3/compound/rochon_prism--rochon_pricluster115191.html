Rochon prism

noun
optical device for polarizing light
Pronunciation: / roʊˈʃɒn ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / rəʊˈʃɒn ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
An optical device consisting of two birefringent crystals cemented together, used to produce plane-polarized ultraviolet or visible light.

Examples

Rochon prism polarizes light
A Rochon prism is commonly used in scientific laboratories to polarize ultraviolet light.
The technician adjusted the Rochon prism to achieve the desired level of polarization.
Unlike other polarizers, the Rochon prism transmits the ordinary ray undeviated while deviating the extraordinary ray.
