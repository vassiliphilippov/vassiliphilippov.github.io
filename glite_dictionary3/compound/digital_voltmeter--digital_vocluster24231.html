digital voltmeter

noun
device measuring voltage, gives digital readout
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪdʒɪt̬əl ˈvoʊltˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəl ˈvəʊltˌmiːtə / (BrE)
An electronic instrument that measures voltage and displays the reading as numerical digits.

Examples

use a digital voltmeter in lab
Modern laboratories often rely on a digital voltmeter for accurate and easy-to-read measurements.
The technician used a digital voltmeter to check the electrical circuit's voltage.
Unlike analog models, a digital voltmeter displays the result as clear numerical values.

Related Terms

  • antonym – analog voltmeter: a voltmeter that uses a moving pointer to display voltage
