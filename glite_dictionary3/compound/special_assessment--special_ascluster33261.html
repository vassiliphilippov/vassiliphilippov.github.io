special assessment

noun
additional tax for public improvements
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl əˈsɛsmənt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl əˈsɛsmənt / (BrE)
an additional tax imposed on private property by a government, typically to pay for public improvements that increase the property's value

Examples

special assessment for road repairs
A special assessment can significantly increase your annual property tax bill.
Homeowners were surprised by the sudden special assessment for road improvements.
The city issued a special assessment to fund new sidewalks in our neighborhood.
