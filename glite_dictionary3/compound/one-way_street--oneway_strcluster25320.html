one-way street

noun
unilateral situation or relationship
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌn ˈweɪ ˌstrit / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ˈweɪ ˌstriːt / (BrE)
a situation or relationship in which only one side acts or benefits, without reciprocity from the other side

Examples

one-way street relationship
Good communication should not be a one-way street; both sides need to share their thoughts.
He realized too late their friendship had become a one-way street, with him always making the effort.
Trust is a one-way street in this company, as employees can't question management.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reciprocal: a situation in which both sides act or benefit equally
