rhomboideus major muscle

noun
upper back muscle moving scapula
Pronunciation: / ɹɑm.bɔɪˈdiː.əs ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ɹɒm.bɔɪˈdiː.əs ˈmeɪ.dʒə ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a muscle of the upper back that connects the scapula with the vertebral column and draws the scapula toward the spine

Examples

rhomboideus major muscle pain
An injury to the rhomboideus major muscle can cause pain between the shoulder blades.
Physical therapists often focus on strengthening the rhomboideus major muscle to improve posture.
The rhomboideus major muscle helps retract the shoulder blades during rowing exercises.

Related Terms

  • synonym – trapezius muscle: large back muscle that moves the scapula and supports the arm
