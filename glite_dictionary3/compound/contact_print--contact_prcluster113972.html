contact print

noun
photographic print made by direct contact with a negative
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.tækt prɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tækt prɪnt / (BrE)
a photographic print made by exposing a photosensitive surface to direct contact with a photographic negative, producing an image the same size as the negative

Examples

make a contact print
Making a contact print is an important step in the darkroom process for film photographers.
She examined each contact print to choose which photographs to enlarge.
The negatives were arranged on the paper to create a single contact print of all the images.

Related Terms

  • antonym – enlargement: a photographic print larger than the negative, made using a projector
