white dwarf star

noun
very dense, faint stellar remnant
Pronunciation: / waɪt dwɔrf stɑr / (AmE), / waɪt dwɔːf stɑː / (BrE)
A small, very dense, faint star that is the remnant core of a medium or low-mass star after it has exhausted its nuclear fuel.

Examples

a white dwarf star forms
Astronomers have discovered a white dwarf star orbiting very close to a Sun-like star.
Over billions of years, a white dwarf star slowly cools and fades away.
The closest known white dwarf star to Earth is Sirius B.

Related Terms

  • antonym – red giant: a large, bright, cool star in a late phase of stellar evolution
