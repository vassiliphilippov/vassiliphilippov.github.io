electromotive force series

noun
order of metals by electrode potential
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmoʊtɪv fɔrs ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈməʊtɪv fɔːs ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
a sequence of metals or ions arranged by their standard electrode potentials, showing each metal's tendency to reduce ions of other metals lower in the series

Examples

electromotive force series of metals
By referencing the electromotive force series, chemists can determine whether a reaction will be spontaneous.
The electromotive force series is essential for predicting which metals will corrode in a given environment.
Zinc is placed above copper in the electromotive force series, so zinc can displace copper ions from solution.
