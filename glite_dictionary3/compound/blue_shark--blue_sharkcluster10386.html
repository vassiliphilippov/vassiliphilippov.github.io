blue shark

noun
species of slender, blue ocean shark
Pronunciation: / blu ˈʃɑrk / (AmE), / bluː ˈʃɑːk / (BrE)
a slender, fast-swimming shark (Prionace glauca) with a blue back and white underside, found in oceans worldwide and considered potentially dangerous to humans

Examples

a blue shark swimming
A blue shark was spotted swimming near the harbor by a group of marine biologists.
Fishermen sometimes encounter the blue shark during offshore expeditions.
The blue shark is easily identified by its distinct coloration and slender body.
