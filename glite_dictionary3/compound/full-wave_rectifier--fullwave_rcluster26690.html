full-wave rectifier

noun
device converting AC to DC on both cycles
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊl weɪv rɛkˈtɪfaɪər / (AmE), / ˌfʊl weɪv rɛkˈtɪfaɪə / (BrE)
A device that converts both halves of an alternating current waveform into direct current, producing a positive output for both cycles.
