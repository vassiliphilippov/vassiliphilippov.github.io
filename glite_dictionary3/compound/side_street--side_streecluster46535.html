side street

noun
a minor street intersecting a main street
Pronunciation: / saɪd strit / (AmE), / saɪd striːt / (BrE)
A minor street that intersects with a main street and usually ends there, rather than continuing through.

Examples

quiet side street
A charming café was tucked away on a little side street near the city center.
Parking is usually easier to find on a side street than on the main road.
They walked down a quiet side street to avoid the heavy traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym – main street: the principal street of a town, typically where most businesses are located
