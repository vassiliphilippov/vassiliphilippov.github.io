judgement by default

noun
legal ruling for plaintiff when defendant defaults
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt baɪ dɪˈfɔlt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt baɪ dɪˈfɔːlt / (BrE)
A legal judgment entered in favor of the plaintiff when the defendant fails to appear in court or otherwise defaults.

Examples

judgement by default in court
Filing for a judgement by default is common when the other side does not contest the case.
Since the defendant failed to respond to the lawsuit, the court issued a judgement by default in favor of the plaintiff.
The company received a judgement by default after the opposing party did not appear in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym – default judgment: a judgment rendered because of the defendant's failure to answer or appear
