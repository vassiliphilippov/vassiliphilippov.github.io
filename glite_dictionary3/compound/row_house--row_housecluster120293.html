row house

noun
one of a series of adjoining houses sharing walls
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house that is one of a series of identical or similar houses situated side by side and sharing common walls.

Examples

old row house
Each row house on the block featured the same red brick facade and ornate doorway.
The row house had a narrow backyard but plenty of space inside for her family.
They decided to buy a row house in the heart of the city for easy access to work.

Related Terms

  • antonym – detached house: a stand-alone house not joined to another
  • synonym – townhouse: a tall, narrow, traditional terraced house, generally having three or more floors
