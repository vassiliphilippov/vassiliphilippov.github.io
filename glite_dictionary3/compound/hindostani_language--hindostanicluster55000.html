Hindostani language

noun
language form spoken around Delhi
Pronunciation: / hɪnˌdoʊˈstɑni ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / hɪnˌdɒˈstɑːni ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a form of Hindi spoken primarily around Delhi and in northern India, often considered a predecessor to both standard Hindi and Urdu.

Examples

the Hindostani language region
Before the formalization of Hindi and Urdu, people spoke a variant known as the Hindostani language.
Many classic songs and poems were originally composed in Hindostani language.
The Hindostani language served as the linguistic bridge between North India's diverse communities.
