saddle horn

noun
high front part of a Western saddle
Pronunciation: / ˈsædəl hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈsædəl hɔːn / (BrE)
the high, rounded part at the front of a Western saddle, often covered with leather, used especially for securing a rope

Examples

saddle horn on a Western saddle
A sturdy saddle horn is essential for roping cattle on a ranch.
She gripped the saddle horn tightly as the horse began to run.
The cowboy wrapped the lasso around the saddle horn to control the calf.
