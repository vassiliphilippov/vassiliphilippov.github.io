condenser mike

noun
type of sensitive microphone
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛnsɚ maɪk / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə maɪk / (BrE)
A microphone that uses a capacitor with one fixed plate and one movable diaphragm, sensitive to sound waves.

Examples

condenser mike for recording
Many podcasters prefer a condenser mike for capturing clear and detailed sound.
The studio engineer recommended using a condenser mike for recording vocals because of its superior sensitivity.
You'll need a power source, such as phantom power, to operate a condenser mike.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dynamic microphone: a microphone that converts sound to electrical signals using electromagnetic induction
