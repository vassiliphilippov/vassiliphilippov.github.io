orthotropous ovule

noun
a straight ovule with micropyle at apex
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθəˈtroʊpəs ˈoʊvjul / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈtrəʊpəs ˈəʊvjuːl / (BrE)
an ovule that is completely straight with the micropyle located at its apex, characteristic of certain plants

Examples

orthotropous ovule in plants
In some primitive plant species, the orthotropous ovule structure is commonly found.
The orchid produces a single orthotropous ovule in each of its ovary compartments.
Unlike anatropous ovules, a orthotropous ovule retains a straight alignment from base to tip.
