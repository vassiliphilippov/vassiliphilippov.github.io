focal seizure

noun
localized seizure affecting part of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.kəl ˈsiː.ʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kəl ˈsiː.ʒə / (BrE)
a seizure involving a temporary disturbance in motor or sensory function due to abnormal activity in a specific area of the brain

Examples

focal seizure with arm twitching
Recent research has improved treatments for people suffering from focal seizures with unusual sensory symptoms.
She experienced a focal seizure that caused twitching in her right arm but did not lose consciousness.
The doctor explained that a focal seizure typically starts in just one part of the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – generalized seizure: a seizure that affects both sides of the brain
  • synonym – partial seizure: another term for a seizure originating in a local area of the brain
