tine weeder

noun
spring-tined weeding tool for crops
Pronunciation: / taɪn ˈwiːdər / (AmE), / taɪn ˈwiːdə / (BrE)
an agricultural implement consisting of fine spring tines on a flexible frame, used to weed crops without damaging them

Examples

use a tine weeder in fields
The farmer attached a tine weeder to his tractor to remove weeds from the young wheat.
They invested in a new tine weeder to improve the efficiency of their organic farm.
Using a tine weeder helps aerate the soil and control unwanted plants without chemicals.
