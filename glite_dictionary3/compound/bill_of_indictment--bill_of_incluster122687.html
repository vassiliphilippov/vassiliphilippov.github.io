bill of indictment

noun
formal legal charge document
Pronunciation: / bɪl əv ɪnˈdaɪtmənt / (AmE), / bɪl əv ɪnˈdaɪtmənt / (BrE)
a formal written document prepared by a prosecutor that officially charges someone with a criminal offence

Examples

a bill of indictment against a suspect
Her lawyer argued that the bill of indictment was based on insufficient evidence.
The grand jury issued a bill of indictment against the suspect for fraud.
Without a bill of indictment, the case could not proceed to trial.
