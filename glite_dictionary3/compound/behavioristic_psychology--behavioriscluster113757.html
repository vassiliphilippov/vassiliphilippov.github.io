behavioristic psychology

noun
approach emphasizing observable behavior in psychology
Pronunciation: / bɪˌheɪvjɚˈɪstɪk saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / bɪˌheɪvjəˈrɪstɪk saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
an approach to psychology that emphasizes the study of observable and measurable behavior rather than internal mental states

Examples

behavioristic psychology studies behavior
Behavioristic psychology focuses on actions that can be observed and measured, rather than internal mental processes.
Many early experiments in behavioristic psychology used animals to study the principles of learning.
The development of behavioristic psychology marked a shift away from introspective methods in the early 20th century.
