lauric acid

noun
a fatty acid in coconut oil and palm kernel oil
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a saturated fatty acid, found in coconut oil and palm kernel oil, used in making soaps and cosmetics

Examples

lauric acid in coconut oil
Coconut oil is especially rich in lauric acid, making it popular in soap-making.
Palm kernel oil contains high levels of lauric acid, which gives it its solid texture.
Studies have shown that lauric acid may have antimicrobial properties.
