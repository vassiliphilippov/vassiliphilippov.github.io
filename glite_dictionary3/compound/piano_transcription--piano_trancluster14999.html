piano transcription

noun
music arranged for piano from other sources
Pronunciation: / piˈænoʊ trænˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / pɪˈanəʊ trænsˈkrɪpʃən / (BrE)
An arrangement of music originally composed for other instruments or ensembles, rewritten for performance on the piano.

Examples

piano transcription of an orchestra piece
Liszt was famous for his virtuosic piano transcriptions of operatic works.
Many students find it helpful to study the piano transcription before learning the orchestral arrangement.
The composer published a piano transcription of the symphony so that pianists could perform it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – piano reduction: a simplified version of music for piano, especially for rehearsal
