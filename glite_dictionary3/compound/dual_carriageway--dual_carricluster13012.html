dual carriageway

noun
divided highway with lanes in each direction
Pronunciation: / ˈduː.əl ˈkær.ɪdʒ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.əl ˈkær.ɪdʒ.weɪ / (BrE)
A highway with two or more separate carriageways carrying traffic in opposite directions, divided by a central barrier or strip, chiefly in the UK.

Examples

busy dual carriageway
Accidents are less likely on a dual carriageway due to the central barrier.
The A1 was upgraded to a dual carriageway to improve safety and traffic flow.
The new dual carriageway reduced travel time between the cities considerably.
