graduated cylinder

noun
laboratory vessel for measuring liquids
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræd͡ʒuˌeɪtɪd ˈsɪlɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡrædjʊeɪtɪd ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
A tall, narrow container with marked lines used for accurately measuring the volume of liquids in laboratories.

Examples

Each student must wash their graduated cylinder thoroughly after the experiment.
The chemist poured the solution into a graduated cylinder to measure exactly 50 milliliters.
