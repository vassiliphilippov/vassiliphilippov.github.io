pump priming

noun
government spending to stimulate the economy
Pronunciation: / pʌmp ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / pʌmp ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the introduction of government money into the economy, especially through spending or investment, to encourage economic activity and growth

Examples

government pump priming
Government pump priming is often used during periods of high unemployment.
Some economists argue that pump priming can help economies recover from downturns more quickly.
The new administration enacted pump priming measures to counter the effects of the recession.

Related Terms

  • antonym – austerity: government policy of reducing spending to control deficit
  • synonym – stimulus: government action designed to encourage economic growth
