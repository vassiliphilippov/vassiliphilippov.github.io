nicotine poisoning

noun
toxic illness from too much nicotine
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪkətiːn ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɪkətiːn ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
a toxic condition resulting from ingesting or inhaling excessive amounts of nicotine, often causing symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and convulsions

Examples

accidental nicotine poisoning
Accidental ingestion of e-cigarette liquid can result in nicotine poisoning, especially in young children.
Paramedics treated the man for nicotine poisoning after he swallowed several nicotine gum tablets.
The symptoms of nicotine poisoning include dizziness, confusion, and sometimes seizures.
