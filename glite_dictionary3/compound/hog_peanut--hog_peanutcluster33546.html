hog peanut

noun
North American vine with edible underground pods
Pronunciation: / hɑɡ ˈpiː.nʌt / (AmE), / hɒɡ ˈpiː.nʌt / (BrE)
a North American vine (Amphicarpaea bracteata) with racemes of purple to maroon flowers and edible, usually underground pods resembling peanuts

Examples

hog peanut vine
The hog peanut produces underground pods that can be eaten raw or roasted.
The roots of the hog peanut were once an important food source for indigenous peoples.
We found a patch of hog peanut in the shaded woods near the lake.
