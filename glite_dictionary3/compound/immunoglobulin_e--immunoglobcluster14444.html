immunoglobulin E

noun
a class of antibody involved in allergies
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.ju.noʊˌɡlɑ.bjə.lɪn i / (AmE), / ˌɪm.juː.nəʊˌɡlɒb.jʊ.lɪn iː / (BrE)
A class of antibody found mainly in the skin and mucous membranes, involved in allergic reactions and immune responses to parasites.

Examples

immunoglobulin E allergy test
Doctors measured the patient's immunoglobulin E to help diagnose his symptoms.
Exposure to pollen increased her immunoglobulin E levels dramatically.
High levels of immunoglobulin E can indicate an allergic response.
