purulent pleurisy

noun
pus in pleural cavity (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈpjʊrələnt pluˈrɪzi / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərʊlənt ˈplʊərɪzi / (BrE)
A medical condition in which pus accumulates in the pleural cavity (the space between the lung and the chest wall), often causing inflammation and infection.

Examples

treat purulent pleurisy with antibiotics
Antibiotics and drainage are commonly required to treat purulent pleurisy.
The patient developed purulent pleurisy following a severe lung infection.
Untreated purulent pleurisy can lead to serious complications and even respiratory failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – empyema: collection of pus in a body cavity, especially the pleural cavity
