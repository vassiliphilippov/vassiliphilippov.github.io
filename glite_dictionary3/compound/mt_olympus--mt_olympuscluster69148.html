Mt. Olympus

proper noun
mountain in Greece, home of the gods in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / maʊnt əˈlɪmpəs / (AmE), / maʊnt əˈlɪmpəs / (BrE)
a mountain peak in northeastern Greece, traditionally regarded in ancient Greek mythology as the home of the gods; rises to 2,917 meters (9,570 feet) near the Aegean Sea

Examples

Mt. Olympus in Greek mythology
Ancient myths claim that Mt. Olympus was home to the gods such as Zeus and Hera.
Climbers from around the world travel to Greece to ascend Mt. Olympus each year.
We could see the snow-capped peak of Mt. Olympus from the coastal village.
