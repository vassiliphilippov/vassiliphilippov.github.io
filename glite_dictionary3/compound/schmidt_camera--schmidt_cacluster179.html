Schmidt camera

noun
wide-field reflecting telescope with correcting plate
Pronunciation: / ʃmɪt ˈkæmɚ / (AmE), / ʃmɪt ˈkamərə / (BrE)
a type of reflecting telescope with a correcting plate that allows a wide area of sky to be photographed without optical aberrations

Examples

Schmidt camera wide sky
A Schmidt camera corrects for optical aberrations, producing clear photographs over a large field of view.
Advancements in astronomy owe much to the invention of the Schmidt camera.
The observatory uses a Schmidt camera to capture wide-field images of the night sky.
