grey sage

noun
perennial western US plant with silvery leaves
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A low, much-branched perennial plant of the western United States with silvery leaves, valued as browse and shelter for wildlife.

Examples

grey sage plant
Many animals rely on the grey sage for shelter during harsh winters.
Ranchers often protect areas of grey sage because of its importance for grazing wildlife.
