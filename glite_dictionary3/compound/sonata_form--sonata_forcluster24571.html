sonata form

noun
musical structure with exposition, development, recapitulation
Pronunciation: / səˈnɑː.t̬ə fɔɹm / (AmE), / səˈnɑː.tə fɔːm / (BrE)
a musical structure consisting of three main sections—exposition, development, and recapitulation—commonly used in the first movements of sonatas, symphonies, and chamber works
