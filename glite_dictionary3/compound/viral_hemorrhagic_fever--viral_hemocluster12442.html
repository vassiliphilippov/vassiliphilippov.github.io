viral hemorrhagic fever

noun
serious illness involving viral infection and bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪɹəl ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪə.rəl ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
Any of a group of severe illnesses caused by viral infections, characterized by fever, bleeding, and damage to blood vessels, often found in specific geographic areas.

Outbreaks of viral hemorrhagic fever are often difficult to control in remote areas.
