fish fly

noun
a bait insect, smaller than the dobsonfly
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ flaɪ / (AmE), / fɪʃ flaɪ / (BrE)
An insect similar to but smaller than the dobsonfly, whose larvae are used as fishing bait.

Examples

fish fly larvae bait
In the summer, swarms of fish flies gather near the water's edge.
Some anglers prefer fish fly larvae over worms when fishing for trout.
The fisherman collected fish flies from the riverbank to use as bait.
