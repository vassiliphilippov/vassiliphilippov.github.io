free grace

noun
God's unmerited favor (Christianity)
Pronunciation: / fri ˈɡreɪs / (AmE), / friː ˈɡreɪs / (BrE)
(Christian theology) the unearned and undeserved favor or kindness shown by God, especially in the context of salvation.

Examples

God's free grace
Many sermons focus on the abundance of God's free grace toward humanity.
She was deeply moved by the idea of free grace offered to all believers.
The doctrine of free grace emphasizes that salvation is not earned by good works.
