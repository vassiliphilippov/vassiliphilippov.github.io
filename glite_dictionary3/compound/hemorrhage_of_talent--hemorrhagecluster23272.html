hemorrhage of talent

noun
loss of skilled employees
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛmərɪdʒ əv ˈtæl.ənt / (AmE), / ˈhɛm(ə)rɪdʒ əv ˈtælənt / (BrE)
a rapid or significant loss of skilled or valuable employees from an organization

Examples

hemorrhage of talent after layoffs
After the merger, the company experienced a hemorrhage of talent as many top engineers resigned.
The new CEO is determined to stop the hemorrhage of talent by improving working conditions.
Universities worry about a hemorrhage of talent to the private sector due to better salaries.

  • synonym – brain drain: the emigration or loss of highly trained or intelligent people from a country or organization
