ceteris paribus

adverb
all other things being equal
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛ.tə.rɪs ˈpɛr.ə.bəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛt.ə.rɪs ˈpæ.rɪ.bəs / (BrE)
When all other relevant factors or variables remain unchanged.

Examples

ceteris paribus in economics
Ceteris paribus, a rise in income will result in higher spending by consumers.
The law of demand states that, ceteris paribus, an increase in price leads to a decrease in quantity demanded.
The study assumes, ceteris paribus, that other market conditions remain constant.
