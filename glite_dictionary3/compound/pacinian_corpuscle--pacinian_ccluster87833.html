Pacinian corpuscle

noun
nerve ending for pressure/vibration
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪniən ˈkɔːrpəsl̩ / (AmE), / pəˈsɪniən ˈkɔːpəsl / (BrE)
a specialized nerve ending in the skin that detects pressure and vibration, found mainly in the palms, soles, joints, and genitals

Examples

Pacinian corpuscle detects vibration
Because a Pacinian corpuscle reacts to pressure, damage may reduce touch sensitivity.
