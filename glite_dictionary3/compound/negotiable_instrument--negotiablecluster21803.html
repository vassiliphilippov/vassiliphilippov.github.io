negotiable instrument

noun
written order or promise to pay an amount of money
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡoʊʃiəbl ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊʃəbl ˈɪnstrəmənt / (BrE)
a written document guaranteeing the payment of a specific amount of money, either on demand or at a set time, with the payee named on the document or payable to the bearer

Examples

bank negotiable instrument
A check is a common type of negotiable instrument used in business transactions.
He transferred funds using a negotiable instrument instead of cash.
The bank refused to accept the negotiable instrument because it was incomplete.
