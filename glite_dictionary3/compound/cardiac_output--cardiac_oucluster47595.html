cardiac output

noun
amount of blood pumped by the heart per time
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrdiæk ˈaʊtpʊt / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiæk ˈaʊtpʊt / (BrE)
the amount of blood the heart's ventricles pump out in a specified period, usually measured per minute

Examples

cardiac output measurement
Low cardiac output may indicate an underlying heart condition that requires medical attention.
Regular exercise can help improve your cardiac output over time.
The doctor measured the patient's cardiac output during the stress test.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stroke volume: the amount of blood pumped by the left ventricle in one contraction
