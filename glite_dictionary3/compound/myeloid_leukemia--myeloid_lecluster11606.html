myeloid leukemia

noun
chronic leukemia affecting white blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪ.ə.lɔɪd ləˈkiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ə.lɔɪd lɪˈkiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of chronic leukemia marked by an abnormal increase in granular white blood cells, more commonly affecting older adults.

Examples

myeloid leukemia diagnosis
Older adults are at a higher risk of developing myeloid leukemia.
Recent advances in treatment have improved survival rates for people with myeloid leukemia.
The patient was diagnosed with myeloid leukemia after presenting with fatigue and frequent infections.
