high relief

noun
sculpture with deep projection
Pronunciation: / haɪ rəˈlif / (AmE), / haɪ rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
a sculptural relief in which figures project from the background by at least half their depth

Examples

high relief sculpture
The ancient temple featured intricate high relief sculptures on its walls.
The artist preferred high relief for its dramatic play of light and shadow.
You can clearly see the figures' details thanks to the high relief technique used.
