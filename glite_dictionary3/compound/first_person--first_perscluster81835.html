first person

noun
pronouns or verbs referring to speaker or writer
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈpɝsən / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
(in grammar) the form of pronouns and verbs used by the speaker or writer to refer to themselves, such as 'I' and 'we'.

Examples

first person pronoun
It is common in English to use the first person when describing personal experiences.
Many novels are told from the first person point of view.
When writing in the first person, you use words like 'I' and 'we'.

Related Terms

  • antonym – third person: pronouns and verb forms referring to someone other than speaker or listener (e.g., 'he', 'she', 'they')
