Irish stew

noun
traditional Irish meat and potato stew
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrɪʃ stu / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ stjuː / (BrE)
A traditional Irish dish consisting of meat, usually mutton or lamb, slow-cooked with potatoes, onions, and sometimes other vegetables.

Examples

bowl of Irish stew
Nothing warms you up on a cold evening like a bowl of Irish stew.
She made a hearty Irish stew for dinner using fresh lamb and root vegetables.
The restaurant's signature dish is a classic Irish stew served with brown bread.
