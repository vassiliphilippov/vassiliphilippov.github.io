slime mold

noun
a simple organism with plant and animal traits
Pronunciation: / slaɪm moʊld / (AmE), / slaɪm məʊld / (BrE)
a simple organism consisting of a mass of protoplasm, showing traits of both plants and animals, and sometimes classified among protoctists

Examples

slime mold on a log
A slime mold can expand to cover a large area as it searches for food.
Scientists study slime molds to learn about cellular organization and movement.
Under a microscope, the slime mold flowed slowly over the decaying leaf, consuming bacteria.
