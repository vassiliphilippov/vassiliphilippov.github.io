witness stand

noun
courtroom enclosure for witness testimony
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtnəs stænd / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs stand / (BrE)
the enclosed space in a courtroom where a witness gives evidence

Examples

testify from the witness stand
From the witness stand, the doctor explained the patient's injuries to the jury.
She nervously entered the witness stand and took the oath before answering questions.
The lawyer called him to the witness stand to testify about what he had seen.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dock: an enclosure in a courtroom for the defendant
  • synonym – witness box: another term for 'witness stand', especially in British English
