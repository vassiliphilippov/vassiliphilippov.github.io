dado plane

noun
tool for cutting grooves in wood
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪdoʊ pleɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪdəʊ pleɪn / (BrE)
a hand tool used in woodworking for cutting grooves (dados) across the grain of a board.

Examples

use a dado plane for grooves
Before routers became common, the dado plane was essential for cabinet making.
He adjusted the blade on the dado plane to match the width of the required groove.
The carpenter used a dado plane to cut precise grooves for the shelf supports.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dado: a groove cut in wood, especially across the grain
  • synonym – plough plane: a woodworking plane for cutting grooves along the grain
