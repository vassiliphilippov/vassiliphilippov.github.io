cable railway

noun
railway with cars pulled by cable
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪbəl ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪbəl ˈreɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
a steep railway system in which cars are pulled by a moving cable, often used to transport passengers or goods up and down slopes such as mountainsides

Examples

cable railway up a mountain
A century-old cable railway still operates in the hills above the city.
Engineers inspected the cable railway to ensure that the cable and safety systems were functioning properly.
The cable railway carried tourists to the summit of the mountain each summer.

  • synonym – funicular: a railway that uses cable traction, typically on steep slopes
