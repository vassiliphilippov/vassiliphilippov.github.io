Manila hemp

noun
fiber-producing Philippine plant
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə hɛmp / (BrE)
A plant native to the Philippines, known for its leafstalks which produce strong fiber used in making rope, paper, and textiles.

Examples

Manila hemp plant leaves
Exports of Manila hemp have played a significant role in the economy of the Philippines.
The coarse fiber obtained from Manila hemp is highly valued for making ropes and marine cables.
Unlike regular hemp, Manila hemp comes from the abaca plant, a type of banana.
