clarified butter

noun
butter with milk solids removed
Pronunciation: / ˈklær.əˌfaɪd ˈbʌt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.rɪˌfaɪd ˈbʌt.ə / (BrE)
Butter that has been melted and separated from its milk solids and water, resulting in a clear fat used in cooking.

Examples

clarified butter for frying
Indian cuisine often uses clarified butter, known as ghee, for cooking and frying.
Recipes that require high heat often recommend clarified butter because it has a higher smoke point.
You can make clarified butter at home by carefully melting and skimming off the milk solids.
