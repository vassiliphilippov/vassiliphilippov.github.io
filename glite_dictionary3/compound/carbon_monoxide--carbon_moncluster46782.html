carbon monoxide

noun
poisonous gas from burning carbon
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrbən məˈnɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən məˈnɒksaɪd /; / ˌkɑːbən ˌmɒˈnɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless, and highly poisonous gas produced by the incomplete burning of carbon materials

Examples

carbon monoxide poisoning
A faulty heater can give off dangerous amounts of carbon monoxide.
Long-term exposure to carbon monoxide can cause serious health problems.
They installed a detector to warn of any carbon monoxide leaks in the house.
Detectors are required in many homes to alert residents to carbon monoxide poisoning risks.
Faulty furnaces can leak carbon monoxide, which is hazardous to your health.
Leaving your car running in a closed garage produces dangerous levels of carbon monoxide.
