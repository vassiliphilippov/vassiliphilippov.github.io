primary sex characteristic

noun
feature directly involved in reproduction
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri sɛks ˌkærɪktəˌrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈprɪːməri sɛks kəˌrɪktəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
a physical feature, present at birth, directly involved in reproduction, such as the genitals and reproductive organs

Examples

primary sex characteristic at birth
Abnormal development of primary sex characteristics can indicate certain medical conditions.
At birth, doctors identify the baby's gender based on primary sex characteristics.
In mammals, the presence of ovaries or testes is a primary sex characteristic.
