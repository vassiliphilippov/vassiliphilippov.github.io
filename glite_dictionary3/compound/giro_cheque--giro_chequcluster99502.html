giro cheque

noun
unemployment benefit cheque (Britain)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪroʊ tʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəʊ tʃɛk / (BrE)
(British) A cheque issued by the government, especially to an unemployed person as a welfare payment, that can be cashed at a bank or post office.

Examples

cash a giro cheque
After losing his job, he relied on the giro cheque to cover his basic expenses.
She went to the post office every Thursday to cash her giro cheque.
The giro cheque system was eventually replaced by direct bank transfers.
