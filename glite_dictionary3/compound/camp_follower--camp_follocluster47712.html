camp follower

noun
non-member accompanying a group or army
Pronunciation: / kæmp ˈfɑloʊɚ / (AmE), / kæmp ˈfɒləʊə / (BrE)
a person who accompanies or follows an army, group, or organization, but is not an official member, often for personal gain or support roles

Examples

camp follower selling supplies
During the war, many camp followers provided food and laundry services to the soldiers.
Historically, camp followers included merchants, families, and others seeking to benefit from the army's movements.
The general discouraged camp followers from joining the march to keep the unit disciplined.

  • synonym – hanger-on: a person who associates with a group for personal advantage
