wild cinnamon

noun
West Indian tree; source of bay rum
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / waɪld ˈsɪnəmɪn / (BrE)
A West Indian tree (Pimenta racemosa) whose leaves are the source of bay rum, an aromatic oil used in perfumes and toiletries.

Examples

wild cinnamon leaves for perfume
He planted a wild cinnamon tree in his garden for its fragrant leaves.
In the Caribbean, wild cinnamon is prized for its aromatic oils.
The leaves of the wild cinnamon are distilled to produce the famous bay rum fragrance.
