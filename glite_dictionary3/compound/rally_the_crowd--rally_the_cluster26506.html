to rally the crowd

verb
motivate and gather an audience
Pronunciation: / ˈræli ðə kraʊd / (AmE), / ˈræli ðə kraʊd / (BrE)
to bring people together and inspire or motivate them as a group, especially to action or excitement

Examples

to rally the crowd with energy
He quickly rallied the crowd with a rousing cheer after the team's victory.
Music blared as organizers rallied the crowd before the march began.
The speaker's passion rallied the crowd to support the new initiative.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disperse the crowd: to cause a group of people to break up and leave
  • synonym – inspire: to fill someone with the urge or ability to do something
