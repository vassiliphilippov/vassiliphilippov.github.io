red pill

noun
metaphor for awakening to reality
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd pɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛd pɪl / (BrE)
(informal) A metaphor for choosing to become aware of the often harsh or uncomfortable reality, rather than remaining in blissful ignorance.

Examples

take the red pill of truth
After reading the book, he felt like he had finally taken the red pill.
Many people online refer to their political awakening as taking the red pill.
She warns that taking the red pill can dramatically change your views on the world.
