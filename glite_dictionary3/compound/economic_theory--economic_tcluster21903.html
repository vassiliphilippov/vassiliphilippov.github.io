economic theory

noun
principles explaining economic activities
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkɑnəmɪk ˈθɪri / (AmE), / iˌkɒnəmɪk ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
a set of principles or explanations developed to understand, predict, or guide the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services in an economy.

Examples

economic theory explains markets
Every economic theory relies on assumptions about human behavior and market conditions.
Keynes developed a groundbreaking economic theory that influenced government policy worldwide.
Modern economic theory helps explain the causes and effects of inflation.
