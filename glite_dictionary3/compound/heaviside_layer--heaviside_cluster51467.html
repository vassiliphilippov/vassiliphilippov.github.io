Heaviside layer

noun
region of ionosphere reflecting radio waves
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛviˌsaɪd ˌleɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvɪsaɪd ˌleɪ.ə / (BrE)
a region of the ionosphere, about 50 to 90 miles above Earth, that reflects medium-length radio waves

Examples

the Heaviside layer reflects waves
At night, changes in the Heaviside layer can affect the quality of AM radio broadcasts.
Early radio signals were transmitted over long distances by bouncing off the Heaviside layer.
Scientists study the Heaviside layer to better understand radio communication and atmospheric conditions.
