ice pack

noun
large area of floating sea ice
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪs ˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈaɪs ˌpak / (BrE)
A large area of floating ice masses that have become pressed together, typically found in polar or subpolar seas.

Examples

Arctic ice pack forms
During summer, the ice pack melts and breaks into smaller floes, opening up new areas of ocean.
Satellite images showed the ice pack extending hundreds of miles from the Antarctic coast.
The ship became trapped in a thick ice pack north of Greenland.
