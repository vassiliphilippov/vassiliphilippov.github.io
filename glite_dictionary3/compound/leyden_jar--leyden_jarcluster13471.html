Leyden jar

noun
early device for storing static electricity
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪdən dʒɑr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪdən dʒɑː / (BrE)
a device for storing static electricity, consisting of a glass jar lined inside and outside with metal foil, used historically as an early capacitor

Examples

Leyden jar stores electricity
A Leyden jar consists of a glass container with metal foil on both the inside and outside.
In the eighteenth century, the Leyden jar was used in early experiments with electricity.
The scientist demonstrated how a Leyden jar could store and release static electricity.
