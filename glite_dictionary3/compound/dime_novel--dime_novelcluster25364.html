dime novel

noun
sensational, cheap paperback novel
Pronunciation: / daɪm ˈnɑvəl / (AmE), / daɪm ˈnɒvəl / (BrE)
a cheaply produced, sensational paperback novel, especially one popular in the United States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, often featuring melodramatic plots and adventure

Examples

old dime novel
He dismissed the story as just another dime novel full of improbable heroes and villains.
In the late 1800s, dime novels were wildly popular among young readers seeking excitement.
She found an old dime novel at the flea market, its cover promising outlaw adventures.
