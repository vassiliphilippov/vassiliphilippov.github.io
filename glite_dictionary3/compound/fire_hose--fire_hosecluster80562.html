fire hose

noun
overwhelming stream of information
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪə hoʊz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə həʊz / (BrE)
a very large, rapid, and often overwhelming stream of information, messages, or questions, especially from the media, users, or the public.

Examples

fire hose of information
After the announcement, reporters unleashed a fire hose of questions about the company's leadership changes.
During the product launch, the team received a fire hose of user feedback they couldn't process immediately.
The research lab struggled to filter the fire hose of sensor readings during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deluge: a great quantity of something arriving at the same time
  • synonym – flood: an overwhelming amount of information or messages
