daily double

noun
bet on winners of two races in one day
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪli ˈdʌbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪli ˈdʌbl / (BrE)
A single bet in which the bettor selects the winners of two designated horse races to be run on the same day.

Examples

daily double bet at racetrack
He placed a daily double at the track, hoping to win big if both of his horses finished first.
Many gamblers focus on the daily double because of its potential for higher returns.
The daily double paid out much more than any single race bet that day.
