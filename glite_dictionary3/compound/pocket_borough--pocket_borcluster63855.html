pocket borough

noun
historical borough controlled by one person or family
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt ˈbɜroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˈbʌrə / (BrE)
(historical) a parliamentary borough with a very small electorate or population, controlled by a single person or family who could ensure the selection of their chosen candidate.

Examples

a pocket borough controlled by a family
Before the Reform Act of 1832, many seats in Parliament were controlled by pocket boroughs.
Political influence in a pocket borough could be secured with little effort due to the tiny electorate.
The Duke owned the land and treated the pocket borough as his private domain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – rotten borough: parliamentary borough with a very small electorate, often controlled by a patron
