photochemical reaction

noun
chemical reaction caused by light
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˈkɛmɪkəl riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈkɛmɪkəl rɪˈækʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction initiated or accelerated by the presence of light, especially ultraviolet or visible light

Examples

photochemical reaction with sunlight
A photochemical reaction can change the properties of a substance when exposed to ultraviolet light.
Ozone formation in the atmosphere involves a photochemical reaction triggered by sunlight.
Photosynthesis is a classic example of a photochemical reaction in plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym – photolysis: decomposition of a chemical compound by light
