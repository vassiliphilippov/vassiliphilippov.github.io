directional antenna

noun
antenna that focuses signal in one direction
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkʃənl ænˈtɛnə / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃənl ænˈtɛnə / (BrE)
an antenna designed to transmit or receive radio waves primarily in a specific direction, rather than equally in all directions

Examples

directional antenna boosts signal
A directional antenna can reduce interference from unwanted sources by focusing the beam.
Cell towers often use directional antennas to cover specific geographic areas efficiently.
The engineer recommended installing a directional antenna to improve the signal strength toward the north tower.

Related Terms

  • antonym – omnidirectional antenna: an antenna that radiates or receives equally in all directions
