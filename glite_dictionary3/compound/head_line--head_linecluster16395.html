head line

noun
palmistry line relating to intellect
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛd ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛd ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
In palmistry, a crease on the palm believed to represent a person's mind, learning style, and approach to knowledge.

Examples

head line in palm reading
In palmistry, the head line is thought to reveal a person's analytical abilities.
She asked the fortune teller what her head line said about her character.
The head line on his palm was long and deeply etched, which was interpreted as a sign of intelligence.
