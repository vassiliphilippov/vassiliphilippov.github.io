linking verb

noun
verb connecting subject and complement
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋkɪŋ vɜrb / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkɪŋ vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb, such as 'be' or 'become,' that connects the subject of a sentence with its complement, typically identifying or describing the subject.

Examples

linking verb 'is' in a sentence
In the sentence 'She is happy,' 'is' acts as a linking verb connecting the subject to its description.
Teachers explain that a linking verb does not show action but links the subject to more information.
The word 'become' is often used as a linking verb in English sentences.
