St John's Day

noun
Christian festival on June 24th, quarter day
Pronunciation: / sɛnt ˈdʒɑnz deɪ / (AmE), / sənt ˈdʒɒnz deɪ / (BrE)
A festival observed on June 24th, marking the birth of St John the Baptist; recognized as a quarter day in England, Wales, and Ireland.

Examples

St John's Day festival
Church services are held across the UK to mark St John's Day.
Many European countries light bonfires to celebrate St John's Day.
Traditionally, St John's Day is one of the quarter days when rents were paid in England.
