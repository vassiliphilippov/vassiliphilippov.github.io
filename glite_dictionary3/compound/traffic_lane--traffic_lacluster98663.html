traffic lane

noun
part of a road marked for one line of traffic
Pronunciation: / ˈtræfɪk leɪn / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk leɪn / (BrE)
a division of a main road marked by painted lines, intended for a single line of moving vehicles

Examples

wide traffic lane
During rush hour, the leftmost traffic lane is reserved for carpool vehicles.
He changed traffic lanes without signaling, which is dangerous.
Roadwork blocked one traffic lane, causing a long delay.
