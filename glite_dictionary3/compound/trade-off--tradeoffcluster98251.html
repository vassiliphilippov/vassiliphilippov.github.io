trade-off

noun
a compromise between two desirable but incompatible things
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪd.ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd.ɒf / (BrE)
A situation in which one thing must be given up in exchange for another, typically to achieve a compromise between competing needs or values.

Examples

a trade-off decision
Deciding to live closer to work involved a trade-off with higher rent.
Many environmental policies require a trade-off between economic growth and conservation.
There is always a trade-off between speed and accuracy in scientific research.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compromise: an agreement reached by each side making concessions
