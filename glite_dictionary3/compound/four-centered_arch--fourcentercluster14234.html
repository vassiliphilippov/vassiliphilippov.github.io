four-centered arch

noun
type of elliptical or pointed arch
Pronunciation: / fɔr ˈsɛntɚd ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / fɔː ˈsɛntəd ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch with a low, elliptical or pointed form, produced by drawing arcs from four different centers; commonly used in Gothic architecture.
