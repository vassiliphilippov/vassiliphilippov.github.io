mortality table

noun
table showing life expectancy and death rates
Pronunciation: / mɔrˈtæləti ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / mɔːˈtælɪti ˈteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
A statistical table showing the probability of death and expected remaining life at each age for a population, used especially in actuarial work.

Examples

mortality table for insurance
Insurance companies rely on mortality tables to set premium rates for life insurance policies.
Modern mortality tables consider factors such as occupation, lifestyle, and gender.
The actuary consulted the mortality table to estimate the average life expectancy for their calculations.
