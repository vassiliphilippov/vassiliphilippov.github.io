shunt DC motor

noun
direct current motor with parallel field and armature windings
Pronunciation: / ʃʌnt ˌdiː ˈsiː ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ʃʌnt ˌdiː ˈsiː ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
a type of direct current motor in which the field windings and the armature are connected in parallel across the same DC power source, commonly used in applications requiring good speed regulation

Examples

industrial shunt DC motor
A shunt DC motor maintains nearly constant speed under varying loads.
The technician recommended installing a shunt DC motor for better control over machine speed.
Unlike a series DC motor, a shunt DC motor is preferred for its stable operation in industrial settings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – series DC motor: a DC motor with field windings in series with the armature
