grey substance

noun
greyish nervous tissue in the central nervous system
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
The greyish nervous tissue in the brain and spinal cord, containing cell bodies and unmyelinated fibers; forms the cerebral cortex and other regions.

Examples

the grey substance of the brain
Damage to the grey substance of the spinal cord can result in loss of certain bodily functions.
Researchers studied changes in grey substance density in patients with neurological disorders.
The cerebral cortex is composed primarily of grey substance.
