Pallas Athena

proper noun
Greek goddess of wisdom and warfare
Pronunciation: / ˈpæləs əˈθinə / (AmE), / ˈpæləs əˈθiːnə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of wisdom, useful arts, and prudent warfare, and the protector of Athens; identified with the Roman goddess Minerva.

Examples

Pallas Athena goddess of wisdom
In mythology, Pallas Athena was known for her wisdom as well as her skill in battle.
Many ancient artworks depict Pallas Athena wearing a helmet and carrying a shield.
The temple on the Acropolis was dedicated to Pallas Athena, the patron goddess of Athens.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Athena: Greek goddess of wisdom and warfare, another name for Pallas Athena
  • synonym – Minerva: Roman goddess of wisdom and strategic warfare, equivalent to Athena
