bustard quail

noun
small, quail-like ground bird
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌstərd kweɪl / (AmE), / ˈbʌstəd kweɪl / (BrE)
a small, quail-like ground-dwelling bird of southern Eurasia and northern Africa, lacking a hind toe and inhabiting grassy plains

Examples

a bustard quail in grass
The bustard quail is well adapted to life in open grasslands and cultivated fields.
Unlike true quails, the bustard quail lacks a hind toe and has unique feeding habits.
We spotted a bustard quail darting through the tall grass during our trip to northern Africa.
