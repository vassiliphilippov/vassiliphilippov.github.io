melting point

noun
temperature at which a solid melts
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the temperature at which a solid becomes a liquid; the fixed temperature at which a substance melts

Examples

melting point of iron
At the melting point, a solid changes to a liquid.
Gold has a much higher melting point than aluminum.
The melting point of ice is 0 degrees Celsius.
