page printer

noun
printer that prints one full page at a time
Pronunciation: / peɪdʒ ˈprɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / peɪdʒ ˈprɪn.tə / (BrE)
A printer device that produces one complete page at a time, usually by assembling the entire page's content in memory before printing.

Examples

laser page printer
Before printing, a page printer assembles the whole page in memory to ensure quality output.
The office manager ordered a new page printer for faster and more efficient document production.
Unlike older printers, a modern page printer can handle large volumes of reports with ease.
