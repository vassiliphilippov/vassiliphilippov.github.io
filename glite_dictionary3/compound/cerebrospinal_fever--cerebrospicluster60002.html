cerebrospinal fever

noun
bacterial meningitis
Pronunciation: / səˌriːbroʊˌspaɪnəl ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / sɪˌriːbrəʊˌspaɪnəl ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A serious illness caused by bacterial infection of the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord, often leading to meningitis.

Examples

cerebrospinal fever outbreak
After an outbreak of cerebrospinal fever, the school was temporarily closed for deep cleaning.
Early treatment is crucial for patients diagnosed with cerebrospinal fever.
In the past, cerebrospinal fever claimed many lives before antibiotics became widely available.
