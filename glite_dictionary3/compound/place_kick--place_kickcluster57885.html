place kick

noun
kick with ball placed on ground (sports)
Pronunciation: / pleɪs kɪk / (AmE), / pleɪs kɪk / (BrE)
In sports, a kick in which the ball is placed on the ground before being kicked, often used in football or rugby for scoring or restarting play.

Examples

place kick for a goal
A successful place kick can turn the tide in a rugby game.
He scored a goal with a powerful place kick from the 40-yard line.
The team's strategy relies heavily on accurate place kicks during important matches.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drop kick: a kick made by dropping the ball and kicking it as it bounces from the ground
  • antonym – punt: kick where the ball is dropped and kicked before hitting the ground
