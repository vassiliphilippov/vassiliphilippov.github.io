precession of the equinoxes

noun
slow westward shift of equinoxes due to Earth's axis motion
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɛʃ.ən əv ði iˈkwɪn.ɑks.ɪz / (AmE), / prɪˈsɛʃ.ən əv ði ɪˈkwɪn.ɒks.ɪz / (BrE)
the gradual westward movement of the equinoxes along the ecliptic, caused by the slow, conical motion of the Earth's axis of rotation

Examples

the precession of the equinoxes shifts stars
Ancient astronomers gradually recognized the precession of the equinoxes as an explanation for changes in the position of the stars.
Over thousands of years, the precession of the equinoxes shifts the timing of Earth's seasons in relation to the stars.
The precession of the equinoxes affects how we calculate celestial coordinates over centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym – axial precession: the slow rotation of the Earth's axis itself
