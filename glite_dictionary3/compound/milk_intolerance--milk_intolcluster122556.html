milk intolerance

noun
inability to digest milk due to lactase deficiency
Pronunciation: / mɪlk ɪnˈtɑl.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / mɪlk ɪnˈtɒl.ə.rəns / (BrE)
A condition in which the body is unable to properly digest milk and milk products due to a deficiency of lactase, resulting in an inability to break down lactose.

Examples

milk intolerance symptoms
Many adults suffer from milk intolerance, experiencing discomfort after drinking even small amounts of milk.
People with milk intolerance often need to find alternatives to dairy in their diets.
Symptoms of milk intolerance can include bloating, gas, and abdominal pain after consuming milk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lactose intolerance: inability to digest lactose, a sugar found in milk
