fudge factor

noun
arbitrary adjustment in calculations
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌdʒ ˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈfʌdʒ ˌfæktə / (BrE)
an arbitrary or estimated value added to a calculation to allow for uncertainties or errors, often to increase its accuracy

Examples

add a fudge factor
Engineers often use a fudge factor when precise data is unavailable.
He added a small fudge factor to the total to ensure the calculation was safe.
The researchers included a fudge factor to compensate for unknown variables in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym – margin of error: an allowed amount of variation in statistical results or measurements
