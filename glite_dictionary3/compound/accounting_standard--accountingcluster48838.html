accounting standard

noun
established rule for accounting practices
Pronunciation: / əˈkaʊntɪŋ ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntɪŋ ˈstændəd / (BrE)
a principle or rule established by an authority that governs current accounting practices and serves as a guide for the appropriate treatment of financial transactions

Examples

accounting standard for companies
International accounting standards help ensure consistency in financial reporting across countries.
The company updated its processes to comply with the revised accounting standard.
The new accounting standard requires all leases to be reported on the balance sheet.
