barbette carriage

noun
gun carriage for firing over a parapet
Pronunciation: / bɑrˈbɛt ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɛt ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a type of gun carriage positioned so that the gun can be fired over a parapet or protective wall, commonly used in fortifications and naval artillery.

Examples

barbette carriage for cannons
By raising the gun above the parapet, the barbette carriage offered a tactical advantage.
Historians studied the design of the barbette carriage used in 19th-century coastal defenses.
The fort's cannons were mounted on barbette carriages to maximize their field of fire.
