specific gravity

noun
density ratio relative to water
Pronunciation: / spəˈsɪfɪk ˈɡrævəti / (AmE), / spəˈsɪfɪk ˈɡrævɪti / (BrE)
the ratio of the density of a substance to the density of water, often used to compare the heaviness of liquids or solids to water

Examples

specific gravity of water
Oil floats on water because its specific gravity is less than one.
The laboratory technician recorded the specific gravity of the patient's urine as part of the test.
To determine the purity of the sample, they measured its specific gravity.
