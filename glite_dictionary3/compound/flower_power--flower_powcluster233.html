flower power

noun
1960s/70s US counterculture movement
Pronunciation: / ˈflaʊ.ɚ ˌpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ə ˌpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A movement among young people in the United States during the 1960s and 70s that promoted peace, love, and nonviolence, often symbolized by flowers.

Examples

flower power movement
Decades later, the ideals of flower power remain influential in calls for peace and love.
Many protesters carried signs and wore garlands to express their support for flower power.
