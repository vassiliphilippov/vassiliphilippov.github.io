cyber-terrorist

noun
someone who commits acts of cyber-terrorism
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪbɚ tɛrərɪst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbə tɛrərɪst / (BrE)
a person who uses technology, especially computers and the internet, to carry out attacks intended to cause fear or disruption for political or ideological reasons

Examples

cyber-terrorist attacks computers
Authorities arrested a cyber-terrorist who gained access to sensitive financial data.
Companies often hire cybersecurity experts to protect their systems from potential cyber-terrorists.
The government increased security measures after a renowned cyber-terrorist threatened the country's electric grid.
