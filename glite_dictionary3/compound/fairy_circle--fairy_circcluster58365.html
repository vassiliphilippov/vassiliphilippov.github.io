fairy circle

noun
ring of mushrooms in grasslands
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛri ˌsɝkəl / (AmE), / ˈfeəri ˌsɜːkl / (BrE)
a naturally occurring ring or arc of mushrooms, typically marking the edge of the underground growth of fungal mycelium

Examples

a fairy circle of mushrooms
Local legends say that dancing fairies create every fairy circle that appears in the field.
Scientists studied the soil inside the fairy circle to understand fungal growth patterns.
We discovered a fairy circle in the meadow, its mushrooms forming a perfect ring.
