sodium nitrite

noun
chemical used to preserve foods
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm ˈnaɪtraɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˈnaɪtraɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt used to preserve and color food, especially meat and fish products, and in industrial and laboratory processes

Examples

sodium nitrite in cured meat
Adding sodium nitrite helps cured meats retain their pink color and prevents bacterial growth.
Scientists are studying the health risks associated with consuming foods containing sodium nitrite.
The manufacturer lists sodium nitrite as an ingredient on the packaging.
