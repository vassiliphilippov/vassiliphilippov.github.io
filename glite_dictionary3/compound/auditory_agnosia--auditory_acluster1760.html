auditory agnosia

noun
inability to recognize sounds despite normal hearing
Pronunciation: / ˈɔdəˌtɔri ægˈnoʊʒə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri ægˈnəʊziə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the inability to recognize or interpret the meaning of sounds, especially spoken words, despite normal hearing.

Examples

auditory agnosia affects speech
After his illness, he was diagnosed with auditory agnosia, which made it difficult for him to understand spoken language.
People with auditory agnosia can hear sounds but cannot interpret their meaning, even familiar voices or words.
The neurologist explained that auditory agnosia is rare and different from hearing loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym – agnosia: loss of ability to recognize objects, people, sounds, shapes, or smells
