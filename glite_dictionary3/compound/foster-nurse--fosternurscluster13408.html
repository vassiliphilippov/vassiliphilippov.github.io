foster-nurse

noun
woman who raises another's child
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑstər nɜrs / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə nɜːs / (BrE)
a woman who raises and cares for another woman's child as her own, often serving as a nurse or surrogate mother

Examples

foster-nurse an infant
In the ancient story, the queen appointed a foster-nurse to care for the infant prince.
Legends often mention a loyal foster-nurse who protects the hero during childhood.
The child's foster-nurse loved her as if she were her own daughter.
