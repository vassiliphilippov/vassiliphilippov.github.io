G suit

noun
pressure suit for pilots and astronauts
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒi ˌsuːt / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː ˌsuːt / (BrE)
A garment worn by pilots and astronauts that applies pressure to the body to counteract the effects of high acceleration forces and prevent loss of consciousness.

Examples

G suit for pilots
Astronauts sometimes test their G suit before training in simulated high-gravity conditions.
Pilots wear a G suit to prevent blackouts during high-speed maneuvers.
The G suit inflates to apply pressure to the body as the aircraft turns sharply.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pressure suit: airtight suit designed to maintain normal pressure on the wearer
