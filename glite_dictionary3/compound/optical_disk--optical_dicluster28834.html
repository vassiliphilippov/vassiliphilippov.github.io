optical disk

noun
data storage disk read by laser
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl dɪsk / (BrE)
A flat, circular disk coated with plastic that stores digital data as tiny pits on its surface, read by a laser.

Examples

optical disk stores data
A DVD is a type of optical disk capable of holding movies and other data.
Most computers are able to read an optical disk to access stored files and media.
She burned her vacation photos onto an optical disk for safekeeping.

Related Terms

  • synonym – compact disc: a small optical disk used for storing music or data
  • antonym – magnetic disk: a data storage disk that uses magnetic storage technology rather than optical
