lodgepole pine

noun
a pine tree native to western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑdʒˌpoʊl paɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒˌpəʊl paɪn / (BrE)
a species of pine tree native to western North America, with slender, often tall trunks, two-needled leaves, and bark that is red to yellow-brown and fissured into small squares

Examples

lodgepole pine forest
Many log cabins in Canada were built using lodgepole pine for their sturdy walls.
Researchers are studying how the lodgepole pine adapts to changes in climate.
The lodgepole pine forests are home to a diversity of wildlife in the Rocky Mountains.
