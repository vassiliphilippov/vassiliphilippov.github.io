minor tranquilizer

noun
antianxiety drug; mild tranquilizer
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnɚ træŋˈkwɪlaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə træŋˈkwɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
A drug that is used to relieve anxiety and reduce tension and irritability, without causing major sedation or mental dullness.

Examples

take a minor tranquilizer pill
She was advised to avoid alcohol while taking a minor tranquilizer for her nerves.
Some patients respond well to minor tranquilizers during periods of acute stress.
The doctor prescribed a minor tranquilizer to help manage his anxiety symptoms.
