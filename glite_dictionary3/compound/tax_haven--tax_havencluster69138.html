tax haven

noun
country or region with low taxes for foreigners
Pronunciation: / tæks ˈheɪvən / (AmE), / tæks ˈheɪvən / (BrE)
A country or region where taxes are levied at a low rate, often attracting foreign individuals or businesses seeking to reduce their tax burden.

Examples

tax haven for companies
Many multinational companies shift profits to a tax haven to reduce their tax bills.
The Caribbean is known for having several popular tax havens used by wealthy individuals.
The government is cracking down on the use of tax havens to hide assets.
