serum globulin

noun
proteins in blood serum with most antibodies
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəm ˈɡlɑːbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəm ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a group of globular proteins found in blood serum, containing most of the blood's antibodies

Examples

serum globulin level test
A blood test revealed an elevated level of serum globulin, indicating a possible infection.
Doctors often measure serum globulin to evaluate a patient's immune function.
Reduced serum globulin can be a sign of liver disease or other health problems.
