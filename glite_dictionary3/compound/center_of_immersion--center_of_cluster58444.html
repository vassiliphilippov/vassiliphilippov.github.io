center of immersion

noun
physics: mass center of submerged part of vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntɚ əv ɪˈmɝʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv ɪˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
(physics) the point representing the center of mass of the submerged part of a ship or other floating object.

Examples

center of immersion of a ship
Engineers calculated the center of immersion to ensure the vessel's stability in the water.
The ship's center of immersion shifts as the cargo is loaded and unloaded.
Understanding the center of immersion is crucial for designing safe and efficient boats.
