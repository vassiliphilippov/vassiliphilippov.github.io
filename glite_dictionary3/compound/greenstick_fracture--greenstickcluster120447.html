greenstick fracture

noun
partial bone fracture, common in children
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrin.stɪk ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn.stɪk ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
A type of bone fracture, most common in children, where the bone bends and cracks on one side without breaking completely through.

Examples

child with greenstick fracture
A greenstick fracture often occurs because children's bones are softer and more flexible than those of adults.
After falling off his bike, Ben was diagnosed with a greenstick fracture that required a cast but no surgery.
The X-ray revealed a greenstick fracture in her left forearm, which is typical in young children.
