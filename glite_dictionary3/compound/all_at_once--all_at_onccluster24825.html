all at once

adverb
suddenly; without warning
Pronunciation: / ɔl ət wʌns / (AmE), / ɔːl ət wʌns / (BrE)
suddenly and unexpectedly; without warning or delay

Examples

all at once it happened
All at once, the lights went out and the room was plunged into darkness.
It started raining heavily all at once, catching us without umbrellas.
The baby began to cry all at once, startling everyone in the room.
