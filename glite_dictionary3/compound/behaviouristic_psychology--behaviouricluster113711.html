behaviouristic psychology

noun
psychology focusing on observable behavior
Pronunciation: / bɪˌheɪvjɚˈɪstɪk saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / bɪˌheɪvjʊˈrɪstɪk saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
an approach to psychology that emphasizes the study of observable and measurable behavior rather than internal mental processes

Examples

behaviouristic psychology focuses on actions
Behaviouristic psychology rejects the idea of studying thoughts and feelings directly.
Many early experiments in behaviouristic psychology focused on animal learning.
The rise of behaviouristic psychology shifted attention from introspection to observable actions.

Related Terms

  • synonym – behaviorism: the theory that psychology should be based only on observable behavior
