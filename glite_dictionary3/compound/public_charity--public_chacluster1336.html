public charity

noun
charity mainly funded by the public
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk ˈtʃærəti / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈtʃærəti / (BrE)
A charity that receives most of its funding from the general public, rather than from a small group of individuals or sources.

Examples

local public charity
She volunteers every weekend at a local public charity that supports homeless families.
The new law provides tax benefits for donations made to a public charity.
Unlike a private foundation, a public charity must demonstrate broad public support.

Related Terms

  • antonym – private foundation: a charity primarily funded by a single source or a small group, not the public
