bee fly

noun
nectar-eating fly resembling a bee
Pronunciation: / ˈbiˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbiː flaɪ / (BrE)
A hairy, nectar-feeding fly that resembles a bee; its larvae are parasitic on the larvae of bees and related insects.

Examples

fuzzy bee fly
A bee fly can be mistaken for a real bee due to its fuzzy appearance and flight pattern.
Gardeners noticed that bee flies play a role in controlling bee populations through their parasitic larvae.
The bee fly hovered above the flowers, sipping nectar with its long proboscis.
