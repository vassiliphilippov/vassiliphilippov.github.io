tobacco moth

noun
moth pest feeding on tobacco products
Pronunciation: / təˈbækoʊ mɒθ / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ mɒθ / (BrE)
A small moth whose larvae feed on tobacco and other dried plant products, often considered a pest in tobacco storage.

Examples

tobacco moth infestation
Farmers implement strict controls to prevent tobacco moth outbreaks during the curing process.
The larvae of the tobacco moth can also attack dried herbs and spices in addition to tobacco.
The warehouse suffered significant damage when a colony of tobacco moths infested the stored leaves.
