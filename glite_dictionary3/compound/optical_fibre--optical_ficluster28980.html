optical fibre

noun
thin fiber transmitting light/data
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A very thin, flexible strand of glass or plastic that transmits light along its length, often used for transmitting data or images.

Examples

optical fibre cable
Doctors sometimes use optical fibre cables in medical instruments to look inside the body.
Modern communication networks rely heavily on optical fibre for fast data transmission over long distances.
The high-speed internet connection in our office is made possible by optical fibre technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fiber optics: the use or application of optical fibres to transmit data as light pulses
