hackney coach

noun
a horse-drawn carriage for hire
Pronunciation: / ˈhækni koʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhakni kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a four-wheeled horse-drawn carriage for hire, especially common in London in the 17th to 19th centuries

Examples

ride a hackney coach
In Victorian London, a hackney coach was a common sight on busy streets.
She hailed a hackney coach to carry her luggage across town.
Writers of the era often describe traveling by hackney coach through the city.
