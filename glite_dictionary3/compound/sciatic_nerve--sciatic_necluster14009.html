sciatic nerve

noun
major nerve running from lower back down the leg
Pronunciation: / saɪˈætɪk nɝːv / (AmE), / saɪˈætɪk nɜːv / (BrE)
the largest nerve in the human body, running from the lower back through the hip and down the back of each leg

Examples

sciatic nerve pain
A herniated disc can put pressure on the sciatic nerve, causing numbness or weakness in the leg.
Surgery was required to repair damage to her sciatic nerve.
The sciatic nerve is often the source of pain in cases of sciatica.
