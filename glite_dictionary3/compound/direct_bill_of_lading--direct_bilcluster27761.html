direct bill of lading

noun
bill of lading for shipment on same vessel
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt bɪl əv ˈleɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt bɪl əv ˈleɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
A bill of lading issued when the shipment is delivered by the same vessel that picked it up, without transshipment.

Examples

direct bill of lading shipment
The direct bill of lading ensured the cargo stayed on the same vessel throughout the journey.
They requested a direct bill of lading to reduce the risk of delays during transit.
When using a direct bill of lading, shippers know there will be no transfer between ships.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bill of lading: a document issued by a carrier to acknowledge receipt of cargo for shipment
