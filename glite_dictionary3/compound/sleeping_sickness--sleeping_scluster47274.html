sleeping sickness

noun
epidemic encephalitis (1915–1926)
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˌsɪknəs / (BrE)
A severe form of encephalitis that was epidemic between 1915 and 1926, characterized by paralysis of the extrinsic eye muscles and extreme muscular weakness.

Examples

sleeping sickness outbreak
Many patients affected by sleeping sickness experienced profound muscle weakness and difficulty moving their eyes.
Symptoms of sleeping sickness include extreme fatigue and paralysis of certain eye muscles.
The epidemic of sleeping sickness in the early 20th century caused widespread concern among medical professionals.
