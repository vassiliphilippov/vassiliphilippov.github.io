no ball

noun
illegal delivery in cricket
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊ bɔl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ bɔːl / (BrE)
(in cricket) a ball bowled illegally, usually because of an infringement of the rules by the bowler, resulting in a penalty for the fielding side.

Examples

no ball called by umpire
A no ball gave the batsman a free hit on the next delivery.
Bowling a no ball at a crucial moment can change the course of the match.
The umpire signaled a no ball after the bowler overstepped the line.
