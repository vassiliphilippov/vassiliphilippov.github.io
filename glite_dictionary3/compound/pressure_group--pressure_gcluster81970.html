pressure group

noun
group seeking political influence
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə ɡruːp / (BrE)
an organized group that attempts to influence government policies or decisions to benefit its particular interests, often through lobbying or advocacy

Examples

pressure group influences government
Several pressure groups are lobbying Parliament to change the education policy.
She joined a pressure group advocating for human rights reforms.
The environmental pressure group organized a campaign to reduce plastic waste in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – interest group: an organized group that tries to influence public policy
  • synonym – lobby: a group seeking to influence legislators, or the activity of attempting to do so
