isochoric process

noun
thermodynamic process at constant volume
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈkɔrɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈkɔːrɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the volume of the system remains constant

Examples

isochoric process in a gas
Cooling a gas at constant volume is an example of an isochoric process.
In an isochoric process, the volume of the gas does not change while heat is added or removed.
The pressure inside the vessel increased during the isochoric process.
