prescriptive grammar

noun
grammar based on prescribed rules
Pronunciation: / prɪˈskrɪptɪv ˈɡræmər / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪptɪv ˈɡræmə / (BrE)
a set of rules concerning how a language should be used, based on prescribed norms rather than actual usage

Examples

strict prescriptive grammar rules
Debates about split infinitives often arise from strict prescriptive grammar rules.
Many traditional textbooks are based on prescriptive grammar rather than how people actually speak.
She prefers a prescriptive grammar approach when teaching English to her students.

Related Terms

  • antonym – descriptive grammar: grammar that describes how language is actually used
