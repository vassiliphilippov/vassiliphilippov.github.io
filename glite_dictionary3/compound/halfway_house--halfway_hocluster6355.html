halfway house

noun
facility for recovery or transition from prison
Pronunciation: / ˈhæfˌweɪ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈweɪ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A residential facility for people recovering from addiction or transitioning from incarceration, providing structure and support before independent living.

Examples

stay at a halfway house
After completing her sentence, Maria stayed at a halfway house to ease her transition back into society.
Living in a halfway house provided him with the structure he needed to rebuild his life.
The city opened a new halfway house to assist individuals recovering from drug addiction.
