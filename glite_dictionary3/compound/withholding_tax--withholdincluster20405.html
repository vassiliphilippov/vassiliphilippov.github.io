withholding tax

noun
income tax withheld from wages by the employer
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈhoʊldɪŋ tæks / (AmE), / wɪðˈhəʊldɪŋ tæks / (BrE)
A tax on income that is withheld from employees' wages and paid directly to the government by the employer.

Examples

withholding tax on salary
Foreign investors may face a withholding tax when receiving dividends from U.S. companies.
The withholding tax is remitted to the government on a monthly basis by employers.
The company deducted a withholding tax from each paycheck as required by law.

Related Terms

  • synonym – payroll tax: a tax an employer withholds and pays on behalf of employees
