excess baggage

noun
luggage exceeding allowed limit
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛs ˈbægɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛs ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
luggage or personal belongings that exceed the weight or number allowed by a transport provider, typically incurring extra charges

Examples

excess baggage charges
If you travel light, you won’t have to worry about excess baggage fees.
She was frustrated to find out she had excess baggage after checking in at the airport.
The airline charged passengers for excess baggage above 20 kilograms.
