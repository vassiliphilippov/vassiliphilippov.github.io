dorsal horn

noun
region of the spinal cord for sensory input
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔrsəl hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈdɔːsəl hɔːn / (BrE)
the region at the back of the spinal cord's gray matter where sensory nerve fibers synapse

Examples

spinal cord dorsal horn
Injury to the dorsal horn can result in loss of sensation below the damaged area.
Researchers studied changes in the dorsal horn in response to chronic pain.
The sensory neurons transmit signals to the dorsal horn of the spinal cord.
