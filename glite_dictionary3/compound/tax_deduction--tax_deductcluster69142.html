tax deduction

noun
amount subtracted from taxable income
Pronunciation: / tæks dɪˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / tæks dɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
an amount of money or expense that can be subtracted from gross income when calculating the amount of tax owed

Examples

claim a tax deduction
Claiming every allowable tax deduction can significantly reduce your annual tax bill.
Expenses related to your home office can sometimes qualify as a tax deduction.
You may be eligible for a tax deduction if you donate to charity.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tax credit: an amount subtracted directly from the tax owed
