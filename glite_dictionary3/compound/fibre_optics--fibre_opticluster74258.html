fibre optics

noun
technology for transmitting light signals via glass fibers
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbər ˈɑptɪks / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə ˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
The technology of transmitting light signals along thin glass or plastic fibers, used especially for telecommunications and data transmission.

Examples

fibre optics communication cables
Engineers are developing new applications for fibre optics in medical imaging.
Many modern internet connections rely on fibre optics for high-speed data transfer.
The use of fibre optics has revolutionized the telecommunications industry.
