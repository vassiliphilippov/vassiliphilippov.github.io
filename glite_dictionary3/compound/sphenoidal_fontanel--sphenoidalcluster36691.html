sphenoidal fontanel

noun
soft spot where several skull bones meet in infants
Pronunciation: / sfɪˌnɔɪdəl fɑnˈtænəl / (AmE), / sfɪˌnɔɪdəl fɒnˈtænəl / (BrE)
The soft membranous gap at the intersection of the frontal, parietal, temporal, and sphenoid bones in an infant's skull; becomes the pterion after ossification.

Examples

infant sphenoidal fontanel
During development, the sphenoidal fontanel gradually ossifies to become the pterion.
Palpation of the sphenoidal fontanel can help identify certain cranial abnormalities in newborns.
The doctor checked the infant's sphenoidal fontanel for signs of premature closure.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pterion: the region where the sphenoidal fontanel closes in adults
