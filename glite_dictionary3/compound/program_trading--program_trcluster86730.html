program trading

noun
computer-initiated stock trading method
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊɡræm ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡræm ˈtreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of buying and selling large blocks of stocks, in which the trades are initiated by computer programs rather than manually.

Examples

program trading in stocks
Many investment banks rely on program trading to execute large stock trades quickly and efficiently.
Regulators imposed new rules to monitor program trading after it was linked to rapid market movements.
The rise of program trading has significantly changed how stocks are bought and sold on Wall Street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – algorithmic trading: the use of computer algorithms to automate trading decisions
  • antonym – manual trading: trading in which orders are entered by humans rather than computers
