apple maggot

noun
larva that infests apples
Pronunciation: / ˈæpəl ˌmæɡət / (AmE), / ˈæpəl ˌmæɡət / (BrE)
a small larva of a fruit fly (Rhagoletis pomonella) that bores into and feeds on apples, causing damage to the fruit

Examples

apple maggot infestation
If you notice tiny tunnels inside your apples, it could be a sign of the apple maggot.
Many farmers lost a large portion of their crop to the apple maggot this year.
Proper pest control methods help prevent apple maggots from destroying orchards.
