drilling fluid

noun
mixture used to lubricate and cool drill bit
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪlɪŋ ˌfluɪd / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ ˌfluːɪd / (BrE)
A mixture of water, clays, and chemicals used in drilling operations to lubricate and cool the drill bit, remove cuttings from the hole, and stabilize the wellbore walls.
