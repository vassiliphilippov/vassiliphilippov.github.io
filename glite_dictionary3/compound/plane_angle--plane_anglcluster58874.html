plane angle

noun
angle formed by two intersecting lines
Pronunciation: / pleɪn ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / pleɪn ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
An angle formed by two straight lines lying in the same plane.

Examples

right plane angle
A right angle is a type of plane angle that measures exactly 90 degrees.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the measure of a plane angle using a protractor.
The sum of the interior plane angles in a triangle is always 180 degrees.
