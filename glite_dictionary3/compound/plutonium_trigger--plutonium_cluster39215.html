plutonium trigger

noun
device used to trigger nuclear fission
Pronunciation: / pluːˈtoʊniəm ˈtrɪɡər / (AmE), / pluːˈtəʊniəm ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
A device consisting of a sphere (usually steel or beryllium) containing plutonium-239, used to initiate nuclear fission when compressed by explosives.

Examples

plutonium trigger in a bomb
Scientists studied how the plutonium trigger would respond to different modes of explosive compression.
Security protocols were tightened around storage areas containing a plutonium trigger.
The design of the atomic bomb included a plutonium trigger for initiating the nuclear reaction.
