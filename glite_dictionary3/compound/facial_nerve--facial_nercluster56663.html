facial nerve

noun
seventh cranial nerve controlling facial muscles
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪʃəl nɝv / (AmE), / ˈfeɪʃəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the seventh cranial nerve, responsible for the movement of the muscles of facial expression and conveying some sensory information from the face

Examples

damage to the facial nerve
Bell's palsy is a condition that affects the facial nerve, leading to temporary weakness of facial muscles.
Damage to the facial nerve can result in paralysis on one side of the face.
The surgeon carefully avoided the facial nerve during the procedure.
