woolly mullein

noun
tall woolly plant with yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli məˈlɪn / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˈmʌlɪn / (BrE)
A tall plant with densely packed yellow flowers and woolly leaves, historically used by the Greeks and Romans as funeral torches.

Examples

woolly mullein plant stalk
Ancient Romans once used woolly mullein stalks as torches in ceremonial processions.
Gardeners sometimes plant woolly mullein for its unique texture and striking appearance.
The hillside was dotted with woolly mullein, their yellow flowers standing tall among the grass.
