conversion hysteria

noun
disorder converting mental conflict into physical symptoms
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɜrʒən hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːʃən hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A psychological disorder in which emotional distress or mental conflict is unconsciously converted into physical symptoms such as paralysis or loss of sensation without a clear medical cause.

Examples

conversion hysteria causes paralysis
Although rare today, cases of conversion hysteria were once common in psychiatric hospitals.
Many early psychiatrists believed conversion hysteria was related to repressed trauma or conflicts.
The patient was diagnosed with conversion hysteria after developing unexplained paralysis in her right arm.
