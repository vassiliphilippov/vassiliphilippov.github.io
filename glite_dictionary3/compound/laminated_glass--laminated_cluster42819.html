laminated glass

noun
glass with plastic/resin layer to stop shattering
Pronunciation: / ˈlæmɪˌneɪtɪd ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈlæmɪneɪtɪd ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
glass made by sandwiching a layer of plastic or resin between two sheets of glass to prevent shattering

Examples

laminated glass window
New building codes require the use of laminated glass in all skylights.
The car's windshield is made of laminated glass for enhanced safety.
The museum installed laminated glass display cases to protect valuable artifacts.
