neuropterous insect

noun
insect with net-veined wings (order Neuroptera)
Pronunciation: / nuːˈrɑːptəɹəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / njʊəˈrɒptərəs ˈɪn.sɛkt / (BrE)
An insect with biting mouthparts and four large membranous wings marked with netlike veins, belonging to the order Neuroptera.

Examples

a neuropterous insect with netted wings
A neuropterous insect can often be identified by its large, transparent, net-veined wings.
Scientists study neuropterous insects like antlions and lacewings for their role in pest control.
The delicate wings of a neuropterous insect are covered in a fine network of veins.
