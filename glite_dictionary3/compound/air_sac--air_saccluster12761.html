air sac

noun
thin-walled respiratory extension in insects
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr ˌsæk / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌsæk / (BrE)
A thin-walled extension of the tracheae in insects that helps with respiration and air circulation within the body.

Examples

insect air sac
Damage to the air sac system can impair an insect's ability to breathe.
Many flying insects rely on air sacs to efficiently deliver oxygen throughout their bodies.
The structure of an air sac allows it to expand and contract with changing pressure.
