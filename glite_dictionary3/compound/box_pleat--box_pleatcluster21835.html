box pleat

noun
a type of fabric pleat with parallel folds
Pronunciation: / bɑks pliːt / (AmE), / bɒks pliːt / (BrE)
A flat double pleat in fabric, created by folding material under on both sides in opposite directions, forming a box-like appearance.

Examples

box pleat skirt
Box pleats are often used in uniforms to allow greater freedom of movement.
He learned how to sew a box pleat in his fashion design class last semester.
The skirt featured a wide box pleat down the front, giving it extra volume.
