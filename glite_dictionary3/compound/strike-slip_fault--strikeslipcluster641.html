strike-slip fault

noun
geological fault with horizontal movement
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪk ˌslɪp fɔlt / (AmE), / ˈstraɪk ˌslɪp fɔːlt / (BrE)
a geological fault in which the blocks of rock on either side move mainly in a horizontal direction, parallel to the fault line

Examples

major strike-slip fault
During an earthquake, movement along a strike-slip fault can cause significant horizontal displacement at the surface.
Geologists study the pattern of rocks on either side of a strike-slip fault to understand ancient tectonic activity.
The San Andreas Fault is a well-known example of a strike-slip fault in California.

  • synonym – fault: a fracture in the Earth's crust where movement has occurred
