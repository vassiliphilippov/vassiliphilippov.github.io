deep sleep

noun
low-power device sleep state
Pronunciation: / diːp sliːp / (AmE), / diːp sliːp / (BrE)
(in computing) a low-power sleep state in which a device or processor shuts down most functions to conserve energy while remaining able to wake quickly when needed.

Examples

device enters deep sleep
The fitness tracker automatically entered deep sleep after two hours of inactivity to save battery.
Developers test whether the IoT sensor wakes correctly from deep sleep when motion is detected.
After the update, the phone stayed in deep sleep during idle periods, significantly extending uptime.

Related Terms

  • synonym – low-power mode: a setting or state that reduces a device's energy consumption by limiting background activity
  • synonym – sleep mode: a power-saving state for electronic devices in which most activity is suspended but can be resumed quickly
