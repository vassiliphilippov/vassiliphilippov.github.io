plagal cadence

noun
musical ending: subdominant to tonic
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪɡl ˈkeɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡəl ˈkeɪd(ə)ns / (BrE)
a cadence in which the chord of the subdominant is followed by the chord of the tonic, often used to end church music

Examples

plagal cadence in church music
A plagal cadence moves from the subdominant to the tonic, distinguishing it from other cadences.
Many traditional church songs end on a plagal cadence, sometimes called the 'Amen cadence'.
The final hymn concluded with a plagal cadence that created a sense of solemnity.
