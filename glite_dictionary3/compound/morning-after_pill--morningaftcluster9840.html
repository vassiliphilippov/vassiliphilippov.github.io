morning-after pill

noun
emergency contraceptive pill
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔrnɪŋˈæftər pɪl / (AmE), / ˌmɔːnɪŋˈɑːftə pɪl / (BrE)
a pill taken after unprotected sexual intercourse to prevent pregnancy, usually containing a high dose of hormones and effective within a few days after intercourse

Examples

take the morning-after pill
After the accident with her usual contraception, she decided to take the morning-after pill.
Some people use the morning-after pill as a precaution after unprotected sex.
The pharmacy requires a consultation before dispensing the morning-after pill.
