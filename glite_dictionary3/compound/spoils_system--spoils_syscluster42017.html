spoils system

noun
system of favors in government hiring
Pronunciation: / spɔɪlz ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / spɔɪlz ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system of public employment based on rewarding party loyalists and friends by appointing them to government positions

Examples

the spoils system rewards supporters
After the election, the new administration quickly adopted the spoils system to reward their supporters.
Many critics argued that the spoils system led to widespread corruption and inefficiency in government.
The pendulum later swung toward merit-based hiring, replacing the spoils system with civil service reforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym – merit system: system of hiring and promoting based on ability and qualifications
