paroxysmal trepidant abasia

noun
abasia from leg spasticity
Pronunciation: / ˌpæɹəkˈsɪzməl ˌtrɛpɪˈdænt əˈbeɪziə / (AmE), / ˌpæɹəkˈsɪzməl ˌtrɛpɪˈdænt əˈbeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A form of abasia characterized by sudden attacks of inability to walk caused by spasticity of the legs.

Examples

paroxysmal trepidant abasia episode
Doctors examined her gait and concluded that her symptoms matched paroxysmal trepidant abasia due to leg spasticity.
Episodes of paroxysmal trepidant abasia can occur without warning, severely limiting the ability to walk.
The patient was diagnosed with paroxysmal trepidant abasia after experiencing sudden, uncontrollable stiffness in his legs.
