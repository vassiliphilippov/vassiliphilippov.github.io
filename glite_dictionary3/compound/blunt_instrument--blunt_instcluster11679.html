blunt instrument

noun
action or tool lacking precision
Pronunciation: / blʌnt ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / blʌnt ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (BrE)
a tool, action, or method that lacks precision and subtlety, often achieving results in a rough or indiscriminate way

Examples

a blunt instrument for change
He argued that the law was a blunt instrument when what was needed was a more surgical solution.
The government's tax policy was criticized as a blunt instrument that hurt the poor and the rich alike.
Using a blunt instrument approach to solve complex problems often leads to unintended consequences.

Related Terms

  • antonym – scalpel: a small and precise instrument, used figuratively for precise actions
  • synonym – sledgehammer: a heavy tool, also used figuratively for forceful, unrefined approaches
