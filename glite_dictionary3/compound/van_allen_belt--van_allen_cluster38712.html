Van Allen belt

noun
zone of charged particles around the Earth
Pronunciation: / væn ˈælən bɛlt / (AmE), / væn ˈælən bɛlt / (BrE)
A zone of charged particles held by Earth's magnetic field, forming belts encircling the planet above the atmosphere.

Examples

Earth's Van Allen belt
Earth's Van Allen belt protects us from harmful solar wind particles.
Satellites passing through the Van Allen belt must be shielded from intense radiation.
The discovery of the Van Allen belt was a major milestone in space science.
