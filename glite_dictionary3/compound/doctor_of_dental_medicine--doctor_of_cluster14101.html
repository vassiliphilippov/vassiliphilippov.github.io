Doctor of Dental Medicine

noun
degree qualifying one to practice dentistry
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktər əv ˈdɛntəl ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ˈdɛntəl ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
A professional doctoral degree awarded for studies in dental medicine, qualifying the holder to practice as a dentist.

Examples

Doctor of Dental Medicine degree
A Doctor of Dental Medicine is qualified to diagnose and treat oral diseases.
After years of study, she received her Doctor of Dental Medicine degree from Harvard.
The clinic only hires dentists with a Doctor of Dental Medicine or equivalent.
