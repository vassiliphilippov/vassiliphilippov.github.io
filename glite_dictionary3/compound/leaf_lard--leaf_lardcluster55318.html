leaf lard

noun
high-quality pork fat from around hog kidneys
Pronunciation: / lif lɑrd / (AmE), / liːf lɑːd / (BrE)
the soft, high-quality fat located around the kidneys and abdomen of a hog, often used in baking for its mild flavor and texture

Examples

leaf lard for pie crusts
The baker sourced leaf lard from a local farm to enhance the flavor of his pastries.
Traditional pie crust recipes often call for leaf lard because of its superior texture.
Unlike regular lard, leaf lard has a neutral taste, making it ideal for delicate baked goods.
