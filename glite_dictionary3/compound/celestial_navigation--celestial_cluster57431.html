celestial navigation

noun
navigation using stars and celestial bodies
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛstʃəl ˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstɪəl ˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining one's position and course by observing the positions of celestial bodies, such as stars, the Sun, and the Moon

Examples

celestial navigation at sea
Before GPS technology, sailors relied on celestial navigation to cross the oceans safely.
He mastered celestial navigation during his years in the merchant navy.
Modern yachtsmen sometimes practice celestial navigation as a backup to electronic systems.
