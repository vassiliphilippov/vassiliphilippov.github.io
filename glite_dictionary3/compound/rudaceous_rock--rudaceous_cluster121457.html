rudaceous rock

noun
sedimentary rock of coarse-grained material
Pronunciation: / ruˈdeɪʃəs rɑk / (AmE), / ruˈdeɪsɪəs rɒk / (BrE)
A sedimentary rock consisting of coarse-grained material, such as fragments larger than sand grains.

Examples

rudaceous rock with large pebbles
A rudaceous rock is typically formed in environments with strong water currents, like rivers or beaches.
Coarse fragments in a rudaceous rock can sometimes be identified with the naked eye.
The geologists found a layer of rudaceous rock beneath the riverbed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conglomerate: sedimentary rock made of rounded coarse fragments cemented together
  • degree lower – sandstone: sedimentary rock consisting mainly of sand-sized minerals or rock grains
