rubber-hose cryptanalysis

noun
coercive technique to extract cryptographic secrets
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌbər hoʊz ˌkrɪptəˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə həʊz ˌkrɪptəˈnaləsɪs / (BrE)
The use of coercion or violence, rather than technical methods, to force someone to reveal cryptographic secrets such as passwords or encryption keys.

Examples

rubber-hose cryptanalysis to get passwords
Discussions of rubber-hose cryptanalysis highlight the limits of relying solely on technical security measures.
Even the most secure encryption is powerless against rubber-hose cryptanalysis.
The company trains its staff on how to avoid rubber-hose cryptanalysis situations in case of a security breach.

Related Terms

  • antonym – brute-force attack: trying all possible keys to break cryptography
  • synonym – cryptanalysis: the art or process of deciphering coded messages
