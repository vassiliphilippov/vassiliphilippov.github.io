central heating

noun
system for heating buildings
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəl ˈhitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
A system for heating a building in which air or water is warmed in one place and distributed throughout the building via pipes, vents, or radiators.

Examples

central heating system
Most modern homes in the UK are equipped with central heating.
We had to replace the central heating last winter because it stopped working.
When the central heating comes on, the whole house warms up quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – radiator: a device that radiates heat from hot water or steam
