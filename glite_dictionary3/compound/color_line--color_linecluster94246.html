color line

noun
barrier separating Black and white people
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər laɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə laɪn / (BrE)
a social or legal barrier preventing Black people from participating equally with white people in various activities

Examples

Baseball legend Jackie Robinson’s career helped to challenge the color line in professional sports.
Many civil rights activists fought to break down the color line in public schools.
The existence of a strict color line deeply affected social relations in the early twentieth century.
