memory loss

noun
partial or total inability to remember
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛməri lɔs / (AmE), / ˈmɛməri lɒs / (BrE)
the condition of being unable to remember information or past experiences, either partially or completely

Examples

suffer memory loss
After the accident, he began to experience memory loss and confusion.
Some medications may have side effects that include memory loss.
The patient's memory loss was gradual, making it difficult for his family to notice at first.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amnesia: partial or total loss of memory, often caused by injury or trauma
