Carlovingian dynasty

noun
Frankish royal dynasty (751–987)
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːr.loʊˈvɪn.dʒi.ən ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.ləˈvɪn.dʒɪ.ən ˈdɪ.nəs.ti / (BrE)
the Frankish ruling family founded by Charles Martel and notably led by Charlemagne, governing from 751 to 987

Examples

the Carlovingian dynasty ruled France
By the end of the 10th century, the Carlovingian dynasty had been replaced by the Capetian dynasty.
Charlemagne was the most famous ruler of the Carlovingian dynasty.
The Carlovingian dynasty played a crucial role in shaping the medieval history of Western Europe.
