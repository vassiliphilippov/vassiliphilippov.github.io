spring cankerworm

noun
caterpillar of Paleacrita vernata
Pronunciation: / sprɪŋ ˈkæŋkərˌwɜrm / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈkaŋkəˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A species of looper caterpillar, the larva of the moth Paleacrita vernata, known for its variable coloration and as a pest of deciduous trees, especially in spring.

Examples

spring cankerworm damages trees
Damage by the spring cankerworm can be minimized with timely pest management.
Orchard owners are concerned about the increasing population of spring cankerworms this year.
The spring cankerworm often causes noticeable defoliation of elm and maple trees in early spring.
