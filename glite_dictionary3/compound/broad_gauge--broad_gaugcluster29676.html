broad gauge

noun
railway track wider than standard gauge
Pronunciation: / brɔd ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɔːd ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a railway track with a width greater than the standard gauge of 56.5 inches (1,435 mm) between the rails

Examples

broad gauge railway track
Converting from narrow gauge to broad gauge improved the line's capacity and stability.
India has one of the world's largest networks of broad gauge railways.
The new high-speed train operates on broad gauge tracks to accommodate larger carriages.

Related Terms

  • antonym – narrow gauge: a railway track narrower than the standard gauge
  • antonym – standard gauge: the standard width of railway tracks (56.5 inches/1,435 mm)
