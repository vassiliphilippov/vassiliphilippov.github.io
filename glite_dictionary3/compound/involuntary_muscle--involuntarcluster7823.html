involuntary muscle

noun
a muscle that contracts automatically, not under conscious control
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɑːlənˌtɛri ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlənˌtɛri ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
A muscle that contracts without conscious control, found in the walls of internal organs such as the stomach, intestines, bladder, and blood vessels, but not the heart.

Examples

involuntary muscle movement
Damage to the bladder's involuntary muscle can cause problems with urination.
The walls of the intestine are lined with involuntary muscle that aids digestion.
Unlike your biceps, an involuntary muscle works without you thinking about it.

Related Terms

  • antonym – skeletal muscle: muscle attached to bones and under voluntary control
  • synonym – smooth muscle: muscle tissue in organs not under voluntary control
