empty tomb

noun
a commemorative monument with no body inside
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmpti tuːm / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti təʊm / (BrE)
a monument erected in honor of a person whose remains lie elsewhere or cannot be found or recovered

Examples

a famous empty tomb
A famous empty tomb stands in the center of the square as a symbol of remembrance.
Many visitors pay their respects at the empty tomb, even though the person's remains are buried elsewhere.
The city constructed an empty tomb to commemorate the soldiers lost at sea whose bodies were never recovered.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cenotaph: a monument built to honor people whose remains are elsewhere
