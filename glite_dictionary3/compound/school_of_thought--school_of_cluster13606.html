school of thought

noun
group's shared set of beliefs or approach
Pronunciation: / skuːl əv ˈθɔt / (AmE), / skuːl əv ˈθɒt / (BrE)
a particular way of thinking, or set of beliefs, shared by a group, especially in scholarly or professional contexts

Examples

Keynesian school of thought
In economics, the Keynesian school of thought advocates for government intervention in markets.
Some experts belong to the traditional school of thought, while others favor more modern approaches.
There is more than one school of thought regarding the best way to teach children.
