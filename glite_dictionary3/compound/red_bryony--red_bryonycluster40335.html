red bryony

noun
European climbing plant with red berries
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˈbraɪ.oʊ.ni / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˈbraɪ.əni / (BrE)
A climbing plant of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, with fleshy roots, pale green flowers, and small red berries.

Examples

red bryony climbing hedges
Red bryony climbs hedges and bushes, producing small clusters of red berries in late summer.
Gardeners should be cautious, as red bryony berries are considered toxic if ingested.
