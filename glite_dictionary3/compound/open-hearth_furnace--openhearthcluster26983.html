open-hearth furnace

noun
furnace for making steel using burning gas and hot air
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.pən ˈhɑrθ ˌfɝː.nəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.pən ˈhɑːθ ˌfɜː.nɪs / (BrE)
a furnace used in steelmaking, where steel is placed on a shallow hearth and heated by flames of burning gas and hot air above it
