beta-adrenergic receptor

noun
receptor responding to adrenaline in heart and vessels
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪ.t̬ə əˌdrɛ.nərˈdʒɪk rɪˈsɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌbiː.tə əˌdrɛn.əˈdʒɛt.ɪk rɪˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
a receptor on certain cells, especially in the heart and blood vessels, that responds to the hormone adrenaline and similar substances, producing effects such as increased heart rate and vasodilation

Examples

beta-adrenergic receptor in the heart
Drugs that block the beta-adrenergic receptor are commonly used to treat high blood pressure.
Researchers identified a mutation in the beta-adrenergic receptor affecting cardiac response.
Stimulation of the beta-adrenergic receptor leads to increased heart rate and relaxation of airway muscles.
