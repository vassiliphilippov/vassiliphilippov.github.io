white lotus

noun
a white-flowered Egyptian water lily
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈloʊ.təs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
A water lily (Nymphaea lotus), native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, with white fragrant flowers, held sacred by the ancient Egyptians.

white lotus flower blooms
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the white lotus found along the Nile.

The ancient Egyptians revered the white lotus as a symbol of purity and rebirth.
