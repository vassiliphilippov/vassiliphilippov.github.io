borscht belt

noun
resort area in the Catskills, New York, with Jewish heritage
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrʃt ˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːʃt ˌbɛlt / (BrE)
(informal) A region in the Catskill Mountains of New York, known for its resorts frequented by Jewish vacationers from the 1920s to the 1970s, and noted for its impact on popular entertainment, especially comedy.

Examples

the borscht belt resorts
Many famous comedians began their careers performing in the borscht belt resorts.
Numerous movies and books pay tribute to the humor and spirit of the borscht belt.
The decline of the borscht belt marked the end of a unique era in American vacation culture.
