disseminated sclerosis

noun
chronic nervous system disorder; multiple sclerosis
Pronunciation: / dɪˌsɛm.ɪˈneɪ.tɪd sklɚˈroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛm.ɪˈneɪ.tɪd sklɪˈrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A chronic and progressive disorder of the nervous system in which the myelin sheath around certain nerve fibers is lost, leading to neurological symptoms; more commonly known as multiple sclerosis.
