original version

noun
the first or unaltered form of a work
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪdʒənl vɚˈʒən / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəl ˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the first or unaltered form of a work, especially before translation, adaptation, or modification

Examples

the original version of a book
Please read the original version of the novel before watching the adaptation.
She prefers to watch anime in the original version with subtitles.
The film was much better in its original version than in the dubbed release.

Related Terms

  • antonym – adaptation: a changed form of an original work, often for a different medium or audience
  • antonym – dub: a version of a film or show with audio translated and re-recorded in another language
  • antonym – translation: a version of a work rendered in a different language
