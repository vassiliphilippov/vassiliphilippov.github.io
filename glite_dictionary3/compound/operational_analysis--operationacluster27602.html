operational analysis

noun
mathematical method transforming equations
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənl əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
a mathematical method in which problems, particularly differential equations, are converted into algebraic problems, often involving the solution of polynomial equations

Examples

operational analysis of equations
Operational analysis is widely used in engineering to simplify complex differential equation problems.
By translating the issue into a polynomial equation, operational analysis can make difficult problems more manageable.
Oliver Heaviside made significant contributions to operational analysis in the field of electrical circuits.
