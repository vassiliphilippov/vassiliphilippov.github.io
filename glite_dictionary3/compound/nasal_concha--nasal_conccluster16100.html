nasal concha

noun
thin bone in the nasal cavity
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪzəl ˈkɑːn.kə / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.zəl ˈkɒŋ.kə / (BrE)
any of several thin, curved bones that project from the lateral walls of the nasal cavity, helping to form its structure and increase surface area for air filtration.

Examples

nasal concha bone structure
Inflammation of the nasal concha can cause breathing difficulties.
The inferior nasal concha is the largest of the three turbinate bones.
The surgeon carefully avoided the nasal concha during the procedure.
