perfect storm

noun
multiple crises occurring together
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜr.fɪkt stɔrm / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt stɔːm / (BrE)
a situation in which several negative events or conditions happen at the same time, producing an especially bad result

Examples

a perfect storm of problems
A perfect storm of bad weather and poor planning led to the event's cancellation.
The company suffered a perfect storm of technical failures and staffing shortages.
We were caught in a perfect storm of rising costs and decreased demand.
