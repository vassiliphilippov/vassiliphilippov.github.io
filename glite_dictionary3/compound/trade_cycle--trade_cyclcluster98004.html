trade cycle

noun
recurring fluctuations in economic activity
Pronunciation: / treɪd ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / treɪd ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a recurring pattern of fluctuations in economic activity, including periods of recession, recovery, growth, and decline

Examples

the trade cycle repeats
Economists study the trade cycle to understand periods of economic boom and bust.
The global recession was part of a larger trade cycle affecting many countries.
The government introduced policies to minimize the impact of the trade cycle on employment.
