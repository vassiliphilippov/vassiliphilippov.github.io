sodium silicate

noun
a soluble glassy compound for cement and coatings
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm ˈsɪlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈsɪlɪkeɪt / (BrE)
A soluble, glassy, and often viscous compound of sodium oxide and silica, used as a cement, protective coating, or preservative.

Examples

sodium silicate solution
A solution of sodium silicate helps preserve eggs for a longer period.
Engineers sometimes refer to sodium silicate as 'liquid glass' due to its appearance and properties.
The workshop used sodium silicate as a sealant for the concrete floor.
