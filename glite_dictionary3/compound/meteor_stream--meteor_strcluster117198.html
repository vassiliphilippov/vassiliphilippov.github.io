meteor stream

noun
group of meteoroids causing meteor showers
Pronunciation: / ˈmitiɚ strim / (AmE), / ˈmiːtiə striːm / (BrE)
A group of meteoroids traveling together in space, which can produce a transient meteor shower when entering the Earth's atmosphere.

Examples

meteor stream in space
As Earth passes through a meteor stream, we experience an increase in meteors visible at night.
Astronomers study the orbit of the meteor stream to predict future meteor showers.
The Perseids are a well-known meteor stream that appears every August.

Related Terms

  • synonym – meteor shower: a visible event when many meteors are seen in the sky at once
