herbaceous plant

noun
plant without a persistent woody stem
Pronunciation: / ɝːˈbeɪʃəs plænt / (AmE), / hɜːˈbeɪʃəs plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that does not develop a persistent woody stem above ground, often dying back at the end of the growing season.

Examples

garden herbaceous plant
Most garden flowers, such as tulips and daffodils, are considered herbaceous plants.
Some herbaceous plants are used as culinary herbs or for medicinal purposes.
Unlike trees, herbaceous plants generally die back to the ground each winter.
