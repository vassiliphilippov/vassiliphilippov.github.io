silent partner

noun
non-managing, non-public business investor
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪlənt ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
A person who invests in a business partnership but does not take part in its management and whose association is not publicly disclosed.

Examples

silent partner invests money
After investing half a million dollars, he became a silent partner in the tech startup.
It's common for celebrities to act as a silent partner in new businesses.
The restaurant's silent partner never attends meetings or interacts with customers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sleeping partner: A partner who contributes capital to a business but does not participate in management
