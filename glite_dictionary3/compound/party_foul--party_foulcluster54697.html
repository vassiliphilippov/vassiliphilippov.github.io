party foul

noun
social blunder at a party
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrti faʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti faʊl / (BrE)
an action or mistake at a social gathering that is considered inappropriate, disruptive, or embarrassing

Examples

commit a party foul
Bringing store-bought cookies to a homemade dessert competition is a classic party foul.
Forgetting to refill the ice bucket can quickly become a party foul during a summer barbecue.
She realized she had committed a party foul by accidentally knocking over several cups.
