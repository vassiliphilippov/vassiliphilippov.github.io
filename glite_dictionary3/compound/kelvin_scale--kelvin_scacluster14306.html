Kelvin scale

noun
absolute temperature scale used in science
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛlvɪn skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɛlvɪn skeɪl / (BrE)
a temperature scale where absolute zero is 0 kelvins, and each unit is equal to one degree Celsius; commonly used in scientific measurements

Examples

the Kelvin scale in science
Physicists prefer to use the Kelvin scale when discussing thermodynamic laws.
Temperatures on the Kelvin scale start at absolute zero, the theoretical point where all motion stops.
When reporting their results, the researchers converted Celsius readings to the Kelvin scale.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Celsius scale: temperature scale with water freezing at 0°C and boiling at 100°C
