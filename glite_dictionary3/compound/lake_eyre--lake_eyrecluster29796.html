Lake Eyre

proper noun
largest salt lake in Australia
Pronunciation: / leɪk ɛər / (AmE), / leɪk ɛə / (BrE)
a shallow salt lake in south central Australia, about 35 feet (11 metres) below sea level; the country's largest lake and lowest point on the continent

Rainfall in the region can cause Lake Eyre to briefly fill with water, attracting many species of birds.

When Lake Eyre is dry, it becomes an expansive salt pan visible from space.
