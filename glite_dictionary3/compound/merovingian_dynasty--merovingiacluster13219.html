Merovingian dynasty

noun
early Frankish royal family (c. 500–750 AD)
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛr.əˈvɪn.dʒi.ən ˈdaɪ.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˌmɛr.əˈvɪn.dʒi.ən ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
the Frankish royal family that ruled parts of present-day France and Germany from about 500 to 750 AD, founded by Clovis I.

Examples

the Merovingian dynasty ruled France
After the fall of the Merovingian dynasty, the Carolingians rose to power in the Frankish kingdom.
Many archaeological finds have been linked to the reign of the Merovingian dynasty.
The Merovingian dynasty played a crucial role in shaping the early medieval history of France and Germany.
