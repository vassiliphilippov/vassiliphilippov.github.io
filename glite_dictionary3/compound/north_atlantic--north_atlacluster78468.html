North Atlantic

noun
northern part of the Atlantic Ocean
Pronunciation: / nɔrθ ətˈlæntɪk / (AmE), / nɔːθ ətˈlæntɪk / (BrE)
the part of the Atlantic Ocean that lies north of the equator, between Europe/Africa and the Americas.

Examples

the North Atlantic Ocean
Many transatlantic flights cross the North Atlantic between North America and Europe.
The North Atlantic is known for its strong ocean currents and unpredictable weather.
The Titanic sank in the icy waters of the North Atlantic in 1912.
