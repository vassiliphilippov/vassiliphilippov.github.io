sodium hydrogen carbonate

noun
a compound used in baking and medicine
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm haɪˈdrɑdʒən kɑrˈbɒneɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm haɪˈdrɒdʒən kɑːˈbɒneɪt / (BrE)
a white, soluble chemical compound (NaHCO₃) used in baking powders, effervescent drinks, and as an antacid

Examples

sodium hydrogen carbonate in baking
Sodium hydrogen carbonate is often used in baking as a leavening agent to help dough rise.
Adding sodium hydrogen carbonate to water produces a fizzing reaction due to the release of carbon dioxide.
Doctors sometimes recommend sodium hydrogen carbonate as an antacid to relieve heartburn.
