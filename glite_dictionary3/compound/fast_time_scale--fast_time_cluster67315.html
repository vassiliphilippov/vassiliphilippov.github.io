fast time scale

noun
simulation time scale compressed relative to real time
Pronunciation: / fæst taɪm skeɪl / (AmE), / fɑːst taɪm skeɪl / (BrE)
a time scale used in simulations or data processing in which the simulated time is compressed relative to real time, typically by a factor less than one

Examples

fast time scale simulation
The simulation was run on a fast time scale to observe long-term effects quickly.
To meet the project's deadline, they had to process the data on a fast time scale.
Using a fast time scale, researchers can study processes that would otherwise take years in reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym – slow time scale: a time scale where the simulated time is stretched relative to real time, typically with a factor greater than one
