Arctic ground squirrel

noun
large northern North American ground squirrel
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrtɪk ɡraʊnd ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪk ɡraʊnd ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a large species of ground squirrel native to the far northern regions of North America, known for its burrowing habits and long hibernation period

Examples

Arctic ground squirrel hibernation
The Arctic ground squirrel hibernates for up to eight months during the harsh northern winter.
The Arctic ground squirrel is well adapted to cold climates and extreme environments.
We saw a group of Arctic ground squirrels gathering seeds near the tundra.
