crimson tide

noun
a wave of deep red color; metaphorically, intense surge
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪmzən taɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmzən tʌɪd / (BrE)
A wave or flood of deep red color; also used metaphorically to describe a surge of intense emotion or enthusiasm.

Examples

a crimson tide of color
A crimson tide swept across the sky during the spectacular sunset.
The city was caught up in a crimson tide of celebration after the championship win.
The poet described her anger as a crimson tide rising within her.
