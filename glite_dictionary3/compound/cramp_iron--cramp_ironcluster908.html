cramp iron

noun
metal strip for securing masonry
Pronunciation: / kræmp ˈaɪ.ɚn / (AmE), / kræmp ˈaɪ.ən / (BrE)
a metal strip with the ends bent at right angles, used to hold pieces of masonry together

Examples

cramp iron joins stones
A cramp iron helps to prevent the movement of large masonry stones in historic buildings.
Over time, the cramp irons in the old wall became rusty and needed to be replaced.
The stone blocks were fastened securely with a cramp iron.
