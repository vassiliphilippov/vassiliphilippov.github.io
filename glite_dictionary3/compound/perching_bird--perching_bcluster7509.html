perching bird

noun
bird adapted for perching
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːtʃɪŋ bɝːd / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃɪŋ bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird with feet adapted for grasping and perching, especially on tree branches; once classified under the order Passeriformes.

Examples

perching bird on a branch
Most perching birds have three toes facing forward and one facing backward to grip branches.
Scientists once classified the majority of songbirds as members of the perching bird order.
The robin, a common perching bird, is often seen hopping along garden fences.
