Bible-worship

noun
worship or excessive reverence of the Bible
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪbəl ˌwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbaɪbl̩ ˌwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
The practice of revering or treating the Bible as an object of worship rather than as a religious text to be studied and followed.

Examples

Bible-worship in churches
He warned against Bible-worship, insisting that the spirit of the text was more important than the words themselves.
Some critics accuse certain denominations of Bible-worship rather than genuine faith.
The movement grew concerned about the rise of Bible-worship in certain religious circles.
