lachrymal duct

noun
duct carrying tears from the eye gland
Pronunciation: / ˈlækrəməl dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈlakrɪməl dʌkt / (BrE)
A small tube that carries tears from the lacrimal glands to the surface of the eye.

Examples

lachrymal duct blockage
A blockage in the lachrymal duct can cause excessive tearing and discomfort.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the lachrymal duct.
The ophthalmologist examined the patient's lachrymal duct for signs of infection.

  • synonym – lacrimal gland: gland that produces tears
