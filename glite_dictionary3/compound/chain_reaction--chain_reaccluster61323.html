chain reaction

noun
self-sustaining nuclear reaction process
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (AmE), / tʃeɪn riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a process in which each event causes another similar event, especially in nuclear or chemical reactions where the product of one reaction starts the next

Examples

start a chain reaction
A single neutron can start a chain reaction that releases enormous amounts of energy.
Scientists carefully monitor the chain reaction to prevent it from getting out of control.
The explosion was caused by a chain reaction set off in the nuclear reactor.
Polymerization often proceeds through a chain reaction mechanism in which active sites propagate the process.
Scientists carefully control conditions in reactors to maintain a safe chain reaction.
The nuclear explosion was the result of a self-sustaining chain reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym – nuclear fission: the splitting of a nucleus into smaller parts accompanied by energy release
