weather vane

noun
rotating device showing wind direction
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛðɚ veɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə veɪn / (BrE)
an instrument fixed on a high point, such as a roof, that turns to indicate the direction from which the wind is blowing

Examples

weather vane on a roof
A sudden gust of wind spun the weather vane around to the north.
By watching the weather vane, sailors could easily tell which way the wind was blowing.
The old farmhouse had a rusty weather vane shaped like a rooster on its roof.
