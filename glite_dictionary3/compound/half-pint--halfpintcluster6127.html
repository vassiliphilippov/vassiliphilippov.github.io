half-pint

noun
a small or short person (derogatory)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæfˌpaɪnt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌpaɪnt / (BrE)
A mildly disparaging term for a small or short person, especially a child.

Examples

little half-pint
Don’t let that half-pint fool you—she’s stronger than she looks.
He was always called half-pint at school because of his short stature.
She found it rude when her uncle referred to her as a half-pint at the party.

Related Terms

  • antonym – giant: a person of great size
  • synonym – runt: a person who is small and weak
