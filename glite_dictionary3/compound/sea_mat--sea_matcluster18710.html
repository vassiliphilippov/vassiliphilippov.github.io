sea mat

noun
colonial aquatic animal (bryozoan) forming moss-like growths
Pronunciation: / siː mæt / (AmE), / siː mæt / (BrE)
A colonial aquatic animal with moss-like growths formed by sessile polyps, often found attached to stones or seaweed, and reproducing by budding.

Examples

sea mat on a rock
Divers found a large sea mat attached to a rock near the shore.
Many marine biologists study the growth patterns of the sea mat for ecological research.
The sea mat colonies can resemble patches of underwater moss.
