cytophilic antibody

noun
antibody with affinity for certain cells
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪ.toʊˈfɪl.ɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɑː.di / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.təʊˈfɪl.ɪk ˈæn.tɪˌbɒd.i / (BrE)
an antibody that binds to certain cells due to the properties of its Fc portion, independently of its specific antigen affinity

Examples

cytophilic antibody binds cells
Researchers observed that the cytophilic antibody bound more effectively to specific cell types.

The presence of cytophilic antibody is a key factor in certain immune responses.
