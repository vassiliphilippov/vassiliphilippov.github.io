conditioned avoidance response

noun
learned response to avoid a predicted threat
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənd əˈvɔɪdəns rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənd əˈvɔɪdəns rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a learned response in which an organism takes action to avoid a predicted negative or unpleasant stimulus, typically after repeated pairings of a cue with the aversive event

Examples

conditioned avoidance response to danger
In therapy, understanding a patient's conditioned avoidance response is crucial to addressing anxiety-driven behaviors.
Researchers observed a conditioned avoidance response after repeated exposure to a signal predicting a mild electric shock.
The rat demonstrated a conditioned avoidance response by jumping onto the platform when it heard the warning tone.
