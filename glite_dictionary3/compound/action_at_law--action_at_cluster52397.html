action at law

noun
legal lawsuit or court proceeding
Pronunciation: / ˌækʃən æt lɔ / (AmE), / ˌækʃən ət lɔː / (BrE)
a judicial proceeding in which one party sues another to enforce or protect a right, or to prevent or redress a wrong, in a court of law

Examples

file an action at law
He advised his client to take an action at law rather than settling out of court.
Several actions at law were filed after the breach of contract was discovered.
The company initiated an action at law to recover damages from the contractor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – criminal proceeding: legal process against someone accused of a crime
  • synonym – lawsuit: a claim or dispute brought to a law court for resolution
