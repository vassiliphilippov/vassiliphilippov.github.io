to read the green

verb
assess a golf putting surface's slope
Pronunciation: / rid ðə ɡrin / (AmE), / riːd ðə ɡriːn / (BrE)
To assess the surface and slope of a golf putting green in order to determine how to strike a putt.

Examples

read the green before putting
Golfers must read the green to judge the direction and speed of their putt.
He took his time to read the green before making a decisive stroke.
Professional players are skilled at reading the green under various weather conditions.
