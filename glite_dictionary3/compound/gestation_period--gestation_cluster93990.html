gestation period

noun
length of time an embryo develops before birth
Pronunciation: / dʒɛˈsteɪʃən ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / dʒɛˈsteɪʃən ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the length of time during which an embryo or fetus develops inside its mother, from conception until birth

Examples

elephant gestation period
Doctors closely monitor the gestation period to ensure the health of both mother and child.
Each animal species has its own typical gestation period, which can vary greatly.
The gestation period for elephants is nearly two years, making it one of the longest among mammals.
