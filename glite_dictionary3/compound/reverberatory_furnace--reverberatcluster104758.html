reverberatory furnace

noun
type of indirect-heating furnace
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝː.bɚˌtɔːr.i ˈfɝː.nɪs / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜː.bər.ə.tər.i ˈfɜː.nɪs / (BrE)
a type of furnace in which material is heated indirectly by flames directed at the roof and walls, rather than directly on the material

Examples

smelt ore in a reverberatory furnace
Because the material is not in contact with the flames, a reverberatory furnace allows for better temperature control.
Many early steelworks used the reverberatory furnace method before more efficient technologies emerged.
The copper ore was smelted in a reverberatory furnace to extract pure metal.
