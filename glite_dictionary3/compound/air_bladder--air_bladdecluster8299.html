air bladder

noun
buoyancy sac in fish
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr ˌblædər / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌbladə / (BrE)
an air-filled sac located near the spinal column in many fish species that helps them control buoyancy in the water

Examples

fish air bladder
A ruptured air bladder can cause a fish to lose its balance and float awkwardly.
Many fish use their air bladder to stay at a certain depth without expending energy.
The development of the air bladder was a major adaptation in the evolution of bony fish.

  • synonym – swim bladder: the same organ, also called a swim bladder
