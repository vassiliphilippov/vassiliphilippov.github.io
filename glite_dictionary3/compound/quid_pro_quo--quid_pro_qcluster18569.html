quid pro quo

noun
something given in exchange for something else
Pronunciation: / kwɪd proʊ ˈkwoʊ / (AmE), / kwɪd prəʊ ˈkwəʊ / (BrE)
something that is given or received in exchange for something else, especially as part of an agreement or deal.

Examples

quid pro quo deal
He offered to fix the car as a quid pro quo for her helping him move.
In politics, a quid pro quo can sometimes cross ethical lines if it involves favors for votes.
The agreement was a clear quid pro quo where each party got what they wanted.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exchange: act of giving one thing and receiving another in return
  • synonym – reciprocity: practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit
