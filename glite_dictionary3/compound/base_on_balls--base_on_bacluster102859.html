base on balls

noun
advance to first base after four balls (baseball)
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪs ɑn ˈbɔlz / (AmE), / ˌbeɪs ɒn ˈbɔːlz / (BrE)
(Baseball) an advance to first base by a batter who receives four balls thrown by the pitcher outside the strike zone.

Examples

base on balls in baseball
He led the league in bases on balls last season.
The batter earned a base on balls after four pitches missed the strike zone.
The pitcher lost his control and gave up a crucial base on balls in the ninth inning.
