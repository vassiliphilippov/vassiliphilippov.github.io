majority opinion

noun
official court decision agreed by most judges
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒɔrəti əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒrəti əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
the judicial opinion agreed to by more than half of the members of a court, stating the reasoning and decision of the court

Examples

majority opinion of the court
Justice Brown wrote the majority opinion in the historic case.
The majority opinion set a new legal precedent.
The Supreme Court issued its majority opinion after months of deliberation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – concurring opinion: an opinion that agrees with the majority's conclusion but offers different reasoning
  • antonym – dissenting opinion: an opinion in a legal case written by one or more judges expressing disagreement with the majority
