First Lateran Council

proper noun
1123 Western Church council at the Lateran Palace
Pronunciation: / ˌfɝst ˌlætəˌræn ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˌlætəˈran ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
the first ecumenical council of the Western Church, held in the Lateran Palace in 1123, which addressed church discipline and discussed efforts to reclaim the Holy Land.

Examples

the First Lateran Council met in Rome
At the First Lateran Council, church leaders discussed strategies to organize a new crusade.
Many decrees issued by the First Lateran Council influenced Western canon law for centuries.
The First Lateran Council marked a significant turning point in the history of church governance.
