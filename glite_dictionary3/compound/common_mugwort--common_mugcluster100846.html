common mugwort

noun
European aromatic herb (Artemisia vulgaris)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈmʌɡˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmʌɡˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A European perennial herb (Artemisia vulgaris) with hairy red or purple stems and dark green leaves that are downy white underneath and bear red-brown florets.

Gardeners often find common mugwort growing wild along roadsides and riverbanks.
The common mugwort blooms with small, reddish-brown flowers in late summer.
