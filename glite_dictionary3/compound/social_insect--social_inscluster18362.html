social insect

noun
insect that lives in colonies
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈɪnsekt / (BrE)
an insect that lives in a colony with others of the same species, exhibiting organized social behavior

Examples

social insect colony
Ants are a classic example of social insects that work together in highly organized colonies.
Bees are social insects known for their complex behaviors and communication.
Most wasps are solitary, but some species are social insects forming communal nests.
