Battle of Lepanto

proper noun
1571 naval battle; defeat of Ottoman fleet
Pronunciation: / ˈbætəl əv ləˈpɑnˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv lɛˈpæntəʊ / (BrE)
A naval battle fought in 1571 in which a fleet of the Holy League, organized by the Pope, defeated the Ottoman Empire, marking the end of Ottoman naval dominance in the Mediterranean.

Examples

the Battle of Lepanto in 1571
Many historians consider the Battle of Lepanto pivotal in halting Ottoman expansion into Europe.
Paintings of the Battle of Lepanto can be found in several European museums.
The Battle of Lepanto marked a turning point in Mediterranean naval power.
