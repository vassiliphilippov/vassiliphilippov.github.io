cat's-paw

noun
person used by another
Pronunciation: / ˈkæts pɔ / (AmE), / ˈkats pɔː / (BrE)
A person who is used by another to achieve their own purpose, often unwittingly.

Examples

used as a cat's-paw
He realized too late that he had been a cat's-paw in their political scheme.
She refuses to be anyone’s cat's-paw and blindly follow orders.
The young assistant was merely a cat's-paw for the unscrupulous manager.
