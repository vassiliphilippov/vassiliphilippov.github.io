tussock moth

noun
moth with hairy larvae that eat tree leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌsək mɒθ / (AmE), / ˈtʌsək mɒθ / (BrE)
A dull-colored moth whose larvae have tufts of hair on their bodies and feed on the leaves of many deciduous trees.

Examples

tussock moth caterpillar
Many gardeners dread the arrival of tussock moth caterpillars because they can defoliate entire trees.
Several species of tussock moth are considered pests in apple and oak forests.
The caterpillars of the tussock moth are easily identified by the tufts of bristly hair on their backs.
