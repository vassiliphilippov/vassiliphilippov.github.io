show trial

noun
public trial with predetermined verdict
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊ ˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
a trial arranged primarily for public display, where the verdict is predetermined and the accused's guilt has usually been decided in advance

Examples

political show trial
History is full of show trials where the accused never stood a chance of acquittal.
International observers condemned the show trial as a violation of human rights.
The dictator staged a show trial to convince the world that justice had been served.

Related Terms

  • synonym – kangaroo court: an unofficial court that disregards legal standards
