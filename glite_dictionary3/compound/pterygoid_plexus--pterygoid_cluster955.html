pterygoid plexus

noun
network of veins near the pterygoid muscles
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛr.ɪˌɡɔɪd ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪˌɡɔɪd ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a network of veins located near the pterygoid muscles, draining blood from that area into the internal maxillary and anterior facial veins

Examples

the pterygoid plexus of veins
An infection can spread from the teeth into the pterygoid plexus, leading to potentially serious complications.
The pterygoid plexus is susceptible to injury during dental procedures involving the maxillary region.
Venous blood from the area around the jaw and mouth is collected by the pterygoid plexus.
