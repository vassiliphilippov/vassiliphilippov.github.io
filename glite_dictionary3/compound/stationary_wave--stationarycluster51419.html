stationary wave

noun
wave with fixed nodes and antinodes
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪʃəˌnɛri weɪv / (AmE), / ˈsteɪʃənəri weɪv / (BrE)
a wave in which certain points, known as nodes, remain fixed while others between them reach maximum amplitude, as in sound or electromagnetic waves confined within specific boundaries

Examples

stationary wave on a string
A stationary wave can be formed on a string fixed at both ends when vibrated at certain frequencies.
Physicists often study stationary waves to better understand resonance in musical instruments.
The formation of a stationary wave leads to regions of constructive and destructive interference.

  • synonym – standing wave: a wave with stationary nodes and antinodes; another term for stationary wave
