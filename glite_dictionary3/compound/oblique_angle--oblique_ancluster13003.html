oblique angle

noun
angle not a multiple of 90 degrees
Pronunciation: / əˈbliːk ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əˈbliːk ˈaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
an angle that is not a right angle (90°) or a multiple of a right angle; specifically, any angle other than 90°, 180°, etc.

Examples

oblique angle in geometry
In geometry class, we learned that an oblique angle cannot be 90 or 180 degrees.
The painter preferred to frame her portrait with an oblique angle to give a dynamic effect.
Triangles with all oblique angles are called scalene triangles.
