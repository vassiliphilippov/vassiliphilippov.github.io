instrument flying

noun
flying an aircraft using only instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈflaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnstrʊmənt ˈflaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the navigation and control of an aircraft using only flight instruments, without visual reference to the ground or horizon

Examples

instrument flying in bad weather
After years of training, she finally mastered instrument flying in difficult weather conditions.
During the storm, the crew relied solely on instrument flying to navigate through the clouds.
Pilots must be certified in instrument flying to operate commercial jets safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym – visual flying: navigation of an aircraft by visual reference to the ground or horizon
