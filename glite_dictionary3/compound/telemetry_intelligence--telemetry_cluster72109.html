telemetry intelligence

noun
intelligence from intercepted telemetry data
Pronunciation: / təˈlɛmətri ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛmɪtri ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
intelligence obtained by intercepting, processing, and analyzing foreign telemetry data, especially for military or strategic purposes.

Examples

telemetry intelligence on enemy missiles
Advances in technology have made gathering telemetry intelligence both more effective and more challenging for analysts.
During the briefing, the officer presented new telemetry intelligence on the adversary's space program.
The agency specializes in collecting telemetry intelligence to monitor missile launches in foreign countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym – human intelligence: intelligence gathered from human sources
  • synonym – signal intelligence: intelligence gained from intercepting electronic signals
