zone fire

noun
artillery fire varied in elevation for area coverage
Pronunciation: / zoʊn ˈfaɪər / (AmE), / zəʊn ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
artillery or mortar fire sustained in one direction but varied in elevation to strike different ranges across a target area.

Examples

zone fire covers targets
Effective use of zone fire requires precise calculation of quadrant elevations for each gun.
Orders were given to lay down zone fire along the ridge to support the infantry advance.
The unit coordinated zone fire to disrupt enemy movement across the field.
