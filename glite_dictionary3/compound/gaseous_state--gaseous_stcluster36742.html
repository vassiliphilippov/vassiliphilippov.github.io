gaseous state

noun
state of matter with no fixed shape or volume
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæsiəs steɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪsɪəs steɪt / (BrE)
the physical state of matter in which a substance has neither a definite shape nor a fixed volume, and spreads to fill any container completely

Examples

gaseous state of oxygen
At room temperature, oxygen exists in the gaseous state and is essential for human life.
In the gaseous state, molecules are far apart and move freely compared to liquids or solids.
When water is heated to 100°C at standard pressure, it changes from liquid to gaseous state.

Related Terms

  • antonym – liquid state: the state of matter with definite volume but no definite shape
  • antonym – solid state: the state of matter with definite shape and volume
