acute brain disorder

noun
sudden, reversible brain disturbance
Pronunciation: / əˈkjuːt breɪn dɪsˌɔrdər / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt breɪn dɪsˌɔːdə / (BrE)
a sudden and reversible disturbance of brain function, often causing confusion or disorientation, typically resulting from injury, drugs, or infection

Examples

sudden acute brain disorder
Delirium is a common type of acute brain disorder in hospitalized elderly patients.
