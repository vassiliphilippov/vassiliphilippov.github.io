heat dissipation

noun
loss or release of heat from a system
Pronunciation: / hiːt ˌdɪsəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt ˌdɪsɪˈpeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process by which heat is released or lost from a system, especially in order to maintain efficiency or prevent overheating.

Examples

heat dissipation in electronics
Engineers must account for heat dissipation when developing high-powered processors.
Proper heat dissipation is essential to prevent electronic devices from overheating.
The new design improves heat dissipation, resulting in a longer lifespan for the machinery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cooling: the process of becoming or making something less hot
