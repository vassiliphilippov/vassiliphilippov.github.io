to court favour

verb
try to win approval by flattery
Pronunciation: / kɔrt ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or support by flattering them or behaving in a way intended to please them

Examples

to court favour with a boss
He tried to court favour with the manager by complimenting her ideas at every meeting.
Instead of working hard, she spent her time courting favour with influential colleagues.
Politicians often court favour with powerful interest groups before elections.

Related Terms

  • synonym – curry favour: to seek to gain favor by flattery or obsequious behavior
