great gray owl

noun
a large grey owl native to the northern hemisphere
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt ɡreɪ aʊl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɡreɪ aʊl / (BrE)
a very large owl of northern North America and western Eurasia, notable for its round, dish-like face and grey plumage

Examples

a great gray owl in the forest
Birdwatchers traveled for miles to catch a glimpse of the elusive great gray owl.
The great gray owl is a master of camouflage in snowy forests.
Unlike many other owls, the great gray owl hunts mainly by listening for movement under the snow.
