ground bait

noun
bait scattered to attract fish
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd ˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌbeɪt / (BrE)
Bait that is scattered on the surface of the water or the bottom to attract fish to a particular area.

Examples

ground bait attracts fish
Before casting his line, he threw some ground bait to increase his chances of catching fish.
Many anglers prepare their own mixtures of ground bait for use during competitions.
The fisherman scattered ground bait into the lake to lure the carp to his spot.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chum: bait consisting of chopped fish or other material thrown overboard to attract fish
