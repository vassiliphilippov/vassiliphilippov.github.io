parallel of latitude

noun
imaginary line parallel to the equator
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl əv ˈlætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈpærəlɛl əv ˈlætɪtjuːd / (BrE)
an imaginary line around the Earth that runs parallel to the equator, used to indicate latitude on maps and globes

Examples

parallel of latitude on a map
Explorers often relied on each parallel of latitude to determine their position at sea.
Maps show each parallel of latitude as a horizontal line across the globe.
The Tropic of Cancer is a well-known parallel of latitude.
