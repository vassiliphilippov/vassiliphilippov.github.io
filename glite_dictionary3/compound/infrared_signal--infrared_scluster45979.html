infrared signal

noun
electromagnetic wave beyond visible light used for communication
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfrəˌrɛd ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrəˌrɛd ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
an electromagnetic wave with a wavelength longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves, used for transmitting information, especially in remote controls and communication devices

Examples

infrared signal from remote
An infrared signal is invisible to the human eye but can be detected by sensors.
Many wireless headphones receive their audio via an infrared signal from the transmitter.
The TV remote sends an infrared signal to change the channel.

Related Terms

  • synonym – infrared: electromagnetic radiation with wavelength just beyond red light
