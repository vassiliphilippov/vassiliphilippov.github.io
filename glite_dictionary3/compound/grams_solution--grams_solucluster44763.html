Gram's solution

noun
iodine-potassium iodide solution for Gram staining
Pronunciation: / ɡræmz səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ɡræmz səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a solution of iodine and potassium iodide in water, used as a reagent in the Gram staining method for bacteria

Examples

Gram's solution for bacteria
Fresh Gram's solution should be used for reliable results during microscopic analysis.
The laboratory technician prepared Gram's solution to carry out the staining procedure.
Without Gram's solution, the differences between Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria cannot be visualized.
