congressional district

noun
U.S. voting area for House of Representatives
Pronunciation: / kənˈɡrɛʃənəl ˈdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈɡrɛʃənəl ˈdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
a territorial division of a U.S. state, each of which elects a single member to the United States House of Representatives

Examples

a congressional district map
Each congressional district is designed to have roughly equal population for fair representation.
