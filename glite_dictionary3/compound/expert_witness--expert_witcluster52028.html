expert witness

noun
specialist who testifies in court
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkspɝt ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈekspɜːt ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who is qualified by knowledge, skill, experience, training, or education to provide opinion testimony in a court of law on matters within their expertise

Examples

call an expert witness to court
After reviewing the facts, the court agreed to allow testimony from an expert witness.
Her experience as a medical expert witness was crucial in winning the case.
The prosecution called an expert witness to explain the forensic evidence to the jury.
