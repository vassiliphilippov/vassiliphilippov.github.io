pulley-block

noun
device with a grooved wheel for ropes, used to lift or move loads
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊli ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈpʊli ˌblɒk / (BrE)
a device consisting of a wheel with a grooved rim in which a rope or chain runs, used to change the direction of force applied to the rope or to lift loads

Examples

pulley-block lifts weight
A pulley-block reduces the amount of force needed to lift an object.
The old barn still had a rusty pulley-block hanging from the rafters.
They used a pulley-block to hoist the heavy crate onto the ship.
