intradermal injection

noun
injection into the skin
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈdɜrməl ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdɜːməl ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
an injection given into the dermis, the layer of skin just below the outermost epidermis

Examples

intradermal injection for allergy test
Receiving an intradermal injection can sometimes cause mild irritation at the injection site.
Skin tests for allergies often require an intradermal injection of small amounts of allergens.
The nurse administered the tuberculosis test using an intradermal injection on his forearm.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – intramuscular injection: an injection made directly into a muscle
  • degree lower – subcutaneous injection: an injection made into the layer of fat and tissue just under the skin
