fanjet engine

noun
jet engine with turbine-driven fan for extra thrust
Pronunciation: / ˈfænˌdʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈfanˌdʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A jet engine in which a turbine-driven fan supplies additional air to the burner, increasing thrust and efficiency.

Examples

modern fanjet engine
Engineers are continually working to improve the performance of the fanjet engine for future airliners.
Most modern commercial aircraft use fanjet engines due to their superior fuel efficiency and noise reduction.
The Boeing 747 is powered by four fanjet engines that enable it to fly long distances efficiently.
