Grub Street

noun
world of literary hacks or hack writers
Pronunciation: / ɡrʌb strit / (AmE), / ɡrʌb striːt / (BrE)
The world or class of literary writers, especially those considered low-status or hack writers producing work for hire rather than for artistic merit.

Examples

life in Grub Street
After years of struggling in Grub Street, he finally published a novel of critical acclaim.
Many poets feared ending up in Grub Street, churning out verse for little money.
The magazine was dismissed as a product of Grub Street, lacking literary ambition.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hack writer: A writer who produces routine or commercial writing of low quality
