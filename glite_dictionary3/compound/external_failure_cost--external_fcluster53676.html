external failure cost

noun
costs from defects reaching customers
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɜrnəl ˈfeɪljɚ kɔst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈfeɪljə kɒst / (BrE)
the costs incurred by a business when defective products or services reach customers, such as warranty claims or returns.
