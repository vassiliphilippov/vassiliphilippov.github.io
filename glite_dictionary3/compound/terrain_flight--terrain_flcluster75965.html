terrain flight

noun
flight at very low altitude
Pronunciation: / təˈreɪn flaɪt / (AmE), / təˈreɪn flaɪt / (BrE)
flight at very low altitudes, often to avoid detection or obstacles, especially in military or helicopter operations

Examples

terrain flight in helicopters
Effective terrain flight requires careful navigation to avoid obstacles and maintain safety.
Helicopters often use terrain flight techniques to avoid radar detection during missions.
The pilot was trained specifically in terrain flight to minimize the risk of being spotted.
