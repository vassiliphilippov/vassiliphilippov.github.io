infrared frequency

noun
frequency range below visible light
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.frəˌrɛd ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˌrɛd ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
The range of electromagnetic wave frequencies that lies just below the visible spectrum, corresponding to infrared radiation.

Examples

infrared frequency range
Astronomers study infrared frequencies to learn more about stars and galaxies obscured by dust.
Many remote controls operate by emitting light at an infrared frequency that the device can detect.
Some cameras are sensitive to infrared frequencies and can capture images invisible to the naked eye.
