hard tick

noun
tick with a hard shield on its back
Pronunciation: / hɑrd tɪk / (AmE), / hɑːd tɪk / (BrE)
A tick belonging to the family Ixodidae, characterized by a hard shield on its back and mouthparts that protrude from the head.

Examples

hard tick bites skin
A hard tick attaches itself to its host for several days while feeding.
Scientists collected specimens of the hard tick for their study on Lyme disease.
Unlike the soft tick, a hard tick has a visible scutum or dorsal shield.

Related Terms

  • antonym – soft tick: tick lacking a hard shield on its back
