on board

adverb
aboard a ship, plane, or vehicle
Pronunciation: / ɑn bɔrd / (AmE), / ɒn bɔːd / (BrE)
Physically present on or inside a ship, train, plane, or other vehicle.

Examples

on board the plane
All passengers must be on board before the train departs.
Once on board the aircraft, please take your seat promptly.
There are more than 200 people on board the ship.
