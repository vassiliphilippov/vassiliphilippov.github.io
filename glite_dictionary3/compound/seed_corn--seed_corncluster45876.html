seed corn

noun
resources reserved for future use
Pronunciation: / sid kɔrn / (AmE), / siːd kɔːn / (BrE)
resources or assets reserved to ensure future growth or investment, not to be used for immediate needs

Examples

seed corn for investment
Good leaders know not to use the company's seed corn during a brief financial crisis.
Investors often keep some seed corn to take advantage of unexpected opportunities.
Many small businesses fail because they spend their seed corn before establishing steady revenue.
