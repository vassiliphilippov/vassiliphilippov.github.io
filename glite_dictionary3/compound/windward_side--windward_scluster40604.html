windward side

noun
side facing into the wind
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndwɚd saɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪndwəd saɪd / (BrE)
the side of something, especially a landmass or vessel, that faces into the wind or from which the wind blows.

Examples

the windward side of a mountain
Sailors prefer to approach the dock from the windward side for greater control.
The house was built with extra protection on its windward side to withstand strong storms.
The northern windward side of the island receives the most rainfall throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – leeward side: the side sheltered from the wind
