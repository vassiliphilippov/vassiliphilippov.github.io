Carboniferous period

noun
a geologic period, 345–280 million years ago
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrbəˈnɪfərəs ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbəˈnɪfərəs ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
The period of geological time, about 345 to 280 million years ago, characterized by extensive forests and the formation of vast coal beds.

Examples

the Carboniferous period forests
Many amphibians and insects greatly diversified during the Carboniferous period.
The Carboniferous period saw the development of vast swampy forests that would later form major coal deposits.
The fossils preserved from the Carboniferous period provide important evidence of early terrestrial life.
