cockspur thorn

noun
North American hawthorn with long thorns
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑkˌspɜr θɔrn / (AmE), / ˈkɒkspə ˌθɔːn / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree of eastern North America (Crataegus crus-galli), having long, straight thorns and clusters of white flowers followed by small red fruits.

Examples

cockspur thorn tree
Be careful walking near a cockspur thorn; its long thorns can be quite sharp.
In autumn, the cockspur thorn produces clusters of bright red berries.
The cockspur thorn is often used as an ornamental tree in parks and gardens.
