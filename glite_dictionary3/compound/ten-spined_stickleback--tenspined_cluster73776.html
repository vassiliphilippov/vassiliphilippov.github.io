ten-spined stickleback

noun
a river fish with ten dorsal spines
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛnˈspaɪnd ˈstɪkəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˌtɛnˈspaɪnd ˈstɪk(ə)lbæk / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish (Pungitius pungitius) found in rivers, characterized by having ten dorsal spines.

Examples

The ten-spined stickleback is often found in slow-moving rivers and streams.
Unlike its close relative, the ten-spined stickleback thrives in freshwater rather than brackish environments.
