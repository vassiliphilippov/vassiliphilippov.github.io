appraisal cost

noun
costs for assessing quality conformance
Pronunciation: / əˈpreɪzl kɑst / (AmE), / əˈpreɪzl kɒst / (BrE)
the costs incurred to assess whether products or services meet specified quality requirements

Examples

appraisal cost for quality checks
Investing in appraisal cost can reduce the likelihood of defects reaching customers.
The annual report detailed the firm's appraisal cost in the quality control section.
The company increased its appraisal cost to ensure all products met safety standards.

Related Terms

  • antonym – failure cost: the cost arising from products or services not conforming to requirements
  • synonym – prevention cost: the cost of activities aimed at preventing defects
