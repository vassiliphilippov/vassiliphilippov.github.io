soft water

noun
water low in mineral salts
Pronunciation: / sɔft ˈwɔt̬ɚ / (AmE), / sɒft ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water that contains low concentrations of dissolved mineral salts, particularly calcium and magnesium, making it easy to form lather with soap.

Examples

soft water from the tap
After installing a filter, the tap now delivers soft water that's gentle on my skin.
Laundry comes out cleaner when washed in soft water as soap rinses out more easily.
Some people prefer the taste of soft water in their tea and coffee.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hard water: water high in mineral salts that makes lathering with soap difficult
