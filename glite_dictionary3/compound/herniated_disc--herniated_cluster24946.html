herniated disc

noun
ruptured spinal disc causing nerve pain
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜrniˌeɪtɪd dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈhɜːniˌeɪtɪd dɪsk / (BrE)
a condition in which the soft tissue inside a spinal disc ruptures, causing pain by pressing on nearby nerves

Examples

herniated disc causes pain
After lifting a heavy box, she suffered from a herniated disc and needed physical therapy.
People with a herniated disc may experience numbness or weakness in their legs.
The doctor explained that my back pain was caused by a herniated disc in my lower spine.

Related Terms

  • synonym – slipped disc: another term for a herniated disc
