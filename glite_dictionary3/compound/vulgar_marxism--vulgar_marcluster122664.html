vulgar Marxism

noun
simplistic interpretation of Marxism
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlɡɚ ˈmɑɹksɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡə ˈmɑːksɪzəm / (BrE)
An oversimplified or crude interpretation of Marxist theory that ignores its complexities and nuances.

Examples

vulgar Marxism ignores complexity
Many scholars reject vulgar Marxism because it fails to consider the complexities of social structures.
She accused her opponent of relying on vulgar Marxism rather than engaging in substantive analysis.
The author's work was dismissed as vulgar Marxism by critics for lacking depth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – orthodox Marxism: a traditional or standard interpretation of Marxist theory
