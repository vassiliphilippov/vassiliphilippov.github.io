prairie fowl

noun
North American grouse of western prairies
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛri faʊl / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri faʊl / (BrE)
A brown, mottled North American grouse found in western prairies, especially the greater prairie chicken.

Examples

wild prairie fowl
Habitat loss has contributed to the decline of prairie fowl populations across the Midwest.
The prairie fowl is known for its elaborate mating dance in the spring.
We spotted a flock of prairie fowl near the edge of the grassland at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • synonym – prairie chicken: A species of grouse native to North American prairies, also called greater prairie chicken
