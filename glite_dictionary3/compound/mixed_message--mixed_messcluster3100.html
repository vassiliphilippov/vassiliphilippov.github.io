mixed message

noun
conflicting or unclear communication
Pronunciation: / mɪkst ˈmesɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪkst ˈmesɪdʒ / (BrE)
a statement or communication in which the intended meaning is unclear or contradictory, often causing confusion

Examples

send a mixed message
Politicians often send a mixed message when talking about sensitive issues.
Receiving a mixed message from her friend left her feeling uncertain.
The manager's speech gave a mixed message about the new changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clear message: a communication that is unambiguous and easy to understand
