window sash

noun
framework holding window panes
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndoʊ sæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊ sæʃ / (BrE)
A framework that holds the panes of glass in a window and fits inside the window frame, often movable to open or close the window.

Examples

window sash repair
A wooden window sash needs regular painting to protect it from the weather.
Replacing a window sash can improve a home's insulation and energy efficiency.
The old house had a broken window sash that made it hard to open the window.
