roller bearing

noun
type of bearing with rollers
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlər ˌberɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə ˌbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
A bearing in which the moving parts are cylindrical or spherical rollers that reduce friction between moving parts.

Examples

roller bearing in machinery
A roller bearing is essential for smooth operation in many types of machinery.
Modern bicycles often use roller bearings to improve wheel rotation.
The mechanic replaced the worn roller bearing to prevent further damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ball bearing: bearing using balls instead of rollers to reduce friction
