Wernicke's aphasia

noun
type of aphasia with impaired comprehension
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrnɪkəz əˈfeɪziə / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪkəz əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A type of aphasia characterized by fluent but unintelligible speech and severe difficulties in understanding spoken or written language.

Examples

Wernicke's aphasia affects understanding
Patients with Wernicke's aphasia often produce fluent speech that lacks meaning.
The neurologist diagnosed her with Wernicke's aphasia after noticing her severe comprehension difficulties.
Treatment for Wernicke's aphasia focuses on improving language understanding and communication skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Broca's aphasia: a type of aphasia marked by impaired speech production but relatively preserved comprehension
