auditory ossicle

noun
small bone in the middle ear
Pronunciation: / əˈdɑː.təˌri ˈɑː.sɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈdɪ.tər.i ˈɒs.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
any of the three small bones (the malleus, incus, and stapes) in the middle ear that transmit sound vibrations from the eardrum to the inner ear

Examples

auditory ossicle transmits sound
Damage to an auditory ossicle can result in hearing loss.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's auditory ossicle during the procedure.
The three auditory ossicles work together to transmit sounds efficiently.
