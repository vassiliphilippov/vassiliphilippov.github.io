upper mantle

noun
the upper part of the Earth's mantle
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpɚ ˈmæntəl / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˈmæntl̩ / (BrE)
the upper part of the Earth's mantle, located beneath the crust and above the lower mantle, distinguished by its physical and chemical properties

Examples

Earth's upper mantle
Heat and pressure in the upper mantle cause rocks to flow slowly over time.
Scientists study the upper mantle to understand how tectonic plates move.
Seismic waves change speed as they pass through the upper mantle.
