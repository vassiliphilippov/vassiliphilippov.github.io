parietal cortex

noun
region of the brain's cerebral cortex
Pronunciation: / pəˈraɪət̬l ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / pəˈraɪətl ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex located in either hemisphere of the brain beneath the crown of the head, associated with processing sensory information and spatial navigation

Examples

the parietal cortex processes touch
Damage to the parietal cortex can affect a person's ability to perceive touch and spatial relationships.
Researchers found increased activity in the parietal cortex during complex mathematical tasks.
The parietal cortex helps integrate sensory information from different parts of the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym – frontal cortex: region of the cerebral cortex at the front of the brain, involved in reasoning and movement
  • synonym – occipital cortex: region of the cerebral cortex at the back of the brain, involved in visual processing
  • synonym – temporal cortex: region of the cerebral cortex located at the sides of the brain, involved in auditory processing and memory
