court of appeals

noun
a court that reviews lower court decisions
Pronunciation: / kɔrt əv əˈpiəlz / (AmE), / kɔːt əv əˈpiːlz / (BrE)
A court that has the authority to review and overturn decisions made by lower courts or agencies.

Examples

the court of appeals reviews cases
A decision by the court of appeals can set important legal precedents.
After losing the initial case, she took her complaint to the court of appeals.
The court of appeals overturned the verdict after reviewing new evidence.
