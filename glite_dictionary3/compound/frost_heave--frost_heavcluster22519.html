frost heave

noun
soil/pavement swelling due to freezing moisture
Pronunciation: / frɔst hiv / (AmE), / frɒst hiːv / (BrE)
the upward movement of soil or pavement caused by the freezing and expansion of moisture in the ground

Examples

frost heave damages roads
Engineers designed the foundation to resist frost heave in cold climates.
Patches of frost heave appeared along the unpaved path after several freezing nights.
The road was damaged by frost heave during the harsh winter.
