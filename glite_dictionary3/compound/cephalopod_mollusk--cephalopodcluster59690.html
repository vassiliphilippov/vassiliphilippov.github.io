cephalopod mollusk

noun
marine mollusk with tentacles, e.g., octopus
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛfələˌpɑd məˈlʌsk / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələˌpɒd məˈlʌsk / (BrE)
A marine mollusk distinguished by a well-developed head, prominent eyes, and arms or tentacles with suckers, including creatures such as octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.
