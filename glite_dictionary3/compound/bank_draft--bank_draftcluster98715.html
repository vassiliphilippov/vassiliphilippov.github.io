bank draft

noun
document ordering payment from one bank to another
Pronunciation: / bæŋk dræft / (AmE), / bæŋk drɑːft / (BrE)
a written order for payment of money drawn by a bank on another bank, payable to a specified person or entity

Examples

bank draft payment
A bank draft is often required for large transactions instead of personal checks.
He requested a bank draft to pay for the equipment overseas.
The car dealership would only accept payment by bank draft or certified check.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cashier's check: a check drawn on a bank's own funds, signed by a cashier
