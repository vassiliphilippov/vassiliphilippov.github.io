sonic boom

noun
explosive sound from breaking the sound barrier
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːnɪk buːm / (AmE), / ˈsɒnɪk buːm / (BrE)
an explosive sound caused when an object, especially an aircraft, travels faster than the speed of sound, creating a shock wave

Examples

a sonic boom from a jet
Pilots must use caution when approaching speeds that produce a sonic boom.
Residents complained about the disturbance caused by the repeated sonic boom during the tests.
The windows shook as a sonic boom echoed over the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym – shock wave: a wave of pressure traveling through a medium, often caused by explosions or sudden impacts
