melting pot

noun
place of cultural assimilation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ pɒt / (BrE)
a place or environment where people of different backgrounds, cultures, or ideas mix and blend together, often forming a new, unified whole

Examples

city as a melting pot
America has been called a melting pot for its history of embracing immigrants from many countries.
New York City is often described as a melting pot because of its diverse population.

Her classroom was a melting pot of students from all over the world.
Modern cities often serve as a melting pot, blending languages, foods, and beliefs.
New York has long been celebrated as a melting pot of different cultures and traditions.
