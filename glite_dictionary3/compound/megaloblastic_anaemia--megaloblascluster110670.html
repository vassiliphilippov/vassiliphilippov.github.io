megaloblastic anaemia

noun
anaemia with large, immature red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡələˌblæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələˌblɑːstɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A form of anaemia characterized by the presence of unusually large, immature, and dysfunctional red blood cells (megaloblasts) in the bone marrow, often caused by vitamin B12 or folate deficiency.

Examples

megaloblastic anaemia from vitamin deficiency
A deficiency of vitamin B12 commonly leads to megaloblastic anaemia in adults.
Her doctor diagnosed her with megaloblastic anaemia after routine blood tests revealed abnormal red blood cells.
Treatment for megaloblastic anaemia usually involves supplements of folic acid or vitamin B12.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – anaemia: a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • synonym – pernicious anaemia: anaemia caused by vitamin B12 deficiency, a type of megaloblastic anaemia
