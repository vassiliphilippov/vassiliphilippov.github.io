brake pad

noun
friction component in vehicle brakes
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪk ˌpæd / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ˌpad / (BrE)
a flat component in a vehicle's braking system that presses against the brake disc to slow or stop the wheel by friction

Examples

brake pad replacement
A squealing noise often means your brake pads need attention.
It's important to check your brake pads regularly for safety reasons.
The mechanic replaced the worn-out brake pad on my car.
