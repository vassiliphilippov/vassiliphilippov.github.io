neritic zone

noun
shallow ocean region above continental shelf
Pronunciation: / nəˈrɪtɪk zoʊn / (AmE), / nəˈrɪtɪk zəʊn / (BrE)
the shallow part of the ocean extending from the low tide mark to a depth of about 200 meters (100 fathoms), above the continental shelf

Examples

shallow neritic zone
Coral reefs are often found within the neritic zone of warm oceans.
Fishermen rely on the neritic zone for its rich supply of fish and other sea life.
Many marine species thrive in the neritic zone due to abundant sunlight and nutrients.

Related Terms

  • antonym – oceanic zone: the deeper waters of the open sea beyond the continental shelf
