flying brick

noun
poorly executed shot; thrown object (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪ.ɪŋ brɪk / (AmE), / ˈflaɪ.ɪŋ brɪk / (BrE)
a poorly executed shot or an object thrown forcefully, often missing its intended target, especially in sports or colloquial contexts

Examples

a flying brick in sports
After losing his grip, the frisbee became a flying brick, crashing into the bleachers.
His attempt at the goal turned into a flying brick that soared over the crossbar.
The quarterback's pass was a flying brick and landed nowhere near his teammate.
