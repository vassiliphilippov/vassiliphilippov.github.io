Sanson-Flamsteed projection

noun
equal-area map projection with curved meridians
Pronunciation: / ˌsæn.sən ˈflæm.stiːd prəˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsan.sən ˈflam.stiːd prəˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
an equal-area map projection in which the parallels and the equator are straight lines and other meridians are curved, commonly used to represent tropical latitudes

Examples

Sanson-Flamsteed projection world map
Because of its equal-area property, the Sanson-Flamsteed projection is suitable for statistical world maps.
In her geography class, Maria analyzed maps created using the Sanson-Flamsteed projection.
The Sanson-Flamsteed projection is often used by cartographers to accurately show areas near the equator.
