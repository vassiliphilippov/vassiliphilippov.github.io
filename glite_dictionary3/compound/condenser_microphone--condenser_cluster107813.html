condenser microphone

noun
microphone using a capacitor for sound conversion
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛnsɚ maɪkˌroʊfoʊn / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə ˈmaɪkrəfəʊn / (BrE)
A microphone that uses a capacitor to convert sound waves into electrical signals, typically with one fixed and one movable plate forming a diaphragm.

Examples

condenser microphone for recording
Due to its design, a condenser microphone requires external power, such as phantom power, to operate.
Studio recordings often employ a condenser microphone to capture subtle vocal nuances.
The singer preferred using a condenser microphone for its sensitivity and accurate sound reproduction.
