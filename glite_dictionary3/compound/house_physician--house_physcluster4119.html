house physician

noun
hospital-based resident doctor
Pronunciation: / haʊs fəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / haʊs fɪˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a doctor, especially a medical intern, who lives in a hospital and cares for patients under the supervision of the hospital's medical staff

Examples

house physician on duty
As a house physician, she gained valuable experience treating a wide variety of medical cases.
During his internship, he worked as a house physician at City General Hospital.
The house physician was called to assess the patient after her condition suddenly worsened.

Related Terms

  • synonym – resident: a physician receiving specialist training while working in a hospital
