Elf and safety guideline

noun
humorous safety rule parody term
Pronunciation: / ɛlf ənd ˈseɪfti ˌɡaɪdlaɪn / (AmE), / ɛlf ən ˈseɪfti ˌɡaɪdlaɪn / (BrE)
A humorous term parodying 'health and safety guideline', referring to fictitious or exaggerated rules imposed to ensure safety, especially during Christmas or festive events.

Examples

Elf and safety guideline for Christmas events
Every year, the school distributes a list of Elf and safety guidelines before the Christmas play.
He joked that the Elf and safety guidelines banned tinsel near computers.
The workplace newsletter included some lighthearted Elf and safety guidelines for decorating the office.
