equal-area projection

noun
map projection preserving area
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkwəl ˈɛriə prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəl ˈɛəriə prəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a type of map projection in which regions have areas in proportion to their areas on the globe

Examples

equal-area projection of world map
Choosing the right equal-area projection is important when comparing the area of different countries.
Many thematic maps use an equal-area projection to accurately represent spatial data.
The equal-area projection ensures that Africa and South America are shown in their true relative sizes.
