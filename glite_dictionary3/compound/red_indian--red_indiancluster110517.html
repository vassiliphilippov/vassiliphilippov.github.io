Red Indian

noun
offensive term for Indigenous American peoples
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌɪndiən / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌɪndiən / (BrE)
(offensive) A term formerly used to refer to the Indigenous peoples of North and South America at the time of European arrival.

Examples

a Red Indian tribe
It is now recognized that Red Indian is an offensive and outdated term.
Some literature from the 19th century refers to Native Americans as Red Indians.
The old book used the term Red Indian to describe people who lived in America before colonization.
