black pepper

noun
climbing plant producing peppercorns
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
a climbing vine native to southern India and Sri Lanka, cultivated for its dark red berries (peppercorns) used as a spice

Examples

black pepper plant
Black pepper is cultivated in tropical regions for its aromatic berries.
Farmers in Kerala depend on black pepper as a major cash crop.
The black pepper plant climbs up trees or trellises by means of its aerial roots.
