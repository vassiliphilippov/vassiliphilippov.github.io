daisy cutter

noun
ball skimming close to the ground (in sports)
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪzi ˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi ˌkʌtə / (BrE)
A batted or served ball that travels very low, skimming just above the ground.

Examples

a daisy cutter shot
Her serve turned into a daisy cutter, bouncing just once before hitting the fence.
Spectators gasped as the ball rolled like a daisy cutter across the outfield.
The batsman hit a daisy cutter that sped past the fielder.
