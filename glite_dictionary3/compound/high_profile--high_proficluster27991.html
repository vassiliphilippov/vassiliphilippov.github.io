high profile

noun
position attracting much public attention
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈproʊfaɪl / (AmE), / haɪ ˈprəʊfaɪl / (BrE)
a position or situation that attracts a great deal of public attention or publicity

Examples

a high profile job
After the scandal, she accepted a high profile job at a major law firm.
He prefers to avoid high profile positions due to the intense media scrutiny.
The company took a high profile approach to marketing their new product.

Related Terms

  • antonym – low profile: a position or situation attracting little attention or publicity
