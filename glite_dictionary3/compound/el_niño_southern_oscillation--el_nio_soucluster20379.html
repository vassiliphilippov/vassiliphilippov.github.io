El Niño Southern Oscillation

noun
recurring Pacific Ocean weather phenomenon
Pronunciation: / ɛl ˈniːnjoʊ ˈsʌðɚn ˌɑːsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛl ˈniːnjəʊ ˈsʌðən ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a recurring climatic pattern involving variations in the temperature of the surface waters of the tropical eastern Pacific Ocean, leading to significant global weather effects

Examples

El Nino Southern Oscillation changes climate
Droughts and floods around the world are often linked to changes in the El Niño Southern Oscillation.
Scientists are monitoring the El Niño Southern Oscillation for its potential impact on global weather this year.
The El Niño Southern Oscillation can disrupt fisheries by reducing the upwelling of nutrient-rich water.

Related Terms

  • synonym – El Niño: a periodic warming of Pacific Ocean waters affecting global weather
  • antonym – La Niña: a periodic cooling of Pacific Ocean waters, opposite of El Niño
