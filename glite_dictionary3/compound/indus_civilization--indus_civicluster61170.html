Indus civilization

noun
Bronze Age Indus Valley culture, ancient South Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndəs ˌsɪvələˌzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪndəs ˌsɪvɪlʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an ancient Bronze Age culture that flourished in the Indus Valley from about 2600 to 1750 BC, known for its advanced urban planning and architecture

Examples

ancient Indus civilization
Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable artifacts from the Indus civilization in present-day Pakistan and northwest India.
Many historians believe the script of the Indus civilization remains undeciphered to this day.
The urban planning of the Indus civilization included sophisticated drainage systems and well-organized streets.
