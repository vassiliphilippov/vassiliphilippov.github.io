in-crowd

noun
group of fashionable or popular people
Pronunciation: / ɪn kraʊd / (AmE), / ɪn kraʊd / (BrE)
A group of people who are considered fashionable, popular, or influential within a particular social scene.

Examples

the in-crowd at school
Only members of the in-crowd were invited to the exclusive party.
She desperately wanted to be accepted by the in-crowd at her new school.
The in-crowd sets the trends that everyone else seems to follow.
