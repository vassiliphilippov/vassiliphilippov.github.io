polytropic process

noun
thermodynamic process with constant pVⁿ
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈtroʊpɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈtrɒpɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
a thermodynamic process in which the pressure times the volume raised to a constant power remains the same throughout the expansion or compression of a gas

Examples

polytropic process in gas expansion
During the engine cycle, the expansion is treated as a polytropic process to model real gas behavior.
The polytropic process allows engineers to relate pressure and volume changes analytically.
Understanding a polytropic process is vital in compressor and turbine design.

Related Terms

  • synonym – adiabatic process: a thermodynamic process with no heat exchange
  • synonym – isothermal process: a thermodynamic process with constant temperature
