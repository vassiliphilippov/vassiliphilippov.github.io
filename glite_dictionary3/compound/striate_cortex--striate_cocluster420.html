striate cortex

noun
primary visual region of the occipital cortex
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪ.eɪt ˈkɔr.teks / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪt ˈkɔː.teks / (BrE)
the part of the occipital lobe in the brain responsible for receiving visual information from the eyes; also known as the primary visual cortex

Examples

the striate cortex processes vision
Damage to the striate cortex can result in loss of vision in specific parts of the visual field.
Researchers found increased activity in the striate cortex when subjects viewed complex patterns.
The striate cortex receives visual signals relayed from the retina via the lateral geniculate nucleus.
