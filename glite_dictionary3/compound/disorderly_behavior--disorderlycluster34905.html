disorderly behavior

noun
unruly or disruptive public conduct
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔrdərli bɪˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəli bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
behavior that disturbs public peace, disrupts order, or causes inconvenience to others, especially in a public place.

Examples

disorderly behavior in public
Residents complained about disorderly behavior in the park late at night.
The police arrested him for disorderly behavior after he caused a disturbance at the concert.
The university has a strict policy against disorderly behavior on campus.

Related Terms

  • antonym – orderly conduct: behavior conforming to rules and norms, not disruptive
  • synonym – public nuisance: behavior or situation harmful to the public's comfort or safety
