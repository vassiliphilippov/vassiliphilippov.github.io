half-staff

noun
flag position signifying mourning or distress
Pronunciation: / hæf ˈstæf / (AmE), / hɑːf ˈstɑːf / (BrE)
a position partway down from the top of a flagpole or mast, where a flag is flown as a symbol of mourning or distress

Examples

flag at half-staff
A decree ordered all government buildings to display their flags at half-staff for a week.
Flags remained at half-staff following the tragic event.
The national flag was flown at half-staff to honor the late president.

Related Terms

  • synonym – half-mast: position of a flag below the top of its pole as a sign of mourning
