culture medium

noun
substance for cultivating microorganisms
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltʃɚ ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌltʃə ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A nutrient substance, either solid or liquid, used for growing microorganisms in laboratory conditions.

Examples

culture medium for bacteria
Different types of culture medium are required for cultivating various microorganisms.
Fungi and other microbes can be grown on a specific culture medium in the laboratory.
The scientist prepared a culture medium to study the bacteria collected from the lake.

  • synonym – nutrient agar: a gelatinous culture medium used to grow bacteria
