snap line

noun
chalked string for marking straight lines
Pronunciation: / snæp laɪn / (AmE), / snæp laɪn / (BrE)
A length of chalked string stretched and snapped against a surface to mark a straight line, especially in building and carpentry.

Examples

use a snap line for marking
A quick pull of the snap line leaves a visible chalk mark to follow during installation.
Before laying the tiles, they used a snap line to mark the guidelines on the floor.
The carpenter stretched the snap line across the wall to ensure the shelves were level.
