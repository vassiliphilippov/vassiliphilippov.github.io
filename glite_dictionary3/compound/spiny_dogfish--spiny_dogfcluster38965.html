spiny dogfish

noun
a small shark with dorsal spines
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪni ˈdɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni ˈdɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small species of dogfish shark, Squalus acanthias, found in temperate oceans and characterized by sharp dorsal spines

Examples

a spiny dogfish shark
Fishermen often catch spiny dogfish off the coast during the colder months.
Populations of spiny dogfish have been declining in some areas due to overfishing.
The spiny dogfish is recognized by the sharp spines in front of its dorsal fins.
