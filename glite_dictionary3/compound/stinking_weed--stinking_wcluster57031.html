stinking weed

noun
malodorous tropical weedy shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ wiːd / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ wiːd / (BrE)
a tropical weedy shrub with a strong, unpleasant odor, whose seeds have sometimes been used as an adulterant for coffee; sometimes classified in the genus Cassia

Examples

stinking weed plant smell
Because of its unpleasant smell, most gardeners avoid planting stinking weed in ornamental beds.
Botanists have long debated whether stinking weed belongs in the genus Cassia or a separate category.
The seeds of the stinking weed can be used to adulterate coffee, though this practice is now rare.
