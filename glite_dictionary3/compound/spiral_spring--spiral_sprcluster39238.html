spiral spring

noun
a coiled mechanical spring
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪrəl sprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəl sprɪŋ / (BrE)
a mechanical spring made from a wire coiled in a spiral shape, used to store and release rotational energy

Examples

spiral spring in a clock
A broken spiral spring can cause the mechanism to malfunction.
The clock is powered by a tightly wound spiral spring that stores energy.
When the toy is wound up, its spiral spring releases energy to make it move.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coil spring: a spring made of coiled wire, not necessarily spiraled in a plane
