action painting

noun
an abstract painting style emphasizing spontaneous movement
Pronunciation: / ˈækʃən ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈakʃən ˌpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of abstract painting developed in New York in the 1940s and 1950s, characterized by energetic, spontaneous brushwork and the application of paint in sweeping or gestural movements
