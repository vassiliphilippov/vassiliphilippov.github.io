dwarf grey willow

noun
a low willow shrub with greyish leaves
Pronunciation: / dwɔrf ɡreɪ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / dwɔːf ɡreɪ ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A low-growing willow shrub found in dry areas of the eastern United States, characterized by long, narrow leaves with a greyish, hairy underside.

The dwarf grey willow thrives in sandy soils along the riverbank.
