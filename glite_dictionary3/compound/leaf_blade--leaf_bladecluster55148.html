leaf blade

noun
broad, flat part of a leaf
Pronunciation: / liːf bleɪd / (AmE), / liːf bleɪd / (BrE)
the broad, flat part of a leaf, distinct from the stalk or petiole, especially in grasses and many other plants

Examples

leaf blade of grass
Each leaf blade is connected to the stem by a thin petiole.
Some plants have long, narrow leaf blades that help reduce water loss.
The leaf blade of the grass was covered in morning dew.

Related Terms

  • antonym – petiole: the stalk attaching the leaf blade to the stem
