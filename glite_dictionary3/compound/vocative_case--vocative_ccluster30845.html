vocative case

noun
grammatical case for direct address
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑk.ətɪv keɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɒk.ətɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case used in some languages for a noun when the person or thing is being directly addressed

Examples

vocative case in Greek
Greek uses the vocative case when speaking directly to a person, as in 'ὦ φίλε' (O friend).
In Latin, the word 'puer' (boy) changes to 'puer' in the vocative case when calling out to someone.
Linguists study the vocative case to understand how languages mark someone being addressed.
