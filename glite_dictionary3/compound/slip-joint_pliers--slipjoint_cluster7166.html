slip-joint pliers

noun
pliers with adjustable joint for wider opening
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪp dʒɔɪnt ˈplaɪɚz / (AmE), / ˈslɪp dʒɔɪnt ˈplaɪəz / (BrE)
A type of pliers with a pivot joint that can be adjusted to two positions, allowing the jaws to open wider.

Examples

use slip-joint pliers to grip
Slip-joint pliers are useful for gripping objects of different sizes.
He used slip-joint pliers to tighten the loose bolt under the sink.
The toolbox contains several tools, including a pair of slip-joint pliers.
