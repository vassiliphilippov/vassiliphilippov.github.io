field test

noun
test of a product in real-world conditions
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌtɛst / (BrE)
an examination of a new product or device conducted under actual conditions of use to assess its performance

Examples

field test new equipment
Before launching the app nationwide, the company did a field test with a small group of users.
Several prototypes failed the initial field test due to unexpected weather conditions.
The engineers conducted a field test to evaluate the new engine's performance in rugged terrain.

Related Terms

  • antonym – laboratory test: a test carried out in a controlled lab setting
  • synonym – trial: a test, usually of performance or suitability
