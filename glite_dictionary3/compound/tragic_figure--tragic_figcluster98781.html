tragic figure

noun
character brought down by fatal flaw
Pronunciation: / ˈtrædʒɪk ˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪk ˈfɪɡə / (BrE)
A character in a story or play whose fatal flaw or error in judgment leads to their downfall or destruction.

Examples

classic tragic figure
Many saw Hamlet as the ultimate tragic figure in Shakespearean drama.
Oedipus is a classic tragic figure whose fate is sealed by his own actions.
The novel centers around a tragic figure whose ambition leads to his ruin.
