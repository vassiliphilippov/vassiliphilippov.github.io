cruel and unusual punishment

noun
punishment that is inhumane or excessively harsh (U.S. legal)
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːəl ənd ənˈjuːʒuəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈkruːəl ənd ʌnˈjuːʒʊəl ˈpʌnɪʃm(ə)nt / (BrE)
punishment that is considered inhumane, degrading, or excessively harsh, and is prohibited by the Eighth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution

Examples

cruel and unusual punishment banned by law
Some argue that solitary confinement is a form of cruel and unusual punishment.
The Eighth Amendment protects citizens from cruel and unusual punishment by the government.
The court ruled that the punishment constituted cruel and unusual punishment under the Constitution.

Related Terms

  • antonym – humane treatment: treatment that shows compassion and respect for human dignity
  • synonym – torture: deliberate infliction of severe pain for punishment or coercion
