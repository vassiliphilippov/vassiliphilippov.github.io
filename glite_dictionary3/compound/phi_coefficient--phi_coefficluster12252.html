phi coefficient

noun
statistical measure for two binary variables
Pronunciation: / faɪ koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / faɪ kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure of association for two binary variables, indicating the strength and direction of their relationship

Examples

phi coefficient between variables
A significant phi coefficient indicated a strong relationship between the treatment and patient recovery.
Researchers calculated the phi coefficient to determine if gender was related to preference for the new product.
The phi coefficient is commonly used to assess the association between two dichotomous variables.

  • synonym – correlation coefficient: a statistical measure that describes the size and direction of a relationship between variables
