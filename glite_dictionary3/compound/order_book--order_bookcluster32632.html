order book

noun
book for recording customers' orders
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər bʊk / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə bʊk / (BrE)
a book used to record customers' orders, often producing duplicate copies for record-keeping or processing

Examples

order book for sales
At the end of each day, she reviewed the order book to track sales.
Each carbon copy from the order book was sent to a different department for processing.
The shopkeeper wrote my purchase details in the order book.
