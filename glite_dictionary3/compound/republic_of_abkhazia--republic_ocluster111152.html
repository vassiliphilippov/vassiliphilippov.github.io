Republic of Abkhazia

proper noun
self-declared republic in the Caucasus region
Pronunciation: / rəˈpʌblɪk əv æbˈkɑːziə / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv abˈkɑːzɪə / (BrE)
A self-declared republic with limited international recognition, located in the northwestern region of Georgia, in the Caucasus.

Examples

the Republic of Abkhazia borders Georgia
Only a few countries officially recognize the Republic of Abkhazia as an independent state.
The Republic of Abkhazia declared independence from Georgia in the early 1990s.
Travel to the Republic of Abkhazia requires special permits due to its political status.
