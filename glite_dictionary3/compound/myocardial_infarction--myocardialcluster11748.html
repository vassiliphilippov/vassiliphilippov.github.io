myocardial infarction

noun
heart tissue damage from blocked blood supply
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.oʊˈkɑːr.di.əl ɪnˈfɑːrk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈkɑː.di.əl ɪnˈfɑːk.ʃən / (BrE)
Destruction of heart muscle tissue caused by a blockage in the blood supply to the heart; commonly known as a heart attack.

Examples

suffer a myocardial infarction
Early medical intervention is crucial after a myocardial infarction to minimize heart damage.
Lifestyle changes can significantly reduce the risk of myocardial infarction.
The patient suffered a myocardial infarction and was rushed to the emergency room.

Related Terms

  • synonym – heart attack: a sudden and sometimes fatal occurrence of coronary thrombosis, typically resulting in the death of part of a heart muscle
