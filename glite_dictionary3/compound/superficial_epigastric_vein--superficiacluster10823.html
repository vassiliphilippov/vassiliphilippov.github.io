superficial epigastric vein

noun
vein draining lower abdomen
Pronunciation: / suːˈpɚˌfɪʃəl ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / suːˈpɪʃəl ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a small vein that drains blood from the lower abdomen and empties into the great saphenous vein

Examples

the superficial epigastric vein drains blood
Anatomical diagrams clearly show the path of the superficial epigastric vein toward the thigh.
Blood from the lower abdominal wall is collected by the superficial epigastric vein.
The superficial epigastric vein is often visible during lower abdominal surgeries.
