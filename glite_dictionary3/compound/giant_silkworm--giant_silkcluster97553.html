giant silkworm

noun
large saturniid moth larva that spins strong silk
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈsɪlkˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈsɪlkˌwɜːm / (BrE)
The large larva of certain North American or Asian saturniid moths, known for producing a substantial amount of strong silk for its cocoon.

Examples

a giant silkworm cocoon
A giant silkworm can grow quickly before entering the pupal stage.
Farmers study the giant silkworm for its potential in producing natural silk.
The giant silkworm spins a cocoon with impressively strong silk fibers.
