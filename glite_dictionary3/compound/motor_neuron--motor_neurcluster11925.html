motor neuron

noun
nerve cell transmitting signals to muscles
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊtər ˌnuːˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˌnjʊərɒn / (BrE)
a nerve cell that transmits impulses from the brain or spinal cord to a muscle or gland, causing movement or action

Examples

motor neuron disease
A damaged motor neuron can result in the loss of muscle movement.
Diseases like ALS primarily affect motor neurons in the spinal cord.
The basic reflex arc includes both a sensory neuron and a motor neuron.
