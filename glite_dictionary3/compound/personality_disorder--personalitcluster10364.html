personality disorder

noun
mental disorder affecting personality patterns
Pronunciation: / pɚˌsæn.əˈlɑː.t̬i dɪˌsɔr.dɚ / (AmE), / pɜːˌsæn.əˈlɪt.i dɪˌsɔː.də / (BrE)
A type of mental disorder characterized by enduring patterns of behavior, thinking, and feeling that are inflexible, maladaptive, and cause distress or impaired functioning.

Examples

diagnosed with a personality disorder
People with a personality disorder may struggle to adapt their behavior to different situations.
She specialized in treating adolescents suffering from borderline personality disorder.
The patient was diagnosed with a personality disorder after several years of difficult social interactions.
