smoke tree

noun
desert shrub of SW US/Mexico with bluish flowers
Pronunciation: / smoʊk tri / (AmE), / sməʊk triː / (BrE)
a greyish-green shrub (Psorothamnus spinosus) found in desert regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its sparse foliage and bluish-violet flower spikes; valued locally for producing light-colored honey

Examples

smoke tree desert honey
Beekeepers prize the honey made from the nectar of the smoke tree.
The smoke tree blooms in late spring, filling the desert with its purple flowers.
We spotted a beautiful smoke tree while hiking in the Sonoran Desert.
