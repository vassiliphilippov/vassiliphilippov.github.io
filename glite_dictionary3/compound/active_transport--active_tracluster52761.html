active transport

noun
energy-requiring movement of substances in cells
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv trænsˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈaktɪv trænsˌpɔːt / (BrE)
the movement of a substance across a cell membrane against its concentration gradient, requiring energy expenditure

Examples

active transport of ions
Active transport allows cells to absorb nutrients even when concentrations are higher inside the cell than outside.
During active transport, energy from ATP is used to move ions across cell membranes.
The sodium-potassium pump is a well-known example of active transport in human cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym – passive transport: movement of substances across membranes without energy input
