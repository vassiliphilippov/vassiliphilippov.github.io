acoustic resistance

noun
real part of acoustic impedance in sound transmission
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the opposition to the flow of sound through a medium or surface, representing the real component of acoustic impedance

Examples

acoustic resistance of a panel
Engineers measured the acoustic resistance to determine the sound absorption properties of the panel.
Increasing acoustic resistance can improve the efficiency of certain types of microphones.
The acoustic resistance of this material helps reduce noise transmission through the walls.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – acoustic impedance: total opposition to sound flow in a medium, including resistance and reactance
  • antonym – acoustic reactance: the imaginary component of acoustic impedance, representing stored inertial and elastic effects
