crosscut saw

noun
handsaw for cutting across the grain
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌkʌt sɔ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌt sɔː / (BrE)
a handsaw with teeth set and angled to cut wood across the grain, or at right angles to the main axis

Examples

use a crosscut saw on wood
A crosscut saw is ideal for cutting boards to length cleanly and efficiently.
The carpenter used a crosscut saw to trim the wood perpendicular to the grain.
Vintage crosscut saws are often collected as antique tools by enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rip saw: a saw designed for cutting along the grain of wood
