rest stop

noun
area beside a road for drivers to rest
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛst ˌstɑp /; / ˈrɛst stɑp / (AmE), / ˈrɛst ˌstɒp /; / ˈrɛst stɒp / (BrE)
a place next to a road or highway where travelers can stop to rest, often with toilets, picnic areas, or refreshments

Examples

Most highways have at least one rest stop every fifty miles or so.
There’s a clean rest stop about twenty miles ahead if you need a break.
