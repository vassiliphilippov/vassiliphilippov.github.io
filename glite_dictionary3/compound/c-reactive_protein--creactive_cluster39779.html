C-reactive protein

noun
protein in blood indicating inflammation
Pronunciation: / si riˈæktɪv ˈproʊtin / (AmE), / si riˈæktɪv ˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a protein produced by the liver and released into the blood in response to inflammation, commonly measured as a marker of inflammatory disease

Examples

high C-reactive protein level
Elevated C-reactive protein may indicate the presence of an infection or chronic disease.
Researchers found a correlation between high C-reactive protein and increased risk of heart attacks.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check her C-reactive protein levels for signs of inflammation.
