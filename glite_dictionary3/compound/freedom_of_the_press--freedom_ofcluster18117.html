freedom of the press

noun
right to publish news without government control
Pronunciation: / ˈfriː.dəm əv ðə pres / (AmE), / ˈfriː.dəm əv ðə pres / (BrE)
the right of newspapers, magazines, and other media to report news without censorship or restraint by the government

Examples

freedom of the press protects journalists
A country that values freedom of the press ensures journalists can report the news without fear of government retaliation.
Debates about freedom of the press often arise when controversial stories are published.
Many organizations advocate for freedom of the press as a fundamental human right.
