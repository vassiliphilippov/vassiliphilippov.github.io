bone marrow

noun
soft, edible tissue inside bones
Pronunciation: / boʊn ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
Very soft, nutritious tissue found inside certain bones, used as food and considered a delicacy in some cuisines.

Examples

bone marrow soup
He scooped out the bone marrow and described its taste as rich and buttery.
Many gourmet restaurants serve bone marrow roasted and spread on toast.
Traditional beef stew is sometimes enriched with bone marrow for added flavor and nutrition.
