acute lymphocytic leukemia

noun
a fast-growing leukemia affecting lymphocytes, common in children
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ˌlɪmfəˈsɪtɪk luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˌlɪm.fəˈsɪt.ɪk luːˈkiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of acute leukemia marked by the rapid proliferation of immature lymphoblast-like cells in the bone marrow, lymph nodes, spleen, and blood, most common in children.

Examples

acute lymphocytic leukemia diagnosis
Survival rates for children with acute lymphocytic leukemia have improved significantly in recent decades.
The child was diagnosed with acute lymphocytic leukemia after a series of blood tests.
Treatment for acute lymphocytic leukemia often begins with intensive chemotherapy.
