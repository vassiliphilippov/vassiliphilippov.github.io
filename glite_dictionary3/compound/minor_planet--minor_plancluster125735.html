minor planet

noun
small celestial body orbiting the Sun
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnər ˈplænət / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
any of a large number of small celestial bodies, such as asteroids, that orbit the Sun, especially those distinct from comets or planets

Examples

discover a minor planet
Astronomers continuously track many minor planets as they move around the Sun.
Hundreds of minor planets have been discovered in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
The status of Pluto changed from planet to minor planet by the International Astronomical Union.

  • synonym – asteroid: a small rocky body orbiting the Sun, mainly between Mars and Jupiter
