no more

adverb
not more than; only as much as
Pronunciation: / noʊ mɔr / (AmE), / nəʊ mɔː / (BrE)
used to indicate that a person or thing does not have a greater degree of a quality than another; only as much as or no greater than.

Examples

no more expensive than
Although the painting is beautiful, it is no more valuable than the others in the gallery.
At seven years old, he weighed no more than fifty pounds.
She is no more talented than her classmates, despite her confidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – no less: used for emphasis to indicate the same or even more degree
