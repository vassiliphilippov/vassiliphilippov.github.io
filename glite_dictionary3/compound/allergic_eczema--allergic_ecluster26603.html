allergic eczema

noun
eczema from allergic reaction
Pronunciation: / əˈlɝːdʒɪk ˈɛksɪmə / (AmE), / əˈlɜːdʒɪk ˈɛksɪmə / (BrE)
A form of eczema caused by an allergic reaction, resulting in itchy, inflamed skin.

Examples

allergic eczema flare-up
Many children outgrow allergic eczema as they get older, but some continue to have symptoms.
She was diagnosed with allergic eczema after breaking out in a rash caused by her new lotion.
Wearing gloves can help prevent flare-ups of allergic eczema due to contact with cleaning products.
