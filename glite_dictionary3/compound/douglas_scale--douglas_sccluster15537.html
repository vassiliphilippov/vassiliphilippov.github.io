Douglas scale

noun
scale for measuring wave height
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡləs skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale used for measuring the height of sea waves, typically from 0 (calm) to 9 (phenomenal)

Examples

Douglas scale for sea waves
Before sailing, the captain checked the Douglas scale to assess sea conditions.
Researchers plotted the daily wave heights according to the Douglas scale for their study.
The weather report indicated a Douglas scale reading of 4, meaning the waves were moderate.
