running title

noun
title printed at top of book pages
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnɪŋ ˈtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ ˈtaɪtəl / (BrE)
a title or abbreviated title printed at the top of each page of a book or section to indicate its name or subject

Examples

running title on each page
Editors often abbreviate chapter names for the running title on each page.
The running title helps readers keep track of where they are in the book.
Without a running title, navigating a lengthy document can be more difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym – running head: a heading printed at the top of each page in a book
