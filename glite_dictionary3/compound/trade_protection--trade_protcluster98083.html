trade protection

noun
economic policy to limit imports
Pronunciation: / treɪd prəˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / treɪd prəˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the practice of imposing duties or quotas on imports to shield domestic industries from foreign competition

Examples

trade protection policies
Some economists argue that trade protection can lead to retaliatory tariffs from other countries.
The government considered increased trade protection to support local manufacturing.
The party's platform explicitly promised stronger trade protection for vulnerable industries.

Related Terms

  • antonym – free trade: international trade left to its natural course without tariffs, quotas, or other restrictions
  • synonym – protectionism: the theory or practice of protecting a country's domestic industries from foreign competition by taxing imports
