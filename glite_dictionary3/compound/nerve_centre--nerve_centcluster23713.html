nerve centre

noun
a group of nerve cells controlling a function
Pronunciation: / nɝv ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / nɜːv ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
a group of nerve cells in the body that controls a particular function or process

Examples

Damage to the spinal nerve centre can affect movement and sensation.
