end-to-end

adverb
from beginning to end
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnd tə ˈɛnd / (AmE), / ˌɛnd tʊ ˈend / (BrE)
from the very beginning to the very end; covering the entire process or period continuously.

Examples

end-to-end coverage
He watched the marathon end-to-end on television, cheering for every runner.
Her commitment to excellence was visible end-to-end in the project.
The concert was entertaining end-to-end and never lost its energy.
