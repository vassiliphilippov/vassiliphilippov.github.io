tardive dyskinesia

noun
involuntary movement disorder (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrdɪv dɪskɪˈniʒə / (AmE), / ˈtɑːdɪv dɪskɪˈniːziə / (BrE)
A neurological disorder characterized by involuntary movements of the tongue, face, trunk, or limbs, often resulting from long-term use of certain antipsychotic medications.

Examples

tardive dyskinesia after medication
Researchers are seeking new ways to prevent tardive dyskinesia in vulnerable patients.
Some patients develop tardive dyskinesia after years of antipsychotic treatment.
Symptoms of tardive dyskinesia include repetitive, involuntary movements of the facial muscles.
