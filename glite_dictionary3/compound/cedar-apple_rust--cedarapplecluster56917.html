cedar-apple rust

noun
fungal disease affecting apples and pears
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːdər ˈæpəl rʌst / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə ˈapəl rʌst / (BrE)
a fungal disease caused by a rust fungus (Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae), resulting in orange or rust-colored spots on apples, pears, and related plants

Examples

cedar-apple rust infection on apples
Cedar-apple rust can be identified by the orange spots it causes on infected leaves and fruit.
Farmers use fungicides to control the spread of cedar-apple rust on apple trees.
The orchard lost much of its crop to cedar-apple rust this season.
