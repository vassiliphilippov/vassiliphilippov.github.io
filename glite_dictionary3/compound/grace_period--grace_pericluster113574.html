grace period

noun
extra time allowed after a deadline
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪs ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ɡreɪs ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
an additional period of time allowed after the official deadline for fulfilling an obligation, during which penalties are not enforced

Examples

a grace period for payment
If you miss the renewal date, you still have a ten-day grace period to submit your paperwork.
The credit card company offers a grace period so you can pay your bill without late fees.
University students were relieved to learn there was a grace period for tuition payments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deadline: the latest time by which something must be completed
  • synonym – extension: an additional time allowed to complete something
