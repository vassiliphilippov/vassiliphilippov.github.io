scissors grip

noun
wrestling hold using locked legs
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪzərz ɡrɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəz ɡrɪp / (BrE)
a wrestling hold in which a person wraps their legs around an opponent's body or head and squeezes by pressing the feet together

Examples

scissors grip on opponent
Applying a scissors grip can be an effective way to immobilize your rival during competition.
The wrestler broke free from the scissors grip just before the referee called the match.
With a quick move, she locked her opponent in a scissors grip and forced him to submit.
