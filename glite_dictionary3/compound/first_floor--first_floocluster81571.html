first floor

noun
ground-level (US) or first above ground (UK) floor in a building
Pronunciation: / fɝst flɔɹ / (AmE), / fɜːst flɔː / (BrE)
the floor of a building at or nearest to the ground level; in American English, the ground-level floor, while in British English, the floor above the ground floor

Examples

British hotels often have the restaurant on the first floor, just above the lobby.
