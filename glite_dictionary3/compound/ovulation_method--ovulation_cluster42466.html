ovulation method

noun
natural family planning based on cervical mucus changes
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvjəˈleɪʃən ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˌɒvjʊˈleɪʃən ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of natural family planning in which a woman's fertile period is identified by observing changes in cervical mucus, which becomes clearer and more slippery around ovulation

Examples

ovulation method for family planning
Many couples use the ovulation method to help plan or avoid pregnancy.
Medical professionals sometimes recommend the ovulation method to women seeking non-hormonal birth control.
She learned how to observe her body using the ovulation method as a form of contraception.
