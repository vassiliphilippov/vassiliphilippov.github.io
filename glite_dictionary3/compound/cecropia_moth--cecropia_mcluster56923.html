cecropia moth

noun
large North American silkworm moth
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkroʊpiə mɔθ / (AmE), / sɪˈkrəʊpiə mɒθ / (BrE)
A large North American silkworm moth (Hyalophora cecropia) with distinctive reddish-brown wings and eye-like spots; its larvae feed on the leaves of various forest trees.

cecropia moth wingspan
A cecropia moth can have a wingspan of up to six inches, making it one of the largest moths in North America.
The larvae of the cecropia moth are known for their impressive size and bright colors.
