Scots pine

noun
a species of pine tree found in Europe and Asia
Pronunciation: / skɑts paɪn / (AmE), / skɒts paɪn / (BrE)
a medium to large pine tree (Pinus sylvestris) native to northern Europe and Asia, with reddish-brown, flaky bark and two needles per cluster

Examples

tall Scots pine tree
In the Scottish Highlands, Scots pine forests are home to a variety of wildlife.
The Scots pine is often used for timber and reforestation projects across Europe.
The distinctive red-brown bark of the Scots pine helps to identify it among other trees.
