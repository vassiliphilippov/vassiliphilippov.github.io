criminal offence

noun
an act punishable by law
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪmɪnl əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnl əˈfɛns / (BrE)
An act that breaks the law and is punishable by the state; usually regarded as morally wrong or harmful to society.

Examples

serious criminal offence
Forgery is considered a serious criminal offence in most countries.
He was arrested and charged with a criminal offence after the incident.
The new law aims to make cyberbullying a criminal offence.

Related Terms

  • synonym – crime: an act that is against the law and punishable by the authorities
