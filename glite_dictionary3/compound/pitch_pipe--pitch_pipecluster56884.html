pitch pipe

noun
device for producing standard musical pitch
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ paɪp / (AmE), / pɪtʃ paɪp / (BrE)
a small device that produces a musical note of standard pitch, used to help singers or musicians find the correct starting note

Examples

pitch pipe for singers
Before each song, the a cappella group used a pitch pipe to find the right key.
She carried a pitch pipe to help her tune her violin before concerts.
The choir director blew into the pitch pipe to give everyone their starting note.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tuning fork: a metal device used to produce a fixed pitch for tuning instruments
