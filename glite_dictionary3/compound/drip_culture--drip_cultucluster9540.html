drip culture

noun
hydroponic technique with nutrient drip
Pronunciation: / drɪp ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / drɪp ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
a method of hydroponic cultivation in which nutrient solutions are allowed to drip slowly onto an inert medium to nourish growing plants

Examples

drip culture system
Many growers prefer drip culture because it reduces water waste and maintains optimal root moisture.
Switching to drip culture improved the yields and health of their tomato plants.
The greenhouse relies on a drip culture system to ensure plants receive a constant supply of nutrients.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hydroponics: method of growing plants without soil using mineral nutrient solutions
