carbon dating

noun
method of dating organic remains using carbon-14
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˌdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a method for determining the age of ancient organic materials by measuring the amount of radioactive carbon-14 present in them

Examples

carbon dating ancient bones
Carbon dating has revolutionized how archaeologists estimate the age of artifacts and fossils.
Scientists used carbon dating to determine the age of the ancient bones found at the site.
The results from carbon dating indicated that the manuscript was over a thousand years old.
