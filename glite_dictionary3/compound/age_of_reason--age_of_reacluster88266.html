Age of Reason

noun
European intellectual movement (1650–1800) favoring reason over tradition
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒ əv ˈriːzən / (AmE), / eɪdʒ əv ˈriːzn̩ / (BrE)
the period in European history from about 1650 to 1800, during which reason, science, and individualism were emphasized over tradition and established doctrine; also known as the Enlightenment

Examples

the Age of Reason in Europe
Many modern political ideals have their roots in the Age of Reason.
The Age of Reason encouraged questioning of traditional authority in religion and society.
The scientific discoveries of the Age of Reason transformed the way people understood the world.
