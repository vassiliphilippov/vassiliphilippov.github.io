conditional contract

noun
contract dependent on a condition
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənl ˈkɑntrækt / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃnəl ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
a contract whose performance depends on the occurrence or non-occurrence of a specific event or fact

Examples

sign a conditional contract
A conditional contract does not become enforceable until its stipulated event happens.
The sale was governed by a conditional contract that required the buyer to secure financing within 30 days.
They signed a conditional contract stating that payment would be made only if the project was completed on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – contract: a binding agreement between two or more persons that is enforceable by law
