superior thyroid vein

noun
vein draining upper thyroid into jugular
Pronunciation: / suːˈpɪriɚ ˈθaɪrɔɪd veɪn / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˈθaɪərɔɪd veɪn / (BrE)
a vein on each side of the neck that drains blood from the upper part of the thyroid gland into the internal jugular vein

Examples

superior thyroid vein drains blood
Anatomical diagrams often label the superior thyroid vein as an important structure in thyroid surgery.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully identified and tied off the superior thyroid vein to prevent bleeding.
The superior thyroid vein drains blood from the upper part of the thyroid gland directly into the internal jugular vein.
