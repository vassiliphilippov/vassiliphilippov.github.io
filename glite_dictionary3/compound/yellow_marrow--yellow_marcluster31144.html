yellow marrow

noun
fatty bone marrow in adults
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
A type of bone marrow that is yellow due to its high fat content, found mainly in the long bones of adults.

Examples

yellow marrow in long bones
As people age, red marrow is gradually replaced by yellow marrow in certain bones.
Extreme blood loss can sometimes cause yellow marrow to revert back to red marrow.
In adults, most of the yellow marrow is found in the long bones of the arms and legs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – red marrow: bone marrow that is red and produces blood cells
