potential divider

noun
series resistor circuit reducing voltage
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəl dɪˈvaɪdər / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃl dɪˈvaɪdə / (BrE)
an arrangement of resistors in series used to produce a specific fraction of a voltage from a source

Examples

potential divider circuit
A potential divider is commonly used to generate reference voltages in electronic circuits.
By adjusting the resistances in a potential divider, you can control the output voltage.
The sensor was connected as part of a potential divider to measure changing resistance.
