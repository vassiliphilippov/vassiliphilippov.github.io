bush hibiscus

noun
Australian shrub with purple flowers
Pronunciation: / bʊʃ haɪˈbɪskəs / (AmE), / bʊʃ hɪˈbɪskəs / (BrE)
A shrub native to southern and western Australia, with unlobed or shallowly lobed toothed leaves and purple flowers; sometimes classified within the genus Hibiscus.

Examples

bush hibiscus plant
Gardeners value the bush hibiscus for its resilience in dry climates.
In parts of Australia, the bush hibiscus grows wild along the roadsides.
The bush hibiscus brightened the garden with its striking purple flowers.
