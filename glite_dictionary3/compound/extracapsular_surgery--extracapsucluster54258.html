extracapsular surgery

noun
type of cataract surgery
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.strəˈkæp.sjə.lɚ ˈsɝː.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈkæp.sjʊ.lə ˈsɜː.dʒəri / (BrE)
a type of cataract surgery where only the front of the lens is removed, while the back of the lens capsule is left intact to support a lens implant

Examples

extracapsular surgery for cataracts
After undergoing extracapsular surgery, the patient noticed improved vision within a few weeks.
The ophthalmologist recommended extracapsular surgery for her cataract removal.
Unlike older techniques, extracapsular surgery leaves the back of the lens capsule in place.
