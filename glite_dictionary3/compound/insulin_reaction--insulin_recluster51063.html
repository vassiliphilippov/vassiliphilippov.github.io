insulin reaction

noun
low blood sugar due to excess insulin
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnsəlɪn riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪnsjʊlɪn riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a medical condition caused by an excessive amount of insulin in the body, leading to abnormally low blood sugar and potentially causing coma

Examples

insulin reaction after injection
Doctors educate their diabetic patients about how to recognize the signs of an insulin reaction.
Quick action is important when someone is experiencing an insulin reaction to prevent further complications.
The patient suffered an insulin reaction after accidentally taking too much of her medication.
