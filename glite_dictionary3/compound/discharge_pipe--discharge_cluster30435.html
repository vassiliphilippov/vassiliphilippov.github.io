discharge pipe

noun
pipe for expelling fluids
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.tʃɑrd paɪp / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ paɪp / (BrE)
A pipe through which fluids are released or carried away, especially from a system or container.

Examples

discharge pipe carries water
After the heavy storm, water gushed out of the discharge pipe into the drainage ditch.
Engineers inspected the discharge pipe for blockages that could impede the flow of water.
The discharge pipe carries wastewater away from the building to the main sewer line.
