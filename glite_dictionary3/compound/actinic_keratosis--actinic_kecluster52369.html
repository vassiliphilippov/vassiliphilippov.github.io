actinic keratosis

noun
precancerous skin lesion from sun exposure
Pronunciation: / ækˈtɪnɪk ˌkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪk ˌkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A rough, scaly patch on the skin caused by long-term exposure to sunlight, considered a precancerous condition.

Examples

actinic keratosis on skin
After years of working outdoors, he developed actinic keratosis on his hands and face.
Dermatologists recommend regular skin checks to detect any signs of actinic keratosis early.
Wearing sunscreen can help prevent actinic keratosis and other sun-related skin problems.
