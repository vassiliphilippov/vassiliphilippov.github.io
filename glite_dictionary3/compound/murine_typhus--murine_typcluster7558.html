murine typhus

noun
acute flea-borne rickettsial disease
Pronunciation: / ˈmjʊr.iːn ˈtaɪ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈmjʊə.raɪn ˈtɪ.fəs / (BrE)
an acute infectious disease caused by rickettsia bacteria and transmitted by fleas, marked by fever, chills, muscle aches, and a rash

Examples

murine typhus infection symptoms
Cases of murine typhus are rare in urban areas but can still occur where rodents and fleas are present.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with murine typhus after she developed a high fever and rash following a flea bite.
Prompt treatment with antibiotics is essential to recover from murine typhus.
