Tudor architecture

noun
English architectural style (Tudor era)
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː.dɚ ˌɑːr.kɪˈtɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.də ˌɑː.kɪˈtɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
A style of English-Gothic architecture from the Tudor period (1485–1603), characterized by half-timbered houses, steeply pitched roofs, and ornate brickwork.
