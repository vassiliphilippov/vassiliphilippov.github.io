arteria centralis retinae

noun
central artery of the retina
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪriə sɛnˈtreɪlɪs rɛˈtiniː / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə sɛnˈtreɪlaɪ rɛˈtɪniː / (BrE)
the central artery of the retina; a branch of the ophthalmic artery that enters the eyeball with the optic nerve and supplies blood to the retina

Examples

arteria centralis retinae supplies retina
Blockage of the arteria centralis retinae can lead to sudden vision loss.
Damage to the arteria centralis retinae may cause irreversible retinal damage.
The arteria centralis retinae enters the eye alongside the optic nerve.
