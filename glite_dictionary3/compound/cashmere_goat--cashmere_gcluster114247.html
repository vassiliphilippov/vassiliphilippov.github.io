Cashmere goat

noun
breed of goat producing cashmere wool
Pronunciation: / ˈkæʒmɪr ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæʃmɪə ɡəʊt / (BrE)
A breed of goat from the Himalayas noted for its soft, silky undercoat, which is the source of cashmere wool.

Examples

Cashmere goat produces wool
A single Cashmere goat can produce only a few ounces of cashmere wool each year.
Farmers in mountainous areas raise Cashmere goats for their prized undercoat.
The fine wool obtained from the Cashmere goat is used to make luxurious sweaters and scarves.
