neon induction lamp

noun
lamp using neon and induction for luminescence
Pronunciation: / ˈniːɑn ɪnˈdʌkʃən læmp / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn ɪnˈdʌkʃən læmp / (BrE)
A type of gas-discharge lamp containing neon at low pressure, illuminated by high-frequency currents induced around the tube rather than by direct electrodes.

Examples

neon induction lamp technology
Compared to traditional bulbs, a neon induction lamp lasts significantly longer and generates less heat.
He replaced his old sign with a neon induction lamp for better visibility and lower maintenance.
The neon induction lamp is used in some scientific instruments due to its efficient light output.

Related Terms

  • synonym – induction lamp: lamp that produces light by inducing currents in a gas or phosphor without electrodes
