terminus ad quem

noun
final or limiting point; endpoint
Pronunciation: / ˌtɝː.mə.nəs æd ˈkweɪm / (AmE), / ˌtɜː.mɪ.nəs æd ˈkweɪm / (BrE)
the final or latest limiting point in a period of time or a process, especially as the end limit in legal, philosophical, or academic contexts

Examples

terminus ad quem of a period
Historians debate the terminus ad quem for the manuscript's completion.
In contract law, the terminus ad quem determines the end of the period of liability.
The court set July 1 as the terminus ad quem for all submissions.
