prison house

noun
correctional institution for confinement or punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzən ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈprɪz(ə)n ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A building or institution where people are kept as punishment for crimes or while awaiting trial; a prison.

He spent several years in the prison house before his sentence was overturned.
Literature often uses the prison house as a symbol of lost freedom.
