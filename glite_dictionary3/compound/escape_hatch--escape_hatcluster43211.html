escape hatch

noun
emergency exit hatch
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪp hætʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp hætʃ / (BrE)
a small door or opening, especially in a vehicle or building, that provides a way to get out quickly in an emergency

Examples

escape hatch in a submarine
All crew members must know the location of the nearest escape hatch.
Smoke filled the cockpit, so the pilot quickly opened the escape hatch and climbed out.
The submarine was equipped with an escape hatch for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – emergency exit: a door or way out used only in emergencies
