residual standard deviation

noun
statistical measure of regression error
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪdʒuəl ˈstændərd dɪviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjʊəl ˈstandəd dɪvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a statistical measure representing the standard deviation of the errors (residuals) from a linear regression model, used to assess the accuracy of predictions.

Examples

residual standard deviation in regression analysis
A lower residual standard deviation indicates that the data points are closer to the predicted values.
Researchers reported a residual standard deviation of 1.2, suggesting good model fit.
The residual standard deviation was calculated to assess the accuracy of the regression model.

Related Terms

  • synonym – standard deviation: a measure of the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of values
