to steal a base

verb
advance to next base in baseball without a hit
Pronunciation: / stil ə beɪs / (AmE), / stiːl ə beɪs / (BrE)
(in baseball) to advance to the next base while the pitcher is delivering the ball to home plate, without a hit or error from the opposing team.

Examples

steal a base in baseball
He managed to steal a base in the seventh inning, giving his team a better chance to score.
She became known for her ability to steal a base even against the best catchers.
The coach encourages players to try to steal a base when the pitcher is distracted.
