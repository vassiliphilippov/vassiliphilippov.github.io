right to life

noun
fundamental right not to be unjustly killed
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt tə laɪf / (AmE), / ˈraɪt tʊ laɪf / (BrE)
the fundamental right of every human being to not be unjustly killed or deprived of life by others, often protected by law or moral principle

Examples

protect the right to life
International law recognizes the right to life as a basic human entitlement.
Many debates about the death penalty center around the right to life of the accused.
The constitution guarantees every citizen the right to life and personal security.

Related Terms

  • antonym – right to die: the right of a person to refuse life-prolonging measures
  • synonym – right to liberty: the right to personal freedom and autonomy
