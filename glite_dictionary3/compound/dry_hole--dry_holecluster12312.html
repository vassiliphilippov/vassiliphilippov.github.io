dry hole

noun
an unsuccessful attempt, esp. in drilling
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ həʊl / (BrE)
An attempt or operation that produces no result or success, especially a well drilled for oil or gas that yields no resources.

Examples

hit a dry hole
After investing heavily, the company hit a dry hole and lost millions.
The explorers drilled several wells, but each one was a dry hole.
Their search for evidence proved to be a dry hole.

Related Terms

  • synonym – failure: lack of success
  • antonym – strike oil: to discover something valuable or successful
