fee simple

noun
absolute ownership of land in law
Pronunciation: / fi ˈsɪmpəl / (AmE), / fiː ˈsɪmpəl / (BrE)
An estate in land without limitation to any particular class of heirs, giving the owner absolute ownership and freedom to sell or bequeath it.

Examples

fee simple ownership rights
Because she held the land in fee simple, she could leave it to anyone in her will.
Purchasing property in fee simple gives you the most comprehensive rights of ownership.
The family owned the estate in fee simple for several generations.
