amniotic fluid

noun
fluid surrounding embryo or fetus in pregnancy
Pronunciation: / æmˈniːɑːtɪk ˈfluːɪd / (AmE), / æmˈnɪətɪk ˈfluːɪd / (BrE)
the clear, slightly yellowish fluid that surrounds and protects the embryo or fetus in the amnion during pregnancy

Examples

low amniotic fluid level
A low level of amniotic fluid can indicate complications during pregnancy.
Doctors may test amniotic fluid to check for certain genetic disorders in a developing baby.
During pregnancy, amniotic fluid cushions the fetus and helps regulate its temperature.

Related Terms

  • synonym – amniotic sac: the membrane that encloses the fetus and amniotic fluid
