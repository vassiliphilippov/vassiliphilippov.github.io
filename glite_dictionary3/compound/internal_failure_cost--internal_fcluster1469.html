internal failure cost

noun
costs from correcting defects internally
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɝnəl ˈfeɪljɚ kɑst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˈfeɪljə kɒst / (BrE)
the costs incurred from defects that are identified and corrected within a company before products reach customers, including the expenses of discarding or repairing defective items

Examples

internal failure cost of repairs
Identifying packaging errors in-house helped minimize overall internal failure costs.
Repairing defective units before shipping contributes to internal failure cost.
The company reduced its internal failure costs by improving its inspection processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – external failure cost: costs arising from defects found after delivery to customers
