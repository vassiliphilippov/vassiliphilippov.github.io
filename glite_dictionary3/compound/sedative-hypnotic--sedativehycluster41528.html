sedative-hypnotic

noun
drug reducing anxiety and inducing sleep
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛdəˌtɪv haɪˈpnɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛd.ə.tɪv haɪˈnɒt.ɪk / (BrE)
A drug that depresses central nervous system activity, reduces anxiety, and induces sleep; used to treat insomnia and anxiety disorders.

Examples

a sedative-hypnotic drug
Doctors may prescribe a sedative-hypnotic to help patients with severe insomnia.
Overuse of sedative-hypnotic medications can lead to dependency and withdrawal symptoms.
The pharmacist explained the side effects associated with sedative-hypnotic drugs.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stimulant: drug that increases activity of the central nervous system
  • synonym – tranquilizer: drug that reduces tension or anxiety
