cartilage bone

noun
bone developing within cartilage
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrtɪlɪdʒ boʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtɪlɪdʒ bəʊn / (BrE)
A bone that forms within cartilage, rather than from fibrous tissue, during embryonic development; also called endochondral bone.

Examples

cartilage bone formation
Certain fractures can heal differently depending on whether the bone is a cartilage bone or a membrane bone.
During fetal development, the femur is first formed as a cartilage bone before becoming fully ossified.
Most of the long bones in the human body are classified as cartilage bones.

Related Terms

  • synonym – endochondral bone: another term for cartilage bone, bone formed within cartilage
  • antonym – membrane bone: bone formed directly from connective tissue without cartilage stage
