genetic engineering

noun
deliberate modification of genetic material
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate modification of the characteristics of an organism by manipulating its genetic material, especially by introducing or combining DNA from different sources

Examples

genetic engineering of crops
Many crops are improved through genetic engineering to increase their resistance to pests.
She is researching ways to use genetic engineering to treat hereditary diseases.
The ethics of genetic engineering remain a topic of intense debate among scientists and the public.
