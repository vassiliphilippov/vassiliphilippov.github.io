bacterial plaque

noun
film of bacteria on teeth
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɪriəl plæk / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriəl plɑːk / (BrE)
a soft film of bacteria and mucus that forms on the teeth and can lead to tooth decay and gum disease if not removed

Examples

bacterial plaque on teeth
Dentists recommend flossing daily to remove bacterial plaque from between the teeth.
If not treated, bacterial plaque can harden into tartar, increasing the risk of gum disease.
Regular brushing helps prevent the buildup of bacterial plaque on your teeth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dental plaque: a sticky deposit on teeth in which bacteria proliferate
  • degree higher – tartar: hardened dental plaque
