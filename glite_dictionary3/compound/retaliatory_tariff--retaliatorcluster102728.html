retaliatory tariff

noun
tariff imposed in reprisal against another country's trade measures
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtæliəˌtɔri ˈtærɪf / (AmE), / rɪˈtæliətəri ˈtærɪf / (BrE)
A tariff imposed by a country on imports from another country as a response to that country's trade barriers or tariffs.

Examples

impose a retaliatory tariff
After the U.S. imposed steel tariffs, China quickly announced its own retaliatory tariff on American goods.
The government introduced a retaliatory tariff in response to new duties on its exports.
The threat of a retaliatory tariff can escalate a trade dispute between two countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym – free trade: international trade left to its natural course without tariffs or restrictions
  • synonym – protective tariff: a tariff intended to protect domestic industries from foreign competition
