carbon dioxide

noun
a colorless, odorless gas (CO₂)
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrbən daɪˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a heavy, odorless, colorless gas (CO₂) formed during respiration and combustion, and absorbed from the air by plants in photosynthesis

Examples

carbon dioxide emissions
Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide into the environment.
Plants convert carbon dioxide into oxygen through the process of photosynthesis.
The amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased significantly over the past century.
