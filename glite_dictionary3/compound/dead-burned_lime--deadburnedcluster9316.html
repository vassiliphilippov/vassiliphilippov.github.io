dead-burned lime

noun
lime over-fired and rendered chemically unreactive
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛd bɝnd ˈlaɪm / (AmE), / ˌdɛd bɜːnd ˈlaɪm / (BrE)
Lime that has been heated to a temperature high enough to lose its chemical reactivity, resulting in poor or no bonding strength.

Examples

dead-burned lime in cement
Construction quality suffers when dead-burned lime is accidentally produced instead of reactive quicklime.
If you fire limestone too long, you end up with dead-burned lime that won't react properly.
The chemical industry uses dead-burned lime for certain refractory applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overburned lime: lime that has been heated too much
