Dutch-processed cocoa

noun
alkalized cocoa powder used in baking
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌtʃ ˌproʊˌsɛst ˈkoʊˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃ ˌprəʊ.sɛst ˈkəʊ.kəʊ / (BrE)
cocoa powder that has been treated with an alkalizing agent to reduce acidity and give a milder flavor and darker color

Examples

Dutch-processed cocoa in baking
Many chocolate cake recipes call for Dutch-processed cocoa for its subtle flavor and rich color.
The Dutch-processed cocoa gave the brownies a smooth, mellow taste.
You should not substitute Dutch-processed cocoa for natural cocoa powder without adjusting the recipe.

Related Terms

  • antonym – natural cocoa: unsweetened cocoa powder that has not been alkalized
