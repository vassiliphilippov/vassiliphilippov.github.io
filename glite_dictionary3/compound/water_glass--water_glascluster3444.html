water glass

noun
ancient time-measuring device using water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
An ancient device for measuring time, consisting of a vessel from which water escapes at a steady rate.

Examples

ancient water glass timer
A water glass was commonly found in medieval courts before mechanical clocks became widespread.
By marking the vessel, the accuracy of the water glass could be improved significantly.
The ancient Greeks used a water glass to keep time during speeches.
