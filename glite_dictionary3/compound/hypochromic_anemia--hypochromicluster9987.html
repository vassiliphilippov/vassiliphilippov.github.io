hypochromic anemia

noun
anemia with reduced hemoglobin in red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈkroʊmɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrəʊmɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
a type of anemia characterized by red blood cells that have less than the normal amount of hemoglobin, resulting in paler cells

Examples

hypochromic anemia diagnosis
Doctors monitor patients with hypochromic anemia for symptoms, including fatigue and pale skin.
Iron deficiency is the most common cause of hypochromic anemia in children.
The patient's blood tests confirmed a diagnosis of hypochromic anemia.
