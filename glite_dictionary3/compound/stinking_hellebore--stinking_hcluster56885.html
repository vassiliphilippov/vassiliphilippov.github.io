stinking hellebore

noun
a poisonous plant with foul-smelling leaves and flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈhɛlɪˌbɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈhɛlɪbɔː / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial plant (Helleborus foetidus) with divided leaves and clusters of greenish flowers, known for its foul smell and poisonous qualities if ingested

Examples

stinking hellebore plant
Despite its attractive appearance, the stinking hellebore gives off a strong, unpleasant odor.
Gardeners are often warned not to ingest stinking hellebore because of its toxic properties.
The stinking hellebore produces clusters of pale green flowers early in spring.
