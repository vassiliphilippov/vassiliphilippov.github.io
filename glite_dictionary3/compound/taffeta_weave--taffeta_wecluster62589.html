taffeta weave

noun
basic textile weave with checkerboard effect
Pronunciation: / ˈtæf.ə.tə wiːv / (AmE), / ˈtaf.ɪ.tə wiːv / (BrE)
a basic weave in which the weft and warp threads cross alternately, creating a checkerboard effect, typical of taffeta fabric

Examples

taffeta weave pattern
Learning to identify a taffeta weave is essential for students of textile design.
Silk dresses often feature a taffeta weave for a crisp and smooth finish.
The distinctive sheen of taffeta is achieved through its tight taffeta weave.
