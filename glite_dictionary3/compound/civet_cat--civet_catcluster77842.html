civet cat

noun
North American mammal with a ringed tail
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvət ˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪt ˌkæt / (BrE)
A raccoon-like mammal native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for its long bushy tail with black and white rings; also called ringtail or bassarisk.

Examples

ring-tailed civet cat
Children were excited to see the bushy tail of a civet cat in the zoo.
Farmers sometimes spot a civet cat looking for food near their barns.
The civet cat is often seen prowling around the forests at dusk.
