inhalation anthrax

noun
severe anthrax infection from inhaling spores
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌheɪlˈeɪʃən ˈænθræks / (AmE), / ɪnˌheɪlˈeɪʃən ˈænθræks / (BrE)
A severe form of anthrax infection contracted by inhaling spores of Bacillus anthracis, leading to acute respiratory illness and often fatal cardiovascular complications if untreated.

Examples

severe inhalation anthrax case
Prompt antibiotic treatment is crucial for anyone suspected of having inhalation anthrax.
The patient developed inhalation anthrax after being exposed to contaminated mail.
Unlike cutaneous anthrax, inhalation anthrax often presents with severe respiratory distress.
