old growth

noun
relatively untouched mature forest
Pronunciation: / oʊld ɡroʊθ / (AmE), / əʊld ɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
A forest or woodland characterized by a mature or over-mature ecosystem that is largely unaffected by human activity.

Examples

old growth forest
Biologists studied the unique biodiversity found only in old growth forests.
The park protects a rare stand of old growth that has survived for centuries.
Timber companies are often prohibited from logging areas of old growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym – primary forest: a forest that has never been logged or significantly disturbed by humans
  • antonym – secondary forest: a forest that has regrown after significant disturbance or logging
