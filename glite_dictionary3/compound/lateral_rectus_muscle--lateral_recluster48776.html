lateral rectus muscle

noun
eye muscle that turns the eyeball outward
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl ˈrɛk.təs ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈlat.ər.əl ˈrɛk.təs ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
a muscle on the outer side of the eyeball that, when it contracts, turns the eye outward away from the nose

Examples

lateral rectus muscle turns eye
Because the lateral rectus muscle contracts, the eye is able to move outward away from the nose.
Damage to the lateral rectus muscle can cause the eye to turn inward, leading to double vision.
The surgeon carefully examined the lateral rectus muscle during the procedure to ensure it was functioning properly.
