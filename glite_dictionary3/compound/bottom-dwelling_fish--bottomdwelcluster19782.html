bottom-dwelling fish

noun
fish living near the bottom of water
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑtəm ˌdwɛlɪŋ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˌdwɛlɪŋ fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish that lives near or on the bottom of a body of water, such as a lake, river, or ocean

Examples

bottom-dwelling fish in rivers
Catfish are a common type of bottom-dwelling fish found in rivers and lakes.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep bottom-dwelling fish to help clean algae from the tank.
Scientists study bottom-dwelling fish to monitor the health of marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • synonym – benthic: relating to the lowest level of a body of water
  • antonym – pelagic fish: fish that live in the open water, not near the bottom
