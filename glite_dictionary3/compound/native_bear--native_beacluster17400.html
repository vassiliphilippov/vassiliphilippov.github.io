native bear

noun
koala; Australian tree-dwelling marsupial
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪ.tɪv bɛr / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv beə / (BrE)
an Australian arboreal marsupial, also known as the koala, with a stout, grey body, no tail, and large furry ears, that feeds mainly on eucalyptus leaves and bark.

Examples

native bear climbing trees
The native bear is threatened by habitat loss and disease.
The native bear spends most of its time sleeping in eucalyptus trees.
Tourists often hope to see a native bear during their visit to Australia.
