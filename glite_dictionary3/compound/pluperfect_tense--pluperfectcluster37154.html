pluperfect tense

noun
verb tense showing completed past action
Pronunciation: / ˌpluˈpɜrfɪkt tɛns / (AmE), / ˌpluːˈpɜːfɪkt tɛns / (BrE)
a verb tense used to indicate an action that was completed before another action in the past; also known as the past perfect tense

Examples

pluperfect tense in a sentence
In the sentence 'She had already left,' the verb is in the pluperfect tense.
Students sometimes confuse the pluperfect tense with the simple past.
The pluperfect tense is often used in storytelling to show an event that happened before the main story.
