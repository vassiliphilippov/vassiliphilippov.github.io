long-staple

adjective
having relatively long fibers (esp. cotton)
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈsteɪpəl / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈsteɪpəl / (BrE)
(of fibers, especially cotton) having fibers that are relatively long, typically resulting in higher quality and strength.

Examples

long-staple cotton fibers
Egyptian cotton is prized for its long-staple fibers, which make for softer and stronger fabrics.
Only long-staple varieties are used to produce the finest quality yarns.
The manufacturer advertises this sheet set as being made from 100% long-staple cotton.

Related Terms

  • antonym – short-staple: having relatively short fibers (especially of cotton)
