moral excellence

noun
the quality of always acting rightly; virtue
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəl ˌɛksəˈlɛns / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˌɛksəˈlɛns / (BrE)
The quality or state of consistently doing what is right and avoiding what is wrong; virtue.

Examples

moral excellence in character
Achieving moral excellence often requires great self-discipline and reflection.
She was admired by all for her moral excellence and unwavering sense of justice.
Throughout history, leaders known for their moral excellence have inspired their followers to do good.

Related Terms

  • antonym – vice: immoral or wicked behavior
  • synonym – virtue: behavior showing high moral standards
