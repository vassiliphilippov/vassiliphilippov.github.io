heavy metal

noun
dense, often toxic, metallic element
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈmɛtl / (BrE)
a metallic element having a high density or high atomic weight, especially those that are toxic such as lead or mercury

Examples

heavy metal pollution
Exposure to heavy metal fumes in factories poses a significant risk to workers.
Many environmental regulations limit the release of heavy metal waste into rivers.
Some heavy metals, like cadmium and mercury, can accumulate in living organisms and cause health problems.
