occupational therapy

noun
therapy using daily activities for rehabilitation
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːkjuˈpeɪʃənl ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən(ə)l ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a type of therapy that helps people participate in daily life activities despite physical or mental limitations, through engagement in meaningful activities

Examples

occupational therapy after injury
After her accident, she began occupational therapy to regain her independence at home.
Children with developmental delays often benefit from occupational therapy sessions at school.
He chose a career in occupational therapy because he wanted to help people overcome challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym – physical therapy: treatment using physical methods, such as exercise or massage
