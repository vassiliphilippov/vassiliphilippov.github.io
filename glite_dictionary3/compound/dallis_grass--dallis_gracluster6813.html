dallis grass

noun
a pasture grass native to South America
Pronunciation: / ˈdælɪs ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈdælɪs ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A tall, tufted perennial grass (Paspalum dilatatum) native to South America and widely naturalized in the southern United States, often used as pasture and forage.

Examples

dallis grass in lawns
Dallis grass quickly spreads throughout lawns in the southern states during the warm season.
The farmer chose to sow dallis grass for its hardiness and nutritional value as forage.
You can identify dallis grass by its coarse texture and distinctive seed heads.
