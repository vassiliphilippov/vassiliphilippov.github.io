national censorship

noun
government censorship of cross-border communications
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəl ˈsɛnsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈnaʃənəl ˈsɛnsəʃɪp / (BrE)
the control or suppression by a civil authority of information and communications entering, leaving, or crossing a country's borders, especially by a national government

Examples

national censorship of news
Documents revealed the extent of national censorship on international correspondence during that era.

Some activists criticize national censorship as a violation of the right to free speech.
