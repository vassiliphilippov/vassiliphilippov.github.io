carbon filter

noun
filter using activated carbon
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˈfɪltər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
A filter that uses activated carbon to remove contaminants and impurities from liquids or gases by chemical absorption.

Examples

carbon filter removes odors
Industrial facilities often rely on a carbon filter system to treat exhaust gases before release.
Many water pitchers come equipped with a carbon filter to improve taste and eliminate chemicals.
The air purifier needs a new carbon filter to effectively remove smoke and odors from the room.
