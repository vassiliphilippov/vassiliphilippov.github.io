sea lion

noun
online tactic of bad-faith questioning
Pronunciation: / ˈsi ˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
(informal, internet) a person or tactic in online discussions that involves repeatedly, politely requesting evidence or explanations in a way that avoids engaging with the argument and frustrates opponents.

Examples

online sea lion behavior
During the online discussion, several users accused him of being a sea lion constantly demanding sources.
Reporters warned the forum about persistent sea lions derailing threads with polite but bad-faith questions.
She lost patience after a single sea lion repeatedly requested evidence without engaging with arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym – good-faith questioning: genuine requests for clarification or evidence intended to understand and engage with an argument
  • synonym – trolling: posting inflammatory or disruptive messages online to provoke, distract, or upset others
