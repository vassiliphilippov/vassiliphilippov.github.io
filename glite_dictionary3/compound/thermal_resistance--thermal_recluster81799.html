thermal resistance

noun
measure of an object's resistance to heat flow
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜr.məl rɪˈzɪs.təns / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məl rɪˈzɪs.təns / (BrE)
The degree to which a material or object resists the transfer of heat, measured by the temperature difference needed for a given heat flow.

Examples

thermal resistance of insulation
Engineers measured the thermal resistance of the new insulating material before recommending it for construction.
Poor thermal resistance in windows can lead to significant energy loss in a building.
The wall's thermal resistance determines how much heat escapes during winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym – insulation: material or process that reduces heat transfer
