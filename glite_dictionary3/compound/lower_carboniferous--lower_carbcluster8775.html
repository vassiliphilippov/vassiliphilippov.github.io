Lower Carboniferous

noun
early subdivision of the Carboniferous period
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊər ˌkɑr.bəˈnɪf.ɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə ˌkɑː.bəˈnɪf.ər.əs / (BrE)
the earlier subdivision of the Carboniferous period, occurring approximately 345 to 310 million years ago, marked by an increase in land areas and the presence of primitive ammonites and winged insects

Examples

the Lower Carboniferous period
Major geological changes occurred during the Lower Carboniferous, influencing the development of primitive amphibians.
Scientists estimate that the Lower Carboniferous lasted for approximately 35 million years.
The fossils found in the Lower Carboniferous strata provide valuable information about early land plants.
