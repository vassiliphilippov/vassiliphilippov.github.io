hypochromic anaemia

noun
anaemia with decreased hemoglobin in red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈkroʊmɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrəʊmɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A type of anaemia characterized by a reduced concentration of hemoglobin in the red blood cells.

Examples

hypochromic anaemia from iron deficiency
Iron deficiency is one of the most common causes of hypochromic anaemia worldwide.
Patients with hypochromic anaemia often experience fatigue, weakness, and pale skin.
The doctor diagnosed hypochromic anaemia after noting low hemoglobin levels in the blood test.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anemia: condition marked by a deficiency of red cells or of hemoglobin in the blood
