flesh fly

noun
fly whose larvae feed on flesh or carrion
Pronunciation: / flɛʃ flaɪ / (AmE), / flɛʃ flaɪ / (BrE)
A fly of the family Sarcophagidae, whose larvae feed on decaying flesh or occasionally the flesh of living animals.

Examples

flesh fly larvae feed
Infestations of flesh flies can cause serious problems for livestock farmers.
The forensic entomologist identified the insect as a flesh fly based on its striped thorax.
Unlike many other species, the flesh fly often lays live larvae instead of eggs.
