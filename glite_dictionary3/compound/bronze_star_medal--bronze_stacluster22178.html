Bronze Star Medal

noun
U.S. military decoration for heroism or service
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑːnz stɑr ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɒnz stɑː ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
a United States military decoration awarded for acts of heroism, meritorious achievement, or meritorious service, not involving aerial flight, in a combat zone

Examples

receive the Bronze Star Medal
His actions on the battlefield earned him a Bronze Star Medal from the U.S. Army.
