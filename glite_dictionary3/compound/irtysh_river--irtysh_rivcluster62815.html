Irtysh River

proper noun
major river in central Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈɜr.tɪʃ ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɜː.tɪʃ ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
A major river in central Asia that originates in the Altai Mountains in northern China and flows northwest through Kazakhstan and Russia to join the Ob River.

Examples

the Irtysh River flows
Many ships travel along the Irtysh River to transport goods through central Asia.
The Irtysh River plays a crucial role in the hydroelectric power generation of the region.
The source of the Irtysh River can be traced to the Altai Mountains in China.
