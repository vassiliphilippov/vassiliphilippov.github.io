common iliac artery

noun
major artery branching from the abdominal aorta
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ɪˈlaɪ.ək ˈɑːr.t̬əri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ɪˈlaɪ.ək ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
either of the two large arteries that branch from the abdominal aorta and supply blood to the pelvis and lower limbs

Examples

common iliac artery supplies blood
Blockage in the common iliac artery can restrict blood flow to the lower limbs.
During anatomy class, we traced the path of the common iliac artery from the aorta.
The surgeon carefully located the common iliac artery before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – external iliac artery: continuation of the common iliac artery toward the leg
  • degree lower – internal iliac artery: branch of the common iliac artery that supplies the pelvis
