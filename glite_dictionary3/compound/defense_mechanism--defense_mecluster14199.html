defense mechanism

noun
unconscious process reducing anxiety
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
An unconscious psychological process employed to reduce anxiety arising from unacceptable or potentially harmful impulses and feelings.

Examples

psychological defense mechanism
Denial is a common defense mechanism people use when faced with difficult truths.
Projection can serve as a defense mechanism when someone attributes their own feelings to others.
Therapists help patients understand how a defense mechanism might be influencing their behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coping mechanism: a conscious or unconscious strategy to manage stress or anxiety
