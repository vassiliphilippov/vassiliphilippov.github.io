seed coat

noun
outer covering of a seed
Pronunciation: / sid koʊt / (AmE), / siːd kəʊt / (BrE)
the protective outer covering of a seed, especially in flowering plants

Examples

seed coat protects seed
Some seeds have a very hard seed coat that requires scarification to sprout.
The seed coat protects the embryo from physical damage and pathogens.
Water must penetrate the seed coat before germination can begin.
