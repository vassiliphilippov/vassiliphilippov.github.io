middle meningeal artery

noun
artery supplying brain's meninges
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl məˌnɪn.dʒi.əl ˈɑːr.təɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl məˌnɪn.dʒi.əl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
a branch of the maxillary artery that supplies blood to the meninges of the brain

Examples

ruptured middle meningeal artery
A fracture at the temple can rupture the middle meningeal artery, leading to life-threatening bleeding.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the middle meningeal artery.
The middle meningeal artery is often involved in epidural hematomas following head injuries.
