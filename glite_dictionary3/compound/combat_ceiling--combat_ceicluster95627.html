combat ceiling

noun
maximum altitude for aircraft's required climb rate
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌbæt ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌbat ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the maximum altitude at which an aircraft can maintain a specified minimum rate of climb, typically under combat conditions

Examples

combat ceiling of a fighter jet
Due to engine limitations, the combat ceiling of the aircraft is lower than its maximum altitude.
Pilots must be aware of their aircraft's combat ceiling during high-altitude missions.
The fighter jet's combat ceiling allows it to engage threats at very high altitudes.
