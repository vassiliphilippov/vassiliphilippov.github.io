sight gag

noun
joke based on visual action
Pronunciation: / saɪt gæɡ / (AmE), / saɪt ɡæɡ / (BrE)
a joke whose effect is achieved by visual means rather than words, especially in films or television

Examples

funny sight gag
Classic silent films often rely on the sight gag for humor instead of spoken dialogue.
She planned a clever sight gag for the school play, which had everyone laughing.
The comedian's best sight gag involved slipping on a banana peel.
