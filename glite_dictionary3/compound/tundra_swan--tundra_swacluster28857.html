tundra swan

noun
a swan species that nests in tundra regions
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌn.drə swɑn / (AmE), / ˈtʌn.drə swɒn / (BrE)
a large white swan that breeds in Arctic and subarctic tundra regions of both North America and Eurasia

Examples

The tundra swan migrates thousands of miles each year between its Arctic breeding grounds and wintering sites.
Unlike the larger trumpeter swan, the tundra swan has a more delicate call and a yellow spot at the base of its bill.
