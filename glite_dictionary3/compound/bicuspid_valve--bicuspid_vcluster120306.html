bicuspid valve

noun
heart valve between left atrium and ventricle
Pronunciation: / baɪˈkʌspɪd vælv / (AmE), / baɪˈkʌspɪd vælv / (BrE)
The valve with two cusps situated between the left atrium and the left ventricle of the heart, also called the mitral valve.

Examples

leaky bicuspid valve
A defect in the bicuspid valve can cause blood to flow backward into the left atrium.
During surgery, the doctor examined the patient's bicuspid valve for any signs of damage.
The bicuspid valve is essential for directing blood flow from the atrium to the ventricle.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mitral valve: the valve between the left atrium and left ventricle of the heart
