spaghetti Western

noun
European-made Western film genre
Pronunciation: / spəˈɡɛti ˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / spəˈɡɛti ˈwɛstən / (BrE)
A low-budget Western film produced by a European, especially Italian, film company, typically in the 1960s and 1970s.

Examples

classic spaghetti Western movie
Clint Eastwood got his big break starring in several spaghetti Westerns.
Many spaghetti Westerns were filmed in the deserts of Spain rather than the American West.
The director became famous for making classic spaghetti Westerns in the 1960s.
