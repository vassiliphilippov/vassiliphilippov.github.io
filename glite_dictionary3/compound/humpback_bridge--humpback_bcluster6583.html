humpback bridge

noun
a bridge with a steep curved arch
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmpˌbæk brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhʌmpbæk brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small bridge with a steeply curved arch, typically allowing passage over a narrow river or road, where the highest point is in the middle.

Examples

old humpback bridge
From the hill, you could see the humpback bridge spanning the quiet stream.
The car slowed down carefully before crossing the narrow humpback bridge.
Tourists often stop to admire the old humpback bridge in the village.
