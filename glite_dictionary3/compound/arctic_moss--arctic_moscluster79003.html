arctic moss

noun
a branching lichen found in Arctic regions
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrktɪk mɑs / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk mɒs / (BrE)
a greyish branching lichen found in Arctic and some northern temperate regions, serving as the chief food for reindeer and caribou, and sometimes eaten by humans

Examples

arctic moss covers tundra
Caribou feed on arctic moss throughout the winter when other vegetation is scarce.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the distribution of arctic moss in northern ecosystems.
The arctic moss grows in cold tundra environments and is crucial for the survival of many Arctic animals.
