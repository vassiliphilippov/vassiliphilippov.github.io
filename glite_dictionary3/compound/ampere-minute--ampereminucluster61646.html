ampere-minute

noun
unit of electric charge (60 coulombs)
Pronunciation: / ˈæmˌpɪr ˈmɪnɪt / (AmE), / ˈæmˌpɪə ˈmɪnɪt / (BrE)
A unit of electric charge equal to the charge transferred by a current of one ampere flowing for one minute (60 coulombs).

Examples

one ampere-minute of charge
A current of two amperes flowing for thirty minutes delivers sixty ampere-minutes of charge.
For this experiment, please record all results in ampere-minutes rather than coulombs.
The battery capacity is often rated in ampere-minutes for high-drain devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coulomb: SI unit of electric charge equal to the quantity of electricity transported in one second by a current of one ampere
