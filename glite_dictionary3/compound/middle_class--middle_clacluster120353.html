middle class

noun
group between lower and upper classes
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl ˌklæs / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl ˌklɑːs / (BrE)
the social group between the working (lower) class and the upper class, typically characterized by moderate income, education, and social status

Examples

the middle class families
Many families in the middle class feel pressure from rising living costs.
Over the decades, the size of the middle class has shifted due to economic changes.
The middle class often aspires to provide better opportunities for their children.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – upper class: the social group with the highest status and wealth
  • degree lower – working class: the social group with lower status, often performing manual labor
