balaclava helmet

noun
close-fitting head-and-neck woolen cap
Pronunciation: / ˌbæləˈklɑvə ˈhɛlmət / (AmE), / ˌbæləˈklɑːvə ˈhɛlmɪt / (BrE)
A close-fitting woolen cap that covers the whole head and neck except for parts of the face, often leaving only the eyes and sometimes the mouth exposed.

Examples

balaclava helmet for warmth
During the winter hike, Tom wore a balaclava helmet to keep his head and neck warm.
Skiers often use a balaclava helmet for protection against wind and cold temperatures.
The soldier pulled down his balaclava helmet before stepping out into the snow.
