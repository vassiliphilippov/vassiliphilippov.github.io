to hear things

verb
perceive nonexistent sounds
Pronunciation: / hɪr ˈθɪŋz / (AmE), / hɪə ˈθɪŋz / (BrE)
to perceive sounds or voices that are not actually present, often as a result of imagination or hallucination

Examples

to hear things at night
After staying awake for two nights, I began to hear things that no one else could hear.
She worried that she was starting to hear things when she repeatedly heard footsteps in the empty house.
You might think you're just tired, but if you consistently hear things, it's best to see a doctor.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hallucinate: to experience something that does not exist outside the mind, especially as a result of a mental disorder or taking drugs
