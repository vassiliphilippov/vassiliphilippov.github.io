to feel dumb

verb
to feel foolish or unintelligent
Pronunciation: / fil dʌm / (AmE), / fiːl dʌm / (BrE)
to experience a sense of foolishness or lack of intelligence, often as a result of a mistake or oversight

Examples

to feel dumb after a mistake
After answering the question incorrectly, she couldn't help but feel dumb.
He realized he'd lost his keys and began to feel dumb for not keeping track of them.
I feel dumb for forgetting your birthday again.
