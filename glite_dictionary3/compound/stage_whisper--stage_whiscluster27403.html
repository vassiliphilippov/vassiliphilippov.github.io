stage whisper

noun
a loud, intentionally overheard whisper
Pronunciation: / steɪdʒ ˈwɪspər / (AmE), / steɪdʒ ˈwɪspə / (BrE)
a loud whisper spoken so that it can be overheard, especially in a play, intended to be heard by the audience but not by other characters on stage

Examples

stage whisper in a play
During the play, a stage whisper let the audience in on his secret motives.
She leaned over and, in a deliberate stage whisper, revealed the surprise ending.
The actor delivered his stage whisper so the entire audience could catch the joke.

Related Terms

  • synonym – aside: a remark made by a character in a play intended to be heard by the audience but not by the other characters
