in effect

adverb
currently in force (of a law, rule, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ɪn ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪn ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
(of a law, rule, or policy) currently valid, in force, or being enforced

Examples

in effect as a law
Despite recent debates, the old policy is still in effect.
The curfew will remain in effect until further notice from the authorities.
The new tax regulations are now in effect and must be followed by all businesses.
His license was not in effect at the time of the accident.
The ban will remain in effect until further notice.
The new regulations are now in effect across the country.
