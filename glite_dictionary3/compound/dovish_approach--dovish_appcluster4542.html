dovish approach

noun
favoring peaceful, non-aggressive solutions
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.vɪʃ əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.vɪʃ əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
An attitude or policy that favors peaceful, non-aggressive solutions, especially in economic or international affairs.

Examples

adopt a dovish approach
Analysts believe the central bank's dovish approach will help stimulate economic growth.
Her dovish approach to conflict resolution made her well-respected among international diplomats.
The new administration adopted a dovish approach to foreign relations, prioritizing negotiation over military action.
