Caspian Sea

proper noun
the largest inland saltwater lake
Pronunciation: / ˈkæspiən si / (AmE), / ˈkaspɪən siː / (BrE)
a large saltwater lake between Iran and Russia, fed by the Volga River; the largest inland body of water in the world

Examples

the Caspian Sea region
Many species of fish, such as sturgeon, thrive in the waters of the Caspian Sea.
Oil extraction from the Caspian Sea has significant economic impact for the region.
The Caspian Sea is bordered by five countries, including Kazakhstan and Azerbaijan.
