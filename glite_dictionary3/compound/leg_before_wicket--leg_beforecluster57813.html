leg before wicket

noun
cricket method of dismissal
Pronunciation: / lɛɡ bɪˈfɔr ˈwɪkɪt / (AmE), / lɛɡ bɪˈfɔː ˈwɪkɪt / (BrE)
(in cricket) a method of dismissing a batsman when their leg or body intercepts the ball and prevents it from hitting the wicket, according to specific rules.

Examples

leg before wicket decision
He has been dismissed leg before wicket several times this season.
The umpire ruled the batsman out leg before wicket after the ball struck his pad in front of the stumps.
There was some debate over whether it was really leg before wicket or not.
