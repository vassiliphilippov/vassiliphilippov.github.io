Bermudian rig

noun
triangular-sail yacht rig
Pronunciation: / bɚˈmjuːdiən rɪɡ / (AmE), / bəˈmjuːdiən rɪɡ / (BrE)
a type of sail arrangement for yachts that uses triangular sails set fore-and-aft, typically with a single mast and a mainsail.

Examples

Bermudian rig sailboat
He learned to sail on small boats with a traditional Bermudian rig.
Most modern sailboats use the Bermudian rig because of its efficiency and ease of handling.
The yacht is fitted with a Bermudian rig for optimal performance in racing.
