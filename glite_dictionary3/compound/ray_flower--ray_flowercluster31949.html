ray flower

noun
strap-shaped outer flower of composite plants
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪ ˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ ˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a small, flat, strap-shaped flower found around the edge of the flower head in certain composite plants, such as daisies and sunflowers

Examples

ray flower of a sunflower
Botanists examined each ray flower under a microscope to study its structure.
Sunflowers have yellow ray flowers surrounding a cluster of disk flowers in the middle.
The bright petals around the daisy are each a ray flower forming the plant's outer circle.

Related Terms

  • antonym – disk flower: the small tubular flowers in the center of a composite flower head
