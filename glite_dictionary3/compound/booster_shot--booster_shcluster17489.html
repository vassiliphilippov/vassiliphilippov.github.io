booster shot

noun
an extra vaccine dose for renewed effectiveness
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːstər ʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈbuːstə ʃɒt / (BrE)
an extra dose of a vaccine given after the initial dose to increase or renew its effectiveness

Examples

get a booster shot
Children often need a booster shot before starting school to ensure immunity.
During the pandemic, health officials recommended a booster shot to enhance vaccine protection.
Many adults receive a booster shot for tetanus every ten years.
