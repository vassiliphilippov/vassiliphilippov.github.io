bank rate

noun
interest rate set by a central bank
Pronunciation: / bæŋk reɪt / (AmE), / bæŋk reɪt / (BrE)
The rate of interest set by a country's central bank at which it lends to commercial banks, used to influence monetary policy and control the money supply.

Examples

bank rate change
A lower bank rate can encourage commercial banks to lend more money.
Businesses closely monitor changes in the bank rate as it impacts borrowing costs.
The central bank increased the bank rate to curb inflation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – discount rate: the interest rate charged by central banks on loans to commercial banks
