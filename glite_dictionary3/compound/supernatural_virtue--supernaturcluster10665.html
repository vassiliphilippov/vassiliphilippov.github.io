supernatural virtue

noun
theological virtue in Christian ethics
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈnætʃrəl ˈvɝːtʃu / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈnætʃrəl ˈvɜːtʃuː / (BrE)
In Christian ethics, one of the three virtues (faith, hope, and charity) believed to be bestowed by God to complete natural virtues.

Examples

supernatural virtue of faith
According to Christian teaching, hope is a supernatural virtue that supports believers through hardship.
Charity, as a supernatural virtue, calls Christians to love others selflessly as God loves them.
Faith is considered a supernatural virtue because it is infused by God rather than developed by human effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym – natural virtue: virtues attained by human effort, as opposed to supernatural virtues
  • synonym – theological virtue: a virtue associated with faith, hope, and charity in Christian theology
