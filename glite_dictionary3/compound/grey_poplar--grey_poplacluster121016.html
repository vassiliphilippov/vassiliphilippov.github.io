grey poplar

noun
a European species of poplar tree, leaves grey underneath
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈpɑːplɚ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
a tall, fast-growing species of poplar tree with faintly lobed, toothed leaves that are greyish on the underside, native to Europe and naturalized in other regions

Examples

tall grey poplar tree
Many parks in Europe feature the grey poplar because of its impressive height and shade.
The grey poplar is recognized by its greyish undersides of the leaves and rapid growth rate.
The leaves of the grey poplar turn a beautiful silver-grey in the breeze.
