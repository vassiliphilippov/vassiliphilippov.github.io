subsistence level

noun
bare minimum standard of living
Pronunciation: / səbˈsɪstəns ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
the minimum standard of living necessary to maintain basic health and survival, with just enough resources for food, clothing, and shelter

Examples

below the subsistence level
Humanitarian aid aims to lift people above the subsistence level in crisis areas.
Many families in the region live at the subsistence level due to widespread unemployment.
Wages have failed to keep up with inflation, pushing some workers close to the subsistence level.
