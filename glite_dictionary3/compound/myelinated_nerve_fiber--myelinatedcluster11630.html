myelinated nerve fiber

noun
nerve fiber with a myelin sheath
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəˌleɪnɪtɪd nɝːv ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəleɪtɪd nɜːv ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that is surrounded by a sheath of myelin, which helps increase the speed at which nerve impulses are conducted

Examples

myelinated nerve fiber conducts signals
A myelinated nerve fiber conducts impulses much faster than an unmyelinated one.
Damage to a myelinated nerve fiber can slow down the transmission of nerve signals.
Multiple sclerosis affects the myelinated nerve fibers in the brain and spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unmyelinated nerve fiber: a nerve fiber not surrounded by a myelin sheath
