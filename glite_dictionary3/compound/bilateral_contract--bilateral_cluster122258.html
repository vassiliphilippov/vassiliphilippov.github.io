bilateral contract

noun
contract with mutual promises by both sides
Pronunciation: / baɪˈlætərəl ˈkɑntrækt / (AmE), / baɪˈlætərəl ˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
A contract in which both parties make mutual promises and are both bound as promisor and promisee.

Examples

sign a bilateral contract
A bilateral contract requires that both the buyer and the seller agree to the terms and make promises to each other.
Employment agreements are typically considered bilateral contracts because both employer and employee make binding commitments.
The company entered into a bilateral contract with its supplier to provide materials at agreed prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym – unilateral contract: a contract obligating only one party until an act is performed by the other
