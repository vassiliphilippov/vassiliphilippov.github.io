dead reckoning

noun
estimation without precise data
Pronunciation: / dɛd ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (AmE), / dɛd ˈrɛkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of calculating one's position or an outcome by estimating rather than using precise information, especially based on previously known positions or facts

Examples

dead reckoning prediction method
His prediction of the election result was just dead reckoning, with little real data to support it.
Pilots often rely on dead reckoning when their navigation instruments fail.
Without a map, the hikers used dead reckoning to find their way back to camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym – estimate: a rough calculation or judgment
  • synonym – guesswork: the process or result of making a guess
