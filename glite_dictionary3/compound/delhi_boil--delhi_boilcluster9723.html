Delhi boil

noun
skin disease with ulcerative lesions
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛli bɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɛli bɔɪl / (BrE)
A skin disease caused by leishmaniasis, characterized by ulcerative lesions, especially on exposed parts of the body.

Examples

Delhi boil infection
His untreated Delhi boil became more painful and required medical intervention to prevent secondary infection.
The doctor diagnosed the ulcer as a Delhi boil after observing the lesion and taking a tissue sample.
Travelers to certain regions may be at risk for developing Delhi boil if bitten by infected sandflies.
