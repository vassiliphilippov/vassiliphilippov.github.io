cane toad invasion

noun
spread of cane toads causing ecological harm
Pronunciation: / keɪn toʊd ɪnˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / keɪn təʊd ɪnˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the rapid spread of cane toads into new areas, often causing significant ecological damage to native wildlife and habitats

Examples

cane toad invasion spreads quickly
Biologists are monitoring the cane toad invasion across northern Australia to assess its impact on native species.
Since the cane toad invasion began, several local predators have declined dramatically.
The government launched a campaign to control the cane toad invasion and protect vulnerable habitats.
