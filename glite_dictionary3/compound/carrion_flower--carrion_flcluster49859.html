carrion flower

noun
succulent plant with foul-smelling flower
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriən ˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæriən ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
Any of several succulent, leafless plants of the genus Stapelia, noted for their large, star-shaped, foul-smelling flowers that attract pollinators.

Examples

carrion flower star-shaped bloom
Botanists were fascinated by the star-shaped blooms of the carrion flower.
The carrion flower emits a pungent odor to attract flies for pollination.
The gardener warned that the carrion flower can smell like rotting meat when in full bloom.
