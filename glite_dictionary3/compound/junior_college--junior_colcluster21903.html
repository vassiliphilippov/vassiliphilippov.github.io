junior college

noun
two-year college; associate degree college
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːniər kəˈlɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnɪə kəˈlɛdʒ / (BrE)
A college that offers only the first two years of undergraduate study, usually leading to an associate degree.

Examples

junior college degree
After high school, she decided to attend a junior college to save money before transferring to a university.
Many students earn an associate degree at a junior college before continuing their education.
The local junior college offers a variety of technical programs and general education courses.
