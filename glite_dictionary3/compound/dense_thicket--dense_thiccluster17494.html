dense thicket

noun
area of closely growing bushes or trees
Pronunciation: / dɛns ˈθɪkɪt / (AmE), / dɛns ˈθɪkɪt / (BrE)
An area where bushes or small trees grow very close together, making passage difficult.

Examples

walk through a dense thicket
After hours of searching, the rescue team finally spotted the lost hiker in a dense thicket.
Birds often build their nests in a dense thicket for protection from predators.
The children stumbled upon a fox hiding in the dense thicket near the stream.
