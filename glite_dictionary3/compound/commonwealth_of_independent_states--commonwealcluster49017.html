Commonwealth of Independent States

proper noun
alliance of former Soviet republics
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmənˌwɛlθ əv ˌɪndəˈpɛndənt steɪts / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌwɛlθ əv ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt steɪts / (BrE)
an alliance of former Soviet Socialist Republics formed after the dissolution of the Soviet Union in December 1991, aimed at fostering cooperation among member states

Examples

the Commonwealth of Independent States countries
After the collapse of the Soviet Union, the Commonwealth of Independent States was established to maintain economic ties.
Belarus and Kazakhstan are two of the founding members of the Commonwealth of Independent States.
Several member countries of the Commonwealth of Independent States cooperate on trade and security policies.
