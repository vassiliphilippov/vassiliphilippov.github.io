track record

noun
record of past performance
Pronunciation: / ˈtræk ˌrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈtræk ˌrekɔːd / (BrE)
the record of a person's or organization's past performance or achievements, especially as an indicator of ability or reliability

Examples

strong track record
He has a proven track record of managing complex projects successfully.
She was hired because her track record in sales was exceptional.
The company's strong track record makes it a reliable choice for investors.
