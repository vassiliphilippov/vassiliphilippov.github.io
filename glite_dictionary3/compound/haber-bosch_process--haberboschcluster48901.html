Haber-Bosch process

noun
industrial ammonia production method
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːbər bɑːʃ ˌproʊses / (AmE), / ˈheɪbə bɒʃ ˌprəʊses / (BrE)
an industrial method for producing ammonia by combining nitrogen and hydrogen gases under high pressure and temperature, using an iron catalyst

Examples

Haber-Bosch process makes ammonia
Ammonia produced by the Haber-Bosch process is essential for making many chemical fertilizers.
The Haber-Bosch process revolutionized fertilizer production and helped support global agriculture.
The efficiency of the Haber-Bosch process depends on temperature, pressure, and the presence of an iron catalyst.
