anemic anoxia

noun
anoxia due to low hemoglobin
Pronunciation: / əˈniːmɪk æˈnɑksiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪk əˈnɒksiə / (BrE)
A condition in which an abnormally low concentration of hemoglobin leads to insufficient oxygen in the body tissues.

Examples

anemic anoxia in blood
Anemic anoxia can result when certain diseases interfere with hemoglobin production.
Doctors treated her anemic anoxia by increasing her red blood cell count through transfusion.
The patient developed anemic anoxia following severe blood loss during surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym – anoxia: absence or deficiency of oxygen in the tissues
  • synonym – hypoxemia: abnormally low levels of oxygen in the blood
