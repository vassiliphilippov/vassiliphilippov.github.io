miotic eyedrop

noun
eyedrop for glaucoma that constricts the pupil
Pronunciation: / maɪˈɑtɪk ˈaɪˌdrɑp / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk ˈaɪˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A type of eye drop used to treat glaucoma by constricting the pupil and increasing the outflow of aqueous humor.

Examples

to use miotic eyedrop for glaucoma
She experienced mild stinging after putting the miotic eyedrop in her left eye.
The doctor prescribed a miotic eyedrop to help control the patient's glaucoma.
Using a miotic eyedrop regularly can help reduce pressure inside the eye.
