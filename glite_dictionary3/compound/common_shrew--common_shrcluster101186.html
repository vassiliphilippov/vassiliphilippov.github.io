common shrew

noun
a small, widespread insectivorous mammal
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈʃru / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈʃruː / (BrE)
A small, widespread shrew species (Sorex araneus), commonly found in Europe and parts of northern Asia, known for its pointed snout and dense fur.

Examples

a common shrew in grass
The common shrew has a high metabolism and must eat frequently throughout the day.
Unlike mice, the common shrew has a distinctly pointed snout and tiny eyes.
We spotted a common shrew darting through the undergrowth during our nature walk.
