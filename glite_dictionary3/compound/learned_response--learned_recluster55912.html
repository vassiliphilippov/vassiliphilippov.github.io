learned response

noun
a reaction acquired by learning
Pronunciation: / ˈlɝːnɪd rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a reaction that is acquired through experience or training rather than being innate

Examples

a learned response to fear
A phobia often represents a learned response to a previously neutral stimulus.
Rewarding good behavior reinforces a learned response in children.
Through repeated practice, the pianist developed a learned response to complex finger movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym – conditioned response: a reaction produced by conditioning
  • antonym – innate response: a reaction present from birth, not learned
