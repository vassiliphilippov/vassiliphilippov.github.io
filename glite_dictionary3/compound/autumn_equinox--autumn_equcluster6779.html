autumn equinox

noun
start of autumn when day and night are equal
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtəm ˈiˌkwɑnɑks / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəm ˈiːkwɪnɒks / (BrE)
the time at which the sun crosses the celestial equator and night and day are of roughly equal length, marking the start of autumn

Examples

the autumn equinox marks autumn
At the autumn equinox, day and night are nearly the same length.
Many cultures celebrate festivals around the autumn equinox.
The autumn equinox usually occurs around September 22 or 23 in the Northern Hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym – vernal equinox: the time in spring when day and night are equal length
