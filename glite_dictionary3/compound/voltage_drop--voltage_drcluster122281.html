voltage drop

noun
decrease in voltage along a circuit
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊltɪdʒ drɑp / (AmE), / ˈvɒltɪdʒ drɒp / (BrE)
a reduction in electrical voltage as current passes through a part of a circuit, typically caused by resistance in the conductor.

Examples

voltage drop across a wire
Electricians must consider voltage drop when designing long cable runs.
If the voltage drop is too high, the device may not function properly.
The voltage drop across the resistor caused the light to dim.
