Lag b'Omer

noun
Jewish holiday on the 33rd day of the Omer
Pronunciation: / lɑg bəˈoʊmər / (AmE), / lɑːg bəˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A Jewish holiday celebrated on the 33rd day of the Omer, marking the 18th day of the Hebrew month of Iyar, often observed with bonfires and festivities.

Examples

Lag b'Omer bonfire celebration
During Lag b'Omer, children often play with toy bows and arrows as part of the tradition.
Every year, thousands of people celebrate Lag b'Omer with bonfires across Israel.
My family attends a picnic in the park for Lag b'Omer to mark the holiday.
