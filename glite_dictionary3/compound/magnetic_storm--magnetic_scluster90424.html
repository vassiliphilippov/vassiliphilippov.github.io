magnetic storm

noun
disturbance of Earth's magnetic field
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk stɔrm / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk stɔːm / (BrE)
a sudden disturbance of the Earth's magnetic field, usually caused by charged particles emitted from the sun

Examples

a magnetic storm disrupts satellites
A powerful magnetic storm can disrupt power grids and navigation systems.
Scientists are studying the effects of a magnetic storm on satellite communications.
The aurora borealis became visible as a result of the magnetic storm last night.
