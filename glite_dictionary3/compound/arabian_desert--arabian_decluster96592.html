Arabian Desert

noun
desert in Egypt between the Nile and the Red Sea
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪbiən ˈdɛzɚt / (AmE), / əˈreɪbiən ˈdɛzət / (BrE)
a large desert region in Egypt, situated between the Nile River and the Red Sea, known for its arid landscape and historical significance

Examples

the Arabian Desert in Egypt
Ancient trade routes often passed through the Arabian Desert, connecting different civilizations.
Rare species of wildlife have adapted to survive in the extreme conditions of the Arabian Desert.
The Arabian Desert stretches for hundreds of miles across Egypt, presenting a harsh and beautiful landscape.
