velar consonant

noun
consonant made at the soft palate
Pronunciation: / ˈviːlər ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈviːlə ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by the back of the tongue approaching or touching the soft palate (velum)

Examples

velar consonant sound
Children often find it difficult to pronounce velar consonants correctly when they first learn to speak.
In some languages, the range of velar consonant sounds is much broader than in English.
The English sound k in 'cat' is a classic example of a velar consonant.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – palatal consonant: a consonant articulated with the body of the tongue against the hard palate
  • degree higher – uvular consonant: a consonant produced with the back of the tongue against or near the uvula
