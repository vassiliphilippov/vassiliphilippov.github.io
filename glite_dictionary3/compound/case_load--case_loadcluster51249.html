case load

noun
number of cases handled over time
Pronunciation: / keɪs loʊd / (AmE), / keɪs ləʊd / (BrE)
the number of cases dealt with by a court, agency, or professional in a particular period

Examples

heavy case load
Doctors are struggling to keep up with their current case load in the hospital.
Social workers must manage a heavy case load with limited resources.
The court's case load increased significantly after the new law was passed.
