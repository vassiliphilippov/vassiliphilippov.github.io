Johnson grass

noun
a weedy grass species, often a pest
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑnsən ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒnsən ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass (Sorghum halepense) that spreads by creeping rhizomes; grown for fodder but considered a noxious weed in some regions, especially the southern United States
