ingrained in

adjective
deeply fixed or established
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡreɪnd ɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪnd ɪn / (BrE)
(of a quality, attitude, or habit) firmly established and difficult to change in a particular context, person, or thing.

Examples

ingrained in culture
A sense of respect for elders is ingrained in many traditional cultures.
The habit of saving money was ingrained in him from childhood.
These customs are so ingrained in the community that they are rarely questioned.

  • synonym – entrenched in: firmly established and difficult to change (especially of ideas or attitudes)
