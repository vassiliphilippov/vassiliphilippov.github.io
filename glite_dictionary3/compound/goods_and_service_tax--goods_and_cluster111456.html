goods and service tax

noun
tax on supply of goods and services
Pronunciation: / ɡʊdz ənd ˈsɜrvɪs tæks / (AmE), / ɡʊdz ənd ˈsɜːvɪs tæks / (BrE)
a tax on the supply of goods and services, usually collected by the seller from the end consumer, based on the value of the transaction

Examples

goods and service tax on sales
Businesses must register for the goods and service tax if their turnover exceeds a certain threshold.
Several countries have adopted the goods and service tax to simplify their tax systems.
The government implemented a goods and service tax to streamline indirect taxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – value-added tax: a tax on the amount by which the value of an article has been increased at each stage of its production or distribution
