white horse nettle

noun
a weedy nightshade plant with silvery leaves and colorful flowers
Pronunciation: / waɪt hɔrs ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / waɪt hɔːs ˈnɛt(ə)l / (BrE)
a weedy plant of the nightshade family (Solanum elaeagnifolium) with silvery leaves and violet, blue, or white flowers, found from central United States to South America, whose round berry is sometimes used to curdle milk

Examples

white horse nettle plant
Across much of the Midwest, the resilient white horse nettle is a familiar sight along roadsides.
Local cheesemakers use berries from the white horse nettle to curdle milk naturally.
The pasture was overrun with white horse nettle, making it unsafe for the cattle.
