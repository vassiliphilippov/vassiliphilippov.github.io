outer rason

noun
outer clerical garment in Eastern Christianity
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtɚ ˈræs.ɒn / (AmE), / ˈaʊ.tə ˈræs.ɒn / (BrE)
a full, flowing outer garment worn over the inner cassock by bishops, priests, deacons, and monastics in Eastern Orthodox and Eastern Catholic traditions

Examples

wear an outer rason
It is customary for monastics to wear the outer rason over their inner garments.
The outer rason is designed to flow elegantly, symbolizing the wearer's commitment to the church.
The bishop donned his outer rason before entering the sanctuary for the evening service.
