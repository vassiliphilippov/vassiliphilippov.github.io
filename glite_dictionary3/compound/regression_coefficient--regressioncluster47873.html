regression coefficient

noun
statistical coefficient; rate of change in regression
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛʃən koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən kəʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a constant in a regression equation that represents the amount by which the dependent variable changes for a one-unit increase in the independent variable; the slope of the regression line

Examples

regression coefficient in analysis
A high regression coefficient means there is a strong relationship between the two variables.
She calculated the regression coefficient to interpret the effect of temperature on sales.
The regression coefficient indicates how much the outcome variable will change for every unit increase in the predictor.

Related Terms

  • antonym – intercept: the value where a regression line crosses the y-axis
  • synonym – slope: the measure of steepness or incline of a line
