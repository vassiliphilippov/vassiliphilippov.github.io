white cypress

noun
a North American cedar tree species
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsɪprəs / (BrE)
a slow-growing, medium-sized cedar native to the east coast of the United States, resembling the American arborvitae

Examples

tall white cypress tree
A stand of white cypress provided a welcome windbreak near the farmhouse.
The white cypress is often used in landscaping along the east coast for its hardy nature.
Unlike other cedars, white cypress grows slowly but can live for hundreds of years.
