African-American music

noun
music originating in African-American communities
Pronunciation: / ˌæfrɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌæfrɪkən əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A broad genre of music originating from African-American communities, characterized by distinctive rhythms, melodies, and often featuring repeated choruses; encompasses spirituals, blues, jazz, and more.

Examples

African-American music influences jazz
Many genres of American popular culture are rooted in African-American music from the twentieth century.
She gave a lecture on the influence of African-American music in the development of jazz.
The museum showcases the history and legacy of African-American music through interactive exhibits.
