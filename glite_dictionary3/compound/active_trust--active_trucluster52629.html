active trust

noun
trust with active trustee duties
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv trʌst / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv trʌst / (BrE)
a trust where the trustee is required to carry out certain duties or manage property on behalf of beneficiaries

Examples

active trust duties
An active trust imposes clear obligations on the trustee to act for the beneficiaries.
The manager of the active trust is responsible for investing and distributing assets.
Unlike a passive trust, an active trust requires the trustee to make decisions and take action.

Related Terms

  • antonym – passive trust: a trust in which the trustee has little or no duties
