circuit breaker

noun
device that interrupts electrical circuits when overloaded
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːkɪt ˌbreɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkɪt ˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a safety device that automatically stops the flow of an electric current in a circuit as a protection against overload or short circuit

Examples

circuit breaker trips power
Always turn off the circuit breaker before working on electrical wiring.
An electrician replaced the faulty circuit breaker in the main panel.
The circuit breaker tripped when the heater overloaded the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fuse: a safety device that melts and breaks an electric circuit if the current is too high
