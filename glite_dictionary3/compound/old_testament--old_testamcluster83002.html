Old Testament

proper noun
first part of the Christian Bible
Pronunciation: / oʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / əʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
the first part of the Christian Bible, comprising books written before the life of Jesus Christ and central to both Jewish and Christian traditions

Examples

the Old Testament books
Many early laws and customs in Western society have roots in the Old Testament.
She spent a year studying the prophets of the Old Testament at university.
The Old Testament is regarded as sacred scripture in both Judaism and Christianity.
