kauri pine

noun
large resinous tree of genus Agathis
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊri paɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri paɪn / (BrE)
any of several large evergreen trees of the genus Agathis, native to Australasia and Southeast Asia, valued for their timber and resin (dammar).

Examples

kauri pine tree trunk
Craftsmen highly value the wood of the kauri pine for making furniture and boats.
The ancient kauri pine forests of New Zealand are renowned for their immense size and longevity.
The resin collected from the kauri pine is known as dammar and is used in varnishes.
