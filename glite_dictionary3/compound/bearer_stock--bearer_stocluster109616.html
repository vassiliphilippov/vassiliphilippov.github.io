bearer stock

noun
stock with ownership tied to its holder
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrər stɑk / (AmE), / ˈbeərə stɒk / (BrE)
A share or stock certificate that does not record the owner's name and is presumed to belong to whoever physically holds it.

Examples

bearer stock certificate
He transferred the bearer stock to a trusted associate for safekeeping overseas.
In some jurisdictions, the use of bearer stock is prohibited to prevent money laundering.
Ownership of bearer stock is established simply by possessing the certificate.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bearer bond: a bond that is owned by whoever holds it, not registered to any name
