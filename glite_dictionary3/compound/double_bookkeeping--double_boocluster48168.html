double bookkeeping

noun
system of recording transactions twice in accounting
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl ˈbʊk.kiˌpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈbʊk.kiː.pɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of recording each financial transaction twice, as a debit in one account and a credit in another, to ensure accuracy in accounts

Examples

double bookkeeping in accounting
Errors can be spotted more easily when you implement double bookkeeping in your accounting system.
Many businesses use double bookkeeping to keep their financial records accurate.
The accountant explained the benefits of double bookkeeping during the meeting.
