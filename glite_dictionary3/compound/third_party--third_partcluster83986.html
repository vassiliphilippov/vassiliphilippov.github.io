third party

noun
minor political party in a two-party system
Pronunciation: / θɜrd ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A political party organized in opposition to the two major parties in a two-party system.

Examples

third party candidate
Many voters considered supporting a third party instead of the main two parties in the election.
The third party candidate drew significant support from dissatisfied voters.
The rise of a strong third party changed the dynamics of the political race.
