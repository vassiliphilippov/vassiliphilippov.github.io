parly train

noun
train scheduled for parliamentary or legal service
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrli treɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːli treɪn / (BrE)
(historical, British) A train scheduled to satisfy parliamentary regulations requiring minimal passenger railway service, especially at low fares or to attend parliamentary sessions.

Examples

parly train for cheap travel
Due to the old laws, they still run a parly train on this rural line.
She always catches the parly train because it's the cheapest way to travel to London.
The parly train was packed this morning with commuters and railway enthusiasts.
