sinking ship

noun
failing project or organization (metaphor)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ ʃɪp / (BrE)
a project, organization, or situation that is failing and likely to collapse or be unsuccessful

Examples

abandon a sinking ship
Many investors abandoned the company, convinced it was a sinking ship.
She refused to join what she considered a sinking ship on the verge of collapse.
Staying on a sinking ship rarely leads to success, so they found new jobs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – going under: failing (e.g., a business going bankrupt)
  • synonym – lost cause: something that is certain to fail or that cannot be changed
