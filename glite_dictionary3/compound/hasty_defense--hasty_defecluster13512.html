hasty defense

noun
quickly organized military defense
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪsti dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
a military defense organized quickly, often while in contact with the enemy or when there is little time to prepare

Examples

set up a hasty defense
In situations with limited preparation time, commanders may rely on a hasty defense to hold their ground.
The battalion was forced to form a hasty defense after the surprise attack began.
The unit established a hasty defense along the ridge as enemy forces approached.

  • antonym – deliberate defense: a defense planned and prepared with adequate time
