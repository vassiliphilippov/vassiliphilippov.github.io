bitter salts

noun
hydrated magnesium sulfate, used medicinally
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪtər sɔlts / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə sɔːlts / (BrE)
A hydrated form of magnesium sulfate used orally as a remedy for heartburn and constipation, and by injection to prevent seizures.

Examples

take bitter salts for constipation
After taking bitter salts, he felt his symptoms of heartburn ease considerably.
In the hospital, bitter salts may be injected to help prevent dangerous seizures.
The doctor recommended bitter salts to relieve her occasional constipation.
