long moss

noun
plant with hanging gray-green strands; Spanish moss
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ mɑs / (AmE), / lɒŋ mɒs / (BrE)
A plant consisting of dense, hairlike, greenish-grey strands hanging from tree branches, especially in the southeastern United States and West Indies; also known as Spanish moss.

Examples

long moss hanging from trees
Birds sometimes build their nests using the soft fibers of long moss.
In the South, long moss hangs from branches like natural garlands.
The ancient oaks were draped with long moss that swayed gently in the breeze.
