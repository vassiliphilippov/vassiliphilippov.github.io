liquid crystal

noun
substance with both liquid and crystal properties
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈkrɪstəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈkrɪstl̩ / (BrE)
A substance that flows like a liquid but has some properties of a solid crystal, especially in how its molecules are oriented.

Examples

liquid crystal display screen
Scientists are researching new uses for liquid crystal materials in electronics.
The display uses liquid crystal technology for bright, clear images.
When heated, certain compounds change from a solid to a liquid crystal state.
