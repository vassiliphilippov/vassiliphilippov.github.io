Columbia River

proper noun
major river in western North America
Pronunciation: / kəˈlʌmbiə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbiə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river of North America rising in Canada and flowing south through Washington, partly forming the border between Washington and Oregon before emptying into the Pacific Ocean; noted for its salmon runs

Examples

the Columbia River flows
Fishing is a popular activity along the Columbia River, especially during the salmon season.
The Columbia River is famous for its spectacular scenery and hydroelectric dams.
The border between Washington and Oregon largely follows the Columbia River.
