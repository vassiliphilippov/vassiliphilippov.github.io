sinking spell

noun
short period of worsening health or mood
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ spɛl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ spel / (BrE)
A brief period in which a person's health or mood temporarily worsens or declines.

Examples

sudden sinking spell
After walking up the stairs, she experienced a sinking spell and needed to sit down.
He worried that his recent sinking spell might signal a more serious health problem.
The nurse explained that sinking spells are common during recovery from surgery.
