mise en scene

noun
stage or film visual arrangement
Pronunciation: / miz ɑ̃ sɛn / (AmE), / miːz ɑ̃ sɛn / (BrE)
the arrangement of scenery, props, and other visual elements on a stage or in a film, creating the overall look and feel of a scene

Examples

mise en scene of a film
A well-crafted mise en scene can transport the audience into the story's world.
Film critics praised the movie's mise en scene for its rich detail and symbolism.
The director paid meticulous attention to the mise en scene to create a haunting atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – staging: the process of arranging movement and positions of actors and props on stage
