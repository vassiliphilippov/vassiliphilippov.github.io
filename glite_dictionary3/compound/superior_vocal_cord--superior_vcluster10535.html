superior vocal cord

noun
upper, non-sound-producing vocal fold
Pronunciation: / səˈpɪriɚ ˈvoʊkl kɔrd / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˈvəʊkəl kɔːd / (BrE)
either of the pair of upper folds in the larynx, not directly involved in producing sound, also known as the false vocal cord

Examples

superior vocal cord injury
During surgery, the superior vocal cord is identified to avoid damaging important structures.
Inflammation of the superior vocal cord can sometimes be mistaken for other throat conditions.
The superior vocal cord is also called the false vocal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym – true vocal cord: the lower pair of vocal cords involved in sound production
  • synonym – vestibular fold: another term for the superior vocal cord, the upper pair of folds in the larynx
