urinary tract infection

noun
infection of the urinary system
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrəˌnɛri trækt ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri trækt ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
An infection affecting any part of the urinary system, including the kidneys, bladder, ureters, or urethra.

Examples

treat a urinary tract infection
Drinking plenty of water may help prevent a urinary tract infection from developing.
Symptoms of a urinary tract infection can include a frequent urge to urinate and a burning sensation.
The doctor diagnosed her with a urinary tract infection after reviewing her symptoms.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – bladder infection: an infection specifically of the bladder (cystitis)
  • degree higher – kidney infection: an infection affecting the kidneys (pyelonephritis)
