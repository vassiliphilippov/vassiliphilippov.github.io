floppy disk

noun
magnetic data storage device
Pronunciation: / ˈflɑːpi dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈflɒpi dɪsk / (BrE)
A thin, flexible magnetic disk enclosed in a square or rectangular plastic shell, used for storing computer data.

Examples

insert a floppy disk
Before USB drives, people commonly used a floppy disk to transfer files between computers.
He found a box of floppy disks in the attic, filled with vintage games and documents.
The old computer could only read data from a floppy disk.

  • antonym – hard disk: a rigid magnetic disk used for storing computer data
