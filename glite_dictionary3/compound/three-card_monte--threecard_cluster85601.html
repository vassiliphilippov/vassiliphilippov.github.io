three-card monte

noun
confidence card game involving three cards
Pronunciation: / ˌθriː kɑrd ˈmɑnti / (AmE), / ˌθriː kɑːd ˈmɒnti / (BrE)
A gambling card game in which a dealer shows three cards, rapidly manipulates them, and players bet on which card is the target card; often used for tricking people out of money.

Examples

play three-card monte on the street
He lost $100 after trying his luck at three-card monte on the street.
Police arrested the con artist who had set up a three-card monte table in the park.
Tourists are often warned to avoid street games like three-card monte.
