electronic counter-countermeasures

noun
techniques to protect friendly electromagnetic operations from enemy electronic attack
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ˈkaʊntər ˌkaʊntər məˈʒɚz / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ˈkaʊntə ˌkaʊntə məˈʒəːz / (BrE)
actions or techniques in electronic warfare intended to ensure the effective use of the electromagnetic spectrum by friendly forces, despite enemy interference or countermeasures.
