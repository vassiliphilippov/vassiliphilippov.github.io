perpetual motion

noun
motion continuing forever without energy input
Pronunciation: / pərˈpɛtʃuəl ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəl ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
The hypothetical state of motion that continues indefinitely without any external source of energy, which is impossible in practice due to friction and other dissipative forces.

Examples

perpetual motion machine
Discussions about perpetual motion often arise in debates about free energy and alternative power sources.
Many inventors have tried to create a perpetual motion machine, but none have ever succeeded.
The laws of thermodynamics prove that perpetual motion is physically impossible.
