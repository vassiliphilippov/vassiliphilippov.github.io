macaroni philosopher

noun
an impractical theorist; a dreamer
Pronunciation: / ˌmækəˈroʊni fəˈlɑsəfər / (AmE), / ˌmækəˈrəʊni fəˈlɒsəfə / (BrE)
A person who indulges in theorizing or speculation without taking practical action, often regarded as impractical or unrealistic.

Examples

macaroni philosopher talks ideas
Don't be a macaroni philosopher; the world needs people who take real steps.
He was criticized as a macaroni philosopher whose ideas never left the drawing board.
Her friends called her a macaroni philosopher because she talked more than she did.
