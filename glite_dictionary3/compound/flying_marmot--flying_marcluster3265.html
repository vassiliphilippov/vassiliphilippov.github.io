flying marmot

noun
East Indian flying squirrel
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈmɑrmət / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈmɑːmət / (BrE)
An East Indian flying squirrel; a rodent species known for its ability to glide between trees using a membrane of skin.

Examples

a flying marmot gliding
Local folklore often describes sightings of the elusive flying marmot during dusk.
The flying marmot uses a fold of skin to glide from tree to tree in its native forests.
Unlike other squirrels, the flying marmot spends much of its life in the treetops.
