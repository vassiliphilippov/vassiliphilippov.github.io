to wait your turn

verb
be patient until it's your opportunity
Pronunciation: / weɪt jʊr tɝn / (AmE), / weɪt jɔː tɜːn / (BrE)
to remain patient until your opportunity to do something arrives, especially when in a line or waiting with others

Examples

to wait your turn in line
Please wait your turn at the counter rather than pushing ahead of others.
The children learned to wait their turn before going down the slide.
You need to wait your turn if you want to speak during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – queue: a line of people or vehicles waiting their turn to proceed
