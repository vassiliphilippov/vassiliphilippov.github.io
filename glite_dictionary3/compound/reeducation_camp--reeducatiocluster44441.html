reeducation camp

noun
facility for forced ideological indoctrination
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɛdʒuˈkeɪʃən kæmp / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɛdjuːˈkeɪʃən kamp / (BrE)
a camp or facility where people are detained and subjected to ideological indoctrination or forced to change their beliefs, typically by a government or authority

Examples

sent to a reeducation camp
Many political prisoners were sent to reeducation camps after the new regime took power.
Reports of harsh conditions in reeducation camps have drawn international criticism.
The government claimed that the reeducation camp was intended to help integrate minorities into mainstream society.
