sticky end

noun
overhanging DNA end for bonding
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪki end / (AmE), / ˈstɪki end / (BrE)
an end of a DNA molecule in which one strand of the double helix extends beyond the other, allowing it to form bonds with complementary sequences

Examples

DNA sticky end
Before cloning, scientists ensure that the donor and vector DNA have compatible sticky ends.
Restriction enzymes often produce sticky ends that facilitate the joining of DNA fragments.
The sticky ends generated by the enzyme make the DNA fragments easier to ligate together.

Related Terms

  • antonym – blunt end: an end of a DNA molecule in which both strands terminate evenly
