woody plant

noun
plant with hard stems or tissues
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdi ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈwʊdi ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that has hard, lignified tissues, especially in its stems, such as a tree or shrub.

Examples

a woody plant grows tall
Botanists classify shrubs and trees as types of woody plant.
Most woody plants survive winter by storing nutrients in their roots and stems.
The forest is densely populated with woody plants such as oaks and pines.

Related Terms

  • antonym – herbaceous plant: a plant lacking woody stems above ground; non-woody plant
  • synonym – shrub: a woody plant smaller than a tree, usually with multiple stems
  • synonym – tree: a perennial woody plant, typically with a main stem and branches
