Rhus dermatitis

noun
skin irritation from poison ivy, oak, or sumac
Pronunciation: / ruːs dɚˌmætəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ruːs ˌdɜːməˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A form of skin inflammation caused by contact with plants of the genus Toxicodendron, such as poison ivy, oak, or sumac.

Examples

Rhus dermatitis from poison ivy
After hiking in the woods, she developed a severe case of Rhus dermatitis on her arms.
Doctors often prescribe topical steroids to treat Rhus dermatitis after exposure to poison ivy.
Prevention is the best strategy to avoid Rhus dermatitis when spending time outdoors in affected areas.

  • synonym – contact dermatitis: skin inflammation caused by direct contact with an irritant or allergen
