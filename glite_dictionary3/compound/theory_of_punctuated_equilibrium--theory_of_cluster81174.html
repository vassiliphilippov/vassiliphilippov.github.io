theory of punctuated equilibrium

noun
evolutionary theory: rapid change and long stasis
Pronunciation: / ˈθiəri əv ˌpʌŋktʃueɪtɪd ˌikwɪˈlɪbriəm / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˌpʌŋktjʊeɪtɪd ˌiːkwɪˈlɪbriəm / (BrE)
the scientific theory that evolutionary development is marked by isolated episodes of rapid speciation between long periods of little or no change

Examples

theory of punctuated equilibrium in evolution
According to the theory of punctuated equilibrium, species often remain unchanged for long periods before undergoing rapid evolution.
Many paleontologists now consider the theory of punctuated equilibrium when analyzing fossil records.
The theory of punctuated equilibrium challenged traditional views of gradual evolutionary change.

Related Terms

  • antonym – gradualism: the evolutionary theory that species evolve slowly and continuously over time
