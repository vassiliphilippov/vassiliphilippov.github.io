foot-poundal

noun
unit of work (force x distance)
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊt ˈpaʊnˌdəl / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˈpaʊn.dəl / (BrE)
A unit of work equal to the work done by a force of one poundal acting through a distance of one foot.

Examples

one foot-poundal of work
In older textbooks, work was sometimes measured in foot-poundals instead of joules.
One foot-poundal is a relatively small unit compared to the joule.
The engineer calculated the torque in foot-poundals for the rotating shaft.
