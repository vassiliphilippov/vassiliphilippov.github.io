castor-oil plant

noun
tropical shrub yielding castor oil
Pronunciation: / ˈkæs.tɚ ˌɔɪl ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.stə ˌɔɪl ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a large tropical shrub (Ricinus communis) with broad palmate leaves and spiny capsules, cultivated for its seeds, which produce castor oil and the toxin ricin

Examples

castor-oil plant seeds
Farmers in tropical regions commonly cultivate the castor-oil plant for medicinal and industrial purposes.
The castor-oil plant produces seeds that are processed to extract castor oil for various uses.
The spiny fruit of the castor-oil plant contains both valuable oil and toxic ricin.
