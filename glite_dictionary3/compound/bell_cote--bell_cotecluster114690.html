bell cote

noun
small roofed shelter for bells
Pronunciation: / bɛl koʊt / (AmE), / bɛl kəʊt / (BrE)
a small structure, often with a gable or shed roof, built to house one or more bells, typically on the roof of a church or other building

Examples

During restoration, workers carefully repaired the damaged bell cote on the old schoolhouse.
The ancient chapel had a stone bell cote perched above its entrance.
