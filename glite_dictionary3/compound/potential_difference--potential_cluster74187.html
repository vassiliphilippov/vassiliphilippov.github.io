potential difference

noun
difference in electric charge, measured in volts
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəl ˈdɪfrəns / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃl ˈdɪfrəns / (BrE)
the difference in electric charge between two points in a circuit, measured in volts

Examples

potential difference across a circuit
A battery creates a potential difference that drives current through a circuit.
If the potential difference increases, more current will flow through the resistor.
The potential difference between the two terminals is measured in volts.
