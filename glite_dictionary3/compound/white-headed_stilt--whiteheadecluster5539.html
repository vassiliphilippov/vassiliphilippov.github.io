white-headed stilt

noun
Australian and New Zealand wading bird with white plumage
Pronunciation: / ˌwaɪtˈhɛdəd ˈstɪlt / (AmE), / ˌwaɪtˈhɛdɪd ˈstɪlt / (BrE)
a stilt (Himantopus leucocephalus) native to Australia and New Zealand, characterized by mostly white plumage with black wings and the nape of the neck

Examples

a white-headed stilt wading
A flock of white-headed stilts can often be seen near the shallow lagoons of New Zealand.
Birdwatchers travelled from across the country to photograph the rare white-headed stilt at the wetland reserve.
The white-headed stilt is easily recognized by its striking black-and-white plumage.
