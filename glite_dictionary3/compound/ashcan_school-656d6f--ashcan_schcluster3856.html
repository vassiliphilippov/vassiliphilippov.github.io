Ashcan school

noun
early 20th-century American art movement
Pronunciation: / ˈæʃˌkæn skuːl / (AmE), / ˈæʃˌkan skuːl / (BrE)
a group of early 20th-century American artists whose work depicted realistic and often gritty scenes of urban life

Examples

Ashcan school painters
Many critics praised the Ashcan school for its commitment to realism over romanticism.
