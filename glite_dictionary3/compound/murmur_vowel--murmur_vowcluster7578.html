murmur vowel

noun
incidental schwa sound with consonant
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝːmɚ ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜːmə ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
A schwa sound that is produced incidentally during the articulation of certain consonants, especially in unstressed syllables.

Examples

murmur vowel after consonant
Phoneticians study how a murmur vowel can occur after syllabic consonants.

The word 'button' contains a murmur vowel in the pronunciation of the 't' and 'n'.
