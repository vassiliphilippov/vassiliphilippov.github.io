selective service

noun
system of compulsory military service
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛktɪv ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
a system in which individuals are required by law to serve in the armed forces if selected, especially as operated in the United States

Examples

selective service registration
All 18-year-old men were required to register for the selective service.
During the war, the government reinstated the selective service system to increase military enlistment.
