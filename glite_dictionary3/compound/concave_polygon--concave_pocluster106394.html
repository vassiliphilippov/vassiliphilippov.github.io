concave polygon

noun
polygon with an interior angle over 180°
Pronunciation: / kɑnˌkeɪv ˈpɑliˌɡɑn / (AmE), / kɒnˌkeɪv ˈpɒliɡən / (BrE)
A polygon with at least one interior angle greater than 180°, so that at least one line segment between two points inside the polygon passes outside its boundary.

Examples

irregular concave polygon
A concave polygon can be divided into simpler shapes for area calculation.
The shape of the star is a classic example of a concave polygon.
Unlike a convex polygon, a concave polygon has at least one vertex pointing inward.

Related Terms

  • antonym – convex polygon: a polygon with all interior angles less than 180°
