low frequency

noun
frequency lower than others
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈfriː.kwən.si / (BrE)
a frequency that is lower in pitch compared to others, especially referring to the lower end of the audible spectrum or electromagnetic spectrum

Examples

low frequency sound
Elephants can communicate using low frequency rumbles that travel long distances.
Many subwoofers are designed to reproduce low frequency audio for home theaters.
The storm produced sounds at such a low frequency that they could barely be heard.

Related Terms

  • antonym – high frequency: a frequency that is greater in pitch or cycles per second than others
