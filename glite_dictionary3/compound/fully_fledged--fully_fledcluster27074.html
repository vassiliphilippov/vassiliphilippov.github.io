fully fledged

adjective
having full adult development or plumage
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊli flɛdʒd / (AmE), / ˈfʊli ˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a bird) having developed all the feathers necessary for flight; fully developed or mature.

Examples

fully fledged bird
After months of care, the injured sparrow is now fully fledged and able to fly on its own.
Most eagles do not leave the nest until they are fully fledged and strong enough to soar.
The chick became fully fledged in just a few weeks and was soon ready to leave the nest.
