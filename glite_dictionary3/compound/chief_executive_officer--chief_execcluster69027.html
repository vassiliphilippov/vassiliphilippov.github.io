chief executive officer

noun
most senior company executive
Pronunciation: / tʃiːf ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv ˈɑːfɪsər / (AmE), / tʃiːf ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the highest-ranking executive in a company, responsible for managing the overall operations and making major corporate decisions

Examples

chief executive officer of a company
After the merger, she was appointed chief executive officer of the newly formed organization.
Many investors judge a company's prospects based on the reputation of its chief executive officer.
The chief executive officer addressed the company's employees at the annual meeting.
