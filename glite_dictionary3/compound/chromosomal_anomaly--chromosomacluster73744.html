chromosomal anomaly

noun
abnormality in chromosome structure or number
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmoʊsəməl əˈnɑːməli / (AmE), / krəˈməʊsəməl əˈnɒməli / (BrE)
any abnormal change in the structure or number of chromosomes, often resulting in physical or mental health problems

Examples

chromosomal anomaly in babies
Doctors explained that the baby's health problems were related to a chromosomal anomaly.
Down syndrome is caused by a chromosomal anomaly involving an extra copy of chromosome 21.
Genetic testing can identify certain chromosomal anomalies before birth.
