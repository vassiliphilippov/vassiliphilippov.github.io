Gram method

noun
bacterial staining technique for classification
Pronunciation: / ɡræm ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ɡræm ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a technique for classifying bacteria by staining them with gentian violet and other reagents to determine if they are Gram-positive or Gram-negative

Examples

Gram method for bacteria
After using the Gram method, the microbiologist determined the bacteria were Gram-negative.
Medical students must learn the proper steps of the Gram method during their training.
The Gram method is essential for identifying bacterial species in clinical laboratories.
