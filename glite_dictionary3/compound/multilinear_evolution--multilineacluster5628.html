multilinear evolution

noun
theory of cultural evolution with multiple independent paths
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈlɪniɚ ˌɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈlɪniə ˌiːvəˈljuːʃən / (BrE)
a neo-evolutionary theory in anthropology proposing that cultures evolve along multiple different and independent paths, rather than following a single universal sequence of development.

Examples

multilinear evolution in cultures
Recent anthropological research often references multilinear evolution when analyzing societal changes.
Steward's theory of multilinear evolution emphasizes the diversity of cultural development across societies.
The concept of multilinear evolution challenges earlier assumptions of a universal trajectory for all cultures.
