certificate of incorporation

noun
official document authorizing formation of a corporation
Pronunciation: / sərˈtɪf.ɪ.kət əv ɪnˌkɔr.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / səˈtɪf.ɪ.kət əv ɪnˌkɔː.pərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an official document issued by a government authority registering and approving the formation of a corporation

Examples

certificate of incorporation issued by government
The company received its certificate of incorporation from the state authorities last month.
Without a certificate of incorporation, the business could not legally operate as a corporation.
You must submit the certificate of incorporation when opening a corporate bank account.
