dispersing phase

noun
medium that disperses another in a colloid
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɝːsɪŋ feɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsɪŋ feɪz / (BrE)
In colloids, the substance in which another substance is dispersed.

Examples

dispersing phase in a colloid
In smoke, air serves as the dispersing phase for solid particles.
The dispersing phase in an emulsion is typically water.
The properties of a colloid depend on both the dispersing phase and the dispersed phase.
