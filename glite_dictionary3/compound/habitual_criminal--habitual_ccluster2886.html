habitual criminal

noun
person frequently convicted of crimes
Pronunciation: / həˈbɪtʃuəl ˈkrɪmɪnl̩ / (AmE), / həˈbɪtʃʊəl ˈkrɪmɪnəl / (BrE)
a person who is repeatedly arrested or convicted for criminal behavior, especially of the same type.

Examples

convicted habitual criminal
Despite numerous warnings and arrests, he remained a habitual criminal throughout his adult life.
Police are working to keep habitual criminals off the streets and protect the community.
The judge sentenced the habitual criminal to ten years in prison after his latest offense.

Related Terms

  • synonym – recidivist: a person who continues to commit crimes despite previous punishments
