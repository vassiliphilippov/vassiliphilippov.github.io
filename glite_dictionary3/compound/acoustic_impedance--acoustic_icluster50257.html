acoustic impedance

noun
opposition to sound transmission in a medium
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk ɪmˈpiːdəns / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk ɪmˈpiːdəns / (BrE)
the measure of opposition to the passage of sound waves through a surface or medium, combining both resistance and reactance components

Examples

acoustic impedance of a material
Matching the acoustic impedance of two materials can reduce sound reflection at their boundary.
The acoustic impedance of the wall determines how much sound passes through into the next room.
Ultrasound imaging relies on differences in acoustic impedance between tissues to create clear images.

Related Terms

  • synonym – acoustic reactance: the imaginary part of acoustic impedance representing energy storage in the medium
  • synonym – acoustic resistance: the real part of acoustic impedance representing actual energy loss
