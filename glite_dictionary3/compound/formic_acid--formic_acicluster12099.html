formic acid

noun
a colorless pungent acid found in ants
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent, fuming liquid acid (HCOOH) found naturally in ants and some plants, or manufactured for use in textiles, paper, and insecticides

Examples

formic acid in ant venom
Industrial applications of formic acid include leather processing and dyeing textiles.
Scientists isolated formic acid from red ants for use in laboratory experiments.
The burning sensation after an ant bite is caused by formic acid released into the skin.
