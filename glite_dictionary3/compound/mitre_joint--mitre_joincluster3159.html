mitre joint

noun
bevelled corner joint in woodwork
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtər dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A joint that forms a corner, typically with both pieces bevelled at a 45-degree angle to make a 90-degree corner.

Examples

mitre joint in woodwork
Carpenters often use glue to reinforce a mitre joint and ensure its stability.
The picture frame was assembled using a mitre joint at each corner to achieve a neat finish.
When making a box, a mitre joint provides a more attractive edge than a simple butt joint.

Related Terms

  • antonym – butt joint: Joint where two pieces are joined end to end without beveling
  • synonym – dovetail joint: Interlocking woodworking joint often used for drawers
