tax advantage

noun
benefit that reduces tax liability
Pronunciation: / tæks ədˈvæntɪdʒ / (AmE), / tæks ədˈvɑːntɪdʒ / (BrE)
A benefit provided by laws or regulations that reduces the amount of tax owed on certain activities or investments.

Examples

tax advantage for investments
Many investors seek a tax advantage by contributing to retirement accounts.
Owning a home can confer a tax advantage through mortgage interest deductions.
The government introduced a tax advantage to encourage the use of renewable energy.
