solidarity surcharge

noun
additional government tax for solidarity funding
Pronunciation: / sɑːˌlɪd.əˈɹɪ.t̬i ˈsɝː.tʃɑːrdʒ / (AmE), / sɒˌlɪd.əˈrɪ.ti ˈsɜː.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an additional tax levied by a government in special circumstances to raise funds for programs or objectives intended to promote social or national solidarity, often for reconstruction or support purposes

Examples

solidarity surcharge on income
Debate continues over whether the solidarity surcharge should be retained or abolished in the coming years.

The German government introduced a solidarity surcharge after reunification to help fund infrastructure in the eastern states.
