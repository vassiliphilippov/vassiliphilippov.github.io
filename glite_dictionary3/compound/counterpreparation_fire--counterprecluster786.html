counterpreparation fire

noun
preplanned fire to disrupt imminent enemy attack
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən faɪr / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən faɪə / (BrE)
intensive, preplanned artillery or gunfire directed at enemy forces when an imminent attack is detected, to disrupt or weaken their assault.

Examples

counterpreparation fire disrupts assault
Commanders ordered counterpreparation fire to delay the advancing forces before they reached the main defensive positions.
Effective counterpreparation fire disrupted the enemy's ability to launch a coordinated assault.
The artillery unit opened counterpreparation fire as soon as enemy movement was detected beyond the ridge.
