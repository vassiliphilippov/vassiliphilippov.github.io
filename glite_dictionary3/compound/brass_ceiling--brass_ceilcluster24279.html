brass ceiling

noun
barrier to advancement for minorities or women
Pronunciation: / bræs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / brɑːs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an informal barrier to advancement or promotion, particularly for women or minorities in organizations such as the military or law enforcement

Examples

break the brass ceiling
Many women in law enforcement have struggled against the brass ceiling for years.
She was the first woman in her department to break through the brass ceiling.
The company's new diversity policies aim to address the persistent brass ceiling.
