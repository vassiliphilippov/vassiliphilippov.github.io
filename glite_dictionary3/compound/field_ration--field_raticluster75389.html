field ration

noun
meal supplied to soldiers in the field
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ˌræʃən / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌræʃən / (BrE)
a pre-packaged meal provided to soldiers for use in the field, especially during military operations

Examples

field ration for soldiers
During training exercises, we had to rely on our field rations for several days.
Each soldier was given a field ration before the march began.
He opened his field ration and found a can of beans and some crackers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ration pack: a package containing individually wrapped rations, used in the military
