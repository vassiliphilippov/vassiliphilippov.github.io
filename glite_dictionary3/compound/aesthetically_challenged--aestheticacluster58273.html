aesthetically challenged

adjective
lacking visual appeal; poor aesthetic sense
Pronunciation: / ɛsˈθɛtɪkli tʃəˈlændʒd / (AmE), / iːsˈθɛtɪkli tʃəˈlændʒd / (BrE)
lacking in visual appeal or having poor aesthetic judgment, often used humorously or euphemistically.

Examples

aesthetically challenged appearance
He joked that his old car is aesthetically challenged but still reliable.
Some critics called the new sculpture aesthetically challenged, but others admired its boldness.
The website’s outdated layout makes it rather aesthetically challenged compared to its competitors.
