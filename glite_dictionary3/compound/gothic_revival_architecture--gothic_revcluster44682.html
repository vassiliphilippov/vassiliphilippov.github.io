Gothic revival architecture

noun
architectural style inspired by medieval Gothic design
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk rɪˈvaɪvəl ˈɑːrkɪˌtɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk rɪˈvaɪvəl ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a style of architecture that originated in the late 18th and 19th centuries, inspired by medieval Gothic design and characterized by features such as pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and ornate decoration
