angular momentum

noun
rotational quantity in physics
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡjələr məˈmɛn t̬əm / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊlə məˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
the quantity of rotation of a body, equal to the product of its moment of inertia and its angular velocity, usually measured about a particular axis

Examples

increase angular momentum
Any spinning figure skater relies on angular momentum to execute fast spins.
The conservation of angular momentum explains why a planet continues to orbit the Sun.
When the diver tucks in during a somersault, her angular momentum increases, making her spin faster.
