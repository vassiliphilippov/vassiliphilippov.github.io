price support

noun
government action to keep prices from falling
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪs səˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪs səˌpɔːt / (BrE)
a government policy or subsidy designed to keep the market price of a commodity at a designated minimum level

Examples

price support for farmers
Critics argue that price support policies can distort natural market forces.
Dairy producers benefit from price support programs to avoid losses during years of surplus.
The government implemented a price support to help struggling farmers maintain their income.

Related Terms

  • synonym – subsidy: a sum of money granted by the government to assist an industry or business
