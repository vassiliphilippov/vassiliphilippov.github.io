frost mist

noun
thin fog of ice crystals in cold air
Pronunciation: / frɔst mɪst / (AmE), / frɒst mɪst / (BrE)
A thin fog composed of small ice crystals suspended in the air, typically formed in very cold weather.

Examples

morning frost mist
After a clear night, a delicate frost mist formed along the quiet country road.
In the early morning, a frost mist enveloped the fields, giving them a mysterious look.
Travelers could barely see through the frost mist that hung over the frozen lake.
