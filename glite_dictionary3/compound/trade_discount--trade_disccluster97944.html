trade discount

noun
discount offered by manufacturer to merchant
Pronunciation: / treɪd ˈdɪskaʊnt / (AmE), / treɪd ˈdɪskaʊnt / (BrE)
a reduction in the listed price of goods offered by a manufacturer or wholesaler to a retailer or merchant, usually for commercial reasons

Examples

trade discount on goods
Manufacturers often offer a trade discount to merchants as an incentive to purchase large quantities.
The trade discount was deducted before calculating the net invoice price.
The retailer received a trade discount for ordering merchandise in bulk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cash discount: a reduction in price given for immediate payment
  • antonym – list price: the regular price of an item, without any discount
