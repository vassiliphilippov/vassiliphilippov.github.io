palatine bone

noun
bone forming part of the hard palate
Pronunciation: / ˈpæləˌtaɪn boʊn / (AmE), / ˈpæləˌtaɪn bəʊn / (BrE)
Either of two irregularly shaped bones located at the back of the hard palate, contributing to the formation of the nasal cavity and the floor of the orbits.

Examples

fractured palatine bone
A fracture in the palatine bone can severely affect speech and eating functions.
The anatomy class included a detailed diagram of the palatine bone and its relationship to other facial structures.
The surgeon carefully examined the palatine bone before proceeding with the corrective procedure.
