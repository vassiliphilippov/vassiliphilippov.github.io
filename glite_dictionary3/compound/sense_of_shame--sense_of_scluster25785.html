sense of shame

noun
awareness of ethical responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛns əv ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ˈsɛns əv ʃeɪm / (BrE)
an awareness of one's own ethical responsibility, often motivating a person to act morally or avoid wrongdoing

Examples

deep sense of shame
A strong sense of shame can prevent people from repeating their mistakes.
His sense of shame stopped him from lying to his friends.
She acted out of a sense of shame after realizing she had hurt someone.
