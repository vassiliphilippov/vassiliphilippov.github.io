snarl-up

noun
traffic jam or severe blockage
Pronunciation: / snɑrl ʌp / (AmE), / snɑːl ʌp / (BrE)
A situation in which traffic is prevented from moving normally, usually because of vehicles blocking each other; a traffic jam.

Examples

massive snarl-up on highway
A serious snarl-up delayed our journey by more than an hour.
Rush hour often causes a snarl-up near the city center.
There was a massive snarl-up on the motorway after the accident.
