anti-torque rotor

noun
rotor that prevents helicopter spin
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.taɪˈtɔrk ˈroʊ.tər / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtɔːk ˈrəʊ.tə / (BrE)
a rotor mounted on the tail of a single-rotor helicopter, used to counteract the torque produced by the main rotor and keep the helicopter from spinning uncontrollably

Examples

anti-torque rotor on helicopter tail
A malfunction in the anti-torque rotor can cause the helicopter to spin uncontrollably.
During pre-flight checks, pilots must inspect the anti-torque rotor for any signs of damage.
The anti-torque rotor ensures the helicopter remains stable while hovering in place.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tail rotor: rotor on the tail of a helicopter that controls its yaw
