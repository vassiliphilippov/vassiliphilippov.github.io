North Atlantic Council

noun
main political decision-making body of NATO
Pronunciation: / nɔrθ ætˈlæntɪk ˈkaʊnsl̩ / (AmE), / nɔːθ ətˈlæntɪk ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
the principal political decision-making body of NATO, consisting of permanent representatives from all member countries

Examples

the North Atlantic Council meeting
Decisions made by the North Atlantic Council are binding on all NATO member states.
Each country appoints a representative to the North Atlantic Council to participate in its deliberations.
The North Atlantic Council met to discuss the recent security developments in Eastern Europe.
