visual cell

noun
light-sensitive cell in the retina
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒjuəl sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the retina of the eye that is sensitive to light and transmits visual information to the brain

Examples

visual cell in retina
Damage to visual cells in the retina can result in impaired eyesight or blindness.
Rod and cone visual cells work together to allow vision in both dim and bright light conditions.
Scientists study visual cells to better understand how the eye processes images.
