white oak

noun
a type of pale-wooded oak tree
Pronunciation: / waɪt oʊk / (AmE), / waɪt əʊk / (BrE)
A type of oak tree, mostly found in Europe and North America, known for its pale wood, acorns maturing in one year, and leaves with veins that do not extend beyond the margin.

Examples

majestic white oak tree
In autumn, the leaves of the white oak turn a rich, deep red before falling.
Many parks plant white oak trees because of their impressive size and longevity.
The furniture was crafted from sturdy white oak, giving it a classic and durable finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym – red oak: a species of oak with reddish wood, acorns taking two years to mature
