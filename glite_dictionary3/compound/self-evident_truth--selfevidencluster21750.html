self-evident truth

noun
an obvious truth needing no proof
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ˈɛvədənt truθ / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈɛvɪdənt truːθ / (BrE)
A statement or principle that is considered to be so obvious that it does not require proof or explanation.

Examples

basic self-evident truth
He based his argument on the self-evident truth that all people deserve respect.
One self-evident truth is that water flows downhill.
The Declaration of Independence begins by referencing certain self-evident truths about human rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym – axiom: a statement accepted as true without proof
  • synonym – postulate: a basic principle assumed without proof
