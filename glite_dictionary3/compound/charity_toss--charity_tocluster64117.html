charity toss

noun
unhindered basketball shot from the foul line
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃærəti tɑs / (AmE), / ˈtʃærəti tɒs / (BrE)
An unhindered basketball shot taken from the foul line, awarded to penalize the opposing team for a foul (also called a free throw).

Examples

charity toss after foul
He made both charity tosses after being fouled late in the game.
Missing a charity toss can be costly in a close basketball match.
The team practiced charity tosses to improve their free throw percentage.
