light hour

noun
distance light travels in one hour
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌaʊə / (BrE)
the distance light travels in a vacuum in one hour, approximately one billion kilometers (about 670 million miles)

Examples

one light hour distance
Astronomers use the light hour as a unit when discussing the vast distances within our solar system.
Signals sent from Earth to Jupiter can take nearly an entire light hour to arrive.
The nearest star is over four light hours away from the edge of our solar system.
