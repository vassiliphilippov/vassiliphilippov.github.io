cosmic string

noun
hypothetical one-dimensional energy structure in cosmology
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːz.mɪk strɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɪk strɪŋ / (BrE)
(in cosmology) a hypothetical, extremely thin, one-dimensional structure, theorized to have a high concentration of energy and to behave like a flexible loop.

Examples

a cosmic string in space
Researchers have proposed methods to detect cosmic strings using gravitational waves.
Some physicists believe that cosmic strings may have played a role in the early universe's structure formation.
The concept of a cosmic string is rooted in theoretical predictions from particle physics.
