open season

noun
time when an activity is allowed or encouraged
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈsiːzən / (BrE)
a period during which a particular activity is officially permitted or widely encouraged, often used literally for hunting and figuratively for other pursuits

Examples

open season for hunting
Ever since the merger was announced, it's been open season for new job applicants.
It's open season for fishing in the lakes until September.
The politician declared that it was open season on corruption.

Related Terms

  • antonym – closed season: period during which an activity, especially hunting, is prohibited
