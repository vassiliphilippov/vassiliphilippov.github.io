tunnel vision

noun
loss of peripheral vision; visual impairment
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌnəl ˌvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈtʌnəl ˌvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a condition of visual impairment in which only the central part of the visual field can be seen, with loss of peripheral vision

Examples

tunnel vision after injury
After his accident, the patient developed tunnel vision and could only see things directly in front of him.
Glaucoma is one of the eye conditions that can cause tunnel vision.
She struggled to drive at night because her tunnel vision made it hard to spot hazards at the sides.

Related Terms

  • antonym – peripheral vision: the ability to see objects outside the direct line of vision
