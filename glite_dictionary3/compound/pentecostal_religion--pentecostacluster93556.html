Pentecostal religion

noun
movement in Protestant Christianity noted for revivalist worship and Holy Spirit experiences
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɑs.təl rɪˈlɪdʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɒs.təl rɪˈlɪdʒ.ən / (BrE)
A branch of fundamentalist Protestant Christianity characterized by revivalist worship and an emphasis on personal experiences of the Holy Spirit, similar to those described in the New Testament Pentecost.

Examples

Pentecostal religion worship service
Many members of the Pentecostal religion participate in lively worship services featuring singing and spontaneous prayer.
She was raised in a Pentecostal religion that emphasized speaking in tongues and spiritual healing.
The spread of the Pentecostal religion has been especially rapid in parts of Africa and South America.

Related Terms

  • antonym – Catholicism: The faith and practices of the Roman Catholic Church
  • synonym – Pentecostalism: Movement within Protestant Christianity emphasizing experiences of the Holy Spirit
  • synonym – fundamentalism: Strict adherence to basic principles, especially in a religious context
