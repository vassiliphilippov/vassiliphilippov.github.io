getting even

noun
act of taking revenge or reciprocating a wrong
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛtɪŋ ˈivən / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtɪŋ ˈiːvn̩ / (BrE)
the act of seeking to reciprocate a wrong or harm done to oneself, especially by taking revenge

Examples

getting even with someone
After the prank, he focused all his efforts on getting even with his colleagues.
For some people, getting even is more satisfying than an apology.
She believed in getting even rather than turning the other cheek.

Related Terms

  • antonym – forgiveness: the action of forgiving or being forgiven
  • synonym – retaliation: the act of returning a like for like, especially evil for evil
  • synonym – revenge: the action of inflicting hurt or harm on someone for an injury or wrong suffered at their hands
