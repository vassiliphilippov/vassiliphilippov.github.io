form family

noun
artificial taxonomic category in biology
Pronunciation: / fɔrm ˈfæməli / (AmE), / fɔːm ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
(Biology) An artificial category used in taxonomy for classifying organisms whose true evolutionary relationships are unclear, typically ranked between order and genus.

Examples

form family classification group
Because the relationships among these plants remain unclear, they are grouped within a provisional form family.
Taxonomists sometimes use a form family when a group does not fit neatly into established families.
