International Finance Corporation

proper noun
World Bank Group agency for private investment
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənl faɪˈnæns ˌkɔrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃ(ə)nəl faɪˈnæns ˌkɔːp(ə)ˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an international agency, part of the World Bank Group, that invests in private companies and guarantees loans to encourage economic development.

Examples

International Finance Corporation loan support
Our company received a loan guarantee from the International Finance Corporation to expand operations.
The International Finance Corporation is a key member of the World Bank Group focused on private sector growth.
The International Finance Corporation provides funding for sustainable development projects in emerging markets.
