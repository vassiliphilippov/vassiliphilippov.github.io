noble metal

noun
metal resistant to corrosion or oxidation
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊbəl ˈmɛtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl ˈmɛtl / (BrE)
a metal that resists corrosion and oxidation, even at high temperatures; examples include gold, platinum, and silver

Examples

noble metal like gold
Gold is a noble metal prized for its beauty and resistance to tarnishing.
Silver, though a noble metal, can still tarnish under certain conditions.
Unlike iron, a noble metal like platinum does not rust easily.

Related Terms

  • antonym – base metal: a common and chemically reactive metal not considered noble
