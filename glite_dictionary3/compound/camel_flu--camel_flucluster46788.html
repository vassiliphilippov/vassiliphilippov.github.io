camel flu

noun
MERS viral disease transmitted from camels
Pronunciation: / ˈkæməl flu / (AmE), / ˈkaməl fluː / (BrE)
a viral respiratory disease, known medically as MERS, caused by Middle East respiratory syndrome coronavirus (MERS-CoV) and typically transmitted to humans from camels

Examples

camel flu infection
He contracted camel flu after coming into contact with infected camels at a farm.
Researchers are studying how camel flu spreads from animals to humans.
The recent outbreak of camel flu in the region has prompted increased health screenings at airports.
