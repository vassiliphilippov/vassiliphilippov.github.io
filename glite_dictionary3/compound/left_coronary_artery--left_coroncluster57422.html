left coronary artery

noun
main artery supplying the left side of the heart
Pronunciation: / lɛft ˌkɔːr.iˈɛr.i ˈɑːr.tə.ri / (AmE), / lɛft ˌkɒr.ɪˈær.ɪ ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
the main artery that branches from the left side of the aorta and supplies blood to the left side of the heart

Examples

blocked left coronary artery
A blockage in the left coronary artery can lead to a serious heart attack.
During the operation, the surgeon examined the left coronary artery for any signs of narrowing.
The left coronary artery is responsible for supplying oxygenated blood to much of the heart muscle.
