buffalo clover

noun
poisonous southern US herb with blue flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfəloʊ ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfələʊ ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States, particularly Texas, with silky leaves and blue flowers; noted as a leading cause of livestock poisoning in the region

Examples

buffalo clover poisons cattle
Cattle grazing on buffalo clover may become seriously ill due to its toxic properties.
Ranchers in Texas routinely inspect their land for buffalo clover to protect their livestock.
The fields were dotted with patches of buffalo clover in early spring.
