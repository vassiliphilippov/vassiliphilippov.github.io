Ewing's tumor

noun
rare malignant bone marrow tumor
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːɪŋz ˌtuːmər / (AmE), / ˈjuːɪŋz ˌtjuːmə / (BrE)
A rare malignant tumor found in bone marrow, most often occurring in the pelvis or long bones, primarily affecting children and young adults.

Examples

Ewing's tumor in bone
Biopsy results confirmed the diagnosis of Ewing's tumor in the patient’s pelvis.
The doctor explained that the pain in his leg was caused by a Ewing's tumor.
Treatment for Ewing's tumor often involves a combination of chemotherapy, surgery, and radiation therapy.
