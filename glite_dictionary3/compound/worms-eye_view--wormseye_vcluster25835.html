worm's-eye view

noun
view or perspective from a low position
Pronunciation: / wɜrmz aɪ vju / (AmE), / wɜːmz aɪ vjuː / (BrE)
a perspective seen from a low or inferior position, often from the ground or from the point of view of someone with little power or stature

Examples

a worm's-eye view photo
The director chose to film the scene from a worm's-eye view, emphasizing the character's vulnerability.
The novel gives us a worm's-eye view of the factory workers' daily struggles.
Through her documentary, she offers a worm's-eye view of life on the city streets.
