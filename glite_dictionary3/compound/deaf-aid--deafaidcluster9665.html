deaf-aid

noun
device to assist hearing
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfˌeɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛfˌeɪd / (BrE)
an electronic device worn in or behind the ear to amplify sound for people with hearing loss

Examples

wear a deaf-aid
He wears a deaf-aid to help him follow conversations in noisy places.
Some modern deaf-aids are almost invisible when worn.
The doctor recommended a new deaf-aid after her hearing worsened.
