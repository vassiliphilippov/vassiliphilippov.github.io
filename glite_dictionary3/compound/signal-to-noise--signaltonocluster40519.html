signal-to-noise

noun
ratio of signal strength to noise
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪz / (BrE)
The ratio of the strength of a desired signal to the level of background noise, used to measure the quality or clarity of information, especially in electronics and communications.

Examples

signal-to-noise ratio
Before transmitting the data, engineers measured the signal-to-noise to ensure the integrity of the information.
Improving the signal-to-noise can make data analysis more reliable in scientific experiments.
The signal-to-noise in this audio recording is unusually high, so the music sounds exceptionally clear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – SNR: abbreviation for signal-to-noise ratio
  • antonym – noise: unwanted background interference
