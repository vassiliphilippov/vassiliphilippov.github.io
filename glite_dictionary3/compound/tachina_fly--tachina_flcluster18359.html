tachina fly

noun
parasitic fly used in pest control
Pronunciation: / təˈkaɪnə flaɪ / (AmE), / təˈkiːnə flaɪ / (BrE)
A bristly fly whose larvae are parasitic in caterpillars and other insects, important in the biological control of pests.

Examples

tachina fly controls pests
A tachina fly can help reduce populations of harmful caterpillars in crops.
Farmers sometimes introduce tachina flies as a natural form of pest control.
The larvae of a tachina fly develop inside the bodies of their insect hosts.
