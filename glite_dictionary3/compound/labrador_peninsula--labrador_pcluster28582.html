Labrador Peninsula

proper noun
large peninsula in eastern Canada
Pronunciation: / ˈlæbrədɔr pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈlæbrədɔː pəˈnɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
a large peninsular region in eastern Canada, located between Hudson Bay and the Labrador Sea, encompassing most of Quebec and the mainland part of Newfoundland and Labrador.

Examples

the Labrador Peninsula region
Explorers crossed the Labrador Peninsula in search of new trade routes to the north.
Much of the Labrador Peninsula is covered by boreal forest and numerous lakes.
The remote communities of the Labrador Peninsula are known for their rugged terrain and harsh climate.
