animal starch

noun
glycogen stored in animal tissues
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪml ˌstɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈænɪml ˌstɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a form of carbohydrate (glycogen) stored mainly in the liver and muscles of animals, which breaks down into glucose when needed by the body for energy

Examples

store animal starch in muscles
During intense exercise, muscles draw on stored animal starch to produce energy.
Some biology textbooks use the term animal starch as a synonym for glycogen.
The liver converts excess glucose into animal starch for later use.

Related Terms

  • synonym – glycogen: the main form in which carbohydrate is stored in animals
