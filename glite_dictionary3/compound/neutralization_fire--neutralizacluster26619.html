neutralization fire

noun
military fire to render target ineffective
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
Firepower directed at a target to render it ineffective or unusable, usually temporarily, without necessarily destroying it.

Examples

neutralization fire on enemy position
Commanders called for neutralization fire on the bunker to prevent its use by the advancing troops.
Effective neutralization fire can disrupt communications and immobilize enemy equipment.
The artillery unit provided neutralization fire to halt enemy operations at the bridge.

  • synonym – suppressive fire: gunfire intended to prevent enemy movement or actions
