ice cap

noun
large, permanent land-covering of ice
Pronunciation: / aɪs kæp / (AmE), / aɪs kap / (BrE)
a thick layer of ice and snow that permanently covers a large area of land, especially at the poles or on mountain peaks

Examples

polar ice cap
A thick ice cap covers the summit of the mountain year-round.
Scientists are studying the melting of the Antarctic ice cap.
The Arctic ice cap is shrinking due to rising global temperatures.

  • synonym – glacier: a slowly moving river of ice, typically flowing down mountainsides
