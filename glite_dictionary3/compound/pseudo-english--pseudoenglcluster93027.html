pseudo-English

noun
imitation or incorrect form of English
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
Speech or writing that imitates English in form but is incorrect, artificially constructed, or not genuinely English.

Examples

pseudo-English phrases
Her essay contained several pseudo-English expressions that confused the reader.
The commercial used pseudo-English slogans that made little sense to native speakers.
We laughed at the film's cheesy pseudo-English dialogue.
