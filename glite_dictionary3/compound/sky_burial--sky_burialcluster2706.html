sky burial

noun
Tibetan ritual exposing corpse to birds
Pronunciation: / skaɪ ˈbɜriəl / (AmE), / skaɪ ˈbɜːriəl / (BrE)
a traditional Tibetan funeral practice in which a human corpse is left exposed on a mountaintop to be consumed by vultures or other scavenging birds

Examples

sky burial ceremony
Few tourists are allowed to witness a traditional sky burial ceremony.
In Tibet, the sky burial is seen as a way of returning the body to nature.
The documentary explored the ancient practice of sky burial in remote regions.
