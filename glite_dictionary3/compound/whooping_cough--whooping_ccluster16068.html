whooping cough

noun
infectious coughing disease (pertussis)
Pronunciation: / ˈhuːpɪŋ kɔf / (AmE), / ˈhuːpɪŋ kɒf / (BrE)
an infectious disease of the respiratory system, especially affecting children, marked by severe coughing fits followed by a 'whoop' sound during inhalation

Examples

whooping cough vaccine
The doctor diagnosed her with whooping cough after she developed a persistent, severe cough.
Vaccination has greatly reduced the number of whooping cough cases in most countries.
Young children are especially at risk of complications from whooping cough.
