cylinder lock

noun
lock operated by a rotating cylinder mechanism
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlɪndər lɑk / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪndə lɒk / (BrE)
A lock operated by a cylinder mechanism, in which the correct key aligns pins to allow the cylinder to rotate and move the bolt.

Examples

cylinder lock keyhole
He lost the key to his cylinder lock and had to call a locksmith.
Most apartment buildings use cylinder locks because they're easy to rekey.
The front door was secured with a new cylinder lock for added security.
