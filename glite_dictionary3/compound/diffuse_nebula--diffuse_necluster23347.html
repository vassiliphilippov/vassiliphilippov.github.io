diffuse nebula

noun
cloud of gas and dust in space
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjus nəˈbjuːlə / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːs nɪˈbjuːlə / (BrE)
an interstellar cloud of gas and dust illuminated by stars within or near it, appearing as a faint, hazy patch in the sky

Examples

bright diffuse nebula
Astronomers study the composition of a diffuse nebula to learn about the birth of new stars.
Photographs of the Milky Way often highlight a glowing diffuse nebula among the star clusters.
The Orion region contains a beautiful diffuse nebula visible to the naked eye under dark skies.
