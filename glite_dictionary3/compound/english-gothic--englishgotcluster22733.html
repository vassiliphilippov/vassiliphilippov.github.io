English-Gothic

noun
Gothic architectural style in 14th–15th century England
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈɡɑːθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
A Gothic architectural style that developed in England during the 14th and 15th centuries, noted for its vertical lines, four-centered arches (Tudor arches), and fan vaulting.

Examples

English-Gothic church architecture
During the 15th century, many churches were rebuilt in the English-Gothic style, featuring characteristic Tudor arches.
The English-Gothic period saw the introduction of fan vaulting to English cathedrals.
The cathedral is a stunning example of English-Gothic architecture, with its soaring vaults and intricate stonework.
