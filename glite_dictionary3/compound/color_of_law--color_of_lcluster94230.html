color of law

noun
apparent but unlawful legal authority
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚ əv lɑː / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə əv lɔː / (BrE)
The appearance or pretense of legal authority, especially when used to commit acts that are actually illegal or unauthorized.

Examples

color of law abuse
The lawsuit argues that the eviction was conducted under color of law and was therefore unlawful.
The police officer was found guilty of violating the man's rights under color of law.
Various abuses have occurred when officials acted under color of law without proper legal authorization.

Related Terms

  • synonym – false pretense: a claim or act intended to deceive
  • antonym – legitimate authority: actual, lawful authority
