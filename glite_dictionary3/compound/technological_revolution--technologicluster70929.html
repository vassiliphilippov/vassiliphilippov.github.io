technological revolution

noun
period of radical technological change
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛk.nəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛk.nəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a period of rapid and significant change, especially when a society transitions from an agricultural to an industrial-based economy through major technological advances

Examples

the technological revolution transformed society
Countries that embraced the technological revolution often saw improvements in economic output and quality of life.
Many historians argue that the technological revolution dramatically increased the pace of urbanization in Europe.
The technological revolution has transformed the way we communicate, work, and live.
