relative quantity

noun
amount measured in relation to another
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈkwɑntəti / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈkwɒntɪti / (BrE)
an amount of something measured or considered in relation to another amount or standard

Examples

relative quantity of water
The relative quantity of students choosing science over art has increased this year.
The relative quantity of sugar compared to flour determines the cake's sweetness.
We are more interested in the relative quantity of rainfall each month than the absolute totals.

Related Terms

  • antonym – absolute quantity: an amount measured on its own, not relative to anything else
