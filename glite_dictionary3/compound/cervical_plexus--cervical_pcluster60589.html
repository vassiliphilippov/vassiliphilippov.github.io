cervical plexus

noun
nerve network in the neck
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.vɪ.kəl ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪ.kəl ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a network of nerves located beneath the sternocleidomastoid muscle in the neck, supplying sensation and movement to parts of the neck, shoulders, and diaphragm

Examples

cervical plexus injury
Damage to the cervical plexus can result in loss of sensation in the shoulder region.
During surgery in the neck, care must be taken to avoid injury to the cervical plexus.
The cervical plexus innervates several muscles responsible for movements in the neck.
