great duckweed

noun
widespread floating aquatic plant
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt ˈdʌkˌwid / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈdʌkwiːd / (BrE)
A widespread aquatic plant (Spirodela polyrhiza) with small floating leaves, found in many parts of the world except South America, New Zealand, and some oceanic islands.

Examples

Large mats of great duckweed can reduce oxygen levels in stagnant water.
