body armour

noun
protective clothing covering the whole body
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːdi ˌɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˌɑːmə / (BrE)
Protective clothing made of strong materials, designed to shield the wearer's whole body from injury, especially in combat or hazardous situations.

Examples

wear body armour in battle
Modern soldiers are often issued lightweight body armour for protection in the field.
The museum displayed a medieval suit of body armour among its ancient weapons.
The police officer wore body armour during the dangerous operation.
