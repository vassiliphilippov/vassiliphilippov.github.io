silver thaw

noun
coating of ice from freezing rain
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvɚ θɔ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə θɔː / (BrE)
A weather phenomenon in which freezing rain forms a coating of ice on surfaces, giving them a shiny or silvery appearance and often creating hazardous conditions.

Examples

silver thaw covers trees
A silver thaw coated the trees in a beautiful sheen, making the landscape look magical.
After the silver thaw, power lines and roads were dangerously slick and covered in ice.
Driving during the silver thaw was quite treacherous.
