black disease

noun
liver disease in livestock caused by flukes
Pronunciation: / blæk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / blæk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease of the liver in sheep and cattle, caused by liver flukes and their toxins, characterized by sudden death and darkening of the skin.

Examples

black disease in sheep
Sudden death in sheep can often be traced back to black disease during the wet season.
The entire flock was vaccinated to prevent black disease from spreading.
Veterinarians recommend regular fluke control to reduce cases of black disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym – infectious necrotic hepatitis: another term for black disease, emphasizing the inflammation of the liver
