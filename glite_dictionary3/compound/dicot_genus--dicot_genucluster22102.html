dicot genus

noun
genus of two-seed-leaved plants
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌkɑt ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.kɒt ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants that have two embryonic seed leaves (cotyledons), typically visible at germination.

Examples

dicot genus classification
Botanists classify roses as part of a dicot genus due to their seed structure.
The oak belongs to a dicot genus that includes many common trees.
When identifying a dicot genus, look for plants with two embryonic leaves.
