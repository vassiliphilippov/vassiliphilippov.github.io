azimuth mark

noun
surveying point for azimuth direction
Pronunciation: / ˈæzɪməθ mɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈæzɪməθ mɑːk / (BrE)
a marked point used in surveying to indicate an exact azimuth direction from a reference station, often based on true North

Examples

azimuth mark for surveying
An azimuth mark is crucial for aligning instruments to true North in field surveys.
The survey team used the azimuth mark to establish precise directional measurements.
They installed a new azimuth mark to improve the accuracy of geodetic observations.
