OTC derivative

noun
privately traded financial contract
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ ti ˈsi dəˌrɪvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊ tiː ˈsiː dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (BrE)
a financial contract whose terms are privately negotiated and traded directly between two parties, rather than through an exchange

Examples

OTC derivative contract terms
He managed a portfolio that included both exchange-traded and OTC derivatives.
Many financial institutions prefer to use OTC derivatives for greater flexibility in contract terms.
The risks associated with OTC derivatives can be higher due to the lack of standardization and regulation.
