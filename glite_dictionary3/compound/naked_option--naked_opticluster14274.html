naked option

noun
option contract without underlying security
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪkɪd ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪd ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
a put or call option sold or bought without owning the underlying security, exposing the trader to higher risk

Examples

sell a naked option
Because a naked option exposes you to unlimited losses, it is not suitable for beginners.
He lost a significant sum when his naked option position went against the market.
Traders should understand the risks involved before selling a naked option.

Related Terms

  • antonym – covered option: an option for which the seller holds the underlying security
