heat shield

noun
protective covering against extreme heat (esp. for spacecraft)
Pronunciation: / hit ʃild / (AmE), / hiːt ʃiːld / (BrE)
a layer or device that protects a spacecraft or other vehicle from extreme heat, especially during reentry into the atmosphere

Examples

heat shield protects spacecraft
During descent, the damaged heat shield caused serious concern for the crew.
Engineers designed a new heat shield to withstand higher temperatures.
The heat shield prevented the spacecraft from burning up on reentry.

Related Terms

  • synonym – insulation: material used to protect against heat, cold, or sound
