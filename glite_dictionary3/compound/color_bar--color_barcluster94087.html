color bar

noun
barrier excluding people based on race
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər bɑr / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə bɑː / (BrE)
a social or legal system that prevents people of different races, especially black people and white people, from mixing, particularly by barring black people from certain rights or activities

Examples

color bar in schools
After years of activism, the color bar was finally abolished in public institutions.
He campaigned tirelessly against the color bar in sports clubs.
The color bar in schools denied many children access to quality education.

  • synonym – segregation: the enforced separation of different racial groups
