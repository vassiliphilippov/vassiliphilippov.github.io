respiratory disease

noun
disease affecting the respiratory system
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɪrəˌtɔri dɪˈziz / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease that affects the organs and tissues involved in breathing, such as the lungs, trachea, and bronchi.

Examples

chronic respiratory disease
Asthma is a common respiratory disease that affects millions of people worldwide.
Doctors are researching new treatments for chronic respiratory diseases such as COPD and bronchitis.
Exposure to pollution can increase your risk of developing a respiratory disease.
