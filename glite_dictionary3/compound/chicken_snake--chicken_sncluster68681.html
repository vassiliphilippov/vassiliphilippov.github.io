chicken snake

noun
large North American non-venomous snake
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪkɪn sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn sneɪk / (BrE)
a large, non-venomous North American snake, especially one that preys on birds and eggs, such as the rat snake

Examples

a chicken snake in a barn
Chicken snakes are generally harmless to humans but can be a nuisance to poultry farmers.
The farmer found a chicken snake in the henhouse searching for eggs.
When threatened, a chicken snake may vibrate its tail to mimic a rattlesnake.
