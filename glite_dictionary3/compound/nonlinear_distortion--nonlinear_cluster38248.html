nonlinear distortion

noun
signal distortion due to nonlinearity
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈlɪniɚ dɪsˈtɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈlɪniə dɪsˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
distortion in a system where the output signal does not vary in direct proportion to the input signal, often causing unwanted changes in sound or signal quality.

Examples

nonlinear distortion in audio signal
Amplifiers must be designed to minimize nonlinear distortion for high-fidelity audio reproduction.
Measuring nonlinear distortion is essential when evaluating the performance of electronic filters.
The presence of nonlinear distortion can introduce unwanted harmonics into the output signal.

Related Terms

  • antonym – linear distortion: signal distortion where output remains directly proportional to input, affecting amplitude or phase but not introducing new frequencies
