malignant pustule

noun
severe anthrax skin lesion
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt ˈpʌstʃuːl / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt ˈpʌstjuːl / (BrE)
a severe bacterial skin lesion, usually caused by anthrax, that starts as a small bump and develops into an ulcer with toxin discharge, often leading to serious systemic symptoms and can be fatal if untreated.

Examples

painful malignant pustule
If untreated, a malignant pustule can lead to life-threatening complications such as septicemia and organ failure.
Swift medical intervention is critical when someone develops a malignant pustule following contact with infected livestock.
The physician identified a malignant pustule on the patient's forearm after exposure to contaminated animal hides.
