ocean sunfish

noun
very large flat-bodied marine fish
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊʃən ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a very large, flat-bodied marine fish with high dorsal and anal fins and a rudder-like tail, found in warm oceans worldwide; one of the largest bony fish

Examples

giant ocean sunfish
Scientists study the migration patterns of the ocean sunfish around the world.
The ocean sunfish is known for its massive size and odd, disk-shaped body.
While diving off the coast, she spotted an ocean sunfish gliding near the surface.
