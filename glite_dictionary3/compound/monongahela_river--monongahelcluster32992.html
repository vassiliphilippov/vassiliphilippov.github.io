Monongahela River

proper noun
river in the eastern United States
Pronunciation: / məˌnɑːŋgəˈhiːlə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / məˌnɒŋgəˈhiːlə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in the eastern United States that rises in northern West Virginia and flows north through Pennsylvania, joining the Allegheny River at Pittsburgh to form the Ohio River.

Examples

the Monongahela River flows north
A popular activity for locals is boating on the Monongahela River during the summer months.
Many towns along the Monongahela River developed due to the river's importance for transportation and industry.
The Monongahela River flows north from West Virginia into Pennsylvania, forming part of the Ohio River at Pittsburgh.
