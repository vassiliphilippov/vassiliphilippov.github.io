X-ray photograph

noun
photographic image made using X-rays
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks reɪ ˈfəʊtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɛks reɪ ˈfəʊtəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a photographic image produced by exposing film or a digital detector to X-rays, typically used to examine the inside of the body for medical diagnosis

Examples

X-ray photograph of a broken bone
A clear X-ray photograph is essential for accurate diagnosis of bone injuries.
She kept the X-ray photograph as part of her medical records.
The doctor examined the X-ray photograph to look for any signs of a fracture.

Related Terms

  • synonym – X-ray image: an image produced by passing X-rays through an object or body
  • synonym – radiograph: an image produced on a sensitive plate or film by X-rays, gamma rays, or similar radiation
