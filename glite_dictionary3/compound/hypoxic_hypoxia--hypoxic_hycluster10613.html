hypoxic hypoxia

noun
insufficient blood oxygenation in lungs
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑksɪk haɪˈpɑksiə / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒksɪk haɪˈpɒksiə / (BrE)
A type of hypoxia caused by inadequate oxygenation of the blood in the lungs.

Examples

hypoxic hypoxia at high altitude
High altitudes can sometimes cause hypoxic hypoxia due to lower oxygen levels in the air.
The patient developed hypoxic hypoxia as a result of severe pneumonia.
Treatment for hypoxic hypoxia often includes supplemental oxygen therapy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – anemic hypoxia: hypoxia due to reduced oxygen-carrying capacity of blood
  • degree lower – histotoxic hypoxia: hypoxia caused by inability of tissues to use available oxygen
