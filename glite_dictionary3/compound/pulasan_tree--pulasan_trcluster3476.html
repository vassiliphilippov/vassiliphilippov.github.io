pulasan tree

noun
tropical fruit tree of Southeast Asia
Pronunciation: / puˈlɑːsən ˌtriː / (AmE), / pʊˈlɑːsən ˌtriː / (BrE)
An East Indian tropical fruit tree (Nephelium mutabile) that bears fruit similar to but sweeter than the rambutan.

Examples

pulasan tree fruit harvest
A mature pulasan tree can yield hundreds of fruits each season.
Many farmers prefer the pulasan tree because its fruit tastes sweeter than rambutan.
The pulasan tree thrives in the humid lowlands of Malaysia and Indonesia.
