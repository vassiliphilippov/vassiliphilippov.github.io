height of capillary rise

noun
vertical rise of liquid in a capillary tube
Pronunciation: / haɪt əv kəˈpɪləri raɪz / (AmE), / haɪt əv kəˈpɪləri raɪz / (BrE)
the vertical distance that a liquid ascends in a capillary tube due to capillary action

Examples

height of capillary rise in a tube
Scientists measure the height of capillary rise to determine the surface tension of a liquid.
The height of capillary rise decreases as the diameter of the tube increases.
The unique properties of water result in a noticeable height of capillary rise in thin glass tubes.
