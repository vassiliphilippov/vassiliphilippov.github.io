four-wing flying fish

noun
flying fish with enlarged pectoral and pelvic fins
Pronunciation: / fɔr wɪŋ ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪʃ / (AmE), / fɔː wɪŋ ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪʃ / (BrE)
A species of flying fish with both pectoral and pelvic fins enlarged, allowing them to glide above the water.

Examples

a four-wing flying fish gliding
Researchers observed a four-wing flying fish escaping predators by leaping out of the water.
The four-wing flying fish can glide impressive distances above the ocean surface.
The distinctive look of the four-wing flying fish makes it easy to spot among other sea life.
