sodium lauryl sulphate

noun
synthetic detergent and surfactant
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm ˈlɔːril ˈsʌlfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm ˈlɔːrɪl ˈsʌlfeɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic detergent and surfactant used in cleaning products and personal care items, known for removing grease but sometimes causing skin irritation

Examples

sodium lauryl sulphate in shampoo
It is important not to swallow toothpaste containing sodium lauryl sulphate.
Many shampoos contain sodium lauryl sulphate to create a rich lather.
Some people may experience skin irritation from products with sodium lauryl sulphate.
