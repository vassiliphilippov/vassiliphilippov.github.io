proxy fight

noun
shareholder battle to control company management
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑksi ˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒksi ˌfaɪt / (BrE)
an attempt by a group of shareholders, usually an acquirer, to win enough shareholder votes to replace a company's management or board, typically as a way to gain control over the company

Examples

proxy fight for control
A heated proxy fight erupted between the current management and a group of shareholders seeking greater control.
During the proxy fight, both sides spent millions trying to sway other investors to their cause.
The company was forced into a proxy fight after activist investors sought to oust its board of directors.
