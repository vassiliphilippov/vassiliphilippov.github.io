disk brake

noun
friction brake using a spinning disk and pads
Pronunciation: / dɪsk breɪk / (AmE), / dɪsk breɪk / (BrE)
A type of brake in which friction is applied to both sides of a rotating disk by brake pads, typically operated hydraulically.

Examples

disk brake on a car
Modern cars are often equipped with disk brakes on all four wheels.
The mechanic inspected the disk brake for signs of wear and tear.
Upgrading to a larger disk brake can improve a vehicle's stopping power.
