high-altitude climbing

noun
mountain climbing at high elevations
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈæltɪˌtjud ˈklaɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈæltɪˌtjuːd ˈklaɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of climbing mountains or rock faces at elevations considered to be significantly above sea level, often where reduced oxygen and harsh conditions present specialized challenges

Examples

high-altitude climbing expedition
Proper acclimatization is essential when engaging in high-altitude climbing above 5,000 meters.
She trained for months to prepare for the difficulties of high-altitude climbing.
The team faced severe weather conditions during their high-altitude climbing expedition.
