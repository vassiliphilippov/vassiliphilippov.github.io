apodictic certainty

noun
absolute logical certainty
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈdɪktɪk sɚˈtɪnti / (AmE), / ˌapəˈdɪktɪk səˈtɪnti / (BrE)
The quality of being logically or necessarily true, so that denial is impossible; absolute certainty, especially in a philosophical or logical context.

Examples

apodictic certainty in logic
Euclidean geometry was once believed to exemplify apodictic certainty before the discovery of non-Euclidean alternatives.
Philosophers debate whether any statement can possess true apodictic certainty outside of mathematics or logic.
She challenged the idea that scientific theories could ever reach apodictic certainty instead of always being open to revision.
