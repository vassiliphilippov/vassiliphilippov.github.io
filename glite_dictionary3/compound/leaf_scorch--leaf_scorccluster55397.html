leaf scorch

noun
disease causing scorched leaves in plants
Pronunciation: / lif skɔrtʃ / (AmE), / liːf skɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A plant disease characterized by the browning or 'burned' appearance of leaf edges or surfaces, typically caused by environmental stress, pathogens, or poor water conditions.

Examples

leaf scorch on trees
Gardeners recommend deep watering to prevent leaf scorch in newly planted shrubs.
