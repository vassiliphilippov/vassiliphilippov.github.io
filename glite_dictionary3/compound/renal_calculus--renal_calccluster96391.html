renal calculus

noun
kidney stone; kidney-formed mineral mass
Pronunciation: / ˈriːnəl ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈriːnəl ˈkalkjʊləs / (BrE)
A hard, stone-like mass that forms in the kidney, usually consisting of mineral salts.

Examples

A renal calculus may block the flow of urine and require surgical intervention.
