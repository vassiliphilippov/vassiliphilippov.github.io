Bering Sea

proper noun
sea between Alaska and Siberia
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrɪŋ si / (AmE), / ˈbɛrɪŋ siː / (BrE)
a body of water in the North Pacific, located between Alaska and Siberia, connected to the Arctic Ocean by the Bering Strait

Examples

the Bering Sea region
Ice floes often drift across the Bering Sea during the winter months.
Many migratory birds use the Bering Sea as a stopover during their journeys.
The Bering Sea is rich in marine life, making it important for the fishing industry.
