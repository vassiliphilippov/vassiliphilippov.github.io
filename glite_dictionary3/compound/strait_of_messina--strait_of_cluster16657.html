Strait of Messina

proper noun
strait between Sicily and mainland Italy
Pronunciation: / streɪt əv məˈsɪnə / (AmE), / streɪt əv mɛˈsɪnə / (BrE)
the narrow strait that separates the island of Sicily from the mainland of southern Italy

Examples

the Strait of Messina between Sicily
Ships frequently pass through the Strait of Messina en route to other Mediterranean ports.
Strong tidal currents make the Strait of Messina a challenging crossing for swimmers and sailors.
The Strait of Messina offers stunning views of both Sicily and the Calabrian coast.
