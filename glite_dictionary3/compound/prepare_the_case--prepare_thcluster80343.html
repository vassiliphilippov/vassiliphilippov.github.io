to prepare the case

verb
organize legal arguments and evidence
Pronunciation: / prɪˈpɛr ðə keɪs / (AmE), / prɪˈpeə ðə keɪs / (BrE)
to organize and arrange legal arguments, evidence, and documentation in readiness for a trial or hearing

Examples

to prepare the case for court
She worked late hours to prepare the case for her client.
The attorney spent weeks to prepare the case before the court date.
They collaborated closely to prepare the case and ensure a strong defense.
