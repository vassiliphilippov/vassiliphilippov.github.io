banian tree

noun
East Indian tree with aerial roots and multiple trunks
Pronunciation: / ˈbæni.ən triː / (AmE), / ˈbæni.ən triː / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Ficus benghalensis) that produces aerial roots which grow down to the soil and form additional trunks, eventually spreading to cover a wide area.

Examples

huge banian tree
Over time, the banian tree can spread over a large area, creating a complex network of trunks.
The banian tree in the village square provides shade for dozens of people.
We stopped to admire the impressive aerial roots of a century-old banian tree.
