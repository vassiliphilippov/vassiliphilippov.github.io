posttraumatic amnesia

noun
memory loss after trauma
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊsttrəˈmætɪk æmˈniʒə / (AmE), / ˌpəʊsttrəˈmatɪk amˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
A loss of memory for events that occur immediately after a traumatic event, sometimes extending to events during and for a long time following the trauma.

Examples

posttraumatic amnesia after injury
Doctors monitored the duration of her posttraumatic amnesia to assess the severity of her brain injury.
Extended periods of posttraumatic amnesia are often considered a sign of serious head trauma.
The patient suffered from posttraumatic amnesia after the car accident, unable to recall anything from the hours following the event.
