parabolic geometry

noun
geometry based on Euclidean axioms
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈbɑlɪk dʒiˈɑmətri / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈbɒlɪk dʒiˈɒmətri / (BrE)
A branch of geometry that follows Euclid's axioms, distinguishing it from hyperbolic and elliptic geometries.
