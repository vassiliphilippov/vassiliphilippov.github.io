planographic printing

noun
flat-surface printing technique
Pronunciation: / ˌplænəˈɡræfɪk ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌplænəˈɡræfɪk ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a printing process in which the printing areas are on a flat surface that is ink-receptive, as opposed to areas that repel ink

Examples

planographic printing process
Lithography is a common type of planographic printing used for posters and fine art prints.
Many art students learn planographic printing methods such as lithography in their studio courses.
Modern newspapers were once produced using planographic printing for high-quality images and text.
