special jury

noun
jury with special expertise for complex cases
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈdʒɝːi / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A jury selected for its members' special knowledge to decide cases involving complicated issues.

Examples

special jury for expert cases
A special jury may be called when the facts require expertise outside that of a typical juror.
Due to the technical nature of the evidence, a special jury was deemed necessary for the trial.
The court convened a special jury to consider the intricate business fraud allegations.
