to weigh the anchor

verb
to raise an anchor before departure
Pronunciation: / weɪ ði ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / weɪ ði ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to raise an anchor from the sea or riverbed in preparation for sailing

Examples

to weigh the anchor before sailing
At dawn, the captain instructed them to weigh the anchor and head out to sea.
Before leaving the harbor, the sailors had to weigh the anchor and stow it securely.
The crew moved quickly to weigh the anchor as the ship prepared to depart.

  • antonym – drop anchor: to lower an anchor for a ship to stay in place
