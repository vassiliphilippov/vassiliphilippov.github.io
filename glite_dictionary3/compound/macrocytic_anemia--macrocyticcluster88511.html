macrocytic anemia

noun
anemia with large red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊˈsɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊˈsɪt.ɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
Anemia characterized by unusually large red blood cells (erythrocytes).

Examples

macrocytic anemia blood test
The doctor diagnosed her with macrocytic anemia after reviewing her blood tests.
Treatment of macrocytic anemia often involves addressing the underlying nutritional deficiencies.
Vitamin B12 deficiency is a common cause of macrocytic anemia in adults.
