early warning system

noun
network to detect incoming threats early
Pronunciation: / ˈɝːli ˈwɔrnɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈɜːli ˈwɔːnɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system of equipment and personnel for detecting and reporting approaching threats such as enemy missiles or aircraft, allowing time for defensive action

Examples

military early warning system
After the radar detected an unidentified aircraft, the early warning system sent an automatic alert to command.
The development of the new early warning system significantly improved national air defense capabilities.
The military relies on an early warning system to detect incoming missiles before they reach their targets.
