paralytic abasia

noun
inability to walk due to leg paralysis
Pronunciation: / pəˈrælɪtɪk əˈbeɪziə / (AmE), / pəˈralɪtɪk əˈbeɪzɪə / (BrE)
A form of abasia in which the inability to walk is due to paralysis of the leg muscles.

Examples

paralytic abasia after nerve injury
In rare cases, paralytic abasia may result from severe nerve injury or neurological disease.
Physiotherapy is often ineffective for paralytic abasia if the underlying paralysis cannot be reversed.
The patient was diagnosed with paralytic abasia after experiencing complete paralysis in both legs.
