replacement cost

noun
cost of replacing an asset with a new one
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpleɪsmənt kɔst / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪsmənt kɒst / (BrE)
the current cost of replacing an existing fixed asset with a new one of equivalent function and capacity

Examples

replacement cost of equipment
Before buying new machinery, they calculated the replacement cost to assess if it was affordable.
Many property owners underestimate the replacement cost when setting their insurance coverage.
The company's insurance policy covers the replacement cost of damaged equipment.
