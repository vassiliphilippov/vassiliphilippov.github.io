hydrocyanic acid

noun
poisonous solution of hydrogen cyanide in water
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊsaɪˈænɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊsaɪˈanɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous solution of hydrogen cyanide in water, used in fumigation and the manufacture of organic compounds

Examples

hydrocyanic acid poisoning risk
Exposure to even small amounts of hydrocyanic acid can be extremely dangerous to humans.
In the laboratory, hydrocyanic acid must be handled with great care due to its toxicity.
Some early pesticide formulations contained hydrocyanic acid as the active fumigant.
