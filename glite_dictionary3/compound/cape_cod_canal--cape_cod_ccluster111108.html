Cape Cod Canal

noun
canal in Massachusetts connecting two bays
Pronunciation: / keɪp kɑd kəˈnæl / (AmE), / keɪp kɒd kəˈnæl / (BrE)
A canal in southeastern Massachusetts, USA, connecting Cape Cod Bay with Buzzards Bay, providing a navigable waterway across the base of Cape Cod.

Examples

the Cape Cod Canal bridges
Heavy traffic on the Cape Cod Canal bridges is common during summer weekends.
Many ships use the Cape Cod Canal to save time when traveling between Boston and New York.
The Cape Cod Canal is a popular spot for scenic walks and cycling along its banks.
