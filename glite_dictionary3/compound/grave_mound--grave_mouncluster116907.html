grave mound

noun
heap of earth over an ancient grave
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪv maʊnd / (AmE), / ɡreɪv maʊnd / (BrE)
an artificial heap of earth built over a grave, especially in prehistoric times

Examples

ancient grave mound
A large grave mound marks the site where the prehistoric ruler was buried.
Some grave mounds are still visible in the fields outside the old village.
The team of archaeologists excavated the ancient grave mound and discovered several artifacts.
