oak leaf cluster

noun
U.S. military award device for multiple medals
Pronunciation: / oʊk liːf ˈklʌstər / (AmE), / əʊk liːf ˈklʌstə / (BrE)
a United States military decoration in the form of bronze or silver oak leaves and acorns, attached to a medal to indicate that the recipient has been awarded the same medal more than once

Examples

receive an oak leaf cluster
He was awarded another oak leaf cluster after the successful mission.
Her ribbon displayed two silver oak leaf clusters, signifying multiple awards of the medal.
The colonel received an oak leaf cluster for his outstanding service.
