ante-nuptial agreement

noun
contract made before marriage
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈnʌp.ʃəl əˈɡriː.mənt / (AmE), / ˌantiˈnʌp.ʃəl əˈɡriː.mənt / (BrE)
A legal contract entered into by a couple before marriage, specifying the ownership of their respective assets should the marriage fail.

Examples

ante-nuptial agreement before marriage
Some people believe an ante-nuptial agreement is a practical step for protecting assets.
The couple spent weeks negotiating the terms of their ante-nuptial agreement.
They hired a lawyer to draft an ante-nuptial agreement before their wedding.
