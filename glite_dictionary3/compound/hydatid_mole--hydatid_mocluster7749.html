hydatid mole

noun
abnormal pregnancy with fluid-filled sacs
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdəˌtɪd moʊl / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdətɪd məʊl / (BrE)
a pregnancy abnormality in which the chorionic villi develop into clusters of fluid-filled sacs, usually leading to the death of the fetus

Examples

hydatid mole during pregnancy
A hydatid mole can cause severe complications if not diagnosed early during pregnancy.
Doctors explained that the presence of a hydatid mole meant the pregnancy could not continue.
The ultrasound revealed a hydatid mole, which required immediate medical intervention.
