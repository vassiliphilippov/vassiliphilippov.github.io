British West Indies

proper noun
former British-controlled Caribbean islands
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪtɪʃ wɛst ˈɪndiːz / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ wɛst ˈɪndiːz / (BrE)
the islands in the Caribbean that were formerly colonies of Britain, including the Bahamas, Saint Lucia, Antigua, Grenada, Jamaica, Barbados, and Trinidad.

Examples

the British West Indies islands
Many of the cricket teams in the British West Indies have a long and storied rivalry.
My ancestors emigrated from one of the British West Indies islands in the nineteenth century.
Sugar production was a major industry in the British West Indies during the colonial era.
