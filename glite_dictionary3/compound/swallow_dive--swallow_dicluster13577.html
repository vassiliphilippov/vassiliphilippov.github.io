swallow dive

noun
dive with arched back and arms outstretched
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑːloʊ daɪv / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ daɪv / (BrE)
A dive in which the diver arches the back with arms outstretched before entering the water, resembling the flight of a swallow.

Examples

perfect swallow dive
Her swallow dive was so graceful it drew applause from the audience.
Many professional divers practice the swallow dive to improve their form and aesthetics.
The gymnast executed a perfect swallow dive into the pool, impressing the judges.

Related Terms

  • antonym – jackknife: a different type of dive in which the body is bent at the waist and then straightened before entry
