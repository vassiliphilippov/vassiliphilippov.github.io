Korea Strait

noun
strait between Korea and Japan
Pronunciation: / kəˈriː.ə streɪt / (AmE), / kəˈrɪə streɪt / (BrE)
A narrow body of water between South Korea and Japan, connecting the East China Sea with the Sea of Japan (East Sea).

Examples

the Korea Strait between countries
During World War II, naval battles took place in the Korea Strait.
Many ships travel through the Korea Strait as they move between the Pacific and East Asian ports.
The Korea Strait is known for its rich marine biodiversity and strategic importance.
