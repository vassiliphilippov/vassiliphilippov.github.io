steroid hormone

noun
hormone regulating sex organ development
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛr ɔɪd ˈhɔr moʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɪə rɔɪd ˈhɔː məʊn / (BrE)
any hormone synthesized from cholesterol that regulates various physiological processes, especially those affecting growth and development of sex organs

Examples

steroid hormone controls growth
Doctors sometimes prescribe a steroid hormone to treat hormonal imbalances.
Estrogen is a well-known steroid hormone involved in female reproductive development.
The production of steroid hormones is essential for normal growth and development.
