anchor light

noun
light displayed by anchored ships
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋkɚ laɪt / (AmE), / ˈaŋkə laɪt / (BrE)
a light, usually white, displayed on a ship to indicate that it is at anchor and not underway

Examples

anchor light on ship
Every vessel must display an anchor light after sunset when it is not making way.
The crew turned on the anchor light as night fell to warn other boats of their position.
Without a visible anchor light, the ship could be at risk of being hit by passing traffic.

  • synonym – navigation light: a light used on ships for navigation and to indicate position or status
