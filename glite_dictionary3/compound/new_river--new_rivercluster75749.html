New River

proper noun
river in southeastern United States
Pronunciation: / ˈnu ˌrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː ˌrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in the southeastern United States, flowing north from North Carolina through Virginia to West Virginia, where it joins the Kanawha River.

Examples

the New River in West Virginia
Many people enjoy whitewater rafting on the New River in West Virginia.
The New River Gorge Bridge is a famous landmark spanning the river.
The New River is one of the oldest rivers on the North American continent.
