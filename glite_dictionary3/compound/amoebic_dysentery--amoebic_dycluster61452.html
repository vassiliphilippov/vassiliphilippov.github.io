amoebic dysentery

noun
intestinal infection causing severe diarrhea
Pronunciation: / əˈmibɪk dɪˈsɛnˌtɛri / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɪk dɪˈsɛnt(ə)ri / (BrE)
A severe intestinal infection caused by the protozoan parasite Entamoeba histolytica, typically transmitted through food or water contaminated with feces, and characterized by diarrhea, abdominal pain, and often blood in the stool.

Examples

amoebic dysentery causes diarrhea
Drinking untreated water in the area increased the risk of contracting amoebic dysentery.
Severe abdominal cramps can be a symptom of amoebic dysentery in infected individuals.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with amoebic dysentery after a series of laboratory tests.
