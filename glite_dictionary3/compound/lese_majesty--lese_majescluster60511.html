lese majesty

noun
crime of offending a sovereign or state
Pronunciation: / leɪz məˈʒɛsti / (AmE), / leɪz ˈmadʒɪsti / (BrE)
the crime of violating, offending, or showing disrespect toward a sovereign or government authority, especially considered a serious offense against the dignity of a ruler or state

Examples

lese majesty against the king
In some countries, lese majesty is still punishable by law, with severe penalties for offenders.
The journalist was arrested for lese majesty after publishing an article critical of the monarch.
The old laws regarding lese majesty are rarely enforced in modern democracies.
