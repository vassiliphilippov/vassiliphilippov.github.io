free market policy

noun
economic policy favoring market competition
Pronunciation: / ˌfriː ˈmɑrkɪt ˈpɑlɪsi / (AmE), / ˌfriː ˈmɑːkɪt ˈpɒlɪsi / (BrE)
an economic policy that encourages little or no government interference and promotes open competition among businesses

Examples

Many economists argue that a free market policy leads to greater efficiency and innovation.
Opponents of free market policy claim it can increase inequality within society.
