sabbatical year

noun
one-year sabbatical leave
Pronunciation: / səˈbætɪkəl jɪr / (AmE), / səˈbætɪkəl jɪə / (BrE)
A period of leave from work or study lasting one year, typically taken by academics or professionals for rest, research, or travel.

Examples

take a sabbatical year
After ten years of teaching, she took a sabbatical year to conduct research abroad.
During his sabbatical year, he published two books and attended several international conferences.
Many universities allow professors to apply for a sabbatical year every seven years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sabbatical: a period of paid leave granted to a worker for study or travel
