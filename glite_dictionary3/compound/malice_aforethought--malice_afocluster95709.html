malice aforethought

noun
criminal intent, especially in murder cases
Pronunciation: / ˈmælɪs əˈfɔrˌθɔt / (AmE), / ˈmælɪs əˈfɔːˌθɔːt / (BrE)
(Law) The intention to commit a wrongful act, especially murder, with knowledge of its illegality, often a necessary element in proving murder.

Examples

malice aforethought in murder
Because he lacked malice aforethought, the charge was reduced from murder to manslaughter.
The jury was asked to consider whether the defendant acted with malice aforethought.
To convict someone of murder, the prosecution must prove malice aforethought beyond a reasonable doubt.
