unctuous behavior

noun
excessively ingratiating conduct
Pronunciation: / ˈʌŋk.tʃu.əs bəˈheɪ.vjɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌŋk.tjʊ.əs bɪˈheɪ.vjə / (BrE)
behavior that is excessively flattering, ingratiating, or insincerely earnest, often to gain favor or advantage.

Examples

unctuous behavior towards bosses
Her unctuous behavior towards the director was obvious to her colleagues.
People often see through unctuous behavior and regard it as insincere.
The manager's unctuous behavior made everyone in the meeting uncomfortable.
