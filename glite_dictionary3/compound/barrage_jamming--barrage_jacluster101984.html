barrage jamming

noun
simultaneous jamming across many frequencies
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɑʒ ˈdʒæmɪŋ / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ ˈdʒamɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of electronic jamming in which a wide range of frequencies are jammed at the same time

Examples

barrage jamming radio signals
Modern radar systems are often designed to resist barrage jamming tactics.
The effectiveness of barrage jamming depends on the power and bandwidth of the transmitter.
The military used barrage jamming to disrupt all enemy radio communications in the region.
