visual system

noun
the body's system for vision
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the system of anatomical structures and processes in the body responsible for vision, including the eyes, optic nerves, and brain regions involved in sight

Examples

the visual system of the brain
Damage to the brain can impair the visual system and affect a person's ability to interpret images.
Scientists study the visual system to better understand how we perceive color and motion.
The human visual system can adapt to a wide range of lighting conditions.
