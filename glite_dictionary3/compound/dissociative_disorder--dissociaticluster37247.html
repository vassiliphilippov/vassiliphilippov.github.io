dissociative disorder

noun
psychiatric disorder involving severe dissociation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsoʊsiˌeɪtɪv dɪsˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsɪətɪv dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental disorder characterized by severe dissociation, where the usual integration of consciousness, memory, identity, or perception of self breaks down.

Examples

dissociative disorder symptoms
Researchers are studying the causes of dissociative disorder to better understand how trauma affects the mind.
The patient was diagnosed with a dissociative disorder after experiencing episodes of memory loss and identity confusion.
Treatment for dissociative disorder often involves psychotherapy and support groups.
