Congregational Church

noun
autonomous Protestant Christian denomination
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəl tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənəl tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination in which each local church is independently self-governing.

Examples

local Congregational Church
He was baptized at the local Congregational Church in his hometown.
Many of the early settlers in New England were members of the Congregational Church.
The Congregational Church practices self-governance, allowing each congregation to make its own decisions.

  • synonym – Presbyterian Church: A Protestant denomination with a governing body of elders
