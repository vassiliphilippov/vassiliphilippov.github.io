blood poisoning

noun
serious infection of the bloodstream
Pronunciation: / blʌd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / blʌd ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
A serious condition in which harmful bacteria or toxins invade the bloodstream from an infection elsewhere in the body, often leading to widespread inflammation.

Examples

blood poisoning after infection
After a minor cut became infected, he developed blood poisoning and had to be hospitalized.
Doctors treated her blood poisoning quickly to prevent life-threatening complications.
Early symptoms of blood poisoning can include fever, chills, and rapid heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sepsis: a serious medical condition caused by the body's response to infection in the bloodstream
