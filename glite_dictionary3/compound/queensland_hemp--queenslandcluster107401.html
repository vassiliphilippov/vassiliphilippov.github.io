Queensland hemp

noun
tropical herb yielding strong fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈkwiːnzˌlænd hɛmp / (AmE), / ˈkwiːnzlənd hɛmp / (BrE)
a tall tropical herb (Hibiscus cannabinus) cultivated for its strong, soft fiber, sometimes grown for forage or medicinal purposes, and occasionally considered a weed

Examples

Queensland hemp fiber production
Queensland hemp is valued for its fine bast fibers, which are used in making ropes and sacks.
Farmers in some regions grow Queensland hemp as a forage crop for livestock.
If not properly managed, Queensland hemp can become an aggressive weed in the fields.
