A level

noun
advanced school qualification (UK)
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
An advanced level qualification in a specific subject, typically taken by students in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland after two years of study, usually at age 18.

Examples

pass A level exams
He decided to retake his A level exams to improve his results.
She took biology, chemistry, and mathematics for her A levels.
To study medicine at university, you usually need high A level grades.
