tongueless frog

noun
frog species lacking a tongue
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌŋ.ləs frɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ.ləs frɒɡ / (BrE)
A nearly aquatic species of frog native to Africa, Panama, and northern South America, notable for lacking a tongue.

Examples

tongueless frog in wetlands
Scientists were surprised to find a population of tongueless frogs thriving in Panama’s wetland reserves.
The tongueless frog adapts well to a fully aquatic lifestyle in its native African rivers.
Unlike most amphibians, the tongueless frog cannot use a tongue to catch its prey.
