Danth's Law

noun
internet adage about argument dynamics
Pronunciation: / dænθz lɔ / (AmE), / dænθz lɔː / (BrE)
An internet adage stating that when someone in an online discussion insists that they have won an argument, it likely means they have lost it; more broadly, it refers to the evolution of memes and humor in discussions.

Examples

Danth's Law in arguments
In heated debates online, someone invoking Danth's Law often signals the discussion is over.
Many internet communities are familiar with Danth's Law and reference it during meme discussions.
She joked about Danth's Law when someone loudly claimed victory in the comment section.
