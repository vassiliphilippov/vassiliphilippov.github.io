beggar-my-neighbor policy

noun
policy benefiting self by harming others
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛɡər maɪ ˈneɪbər ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə maɪ ˈneɪbə ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
an economic or political strategy that seeks to advance one's own interests by damaging or disadvantaging others, especially other countries

Examples

beggar-my-neighbor policy in trade
During the 1930s, several nations implemented beggar-my-neighbor policies to try to escape the Great Depression.
Many economists warn that a beggar-my-neighbor policy can lead to trade wars and global instability.
The country adopted a beggar-my-neighbor policy by imposing tariffs that harmed its trading partners.

Related Terms

  • antonym – free trade: international trade left to its natural course without tariffs or other restrictions
  • synonym – protectionism: an economic policy of restricting imports to protect domestic industries
