appeal board

noun
official panel for hearing appeals
Pronunciation: / əˈpil bɔrd / (AmE), / əˈpiːl bɔːd / (BrE)
a committee or panel of officials appointed to review and decide on appeals, especially in administrative or regulatory matters, but which does not function as a court of law

Examples

appeal board decision
Any decision made by the department can be challenged before an appeal board.
The appeal board reviewed the case and recommended a reversal of the original ruling.
The employee submitted his complaint to the appeal board after being dismissed.

Related Terms

  • antonym – court: a judicial body that hears and decides cases; not usually administrative
  • synonym – tribunal: a body established to settle certain types of disputes; judicial or quasi-judicial
