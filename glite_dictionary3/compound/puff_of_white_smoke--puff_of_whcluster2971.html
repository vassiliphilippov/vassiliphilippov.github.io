puff of white smoke

noun
a brief burst of white smoke
Pronunciation: / pʌf ʌv waɪt smoʊk / (AmE), / pʌf ɒv waɪt sməʊk / (BrE)
a small, brief burst of white smoke, typically seen coming from a chimney, engine, or similar source

Examples

a puff of white smoke rises
A sudden puff of white smoke drifted from the old locomotive as it started moving.
They watched a puff of white smoke rise from the campfire and disappear into the morning sky.
When the new pope is elected, a puff of white smoke is released from the Sistine Chapel chimney.
