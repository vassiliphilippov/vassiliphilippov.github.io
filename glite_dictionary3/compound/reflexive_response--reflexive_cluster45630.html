reflexive response

noun
automatic or instinctive reaction to a stimulus
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛksɪv rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
an automatic or instinctive reaction to a stimulus, not involving conscious thought

Examples

reflexive response to pain
A doctor may check your knee’s reflexive response with a soft hammer during a physical examination.
Her reflexive response to the sudden noise was to jump back in surprise.
His reflexive response to criticism was to become defensive, without even thinking about it.
