tidal wave

noun
large wave caused by tides
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.dəl weɪv /; / ˈtaɪ.dl ˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dəl weɪv /; / ˈtaɪ.dl ˌweɪv / (BrE)
an extremely large wave or sudden rise of water along the coast, often caused by tides, storms, or natural forces, and sometimes causing destruction
