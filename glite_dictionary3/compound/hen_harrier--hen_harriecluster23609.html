hen harrier

noun
medium-sized bird of prey (Circus cyaneus)
Pronunciation: / hɛn ˈhæriər / (AmE), / hɛn ˈhæriə / (BrE)
A medium-sized bird of prey (Circus cyaneus) found in North America and Europe, with long wings and tail; it nests in marshes and open land and is known for its low flight while hunting.

Examples

a hen harrier hunting
During migration season, several hen harriers were observed hunting over the marshes.
The hen harrier population is declining due to habitat loss and persecution.
We spotted a hen harrier gliding low over the moorland.
