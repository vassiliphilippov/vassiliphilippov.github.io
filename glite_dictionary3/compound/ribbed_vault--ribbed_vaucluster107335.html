ribbed vault

noun
vault with arched rib framework
Pronunciation: / ɹɪbd vɔlt / (AmE), / ɹɪbd vɔːlt / (BrE)
an architectural vault with a framework of intersecting arched ribs supporting the ceiling, typical in Gothic architecture

Examples

Gothic ribbed vault
Many Gothic churches feature ribbed vaults as a key element of their architectural style.
Restoration work revealed the original colors of the ribbed vault above the nave.
The cathedral's ceiling is supported by a series of ribbed vaults that date back to the 13th century.
