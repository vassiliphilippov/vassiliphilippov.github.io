fire walker

noun
person who walks on burning coals
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ˌwɔkɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌwɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who walks barefoot over burning coals, typically as part of a demonstration of courage or a ritual

Examples

a fire walker crosses coals
Many cultures view the fire walker as a symbol of bravery and resilience.
She trained for weeks before becoming a skilled fire walker at the local festival.
The crowd watched in awe as the fire walker calmly crossed the bed of hot coals.
