participation financing

noun
loan shared by several banks
Pronunciation: / pɑrˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən ˈfaɪnænsɪŋ / (AmE), / pɑːˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən ˈfaɪnænsɪŋ / (BrE)
a loan arrangement in which a group of banks jointly provide funds to a borrower for a loan that is too large for any one bank alone

Examples

participation financing for big projects
Many large projects require participation financing because the loan amounts exceed the risk appetite of individual banks.
The company secured participation financing from a syndicate of international banks to fund its expansion.
Through participation financing, multiple lenders share both the risk and the rewards of a substantial loan.
