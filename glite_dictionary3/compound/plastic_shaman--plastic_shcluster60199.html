plastic shaman

noun
false or superficial shamanic leader
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstɪk ˈʃɑːmən / (AmE), / ˈplæstɪk ˈʃɑːmən / (BrE)
A person who falsely claims to have spiritual or shamanic powers, often appropriating the traditions and rituals of indigenous peoples without authenticity or authority.

Examples

fake plastic shaman ceremony
Many indigenous groups warn against those they call plastic shamans exploiting their sacred traditions.
She realized the so-called guru was just another plastic shaman with no real knowledge of the rituals.
The self-proclaimed plastic shaman charged people huge sums for supposed healing ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • synonym – charlatan: A person falsely claiming to possess special knowledge or skills
  • antonym – shaman: A person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of spirits, especially in indigenous cultures
