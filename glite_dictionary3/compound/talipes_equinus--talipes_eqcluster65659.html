talipes equinus

noun
clubfoot with toes pointed downward
Pronunciation: / ˈtæl.ɪˌpiːz ɪˈkwaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtæl.ɪ.piːz ɪˈkwiː.nəs / (BrE)
A form of clubfoot in which the toes point downward due to muscular abnormalities in the leg.

Examples

talipes equinus toes downward
Talipes equinus often affects walking because the heel cannot touch the ground.
Physical therapy can help improve mobility in children with talipes equinus.
The patient was diagnosed with talipes equinus at birth and required corrective surgery.
