white water rapid

noun
fast, turbulent river section
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt ˌwɔtər ˈræpɪd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌwɔːtə ˈræpɪd / (BrE)
a section of a river where the water flows quickly and turbulently, typically creating white, frothy waves

Examples

dangerous white water rapid
Adventure seekers often travel to rivers with challenging white water rapids for the thrill of rafting.
The guide explained the different levels of difficulty found in various white water rapids.
The kayakers paddled carefully through the white water rapid to avoid overturning.
