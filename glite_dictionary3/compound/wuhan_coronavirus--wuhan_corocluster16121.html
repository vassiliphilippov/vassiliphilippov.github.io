Wuhan coronavirus

noun
disease caused by SARS-CoV-2 virus, pandemic of 2020
Pronunciation: / wuːˈhɑn kəˌroʊnəˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / wuːˈhæn kəˈrəʊnəˌvʌɪrəs / (BrE)
the viral disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, first identified in Wuhan, China in late 2019, which led to a global pandemic in 2020

Examples

the Wuhan coronavirus outbreak
Many early news reports referred to COVID-19 as the Wuhan coronavirus before the official name was assigned.
Researchers quickly sequenced the genome of the Wuhan coronavirus to develop diagnostic tests.
The global outbreak of the Wuhan coronavirus led to widespread travel restrictions.
