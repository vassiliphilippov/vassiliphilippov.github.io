case-to-infection ratio

noun
ratio of disease cases to infections
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪs tu ɪnˈfɛkʃən ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs tʊ ɪnˈfɛkʃən ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
The number of cases of a disease divided by the number of infections with the agent that causes the disease.

Examples

low case-to-infection ratio
A low case-to-infection ratio suggests that many people who become infected do not get seriously ill.
Researchers estimated the case-to-infection ratio to determine how many infected individuals actually developed symptoms.
Understanding the case-to-infection ratio helps public health officials assess the severity of an outbreak.
