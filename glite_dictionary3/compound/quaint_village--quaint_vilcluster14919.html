quaint village

noun
charming, old-fashioned small village
Pronunciation: / kweɪnt ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / kweɪnt ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A small village that is charmingly old-fashioned or attractively unusual in appearance or character.

Examples

quaint village in the countryside
She dreamed of retiring to a quaint village with cobblestone streets and friendly neighbors.
The movie was set in a quaint village on the coast of Ireland.
We spent the afternoon strolling through the quaint village, admiring the flower-filled cottages.
