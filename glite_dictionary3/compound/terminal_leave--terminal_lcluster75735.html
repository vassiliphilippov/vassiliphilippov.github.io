terminal leave

noun
final leave before military discharge
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.mɪ.nəl liːv / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪ.nəl liːv / (BrE)
A period of final leave granted to a service member before discharge from military service, during which they are still officially employed.

Examples

on terminal leave before discharge
After receiving his papers, he spent two weeks on terminal leave before officially leaving the army.
Many soldiers use their terminal leave to search for new jobs or relocate.
She moved home during her terminal leave as she prepared for civilian life.
