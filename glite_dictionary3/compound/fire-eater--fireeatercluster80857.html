fire-eater

noun
an aggressively belligerent person
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌiːtər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌiːtə / (BrE)
a person who is aggressively belligerent or eager to fight or argue, often in a political or controversial context

Examples

political fire-eater
In his youth, Mark had a reputation for being something of a fire-eater at work.
The newspaper described the protest leader as a fire-eater unafraid to challenge authority.
The senator was known as a fire-eater who relished heated debates in Congress.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dove: a person who opposes war or conflict, especially in politics
  • synonym – hothead: an impetuous or easily angered person
