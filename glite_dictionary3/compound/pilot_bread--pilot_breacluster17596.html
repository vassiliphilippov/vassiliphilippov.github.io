pilot bread

noun
hard, unsalted maritime biscuit
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlət brɛd / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət brɛd / (BrE)
a very hard, unsalted biscuit or bread, traditionally used as a staple food on ships

Examples

pilot bread for sailors
During long voyages, sailors often survived on pilot bread and salted meat.
She packed pilot bread for their camping trip, knowing it would stay fresh for weeks.
We tasted pilot bread at the maritime museum, and it was remarkably hard and bland.
