retinal cone

noun
cell in the retina for color vision
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛt.ən.əl koʊn / (AmE), / ˈret.ɪ.nəl kəʊn / (BrE)
a type of cell in the retina of the eye that is sensitive to bright light and responsible for color vision

Examples

retinal cone cell in eye
Damage to a retinal cone can lead to difficulties distinguishing between certain colors.
Scientists study how each retinal cone responds to different wavelengths of light.
The human eye contains three types of retinal cone that allow us to perceive a wide range of colors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – rod cell: a type of retinal cell sensitive to low light, not color
