salt depletion

noun
excessive loss of salt from the body
Pronunciation: / sɔlt dɪˈpliʃən / (AmE), / sɒlt dɪˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
a condition in which the body loses too much salt, disrupting the balance of electrolytes, often due to vomiting, sweating, urination, or diarrhea

Examples

salt depletion after sweating
Athletes who train in hot weather are at higher risk of salt depletion due to excessive sweating.
The doctor explained that the patient's vomiting resulted in severe salt depletion.
Untreated salt depletion can lead to serious complications such as muscle cramps and confusion.
