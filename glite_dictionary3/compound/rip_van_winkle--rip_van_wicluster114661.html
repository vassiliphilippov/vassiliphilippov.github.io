Rip van Winkle

proper noun
title character who sleeps for 20 years
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪp væn ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˌrɪp van ˈwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
the title character in a story by Washington Irving who sleeps for twenty years and wakes to a changed world; often used to describe someone oblivious to change over time

Examples

the story of Rip van Winkle
After taking a long break from technology, she joked she was a Rip van Winkle in the digital world.
He felt like a Rip van Winkle returning after years abroad, unable to recognize the city.
Many called him a modern-day Rip van Winkle for being unaware of all the recent changes.
