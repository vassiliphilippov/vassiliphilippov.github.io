mess-up

noun
a badly botched or muddled thing
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛs ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmɛs ʌp / (BrE)
a situation or thing that has been badly organized, done, or handled; a botched or muddled job or result

Examples

a big mess-up
His attempt to fix the sink resulted in a major mess-up.
The project turned into a complete mess-up after they lost the original files.
We'll need extra time to sort out this mess-up before the bosses arrive.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blunder: a careless or stupid mistake
  • synonym – fiasco: a complete failure, especially in a ludicrous or humiliating way
  • antonym – success: the achievement of a desired aim or result; the opposite of a mess-up
