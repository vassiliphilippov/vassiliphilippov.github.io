hypoglycemic agent

noun
drug lowering blood sugar
Pronunciation: / haɪpoʊɡlaɪˈsiːmɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / hʌɪpəʊɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug or substance that lowers the level of glucose in the blood, used to treat diabetes mellitus

Examples

hypoglycemic agent for diabetes
Insulin is a well-known hypoglycemic agent that lowers blood sugar levels quickly.
Some patients may require more than one hypoglycemic agent for effective glucose control.
The doctor prescribed a hypoglycemic agent to help manage her type 2 diabetes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – antidiabetic: a substance used to control blood sugar in diabetes
  • antonym – hyperglycemic agent: a substance that increases blood glucose
