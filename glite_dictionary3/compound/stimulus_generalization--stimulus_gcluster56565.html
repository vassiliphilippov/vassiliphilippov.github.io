stimulus generalization

noun
psychological phenomenon: response to similar stimuli
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪmjələs ˌdʒɛnərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjələs ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the tendency for a learned response to occur to stimuli similar to, but not identical with, the original conditioned stimulus

Examples

stimulus generalization in learning
After being bitten by a dog, she developed stimulus generalization and became afraid of all dogs, not just the one that bit her.
Children often display stimulus generalization by responding similarly to sounds that resemble the original bell used in conditioning experiments.
Successful therapy sometimes involves reversing stimulus generalization so that a person's learned fears do not extend to unrelated situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stimulus discrimination: the learned ability to distinguish between a conditioned stimulus and other similar stimuli
