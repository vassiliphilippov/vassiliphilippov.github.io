break-in

noun
illegal entry into premises to commit a crime
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ɪn / (BrE)
An act of illegally entering a building or premises, typically to commit a crime such as theft.

Examples

break-in at a house
After the break-in, they installed a new security system at their house.
Police are investigating a recent break-in at the local museum.
The shop owner discovered a break-in had occurred during the night.

  • synonym – burglary: illegally entering a building, especially to steal
