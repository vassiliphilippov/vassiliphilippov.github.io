indeterminacy principle

noun
physics: quantum rule on measurement limits
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈtɜrmɪnəsi ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈtɜːmɪnəsi ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
the theory in quantum mechanics stating that it is impossible to measure certain pairs of physical properties, such as position and momentum, with complete accuracy at the same time.

Examples

Heisenberg's indeterminacy principle in physics
Heisenberg's famous indeterminacy principle challenged classical assumptions about the measurement of physical systems.
Many experiments in quantum mechanics are influenced by the indeterminacy principle.
The indeterminacy principle explains why we cannot know both the position and momentum of a particle precisely.
