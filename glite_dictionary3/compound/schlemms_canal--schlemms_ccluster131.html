Schlemm's canal

noun
circular canal in the eye for fluid drainage
Pronunciation: / ʃlɛmz kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ʃlɛmz kəˈnal / (BrE)
a circular channel located in the eye that drains aqueous humor from the anterior chamber into the anterior ciliary veins

Examples

Schlemm's canal drains fluid
A healthy Schlemm's canal is essential for maintaining normal intraocular pressure.
During surgery, the ophthalmologist checked the condition of the Schlemm's canal to ensure proper drainage.
Glaucoma can develop if the Schlemm's canal becomes blocked and fluid builds up in the eye.
