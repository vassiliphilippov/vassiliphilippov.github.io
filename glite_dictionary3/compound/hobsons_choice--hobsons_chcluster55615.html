Hobson's choice

noun
lack of real choice; only one option available
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑb.sənz ˌtʃɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɒb.sənz ˌtʃɔɪs / (BrE)
a situation in which there is only one option offered, so the real choice is to take it or have nothing

Examples

Hobson's choice situation
Accepting the overnight shift was a Hobson's choice—either take it or lose the job.
She faced a Hobson's choice when she had to agree to the terms or walk away empty-handed.
With only one meal left on the menu, dinner was a Hobson's choice.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dilemma: a situation where a difficult choice has to be made between two or more options
  • synonym – fait accompli: something that has already happened or been decided before those affected hear about it, leaving them with no option but to accept
