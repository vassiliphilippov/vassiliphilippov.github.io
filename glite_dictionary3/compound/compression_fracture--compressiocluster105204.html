compression fracture

noun
bone fracture involving collapse, especially of the vertebrae
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛʃ.ən ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃ.ən ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
a type of bone fracture in which a bone collapses, often occurring in short bones such as the vertebrae

Examples

compression fracture of the spine
After the accident, the X-ray revealed a mild compression fracture in his thoracic vertebra.
Osteoporosis increases the risk of compression fractures in the vertebrae.
The elderly patient suffered a compression fracture in her lower spine after a minor fall.
