vena azygos

noun
vein draining thoracic/abdominal walls
Pronunciation: / ˌvinə ˈæzɪˌɡɑs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ˈæzɪɡɒs / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the thoracic and abdominal walls into the superior vena cava, arising as a continuation of the right ascending lumbar vein

Examples

vena azygos drains blood
A blockage in the vena azygos can cause complications in blood drainage from the thoracic region.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the vena azygos.
The vena azygos plays a crucial role in returning blood from the chest wall to the heart.
