sulphur dioxide

noun
colorless toxic industrial gas, SO₂
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌlfər daɪˈɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə daɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, toxic gas (SO₂) used in industry and present in volcanic gases and air pollution; it can cause acid rain.

Examples

sulphur dioxide pollution
High levels of sulphur dioxide in the air can trigger respiratory problems in sensitive individuals.
Industrial plants must monitor their sulphur dioxide emissions to reduce environmental impact.
When sulphur dioxide is released from volcanoes, it can lead to the formation of acid rain.
