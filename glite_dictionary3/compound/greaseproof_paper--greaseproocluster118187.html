greaseproof paper

noun
oil-resistant paper for cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrisˌpruf ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːsˌpruːf ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
A type of paper that does not absorb oil or grease, used in cooking to line baking trays or wrap food.

Examples

greaseproof paper for baking
Always use greaseproof paper when baking cookies to ensure easy removal.
Line the cake tin with greaseproof paper to prevent the cake from sticking.
She wrapped the sandwiches in greaseproof paper for the picnic.
