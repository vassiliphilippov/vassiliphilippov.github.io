peer-to-peer

adjective
direct interaction between equal participants
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪr tə ˈpɪr / (AmE), / ˌpɪə tə ˈpɪə / (BrE)
involving direct connection or interaction between participants of equal status, especially in computer networks, without a central authority or server

Examples

peer-to-peer network system
A peer-to-peer lending platform connects borrowers and investors directly, bypassing traditional banks.
Many file-sharing programs use peer-to-peer technology to distribute content efficiently.
The company offers a peer-to-peer payment service that lets users send money instantly.
Many file-sharing applications use a peer-to-peer network architecture to distribute data efficiently.
Setting up a peer-to-peer connection can enable users to share files directly without a central authority.
Unlike traditional client-server models, peer-to-peer networks allow all computers to act as both clients and servers.
