any-and-all bid

noun
a takeover bid to buy all available shares
Pronunciation: / ˌɛni ən ˈɔl bɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛni ənd ˈɔːl bɪd / (BrE)
a takeover offer in which the acquirer agrees to buy any and all outstanding shares that shareholders wish to sell, rather than a minimum or set quantity

Examples

any-and-all bid for shares
After receiving the any-and-all bid, many investors decided to sell their shares at the offered price.
An any-and-all bid signals the acquirer's readiness to purchase all available equity in the target company.
The company launched an any-and-all bid to acquire the outstanding shares from existing shareholders.

Related Terms

  • antonym – partial bid: a takeover bid to buy only a specified number of shares
  • synonym – tender offer: a general offer to purchase shares at a specified price
