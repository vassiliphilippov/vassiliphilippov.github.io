sinus rectus

noun
a specific venous sinus in the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪnəs ˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnəs ˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
an unpaired venous sinus of the dura mater in the brain, located along the junction of the falx cerebri and tentorium cerebelli

Examples

the sinus rectus in the brain
In neurosurgery, injury to the sinus rectus must be avoided to prevent complications.
MRI revealed a thrombosis in the patient's sinus rectus.
The sinus rectus plays a crucial role in the venous drainage of the brain.
