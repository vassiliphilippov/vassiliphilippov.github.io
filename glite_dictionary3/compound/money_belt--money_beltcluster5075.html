money belt

noun
belt with hidden compartment for money
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌni bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈmʌni bɛlt / (BrE)
A belt with one or more concealed compartments, worn around the waist for safely carrying money and valuables, especially while traveling.

Examples

wear a money belt
She discreetly reached into her money belt for a few coins to buy a bottle of water.
Tour guides often recommend using a money belt to avoid pickpockets in crowded areas.
While traveling abroad, I wore a money belt under my clothes to keep my passport and cash safe.
