uric acid

noun
a metabolic waste product in blood and urine
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a white, odorless, crystalline substance produced by the breakdown of purines in the body, found in blood and urine

Examples

uric acid level test
Doctors often check uric acid levels to diagnose kidney function.
