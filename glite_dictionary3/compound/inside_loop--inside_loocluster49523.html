inside loop

noun
aerobatic flight maneuver forming a vertical loop
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌsaɪd lup / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪd luːp / (BrE)
an aerobatic maneuver in which an aircraft climbs, flies inverted, then dives to return to horizontal flight, tracing a loop in the vertical plane with the pilot on the inside of the loop

Executing an inside loop requires great skill and precise control of the aircraft.
