crown of thorns

noun
mock crown of thorns put on Jesus
Pronunciation: / kraʊn əv θɔrnz / (AmE), / kraʊn əv θɔːnz / (BrE)
In Christian tradition, a mock crown made of thorn branches that was placed on the head of Jesus before the Crucifixion as a form of torture and humiliation.

Examples

Jesus's crown of thorns
During the reenactment, an actor wore a replica of the crown of thorns to represent the Passion of Christ.
Historians continue to debate the fate of the alleged crown of thorns after it was brought to Paris.
The crown of thorns is a powerful symbol in Christian art and iconography.
