work permit

noun
official permission to work in a country
Pronunciation: / wɝːk ˈpɝː.mɪt / (AmE), / wɜːk ˈpɜː.mɪt / (BrE)
an official document granting a foreign national the legal right to work in a country for a specified period or purpose

Examples

apply for a work permit
After moving to Canada, she applied for a work permit so she could start her new job.
Many international students need a work permit to take up employment during their studies.
Without a valid work permit, he was not allowed to legally work in the country.

Related Terms

  • synonym – visa: an endorsement in a passport allowing entry or stay in a country
