hyperchromic anaemia

noun
anemia with increased hemoglobin in red cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌkroʊmɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkˌrəʊmɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A type of anemia in which the concentration of hemoglobin in red blood cells is abnormally high.

Examples

hyperchromic anaemia diagnosis
Patients with hyperchromic anaemia may require further tests to determine the underlying cause.
The doctor explained that hyperchromic anaemia indicated unusually high levels of hemoglobin in her red blood cells.
Unlike other forms of anemia, hyperchromic anaemia can be identified by its distinctive blood test results.
