alcohol amnestic disorder

noun
dementia from chronic alcoholism with memory loss
Pronunciation: / ˈælkoʊˌhɑl æmˈnɛstɪk dɪsˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈælkəhɒl æmˈnɛstɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A form of dementia seen in the late stages of severe chronic alcoholism, marked by loss of memory for recent events while long-term memory remains intact.

Examples

alcohol amnestic disorder affects memory
People suffering from alcohol amnestic disorder often struggle to recall recent conversations or activities.
The psychiatrist diagnosed him with alcohol amnestic disorder after years of severe drinking.
Treatment for alcohol amnestic disorder may slow progression, but improvement in memory is often limited.
