tibia valga

noun
inward angling of the tibia (shinbone)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪbiə ˈvælɡə / (AmE), / ˈtɪbiə ˈvalɡə / (BrE)
A condition in which the tibia (shinbone) angles inward in relation to the thigh, causing the knees to be closer together than the ankles.

Examples

tibia valga in children
Children with tibia valga often require physical therapy or special braces to help correct their gait.
Excessive tibia valga can lead to knee discomfort and difficulties in running or walking.
The orthopedic specialist diagnosed the patient with tibia valga after observing the alignment of his legs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – genu valgum: knock-knees; condition where knees touch but ankles do not
  • antonym – tibia vara: outward angling of the tibia (bowlegs)
