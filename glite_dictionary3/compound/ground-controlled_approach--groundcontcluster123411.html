ground-controlled approach

noun
aircraft landing guided by ground control
Pronunciation: / ˌɡraʊnd kənˈtroʊld əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɡraʊnd kənˈtrəʊld əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A method of landing an aircraft in poor visibility where a ground controller uses radar to guide the pilot verbally to the runway.

Examples

ground-controlled approach in fog
Because of the fog, the pilot executed a ground-controlled approach to ensure a safe landing.
During bad weather, airports often rely on the ground-controlled approach system to assist incoming flights.
The air traffic controller talked the plane down using a ground-controlled approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym – instrument landing: landing using aircraft instruments for guidance
