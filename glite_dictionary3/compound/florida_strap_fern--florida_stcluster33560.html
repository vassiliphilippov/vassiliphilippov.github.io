Florida strap fern

noun
a tropical fern with strap-shaped leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈflɔrɪdə ˈstræp fɝn / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪdə ˈstræp fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern native to Florida, the West Indies, Mexico, and south to Uruguay, with pale yellow-green, strap-shaped leaves; often growing as an epiphyte or on the ground

Examples

wild Florida strap fern
Botanists observed a rare Florida strap fern clinging to the branches of a cypress tree.
Many gardeners appreciate the Florida strap fern for its graceful, strap-like leaves.
The Florida strap fern thrives in humid, shaded environments in southern Florida.
