tar paper

noun
waterproof building paper impregnated with tar
Pronunciation: / tɑr ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / tɑː ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
a heavy, water-resistant paper impregnated with tar, commonly used in roofing as a waterproof layer

Examples

tar paper under roof tiles
Builders often lay tar paper under roof tiles for added protection.
The old shed was covered in tar paper to keep out the rain.
We had to replace the damaged tar paper before installing new shingles.
