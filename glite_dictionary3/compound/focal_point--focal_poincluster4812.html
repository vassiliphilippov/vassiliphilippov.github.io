focal point

noun
point where light rays converge or diverge
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
The point at which rays of light or other radiation meet after being focused, or from which they appear to diverge.

Examples

focal point of light rays
In a convex mirror, the focal point is located behind the mirror's surface.
The lens brings the rays of light to a focal point on the screen.
The scientist adjusted the apparatus so that the focal point was exactly at the sensor's position.

Related Terms

  • synonym – focus: the point at which rays of light meet after reflection or refraction
