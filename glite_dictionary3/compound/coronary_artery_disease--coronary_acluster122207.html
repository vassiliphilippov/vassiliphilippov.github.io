coronary artery disease

noun
disease of the heart's arteries
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːr.əˌner.i ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri dɪˈziz /; / ˈkɔrəˌnɛri ˈɑrtɚi dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əˌnɛr.i ˈɑː.tə.ri dɪˈziːz /; / ˈkɒrənəri ˈɑːtəri dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a disease in which the arteries that supply blood to the heart become narrowed or blocked, usually because of fatty deposits building up in the artery walls

Examples

coronary artery disease diagnosis
After being diagnosed with coronary artery disease, he changed his diet and began regular exercise.

Treatments for coronary artery disease may include lifestyle changes, medication, or surgery such as angioplasty.
