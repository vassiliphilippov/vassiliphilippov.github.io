free-tailed bat

noun
bat with protruding tail, found in warm climates
Pronunciation: / ˌfri teɪld ˈbæt / (AmE), / ˌfriː teɪld ˈbat / (BrE)
a small, fast-flying insect-eating bat with leathery ears and a tail that projects beyond the tail membrane, found in warm regions

Examples

One free-tailed bat can eat hundreds of insects in a single night.

The free-tailed bat is well known for its ability to cover long distances in search of food.
