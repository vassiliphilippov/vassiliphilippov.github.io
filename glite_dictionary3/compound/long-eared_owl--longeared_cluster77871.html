long-eared owl

noun
an owl with long ear tufts
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈɪrd aʊl / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈɪəd aʊl / (BrE)
a slender owl native to Europe and parts of Asia and North America, distinguished by its long ear tufts and preference for coniferous forests

Examples

a long-eared owl in the forest
During our hike in the forest, we heard the call of a long-eared owl nearby.
The distinctive ear tufts make the long-eared owl easy to identify.
We spotted a long-eared owl perched quietly in the branches at dusk.
