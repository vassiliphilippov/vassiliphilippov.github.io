plum liqueur

noun
alcoholic drink made from plums
Pronunciation: / plʌm lɪˈkɜr / (AmE), / plʌm lɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
A sweet, alcoholic beverage made by infusing or distilling plums, often served as a digestif or used in mixed drinks.

Examples

plum liqueur cocktail
He used plum liqueur to add a fruity note to his cocktail.
Japanese restaurants often serve plum liqueur as a sweet aperitif.
She offered her guests a glass of plum liqueur after dinner.
