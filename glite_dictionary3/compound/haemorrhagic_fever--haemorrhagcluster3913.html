haemorrhagic fever

noun
viral disease causing fever and bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmərˈædʒɪk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɒˈrædʒɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
Any of a group of severe infectious diseases caused by certain viruses, characterized by fever, internal bleeding, and often affecting specific geographic regions.
