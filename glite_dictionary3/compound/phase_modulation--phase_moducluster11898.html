phase modulation

noun
variation of a wave's phase to transmit signals
Pronunciation: / feɪz ˌmɑdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / feɪz ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a method of sending information by varying the phase of a carrier wave, often used in radio transmission and telecommunications
