boron chamber

noun
neutron-detecting ionization chamber
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔr.ɑn ˈtʃeɪm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔː.rɒn ˈtʃeɪm.bə / (BrE)
an ionization chamber lined with boron or filled with boron trifluoride gas, used for detecting and counting low velocity neutrons

Examples

boron chamber detects neutrons
A boron chamber is essential equipment when monitoring radiation levels in nuclear reactors.
Technicians regularly calibrate the boron chamber to ensure accurate neutron counts during experiments.
The laboratory installed a new boron chamber to improve the detection of low-energy neutrons.
