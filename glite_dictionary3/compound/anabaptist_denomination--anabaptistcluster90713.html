Anabaptist denomination

noun
Protestant sect denying infant baptism
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈbæptɪst ˌdɛnəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌanəˈbaptɪst ˌdɛnəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a Protestant Christian sect that denies infant baptism and baptizes only those who personally profess faith

Examples

historic Anabaptist denomination
Many Anabaptist denominations developed unique practices in response to their persecution in Europe.
The Amish and Mennonites are examples of Anabaptist denominations that emphasize adult baptism.
The beliefs of an Anabaptist denomination often center around community living and nonviolence.
