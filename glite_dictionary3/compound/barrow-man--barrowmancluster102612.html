barrow-man

noun
male street vendor using a barrow
Pronunciation: / ˈbæroʊ mæn / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊ mæn / (BrE)
A man who sells fruit and vegetables from a handcart in the street; a hawker with a barrow.

Examples

barrow-man selling apples
In Victorian London, the barrow-man was a familiar sight at local markets.
She bought fresh apples every morning from the friendly barrow-man outside her home.
The barrow-man called out his wares as he pushed his cart down the cobbled street.

Related Terms

  • synonym – costermonger: a person who sells fruit and vegetables from a handcart, especially in Britain
  • synonym – hawker: a person who travels about selling goods, typically advertising them loudly
