holy oil

noun
consecrated oil used in religious ceremonies
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊli ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ɔɪl / (BrE)
Oil that has been consecrated and is used in certain religious ceremonies, especially in Christian rites such as baptism, confirmation, and anointing of the sick.

Examples

holy oil for baptism
During the ritual, the priest anointed her forehead with holy oil.
The holy oil is stored carefully in a special container at the church.
The bishop blessed the holy oil before using it in the confirmation ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym – chrism: a consecrated blend of oil and balm used in Catholic and Orthodox rites
