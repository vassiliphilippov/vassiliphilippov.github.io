ruling by decree

noun
governing without legislature
Pronunciation: / ˈruːlɪŋ baɪ dɪˈkri / (AmE), / ˈruːlɪŋ baɪ dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
the act of governing by making decisions unilaterally, without consulting or gaining approval from a legislative body

Examples

ruling by decree during crisis
Critics say ruling by decree sets a dangerous precedent for the country's future.
The president was accused of ruling by decree and undermining democratic institutions.
Throughout the crisis, the leader continued ruling by decree, bypassing parliament entirely.
