gold standard

noun
monetary system linked to gold
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊld ˈstændərd / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ˈstændəd / (BrE)
A monetary system in which the value of a country's currency is directly linked to a specified amount of gold.

Examples

the gold standard in money
Many countries abandoned the gold standard during the twentieth century.
The debate about returning to the gold standard resurfaces occasionally among economists.
Under the gold standard, each dollar could be exchanged for a fixed amount of gold.
