court plaster

noun
adhesive dressing for minor wounds (historical)
Pronunciation: / kɔrt ˈplæstər / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
an adhesive plaster made of isinglass spread on silk, formerly used to cover small wounds or cuts

Examples

court plaster for small cuts
In Victorian times, people often carried court plaster in small tins to treat minor scrapes.
She applied a piece of court plaster to her finger after pricking it with a needle.
Though rarely used today, court plaster was once a standard remedy for small cuts.
