antihemophilic factor

noun
blood-clotting protein (Factor VIII)
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.hɪˈmoʊ.fɪ.lɪk ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.hiːˈməʊ.fɪ.lɪk ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
a protein present in blood that is essential for normal blood clotting, whose deficiency causes hemophilia A; also known as Factor VIII
