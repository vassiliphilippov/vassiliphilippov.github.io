soft rot

noun
decay of plants caused by bacteria or fungi
Pronunciation: / sɑft rɑt / (AmE), / sɒft rɒt / (BrE)
Mushy or slimy decay of plant tissues, usually caused by bacteria or fungi.

Examples

soft rot in potatoes
Bacterial soft rot is a major problem for vegetable farmers during wet seasons.
Proper ventilation can help prevent soft rot in harvested crops.
The potatoes in storage showed signs of soft rot, making them unfit for sale.

Related Terms

  • antonym – dry rot: decay of wood or plants, usually dry and crumbly, as opposed to soft and mushy
