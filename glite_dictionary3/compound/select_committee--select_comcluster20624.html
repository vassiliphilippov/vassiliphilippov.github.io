select committee

noun
parliamentary committee for a special purpose
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛkt kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkt kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A committee appointed by a parliament or legislative body for a specific purpose, often to investigate or consider a particular issue.

Examples

select committee investigates issue
A select committee was formed to review the impact of new environmental regulations.
Members of the select committee presented their findings to the full parliament.
The government announced that a select committee would investigate the allegations of misconduct.
