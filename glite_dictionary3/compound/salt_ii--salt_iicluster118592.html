SALT II

proper noun
the second Strategic Arms Limitation treaty
Pronunciation: / sɔlt tu / (AmE), / sɔːlt tuː / (BrE)
the second treaty between the United States and the Soviet Union resulting from the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks, aimed at curbing the arms race between the two superpowers

Examples

SALT II treaty signed
Historians often debate the impact of SALT II on subsequent arms control agreements.
Negotiations for SALT II marked a significant step in attempts to limit nuclear weapon stockpiles during the Cold War.
The SALT II agreement was signed in 1979 but was never formally ratified by the United States Senate.
