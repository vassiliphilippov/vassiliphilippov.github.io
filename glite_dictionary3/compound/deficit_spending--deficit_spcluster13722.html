deficit spending

noun
government spending in excess of revenue
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfɪsɪt ˈspɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪsɪt ˈspɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of a government spending more money than it receives in revenue, typically by borrowing to cover the shortfall.

Examples

government deficit spending
Economists often debate whether deficit spending is an effective way to boost economic growth.
The country's deficit spending increased significantly during the financial crisis.
The government's reliance on deficit spending has led to a growing national debt.

Related Terms

  • antonym – balanced budget: a budget in which revenues are equal to expenditures
