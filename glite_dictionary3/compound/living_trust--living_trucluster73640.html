living trust

noun
trust created during a person's lifetime
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvɪŋ trʌst / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ trʌst / (BrE)
a trust set up and effective during the lifetime of the person who creates it, typically to manage their assets and avoid probate

Examples

create a living trust
A living trust allows you to retain control over your assets while you are alive.
Many people set up a living trust to avoid probate and keep their estate matters private.
The attorney explained the benefits of creating a living trust for estate planning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – testamentary trust: a trust created by a will and effective after the grantor's death
