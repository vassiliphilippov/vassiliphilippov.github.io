reproductive cloning

noun
copying an organism to create a living individual
Pronunciation: / ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv ˈkloʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv ˈkləʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of creating a genetically identical copy of an organism, resulting in a new living individual

Examples

reproductive cloning of animals
Most countries have laws restricting reproductive cloning of humans due to moral concerns.
Research into reproductive cloning has raised important ethical questions around the world.
Scientists used reproductive cloning techniques to produce the famous sheep Dolly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – therapeutic cloning: the creation of cloned embryos for medical research or treatment, not for creating living organisms
