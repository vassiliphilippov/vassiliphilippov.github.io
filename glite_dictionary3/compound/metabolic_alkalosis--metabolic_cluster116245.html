metabolic alkalosis

noun
abnormally high body pH due to metabolism
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk ˌælkəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk ˌælkəˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
a condition in which the body's pH becomes abnormally high due to loss of hydrogen ions or excessive uptake of alkaline substances

Examples

metabolic alkalosis after vomiting
Prolonged use of certain diuretics can increase the risk of developing metabolic alkalosis.
The patient was diagnosed with metabolic alkalosis after excessive vomiting led to a loss of stomach acid.
Treatment for metabolic alkalosis often involves correcting the underlying cause and restoring electrolyte balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acidosis: abnormally high acidity in the blood or body tissues
  • degree lower – respiratory alkalosis: alkalosis caused by lack of carbon dioxide due to breathing problems
