ground loop

noun
uncontrollable turn of an aircraft on the ground
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd luːp / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd luːp / (BrE)
an abrupt and uncontrollable turn made by an aircraft while it is moving on the ground, typically during landing or takeoff

Examples

aircraft ground loop
Improper braking can sometimes result in a dangerous ground loop.
Pilots are trained to avoid a ground loop during taxi and takeoff operations.
The small plane suffered a ground loop while landing in strong crosswinds.
