universal gravitational constant

noun
constant in Newton's law of gravitation
Pronunciation: / juːˈnɪvɚsəl ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəsəl ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a physical constant (denoted by G) that appears in Newton's law of gravitation, relating the force of attraction between two masses to their distance apart

Examples

universal gravitational constant in physics
Newton's law uses the universal gravitational constant to relate mass and distance to gravitational force.
Scientists have measured the universal gravitational constant with increasing precision over the years.
The value of the universal gravitational constant is essential in calculating the force between two planets.
