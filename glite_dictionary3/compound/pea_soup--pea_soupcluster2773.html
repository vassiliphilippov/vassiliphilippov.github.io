pea soup

noun
thick yellowish fog
Pronunciation: / ˈpi ˌsuːp / (AmE), / ˈpiː ˌsuːp / (BrE)
(informal) A heavy, thick, yellowish fog that reduces visibility, especially in urban areas.

Examples

dense pea soup fog
In Victorian London, pea soup would often shroud the city for days at a time.
The thick pea soup that rolled in off the Thames made driving nearly impossible.
We woke up to a dense pea soup that turned the city’s streets into a blur.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fog: a thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere near the earth’s surface
  • degree lower – mist: a less dense form of cloud covering close to the ground
