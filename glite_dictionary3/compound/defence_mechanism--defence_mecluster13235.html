defence mechanism

noun
unconscious process protecting from anxiety
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
(in psychology) an unconscious mental process used to protect oneself from anxiety or distress, often by distorting reality or avoiding unpleasant thoughts.

Examples

defence mechanism against stress
Projection is a common defence mechanism in which people attribute their own feelings to others.
Repression, a defence mechanism, prevents distressing thoughts from entering conscious awareness.
Using humor as a defence mechanism can sometimes mask deeper emotional pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym – coping mechanism: a strategy for managing stress or difficult emotions
  • synonym – rationalization: a specific type of defence mechanism involving explaining away undesirable feelings or behaviors
