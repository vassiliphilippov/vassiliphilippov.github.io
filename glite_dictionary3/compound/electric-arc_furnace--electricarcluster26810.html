electric-arc furnace

noun
furnace heated by electric arc
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrɪk ɑrk ˈfɜrnɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪk ɑːk ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
A furnace in which an electric arc is used as the source of heat, especially for making steel.

Examples

electric-arc furnace melts steel
Modern electric-arc furnaces are more energy-efficient than traditional blast furnaces.
Scrap metal is often melted down in an electric-arc furnace before being recast.
The steel plant installed a new electric-arc furnace to improve production efficiency.
