futures contract

noun
agreement to buy/sell at a set future date and price
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːtʃərz ˌkɑːntrækt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃəz ˌkɒntrækt / (BrE)
An agreement to buy or sell a particular quantity of a commodity or financial instrument at a set price on a specified future date, with the option to sell the contract before settlement.

Examples

trade a futures contract
A futures contract can be settled either by physical delivery or cash settlement.
She invested heavily in oil futures contracts to hedge against price fluctuations.
Traders often sell their futures contracts before the settlement date to avoid taking possession of the goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forward contract: an agreement to buy or sell asset at a specified future date for a price agreed today; not standardized or traded on exchanges
