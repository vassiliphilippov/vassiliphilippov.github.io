quarter horse

noun
a small, muscular American horse breed
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrtər hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə hɔːs / (BrE)
a breed of small, muscular horse that was originally developed in the United States for sprinting over short distances, particularly quarter-mile races

Examples

quarter horse race winner
Her quarter horse won first place in the barrel racing competition.
Known for their speed, quarter horses are popular choices among horse trainers throughout the United States.
The ranch specialized in breeding quarter horses for rodeo and ranch work.
