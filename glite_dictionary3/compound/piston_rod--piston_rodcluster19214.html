piston rod

noun
rod connecting a piston to other components
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪstən rɑd / (AmE), / ˈpɪstən rɒd / (BrE)
A rod that connects to and transfers motion from a piston, especially in engines or hydraulic cylinders.

Examples

piston rod in an engine
A bent piston rod can cause severe engine problems if not replaced promptly.
Regular inspection of the piston rod helps prevent machinery breakdown.
The hydraulic pump's piston rod was damaged during operation.
