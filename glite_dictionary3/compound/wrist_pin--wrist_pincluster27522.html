wrist pin

noun
surgical pin for wrist fractures
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪst pɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪst pɪn / (BrE)
A metal pin inserted surgically to hold bones of the wrist (for example the distal radius or carpal bones) in position while they heal.

Examples

surgical wrist pin
After the fall, the surgeon inserted a wrist pin to stabilize the broken distal radius.
The post‑op X‑ray showed the wrist pin was correctly placed and the bones were aligned.
They scheduled a minor procedure to remove the wrist pin once the fracture had healed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – Kirschner wire: a thin metal pin (also called K‑wire) commonly used to fix small bones during orthopaedic surgery
  • synonym – internal fixation: surgical methods and devices used to hold broken bones in the correct position while they heal
