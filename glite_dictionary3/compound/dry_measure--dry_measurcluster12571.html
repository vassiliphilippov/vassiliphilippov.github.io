dry measure

noun
unit of capacity for dry goods
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ ˌmɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌmɛʒə / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for the volume of dry goods such as grain or fruit, typically different from liquid measure

Examples

dry measure of grain
A peck is a commonly used dry measure for fruits such as apples.
Farmers often refer to dry measure units when discussing grain harvests.
The old recipe called for a dry measure of one bushel of wheat.
