sonic barrier

noun
sudden drag increase near the speed of sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑnɪk ˈbɛriər / (AmE), / ˈsɒnɪk ˈbæriə / (BrE)
a sudden rise in aerodynamic drag experienced by an aircraft as it nears the speed of sound

Examples

break the sonic barrier
As the jet approached the sonic barrier, its controls became less responsive due to increased drag.
Breaking the sonic barrier was once believed impossible for aircraft.
Engineers studied the effects of the sonic barrier to design faster planes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sound barrier: the speed at which sound travels in air, considered as an obstacle for aircraft
