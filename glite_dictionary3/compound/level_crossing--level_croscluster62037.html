level crossing

noun
where railway and road cross at same level
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvəl ˌkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl ˌkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
A place where a railway and a road cross at the same level, usually with barriers or gates that close when a train passes.

Examples

at the level crossing barriers
Drivers must always stop when the lights flash at a level crossing.
Several accidents have occurred at this dangerous level crossing over the years.
The barriers were lowered at the level crossing just as we arrived, delaying our journey.
