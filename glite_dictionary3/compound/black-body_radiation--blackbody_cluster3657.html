black-body radiation

noun
radiation emitted by an ideal black body
Pronunciation: / ˌblæk ˈbɑdi reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈbɒdi reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The electromagnetic radiation emitted by an ideal black body, with energy distribution dependent only on temperature as described by Planck's law.

Examples

black-body radiation from a hot object
A perfect absorber and emitter of energy, such as a star, radiates black-body radiation.
Planck's study of black-body radiation led to the development of quantum theory.
The spectrum of black-body radiation shifts with temperature, resulting in different colors for hot objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym – thermal radiation: electromagnetic radiation generated by the thermal motion of particles
