nitro-chalk

noun
fertilizer with calcium carbonate and ammonium nitrate
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtroʊ tʃɔk / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəʊ tʃɔːk / (BrE)
A chemical fertilizer containing calcium carbonate and ammonium nitrate, used primarily to enrich soil with nitrogen.

Examples

apply nitro-chalk fertilizer
Many farmers apply nitro-chalk to boost their crop yields in spring.
The use of nitro-chalk can improve soil structure and provide essential nutrients.
To avoid over-fertilization, it's important to measure the amount of nitro-chalk used each season.
