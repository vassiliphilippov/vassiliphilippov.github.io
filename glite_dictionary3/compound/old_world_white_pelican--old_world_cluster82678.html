Old World white pelican

noun
large white pelican of Europe, Asia, and Africa
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊld wɜrld waɪt ˈpɛlɪkən / (AmE), / ˈəʊld wɜːld waɪt ˈpɛlɪkən / (BrE)
a large white pelican (Pelecanus onocrotalus) found in parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa, similar to the American white pelican

Examples

a flock of Old World white pelicans
A flock of Old World white pelicans glided gracefully over the African wetlands.
The Old World white pelican breeds in shallow lakes and marshes across southern Eurasia and Africa.
Unlike its American relative, the Old World white pelican often fishes in large groups.
