frank breech

noun
fetal position with buttocks first and legs up
Pronunciation: / fræŋk briːtʃ / (AmE), / fræŋk briːtʃ / (BrE)
a position of a fetus during childbirth in which the buttocks, rather than the feet or head, are closest to the birth canal, with the legs straight up in front of the body

Examples

frank breech position at birth
A frank breech presentation can make natural delivery more complicated.
Babies in the frank breech position have their legs straight up, with feet near their heads.
The ultrasound showed the baby in a frank breech position, so the doctors discussed delivery options.
