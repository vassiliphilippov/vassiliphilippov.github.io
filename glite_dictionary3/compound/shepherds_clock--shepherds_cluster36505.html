shepherd's clock

noun
European annual plant with yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛpɚdz klɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpədz klɒk / (BrE)
a weedy European annual plant with yellow flowers, also naturalized in the United States, noted for its use in traditional timekeeping by opening and closing at certain times of day

Examples

Along the edge of the meadow, shepherd's clock blooms created a swath of yellow.
Gardeners sometimes call the hawkweed shepherd's clock because of its timekeeping flowers.
The shepherd's clock opens its flowers early in the morning and closes them by early afternoon.
