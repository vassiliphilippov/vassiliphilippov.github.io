theatrical performance

noun
public staging of a play or drama
Pronunciation: / θiˈætrɪkəl pərˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / θɪˈætrɪkəl pəˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
an event in which actors perform a play or drama before an audience, typically in a theatre

Examples

attend a theatrical performance
Her passion for drama was evident in her dedicated participation in every theatrical performance the club produced.
The school is putting on a theatrical performance of Shakespeare's "Romeo and Juliet" this spring.
We purchased tickets to see a theatrical performance at the town's historic playhouse.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – musical: a theatrical performance that combines songs, spoken dialogue, acting, and dance
  • synonym – play: a dramatic work intended for performance by actors
  • degree lower – reading: a performance where a play is read aloud by actors, usually without staging or costumes
