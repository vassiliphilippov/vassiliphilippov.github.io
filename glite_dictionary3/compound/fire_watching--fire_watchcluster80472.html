fire watching

noun
watching for fires (esp. WWII Britain)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ ˌwɑtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌwɒtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of keeping watch for fires, especially those started by incendiary bombs, typically during World War II in Britain.

Examples

fire watching for bombs
During the Blitz, many civilians participated in fire watching to protect their neighborhoods from incendiary bombs.
My grandmother often shares stories about her nights spent fire watching during the war.
The duties of fire watching included reporting and extinguishing small fires on rooftops.
