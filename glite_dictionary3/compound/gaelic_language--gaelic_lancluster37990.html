Gaelic language

noun
Celtic language of Ireland and Scotland
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪlɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪlɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of several closely related Celtic languages spoken in Ireland and Scotland, primarily Irish Gaelic and Scottish Gaelic.

Examples

the Gaelic language spoken in Ireland
Many traditional folk songs are sung in the Gaelic language.
She is fluent in the Gaelic language of Ireland and often visits her relatives there.
The preservation of the Gaelic language is important for Scottish cultural identity.
