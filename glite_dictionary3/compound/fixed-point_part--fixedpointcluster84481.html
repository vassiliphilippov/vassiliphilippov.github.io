fixed-point part

noun
fractional part of a logarithm (mantissa)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkst pɔɪnt pɑrt / (AmE), / ˈfɪkst pɔɪnt pɑːt / (BrE)
the positive fractional part of the representation of a logarithm; also called the mantissa. For example, in the expression 'log 643 = 2.808', the fixed-point part is .808.

Examples

fixed-point part of a logarithm
In logarithmic tables, only the fixed-point part is usually listed.
Scientists use the fixed-point part to simplify calculations with logarithms.
The fixed-point part of log 1000 is exactly zero.
