eccrine gland

noun
type of sweat gland in human skin
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkrɪn ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈiːkrɪn ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
a small sweat gland found in human skin that secretes a watery fluid, important for regulating body temperature

Examples

eccrine gland produces sweat
Doctors studied how the eccrine gland responds to extreme temperatures.
The eccrine gland helps cool the body by producing sweat during exercise.
Unlike the apocrine glands, the eccrine glands are distributed all over the skin.

Related Terms

  • antonym – apocrine gland: a type of sweat gland that produces a thicker secretion, mainly in the armpits and groin
