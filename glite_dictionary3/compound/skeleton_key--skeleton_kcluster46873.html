skeleton key

noun
universal backdoor or credential
Pronunciation: / c8sk5bl59t59n ki / (AmE), / c8sk5bl59t59n kid0 / (BrE)
(in computing, security) a password, secret, backdoor, or credential that can bypass authentication and grant access to multiple accounts, systems, or protected resources.

Examples

skeleton key bypasses security
Security researchers warned the malware carried a skeleton key capable of bypassing most authentication.
Administrators discovered a forgotten master password acting as a skeleton key across several enterprise services.
The update removed a hard-coded skeleton key that allowed remote attackers to access user accounts.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backdoor: a hidden or undocumented way of bypassing normal authentication to access a system
  • synonym – master key: a single key or credential that opens or controls many locks or accounts
