computed tomography

noun
medical imaging using computer-processed X-rays
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpjuːtɪd təˈmɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtɪd təˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique in which computer-processed X-ray measurements are used to create cross-sectional images of internal body structures

Examples

computed tomography scan
Advances in computed tomography have greatly improved the detection of tumors.
Modern hospitals often rely on computed tomography for diagnosing internal injuries.
The doctor recommended a computed tomography scan to get clearer images of the abdomen.
