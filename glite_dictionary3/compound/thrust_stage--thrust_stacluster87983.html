thrust stage

noun
stage extending into audience, three sides seated
Pronunciation: / θrʌst steɪdʒ / (AmE), / θrʌst steɪdʒ / (BrE)
A stage that extends into the audience area, with seats on three sides, used in some theaters.

Examples

thrust stage theater design
Actors must adjust their performance style when working on a thrust stage due to the audience being on three sides.
Many Shakespearean plays are performed on a thrust stage to encourage greater interaction with viewers.
The new theater features a thrust stage to create a more immersive experience for the audience.
