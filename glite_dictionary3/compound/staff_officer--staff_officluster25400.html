staff officer

noun
commissioned officer on a commander's staff
Pronunciation: / stæf ˈɑfɪsər / (AmE), / stɑːf ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a commissioned officer who serves as a member of a military commander's staff, usually assisting with planning, administration, or specialized duties

Examples

staff officer in headquarters
After several years as a platoon leader, she was promoted to staff officer in the headquarters.
Every division relies on the expertise of its staff officers to manage complex operations.
The staff officer presented his report to the commanding general during the morning meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – line officer: a commissioned officer who commands combat units rather than serving on a staff
