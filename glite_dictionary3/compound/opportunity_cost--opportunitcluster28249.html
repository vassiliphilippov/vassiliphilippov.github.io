opportunity cost

noun
cost of forgoing alternatives
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpərˈtuːnəti kɔst / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈtjuːnəti kɒst / (BrE)
the loss of potential gain from other alternatives when one alternative is chosen

Examples

opportunity cost of a decision
Choosing to spend money on a vacation rather than saving for retirement involves an opportunity cost.
The opportunity cost of attending college includes the income you could have earned by working instead.
When deciding to invest in new equipment, the manager considered the opportunity cost of not expanding marketing efforts instead.
