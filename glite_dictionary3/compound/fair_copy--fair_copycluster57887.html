fair copy

noun
final clean copy of a corrected draft
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛr ˌkɑpi / (AmE), / ˈfeə ˌkɒpi / (BrE)
a clean and final version of a document that has been copied from a draft and incorporates all corrections

Examples

make a fair copy
After revising the manuscript, she prepared a fair copy to submit to the publisher.
Please ensure the fair copy is free from any spelling errors before handing it in.
The scribe wrote out a fair copy of the letter on fresh parchment.
