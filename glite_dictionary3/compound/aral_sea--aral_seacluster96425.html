Aral Sea

proper noun
large, shrinking lake in Central Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrəl si / (AmE), / ˈɑːrəl siː / (BrE)
a large lake in Central Asia, situated between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan, formerly one of the four largest lakes in the world but now greatly reduced in size due to water diversion.

Examples

the Aral Sea disaster
Once thriving communities around the Aral Sea have struggled as water levels dropped dramatically.
Restoration projects aim to bring some life back to the barren areas left by the shrinking Aral Sea.
Scientists have studied the rapid decline of the Aral Sea for decades.
