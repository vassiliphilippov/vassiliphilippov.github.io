buffer country

noun
neutral state separating rival powers
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌf.ɚ ˈkʌn.tri / (AmE), / ˈbʌf.ə ˈkʌn.tri / (BrE)
a small, usually neutral country situated between two larger rival or hostile powers, serving to prevent conflict between them

Examples

neutral buffer country
During the Cold War, Mongolia was considered a buffer country between China and the Soviet Union.
Switzerland sometimes acted as a buffer country between European powers during times of conflict.
The existence of a buffer country can reduce direct tensions between neighbors.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buffer state: a country lying between two rival or potentially hostile nations, serving to prevent conflict
