Link trainer

noun
early mechanical flight simulator
Pronunciation: / lɪŋk ˈtreɪnər / (AmE), / lɪŋk ˈtreɪnə / (BrE)
an early mechanical device used for training pilots by simulating flight controls and aircraft movement

Examples

a Link trainer session
During World War II, thousands of pilots trained using the Link trainer before flying real aircraft.
Learning to operate a Link trainer was often a pilot's first step toward getting their wings.
The museum had a restored Link trainer on display, highlighting its importance in aviation history.
