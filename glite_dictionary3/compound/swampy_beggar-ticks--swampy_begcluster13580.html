swampy beggar-ticks

noun
North American wetland plant; Bidens connata
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑːmpi ˈbɛɡər tɪks / (AmE), / ˈswɒmpi ˈbɛɡə tɪks / (BrE)
A North American annual plant of wet pastures and meadows, with yellow flowers and bristly seeds that stick to fur or clothing (Bidens connata).

Examples

swampy beggar-ticks seeds stick
Many bird species feed on the seeds produced by swampy beggar-ticks in the late summer.
The swampy beggar-ticks are thriving in the wet meadows after the heavy rains this spring.
Watch out for swampy beggar-ticks; their seeds will cling to your socks if you walk through the marsh.
