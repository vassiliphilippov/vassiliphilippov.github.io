Gulf of Oman

proper noun
a sea inlet linking Arabian Sea to Persian Gulf
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌlf əv oʊˈmɑːn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlf əv əʊˈmɑːn / (BrE)
an arm of the Arabian Sea that connects it with the Persian Gulf, located between Iran on the north and Oman and the United Arab Emirates on the south and west.

Examples

the Gulf of Oman coast
Fishing is an important industry for communities along the Gulf of Oman.
Several countries share a coastline with the Gulf of Oman, making it a region of strategic importance.
The Gulf of Oman plays a vital role in the global oil trade and shipping routes.
