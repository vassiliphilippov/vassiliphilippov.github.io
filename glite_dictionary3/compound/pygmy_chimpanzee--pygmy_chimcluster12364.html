pygmy chimpanzee

noun
small species of chimpanzee; bonobo
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪɡmi tʃɪmˈpænzi / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmi tʃɪmˈpanzi / (BrE)
A small species of great ape (Pan paniscus) native to swamp forests of central Africa, closely related to the common chimpanzee and also known as the bonobo.

Examples

pygmy chimpanzee group
Researchers have studied the social behavior of the pygmy chimpanzee for clues about human evolution.
The pygmy chimpanzee is an endangered species found primarily in the forests south of the Congo River.
Unlike their larger relatives, pygmy chimpanzees are known for their peaceful and cooperative nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bonobo: another name for the pygmy chimpanzee (Pan paniscus)
  • degree higher – chimpanzee: a larger species of great ape (Pan troglodytes) closely related to the pygmy chimpanzee
