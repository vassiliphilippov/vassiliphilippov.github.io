mean solar day

noun
average length of a day based on Earth's rotation
Pronunciation: / ˌmiːn ˈsoʊlər deɪ / (AmE), / ˌmiːn ˈsəʊlə deɪ / (BrE)
the average time taken for the Earth to complete one rotation on its axis, measured relative to the Sun; 24 hours.

Examples

mean solar day calculation
A calendar day is based on the mean solar day, not the actual time between sunsets.
Astronomers use the mean solar day as a standard for measuring time.
The length of a mean solar day is almost exactly 24 hours.

  • antonym – sidereal day: time for Earth to complete one rotation relative to the stars
