star topology

noun
network with all nodes connected to a central hub
Pronunciation: / stɑr təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / stɑː təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A network configuration in which all components are connected to a central hub or switch.

Examples

star topology network design
A star topology is easier to troubleshoot than a ring or bus design.
If the hub fails in a star topology, none of the devices can communicate.
Many modern office networks use a star topology for better performance and reliability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bus topology: network in which components are connected to a single central cable
  • antonym – ring topology: network where each component is connected to two others to form a ring
