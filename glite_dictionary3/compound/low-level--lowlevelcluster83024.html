low-level

adjective
occurring at a low altitude
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
(of flight or occurrence) taking place at a relatively low altitude, especially close to the ground.

Examples

low-level flight
Helicopters often perform low-level rescues in mountainous regions.
The mission required a low-level approach to avoid radar detection.
The plane made a low-level pass over the runway during the air show.
