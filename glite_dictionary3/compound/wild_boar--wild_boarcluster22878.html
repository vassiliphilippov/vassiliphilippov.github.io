wild boar

noun
a large wild pig with tusks
Pronunciation: / waɪld bɔr / (AmE), / waɪld bɔː / (BrE)
A large, wild pig with a narrow body and prominent tusks, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, from which most domestic pigs are descended.

Examples

hunt wild boar
Archaeologists found ancient remains of wild boar near the settlement.
In some European countries, wild boar are hunted for their meat.
The wild boar foraged for roots in the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – domestic pig: domesticated form descended from the wild boar
