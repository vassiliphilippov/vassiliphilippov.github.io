feed grain

noun
grain grown for animal feed
Pronunciation: / fid ɡreɪn / (AmE), / fiːd ɡreɪn / (BrE)
grain, such as corn or barley, grown primarily to be used as animal feed rather than for human consumption.

Examples

feed grain for cattle
Corn is commonly used as a feed grain for dairy cows.
Farmers planted more feed grain this year to supply the growing livestock industry.
The price of feed grain directly affects the cost of raising cattle and pigs.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forage: food for animals, especially cattle and horses; related in purpose but not limited to grain
