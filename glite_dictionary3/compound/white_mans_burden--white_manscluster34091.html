white man's burden

noun
colonialist concept of duty to non-whites
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt mænz ˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt mænz ˈbɜːdən / (BrE)
the historical idea that white people have a moral obligation to govern and civilize non-white peoples, especially during the colonial era

Examples

white man's burden in colonialism
Critics argue that the white man's burden was rooted in racist assumptions about non-European peoples.
Rudyard Kipling's poem popularized the phrase white man's burden and its underlying ideology.
The idea of the white man's burden was used to justify imperialist policies in the nineteenth century.
