black elder

noun
European shrub with black berries
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A common European and Asian shrub or small tree (Sambucus nigra) with clusters of black berries, whose fruit is used for making wines and jellies.

Examples

black elder berries
A hedgerow of black elder provided shade and food for birds.
The black elder in the garden was full of ripe, dark berries.
Traditionally, people have used black elder berries to make cordials and medicinal syrups.
