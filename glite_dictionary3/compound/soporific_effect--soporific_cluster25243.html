soporific effect

noun
effect that causes drowsiness
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːpəˈrɪfɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌsɒpəˈrɪfɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
An effect that causes sleepiness or induces drowsiness.

Examples

soporific effect of medicine
After a large meal, the wine's soporific effect made everyone feel ready for a nap.
Some allergy medications are known for their soporific effect, so you should not drive after taking them.
The gentle music had a soporific effect on the audience, and several people nodded off.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sedative effect: an effect that calms or reduces excitement
  • antonym – stimulating effect: an effect that increases alertness or activity
