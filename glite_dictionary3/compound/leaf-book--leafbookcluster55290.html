leaf-book

noun
an unbound ancient manuscript
Pronunciation: / ˈliːf ˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌbʊk / (BrE)
An unbound manuscript of an ancient classic, consisting of loose leaves rather than being rolled into a scroll.

Examples

ancient leaf-book
Scholars value the leaf-book for its insight into early manuscript production.
The museum exhibited a rare leaf-book from the 3rd century.
Unlike a scroll, a leaf-book is comprised of individual leaves that were not bound together.
