chromatic vision

noun
ability to see colors
Pronunciation: / krəˈmætɪk ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / krəˈmatɪk ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the ability to perceive and distinguish colors in vision, as opposed to seeing in shades of gray only.

Examples

normal chromatic vision
Deficiencies in chromatic vision can make it difficult to differentiate between similar hues.
Most people have chromatic vision, which allows them to distinguish a wide range of colors.
Some animals lack chromatic vision and see the world mainly in shades of gray.

Related Terms

  • synonym – color vision: the faculty of perceiving colors
  • antonym – monochromacy: the condition of seeing only shades of one color or gray
