chronic glaucoma

noun
slowly progressive type of glaucoma
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːnɪk ɡloʊˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnɪk ɡləʊˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A form of glaucoma characterized by a slow, progressive loss of peripheral vision, typically caused by blockage of the canal of Schlemm.

Examples

chronic glaucoma affects vision
Early detection and treatment of chronic glaucoma are essential to prevent vision loss.
Patients with chronic glaucoma often do not experience noticeable symptoms until significant damage has occurred.
Regular eye exams are important because chronic glaucoma can progress without warning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acute glaucoma: a form of glaucoma characterized by sudden onset and rapid loss of vision
