drogue chute

noun
parachute for slowing a fast-moving object
Pronunciation: / droʊɡ tʃut / (AmE), / drəʊɡ tʃuːt / (BrE)
a parachute used to slow down an object that is moving quickly, often deployed to assist in the safe landing or deceleration of aircraft, spacecraft, or vehicles.

Examples

deploy drogue chute after landing
After touchdown, the jet deployed its drogue chute to decelerate on the short runway.
High-speed race cars sometimes use a drogue chute to help reduce speed after crossing the finish line.
The spacecraft released a drogue chute to slow its descent before the main parachute deployed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – parachute: a device used to slow the motion of a person or object through the atmosphere by creating drag
