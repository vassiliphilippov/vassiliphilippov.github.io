closed-circuit television

noun
private television monitoring system
Pronunciation: / kloʊzd ˈsɝːkət ˈtɛləˌvɪʒən / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈsɜːkɪt ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən / (BrE)
A television system in which signals are sent by cables to a limited number of monitors, not intended for public broadcast, often used for surveillance.

Examples

closed-circuit television cameras
Many schools have installed closed-circuit television to enhance security on their campuses.
The bank uses closed-circuit television to monitor activity inside the building at all hours.
The theater manager watched the audience from his office through the closed-circuit television system.
