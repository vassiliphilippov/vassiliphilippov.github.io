black humour

noun
humour treating taboo or morbid topics satirically
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
A kind of humour that treats serious, disturbing, or taboo subjects—such as death or suffering—in a satirical or farcical way, often producing a disturbing or ironic effect.

Examples

black humour about death
He appreciates black humour because it challenges our feelings about sensitive topics.
Her novel is full of black humour, blending laughter with discomfort.
The film's use of black humour made the tragic scenes oddly amusing.

Related Terms

  • synonym – dark humor: humour that makes light of serious or taboo topics
  • synonym – gallows humour: grim or ironic humour relating to death or misfortune
