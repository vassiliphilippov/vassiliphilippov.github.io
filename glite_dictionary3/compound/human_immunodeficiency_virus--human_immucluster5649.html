human immunodeficiency virus

noun
the virus that causes AIDS
Pronunciation: / ˌhjuːmən ɪˌmjuːnəʊdɪˈfɪʃənsi ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmən ɪˌmjuːnəʊdɪˈfɪʃənsi ˈvaɪrəs / (BrE)
a virus that attacks the immune system and causes acquired immune deficiency syndrome (AIDS) by destroying helper T cells
