tramp steamer

noun
steamship for hire, no fixed route
Pronunciation: / træmp ˈstiːmɚ / (AmE), / træmp ˈstiːmə / (BrE)
a commercial steamship that is available for hire and does not operate on a fixed schedule or route

Examples

old tramp steamer
He spent years at sea aboard a tramp steamer, traveling to ports around the world.
The old tramp steamer set out from the port, headed wherever cargo was available.
Unlike regular liners, a tramp steamer took jobs wherever and whenever it could find them.

Related Terms

  • antonym – liner: a passenger or cargo ship with a regular schedule and route
