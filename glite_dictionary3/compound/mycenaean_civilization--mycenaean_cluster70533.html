Mycenaean civilization

noun
ancient Greek Bronze Age culture (c. 1400–1100 BC)
Pronunciation: / maɪˌsiːniːən ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˌsiːniːən ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the ancient Greek civilization of the Late Bronze Age centered on Mycenae, flourishing from about 1400 to 1100 BC, known for its palatial states, art, and writing system.

Examples

ancient Mycenaean civilization
Linear B script was used by the Mycenaean civilization to keep administrative records.
Many myths and legends of Greece are rooted in the Mycenaean civilization.
Scholars have studied the architecture of the Mycenaean civilization to understand its social structure.
