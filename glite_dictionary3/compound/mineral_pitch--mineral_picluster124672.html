mineral pitch

noun
bituminous substance from natural beds or petroleum
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnərəl pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl pɪtʃ / (BrE)
A dark, sticky, bituminous substance found in natural deposits or as a residue from petroleum distillation, mainly composed of hydrocarbons.

Examples

sticky mineral pitch
After petroleum distillation, a heavy residue known as mineral pitch remained at the bottom of the tanks.
Some natural springs in mountainous regions ooze mineral pitch, which locals sometimes collect and use.
The ancient builders used mineral pitch as a waterproofing agent for their structures.

  • synonym – bitumen: a viscous black mixture of hydrocarbons found in tar sands and used for road surfacing
