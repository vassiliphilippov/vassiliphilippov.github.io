clear-air turbulence

noun
dangerous turbulence in cloudless skies
Pronunciation: / ˌklɪr ˈɛr ˈtɝːbjələns / (AmE), / ˌklɪə ˈeə ˈtɜːbjʊləns / (BrE)
Sudden and severe air movement in cloudless regions, causing potentially dangerous turbulence for aircraft.

Examples

clear-air turbulence shakes plane
Many passengers were injured when unexpected clear-air turbulence shook the plane.
Pilots are trained to respond quickly to clear-air turbulence since it is difficult to detect on radar.
The flight encountered severe clear-air turbulence as it crossed the jet stream.
