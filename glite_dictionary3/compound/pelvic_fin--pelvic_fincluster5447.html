pelvic fin

noun
paired fins on the underside of fish
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛlvɪk fɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛlvɪk fɪn / (BrE)
either of a pair of fins attached to the pelvic girdle in fishes that help control the direction of movement; they correspond to the hind limbs of land vertebrates.

Examples

pelvic fin of a fish
Biologists examined the pelvic fin structure to compare evolutionary traits among different fish.
In some species, the pelvic fins are located far forward on the body.
The pelvic fin helps fish stabilize themselves while swimming.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pectoral fin: either of a pair of fins located on each side of a fish just behind the head, corresponding to the forelimbs of land vertebrates
