flying jib

noun
outermost jib sail on a vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ dʒɪb / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ dʒɪb / (BrE)
the outermost of two or more jibs on a sailing vessel, set forward of the other jibs on the bowsprit

Examples

flying jib on bowsprit
During the storm, the captain ordered the flying jib to be taken down for safety.
The crew set the flying jib to catch the strong wind ahead of the bow.
With the flying jib unfurled, the ship gained extra speed across the open sea.
