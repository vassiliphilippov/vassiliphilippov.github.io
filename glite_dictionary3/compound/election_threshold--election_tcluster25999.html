election threshold

noun
minimum vote share for parliamentary representation
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛkʃən ˈθrɛʃhoʊld / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkʃən ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
the minimum percentage of votes a political party must receive in an election to gain any seats or representation in a legislature

Examples

election threshold for parliament
After the election threshold was lowered, more minority groups gained representation in the assembly.
Several small parties failed to reach the election threshold and were excluded from parliament.
The country's high election threshold favors larger parties and makes it difficult for new ones to enter.
