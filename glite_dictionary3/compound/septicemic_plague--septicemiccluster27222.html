septicemic plague

noun
form of plague infecting the bloodstream
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛptɪˈsiːmɪk pleɪɡ / (AmE), / ˌsɛptɪˈsiːmɪk pleɪɡ / (BrE)
An especially dangerous and generally fatal form of plague in which the infection invades the bloodstream, not transmitted from person to person.

Examples

septicemic plague infection spreads
Doctors identified the infection as septicemic plague after the patient's blood tested positive for Yersinia pestis.
The fatality rate for septicemic plague is extremely high without prompt antibiotic treatment.
Unlike other types, septicemic plague cannot be transmitted from one person to another.
