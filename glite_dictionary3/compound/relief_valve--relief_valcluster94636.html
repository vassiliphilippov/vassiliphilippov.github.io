relief valve

noun
valve releasing excess pressure
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlif vælv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːf vælv / (BrE)
A valve that automatically opens to release pressure from a container when it reaches a potentially dangerous level.

Examples

relief valve releases pressure
During the inspection, the engineer checked the relief valve to ensure it was functioning properly.
The relief valve activated, releasing steam when the pressure exceeded safe limits.
Without a working relief valve, the pressure in the boiler could reach dangerous levels.
