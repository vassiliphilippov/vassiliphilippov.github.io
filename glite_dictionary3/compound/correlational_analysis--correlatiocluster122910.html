correlational analysis

noun
statistical evaluation of variable relationships
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃənl əˈnælɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃənl əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the process of using statistical methods to evaluate the strength and direction of relationships between two or more variables

Examples

correlational analysis of data
Before drawing conclusions, the team performed a correlational analysis on the experimental data.
In her research, she conducted a correlational analysis to explore the link between diet and mood.
The results of the correlational analysis indicated a strong relationship between stress levels and productivity.

  • synonym – correlation: a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things
