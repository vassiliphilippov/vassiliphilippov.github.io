filet mignon

noun
tender beef steak cut
Pronunciation: / fiˌleɪ mɪnˈjɑn / (AmE), / fiˌleɪ mɪnˈjɒ̃ / (BrE)
A small, tender steak cut from the thick end of a beef tenderloin, considered a delicacy in fine dining.

Examples

juicy filet mignon steak
At special occasions, we serve filet mignon because of its tenderness and rich taste.
She ordered the filet mignon with a side of roasted vegetables for dinner.
The chef recommends grilling the filet mignon to medium rare for the best flavor.
