cantilever bridge

noun
bridge with projecting beams meeting at center
Pronunciation: / ˈkæntɪˌlivɚ brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæntɪˌliːvə brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A bridge constructed of projecting beams (cantilevers) that extend from piers and meet in the middle, supporting the roadway.

Examples

cantilever bridge spans river
Engineers chose a cantilever bridge design to span the wide river without central supports.
Heavy traffic crosses the cantilever bridge every day, making it an essential part of the local infrastructure.
The Forth Bridge in Scotland is a famous cantilever bridge and a marvel of engineering.

Related Terms

  • antonym – suspension bridge: bridge where the deck is hung below suspension cables
  • synonym – truss bridge: bridge whose load-bearing superstructure is composed of a truss
