teleost fish

noun
a bony fish of the subclass Teleostei
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛliɑst ˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɒst ˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a bony fish belonging to the large subclass Teleostei, characterized by a movable jaw and symmetrical tail, comprising the vast majority of modern fish species

Examples

common teleost fish species
As a teleost fish, the salmon possesses a highly developed internal skeleton.
Scientists have identified more than 25,000 species of teleost fish worldwide.
The goldfish, a popular pet, is actually a type of teleost fish.
