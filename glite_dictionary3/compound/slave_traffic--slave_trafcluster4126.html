slave traffic

noun
trade in slaves, especially to the Americas
Pronunciation: / sleɪv ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / sleɪv ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the buying, selling, and transportation of slaves, especially the historical trade in Black Africans transported to the Americas between the 16th and 19th centuries

Examples

Atlantic slave traffic
Abolitionists fought tirelessly to end slave traffic across the Atlantic.

The horrors of the slave traffic left a lasting mark on countless generations.
