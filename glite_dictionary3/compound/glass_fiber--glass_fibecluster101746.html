glass fiber

noun
thin strand of glass used in transmission
Pronunciation: / ɡlæs ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A very thin strand of glass, used as a waveguide for light, especially in optical transmission and materials.

Examples

glass fiber cable
Light travels efficiently through each glass fiber in a fiber-optic cable.
Modern medical instruments often use glass fiber bundles to carry images from inside the body.
The cables are made of glass fiber to transmit data at high speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fiber optic: a technology that uses glass fibers for transmitting data as light
