false witness

noun
person giving untrue testimony
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who deliberately gives untrue testimony, especially in a court of law

Examples

a false witness in court
Giving false witness is a serious crime in many legal systems.
He was accused of being a false witness and faced charges of perjury.
The jury was misled by a false witness during the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – perjurer: a person who commits perjury by lying under oath
  • antonym – witness: a person who sees an event and can give a first-hand account
