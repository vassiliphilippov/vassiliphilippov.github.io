rocket propulsion

noun
propulsion using stored oxygen for combustion
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːkət prəˈpoʊlʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt prəˈpɒlʃən / (BrE)
a method of propelling vehicles by expelling exhaust at high speed, using stored oxygen for combustion, typically used where atmospheric oxygen is insufficient

Examples

rocket propulsion system
Spacecraft rely on rocket propulsion to leave Earth's atmosphere and travel in space.
The development of rocket propulsion revolutionized satellite launches and space exploration.
Unlike jet engines, rocket propulsion allows vehicles to operate in the vacuum of space.
