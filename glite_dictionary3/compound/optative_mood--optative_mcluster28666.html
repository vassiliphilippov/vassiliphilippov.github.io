optative mood

noun
grammatical mood expressing wishes or hopes
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptətɪv muːd / (AmE), / ˈɒptətɪv muːd / (BrE)
a grammatical mood used to express wishes, hopes, or desires, found in some languages such as Greek and Sanskrit; in English, equivalent meanings are usually conveyed by modal verbs

Examples

optative mood in Greek
In English, we often use 'may' or 'might' instead of having a true optative mood.
Knowledge of the optative mood is essential for understanding classical Sanskrit texts.
The ancient Greek language makes frequent use of the optative mood to express wishes.
