rye flour

noun
flour made from rye grain
Pronunciation: / raɪ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / raɪ ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
flour made by grinding rye grain, often used in baking breads and other baked goods

Examples

rye flour bread
German pumpernickel bread is typically made with coarsely ground rye flour.
In Scandinavia, traditional crispbreads often use a mixture of rye flour and whole grains.
Some people prefer rye flour over wheat flour for its distinct flavor and dense texture.
