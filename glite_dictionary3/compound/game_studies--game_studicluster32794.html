game studies

noun
academic study of games and gaming
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪm ˈstʌdiz / (AmE), / ɡeɪm ˈstʌdiz / (BrE)
The academic field concerned with the study of games and gaming, especially video games, including their design, culture, and societal impact.

Examples

game studies research papers
Recent conferences in game studies have focused on the role of storytelling in video games.
She is completing her doctorate in game studies at a well-known university.
The rise of e-sports has brought new topics into game studies research.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ludology: the study of games, especially structures and elements of games
  • synonym – media studies: the study of media, especially mass media and its effects
