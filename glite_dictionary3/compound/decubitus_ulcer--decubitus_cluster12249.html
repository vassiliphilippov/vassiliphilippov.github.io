decubitus ulcer

noun
sore from prolonged pressure on skin
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkjuːbɪtəs ˈʌlsər / (AmE), / dɪˈkjuːbɪtəs ˈʌlsə / (BrE)
A sore or open wound on the skin caused by prolonged pressure, especially in patients confined to bed for extended periods.

Examples

decubitus ulcer on back
Elderly patients with limited mobility are at greater risk of developing a decubitus ulcer.
Prevention of decubitus ulcers is a key aspect of long-term patient care.
The nurse carefully checked the patient's back for any signs of a decubitus ulcer.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bedsore: an ulcer formed on the skin of a person who is bedridden
  • synonym – pressure sore: an ulcerated area of skin caused by prolonged pressure
