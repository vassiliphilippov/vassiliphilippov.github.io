semaphore telegraph

noun
visual signaling device with movable arms
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛməˌfɔr ˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪˌfɔː ˈtɛlɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a tower equipped with movable shutters or arms used to convey information visually over a long distance, especially before the advent of electric telegraphy

Examples

semaphore telegraph tower
A semaphore telegraph station was located on the hilltop for maximum visibility.
Before the electric telegraph, networks of semaphore telegraphs stretched across Europe.
The army relied on the semaphore telegraph to send urgent messages across the countryside.
