radish plant

noun
plant producing edible pungent root
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪʃ plænt / (AmE), / ˈrædɪʃ plɑːnt / (BrE)
A cruciferous plant of the genus Raphanus, cultivated for its pungent, edible root.

Examples

radish plant in garden
Gardeners appreciate the radish plant for both its crunchy roots and attractive flowers.
If left unharvested, the radish plant will eventually produce small white or purple flowers.
The radish plant grows quickly and is often ready to harvest in just a few weeks.
