rocket engine

noun
engine using stored propellant for thrust
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːkɪt ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt ˌɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an engine that produces thrust by expelling its own stored propellant at high speed, used typically in spacecraft and missiles

Examples

rocket engine powers spacecraft
A rocket engine differs from a jet engine because it carries its own oxidizer.
Engineers designed a powerful new rocket engine for the upcoming lunar mission.
The rocket engine was tested extensively before being installed in the spacecraft.

Related Terms

  • antonym – internal combustion engine: an engine that generates power by burning fuel within a confined space
  • synonym – jet engine: an engine that generates thrust by expelling a high-speed jet of gas
