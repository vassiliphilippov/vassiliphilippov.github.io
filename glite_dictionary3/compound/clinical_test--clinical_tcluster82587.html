clinical test

noun
controlled trial of drugs or devices on humans
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪnɪkəl tɛst / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪkəl tɛst / (BrE)
a rigorously controlled trial in which a new drug or medical device is tested on human subjects to assess its safety and effectiveness before approval for general clinical use

Examples

clinical test on patients
Before a vaccine is approved, it must pass several phases of clinical tests.
Patients volunteered to participate in a clinical test of the experimental device.
The new drug underwent a clinical test to evaluate its effectiveness against the disease.
