coordinate axis

noun
reference line in a coordinate system
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɔːrdɪnət ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪnət ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
one of the fixed reference lines used to define the position of points in a coordinate system, typically the x-axis or y-axis in two dimensions, and also the z-axis in three dimensions.

Examples

coordinate axis on a graph
Every location on the map can be described by its position relative to each coordinate axis.
In three-dimensional space, there is an extra coordinate axis known as the z-axis.
The point where the coordinate axis meets the graph is called the origin.

Related Terms

  • synonym – axis: a straight line about which a body or geometric object rotates or may be symmetrically arranged
  • synonym – coordinate plane: a two-dimensional surface defined by a horizontal and a vertical axis (x and y axes)
