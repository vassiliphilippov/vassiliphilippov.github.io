chlorophyll b

noun
a green pigment in plants aiding photosynthesis
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːrəˌfɪl ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˈklɒrəˌfɪl ˈbiː / (BrE)
a green pigment found in plants, especially higher plants, that aids in photosynthesis by capturing light energy

Examples

chlorophyll b in leaves
Leaves contain both chlorophyll a and chlorophyll b, which work together to absorb light for photosynthesis.
Scientists can measure chlorophyll b to assess plant health and light absorption capacity.
The presence of chlorophyll b gives certain plants a darker shade of green.
