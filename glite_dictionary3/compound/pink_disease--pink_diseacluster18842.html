pink disease

noun
fungal disease of crop trees
Pronunciation: / pɪŋk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / pɪŋk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A serious fungal disease affecting the bark of many tropical crop trees, such as coffee, citrus, and rubber, characterized by a covering of pink fungal hyphae on the branches.

Examples

pink disease damages trees
Agricultural experts recommend regular inspections to prevent the spread of pink disease among rubber plantations.

The orange grove was devastated by pink disease, leaving many trees with discolored, dying branches.
