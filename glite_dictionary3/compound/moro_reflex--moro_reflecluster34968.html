Moro reflex

noun
infant startle reflex
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːroʊ rɪˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrəʊ rɪˌflɛks / (BrE)
a normal reflex in young infants in which a sudden loud noise or movement causes the child to stretch out the arms and flex the legs

Examples

Moro reflex in babies
A lack of the Moro reflex in newborns can indicate neurological problems.
The doctor tested the baby's Moro reflex during the check-up.
The nurse explained to the parents that the Moro reflex usually disappears after a few months.
