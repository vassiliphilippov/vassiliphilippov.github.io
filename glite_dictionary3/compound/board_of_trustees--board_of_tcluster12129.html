board of trustees

noun
governing body of an institution
Pronunciation: / bɔrd əv trʌˈstiːz / (AmE), / bɔːd əv trʌsˈtiːz / (BrE)
A group of people elected or appointed to oversee and direct the policies of an educational institution or organization.

Examples

board of trustees meeting
After much debate, the board of trustees decided to increase funding for student scholarships.
Every year, the school's board of trustees reviews the institution's financial statement and strategic plan.
The board of trustees voted unanimously to approve the university's new research center.
