admiralty law

noun
law governing maritime affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈædmɪrəlti lɔ / (AmE), / ˈædmɪrəlti lɔː / (BrE)
The branch of law dealing with maritime affairs, including shipping, navigation, waters, and ocean fisheries.

Examples

admiralty law case
He specializes in admiralty law, representing clients involved in shipping disputes.
Many coastal nations have their own regulations, but admiralty law provides an important legal framework for international waters.
