hidden reserve

noun
undisclosed reserves in accounting
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪdən rɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / ˈhɪdən rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
An amount of money or assets not disclosed on a company's balance sheet, often due to deliberately understating values.

Examples

hidden reserve in accounts
Auditors are trained to detect any hidden reserve that could distort the financial statements.
Disclosure of the hidden reserve led to an increase in the firm's reported profits.
The company maintained a substantial hidden reserve by undervaluing its real estate assets.
