von Willebrand's disease

noun
inherited bleeding disorder
Pronunciation: / vɑn ˈwɪlɪˌbrændz dɪˈziz / (AmE), / vɒn ˈwɪlɪˌbrændz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A genetic disorder characterized by a deficiency of a coagulation factor, leading to mucosal bleeding and easy bruising; inherited as an autosomal disorder.

Examples

von Willebrand's disease causes bleeding
Children with von Willebrand's disease may require special care during dental procedures to prevent excessive bleeding.
Even minor injuries can cause significant bleeding in people affected by von Willebrand's disease.
The doctor diagnosed her with von Willebrand's disease after she experienced frequent nosebleeds and easy bruising.
