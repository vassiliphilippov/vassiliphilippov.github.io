debt limit

noun
maximum legal borrowing by a government
Pronunciation: / dɛt ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / dɛt ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
the maximum amount of money that a government is allowed to borrow by law or regulation

Examples

raise the debt limit
Congress is currently debating whether to raise the debt limit to avoid a government shutdown.
The concept of a debt limit is intended to control government borrowing.
When the nation's debt limit was reached, the treasury could no longer issue new bonds.
