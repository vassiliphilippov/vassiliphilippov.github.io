hooded pitcher plant

noun
yellow-flowered pitcher plant with an arched hood
Pronunciation: / ˈhʊdɪd ˈpɪtʃər plænt / (AmE), / ˈhʊdɪd ˈpɪtʃə plɑːnt / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered pitcher plant native to the southeastern United States, distinguished by trumpet-shaped leaves with an orifice covered by an arched hood

Examples

hooded pitcher plant in wetlands
Many nature enthusiasts travel to wetlands in the Southeast to observe the rare hooded pitcher plant.
The hooded pitcher plant attracts insects with its bright yellow flowers and traps them in its tubular leaves.
The distinctive hood on the hooded pitcher plant helps prevent rainwater from diluting its digestive enzymes.
