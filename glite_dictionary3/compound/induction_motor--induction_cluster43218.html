induction motor

noun
AC motor using electromagnetic induction
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkʃən ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃən ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
An electric motor powered by alternating current, in which the current in the rotor is produced by electromagnetic induction from the stator's magnetic field.

Examples

induction motor runs machines
A three-phase induction motor is commonly used in industrial applications due to its robustness.
The factory installed a new induction motor to improve energy efficiency.
When the induction motor starts, it draws a high inrush current from the supply.

Related Terms

  • antonym – synchronous motor: AC motor where the rotor turns at the same speed as the stator field
