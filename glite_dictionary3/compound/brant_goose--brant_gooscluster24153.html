brant goose

noun
a species of small dark goose
Pronunciation: / brænt ɡus / (AmE), / brɑːnt ɡuːs / (BrE)
a small, dark-colored species of goose (Branta bernicla) that breeds in northern regions and migrates south for the winter.

Examples

a brant goose flock
During migration, brant geese travel thousands of miles between their breeding and wintering grounds.
Flocks of brant geese can be seen along the Atlantic coast in winter.
The brant goose is known for its distinctive dark plumage and white neck patch.
