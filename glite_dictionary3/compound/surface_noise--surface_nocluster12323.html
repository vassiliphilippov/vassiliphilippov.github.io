surface noise

noun
background noise on vinyl records
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːfɪs nɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs nɔɪz / (BrE)
the background noise produced by the friction of a record player's stylus moving over a vinyl record

Examples

reduce surface noise on records
Cleaning your vinyl records can help reduce surface noise during playback.
Some collectors feel that a little surface noise adds character to vintage recordings.
The old album had noticeable surface noise, but the music was still enjoyable.
