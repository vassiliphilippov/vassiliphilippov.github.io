chiasma opticum

noun
optic nerve crossing in the brain
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈæzmə ɑpˈtɪkəm / (AmE), / kaɪˈazmə ɒpˈtɪkəm / (BrE)
the crossing point of the optic nerves from the two eyes at the base of the brain

Examples

chiasma opticum damage
Damage to the chiasma opticum can lead to visual field defects in both eyes.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided injuring the chiasma opticum.
The chiasma opticum is crucial for transmitting visual information to both hemispheres of the brain.
