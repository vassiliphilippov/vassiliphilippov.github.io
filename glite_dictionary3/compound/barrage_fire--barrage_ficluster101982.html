barrage fire

noun
heavy, area-saturating artillery fire
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɑːʒ ˌfaɪr / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A heavy, continuous artillery fire directed over a wide area to saturate it, rather than aimed at a specific target.

Examples

Before the assault, a barrage fire was used to weaken enemy defenses.
