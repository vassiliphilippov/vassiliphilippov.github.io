gravity wave

noun
wave propagating gravity through space
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrævəti weɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪti weɪv / (BrE)
A wave in which fluctuations in gravitational fields propagate through space, traveling at the speed of light, as predicted by general relativity.

Examples

detect gravity wave
Einstein predicted the existence of gravity waves a century before they were directly observed.
Scientists detected a gravity wave passing through Earth using highly sensitive equipment.
The collision of two black holes creates powerful gravity waves that ripple across the universe.

  • synonym – gravitational wave: a ripple in spacetime caused by accelerating masses
