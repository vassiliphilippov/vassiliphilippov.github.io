foster-sister

noun
female raised as your foster sibling
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑstər ˌsɪstər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˌsɪstə / (BrE)
a girl or woman who is not a biological daughter of your parents but who is raised as part of your family, typically because she is cared for in your family as a foster child

Examples

my foster-sister at home
After her parents adopted Maria, she became my foster-sister and we grew up together.
Although we are not related by blood, I consider my foster-sister a true part of my family.
Every summer, my foster-sister and I would visit our grandparents in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • synonym – foster-brother: a boy or man who is not a biological son of your parents but who is raised as part of your family
  • antonym – sister: a female who has the same parents as another person
  • antonym – stepsister: the daughter of your stepparent by a former relationship
