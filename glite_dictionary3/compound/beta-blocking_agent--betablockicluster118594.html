beta-blocking agent

noun
drug blocking beta-adrenergic receptors
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.t̬ə ˈblɑː.kɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə ˈblɒk.ɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
Any of a group of drugs used to treat conditions such as high blood pressure or arrhythmia by blocking beta-adrenergic receptors, thereby reducing the force and rate of heart contractions.

Examples

take a beta-blocking agent pill
Athletes are sometimes tested for beta-blocking agents because they can affect physical performance.
Some people experience fatigue as a side effect of taking a beta-blocking agent.
The doctor prescribed a beta-blocking agent to control her blood pressure.
