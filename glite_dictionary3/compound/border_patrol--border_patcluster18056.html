border patrol

noun
group monitoring national borders
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdər pəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə pəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a government agency or group of officers responsible for monitoring and securing a country's borders to prevent illegal entry or activities

Examples

border patrol agents
During her trip, she saw vehicles marked with border patrol along the highway near the frontier.
Members of the border patrol undergo rigorous training to prepare for their duties.
The border patrol apprehended several people attempting to cross the border illegally last night.
