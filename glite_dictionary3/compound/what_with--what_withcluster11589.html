what with

conjunction
used to introduce reasons for something
Pronunciation: / wʌt wɪθ / (AmE), / wɒt wɪð / (BrE)
Used to introduce one or more reasons that together explain or cause something.

Examples

what with exams coming
What with exams coming up and my job, I hardly have any free time these days.
What with her new project and family commitments, she has been quite busy lately.
What with the rain and heavy traffic, we arrived much later than expected.

Related Terms

  • synonym – because of: due to; on account of
