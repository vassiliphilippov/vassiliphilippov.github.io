Slavic people

noun
ethnic group speaking Slavic languages
Pronunciation: / ˈslævɪk ˈpiːpəl / (AmE), / ˈslævɪk ˈpiːpl̩ / (BrE)
A group of people belonging to the various ethnic groups who speak a Slavic (Slavonic) language, mainly indigenous to Eastern Europe, the Balkans, and parts of Northern Asia.

Examples

Slavic people in Eastern Europe
Many Slavic people celebrate religious holidays with unique regional customs.

The Slavic people have a rich tradition of folk music and storytelling.
