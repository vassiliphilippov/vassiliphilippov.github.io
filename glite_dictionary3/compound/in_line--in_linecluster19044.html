in line

adjective
next in succession or entitlement
Pronunciation: / ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
having a place in a sequence of succession or entitlement, especially for a position or reward

Examples

in line for promotion
After the manager retires, Sarah is in line for the promotion.
He was in line to become chairman until the board selected someone else.
Several candidates are in line to inherit the estate, but he is the favorite.

Related Terms

  • synonym – next in line: following directly after someone else in a sequence of succession
