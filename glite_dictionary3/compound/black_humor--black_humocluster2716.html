black humor

noun
morbid or darkly satirical humor
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
a form of humor that treats serious, disturbing, or morbid subjects in a light or satirical way

Examples

black humor about death
Comedians sometimes rely on black humor to address topics that are otherwise too painful to discuss.
Her novel is full of black humor, making the reader laugh at life’s darkest moments.
The movie’s use of black humor helped audiences cope with its tragic themes.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gallows humor: grim or ironic humor in desperate situations
  • antonym – slapstick: broad, physical comedy; not dark or morbid
