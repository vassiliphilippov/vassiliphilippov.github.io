acute angle

noun
angle less than 90 degrees
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ˈæŋɡl̩ / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
an angle that measures less than 90 degrees but more than 0 degrees

Examples

acute angle in a triangle
If the triangle has all acute angles, it is called an acute triangle.
The clock hands form an acute angle at five o'clock.
You can identify an acute angle by checking if it is less than 90 degrees.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – obtuse angle: an angle greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees
  • degree higher – right angle: an angle equal to 90 degrees
