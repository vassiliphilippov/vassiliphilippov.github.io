spiral galaxy

noun
galaxy with spiral-shaped arms
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪrəl ˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəl ˈɡæləksi / (BrE)
A galaxy characterized by a central nucleus of older stars surrounded by curved arms containing younger stars that spiral outward.

Examples

distant spiral galaxy
Astronomers used the telescope to discover a new spiral galaxy millions of light-years away.
The Milky Way is classified as a spiral galaxy, with its distinctive arms winding out from the center.
The structure of a spiral galaxy includes both a bright central bulge and swirling arms.
