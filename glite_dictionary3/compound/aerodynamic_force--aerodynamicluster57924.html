aerodynamic force

noun
force exerted by air on a moving object
Pronunciation: / ˌeroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk fɔrs / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk fɔːs / (BrE)
The force exerted by air (or another gas) on a body moving through it, such as lift or drag on an airfoil.

Examples

aerodynamic force on a car
Designers reduced aerodynamic force by streamlining the shape of the car.
The aerodynamic force generated by the wings allows the airplane to remain airborne.
The balance between aerodynamic force and gravity is crucial in helicopter flight.

Related Terms

  • synonym – drag: the aerodynamic force opposing motion through air
  • synonym – lift: the upward aerodynamic force that opposes gravity
