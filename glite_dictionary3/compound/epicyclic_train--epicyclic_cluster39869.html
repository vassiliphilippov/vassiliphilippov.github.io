epicyclic train

noun
system of revolving gears
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈsaɪklɪk treɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsɪklɪk treɪn / (BrE)
a system of gears in which at least one gear axis revolves about another fixed axis, commonly used in mechanical engineering for complex gear arrangements.

Examples

epicyclic train of gears
A classic bicycle hub sometimes contains an epicyclic train for multiple speed options.
By using an epicyclic train, engineers reduced the size of the gearbox without sacrificing performance.
The epicyclic train in this transmission allows the vehicle to shift smoothly between gears.
