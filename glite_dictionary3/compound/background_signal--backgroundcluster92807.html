background signal

noun
extraneous signal that may interfere with measurement
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌɡraʊnd ˈsɪɡnəl / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd ˈsɪɡnəl / (BrE)
an extraneous signal that can interfere with or be confused with the phenomenon being measured or observed

Examples

background signal in data
Careful calibration is needed to distinguish the target data from any background signal.
In radio astronomy, a strong background signal can make it difficult to detect weak cosmic emissions.
The scientist had to filter out the background signal before analyzing the experiment's true results.
