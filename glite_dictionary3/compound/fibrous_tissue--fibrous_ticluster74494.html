fibrous tissue

noun
tissue with fibrous structure
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɹəs ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbɹəs ˈtɪsjuː / (BrE)
Tissue in animals or plants consisting of or containing fibers, especially connective tissue that supports, binds, or separates other tissues or organs.

Examples

fibrous tissue forms scars
Damaged muscles are sometimes replaced by fibrous tissue during the healing process.
In plants, fibrous tissue provides structural support to stems and leaves.
