stock purchase plan

noun
company plan letting employees buy stock
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈpɜrtʃəs plæn / (AmE), / stɒk ˈpɜːtʃəs plan / (BrE)
an arrangement, often offered by companies, that allows employees to buy shares of the company's stock, sometimes at a discounted rate

Examples

stock purchase plan for employees
He decided to join the stock purchase plan to invest in his employer's future.
Many companies offer a stock purchase plan as part of their employee benefits package.
Through the stock purchase plan, she was able to acquire company shares at a reduced price.
