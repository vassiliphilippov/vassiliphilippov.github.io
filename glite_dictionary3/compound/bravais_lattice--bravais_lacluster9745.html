Bravais lattice

noun
geometric arrangement in crystals
Pronunciation: / brəˈveɪ ˈlætɪs / (AmE), / brəˈveɪ ˈlætɪs / (BrE)
A three-dimensional geometric arrangement of points representing the periodic structure of atoms, molecules, or ions in a crystal.

Examples

Bravais lattice in crystals
Crystallographers use the concept of a Bravais lattice to classify different crystal systems.
Each Bravais lattice describes the symmetrical repetition of particles in three-dimensional space.
The structure of common table salt is based on a cubic Bravais lattice.
