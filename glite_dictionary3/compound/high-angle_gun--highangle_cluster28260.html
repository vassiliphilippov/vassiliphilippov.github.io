high-angle gun

noun
artillery fired at steep angles
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ ˈæŋɡəl ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ ˈæŋɡl ɡʌn / (BrE)
a type of artillery piece designed to fire at high elevations for relatively short ranges

Examples

high-angle gun fires upward
A high-angle gun can deliver shells over obstacles that would block flatter trajectories.
During the battle, their unit relied heavily on a single high-angle gun for close support fire.
The soldiers positioned the high-angle gun to target enemy trenches on the hillside.
