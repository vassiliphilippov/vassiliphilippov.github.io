genital phase

noun
final psychosexual stage (psychoanalysis)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl feɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtəl feɪz / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the final stage of psychosexual development, typically during adolescence, when mature sexual interests emerge.

Examples

Freud's genital phase stage
According to Freud, the genital phase marks the onset of mature adult sexuality.
During the genital phase, individuals seek to establish balanced and loving relationships.
Psychologists believe unresolved issues in earlier stages can affect the genital phase of development.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – latency period: the stage preceding the genital phase, marked by a decrease in sexual urges
  • degree lower – phallic stage: the third stage of psychosexual development, before the latency and genital phases
