uterine vein

noun
vein draining blood from the uterus
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːtəˌraɪn veɪn / (AmE), / ˈjuːtəriːn veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins on each side of the uterus that drain blood from the uterine plexus into the internal iliac vein

Examples

uterine vein thrombosis
A thrombosis in the uterine vein can lead to serious complications after childbirth.
During pregnancy, the uterine vein increases in size to accommodate greater blood flow.
The surgeon carefully isolated the uterine vein before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – uterine artery: artery supplying blood to the uterus
  • synonym – vein: blood vessel carrying blood back to the heart
