rib cage

noun
bony chest enclosure of ribs
Pronunciation: / rɪb keɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɪb keɪdʒ / (BrE)
the set of curved bones (ribs) that form the protective enclosure around the chest, enclosing the heart and lungs

Examples

broken rib cage
He suffered a bruise to his rib cage after falling off his bike.
The rib cage protects vital organs such as the heart and lungs.
The doctor listened to her lungs through her rib cage.
