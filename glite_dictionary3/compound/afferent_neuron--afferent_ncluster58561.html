afferent neuron

noun
a neuron that carries signals to the brain or spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.ɚ.ənt ˈnʊr.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt ˈnjʊə.rɒn / (BrE)
a nerve cell that carries sensory impulses toward the central nervous system (brain or spinal cord) from the periphery of the body

Examples

afferent neuron carries signals
An afferent neuron transmits sensory signals from the skin to the spinal cord.
Damage to an afferent neuron can cause loss of sensation in a limb.
The reflex arc involves both an afferent neuron and an efferent neuron.

Related Terms

  • antonym – efferent neuron: a neuron that carries impulses away from the central nervous system
