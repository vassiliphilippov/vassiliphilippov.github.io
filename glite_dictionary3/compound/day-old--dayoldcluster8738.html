day-old

adjective
made or prepared the previous day
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ ˌoʊld / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ˌəʊld / (BrE)
(of food, especially bread or baked goods) made or prepared the previous day and therefore not fresh today.

Examples

day-old bread
Many restaurants donate their day-old pastries to local charities.
She used day-old muffins to make a delicious bread pudding.
The bakery sells day-old bread at a reduced price every morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fresh: recently made or produced, not stale
