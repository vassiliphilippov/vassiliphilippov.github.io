blood blister

noun
blister filled with blood, from injury
Pronunciation: / blʌd ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / blʌd ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
A blister filled with blood or blood-stained fluid, usually caused by pinching, friction, or injury to the skin.

Examples

painful blood blister
If you pop a blood blister, you increase the risk of infection.
She developed a blood blister on her finger after accidentally pinching it in the door.
The athlete showed the trainer a painful blood blister on his heel from his new shoes.
