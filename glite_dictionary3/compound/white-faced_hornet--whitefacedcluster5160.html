white-faced hornet

noun
North American black-and-white hornet
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt feɪst ˈhɔrnɪt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt feɪst ˈhɔːnɪt / (BrE)
A large black-and-white hornet native to North America, known for its distinctive pale face and aggressive behavior when threatened.

Examples

a white-faced hornet nest
A white-faced hornet nest was discovered in the old oak tree behind the house.
Gardeners should take care when approaching a white-faced hornet nest during the summer months.
The painful sting of a white-faced hornet can cause severe reactions in some people.
