freedom of religion

noun
the right to practice any or no religion
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdəm əv rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm əv rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
the right to practice any religion or to live without following any religion, without interference from the government

Examples

freedom of religion protected by law
She moved to a nation that respected freedom of religion so she could worship without fear.
Some countries place restrictions on freedom of religion, affecting minority groups.
The constitution guarantees freedom of religion to all citizens.
