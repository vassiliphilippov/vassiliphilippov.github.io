no man's land

noun
ambiguous area between two states or categories
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊ ˌmænz ˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ ˌmænz ˌlænd / (BrE)
an area, situation, or state that is ambiguous or undefined, lying between two clearly defined positions or conditions

Examples

a no man's land of uncertainty
After the merger, some employees found themselves in a no man's land between departments, unsure of their roles.
Digital privacy is a no man's land as regulations struggle to keep up with technology.
The debate falls into a no man's land where law and ethics do not clearly align.

Related Terms

  • synonym – limbo: an indefinite state or condition; an intermediate place
