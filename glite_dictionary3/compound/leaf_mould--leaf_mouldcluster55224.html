leaf mould

noun
soil from decayed leaves
Pronunciation: / lif moʊld / (AmE), / liːf məʊld / (BrE)
a soil-like substance formed from decaying leaves, used to improve soil structure and fertility

Examples

leaf mould for soil
Roses thrive when a layer of leaf mould is mixed into the planting area.
The gardener spread leaf mould over the flower beds to enrich the soil.
You can make your own leaf mould by piling fallen leaves in a corner of the garden.
