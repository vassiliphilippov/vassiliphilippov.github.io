coal tar

noun
dark liquid from coal distillation
Pronunciation: / koʊl tɑr / (AmE), / kəʊl tɑː / (BrE)
a thick, dark, viscous liquid obtained by the destructive distillation of bituminous coal, used in making dyes, medicines, and as a preservative

Examples

coal tar smell
After the fire, the smell of coal tar hung heavily in the air around the railway yard.
Medicinal shampoos containing coal tar are used to treat certain scalp conditions.
