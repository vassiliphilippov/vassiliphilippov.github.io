line of succession

noun
the order of succession to a position
Pronunciation: / laɪn əv səˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / laɪn əv səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the order in which people have the right to succeed to a specific official position, especially to a throne or leadership role

Examples

line of succession to the throne
A dispute arose over who was truly next in the line of succession.
After the king's death, the next in the line of succession became the new monarch.
The line of succession to the British throne is carefully regulated by law.
