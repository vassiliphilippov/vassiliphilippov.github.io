immigrant class

noun
grouping of recent low-status immigrants
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmɪɡrənt klæs / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪɡrənt klɑːs / (BrE)
A group of recent immigrants who, despite varied cultural backgrounds, are categorized together due to their low socioeconomic status.

Examples

urban immigrant class
Many policies failed to address the needs of the immigrant class living in urban neighborhoods.
Sociologists have studied the integration of the immigrant class into mainstream society.
The immigrant class often faces language barriers and limited job opportunities.
