photographic emulsion

noun
light-sensitive coating for photographic film or paper
Pronunciation: / fəˌtəɡræfɪk ɪˈmʌlʃən / (AmE), / fəˌtəɡrɑːfɪk ɪˈmʌlʃən / (BrE)
a light-sensitive coating applied to photographic paper or film, consisting of fine grains of silver halide (usually silver bromide) suspended in gelatin, used to capture images when exposed to light

Examples

photographic emulsion on film
Older cameras required manual loading of film with photographic emulsion applied to it.
She developed her own film by preparing the photographic emulsion in the darkroom.
The quality of the print depends largely on the type of photographic emulsion used.
