universal agent

noun
person authorized to transact all business for another
Pronunciation: / juˈnɪvɚsəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəsəl ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person authorized to conduct all types of business on behalf of another (the principal)

Examples

universal agent manages business
As a universal agent, he could sign documents, make deals, and act on behalf of his client in any matter.
In rare cases, a universal agent is appointed to manage every aspect of a principal's affairs.
The contract named her as the universal agent for all transactions related to the estate.

Related Terms

  • degree lower – general agent: agent authorized for a range of business within a particular field
  • degree lower – special agent: agent authorized for a specific act or transaction only
