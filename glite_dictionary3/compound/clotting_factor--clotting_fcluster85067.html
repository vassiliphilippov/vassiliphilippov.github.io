clotting factor

noun
blood substance essential for coagulation
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑtɪŋ ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈklɒtɪŋ ˈfæktə / (BrE)
any of several substances in the blood that are necessary for the process of blood clotting (coagulation)

Examples

missing clotting factor
Genetic mutations can lead to deficiencies in a clotting factor, significantly increasing the risk of bleeding disorders.
People with hemophilia are missing a specific clotting factor in their blood, which makes it difficult to stop bleeding.
The doctor ordered a test to determine the patient's clotting factor levels after noticing abnormal bruising.

Related Terms

  • antonym – anticoagulant: a substance that prevents or reduces coagulation of blood
  • synonym – coagulation factor: another term for clotting factor, especially in medical literature
