jumping seed

noun
seed that jumps due to moth larva inside
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ siːd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ siːd / (BrE)
the seed of certain Mexican shrubs, especially of the genus Sebastiana, which contains the larva of a moth; the larva's movements cause the seed to jump or tumble

Examples

jumping seed moves suddenly
A jumping seed contains a moth larva that causes it to twitch and leap.
She bought a souvenir bag of jumping seeds from a market in Mexico.
The children watched the jumping seed move erratically across the table.
