preliminary examination

noun
graduate-level qualifying exam
Pronunciation: / prɪˈlɪmɪˌnɛri ɪɡˌzæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈlɪmɪnəri ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an examination taken by graduate students to assess their readiness to continue with advanced study or research.

Examples

preliminary examination for PhD students
All PhD candidates must pass a preliminary examination before advancing to dissertation work.
Failure on the preliminary examination may result in dismissal from the graduate program.
She spent months preparing for her preliminary examination in the chemistry department.
