right gastric vein

noun
vein of the stomach draining into portal vein
Pronunciation: / raɪt ˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / raɪt ˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that receives blood from the upper surfaces of the stomach and drains into the portal vein

Examples

right gastric vein blockage
An obstruction in the right gastric vein can contribute to portal hypertension.
During surgery, care must be taken to identify the right gastric vein to avoid excessive bleeding.
The right gastric vein drains the lesser curvature of the stomach and connects to the portal vein.
