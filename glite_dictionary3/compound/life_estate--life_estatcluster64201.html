life estate

noun
property right lasting for a lifetime
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪf ɪˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ɪˌsteɪt / (BrE)
An interest in property which lasts only for the duration of the life of the person holding it or another specified person.

Examples

life estate in property
A life estate allows someone to use property for their lifetime without owning it outright.
The terms of the will granted him a life estate, after which the property would pass to his children.
Upon her husband's death, Mary received a life estate in the family home.
