Little Dipper

noun
a star pattern in Ursa Minor
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬l ˈdɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl̩ ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a group of seven stars in the constellation Ursa Minor, forming the shape of a dipper, with Polaris at the end of its handle

Examples

the Little Dipper stars
Children often learn to recognize the Little Dipper when they study constellations.
On a clear night, you can easily spot the Little Dipper in the northern sky.
Polaris, the North Star, is located at the tip of the Little Dipper's handle.
