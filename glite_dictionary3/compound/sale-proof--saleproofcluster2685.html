sale-proof

adjective
resistant to being discounted or sold cheaply
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪl ˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈseɪl ˌpruːf / (BrE)
(Of a product or item) designed or regarded as unlikely to be sold at a discount, often because of high quality or unique features.

Examples

sale-proof luxury item
By adding exclusive features, the brand made its backpack nearly sale-proof for an entire year.
The company claims their flagship phone is sale-proof because of its enduring popularity.
Those designer sneakers stayed sale-proof even during storewide clearance events.
