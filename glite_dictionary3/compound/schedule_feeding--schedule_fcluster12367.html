schedule feeding

noun
feeding at regular, set intervals
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛdʒuːl ˈfiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛdjuːl ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of feeding a baby or animal at regular, fixed intervals according to a set schedule

Examples

schedule feeding for babies
Some parents prefer schedule feeding to better manage their baby's sleep patterns.
Veterinarians often recommend schedule feeding for dogs to avoid overeating.
With schedule feeding, the kittens quickly adapted to regular mealtimes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demand feeding: feeding whenever the baby or animal appears hungry, rather than on a fixed schedule
