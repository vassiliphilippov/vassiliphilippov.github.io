obtuse triangle

noun
triangle with an angle over 90°
Pronunciation: / əbˈtus ˈtraɪˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs ˈtraɪæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a triangle with one interior angle greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees

Examples

an obtuse triangle diagram
In an obtuse triangle, one of the angles is always greater than 90 degrees.
She measured the angles and realized the figure was an obtuse triangle.
Unlike a right or acute triangle, an obtuse triangle has a single obtuse angle.

Related Terms

  • antonym – acute triangle: a triangle with all angles less than 90 degrees
  • antonym – right triangle: a triangle with one angle equal to 90 degrees
