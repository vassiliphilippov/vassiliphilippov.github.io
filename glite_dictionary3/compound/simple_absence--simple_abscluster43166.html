simple absence

noun
brief, uncomplicated seizure type
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəl æbˈsɛns / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl abˈsɒns / (BrE)
A type of absence seizure marked by brief loss of awareness and rapid 3-per-second brainwave spikes, without additional complications.

Examples

simple absence seizure
A classic EEG finding in simple absence is the presence of 3-per-second spike-and-wave discharges.
Children with epilepsy may have multiple simple absences throughout the day without even realizing it.
The patient experienced a simple absence during the neurological examination, lasting just a few seconds.
