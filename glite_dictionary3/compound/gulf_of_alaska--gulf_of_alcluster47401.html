Gulf of Alaska

proper noun
large gulf of the Pacific near southern Alaska
Pronunciation: / ɡʌlf əv əˈlæskə / (AmE), / ɡʌlf əv əˈlæskə / (BrE)
a large gulf of the Pacific Ocean located between the Alaska Peninsula and the Alexander Archipelago, forming part of the southern coast of Alaska

Examples

the Gulf of Alaska coast
Many storms in the North Pacific originate over the Gulf of Alaska.
Researchers study marine life in the Gulf of Alaska to monitor the effects of climate change.
The coastline along the Gulf of Alaska is known for its rugged beauty and glaciers.
