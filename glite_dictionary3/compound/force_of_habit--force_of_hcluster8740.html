force of habit

noun
action done automatically; habitual tendency
Pronunciation: / fɔːrs əv ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / fɔːs əv ˈhabɪt / (BrE)
the tendency to do something automatically because it has been done many times before

Examples

out of force of habit she acts
Even after switching jobs, she wakes up early by force of habit.
He poured himself a second cup of coffee by force of habit, not because he really wanted one.
She locks the door every time she leaves the house, simply out of force of habit.

Related Terms

  • synonym – habit: a regular practice or tendency, especially one that is hard to give up
