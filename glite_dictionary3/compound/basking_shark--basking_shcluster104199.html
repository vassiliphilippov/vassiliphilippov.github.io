basking shark

noun
large plankton-eating surface-dwelling shark
Pronunciation: / ˈbæskɪŋ ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪŋ ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, harmless shark of temperate oceans, often seen feeding near the surface on plankton

Examples

basking shark feeding
During the summer, basking sharks are frequently seen close to the shores of the British Isles.
The basking shark is often mistaken for a dangerous predator, but it poses no threat to humans.
