helper cell

noun
immune cell that stimulates other immune cells
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛlpɚ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpə sɛl / (BrE)
A type of T lymphocyte (T cell) that expresses the CD4 receptor, recognizes antigens on infected cells, and secretes substances to stimulate B cells and killer T cells; helper T cells are vital for immune response and are targeted by HIV/AIDS.

Examples

helper cell count
Doctors monitor the number of helper cells in patients with AIDS to assess immune function.
HIV attacks and destroys helper cells, weakening the body's immune response.
The immune system relies on helper cells to coordinate its defense against infections.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cytotoxic T cell: a T cell that destroys infected or cancerous cells directly
  • synonym – helper T cell: a subtype of helper cell; the most precise term for CD4+ T cells
