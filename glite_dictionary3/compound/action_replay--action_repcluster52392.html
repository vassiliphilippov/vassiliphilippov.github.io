action replay

noun
instant replay of an action (esp. in sports)
Pronunciation: / ˈækʃən rɪˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈækʃ(ə)n rɪˌpleɪ / (BrE)
an immediate rebroadcast of a recent action, especially in sports, usually shown shortly after it happens to allow viewers to see it again

Examples

action replay of a goal
Thanks to action replays, fans could analyze every detail of the winning shot.
The controversial goal was shown again on the action replay to help viewers understand what happened.
We waited for the action replay to see if the referee's decision was correct.
