superiority complex

noun
attitude of exaggerated self-importance
Pronunciation: / suːˌpɪriˈɔrəti kəmˈplɛks / (AmE), / suːˌpɪəriˈɒrəti kəmˈplɛks / (BrE)
an attitude or feeling of superiority over others, often characterized by an exaggerated sense of one's own worth or abilities

Examples

develop a superiority complex
He developed a superiority complex after winning several awards at a young age.
Her superiority complex made it difficult for her to accept criticism from colleagues.
Some people hide their insecurities behind a superiority complex and arrogance.

Related Terms

  • antonym – inferiority complex: a persistent sense of inadequacy or inferiority
