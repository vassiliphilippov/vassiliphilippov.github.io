column chromatography

noun
separation technique using an adsorbent column
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːləm kɹoʊˌmætəˈɡɹæfi / (AmE), / ˈkɒləm krəʊˌmætəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
A method of chromatography in which mixtures are separated based on selective adsorption as they pass through a column packed with a solid adsorbent.

column chromatography experiment
A glass tube filled with silica gel is typically used in column chromatography experiments.
