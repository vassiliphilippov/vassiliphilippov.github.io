firing party

noun
group firing rifles at funerals or executions
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrɪŋ ˌpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈfaɪərɪŋ ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a group of soldiers assigned to fire rifles as a salute at a military funeral or to carry out a military execution

Examples

military firing party
A seven-member firing party performed the traditional three-volley salute at the ceremony.
The firing party assembled in formation as the casket was carried to the gravesite.
The condemned man faced the firing party without showing any fear.
