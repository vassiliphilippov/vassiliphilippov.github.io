Scotch whisky

noun
whisky made in Scotland
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈwɪski / (BrE)
Whisky that is distilled in Scotland, especially one made from malted barley in a pot still, and typically aged in oak barrels.

Examples

glass of Scotch whisky
He poured himself a glass of Scotch whisky after dinner to relax.
Many connoisseurs consider single malt Scotch whisky to be the finest type of whisky.
The distillery has been producing Scotch whisky for over two centuries.
