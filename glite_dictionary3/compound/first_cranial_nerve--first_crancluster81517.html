first cranial nerve

noun
the nerve of smell; olfactory nerve
Pronunciation: / fɝst ˈkreɪniəl nɝv / (AmE), / fɜːst ˈkreɪniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the cranial nerve responsible for the sense of smell, consisting of many olfactory filaments within the nasal mucosa; also called the olfactory nerve

Examples

first cranial nerve damage
Damage to the first cranial nerve can result in a loss of the sense of smell.
Injury to the first cranial nerve is rare but may occur following head trauma.
The doctor tested her first cranial nerve function during the neurological examination.
