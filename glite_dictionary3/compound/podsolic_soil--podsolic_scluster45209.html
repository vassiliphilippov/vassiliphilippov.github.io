podsolic soil

noun
soil in temperate/cold, moist climates with a leached layer
Pronunciation: / pɑdˈsoʊlɪk sɔɪl / (AmE), / pɒdˈsɒlɪk sɔɪl / (BrE)
A type of soil that forms in temperate to cold, moist climates under coniferous or heath vegetation, consisting of an organic mat above a grey, leached layer.

Examples

podsolic soil in forests
Foresters must consider the characteristics of podsolic soil when managing boreal forests.
Researchers studied how podsolic soil affects the growth of heathland plants.
The presence of an organic mat above a leached layer is typical of podsolic soil.
