camera lucida

noun
optical device for tracing images
Pronunciation: / ˈkæmərə ˈluːsɪdə / (AmE), / ˈkæmərə ˈluːsɪdə / (BrE)
an optical device that allows an observer to see both a subject and a drawing surface at the same time for tracing or sketching images

Examples

use a camera lucida for drawing
A nineteenth-century scientist relied on a camera lucida for drawing microscopic specimens.
Many art students are fascinated by how the camera lucida blends optical science and drawing techniques.
The artist used a camera lucida to accurately sketch the outlines of the landscape.
