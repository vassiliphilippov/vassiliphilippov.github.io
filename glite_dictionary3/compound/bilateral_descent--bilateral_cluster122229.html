bilateral descent

noun
family lineage traced through both parents
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈlæt.ər.əl dɪˈsent / (BrE)
a system of family lineage in which descent is traced through both the mother's and father's sides of the family

Examples

bilateral descent in families
Anthropologists compare bilateral descent systems to those that are strictly maternal or paternal.
Her family follows bilateral descent, allowing her to claim heritage from both sides.
In societies that practice bilateral descent, individuals inherit property from both their mother's and father's families.
