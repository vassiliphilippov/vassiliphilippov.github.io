to do it mañana

verb
postpone something until tomorrow
Pronunciation: / du ɪt mɑˈnjɑnə / (AmE), / duː ɪt mæˈnɪɑːnə / (BrE)
(informal) To postpone an action until tomorrow; to delay doing something until a later time, often with a relaxed attitude.

Examples

to do it manana with work
I have a lot of paperwork, but I think I'll do it mañana and relax this evening.
She told her friend not to worry and just do it mañana if she was tired.
Whenever faced with chores, he prefers to do it mañana instead of tackling them right away.

Related Terms

  • antonym – do it now: to perform the action immediately, without delay
  • synonym – procrastinate: delay or postpone action; put off doing something
