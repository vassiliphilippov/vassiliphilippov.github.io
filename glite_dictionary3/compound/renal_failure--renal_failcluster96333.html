renal failure

noun
loss of kidney function
Pronunciation: / ˈriːnəl ˈfeɪljər / (AmE), / ˈriːnəl ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the kidneys lose the ability to remove waste products and maintain the body's fluid and electrolyte balance.

Examples

renal failure symptoms
Many symptoms of renal failure include fatigue, swelling, and loss of appetite.
The patient was diagnosed with renal failure after his kidneys stopped functioning properly.
Untreated high blood pressure can eventually lead to renal failure.
