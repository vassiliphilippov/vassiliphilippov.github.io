Council of Vienne

proper noun
medieval church council (1311–1312)
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊnsəl əv viˈɛn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnsəl əv viˈɛn / (BrE)
A church council held in 1311–1312 that investigated the Knights Templar, planned a new crusade, and initiated reforms of the clergy.

Examples

Council of Vienne dissolved Templars
One outcome of the Council of Vienne was the dissolution of the legendary order of knights.
Scholars continue to debate the motivations behind the decisions of the Council of Vienne.
The Council of Vienne marked a turning point in the history of the Knights Templar.
