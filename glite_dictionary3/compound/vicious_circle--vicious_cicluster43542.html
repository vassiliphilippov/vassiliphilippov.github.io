vicious circle

noun
self-perpetuating problematic situation
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃəs ˈsɝːkəl /; / ˈvɪʃ.əs ˈsɝː.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃəs ˈsɜːkl̩ /; / ˈvɪʃ.əs ˈsɜː.kəl / (BrE)
a situation where one problem causes another problem that makes the first problem worse, creating a cycle that is hard to break

Examples

break the vicious circle
The country's economic troubles created a vicious circle of rising unemployment and falling demand.
The debate descended into a vicious circle, with each side repeating their original assumptions.
Trying to lose sleep to reduce stress is a vicious circle that only increases anxiety in the long run.
Breaking the vicious circle of mistrust between the groups required patient negotiation.
The country was trapped in a vicious circle of poverty and debt.
Unemployment and crime form a vicious circle in many urban neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • synonym – circular reasoning: an argument in which the conclusion is assumed in the premises
  • synonym – vicious cycle: A situation where problems compound each other in a continuous cycle
  • antonym – virtuous circle: a beneficial cycle in which positive effects reinforce themselves
