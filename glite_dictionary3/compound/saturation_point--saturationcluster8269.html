saturation point

noun
point where no more can be absorbed or dissolved
Pronunciation: / ˌsætʃəˌreɪʃən pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌsatʃəˈreɪʃən pɔɪnt / (BrE)
in chemistry, the stage at which a substance can absorb or dissolve no more of another substance in solution or vapor

Examples

saturation point in solution
Chemists must know the saturation point of a compound to avoid excess waste.
Cooling the mixture below its saturation point caused crystals to form.
The solution reached its saturation point and no more sugar could dissolve.
