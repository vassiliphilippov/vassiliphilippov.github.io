voice box

noun
part of the throat containing the vocal cords
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔɪs ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪs ˌbɒks / (BrE)
the cartilaginous structure in the throat, also known as the larynx, which contains the vocal cords and produces the sounds involved in speech

Examples

damaged voice box
An injury to the voice box can make it difficult to speak clearly.
Smoking can damage your voice box and affect your voice.
The doctor examined her voice box to find the cause of her hoarseness.
