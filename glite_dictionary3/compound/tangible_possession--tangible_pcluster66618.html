tangible possession

noun
physical property or belonging
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒəbəl pəˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl pəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
something that you own which has a physical form and can be touched; a physical belonging or property

Examples

valuable tangible possession
Insurance policies often cover only tangible possessions lost to fire or theft.
Most tangible possessions depreciate in value over time, except for rare collectibles.
She listed her tangible possessions in the will, including the house and the car.
