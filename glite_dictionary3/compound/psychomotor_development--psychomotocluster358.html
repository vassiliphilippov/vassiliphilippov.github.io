psychomotor development

noun
development of coordinated mental and motor skills
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊˈmoʊtər dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈməʊtə dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
the progressive acquisition of skills that require coordinated mental and motor activities, such as movement, balance, and control

Examples

psychomotor development in children
Doctors monitor a child's psychomotor development to detect any potential delays.
Increased physical activity has been shown to benefit psychomotor development in young children.
Proper assessment of psychomotor development is important in early childhood education.
