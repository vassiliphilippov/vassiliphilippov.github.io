convex polyhedron

noun
solid with only convex polygonal faces
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛks pəˈliːˌhiːdrən / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛks pɒˈlɪˌhiːdrən / (BrE)
A solid figure in three dimensions, each face of which is a convex polygon and any plane section of which is also a convex polygon.

Examples

regular convex polyhedron
A cube is a classic example of a convex polyhedron.
In geometry class, we studied the properties of the convex polyhedron known as a dodecahedron.
Unlike some other solid shapes, every face of a convex polyhedron is a convex polygon.

Related Terms

  • synonym – polyhedron: a solid figure with many plane faces, typically more than six
