monetary unit

noun
standard unit of currency
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌtɛri ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtəri ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
the standard unit of currency in a particular country or economic system, used to measure and express values of money

Examples

monetary unit of a country
Each country typically has its own monetary unit, such as the dollar or the yen.
The euro is the monetary unit used by many countries in the European Union.
The value of a monetary unit can fluctuate with changes in the economy.
