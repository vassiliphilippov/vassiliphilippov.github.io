ground water

noun
underground water in soil and rock
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd ˌwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that collects or flows beneath the earth's surface, filling the spaces in soil and rock.

Examples

ground water level rises
After the heavy rains, the level of ground water in the area rose significantly.
Many rural communities rely on ground water as their primary source of drinking water.
Overuse of ground water can lead to wells drying up and land subsidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym – surface water: water on the surface of the earth, such as rivers and lakes
