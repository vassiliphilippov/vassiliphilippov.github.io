to get away with it

verb
escape blame or punishment
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈweɪ wɪð ɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈweɪ wɪð ɪt / (BrE)
to escape blame, criticism, or punishment for something wrong or dishonest that one has done

Examples

get away with it after cheating
No one noticed the error, so he got away with it completely.
She cheated on her exam and got away with it, though she later felt guilty.
The thief got away with it because there was not enough evidence to convict him.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be caught: to be discovered and punished or blamed
  • synonym – get away with: avoid being punished or blamed for something
