to get my fill

verb
have had enough; be fully satisfied
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt maɪ fɪl / (AmE), / ɡɛt maɪ fɪl / (BrE)
to have had enough of something so that you do not want any more; to be fully satisfied, especially after eating or experiencing something

Examples

to get my fill of food
After hiking all day, I finally got my fill of fresh air and beautiful scenery.
He keeps going back to the dessert table until he gets his fill.
When it comes to watching movies, she can never get her fill.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be satisfied: to feel contented or have one's needs met
  • synonym – have enough: to possess a sufficient amount of something
  • antonym – want more: to desire an additional quantity
