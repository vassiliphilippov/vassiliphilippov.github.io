to have one's chips

verb
be in serious trouble or finished
Pronunciation: / hæv wʌnz tʃɪps / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz tʃɪps / (BrE)
(informal, British) To be in serious trouble, defeated, or out of options, often with the implication that there is no way back.

Examples

have his chips after losing
If he keeps making mistakes like this, he'll have his chips by the end of the month.
She lied to the boss, and now she might have her chips at the company.
When the police arrived, I knew I had my chips and there was no escape.
