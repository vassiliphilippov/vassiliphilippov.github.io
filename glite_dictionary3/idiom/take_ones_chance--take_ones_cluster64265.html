to take one's chance

verb
seize an opportunity despite risk
Pronunciation: / teɪk wʌnz tʃæns / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz tʃɑːns / (BrE)
to act on an opportunity despite the risk of failure; to try in the hope of success

Examples

to take his chance bravely
He knew the risks, but he chose to take his chance rather than wait any longer.
If we want to win, we have to take our chance now and go for it.
She decided to take her chance and apply for the scholarship, even though the odds were slim.

Related Terms

  • antonym – play it safe: to avoid taking risks
  • synonym – seize the day: to make the most of the present moment
