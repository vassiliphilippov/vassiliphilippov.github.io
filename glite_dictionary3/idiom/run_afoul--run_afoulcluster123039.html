to run afoul

verb
come into conflict with rules or laws
Pronunciation: / rʌn əˈfaʊl / (AmE), / rʌn əˈfaʊl / (BrE)
to come into conflict with or go against rules, laws, or authority; to get into trouble by breaking established regulations

Examples

run afoul of the law
By speaking out, she ran afoul of powerful interests in the city.
He warned that ignoring these guidelines could cause us to run afoul of federal laws.
Several companies have run afoul of new environmental regulations this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – break the law: to violate the law; to do something illegal
  • antonym – comply with: to act in accordance with rules or laws; to obey
  • synonym – fall foul of: to come into conflict with, especially rules or authority (British usage)
