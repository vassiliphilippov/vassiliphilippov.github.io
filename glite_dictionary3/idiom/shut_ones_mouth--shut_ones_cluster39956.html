to shut one's mouth

verb
keep quiet, especially about secrets
Pronunciation: / ʃʌt wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / ʃʌt wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to stop talking or remain silent (sometimes used as an imperative to tell someone to be quiet)

Examples

to shut one's mouth quickly
He knew it was best to shut his mouth about the surprise party.
If you can't shut your mouth, you'll ruin the whole plan.
She made sure to shut her mouth when her boss asked about the confidential deal.
He managed to shut his mouth even when the others accused him.
If you don’t have anything nice to say, just shut your mouth.
She had to remind herself to shut her mouth during the heated meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be quiet: to stop talking or making noise
  • synonym – keep one's mouth shut: to remain silent or not reveal information
