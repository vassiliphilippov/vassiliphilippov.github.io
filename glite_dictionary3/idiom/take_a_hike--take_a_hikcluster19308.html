to take a hike

verb
tell someone to leave abruptly (idiom)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə haɪk / (AmE), / teɪk ə haɪk / (BrE)
(informal) to leave, especially in a forceful or abrupt way; to go away.

Examples

take a hike angrily
After their argument, she told him to take a hike.
If you don't like my decision, you can take a hike.
The boss said anyone not willing to work hard could take a hike.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beat it: (informal) to leave immediately
  • synonym – go away: to leave a place or person
