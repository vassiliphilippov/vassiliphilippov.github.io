to have one's hand out

verb
expect or ask for money
Pronunciation: / hæv wʌnz hænd aʊt / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz hænd aʊt / (BrE)
to request or expect to receive money, especially by asking directly or appearing in need

Examples

have one's hand out for money
After the meeting, several charities had their hand out for donations.
If you keep having your hand out, people may stop helping you.
It's his habit to have his hand out whenever there's a chance for extra cash.

Related Terms

  • synonym – beg: to ask for money or help, often on the street
  • synonym – solicit: to ask for or try to obtain something from someone
