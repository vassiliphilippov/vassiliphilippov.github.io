long long ago

adverb
in the distant past; storytellers' phrase
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ lɔŋ əˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / lɒŋ lɒŋ əˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
used to refer to a very distant time in the past, often to introduce a story or legend

Examples

long long ago in a story
Long long ago, dragons were believed to inhabit these mountains.
Many legends have their roots long long ago, in times we can hardly imagine.
My grandmother would always start her tales with 'long long ago there lived a wise old man.'
