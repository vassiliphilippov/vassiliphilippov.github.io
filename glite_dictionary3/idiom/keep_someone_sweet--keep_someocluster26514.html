to keep someone sweet

verb
maintain someone's goodwill or approval
Pronunciation: / kip ˈsʌmwʌn swit / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwən swiːt / (BrE)
to act in a way that maintains someone's goodwill, approval, or favor, often by offering something attractive or beneficial

Examples

keep her sweet with gifts
He always brings her flowers to keep her sweet.
Politicians often try to keep key voters sweet during elections.
The company gave its best customers extra discounts to keep them sweet.

Related Terms

  • synonym – butter someone up: to flatter someone in order to gain favor
  • synonym – curry favor: to try to win someone's favor, often by flattery or servility
