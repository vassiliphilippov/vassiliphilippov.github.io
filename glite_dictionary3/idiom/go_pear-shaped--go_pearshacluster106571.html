to go pear-shaped

verb
to go badly wrong or fail
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈpɛr ʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈpeə ʃeɪpt / (BrE)
(of a situation or plan) to become unsuccessful or go badly wrong.

Examples

plans go pear-shaped
Despite months of careful planning, everything began to go pear-shaped at the last minute.
If we ignore the warning signs, the company could go pear-shaped very quickly.
Our holiday plans went pear-shaped when our flight was cancelled due to bad weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym – go awry: to not go as planned or expected
  • antonym – go smoothly: to proceed as planned without problems
