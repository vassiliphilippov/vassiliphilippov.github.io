to hit the streets

verb
go out into public for a purpose
Pronunciation: / hɪt ðə strits / (AmE), / hɪt ðə striːts / (BrE)
to go out into public, especially to engage in a specific activity such as protesting, campaigning, or distributing something

Examples

to hit the streets protesting
After the announcement, supporters hit the streets to celebrate their team's victory.
Every summer, ice cream vendors hit the streets to serve treats to passersby.
Volunteers will hit the streets at dawn to help with the city cleanup.
