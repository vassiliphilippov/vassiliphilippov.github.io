to beat the drum

verb
advocate strongly for a cause
Pronunciation: / bit ðə drʌm / (AmE), / biːt ðə drʌm / (BrE)
to speak or act forcefully in support of a cause, idea, or campaign, often in a persistent or public way

Examples

to beat the drum for change
Politicians often start beating the drum for new policies before an election.
She is always beating the drum about the importance of voting in local elections.
The activist spent years beating the drum for environmental protection.

Related Terms

  • antonym – oppose: to be against or actively resist a cause or idea
  • synonym – promote: to support or actively encourage a cause or idea
