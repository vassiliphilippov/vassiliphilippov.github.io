to pick up one's marbles

verb
withdraw from a situation (informal)
Pronunciation: / pɪk ʌp wʌnz ˈmɑrbəlz / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp wʌnz ˈmɑːbəlz / (BrE)
(informal) To withdraw from a situation, often as a result of disagreement or dissatisfaction.

Examples

pick up his marbles and leave
After losing the debate, she decided to pick up her marbles and leave the meeting.
Rather than compromise, he just picked up his marbles and walked out of the project.
Whenever he doesn't get his way, he tends to pick up his marbles and quit.
