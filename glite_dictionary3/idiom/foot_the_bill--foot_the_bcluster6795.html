to foot the bill

verb
pay the cost of something
Pronunciation: / fʊt ðə bɪl / (AmE), / fʊt ðə bɪl / (BrE)
to pay for the cost of something, especially when it is large or unexpected

Examples

foot the bill for dinner
After the company lunch, Janet volunteered to foot the bill for everyone.
If no one else helps, I guess I'll have to foot the bill myself.
The government agreed to foot the bill for the repairs after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cover the cost: to pay for all expenses involved in something
  • synonym – pay: to give money to someone for work done or goods received
