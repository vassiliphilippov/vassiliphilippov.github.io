cat in the sack

noun
unknown or risky purchase or situation
Pronunciation: / kæt ɪn ðə sæk / (AmE), / kat ɪn ðə sak / (BrE)
An item or situation whose value or quality is unknown until after it is acquired or committed to, often leading to risk or disappointment.

Examples

buy a cat in the sack
He warned me not to sign the contract, as it might turn out to be a cat in the sack.
The artwork seemed impressive online, but purchasing without seeing it first is a real cat in the sack.
When you invest in that unfamiliar company, you're really buying a cat in the sack.

Related Terms

  • synonym – pig in a poke: an item bought without being seen or checked first, often resulting in disappointment
