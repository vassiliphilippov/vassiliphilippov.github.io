to throw dirt

verb
harm reputation with negative comments
Pronunciation: / θroʊ dɜrt / (AmE), / θrəʊ dɜːt / (BrE)
to attempt to harm someone's reputation by making negative remarks or accusations, often without proof

Examples

throw dirt on someone
During the debate, both candidates tried to throw dirt on each other's character.
People shouldn't throw dirt without knowing all the facts.
The tabloids love to throw dirt at celebrities whenever they can.

Related Terms

  • synonym – defame: to damage the reputation of someone by false statements
  • synonym – slander: the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
