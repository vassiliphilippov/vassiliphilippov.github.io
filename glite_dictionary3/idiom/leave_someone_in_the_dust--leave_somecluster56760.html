to leave someone in the dust

verb
surpass or outpace someone significantly
Pronunciation: / liv ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn ðə dʌst / (AmE), / liːv ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn ðə dʌst / (BrE)
to greatly surpass or outpace someone in achievement, performance, or progress; to leave someone far behind

Examples

leave someone in the dust in a race
By the third lap, she was so fast that she left her rivals in the dust.
When the new runner joined our team, he quickly left everyone in the dust.
With their innovative technology, the company hopes to leave competitors in the dust.

Related Terms

  • antonym – fall behind: to fail to keep up with others in progress
  • synonym – outpace: to move faster or make more progress than someone or something
