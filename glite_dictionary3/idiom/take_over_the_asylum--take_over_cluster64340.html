to take over the asylum

verb
to become chaotic under unfit leadership
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈoʊvɚ ði əˈsaɪləm / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə ði əˈsaɪləm / (BrE)
(of a situation) to become disorderly or chaotic, typically because those least suitable or rational have gained control.

Examples

take over the asylum completely
It sometimes feels like take over the asylum when inexperienced managers make all the decisions.
Many employees joked that upper management would soon take over the asylum if things continued this way.
The project turned into chaos, as if someone had deliberately taken over the asylum.
