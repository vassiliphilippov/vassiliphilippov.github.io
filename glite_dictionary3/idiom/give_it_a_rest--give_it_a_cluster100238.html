to give it a rest

verb
stop doing or talking about something
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ɪt ə rɛst / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪt ə rɛst / (BrE)
to stop doing or talking about something, especially because it is becoming annoying or excessive

Examples

give it a rest for now
After hours of practicing, she decided to give it a rest and relax.
He kept complaining all day until I finally told him to give it a rest.
You've made your point, so why don't you give it a rest now?

Related Terms

  • antonym – carry on: to continue something, especially when asked to stop
  • synonym – let up: to become less intense or to stop
