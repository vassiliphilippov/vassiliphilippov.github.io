to burn at the stake

verb
execute by burning; subject to harsh criticism
Pronunciation: / bɝn æt ðə steɪk / (AmE), / bɜːn æt ðə steɪk / (BrE)
to execute by burning a person tied to a stake, historically used as punishment for crimes such as heresy; often used figuratively to refer to subjecting someone to severe criticism or public condemnation.

Examples

burn at the stake for heresy
After her unpopular decision, some colleagues seemed ready to burn her at the stake.
He joked that if he missed the meeting, his team would burn him at the stake.
In history class, we learned that many people were burned at the stake for alleged witchcraft.

Related Terms

  • synonym – burn alive: to kill someone by burning, often used for executions
  • synonym – crucify: to put someone to death by nailing or binding to a cross; also used figuratively for harsh criticism
