to throw a tantrum

verb
to have an angry outburst
Pronunciation: / θroʊ ə ˈtæntrəm / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə ˈtæntrəm / (BrE)
to have a sudden, uncontrolled display of anger or frustration, often in a dramatic way

Examples

to throw a tantrum loudly
She threatened to throw a tantrum if she didn’t get her way at the meeting.
The toddler began to throw a tantrum when his mother refused to buy him candy.
Whenever things don’t go his way, he tends to throw a tantrum.

Related Terms

  • synonym – have a fit: to become very angry or upset suddenly
  • antonym – keep one's cool: to stay calm and controlled in a difficult situation
