like a lemming off a cliff

adverb
blindly or recklessly following others
Pronunciation: / laɪk ə ˈlɛmɪŋ ɔf ə klɪf / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈlɛmɪŋ ɒf ə klɪf / (BrE)
in a way that blindly or recklessly follows others, especially to one's harm or downfall

Examples

follow like a lemming off a cliff
He invested all his savings in the startup like a lemming off a cliff, without proper research.
Sometimes, people rush to buy the latest gadget like a lemming off a cliff, just because everyone else does.
When the company introduced the new policy, employees accepted it like a lemming off a cliff.

Related Terms

  • antonym – go your own way: to act independently rather than following others
