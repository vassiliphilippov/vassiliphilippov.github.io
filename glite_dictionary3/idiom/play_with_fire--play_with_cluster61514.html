to play with fire

verb
take foolish risks; act dangerously
Pronunciation: / pleɪ wɪð ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / pleɪ wɪð ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to take foolish risks; to engage in actions that are likely to cause harm or trouble

Examples

to play with fire dangerously
By ignoring the doctor's advice, she was playing with fire with her health.
If you cheat on your taxes, you're playing with fire and could get caught.
Investing all your savings in one company is really playing with fire.
