to be on the ball

verb
to be alert or well-informed
Pronunciation: / bi ɑn ðə bɔl / (AmE), / bi ɒn ðə bɔːl / (BrE)
to be alert, attentive, or well-informed, especially in noticing and responding quickly to things

Examples

be on the ball at work
If our team wants to win, everyone will have to be on the ball during the match.
In this fast-paced industry, you need to be on the ball to succeed.
She is always on the ball when it comes to meeting deadlines and solving problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be alert: to be quick to notice or respond to things
  • antonym – be asleep at the wheel: to fail to notice or respond to things quickly
