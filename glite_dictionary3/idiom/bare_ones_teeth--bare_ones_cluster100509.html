to bare one's teeth

verb
show aggression or hostility
Pronunciation: / bɛr wʌnz tiθ / (AmE), / beə wʌnz tiːθ / (BrE)
to show aggression or hostility, often by revealing one's teeth in anger or warning

Examples

bare one's teeth in anger
During the heated argument, Julia suddenly bared her teeth and raised her voice.
If you keep provoking him, he's likely to bare his teeth and retaliate.
When threatened, many animals will bare their teeth to warn off intruders.
