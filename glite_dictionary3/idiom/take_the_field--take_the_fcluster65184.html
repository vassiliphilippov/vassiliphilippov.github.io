to take the field

verb
go onto the playing field (sports idiom)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə fiːld / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fiːld / (BrE)
(of a sports team or player) to go onto the playing field, especially at the start of a game.

Examples

to take the field before kickoff
Cheers erupted as the home team took the field in their new uniforms.
Fans watched anxiously, waiting for their favorite athletes to take the field after the long delay.
The players will take the field at 7 p.m. for the championship game.
