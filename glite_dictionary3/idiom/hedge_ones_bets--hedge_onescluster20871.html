to hedge one's bets

verb
protect against risk by multiple options
Pronunciation: / hɛdʒ wʌnz bɛts / (AmE), / hɛdʒ wʌnz bɛts / (BrE)
to protect oneself against loss or failure by supporting more than one possibility or by taking multiple precautions

Examples

to hedge your bets in business
It's wise to hedge your bets when the outcome is so uncertain.
Many investors hedge their bets by spreading their money across various asset classes.
She decided to hedge her bets by keeping both job offers open until the last minute.
