to wither on the vine

verb
fail to develop or succeed
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪðər ɑn ðə vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɪðə ɒn ðə vaɪn / (BrE)
(of a plan, project, or goal) to fail to thrive, develop, or succeed, often because of neglect or lack of support.

Examples

wither on the vine from neglect
If you ignore your talents, they may simply wither on the vine over time.
Many promising startups wither on the vine without adequate investment.
The committee's recommendations were allowed to wither on the vine after the leadership changed.
