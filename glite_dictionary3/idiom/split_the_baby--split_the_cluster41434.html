to split the baby

verb
reach a compromise in a tough dispute
Pronunciation: / splɪt ðə ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / splɪt ðə ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
to reach a compromise by dividing the outcome between opposing parties, especially in a situation where a clear win for either side is not possible

Examples

split the baby in negotiations
During the merger talks, the executives agreed to split the baby to satisfy both companies’ interests.
Faced with a deadlock, the mediator proposed they split the baby so no party would leave empty-handed.
Rather than favor one team, the coach decided to split the baby and let both play equal minutes.
