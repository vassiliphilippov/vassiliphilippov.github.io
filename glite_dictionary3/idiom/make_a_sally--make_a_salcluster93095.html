to make a sally

verb
set out energetically; go on a short venture
Pronunciation: / meɪk ə ˈsæli / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈsæli / (BrE)
to set out energetically on a short journey or venture, especially one with a purpose or sense of adventure

Examples

to make a sally into town
After hours of studying, they decided to make a sally into the city for some fresh air.
At dawn, the soldiers made a sally from the fortress to surprise the enemy.
The explorers hoped to make a sally beyond the familiar coastline and chart new territory.
