to go to one's head

verb
become conceited due to success
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tu wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tuː wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
(of praise, success, etc.) to make someone become arrogant or conceited.

Examples

go to her head after praise
All the compliments started to go to her head after a while.
Don't let your recent promotion go to your head; remember to stay humble.
Winning the award so quickly seemed to go to his head and made him act superior.
