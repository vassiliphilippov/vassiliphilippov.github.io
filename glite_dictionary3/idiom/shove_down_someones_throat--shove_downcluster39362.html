to shove down someone's throat

verb
forcefully impose ideas or beliefs on someone
Pronunciation: / ʃʌv daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θroʊt / (AmE), / ʃʌv daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θrəʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to accept or support something, especially an idea or opinion, in an aggressive or insistent manner

Examples

shove down someone's throat an opinion
She doesn't like it when her friends shove their opinions down her throat during discussions.
Stop trying to shove down my throat things I don't agree with.
They shoved new procedures down everyone's throat without asking for input.
