to cry foul

verb
complain of unfairness
Pronunciation: / kraɪ faʊl / (AmE), / kraɪ faʊl / (BrE)
to complain loudly or publicly that something is unfair or unjust, especially in sports or competition

Examples

cry foul after a decision
After the referee made a controversial decision, the coach began to cry foul during the press conference.
It's easy to cry foul when you feel the rules aren't being applied equally.
Many citizens cry foul whenever new laws are passed without public consultation.

  • synonym – protest: express strong objection or disapproval
