thin air

noun
disappearance without a trace
Pronunciation: / ˌθɪn ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˌθɪn ˈeə / (BrE)
used to refer to a place or state where someone or something cannot be seen, found, or traced, often after disappearing suddenly or mysteriously.

Examples

disappear into thin air
It was as if the magician pulled a rabbit out of thin air.
She looked everywhere for her keys, but they had vanished into thin air.
The document seemed to disappear into thin air during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – reappear: to come into sight again after vanishing
  • synonym – vanish: to disappear suddenly and completely
