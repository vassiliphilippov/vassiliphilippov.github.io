to take your hook

verb
leave or depart abruptly (idiom)
Pronunciation: / teɪk jər hʊk / (AmE), / teɪk jɔː hʊk / (BrE)
to leave or depart from a place or situation, especially suddenly or abruptly

Examples

to take your hook quickly
He decided to take your hook when the meeting got tense and unproductive.
I knew it was time to take your hook before the argument escalated further.
If you're not wanted here, maybe you should just take your hook and go.
