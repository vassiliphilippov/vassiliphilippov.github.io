Johnny-come-lately

noun
recent newcomer; late arrival
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɑni kəm ˈleɪtli / (AmE), / ˌdʒɒni kəm ˈleɪtli / (BrE)
a person who has recently joined a group, organization, or activity, often regarded as a newcomer or latecomer

Examples

a Johnny-come-lately in politics
After joining the club last month, everyone still treats him like a Johnny-come-lately.
Even as a Johnny-come-lately, she quickly adapted to her new role.
The seasoned employees were unimpressed by the opinions of the Johnny-come-lately in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym – newcomer: a person who has recently arrived, especially in a new place or situation
  • antonym – old-timer: someone who has been part of a place or group for a long time
