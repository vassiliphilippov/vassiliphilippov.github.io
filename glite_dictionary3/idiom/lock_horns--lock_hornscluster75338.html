to lock horns

verb
to engage in conflict or argument
Pronunciation: / lɑk hɔrnz / (AmE), / lɒk hɔːnz / (BrE)
to become involved in a conflict or heated argument with someone

Examples

lock horns over money
The committee members lock horns every year over the budget.
The two scientists locked horns during the heated debate on climate change.
Whenever there's a new project, the managers seem to lock horns about the best approach.

Related Terms

  • antonym – agree: to have the same opinion; to not be in conflict
  • synonym – argue: to exchange or express diverging or opposite views in a heated or angry way
  • synonym – clash: to come into conflict; to have a heated disagreement
