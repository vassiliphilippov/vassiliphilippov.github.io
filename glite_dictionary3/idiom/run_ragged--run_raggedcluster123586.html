to run ragged

verb
make someone very tired or exhausted
Pronunciation: / rʌn ˈræɡɪd / (AmE), / rʌn ˈræɡɪd / (BrE)
to make someone extremely tired or exhausted, especially by giving them too much work or activity

Examples

run ragged by work
He was run ragged by the endless meetings and tight deadlines.
Looking after three toddlers all day can really run you ragged.
The constant demands of her job ran her ragged by the end of the week.
