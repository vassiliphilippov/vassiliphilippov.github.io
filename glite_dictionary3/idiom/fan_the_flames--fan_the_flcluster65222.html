to fan the flames

verb
intensify a conflict or situation
Pronunciation: / fæn ðə fleɪmz / (AmE), / fan ðə fleɪmz / (BrE)
to make a situation, emotion, or conflict worse or more intense by encouraging or provoking it

Examples

to fan the flames of anger
His criticism only served to fan the flames of the argument.
It's best not to fan the flames when tempers are already high.
Social media can fan the flames of misinformation very quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm down: to make a situation less intense or emotional
  • synonym – exacerbate: to make a problem or situation worse
