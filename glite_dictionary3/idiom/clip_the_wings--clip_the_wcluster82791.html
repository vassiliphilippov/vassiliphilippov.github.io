to clip the wings

verb
restrict someone's freedom or potential
Pronunciation: / klɪp ðə wɪŋz / (AmE), / klɪp ðə wɪŋz / (BrE)
to restrict or limit someone's freedom, power, or potential

Examples

clip the wings of ambition
Her parents tried to clip the wings of her ambitions by discouraging her from moving abroad.
The board decided to clip the wings of the CEO by limiting his spending authority.
The new regulations are likely to clip the wings of innovative startups.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hold back: to stop someone or something from making progress
  • antonym – set free: to allow someone to be or act freely; to release restrictions
