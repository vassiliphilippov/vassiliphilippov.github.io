to keep your stick on the ice

verb
hockey: remain alert and ready
Pronunciation: / kip jʊr stɪk ɑn ði aɪs / (AmE), / kiːp jɔː stɪk ɒn ði aɪs / (BrE)
(in ice hockey) to remain alert and ready to react by keeping one's hockey stick in contact with the ice, or (figuratively) to stay focused and prepared.

Examples

keep your stick on the ice during play
Good coaches always remind their players to keep your stick on the ice during the game.
If you keep your stick on the ice, you have a better chance of controlling the puck.
She told her son, "Keep your stick on the ice and be ready for any opportunity!"

  • synonym – stay alert: to remain watchful and attentive
