to tiptoe through a minefield

verb
proceed cautiously through a tricky situation
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪpˌtoʊ θru ə ˈmaɪnˌfild / (AmE), / ˈtɪptəʊ θruː ə ˈmaɪnfiːld / (BrE)
to proceed very cautiously through a difficult or sensitive situation, being careful to avoid mistakes or causing offense

Examples

to tiptoe through a minefield of problems
During the negotiation, she had to tiptoe through a minefield of conflicting interests.
He felt like he was tiptoeing through a minefield every time he discussed company policies.
When talking about religion at dinner, we all tried to tiptoe through a minefield to avoid arguments.
