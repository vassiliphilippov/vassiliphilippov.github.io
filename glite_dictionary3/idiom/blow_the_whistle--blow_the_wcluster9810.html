to blow the whistle

verb
report wrongdoing; expose illicit activity
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ðə ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / bləʊ ðə ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
to report or expose illicit, illegal, or unethical activity, especially by informing authorities or the public

Examples

to blow the whistle on wrongdoing
After discovering the fraud, she blew the whistle and contacted the authorities.
He was praised for having the courage to blow the whistle on the company's illegal dumping practices.
If you blow the whistle on corruption, you may be protected by law.

Related Terms

  • synonym – whistleblower: a person who reports misconduct or illegal activity, especially within an organization
