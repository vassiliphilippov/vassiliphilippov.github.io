to run a mile

verb
try hard to avoid; flee out of fear
Pronunciation: / rʌn ə maɪl / (AmE), / rʌn ə maɪl / (BrE)
to try very hard to avoid someone or something, or to flee quickly out of fear or aversion.

Examples

to run a mile from danger
He would run a mile at the sight of a snake.
Most politicians run a mile from giving a straight answer.
When it comes to public speaking, she would run a mile if given the chance.

Related Terms

  • synonym – avoid: to keep away from or evade
  • antonym – confront: to face up to or deal with a situation
