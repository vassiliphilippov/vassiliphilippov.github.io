to couldn't help but crow

verb
unable to refrain from boasting
Pronunciation: / ˈkʊdənt hɛlp bət kroʊ / (AmE), / ˈkʊdnt hɛlp bət krəʊ / (BrE)
To be unable to refrain from boasting or bragging about one's success.

Examples

couldn't help but crow about victory
After scoring the winning goal, he couldn't help but crow about his achievement.
She couldn't help but crow when the promotion was announced.
They couldn't help but crow over their team's decisive victory.

Related Terms

  • synonym – boast: to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements or possessions
  • synonym – brag: to speak in a self-admiring way about one's accomplishments
  • antonym – downplay: to make something seem less important or impressive than it is
