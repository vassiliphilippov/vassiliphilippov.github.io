to go against the grain

verb
act contrary to custom or norms
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈɡɛnst ðə ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈɡɛn st ðə ɡreɪn / (BrE)
to do something that is very different from what is usual or normal, or from what most people expect or accept

Examples

to go against the grain of society
He found that lying went against the grain of everything he believed in.
It really goes against the grain for her to ask for help, as she is usually very independent.
Joining the rival company went against the grain of his family's expectations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – go with the flow: to act in accordance with what others are doing or what is expected
