to let him have it

verb
scold or reprimand someone sharply
Pronunciation: / lɛt ɪm ˈhæv ɪt / (AmE), / lɛt ɪm ˈhæv ɪt / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval or criticism of someone, especially by scolding or reprimanding them sharply

Examples

let him have it angrily
After hearing about the prank, the teacher let them have it in front of the whole class.
If you make another mistake, the boss will let you have it during the meeting.
When her brother broke the vase, their mother really let him have it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – tell off: to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
