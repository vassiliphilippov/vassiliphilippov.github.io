to take a flyer

verb
take a risk or chance
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə ˈflaɪɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈflaɪə / (BrE)
to take a risk or chance, especially when the outcome is uncertain

Examples

to take a flyer on a new idea
Despite having little experience, I decided to take a flyer on the new job opportunity.
She occasionally likes to take a flyer on new restaurants she hasn’t heard of before.
They’re going to take a flyer and invest in that startup company.
