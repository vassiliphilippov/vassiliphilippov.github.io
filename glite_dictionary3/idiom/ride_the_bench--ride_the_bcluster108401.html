to ride the bench

verb
remain out of play as a substitute (sports)
Pronunciation: / raɪd ðə bɛntʃ / (AmE), / raɪd ðə bɛntʃ / (BrE)
(of a sports player) to remain on the sidelines as a substitute rather than participating actively in a game.

Examples

ride the bench during games
After his injury, he had to ride the bench for the rest of the season.
Many talented rookies start their careers by riding the bench.
She hated to ride the bench when she felt ready to play.
