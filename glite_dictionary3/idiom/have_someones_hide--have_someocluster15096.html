to have someone's hide

verb
scold or punish harshly
Pronunciation: / hæv ˈsʌmwʌnz haɪd / (AmE), / hæv ˈsʌmwənz haɪd / (BrE)
to punish or scold someone severely, often because they have done something wrong or made a mistake

Examples

have his hide for a mistake
If my dad finds out I broke the window, he'll have my hide.
She knew the coach would have her hide for missing practice.
The boss threatened to have their hide if the report wasn't finished by noon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – let someone off easy: to punish or reprimand less harshly
  • synonym – skin someone alive: to punish or criticize someone severely
