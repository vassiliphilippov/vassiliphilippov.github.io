you what

interjection
expression of disbelief or request for repetition
Pronunciation: / ju wɑt / (AmE), / ju wɒt / (BrE)
(informal, British) used to express disbelief or to ask someone to repeat what they have just said, often when surprised or incredulous.

Examples

you what, mate
You what, mate? Could you repeat that?
You what? I can't believe you just said that.
When he told me he quit his job on a whim, I replied, "You what?"

Related Terms

  • synonym – come again: (informal) used to ask someone to repeat something
  • synonym – pardon: used to politely ask someone to repeat what they said
