to hear the grass grow

verb
be extremely quiet or still
Pronunciation: / hɪr ðə ɡræs ɡroʊ / (AmE), / hɪə ðə ɡrɑːs ɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to be in an environment or situation so quiet and still that even the faintest sounds seem noticeable; to experience extreme silence or stillness.

Examples

hear the grass grow at midnight
After midnight, the town became so still you could almost hear the grass grow.
It was so quiet during the exam that you could hear the grass grow.
She stood in the deserted gallery, feeling as if she could hear the grass grow.
