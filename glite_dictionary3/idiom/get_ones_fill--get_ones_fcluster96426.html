to get one's fill

verb
satisfy a desire or have enough
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt wʌnz fɪl / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz fɪl / (BrE)
to have as much as one wants or needs of something; to satisfy a desire or appetite fully

Examples

get one's fill of food
After days of sightseeing in Paris, she finally got her fill of art and culture.
The children got their fill of candy at the birthday party.
You can watch as many movies as you want tonight and get your fill.
