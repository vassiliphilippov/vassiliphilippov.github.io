to pound sand

verb
dismissive phrase meaning 'go away'
Pronunciation: / paʊnd sænd / (AmE), / paʊnd sand / (BrE)
(North American, informal) Used to tell someone to go away or that their request is rejected or not worth considering.

Examples

pound sand after a request
After he asked for a raise twice in one week, his boss told him to pound sand.
If you don't like the rules here, you can just pound sand!
When the salesperson refused to negotiate, I told him to pound sand and left the store.

Related Terms

  • synonym – buzz off: to go away quickly; used as a rude command
  • synonym – get lost: to go away; used to tell someone to leave rudely
