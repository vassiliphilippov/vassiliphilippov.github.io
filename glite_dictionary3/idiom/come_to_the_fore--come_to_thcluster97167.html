to come to the fore

verb
become prominent or active
Pronunciation: / kʌm tu ðə fɔr / (AmE), / kʌm tə ðə fɔː / (BrE)
to become noticeable, active, or important, especially in a particular situation

Examples

come to the fore in a crisis
After the resignation of the manager, several employees came to the fore to lead the team.
Environmental issues have come to the fore in recent political debates.
When problems arise, true leaders come to the fore and take responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym – come forward: to volunteer or present oneself
  • antonym – retreat: to withdraw or move back from a position or situation
