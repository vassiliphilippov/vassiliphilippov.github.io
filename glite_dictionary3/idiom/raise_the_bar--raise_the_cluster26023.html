to raise the bar

verb
set a higher standard or expectation
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪz ðə bɑr / (AmE), / ˈreɪz ðə bɑː / (BrE)
to set a higher standard or level of expectation, particularly in comparison to previous standards

Examples

raise the bar for quality
By incorporating sustainability, the architects managed to raise the bar for future building projects.
Her outstanding performance will raise the bar for all future employees.
The company hopes its new phone will raise the bar in terms of design and functionality.
