to take to the streets

verb
demonstrate or protest publicly
Pronunciation: / teɪk tə ðə striːts / (AmE), / teɪk tə ðə striːts / (BrE)
to go outside in large numbers, especially to demonstrate or protest publicly

Examples

to take to the streets in protest
Activists are expected to take to the streets if the new law is passed.
Thousands of citizens took to the streets to demand political reform.
Whenever there is social injustice, people often take to the streets to make their voices heard.
