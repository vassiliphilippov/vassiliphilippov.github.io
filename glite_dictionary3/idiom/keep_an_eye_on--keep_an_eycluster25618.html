to keep an eye on

verb
watch or monitor closely
Pronunciation: / kip ən aɪ ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp ən aɪ ɒn / (BrE)
to watch or monitor someone or something carefully, usually to make sure nothing bad happens

Examples

keep an eye on the kids

I need you to keep an eye on the kids while I cook dinner.
She asked her neighbor to keep an eye on her house during her vacation.
Could you keep an eye on the soup while I answer the door?
I'll keep an eye on your luggage while you grab some coffee.
Parents are advised to keep an eye on their children at public playgrounds.
