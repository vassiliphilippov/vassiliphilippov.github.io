to blow hot and cold

verb
be inconsistent in feelings or opinions
Pronunciation: / bloʊ hɑt ənd koʊld / (AmE), / bləʊ hɒt ənd kəʊld / (BrE)
to frequently and unpredictably change one's feelings, opinions, or attitudes about something; to be inconsistent in one's stance or commitment.

Examples

blow hot and cold on a decision
He tends to blow hot and cold about his career plans, making it hard to know what he really wants.
She blows hot and cold on the idea of moving abroad, so it's hard to make any plans.
When it comes to commitments, Mark often blows hot and cold, confusing his friends with his unpredictability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be consistent: to act or think in a steady, unchanging way
  • synonym – vacillate: to waver between different opinions or actions; be indecisive
