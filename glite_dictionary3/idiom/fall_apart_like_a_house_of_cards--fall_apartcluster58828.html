to fall apart like a house of cards

verb
collapse or fail suddenly and completely
Pronunciation: / fɔl əˈpɑrt laɪk ə haʊs əv kɑrdz / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈpɑːt laɪk ə haʊs əv kɑːdz / (BrE)
(of a plan, organization, or structure) to collapse or fail suddenly and completely, especially because it is weak or poorly constructed.

Examples

fall apart like a house of cards under pressure
Their arguments were weak and soon fell apart like a house of cards during the debate.
When the CEO resigned, the company began to fall apart like a house of cards.
Without a solid foundation, any project can fall apart like a house of cards under pressure.
