to stand pat

verb
refuse to change one's opinion or position
Pronunciation: / stænd pæt / (AmE), / stænd pæt /; / stand pæt / (BrE)
to refuse to change your opinion, decision, or position; to remain firm

Examples

stand pat on a decision
Despite criticism from her colleagues, she decided to stand pat on her decision.
Even in the face of new evidence, he stood pat on his original statement.
The negotiators will stand pat until the other side makes a concession.
Despite criticism from colleagues, she chose to stand pat on her decision.
Even after the negotiations failed, the company continued to stand pat on its offer.
Some investors prefer to stand pat during market uncertainty rather than risk change.

Related Terms

  • antonym – back down: to withdraw from a position or change one's mind
  • antonym – give in: to yield to pressure or persuasion; to abandon one's position
  • synonym – hold firm: to maintain one's opinion or position firmly
