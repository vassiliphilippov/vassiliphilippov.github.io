to grease the wheels

verb
make a process smoother or easier
Pronunciation: / ɡriːs ðə wilz / (AmE), / ɡriːs ðə wiːlz / (BrE)
to make a process or situation run more smoothly or easily, often by providing help, encouragement, or incentives

Examples

grease the wheels of business
A good relationship with suppliers can grease the wheels of any business operation.
He offered to grease the wheels with a small donation to the local charity.
Sometimes a phone call is all it takes to grease the wheels and get things moving.

Related Terms

  • synonym – smooth the way: to make it easier for something to happen
  • antonym – throw a spanner in the works: to cause problems or delays in a process
