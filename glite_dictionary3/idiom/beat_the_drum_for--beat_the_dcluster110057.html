to beat the drum for

verb
advocate or promote enthusiastically
Pronunciation: / bit ðə drʌm fɔr / (AmE), / biːt ðə drʌm fə / (BrE)
to speak enthusiastically in support of a person, cause, or idea; to advocate passionately for something

Examples

beat the drum for a cause
He always beats the drum for local businesses whenever he speaks in public.
The activist continues to beat the drum for climate change action at every opportunity.
They have been beating the drum for equal access to education for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym – campaign for: to actively work in support of a cause or idea
  • synonym – champion: to support or defend a cause or person enthusiastically
  • antonym – oppose: to be against something, to speak or act in resistance to
