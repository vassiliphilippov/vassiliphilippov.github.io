to lay at the feet of

verb
attribute or credit something to someone
Pronunciation: / leɪ æt ðə fit əv / (AmE), / leɪ æt ðə fiːt əv / (BrE)
to credit or attribute (an achievement, responsibility, etc.) to someone; to acknowledge someone as being the cause or source of something

Examples

lay at the feet of a leader
Many of the team's accomplishments were laid at the feet of their inspirational captain.
Responsibility for the outcome was laid at the feet of the management.
She always lays her achievements at the feet of her supportive family.
