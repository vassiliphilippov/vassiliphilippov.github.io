to beat a retreat

verb
depart or withdraw quickly
Pronunciation: / biːt ə rɪˈtrit / (AmE), / biːt ə rɪˈtriːt / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly, often to avoid trouble or danger; to withdraw hastily

Examples

beat a retreat from danger
As soon as the argument started getting heated, Sarah decided to beat a retreat from the room.
Realizing they were outnumbered, the wildlife poachers quickly beat a retreat into the forest.
The protesters beat a retreat when the police arrived with reinforcements.

Related Terms

  • antonym – advance: to move forward toward a place
  • synonym – retreat: to withdraw from a position or situation
