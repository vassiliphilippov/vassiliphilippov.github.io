to throw in the towel

verb
admit defeat; give up trying
Pronunciation: / θroʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊəl /; / θroʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊ.əl / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊəl /; / θrəʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊ.əl / (BrE)
to stop trying or admit defeat because you realize you cannot succeed

Examples

throw in the towel after losing
After several failed attempts to fix the car, he finally decided to throw in the towel.
She was determined not to throw in the towel, no matter how tough things got.
The company refused to throw in the towel despite their mounting losses.
After countless failed experiments, the scientists finally decided to throw in the towel and start over.
Despite losing the first set, she refused to throw in the towel and fought back to win the match.
If we throw in the towel now, all our hard work will have been for nothing.
