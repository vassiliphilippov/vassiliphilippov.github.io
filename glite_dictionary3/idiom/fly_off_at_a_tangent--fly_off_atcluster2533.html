to fly off at a tangent

verb
suddenly change subject
Pronunciation: / flaɪ ɑf ət ə ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf ət ə ˈtændʒənt / (BrE)
to suddenly start discussing or considering a completely different subject, often unrelated to the current topic

Examples

fly off at a tangent during a meeting
It's important not to fly off at a tangent when explaining complex ideas.
Our discussion kept losing focus because people would fly off at a tangent every few minutes.
Whenever John gets excited, he tends to fly off at a tangent during conversations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – digress: to depart from the main subject temporarily in speech or writing
  • antonym – stick to the point: to remain focused on the main subject or issue
