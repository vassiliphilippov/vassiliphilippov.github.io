nugget of truth

noun
a small, valuable fact or insight
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌɡət əv truθ / (AmE), / ˈnʌɡɪt əv truːθ / (BrE)
a small but valuable piece of information or fact, especially one that stands out amid less useful material

Examples

a nugget of truth in a story
Even among the rumors, we found a nugget of truth.
Her joke, though exaggerated, contained a nugget of truth.
There was a nugget of truth hidden in his long explanation.
