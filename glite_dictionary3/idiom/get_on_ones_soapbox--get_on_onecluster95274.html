to get on one's soapbox

verb
speak passionately about one's opinions
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɑn wʌnz ˈsoʊpˌbɑks / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn wʌnz ˈsəʊpˌbɒks / (BrE)
to speak publicly or at length about one's own opinions, especially in a forceful or passionate way

Examples

to get on one's soapbox and preach
Let's not get on our soapbox about this and just listen to what everyone has to say.
She tends to get on her soapbox when discussing environmental issues.
Whenever the topic of education comes up, he can't resist getting on his soapbox.
