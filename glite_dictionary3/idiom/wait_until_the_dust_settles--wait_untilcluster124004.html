to wait until the dust settles

verb
wait for a situation to become clear
Pronunciation: / weɪt ənˈtɪl ðə dʌst ˈsɛt̬əlz / (AmE), / weɪt ənˈtɪl ðə dʌst ˈsɛtlz / (BrE)
to wait for a situation to become clearer or less confused before taking action or making a decision

Examples

to wait until the dust settles after trouble
I think we should wait until the dust settles in the market before investing further.
It's best to wait until the dust settles before responding to their proposal.
They decided to wait until the dust settles after the merger before making any big changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym – act immediately: to take action without waiting for circumstances to settle
  • synonym – wait and see: to delay taking action until the outcome is clearer
