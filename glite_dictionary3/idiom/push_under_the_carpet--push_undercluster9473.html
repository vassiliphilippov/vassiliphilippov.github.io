to push under the carpet

verb
ignore or hide problems
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ ˈʌndɚ ðə ˈkɑr.pɪt / (AmE), / pʊʃ ˈʌndə ðə ˈkɑː.pɪt / (BrE)
to ignore or conceal problems or unpleasant issues rather than dealing with them

Examples

to push under the carpet bad news
Rather than address corruption, officials have continued to push under the carpet any allegations.

Too often, institutions push under the carpet issues that make them uncomfortable.
