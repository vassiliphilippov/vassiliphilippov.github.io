to take the wheel

verb
assume control or responsibility
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə wil / (AmE), / teɪk ðə wiːl / (BrE)
to assume control or responsibility, either literally by driving or figuratively by taking charge of a situation

Examples

to take the wheel during a trip
During the long road trip, I offered to take the wheel so he could rest.
It's time for someone else to take the wheel if we want this project to succeed.
When the manager left unexpectedly, Janet had to take the wheel and lead the team.

Related Terms

  • antonym – hand over the reins: to give control or responsibility to someone else
  • synonym – take charge: to assume control or responsibility
