Jack of all trades

noun
person competent in many skills
Pronunciation: / dʒæk əv ɔl treɪdz / (AmE), / dʒak əv ɔːl treɪdz / (BrE)
a person who can do many different types of work, usually competently but not necessarily expertly in any

Examples

Jack of all trades fixes problems
As a Jack of all trades, he fixed computers, painted houses, and even taught yoga on weekends.
The company relies on her as a Jack of all trades to keep things running smoothly.
You need a Jack of all trades in a small startup, because everyone wears many hats.

Related Terms

  • synonym – generalist: a person competent in several different fields
  • antonym – specialist: a person highly skilled in a specific field
