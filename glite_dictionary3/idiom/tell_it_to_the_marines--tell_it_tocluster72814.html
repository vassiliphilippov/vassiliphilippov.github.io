to tell it to the marines

verb
expression dismissing someone's account as untrue or trivial
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛl ɪt tu ðə məˈrinz / (AmE), / ˈtɛl ɪt tə ðə məˈriːnz / (BrE)
used to express disbelief or dismiss someone's story or complaint as unimportant or unlikely to be true.

Examples

tell it to the marines after a wild story
If you think anyone will believe that story, tell it to the marines.
When he gave yet another excuse for being late, she said, 'Tell it to the marines.'
You expect me to believe that? Tell it to the marines!
