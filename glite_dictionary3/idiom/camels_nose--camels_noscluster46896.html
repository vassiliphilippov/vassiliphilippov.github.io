camel's nose

noun
small step leading to larger problems
Pronunciation: / ˈkæməlz ˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈkæməlz ˌnəʊz / (BrE)
A situation where a small initial concession leads to more significant and often unwanted demands or problems.

Examples

the camel's nose in the tent
Granting that exception was the camel's nose that opened the door to further requests.
Some worry that the new policy is just the camel's nose and will lead to more regulation.
They saw the initial compromise as the camel's nose inside the tent.
