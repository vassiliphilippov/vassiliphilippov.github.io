to take to heart

verb
be deeply affected by; take seriously
Pronunciation: / teɪk tə hɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk tə hɑːt / (BrE)
to be deeply affected by something or treat it very seriously, especially advice, criticism, or comments

Examples

take to heart advice
He takes to heart any negative comment, no matter how minor.
She really took to heart her teacher's criticism and worked hard to improve.
When you give advice, I hope you know that I always take to heart what you say.
After the meeting, he really took their concerns to heart and changed his approach.
She took the feedback to heart and worked hard to improve her performance.
Try not to take his comments to heart—he didn't mean to upset you.

Related Terms

  • antonym – shrug off: to disregard or treat something as unimportant
  • synonym – take personally: to feel offended or affected by something said or done
  • synonym – take seriously: to regard with importance or seriousness
