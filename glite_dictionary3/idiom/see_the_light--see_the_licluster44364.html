to see the light

verb
realize and change for the better
Pronunciation: / si ðə laɪt / (AmE), / siː ðə laɪt / (BrE)
to realize or understand something important, often resulting in a change for the better after a period of ignorance or error

Examples

to see the light about mistakes
After years of reckless spending, she finally saw the light and started saving money.
He refused to apologize at first, but eventually he saw the light and made amends.
Many people only see the light after experiencing significant consequences for their actions.
