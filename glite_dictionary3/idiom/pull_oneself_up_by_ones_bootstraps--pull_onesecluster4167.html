to pull oneself up by one's bootstraps

verb
improve one's situation by personal effort
Pronunciation: / pʊl wʌnˈsɛlf ʌp baɪ wʌnz ˈbuːtstræps / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnˈsɛlf ʌp baɪ wʌnz ˈbuːtstraps / (BrE)
to improve one's situation or achieve success through one's own effort and hard work, without outside help

Examples

to pull oneself up by one's bootstraps after failure
After losing his job, he decided to pull himself up by his bootstraps and start his own business.
Many believe that anyone can pull themselves up by their bootstraps regardless of background.
She pulled herself up by her bootstraps to become a successful lawyer.
