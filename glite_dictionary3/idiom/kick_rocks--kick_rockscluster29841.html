to kick rocks

verb
tell someone to go away (informal)
Pronunciation: / kɪk rɑks / (AmE), / kɪk rɒks / (BrE)
(informal) to go away or leave someone alone, often used as a dismissive command.

Examples

to kick rocks angrily
After he insulted her, she told him to kick rocks and never come back.
He was bothering me, so I told him to kick rocks and leave me alone.
If you don't like the rules, you can just kick rocks.

  • synonym – get lost: to go away; leave someone alone
  • antonym – stay: remain in place; not leave
