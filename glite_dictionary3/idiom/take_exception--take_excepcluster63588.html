to take exception

verb
raise a legal objection
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪkˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / teɪk ɪkˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(legal) To raise a formal objection, especially in a court of law.

Examples

take exception to a statement
Counsel may take exception at any point if the rules of evidence are not observed.
She took exception to the judge’s refusal to allow certain testimony.
The defense attorney took exception to the prosecutor’s line of questioning.
