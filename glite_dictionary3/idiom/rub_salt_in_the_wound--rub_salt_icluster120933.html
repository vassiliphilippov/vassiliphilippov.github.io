to rub salt in the wound

verb
make a painful situation worse
Pronunciation: / rʌb sɔlt ɪn ðə ˈwund / (AmE), / rʌb sɒlt ɪn ðə ˈwuːnd / (BrE)
to make a bad situation or someone's feelings even worse, especially by reminding them of their disappointment or failure

Examples

rub salt in the wound after a loss
Bringing up my failed project only rubbed salt in the wound for me.
Her criticism rubbed salt in the wound on a day that was already going badly.
It really rubbed salt in the wound when he laughed at my mistake.
