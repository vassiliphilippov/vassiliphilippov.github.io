off the record

adverb
not intended for publication or quotation
Pronunciation: / ˌɔf ðə ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˌɒf ðə ˈrek.əd / (BrE)
(of a remark, statement, or information) not intended to be published or made public; not for official record or quotation.

Examples

speak off the record
If you speak off the record, your comments won't be attributed to you.
She told the journalist off the record about the company's financial troubles.
The politician admitted, off the record, that he might resign soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – on the record: intended to be published or officially recorded
