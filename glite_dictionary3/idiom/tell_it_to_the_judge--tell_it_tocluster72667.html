to tell it to the judge

verb
dismissive phrase toward complaints or excuses
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛl ɪt tə ðə dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɛl ɪt tə ðə dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
used to dismiss someone's complaint or excuse, suggesting that only a figure of authority might care or take it seriously

Examples

tell it to the judge sarcastically
I don't want to hear any more about why you're late—tell it to the judge.
If you really think you have a case, go ahead and tell it to the judge.
Whenever he made excuses, his sister would just say, 'tell it to the judge.'
