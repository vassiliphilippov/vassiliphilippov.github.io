to damn with faint praise

verb
criticize by giving weak praise
Pronunciation: / dæm wɪð feɪnt preɪz / (AmE), / dam wɪð feɪnt preɪz / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something indirectly by giving only a weak compliment that is barely positive

Examples

damn with faint praise a performance
It's easy to damn with faint praise when you don't want to offend but also don't want to endorse.
She knew her supervisor would damn with faint praise, never saying anything truly positive.
The reviewer didn't outright condemn the play but chose to damn with faint praise instead.
