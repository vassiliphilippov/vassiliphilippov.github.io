to jump through the hoop

verb
perform a difficult or required task
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp θru ðə hup / (AmE), / dʒʌmp θruː ðə huːp / (BrE)
to do something difficult or demanding, often because it is required by someone in authority or by rules, even if it seems unnecessary

Examples

to jump through the hoop for approval
In order to get the permit, we had to jump through the hoop of endless paperwork.
She was willing to jump through the hoop just to please her new boss.
They refused to jump through the hoop for such an unreasonable request.

Related Terms

  • synonym – jump through hoops: to go through a series of challenging or unnecessary tasks
