to open the kimono

verb
reveal confidential information; be transparent
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.pən ðə kɪˈmoʊ.noʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən ðə kɪˈməʊ.nəʊ / (BrE)
to reveal confidential or proprietary information; to be transparent, especially in business or negotiation contexts

Examples

open the kimono to investors
After months of secrecy, the startup finally decided to open the kimono and explain its technology.
The company refused to open the kimono during the early negotiations with investors.
The lawyer warned them not to open the kimono too soon in the process.
