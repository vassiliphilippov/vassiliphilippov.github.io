hot and bothered

adjective
agitated or flustered
Pronunciation: / hɑt ənd bəˈðɚd / (AmE), / hɒt ənd bəˈðəd /; / hɒt ənd ˈbɒðəd / (BrE)
feeling anxious, upset, or agitated, especially because of stress, excitement, or embarrassment

Examples

get hot and bothered over mistakes
Don't get hot and bothered about small mistakes—everyone makes them.
He became hot and bothered when he couldn't find his keys.
She was getting hot and bothered over the upcoming presentation.
He always gets hot and bothered before important meetings.
Sara was hot and bothered after running late for her flight.
The teachers became hot and bothered when the fire alarm rang unexpectedly.

  • antonym – calm: not showing agitation or nervousness
  • synonym – flustered: nervous or confused state
  • synonym – rattled: nervous or confused, especially suddenly
