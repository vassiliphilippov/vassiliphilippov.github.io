in name only

adverb
having a title but not the reality
Pronunciation: / ɪn neɪm ˈoʊnli / (AmE), / ɪn neɪm ˈəʊnli / (BrE)
Used to describe someone or something that has a title or label, but lacks the qualities, power, or reality that the title suggests.

Examples

in name only leader
Although he was elected chairman, he was chairman in name only, with no real authority.
She is a partner in name only and is not involved in any decisions.
The company exists in name only after it stopped all operations last year.
