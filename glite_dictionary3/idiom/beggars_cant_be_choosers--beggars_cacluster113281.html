beggars can't be choosers

x
those in need must accept what's offered
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛɡɚz kænt bi ˈtʃuzɚz / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəz kɑːnt bi ˈtʃuːzəz / (BrE)
used to say that people who are in need must accept whatever they are given, and should not criticize or demand more.

Examples

beggars can't be choosers with gifts
I wasn't thrilled about the secondhand bike, but beggars can't be choosers.
We would have liked a bigger apartment, but beggars can't be choosers in this city.
When you don't have much money to spend, beggars can't be choosers.

Related Terms

  • synonym – you can't have your cake and eat it too: you can't have two incompatible things at once
