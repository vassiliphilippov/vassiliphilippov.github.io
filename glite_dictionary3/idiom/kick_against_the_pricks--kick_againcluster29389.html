to kick against the pricks

verb
resist futilely against difficulties or authority
Pronunciation: / kɪk əˈɡɛnst ðə prɪks / (AmE), / kɪk əˈɡɛnst ðə prɪks / (BrE)
to resist or rebel against difficulties or authority, especially when such opposition is futile or leads to more harm

Examples

to kick against the pricks rules
He spent years kicking against the pricks instead of adapting to his new circumstances.
Sometimes, no matter how hard you kick against the pricks, change is unavoidable.
Trying to reform the system felt like kicking against the pricks, but she persisted nonetheless.

  • antonym – go with the flow: to accept a situation rather than opposing it
  • synonym – swim against the tide: to act or think in a way that goes against current trends or beliefs
