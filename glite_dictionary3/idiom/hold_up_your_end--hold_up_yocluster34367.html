to hold up your end

verb
fulfill your share of responsibilities
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ʌp jʊr ɛnd / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp jɔːr end / (BrE)
to fulfill your share of the responsibilities or obligations in a joint task or agreement

Examples

hold up your end of the deal

If you don't hold up your end, the whole project could fall apart.
She always holds up her end of the bargain, no matter how difficult the task.
