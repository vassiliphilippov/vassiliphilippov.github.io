to blow your mind

verb
to astonish or amaze
Pronunciation: / bloʊ jʊr maɪnd / (AmE), / bləʊ jɔː maɪnd / (BrE)
to astonish or profoundly impress someone; to cause someone to feel intense surprise or excitement

Examples

blow your mind with excitement
The special effects in the new movie will blow your mind.
Traveling to the Grand Canyon for the first time is sure to blow your mind.
When she told me her secret, it really blew my mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – astound: to surprise or shock someone very much
  • synonym – blow you away: to impress or surprise someone very strongly
  • antonym – underwhelm: to fail to impress or excite someone
