on your tod

adverb
alone; by yourself (British informal idiom)
Pronunciation: / ɑn jɔɹ tɑd / (AmE), / ɒn jɔː təd / (BrE)
Alone or by yourself, with no one else present or helping you.

Examples

on your tod all evening
After everyone left, I was stuck on my tod for the rest of the night.
He went to the cinema on his tod because no one else was free.
It's hard to finish this job on your tod without any help.
