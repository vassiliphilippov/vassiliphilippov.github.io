to take a leap

verb
make a bold or risky decision
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə liːp / (AmE), / teɪk ə liːp / (BrE)
to make a bold or risky decision; to take a chance or decisive action, often involving uncertainty or risk

Examples

to take a leap of faith
After years of doubting herself, Jenna finally decided to take a leap and move abroad for work.
If you want to start your own business, you have to take a leap at some point.
Sometimes you need to take a leap to discover what you are truly capable of.

Related Terms

  • antonym – play it safe: to avoid risks
  • synonym – take the plunge: to decide to do something important or risky, especially after hesitation
