to take a dump

verb
create a memory or database dump (computing)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə dʌmp / (AmE), / teɪk ə dʌmp / (BrE)
(in computing) to create a copy of memory, a process state, or a database (a 'dump') for debugging, analysis, or backup.

Examples

to take a dump of data
Before upgrading the server, the admin decided to take a dump of the database for safety.
When the app crashed, the engineer asked the tester to take a dump of memory immediately.
The monitoring script can take a dump on failure and upload it to the debugging server.

Related Terms

  • synonym – core dump: a file capturing a program's memory at a specific time, especially when it crashes
  • synonym – dump (computing): a saved copy of memory, process state, or database contents used for debugging or recovery
