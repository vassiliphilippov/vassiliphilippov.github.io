to trust your hunch

verb
rely on intuition
Pronunciation: / trʌst jʊr hʌntʃ / (AmE), / trʌst jɔː hʌntʃ / (BrE)
to rely on your intuition or instinct rather than on logic or evidence

Examples

to trust your hunch in decisions
If you can't decide, sometimes it's best to trust your hunch and go with your first choice.
She decided to trust her hunch and take a different route home, which turned out to be wise.
Whenever I'm uncertain, I usually trust my hunch rather than overthink the problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym – follow your instincts: act according to your natural intuition or feeling
  • antonym – ignore your intuition: to not act based on intuition or feelings
