at the coal face

adverb
directly involved in essential, practical work
Pronunciation: / ət ðə koʊl feɪs / (AmE), / ət ðə kəʊl feɪs / (BrE)
directly engaged in the most active, practical, or important part of a job or activity, especially where the real work is done

Examples

working at the coal face
As a nurse, she spends every day at the coal face caring for patients.
I've been at the coal face during this critical merger, handling the toughest negotiations.
Management often forgets what it's like at the coal face in busy retail stores.

Related Terms

  • antonym – behind the scenes: not in the main area of activity; working indirectly
  • synonym – on the front lines: actively engaged in the most important work or conflict
