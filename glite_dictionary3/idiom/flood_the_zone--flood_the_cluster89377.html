to flood the zone

verb
overwhelm by excess resources or activity
Pronunciation: / flʌd ðə zoʊn / (AmE), / flʌd ðə zəʊn / (BrE)
to inundate an area, group, or situation with a large quantity of something, often to achieve an advantage or overwhelm

Examples

flood the zone with messages
During the campaign, they decided to flood the zone with volunteers to increase visibility.
The company plans to flood the zone with advertisements in the weeks before the product launches.
To sway public opinion, officials chose to flood the zone with information from multiple sources.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overwhelm: to give someone too much of something; to overpower by amount or intensity
