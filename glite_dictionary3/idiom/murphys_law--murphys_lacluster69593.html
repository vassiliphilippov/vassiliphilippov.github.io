Murphy's Law

noun
if anything can go wrong, it will
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝː.fiz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.fiz lɔː / (BrE)
A humorous principle stating that if something can go wrong, it will go wrong.

Examples

Murphy's Law strikes again
According to Murphy's Law, the toast will always land butter-side down.
Travel delays are to be expected; after all, Murphy's Law is always lurking around the corner.
When the computer crashed just before the deadline, I could only laugh at Murphy's Law in action.
