in the same breath

adverb
almost simultaneously; unexpectedly together
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə seɪm brɛθ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə seɪm brɛθ / (BrE)
used to say that two things are said or done at almost the same time, often when they are surprising or contradictory

Examples

in the same breath praise
He apologized and made another complaint in the same breath.
She admired his intelligence but doubted his honesty in the same breath.
The manager promised improvements and warned about possible layoffs in the same breath.
He managed to encourage her and criticize her in the same breath.
The politician denied the accusation and in the same breath admitted his mistake.
You can’t say you’re too busy and in the same breath offer to help.
