to grab the bull by the horns

verb
face a challenge boldly
Pronunciation: / ɡræb ðə bʊl baɪ ðə hɔrnz / (AmE), / ɡræb ðə bʊl baɪ ðə hɔːnz / (BrE)
to confront a difficult situation or problem directly and with courage, rather than avoiding it

Examples

to grab the bull by the horns and act
If you want to succeed, sometimes you have to grab the bull by the horns and tackle problems head-on.
Rather than ignoring the issue, she chose to grab the bull by the horns and hold a difficult meeting with her team.
When faced with declining sales, the manager decided to grab the bull by the horns and overhaul the team's strategy.
