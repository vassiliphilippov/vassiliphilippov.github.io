to swear blind

verb
insist very firmly that something is true
Pronunciation: / swɛr blaɪnd / (AmE), / swɛə blaɪnd / (BrE)
to state something very firmly, often insisting it is true, even if others doubt it

Examples

to swear blind about facts
He will swear blind that he never received the email, even though we have proof.
I saw her leave the office, and I swear blind she wasn't carrying anything.
You can swear blind all you like, but I don't believe your story.

Related Terms

  • antonym – deny: to state that something is not true
  • synonym – insist: to demand something forcefully, not accepting refusal
