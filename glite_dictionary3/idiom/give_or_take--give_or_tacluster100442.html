give or take

adverb
plus or minus a small amount
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪv ər teɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪv ɔː teɪk / (BrE)
used to show that an amount, distance, or figure is approximate and may vary by a small amount; plus or minus a small amount.

Examples

give or take a few dollars
The new house cost $350,000, give or take a few thousand dollars.
The repairs will take three days, give or take a day.
There were about fifty people at the party, give or take a few.
