to take your hat off

verb
show respect or admiration
Pronunciation: / teɪk jɔr hæt ɑf / (AmE), / teɪk jɔː hæt ɒf / (BrE)
to show respect or admiration for someone's achievements or qualities

Examples

to take your hat off to someone
I really take your hat off to her for overcoming so many challenges.
If he manages to finish this project on time, I'll have to take my hat off to him.
We should all take our hats off to the volunteers who worked through the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym – doff your cap: to remove your hat as a gesture of respect; old-fashioned form
