patience of Job

noun
extreme patience and endurance in adversity
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪ.ʃəns əv dʒoʊb / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.ʃəns əv dʒəʊb / (BrE)
the ability to remain patient and endure difficulties or adversity for a prolonged period, alluding to the biblical figure Job who suffered great hardships without losing faith or patience

Examples

show patience of Job
He dealt with the delays with the patience of Job.
Her patience of Job was tested by the endless paperwork.
It takes the patience of Job to raise four young children.
