to cut down to size

verb
reduce someone's arrogance or self-importance
Pronunciation: / kʌt daʊn tə saɪz / (AmE), / kʌt daʊn tə saɪz / (BrE)
to reduce someone's arrogance or self-importance by showing they are not as impressive as they think they are

Examples

cut him down to size with jokes
After his big win, his friends quickly cut him down to size with some teasing remarks.
Sometimes, competitors need to be cut down to size to remind them not to underestimate others.
The new CEO was able to cut down to size some overly confident executives.
