to put to the test

verb
to challenge or evaluate
Pronunciation: / pʊt tə ðə tɛst / (AmE), / pʊt tə ðə tɛst / (BrE)
to challenge someone or something to prove ability, quality, or truth, often by subjecting them to a demanding situation or examination

Examples

put to the test under pressure
During her internship, her abilities were truly put to the test.
My resolve was put to the test by the difficult circumstances.
The new safety system will be put to the test during the upcoming inspection.

Related Terms

  • synonym – test: a procedure intended to establish the quality, performance, or reliability of something
  • synonym – try: to attempt or to put to a trial
