to break the Sabbath

verb
violate the Sabbath observance
Pronunciation: / breɪk ðə ˈsæbəθ / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ˈsæbəθ / (BrE)
to engage in activities that are traditionally forbidden on the Sabbath, such as working, thereby violating its observance

Examples

to break the Sabbath by working
He struggled with the decision to break the Sabbath by working on Sunday.
Some people choose to break the Sabbath for important family events.
They worried they would break the Sabbath if they traveled on that day.

Related Terms

  • antonym – keep the Sabbath: to observe the Sabbath as a day of rest and worship
