hurler on the ditch

noun
someone who criticizes but does not take part
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrlər ɑn ðə dɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːlə ɒn ðə dɪtʃ / (BrE)
a person who criticizes or complains about others' actions without actively participating or taking responsibility themselves

Examples

hurler on the ditch criticizes work
Instead of being a hurler on the ditch, why not help us finish the project?
It's easy to become a hurler on the ditch when you're not involved in the decision-making process.
Tired of all the hurlers on the ditch, she asked for constructive suggestions instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym – armchair critic: a person who offers advice or criticism from a position of non-involvement
  • antonym – participant: a person who takes part in an activity, the opposite of a bystander
