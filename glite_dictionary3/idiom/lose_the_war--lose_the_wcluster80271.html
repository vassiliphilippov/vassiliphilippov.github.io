to lose the war

verb
ultimately fail despite small victories
Pronunciation: / luːz ðə wɔr / (AmE), / luːz ðə wɔː / (BrE)
to ultimately fail or be defeated in the larger context, despite achieving smaller victories or successes along the way

Examples

to lose the war after battles
Don't get distracted by small setbacks; it's more important not to lose the war.
Their focus on short-term profits helped them initially, but they ultimately lost the war when the company collapsed.
You may win these little arguments, but in the end, you'll lose the war.
