in spite of appearance

adverb
despite how things look
Pronunciation: / ɪn spaɪt əv əˈpɪrəns / (AmE), / ɪn spaɪt ɒv əˈpɪərəns / (BrE)
despite how things may look; used to indicate that the reality differs from what is apparent

Examples

in spite of appearance reality differs
In spite of appearance, he is very generous with his time and money.
She seems strict, but in spite of appearance, she truly cares for her students.
The house looked abandoned, but in spite of appearance, a family still lived there.

Related Terms

  • synonym – actually: used to emphasize the real situation
  • antonym – seemingly: apparently but not necessarily so
