to ride the wave of popularity

verb
benefit from a surge in popularity
Pronunciation: / raɪd ðə weɪv əv pɑːpjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / raɪd ðə weɪv əv pɒpjʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
to benefit from a sudden or temporary increase in public attention or approval

Examples

to ride the wave of popularity after success
After her book won the award, she was able to ride the wave of popularity and secure several speaking engagements.
He is well aware that he can't ride the wave of popularity forever and needs to plan for the future.
The brand tried to ride the wave of popularity by launching new products while interest was still high.
