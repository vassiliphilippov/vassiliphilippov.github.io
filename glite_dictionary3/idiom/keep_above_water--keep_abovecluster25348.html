to keep above water

verb
manage to survive or cope
Pronunciation: / kip əˈbʌv ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / kiːp əˈbʌv ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to manage to survive or cope, especially with financial or personal difficulties

Examples

to keep above water during hardship
Despite mounting bills, she managed to keep above water by working two jobs.
He found it difficult to keep above water after losing his main source of income.
Many small businesses struggled to keep above water during the economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • antonym – go under: to fail or be unable to cope, especially financially
  • synonym – stay afloat: to remain financially or emotionally stable enough to survive
