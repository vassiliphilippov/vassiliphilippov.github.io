to make amends

verb
to compensate or repair after a wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / meɪk əˈmɛndz / (AmE), / meɪk əˈmɛndz / (BrE)
to do something to correct a mistake or bad situation, especially in order to repair a relationship or show regret

Examples

make amends for mistakes
After realizing her mistake, she tried to make amends with her coworkers.
He brought flowers in an effort to make amends for forgetting her birthday.
They finally decided to make amends after years of misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym – atone: to make up for a wrongdoing or sin
  • synonym – make reparations: to provide payment or other action to make up for wrongdoing
  • antonym – offend: to cause hurt or do wrong
