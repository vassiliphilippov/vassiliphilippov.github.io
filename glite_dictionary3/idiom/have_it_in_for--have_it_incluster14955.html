to have it in for

verb
to hold a grudge against someone
Pronunciation: / hæv ɪt ɪn fər / (AmE), / hæv ɪt ɪn fə / (BrE)
to be hostile toward someone or hold a grudge, wanting to harm, criticize, or cause trouble for them

Examples

It's obvious the teacher has it in for Jake because she never calls on him.
