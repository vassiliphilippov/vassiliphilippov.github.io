go on then

interjection
expression of encouragement or permission
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɑn ðɛn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn ðɛn / (BrE)
used to encourage someone to proceed or to give permission to do something, often after some hesitation or challenge.

Examples

go on then tell me
"If you really want to tell the story, go on then, I'm listening," she replied with a smile.
He wasn't sure if he should try the spicy sauce, so I said, "Go on then, give it a taste!"
They dared him to jump from the rock, and he finally agreed, saying, "Alright, go on then."
