to dig up dirt

verb
reveal damaging or scandalous information
Pronunciation: / dɪɡ ʌp dɜrt / (AmE), / dɪɡ ʌp dɜːt / (BrE)
to search for and reveal scandalous, damaging, or embarrassing information about someone, often with the intent of harming their reputation

Examples

to dig up dirt on a politician
Her rivals tried to dig up dirt to ruin her chances in the election.
It's wrong to dig up dirt about people just to create gossip.
Journalists sometimes dig up dirt on celebrities to boost their own ratings.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cover up: to hide or keep secret information, especially scandalous or damaging
  • synonym – dish the dirt: to gossip or reveal scandalous information
