to blow up in someone's face

verb
fail or backfire dramatically (plan, action)
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ʌp ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz feɪs / (AmE), / bləʊ ʌp ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz feɪs / (BrE)
(of a plan or action) to fail suddenly and dramatically, often with negative or unexpected consequences for the person responsible.

Examples

blow up in his face badly
His secret scheme blew up in his face when everyone found out about it.
She tried to surprise her team with a last-minute change, but it blew up in her face.
The risky investment plan blew up in their face, resulting in heavy losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backfire: (of a plan) to have the opposite effect to what was intended
  • antonym – succeed: to achieve the desired aim or result
