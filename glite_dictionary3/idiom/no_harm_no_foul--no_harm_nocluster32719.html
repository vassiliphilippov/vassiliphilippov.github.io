no harm, no foul

x
no damage or offense done
Pronunciation: / noʊ hɑrm noʊ faʊl / (AmE), / nəʊ hɑːm nəʊ faʊl / (BrE)
used to indicate that no real damage or offense has resulted from a mistake or accident, so there's no reason for anyone to be upset.

Examples

no harm, no foul after mistake
I borrowed your stapler by mistake, but since you weren't using it, no harm, no foul.
If you forgot to bring the book but nothing was lost, then it's no harm, no foul.
She accidentally knocked over his drink, but he just laughed and said, no harm, no foul.
