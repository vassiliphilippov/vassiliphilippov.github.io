to take the mickey

verb
tease or mock someone (often playfully)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə ˈmɪki / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈmɪki / (BrE)
to tease or make fun of someone, especially in a playful or light-hearted way

Examples

take the mickey out of friends
I wish you’d stop taking the mickey out of my accent.
She didn't realize they were just taking the mickey and meant no harm.
Whenever we play cards, he likes to take the mickey out of everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mock: to make fun of someone by imitating or ridiculing them
  • synonym – tease: to make fun of or provoke someone in a playful way
