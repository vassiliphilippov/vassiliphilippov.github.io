to raise hackles

verb
provoke anger or irritation
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪz ˈhækəlz / (AmE), / ˈreɪz ˈhækəlz / (BrE)
to cause someone to become angry or irritated; to provoke annoyance or hostile feelings

Examples

raise hackles with criticism
Bringing up that subject is sure to raise hackles at the dinner table.
His dismissive remarks raised hackles among the committee members.
The company's decision to cut benefits has raised hackles throughout the workforce.

Related Terms

  • antonym – calm: to make someone less angry or upset
  • synonym – provoke: to make someone angry, especially deliberately
