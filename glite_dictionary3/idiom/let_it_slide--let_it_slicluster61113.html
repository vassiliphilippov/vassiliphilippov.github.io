to let it slide

verb
ignore or overlook a minor offense
Pronunciation: / lɛt ɪt slaɪd / (AmE), / lɛt ɪt slaɪd / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or overlook a mistake, fault, or minor problem, often to avoid confrontation or because it is not important

Examples

let it slide after mistake
Although he was late again, she decided to let it slide this time.
If your coworker makes a minor error, it's sometimes best to let it slide.
You can't just let it slide every time someone breaks the rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym – forgive: to stop feeling angry or resentful towards someone for an offense or mistake
  • synonym – overlook: to fail to notice or ignore something, especially a fault or offense
  • antonym – punish: to impose a penalty on someone for wrongdoing; not letting it slide
