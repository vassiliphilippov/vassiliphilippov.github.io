just in case

adverb
as a precaution
Pronunciation: / dʒʌst ɪn keɪs / (AmE), / dʒʌst ɪn keɪs / (BrE)
as a precaution; to be prepared for something that might happen

Examples

just in case of rain
He set two alarms just in case he didn't wake up to the first.
I always carry an umbrella, just in case it rains unexpectedly.
She brought her passport, just in case she needed to show identification.
Bring a sweater just in case it gets cold tonight.
I always keep some extra cash in my wallet just in case.
Take your umbrella just in case it rains later.
