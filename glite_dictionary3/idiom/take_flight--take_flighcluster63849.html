to take flight

verb
run away suddenly; escape quickly
Pronunciation: / teɪk flaɪt / (AmE), / teɪk flaɪt / (BrE)
to escape or run away quickly, often to avoid danger or trouble

Examples

take flight from danger
Alarmed by the sudden noise, the deer took flight into the woods.
Seeing the approaching storm, the birds took flight to find shelter.
When the police arrived, the thieves took flight before anyone could catch them.
As soon as the alarm rang, the thieves took flight into the darkness.
Hundreds of butterflies take flight when the weather grows warm.
The birds take flight at the slightest sound in the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym – escape: to get away from a place where you are being held or from a dangerous situation
  • synonym – flee: to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym – land: to come down and rest on the ground
