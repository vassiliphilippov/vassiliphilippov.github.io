in and of itself

adverb
by itself; intrinsically
Pronunciation: / ɪn ənd ʌv ɪtˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ɪn ənd ɒv ɪtˈsɛlf / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is considered alone, without considering other factors

Examples

in and of itself important
A high salary, in and of itself, does not guarantee job satisfaction.
Her art, in and of itself, was not controversial, but her statements sparked debate.
The law is not unjust in and of itself, but its application has caused problems.
Success, in and of itself, does not guarantee happiness.
The policy, in and of itself, is not harmful, but it could have unintended effects.
This solution, in and of itself, does not address the root of the problem.
