on the shelf

adverb
postponed or set aside
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə ʃɛlf / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ʃɛlf / (BrE)
postponed or set aside, especially for a period of time, often implying neglect or delay

Examples

on the shelf for years
Many of his ambitious ideas remained on the shelf due to lack of funding.
She felt her career had been left on the shelf after the restructuring.
The committee decided to keep the proposal on the shelf until next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym – active: currently being considered or pursued
  • synonym – put on ice: postpone or delay something
  • synonym – sideline: to remove from active participation or consideration
