to cast a shadow

verb
negatively influence; cause doubt or gloom
Pronunciation: / kæst ə ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / kɑːst ə ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
to have a negative or troubling effect on a person, event, or situation, often causing doubt, sadness, or uncertainty

Examples

cast a shadow over plans
Concerns about funding cast a shadow on the project from the very beginning.
Her sudden departure cast a shadow on what was meant to be a happy reunion.
The scandal cast a shadow over the team's victory, dampening the celebrations.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overshadow: to make something less impressive or successful by comparison or association
  • synonym – taint: to spoil or diminish the quality of something
