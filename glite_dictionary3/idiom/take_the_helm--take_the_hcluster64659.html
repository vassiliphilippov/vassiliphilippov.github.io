to take the helm

verb
assume control or leadership
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə hɛlm / (AmE), / teɪk ðə helm / (BrE)
to assume control or leadership of an organization, group, or project

Examples

to take the helm of a company
After the founder stepped aside, Sarah was chosen to take the helm of the company.
The new coach is expected to take the helm and guide the team to victory.
When a crisis hits, someone must take the helm and make tough decisions.
