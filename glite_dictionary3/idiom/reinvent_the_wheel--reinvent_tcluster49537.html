to reinvent the wheel

verb
waste effort duplicating existing solutions
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.ɪnˈvɛnt ðə wiːl / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˈvɛnt ðə wiːl / (BrE)
to waste time and effort creating something that already exists or has already been done effectively

Examples

to reinvent the wheel at work
Let’s not reinvent the wheel when we can use this existing software for our project.
She realized she was reinventing the wheel and decided to join the group instead.
The new manager warned his team against reinventing the wheel and encouraged them to study existing models.

Related Terms

  • synonym – duplicate: to make an exact copy of something; often unnecessarily
  • antonym – streamline: to simplify a process by eliminating unnecessary work
