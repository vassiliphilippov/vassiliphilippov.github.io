finders keepers

x
whoever finds something may keep it
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪndərz ˈkipərz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndəz ˈkiːpəz / (BrE)
used to say that whoever finds something has the right to keep it, especially when it is lost or abandoned

Examples

finders keepers with lost wallet
After she spotted the dropped ticket, she joked, "finders keepers!" and put it in her pocket.
The children argued over the toy, but the oldest insisted, "finders keepers."
When he discovered the wallet on the bench, he said, "Well, finders keepers, I guess!"

Related Terms

  • antonym – losers weepers: phrase often used in contrast to 'finders keepers', implying those who lose something must accept the loss
