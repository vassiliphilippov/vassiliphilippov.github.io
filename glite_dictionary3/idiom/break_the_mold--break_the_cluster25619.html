to break the mold

verb
do something completely new or different
Pronunciation: / breɪk ðə moʊld / (AmE), / breɪk ðə məʊld / (BrE)
to do something in a completely new, original, or innovative way; to depart from tradition or standard practice

Examples

to break the mold with ideas
By starting his own business at 18, he decided to break the mold rather than follow a conventional path.
Her new novel truly breaks the mold of traditional storytelling.
The company hopes to break the mold with its revolutionary approach to sustainability.

Related Terms

  • antonym – follow the crowd: to do what most other people do, without originality
  • synonym – innovate: to introduce new ideas or methods
