to lose one's life

verb
to die; to be killed
Pronunciation: / luːz wʌnz laɪf / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz laɪf / (BrE)
to die, especially as a result of an accident, violence, or disaster

Examples

lose one's life in battle
Many soldiers lost their lives in the conflict.
Rescue workers risked everything so that no one would lose their life in the fire.
The explorer nearly lost his life crossing the jungle.
