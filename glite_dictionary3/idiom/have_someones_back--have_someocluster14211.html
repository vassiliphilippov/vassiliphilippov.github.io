to have (someone's) back

verb
to support or protect someone
Pronunciation: / hæv maɪ bæk / (AmE), / hæv maɪ bæk / (BrE)
to be ready to support or protect someone, especially in difficult situations

Examples

have your back in trouble
Good friends always have your back when things go wrong.
She promised to have his back during the negotiation.
Whenever I feel unsure, I know you have my back.

Related Terms

  • synonym – stand by (someone): to remain loyal or supportive to someone
