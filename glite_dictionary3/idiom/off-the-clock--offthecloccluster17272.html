off-the-clock

adverb
work done outside paid hours
Pronunciation: / ˌɔf ðə ˈklɑk / (AmE), / ˌɒf ðə ˈklɒk / (BrE)
(of work) done outside official paid hours and without extra compensation.

Examples

off-the-clock work tasks
Laws protect workers from being forced to do tasks off-the-clock.
Many employees are pressured to handle emails off-the-clock after regular office hours.
She reported that she was routinely asked to work off-the-clock without payment.
