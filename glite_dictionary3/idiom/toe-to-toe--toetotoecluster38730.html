toe-to-toe

adverb
direct confrontation or close combat
Pronunciation: / toʊ tə toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ tə təʊ / (BrE)
at close quarters; directly or fiercely confronting an opponent, especially in a contest or argument

Examples

toe-to-toe with a rival
Negotiators met toe-to-toe to resolve the contentious issues.
The boxers stood toe-to-toe, exchanging punches without backing down.
The two candidates went toe-to-toe in a heated debate broadcast nationwide.
