everything in sight

noun
all things visible (often acted upon)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvriˌθɪŋ ɪn saɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛvriˌθɪŋ ɪn sʌɪt / (BrE)
Used to refer to all things that can be seen, especially when someone is acting on every available option or item.

Examples

eat everything in sight
After the race, the hungry athletes devoured everything in sight at the banquet.
During the blackout, looters grabbed everything in sight from the stores.
The children were so excited at the fair that they wanted to try everything in sight.
