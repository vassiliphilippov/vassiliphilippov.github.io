to sit tight

verb
wait without taking action
Pronunciation: / sɪt taɪt / (AmE), / sɪt taɪt / (BrE)
to remain in the same situation or position without taking action; to wait patiently and not make changes

Examples

to sit tight and wait
He advised us to sit tight until new instructions were given.
The best thing to do during the storm is to sit tight and wait for it to pass.
They decided to sit tight rather than invest their money right away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – wait: to stay where one is or delay action until a particular time or event
