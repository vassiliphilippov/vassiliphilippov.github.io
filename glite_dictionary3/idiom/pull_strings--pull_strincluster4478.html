to pull strings

verb
use influence or connections secretly
Pronunciation: / pʊl strɪŋz / (AmE), / pʊl strɪŋz / (BrE)
to use your influence or personal connections to secretly control or achieve something

Examples

to pull strings for a job
He has a reputation for pulling strings whenever he wants things done his way.
She managed to get her son admitted to the school by pulling strings at the education board.
They suspect someone in management pulled strings to secure the contract for their friend.
He managed to get the promotion by pulling strings with his superiors.
If you want to get backstage passes, you’ll have to pull strings.
She admitted that she had pulled strings to secure her son's university place.
