to cut bait

verb
stop wasting time and act or give up
Pronunciation: / kʌt ˈbeɪt / (AmE), / kʌt ˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to stop wasting time and either take decisive action or abandon an unproductive effort

Examples

to cut bait and act
After months of delays, the boss told his team to cut bait and move forward with another plan.
He realized the project was going nowhere and decided to cut bait before wasting further resources.
If this partnership isn’t working, maybe it’s time to cut bait and focus elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym – give up: to stop trying to do something difficult
  • synonym – take action: to do something to achieve a result rather than hesitate or delay
