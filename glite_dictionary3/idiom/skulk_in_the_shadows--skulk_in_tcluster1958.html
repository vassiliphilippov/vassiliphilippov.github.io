to skulk in the shadows

verb
move or hide furtively to avoid notice
Pronunciation: / skʌlk ɪn ðə ˈʃædoʊz / (AmE), / skʌlk ɪn ðə ˈʃædəʊz / (BrE)
to move or hide in a secretive or furtive manner, typically in order to avoid being seen or attracting attention

Examples

to skulk in the shadows quietly
At the party, she preferred to skulk in the shadows instead of mingling with the other guests.
He would often skulk in the shadows, observing the passersby without being noticed.
Thieves tend to skulk in the shadows, waiting for the right moment to strike.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lurk: to remain hidden so as to wait in ambush
  • synonym – slink: to move smoothly and quietly with gliding steps, in a stealthy or sensuous manner
