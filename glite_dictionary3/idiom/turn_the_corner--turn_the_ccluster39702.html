to turn the corner

verb
begin to improve after difficulty
Pronunciation: / tɝn ðə ˈkɔrnɚ / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
to begin to improve after a difficult period or to recover from a setback or illness

Examples

turn the corner after trouble
After months of slow sales, the company finally turned the corner and started making a profit.
Following surgery, her doctor said she had turned the corner and would recover quickly.
The team turned the corner after a string of losses, winning several matches in a row.

  • antonym – deteriorate: to become worse in condition or quality
  • synonym – recover: to return to a normal state after a setback or illness
