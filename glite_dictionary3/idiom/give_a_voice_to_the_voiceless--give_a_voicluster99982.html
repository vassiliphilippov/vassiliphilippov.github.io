to give a voice to the voiceless

verb
to advocate for those unable to speak out
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ə vɔɪs tu ðə ˈvɔɪsləs / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə vɔɪs tuː ðə ˈvɔɪslɪs / (BrE)
to represent, speak up for, or advocate on behalf of those who are marginalized or unable to express themselves

Examples

give a voice to the voiceless in society
Her documentary helped give a voice to the voiceless by sharing the stories of marginalized workers.
Journalists often feel a responsibility to give a voice to the voiceless in their reporting.
The organization aims to give a voice to the voiceless through community outreach programs.

  • synonym – stand up for: to defend or support someone or something
