to drink from a firehose

verb
be overwhelmed by too much information
Pronunciation: / drɪŋk frəm ə ˈfaɪərˌhoʊz / (AmE), / drɪŋk frəm ə ˈfaɪəhəʊz / (BrE)
to be overwhelmed by receiving too much information or too many tasks at once, more than can be processed or handled

Examples

to drink from a firehose at work
During my first week at the new company, I felt like I had to drink from a firehose to keep up.
Graduate school can feel like drinking from a firehose because there is so much to learn so quickly.
She joked that onboarding to the team was like drinking from a firehose due to the sudden influx of information.
