to go up in smoke

verb
fail completely; vanish suddenly (idiom)
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ʌp ɪn smoʊk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ʌp ɪn sməʊk / (BrE)
(of plans, efforts, etc.) to fail completely or disappear suddenly and irretrievably.

Examples

go up in smoke after hard work
After the company lost its biggest client, all their expansion plans went up in smoke.
Her dreams of becoming a professional dancer went up in smoke after she injured her knee.
When the deal fell through, their hopes for a quick profit went up in smoke.

Related Terms

  • synonym – fail: to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • antonym – succeed: to achieve the desired result
