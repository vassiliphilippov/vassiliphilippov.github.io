off your guard

adjective
not prepared or vigilant
Pronunciation: / ɑf jʊr ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒf jɔː ɡɑːd / (BrE)
not prepared to deal with something; not paying attention to possible danger or surprise

Examples

caught off your guard
Don’t let them catch you off your guard during the negotiations.

The question caught him off his guard, and he didn’t know how to respond.
