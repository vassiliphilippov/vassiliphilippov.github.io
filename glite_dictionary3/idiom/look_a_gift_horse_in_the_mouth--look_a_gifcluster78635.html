to look a gift horse in the mouth

verb
question the value of a gift
Pronunciation: / lʊk ə ˈɡɪft hɔrs ɪn ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / lʊk ə ˈɡɪft hɔːs ɪn ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to find fault with something that has been received as a gift or to question its value instead of accepting it gratefully

Examples

look a gift horse in the mouth after receiving help
She may not like the sweater, but it's ungrateful to look a gift horse in the mouth.
The company gave us free tickets, so let's not look a gift horse in the mouth and just enjoy the show.
You shouldn't look a gift horse in the mouth when someone is offering you help.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bite the hand that feeds you: to act against someone who has helped you
