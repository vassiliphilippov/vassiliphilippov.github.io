off the books

adverb
not officially recorded or reported
Pronunciation: / ɑf ðə bʊks / (AmE), / ɒf ðə bʊks / (BrE)
(of financial transactions, employment, etc.) not officially recorded or reported, typically to avoid taxes or regulations.

Examples

off the books payment
He bought the used car off the books to avoid filling out paperwork.
Many small businesses pay some employees off the books to reduce payroll expenses.
Running a company off the books puts both the employer and workers at risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym – under the table: secretly and illegally, especially to avoid taxes
