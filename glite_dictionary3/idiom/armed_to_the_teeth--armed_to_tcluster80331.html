armed to the teeth

adjective
very heavily equipped or prepared
Pronunciation: / ɑrmd tə ðə tiθ / (AmE), / ɑːmd tə ðə tiːθ / (BrE)
equipped with a great amount of weapons or resources; fully prepared or ready for something, often in an excessive way

Examples

armed to the teeth with weapons
Before the camping trip, she made sure she was armed to the teeth with supplies.
The hackers were armed to the teeth with sophisticated software and tools.
The security guards were armed to the teeth during the high-profile event.
