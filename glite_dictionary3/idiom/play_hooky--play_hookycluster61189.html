to play hooky

verb
skip school or work without permission
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ˈhʊki / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈhʊki / (BrE)
to stay away from school or work without permission or a valid reason

Examples

She decided to play hooky on Friday and spend the day at the amusement park instead.
When I was a teenager, I used to play hooky whenever there was a big math test.
