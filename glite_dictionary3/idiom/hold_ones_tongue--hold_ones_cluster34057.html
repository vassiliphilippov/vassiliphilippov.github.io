to hold one's tongue

verb
remain silent; refrain from speaking
Pronunciation: / hoʊld wʌnz tʌŋ / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz tʌŋ / (BrE)
to remain silent or stop oneself from speaking, often to avoid conflict or saying something inappropriate

Examples

hold his tongue during arguments
Despite wanting to complain, he chose to hold his tongue during the meeting.
If you can't say anything nice, it's better to hold your tongue.
She managed to hold her tongue even though she disagreed strongly.

Related Terms

  • synonym – keep quiet: to refrain from speaking
  • antonym – speak out: to express one's opinion openly
