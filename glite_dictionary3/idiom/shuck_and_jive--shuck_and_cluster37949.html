to shuck and jive

verb
to speak evasively or insincerely
Pronunciation: / ʃʌk ənd dʒaɪv / (AmE), / ʃʌk ənd dʒaɪv / (BrE)
to talk or behave evasively or insincerely, especially in order to avoid answering directly or to deceive

Examples

to shuck and jive questions
Instead of answering directly, he just tried to shuck and jive his way through the interview.
The politician began to shuck and jive when asked about the controversial policy.
Tired of all his shuck and jive, the manager insisted on a straightforward explanation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – come clean: to admit the truth without evasion or deceit
  • synonym – evade: to avoid giving a direct answer
  • synonym – prevaricate: to speak or act in an evasive way
