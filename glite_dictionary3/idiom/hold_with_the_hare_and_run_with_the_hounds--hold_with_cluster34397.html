to hold with the hare and run with the hounds

verb
support two opposing sides at once
Pronunciation: / hoʊld wɪð ðə hɛr ənd rʌn wɪð ðə haʊndz / (AmE), / həʊld wɪð ðə heə ənd rʌn wɪð ðə haʊndz / (BrE)
to try to support or please two opposing sides at the same time, often in a way that is insincere or opportunistic

Examples

to hold with the hare and run with the hounds in politics
Her attempt to hold with the hare and run with the hounds left both sides distrustful of her motives.
If you try to hold with the hare and run with the hounds, you risk displeasing everyone in the end.
It is impossible to hold with the hare and run with the hounds during a heated political debate.
