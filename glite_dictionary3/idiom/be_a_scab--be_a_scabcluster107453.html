to be a scab

verb
work during a strike, betray strikers
Pronunciation: / bi ə skæb / (AmE), / biː ə skæb / (BrE)
to work during a strike, thereby betraying one's fellow workers who are striking

Examples

to be a scab during strike
If you be a scab, you undermine the strength of the strike.
She refused to be a scab, choosing instead to join the picket line.
The union warned members not to be a scab during contract negotiations.
