to strain at a gnat

verb
focus on minor details, ignore bigger issues
Pronunciation: / streɪn ət ə næt / (AmE), / streɪn ət ə næt / (BrE)
to pay excessive attention to trivial details while ignoring more important issues

Examples

strain at a gnat over rules
It's easy to strain at a gnat when you're nervous about making mistakes.
The committee tends to strain at a gnat instead of addressing the root problems.
We shouldn't strain at a gnat during this project and overlook the major deadline.
