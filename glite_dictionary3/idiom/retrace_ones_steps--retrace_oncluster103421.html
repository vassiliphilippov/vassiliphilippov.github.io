to retrace one's steps

verb
go back over previous path or actions
Pronunciation: / riˈtreɪs wʌnz stɛps / (AmE), / riˈtreɪs wʌnz stɛps / (BrE)
to go back over the same path or actions that one has previously taken, often to find something lost or reconsider one's actions

Examples

to retrace one's steps to find keys
After realizing he had made a mistake, he decided to retrace his steps and check his work.
I had to retrace my steps through the park to find my missing wallet.
If you can't remember where you put your glasses, try retracing your steps from this morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym – backtrack: to return by the same route one has just taken
