to throw the book at

verb
punish as severely as possible
Pronunciation: / ˌθroʊ ðə ˈbʊk æt / (AmE), / ˌθrəʊ ðə ˈbʊk æt / (BrE)
to punish someone as severely as possible or to impose the maximum penalty allowed

Examples

throw the book at a criminal
After the scandal, the league decided to throw the book at the offending team.
If you break the law again, the judge might throw the book at you.
Regulators vowed to throw the book at companies that break environmental regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym – let off lightly: to punish someone less severely than expected
