to beat to a pulp

verb
injure badly or defeat thoroughly
Pronunciation: / bit tə ə pʌlp / (AmE), / biːt tuː ə pʌlp / (BrE)
to injure someone severely by hitting them repeatedly, or to defeat someone very thoroughly

Examples

beat to a pulp an enemy
If you don't stop, I'll beat you to a pulp!
She felt as if her ideas had been beaten to a pulp by the critics.
The champion beat his opponent to a pulp in the final round of the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose: to be defeated; opposite of beating thoroughly
  • synonym – mangle: to injure or damage badly by beating or crushing
  • synonym – thrash: to defeat someone decisively, often physically or in competition
