to be a weasel

verb
act deceitfully or evasively
Pronunciation: / bi ə ˈwizəl / (AmE), / biː ə ˈwiːzl̩ / (BrE)
to be cunning, deceitful, or evasive in order to avoid blame or responsibility

Examples

be a weasel about blame
Sarah realized Mark was being a weasel about his involvement with the missing files.
Whenever there's work to be done, he tends to be a weasel and avoid responsibility.
You shouldn’t be a weasel when telling the truth could help everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be honest: to act in a truthful and straightforward way
  • synonym – be sneaky: to behave in a secretive or dishonest manner
