to follow the thread

verb
understand the main idea or argument
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊ ðə θrɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ðə θrɛd / (BrE)
to understand or keep track of the main argument, idea, or line of reasoning in something such as a story, conversation, or explanation.

Examples

to follow the thread of a story
During the heated debate, it was difficult to follow the thread of the conversation.
If you don't pay attention in class, you may not follow the thread of the lecture.
She tried hard to follow the thread of the professor's complex explanation.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose the thread: fail to understand, remember, or keep track of the main point
