to go to heck

verb
tell someone to go away (milder form)
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tə hɛk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə hɛk / (BrE)
(informal) Used as a mild or euphemistic way to tell someone to go away, expressing anger or dismissal.

Examples

to go to heck angrily
After hearing the rude remark, Mark told his friend to go to heck and walked away.
She laughed off the criticism and said everyone could just go to heck.
When Jim refused to cooperate, his boss finally told him to go to heck.

Related Terms

  • synonym – go to hell: a strong way of telling someone to go away or expressing anger
