silence conveys meaning or intention
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪləns du ðə ˈtɔkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns də ðə ˈtɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
used to express the idea that remaining silent can communicate meaning, feelings, or intentions as effectively as words

Examples

silence do the talking in meetings
After his unexpected resignation, his silence do the talking about his disappointment with the company.
In the tense meeting, her silence do the talking, making everyone aware of her disapproval.
Sometimes, you don't have to respond; just letting silence do the talking is enough.
