to turn one's back on

verb
to ignore or abandon someone or something
Pronunciation: / tɝn wʌnz bæk ɑn / (AmE), / tɜːn wʌnz bæk ɒn / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or refuse to be involved with someone or something; to abandon or reject.

Examples

turn his back on old friends
After many years at the company, he decided to turn his back on corporate life and travel the world.
She couldn't turn her back on her old friends when they needed help the most.
When things got tough, some people chose to turn their back on the project and walk away.

Related Terms

  • synonym – abandon: to leave someone or something completely
  • antonym – support: to help or give approval to someone or something
