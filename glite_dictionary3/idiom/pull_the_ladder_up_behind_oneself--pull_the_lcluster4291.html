to pull the ladder up behind oneself

verb
prevent others from accessing the same opportunities
Pronunciation: / pʊl ðə ˈlædɚ ʌp bɪˈhaɪnd wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / pʊl ðə ˈlædə ʌp bɪˈhaɪnd wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to achieve success and then prevent others from having the same opportunities, especially by withdrawing support or access

Examples

pull the ladder up behind oneself after success
After becoming a manager, he pulled the ladder up behind himself and stopped promoting junior staff.
She was accused of pulling the ladder up behind herself by opposing scholarships for new students.
Some politicians support social mobility until they succeed, then pull the ladder up behind themselves.
