to grit one's teeth

verb
endure difficulty with determination
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪt wʌnz tiθ / (AmE), / ɡrɪt wʌnz tiːθ / (BrE)
to face pain, difficulty, or unpleasantness with determination and resolve, often by mentally preparing oneself to endure it

Examples

grit one's teeth and continue
Despite her fear, she had to grit her teeth and continue the speech.
He gritted his teeth as the dentist worked on his cavity.
You just have to grit your teeth until the situation improves.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give up: to stop trying to do something difficult
  • synonym – persevere: to keep going despite difficulties
