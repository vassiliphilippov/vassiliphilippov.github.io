to sit on a decision

verb
delay making a decision
Pronunciation: / sɪt ɑn ə dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / sɪt ɒn ə dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
to delay or postpone making a decision, often by purposefully holding off instead of acting promptly

Examples

to sit on a decision for days
He tends to sit on a decision for days before finally giving an answer.
The manager has been sitting on a decision about the new project proposal.
We can't afford to sit on a decision when the deadline is so close.
