to take heart

verb
gain courage or encouragement
Pronunciation: / teɪk hɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk hɑːt / (BrE)
to become encouraged or gain confidence, especially when facing difficulties or adversity

Examples

take heart after bad news
As the storm passed, the villagers began to take heart and rebuild their homes.
Fans can take heart from the team's strong performance in the second half.
Though she failed the exam, her friends told her to take heart and try again.

Related Terms

  • synonym – encourage: to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • antonym – lose heart: to lose confidence or become discouraged
