to sell a bill of goods

verb
to deceive or mislead someone
Pronunciation: / sɛl ə bɪl əv ɡʊdz / (AmE), / sɛl ə bɪl əv ɡʊdz / (BrE)
to deceive or mislead someone, especially by persuading them to accept something false or undesirable as if it were valuable or true.

Examples

sell a bill of goods about a job
He realized too late that his friend had sold him a bill of goods about the investment opportunity.
Politicians sometimes attempt to sell a bill of goods when making unrealistic campaign promises.
The company tried to sell a bill of goods to unsuspecting customers with their misleading advertisements.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deceive: to make someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym – mislead: to cause someone to have the wrong idea or impression
