to get one's finger out

verb
start acting after procrastination (British, informal)
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡə aʊt / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) To start acting promptly after delay or procrastination; to begin to make an effort.

Examples

to get your finger out and work
He finally got his finger out and cleaned the garage after weeks of putting it off.
If you don't get your finger out, we'll miss the deadline.
The team needs to get their finger out and start practicing harder if they want to win.

Related Terms

  • synonym – get going: to start moving or begin to do something
  • antonym – procrastinate: to delay or postpone action; put off doing something
