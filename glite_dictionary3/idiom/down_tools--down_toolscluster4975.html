to down tools

verb
stop working, often in protest
Pronunciation: / daʊn tuːlz / (AmE), / daʊn tuːlz / (BrE)
(of workers) to stop working, especially as a form of protest or industrial action.

Examples

down tools in protest
After the announcement of pay cuts, the workers decided to down tools until management addressed their concerns.
At noon, the foreman told everyone to down tools and take their lunch break.
During negotiations, union leaders threatened to down tools if their demands were not met.
