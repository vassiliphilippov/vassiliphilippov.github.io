to carry a tune in a bucket

verb
sing very badly; be tone-deaf
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ə tun ɪn ə ˈbʌkət / (AmE), / ˈkæri ə tjuːn ɪn ə ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
(informal, usually with 'can't') to sing with very poor pitch or musical accuracy; to be unable to sing well.

Examples

can't carry a tune in a bucket
Despite years of choir practice, Janice still can't carry a tune in a bucket.
He knows he can't carry a tune in a bucket, but he enjoys singing at parties anyway.
My grandfather used to say he couldn't carry a tune in a bucket, yet he hummed happily every morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym – sing like a nightingale: to sing very well and melodiously
  • synonym – sing out of tune: to sing incorrectly with respect to pitch; not in tune
