to get in gear

verb
start working effectively
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɪn ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ɡɪə / (BrE)
to begin to work effectively or with more energy; to start becoming productive or active

Examples

After a slow start, the team finally got in gear and scored three goals.
It's already noon; let's get in gear and clean the house.
You need to get in gear if you want to finish this project on time.
