in the shadow of

preposition
under influence or comparison of someone/something
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə ˈʃædoʊ ʌv / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈʃædəʊ əv / (BrE)
living under the influence, dominance, or comparison of someone or something, often to one's disadvantage; sometimes used literally for being physically close to or under something large.

Examples

in the shadow of a famous person
After joining the company, she found herself working in the shadow of a legendary manager.
He always felt he was in the shadow of his older brother, who excelled at everything.
The small village lies in the shadow of the ancient castle on the hill.

Related Terms

  • synonym – overshadowed: affected or dominated by someone or something stronger or more prominent
  • synonym – under the thumb of: completely controlled or dominated by someone
