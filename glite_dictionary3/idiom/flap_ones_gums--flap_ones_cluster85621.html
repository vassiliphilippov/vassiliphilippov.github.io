to flap one's gums

verb
talk at length, often trivially
Pronunciation: / flæp wʌnz ɡʌmz / (AmE), / flæp wʌnz ɡʌmz / (BrE)
to talk a lot, especially about unimportant matters; to gossip or chatter excessively

Examples

to flap one's gums about rumors
He spent the whole meeting just flapping his gums instead of addressing the real issue.
Instead of flapping your gums, why don't you listen for a change?
They were flapping their gums about the latest office rumors all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be quiet: to stop talking; to be silent
  • synonym – chatter: to talk rapidly, especially about trivial matters
  • synonym – gossip: to engage in idle talk, especially about others
