to make a virtue of necessity

verb
find advantage in necessity
Pronunciation: / meɪk ə ˈvɝːtʃu əv nəˈsɛsəti / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈvɜːtʃuː əv nɪˈsɛsəti / (BrE)
to gain some advantage or find something good in a situation one is obliged to face, especially by adapting positively to it

Examples

to make a virtue of necessity at work
Instead of complaining about his workload, he chose to make a virtue of necessity and improved his skills.
The company had to reduce costs, but they managed to make a virtue of necessity by developing more efficient processes.
When her travel plans fell through, she decided to make a virtue of necessity and explored local attractions.
