to extend an olive branch

verb
make an offer of peace or reconciliation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnd ən ˈɑlɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnd ən ˈɒlɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
to make an offer of peace or reconciliation to someone after a disagreement

Examples

to extend an olive branch after conflict
After months of tension, the president decided to extend an olive branch to the opposition party.
In an effort to rebuild their friendship, Sarah called to extend an olive branch.
Sometimes, it takes courage to extend an olive branch and admit mistakes.
