to shake off the dust from one's feet

verb
depart with finality; move on after negativity
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪk ɑf ðə dʌst frəm wʌnz fit / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ɒf ðə dʌst frəm wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation, often after a negative experience, with no intention of returning or maintaining ties; to move on resolutely

Examples

to shake off the dust from one's feet after trouble
After the bitter dispute, she chose to shake off the dust from her feet and never looked back.
He advised them to shake off the dust from their feet and focus on new opportunities.
Many travelers shake off the dust from their feet when they leave a disappointing town behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym – let go: to stop holding onto or being affected by something negative
  • synonym – move on: to proceed to the next stage or activity; to leave something behind
