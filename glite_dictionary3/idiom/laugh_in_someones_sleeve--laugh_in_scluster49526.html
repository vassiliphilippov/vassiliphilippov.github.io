to laugh in someone's sleeve

verb
secretly find something amusing
Pronunciation: / læf ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz sliːv / (AmE), / lɑːf ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz sliːv / (BrE)
to secretly find something amusing or take quiet pleasure in someone else's misfortune or mistake without showing it openly

Examples

to laugh in her sleeve quietly
Although she kept a straight face, she was clearly laughing in her sleeve during the meeting.
He didn't react, but secretly he was laughing in his sleeve at his rival's blunder.
They tried to hide it, but everyone could tell they were laughing in their sleeves at the situation.
