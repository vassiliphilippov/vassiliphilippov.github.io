to hit the wall

verb
reach a state of exhaustion or blockage
Pronunciation: / hɪt ðə wɔl / (AmE), / hɪt ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
to reach a point at which one is unable to continue or make progress, especially due to exhaustion or a sudden barrier

Examples

hit the wall from tiredness
After hours of discussing the problem, the team finally hit the wall and took a break.
I hit the wall creatively and couldn't complete the final chapter of my book.
Many runners hit the wall around the twentieth mile of a marathon.

Related Terms

  • antonym – break through: to overcome an obstacle or barrier, often after persistence
  • synonym – burn out: to become extremely tired or exhausted, especially from overwork
