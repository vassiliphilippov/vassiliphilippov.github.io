in a rut

adjective
stuck in a monotonous situation
Pronunciation: / ɪn ə rʌt / (AmE), / ɪn ə rʌt / (BrE)
stuck in a monotonous, unproductive, or unchanging situation, especially for a period of time

Examples

feel in a rut
After years of the same commute and work, he realized he was in a rut.
Changing daily routines can help if you’re in a rut and feeling unmotivated.
Lately, I feel in a rut at my job and need some excitement.

Related Terms

  • degree higher – burned out: exhausted and unproductive due to prolonged stress or monotony
  • degree lower – in a groove: finding something easy, often through repeated action; can be a positive or negative habitual state
  • synonym – stuck: unable to move or progress
