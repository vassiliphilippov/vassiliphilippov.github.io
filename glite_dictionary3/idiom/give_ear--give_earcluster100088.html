to give ear

verb
listen carefully; pay attention (archaic/literary)
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪə / (BrE)
to listen carefully or pay attention (especially to what someone is saying)

Examples

to give ear to advice
He gave no sign that he would give ear to their complaints.
The crowd stood in silence, giving ear to the speaker's passionate words.
You would be wise to give ear to the advice of your elders.
It’s wise to give ear to constructive criticism when trying to improve.
She urged her colleagues to give ear to the new ideas presented.
The council members did not give ear to the community's demands during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – ignore: to deliberately pay no attention to
  • synonym – listen: to give attention to sound or to someone speaking
