to run off at the mouth

verb
talk excessively or indiscreetly
Pronunciation: / rʌn ɔf æt ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf ət ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to speak excessively or indiscreetly, often saying more than is appropriate

Examples

run off at the mouth about secrets
She realized too late that she had run off at the mouth about something confidential.
Try not to run off at the mouth in front of the boss during the meeting.
Whenever he gets nervous, he tends to run off at the mouth and reveal too much.

Related Terms

  • antonym – clam up: to become silent suddenly, especially out of shyness or fear
  • synonym – ramble: to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
