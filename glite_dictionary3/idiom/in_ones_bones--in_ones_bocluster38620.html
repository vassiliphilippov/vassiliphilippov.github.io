in one's bones

x
deep, instinctive feeling or certainty
Pronunciation: / ɪn wʌnz boʊnz / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz bəʊnz / (BrE)
used to say that someone has a deep, instinctive feeling or certainty about something, often without clear evidence

Examples

in your bones know truth
In your bones, you can sometimes sense a storm before it arrives.
He felt in his bones that taking the job was the right decision.
She knew in her bones that something was wrong, even without any proof.

Related Terms

  • synonym – gut feeling: an instinctive feeling, as opposed to an opinion based on facts
  • synonym – sixth sense: a supposed intuitive faculty giving awareness not explicable in terms of normal perception
