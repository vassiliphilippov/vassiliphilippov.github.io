neck and neck

adjective, adverb
evenly matched; too close to call
Pronunciation: / nɛk ənd nɛk / (AmE), / nɛk ənd nɛk / (BrE)
so close in a race or competition that it is very difficult to say who will win; exactly even or almost even in progress or achievement

Examples

runners neck and neck

The companies are neck and neck in the race to develop new technology.
