to live in sin

verb
cohabit without marriage (often disapproved)
Pronunciation: / lɪv ɪn sɪn / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn sɪn / (BrE)
(of a couple) to live together and have a sexual relationship without being married, often implying social or moral disapproval.

Examples

live in sin with a partner
Her grandmother was shocked to learn she planned to live in sin with her boyfriend.
Many older people still frown upon couples who live in sin.
They had been living in sin for several years before deciding to get married.

Related Terms
