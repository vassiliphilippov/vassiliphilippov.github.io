late to the party

adjective
involved after events have happened
Pronunciation: / leɪt tə ðə ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / leɪt tə ðə ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
arriving or becoming involved after important events or developments have already occurred

Examples

to be late to the party on news
He's late to the party with that technology; most companies adopted it years ago.
I realized I was late to the party when I saw everyone had already voted.
They felt late to the party after discovering the trend had started months earlier.
