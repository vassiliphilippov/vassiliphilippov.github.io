on the coattails

adverb
benefiting from another's success
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə ˈkoʊtˌteɪlz / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈkəʊtˌteɪlz / (BrE)
immediately benefiting from or following another person's success, achievements, or actions, often undeservedly

Examples

ride on the coattails of success
He gained his reputation on the coattails of his older brother's fame.
Many politicians were elected on the coattails of a popular leader.
The new manager rose to power on the coattails of her predecessor's accomplishments.
