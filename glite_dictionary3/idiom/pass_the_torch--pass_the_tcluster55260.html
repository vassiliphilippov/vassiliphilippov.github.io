to pass the torch

verb
hand over leadership or responsibility
Pronunciation: / pæs ðə tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / pɑːs ðə tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
to transfer responsibility, leadership, or a role to another person, especially to a successor

Examples

pass the torch to a new leader
After twenty years as CEO, he decided to pass the torch to his trusted deputy.
It's important for experienced teachers to pass the torch to the next generation of educators.
With the company's future secure, she felt ready to pass the torch and retire.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hand over the reins: to give control or responsibility to someone else
  • synonym – step down: to resign from a position of leadership
