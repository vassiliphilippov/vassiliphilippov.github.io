to go high

verb
respond with dignity to negativity
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ haɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ haɪ / (BrE)
to respond to negativity or unfairness with dignity and integrity, choosing not to retaliate in kind

Examples

to go high in conflict
Even when others were spreading lies, he decided to go high and kept his response respectful.
If they attack you personally, just go high and focus on the bigger picture.
When faced with harsh criticism, she chose to go high rather than stoop to their level.

Related Terms

  • antonym – retaliate: to respond with counter-attacks or negativity
  • synonym – turn the other cheek: to respond to aggression without retaliation
