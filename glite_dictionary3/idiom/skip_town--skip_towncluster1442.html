to skip town

verb
leave suddenly and secretly
Pronunciation: / skɪp taʊn / (AmE), / skɪp taʊn / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and secretly, especially to avoid trouble, debt, or responsibility

Examples

to skip town after trouble
After the scandal broke, the company executive decided to skip town and was nowhere to be found.
He owed money to several people and chose to skip town rather than face them.
Rumor has it that she skipped town before her court date.
