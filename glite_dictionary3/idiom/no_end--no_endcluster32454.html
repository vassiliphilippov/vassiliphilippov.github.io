no end

adverb
endlessly; for a long time
Pronunciation: / noʊ ɛnd / (AmE), / nəʊ end / (BrE)
(informal) For a very long time; endlessly or to a great extent.

Examples

no end of trouble
It pleased her no end to know that her work was appreciated.
The meeting dragged on no end and everyone grew impatient.
The repairs on the car took no end of time to finish.
