deafening silence

noun
conspicuous lack of sound
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfənɪŋ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈdɛf(ə)nɪŋ ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
A striking or overwhelming lack of noise, often used to emphasize the conspicuous absence of expected response or discussion.

Examples

a deafening silence followed
She waited for his answer, but was met with deafening silence.
Their question was followed by a deafening silence that made everyone uncomfortable.
When the news was announced, a deafening silence swept over the crowd.
