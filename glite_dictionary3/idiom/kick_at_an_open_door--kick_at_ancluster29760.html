to kick at an open door

verb
criticize or act against what is already accepted; act unnecessarily
Pronunciation: / kɪk æt ən ˈoʊpən dɔr / (AmE), / kɪk at ən ˈəʊpən dɔː / (BrE)
to criticize something that is already accepted or to waste effort on an easy or unnecessary task

Examples

to kick at an open door pointlessly
He felt like he was kicking at an open door when repeating the proposal to the board.
She realized she was kicking at an open door by insisting on safety regulations everyone already supported.
Trying to introduce these changes now is just kicking at an open door.
