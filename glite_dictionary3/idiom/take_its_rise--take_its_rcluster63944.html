to take its rise

verb
to originate or begin
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪts raɪz / (AmE), / teɪk ɪts raɪz / (BrE)
(of something) to originate or begin; to have its start at a particular time or place.

Examples

take its rise in the mountains
Many major rivers take their rise in the mountains.
The environmental movement took its rise in the 1960s amid growing concerns about pollution.
The tradition of sending greeting cards took its rise in Victorian England.
