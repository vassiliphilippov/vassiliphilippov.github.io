wise to

adjective
aware of hidden or secret information
Pronunciation: / waɪz tə / (AmE), / waɪz tə / (BrE)
Aware of or having knowledge, especially of something secret, dishonest, or hidden; evidencing the possession of inside information.

Examples

wise to a trick
He's wise to the tricks people use to avoid paying taxes.
She became wise to the scam before losing any money.
The manager was wise to their plan, so the meeting ended quickly.
