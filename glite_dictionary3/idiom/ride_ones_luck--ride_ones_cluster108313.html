to ride one's luck

verb
rely on chance or good fortune
Pronunciation: / raɪd wʌnz lʌk / (AmE), / raɪd wʌnz lʌk / (BrE)
to depend on continued good fortune or chance rather than on one's own efforts

Examples

to ride one's luck for success
After winning several games in a row, Carla decided not to ride her luck any further.
He managed to pass every test by riding his luck instead of studying.
If you keep riding your luck, it will eventually run out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – take a chance: to do something risky in the hope of success
  • synonym – tempt fate: to take unnecessary risks that could bring bad luck
