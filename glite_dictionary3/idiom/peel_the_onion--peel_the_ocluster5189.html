to peel the onion

verb
analyze something in depth, layer by layer
Pronunciation: / pil ði ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / piːl ði ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
to examine or analyze a problem, situation, or subject carefully, especially by uncovering its different layers or aspects one at a time

Examples

to peel the onion of a problem
In our investigation, we had to peel the onion to uncover all the facts.
The journalist promised to peel the onion and reveal the truth behind the scandal.
Therapists often help clients peel the onion of their emotions to reach the core issues.

Related Terms
