to lower one's sights

verb
reduce ambitions or expectations
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ɚ wʌnz saɪts / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə wʌnz saɪts / (BrE)
to reduce one's ambitions or expectations, often in response to reality or difficulties

Examples

to lower one's sights after failure
After several failed auditions, he decided to lower his sights and apply for smaller roles in local theater.
Realizing her exam scores were not high enough, she chose to lower her sights and apply to less competitive universities.
When the company faced budget cuts, management had to lower their sights regarding expansion plans.
