to go to blazes

verb
angrily dismiss someone; tell someone to go away
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tu bleɪzɪz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tuː bleɪzɪz / (BrE)
(informal) used to angrily tell someone to go away or to express dismissal or contempt.

Examples

go to blazes angrily
He got fed up with the complaints and told everyone to go to blazes.
If you think you can boss me around, you can just go to blazes.
She shouted at him to go to blazes and slammed the door.
