everything will be fine (Australian informal idiom)
Pronunciation: / ʃɪl bi ˈæpəlz / (AmE), / ʃɪl bi ˈapəlz / (BrE)
(Australian, informal) Used to reassure someone that everything will turn out well; everything will be fine.

Examples

she'll be apples after trouble
Don't worry about your test tomorrow; she'll be apples.
Finish your work and she'll be apples by the end of the day.
They said the weather might be bad, but she'll be apples for the picnic.
