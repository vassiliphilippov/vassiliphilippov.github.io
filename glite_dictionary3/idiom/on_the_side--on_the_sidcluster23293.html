on the side

adverb
unofficially; as an extra activity
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə saɪd / (AmE), / ɒn ðə saɪd / (BrE)
in addition to one's main job or official activities, and often without official approval or permission

Examples

work on the side
He earned extra money on the side by repairing computers.
Many employees sold products on the side without their manager's knowledge.
She takes freelance writing jobs on the side to supplement her income.

Related Terms

  • synonym – moonlighting: having a second job in addition to one's main employment, typically secretly or unofficially
