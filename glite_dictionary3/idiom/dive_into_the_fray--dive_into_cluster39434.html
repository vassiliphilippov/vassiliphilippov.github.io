to dive into the fray

verb
become involved in a chaotic situation
Pronunciation: / daɪv ˈɪntu ðə freɪ / (AmE), / daɪv ˈɪntuː ðə freɪ / (BrE)
to become actively involved in a chaotic, intense, or competitive situation, often when there is conflict or confusion

Examples

to dive into the fray during conflict
As soon as the negotiations heated up, he didn't hesitate to dive into the fray.
The reporter bravely dove into the fray to get a closer look at the protest.
When the debate turned hostile, she decided to dive into the fray to defend her colleague.
