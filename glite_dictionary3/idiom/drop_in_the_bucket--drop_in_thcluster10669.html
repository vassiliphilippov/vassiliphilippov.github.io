drop in the bucket

noun
a very small, insignificant amount
Pronunciation: / drɑp ɪn ðə ˈbʌkɪt / (AmE), / drɒp ɪn ðə ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
a very small amount or effect, especially one that is insignificant compared to what is needed or to a much larger problem

Examples

a drop in the bucket of money
My monthly savings feel like a drop in the bucket when I look at the price of a new house.
Sending one shipment of supplies was a drop in the bucket given the scale of the disaster.
The company's attempt to reduce pollution was just a drop in the bucket in the fight against climate change.
