to keep a low profile

verb
avoid attracting attention
Pronunciation: / kip ə loʊ ˈproʊfaɪl / (AmE), / kiːp ə ləʊ ˈprəʊfaɪl / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid attracting attention to yourself; to act in a way that does not make you noticeable

Examples

keep a low profile at work
After the controversy, the politician decided to keep a low profile for several months.
She managed to keep a low profile at the party, avoiding the center of attention.
When traveling in unfamiliar cities, it's often safer to keep a low profile.

Related Terms

  • antonym – keep a high profile: to attract attention to oneself by being noticeable or active
  • synonym – lie low: to stay out of notice, often to avoid trouble
