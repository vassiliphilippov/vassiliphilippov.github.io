to shout the odds

verb
proclaim chances or risks loudly
Pronunciation: / ʃaʊt ði ɑdz / (AmE), / ʃaʊt ði ɒdz / (BrE)
to loudly proclaim the chances or risks involved, or to announce one's opinion about the probable outcome of a situation

Examples

shout the odds in an argument
During the heated debate, she started to shout the odds about the possible outcomes.
He loves to shout the odds whenever his team is playing, even if no one asks for his opinion.
Some fans shout the odds before every match, confident in their predictions.
