to stand the sight of

verb
unable to tolerate seeing
Pronunciation: / stænd ðə saɪt əv / (AmE), / stænd ðə saɪt ɒv / (BrE)
to dislike or be unable to tolerate seeing something

Examples

to stand the sight of blood
I couldn't stand the sight of that messy room any longer, so I cleaned it.
She can't stand the sight of blood and faints easily during medical procedures.
Some people can't stand the sight of spiders, even on television.
