to let the bedbugs bite

verb
humorous way to say good night
Pronunciation: / lɛt ðə ˈbɛdˌbʌɡz baɪt / (AmE), / lɛt ðə ˈbɛdˌbʌɡz baɪt / (BrE)
A humorous phrase used to wish someone a good night, referring to the idea of bedbugs biting while one sleeps.

Examples

let the bedbugs bite jokingly
As she tucked her son in, she whispered, "Goodnight, and let the bedbugs bite!" with a mischievous smile.
He texted me, "Sweet dreams—let the bedbugs bite!" just to be silly.
We laughed and joked before bed, telling each other not to let the bedbugs bite.

Related Terms

  • synonym – don't let the bedbugs bite: idiomatic warning used when saying good night
