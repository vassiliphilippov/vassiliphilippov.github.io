to burn the candle at both ends

verb
overwork by keeping long hours
Pronunciation: / bɝn ðə ˈkændl ət boʊθ ɛndz / (AmE), / bɜːn ðə ˈkændl ət bəʊθ ɛndz / (BrE)
to exhaust oneself by working or doing other activities late into the night and starting again early in the morning, often resulting in tiredness or stress

Examples

burn the candle at both ends for weeks
