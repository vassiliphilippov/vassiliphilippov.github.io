to put a lid on

verb
stop or limit something
Pronunciation: / pʊt ə lɪd ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ə lɪd ɒn / (BrE)
to stop or limit something, especially to prevent it from continuing or becoming public

Examples

to put a lid on an argument
After hours of debate, the chairperson asked everyone to put a lid on the discussion.
The company has tried to put a lid on leaks by tightening security procedures.
The manager decided to put a lid on rumors by addressing the issue directly in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – encourage: to give support or promote growth of something
  • synonym – shut down: to stop something from operating or continuing
  • synonym – suppress: to prevent something from being expressed or known
