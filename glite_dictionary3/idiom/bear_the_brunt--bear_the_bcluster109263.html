to bear the brunt

verb
suffer the worst part
Pronunciation: / bɛr ðə brʌnt / (AmE), / bɛə ðə brʌnt / (BrE)
to suffer or endure the worst or main part of something unpleasant or harmful

Examples

bear the brunt of criticism
During the recession, young workers often bear the brunt of job cuts.
Local businesses will bear the brunt of the new tax regulations.
The emergency services bore the brunt of the initial rescue efforts.
