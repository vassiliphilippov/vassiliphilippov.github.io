to take a pew

verb
to sit down (informal, chiefly British)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə pjuː / (AmE), / teɪk ə pjuː / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) To sit down, originally referring to sitting in a church seat but also used more generally.

Examples

to take a pew and rest
After standing for so long, he was relieved to finally take a pew near the back.
She gestured to the visitor, saying, 'Please, take a pew.'
You look tired—come in and take a pew for a few minutes.
