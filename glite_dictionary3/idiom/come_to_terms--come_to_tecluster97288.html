to come to terms

verb
accept or reach understanding
Pronunciation: / kʌm tə tɜrmz / (AmE), / kʌm tə tɜːmz / (BrE)
To accept or reconcile oneself to a difficult or unpleasant situation; to reach an agreement or understanding, especially after a period of struggle or reflection.

Examples

come to terms with loss
After the accident, it took her months to come to terms with her new reality.
He has finally come to terms with the fact that he needs help.
They are slowly coming to terms with their financial loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accept: to receive or agree to something, often reluctantly
  • antonym – resist: to oppose something, especially emotionally or mentally
