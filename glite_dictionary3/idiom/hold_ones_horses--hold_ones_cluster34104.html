to hold one's horses

verb
wait or be patient (idiom)
Pronunciation: / hoʊld wʌnz ˈhɔrsɪz / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz ˈhɔːsɪz / (BrE)
used to tell someone to wait or slow down and not act so quickly or impatiently

Examples

hold your horses before acting
Dad told me to hold my horses and not rush into signing the contract.
If you just hold your horses, the results will be announced soon.
Let's hold our horses until we have all the details.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be patient: to remain calm and not become annoyed when dealing with problems or delays
  • synonym – wait: to remain in a place or state until something happens
