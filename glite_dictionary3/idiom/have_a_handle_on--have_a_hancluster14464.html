to have a handle on

verb
to understand or manage (something)
Pronunciation: / hæv ə ˈhændl ɑn / (AmE), / hæv ə ˈhændl ɒn / (BrE)
to have an understanding of something, or to be able to deal with it effectively

Examples

to have a handle on a problem
It took me a while to have a handle on the new software, but now I feel comfortable.
Once you have a handle on the basics, you can start experimenting more freely.
The manager needs to have a handle on everything that's happening in the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be confused: to be unable to understand or manage something
  • synonym – get a grip on: to gain control or understanding of something
  • synonym – understand: to know or grasp the meaning of something
