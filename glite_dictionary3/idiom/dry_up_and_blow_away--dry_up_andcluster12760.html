to dry up and blow away

verb
to disappear or wither away entirely
Pronunciation: / draɪ ʌp ənd bloʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / draɪ ʌp ənd bləʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to disappear completely or to wither and vanish, often as a result of neglect or adverse conditions

Examples

dry up and blow away completely
After the company lost funding, many of its ambitious projects seemed to dry up and blow away.
If friendships are neglected, they may simply dry up and blow away over time.
Without regular rainfall, the stream will dry up and blow away by late summer.
