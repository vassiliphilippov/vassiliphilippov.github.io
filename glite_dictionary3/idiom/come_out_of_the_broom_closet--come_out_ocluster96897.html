to come out of the broom closet

verb
declare witchcraft or Pagan identity open
Pronunciation: / kʌm aʊt əv ðə bruːm ˈklɑːsɪt / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt ɒv ðə bruːm ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to openly declare that one practices or is interested in witchcraft, Paganism, or related spiritual paths, especially after having kept it secret

Examples

to come out of the broom closet as a witch
After years of secrecy, he finally decided to come out of the broom closet to his family and friends.
Many people feel anxious before they come out of the broom closet, fearing judgment or misunderstanding.
Writing her blog was a way for her to come out of the broom closet and share her beliefs with others.
