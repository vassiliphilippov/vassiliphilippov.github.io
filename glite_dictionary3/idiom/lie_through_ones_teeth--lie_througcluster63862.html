to lie through one's teeth

verb
tell a bold, obvious lie
Pronunciation: / laɪ θru wʌnz tiθ / (AmE), / laɪ θruː wʌnz tiːθ / (BrE)
to tell an obvious and deliberate lie, especially in a bold or shameless manner

Examples

lie through your teeth boldly
Don't lie through your teeth; I know you broke the vase.
Politicians sometimes lie through their teeth to avoid admitting mistakes.
She looked straight at me and lied through her teeth about where she had been.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lie: to say something that is not true
  • antonym – tell the truth: to speak honestly and state facts
