to ring false

verb
seem untrue or insincere
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ fɔls / (AmE), / rɪŋ fɔːls / (BrE)
to seem untrue, insincere, or implausible

Examples

ring false to listeners
Her apology seemed to ring false despite her earnest expression.
If a statement rings false, people are likely to doubt your honesty.
The company's explanations ring false to many customers.
