to eat the onion

verb
face an unpleasant reality directly
Pronunciation: / it ði ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / iːt ði ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
to face a difficult or unpleasant situation directly; to confront an unpleasant task or reality without avoiding it

Examples

to eat the onion bravely
If you want things to improve, you need to eat the onion instead of avoiding the issue.
It's time for us to eat the onion and admit our mistakes.
Sometimes, management just has to eat the onion and make tough decisions.
