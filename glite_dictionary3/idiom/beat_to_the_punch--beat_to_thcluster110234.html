to beat to the punch

verb
act before someone else does
Pronunciation: / bit tə ðə pʌntʃ / (AmE), / biːt tə ðə pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to do something before someone else does, especially in a way that gains advantage

Examples

to beat to the punch a competitor
If you want that promotion, you’ll have to beat to the punch your colleagues.
She woke up early and beat to the punch everyone else to get the tickets.
The company beat to the punch its competitors by launching the new product first.

  • synonym – preempt: to act in advance to prevent another action
