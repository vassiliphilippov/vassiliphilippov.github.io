nothing to see here

x
expression dismissing concern or attention
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌθɪŋ tə si hɪr / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ tə si hɪə / (BrE)
used to indicate that there is nothing of interest or concern; often said to dismiss attention or reassure others that no issue exists

Examples

nothing to see here after trouble
After the small fire was quickly extinguished, the staff reassured everyone, 'nothing to see here.'
Reporters gathered outside, but security insisted there was 'nothing to see here' after the alarm sounded.
The manager waved his hand and said, 'nothing to see here' when asked about the technical glitch.
