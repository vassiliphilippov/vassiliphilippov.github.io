a seat at the table

noun
opportunity to participate in decisions
Pronunciation: / ə sit ət ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ə siːt ət ðə ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
the right or opportunity to participate actively in important discussions or decision-making processes

Examples

a seat at the table in meetings
Everyone deserves a seat at the table when policies that affect their lives are being discussed.
The new manager fought hard to earn a seat at the table during planning meetings.
Without a seat at the table, our concerns won't be heard by the board.
