to strain at the leash

verb
be eager or impatient to begin
Pronunciation: / streɪn æt ðə liʃ / (AmE), / streɪn ət ðə liːʃ / (BrE)
to be very eager or impatient to begin or do something, often being held back temporarily

Examples

to strain at the leash with excitement
After months of preparation, the team was straining at the leash to launch their project.
Investors are straining at the leash to get involved in the new technology sector.
The young athletes are straining at the leash to compete in the championship match.
