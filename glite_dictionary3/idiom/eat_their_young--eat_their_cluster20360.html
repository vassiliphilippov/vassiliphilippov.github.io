to eat their young

verb
exploit one's own kind for gain (idiom)
Pronunciation: / it ðɛr jʌŋ / (AmE), / iːt ðeə jʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) to ruthlessly exploit, betray, or harm members of their own group, often for personal gain.

Examples

eat their young for power
During the crisis, the party appeared willing to eat their young to survive.
It's unfortunate when established professionals eat their young instead of mentoring newcomers.
Some corporations will eat their young to stay ahead in a competitive industry.

Related Terms

  • synonym – exploit: to use someone or something unfairly for your own advantage
