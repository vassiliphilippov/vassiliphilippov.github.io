to bark yourself

verb
to do a task yourself instead of delegating
Pronunciation: / bɑrk jərˈsɛlf / (AmE), / bɑːk jəˈsɛlf / (BrE)
(usually in the phrase 'Why keep a dog and bark yourself?') To do something yourself instead of having someone else do it, especially when delegation is expected.

Examples

bark yourself with chores
He hired an assistant but still prefers to bark yourself with every little chore.
It's inefficient to bark yourself when you can trust your team with the details.
The manager assigned tasks to everyone and reminded them not to bark yourself about the paperwork.
