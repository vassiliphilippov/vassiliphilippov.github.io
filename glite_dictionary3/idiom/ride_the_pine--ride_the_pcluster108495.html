to ride the pine

verb
sit on the bench as a substitute (sports idiom)
Pronunciation: / raɪd ðə paɪn / (AmE), / raɪd ðə paɪn / (BrE)
(North American, informal, especially in sports) To spend time sitting on the bench as a substitute rather than playing in a game.

Examples

ride the pine during games
After his injury, he had to ride the pine for the rest of the season.
If you don't practice harder, you'll be riding the pine all year.
No athlete wants to ride the pine when the championship is on the line.

Related Terms

  • synonym – benchwarmer: a player who spends most of the time on the bench
