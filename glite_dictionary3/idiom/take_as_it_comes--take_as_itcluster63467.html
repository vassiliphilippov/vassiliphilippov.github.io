to take as it comes

verb
accept events as they occur
Pronunciation: / teɪk æz ɪt kʌmz / (AmE), / teɪk əz ɪt kʌmz / (BrE)
to accept events or situations calmly or without worrying, as they happen

Examples

to take as it comes calmly
After losing her job, she decided to take as it comes and see what opportunities would arise.
Although the plan fell apart, Mark told himself he would just take as it comes.
Rather than stressing over the future, I try to take as it comes and enjoy each day.

Related Terms

  • synonym – go with the flow: to adapt to circumstances without resistance
  • antonym – plan ahead: to make plans for the future; the opposite mindset
