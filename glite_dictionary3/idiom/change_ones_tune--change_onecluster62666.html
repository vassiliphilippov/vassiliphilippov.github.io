to change one's tune

verb
alter one's opinion or attitude
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪndʒ wʌnz tun / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ wʌnz tjuːn / (BrE)
to alter one's opinion, attitude, or manner, especially suddenly or after being persuaded or proved wrong

Examples

to change one's tune after criticism
After the budget cuts, the director changed her tune and supported the new proposal.
He was against the plan at first, but he changed his tune after the latest report.
Once she realized the benefits, she quickly changed her tune about the project.
