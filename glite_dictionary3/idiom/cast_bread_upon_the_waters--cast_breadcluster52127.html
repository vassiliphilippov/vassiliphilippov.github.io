to cast bread upon the waters

verb
act generously without expecting return
Pronunciation: / kæst brɛd əˈpɑn ði ˈwɑtɚz / (AmE), / kɑːst brɛd əˈpɒn ðə ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
to act generously or do good deeds without expecting anything in return, or to take a risk hoping for a future benefit

Examples

to cast bread upon the waters selflessly
Despite her limited means, she chose to cast bread upon the waters by supporting local charities.
He always believed if you cast bread upon the waters, kindness would eventually return to you.
Sometimes you have to cast bread upon the waters and trust that your good deeds will be rewarded.
