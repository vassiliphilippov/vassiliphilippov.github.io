to take up the gauntlet

verb
accept a challenge (idiom)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈɡɔntlət / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈɡɔːntlət / (BrE)
to accept a challenge, especially one presented publicly or in a confrontational way

Examples

to take up the gauntlet against rivals
After the controversial remarks, he promised to take up the gauntlet and prove his critics wrong.
Though she was hesitant at first, Maria chose to take up the gauntlet and debate her opponent.
When the company challenged its rivals, the CEO was quick to take up the gauntlet.
