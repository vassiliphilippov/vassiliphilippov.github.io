to shoot oneself in the foot

verb
cause self-inflicted harm or setback
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt wʌnˈsɛlf ɪn ðə fʊt / (AmE), / ʃuːt wʌnˈsɛlf ɪn ðə fʊt / (BrE)
to do or say something that unintentionally causes harm or disadvantage to oneself

Examples

shoot oneself in the foot by mistake
By quitting his job before finding a new one, he shot himself in the foot.
She shot herself in the foot when she argued with her boss during the meeting.
They realized too late that reducing the budget had shot them in the foot.

Related Terms

  • synonym – sabotage oneself: to deliberately or accidentally undermine one's own efforts
