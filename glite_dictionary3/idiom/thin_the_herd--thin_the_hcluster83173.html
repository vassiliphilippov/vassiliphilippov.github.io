to thin the herd

verb
reduce group size for health or balance
Pronunciation: / θɪn ðə hɝd / (AmE), / θɪn ðə hɜːd / (BrE)
to deliberately reduce the size of a group of animals or people, usually to improve health, balance, or efficiency

Examples

to thin the herd of animals
Farmers sometimes thin the herd to prevent the spread of disease among animals.
The company decided to thin the herd by laying off some employees during restructuring.
Wildlife managers may thin the herd to keep populations balanced in protected areas.
