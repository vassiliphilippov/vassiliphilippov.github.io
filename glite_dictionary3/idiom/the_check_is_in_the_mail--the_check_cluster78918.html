the check is in the mail

x
said when promising (often insincerely) that payment has been sent
Pronunciation: / ðə tʃɛk ɪz ɪn ðə meɪl / (AmE), / ðə tʃɛk ɪz ɪn ðə meɪl / (BrE)
used to indicate that payment has supposedly been sent, often used to delay or avoid payment or to make a promise that may not be fulfilled.
