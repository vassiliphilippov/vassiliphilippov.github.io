to have a bridge to sell

verb
expression for identifying an unbelievable or dubious offer
Pronunciation: / hæv ə brɪdʒ tə sɛl / (AmE), / hæv ə brɪdʒ tə sɛl / (BrE)
(humorous, often ironic) Used to emphasize that an offer or statement is so unbelievable or deceptive that only a very gullible person would believe it, referencing classic scams of selling ownership of famous bridges.

Examples

have a bridge to sell a fool
Any time I hear a deal that sounds too good to be true, I say I have a bridge to sell them.
He believed the email was real? Wow, do I have a bridge to sell him!
If you think you can get rich overnight with no effort, I have a bridge to sell.
