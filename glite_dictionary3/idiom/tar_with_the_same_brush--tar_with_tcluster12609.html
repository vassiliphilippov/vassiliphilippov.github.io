to tar with the same brush

verb
judge all by one example (idiom)
Pronunciation: / tɑr wɪð ðə seɪm brʌʃ / (AmE), / tɑː wɪð ðə seɪm brʌʃ / (BrE)
to unfairly judge or categorize a group of people or things based on the actions or characteristics of one member or example

Examples

tar with the same brush a group
It is wrong to tar with the same brush everyone who works at the company because of one incident.
Politicians are often tarred with the same brush due to the actions of a few.
We must be careful not to tar with the same brush every student for the mistakes of a handful.
