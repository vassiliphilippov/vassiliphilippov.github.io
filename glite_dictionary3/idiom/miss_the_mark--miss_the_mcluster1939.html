to miss the mark

verb
fail to achieve intended result
Pronunciation: / mɪs ðə mɑrk / (AmE), / mɪs ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
to fail to achieve the intended result or effect; to not be successful in what was intended

Examples

miss the mark completely
Despite his experience, his comments missed the mark and did not address the real issue.
The film's attempt at humor missed the mark for many critics and viewers alike.
We tried a new approach, but unfortunately it missed the mark with our customers.
