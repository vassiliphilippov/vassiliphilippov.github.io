to thread the needle

verb
accomplish a delicate or difficult task
Pronunciation: / θrɛd ðə ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / θrɛd ðə ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
to successfully pass a thread through the eye of a needle, or figuratively, to accomplish a very delicate or difficult task requiring precision or care

Examples

to thread the needle carefully
He skillfully threaded the needle between two defenders to score the winning goal.
It takes patience to thread the needle when sewing tiny buttons onto a shirt.
Negotiators tried to thread the needle between opposing demands during the talks.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bungle: to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • synonym – navigate: to deal effectively with a difficult situation
