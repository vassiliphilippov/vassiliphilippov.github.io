to go out on a limb

verb
take a risk; act boldly
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ aʊt ɑn ə lɪm / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt ɒn ə lɪm / (BrE)
to do something that involves risk, uncertainty, or leaving one's comfort zone, especially when supporting an idea or making a bold decision

Examples

to go out on a limb for an idea
He knew he was going out on a limb by disagreeing with his boss, but he spoke up anyway.
I'm willing to go out on a limb for you because I believe in your idea.
She decided to go out on a limb and propose a new strategy at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym – play it safe: to act cautiously and avoid risk
  • synonym – take a risk: to do something that might have negative consequences
