to do someone any favors

verb
help or benefit someone
Pronunciation: / du ˈsʌmwʌn ˈɛni ˈfeɪvɚz / (AmE), / duː ˈsʌmwʌn ˈeni ˈfeɪvəz / (BrE)
to help or benefit someone; to provide a service or act that assists another person, often with the implication that such help may not be as beneficial as it seems.

Examples

do her any favors by lying
He didn't do her any favors by covering up the mistake.
Quitting now won't do the team any favors before the big game.
You aren't doing yourself any favors by skipping breakfast every morning.
