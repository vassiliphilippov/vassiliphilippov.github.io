to chap someone's hide

verb
scold or reprimand someone harshly
Pronunciation: / tʃæp ˈsʌmwʌnz haɪd / (AmE), / tʃæp ˈsʌmwʌnz haɪd / (BrE)
to scold or reprimand someone severely, especially in an angry or irritated manner

Examples

chap his hide angrily
Don't chap my hide about the report—I did the best I could.
If you miss another deadline, the manager will chap your hide in front of the whole team.
My mother really chapped my hide for tracking mud all through the house.
