in no way, shape, or form

adverb
not at all; under no circumstances
Pronunciation: / ɪn noʊ weɪ ʃeɪp ɔr fɔrm / (AmE), / ɪn nəʊ weɪ ʃeɪp ɔː fəʊm / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is not true or not possible in any way; not at all or under any circumstances.

Examples

in no way, shape, or form allowed
He denied, in no way, shape, or form, being involved in the incident.
I am, in no way, shape, or form, connected to that organization.
The new policy, in no way, shape, or form, benefits small businesses.
