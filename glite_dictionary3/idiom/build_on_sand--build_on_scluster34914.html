to build on sand

verb
base something on an unreliable foundation
Pronunciation: / bɪld ɑn sænd / (AmE), / bɪld ɒn sand / (BrE)
to base a plan, project, or belief on a weak, unreliable, or unstable foundation, so that it is likely to fail

Examples

to build on sand a plan
All his confidence was built on sand once the truth came out.
Trying to start a business without research is like building on sand.
You can't expect lasting results if your methods are built on sand.

Related Terms

  • antonym – build on rock: to base something on a solid or reliable foundation
