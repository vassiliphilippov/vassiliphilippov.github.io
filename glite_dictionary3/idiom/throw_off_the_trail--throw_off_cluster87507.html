to throw off the trail

verb
to mislead or confuse (esp. in investigations)
Pronunciation: / θroʊ ɔf ðə treɪl / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɒf ðə treɪl / (BrE)
to deliberately mislead or confuse someone so that they do not uncover the truth or achieve their aim, especially in investigations or pursuits

Examples

to throw off the trail detectives
She invented stories to throw off the trail anyone asking too many questions.
The company released misleading information to throw off the trail of its competitors.
The thief left false clues to throw off the trail of the police.

Related Terms

  • synonym – mislead: to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • synonym – throw off the scent: to mislead someone who is searching, using the metaphor of hunting dogs
