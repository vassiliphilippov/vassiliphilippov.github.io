to take a bead on

verb
carefully aim or observe closely
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə bid ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ə biːd ɒn / (BrE)
to carefully aim at someone or something, or to observe or consider something closely

Examples

to take a bead on a target
Before speaking, she took a bead on everyone's reactions.
The detective paused to take a bead on the suspicious figure in the crowd.
The hunter took a bead on the deer from behind the tall grass.
