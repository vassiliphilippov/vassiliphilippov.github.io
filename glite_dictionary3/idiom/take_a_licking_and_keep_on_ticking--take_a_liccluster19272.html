to take a licking and keep on ticking

verb
endure trouble and keep going
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə ˈlɪkɪŋ ənd kip ɑn ˈtɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈlɪkɪŋ ənd kiːp ɒn ˈtɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
to withstand hardships, challenges, or adversity and continue to function or persist without giving up

Their relationship has taken a licking and kept on ticking through years of ups and downs.
