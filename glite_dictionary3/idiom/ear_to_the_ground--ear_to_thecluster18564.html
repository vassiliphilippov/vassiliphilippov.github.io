ear to the ground

noun
to be alert to current trends or news
Pronunciation: / ˈɪɹ tu ðə ˈɡɹaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɪə tə ðə ˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to be attentive to the latest news, trends, or developments, especially by paying close attention to what is happening or being said.

Examples

keep an ear to the ground
By keeping his ear to the ground, he learned about the merger before it was announced.
She's a journalist who always keeps her ear to the ground for breaking stories.
You need to have your ear to the ground if you want to succeed in this fast-paced industry.

  • antonym – out of the loop: not informed or aware of current events or developments
