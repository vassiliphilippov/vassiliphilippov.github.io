to carry a tune

verb
sing in tune; sing accurately
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ə tuːn / (AmE), / ˈkæri ə tjuːn / (BrE)
to be able to sing in tune; to sing accurately without going off-key

Examples

to carry a tune well
Despite never taking singing lessons, she can carry a tune remarkably well.
He was asked to leave the choir after it became clear he couldn't carry a tune.
Many people are embarrassed to sing in public because they think they can't carry a tune.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be tone-deaf: unable to distinguish between musical pitches, cannot sing in tune
  • synonym – sing in tune: sing with correct pitch
