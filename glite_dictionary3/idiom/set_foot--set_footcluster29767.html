to set foot

verb
to enter or go into a place
Pronunciation: / sɛt fʊt / (AmE), / sɛt fʊt / (BrE)
to enter or go into a particular place, especially for the first time or to emphasize presence or absence

Examples

set foot in a place
He refuses to set foot in his old office after the disagreement.
She hasn't set foot outside the house all week.
Tourists from around the world dream of setting foot on the Great Wall of China.
