to go their separate ways

verb
to part ways or split up
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ðɛr ˈsɛpɚət weɪz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðeə ˈsɛpərət weɪz / (BrE)
to part company and pursue different paths, especially after a relationship or collaboration ends

Examples

to go their separate ways after graduation
After graduation, most friends go their separate ways and start new chapters in their lives.
After their long journey together, the travelers chose to go their separate ways.
Once the project was completed, the team members decided to go their separate ways.
