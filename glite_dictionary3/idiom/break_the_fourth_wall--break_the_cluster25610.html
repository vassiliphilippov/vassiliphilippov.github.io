to break the fourth wall

verb
address the audience directly (theatre/film)
Pronunciation: / breɪk ðə fɔrθ wɑl / (AmE), / breɪk ðə fɔːθ wɔːl / (BrE)
(of an actor or character) to address the audience directly during a performance, thereby acknowledging the fictional nature of the work.

Examples

break the fourth wall and speak
In the middle of the play, the main character suddenly breaks the fourth wall and greets the audience.
Some movies cleverly break the fourth wall to draw viewers into the story.
When the comedian broke the fourth wall, the crowd erupted with laughter.
