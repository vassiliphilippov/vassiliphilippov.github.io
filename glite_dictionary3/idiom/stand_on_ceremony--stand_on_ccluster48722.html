to stand on ceremony

verb
be overly formal or insist on etiquette
Pronunciation: / stænd ɑn ˈsɛrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / stænd ɒn ˈsɛrɪməni / (BrE)
to behave in a very formal way or to insist on strict observance of social etiquette and formalities

Examples

stand on ceremony at work
Even though he was the boss, he never stood on ceremony with his employees.
Please don't stand on ceremony; help yourself to some food.
You don't need to stand on ceremony—just make yourself at home.

Related Terms

  • synonym – be formal: to act with formality and observe social etiquette
  • antonym – be informal: to act without strict adherence to etiquette or formalities
