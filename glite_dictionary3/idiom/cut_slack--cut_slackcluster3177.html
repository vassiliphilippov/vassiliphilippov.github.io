to cut slack

verb
show leniency or give a break
Pronunciation: / kʌt slæk / (AmE), / kʌt slæk / (BrE)
to treat someone less strictly or to show leniency towards them, especially in a difficult situation

Examples

cut slack for him
I know you're tired, so I'll cut you some slack and finish the project myself.
She hoped her teacher would cut her slack because of her recent illness.
The coach decided to cut the team some slack after their hard week of practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym – be hard on: treat someone very strictly or sternly
  • synonym – let up on: be less severe or give someone a break
