to butter someone's bread on both sides

verb
benefit from two sides simultaneously (idiom)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌt̬ɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz brɛd ɑn boʊθ saɪdz / (AmE), / ˈbʌtə ˈsʌmwənz brɛd ɒn bəʊθ saɪdz / (BrE)
to take advantage of or benefit from two different sources or opportunities at the same time, often implying greed or opportunism

Examples

butter his bread on both sides in business
He always finds a way to butter his bread on both sides in business deals.
If you keep buttering your bread on both sides, people will start to notice your duplicity.
Trying to butter your bread on both sides rarely works without consequences.
