to be sure

adverb
used to admit a concession
Pronunciation: / tə bi ʃʊr / (AmE), / tə bi ʃɔː / (BrE)
Used to admit the truth of a concession before making a contrasting point; admittedly.

Examples

to be sure before disagreeing
To be sure, she apologized, yet the damage had been done.
To be sure, the movie has flaws, but it's still entertaining.
He is, to be sure, very talented, but he lacks experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym – admittedly: used to introduce a fact or idea that weakens the previous statement
