to set the bar high

verb
establish a high standard or expectation
Pronunciation: / sɛt ðə bɑːr haɪ / (AmE), / sɛt ðə bɑː haɪ / (BrE)
to establish a high standard or level of expectation for others to meet or surpass

Examples

set the bar high for success
The new CEO decided to set the bar high for company performance and innovation.
The teacher set the bar high for her students, encouraging them to exceed their own expectations.
Their record-breaking achievements have really set the bar high for future competitors.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lower the bar: to reduce standards or expectations
  • synonym – raise the bar: to increase standards or expectations
