to cash in one's chips

verb
to use resources or quit for benefit
Pronunciation: / kæʃ ɪn wʌnz tʃɪps / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn wʌnz tʃɪps / (BrE)
to use one's resources, assets, or advantages, often to gain a benefit or exit from an activity; also, to give up or quit, sometimes with the implication of taking whatever gains are available at the time.

Examples

cash in her chips and retire
After years of dedication, she decided to cash in her chips and retire early.
Realizing the opportunity, he finally cashed in his chips and moved abroad.
When the company was sold, many long-time employees chose to cash in their chips.
