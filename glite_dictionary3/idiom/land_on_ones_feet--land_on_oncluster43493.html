to land on one's feet

verb
recover successfully from difficulty
Pronunciation: / lænd ɑn wʌnz fit / (AmE), / lænd ɒn wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to recover from a difficult situation in a way that ends well; to come out of trouble successfully

Examples

to land on one's feet after trouble
After a tough year, the team finally landed on their feet and made the playoffs.
Despite losing his job, Mark managed to land on his feet and start a new business.
No matter what happens, she seems to land on her feet every time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bounce back: to recover quickly after a setback
  • antonym – fall on hard times: to experience difficulty or poverty
