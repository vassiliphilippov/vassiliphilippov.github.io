caught between wind and water

adjective
in a difficult, uncertain situation
Pronunciation: / kɔt bɪˈtwin wɪnd ənd ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / kɔːt bɪˈtwiːn wɪnd ənd ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
in a difficult situation where neither option is clearly preferable; in a vulnerable position with no easy solution

Examples

caught between wind and water at work
After the merger, many employees felt caught between wind and water about their future roles.
During the negotiations, he realized he was caught between wind and water, with no ideal outcome.
She was caught between wind and water, unsure whether to accept the new job or stay put.
