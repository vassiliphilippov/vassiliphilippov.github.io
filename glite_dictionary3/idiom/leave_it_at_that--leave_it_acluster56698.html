to leave it at that

verb
to stop discussing a topic
Pronunciation: / liv ɪt ət ðæt / (AmE), / liːv ɪt ət ðæt / (BrE)
to stop discussing or pursuing a topic further; to accept a situation as it is without more comment or action

Examples

leave it at that after arguing
After hours of arguing, they agreed to leave it at that and not revisit the issue.
If you’re unhappy with the result, say so now or leave it at that.
She wanted to ask more questions, but decided to leave it at that.
