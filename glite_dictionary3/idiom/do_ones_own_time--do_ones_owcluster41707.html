to do one's own time

verb
serve a punishment alone
Pronunciation: / du wʌnz oʊn taɪm / (AmE), / duː wʌnz əʊn taɪm / (BrE)
to serve a punishment or prison sentence independently, without relying on others or involving them

Examples

to do one's own time in prison
After the trial, he accepted responsibility and chose to do his own time without blaming anyone else.
It's important to do your own time rather than get others in trouble for your mistakes.
She told her brother to do his own time and not drag her into his legal issues.
