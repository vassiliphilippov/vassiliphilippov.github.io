salt in the wound

noun
to make a bad situation worse
Pronunciation: / sɔlt ɪn ðə wund / (AmE), / sɒlt ɪn ðə wuːnd / (BrE)
something that makes an already bad or painful situation worse

Examples

add salt in the wound
Failing the exam was bad, but hearing others talk about their success added salt in the wound.
Losing the game was hard enough, but his teasing was salt in the wound.
When my boss criticized my work in front of everyone, it was salt in the wound.

Related Terms

  • synonym – add insult to injury: to make a bad situation even worse by doing something else to upset someone
