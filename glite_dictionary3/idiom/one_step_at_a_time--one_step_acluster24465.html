one step at a time

adverb
gradually; step by step
Pronunciation: / wʌn stɛp ət ə taɪm / (AmE), / wʌn stɛp ət ə taɪm / (BrE)
used to indicate that a task or goal should be approached gradually, dealing with each part or stage before moving to the next

Examples

take one step at a time
If you try to do everything at once, remember to take things one step at a time.
The therapist advised her to recover one step at a time after her surgery.
When facing a difficult situation, it's important to handle it one step at a time.
