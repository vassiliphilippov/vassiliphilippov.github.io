why me

interjection
expression of frustration or self-pity
Pronunciation: / waɪ mi / (AmE), / waɪ miː / (BrE)
used to express frustration or self-pity, especially when feeling unfairly treated or singled out for misfortune

Examples

why me after bad news
After hearing about the extra workload, she sighed and said, why me?
He couldn't believe his bad luck and muttered, why me, under his breath.
Whenever something goes wrong, he always asks, why me?
