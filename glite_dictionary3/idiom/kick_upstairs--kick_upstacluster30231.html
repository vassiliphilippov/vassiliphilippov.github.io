to kick upstairs

verb
pass responsibility to a higher authority
Pronunciation: / kɪk ʌpˈstɛrz / (AmE), / kɪk ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
to refer a problem or responsibility to a higher authority for a decision

Examples

to kick upstairs a problem
If you continue to have problems, don't hesitate to kick upstairs your concerns to someone more senior.
Rather than make a decision themselves, the committee chose to kick upstairs the proposal for further review.
When the team couldn't agree on a solution, they decided to kick upstairs the issue to management.

  • synonym – escalate: to refer something to someone with more authority
