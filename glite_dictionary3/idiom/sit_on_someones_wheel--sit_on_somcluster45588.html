to sit on someone's wheel

verb
cycle closely behind for a draft
Pronunciation: / sɪt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz wil / (AmE), / sɪt ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz wiːl / (BrE)
to ride very closely behind another cyclist in order to reduce air resistance and save energy

Examples

to sit on someone's wheel in a race
During the race, she chose to sit on someone's wheel to conserve energy before the final sprint.
He managed to sit on my wheel for most of the climb, only attacking near the top.
New riders are often surprised by how much easier it is to sit on someone's wheel in a strong headwind.

Related Terms

  • antonym – breakaway: a cyclist or group that has separated from the main group in a race
  • synonym – draft: to ride closely behind another cyclist to reduce wind resistance
