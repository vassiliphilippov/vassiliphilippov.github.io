as a matter of fact

adverb
used to emphasize the truth of a statement
Pronunciation: / ˌæz ə ˈmæt̬ɚ əv ˈfækt / (AmE), / ˌæz ə ˈmætə əv ˈfækt / (BrE)
used to emphasize the truth of a statement, typically to introduce information that contrasts with what has been said or expected

Examples

as a matter of fact to clarify
As a matter of fact, I have already finished the report you asked for.
As a matter of fact, it’s colder today than it was yesterday.
He didn’t forget his keys; as a matter of fact, he never even brought them.

Related Terms

  • synonym – in fact: used to emphasize a statement or reveal the truth
