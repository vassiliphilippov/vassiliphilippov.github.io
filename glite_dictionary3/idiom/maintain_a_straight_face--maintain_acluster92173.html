to maintain a straight face

verb
keep a serious expression
Pronunciation: / meɪnˈteɪn ə streɪt feɪs / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn ə streɪt feɪs / (BrE)
To manage to keep one's facial expression serious and not show emotion, especially laughter, in a situation where it is difficult to do so.

Examples

maintain a straight face during jokes
Despite the chaos in the room, the teacher managed to maintain a straight face.
He tried to maintain a straight face when his friend told a ridiculous story.
She struggled to maintain a straight face during the serious meeting after hearing the joke.

Related Terms

  • synonym – keep a straight face: to remain expressionless, especially when trying not to laugh
