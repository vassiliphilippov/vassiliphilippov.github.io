to run afoul of

verb
come into conflict with rules or the law
Pronunciation: / rʌn əˈfaʊl ʌv / (AmE), / rʌn əˈfaʊl ɒv / (BrE)
to come into conflict with or get into trouble with rules, laws, or someone in authority

Examples

run afoul of the law
He ran afoul of local customs while traveling in the remote village.
Several politicians have run afoul of ethics rules in recent years.
The company ran afoul of environmental regulations and was fined heavily.
