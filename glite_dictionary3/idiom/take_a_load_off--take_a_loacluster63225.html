to take a load off

verb
sit down and relax
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə loʊd ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk ə ləʊd ɒf / (BrE)
to sit down and rest, especially after standing or being active for a while

Examples

to take a load off your feet
After a long day at work, she just wanted to take a load off and watch TV.
Come in, take a load off, and I'll get you something to drink.
He told his friend to take a load off while he prepared dinner.
