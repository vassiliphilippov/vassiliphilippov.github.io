to get to one's feet

verb
stand up; recover after difficulty
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt tə wʌnz fit /; / ɡɛt tu wʌnz fit / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to stand up after sitting or lying down, often in response to something or after a setback

Examples

get to his feet quickly
After tripping on the stairs, it took him a moment to get to his feet again.
Despite losing her job, she managed to get to her feet within a few months.
The crowd watched in suspense as the injured player slowly tried to get to his feet.
After the national anthem began, the players got to their feet out of respect.
The entire audience got to their feet to applaud the speaker's powerful message.
When his name was called, he slowly got to his feet and walked to the stage.
