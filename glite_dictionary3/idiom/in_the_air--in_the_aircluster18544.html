in the air

adverb
felt or anticipated by many; pervasive feeling
Pronunciation: / ɪn ði ɛr / (AmE), / ɪn ðiː eə / (BrE)
If something is in the air, it is felt or sensed by many people and seems likely to happen.

Examples

in the air feeling excitement
During the festival, joy seemed to be in the air everywhere you looked.
There was excitement in the air as the crowd awaited the announcement.
With graduation approaching, a sense of anticipation was in the air.
