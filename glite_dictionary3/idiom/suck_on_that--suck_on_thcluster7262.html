x
taunting phrase expressing triumph or defiance
Pronunciation: / sʌk ɑn ðæt / (AmE), / sʌk ɒn ðat / (BrE)
(informal, often vulgar) Used as a taunt, boast, or challenge to someone after achieving something or proving a point, expressing triumph or defiance.

Examples

suck on that after winning
After making the winning shot, John turned to the crowd and yelled, 'suck on that!'
She aced her exam and texted her rival, 'suck on that!'
The comedian ended his roast with, 'suck on that,' drawing laughter from the audience.
