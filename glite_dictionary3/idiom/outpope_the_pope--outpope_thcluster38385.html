to outpope the Pope

verb
be more strict or moral than the authority
Pronunciation: / aʊtˌpoʊp ðə poʊp / (AmE), / aʊtˌpəʊp ðə pəʊp / (BrE)
to be more scrupulously strict or morally upright than the recognized authority, often to an excessive or unnecessary degree

Examples

to outpope the Pope on rules
Critics argued the legislation would outpope the Pope by imposing even stricter moral standards.
In his quest for purity, the new principal tried to outpope the Pope with school policies.
She tends to outpope the Pope during ethical discussions, never tolerating exceptions.
