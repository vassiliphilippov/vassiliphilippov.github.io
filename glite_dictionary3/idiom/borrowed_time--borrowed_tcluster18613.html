borrowed time

noun
period beyond expected limit, often with risk
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔːroʊd taɪm / (AmE), / ˈbɒr.əʊd taɪm / (BrE)
a period after an expected end or limit, especially when one continues to exist or function despite serious danger or risk

Examples

living on borrowed time
After surviving the surgery, the patient felt like he was living on borrowed time.
Environmental experts warn that we are on borrowed time if we do not act to address climate change.
The company has been operating on borrowed time since its major investor withdrew support.

  • synonym – reprieve: a temporary delay or suspension, especially from danger or punishment
