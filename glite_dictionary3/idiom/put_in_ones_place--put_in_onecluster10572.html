to put in one's place

verb
to correct or humiliate someone
Pronunciation: / pʊt ɪn wʌnz pleɪs / (AmE), / pʊt ɪn wʌnz pleɪs / (BrE)
to correct or humiliate someone, making them aware of their proper position, usually by demonstrating authority or superiority

Examples

put him in his place firmly
She finally put her colleague in her place during the heated discussion.
The manager didn't hesitate to put him in his place after the rude remark.
When the student acted out, the teacher calmly put him in his place.
