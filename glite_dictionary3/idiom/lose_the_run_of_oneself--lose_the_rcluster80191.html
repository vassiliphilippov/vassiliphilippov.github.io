to lose the run of oneself

verb
become confused or lose self-control
Pronunciation: / luːz ðə rʌn əv wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / luːz ðə rʌn əv wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to become confused, disoriented, or behave in an irrational or uncontrolled way, especially due to emotion or excitement

Examples

lose the run of oneself after success
After winning the award, he lost the run of himself and started acting arrogant.
Try not to lose the run of yourself in front of the crowd.
