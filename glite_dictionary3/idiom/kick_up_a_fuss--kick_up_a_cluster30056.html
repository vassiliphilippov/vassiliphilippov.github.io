to kick up a fuss

verb
complain loudly or cause a disturbance
Pronunciation: / kɪk ʌp ə fʌs / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp ə fʌs / (BrE)
to complain loudly or make a disturbance, often over something small or trivial

Examples

kick up a fuss over nothing
If you kick up a fuss every time something goes wrong, people may stop listening to you.
The customer started to kick up a fuss when her order arrived late.
There's no need to kick up a fuss; it was just a small mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym – keep quiet: to remain silent or not complain
  • synonym – make a scene: to behave in a loud or noticeable way in public because you are angry or upset
  • synonym – raise a ruckus: to create a commotion or disturbance
