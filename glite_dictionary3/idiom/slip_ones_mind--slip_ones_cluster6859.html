to slip one's mind

verb
to be forgotten or overlooked
Pronunciation: / slɪp wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / slɪp wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
If something slips your mind, you forget to do it or fail to remember it.

Examples

slip my mind completely
Her birthday slipped my mind this year, and I forgot to send a card.
I meant to reserve a table, but it slipped my mind until it was too late.
I'm sorry I didn't call you back; it completely slipped my mind.
