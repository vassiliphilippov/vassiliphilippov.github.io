to bombard with questions

verb
overwhelm with many questions
Pronunciation: / bɑmˈbɑrd wɪð ˈkwɛs.tʃənz / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɑːd wɪð ˈkwɛs.tʃ(ə)nz / (BrE)
to ask someone so many questions in quick succession that they become overwhelmed or unable to answer easily

Examples

bombard with questions during interview
During the interview, the celebrity was bombarded with questions about his recent movie.
Reporters bombarded the mayor with questions at the press conference.
When the teacher arrived, the students immediately bombarded her with questions about the exam.
