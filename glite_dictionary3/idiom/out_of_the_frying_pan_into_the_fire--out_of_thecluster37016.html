out of the frying pan, into the fire

x
from a bad to a worse situation
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ðə ˈfraɪɪŋ ˌpæn ˌɪntu ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ˈfraɪɪŋ ˌpæn ˌɪntuː ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
used to describe a situation where someone escapes a bad or difficult situation only to find themselves in an even worse one

Examples

out of the frying pan, into the fire with a new problem
After quitting her stressful job, she found her new one was even worse—truly out of the frying pan, into the fire.
He moved to a new apartment to escape noisy neighbors, but ended up next to a construction site—out of the frying pan, into the fire.
It was out of the frying pan, into the fire when the company switched managers during the crisis.
