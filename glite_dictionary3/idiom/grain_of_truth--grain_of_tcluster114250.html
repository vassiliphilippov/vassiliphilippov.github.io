grain of truth

noun
a slight element of truth
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪn əv truθ / (AmE), / ɡreɪn əv truːθ / (BrE)
a small but significant amount of truth within a statement, rumor, or story that is otherwise untrue or exaggerated

Examples

a grain of truth in a story
Every legend has a grain of truth at its core, however improbable the rest may seem.
Rumors often spread quickly, even when they contain only a grain of truth.
There may be a grain of truth in his theory, but most of it is speculation.

Related Terms

  • synonym – kernel of truth: a small part of a statement that is true
