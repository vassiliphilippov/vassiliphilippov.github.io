to the bottom falls out of

verb
sudden collapse or failure
Pronunciation: / ðə ˈbɑtəm fɔlz aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / ðə ˈbɒtəm fɔːlz aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which something suddenly fails completely or becomes much worse, especially after a period of stability

Examples

the bottom falls out of the market
After the new regulations were imposed, the bottom fell out of the housing market.
If the bottom falls out of the company’s profits, employees could lose their jobs.
When interest rates rose sharply, the bottom fell out of investment funds.

Related Terms

  • synonym – collapse: to fall down or fail suddenly and completely
  • synonym – crash: a sudden failure, especially financially
