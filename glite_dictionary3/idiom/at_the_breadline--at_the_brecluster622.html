at the breadline

adverb
living at poverty level
Pronunciation: / ət ðə ˈbrɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ət ðə ˈbrɛdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
living at or very near the level of poverty, often just able to afford basic necessities.

Examples

at the breadline financially
Many families have found themselves at the breadline after the economic downturn.
Rising costs pushed more people at the breadline this winter.
She grew up at the breadline, with her parents struggling to afford food and rent.

Related Terms

  • synonym – below the poverty line: earning less than the minimum income needed for basic living
  • synonym – on the breadline: experiencing poverty or subsistence existence
  • antonym – prosperous: having wealth and material comfort
