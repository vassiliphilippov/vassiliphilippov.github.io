to find one's tongue

verb
regain ability to speak after silence
Pronunciation: / faɪnd wʌnz tʌŋ / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnz tʌŋ / (BrE)
to regain the ability to speak, especially after being temporarily silent from shock or surprise

Examples

finally find one's tongue
After moments of stunned silence, she finally found her tongue and answered the question.
As soon as the excitement died down, the child found his tongue and started asking questions.
He was so surprised by the news that it took him a while to find his tongue.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lose one's tongue: to be unable to speak because of shock or nervousness
