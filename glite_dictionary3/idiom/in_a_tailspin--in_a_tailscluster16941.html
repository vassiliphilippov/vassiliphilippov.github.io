in a tailspin

preposition
in a state of crisis or confusion
Pronunciation: / ɪn ə ˈteɪlspɪn / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈteɪlspɪn / (BrE)
in a state of sudden confusion, panic, or crisis, often resulting in loss of control or stability

When the stock market crashed, investors found themselves in a tailspin of uncertainty.
