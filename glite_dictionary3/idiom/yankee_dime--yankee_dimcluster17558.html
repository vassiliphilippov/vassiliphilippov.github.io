Yankee dime

noun
trivial amount; small favor (idiom)
Pronunciation: / ˈjæŋki daɪm / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki daɪm / (BrE)
a small, trivial amount of money or a minor favor, often used jokingly to mean something given as a token rather than real payment

Examples

a Yankee dime as payment
A Yankee dime doesn’t pay the bills, but it can lift your spirits.
After hours of help, all she gave me was a Yankee dime.
He offered nothing but a Yankee dime for all my trouble.
