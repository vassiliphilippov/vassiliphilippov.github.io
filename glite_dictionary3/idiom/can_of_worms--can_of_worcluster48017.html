can of worms

noun
situation causing more problems
Pronunciation: / kæn əv wɜrmz / (AmE), / kæn əv wɜːmz / (BrE)
a situation or problem that, once started or revealed, is likely to lead to many more complications and difficulties

Examples

open a can of worms
Changing the contract now could turn into a can of worms for everyone involved.
If you investigate further, you might be opening a can of worms that you can't close.
Mentioning the budget at the meeting opened a real can of worms.
