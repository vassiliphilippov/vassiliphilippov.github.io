call to arms

noun
strong appeal to take action or mobilize
Pronunciation: / kɔl tə ɑrmz / (AmE), / kɔːl tʊ ɑːmz / (BrE)
a strong appeal or demand for people to take action, especially in support of a cause or in preparation for conflict

Examples

issue a call to arms
After the disaster, the charity issued a call to arms for donations and support.
His article acted as a call to arms for people to join the environmental movement.
The politician's speech was a call to arms for voters to demand change.
