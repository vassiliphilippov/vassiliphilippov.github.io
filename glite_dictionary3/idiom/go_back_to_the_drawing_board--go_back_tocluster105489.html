to go back to the drawing board

verb
start over after failure
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ bæk tə ðə ˈdrɔɪŋ bɔrd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bæk tə ðə ˈdrɔːɪŋ bɔːd / (BrE)
to start again from the beginning, especially after a plan or idea has failed

Examples

to go back to the drawing board after failure
After our proposal was rejected, we had to go back to the drawing board and create a new plan.
Despite their best efforts, the team had to go back to the drawing board several times before succeeding.
If the test results aren't promising, we'll simply go back to the drawing board.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give up: to stop trying due to failure or difficulty
  • synonym – start over: to begin something again from the beginning
