to hang by a thread

verb
be in a very precarious situation
Pronunciation: / hæŋ baɪ ə θɹɛd / (AmE), / hæŋ baɪ ə θɹɛd / (BrE)
to be in a dangerous or delicate situation where only a small thing prevents disaster or failure

Examples

hang by a thread after a scandal
After the scandal, the politician's career hung by a thread for months.
His recovery hangs by a thread as doctors continue to monitor his condition.
The company is hanging by a thread and could go bankrupt at any time.
