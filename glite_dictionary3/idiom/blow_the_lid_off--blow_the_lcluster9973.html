to blow the lid off

verb
reveal hidden or secret information
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ðə lɪd ɔf / (AmE), / bləʊ ðə lɪd ɒf / (BrE)
to reveal or expose secret or hidden information, especially something scandalous or shocking

Examples

blow the lid off a scandal
Her new book promises to blow the lid off the entertainment industry's secrets.
The press investigation finally blew the lid off the government's illegal activities.
The whistleblower's testimony could blow the lid off the corruption scheme.

Related Terms

  • antonym – cover up: to conceal or hide the truth about something
  • synonym – expose: to reveal something hidden or secret
