hand to mouth

adverb
with just enough for immediate needs
Pronunciation: / hænd tə maʊθ / (AmE), / hænd tə maʊθ / (BrE)
with only just enough money or food to meet immediate needs; living from day to day without saving

Examples

live hand to mouth
After losing his job, he and his family lived hand to mouth for several months.
Despite working two jobs, she was still living hand to mouth.
Many artists in the early days survive hand to mouth until their work is recognized.

Related Terms

  • antonym – comfortable: having more than enough resources; opposite of hand to mouth
  • synonym – scraping by: managing to survive with barely enough; synonym for hand to mouth
