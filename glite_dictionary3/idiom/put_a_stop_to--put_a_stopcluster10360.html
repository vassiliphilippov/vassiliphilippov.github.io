to put a stop to

verb
to bring something to an end
Pronunciation: / pʊt ə stɑp tu / (AmE), / pʊt ə stɒp tʊ / (BrE)
to bring an end to something; to cause something to cease or be discontinued

Examples

put a stop to bad behavior
After months of late-night parties, the landlord finally put a stop to the noise.
Environmentalists are urging the government to put a stop to illegal logging.
The principal decided to put a stop to bullying at the school.
