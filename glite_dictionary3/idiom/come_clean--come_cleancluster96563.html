to come clean

verb
confess or admit the truth
Pronunciation: / kʌm klin / (AmE), / kʌm kliːn / (BrE)
to confess or tell the truth about something previously hidden or kept secret

Examples

to come clean about a mistake
After weeks of denial, he finally decided to come clean about the incident.
It's always better to come clean than to keep lying and get into trouble.
She knew she had to come clean with her parents about breaking the vase.
