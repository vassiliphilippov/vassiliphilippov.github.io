to pave the way

verb
make something possible or easier to happen
Pronunciation: / peɪv ðə weɪ / (AmE), / peɪv ðə weɪ / (BrE)
to make it possible or easier for something to happen in the future by preparing for it

Examples

pave the way for change
Digital technology is paving the way for new forms of communication.
Her research paved the way for important medical advances.
The agreement will pave the way for future cooperation between the two companies.

Related Terms

  • antonym – block the way: to prevent something from happening by creating obstacles
  • synonym – prepare the ground: to do what is necessary before an event or action can occur
