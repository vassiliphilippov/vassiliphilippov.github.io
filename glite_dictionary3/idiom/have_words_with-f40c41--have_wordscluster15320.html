to have words with

verb
talk to someone, often to criticise
Pronunciation: / hæv wɝːdz wɪθ / (AmE), / hæv wɜːdz wɪð / (BrE)
to speak to someone, usually to express disapproval or to discuss a problem, often implying a confrontation or criticism.

Examples

have words with a colleague
If you don't stop being rude, I'll have words with you.
She had words with her neighbor after the loud party.
The manager plans to have words with the team about their performance.
