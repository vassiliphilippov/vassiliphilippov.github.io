the hard way

adverb
by learning through difficulties or mistakes
Pronunciation: / ðə hɑrd weɪ / (AmE), / ðə hɑːd weɪ / (BrE)
by experiencing difficulties or making mistakes, rather than taking an easier or more straightforward path

Examples

learn the hard way
He found out the hard way that shortcuts rarely save time in the long run.
She learned the hard way not to leave her keys in the car.
They had to discover how the equipment worked the hard way, by trial and error.
