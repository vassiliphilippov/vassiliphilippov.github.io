to take the Fifth

verb
refuse to answer to avoid self-incrimination
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə fɪfθ / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fɪfθ / (BrE)
to refuse to answer questions in a court of law or other legal setting by invoking the Fifth Amendment right against self-incrimination in the United States.

Examples

take the Fifth in court
Many witnesses began taking the Fifth after their lawyers advised them not to testify.
She repeatedly took the Fifth when questioned by the prosecution in court.
When asked about his involvement in the scandal, the senator chose to take the Fifth during the hearing.
