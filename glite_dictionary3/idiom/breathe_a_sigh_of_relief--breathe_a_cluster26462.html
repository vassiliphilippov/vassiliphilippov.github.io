to breathe a sigh of relief

verb
show relief after stress
Pronunciation: / briːð ə saɪ əv rɪˈlif / (AmE), / briːð ə saɪ əv rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
to show or feel great relief after a period of worry or stress

Examples

breathe a sigh of relief after bad news
Everyone breathed a sigh of relief when the missing child was found safe.
I breathed a sigh of relief after hearing the good news from the doctor.
She breathed a sigh of relief when her presentation went better than expected.

  • synonym – relax: to become less tense or anxious
