to sleep a wink

verb
not get any sleep at all (idiom)
Pronunciation: / sliːp ə wɪŋk / (AmE), / sliːp ə wɪŋk / (BrE)
(with negative) to get any sleep at all; usually used to emphasize that someone did not sleep at all.

Examples

not sleep a wink
Despite the noise outside, she managed to sleep a wink for a few hours.
He looked exhausted, claiming he hadn't sleep a wink in days.
I was so nervous before the exam that I didn't sleep a wink all night.
