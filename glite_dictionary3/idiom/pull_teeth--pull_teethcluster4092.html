to pull teeth

verb
to do something very difficult
Pronunciation: / pʊl tiθ / (AmE), / pʊl tiːθ / (BrE)
to do something that is very difficult or requires a great deal of effort, especially to get information or cooperation from someone

Examples

like pulling teeth answers
Getting him to explain what happened was like pulling teeth.
It's pulling teeth trying to get a straight answer from her.
Sometimes negotiating with them feels like you're pulling teeth.
