primrose path

noun
an easy, pleasurable path (often leading to trouble)
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪmˌroʊz pæθ / (AmE), / ˈprɪmrəʊz pɑːθ / (BrE)
a way of life that is easy and pleasurable, but may lead to ruin or disaster

Examples

led down the primrose path
He was led down the primrose path by wealth and temptation, only to regret it later.
Many novels warn readers about following the primrose path to ruin.
She refused to be lured onto the primrose path of easy living.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bed of roses: a situation or life of ease and comfort
  • antonym – straight and narrow: a morally upright or honest course of conduct
