at an impasse

adverb
at a standstill or deadlock
Pronunciation: / ət ən ˈɪmˌpæs / (AmE), / ət ən ˈɪmpɑːs / (BrE)
in a situation where no progress can be made or no agreement can be reached; at a standstill

Examples

at an impasse in talks
After hours of debate, the committee was at an impasse over the budget proposal.
Negotiations between the two countries were at an impasse by the end of the summit.
The labor talks remained at an impasse for several weeks, delaying any resolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deadlock: a situation where progress is impossible because of disagreement
  • synonym – stalemate: a situation in which further action is blocked
