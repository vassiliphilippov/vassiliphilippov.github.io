to go south

verb
to decline or worsen (informal)
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ saʊθ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ saʊθ / (BrE)
(informal) to decline, fail, or get worse (used about situations or conditions)

Examples

go south after problems
After we lost our biggest client, profits began to go south rapidly.
She invested in tech stocks just before the market started to go south.
The project was going well, but things began to go south when funding was cut.
After the new manager arrived, everything at the factory went south.
Negotiations quickly went south after both sides refused to compromise.
Our vacation plans went south when the airline canceled our flights.

  • synonym – deteriorate: to become worse
  • synonym – fail: to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • antonym – go well: to proceed in a positive and successful way
