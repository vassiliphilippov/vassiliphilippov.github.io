hot and cold

adjective
inconsistently changing in feelings or attitude
Pronunciation: / hɑt ənd koʊld / (AmE), / hɒt ənd kəʊld / (BrE)
describing a person whose feelings, behavior, or attitude changes frequently and unpredictably, especially between enthusiasm and indifference

Examples

hot and cold about plans
It’s hard to rely on someone who is always hot and cold when it comes to plans.
James has been hot and cold about accepting the job offer, which is frustrating for the recruiter.
Their friendship grew strained because she would run hot and cold with her affections.

Related Terms

  • synonym – inconsistent: not staying the same throughout; lacking consistency
  • antonym – steady: not changing and remaining constant over a period of time
