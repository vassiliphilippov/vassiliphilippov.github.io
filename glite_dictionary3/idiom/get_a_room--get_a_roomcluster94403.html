to get a room

verb
ask affectionate people to be private
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ə ɹum / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə ɹuːm / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell people who are showing romantic or sexual affection in public that they should do so in private.

Examples

to get a room for kissing
If you're going to kiss like that in front of everyone, maybe you should get a room.
Their constant flirting made everyone want to yell, "Get a room!"
When the couple started cuddling at the table, her friend laughed and said, "Go get a room!"
