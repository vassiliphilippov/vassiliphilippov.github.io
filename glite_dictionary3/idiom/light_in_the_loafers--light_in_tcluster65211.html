light in the loafers

adjective
suggesting someone is effeminate or homosexual (offensive)
Pronunciation: / laɪt ɪn ðə ˈloʊfərz / (AmE), / laɪt ɪn ðə ˈləʊfəz / (BrE)
(offensive, informal) Used to suggest that a man is effeminate or gay, often in a derogatory or mocking way.

Examples

He was often called light in the loafers by classmates who made jokes about his mannerisms.
Some people wrongly assume someone is light in the loafers based on their voice or clothing choices.
The movie uses the phrase light in the loafers to stereotype a character, which many find offensive today.
