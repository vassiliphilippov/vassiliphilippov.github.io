all very well

adverb
acceptable but not sufficient
Pronunciation: / ɔl ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (AmE), / ɔːl ˌvɛri ˈwɛl / (BrE)
used to indicate that something may be acceptable or correct in theory, but is not enough or not suitable in practice

Examples

all very well in theory
Her suggestion was all very well, but it didn't solve the underlying problem.
It's all very well to say you'll help, but will you actually do it?
That's all very well for you, but some of us can't afford it.
