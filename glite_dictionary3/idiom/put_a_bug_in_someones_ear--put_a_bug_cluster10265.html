to put a bug in someone's ear

verb
give someone a suggestion or idea
Pronunciation: / pʊt ə bʌɡ ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪr / (AmE), / pʊt ə bʌɡ ɪn ˈsʌmwənz ɪə / (BrE)
to suggest an idea or hint to someone, often to prompt future action or consideration

Examples

to put a bug in someone's ear about an idea

If you want something done, it helps to put a bug in someone's ear early on.
She put a bug in my ear regarding possible changes at the company.
