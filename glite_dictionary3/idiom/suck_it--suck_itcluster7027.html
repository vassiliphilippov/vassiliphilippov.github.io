to suck it

verb
accept criticism or defeat without complaint (slang)
Pronunciation: / sʌk ɪt / (AmE), / sʌk ɪt / (BrE)
(slang) Used to tell someone to accept criticism, defeat, or an unpleasant situation without complaining.

Examples

suck it up bravely
If you can't handle a little criticism, you need to learn to suck it.
She wasn't happy with the outcome, but was told to suck it and move on.
When his team lost the match, he just told the disappointed fans to suck it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – deal with it: to accept and cope with a situation as it is
  • synonym – put up with: to tolerate or endure something
