pack of lies

noun
a collection of deliberate falsehoods
Pronunciation: / pæk əv laɪz / (AmE), / pæk əv laɪz / (BrE)
a group or series of completely untrue statements or stories; a fabricated account intended to deceive

Examples

a pack of lies story
Don't believe his story—it's nothing more than a pack of lies.
She realized too late that his promises were just a pack of lies.
The witness's testimony was exposed as a pack of lies during the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym – lie: a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
  • antonym – truth: something that is true; the real facts
