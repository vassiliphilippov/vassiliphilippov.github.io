to turn a deaf ear

verb
refuse to listen; ignore
Pronunciation: / tɝn ə dɛf ɪɚ / (AmE), / tɜːn ə dɛf ɪə / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or refuse to listen to someone or something

Examples

turn a deaf ear to complaints
Despite repeated warnings, the company continued to turn a deaf ear to environmental concerns.
The government cannot afford to turn a deaf ear to the needs of its citizens.
Whenever people tried to give him advice, he would turn a deaf ear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – ignore: to pay no attention to something or someone
  • antonym – pay attention: to listen to or take notice of something
