flying blind

adjective
acting without guidance or knowledge
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈblaɪnd / (BrE)
acting without guidance, information, or clear understanding, especially in a difficult or uncertain situation

Examples

flying blind on a project
After the manager quit suddenly, the team was flying blind on the new project.
Making investment decisions without research is simply flying blind.
Without clear instructions, the students felt like they were flying blind.
