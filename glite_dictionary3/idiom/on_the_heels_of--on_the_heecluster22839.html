on the heels of

preposition
immediately after something
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə hilz əv / (AmE), / ɒn ðə hiːlz ɒv / (BrE)
happening very soon after or as a direct result of an event.

Examples

on the heels of a disaster
A strong storm hit the coast on the heels of last week's flooding.
Profits soared on the heels of the company's rebranding efforts.
Several new regulations were implemented on the heels of the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym – immediately after: at once following something
  • synonym – in the wake of: following closely behind, especially as a result of something
