to get a load of

verb
draw attention to something surprising
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ə loʊd əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə ləʊd əv / (BrE)
(informal) Used to draw someone's attention to something surprising or noteworthy; to look at or notice something carefully.

Examples

get a load of that car
Get a load of that car over there—it's covered in neon lights!
He said, 'Get a load of her new haircut!'
When you have a minute, get a load of this report; the results are incredible.
