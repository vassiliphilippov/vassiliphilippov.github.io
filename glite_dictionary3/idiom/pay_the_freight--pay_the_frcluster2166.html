to pay the freight

verb
accept consequences or bear costs
Pronunciation: / peɪ ðə freɪt / (AmE), / peɪ ðə freɪt / (BrE)
to accept the consequences of one's actions or to bear the costs or expenses involved in something

Examples

pay the freight for mistakes
After the accident, the company was forced to pay the freight for its poor safety record.
He knew he'd have to pay the freight for his risky investment.
If you want the upgraded package, you'll have to pay the freight yourself.

Related Terms

  • synonym – face the music: to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
  • synonym – foot the bill: to pay the entire cost of something
