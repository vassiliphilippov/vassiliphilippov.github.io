to take the count

verb
be counted out in boxing
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə kaʊnt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə kaʊnt / (BrE)
(of a boxer) to remain down on the floor during the referee's ten count, resulting in a knockout defeat.

Examples

take the count after knockout
After a powerful uppercut, the boxer decided to take the count rather than get up.
He fought bravely, but in the end he took the count and lost the match.
The champion was knocked to the mat and had to take the count in the final round.
