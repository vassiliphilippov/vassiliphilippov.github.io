far be it from

x
used to deny intent or claim
Pronunciation: / fɑr bi ɪt frəm / (AmE), / fɑː biː ɪt frəm / (BrE)
used to say that someone certainly does not want or intend to do something, often appearing before denying involvement or intent

Examples

far be it from me to judge
Far be it from anyone to dissuade you from pursuing your goals.
Far be it from me to criticize your choices, but I think there might be a better way.
Far be it from us to judge, but the facts must be stated clearly.
