to take a crack at

verb
attempt or try (informal idiom)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə kræk æt / (AmE), / teɪk ə kræk ət / (BrE)
to attempt or try to do something, often for the first time or to see if one can succeed

Examples

to take a crack at fixing
If you don't mind, I'd like to take a crack at fixing the computer myself.
She decided to take a crack at writing a novel during the summer.
Why not take a crack at the crossword if you're feeling bored?
