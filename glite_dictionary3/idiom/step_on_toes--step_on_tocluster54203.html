to step on toes

verb
offend or upset someone
Pronunciation: / stɛp ɑn toʊz / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn təʊz / (BrE)
to offend or upset someone by interfering in their area of responsibility or authority

Examples

step on toes at work
By offering her suggestions, she unintentionally stepped on his toes during the meeting.
He's careful not to step on toes when introducing new policies.
I don't want to step on anyone's toes by taking over their project at work.
