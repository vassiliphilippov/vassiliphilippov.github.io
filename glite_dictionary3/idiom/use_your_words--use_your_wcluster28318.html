to use your words

verb
encourage verbal expression of feelings or needs
Pronunciation: / juːz jɔːr wɝːdz / (AmE), / juːz jɔː wɜːdz / (BrE)
to express one's feelings, needs, or thoughts verbally rather than through actions or silence, often said to encourage clear communication.

Examples

use your words to explain
If you need help, please use your words to tell me what's wrong.
Teachers often remind young children to use your words when they are upset.
When you feel angry, try to use your words instead of shouting.
