to never hear the end of it

verb
be persistently reminded of something
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛvɚ hɪr ðə ˈɛnd əv ɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɛvə hɪə ði ˈɛnd əv ɪt / (BrE)
to be reminded of or confronted with something (usually annoying or embarrassing) repeatedly and for a long time, especially by others

Examples

never hear the end of it after a mistake
He keeps teasing me about my old haircut—I’ll never hear the end of it!
If I tell her I crashed the car, I'll never hear the end of it.
Whenever I make a mistake at work, I never hear the end of it from my boss.

Related Terms

  • synonym – bring up: mention something repeatedly
  • antonym – let it go: to stop mentioning or worrying about something
