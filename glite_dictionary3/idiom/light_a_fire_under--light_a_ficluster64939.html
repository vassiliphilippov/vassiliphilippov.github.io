to light a fire under

verb
motivate or spur someone to act
Pronunciation: / laɪt ə ˈfaɪɚ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / laɪt ə ˈfaɪə ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to motivate or energize someone to take action, often more quickly or with greater enthusiasm

Examples

to light a fire under a team
Her urgent tone really lit a fire under us, so we finished the work on time.
Sometimes, you need to light a fire under your employees to boost productivity.
The coach was determined to light a fire under the team before the big game.

Related Terms

  • antonym – demotivate: to make someone less eager to do something
  • synonym – prod: to stimulate or motivate someone into action
  • synonym – spur on: to encourage or urge someone to do something
