to keep one's distance (from)

verb
avoid close contact or involvement
Pronunciation: / kip wʌnz ˈdɪstəns frəm / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ˈdɪstəns frɒm / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid close contact or involvement with someone or something; to maintain separation.

Examples

keep her distance from a colleague
After the argument, she decided to keep her distance from her colleague for a while.
Even at parties, he prefers to keep his distance from people he doesn't know well.
The wildlife experts advise tourists to keep their distance from the animals for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym – approach: to come near or closer to something or someone
  • synonym – avoid: to keep away from or not do something
