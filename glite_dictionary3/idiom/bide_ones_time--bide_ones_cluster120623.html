to bide one's time

verb
wait patiently for the right time
Pronunciation: / baɪd wʌnz taɪm / (AmE), / baɪd wʌnz taɪm / (BrE)
to wait patiently for a suitable or favorable opportunity before acting

Examples

to bide one's time for a chance
After being passed over for promotion, he decided to bide his time until a better opportunity arose.
Investors often bide their time during volatile markets before making new investments.
She kept a low profile, biding her time until she could launch her own business.

Related Terms

  • antonym – seize the moment: to act immediately when an opportunity presents itself
  • synonym – wait: to stay in a place or remain inactive until something happens
