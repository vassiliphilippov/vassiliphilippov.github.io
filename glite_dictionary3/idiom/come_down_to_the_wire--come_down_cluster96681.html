to come down to the wire

verb
reach a critical point near a deadline
Pronunciation: / kʌm daʊn tə ðə ˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn tə ðə ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
to reach a point at which the outcome is not decided until the very last moment, especially just before a deadline or at the end of a competition

Examples

come down to the wire in a race
Her decision about which college to attend came down to the wire, and she chose just before the deadline.
Negotiations came down to the wire, with both sides refusing to compromise until the last minute.
The championship basketball game came down to the wire, ending with a buzzer-beating shot.

  • synonym – go down to the wire: be decided at the last possible moment
