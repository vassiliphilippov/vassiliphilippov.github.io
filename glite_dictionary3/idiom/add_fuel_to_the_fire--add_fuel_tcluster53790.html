to add fuel to the fire

verb
make a bad situation worse
Pronunciation: / æd ˈfjuːəl tə ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / æd ˈfjʊːəl tə ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to make a bad situation worse by saying or doing something that increases anger, excitement, or trouble

Examples

add fuel to the fire with comments
His sarcastic remarks only added fuel to the fire during the heated discussion.
If you criticize her now, you'll just add fuel to the fire and make things worse.
Instead of helping, his latest tweet added fuel to the fire of public outrage.

Related Terms

  • synonym – make matters worse: to worsen a situation
  • antonym – pour oil on the flames: to calm or soothe a tense situation (opposite meaning)
