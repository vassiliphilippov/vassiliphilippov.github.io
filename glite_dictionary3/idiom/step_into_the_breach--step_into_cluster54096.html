to step into the breach

verb
take action in a difficult situation
Pronunciation: / stɛp ˈɪntu ðə britʃ / (AmE), / stɛp ˈɪntuː ðə briːtʃ / (BrE)
to take responsibility or action in a situation where someone else is unable or unwilling to do so, especially in a difficult or emergency moment

Examples

to step into the breach during crisis
During the teacher's absence, a substitute stepped into the breach to keep the class on track.
When a volunteer dropped out, Mark quickly stepped into the breach to help.
When the manager fell ill, Sarah stepped into the breach without hesitation.
