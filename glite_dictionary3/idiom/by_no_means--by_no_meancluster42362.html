by no means

adverb
not at all; definitely not
Pronunciation: / baɪ ˈnoʊ ˌminz /; / baɪ noʊ minz / (AmE), / baɪ ˈnəʊ ˌmiːnz /; / baɪ nəʊ miːnz / (BrE)
used to strongly say that something is not true or not possible; not at all

Examples

by no means certain
She is by no means convinced that this is the best solution.
The outcome of the project is by no means guaranteed despite everyone's hard work.
The weather is by no means perfect for a picnic today.
By no means should you disclose this information to anyone outside the company.
Success is by no means guaranteed, even with hard work.
The work is by no means finished yet, so please be patient.

Related Terms

  • antonym – by all means: certainly; of course (opposite meaning)
  • antonym – by any means: in any way at all; whatsoever
  • synonym – not at all: not in any way or to any degree
