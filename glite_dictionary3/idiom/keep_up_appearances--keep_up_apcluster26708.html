to keep up appearances

verb
maintain a false show to protect one's reputation
Pronunciation: / kip ʌp əˈpɪrənsɪz / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp əˈpɪərənsɪz / (BrE)
to pretend that everything is normal or satisfactory, especially to maintain one's reputation or social standing, even if that is not the case

Examples

keep up appearances at parties
Even as their business was failing, they continued to keep up appearances in front of their friends.
It takes a lot of effort to keep up appearances when your personal life is falling apart.
She felt obliged to keep up appearances at the party, even though she was unhappy.

Related Terms

  • antonym – let one's guard down: to stop pretending or hiding one's feelings
  • synonym – save face: to avoid humiliation or preserve dignity
