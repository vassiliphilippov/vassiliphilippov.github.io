to get a life

verb
do something more interesting or meaningful
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ə laɪf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə laɪf / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone to do something more interesting or worthwhile with their time, usually when seen as boring, unproductive, or overly concerned with trivial matters.

Examples

to get a life and travel
After years of working nonstop, he decided to get a life and travel the world.
If you spend all day watching television, you really should get a life.
It’s time for you to get a life and stop worrying about other people’s business.
