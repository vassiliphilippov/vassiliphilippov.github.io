to take a bullet

verb
endure risk or hardship for another
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
to endure pain, hardship, or risk, especially for the sake of protecting or helping someone else

Examples

to take a bullet for a friend
During tough negotiations, a good manager might take a bullet for the team.
He didn’t hesitate to take a bullet when his friend needed him most.
She would take a bullet for her little brother without hesitation.
