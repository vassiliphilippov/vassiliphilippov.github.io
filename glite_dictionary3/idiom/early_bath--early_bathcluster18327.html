early bath

noun
premature removal from a game or event
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrli bæθ / (AmE), / ˈɜːli bɑːθ / (BrE)
being sent off or removed from a game or activity before its normal end, often as a punishment or penalty; especially used in sports contexts.

Examples

get an early bath after a foul
After the red card, the referee gave him an early bath in just the 20th minute.
Missing training sessions could earn anyone an early bath from the team.
When she argued with the manager, she was given an early bath and left the meeting early.
