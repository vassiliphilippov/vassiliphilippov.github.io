to come to judgement

verb
reach a decision or verdict
Pronunciation: / kʌm tə ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / kʌm tə ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
to make a decision or pronounce a verdict; to reach a final conclusion, especially in a situation involving authority or resolution of a dispute.

Examples

to come to judgement on a case
After reviewing all the evidence, the committee finally came to judgement on the case.
She hesitated to come to judgement without hearing both sides of the story.
The panel must come to judgement before the end of the week.

Related Terms

  • synonym – judge: to form an opinion or conclusion about something or someone
