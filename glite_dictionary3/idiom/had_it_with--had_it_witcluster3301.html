to had it with

verb
lost tolerance or patience
Pronunciation: / ˈhæd ɪt wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈhæd ɪt wɪð / (BrE)
(informal) To be no longer able or willing to tolerate someone or something; to have reached one's limit of patience or endurance.

Examples

had it with his attitude
I've had it with your complaining and negativity.
She finally had it with the broken promises and walked away.
We had it with the constant delays, so we called management.
