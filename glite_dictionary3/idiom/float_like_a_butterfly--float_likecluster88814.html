to float like a butterfly

verb
move gracefully and lightly
Pronunciation: / floʊt laɪk ə ˈbʌtɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / fləʊt laɪk ə ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
to move with light, graceful, and agile steps, often with a sense of ease and elegance

Examples

to float like a butterfly on stage
During the performance, the ballerina seemed to float like a butterfly across the stage.
He tried to float like a butterfly while sparring in boxing practice.
Her footsteps were so light, she appeared to float like a butterfly through the garden.
