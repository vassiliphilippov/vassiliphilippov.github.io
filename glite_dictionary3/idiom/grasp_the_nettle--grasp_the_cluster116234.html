to grasp the nettle

verb
confront something difficult directly
Pronunciation: / ɡræsp ðə ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp ðə ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
to confront a difficult or unpleasant situation directly and with determination

Examples

The government must grasp the nettle and implement necessary reforms.
