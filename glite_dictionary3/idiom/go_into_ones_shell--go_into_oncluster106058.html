to go into one's shell

verb
become withdrawn or shy
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈɪntu ˈwʌnz ʃɛl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntu ˈwʌnz ʃɛl / (BrE)
(of a person) to become withdrawn and uncommunicative, especially as a reaction to shyness or anxiety.

Examples

to go into one's shell after criticism
After the argument, he began to go into his shell and avoided talking to anyone.
It's important not to go into your shell after experiencing setbacks.
Whenever she feels uncomfortable at parties, she tends to go into her shell.

Related Terms

  • antonym – open up: to become more communicative and less reserved
  • synonym – retreat: to move back or withdraw, often for protection
  • synonym – withdraw: to move back or away, become less involved
