to cast the first stone

verb
to be the first to criticize or accuse
Pronunciation: / kæst ðə fɝst stoʊn / (AmE), / kɑːst ðə fɜːst stəʊn / (BrE)
to be the first to criticize or accuse someone, especially of a fault that the critic may also have

Examples

to cast the first stone at someone
Before you cast the first stone, remember that you have made similar mistakes yourself.
He hesitated to cast the first stone, fearing he was not blameless either.
No one in the meeting was willing to cast the first stone against the manager.

Related Terms

  • synonym – accuse: to say that someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • synonym – criticize: to express disapproval or find fault with someone or something
