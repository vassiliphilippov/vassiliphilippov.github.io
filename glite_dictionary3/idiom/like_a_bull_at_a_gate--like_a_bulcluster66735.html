like a bull at a gate

adverb
with excessive eagerness or impatience
Pronunciation: / laɪk ə bʊl ət ə ɡeɪt / (AmE), / laɪk ə bʊl ət ə ɡeɪt / (BrE)
with great eagerness or impatience, often in a sudden, forceful, or reckless manner

Examples

rushes like a bull at a gate
He jumped into the conversation like a bull at a gate, barely waiting for his turn to speak.
She tends to tackle new projects like a bull at a gate, without thinking them through.
The puppy dashed through the open door like a bull at a gate, eager to explore the garden.

  • synonym – headlong: with the head leading; in a rush, with reckless haste
  • synonym – recklessly: in a way that shows a lack of caution or care
