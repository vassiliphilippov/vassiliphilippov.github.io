to hide in plain sight

verb
to be obvious but unnoticed
Pronunciation: / haɪd ɪn pleɪn saɪt / (AmE), / haɪd ɪn pleɪn saɪt / (BrE)
To be easily noticeable or obvious but not recognized or detected by others.

Examples

hide in plain sight as a spy
Spies often hide in plain sight, disguising themselves as ordinary citizens.
The document managed to hide in plain sight among a stack of old papers for years.
The solution was so simple it had been hiding in plain sight the whole time.

Related Terms

  • synonym – blend in: to seem part of the surroundings and not be noticed
