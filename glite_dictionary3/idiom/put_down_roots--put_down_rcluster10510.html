to put down roots

verb
settle permanently in a place
Pronunciation: / pʊt daʊn ruːts / (AmE), / pʊt daʊn ruːts / (BrE)
to settle permanently or for a long time in a particular place, especially by making it one's home

Examples

to put down roots in a city
After moving from city to city for years, they wanted to put down roots in a quiet suburb.
Many people put down roots after finding stable jobs and starting families.
She never truly felt at home until she managed to put down roots in the local community.

Related Terms

  • synonym – settle down: to adopt a more stable or permanent way of life, especially in one place
