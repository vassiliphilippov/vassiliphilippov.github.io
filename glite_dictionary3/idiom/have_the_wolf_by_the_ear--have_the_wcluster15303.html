to have the wolf by the ear

verb
be in a precarious situation
Pronunciation: / hæv ðə wʊlf baɪ ði ɪr / (AmE), / hæv ðə wʊlf baɪ ði ɪə / (BrE)
to be in a difficult or dangerous situation where letting go may be as risky as holding on

Examples

have the wolf by the ear with a problem
After taking on so many risky investments, he realized he had the wolf by the ear.
She knows that quitting suddenly would be dangerous, so she's got the wolf by the ear.
With mounting debts and no plan, they really have the wolf by the ear now.
