to hold the floor

verb
to speak at length in a meeting
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ðə flɔr / (AmE), / həʊld ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to speak continuously or have the right to speak in a discussion or meeting, often for an extended period

Examples

hold the floor during debate
During heated debates, some politicians are known to hold the floor for hours to delay a vote.
Even though others wished to contribute, Maria continued to hold the floor with her detailed report.
Please don't hold the floor too long, as everyone should have a chance to speak.

Related Terms

  • synonym – have the floor: to have the right to speak
