to have a beef

verb
have a complaint or grievance
Pronunciation: / hæv ə bif / (AmE), / hæv ə biːf / (BrE)
to have a complaint or a grievance about something

Examples

have a beef with someone
If you have a beef with the decision, you should bring it up at the meeting.
She told me she had a beef with the new manager's attitude.
Whenever he's upset, he usually has a beef about something at work.
