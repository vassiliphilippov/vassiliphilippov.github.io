in one's bones

adverb
to have a strong intuition or feeling
Pronunciation: / ɪn wʌnz boʊnz / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz bəʊnz / (BrE)
to have a strong, intuitive feeling or sense about something, often without concrete evidence

Examples

in her bones felt certain
Even though the weather forecast was clear, she felt a storm coming in her bones.
I just knew in my bones that he was telling the truth.
The players could sense victory in their bones before the final whistle blew.

Related Terms

  • synonym – intuition: the ability to understand something instinctively
  • synonym – sense: become aware of; perceive
