to a pulp

adverb
severely crushed or damaged
Pronunciation: / tə ə pʌlp / (AmE), / tə ə pʌlp / (BrE)
so severely as to be completely crushed or damaged, often used in the context of physical violence

Examples

beaten to a pulp
He was beaten to a pulp in the alley by the gang.
The critics tore the new film to a pulp in their reviews.
The fruit was mashed to a pulp for the jam recipe.

Related Terms

  • synonym – black and blue: badly bruised, especially after being beaten
