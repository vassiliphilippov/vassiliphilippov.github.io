to have someone's ear

verb
to have someone's attention or influence
Pronunciation: / hæv sʌmˌwʌnz ɪr / (AmE), / hav ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪə / (BrE)
to have someone's attention or to be in a position to be listened to or heeded by them, especially for advice or influence

Examples

have someone's ear for advice
If you want changes in the company, talk to Anna—she has the boss's ear.
The senator really has the president's ear on environmental issues.
Whenever I needed advice, my grandfather always had my ear.

Related Terms

  • synonym – have someone's attention: to be listened to by someone
