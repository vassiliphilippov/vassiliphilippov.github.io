to lose my touch

verb
to lose a previously held skill
Pronunciation: / luːz maɪ tʌtʃ / (AmE), / luːz maɪ tʌtʃ / (BrE)
to no longer have the skill or ability that one once had, especially after being proficient at it

Examples

lose my touch with painting
After a long break from painting, she worried she had lost her touch.
He used to be unbeatable at chess, but it seems he's lost his touch.
Lately, I can't seem to bake a decent cake; I think I've lost my touch.

Related Terms

  • antonym – regain my touch: to recover a lost skill or ability
