to keep at bay

verb
to prevent approach or harm
Pronunciation: / kip ət beɪ / (AmE), / kiːp ət beɪ / (BrE)
to prevent something or someone from coming too close or causing trouble or harm

Examples

keep at bay danger
In the winter, villagers burned fires to keep at bay the wolves roaming nearby.
Regular exercise can help keep at bay many health problems.
She tried to keep at bay the persistent worries that plagued her at night.

  • synonym – ward off: to protect or defend against something
