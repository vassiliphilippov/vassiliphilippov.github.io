off the shelf

adjective
available for immediate purchase or use
Pronunciation: / ɔf ðə ʃɛlf / (AmE), / ɒf ðə ʃɛlf / (BrE)
(of a product) available for immediate use or purchase, rather than being specially made or customized.

Examples

off the shelf software
Many small businesses prefer off the shelf software instead of custom solutions.
The company uses off the shelf components to keep production costs low.
You can buy an off the shelf solution and start using it right away.

Related Terms

  • antonym – custom-made: made to order for a specific customer
  • synonym – ready-made: made and ready to use or buy immediately
