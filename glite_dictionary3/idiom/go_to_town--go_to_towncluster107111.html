to go to town

verb
do something energetically or thoroughly
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tə taʊn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə taʊn / (BrE)
to do something with a lot of enthusiasm, energy, or thoroughness, often producing impressive or excessive results

Examples

to go to town decorating
After receiving their bonus, the couple went to town on shopping for new furniture.
Sarah really went to town on preparing dinner for the guests.
The marketing team went to town with their new advertising campaign.

Related Terms

  • synonym – go all out: to make a great effort; do something wholeheartedly
  • antonym – hold back: to restrain oneself; not do something fully
