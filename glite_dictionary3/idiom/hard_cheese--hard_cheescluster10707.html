hard cheese

noun
expression meaning 'bad luck'
Pronunciation: / hɑrd tʃiz / (AmE), / hɑːd tʃiːz / (BrE)
Used to express sympathy or, more often, no sympathy for someone's misfortune; bad luck.

Examples

hard cheese for you
He failed his driving test again—hard cheese for him.
If you don't like their decision, hard cheese—there's nothing you can do about it.
When his vacation was canceled at the last minute, all his colleagues said, 'hard cheese!'

Related Terms

  • antonym – good luck: favorable outcome; opposite of bad luck
  • synonym – tough luck: expression of sympathy for someone's misfortune
