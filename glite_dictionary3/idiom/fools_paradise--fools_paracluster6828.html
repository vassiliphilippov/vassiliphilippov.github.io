fool's paradise

noun
illusory happiness or wellbeing
Pronunciation: / fʊlz ˈpɛr.ə.daɪs /; / fʊlz ˈpɛrəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈpær.ə.daɪs /; / fuːlz ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
a state of happiness or contentment based on false beliefs, hopes, or illusions, especially one that is likely to end badly

Examples

live in a fool's paradise
After hearing the positive rumors, she lived in a fool's paradise, believing everything would work out perfectly.
Don't let temporary success lead you into a fool's paradise—the challenges are not over yet.
He thought his job was secure, but he was in a fool's paradise until the layoffs began.
After winning the lottery, he lived in a fool's paradise, ignoring the risks of overspending.
Believing the project would succeed without effort was a fool's paradise.
Many people choose to stay in a fool's paradise rather than face difficult truths.
