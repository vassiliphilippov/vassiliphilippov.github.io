one-horse race

noun
a contest with only one likely winner
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌn hɔrs reɪs / (AmE), / ˈwʌn hɔːs reɪs / (BrE)
a contest or competition in which only one participant or candidate is likely to win, with no real competition from others.

Examples

easy one-horse race
After the league leaders dominated all season, the championship turned into a one-horse race.
Many analysts thought the election would be a one-horse race, but a surprise candidate made it competitive.
With only one truly strong team remaining, the tournament was widely seen as a one-horse race.

Related Terms

  • synonym – walkover: an easy or unopposed victory in a competition
