when it comes to

conjunction
used to introduce a specific subject
Pronunciation: / wɛn ɪt kʌmz tu / (AmE), / wɛn ɪt kʌmz tu / (BrE)
used to introduce the subject of what is being discussed, especially to say how someone or something is handled or regarded in that context.

Examples

when it comes to money
When it comes to making decisions, he is very cautious.
When it comes to punctuality, Alice never disappoints.
When it comes to technology, I always turn to my brother for advice.
