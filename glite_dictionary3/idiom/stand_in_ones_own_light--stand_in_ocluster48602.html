to stand in one's own light

verb
hinder one's own progress or success
Pronunciation: / stænd ɪn wʌnz oʊn laɪt / (AmE), / stænd ɪn wʌnz əʊn laɪt / (BrE)
to act in a way that prevents oneself from succeeding or reaching one's full potential

Examples

to stand in one's own light at work
By doubting her abilities, she often stands in her own light when opportunities arise.
He realized too late that he had stood in his own light during his early career.
You shouldn't stand in your own light by refusing to take credit for your achievements.
