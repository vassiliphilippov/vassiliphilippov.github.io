in someone's shoes

preposition
in another person's situation
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuːz / (BrE)
in the same situation or position as someone else, especially to understand their feelings or actions

Examples

be in her shoes
If I were in your shoes, I would apologize for the mistake.
It's difficult to know what to do unless you've been in someone's shoes.
She tried to put herself in his shoes before judging his decision.
