into the breach

adverb
take action in a difficult situation
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.tu ðə briːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə ðə briːtʃ / (BrE)
to step forward and take action in a difficult or challenging situation, especially when others are unwilling or unable to do so

Examples

step into the breach
As volunteers quit during the busy season, more seniors moved into the breach to help out.
When the power failed, it was Mark who went into the breach and fixed the generator.
When the team leader fell ill, Sarah bravely stepped into the breach to keep the project on track.

Related Terms

  • antonym – stand back: to not take action or remain uninvolved in a situation
  • synonym – step up: to take responsibility or action, especially in a crisis
