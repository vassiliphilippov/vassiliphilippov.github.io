to shoot one's bolt

verb
to exhaust one's efforts or options
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt wʌnz boʊlt / (AmE), / ʃuːt wʌnz bəʊlt / (BrE)
to use up all one's energy, resources, or opportunities; to make one's final or only effort

Examples

shoot one's bolt too early
After working on the project for hours, he realized he had shot his bolt and couldn't continue.
By the final round, the athlete had shot his bolt and simply couldn't keep up with the competition.
Negotiators warned that the company had shot its bolt in earlier talks and had little left to offer.

Related Terms
