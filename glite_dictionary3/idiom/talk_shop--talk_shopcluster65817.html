to talk shop

verb
discuss work-related matters socially
Pronunciation: / tɔk ʃɑp / (AmE), / tɔːk ʃɒp / (BrE)
to discuss work-related matters, especially in a social setting where such conversation is not relevant or of interest to others

Examples

to talk shop at dinner
I wish you wouldn't talk shop during dinner; let's discuss something else for a change.
The teachers were so engrossed in talking shop that they hardly noticed anyone else at the party.
Whenever engineers get together, they inevitably talk shop about the latest technology.
