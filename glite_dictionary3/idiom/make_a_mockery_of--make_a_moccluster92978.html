to make a mockery of

verb
treat (something) with ridicule or contempt
Pronunciation: / meɪk ə ˈmɑkəri əv / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈmɒkəri ɒv / (BrE)
to treat something as if it is worthless or ridiculous, thereby showing no respect for it

Examples

make a mockery of the rules
Her careless response made a mockery of the team's hard work.
If you ignore the agreed rules, you make a mockery of the competition.
The defense lawyer's tactics made a mockery of the entire trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym – honor: to show respect or high regard for something
  • synonym – ridicule: to make fun of or mock someone or something
