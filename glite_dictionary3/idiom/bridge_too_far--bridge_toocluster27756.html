bridge too far

noun
an overly ambitious or excessive effort
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ tu fɑr / (AmE), / brɪdʒ tuː fɑː / (BrE)
an effort or demand that is excessive and likely to fail because it is too ambitious

Examples

bridge too far for success
For many small businesses, complying with the new regulations could be a bridge too far.
He found leading the project alone was a bridge too far for him.
Trying to renovate the entire house by next month is a bridge too far.
