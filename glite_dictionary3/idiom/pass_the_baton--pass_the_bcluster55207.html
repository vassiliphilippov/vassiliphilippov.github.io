to pass the baton

verb
transfer responsibility or leadership
Pronunciation: / pæs ðə bəˈtɑn / (AmE), / pɑːs ðə bəˈtɒn / (BrE)
to give responsibility or control to another person, especially as part of a sequence or succession

Examples

to pass the baton to a teammate
After completing his part of the project, John was ready to pass the baton to his colleague.
As the company's founder retires, he will pass the baton to his daughter.
The principal will pass the baton to her successor at the end of the school year.

Related Terms

  • synonym – hand over: to give something to someone else, especially power or responsibility
  • antonym – take up the baton: to assume responsibility or leadership after someone else
