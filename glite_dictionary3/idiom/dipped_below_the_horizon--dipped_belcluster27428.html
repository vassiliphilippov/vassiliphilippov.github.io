to dipped below the horizon

verb
disappear below the horizon
Pronunciation: / dɪpt bəˈloʊ ðə həˈraɪzn̩ / (AmE), / dɪpt bɪˈləʊ ðə həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
(of the sun, moon, or another celestial body) to disappear from view as it passes below the horizon.

Examples

the sun dipped below the horizon
As the moon dipped below the horizon, the night grew considerably darker.
Her gaze lingered on the sea until the sun finally dipped below the horizon in a blaze of color.
We watched as the last rays of the sun dipped below the horizon and the stars began to appear.
