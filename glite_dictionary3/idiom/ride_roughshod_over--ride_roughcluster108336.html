to ride roughshod over

verb
treat others' feelings or rights with disregard
Pronunciation: / raɪd ˈrʌf.ʃɑd ˌoʊ.vɚ / (AmE), / raɪd ˈrʌf.ʃɒd ˌəʊ.və / (BrE)
to treat someone's feelings, rights, or opinions as unimportant and disregard them completely, often in an aggressive or insensitive way

Examples

ride roughshod over other people
If you ride roughshod over your friends' feelings, you'll soon find yourself alone.
Many activists accused the government of riding roughshod over environmental protections.
The new director tends to ride roughshod over the concerns of her employees.
