cold feet

noun
sudden loss of courage or confidence
Pronunciation: / koʊld fit / (AmE), / kəʊld fiːt / (BrE)
a sudden loss of courage or confidence that prevents someone from continuing with a planned course of action

Examples

get cold feet before a wedding
Despite getting cold feet, she went ahead with the performance.
He wanted to quit his job, but cold feet stopped him from handing in his resignation.
She almost called off the wedding because she got cold feet at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • antonym – bravery: courageous behavior; opposite of timidity
  • synonym – nerves: a feeling of anxiety or nervousness
