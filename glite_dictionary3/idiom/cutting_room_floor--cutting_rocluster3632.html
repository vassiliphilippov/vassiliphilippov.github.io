cutting room floor

noun
removed or unused material during editing
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtɪŋ ruːm flɔr / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ ruːm flɔː / (BrE)
used to refer to parts of a film, text, or other creative work that are removed or omitted before the final version is released

Examples

left on the cutting room floor
Many brilliant story ideas end up on the cutting room floor before a novel is published.
Several key scenes were left on the cutting room floor during the final edit of the movie.
The director joked that the documentary could have been twice as long if not for the cutting room floor.

  • synonym – outtake: a scene or material cut from the final version of a film or recording
