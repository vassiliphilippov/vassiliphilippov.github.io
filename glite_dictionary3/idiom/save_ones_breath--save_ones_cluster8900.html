to save one's breath

verb
not waste effort stating something pointless
Pronunciation: / seɪv wʌnz brɛθ / (AmE), / seɪv wʌnz brɛθ / (BrE)
to stop wasting time or effort trying to persuade or explain something to someone who will not listen or care

Examples

save your breath arguing
I might as well save my breath since nobody is listening.
She decided to save her breath instead of arguing with him again.
You can save your breath; he's made up his mind and won't change it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – waste one's breath: to speak or argue in vain; to make a futile effort to persuade
