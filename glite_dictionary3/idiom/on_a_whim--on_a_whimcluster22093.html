on a whim

adverb
suddenly and impulsively
Pronunciation: / ɑn ə wɪm / (AmE), / ɒn ə wɪm / (BrE)
Suddenly and impulsively, without planning or forethought.

Examples

travel on a whim
He bought a plane ticket to Italy on a whim after seeing a travel ad online.
Sometimes I rearrange my entire living room on a whim when I feel bored.
They decided to try the new restaurant on a whim and loved it.

Related Terms

  • synonym – impulsively: without thinking about the consequences
  • antonym – on purpose: deliberately; with intention
