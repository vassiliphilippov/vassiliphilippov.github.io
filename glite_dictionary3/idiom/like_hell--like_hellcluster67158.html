like hell

adverb
used to show strong disbelief or denial
Pronunciation: / laɪk hɛl / (AmE), / laɪk hɛl / (BrE)
used ironically to express strong disagreement or disbelief about something just stated or implied; not at all

Examples

like hell he did
He said he would arrive on time, but like hell he did!
She claims she forgot, but like hell she did.
You think I'm going to apologize? Like hell I am!
