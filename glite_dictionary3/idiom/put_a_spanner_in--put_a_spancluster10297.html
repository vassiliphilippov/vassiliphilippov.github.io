to put a spanner in

verb
cause disruption or complications
Pronunciation: / pʊt ə ˈspænər ɪn / (AmE), / pʊt ə ˈspænə ɪn / (BrE)
To cause disruption or complications in a plan, process, or situation.

Examples

to put a spanner in the works
If you cancel now, it will really put a spanner in everyone's plans.
The last-minute change in requirements put a spanner in the development process.
The sudden rainstorm put a spanner in our outdoor event preparations.
