to tread lightly

verb
act with caution; be careful
Pronunciation: / trɛd ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / trɛd ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
to proceed carefully or act with caution, especially to avoid causing offense or making mistakes

Examples

to tread lightly around problems
He decided to tread lightly when meeting his partner's parents for the first time.
Journalists must tread lightly when reporting on sensitive community issues.
When discussing politics at work, it's wise to tread lightly to avoid conflict.
