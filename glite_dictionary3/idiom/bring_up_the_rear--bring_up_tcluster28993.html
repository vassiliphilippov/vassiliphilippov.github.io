to bring up the rear

verb
be last in a moving group or line
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ ʌp ðə rɪr / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp ðə rɪə / (BrE)
to be at the last position in a group or line, especially while moving

Examples

bring up the rear in a parade
At the end of the tour group, the guide brought up the rear to make sure no one got lost.
During the marathon, Michael decided to bring up the rear and encourage the slower runners.
When moving the cattle, one herder always brings up the rear to keep stragglers in line.

Related Terms

  • antonym – lead the way: to be at the front or take the initiative in a group or movement
