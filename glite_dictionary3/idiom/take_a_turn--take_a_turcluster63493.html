to take a turn

verb
to change direction or course
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə tɜrn / (AmE), / teɪk ə tɜːn / (BrE)
(of a situation, event, or conversation) to change direction, course, or character, often unexpectedly.

Examples

take a turn for the worse
After hours of negotiations, the discussion took a turn when new evidence was presented.
His career took a turn once he decided to move abroad.
The evening took a turn for the unexpected when the power suddenly went out.

Related Terms

  • synonym – change course: to alter one's direction or plan
  • degree lower – take a turn for the worse: to become worse, especially suddenly
