to stick to one's guns

verb
refuse to change beliefs despite opposition
Pronunciation: / stɪk tə wʌnz ɡʌnz / (AmE), / stɪk tʊ wʌnz ɡʌnz / (BrE)
to continue to support or defend your ideas, beliefs, or decisions, even when others oppose or criticize you

Examples

stick to one's guns under pressure
Despite criticism, he continued to stick to his guns throughout the debate.
Even under intense pressure from her colleagues, she chose to stick to her guns.
It's important to stick to your guns when you know you're right.

Related Terms

  • antonym – give in: to yield or surrender, especially under pressure
  • synonym – stand one's ground: to refuse to be pushed backwards, or to refuse to change a decision
