to go through the mill

verb
to undergo hardship or difficulty
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ θru ðə mɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ θruː ðə mɪl / (BrE)
to experience a series of difficult or testing experiences; to be subjected to hardship or scrutiny

Examples

to go through the mill at work
After months of going through the mill, the team finally launched their product.
His ideas had to go through the mill before being accepted by the board.
She really went through the mill during her divorce, but came out stronger on the other side.
