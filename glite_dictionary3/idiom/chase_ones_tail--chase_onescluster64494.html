to chase one's tail

verb
engage in futile or pointless activity
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs wʌnz teɪl / (AmE), / tʃeɪs wʌnz teɪl / (BrE)
to waste time or effort on activities that do not achieve anything; to keep busy but make no progress

Examples

to chase one's tail at work
I've been chasing my tail trying to sort out these paperwork issues all week.
If we don't change our strategy, we'll just keep chasing our tails and never finish the project.
Whenever a deadline approaches, she feels like she's chasing her tail and not accomplishing much.
