too big for one's boots

adjective
overconfident; arrogant
Pronunciation: / tuː bɪɡ fɔr wʌnz buːts / (AmE), / tuː bɪɡ fə wʌnz buːts / (BrE)
acting in a way that shows too much self-confidence or arrogance, as if considering oneself more important than one really is

Examples

acting too big for his boots
After his promotion, he started acting too big for his boots and stopped listening to advice.
If you ask me, she's getting too big for her boots since she won that award.
Someone needs to remind him not to get too big for his boots despite his recent success.

Related Terms

  • synonym – cocky: over-confident, showing too much self-assurance
  • antonym – humble: having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
