compaction

noun
the act of making something more compact
8.3 5.3
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or result of making something more compact or dense by pressing or packing it together

Examples

Natural compaction of earth
Heavy rains can cause natural compaction of earth materials in the landscape.
Proper compaction is essential when laying a foundation to prevent future settling.
The soil underwent compaction due to the heavy construction equipment.
Overuse of the field caused compaction, making it difficult for plants to grow.
Repeated rainfall led to significant compaction of the soil in the garden.
The roadbed requires proper compaction before paving can begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compression - the act of pressing something into a smaller space
