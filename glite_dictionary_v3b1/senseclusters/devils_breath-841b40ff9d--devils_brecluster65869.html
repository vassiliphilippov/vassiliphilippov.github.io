devil's breath

noun
metaphor for heat, danger, or sinister presence
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛvəlz brɛθ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz brɛθ / (BrE)
A metaphor describing an intense sensation of heat, danger, a chilling presence, or a strong, unpleasant odor, often used to evoke a sinister or ominous atmosphere.

Examples

The devil's breath wind
As the flames raged, firefighters described the scorching wind as the devil's breath.
The abandoned building was filled with the devil's breath of mold and decay.
The night was so still, she imagined the slightest breeze carried the devil's breath across the fields.
