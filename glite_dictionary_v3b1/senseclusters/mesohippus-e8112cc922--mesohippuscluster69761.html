Mesohippus

noun
extinct genus of early horse
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛs.oʊˈhɪp.əs / (AmE), / ˌmiː.səʊˈhɪp.əs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of small, long-legged horses that lived during the Oligocene epoch, considered an early ancestor of the modern horse.

Examples

The Mesohippus fossil
Fossil evidence shows that Mesohippus had three toes on each foot.
Scientists study Mesohippus to understand the evolution of modern horses.
The Mesohippus roamed North America millions of years ago.

Related Terms
