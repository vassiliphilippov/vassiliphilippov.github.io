parallel circuit

noun
electrical circuit with branches for current
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl ˈsɝːkət / (AmE), / ˈpærəlɛl ˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
a closed electrical circuit in which the current splits into two or more branches before recombining to complete the circuit

Examples

A parallel circuit branch
A parallel circuit is often used in household wiring to ensure that devices can operate independently.
Students constructed a parallel circuit to learn how electricity flows through multiple paths.
When one bulb burns out in a parallel circuit, the others stay lit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: series circuit - an electrical circuit with a single path for current
