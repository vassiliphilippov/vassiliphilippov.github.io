redrive

verb
to drive again
Pronunciation: / riːˈdraɪv /; / ˌriːˈdraɪv / (AmE), / riːˈdraɪv /; / ˌriːˈdraɪv / (BrE)
to drive or cause something to be driven again, especially after a previous attempt has failed

Examples

To redrive the process
After the first attempt failed, the engineer had to redrive the bolt into the frame.
If the nail comes loose, you may need to redrive it to secure the board.
The technician carefully redrove the pin to ensure it was properly in place.
If the process fails, the system will automatically redrive the task until it succeeds.
Engineers set up the software to redrive any messages that were not delivered the first time.
You can manually redrive the operation if the initial attempt does not complete successfully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repeat - to do something again
  • synonym: retry - to try again after a failure
  • antonym: undo - to reverse or cancel an action
