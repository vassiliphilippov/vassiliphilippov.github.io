independent

noun
person not aligned with a political party
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who does not belong to or consistently support any one political party or group

Examples

An independent voter
He described himself as an independent who evaluates each candidate on merit.
Many independents remain undecided before elections, making them a key demographic.
She has always voted as an independent, never tying herself to any one party.

Related Terms

  • antonym: partisan - a strong supporter of a particular party, cause, or person, especially in politics
