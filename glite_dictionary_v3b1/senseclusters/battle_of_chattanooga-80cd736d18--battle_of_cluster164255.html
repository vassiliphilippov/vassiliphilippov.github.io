Battle of Chattanooga

noun
Civil War battle, Union victory over Confederates in 1863
Pronunciation: / ˈbæɾl əv ˌtʃætəˈnuːɡə / (AmE), / ˈbatl əv ˌtʃætəˈnuːɡə / (BrE)
a decisive battle in the American Civil War (1863) in which Union forces under Ulysses S. Grant defeated the Confederate Army led by Braxton Bragg

Examples

The Battle of Chattanooga fight
After the Battle of Chattanooga, Union forces gained control of a vital railroad hub.
Many historians consider the Battle of Chattanooga a masterpiece of military strategy.
The Battle of Chattanooga marked a turning point in the American Civil War.
