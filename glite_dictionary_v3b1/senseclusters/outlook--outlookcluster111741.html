outlook

noun
general attitude or expectation about the future
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊt.lʊk / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lʊk / (BrE)
a person's general attitude, expectation, or way of thinking about life or the future

Examples

A positive outlook
Her positive outlook helped her stay hopeful despite difficult times.
The company revised its financial outlook for the year due to market changes.
With a more optimistic outlook, he approached new opportunities eagerly.
Despite her difficulties, Maria maintained a positive outlook on life.
His pessimistic outlook made it hard for him to appreciate good news.
Traveling to new countries can broaden your outlook and change how you see the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attitude - a settled way of thinking or feeling about something
  • antonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future
  • synonym: perspective - a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something
  • antonym: pessimism - a tendency to see the worst aspect of things or believe the worst will happen
  • synonym: prospect - the possibility or likelihood of some future event
