humanism

noun
Renaissance cultural movement based on classical studies
3.1 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (BrE)
The cultural and intellectual movement of the Renaissance that focused on the study of classical antiquity and emphasized human values, achievements, and capacities.

Examples

Renaissance humanism
Many Renaissance artists were influenced by the ideals of humanism and sought inspiration from classical art.
Studying Latin and Greek texts was central to the spread of humanism in the fifteenth century.
The rise of humanism transformed education and philosophy in Europe during the Renaissance.
