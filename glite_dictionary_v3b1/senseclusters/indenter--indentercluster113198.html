indenter

noun
person placing order
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛntər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdentə / (BrE)
a person or agent who places an order for goods using a formal purchase order, especially in historical or regional contexts

Examples

A textile indenter
The indenter submitted a request for textiles to be shipped from overseas.
In colonial times, an indenter would coordinate large orders for merchants.
As the company's indenter, she ensured all purchase orders were completed accurately.
