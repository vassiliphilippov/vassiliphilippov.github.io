professional

noun
person with a learned profession
1.0
Pronunciation: / prəˈfɛʃənl /; / prəˈfɛʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a person who is engaged in a profession, especially one requiring special training or qualifications

Examples

A medical professional
As a medical professional, he takes his responsibilities seriously.
She is a respected professional in the field of law.
The team hired a professional to handle the complex project.
As a professional, she is responsible for training the next generation of engineers.
Each professional must demonstrate mastery before being entrusted with teaching others.
The professional guided his apprentices through complex procedures with patience and clarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amateur - a person who does something for pleasure, not as a job
  • antonym: apprentice - a person learning a trade from a skilled employer
  • synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
