obtrusion

noun
forcing in or upon
Pronunciation: / əbˈtruːʒən / (AmE), / əbˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the act or state of being intrusive or forcing something in an unwelcome way

Examples

Engineers must prevent the obtrusion of dust into sensitive equipment.
She apologized for the obtrusion of her personal opinions during the meeting.
The obtrusion of loud advertisements ruined the peaceful atmosphere of the park.
He apologized for the obtrusion of his personal opinions during the meeting.
Many critics noted the obtrusion of special effects in the otherwise simple film.

  • synonym: intrusion - the act of entering or forcing in without permission
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something away or out
  • antonym: subtlety - the quality of being delicate or not obvious
