chuppah

noun
Jewish wedding canopy
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈxʊpə / (AmE), / ˈxʊpə / (BrE)
a canopy under which a Jewish couple stands during their wedding ceremony, symbolizing their new home together

Examples

A chuppah canopy
The couple exchanged vows beneath the chuppah in front of their families.
A beautifully decorated chuppah stood at the center of the wedding hall.
Traditionally, the chuppah symbolizes the new home the couple will build together.
The couple stood beneath the chuppah as the rabbi recited the blessings.
Family members held the corners of the chuppah during the traditional Jewish wedding ceremony.
Decorated with flowers, the chuppah was the centerpiece of the outdoor wedding.
