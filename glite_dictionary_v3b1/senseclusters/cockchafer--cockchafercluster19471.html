cockchafer

noun
large European beetle harmful to plants
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑkˌtʃeɪfər / (AmE), / ˈkɒkˌtʃeɪfə / (BrE)
a large European beetle (genus Melolontha) that is destructive to vegetation both as a larva and as an adult

Examples

A cockchafer beetle
A farmer discovered a cockchafer infestation destroying his young potato plants.
Larvae of the cockchafer can cause significant damage to lawns and crops.
The children were fascinated by the buzzing flight of the cockchafer in the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: May bug - another common name for the cockchafer beetle
