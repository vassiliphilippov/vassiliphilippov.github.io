just under the wire

adverb
very narrowly in time; just in time
6.5
Pronunciation: / dʒəst ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / dʒʌst ˌʌndə ðə ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
At the last possible moment; just in time to meet a deadline or requirement.

Examples

To arrive just under the wire
He renewed his driver's license just under the wire on the final day possible.
She finished the exam just under the wire before time ran out.
The team delivered the completed proposal just under the wire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at the last minute - just before the latest possible time
  • antonym: too late - after the necessary or allowed time; not soon enough
