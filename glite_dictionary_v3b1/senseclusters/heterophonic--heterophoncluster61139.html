heterophonic

adjective
musical texture type
9.9
Pronunciation: /he.tə.rə.ˈfoʊ.nɪk/ (AmE), /he.tə.rə.ˈfɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
describing music in which two or more performers play or sing simultaneous variations of a single melody

Examples

Heterophonic music style
The traditional folk ensemble performed a heterophonic arrangement of the song, each instrument adding subtle variations.
In some world music, a heterophonic texture is created when singers improvise around the main melody.
The composer's use of heterophonic techniques gave the piece a rich and complex sound.
