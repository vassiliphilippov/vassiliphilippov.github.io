keep

verb
provide room and board
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiːp / (BrE)
to look after, support, or provide for someone or animals, including giving food, shelter, or care

Examples

To keep a pet
For years, she has been keeping her elderly aunt in her home.
He agreed to keep the students during the summer holidays.
They kept several boarders at their house to earn extra income.
He used to keep goats for milk and cheese.
Many people in rural areas keep livestock as a source of income.
My grandparents keep hens and ducks on their farm.
After his father lost his job, Tom had to keep the whole family by working two jobs.
It is difficult for one person alone to keep a large household.
She worked hard every day to keep her younger siblings and pay the bills.
