electroforming

noun
metal forming process
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
a process of forming metal objects by depositing metal onto a mold using electricity

Examples

Electroforming metal objects
The jewelry manufacturer uses electroforming to produce lightweight gold pieces with intricate designs.
Advances in electroforming have allowed for the creation of highly detailed metal components in electronics.
Because of its precision, electroforming is often chosen for making custom scientific instruments.
The artist used electroforming to create intricate metal sculptures with fine details.
Modern jewelry designers often rely on electroforming for lightweight yet durable pieces.
Through electroforming, manufacturers can produce complex metal parts that are difficult to make by other methods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: casting - the process of shaping material by pouring it into a mold
  • synonym: electroplating - the process of coating an object with a thin layer of metal using electricity
