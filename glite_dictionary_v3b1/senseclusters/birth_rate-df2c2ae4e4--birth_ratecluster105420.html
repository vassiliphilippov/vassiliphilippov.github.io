birth rate

noun
number of live births per 1,000 people per year
Pronunciation: / bɝθ reɪt / (AmE), / bɜːθ reɪt / (BrE)
The number of live births per 1,000 people in a specified area during a given year.

Examples

High birth rate
A high birth rate can put pressure on a nation's resources and infrastructure.
Many European countries are concerned about falling birth rates and an aging population.
The country's birth rate has declined steadily over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: death rate - the number of deaths per 1,000 people per year
  • synonym: fertility rate - the average number of children born to a woman over her lifetime
