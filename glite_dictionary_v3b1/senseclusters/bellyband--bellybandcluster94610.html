bellyband

noun
strap securing wagon shafts on draft animals
2.2 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbe.lɪ.bænd/ (AmE), /ˈbe.lɪ.bænd/ (BrE)
a strap around the belly of a draft animal used to secure the shafts of a wagon or carriage

Examples

Horse bellyband
Before setting off, he tightened the bellyband around the mule's stomach.
Old carriages often used a leather bellyband to keep the shafts in place.
The farmer checked the bellyband to make sure the wagon was securely attached to the horse.
