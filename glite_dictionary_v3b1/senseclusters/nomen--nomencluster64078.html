nomen

noun
Roman family name
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊmən / (AmE), / ˈnəʊmən / (BrE)
the hereditary family name of a Roman gens (clan) used in ancient Roman naming conventions

Examples

Roman nomen
In ancient Rome, a man's nomen identified his clan and distinguished his extended family.
The historian explained how the nomen was passed down through generations in Roman society.
Julius Caesar's nomen was 'Julius', showing his membership in the Julian gens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cognomen - the third name in Roman naming, indicating a family branch
  • synonym: praenomen - the personal first name in Roman naming
