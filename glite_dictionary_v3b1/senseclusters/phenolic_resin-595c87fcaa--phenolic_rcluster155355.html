phenolic resin

noun
synthetic resin from phenol and formaldehyde
8.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /fən.ˈɑː.lɪk ˈre.zɪn/ /; / /fən.ˈɑː.lɪk ˈre.zən/ (AmE), /fə.ˈnɒ.lɪk ˈre.zɪn/ /; / /fiː.ˈnɒ.lɪk ˈre.zɪn/ (BrE)
A synthetic resin produced by the polymerization of phenol with formaldehyde, known for its heat resistance and use in molding, laminates, and adhesives.

Examples

Phenolic resin molding
Engineers selected phenolic resin for its excellent insulating properties in electronic components.
Many adhesive formulations employ phenolic resin for additional strength and durability.
