transom

noun
window above a door
7.5 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtræns.əm/ (AmE), /ˈtræns.əm/ (BrE)
a window or horizontal bar above a door, or a bar across a window

Examples

A transom window
She left the transom open for ventilation on warm evenings.
Sunlight poured in through the transom, brightening the entryway.
The antique house featured a stained-glass transom above its main door.
Light streamed through the window above the door, which was divided by a transom.
Old houses often feature a transom to allow circulation even when the door is closed.
The carpenter carefully measured the height of the transom before installing the stained glass panel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fanlight - a small semicircular or rectangular window above a door, often with decorative glazing
  • synonym: lintel - a horizontal support across the top of a door or window
