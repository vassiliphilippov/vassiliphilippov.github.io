A measure of a fluid's resistance to flow when an external force is applied; an intrinsic property describing internal friction within the fluid.

Examples

Dynamic viscosity of fluid
Engineers calculate dynamic viscosity to design efficient pipelines for liquid transport.
The dynamic viscosity of oil decreases as its temperature increases.
Water has a lower dynamic viscosity than honey, which allows it to flow more easily.
