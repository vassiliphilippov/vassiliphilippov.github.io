nullified

adjective
deprived of legal force or effect
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈnʌlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a law, agreement, or decision) made legally void or deprived of legal force or effect.

Examples

Nullified court decision
His conviction was nullified by the appeals court due to lack of evidence.
The contract was nullified when both parties agreed to terminate it.
The court order is nullified if not signed by a judge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enforced - put into effect or carried out
  • synonym: invalid - not legally effective or binding
  • synonym: void - having no legal force or effect
