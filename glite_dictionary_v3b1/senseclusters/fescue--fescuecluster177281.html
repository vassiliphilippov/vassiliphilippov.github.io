fescue

noun
type of pasture or lawn grass
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfe.s.kjuː/ (AmE), /ˈfe.s.kjuː/ (BrE)
A type of grass with wide flat leaves, widely cultivated in Europe and America for permanent pasture, hay, and lawns.

Examples

A fescue grass
Many farmers prefer fescue for their pastures because it tolerates drought well.
The golf course was planted with fescue to withstand heavy foot traffic.
The lush, green fescue on the lawn requires regular mowing.
