defensibility

noun
quality of being defensible
Pronunciation: / dɪˌfɛnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌfɛnsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be defended against attack, criticism, or challenge.

Examples

Defensibility of a fortress
Architects considered the defensibility of the site before choosing a location for the fortress.
The defensibility of the company's position was seriously questioned during the trial.
The lawyer argued for the defensibility of his client's actions in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defensibleness - the quality of being defensible
  • antonym: indefensibility - the quality of being impossible to defend
