baserunning

noun
running between bases
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsˌrʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsˌrʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or skill of running between bases in baseball or softball, including speed, timing, and strategy

Examples

Aggressive baserunning
Smart baserunning helped the team score the winning run in the ninth inning.
The coach praised her for her aggressive baserunning during the tournament.
Mistakes in baserunning can cost a team valuable opportunities to score.
Good baserunning can turn a single into a double and help a team score more runs.
The coach praised her baserunning after she advanced to third base on a wild pitch.
Improving baserunning is essential for players who want to be more effective on offense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base stealing - the act of advancing to the next base while the pitcher is delivering the ball
  • antonym: fielding - the act of catching or retrieving the ball in baseball
