nuclear meltdown

noun
catastrophic reactor overheating and core melting
7.2 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀê.kli.…ö Ààm…õlt.da än / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀê.kl…™.…ô Ààm…õlt.da än / (BrE)
A severe overheating of the core of a nuclear reactor, causing the core to melt and allowing radiation to escape.

Examples

A nuclear meltdown accident
A nuclear meltdown at the plant released dangerous levels of radiation into the environment.
Many safety protocols exist to prevent a nuclear meltdown during power generation.
The disaster was caused by a nuclear meltdown after the cooling system failed.

Synonyms

  • meltdown
    7.4 1.08/m
