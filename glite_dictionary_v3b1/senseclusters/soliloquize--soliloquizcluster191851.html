soliloquize

verb
speak thoughts aloud to oneself
9.8 8.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈlɪl.ə.kwaɪz/ (AmE), /sə.ˈlɪl.ə.kwaɪz/ (BrE)
to speak one's thoughts aloud when alone or regardless of any hearers, especially as a theatrical device

Examples

To soliloquize alone
In the quiet of the night, he tended to soliloquize, unaware that anyone could overhear his musings.
Left alone in the garden, she would often soliloquize about her dreams and fears.
The actor began to soliloquize at center stage, sharing his inner thoughts with the audience.
Late at night, he found himself soliloquising in the kitchen as he made tea.
Sometimes the actress would soliloquise at length, revealing her character's deepest fears to the audience.
The playwright allows the protagonist to soliloquise so the audience can understand his motives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aside - a remark or passage in a play intended to be heard by the audience but not by the other characters
  • antonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people
  • synonym: monologue - a long speech by one person, especially in a play
