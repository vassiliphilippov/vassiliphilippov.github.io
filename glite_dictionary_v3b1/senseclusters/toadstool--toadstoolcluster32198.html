toadstool

noun
inedible or poisonous fungus
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊdˌstuːl / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdˌstuːl / (BrE)
A fungus, typically with a stalk and cap, that is considered inedible or poisonous, especially as distinguished from edible mushrooms.

Examples

A poisonous toadstool
He photographed a large toadstool growing in the damp shade beneath the trees.
Many poisonous toadstools resemble edible mushrooms, making foraging risky.
The children were warned not to touch the bright red toadstool in the garden.
