nonbiodegradable pollution

noun
pollution that does not break down naturally
2.3 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌbaɪ.oʊ.dɪˈɡreɪ.dəbəl pəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌbaɪ.əʊ.dɪˈɡreɪ.də.bəl pəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
pollution consisting of substances that do not break down naturally and accumulate in the environment, potentially entering the food chain

Examples

Nonbiodegradable pollution harms wildlife
Because it does not break down, nonbiodegradable pollution poses long-term risks to wildlife and humans.
Efforts to reduce nonbiodegradable pollution include banning single-use plastic bags.
Plastics are a major source of nonbiodegradable pollution in our oceans and rivers.
