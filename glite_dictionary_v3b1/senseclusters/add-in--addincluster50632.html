add-in

noun
hardware component for computer expansion
7.8 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæd ɪn / (AmE), / ˈæd ɪn / (BrE)
a hardware component, such as a circuit board, that can be inserted into a computer's expansion slot to enhance its functions

Examples

A add-in card
Technicians installed a sound add-in to enhance audio output on the desktop.
The new graphics add-in significantly improved the computer's performance for gaming applications.
Upgrading your system with a network add-in allows for faster internet connectivity.
