replot

verb
to divide land anew
6.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈplɑːt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈplɑːt/ (AmE), /riː.ˈplɒt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈplɒt/ (BrE)
to rearrange, divide, or map out land into new plots or boundaries, often for development or legal purposes

Examples

To replot the land
The city decided to replot the old industrial area into new housing lots.
After the purchase, the developer hired a surveyor to replot the property boundaries.
Due to new zoning laws, the farmland was replotted into several smaller parcels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subdivide - to divide land into smaller parts
