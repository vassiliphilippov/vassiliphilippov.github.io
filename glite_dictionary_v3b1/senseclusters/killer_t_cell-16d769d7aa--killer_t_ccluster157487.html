killer T cell

noun
immune cell that kills infected cells
7.6 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkɪl.ər tiː sɛl/ (AmE), /ˈkɪl.ə tiː sɛl/ (BrE)
A type of T cell with a CD8 receptor that recognizes antigens on virus-infected or cancerous cells and destroys them.

Examples

Killer T cells destroy viruses
After vaccination, the number of killer T cells in the bloodstream often increases.
Researchers are developing therapies that boost killer T cell activity to fight cancer.
The body's immune response relies heavily on killer T cells to identify and destroy virus-infected cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cytotoxic T cell - another term for killer T cell, emphasizing its cell-killing function
  • antonym: helper T cell - T cell type that activates and regulates other immune cells, not directly killing infected cells
