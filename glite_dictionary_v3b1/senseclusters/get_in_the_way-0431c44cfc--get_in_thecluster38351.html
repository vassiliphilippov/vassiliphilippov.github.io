get in the way

verb
to obstruct or hinder progress
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɪn ðə weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ðə weɪ / (BrE)
to prevent or obstruct progress or the occurrence of something

Examples

To get in the way of plans
He didn't want anyone to get in the way of his plans for the company.
Make sure your personal feelings don't get in the way of making a fair decision.
The tall plant can get in the way of sunlight reaching the other flowers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hinder - to create difficulties, resulting in delay or obstruction
  • synonym: obstruct - to block or get in the way of
