rudaceous

adjective
rock with coarse fragments
Pronunciation: / ruːˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ruːˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to rocks that are made up of coarse fragments or rubble

Examples

Rudaceous rock fragments
The geologist identified the layer as rudaceous because of its large, angular rock fragments.
A rudaceous deposit often forms at the base of steep slopes where rocks break apart.
The museum displayed a sample of rudaceous rock collected from the mountain valley.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arenaceous - relating to rocks composed mainly of sand or sand-sized particles
  • synonym: conglomeratic - relating to rocks composed of rounded fragments cemented together
