dopamine

noun
a neurotransmitter and medication
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.pəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.pəˌmiːn / (BrE)
a chemical substance in the brain that acts as a neurotransmitter, essential for normal nervous system function, and used as a drug to treat conditions like shock and low blood pressure

Examples

Dopamine drug treatment
Doctors sometimes administer dopamine as a drug to treat patients with dangerously low blood pressure.
High levels of dopamine in the brain are associated with feelings of pleasure and motivation.
Research on dopamine has provided new insights into the treatment of neurological disorders like Parkinson's disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: norepinephrine - a neurotransmitter involved in arousal and stress response
  • synonym: serotonin - a neurotransmitter in the brain affecting mood and emotion
