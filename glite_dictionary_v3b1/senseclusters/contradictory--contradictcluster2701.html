contradictory

adjective
involving disagreement or conflict
1.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.trə.ˈdɪk.tər.iː/ /; / /kən.trə.ˈdɪk.tər.iː/ (AmE), /kɒn.trə.ˈdɪk.tə.riː/ /; / /kən.trə.ˈdɪk.tə.riː/ (BrE)
containing or involving elements that are in conflict, opposed, or inconsistent; mutually exclusive so that both cannot be true at the same time

Examples

Contradictory statements
His explanation was full of contradictory statements, making it difficult to trust his story.
The scientist noticed several contradictory results in the experiment.
The witness gave contradictory accounts of the events in court.
He displays contradictory behavior, sometimes friendly and other times hostile.
The witness gave a contradictory account of the events, making it hard to know the truth.
Their statements are contradictory, so one of them must be mistaken.
A statement and its direct negation are contradictory and cannot both be true.
In logic, 'alive' and 'dead' function as contradictory predicates.
The terms 'internal' and 'external' are contradictory in philosophical discussions.
