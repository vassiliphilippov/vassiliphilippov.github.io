amended

adjective
changed or revised (esp. of laws)
4.1
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛndɪd / (AmE), / əˈmɛndɪd / (BrE)
changed or revised, especially to make something more correct or suitable

Examples

The amended contract
She reviewed the amended contract carefully before signing.
The amended bill passed both houses of Congress after several changes were made.
The constitution was amended to grant women the right to vote.
After receiving feedback, the manager submitted the amended report to the board.
His amended statement clarified the earlier misunderstanding.
The law was amended to better protect citizens' rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amend - to make changes to a document, law, or statement, usually for improvement
  • synonym: corrected - made right or free from errors
  • synonym: improved - made or become better
  • antonym: repealed - (of a law) revoked or withdrawn officially
  • synonym: revised - changed or altered in order to improve or make corrections
