flock

noun
group of sheep or goats
4.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /flɑːk/ (AmE), /flɒk/ (BrE)
a group of animals, especially birds, sheep, or goats, that are kept or move together

Examples

A flock of birds
A flock of goats was grazing near the riverbank.
The shepherd led his flock across the green meadow.
We watched as the entire flock moved toward the shelter for the night.
A flock of geese flew over the lake at sunrise.
Every evening, a flock of starlings gathers on the telephone wires.
The children watched a flock of sparrows settle in the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swarm - a large group of insects, especially flying ones, moving together
