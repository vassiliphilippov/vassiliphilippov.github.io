rescission

noun
cancellation of a contract (law)
7.5 8.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈsɪ.ʒən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈsɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsɪ.ʒən/ /; / /rɪ.ˈsɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(law) the act of cancelling a contract, with the parties restored as far as possible to their pre-contractual positions.

Examples

The rescission of contract
Following the mutual agreement, both parties prepared to execute the rescission of the contract.
In some cases, rescission may be the only remedy available for breach of a legal agreement.
The judge ruled in favor of rescission after evidence of misrepresentation was presented.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cancellation - the act of making a contract or agreement no longer valid
