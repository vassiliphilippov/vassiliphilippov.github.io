isochron

noun
line of equal age
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌkrɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəkrɒn / (BrE)
a line on a diagram or map that connects points representing the same time or age

Examples

An isochron line
The geologist drew an isochron to show areas where the rocks formed at the same time.
On the map, each isochron represents a different period in the region's geological history.
Researchers used an isochron to compare the ages of volcanic layers across the continent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contour line - a line on a map joining points of equal value, such as elevation
