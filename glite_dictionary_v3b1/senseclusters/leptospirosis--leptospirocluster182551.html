leptospirosis

noun
infectious disease caused by Leptospira bacteria
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛp.toʊ.spaɪˈroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təʊ.spaɪˈrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by bacteria of the genus Leptospira, typically transmitted from animals to humans and characterized by symptoms such as fever and jaundice

Examples

Leptospirosis infection
Farmers are at higher risk of contracting leptospirosis due to their frequent contact with livestock.
Symptoms of leptospirosis often include high fever, headache, and yellowing of the skin.
The doctor suspected leptospirosis after the patient reported recent exposure to floodwater and developed jaundice.
