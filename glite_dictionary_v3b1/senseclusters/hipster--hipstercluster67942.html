hipster

noun
member of a 1960s countercultural youth movement
4.7 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈhɪp.stər/ (AmE), /ˈhɪp.stə/ (BrE)
a person who rejects mainstream culture and prefers alternative or non-mainstream styles, ideas, or lifestyles

Examples

A hipster style
Many hipsters in the 1960s embraced communal living and alternative forms of artistic expression.
Some parents worried when their children started dressing like hipsters and listening to psychedelic rock.
The hipsters gathered in San Francisco to celebrate free love, peace, and music together.
Being a hipster is often associated with supporting local businesses and alternative culture.
He dresses like a hipster, always sporting vintage clothes and a quirky hat.
The coffee shop was full of hipsters working on their laptops and discussing indie music.
The new coffee shop is popular with hipsters in the city.
He considers himself a hipster because he listens to indie bands and wears vintage clothes.
Many hipsters are known for supporting local art and unique cafes.

  • synonym: bohemian - person with unconventional, artistic, or alternative lifestyle
  • antonym: mainstream - the ideas, attitudes, or activities regarded as normal or conventional
  • antonym: square - a conventional, unadventurous person, opposite of a hipster
  • synonym: trendsetter - a person who starts or popularizes new styles or ideas
