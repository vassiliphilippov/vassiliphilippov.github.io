call off

verb
cancel or postpone an event
2.8 2.10/m
Pronunciation: / k…îl …îf / (AmE), / k…îÀêl …íf / (BrE)
To cancel or postpone something that was planned or scheduled, often with no intention to reschedule.

Examples

To call off a meeting
Due to the heavy rain, they had to call off the football match.
If anyone gets sick, we may have to call off the party.
The CEO decided to call off the merger at the last minute.

Synonyms

  • cancel
    1.0 22/m
    cancel stops a planned event in a neutral way, while call off often suggests no intention to reschedule.
  • scrub
    5.6 0.27/m
    scrub cancels a scheduled event often at short notice and informally, while call off is more general and neutral.
  • scratch
    3.4 3.02/m
    scratch removes something from a list or record, while call off means cancelling or postponing a planned event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cancel - to decide that an organized event will not happen
  • antonym: proceed with - to continue as planned
