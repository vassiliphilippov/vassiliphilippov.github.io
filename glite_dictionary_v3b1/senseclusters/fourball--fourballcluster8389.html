fourball

noun
golf team format
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːbɔːl / (BrE)
a golf competition or match where two-player teams each play their own ball and the team's score for each hole is the lower (best) of the two players' scores

Examples

Fourball golf match
They entered the club's annual fourball tournament and enjoyed the friendly competition.
In a fourball, each player plays their own ball, and the best score counts for the team.
The fourball format encourages teamwork while allowing individual play on every hole.
They signed up for the annual fourball competition at their local golf club.
In a fourball, each player plays their own ball, and the best score counts for the team.
The fourball format encourages teamwork while allowing individual play in golf tournaments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: best ball - a golf format where the best score among team members counts for the team
  • antonym: foursome - a golf match where two teams of two players play one ball per team, taking alternate shots
  • antonym: foursomes - a golf match where two-player teams play one ball alternately
  • synonym: match play - a golf scoring system where players or teams compete to win individual holes
