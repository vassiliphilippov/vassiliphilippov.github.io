constitutionalization

noun
making constitutional
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˌstɪtuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌstɪtjuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something governed by, protected by, or included in a constitution (such as a right, principle, or organization)

Examples

The constitutionalization of human rights has transformed the country's legal system.

Legal scholars discussed the impact of constitutionalization on the separation of powers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolition - the act of formally ending a system or practice
  • synonym: codification - the process of arranging laws or rules into a systematic code
  • synonym: constitutionalism - the belief in limiting government power by a constitution
  • synonym: democratization - the process of making something more democratic
  • synonym: legalization - the process of making something legal by law
  • antonym: repeal - the action of revoking or annulling a law or constitutional act
