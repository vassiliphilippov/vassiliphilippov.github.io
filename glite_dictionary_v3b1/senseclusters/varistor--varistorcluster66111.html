varistor

noun
voltage-dependent resistor
Pronunciation: / vəˈrɪstər / (AmE), / vəˈrɪstə / (BrE)
an electronic component whose resistance changes with applied voltage, used to protect circuits from voltage spikes

Examples

A varistor component
A varistor is often used to protect sensitive electronics from sudden voltage surges.
The technician replaced the damaged varistor to restore the circuit's protection.
If a varistor fails, the device may become vulnerable to power spikes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fuse - a safety device that protects electrical circuits by breaking the connection if the current is too high
  • synonym: resistor - an electronic component that resists the flow of electric current
