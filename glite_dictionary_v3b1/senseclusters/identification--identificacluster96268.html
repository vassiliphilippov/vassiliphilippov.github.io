identification

noun
attributing traits of others to oneself
1.1
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdɛntəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person attributes the characteristics or traits of another person or group to themselves, either consciously or unconsciously, often as a psychological or social mechanism

Examples

Identification with a group
His identification with the sports team was so strong that their victories felt like his own.
The concept of identification is important in understanding how children develop their sense of self.
Through identification with her mentor, she adopted many of his professional habits.
