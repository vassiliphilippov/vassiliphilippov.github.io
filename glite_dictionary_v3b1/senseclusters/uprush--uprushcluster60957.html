uprush

verb
to surge upward
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
to surge or move upward suddenly, often referring to a quick rise of physical movement or emotion

Examples

Excitement uprushed suddenly
A wave of excitement uprushed in the crowd as the band took the stage.
Tears uprushed to her eyes when she heard the unexpected news.
As the volcano erupted, hot gases uprushed from the crater into the sky.
