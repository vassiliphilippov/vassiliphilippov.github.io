biosocial

adjective
biological and social
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
relating to the interaction between biological and social factors or influences

Examples

Biosocial factors
Researchers are interested in the biosocial factors that influence childhood development.
The biosocial approach considers both genetics and environment in explaining behavior.
Many health issues have biosocial causes, involving both biology and social conditions.
