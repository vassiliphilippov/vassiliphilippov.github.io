omnibenevolent

adjective
all-good
Pronunciation: / ˌɑmnɪbəˈnɛvələnt / (AmE), / ˌɒmnɪbəˈnɛvələnt / (BrE)
having unlimited or universal goodness and kindness toward all beings

Examples

Omnibenevolent deity
Many religions describe their deity as omnibenevolent, caring for all people equally.
The philosopher discussed whether an omnibenevolent being could allow suffering in the world.
Stories often portray heroes as omnibenevolent figures who help everyone in need.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: benevolent - showing kindness and goodwill
  • antonym: malevolent - having or showing a wish to do evil to others
