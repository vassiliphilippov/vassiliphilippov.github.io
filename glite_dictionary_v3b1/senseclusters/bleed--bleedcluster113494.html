bleed

verb
remove liquid, air, or steam from a system
8.2
Pronunciation: / bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
to remove or drain liquid, air, or steam from a container or system, especially to ensure it functions correctly

Examples

To bleed a radiator
After repairs, remember to bleed the fuel line to release any air.
Before winter, it's important to bleed the radiators to remove trapped air.
The mechanic had to bleed the brakes to ensure they worked properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - to cause liquid to flow out from something
