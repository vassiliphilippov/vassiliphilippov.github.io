underweight

adjective
allocated below benchmark weight (finance)
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.weɪt/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.weɪt/ (BrE)
(finance) having a smaller allocation in a portfolio than in a benchmark or than a recommended/target weight.

Examples

Underweight in stocks
The fund remains underweight in energy stocks relative to its benchmark index this quarter.
After research, the portfolio manager decided to hold an underweight position in small-cap companies.
Analysts recommended investors remain underweight on the sector until oil prices stabilize over months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overweight - assigned a larger allocation in a portfolio than in the benchmark or target.
  • synonym: underallocate - to assign less capital or weight to an asset or sector than is typical or required.
