much

determiner
large quantity (with mass nouns)
Pronunciation: / mʌtʃ / (AmE), / mʌtʃ / (BrE)
Used to refer to a large quantity or degree, especially with mass nouns that cannot be counted individually.

Examples

Much sugar in tea
How much sugar do you usually put in your tea?
There isn’t much coffee left in the pot, so we should brew more.
We don’t have much information about the new project yet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: many - large number (used with count nouns)
