lecture

verb
scold or criticize sternly
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃə / (BrE)
to criticize or scold someone in a long and serious way, often giving advice or moral instruction

Examples

To lecture a child
His father lectured him about responsibility after he broke the window.
She lectured her team for arriving late to the meeting.
The coach lectured the players on the importance of discipline during practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: scold - to angrily criticize someone, especially a child; to rebuke
