manslaughter

noun
the crime of unintentional killing (not murder)
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌslɔtər / (AmE), / ˈmænˌslɔːtə / (BrE)
The crime of killing a person without having intended to do so, especially when it is not considered as murder because there was no prior intent or malice.

Examples

Convicted of manslaughter
He was convicted of manslaughter after the jury found there was no intention to kill.
Her lawyer argued that the facts supported a verdict of manslaughter rather than murder.
The accident led to charges of manslaughter against the driver.

Related Terms

  • antonym: murder - intentional unlawful killing of another person
