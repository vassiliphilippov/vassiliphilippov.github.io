tackle

noun
act of stopping a player (American football)
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtæk.əl/ (AmE), /ˈtæk.əl/ (BrE)
In American football, an act of stopping an opposing player by grabbing and throwing them to the ground.

Examples

A safe tackle
Every defensive player has to practice safe tackle techniques to avoid penalties.
He made a spectacular tackle that stopped the running back just short of the first down line.
The linebacker’s tackle changed the momentum of the game in the third quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - an act of stopping an opponent's movement, often without throwing to the ground
  • degree lower: dive - attempt in American football to bring down a player by lunging
