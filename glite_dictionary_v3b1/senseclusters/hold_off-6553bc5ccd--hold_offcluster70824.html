hold off

verb
delay taking action
2.6
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ɔf / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf / (BrE)
to delay doing something; to wait before taking action or making a decision

Examples

To hold off a decision
Can you hold off making a decision for another week?
The rain held off just long enough for us to finish the game.
They decided to hold off on buying a new car until next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defer - to put off to a future time
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • antonym: proceed - to begin or continue with an action
