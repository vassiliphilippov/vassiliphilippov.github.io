Synonyms

  • tired
    1.0 80/m
    tired is a common feeling of needing rest or sleep, while weary suggests deeper, long-term physical and mental fatigue.
  • fatigued
    4.1 0.6/m
    fatigued sounds more formal or medical and describes severe tiredness, while weary implies tiredness from long effort.
  • exhausted
    2.0 5/m
    exhausted means completely used up and unable to continue, while weary is tired but may still keep going.
  • worn-out
    2.4 0.00/m
    worn-out describes something or someone extremely tired and no longer effective, while weary is tired but not always unusable.
  • drained
    3.4 0.00/m
    drained feels empty of energy, often after strong emotion or work, while weary is general physical and mental tiredness.
  • worn
    2.8 0.09/m
    worn shows a tired, weathered appearance from worry or hard work, while weary emphasizes the person's inner tired feeling.
  • lethargic
    5.3 0.7/m
    lethargic means slow, inactive, and lacking interest, while weary focuses on tiredness from effort, both can lower energy.
