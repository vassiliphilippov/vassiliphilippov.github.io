drove

verb
to herd animals
Pronunciation: / droʊv / (AmE), / drəʊv / (BrE)
to move animals from one place to another, especially as a group

Examples

He drove the cattle
The farmer drove the cattle down the dusty road to the new pasture.
Every spring, they drove the sheep from the lowlands to the mountain meadows.
He drove the herd carefully to avoid losing any animals along the way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: herd - to gather and move animals together
  • antonym: scatter - to cause animals to move apart in different directions
