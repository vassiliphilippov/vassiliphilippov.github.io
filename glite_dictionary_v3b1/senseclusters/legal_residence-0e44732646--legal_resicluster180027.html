legal residence

noun
a person's official, permanent home for legal purposes
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈliː.gəl ˈrez.ɪ.dəns/ /; / /ˈlɪ.gæl ˈrez.ɪ.dəns/ (AmE), /ˈliː.gəl ˈrez.ɪ.dəns/ /; / /ˈlɪ.gæl ˈrez.ɪ.dəns/ (BrE)
the place where a person has their permanent home or principal establishment and to which they intend to return, especially for legal purposes such as taxation or voting

Examples

Change legal residence
He changed his legal residence after accepting a new job in another state.
Her legal residence is in California, although she spends most of the year abroad.
To vote in a local election, you must prove your legal residence in the district.
