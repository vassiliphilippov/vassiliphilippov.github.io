commercialization

noun
the act of making something commercial
Pronunciation: / kəˌmɜːrʃələˌzeɪʃən /; / kəˌmɝː.ʃə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɜːʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / kəˌmɜː.ʃə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of managing or running something principally for financial gain; bringing something into commercial use or trade

Examples

The commercialization of sports
Many critics argue that the commercialization of sports damages their traditional values.
The commercialization of new technologies often leads to widespread innovation.
The commercialization of space travel is rapidly changing the aerospace industry.
Many scientists worry that the commercialisation of research will compromise academic integrity.
Rapid commercialisation of the technology led to its widespread adoption.
The commercialisation of traditional holidays has faced criticism from cultural groups.
