sheep shed

noun
shelter for sheep
2.8
Pronunciation: /ʃiːp ʃed/ (AmE), /ʃiːp ʃed/ (BrE)
a building or shelter on a farm where sheep are kept, and where they may be shorn and their wool handled

Examples

A sheep shed on farm
The farmer built a new sheep shed to protect the flock from harsh weather.
Every morning, the shepherd checks the sheep shed to make sure the animals are safe.
During the storm, all the lambs were kept inside the sheep shed for warmth.
After the storm, the farmers checked the sheep shed to make sure all the animals were safe.
The freshly shorn wool was piled high inside the sheep shed awaiting transport.
Every spring, the sheep shed becomes the busiest place on the farm during shearing season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barn - a large farm building for storing animals or crops
  • synonym: stable - a building where horses or other livestock are kept
