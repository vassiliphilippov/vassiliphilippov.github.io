diaphragm

noun
device controlling a camera's aperture
8.3 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəfræm / (BrE)
a mechanical device in a camera that controls the size of the lens aperture, regulating the amount of light that enters

Examples

Camera diaphragm
If the diaphragm is set too wide, the resulting image may be overexposed.
Many modern cameras allow the diaphragm to be adjusted automatically for different lighting conditions.
The photographer selected a small diaphragm opening to achieve a greater depth of field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aperture - the opening in a camera through which light passes
