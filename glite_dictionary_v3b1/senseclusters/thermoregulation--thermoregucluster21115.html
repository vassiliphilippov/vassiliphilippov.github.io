thermoregulation

noun
temperature control process
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of controlling or maintaining temperature in mechanical, electronic, or architectural systems to ensure safe or efficient operation

Examples

Building thermoregulation
The building's thermoregulation system adjusts heating and cooling to maintain a comfortable temperature year-round.
Engineers improved the thermoregulation of the vehicle to prevent the battery from overheating.
Effective thermoregulation is essential for keeping sensitive equipment safe in extreme environments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cooling - the process of making something less hot
  • antonym: overheating - the state of becoming too hot
  • synonym: temperature control - the regulation of temperature in a system
