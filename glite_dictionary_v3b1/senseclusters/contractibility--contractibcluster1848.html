contractibility

noun
ability to contract
Pronunciation: / kənˌtræktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌtræktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or ability of being able to contract or become shorter or smaller

Examples

Muscle contractibility
The contractibility of muscle fibers is essential for movement.
Scientists study the contractibility of different materials to understand their properties.
A decrease in contractibility can lead to muscle weakness or fatigue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the ability to bend or be bent easily
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not able to bend or contract
