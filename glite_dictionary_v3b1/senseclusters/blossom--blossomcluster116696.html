blossom

verb
produce flowers (of a plant)
1.4 0.99/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbl…ëÀês…ôm / (AmE), / Ààbl…ís…ôm / (BrE)
(of a tree or plant) to produce or yield flowers.

Examples

Trees blossom in spring
Every spring, the apple trees blossom with delicate white flowers.
If you water your plants regularly, they will blossom beautifully.
The roses in the garden have just begun to blossom.

Synonyms

  • flower
    1.0 5.34/m
    flower is used for a plant producing or being covered with flowers in general, while blossom often refers to trees producing flowers.
  • bloom
    2.3 7.47/m
    bloom means to produce flowers or to thrive for plants and people, while blossom usually describes a plant producing flowers.
  • effloresce
    9.9 0.00/m
    effloresce is a rare formal word for coming into flower or developing fully, while blossom is the common plant term.
