demythologization

noun
reinterpretation to remove mythical elements
7.2 9.1
Pronunciation: /diː.mɪ.θɑː.lə.dʒə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.mɪ.θɑː.lə.dʒə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.mɪ.θɒ.lə.dʒə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.mɪ.θɒ.lə.dʒə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the reinterpretation of a message, especially a religious one, to remove mythological elements and express it in rational terms

Examples

The demythologization of miracles
His approach to theology included a rigorous demythologization of miracles and supernatural events.
Many scholars debated the value of demythologization in making religious texts accessible to a rational audience.
The process of demythologization seeks to reinterpret biblical stories in a way that is meaningful for modern readers.
His approach to demythologisation sought to remove supernatural elements from the traditional narrative.
Many scholars debate the limits and value of demythologisation in interpreting sacred texts.
The theologian argued that demythologisation is necessary to make ancient scriptures relevant to modern audiences.
