encroacher

noun
one who intrudes or seizes territory or rights
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkroʊtʃər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃə / (BrE)
A person who intrudes or advances gradually, often by force, on another's territory, rights, or property.

Examples

An encroacher on land
As an encroacher, he faced stiff resistance from the village defending their border.
The law aims to protect property owners from any encroacher attempting to seize land unlawfully.
The report identified the company as an encroacher on indigenous lands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intruder - a person who enters without permission
  • synonym: invader - a person who enters forcibly with hostile intent
