fricative consonant

noun
consonant made by breath friction
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪk.ə.tɪv ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈfrɪk.ə.tɪv ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by forcing breath through a narrow gap in the vocal tract, causing friction

Examples

Fricative consonants sounds
English contains several fricative consonants, such as /s/, /z/, and /ʃ/.
Linguists study how airflow and tongue position result in different fricative consonant sounds.
The sound /f/ in 'fish' is a classic example of a fricative consonant.
