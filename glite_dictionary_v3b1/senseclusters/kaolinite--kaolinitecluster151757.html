kaolinite

noun
a mineral; main component of kaolin clay
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪələˌnaɪt / (AmE), / keɪˈɒlɪnʌɪt / (BrE)
a white or clay-colored mineral consisting of hydrated aluminum silicate, the main constituent of kaolin clay

Examples

White kaolinite mineral
Geologists analyzed the sediment samples and found significant amounts of kaolinite.
Pure kaolinite is often used in the production of porcelain and ceramics.
The white color of the soil indicates a high concentration of kaolinite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kaolin - fine clay used in ceramics and medicine, mainly composed of kaolinite
