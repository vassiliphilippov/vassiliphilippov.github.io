cotton thistle

noun
a biennial Eurasian thistle with pale purple flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑt̬n ˌθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn ˌθɪsəl / (BrE)
A tall, white, hairy biennial thistle with pale purple flowers, native to Eurasia and naturalized in North America.

Examples

Tall cotton thistle
Gardeners sometimes remove cotton thistle to prevent it from spreading in the wild.
The cotton thistle can grow up to three meters tall in ideal conditions.
The pale purple flowers of the cotton thistle are attractive to bees.
