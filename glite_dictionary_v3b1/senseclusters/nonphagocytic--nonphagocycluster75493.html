nonphagocytic

adjective
resistant to phagocytosis
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌfæɡəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌfæɡəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
not able to be engulfed and digested by phagocytic cells; resistant to phagocytosis

Examples

Nonphagocytic bacteria
The bacteria's thick capsule made it nonphagocytic and difficult for immune cells to destroy.
Some pathogens are nonphagocytic, allowing them to persist in the body despite immune responses.
Researchers studied how nonphagocytic organisms evade the body's natural defenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phagocytic - able to engulf and digest particles or cells
