trusteeism

noun
church property dispute
Pronunciation: / trʌsˈtiːɪzəm / (AmE), / trʌsˈtiːɪzəm / (BrE)
a system or practice in which lay (non-clergy) members manage church property or affairs, often leading to conflict with church authorities

Examples

Trusteeism in churches
The rise of trusteeism in the 1800s challenged the authority of Catholic bishops in the United States.
Many parishes were divided by trusteeism, as lay members demanded a greater say in church affairs.
Historians study trusteeism to understand conflicts between church leadership and local congregations.
Trusteeism was a source of conflict between church leaders and lay members in the 19th century.
The history class discussed how trusteeism affected the development of American Catholic parishes.
Disagreements over trusteeism sometimes resulted in legal battles over church ownership.

Related Terms

  • antonym: episcopal authority - the power held by bishops in church governance
  • synonym: lay trustee - a non-clergy member managing church property
  • synonym: trusteeship - the position or responsibility of being a trustee
