unclouded

adjective
free from clouds; clear
7.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
clear and not clouded, either literally (like the sky, a liquid, or a view) or figuratively (like a mind, sound, or color)

Examples

An unclouded sky
Her future looked bright and unclouded after graduation.
The mountain was visible only on rare, unclouded mornings.
We enjoyed a picnic beneath an unclouded sky.
His memory of that day remained unclouded by sadness.
The wine was perfectly unclouded, showing its fine quality.
Through the unclouded glass, she could see the sun setting.
After a good night's sleep, her unclouded mind was ready to face the day's challenges.
He approached the problem with an unclouded sense of judgment and made the right decision.
Meditation helped him maintain an unclouded perspective even during stressful times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clear - free of cloudiness, impurities, or obstructions
  • synonym: clear-headed - able to think clearly; not confused
  • synonym: cloudless - (of the sky) without any clouds; clear
  • antonym: confused - unable to think clearly; mentally disordered
  • antonym: dim - not bright or clear; lacking sharpness or clarity
