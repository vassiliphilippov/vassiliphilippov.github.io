biodiverse

adjective
rich in species
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊdaɪˈvɜrs / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊdaɪˈvɜːs / (BrE)
containing a large variety of different living organisms, such as plants and animals

Examples

A biodiverse area
The coral reef is incredibly biodiverse, supporting thousands of marine species.
Conservationists work to protect biodiverse habitats from destruction.
A biodiverse ecosystem is more resilient to environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety
  • antonym: monotonous - lacking in variety or diversity
