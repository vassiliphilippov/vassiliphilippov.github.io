embattled

adjective
experiencing difficulties or conflict
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbætəld / (AmE), / ɪmˈbætld /; / ɪmˈbætəld / (BrE)
experiencing serious difficulties, conflict, or attack (often used for people, organizations, or places)

Examples

Embattled leader
Despite being embattled on all sides, she remained determined to accomplish her goals.
The embattled CEO faced growing pressure from shareholders to resign.
The embattled company struggled to stay afloat amid fierce competition and public scrutiny.
Residents evacuated the embattled city as the fighting intensified.
The embattled fortress held out against enemy attacks for weeks.
The embattled mayor refused to resign despite mounting criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besieged - being surrounded and under attack, especially metaphorically
  • antonym: defenseless - without defense or protection
  • synonym: troubled - experiencing problems or difficulties
