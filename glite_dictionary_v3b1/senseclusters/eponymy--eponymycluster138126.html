eponymy

noun
naming after a person
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒnɪmi / (BrE)
The process by which a name or term is derived from the name of a person, especially a famous one.

Examples

Eponymy from names
'Diesel' is a case of eponymy, deriving from the inventor Rudolf Diesel.
Many places exhibit eponymy, such as Washington, D.C., named after George Washington.
The word 'sandwich' is an example of eponymy, named after the Earl of Sandwich.
