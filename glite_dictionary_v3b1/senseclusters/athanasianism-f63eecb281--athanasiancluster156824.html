Athanasianism

noun
Christian doctrine: Christ of same substance as God the Father
Pronunciation: / ˌæθ.əˈneɪ.ʒəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæθ.əˈneɪ.zɪəˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
The Christian doctrine, taught by Athanasius, asserting that Christ the Son is of the same substance as God the Father.

Examples

Athanasianism doctrine
Many early church councils debated the tenets of Athanasianism and Arianism.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of Athanasianism in medieval theology.
The doctrine of Athanasianism became central to mainstream Christian belief in the Trinity.
