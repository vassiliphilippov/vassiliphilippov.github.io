nativistic

adjective
relating to nativism or native beliefs
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌneɪ.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌneɪ.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the protection, preservation, or revival of native or traditional ideas, interests, or cultures, especially against outside influences

Examples

Nativistic views on culture
His views on immigration were shaped by a nativistic perspective.
Some psychologists support nativistic explanations of language acquisition.
There is a long-standing debate between nativistic and environmental approaches in philosophy.
Critics argue that nativistic sentiments can lead to resistance against beneficial innovation.
Many leaders in the movement held nativistic beliefs, promoting traditional customs over foreign ones.
The nativistic approach seeks to revive cultural practices that are at risk of disappearing.
