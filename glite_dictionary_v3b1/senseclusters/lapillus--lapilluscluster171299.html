lapillus

noun
volcanic rock fragment
Pronunciation: / ləˈpɪləs / (AmE), / ləˈpɪləs / (BrE)
a small rock fragment, typically 2–64 mm in diameter, ejected by a volcano during an eruption

Examples

Volcanic lapillus
During the eruption, lapillus fragments rained down on the nearby village.
Scientists collected several lapillus samples to study the volcano's activity.
The ground was covered with lapillus after the explosive event.
