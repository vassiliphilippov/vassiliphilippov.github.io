reinforcement

noun
strengthening support or evidence
3.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪn.ˈfɔːr.smənt/ /; / /rɪn.ˈfɔːr.smənt/ (AmE), /riː.ɪn.ˈfɔː.smənt/ /; / /rɪn.ˈfɔː.smənt/ (BrE)
additional information, evidence, or support that makes an argument, feeling, or statement stronger or more convincing.

Examples

Verbal reinforcement of ideas
Her confident tone acted as reinforcement for the argument she was making.
The expert's testimony served as important reinforcement for the defendant's case.
Visual aids provided helpful reinforcement to the main points of her presentation.
