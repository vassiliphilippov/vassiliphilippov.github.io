part

verb
to separate and go in different directions
2.9 13/m
Pronunciation: / p…ërt / (AmE), / p…ëÀêt / (BrE)
to separate, divide, or go in different directions after being together; to break apart

Examples

To part ways
After the meeting, we parted ways and each went home separately.
Once the event was over, the group parted in different directions.
The two friends parted at the train station, promising to stay in touch.
As the rain fell harder, the crowd began to part and seek shelter.
The curtains part to reveal the stage at the start of the play.
We have to part ways at the crossroads, but I hope we meet again.
After many disagreements, the two friends parted and chose not to see each other again.
We decided to part ways after completing the project.
When the band members parted, each of them started a solo career.

Synonyms

  • split
    2.3 20/m
    split means leaving or going different ways, often suddenly, while part means to separate or go in different directions.
  • separate
    2.9 25/m
    separate means to move apart or end an association in a calm, neutral way, while part means to break apart or go different ways.
  • break apart
    3.0 1.62/m
    break apart means to come into pieces or collapse, often with force, while part means to separate or go in different directions.
  • divide
    1.1 52/m
    divide means to split into parts, groups, or sections, often for counting or sharing, while part means to separate or go different ways.
