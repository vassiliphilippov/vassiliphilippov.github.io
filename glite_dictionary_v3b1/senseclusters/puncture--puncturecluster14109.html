puncture

noun
small hole made by a sharp object
1.3 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌŋk.tʃɚ /; / ˈpʌŋktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋk.tʃə /; / ˈpʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
a small hole made by a sharp object, especially in a tire, skin, or similar surface, or the act of making such a hole

Examples

A tire puncture
Be careful not to get a puncture when walking through the construction site.
She found a puncture in the car tire, probably caused by a nail on the road.
The doctor said the wound was a simple puncture that would heal quickly.
A tire puncture can cause a sudden loss of pressure while driving.
The doctor made a small puncture in the patient's skin to draw blood.
There was evidence of a single puncture in the balloon, causing it to deflate rapidly.
She discovered a slow puncture after noticing the car was pulling to one side.
The cyclist fixed his puncture quickly and continued the race.
We had to stop on the highway because of a puncture in the front tire.
