patellar reflex

noun
reflex leg extension after tapping the knee
9.7
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛlɚ rəˈflɛks / (AmE), / pəˈtɛlə rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
a reflex action resulting in the sudden extension of the lower leg after a sharp tap on the patellar tendon, often tested in medical examinations

Examples

A patellar reflex test
A brisk patellar reflex is often seen in patients with certain neurological conditions.
The doctor tested my patellar reflex by tapping just below my kneecap.
When the patellar reflex is absent, it may indicate nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knee-jerk reflex - reflex in which the knee jerks involuntarily when tapped
