tripwire

noun
triggering event
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪpˌwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpˌwaɪə / (BrE)
a small action, event, or condition that automatically triggers a much larger reaction or response

Examples

Tripwire for protests
The announcement acted as a tripwire for widespread protests across the city.
A single mistake can serve as a tripwire for a much larger investigation.
The new regulation became a tripwire that set off heated debates in parliament.
