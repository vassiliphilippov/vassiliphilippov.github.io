counterexample

noun
an example disproving a statement
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərɪɡˌzæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntərəɡˌzɑːmpəl / (BrE)
An example that disproves a proposition or theory by showing an exception to it.

Examples

A counterexample disproves
Her argument was weakened when he provided a convincing counterexample.
If you believe all swans are white, a single black swan is a counterexample.
The mathematician found a counterexample that showed the conjecture was false.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement
