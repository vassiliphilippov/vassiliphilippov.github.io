burst

verb
emerge suddenly
Pronunciation: / bɝst / (AmE), / bɜːst / (BrE)
to appear, move, or enter suddenly and energetically

Examples

Burst onto the stage
She burst onto the stage with energy and confidence.
The clouds parted and the sun burst into view, warming the chilly air.
The flowers burst into bloom after the spring rain.
He burst into the meeting room, out of breath and full of excitement.
The players burst onto the field, energized by the cheering crowd.
When the alarm went off, she burst out of bed and ran to the door.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: appear - to come into view
  • antonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully in order to avoid being heard or noticed
  • synonym: dash - to move quickly or suddenly in a particular direction
  • synonym: erupt - to break out or burst forth suddenly
  • synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
