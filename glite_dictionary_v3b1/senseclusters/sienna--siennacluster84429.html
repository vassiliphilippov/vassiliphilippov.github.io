sienna

noun
reddish-brown earth pigment
Pronunciation: / siˈɛnə / (AmE), / siˈɛnə / (BrE)
A reddish-brown earth pigment containing ferric oxides, used especially as a coloring agent in painting and art.

Examples

Rich sienna pigment
He preferred the rich, earthy shade of sienna for the background of his landscape.
Natural sienna has been used in painting since the Renaissance period.
The artist mixed sienna with other pigments to achieve the perfect warm tone for the portrait.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ochre - an earthy pigment containing ferric oxide, typically with clay
  • synonym: umber - a natural brown earth pigment containing iron and manganese oxides
