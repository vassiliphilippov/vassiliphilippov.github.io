deterministic

adjective
entirely determined by prior causes
Pronunciation: / dɪˌtɝmɪˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / dɪˌtɜːmɪˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
(of a process or event) having outcomes that are entirely determined by previously existing causes or conditions; not random.

Examples

A deterministic system
In physics, classical mechanics describes the universe as a deterministic system.
Some philosophers argue that free will cannot exist in a deterministic universe.
The behavior of the machine is entirely deterministic and follows strict rules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: random - having no definite plan, purpose, or pattern
