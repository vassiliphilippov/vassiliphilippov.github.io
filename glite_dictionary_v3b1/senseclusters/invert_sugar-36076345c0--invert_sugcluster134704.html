invert sugar

noun
mixture of glucose and fructose (sweetener)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɝːt ˈʃuːɡɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːt ˈʃuːɡə / (BrE)
a mixture of glucose and fructose produced by the hydrolysis of sucrose, used as a sweetener and found in fruits

Examples

Invert sugar syrup
Because invert sugar attracts moisture, baked goods made with it often stay softer for longer.
Natural invert sugar is found in honey and certain fruits.
Some commercial candies use invert sugar to enhance sweetness and improve texture.
