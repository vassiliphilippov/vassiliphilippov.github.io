restructuring

noun
organizational change
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈstrʌktʃərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈstrʌktʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of organizing something, especially a company or system, in a new or different way (often to improve efficiency or solve problems)

Examples

Company restructuring
The company announced a major restructuring to improve efficiency.
After the economic crisis, the government began a restructuring of the financial sector.
Many employees were affected by the recent restructuring in the organization.
After the merger, the company underwent a major restructuring to combine its departments.
The government announced a restructuring of the education system to improve quality.
Many employees were affected by the recent restructuring in the organization.
The company announced a major restructuring to address its growing financial problems.
After the merger, a restructuring of management roles was necessary to streamline operations.
The bank avoided bankruptcy through a successful restructuring of its debts and assets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liquidation - the process of closing a business and selling its assets
  • synonym: reform - the process of making changes to improve something
  • synonym: reorganization - the process of arranging something in a new way
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and unchanging
