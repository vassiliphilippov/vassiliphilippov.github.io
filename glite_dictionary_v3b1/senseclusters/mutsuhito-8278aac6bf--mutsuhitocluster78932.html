Mutsuhito

proper noun
emperor of Japan (1867–1912); Emperor Meiji
Pronunciation: / muːtsuːˈhiːtoʊ / (AmE), / muːtsuːˈhiːtəʊ / (BrE)
the emperor of Japan from 1867 to 1912, known as Emperor Meiji, who led the country through a period of modernization

Examples

Mutsuhito emperor Japan
During his reign, Mutsuhito transformed Japan into a major world power.
Many regard Mutsuhito as a visionary leader who embraced Western ideas.
The Meiji Restoration was initiated under the rule of Mutsuhito.
