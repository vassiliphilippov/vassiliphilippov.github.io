prepurchase

verb
to buy in advance
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈpɜːrtʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
to buy something in advance, especially before it is needed or before its price increases

Examples

To prepurchase tickets
We decided to prepurchase our train tickets to avoid higher prices on the day of travel.
You can prepurchase concert tickets online before they sell out.
Many customers choose to prepurchase software licenses for the upcoming year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buy - to obtain something by paying money for it
  • antonym: refund - to return money for something purchased
  • synonym: reserve - to arrange for something to be kept for your use in advance
