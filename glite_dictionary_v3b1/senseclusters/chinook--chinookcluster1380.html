chinook

noun
warm, dry wind from the Rockies
7.0
Pronunciation: / ʃɪˈnʊk / (AmE), / ʃɪˈnʊk / (BrE)
a warm, dry wind that blows down the eastern slopes of the Rocky Mountains, especially in North America

Examples

Chinook wind
Farmers in Alberta welcomed the chinook after a long spell of cold weather.
Temperatures can rise dramatically when a chinook sweeps through the region.
The arrival of a chinook melted much of the snow overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foehn - a warm dry wind descending a mountain slope, especially in the Alps
