Aramaean

noun
member of an ancient Semitic people of Aram
9.9
Pronunciation: /ær.ə.ˈmiː.ən/ /; / /e.rə.ˈmiː.ən/ (AmE), /ær.ə.ˈmiː.ən/ (BrE)
a member of an ancient Semitic people inhabiting Aram (modern-day Syria and surrounding regions) and parts of Mesopotamia from the 11th to the 8th century BC.

Examples

An Aramaean king
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts believed to belong to the Aramaean civilization.
Several Old Testament passages mention interactions with the Aramaeans of Syria.
The ancient Aramaeans played a significant role in the history of the Middle East.
