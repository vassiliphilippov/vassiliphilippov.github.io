internal combustion

noun
fuel burned inside an engine
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrnəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː.nəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (BrE)
A process in which fuel is burned within a cylinder or chamber of an engine, producing energy to drive the engine.

Examples

Internal combustion engine
In internal combustion, fuel and air mix and ignite within the engine cylinder.
Most cars today rely on internal combustion to power their engines.
The invention of the internal combustion engine revolutionized transportation in the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: external combustion - combustion of fuel outside the engine cylinder
