transversion

noun
altered version
Pronunciation: / trænzˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a change or alteration of something, especially a distortion or corrupted version of words or meaning, often in literary or archaic contexts

Examples

A transversion of meaning
The scholar criticized the transversion of the ancient text for its many inaccuracies.
A transversion of the story appeared in later editions, changing key details.
Historians debated whether the document was an authentic copy or a transversion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distortion - a change that makes something no longer true or accurate
  • antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something
  • synonym: version - a particular form or variant of something
