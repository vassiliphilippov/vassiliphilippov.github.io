telling

adjective
having strong or notable effect
2.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtel.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtel.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
having a strong effect or revealing something, especially in a way that is striking or shows hidden information

Examples

A telling sign
A telling look passed between the two friends, revealing more than words could.
Her silence was a telling indication of her disapproval.
The lawyer's argument had a telling impact on the jury's decision.
Her most telling argument convinced the committee to change its policy.
That was a telling detail in his testimony that changed many minds.
The prosecutor's telling evidence swayed the jury toward a guilty verdict.
Her telling glance betrayed her true feelings.
The detective noticed a telling detail in the photograph.
There was a telling silence after he made the announcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cogent - clear, logical, and convincing
  • antonym: discreet - careful not to reveal secrets or private information
  • synonym: impressive - evoking admiration through size, quality, or skill
  • antonym: insignificant - having little or no importance or effect
  • synonym: revealing - making something known that was hidden
  • synonym: weighty - having great influence or importance
