seriality

noun
relation property in logic
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˌriːˈæləti / (AmE), / sɪəˈrɪəlɪti / (BrE)
the property of a binary relation in which every element is related to at least one element, especially in logic or mathematics

Examples

Seriality in logic
The professor discussed seriality as a key property in the study of binary relations.
Understanding seriality helps students grasp more complex concepts in mathematical logic.
A relation lacking seriality may not satisfy certain logical requirements in formal proofs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflexivity - the property of a relation where every element is related to itself
  • synonym: transitivity - the property of a relation where if one element is related to a second, and the second to a third, then the first is related to the third
