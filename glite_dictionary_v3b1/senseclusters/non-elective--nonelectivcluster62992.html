non-elective

adjective
appointed, not elected
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.ɪˈlɛk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.ɪˈlɛk.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a position or office) filled by appointment rather than by election.

Examples

Non-elective position
Many advisory boards are comprised of non-elective members chosen for their specific expertise.
The Supreme Court is a non-elective office with justices selected by the president.
Unlike the mayor, the city manager holds a non-elective position appointed by the city council.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elective - chosen by election rather than appointment
