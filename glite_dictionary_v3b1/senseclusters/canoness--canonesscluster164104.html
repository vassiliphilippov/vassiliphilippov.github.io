canoness

noun
female church member
Pronunciation: / ˈkænənɪs /; / ˈkænənɛs / (AmE), / ˈkanənɪs /; / ˈkanənɛs / (BrE)
a woman who is a member of a religious community attached to a church, sometimes with special rules or privileges

Examples

A canoness in church

Unlike a nun, a canoness could own property and was not required to take lifelong vows.

As a canoness, she participated in daily prayers and community service.
The history of the abbey includes several notable canonesses who contributed to education.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nun - a woman who has taken religious vows and lives in a convent
