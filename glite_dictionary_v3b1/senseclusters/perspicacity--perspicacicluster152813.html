perspicacity

noun
keen insight or perceptiveness
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜrspɪˈkæsəti / (AmE), / ˌpɜːspɪˈkæsəti /; / ˌpɜːspɪˈkæsɪti / (BrE)
the ability to quickly and accurately understand or notice things; keen insight or sharp judgment

Examples

Her perspicacity showed
Her perspicacity allowed her to quickly identify the real issues behind the team's failure.
The CEO's business perspicacity helped the company stay ahead of its competitors.
With remarkable perspicacity, he noticed a flaw in the otherwise convincing argument.
Her perspicacity allowed her to anticipate problems before they arose.
Investors admired his perspicacity in predicting market trends.
The detective's perspicacity led to the swift resolution of the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acuity - sharpness or keenness of thought, vision, or hearing
  • antonym: obtuseness - lack of sharpness or quickness of sensation or intellect
  • synonym: shrewdness - the quality of having or showing sharp powers of judgment
