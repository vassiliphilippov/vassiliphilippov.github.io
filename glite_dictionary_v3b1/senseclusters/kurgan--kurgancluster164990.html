kurgan

noun
ancient burial mound
Pronunciation: / kʊrˈɡɑn / (AmE), / kʊəˈɡɑːn / (BrE)
a large burial mound or barrow built by ancient peoples in Eastern Europe or Central Asia

Examples

Ancient kurgan mound
The archaeologists discovered a Bronze Age kurgan on the windswept steppe.
Many treasures were found inside the ancient kurgan during the excavation.
Historians believe the kurgan was built for a powerful tribal leader.
Archaeologists discovered a Bronze Age kurgan in the steppes of Kazakhstan.
The ancient kurgan contained valuable artifacts and the remains of a chieftain.
Many kurgans are important sources of information about early nomadic cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tumulus - an earthen mound raised over a grave
