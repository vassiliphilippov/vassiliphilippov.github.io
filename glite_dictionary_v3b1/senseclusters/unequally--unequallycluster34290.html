unequally

adverb
in an unequal manner
4.3 0.54/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàiÀêkw…ôli / (AmE), /  ånÀàiÀêkw…ôli / (BrE)
In a way that is not equal, fair, or uniform.

Examples

Distributed unequally
Opportunities are still distributed unequally in many parts of society.
Students from different backgrounds are sometimes treated unequally in the classroom.
The inheritance was divided unequally among the siblings, causing resentment.

Synonyms

  • unevenly
    3.5 1.16/m
    unevenly describes things that are not regular or smooth, while unequally focuses on being not the same or fair.
  • inequitably
    8.0 0.05/m
    inequitably describes actions that treat people unfairly in formal contexts, while unequally simply means not equal or uniform.
  • unfairly
    1.8 4.80/m
    unfairly means doing something without justice or kindness, while unequally can mean not equal in amount or treatment.
  • unjustly
    5.2 1.35/m
    unjustly means acting in a morally wrong or illegal way, while unequally may only mean not equally balanced.
  • disparately
    7.9 0.01/m
    disparately describes different effects or treatment across groups, while unequally just means not equal or uniform in general.
  • disproportionately
    7.1 5.60/m
    disproportionately means to a much larger or smaller degree than expected, while unequally means simply not equal.
