Messalian

noun
early Christian sect member
Pronunciation: / mɛˈseɪliən / (AmE), / mɛˈseɪliən / (BrE)
a member of an early Christian sect that emphasized continual prayer and was considered heretical by mainstream churches

Examples

A Messalian mystic
The Messalian sect was active in the eastern regions of the Roman Empire.
Historians debate the true beliefs and practices of the Messalian community.
Church authorities condemned the Messalian movement for its unorthodox teachings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to orthodox religious doctrine
