dream

noun
imaginative thought or fantasy while awake
2.6
Pronunciation: / driːm / (AmE), / driːm / (BrE)
a hope, ambition, or fantasy; something you strongly wish for, imagine, or think about, even if it may not be real or possible

Examples

A lifelong dream
His dream of becoming a famous artist kept him motivated.
Lost in a dream, she barely noticed the passing cars.
Sometimes he escapes reality by living in a dream.
Becoming an astronaut seemed like only a dream to her when she was a child.
His dream of retiring before forty was never realized.
Owning a private island is just a dream for most people.
He worked hard every day to make his dream of becoming an architect come true.
Her lifelong dream was to perform on the world’s biggest stages.
Owning a small café by the beach is my mother’s dream.
