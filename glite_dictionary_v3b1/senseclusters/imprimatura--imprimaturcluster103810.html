imprimatura

noun
base paint layer
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmprɪməˈtʊrə / (AmE), / ˌɪmprɪməˈtjʊərə / (BrE)
a thin, first layer of paint applied to a canvas or panel to prepare it for painting and set the overall tone

Examples

A imprimatura layer
Before starting the portrait, the painter applied a warm-toned imprimatura to the canvas.
Using a transparent imprimatura helps artists judge values and colors more accurately.
The subtle imprimatura gave the finished painting a harmonious undertone.
The artist began with an imprimatura to set the overall tone of the painting.
Applying a warm imprimatura can help unify the colors in the final artwork.
Before painting details, she covered the canvas with a light brown imprimatura.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: glaze - a thin, transparent layer of paint applied over another
  • synonym: ground - a prepared surface on a canvas or panel for painting
  • synonym: underpainting - an initial layer of paint that serves as a base for later layers
