stampede

noun
wild rush of panicked animals
5.2 4.0
Pronunciation: /stæm.ˈpiːd/ /; / /stæm.ˈpɪd/ (AmE), /stæm.ˈpiːd/ /; / /stæm.ˈpɪd/ (BrE)
a sudden, uncontrolled rush of a group of animals or people, usually caused by fear or excitement

Examples

A cattle stampede
A sudden movement in the herd triggered a stampede across the plain.
The cowboys managed to stop the stampede just in time to prevent injuries.
There was a stampede when the cattle were startled by a loud noise.
A rumor in the marketplace triggered a stampede of shoppers.
The crowd began to stampede when the fire alarm rang.
There was a desperate stampede towards the exits after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - state of tranquility or peace, absence of panic
  • synonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • synonym: rout - A disorderly or chaotic retreat, especially of a crowd or army
