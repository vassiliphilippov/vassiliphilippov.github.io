state

noun
form of matter, e.g., solid, liquid, or gas
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /steɪt/ (AmE), /steɪt/ (BrE)
A particular form or condition in which matter exists, especially one of the three main forms: solid, liquid, or gas.

Examples

State of matter
At room temperature, oxygen is found in the gaseous state.
Changing the temperature can cause a substance to change its state.
Water can exist in the state of a solid, liquid, or gas.
