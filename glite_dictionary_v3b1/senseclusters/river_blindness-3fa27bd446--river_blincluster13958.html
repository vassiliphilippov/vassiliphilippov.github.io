river blindness

noun
a parasitic tropical disease causing blindness
5.3 0.05/m

A tropical disease caused by infection with the parasitic worm Onchocerca volvulus, transmitted by blackflies, which can result in severe itching, skin changes, and, if the eyes are affected, blindness.

Examples

River blindness disease
Efforts to control river blindness include the mass distribution of anti-parasitic medication.
If not treated, river blindness can cause severe visual impairment and even total blindness.
River blindness has affected millions of people living near fast-flowing rivers in sub-Saharan Africa.
