prenegotiation

noun
preliminary agreement
7.4
Pronunciation: /priː.nə.goʊ.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.nə.goʊ.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.nə.gəʊ.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.nə.gəʊ.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the period, activities, or informal arrangements that take place before formal negotiations begin

Examples

A prenegotiation phase
The two companies held a prenegotiation to outline their main goals before starting the official talks.

The prenegotiation stage allowed both companies to identify their main objectives.
Effective prenegotiation can lead to smoother and more productive talks later on.
During prenegotiation, the team gathered information about the other party's interests.
