gradualistic

adjective
favoring gradual change
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrædʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡrædʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
favoring or relating to gradual change, especially as an explanation for evolution or development

Examples

A gradualistic process
The theory proposes a gradualistic process of change rather than abrupt shifts.
Many historians take a gradualistic approach to explaining social transformation.
Her gradualistic perspective influenced the way she managed organizational change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incremental - happening in small steps or stages
  • antonym: sudden - happening quickly and unexpectedly
