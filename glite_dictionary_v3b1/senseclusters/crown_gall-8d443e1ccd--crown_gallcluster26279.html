crown gall

noun
plant disease causing stem growths
8.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /kraʊn gɔːl/ /; / /kroʊn gɔːl/ (AmE), /kraʊn gɔːl/ (BrE)
A bacterial disease of plants, especially fruit trees and roses, causing tumor-like growths near the base of the stem.

Examples

Crown gall disease
Gardeners are advised to inspect the roots carefully for any signs of crown gall.
Roses are particularly susceptible to crown gall, which can stunt their growth.
The cherry orchard suffered severe losses due to crown gall infecting many of the trees.
