enigmatic canon

noun
type of musical canon with cryptic notation
9.2 9.8
Pronunciation: /e.nɪg.ˈmæt.ɪk ˈkæn.ən/ (AmE), /e.nɪg.ˈmæt.ɪk ˈkæn.ən/ (BrE)
A musical canon in which the entrances of successive voices are indicated by cryptic symbols or devices, popular in the 15th and 16th centuries.

Examples

An enigmatic canon composition

Scholars studied the enigmatic canon to understand the intricate notational puzzles left by 16th-century composers.
The composer wrote an enigmatic canon that required performers to decipher the entry points from cryptic cues.
