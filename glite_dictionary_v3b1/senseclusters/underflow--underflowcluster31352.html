underflow

noun
subsurface fluid flow
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌfləʊ / (BrE)
a flow of water or other fluid beneath the surface or below another current, often carrying sediment or moving differently from the surface flow

Examples

River underflow carries sand
The river's underflow transported fine sand and silt far downstream after the heavy rains.
Engineers studied the underflow to understand how nutrients moved beneath the wetland surface.
A strong underflow can erode the riverbed even when the surface appears calm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subsurface flow - movement of water beneath the ground or surface
  • antonym: surface flow - movement of water along the top layer of a body of water
