banderole

noun
decorative scroll in art
Pronunciation: / ˈbændəˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈbændərəʊl / (BrE)
a ribbonlike scroll, often painted or drawn in medieval or Renaissance art, displaying words such as names, speech, or mottos

Examples

Painted banderole
The artist painted a banderole above the knight, inscribed with his family motto.
In many medieval manuscripts, a banderole is used to show what a figure is saying.
The church mural features a banderole with a Latin phrase flowing from the angel's mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design
  • synonym: scroll - a roll of parchment or paper for writing or decoration
