aldolase

noun
sugar-splitting enzyme
Pronunciation: / ˈældəˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˈældəˌleɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that helps break down sugars during metabolism by catalyzing the splitting of certain sugars

Examples

aldolase enzyme activity
Doctors measured the patient's aldolase levels to assess muscle health.
A deficiency in aldolase can lead to problems with energy production in cells.
Researchers studied how aldolase activity changes during intense exercise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in the body
  • synonym: hexokinase - an enzyme that helps start the breakdown of glucose
