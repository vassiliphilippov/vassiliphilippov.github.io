drag one's heels

verb
to delay or procrastinate
7.0
Pronunciation: / dræɡ wʌnz hilz / (AmE), / dræɡ wʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
to delay taking action or to be deliberately slow in doing something, especially because you do not want to do it

Examples

To drag one's heels
She started dragging her heels when asked to fill out the tedious paperwork.
The committee has been accused of dragging its heels on this important issue.
Whenever it's time to clean his room, he tends to drag his heels for as long as possible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hasten - to move or act quickly
  • synonym: procrastinate - to delay or postpone action
