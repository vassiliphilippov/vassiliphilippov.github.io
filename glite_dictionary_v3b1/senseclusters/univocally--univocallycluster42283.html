univocally

adverb
with one meaning
8.2
Pronunciation: / juːˈnɪvəkli / (AmE), / juːˈnɪvəkəli / (BrE)
in a way that has only one meaning or interpretation; clearly and unambiguously

Examples

To speak univocally
The instructions were written univocally so that everyone could understand them.
To avoid misunderstandings, the policy must be stated univocally in the contract.
The scientist explained the results univocally, leaving no room for doubt.
The term must be defined univocally to prevent confusion in scientific discussions.
In logic, words are used univocally so that their meaning does not change between statements.
The philosopher argued that the concept applies univocally to all members of the group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ambiguously - in a way that is open to more than one interpretation
  • synonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to understand and not confusing
  • antonym: equivocally - in a way that allows for more than one interpretation
