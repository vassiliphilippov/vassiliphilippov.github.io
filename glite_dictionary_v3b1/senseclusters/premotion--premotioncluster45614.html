premotion

noun
divine influence on will
9.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.ˈməʊ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈməʊ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.ˈməʊ.ʃən/ (BrE)
in Christian theology, a divine influence or prior action by God that moves a person's will toward a particular choice or action

Examples

Divine premotion of will
The doctrine of premotion is central to some theological debates about human freedom and divine power.
Scholars discussed how premotion relates to the concept of predestination in Christian thought.
Some philosophers argue that premotion does not eliminate human responsibility for actions.

  • synonym: grace - divine help or favor given by God
