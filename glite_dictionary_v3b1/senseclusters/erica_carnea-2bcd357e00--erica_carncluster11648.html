Erica carnea

noun
dwarf European shrub with early bell-shaped flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrɪkə kɑrˈniə / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪkə kɑːˈniːə / (BrE)
a dwarf European shrub with very early-blooming, bell-shaped red or pink flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant

Examples

Erica carnea shrub
Although hardy and easy to care for, Erica carnea prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
Gardeners often choose Erica carnea for its ability to bloom in late winter.
The early blossoms of Erica carnea add vibrant color to rock gardens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heather - low evergreen shrub with small, pinkish-purple flowers; often used as an ornamental plant
