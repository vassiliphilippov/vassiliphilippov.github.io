seropositivity

noun
presence of antibodies
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪəroʊˌpɑzəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌsɪərəʊˌpɒzɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the presence of specific antibodies in the blood, showing that someone has been exposed to or infected by a particular disease or pathogen

Examples

Seropositivity for HIV
A positive HIV test indicates seropositivity for the virus.
Researchers studied the rate of seropositivity among healthcare workers after the outbreak.
Routine screening can help detect seropositivity in people exposed to infectious diseases.
A high rate of seropositivity in the community suggests widespread exposure to the virus.
Doctors tested for seropositivity to determine if the patient had developed immunity.
The research focused on changes in seropositivity after the vaccination campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym: seronegativity - the absence of specific antibodies in the blood
