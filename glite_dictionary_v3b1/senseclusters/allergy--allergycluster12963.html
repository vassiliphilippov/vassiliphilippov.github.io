allergy

noun
abnormal reaction to a substance
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈælərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈælədʒi / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the body reacts abnormally to a particular food, substance, or environmental factor, typically causing symptoms such as sneezing, rash, or difficulty breathing.

Examples

A pollen allergy
During spring, many people suffer from a pollen allergy that causes sneezing and itchy eyes.
My brother has a severe allergy to peanuts and must carry an epinephrine auto-injector.
She developed an allergy after being exposed to cat fur several times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypersensitivity - increased or abnormal sensitivity to a substance
  • antonym: tolerance - ability to endure a substance without ill effect
