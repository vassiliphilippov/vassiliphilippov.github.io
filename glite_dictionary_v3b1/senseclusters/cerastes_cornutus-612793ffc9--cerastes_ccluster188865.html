Cerastes cornutus

noun
horned viper species of northern Africa/SW Asia
Pronunciation: / səˈræstiːz kɔːrˈnuːtəs / (AmE), / səˈrɑːstiːz kɔːˈnjuːtəs / (BrE)
A highly venomous viper native to northern Africa and southwestern Asia, distinguished by a horny spine above each eye.

Examples

The Cerastes cornutus viper
Researchers observed a Cerastes cornutus moving silently across the dunes at dusk.
The Cerastes cornutus is adapted to desert environments, making it difficult to spot in the sand.
Venom from the Cerastes cornutus is considered dangerous and can be fatal to humans.

Related Terms
