light-fingered

adjective
quick or deft, especially at stealing
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌfɪŋɡərd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌfɪŋɡəd / (BrE)
skilled at stealing, especially in a quick and unobtrusive way; having nimble fingers, often used figuratively for deftness or dexterity

Examples

A light-fingered thief
He proved light-fingered when handling the delicate antique watch, impressing the appraiser.
Some tourists fell victim to light-fingered thieves near the popular landmark.
The police arrested a light-fingered pickpocket in the crowded market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honest - not disposed to steal or be deceitful
  • synonym: nimble-fingered - having quick and skillful fingers
