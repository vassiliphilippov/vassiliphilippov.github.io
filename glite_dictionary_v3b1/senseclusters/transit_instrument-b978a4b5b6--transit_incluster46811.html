transit instrument

noun
telescope for timing celestial transits
7.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.zɪt ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt ˈɪn.strə.mənt / (BrE)
a telescope mounted on an axis aligned east–west, used to observe and record the exact time when a celestial body crosses the meridian

Examples

A transit instrument telescope
Astronomers relied on the transit instrument to determine the exact moment a star crossed the meridian.
Calibration of the transit instrument is essential for accurate astronomical observations.
The old observatory still houses a nineteenth-century transit instrument used for precise timekeeping.
