common comfrey

noun
a European herb with clusters of small flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈkʌmfri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkʌmfri / (BrE)
a perennial European herb (Symphytum officinale) with small white, pink, or purple flowers, often naturalized as a weed in North America

Examples

Common comfrey flowers
Common comfrey produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers each spring.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control the spread of common comfrey in their yards.
The leaves of common comfrey have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
