feudal system

noun
social structure of medieval Europe under feudalism
6.0 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːdəl ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdəl ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
the social and economic structure of medieval Europe, where land was held by lords who protected vassals in exchange for service and loyalty, especially in war

Examples

The feudal system in Europe
Historians debate how rigid the feudal system actually was across different regions.
The feudal system shaped much of European society during the Middle Ages.
Under the feudal system, peasants worked the land in exchange for protection from local lords.
