normativeness

noun
quality of setting norms
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrmətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of establishing, relating to, or prescribing standards or norms

Examples

Normativeness of rules
The normativeness of social rules influences how people behave in different cultures.
Some critics questioned the normativeness of the new educational guidelines.
Debates about the normativeness of laws are common in legal philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or favoring any side or standard
  • synonym: normativity - the state of being based on norms or standards
