nonwaiver

noun
retention of rights
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈweɪvər / (AmE), / nɒnˈweɪvə / (BrE)
a situation or document stating that a right or claim is not given up or relinquished

Examples

Nonwaiver provision
The agreement contained a nonwaiver provision to ensure all parties retained their legal rights.
A nonwaiver letter was sent to clarify that accepting payment did not forfeit future claims.
Lawyers often recommend including a nonwaiver clause in important contracts.
