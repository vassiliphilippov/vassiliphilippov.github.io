underdose

verb
to give too little
7.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈdoʊs/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər.doʊs/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈdəʊs/ /; / /ˈʌn.də.dəʊs/ (BrE)
to give or take less than the recommended or effective amount of a drug or treatment

Examples

To underdose a patient
The doctor warned that it is dangerous to underdose antibiotics, as it may lead to resistance.
Some patients accidentally underdose themselves by misunderstanding the prescription instructions.
Hospitals must ensure they do not underdose critical medications during treatment.
If you underdose the medication, it may not be effective in treating your symptoms.
Doctors warn not to underdose antibiotics, as this can lead to resistance.
She accidentally underdosed her child by misreading the instructions on the bottle.
If you underdose on antibiotics, the infection may not be fully treated.
Doctors warn patients not to underdose because it can reduce the effectiveness of the medication.
Some people accidentally underdose when they forget to take all their prescribed pills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dose - to give or take a measured amount of a drug
  • antonym: overdose - to take or give more than the recommended amount of a drug
