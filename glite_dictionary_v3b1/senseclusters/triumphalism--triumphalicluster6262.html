triumphalism

noun
excessive pride in victory
8.5
Pronunciation: / traɪˈʌmfəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmfəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an attitude or belief of excessive pride and superiority after a victory or achievement

Examples

Triumphalism after victory
The politician's speech was filled with triumphalism after the election results were announced.
Critics warned that triumphalism could lead to complacency and future mistakes.
After the historic win, there was a mood of triumphalism among the supporters.
The politician's triumphalism after the election victory alienated many former supporters.
Religious triumphalism can lead to intolerance of other beliefs and cultures.
Critics accused the company of triumphalism when it boasted about dominating the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrogance - an attitude of superiority and self-importance
  • antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance
