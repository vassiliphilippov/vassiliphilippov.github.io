uncorrupt

adjective
not decayed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
not decayed, decomposed, or spoiled; preserved in an unaltered or pure state.

Examples

Uncorrupt remains
The ancient manuscript was found uncorrupt after centuries hidden in the monastery.
Despite the harsh climate, the fruit remained uncorrupt for weeks.
Historians marveled at the uncorrupt condition of the medieval relic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decayed - having undergone decomposition or rot
  • synonym: incorrupt - not subject to decay or corruption
