party

noun
person or group in a legal case
7.3 18/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ërti / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀêti / (BrE)
a person or group involved in a legal proceeding as a participant, such as a plaintiff or defendant

Examples

The party in court
Each party must submit evidence to support their case.
The party of the second part agrees to the conditions stated above.
The contract was signed by both parties involved in the dispute.

Synonyms

  • party to the action
    7.9 0.04/m
    party to the action is a formal legal phrase for someone directly involved in proceedings, while party is the general term.
  • litigant
    7.7 1.49/m
    litigant refers to someone involved in a lawsuit as plaintiff or defendant, while party is a broader legal participant term.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: defendant - the party against whom a lawsuit is filed
  • synonym: litigant - a person involved in a lawsuit
  • degree lower: plaintiff - the party who initiates a lawsuit
