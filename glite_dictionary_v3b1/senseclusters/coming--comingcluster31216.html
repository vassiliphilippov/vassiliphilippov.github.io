Synonyms

  • upcoming
    1.4 12/m
    upcoming is used for scheduled events like concerts or meetings, while coming is a more general word for things expected soon.
  • forthcoming
    4.2 2/m
    forthcoming is more formal and often used in announcements or books, while coming is everyday and common in speech.
  • approaching
    2.3 0.2/m
    approaching suggests something is drawing physically or in time toward you, while coming simply means it is expected to happen soon.
  • imminent
    4.8 8/m
    imminent means it will happen very soon, often at once, while coming is a softer, more general idea of near time.
  • in the offing
    5.6 0.4/m
    in the offing means something is likely to happen and sounds idiomatic, while coming is a simple adjective for things expected soon.
  • impending
    4.0 3/m
    impending often suggests an unpleasant or threatening event is near, while coming is neutral and does not imply danger.
