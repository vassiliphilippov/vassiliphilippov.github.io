vertical combination

noun
integration of supply chain companies into one firm
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtɪkəl ˌkɑmbəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the merging or integration of companies that handle different stages of production and distribution of a product within one firm

Examples

A vertical combination of companies
A vertical combination can reduce costs and improve efficiency across the supply chain.
The steel company undertook a vertical combination by acquiring coal mines and shipping lines.
Through vertical combination, the corporation controlled every step from growing raw materials to selling finished goods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horizontal combination - merging of companies at the same stage of production
