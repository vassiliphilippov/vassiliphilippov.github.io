scorn

verb
reject with contempt or disdain
5.5 3.12/m
Pronunciation: / sk…îrn / (AmE), / sk…îÀên / (BrE)
to show or feel contempt or disdain for someone or something; to reject as unworthy

Examples

To scorn an offer
After much thought, he scorned the idea of collaboration.
She scorned the offer of help and insisted on managing alone.
The board scorned his proposal, calling it impractical.
He scorns anyone who doesn't share his political views.
She scorned the idea of buying secondhand clothes, believing only in new fashion.
The critics scorned his latest book as unoriginal and dull.

Synonyms

  • despise
    4.9 11/m
    despise means to feel strong dislike and disrespect, while scorn means to show contempt and reject as unworthy.
  • disdain
    6.4 1.79/m
    disdain is a formal feeling of treating someone as unworthy, while scorn is open contempt that may lead to rejection.
  • hold in contempt
    7.8 0.80/m
    hold in contempt is a phrase for regarding someone with strong disrespect, while scorn often means active rejection as unworthy.
  • spurn
    7.7 2.13/m
    spurn means to reject or turn someone away in a cold, active way, while scorn focuses on feeling or showing contempt.
  • deride
    7.8 2.42/m
    deride means to mock or laugh at someone to show contempt, while scorn can be a quieter feeling or open rejection.
  • scoff
    6.5 4.09/m
    scoff is to laugh or speak in a mocking way at someone, while scorn can be deeper disdain or formal rejection.
