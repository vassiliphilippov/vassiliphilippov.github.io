scorn

verb
reject with contempt or disdain
3.4 4.4
Pronunciation: /skɔːrn/ (AmE), /skɔːn/ (BrE)
to show or feel contempt or disdain for someone or something; to reject as unworthy

Examples

To scorn an offer
After much thought, he scorned the idea of collaboration.
She scorned the offer of help and insisted on managing alone.
The board scorned his proposal, calling it impractical.
He scorns anyone who doesn't share his political views.
She scorned the idea of buying secondhand clothes, believing only in new fashion.
The critics scorned his latest book as unoriginal and dull.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to receive or agree to something offered
  • antonym: admire - to regard someone or something with respect or approval
  • synonym: despise - to feel a strong dislike or lack of respect for someone or something
  • synonym: spurn - to reject with disdain or contempt
