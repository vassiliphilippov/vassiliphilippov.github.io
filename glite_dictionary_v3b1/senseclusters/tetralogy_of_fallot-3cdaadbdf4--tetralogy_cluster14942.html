tetralogy of Fallot

noun
congenital heart defect with four abnormalities
7.3
Pronunciation: /te.ˈtræl.ə.dʒiː əv ˈfæl.oʊ/ (AmE), /te.ˈtræl.ə.dʒiː əv ˈfæl.əʊ/ (BrE)
A congenital heart defect presenting four key abnormalities: pulmonary stenosis, ventricular septal defect, malposition of the aorta, and right ventricular hypertrophy.

Examples

Tetralogy of Fallot heart defect
A heart murmur can be a sign of tetralogy of Fallot in infants.
Children with tetralogy of Fallot often require surgery early in life.
The surgeon discussed treatment options for the baby's tetralogy of Fallot.
