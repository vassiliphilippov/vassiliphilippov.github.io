Abila

proper noun
promontory in Morocco, Pillar of Hercules
Pronunciation: / əˈbiːlə / (AmE), / əˈbiːlə / (BrE)
A promontory in northern Morocco, situated opposite the Rock of Gibraltar, and known as one of the Pillars of Hercules in classical mythology.

Examples

Abila promontory Morocco
From the shore, Abila is visible as a dramatic outcrop across the strait.
Sailors once considered Abila the gateway between the Mediterranean and the Atlantic Ocean.
The ancient city was believed to lie near Abila, one of the legendary Pillars of Hercules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Rock of Gibraltar - limestone promontory opposite Abila, forming the other Pillar of Hercules
