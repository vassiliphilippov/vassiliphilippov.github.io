thripid

noun
small plant-feeding insect of the family Thripidae
7.8 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɪpɪd / (AmE), / ˈθrɪpɪd / (BrE)
Any of various small or minute insects of the family Thripidae, which have narrow, feathery wings and feed on plant sap, often causing damage to crops.

Examples

A thripid insect
A microscope is often needed to properly identify a thripid infestation on crops.
Gardeners often struggle to control thripids because they are so small and multiply quickly.
The greenhouse was infested with thripids that damaged the tomato plants.
