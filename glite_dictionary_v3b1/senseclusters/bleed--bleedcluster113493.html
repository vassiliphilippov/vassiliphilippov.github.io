bleed

verb
draw blood (formerly as treatment)
6.7 1.0
Pronunciation: /bliːd/ (AmE), /bliːd/ (BrE)
to lose blood from the body, either because of an injury, illness, or as part of a medical procedure

Examples

To bleed from a cut
In the 18th century, doctors would often bleed patients to treat fevers.
Many believed that to bleed someone could balance their bodily humors.
The surgeon decided not to bleed the patient, arguing it was an outdated practice.
He began to bleed after accidentally cutting his finger with a knife.
If your nose starts to bleed, lean forward to prevent swallowing blood.
The patient continued to bleed during the surgery, requiring immediate attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloodlet - to remove blood from a person as a treatment
  • synonym: hemorrhage - to bleed, especially heavily or internally
