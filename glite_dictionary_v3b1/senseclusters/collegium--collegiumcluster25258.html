collegium

noun
Roman association
Pronunciation: / kəˈliːdʒiəm / (AmE), / kəˈlɛdʒiəm / (BrE)
a legally recognized association or society in ancient Rome, such as a guild, trade group, or burial club

Examples

Roman collegium guild
In ancient Rome, a collegium could manage religious ceremonies or provide support for its members.
The potters joined a collegium to protect their trade interests and organize communal events.
Membership in a collegium often offered social and financial benefits to Roman citizens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guild - an association of people with similar interests or professions
  • synonym: society - an organized group of people with a common purpose
