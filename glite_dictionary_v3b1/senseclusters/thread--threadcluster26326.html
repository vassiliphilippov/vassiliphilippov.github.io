thread

noun
thin line or strand-like object
Pronunciation: / θrɛd / (AmE), / θrɛd / (BrE)
a long, thin line or object that resembles a strand; often used figuratively to describe things such as smoke, light, or roads

Examples

A thread of smoke
A thread of smoke curled up from the chimney into the cold morning air.
A faint thread of light shone through the crack in the door.
From above, the river looked like a silver thread winding through the valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strand - a single thin length of something, especially hair, thread, or wire
