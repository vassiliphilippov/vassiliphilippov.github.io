counterprotest

verb
to protest in opposition
5.8
Pronunciation: /kaʊn.tər.prə.ˈtest/ (AmE), /kaʊn.tə.prə.ˈtest/ (BrE)
to organize or take part in a protest that opposes or responds to another protest.

Examples

To counterprotest a rally
A group of students decided to counterprotest the demonstration held in front of the city hall.
Several organizations plan to counterprotest the march scheduled for next weekend.
Police were present to ensure safety as people began to counterprotest the controversial event.

  • antonym: support - to agree with or back a cause or action
