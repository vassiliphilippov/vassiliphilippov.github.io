startup

noun
newly formed company, often tech
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrtʌp / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtʌp / (BrE)
a newly created company, especially in technology or high-growth industries

Examples

A tech startup
The local startup attracted investors with its innovative app and scalable revenue model.
After the accelerator program, our startup doubled its team and launched a paid beta.
Many founders dream of turning a small idea into a successful startup in a few years.
The startup secured its first round of funding and hired five new engineers.
Investors are excited about the startup's innovative app for teaching children to code.
Many startups fail within their first three years, but a few become household names.

Related Terms

  • antonym: established company - a business that has been operating for a long time and is no longer in its early stage
  • synonym: new business - a company or commercial enterprise that has been recently established
  • synonym: venture - a business enterprise that involves risk and initiative; often used for new businesses
