coarse-grained sand

noun
sand with large, rough grains
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrs ɡreɪnd sænd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːs ɡreɪnd sand / (BrE)
sand composed of relatively large and rough grains, often used for construction, gardening, or specialized sports surfaces

Examples

Coarse-grained sand texture
Gardeners often prefer coarse-grained sand to improve drainage in soil.
Playgrounds use coarse-grained sand for safer and more durable surfaces.
The builders requested coarse-grained sand for mixing with concrete.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fine sand - sand with much smaller, smoother grains
