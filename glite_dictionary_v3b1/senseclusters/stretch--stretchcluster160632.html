stretch

verb
become longer by being pulled
Pronunciation: / stɹɛtʃ /; / strɛtʃ / (AmE), / stɹɛtʃ /; / strɛtʃ / (BrE)
to make something longer or wider by pulling or extending it; to become longer or wider

Examples

To stretch a rope
If you pull gently, the elastic will stretch several inches.
The dough needs time to stretch before it can be shaped.
These jeans stretch after a few hours of wear.
Don't stretch the fabric too much or it might tear.
She stretched the plastic wrap tightly over the bowl to cover it.
The metal wires were stretched between two poles to support the fence.
He gently stretched the canvas over the frame before painting.
She tried to stretch the sweater so it would fit better.
The rubber band can stretch up to twice its original length.
