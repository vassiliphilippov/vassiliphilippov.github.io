interstitial plasma cell pneumonia

noun
form of pneumonia in immunocompromised patients
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈstɪʃəl ˈplæzmə sɛl nuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl ˈplæzmə sɛl njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A form of pneumonia, especially affecting infants or people with weakened immune systems, characterized by inflammation of the lung's interstitial tissue and the presence of plasma cells.

Examples

Interstitial plasma cell pneumonia in infants
Interstitial plasma cell pneumonia is a significant risk for infants born prematurely.
Doctors monitored the AIDS patient for signs of interstitial plasma cell pneumonia due to his compromised immune system.
Treatment of interstitial plasma cell pneumonia often requires antiviral medication and immune support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pneumonia - inflammation of the lungs, usually due to infection
