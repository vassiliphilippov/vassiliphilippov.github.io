cystoid macular edema

noun
swelling of the central retina
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstɔɪd ˈmækjələr ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / ˈsɪstɔɪd ˈmækjʊlə ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by swelling and fluid-filled cysts in the central part of the retina (macula), leading to visual impairment

Examples

Cystoid macular edema swelling
If left untreated, cystoid macular edema can lead to permanent loss of central vision.
The patient developed cystoid macular edema following cataract surgery, which affected his vision.
Treatment for cystoid macular edema often includes anti-inflammatory eye drops or injections.
