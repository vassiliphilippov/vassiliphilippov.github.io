lighterage

noun
fee for shipping goods in lighters
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process or cost of transporting goods by lighter (a type of flat-bottomed barge)

Examples

lighterage costs
Due to the use of lighters in the harbor, their lighterage costs increased last year.
Negotiating lower lighterage fees helped the exporters save money on transportation.
The shipping company added lighterage to the final invoice for delivering the cargo to the shore.
Due to the shallow harbor, lighterage was necessary to move goods to the main dock.
In the 19th century, many ports relied heavily on lighterage to facilitate trade.
The port charged a fee for lighterage when the cargo had to be transferred from the ship to shore.
