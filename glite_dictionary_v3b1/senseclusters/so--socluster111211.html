so

adverb
to a very great extent or degree
1.0
Pronunciation: / soʊ / (AmE), / səʊ / (BrE)
to a great or certain extent; very; used to show how much or how far

Examples

So tired
She had never felt so nervous before an interview.
The movie was so good that I watched it twice in one week.
The weather is so hot today that I can't stand being outside.
I can only go so far on this issue before I need more information.
There are only so many hours in the day to finish your work.
You can push the team so much before they start to feel stressed.
She was so nervous that she forgot her lines on stage.
The soup was so hot that I had to wait before eating it.
They were so excited about the trip that they couldn't sleep.
