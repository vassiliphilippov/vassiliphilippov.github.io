vocality

noun
voice quality or use
Pronunciation: / voʊˈkæləti / (AmE), / vəʊˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or use of the voice, especially in speaking or singing

Examples

Her vocality in singing
Her impressive vocality made her a standout in the choir.
The teacher praised the student's vocality during the class presentation.
Opera singers are often admired for the strength and clarity of their vocality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: silence - the absence of sound or voice
  • synonym: vocalism - the use or quality of the voice in speech or singing
