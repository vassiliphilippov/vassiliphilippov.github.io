Dalbergia nigra

noun
Brazilian tree valued for hard, dark wood
Pronunciation: / dalˈbɜr.dʒi.ə ˈnaɪ.ɡrə / (AmE), / dælˈbɜː.dʒi.ə ˈnɪ.ɡrə / (BrE)
a species of Brazilian tree valued for its hard, dark, and richly streaked wood, commonly known as Brazilian rosewood

Examples

Dalbergia nigra wood
Many luxury guitars use wood from Dalbergia nigra for its rich tonal quality.
Overharvesting has made Dalbergia nigra a protected species under international law.
The furniture was crafted from rare Dalbergia nigra, prized for its striking grain and durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Brazilian rosewood - another name for Dalbergia nigra, especially in woodworking and trade
