Feast of Tabernacles

noun
Jewish festival commemorating Israelites' shelter in the wilderness
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːst əv ˈtæbɚˌnækəlz / (AmE), / ˈfiːst əv ˈtæbənæklz / (BrE)
a major Jewish festival (also called Sukkot) beginning on the eve of the 15th of Tishri, commemorating the shelter of the Israelites during their 40 years in the wilderness

Examples

Feast of Tabernacles festival
During the Feast of Tabernacles, families build temporary shelters called sukkahs.
Many communities hold special services at the synagogue for the Feast of Tabernacles each autumn.
The Feast of Tabernacles is celebrated with prayers and festive meals in many Jewish homes.
