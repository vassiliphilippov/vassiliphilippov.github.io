vinifera

noun
European grape species for wines and table grapes
Pronunciation: / vɪˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / vɪˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
A common species of European grape (Vitis vinifera) cultivated worldwide for use in wine and as table grapes.

Examples

Vinifera grape species
Most of the world's wines are made from the vinifera grape species.
The vineyard specializes in cultivating high-quality vinifera for both red and white wines.
Unlike native American grape species, vinifera produces the flavors characteristic of classic European wines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grape - a berry, typically green or purple, used for eating or making wine
