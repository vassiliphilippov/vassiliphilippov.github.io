absolve

verb
declare free from blame or responsibility
8.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈzɑːlv/ /; / /æb.ˈzɑːlv/ (AmE), /əb.ˈzɒlv/ (BrE)
to officially declare someone free from blame, responsibility, or guilt (including in religious contexts)

Examples

To absolve guilt
The committee voted to absolve the chairman from any wrongdoing.
The priest promised to absolve him of his sins.
You cannot simply absolve yourself of responsibility for the damage caused.
He hoped that apologizing would absolve him from any blame.
The church has the authority to absolve those who truly repent.
The priest promised to absolve her of all her sins if she confessed sincerely.
