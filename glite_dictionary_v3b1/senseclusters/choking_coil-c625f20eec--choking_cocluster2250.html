choking coil

noun
coil that blocks AC but allows DC in circuits
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃoʊkɪŋ kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkɪŋ kɔɪl / (BrE)
an electrical coil with low resistance and high inductance designed to allow direct current to pass while limiting or attenuating alternating current

Examples

A choking coil filter
A choking coil is often used in radio receivers to filter out alternating current signals.
Power supplies typically employ a choking coil to smooth the flow of direct current.
The engineer added a choking coil to the circuit to reduce unwanted interference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inductor - a component that stores energy in a magnetic field, used in electrical circuits
