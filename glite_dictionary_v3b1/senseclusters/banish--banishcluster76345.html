Examples

To banish someone forever
She was banished from the group for spreading false rumors.
The club decided to banish him after repeated violations of the rules.
They threatened to banish anyone who refused to cooperate with the authorities.
After the trial, the judge decided to banish her from the city for five years.
Many were banished to distant lands as punishment for their crimes.
The dictator threatened to banish anyone who opposed his rule.
After the coup, the new government decided to banish all former leaders from the country.

Legend says the witch was banished to a remote island for her crimes.

Synonyms

  • expel
    5.0 7.71/m
    expel forces someone out of an organization or school, while banish usually forbids them from returning to a community or place.
  • exile
    5.6 2.64/m
    exile forces a person from their country for political reasons, while banish covers removal from any place and forbids return.
  • cast out
    6.2 1.41/m
    cast out pushes someone away from a group in a harsh, social way, while banish is an official forced removal that bars return.
  • ban
    1.4 27/m
    ban officially forbids entry or use by law or rule, while banish forces a person to leave and not come back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exile - to force someone to live away from their home country, often as punishment
  • synonym: expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
  • synonym: ostracize - to exclude someone from a group or society
  • antonym: welcome - to accept or receive someone gladly into a group or place
