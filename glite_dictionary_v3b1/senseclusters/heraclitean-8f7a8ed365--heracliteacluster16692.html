Heraclitean

adjective
obscure or paradoxical
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛrəˈklaɪtiən / (AmE), / ˌhɛrəˈklaɪtiən / (BrE)
expressed in a style that is obscure, enigmatic, or paradoxical, making it difficult to understand

Examples

Heraclitean style
The philosopher's Heraclitean statements left the audience puzzled and intrigued.
Many readers find his poetry Heraclitean because of its paradoxical and mysterious nature.
Her argument was so Heraclitean that even experts struggled to interpret its meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • synonym: enigmatic - difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious
