play

noun
humorous or mocking speech; jest
3.8
Pronunciation: / pleɪ / (AmE), / pleɪ / (BrE)
humorous speech or mocking remarks made in a playful or joking manner, usually not intended to offend

Examples

Friendly play at work
During the meeting, their friendly play broke the tension and put everyone at ease.
His sharp play at dinner made everyone laugh, even the target of his jokes.
There was nothing malicious in her play; it was all in good fun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jest - a thing said or done for amusement; a joke
  • synonym: mockery - teasing or contemptuous language or behavior directed at a particular person or thing
  • antonym: seriousness - the absence of humor or jest; earnestness
