Giuseppe Guarneri

proper noun
Italian violin maker (c.1687–1745)
Pronunciation: / dʒuˈzɛpeɪ ɡwɑːrˈnɛri / (AmE), / dʒuːˈzɛpeɪ ɡwɑːˈnɛəri / (BrE)
(c. 1687–1745) An Italian violin maker, considered one of the greatest craftsmen of string instruments and grandson of Andrea Guarneri.

Examples

Giuseppe Guarneri violin
A Giuseppe Guarneri violin from the early 18th century fetched a record price at the auction.
Many leading soloists prefer instruments crafted by Giuseppe Guarneri over those by other makers.
The violin made by Giuseppe Guarneri is renowned for its rich, powerful tone.
