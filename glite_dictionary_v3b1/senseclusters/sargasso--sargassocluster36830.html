sargasso

noun
floating brown seaweed of the Atlantic
7.3
Pronunciation: / sɑːrˈɡæsoʊ / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡæsəʊ / (BrE)
A type of brown seaweed with rounded air bladders, often forming large floating masses in the tropical Atlantic Ocean, particularly the Sargasso Sea.

Examples

Sargasso seaweed
Sailors once feared getting trapped in thick beds of sargasso in the Atlantic.
Scientists studied the role that sargasso plays in the ocean ecosystem.
Vast mats of sargasso drift across the surface of the Sargasso Sea, providing habitat for marine life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sargassum - a genus of brown seaweed to which sargasso belongs
