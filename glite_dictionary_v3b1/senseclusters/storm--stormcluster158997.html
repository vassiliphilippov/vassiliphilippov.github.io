storm

noun
violent disturbance; intense commotion
3.7 9.01/m
Pronunciation: / st…îrm / (AmE), / st…îÀêm / (BrE)
a situation in which there is a violent disturbance or intense commotion, often involving emotional or social upheaval

Examples

A storm of anger
Her resignation caused a storm in the company.
The politician weathered a storm of criticism over his remarks.
There was a storm of protest after the announcement.

Synonyms

  • tempest
    7.4 0.62/m
    tempest is a strong, often literary word for violent turmoil, while storm describes any intense disturbance or social upheaval.
  • turbulence
    7.7 1.33/m
    turbulence is a formal word for disorder in politics or society, while storm suggests a violent, emotional upheaval.
  • uproar
    3.9 2.86/m
    uproar is noisy, confused public protest by a group, while storm often means a broader violent disturbance or emotional upheaval.
