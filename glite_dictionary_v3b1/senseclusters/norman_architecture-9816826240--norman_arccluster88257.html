Norman architecture

noun
Romanesque architecture from Normandy and Britain, 11th–12th centuries
3.1 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈnɔːr.mən ˈɑːr.kə.tek.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈnɔː.mən ˈɑː.kɪ.tek.tʃə/ (BrE)
A Romanesque style of architecture that originated in Normandy around 950 AD and was introduced to Britain after the Norman Conquest, characterized by massive masonry, rounded arches, and large towers, and used until the 12th century.

Examples

Norman architecture style
After 1066, many English castles were constructed in the Norman architecture style.
Durham Cathedral is a famous example of Norman architecture in England.
The thick walls and rounded arches are hallmarks of Norman architecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Romanesque - Architectural style of medieval Europe, predecessor to Gothic
