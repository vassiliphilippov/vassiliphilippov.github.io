fisherfolk

noun
people who fish
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃərˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃəfəʊk / (BrE)
people who catch fish, especially as their main job or traditional way of life

Examples

The fisherfolk catch fish
The fisherfolk set out before dawn to make the most of the calm sea.
Local fisherfolk rely on the river for their daily catch and income.
During the festival, the fisherfolk share stories about their experiences at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anglers - people who fish with a rod and line, often as a hobby
  • synonym: fishermen - people, especially men, who catch fish for a living
