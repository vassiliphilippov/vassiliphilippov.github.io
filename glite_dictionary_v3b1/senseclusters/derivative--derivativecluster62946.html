derivative

noun
word formed from another word
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɪv.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪv.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
A word that is formed from another word, usually by adding a prefix or suffix or through other morphological processes.

Examples

A derivative word
Many scientific terms are derivatives of Latin or Greek words.
The word ‘happiness’ is a derivative of ‘happy’.
‘Electricity’ is a derivative of ‘electric’.

  • synonym: derived - formed or developed from something else; not original (linguistic sense)
