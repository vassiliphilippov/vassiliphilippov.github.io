sharia law

noun
Islamic religious legal system
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈriːə lɔ / (AmE), / ʃəˈriːə lɔː / (BrE)
The system of law based on Islamic principles, derived from the Koran and the teachings and practices of Muhammad.

Examples

Sharia law principles
Debates about introducing elements of sharia law have arisen in some Western countries with significant Muslim populations.
Family matters such as marriage and inheritance are often governed by sharia law in Islamic communities.
Many countries in the Middle East implement sharia law to varying extents within their legal systems.
