multivariable

adjective
involving several variables
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈvɛriəbəl / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈvɛːrɪəbəl /; / ˌmʌltɪˈvɛəriəbəl / (BrE)
involving or relating to more than one variable, especially in mathematics or statistics

Examples

Multivariable analysis
The scientist used a multivariable approach to study the effects of diet and exercise.
A multivariable equation can be more complex than a single-variable one.
In statistics, multivariable analysis helps identify relationships between several factors.
The scientist used a multivariable approach to analyze the effects of diet and exercise on health.
A multivariable model can help identify which factors most influence the outcome.
Doctors often rely on multivariable analyses to understand complex medical data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multivariate - involving multiple dependent variables
  • antonym: univariable - involving only one variable
  • antonym: univariate - involving only one variable
