Legionella pneumophila

noun
bacterium causing Legionnaires' disease
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌliːdʒəˈnɛlə njuːˌmɒfɪˈlə / (AmE), / ˌliːdʒəˈnɛlə njuːˌmɒfɪˈlə / (BrE)
a species of motile, rod-shaped Gram-negative bacterium that thrives in water systems and is the main cause of Legionnaires' disease in humans

Examples

Legionella pneumophila infection
A sample from the patient's lungs confirmed an infection with Legionella pneumophila.
Cooling towers and plumbing networks can harbor Legionella pneumophila, posing a risk to public health.
The hospital took immediate action after Legionella pneumophila was detected in its water system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Legionnaires' disease - a severe form of pneumonia caused by Legionella bacteria
