provolone

noun
Italian aged cheese
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊvəˈloʊni / (AmE), / ˌprəʊvəˈləʊneɪ / (BrE)
a type of Italian cheese that is firm or semi-hard and can be mild or sharp in flavor, often used sliced or grated in dishes

Examples

Slices of provolone
She added slices of provolone to her sandwich for extra flavor.
The chef recommended using provolone when making baked pasta dishes.

She added sliced provolone to her sandwich for a creamy flavor.
The chef used melted provolone on top of the baked pasta dish.
At the deli, he ordered a sub with turkey and provolone cheese.

  • synonym: mozzarella - a soft, mild Italian cheese often used on pizza
