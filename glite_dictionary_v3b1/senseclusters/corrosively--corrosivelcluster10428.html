corrosively

adverb
in a damaging way
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊsɪvli / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊsɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that causes gradual destruction or damage, especially through chemical action

Examples

Corrosively eats metal
The cleaning solution acted corrosively on the old paint, stripping it away quickly.
Over time, salty air can affect metal surfaces corrosively near the ocean.
He spoke corrosively, his words slowly undermining her confidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gently - in a mild or non-damaging way
