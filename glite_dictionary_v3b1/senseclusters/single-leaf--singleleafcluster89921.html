single-leaf

noun
a pine tree with single needles; nut pine
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl liːf / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl liːf / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to the southwestern United States, having solitary needles and often many stems; valued for its edible nuts.

Examples

The single-leaf pine
Local people often harvest pine nuts from the single-leaf each fall.
The single-leaf is found in dry, rocky soils throughout Nevada and eastern California.
Unlike most pines, the single-leaf has only one needle per bundle.
