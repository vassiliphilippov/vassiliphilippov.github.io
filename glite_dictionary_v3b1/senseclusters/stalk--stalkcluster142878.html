stalk

verb
walk in a stiff or proud manner
7.3 4.8
Pronunciation: /stɔːk/ /; / /stɑːk/ (AmE), /stɔːk/ (BrE)
to walk in a proud, stiff, or angry manner, often deliberately and with long steps

Examples

To stalk angrily
After the argument, Emily stalked past her coworkers, trying not to make eye contact.
He stalked down the hallway, his back straight and his steps deliberate.
She stalked out of the room without saying a word, obviously furious.
