strain

verb
to deform by applying force
1.7
Pronunciation: / streɪn / (AmE), / streɪn / (BrE)
to alter the shape of something by applying force or stress

Examples

To strain a metal bar
Excessive weight can strain the frame of a bicycle and cause permanent deformation.
If you strain the plastic too much, it might crack or tear.
The heavy machinery strained the metal beams until they began to bend.
