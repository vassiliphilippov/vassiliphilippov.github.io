spasm

verb
to contract suddenly
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈspazəm /; / ˈspæzəm / (BrE)
to suddenly contract or twitch in a brief, involuntary way, especially of a muscle

Examples

Muscles spasm suddenly
After swimming in cold water, his calf began to spasm painfully.
If you do not stretch properly, your muscles might spasm during exercise.
Her eyelid would sometimes spasm when she was very tired.
After sitting for hours, his leg spasmed unexpectedly and made him jump.
The patient's eyelid spasmed several times during the examination.
She felt her hand spasm as she tried to grip the heavy bag.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relax - to become less tense or tight
  • synonym: twitch - to make a small, sudden movement
