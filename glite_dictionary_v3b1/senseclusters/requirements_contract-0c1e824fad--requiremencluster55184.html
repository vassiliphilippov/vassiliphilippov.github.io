requirements contract

noun
contract to buy all needs from one supplier
2.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkwaɪr.mənts ˈkɑːn.trækt/ /; / /rə.ˈkwaɪr.mənts ˈkɑːn.trækt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkwaɪ.ə.mənts ˈkɒn.trækt/ /; / /rə.ˈkwaɪ.ə.mənts ˈkɒn.trækt/ (BrE)
a contract in which a buyer agrees to purchase all of its requirements of a particular product or service from one supplier

Examples

A requirements contract agreement
A requirements contract ensures that the vendor will meet all the buyer's needs for that product.
The company signed a requirements contract to buy all of its steel from a single supplier.
Under the requirements contract, the manufacturer is obligated to supply whatever quantity the client demands.
