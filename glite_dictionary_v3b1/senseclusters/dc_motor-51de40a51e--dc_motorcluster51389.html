DC motor

noun
electric motor powered by direct current
Pronunciation: / diː siː ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / diː siː ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
An electric motor powered by direct current that converts electrical energy into mechanical motion.

Examples

A DC motor device
Many household appliances use a DC motor because it offers better speed control than an AC motor.
The engineer repaired the DC motor in the robotics lab, ensuring it would function smoothly for the next project.
When the battery voltage dropped, the DC motor began to slow down and eventually stopped.

Related Terms

  • antonym: AC motor - an electric motor powered by alternating current
  • synonym: electric motor - a motor powered by electricity
