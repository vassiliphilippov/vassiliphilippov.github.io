genie

noun
invisible spirit in Islamic folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːni / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːni / (BrE)
a magical spirit or being, often in stories or folklore, that can grant wishes or influence events, sometimes living in a lamp or bottle

Examples

A genie in a lamp
She dressed as a genie for the costume party, complete with a magic lamp.
Stories about a genie helping those who freed it are common in Middle Eastern folklore.
The old lamp was said to contain a powerful genie who could grant three wishes.
The genie appeared in a cloud of smoke when Aladdin rubbed the lamp.
She wished for three things after the genie was released from the bottle.
In many tales, a genie can grant any wish but often with unexpected results.
