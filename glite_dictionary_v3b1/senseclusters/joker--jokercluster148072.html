joker

noun
hidden clause altering a document's meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊkə / (BrE)
an inconspicuous clause or element in a document or bill that unexpectedly changes its meaning or effect.

Examples

A joker clause
Always read the fine print to avoid being caught by a joker in the agreement.
She thought the contract was straightforward until the joker was revealed in court.
The lawyer found a joker in the lease that increased the rent after six months.
