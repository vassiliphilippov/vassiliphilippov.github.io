interior monologue

noun
literary technique presenting a character's internal thoughts
4.5 6.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɪr.iː.ər ˈmɑː.nə.lɔːg/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtɪr.iː.ər ˈmɑː.nə.loʊg/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɪə.ri.ə ˈmɒn.ə.lɒɡ/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtɪə.ri.ə ˈmɒn.ə.ləʊg/ (BrE)
a literary technique in which a character's sequence of thoughts is presented as if spoken or directly experienced in their mind

Examples

Interior monologue technique
The novel uses interior monologue to reveal the protagonist’s doubts and fears.
Through interior monologue, readers gain insight into the character’s private emotions.
Writers often employ interior monologue to create a deeper psychological portrait.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stream of consciousness - a literary style that attempts to depict the flow of thoughts in a character's mind
