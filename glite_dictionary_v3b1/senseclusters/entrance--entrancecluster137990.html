entrance

verb
fill with wonder; put in a trance
6.4 5.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtræns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɑːns / (BrE)
to fill someone with wonder and delight; to captivate or enchant

Examples

To entrance the audience
She was so entranced by the music that she barely noticed the time passing.
The magician's performance seemed to entrance the entire audience, leaving them speechless.
The storyteller's voice could entrance even the most restless children.
Her voice was so beautiful that it seemed to entrance everyone who heard her sing.
The dancers entranced the crowd with their graceful movements and colorful costumes.
The magician's performance managed to entrance the entire audience for over an hour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or weary
  • synonym: enchant - to fill with great delight; charm
  • synonym: fascinate - to attract and hold the attention of someone deeply
  • synonym: hypnotize - to put someone into a hypnotic trance
