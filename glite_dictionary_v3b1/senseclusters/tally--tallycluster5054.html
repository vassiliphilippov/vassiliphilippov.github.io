tally

noun
bill or statement of account
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtæli / (AmE), / ˈtæli / (BrE)
a bill or account showing the amount owed for goods or services received, especially formerly or in less formal use

Examples

A tally for dinner
After dinner, the waiter brought a tally to the table for the group to settle.
In old country stores, customers often bought on credit and paid their tally later.
The shopkeeper handed him a tally for the month's groceries, to be paid at the end of the week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bill - a written statement of money owed for goods or services
  • synonym: invoice - a detailed list of goods sent or services provided with a statement of the sum due
