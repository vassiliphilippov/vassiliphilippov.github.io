nervus vagus

noun
tenth cranial nerve, also known as the vagus nerve
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs ˈveɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ˈveɪɡəs / (BrE)
the tenth cranial nerve, responsible for supplying the pharynx, larynx, lungs, heart, esophagus, stomach, and most abdominal organs

Examples

The nervus vagus nerve
Anatomy courses often cover the nervus vagus when discussing cranial nerves.
Damage to the nervus vagus can affect a wide range of bodily functions.
The surgeon identified the nervus vagus during the procedure to avoid accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vagus nerve - the tenth cranial nerve; supplies thoracic and abdominal organs
