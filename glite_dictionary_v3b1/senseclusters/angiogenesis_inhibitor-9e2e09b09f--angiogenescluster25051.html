angiogenesis inhibitor

noun
drug preventing blood vessel growth
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒi.oʊˈdʒɛnəs ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / ˌændʒi.əʊˈdʒɛnɪs ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a drug or substance that blocks the formation of new blood vessels, often used to prevent tumors from growing

Examples

Angiogenesis inhibitor drug
An angiogenesis inhibitor can prevent the formation of new blood vessels that feed a tumor.
Researchers are developing new angiogenesis inhibitors for cancer therapy.
The doctor prescribed an angiogenesis inhibitor to help slow the tumor's growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemotherapy - the use of drugs to treat cancer
