hemisphere

noun
half of the cerebrum in the brain
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛm.ɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.ɪ.sfɪə / (BrE)
either of the two halves of the cerebrum, the largest part of the brain, separated by a groove.

Examples

Left hemisphere of the brain
Damage to the left hemisphere of the brain can affect speech and language abilities.
Doctors studied how the two hemispheres communicate with each other.
The right hemisphere controls the muscles on the left side of the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cerebrum - the principal and most anterior part of the brain
