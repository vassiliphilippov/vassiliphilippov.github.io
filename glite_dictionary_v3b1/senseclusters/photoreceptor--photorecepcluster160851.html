photoreceptor

noun
light-detecting cell
6.5
Pronunciation: /foʊ.tə.rɪ.ˈsep.tər/ /; / /foʊ.toʊ.rɪ.ˈsep.tər/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.rɪ.ˈsep.tə/ (BrE)
a cell or structure in a living organism (especially in the eye) that detects and responds to light

Examples

Eye photoreceptor cell
The retina contains millions of photoreceptors that allow us to see in different lighting conditions.
Damage to photoreceptors can lead to vision problems or blindness.
Scientists study photoreceptors to better understand how animals perceive light and color.
Rods and cones are the main photoreceptors in the human eye.
A photoreceptor converts light into signals that the brain can interpret as images.
Damage to photoreceptors can lead to vision problems or blindness.
The human eye contains millions of photoreceptors that allow us to see in different lighting conditions.
Plants use photoreceptors to sense sunlight and adjust their growth direction.
Scientists study photoreceptors in algae to understand how they respond to changes in light.

  • synonym: photopigment - a pigment that absorbs light and initiates a biological process
  • synonym: rod - a type of photoreceptor cell in the retina sensitive to low light
