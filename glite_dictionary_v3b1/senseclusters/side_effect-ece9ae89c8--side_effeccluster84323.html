side effect

noun
unexpected or adverse effect of a drug or therapy
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪd ɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd ɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
an unintended and usually unwanted effect that happens in addition to the main effect of a drug or medical treatment

Examples

Drug side effects
It's important to discuss possible side effects with your doctor before starting any new drug.
She stopped taking the medication because the side effects were too severe.
Some painkillers may cause drowsiness as a side effect.
One common side effect of chemotherapy is hair loss.
Some people experience dizziness as a side effect of this drug.
The doctor warned me about the possible side effects of the new medication.
Reading the leaflet, she noticed that nausea was listed as a possible side effect.
Some patients experience headaches as a side effect of the treatment.
The doctor explained that drowsiness could be a side effect of the medication.

  • synonym: adverse reaction - a harmful or unpleasant response to a drug or medical procedure
