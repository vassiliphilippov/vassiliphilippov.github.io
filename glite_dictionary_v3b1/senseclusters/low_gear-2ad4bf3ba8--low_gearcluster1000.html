low gear

noun
lowest forward gear in a vehicle
4.5 2.7
Pronunciation: / loʊ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ləʊ ɡɪə / (BrE)
a gear position in a vehicle that gives more power and less speed, used for starting, climbing, or driving in difficult conditions

Examples

Starting in low gear
Starting in low gear helps the car move smoothly from a stop.
The truck remained in low gear while navigating the narrow, winding roads.
When driving up a steep hill, it's best to shift into low gear.
Descending the steep slope, the driver stayed in low gear to control the speed.
To climb the mountain road safely, he put the truck into low gear.
When approaching the muddy path, it's best to use low gear for better traction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high gear - the highest forward gear in a vehicle's gearbox
