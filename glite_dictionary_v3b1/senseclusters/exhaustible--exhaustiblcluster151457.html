exhaustible

adjective
able to be used up or depleted
6.1 5.3
Pronunciation: /ɪɡ.ˈzɑː.stɪ.bəl/ /; / /ɪɡ.ˈzɔː.stɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪɡ.ˈzɔː.stɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a resource or supply) able to be used up or depleted; not unlimited

Examples

Exhaustible natural resource
Oil is an exhaustible resource that may not last forever.
The team must manage the exhaustible funds carefully to sustain the project.
Unlike wind or solar power, coal is an exhaustible energy source.
The company is aware that its raw materials are exhaustible and is seeking alternative sources.
The supply of fossil fuels is exhaustible and will eventually run out if not managed responsibly.
Unlike solar energy, coal is an exhaustible resource that cannot be replenished once consumed.
