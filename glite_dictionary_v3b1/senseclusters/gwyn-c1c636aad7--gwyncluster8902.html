Gwyn

proper noun
Welsh underworld god
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡwɪn / (AmE), / ɡwɪn / (BrE)
In Welsh mythology, the god of the underworld and leader of the wild hunt.

Examples

Gwyn in mythology
As a powerful figure in Celtic tales, Gwyn is associated with both death and guidance.
In Welsh mythology, Gwyn is often depicted as the ruler of the otherworldly realm Annwn.
Legends say that Gwyn leads the wild hunt, a ghostly procession across the sky.
