sympatry

noun
coexistence of species in same area
9.3
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈpætri / (AmE), / sɪmˈpatri / (BrE)
the occurrence of two or more species or populations in the same geographic area without interbreeding

Examples

Sympatry of species
Hybrid zones can develop where sympatry leads to limited interbreeding between species.
Many bird species exhibit sympatry in tropical forests, sharing habitats but not interbreeding.
The study focused on the sympatry between two closely related frog populations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allopatry - occurrence of species in separate, non-overlapping geographic areas
