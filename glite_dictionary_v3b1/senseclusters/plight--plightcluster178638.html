plight

verb
to make a solemn promise
7.0
Pronunciation: /plaɪt/ (AmE), /plaɪt/ (BrE)
to make a serious promise or pledge, especially in the context of marriage or loyalty

Examples

To plight one's word
The couple plighted their troth in a traditional ceremony.
He plighted his loyalty to the king before the court.
They plight their faith to each other as a sign of commitment.
In the old tale, the knight plighted his loyalty to the queen before leaving for battle.
They chose to plight their troth in a quiet ceremony attended by close friends.
She plighted her word to always stand by his side, no matter the circumstances.

  • synonym: promise - to give your word to do something
