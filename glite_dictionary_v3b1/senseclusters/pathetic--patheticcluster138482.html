pathetic

adjective
arousing scornful pity
8.5
Pronunciation: / pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
arousing feelings of pity, often mixed with contempt, because of weakness, sadness, or inadequacy

Examples

A pathetic excuse
He offered a pathetic excuse that no one believed for a minute.
Her pathetic attempt to apologize only made things worse.
The team's pathetic performance disappointed even their most loyal fans.
His excuses for arriving late sounded pathetic and insincere.
Many found the broken toy lying by the roadside to be a pathetic sight.
Their attempt to climb the mountain was pathetic and ended after just an hour.
After the storm, their damaged house was in a truly pathetic state.
Her pathetic attempt to fix the car only made it worse.
The abandoned puppy looked so pathetic that I couldn't help but take it home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admirable - arousing respect or approval
  • antonym: admired - regarded with respect or warm approval (opposite of pathetic in some contexts)
  • synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt or scorn
  • synonym: pitiable - deserving or arousing pity
  • synonym: pitiful - arousing pity, especially in a way that evokes contempt
  • synonym: pity - the feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering of others
  • synonym: wretched - in a very unhappy or unfortunate state
