laughing gas

noun
nitrous oxide used as an anesthetic
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlæfɪŋ ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfɪŋ ˌɡæs / (BrE)
a colorless gas, nitrous oxide, used as an anesthetic, especially in dentistry, that produces mild euphoria and sometimes laughter when inhaled

Examples

Laughing gas anesthetic
During the operation, the surgeon used laughing gas as an anesthetic.
She started giggling uncontrollably after inhaling a small amount of laughing gas.
The dentist administered laughing gas to help calm the patient before the procedure.
