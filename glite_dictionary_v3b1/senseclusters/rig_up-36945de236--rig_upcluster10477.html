rig up

verb
assemble or construct temporarily
Pronunciation: / rɪɡ ʌp / (AmE), / rɪɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to assemble or construct something quickly, often as a temporary or makeshift solution

Examples

To rig up lights
Can you rig up some lights for the stage before the show starts?
The engineers had to rig up a temporary bridge after the flood.
We managed to rig up a tent using some rope and old sheets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: set up - to arrange or assemble something for use
  • antonym: take down - to dismantle or disassemble something
