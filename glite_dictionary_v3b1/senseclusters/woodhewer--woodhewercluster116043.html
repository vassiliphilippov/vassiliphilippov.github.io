woodhewer

noun
a tree-climbing bird of the Americas
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌhuːɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌhjuːə / (BrE)
A bird of Central or South America with a curved bill and stiff tail feathers, known for climbing tree trunks and feeding like a woodpecker.

Examples

A woodhewer bird
The Amazon rainforest is home to several species of woodhewer.
Unlike woodpeckers, the woodhewer uses its curved bill to probe for insects beneath bark.
We spotted a woodhewer climbing a tree during our birdwatching trip in Costa Rica.
