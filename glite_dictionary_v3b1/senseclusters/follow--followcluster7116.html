follow

verb
come or be next in order
4.7 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈfɒl.əʊ/ (BrE)
to come after something or someone in time, order, or position; to happen or appear after

Examples

To follow an act
After the opening act, the main band will follow on stage.
Darkness follows sunset as the day comes to an end.
The letter 'B' follows the letter 'A' in the alphabet.
After the CEO retired, his daughter followed him as head of the company.
Queen Elizabeth II followed her father, King George VI, to the throne.
Traditionally, the eldest son follows his parent as the family leader.
After her presentation, she followed it with a brief Q&A session.
It's common to follow a workout with stretching exercises.
The chef often follows a spicy dish with a cool dessert to balance the meal.

  • synonym: result - to occur as a result, to be a consequence
