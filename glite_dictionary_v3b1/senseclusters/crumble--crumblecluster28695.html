crumble

verb
to break or fall apart
1.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkrʌm.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈkrʌm.bəl/ (BrE)
to break or fall apart into small pieces, or to become weaker and collapse (can be used for objects or in a metaphorical sense)

Examples

To crumble under pressure
His resolve seemed to crumble under pressure from his opponents.
The ancient walls began to crumble after centuries of neglect.
The cookie will crumble if you handle it too roughly.
The old theatre began to crumble after years of neglect.
Their once strong partnership started to crumble as trust faded.
Without maintenance, the fortress walls would slowly crumble to dust.
If you squeeze the dry cake too hard, it will crumble easily.
She watched the paper crumble to dust in her hands.
The old wall began to crumble after years of neglect.
