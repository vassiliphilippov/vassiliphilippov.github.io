Synonyms

  • fall apart
    2.8 6.48/m
    fall apart is to break into loose pieces or stop working suddenly, while crumble suggests slowly breaking into small bits.
  • come apart
    3.1 1.90/m
    come apart means to separate into pieces or fall off, while crumble usually implies slowly turning into many small flakes.
  • break apart
    3.0 1.62/m
    break apart is to split into parts often by force, while crumble suggests gentle, brittle falling into many small pieces.
  • disintegrate
    5.8 3.13/m
    disintegrate means to fall into tiny parts or lose cohesion completely, while crumble often means a slower, crumbly fall into pieces.
  • collapse
    3.9 31/m
    collapse is to fall down or fail suddenly under pressure, while crumble implies breaking gradually into many small pieces.
  • break down
    3.2 8.68/m
    break down is to stop working or fail, often for machines or systems, while crumble refers to breaking into pieces.
