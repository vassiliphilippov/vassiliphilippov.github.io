above board

adjective
open and honest
Pronunciation: / əˈbʌv bɔrd / (AmE), / əˈbʌv bɔːd / (BrE)
open and honest; without deceit or concealment, especially in business or legal matters.

Examples

An above board deal
Make sure any agreement you make with the vendor is above board.
She insisted everything be above board so there would be no misunderstandings.
The company prides itself on keeping all financial activities above board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: transparent - open and clear, without secrets or hidden motives
  • antonym: underhanded - acting or done in a secret or dishonest way
