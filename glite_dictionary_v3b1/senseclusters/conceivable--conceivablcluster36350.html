conceivable

adjective
able to be imagined or believed
Pronunciation: / kənˈsiːvəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈsiːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be believed or imagined; possible to think of

Examples

Every conceivable precaution
Every conceivable precaution was taken before launching the experiment.
It is conceivable that life exists on other planets.
The story presents a conceivable explanation for the mysterious disappearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imaginable - able to be imagined; conceivable
  • antonym: inconceivable - impossible to imagine or believe
  • synonym: possible - able to be done, achieved, or exist
