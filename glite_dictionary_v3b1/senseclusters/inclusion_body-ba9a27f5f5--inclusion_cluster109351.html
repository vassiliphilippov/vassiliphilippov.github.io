inclusion body

noun
intracellular structure, often disease-related
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkluʒən ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a small structure found within the cytoplasm or nucleus of a cell, often characteristic of certain diseases or infections

Examples

Inclusion bodies in cells
Researchers are studying how inclusion bodies form in cells affected by genetic disorders.
The presence of a specific inclusion body can help pathologists diagnose certain viral infections.
Under microscopic examination, several inclusion bodies were observed in the infected tissue sample.
