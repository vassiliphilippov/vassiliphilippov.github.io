vain

adjective
excessively proud; conceited
6.0
Pronunciation: / veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
having or showing an excessively high opinion of one's appearance, abilities, or worth; conceited

Examples

A vain person
His vain attempts to impress everyone only made him seem arrogant.
Never be too vain to accept good advice from others.
She was so vain that she spent hours admiring herself in the mirror.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceited - having an excessively high opinion of oneself
  • antonym: modest - having a humble or moderate view of one's importance
