bonavist

noun
tropical vine grown for edible pods and seeds
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːnəˌvɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɒnəˌvɪst / (BrE)
A perennial twining vine (Lablab purpureus) of the Old World tropics, with trifoliate leaves, racemes of fragrant purple pea-like flowers, and maroon pods containing edible seeds; cultivated as an ornamental and vegetable, especially on the Indian subcontinent.

Examples

The bonavist vine
Horticulturists often recommend planting bonavist for its ornamental value and edible pods.
In some Indian recipes, the seeds of bonavist are used as a nutritious ingredient.
The bonavist produces striking purple flowers that brighten up the garden in summer.
