mother-of-pearl

noun
iridescent layer inside a mollusk shell
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌðər əv ˈpɝl / (AmE), / ˌmʌðər əv ˈpɜːl / (BrE)
a smooth, shiny substance with iridescent colors forming the inner layer of the shell of some mollusks, often used in jewelry and decoration

Examples

Mother-of-pearl inlay
Artisans often inlay mother-of-pearl into wooden boxes to create intricate patterns.
He admired the delicate mother-of-pearl surface inside the seashell.
The buttons on her blouse were made of mother-of-pearl, giving them a lovely shimmer.
