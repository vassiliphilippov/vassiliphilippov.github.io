mulga

noun
Australian hardwood
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌlɡə / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɡə / (BrE)
the hard, dense wood of the mulga tree, commonly used for fuel, fence posts, tools, and woodworking in Australia

Examples

Mulga wood carving
He carved a sturdy walking stick from a piece of fallen mulga he had collected.
The fence was built using strong mulga posts that would last for years.
Traditional tools made from mulga are valued for their durability in the Australian outback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acacia - a type of tree or shrub found in warm climates, including the mulga tree
