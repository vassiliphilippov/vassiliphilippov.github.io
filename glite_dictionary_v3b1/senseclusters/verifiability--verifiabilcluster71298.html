verifiability

noun
ability to be verified
6.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /vər.ɪ.faɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tɪ/ /; / /vər.ɪ.fɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /və.rɪ.faɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tɪ/ /; / /və.rɪ.fɪ.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be confirmed as true or accurate; the capability of being verified.

Examples

The verifiability of facts
Journalists must consider the verifiability of information before publishing a report.
The verifiability of the results is essential for the scientific community.
The theory has been criticized for its lack of verifiability.
