taqlid

noun
following religious authority
Pronunciation: / tɑːˈkliːd / (AmE), / tæˈkliːd / (BrE)
the practice of following traditional religious interpretations or authorities, especially in Islamic law

Examples

Religious taqlid
Many traditional scholars emphasize the importance of taqlid in religious practice.
Debates about taqlid and independent reasoning are common in Islamic jurisprudence.
Some communities encourage taqlid to preserve established interpretations of religious law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ijtihad - independent reasoning in Islamic law
