decretum

proper noun
Gratian's canon law text
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkriːtəm / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːtəm / (BrE)
the title of a 12th-century collection of canon law compiled by Gratian, which became the foundation of medieval church law

Examples

The Decretum of Gratian
Medieval scholars studied the decretum to understand the principles of church law.
The decretum by Gratian played a crucial role in shaping canon law in Europe.
Many legal historians reference the decretum when discussing the origins of ecclesiastical law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Corpus Juris Canonici - the body of canon law of the Catholic Church
