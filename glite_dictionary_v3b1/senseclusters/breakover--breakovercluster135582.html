breakover

noun
electrical conduction point
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbreɪ.k.oʊ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈbreɪ.k.əʊ.və/ (BrE)
the voltage or point at which an electrical device or circuit suddenly begins to conduct or experiences electrical breakdown

Examples

Breakover voltage

Engineers must know the breakover value to design reliable electronic components.
