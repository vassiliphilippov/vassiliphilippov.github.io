convey

verb

to communicate, express, or transmit information, ideas, or feelings from one person or place to another

Examples

Convey a message
Radio waves convey information over great distances.
The pipes convey water from the reservoir to the city.
Wires are used to convey electricity throughout the building.
A friendly smile can often convey more warmth than words.
His tone of voice conveyed deep sympathy for the grieving family.
The novel skillfully conveys a sense of hope and resilience.
He found it hard to convey his true feelings in words.
Please convey my congratulations to your team for their success.
The teacher did her best to convey the complex topic in simple terms.

Synonyms

  • communicate
    3.4 0.08/m
    communicate is used for sending information or feelings between people clearly, while convey is a more general word for expressing or transmitting ideas.
  • transmit
    3.7 12/m
    transmit is often used for sending signals, diseases, or data between places, while convey focuses on sharing ideas or feelings between people.
  • get across
    3.0 3.78/m
    get across means make an idea understood by listeners, while convey is a broader term for expressing or passing on information.
  • put across
    3.5 0.13/m
    put across means present an idea so others understand it, while convey has a wider sense of expressing or transmitting information.
  • impart
    4.3 2.66/m
    impart means give knowledge or skills to someone in a teaching way, while convey is more general communication of ideas or feelings.
  • relay
    3.5 4.99/m
    relay means receive and then pass a message along, while convey can mean directly express or transfer ideas without being an intermediary.
  • pass on
    2.7 6.49/m
    pass on means give or tell something to someone else, while convey covers any way of communicating or expressing information or feelings.
  • pass along
    2.5 3.06/m
    pass along means pass information through a chain of people, while convey is a general act of communicating or expressing ideas directly.
  • share
    1.0 43/m
    share means tell others your thoughts or feelings, often in a mutual way, while convey is a neutral, one-way act of communicating.
  • express
    2.3 112/m
    express means show emotions or ideas through words, art, or actions, while convey focuses on transmitting those ideas to someone else.
  • carry
    2.0 19/m
    carry can mean move sound, disease, or information physically from place to place, while convey is mainly about communicating ideas or feelings between people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transfer - to move from one place to another
