convey

verb
transmit or serve as medium for transmission
4.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈveɪ / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ / (BrE)
to communicate, express, or transmit information, ideas, or feelings from one person or place to another

Examples

Convey a message
Radio waves convey information over great distances.
The pipes convey water from the reservoir to the city.
Wires are used to convey electricity throughout the building.
A friendly smile can often convey more warmth than words.
His tone of voice conveyed deep sympathy for the grieving family.
The novel skillfully conveys a sense of hope and resilience.
He found it hard to convey his true feelings in words.
Please convey my congratulations to your team for their success.
The teacher did her best to convey the complex topic in simple terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communicate - to share or exchange information, news, or ideas
  • synonym: express - to put an idea or feeling into words or actions
  • synonym: transfer - to move from one place to another
  • synonym: transmit - to send or pass from one person or place to another
