disabling

adjective
making legally disqualified
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (BrE)
depriving someone of a legal right or making them legally disqualified to do something

Examples

Disabling law
His conviction created a disabling condition, barring him from public office.
The law imposed disabling restrictions on foreign investors.
The new policy introduced several disabling clauses that prevent certain groups from applying.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enabling - giving someone legal power or right; opposite of disabling in legal context
