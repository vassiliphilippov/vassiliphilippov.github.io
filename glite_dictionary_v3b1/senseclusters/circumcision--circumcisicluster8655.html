circumcision

noun
removal of the foreskin, often as a religious rite
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrkəmˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
the removal of the foreskin from the penis, usually for religious, cultural, or medical reasons

Examples

A circumcision ceremony
He attended his nephew's circumcision ceremony at the synagogue.
In many cultures, circumcision is performed as a religious or traditional practice shortly after birth.
The debate over the health benefits of circumcision continues among medical professionals.
Infant circumcision is a common practice in many cultures around the world.
Religious reasons often influence the decision to perform circumcision on newborn boys.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits associated with circumcision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncircumcised - not having undergone circumcision
