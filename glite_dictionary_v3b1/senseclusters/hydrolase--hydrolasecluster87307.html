hydrolase

noun
enzyme for hydrolysis
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəˌleɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that speeds up the chemical breakdown of a compound by adding water

Examples

A hydrolase enzyme
A hydrolase helps break down complex molecules in the body.
Scientists studied how the hydrolase acted on different types of sugars.
Without a specific hydrolase, the reaction would occur much more slowly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in living things
  • antonym: lyase - an enzyme that breaks chemical bonds by means other than hydrolysis or oxidation
