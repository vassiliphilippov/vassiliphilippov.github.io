moralism

noun
making judgments about others' morality
4.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːr.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈmɒr.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice or tendency of making or imposing moral judgments, often with criticism or a sense of superiority

Examples

Public moralism
Critics accused the author of slipping into moralism in his later works.
He grew tired of her constant moralism and judgmental remarks.
Many people resent unsolicited moralism from strangers about their lifestyle choices.
Critics accused the speech of excessive moralism, rather than offering practical solutions.
He disliked the preachy moralism sometimes found in children's stories.
The novel explores the moralism of its main character through her judgments of others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moralizing - expressing judgments about right and wrong in a disapproving way
  • synonym: self-righteousness - an unfounded confidence in one's own moral superiority
