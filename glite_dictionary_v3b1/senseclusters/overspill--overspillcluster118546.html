overspill

verb
to spill over
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈspɪl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɪl / (BrE)
to flow or spill over the edge of a container or limit, especially in excess.

Examples

To overspill the edge
Rainwater overspilled the gutters during the heavy storm last night.
If you pour too quickly, the juice might overspill the glass and make a mess.
The excitement in the stadium overspilled into the streets after the team's victory.
