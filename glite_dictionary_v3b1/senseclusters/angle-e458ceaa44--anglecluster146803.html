Angle

noun
member of a Germanic people in early medieval Britain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
a member of a Germanic people who settled in Britain in the 5th and 6th centuries and merged with the Saxons and Jutes to form the Anglo-Saxons.

Examples

The Angles tribe
Ancestors of the English language can be traced back to the Angles and related Germanic tribes.
Historians believe that the Angles played a major role in shaping early English culture.
The migration of the Angles to Britain marked the beginning of Anglo-Saxon history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Anglo-Saxon - a member of the combined peoples of Angles, Saxons, and Jutes in England
  • synonym: Jute - a member of the Germanic tribe who settled in parts of Britain
  • synonym: Saxon - a member of another Germanic people who settled in Britain
