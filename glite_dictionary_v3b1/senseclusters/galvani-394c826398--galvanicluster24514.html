Galvani

proper noun
Italian physiologist; pioneer of bioelectricity
9.5
Pronunciation: /gæl.ˈvɑː.niː/ /; / /gæl.ˈvæn.iː/ (AmE), /gæl.ˈvɑː.niː/ /; / /gæl.ˈvæn.iː/ (BrE)
Luigi Galvani (1737–1798), Italian physiologist renowned for discovering that the muscles of frogs contracted when exposed to an electric field, leading to the development of the galvanic cell.

Examples

Galvani muscle experiments
Many scientists admire Galvani for his pioneering research into bioelectricity.
Several terms in science, such as 'galvanic cell,' are named after Galvani.
The experiment by Galvani with frog legs led to significant advances in electrophysiology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Volta - Italian physicist and Galvani's contemporary; invented the electric battery
  • synonym: galvanism - The contraction of muscle stimulated by an electric current, as discovered by Galvani
