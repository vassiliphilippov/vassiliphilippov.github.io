provoked

adjective
made angry by incitement
3.3 3.2
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈvoʊkt/ (AmE), /prə.ˈvəʊkt/ (BrE)
Feeling or showing anger as a result of being deliberately incited or annoyed.

Examples

He was provoked to anger
He was so provoked that he raised his voice, which he rarely did.
She felt provoked by the constant criticism from her manager.
The provoked dog barked furiously at the children taunting it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or violence
  • degree higher: incensed - very angry, enraged
