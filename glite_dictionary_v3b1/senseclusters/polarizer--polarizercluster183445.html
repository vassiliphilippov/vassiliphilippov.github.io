polarizer

noun
one who divides opinions
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊləˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊləˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes strong division or extreme differences of opinion within a group

Examples

A polarizer of opinions
The controversial leader became a polarizer in the community, splitting people into opposing camps.
Social media can act as a polarizer, amplifying extreme views and deepening divisions.
Her remarks made her a polarizer among her colleagues, with some strongly supporting and others opposing her.
