process

verb
deal with systematically, often officially
3.0 28/m

to handle, treat, or transform something by carrying out a series of actions or steps (such as in administration, manufacturing, or data)

Examples

To process data
All passport renewals are processed by the government office downtown.
She is currently processing orders for the holiday rush.
The bank will process your application within three business days.
Engineers processed the metal to increase its strength and durability.
Some foods are heavily processed before they reach supermarket shelves.
The factory processes raw cotton into fine thread used for textiles.
Most store-bought orange juice is processed to improve its shelf life.
The company will process the raw materials before shipping them to customers.
The water must be processed before it is safe to drink.
