equability

noun
calm temperament
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌekwəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌekwəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being calm, even-tempered, and not easily disturbed or changed

Examples

Calm equability
Despite the stressful situation, his equability helped everyone stay focused.
The teacher's equability made her popular among students who appreciated her steady mood.
Maintaining equability is important for leaders during times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calmness - the state of being peaceful and not agitated
  • antonym: irritability - the tendency to become easily annoyed or upset
  • synonym: serenity - the state of being calm and untroubled
