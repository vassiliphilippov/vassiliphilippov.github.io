substrate

noun
linguistics: native language influencing a dominant language
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbstreɪt / (BrE)
(Linguistics) An indigenous language that contributes features to the language of an invading or dominant people, especially when the indigenous population adopts the conquerors' language.

Examples

Language substrate
In Morocco, Berber languages serve as a substrate for Moroccan Arabic.
Many local words in South American Spanish come from indigenous languages acting as a substrate.
The Celtic languages of Britain are considered a substrate for English.

Related Terms

  • antonym: superstrate - a dominant language that influences the language of a conquered or subordinate group
