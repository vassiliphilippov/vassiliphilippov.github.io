necessitation

noun
logical necessity rule
9.9
Pronunciation: / nəˌsɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / nɪˌsɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the logical rule or relation by which a proposition is shown to be necessary or to necessarily follow from another

Examples

Necessitation rule
The necessitation rule is fundamental in modal logic for establishing what must be true.
Philosophers debate whether necessitation applies to all logical systems or only certain ones.
By applying necessitation, the proof shows that the statement holds in every possible world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: necessity - the quality of being necessary or unavoidable
  • antonym: possibility - the quality of being possible but not certain or necessary
