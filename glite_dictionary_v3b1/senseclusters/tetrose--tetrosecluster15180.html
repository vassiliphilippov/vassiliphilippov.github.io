tetrose

noun
four-carbon monosaccharide sugar
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˈtɛt.rəʊs / (BrE)
a monosaccharide sugar containing four carbon atoms per molecule

Examples

A tetrose sugar
A tetrose is less common in nature than sugars with five or six carbon atoms.
Scientists identified a new type of tetrose during their carbohydrate studies.
The structure of a tetrose is simpler than that of a hexose.
