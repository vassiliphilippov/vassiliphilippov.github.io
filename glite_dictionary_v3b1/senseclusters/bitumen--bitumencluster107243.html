bitumen

noun
black, sticky hydrocarbon mixture
Pronunciation: / bəˈtjuːmən / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːmən / (BrE)
any of various black, sticky substances consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, found naturally or obtained as a residue from petroleum distillation, used especially in road surfacing and roofing.

Examples

Black bitumen layer
Large deposits of bitumen are found in the oil sands of Alberta, Canada.
The road construction crew used bitumen to create a smooth, durable surface.
Traditional roofing materials often included bitumen because of its waterproof properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphalt - a mixture of bitumen with other materials used for surfacing roads
  • synonym: tar - a dark, thick, flammable liquid distilled from wood or coal
