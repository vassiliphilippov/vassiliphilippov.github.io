decarbonization

noun
reducing carbon emissions
5.5
Pronunciation: / diˌkɑrbənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌkɑːbənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing or eliminating carbon emissions, especially from energy production or industry

Examples

Energy decarbonization
The government announced new policies to accelerate decarbonization in the transportation sector.
Many companies are investing in decarbonization technologies to meet environmental targets.
Achieving global decarbonization is essential for limiting the effects of climate change.
Many governments are setting ambitious decarbonization targets to fight climate change.
The energy sector is undergoing rapid decarbonization by switching to renewable sources.
Investing in decarbonization technologies can help companies meet environmental regulations.
