talk the talk

verb
speak convincingly or confidently
7.8
Pronunciation: / tɑk ðə tɑk / (AmE), / tɔːk ðə tɔːk / (BrE)
to speak convincingly or credibly, especially when suggesting expertise or confidence, often without necessarily performing the actions to match one's words

Examples

To talk the talk confidently
He certainly knows how to talk the talk, but his actions never live up to his promises.
If you want to earn respect here, you can't just talk the talk; you must prove yourself.
Many politicians talk the talk about reform, but few take real steps to make changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: walk the walk - to put words into action; to show ability or truth by actions
