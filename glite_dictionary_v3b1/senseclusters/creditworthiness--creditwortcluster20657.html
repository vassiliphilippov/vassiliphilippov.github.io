creditworthiness

noun
suitability for receiving credit or loans
5.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /kred.ɪt.ˈwɜːr.ðɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkred.ɪt.wɜːr.ðɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /kred.ɪt.ˈwɜː.ðɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkred.ɪt.wɜː.ðɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality of being trusted to repay money borrowed, based on a person's or entity's credit history and financial situation.

Examples

Good creditworthiness
A poor payment history can negatively affect your creditworthiness.
Lenders consider income, debts, and assets to determine an applicant's creditworthiness.
The bank will assess your creditworthiness before approving the loan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credibility - the quality of being trusted and believed in
  • antonym: unreliability - the quality of not being trusted or depended upon
