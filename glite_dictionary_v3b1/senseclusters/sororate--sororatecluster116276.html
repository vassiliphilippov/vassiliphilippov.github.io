sororate

noun
marriage to wife's sister
Pronunciation: / səˈrɔːrət / (AmE), / səˈrɔːrət / (BrE)
a custom or practice in which a man marries the sister of his deceased wife

Examples

The sororate custom
In some cultures, the sororate was practiced to maintain family alliances after a woman's death.
Anthropologists studied the sororate as part of traditional marriage customs in the region.
The sororate ensured that children remained within the same extended family after their mother's passing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: levirate - a custom where a widow marries her deceased husband's brother
