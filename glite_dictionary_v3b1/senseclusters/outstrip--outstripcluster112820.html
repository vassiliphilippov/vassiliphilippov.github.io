outstrip

verb
to surpass or go beyond
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈstrɪp / (AmE), / aʊtˈstrɪp / (BrE)
to move faster, do better, or become greater than someone or something; to surpass or exceed

Examples

To outstrip rivals
She trained hard and managed to outstrip all her rivals in the final race.
Technology companies continue to outstrip their competitors in terms of innovation.
The company's sales outstripped expectations this quarter, leading to a rise in its stock price.
Her achievements in science easily outstrip those of her peers.
Sales of electric cars continue to outstrip those of traditional vehicles in many countries.
The company's growth has outstripped initial expectations this quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exceed - to go beyond in quantity, degree, or value
  • antonym: fall behind - to fail to keep up with
  • synonym: outperform - to perform better than someone or something
  • synonym: surpass - to exceed or be greater than something or someone
