no-hit game

noun
baseball game with no hits allowed
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊ hɪt ˈɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ hɪt ˈɡeɪm / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher does not allow the opposing team any hits.

Examples

A no-hit game pitched
Fans erupted in applause as they realized they were witnessing a no-hit game.
She studied every no-hit game from the past decade to analyze pitching techniques.
The pitcher threw a no-hit game last night, making history for his team.
