supersocial

adjective
extremely social
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
extremely social or cooperative, especially describing organisms or people with highly developed social behavior

Examples

A supersocial animal
Some scientists describe ants as supersocial because of their complex colonies and teamwork.
Her supersocial nature made her popular at every event she attended.
Certain primates are considered supersocial due to their strong group cooperation and communication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antisocial - not wanting the company of others; avoiding social interaction
  • synonym: gregarious - enjoying the company of others; sociable
