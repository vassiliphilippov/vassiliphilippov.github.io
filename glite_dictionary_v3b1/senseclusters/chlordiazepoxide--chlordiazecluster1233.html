chlordiazepoxide

noun
tranquilizer drug for anxiety and alcoholism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌklɔːr.daɪˌæz.ɪˈpɑɪd / (AmE), / ˌklɔː.daɪ.ˌæz.ɪˈpɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
A benzodiazepine tranquilizer used to treat anxiety and withdrawal symptoms in alcoholism, known by the trade name Librium.

Examples

Chlordiazepoxide medicine
Chlordiazepoxide is often used in hospitals for short-term treatment of severe anxiety.
Patients taking chlordiazepoxide should be monitored for side effects like drowsiness.
The doctor prescribed chlordiazepoxide to help manage her withdrawal symptoms.
