outward-bound

adjective
going away from a place; departing
5.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆaÊŠtwÉšd ËŒbaÊŠnd / (AmE), / ËˆaÊŠtwÉ™d ËŒbaÊŠnd / (BrE)
(of a ship, train, etc.) going away from a particular place, especially a port or city, on the first stage of its journey.

Examples

An outward-bound ship
As the outward-bound train pulled away, she waved goodbye to her family.
Customs officers inspected all outward-bound cargo leaving the country.
The port was bustling with outward-bound ships ready to sail at dawn.

Synonyms

  • outward
    2.4 1.75/m
    outward means going away from a place at the start of a journey, while outward-bound labels vehicles on their first outward leg from port or city.
  • outbound
    3.5 0.47/m
    outbound describes travel directed away from the origin or centre for a trip, while outward-bound often refers to a vessel's first outward stage.
  • departing
    1.3 0.00/m
    departing means a person or vehicle is about to leave or has just left, while outward-bound stresses travel away on the first stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homeward-bound - heading toward home or returning
