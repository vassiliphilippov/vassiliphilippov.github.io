meanspiritedness

noun
unkind or petty nature
Pronunciation: / ˌmiːnˈspɪrɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌmiːnˈspɪrɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unkind, petty, or deliberately cruel to others

Examples

Meanspiritedness and cruelty
His meanspiritedness was obvious when he laughed at others' mistakes.
The team's progress was slowed by the meanspiritedness of a few members.
She regretted her meanspiritedness after realizing how much it hurt her friend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil or cause harm
