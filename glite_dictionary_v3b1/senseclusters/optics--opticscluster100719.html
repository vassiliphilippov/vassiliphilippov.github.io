optics

noun
public appearance or perception
7.0
Pronunciation: / c851d0pt6aks / (AmE), / c852pt6aks / (BrE)
the way an action, decision, or event appears to the public; the perceived public impression or appearance of something.

Examples

Political optics
Campaign advisers worried the optics of the candidate's remarks would harm turnout next month.
The company changed its plan because the optics of the proposed layoffs were politically damaging.
She managed the meeting carefully to improve the optics around the project's slow start.

Related Terms

  • synonym: public perception - the way something is viewed or understood by the general public
  • antonym: reality - the actual state of things as opposed to how they may appear
  • synonym: reputation - the general opinion that people have about someone or something
