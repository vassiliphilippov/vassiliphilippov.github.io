sanction

verb
to give official or religious approval
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
to give official or religious approval to something, especially through a formal ceremony or oath

Examples

To sanction an oath
It is tradition to sanction the agreement with a formal oath.
The priest sanctioned the union with a solemn prayer.
Their marriage was sanctioned by the church with a special ceremony.
