manness

noun
courage or bravery
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmæn.nəs / (BrE)
courage or bravery, especially the kind traditionally associated with men (archaic or literary)

Examples

Heroic manness
The hero's manness was praised in songs and stories for generations.
In the old tale, she displayed great manness by defending her village alone.
Writers of the past often admired manness as a noble quality.
