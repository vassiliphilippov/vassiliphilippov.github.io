ease

noun
freedom from financial difficulty
2.6 1.0
Pronunciation: /iːz/ /; / /ɪz/ (AmE), /iːz/ (BrE)
a state of comfort, relaxation, or freedom from difficulty, worry, or embarrassment

Examples

A life of ease
After years of hard work, he was finally able to live a life of ease.
She retired early and enjoyed her ease, traveling the world without concern for money.
Winning the lottery brought him a level of ease he had never experienced before.
After finishing the project, she felt a great sense of ease.
His mind was finally at ease once he resolved the misunderstanding.
You could see the ease in his posture after the stressful meeting ended.
He adapted to his new role with remarkable ease.
Modern technology allows for the ease of communication across the world.
She completed the puzzle with surprising ease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comfort - a state of physical or financial ease and security
  • antonym: difficulty - the state of being hard to do or understand

  • synonym: relaxation - the state of being free from tension or anxiety
  • synonym: relief - the feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress
  • synonym: rest - a period of relaxing or ceasing to work or exert oneself
