tercet

noun
three-line stanza
8.6
Pronunciation: / tərˈsɛt / (AmE), / təˈsɛt / (BrE)
a group of three lines in a poem, often forming a stanza and sometimes with a rhyme scheme

Examples

A tercet stanza
The poet used a tercet to give the stanza a unique rhythm.
Each tercet in the poem follows a strict rhyme scheme.
Dante's Divine Comedy is famous for its use of the tercet structure.
The poet ended each section with a tercet to emphasize the main idea.
A classic sonnet sometimes concludes with a tercet that summarizes the poem's message.
She admired how the tercet created a sense of rhythm and closure in the poem.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: couplet - a pair of lines of verse, usually rhymed
  • degree higher: quatrain - a stanza of four lines
  • synonym: stanza - a grouped set of lines in a poem
