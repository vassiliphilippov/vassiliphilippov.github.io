regurgitation

noun
ejecting stomach contents through the mouth
8.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˌɡɝːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌɡɜːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing swallowed food or liquid back up from the stomach and out through the mouth, often involuntarily

Examples

Acid regurgitation
Chronic acid regurgitation can be a symptom of gastroesophageal reflux disease.
Some birds feed their chicks by regurgitation of partially digested food.
The patient experienced sudden regurgitation after eating a large meal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vomiting - forcing the contents of the stomach out through the mouth; more forceful than regurgitation
