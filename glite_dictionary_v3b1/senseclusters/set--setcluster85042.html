set

verb
to become firm or gelatinous
3.8 9.08/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ›t / (AmE), / sÉ›t / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to become firm or solid, especially by cooling or chemical reaction, often forming a jelly-like consistency.

Examples

Jelly sets overnight
As the pudding cooled, it began to set and thicken.
Leave the jelly in the fridge until it has set.
The cement will set in about 30 minutes, so work quickly.
