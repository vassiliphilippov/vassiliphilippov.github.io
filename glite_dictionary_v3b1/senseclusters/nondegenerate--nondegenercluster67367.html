nondegenerate

adjective
invertible or stable
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈdʒɛnərət / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈdʒɛnərət / (BrE)
not singular or defective; having full rank or a nonzero determinant, and therefore invertible or stable in mathematics.

Examples

Nondegenerate matrix
A nondegenerate matrix guarantees that the system of equations has a unique solution.
The theorem applies only to nondegenerate critical points in the function.
