demoralize

verb
corrupt morally
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪz /; / dɪˈmɔːrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to corrupt the morals or character of someone, especially by encouraging intemperance or sensuality

Examples

To demoralize youth
He sought to demoralize his rivals by tempting them with extravagance and vice.
Some argue that violent media can demoralize young viewers over time.
The corrupt regime aimed to demoralize the population by promoting decadence.
He warned that excessive wealth could demoralise even the most principled leaders.
It is dangerous to demoralise young people by encouraging reckless drinking and partying.
Some believed the decadent lifestyle would demoralise the entire community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - to make someone or something morally depraved
  • antonym: edify - to improve someone's mind or character
  • antonym: uplift - to improve morally or spiritually
