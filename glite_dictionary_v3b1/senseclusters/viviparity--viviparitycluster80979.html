viviparity

noun
live birth process
9.1
Pronunciation: /vɪ.vɪ.ˈpær.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /vɪ.vɪ.ˈpær.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the reproductive process in which animals give birth to live young rather than laying eggs

Examples

Mammal viviparity
Most mammals, including humans, exhibit viviparity as their primary mode of reproduction.
The evolution of viviparity allowed certain reptiles to survive in colder climates.
Scientists study viviparity to understand how different animals adapt their reproductive strategies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oviparity - the process of laying eggs that hatch outside the body
  • synonym: ovoviviparity - producing eggs that hatch inside the body, so young are born live
