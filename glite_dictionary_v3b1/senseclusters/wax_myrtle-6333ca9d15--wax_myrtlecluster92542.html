wax myrtle

noun
aromatic shrub or tree with waxy berries
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /wæks ˈmɜːr.təl/ (AmE), /wæks ˈmɜː.təl/ (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Myrica, with aromatic leaves and small berries coated in wax.

Examples

The wax myrtle shrub
Birds are attracted to the wax myrtle because of its waxy berries.
Many people use leaves from the wax myrtle to make fragrant sachets.
The wax myrtle is often planted as an ornamental shrub in gardens.
