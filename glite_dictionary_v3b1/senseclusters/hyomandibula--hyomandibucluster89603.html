hyomandibula

noun
jaw and ear bone
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪoʊmænˈdɪbjələ / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəʊmænˈdɪbjʊlə / (BrE)
a bone in fishes and early vertebrates that supports the jaw, and in tetrapods, evolved to help transmit sound in the middle ear

Examples

The hyomandibula bone
The hyomandibula played a crucial role in the evolution of hearing in early tetrapods.
In fish, the hyomandibula supports the jaw, but in mammals, it becomes part of the ear.
Scientists study the hyomandibula to understand the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.
The hyomandibula connects the jaw to the skull in many species of fish.
Scientists studied the evolution of the hyomandibula in early vertebrates.
Damage to the hyomandibula can affect how a fish opens its mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ossicle - a small bone, especially one of the three in the middle ear
  • synonym: stapes - a small bone in the middle ear that transmits sound vibrations
