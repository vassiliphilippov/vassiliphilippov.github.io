pact

noun
formal agreement between states
3.4 8.34/m
Pronunciation: / pÃ¦kt / (AmE), / pÃ¦kt / (BrE)
A formal agreement between two or more states or sovereigns; a treaty or alliance.

Examples

A pact between countries
After months of negotiation, a pact was finally reached between the leaders.
The environmental pact aims to reduce global emissions over the next decade.
The two nations signed a historic pact to end hostilities.

Synonyms

    4.1 31/m
    treaty is a formal written agreement between countries about peace or trade, while pact is a state agreement or alliance.
  • accord
    6.0 4.36/m
    accord is a formal written agreement often between nations, while pact is used for state treaties or political alliances.
  • compact
    6.5 1.72/m
    compact is a signed, often legal agreement between states, while pact is a general term for a state treaty or alliance.
  • covenant
    7.2 4.53/m
    covenant is a solemn written promise sometimes with religious or legal tone, while pact is a political agreement between states.
  • alliance
    4.0 36/m
    alliance is a union of countries or groups to work together, while pact often names a formal treaty or agreement between states.
  • agreement
    1.4 66/m
    agreement is any promise or deal between parties and can be informal, while pact is a formal state treaty or alliance.
