discitis

noun
spinal disc inflammation
9.7
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of an intervertebral disc, usually causing back pain

Examples

Discitis in spine
The MRI scan confirmed that the patient had discitis in the lower spine.
Children with unexplained back pain should be evaluated for possible discitis.
Treatment for discitis often involves antibiotics and rest to reduce inflammation.
The patient was diagnosed with discitis after complaining of severe back pain following surgery.
Treatment for aseptic discitis usually involves rest and physical therapy rather than antibiotics.
Children can also develop discitis, often presenting with reluctance to walk or back discomfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: osteomyelitis - infection and inflammation of bone or bone marrow
  • synonym: spondylitis - inflammation of the vertebrae
