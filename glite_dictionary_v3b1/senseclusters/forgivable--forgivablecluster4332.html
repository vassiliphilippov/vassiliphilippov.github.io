Easily excused or forgiven; not serious enough to warrant harsh punishment.

Examples

A forgivable mistake
Her mistake was understandable and entirely forgivable under the circumstances.
Many people consider minor rudeness to be forgivable if someone is having a bad day.
The offense was deemed forgivable because no lasting harm was done.

Synonyms

  • excusable
    3.6 0.37/m
    excusable is often used to say behavior can be justified or overlooked in everyday speech, while forgivable means not serious enough for harsh punishment.
  • venial
    8.3 0.26/m
    venial describes a minor, often moral or religious fault that can be forgiven, while forgivable refers to any not-serious wrong.
  • pardonable
    4.4 0.07/m
    pardonable is used in formal or legal contexts to mean deserving official forgiveness, while forgivable sounds more general and everyday.
