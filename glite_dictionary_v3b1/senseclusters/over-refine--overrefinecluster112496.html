over-refine

verb
refine excessively or too much
7.3 6.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.və.rɪ.ˈfaɪn/ /; / /oʊ.və.rə.ˈfaɪn/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.rɪ.ˈfaɪn/ /; / /əʊ.və.rə.ˈfaɪn/ (BrE)
to refine something too much or with excessive subtlety; to make excessively fine or subtle distinctions

Examples

To over-refine writing
If you over-refine your writing, it may lose its originality and impact.
She over-refined her argument until it became confusing rather than convincing.
The chef tends to over-refine his dishes, sometimes masking the natural flavors.
