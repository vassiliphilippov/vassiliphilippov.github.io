appellant

noun
party appealing a court decision
2.5
Pronunciation: / əˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / əˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
a person or party who appeals to a higher court for a reversal of a lower court's decision

Examples

The appellant appeals
In this case, the appellant sought to overturn the previous ruling.
The appellant argued that the trial court had made several legal errors.
The judge granted the appellant permission to present new evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appellee - the party against whom an appeal is taken
