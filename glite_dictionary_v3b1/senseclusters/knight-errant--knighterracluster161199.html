knight-errant

noun
wandering knight seeking adventure
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪtˈɛrənt / (AmE), / ˌnaɪtˈɛərənt / (BrE)
(in historical and literary contexts) a medieval knight who roamed in search of adventures, often with the aim of upholding chivalry and helping the needy.

Examples

A knight-errant roams
Inspired by tales of valor, the young man dreamed of becoming a knight-errant and traveling the world.
Many medieval romances feature a knight-errant battling monsters and rescuing damsels in distress.
The knight-errant pledged to defend the weak and uphold justice wherever his journeys took him.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paladin - a heroic champion or knight, especially one of the legendary Twelve Peers of Charlemagne
