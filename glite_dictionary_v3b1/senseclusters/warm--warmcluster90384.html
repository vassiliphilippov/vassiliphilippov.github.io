warm

adjective
easily aroused or excited temperament
2.8 1.55/m
Pronunciation: / w…îrm / (AmE), / w…îÀêm / (BrE)
marked by liveliness, excitement, or strong feeling, especially in discussions or exchanges; easily aroused or excited emotionally

Examples

Warm debate
Despite his warm disposition, he rarely lost his temper without good reason.
His warm temper often led to passionate debates during meetings.
She responded with a warm enthusiasm that energized everyone in the room.
After a warm exchange of views, they finally reached an agreement.
The committee became divided during a warm debate over the policy changes.
Their discussions grew increasingly warm as both sides defended their positions passionately.

Synonyms

  • hot

  • hot-blooded
    5.6 0.24/m

  • feverish
    3.8 1.17/m

  • sizzling
