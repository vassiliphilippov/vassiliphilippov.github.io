witchy

adjective
spiteful or ill-tempered
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃi / (BrE)
unpleasantly ill-tempered, spiteful, or malicious in manner or speech

Examples

Witchy remarks
Her witchy remarks made the atmosphere tense during the meeting.
He avoided his witchy neighbor, who was always quick to criticize.
The teacher's witchy tone discouraged students from asking questions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kind - having a friendly, generous, or considerate nature
  • synonym: malicious - intending to do harm or cause pain
  • synonym: spiteful - showing or caused by malice
