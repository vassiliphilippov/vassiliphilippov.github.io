start a crusade

verb
begin an energetic campaign for a cause
Pronunciation: / stɑrt ə kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / stɑːt ə kruːˈseɪd / (BrE)
to begin an intense and determined campaign to achieve a particular goal, often for a cause or reform

Examples

To start a crusade for change
After seeing the pollution in their local river, she decided to start a crusade to clean it up.
Many activists start a crusade when they are passionate about making social change.
The group plans to start a crusade against single-use plastics in their city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give up - to stop making an effort or attempting to achieve something
  • synonym: launch a campaign - to start a planned series of activities to achieve a goal
