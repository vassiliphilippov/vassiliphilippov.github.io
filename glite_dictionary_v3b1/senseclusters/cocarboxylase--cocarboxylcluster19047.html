cocarboxylase

noun
a coenzyme in the Krebs cycle
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊ.kɑːrˈbɑːk.səˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.kɑːˈbɒk.sɪˌleɪz / (BrE)
a coenzyme (the coenzyme form of thiamine, or vitamin B1) involved in cellular respiration and crucial for reactions in the Krebs cycle

Examples

The cocarboxylase enzyme
Deficiency in cocarboxylase can disrupt normal energy production in cells.
Researchers measured the activity of cocarboxylase in tissue samples to assess mitochondrial health.
The enzyme requires cocarboxylase as a cofactor for proper metabolic function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thiamine pyrophosphate - the coenzyme form of vitamin B1, synonymous with cocarboxylase
