yerk

noun
sudden sharp movement
Pronunciation: / jɜrk / (AmE), / jɜːk / (BrE)
a sudden sharp movement, pull, or blow, often quick and forceful.

Examples

A sudden yerk
She felt a sudden yerk as the train started and nearly dropped her bag.
With a quick yerk, he pulled the rope free from the hook.
The horse gave a yerk and broke away from its handler.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jerk - a quick, sharp movement or pull
  • synonym: tug - a strong, sudden pull
