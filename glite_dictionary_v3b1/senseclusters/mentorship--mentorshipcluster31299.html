mentorship

noun
guidance by a mentor
3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmen.tɔːr.ʃɪp/ /; / /ˈmen.tər.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈmen.tɔː.ʃɪp/ /; / /ˈmen.tə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
a relationship or program in which a more experienced person guides and supports someone less experienced, especially for career or personal development

Examples

A mentorship program
The university offers a mentorship program to help students connect with professionals in their field.
Through mentorship, young employees can learn valuable skills from experienced colleagues.
She credited her success to the mentorship she received early in her career.
The company's mentorship program pairs new employees with experienced staff for support.
He credited his success to the mentorship he received from his university professor.
Many students seek mentorship to help them navigate their career choices.
The company offers a mentorship program to help new employees adjust to their roles.
Her mentorship with a senior scientist inspired her to pursue a career in research.
Many students benefit from mentorship during their university studies.

  • synonym: coaching - the process of training or instructing someone to improve skills or performance
  • antonym: independence - the state of not relying on others for guidance or support
  • synonym: supervision - the act of overseeing or directing someone’s work or progress
