Great Revolt

proper noun
1381 English uprising against taxes
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt rɪˈvoʊlt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt rɪˈvəʊlt / (BrE)
a major uprising in England in 1381 against poll taxes and social injustices, especially affecting the poorer population, ultimately suppressed by King Richard II

Examples

The Great Revolt of 1381
Historians have studied the causes and aftermath of the Great Revolt extensively.
The Great Revolt of 1381 marked a turning point in medieval English history.
The suppression of the Great Revolt led to harsh penalties for many peasants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Peasants' Revolt - the uprising by peasants in England in 1381, also called the Great Revolt
