reasonable care

noun
standard of caution in law
Pronunciation: / ˈɹiːzənəbl̩ kɛɹ / (AmE), / ˈɹiːzənəbl kɛə / (BrE)
the degree of caution and attention that a sensible person would exercise in a particular situation, used as a standard to determine legal duty or negligence.

Examples

Reasonable care exercised
A doctor is expected to exercise reasonable care when treating patients.
Employers must show that they have taken reasonable care to ensure workplace safety.
The court will decide if the company took reasonable care to prevent the accident.
