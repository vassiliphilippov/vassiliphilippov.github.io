study

verb
think deeply or meditate
7.3 4.27/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÊŒdi / (AmE), / ËˆstÊŒdi / (BrE)
to think deeply and at length, often for spiritual or philosophical purposes; to meditate

Examples

To study in silence
Each morning, she would study in silence, reflecting on her life and spiritual path.
He sat by the window to study the mysteries of existence in solitude.
In the old monastery, monks study for hours, seeking enlightenment.

Synonyms

  • meditate
    4.4 5.52/m
    meditate is a quiet, focused practice often used for spiritual calm, while study is thinking deeply and at length for philosophy or reflection.
  • contemplate
    4.8 17/m
    contemplate means looking at an idea slowly with quiet attention, while study means long, deep thinking often for spiritual or philosophical insight.
  • ponder
    4.3 11/m
    ponder is slowly turning ideas over in your mind for a long time, while study is deep, often spiritual or philosophical, reflection.
  • reflect on
    4.0 12/m
    reflect on is to think carefully about a specific matter and its reasons, while study is broader deep thinking often tied to spiritual thought.
  • reflect
    3.9 32/m
    reflect is to think carefully and often analyze facts quietly, while study is longer, deeper thought often used for spiritual or philosophical aims.

Related Terms
