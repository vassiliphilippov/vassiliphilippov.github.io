menstruum

noun
archaic term for a solvent
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnstruəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstruːəm / (BrE)
(archaic) A substance, typically a liquid, that dissolves another substance; a solvent.

Examples

A chemical menstruum
Early chemists experimented with wine as a menstruum to dissolve medicinal compounds.
In alchemical texts, mercury often served as the menstruum for extracting the essence of gold.
The herbalist described alcohol as a powerful menstruum for tincture preparation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves another to form a solution
