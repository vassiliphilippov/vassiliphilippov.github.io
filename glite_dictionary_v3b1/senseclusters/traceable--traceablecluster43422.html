traceable

adjective
able to be found or followed
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsəbl / (BrE)
capable of being found, discovered, or followed by examining evidence or marks left by something

Examples

Traceable by police
All phone calls made from the hotel were easily traceable by police.
The origins of this rumor are barely traceable after so many years have passed.
They chose to donate anonymously to avoid their gift being traceable to them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: untraceable - unable to be traced or tracked
