microphysics

noun
study of tiny particles
9.1
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈfɪz.ɪks/ /; / /maɪ.krə.ˈfɪz.ɪks/ (AmE), /maɪ.krəʊ.ˈfɪz.ɪks/ /; / /maɪ.krə.ˈfɪz.ɪks/ (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies extremely small particles and their interactions, including physical processes at microscopic or atomic scales (such as in clouds or the atmosphere)

Examples

Microphysics of particles
She decided to focus her research on microphysics after taking an advanced physics course.
The discoveries in microphysics have changed our understanding of the universe at the smallest scales.
Many technological advances are based on principles from microphysics.
Researchers use advanced instruments to investigate the microphysics of water droplets in clouds.
Understanding the microphysics of materials helps scientists develop stronger and lighter metals.
The course covers the basic principles of microphysics and their applications in modern technology.
Researchers use microphysics to better understand how clouds form and change in the atmosphere.
Advances in microphysics have improved the accuracy of modern weather prediction models.
The course on microphysics covered the behavior of tiny particles in different atmospheric conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrophysics - the branch of physics dealing with large-scale phenomena
  • synonym: micrometeorology - the study of atmospheric phenomena on a small scale
  • synonym: quantum mechanics - the branch of physics dealing with atomic and subatomic systems
  • synonym: quantum physics - the study of the behavior of matter and energy at the smallest scales
