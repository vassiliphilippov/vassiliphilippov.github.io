metasedimentary

noun
metamorphosed sedimentary rock
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˌsɛdəˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˌsɛdɪˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
a rock that was originally sedimentary but has been changed by metamorphism, especially in geological contexts

Examples

A metasedimentary sample
Geologists collected samples of metasedimentary rocks to study their mineral composition.
The mountain range is largely composed of ancient metasedimentary formations.
Researchers identified a layer of metasedimentary material beneath the volcanic deposits.
