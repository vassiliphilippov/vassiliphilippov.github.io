rigidification

noun
process of becoming rigid or stiff
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪdʒɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪdʒɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming stiff or rigid, or of making something stiff or rigid

Examples

Rigidification of materials
Engineers need to prevent excessive rigidification of building materials to allow for flexibility during earthquakes.
Osteoporosis can lead to the rigidification of joints, reducing mobility in elderly patients.
The rigidification of polymers occurs when they cool below a certain temperature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stiffening - the act or process of making stiff or becoming stiff
