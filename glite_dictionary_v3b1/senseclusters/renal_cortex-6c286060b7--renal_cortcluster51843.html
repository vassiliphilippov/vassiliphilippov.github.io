renal cortex

noun
outer part of the kidney
Pronunciation: / ˈriːnəl ˈkɔːr.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈriː.nəl ˈkɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
the outer region of the kidney containing the glomeruli and convoluted tubules

Examples

The renal cortex region
Damage to the renal cortex can impair kidney function significantly.
Microscopic examination showed inflammation in the renal cortex of the patient’s kidney.
The renal cortex plays a vital role in filtering blood and removing waste products.

Related Terms

  • antonym: renal medulla - the inner part of the kidney
