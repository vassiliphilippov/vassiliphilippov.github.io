dally

verb
waste time; be slow or indecisive
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈdæli / (BrE)
to waste time, delay, or act slowly without making decisions

Examples

To dally over work
If you dally too long, you'll miss your train.
She tends to dally over her coffee every morning, making her late.
The students were told not to dally in the hallway after the bell rang.
He tended to dally with his assignments, leaving them unfinished until the last minute.
She warned him not to dally with her emotions if he wasn't serious.
The guests dallied in the garden, unconcerned about the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hasten - to move or act quickly, opposite of delay
  • synonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary, often wasting time
  • synonym: trifle - to treat someone or something without seriousness or respect
