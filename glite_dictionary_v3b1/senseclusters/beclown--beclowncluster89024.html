beclown

verb
to make look foolish
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪˈklaʊn / (AmE), / bɪˈklaʊn / (BrE)
to make someone appear foolish or ridiculous, as if turning them into a clown

Examples

To beclown a rival
The comedian tried to beclown his rival during the live show.
She refused to beclown herself by participating in the silly contest.
Online trolls often attempt to beclown public figures with edited photos.

  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: ridicule - to make fun of or mock someone
