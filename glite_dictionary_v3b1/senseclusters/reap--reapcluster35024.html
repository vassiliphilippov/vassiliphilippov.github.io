reap

verb
obtain or receive as a result
3.1 7.72/m
Pronunciation: / riÀêp / (AmE), / riÀêp / (BrE)
to get or receive something as a result of your actions or efforts, especially to cut and gather crops like grain

Examples

To reap the rewards
After years of hard work, she finally reaped the rewards of her dedication.
The company hopes to reap substantial profits from the new product line.
You only reap what you sow, so make wise choices.
After months of hard work, the company began to reap the benefits of its new strategy.
Farmers reap wheat in late summer, when the grain is fully ripe.
If you sow kindness, you will eventually reap rewards in return.

Synonyms

  • harvest
    1.6 10/m
    harvest is gathering crops when they are ripe, while reap often means cutting crops or getting results from effort.
  • earn
    1.0 58/m
    earn means receiving something you deserve for work or effort, while reap can mean getting results or crops.
  • gain
    1.4 83/m
    gain means obtaining an advantage, knowledge, or item often through effort, while reap focuses on crops or results.
