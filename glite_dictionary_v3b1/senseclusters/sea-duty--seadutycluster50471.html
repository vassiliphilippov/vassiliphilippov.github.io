sea-duty

noun
naval service aboard a ship at sea
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsiˌduːti / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌdjuːti / (BrE)
naval service performed aboard a ship at sea, as opposed to service on shore.

Examples

On sea-duty ship
After months of sea-duty, the crew looked forward to returning home.
He was assigned to sea-duty on an aircraft carrier in the Pacific.
Naval officers often alternate between shore assignments and sea-duty throughout their careers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shore-duty - naval service performed on land, not at sea
