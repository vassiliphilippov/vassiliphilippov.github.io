gully

noun
deep ditch cut by water
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌli / (AmE), / ˈɡʌli / (BrE)
A deep ditch or channel cut into the earth by running water, especially after heavy rain.

Examples

A deep gully
After the storm, a deep gully formed at the edge of the field.
Heavy rains each year cause the gully behind the house to grow wider.
The hikers carefully crossed the gully to avoid slipping on the wet stones.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: canyon - a deep gorge, typically with a river flowing through it
  • degree lower: ditch - a narrow channel dug in the ground, typically to hold or move water
  • synonym: ravine - a deep, narrow gorge with steep sides
