positive

noun
photograph with true-to-life tones
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
a photographic image in which the light and dark areas correspond to those of the original subject, as opposed to a negative

Examples

Positive print
After developing the film, she compared the positive print to the original scene.
The photographer made a positive from the original negative in the darkroom.
Viewing a slide projector, you see the image as a positive rather than as a reversed negative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: negative - a photographic image in which the tones are reversed
