brought to nought

verb
rendered ineffective or nullified
7.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / br…ît t…ô n…ît / (AmE), / br…îÀêt t…ô n…îÀêt / (BrE)
Rendered completely ineffective or nullified; reduced to nothing.

Examples

Was brought to nought
After months of negotiations, the merger was brought to nought by last-minute disagreements.
All their investments were brought to nought by the economic collapse.
Despite his determination, his efforts were brought to nought by unforeseen circumstances.

Synonyms

  • nullify
    6.8 0.83/m
    nullify is a common verb used to cancel an action or law, while brought to nought stresses being reduced to nothing completely.
  • null
    7.8 0.05/m
    null is a shorter, more technical verb often used in law or code, while brought to nought sounds more vivid and literary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: come to nothing - to fail; to amount to nothing
