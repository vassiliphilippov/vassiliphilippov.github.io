rest

verb
to stop moving or be at ease
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /rest/ (AmE), /rest/ (BrE)
to stop activity or movement in order to relax, recover strength, or sleep

Examples

To rest on the sofa
After a long day, she likes to rest on the sofa with a book.
Please rest while I prepare us some tea.
The hikers decided to rest under a tree before continuing their journey.
After finishing the report, the team decided to rest for the afternoon.
Our club will rest during the holidays and resume meetings in January.
The fields rest in winter before the next planting season begins.
After a long hike, we stopped to rest under the shade of a tree.
Doctors recommend that you rest if you feel unwell.
She prefers to rest quietly with a book after dinner.
