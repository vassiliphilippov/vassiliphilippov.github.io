agency

noun
capacity to act or exert power
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
the ability or power to act, make choices, or produce effects

Examples

Personal agency
In difficult situations, individuals may feel their agency is limited.
She exercised her agency by making her own decisions.
Social movements can restore a sense of agency to marginalized communities.
Personal agency plays a crucial role in shaping one's destiny.
Technology has given people more agency to express their ideas globally.
The education system should empower students with agency in their own learning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: helplessness - the inability to act or exert power
  • synonym: means - an action or system by which a result is achieved
  • synonym: power - the ability to do something or act in a particular way
