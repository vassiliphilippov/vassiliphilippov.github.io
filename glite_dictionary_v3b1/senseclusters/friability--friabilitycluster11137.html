friability

noun
easily crumbled state or quality
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfraɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌfraɪ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being easily crumbled, broken, or reduced to powder.

Examples

Low friability tablets
Pharmaceutical tablets must have low friability to remain intact during packaging and transport.
The friability of the ancient sandstone made it unsuitable for construction.
The geologist tested the friability of the soil samples by gently crushing them between her fingers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brittleness - easily broken without significant deformation
  • antonym: durability - ability to withstand wear or pressure
