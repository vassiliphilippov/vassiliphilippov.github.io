tramp through the woods

verb
walk extensively in a forest
Pronunciation: / træmp θru ðə wʊdz / (AmE), / tramp θruː ðə wʊdz / (BrE)
to walk for a long distance or duration through a forest or wooded area, especially in a deliberate, steady, and sometimes laborious way

Examples

To tramp through the woods steadily
Despite the rain, the scouts still tramped through the woods, exploring every trail they could find.
Every autumn, she likes to tramp through the woods looking for colorful leaves.
We tramped through the woods for hours before finding the perfect spot for our picnic.
