parenthesize

verb
to treat as an aside
8.2
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈren.θə.saɪz/ (AmE), /pə.ˈren.θə.saɪz/ (BrE)
to present or treat something as an aside or of secondary importance, often by setting it off in parentheses or as a digression

Examples

To parenthesize a comment
The author chose to parenthesize the historical background, focusing instead on the main argument.
During the meeting, he parenthesized his comments about the budget as a minor point.
Sometimes teachers parenthesize personal anecdotes to keep the lesson on track.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digress - to temporarily depart from the main subject
  • antonym: emphasize - to give special importance to something
