Anaplasma phagocytophilum

noun
tick-borne bacterium
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈplæzmə ˌfæɡəˌsaɪtəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌænəˈplæzmə ˌfæɡəˌsaɪtəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a species of bacteria that causes tick-borne diseases in humans and animals, such as human granulocytic anaplasmosis

Examples

Anaplasma phagocytophilum bacteria
Ticks can transmit Anaplasma phagocytophilum to both humans and animals during feeding.
Doctors test for Anaplasma phagocytophilum when patients show symptoms of tick-borne illness.
Livestock infected with Anaplasma phagocytophilum may develop fever and other health problems.

Related Terms
