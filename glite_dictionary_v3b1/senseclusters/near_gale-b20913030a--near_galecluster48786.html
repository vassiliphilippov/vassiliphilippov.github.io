near gale

noun
strong wind (Beaufort force 7)
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪr ɡeɪl / (AmE), / nɪə ɡeɪl / (BrE)
A wind of 32–38 knots (37–44 mph or 59–70 km/h), corresponding to force 7 on the Beaufort scale.

Examples

A near gale wind
A near gale made sailing conditions challenging during the regatta.
According to the forecast, a near gale is expected to hit the coast by evening.
The captain delayed departure due to the presence of a near gale in the open sea.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gale - very strong wind, force 8 on the Beaufort scale
  • degree lower: strong breeze - moderately strong wind, force 6 on the Beaufort scale
