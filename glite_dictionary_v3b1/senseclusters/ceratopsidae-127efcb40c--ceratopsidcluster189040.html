Ceratopsidae

noun
family of horned dinosaurs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛrəˈtɒpsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈtɒpsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, horned herbivorous dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, including well-known genera such as Triceratops.

Examples

The Ceratopsidae family
Members of the Ceratopsidae are known for their elaborate facial horns and frills.
Paleontologists discovered new fossils belonging to the Ceratopsidae in North America.
The Ceratopsidae family includes some of the most recognizable dinosaurs, such as Triceratops and Styracosaurus.
