common carotid

noun
main neck artery dividing into carotids
3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mən kəˈrɑː.tɪd/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.mən kəˈrɒ.tɪd/ (BrE)
either of the two main arteries on each side of the neck that supply blood to the head and neck, dividing into the external and internal carotid arteries.

Examples

The common carotid artery
A blockage in the common carotid can lead to serious health complications.
The doctor checked the pulse at the common carotid when examining the patient.
The surgeon carefully exposed the common carotid during the operation.
