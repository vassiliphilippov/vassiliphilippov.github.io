shimmy

verb
move or shake body in a dance
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
to shake or wobble the body or part of the body quickly from side to side, especially as a dance move

Examples

To shimmy on stage
During the performance, he shimmied with so much energy that the crowd cheered.
She loves to shimmy when her favorite song plays at parties.
The dancers began to shimmy across the stage in perfect unison.
