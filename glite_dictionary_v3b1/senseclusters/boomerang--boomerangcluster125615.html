boomerang

noun
something that backfires on its originator
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːməræŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːməræŋ / (BrE)
An action or plan that backfires, causing problems for the person who initiated it.

Examples

A plan boomerang
His attempt to hurt his rival became a boomerang that damaged his own career.
Spreading rumors can act as a boomerang and end up hurting you instead.
The new policy turned out to be a boomerang, harming the company’s reputation instead of improving it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backfire - Have the opposite effect to what was intended, especially causing harm to the initiator
  • synonym: misfire - Fail to have the intended effect or outcome
