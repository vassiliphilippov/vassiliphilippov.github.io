aggravated

adjective
made angry or provoked
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəveɪtɪd / (BrE)
made more angry or irritated, especially as a result of deliberate provocation.

Examples

Aggravated by delays
After hours of delays, passengers grew visibly aggravated at the airport.
He seemed aggravated by the constant noise outside his window.
The manager became aggravated when his instructions were deliberately ignored.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not angry or agitated
  • synonym: provoked - incited to anger or a strong reaction
