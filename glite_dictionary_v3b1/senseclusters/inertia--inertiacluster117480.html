inertia

noun
tendency to remain inactive or unchanged
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɜrʃə / (AmE), / ɪnˈɜːtɪə / (BrE)
a tendency to remain inactive or unchanged; a lack of willingness or ability to move or change

Examples

Personal inertia
After the long holiday, his inertia made it hard to start working again.
She finally overcame her inertia and began exercising regularly.
The committee's inertia prevented any real progress on the issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activity - the state of being active; doing something
  • synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
