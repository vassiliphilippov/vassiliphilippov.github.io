temperately

adverb
in a moderate way, without excess
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚətli /; / ˈtɛmpərətli / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətli / (BrE)
in a moderate or restrained way; without excess or overindulgence

Examples

To live temperately
He indulges in desserts temperately, never eating more than a small piece.
The judge spoke temperately, maintaining a calm and balanced tone throughout the proceedings.
We should approach the problem temperately rather than reacting with anger or impulsiveness.
He ate temperately, never overindulging even during celebrations.
She enjoyed her wine temperately, never having more than a single glass with dinner.
The judge spoke temperately, choosing his words with care and balance.
Alcohol should be consumed temperately for a healthy lifestyle.
He spoke temperately during the heated debate, careful not to provoke anyone.
Judges are expected to act temperately and avoid showing bias.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excessively - to an extreme degree; more than is necessary
  • synonym: moderately - in a moderate manner, avoiding extremes
