cupel

noun
porous container for assaying metals
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpəl / (BrE)
A small, porous container made of bone ash, used in assaying to separate precious metals from base metals such as lead by heating.

Examples

A cupel container
During the refining of gold, a cupel is essential for removing lead.
The chemist placed the sample in a cupel to begin the assay process.
They inspected the used cupel for traces of silver after the test.
