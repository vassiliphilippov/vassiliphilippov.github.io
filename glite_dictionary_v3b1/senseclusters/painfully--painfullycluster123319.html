painfully

adverb
with or as if with pain
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪnfəli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnfəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves pain or is extremely unpleasant or distressing (physically or emotionally)

Examples

To speak painfully
He spoke painfully about his difficult childhood.
She walked painfully across the room after her injury.
The decision to resign was painfully obvious to everyone involved.
He was painfully aware of his lack of experience in the new job.
Her mistake was painfully obvious to everyone in the room.
The meeting dragged on painfully for hours without any progress.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: excruciatingly - to an unbearably intense or severe degree
  • antonym: pleasantly - in a pleasant or enjoyable way
  • degree lower: slightly - to a small degree
  • synonym: sorely - in a severe or distressing way; very much
