gutter

verb
to gasp for air
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgʌt.ər/ (AmE), /ˈgʌt.ə/ (BrE)
to breathe with choking or gasping sounds, especially as a result of injury or while dying

Examples

He guttered for air
After the accident, he was guttering on the ground, struggling to breathe.
The wounded animal lay guttering in the grass, its breaths shallow and uneven.
She heard her grandfather guttering in his final moments, unable to call for help.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: breathe - to take air into and expel it from the lungs
  • synonym: choke - to have difficulty breathing because of a blocked throat
  • synonym: gasp - to inhale suddenly with the mouth open, out of pain or astonishment
