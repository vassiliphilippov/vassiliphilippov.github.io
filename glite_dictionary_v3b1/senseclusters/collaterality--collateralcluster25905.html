collaterality

noun
lateral kinship
9.8
Pronunciation: / kəˌlætəˈræləti / (AmE), / kəˌlætəˈralɪti / (BrE)
the state of being related to someone through a common ancestor but not in a direct line, such as siblings or cousins

Examples

Family collaterality
Genealogists often examine collaterality to understand the relationships between extended family members.
The concept of collaterality helps explain how cousins are related through shared ancestors.
Inheritance laws sometimes consider collaterality when determining who is eligible to receive property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kinship - the state of being related to others by blood or marriage
  • antonym: lineality - direct descent in a family line, such as parent to child
