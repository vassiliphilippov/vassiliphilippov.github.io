defluvium

noun
washed-down debris
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfluːviəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfluːviəm / (BrE)
loose material such as soil, rock, or debris that is carried down and deposited by surface runoff or slope wash

Examples

River defluvium
After the storm, a thick layer of defluvium covered the lower fields near the river.
The geologist collected samples of defluvium to study the effects of erosion on the hillside.
Heavy rains caused defluvium to accumulate at the base of the mountain, blocking the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - soil or sediment deposited by flowing water
  • synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked or destroyed
