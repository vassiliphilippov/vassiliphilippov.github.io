nescient

adjective
believing only material phenomena can be known
9.4 9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈne.ʃənt/ (AmE), /ˈne.ʃənt/ (BrE)
lacking knowledge or awareness; ignorant (sometimes specifically about spiritual or ultimate matters)

Examples

A nescient person
A nescient perspective leaves no room for knowledge of ultimate causes or spiritual matters.
Her nescient view led her to dismiss all discussions of the divine as unknowable.
The philosopher adopted a nescient stance, focusing solely on observable phenomena.
Despite his intelligence, he was surprisingly nescient about modern technology.
His nescient comments during the seminar showed he hadn't read the assigned material.
She remained nescient of the political changes happening beyond her small village.
