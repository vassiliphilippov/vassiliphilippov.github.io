circulate

verb
cause to move around in a system
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːkjəˌleɪt /; / ˈsɜːrkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to move continuously or freely through a closed system or area (used for liquids or gases)

Examples

Circulate blood
Doctors use a machine to circulate blood during heart surgery.
He installed a pump to circulate the water in the fish tank.
The ceiling fan helps to circulate the air throughout the house.
Blood circulates in the body to deliver oxygen to every cell.
The heating system ensures that warm air circulates throughout the whole building.
Without an open window, the air doesn't properly circulate in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flow - to move steadily and continuously in a current or stream
  • antonym: stagnate - to stop moving or flowing and become inactive or still
