fluctuating exchange rate

noun
currency value set by market forces
1.0 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌktʃuˌeɪtɪŋ ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌktjʊeɪtɪŋ ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˌreɪt / (BrE)
An exchange rate whose value is determined by supply and demand in the open market, rather than being fixed by a government.

Examples

The fluctuating exchange rate
Investors track the fluctuating exchange rate to make informed decisions about international trade.
Many developing countries now use a fluctuating exchange rate to respond to external economic shocks.
The risk of a fluctuating exchange rate can affect profits for multinational companies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed exchange rate - An exchange rate set by a government or central bank and held constant
  • synonym: floating exchange rate - An exchange rate system where the value is determined by market forces
