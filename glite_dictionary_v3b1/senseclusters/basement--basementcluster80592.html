basement

noun
ground floor in Renaissance architecture
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the ground floor facade or interior in Renaissance architecture, often forming a distinct horizontal division at the base of a building

Examples

Renaissance basement
Architects often emphasized the basement to visually anchor the building within its surroundings.
Columns rise from the basement, giving the facade a sense of grandeur and stability.
The palace's basement was adorned with decorative stonework characteristic of Renaissance style.
