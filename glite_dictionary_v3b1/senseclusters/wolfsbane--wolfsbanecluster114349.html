wolfsbane

noun
poisonous Eurasian herb (Aconitum)
9.9 0.09/m

Examples

The wolfsbane plant
Botanists studied the wolfsbane to understand how its poison affects the nervous system.
In medieval times, wolfsbane was believed to protect against werewolves and witches.
The gardener warned us not to touch the wolfsbane because of its toxic properties.

Synonyms

  • wolfbane
    9.9 0.01/m
    wolfbane is the same common name for the poisonous Eurasian perennial, while wolfsbane appears as an alternative spelling in many texts.
  • monkshood
    9.9 0.10/m
    monkshood has hood-shaped blue or purple flowers and common use in Europe, while wolfsbane refers to several aconite species.
  • Aconitum
    9.9 0.03/m
    Aconitum is the botanical genus name for many poisonous plants, while wolfsbane is a common name for some species in that genus.
