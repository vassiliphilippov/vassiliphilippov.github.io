full nelson

noun
wrestling hold with hands locked behind opponent's neck
8.8 6.4
Pronunciation: /fʊl ˈnel.sən/ (AmE), /fʊl ˈnel.sən/ (BrE)
a wrestling hold in which both arms are passed under the opponent's arms and hands are locked behind their neck, exerting pressure (illegal in amateur wrestling)

Examples

A full nelson hold
He quickly put his opponent in a full nelson to subdue him.
She escaped from his attempted full nelson with a swift counter move.
The coach explained why the full nelson is considered dangerous and banned in amateur matches.
