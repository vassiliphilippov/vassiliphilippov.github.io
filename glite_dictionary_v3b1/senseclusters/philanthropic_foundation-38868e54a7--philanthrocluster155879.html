philanthropic foundation

noun
organization for charitable funding
Pronunciation: / fɪˌlænθrəˈpɪk faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪˌlanθrəˈpɪk faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
an organization established to provide financial support for charitable purposes, such as science, art, education, religion, or relief from disease or poverty

Examples

A philanthropic foundation awarded scholarships to students from underserved communities.
Several medical breakthroughs have been funded by a philanthropic foundation dedicated to fighting rare diseases.
The university received a generous grant from a philanthropic foundation to build a new research center.
