poison hemlock

noun
a highly toxic plant (Conium maculatum)
8.3 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔɪ.zən ˈhem.lɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈpɔɪ.zən ˈhem.lɒk/ (BrE)
a tall, highly poisonous plant (Conium maculatum) with fernlike leaves and small white flowers, native to Eurasia and Africa and now found in North America, typically in damp areas

Examples

Tall poison hemlock plant
Gardeners are warned to avoid handling poison hemlock due to its extreme toxicity.
Socrates was famously put to death by drinking a concoction containing poison hemlock.
The field was overgrown with poison hemlock, making it dangerous for grazing animals.
