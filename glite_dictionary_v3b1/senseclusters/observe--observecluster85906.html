observe

verb
make a remark or comment
4.1 19/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàz…ùv / (AmE), / …ôbÀàz…úÀêv / (BrE)
to say or mention something as a comment, especially formally or thoughtfully

Examples

She observed a fact
During the meeting, she observed that the budget needed closer review.
He observed in his speech that teamwork was essential to success.
The professor observed that several students were absent today.

Synonyms

  • remark
    2.1 15/m
    remark is a spoken or written comment often aimed at opinion or reaction, while observe is more formal and thoughtful.
  • note
    1.6 125/m
    note is a brief way to point out facts in speech or writing, while observe suggests a careful, formal comment.
  • mention
    1.0 180/m
    mention is a quick, simple reference made without detail, while observe implies a thoughtful, often formal spoken comment.
  • comment
    2.4 6.62/m
    comment is often an explanatory note written beside a text, while observe means saying something as a formal, thoughtful remark.

Related Terms
