divertor

noun
water flow redirector
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɜrtər / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
an engineered structure or device used to redirect the flow of water, such as a river or stream, for flood control or water supply

Examples

Flood control divertor
The city installed a divertor to prevent flooding during heavy rains.
A new divertor was constructed to channel water toward the irrigation fields.
Engineers regularly inspect the divertor to ensure it functions properly during storms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dam - a barrier built to hold back water and raise its level
  • synonym: sluice - a sliding gate or other device for controlling the flow of water
