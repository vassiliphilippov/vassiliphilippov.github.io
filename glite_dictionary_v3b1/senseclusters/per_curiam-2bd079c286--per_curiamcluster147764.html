per curiam

noun
unsigned court opinion
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɜːr ˈkjuː.riː.əm/ (AmE), /pɜː ˈkjuː.riː.əm/ (BrE)
a court opinion or decision issued collectively by the judges, usually without naming an individual author

Examples

A per curiam opinion
The Supreme Court issued a per curiam that resolved the case without a lengthy explanation.
Legal scholars discussed the implications of the recent per curiam in constitutional law.
Because it was a per curiam, no single justice was credited as the author.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissenting opinion - an opinion by one or more judges expressing disagreement with the majority
  • antonym: majority opinion - a court opinion reflecting the views of the majority of judges, usually signed
