underpaid

adjective
paid too little
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpeɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpeɪd / (BrE)
receiving less money than is fair or deserved for work done

Examples

An underpaid worker
Many teachers feel underpaid for the important work they do.
The employees protested because they believed they were underpaid compared to others in the industry.
Despite working long hours, she remained underpaid for her efforts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overpaid - paid more than is fair or deserved
  • synonym: undercompensated - not paid enough for work or effort
