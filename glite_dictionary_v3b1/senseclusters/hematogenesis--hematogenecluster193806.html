hematogenesis

noun
lab production of blood cells
9.9
Pronunciation: /hiː.mə.tə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ /; / /ɪ.mə.tə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /hiː.mə.tə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ /; / /ɪ.mə.tə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the production or formation of blood cells outside a living organism, especially in laboratory cultures or ex vivo systems.

Examples

Lab hematogenesis in vitro
Researchers achieved haematogenesis in vitro to generate red blood cells for transfusion trials.
The lab reported successful haematogenesis from stem cells after several weeks of culture.
A breakthrough in haematogenesis could reduce the need for donor blood in emergencies.
