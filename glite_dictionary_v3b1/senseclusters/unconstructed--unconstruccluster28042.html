unconstructed

adjective
spontaneous; natural
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˈstrʌktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
not deliberately planned, arranged, or made to seem artificial; spontaneous or natural.

Examples

Unconstructed style
Her unconstructed laughter made the room feel warm and unexpectedly joyful.
The director preferred an unconstructed style of acting that felt more genuine.
He gave an unconstructed response, speaking honestly without rehearsed words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contrived - deliberately created rather than arising naturally or spontaneously
  • synonym: spontaneous - done or said in a natural and sudden way, without planning
