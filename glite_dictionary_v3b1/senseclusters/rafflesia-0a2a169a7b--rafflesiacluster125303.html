Rafflesia

proper noun
giant parasitic plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ræˈfliːʒə / (AmE), / ræˈfliːzɪə / (BrE)
a genus of large parasitic flowering plants native to Southeast Asia, known for producing the world's largest individual flowers

Examples

The Rafflesia flower
The Rafflesia is famous for producing the largest single flower in the world.
Many tourists visit the rainforest hoping to see a blooming Rafflesia in its natural habitat.
Because Rafflesia is a parasitic plant, it relies on other plants for nutrients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corpse flower - a large, foul-smelling flower, often referring to Rafflesia or Amorphophallus titanum
