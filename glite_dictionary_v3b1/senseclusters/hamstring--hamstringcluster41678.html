hamstring

verb
cripple by cutting the hamstring
7.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhæm.strɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈhæm.strɪŋ/ (BrE)
to cripple or severely disable by cutting the hamstring tendon, especially in the leg of a person or animal

Examples

To hamstring a prisoner
In medieval times, traitors might be hamstrung as punishment for their disloyalty.
The cruel practice of hamstringing horses rendered them useless for work or war.
The invaders would sometimes hamstring their captives to prevent escape.
