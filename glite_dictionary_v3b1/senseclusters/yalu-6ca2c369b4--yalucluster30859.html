Yalu

proper noun
river on the North Korea–China border
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːluː / (AmE), / ˈjɑːluː / (BrE)
A river in eastern Asia forming part of the border between North Korea and China, flowing southwest to the Korea Bay.

Examples

Yalu River
Chinese troops crossed the Yalu into North Korea in 1950.
Ice floes move down the Yalu each winter, marking the border's harsh conditions.
The Yalu River played a crucial role during the Korean War.
