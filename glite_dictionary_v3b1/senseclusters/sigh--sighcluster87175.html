sigh

verb
let out a deep audible breath
Pronunciation: / saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
to let out a long, deep, audible breath expressing tiredness, relief, sadness, or another emotion

Examples

To sigh with relief
After finishing the long hike, they sighed and sat down to rest.
He sighs heavily every time he thinks about his old hometown.
She sighed with relief when she heard the good news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gasp - inhale suddenly with the mouth open, out of pain or astonishment
