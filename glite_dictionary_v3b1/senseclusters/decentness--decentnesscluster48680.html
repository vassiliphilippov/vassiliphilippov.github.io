decentness

noun
acceptable quality
3.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀês…ôntn…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀêsntn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being acceptable, adequate, or meeting a reasonable standard

Examples

The decentness of a place
The decentness of the hotel surprised us, given its low price.
She appreciated the decentness of his behavior during the meeting.
We were satisfied with the decentness of the meal, even if it was not exceptional.

Synonyms

  • acceptability
    4.8 3.36/m
    acceptability is how much people or rules approve something, while decentness is the general quality of meeting a simple reasonable standard.
  • adequacy
    3.7 3.30/m
    adequacy is being enough for a specific purpose or need, while decentness is a broader sense of meeting a reasonable standard.
  • satisfactoriness
    7.7 0.01/m
    satisfactoriness is meeting particular demands or expectations in detail, while decentness means being acceptable at a general or simple level.
  • properness
    6.5 0.01/m
    properness is being correct or suitable for a specific situation, while decentness is about being generally acceptable or adequate.
