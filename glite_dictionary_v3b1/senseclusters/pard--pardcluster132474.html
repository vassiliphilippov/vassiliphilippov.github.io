pard

noun
archaic for leopard
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɑrd / (AmE), / pɑːd / (BrE)
an old or poetic word for a leopard or large wild cat (sometimes, in US English, an informal term for a companion)

Examples

In ancient tales, the pard was known for its speed and cunning.

In the old poem, the pard prowled silently through the jungle.
The cowboy never left his pard behind on the trail.
Legends spoke of a fierce pard guarding the mountain pass.
