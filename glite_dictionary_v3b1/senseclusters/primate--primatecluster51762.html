primate

noun
order of mammals including apes, monkeys, and humans
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmeɪt /; / ˈpraɪ.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
a mammal of the order Primates, such as humans, apes, monkeys, and lemurs

Examples

A primate like a monkey
Humans, chimpanzees, and gorillas are all classified as primates.
Many primates are known for their complex social behaviors and high intelligence.
Some species of primates live in large groups, while others are more solitary.
Humans are classified as primates in biological taxonomy.
The lemur is a type of primate found only in Madagascar.
The study focused on the behavior of various primate species in the wild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ape - a tailless primate of the family Hominoidea
  • degree higher: mammal - warm-blooded vertebrate animal with hair or fur, and females secrete milk
  • synonym: monkey - a primate of the order Primates, generally with a tail
