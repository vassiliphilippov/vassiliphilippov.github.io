vertical tail

noun
upright tail airfoil of an aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːtɪkəl teɪl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl teɪl / (BrE)
the upright airfoil forming part of the tail assembly of an aircraft, typically providing directional stability

Examples

The vertical tail fin
A damaged vertical tail can seriously affect an aircraft's ability to turn correctly.
Engineers tested several designs for the vertical tail to improve its aerodynamic efficiency.
The vertical tail of the airplane helps maintain its stability during flight.
