reprice

verb
to change price
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈpraɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpraɪs / (BrE)
to change or set a new price for something

Examples

To reprice products
The company decided to reprice its products to stay competitive in the market.
After the stock market crash, the board voted to reprice employee stock options.
Retailers may reprice items during a sale to attract more customers.
The company decided to reprice its products after reviewing market trends.
After the holiday season, the store repriced many items to attract more customers.
If costs go up, we may need to reprice our services next year.
The company decided to reprice its products after the cost of materials increased.
After the holiday season, the store will reprice many items to attract more customers.
Online retailers often reprice goods to stay competitive in the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjust - to change something slightly to make it more suitable
  • degree lower: discount - to reduce the price of something
  • antonym: fix - to set or decide on a price or value
  • degree higher: increase - to make something larger in amount or price
  • antonym: overprice - to set a price too high
