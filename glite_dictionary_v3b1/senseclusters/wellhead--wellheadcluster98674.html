wellhead

noun
the source of water for a well
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌhɛd / (BrE)
the source or origin of water for a well; the point at which water emerges from the ground to supply a well

Examples

Natural wellhead
After the earthquake, the wellhead was damaged and needed urgent repair.
The villagers gathered at the wellhead every morning to collect fresh water.
They built a small shelter over the wellhead to protect it from contamination.
