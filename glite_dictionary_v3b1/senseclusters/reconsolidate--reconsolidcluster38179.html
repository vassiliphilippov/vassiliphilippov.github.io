reconsolidate

verb
to bring together again
7.4
Pronunciation: /riː.kən.ˈsɑː.lə.deɪt/ /; / /rɪ.kən.ˈsɑː.lə.deɪt/ (AmE), /riː.kən.ˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt/ /; / /rɪ.kən.ˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt/ (BrE)
to bring things back together into a single group or whole after they have been separated

Examples

To reconsolidate a team
After the departments were split, management worked hard to reconsolidate their efforts.
The company plans to reconsolidate its assets following the restructuring.
It took several meetings to reconsolidate the divided teams into one group.
After the merger, the company decided to reconsolidate its various branches into one headquarters.
The government worked to reconsolidate the regions that had been divided during the conflict.
To improve efficiency, management will reconsolidate overlapping teams into a single department.
