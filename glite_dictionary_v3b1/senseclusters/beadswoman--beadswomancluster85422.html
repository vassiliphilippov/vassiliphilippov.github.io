beadswoman

noun
woman paid to pray for another's soul
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbidzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbiːdzwʊmən / (BrE)
an old woman paid to pray for the soul of someone, often by counting prayers on beads

Examples

A beadswoman praying
A solitary beadswoman could be seen in the church, her lips moving silently in prayer.
In medieval England, families sometimes employed a beadswoman after a loved one's death.
The wealthy lord left money to a beadswoman who was to pray for his soul each day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beadsman - a man who is paid to pray for another's soul
