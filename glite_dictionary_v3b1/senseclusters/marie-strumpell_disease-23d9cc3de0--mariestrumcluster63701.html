A chronic inflammatory disease, primarily affecting males, in which spinal mobility is progressively impaired and which may result in spinal fusion (ankylosis).

Examples

Marie-Strumpell disease symptoms
Early detection of Marie-Strumpell disease can help slow the progression of spinal ankylosis.
Many individuals with Marie-Strumpell disease require physical therapy to maintain spinal mobility.
The patient was diagnosed with Marie-Strumpell disease after experiencing persistent back pain and stiffness for years.
