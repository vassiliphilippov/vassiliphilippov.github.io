heparin

noun
anticoagulant substance used to prevent blood clots
8.8 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhe.pə.rɪn/ (AmE), /ˈhe.pə.rɪn/ (BrE)
a naturally occurring substance in the body that prevents blood from clotting, often used as a medication to reduce the risk of blood clots

Examples

Heparin therapy
Heparin therapy is often recommended for people at high risk of developing deep vein thrombosis.
Doctors carefully monitor heparin levels to avoid excessive bleeding complications.
The patient was given a dose of heparin to prevent blood clots during surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticoagulant - a substance that prevents blood coagulation
