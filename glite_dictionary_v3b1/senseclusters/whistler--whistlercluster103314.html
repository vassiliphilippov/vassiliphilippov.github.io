whistler

noun
songbird with a whistling call
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪs.tlər / (AmE), / ˈwɪs.lə / (BrE)
an Australian or southeastern Asian songbird known for its melodious whistling call.

Examples

A songbird whistler
A brightly colored male whistler perched on the branch, singing its signature tune.
Many birdwatchers travel to Australia to spot rare species of whistler.
The forests echoed with the call of a whistler early in the morning.
