nonacute

adjective
not severe or intense
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnəˈkjuːt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnəˈkjuːt / (BrE)
not sharp, severe, or intense in degree; not acute, especially in a medical context

Examples

Nonacute illness
The doctor explained that her condition was nonacute and did not require immediate treatment.
Patients with nonacute symptoms are often monitored over a longer period.
A nonacute injury may still need care, even if it is not severe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acute - sharp, severe, or intense in degree
  • synonym: chronic - lasting for a long time or constantly recurring
