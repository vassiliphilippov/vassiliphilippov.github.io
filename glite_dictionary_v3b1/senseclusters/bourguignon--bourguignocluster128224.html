bourguignon

noun
dish cooked in red wine with onions and herbs
9.3
Pronunciation: / bʊr.giˈnjɔn / (AmE), / bʊə.giˈnjɒ̃ / (BrE)
A style of cooking, typically involving meat braised in red wine with onions, herbs, and butter, originating from the Burgundy region of France.

Examples

Mushroom bourguignon
Mushroom bourguignon is a popular vegetarian twist on the classic recipe.
She learned to cook a traditional beef bourguignon in her French cuisine class.
The chef prepared a delicious bourguignon for the restaurant's special menu.
