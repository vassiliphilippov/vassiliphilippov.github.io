Nereus

proper noun
sea god in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪəriəs / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) an ancient sea god, son of Pontus and Gaea, husband of Doris, and father of the Nereids.

Examples

Nereus sea god
According to legend, Nereus could change his shape at will when asked questions.
Many ancient Greeks regarded Nereus as a symbol of wisdom and truth.
The daughters of Nereus and Doris were known as the Nereids.

  • degree higher: Poseidon - Greek god of the sea, often regarded as more powerful than Nereus
