not knowing or noticing something; not having knowledge or awareness of something, often used with 'of'.

Examples

Unaware of danger
He remained unaware of the criticism directed at his recent statements.
Many people are unaware of the environmental impact of their daily choices.
She walked into the room, completely unaware that everyone was waiting to surprise her.

Synonyms

  • uninformed
    3.7 3.41/m
    uninformed is lacking specific facts or information about a topic, while unaware means not knowing or noticing something in general.
  • incognizant
    8.8 0.01/m
    incognizant is a formal word meaning not aware of facts, while unaware is common and used in everyday speech.
  • unknowing
    5.8 0.40/m
    unknowing describes not having knowledge or intention, while unaware focuses on simply not knowing or noticing something.
  • oblivious
    6.3 7.05/m
    oblivious means not noticing what happens around you, while unaware is more general and covers missing facts or details.
  • unacquainted
    6.5 0.07/m
    unacquainted means not familiar from experience or contact, while unaware means not knowing or noticing a fact or event.
  • unfamiliar with
    4.1 2.35/m
    unfamiliar with says someone has little or no experience with something, while unaware means they simply do not know or notice it.

  • synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something
