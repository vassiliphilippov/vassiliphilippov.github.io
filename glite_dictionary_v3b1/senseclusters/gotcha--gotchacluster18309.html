gotcha

noun
trap question
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑtʃə / (AmE), / ˈɡɒtʃə / (BrE)
a question, action, or remark designed to catch someone off guard or embarrass them, especially in a debate or interview

Examples

A tough gotcha question
The journalist ended the interview with a tough gotcha that surprised the politician.
She prepared carefully so she would not fall for any gotcha during the debate.
That last gotcha in the meeting made everyone laugh, but it was a little embarrassing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - a surprise attack or action
  • antonym: softball - an easy or gentle question
  • synonym: trap - a trick or device intended to catch someone
