subsume

verb
include or absorb in a larger category
Pronunciation: / səbˈsuːm / (AmE), / səbˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to include or absorb something as part of a larger group, category, or principle

Examples

To subsume under a group
In philosophy, individual cases are often subsumed under general principles.
Many small tasks were subsumed into one large project.
The new training manual will subsume all previous guidelines.
In his theory, personal interests are subsumed by the needs of society.
Many minor offenses are subsumed within this broader category.
The law subsumes individual cases under a general principle.
