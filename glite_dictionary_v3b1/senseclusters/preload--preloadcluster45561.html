preload

noun
initial mechanical force
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌləʊd / (BrE)
an initial force, tension, or compression applied to a mechanical part to ensure proper function or fit

Examples

Preload on a spring
The engineer checked the preload on the bearing before starting the machine.
Proper preload helps prevent bolts from loosening during operation.
Adjusting the preload can improve the performance and safety of mechanical assemblies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tension - the state of being stretched tight
