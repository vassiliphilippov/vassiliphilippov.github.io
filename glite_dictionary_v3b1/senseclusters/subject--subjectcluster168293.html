subject

adjective
likely to be affected by something
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt /; / ˈsʌb.dʒɪkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt /; / ˈsʌb.dʒɪkt / (BrE)
likely to be affected by, dependent on, or under the authority or control of someone or something

Examples

Subject to change
Admission to the event is subject to availability of seats.
All food imports are subject to health and safety checks at the border.
These prices are subject to change without prior notice.
The agreement granted independence to states previously subject to colonial authority.
The conquered territories were made subject to the will of the emperor.
These laws applied only to people subject to British rule at the time.
All reservations are subject to confirmation by the hotel.
Any refund is subject to approval by the finance department.
The schedule is subject to change without notice due to weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sovereign - possessing supreme or ultimate power (antonym in this context)
  • synonym: susceptible - likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
