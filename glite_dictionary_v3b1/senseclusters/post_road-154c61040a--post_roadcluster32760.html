post road

noun
road used for mail delivery
1.9
Pronunciation: / poʊst roʊd / (AmE), / pəʊst rəʊd / (BrE)
A road designated for the transportation of mail, especially in former times before modern mail systems.

Examples

The post road route
Many towns developed along the post road because of its importance to communication.
Stagecoaches once traveled the post road to deliver letters and packages across the region.
The old post road runs through several historic villages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mail route - a route regularly followed by mail carriers
