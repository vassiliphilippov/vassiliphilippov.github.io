Synonyms

  • use up
    2.7 3/m
    use up means to consume all of something so nothing remains, while eat up usually means using resources quickly or completely.
  • consume
    3.2 8/m
    consume is a formal word for using or spending resources fully, while eat up feels more informal and quick.
  • exhaust
    3.7 3/m
    exhaust means to use something until nothing is left, while eat up often suggests quick or active consumption.
  • deplete
    6.3 0.6/m
    deplete means to reduce a supply until little remains, while eat up often implies fast or complete use.
  • run through
    4.4 0.8/m
    run through means to spend or use resources quickly and sometimes wastefully, while eat up stresses rapid consumption.
  • chew up
    4.6 0.10/m
    chew up means to use resources wastefully or cause problems, while eat up often simply means to consume quickly.
  • drain resources
    4.6 0.09/m
    drain resources means to steadily remove assets or money over time, while eat up often suggests faster, direct consumption.
  • devour
    3.9 2/m
    devour describes eating eagerly or destroying something completely, while eat up focuses on using resources or supplies rapidly.
  • wipe out
    4.2 9/m
    wipe out means to destroy or remove something completely, while eat up usually means to use or consume a resource.
