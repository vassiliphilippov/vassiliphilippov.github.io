pastoralist

noun
livestock farm manager
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpæ.stə.rə.lɪst/ /; / /ˈpɑː.stə.rə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.stə.rə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person whose job or way of life is raising and herding grazing animals, such as cattle, sheep, or goats

Examples

A pastoralist raises cattle
The pastoralist inspected the fence lines on his sprawling cattle station before the dry season.
Many pastoralists in Australia rely on rainfall patterns to manage their herds.
As a successful pastoralist, she introduced new grazing techniques to improve the land.
The pastoralist moved his cattle to new grazing land during the dry season.
Many pastoralists travel long distances each year to find fresh pastures for their animals.
In some regions, a pastoralist community relies entirely on sheep and goats for their livelihood.
Many pastoralists travel long distances each year to find fresh grass for their animals.
The government introduced new policies to support pastoralist communities during droughts.
Traditional pastoralists often rely on cattle, sheep, or goats for their livelihood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crop farmer - a person who grows crops rather than raising animals
  • synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm
  • synonym: herder - a person who looks after a herd of animals
  • synonym: herdsman - a person who looks after a herd of livestock
  • synonym: rancher - a person who owns or manages a large farm for raising animals
