harvester

noun
person or machine that gathers crops
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrvəstər /; / ˈhɑːrvəstər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvəstə /; / ˈhɑːvɪstə / (BrE)
a person or machine that gathers crops at harvest time

Examples

A harvester in the field
Every autumn, local harvesters work long hours in the fields.
In some regions, mechanical harvesters have replaced manual laborers.
The harvester carried baskets filled with ripe apples to the barn.
After the storm, the damaged harvester had to be repaired before the harvest could continue.
Farmers invested in a new harvester to improve efficiency during the busy season.
The harvester moved slowly across the wheat field, collecting the ripe grain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combine - a machine that cuts, threshes, and cleans grain crops
  • synonym: reaper - a person or machine that cuts and gathers crops at harvest
