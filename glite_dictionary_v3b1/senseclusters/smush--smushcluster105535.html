smush

verb
to squash or crush
7.5
Pronunciation: / smʊʃ / (AmE), / smʊʃ / (BrE)
to squash or press something so that it becomes flat, soft, or misshapen

Examples

To smush a hat
If you sit on your hat, you might smush it out of shape.
The cookies got smushed together in the bottom of the bag.
Be careful not to smush the berries when you pack them.
He accidentally smushed the sandwich in his backpack, making it completely flat.
The child smushed the clay between her fingers to make a new shape.
If you sit on those berries, you will smush them all over the seat.
