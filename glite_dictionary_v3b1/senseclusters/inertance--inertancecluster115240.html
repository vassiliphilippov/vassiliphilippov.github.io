inertance

noun
fluid flow resistance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɜːrtəns / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːtəns / (BrE)
a measure of the opposition to a change in flow in a fluid system, similar to inertia in mechanics

Examples

The inertance of pipe
The inertance of the pipe affects how quickly the fluid accelerates.
Engineers must consider inertance when designing systems for rapid fluid movement.
High inertance can cause delays in the response of medical ventilators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impedance - opposition to the flow of alternating current or other oscillating signals
  • synonym: inertia - resistance of an object to a change in its state of motion
