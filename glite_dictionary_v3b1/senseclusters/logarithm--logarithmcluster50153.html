logarithm

noun
mathematical exponent needed for a base to reach a number
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːɡəˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
a mathematical quantity representing the exponent to which a fixed base must be raised to produce a given number

Examples

Natural logarithm
Natural logarithm uses the base e, a fundamental constant in mathematics.
The logarithm of 1000 to the base 10 is 3 because 10 cubed equals 1000.
To solve the equation, she calculated the logarithm of both sides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exponent - a number indicating how many times another number is used as a factor
