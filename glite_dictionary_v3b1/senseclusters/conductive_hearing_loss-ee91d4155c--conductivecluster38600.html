conductive hearing loss

noun
hearing loss from middle ear problems
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌktɪv ˈhɪrɪŋ lɔs / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktɪv ˈhɪə.rɪŋ lɒs / (BrE)
a type of hearing loss caused by problems with the bones or structures of the middle ear, preventing sound from being conducted to the inner ear

Examples

Conductive hearing loss from bones
Surgery may sometimes repair conductive hearing loss if it is caused by damaged bones.
The doctor diagnosed her with conductive hearing loss after examining her middle ear.
Unlike sensorineural types, conductive hearing loss is often temporary and treatable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sensorineural hearing loss - hearing loss caused by damage to the inner ear or nerves
