deposit

verb
put money in a bank account
1.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɑːzɪt /; / dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to put money or valuables in a bank or safe place

Examples

To deposit money
Every Friday, she deposits a portion of her salary into her savings account.
He deposited the payment electronically to avoid going to the bank in person.
You can deposit cash or checks at any branch of your bank.
Every month, she deposits a portion of her salary into her savings account.
The museum deposited the ancient artifact in a secure vault for protection.
You can deposit your luggage at the hotel reception before check-in.

  • antonym: withdraw - to take money out of a bank account
