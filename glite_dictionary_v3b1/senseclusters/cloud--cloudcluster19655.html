cloud

verb
mark with streaks or blotches
5.6 3.6
Pronunciation: /klaʊd/ (AmE), /klaʊd/ (BrE)
to cover, obscure, or make something less clear, distinct, or bright (literally or figuratively)

Examples

To cloud the view
Moisture can cloud the photograph, leaving discoloration on the edges.
The bare wall was clouded by years of smoke from the fireplace.
The sky clouds with streaks of orange and pink at sunset.
Dust clouded into the sky as the car sped down the dirt road.
Steam clouded up from the cracked pipe in the winter air.
Thick smoke began to cloud above the burning warehouse.
Heavy smoke can cloud your vision after a fire.
Her judgment was clouded by emotion at the time.
Sadness began to cloud his usually bright expression.
