dump

verb
to drop or deposit carelessly
2.4 2.2
Pronunciation: / dʌmp / (AmE), / dʌmp / (BrE)
to drop, throw down, or get rid of something in a careless or abrupt way, especially as waste

Examples

To dump rubbish
She dumped all the clothes onto the bed without folding them.
The truck dumped its load of sand by the roadside.
Workers dumping bricks onto the ground caused a loud crash.
Factories used to dump waste into the river.
He dumped all the broken toys in the trash.
Please don't dump your old mattresses on the sidewalk.
The box slipped from her hands and dumped onto the floor.
The canoe suddenly dumped him into the freezing river.
When the shelf broke, all the books dumped onto the ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of something because it is no longer useful
  • synonym: drop - to fall or let something fall
  • antonym: keep - to retain or preserve something instead of throwing it away
  • synonym: plunge - to fall or move suddenly and quickly downwards
  • synonym: tip - to overturn or empty a container to let its contents fall out
