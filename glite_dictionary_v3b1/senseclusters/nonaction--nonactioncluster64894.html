nonaction

noun
lack of action
7.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /nə.ˈnæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /nə.ˈnæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of not doing anything or choosing not to act, especially when action is expected or needed

Examples

Government nonaction
The government’s nonaction in response to the crisis disappointed many citizens.
Her nonaction during the meeting was interpreted as silent agreement.
Prolonged nonaction can sometimes make a problem worse than taking the wrong action.
The company's nonaction in addressing the safety issue led to a costly lawsuit.
His repeated nonaction on important matters frustrated his colleagues.
In some cases, nonaction can be just as damaging as making the wrong decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inaction - failure to take any action
  • synonym: omission - the act of leaving something out or failing to do something
