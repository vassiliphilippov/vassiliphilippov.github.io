teetery

adjective
unsteady or wobbly
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːtəri / (AmE), / ˈtiːtəri / (BrE)
unsteady and likely to tip, wobble, or fall over

Examples

A teetery ladder
The ladder looked teetery, so I was afraid to climb it.
She carried the stack of books in a teetery pile across the room.
After the earthquake, the building stood in a teetery state, ready to collapse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • synonym: unstable - not firm or fixed; likely to move or fall
  • synonym: wobbly - moving unsteadily from side to side
