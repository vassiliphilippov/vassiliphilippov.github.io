dysregulation

noun
impaired regulation
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
abnormal or impaired control of a biological or psychological process, often leading to disease or dysfunction

Examples

Dysregulation of the immune system
Chronic stress can lead to dysregulation of the body's immune response.
Scientists are studying how dysregulation of hormones affects mental health.
The patient's symptoms were traced to dysregulation of blood sugar levels.
