pseudovariola

noun
mild form of smallpox
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˌvɛəriˈoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˌvæəriˈəʊlə / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent form of the virus.

Examples

Mild pseudovariola infection
During the outbreak, some patients contracted pseudovariola rather than the typical severe smallpox.
Researchers studied cases of pseudovariola to understand why the infection remained less severe.
The symptoms of pseudovariola are generally milder and less life-threatening than ordinary smallpox.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smallpox - an acute, contagious viral disease with fever and pustules, often fatal
