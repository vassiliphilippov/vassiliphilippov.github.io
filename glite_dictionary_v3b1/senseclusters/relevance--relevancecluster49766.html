relevance

noun
being connected to the subject
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈre.lə.vəns/ /; / /ˈre.lɪ.vəns/ (AmE), /ˈre.lə.vəns/ /; / /ˈre.lɪ.vəns/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being closely connected or appropriate to the matter at hand

Examples

Direct relevance
Her comments have direct relevance to the topic under discussion.
The evidence presented in court had no relevance to the defendant's actions.
You should always consider the relevance of each fact before drawing conclusions.
