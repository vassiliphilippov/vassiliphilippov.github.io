self-seeded

adjective
grown from naturally dispersed seed
2.8 5.2
Pronunciation: / sɛlfˈsiːdɪd / (AmE), / sɛlfˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing from seed that has naturally fallen or been dispersed without deliberate planting.

Examples

Self-seeded plants
Many wildflowers in the meadow are self-seeded and thrive with minimal intervention.
She was delighted to see self-seeded herbs sprouting up between the cracks in the pavement.
The self-seeded poppies returned every year without any help from the gardener.
