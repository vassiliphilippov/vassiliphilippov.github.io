non-normal

adjective
not commuting with adjoint
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / nɒnˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
in linear algebra, describing a matrix or operator that does not commute with its adjoint, often resulting in non-orthogonal eigenvectors

Examples

Non-normal matrix
Because the operator is non-normal, its eigenvectors are not guaranteed to be orthogonal.
The non-normal matrix presented unique challenges in the spectral analysis.
Researchers often encounter non-normal operators when studying complex systems in physics.
