leash

verb
to attach a strap to control
Pronunciation: / liːʃ / (AmE), / liːʃ / (BrE)
to attach a leash to an animal in order to control it

Examples

To leash a dog
You should always leash your dog when walking in busy areas.
The park requires owners to leash their pets at all times.
She quickly leashed her puppy before leaving the house.
You must leash your dog before entering the playground area.
The park ranger reminded visitors to always leash their pets for safety.
She quickly leashed her puppy when she saw another dog approaching.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restrain - to hold back or keep under control
  • antonym: unleash - to release from a leash or restraint
