lyricism

noun
suitability for singing; emotional expressiveness
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪrɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality of being suitable for singing, especially in expressing emotion through words or music

Examples

Musical lyricism
Her writing is celebrated for its musical lyricism and emotional depth.
Many composers value lyricism in operatic arias above technical complexity.
The lyricism of the poem made it feel as if it could be sung.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poetry - literature in verse; poems collectively
