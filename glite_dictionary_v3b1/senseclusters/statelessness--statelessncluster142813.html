statelessness

noun
lack of stored state
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsteɪt.ləs.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈsteɪt.ləs.nəs/ (BrE)
the property of a system, protocol, or service that does not retain information about previous interactions or sessions

Examples

Statelessness in servers
The statelessness of the web server allows it to handle many requests efficiently without tracking users.
RESTful APIs are designed with statelessness in mind to improve scalability and reliability.
Because of its statelessness, the application can be easily distributed across multiple servers.
