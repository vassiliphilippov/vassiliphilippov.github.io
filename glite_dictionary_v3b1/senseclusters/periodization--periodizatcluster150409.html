periodization

noun
dividing into periods
8.3
Pronunciation: /pɪr.ɪ.ə.daɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pɪə.rɪ.ə.daɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pɪə.rɪ.ə.daɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pɪr.ɪ.ə.daɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of dividing history, art, literature, or other fields into distinct time periods for study or classification

Examples

Periodization of history
The periodization of ancient history helps students understand major cultural changes over time.
Debates about periodization often arise when scholars disagree on when an era begins or ends.
In art history, periodization allows experts to group works by style and influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronology - the arrangement of events in the order they occurred
  • synonym: classification - the act of organizing things into categories
