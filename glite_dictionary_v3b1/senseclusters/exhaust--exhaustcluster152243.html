exhaust

verb
use up completely (resources)
4.4 3.1
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɔst /; / ɪgˈzɔst / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːst /; / ɪgˈzɔːst / (BrE)
to use up or completely deplete a resource, supply, or material

Examples

To exhaust all options
If you exhaust all your resources now, you may have nothing left for an emergency.
The company managed to exhaust its budget halfway through the year.
They quickly exhausted the food supplies during the long winter.
Continuous droughts can exhaust the region's water supply, leading to severe shortages.
If we don't budget carefully, we may exhaust our savings before the end of the year.

You should rest before you exhaust all your strength on the hike.
