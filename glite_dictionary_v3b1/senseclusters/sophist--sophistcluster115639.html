sophist

noun
someone skilled in deceptive reasoning
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.fɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɒ.fɪst / (BrE)
a person who uses clever but misleading or fallacious reasoning, especially one skilled in elaborate or deceptive arguments

Examples

A sophist uses arguments
Ancient philosophers often criticized sophists for using rhetoric to deceive rather than to enlighten.
Her argument seemed clever at first, but it soon became clear she was a sophist relying on wordplay.
The politician was accused of being a sophist, twisting facts to win debates rather than seeking the truth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debater - a person who argues or discusses in formal debate
  • synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy
