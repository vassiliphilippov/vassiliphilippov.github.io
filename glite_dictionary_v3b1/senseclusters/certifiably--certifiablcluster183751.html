certifiably

adverb
completely; extremely
5.9
Pronunciation: / sərˈtɪfəbl̩i / (AmE), / səˈtɪfəbl̩i / (BrE)
used to emphasize that someone or something is completely or extremely a particular way, especially in a negative sense.

Examples

Certifiably crazy
He was certifiably exhausted after running the marathon in the heat.
Some people thought her plan was certifiably impossible to achieve.
The movie was certifiably hilarious, and the whole audience was laughing nonstop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolutely - completely; totally
  • antonym: partially - not completely; in part
  • synonym: utterly - completely; extremely
