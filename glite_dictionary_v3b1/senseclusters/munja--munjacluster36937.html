munja

noun
tough grass used for ropes and baskets
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌndʒə / (AmE), / ˈmʌndʒə / (BrE)
A tough Asiatic grass, especially Saccharum munja, whose stems are used for making ropes and baskets.

Examples

The munja grass
In some regions, munja is also used for making mats and thatching roofs.
Ropes made from munja are valued for their durability and strength.
The villagers gathered munja to weave strong baskets for the market.
