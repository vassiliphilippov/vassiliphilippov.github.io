primary tooth

noun
first set of temporary teeth in children
3.1 0.03/m

A tooth in young children that is part of the first set of temporary teeth and is eventually replaced by a permanent tooth.

Examples

A primary tooth in children
By age six, most children will begin to lose their primary teeth as permanent ones emerge.
Decay in a primary tooth can affect the health of developing permanent teeth.
Dentists recommend regular check-ups even when a child only has primary teeth.

Synonyms

  • deciduous tooth
    3.3 0.01/m
    deciduous tooth is a clinical term for a child's temporary tooth, while primary tooth names the same first set in dentistry.
  • baby tooth
    1.2 0.38/m
    baby tooth is the everyday, child-focused word people use, while primary tooth is the dental term for those temporary teeth.
  • milk tooth
    3.4 0.04/m
    milk tooth is a traditional or regional name for early baby teeth, whereas primary tooth is the standard dental term used in clinics.
