floater

noun
variable-rate debt instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtər / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtə / (BrE)
A debt instrument with a variable interest rate that is adjusted periodically, typically in relation to a benchmark such as the rate paid by Treasury bills.

Examples

Bond floater
Because a floater adjusts its rate periodically, it can offer more stability in a volatile market.
Many investors prefer to buy floaters to protect against rising interest rates.
The bank issued a new series of floaters linked to the three-month LIBOR rate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed-rate bond - a debt security with a constant interest rate
