dive

verb
drop or fall steeply
2.7
Pronunciation: / daɪv / (AmE), / daɪv / (BrE)
(of prices, values, or amounts) to fall or decrease quickly and steeply.

Examples

Prices dive fast
As soon as interest rates rose, house prices began to dive.
Tech stocks dived last week after disappointing earnings reports.
The value of the currency dived to a historic low overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plunge - to fall suddenly and steeply
  • antonym: soar - to rise rapidly and extremely
