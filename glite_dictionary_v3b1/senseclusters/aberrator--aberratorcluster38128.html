aberrator

noun
deviant person
Pronunciation: / ˌæbəˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who deviates from accepted beliefs, standards, or norms, especially in a noticeable or unusual way

Examples

An aberrator of norms
The artist was often labeled an aberrator for challenging traditional styles in her work.
In a society that values conformity, an aberrator may face criticism or misunderstanding.
He admired the aberrator who dared to question long-held assumptions in the field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - a person who behaves according to accepted standards
  • synonym: deviant - a person who differs from accepted social standards
  • synonym: eccentric - a person with unconventional or odd behavior
