finagle

verb
achieve by trickery or clever methods
8.3
Pronunciation: / fəˈneɪɡəl / (AmE), / fɪˈneɪɡəl / (BrE)
to obtain or achieve something by using dishonest or clever methods, especially by trickery or manipulation

Examples

To finagle a ticket
He tried to finagle his way into the sold-out concert without a ticket.
She managed to finagle a day off from work by swapping shifts with a colleague.
The student finagled an extension on his paper by claiming he was ill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or assets
