ground fire

noun
fire burning subsurface forest humus
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A fire in a forest that burns the layer of decaying organic matter (humus) below the surface, often without visible flames above ground.

Examples

A ground fire in forest
After the lightning strike, a ground fire smoldered unnoticed for days beneath the forest floor.
Scientists studied the effects of a ground fire on soil nutrients in the national park.
Unlike a surface blaze, a ground fire can be especially difficult to detect and extinguish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crown fire - a forest fire that advances through the tops of trees or shrubs
  • antonym: surface fire - a forest fire that burns only the surface litter and undergrowth
