teach

verb
accustom someone to an action or attitude
1.0 283/m
Pronunciation: / tit É / (AmE), / tiÀêt É / (BrE)
to give instruction or help someone learn something, including habits, skills, or knowledge

Examples

To teach new skills
It's important to teach pets not to jump on visitors.
Parents often teach their children to say 'please' and 'thank you' from a young age.
She patiently teaches her students to take responsibility for their work.
Many volunteers teach English as a second language around the world.
My grandfather used to teach me how to fish every summer.
She loves to teach young children how to read and write.

Synonyms

  • instruct
    2.6 7.55/m
    instruct gives formal, step-by-step directions for how to do something, while teach means helping someone learn skills or knowledge in general.
  • educate
    2.2 53/m
    educate teaches broad knowledge and skills over time in schools or life, while teach can be short or informal.
  • train
    1.0 70/m
    train focuses on repeated practice to build skills and habits, while teach can mean giving facts or brief instruction.
  • impart
    4.3 2.66/m
    impart gives knowledge or information in a one-way manner, while teach often involves guiding someone to learn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condition - to accustom someone gradually to a pattern of behavior, often by repeated reinforcement
  • synonym: instruct - to teach or train in a formal way
  • antonym: learn - to acquire knowledge or skill (as the receiver)
  • synonym: train - to teach someone the skills for a particular task or job
