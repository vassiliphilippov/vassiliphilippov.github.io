impracticable

adjective
not able to be done or used
6.4
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpræktɪkəbl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpræktɪkəbl / (BrE)
Impossible to carry out or put into practice.

Examples

An impracticable plan
Extending the policy to every department would be impracticable at the moment.
Rescuing the stranded hikers by helicopter became impracticable because of the severe weather conditions.
The plan to build a bridge across that section of river proved impracticable due to the strong currents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • antonym: practicable - able to be done or put into practice
