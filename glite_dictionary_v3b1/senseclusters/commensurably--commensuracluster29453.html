commensurably

adverb
in a proportional way
7.7
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmen.sə.rə.bliː/ /; / /kə.ˈmen.sər.ə.bliː/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmen.sə.rə.blɪ/ /; / /kə.ˈmen.sər.ə.blɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that is proportional or corresponds in amount, size, or degree

Examples

Increased commensurably with profits
The bonus was increased commensurably with the company's profits this year.
If the workload rises, your responsibilities will grow commensurably.
The two teams' efforts were rewarded commensurably based on their contributions.
The employees' salaries increased commensurably with their added responsibilities.
As the workload grew, the resources were allocated commensurably to each department.
The risks and rewards in this investment are balanced commensurably.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disproportionately - in a way that is not in correct relation in size or amount
  • antonym: incommensurably - in a way that cannot be measured by the same standard
  • synonym: proportionally - in a way that corresponds in size or amount
