unhoused

adjective
not enclosed
7.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhaʊzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈhaʊzd / (BrE)
not placed in a housing, case, or protective enclosure; not enclosed or installed, especially referring to objects or equipment

Examples

Unhoused equipment
The delicate instrument was left unhoused on the workbench during repairs.
Leaving electrical wires unhoused can be dangerous in a busy workshop.
The engine parts remained unhoused until the new casing arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposed - not covered or protected
  • antonym: housed - placed in a housing or protective enclosure
