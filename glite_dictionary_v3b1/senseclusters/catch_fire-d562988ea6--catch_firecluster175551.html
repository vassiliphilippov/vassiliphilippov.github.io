catch fire

verb
begin to burn
2.1
Pronunciation: / kætʃ ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / kætʃ ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to start burning or burst into flames suddenly

Examples

Catch fire quickly
Be careful not to let the paper catch fire in the fireplace.
The curtains began to catch fire after being too close to the candle.
The old barn caught fire during the thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning
  • synonym: ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
