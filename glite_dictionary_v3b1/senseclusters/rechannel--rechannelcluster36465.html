rechannel

verb
to change signal frequency
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtʃænəl / (BrE)
to change the channel or frequency used for transmitting or receiving signals or broadcasts

Examples

To rechannel a signal
The technician had to rechannel the radio to avoid static from nearby equipment.
After the update, users needed to rechannel their devices for better reception.
Engineers decided to rechannel the broadcast to a less crowded frequency band.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interfere - to disrupt or block a signal
  • synonym: retune - to adjust to a different frequency or channel
