snob

noun
person who thinks they are superior to others
5.1 2.97/m
Pronunciation: / snÉ‘b / (AmE), / snÉ’b / (BrE)
A person who believes they are superior to others, especially because of their social status, education, or taste, and often looks down on people they consider inferior.

Examples

A snob person
Although he was wealthy, he was never a snob and treated everyone with respect.
Nobody wanted to sit next to the snob who kept bragging about his expensive car.
She acts like a snob and refuses to talk to people who don't share her interests.

Synonyms

  • toffee-nose
    9.9 0.00/m
    a toffee-nose acts pretentious and upper-class in style, while a snob judges others for social status, education, or taste.
  • snoot
    9.4 0.02/m
    a snoot behaves in a cold, annoying way with others, while a snob thinks they are better because of status or taste.
  • elitist
    6.8 1.51/m
    an elitist believes certain groups should have more power and privilege, while a snob mainly feels superior in taste or social rank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elitist - a person who believes society should be led by an elite
  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance
