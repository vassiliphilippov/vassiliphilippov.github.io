blood coagulation

noun
process of blood clotting
2.5
Pronunciation: / blʌd koʊˌæɡjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / blʌd kəʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which liquid blood changes into a semisolid mass, forming a clot

Examples

Blood coagulation process
A deficiency in vitamin K can impair blood coagulation and lead to excessive bleeding.
Some medications are prescribed to slow blood coagulation in patients at risk for blood clots.
The laboratory ran several tests to evaluate the speed of blood coagulation in the patient.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bleeding - the loss of blood from the circulatory system
  • synonym: clotting - the process by which blood forms clots
