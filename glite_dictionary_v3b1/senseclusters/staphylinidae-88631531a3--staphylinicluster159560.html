Staphylinidae

noun
family of rove beetles
Pronunciation: / stæfəˌlɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / stæfɪˈlɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles, commonly known as rove beetles, characterized by their short elytra and flexible abdomens.

Examples

The Staphylinidae beetles
Entomologists often study Staphylinidae to understand ecological diversity and predator-prey relationships.
Many members of the Staphylinidae can be found under logs and leaf litter, where they hunt for small invertebrates.
The Staphylinidae are among the largest families of beetles, with thousands of described species worldwide.
