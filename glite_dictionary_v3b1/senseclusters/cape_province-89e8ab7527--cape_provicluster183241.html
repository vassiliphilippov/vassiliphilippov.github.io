Cape Province

proper noun
former province in southern South Africa
Pronunciation: / keɪp ˈprɑːvɪns / (AmE), / keɪp ˈprɒvɪns / (BrE)
a former province in southern South Africa, settled by the Dutch in 1652, later ceded to Britain, and split into new provinces in 1994

Examples

The Cape Province region
After 1994, the Cape Province was divided into several smaller provinces.
Many families in the Cape Province trace their ancestry to Dutch settlers.
The Cape Province played a significant role in South African history during colonial times.
