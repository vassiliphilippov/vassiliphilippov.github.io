flashforward

noun
future scene in story
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌfɔːwəd / (BrE)
a scene in a story that temporarily moves ahead to show events that will happen in the future

Examples

A movie flashforward
The film opens with a flashforward that hints at the dramatic ending.
Writers sometimes use a flashforward to build suspense about what will happen to the characters.
A sudden flashforward in the novel revealed the consequences of the hero's decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flashback - a scene that shows events from the past in a story
