brrr

interjection
shiver exclamation
Pronunciation: / bɜr / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
an exclamation used to show that you feel cold or are shivering (sometimes also from fear, unease, or disgust)

Examples

brrr cold wind
When the icy wind blew through the window, she shivered and said, "brrr, it's freezing in here!"
He whispered, "brrr, that horror movie gave me chills down my spine."
Walking alone at night past the old cemetery, I couldn't help but mutter, "brrr, this place is creepy."
When he stepped outside into the snow, he shivered and said, "brrr, it's freezing out here!"
She wrinkled her nose and exclaimed, "brrr, what a horrible smell coming from the fridge!"
After jumping into the cold lake, he gasped, "brrr, that water is icy!"
He stepped outside and immediately exclaimed, "brrr, it's colder than I expected!"
After jumping into the icy lake, she shouted, "brrr! That water is freezing!"
The wind blew through the open window, making everyone say, "brrr, close it please!"
