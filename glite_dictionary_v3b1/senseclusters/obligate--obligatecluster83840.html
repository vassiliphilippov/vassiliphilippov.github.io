obligate

adjective
restricted to a particular life condition (biology)
6.4 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.blə.ɡeɪt/ /; / /ˈɑː.blɪ.ɡət/ (AmE), /ˈɒb.lɪ.ɡət/ (BrE)
(of an organism) restricted to a particular condition of life and unable to survive or function elsewhere.

Examples

Obligate parasite
An obligate parasite cannot complete its life cycle without exploiting a suitable host.
Some fungi are obligate symbionts, forming essential relationships with plants.
The bacterium is an obligate aerobe, requiring oxygen to survive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: facultative - able to live under varying conditions (biology)
