New Zealand wren

noun
small New Zealand bird, resembles a wren
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈzɪl.ənd rɛn / (AmE), / njuː ˈzɪl.ənd rɛn / (BrE)
a small bird of New Zealand belonging to the family Acanthisittidae, which superficially resembles true wrens but is not closely related to them

Examples

The New Zealand wren bird
Researchers are studying the adaptation of the New Zealand wren to its isolated environment.
The New Zealand wren is one of the most distinctive bird species found in the region.
Unlike the true wrens, the New Zealand wren belongs to a completely different bird family.
