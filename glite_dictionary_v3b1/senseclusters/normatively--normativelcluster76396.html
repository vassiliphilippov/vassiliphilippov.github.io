normatively

adverb
according to norms
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔːrmətɪvli / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on what is considered normal, standard, or typical.

Examples

Normatively expected time
University programs are normatively completed in four years, but some students take longer.
The law was interpreted normatively, reflecting what is generally accepted in society.
Children are normatively expected to start school at the age of five.

Related Terms

  • synonym: typically - in a way that is usual or expected
  • antonym: unusually - in a way that is not typical or expected
