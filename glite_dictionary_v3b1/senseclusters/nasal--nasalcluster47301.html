nasal

noun
consonant pronounced through the nose
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪ.zəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.zəl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced by allowing air to escape through the nose while keeping the mouth closed.

Examples

Nasal consonant sound
In English, the sounds represented by 'm', 'n', and 'ng' are all nasal consonants.
The linguist explained why some languages have more nasals than others.
When he had a cold, even his normal speech sounded like a nasal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oral - a speech sound made through the mouth without nasal passage of air
