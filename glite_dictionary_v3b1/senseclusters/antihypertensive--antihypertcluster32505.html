antihypertensive

noun
drug that lowers blood pressure
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˌhaɪ.pɚˈtɛn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌhaɪ.pəˈtɛn.sɪv / (BrE)
a drug used to reduce or control high blood pressure

Examples

An antihypertensive drug
Her doctor prescribed an antihypertensive to help lower her blood pressure.
Some antihypertensives may cause side effects, so it's important to find the right medication.
Taking an antihypertensive regularly can reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke.
