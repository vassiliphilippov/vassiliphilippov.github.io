reinjury

noun
repeat injury
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪndʒəri / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
an injury that happens again in the same place or in the same way after recovering from a previous injury

Examples

Avoid reinjury after recovery
The athlete took extra precautions to avoid reinjury after returning to the field.
Physical therapy can help reduce the risk of reinjury following a serious accident.
Doctors warned that a reinjury could delay his full recovery by several months.
After months of recovery, the athlete feared a reinjury during his first game back.
Proper warm-up exercises can help prevent reinjury to previously damaged muscles.
Ignoring medical advice increases the risk of reinjury after an initial accident.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: injury - physical harm or damage to the body
  • synonym: relapse - a return of a previous condition or illness
