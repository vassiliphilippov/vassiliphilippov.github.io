cavity

noun
decayed area in a tooth
Pronunciation: / ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈkævəti / (BrE)
A soft, decayed area in a tooth caused by progressive tooth decay.

Examples

Tooth cavity
He needed a filling to repair the cavity in his tooth.
If left untreated, a cavity can cause severe tooth pain and infection.
The dentist found a cavity in my molar during the check-up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caries - decay and crumbling of a tooth or bone
