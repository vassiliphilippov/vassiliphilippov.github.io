electrodynamic

adjective
using electromagnetic forces
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the forces or effects produced by moving electric charges or electric currents, or to devices and actions involving these forces

Examples

Electrodynamic braking system
The new train uses an electrodynamic braking system to slow down smoothly and efficiently.
Researchers studied the electrodynamic properties of the material to improve its performance in motors.
An electrodynamic microphone converts sound into an electrical signal using electromagnetic principles.
The electrodynamic forces between the particles were measured in the laboratory.
Researchers developed an electrodynamic model to explain the behavior of the plasma.
Understanding electrodynamic interactions is essential for designing advanced electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic - relating to the interaction of electric and magnetic fields
  • antonym: electrostatic - relating to stationary electric charges
  • antonym: mechanical - relating to machines or physical movement, not involving electricity
