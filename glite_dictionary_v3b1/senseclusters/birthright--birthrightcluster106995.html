birthright

noun
right or privilege by birth
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrθˌraɪt /; / ˈbɜrˌθraɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθˌraɪt /; / ˈbɜːθraɪt / (BrE)
a right, privilege, or possession that someone is entitled to from birth, either by family or by being born into a society

Examples

The birthright of inheritance
As the eldest son, the birthright of the family estate belonged to him.
He considered freedom of expression to be his birthright.
They believed education was a basic birthright of every citizen.
Education is considered a birthright in many modern societies.
Freedom of speech is seen as a basic birthright in democratic countries.
Many people fought to secure civil liberties as their birthright.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inheritance - money, property, or title received as an heir at someone's death
  • synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage granted to a person or group
