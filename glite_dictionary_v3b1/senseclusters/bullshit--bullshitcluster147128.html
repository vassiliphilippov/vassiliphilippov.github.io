bullshit

verb
talk nonsense or insincerely
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbʊl.ʃɪt/ /; / /ˈbʊl.ʃət/ (AmE), /ˈbʊl.ʃɪt/ /; / /ˈbʊl.ʃət/ (BrE)
to talk nonsense, especially by speaking insincerely or without regard for the truth, often with the intention to deceive or impress

Examples

To bullshit a teacher
He tried to bullshit the professor when he hadn't read the book, but it didn't work.
Stop bullshitting and tell me what really happened.
You can't just bullshit your way through the interview; they'll see right through you.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluff - to mislead or deceive, especially by a false display of confidence
  • antonym: come clean - to be honest or tell the truth
  • synonym: lie - to make a false statement with deliberate intent to deceive
