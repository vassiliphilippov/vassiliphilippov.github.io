Rex

noun
a male king or sovereign
Pronunciation: / rɛks / (AmE), / rɛks / (BrE)
a king; a male sovereign or ruler of a kingdom, especially as used in legal, historical, or formal contexts

Examples

King Rex
In British law, the Crown is sometimes referred to as Rex or Regina, depending on the monarch's gender.
The title Rex was often inscribed on the coins of ancient Rome.
The tomb bore the inscription 'Here lies Rex Arthurus', honoring the legendary king.

Related Terms

  • synonym: king - male ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth
  • antonym: queen - female sovereign; ruler of a kingdom
