barnacled

adjective
encrusted or aged
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrnəkəld / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnəkəld / (BrE)
covered with barnacles or, figuratively, encrusted or aged with layers over time

Examples

Barnacled boat hull
The hull of the abandoned boat was barnacled after years in the harbor.
His barnacled desk was piled high with old papers and forgotten trinkets.
The ancient statue stood barnacled with moss and lichen in the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encrusted - covered with a hard layer or crust
  • antonym: pristine - in its original, clean and unspoiled condition
