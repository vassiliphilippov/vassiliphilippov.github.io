plaster

verb
cover thickly with a substance
2.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈplæs.tər/ (AmE), /ˈplɑː.stə/ (BrE)
to cover a surface thickly or completely with a substance, or by sticking things all over it

Examples

To plaster bread with butter
He plastered his bread with butter, hardly leaving any room for the jam.
She plastered her face with sunscreen before going out.
The walls were plastered with posters for the concert.
After the concert, fans plastered the city with photos of the band.
She plastered warning posters all over the school entrance.
The protesters plastered the walls with slogans and flyers.
After the show, the band plastered the city with their colorful flyers.
He plastered his bedroom ceiling with glow-in-the-dark stars.
Political activists plastered the campus walls with protest posters overnight.
