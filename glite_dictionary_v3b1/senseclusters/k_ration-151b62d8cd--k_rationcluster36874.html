K ration

noun
emergency military food package (WWII, US)
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ ˌreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ ˌreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a small package of emergency food rations issued to United States troops, especially during World War II

Examples

A K ration package
Each K ration included canned meat, biscuits, and a small chocolate bar.
My grandfather kept an unused K ration as a memento from his service in World War II.
The soldiers relied on their K ration during long patrols in the field.
