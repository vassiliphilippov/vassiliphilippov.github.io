cacophony

noun
harsh, discordant sounds
Pronunciation: / kəˈkɑfəni / (AmE), / kəˈkɒfəni / (BrE)
a harsh, discordant mixture of loud and unpleasant sounds

Examples

A cacophony of sounds
As the orchestra tuned their instruments, a cacophony erupted in the concert hall.
Birds and engines created a cacophony outside my window early in the morning.
The construction site was filled with a cacophony of horns, drills, and shouting workers.
A cacophony erupted from the playground as the children rushed outside.
The cacophony of car horns and shouting filled the city street.
We were startled by the cacophony coming from the construction site next door.

Related Terms

  • synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • synonym: dissonance - lack of harmony among musical notes; jarring sound
  • antonym: harmony - a pleasing combination of musical notes
  • synonym: racket - a loud and disturbing noise
