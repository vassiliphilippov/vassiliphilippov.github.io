yield

verb
produce or provide something
3.6 28/m
Pronunciation: / jiÀêld / (AmE), / jiÀêld / (BrE)
to produce, provide, or result in something such as a profit, result, or product

Examples

To yield results
Careful analysis can yield valuable insights into the problem.
The experiment yielded surprising results that changed the direction of the research.
This investment is expected to yield higher profits next year.
The experiment yielded fascinating results that surprised the researchers.
The investment is expected to yield a substantial profit within a year.
This land regularly yields a rich harvest of wheat every autumn.
His efforts finally yielded a breakthrough in the research project.

The investigation yielded no new evidence for the case.

Synonyms

  • return
    3.6 5.59/m
    return is what an investment produces as profit or interest, while yield is the general amount produced over time.
  • produce
    2.0 143/m
    produce makes or creates things actively, while yield often means what something provides or results in without focusing on making.
  • generate
    3.2 61/m
    generate creates or brings into being as a result of a process, while yield names what is produced or given.
  • give
    1.0 371/m
    give provides or hands over things, feelings, or results directly, while yield usually describes what something produces as output.
  • turn out
    3.5 1.23/m
    turn out produces goods quickly and in large amounts, while yield names what a process or farm gives back.
  • pay
    5.2 58/m
    pay returns a set amount of money from an investment, while yield describes the overall amount or result produced.
  • bear
    8.2 6.91/m
    bear produces interest or returns for an account over time, while yield is the general term for what something produces.
