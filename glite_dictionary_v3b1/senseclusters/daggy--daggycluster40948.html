daggy

adjective
unfashionably endearing
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɡi / (BrE)
unfashionable, uncool, or socially awkward in appearance or style, sometimes in an amusing or endearing way

Examples

Daggy clothes
His daggy sweater made everyone smile at the party.
She has a daggy sense of humor that makes her very likable.
They love his daggy dance moves, even if they are not stylish.
He wore a daggy sweater to the party and didn't seem to care.
Her friends teased her about her daggy shoes, but she liked them anyway.
The movie's costumes looked a bit daggy compared to today's fashion.
