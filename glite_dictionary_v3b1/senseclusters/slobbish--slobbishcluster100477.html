slobbish

adjective
rude or coarse
Pronunciation: / ˈslɑːbɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈslɒbɪʃ / (BrE)
rude, coarse, or lacking refinement in manners or taste; behaving in an uncivilized way.

Examples

Slobbish manners
His slobbish behavior at the party made everyone uncomfortable.
She disliked her roommate's slobbish habits, especially leaving dirty dishes everywhere.
The manager warned the staff not to act in a slobbish manner in front of customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refined - showing good manners and elegance
  • synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners or refinement
