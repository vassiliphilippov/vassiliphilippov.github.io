cross vine

noun
woody flowering vine of the southern U.S.
Pronunciation: / krɔs vaɪn / (AmE), / krɒs vaɪn / (BrE)
A woody flowering vine native to the southern United States, notable for stems that show a cross-shaped pattern in cross-section.

Examples

A cross vine plant
Gardeners in the South often plant cross vine on trellises for its ornamental value.
The cross vine produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers in the spring.
When cut, the stem of a cross vine reveals a unique cross-shaped pattern.
