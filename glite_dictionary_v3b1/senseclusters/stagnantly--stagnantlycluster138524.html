stagnantly

adverb
without movement
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡnəntli / (AmE), / ˈstæɡnəntli / (BrE)
in a way that is motionless, stale, or lacking movement or freshness, especially of air or water

Examples

Air hangs stagnantly
The humid air hung stagnantly in the small, windowless room all afternoon.
After the rain stopped, water collected stagnantly in the ditch, attracting mosquitoes.
The conversation moved stagnantly, with no one offering new ideas or suggestions.
The air hung stagnantly in the small, windowless room.
Economic growth proceeded stagnantly for several years after the crisis.
The river moved stagnantly through the marsh, barely making a sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actively - in a way that involves movement or activity
  • antonym: freshly - in a way that is new, clean, or full of life
  • synonym: motionlessly - in a way that does not move
