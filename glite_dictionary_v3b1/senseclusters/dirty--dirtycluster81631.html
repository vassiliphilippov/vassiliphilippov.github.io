dirty

adjective
manuscript with many corrections or alterations
6.4 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ùti / (AmE), / Ààd…úÀêti / (BrE)
(of a manuscript or document) marked or defaced with numerous corrections, edits, or alterations.

Examples

Dirty copy
After several rounds of revisions, the dirty copy was almost unreadable.
Please send me a dirty manuscript so I can check your suggested edits.
The editor returned the dirty draft covered in red ink corrections.

Synonyms

  • marked-up
    5.6 0.10/m
    marked-up shows clear edits, notes, and corrections on the page, while dirty suggests a messy, defaced manuscript.
  • foul
    8.1 0.09/m
    foul looks damaged and unfinished from heavy corrections and erasures, while dirty describes a manuscript marked or defaced.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean - (of a manuscript) free from corrections or defacements
  • synonym: foul - (of a manuscript) similarly marked with corrections or edits
