liquidus

adjective
in liquid state
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdəs / (BrE)
in or of a liquid state, especially referring to a substance that is molten or melted.

Examples

Liquidus metal
The metal becomes liquidus at temperatures above its melting point.
Scientists observed the liquidus phase during the experiment on volcanic rocks.
Above the liquidus boundary, the rock existed entirely in molten form inside the shallow magma chamber.

Related Terms

  • synonym: molten - in a melted or liquid state due to heat
  • antonym: solidus - in a solid state; not melted
