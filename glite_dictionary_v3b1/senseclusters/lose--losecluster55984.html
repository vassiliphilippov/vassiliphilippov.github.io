lose

verb
become absorbed and unaware of reality
5.5 21/m
Pronunciation: / luÀêz / (AmE), / luÀêz / (BrE)
(of a person) to become so absorbed in something that one is unaware of one's surroundings or reality.

Examples

To lose yourself in music
He sometimes loses himself in thought during long car journeys.
She would often lose herself in the pages of a good novel for hours.
They lost themselves in the beauty of the mountain landscape, forgetting their worries.

Synonyms

  • lose oneself
    5.1 0.98/m
    lose oneself means becoming deeply absorbed in something and unaware of the surroundings, while lose names that same state.
  • lost in thought
    4.2 0.89/m
    lost in thought describes being deeply occupied by your thoughts and not noticing things, while lose is more general.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immerse - to become completely involved in something
  • synonym: withdraw - to remove oneself from reality or surroundings
