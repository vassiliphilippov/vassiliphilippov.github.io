dodgy

adjective
deceitful; skilled at deception
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑdʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒi / (BrE)
clever in a dishonest or evasive way; skillful at deception or trickery.

Examples

Dodgy business deal
He came up with a dodgy plan to avoid paying taxes.
People avoid buying from that shop because it has a dodgy reputation.
The lawyer's dodgy tactics helped him win the case but raised ethical questions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cunning - clever at being deceitful or achieving one's ends by trickery
  • antonym: honest - free from deceit or fraud
  • synonym: wily - skilled at gaining an advantage by deceit
