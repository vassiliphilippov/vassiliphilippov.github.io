ultrastructure

noun
microscopic cell details
Pronunciation: / ˈʌltrəˌstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəˌstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the detailed structure of a cell, tissue, or material that can only be seen with an electron microscope or other high-resolution imaging

Examples

Cell ultrastructure
Scientists studied the ultrastructure of the virus to understand how it infects cells.
The ultrastructure of muscle tissue reveals details not visible with a light microscope.
Advances in technology have made it easier to observe the ultrastructure of plant cells.
Scientists used electron microscopy to study the ultrastructure of the virus particles.
The ultrastructure of plant cells reveals details not visible with standard microscopes.
Understanding the ultrastructure of bone tissue helps researchers develop better treatments for injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microstructure - the structure of a material as seen under a microscope
  • degree lower: structure - the arrangement of parts in something
