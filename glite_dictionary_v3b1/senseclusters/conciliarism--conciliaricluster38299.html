conciliarism

noun
church council movement
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪliərɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈsɪliərɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief, principle, or historical movement that church councils have more authority than the pope, especially in the Roman Catholic Church during the Middle Ages
