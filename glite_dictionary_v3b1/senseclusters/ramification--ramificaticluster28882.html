ramification

noun
complicating consequence or result
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌræməfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a complex or unwelcome consequence of an action or decision, especially one that makes a situation more complicated

Examples

Serious ramification
Political leaders are worried about the economic ramifications of the trade sanctions.
She did not consider the possible ramifications of her decision before acting.
The new law could have serious ramifications for small businesses across the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cause - a reason for an action or condition; the opposite of a result
  • synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • synonym: result - something that happens because of something else
