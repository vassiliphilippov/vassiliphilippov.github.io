subpar

adjective
below face value
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈpɑr / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈpɑː / (BrE)
trading below its nominal or face value, especially referring to a bond or financial instrument

Examples

Subpar bond
The bond was considered subpar because it was selling for less than its face value.
Investors sometimes seek subpar securities, hoping their prices will rise in the future.
When the market declined, many financial instruments became subpar overnight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: at par - trading at its nominal or face value
  • synonym: discounted - sold for less than its nominal or original value
  • antonym: premium - trading above its nominal or face value
