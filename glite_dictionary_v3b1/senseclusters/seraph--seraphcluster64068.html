seraph

noun
highest order of angel
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛr.əf / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.əf / (BrE)
An angel of the highest order, often depicted in Christian theology as having three pairs of wings.

Examples

A seraph angel
He studied the painting carefully, noting the delicate features of the seraph.
In religious art, a seraph is often shown with six wings and a glowing presence.
The ancient texts describe the seraph as attending to the throne of God.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angel - a spiritual being acting as a messenger of God
  • degree lower: cherub - an angel of a lower order, often depicted as a chubby child with wings
