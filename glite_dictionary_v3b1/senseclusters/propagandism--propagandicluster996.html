propagandism

noun
spreading propaganda
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːpəˈɡændɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈɡændɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or advocacy of spreading propaganda to influence people's opinions or beliefs

Examples

Propagandism in politics
The government was accused of propagandism during the election campaign.
Critics argued that the film was an example of blatant propagandism rather than objective reporting.
Schools should avoid propagandism and encourage students to think independently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectivity - the quality of being unbiased and impartial
  • synonym: propaganda - information, often biased, used to promote a particular cause or view
