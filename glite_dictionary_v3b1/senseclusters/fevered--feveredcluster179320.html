fevered

adjective
highly excited or agitated
5.3 0.64/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfiÀêv…ôrd / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀêv…ôd / (BrE)
highly excited, agitated, or influenced by intense emotion

Examples

Fevered imagination
After hearing the news, his fevered mind could not rest for hours.
Her fevered imagination led her to dream up elaborate stories.
The team worked through the night in a fevered rush to meet the deadline.

Synonyms

    3.8 1.17/m

  • frenzied
    6.5 2.24/m

  • excited
    1.0 40/m
    excited is eager and happy about something, while fevered means intensely stirred by emotion and may be less controlled.
  • agitated
    4.1 2.20/m

  • heated
    4.2 0.01/m
    heated describes angry or intense discussions or arguments, while fevered describes a person's strong, often uncontrolled emotional state.
