wrench

verb
twist and sprain part of the body
Pronunciation: / rɛntʃ / (AmE), / rɛntʃ / (BrE)
to twist or contort a part of the body suddenly and forcibly, often causing pain or as a result of strong emotion

Examples

To wrench an ankle
He wrenched his ankle while playing soccer and had to sit out the rest of the game.
If you wrench your knee during the hike, you should stop and rest immediately.
She accidentally wrenched her wrist when she slipped on the icy pavement.
He wrenched his hands in agony as he received the terrible news.
Her face wrenched with pain when she heard the diagnosis.
The child wrenched his fingers together anxiously while waiting for the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sprain - to injure a joint by a sudden twist
  • synonym: twist - to turn something sharply or forcibly
  • synonym: wring - to twist and squeeze something
