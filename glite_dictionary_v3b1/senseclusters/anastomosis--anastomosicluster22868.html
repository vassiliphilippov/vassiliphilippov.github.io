A natural or surgical connection between two tubular structures, such as blood vessels or loops of intestine, that allows flow between them.

Examples

A anastomosis between vessels
A vascular anastomosis can restore blood flow after an artery has been damaged.
Abnormal anastomosis may sometimes occur naturally between veins.
The surgeon created an anastomosis to connect the two sections of the intestine.
