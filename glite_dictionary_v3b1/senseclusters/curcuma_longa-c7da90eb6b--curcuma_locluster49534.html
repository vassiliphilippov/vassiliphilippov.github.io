Curcuma longa

noun
tropical plant; source of turmeric
9.1
Pronunciation: / kɝːˈkuːmə ˈlɔŋɡə / (AmE), / kɜːˈkjuːmə ˈlɒŋɡə / (BrE)
a tropical plant of the ginger family, native to India, with yellow flowers and an aromatic yellow rhizome used primarily as the source of turmeric, a spice and yellow dye

Examples

Curcuma longa plant
Curcuma longa is cultivated extensively in India for its valuable rhizomes, which yield turmeric.
Scientists have studied the medicinal properties of Curcuma longa for centuries.
The roots of Curcuma longa are ground to produce the bright yellow spice used in cooking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turmeric - the yellow spice made from Curcuma longa rhizome
