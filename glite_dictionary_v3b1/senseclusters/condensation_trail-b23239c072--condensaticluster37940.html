condensation trail

noun
cloudlike streak left by an aircraft
2.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /kən.den.ˈseɪ.ʃən treɪl/ /; / /kɑːn.den.ˈseɪ.ʃən treɪl/ (AmE), /kən.den.ˈseɪ.ʃən treɪl/ /; / /kɒn.den.ˈseɪ.ʃən treɪl/ (BrE)
A visible cloud-like streak in the sky formed by water vapor from an aircraft's engine exhaust or by air pressure changes around the aircraft, usually at high altitudes.

Examples

An aircraft condensation trail
Pilots often see their own condensation trails while cruising at high altitudes.
Scientists study condensation trails to better understand their impact on climate.
The jet left a long condensation trail that stretched across the blue sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrail - abbreviation for condensation trail; artificial cloud behind aircraft
