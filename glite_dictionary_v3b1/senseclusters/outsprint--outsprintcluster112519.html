outsprint

verb
to move faster than another
4.8
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈsprɪnt/ /; / /ˈaʊt.sprɪnt/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈsprɪnt/ /; / /ˈaʊt.sprɪnt/ (BrE)
to move or act faster than someone else, especially to gain an advantage in a competitive situation

Examples

To outsprint competitors
The new company managed to outsprint its competitors by releasing updates more quickly.
In the final moments of the debate, she outsprinted her opponent with a sharp response.
If we want to win this contract, we must outsprint the other bidders in submitting our proposal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lag - to move or progress more slowly than others
  • synonym: outpace - to move or develop faster than someone or something else
  • synonym: overtake - to catch up with and pass someone ahead
