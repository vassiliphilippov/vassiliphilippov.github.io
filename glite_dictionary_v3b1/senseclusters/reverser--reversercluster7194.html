reverser

noun
direction-changing device
7.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrsər / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsə / (BrE)
a device or mechanism that makes something move or operate in the opposite direction (such as in vehicles, machinery, or electrical circuits)

Examples

The train's reverser allows the driver to change the direction of travel safely.
After landing, the pilot activated the thrust reverser to help slow down the aircraft.
Engineers inspected the reverser to ensure it was functioning correctly before the machine was used.
The aircraft's reverser is activated to help slow it down after landing.
A train engineer used the reverser to change the direction of the locomotive.
Some machines are equipped with a reverser to allow for backward movement when needed.
The technician installed a reverser to allow the motor to run in both directions.
A faulty reverser can cause the machine to operate incorrectly or not at all.
Modern trains use an electronic reverser to change the direction of travel safely.
