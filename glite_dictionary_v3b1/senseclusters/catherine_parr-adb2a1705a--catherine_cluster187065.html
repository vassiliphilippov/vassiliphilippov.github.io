Catherine Parr

proper noun
sixth wife of Henry VIII; Queen of England
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθərɪn pɑr / (AmE), / ˈkæθərɪn pɑː / (BrE)
(1512–1548), Queen of England, the sixth and last wife of Henry VIII, noted for her influence at court and her role in the English Reformation.

Examples

Catherine Parr queen of England
Catherine Parr was instrumental in the education of Henry VIII’s children.
After the death of Henry VIII, Catherine Parr married Thomas Seymour.
Historians recognize Catherine Parr as a key figure in supporting religious reform in England.
