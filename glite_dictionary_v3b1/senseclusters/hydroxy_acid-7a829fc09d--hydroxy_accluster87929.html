hydroxy acid

noun
acid with extra hydroxyl groups
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑksi ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksi ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a type of acid that contains one or more hydroxyl groups in addition to the one present in the acid group

Examples

Alpha-hydroxy acid
Alpha-hydroxy acids are a common type of hydroxy acid used in skincare products.
Citric acid is an example of a naturally occurring hydroxy acid found in citrus fruits.
The chemist explained how a hydroxy acid differs from a regular carboxylic acid.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: carboxylic acid - organic acids with a carboxyl group
  • degree higher: polyhydroxy acid - an acid with multiple hydroxyl groups
