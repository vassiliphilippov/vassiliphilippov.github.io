orchestral bells

noun
percussion instrument of metal bars
4.9
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈkɛstrəl bɛlz / (AmE), / ɔːˈkɛstrəl bɛlz / (BrE)
a percussion instrument composed of metal bars of varying lengths arranged on a frame and played with small hammers, producing a clear, bell-like sound

Examples

The orchestral bells sound
Children were fascinated by the shimmering sound of the orchestral bells at the concert.
Composers often use orchestral bells to add bright, ethereal tones to orchestral music.
The percussionist carefully played the orchestral bells during the symphony’s delicate passage.
