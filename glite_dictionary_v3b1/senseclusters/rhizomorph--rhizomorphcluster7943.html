rhizomorph

noun
root-like fungal structure
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪzəˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈrʌɪzəmɔːf / (BrE)
A dense, root-like mass of fungal hyphae that resembles a root and is characteristic of certain fungi.

Examples

A rhizomorph network
Certain pathogenic fungi use rhizomorph networks to colonize new areas.
Researchers observed several dark rhizomorphs extending from the decomposing log.
The fungus developed rhizomorphs to spread underneath the bark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mycelium - the network of hyphae forming the vegetative part of a fungus
