bow down

verb
lower the body or head as a sign of respect, etc.
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: / baʊ daʊn / (AmE), / baʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to bend your body or lower your head, or to show respect, submission, or obedience (physically or figuratively)

Examples

To bow down respectfully
Everyone in the hall bowed down as the emperor entered.
She bowed down her head in front of the audience, showing her gratitude.
You should never feel the need to bow down to unreasonable demands.
Many people bow down to tradition, even when they don't agree with it.
She refused to bow down to pressure from her peers.
The servants were required to bow down before the king when entering the throne room.
In the face of overwhelming evidence, the company had to bow down to public pressure.
She refused to bow down to his unreasonable demands.
Some traditions require children to bow down before their elders during ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • synonym: kneel - to rest on one's knees, often as a gesture of respect or worship
  • synonym: prostrate oneself - to lay oneself flat on the ground face downward, especially in reverence or submission
  • antonym: stand tall - to behave in a proud and confident way, not submitting
  • synonym: submit - to yield or give in to governance or authority
