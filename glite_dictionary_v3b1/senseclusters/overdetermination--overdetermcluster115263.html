overdetermination

noun
too many constraints
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˌtɜrmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition in which a system has more equations or constraints than unknowns, often making an exact solution impossible

Examples

Overdetermination in equations
The engineer noticed an overdetermination in the system, which made finding a unique solution difficult.
When there is overdetermination, mathematicians often use approximate methods like least-squares solutions.
The data set's overdetermination required the team to adjust their approach to solve the equations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underdetermination - a condition where there are fewer equations than unknowns
