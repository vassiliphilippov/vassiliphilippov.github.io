fire extinguisher

noun
portable device for putting out fires
3.2 2.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər ɪk.ˈstɪŋ.gwɪ.ʃər/ /; / /ˈfaɪr ɪk.ˈstɪŋ.gwɪ.ʃər/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə ɪk.ˈstɪŋ.gwɪ.ʃə/ (BrE)
A portable device filled with chemicals for putting out small fires, typically by spraying them onto the flames.

Examples

A fire extinguisher device
Every office building is required by law to have a fire extinguisher in an accessible location.
He quickly grabbed the fire extinguisher and put out the small kitchen fire.
The safety demonstration included instructions on how to properly use a fire extinguisher.
