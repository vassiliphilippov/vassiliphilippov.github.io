Freddie Mac

proper noun
US mortgage corporation
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɛdi ˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdi ˌmæk / (BrE)
A US government-sponsored corporation that purchases residential mortgages and resells them as securities to investors.

Examples

Freddie Mac mortgage buyer
Freddie Mac plays a key role in the US housing market by buying home loans from lenders.
Many experts believe that Freddie Mac helps keep mortgage rates stable for homebuyers.
The financial crisis of 2008 brought significant scrutiny to both Freddie Mac and Fannie Mae.
