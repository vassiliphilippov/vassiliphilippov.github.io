primary

noun
main flight feather of a bird's wing
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.məri / (BrE)
one of the main flight feathers projecting along the outer edge of a bird's wing

Examples

A bird's primary feather
A broken primary can affect a bird's ability to fly efficiently.
Scientists measured the length of each primary on the bird's wing.
The eagle lost a primary during its last molt.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: secondary - a flight feather nearer to the body than the primary
