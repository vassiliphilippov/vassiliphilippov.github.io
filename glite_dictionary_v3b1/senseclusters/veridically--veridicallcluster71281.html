veridically

adverb
truthfully; accurately
9.6
Pronunciation: / vəˈrɪdɪkli / (AmE), / vəˈrɪdɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is truthful or accurately reflects reality

Examples

To speak veridically
The scientist described the results veridically, without exaggeration or omission.
It is important to remember events veridically when giving a statement to the police.
The documentary aimed to present history veridically for its viewers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: falsely - in a way that is not true or is deceptive
  • synonym: truthfully - in an honest or truthful manner
