impressionability

noun
easily influenced
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌprɛʃənəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɛʃənəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of being easily influenced or affected by others, experiences, or external factors

Examples

High impressionability
The impressionability of teenagers makes them more likely to follow trends set by their peers.
Her impressionability led her to believe everything she read online without question.
Teachers should be aware of the impressionability of young children when discussing sensitive topics.
The impressionability of teenagers can make them vulnerable to peer pressure.
Advertisers often target the impressionability of young audiences with catchy slogans.
Her impressionability led her to quickly adopt the opinions of those around her.
Children often show great impressionability when exposed to new ideas or experiences.
The artist's impressionability allowed her to find inspiration in everyday life.
His impressionability made him easily swayed by persuasive advertisements.

  • antonym: resilience - the ability to recover quickly or resist being influenced
  • synonym: susceptibility - the state of being easily affected or influenced
