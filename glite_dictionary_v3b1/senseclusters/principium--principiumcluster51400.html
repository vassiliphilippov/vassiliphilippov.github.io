principium

noun
fundamental principle
9.9
Pronunciation: / prɪnˈsɪpiəm / (AmE), / prɪnˈsɪpiəm / (BrE)
a fundamental principle or starting point, especially in philosophy or science

Examples

The principium of science
The scientist based his theory on the principium of conservation of energy.
Understanding the principium behind the law helps students grasp its applications.
In her essay, she explored the principium of equality in modern society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axiom - a statement or principle accepted as true without proof
  • antonym: conclusion - a result or decision reached after consideration
  • synonym: postulate - a basic assumption accepted as true
