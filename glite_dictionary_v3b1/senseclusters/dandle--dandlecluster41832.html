dandle

verb
move a baby up and down to amuse or soothe
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdændl̩ / (BrE)
to gently move a baby or small child up and down in your arms or on your knees, usually to amuse or soothe them

Examples

To dandle a baby
He dandled his daughter in his arms to calm her when she cried.
She gently dandled the baby on her knee until he started to giggle.
The grandmother loved to dandle her grandson every time she visited.
He used to dandle his niece in his arms every time he visited.
She gently dandled the baby to calm him before bedtime.
The grandfather dandled the laughing toddler on his knee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bounce - to move something up and down quickly, especially a child
  • synonym: rock - to move gently back and forth or from side to side, especially to calm a baby
