torn

adjective
jagged or ragged from being ripped
1.0 2.44/m
Pronunciation: / t…îrn / (AmE), / t…îÀên / (BrE)
Having edges that are jagged or ragged from being ripped or injured.

Examples

Torn jeans
She covered her torn sleeve with a scarf to hide the damage.
The child's jeans were torn at the knee after he fell on the pavement.
The flag hung by the gate was torn and faded from years of wind and sun.

Synonyms

  • ragged
    3.5 2.69/m
    ragged is uneven and frayed at the edges from wear or tearing, while torn means having jagged or ripped edges from damage.
  • jagged
    3.6 2.54/m
    jagged has sharp, uneven points along an edge, while torn describes material with jagged or ragged edges from ripping.
  • lacerated
    6.0 0.00/m
    lacerated is often used for rough cuts in skin after injury, while torn describes ripped material or fabric edges.
  • mangled
    5.3 0.33/m
    mangled looks badly crushed and twisted with missing parts and deep damage, while torn simply means having ripped or jagged edges.
  • tattered
    4.5 0.07/m
    tattered is worn and full of many small holes or frays on cloth, while torn focuses on a single rip or jagged edge.
