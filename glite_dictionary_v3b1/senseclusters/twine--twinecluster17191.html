twine

noun
lightweight cord made by twisting strands
7.9
Pronunciation: / twaɪn / (AmE), / twaɪn / (BrE)
strong thread or light rope made of two or more strands twisted together, used for tying, binding, or packing.

Examples

A piece of twine
Pieces of twine were scattered across the workshop floor.
She bought a roll of twine to tie up the packages.
The gardener used twine to support the tomato plants.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: rope - thick, strong cord made by twisting strands together
  • synonym: string - thin cord used for tying or fastening
  • degree lower: thread - very thin strand of fiber
