alert

noun
state of heightened readiness
3.5 4.72/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàl…ùt / (AmE), / …ôÀàl…úÀêt / (BrE)
a state of heightened readiness or watchfulness, especially in preparation for possible danger or action

Examples

On high alert
Flood warnings kept the entire region on high alert for several days.
Following the earthquake, the city was put on alert for potential aftershocks.
The army remained on alert throughout the night in case of attack.

Synonyms

  • vigilance
    5.2 2.54/m
    vigilance is steady, trained watching for danger over time, while alert is a sudden state ready for immediate action.
  • watchfulness
    4.2 0.31/m
    watchfulness is careful attention to small changes and risks, while alert is a higher, ready state for possible danger.
  • qui vive
    9.9 0.01/m
    qui vive is an old idiom for being watchful and ready, while alert names the same state in normal modern speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarm - a sudden fear or concern about a danger or problem
  • antonym: relaxation - a state of being free from tension or anxiety
