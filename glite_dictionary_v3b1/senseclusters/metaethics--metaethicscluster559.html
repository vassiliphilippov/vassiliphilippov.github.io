metaethics

noun
study of moral meaning
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈɛθɪks / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈiːθɪks / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy that studies the nature, meaning, and foundations of moral judgments and ethical language

Examples

Metaethics study
Her research in metaethics explores how people understand the concept of moral truth.
The professor introduced metaethics as a way to analyze the language used in moral debates.
Many philosophers debate whether metaethics can provide objective answers about right and wrong.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethics - the study of moral principles that govern behavior
  • antonym: normative ethics - the study of ethical action and moral standards
