territorial

adjective
defending an area against intruders
1.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) displaying protective or defensive behavior over a particular area against intruders.

Examples

Territorial animals
Cats can become territorial when another animal enters their home.
Many species of birds are territorial during the breeding season.
The territorial instincts of the wolf pack were evident in their behavior.
