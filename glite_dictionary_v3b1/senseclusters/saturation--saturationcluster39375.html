saturation

noun
the act of thoroughly soaking or filling
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsæ.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən /; / ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsætʃ.əˈreɪ.ʃən /; / ˌsatʃəˈreɪʃən /; / ˌsætjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of being completely soaked or filled with a liquid or substance so that no more can be absorbed

Examples

Saturation of the soil
The saturation of the soil with water caused flooding in the basement.
The fabric reached full saturation after being submerged in dye for several hours.
They measured the saturation of the air to determine humidity levels.
After an hour of heavy rain, the soil reached full saturation and began to flood.
Proper saturation of the sponge is necessary before cleaning the surface.
The saturation of the towel made it drip with water when picked up.
The saturation of the soil after the heavy rain made it impossible to plant new seeds.
Photographers often adjust the saturation of images to make the colors appear more vibrant.
When the air reaches full saturation, it can no longer hold any more water vapor.
