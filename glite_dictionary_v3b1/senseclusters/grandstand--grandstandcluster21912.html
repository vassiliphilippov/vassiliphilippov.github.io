grandstand

verb
perform in a showy way to impress
7.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈgrænd.stænd/ (AmE), /ˈgrænd.stænd/ (BrE)
to behave or perform in a showy or ostentatious manner, especially to attract attention or impress others

Examples

To grandstand for attention
Critics complain that some athletes grandstand after scoring, turning celebrations into personal performances.
Rather than focus on the facts, she preferred to grandstand for the cameras whenever possible.
The politician was accused of trying to grandstand during the debate to win public admiration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or impressive
  • synonym: show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration, often without merit
