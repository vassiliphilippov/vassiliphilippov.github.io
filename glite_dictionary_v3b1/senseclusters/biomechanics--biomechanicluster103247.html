biomechanics

noun
study of movement forces
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊməˈkænɪks / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊmɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the movement of living things and the forces acting on them

Examples

Study of biomechanics
She decided to major in biomechanics to help athletes improve their performance.
The engineer used biomechanics to design a more comfortable prosthetic limb.
Understanding biomechanics is essential for preventing injuries in sports.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kinesiology - the study of human movement
  • synonym: mechanics - the branch of physics dealing with motion and forces
