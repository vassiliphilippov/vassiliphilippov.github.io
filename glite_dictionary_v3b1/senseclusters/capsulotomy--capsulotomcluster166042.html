capsulotomy

noun
surgical capsule incision
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpsjəˈlɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌkæpsjʊˈlɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the capsule of a joint or organ is cut to relieve stiffness or improve movement

Examples

Joint capsulotomy operation
The patient regained shoulder mobility after undergoing a capsulotomy to treat his frozen shoulder.
A capsulotomy may be recommended when less invasive treatments fail to relieve joint stiffness.
During the operation, the surgeon performed a capsulotomy to release the tight joint capsule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthrotomy - surgical opening of a joint
  • synonym: capsulectomy - surgical removal of a capsule
