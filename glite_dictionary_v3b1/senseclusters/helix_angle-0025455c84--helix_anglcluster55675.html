helix angle

noun
angle between a helix and cylinder axis
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːlɪks ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪks ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
the constant angle between a helical curve and a line parallel to the axis of the cylinder or cone on which it lies

Examples

Gear helix angle
Gear teeth are designed with a specific helix angle to reduce noise and improve efficiency.
The helix angle of a drill bit affects how it cuts into material.
When calculating the helix angle, engineers must consider the diameter of the cylinder.
