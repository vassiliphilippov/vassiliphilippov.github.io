inhomogeneity

noun
lack of uniformity; not homogeneous
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnhoʊməˌdʒiːˈnɛɾi / (AmE), / ˌɪnhəʊməˌdʒiːnɪˈɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being inhomogeneous; the condition of lacking uniformity in structure or composition.

Examples

Rock inhomogeneity
Geologists study the inhomogeneity of rocks to better understand Earth's history.
The inhomogeneity of the sample affected the reliability of the experiment.
There was significant inhomogeneity in the distribution of the medication in the tissue.
