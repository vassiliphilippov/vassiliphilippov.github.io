remanence

noun
residual magnetization
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛmənəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛmənəns / (BrE)
the magnetization remaining in a material after an external magnetic field has been removed

Examples

Magnetic remanence
The iron bar showed significant remanence after the strong magnetic field was turned off.
Scientists measured the remanence of the rock sample to study its magnetic history.
Even after demagnetization, some remanence could still be detected in the material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coercivity - the resistance of a material to becoming demagnetized
  • synonym: residual magnetism - magnetism left in a material after the external field is gone
