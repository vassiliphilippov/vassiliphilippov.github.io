close

adjective, adverb
evenly matched or uncertain contest
Pronunciation: / kloʊs / (AmE), / kləʊs / (BrE)
near in space, time, degree, or relationship; not far away or very similar

Examples

A close match
Despite their differences, their tennis matches are always close and exciting to watch.
It was such a close election that the winner was decided by just a few votes.
The soccer final was a close match, with both teams tied until the last minute.
Keep the boxes close together so they don't fall over.
The concert is scheduled for a date very close to my birthday.
We arrived close to midnight and found the streets empty.
He sat close to the window, watching the rain.
She always keeps her keys close so she doesn't lose them.
The deadline is close, so we need to finish the project soon.
