enophthalmos

noun
sunken eyeball
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.ɑfˈθæl.moʊs / (AmE), / ˌɛn.ɒfˈθæl.mɒs / (BrE)
a condition in which the eyeball is abnormally sunken into the eye socket

Examples

Abnormal enophthalmos condition
After the accident, the doctor noticed enophthalmos in the patient's right eye.
Chronic sinus infections can sometimes lead to enophthalmos due to changes in the eye socket.
Treatment for enophthalmos may involve surgery to restore the normal position of the eye.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exophthalmos - a condition in which the eyeball protrudes abnormally
