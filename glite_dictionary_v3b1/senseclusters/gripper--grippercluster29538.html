gripper

noun
object-holding device
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɪpər / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪpə / (BrE)
a device or tool (or sometimes a person) that grips, holds, or picks up objects firmly

Examples

Robotic gripper
The robotic arm uses a gripper to pick up and move small parts on the assembly line.
He attached a new gripper to the end of the tool to improve its hold.
Some exercise equipment includes a gripper to help strengthen your hand muscles.
The mechanic used a gripper to remove the stubborn bolt from the engine.
She bought a new gripper to help her open jars more easily in the kitchen.
Robots in factories often have a gripper to pick up and move parts.
The robotic gripper carefully picked up the fragile glass without breaking it.
He used a special gripper to open the tight jar lid.
The gripper on the assembly line moved products from one station to another.

  • antonym: release - to let go of or stop holding something
