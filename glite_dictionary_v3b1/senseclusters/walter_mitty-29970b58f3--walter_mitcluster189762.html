Walter Mitty

proper noun
an ineffectual dreamer or escapist
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔltər ˈmɪti / (AmE), / ˈwɔːltə ˈmɪti / (BrE)
a fictional character who indulges in extravagant daydreams of personal triumph or adventure; also used to refer to someone who is prone to such fantasies.

Examples

Walter Mitty daydreams
Ever since he was a child, Tom has been a bit of a Walter Mitty, always drifting off into heroic fantasies.
She described her brother as a Walter Mitty, happier in his own imagined world than in reality.
The new recruit was quickly nicknamed Walter Mitty because of his unbelievable stories about secret missions.
