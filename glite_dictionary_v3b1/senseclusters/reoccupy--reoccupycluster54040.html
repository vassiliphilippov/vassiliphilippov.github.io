reoccupy

verb
to fill again
5.6
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈɔː.kjuː.paɪ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈɔː.kjuː.paɪ/ (AmE), /riː.ˈɒk.jʊ.paɪ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈɒk.jʊ.paɪ/ (BrE)
to fill or take up someone's thoughts, attention, or time again after a pause or interruption

Examples

Thoughts reoccupy the mind
After a short break, worries about the exam began to reoccupy her mind.
The old debate would reoccupy the headlines whenever new evidence appeared.
Memories of his childhood home would sometimes reoccupy his thoughts late at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distract - to divert attention away from something
  • synonym: occupy - to fill or take up space, time, or attention
