shlep

verb
carry or drag with effort
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /ʃlep/ (AmE), /ʃlep/ (BrE)
to carry, drag, or move something (or yourself) with effort, especially if it is heavy or awkward

Examples

To shlep heavy bags
He had to shlep his suitcase up three flights of stairs.
It's exhausting to shlep these groceries all the way home.
Why do I always have to shlep everyone's stuff to the car?
I had to shlep all those boxes up three flights of stairs.
She was shlepping around the city all day looking for her lost phone.
Why do we always have to shlep everywhere when we visit your family?
