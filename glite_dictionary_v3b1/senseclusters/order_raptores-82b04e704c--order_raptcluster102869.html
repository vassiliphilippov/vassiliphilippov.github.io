order Raptores

noun
obsolete grouping of birds of prey

Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.dər ræp.ˈtɔːr.iːz/ /; / /ˈɔːr.dər ræp.ˈtɔːr.əz/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.də ræp.ˈtɔː.riːz/ /; / /ˈɔː.də ræp.ˈtɒ.riːz/ (BrE)
An obsolete taxonomic grouping that, in former classifications, erroneously combined birds of prey from the orders Falconiformes (diurnal raptors) and Strigiformes (owls).

Examples

The order Raptores group
In 19th-century ornithology, the order Raptores was widely accepted before taxonomists separated owls and falcons.
Some older zoological texts group eagles and owls together under the order Raptores.
The order Raptores is no longer recognized in modern avian classification systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Falconiformes - order of diurnal birds of prey such as falcons and hawks
  • synonym: Strigiformes - order of nocturnal birds of prey, namely owls
