uptrend

noun
rising price period
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌtrɛnd /; / ˈʌptrɛnd / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌtrɛnd /; / ˈʌptrɛnd / (BrE)
a period during which prices or values are steadily increasing, especially in financial markets

Examples

Market uptrend
Investors are optimistic because the market is currently in an uptrend.
The analyst predicted that the uptrend in housing prices would continue for another year.
After months of decline, the company finally saw an uptrend in its sales figures.
Investors were optimistic as the stock market entered a clear uptrend this quarter.
The analyst predicted the uptrend in oil prices would continue for several months.
During the uptrend, many companies reported record profits and increased hiring.
Investors became optimistic as the stock market entered a clear uptrend lasting several months.
Technical analysts look for patterns to confirm an uptrend before making buying decisions.
The uptrend in housing prices encouraged more people to sell their homes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bull market - a market in which prices are rising or expected to rise
  • antonym: downtrend - a period during which prices or values are steadily decreasing
