systolic pressure

noun
blood pressure during heart contraction
4.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈstɑlɪk ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / sɪˈstɒlɪk ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the highest pressure in the arteries when the heart's left ventricle contracts, measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg)

Examples

High systolic pressure
A high systolic pressure can increase the risk of heart disease.
For most adults, a normal systolic pressure should be less than 120 mmHg.
The doctor measured my systolic pressure as part of the routine checkup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diastolic pressure - blood pressure during the relaxation of the heart
