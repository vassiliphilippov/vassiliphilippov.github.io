foregut

noun
front digestive tract
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌɡʌt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌɡʌt / (BrE)
the front part of the digestive tract in an embryo or animal, which develops into the esophagus, stomach, and related organs

Examples

The foregut development
During early development, the foregut forms important organs like the esophagus and stomach.
Some diseases specifically affect the foregut region in both humans and animals.
The anatomy class focused on the structure and function of the foregut in mammals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hindgut - the rear part of the digestive tract in an embryo or animal
  • antonym: midgut - the middle part of the digestive tract in an embryo or animal
