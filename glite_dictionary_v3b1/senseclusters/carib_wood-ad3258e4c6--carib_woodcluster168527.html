carib wood

noun
Dominican tree with crimson flowers
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkærɪb wʊd / (AmE), / ˈkærɪb wʊd / (BrE)
A small tree native to Dominica, known for producing masses of large crimson flowers before its pinnate leaves appear.

Examples

The carib wood tree
During spring, the carib wood in our garden bursts into vibrant crimson blooms.
Local artisans often use carib wood for handicrafts because of its attractive appearance.
Many botanists travel to Dominica to study the rare carib wood tree.
