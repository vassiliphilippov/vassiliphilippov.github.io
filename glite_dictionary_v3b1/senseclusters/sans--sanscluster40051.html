sans

noun
sans-serif font
7.7
Pronunciation: /sænz/ /; / /sɑː/ (AmE), /sɑː/ /; / /sænz/ (BrE)
an informal term for a sans-serif typeface, a font style without small projecting features at the ends of strokes

Examples

Sans font
The designer recommended using a sans for a modern and clean look.
Many websites prefer a sans because it is easier to read on screens.
He switched the document's title to a bold sans for emphasis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sans-serif - a typeface without serifs
  • antonym: serif - a typeface with small lines or strokes at the ends of letters
