thicken

verb
become thick or thicker
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪkən / (AmE), / ˈθɪkən / (BrE)
to make or become thicker, less fluid, or more dense

Examples

To thicken a sauce
As the stew simmered, it began to thicken and fill the kitchen with a delicious aroma.
Cornstarch is often used to help soups thicken without altering the flavor.
The fog started to thicken as night approached, making it difficult to see the road.
Cornstarch can be used to thicken a sauce quickly.
Simmer the soup until it thickens to your desired consistency.
The artist let the paint thicken on the canvas to create texture.
Add cornstarch to thicken the soup if it is too watery.
As the paint dries, it will gradually thicken and become more difficult to spread.
The fog began to thicken, making it impossible to see the road ahead.
