supermassive

adjective
extremely massive
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈmæsɪv / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈmæsɪv / (BrE)
extremely large or massive, especially used to describe astronomical objects such as black holes

Examples

Supermassive black hole
Astronomers discovered a supermassive black hole at the center of the distant galaxy.
The supermassive star emits an incredible amount of energy compared to our sun.
Scientists study supermassive objects to understand the formation of galaxies.

  • synonym: gigantic - extremely large or huge
  • antonym: tiny - very small in size
