foothold

noun
initial unauthorized access in a network
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfʊt.hoʊld/ (AmE), /ˈfʊt.həʊld/ (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) an initial, often unauthorized access or presence gained within a computer system or network that an attacker can use to continue exploitation or move laterally.

Examples

A foothold in a network
The attacker established a foothold in the organization's network to move laterally.
Once the malware created a persistent foothold, defenders struggled to remove it.
Security teams detected multiple footholds across the company's cloud environment after the breach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beachhead - an initial secured position used as a base for further operations
  • synonym: initial access - the first stage of a cyberattack in which an attacker gains entry to a system or network
  • synonym: toehold - a small or initial advantage or support that allows further progress
