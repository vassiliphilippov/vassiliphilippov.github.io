catastrophist

noun
disaster believer
9.6
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæstrəfɪst / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəfɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in, supports, or studies the idea that sudden, major disasters can happen or have shaped history or the earth

Examples

A catastrophist scientist
The catastrophist warned that ignoring climate change could lead to sudden global disasters.
Some historians are catastrophists, believing that history is shaped by rare, dramatic events.
Her catastrophist outlook made her always prepare for the worst possible scenario.
The catastrophist argued that mass extinctions were caused by sudden asteroid impacts.
As a leading catastrophist, she challenged the idea that landscapes change only slowly over time.
Many scientists once dismissed the catastrophist view in favor of gradual geological processes.
The catastrophist presented new evidence about the sudden extinction of the dinosaurs.
As a leading catastrophist, she studies the effects of massive volcanic eruptions on climate.
Some scientists disagree with the catastrophist view of Earth's history, favoring gradual change instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geologist - a scientist who studies the Earth's physical structure and processes
  • antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future
  • synonym: pessimist - a person who tends to see the worst aspect of things
  • antonym: uniformitarian - a person who believes geological changes occur gradually over time
