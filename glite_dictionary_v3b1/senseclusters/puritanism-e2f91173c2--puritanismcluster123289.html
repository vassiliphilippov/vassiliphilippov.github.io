Puritanism

noun
strict religious or moral discipline
4.5 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpjʊr.ɪ.tən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpjʊr.ə.tən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈpjʊə.rɪ.tən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpjʊə.rə.tən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief in or practice of strict moral or religious behavior and self-discipline, especially associated with the Puritans; strictness and austerity in conduct and religion

Examples

Strict Puritanism
He criticized the Puritanism of his upbringing, calling it repressive and joyless.
The community's Puritanism was evident in its strict laws and social norms.
The novel explores the tension between Puritanism and personal freedom in early American society.
