hyperimmune

adjective
very high immunity
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɪˈmjun / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərɪˈmjuːn / (BrE)
having an unusually high level of immunity, especially due to a large amount of specific antibodies

Examples

Hyperimmune serum
Doctors used a hyperimmune serum to treat patients exposed to the virus.
The hyperimmune response helped the patient recover more quickly from the infection.
Researchers are studying hyperimmune animals to develop new vaccines.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: immune - protected against a particular disease or infection
  • antonym: nonimmune - not having immunity to a particular disease
