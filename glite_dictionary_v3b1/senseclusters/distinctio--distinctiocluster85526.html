distinctio

noun
philosophical distinction
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɪŋktioʊ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktɪəʊ / (BrE)
a distinction or division, especially as used in medieval philosophy or law to separate concepts or categories

Examples

Philosophical distinctio
In medieval philosophy, the concept of distinctio was crucial for classifying different types of knowledge.
The legal scholar explained the distinctio between civil and criminal law in his lecture.
Understanding the distinctio made it easier to follow the complex argument in the ancient text.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinction - a difference or contrast between similar things or people
  • synonym: division - the act of separating something into parts or groups
