al-Aqsa Martyrs' Brigades

noun
Palestinian militant group, offshoot of Fatah
9.9
Pronunciation: / æl ˈɑksə ˈmɑrtərz brəˈgeɪdz / (AmE), / æl ˈæksə ˈmɑːtəz brɪˈgeɪdz / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant organization, an armed offshoot of Fatah, active mainly in the West Bank and known for carrying out attacks during the Second Intifada.

Examples

al-Aqsa Martyrs' Brigades fighters
A ceasefire was negotiated involving members of the al-Aqsa Martyrs' Brigades and other factions.
International organizations have condemned the violent actions of the al-Aqsa Martyrs' Brigades.
The al-Aqsa Martyrs' Brigades has claimed responsibility for several attacks in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Fatah - Palestinian political and military organization from which the group split
