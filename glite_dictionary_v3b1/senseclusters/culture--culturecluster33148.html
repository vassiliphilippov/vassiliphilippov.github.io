culture

verb
grow cells or microorganisms in lab
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
to grow microorganisms, cells, or tissues in a specially prepared nutrient environment for study or medical purposes

Examples

To culture cells
Scientists need to culture cells under sterile conditions to prevent contamination.
The lab technician will culture bacteria from the patient’s sample to identify the infection.
They have successfully cultured human skin cells for transplantation purposes.

Related Terms
