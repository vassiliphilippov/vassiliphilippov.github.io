discursive

adjective
analytical, logical reasoning
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkɜːrsɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜːsɪv / (BrE)
proceeding to a conclusion by means of logical reasoning or argument rather than intuition; analytical.

Examples

Discursive analysis
Her discursive reasoning made the debate more structured and logical.
The essay adopted a discursive approach, carefully analyzing each argument before drawing a conclusion.
Unlike intuition, discursive thinking requires time and careful analysis of evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intuitive - using instinct or immediate understanding, not reasoning
