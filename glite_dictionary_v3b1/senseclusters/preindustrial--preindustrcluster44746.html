preindustrial

noun
era before industry
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪnˈdʌstriəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪnˈdʌstriəl / (BrE)
the period or condition before the development of large-scale industry, typically marked by agriculture and manual labor

Examples

The preindustrial era
Many traditions from the preindustrial were lost as factories became common.
The preindustrial relied heavily on farming and handmade goods.
Researchers compare preindustrial societies to modern ones to understand economic changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agrarian society - a society based on farming and agriculture
  • antonym: industrial age - the period marked by the rise of industry and factories
