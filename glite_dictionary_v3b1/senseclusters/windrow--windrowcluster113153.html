windrow

noun
row of drying crops
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.droʊ/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.drəʊ/ (BrE)
a long line or pile of cut crops, grass, or other material (such as hay, snow, or leaves) left in a field to dry or for later collection

Examples

Birds often search for insects in the windrow left after harvesting.
