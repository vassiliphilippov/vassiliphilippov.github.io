bewail

verb
express deep sorrow or regret
9.6 0.24/m

to express great sorrow or regret, often by wailing or lamenting

Examples

To bewail a loss
Many citizens publicly bewailed the government's decision to cut social programs.
She continued to bewail the loss of her childhood home.
The old man would bewail his missed opportunities late into the night.

Synonyms

  • lament
    5.0 6.88/m
    lament is to express sorrow aloud, often by crying out or complaining, while bewail means to show great sorrow and regret.
  • bemoan
    7.1 2.30/m
    bemoan is to express deep regret about something, often in speech, while bewail means to show great sorrow or lament loudly.
  • mourn
    3.0 9.02/m
    mourn is to feel or show deep sadness, often for a death and in private or ritual, while bewail means to express great sorrow openly.

Related Terms
