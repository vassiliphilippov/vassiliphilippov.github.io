ruderal

noun
plant in disturbed soil
Pronunciation: / ˈruːdərəl / (AmE), / ˈruːdərəl / (BrE)
a plant that grows in disturbed soils or waste places, often thriving where other plants cannot

Examples

A ruderal weed
Dandelions are a common ruderal found in city parks and along roadsides.
After the construction ended, ruderal plants quickly colonized the empty lot.
Many ruderal species are able to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cultivar - a plant variety that has been produced in cultivation
  • synonym: weed - a wild plant growing where it is not wanted
