ventral placentation

noun
ovule arrangement along the ventral ovary wall
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈven.trəl plə.sen.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈven.trəl plə.sen.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a type of placentation in which the ovules are attached to the ventral wall or suture of a simple ovary

Examples

Ventral placentation in plants
Plants with ventral placentation have their ovules aligned along the ovary's inner wall.
The botanist explained how ventral placentation differs from axile and parietal types.
Understanding ventral placentation is important for identifying certain plant families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: axile placentation - ovules attached to a central axis in the ovary
  • synonym: parietal placentation - ovules attached to the ovary wall, but not restricted to the ventral suture
