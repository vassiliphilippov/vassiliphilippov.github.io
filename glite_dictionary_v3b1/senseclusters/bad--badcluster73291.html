bad

adjective
not collectible (of a debt)
1.0
Pronunciation: / bæd / (AmE), / bæd /; / bad / (BrE)
not financially safe, risky, or unlikely to be paid (finance, debt)

Examples

Bad debt
Banks often set aside reserves to cover bad loans that are unlikely to be repaid.
That account was declared a bad debt after repeated attempts to collect payment failed.
The company had to write off several bad debts at the end of the year.
He lost his savings due to a bad investment in a speculative overseas fund.
Investing in that startup was a bad financial decision, given its lack of proven revenue.
Many experts warned that the property market was a bad bet during the unstable economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: risky - involving the possibility of loss or danger
  • antonym: safe - free from financial risk of loss
  • synonym: uncollectible - (of a debt) not able to be collected
