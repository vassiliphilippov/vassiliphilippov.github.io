draft

noun
depth of a ship's keel below water
Pronunciation: / dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
the vertical distance between the waterline and the lowest point of a ship's keel, especially when the ship is loaded

Examples

Ship's draft depth
A vessel with a deep draft cannot pass through the canal during low tide.
Before entering shallow waters, the captain checked the ship’s draft.
The fully loaded tanker had a draft of over thirty feet.
Before departure, the captain checked the draught to ensure the vessel was safe to sail.
Some riverboats are designed with a shallow draught for easier navigation in low waters.
The ship's draught limited its ability to enter the shallow harbor.
