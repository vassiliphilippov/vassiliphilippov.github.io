Titus Flavius Vespasianus

proper noun
Roman Emperor, son of Vespasian
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtəs fləˈviːəs vɛsˌpeɪziˈænəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtəs fləˈviːəs vɛsˌpeɪziˈænəs / (BrE)
(39–81) Roman Emperor, son of Vespasian, who ruled from AD 79 to 81 and is known for completing the Colosseum and dealing with the eruption of Mount Vesuvius.

Examples

Titus Flavius Vespasianus Roman Emperor
Titus Flavius Vespasianus is credited with completing the construction of the Roman Colosseum.
Titus Flavius Vespasianus succeeded his father Vespasian as Emperor of Rome.
The eruption of Mount Vesuvius occurred during the reign of Titus Flavius Vespasianus.
