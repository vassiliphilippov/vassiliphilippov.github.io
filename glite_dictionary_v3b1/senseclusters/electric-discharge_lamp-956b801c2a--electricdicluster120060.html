electric-discharge lamp

noun
lamp producing light via electric discharge
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrɪk dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ læmp / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪk dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ læmp / (BrE)
an electric lamp that produces light by passing an electric discharge between two electrodes in a glass tube

Examples

An electric-discharge lamp tube
A electric-discharge lamp is commonly used in street lighting because of its efficiency.
Replacing incandescent bulbs with a electric-discharge lamp can save energy in large facilities.
The laboratory was equipped with several types of electric-discharge lamp for experiments.
