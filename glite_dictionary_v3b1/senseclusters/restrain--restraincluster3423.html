restrain

verb
prevent action or expression
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or yourself from doing something, especially by controlling emotions or actions

Examples

To restrain your anger
He tried hard to restrain his anger when he heard the news.
She struggled to restrain her laughter during the serious meeting.
The teacher had to restrain the students from running in the hallway.

Please restrain your dog when visitors arrive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let someone do something; to permit
  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening or someone from doing something
  • antonym: release - allow or enable to escape from confinement; set free
  • synonym: restrain - to keep within limits (another sense of restrain)
  • synonym: suppress - forcibly put an end to or prevent from being expressed
