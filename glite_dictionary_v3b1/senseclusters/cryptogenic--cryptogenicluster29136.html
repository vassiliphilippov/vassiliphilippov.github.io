cryptogenic

adjective

Pronunciation: /krɪp.tə.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (AmE), /krɪp.tə.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (BrE)
having an unknown or obscure cause or origin (used especially in science and medicine)

Examples

Cryptogenic disease
The plant was labeled cryptogenic because scientists could not determine its original habitat.
Many marine species in the harbor are considered cryptogenic due to uncertain historical records.
The status of the bird as native or introduced remains cryptogenic after years of research.
The patient was diagnosed with cryptogenic epilepsy after all known causes were ruled out.
Doctors often use the term cryptogenic when the source of an illness cannot be determined.
A cryptogenic stroke is one where the underlying cause remains unidentified despite thorough testing.
