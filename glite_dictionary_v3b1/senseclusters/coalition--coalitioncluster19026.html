coalition

noun
union or alliance of diverse elements
6.2 4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a group or alliance formed by different people, groups, or organizations joining together for a common purpose

Examples

A coalition government
Several political parties formed a coalition to oppose the current government.
Some plants show a natural coalition of their stems during early development.
The environmental coalition brought together activists from different backgrounds.
After months of negotiations, a coalition emerged that represented various social groups.
The fragile coalition quickly dissolved when disagreements arose over policy priorities.
The two parties formed a coalition to address the economic crisis facing the country.
A new political coalition emerged ahead of the election to challenge the ruling party.
Several organizations joined the coalition to support environmental reform.
The countries formed a coalition to respond to the crisis.
