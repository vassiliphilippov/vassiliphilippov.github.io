humeral veil

noun
Catholic liturgical vestment; silk shoulder veil
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmərəl veɪl / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmərəl veɪl / (BrE)
a long, narrow vestment or silk shawl worn over the shoulders by a priest or other minister during certain Roman Catholic liturgical ceremonies, especially at High Mass

Examples

The humeral veil worn
During the procession, the deacon respectfully carried the monstrance using the humeral veil.
The ornate humeral veil was embroidered with gold thread for the special liturgy.
The priest draped the humeral veil across his shoulders before the Benediction.
