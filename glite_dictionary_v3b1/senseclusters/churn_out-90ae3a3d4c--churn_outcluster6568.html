churn out

verb
produce something routinely or mechanically
4.0 2.05/m
Pronunciation: / t É…úrn a ät / (AmE), / t É…úÀên a ät / (BrE)
to produce something quickly and in large quantities, often without much care or attention to quality

Examples

To churn out books
She can churn out a new novel in just a few months.
The factory continues to churn out cheap toys for export every year.
Unfortunately, these students only churn out essays without real understanding.
She managed to churn out three novels in just two years.
Some websites churn out misleading information just to attract more visitors.
The factory used to churn out thousands of toys every month before closing down.

Synonyms

  • crank out
    4.0 1.29/m
    crank out means producing things quickly and routinely in large numbers, while churn out often suggests low quality and little care.
  • bang out
    6.6 0.03/m
    bang out is producing things fast and casually, often with little skill, while churn out stresses high volume and low quality.
  • grind out
    6.0 0.15/m
    grind out is producing many items in a slow, routine way that feels tiring, while churn out means quick mass production often lacking quality.
  • turn out
    3.5 1.23/m
    turn out is producing or making goods, often by machines in normal processes, while churn out implies hurried, lower-quality mass production.
