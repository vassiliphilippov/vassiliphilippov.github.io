suicide mission

noun
deadly mission with near-certain death
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːɪˌsaɪd ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsuːɪsaɪd ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a mission or operation in which those involved expect to die, often in order to achieve a particular objective, sometimes involving attempting to kill others as well

Examples

A suicide mission
The general refused to send his troops on a suicide mission with no chance of survival.
They knew it was a suicide mission, but they went ahead to save their comrades.
Volunteering for the suicide mission was seen as the ultimate act of bravery or desperation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kamikaze - a World War II Japanese pilot who undertook a suicide mission; also used more generally for similar attacks
  • antonym: rescue mission - an operation to save people from danger
