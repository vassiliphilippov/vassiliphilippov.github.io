Leucaena

noun
genus of tropical evergreen trees or shrubs
Pronunciation: / luːˈsiːnə / (AmE), / luːˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical evergreen trees or shrubs with acacia-like pods, commonly used as forage and for soil improvement.

Examples

Leucaena trees
Leucaena is often planted in tropical regions to improve soil fertility and provide fodder for livestock.
Farmers value Leucaena trees for their rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
The pods of Leucaena resemble those of the acacia but are slightly larger and more fleshy.
