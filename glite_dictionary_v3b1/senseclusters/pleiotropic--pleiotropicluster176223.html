pleiotropic

adjective
affecting multiple traits
Pronunciation: / ˌplaɪəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌpliːəˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
describing a gene or factor that influences multiple traits or functions in an organism

Examples

A pleiotropic gene
The pleiotropic effects of this gene make it important in several different biological processes.
Researchers discovered that a pleiotropic mutation can impact both metabolism and growth rate.
Some drugs have pleiotropic benefits, influencing more than one system in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multifunctional - having or performing many functions
