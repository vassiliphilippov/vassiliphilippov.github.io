cavitation

noun
formation of cavities
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkævəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkævɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation of hollow spaces or cavities within a solid material or biological tissue, often due to disease, decay, or injury

Examples

Tooth cavitation
The dentist explained that cavitation in the tooth was caused by untreated decay.
Advanced tuberculosis can lead to cavitation in the lungs, visible on medical scans.
Engineers study cavitation to prevent damage in pumps and propellers caused by bubble formation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perforation - a hole or break in a structure or tissue
  • antonym: solidification - the process of becoming solid or making something solid
