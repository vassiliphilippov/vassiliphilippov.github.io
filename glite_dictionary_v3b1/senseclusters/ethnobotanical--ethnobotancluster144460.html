ethnobotanical

adjective
relating to cultural plant use
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθnoʊbəˈtænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɛθnəʊbəˈtænɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to how people in different cultures use plants, or the study of this

Examples

Ethnobotanical study
Researchers documented several ethnobotanical practices among the indigenous community for treating illnesses.
The museum displayed an ethnobotanical collection showing how different cultures use local plants.
She wrote her thesis on the ethnobotanical significance of wild herbs in rural traditions.
The museum features an ethnobotanical exhibit showing how local tribes use native plants.
She conducted ethnobotanical research to document traditional herbal remedies.
An ethnobotanical approach helps scientists understand the cultural importance of certain crops.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: botanical - relating to plants or plant science
  • synonym: cultural - relating to the ideas, customs, and social behavior of a society
  • synonym: ethnobotany - the study of how people use plants in their culture
