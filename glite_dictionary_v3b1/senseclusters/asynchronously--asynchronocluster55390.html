asynchronously

adverb
not at the same time
7.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs.lɪ/ /; / /eɪ.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs.lɪ/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs.lɪ/ /; / /eɪ.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that things do not happen at the same time or do not require waiting for each other

Examples

To work asynchronously
The two systems communicate asynchronously, so they do not need to be online at the same time.
Assignments can be submitted asynchronously, allowing students to work at their own pace.
Messages sent asynchronously may not be read immediately by the recipient.
The application updates data asynchronously to avoid freezing the user interface.
Messages are sent asynchronously so that each device can process them at its own pace.
By loading images asynchronously, the website becomes much faster for users.

Related Terms

  • synonym: independently - without depending on or being connected to something else
  • antonym: simultaneously - at the same time
  • antonym: synchronously - at the same time or in a coordinated manner
