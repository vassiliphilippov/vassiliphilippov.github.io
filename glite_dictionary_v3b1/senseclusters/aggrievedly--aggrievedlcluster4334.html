aggrievedly

adverb
with a sense of injustice
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrivɪdli / (AmE), / əˈɡriːvɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows feelings of being wronged or treated unfairly

Examples

To speak aggrievedly
He replied aggrievedly when he was accused of making a mistake he did not commit.
The student looked aggrievedly at the teacher after receiving a low grade.
She aggrievedly recounted the events that led to her dismissal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerfully - in a happy and positive way
  • synonym: resentfully - in a way that shows bitterness or anger about being treated unfairly
