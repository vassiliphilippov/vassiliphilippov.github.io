eohippus

noun
extinct early ancestor of the horse
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåiÀê.o äÀàh…™p.…ôs / (AmE), / ÀåiÀê.…ô äÀàh…™p.…ôs / (BrE)
an extinct small, four-toed mammal of the Eocene epoch, regarded as the earliest known ancestor of the modern horse.

Examples

The eohippus fossil
Many fossils of the eohippus have been discovered in North America, helping scientists trace horse evolution.
Paleontologists often refer to the eohippus as the 'dawn horse' due to its ancient lineage.
The eohippus was only about the size of a small dog and had four toes on each front foot.

Synonyms

  • Hyracotherium
    9.9 0.00/m

  • dawn horse
    9.9 0.00/m
    dawn horse is a common name for a small early horse, while eohippus is the scientific name used in fossils and books.
