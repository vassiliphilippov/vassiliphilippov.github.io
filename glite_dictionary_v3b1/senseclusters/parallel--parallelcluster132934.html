parallel

noun
imaginary line around Earth (latitude)
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛl / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛl / (BrE)
An imaginary line around the Earth that is parallel to the equator, used to indicate latitude.

Examples

The 30th parallel
Explorers used the 30th parallel as a reference point for their journey.
Many cities are located along the same parallel, experiencing similar climates.
The 49th parallel marks part of the border between the United States and Canada.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latitude - the angular distance north or south of the equator, measured in degrees
  • antonym: meridian - an imaginary line on Earth's surface from pole to pole, used to measure longitude
