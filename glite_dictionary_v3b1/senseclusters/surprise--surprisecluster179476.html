surprise

verb
come upon unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / sərˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈpraɪz / (BrE)
to encounter or do something unexpectedly; to cause someone to feel surprise

Examples

To surprise a friend
He surprised an old friend at the café unexpectedly.
The police surprised the suspect as he was leaving the building.
We surprised a deer in the field during our morning walk.
She likes to surprise her friends with thoughtful gifts on their birthdays.
The magician surprised the audience with his final trick.
The new policy announcement really surprised the employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - to attack suddenly and unexpectedly
  • synonym: astonish - to fill with sudden and overpowering surprise or wonder
  • antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested (opposite of surprise)
  • synonym: shock - to cause (someone) to feel surprised or upset
  • synonym: startle - to cause a sudden shock or surprise
