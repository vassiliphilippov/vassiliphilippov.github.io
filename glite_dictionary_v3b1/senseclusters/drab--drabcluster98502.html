drab

adjective
dull, lacking color or brightness
Pronunciation: / dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
dull, lacking brightness, color, or liveliness; not interesting or exciting

Examples

Drab city life
Despite the drab weather, they decided to go for a walk in the park.
She avoided wearing drab colors and always chose bright, cheerful outfits.
The walls of the old office were painted a drab shade of gray, making the room feel gloomy.
After months of drab routine, she craved something new and exciting.
His clothes were so drab that he blended into the crowd without anyone noticing.
She found the room drab and uninspiring, so she decided to redecorate it.
Her apartment felt drab after all the lively guests had left.
The weather was so drab that no one wanted to go outside.
We were all in a drab mood during that long, rainy week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic; showing happiness or brightness
  • synonym: dreary - dull, bleak, and lifeless; depressing
  • synonym: dull - lacking brightness, vividness, or sheen
  • synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening; causing sadness
  • antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images; bright and distinct
