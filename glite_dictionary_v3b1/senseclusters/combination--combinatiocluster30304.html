combination

noun
sequence of chess moves for advantage
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmbəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
In chess, a coordinated sequence of moves, often involving a sacrifice, intended to gain a decisive advantage.

Examples

Chess combination
He studied classic combinations to improve his tactical understanding of the game.
Her stunning combination involved a daring queen sacrifice followed by a mate in three.
The grandmaster set a brilliant combination that forced his opponent into a checkmate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tactic - an action or strategy carefully planned to achieve a specific end in chess
