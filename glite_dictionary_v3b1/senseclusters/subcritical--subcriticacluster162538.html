subcritical

adjective
flow slower than waves
8.1
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
describing a flow or condition in which the speed is less than the speed of surface waves, allowing waves to travel upstream.

Examples

Subcritical flow
Engineers measured the river and confirmed it was in a subcritical state after the rainfall.
In subcritical flow, disturbances can move both upstream and downstream in the channel.
The design of the dam must account for subcritical conditions to ensure safety during floods.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: critical - describing flow at the speed of surface waves
  • antonym: supercritical - describing flow faster than the speed of surface waves
