goodwill

noun
friendly hope for success
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɡʊdˈwɪl /; / ɡʊdˌwɪl / (AmE), / ɡʊdˈwɪl /; / ɡʊdˌwɪl / (BrE)
friendly, helpful, or kindly feelings or attitude towards others; kindness and compassion

Examples

A gesture of goodwill
After the debate, the candidates shook hands as a gesture of goodwill.
The company donated to charity as an act of goodwill toward the community.
They sent a letter of goodwill to wish us luck with the new project.
After the storm, neighbors showed their goodwill by helping each other with repairs.
The company built strong community ties through acts of goodwill and charity.
The festival was organized to promote peace and goodwill among different communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning; kindness
  • antonym: ill will - hostile or unfriendly feeling; the opposite of goodwill
