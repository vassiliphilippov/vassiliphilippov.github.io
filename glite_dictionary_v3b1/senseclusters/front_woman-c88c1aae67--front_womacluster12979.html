front woman

noun
female figurehead for cover, especially illicit activity
2.8 6.8
Pronunciation: / frʌnt ˈwʊmən / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈwʊm.ən / (BrE)
A woman who serves as the public face or representative for an organization, especially to conceal the activities or identity of those actually in control, often in connection with dubious or illegal enterprises.

Examples

The front woman speaks
Authorities discovered that the charity's front woman was covering for a network of fraud.
She acted as the front woman, while the real leaders remained in the shadows.
The company hired a front woman to make their business appear legitimate.
