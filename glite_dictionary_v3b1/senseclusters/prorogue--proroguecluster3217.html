prorogue

verb
suspend a legislature's session without dissolution
8.4
Pronunciation: / proʊˈroʊɡ / (AmE), / prəˈrəʊɡ / (BrE)
to formally end or postpone a session of a parliament or other legislative assembly without dissolving it

Examples

To prorogue Parliament
It is controversial to prorogue Parliament during a period of political uncertainty.
The Prime Minister announced his intention to prorogue the legislative session until autumn.
The monarch retains the power to prorogue Parliament at the government's request.
After much debate, the committee decided to prorogue the vote until next week.
The hearing was prorogued until further evidence could be gathered.
The president has the power to prorogue parliament during a political crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjourn - suspend proceedings to another time or place
  • antonym: dissolve - to officially bring a legislative body to an end
  • synonym: postpone - defer to a later time
