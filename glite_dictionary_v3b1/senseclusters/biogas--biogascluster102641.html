biogas

noun
fuel from organic waste
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪoʊˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəʊˌɡas / (BrE)
a fuel produced by the breakdown of organic matter such as plants, food waste, or manure in the absence of oxygen

Examples

Farm biogas energy
Many farms use biogas to generate electricity and reduce waste.
The city invested in a plant that turns food scraps into biogas for public buses.
Producing biogas from manure helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions on dairy farms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: methane - a colorless, odorless gas often found in biogas
  • antonym: natural gas - a fossil fuel used for heating and energy
