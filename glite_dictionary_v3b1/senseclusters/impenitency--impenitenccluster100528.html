impenitency

noun
refusal to repent; lack of remorse
6.1 9.6
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpen.ɪ.tən.siː/ /; / /ɪm.pə.ˈnɪt.ən.siː/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpen.ɪ.tən.siː/ /; / /ɪm.pə.ˈnɪt.ən.siː/ (BrE)
the state or quality of not feeling remorse or regret for one's actions, especially after committing a wrongdoing; refusal to repent.

Examples

His impenitency shocked
Despite his obvious guilt, his impenitency shocked those present in the courtroom.
Historians noted the king’s impenitency in the face of widespread suffering.
The preacher warned that impenitency would lead to spiritual ruin.
