gaiety

noun
cheerful festivity; joyful feeling
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪəti / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.ə.ti /; / ˈɡeɪəti / (BrE)
a feeling or atmosphere of cheerful, lively fun and joyful excitement

Examples

A gaiety of spirit
A sense of gaiety swept through the crowd as the parade began.
The party was filled with gaiety and laughter that lasted well into the night.
Their cheerful decorations added gaiety to the old, somber hall.
Despite the winter chill, the room was filled with warmth and gaiety.
Her laugh brought a sense of gaiety to the otherwise dull gathering.
The gaiety of the festival was contagious, making everyone smile and dance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being happy and optimistic
  • synonym: merriment - cheerful fun; gaiety
  • antonym: solemnity - the state of being serious and dignified
  • antonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress; opposite of gaiety
