wash

noun
dry streambed in arid regions
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ / (AmE), / wɒʃ / (BrE)
a dry stream bed that only carries water after heavy rain, or the gradual wearing away and removal of soil or gravel by water

Examples

A dry wash
After the storm, water rushed through the usually dry wash at the bottom of the canyon.
Flash floods are common in this wash during the summer monsoon season.
We hiked along the wash, searching for animal tracks left in the sandy bed.
Heavy rains can cause substantial wash on unpaved mountain roads, leading to dangerous driving conditions.
Repeated wash from the spring floods had carved deep grooves into the gravel path.
The construction crew worked to repair the wash left behind after the river overflowed its banks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arroyo - a steep-sided gully or channel in arid regions, usually dry except after rain
  • synonym: erosion - the process by which soil and rock are removed from the Earth's surface by wind or water flow
