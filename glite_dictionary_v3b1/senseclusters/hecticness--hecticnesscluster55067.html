hecticness

noun
prolonged fever state
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhek.tɪk.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈhek.tɪk.nəs/ (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by prolonged fever, flushed skin, and night sweats, especially in chronic illness (archaic/medical)

Examples

Hecticness with fever
The patient's hecticness was evident from his flushed cheeks and constant sweating.
Doctors in the 19th century often noted hecticness as a sign of serious underlying disease.
Persistent hecticness can indicate a chronic infection such as tuberculosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hectic fever - a fever with recurring high temperature and sweating, often in tuberculosis
  • antonym: remission - a period when symptoms of illness decrease or disappear
