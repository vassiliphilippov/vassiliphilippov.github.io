like sin

adverb
to a great degree; very intensely (informal)
8.1
Pronunciation: / laɪk sɪn / (AmE), / laɪk sɪn / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize great speed, effort, or intensity; very much or very hard.

Examples

Run like sin
He ran like sin to catch the last bus.
She worked like sin to finish the project on time.
The engine roared like sin as the car sped down the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: like crazy - informal: with great speed or energy, very much
  • synonym: like hell - used to emphasize intensity or speed, very much (informal)
