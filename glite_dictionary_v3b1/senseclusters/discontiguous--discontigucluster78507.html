discontiguous

adjective
not continuous
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskənˈtɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɪɡjuːəs / (BrE)
not touching or not forming a continuous whole; separated into distinct parts

Examples

Discontiguous memory sections
The files were saved in discontiguous sections of the memory, making retrieval slower.
The country is made up of several discontiguous regions separated by mountains.
Because the land was discontiguous, building a single road was challenging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - forming an unbroken whole without interruption
  • synonym: disconnected - not joined or linked together
