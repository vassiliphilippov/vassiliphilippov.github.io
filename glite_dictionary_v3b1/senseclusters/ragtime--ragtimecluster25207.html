ragtime

noun
syncopated piano music style
Pronunciation: / ˈræɡ.taɪm / (AmE), / ˈræɡ.taɪm / (BrE)
a style of music characterized by a syncopated melody, usually written for piano, that was popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

Classic ragtime music
Many piano students enjoy learning classic ragtime pieces.
Scott Joplin is often called the king of ragtime for his innovative compositions.
The lively rhythms of ragtime filled the old dance hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blues - a music genre characterized by its melancholy sound, which influenced jazz and ragtime
  • degree higher: jazz - a style of music originating in New Orleans, characterized by improvisation and strong rhythms
