abstractionism

noun
art based on non-representational forms
4.4
Pronunciation: / əbˈstrækʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃənɪzəm /; / əbˈstrakʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an art style or movement that does not show real objects, but uses shapes, colors, and forms to express ideas or feelings

Examples

Abstractionism art style
Many modern artists favor abstractionism over traditional realism in their work.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of abstractionism in contemporary sculpture.
The rise of abstractionism marked a turning point in twentieth-century painting.
Her latest exhibition showcases the influence of abstractionism through bold shapes and vivid colors.
Many critics credit abstractionism with transforming modern painting in the twentieth century.
Some viewers find abstractionism difficult to interpret without background knowledge of the artist's intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstract art - art that does not attempt to represent external reality
  • antonym: realism - artistic movement focused on representing subjects realistically
