in cold blood

adverb
without emotion or pity; deliberately cruelly
Pronunciation: / ɪn koʊld blʌd / (AmE), / ɪn kəʊld blʌd / (BrE)
in a calm and deliberate way, without emotion or pity, especially when committing a violent or cruel act

Examples

Act in cold blood
He shot the animal in cold blood, not once hesitating.
The jury was horrified that the crime was committed in cold blood.
The victim was murdered in cold blood, shocking the entire neighborhood.
Many were horrified to learn the crime had been planned and committed in cold blood.
The journalist reported that the soldiers acted in cold blood during the attack.
The victim was shot in cold blood while leaving his office.
