benefactive role

noun
linguistic role: intended beneficiary of action
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛn.əˈfæk.tɪv roʊl / (AmE), / ˌbɛn.ɪˈfæk.tɪv rəʊl / (BrE)
the semantic role of a person or entity who benefits from the action described by the verb in a clause

Examples

The benefactive role in a sentence
In the sentence 'She baked him a cake,' 'him' has the benefactive role.
Linguists analyze the benefactive role to understand who benefits from an action in a sentence.
The benefactive role differs from the recipient role in some linguistic theories.
