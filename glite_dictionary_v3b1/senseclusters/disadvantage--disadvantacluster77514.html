disadvantage

noun
unfavorable condition; inferior position
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsədˈvɑːntɪdʒ / (BrE)
an unfavorable condition or position that reduces chances of success or effectiveness; the quality or state of being disadvantaged

Examples

A big disadvantage
Coming from a small town can be a disadvantage in the competitive job market.
His lack of experience put him at a disadvantage compared to other candidates.
The team's injuries gave their opponents a clear disadvantage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position
