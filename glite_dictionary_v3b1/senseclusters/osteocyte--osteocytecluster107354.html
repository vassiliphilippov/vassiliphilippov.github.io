osteocyte

noun
mature bone cell
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɑstiəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
A mature bone cell formed when an osteoblast becomes embedded in the matrix it has secreted.

Examples

A mature osteocyte cell
During bone development, an osteoblast becomes an osteocyte when it is surrounded by matrix.
Microscopic analysis revealed that the osteocytes were distributed evenly throughout the tissue.
The osteocyte is responsible for maintaining the mineral concentration of bone.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: osteoblast - a cell that forms new bone
  • antonym: osteoclast - a cell that breaks down bone tissue
