nonconsecutive

adjective
not in sequence
6.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.kən.ˈsek.jʊ.tɪv/ /; / /nən.kən.ˈsek.jʊ.tɪv/ (AmE), /nɒn.kən.ˈsek.jʊ.tɪv/ /; / /nən.kən.ˈsek.jʊ.tɪv/ (BrE)
not following one after another in order, with gaps or intervals between occurrences

Examples

Two nonconsecutive terms
The president was elected for two nonconsecutive terms, with a break in between.
He attended classes on nonconsecutive days to fit his work schedule.
The meetings were held on nonconsecutive Mondays throughout the month.
