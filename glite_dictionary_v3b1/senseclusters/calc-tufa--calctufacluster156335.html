calc-tufa

noun
soft porous rock formed from lime springs
Pronunciation: / ˈkælkˌtuːfə / (AmE), / ˈkalkˌtjuːfə / (BrE)
a soft, porous rock composed of calcium carbonate, deposited from lime-rich spring water

Examples

Soft calc-tufa rock
Over centuries, calc-tufa deposits have created unique cave formations in the region.
Scientists studied the formation of calc-tufa around the mineral springs.
The ancient bridge was built from locally quarried calc-tufa.

Related Terms

  • synonym: travertine - a dense form of calcium carbonate rock deposited by mineral springs
