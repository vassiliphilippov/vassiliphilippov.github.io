make way

verb
move aside to let someone pass
Pronunciation: / meɪk weɪ / (AmE), / meɪk weɪ / (BrE)
to move aside or create space so that someone or something can pass or so that something new can happen

Examples

To make way for cars
As the parade approached, people quickly made way along the curb.
Please make way so the technicians can access the control panel.
The crowd was asked to make way for the ambulance rushing down the street.
As old traditions fade, societies often make way for new ways of thinking.
The crowd quickly stepped aside to make way for the ambulance rushing down the street.
The movers asked people to make way while they carried the heavy furniture out of the building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block the way - to prevent passage or movement by being in the way
  • synonym: clear the way - to remove obstacles or obstructions, typically to allow passage
  • synonym: give way - to yield or move aside, often under pressure
