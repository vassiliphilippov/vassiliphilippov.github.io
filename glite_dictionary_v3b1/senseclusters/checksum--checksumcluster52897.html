checksum

verb
to verify data integrity
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌsʌm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌsʌm / (BrE)
to verify the integrity of data by calculating and comparing a checksum value.

Examples

To checksum a file
The technician checksummed the downloads to confirm no corruption had occurred during transfer.
Before uploading the files, she checksummed them to ensure they were not altered.
It is good practice to checksum important data after copying it to a new device.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrupt - to make data unreliable or damaged
  • synonym: validate - to check or prove the accuracy of something
