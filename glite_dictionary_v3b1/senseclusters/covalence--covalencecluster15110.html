covalence

noun
electron-sharing capacity in chemical bonding
7.5 8.8
Pronunciation: / koʊˈveɪləns / (AmE), / kəʊˈveɪləns / (BrE)
the number of electron pairs an atom can share with other atoms in forming covalent bonds

Examples

Covalence number
Carbon has a covalence of four, allowing it to form complex organic molecules.
In water, the covalence of oxygen is two, matching its number of shared electron pairs.
The covalence of an atom determines how many chemical bonds it can form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: covalent bond - chemical bond involving shared electron pairs
  • synonym: valence - the combining capacity of an atom or group
