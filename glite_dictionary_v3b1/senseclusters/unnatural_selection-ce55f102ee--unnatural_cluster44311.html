unnatural selection

noun
selection caused by human or artificial influences
3.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈnætʃɚəl səˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃ(ə)rəl sɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
A process in which human intervention or artificial factors determine which individuals or characteristics persist in a population, rather than natural environmental pressures.

Examples

Unnatural selection by humans
Due to human activity, unnatural selection has altered the behavior and genetics of many urban animal species.
Many critics argue that cosmetic surgery trends reflect an era of unnatural selection based on appearance rather than health.
The new fishing regulations have created a form of unnatural selection in the local fish population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artificial selection - the intentional breeding of organisms by humans to produce desired traits
  • antonym: natural selection - the process where organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring
