antithrombin

noun
blood clotting inhibitor
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈθrɑːmbɪn / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈθrɒmbɪn / (BrE)
a protein (or medicine) that helps prevent blood clots by stopping certain enzymes in the blood

Examples

Antithrombin protein
Doctors may test for antithrombin levels in patients with unexplained blood clots.
A deficiency in antithrombin can lead to an increased risk of thrombosis.
Certain genetic conditions can affect the amount of antithrombin in the bloodstream.
The doctor administered antithrombin to help prevent dangerous blood clots after surgery.
Patients with certain genetic conditions may require regular antithrombin therapy.
A deficiency in antithrombin can increase the risk of developing blood clots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heparin - a medication and natural substance that prevents blood clotting
  • antonym: thrombin - an enzyme that promotes blood clotting
