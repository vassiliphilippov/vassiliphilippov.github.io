color blindness

noun
inability to distinguish some colors
1.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌl.ər ˈblaɪnd.nəs/ /; / /ˈkɔːl.ər ˈblaɪnd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkʌl.ə ˈblaɪnd.nəs/ (BrE)
an inability or decreased ability to distinguish certain colors, usually due to a genetic or physical condition affecting vision

Examples

Test for color blindness
He discovered his color blindness during a school vision test.
New technology helps those affected by color blindness to distinguish colors.
People with color blindness may confuse red and green traffic lights.
Art teachers often adapt lessons for students with color blindness.
Because of her color blindness, she finds it hard to match clothes by color.
He didn't realize he had color blindness until he failed the vision test.
Colour blindness is more common in men than in women.
He was diagnosed with colour blindness after failing a vision test at school.
Her career as a pilot was restricted due to her colour blindness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color blindness - Inability to distinguish between certain colours; American English spelling
  • synonym: color vision deficiency - inability to perceive differences between some colors; technical term for color blindness
  • antonym: normal vision - ability to see and distinguish all colors
