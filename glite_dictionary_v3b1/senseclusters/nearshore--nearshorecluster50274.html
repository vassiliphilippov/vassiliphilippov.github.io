nearshore

adjective
outsourced to nearby country
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪrˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈnɪəˌʃɔː / (BrE)
located in or relating to a nearby foreign country, especially for outsourcing work or services.

Examples

Nearshore outsourcing
The company set up a nearshore development center in Costa Rica to improve communication.
Many businesses choose nearshore outsourcing for easier time zone coordination.
A nearshore team can offer cultural similarities and faster response times compared to offshore options.

Related Terms

  • antonym: offshore - located in a distant foreign country, especially for outsourcing
  • antonym: onshore - located in the same country as the business
