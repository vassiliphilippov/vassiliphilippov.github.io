pushdown

noun
accounting method
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpʊʃ.daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈpʊʃ.daʊn/ (BrE)
an accounting method where changes in asset or liability values by a parent company are reflected in the financial records of its acquired subsidiary

Examples

Pushdown accounting
The company adopted pushdown accounting after being acquired by a larger corporation.
Auditors reviewed the subsidiary's books to ensure proper application of pushdown adjustments.
Financial statements prepared using pushdown reflect the new values set by the parent company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consolidation - combining financial statements of parent and subsidiary companies
  • antonym: historical cost accounting - an accounting method using original purchase costs for assets
