GHRF

noun
hormone stimulating growth hormone release
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒiː eɪtʃ ɑːr ɛf / (AmE), / dʒiː eɪtʃ ɑːr ɛf / (BrE)
a hormone that stimulates the anterior pituitary gland to release growth hormone

Examples

GHRF hormone
Administration of GHRF may benefit patients with certain pituitary deficiencies.
Researchers are studying how GHRF levels affect childhood growth disorders.
The hypothalamus releases GHRF to trigger the secretion of growth hormone in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: GHRH - growth hormone-releasing hormone, another name for GHRF
  • antonym: somatostatin - a hormone that inhibits the release of growth hormone
