apodyterium

noun
Roman bath changing room
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæpədaɪˈtɪəriəm / (AmE), / ˌapədɪˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a changing room in ancient Roman public baths where visitors left their clothes before bathing

Examples

The apodyterium room
Before entering the baths, Romans would leave their belongings in the apodyterium.
The guide explained that the apodyterium was the first room visitors entered in a Roman bathhouse.
Archaeologists discovered coins and sandals in the remains of an ancient apodyterium.
