cross-ply

adjective
of tires with diagonal ply layers
5.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɔːs.plaɪ/ /; / /ˈkrɑːs.plaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.plaɪ/ (BrE)
(of a tire) constructed with plies of fabric arranged in alternating diagonal layers, providing a flexible tread and relatively stiff sidewalls.

Examples

Cross-ply tires
Many classic cars are fitted with cross-ply tires to maintain their authentic appearance.
Switching from cross-ply to radial tires improved both ride comfort and handling.
The performance of cross-ply tires differs significantly from that of radial tires.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radial - of tires with plies arranged at 90 degrees to the direction of travel (the main modern alternative)
