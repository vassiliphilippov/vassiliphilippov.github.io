mishpachah

noun
extended family network (Yiddish)
9.7
Pronunciation: / mɪʃˈpɑxə / (AmE), / mɪʃˈpɑːxə / (BrE)
the entire network of family relatives by blood or marriage, and sometimes close friends, especially in Jewish communities

Examples

The mishpachah gathers
Every holiday, the entire mishpachah would gather at their grandmother's house for a feast.
She considered her closest friends to be part of her mishpachah, not just her relatives.
The news quickly spread through the mishpachah, bringing everyone together in support.
