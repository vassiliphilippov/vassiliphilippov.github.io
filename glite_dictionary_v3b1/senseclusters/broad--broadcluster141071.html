broad

adjective
not detailed or specific; general
Pronunciation: / brɑd /; / brɔd / (AmE), / brɔːd / (BrE)
general and not detailed; covering a wide range or scope

Examples

Broad topic
Her description of the job was very broad, without mention of specific duties.
The committee agreed on a broad principle, leaving details for later.
We discussed only the broad outlines of the report, not the specifics.
Her research has broad implications for the treatment of the disease.
The proposal received broad support from members of all political parties.
We need a broad strategy that addresses all aspects of the issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: general - not specific; concerning only the overall or broad aspects
  • antonym: narrow - of limited scope or range
  • antonym: specific - clearly defined or identified; detailed and precise
  • synonym: wide - having great extent from one side to the other; broad
