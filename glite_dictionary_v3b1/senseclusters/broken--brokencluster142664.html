broken

adjective
weakened and infirm (of a person)
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkən /; / ˈbroʊ.kən / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən /; / ˈbrəʊ.kən / (BrE)
emotionally or physically weakened; brought low by hardship or misfortune

Examples

Broken spirit
After years of hard labor, his body was broken and frail.
Decades of illness left him with broken health and little hope for recovery.
She returned from the war a broken woman, both physically and emotionally.
After years of personal loss, he became a broken man, struggling to find hope.
Her broken spirit was evident to everyone after the devastating news.
The company emerged from bankruptcy as a broken organization, but with plans to rebuild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debilitated - (of a person) made weak or infirm
  • synonym: defeated - having been beaten or overcome, especially emotionally
  • antonym: robust - (of a person) strong and healthy
  • antonym: whole - not damaged or impaired in any way; complete
