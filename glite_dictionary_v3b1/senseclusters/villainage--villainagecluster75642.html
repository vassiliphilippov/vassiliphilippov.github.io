villainage

noun
status of a serf or villein under feudalism
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪlɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the legal status or condition of servitude of a villein or feudal serf, especially in medieval England

Examples

The villainage status
Documents from the era detail the strict rules governing the lives of those in villainage.
During the Middle Ages, many peasants lived under villainage and could not leave the lord's land without permission.
The abolition of villainage marked a significant change in feudal society.
