hire

verb
to employ someone for work
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ.ɚ /; / ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.ə /; / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to employ someone for a job or position, usually for pay

Examples

To hire a worker
She was hired as the new marketing coordinator after a series of interviews.
The company plans to hire more engineers next year to expand its development team.
They are currently hiring seasonal workers for the busy holiday period.
She was hired as a consultant for six months.
The company needs to hire more engineers this year.
We decided to hire a car for our trip around the island.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fire - to dismiss someone from employment
  • synonym: rent - pay to use something for a period of time
