gum benzoin

noun
fragrant resin used in medicine and perfume
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɡəm ˈbɛn.zɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡʌm ˈbɛn.zɔɪn / (BrE)
A fragrant gum resin obtained from certain trees, used especially in treating skin irritation and in perfumes and incense.

Examples

Gum benzoin resin
Many traditional remedies include gum benzoin for its soothing and antiseptic properties.
The doctor applied gum benzoin to the wound to protect the skin from irritation.
The sweet scent of gum benzoin filled the air as the incense burned.
