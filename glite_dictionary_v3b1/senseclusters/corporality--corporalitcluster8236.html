corporality

noun
the quality of being physical or bodily
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrpəˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpɒˈralɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being physical; having a body or consisting of matter.

Examples

The corporality of objects
Exploring the corporality of objects, the physicist explained how everything we see is made of matter.
Many philosophers have debated whether human consciousness is dependent on corporality or can exist independently of the body.
The sculpture's corporality makes it stand out amid more abstract works in the gallery.

  • antonym: immateriality - the state of not being physical or material
