brood

verb
sit on eggs to incubate them
6.0 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / brud / (AmE), / bruÀêd / (BrE)
(of a bird) to sit on eggs to keep them warm until they hatch.

Examples

To brood eggs
If the bird does not brood the eggs, they will not hatch.
Some species of penguins brood their eggs for weeks in harsh conditions.
The hen began to brood as soon as she laid her eggs.

Synonyms

  • incubate
    5.1 0.33/m
    incubate is used for birds and other animals and sounds more scientific, while brood refers especially to a bird sitting on eggs.
  • cover
    8.2 1.12/m
    cover is a general, everyday verb for putting something on top, while brood specifically means a bird sitting on eggs to keep them warm.
  • hatch
    2.6 0.36/m
    hatch means to break out of an egg and appear, while brood means to sit on eggs and keep them warm until they hatch.
