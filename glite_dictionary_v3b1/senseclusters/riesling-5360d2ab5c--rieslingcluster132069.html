Riesling

noun
type of white wine; Rhine grape variety
Pronunciation: / ˈriːzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːzlɪŋ / (BrE)
A fragrant dry or sweet white wine made from a variety of grape grown especially in the Rhine valley and similar regions.

Examples

A glass of Riesling
A chilled glass of Riesling pairs perfectly with spicy Asian cuisine.
At dinner, he recommended a crisp Riesling to complement the seafood.
They toured German vineyards and sampled several Rieslings from the Mosel region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Chardonnay - a popular white wine grape and the wine made from it
  • antonym: red wine - wine made from dark-colored grape varieties
