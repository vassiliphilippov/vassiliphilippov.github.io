nerve

noun
impudent boldness; audacity
Pronunciation: / nɜrv / (AmE), / nɜːv / (BrE)
impudent boldness or audacity, especially in challenging or disrespecting others

Examples

The nerve to insult
I can't believe you had the nerve to speak to the boss like that.
It takes a lot of nerve to challenge tradition in such a public way.
She had the nerve to ask for a pay raise just after joining the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheek - impudent or disrespectful boldness
  • synonym: gall - rude boldness or impudence
  • antonym: timidity - lack of courage or boldness
