amphitropous ovule

noun
ovule partly inverted, turned 90 degrees
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.fɪˈtroʊ.pəs ˈoʊ.vjuːl / (AmE), / ˌæm.fɪˈtrəʊ.pəs ˈəʊ.vjuːl / (BrE)
a type of ovule in which the body is partly inverted and turned about 90 degrees on its stalk, so that its micropyle and chalaza are close together

Examples

The amphitropous ovule structure
Botanists distinguish the amphitropous ovule by its curved structure and orientation.
In plant anatomy classes, students examine diagrams of an amphitropous ovule to learn about different ovule types.
The amphitropous ovule is commonly found in families such as the Chenopodiaceae.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anatropous ovule - ovule completely inverted with micropyle near the funiculus
  • degree lower: orthotropous ovule - ovule where the body remains straight, not inverted
