A (sometimes journalistic) epithet for a woman political leader who is perceived as strong, strict, or uncompromising in style or policy; used of leaders other than Margaret Thatcher.

Examples

The mayor earned a reputation as an Iron Lady for refusing to negotiate on city budget cuts.
Reporters dubbed the new party leader an Iron Lady because she demanded strict discipline from members.
