Adams

proper noun
mountain in Washington, Cascade Range
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈædəmz / (AmE), / ˈædəmz / (BrE)
A mountain peak in southwestern Washington state, part of the Cascade Range, reaching 12,307 feet (3,751 meters) in elevation.

Examples

Adams mountain peak
From Portland, you can see the snow-capped peak of Adams on a clear day.
Hikers often climb Adams in late summer when the snow has melted from the trails.
The view from the summit of Adams is breathtaking, stretching across the Cascade Range.
