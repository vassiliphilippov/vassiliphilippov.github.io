conflict of interest

noun
competing interests influencing decisions
1.0 1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnflɪkt əv ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈkɒnflɪkt əv ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
a situation in which a person or organization has competing interests or loyalties that could influence their decisions, especially in a professional or official position

Examples

A conflict of interest situation
Journalists must avoid any conflict of interest that could compromise their reporting.
The judge recused herself from the case due to a conflict of interest.
Working for both companies would create a conflict of interest for the consultant.
