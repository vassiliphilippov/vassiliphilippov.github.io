sucka

noun
slang for naive person
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkə / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
a slang term for a person who is easily tricked, fooled, or tempted by something

Examples

A real sucka
He trusted the scammer and lost his money like a true sucka.
Don't be a sucka—always double-check before clicking suspicious links.
They called him a sucka for believing such an obvious lie.
He's a real sucka for chocolate and can't resist buying it every time.
Don't be a sucka and fall for every sales pitch you hear.
My brother is such a sucka for cute animals that he wants to adopt every stray he sees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cynic - a person who is difficult to fool or persuade
  • synonym: dupe - someone who is easily fooled or deceived
  • synonym: pushover - a person who is easy to influence or persuade
  • synonym: sucker - a person who is easily deceived or tricked
  • antonym: trickster - a person who deceives or cheats others
