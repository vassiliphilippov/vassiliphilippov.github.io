plough one's way through

verb
move with effort through something
8.2
Pronunciation: / plaʊ wʌnz weɪ θruː / (AmE), / plaʊ wʌnz weɪ θruː / (BrE)
to move forward with difficulty through an obstacle or crowd, often using effort to push past or through it

Examples

To plough my way through crowd
After the concert, I had to plough my way through a sea of fans to reach the exit.
He ploughed his way through the dense undergrowth to reach the clearing.
It took her an hour to plough her way through the long and complicated legal documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: push one's way through - to force oneself past obstacles or people
