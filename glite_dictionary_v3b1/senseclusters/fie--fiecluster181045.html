fie

verb
to express disapproval
Pronunciation: / faɪ / (AmE), / faɪ / (BrE)
to express disgust, shame, or strong disapproval toward someone or something, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

To fie on traitors
In old plays, characters would often fie on those who betrayed their friends.
The teacher exclaimed 'fie upon such dishonesty!' when she heard about the cheating.
He would fie at any sign of cowardice among his companions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: scold - to criticize or express disapproval of someone angrily
