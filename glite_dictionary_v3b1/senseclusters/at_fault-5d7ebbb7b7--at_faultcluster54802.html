at fault

adjective
deserving blame; responsible for a problem
Pronunciation: / æt fɔlt / (AmE), / ət fɔːlt / (BrE)
responsible for a problem, mistake, or wrongdoing; deserving blame

Examples

At fault for mistake
He admitted he was at fault for the error in the report.
If you were not at fault, you should not have to pay for the damages.
The mechanic was clearly at fault for the car breakdown.

Related Terms

  • antonym: innocent - not responsible for a fault or wrongdoing
  • synonym: to blame - responsible for a fault or wrong
