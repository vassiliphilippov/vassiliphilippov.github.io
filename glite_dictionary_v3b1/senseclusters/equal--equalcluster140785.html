equal

adjective
having sufficient ability for a task
2.8 9.32/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêkw…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêkw…ôl / (BrE)
having the necessary qualities, strength, or resources to meet a particular challenge or task

Examples

Equal to the task
Despite her doubts, she proved equal to the challenge and completed the project successfully.
Do you think the team is equal to facing such a tough opponent?
He doubted whether he was equal to the demands of his new role.

Synonyms

  • equal to
    2.8 9.08/m
    equal to says someone has the ability to meet a particular task, while equal means being capable or matching in that situation.
  • adequate
    2.7 28/m
    adequate means just enough to meet a need and sounds modest, while equal suggests stronger full ability to meet a challenge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adequate - having the necessary qualities or resources
  • antonym: inadequate - lacking the necessary qualities or resources
