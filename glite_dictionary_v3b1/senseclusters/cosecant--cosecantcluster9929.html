cosecant

noun
trigonometric function; reciprocal of sine
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈsiː.kənt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.siː.kənt / (BrE)
The trigonometric function of an angle, equal to the ratio of the length of the hypotenuse to that of the side opposite the angle in a right-angled triangle, or the reciprocal of the sine.

Examples

The cosecant function
Plotting the cosecant function helps students understand its properties.
She learned to calculate the cosecant in her trigonometry class.
The cosecant of 30 degrees is two.

Related Terms
