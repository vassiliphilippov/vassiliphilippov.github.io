broken-backed

adjective
sagging at both ends due to structural weakness
6.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbroʊ.kən.bækt/ /; / /ˈbroʊ.kɪn.bækt/ (AmE), /ˈbrəʊ.kən.bækt/ /; / /ˈbrəʊ.kɪn.bækt/ (BrE)
(of a ship or similar structure) so damaged or weakened that it sags at both ends, typically due to structural failure.

Examples

Broken-backed ship
A broken-backed ship is considered beyond repair and must be taken out of service.
After the storm, the once-proud vessel was now broken-backed and barely afloat.
The old pier stood broken-backed after years of neglect and battering by the waves.
