Judah

proper noun
biblical son of Jacob; tribe ancestor
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒuː.də/ (AmE), /ˈdʒuː.də/ (BrE)
(in the Bible) the fourth son of Jacob and Leah, forebear of one of the twelve tribes of Israel, and an ancestor of the line from which the Messiah was prophesied to come.

Examples

Judah son of Jacob
According to tradition, the royal line of David descended from Judah.
Many biblical stories center on Judah and his brothers.
The tribe of Judah played a significant role in the history of ancient Israel.
