asymptotically

adverb
in a way that approaches an asymptote
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæs.ɪmpˈtɑ.tɪk.li / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪmpˈtɒ.tɪk.li / (BrE)
In a way that approaches a given value or line (an asymptote) more and more closely, usually without ever reaching it, especially in mathematics and science.

Examples

Reduced asymptotically over time
The error rate was reduced asymptotically with each iteration of the algorithm.
The function increases asymptotically towards a maximum value but never exceeds it.
Their model assumes the population grows asymptotically over time.

  • synonym: asymptotic - approaching an asymptote (adjective form)
