ciborium

noun
altar canopy
Pronunciation: / sɪˈbɔriəm / (AmE), / sɪˈbɔːriəm / (BrE)
a canopy or ornamental covering, often supported by columns, placed over an altar in a church

Examples

Church ciborium canopy
The ornate ciborium above the altar was decorated with gold leaf and intricate carvings.
Visitors admired the marble columns supporting the ciborium in the ancient basilica.
During the ceremony, the priest stood beneath the ciborium to address the congregation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baldachin - a ceremonial canopy over an altar or throne
