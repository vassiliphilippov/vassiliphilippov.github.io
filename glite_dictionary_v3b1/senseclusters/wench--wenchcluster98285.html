wench

verb
to pursue women
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɛntʃ / (AmE), / wɛntʃ / (BrE)
to seek the company of women, especially in a flirtatious or disreputable manner

Examples

To wench and drink
In old tales, knights would often wench and drink after returning from battle.
He was known to wench in the taverns whenever he visited the port city.
The sailors spent their evenings wenching and singing in the lively inns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carouse - to drink and enjoy oneself in a lively way
  • synonym: flirt - to behave in a way that shows romantic or sexual attraction
