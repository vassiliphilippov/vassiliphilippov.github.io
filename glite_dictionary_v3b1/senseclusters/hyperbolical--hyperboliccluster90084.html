hyperbolical

adjective
exaggerated language
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈbɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈbɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
using language or claims that are greatly exaggerated and not meant to be taken literally

Examples

Hyperbolical praise
His hyperbolical praise made the movie sound much better than it actually was.
The politician's hyperbolical promises were met with skepticism by the public.
She tends to use hyperbolical expressions when telling stories to make them more entertaining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerated - described as greater or more extreme than it really is
  • antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
