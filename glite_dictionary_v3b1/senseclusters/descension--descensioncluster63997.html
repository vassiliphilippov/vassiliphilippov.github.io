descension

noun
religious descent
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
a formal or religious act of coming down, especially the descent of a deity, spirit, or sacred presence.

Examples

The descension of a spirit
The priest spoke of the descension of the Holy Spirit during the ceremony.
In the ancient myth, the descension of the goddess brought peace to the land.
The choir sang hymns about the descension of divine grace upon the congregation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ascension - the act of rising or going up, especially in a religious context
  • synonym: descent - the act of moving downward
