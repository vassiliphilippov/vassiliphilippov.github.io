wind chill

noun
apparent cold from wind
3.6
Pronunciation: / wɪnd tʃɪl / (AmE), / wɪnd tʃɪl / (BrE)
the effect or measurement of wind making the air feel colder than the actual temperature

Examples

The wind chill factor
The wind chill made it feel much colder than the actual temperature on the thermometer.
When the wind chill drops below zero, it is important to wear extra layers outside.
Meteorologists warned that the wind chill could cause frostbite in less than thirty minutes.
The wind chill made it feel much colder than the thermometer showed.
Always check the wind chill before going outside in winter to dress appropriately.
With a strong breeze, the wind chill can drop temperatures to dangerous levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apparent temperature - the temperature perceived by humans, considering factors like wind or humidity
  • antonym: heat index - a measure of how hot it feels, combining air temperature and humidity
