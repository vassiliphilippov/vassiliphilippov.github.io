to consider or allow oneself to think about an idea, thought, or possibility

Examples

To entertain an idea
He often entertains doubts about his decisions late at night.
It's difficult not to entertain thoughts of revenge in such a situation.
She refused to entertain the possibility of failure.
Despite her doubts, Julia entertained the idea of going back to school.
He never seriously entertained their objections to the plan.
She decided to entertain the possibility of applying for the new job.

Synonyms

  • think about
    1.0 234/m
    think about means giving careful attention to an idea or problem, while entertain is a more formal word for considering possibilities.
  • think of
    1.0 142/m
    think of often suggests keeping an idea briefly in mind, while entertain implies actively considering it as a real possibility.
  • toy with
    4.5 1.75/m
    toy with usually means playing with an idea without deciding, while entertain is more neutral and can mean considering it seriously.

  • antonym: dismiss - to decide not to consider
