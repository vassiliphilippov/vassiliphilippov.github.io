lute

noun
substance for sealing joints or surfaces
7.6
Pronunciation: / luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
A substance used for packing a joint or coating a porous surface to make it impervious to gas or liquid, especially in laboratory or engineering contexts.

Examples

Lute for pipes
A suitable lute must be used to coat porous surfaces to keep them airtight under pressure.
Before welding, the engineer made sure to spread lute over the pipe connections to prevent leaks.
In the laboratory, a thick lute was applied to seal the glass joints during the experiment.
