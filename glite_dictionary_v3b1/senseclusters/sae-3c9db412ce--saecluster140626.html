SAE

noun
imperial tool sizing
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs.eɪˈi / (AmE), / ˌɛs.eɪˈi / (BrE)
a system of inch-based sizes for bolts, nuts, and tools, commonly used in the United States and based on imperial measurements

Examples

SAE wrench size
You will need an SAE wrench to loosen those bolts on the old car.
The toolbox contains both SAE and metric sockets for different types of fasteners.
Many American vehicles still use SAE measurements for their hardware.

Related Terms

  • antonym: metric - a system of measurement based on the meter, using millimeters and centimeters for tool sizes
