salal

noun
Pacific coast shrub with edible berries
Pronunciation: / səˈlæl / (AmE), / səˈlæl / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub (Gaultheria shallon) native to the Pacific coast of North America, with edible dark purple berries.

Examples

The salal shrub
Salal is often used in floral arrangements because of its glossy green leaves.
The dense leaves of salal provide valuable shelter for wildlife along the Pacific coast.
We picked handfuls of ripe salal berries during our hike in Oregon.
