neutrophilic

adjective
preferring neutral pH
8.5
Pronunciation: /nuː.trə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ /; / /njuː.trə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ (AmE), /njuː.trə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ /; / /nuː.trə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ (BrE)
preferring or thriving in environments with a neutral pH, especially referring to microorganisms that grow best around pH 7

Examples

Neutrophilic bacteria
Certain bacteria are neutrophilic and will only grow in soils with a pH close to seven.
The laboratory identified the microorganism as neutrophilic because it thrived in neutral conditions.
Unlike acid-loving species, neutrophilic microbes cannot survive in highly acidic or alkaline environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidophilic - preferring acidic environments
  • antonym: alkaliphilic - preferring alkaline environments
