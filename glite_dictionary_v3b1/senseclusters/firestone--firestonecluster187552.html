firestone

noun
heat-resistant sandstone for lining fireplaces
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəstəʊn / (BrE)
A type of sandstone that can withstand intense heat, used to line fireplaces, furnaces, and kilns.

Examples

Firestone in a kiln
Kilns are often constructed using firestone because of its resistance to high temperatures.
She sourced firestone for the fireplace renovation to ensure durability and safety.
The old cottage had a hearth lined with firestone to withstand the flames.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together
