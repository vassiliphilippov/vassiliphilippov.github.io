channelization

noun
modifying a waterway
Pronunciation: / ˌtʃænələlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtʃænəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of modifying a waterway, such as a river or stream, to control its flow or direction

Examples

Channelization of a river
The channelization of the creek helped prevent flooding in the nearby neighborhoods.
Environmentalists debated the impact of channelization on local wildlife and plant habitats.
After years of planning, the city approved the channelization project to improve water management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canalization - the construction of canals or the process of making a waterway into a canal
  • antonym: restoration - the process of returning a waterway to its natural state
