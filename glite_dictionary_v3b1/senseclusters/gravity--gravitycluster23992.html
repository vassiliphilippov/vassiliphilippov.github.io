The force by which objects with mass attract each other, especially the force that makes things fall to the ground on Earth.

Examples

Earth's gravity
Because of gravity, objects fall to the ground when dropped.
Scientists study how gravity affects planets and stars throughout the universe.
The Moon's gravity is about one-sixth that of Earth's.

Synonyms

  • gravitation
    4.1 0.68/m
    gravitation is the formal scientific term for attraction between masses, while gravity is the common everyday word for that force.
  • gravitational attraction
    4.1 0.20/m
    gravitational attraction names the pull between two masses in physics, while gravity is used more broadly for falling objects.
  • gravitational force
    4.2 0.69/m
    gravitational force describes the measurable pull between masses, while gravity is the everyday term for the same falling force.
  • gravity's pull
    3.4 0.03/m
    gravity's pull is a casual phrase that pictures things being pulled down, while gravity names the physical force causing that fall.
  • gravitational interaction
    6.8 0.11/m
    gravitational interaction refers to the weak fundamental force between masses in physics, while gravity is the common word for Earthly falling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: weightlessness - the condition of being free from the effect of gravity
