deuterocanonical

noun
biblical book status
9.9
Pronunciation: /duː.tə.roʊ.kə.ˈnɪk.əl/ /; / /djuː.tə.roʊ.kə.ˈnɪk.əl/ (AmE), /djuː.tə.rəʊ.kə.ˈnɪk.əl/ /; / /duː.tə.rəʊ.kə.ˈnɪk.əl/ (BrE)
a book or group of books included in the biblical canon by some Christian traditions but not by others, especially in the Old Testament

Examples

A deuterocanonical text
Some Christian denominations include the deuterocanonical books in their Old Testament, while others do not.
The debate over the deuterocanonical texts has influenced many theological discussions throughout history.
Scholars often study the deuterocanonical writings to understand differences in biblical canons.
