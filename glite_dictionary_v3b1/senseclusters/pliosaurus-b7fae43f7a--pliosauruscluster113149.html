Pliosaurus

proper noun
extinct marine reptile
9.9
Pronunciation: / plaɪˈoʊsɔːrəs / (AmE), / plaɪˈəʊsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, extinct marine reptiles with a short neck and powerful jaws that lived during the Jurassic period

Examples

The Pliosaurus fossil
The Pliosaurus was one of the top predators in the Jurassic seas.
Scientists discovered a nearly complete Pliosaurus skeleton in the cliffs of Dorset.
With its massive jaws, the Pliosaurus could hunt large fish and other marine reptiles.
