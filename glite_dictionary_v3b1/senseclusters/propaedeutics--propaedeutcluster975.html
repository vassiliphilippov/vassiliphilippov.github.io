propaedeutics

noun
introductory instruction to a field
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊpɪˈduːtɪks / (AmE), / ˌprəʊpɪˈdjuːtɪks / (BrE)
A course or body of introductory instruction designed to prepare students for more advanced study of an art or science.

Examples

A propaedeutics course
His interest in philosophy began with a propaedeutics class during his first year at college.
Many academic programs include propaedeutics to help students build a solid foundation in the subject.
The university requires all students to take propaedeutics in biology before enrolling in advanced courses.
