polyp

noun
sessile cylindrical animal form (e.g. hydra, coral)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.lɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪp / (BrE)
a small, usually sedentary, hollow cylindrical animal of group Coelenterata (such as hydras or corals), typically with a ring of tentacles around its mouth

Examples

Coral polyp
A coral reef is built by thousands of tiny polyps living together.
Hydras are freshwater animals that remain in the polyp stage throughout their lives.
The polyp attaches itself to a rock and catches food with its tentacles.
