mountain

noun
very large amount or number (metaphoric)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntən / (BrE)
A very large amount or number of something, especially when it seems overwhelming or difficult to manage, often used metaphorically and followed by 'of'.

Examples

A mountain of work
After the party, we had a mountain of dishes to wash.
He left a mountain of paperwork on my desk before leaving.
There is a mountain of evidence against the defendant in this case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: handful - a small quantity or amount
  • synonym: heap - a large pile or mass of something
  • synonym: pile - a large quantity of something stacked together
