pyloroplasty

noun
stomach opening surgery
9.7
Pronunciation: / paɪˈlɔrəˌplæsti / (AmE), / paɪˈlɔːrəˌplæsti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to widen the opening between the stomach and the small intestine to improve food passage

Examples

pyloroplasty surgery
After being diagnosed with a blockage, the patient underwent a pyloroplasty to ease digestion.
The surgeon explained that pyloroplasty would help food pass more easily from the stomach to the intestine.
Recovery from pyloroplasty usually involves a short hospital stay and gradual return to normal eating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gastrectomy - surgical removal of part or all of the stomach
  • antonym: pyloric stenosis - narrowing of the opening between the stomach and small intestine
