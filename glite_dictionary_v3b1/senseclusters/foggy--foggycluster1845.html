foggy

adjective
full of fog or mist
1.0 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëÀê…°i /; / Ààf…ëgi / (AmE), / Ààf…í…°i /; / Ààf…ígi / (BrE)
full of or obscured by fog or mist

Examples

Foggy morning
Driving was difficult because the road was so foggy this morning.
It was a foggy night, and you could barely see the streetlights.
London is famous for its foggy weather in the winter.
His glasses became foggy as he entered the warm house from the cold outside.
It was so foggy that the cars could barely move along the road.
The foggy morning made the city skyline completely invisible.

Synonyms

  • misty
    1.4 2.81/m
    misty is covered in light, thin fog and often sounds more poetic, while foggy is a plain weather word.
  • shrouded in mist
    5.7 0.04/m
    shrouded in mist describes something tightly hidden by mist and feels dramatic, while foggy simply describes low visibility.
  • hazy
    3.9 3.45/m
    hazy means not clear from mist, smoke, or memory and can be vague, while foggy refers to actual low visibility.
  • fogbound
    7.2 0.03/m
    fogbound describes places stopped or delayed by very thick fog, while foggy just says visibility is low.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - free from fog, mist, or cloud; opposite of foggy
  • synonym: misty - filled or covered with mist; similar meaning
