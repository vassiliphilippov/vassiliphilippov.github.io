CRP

noun
protein marker of inflammation
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.ɑːr.piː/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.ɑː.piː/ (BrE)
a protein found in the blood whose level increases in response to inflammation, used as a marker in medical diagnostics

Examples

CRP blood test
A decline in CRP after treatment suggests that the inflammation is resolving.
Elevated CRP can indicate an ongoing inflammatory process in the body.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check the patient's CRP levels for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: C-reactive protein - a protein that increases in the blood with inflammation
