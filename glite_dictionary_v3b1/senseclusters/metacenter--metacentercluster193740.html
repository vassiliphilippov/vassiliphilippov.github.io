metacenter

noun
point measuring ship's stability
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtəˌsɛntɚ /; / ˈmɛtəˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌsɛntə /; / ˈmɛtəsɛntə / (BrE)
(shipbuilding) The point where a vertical line through the center of buoyancy of a floating body in equilibrium intersects the corresponding line when the body is tilted; the distance of the metacenter above the center of gravity indicates the stability of the vessel.

Examples

Ship metacenter point
After modifications, the ship's metacenter was reassessed for safety reasons.
Naval architects carefully calculate the metacenter to ensure that a vessel will not capsize easily.
The height of the metacenter above the ship's center of gravity determines its stability.
Engineers adjusted the cargo distribution to raise the metacentre and improve safety.
Knowledge of the metacentre is essential in the design of stable vessels.
The ship's metacentre was carefully calculated to ensure maximum stability at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buoyancy - the ability of an object to float in water or air
  • synonym: center of buoyancy - the point in a submerged body through which buoyant force acts
  • synonym: center of gravity - the point through which the force of gravity acts on an object
  • antonym: instability - state of being unstable, lacking stability
