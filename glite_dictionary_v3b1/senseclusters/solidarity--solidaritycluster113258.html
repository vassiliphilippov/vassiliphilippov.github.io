solidarity

noun
unity or mutual support within a group
5.2 10/m
Pronunciation: / Àås…ëÀê.l…ôÀàd…õr.…™.ti / (AmE), / Àås…íl.…™Ààd√¶r.…™.ti / (BrE)
Unity or agreement of feeling or action, especially among individuals with a common interest; mutual support within a group.

Examples

Strong solidarity among workers
A feeling of solidarity developed among the volunteers as they worked together toward a common goal.
International solidarity is crucial when responding to humanitarian crises.
The workers showed strong solidarity during the strike, supporting each other's demands.

Synonyms

  • brotherhood
    3.3 1.63/m
    brotherhood feels like close family support among people, while solidarity focuses on organized mutual support for a common interest.
  • unity
    2.8 24/m
    unity means being joined or acting as one, while solidarity stresses mutual support and shared commitment among group members.
  • unitedness
    7.0 0.01/m
    unitedness describes the state of acting as a single group, while solidarity adds active mutual support and common purpose.
  • comradeship
    4.9 0.36/m
    comradeship means friendship and trust among companions, while solidarity highlights shared goals and active support within the whole group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: division - the state of being divided; disunity
  • synonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
