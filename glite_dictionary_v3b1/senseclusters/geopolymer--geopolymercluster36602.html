geopolymer

noun
synthetic construction material
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːoʊˈpɑːlɪmər / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˈpɒlɪmə / (BrE)
a synthetic material made by activating natural minerals with alkaline solutions, often used as an alternative to traditional cement in construction

Examples

A geopolymer material
Engineers chose geopolymer for the foundation because of its environmental benefits.
The company is developing new geopolymer products to replace traditional concrete.
Using geopolymer can reduce carbon emissions in large construction projects.
