alignment

noun
political or strategic alliance
Pronunciation: / əˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
an organization or alliance of people or countries united by a pact or treaty, often for political or strategic purposes

Examples

Political alignment
During the Cold War, many nations chose an alignment with either the United States or the Soviet Union.
The new alignment of member states was established after the treaty was signed.
The two countries formed an alignment to promote economic cooperation in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit
  • synonym: coalition - a temporary alliance for combined action, especially of political parties
  • antonym: opposition - a group that actively resists or is against something
