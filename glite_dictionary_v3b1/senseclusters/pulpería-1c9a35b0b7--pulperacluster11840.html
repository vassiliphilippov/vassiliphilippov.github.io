pulpería

noun
rural inn in Argentina
Pronunciation: / pulpeˈɾi.a / (AmE), / pulpeˈɾi.a / (BrE)
a rural store or tavern in Latin America where people gather to buy basic goods, have drinks, and socialize (often historical or traditional)

Examples

A pulpería store
In the 19th century, gauchos would gather at the pulpería to share stories and enjoy music.
The old pulpería at the edge of town is now a museum showing rural life in Argentina.
Locals met at the pulpería every weekend for drinks, dancing, and lively conversation.
On their way through the countryside, they stopped at a pulpería to buy bread and cheese.
The local pulpería was a gathering place for villagers to share news and stories.
He worked at the pulpería every weekend, helping his family run the business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inn - a place offering food, drink, and lodging to travelers
  • synonym: saloon - a public bar or inn, especially in rural areas
  • antonym: supermarket - a large self-service store selling food and other goods
  • synonym: tavern - a place where people gather to drink and socialize
