federation

noun
cross-domain identity sharing (SSO)
6.3
Pronunciation: /fe.də.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /fə.də.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /fe.də.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /fə.də.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in computing, especially identity management) a system or arrangement in which separate organizations agree to share authentication and identity information so users can access multiple services with a single sign-on across domains.

Examples

An federation of systems
Many organizations joined an federation of identity providers to enable seamless cross-domain logins.
The university used an federation so students could access external library systems with campus accounts.
Setting up an federation between companies required agreeing on trusted identity standards and protocols.

Related Terms

  • synonym: identity provider - a service that authenticates users and supplies identity attributes to other services within a federation
  • synonym: single sign-on - a user authentication process that allows access to multiple services with one set of credentials
