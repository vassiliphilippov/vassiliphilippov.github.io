snobbery

noun
condescending attitude based on social class
6.3 1.19/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsnÉ‘b.Éš.i / (AmE), / ËˆsnÉ’b.É™r.i / (BrE)
the behavior or attitude of people who think they are better than others, especially because of social class or wealth

Examples

Snobbery attitude
Her snobbery was obvious when she refused to eat at any restaurant without a Michelin star.
His snobbery made it difficult for him to relate to people from different backgrounds.
The film satirizes the snobbery of the upper classes in early 20th-century England.

Synonyms

  • snobbishness
    7.4 0.08/m
    snobbishness is behaving as if one is better than others, while snobbery focuses on social class or wealth-based superiority.
  • snobbism
    8.4 0.05/m
    snobbism is the same attitude of looking down on others, while snobbery highlights class or wealth as the reason.
  • snootiness
    8.1 0.01/m
    snootiness sounds informal and shows pretension in style or manners, while snobbery usually means looking down because of class or wealth.
  • superciliousness
    9.8 0.03/m
    superciliousness describes a haughty, patronizing look or tone, while snobbery means feeling superior because of class or wealth.
  • haughtiness
    7.9 0.13/m
    haughtiness is proud and scornful in behavior or manner, while snobbery is judging others as inferior because of class or wealth.
  • uppishness
    9.2 0.00/m
    uppishness means acting above one's position in a social way, while snobbery is broader pride tied to class or wealth.
