dehisce

verb
to split open to release contents
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈhɪs/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈhɪs/ (BrE)
(of a plant structure or organ) to burst open or split apart in order to release its contents, such as seeds or pollen.

Examples

Fruits dehisce naturally
Some fruits dehisce by splitting along a natural seam when they are ripe.
The capsule will dehisce and release numerous small seeds.
When the pod matures, it dehisces to scatter seeds in the surrounding soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burst - to break open or apart suddenly and violently
