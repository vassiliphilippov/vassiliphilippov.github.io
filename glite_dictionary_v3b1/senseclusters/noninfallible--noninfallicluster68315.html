noninfallible

adjective
capable of error
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɪnˈfæləbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪnˈfæləbəl / (BrE)
capable of making mistakes or being wrong; not infallible

Examples

A noninfallible person
Even the most experienced professionals are noninfallible and can make mistakes.
The judge reminded the jury that all witnesses are noninfallible in their recollections.
Scientific theories are developed by noninfallible humans and can be revised over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infallible - incapable of making mistakes or being wrong
