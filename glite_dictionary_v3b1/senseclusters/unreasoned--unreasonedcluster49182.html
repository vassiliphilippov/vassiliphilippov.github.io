unreasoned

adjective
without logical basis
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈriːzənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːzənd / (BrE)
not based on logical thinking, evidence, or reasoning

Examples

Unreasoned anger
Her unreasoned anger surprised everyone at the meeting.
The decision seemed unreasoned and was quickly criticized by the board.
He acted on an unreasoned impulse rather than thinking things through.
The manager's unreasoned decision left the team confused and frustrated.
Critics argued that the policy was unreasoned and lacked proper analysis.
She refused to accept any unreasoned arguments during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable
  • antonym: justified - supported by good reasons or evidence
  • antonym: reasoned - based on careful thought or logic
