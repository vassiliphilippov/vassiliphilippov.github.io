felled seam

noun
a seam with folded and stitched edges
8.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / f…õld sim / (AmE), / f…õld siÀêm / (BrE)
a seam made by folding the raw edges under and stitching them down to prevent fraying, leaving a neat finish on both sides

Examples

A felled seam finish
A felled seam helps to prevent the fabric from fraying at the edges.
Shirts often feature a felled seam along the sides and sleeves for a neat appearance.
The tailor used a felled seam on the trousers for extra durability.
