apparatus

noun
group of body parts/organs with a function
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈrætəs / (AmE), / ˌapəˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A group of body parts or organs that work together to perform a specific function.

Examples

Respiratory apparatus
Damage to the respiratory apparatus can make breathing difficult.
Scientists studied the nervous apparatus to understand reflex actions.
The apparatus responsible for digestion includes several major organs.
