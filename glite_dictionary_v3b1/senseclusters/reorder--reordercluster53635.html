reorder

noun
a repeated commercial order
1.8
Pronunciation: / riˈɔrdər / (AmE), / riːˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an order placed again for the same goods or merchandise as previously ordered

Examples

A reorder of supplies
Our supplier offers discounts when a reorder is placed within 30 days of the original purchase.
Please check if a reorder is necessary for the bestselling items.
The manager submitted a reorder to replenish the store's inventory.
