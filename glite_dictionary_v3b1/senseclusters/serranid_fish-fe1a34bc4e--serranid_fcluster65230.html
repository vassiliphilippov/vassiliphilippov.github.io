serranid fish

noun
marine fish of the Serranidae family
Pronunciation: / səˈræn.ɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / səˈræn.ɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish of the family Serranidae, found mainly in warm coastal waters, valued for food and sport

Examples

A large serranid fish
A large serranid fish darted among the coral reefs as we snorkeled above.
Many local markets offer freshly caught serranid fish due to their popularity as food.
The Caribbean is home to several species of serranid fish prized by anglers and chefs alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grouper - a large serranid fish, often from warm seas
