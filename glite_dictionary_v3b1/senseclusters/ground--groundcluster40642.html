ground

verb
to restrict as punishment
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to punish a child or teenager by not allowing them to go out or use certain privileges, such as electronic devices, for a period of time.

Examples

To ground a teenager
If you break the rules again, you will be grounded for two weeks.
Her parents grounded her after she failed to finish her homework.
He was grounded and could not go to the party with his friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punish - to make someone suffer for breaking a rule or law
  • antonym: reward - to give something positive for good behavior
