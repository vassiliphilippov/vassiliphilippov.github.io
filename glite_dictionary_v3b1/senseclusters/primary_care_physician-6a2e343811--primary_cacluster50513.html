primary care physician

noun
doctor providing general and initial medical care
3.8 1.59/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpra…™Àåm…õri k…õr f…ôÀàs…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Ààpr å…™m…ôri k…õÀê f…™Ààs…™ É(…ô)n / (BrE)
a doctor who provides general medical care and coordinates other health care for patients, often serving as the first point of contact in the healthcare system

Examples

A primary care physician
A primary care physician often refers patients to specialists when necessary.
She scheduled an appointment with her primary care physician for a yearly check-up.
Your primary care physician manages routine vaccinations and preventive health screenings.

Synonyms

  • general practitioner
    2.7 0.79/m
    a general practitioner treats many illnesses and often works in clinics, while a primary care physician also coordinates patient care across specialists.
  • GP
    3.2 11/m
    GP is the short form used in speech and notes, while a primary care physician is the formal term used in hospitals and records.
  • family doctor
    2.0 1.31/m
    a family doctor cares for all ages in a family over many years, while a primary care physician may serve a wider patient group.
