reform-minded

adjective
favoring or promoting reform
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrm ˌmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm ˌmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
favoring or promoting reform, especially through government action

Examples

Reform-minded activists
Many reform-minded activists gathered at the rally to demand improvements in education.
The new governor is known to be reform-minded and eager to tackle corruption.
Voters supported the reform-minded candidates who promised to change outdated policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - holding to traditional attitudes and values, cautious about change or innovation
  • synonym: progressive - favoring or advocating progress, change, improvement, or reform
