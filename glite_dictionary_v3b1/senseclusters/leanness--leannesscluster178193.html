leanness

noun
efficiency; minimal waste (business)
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈliːn.nəs/ /; / /ˈliː.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈliːn.nəs/ /; / /ˈliː.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or state of minimizing waste and maximizing efficiency in a product, process, or organization (often used in business and manufacturing contexts).

Examples

Business leanness
The factory improved its processes to increase leanness and cut production costs by twenty percent.
Customers noticed the product's leanness in packaging—minimal materials and clear instructions reduced waste.
Startups often highlight leanness in their models to show efficient use of capital and resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: efficiency - achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense
  • antonym: wastefulness - the tendency to use more resources than necessary; opposite of leanness in operations
