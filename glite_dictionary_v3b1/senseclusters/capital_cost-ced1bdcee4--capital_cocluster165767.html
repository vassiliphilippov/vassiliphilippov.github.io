The upfront expenditure required to acquire, construct, or install fixed assets for a project or business (for example, buildings, machinery, or major equipment); also called capital expenditure (CAPEX).

Examples

Capital cost of equipment
The capital cost of the new factory includes machinery purchase and installation expenses.
When preparing the budget, they underestimated the project's capital cost and ran out of funds.
Banks review expected cash flow carefully before financing high capital cost infrastructure projects.

Synonyms

  • capital expenditure
    6.4 0.66/m
    capital expenditure refers to money spent on buying, upgrading, or maintaining long-term assets, while capital cost emphasizes the upfront purchase price.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capital expenditure (CAPEX) - money spent to buy or improve long-term assets such as equipment or buildings
  • antonym: operating cost - the ongoing day-to-day expenses of running a business, as opposed to one-time capital purchases
