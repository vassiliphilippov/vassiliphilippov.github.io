union

noun
the set of all elements in two or more sets
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈjun.jən / (AmE), / ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
the set that consists of all elements that belong to at least one of two or more given sets

Examples

The union of sets
In mathematics, the union symbol is often used to combine sets.
The union of sets A and B contains all elements from both sets.
To solve the problem, find the union of the given lists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intersection - the set containing only elements common to two or more sets
