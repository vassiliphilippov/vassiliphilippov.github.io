reintroduce

verb
introduce again or anew
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɪntrəˈduːs / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪntrəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to introduce something or someone again after it has been absent, removed, or not used for a while

Examples

To reintroduce wolves
After years of decline, conservationists hope to reintroduce wolves into the national park.
The professor decided to reintroduce the topic during the next lecture to ensure everyone understood.
With the renovations complete, the museum will reintroduce its famous Impressionist collection.
After a brief break, he wanted to reintroduce himself to the audience.
She decided to reintroduce classical music to the school's curriculum.
The organization plans to reintroduce wolves into the park after many years.
