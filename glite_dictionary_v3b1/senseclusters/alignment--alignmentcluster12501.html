alignment

noun
aligning AI with human values
5.7
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈlaɪn.mənt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈlaɪn.mənt/ (BrE)
(in computer science, especially AI) the study and practice of designing artificial intelligence systems so their behaviour, goals, and outputs conform to human values, intentions, and safety constraints.

Examples

AI alignment
Researchers study alignment to ensure advanced AI systems act according to human values and safety constraints.
The workshop focused on alignment problems, including reward specification and preventing unintended side effects.
Governments are increasingly funding alignment research to reduce risks from powerful artificial intelligence systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: AI safety - the field concerned with ensuring artificial intelligence systems are safe and beneficial
  • antonym: misalignment - a failure of an AI system to act in accordance with intended human goals or values
