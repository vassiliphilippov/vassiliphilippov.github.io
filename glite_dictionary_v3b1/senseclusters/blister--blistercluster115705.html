blister

verb
to form blisters on the skin or surface
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
to form or cause blisters (raised areas filled with fluid or air) on the skin, a surface, or a plant

Examples

Feet blister easily
If you walk too far in new shoes, your heels might blister.
My hands began to blister after hours of raking leaves without gloves.
The paint on the old wall started to blister in the summer heat.
New hiking boots often blister my heels if I don't wear thick socks.
The hot sun blistered the paint on the car's roof after a long summer.
Without gloves, the harsh chemicals blistered her hands within minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blister - a small swelling on the skin filled with liquid, caused by burning or irritation
