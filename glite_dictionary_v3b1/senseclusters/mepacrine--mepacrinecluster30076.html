mepacrine

noun
drug for malaria and some worms
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛpəˌkriːn / (AmE), / ˈmiːpəˌkriːn / (BrE)
a drug formerly used to treat malaria and still used for certain worm infestations; also known by the trade name Atabrine

Examples

mepacrine drug
For many years, mepacrine was the principal drug used against malaria before newer medications appeared.
Researchers studied the effect of mepacrine on different types of worm infestations.
The doctor prescribed mepacrine to treat the patient's parasitic infection.
