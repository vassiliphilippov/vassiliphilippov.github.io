greet

verb
react or respond to upon meeting
1.0 4.12/m
Pronunciation: / …°rit / (AmE), / …°riÀêt / (BrE)
to react or respond to someone or something in a particular way when you meet or encounter them

Examples

Greeted with silence
His proposal was greeted by silence, making him feel uneasy.
The arrival of the new policy was greeted with skepticism by many employees.
The audience greeted the performer with thunderous applause as she walked on stage.

Synonyms

  • welcome
    1.0 22/m
    welcome is friendly and warm when someone arrives, while greet is any response you make on meeting someone.
  • hail
    4.1 5.16/m
    hail is an enthusiastic or public greeting that praises someone, while greet is a neutral reaction when you meet them.
  • receive
    1.6 13/m
    receive means accepting or letting someone into a place or group, while greet focuses on the first response when you meet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone when encountered
  • synonym: welcome - to greet someone in a warm and friendly way
