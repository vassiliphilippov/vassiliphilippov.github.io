May queen

noun
girl chosen to lead May Day festival
Pronunciation: / meɪ kwiːn / (AmE), / meɪ kwiːn / (BrE)
a girl chosen as queen to preside over a May Day festival, often representing the spirit of spring

Examples

The May queen parade
Each year, the village celebrates by crowning a new May queen during the parade.
She was honored to be named the May queen at her school's spring festival.
The tradition of the May queen goes back centuries in England.
