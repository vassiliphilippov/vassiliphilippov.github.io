geological formation

noun
distinct layer or structure of rock or earth
Pronunciation: / dʒiːəˈlɑdʒɪkəl fɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒiːəˈlɒdʒɪkəl fɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a naturally occurring layer or structure of rock, soil, or mineral with distinct characteristics, typically recognized and named by geologists

Examples

A geological formation layer
A distinct geological formation can often be identified by its rock composition and arrangement.
Scientists studied the geological formation to understand the region's history.
The Grand Canyon exposes a variety of geological formations that span millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outcrop - a visible exposure of bedrock or ancient geological formation on the surface
  • synonym: stratum - a layer of rock or soil with consistent characteristics
