inanimate

adjective
not alive; not animate
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnænɪmət / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪmət / (BrE)
not alive; lacking the qualities of living things such as movement or consciousness

Examples

Inanimate object
During the demonstration, the magician seemed to bring the inanimate statue to life.
She arranged the inanimate objects on the shelf with great care.
The scientist explained how inanimate matter could never possess consciousness.
After hours underwater, the diver was pulled out, inanimate and pale.
The doctor checked for a pulse but found the body completely inanimate.
The victim lay inanimate on the ground until help arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: animate - having life; alive
  • degree higher: dead - no longer alive
  • synonym: lifeless - without life; dead or apparently dead
  • degree lower: unconscious - not awake and aware of and responding to one's environment
