multiplexing

noun
combining signals
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪˌplɛksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌplɛksɪŋ / (BrE)
a method or technique for sending, processing, or analyzing several signals or data streams at the same time using a single system or channel

Examples

Multiplexing signals
Modern communication systems rely on multiplexing to transmit several phone calls over a single cable.
The engineer explained how multiplexing improves the efficiency of data transfer in networks.
Without multiplexing, each signal would require its own dedicated channel, increasing costs.
Modern laboratories use multiplexing to analyze several DNA samples in a single test.
The efficiency of multiplexing allows researchers to save time and resources during experiments.
Advances in multiplexing have improved the detection of multiple diseases from one blood sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggregation - the act of collecting or combining multiple items into one
  • synonym: combining - the act of joining or merging things together
  • antonym: demultiplexing - the process of separating combined signals into their original streams
