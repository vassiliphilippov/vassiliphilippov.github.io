manufacture

verb
produce or create, often mechanically
Pronunciation: / ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃə /; / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃər / (BrE)
to make or produce something in large quantities, especially using machines

Examples

To manufacture cars
After his debut novel became a bestseller, the author began to manufacture plotlines to meet demand.
Some companies manufacture evidence to support their claims, which is both unethical and illegal.
The factory manufactures thousands of cars every year for export.
Our company manufactured all the electronic components for the new smartphones.
The factory manufactures thousands of bicycles every month for export.
They are planning to manufacture solar panels using recycled materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assemble - to fit together parts or pieces to make a finished product
  • antonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something
  • synonym: fabricate - to construct or manufacture an industrial product
  • synonym: produce - to make or create something
