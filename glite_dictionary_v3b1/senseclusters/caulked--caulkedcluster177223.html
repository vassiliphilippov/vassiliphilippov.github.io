caulked

adjective
sealed with caulk; cracks filled
5.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / k…îkt / (AmE), / k…îÀêkt / (BrE)
(of a surface, seam, or joint) having cracks and crevices sealed with a filler to prevent leakage.

Examples

A caulked boat
Each plank was carefully caulked to ensure the boat remained watertight.
The contractor inspected the caulked joints around the bathroom tiles for any signs of leaks.
The newly caulked windows helped reduce drafts throughout the house.
