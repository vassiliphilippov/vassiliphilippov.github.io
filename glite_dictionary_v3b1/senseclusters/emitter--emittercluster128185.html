emitter

noun
source of emission
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a device or object that gives off or sends out light, energy, sound, or signals

Examples

Light emitter
The sun is the most powerful emitter of energy in our solar system.
Modern televisions use tiny emitters to create bright and colorful images.
Scientists measured the amount of gas released by each emitter in the experiment.
The emitter in the remote control sends signals to the television.
A laser emitter produces a focused beam of light for precise measurements.
Each smoke detector contains an infrared emitter to sense particles in the air.
