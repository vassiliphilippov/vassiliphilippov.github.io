national monument

noun
historic structure or site protected by government
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənl ˈmɑnjuˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈnaʃənəl ˈmɒnjuːmənt / (BrE)
A structure or natural landmark of historic interest that has been set aside by a national government for preservation and public enjoyment.

Examples

A national monument site
After it was declared a national monument, the area was protected from development.
The Statue of Liberty is a well-known national monument in the United States.
Tourists flock each year to visit the famous national monument in the heart of the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monument - a statue, building, or other structure erected to commemorate a notable person or event
