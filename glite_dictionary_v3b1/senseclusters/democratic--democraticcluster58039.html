democratic

adjective
relating to democracy or equality
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈkræt.ɪk /; / ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
relating to democracy, equality, or being accessible and fair to all people

Examples

Democratic leadership style
His leadership style is very democratic, always seeking input from everyone on the team.
She strongly supports democratic values such as equality and free speech.
The country established a democratic system of government after the revolution.
Literature that is written in a simple style can be considered democratic because it is accessible to everyone.
Public radio is often praised as a democratic form of media, serving the interests of the entire community.
The artist aimed to create democratic music that appealed to people from all backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • synonym: egalitarian - believing in or based on the principle that all people are equal and deserve equal rights and opportunities
  • synonym: popular - liked or enjoyed by many people or by the general public
