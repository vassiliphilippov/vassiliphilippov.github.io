cost

verb
require loss or sacrifice
Pronunciation: / kɑst / (AmE), / kɒst / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose, suffer, or sacrifice something as a result or consequence

Examples

Cost her a chance
Her carelessness nearly cost her her life in the accident.
Speaking out against the company cost him his promotion.
The scandal cost the senator his reputation.
A single missed shot cost the team the championship.
Careless errors in the report cost the company an important client.
His late arrival cost him the job interview.
