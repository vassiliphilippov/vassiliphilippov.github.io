fdisk

verb
to partition a disk
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈe.f.dɪsk/ (AmE), /ˈe.f.dɪsk/ (BrE)
to use the fdisk utility to create, delete, or modify disk partitions on a storage device

Examples

To fdisk a drive
Before installing Linux, you may need to fdisk your hard drive to create new partitions.
The technician decided to fdisk the disk to remove all existing partitions and start fresh.
If you accidentally fdisk the wrong drive, you could lose all your data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: format - to prepare a storage device for use by erasing all data and setting up a file system
  • synonym: partition - to divide a storage device into separate sections
