acanthosis nigricans

noun
skin disorder with dark patches
Pronunciation: / əˌkænˈθoʊsɪs ˌnɪˈɡrɪkænz / (AmE), / əˌkanˈθəʊsɪs ˌnɪˈɡrɪkanz / (BrE)
A skin disorder marked by dark, thickened, velvety patches, typically appearing in body folds such as the armpits, neck, or groin.

Examples

Acanthosis nigricans skin patches
Acanthosis nigricans can be a sign of insulin resistance in some patients.
The doctor diagnosed her with acanthosis nigricans after noticing dark, velvety patches on her neck.
Treatment of the underlying cause often improves the appearance of acanthosis nigricans.
