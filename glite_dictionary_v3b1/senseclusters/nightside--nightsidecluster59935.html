nightside

noun
period of darkness
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtsaɪd / (BrE)
the side of a planet, moon, or area that is in darkness and not illuminated by the sun

Examples

Earth's nightside
The astronauts observed the Earth's nightside as they orbited the planet.
Animals that are active on the nightside have special adaptations for seeing in the dark.
The city looked peaceful from the nightside, with only a few lights shining.
The spacecraft entered the nightside of the planet, where temperatures dropped dramatically.
Scientists study the nightside to understand how the atmosphere behaves without sunlight.
Auroras are sometimes visible on the nightside of Earth during periods of high solar activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dark side - the side of a celestial body not facing the sun
  • antonym: dayside - the part of a day or area that is in daylight
  • synonym: nighttime - the period of time when it is night
