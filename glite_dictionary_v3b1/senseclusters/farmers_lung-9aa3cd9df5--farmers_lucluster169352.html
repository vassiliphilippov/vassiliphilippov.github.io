farmer's lung

noun
allergic lung disease from inhaling mold spores
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑrmərz lʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməz lʌŋ / (BrE)
An allergic inflammation of the lungs caused by inhaling fungal spores from moldy hay or crops, commonly affecting agricultural workers.

Examples

Farmer's lung disease
Repeated exposure to moldy hay can greatly increase the risk of farmer's lung.
The doctor diagnosed him with farmer's lung after he developed persistent cough and shortness of breath.
Wearing a protective mask can help prevent farmer's lung among those working with hay and straw.
