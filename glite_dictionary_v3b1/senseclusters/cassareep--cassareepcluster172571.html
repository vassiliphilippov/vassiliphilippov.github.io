cassareep

noun
sauce from cassava used in West Indian cooking
Pronunciation: / ˌkæsəˈrip / (AmE), / ˌkæsəˈriːp / (BrE)
A thick, dark sauce made by boiling down the juice of bitter cassava, used especially as a flavoring in West Indian cooking.

Examples

Thick cassareep sauce
The chef added cassareep to the stew to give it an authentic Guyanese flavor.
Traditional pepperpot is not complete without a generous amount of cassareep.
You can find bottles of cassareep in Caribbean grocery stores.
