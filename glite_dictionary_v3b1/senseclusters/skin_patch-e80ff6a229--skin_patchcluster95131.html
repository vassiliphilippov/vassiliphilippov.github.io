skin patch

noun
medicated adhesive for drug delivery
Pronunciation: / skɪn pætʃ / (AmE), / skɪn pætʃ / (BrE)
A medicated adhesive pad applied to the skin to deliver a time-released dose of medication into the bloodstream.

Examples

Nicotine skin patch
He wore a skin patch on his arm to manage his pain throughout the day.
Some allergy treatments are administered using a skin patch rather than pills.
The doctor prescribed a skin patch to help her quit smoking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transdermal patch - a patch applied to the skin to deliver drugs through the skin
