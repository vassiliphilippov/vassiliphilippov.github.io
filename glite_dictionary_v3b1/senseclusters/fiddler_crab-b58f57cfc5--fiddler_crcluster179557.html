fiddler crab

noun
small crab with one large claw
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪd.lɚ kræb / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.lə kræb / (BrE)
a small burrowing crab found in American coastal regions, with the male having one greatly enlarged claw

Examples

Male fiddler crabs
Male fiddler crabs use their oversized claw to attract mates during the breeding season.
The fiddler crab digs burrows in the sand as a retreat from predators and heat.
We watched a fiddler crab wave its giant claw on the muddy shore.
