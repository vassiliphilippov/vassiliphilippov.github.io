quaddie bet

noun
bet on winners of four races
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑdi bɛt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdi bɛt / (BrE)
A bet where the bettor attempts to pick the winners of four designated races, typically the last four races of a meeting, often used in horse racing.

Examples

Place a quaddie bet
He placed a quaddie bet on the last four races of the day.
Many racegoers try a quaddie bet in hopes of a huge return from a small stake.
She explained how a quaddie bet can lead to big payouts if all selections win.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulator - a bet combining multiple selections into one wager
