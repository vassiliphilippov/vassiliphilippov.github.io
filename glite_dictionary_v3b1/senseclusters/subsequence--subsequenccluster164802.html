subsequence

noun
thing or event that follows another
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsiː.kwəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.siː.kwəns / (BrE)
a thing or event that comes after something else, especially as a follow-up or a part of a sequence following certain elements, often used in mathematics and linguistics

Examples

A subsequence in math
In mathematics, a subsequence is derived from another sequence by deleting some elements without changing the order of the remaining elements.
The DNA sample contained a subsequence that was crucial for the experiment.
The second movement can be seen as a subsequence to the first in this symphony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sequence - the order in which things follow each other
