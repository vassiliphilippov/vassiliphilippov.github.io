cholesteatoma

noun
middle ear growth
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˌlɛstiəˈtoʊmə / (AmE), / kɒˌlɛstiːəˈtəʊmə / (BrE)
an abnormal, noncancerous growth of skin cells in the middle ear that can damage hearing and nearby structures

Examples

A cholesteatoma growth
A cholesteatoma can cause hearing loss if not treated promptly by a specialist.
Doctors discovered a cholesteatoma during the patient's routine ear examination.
Surgery is often required to remove a cholesteatoma and prevent complications.
