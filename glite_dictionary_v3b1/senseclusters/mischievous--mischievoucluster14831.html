Synonyms

  • malicious
    5.8 4.90/m
    malicious is intended to harm or hurt someone on purpose, while mischievous suggests causing trouble in a less cruel, playful way.
  • malevolent
    8.0 1.97/m
    malevolent shows a strong wish to do evil or harm to others, while mischievous often means causing trouble without deep malice.
  • spiteful
    5.1 1.21/m
    spiteful means wanting to hurt or annoy someone out of anger, while mischievous can be less angry and more playful trouble.
  • mean
    2.2 22/m
    mean describes unkind or cruel behavior aimed to upset people, while mischievous often implies playful or minor troublemaking rather than cruelty.
