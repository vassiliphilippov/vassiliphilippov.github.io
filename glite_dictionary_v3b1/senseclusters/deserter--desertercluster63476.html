deserter

noun
person who abandons a cause or group
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɝːtər / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːtə / (BrE)
a person who leaves or abandons a cause, group, or belief, especially in a disloyal or treacherous manner

Examples

Political deserter
Many viewed him as a deserter after he switched political parties.
She felt betrayed when her longtime friend became a deserter of their shared beliefs.
The group treated the deserter with suspicion and anger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause, party, or group
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays a friend, country, principle, etc.
