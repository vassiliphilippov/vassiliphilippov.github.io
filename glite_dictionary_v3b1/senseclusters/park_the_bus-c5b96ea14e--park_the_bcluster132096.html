park the bus

verb
play extremely defensively (in sports)
Pronunciation: / pɑrk ðə bʌs / (AmE), / pɑːk ðə bʌs / (BrE)
(in sports, especially football/soccer) To adopt a very defensive strategy, usually by positioning most or all players near one’s own goal to prevent the opposition from scoring.

Examples

To park the bus defensively
Critics say that teams who park the bus make the game less exciting to watch.
The team decided to park the bus after taking the lead in the second half.
When down to ten men, they had no choice but to park the bus and hope for a draw.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attack - to play in an offensive or aggressive manner
  • synonym: defend - to protect one’s goal or area from attack in sports
