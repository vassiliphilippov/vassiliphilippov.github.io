Saratoga

proper noun
battle in the American Revolution, 1777
Pronunciation: / ˌsærəˈtoʊɡə / (AmE), / ˌsærəˈtəʊɡə / (BrE)
a decisive battle in 1777 during the American Revolution in which British forces under General Burgoyne were defeated by American troops, marking a turning point in the war

Examples

Saratoga battle 1777
General Burgoyne surrendered to American forces at Saratoga in 1777.
Many historians credit the American victory at Saratoga with persuading France to join the war.
The Battle of Saratoga is often considered the turning point of the American Revolutionary War.

  • synonym: Yorktown - the final major battle of the American Revolution
