stereotypy

noun
repetitive movement
9.9
Pronunciation: /ste.rɪ.ə.ˈtaɪ.piː/ (AmE), /ste.rɪ.ə.ˈtaɪ.pɪ/ (BrE)
a repetitive or ritualistic behavior, movement, or action, often seen in people or animals, especially under stress or with certain conditions

Examples

Repetitive stereotypy in children
The child exhibited stereotypy by repeatedly flapping his hands when excited.
Researchers observed stereotypy in animals kept in small enclosures for long periods.
Certain neurological disorders can cause stereotypy such as rocking or repetitive speech.
The child exhibited stereotypy by rocking back and forth for several minutes.
Researchers observed stereotypy in the laboratory mice as they repeatedly circled their cages.
Veterinarians often see stereotypy in horses kept in small stalls for long periods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compulsion - an irresistible urge to behave in a certain way
  • degree lower: habit - a regular repeated behavior
