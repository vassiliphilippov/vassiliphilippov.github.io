palatalize

verb
pronounce with tongue near the palate
9.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpælətəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpalətəlaɪz /; / ˈpalətəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
To pronounce a consonant with the tongue against or close to the hard palate.

Examples

To palatalize consonants
In some languages, speakers often palatalize the letter 't' before certain vowels.
Linguists study how people palatalize sounds in different dialects.
Russian consonants can be palatalized to change the meaning of words.
He carefully palatalised the /k/ sound, following the teacher's instruction.
In Russian, you must palatalise the consonant before certain vowels.
Many Slavic languages palatalise consonants before front vowels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depalatalize - to pronounce without involving the palate, especially after originally being palatalized
  • synonym: palatalize - pronounce a consonant with the tongue against the palate (American spelling)
