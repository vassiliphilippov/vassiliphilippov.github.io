ephyra

noun
young jellyfish stage
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfaɪrə / (AmE), / ɪˈfaɪrə / (BrE)
a young, free-swimming stage in the life cycle of a jellyfish, following the polyp stage

Examples

Young ephyra stage
The ephyra swims freely in the ocean before maturing into an adult jellyfish.
Scientists observed the transformation of the ephyra into the medusa stage in the laboratory.
Each ephyra eventually develops the characteristic bell shape of a mature jellyfish.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: medusa - the adult, sexually mature stage of a jellyfish
  • degree lower: polyp - the earlier, attached stage in the jellyfish life cycle
