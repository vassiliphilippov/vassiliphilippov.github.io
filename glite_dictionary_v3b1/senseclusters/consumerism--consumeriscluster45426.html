consumerism

noun
advocacy of consumer protection
Pronunciation: / kənˈsuːmərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːmərɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice or movement of protecting the interests and rights of consumers, especially by promoting fair trade and product safety.

Examples

Consumerism protects buyers
Many activists support consumerism to ensure that products are safe and honestly advertised.
Organizations dedicated to consumerism often campaign against misleading advertising and unfair business practices.
The rise of consumerism in the 20th century led to new laws concerning product quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consumer protection - the safeguarding of buyers' rights and interests
  • antonym: producerism - advocacy of the interests of producers as opposed to consumers
