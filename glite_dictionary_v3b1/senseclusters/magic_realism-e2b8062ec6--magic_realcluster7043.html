magic realism

noun
literary or artistic style mixing realism and fantasy
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒɪk ˈriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪk ˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a style or genre in literature or art in which realistic depiction is combined with surreal or fantastical elements

Examples

Magic realism style
Critics praised the film's use of magic realism to explore the boundaries between reality and imagination.
Many Latin American novels are written in the style of magic realism, blending fantasy with everyday life.
The artist's paintings, rooted in magic realism, depict ordinary scenes with extraordinary, dreamlike qualities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surrealism - art and literature movement focused on the irrational and dream-like
