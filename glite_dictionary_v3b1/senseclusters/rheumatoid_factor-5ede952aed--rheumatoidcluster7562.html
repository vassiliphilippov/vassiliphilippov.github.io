rheumatoid factor

noun
autoantibody found in rheumatoid arthritis
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈruːməˌtɔɪd ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɔɪd ˈfæktə / (BrE)
an autoantibody found in the blood of many people with rheumatoid arthritis and some other autoimmune diseases, used as a marker in diagnosis

Examples

High rheumatoid factor reading
A high rheumatoid factor reading can support a diagnosis of rheumatoid arthritis.
Some patients with autoimmune diseases test positive for rheumatoid factor even without arthritis symptoms.
The doctor ordered a blood test to measure the rheumatoid factor level in her serum.
