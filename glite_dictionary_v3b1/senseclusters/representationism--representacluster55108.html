representationism

noun
theory of mental representation
9.9
Pronunciation: /re.prɪ.zen.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪzəm/ /; / /rə.prə.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪzəm/ (AmE), /re.prɪ.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪzəm/ /; / /rə.prə.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪzəm/ (BrE)
the philosophical theory that mental states or perceptions represent the external world and that knowledge is based on these representations

Examples

Representationism in philosophy
According to representationism, our minds do not perceive objects directly but through mental images.
Many debates in philosophy of mind center around the validity of representationism.
Some critics argue that representationism cannot fully explain conscious experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct realism - the theory that perception allows direct awareness of the external world
  • synonym: representationalism - the view that perceptions are mental representations of the world
