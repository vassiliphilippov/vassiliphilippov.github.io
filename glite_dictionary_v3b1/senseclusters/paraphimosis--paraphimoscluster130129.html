paraphimosis

noun
trapped retracted foreskin
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəfaɪˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəfaɪˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the retracted foreskin of the penis cannot be returned to its normal position, causing swelling and pain

Examples

Paraphimosis causes swelling
If left untreated, paraphimosis can lead to serious complications such as restricted blood flow.
The doctor explained that paraphimosis is a urological emergency requiring prompt attention.
Swelling and pain are common symptoms when someone develops paraphimosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phimosis - a condition where the foreskin cannot be pulled back from the tip of the penis
