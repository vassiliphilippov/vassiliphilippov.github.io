statutory

adjective
prescribed or authorized by law
Pronunciation: / ˈstætʃ.uˌtɔr.i /; / ˈstætʃuˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈstætjʊtəri /; / ˈstatjʊtəri / (BrE)
required, permitted, created, or authorized by law or statute; relating to written laws made by a legislative body

Examples

A statutory law
He was accused of statutory violations connected to environmental regulations.
The company failed to comply with its statutory obligations regarding employee safety.
There are statutory requirements that all buildings must meet before they can be inhabited.
He gave evidence to the statutory committee investigating financial misconduct.
Her statutory rights as a tenant are protected by law.
The company must comply with all statutory obligations regarding employee safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: customary - based on custom or tradition, not written law
  • antonym: illegal - forbidden by law
  • synonym: legal - relating to the law
  • synonym: regulatory - relating to regulations or rules, not necessarily statutes
