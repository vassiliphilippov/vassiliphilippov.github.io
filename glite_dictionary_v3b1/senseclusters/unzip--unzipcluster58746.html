unzip

verb
extract files from ZIP archive
3.2
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈzɪp/ /; / /ʌn.ˈzɪp/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈzɪp/ /; / /ən.ˈzɪp/ (BrE)
(in computing) to decompress or extract the contents of a ZIP archive or other compressed file so individual files can be used.

Examples

To unzip a file
I had to unzip the downloaded folder before I could edit the documents.
She unzipped the archive and copied the images into the project's assets folder.
The installer automatically unzips the package and copies files to the installation folder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decompress - to restore compressed data to its original form.
  • synonym: extract - to take files out of an archive or container.
  • antonym: zip - to compress files into a ZIP archive (opposite operation).
