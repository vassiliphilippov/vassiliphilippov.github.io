noncumulative

adjective
not adding up totals
6.1
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈkjuː.mjə.lə.tɪv/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈkjuː.mjə.lə.tɪv/ (BrE)
not increasing or accumulating over time; not adding up from previous amounts or results

Examples

Noncumulative data report
The report presented noncumulative data for each quarter, making it easier to compare individual periods.
In statistics, noncumulative frequencies show the count for each category separately.
The teacher explained the difference between cumulative and noncumulative test scores to the class.
The final grade was based on a noncumulative exam, so earlier tests did not affect the result.
In this course, assignments are noncumulative, and each one is graded separately.
Because the policy is noncumulative, previous infractions are not considered when making decisions.
