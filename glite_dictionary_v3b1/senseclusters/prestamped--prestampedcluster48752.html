prestamped

adjective
marked in advance
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈstæmpt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈstæmpt / (BrE)
having a stamp or postage already applied before use, especially for mailing

Examples

A prestamped envelope
The envelope was prestamped, so I did not need to buy postage at the post office.
All official documents must be prestamped before they are distributed to the public.
He used a prestamped postcard to quickly send his reply.
She mailed the invitation in a prestamped envelope to make it easier for guests to reply.
The company provided prestamped postcards for customer feedback.
You can buy prestamped envelopes at the post office for convenience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unstamped - not bearing a stamp or official mark
