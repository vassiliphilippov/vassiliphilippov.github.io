intractably

adverb
in a difficult-to-control manner
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtræktəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtræktəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is difficult or impossible to manage, control, or deal with.

Examples

To be intractably difficult
Poverty can sometimes be intractably linked to systemic issues.
The situation became intractably complex as new problems arose each day.
The two parties were intractably opposed on the main points of the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intractable - hard to manage or solve
