shorebird

noun
wading bird of seashores and estuaries
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔːrˌbɝːd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a wading bird that frequents seashores and estuaries, such as sandpipers, plovers, or avocets

Examples

A shorebird on beach
Along the muddy flats, a solitary shorebird probed the sand for food.
Many species of shorebirds migrate thousands of miles between breeding and wintering grounds.
We watched a group of shorebirds feeding at the edge of the estuary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wader - a bird that wades in shallow water; synonym for shorebird
