phatic speech

noun
conversational language for social interaction

Language used in conversation primarily to establish or maintain social relationships rather than to convey information.

Examples

Phatic speech greetings
Every morning, the team exchanged phatic speech, such as greetings and comments about the weather, before starting work.
In many cultures, phatic speech like 'How are you?' serves to build rapport between speakers.
The conversation was filled with phatic speech rather than meaningful discussion of the issue.
