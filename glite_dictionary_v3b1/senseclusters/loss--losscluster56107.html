loss

noun
money or valuables lost, often in gambling or business
4.4 1.9
Pronunciation: /lɔːs/ /; / /lɑːs/ (AmE), /lɒs/ (BrE)
an amount of money or valuables lost, especially in business, gambling, or investment; when a business spends more than it earns

Examples

A business loss
After several losses on the stock market, he decided to seek professional advice.
The gambler accepted his losses and left the casino quietly.
Their insurance covered most of the losses from last year’s flood.
Due to decreased sales, the company reported a significant loss in the last quarter.
If the expenses outweigh the income, the business operates at a loss.
The startup managed to reduce its loss by cutting operational costs.
