rejector

noun
signal-blocking circuit
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛktər / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
an electronic circuit or component that blocks or reduces signals within a specific frequency band

Examples

Signal rejector circuit
The engineer installed a rejector to eliminate interference from nearby radio stations.
A rejector is essential in circuits where certain frequencies must be blocked.
They adjusted the rejector to target only the unwanted frequency band.

Related Terms

  • synonym: band-stop filter - a filter that attenuates signals within a certain frequency range
  • synonym: notch filter - a filter that removes a narrow band of frequencies
  • antonym: pass filter - a filter that allows certain frequencies to pass through
