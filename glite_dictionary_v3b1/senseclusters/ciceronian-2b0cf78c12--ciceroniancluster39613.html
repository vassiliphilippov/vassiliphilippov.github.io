Ciceronian

noun
admirer of Cicero
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.sə.ˈroʊ.nɪ.ən/ (AmE), /sɪ.sə.ˈrəʊ.nɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a person who admires, follows, or imitates Cicero or his style, especially in rhetoric or writing

Examples

A Ciceronian writer
As a proud Ciceronian, he valued eloquence and clarity in every speech he gave.
The debate club was led by a Ciceronian who encouraged students to study ancient Roman rhetoric.
Many writers in the Renaissance considered themselves Ciceronians and tried to imitate Cicero's style.
