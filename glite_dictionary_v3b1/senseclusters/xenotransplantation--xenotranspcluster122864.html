xenotransplantation

noun
transplant of tissue/organs between species
9.7 8.5
Pronunciation: /zen.oʊ.træns.plæn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ziː.noʊ.træns.plæn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /zen.əʊ.træns.plɑːn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ziː.əʊ.træns.plɑːn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of transplanting tissue or an organ from one species into another species, especially from animals to humans

Examples

Xenotransplantation of organs
Public debate about xenotransplantation often centers on ethical and medical concerns.
Recent advances in xenotransplantation could help address the shortage of human organs for transplantation.
Scientists are researching the safety risks associated with xenotransplantation from pigs to humans.
