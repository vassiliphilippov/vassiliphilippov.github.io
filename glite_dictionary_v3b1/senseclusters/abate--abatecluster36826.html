abate

verb
become less intense or strong
Pronunciation: / əˈbeɪt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to become less strong, intense, or severe, or to make something become less strong, intense, or severe

Examples

To abate pain
As the fever began to abate, the patient started to feel better.
Public anger over the scandal failed to abate even after the politician apologized.
The strong winds did not abate until late in the evening.
After several hours, the storm finally began to abate and the skies started to clear.
She waited for his anger to abate before speaking to him again.
The city took measures to abate air pollution during summer months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diminish - to become or make less
  • antonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
