paleoethnography

noun
study of Paleolithic human cultures
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊˌɛθˈnɑːɡrəfi /; / ˌpeɪlioʊˌɛθˈnɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊˌɛθˈnɒɡrəfi /; / ˌpæliəʊˌɛθˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The study and description of the cultures and lifestyles of Paleolithic humans based on archaeological evidence.

Examples

Paleoethnography of humans
Advances in archaeology have greatly expanded our understanding of paleoethnography.
Her research in paleoethnography revealed fascinating details about early human social structures.
Studying cave art is one method employed in paleoethnography to reconstruct ancient lifeways.
Advances in palaeoethnography have revealed much about tool use among Paleolithic societies.
She specializes in palaeoethnography, focusing on the daily lives of early humans in Europe.
The museum's new exhibit highlights the importance of palaeoethnography in understanding human prehistory.
