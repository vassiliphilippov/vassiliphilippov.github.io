Boris Spassky

proper noun
Russian chess world champion (1969–1972)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrɪs ˈspæski / (AmE), / ˈbɒrɪs ˈspæski / (BrE)
(born 1937) Russian chess grandmaster who was world champion from 1969 to 1972 and later moved to Paris.

Examples

Boris Spassky world champion
Boris Spassky later moved to France, where he continued to play chess at a high level.
Boris Spassky won the World Chess Championship in 1969 by defeating Tigran Petrosian.
The famous 1972 match between Boris Spassky and Bobby Fischer was watched around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Bobby Fischer - American chess grandmaster and Spassky's opponent in the 1972 world championship match
