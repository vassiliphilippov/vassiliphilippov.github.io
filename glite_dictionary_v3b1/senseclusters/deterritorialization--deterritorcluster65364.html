deterritorialization

noun
removal of territorial ties
9.9
Pronunciation: /diː.tə.rɪ.tɑːr.ɪ.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.tə.rɪ.tɔːr.ɪ.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.tə.rɪ.tɔːr.ɪ.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which something loses its connection to a specific place, territory, or cultural context

Examples

The deterritorialization of culture
The rise of digital media has contributed to the deterritorialization of cultural production.
Economic deterritorialization allows companies to operate beyond traditional national borders.
Some scholars argue that deterritorialization challenges the power of nation-states in the global era.
The spread of international media has contributed to the deterritorialization of cultural identities.
Some fear that deterritorialization weakens traditional customs and local languages.
Modern technology accelerates the deterritorialization of economic and social activities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: globalization - the process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence
  • antonym: localization - the process of adapting something to a specific place or culture
  • antonym: territorialization - the process of establishing control over a specific territory
