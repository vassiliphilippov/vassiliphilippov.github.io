try square

noun
carpentry/measuring tool for right angles
Pronunciation: / traɪ skwɛr / (AmE), / traɪ skweə / (BrE)
A tool consisting of a metal ruler fixed at a right angle to a thicker handle, used to test or mark right angles in carpentry and metalworking.

Examples

A try square tool
Always use a try square when marking lines if you want accurate corners in your project.
He bought a new try square from the hardware store to replace his old, rusty one.
The carpenter used a try square to check that the edges of the wood were perfectly perpendicular.
