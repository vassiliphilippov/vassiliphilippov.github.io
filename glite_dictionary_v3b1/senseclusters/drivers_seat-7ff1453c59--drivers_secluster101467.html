driver's seat

noun
position of control or authority
4.1 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / Ààdra…™v…ôrz siÀêt / (AmE), / Ààdra…™v…ôz siÀêt / (BrE)
a position of control or authority, especially one in which important decisions can be made or leadership is exercised

Examples

In the driver's seat of power
After years of hard work, she finally found herself in the driver's seat of the company.
He likes to be in the driver's seat when it comes to making important decisions.
Now that we've secured the funding, we're in the driver's seat for the next phase of development.

Synonyms

  • in the driver's seat
    4.4 0.77/m
    in the driver's seat means someone controls a situation and makes decisions, while driver's seat refers to that position of authority.
  • at the wheel
    4.3 1.35/m
    at the wheel describes someone guiding or leading an effort closely, while driver's seat emphasizes overall authority to make big decisions.
  • helm
    6.7 3.29/m
    helm names a formal leadership role often used for organizations or ships, while driver's seat is an everyday image for being in charge.
  • in the catbird seat
    9.1 0.08/m
    in the catbird seat means having an advantage or upper hand over others, while driver's seat focuses on being in control and making decisions.
  • behind the wheel
    3.4 4.02/m
    behind the wheel usually means physically driving a car, while driver's seat mainly means holding control or leadership in situations.
