universal

noun
trait or pattern found universally among people
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəl /; / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːrsəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəl / (BrE)
a trait, value, principle, or concept that is true or found everywhere or among all people or things

Examples

A moral universal
Some researchers believe that moral codes have certain universals across civilizations.
The anthropologist argued that language is a universal among human societies.
The study of emotions explores whether smiling is a universal or varies by culture.
A universal asserts that every entity in a certain class shares a property.
In medieval philosophy, the debate over universals was central to understanding the nature of reality.
The statement 'All humans are mortal' is an example of a universal in logic.
Philosophers have long discussed whether moral universals exist across all cultures.
The search for scientific universals helps us understand the laws of nature.
Some argue that language contains certain universals shared by all human societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolute - something that is universally valid or true
  • antonym: particular - a specific instance distinguished from a universal; the opposite of a universal in philosophy
