recession

noun
the act of returning something ceded
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
The act of giving back or returning something, such as land or rights, that was previously held.

Examples

Recession of land
During the negotiations, both parties discussed the recession of certain exclusive rights.
Historical documents confirmed the recession of the island to its original owners.
The treaty called for the recession of disputed territories to the neighboring state.
