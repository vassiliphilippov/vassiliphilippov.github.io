firmness

noun
solidity; resistance to pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝːmnəs /; / ˈfɜrmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being solid and not giving way when pressed; physical solidness or resilience (including muscle tone)

Examples

Firmness of the gums
Dentists often examine the firmness of your gums during a check-up.
She tested the firmness of the avocado by gently squeezing it.
The firmness of the mattress provided excellent support for his back.
Physical therapists often assess the firmness of muscles to monitor patient progress.
The athlete's daily workouts improved the firmness of his arms and legs.
With age, people may notice a decrease in muscle firmness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flaccidity - lacking firmness or muscle tone
  • synonym: hardness - the quality of being hard and resistant to pressure
  • antonym: softness - the quality of giving way easily to pressure
