Arenga pinnata

proper noun
Southeast Asian palm; source of sago and sap
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈrɛŋgə pɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / əˈrɛŋgə pɪˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A large Southeast Asian palm tree whose fibers, sweet sap, and pith are used for making wine and sago.

Examples

Arenga pinnata palm tree
Local artisans use fibers from Arenga pinnata to weave strong ropes and baskets.
The Arenga pinnata is widely cultivated for its sweet sap, which can be fermented into palm wine.
Traditional sago is often made from the trunk of the Arenga pinnata palm.
