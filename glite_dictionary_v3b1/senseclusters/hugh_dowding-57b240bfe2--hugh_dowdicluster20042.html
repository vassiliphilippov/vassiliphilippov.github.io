Hugh Dowding

proper noun
British RAF marshal; led air defense in WWII
Pronunciation: / hjuː ˈdaʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / hjuː ˈdaʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
(1882–1970) British marshal of the Royal Air Force who led the air defense during the Battle of Britain in World War II.

Examples

Hugh Dowding RAF leader
Hugh Dowding played a crucial role in organizing Britain's air defense during World War II.
A statue of Hugh Dowding stands in London to commemorate his leadership.
Many historians credit Hugh Dowding with helping to win the Battle of Britain.
