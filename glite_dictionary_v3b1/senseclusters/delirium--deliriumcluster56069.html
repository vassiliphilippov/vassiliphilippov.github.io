delirium

noun
disturbed, agitated mental state
5.6
Pronunciation: / dəˈlɪriəm / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəm / (BrE)
a disturbed state of mind with confusion, excitement, and sometimes hallucinations, often caused by illness or fever

Examples

A delirium state
After three days of high fever, she fell into a state of delirium.
In his delirium, he imagined that he was being chased by invisible enemies.
The patient shouted incoherently during his delirium, alarming the hospital staff.
After the surgery, he experienced a brief episode of delirium and did not recognize his family.
High fever in children can sometimes lead to delirium, with vivid hallucinations and disorientation.
The patient emerged from delirium after a few days of careful medical supervision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confusion - lack of clear and orderly thought
  • antonym: lucidity - clarity of thought, opposite of mental confusion or delirium
