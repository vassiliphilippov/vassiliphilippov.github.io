distrainment

noun
seized property
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstreɪnmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈstreɪnmənt / (BrE)
property or goods that have been seized by legal authority to satisfy a debt or claim

Examples

Distrainment of goods
The court ordered the distrainment of the tenant's furniture to cover unpaid rent.
After the distrainment, the goods were listed for public auction.
The landlord initiated distrainment proceedings to recover the debt owed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forfeiture - loss of property or money because of a breach of law
  • synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession of property by legal right
