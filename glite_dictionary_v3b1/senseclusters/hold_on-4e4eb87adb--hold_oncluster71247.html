hold on

verb
wait on the telephone line
1.1 1.2
Pronunciation: /hoʊld ɑːn/ /; / /hoʊld ən/ /; / /hoʊld ɔːn/ (AmE), /həʊld ɒn/ /; / /həʊld ən/ (BrE)
to wait or pause for a short time, especially during a conversation or on the phone

Examples

To hold on a moment
Could you hold on while I transfer your call to the manager?
If you hold on a moment, I'll check if she's available.
Please hold on; your call is important to us and will be answered shortly.
Hold on a minute while I check if he's in his office.
If you hold on, I'll be right with you.
She asked me to hold on during the phone call while she found the document.
Hold on a minute, I'll be right back with your order.
Could you hold on until the meeting starts?
She asked him to hold on while she checked the schedule.
