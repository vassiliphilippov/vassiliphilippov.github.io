European lobster

noun
a species of lobster native to European waters
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈlɑbstər / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈlɒbstə / (BrE)
a species of lobster (Homarus gammarus) found along the Atlantic coast of Europe, with a blue-black shell that turns red when cooked

Examples

The European lobster species
Fishermen along the coast of France often catch the European lobster during the summer months.
The European lobster is prized for its delicate flavor and firm texture.
Unlike its American cousin, the European lobster is generally smaller and has a darker shell.
European lobster has a distinctive blue-black shell that turns red when cooked.
Overfishing has threatened the populations of European lobster along several parts of the coast.
The chef prepared a delicious meal using freshly caught European lobster.
