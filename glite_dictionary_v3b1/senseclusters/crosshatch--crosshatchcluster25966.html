crosshatch

verb
to shade with crossing lines (in drawing)
7.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɔːs.hætʃ/ /; / /ˈkrɑːs.hætʃ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.hætʃ/ (BrE)
to shade or fill an area with intersecting sets of parallel lines, especially in drawing or engraving

Examples

To crosshatch a drawing
She crosshatched the shaded area to make the drawing look more realistic.
The artist began to crosshatch the sky to create texture and depth.
You can crosshatch gently to suggest shadow without overpowering the sketch.
