ventral root

noun
front branch of a spinal nerve
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛntrəl ruːt / (AmE), / ˈvɛntrəl ruːt / (BrE)
the front branch of a spinal nerve that carries motor fibers from the spinal cord to the muscles

Examples

The ventral root nerve
Damage to the ventral root may result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Motor impulses travel away from the spinal cord through the ventral root.
The surgeon carefully identified the ventral root during the spinal operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dorsal root - the rear branch of a spinal nerve that carries sensory fibers to the spinal cord
