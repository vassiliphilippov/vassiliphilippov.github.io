Scottish Lowlands

proper noun
southern low-lying region of Scotland
Pronunciation: / ˌskɑː.tɪʃ ˈloʊ.ləndz / (AmE), / ˌskɒ.tɪʃ ˈləʊ.ləndz / (BrE)
the southern, relatively low-lying region of Scotland, distinguished from the more mountainous Highlands

Examples

The Scottish Lowlands region
Many people settled in the Scottish Lowlands during the Industrial Revolution.
The Scottish Lowlands are known for their rolling hills, fertile farmland, and historic cities.
Tourists often visit the Scottish Lowlands to explore castles and cultural sites.
