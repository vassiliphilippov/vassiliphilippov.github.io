homely

adjective
cozy and comfortable like home
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊm.liː/ /; / /ˈhoʊm.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊm.liː/ /; / /ˈhəʊm.lɪ/ (BrE)
pleasantly simple, cozy, comfortable, or plain; not showy or elaborate (especially British English)

Examples

Homely atmosphere
She prefers a homely atmosphere to the formality of a luxury hotel.
The cottage had a homely charm that made everyone feel at ease.
The tearoom was unmistakably homely, with mismatched chairs and homemade cakes.
He admired the homely simplicity of their wedding ceremony.
Her homely advice helped me see things more clearly.
The restaurant serves homely meals that remind me of my childhood.
Despite its homely appearance, the chair was sturdy and comfortable.
She prefers homely meals to gourmet dishes served in fancy restaurants.
The homely decor gave the cottage a modest, welcoming feel.
