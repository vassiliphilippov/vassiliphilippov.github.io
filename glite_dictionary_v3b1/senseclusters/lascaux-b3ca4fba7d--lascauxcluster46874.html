Lascaux

proper noun
prehistoric cave with paintings in France
9.5
Pronunciation: / læsˈkoʊ / (AmE), / læsˈkəʊ / (BrE)
a cave in southwestern France famous for its Paleolithic wall paintings, depicting animals and other scenes from prehistoric times

Examples

Lascaux cave paintings
Archaeologists have studied Lascaux extensively to learn more about early human art and life.
Many tourists hope to visit Lascaux, though the original cave is closed to the public to preserve the paintings.
The paintings found in Lascaux are considered some of the world's finest examples of prehistoric artwork.
