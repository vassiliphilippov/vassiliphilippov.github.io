sharp

adverb
suddenly or abruptly, especially in movement
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrp / (AmE), / ʃɑːp / (BrE)
used to describe a sudden or abrupt change, especially in direction or degree

Examples

Sharp turn
At the intersection, you need to go sharp left.
He stopped sharp when he saw the warning sign.
The road turns sharp right after the bridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abruptly - suddenly and unexpectedly
  • antonym: gradually - slowly over time, not suddenly
