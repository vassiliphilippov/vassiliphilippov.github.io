epithalamus

noun
brain region with pineal gland
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈθæləməs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈθæləməs / (BrE)
a part of the brain located near the thalamus that is involved in hormone regulation and contains the pineal gland

Examples

The epithalamus brain part
The epithalamus plays a key role in regulating the body's sleep-wake cycle.
Damage to the epithalamus can affect hormone production and daily rhythms.
Scientists study the epithalamus to better understand how the brain controls hormones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypothalamus - a region of the brain controlling body temperature and hormone release
  • synonym: thalamus - a large mass of gray matter in the brain that relays sensory signals
