atrial artery

noun
artery supplying the heart's atria
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
a branch of the coronary artery that supplies blood to the muscles of the atria in the heart

Examples

The atrial artery branch
Blockage in an atrial artery may contribute to certain heart rhythm problems.
Damage to the atrial artery can reduce blood supply to the upper chambers of the heart.
The surgeon carefully identified the atrial artery during the procedure to avoid accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coronary artery - the main artery that supplies blood to the heart muscle
