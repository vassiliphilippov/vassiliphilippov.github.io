unsurprise

verb
to remove surprise
9.3
Pronunciation: /ʌn.sər.ˈpraɪz/ /; / /ən.sər.ˈpraɪz/ (AmE), /ʌn.sə.ˈpraɪz/ /; / /ən.sə.ˈpraɪz/ (BrE)
to make something no longer surprising by revealing it or making it predictable

Examples

To unsurprise a friend
She decided to unsurprise him by telling him about the party before he arrived.
The leaked information unsurprised the audience, who already knew the announcement.
He did not want to unsurprise his friends, so he kept the secret.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reveal - to make something known that was secret
  • synonym: spoil - to ruin the surprise or enjoyment of something
  • antonym: surprise - to cause someone to feel sudden wonder or amazement
