display

verb
animal attract attention by showing or posing
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
(of animals) to attract attention by showing a body part or adopting a particular posture, often as part of courtship or territorial behavior.

Examples

Bird displays feathers
During the mating season, the bird displays by puffing out its chest and singing.
The lizard displayed its bright throat pouch when threatened by a rival.
The peacock displays his feathers to attract a mate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhibit - to show something publicly so it can be seen
