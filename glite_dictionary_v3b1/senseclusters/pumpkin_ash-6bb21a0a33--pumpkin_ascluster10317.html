pumpkin ash

noun
a type of ash tree native to the U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌmpkɪn æʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌmpkɪn æʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous tree (Fraxinus profunda) native to central and southeastern United States, recognized for its hairy branchlets and characteristically swollen trunk base.

Examples

The pumpkin ash tree
Lumber from the pumpkin ash is valued for its strength and workability.
Researchers studied the effects of flooding on the growth of pumpkin ash.
The swamps in the region are dominated by pumpkin ash and bald cypress trees.
