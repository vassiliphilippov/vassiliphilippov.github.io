countermove

noun
a responsive military or strategic move
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntɚˌmuːv / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəməʊv / (BrE)
a move made in response to an opponent's action, especially an attacking action made by a defending force to regain lost ground or hinder an enemy's progress

Examples

A countermove in battle
After losing ground in the negotiation, the company launched a strategic countermove to regain its advantage.
The general ordered a swift countermove to repel the invading forces from the captured territory.
Their opponent's aggressive play forced the team to come up with a clever countermove.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterattack - a military attack made in response to one by an enemy
