flash welding

noun
welding process using electric arc to join metals
5.4
Pronunciation: / flæʃ ˈwɛldɪŋ / (AmE), / flæʃ ˈwɛldɪŋ / (BrE)
A process of butt welding where an electric arc is struck between two metal pieces, causing them to melt and join; commonly used for joining segments of metal pipe.

Examples

Flash welding process
Flash welding is commonly used to join railway tracks efficiently and securely.
Due to the speed and strength it offers, flash welding is favored in many manufacturing industries.
The technician performed flash welding to connect the two sections of steel pipe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butt welding - welding process in which two pieces are joined end-to-end
