gas-discharge lamp

noun
lamp using electric discharge through gas
3.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /gæs ˈdɪs.tʃɑːrdʒ læmp/ /; / /gæs dɪs.ˈtʃɑːrdʒ læmp/ (AmE), /gæs ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ læmp/ /; / /gæs dɪs.ˈtʃɑːdʒ læmp/ (BrE)
an electric lamp in which light is produced by an electric discharge between two electrodes through a gas in a glass tube

Examples

A gas-discharge lamp tube
A gas-discharge lamp typically requires a ballast to regulate the electric current.
Many streetlights use gas-discharge lamps because of their high efficiency and long lifespan.
The laboratory was illuminated by a row of gas-discharge lamps suspended from the ceiling.
