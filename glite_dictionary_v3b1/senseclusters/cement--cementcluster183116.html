cement

verb
make a relationship or bond strong/permanent
2.2 3.6
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈment/ /; / /sɪ.ˈment/ (AmE), /sə.ˈment/ (BrE)
to make a relationship, agreement, or connection strong or permanent; to join things together in a lasting way (figurative)

Examples

To cement a friendship
The leader’s speech did much to cement the group’s unity.
The two companies decided to cement their partnership with a long-term contract.
Their summer travels helped cement their close bond.
A sense of duty can cement a team together in times of crisis.
The merger was intended to cement their position as market leaders.
Their shared values helped to cement a strong friendship over the years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to put apart
  • synonym: strengthen - make or become stronger
  • synonym: unite - to bring or come together as a whole
  • antonym: weaken - make or become weaker
