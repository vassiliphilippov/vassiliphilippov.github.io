amphora

noun
ancient jar with two handles for wine or oil
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.fə.rə / (AmE), / ˈam.fə.rə / (BrE)
A tall ancient jar with two handles and a narrow neck, used for holding oil, wine, or other liquids.

Examples

An ancient amphora
Archaeologists discovered an intact amphora buried beneath the ruins of the old city.
Merchants often transported wine in large ceramic amphorae across the Mediterranean.
The museum displayed a beautifully painted amphora from ancient Greece.
