steamroller

verb
proceed forcefully, ignore opposition
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːmˌroʊlər /; / ˈstiːmˌroʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstiːmˌrəʊlə / (BrE)
to forcefully overcome, defeat, or make something happen by using overwhelming power or pressure, often ignoring opposition

Examples

To steamroller opponents
Public opinion was steamrollered by the government's aggressive campaign.
She tends to steamroller over others' ideas during meetings.
The company tried to steamroller the new policy through despite objections.
His company is known for steamrollering smaller competitors out of the market.
She felt her ideas were steamrollered during the meeting by senior managers.
The government tried to steamroller the new law through parliament without debate.
He tends to steamroller any opposition in meetings with his assertive arguments.
The construction crew steamrollered the old pavement before laying down new asphalt.
The new law was quickly steamrollered through the parliament despite protests.
