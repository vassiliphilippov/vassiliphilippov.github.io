excuse you

interjection
sarcastic phrase to signal rudeness
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskjuz ju / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːz juː / (BrE)
(informal, often sarcastic) Used to express annoyance or to call attention to someone’s rude behavior.

Examples

Excuse you rude behavior
Excuse you, but I wasn't finished talking.
If you're going to sneeze without covering your mouth, excuse you!
She rolled her eyes and said, "excuse you", when he interrupted her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excuse me - used to politely get someone's attention or ask to be excused
