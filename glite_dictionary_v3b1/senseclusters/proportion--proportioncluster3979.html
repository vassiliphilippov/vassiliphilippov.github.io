proportion

noun
relationship between part and whole (fraction)
6.4
Pronunciation: / pɹəˈpɔɹʃən /; / prəˈpɔrʃən / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
the relationship of a part to the whole, often expressed as a fraction or ratio

Examples

A proportion of people
A small proportion of the population voted in the last election.
Only a tiny proportion of the budget is spent on education.
The proportion of sugar to flour is important in this recipe.
A good architect pays close attention to the proportion of windows to walls in a building's design.
Only a small proportion of applicants were accepted into the program this year.
The proportion of sugar in the recipe is critical for the desired flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ratio - a comparison of two quantities by division
