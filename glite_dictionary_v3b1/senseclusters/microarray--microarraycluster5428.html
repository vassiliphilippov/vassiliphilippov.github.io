microarray

noun
gene analysis tool
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊəˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊəˌreɪ / (BrE)
a laboratory device with many tiny spots of DNA or other molecules, used to analyze gene expression or detect biological molecules

Examples

A microarray device
Scientists used a microarray to compare gene expression between healthy and diseased cells.
The results from the microarray provided valuable insights into the genetic basis of the disorder.
A microarray can test thousands of genes in a single experiment, saving time and resources.
Researchers used a microarray to analyze gene expression in thousands of cells at once.
The new microarray technology allows scientists to detect multiple diseases from a single blood sample.
A microarray can help identify which genes are active in different types of cancer.
Scientists used a microarray to compare gene activity in healthy and diseased cells.
The laboratory technician prepared the microarray for the genetic analysis experiment.
Advances in microarray technology have made large-scale gene studies much faster.
