ramshackle

adjective
in a very poor or dilapidated condition
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈræmˌʃækəl / (AmE), / ˈræm.ʃæk.əl / (BrE)
(of a building or object) in a state of severe disrepair; badly constructed or maintained.

Examples

A ramshackle old house
A ramshackle car rattled down the street, barely holding together.
The old barn was so ramshackle that it looked ready to collapse at any moment.
They lived in a ramshackle cottage at the edge of the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broken-down - in bad condition due to age or neglect
  • synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin
  • antonym: sturdy - strongly and solidly built
