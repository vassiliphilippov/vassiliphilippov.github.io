shew

verb
archaic form of 'show'; to demonstrate
7.3
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ʃəʊ / (BrE)
(archaic) To demonstrate the truth or validity of something, as by example, explanation, or experiment.

Examples

He shewed proof
In his treatise, the scientist shewed the effects of the treatment beyond doubt.
The author did shew the accuracy of his argument through numerous historical references.
They sought to shew the existence of the new element by careful experimentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: show - to demonstrate or prove something by example or evidence
