overdetermined

adjective
excessively caused or constrained
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈtɜːrmɪnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
having more causes, reasons, or constraints than are necessary to explain or determine something

Examples

Overdetermined outcome
The outcome of the experiment was overdetermined by multiple overlapping variables.
In literature, a character's actions can sometimes feel overdetermined by their background and circumstances.
The system is overdetermined when there are more equations than unknowns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overexplained - explained with more detail or reasons than necessary
  • antonym: underdetermined - having too few causes or constraints to determine something
