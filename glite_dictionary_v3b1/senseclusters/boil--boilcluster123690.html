boil

verb
heat to boiling point
Pronunciation: / bɔɪl / (AmE), / bɔɪl / (BrE)
to heat a liquid until it boils, or to cook something in boiling liquid

Examples

To boil water
Let the water boil for ten minutes before adding the pasta.
Please boil the soup on high heat until it starts bubbling.
She forgot to boil the milk before using it in the recipe.
Please boil the eggs for exactly six minutes.
She always boils the water before making tea to ensure it's safe to drink.
The recipe says to boil the pasta until it is tender.
At higher altitudes, water boils at a lower temperature.
Please wait until the water boils before adding the pasta.
Turn off the heat as soon as the soup starts to boil.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: evaporate - to change from liquid to vapor without necessarily reaching boiling point
  • degree lower: simmer - to cook something just below boiling point
  • degree higher: vaporize - to be converted into vapor, often intensively
