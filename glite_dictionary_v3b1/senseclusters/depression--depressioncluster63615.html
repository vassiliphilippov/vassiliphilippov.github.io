depression

noun
angular distance below the horizon
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The angular distance of an object below the horizon, especially in relation to celestial bodies.

Examples

Sun's depression below horizon

The angle of depression helps astronomers determine the position of celestial objects.
The navigator measured the depression of the star to calculate their latitude.
