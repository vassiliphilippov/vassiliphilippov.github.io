muzzle

verb
to prevent speaking by covering the mouth
6.7 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌz.əl /; / ˈmʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌz.əl /; / ˈmʌzəl / (BrE)
to put a covering over the mouth of an animal or person to prevent biting, eating, or speaking

Examples

To muzzle a dog
During the heist, the guards were muzzled and tied up.
He threatened to muzzle anyone who tried to reveal the secret plans.
The kidnappers muzzled the witness to stop her from calling for help.
Some laws require owners to muzzle their pets in public spaces.
The veterinarian had to muzzle the cat before performing the examination.
You should muzzle the dog if it tends to snap at strangers.
He refuses to muzzle his opinions, even when they are controversial.
The government tried to muzzle the press during the crisis.
The park requires owners to muzzle aggressive dogs for everyone’s safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to express opinions; opposite of suppressing speech
  • synonym: gag - to put something over or in someone's mouth to prevent them from speaking or making noise
  • synonym: silence - to stop someone from speaking or expressing opinions
  • antonym: unmuzzle - to remove a muzzle from an animal
