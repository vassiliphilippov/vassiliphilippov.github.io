outperform

verb
perform better than others
5.5 3.9
Pronunciation: /aʊt.pər.ˈfɔːrm/ /; / /ˈaʊt.pər.fɔːrm/ (AmE), /aʊt.pə.ˈfɔːm/ /; / /ˈaʊt.pə.fɔːm/ (BrE)
to do better than someone or something; to achieve superior results compared to others

Examples

To outperform competitors
Despite his young age, the swimmer outperformed all the other finalists at the event.
Our sales team is on track to outperform last year's record-breaking results.
This new phone consistently outperforms its competitors in battery life.

  • antonym: underperform - to perform less well than expected or less well than others
