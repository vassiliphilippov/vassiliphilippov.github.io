enter the picture

verb
become involved or relevant in a situation
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɛntɚ ðə ˈpɪktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛntə ðə ˈpɪktʃə / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation or to become newly relevant or significant to an event or process

Examples

When problems enter the picture
Problems began to arise when regulatory issues entered the picture.
Several new investors entered the picture as the company began to expand.
We were making good progress until unforeseen expenses entered the picture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get involved - to take part in a situation or activity
  • antonym: leave the picture - to cease being involved or relevant; to be no longer part of a situation
