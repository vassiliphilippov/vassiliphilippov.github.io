decompressive

adjective
reducing pressure
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
relating to or causing a reduction in pressure, especially as part of a medical procedure

Examples

Decompressive surgery
The surgeon performed a decompressive procedure to relieve pressure on the patient's spine.
A decompressive craniectomy may be necessary after severe head trauma.
Doctors recommended a decompressive operation to prevent further nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compressive - causing or relating to increased pressure
  • synonym: relieving - reducing pain or pressure
