city slicker

noun
sophisticated city dweller
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪti ˌslɪkɚ /; / ˈsɪti ˌslɪkər / (AmE), / ˈsɪti ˌslɪkə / (BrE)
a person from the city, especially one who is sophisticated or not used to rural life

Examples

A city slicker person
Despite being a city slicker, she quickly adapted to life on the ranch.
He looked like a real city slicker in his expensive suit and shiny shoes.
The farmers laughed at the city slicker who had never seen a cow up close before.
He was such a city slicker that he had never seen a cow up close before.
Locals could always spot a city slicker by their expensive clothes and lost expression.
The city slicker struggled to understand the customs of the small farming town.

Related Terms

  • antonym: country bumpkin - a person from the countryside regarded as unsophisticated
