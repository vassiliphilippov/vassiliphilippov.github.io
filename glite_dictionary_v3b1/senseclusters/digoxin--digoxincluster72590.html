digoxin

noun
cardiac drug for heart failure and arrhythmia
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡɑksɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈɡɒksɪn / (BrE)
A medicinal drug used to treat certain heart conditions, including congestive heart failure and irregular heart rhythms; it helps the heart beat more strongly.

Examples

Digoxin medicine
It is important to monitor blood levels when administering digoxin to avoid toxicity.
Patients with heart failure may benefit from taking digoxin as part of their treatment.
The doctor prescribed digoxin to help regulate her heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digitalis - a genus of plants that are the source of digoxin
