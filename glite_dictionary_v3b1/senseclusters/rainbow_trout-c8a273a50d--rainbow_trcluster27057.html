rainbow trout

noun
a species of trout with a reddish stripe
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪnˌboʊ traʊt / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ traʊt / (BrE)
A species of trout with a reddish stripe along its sides, native to the rivers and coastal waters of western North America and often used in sport fishing and aquaculture.

Examples

Wild rainbow trout
Many chefs prize rainbow trout for its delicate flavor and firm texture.
She caught a large rainbow trout during their fishing trip in Alaska.
The river is well known for its population of rainbow trout.
