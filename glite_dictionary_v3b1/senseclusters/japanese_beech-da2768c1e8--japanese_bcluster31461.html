Japanese beech

noun
a beech tree species native to Japan
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz bitʃ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpɑːniːz biːtʃ / (BrE)
a species of beech tree native to Japan, known for its soft, light yellowish-brown wood

Examples

The Japanese beech tree

Many furniture makers in Japan use Japanese beech because of its fine texture.
The Japanese beech is prized for its beautiful wood and elegant form.
