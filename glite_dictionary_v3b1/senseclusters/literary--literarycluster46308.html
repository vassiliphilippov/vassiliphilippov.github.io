literary

adjective
relating to literature or writing
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri /; / ˈlɪt̬.ə.rer.i / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri /; / ˈlɪt.ər.əri / (BrE)
relating to literature, books, or the study and appreciation of written works; having knowledge or interest in literature

Examples

Literary works
He took a great interest in literary history and classic works.
She is well-known for her literary criticism of 20th-century novels.
The festival celebrates the literary achievements of local authors.
Although he's an engineer by trade, he is remarkably literary in his interests and reading habits.
His literary background helped him write insightful book reviews.
She joined a literary society to discuss classic novels with fellow enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bookish - fond of books or studying; literary in nature
  • antonym: nonliterary - not relating to literature
  • antonym: unliterary - not having or showing knowledge of literature
