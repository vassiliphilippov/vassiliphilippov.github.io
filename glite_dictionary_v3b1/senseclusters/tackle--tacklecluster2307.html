tackle

noun
nautical equipment for sails and masts
Pronunciation: / ˈtæk.əl / (AmE), / ˈtæk.əl / (BrE)
the equipment of ropes, pulleys, and other gear used to support and control a ship's masts and sails

Examples

Ship tackle
A sudden gust put great strain on the ship’s tackle, but it held firm.
The crew checked the tackle before setting sail to ensure everything was secure.
Years at sea had taught her to repair damaged tackle in all weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rigging - the system of ropes and cables used to support a ship's masts and sails
