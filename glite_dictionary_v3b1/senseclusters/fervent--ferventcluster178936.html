fervent

adjective
having intense or passionate feelings
6.1 2.25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…úrv…ônt / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀêv…ônt / (BrE)
having or displaying very strong and sincere feelings; passionate or intense.

Examples

Fervent supporter
A fervent admirer wrote letters expressing her deep admiration for the artist.
His fervent hope was that the team would win the championship.
She gave a fervent speech urging everyone to support the cause.

Synonyms

  • fervid
    8.4 0.18/m
    fervid is intense and full of strong feeling in speech or action, while fervent is broadly passionate and sincere.
  • passionate
    2.8 20/m
    passionate shows strong, often visible emotion and desire, while fervent suggests deep, steady belief or sincere feeling.
  • impassioned
    5.8 2.10/m
    impassioned feels like a heated speech or plea, while fervent describes long-lasting inner passion and sincere belief.
  • ardent
    6.3 3.70/m
    ardent often shows eager, active support or love, while fervent stresses deep, heartfelt feeling and sincerity.
  • perfervid
    9.9 0.04/m
    perfervid sounds rare and intensely emotional, often stronger than usual, while fervent is common and sincerely passionate.
