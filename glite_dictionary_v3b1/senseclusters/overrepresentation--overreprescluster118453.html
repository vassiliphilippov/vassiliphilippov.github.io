overrepresentation

noun
excess representation
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition of being represented, present, or shown in greater numbers or influence than is fair or accurate

Examples

An overrepresentation of groups
The committee found an overrepresentation of certain groups in leadership positions.
Activists argued that overrepresentation of rural areas led to unfair policy decisions.
The study highlighted the overrepresentation of men in senior management roles.
The overrepresentation of men in executive roles has sparked discussions about workplace equality.
Researchers found an overrepresentation of certain species in the protected area compared to others.
Activists are concerned about the overrepresentation of minority groups in the prison population.
The overrepresentation of certain groups in the media can lead to stereotypes.
Researchers found an overrepresentation of urban residents in the survey results.
There is concern about the overrepresentation of negative news stories on television.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disproportion - an unequal or unfair balance between parts
  • synonym: exaggeration - the act of making something seem more important or larger than it is
  • antonym: underrepresentation - having less representation than is proportional to one's share
