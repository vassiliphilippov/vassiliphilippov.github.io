Ebola hemorrhagic fever

A severe, often fatal infectious disease in humans and primates, caused by the Ebola virus, characterized by high fever, severe internal bleeding, and organ failure, and transmitted through contact with bodily fluids.

Examples

The Ebola hemorrhagic fever outbreak
During the 2014 outbreak, many West African countries struggled to contain Ebola hemorrhagic fever.
Individuals infected with Ebola hemorrhagic fever require immediate isolation to prevent transmission.
Symptoms of Ebola hemorrhagic fever include a sudden high fever, vomiting, and internal bleeding.

Synonyms

  • Ebola
    4.8 0.08/m
    Ebola is the common name for the disease people get, while Ebola hemorrhagic fever is the formal term highlighting severe bleeding and organ failure.
  • Ebola fever
    4.6 0.01/m
    Ebola fever is a widely used name in outbreaks, while Ebola hemorrhagic fever is a medical term that stresses heavy bleeding and organ failure.
