validation

noun
process of confirming validity or accuracy
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˌvælɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvælɪˈdeɪʃən /; / ˌvalɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of checking or proving that something is valid, accurate, or acceptable

Examples

The validation process
Her theory gained credibility after extensive validation by other experts.
The results will require validation before they are published in the journal.
We used independent testing for validation of our findings.
He sought validation for his theory through a series of rigorous tests.
Scientific experiments require validation by independent researchers to confirm the results.
The software underwent a thorough validation process before its release to ensure reliability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invalidate - to make something not valid
  • antonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong or false
  • synonym: verification - the process of establishing the truth or accuracy of something
