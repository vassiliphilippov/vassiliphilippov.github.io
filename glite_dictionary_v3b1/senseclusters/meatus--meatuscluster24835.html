meatus

noun
an anatomical body passageway
Pronunciation: / miˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / miˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
A natural body passageway or opening, especially in anatomical contexts.

Examples

A meatus opening
A narrow meatus can cause difficulties in bodily functions depending on its location.
Doctors examined the nasal meatus to check for blockages.
The external auditory meatus directs sound towards the eardrum.
