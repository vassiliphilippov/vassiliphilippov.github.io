as we say

adverb
used to introduce a conventional or figurative phrase
Pronunciation: / æz wi seɪ / (AmE), / æz wiː seɪ / (BrE)
used to indicate that a word or expression is being used in a conventional or figurative way; in a manner of speaking.

Examples

As we say in English
His career has, as we say, hit a rough patch lately.
She picked herself up and started again, as we say, from scratch.
The project was, as we say, dead in the water before it even started.

Related Terms

  • synonym: so to speak - used to indicate that a phrase is not to be taken literally
