adjourn

verb
suspend or pause a meeting
5.3 3/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàd í…ùn / (AmE), / …ôÀàd í…úÀên / (BrE)
to pause or end a meeting, session, or gathering, usually with the intention to continue later

Examples

To adjourn the meeting
Let's adjourn for a fifteen-minute coffee break.
The chairman decided to adjourn the meeting until after lunch.
The court will adjourn at noon and reconvene in the afternoon.
After a long debate, the committee finally adjourned for lunch.
The judge decided to adjourn the hearing until next week.
The meeting will adjourn at 5 pm sharp.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • recess
    4.8 0.5/m
    recess temporarily suspends proceedings for a short break in meetings or courts, while adjourn means pausing or ending a meeting to continue later.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convene - to begin or bring together for a meeting or session
  • synonym: recess - to take a break from a formal meeting or session
  • antonym: resume - to begin again after a pause or interruption
