grommet

noun
metal or plastic ring reinforcing a hole
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɑːmət / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒmɪt / (BrE)
A metal or plastic ring used to reinforce a hole in fabric, leather, or other material, often to allow the passage of a cord or fastener.

Examples

A grommet hole
Most shoes have a grommet for the laces near the top.
She inserted a metal grommet into the curtain to reinforce the hole.
The tarp had a grommet at each corner for securing it with rope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eyelet - a small hole in cloth or leather, often surrounded with stitching or a metal ring
