ammonia

noun
pungent gas, NH3, used in industry and cleaning
Pronunciation: / əˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / əˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a strong-smelling gas (NH3) or its solution in water, commonly used in cleaning products and fertilizers

Examples

Strong ammonia gas
Farmers often use ammonia as a fertilizer to enrich their soil.
Mixing bleach with ammonia can produce dangerous fumes.
The strong smell of ammonia made it difficult to breathe in the room.
Be careful when using ammonia as a cleaning product, as its fumes can be harmful.
Never combine ammonia with bleach, as the reaction produces dangerous gases.
The janitor mixed water with ammonia to clean the floor tiles more effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ammonium - the ion NH4+, a related compound
  • synonym: ammonium hydroxide - another name for aqueous ammonia solution
