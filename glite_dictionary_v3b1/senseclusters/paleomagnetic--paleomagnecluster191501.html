paleomagnetic

adjective
ancient magnetic properties
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌoʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk /; / ˌpæliəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the ancient magnetic properties recorded in rocks or the Earth's crust

Examples

Paleomagnetic evidence
Researchers use paleomagnetic evidence to trace the movement of tectonic plates over millions of years.
The paleomagnetic data from volcanic rocks helped confirm the theory of continental drift.
By analyzing paleomagnetic signatures, scientists can reconstruct the history of Earth's magnetic field.
Researchers analyzed the palaeomagnetic data to understand the movement of tectonic plates.
The palaeomagnetic record in these rocks reveals changes in Earth's magnetic field over millions of years.
By studying palaeomagnetic signatures, scientists can reconstruct the history of continental drift.

Related Terms

  • antonym: geomagnetic - relating to the Earth's present magnetic field
