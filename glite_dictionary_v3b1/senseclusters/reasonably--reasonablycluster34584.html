reasonably

adverb
to a moderate or fair degree
2.2 11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêz…ôn…ôbli / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêz…ôn…ôbli / (BrE)
to a fair or moderate degree; fairly, moderately, or to some extent.

Examples

Reasonably fast
She managed to finish the project reasonably quickly.
The house is reasonably priced for the area.
The weather was reasonably good during our trip.

Synonyms

    1.0 57/m
    fairly is common in everyday speech for a moderate degree, while reasonably sounds a bit more formal and sensible.
  • moderately
    2.8 6.68/m
    moderately describes a middle level or amount, while reasonably often adds a sense of fairness or sensible judgment.
  • somewhat
    2.0 67/m
    somewhat is used for a slight or small degree, while reasonably suggests a fairer, more moderate amount.
