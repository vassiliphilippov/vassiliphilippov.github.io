B.Th.U.

noun
British thermal unit (unit of heat)
Pronunciation: / ˌbiː tiː ɛtʃ ˈjuː / (AmE), / ˌbiː tiː ɛtʃ ˈjuː / (BrE)
a unit of heat equal to the amount required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at one atmosphere pressure

Examples

One B.Th.U. unit
Heating values are often measured in B.Th.U. in engineering contexts.
One B.Th.U. is equivalent to approximately 252 calories.
The air conditioner has a capacity of 5,000 B.Th.U. per hour.
