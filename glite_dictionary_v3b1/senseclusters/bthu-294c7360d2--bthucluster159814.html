B.Th.U.

noun
British thermal unit (unit of heat)
9.9
Pronunciation: /biː tiː juː/ (AmE), /biː tiː juː/ (BrE)
a unit of heat equal to the amount required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at one atmosphere pressure
