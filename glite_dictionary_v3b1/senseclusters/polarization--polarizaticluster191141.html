polarization

noun
social or political division
7.2
Pronunciation: /poʊ.lə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /poʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pəʊ.lə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pəʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process or condition in which opinions, attitudes, or groups become divided into two sharply contrasting camps, reducing compromise or constructive dialogue.

Examples

Political polarization
Recent studies show growing polarization between urban and rural voters on many key issues.
The mayor called for dialogue to reduce polarization and rebuild trust across community groups.
Social media algorithms can intensify political polarization by showing users content that matches their views.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consensus - general agreement or shared opinion among a group; often reduced when polarization increases
  • synonym: division - a separation or split between groups or opinions
