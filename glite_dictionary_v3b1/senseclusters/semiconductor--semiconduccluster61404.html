semiconductor

noun
substance with conductivity between conductor and insulator
6.6 4.5
Pronunciation: /se.mɪ.kən.ˈdʌk.tər/ /; / /se.miː.kən.ˈdʌk.tər/ /; / /se.aɪ.kən.ˈdʌk.tər/ (AmE), /se.mɪ.kən.ˈdʌk.tə/ /; / /se.miː.kən.ˈdʌk.tə/ /; / /se.aɪ.kən.ˈdʌk.tə/ (BrE)
a substance (such as silicon) whose ability to conduct electricity is between that of a conductor and an insulator, and is used in electronic devices

Examples

New semiconductors materials
Researchers are developing new types of semiconductors for faster computer chips.

The electrical properties of a semiconductor can be modified by adding impurities in a process called doping.
A semiconductor can both conduct and insulate electricity, depending on conditions.
Modern smartphones contain millions of tiny semiconductors that make computation possible.
Silicon is the most widely used semiconductor in the electronics industry.
