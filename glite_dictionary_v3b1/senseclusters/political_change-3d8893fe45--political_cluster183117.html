political change

noun
modification in government or policy
1.0
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪdɪkəl tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪk(ə)l tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a shift or modification in the structures, policies, or leaders of government, typically as a result of elections, reforms, or social movements

Examples

Political change after election
Many citizens demanded political change after years of corruption and mismanagement.
Rapid political change can lead to instability if not carefully managed.
The country experienced significant political change following the free elections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reform - the process of making changes to institutions or practices
  • degree higher: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order
  • antonym: status quo - the existing state of affairs, especially regarding social or political issues
