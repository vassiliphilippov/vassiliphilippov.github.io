Synonyms

  • throw away
    1.8 20/m
    throw away is putting something in a bin or taking it to the rubbish collection to get rid of it, while trash means to dispose of things generally.
  • discard
    2.7 6/m
    discard is a formal word for getting rid of an item, while trash is more casual and used in everyday speech.
  • throw out
    1.8 9/m
    throw out is removing something by putting it in the bin or outside, while trash covers any way of getting rid.
  • toss out
    2.7 2/m
    toss out is casually throwing something away in everyday talk, while trash is neutral and fits formal or casual use.
  • toss away
    2.0 0.2/m
    toss away is a casual way to throw small items into the bin, while trash is a more general term.
  • junk
    3.0 0.6/m
    junk means to get rid of something as useless or old, while trash is neutral and less judgmental about value.
  • scrap
    3.7 1/m
    scrap means to break up or stop using an old object for parts, while trash simply means to throw it away.
  • dispose
    3.4 7/m
    dispose often means get rid of something by selling, giving, or throwing away, while trash only means throwing away.
  • dump
    2.6 30/m
    dump means to drop or get rid of large amounts carelessly or illegally, while trash is neutral about method or scale.
  • chuck out
