trustful

adjective
showing or feeling trust
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌst.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌst.fəl / (BrE)
willing to trust people or believe what they say; showing or feeling trust

Examples

Trustful children
Children are often trustful by nature, believing what adults tell them.
Even after several disappointments, she remained trustful of her friends.
His trustful attitude made him popular but sometimes vulnerable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distrustful - unwilling to trust others; suspicious
  • synonym: trusting - willing to trust others easily
