arcuate artery

noun
curved artery in the foot
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrkjuˌeɪt ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˈɑːkjʊət ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
a curved artery in the foot that forms an arch and supplies blood to the toes

Examples

The arcuate artery in foot
Blood flow to the toes depends partly on a healthy arcuate artery.
In some people, the arcuate artery may be more prominent than in others.
The surgeon identified the arcuate artery during the foot operation to avoid damaging it.
