ethyl group

noun
C2H5 chemical group in organic compounds
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈiːθɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of atoms with the formula C2H5, derived from ethane by the removal of one hydrogen atom, often present in organic compounds

Examples

An ethyl group molecule
Each molecule of ethanol contains an ethyl group bonded to a hydroxyl group.
Substituting a methyl group with an ethyl group can affect drug metabolism.
The addition of an ethyl group changes the compound's properties significantly.
