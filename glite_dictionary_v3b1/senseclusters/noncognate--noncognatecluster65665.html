noncognate

adjective
not usual partner
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɑɡneɪt / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒɡneɪt / (BrE)
not being the usual or specific partner, ligand, substrate, or matching molecule in biological or chemical interactions

Examples

Noncognate substrate
The enzyme was able to bind a noncognate substrate, indicating a lack of strict specificity.
Researchers observed that the protein interacted with several noncognate molecules in the experiment.
A noncognate ligand can sometimes trigger unexpected biological responses in a cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cognate - being the usual or specific partner or matching molecule
