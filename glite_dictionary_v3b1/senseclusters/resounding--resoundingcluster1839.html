resounding

adjective
loud and echoing (of sound)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈzaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
very loud, strong, and clear; echoing or emphatic (often used for sounds or to emphasize success)

Examples

A resounding cheer
A resounding cheer echoed across the stadium as the team scored.
Her voice was resounding enough to be heard at the back of the auditorium.
The hall was filled with the resounding sound of applause after the speech.
The orchestra received a resounding applause at the end of the concert.
Their new product launch was a resounding success, exceeding all expectations.
A resounding crash echoed through the empty hallway late at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faint - not strong or clear; weak
  • synonym: resonant - deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • synonym: thunderous - extremely loud or forceful
