prefocusing

noun
advance focus adjustment
7.2
Pronunciation: / priːˈfoʊkəsɪŋ / (AmE), / priːˈfəʊkəsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of adjusting the focus of a device in advance, especially in optics or photography

Examples

Prefocusing a camera
Photographers often use prefocusing to capture fast-moving subjects with greater sharpness.
By prefocusing the lens, you can reduce the time needed to take a clear picture.
In sports photography, prefocusing is a useful technique for anticipating the action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: focusing - the act of adjusting a device to make an image clear
