gharial

noun
Indian crocodilian
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈriːəl /; / ˈɡɛriəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːrɪəl /; / ˈɡɑːriɑːl / (BrE)
a large, fish-eating crocodilian with a very long, narrow snout, native to rivers in India and nearby regions

Examples

The gharial crocodile
The gharial is easily recognized by its extremely long and narrow snout.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining wild gharial populations in India.
Unlike other crocodilians, the gharial feeds almost exclusively on fish.
The endangered gharial can be found in the rivers of northern India and Nepal.
Unlike other crocodilians, the gharial has a long, thin snout adapted for catching fish.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining wild gharial populations from extinction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alligator - a large reptile similar to a crocodile but with a broader snout
  • synonym: crocodile - a large aquatic reptile with a long snout and powerful jaws
