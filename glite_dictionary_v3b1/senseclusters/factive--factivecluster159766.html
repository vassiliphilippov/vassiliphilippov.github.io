factive

noun
truth-presupposing word
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfæk.tɪv/ /; / /ˈfæk.təv/ (AmE), /ˈfæk.tɪv/ /; / /ˈfæk.təv/ (BrE)
a word, especially a verb, that presupposes the truth of its following clause or complement

Examples

A factive like 'know'
The verb 'know' is a common factive because it implies the truth of what follows.
Linguists often analyze whether a verb is a factive or not in complex sentences.
In the exam, students had to identify each factive in the given text.
