loose sentence

noun
sentence with main clause first, followed by subordinate elements
Pronunciation: / lus ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / luːs ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
A sentence structure in which the main clause appears at the beginning, followed by one or more subordinate clauses or phrases.

Examples

A loose sentence structure
In a loose sentence, the main idea comes first, followed by additional information.
She decided to go to the park, even though it was raining heavily, making her a little late—a classic loose sentence.
The author often uses a loose sentence structure to add descriptive detail after the main point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: periodic sentence - a sentence in which the main clause or predicate is withheld until the end
