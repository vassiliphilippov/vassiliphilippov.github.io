parochially

adverb
in a narrow or limited manner
Pronunciation: / pəˈroʊkiəli / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəli / (BrE)
In a parochial way; with a narrow or limited outlook or concern, especially restricted to a local or small area.

Examples

To think parochially
He judged the decision parochially, failing to consider its impact beyond his own community.
The committee continued to act parochially, ignoring suggestions from outside experts.
Their interests were pursued parochially, with little regard for national issues.

  • synonym: parochial - having a narrow or limited outlook or scope
