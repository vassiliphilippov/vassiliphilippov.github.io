brinkmanship

noun
pushing a risky situation to the limit
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪŋk.mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbrɪŋk.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice, especially in politics or diplomacy, of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety before stopping, typically to force the desired outcome

Examples

Brinkmanship in politics
After months of brinkmanship between the two countries, an agreement was finally reached.
His style of brinkmanship worries his allies, who fear a miscalculation could have serious consequences.
The negotiations were marked by brinkmanship as each side refused to compromise until the very last minute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: risk-taking - the act or fact of doing something that involves danger or risk
