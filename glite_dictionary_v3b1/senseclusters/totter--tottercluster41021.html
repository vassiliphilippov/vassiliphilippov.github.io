Synonyms

    5.7 3/m
    teeter is to rock or sway in place, while totter means walk unsteadily as if about to fall.
  • falter
    5.6 0.4/m
    falter moves hesitantly or loses strength, while totter describes weak walking that seems about to make someone fall.
  • stagger
    4.0 6/m
    stagger walks in a wide or heavy way from weakness, while totter is a smaller, shaky walk as if about to fall.
  • reel
    4.6 2/m
    reel moves in a dizzy, uncontrolled way, while totter is an unsteady walk that looks likely to end in falling.
  • stumble
    1.6 11/m
    stumble trips or loses balance in one step, while totter means keep walking in a shaky, unsteady way.
  • wobble
    3.8 3/m
    wobble moves or shakes side to side, often without walking, while totter specifically means to walk unsteadily as if falling.
