seek

verb
try or attempt to achieve
1.0 90/m
Pronunciation: / siÀêk / (AmE), / siÀêk / (BrE)
to try to find, get, or achieve something

Examples

To seek a new job
Despite many failures, he sought to overcome the challenge.
She always seeks to learn new skills in her career.
The committee is seeking to improve public safety in the city.
Every spring, many birds seek nesting sites in the trees.
The investigators seek evidence to solve the case.
Tourists often seek out hidden gems when visiting a new city.
Many graduates seek jobs in the city after finishing university.
She decided to seek help from a professional counselor.
They are seeking new opportunities abroad.

Synonyms

  • look for
    1.0 202/m
    look for means trying to find someone or something in everyday speech, while seek sounds more formal and general.
  • search
    1.0 95/m
    search means looking carefully or systematically for something, while seek is broader and can mean trying to obtain or achieve.
  • pursue
    3.5 71/m
    pursue means working steadily to get or achieve a goal, while seek is more neutral and can mean look for or try to obtain.
  • go after
    2.1 25/m
    go after means making an active effort or following to get something, while seek is more general and less physical.
  • go for
    2.5 6.77/m
    go for means choosing to try to get or achieve something, often in an informal way, while seek is more neutral.

Related Terms

  • antonym: find - to discover or locate something
  • antonym: give up - to stop making an effort to achieve or obtain something
  • synonym: look for - to attempt to find something
  • synonym: pursue - to follow or try to obtain a goal, especially over a long period
  • synonym: search - to try to find something by looking carefully
  • synonym: try - to make an effort to do something
