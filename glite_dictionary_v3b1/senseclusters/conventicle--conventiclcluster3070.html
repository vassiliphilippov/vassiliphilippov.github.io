conventicle

noun
secret religious meeting
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛn.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɛntɪkəl / (BrE)
a secret or unauthorized meeting, especially for religious worship, held in defiance of official regulations

Examples

A secret conventicle
During the persecution, villagers gathered at a conventicle deep in the forest to worship in secret.
Early Protestants risked imprisonment for attending a conventicle outside the state-sanctioned churches.
The government outlawed the conventicle, fearing it would spread dissent against the established church.
