start-off

noun
beginning of a contest or activity
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrt ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈstɑːt ˌɒf / (BrE)
the initial moment or event that marks the beginning of a contest, race, or activity

Examples

The start-off moment
At the start-off of the event, the crowd cheered loudly.
He missed the start-off and had to catch up with the others.
The runners took their positions, waiting anxiously for the start-off.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finish - the end of a race or contest
  • synonym: kickoff - the start of an event, especially in sports
