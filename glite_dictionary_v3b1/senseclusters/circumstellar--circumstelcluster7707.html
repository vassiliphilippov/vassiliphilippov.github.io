circumstellar

adjective
around a star
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrkəmˈstɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstelə / (BrE)
located or occurring in the region surrounding a star

Examples

circumstellar disk
Scientists detected a circumstellar disk of gas and dust around the newly formed star.
The presence of circumstellar material can influence the evolution of planetary systems.
Astronomers study circumstellar environments to understand how planets are born.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interstellar - located or occurring between stars
