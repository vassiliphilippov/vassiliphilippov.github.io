creep

verb
move slowly and close to the ground
2.5 14/m
Pronunciation: / krip / (AmE), / kriÀêp / (BrE)
to move slowly and carefully, often to avoid being noticed, usually with the body close to the ground

Examples

To creep along quietly
A lizard crept along the garden wall, blending in with the stones.
She watched the child creep under the table during the game of hide and seek.
The cat tried to creep silently across the floor to catch the mouse.
He tried to creep into the room without waking anyone.
She crept down the hallway to avoid being seen.
The cat began to creep closer to the unsuspecting bird.

Synonyms

  • crawl
    1.0 22/m
    crawl moves forward slowly with the body close to the ground, while creep means moving slowly and quietly to avoid being noticed.
  • sneak
    2.8 23/m
    sneak means moving quietly or secretly to avoid being seen, while creep usually involves moving slowly close to the ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crawl - to move slowly with the body close to the ground
  • synonym: sneak - to move quietly and secretly to avoid being noticed
  • synonym: tiptoe - to walk quietly and carefully on one's toes
