stooper

noun
person who searches for discarded winning tickets
Pronunciation: / ˈstuːpər / (AmE), / ˈstuːpə / (BrE)
a person at a racetrack who searches for winning parimutuel tickets carelessly discarded by others

Examples

Racetrack stooper
After the last race, the stooper managed to find two tickets worth fifty dollars.
Some racetracks have posted signs warning stoopers to stay clear of restricted areas.
The stooper spent hours scanning the grandstand for forgotten winning tickets.
