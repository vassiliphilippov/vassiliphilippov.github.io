gateway drug

noun
a drug leading to use of more harmful drugs
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪtˌweɪ drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪtˌweɪ drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that is not very harmful or addictive itself, but may lead people to try more dangerous or addictive drugs

Examples

A gateway drug for teens
He warned that vaping could serve as a gateway drug for teenagers.
Many experts debate whether marijuana should be classified as a gateway drug.
The concept of a gateway drug remains controversial among scientists and policymakers.
Parents worry that vaping may serve as a gateway drug for teenagers exploring other substances.
Some experts argue that marijuana is often used as a gateway drug before trying harder substances.
The idea of alcohol being a gateway drug has sparked much debate among health professionals.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hard drug - a drug that is considered highly addictive or dangerous, such as heroin or cocaine
  • degree lower: soft drug - a drug that is considered less harmful, such as cannabis or nicotine
