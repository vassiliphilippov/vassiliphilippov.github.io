special court-martial

noun
military court for minor offenses

A military court that tries members of the armed forces for offenses less serious than those tried by a general court-martial, typically consisting of at least three officers.

Examples

A special court-martial trial
In certain cases, a special court-martial may impose confinement for up to one year.
The accused was brought before a special court-martial for misconduct during training exercises.
Three officers served as members on the special court-martial panel.
