reedy

adjective
having a thin, high sound like a reed instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈridi / (AmE), / ˈriːdi / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) having a tone that is thin, high, and somewhat like a reed instrument.

Examples

Reedy voice
His reedy voice carried easily across the crowded hall.
She sang in a reedy tenor that was both fragile and bright.
The clarinet produced a reedy sound that stood out in the orchestra.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sonorous - having a deep, full sound
