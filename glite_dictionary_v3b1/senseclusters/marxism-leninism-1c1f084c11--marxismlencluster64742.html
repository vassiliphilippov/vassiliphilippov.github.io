Marxism-Leninism

noun
political theory combining Marx and Lenin's ideas
7.4 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / Àåm…ërkÀås…™z…ôm Ààl…õn…ôÀån…™z…ôm / (AmE), / Àåm…ëÀêks…™z…ôm Ààl…õn…™n…™z…ôm / (BrE)
A system of political, economic, and social ideas based on the theories of Karl Marx and Vladimir Lenin, especially as practiced in the former Soviet Union, which holds that imperialism is the highest stage of capitalism and emphasizes the leadership of a vanguard party.

Examples

Marxism-Leninism theory
She studied Marxism-Leninism to understand the ideological foundations of the Eastern Bloc.
The Soviet Union was governed according to the principles of Marxism-Leninism.
The party's policies were rooted in a strict interpretation of Marxism-Leninism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and profit motive
  • synonym: communism - a political theory advocating class war and leading to a society in which property is publicly owned
