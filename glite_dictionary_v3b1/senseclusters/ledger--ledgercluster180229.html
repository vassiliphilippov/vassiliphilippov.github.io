ledger

noun
book of financial accounts
1.3 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒɚ /; / ˈlɛdʒər / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒə / (BrE)
a book or other record used to keep financial accounts, especially in business or accounting

Examples

Company ledger
At the end of the quarter, the company's ledger showed a significant profit.
Each department is responsible for keeping its own ledger to track expenses.
The accountant entered each transaction into the ledger before closing the books.
After years of use, the company's old ledger was worn and filled with handwritten numbers.
He carefully balanced the accounts in his ledger before submitting the report.
The accountant recorded all expenses in the ledger at the end of each day.
Our accountant keeps a detailed ledger to track all business transactions.
She checked the ledger to ensure all credits and debits matched.
The company discovered a discrepancy in the ledger during the annual audit.
