mean distance

noun
average of closest and farthest distances in orbit
2.9
Pronunciation: / miːn ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / miːn ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
the arithmetic mean of the greatest and least distances of a celestial body (such as a satellite or secondary star) from its primary, especially as used in astronomy

Examples

The mean distance of planets
Astronomers calculate the mean distance to describe the orbits of planets around the sun.
The mean distance from Earth to the Moon is approximately 384,400 kilometers.
The satellite's mean distance helps scientists predict its position at different times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: average distance - the average separation between two objects
