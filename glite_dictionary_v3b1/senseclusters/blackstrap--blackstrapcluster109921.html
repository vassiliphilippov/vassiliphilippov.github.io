blackstrap

noun
dark syrup from molasses
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌstræp / (AmE), / ˈblækˌstræp / (BrE)
a dark, thick syrup that remains after the final extraction of sugar from molasses

Examples

Dark blackstrap syrup
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of blackstrap to give the cake a rich flavor.
Some people use blackstrap as a natural sweetener in their morning oatmeal.
Because of its strong taste, blackstrap is often used in gingerbread and baked beans.
