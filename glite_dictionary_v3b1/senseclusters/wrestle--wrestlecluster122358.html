wrestle

verb
to twist and struggle the body
3.7 1.6
Pronunciation: /ˈre.səl/ (AmE), /ˈre.səl/ (BrE)
to fight or struggle physically with someone or something, especially by holding and trying to force them down

Examples

To wrestle an opponent
She wrestled with the tangled ropes, barely able to move her arms.
The injured bird wrestled in the grass, trying to get back on its feet.
The toddler wrestled in his sleep, turning and twisting under the blankets.
She used to wrestle competitively while she was in college.
The two boys wrestled in the backyard until they were out of breath.
We watched the children wrestling on the grass, laughing and rolling over each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grapple - to engage in a close hand-to-hand struggle
  • synonym: wriggle - to twist and turn the body with quick movements
  • synonym: writhe - to twist the body, especially as from pain or struggle
