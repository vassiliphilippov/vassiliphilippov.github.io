chinook salmon

noun
flesh of a large Pacific salmon used as food
Pronunciation: / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈsælmən / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈsæmən / (BrE)
the pink or white flesh of a large Pacific salmon, prized for its flavor and commonly used as food

Examples

Grilled chinook salmon
Fresh chinook salmon is highly sought after in gourmet restaurants.
The chef prepared grilled chinook salmon with a lemon-dill sauce for the special.
We bought a fillet of chinook salmon at the local fish market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: king salmon - another name for chinook salmon, the species Oncorhynchus tshawytscha
