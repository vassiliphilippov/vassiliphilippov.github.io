aleatory contract

noun
contract dependent on an uncertain event
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.li.ə.tɔːr.iː ˈkɑːn.trækt/ /; / /ˈæl.i.ə.tɔːr.iː ˈkɑːn.trækt/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.lɪ.ə.tə.riː ˈkɒn.trækt/ /; / /ˈæl.ɪ.ə.tə.riː ˈkɒn.trækt/ (BrE)
A contract in which the obligation of one or both parties depends on the occurrence of an uncertain event.

Examples

An aleatory contract
An aleatory contract involves obligations that are triggered only if a specific uncertain event occurs.
Insurance policies are typical examples of aleatory contracts because payouts depend on accident or loss.
The court ruled that the agreement was an aleatory contract and not enforceable as a wager.
