anastomotic vein

noun
vein connecting parts of the brain's venous system
Pronunciation: / əˌnæstəˈmɑtɪk veɪn / (AmE), / əˌnastəˈmɒtɪk veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that connect different parts of the cerebral venous system and facilitate blood flow within the brain

Examples

Anastomotic vein in brain
Blockage of an anastomotic vein can impair cerebral blood drainage.
The study mapped the connections between each anastomotic vein and major sinuses in the brain.
The surgeon carefully avoided the anastomotic vein during the brain procedure.
