light-sensitive

adjective
reacting to visible light
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌsɛnsətɪv / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌsɛnsətɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) sensitive to visible light, especially in a way that causes a physical or chemical change.

Examples

Light-sensitive film
Photographic film is light-sensitive and must be handled in darkrooms to avoid exposure.
Some plants have light-sensitive leaves that close at night and open in sunlight.
The security device uses a light-sensitive sensor to detect movement in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photosensitive - sensitive to light, especially ultraviolet or visible light
