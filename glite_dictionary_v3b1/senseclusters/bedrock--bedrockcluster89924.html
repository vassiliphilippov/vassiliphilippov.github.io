bedrock

noun
solid rock under soil
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛdˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɛd.rɒk / (BrE)
the solid rock layer that lies beneath soil and loose surface materials

Examples

Exposed bedrock
Over millions of years, rivers have eroded the soil, exposing the bedrock below.
The drill could not penetrate the bedrock beneath the construction site.
The stability of the building depends on its foundation being anchored to bedrock.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: subsoil - the layer of soil directly beneath the topsoil
