microlensing

noun
gravitational light bending
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌlɛnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌlɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
a phenomenon in which the light from a distant object is bent and magnified by the gravity of a smaller, intervening object

Examples

Microlensing event
Scientists detected a distant exoplanet through microlensing when its star passed in front of another.
The discovery of dark matter objects has been aided by the study of microlensing events.
Astronomers use microlensing to find planets that are otherwise invisible to telescopes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravitational lensing - the bending of light by gravity from a massive object
