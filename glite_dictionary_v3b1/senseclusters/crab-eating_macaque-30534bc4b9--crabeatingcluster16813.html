crab-eating macaque

noun
monkey native to Southeast Asia
Pronunciation: / kræb ˈitɪŋ məˈkæk / (AmE), / kræb ˈiːtɪŋ məˈkɑːk / (BrE)
a monkey of Southeast Asia, Borneo, and the Philippines, known for its adaptability and occasional consumption of crabs

Examples

The crab-eating macaque monkey
In some regions, the crab-eating macaque is considered both a sacred animal and a crop pest.
Researchers observed a troop of crab-eating macaques using rocks to open shellfish.
The crab-eating macaque is often found near rivers and mangrove forests.
