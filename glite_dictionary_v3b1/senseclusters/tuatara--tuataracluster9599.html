tuatara

noun
lizard-like reptile native to New Zealand
8.6 9.8
Pronunciation: /tuː.ə.ˈtɑː.rə/ (AmE), /tuː.ə.ˈtɑː.rə/ (BrE)
a nocturnal, lizard-like reptile found only in New Zealand, the sole surviving member of the order Rhynchocephalia

Examples

A tuatara reptile
A tuatara can live for more than a hundred years in the wild.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the endangered tuatara on New Zealand's islands.
The tuatara is sometimes called a living fossil because it has changed little over millions of years.
