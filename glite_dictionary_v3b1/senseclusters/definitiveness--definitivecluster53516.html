definitiveness

noun
quality of being definite
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɪnətɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪnətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being definite, especially the grammatical property that marks a noun phrase as referring to a specific, identifiable entity

Examples

The definitiveness of the article
The definitiveness of the article in the sentence makes the noun refer to a specific object.
Linguists study how definitiveness affects the meaning of noun phrases in different languages.
Teachers often highlight definitiveness when explaining the use of 'the' and 'a' in English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: definiteness - the state of being definite or clearly defined
  • antonym: indefiniteness - the state of not being specific or clearly identified
