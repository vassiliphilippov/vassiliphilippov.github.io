blatter

noun
noisy chatterer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblætər / (AmE), / ˈblætə / (BrE)
a person who talks foolishly, noisily, or at great length, often without saying anything important

Examples

A noisy blatter
The blatter in the office made it hard for anyone to concentrate on their work.
During the lecture, a persistent blatter kept interrupting the speaker with irrelevant comments.
She was known as a blatter who could talk for hours without ever getting to the point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk quickly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about unimportant things
  • antonym: listener - a person who listens rather than talks
