providentially

adverb
by divine intervention or good fortune
7.4
Pronunciation: / prəˈvɑːdənʃəli /; / prəˈvɪdənʃəli / (AmE), / prəˈvɪdənʃəli /; / prəˈvɪd(ə)ntʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that seems to happen by divine intervention or by lucky chance

Examples

Arrived providentially on time
He arrived providentially just in time to prevent a serious accident.
The missing documents were providentially discovered the day before the trial.
The storm changed course providentially, sparing the coastal villages from destruction.
Providentially, help arrived before the situation became dangerous.
Providentially, their flight was delayed just long enough for them to catch the last bus home.
The couple met again, providentially, at a café they both rarely visited.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fortuitously - by chance, especially in a lucky way
  • synonym: fortunately - in a lucky or fortunate manner
  • antonym: unluckily - in an unlucky or unfortunate manner
