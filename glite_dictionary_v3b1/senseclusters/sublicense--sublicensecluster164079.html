sublicense

verb
to grant a third-party license
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌlaɪsəns / (BrE)
to grant a license to a third party under the terms of an existing license

Examples

To sublicense rights
The publisher decided to sublicense the translation rights to a company overseas.
You must obtain permission before you sublicense the patented technology to another manufacturer.
Their agreement allows them to sublicense the software to third-party developers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: license - to give official permission to use something
