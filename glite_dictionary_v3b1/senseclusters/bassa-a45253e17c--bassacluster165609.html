Bassa

proper noun
West African script
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːsə / (AmE), / ˈbæsə / (BrE)
the traditional script, also known as Bassa Vah, used to write the Bassa language of West Africa

Examples

Bassa script
The ancient Bassa script is an important part of the region's cultural heritage.
Linguists are working to preserve the Bassa writing system for future generations.
A museum exhibit displayed rare manuscripts written in Bassa.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Latin alphabet - the writing system used for many world languages, including English
  • synonym: Vai - a syllabic script used for the Vai language of Liberia
