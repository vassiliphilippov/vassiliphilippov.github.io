Prunus japonica

noun
East Asian shrub with pale blossoms and red fruit
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːnəs dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of woody shrub native to East Asia, cultivated for its white or pale pink blossoms and smooth red fruit.

Examples

Prunus japonica shrub
Horticulturists prize Prunus japonica for its compact size and ornamental value.
In late summer, the Prunus japonica produces small, bright red fruit.
The Prunus japonica is often planted in gardens for its beautiful spring flowers.
