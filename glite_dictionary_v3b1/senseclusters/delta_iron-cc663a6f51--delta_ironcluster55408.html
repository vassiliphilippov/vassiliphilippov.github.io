delta iron

noun
high-temperature allotrope of iron
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛltə ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈdɛltə ˈaɪən / (BrE)
an allotrope of iron that is stable at temperatures between 1403°C and 1532°C, just below its melting point.

Examples

Delta iron phase
Delta iron forms when iron is heated above 1403°C in a laboratory setting.
The crystal structure of delta iron differs from other allotropes due to its body-centered cubic arrangement.
Understanding the transformation to delta iron helps metallurgists control the properties of steel.
