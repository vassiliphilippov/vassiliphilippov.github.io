workaround

verb
to bypass a problem
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜrkəˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkəˌraʊnd / (BrE)
to avoid or bypass a problem, restriction, or limitation by using a temporary or improvised method

Examples

To workaround a problem
When the software crashed, the engineers had to workaround the issue to meet the deadline.
She managed to workaround the new policy by finding a temporary solution.
They often workaround technical problems instead of fixing them permanently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confront - to face a problem directly
