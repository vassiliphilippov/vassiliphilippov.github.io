harden

verb
become less sensitive, get used to
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to make or become less sensitive or more used to something, especially something unpleasant, through repeated exposure

Examples

To harden a person
His experiences in the military hardened him to the harsh realities of life.
Repeated losses gradually hardened the team to disappointment.
Years of working in the emergency room had hardened her to the sight of injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inure - to accustom someone to something unpleasant
  • antonym: soften - to make or become less severe or less hard
