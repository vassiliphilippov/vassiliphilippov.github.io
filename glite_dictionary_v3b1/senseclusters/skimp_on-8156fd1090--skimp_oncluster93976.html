skimp on

verb
use less than needed; economize negatively
6.8 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / skÉªmp É‘n / (AmE), / skÉªmp É’n / (BrE)
to use or provide less of something than is needed, often in order to save money or effort

Examples

To skimp on food
He often skimped on meals during college to save money.
Many companies try to skimp on employee benefits to reduce costs.
You shouldn't skimp on sunscreen when spending time outdoors in the summer.

Synonyms

  • scrimp
    7.4 0.41/m
    scrimp means saving or spending very little and living frugally, while skimp on means providing too little to save money.
