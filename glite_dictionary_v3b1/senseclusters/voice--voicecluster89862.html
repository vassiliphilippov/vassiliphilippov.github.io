voice

noun
medium or agent of expression
2.2 2.8
Pronunciation: /vɔɪs/ (AmE), /vɔɪs/ (BrE)
a person, group, or medium that expresses opinions or represents others; a means or right to express ideas or feelings

Examples

A voice for change
She became a voice for environmental activists worldwide.
The committee acted as the voice of small business owners in parliament.
The newspaper served as the voice of the community during the crisis.
She finally found the courage to voice her concerns during the meeting.
The novel gave voice to issues often overlooked in society.
When people unite, their voice can bring about change.
His inner voice always urges him to do the right thing.
Many activists believe they are giving a voice to the voiceless.
The voice of reason helped restore calm after the argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: articulate - to express an idea or feeling fluently and coherently
  • synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings
  • antonym: silence - the state of not expressing or speaking
  • synonym: spokesperson - a person who speaks for another or for a group
