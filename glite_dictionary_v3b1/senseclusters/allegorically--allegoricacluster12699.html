allegorically

adverb
in an allegorical manner
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪˈɡɔrɪkli / (AmE), / ˌalɪˈɡɒrɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses symbols or stories to represent ideas or moral qualities; in an allegorical manner.

Examples

To speak allegorically
In many fables, moral lessons are presented allegorically rather than stated explicitly.
The artist chose to depict struggle allegorically through a stormy sea painting.
The poem explores political themes allegorically, using animals to represent real leaders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literally - in a literal or non-figurative manner
  • synonym: symbolically - in a symbolic rather than literal manner
