social movement

noun
organized group seeking social change
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a large, organized group of people who work together to achieve social or political change

Examples

A social movement group
A strong social movement can bring about significant reforms in society.
The civil rights social movement of the 1960s changed the course of American history.
The environmental social movement has gained momentum worldwide in recent years.
Environmentalists launched a social movement to address climate change and promote sustainability.
Many young people participate in social movements that advocate for equality and justice.
The civil rights social movement of the 1960s changed laws and attitudes across the United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: movement - a group of people working together to advance their shared political, social, or artistic ideas
  • synonym: protest movement - group organized to achieve political or social goals, often by protest
  • synonym: reform movement - organized effort aimed at reforming policies or institutions
