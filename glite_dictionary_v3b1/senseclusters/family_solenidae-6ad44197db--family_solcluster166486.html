family Solenidae

noun
a family of marine bivalve molluscs (razor clams)
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli soʊˈlɛnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli səʊˈlɛnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve molluscs known as razor clams, distinguished by their elongated, laterally compressed shells.

Examples

The family Solenidae
Marine biologists study the family Solenidae for its ecological role in coastal habitats.
Members of the family Solenidae are easily identified by their long, narrow shells.
The family Solenidae includes many species of razor clams found along sandy beaches.
