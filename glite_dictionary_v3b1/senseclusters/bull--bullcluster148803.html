bull

noun
optimistic investor expecting prices to rise
Pronunciation: / bʊl / (AmE), / bʊl / (BrE)
an investor who expects prices to rise and buys securities now with the intention of selling them later for a profit

Examples

Stock market bull
He made a fortune on Wall Street by acting as a bull during the boom years.
The bulls are confident the market will rebound soon.
When the market is bullish, a bull tends to buy aggressively in anticipation of rising prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bear - an investor who expects prices to fall
