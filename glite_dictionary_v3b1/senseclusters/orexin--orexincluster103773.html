orexin

noun
brain hormone
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈrɛksɪn / (AmE), / ɒˈrɛksɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced in the brain that regulates wakefulness, arousal, and appetite

Examples

Orexin hormone
Scientists discovered that orexin plays a key role in keeping people awake during the day.
A lack of orexin in the brain can lead to sleep disorders such as narcolepsy.
Researchers are developing drugs that target orexin to help treat insomnia.
