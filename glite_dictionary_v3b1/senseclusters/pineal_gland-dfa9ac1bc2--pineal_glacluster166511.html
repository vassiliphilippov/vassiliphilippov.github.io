pineal gland

noun
small brain gland producing melatonin
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪniəl ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈpɪniəl ˌɡlænd / (BrE)
a small, pea-shaped endocrine gland located in the brain that produces and regulates melatonin, a hormone affecting sleep patterns

Examples

A tumor of the pineal gland can disrupt melatonin production and cause sleep disorders.
Scientists are still studying the full range of the pineal gland's functions in humans.
The pineal gland plays a crucial role in regulating the body's sleep-wake cycle.
