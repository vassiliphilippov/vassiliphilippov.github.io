Ophelia

proper noun
Millais painting
8.9
Pronunciation: / oʊˈfiːliə / (AmE), / əʊˈfiːliə / (BrE)
a famous 1852 oil painting by John Everett Millais depicting the character Ophelia from Shakespeare's Hamlet

Examples

Millais's Ophelia painting
The museum's most popular exhibit was Millais's Ophelia, admired for its vivid colors and emotional depth.
Art students studied Ophelia to learn about the Pre-Raphaelite style and attention to natural detail.
Many visitors were moved by the tragic beauty of Ophelia when they saw it in person.
