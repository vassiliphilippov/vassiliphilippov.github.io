issue

verb
to produce/distribute an official document
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɪ.ʃuː/ /; / /ˈɪʃ.juː/ /; / /ˈɪʃ.jə/ (AmE), /ˈɪ.ʃuː/ /; / /ˈɪ.sjuː/ /; / /ˈɪʃ.juː/ /; / /ˈɪʃ.jə/ (BrE)
to officially give, supply, or produce something (such as documents, statements, or supplies)

Examples

To issue a statement
The bank issues statements to customers every month.
The government issued new guidelines on public health.
The police have issued a warrant for his arrest.
I need you to issue a ticket for the next available flight.
The bank will issue the cashier's check within two business days.
The office will issue travel documents to all participants immediately.
The organization will issue new identification cards to all employees next week.
The school issues books to students at the start of each term.
Warm clothes were issued to the volunteers before they went out in the cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - to make out or issue, especially referring to checks or tickets
  • synonym: distribute - to give out among a number of recipients
  • synonym: publish - to prepare and issue printed material for public sale or distribution
  • synonym: release - to make something available to the public
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove something officially from availability
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give or distribute something
  • synonym: write out - to fill in and complete a document, such as a check, in writing
