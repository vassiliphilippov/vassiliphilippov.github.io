parietal

noun
skull side bone
8.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈraɪətəl / (AmE), / pəˈraɪətəl / (BrE)
either of a pair of bones forming the sides and roof of the skull

Examples

Parietal bone fracture
The doctor explained that the parietal is important for protecting the brain.
In the X-ray, the fracture was clearly visible on the left parietal.
The anatomy student identified the parietal among the other skull bones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frontal - the bone forming the front part of the skull
  • synonym: temporal - a bone on the side of the skull near the ear
