clash

verb
be incompatible or in conflict
1.0
Pronunciation: / klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)
(of people, ideas, or things) to be incompatible or to come into conflict; to not match or agree.

Examples

Colors clash badly
Red and orange often clash when used together in design.
Sometimes our personalities simply clash, despite our best efforts.
The manager's approach tends to clash with the company culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to be in harmony or accordance
  • synonym: conflict - to be in opposition or disagreement
