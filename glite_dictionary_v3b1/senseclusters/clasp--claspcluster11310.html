clasp

verb
hold tightly with hands or arms
Pronunciation: / klæsp / (AmE), / klɑːsp / (BrE)
to hold someone or something tightly with your hands or arms

Examples

The old woman sat by the fire, clasping a faded photograph.
He clasped her hand tightly as they walked through the crowded market.
She clasped the letter to her chest, unwilling to let it go.
The child clasped the toy in his arms, refusing to share it.
