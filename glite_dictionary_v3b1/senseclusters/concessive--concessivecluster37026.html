concessive

adjective
relating to concession or yielding
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or expressing concession; indicating the yielding of a point or admission of an opposing argument, especially in grammar or discourse.

Examples

Concessive clause
Although her remarks were concessive, she still strongly defended her main points.
His tone grew more concessive as he realized he couldn't win the argument.
In grammar, a concessive clause shows contrast, such as 'Although it was raining, we went out.'

Related Terms

  • synonym: concession - the act of conceding or yielding
