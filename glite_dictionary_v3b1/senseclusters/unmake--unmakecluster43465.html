unmake

verb
deprive of certain characteristics
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmeɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmeɪk / (BrE)
to reverse, undo, or destroy something that was previously made or created

Examples

To unmake a plan
It is far easier to unmake a plan than to create one from scratch.
Recent revelations may unmake his reputation as an honest politician.
The crisis threatened to unmake the fragile peace that had been established.
The committee decided to unmake the previous policy after receiving new information.
She tried to unmake the arrangement, but it was already too late.
Engineers are unmaking the old bridge to make way for a new one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: create - to bring something into existence
  • synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • synonym: undo - to reverse or annul an action or effect
