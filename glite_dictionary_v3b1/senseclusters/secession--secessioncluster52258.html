secession

noun
formal withdrawal from a group or union
6.3
Pronunciation: / səˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally withdrawing from an alliance, federation, or political union

Examples

The secession vote
After months of protest, the community voted in favor of secession from the federation.
Lincoln opposed the secession of Southern states from the Union.
The region's declaration of secession sparked a lengthy political crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart
  • antonym: union - the action or fact of joining together; the state of being united
