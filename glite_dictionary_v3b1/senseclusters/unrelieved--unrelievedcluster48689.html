unrelieved

adjective
not lessened or diminished; complete
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈlivd / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈliːvd / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant or undesirable) not lessened, softened, or diminished; complete or continuing without relief.

Examples

An unrelieved feeling
Her unrelieved anxiety made it difficult to enjoy the holiday.
The landscape was gray and unrelieved by any sign of life.
The war left many families in unrelieved poverty for years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately without change
  • antonym: relieved - feeling a reduction of pain or distress; lessened in intensity
