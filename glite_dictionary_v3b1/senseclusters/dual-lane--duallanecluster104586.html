dual-lane

adjective
road with separated opposite-direction lanes
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈduːəl leɪn / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəl leɪn / (BrE)
(of a road or highway) having a median strip or island separating lanes of traffic moving in opposite directions.

Examples

A dual-lane road
Accidents are less common on dual-lane roads due to the central median.
They upgraded the old road to a dual-lane highway for improved safety.
Traffic moved smoothly along the newly constructed dual-lane bypass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divided highway - a highway with lanes in each direction separated by a median or barrier
