electrodialysis

noun
ion separation process
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trə.daɪ.ˈæl.ɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ə.lek.trə.daɪ.ˈæl.ɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trə.daɪ.ˈæl.ɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ə.lek.trə.daɪ.ˈæl.ɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a process that uses electric current and special membranes to move ions and separate or purify substances, especially water

Examples

Electrodialysis water purification
The laboratory used electrodialysis to remove unwanted salts from the water sample.
Modern water treatment plants often rely on electrodialysis for efficient desalination.
By applying electrodialysis, the company reduced the mineral content in its industrial wastewater.
Electrodialysis is often used to desalinate brackish water in areas with limited freshwater resources.
The laboratory installed a new electrodialysis unit to purify chemical solutions more efficiently.
Advances in electrodialysis technology have made water treatment more cost-effective for many industries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialysis - a process for separating dissolved substances from a liquid
  • synonym: filtration - the process of removing particles from a liquid or gas by passing it through a filter
