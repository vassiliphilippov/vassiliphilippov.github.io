resegregation

noun
separating again
8.9
Pronunciation: /riː.seg.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.seg.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.seg.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.seg.rə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of separating things that were previously combined back into distinct categories or groups again, especially in technical or industrial contexts

Examples

Sample resegregation
After the samples were mixed by mistake, a careful resegregation was necessary to restore the original categories.
The laboratory's protocol required resegregation of materials if any contamination was detected.
Due to a sorting error, the recycling center had to perform resegregation of the collected plastics.
