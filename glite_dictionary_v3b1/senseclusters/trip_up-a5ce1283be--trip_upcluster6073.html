trip up

verb
make an error
6.1 4.0
Pronunciation: / trɪp ʌp / (AmE), / trɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mistake or cause someone to make a mistake, often unintentionally or through carelessness

Examples

To trip up a speaker
He tends to trip up when speaking in public because of his nerves.
She tripped up during the interview and answered one question incorrectly.
The complicated instructions might trip up even experienced users.
It's easy to trip up during a complex presentation if you're not well-prepared.
She almost tripped up her opponent by pointing out a contradiction in his statement.
The interviewer tried to trip up the politician with a tricky question.
It's easy to trip up on these complex calculations if you're not careful.
She tried to trip up her opponent with a tricky question during the debate.
The uneven carpet could trip up anyone not watching their step.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunder - to make a serious or careless mistake
  • synonym: catch out - to detect someone in a mistake, especially by surprise
  • antonym: get right - to perform something correctly
  • degree higher: mislead - to guide someone into error; to cause to make a mistake
  • synonym: slip up - to make a mistake, often through carelessness
  • synonym: stumble - to trip and almost fall
