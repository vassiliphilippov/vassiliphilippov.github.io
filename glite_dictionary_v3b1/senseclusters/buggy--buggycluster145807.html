buggy

adjective
full of errors
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡi / (BrE)
containing many errors or problems, especially in software or machinery

Examples

Buggy software
The new video game was so buggy that players complained about crashes and glitches.
After the update, the software became buggy and difficult to use for most users.
He warned me that the beta version is still quite buggy and may not work properly.
The first version of the app was extremely buggy and crashed often.
After the update, users complained that the software became even buggier than before.
We had to delay the launch because the system was still too buggy to use safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faulty - having defects or not working correctly
  • synonym: flawed - having mistakes or weaknesses
  • antonym: reliable - working well and without problems
  • synonym: unstable - likely to fail or crash; not reliable
