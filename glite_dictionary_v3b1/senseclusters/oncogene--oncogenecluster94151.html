oncogene

noun
gene that can cause a cell to become cancerous
Pronunciation: / ˈɑŋkəˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈɒŋkəˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that has the potential to cause a normal cell to become cancerous, especially by triggering uncontrolled growth

Examples

A oncogene mutation
Mutation in a single oncogene can sometimes trigger uncontrolled cell growth.
Scientists have identified a new oncogene that may play a role in breast cancer.
Understanding how an oncogene functions is vital for developing targeted cancer therapies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tumor suppressor gene - a gene that protects a cell from becoming cancerous
