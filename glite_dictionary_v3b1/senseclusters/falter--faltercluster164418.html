falter

verb
to walk unsteadily; trip repeatedly
6.1 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëlt…ôr / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêlt…ô / (BrE)
to walk unsteadily or with repeated tripping, often due to weakness or uncertainty

Examples

To falter on steps
Exhausted after the long hike, she began to falter along the rocky path.
He faltered as he tried to make his way through the darkness, nearly losing his balance.
The injured athlete faltered down the corridor, barely able to stay upright.

Synonyms

  • stumble
    1.6 12/m
    stumble is a quick trip that makes you lose balance, while falter is repeated unsteady walking from weakness or doubt.
  • stagger
    4.0 6.84/m
    stagger is a heavy, swaying walk as if about to fall from tiredness or drink, while falter is shaky, hesitant walking from weakness or doubt.
  • totter
    4.8 1.35/m
    totter is to move in small, unsteady steps as if barely keeping balance, while falter is repeated tripping or weak, unsure walking.
  • reel
    4.6 1.67/m
    reel is to move in a wild, uncontrolled way from dizziness or shock, while falter is hesitant, unsteady walking from weakness or doubt.
