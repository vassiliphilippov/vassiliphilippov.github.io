ragsorter's disease

noun
inhalational anthrax historically affecting rag sorters
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈræɡˌsɔrtərz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌsɔːtəz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a form of anthrax infection contracted by inhaling dust containing Bacillus anthracis, historically affecting people working with contaminated rags; initial symptoms include chills, cough, and shortness of breath, followed by severe cardiovascular collapse

Examples

Ragsorter's disease infection
Doctors quickly recognized the symptoms of ragsorter's disease after the exposed worker began coughing severely.
In the early 1900s, many factory workers developed ragsorter's disease from handling contaminated wool and rags.
The decline of the textile industry led to a significant reduction in cases of ragsorter's disease.
