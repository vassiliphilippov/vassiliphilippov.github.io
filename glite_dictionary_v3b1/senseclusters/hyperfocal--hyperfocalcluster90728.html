hyperfocal

adjective
lens focus distance
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈfoʊkəl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈfəʊkəl / (BrE)
relating to the distance at which a camera lens is focused so that everything from half that distance to infinity appears sharp

Examples

Hyperfocal setting
To maximize sharpness in his landscape photo, he set the lens to the hyperfocal distance.
Understanding hyperfocal settings helps photographers keep both foreground and background in focus.
She explained how the hyperfocal point affects the depth of field in wide-angle shots.
