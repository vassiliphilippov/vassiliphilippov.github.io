(of a statement or publication) containing or constituting a libel; harmful and often untrue, tending to discredit or malign someone.

Examples

Libelous statement
He denied all allegations, claiming the accusations were completely libelous.
Making libelous remarks can result in serious legal consequences.
The newspaper was sued for publishing a libelous article about the celebrity.
She denied making any libellous statements about her competitor.
The article was so libellous that the newspaper was sued for defamation.
To publish libellous material can have serious legal consequences.

Synonyms

  • defamatory
    7.9 0.76/m
    defamatory describes words that harm someone's reputation in any form, while libelous usually means written or published false statements.
  • calumnious
    9.9 0.02/m
    calumnious labels false accusations meant to damage someone's reputation in a strong, often literary way, while libelous refers to published defamation.
  • calumniatory
    9.9 0.00/m
    calumniatory describes statements made to harm someone's reputation in a formal or old-fashioned tone, while libelous refers to published defamatory material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defamatory - damaging the good reputation of someone; slanderous or libelous
  • antonym: praiseworthy - deserving approval or admiration
  • synonym: slanderous - containing or constituting slander; defamatory in spoken form
