Chickasaw

proper noun
Native American people
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪkəsɔː / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəsɔː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the southeastern United States, especially Mississippi and Oklahoma

Examples

The Chickasaw nation
The Chickasaw were known for their skilled warriors and strong communities.
Many descendants of the Chickasaw now live in Oklahoma.
The history of the Chickasaw is taught in local schools in Mississippi.
