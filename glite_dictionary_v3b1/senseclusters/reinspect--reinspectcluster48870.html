reinspect

verb
to examine again
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to examine something again, especially to check for problems, changes, or if it now meets standards

Examples

To reinspect the building
The safety officer will reinspect the building after the fire alarm system is fixed.
After making the necessary corrections, the teacher decided to reinspect the students' assignments.
It is important to reinspect your work before submitting it to avoid mistakes.
After the repairs were completed, the engineer had to reinspect the bridge for safety.
The health department will reinspect the restaurant next week to ensure all violations have been corrected.
If any issues are found, the team will reinspect the equipment before approving its use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully to check its condition
  • antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or check something
