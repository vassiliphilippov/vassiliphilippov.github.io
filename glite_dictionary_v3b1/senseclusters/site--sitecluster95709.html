site

verb
assign a location to
1.4
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
to place or build something in a particular location, especially for construction or installation

Examples

To site a school
Environmental concerns affect where developers site housing projects.
Planners are considering several locations to site the new school.
The new factory was carefully sited near sources of raw materials.
The city is siting a new library in the center of the neighborhood.
Engineers spent weeks siting the wind turbines to maximize efficiency.
Careful planning is required when siting a hospital to ensure accessibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: install - to put something in position and make it ready for use
  • synonym: locate - to establish in a particular place
  • antonym: remove - to take something away from a place
