esophageal reflux

noun
backflow of stomach contents into the esophagus
3.1 3.6
Pronunciation: /ɪs.ə.ˈfɑː.dʒiː.əl ˈriː.flʌks/ (AmE), /ɪs.ɒf.ə.ˈdʒiː.əl ˈriː.flʌks/ (BrE)
The backward flow of stomach contents into the esophagus, often causing heartburn or discomfort.

Examples

Esophageal reflux symptoms
After eating a large meal, he often experiences esophageal reflux accompanied by a burning sensation in his chest.
Chronic esophageal reflux can lead to inflammation and damage to the esophageal lining.
Medications are available to help control the symptoms of esophageal reflux.
Eating late at night can sometimes trigger oesophageal reflux in susceptible individuals.
He takes medication every day to control his oesophageal reflux symptoms.
The doctor diagnosed her with oesophageal reflux after she complained of frequent heartburn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acid reflux - backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus
  • synonym: gastroesophageal reflux - reflux of stomach contents into the esophagus (medical term often used interchangeably)
  • degree higher: gastroesophageal reflux disease - chronic condition involving regular esophageal reflux
