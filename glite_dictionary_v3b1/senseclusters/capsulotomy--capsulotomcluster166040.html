capsulotomy

noun
surgical capsule incision
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpsjəˈlɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌkæpsjʊˈlɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a capsule, especially in the eye, is cut open

Examples

Eye capsulotomy surgery
A capsulotomy is often performed to restore vision after cataract surgery.
The doctor explained that a laser capsulotomy would be a quick outpatient procedure.
After the capsulotomy, the patient's eyesight improved significantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capsulectomy - surgical removal of a capsule
