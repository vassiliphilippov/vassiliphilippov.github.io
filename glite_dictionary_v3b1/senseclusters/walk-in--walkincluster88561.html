walk-in

noun
small room large enough to enter
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑk.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk.ɪn / (BrE)
a small room or enclosed area that is large enough for a person to enter, especially for storage or changing purposes

Examples

A walk-in closet
Our new house has a walk-in that serves as both a closet and a dressing room.
She hung her coats in the walk-in near the entryway.
The apartment features a spacious walk-in for extra storage.
