backfire

noun
an action with an unintended negative effect

an action or plan that produces an unintended, harmful, or opposite effect to what was expected; a miscalculation that rebounds on its originator

Examples

A plan backfire
His attempt to discredit his opponent turned out to be a backfire that damaged his own reputation.
The new policy was a backfire, making the situation worse instead of better.
Their marketing campaign was a huge backfire and lost them many customers.

Synonyms

  • boomerang
    6.5 0.13/m
    boomerang is an action that returns to hurt the person who started it, while backfire is an unintended harmful result.
  • blowback
    6.8 0.81/m
    blowback is an unexpected harmful reaction, often political or public, while backfire is a general unintended harm from a plan.
  • backlash
    6.8 0.01/m
    backlash is a strong public or group reaction against something, whereas backfire is an unintended negative outcome for the originator.
