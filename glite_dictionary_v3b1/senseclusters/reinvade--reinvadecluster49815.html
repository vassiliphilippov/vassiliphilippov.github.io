reinvade

verb
to return to mind
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈveɪd / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈveɪd / (BrE)
to enter or affect someone's mind or emotions again, especially after a period of absence.

Examples

Reinvade his mind
Old memories reinvaded him as he walked through his childhood home.
Doubt can reinvade your thoughts even after you have made a decision.
As the anniversary approached, sadness reinvaded her mind late that night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to stop thinking about something
  • synonym: haunt - to return repeatedly to someone's mind
  • synonym: reoccur - to happen or appear again
