kefir

noun
fermented milk drink
Pronunciation: / kəˈfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkiːfɪə / (BrE)
a fermented drink made from milk or water using kefir grains, which contain live bacteria and yeast

Examples

A glass of kefir
Every morning, she drinks a glass of homemade kefir for its probiotic benefits.
The recipe calls for fresh kefir to give the bread a unique flavor and texture.
You can make kefir at home by fermenting milk with special grains.
Many people drink kefir for its probiotic benefits and refreshing taste.
You can make homemade kefir by adding grains to milk and letting it ferment overnight.
The store offers both dairy and water-based kefir for customers with different preferences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buttermilk - a tangy liquid left after churning butter or made by fermenting milk
  • synonym: yogurt - a thick, tangy dairy product made by fermenting milk with bacteria
