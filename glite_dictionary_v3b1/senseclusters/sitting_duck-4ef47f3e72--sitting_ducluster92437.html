sitting duck

noun
defenseless or vulnerable victim
7.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪ.tɪŋ dʌk/ (AmE), /ˈsɪ.tɪŋ dʌk/ (BrE)
a person or thing that is easy to attack, harm, or take advantage of because they are vulnerable or unprotected

Examples

A sitting duck target
After the storm knocked out the power, our house was a sitting duck for thieves.
New businesses without proper insurance can be sitting ducks for lawsuits.
With no security cameras in the shop, it was a sitting duck for burglars.
During the financial crisis, many small investors were sitting ducks for fraudsters.
Walking in the open field made the soldiers sitting ducks for enemy snipers.
Without proper security, the computer system is a sitting duck for hackers.
