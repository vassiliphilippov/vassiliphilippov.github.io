Aswan Dam

proper noun
major dam on the Nile River in Egypt
Pronunciation: / æzˈwɑn dæm / (AmE), / azˈwɑːn dam / (BrE)
a massive dam across the Nile River in southern Egypt, constructed to control flooding, provide water for irrigation, and generate hydroelectric power

Examples

The Aswan Dam project
Thanks to the Aswan Dam, Egypt has improved control over Nile River flooding.
The construction of the Aswan Dam dramatically changed the agriculture of Egypt.
Thousands of people visited the Aswan Dam during its opening ceremony in the 1970s.
