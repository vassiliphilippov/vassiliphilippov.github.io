electrostatic charge

noun
electric charge at rest on a surface
6.9 5.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈstæt.ɪk tʃɑːrdʒ/ /; / /ɪ.lek.trəʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk tʃɑːrdʒ/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈstæt.ɪk tʃɑːdʒ/ /; / /ɪ.lek.trəʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk tʃɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
the electric charge that remains stationary on the surface of an insulated object, causing an electrostatic field in the surrounding area

Examples

Electrostatic charge on surface
Electronic components can be damaged by even a tiny electrostatic charge during handling.
If you rub a balloon on your hair, it will often develop an electrostatic charge and stick to the wall.
The technicians wore special wrist straps to prevent the buildup of electrostatic charge while assembling the computer parts.

  • synonym: static electricity - an imbalance of electric charges within or on the surface of a material
