behoove

verb
to be appropriate or necessary
Pronunciation: / bɪˈhuv / (AmE), / bɪˈhuːv /; / bɪˈhəʊv / (BrE)
(formal) To be appropriate or necessary for someone; to be a duty or responsibility.

Examples

It behooves you
As a leader, it behooves you to set a good example for your team.
It behooved her to apologize after making such a serious mistake.
It behooves us to consider the consequences carefully before making a decision.
If you want to succeed, it behoves you to work hard and stay focused.
It behoves all citizens to act with integrity and honesty.
It behoves the committee to consider all possible outcomes before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: behoove - to be appropriate or necessary for someone; American spelling and usage
  • antonym: improper - not appropriate or suitable
  • synonym: incumbent - necessary as a duty or responsibility
