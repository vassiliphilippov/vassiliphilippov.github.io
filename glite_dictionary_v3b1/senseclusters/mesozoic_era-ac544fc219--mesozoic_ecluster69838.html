Mesozoic era

noun
geological era of dinosaurs
7.2
Pronunciation: /mes.ə.ˈzoʊ.ɪk ˈer.ə/ /; / /mes.ə.ˈzoʊ.ɪk ˈɪr.ə/ (AmE), /mes.ə.ˈzəʊ.ɪk ˈɪə.rə/ /; / /mes.ə.ˈzəʊ.ɪk ˈeə.rə/ (BrE)
The geological era from about 230 million to 63 million years ago, characterized by the rise and fall of dinosaurs and the development of early mammals and birds.

Examples

The Mesozoic era fossils
Many famous fossils have been discovered from rocks dating to the Mesozoic era.
The Mesozoic era included the Triassic, Jurassic, and Cretaceous periods.
The extinction of the dinosaurs marked the end of the Mesozoic era.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Cenozoic era - The geological era following the Mesozoic era, known as the 'Age of Mammals.'
  • degree lower: Paleozoic era - The geological era preceding the Mesozoic era, marked by early life forms.
