lurch

noun
sudden uncontrolled movement
9.7 3.8
Pronunciation: /lɜːrtʃ/ (AmE), /lɜːtʃ/ (BrE)
a sudden, uncontrolled, or unsteady movement, especially forward or sideways

Examples

A sudden lurch
She felt a lurch in her stomach when the roller coaster dropped.
The train came to a sudden lurch as it halted at the station.
With a lurch, the boat tipped to one side in the rough sea.
After spinning too quickly, he walked with a noticeable lurch toward the door.
At the sight of the cat, the old dog began a slow, uneven lurch across the yard.
The drunkard's lurch down the street drew curious glances from passersby.
The roller coaster started with a dramatic lurch that made everyone scream.
The ship gave a sudden lurch as it hit the rough waters.
With every lurch of the bus, the passengers grabbed the nearest support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jerk - a quick, sudden movement
  • synonym: pitch - a rise and fall in movement, especially of a ship
  • synonym: roll - side-to-side motion, especially of a ship or aircraft
  • synonym: stagger - an unsteady, swaying movement
