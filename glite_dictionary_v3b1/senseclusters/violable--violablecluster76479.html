violable

adjective
able to be violated or broken
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪ.ə.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ələ.b(ə)l / (BrE)
Able to be violated or broken; not inviolable.

Examples

Violable rules
Some ancient traditions are seen as sacred and not violable by anyone.
The board realized that the confidentiality agreement was violable without stronger enforcement mechanisms.
The contract was considered violable under certain circumstances by both parties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inviolable - not able to be violated or broken
