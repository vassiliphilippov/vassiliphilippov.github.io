fade out

verb
gradually disappear or become less distinct
3.9 0.99/m
Pronunciation: / feÉªd aÊŠt / (AmE), / feÉªd aÊŠt / (BrE)
to gradually become less visible, audible, or noticeable until disappearing

Examples

To fade out slowly
As the song ended, the music faded out until there was only silence.
His voice faded out as he walked away from the microphone.
The image on the screen slowly faded out and was replaced by a new scene.
As the credits rolled, the movie soundtrack began to fade out gently.
Over time, the paint on the old sign started to fade out until it was barely readable.
The announcer's voice will fade out as the commercial begins.

Synonyms

  • fade away
    3.0 4.02/m
    fade away means slowly disappear completely, while fade out describes becoming less visible, audible, or noticeable until gone.
  • fade
    1.2 40/m
    fade is the simple verb for slowly losing color, sound, or strength, while fade out stresses ending by becoming less noticeable.
  • die away
    4.2 0.88/m
    die away is used for sounds or feelings that slowly weaken, while fade out covers things becoming less visible or audible.
  • taper off
    4.5 0.54/m
    taper off usually describes amounts or actions that gradually reduce, while fade out focuses on becoming less visible or audible.
  • dissolve
    3.9 4.68/m
    dissolve means slowly disappear or break apart into no clear shape, while fade out means lose visibility, sound, or notice slowly.
  • wear off
    4.0 2.91/m
    wear off refers to an effect or feeling that slowly loses power, while fade out is about things becoming less visible or audible.
  • go away
    1.0 18/m
    go away means simply disappear or stop being present, while fade out implies a gradual loss of visibility, sound, or being noticed.
