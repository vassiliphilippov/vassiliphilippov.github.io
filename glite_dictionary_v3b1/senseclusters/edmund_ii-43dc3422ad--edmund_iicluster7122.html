Edmund II

proper noun
English king who resisted Canute
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdmənd ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈɛdmənd ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(c. 993–1016) King of England who resisted the Danish invasion led by Canute and briefly ruled before the kingdom was divided.

Examples

Edmund II king England
Edmund II became king in 1016 during a period of great turmoil in England.
After fierce battles with Canute, Edmund II agreed to divide the kingdom.
Historians often refer to Edmund II by his nickname, Edmund Ironside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Canute - Danish king who conquered England
