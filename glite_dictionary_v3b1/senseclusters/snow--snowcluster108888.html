snow

verb
to deceive or mislead, especially by persuasion
Pronunciation: / snoʊ / (AmE), / snəʊ / (BrE)
to mislead or deceive someone, especially by pretending good intentions or by being persuasive

Examples

To snow a customer
Don't let the salesman snow you into buying something you don't need.
He tried to snow his boss with flattery and charming stories.
She snowed her way into getting extra time on the assignment by pretending to be sick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bamboozle - to fool or deceive someone
  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions
