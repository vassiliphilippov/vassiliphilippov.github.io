Pronunciation: / ɑl hæt ənd noʊ ˈkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɔːl hæt ənd nəʊ ˈkætl̩ / (BrE)
Pretending to be important or capable while lacking real ability, achievement, or substance.

Examples

All hat and no cattle bragging
He's always bragging about his business, but he's all hat and no cattle.
Many politicians seem all hat and no cattle when it comes to delivering on promises.
She sounds impressive, but when it comes time to act, she's all hat and no cattle.
