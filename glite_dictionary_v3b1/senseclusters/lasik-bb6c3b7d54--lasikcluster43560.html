LASIK

noun
laser eye surgery for vision correction
7.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.sɪk/ /; / /ˈleɪ.zɪk/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.sɪk/ /; / /ˈleɪ.zɪk/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure for correcting vision by reshaping the cornea using a laser, typically to treat nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism.

Examples

LASIK eye surgery
Before deciding on LASIK, consult an ophthalmologist to discuss potential risks and benefits.
Many people choose LASIK for its quick recovery time compared to other eye surgeries.
She underwent LASIK to improve her vision and no longer needs glasses.
