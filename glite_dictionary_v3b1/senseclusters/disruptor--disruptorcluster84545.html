disruptor

noun
one who causes disruption
6.5
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌptər / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a person or thing that interrupts or causes disorder in an event, meeting, or process

Examples

Meeting disruptor
The protest was delayed when a disruptor began shouting from the audience.
Every classroom has at least one disruptor who makes it hard for others to concentrate.
Security quickly removed the disruptor to allow the meeting to continue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturber - a person who causes trouble or interrupts
  • antonym: peacemaker - a person who helps to prevent or stop conflict
  • synonym: troublemaker - a person who deliberately causes problems
