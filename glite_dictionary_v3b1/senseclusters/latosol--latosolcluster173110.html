latosol

noun
tropical red soil
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlætəˌsɑl / (AmE), / ˈlætəsɒl / (BrE)
a type of reddish, iron- and aluminum-rich soil found in tropical regions, often low in nutrients

Examples

Reddish latosol soil
The Amazon rainforest is supported by vast areas of latosol that are surprisingly poor in nutrients.
Farmers often struggle to grow crops on latosol without adding fertilizers.
Scientists study latosol to understand how tropical soils affect plant growth.
