fid

noun
rope splicing tool
9.9
Pronunciation: /fɪd/ (AmE), /fɪd/ (BrE)
a small tapered tool, usually made of wood or metal, used to separate or manipulate rope strands when splicing or repairing them

Examples

Rope fid
The sailor used a fid to carefully separate the rope strands before splicing.
A wooden fid is often preferred for working with natural fiber ropes.
Before repairing the damaged line, she reached for her fid from the toolbox.
