dissemblance

noun
concealment of feelings
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛmbləns / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmbləns / (BrE)
the act of hiding one's true feelings, intentions, or thoughts; pretense or concealment

Examples

Constant dissemblance
Her constant dissemblance made it difficult for her friends to trust her.
The politician's dissemblance was eventually exposed by investigative journalists.
He used dissemblance to hide his true intentions during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and open
