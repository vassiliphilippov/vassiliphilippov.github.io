a person who supervises and manages the work of others, especially in an organization or on a work site

Examples

The overseer manages workers
A new overseer was appointed to manage daily operations at the factory.
As overseer of the department, she was responsible for training new employees.
The overseer ensured that all the workers followed safety procedures on the construction site.

Synonyms

  • supervisor
    2.8 23/m
    a supervisor checks daily tasks, guides workers directly, and enforces standards, while an overseer manages broader site operations and staff.
  • foreman
    3.1 6.76/m
    a foreman leads workers on a building or factory site and gives hands-on orders, while an overseer manages overall work and site responsibilities.
  • gaffer
    8.1 0.04/m
    a gaffer acts as an informal work-site leader who gives practical orders, while an overseer sounds more formal and manages staff.
  • manager
    1.6 108/m
    a manager controls resources, finances, and operations across a company, while an overseer focuses more on supervising workers and site tasks.
  • superintendent
    6.0 9.90/m
    a superintendent runs a department or institution and often holds formal authority, while an overseer usually supervises workers directly on a site.
  • administrator
    3.7 44/m
    an administrator handles office systems, policy, and paperwork across an organization, while an overseer deals mainly with on-site supervision of workers.
