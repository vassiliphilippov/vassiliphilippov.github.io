straight

adverb
directly, without deviation
Pronunciation: / streɪt / (AmE), / streɪt / (BrE)
in a direct line or manner; without turning or deviation

Examples

Go straight ahead
Go straight down this road and you'll see the sign.
She walked straight home without stopping.
The arrow flew straight to its target.
Go straight ahead at the intersection and you'll see the museum on your left.
She walked straight across the field to reach the gate.
The highway runs straight through the desert, with no turns for miles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: directly - without deviation; in a straight line or manner
  • antonym: indirectly - not in a straight path or way; with deviation
