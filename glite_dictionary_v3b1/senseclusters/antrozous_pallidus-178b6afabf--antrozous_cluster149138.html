Antrozous pallidus

noun
a species of yellowish big-eared bat
Pronunciation: / ænˈtroʊzoʊs pəˈlɪdəs / (AmE), / ænˈtrəʊzəs pəˈlɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of drab yellowish big-eared bat found in caves, commonly known as the pallid bat.

Examples

The Antrozous pallidus bat
Researchers observed the Antrozous pallidus hunting insects in the desert at dusk.
The Antrozous pallidus prefers roosting in caves and abandoned mines.
Unlike most bats, the Antrozous pallidus is known to crawl along the ground in search of prey.
