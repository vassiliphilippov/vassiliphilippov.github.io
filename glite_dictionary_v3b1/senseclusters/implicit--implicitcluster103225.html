implicit

adjective
not directly stated, implied
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪsɪt / (BrE)
suggested or understood without being directly stated; existing as an inherent part of something.

Examples

Implicit meaning
An implicit threat could be felt in his words, though he spoke calmly.
Her trust in him was implicit, even though he had never promised anything.
The contract contained implicit terms that both parties understood without discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion
  • synonym: implied - suggested but not directly stated
