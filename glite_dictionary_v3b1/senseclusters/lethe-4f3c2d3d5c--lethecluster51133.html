Lethe

proper noun
mythical river of forgetfulness
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈliːθi / (AmE), / ˈliːθi / (BrE)
in Greek mythology, a river in the underworld whose water caused souls to forget their past lives

Examples

Lethe river
According to legend, souls drank from the Lethe before entering a new life.
The poet described memory loss as if he had crossed the Lethe.
In many stories, the waters of the Lethe symbolize forgetting pain and sorrow.
