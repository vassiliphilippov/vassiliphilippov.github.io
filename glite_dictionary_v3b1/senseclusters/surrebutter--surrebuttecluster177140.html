surrebutter

noun
legal reply to a rebutter
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜːrɪˈbʌtər / (AmE), / ˌsʌrɪˈbʌtə / (BrE)
(law) A plaintiff's pleading in response to the defendant's rebutter.

Examples

The surrebutter pleading
Legal scholars often reference the surrebutter when outlining the sequence of pleadings in historical cases.
The court accepted the surrebutter as a valid response under procedural law.
The plaintiff filed a surrebutter to address new issues raised in the defendant's rebutter.
