outwash

noun
glacial sand and gravel
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
sand and gravel carried by meltwater from a glacier and deposited beyond the glacier's edge

Examples

Outwash plain
The geologist studied the outwash plain to learn about the glacier's movement.
Large deposits of outwash can be found where ancient glaciers once melted.
Farmers sometimes use land formed by outwash because of its well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - soil or sediments deposited by flowing water
  • antonym: till - unsorted glacial sediment deposited directly by a glacier
