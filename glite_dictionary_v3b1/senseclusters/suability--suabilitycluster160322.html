suability

noun
capacity to be sued (legal term)
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsjuːəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
(Law) the quality or condition of being liable to be sued; the capacity to be the subject of a legal action.

Examples

The suability of defendant
His lawyer challenged the suability of the defendant on jurisdictional grounds.
Questions of suability often arise when dealing with government agencies.
The court ruled on the suability of the corporation under federal law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immunity - protection or exemption from being sued
