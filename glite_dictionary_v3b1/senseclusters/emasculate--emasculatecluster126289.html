emasculate

verb
make weaker or less effective
7.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmæskjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmæskjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to make something weaker or less effective; to deprive of strength, power, or effectiveness

Examples

To emasculate a company
Heavy regulation can sometimes emasculate innovation in a company.
Many critics say the decision will only emasculate the power of the organization.
The amendments served to emasculate the environmental protection bill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger
  • synonym: weaken - to make or become weaker
