Lupinus albus

A white-flowered Eurasian species of lupin widely grown for animal fodder and erosion control.

Examples

Lupinus albus plant
Lupinus albus is often grown as a green manure crop to improve soil fertility.
Because of its deep roots, Lupinus albus is effective in preventing soil erosion on slopes.
Farmers value Lupinus albus for its high protein seeds used in animal feed.
