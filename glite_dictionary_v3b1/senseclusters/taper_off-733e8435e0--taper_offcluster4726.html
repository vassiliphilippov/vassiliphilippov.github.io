taper off

verb
gradually decrease or diminish
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪpər ɔf /; / ˈteɪpɚ ɑf / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə ɒf / (BrE)
to gradually become smaller, weaker, or less until stopping or fading away

Examples

To taper off slowly
After the holidays, demand for the product began to taper off significantly.
Attendance at the evening lectures tends to taper off toward the end of the semester.
The doctor advised her to taper off the medication slowly rather than stopping it abruptly.
Her interest in the project seemed to taper off over time.
Sales usually taper off after the holiday season.
The applause began to taper off as the speaker left the stage.
Attendance at the weekly meetings began to taper off as the year progressed.
If you suddenly stop exercising, it's better to taper off rather than quit abruptly.
The doctor's instructions said to taper off the medication over two weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diminish - to become or make something smaller or less
  • antonym: increase - to become greater in size, amount, or degree
  • synonym: peter out - to gradually diminish and come to an end
  • synonym: wane - to decrease in size, extent, or degree
