sequestrum

noun
seized property
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkwɛstrəm / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstrəm / (BrE)
property, goods, or items that have been seized or placed in custody by a legal order, especially in archaic or legal contexts

Examples

Court sequestrum
The court ordered the sequestrum of the disputed land until the case was resolved.
All goods listed as sequestrum must remain in official custody until further notice.
The lawyer explained that the sequestrum would be returned once ownership was determined.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confiscation - the act of taking property by authority
  • synonym: sequestration - the act of legally seizing property
