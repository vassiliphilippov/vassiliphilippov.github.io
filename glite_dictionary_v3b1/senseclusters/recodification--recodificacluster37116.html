recodification

noun
changing codes
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkoʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkəʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of changing or standardizing identification or classification codes in records, databases, or inventories

Examples

Recodification of records
The company began a recodification of its inventory system to improve accuracy.
After the merger, a complete recodification of employee records was necessary.
Government agencies often require recodification of data to comply with new regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reclassification - the act of assigning to a different class or category
  • synonym: standardization - the process of making things conform to a standard
