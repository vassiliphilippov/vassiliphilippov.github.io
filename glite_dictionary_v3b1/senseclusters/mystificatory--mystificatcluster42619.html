mystificatory

adjective
intended to confuse
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪstɪˈfɪkətɔːri / (AmE), / ˌmɪstɪˈfɪkətəri / (BrE)
intended to obscure, confuse, or make something difficult to understand

Examples

A mystificatory explanation
The professor's mystificatory explanations left many students feeling lost.
Some politicians use mystificatory language to avoid giving direct answers.
The film's plot was so mystificatory that few viewers understood its meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarifying - making something easier to understand
  • synonym: obscure - not clear or difficult to understand
