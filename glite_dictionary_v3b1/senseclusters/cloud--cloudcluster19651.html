cloud

noun
visible mass of water or ice in the sky
Pronunciation: / klaʊd / (AmE), / klaʊd / (BrE)
a visible mass of tiny water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the atmosphere, typically at some height above the Earth's surface

Examples

A white cloud
Looking up, she saw a cloud shaped like a dragon.
The sun disappeared behind a dark cloud, making the afternoon suddenly cooler.
White clouds drifted lazily across the summer sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fog - thick cloud of water droplets at ground level
