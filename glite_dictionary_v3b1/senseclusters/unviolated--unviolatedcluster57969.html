unviolated

adjective
not broken or infringed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈvaɪəleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈvaɪəleɪtɪd / (BrE)
not infringed, broken, or disturbed, especially referring to laws, rights, or agreements remaining intact.

Examples

Unviolated agreement
The treaty remained unviolated throughout the entire period of peace.
They were proud that their privacy had been unviolated despite the investigation.
The sacred site was left unviolated by visitors, preserving its original state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: violated - broken or infringed, especially of laws or rights
