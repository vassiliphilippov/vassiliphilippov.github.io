loginess

noun
dull or listless state from weariness
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑɡ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡ.i.nəs / (BrE)
a state of feeling dull, heavy, and listless, especially due to tiredness or sluggishness

Examples

Feeling loginess after lunch
After overeating at lunch, a sense of loginess made it hard for him to concentrate at work.
She couldn't shake off her morning loginess, even after a strong cup of coffee.
The humid weather increased their loginess and slowed down the team's performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life and energy
  • synonym: sluggishness - the quality of being slow-moving or inactive
