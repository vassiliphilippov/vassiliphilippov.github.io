overexpress

verb
to produce excessively
8.5
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈspres/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.rɪk.ˈspres/ (BrE)
to cause a gene or protein to be produced in greater than normal amounts, especially in a scientific or laboratory context

Examples

Researchers can overexpress a specific gene in mice to observe its role in development.
The scientists overexpressed the protein to increase its concentration in the cell culture.
By using genetic engineering, they managed to overexpress the enzyme in bacteria for industrial use.

The cancer cells overexpress this gene, leading to rapid growth.
