raddled

adjective
worn out in appearance
Pronunciation: / ˈræd.əld / (AmE), / ˈræd.əld / (BrE)
Looking worn out or disheveled as a result of fatigue, hardship, or suffering.

Examples

Raddled face
His raddled face revealed years of hardship on the road.
She felt raddled after weeks of caring for the sick.
The actor portrayed a raddled detective worn down by endless cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: careworn - showing the effects of worry or anxiety
  • antonym: fresh - looking healthy, rested, or not tired
  • synonym: haggard - looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue or worry
