cliff

noun
steep high face of rock/earth
1.0 30/m
Pronunciation: / klÉªf / (AmE), / klÉªf / (BrE)
A high, steep face of rock or earth, especially at the edge of the sea or along a river.

Examples

Rocky cliffs edge
Many birds nested on the rocky cliffs along the northern coast.
She stood near the edge of the cliff, feeling the wind rush past her face.
The hiker admired the view from the top of the cliff that towered above the ocean.
