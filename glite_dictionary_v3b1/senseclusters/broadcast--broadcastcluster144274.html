broadcast

verb
make information widely known
3.0 3.62/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbr…îdÀåk√¶st / (AmE), / Ààbr…îÀêdÀåk…ëÀêst / (BrE)
to make information widely known; to spread news or ideas publicly or to many people

Examples

To broadcast news
Rumors about the politician were quickly broadcast on social media.
The organization broadcast a warning to all its members about the scam.
They broadcast news of their engagement to friends and family.

Synonyms

  • disseminate
    6.8 7.58/m
    disseminate spreads information widely in formal or academic settings, while broadcast shares news to the general public.
  • publicize
    4.0 7.69/m
    publicize makes something known to the public, often for promotion, while broadcast describes sending news or programmes broadly.
  • propagate
    5.9 2.16/m
    propagate spreads ideas or beliefs across groups, often with purpose, while broadcast means sending news widely to many people.
  • spread
    1.6 45/m
    spread becomes known or moves quickly among people, often without plan, while broadcast implies planned public transmission.
  • circulate
    3.9 9.19/m
    circulate passes information or documents among a group, often privately, while broadcast reaches a wide public audience at once.
  • air
    3.7 2.27/m
    air expresses opinions or complaints publicly, often in speech, while broadcast usually means formally sending news or programmes to many.
  • diffuse
    5.5 4.17/m
    diffuse spreads out over an area or among people more slowly, while broadcast implies active public announcement to many.
  • distribute
    2.0 8.85/m
    distribute gives out physical items or copies to people or places, while broadcast shares information publicly without handing things out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circulate - to pass from person to person or place to place, especially information or rumors
  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • synonym: spread - to extend or distribute something over a wide area or among many people
