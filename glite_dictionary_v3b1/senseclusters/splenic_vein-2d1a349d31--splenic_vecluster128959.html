splenic vein

noun
vein draining blood from the spleen
Pronunciation: / ˈsplinɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈspliːnɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that collects blood from the spleen and joins with the superior mesenteric vein to form the portal vein

Examples

The splenic vein blood
An obstruction in the splenic vein can lead to complications in the portal circulation.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully identified the splenic vein before proceeding with the procedure.
The splenic vein joins with the superior mesenteric vein to form the portal vein.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: portal vein - large vein formed by the union of the splenic and superior mesenteric veins, directing blood to the liver
