fixed costs

noun
business expenses not changing with production
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪkst ˈkɑsts / (AmE), / ˌfɪkst ˈkɒsts / (BrE)
business expenses that remain constant regardless of the volume of goods or services produced, such as rent or insurance

Examples

Fixed costs like rent
Because rent and salaries are fixed costs, they must be paid even when business is slow.
Insurance premiums are often classified as fixed costs in a company's financial statements.
When analyzing expenses, it's important to distinguish fixed costs from variable costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: variable costs - costs that change according to production volume
