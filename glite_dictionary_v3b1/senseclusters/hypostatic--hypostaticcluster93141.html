hypostatic

adjective
relating to hypostasis
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈstætɪk / (BrE)
relating to hypostasis, especially describing conditions caused by the settling of blood or fluids in dependent parts of the body

Examples

Hypostatic pneumonia
The patient developed hypostatic pneumonia after being bedridden for several weeks.
Doctors monitor for hypostatic changes in patients who are unable to move regularly.
Prolonged immobility can lead to hypostatic congestion in the lungs or other organs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dependent - affected by or resulting from dependence on gravity or position
