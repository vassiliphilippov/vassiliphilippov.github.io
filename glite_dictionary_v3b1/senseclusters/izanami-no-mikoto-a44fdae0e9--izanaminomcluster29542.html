Izanami-no-Mikoto

proper noun
Japanese mother goddess in mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: /iː.zə.ˈnɑː.mi noʊ ˈmiː.koʊ.toʊ/ /; / /ɪ.zə.ˈnɑː.mi noʊ miː.ˈkoʊ.toʊ/ (AmE), /iː.zə.ˈnɑː.mi nəʊ ˈmiː.kəʊ.tə/ /; / /ɪ.zə.ˈnɑː.mi nəʊ miː.ˈkəʊ.tə/ (BrE)
A goddess in Japanese mythology who, with her brother and husband Izanagi, is considered the mother of many islands and deities of Japan.

Examples

Izanami-no-Mikoto goddess
In Shinto mythology, Izanami-no-Mikoto gave birth to the islands of Japan.
Legends say Izanami-no-Mikoto descended to Yomi, the land of the dead.
Worshippers pray to Izanami-no-Mikoto for blessings of fertility and creation.
