nose

verb
to detect by smell or investigation
Pronunciation: / noʊz / (AmE), / nəʊz / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to detect or discover something by means of the sense of smell; to search for or identify by smelling or investigating closely.

Examples

To nose out a clue
She nosed out the truth despite everyone's efforts to disguise it.
The dog nosed around the garden, searching for hidden treats.
The police managed to nose out the criminal's hideout after days of investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sniff - to inhale air audibly through the nose, often to detect a smell
