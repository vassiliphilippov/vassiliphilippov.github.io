peliosis

noun
blood disease causing subcutaneous bleeding
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈlaɪ.oʊ.sɪs/ /; / /pə.ˈlaɪ.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /pə.ˈlaɪ.əʊ.sɪs/ /; / /pə.ˈlaɪ.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
Any of several blood diseases characterized by subcutaneous bleeding, resulting in purplish discolorations or lesions on the skin.

Examples

Peliosis skin spots
A visible symptom of peliosis is the sudden appearance of bruiselike spots without any known trauma.
Liver peliosis is a rare condition sometimes associated with certain medications or infections.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with peliosis after observing distinct purplish patches under her skin.
