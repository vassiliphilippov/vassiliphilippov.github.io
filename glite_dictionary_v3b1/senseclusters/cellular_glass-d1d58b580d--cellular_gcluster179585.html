cellular glass

noun
lightweight insulating foam glass
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛljələr ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A lightweight, opaque material made by adding powdered carbon to crushed glass and firing the mixture to produce a foam-like, insulating glass.

Examples

Lightweight cellular glass
Engineers chose cellular glass for its excellent moisture resistance and durability.
Some industrial tanks are protected with cellular glass to prevent heat loss.
The new building was insulated with cellular glass panels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foam glass - a type of glass with a porous, cellular structure
