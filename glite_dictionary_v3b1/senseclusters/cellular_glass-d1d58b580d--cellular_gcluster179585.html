cellular glass

noun
lightweight insulating foam glass
7.0 0.00/m

A lightweight, opaque material made by adding powdered carbon to crushed glass and firing the mixture to produce a foam-like, insulating glass.

Examples

Lightweight cellular glass
Engineers chose cellular glass for its excellent moisture resistance and durability.
Some industrial tanks are protected with cellular glass to prevent heat loss.
The new building was insulated with cellular glass panels.

Synonyms

  • expanded glass
    6.8 0.00/m
    expanded glass is made by heating crushed glass with powdered carbon into a rigid insulating foam, while cellular glass refers to the same lightweight insulating material.
  • foam glass
    6.7 0.01/m
    foam glass is a lightweight porous material made by expanding crushed glass for insulation, while cellular glass implies a rigid, closed-cell insulating foam used in construction.
