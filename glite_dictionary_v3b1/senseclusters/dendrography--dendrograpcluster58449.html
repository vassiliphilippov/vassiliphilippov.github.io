dendrography

noun
tree growth measurement
9.9
Pronunciation: /den.drə.ˈgræf.iː/ /; / /den.droʊ.ˈgræf.iː/ (AmE), /den.drə.ˈgræf.iː/ /; / /den.drəʊ.ˈgræf.iː/ (BrE)
the measurement and recording of tree growth, especially by using instruments or charts to track annual or seasonal changes

Examples

Dendrography for tree growth
Researchers used dendrography to monitor how the trees responded to changes in rainfall over several years.
Advances in dendrography have helped scientists understand the effects of climate change on forests.
The annual report included detailed dendrography data showing the growth patterns of different tree species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dendrochronology - the scientific study of tree rings to date events and environmental changes
  • synonym: forestry - the science and practice of managing forests and trees
