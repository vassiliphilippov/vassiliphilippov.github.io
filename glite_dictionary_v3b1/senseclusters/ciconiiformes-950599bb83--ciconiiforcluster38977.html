Ciconiiformes

noun
order of long-legged wading birds (herons, storks etc.)
Pronunciation: / sɪˌkoʊniˈɪfɔrmiːz /; / saɪˌkoʊni.əˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / sɪˌkəʊniˈɪfɔːmiːz /; / sɪˌkəʊnɪ.ɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of tropical, marsh-dwelling wading birds with long legs and bills, including herons, storks, spoonbills, flamingos, and ibises

Examples

The Ciconiiformes birds
Herons and storks are both members of the order Ciconiiformes.
Scientists debated the classification of flamingos within the Ciconiiformes.
The Ciconiiformes are found in marshes and wetlands around the world.
Herons, a type of Ciconiiformes, are commonly seen wading in shallow waters.
The migration patterns of Ciconiiformes have long fascinated ornithologists.
There are several species of Ciconiiformes found in the wetlands of Africa.
