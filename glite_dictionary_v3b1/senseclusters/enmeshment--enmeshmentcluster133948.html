enmeshment

noun
state of entanglement
7.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈmɛʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈmɛʃmənt / (BrE)
the state of being entangled or caught, especially in a net, mesh, or similar material

Examples

Enmeshment in a net
The bird struggled to escape from the enmeshment of the plastic netting.
Divers carefully removed the dolphin from the enmeshment of discarded fishing lines.
The enmeshment of roots made it difficult to dig up the old tree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entanglement - the state of being twisted together or caught in something
  • antonym: freedom - the state of not being restricted or entangled
