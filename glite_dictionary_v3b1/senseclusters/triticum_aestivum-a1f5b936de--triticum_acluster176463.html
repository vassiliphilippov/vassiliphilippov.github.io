Triticum aestivum

noun
species of common wheat
Pronunciation: / trɪˈtɪkəm iːˈstiːvəm / (AmE), / trɪˈtɪkəm iːˈstiːvəm / (BrE)
the species of wheat most widely grown for its grain, used in bread, pasta, and many other foods

Examples

Triticum aestivum wheat grain
Triticum aestivum is the most commonly cultivated type of wheat worldwide.
Bread flour is typically milled from Triticum aestivum because of its high gluten content.
Researchers are studying Triticum aestivum to develop strains more resistant to disease.
