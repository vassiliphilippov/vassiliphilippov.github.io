biconditional

adjective
logical equivalence
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪkənˈdɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪkənˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to a logical statement in which two conditions are true if and only if each implies the other

Examples

Biconditional statement
A biconditional statement is true only when both parts are either true or false.
The theorem was rewritten in biconditional form to clarify the relationship between the two properties.
In logic, a biconditional expression is often represented by a double-headed arrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equivalent - having the same value, function, or meaning
