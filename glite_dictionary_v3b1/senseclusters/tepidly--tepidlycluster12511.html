tepidly

adverb
in an unenthusiastic or lukewarm way
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛpɪdli / (AmE), / ˈtɛpɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows little enthusiasm or warmth; in a lukewarm manner

Examples

He tepidly agreed
He tepidly agreed to join the committee, showing little real interest.
Her proposal was tepidly received by the board, with only a few polite questions.
The audience tepidly applauded the singer after her performance.

Related Terms
