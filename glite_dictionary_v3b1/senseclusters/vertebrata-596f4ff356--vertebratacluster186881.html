Vertebrata

noun
animals with backbones (subphylum)
Pronunciation: / ˌvɝːtəˈbreɪtə /; / ˌvɜːrtəˈbreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌvɜːtɪˈbreɪtə / (BrE)
a major group (subphylum) of animals that have a backbone, including fishes, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals

Examples

The Vertebrata group
Biologists classify humans as members of the Vertebrata because we possess a backbone.
The subphylum Vertebrata includes fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals.
Unlike invertebrates, Vertebrata exhibit a high level of organization and complexity.
Both birds and reptiles belong to the subphylum Vertebrata.
Members of the Vertebrata include a wide range of species, from tiny fish to large mammals.
The evolutionary history of the Vertebrata is a central topic in zoology.
