multiple

noun
number divisible by another
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltəpəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl / (BrE)
A number that can be divided by another number without a remainder; the product of a given number and an integer.

Examples

Multiple of five
In the times tables, each result is a multiple of the starting number.
Twelve is a multiple of three, as twelve divided by three equals four.
When checking your answer, ask if the number is a multiple of five.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divisor - a number by which another number is divided
  • antonym: factor - a number that divides another number exactly
