iatrogenic

adjective
resulting from medical treatment
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.ə.trəˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪ.ə.trəˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) caused by medical examination or treatment.

Examples

Iatrogenic disease
Doctors strive to minimize iatrogenic effects when prescribing new medications.
Rare iatrogenic complications may arise from even routine medical procedures.
The patient suffered an iatrogenic infection after undergoing surgery.
