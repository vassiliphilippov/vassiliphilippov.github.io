glissade

noun
gliding step in ballet
Pronunciation: / ɡlɪˈsɑd / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɑːd / (BrE)
a gliding or sliding step in ballet or dance, where one foot slides along the floor before both feet come together, or any smooth gliding movement in dance

Examples

Ballet glissade
During rehearsals, students practiced their glissade to improve their transitions between movements.
She incorporated a quick glissade into her routine to seamlessly link the two sequences.
The dancer performed a perfect glissade across the stage, earning applause from the audience.
The ballerina executed a perfect glissade as she crossed the stage.
Skiers often perform a controlled glissade to descend steep slopes safely.
He watched the children attempt a playful glissade down the icy hill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leap - a jump or spring through the air
  • synonym: slide - a smooth movement along a surface
