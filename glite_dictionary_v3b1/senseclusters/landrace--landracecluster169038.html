landrace

noun
local adapted variety
Pronunciation: / ˈlændˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˈlændˌreɪs / (BrE)
a local variety of a domesticated animal or plant that has adapted to its natural environment over time

Examples

A landrace variety
The farmer chose a landrace of corn that thrives in the local climate.
Many traditional landraces are valued for their resistance to disease.
Researchers are studying a landrace of sheep unique to the mountainous region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cultivar - a plant variety that has been produced in cultivation by selective breeding
  • antonym: hybrid - an organism produced by crossing different varieties or species
