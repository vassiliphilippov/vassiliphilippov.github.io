cementation

noun
metallurgical reduction process
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsiːmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in metallurgy where metal ions in solution are reduced to solid metal by a more reactive metal

Examples

Cementation with iron
Copper can be extracted from solution by cementation using iron as the reducing agent.
The cementation process is widely used in the recovery of precious metals from industrial waste.
Engineers improved the efficiency of cementation by optimizing the contact between the metals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction in which a substance loses electrons
  • synonym: precipitation - the process of a substance being separated from a solution as a solid
