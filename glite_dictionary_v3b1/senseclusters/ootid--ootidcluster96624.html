ootid

noun
ovum after fertilization but before zygote formation
9.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.tɪd / (BrE)
a cell formed in the final stages of oogenesis, specifically an ovum after being penetrated by a sperm but before becoming a zygote

Examples

The ootid cell
In mammals, the ootid represents the stage just after sperm entry into the egg.
Scientists observed the transformation of the ootid as it developed into an embryo.
The ootid undergoes rapid changes upon fertilization, leading to the formation of a zygote.
