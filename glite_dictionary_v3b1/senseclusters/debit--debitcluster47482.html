debit

noun
accounting entry for money owed or deducted
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛbɪt / (BrE)
An entry on the left side of an account in bookkeeping, representing an amount owed or the deduction of funds.

Examples

Bank debit entry
Each debit must be balanced by a corresponding credit in double-entry bookkeeping.
My bank statement showed a debit for the online purchase.
The accountant recorded a debit of $500 to the company's cash account.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credit - an accounting entry recording a sum received or added
