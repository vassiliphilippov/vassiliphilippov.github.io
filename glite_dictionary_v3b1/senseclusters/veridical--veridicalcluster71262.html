veridical

adjective
coinciding with reality; truthful
9.5
Pronunciation: / vəˈrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / vəˈrɪdɪkəl / (BrE)
(formal) Representing or corresponding to reality; truthful or accurate.

Examples

A veridical memory
Scientists study whether certain memories are veridical or distorted by imagination.
The philosopher distinguished between veridical and illusory perceptions.
The witness claimed to have had a veridical dream that matched the crime scene exactly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illusory - based on illusion; not real
  • synonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth
