loyal steed

noun
faithful horse companion (often figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔɪəl stid / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəl stiːd / (BrE)
a horse that is devoted and reliable, often used figuratively to mean a faithful companion

Examples

A loyal steed
After years together, Jack considered his old mare more than just a loyal steed.
In every legend, the knight sets off on his loyal steed to face danger.
She called her bike her loyal steed as it carried her to work each day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trusty steed - a reliable and trustworthy horse (often used metaphorically)
