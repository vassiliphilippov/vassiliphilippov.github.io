aluminum bronze

noun
a strong, corrosion-resistant copper-aluminum alloy
4.1
Pronunciation: / əˌluːmɪnəm ˈbroʊnz /; / əˌluːmɪniəm ˈbroʊnz / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˌmɪniəm ˈbrəʊnz /; / ˌæljuːˈmɪniəm ˈbrəʊnz / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and aluminum known for its high strength and resistance to corrosion

Examples

Aluminum bronze alloy
Because of its strength, aluminum bronze is often used in the manufacture of industrial parts.
The ship's propeller was cast from aluminum bronze to prevent corrosion from seawater.
Valves made of aluminum bronze are commonly used in chemical processing plants.
Engineers selected aluminium bronze for the valve due to its excellent tensile properties.
Many marine components are made from aluminium bronze because of its resistance to seawater corrosion.
The statue was cast in aluminium bronze to achieve both strength and an attractive finish.
