endorse

verb
give support or approval
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to give support or approval to someone or something, often publicly or officially

Examples

To endorse a candidate
Many public figures endorse environmental protection efforts to encourage action.
She refused to endorse any candidate in the election.
The committee unanimously endorsed the new proposal for expansion.
He refused to endorse any product he didn't believe in.
Several major newspapers endorsed the candidate before the election.
The committee decided to endorse the new policy after much debate.
The organization endorsed the company's eco-friendly products as meeting strict quality standards.
The tour operator is endorsed by the National Tourism Board for its high safety standards.
This label endorses the appliance as energy efficient according to federal guidelines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approve - to officially agree to or accept as satisfactory
  • synonym: certify - to attest or guarantee formally as meeting requirements
  • antonym: oppose - to act against or refuse to support
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, approve, or believe something
  • synonym: support - to give assistance or encouragement to
