Lilliputian

noun
a tiny person or thing; inhabitant of Lilliput
9.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪlɪˈpjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪlɪˈpjuːʃən / (BrE)
a very small person or being, originally one of the tiny inhabitants of Lilliput in Jonathan Swift's novel 'Gulliver's Travels'; by extension, a person or thing that is unusually small or miniature.

Examples

A Lilliputian figure
He marveled at the Lilliputian proportions of the rare bonsai trees.
In Swift's tale, a Lilliputian could easily fit in the palm of a hand.
The museum displayed a collection of Lilliputian chairs, each perfectly crafted to scale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: giant - a very large person or thing, opposite of tiny
  • synonym: miniature - something much smaller than usual size
