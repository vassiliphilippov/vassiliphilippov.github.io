fake

verb
act or talk insincerely; bluff
1.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /feɪk/ (AmE), /feɪk/ (BrE)
to speak or act insincerely, without regard for facts or truth; to pretend or bluff, especially to cover ignorance or lack of preparation

Examples

To fake an answer
Caught off guard by the question, he decided to fake his way through the answer.
She often tries to fake confidence during presentations, even when she's unsure.
When you don't know the facts, it's better to admit it than to fake it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluff - to pretend to have confidence or knowledge, especially to deceive others
  • synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • antonym: speak honestly - to communicate sincerely and truthfully
