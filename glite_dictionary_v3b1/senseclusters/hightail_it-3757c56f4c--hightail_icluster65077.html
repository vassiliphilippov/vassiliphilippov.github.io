hightail it

verb
leave or escape quickly
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌteɪl ɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌteɪl ɪt / (BrE)
to leave or flee a place very quickly, especially in order to escape trouble or danger

Examples

To hightail it quickly
As soon as the alarm sounded, the thieves hightailed it out of the building.
If we don't hightail it, we'll miss the last train home.
She saw the angry dog and immediately hightailed it in the opposite direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
