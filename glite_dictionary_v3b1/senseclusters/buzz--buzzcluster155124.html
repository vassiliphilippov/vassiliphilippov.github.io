buzz

noun
lively activity and excited talk
1.0
Pronunciation: / bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
a situation of lively activity and excited talk or rumors, especially among a group of people

Examples

Buzz of excitement
A buzz of excitement filled the stadium before the big match began.
The party had a real buzz about it, with conversations happening in every corner.
There was a buzz in the office as everyone discussed the surprising news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hum - a low, continuous background sound or feeling of activity
  • antonym: silence - the absence of noise, talk, or activity
