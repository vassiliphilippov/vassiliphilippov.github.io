sycophantic

adjective
excessively flattering to gain favor
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪkəˈfæntɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪkəˈfæntɪk / (BrE)
behaving in an overly flattering or praising way towards someone important or powerful to gain favor or advantage

Examples

Sycophantic behavior
He disliked the sycophantic atmosphere at the royal court, where everyone sought favor through flattery.
Her sycophantic praise of the CEO was embarrassing to watch in the meeting.
The manager was surrounded by sycophantic assistants who laughed at all his jokes.
His sycophantic behavior towards celebrities was embarrassing to watch.
She adopted a sycophantic tone when speaking to her boss, hoping for a promotion.
The manager grew tired of his team's sycophantic compliments and insincere praise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critical - expressing adverse or disapproving comments
  • synonym: fawning - displaying exaggerated flattery or affection
  • antonym: insolent - showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect
  • synonym: obsequious - excessively eager to please or obey
