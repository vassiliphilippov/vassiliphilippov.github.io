benzodiazepine

noun
psychoactive drug used as a tranquilizer or sedative
8.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /ben.zoʊ.daɪ.ˈæz.ə.piːn/ /; / /ben.zoʊ.daɪ.ˈæz.ɪ.piːn/ (AmE), /ben.zəʊ.daɪ.ˈæz.ə.piːn/ /; / /ben.zəʊ.daɪ.ˈæz.ɪ.piːn/ (BrE)
any of a class of psychoactive drugs used as tranquilizers, sedatives, hypnotics, or muscle relaxants, which can cause dependency with long-term use

Examples

Benzodiazepine drug use
Long-term use of benzodiazepines can lead to physical dependence and withdrawal symptoms.
Some patients receive a benzodiazepine before surgery to help them relax.
The doctor prescribed a benzodiazepine to help manage her anxiety symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sedative - a drug or substance that calms or relaxes someone
  • antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
