filariasis

noun
parasitic disease with worm-caused lymph blockage
7.4
Pronunciation: / fɪˌlɛəriˈaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / fɪˌlæriˈeɪsɪs / (BrE)
A tropical disease caused by infection with filarial nematode worms, leading to blockage of the lymphatic vessels and resulting in swelling or tissue damage.

Examples

Filariasis infection
Doctors identified the swelling in his legs as a symptom of filariasis.
In some regions, filariasis remains a major public health challenge due to poor sanitation.
Prevention of filariasis involves controlling the mosquitoes that spread the parasite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elephantiasis - gross enlargement of body parts from lymphatic obstruction, especially a complication of filariasis
