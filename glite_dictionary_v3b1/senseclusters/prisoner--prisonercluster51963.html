prisoner

noun
a person held in prison or captivity
1.0 1.1
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪz.ən.ər/ (AmE), /ˈprɪz.ən.ə/ (BrE)
a person who is kept in prison or confined, especially as a result of a crime or during a war

Examples

A prisoner in jail
As a prisoner, he was allowed one phone call per week.
During the war, many soldiers became prisoners behind enemy lines.
The court sentenced him to ten years as a prisoner for armed robbery.

Related Terms
