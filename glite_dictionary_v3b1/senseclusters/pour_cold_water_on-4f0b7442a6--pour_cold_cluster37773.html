pour cold water on

verb
discourage or express disapproval
4.7
Pronunciation: / pɔr koʊld ˈwɔtɚ ɑn /; / pɔr koʊld ˈwɔt̬ɚ ɑn / (AmE), / pɔː kəʊld ˈwɔːtə ɒn / (BrE)
to criticize or express doubt about an idea, plan, or enthusiasm, making people feel less excited or discouraged

Examples

To pour cold water on ideas
It's discouraging when people pour cold water on your dreams instead of supporting them.
Sara was excited about her new idea, but Tom quickly poured cold water on it.
The manager poured cold water on our proposal, saying it was unrealistic.
Every time we mention a new idea, he pours cold water on it.
She didn't want to pour cold water on their excitement, but the plan seemed unworkable.
The critics poured cold water on the film before it was even released.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticize - to find fault with, express disapproval of
  • synonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic
  • antonym: support - to give approval or encouragement to something
