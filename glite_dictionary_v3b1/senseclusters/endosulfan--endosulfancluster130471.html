endosulfan

noun
toxic agricultural insecticide
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈsʌlfæn / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈsʌlfæn / (BrE)
a toxic chemical insecticide used in agriculture, now banned or restricted in many countries due to health and environmental risks

Examples

endosulfan insecticide use
The government banned endosulfan after studies showed its harmful effects on human health.
Farmers once used endosulfan to protect crops from insect pests, but safer alternatives are now preferred.
Exposure to endosulfan has been linked to serious environmental and health problems in several regions.
