blistered

adjective
covered with blisters
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪstərd / (AmE), / ˈblɪstəd / (BrE)
having raised areas on the skin or surface filled with liquid, usually caused by injury, heat, or irritation

Examples

Blistered feet
After walking all day in new shoes, his heels were blistered and sore.
The chef's hands became blistered from accidentally touching the hot pan.
The paint on the old wall was blistered and peeling after years of sun exposure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blister - a small pocket of fluid on the skin caused by friction or burning
  • antonym: unblemished - not marked or damaged; without blisters or flaws
