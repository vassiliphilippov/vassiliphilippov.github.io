red sorrel

noun
a plant cultivated for edible calyxes and fiber
9.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / r…õd Ààs…îÀêr…ôl / (AmE), / r…õd Ààs…ír…ôl / (BrE)
An East Indian annual herb or perennial subshrub (Hibiscus sabdariffa) widely cultivated for its edible fleshy calyxes used in foods like tarts and jelly, and for its bast fiber.

Examples

Red sorrel calyxes
Farmers harvested red sorrel for its calyxes, which are used to make jams and jellies.
The bast fiber of red sorrel is used in making coarse textiles in some regions.
The refreshing drink was made from dried red sorrel petals and sweet spices.
