icecap

noun
permanent mass of ice and snow covering land
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌkæp / (BrE)
A thick, permanent mass of ice and snow covering a large area of land, especially in polar regions or on mountain peaks.

Examples

The icecap melts
Global warming threatens to shrink the Antarctic icecap dramatically over the next century.
Melting of the Arctic icecap has raised concerns among climate scientists.
The summit of the mountain is hidden beneath a permanent icecap.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glacier - a slowly moving mass of ice
  • synonym: ice sheet - a very large icecap covering a continent-sized area
