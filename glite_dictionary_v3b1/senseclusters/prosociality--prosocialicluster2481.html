prosociality

noun
the tendency to act for others' benefit
Pronunciation: / proʊˌsoʊʃiˈælɪti / (AmE), / prəʊˌsəʊʃɪˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of acting in ways that benefit others or contribute positively to society

Examples

Strong prosociality behavior
Her commitment to volunteering reflects her strong sense of prosociality.
Research shows that children who display prosociality tend to have better relationships with their peers.
The school encourages prosociality by rewarding cooperation and kindness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • antonym: antisociality - behavior or quality contrary to the interests or well-being of society
