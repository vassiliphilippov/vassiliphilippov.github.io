chlorination

noun
addition or substitution of chlorine in compounds
Pronunciation: / ˌklɔːrəˈneɪʃən /; / ˌklɔrəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌklɔːrəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding chlorine to something, especially to disinfect water or cause a chemical change

Examples

Chlorination of water
Chlorination is often used to disinfect drinking water and kill harmful bacteria.
Incomplete chlorination during water treatment can result in the formation of hazardous by-products.
The industrial chlorination of organic compounds can produce a variety of useful chemicals.
Chlorination is an essential step in making tap water safe to drink.
Many swimming pools rely on chlorination to keep the water clean and free from harmful microorganisms.
The city's chlorination process ensures bacteria and viruses are eliminated from the water supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chlorinate - to treat or combine with chlorine
  • synonym: disinfection - the process of cleaning something to destroy bacteria or viruses
