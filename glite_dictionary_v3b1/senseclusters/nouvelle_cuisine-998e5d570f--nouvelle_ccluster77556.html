nouvelle cuisine

noun
style of French cooking with light sauces
3.8
Pronunciation: / nuːˈvɛl kwiˈzin / (AmE), / nuːˈvɛl kwiːˈziːn / (BrE)
a style of French cooking characterized by light sauces and an emphasis on bringing out the natural flavors of fresh ingredients, rather than using heavy butter and cream-based sauces

Examples

Nouvelle cuisine style
After years of traditional recipes, she decided to experiment with nouvelle cuisine for a lighter dining experience.
Many top restaurants adopted nouvelle cuisine in the 1970s to highlight the freshness of local ingredients.
The chef prefers nouvelle cuisine because it allows each flavor to stand out on its own.
