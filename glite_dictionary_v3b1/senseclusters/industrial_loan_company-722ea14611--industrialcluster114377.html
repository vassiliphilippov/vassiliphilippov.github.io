industrial loan company

noun
finance company making small loans
2.5 7.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌstriəl loʊn ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl ləʊn ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a financial institution that makes small loans, traditionally to industrial workers, but now often to a wider range of customers; especially in the United States, a state-chartered bank that may lend and accept deposits but usually does not offer checking accounts.

Examples

An industrial loan company loan
He secured a car loan through an industrial loan company rather than a traditional bank.
Many industrial loan companies originated to help factory workers access credit quickly.
The state approved a new industrial loan company focused on serving manufacturing employees.
