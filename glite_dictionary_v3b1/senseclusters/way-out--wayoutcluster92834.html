way-out

adjective
strikingly unconventional; unusual
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈweɪ aʊt / (BrE)
Strikingly unconventional or unusual; very far from what is considered normal, especially in ideas, appearance, or behavior.

Examples

A way-out idea
His way-out theories about time travel were met with disbelief.
She always wears the most way-out clothes at parties, making her stand out.
Some of the artist's way-out ideas shocked his more conservative fans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - conforming to accepted standards; traditional
  • synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange
