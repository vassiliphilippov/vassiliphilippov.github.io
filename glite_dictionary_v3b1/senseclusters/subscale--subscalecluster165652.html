subscale

noun
secondary measurement scale
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.skeɪl/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.skeɪl/ (BrE)
a smaller, secondary scale on a measuring instrument or chart used for more precise or detailed measurements

Examples

Instrument subscale
The technician used the subscale on the instrument to record a more accurate reading.
By carefully aligning the subscale, she could measure the object's length to a fraction of a millimeter.
Some advanced rulers include a subscale for measuring smaller increments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main scale - the primary scale on a measuring instrument
  • synonym: vernier - a small movable scale for obtaining fractional parts of divisions on a main scale
