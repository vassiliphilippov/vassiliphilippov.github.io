complicated

adjective
hard to analyze or understand
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmpləˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
difficult to understand or deal with because of having many interconnected parts or aspects.

Examples

A complicated problem
She found the math problem too complicated to solve without help.
The instructions for assembling the furniture were so complicated that I had to call customer support.
The relationship between the two countries is highly complicated and has deep historical roots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complex - consisting of many different and connected parts; not simple
  • antonym: simple - easy to understand or do; not complicated
