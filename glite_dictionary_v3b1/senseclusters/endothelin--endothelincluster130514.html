endothelin

noun
blood vessel-constricting protein
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈθiːlɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈθiːlɪn / (BrE)
a protein produced by cells that causes blood vessels to narrow, increasing blood pressure

Examples

endothelin protein
Researchers discovered that endothelin plays a key role in regulating blood pressure.
Doctors measured the patient's endothelin levels to assess the risk of hypertension.
Certain medications can block the effects of endothelin to help treat heart disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vasoconstrictor - a substance that causes blood vessels to become narrower
  • antonym: vasodilator - a substance that causes blood vessels to widen
