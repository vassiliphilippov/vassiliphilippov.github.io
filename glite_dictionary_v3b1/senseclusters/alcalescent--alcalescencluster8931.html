alcalescent

adjective
tending to become alkaline
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.kəˈlɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈlɛs.ənt / (BrE)
tending to become alkaline or slightly alkaline; having a pH slightly above neutral

Examples

Slightly alcalescent liquid
After several reactions, the liquid became slightly alcalescent.
An alcalescent solution may be preferred in certain chemical processes.
The soil in this region is alcalescent, supporting unique types of vegetation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: alkaline - having the properties of an alkali; having a pH greater than 7
  • degree lower: neutral - neither acidic nor alkaline; having a pH of 7
