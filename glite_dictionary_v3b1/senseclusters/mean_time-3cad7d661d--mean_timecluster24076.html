mean time

noun
time based on the mean sun's motion
Pronunciation: / min taɪm / (AmE), / miːn taɪm / (BrE)
(Astronomy) Time measured by the motion of the mean sun, an imaginary sun moving uniformly along the celestial equator, used as a standard for civil timekeeping.

Examples

Mean time in astronomy
Before modern timekeeping, cities often maintained their own mean time based on longitude.
Many old clocks were set according to mean time rather than local apparent solar time.
The concept of mean time is crucial in determining standard civil time zones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apparent time - time determined by the actual position of the real sun
