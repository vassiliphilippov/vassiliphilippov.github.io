repressive

adjective
suppressing gene expression (biology)
7.2
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈpre.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈpre.sɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈpre.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈpre.sɪv/ (BrE)
(in biology, especially genetics and epigenetics) causing or associated with the suppression of gene expression, for example through chromatin states, histone modifications, or repressor proteins.

Examples

Repressive chromatin
The repressive chromatin state prevents transcription of nearby genes during early development.
Researchers found that specific histone modifications were repressive to gene expression in cancer cells.
Mutations in the protein complex made previously active promoters become repressive and silent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permissive - in chromatin biology, allowing or promoting gene expression; opposite of repressive
  • synonym: silencing - the process by which gene transcription is blocked or greatly reduced
