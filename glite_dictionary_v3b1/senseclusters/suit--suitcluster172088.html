suit

verb
to be convenient or acceptable to
1.9 24/m
Pronunciation: / suÀêt / (AmE), / suÀêt /; / sjuÀêt / (BrE)
to be convenient, acceptable, or appropriate for someone; to fit someone's needs or wishes

Examples

Suit your needs
Does this solution suit your requirements?
Monday would suit me better if that's possible.
The arrangement suited everyone involved perfectly.
Does Monday suit you for the meeting?
That arrangement suited us perfectly.
You can choose any time that suits you best.
It suited her nature to work independently rather than in a large team.
That color really suits you; you should wear it more often.
The quiet lifestyle of the countryside suits him much better than city life.

Synonyms

  • fit
    1.0 60/m
    fit means to match or meet needs in a direct, practical way, while suit means to be convenient or pleasing to someone.
  • befit
    6.8 1.95/m
    befit is a formal verb meaning to be proper or fitting for someone, while suit means to be convenient or meet someone's wishes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: befit - to be suitable or proper for someone or something
  • synonym: fit - to be of the correct size or shape; to be suitable
  • antonym: misfit - to not be suitable or appropriate for someone or something
  • synonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied
