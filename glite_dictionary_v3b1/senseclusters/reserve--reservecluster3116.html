reserve

noun
substitute player in sports
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɝv /; / rɪˈzɝːv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
an athlete or member of a group who is not a regular participant but is available to take part if needed

Examples

A team reserve
Every team needs a strong reserve in case of unexpected injuries.
She spent most of the season as a reserve but finally got a chance to start.
The coach decided to put a reserve on the field after the main striker was injured.
After retiring from the army, he continued to serve in the reserve.
She joined the reserve to help in times of national emergency.
The government quickly mobilized the reserves during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active duty - military service when a person is in full-time service, not reserve
  • synonym: substitute - a player who replaces another during a game
