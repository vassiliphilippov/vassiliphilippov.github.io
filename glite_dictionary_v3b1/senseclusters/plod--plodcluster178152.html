plod

verb
walk with slow, heavy steps
5.5 0.51/m
Pronunciation: / plÉ‘d / (AmE), / plÉ’d / (BrE)
to walk with slow, heavy steps, often because of tiredness or difficulty

Examples

To plod home
After a long day of work, she plodded home in silence.
The old horse continued to plod along the winding country road.
We had to plod through thick mud to reach the campsite.
