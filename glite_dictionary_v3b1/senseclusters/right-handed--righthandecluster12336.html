right-handed

adjective
rotating to the right (technical sense)
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪtˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌraɪtˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a rotation, helix, or screw) turning or rotating to the right, particularly in a clockwise direction when viewed from a specific angle.

Examples

Right-handed screw
In physics, a right-handed helix follows the direction of the fingers when the right hand is curled.
The scientist explained the difference between right-handed and left-handed rotation to the class.
The screw has a right-handed thread that tightens when turned clockwise.
