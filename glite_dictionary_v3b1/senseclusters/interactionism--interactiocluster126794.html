interactionism

noun
mind-body theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən.ɪ.zɪm/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən.ɪ.zɪm/ (BrE)
the philosophical theory that mental and physical states are distinct but can causally influence each other

Examples

Mind-body interactionism
Many philosophers debate whether interactionism can truly explain the relationship between mind and body.
In his lecture, the professor introduced interactionism as an alternative to strict materialism.
Some scientists criticize interactionism for lacking empirical evidence of mental-physical causation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dualism - the belief that mind and body are fundamentally different substances
  • antonym: materialism - the belief that only physical matter exists and mental states are physical
