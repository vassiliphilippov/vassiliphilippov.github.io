decriminalization

noun
making something no longer a crime
6.9
Pronunciation: / diˌkrɪmɪnələˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of changing laws so that something that was previously illegal is no longer a criminal offense.

Examples

The decriminalization of drugs
Advocates have pushed for the decriminalization of sex work across the country.
After years of protest, the government announced the decriminalization of minor traffic offenses.
The decriminalization of certain drugs has sparked debates about public health and safety.
Advocates argue that decriminalisation could reduce harm and free up police resources.
Since the decriminalisation of certain offenses, there has been a decrease in related arrests.
The decriminalisation of cannabis has been a contentious issue in many countries.
