flyback

noun
rapid return movement
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbæk / (BrE)
a rapid return of a moving part to its starting position, especially in electronic devices such as oscilloscopes or televisions

Examples

Flyback in electronics
The flyback in the circuit allows the scanning beam to return quickly to the starting point.
Older televisions rely on a flyback transformer to generate the high voltage needed for the display.
During the flyback phase, the moving part resets to begin the next cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward to a new position
  • synonym: reset - to return something to its original position or state
