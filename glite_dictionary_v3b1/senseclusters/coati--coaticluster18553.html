coati

noun
Central and South American mammal
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɑːti / (AmE), / kəʊˈɑːti / (BrE)
An omnivorous mammal native to Central and South America, related to raccoons and recognized by its long snout and ringed tail.

Examples

A coati animal
A coati climbed the tree quickly when it sensed danger nearby.
The coati uses its long snout to forage for insects among the fallen leaves.
We saw a group of coatis crossing the rainforest trail during our trip.
