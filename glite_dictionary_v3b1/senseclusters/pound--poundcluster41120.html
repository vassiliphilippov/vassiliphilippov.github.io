pound

verb
strike repeatedly and forcefully
4.0 1.2
Pronunciation: /paʊnd/ (AmE), /paʊnd/ (BrE)
to hit, strike, or crush something repeatedly and forcefully, often making a loud noise

Examples

To pound the table
He angrily pounded his fists on the table during the meeting.
She pounded the dough until it became smooth and elastic.
The rain began to pound the roof, making it difficult to sleep.
Before cooking, you should pound the garlic cloves with a pestle to release their flavor.
She pounded the medicinal herbs into a fine paste using a stone bowl.
The chef pounds spices daily to make fresh curry powder for the restaurant.
He pounded on the door until someone answered.
Heavy rain pounded the windows all night long.
She pounded the meat to make it tender.

  • synonym: crush - to press or squeeze forcefully so as to break, damage, or distort in shape
  • synonym: grind - to reduce something to small particles or powder by crushing it

  • degree lower: tap - to hit lightly
