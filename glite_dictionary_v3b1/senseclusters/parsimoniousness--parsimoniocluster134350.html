parsimoniousness

noun
preference for simplicity in explanations
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɑːr.sə.ˈmoʊ.niː.əs.nəs/ /; / /pɑːr.sɪ.ˈmoʊ.niː.əs.nəs/ (AmE), /pɑː.sə.ˈməʊ.niː.əs.nəs/ /; / /pɑː.sɪ.ˈməʊ.niː.əs.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality of preferring simple explanations or models that use few assumptions; economy of assumptions in reasoning or analysis.

Examples

Parsimoniousness in explanations
Researchers favored parsimoniousness in their climate model to avoid overfitting and improve interpretability.
The statistician argued that the model's parsimoniousness made its predictions easier to explain to policymakers.
Philosophers often invoke parsimoniousness as a virtue when comparing competing scientific explanations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complexity - the state of having many parts or being intricate; opposite of simplicity
  • synonym: parsimony - the principle of preferring the simplest explanation with the fewest assumptions
  • synonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or uncomplicated
