disengaged

verb
to separate or release
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (BrE)
past tense and past participle of disengage: to separate or release something from what it is attached to.

Examples

He disengaged the lock
He disengaged the trailer from the truck and rolled it into the yard.
After the meeting, she disengaged herself from the conversation and left quietly.
The safety mechanism disengaged automatically when the machine was turned off.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attach - to fasten or join one thing to another
  • synonym: detach - to remove or separate something from something else
  • synonym: separate - to set or keep apart
