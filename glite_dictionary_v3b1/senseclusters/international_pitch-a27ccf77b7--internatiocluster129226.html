international pitch

noun
standard musical tuning pitch (A=440 Hz)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəɹˈnæʃənəl pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl pɪtʃ / (BrE)
the standard pitch used for tuning musical instruments for concert performances, typically assigning 440 Hz to the A above middle C.

Examples

The international pitch standard
All orchestras tuned their instruments to international pitch before the concert began.
Many pianos are calibrated to international pitch for consistency across performances.
The adoption of international pitch allowed musicians from different countries to play together more easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concert pitch - standard pitch for tuning musical instruments, synonymous with international pitch
