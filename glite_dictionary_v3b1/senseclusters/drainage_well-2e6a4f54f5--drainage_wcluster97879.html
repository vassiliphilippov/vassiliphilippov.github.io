drainage well

noun
well for disposing of excess water
6.6 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdreɪnɪdʒ wɛl / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnɪdʒ wɛl / (BrE)
A well constructed to receive and dispose of excess surface or groundwater by allowing it to drain into the ground or a lower stratum.

Examples

A drainage well system

Inspectors checked the drainage well for any blockages that could impede water flow.
The city installed a new drainage well to prevent waterlogging in the park.
