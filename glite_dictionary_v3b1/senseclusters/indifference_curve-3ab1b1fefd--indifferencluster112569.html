indifference curve

noun
curve showing equally preferred bundles (economics)
5.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdɪ.frəns kɜːrv/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdɪf.ər.əns kɜːrv/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdɪ.frəns kɜːv/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdɪf.ərəns kɜːv/ (BrE)
(in economics) a graph depicting different combinations of goods that a consumer values equally, showing levels at which they are indifferent between the options.

Examples

An indifference curve graph
Each point on an indifference curve represents a combination of goods that the consumer values equally.
The concept of the indifference curve is fundamental in modern consumer choice theory.
When designing new products, economists may analyze how the indifference curve shifts with changes in income.
