mind-altering

adjective
causing mood or perception changes
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnd ˌɔltɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ˌɔːltərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a drug or experience) causing changes in mood, perception, or consciousness.

Examples

Mind-altering drug effects
He warned that drinking the tea could have mind-altering consequences.
Some people seek out mind-altering experiences through meditation or psychedelics.
The researchers studied the effects of several mind-altering substances on memory.
