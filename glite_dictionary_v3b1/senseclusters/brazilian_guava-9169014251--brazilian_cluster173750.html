Brazilian guava

noun
South American fruit tree, like guava
2.8 6.5
Pronunciation: / brəˈzɪliən ˈɡwɑvə / (AmE), / brəˈzɪliən ˈɡwɑːvə / (BrE)
A South American tree (Psidium guineense) of the myrtle family, producing fruit similar to the common guava.

Examples

The Brazilian guava tree
Botanists often compare the flavor of Brazilian guava to that of the common guava, though it is less sweet.
She planted a Brazilian guava in her garden to enjoy its tangy, yellow fruit each summer.
The Brazilian guava thrives in warm, humid climates and is prized for its aromatic fruit.
