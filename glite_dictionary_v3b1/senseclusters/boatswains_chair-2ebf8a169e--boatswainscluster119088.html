boatswain's chair

noun
suspended seat for working on ships
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊts(ə)nz tʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈbəʊts(w)ənz tʃeə / (BrE)
a strong seat, usually made from a board and suspended by ropes, used to support a person working aloft or over the side of a ship

Examples

A boatswain's chair seat
A boatswain's chair provides a secure seat for sailors doing maintenance aloft.
He was hoisted up the mast in a boatswain's chair to repair the rigging.
The worker sat in a boatswain's chair as he painted the ship's hull.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bosun's chair - an alternative spelling referring to the same suspended seat used on ships
