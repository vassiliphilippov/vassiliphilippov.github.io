disciplinarity

noun
academic field characteristics
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəplɪˈnærɪti /; / ˌdɪsəplɪˈnærəti / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪplɪˈnærɪti / (BrE)
the qualities, organization, or state of being related to a particular academic discipline or field of study

Examples

Academic disciplinarity
Understanding the disciplinarity of sociology helps students approach research questions in a structured way.
The conference explored the disciplinarity of different scientific fields and how they interact.
Her thesis examined the disciplinarity of art history compared to cultural studies.
The university's strong disciplinarity can make collaboration between departments more challenging.
Some scholars argue that strict disciplinarity limits innovation in research.
Recent trends in education encourage moving beyond traditional disciplinarity to solve complex problems.
The university values disciplinarity in its traditional departments.
Her research demonstrates strong disciplinarity within the field of sociology.
Some scholars argue that strict disciplinarity can limit creative problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interdisciplinarity - involving two or more academic disciplines
  • antonym: multidisciplinarity - the involvement of several academic disciplines in an approach
  • synonym: specialization - focus on a specific area of study or work
