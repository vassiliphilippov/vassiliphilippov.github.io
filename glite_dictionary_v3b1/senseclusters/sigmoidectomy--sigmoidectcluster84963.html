sigmoidectomy

noun
removal of (part of) the sigmoid colon
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪɡmɔɪˈdɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌsɪɡmɔɪˈdɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of part or all of the sigmoid colon, commonly to treat disease such as cancer

Examples

A sigmoidectomy surgery
After her sigmoidectomy, she spent a few days recovering in the hospital before going home.
Complications following a sigmoidectomy may include infection or bleeding, which are carefully monitored.
The surgeon performed a sigmoidectomy to remove the cancerous section of the patient's colon.
