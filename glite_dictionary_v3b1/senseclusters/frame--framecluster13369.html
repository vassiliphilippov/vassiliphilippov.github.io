frame

verb
falsely make someone appear guilty
2.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /freɪm/ (AmE), /freɪm/ (BrE)
to make someone appear guilty of a crime by deliberately falsifying evidence or setting them up

Examples

To frame someone
He feared someone was framing him for a crime he didn't commit.
She was framed for the robbery by a jealous colleague.
The detective tried to frame the suspect with false evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exonerate - to officially state that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing
  • synonym: set up - to trick someone into being blamed for something they didn't do
