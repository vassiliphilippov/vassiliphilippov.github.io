hyperkalemia

noun
excess potassium in the blood (medical)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərkəˈliːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəkəˈliːmɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the level of potassium in the blood is higher than normal, often associated with kidney failure or the use of certain drugs

Examples

High hyperkalemia level
Certain medications can increase the risk of hyperkalemia in patients with kidney problems.
Prompt treatment is necessary to prevent complications from severe hyperkalemia.
The patient was diagnosed with hyperkalemia after blood tests revealed elevated potassium levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypokalemia - lower than normal levels of potassium in blood
