mallard

noun
common wild duck species
Pronunciation: / ˈmæl.ərd / (AmE), / ˈmæl.ɑːd / (BrE)
a common wild duck (Anas platyrhynchos), native to the Northern Hemisphere, from which most domestic ducks are descended

Examples

A mallard duck
A flock of mallards landed gracefully on the lake.
Male mallards are known for their distinctive iridescent green heads.
We spotted a female mallard nesting near the riverbank.
