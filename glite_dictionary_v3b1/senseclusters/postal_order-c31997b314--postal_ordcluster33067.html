postal order

noun
money order issued by a post office
1.2 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpoʊ.stəl ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /ˈpəʊ.stəl ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
a written document purchased at a post office allowing the stated sum to be paid to a named person at another post office

Examples

A postal order form

She received a postal order for her birthday from her aunt.
You can pay the fee by postal order or cheque.
