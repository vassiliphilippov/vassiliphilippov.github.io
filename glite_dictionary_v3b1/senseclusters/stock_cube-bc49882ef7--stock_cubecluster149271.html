stock cube

noun
cube of dehydrated stock for cooking
5.9 2.5
Pronunciation: /stɑːk kjuːb/ /; / /stɑːk kuːb/ (AmE), /stɒk kjuːb/ (BrE)
A small, solid cube made of dehydrated stock, used to add flavor to soups, stews, and other dishes.

Examples

A stock cube for soup
Many recipes call for a stock cube when fresh stock isn't available.
She added a stock cube to the boiling water to make a quick broth.
The chef prefers homemade broth but sometimes uses a stock cube for convenience.
