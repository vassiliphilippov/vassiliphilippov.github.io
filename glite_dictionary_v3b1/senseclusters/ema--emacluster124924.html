ema

noun
exponential moving average
Pronunciation: / ˌiː ɛm ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː ɛm ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a statistical indicator called the exponential moving average, which gives more weight to recent data points in a series

Examples

Stock ema line
Many traders use the ema to help identify trends in the stock market.
The 50-day ema is often used to signal potential buy or sell opportunities.
Analysts compared the ema with other indicators to confirm the market's direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moving average - a statistical calculation that smooths data by averaging values over a period
  • antonym: simple moving average - an average of data points over a period, giving equal weight to each point
