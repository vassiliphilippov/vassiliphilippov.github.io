locust swarm

noun
large group of locusts causing crop damage
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəst swɔrm / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəst swɔːm / (BrE)
a large group of locusts moving together that can cause extensive damage to crops and vegetation

Examples

A locust swarm moves
A locust swarm can travel hundreds of miles in search of new feeding grounds.
Farmers feared that the approaching locust swarm would ruin their entire harvest.
The sudden appearance of a locust swarm is often considered a natural disaster in many regions.
