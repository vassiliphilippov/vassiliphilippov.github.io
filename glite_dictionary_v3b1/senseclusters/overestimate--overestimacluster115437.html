overestimate

noun
an estimate that is too high
1.0 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.es.tə.mət/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.es.tə.mət/ (BrE)
an estimate or calculation that is higher than the actual value or reality

Examples

A big overestimate
Any overestimate in the data could lead to wasted resources.
Her sales forecast turned out to be an overestimate of actual demand.
The project's cost was based on an overestimate of the required materials.
His overestimate of the audience size led to unnecessary expenses.
It turned out the budget was based on an overestimate of the actual costs.
The company blamed the shortfall on an overestimate of expected sales.
