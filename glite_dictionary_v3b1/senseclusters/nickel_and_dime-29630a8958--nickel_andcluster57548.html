nickel and dime

verb
charge small amounts repeatedly
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪkəl ənd daɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ənd daɪm / (BrE)
to charge or extract small amounts of money repeatedly, often excessively or unfairly

Examples

To nickel and dime workers
He was annoyed that his paycheck was nickel and dimed by so many small deductions.
Many customers feel frustrated when companies nickel and dime them on routine services.
The hotel tried to nickel and dime us with extra fees for everything.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcharge - to charge too much for something
  • synonym: rip off - to cheat someone by charging too much
