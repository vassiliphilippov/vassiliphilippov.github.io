oke

noun
Ottoman weight unit
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈəʊkeɪ / (BrE)
a historical unit of mass used in the Ottoman Empire, approximately equal to 1.28 kilograms

Examples

An oke of sugar
In the Ottoman markets, merchants often sold spices by the oke.
Historical records list the price of wheat per oke in the 19th century.
A single oke of cotton was considered a significant amount for trade.
