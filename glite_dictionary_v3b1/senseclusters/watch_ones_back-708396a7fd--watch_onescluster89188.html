watch one's back

verb
be careful and protect oneself against others
Pronunciation: / wɑtʃ wʌnz bæk / (AmE), / wɒtʃ wʌnz bæk / (BrE)
to remain alert and be cautious about possible threats or dangers from others

Examples

To watch his back
After the argument with his boss, Tom started to watch his back at work.
In politics, you always have to watch your back because rivals can appear at any moment.
She reminded her friend to watch her back when dealing with unfamiliar colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be on one's guard - to stay alert to possible danger or deceit
  • antonym: let one's guard down - to stop being alert and cautious
