quoll

noun
Australian/New Guinea carnivorous marsupial
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / kwÉ‘l / (AmE), / kwÉ’l / (BrE)
A carnivorous marsupial native to Australia and New Guinea, belonging to the genus Dasyurus, characterized by a pointed snout and spotted coat.

Examples

A quoll animal
A quoll darted through the underbrush, its spotted coat blending into the forest floor.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered eastern quoll from extinction.
The quoll is rarely seen during the day as it is primarily nocturnal.

Synonyms

  • Dasyurus
    9.9 0.00/m
