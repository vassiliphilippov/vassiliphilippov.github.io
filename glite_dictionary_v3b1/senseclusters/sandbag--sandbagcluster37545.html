sandbag

verb
to downplay ability to deceive in games
5.1 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsænd.bæg/ (AmE), /ˈsænd.bæg/ (BrE)
to deceive, pressure, or treat someone unfairly, especially by downplaying abilities or using underhanded tactics

Examples

To sandbag in a game
After sandbagging in earlier rounds, he revealed his real talent and easily won the final.
He tried to sandbag his opponents by playing poorly during the warm-up.
She suspected he was sandbagging and hiding his true poker skills for the big tournaments.
He tried to sandbag his colleague into working late by threatening to report him.
She refused to let her friends sandbag her into organizing the entire event.
They sandbagged the manager into changing the policy against her wishes.
In a clever move, she decided to sandbag her opponents by keeping quiet about her true abilities.
Residents rushed to sandbag the riverbanks before the storm hit the area.
The team accused their rivals of trying to sandbag them with misleading information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluff - to mislead opponents by pretending to have a weaker or stronger ability
  • synonym: coerce - to force someone to do something by use of threats or pressure
  • synonym: deceive - to mislead or trick someone
  • synonym: hustle - to deceive or trick someone, often in gambling or games
  • synonym: protect - to guard or shield from harm
