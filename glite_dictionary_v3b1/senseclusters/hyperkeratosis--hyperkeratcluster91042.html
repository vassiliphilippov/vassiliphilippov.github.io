hyperkeratosis

noun
thickened skin condition
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˌkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition where the outer layer of the skin (or sometimes a mucous membrane) becomes abnormally thickened, often forming a hard or rough area

Examples

Hyperkeratosis of skin
The dermatologist explained that hyperkeratosis can result from repeated friction or pressure on the skin.
After months of manual labor, he developed hyperkeratosis on his palms.
Treatment for hyperkeratosis often includes moisturizing creams and removing the thickened skin.
The dermatologist diagnosed the rough patch on his heel as hyperkeratosis caused by repeated friction.
Wearing tight shoes for long periods can lead to hyperkeratosis on the toes or soles.
