relight

verb
to rekindle interest
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlaɪt / (BrE)
to make a feeling, interest, or memory become active or strong again, especially after it has faded

Examples

To relight old passion
The inspiring speech helped relight his passion for teaching after years of routine.
Their trip to the old city managed to relight memories of childhood adventures.
A new project can sometimes relight your enthusiasm for your work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extinguish - to cause something to end or stop completely
  • synonym: rekindle - to make something such as a feeling or interest become active again
  • synonym: revive - to bring something back to life, consciousness, or activity
