wild crab

noun
European wild crab apple tree
7.3
Pronunciation: / waɪld kræb / (AmE), / waɪld krab / (BrE)
a species of wild crab apple native to Europe, regarded as a primary ancestor of cultivated apples

Examples

The wild crab apple
Early botanists identified the wild crab as a key ancestor of today's domestic apples.
In spring, the blossoms of the wild crab attract many pollinators to the hedgerows.
The ancient forest was home to several wild crab trees, their fruit scattered among the leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crab apple - a small sour apple; also the tree that bears it
