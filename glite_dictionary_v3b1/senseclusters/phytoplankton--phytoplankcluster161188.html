phytoplankton

noun
microscopic plant-like plankton
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪtoʊˈplæŋktən / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪtəʊˈplaŋktən / (BrE)
Microscopic photosynthetic organisms, mainly unicellular algae, that drift in water and form the plant component of plankton.

Examples

Phytoplankton populations
Researchers observed a sudden decline in phytoplankton populations due to rising sea temperatures.
The ocean's phytoplankton are responsible for producing much of the world's oxygen.
Tiny phytoplankton bloom every spring, supporting marine food webs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: zooplankton - the animal constituent of plankton
