nucleosynthesis

noun
formation of new atomic nuclei in the cosmos
Pronunciation: / ˌnuː.kli.oʊˈsɪn.θə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌnjuː.klɪ.əʊˈsɪn.θɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
The process by which new atomic nuclei are created from pre-existing nucleons, especially as occurs in stars or during the early universe.

Examples

Nucleosynthesis in stars
Elements heavier than hydrogen were formed through nucleosynthesis during the early universe.
Scientists study nucleosynthesis to understand the origins of different elements in the universe.
Stellar nucleosynthesis is responsible for producing elements like carbon and oxygen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fission - the process of splitting a heavy atomic nucleus into lighter nuclei
  • synonym: fusion - the process of combining lighter atomic nuclei to form a heavier nucleus
