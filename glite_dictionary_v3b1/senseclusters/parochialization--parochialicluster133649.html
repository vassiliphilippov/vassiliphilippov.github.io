parochialization

noun
making more narrow
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˌroʊkiəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌrəʊkiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more limited, narrow, or focused on local interests or concerns

Examples

Parochialization of education
The parochialization of the curriculum reduced students' awareness of global issues.
Critics argue that parochialization can hinder innovation by discouraging outside ideas.
Some communities resist parochialization to maintain openness to diverse perspectives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broadening - the process of making something wider in scope
  • synonym: localization - the process of making something local in character
