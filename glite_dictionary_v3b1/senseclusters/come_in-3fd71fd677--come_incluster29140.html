come in

verb
to enter a place
1.0 99/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm Éªn / (AmE), / kÊŒm Éªn / (BrE)
to enter a place, especially a room or building

Examples

To come in the room

She knocked on the door and then decided to come in when she heard her name.

Synonyms

  • enter
    1.0 107/m
    enter is a neutral, slightly formal word for going inside a building or room, while come in is used when inviting someone.
  • go in
    1.0 52/m
    go in means moving into a room or place in everyday speech, while come in often shows the speaker invites someone inside.
  • go into
    1.0 47/m
    go into means enter an area or a state, often with an object, while come in usually refers to physically entering a room or building.
  • get into
    2.0 15/m
    get into means move inside something or become involved in something, while come in usually means entering a room or building.
  • get in
    1.0 26/m
    get in means enter a vehicle or small space quickly, while come in usually means someone is invited into a room or building.
