ecophobia

noun
fear of nature
9.9
Pronunciation: /iː.kə.ˈfoʊ.bɪ.ə/ /; / /e.kə.ˈfoʊ.bɪ.ə/ (AmE), /iː.kə.ˈfəʊ.bɪ.ə/ /; / /e.kə.ˈfəʊ.bɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a strong fear, aversion, or anxiety about the natural environment or environmental problems such as climate change or ecological destruction

Examples

Ecophobia about nature
Some educators believe ecophobia can prevent children from engaging with nature.
Raising awareness about environmental issues can help reduce ecophobia in communities.
Her ecophobia made her anxious whenever she heard news about climate change.
Many students experience ecophobia when they learn about the rapid loss of biodiversity.
The constant stream of negative environmental news can lead to feelings of ecophobia in some people.
Programs that focus on positive action can help reduce ecophobia among young activists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apathy - lack of interest or concern, especially about important issues
  • synonym: biophobia - fear or aversion to living things or nature
  • synonym: climate anxiety - worry or distress about climate change and its effects
  • synonym: eco-anxiety - chronic fear of environmental doom
  • antonym: ecophilia - love or strong affection for the natural environment
