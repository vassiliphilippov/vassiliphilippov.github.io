mold

noun
rich, loose organic soil
4.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /moʊld/ (AmE), /məʊld/ (BrE)
loose, fertile soil rich in organic matter, often found on the forest floor or in garden beds, suitable for plant growth.

Examples

Rich garden mold
After years of composting, the soil became a fertile mold perfect for seedlings.
Gardeners prize the dark mold that forms under decaying leaves each autumn.
The potted plants thrived in a deep layer of rich mold.
Gardeners value leaf mould for its ability to improve soil structure and fertility.
He spread a layer of dark mould over the vegetable bed before planting seeds.
The plants flourished in the rich mould of the forest floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compost - decayed organic material used as fertilizer
  • synonym: humus - dark organic material in soil formed by decay of plant and animal matter
