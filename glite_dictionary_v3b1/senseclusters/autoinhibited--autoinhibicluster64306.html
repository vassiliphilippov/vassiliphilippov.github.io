autoinhibited

adjective
self-inhibited state
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtoʊɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd / (BrE)
describes a molecule or process that is prevented from acting by its own structure or components

Examples

Autoinhibited protein
The protein remains autoinhibited until it interacts with a specific signaling molecule.
Researchers discovered that the enzyme is autoinhibited by a segment of its own structure.
In its autoinhibited form, the molecule cannot perform its normal biological function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activated - made active or able to function
  • synonym: inhibited - prevented from acting or functioning
