Nietzschean

adjective
of Nietzsche's philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈniːtʃiən / (AmE), / ˈniːtʃiən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the ideas, philosophy, or style of Friedrich Nietzsche

Examples

Nietzschean philosophy
The philosopher's Nietzschean arguments challenged the very foundations of traditional morality.
Her writing style is often described as Nietzschean due to its bold and provocative tone.
Many modern thinkers have been influenced by Nietzschean concepts of individualism and self-overcoming.
The philosopher's Nietzschean outlook influenced his views on morality and society.
Her writing often explores Nietzschean themes such as the will to power and individualism.
Critics described the film as having a distinctly Nietzschean perspective on human nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - following traditional or accepted ideas
  • synonym: existentialist - relating to existentialism or its philosophers
  • synonym: nihilistic - relating to the rejection of all religious and moral principles
