fletch

noun
arrow feather
9.9
Pronunciation: / flɛtʃ / (AmE), / flɛtʃ / (BrE)
one of the feathers or vanes attached near the end of an arrow to help stabilize its flight

Examples

Arrow fletch
The archer carefully glued a new fletch onto his arrow before the competition.
Without a proper fletch, the arrow wobbled and missed the target.
She learned how to repair a damaged fletch during her archery class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feather - a light, flat structure growing from a bird's skin
  • antonym: nock - the notch at the end of an arrow for the bowstring
