placode

noun
embryonic cell patch
Pronunciation: / ˈplækoʊd / (AmE), / ˈplækəʊd / (BrE)
a thickened patch of cells in an embryo that develops into a specific organ or structure

Examples

Lens placode
During development, the lens placode gives rise to the lens of the eye.
Researchers observed the formation of a neural placode in the early stages of the embryo.
Each sensory organ begins as a specialized placode in the embryonic tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: primordium - an organ or tissue in its earliest stage of development
