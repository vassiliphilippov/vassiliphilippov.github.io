trisomic

adjective
with extra chromosome
9.3
Pronunciation: /traɪ.ˈsoʊ.mɪk/ /; / /triː.ˈsoʊ.mɪk/ (AmE), /traɪ.ˈsəʊ.mɪk/ /; / /triː.ˈsəʊ.mɪk/ (BrE)
having an extra chromosome, resulting in three copies of a particular chromosome instead of the usual two

Examples

Trisomic chromosome set
A trisomic cell contains three copies of a specific chromosome instead of the usual two.
Down syndrome is caused by a trisomic condition involving chromosome 21.
Researchers studied the effects of trisomic mutations in laboratory mice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disomic - having two copies of a particular chromosome, the normal number in most organisms
  • antonym: monosomic - having only one copy of a particular chromosome instead of the usual two
