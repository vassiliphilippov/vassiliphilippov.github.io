octahedral

adjective
eight-faced shape
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑktəˈhidrəl / (AmE), / ˌɒktəˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
having the shape or characteristics of an octahedron (a solid with eight faces), often used in geometry, chemistry, or mineralogy

Examples

In chemistry, many metal complexes have an octahedral arrangement of ligands around the central atom.

Scientists observed that the molecule had an octahedral geometry in its crystal structure.
The salt crystals grew into perfect octahedral shapes in the laboratory experiment.
