electrode

noun
conductor for electric current in a circuit
2.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛkˌtroʊd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊd / (BrE)
a conductor through which electricity enters or leaves an object, substance, or region, typically forming part of a circuit

Examples

A electrode in circuit
A battery has a positive electrode called the anode and a negative electrode called the cathode.
Corrosion can occur if the metal electrode is exposed to certain chemicals.
The technician carefully attached the electrode to the patient’s skin to measure heart activity.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anode - the positively charged electrode by which electrons leave a device
  • degree lower: cathode - the negatively charged electrode by which electrons enter a device
