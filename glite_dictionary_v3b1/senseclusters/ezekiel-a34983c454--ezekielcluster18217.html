Ezekiel

proper noun
a Hebrew prophet exiled to Babylon
Pronunciation: / ɪˈziːkiəl / (AmE), / ɪˈziːkiəl / (BrE)
a Hebrew prophet of the 6th century BC, regarded as the author of the Book of Ezekiel in the Old Testament, who was exiled to Babylon.

Examples

Prophet Ezekiel
In art, Ezekiel is often depicted receiving divine revelations.
Many biblical scholars have analyzed the visions of Ezekiel for their symbolic meaning.
