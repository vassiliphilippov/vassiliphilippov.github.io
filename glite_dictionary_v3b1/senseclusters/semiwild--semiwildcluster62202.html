semiwild

adjective
partly wild
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈwaɪld / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈwaɪld / (BrE)
partly wild; not fully domesticated, cultivated, tamed, or controlled

Examples

A semiwild animal
The semiwild dogs lived near the village but still hunted for food.
Some semiwild plants grow in gardens but can also be found in the forest.
The reserve is home to a herd of semiwild ponies that are occasionally fed by rangers.
The garden was filled with semiwild flowers that grew freely but were occasionally trimmed.
After years in captivity, the animals had become semiwild and showed little fear of humans.
Her semiwild hairstyle gave her a carefree and adventurous look.
