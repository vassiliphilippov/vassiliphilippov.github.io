Holy Communion

noun
the Christian rite of receiving the Eucharist
6.3 0.62/m
Pronunciation: / Àåho äli k…ôÀàmjunj…ôn / (AmE), / Àåh…ô äli k…ôÀàmjuÀênj…ôn / (BrE)
the Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper by partaking of bread and wine; also, the act of receiving the Eucharist

Examples

Holy Communion ceremony
Children usually receive their first Holy Communion around the age of seven.
She attended Holy Communion every Sunday morning at her local church.
Visitors are invited to join the congregation in Holy Communion during the service.

Synonyms

  • Eucharist
    9.3 3.75/m
    Eucharist is the formal church name for the consecrated bread and wine in the liturgy, while Holy Communion is the common name for the same rite.
  • Communion
    7.6 4.84/m
    Communion is a general, everyday word for receiving bread and wine at church, while Holy Communion sounds more formal and specific.
  • Lord's Supper
    7.7 0.45/m
    Lord's Supper is a Protestant phrase that highlights the shared meal and remembrance, while Holy Communion is the wider church name for the sacrament.
