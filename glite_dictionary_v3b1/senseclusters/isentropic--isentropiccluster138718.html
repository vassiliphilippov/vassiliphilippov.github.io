isentropic

adjective
at constant entropy (physics/engineering)
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsɛnˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsɛnˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of a process or condition) occurring at constant entropy; involving no change in entropy.

Examples

An isentropic process
Compression in an ideal gas can be approximated as an isentropic process under certain conditions.
In thermodynamics, an isentropic expansion assumes no entropy is exchanged with the surroundings.
The isentropic efficiency of a turbine measures how closely it approaches an ideal, entropy-conserving process.
