oversubscribe

verb
to request too much
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərsəbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəsəbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to apply for or request more of something than is available or can be provided

Examples

Fans oversubscribe tickets
The concert was so popular that fans oversubscribed the ticket sales within minutes.
The new course was oversubscribed, so not everyone who applied could attend.
If too many people oversubscribe to the service, some may be turned away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overbook - to accept more reservations than there is space for
  • antonym: undersubscribe - to apply for less than the available amount
