butterfly

verb
cut and spread open (for cooking)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌt̬ərˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
to cut and spread open (meat, fish, etc.) so that it lies flat, especially before cooking

Examples

To butterfly a chicken
She carefully butterflied the pork chop to make stuffing easier.
The recipe instructs you to butterfly the shrimp before grilling them on skewers.
To cook the chicken evenly, first butterfly the breast by slicing it almost in half and opening it flat.
