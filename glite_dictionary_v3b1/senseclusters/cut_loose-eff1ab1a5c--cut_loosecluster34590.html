cut loose

verb
release from control; behave freely
4.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /kʌt luːs/ (AmE), /kʌt luːs/ (BrE)
to release or set someone or something free from responsibility or restraint; to behave freely and without inhibition, especially for enjoyment

Examples

To cut loose and dance
After the stressful week, she wanted to cut loose and dance all night.
It's important to cut loose and enjoy yourself from time to time.
When his performance dropped, the company decided to cut loose their least productive employee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restrain - to hold back or prevent from acting freely
