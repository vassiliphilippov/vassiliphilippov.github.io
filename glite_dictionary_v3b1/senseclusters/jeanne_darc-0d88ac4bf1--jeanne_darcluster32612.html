Jeanne d'Arc

proper noun
French heroine known as Joan of Arc
8.3
Pronunciation: / ʒɑn dɑrk / (AmE), / ʒan daʁk / (BrE)
A French heroine and military leader (1412–1431) who, inspired by religious visions, led French forces against the English during the Hundred Years' War and was later executed for heresy; also known as Joan of Arc.

Examples

Jeanne d'Arc the heroine
In 1920, Jeanne d'Arc was canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church.
Many books and films have been made about Jeanne d'Arc and her remarkable life.
The bravery of Jeanne d'Arc inspired generations of French people.
