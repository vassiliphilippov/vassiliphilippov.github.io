trub

noun
fermentation sediment
9.9
Pronunciation: / trʌb / (AmE), / trʌb / (BrE)
the sediment or solid material that settles at the bottom of a liquid, especially during brewing or fermentation

Examples

Beer trub
The brewer carefully removed the trub from the bottom of the fermentation vessel.
After filtering, the thick layer of trub was discarded to improve the clarity of the cider.
Winemakers often leave the trub behind to ensure a cleaner final product.
After fermentation, the brewer carefully removed the trub from the bottom of the tank.
Too much trub in the bottle can affect the taste of the finished beer.
Homebrewers often filter out the trub to make their beer clearer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dregs - the remnants of a liquid left in a container, together with any sediment
  • synonym: lees - sediment that settles at the bottom of wine or other liquids
  • synonym: residue - a small amount of something that remains after the main part has gone
