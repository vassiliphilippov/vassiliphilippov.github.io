dovishness

noun
accommodative monetary policy stance
8.6
Pronunciation: / do8av6a83n59s / (AmE), / d598av6a83n59s / (BrE)
(in economics, monetary policy) a preference for accommodative measures—such as low interest rates or asset purchases—intended to support growth and employment rather than prioritizing the fight against inflation.

Examples

Central bank dovishness
Market analysts warned that the committee's dovishness could keep inflation stubbornly low for years.
After the meeting, traders interpreted the governor's speech as clear dovishness and raised bond prices.
Persistent dovishness from central banks reduced volatility and encouraged risky asset purchases worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accommodative monetary policy - a policy approach that keeps interest rates low or uses asset purchases to stimulate economic activity
  • antonym: hawkishness - a preference for tighter monetary policy, higher interest rates, or other measures to reduce inflation
