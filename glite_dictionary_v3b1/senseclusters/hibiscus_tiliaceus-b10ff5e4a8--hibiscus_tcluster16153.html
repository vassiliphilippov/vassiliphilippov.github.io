Hibiscus tiliaceus

noun
tropical shrubby tree used for wood and fiber
Pronunciation: / haɪˈbɪskəs tɪliˈeɪsi.əs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs tɪliˈeɪsɪ.əs / (BrE)
a shrubby tree native to tropical shores, valued for its light wood used in canoe construction and its strong fibers for cordage and caulking; also cultivated as an ornamental plant

Examples

Hibiscus tiliaceus tree
Gardeners often cultivate Hibiscus tiliaceus for its attractive yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves.
Ropes made from the fiber of Hibiscus tiliaceus are prized for their durability in many coastal communities.
The wood of Hibiscus tiliaceus is traditionally crafted into canoe outriggers in Pacific islands.
