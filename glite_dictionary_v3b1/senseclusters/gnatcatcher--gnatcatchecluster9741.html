gnatcatcher

noun
small insect-eating songbird
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnætˌkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈnætˌkætʃə / (BrE)
A very small insect-eating songbird found in North and South America, belonging to the family Polioptilidae.

Examples

A gnatcatcher bird
Several gnatcatchers built their nests near the riverbank this spring.
The blue-gray gnatcatcher is one of the smallest birds in North America.
We spotted a gnatcatcher flitting through the branches in search of insects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its trilling songs
