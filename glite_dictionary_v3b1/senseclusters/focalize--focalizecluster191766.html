focalize

verb
to bring into focus or alignment
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz /; / ˈfoʊ.kə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz /; / ˈfəʊ.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to focus or concentrate something in a particular place, area, or idea; to bring something into clear focus or alignment, or to cause it to converge, either literally (for example, by adjusting a lens or optical instrument) or figuratively (for example, ideas or emotions).

Examples

To focalize emotions
The therapist helped him focalize his scattered emotions after the traumatic event.
We need to focalize our efforts on the key issues if we want to make progress.
With precise adjustments, the engineer managed to focalize the beam of light onto a single point.
Efforts were made to focalise the campaign on areas most affected by the drought.
Scientists observed that the disease tended to focalise in specific regions of the brain.
The doctor explained how the infection would focalise in one organ before spreading.
Her speech focalised the emotions of the crowd into a single purpose.
The meeting helped to focalise the team's efforts on the most pressing issues.
These disparate ideas began to focalise into a coherent strategy over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disperse - to spread widely or move apart
  • synonym: focalize - American spelling for 'focalise'
  • synonym: focus - to direct attention or effort to a particular point
  • synonym: localize - to restrict something to a particular place
