backspace character

noun
control character to move left or delete
6.6 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌspeɪs ˈkærɪktɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌspeɪs ˈkærɪktə / (BrE)
A control character that moves the cursor or printing position one space to the left, typically used to delete the previous character.

Examples

The backspace character key
In programming, the backspace character is often represented as '\b'.
The old typewriters could not interpret the backspace character electronically.
