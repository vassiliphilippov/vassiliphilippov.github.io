obstinance

noun
stubbornness; difficult to persuade
Pronunciation: / ˈɑb.stɪ.nəns /; / ˈɑbstɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stɪ.nəns /; / ˈɒbstɪnəns / (BrE)
the quality of being stubborn and refusing to change your mind or position

Examples

His obstinance in argument
Despite the evidence, his obstinance kept him from admitting he was wrong.
Her obstinance made it nearly impossible to reach a compromise during the negotiation.
The team's obstinance frustrated their coach, who wanted them to try new tactics.
Despite the advice of his friends, Tom’s obstinance prevented him from changing his mind.
His obstinance made it impossible to reach a compromise during the meeting.
She admired his obstinance in standing up for what he believed was right.
