subordination

noun
ranking of debts; junior claim
8.6
Pronunciation: / s59ccb54rdnc8e6a8359n / (AmE), / s59ccb54d0d6ac8ne6a8359n / (BrE)
(in law and finance) the arrangement or condition in which one debt, claim, or lien is ranked as junior to another, determining priority of repayment or enforcement.

Examples

Subordination of a loan
The loan included a subordination clause that placed the mezzanine debt below senior lenders in priority.
Before approving the mortgage, the bank required written subordination from existing creditors to protect the first lien.
Investors examined whether the bond's subordination would reduce recovery prospects after a default.

Related Terms

  • antonym: senior debt - debt that has priority for repayment over junior or subordinated debt
  • synonym: subordination agreement - a legal agreement that specifies which debts or liens are subordinate to others
