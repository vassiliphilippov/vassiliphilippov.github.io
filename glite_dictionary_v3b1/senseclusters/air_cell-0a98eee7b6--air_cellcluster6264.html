air cell

noun
tiny air sac in the lungs
5.3 4.3
Pronunciation: /er sel/ (AmE), /eə sel/ (BrE)
A tiny sac in the lungs that holds air, formed by the terminal dilation of small air passageways.

Examples

Tiny air cells in lungs
Damage to the air cells can significantly impair breathing efficiency.
Doctors observed a reduction in the number of healthy air cells in the patient's lungs.
The oxygen passes from each air cell into the bloodstream during respiration.
