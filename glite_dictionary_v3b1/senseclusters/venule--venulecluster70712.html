venule

noun
a very small vein
9.0 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…õnjuÀål / (AmE), / Ààv…õnjuÀêl / (BrE)
a very small vein that collects blood from capillaries and joins with other venules to form a vein

Examples

Small venule blood vessel
Blood flows from the capillaries into a venule before entering larger veins.
Damage to a venule can lead to minor bleeding and bruising.
The walls of a venule are thin and allow for the exchange of small molecules.

Synonyms

  • venula
    9.9 0.00/m
    venula is used by anatomists in formal descriptions of tiny veins near capillaries, while venule is the general English term.
  • capillary vein
    5.7 0.00/m
    capillary vein describes a vessel directly continuous with capillaries and leading toward larger veins, while venule names the small collecting vein.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: capillary - the smallest blood vessels where exchanges occur between blood and tissues
  • degree higher: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood towards the heart
