ectropion

noun
outward-turned eyelid
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈtroʊpiˌɑn / (AmE), / ɛkˈtrəʊpɪən / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the edge of the eyelid turns outward, exposing the inner surface

Examples

Eyelid ectropion
The doctor explained that ectropion could cause irritation and dryness in the eye.
Surgery is sometimes required to correct severe ectropion in elderly patients.
If left untreated, ectropion may lead to further eye complications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: entropion - a condition where the eyelid turns inward
