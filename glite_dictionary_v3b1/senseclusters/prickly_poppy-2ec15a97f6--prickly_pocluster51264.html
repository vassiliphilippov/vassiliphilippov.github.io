prickly poppy

noun
spiny-flowered poppy plant
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪkli ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
An annual plant of the poppy family, native to the Old World, with orange-red flowers and spiny fruits.

Examples

Old World prickly poppy
Gardeners often avoid touching the prickly poppy because of its bristly seed pods.
In the wild, the prickly poppy grows along dry roadsides and fields.
The prickly poppy has distinctive orange-red flowers and sharp spines on its fruit.
