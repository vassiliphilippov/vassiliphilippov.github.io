commonwealth

noun
a political system ruled by the people
6.7 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêm…ônÀåw…õlŒ∏ / (AmE), / Ààk…ím…ônÀåw…õlŒ∏ / (BrE)
A political system in which the supreme power lies with the citizens, who elect representatives to govern on their behalf; a republic.

Examples

Roman commonwealth
In history class, we learned how the Roman commonwealth gave citizens the power to elect leaders.
Many nations today strive for a commonwealth where the people participate in government.
The philosopher argued that a commonwealth best protects individual rights and freedoms.
