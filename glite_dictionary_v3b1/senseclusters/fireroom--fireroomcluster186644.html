fireroom

noun
ship's furnace compartment (nautical)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌruːm / (BrE)
(nautical) A chamber or compartment in which the furnaces of a ship are stoked or fired.

Examples

The fireroom on ship
He was assigned to the fireroom during his time aboard the old steamship.
Temperatures in the fireroom often soared during long journeys.
The sailors worked tirelessly in the fireroom, keeping the ship's engines running.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boiler room - room in a ship where boilers are housed
