anthrax

noun
infectious disease (affects animals, can infect humans)
Pronunciation: / ˈænˌθræks / (AmE), / ˈænθræks /; / ˈæn.θræks / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease that mainly affects animals like cattle and sheep, but can also infect humans

Examples

The laboratory is equipped to handle dangerous pathogens, including anthrax spores.

Inhalation of anthrax spores can be fatal if not treated promptly.
There was an outbreak of anthrax among the livestock in the region.
