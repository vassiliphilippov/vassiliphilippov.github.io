retrograde

adjective
amnesia affecting pre-trauma memories
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of amnesia) affecting memory for events that occurred immediately before a trauma.

Examples

Retrograde amnesia
After the accident, she suffered from retrograde amnesia and could not recall the hours before the crash.
Doctors explained that retrograde memory loss is common among head trauma victims.
The patient’s retrograde amnesia made it difficult to remember recent conversations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anterograde - amnesia affecting memory for events after trauma
