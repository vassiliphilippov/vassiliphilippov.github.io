toady

noun
sycophantic person; fawner
6.4 9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊdi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdi / (BrE)
A person who behaves obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain favor or advantage.

Examples

A toady at work
He acted like a toady around his boss, agreeing with everything she said.
No one respected him because he was seen as a mere toady to those in power.
The king surrounded himself with toadies who told him only what he wanted to hear.

  • synonym: flatterer - a person who excessively praises someone
