gutted

adjective
with insides removed
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtɪd / (BrE)
having had the internal organs or contents removed, especially from an animal, fish, or building.

Examples

Gutted fish for dinner
The gutted fish were ready to be cooked for dinner.
After the fire, the gutted building stood empty and abandoned.
Workers cleared out the gutted warehouse, leaving only the outer walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hollow - having an empty space inside
  • antonym: intact - remaining complete or whole; not damaged or removed
