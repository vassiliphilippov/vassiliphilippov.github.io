mulligan stew

noun
thick, improvised meat and vegetable stew
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌlɪɡən stu / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪɡən stjuː / (BrE)
A thick stew made of whatever meat and vegetables are available, traditionally associated with hobos or improvised meals.

Examples

A mulligan stew meal
During the Depression, many families survived on mulligan stew shared in community camps.
She learned to prepare a classic mulligan stew from her grandfather's old recipe.
The travelers made a mulligan stew from whatever they could find in their packs.
