prevaricate

verb
avoid the truth; be deliberately vague
Pronunciation: / prɪˈværɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈvarɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to avoid giving a direct or clear answer in order to hide the truth or mislead someone

Examples

To prevaricate in answers
Politicians who prevaricate during interviews often lose the public's trust.
Rather than admit her mistake, she tried to prevaricate and change the subject.
When asked about the missing money, the manager began to prevaricate instead of giving a straight response.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be honest - to tell the truth, not to mislead or deceive
  • synonym: equivocate - to use ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself
  • synonym: lie - to make a false statement with the intention to deceive
