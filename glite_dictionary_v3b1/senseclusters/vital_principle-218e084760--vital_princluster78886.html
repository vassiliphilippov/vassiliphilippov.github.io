vital principle

noun
hypothetical force giving life
9.9 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪt̬l ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtl ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
a hypothetical force believed in some philosophies and systems to govern the functions and qualities unique to living beings

Examples

The vital principle of life
Ancient philosophers proposed a vital principle that distinguished living from nonliving matter.
Some early biologists argued that a vital principle was necessary to explain growth and reproduction.
The theory of a vital principle has largely been abandoned in modern science.

Related Terms

  • synonym: life force - a force or influence believed to give life to living beings
  • synonym: soul - the spiritual or immaterial part of a living being, often considered immortal
