ghosting

noun
faint duplicate image
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈgoʊ.stɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgəʊ.stɪŋ/ (BrE)
a faint duplicate image or shadow that appears on a photograph, screen, or printed page due to reflection or technical issues

Examples

Ghosting on the screen
The old television suffered from ghosting, making the images appear blurry and doubled.
Photographers try to avoid ghosting by using special filters on their lenses.
You can see ghosting on the printed page where the text appears faintly repeated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afterimage - an image that remains after the original has disappeared
  • synonym: artifact - an unwanted visual effect in images or video
