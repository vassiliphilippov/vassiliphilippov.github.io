eutherian

adjective
relating to placental mammals
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / juÀêÀàŒ∏…™ri…ôn / (AmE), / juÀêÀàŒ∏…™…ôri…ôn / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the group of mammals (Eutheria) that give birth to live young who have developed within the uterus

Examples

Eutherian mammals
Humans are classified as eutherian mammals because they give birth to well-developed young.
Many scientists study eutherian evolution to understand the diversity of placental mammals.
The eutherian lineage is distinguished from marsupials and monotremes by its complex placenta.

Synonyms

  • placental
    7.0 0.52/m
    placental refers to animals that develop a placenta during pregnancy, while eutherian names the mammal group whose young develop inside the uterus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: marsupial - mammal of the order Marsupialia, such as kangaroos and koalas
  • antonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal, such as the platypus or echidna
