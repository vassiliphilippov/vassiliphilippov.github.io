dust

noun
fine dry particles; powdery matter
Pronunciation: / dʌst / (AmE), / dʌst / (BrE)
fine, dry powder or tiny particles, such as dirt, that collect on surfaces or float in the air

Examples

A layer of dust
A thin layer of dust covered the shelves after weeks of neglect.
She wiped the dust from her desk before starting work.
The sunlight streaming through the window revealed floating dust particles.
After the ancient building collapsed, all that remained was dust and rubble.
Centuries had passed, and the castle had crumbled into dust.
When the meteorite hit, the village was reduced to dust.
A cloud of dust rose as the car sped down the dirt road.
After a week without cleaning, the shelves were thick with dust.
Pollen dust can cause allergies in the spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ashes - the remains of something that has been burned
  • synonym: debris - the scattered remains of something broken or destroyed
  • synonym: dirt - earth or soil, or anything unclean
  • synonym: powder - a dry substance composed of fine particles
