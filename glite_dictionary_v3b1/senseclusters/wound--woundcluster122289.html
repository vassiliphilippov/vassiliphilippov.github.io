wound

noun
the act of causing bodily injury
2.2 30/m
Pronunciation: / wuÀênd /; / wund / (AmE), / wuÀênd / (BrE)
an injury to the body, especially one where the skin or flesh is cut, torn, or damaged

Examples

A deep wound
Her words felt like a deep wound to his pride, more than any physical harm.
The prosecution argued that the wound was inflicted intentionally during the altercation.
The soldier's bravery was recognized despite the wound he suffered in battle.
He was awarded a medal for bravery after receiving a wound while protecting his fellow soldiers.
Many veterans bore the scars of old wounds sustained during battle.
The soldier suffered a serious wound in combat but survived thanks to prompt medical attention.
Clean the wound thoroughly to prevent infection.
Her wound required stitches at the hospital.
The soldier had a deep wound on his arm after the battle.

Synonyms

  • cut
    1.0 15/m
    a cut is a shallow or deep slice made by something sharp, while a wound is any injury where skin or flesh is damaged.
  • injury
    1.4 78/m
    an injury is harm to the body from accidents or attacks, while a wound is a specific cut or tearing of the skin or flesh.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bruise - an injury appearing as a discolored area on the skin
  • synonym: cut - an opening or incision made by something sharp
  • antonym: heal - To recover from a wound or injury
  • synonym: injury - the act of being injured
