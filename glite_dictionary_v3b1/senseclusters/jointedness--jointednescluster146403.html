jointedness

noun
state of having joints
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔɪntɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪntɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having joints or being made up of connected parts that can move relative to each other

Examples

The jointedness of legs
The jointedness of the insect's legs allows it to move quickly and efficiently.
Engineers studied the jointedness of the machine to improve its range of motion.
The jointedness in the puppet's arms makes its movements appear more lifelike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: articulation - the state of being jointed or connected
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not flexible
