German shepherd

noun
large breed of working dog
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɜrmən ˈʃɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmən ˈʃɛpəd / (BrE)
a large and intelligent breed of dog, often used in police work and as a guide for the blind

Examples

A German shepherd dog
Her German shepherd is both a loyal companion and an excellent guard dog.
Many German shepherds are chosen as guide dogs for visually impaired people.
The police dog unit uses a trained German shepherd for search and rescue operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Alsatian - alternative name for the German shepherd, especially in Britain
