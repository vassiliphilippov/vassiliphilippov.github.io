rebend

noun
additional bend
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbɛnd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbɛnd / (BrE)
an additional bend or curve made in something, especially metal, wire, or pipe, after it has already been bent

Examples

A rebend in metal
The engineer detected a rebend in the metal rod that affected its alignment.
A small rebend in the wire caused the circuit to malfunction.
After the initial shaping, a rebend was needed to fit the pipe properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bend - a curve or angle formed in something that is not straight
  • antonym: straighten - to make something straight again
