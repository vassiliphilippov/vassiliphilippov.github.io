bursary

noun
student financial award
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːsəri / (AmE), / ˈbɜːsəri / (BrE)
a sum of money given to a student to help pay for their education (a type of scholarship or grant)

Examples

A bursary for students
She applied for a bursary to help cover her university tuition fees.
The college offers a bursary program for students from low-income families.
Winning the bursary allowed him to focus on his studies without worrying about finances.
She was able to attend university thanks to a generous bursary from the college.
Applicants must demonstrate financial need to qualify for the bursary program.
The school offers a bursary to help students from low-income families cover tuition costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grant - money given by an organization for a specific purpose, often education
  • antonym: loan - money borrowed for education that must be repaid
  • synonym: scholarship - a financial award given to a student for academic achievement
