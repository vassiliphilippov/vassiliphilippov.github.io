restorationist

noun
advocate of early Christianity
9.1
Pronunciation: /re.stə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ /; / /rə.stə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /re.stə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ /; / /rə.stə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports or belongs to a religious movement aiming to restore the original beliefs and practices of early Christianity

Examples

Christian restorationist
The restorationist argued that modern churches should follow the teachings of the early apostles.
Many restorationists believe in returning to simple forms of worship and church organization.
As a restorationist, she studied the practices of the first-century Christian communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reformer - a person who works to change and improve a system or institution
  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports maintaining established customs and beliefs
