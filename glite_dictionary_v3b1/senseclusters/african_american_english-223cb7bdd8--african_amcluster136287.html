African American English

noun
dialect of American English spoken by African Americans

Pronunciation: /ˈæf.rɪ.kən ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən ˈɪŋ.ɡlɪʃ/ /; / /ˈæf.rɪ.kɑːn ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən ˈɪŋ.ɡlɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈæf.rɪ.kən ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən ˈɪŋ.ɡlɪʃ/ (BrE)
A variety of American English characteristically spoken by many African Americans in the United States, with its own distinct linguistic features.

Examples

African American English variety
Linguists have studied African American English to better understand its grammar and history.
Many features of African American English differ from Standard American English, especially in pronunciation and syntax.
Teachers are encouraged to recognize African American English as a legitimate variety of English in the classroom.
