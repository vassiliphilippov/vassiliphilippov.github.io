oxidizing agent

noun
substance that causes oxidation in chemistry
7.6 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːk.sə.daɪ.zɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈɑːk.sɪ.daɪ.zɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ (AmE), /ˈɒk.sɪ.daɪ.zɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ /; / /ˈɒk.sə.daɪ.zɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ (BrE)
a substance that causes another substance to lose electrons, typically by accepting electrons itself during a chemical reaction

Examples

A common oxidizing agent
Hydrogen peroxide is a common oxidizing agent used for disinfection and bleaching.
In the reaction, the chlorine molecule acts as the oxidizing agent.
Many explosives contain an oxidizing agent that supplies oxygen for rapid combustion.
