Hebrews

proper noun
book of the New Testament, addressed to Jewish Christians
Pronunciation: / ˈhiː.bruːz / (AmE), / ˈhiː.bruːz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, traditionally attributed to Saint Paul, but now generally regarded as of unknown authorship, addressed to Jewish Christians

Examples

The book of Hebrews
During our Bible study, we focused on the opening verses of Hebrews.
Many scholars debate who actually wrote Hebrews, as its style differs from Paul's other letters.
The theme of Hebrews emphasizes the superiority of Christ over previous religious traditions.
