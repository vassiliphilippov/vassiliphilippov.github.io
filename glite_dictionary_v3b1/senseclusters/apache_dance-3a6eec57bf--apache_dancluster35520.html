apache dance

noun
a dramatic French vaudeville dance
Pronunciation: / əˈpætʃi dæns / (AmE), / əˈpæʃi dɑːns / (BrE)
A violent, fast-paced dance originating in early 20th-century French vaudeville, characterized by dramatic movements that mimic a struggle, often between a man and a woman.

Examples

The apache dance performance
In Parisian cabarets, the apache dance became a symbol of rebellious youth culture.
She studied the intricate steps required for the apache dance as part of her dance training.
The performers captivated the audience with a passionate apache dance on stage.
