coccidiosis

noun
intestinal disease caused by coccidia
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːksɪdiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒksɪdiˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation with coccidia, typically affecting the intestines of animals such as poultry, cattle, and rabbits.

Examples

Poultry coccidiosis disease
Poultry farmers routinely vaccinate chicks against coccidiosis to prevent severe outbreaks.
Symptoms of coccidiosis include diarrhea and weight loss in young animals.
Veterinarians recommend regular monitoring to reduce the risk of coccidiosis in livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coccidia - microscopic, spore-forming, single-celled parasites that cause coccidiosis
