take a leaf out of someone's book

verb
imitate someone's example or behavior
8.2
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə liːf ˈaʊt ʌv ˈsʌmwʌnz bʊk / (AmE), / teɪk ə liːf ˈaʊt ɒv ˈsʌmwʌnz bʊk / (BrE)
to imitate someone's actions, behavior, or methods, usually to achieve similar success or results

Examples

To take a leaf out of someone's book
After watching the team leader handle criticism gracefully, I decided to take a leaf out of his book.
If you want to improve your study habits, you could take a leaf out of her book and make a schedule.
We could all take a leaf out of their book when it comes to customer service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: follow someone's example - do what someone else has done, especially successfully
  • antonym: set an example - act as a role model for others to imitate
