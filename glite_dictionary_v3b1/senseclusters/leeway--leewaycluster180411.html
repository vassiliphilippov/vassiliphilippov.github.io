leeway

noun
sideways drift off course (ship/plane)
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈliˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈliːweɪ / (BrE)
The sideways drift of a ship or aircraft away from its intended course, especially due to wind or currents.

Examples

Leeway in sailing
Careful navigation is required to account for leeway in rough seas.
Strong winds caused the ship to develop significant leeway during the storm.
The pilot compensated for leeway to stay on the correct flight path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - gradual movement away from course or position
