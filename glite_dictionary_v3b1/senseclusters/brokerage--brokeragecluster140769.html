brokerage

noun
the business or services of a broker
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkərɪdʒ /; / ˈbroʊ.kɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkərɪdʒ /; / ˈbrəʊ.kər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a company, business, or place that arranges buying and selling transactions between people, usually for a fee or commission

Examples

A brokerage firm

Online brokerage services have made investing more accessible to individuals.
The brokerage earned a substantial fee for facilitating the merger.
After graduating, he found a job at a large real estate brokerage.
She visited the brokerage downtown to discuss her investment options.
The new brokerage offers both stock and insurance services.
Many investors compare brokerage fees before choosing where to trade.
She opened an account with a reputable brokerage to start investing in stocks.
The brokerage earns a commission for every transaction made on its platform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agency - the business or function of acting on another's behalf, often for a fee
  • synonym: broker - a person or firm that arranges transactions between a buyer and a seller
  • synonym: broker's office - an office where a broker works
  • synonym: commission - a fee paid for services, especially to a broker or agent
