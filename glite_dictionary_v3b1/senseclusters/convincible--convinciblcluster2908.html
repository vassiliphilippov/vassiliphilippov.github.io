convincible

adjective
easily persuaded; susceptible to persuasion
1.7
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɪnsəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɪnsəbəl / (BrE)
Easily persuaded; capable of being convinced or influenced by argument or evidence.

Examples

A convincible audience
A convincible audience is crucial for a speaker wishing to inspire change.
Children are often more convincible than adults, especially when presented with clear evidence.
The jury was convincible, responding readily to the lawyer's arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inconvincible - not able to be convinced
  • synonym: persuadable - easily influenced or convinced
