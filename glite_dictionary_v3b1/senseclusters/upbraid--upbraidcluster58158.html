upbraid

verb
to scold or criticize severely
8.8 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒpËˆbreÉªd / (AmE), / ÊŒpËˆbreÉªd / (BrE)
to criticize or scold someone severely for their actions or behavior

Examples

To upbraid an employee
He was upbraided by his boss for missing the deadline.
She upbraided herself for making such a careless mistake.
The teacher upbraided the student for not completing his homework on time.

Synonyms

  • chastise
    7.6 2.94/m
    chastise often implies correcting someone to improve behavior, while upbraid focuses on formal, severe verbal criticism.
  • scold
    2.2 4.82/m
    scold is a common, everyday rebuke spoken in anger, while upbraid is more formal and serious.
  • take to task
    6.3 1.25/m
    take to task means to criticize or reprimand someone directly, while upbraid is more formal and often stronger.
  • castigate
    8.8 1.36/m
    castigate means to punish or criticize very harshly, while upbraid mainly means to scold someone severely.
  • excoriate
    9.8 0.75/m
    excoriate means to attack someone publicly with very strong criticism, while upbraid is a private, formal scolding.
  • lambaste
    8.0 0.09/m
    lambaste means to criticize someone fiercely, often in public and informally, while upbraid is a formal, direct scolding.
