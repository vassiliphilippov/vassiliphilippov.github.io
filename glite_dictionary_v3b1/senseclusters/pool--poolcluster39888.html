pool

noun
business association for joint purpose
3.7
Pronunciation: / puːl /; / pul / (AmE), / puːl / (BrE)
an association, group, or sum formed by sharing resources, people, or money for a common purpose

Examples

A pool of workers
Historically, railroad companies established a pool to divide markets and reduce fare wars.
Several oil companies formed a pool to regulate production and stabilize prices.
The new trade agreement prohibits the creation of any pool that would limit free competition.
She worked in the pool of administrative assistants, supporting several managers.
The researchers formed a pool of volunteers to help with the study.
When the company bought new cars, employees could sign up for the pool to share them.
At the office holiday party, there was a pool for guessing the number of candies in the jar.
Each team member added five dollars to the pool before the start of the tournament.
The neighbors set up a pool to help cover the unexpected maintenance costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartel - an association of manufacturers or suppliers that maintains prices at a high level and restricts competition
  • synonym: collective - a group acting as a whole, especially for sharing resources
  • synonym: fund - an amount of money saved or made available for a particular purpose
  • antonym: individual - a single person or thing, considered separately from a group
  • synonym: kitty - a fund of money for communal use, especially in games
