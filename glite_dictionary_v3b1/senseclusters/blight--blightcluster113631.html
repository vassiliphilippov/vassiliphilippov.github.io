blight

verb
to cause harm or ruin; damage growth
6.7
Pronunciation: / blaɪt / (AmE), / blaɪt / (BrE)
to cause to suffer from blight, especially by damaging growth or ruining prospects for success

Examples

To blight hopes
A sudden frost can blight the crops overnight, leaving farmers with huge losses.
Harsh criticism can sometimes blight a young musician's enthusiasm and confidence.
The economic crisis threatened to blight the progress the city had made in recent years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ruin - to spoil or destroy completely
  • synonym: wither - to become dry and shriveled
