life-support system

noun
machine aiding vital bodily functions
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪf səˌpɔrt ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf səˌpɔːt ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
equipment that keeps a person alive by providing or replacing vital functions, especially in medical, space, or underwater situations

Examples

A life-support system machine
After the accident, she was kept alive by a life-support system in the intensive care unit.
Doctors debated whether to continue treatment with the life-support system.
New advancements in life-support systems have improved survival rates for critically ill patients.
Astronauts depend on their life-support systems to survive in the vacuum of space.
Submarines are fitted with life-support systems to sustain the crew during long underwater missions.
The intensive care unit is equipped with advanced life-support systems for critically ill patients.
