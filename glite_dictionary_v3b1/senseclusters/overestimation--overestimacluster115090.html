overestimation

noun
an estimate that is too high
1.8 4.6
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.e.stɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ə.stɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.e.stɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /əʊ.və.ə.stɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or result of judging something to be greater or more important than it really is

Examples

An overestimation of demand
His overestimation of demand led to surplus inventory.
The project's budget suffered because of an overestimation of potential profits.
There was a clear overestimation of the team's abilities before the season began.
His overestimation of the project's income led to budgeting problems.
It's easy to make an overestimation of your abilities when you're feeling confident.
The scientist admitted that the results were skewed by an overestimation of participants.

  • antonym: underestimation - the act or result of estimating something as less than it actually is
