rally

verb
to recover after a decline
Pronunciation: / ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
to recover, improve, or come together for support or action after a decline or difficulty

Examples

To rally after illness
After a brief illness, he began to rally and regain his strength.
The company's shares rallied after the announcement of a new product.
The team managed to rally in the second half and win the game.
After a discouraging loss, the coach helped the players rally for the next game.
Despite setbacks, the team managed to rally their energy and push forward.
She tried to rally the community to support the new project.
Desperate, she rallied the defenders for a final stand at the fortress.
Leaders tried to rally their forces after the surprise attack.
The commander quickly rallied his troops to defend the encampment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assemble - to bring together (people) in one place for a common purpose
  • antonym: deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • antonym: disperse - to scatter or break apart, the opposite of gathering together
  • synonym: muster - gather or bring together (especially people or resources)
  • synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
