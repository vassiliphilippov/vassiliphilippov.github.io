literal

adjective
exactly true; not exaggerated
2.5 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪt.ər.əl/ /; / /ˈlɪt.rəl/ (AmE), /ˈlɪt.ər.əl/ /; / /ˈlɪt.rəl/ (BrE)
exact, true, or not figurative; using words in their most basic or usual sense, without exaggeration or metaphor

Examples

Literal meaning
His account was the literal version of what happened, unembellished and direct.
The judge asked for the literal facts, without any exaggeration.
When she said she was starving, she didn't mean it was the literal truth.
Her account of the accident was a literal description of what happened.
The reporter gave a literal translation of the president's statement.
This passage should be understood in its literal sense, not as a metaphor.
He experienced a literal solitude during his months in the remote cabin.
Her literal motive for leaving was concern for her family, not ambition.
The landscape offered a literal beauty rarely seen elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actual - existing in fact; real and not imagined
  • synonym: exact - accurate, not approximate or metaphorical
  • antonym: figurative - not literal; using metaphorical or symbolic meaning
  • synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
