recompression

noun
compressing again
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of compressing digital data, audio, or video again to reduce size or change quality after editing or processing

Examples

Video recompression
The software performs recompression to make large video files easier to share online.
Repeated recompression can lower the quality of an audio track.
After making edits, he used recompression to save storage space on his device.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compression - the process of reducing the size of data files
  • antonym: decompression - the process of restoring compressed data to its original form
