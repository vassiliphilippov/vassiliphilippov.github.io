unmodern

adjective
old-fashioned
6.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmɑdərn / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɒdən / (BrE)
not modern; old-fashioned or not in keeping with current styles or ideas

Examples

An unmodern style
The kitchen looked unmodern compared to the rest of the renovated house.
Her taste in music is quite unmodern, preferring records from the 1960s.
Some people appreciate the charm of unmodern architecture in historic towns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • synonym: old-fashioned - not in style or fashion; typical of the past
  • synonym: outdated - no longer useful or fashionable
