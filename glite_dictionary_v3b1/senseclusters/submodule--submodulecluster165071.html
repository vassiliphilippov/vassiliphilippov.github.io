submodule

noun
module subset in algebra
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌmɑːdjuːl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌmɒdjuːl / (BrE)
in abstract algebra, a subset of a module that is itself a module over the same ring, closed under addition and scalar multiplication

Examples

Algebraic submodule
The set of all even integers forms a submodule of the module of all integers.
In the lecture, the professor explained how to identify a submodule within a given module.
Every submodule must be closed under both addition and scalar multiplication.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: module - an algebraic structure generalizing vector spaces over a ring
  • synonym: subgroup - a subset of a group that is itself a group under the same operation
