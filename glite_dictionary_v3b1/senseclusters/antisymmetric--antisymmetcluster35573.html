antisymmetric

adjective
mathematical relation property
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
describing a relation in which if one element is related to another, the reverse holds only if the elements are equal

Examples

Antisymmetric relation rule
In mathematics, the 'less than or equal to' relation is antisymmetric on the set of real numbers.
A relation is antisymmetric if whenever a is related to b and b to a, then a and b must be the same.
The teacher explained that the subset relation is antisymmetric for all sets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: symmetric - having a relation that holds in both directions between elements
