mope

verb
move around slowly and aimlessly
Pronunciation: / moʊp / (AmE), / məʊp / (BrE)
to move or behave in a sad, bored, or unenthusiastic way, often showing little energy

Examples

To mope around
After hearing the bad news, she just moped in her room all afternoon, barely saying a word.
He would often mope around the house with nothing to do on rainy days.
The children started to mope when their plans for the park were cancelled.
He spent the whole weekend moping around the house after his team lost.
She moped for days when her best friend moved away.
There's no point in moping; let's try to solve the problem instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brood - to think about something that makes one unhappy, angry, or worried over a long period
  • antonym: cheer up - to become happier or less sad
  • synonym: sulk - to be silent, morose, and bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment
  • synonym: wander - to move about without a fixed course, aim, or goal
