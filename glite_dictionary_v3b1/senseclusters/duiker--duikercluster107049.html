duiker

noun
African antelope
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪkər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪkə / (BrE)
a small or medium-sized African antelope known for its shy and elusive behavior, often found in forests or bushland

Examples

Forest duiker
We spotted a shy duiker darting through the undergrowth during our safari.
The duiker relies on its camouflage and quick movements to avoid predators.
Researchers observed several duikers feeding quietly at the edge of the forest.
The duiker quickly disappeared into the dense forest when it sensed danger.
Researchers observed a rare blue duiker feeding on fallen fruit in the reserve.
Many species of duiker are threatened by habitat loss and hunting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antelope - a grazing mammal with slender legs and horns, found in Africa and Asia
  • synonym: gazelle - a small, graceful antelope found in Africa and Asia
