desaturation

noun
removal of hydrogen
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which hydrogen atoms are removed from a molecule, resulting in the formation of double bonds

Examples

Chemical desaturation process
The desaturation of the compound was confirmed by the appearance of a double bond in its structure.
During the experiment, desaturation changed the properties of the fatty acid.
Enzymes can catalyze the desaturation of certain organic molecules in living organisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrogenation - a chemical reaction that adds hydrogen atoms to a molecule
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical process involving the loss of electrons or increase in oxidation state
