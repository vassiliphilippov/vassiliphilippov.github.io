relationism

noun
focus on relationships
Pronunciation: / rɪˈleɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
a theory or approach that explains social phenomena mainly through relationships, networks, or interactions rather than individual characteristics

Examples

Relationism in society
Many sociologists use relationism to analyze how people interact within communities.
Her research paper argued that relationism offers a better understanding of social networks.
The debate between relationism and individualism is central in social theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individualism - the belief that individual characteristics are most important in explaining social phenomena
  • synonym: network theory - the study of how relationships and connections shape social structures
