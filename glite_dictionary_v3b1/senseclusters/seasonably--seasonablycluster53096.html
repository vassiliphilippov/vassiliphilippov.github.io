seasonably

adverb
appropriately for the season
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːzənəbli / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəbli / (BrE)
in a way that is appropriate for or typical of the season; as expected for the time of year

Examples

Seasonably fresh fruit
Apples are seasonably available in the fall, when they are harvested fresh from orchards.
The chef changes the menu seasonably to incorporate the freshest ingredients each month.
The weather was seasonably warm for late spring, making it perfect for an outdoor picnic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unseasonably - not typical for the season; out of season
