paraformaldehyde

noun
chemical tissue preservative
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəfɔːrˈmældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpærəfɔːˈmældɪˌhaɪd / (BrE)
a white, solid chemical made from formaldehyde, used in laboratories and industry to preserve tissues or as a source of formaldehyde

Examples

White paraformaldehyde powder
The laboratory technician used paraformaldehyde to fix the tissue samples before analysis.
Proper ventilation is important when working with paraformaldehyde due to its chemical fumes.
Researchers prepared a solution of paraformaldehyde for the experiment on cell preservation.
The laboratory used paraformaldehyde to fix tissue samples for microscopic examination.
Workers must wear protective equipment when handling paraformaldehyde due to its potential health risks.
Heating paraformaldehyde releases formaldehyde gas needed for certain chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formaldehyde - a colorless, strong-smelling gas used in chemical manufacturing and preservation
  • synonym: glutaraldehyde - a chemical compound used to disinfect and preserve biological tissues
  • synonym: resin - a solid or semi-solid organic substance used in plastics and adhesives
