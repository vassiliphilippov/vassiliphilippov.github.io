naw

verb
to chew persistently
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɔ / (AmE), / nɔː / (BrE)
to bite, chew, or wear away something persistently, especially in a dialectal or nonstandard form of 'gnaw'.

Examples

To naw a bone
The puppy began to naw the old slipper steadily, leaving bits of fabric and foam everywhere.
He watched the mouse naw at the piece of cheese in the corner of the room.
Kids sometimes naw on pencils when they are nervous or bored in class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gnaw - to bite or chew something repeatedly
  • degree lower: nibble - to take small bites repeatedly
