pushrod

noun
engine motion rod
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpʊ.ʃrɑːd/ /; / /ˈpʊʃ.rɑːd/ (AmE), /ˈpʊ.ʃrɒd/ /; / /ˈpʊʃ.rɒd/ (BrE)
a metal rod in a machine, especially an engine, that transmits motion from one part to another

Examples

Engine pushrod
The mechanic discovered that the pushrod was bent and needed to be replaced.
A broken pushrod can cause serious engine problems if not fixed quickly.
In older car engines, the pushrod system is commonly used to operate the valves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camshaft - a rotating shaft in an engine that operates the valves
  • synonym: valve - a device in an engine that controls the flow of fuel or air
