visual impairment

noun
loss or reduction of vision
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl ɪmˈpɛr.mənt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒu.əl ɪmˈpeə.mənt / (BrE)
a condition in which a person has partial or total loss of vision, affecting their ability to see clearly or at all

Examples

Visual impairment condition
New technologies help people with visual impairment navigate city streets more safely.
Regular eye exams can help detect early signs of visual impairment.
The school provides resources for students with visual impairment.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blindness - complete lack of vision
  • antonym: sightedness - having normal vision
