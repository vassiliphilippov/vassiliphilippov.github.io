sone

noun
unit of loudness
Pronunciation: / soʊn / (AmE), / səʊn / (BrE)
a unit for measuring perceived loudness, equal to the loudness of a 1000-hertz tone at 40 decibels above the threshold of hearing

Examples

Two sones loudness
An air conditioner producing 2 sones is considered very quiet for home use.
Manufacturers often rate fans by sone levels to indicate how quiet they are during operation.
The noise in the lab reached 16 sones, which is extremely loud for most people.
