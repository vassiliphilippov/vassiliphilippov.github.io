understatement

noun
the act of downplaying or minimizing
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of saying something in a way that makes it seem less important, serious, or bad than it really is, often for ironic effect

Examples

An understatement of fact
Calling the marathon "challenging" would be quite an understatement.
Describing the film as 'interesting' is such an understatement of how good it really is.
To say the storm was 'a bit of rain' is an understatement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overstatement - the act of stating something too strongly or exaggerating
