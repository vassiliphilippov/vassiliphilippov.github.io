mansplain

verb
to explain condescendingly
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌspleɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænˌspleɪn / (BrE)
to explain something to someone, typically a man to a woman, in a condescending or patronizing manner, often disregarding the listener's knowledge or expertise

Examples

To mansplain a topic
She was frustrated when her colleague started mansplaining her own research findings to her.
During the meeting, he tried to mansplain basic concepts to the experienced team.
Many people dislike being mansplained to, especially when they are already experts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condescend - to behave as if you are more important or intelligent than others
  • antonym: listen - to pay attention to what someone is saying
  • synonym: patronize - to treat someone as if they are less intelligent or important
