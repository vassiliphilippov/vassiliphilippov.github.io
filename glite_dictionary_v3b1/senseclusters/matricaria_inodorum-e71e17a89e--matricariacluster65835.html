Matricaria inodorum

noun
annual European weed with white flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmætrɪˈkɛəriə ɪnˈɑdərəm / (AmE), / ˌmatrɪˈkeərɪə ɪnˈɒdərəm / (BrE)
A widespread annual plant native to Europe, with finely divided leaves and white flowers, often considered a weed and sometimes cultivated; also found in eastern North America.

Examples

Matricaria inodorum plant
Matricaria inodorum often invades disturbed soils and roadsides across much of Europe and North America.
Although called scentless mayweed, Matricaria inodorum sometimes has a faint fragrance when crushed.
Farmers frequently identify Matricaria inodorum as a troublesome weed in their fields.
