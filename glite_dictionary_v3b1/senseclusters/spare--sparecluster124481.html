spare

adjective
lacking in quantity, meager
Pronunciation: / spɛr / (AmE), / spɛː /; / spɛə / (BrE)
simple, plain, or minimal; lacking in quantity, decoration, or excess

Examples

Spare room
During the war, families survived on a spare diet of bread and broth.
The apartment was furnished in a spare style, with only a bed and a table.
Their savings were becoming dangerously spare after months of unemployment.
Despite his age, the athlete maintained a spare and energetic appearance.
Her spare frame was the result of years of disciplined running.
In person, he appeared tall and spare, with sharp features.
Critics praised the novel for its spare prose and emotional restraint.
Her living room is beautifully spare, with only essential furniture and no clutter.
The artist preferred a spare style, focusing on shape rather than color.

  • synonym: scant - barely sufficient in amount or quantity
  • synonym: slender - gracefully thin, especially of a person or part of the body
