fish-liver oil

noun
oil from fish livers, used as supplement
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃ ˌlɪvər ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃ ˌlɪvə ɔɪl / (BrE)
a fatty oil extracted from the livers of certain fish, used as a dietary supplement, especially for its vitamin A and D content

Examples

A bottle of fish-liver oil
Doctors sometimes recommend fish-liver oil to improve vitamin D intake.
Many supplements on the shelf include fish-liver oil as a primary ingredient.
My grandmother used to give us a spoonful of fish-liver oil every morning during winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cod-liver oil - oil obtained from cod livers, rich in vitamins A and D
