glottal stop

noun
speech sound made by closing the glottis
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈglɑː.təl stɑːp/ (AmE), /ˈglɒt.əl stɒp/ (BrE)
a speech sound produced by briefly blocking and then releasing airflow at the glottis, as in the sudden onset of a vowel or the break in 'uh-oh'

Examples

The glottal stop sound
In some British accents, the glottal stop replaces the 't' sound in 'bottle'.
Linguists use the symbol ʔ to represent the glottal stop in phonetic transcription.
The Hawaiian word ‘ohana’ begins with a glottal stop.
