psychoanalytical

adjective
relating to psychoanalysis
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊˌænəˈlɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌænəˈlɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or incorporating the theories and methods of psychoanalysis, especially those developed by Sigmund Freud.

Examples

Psychoanalytical theory
His psychoanalytical perspective was shaped by years of studying Freud's original works.
Many literary critics employ psychoanalytical methods when interpreting classic novels.
The therapist used a psychoanalytical approach to understand her patient's dreams.
