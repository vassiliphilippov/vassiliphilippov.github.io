Kartvelian

noun
language family of Georgia
Pronunciation: / kɑrtˈvɛliən / (AmE), / kɑːtˈvɛliən / (BrE)
the family of related languages spoken mainly in Georgia and the southern Caucasus, including Georgian, Mingrelian, Laz, and Svan

Examples

The Kartvelian family
The Kartvelian languages are unique to the Caucasus region and have no known relatives outside it.
Georgian is the most widely spoken Kartvelian language, with millions of speakers.
Linguists compare Kartvelian with other language families to study its origins and development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Georgian - the official language of Georgia, part of the Kartvelian family
  • antonym: Indo-European - a major language family of Europe and Asia, distinct from Kartvelian
