high vowel

noun
phonetic term for tongue-high vowel sound
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈvoʊəl / (AmE), / haɪ ˈvəʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel sound articulated with the tongue positioned high in the mouth, near the roof

Examples

A high vowel sound
In contrast to a high vowel, a low vowel requires the tongue to be much lower.
Linguists use the term high vowel to describe sounds made with the tongue near the roof of the mouth.
The vowel in 'beet' is considered a high vowel in English phonetics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: close vowel - a vowel sound made with the tongue close to the roof of the mouth (synonym of 'high vowel')
  • antonym: low vowel - a vowel sound made with the tongue positioned low in the mouth (opposite of 'high vowel')
