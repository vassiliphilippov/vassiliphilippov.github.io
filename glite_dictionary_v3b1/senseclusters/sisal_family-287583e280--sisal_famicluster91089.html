sisal family

noun
a family of tropical xerophytic plants (Agavaceae)
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.səl ˈfæm.ə.li/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.səl ˈfæm.liː/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.səl ˈfæm.ɪ.li/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.səl ˈfæm.liː/ (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical and xerophytic plants, including species such as the agave, and sometimes containing plants classified under Dracaenaceae, Amaryllidaceae, or Liliaceae.

Examples

The sisal family plants
Botanists sometimes classify the sisal family under different broader families depending on the system used.
Members of the sisal family are often cultivated for their strong fibers used in rope and twine.
The sisal family includes a variety of plants adapted to arid climates, such as agaves.
