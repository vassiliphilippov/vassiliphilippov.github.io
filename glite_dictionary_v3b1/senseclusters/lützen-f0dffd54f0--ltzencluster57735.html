Lützen

proper noun
battle of the Thirty Years' War, 1632
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊt.sən / (AmE), / ˈlʏt.sən / (BrE)
a decisive battle in the Thirty Years' War (1632) where Swedish forces led by Gustavus Adolphus defeated the Holy Roman Empire under Wallenstein, resulting in the death of Gustavus Adolphus

Examples

Battle of Lützen
Gustavus Adolphus lost his life during the clash at Lützen in 1632.
Historians continue to study the strategies used at Lützen between Swedish and Imperial forces.
The Battle of Lützen played a crucial role in the outcome of the Thirty Years' War.
