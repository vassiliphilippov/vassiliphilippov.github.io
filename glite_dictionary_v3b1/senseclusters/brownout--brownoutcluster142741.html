brownout

noun
temporary power reduction
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊnˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary drop in voltage in an electrical power supply, causing lights to dim or equipment to malfunction

Examples

Power brownout
During the summer heatwave, the city experienced a brownout that lasted for several hours.
Sensitive equipment can be damaged if a brownout occurs unexpectedly.
The lights flickered and dimmed because of a brownout in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: blackout - a complete loss of electrical power
  • synonym: power outage - an interruption in the supply of electricity
