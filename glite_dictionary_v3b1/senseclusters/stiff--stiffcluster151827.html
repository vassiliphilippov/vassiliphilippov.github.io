stiff

adjective
resolute or unwavering in attitude
1.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /stɪf/ (AmE), /stɪf/ (BrE)
rigidly formal, lacking natural ease, or showing firm determination

Examples

Stiff attitude
Even under pressure, her stiff convictions never wavered.
His stiff determination inspired confidence in the whole team.
She maintained a stiff resolve despite the challenges she faced.
His speech came across as stiff and rehearsed rather than genuine.
I found the writing style of the report a bit too stiff for casual readers.
The atmosphere at the dinner party was uncomfortably stiff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formal - having a stiff, conventional, or overly proper manner or style
  • antonym: relaxed - free from formality or stiffness in manner or style
  • synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • synonym: steady - firm in purpose or attachment; unwavering
  • antonym: wavering - becoming weaker; faltering or indecisive
