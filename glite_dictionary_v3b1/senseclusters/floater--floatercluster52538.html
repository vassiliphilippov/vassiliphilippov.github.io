floater

noun
employee reassigned as needed
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈfləʊ.tə / (BrE)
an employee who is assigned to different jobs or locations as needed, rather than holding a permanent position in one role or place

Examples

Office floater
As a floater, she became familiar with many departments across the company.
During the busy season, they hired several floaters to help wherever they were needed.
The hospital employs a floater to cover shifts whenever a nurse is absent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: substitute - a person who takes the place of another employee temporarily
