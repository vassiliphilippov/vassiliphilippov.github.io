slabby

adjective
thick and flat
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈslæbi / (AmE), / ˈslæbi / (BrE)
thick, flat, and resembling a slab in shape or appearance

Examples

Thick slabby stones
The old building was made of slabby stones stacked on top of each other.
She described the bread as slabby and difficult to cut evenly.
A slabby piece of concrete blocked the entrance to the tunnel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chunky - thick and solid in form
  • synonym: slab-like - resembling a slab in form
  • antonym: thin - having little thickness
