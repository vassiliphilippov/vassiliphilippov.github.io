Thylacinus cynocephalus

noun
scientific name of thylacine (Tasmanian tiger)
Pronunciation: / θaɪˈlæsɪnəs ˌsaɪnoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / θaɪˈlæsɪnəs ˌsaɪnəʊˈkɛfələs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the thylacine, a rare doglike carnivorous marsupial of Tasmania, also known as the Tasmanian tiger, now probably extinct

Examples

Thylacinus cynocephalus species
Scientists still debate the ecological role played by Thylacinus cynocephalus within Tasmanian habitats.
The last known Thylacinus cynocephalus died in captivity in 1936 at Hobart Zoo.
The striped back of Thylacinus cynocephalus gave rise to its nickname, the Tasmanian tiger.
