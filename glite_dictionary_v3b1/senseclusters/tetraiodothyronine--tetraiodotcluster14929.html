tetraiodothyronine

noun
thyroid hormone regulating metabolism
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛt.ɹaɪˌoʊ.doʊˈθaɪ.ɹəˌnin / (AmE), / ˌtɛt.ɹaɪˌəʊ.dəʊˈθaɪ.ɹəˌniːn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolism by controlling the rate of oxidation in cells; also known as thyroxine

Examples

Tetraiodothyronine hormone
Tetraiodothyronine plays a crucial role in regulating the body's metabolism.
A deficiency in tetraiodothyronine can lead to symptoms of hypothyroidism.
Doctors measured her tetraiodothyronine levels to assess thyroid function.
