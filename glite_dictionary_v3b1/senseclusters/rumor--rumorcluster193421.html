rumor

noun
unverified information passed between people
1.2 33/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀê.m…ö /; / ÀàruÀêm…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàruÀê.m…ô /; / ÀàruÀêm…ô / (BrE)
information or a story that is passed from person to person, often of uncertain or doubtful truth.

Examples

A rumor spreads
Is there any truth to the rumor we heard last night?
She denied the rumor that she was leaving the company.
The rumor about the new manager spread quickly through the office.
She denied the rumour about her engagement to the actor.
The rumour quickly spread throughout the small town.
There was a rumour at school that the principal was leaving.

Synonyms

  • hearsay
    7.0 1.37/m
    hearsay is unverified information often used in reports, while rumor is a more general word for circulating, doubtful stories.
  • scuttlebutt
    8.8 0.32/m
    scuttlebutt is informal, often spoken gossip among workers or friends, while rumor is a general unverified story.
  • word on the street
    7.8 0.52/m
    word on the street refers to casual public talk that many people repeat, while rumor is any unconfirmed story passed between people.
  • gossip
    1.1 11/m
    gossip is talk about people's private lives and often shares personal details, while rumor is any unconfirmed report.
  • talk
    3.2 40/m
    talk is general conversation or chat that may be harmless, while rumor is a specific unverified story spread between people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people
