jus gentium

noun
international law principles
9.9
Pronunciation: / jus ˈdʒɛnʃiəm / (AmE), / jus ˈdʒɛntiʊm / (BrE)
the body of law or principles accepted by the community of nations as binding between them, originally from Roman law and now the basis of international law

Examples

Related Terms

  • synonym: international law - the set of rules governing relations between countries
  • antonym: jus civile - the civil law of a particular state or people
  • synonym: natural law - a system of law based on fundamental moral principles
