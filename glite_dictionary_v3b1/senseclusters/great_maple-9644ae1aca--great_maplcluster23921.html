great maple

noun
a Eurasian maple tree (Acer pseudoplatanus)
1.1 5.7
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A Eurasian maple tree (Acer pseudoplatanus) with pale grey, flaking bark and large leaves with five ovate lobes that turn yellow in autumn.

Examples

A great maple tree
A great maple stood at the corner of the street, its bark flaking in large patches.
Gardeners often plant the great maple for its rapid growth and broad, shady canopy.
The park is home to a majestic great maple whose leaves turn bright yellow every autumn.
