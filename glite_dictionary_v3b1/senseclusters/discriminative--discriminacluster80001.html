discriminative

adjective
relating to ML models that predict classes directly

(in machine learning) relating to models or methods that learn to distinguish or classify by predicting labels or the conditional probability P(y|x) directly, rather than modelling how the data were generated.

Examples

Discriminative model
A discriminative model learns to predict labels directly from inputs in many classification tasks.
Researchers prefer discriminative approaches when accuracy on labelled data is more important than generation.
The textbook contrasted a discriminative classifier with a generative model to explain their differences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classifier - a program or model that assigns labels or categories to inputs; often implemented with discriminative methods.
  • antonym: generative (model) - a model that learns a joint or data-generating distribution (often P(x,y)); commonly presented as the opposite of discriminative models.
