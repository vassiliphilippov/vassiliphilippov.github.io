centerpiece

noun
decorative object placed at a table's center
2.3 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntɚˌpis /; / ˈsɛn.tɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌpiːs /; / ˈsɛn.təˌpiːs / (BrE)
an object, often decorative, placed in the center of a table or space, especially as an ornament

Examples

Table centerpiece
At the wedding, the crystal vase served as the main centerpiece on each table.
She arranged candles and leaves as a festive centerpiece for the table.
The beautiful floral centerpiece drew everyone's attention at the dinner party.
A beautiful sculpture served as the centerpiece of the hotel lobby's decor.
The vase of lilies was the centerpiece on the dining table, drawing everyone's attention.
They chose a large candle arrangement to be the centerpiece for the wedding reception.
A large sculpture served as the centrepiece of the exhibition.
She arranged candles as a centrepiece for the party.
The floral centrepiece was the highlight of the dining table.
