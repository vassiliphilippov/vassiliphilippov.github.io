heterostyly

noun
different flower style lengths
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈstaɪli / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈstaɪli / (BrE)
the occurrence of different style lengths in the flowers of the same species, often to encourage cross-pollination

Examples

Heterostyly in flowers
Some plants use heterostyly to increase the chances of cross-pollination between individuals.
Botanists study heterostyly to understand how flower structure affects reproduction.
Primroses are a classic example of a species that exhibits heterostyly in its flowers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homostyly - having styles of the same length in all flowers of a species
