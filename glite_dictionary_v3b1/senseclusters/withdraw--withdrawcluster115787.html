withdraw

verb
remove or take away from a supply
2.1 3.0
Pronunciation: /wɪð.ˈdrɔː/ /; / /wɪθ.ˈdrɔː/ (AmE), /wɪð.ˈdrɔː/ /; / /wɪθ.ˈdrɔː/ (BrE)
to remove or take away something, such as an object, product, support, or approval

Examples

To withdraw money
Hospitals can withdraw supplies from the central storage in emergencies.
She went to the bank to withdraw cash from her account.
We had to withdraw some food from the pantry for the event.
Following customer complaints, they had to withdraw all the affected batches.
The company decided to withdraw the faulty smartphones from the market.
The food manufacturer will withdraw the product after discovering a contamination issue.
