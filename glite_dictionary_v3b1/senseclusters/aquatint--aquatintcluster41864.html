aquatint

noun
printmaking technique resembling watercolor washes
7.1 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.kwə.tɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈæ.kwə.tɪnt/ (BrE)
a method of etching that produces areas of tone resembling watercolor washes, commonly used in printmaking.

Examples

Aquatint technique
She specializes in making fine aquatint prints that capture the subtle nuances of light and shadow.
The artist used an aquatint technique to achieve the rich tonal textures in her landscape.
We admired the museum’s collection of eighteenth-century aquatint engravings.
