slacken off

verb
become less intense or active
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈslæk.ən ɑf / (AmE), / ˈslæk.ən ɒf / (BrE)
to become less intense, active, or severe; to reduce speed, effort, or force.

Examples

To slacken off speed
After an hour of heavy rain, the storm finally began to slacken off.
As the pain started to slacken off, she was able to move more easily.
Productivity tends to slacken off near the end of a long workday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ease off - to gradually become less intense or severe
  • antonym: tighten - to make or become more tight or intense
