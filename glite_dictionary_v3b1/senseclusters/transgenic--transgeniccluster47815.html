transgenic

noun
genetically modified organism
8.2
Pronunciation: /trænz.ˈdʒe.nɪk/ /; / /træns.ˈdʒe.nɪk/ (AmE), /trænz.ˈdʒe.nɪk/ /; / /trɑːnz.ˈdʒe.nɪk/ (BrE)
an organism, especially a plant or animal, whose genome has been artificially altered by introducing a gene from another species

Examples

A transgenic animal
Scientists created a transgenic to study how the new gene affects growth rates.
The farm tested a transgenic that could resist certain pests without pesticides.
Concerns about the safety of releasing a transgenic into the environment were discussed at the conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genetically modified organism - an organism whose genetic material has been altered using genetic engineering
  • antonym: wild type - an organism with the typical, unaltered genetic makeup found in nature
