recontract

verb
to make new agreement
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkɑːntrækt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkɒntrækt / (BrE)
to make a new contract or agreement, especially after a previous one has ended

Examples

To recontract a lease
After the initial lease expired, they decided to recontract for another two years.
The company will recontract with its main distributor to ensure continued supply.
If both parties agree, they can recontract under new terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: renegotiate - to discuss and change the terms of an existing agreement
  • antonym: terminate - to end a contract or agreement
