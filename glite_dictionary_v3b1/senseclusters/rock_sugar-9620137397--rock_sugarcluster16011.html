rock sugar

noun
sugar in large crystals for decoration or sweetening
7.6
Pronunciation: / rɑk ˈsʌɡɚ / (AmE), / rɒk ˈsʌɡə / (BrE)
Sugar that has been crystallized into large, hard, transparent crystals, often used for decoration or sweetening.

Examples

Rock sugar crystals
Rock sugar is often used to sweeten herbal teas in many Asian countries.
Because of its size, rock sugar dissolves more slowly than regular sugar in hot drinks.
She sprinkled some rock sugar on top of the cake for an elegant touch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confectioner's sugar - very finely powdered sugar used in baking and decorating
  • antonym: granulated sugar - sugar in the form of small, fine crystals
