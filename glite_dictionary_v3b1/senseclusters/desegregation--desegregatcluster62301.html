desegregation

noun
ending separation of groups by race or religion
7.2
Pronunciation: / diˌsɛɡrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsɛɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of ending the separation of different racial or religious groups and allowing them to mix freely

Examples

Racial desegregation
Many activists dedicated their lives to the cause of desegregation during the civil rights movement.
The Supreme Court played a crucial role in advancing desegregation in public schools.
The city celebrated the anniversary of desegregation in its transportation system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: integration - the act of mixing people or groups previously kept apart
  • antonym: segregation - the enforced separation of different racial or religious groups
