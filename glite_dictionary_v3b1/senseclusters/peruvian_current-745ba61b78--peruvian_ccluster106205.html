Peruvian current

noun
cold ocean current off South America
Pronunciation: / pəˈruːviən ˈkɝːrənt / (AmE), / pəˈruːviən ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a cold ocean current flowing northward along the Pacific coast of South America before turning west into the Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Peruvian current flows
Fishing industries in Chile benefit greatly from the nutrient-rich waters brought by the Peruvian current.
Scientists study the Peruvian current to better understand its impact on global climate patterns.
The Peruvian current plays a vital role in the rich marine ecosystem off the coast of Peru.
