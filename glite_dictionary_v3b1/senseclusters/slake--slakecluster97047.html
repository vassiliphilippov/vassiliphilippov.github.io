slake

verb
cause to heat and crumble with water (esp. lime)
Pronunciation: / sleɪk / (AmE), / sleɪk / (BrE)
to cause something, especially lime, to heat and break down by adding water

Examples

To slake lime
The workers slaked the lime before mixing it with sand.
To produce mortar, you must slake quicklime by adding water.
When the powder was slaked, it released intense heat and crumbled into a fine paste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrate - to combine with water or cause to absorb water
