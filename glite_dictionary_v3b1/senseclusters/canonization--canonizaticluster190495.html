The process of accepting or promoting a work, author, idea, or text as part of an established cultural or literary canon; treating it as authoritative or central.

Examples

Literary canonization
Scholars debated the canonization of post-2000 novels as part of the national literary curriculum.
The journal published an essay arguing for the canonization of previously overlooked immigrant writers.
Museums play a role in the canonization of artists by curating permanent exhibitions and retrospectives.
