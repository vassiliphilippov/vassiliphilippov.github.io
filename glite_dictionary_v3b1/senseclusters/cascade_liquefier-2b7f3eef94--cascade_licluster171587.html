cascade liquefier

noun
apparatus for liquefying gases in stages
8.5
Pronunciation: /kæ.ˈskeɪd ˈlɪk.wə.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /kæ.ˈskeɪd ˈlɪk.wə.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
an apparatus that liquefies gases such as air or oxygen by using a series of cooling stages, each employing a different refrigerant.

Examples

A cascade liquefier device
A cascade liquefier makes it possible to obtain liquid oxygen for scientific experiments.
Maintenance of a cascade liquefier requires specialized technical skills.
The laboratory invested in a new cascade liquefier to improve its gas liquefaction process.
