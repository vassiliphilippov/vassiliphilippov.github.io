Augustinian Hermits

noun
Roman Catholic monastic order founded in 1256
Pronunciation: / əˌɡʌsˈtɪniən ˈhɜrmɪts / (AmE), / ɔːˌɡʌsˈtɪnɪən ˈhɜːmɪts / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic monastic order of friars founded in 1256 by papal decree, following the Rule of St Augustine.

Examples

The Augustinian Hermits order
A famous library was established by the Augustinian Hermits in the 14th century.
Several churches in Italy were built by the Augustinian Hermits during the Renaissance.
The Augustinian Hermits played a significant role in the religious life of medieval Europe.
