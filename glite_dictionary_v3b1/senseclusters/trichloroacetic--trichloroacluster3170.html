trichloroacetic

noun
corrosive laboratory acid
Pronunciation: / traɪˌklɔːroʊəˈsiːtɪk / (AmE), / traɪˌklɔːrəʊəˈsiːtɪk / (BrE)
a strong corrosive acid, trichloroacetic acid, used in laboratories and for medical treatments such as chemical peels and wart removal

Examples

Trichloroacetic acid
The doctor used trichloroacetic to treat the patient's skin condition.
In the laboratory, trichloroacetic is often used to precipitate proteins from solutions.
Chemical peels with trichloroacetic can help improve the appearance of sun-damaged skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetic acid - a colorless liquid organic compound with a pungent smell, main component of vinegar
  • synonym: hydrochloric acid - a strong, corrosive acid commonly used in laboratories
