tyrocidine

noun
polypeptide antibiotic from soil bacterium
Pronunciation: / ˌtaɪrəˈsaɪdin / (AmE), / ˌtɪərəˈsɪdiːn / (BrE)
a basic polypeptide antibiotic obtained from a soil bacterium, forming a major component of tyrothricin

Examples

Tyrocidine antibiotic component
Researchers isolated tyrocidine as a component responsible for antibiotic activity in tyrothricin.
The medical team studied the effectiveness of tyrocidine in treating superficial bacterial infections.
The soil bacterium produces tyrocidine, which is effective against Gram-positive bacteria.
