neoplastic cell

noun
cell involved in tumor formation
6.6
Pronunciation: / nioʊˈplæstɪk sɛl / (AmE), / niːəʊˈplæstɪk sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that forms part of a neoplasm (tumor), characterized by abnormal and uncontrolled growth

Examples

Neoplastic cells in tumor
The biopsy revealed an abundance of neoplastic cells in the tissue sample.
Treatment targets rapidly dividing neoplastic cells while sparing healthy tissue.
Under the microscope, pathologists identified the neoplastic cell by its large and irregular nucleus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal cell - a cell not affected by neoplastic change
  • synonym: tumor cell - a cell constituting a tumor
