Myristicaceae

noun
family of aromatic tropical trees (nutmeg family)
9.9
Pronunciation: / maɪˌrɪstɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mɪˌrɪstɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of aromatic tropical trees, including nutmeg, characterized by seeds surrounded by an aril.

Examples

Myristicaceae tropical trees
Many Myristicaceae trees have seeds enveloped by a fleshy aril, attracting various animals.
Researchers studied the ecological roles of Myristicaceae species in Southeast Asian rainforests.
The Myristicaceae family includes the tree that produces nutmeg, widely used as a spice.
