Angliae

proper noun
Latin for 'of England'
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈanɡli.aɪ /; / ˈæŋɡli.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈanɡli.aɪ /; / ˈæŋɡli.aɪ / (BrE)
the Latin form meaning 'of England' or 'to/for England', used in dedications, inscriptions, or scientific names

Examples

Pro salute Angliae
The inscription on the old church wall included the phrase 'pro salute Angliae'.
In medieval manuscripts, dedications often used Angliae to refer to England.
The Latin phrase 'Regina Angliae' means 'Queen of England'.
The butterfly species was named Vanessa atalanta Angliae to show it is found in England.
In scientific classification, Angliae indicates that the species originates from England.
The Latin term Angliae appears in the names of several plants native to England.
