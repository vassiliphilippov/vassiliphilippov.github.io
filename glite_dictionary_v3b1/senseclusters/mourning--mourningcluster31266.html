mourning

noun
sorrow and rituals after a death
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or period of expressing deep sorrow or grief after someone has died, often following certain customs

Examples

Deep mourning
His deep mourning was evident in the way he spoke about his late friend.
She wore black during the entire period of mourning for her grandmother.
The family observed traditional mourning rituals after the loss of their father.
After the funeral, the family observed a period of mourning for several weeks.
Public displays of mourning were visible throughout the city after the tragedy.
Traditional clothing during mourning is often black in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: celebration - the action of marking one's pleasure at an event
  • synonym: grief - intense sorrow caused by loss of someone or something
  • synonym: grieving - feeling or showing grief
