colonialize

verb
to impose colonial rule
7.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈloʊniəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring a place, people, or institution under the control of a foreign or colonial power and establish a colony there

Examples

To colonialize a country
Many local schools remained colonialized in curriculum and language decades after independence.
The region was quickly colonialized by foreign powers, changing its laws and traditions.
Some communities are working to undo the effects of having been colonialized for generations.
During the 1800s, European powers sought to colonialize vast areas of Africa and Asia.
The indigenous population resisted efforts to colonialize their homeland.
Historians debate the long-term effects of attempts to colonialize remote islands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonize - to establish control over a foreign area or people
  • antonym: decolonize - to free a country or area from colonial rule
  • antonym: liberate - to set free from control or oppression
