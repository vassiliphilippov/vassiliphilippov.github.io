correlation coefficient

noun
statistic measuring correlation between two variables
4.6 4.9
Pronunciation: /kɔːr.ə.ˈleɪ.ʃən kəʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ /; / /kɔːr.ə.ˈleɪ.ʃən koʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ (AmE), /kɒ.rə.ˈleɪ.ʃən kəʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ /; / /kɒ.rə.ˈleɪ.ʃən kəʊ.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ (BrE)
a numerical value between -1 and +1 that indicates the strength and direction of the relationship between two variables.

Examples

A correlation coefficient value
A negative correlation coefficient suggests that as one variable increases, the other decreases.
Statisticians use the correlation coefficient to quantify the relationship between economic indicators.
The correlation coefficient between exercise frequency and heart health was remarkably high in this study.
