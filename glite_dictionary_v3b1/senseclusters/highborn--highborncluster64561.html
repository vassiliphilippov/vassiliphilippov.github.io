highborn

of noble or aristocratic birth

of noble or aristocratic birth; belonging to the upper classes by virtue of family lineage.

Examples

Highborn families
Many highborn families owned large estates in the countryside.
The highborn lady wore an elegant gown to the royal ball.
Though he was highborn, he treated everyone with kindness and respect.

Synonyms

  • wellborn
    9.1 0.01/m
    wellborn means born into a respected family and raised with manners, while highborn means of noble family by lineage.
  • noble
    4.4 4.75/m
    noble refers to hereditary rank or title often tied to family history, while highborn stresses birth in the upper classes.
  • highbred
    7.4 0.01/m
    highbred describes good breeding and refined manners from upbringing, while highborn describes being born into upper-class lineage.
  • aristocratic
    7.2 3.38/m
    aristocratic describes traits or life of the upper class, while highborn focuses on being born into noble family lineage.
  • patrician
    8.5 0.40/m
    patrician often names a well-mannered, upper-class person with old family ties, while highborn simply means born into the aristocracy.
  • blue-blooded
    8.2 0.10/m
    blue-blooded is a casual, often playful way to say someone comes from nobility, while highborn is a neutral formal birth label.
