highborn

adjective
of noble or aristocratic birth
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbɔːn / (BrE)
of noble or aristocratic birth; belonging to the upper classes by virtue of family lineage.

Examples

Highborn families
Many highborn families owned large estates in the countryside.
The highborn lady wore an elegant gown to the royal ball.
Though he was highborn, he treated everyone with kindness and respect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lowborn - of humble or common birth
