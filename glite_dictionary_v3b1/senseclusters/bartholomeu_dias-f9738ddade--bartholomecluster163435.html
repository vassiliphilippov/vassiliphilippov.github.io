Bartholomeu Dias

proper noun
Portuguese explorer, first rounded Cape of Good Hope
Pronunciation: / bɑːrˌθɑːləməˌjuː ˈdiːəs / (AmE), / bɑːˌθɒləmjuː ˈdiːas / (BrE)
(c. 1450–1500) A Portuguese explorer who was the first European to sail around the Cape of Good Hope in 1488, opening a sea route from Europe to Asia.

Examples

Bartholomeu Dias explorer
Bartholomeu Dias was the first European to round the Cape of Good Hope in 1488.
Many historians regard Bartholomeu Dias as a pioneer of the Age of Discovery.
The achievements of Bartholomeu Dias paved the way for future expeditions to India.
