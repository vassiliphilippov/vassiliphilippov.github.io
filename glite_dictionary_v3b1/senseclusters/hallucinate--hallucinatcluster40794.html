hallucinate

verb
perceive things that are not real
3.5 7.4
Pronunciation: / həˈluːsɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / həˈluːsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to experience a perception of something that is not actually present, often as a result of illness, medication, or mind-altering substances

Examples

To hallucinate strange things
After taking the medication, he began to hallucinate strange shapes on the walls.
People deprived of sleep for long periods may hallucinate voices or images.
She feared that the heat would cause her to hallucinate in the desert.
