pooh

verb
to dismiss lightly
9.8 0.78/m
Pronunciation: / puÀê / (AmE), / puÀê / (BrE)
to express mild disapproval of or to dismiss something lightly, often with a sound or gesture

Examples

To pooh an idea
She poohed his suggestion, saying it would never work.
The critics poohed the new play, but audiences loved it.
He poohs any idea that doesn't come from him.

Synonyms

  • pooh-pooh
    9.5 0.49/m
    pooh-pooh dismisses something as unimportant with stronger contempt or mockery, while pooh shows milder, lighter dismissal.
  • hmph
    9.9 0.00/m
    hmph is a short low sound that shows annoyance, doubt, or mild disapproval, while pooh is a light verbal or gestural dismissal.
  • pffft
    9.9 0.00/m
    pffft is a sharp dismissive sound often showing disbelief or mocking and casual scorn, while pooh is milder and more polite.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approve - to agree with or accept something
  • synonym: dismiss - to decide that something is not important
