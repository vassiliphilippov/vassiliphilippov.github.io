liquidation

the process of bringing a business to an end and distributing its assets to claimants, typically by selling off its assets to pay debts

Examples

Company liquidation
After months of losses, the company began the process of liquidation to pay its creditors.
Creditors were notified that a court-ordered liquidation would be initiated next week.
During liquidation, the assets of the business were sold to cover outstanding debts.

Synonyms

  • liquidation process
    5.8 0.04/m
    liquidation process describes selling a company's assets step by step in practice, while liquidation is the formal legal closure of a business.
  • wind-up
    7.4 0.00/m
    wind-up names the formal legal process of closing a company and paying creditors, while liquidation focuses on selling assets to settle debts.

Related Terms
