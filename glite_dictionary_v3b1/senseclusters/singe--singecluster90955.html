singe

verb
burn lightly or superficially
9.2
Pronunciation: / sɪndʒ / (AmE), / sɪndʒ / (BrE)
to burn something lightly or on the surface, so it is only slightly damaged

Examples

To singe your hair
Be careful not to singe your shirt when ironing close to the collar.
She singed the edges of the paper to give it an antique appearance.
The flames singed his hair but left him otherwise unharmed.
Be careful not to singe your hair when you light the barbecue.
I could feel my eyebrows singe as I leaned over the open flame.
The heat from the oven door began to singe the edges of her apron.

  • synonym: scorch - to burn the surface of something with flame or heat
