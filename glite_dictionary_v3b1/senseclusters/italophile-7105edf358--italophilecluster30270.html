Italophile

noun
admirer of Italy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæləˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈtæləˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a person who admires or has a strong interest in Italian culture, people, or language

Examples

An Italophile collector
As an Italophile, she collects Italian art and studies the language in her free time.
Many Italophiles dream of living in Rome or Florence one day.
His passion for Italian cuisine and history marks him as a true Italophile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Anglophile - a person who admires English or British culture
  • synonym: Francophile - a person who admires French culture or people
  • antonym: Italophobe - a person who dislikes or has prejudice against Italian culture or people
