infinitesimal

noun
mathematical variable approaching zero
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.fɪ.nəˈtɛs.ə.məl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fɪ.nɪˈtɛs.ɪ.məl / (BrE)
(mathematics) A variable quantity that approaches zero as a limit, often used in calculus to denote quantities so small that they can be considered as nearly zero, but not exactly zero.

Examples

An infinitesimal in calculus
An infinitesimal can be thought of as a quantity smaller than any real number, but not exactly zero.
Physicists sometimes model changes as if they occur in infinitesimals to simplify calculations.
The concept of an infinitesimal is fundamental to the development of calculus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: differential - an infinitesimal change in a variable in calculus
  • synonym: limit - the value that a function or sequence approaches as the input or index approaches some value
