perihelion

noun
closest orbital point to the sun
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˈhiliən / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈhiːlɪən / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of a planet, comet, or other celestial body at which it is nearest to the sun

Examples

Planet perihelion point
Astronomers calculate the exact date and distance of a planet’s perihelion to study its orbital dynamics.
At perihelion, comets often become brighter due to the increased solar radiation.
The Earth reaches its perihelion every year in early January, when it is closest to the sun.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aphelion - the point in the orbit where a planet or comet is farthest from the sun
