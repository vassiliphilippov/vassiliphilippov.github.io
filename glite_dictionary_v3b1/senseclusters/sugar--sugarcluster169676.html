sugar

noun
sweet-tasting crystalline substance; sweetener
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʊɡər /; / ˈsʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə /; / ˈsʊɡə / (BrE)
a sweet, white, crystalline substance from plants (like sugar cane or beet) used to sweeten food and drinks, or any of a group of sweet-tasting substances found in living things

Examples

A spoonful of sugar
She always puts a spoonful of sugar in her tea.
The baker sprinkled sugar over the freshly baked cookies.
Too much sugar in your diet can be unhealthy.
Consuming too much sugar can increase the risk of certain health problems.
Glucose is a simple sugar that is vital for cellular respiration.
Plant cells produce sugar through the process of photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbohydrate - any of a large group of organic compounds including sugars, starch, and cellulose
  • antonym: salt - a white crystalline substance used to season and preserve food, opposite in taste to sugar
  • synonym: sweetener - a substance used to make food or drink taste sweet
