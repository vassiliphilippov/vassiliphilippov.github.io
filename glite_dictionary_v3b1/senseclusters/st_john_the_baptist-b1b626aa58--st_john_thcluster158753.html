St. John the Baptist

proper noun
preacher and forerunner of Jesus in the New Testament
Pronunciation: / seɪnt dʒɑn ðə ˈbæptɪst / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn ðə ˈbæptɪst / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) a preacher and hermit who baptized Jesus and is regarded as his forerunner; he was executed by Herod at the request of Salome.

Examples

St. John the Baptist in the Bible
Accounts of the life and death of St. John the Baptist appear in all four Gospels.
In many works of art, St. John the Baptist is depicted baptizing Jesus in the River Jordan.
The church dedicated to St. John the Baptist is a popular site for pilgrims.
