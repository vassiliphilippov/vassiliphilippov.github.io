geocentric parallax

noun
apparent shift of celestial objects from Earth viewpoint
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒioʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈpærəˌlæks / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈpærəlaks / (BrE)
the apparent shift in the position of a celestial body when observed from two different points on the Earth's surface as the Earth rotates

Examples

Geocentric parallax measurement
Advances in technology have improved the accuracy of geocentric parallax measurements for various celestial bodies.
By measuring the geocentric parallax of the Moon, astronomers can determine its distance from Earth.
The concept of geocentric parallax is important in calculating the positions of nearby planets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parallax - the apparent displacement of an object because of a change in the observer's point of view
