shucker

noun
shell remover
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌkər / (AmE), / ˈʃʌkə / (BrE)
a person or tool that removes the shell or husk from shellfish (like oysters or clams) or corn

Examples

An shucker opens oysters
The experienced shucker opened oysters quickly at the seafood bar.
A good shucker can remove the husk from corn in seconds.
They hired a shucker to prepare shellfish for the festival.
The shucker prepared dozens of oysters for the restaurant guests.
At the seafood festival, a skilled shucker demonstrated how to open clams quickly and safely.
Each shucker wore protective gloves to avoid injury while working with sharp shells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fisherman - a person who catches fish for a living
  • synonym: oysterman - a person who gathers or sells oysters
  • synonym: peeler - a tool or person that removes the outer skin from fruits or vegetables
  • synonym: sheller - a person or device that removes shells from nuts or shellfish
