sarkar

noun
historical district
9.9
Pronunciation: / sərˈkɑr / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɑː / (BrE)
a historical administrative district or revenue division in South Asian states, especially during Mughal and British rule

Examples

A Mughal sarkar
Each sarkar was managed by officials responsible for tax collection and law enforcement.
The boundaries of a sarkar often changed with shifts in political power.
Historical records describe the sarkar as a key unit of administration in Mughal India.
