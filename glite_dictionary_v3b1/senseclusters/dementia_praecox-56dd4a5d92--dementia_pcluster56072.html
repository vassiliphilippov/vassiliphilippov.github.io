dementia praecox

noun
outdated term for schizophrenia
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɛnʃə ˈpreɪkɑks / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛn.ti.ə ˈprɪː.kɒks / (BrE)
an outdated term for several psychotic disorders, especially schizophrenia, marked by a breakdown of thought processes and emotional responsiveness

Examples

dementia praecox diagnosis
Many patients once diagnosed with dementia praecox would today be considered to have schizophrenia.
The concept of dementia praecox was central to early psychiatric classification.
The term dementia praecox is rarely used in modern clinical psychology.
