amphistylic

adjective
jaw supported by two bones
Pronunciation: / ˌæmfɪˈstaɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌæmfɪˈstaɪlɪk / (BrE)
describing a type of jaw suspension in some fishes where the jaw is supported by both the cranium and the hyomandibular

Examples

Amphistylic jaw suspension
Some ancient sharks had amphistylic jaws, allowing for both strength and flexibility in feeding.
The fossil record shows that amphistylic jaw suspension was common in early cartilaginous fishes.
Researchers studied the amphistylic structure to understand the evolution of jaw mechanics in vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autostylic - having jaw suspension supported mainly by the cranium
  • antonym: hyostylic - having jaw suspension supported mainly by the hyomandibular
