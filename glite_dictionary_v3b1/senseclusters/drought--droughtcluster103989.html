drought

noun
prolonged shortage or absence
3.8 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / draÊŠt / (AmE), / draÊŠt / (BrE)
A prolonged period without something that is wanted or needed, such as success, supply, or activity.

Examples

A winning drought
After three years without a hit, the singer finally ended her drought with a chart-topping single.
The city experienced a ten-year drought of major cultural events before the new festival was launched.
The team is determined to break its championship drought this season.

Synonyms

  • dry spell
    3.6 0.68/m
    dry spell is a shorter, more casual term for a temporary lack of rain or success, while drought is a longer, more serious shortage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something; plenty
  • synonym: famine - a severe shortage of food
