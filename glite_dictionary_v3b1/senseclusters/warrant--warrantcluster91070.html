warrant

noun
formal authorization or approval
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔɹənt /; / ˈwɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒɹənt /; / ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official document or formal authorization that gives permission for an action, such as a court order or official approval

Examples

A court warrant
Her actions were taken with the full warrant of the university administration.
No employee should act without the proper warrant from management.
The committee's decision carries the official warrant for the project to proceed.
A judge signed the warrant authorizing her arrest.
The police obtained a warrant to search the suspect's house.
Without a warrant, the officers could not legally enter the premises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorization - official permission or approval
  • synonym: endorsement - formal support or approval for something or someone
  • antonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something by law or authority
  • synonym: subpoena - legal document ordering someone to attend court
  • synonym: summons - official order to appear in court
