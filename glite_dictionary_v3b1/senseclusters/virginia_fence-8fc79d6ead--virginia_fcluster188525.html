Virginia fence

noun
zigzag rail fence; interlocking rails
5.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /vər.ˈdʒɪn.jə fens/ /; / /vər.ˈdʒɪn.jə fents/ (AmE), /vɜː.ˈdʒɪn.jə fens/ /; / /vɜː.ˈdʒɪn.jə fents/ (BrE)
a traditional fence made from wooden rails laid in a zigzag pattern, often without posts, used for marking boundaries or decoration

Examples

A Virginia fence

The zigzag pattern of a Virginia fence makes it stable even on uneven ground.
They built a Virginia fence to mark the boundary of the property without using nails or posts.

The rustic charm of a Virginia fence makes it a popular choice for rural gardens.
They decided to build a Virginia fence around their backyard for extra privacy.
