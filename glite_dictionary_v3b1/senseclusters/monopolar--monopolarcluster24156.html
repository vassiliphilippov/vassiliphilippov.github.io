monopolar

adjective
single dominant power
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊnəˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
characterized by or relating to a single dominant power or center, especially in politics or international relations

Examples

Monopolar world order
The world was considered monopolar after one country emerged as the sole superpower.
Some analysts debate whether the current international system remains monopolar or is shifting toward multipolarity.
A monopolar structure can lead to stability but may also create imbalances in global politics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multipolar - having or relating to multiple centers of power or influence
  • synonym: unipolar - having or relating to a single center of power or influence
