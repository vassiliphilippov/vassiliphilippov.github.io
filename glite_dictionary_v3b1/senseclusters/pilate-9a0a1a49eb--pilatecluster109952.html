Pilate

proper noun
Roman governor who authorized Jesus' crucifixion
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
the Roman governor of Judea who, according to the New Testament, authorized the crucifixion of Jesus Christ

Examples

Pilate the governor
According to the Gospel, Pilate washed his hands before the crowd, signifying his claimed innocence.
Many historians debate whether Pilate actually hesitated before ordering Jesus' execution.
The name Pilate has become synonymous with passing responsibility to others.
