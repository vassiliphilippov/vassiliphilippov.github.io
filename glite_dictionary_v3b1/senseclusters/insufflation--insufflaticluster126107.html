insufflation

noun
the act of blowing air into or onto something
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.səˈfleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sʌˈfleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of blowing or breathing air or gas into or upon something, especially as part of a medical procedure or a ritual

Examples

Ritual insufflation
Certain religious ceremonies include insufflation as a symbolic gesture of spiritual cleansing.
The anesthesiologist performed insufflation of oxygen during the surgery to assist the patient's breathing.
The technician used carbon dioxide insufflation to examine the patient's colon during the procedure.
