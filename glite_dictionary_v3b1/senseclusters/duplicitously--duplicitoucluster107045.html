duplicitously

adverb
deceitfully or dishonestly
Pronunciation: / duˈplɪsɪtəsli / (AmE), / djuˈplɪsɪtəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is deceitful or dishonest, especially by pretending to have one set of feelings or intentions while acting differently

Examples

To speak duplicitously
The politician spoke duplicitously to different groups, promising each something different.
She acted duplicitously by pretending to support her friend while secretly working against her.
The company duplicitously claimed to care about the environment while polluting the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceitfully - in a way that is dishonest or intended to mislead
  • antonym: honestly - in a truthful or sincere way
