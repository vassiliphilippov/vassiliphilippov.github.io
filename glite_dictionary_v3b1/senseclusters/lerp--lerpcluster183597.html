lerp

noun
insect sugary covering
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɜːrp / (AmE), / lɜːp / (BrE)
a small, sugary, crustlike structure secreted by certain psyllid insects, serving as a protective covering or food source, especially on eucalyptus leaves

Examples

A lerp on leaves
The birds pecked at the lerp on the eucalyptus leaves for a sweet treat.
Scientists studied the formation of lerp to understand how psyllid insects protect themselves.
During the dry season, parrots rely on lerp as an important food source.

Related Terms

  • synonym: honeydew - a sweet substance secreted by some insects, such as aphids
