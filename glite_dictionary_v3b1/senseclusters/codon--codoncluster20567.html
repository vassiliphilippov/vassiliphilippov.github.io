codon

noun
genetic code triplet in DNA or RNA
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.dɑn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.dɒn / (BrE)
a sequence of three adjacent nucleotides in DNA or RNA that codes for a specific amino acid or signals the termination of protein synthesis

Examples

A codon sequence
Each codon corresponds to a specific amino acid in the process of translation.
Mutations in a single codon can lead to significant changes in an organism's traits.
The genetic sequence includes a codon that determines the start of protein synthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticodon - a sequence of three nucleotides in tRNA complementary to a codon
