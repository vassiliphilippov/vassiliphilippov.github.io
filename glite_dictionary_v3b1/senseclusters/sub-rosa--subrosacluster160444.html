sub-rosa

adjective
confidential or secret
Pronunciation: / sʌb ˈroʊzə / (AmE), / sʌb ˈrəʊzə / (BrE)
in secret; done or taking place confidentially or clandestinely

Examples

A sub-rosa meeting
He conducted a sub-rosa investigation into the missing documents.
Most of the negotiations happened sub-rosa to avoid media attention.
The company held a sub-rosa meeting to discuss the merger before announcing it publicly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially for an illicit purpose
  • antonym: open - done or conducted without concealment; public
  • synonym: secret - not meant to be known or seen by others; hidden
