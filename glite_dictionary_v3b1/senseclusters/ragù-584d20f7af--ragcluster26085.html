ragù

noun
Italian meat sauce
4.2
Pronunciation: / rɑˈɡu /; / rɑːˈɡuː / (AmE), / ræˈɡuː / (BrE)
a rich, thick Italian meat sauce, usually slow-cooked and served with pasta or polenta

Examples

Italian ragu sauce
The chef simmered the ragù for hours to bring out its deep flavors.
She served homemade ragù over fresh pasta for the family dinner.
A traditional ragù often includes beef, pork, and a mix of vegetables.
The chef prepared a traditional ragù to serve with homemade tagliatelle.
After hours of simmering, the ragù was rich and full of flavor.
He learned to make ragù from his grandmother's family recipe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bolognese - an Italian meat-based sauce for pasta
  • antonym: marinara - an Italian tomato-based pasta sauce without meat
  • synonym: stew - a dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid
