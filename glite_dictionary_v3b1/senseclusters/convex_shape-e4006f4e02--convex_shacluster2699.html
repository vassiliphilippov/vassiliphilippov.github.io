convex shape

noun
a shape curving or bulging outward
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛks ʃeɪp / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛks ʃeɪp / (BrE)
a shape that curves or bulges outward, with no indentations or inward curves

Examples

A convex shape surface
A football can be considered a convex shape because its surface curves outward everywhere.
The lens of her glasses had a convex shape to help focus her vision.
When drawing a convex shape, all points on the edge bulge outward from the center.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concave shape - a shape that curves inward, forming a hollow
