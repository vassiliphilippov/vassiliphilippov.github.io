gribble

noun
wood-boring crustacean
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪbəl / (BrE)
a small marine crustacean that bores into and feeds on wood submerged in seawater

Examples

A gribble in wood
The wooden dock was weakened by gribble over many years.
Marine biologists study how gribble damage ships and underwater structures.
Protective coatings are used to prevent gribble from boring into wooden piers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shipworm - a marine mollusk that bores into wood
