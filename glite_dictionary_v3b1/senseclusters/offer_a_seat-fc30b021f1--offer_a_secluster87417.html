offer a seat

verb
politely provide a seat to someone
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːfər ə siːt / (AmE), / ˈɒfə ə siːt / (BrE)
to politely provide someone with a place to sit, especially by giving up one's own seat or pointing out a seat for them

Examples

To offer a seat politely
At the bus stop, I offered a seat to the elderly man when he looked tired.
She always offers a seat to guests as soon as they arrive at her house.
When the train was crowded, he offered a seat to the woman carrying a child.
