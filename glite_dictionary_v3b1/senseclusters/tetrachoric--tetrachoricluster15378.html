tetrachoric

adjective
binary correlation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈkɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈkɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the statistical correlation between two variables, each measured on a two-level (binary) scale

Examples

Tetrachoric correlation
The tetrachoric coefficient is often used in psychological research to analyze binary data.
A tetrachoric analysis can help determine the relationship between two yes-or-no survey responses.
Researchers calculated the tetrachoric correlation to assess the link between two diagnostic outcomes.
