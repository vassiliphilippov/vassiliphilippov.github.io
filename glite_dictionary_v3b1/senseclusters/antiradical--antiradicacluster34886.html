antiradical

noun
opponent of radicalism
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a person who opposes radical political movements or supports moderate, gradual change

Examples

An antiradical leader
The antiradical voiced concerns about the risks of sudden political change.
During the debate, an antiradical argued for gradual reforms instead of sweeping measures.
Many antiradicals supported policies that promoted stability and incremental progress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moderate - a person with moderate political views
  • antonym: radical - a person who advocates for thorough or complete political or social reform
