louse up

verb
make a mess of; ruin
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: / laʊs ʌp / (AmE), / laʊs ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, ruin, or make a mess of something by doing it badly or making mistakes

Examples

To louse up a project
Don't louse up the project by skipping important steps.
He really loused up his interview by forgetting the manager's name.
She was worried that one wrong move would louse up the entire plan.
He loused up his chances of promotion by arriving late to the interview.
I'm afraid I loused up the report by missing several important details.
They realized too late that they'd loused up the scheduling for the conference.
If you louse up the interview, you won't get another chance.
She really loused up her audition by forgetting her lines.
Try not to louse up the report this time.
