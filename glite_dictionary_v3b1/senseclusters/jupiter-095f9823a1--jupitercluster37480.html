Jupiter

proper noun
Roman supreme god; ruler of gods
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒuː.pɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈdʒuː.pə.tər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒuː.pɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈdʒuː.pə.tə/ (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the supreme god, regarded as the ruler of heaven and god of thunder and lightning; the counterpart of Greek Zeus.

Examples

Roman god Jupiter
In ancient Rome, people would pray to Jupiter for protection and guidance.
Many stories recount Jupiter hurling thunderbolts at those who defied him.
The temple dedicated to Jupiter stood atop the Capitoline Hill.
