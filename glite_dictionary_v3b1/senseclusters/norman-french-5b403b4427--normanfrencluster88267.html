Norman-French

noun
medieval Norman dialect of Old French
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrmən ˈfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmən ˈfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
the form of Old French spoken in Normandy during the Middle Ages, notably influential on English law and vocabulary after the Norman Conquest

Examples

Norman-French language
Documents from medieval England often contain passages written in Norman-French.
Many English legal terms derive from Norman-French after the Norman Conquest of 1066.
The impact of Norman-French can still be seen in modern English vocabulary, particularly in law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Old French - the early form of the French language spoken roughly from the 9th to the 14th century
