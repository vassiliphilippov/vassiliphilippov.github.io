preparation

noun
advance planning or anticipation
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛp.əˈreɪ.ʃən /; / ˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛp.əˈreɪ.ʃən /; / ˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something ready, planning, organizing, or training for an event or activity (including cooking food)

Examples

Event preparation
Proper preparation helped them respond calmly during the unexpected emergency.
She spent weeks in careful preparation for her job interview.
Without preparation, facing sudden changes can lead to confusion and stress.
Proper preparation is essential for a successful job interview.
She spent all morning on the preparation of her presentation for the meeting.
The preparation of the room took longer than expected before the guests arrived.
Proper preparation ensures that the event will run smoothly and efficiently.
The army completed its preparation for the joint military exercise on schedule.
The team went through months of intense preparation before the competition.
