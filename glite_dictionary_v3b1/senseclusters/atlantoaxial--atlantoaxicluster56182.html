atlantoaxial

adjective
neck vertebrae joint
Pronunciation: / ətˌlæntoʊˈæksiəl / (AmE), / ətˌlæntəʊˈæksiəl / (BrE)
relating to the joint between the first (atlas) and second (axis) cervical vertebrae in the neck

Examples

The atlantoaxial joint
The atlantoaxial joint is crucial for allowing the head to turn from side to side.
Doctors examined the patient's atlantoaxial region after the neck injury.
Abnormal movement in the atlantoaxial joint can lead to serious neurological problems.
