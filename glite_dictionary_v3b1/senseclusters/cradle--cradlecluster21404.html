cradle

verb
hold gently in the arms
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪ.dəl /; / ˈkreɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.dl̩ /; / ˈkreɪdəl / (BrE)
to hold or support something or someone gently and carefully, especially in your arms

Examples

To cradle a baby
He cradled the phone between his ear and shoulder as he wrote.
She gently cradled the kitten in her hands.
The mother cradled her newborn and sang softly.
He cradled the basketball while scanning the court for an open teammate.
She gently cradled the kitten, soothing its fears with a soft voice.
The nurse cradled the newborn in her arms and carried him to his mother.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hold - to grasp or support with the hands
  • synonym: rock - to move gently back and forth or from side to side
  • synonym: support - to bear the weight of; hold up
