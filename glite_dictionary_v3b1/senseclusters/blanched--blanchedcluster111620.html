blanched

adjective
grown pale by lack of light (plants)

(of a plant or part of a plant) grown without exposure to light, resulting in a pale or white color due to the absence of chlorophyll.

Examples

Blanched celery stalks
Celery is often blanched in the garden by mounding soil around it to keep the stalks pale and tender.
The chef prefers to use blanched asparagus for its tender texture and delicate flavor.
When grown under a cover, endive becomes blanched and loses its bitterness.
