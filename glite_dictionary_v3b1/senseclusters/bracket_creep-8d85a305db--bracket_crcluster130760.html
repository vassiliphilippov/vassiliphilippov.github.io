bracket creep

noun
taxpayers moving into higher tax brackets due to income increases
9.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbræk.ɪt kriː/ /; / /ˈbræk.ət kriː/ (AmE), /ˈbræk.ɪt kriː/ /; / /ˈbræk.ət kriː/ (BrE)
The gradual movement of taxpayers into higher income tax brackets as their nominal incomes increase, often due to inflation, resulting in higher taxes without an actual increase in real income.

Examples

Bracket creep effect
Governments sometimes adjust tax bands to prevent bracket creep and keep the tax system fair.
Many workers experienced bracket creep as inflation pushed up their salaries, increasing their tax rates.
Without adjusting for inflation, bracket creep can erode real income over time.

  • synonym: tax bracket - a range of incomes taxed at a particular rate
