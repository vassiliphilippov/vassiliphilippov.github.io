Elizabethan age

noun
period of English history under Elizabeth I
6.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lɪ.ˈzæb.ə.θən eɪdʒ/ /; / /el.ɪ.zəb.ˈiː.θən eɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ɪ.lɪ.ˈzæb.ə.θən eɪdʒ/ /; / /ɪ.lɪ.zə.ˈbiː.θən eɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the period of English history during the reign of Queen Elizabeth I (1558–1603), noted for its flourishing of literature, especially drama, and for national prosperity

Examples

The Elizabethan age period
Many historians consider the Elizabethan age a golden period in British history.
Shakespeare wrote many of his plays during the Elizabethan age.
The Elizabethan age is often characterized by a great flowering of English literature and drama.
