inviolably

adverb
securely; unbreakably
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvaɪ.ələ.bli / (AmE), / ɪnˈvʌɪ.ələbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be violated, broken, or dishonored

Examples

To inviolably uphold rules
The ancient laws were inviolably upheld by every member of the community.
Her promise was inviolably kept, no matter the circumstances.
The border between the two countries was inviolably respected for decades.
