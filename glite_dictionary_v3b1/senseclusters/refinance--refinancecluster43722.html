refinance

verb
to finance again, especially a loan or mortgage
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfaɪˌnæns, ˌriːfəˈnæns / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfaɪˌnɑːns, ˌriːfəˈnɑːns / (BrE)
to arrange for new financing of something, such as a loan or mortgage, usually to replace an existing one with different terms

Examples

To refinance a loan
After interest rates fell, they decided to refinance their home mortgage to secure a lower monthly payment.
Many car buyers eventually choose to refinance their auto loans to take advantage of better terms.
The company plans to refinance its debt to improve cash flow and reduce overall expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: finance - to provide funding for; to supply with money
