counterbore

noun
widened screw hole
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəbɔː / (BrE)
a hole with a wider, flat-bottomed opening at the top to allow a screw or bolt head to sit flush with or below the surface

Examples

A counterbore hole
The engineer specified a counterbore to ensure the bolt head would not stick out.
Before inserting the screw, he made a counterbore so the surface would remain smooth.
A proper counterbore is important when assembling furniture with flat surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: countersink - a conical hole for a screw head to sit flush with a surface
