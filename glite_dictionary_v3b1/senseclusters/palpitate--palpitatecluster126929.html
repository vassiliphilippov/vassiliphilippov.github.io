(of the heart) to beat rapidly, strongly, or irregularly, especially because of strong emotion or illness.

Examples

Hearts palpitate quickly
After running up the stairs, she could feel her heart palpitating in her chest.
Her heart began to palpitate with anxiety before the important meeting.
When he saw the frightening scene, his chest seemed to palpitate uncontrollably.

Synonyms

  • flutter
    4.4 0.92/m
    flutter is a quick, irregular beating often from nerves or illness, while palpitate sounds more medical for a strong, fast, or uneven heart.
  • heart quicken
    5.2 0.12/m
    heart quicken makes the heart beat faster with excitement or fear, while palpitate often means a stronger, sometimes irregular, medical term.
