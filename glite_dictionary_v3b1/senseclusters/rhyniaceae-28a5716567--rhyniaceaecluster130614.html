Rhyniaceae

noun
extinct family of early Paleozoic plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / raɪniˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / raɪniˈeɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
an extinct family of primitive vascular plants from the Paleozoic era, known from fossil records; among the earliest land plants.

Examples

The Rhyniaceae family
Fossils from the Rhyniaceae provide valuable insights into the evolution of early land plants.
Paleobotanists study the Rhyniaceae to understand plant life during the Devonian period.
The Rhyniaceae had simple vascular systems and lacked true leaves or roots.
