lose track

verb
fail to remain informed or aware
Pronunciation: / luːz træk / (AmE), / luːz træk / (BrE)
to fail to remain informed or aware of the current status or whereabouts of someone or something

Examples

To lose track of time
I'm sorry, I lost track of time and arrived late.
It's easy to lose track of your goals when you are overwhelmed by day-to-day tasks.
She always loses track of her keys when she's distracted.
