formalism

noun
strict adherence to prescribed forms or rules
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrməˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
strict or excessive adherence to prescribed forms, rules, or conventions, especially in art, religion, or conduct.

Examples

Rigid formalism
His approach to teaching was marked by a rigid formalism that left little room for creativity.
Some critics argue that legal formalism can hinder justice by prioritizing process over fairness.
The ceremony was conducted with such formalism that it felt disconnected from its original purpose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rigorism - the strict application of principles or rules
  • antonym: spontaneity - behavior or actions arising from natural impulse, not rigid adherence to form
