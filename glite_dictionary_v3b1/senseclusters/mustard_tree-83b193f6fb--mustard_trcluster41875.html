mustard tree

noun
evergreen shrub/tree (genus Salvadora) used for brushing teeth
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌstərd triː / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd triː / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree or shrub of the genus Salvadora, native to arid regions, whose fibrous branches are used for brushing teeth and whose shoots are used as fodder.

Examples

Desert mustard tree
Ash from the burned mustard tree is sometimes collected and used as a source of salt.
In some regions, the mustard tree is valued for its branches, which are used as natural toothbrushes.
The arid landscape was dotted with mustard trees providing shade and food for camels.
