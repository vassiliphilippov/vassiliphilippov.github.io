foundational

adjective
basic or essential
4.6
Pronunciation: / faʊnˈdeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
forming the base or core that is necessary for something to exist or develop; essential or fundamental

Examples

Foundational principles
Trust is foundational to any strong relationship.
Reading skills are foundational for academic success in all subjects.
The company’s values are foundational to its business practices.
The country's foundational principles are written in its constitution.
Reading is a foundational skill for all other learning in school.
The scientist's foundational research paved the way for future discoveries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basic - simple and essential; forming the base
  • synonym: fundamental - serving as an essential part or foundation
  • antonym: superficial - not deep or thorough; only on the surface
