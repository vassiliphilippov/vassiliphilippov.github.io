cleistothecium

noun
closed spore-bearing structure of some fungi
Pronunciation: / klaɪstəˈθisiəm / (AmE), / klaɪstəˈθiːsɪəm / (BrE)
A closed spore-bearing structure found in certain fungi, from which spores are released only when the structure decays or disintegrates.

Examples

A cleistothecium structure
The formation of a cleistothecium is characteristic of many fungi within the Erysiphaceae family.
The spores are released from the cleistothecium only after its walls break down.
Under the microscope, the cleistothecium appears as a small, spherical structure.
