catalyze

verb
cause or speed up a reaction or process
3.2 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæt.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈkæt.ə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to cause or accelerate (a reaction) by acting as a catalyst; to make a process or event happen more quickly or easily

Examples

To catalyze chemical reactions
Enzymes catalyze chemical reactions vital for life.
Her arrival helped catalyze changes within the company culture.
The discovery catalyzed a wave of innovation across the industry.
Enzymes in your body catalyse thousands of essential biochemical reactions every day.
Sometimes, even a small amount of a substance can catalyse a dramatic change in the environment.
The scientist wanted to catalyse the process to increase the yield of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyze - American spelling of 'catalyse'
  • antonym: decompose - to break down into simpler components, often the opposite of catalysis in chemistry
  • synonym: facilitate - to make a process easier
  • antonym: inhibit - to slow down or prevent a process
