neritid gastropod

noun
marine snail with a short spiral shell
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈne.rɪ.tɪd ˈgæ.strə.pɑːd/ (AmE), /ˈne.rɪ.tɪd ˈgæ.strə.pɒd/ (BrE)
A marine snail of the family Neritidae, distinguished by having an operculum and a short spiral shell, commonly found in coastal waters.

Examples

A neritid gastropod snail
Marine biologists collected several species of neritid gastropod for their study on coastal biodiversity.

Unlike many other snails, the neritid gastropod closes its shell with a tough, plate-like operculum.
