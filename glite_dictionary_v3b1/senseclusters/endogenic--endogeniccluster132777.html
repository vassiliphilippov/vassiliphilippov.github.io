endogenic

adjective
originating internally
6.8 7.9
Pronunciation: /en.də.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /en.doʊ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (AmE), /en.də.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (BrE)
originating or produced within something, especially within the Earth (in geology)

Examples

Endogenic geological processes
In geology, endogenic processes such as earthquakes and mountain building occur within the planet.
Most volcanic activity is considered endogenic because it originates beneath the Earth's surface.
The researchers studied endogenic factors influencing cell growth.
Granite is considered an endogenic rock because it forms from magma beneath the Earth's surface.
Many endogenic processes contribute to mountain building and geological uplift.
Unlike sedimentary rocks, endogenic rocks undergo formation deep within the Earth.
