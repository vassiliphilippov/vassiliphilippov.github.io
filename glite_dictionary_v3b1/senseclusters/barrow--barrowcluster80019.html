barrow

noun
prehistoric burial mound
Pronunciation: / ˈbæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊ / (BrE)
A large mound of earth or stones placed over a prehistoric tomb; a burial mound.

Examples

Ancient barrow
A Bronze Age barrow can be seen on top of the hill near the village.
Legends often surround the mysterious barrow mounds found across northern Europe.
The archaeologists uncovered several ancient barrows in the English countryside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cairn - a mound of stones built as a memorial or landmark, sometimes covering a burial
  • synonym: tumuli - an ancient burial mound; plural of tumulus
