denitrification

noun
removal of nitrates
Pronunciation: / diˌnaɪtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌnaɪtrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which nitrates are removed from soil or water, usually by bacteria converting them into nitrogen gas

Examples

Denitrification process
Bacteria in wetlands play a key role in denitrification by removing excess nitrates from the water.
Farmers are interested in denitrification because it helps prevent nitrate pollution in groundwater.
The process of denitrification returns nitrogen to the atmosphere as a gas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nitrification - the process of converting ammonia into nitrates
