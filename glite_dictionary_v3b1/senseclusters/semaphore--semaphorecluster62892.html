semaphore

noun
visual signaling device with arms or lights
8.5 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…õm.…ôÀåf…îr / (AmE), / Ààs…õm.…ô.f…îÀê / (BrE)
An apparatus for visual signaling, especially one with moving arms or lights, used to convey information over a distance.

Examples

Railway semaphore signal
A broken semaphore can cause confusion for drivers approaching the crossing.
Pilots learned to interpret the semaphore signals from the airfield tower.
The old railway station still uses a semaphore to control train movements.

Synonyms

  • semaphore telegraph
    9.9 0.00/m
    semaphore telegraph is a tower with moving arms used before electric telegraphs to send long-distance messages, while semaphore is any visual signaling device.
