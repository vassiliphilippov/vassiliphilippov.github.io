blast furnace

noun
industrial furnace for extracting iron
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /blæst ˈfɜːr.nəs/ /; / /blæst ˈfɜːr.nɪs/ (AmE), /blɑːst ˈfɜː.nɪs/ /; / /blæst ˈfɜː.nɪs/ (BrE)
an industrial furnace in which iron is extracted from its ore by means of a hot air blast

Examples

A large blast furnace
A blast furnace operates continuously once it is started, often for several years.
Molten iron flows out of the blast furnace and is collected for further processing.
The steel mill installed a new blast furnace to increase production capacity.
