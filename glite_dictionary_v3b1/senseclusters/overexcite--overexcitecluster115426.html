overexcite

verb
to make too excited
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something too excited or overly stimulated

Examples

To overexcite children
Too much sugar can easily overexcite children at a birthday party.
The loud fireworks seemed to overexcite the dogs, making them bark all night.
Try not to overexcite the students before the exam with too many announcements.
