diskless

adjective
without a disk drive
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪkləs / (AmE), / ˈdɪkləs / (BrE)
lacking a disk drive, especially in reference to computers or workstations

Examples

Diskless computer
A diskless computer accesses all its files from a central server over the network.
Many modern offices use diskless workstations to simplify maintenance and improve security.
The IT department set up a diskless environment to reduce hardware costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disk-equipped - having a disk drive installed
