queen regnant

noun
female monarch reigning in her own right
Pronunciation: / kwiːn ˈrɛɡ.nənt / (AmE), / kwiːn ˈrɛɡ.nənt / (BrE)
A female sovereign who reigns in her own right, having inherited the throne rather than holding the title as the wife of a king.

Examples

A queen regnant rules
Historically, many countries did not allow a queen regnant to inherit the throne.
Queen Elizabeth I was a queen regnant, ruling England and Ireland in her own right.
Unlike a queen consort, a queen regnant holds sovereign power independently of marriage to a king.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: king regnant - a male sovereign ruler, reigning in his own right
  • antonym: queen consort - the wife of a reigning king, not a sovereign in her own right
