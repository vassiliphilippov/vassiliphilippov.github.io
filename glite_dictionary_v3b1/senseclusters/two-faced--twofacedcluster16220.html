two-faced

adjective
deceptive or hypocritical
6.7 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀåfe…™st / (AmE), / ÀàtuÀêÀåfe…™st / (BrE)
deliberately deceptive by pretending to have one set of feelings while acting under the influence of another; insincere or hypocritical.

Examples

A two-faced person
His colleagues discovered he was two-faced, saying kind things in person but criticizing them behind their backs.
I don't trust politicians who seem two-faced during election campaigns.
She acted friendly to everyone but was actually quite two-faced and spread rumors.

Synonyms

  • duplicitous
    8.9 0.75/m
    duplicitous is a formal word often used in writing for hiding intentions, while two-faced is the everyday term for the same behaviour.
  • double-faced
    8.4 0.00/m
    double-faced is a direct variant used to mean pretending friendly feelings while secretly acting against them, while two-faced names this insincerity.
  • Janus-faced
    9.9 0.00/m
    Janus-faced is a literary, formal term from Roman myth for someone who shows opposite faces and motives, while two-faced is common spoken English.
  • double-tongued
    9.4 0.00/m
    double-tongued describes speaking with false or changing words to mislead others, while two-faced refers more broadly to insincere behaviour.
  • hypocritical
    6.1 4.54/m
    hypocritical means claiming moral beliefs you do not follow, while two-faced focuses on pretending feelings to mislead people.
  • deceitful
    5.0 1.46/m
    deceitful means acting in a dishonest way to mislead others, while two-faced specifically means showing false friendly feelings.

  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful
  • synonym: hypocritical - pretending to have virtues or beliefs one doesn't possess
