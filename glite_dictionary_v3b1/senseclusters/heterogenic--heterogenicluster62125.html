heterogenic

adjective
differing generations
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving successive generations or developmental stages that differ greatly in form or structure

Examples

Heterogenic life cycle
Some parasites have heterogenic life cycles, with adult and larval stages that look completely different.
The scientist studied the heterogenic development of insects that change form dramatically as they mature.
A heterogenic process can result in organisms with very distinct appearances at different life stages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heterogeneous - consisting of different or diverse elements
  • antonym: homogenic - relating to or producing uniform offspring or stages
