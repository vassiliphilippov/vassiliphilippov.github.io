common daisy

noun
a low-growing plant with yellow and white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A low-growing Eurasian plant (Bellis perennis) with yellow central disc flowers and pinkish-white outer ray flowers, commonly found in lawns and grassland.

Examples

The common daisy flower
Children love picking common daisy flowers from the lawn in spring.
The common daisy is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
Tiny bees collected nectar from the common daisy blossoms scattered across the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daisy - any plant of the family Asteraceae with a similar flower structure
