choledocholithotomy

noun
bile duct surgery
Pronunciation: / koʊˌlɛdoʊkoʊlɪˈθɑːtəmi /; / koʊˌlɛdoʊkoʊlɪˈθɑtəmi / (AmE), / kəʊˌlɛdəkəʊlɪˈθɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove stones from the common bile duct

Examples

A choledocholithotomy operation
The patient underwent a choledocholithotomy to remove several large stones from the bile duct.
After the choledocholithotomy, the surgeon checked for any remaining stones before closing the incision.
A choledocholithotomy is sometimes necessary when less invasive procedures cannot remove bile duct stones.
After the diagnosis of bile duct stones, the surgeon performed a choledocholithotomy to remove them.
Recovery from a choledocholithotomy usually requires a short hospital stay and careful monitoring.
The doctor explained that a choledocholithotomy was necessary to clear the obstruction in the bile duct.
