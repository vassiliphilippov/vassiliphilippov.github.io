calefaction

noun
process or state of warming
Pronunciation: / ˌkælɪˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of being warmed or becoming warm, especially by artificial means

Examples

Steady calefaction
A steady calefaction noticeable in the laboratory indicated a hidden heat source.
During winter, calefaction of the greenhouse is crucial for the survival of the plants.
The old manuscript described a device for the calefaction of air in large rooms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooling - the process of making something cooler
  • synonym: heating - the process of making something hot or warm
