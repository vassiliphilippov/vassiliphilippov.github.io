sidelight

noun
light from the side
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdlaɪt / (BrE)
light that comes from the side, often producing a soft or angled effect

Examples

Soft sidelight
The photographer used sidelight to create dramatic shadows on the model's face.
Soft sidelight from the window gave the room a warm atmosphere in the evening.
Artists often prefer sidelight because it highlights textures and shapes in their work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backlight - light coming from behind a subject
  • antonym: frontlight - light coming from in front of a subject
