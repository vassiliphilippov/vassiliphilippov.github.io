echoey

adjective
reverberating sound
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊi / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊi / (BrE)
having a sound that repeats or reverberates, especially in a large or empty space

Examples

An echoey hall
The empty hall was so echoey that every footstep sounded loud.
Her voice became echoey as she spoke into the cavernous room.
The recording sounded echoey because it was made in a bathroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: muffled - not loud because of being obstructed in some way
  • synonym: resonant - having a deep, clear, and continuing sound
