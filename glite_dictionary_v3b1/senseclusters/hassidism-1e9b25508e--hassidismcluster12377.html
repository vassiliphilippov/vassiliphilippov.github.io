Hassidism

noun
Jewish mystical movement and its practices
5.4
Pronunciation: / hɑˈsidɪzəm / (AmE), / hæˈsɪdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the religious beliefs and practices of a Jewish movement founded in 18th-century Eastern Europe, emphasizing mysticism, joyful worship, and the spiritual leadership of a rebbe

Examples

Hassidism teachings
He studied the teachings of Hassidism as part of his research into Jewish mysticism.
Many Jews in Eastern Europe were drawn to Hassidism for its emphasis on joyous worship and community.
The principles of Hassidism continue to influence modern Hasidic communities around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hasidic - relating to Hassidism or its followers
  • degree lower: Orthodox Judaism - the branch of Judaism from which Hassidism originates
