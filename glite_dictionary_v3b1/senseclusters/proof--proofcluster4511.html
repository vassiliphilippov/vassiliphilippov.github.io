proof

verb
read and check text for errors
Pronunciation: / pruf /; / pruːf / (AmE), / pruːf / (BrE)
to check or make a preliminary version of something to find mistakes before final production (such as proofreading or making a test print)

Examples

To proof a document
Please proof this article carefully for spelling and punctuation errors.
She was hired to proof the company's annual report before publication.
The editor asked me to proof the manuscript over the weekend.
The photographer will proof the negatives before selecting final prints.
We proofed the etching to check the level of detail in the final print.
You should always proof your typesetting to catch any errors before printing the book.

  • synonym: test - to try something to determine its quality or performance
