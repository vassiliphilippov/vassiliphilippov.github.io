publicize

verb
make widely known; give publicity
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz /; / ˈpʌblɪsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪsʌɪz /; / ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz /; / ˈpʌblɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make something known to the public; to bring something to people's attention

Examples

To publicize an event
She used social media to publicize her art exhibition.
The company decided to publicize its new environmental policy to attract more customers.
The government will publicize the changes in the tax system next week.
Efforts to publicize the fundraising event resulted in a large turnout.
She did not want to publicize her achievements and preferred to remain humble.
The company plans to publicize its new product launch through various media channels.
She decided to publicise her findings in the local newspaper.
The charity event was poorly attended because it wasn't well publicised.
The company plans to publicise the new product through a major advertising campaign.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advertise - to make something known, especially to promote sales
  • synonym: announce - to make a formal public statement about something
  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret; to not make public
