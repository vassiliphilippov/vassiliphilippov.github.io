playability

noun
suitability for play
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpleɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌpleɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a surface, field, or venue that determines if it is suitable or safe for play

Examples

Playability of the field
The referee inspected the playability of the field before allowing the game to start.
Heavy snow affected the playability of the outdoor tennis courts.
Engineers improved the playability of the stadium by installing better drainage systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger or risk
  • antonym: unsuitability - the quality of not being appropriate or fit for a purpose
  • synonym: usability - the quality of being easy or suitable to use
