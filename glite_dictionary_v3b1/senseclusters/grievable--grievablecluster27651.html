grievable

adjective
subject to complaint
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be the subject of a formal complaint or grievance, especially in a workplace or legal context

Examples

Grievable offense
Unfair dismissal is a grievable offense under company policy.
The union argued that the manager's decision was clearly grievable according to the contract.
Not every workplace issue is considered grievable under the current agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-grievable - not eligible to be the subject of a formal complaint
