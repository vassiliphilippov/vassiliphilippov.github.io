smooth muscle

noun
non-striated muscle tissue in organs or vessels
Pronunciation: / smuð ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / smuːð ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a type of muscle tissue that is not striped and works automatically, found in the walls of internal organs and blood vessels

Examples

Smooth muscle tissue
Doctors examined the tissue and confirmed it was composed primarily of smooth muscle cells.
Smooth muscle can be found in the walls of the digestive tract, helping to move food along.
Unlike skeletal muscle, smooth muscle contractions are not under voluntary control.
Smooth muscle controls the movement of food through the intestines.
Blood vessels are lined with smooth muscle that helps regulate blood pressure.
Unlike skeletal muscle, smooth muscle contracts without conscious thought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardiac muscle - muscle tissue of the heart, contracts involuntarily
  • antonym: skeletal muscle - a type of muscle attached to bones, under voluntary control
  • antonym: striated muscle - muscle tissue with visible stripes under a microscope
