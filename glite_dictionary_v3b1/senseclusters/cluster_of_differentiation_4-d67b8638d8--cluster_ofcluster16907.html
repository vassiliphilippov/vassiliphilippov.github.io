cluster of differentiation 4

noun
cell surface glycoprotein on helper T cells
Pronunciation: / ˈklʌstər əv ˌdɪfərɛnʃiˈeɪʃən fɔr / (AmE), / ˈklʌstə əv ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃɪˈeɪʃən fɔː / (BrE)
a glycoprotein mainly found on the surface of helper T cells, serving as a receptor involved in immune response and as a binding site for HIV infection.

Examples

Low cluster of differentiation 4 count
A low cluster of differentiation 4 cell count indicates a weakened immune system in patients with AIDS.
Doctors monitor the levels of cluster of differentiation 4 to assess the progression of HIV infection.
HIV attaches to the cluster of differentiation 4 on helper T cells, allowing the virus to enter and infect the cell.
