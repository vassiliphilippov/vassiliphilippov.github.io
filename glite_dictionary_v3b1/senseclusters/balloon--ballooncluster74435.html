balloon

verb
to become inflated or rapidly increase
4.7 1.67/m
Pronunciation: / b…ôÀàluÀên / (AmE), / b…ôÀàluÀên / (BrE)
(of an object or a quantity) to become swollen, puffed out, or rapidly increase in size or volume, like a balloon being inflated.

Examples

To balloon in size
As the wind caught them, the curtains began to balloon outwards.
Her workload ballooned after her colleague left the company.
Rising prices have caused the budget to balloon in recent years.

Synonyms

  • inflate
    2.9 3.08/m
    inflate means filling something with air or gas, often by people or machines, while balloon means to swell rapidly like being filled.
  • swell
    3.2 7.61/m
    swell means to increase in size, amount, or strength, often from pressure or injury, while balloon means to grow quickly like being inflated.
  • bloat
    4.9 1.13/m
    bloat means to become uncomfortably swollen, often with gas or fluid, while balloon means to expand quickly and does not imply discomfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expand - to increase in size, number, or importance
  • antonym: shrink - become or make smaller in size or amount
