scorch

verb
destroy by fire
5.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /skɔːrtʃ/ (AmE), /skɔːtʃ/ (BrE)
to destroy completely by or as if by fire

Examples

To scorch the forest
A massive wildfire scorched acres of woodland in just a few hours.
The intense heat scorched the earth, leaving nothing but ash behind.
The invading army scorched the entire village as they advanced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - to be damaged, injured, or destroyed by fire or extreme heat
  • synonym: char - to burn or be burned partially so as to blacken the surface
  • antonym: extinguish - to put an end to something, often by using water to stop a fire
