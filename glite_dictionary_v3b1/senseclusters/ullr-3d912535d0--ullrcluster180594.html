Ullr

proper noun
Norse god skilled with bow and skis
Pronunciation: / ˈʊl.ər / (AmE), / ˈʊl.ə / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the Aesir, known for his beauty and skill with the bow and skis; the son of Sif and stepson of Thor.

Examples

Ullr in Norse myths
In the sagas, Ullr is often depicted gliding across snowy fields on his skis.
Legends say that Ullr was unmatched in archery and skiing among the gods.
Some modern pagans celebrate winter festivals in honor of Ullr.
