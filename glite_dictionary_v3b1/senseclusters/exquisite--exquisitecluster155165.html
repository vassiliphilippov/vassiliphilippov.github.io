exquisite

adjective
intensely felt; acute or sharp (of sensation)
5.7 5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskwɪzɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkskwɪzɪt / (BrE)
(of a feeling or sensation) intensely felt; very acute or sharp, often in a positive or negative sense.

Examples

Exquisite pain
She experienced exquisite pain after the surgery, sharper than anything she had felt before.
The chocolate melted on his tongue, bringing exquisite pleasure with every bite.
They described the grief as an exquisite ache, both beautiful and devastating in its intensity.
