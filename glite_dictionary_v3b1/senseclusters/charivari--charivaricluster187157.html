charivari

noun
noisy mock serenade (often for newlyweds)
Pronunciation: / ʃæˈrɪvəri / (AmE), / ʃarɪˈvɑːri / (BrE)
a noisy, mock serenade performed by neighbors or friends—often by banging pans and kettles—traditionally to express disapproval or as a humorous gesture, especially towards newlyweds

Examples

A noisy charivari
A traditional charivari was held to playfully tease the bride and groom.
After the wedding, the couple was surprised by a charivari outside their home.
The villagers organized a charivari for the newlyweds, making a racket with pots and pans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shivaree - noisy mock serenade to a newly married couple (chiefly North American)
