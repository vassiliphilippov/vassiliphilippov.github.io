proactively

adverb
taking initiative
Pronunciation: / proʊˈæktɪvli / (AmE), / prəʊˈæktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that takes action in advance to make things happen, solve problems, or prevent issues before they occur

Examples

To proactively solve problems
She proactively sought feedback from her manager to improve her performance.
The school proactively addressed potential safety issues before any incidents occurred.
By proactively planning his schedule, he avoided last-minute stress and confusion.
The manager encouraged her team to work proactively to solve potential problems before they grew.
By planning proactively, he avoided many of the issues that others faced later.
Students who study proactively often perform better on exams than those who wait until the last minute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actively - in a way that involves taking action or being engaged
  • synonym: preventively - in a way that aims to stop something from happening
  • antonym: reactively - in a way that responds to events after they happen
