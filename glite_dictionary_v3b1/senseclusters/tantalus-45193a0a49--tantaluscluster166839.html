Tantalus

proper noun
mythical Greek king punished in Hades
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtæn.tə.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈtæn.tə.ləs/ (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a wicked king and son of Zeus who was punished in Hades by being made to stand in water that receded when he tried to drink and beneath fruit that receded when he reached for it.

Examples

Tantalus in mythology
In classical literature, Tantalus is often cited as the ultimate example of eternal punishment.
Many artists have depicted the torment of Tantalus in paintings and sculptures.
The story of Tantalus gave rise to the English word 'tantalize.'
