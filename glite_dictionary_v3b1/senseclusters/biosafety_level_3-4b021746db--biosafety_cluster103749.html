biosafety level 3

noun
laboratory safety level for serious airborne infections
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl θri / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈseɪf.ti ˈlɛv.əl θri / (BrE)
A containment protocol designated for laboratories working with infectious agents that may be transmitted by the respiratory route and can cause serious or potentially lethal disease.

Examples

The biosafety level 3 lab
Access to the biosafety level 3 suite is strictly monitored and limited to trained personnel.
All work with the virus must be conducted in a biosafety level 3 laboratory to ensure safety.
Researchers upgraded their facility to meet biosafety level 3 standards before handling the bacteria.
