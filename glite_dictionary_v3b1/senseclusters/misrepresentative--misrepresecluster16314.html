misrepresentative

adjective
giving a false impression
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv / (BrE)
not accurately showing the true situation; giving a false or misleading impression

Examples

Misrepresentative statistics
The statistics in the report were misrepresentative and did not reflect the true situation.
Using outdated information can lead to a misrepresentative analysis of the market.
Her comments were misrepresentative of the team's actual performance.
The survey results were misrepresentative because they only included responses from one neighborhood.
Using a misrepresentative sample can lead to incorrect conclusions about the entire population.
Her comments were misrepresentative of the team's actual opinions and caused confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accurate - correct and without any mistakes
  • synonym: deceptive - intended to make someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym: misleading - giving the wrong idea or impression
  • antonym: representative - typical or characteristic of a group or situation
  • synonym: unrepresentative - not typical or characteristic of a group or situation
