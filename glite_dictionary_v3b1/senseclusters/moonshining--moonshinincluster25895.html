moonshining

noun
foolish ideas
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːnˌʃaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌʃaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
talk, plans, or ideas that are foolish, unrealistic, or not to be taken seriously; nonsense

Examples

Empty moonshining
The committee quickly dismissed his suggestions as pure moonshining with no practical value.
Some people thought her business plan was just moonshining, but she proved them wrong.
Whenever he starts talking about his inventions, it sounds like moonshining to me.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fantasy - imaginative ideas that are not based on reality
  • synonym: nonsense - ideas or talk that are silly or have no meaning
  • antonym: sense - reasonable or practical ideas
