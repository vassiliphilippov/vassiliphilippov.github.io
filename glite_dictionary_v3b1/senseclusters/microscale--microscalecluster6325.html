microscale

noun
very small scale
5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.kroʊ.skeɪl/ /; / /ˈmaɪ.krə.skeɪl/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.krəʊ.skeɪl/ /; / /ˈmaɪ.krə.skeɪl/ (BrE)
a very small scale, especially used in scientific or technical contexts to refer to microscopic dimensions or processes

Examples

Microscale level
Scientists study bacteria at the microscale to understand their behavior in different environments.
The new technology allows engineers to manipulate materials on the microscale for advanced manufacturing.
At the microscale, physical properties can differ greatly from those observed at larger scales.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: macroscale - a larger scale of measurement, visible to the naked eye
  • degree lower: nanoscale - a scale of measurement at the level of nanometers, even smaller than microscale
