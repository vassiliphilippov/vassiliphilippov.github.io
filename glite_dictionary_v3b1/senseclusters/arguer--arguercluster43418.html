arguer

noun
person who argues or debates
3.9 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ër…°ju…ôr / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀê…°ju…ô / (BrE)
a person who engages in argument or debate, often putting forward opinions or reasoning

Examples

A skilled arguer
As a skilled arguer, she often persuaded others to change their minds.
Even in friendly discussions, he could not resist acting as the arguer in the group.
The judge grew impatient with the persistent arguer disrupting the proceedings.

Synonyms

  • debater
    3.7 0.80/m
    a debater takes part in formal arguments and uses rules and topics, while an arguer simply expresses opinions or reasons.
  • disputer
    6.7 0.01/m
    a disputer challenges claims and argues to oppose others, while an arguer may argue more generally to state opinions.
  • disputant
    8.4 0.36/m
    a disputant joins a formal or personal dispute often as an opposing party, while an arguer may simply give reasons or opinions.
  • reasoner
    5.5 0.05/m
    a reasoner thinks and shows logical steps quietly, while an arguer takes part in debate or argues out loud with others.
