deprogram

verb
to reverse indoctrination
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈproʊɡræm /; / diːˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈprəʊɡræm /; / diːˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
to help someone abandon beliefs or behaviors learned in a cult or through intense indoctrination

Examples

To deprogram a cult member
Specialists were brought in to deprogram members who had recently left the extremist group.
It can take months to deprogram someone from deeply held cult beliefs.
The organization offers support to families trying to deprogram loved ones from radical ideologies.
The therapist tried to deprogram the young man after he left the cult.
Specialists are trained to deprogram individuals who have been exposed to extremist groups.
It can take months to fully deprogram someone from deeply held beliefs.
The therapist worked to deprogram the young man after he left the cult.
Specialists are sometimes called in to deprogram individuals who have been heavily indoctrinated.
It can take months to fully deprogram someone from extreme beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brainwash - to force someone to adopt new beliefs, often through pressure
  • antonym: indoctrinate - to teach someone to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • synonym: rehabilitate - to help someone return to normal life or beliefs after a period of difficulty
