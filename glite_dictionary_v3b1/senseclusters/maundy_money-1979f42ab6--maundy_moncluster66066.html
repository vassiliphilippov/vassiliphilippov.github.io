Maundy money

noun
special British coins given on Maundy Thursday
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔndi ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈmɔːndi ˌmʌni / (BrE)
special silver coins minted and distributed by the British monarch on Maundy Thursday as part of a royal ceremony

Examples

Special Maundy money
Collectors prize Maundy money for its historical significance and silver content.
Each year, the Queen distributes Maundy money to elderly recipients in a different cathedral.
The tradition of giving Maundy money dates back to the reign of King Charles II.
