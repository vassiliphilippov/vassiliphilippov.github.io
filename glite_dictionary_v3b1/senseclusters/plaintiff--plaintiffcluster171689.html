plaintiff

noun
person initiating a lawsuit
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪn.tɪf / (AmE), / ˈpleɪn.tɪf / (BrE)
a person who brings a case against another in a court of law

Examples

The plaintiff in court
After several months in court, the plaintiff finally received compensation.
In this lawsuit, the plaintiff must present sufficient evidence to prove the allegations.
The plaintiff claimed that her property had been damaged by negligence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - person being sued or accused in court
