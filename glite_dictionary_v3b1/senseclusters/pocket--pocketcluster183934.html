pocket

verb
put something into one's pocket
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːkɪt /; / ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
to put something into your pocket or to take something secretly

Examples

To pocket the money
He quickly pocketed the coin before anyone noticed.
She discreetly pocketed the note her friend passed to her.
The student pocketed his phone after receiving a message.
He quietly pocketed the pen when no one was looking.
She was caught trying to pocket a few coins from the cash register.
The shoplifter managed to pocket several watches before leaving the store.
