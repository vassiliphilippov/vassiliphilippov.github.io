corpus geniculatum laterale

noun
neural processing station for vision in the brain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔrpəs dʒəˌnɪkjəˈleɪtəm ləˈtɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpəs dʒɪˌnɪkjʊˈleɪtəm læˈtɪərəli / (BrE)
a structure in the thalamus of the brain that acts as a relay station for visual information from the retina to the occipital lobe of the cerebral cortex

The corpus geniculatum laterale plays a crucial role in visual signal processing.
