merchant-venturer

noun
merchant engaged in risky or overseas trade
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜrtʃənt vɛnˈtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tʃənt vɛnˈtʃʊərə / (BrE)
a merchant who undertakes a trading venture, especially one involving overseas trade or significant risk

Examples

A merchant-venturer overseas
During the 17th century, the merchant-venturer played a vital role in expanding trade routes to the Americas.
He aspired to become a successful merchant-venturer like his father before him.
The risks taken by a merchant-venturer were often great, but so were the rewards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trader - a person who buys and sells goods, currency, or stocks
