chitosan

noun
biodegradable medical material
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪtəˌsæn / (AmE), / ˈkɪtəsæn / (BrE)
a natural, biodegradable substance derived from chitin (usually from shellfish), used in medical, scientific, and industrial applications such as wound dressings and as a polymer

Examples

Chitosan bandage
The doctor recommended a chitosan bandage to help the wound heal faster.
Researchers are developing new drug-delivery systems using chitosan for better absorption.
Because chitosan is biodegradable, it is often chosen for environmentally friendly medical products.
Researchers are developing new wound dressings using chitosan for its natural healing properties.
Many water purification systems use chitosan to remove impurities safely and effectively.
The biodegradable packaging was made from chitosan extracted from shrimp shells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cellulose - a natural polysaccharide found in plant cell walls
  • synonym: chitin - a natural polymer found in the shells of crustaceans and insects
