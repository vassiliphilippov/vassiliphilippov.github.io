investigational

adjective
under study or testing
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvɛstəˈɡeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɛstɪˈɡeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the testing, study, or examination of something new, especially in science or medicine

Examples

Investigational drug
The investigational vaccine is currently being evaluated in several countries.
Doctors discussed the risks and benefits of using an investigational treatment for the rare disease.
Patients can only access the investigational drug through a clinical trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approved - officially accepted or authorized
  • synonym: experimental - being tested or tried out
