base

noun
principal ingredient of a mixture
9.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /beɪs/ (AmE), /beɪs/ (BrE)
the main or most important part, ingredient, or starting point of something (such as a mixture, idea, or development)

Examples

Base ingredient
Everything she cooked seemed to have rice as the base.
Glycerinated gelatin is used as a base for many ointments.
He told the painter that he wanted a yellow base with just a hint of green.
A strong technological base is essential for economic growth.
Japan's manufacturing base supports its global exports.
The country's industrial base has expanded rapidly over the last decade.
The entire theory relies on a strong base of scientific evidence.
Their friendship formed the base for a successful business partnership.
Without a solid base of facts, the conclusions were unconvincing.
