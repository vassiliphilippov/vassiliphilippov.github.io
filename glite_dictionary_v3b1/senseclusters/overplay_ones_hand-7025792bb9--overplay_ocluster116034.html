overplay one's hand

verb
act too boldly and lose advantage
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚˈpleɪ wʌnz hænd / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈpleɪ wʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to act with excessive confidence or aggressiveness, taking too many risks, and thus undermining one's own position or advantage

Examples

To overplay one's hand in business
Investors overplayed their hand by expecting too much, and the opportunity slipped away.
She overplayed her hand in the negotiations and ended up with a worse deal than she started.
When he demanded a higher salary, he overplayed his hand and lost the job offer entirely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overreach - to fail by trying to do or achieve too much
  • synonym: push one's luck - to take more risks than is wise, risking loss of gains
