vicariousness

noun
indirect experience
8.2
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈkɛriəsnəs / (AmE), / vɪˈkeəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of experiencing or feeling something indirectly through another person or on their behalf

Examples

A vicariousness feeling
He lived with a sense of vicariousness as he followed his daughter's adventures abroad.
The movie gave her a feeling of vicariousness, as if she were living the story herself.
Some people seek vicariousness by reading about the lives of famous athletes.
Her vicariousness allowed her to feel proud of her friend's achievements as if they were her own.
The manager's vicariousness was clear when he accepted the award for the entire team.
Through books and movies, people often experience vicariousness by living through the characters' adventures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directness - the quality of experiencing something firsthand
  • synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share another person's feelings
  • synonym: representation - the act of speaking or acting on behalf of someone
  • synonym: substitution - the act of taking the place of someone or something else
