ether

noun
the fifth element in ancient and medieval science
8.6 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.θər/ (AmE), /ˈiː.θə/ (BrE)
the clear upper air or sky; historically, the fifth and highest element believed to fill the universe beyond the terrestrial sphere and compose the heavenly bodies.

Examples

Heavenly ether
In Aristotle’s cosmology, ether was thought to make up the stars and planets.
Medieval scholars debated the properties of ether as the purest substance in the universe.
The concept of ether as a celestial element is now considered obsolete by modern science.
The ancient Greeks believed the gods lived in the aether above the clouds.
Poets often describe the stars shining in the silent aether of night.
Legends say that souls ascend into the aether after leaving the earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ether - the upper regions of space or sky in ancient and poetic use
  • synonym: sky - the region of the atmosphere and outer space seen from the earth
