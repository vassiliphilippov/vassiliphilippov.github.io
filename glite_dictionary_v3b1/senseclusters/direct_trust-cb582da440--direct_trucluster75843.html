direct trust

noun
trust created deliberately by parties
2.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈrɛkt trʌst / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt trʌst / (BrE)
A trust created intentionally by the act of the parties involved, often documented in writing, rather than imposed by law.

Examples

A direct trust agreement
Her estate plan includes a direct trust to ensure her assets are distributed according to her wishes.
The family set up a direct trust to manage the inheritance for their children.
Unlike an implied trust, a direct trust is established through clear, deliberate action and documentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructive trust - a trust imposed by law to address wrongdoing or prevent unjust enrichment
