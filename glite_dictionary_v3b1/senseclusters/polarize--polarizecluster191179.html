polarize

verb
become divided into opposing groups
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊ.ləˌraɪz /; / ˈpoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ləˌraɪz /; / ˈpəʊləraɪz /; / ˈpəʊ.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
to cause people, groups, or opinions to divide into two opposing sides

Examples

Polarize public opinion
Political issues can quickly polarize communities, making compromise difficult.
Social media tends to polarize people by exposing them mainly to similar viewpoints.
The debate over climate change has begun to polarize public opinion.
Her controversial statements began to polarize the community, forcing people to take sides.
Social media can polarize public opinion by amplifying extreme viewpoints.
The debate over climate change continues to polarize scientists and politicians alike.
Social media can quickly polarise opinions and escalates conflicts between groups.
The debate over the new policy began to polarise the community, creating two opposing camps.
The election results tended to polarise voters along political lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups, especially causing disagreement
  • antonym: unify - bring together or make into a single group
  • antonym: unite - to come together or make things join into a single entity
