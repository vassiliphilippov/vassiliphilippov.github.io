liberalization

noun
the process of making less strict
3.3 6.7
Pronunciation: / lɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪbəraɪləˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making rules, laws, or controls less strict, especially in economic or political contexts

Examples

The liberalization of rules
Many citizens have benefited from the liberalization of travel restrictions.

The government announced a policy of liberalization to encourage economic growth.
Economic liberalisation has led to rapid growth in several sectors.
Many citizens welcomed the liberalisation of the press, which brought greater freedom of expression.
The government announced the liberalisation of trade policies to encourage foreign investment.
