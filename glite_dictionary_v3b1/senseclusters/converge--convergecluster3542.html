converge

verb
(math) approach a limit as terms increase
7.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝrdʒ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(of a sequence, series, or function) to approach a definite limit as more terms are added or as a variable approaches a certain value.

Examples

A series converges to a limit
If the infinite series converges, its sum will approach a finite number.
The function converges to zero as x approaches infinity.
Their model proved that the iterative process converges rapidly under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diverge - (math) to fail to approach a limit as terms increase
