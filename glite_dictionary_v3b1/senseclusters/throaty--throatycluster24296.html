throaty

adjective
having a deep, husky, low sound
3.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθroʊ.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈθrəʊ.tiː/ (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) sounding as if produced low in the throat; deep and husky.

Examples

Throaty laugh
He greeted us with a throaty laugh that echoed through the room.
She sang with a throaty voice that captivated the audience.
The engine made a deep, throaty rumble as the car accelerated.
