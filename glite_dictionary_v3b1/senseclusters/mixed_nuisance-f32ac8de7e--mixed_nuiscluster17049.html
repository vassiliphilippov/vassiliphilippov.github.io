mixed nuisance

noun
a legal nuisance affecting public and private interests
Pronunciation: / mɪkst ˈnuːsəns / (AmE), / mɪkst ˈnjuːsəns / (BrE)
A legal nuisance that constitutes both a public nuisance and a private nuisance simultaneously.

Examples

A mixed nuisance case
Legal disputes involving a mixed nuisance often require balancing competing public and private interests.
The court addressed the issue as a mixed nuisance, since it affected public spaces and private property.
The factory was found liable for creating a mixed nuisance that harmed both local residents and the wider community.

  • degree lower: public nuisance - a nuisance affecting the public at large
