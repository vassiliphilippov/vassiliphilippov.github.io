polonium

noun
a radioactive metallic chemical element
6.4
Pronunciation: / pəˈloʊniəm / (AmE), / pəˈləʊniəm / (BrE)
a rare radioactive metallic element similar to tellurium and bismuth, found in uranium ores and produced by bombarding bismuth with neutrons.

Examples

Polonium radioactive element
Polonium was first identified by Marie Curie during her groundbreaking research on radioactivity.
Because of its intense radioactivity, polonium must be handled with extreme caution in specialized labs.
The scientists detected traces of polonium in the contaminated laboratory equipment.
