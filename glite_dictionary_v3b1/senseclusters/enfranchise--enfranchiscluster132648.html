enfranchise

verb
grant the right to vote
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfrænˌtʃaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
to give someone the right to vote in an election; to grant voting rights

Examples

To enfranchise voters
After years of activism, lawmakers decided to enfranchise all citizens regardless of race or gender.
Efforts to enfranchise women in the early twentieth century changed the course of history.
The new constitution aimed to enfranchise previously excluded groups within the population.
