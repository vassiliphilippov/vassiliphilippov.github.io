contractive

adjective
reducing economic activity
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
causing or intended to cause a reduction in economic activity, growth, or size, especially in reference to policies or measures.

Examples

Contractive economic policy
The government implemented contractive fiscal policies to address the rising inflation rate.
Analysts warned that contractive measures could lead to higher unemployment in the short term.
A contractive approach was necessary to stabilize the overheated economy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expansive - encouraging growth or increase, especially in economic activity
  • synonym: restrictive - limiting or controlling something
