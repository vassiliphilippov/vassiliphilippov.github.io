profits

noun
financial gain after expenses
2.3 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêf…™ts / (AmE), / Ààpr…íf…™ts / (BrE)
the money that is left after deducting all costs, expenses, and taxes from total revenue within a given period

Examples

Company profits
After deducting all expenses, the bakery reported healthy profits for the year.
Many investors are interested in companies with strong quarterly profits.
The company's profits rose significantly after introducing the new product line.

Synonyms

  • net profit
    3.2 0.96/m
    net profit shows the amount left after all expenses and taxes for a period, while profits names that remaining money.
  • net income
    3.0 1.75/m
    net income reports the total left after deductions and taxes over a period, while profits refers to remaining business money.
  • profit
    2.0 74/m
    profit names the financial gain from an activity, while profits refers to the total remaining money over a period.
  • earnings
    2.3 33/m
    earnings are money received from work, business, or investments, while profits are the leftover business money after all costs and taxes.
  • net
    5.6 21/m
    net is the amount remaining after deductions like depreciation or costs, while profits mean the final money left after all taxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earnings - money obtained from work or investments, often used as a synonym for profits
  • antonym: losses - the amount by which expenses or costs exceed revenue
