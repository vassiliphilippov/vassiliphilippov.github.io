straphanger

noun
commuter using public transportation
Pronunciation: / ˈstræpˌhæŋər / (AmE), / ˈstræpˌhæŋə / (BrE)
a person who travels by public transportation, especially one who stands and holds onto a strap or handrail

Examples

A straphanger on train
After moving to the city, she quickly became a regular straphanger on the commuter train.
Every morning, hundreds of straphangers pack into the subway car.
The mayor promised better services for city straphangers during the winter months.
During rush hour, dozens of straphangers swayed back and forth with the train's motion.
He became a daily straphanger, getting used to the standing-room-only commute.
The subway was so crowded that I had to ride as a straphanger all the way downtown.
