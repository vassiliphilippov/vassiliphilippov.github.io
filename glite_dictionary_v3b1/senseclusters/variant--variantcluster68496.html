variant

noun
a random or variable quantity
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / ˈveəriənt / (BrE)
a quantity or value that can change and is subject to randomness, especially in mathematics or statistics

Examples

A random variant
In probability theory, each variant can take a different value depending on random events.
The results depend on the values of several variants in the equation.
When measuring temperatures, the daily high is a variant that fluctuates with weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: variable - a quantity that may assume any of a set of values
