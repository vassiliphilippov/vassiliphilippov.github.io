outgroup

noun
reference group in biology
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈaʊt.gruːp/ (AmE), /ˈaʊt.gruːp/ (BrE)
a group of organisms used as a reference in phylogenetic analysis, assumed to be outside the main group being studied

Examples

Reference outgroup
Scientists chose a distant species as the outgroup to help determine the evolutionary relationships.
Including an outgroup in the analysis allowed researchers to identify the root of the tree.
The outgroup serves as a reference point for comparing traits among related species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ingroup - the main group of organisms being studied in phylogenetic analysis
