precheck

verb
to check in advance
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
to check or review something in advance before an official process or event

Examples

To precheck documents
Please precheck your documents before submitting them to avoid any mistakes.
It is a good idea to precheck the equipment before the meeting starts.
The airline asks passengers to precheck their bags online to save time at the airport.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preview - to look at or inspect something before it is shown or used
