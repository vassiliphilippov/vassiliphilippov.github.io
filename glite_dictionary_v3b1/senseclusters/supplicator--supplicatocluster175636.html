supplicator

noun
formal petitioner
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who formally petitions or pleads to an authority, especially seeking legal or official action

Examples

Courtroom supplicator
The supplicator addressed the court with a carefully prepared statement.
As a supplicator, she hoped her plea would lead to a change in the law.
The council listened patiently as the supplicator presented his case for review.

Related Terms

  • synonym: petitioner - a person who presents a formal request to an authority
  • antonym: respondent - a person who answers or responds to a petition or legal action
