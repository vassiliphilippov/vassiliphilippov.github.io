grade school

noun
elementary school for young children
1.4 3.08/m
Pronunciation: / …°re…™d skuÀêl / (AmE), / …°re…™d skuÀêl / (BrE)
A school for young children, usually covering the first six to eight years of formal education.

Examples

A grade school building
After moving to a new city, she made friends quickly at her new grade school.
My son started first grade at the local grade school last fall.
The annual grade school science fair attracts students from all over the district.

Synonyms

  • elementary school
    1.0 15/m
    elementary school is the common US name for the first grades, while grade school is a slightly older, less formal term.
  • primary school
    1.0 2.15/m
    primary school is used widely outside the US for early years, while grade school is the US term for those first grades.
  • grammar school
    1.1 0.05/m
    grammar school is an older or regional name often with different meanings, while grade school means the general first six to eight years.
