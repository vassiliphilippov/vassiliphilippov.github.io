commit

verb
send someone to prison or hospital by order
8.1 7.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send someone officially to a place, especially a prison or mental hospital, usually by legal order

Examples

To commit someone to hospital
After his breakdown, the court decided to commit him to a psychiatric hospital for treatment.
Her family had to commit her after she became a danger to herself.
Many activists protested when the government moved to commit the accused to prison without trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confine - to keep someone in a closed space, often by force or order
  • antonym: release - to allow someone to leave prison or hospital
