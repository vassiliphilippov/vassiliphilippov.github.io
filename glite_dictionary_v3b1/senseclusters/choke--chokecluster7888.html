choke

noun
coil that blocks AC but passes DC
Pronunciation: / tʃoʊk / (AmE), / tʃəʊk / (BrE)
an electrical component consisting of a coil with low resistance and high inductance, used to allow direct current to pass while blocking or reducing alternating current

Examples

Electrical choke coil
A choke is crucial for preventing alternating current from reaching sensitive components.
If your audio system hums, adding a choke to the power line might solve the problem.
The technician installed a choke to eliminate unwanted interference in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capacitor - an electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field and affects alternating and direct current differently
  • synonym: inductor - a passive electrical component that stores energy in a magnetic field when electric current flows through it
