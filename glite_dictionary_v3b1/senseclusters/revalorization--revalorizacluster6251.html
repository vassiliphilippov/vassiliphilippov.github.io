the act of increasing the value of money, assets, or payments, especially by official decision or to adjust for inflation

Examples

Currency revalorization
The central bank announced a revalorization to strengthen the national currency against the dollar.
After years of inflation, the government implemented a revalorization of public sector salaries.
Experts debated the possible effects of a sudden revalorization on the export market.
The government announced a revalorization of pensions to help retirees cope with inflation.
Annual revalorization of wages is necessary to maintain workers' purchasing power.
Many unions are negotiating for the revalorization of salaries in response to rising prices.

Synonyms

  • valorization
    9.3 0.05/m
    valorization assigns or raises value through policy or markets, while revalorization increases value again often by official decision to adjust for inflation.
  • transvaluation
    9.9 0.00/m
    transvaluation is an official change in the monetary value of a currency or asset, while revalorization raises value to offset inflation by authorities.
  • revaluation
    6.3 0.46/m
    revaluation is a new appraisal that may raise or lower worth, while revalorization specifically increases monetary or asset value by official action.
