horse balm

noun
strong-scented perennial herb, eastern US
Pronunciation: / hɔrs bɑlm / (AmE), / hɔːs bɑːm / (BrE)
A strong-scented perennial herb with serrated pointed leaves and a loose cluster of yellowish flowers, native to the eastern United States.

Examples

A horse balm plant
After the rain, a patch of horse balm brightened the shaded edge of the trail.
Gardeners often plant horse balm for its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers.
The woods were filled with the distinct fragrance of horse balm in late spring.
