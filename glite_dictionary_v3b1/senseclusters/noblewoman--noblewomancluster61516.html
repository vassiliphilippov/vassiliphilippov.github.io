noblewoman

noun
aristocratic woman; female member of the nobility
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊ.bəlˌwʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.bəlˌwʊ.mən / (BrE)
A woman who belongs to the nobility, especially a titled member of the aristocracy in Britain or other countries.

Examples

An English noblewoman
A portrait of an English noblewoman from the 18th century hangs in the grand hall.
In the novel, a young noblewoman rebels against the expectations of her family and society.
The noblewoman attended the royal ceremony in an elegant gown befitting her status.

  • antonym: nobleman - a man belonging to the nobility
