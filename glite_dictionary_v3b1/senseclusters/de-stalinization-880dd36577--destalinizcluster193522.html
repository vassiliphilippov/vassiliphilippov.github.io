The process of eliminating the influence of Joseph Stalin by changing policies, removing monuments, and renaming places previously dedicated to him.

Examples

De-Stalinization process
As part of de-Stalinization, many cities and streets that bore his name were renamed.
Monuments to Stalin were removed during the period of de-Stalinization in the 1950s and 1960s.
The country's leadership began de-Stalinization by releasing political prisoners and revising oppressive laws.
After the leader's death, a wave of de-Stalinisation swept through the country, changing many aspects of public life.
Historians often debate the extent to which de-Stalinisation achieved real political reform.
The removal of statues was a prominent feature of de-Stalinisation in Eastern Europe.
