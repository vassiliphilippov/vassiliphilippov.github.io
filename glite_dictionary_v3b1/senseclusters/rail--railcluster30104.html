rail

verb
protest or criticize angrily
7.5 7.1
Pronunciation: / reɪl / (AmE), / reɪl / (BrE)
to complain or protest angrily or bitterly about something

Examples

To rail against injustice
Political commentators railed against the government's decision in yesterday's news segment.
She often rails at the injustices she sees in the workplace.
The protesters railed against the new law in front of the parliament building.
Many citizens railed at the government's decision to cut funding for education.
She would often rail against the unfair treatment of workers during her speeches.
Throughout the meeting, he kept railing about the new policy changes.
Many citizens have been railing about the poor state of public transportation.
She railed at the unfairness of the new policy during the meeting.
The editor often rails against government corruption in his op-eds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denounce - to publicly declare to be wrong or evil
  • degree lower: grumble - to complain about something in a bad-tempered way
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
  • synonym: rant - to speak or shout at length in an angry, impassioned way
  • synonym: vilify - to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
