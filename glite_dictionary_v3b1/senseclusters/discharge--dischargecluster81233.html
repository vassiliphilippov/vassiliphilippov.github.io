discharge

verb
relieve from duty or obligation
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to officially release someone from a duty, service, or obligation

Examples

To discharge a patient
Once the debt was paid, she was discharged from her legal responsibilities.
The company discharged him from all further obligations after the contract ended.
The court discharged the jury after the verdict was delivered.
After serving for twenty years, he was discharged from the army with full honors.
She hopes to be discharged from active duty later this year.
Upon completion of his enlistment, he was formally discharged and returned home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlist - join the armed forces
  • antonym: oblige - to require someone to do something, often used in legal contexts
  • synonym: relieve - to free from a duty or responsibility
