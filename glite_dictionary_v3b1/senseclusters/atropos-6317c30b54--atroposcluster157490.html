Atropos

proper noun
Greek goddess of death's fate
Pronunciation: / ˈætrəˌpɒs / (AmE), / ˈætrəpɒs / (BrE)
in Greek mythology, the goddess of fate who cuts the thread of life, determining the moment of death

Examples

Atropos cuts thread
In the myth, Atropos holds the shears that end mortal lives.
Some tragedies reference Atropos as the final arbiter of human destiny.
The poet compared fate to Atropos, cold and unyielding.
