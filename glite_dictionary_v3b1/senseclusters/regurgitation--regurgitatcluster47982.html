regurgitation

noun
backflow of blood in the heart
Pronunciation: / rɪˌɡɝːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌɡɜːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the backward flow of blood through a defective heart valve

Examples

Heart valve regurgitation
Mitral valve regurgitation can lead to heart failure if left untreated.
Proper treatment can help manage symptoms of blood regurgitation in heart patients.
The doctor diagnosed him with a mild case of aortic regurgitation.
