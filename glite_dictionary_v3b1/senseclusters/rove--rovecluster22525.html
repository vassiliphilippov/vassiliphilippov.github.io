rove

verb
move about aimlessly; wander
5.9 5.2
Pronunciation: /roʊv/ (AmE), /rəʊv/ (BrE)
to travel or move about aimlessly or without a fixed destination, often in search of something

Examples

To rove around
As a young man, he roved from job to job, never settling in one place.
The wolves rove through the forest in search of prey during the night.
They spent months roving across Europe, never staying in one town for long.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roam - to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically, especially over a wide area
  • antonym: settle - to stay in one place and not move around
  • synonym: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
