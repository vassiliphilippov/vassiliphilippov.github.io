secretory phase

noun
stage of menstrual cycle after ovulation
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkriːtəˌri feɪz / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtəri feɪz / (BrE)
the phase in the menstrual cycle, following ovulation, when the corpus luteum secretes progesterone to prepare the endometrium for potential implantation of an embryo

Examples

The secretory phase of cycle
A biopsy revealed changes in the endometrial tissue consistent with the secretory phase.
During the secretory phase, progesterone levels rise to prepare the uterus for a fertilized egg.
If pregnancy does not occur, the secretory phase ends with the onset of menstruation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proliferative phase - the phase before ovulation when the endometrium is rebuilt
