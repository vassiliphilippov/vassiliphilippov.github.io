gas chamber

noun
sealed room for execution by poison gas
1.7 7.8
Pronunciation: / ɡæs ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
A sealed room or enclosure into which poisonous gas is pumped, used as a method of execution for people or animals.

Examples

A gas chamber room
During World War II, many victims perished in gas chambers constructed in concentration camps.
The condemned prisoner was executed in the gas chamber after his appeals were denied.
The old gas chamber at the prison is now part of a historical exhibit.
