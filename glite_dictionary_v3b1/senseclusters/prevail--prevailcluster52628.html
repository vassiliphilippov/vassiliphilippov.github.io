prevail

verb
be dominant or widespread
Pronunciation: / prəˈveɪl /; / prɪˈveɪl / (AmE), / prɪˈveɪl / (BrE)
to be more powerful, influential, or to win in the end; to be dominant or widespread

Examples

Good ideas prevail
A sense of optimism prevailed among the team despite earlier setbacks.
Although new technologies are emerging, traditional methods still prevail in many rural areas.
In the heated debate, reason and civility did not prevail over anger.
After hours of debate, reason finally prevailed among the group.
Despite many obstacles, the team managed to prevail and secure the championship.
In the courtroom, justice must prevail above all else.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have control or power over
  • antonym: lose - to be defeated in a contest or struggle
  • synonym: predominate - to be the largest in number or have more influence
  • synonym: triumph - to achieve a victory or success
  • antonym: yield - to give way or be overcome by others
