horse nettle

noun
prickly weed with pale yellow flowers and yellow berries
9.1
Pronunciation: / hɔrs ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈnɛtl / (BrE)
a coarse, prickly weed with pale yellow flowers and yellow, berrylike fruit, commonly found in the southern and eastern United States

Examples

Horse nettle plant
Be careful when walking through the fields—horse nettle can cause skin irritation if touched.
Farmers often remove horse nettle to prevent it from spreading among their crops.
The bright yellow berries on the horse nettle looked tempting, but I knew they were toxic.
