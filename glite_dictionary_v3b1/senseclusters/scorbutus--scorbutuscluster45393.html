scorbutus

noun
archaic term for scurvy
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: /skɑːr.ˈbjuː.təs/ (AmE), /skɔː.ˈbjuː.təs/ (BrE)
an old term for scurvy, a condition caused by deficiency of vitamin C, leading to symptoms such as bleeding gums and weakness

Examples

Old scorbutus disease

The physician recognized the symptoms of scorbutus among the malnourished patients.
Treating scorbutus effectively requires the administration of vitamin C-rich foods.
