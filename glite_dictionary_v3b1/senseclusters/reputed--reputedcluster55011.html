reputed

adjective
generally believed
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpjutɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈpjuːtɪd / (BrE)
generally believed or said to be a particular kind of person or to have a particular quality, often without definite proof

Examples

The reputed owner
He is the reputed owner of several successful restaurants in the city.
The reputed cause of the problem has not been confirmed by experts.
She comes from a reputed family known for their charitable work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actual - existing in fact; real
  • synonym: alleged - said to have happened or to be true, but not proven
