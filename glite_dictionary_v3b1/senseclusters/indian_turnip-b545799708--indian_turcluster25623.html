Indian turnip

noun
North American woodland plant (Arisaema triphyllum)
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈtɜrnɪp / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈtɜːnɪp / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant (Arisaema triphyllum) with sheathing leaves, a club-shaped spadix, and a hood-like spathe, producing clusters of scarlet berries.

Examples

The Indian turnip plant
Gardeners appreciate the unique appearance of Indian turnip in shady woodlands.
The berries of the Indian turnip are a bright scarlet in late summer.
We came across a patch of Indian turnip while hiking through the forest.
