shadow

noun
dark area caused by blocked light
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
a dark area or shape produced by an object blocking light; shade with well-defined edges cast on a surface

Examples

Shadow on the wall
A tree cast a long shadow on the grass as the sun set.
He stood in the shadow of the building to escape the heat.
The shadow of the bird flickered across the wall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflection - an image seen in a mirror or shiny surface
  • synonym: shade - relative darkness caused by shelter from direct light
