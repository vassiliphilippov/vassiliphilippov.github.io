leaven

verb
cause dough to rise with leaven
9.4 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈle.vən/ (AmE), /ˈle.vən/ (BrE)
to cause (dough or batter) to rise and become light by adding a leavening agent such as yeast or baking powder

Examples

To leaven bread
Add yeast to leaven the dough before baking.
Bakers often leaven bread to achieve a soft, airy texture.
If you forget to leaven the batter, the cake will turn out dense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unleavened - not leavened; made without a raising agent
