make hard work of

verb
complicate a task unnecessarily
1.1
Pronunciation: / meɪk hɑrd wɝk ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk hɑːd wɜːk ɒv / (BrE)
to make a task more difficult than it needs to be, often by approaching it inefficiently or overcomplicating it

You don’t have to make hard work of this; just follow the instructions step by step.
