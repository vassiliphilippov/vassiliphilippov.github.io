Carthamus

noun
genus of plants including safflower
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːr.θə.məs/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.θə.məs/ (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, best known for the species Carthamus tinctorius (safflower), cultivated for its oil and dye.

Examples

Carthamus has long been valued for its use in producing natural dyes.
Botanists are studying the drought resistance of various Carthamus species.
The genus Carthamus includes several species, but Carthamus tinctorius is the most widely grown.
