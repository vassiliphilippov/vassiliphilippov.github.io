rush off

verb
leave suddenly in a hurry
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /rʌʃ ɔːf/ /; / /rʌʃ ɑːf/ (AmE), /rʌʃ ɒf/ /; / /rʌʃ ɔːf/ (BrE)
to leave or depart suddenly and in a hurry, often without proper preparation or notice.

Examples

To rush off quickly
He rushed off without even saying goodbye.
She had to rush off to catch the last train home.
Sorry to rush off like this, but I have a meeting in ten minutes.
