esprit de corps

noun
group spirit, morale, or shared pride
7.8 6.3
Pronunciation: / ɛsˌprit də ˈkɔr /; / ɛˌsprit də ˈkɔr / (AmE), / ɛsˌpriː də ˈkɔː /; / ɛˌspriː də ˈkɔː / (BrE)
the feeling of pride, loyalty, and unity shared by members of a group that motivates them to work together and achieve something

Examples

Strong esprit de corps
Military units often develop a strong esprit de corps that helps them face challenges together.
Our company's esprit de corps has been essential to overcoming difficult times.
The team's esprit de corps was evident in the way they supported each other during the competition.
A strong esprit de corps kept the volunteers motivated throughout the difficult project.
Military units rely heavily on esprit de corps to operate effectively in challenging situations.
The company's annual retreat is intended to strengthen esprit de corps among employees.

  • synonym: morale - the confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time
