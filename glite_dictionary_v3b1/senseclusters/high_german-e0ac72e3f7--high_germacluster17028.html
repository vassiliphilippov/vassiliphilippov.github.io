High German

noun
the standard form of the German language
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈdʒɝː.mən /; / haɪ ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / haɪ ˈdʒɜː.mən /; / haɪ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the standard form of the German language, developed from West Germanic dialects and used as the official language of Germany, Austria, and parts of Switzerland

Examples

High German language
Luther's Bible translation helped shape High German as the standardized written language of Germany.
Many Swiss people speak a dialect at home but use High German in formal settings.
The university offers courses in both Bavarian dialect and High German.
Many Swiss people speak both Swiss German and High German fluently.
She studied High German at university before traveling to Berlin.
The book is written in High German, making it accessible throughout Germany.

Related Terms

  • synonym: German - the language spoken in Germany and parts of Austria and Switzerland
  • antonym: Low German - a West Germanic language spoken mainly in northern Germany
