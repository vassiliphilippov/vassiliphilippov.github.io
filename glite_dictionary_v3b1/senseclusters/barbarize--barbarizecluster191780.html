barbarize

verb
make crude or savage
8.5 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːr.bə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈbɑː.bə.raɪz/ (BrE)
To make crude, savage, or uncivilized in behavior or speech.

Examples

To barbarize a society
Centuries of war threatened to barbarize even the most advanced societies.
Some argue that constant violence in media can barbarize viewers, desensitizing them to real suffering.
The corrupt regime barbarized its people through years of oppression and brutality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civilize - to make more cultured or refined
