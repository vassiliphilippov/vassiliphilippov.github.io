Doctor of the Church

noun
title for eminent Catholic theologians and saints
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktər əv ðə tʃɝrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ðə tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a title given to certain saints who have made significant contributions to theology or doctrine through their teachings and writings.

Examples

Doctor of the Church title
St. Augustine is recognized as a Doctor of the Church for his influential theological writings.
Teresa of Ávila was declared a Doctor of the Church in 1970, one of the few women to receive this title.
The works of the Doctors of the Church are still studied by theologians today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saint - a holy person recognized by the Church
  • synonym: theologian - an expert in theology, especially within Christianity
