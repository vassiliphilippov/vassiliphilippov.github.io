loft

noun
large open upper floor in a building
Pronunciation: / lɔft /; / lɑːft / (AmE), / lɒft / (BrE)
an upper floor or open space just below the roof of a building, often used for living or storage

Examples

A modern loft
Many modern lofts are desired for their high ceilings and industrial character.
The artist’s loft in the old warehouse was full of natural light and open space.
They converted the top floor into a spacious loft that served as both a studio and an apartment.
After converting the loft, they gained an extra bedroom for guests.
The kids love playing in the loft during rainy afternoons.
We keep our old furniture in the loft above the garage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attic - a room or space directly under the roof of a house
