magic square

noun
square array of numbers with equal sums in rows, columns, and diagonals
7.0 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmædʒ.ɪk skweər/ (AmE), /ˈmædʒ.ɪk skweə/ (BrE)
A square array of numbers arranged so that the sums of the numbers in each row, column, and main diagonal are all the same.

Examples

A magic square grid
A 3x3 magic square has the numbers 1 to 9 arranged so every row, column, and diagonal totals 15.
The ancient Chinese were fascinated by the magic square and used it in their art and architecture.
The puzzle challenged us to create a magic square using only consecutive numbers.
