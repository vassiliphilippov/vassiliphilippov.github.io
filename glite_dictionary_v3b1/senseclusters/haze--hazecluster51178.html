haze

verb
to become hazy or cloudy
4.7 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / heÉªz / (AmE), / heÉªz / (BrE)
(of a substance, view, or surface) to become hazy, dull, or cloudy.

Examples

Windows haze over
Early morning fog can cause the city skyline to haze until the sun rises.
Humidity and dust can haze the view from the mountain top.
The glass will haze if not cleaned regularly, making it hard to see through.

Synonyms

  • fog
    5.2 0.35/m
    fog means making things hard to see with thick moisture, while haze describes a thinner, dull cloud that reduces clarity.
  • cloud
    4.4 2.96/m
    cloud covers or hides things in sky or speech, while haze is thinner and makes views look dull or blurred.
  • becloud
    8.9 0.07/m
    becloud makes something hard to see in a poetic or formal way, while haze is common and means becoming dull or cloudy.
  • mist
    3.1 0.45/m
    mist makes surfaces wet with tiny droplets causing a soft blur, while haze is a thin dull cloud not always wet.
  • fog up
    2.8 0.18/m
    fog up covers glass with water drops and blocks sight, while haze is a lighter dulling of view not caused by wetness.
  • mist over
    5.1 0.02/m
    mist over leaves a thin wet film on mirrors or glass, while haze is a thin dull cloud across air or surfaces.
