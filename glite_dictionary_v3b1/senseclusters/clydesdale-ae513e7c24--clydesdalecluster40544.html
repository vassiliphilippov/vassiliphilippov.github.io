Clydesdale

noun
breed of heavy draft horse from Scotland
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪdzˌdeɪl / (AmE), / ˈklaɪdzdeɪl / (BrE)
a large, heavy breed of draft horse with feathered legs, originally from Scotland, known for its strength and distinctive appearance

Examples

A Clydesdale horse
She admired the majestic Clydesdale as it trotted gracefully in the parade.
The Budweiser commercials often feature a team of Clydesdales pulling a bright red wagon.
The farm uses Clydesdales for heavy work because of their strength and stamina.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Percheron - a French breed of draft horse
  • synonym: Shire - a British breed of large draft horse
