go on

verb
start functioning or operating
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɑn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn / (BrE)
to start running, functioning, or operating (of machines, devices, or systems)

Examples

The lights went on
As soon as I entered the room, the lights went on automatically.
The alarm went on when it detected smoke in the kitchen.
The heating system goes on every morning at six o'clock.

Related Terms

  • antonym: go off - to stop working or functioning (of machines or lights)
  • synonym: start up - to begin operating (of a machine, engine, etc.)
  • synonym: switch on - to turn on (a device); start (a machine) operating
