dyscalculia

noun
learning difficulty in mathematics
8.1 9.7
Pronunciation: /dɪs.kæl.ˈkjuː.lɪə/ /; / /dɪs.kæl.ˈkjuː.liː.ə/ (AmE), /dɪs.kæl.ˈkjuː.lɪə/ /; / /dɪs.kæl.ˈkjuː.liː.ə/ (BrE)
A learning disorder characterized by difficulty in understanding or performing basic mathematical calculations, despite normal intelligence.

Examples

Child with dyscalculia
Children with dyscalculia may struggle with basic arithmetic and understanding numbers.
Her dyscalculia was diagnosed in elementary school after repeated difficulties with math assignments.
Support programs can help students with dyscalculia learn alternative ways to approach math problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acalculia - loss of ability to perform mathematical calculations (often after brain injury)
  • synonym: dyslexia - a learning disorder characterized by difficulty in reading
