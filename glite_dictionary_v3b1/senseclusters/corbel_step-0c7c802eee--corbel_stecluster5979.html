corbel step

noun
a step on the top of a gable wall
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrbəl stɛp / (AmE), / ˈkɔːbəl stɛp / (BrE)
(architecture) A projecting stone or brick step on the top of a gable wall, typically forming a stepped pattern along the sloping edge.

Examples

Decorative corbel steps
Medieval builders often used corbel steps to create decorative gables on important buildings.
Restorers carefully matched the original stonework when replacing the damaged corbel step.
The gable of the old church was lined with corbel steps along its edges.
