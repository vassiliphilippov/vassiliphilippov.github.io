laurel cherry

noun
Eurasian evergreen shrub/tree with glossy leaves and white flowers
9.9 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrəl ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a Eurasian evergreen shrub or small tree (Prunus laurocerasus) with glossy leaves, clusters of white flowers, and fruit bearing oil similar to bitter almond oil, often grown ornamentally

Examples

Eurasian laurel cherry
Birds are attracted to the fruit of the laurel cherry, even though it is toxic to humans.
Gardeners appreciate the glossy leaves and fragrant flowers of the laurel cherry.
The laurel cherry is often used as a decorative hedge in gardens.
