Ophidiidae

noun
a family of eellike marine fishes (cusk-eels)
Pronunciation: / oʊˌfɪdiˈɪdiː / (AmE), / əʊˌfɪdɪˈɪdɪː / (BrE)
A family of eellike marine fishes, known as cusk-eels, found in deep and shallow seas worldwide.

Examples

The Ophidiidae family
Scientists discovered a new member of the Ophidiidae off the coast of New Zealand.
The Ophidiidae are often found at great ocean depths.
The Ophidiidae family includes many species that resemble eels in appearance.
