parasitic worm

noun
disease-causing intestinal worm
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /pə.rə.ˈsɪt.ɪk wɜːrm/ /; / /pær.ə.ˈsɪt.ɪk wɜːrm/ (AmE), /pær.ə.ˈsɪt.ɪk wɜːm/ /; / /pə.rə.ˈsɪt.ɪk wɜːm/ (BrE)
A worm that lives and feeds inside the body of another organism, usually causing disease, especially in the intestines of vertebrates; examples include roundworms, tapeworms, and flukes.

Examples

Parasitic worm infection
Doctors diagnosed the boy with an infection caused by a parasitic worm.
Improved sanitation can help prevent the spread of parasitic worms in developing countries.
Tapeworms are a type of parasitic worm that can live in the human intestines.
