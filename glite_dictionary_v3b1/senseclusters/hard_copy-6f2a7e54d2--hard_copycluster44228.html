hard copy

noun
printed version of digital information
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrd ˌkɑpi / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌkɒpi / (BrE)
a printed or typed version of information originally held in a computer or other electronic form

Examples

A hard copy of document
Many people still prefer to read a hard copy of the newspaper.
Please print out a hard copy of your assignment before class.
The editor reviewed the hard copy for any typographical errors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft copy - an electronic version of information, not printed
