draft

noun
version of a written work
Pronunciation: / dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
a preliminary version, outline, or sketch of a document, plan, or design; or to prepare such a version

Examples

A draft of a letter
After several revisions, the book was finally ready in its final draft.
She submitted her first draft of the essay to her professor for review.
The committee discussed the initial draft of the proposal before making any changes.
He reviewed the draft and suggested several changes to improve the composition.
She made a quick draft of her logo idea on a napkin.
The architect showed us the draft of the new building before finalizing the plans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: final copy - the completed and finished version, as opposed to a draft
  • antonym: final version - the finished and completed form of a written work
  • synonym: manuscript - a written or typed document, especially before publication
  • synonym: sketch - a rough or unfinished drawing or painting
