yet

adverb
in spite of that; nevertheless
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /jet/ (AmE), /jet/ (BrE)
Used to introduce a contrast with what has already been mentioned; nevertheless or in spite of that.

Examples

He failed, yet succeeded
He has little experience; yet he performed exceptionally well during the interview.
She was tired, yet she stayed up late to finish her project.
The weather was terrible, yet they continued with the picnic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
  • synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; nonetheless
  • synonym: still - nevertheless; in spite of that
