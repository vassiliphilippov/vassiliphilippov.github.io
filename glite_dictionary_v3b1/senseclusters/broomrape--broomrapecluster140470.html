broomrape

noun
parasitic plant
Pronunciation: / ˈbruːmˌreɪp / (AmE), / ˈbruːmˌreɪp / (BrE)
a parasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants to obtain nutrients, often harming its host

Examples

The broomrape plant
The botanist studied the broomrape that had invaded the wildflower meadow.
Farmers worry when broomrape appears in their fields because it can damage crops.
We found a cluster of broomrape growing near the roots of the old oak tree.
