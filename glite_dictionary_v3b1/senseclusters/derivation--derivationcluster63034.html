derivation

noun
word formation process in linguistics
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛrəˈveɪʃən /; / ˌdɛrɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a new word from an existing word, or explaining the origin and development of a word (linguistics)

Examples

Word derivation
In English, the derivation of the word 'happiness' from 'happy' involves adding a suffix.
Some languages make frequent use of derivation to expand their vocabularies.
Teachers often introduce students to derivation to help them understand word formation.
His paper focused on the derivation of medical terminology from Greek.
The derivation of the word 'salary' from Latin reflects its ancient association with salt.
Understanding the derivation of English words can reveal fascinating links with other languages.
