moose

noun
large northern deer with broad antlers
3.0
Pronunciation: / mus / (AmE), / muːs / (BrE)
a large deer found in northern regions of North America and Eurasia, with broad, flattened antlers in the male; called 'elk' in Europe

Examples

A large moose
Moose calves stay close to their mother moose for the first year.
The antlers of a male moose can span over six feet across.
We saw a moose drinking from the lake in the early morning light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elk - the same species as moose, used mainly in European English
