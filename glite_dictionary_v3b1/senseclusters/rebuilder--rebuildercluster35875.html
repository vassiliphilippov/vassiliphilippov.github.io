rebuilder

noun
data repair tool
Pronunciation: / riˈbɪldər / (AmE), / riˈbɪldə / (BrE)
a computer program or tool that reconstructs data structures, indexes, or system files after corruption or for maintenance

Examples

Database rebuilder
The IT department used a rebuilder to fix the damaged database after the power outage.
Running the rebuilder can help recover lost files and restore system stability.
After a virus attack, the technician relied on a rebuilder to reconstruct the corrupted indexes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repair tool - a utility used to fix or correct errors in data or systems
  • synonym: restorer - a tool or program that brings something back to its original state
