metacognitive

adjective
about thinking processes
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈkɑːɡnɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈkɒɡnɪtɪv / (BrE)
relating to awareness and understanding of one's own thought processes

Examples

Metacognitive strategies
Teachers often encourage students to use metacognitive strategies to improve their learning.
Developing metacognitive skills can help you become a more effective problem solver.
Her metacognitive awareness allowed her to recognize when she needed to change her study habits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-aware - conscious of oneself and one's actions
  • antonym: unreflective - not thinking about or considering one's own thoughts
