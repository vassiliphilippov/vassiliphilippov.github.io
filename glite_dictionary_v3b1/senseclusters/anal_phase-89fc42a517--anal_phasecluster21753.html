anal phase

noun
psychoanalytic stage of bowel control learning
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.nəl feɪz/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.nəl feɪz/ (BrE)
(In psychoanalysis) the stage of a child's development, typically between 18 months and 3 years, during which the child learns to control bowel movements and derives pleasure from this process.

Examples

The anal phase stage
According to Freud, children experience the anal phase as they learn to control their bodily functions.
Psychologists often refer to the anal phase when discussing early childhood development stages.
Some behavioral issues in adults are sometimes attributed to unresolved conflicts during the anal phase.
