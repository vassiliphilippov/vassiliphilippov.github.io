jig

noun
lively dance music in compound time
Pronunciation: / dʒɪɡ / (AmE), / dʒɪɡ / (BrE)
a lively traditional dance with quick, jumping movements, or the music for this dance

Examples

Irish jig dance
Her fingers flew over the strings as she performed a lively jig on the violin.
The band played a traditional Irish jig that got everyone clapping along.
We learned to dance the jig at the folk music festival last weekend.
At the festival, everyone joined in the energetic jig by the bonfire.
She learned a traditional Irish jig from her grandmother.
The musicians played a fast jig that got the whole crowd dancing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reel - a lively Scottish or Irish folk dance, also the music for such a dance
  • antonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time
