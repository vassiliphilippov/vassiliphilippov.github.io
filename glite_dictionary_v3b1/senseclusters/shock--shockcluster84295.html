shock

noun
seismic disturbance; earth tremor
2.9
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒk / (BrE)
a sudden, violent movement of the earth's crust, especially during an earthquake

Examples

Earthquake shock
After the main shock, several weaker tremors were felt throughout the night.
Geologists studied the patterns created by the seismic shock to predict future earthquakes.
The first shock of the earthquake came while everyone was still asleep.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: aftershock - a smaller earthquake following the main shock
  • synonym: tremor - a small or slight earthquake
