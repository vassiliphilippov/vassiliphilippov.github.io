Examples

Translation of a shape
In physics, translation refers to straight-line movement, as opposed to rotation or vibration.
Robotic arms must coordinate both translation and rotation to position objects accurately.
The translation of the object across the table occurred without any rotation.
During the demonstration, the box underwent a translation along the table rather than spinning.
Mathematicians often distinguish between translation and rotation when describing object movement.
The translation of the car meant it moved forward in a straight line without turning.
Every point on the figure undergoes the same translation, preserving the object's shape and size.
In geometry class, we learned how a translation shifts a shape without changing its orientation.
The translation of the graph moves it three units to the right on the x-axis.
