jam

verb
to pack or crowd to capacity
1.9 2.7
Pronunciation: / dʒæm / (AmE), / dʒam / (BrE)
to fill, pack, or block a space so that movement is restricted; to crowd or cram tightly together

Examples

People jam into trains
Commuters jam onto the train every morning during rush hour.
Fans began to jam the stadium an hour before the match started.
We tried to jam all our clothes into one small suitcase for the weekend trip.
A fallen tree jammed the road, making it impassable for several hours.
She accidentally jammed the printer by feeding too much paper at once.
The suitcase was so full that I had to jam the zipper closed.
