bondman

noun
male slave; serf
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑnd.mæn /; / ˈbɑːndˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈbɒnd.mən /; / ˈbɒndmən / (BrE)
a male slave or serf; a man who is forced to work for another person and is not free

Examples

A bondman slave
He worked as a bondman, toiling in the fields from sunrise to sunset.
In medieval times, a bondman had little hope of gaining his freedom.
The law declared every bondman on the estate belonged to its lord.
He was no longer a free citizen but had become his master’s bondman.
In the feudal era, a bondman worked the land at his lord’s command.
The law emancipated every bondman within the kingdom.
