yaw

verb
swerve off course (vehicles, especially ships or planes)
7.5
Pronunciation: / jɑ / (AmE), / jɔː / (BrE)
to swerve or deviate temporarily from a straight course, especially of a ship, aircraft, or vehicle

Examples

To yaw off course
As the strong gust hit the plane, it began to yaw to the left.
If not corrected swiftly, a sudden yaw can lead to loss of control.
The damaged rudder caused the fishing boat to yaw dangerously in heavy seas.
Improper cargo loading can cause a truck to yaw on sharp turns.
The pilot noticed that the plane would yaw slightly during turbulence.
The ship began to yaw as the storm grew stronger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swerve - to change or cause to change direction abruptly
  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly; to turn aside from a course or direction
