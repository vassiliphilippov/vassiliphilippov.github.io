rush grass

noun
grass with wiry stems and sheathed panicles
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ ɡræs / (AmE), / rʌʃ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A type of grass with wiry stems and sheathed panicles, found in various wet or marshy habitats.

Examples

The rush grass plant
Gardeners often struggle to remove rush grass from their lawns due to its tough stems.
Several species of rush grass thrive in areas with poor drainage.
The wetland was covered in dense patches of rush grass and reeds.
