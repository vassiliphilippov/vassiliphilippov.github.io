earthshaking

adjective
very powerful or loud (like an earthquake)
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound or event) so powerful or loud that it seems to shake the earth.

Examples

Earthshaking noise
An earthshaking roar erupted from the stadium after the final goal.
The explosion was so earthshaking that windows shattered blocks away.
The movie's earthshaking soundtrack rumbled through the theater seats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thunderous - extremely loud, like thunder
