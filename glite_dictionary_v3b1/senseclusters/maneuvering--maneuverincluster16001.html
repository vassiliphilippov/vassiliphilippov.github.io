maneuvering

noun
military movements
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvərɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the movement or handling of troops, vehicles, or equipment, especially as part of military operations or training exercises

Examples

Military maneuvering
The general's careful maneuvering of the tanks helped secure a strategic advantage.
During the exercise, the navy practiced maneuvering in tight formations.
Successful maneuvering requires precise coordination between all units involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deployment - the movement of forces into position for action
  • antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing troops from enemy forces
