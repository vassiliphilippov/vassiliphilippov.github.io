overexcite

verb
to excite excessively
8.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈsaɪt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪk.ˈsaɪt/ (BrE)
to cause atoms, molecules, or a system to become more excited or energetic than is normal or safe, often leading to instability or damage

Examples

To overexcite atoms
If you overexcite the circuit, it may overheat and stop working properly.
The experiment failed because the laser beam overexcited the molecules, causing them to break apart.
Scientists must be careful not to overexcite the sample during testing to avoid damaging it.
