operational casualty

noun
military loss of equipment in operations
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənl ˈkæʒuəlti / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənl ˈkæʒuəlti / (BrE)
the loss or destruction of military equipment during field operations, especially as a result of enemy action or mishaps

Examples

An operational casualty loss
During the exercise, a helicopter was written off as an operational casualty due to mechanical failure.
Several vehicles became operational casualties after the convoy hit a minefield.
The loss of two tanks was recorded as an operational casualty in the official report.
