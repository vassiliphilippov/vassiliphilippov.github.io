nonce

noun
the present occasion (archaic)
9.9 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘ns / (AmE), / nÉ’ns / (BrE)
(archaic) The present or particular occasion; used especially in the phrase 'for the nonce', meaning 'for the time being'.

Examples

For the nonce
He accepted a minor role in the team for the nonce, but expects more responsibility later.
Their alliance was only for the nonce and did not last beyond the campaign.
This rule was made just for the nonce, so it may change soon.

Synonyms

  • time being
    3.0 3.39/m
    time being is the common modern phrase for a short, temporary period, while nonce is archaic and used mainly in older texts.
