wiretap

noun
secret listening to phone or telegraph conversations
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪərˌtæp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəˌtæp / (BrE)
the act of secretly listening to telephone or telegraph conversations by connecting a device to the line, typically to obtain private information

Examples

Illegal wiretap
Installing a wiretap without authorization is a serious crime in many countries.
The police obtained a warrant before placing a wiretap on the suspect’s phone.
The scandal erupted when journalists exposed the illegal wiretap of government officials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eavesdropping - secretly listening to private conversations
