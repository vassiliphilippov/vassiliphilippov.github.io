come-on

noun
enticement or lure
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌm ˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈkʌm ɒn / (BrE)
something intended to attract, entice, or tempt someone (such as a compliment, advertisement, or offer)

Examples

A come-on offer
She realized his compliments were simply a come-on and didn't take them seriously.
That flashy ad is just a come-on for a much more expensive service.
The store's "buy one, get one free" offer was just a come-on to get more customers inside.
He suspected the advertisement was a come-on for a less attractive deal.
Her smile was the perfect come-on at the party.
The free gift was just a clever come-on to get customers into the store.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enticement - something that attracts or tempts
  • synonym: lure - something used to attract or tempt someone
  • antonym: turn-off - something that causes someone to lose interest
