maniple

noun
Roman unit; church vestment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈmænɪpəl / (BrE)
a subdivision of a Roman legion, or a liturgical vestment worn by clergy in some Christian traditions

Examples

Roman maniple
Each maniple in the Roman legion consisted of about 120 soldiers.
The priest wore a purple maniple during the special church service.
Historians study how the maniple system contributed to Roman military success.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: century - a smaller military unit in a Roman legion, below a maniple
  • degree higher: cohort - a military unit in a Roman legion, larger than a maniple
  • synonym: stole - a liturgical vestment worn by clergy, similar to a maniple
