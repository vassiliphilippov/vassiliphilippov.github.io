trespass

verb
enter land without permission
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛs.pæs /; / ˈtrɛspæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛs.pəs /; / ˈtrɛspɑːs / (BrE)
to enter someone's land or property without permission or legal right

Examples

To trespass on property
Anyone caught trespassing after dark will be prosecuted.
Hikers were warned not to trespass on private farmland bordering the forest.
The company put up signs to deter people from trespassing on its property.
He was caught trespassing in the restricted wildlife area.
If you trespass on private land, you may face legal consequences.
The hikers accidentally trespassed onto a farmer’s field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intrude - to enter without invitation or permission
  • antonym: obey - to follow orders or the law, the opposite of breaking rules
