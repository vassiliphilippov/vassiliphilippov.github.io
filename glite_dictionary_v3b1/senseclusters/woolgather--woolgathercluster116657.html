woolgather

verb
to daydream; indulge in fancies
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊlˌɡæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌɡæðə / (BrE)
(dated) To indulge in aimless daydreaming or absent-minded fancies.

Examples

To woolgather in class
Caught woolgathering during the lecture, he missed the professor's key points.
He tends to woolgather when he's supposed to be studying for exams.
Instead of listening to the meeting, she started to woolgather about her upcoming vacation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrate - to focus one's attention or mental effort
  • synonym: daydream - to imagine pleasant things while awake
