trematode worm

noun
parasitic flatworm with suckers
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛməˌtoʊd wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmətəʊd wɜːm / (BrE)
A parasitic flatworm of the class Trematoda, typically with external suckers for attaching to a host.

Examples

The trematode worm parasite
A common freshwater snail can serve as an intermediate host for the trematode worm.
Scientists study the life cycle of the trematode worm to find ways to prevent infections.
The liver trematode worm causes significant disease in cattle and sheep.

  • synonym: fluke - another name for a trematode worm
