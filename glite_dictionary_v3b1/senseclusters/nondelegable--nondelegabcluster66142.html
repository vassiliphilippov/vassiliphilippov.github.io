nondelegable

adjective
cannot be transferred
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈdɛlɪɡəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈdɛlɪɡəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be transferred or assigned to another person or party

Examples

A nondelegable duty
The court ruled that the hospital had a nondelegable duty to care for its patients.
Some responsibilities are nondelegable and must be performed by the person in charge.
Ensuring the safety of workers is a nondelegable obligation for employers under the law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegable - able to be transferred or assigned to another
