overshoot the budget

verb
exceed a planned or allocated budget
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈʃuːt ðə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃuːt ðə ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
to spend more money than was allocated or planned for a project or purpose

Examples

To overshoot the budget
If we overshoot the budget, the company may have to make cuts elsewhere.
The team was careful not to overshoot the budget during development.
They realized too late that they would overshoot the budget by a significant margin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exceed the budget - to go beyond the amount of money budgeted
  • antonym: stay within budget - not spend more than the allocated budget
