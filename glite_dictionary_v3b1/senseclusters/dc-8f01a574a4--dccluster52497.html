DC

noun
direct electric current
3.8
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈsiː/ (AmE), /diː.ˈsiː/ (BrE)
an electric current that flows in one direction only, as opposed to alternating current (AC)

Examples

DC power
Many electronic devices use DC power supplies instead of AC.
Solar panels generate DC electricity, which must be converted for home use.
The battery provides DC to the circuit, ensuring a steady voltage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: AC - an electric current that reverses direction regularly
