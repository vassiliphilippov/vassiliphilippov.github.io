catch a ride

verb
get a lift in a vehicle
Pronunciation: / kætʃ ə raɪd / (AmE), / kætʃ ə raɪd / (BrE)
to get a lift in someone else's vehicle; to travel as a passenger in a car or other transport offered by someone else

Examples

To catch a ride home
Do you want to catch a ride with us to the game?
She managed to catch a ride home after missing the bus.
When his bike broke down, he had to catch a ride from a neighbor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give a ride - to provide someone with transportation in one's car
  • synonym: hitch a ride - to get a ride by asking a passing driver, often by signaling with a raised thumb
