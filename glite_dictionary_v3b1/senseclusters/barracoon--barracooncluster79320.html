barracoon

noun
temporary shelter building
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌbærəˈkuːn / (AmE), / ˌbærəˈkuːn / (BrE)
a simple building or hut used to hold people temporarily, often in poor conditions, especially used in the past to confine enslaved people before transport

Examples

A barracoon hut
The workers were forced to sleep in a crowded barracoon near the construction site.
During the voyage, captives were held in a barracoon before being transported.
Conditions inside the barracoon were harsh, with little space or comfort for the occupants.
The captured men were forced into a barracoon before being loaded onto ships.
Historians have uncovered the remains of a barracoon near the old port.
The novel describes the horrors of the barracoon on the coast.
