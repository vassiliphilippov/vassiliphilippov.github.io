ultrasound

noun
medical imaging using sound waves
Pronunciation: / ˈʌltrəˌsaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəsaʊnd / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal body structures, commonly used to monitor fetal development or examine organs

Examples

A baby ultrasound
During my pregnancy, I looked forward to each ultrasound appointment.
She had an ultrasound to examine her kidneys.
The doctor used an ultrasound to check the baby's development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sonogram - an image produced by ultrasound scanning
