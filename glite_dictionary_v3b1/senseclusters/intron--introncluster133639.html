intron

noun
non-coding segment of a gene
9.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.trən/ /; / /ˈɪn.trɑːn/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.trɒn/ (BrE)
A segment of a gene that does not code for protein and is removed from the RNA transcript before translation.

Examples

A intron segment
During gene expression, the cell removes the intron sequences from the primary RNA transcript.
Mutations can sometimes occur within an intron, affecting gene regulation without altering the protein.
Researchers are studying how certain introns may play roles beyond simply being spliced out.
