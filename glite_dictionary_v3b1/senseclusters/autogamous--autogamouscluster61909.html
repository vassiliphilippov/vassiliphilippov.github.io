autogamous

adjective
capable of self-fertilization
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈtɑɡəməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒɡəməs / (BrE)
(of an organism or a flower) characterized by or capable of self-fertilization (autogamy).

Examples

Autogamous plant species
Certain plant species are autogamous, allowing them to reproduce without pollen from another plant.
In laboratory experiments, the flower's autogamous nature simplifies genetic studies.
Wheat is often autogamous, meaning most pollination occurs within the same flower.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allogamous - reproducing by cross-fertilization, not self-fertilization
  • synonym: autogamy - self-fertilization, especially in flowers or organisms
