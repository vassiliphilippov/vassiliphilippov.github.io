Amharic language

noun
official language of Ethiopia
Pronunciation: / æmˈhɑrɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / amˈhɑːrɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the official and dominant language of Ethiopia, belonging to the Semitic family and influenced by Cushitic languages

Examples

The Amharic language of Ethiopia
Linguists study the Amharic language to understand its Semitic roots.
She learned to speak Amharic language before traveling to Ethiopia.
The Amharic language uses a unique script called Fidel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ge'ez - an ancient Semitic language of Ethiopia, liturgical for Ethiopian churches
