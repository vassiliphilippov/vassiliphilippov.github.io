baulk-line

noun
line on a billiard table for starting play
Pronunciation: / bɔk laɪn / (AmE), / bɔːk laɪn / (BrE)
a line across a billiard table, behind which the cue balls are placed at the start of a game

Examples

The baulk-line on table
According to the rules, you cannot strike the ball until it crosses the baulk-line.
He carefully placed the cue ball behind the baulk-line before taking his first shot.
The referee checked that all balls were positioned correctly behind the baulk-line.
