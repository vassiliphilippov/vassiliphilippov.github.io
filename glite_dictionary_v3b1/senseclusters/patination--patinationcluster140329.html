patination

noun
aged surface sheen
9.3
Pronunciation: /pə.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pæ.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pə.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pæ.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a thin layer or surface appearance that develops on metal or other materials over time, often due to age, use, or chemical treatment

Examples

Natural patination
The bronze statue developed a beautiful green patination after years in the garden.
Collectors often value the natural patination on antique furniture as a sign of authenticity.
The old book's cover had a soft patination that hinted at its long history.
The bronze statue developed a beautiful green patination after years of exposure to the elements.
Collectors often value antiques with natural patination because it shows authenticity and age.
Artists sometimes use chemicals to create an artificial patination on metal sculptures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of materials by chemical reaction
  • synonym: patina - a thin layer that forms on the surface of metals or other materials over time
  • synonym: tarnish - a dullness or discoloration on a surface, especially metal, due to exposure or age
