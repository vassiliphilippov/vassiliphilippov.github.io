liquify

verb
distort an image with a 'liquify' tool
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪk.wɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈlɪk.wə.faɪ/ (AmE), /ˈlɪk.wɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈlɪk.wə.faɪ/ (BrE)
(in digital image editing) to warp or distort a digital image or part of an image using a tool or filter so it appears to flow, melt, or stretch.

Examples

To liquify a photo
The designer used a filter to subtly liquify the model's hair for a softer look.
After hours of editing, she liquified part of the landscape to create a dreamy motion blur.
While liquifying the portrait, he carefully avoided distorting the subject's eyes and mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distort - to change the natural, original, or intended shape or appearance of something
  • synonym: morph - to transform one image smoothly into another or to change shape digitally
  • synonym: warp - to bend, twist, or distort the shape of something (often used for images or surfaces)
