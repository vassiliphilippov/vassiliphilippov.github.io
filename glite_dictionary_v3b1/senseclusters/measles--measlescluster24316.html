measles

noun
contagious viral disease with rash
3.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààmiz…ôlz / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêz…ôlz / (BrE)
An acute and highly contagious viral disease, especially affecting children, characterized by fever and a red rash.

Examples

Catch measles disease
She missed two weeks of school after catching measles from a classmate.
The doctor confirmed that the red rash was a symptom of measles.
Vaccination programs have greatly reduced the number of measles cases worldwide.

Synonyms

  • rubeola
    9.5 0.00/m
    rubeola is the medical or scientific name used in reports and textbooks, while measles is the common everyday name.
  • morbilli
    9.9 0.00/m
    morbilli is an alternative, less used name seen in older medical writing, while measles is the common modern term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rubella - another viral disease causing a similar rash but generally milder
