musicality

noun
quality of sounding like music
3.6 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːzɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪˈkæləti /; / ˌmjuːzɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being musical, either in sound, character, or in having a good sense of music and rhythm

Examples

Good musicality in dance
Many dancers are praised for their musicality as well as their technique.
The musicality of her speech captivated the entire audience.
The musicality of the language made it pleasant to listen to, even without understanding the words.
The dancer's musicality made her performance stand out from the rest.
He was praised for his natural musicality even though he had little formal training.
Children can develop musicality through early exposure to different kinds of music.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cacophony - a harsh, discordant mixture of sounds
  • synonym: musicianship - skill or artistry in performing music
  • antonym: tone-deafness - inability to distinguish musical notes or sing in tune
  • synonym: tunefulness - the quality of being tuneful or melodious
