Nebuchadnezzar II

proper noun
Babylonian king who destroyed Jerusalem
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛbjəkədˈnɛzər səˈkənd / (AmE), / ˌnɛbjʊkədˈnɛzə səˈkənd / (BrE)
(c. 630–562 BC) The king of Babylon who captured and destroyed Jerusalem and exiled the Israelites to Babylonia; a major figure in Old Testament history.

Examples

Nebuchadnezzar II king of Babylon
Historians continue to study the reign of Nebuchadnezzar II for its profound impact on ancient Mesopotamia.
The Hanging Gardens are sometimes attributed to Nebuchadnezzar II, though their existence remains debated.
The conquest of Jerusalem by Nebuchadnezzar II changed the course of Jewish history.
