grim

adjective
bleak; causing dejection
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪm / (AmE), / ɡrɪm / (BrE)
causing or showing sadness, hopelessness, or a depressed mood; bleak or depressing

Examples

Grim news
He spoke in a grim tone about the hardships they had endured.
News of the financial crisis painted a grim picture of the country's future.
The grim weather matched her somber mood all week long.
After hearing the tragic news, she looked grim and barely spoke for the rest of the day.
He took a grim view of the situation, convinced that things were unlikely to improve.
The report painted a grim picture of the company's future, leaving employees worried and uncertain.
