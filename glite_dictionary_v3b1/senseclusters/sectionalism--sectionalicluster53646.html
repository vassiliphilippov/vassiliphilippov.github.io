sectionalism

noun
regional loyalty or partiality
5.1 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.ʃə.nə.lɪz.əm/ /; / /ˈsek.ʃən.ə.lɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ˈsek.ʃə.nə.lɪz.əm/ /; / /ˈsek.ʃən.ə.lɪz.əm/ (BrE)
Excessive or undue concern for the interests of a particular region or section of a country, often at the expense of the whole.

Examples

Strong sectionalism feeling
During debates, his sectionalism was clear—he supported policies favoring only his own region.
Politicians warned that rising sectionalism could threaten the unity of the country.
The Civil War was fueled in part by growing sectionalism between the North and South.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalism - identification with one's own nation and support for its interests
  • synonym: regionalism - loyalty to the interests of a particular region
