Gen X

noun
generation born mid-1960s to early 1980s
3.9
Pronunciation: / dʒɛn ɛks / (AmE), / dʒɛn ɛks / (BrE)
the generation of people born between the mid-1960s and early 1980s, especially in the United States and Canada, following the baby boom generation

Examples

Gen X generation
As a Gen X parent, he values independence and open-mindedness.
Many members of Gen X entered the workforce during an economic recession.
Some believe that Gen X is often overlooked between baby boomers and millennials.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: baby boomer - person born during the post-World War II baby boom
  • degree higher: millennial - member of the generation born in the early 1980s to mid-1990s or early 2000s
