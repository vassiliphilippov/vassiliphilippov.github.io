detach

verb
to come apart or separate

(of something) to come apart or separate from something to which it is attached, often unintentionally or as a result of a process.

Examples

The screen detaches
His retina detached, requiring immediate medical attention.
Over time, the label detached and fell to the floor.
The leaf detached from the branch in the strong wind.

    3.0 0.28/m
    come away means to become separated from what it is attached to, often slowly or by accident, while detach often implies a process.
  • come off
    3.5 4.69/m
    come off means to become detached from a surface or object, usually by pulling or wear, while detach often implies separation.
  • come unstuck
    5.8 0.02/m
    come unstuck means to lose attachment to a surface or position, often suddenly, while detach can be slow or unplanned.
  • fall off
    2.3 3.96/m
    fall off means to drop away and leave what it was fixed to, usually by breaking or gravity, while detach can be gradual.
  • break off
    2.8 1.72/m
    break off means to separate by breaking a piece away from the whole, often suddenly, while detach can be less violent.
  • break away
    3.5 0.18/m
    break away means to separate from a group or whole, often by force or effort, while detach often lacks intent.
  • break apart
    3.0 1.62/m
    break apart means to split into pieces and ruin the object, often violently, while detach means to come loose intact.
  • dislodge
    6.1 1.37/m
    dislodge means to force something out of a fixed place by strong action, while detach can happen by natural wear or accident.
