longfin mako

noun
species of large mackerel shark
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌfɪn ˈmeɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌfɪn ˈmeɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a species of large mackerel shark (Isurus paucus), similar to the shortfin mako but with longer pectoral fins and a darker blue coloration

Examples

A longfin mako shark
A longfin mako was caught off the coast during a research expedition.
The longfin mako can be distinguished by its elongated pectoral fins.
Unlike its close relative, the longfin mako is rarely seen by divers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shortfin mako - a related species of mackerel shark with shorter pectoral fins
