lowbrow

adjective
not intellectual or cultured
9.9 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbraʊ / (BrE)
(of a person or their tastes) not highly intellectual or cultured; regarded as less sophisticated.

Examples

Lowbrow movies
Her lowbrow taste in art surprised her sophisticated friends.
Some people enjoy lowbrow comedies more than highbrow dramas.
The critics dismissed the novel as too lowbrow for serious readers.
