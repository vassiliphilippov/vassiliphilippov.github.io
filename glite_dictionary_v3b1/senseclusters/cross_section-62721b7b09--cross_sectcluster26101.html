cross section

noun
section made by a perpendicular cut
6.6
Pronunciation: / krɔs ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
A surface or shape that is obtained by cutting through an object, especially at right angles to its longest axis.

Examples

A cross section drawing
The architect provided a detailed cross section of the building in the blueprints.
The engineer examined the cross section of the pipe to check for corrosion.
This diagram shows the cross section of a tree trunk, revealing its growth rings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slice - a thin, broad piece cut from a larger object
