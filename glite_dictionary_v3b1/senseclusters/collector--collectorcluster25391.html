collector

noun
electrode in a transistor
7.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
the electrode in a transistor through which the main current flows out, usually collecting charge carriers from the base region

Examples

Transistor collector
Current flows from the emitter to the collector when the base is properly biased.
In this circuit diagram, the collector is connected to the positive voltage supply.
The transistor stopped working because its collector was damaged during assembly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base - the control electrode in a transistor that modulates flow between emitter and collector
  • antonym: emitter - the electrode in a transistor from which charge carriers enter the base region
