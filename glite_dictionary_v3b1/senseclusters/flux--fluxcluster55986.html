flux

noun
continuous flow or discharge
7.7 0.44/m
Pronunciation: / flÊŒks / (AmE), / flÊŒks / (BrE)
a continuous flow or movement of a liquid, gas, or particles, especially in a particular direction; a discharge

Examples

Flux of liquid
Doctors measured the amount of gastric flux during the experiment.
In physics, magnetic flux describes the strength of a magnetic field passing through an area.
The river's flux increased rapidly after the storm.

Synonyms

  • flow
    1.0 35/m
    flow is the steady, continuous movement of a liquid or gas in general, while flux often means movement in a particular direction or discharge.
  • current
    3.2 13/m
    current is a steady movement of water or air with a clear direction, while flux means a continuous flow or discharge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flow - movement of a liquid, gas, or electricity in one direction
