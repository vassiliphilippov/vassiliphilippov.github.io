dacryocystorhinostomy

noun
tear duct surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdækriˌoʊˌsɪstoʊraɪˈnɑstəmi / (AmE), / ˌdækriəˌsɪstəraɪˈnɒstəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to create a new channel for tear drainage from the eye to the nose

Examples

A dacryocystorhinostomy surgery
After months of blocked tear ducts, the doctor recommended a dacryocystorhinostomy to relieve her symptoms.
A dacryocystorhinostomy is often performed when less invasive treatments for tear duct blockage fail.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of dacryocystorhinostomy before scheduling the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lacrimal duct obstruction - blockage of the tear drainage duct
  • synonym: tear duct surgery - surgery to restore or create a tear drainage pathway
