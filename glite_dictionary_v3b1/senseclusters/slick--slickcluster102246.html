slick

noun
glossy magazine
Pronunciation: / slɪk / (AmE), / slɪk / (BrE)
A magazine printed on high-quality glossy paper, often with lavish photographs and advertisements.

Examples

A fashion slick
He bought the latest fashion slick at the newsstand.
The library subscribes to several popular slicks for its reading room.
Unlike tabloids, a slick is often filled with high-quality photography.

Related Terms
