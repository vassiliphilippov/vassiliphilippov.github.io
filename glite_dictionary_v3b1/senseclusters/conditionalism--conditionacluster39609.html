conditionalism

noun
doctrine of conditions
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief, policy, or practice of making something (such as a right or benefit) depend on certain conditions being met

Examples

Conditionalism in religion
Some religious groups base their teachings on conditionalism, arguing that rewards are only given if certain criteria are met.
The debate centered on whether conditionalism or unconditional acceptance should guide the new policy.
Philosophers have discussed conditionalism in relation to moral responsibility and free will.
The new welfare program was criticized for its strict conditionalism regarding eligibility.
Some argue that conditionalism in aid policies can motivate positive behavior among recipients.
The organization adopted a policy of conditionalism, requiring members to meet certain standards to receive benefits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conditionality - the quality of being dependent on certain conditions
  • synonym: contingency - a future event or circumstance that is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty
  • antonym: unconditionalism - the belief that something is not dependent on any conditions
  • antonym: unconditionality - the state of not being subject to any conditions
