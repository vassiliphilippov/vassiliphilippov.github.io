esquire

noun
attendant and candidate for knighthood
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈskwaɪ.ər/ /; / /ɪ.ˈskwaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈskwaɪ.ə/ /; / /ɪ.ˈskwaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
(historical) A young man acting as an attendant and shield-bearer to a knight, regarded as a candidate for knighthood in the Middle Ages.

Examples

A knight's esquire
During the Middle Ages, an esquire would accompany a knight and learn the ways of chivalry.
He served as an esquire before being dubbed a knight for his bravery in battle.
The young esquire polished his master's armor in preparation for the tournament.
