suck

verb
draw into the mouth by suction
6.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / sʌk / (AmE), / sʌk / (BrE)
to draw in liquid, air, or another substance by creating a vacuum, especially with the mouth or a machine

Examples

To suck out poison
After the bee sting, she tried to suck out some of the venom before getting help.
The baby began to suck milk from the bottle as soon as it was given to him.
You should never suck water through a straw that's too short, or you'll get frustrated.
A vacuum cleaner can suck up all the dust from the carpet.
The mud was so deep it seemed to suck at his boots with every step.
The quicksand tried to suck her down as she struggled to escape.
A vacuum cleaner sucks up dust and dirt from carpets.
She tried to suck in all the information during the lecture.
The roots of the plant suck water from the soil.
