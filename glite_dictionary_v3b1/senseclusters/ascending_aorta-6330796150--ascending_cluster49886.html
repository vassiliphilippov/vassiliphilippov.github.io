ascending aorta

noun
upward section of the aorta from the heart
Pronunciation: / əˌsɛndɪŋ eɪˈɔɹtə / (AmE), / əˌsɛndɪŋ eɪˈɔːtə / (BrE)
the section of the aorta that rises upward from the left ventricle of the heart before curving to form the aortic arch

Examples

A rupture in the ascending aorta can be life-threatening if not treated immediately.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully examined the ascending aorta for any signs of damage.
Imaging tests revealed an aneurysm in his ascending aorta, requiring prompt medical intervention.
