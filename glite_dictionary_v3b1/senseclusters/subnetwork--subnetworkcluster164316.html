subnetwork

noun
smaller network part
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.net.wɜːrk/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.net.wɜːk/ (BrE)
a smaller network that is part of a larger computer or communication network

Examples

A subnetwork connection
Each office is connected to the main system through a subnetwork.
The IT team created a secure subnetwork for the research department to protect sensitive data.
Devices on the same subnetwork can communicate with each other more quickly.
Each subnetwork can be managed independently within the larger system.
The company divided its offices into several subnetworks to improve security and efficiency.
A failure in one subnetwork does not affect the entire organization’s communication.
