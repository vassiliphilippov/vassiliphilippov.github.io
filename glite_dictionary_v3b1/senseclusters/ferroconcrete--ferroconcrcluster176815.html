ferroconcrete

noun
concrete reinforced with metal
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛroʊˈkɑːŋkriːt / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊˈkɒŋkriːt / (BrE)
concrete that is reinforced with metal bars or mesh to increase its strength and resistance to stress

Examples

Ferroconcrete structure
Most modern bridges are constructed using ferroconcrete for added durability and strength.
The architect recommended ferroconcrete to ensure the building could withstand earthquakes.
Workers laid steel mesh before pouring the ferroconcrete foundation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reinforced concrete - concrete strengthened with steel bars or mesh
