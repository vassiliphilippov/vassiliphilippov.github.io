barge

verb
push one's way rudely
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /bɑːrdʒ/ (AmE), /bɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
to push or move forcefully and rudely, often into a place or situation without permission or invitation

Examples

To barge in
He didn't knock; he just barged into my office without warning.
People often barge past others when the train doors open.
She apologized for barging in on their conversation, but she needed urgent help.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: enter - to go or come into a place
  • synonym: intrude - to enter without invitation or permission, especially rudely
