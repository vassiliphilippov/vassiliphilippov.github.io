traffic control

noun
directing movement of vehicles or people
3.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtræ.fɪk kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /ˈtræ.fɪk kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
The activity of directing and managing the movement of vehicles and pedestrians to ensure safe and efficient flow, especially by authorities in a building or city.

Examples

City traffic control
Airports rely on traffic control systems to prevent collisions and maintain order.
Improved traffic control in the shopping mall has made it easier for customers to move around.
The city implemented traffic control measures to reduce congestion during rush hour.
