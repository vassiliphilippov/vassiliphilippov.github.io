scratch

verb
cause skin irritation by contact
Pronunciation: / skɹætʃ /; / skrætʃ / (AmE), / skɹatʃ /; / skrætʃ / (BrE)
to rub or scrape the skin, usually with fingernails, to relieve itching or cause irritation

Examples

To scratch an itch
Some people find that wearing synthetic fabrics scratches their skin uncomfortably.
The label inside the shirt scratched me all day until I finally cut it off.
This wool scarf scratches my neck and makes it hard to concentrate.
If you scratch too much, you might break the skin.
My cat likes to scratch behind her ears when she feels uncomfortable.
She tried not to scratch her arm, even though it was very itchy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: itch - to cause an irritating sensation on the skin
  • synonym: rub - to move one's hand or an object back and forth on a surface
  • antonym: soothe - to relieve or ease an uncomfortable feeling
