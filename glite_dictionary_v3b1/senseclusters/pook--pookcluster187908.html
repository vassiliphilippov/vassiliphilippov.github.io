pook

noun
dialect word for sack
Pronunciation: / puːk / (AmE), / puːk / (BrE)
an old or dialect word for a bag or sack, especially for carrying grain or other goods

Examples

A pook of grain
The farmer filled the pook with freshly harvested wheat.
He slung the heavy pook over his shoulder and walked to the mill.
In the old stories, villagers would carry apples home in a pook.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bag - a container made of flexible material used for carrying things
  • synonym: sack - a large bag used for carrying or storing things
