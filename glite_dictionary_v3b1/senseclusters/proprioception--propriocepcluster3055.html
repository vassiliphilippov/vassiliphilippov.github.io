proprioception

noun
body's ability to sense its position and movement
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊ.pri.oʊˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.pri.əʊˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
the sense that allows a person to perceive the position, movement, and orientation of their body and its parts

Examples

Human proprioception
Proprioception is crucial for athletes to maintain balance and coordination during dynamic sports.
After his injury, his proprioception was affected, making it harder to sense the position of his foot.
Yoga and balance exercises can help improve proprioception in people of all ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kinesthesia - the sense of movement of the body
