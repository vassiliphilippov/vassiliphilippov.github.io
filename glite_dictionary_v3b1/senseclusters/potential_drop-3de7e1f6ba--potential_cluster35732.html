potential drop

noun
difference in electrical potential
6.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəl drɑp / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃəl drɒp / (BrE)
the difference in electrical potential between two points in a circuit, measured in volts

Examples

A potential drop in volts
A large potential drop indicates significant energy loss in that section of the circuit.
Engineers monitor the potential drop to ensure the circuit functions efficiently.
The potential drop across the resistor was measured to be 5 volts.
