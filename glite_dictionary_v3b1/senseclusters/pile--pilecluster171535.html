pile

noun
structural support driven into ground
Pronunciation: / paɪl / (AmE), / paɪl / (BrE)
A long, slender column of wood, steel, or concrete driven into the ground to support a structure.

Examples

A wooden pile
A series of wooden piles support the pier above the shore.
Engineers decided to reinforce the foundation with additional piles.
The bridge rests on concrete piles driven deep into the riverbed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beam - a long sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used to support weight in a building
  • synonym: pillar - a tall vertical structure used as a support for buildings
