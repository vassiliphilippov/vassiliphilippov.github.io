lay the blame at the feet of (someone)

verb
attribute responsibility to someone
6.2
Pronunciation: / leɪ ðə bleɪm æt ðə fiːt əv / (AmE), / leɪ ðə bleɪm æt ðə fiːt ɒv / (BrE)
to hold someone responsible for a problem or wrongdoing; to attribute blame to a particular person or group

Examples

To lay the blame at the feet of a person
Instead of taking responsibility, he chose to lay the blame at the feet of his assistant.
Politicians are often quick to lay the blame at the feet of their opponents.
The CEO tried to lay the blame at the feet of the managers after the project's failure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolve - to declare someone free from blame or responsibility
  • synonym: blame - responsibility for a fault or wrong
