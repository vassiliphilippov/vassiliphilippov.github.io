allochthonous

adjective
originating elsewhere, not formed in place
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈlɑːk.θə.nəs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈlɒk.θə.nəs/ (BrE)
(of rocks, deposits, etc.) formed or originating in a place other than where found; transported from elsewhere.

Examples

Allochthonous rocks
Geologists distinguished the allochthonous rocks from those that were native to the region.
The layer contains allochthonous fossils, indicating they were transported from another area before burial.
These sediments are considered allochthonous because they were carried here by ancient rivers from distant mountains.
