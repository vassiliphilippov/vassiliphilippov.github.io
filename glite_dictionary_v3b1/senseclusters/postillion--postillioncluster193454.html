postillion

noun
person riding lead horse to guide carriage
5.4
Pronunciation: / poʊˈstɪliən /; / pəˈstɪliən / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪliən /; / pɒˈstɪlɪən / (BrE)
A person who rides the leading left-hand horse of a pair or team drawing a carriage, especially when there is no coachman.

Examples

A postillion rides horse
A skilled postillion was essential for navigating difficult roads without a coachman.
In old paintings, you can often spot the postillion riding at the front of the horses.
The royal carriage was guided by a postillion dressed in a bright uniform.
During their journey, they relied on a postilion to control the powerful team of horses.
The grand carriage was led by a skilled postilion dressed in fine livery.
Without a coachman, the postilion expertly guided the horses through the busy streets.
