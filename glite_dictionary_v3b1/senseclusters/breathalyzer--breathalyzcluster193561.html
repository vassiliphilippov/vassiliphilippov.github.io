breathalyzer

noun
device measuring alcohol in breath
9.6 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbreθ.ə.laɪ.zər/ /; / /ˈbreθ.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈbreθ.ə.laɪ.zə/ /; / /ˈbreθ.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a device used to measure the amount of alcohol in a person's breath, typically to determine intoxication

Examples

A breathalyzer test
Modern breathalyzers are portable and provide results within seconds.
She failed the breathalyzer and was charged with driving under the influence.
The police officer asked him to take a breathalyzer test after noticing the smell of alcohol.
After the party, he was relieved that the breathalyser showed he was under the legal limit.
New technology has made the breathalyser more accurate and easier to use.
The police officer asked the driver to blow into the breathalyser during the roadside check.
