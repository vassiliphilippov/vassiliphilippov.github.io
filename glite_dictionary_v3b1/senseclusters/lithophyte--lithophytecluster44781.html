lithophyte

noun
plant growing on rocks or stony soil
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪθəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəʊfaɪt / (BrE)
a plant that grows on rocks or stony soil and obtains nutrients mainly from the air and rainwater rather than from the soil

Examples

A lithophyte plant
Lichens and mosses often act as pioneering lithophytes in harsh, stone-filled environments.
Some orchids are classified as lithophytes, thriving directly on rocky surfaces.
The garden expert explained how certain ferns adapt as lithophytes in mountainous regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epiphyte - plant that grows non-parasitically upon another plant
  • antonym: terrestrial plant - plant that grows in the soil on land
