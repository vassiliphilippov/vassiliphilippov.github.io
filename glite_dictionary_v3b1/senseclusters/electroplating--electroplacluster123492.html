electroplating

noun
metal coating process
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈpleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈpleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of coating an object with a thin layer of metal using electricity

Examples

Electroplating process
The silverware was given a shiny finish through electroplating with pure silver.
Many car parts are protected from rust by electroplating them with a layer of nickel.
Jewelry manufacturers often use electroplating to create gold-plated accessories at a lower cost.
The silver electroplating on the cutlery made it look elegant and shiny.
Many jewelry pieces are made more affordable by using electroplating instead of solid gold.
After years of use, the electroplating on the lamp began to wear off.
The company specializes in electroplating car parts to prevent rust and improve appearance.
Advances in electroplating have made it possible to coat delicate jewelry with gold.
He learned the basics of electroplating during his apprenticeship at the factory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anodizing - an electrochemical process that thickens the natural oxide layer on metal surfaces
  • synonym: galvanization - the process of applying a protective zinc coating to steel or iron
  • synonym: plating - the process of covering a surface with a thin layer of metal
