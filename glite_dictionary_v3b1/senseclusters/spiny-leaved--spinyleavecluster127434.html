spiny-leaved

adjective
having leaves with sharp spines or thorns
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪni liːvd / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni liːvd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having leaves that are covered with or edged by sharp spines or thorns.

Examples

Spiny-leaved plants
Gardeners should wear gloves when handling spiny-leaved plants like agave or barberry.
Some spiny-leaved succulents are popular in drought-tolerant gardens.
The spiny-leaved holly bush deterred the children from climbing through the hedge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth-leaved - having leaves without spines or roughness
  • synonym: thorny - covered with or having many thorns
