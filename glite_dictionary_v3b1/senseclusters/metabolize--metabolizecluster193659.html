metabolize

verb
produce or process a substance by metabolism
1.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈtæ.bə.laɪz/ /; / /mɪ.ˈtæ.bə.laɪz/ (AmE), /mə.ˈtæ.bə.laɪz/ /; / /mɪ.ˈtæ.bə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to produce or process a substance through metabolism, especially by chemical or biological means within a living organism

Examples

To metabolize food
Plants metabolize sunlight into energy through the process of photosynthesis.
Some people are unable to metabolize lactose due to a genetic deficiency.
The liver can metabolize certain drugs, making them less effective over time.
After ingestion, the liver metabolises the drug into active compounds.
Certain bacteria can metabolise toxic chemicals into harmless substances.
Some plants are able to metabolise nitrogen efficiently from the soil.

  • synonym: catabolize - to break down complex molecules in metabolism
