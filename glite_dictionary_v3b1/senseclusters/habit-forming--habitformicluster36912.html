habit-forming

adjective
causing addiction or dependency
Pronunciation: / ˈhæb.ɪt ˌfɔr.mɪŋ /; / ˈhæbɪt ˌfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæb.ɪt ˌfɔː.mɪŋ /; / ˈhæbɪt ˌfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
likely to cause addiction or make someone dependent if used or done repeatedly

Examples

Habit-forming drugs
Many painkillers are considered habit-forming and should be taken only as prescribed.
Some people worry that caffeine can be habit-forming if consumed in large amounts on a daily basis.
The doctor warned him about the habit-forming potential of those sleeping pills.
Many prescription drugs are labeled as habit-forming and must be taken with caution.
Social media apps can be surprisingly habit-forming for people of all ages.
The doctor explained that some sleeping pills are habit-forming and should only be used temporarily.
