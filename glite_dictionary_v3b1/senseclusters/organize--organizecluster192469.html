organize

verb
form or join a labor union
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz /; / ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to form or establish a group, organization, or union

Examples

To organize a union
Efforts to organize the warehouse workers were met with resistance from management.
She helped her colleagues organize so they could demand better working conditions.
The employees voted to organize after months of contract disputes.
Activists worked together to organize the student group.
After the war, the community helped organize a relief association.
They decided to organize a new company to develop the product.
Efforts to organise the warehouse staff met with strong resistance from the company.
Many employees are reluctant to organise for fear of retaliation from management.
The workers planned to organise in order to negotiate better pay.
