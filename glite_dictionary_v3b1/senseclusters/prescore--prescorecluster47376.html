prescore

verb
to give preliminary score
6.7
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈskɑːr/ /; / /priː.ˈskɔːr/ (AmE), /priː.ˈskɔː/ (BrE)
to give a preliminary score or mark to something before the official evaluation or event

Examples

To prescore the entries
The judges decided to prescore the entries to speed up the final round of the competition.
Before the official grading, teachers will prescore the essays to identify any major issues.
The committee asked the panel to prescore all applications before the main review meeting.

Related Terms
