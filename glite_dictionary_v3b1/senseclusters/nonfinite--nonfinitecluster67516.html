nonfinite

adjective
not limited by tense, person, or number (of verbs)
9.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈfaɪ.naɪt/ /; / /nə.ˈfaɪ.naɪt/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈfaɪ.naɪt/ /; / /nə.ˈfaɪ.naɪt/ (BrE)
(of a verb form) not limited by tense, person, or number; typically used of infinitives, participles, and gerunds.

Examples

Nonfinite verb form
A nonfinite verb does not change according to the subject or tense.
Participles are one type of nonfinite verb form used in English.
The verb in the phrase 'to run' is a nonfinite form known as an infinitive.

Related Terms
