lynchpin

noun
vital central support or mainstay
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪn(t)ʃˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪn(t)ʃˌpɪn / (BrE)
A person or thing that is the most important part of a system, organization, or group, keeping it together and functioning.

Examples

Lynchpin of the team
Education is often considered the lynchpin of a thriving society.
Her insight has made her the lynchpin of the research team.
Strong leadership acted as the lynchpin holding the company together during tough times.

  • synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main element
