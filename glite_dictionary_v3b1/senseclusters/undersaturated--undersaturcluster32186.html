undersaturated

adjective
not fully supplied
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsætʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
having less than the usual or necessary amount of something, especially supply or competition in a market or area.

Examples

Undersaturated market
The technology sector in this region is still undersaturated, offering many opportunities for startups.
Because the market is undersaturated, new competitors can easily attract customers.
Analysts believe the city remains undersaturated with affordable housing options.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oversaturated - having more than the usual or necessary amount, especially of supply or competition
  • degree higher: saturated - completely filled or supplied, especially in a market or area
