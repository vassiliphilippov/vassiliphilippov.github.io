presurvey

verb
to inspect in advance
Pronunciation: / priːˈsɜːrveɪ / (AmE), / priːˈsɜːveɪ / (BrE)
to carry out a preliminary survey or inspection before a main survey, project, or event

Examples

To presurvey the area
The team will presurvey the land to identify any potential issues before starting the project.
It is important to presurvey the building to ensure safety for all workers.
Before the official count, researchers presurveyed the area to plan their approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully to check its condition
  • degree higher: survey - to examine or measure an area or situation in detail
