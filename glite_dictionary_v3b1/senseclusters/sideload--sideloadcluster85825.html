sideload

noun
lateral force
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌləʊd / (BrE)
a force or load applied to the side of a structure or vehicle, acting laterally rather than vertically or longitudinally

Examples

Sideload from strong winds
Engineers measured the sideload on the bridge during strong crosswinds.
The car's suspension is designed to handle significant sideload when turning sharply.
A sudden sideload caused the scaffolding to sway dangerously during the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: axial load - a force applied along the axis of a structure
  • synonym: side force - a force acting on the side of an object
