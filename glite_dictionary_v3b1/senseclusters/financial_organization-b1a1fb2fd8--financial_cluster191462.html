financial organization

noun
institution managing money and investments
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃl ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / fəˈnænʃəl ˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / faɪˈnænʃəl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an institution that collects funds and invests them in financial assets, such as banks, credit unions, or investment firms

Examples

The bank is a type of financial organization that offers loans and savings accounts.

Many people trust their savings to a reputable financial organisation.
The central bank plays a vital role as a financial organisation in the country's economy.
