octet

noun
group of eight; musical ensemble of eight
Pronunciation: / ɑkˈtɛt / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɛt / (BrE)
a group or set of eight people or things considered as a unit (especially in technical contexts, such as eight bits in computing)

Examples

A data octet
In networking, an octet refers to eight bits, the basic unit of digital data.
She joined an octet to sing choral music at the community event.
The piece was performed by a renowned string octet at the concert.
In computer networks, each octet represents eight bits of data.
The festival featured an octet playing a piece composed especially for eight instruments.
The jazz ensemble performed as an octet for the special concert.
