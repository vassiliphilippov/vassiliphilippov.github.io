enthrone

verb
install in a position of authority
7.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈθroʊn/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈθrəʊn/ (BrE)
to formally give someone a position of power or authority, especially in a ceremony (such as making someone a king, queen, or leader)

Examples

To enthrone a king
The board decided to enthrone him as the company's next CEO after months of deliberation.
The new king was enthroned in a grand ceremony attended by heads of state.
With her popular support, many believed democracy had finally been enthroned in the nation.
Historians note that she was enthroned at a very young age.
It took several months to enthrone the rightful heir after the king's death.
The new king was formally enthroned during an elaborate state ceremony.
In ancient traditions, priests would enthrone the king with elaborate rituals and blessings.
She hopes to enthrone a sense of justice and fairness as her guiding principles.
The board chose to enthrone the new CEO after months of searching for the right candidate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crown - officially declare someone to be king or queen, often with a ceremony
  • antonym: dethrone - to remove from a position of power or authority
