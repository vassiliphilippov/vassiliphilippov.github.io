wear out your welcome

verb
stay too long as a guest, become unwelcome
Pronunciation: / wɛr aʊt jʊr ˈwɛlkəm / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt jɔː ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
to stay too long as a guest and become unwelcome or make your host wish you would leave

Examples

To wear out your welcome quickly
After two weeks on their couch, he definitely wore out his welcome.
I'm starting to feel like I might wear out my welcome if I stay any longer.
She didn't want to wear out her welcome by visiting too often.
