infuscate

verb
darken with a brownish tinge
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfʌskeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfʌskeɪt / (BrE)
to darken or stain with a brownish or dusky tinge, especially describing the coloration of insects or plants

Examples

To infuscate seeds
As the seeds mature, their outer layer tends to infuscate, giving them a characteristic brown hue.
Some beetle species naturally infuscate the edges of their wing covers.
The scientist noted that the wings appeared to infuscate after exposure to sunlight.
