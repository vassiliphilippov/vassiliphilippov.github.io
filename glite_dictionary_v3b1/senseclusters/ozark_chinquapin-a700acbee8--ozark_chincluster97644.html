Ozark chinquapin

noun
a tree native to the southern Midwest US
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.zɑrk ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.zɑːk ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn / (BrE)
a shrubby tree native to the southern Midwestern United States, closely related to the Allegheny chinkapin, with larger leaves

Examples

The Ozark chinquapin tree
Recent restoration efforts have focused on reintroducing the Ozark chinquapin to its native habitat.
The Ozark chinquapin was once a dominant tree species in the region before it was devastated by blight.
The leaves of the Ozark chinquapin are notably larger than those of its close relative, the Allegheny chinkapin.
