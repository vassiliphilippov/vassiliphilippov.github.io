canopic vase

noun
ancient Egyptian jar for organs
5.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈnoʊpɪk veɪs / (AmE), / kəˈnɒpɪk vɑːz / (BrE)
a jar used in ancient Egypt to hold the internal organs removed during the mummification process

Examples

Ancient canopic vases
Archaeologists discovered a set of canopic vases during the excavation of the tomb.
Each canopic vase was dedicated to a specific organ and protected by a different deity.
The museum displayed a beautifully decorated canopic vase in its Egyptian antiquities section.
