adoptionist

noun
believer in Jesus's adoption
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈdɑpʃənɪst / (AmE), / əˈdɒpʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that Jesus was adopted as the Son of God rather than being divine by nature

Examples

An adoptionist thinker
The early church condemned the teachings of the adoptionist movement as heretical.
A prominent adoptionist argued that Jesus became divine at his baptism.
Historians study the influence of adoptionist beliefs on early Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trinitarian - a person who believes in the doctrine of the Trinity
