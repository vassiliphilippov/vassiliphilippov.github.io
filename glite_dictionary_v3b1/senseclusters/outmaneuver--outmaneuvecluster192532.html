outmaneuver

verb
gain advantage by clever action
9.2 6.2
Pronunciation: /aʊt.mə.ˈnuː.vər/ (AmE), /aʊt.mə.ˈnjuː.və/ (BrE)
to defeat or gain an advantage over someone by using greater skill or cleverness in planning or action

Examples

To outmaneuver an army
Despite being outnumbered, the general managed to outmaneuver the opposing army.
In negotiations, the company outmaneuvered its competitors by offering a better deal.
She tried to outmaneuver her colleague to get the promotion, but was unsuccessful.
Despite their experience, the defending champions were outmanoeuvred by the underdogs in the final.
She quickly realised she would need to outmanoeuvre her rivals to win the promotion.
The politician tried to outmanoeuvre his opponents during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outfox - to beat by cleverness or cunning
  • synonym: outmaneuver - to defeat by more skilful maneuvering (American spelling)
  • synonym: outsmart - to defeat or surpass by being more intelligent or cunning
  • synonym: outwit - to gain an advantage over someone by being more clever
