liberal

adjective
not literal; broad or free (esp. interpretation)
5.3 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪ.bə.rəl/ /; / /ˈlɪ.brəl/ (AmE), /ˈlɪ.brəl/ /; / /ˈlɪ.bə.rəl/ (BrE)
(of interpretation or translation) not strict or literal; allowing for broad or free expression of meaning.

Examples

Liberal translation
Her liberal interpretation of the rules allowed for much greater flexibility in the project.
Some critics argued his reading of the contract was too liberal and not supported by the text.
The poem was translated in a liberal way, prioritizing meaning over literal accuracy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literal - Taking words in their usual or most basic sense without metaphor or exaggeration
  • synonym: loose - Not rigid or strict in interpretation or application
  • antonym: strict - Exact or precise, especially in interpretation or application
