incriminate

verb
make someone appear guilty
4.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈkrɪ.mɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ɪŋ.ˈkrɪ.mɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈkrɪ.mə.neɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈkrɪ.mɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ɪŋ.ˈkrɪ.mɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈkrɪ.mə.neɪt/ (BrE)
to make someone seem guilty of a crime or wrongdoing, especially by suggesting or presenting evidence of their guilt

Examples

To incriminate a suspect
He refused to answer the question, fearing it might incriminate him.
Her testimony could potentially incriminate her colleagues in the scheme.
The evidence found at the scene seemed to incriminate the suspect.
His statement seemed designed to incriminate his business partner.
She refused to answer the question, fearing it might incriminate her.
The evidence could easily incriminate an innocent person if misinterpreted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuse - to say that someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • antonym: exonerate - to show or state that someone is not guilty
