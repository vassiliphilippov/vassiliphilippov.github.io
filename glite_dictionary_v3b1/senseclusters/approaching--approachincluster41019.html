(of an event or time) happening or likely to happen soon; imminent.

Examples

An approaching storm

The company is preparing for the approaching deadline.

Synonyms

  • imminent
    4.8 9.12/m
    imminent suggests something will happen very soon and feels urgent, while approaching just means coming nearer in time.
  • close at hand
    4.1 0.09/m
    close at hand indicates an event is almost here and often immediate, while approaching only signals it is coming soon.
  • at hand
    3.2 2.92/m
    at hand means something is ready or very near in time, while approaching describes it as coming closer.
  • impending
    4.0 4.80/m
    impending often feels threatening or negative and is about to happen, while approaching simply says it is coming soon.
  • coming
    1.0 0.00/m
    coming describes events expected to happen soon or planned, while approaching stresses their movement toward the present.
  • upcoming
    1.4 19/m
    upcoming is commonly used for scheduled future events, while approaching is more general and may not imply planning.
  • forthcoming
    4.2 5.35/m
    forthcoming is used in formal or written contexts for events that will happen, while approaching is neutral and widely used.
  • in the offing
    5.6 0.81/m
    in the offing is an informal phrase meaning likely to happen soon, while approaching is a plain adjective with no idiom.
  • coming
    1.0 6.84/m
    coming means the arrival of something or someone, while approaching describes the event or time that is moving nearer.
