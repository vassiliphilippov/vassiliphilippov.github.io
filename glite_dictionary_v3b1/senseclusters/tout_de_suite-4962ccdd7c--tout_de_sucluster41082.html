tout de suite

adverb
immediately; at once
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀê d…ô Ààswit / (AmE), / tuÀê d…ô ÀàswiÀêt / (BrE)
immediately; at once; without delay

Examples

Reserve tout de suite
If you want a seat, you must reserve tout de suite or they will be sold out.
The manager asked for the documents tout de suite, leaving no time to prepare.
When the alarm rang, everyone evacuated the building tout de suite.

Synonyms

  • immediately
    1.0 121/m
    immediately means happening at once without any delay in speech or writing, while tout de suite names that same urgent timing.
  • at once
    1.0 12/m
    at once is used in formal and everyday speech to say do something now, while tout de suite carries the same immediate demand.
  • right away
    1.0 27/m
    right away sounds casual and common in speech for quick action, while tout de suite expresses immediate action often in conversation.
  • instantly
    1.0 28/m
    instantly stresses that something happens in a single very short moment, while tout de suite means without delay but not always instantaneous.
  • straightaway
    3.2 0.19/m
    straightaway is chiefly British and casual for doing something now, while tout de suite is a French phrase meaning immediately.
