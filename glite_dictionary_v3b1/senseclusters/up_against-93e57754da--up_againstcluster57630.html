up against

adjective
facing a difficult challenge
4.0
Pronunciation: / ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
facing a difficult situation, obstacle, or opposing force that must be dealt with

Examples

To be up against problems
The team found themselves up against stronger opponents than they had expected.
We're up against a lot of pressure to finish this project by Friday.
When you are up against financial troubles, it's important to seek advice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confront - to face up to and deal with a problem or difficulty
  • degree higher: overcome - to successfully deal with or gain control of something difficult
