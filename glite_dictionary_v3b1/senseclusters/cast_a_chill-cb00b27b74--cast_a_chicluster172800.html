cast a chill

verb
cause unease or disappointment
Pronunciation: / kæst ə tʃɪl / (AmE), / kɑːst ə tʃɪl / (BrE)
to cause an atmosphere or feeling of unease, disappointment, or discomfort among people

Examples

To cast a chill on mood
Negative comments from the audience cast a chill on the presenter's enthusiasm.
Rumors of budget cuts have cast a chill on future project plans.
The news of the accident cast a chill over the family gathering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dampen spirits - to make people less enthusiastic or cheerful
  • antonym: lift spirits - to make people feel happier or more optimistic
