Petrarchan

adjective
of Petrarch's style
Pronunciation: / pəˈtrɑrkən / (AmE), / pɛˈtrɑːkən / (BrE)
relating to the Italian poet Petrarch or the style of poetry, especially sonnets, that he inspired

Examples

Petrarchan sonnet style
The poet wrote a Petrarchan sonnet to express his admiration for classical forms.
Her analysis focused on the Petrarchan influence in Renaissance literature.
Many English poets experimented with the Petrarchan structure before adopting other forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Shakespearean - relating to William Shakespeare or his works
  • synonym: sonnet - a poem of 14 lines with a specific rhyme scheme
