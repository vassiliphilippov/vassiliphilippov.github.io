even

adjective
having equal scores; tied (in contests)
Pronunciation: / ˈivən / (AmE), / ˈiːvən / (BrE)
equal in amount, value, or score; having no difference; tied in contests

Examples

Even score
After a late goal, the match is even again.
At halftime, the teams were even at 2–2.
The score remained even throughout most of the game.
The match ended with even scores after ninety minutes of play.
The two teams were so even that nobody could predict who would win.
We divided the cake into even pieces so everyone got the same amount.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ahead - having a higher score in a contest
  • antonym: behind - having a lower score in a contest
  • synonym: equal - being the same in quantity, size, degree, or value
  • synonym: tied - having equal scores in a contest
  • antonym: uneven - not equal or balanced; not level or regular
