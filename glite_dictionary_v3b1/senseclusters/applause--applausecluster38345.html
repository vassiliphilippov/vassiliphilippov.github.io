applause

noun
clapping to show approval or enjoyment
1.0 21/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpl…îz / (AmE), / …ôÀàpl…îÀêz / (BrE)
approval or enjoyment expressed by the clapping of hands, usually after a performance or speech

Examples

Loud applause after show
As the curtain fell, thunderous applause filled the theater.
She received a round of applause for her moving speech.
The audience erupted in applause after the final note was played.

Synonyms

  • clapping
    1.0 0.07/m
    clapping is the act of striking the palms to show approval, while applause is the usual name for that audience reaction.
  • hand clapping
    1.0 0.05/m
    hand clapping is repeated striking of the palms to show admiration, while applause is the common word for that audience response.
  • round of applause
    1.9 1.15/m
    round of applause is a short outbreak of audience clapping to praise someone, while applause can mean clapping in general.
  • hand
    4.0 11/m
    hand is a round of applause given to show approval, while applause is the general term for any clapping from the audience.
  • plaudits
    8.2 0.00/m
    plaudits are enthusiastic public praise or approval, while applause is the physical clapping an audience gives to show approval.

  • antonym: boos - shouts or noises expressing disapproval, the opposite of applause
