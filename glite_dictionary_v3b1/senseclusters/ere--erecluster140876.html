ere

conjunction
before (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛr / (AmE), / ɛə / (BrE)
used to mean 'before' in time, especially in literary or archaic contexts, introducing an earlier action or event

Examples

Ere you leave
He must leave ere the sun rises to reach the village by noon.
The letter arrived ere she had a chance to write her reply.
Finish your chores ere you go out to play.

Related Terms

  • antonym: after - later than in time or order
  • synonym: before - earlier than in time or order
