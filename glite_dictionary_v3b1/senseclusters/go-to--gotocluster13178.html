go-to

adjective
reliably preferred choice or resource
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊ tu /; / ɡoʊ tu / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ tuː /; / ɡəʊ tuː / (BrE)
describing someone or something as the most reliable, trusted, or preferred option for a particular purpose

Examples

Go-to solution
Coffee is my go-to drink when I need an energy boost.
She's the go-to person for advice about college applications.
That Italian restaurant is our go-to spot for family celebrations.
My go-to coffee shop always has a table and friendly baristas on busy mornings.
When I need technical help, Jenna is my go-to person because she explains things clearly.
For quick dinner ideas, this cookbook is our go-to resource during the week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: default - the option selected automatically or regarded as standard
  • synonym: fallback - alternative or backup option
  • synonym: preferred - chosen as more desirable or suitable
  • synonym: reliable - dependable or trustworthy
  • antonym: unreliable - not dependable, likely to fail
