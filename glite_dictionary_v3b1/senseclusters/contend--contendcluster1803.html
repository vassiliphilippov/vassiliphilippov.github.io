Examples

To contend a claim

She plans to contend the charges in court, believing they are unfounded.
The company decided to contend the results of the government audit.
He contended that the policy would only benefit the wealthy.

Synonyms

  • argue
    2.8 123/m
    argue is to give reasons and debate an idea openly, while contend often implies formally disputing or claiming something in argument.
  • contest
    4.6 5.56/m
    contest is to formally challenge a result or decision, while contend is used more widely for arguing or claiming in discussion.
  • assert
    3.9 28/m
    assert means to state something firmly as fact, while contend suggests arguing or disputing that claim in discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to agree to or not dispute something, the opposite of challenge
  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion as someone else
  • synonym: argue - to give reasons for or against something, often in disagreement
  • synonym: assert - state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • synonym: contest - to dispute or challenge something, often legally
  • antonym: deny - to state something is not true
