contend

verb
dispute; challenge (formally, legally)
5.4 3.7
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈtend/ (AmE), /kən.ˈtend/ (BrE)
to argue, dispute, or claim something, especially in a discussion or formal argument

Examples

To contend a claim
Environmental groups contended the development permit, citing potential harm to local wildlife.
She plans to contend the charges in court, believing they are unfounded.
The company decided to contend the results of the government audit.
He contended that the policy would only benefit the wealthy.
Many people contend that education should be free for everyone.
The scientist contends that his theory can explain these unusual results.
Some historians contend that the event never occurred as described.
The lawyers contended over the true meaning of the contract.
They contend about the best approach to solve the problem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to agree to or not dispute something, the opposite of challenge
  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion as someone else
  • synonym: argue - to give reasons for or against something, often in disagreement
  • synonym: assert - state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • synonym: contest - to dispute or challenge something, often legally
  • antonym: deny - to state something is not true
