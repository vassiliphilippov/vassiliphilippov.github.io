admission fee

noun
fee charged for entry
2.4 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / …ôdÀàm…™ É.…ôn fiÀê / (AmE), / …ôdÀàm…™ É.…ôn fiÀê / (BrE)
a sum of money charged for entry to a place, event, or institution

Examples

The admission fee
The museum's admission fee is discounted for children under twelve.
They decided not to go to the concert because the admission fee was too high.
We forgot to bring enough cash to pay the admission fee.

Synonyms

  • admission charge
    2.5 0.05/m
    an admission charge is a formal term for the entry payment, while an admission fee is the everyday label used by most organisations.
  • admission price
    1.6 0.05/m
    an admission price states the amount you must pay for entry, while an admission fee names the charge you pay to get in.
  • price of admission
    2.5 0.45/m
    the price of admission shows the amount you must pay to enter, while an admission fee is the standard term for that cost.
  • entrance fee
    1.6 0.49/m
    an entrance fee is the sum paid to enter a place or event, while an admission fee is the more common phrase for entry charges.
  • admission
    2.7 8.83/m
    admission can mean the act of entering or the cost to enter, while an admission fee refers only to the money paid.
  • entrance money
    3.3 0.01/m
    entrance money refers to the payment for entry but sounds old-fashioned or rare, while an admission fee is used commonly today.

  • synonym: entry fee - fee required to enter a place or event
