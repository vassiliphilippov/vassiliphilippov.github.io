Examples

Synonyms

  • partial
    4.7 1.98/m
    partial shows a clear preference for one side in a simple situation, while biased means not neutral or impartial.
  • prejudiced
    5.0 1.25/m
    prejudiced means judging people unfairly based on fixed ideas, while biased covers any unfair favoring of one side.
  • discriminatory
    6.0 3.92/m
    discriminatory describes actions or rules that treat groups unfairly, while biased can mean a private feeling or simple preference.
  • slanted
    6.2 0.02/m
    slanted presents facts in a way that favors one view, while biased describes not being neutral or impartial.
  • one-sided
    4.0 1.99/m
    one-sided gives only one point of view and ignores others, while biased means showing unfair favor or partiality.
  • partisan
    6.7 21/m
    partisan strongly supports a party or cause, often loudly and politically, while biased can be private or less political.
  • unfair
    1.0 22/m
    unfair is a general word for treating people badly or without justice, while biased points to a specific unfair favoring.
