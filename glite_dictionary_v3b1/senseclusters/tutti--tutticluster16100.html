tutti

adjective, adverb
all performers together
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːti / (AmE), / ˈtʊti / (BrE)
in music, an instruction or manner meaning all performers should play or sing together (not solo)

Examples

Tutti passage begins
When the conductor signaled tutti, the entire orchestra joined in for a powerful finale.
The choir sang the verse as a solo, then everyone came in for the tutti chorus.

After the trumpet solo, the orchestra entered tutti for a powerful finale.
The conductor signaled for tutti, and every musician joined in at once.
In this section, the choir sings tutti to create a rich, full sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ensemble - a group of musicians performing together
  • antonym: solo - a passage performed by a single musician or singer
