suicide squeeze

noun
baseball play: runner sprints home on pitch
Pronunciation: / ˈsuː.saɪd skwiz / (AmE), / ˈsuː.saɪd skwiːz / (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which the runner on third base sprints for home as the pitcher delivers the ball, relying on the batter to bunt.

Examples

A suicide squeeze play
Attempting a suicide squeeze is always a risky but exciting move in baseball.
During the suicide squeeze, the runner broke for home as soon as the pitcher started his motion.
The team pulled off a suicide squeeze to win the game in the final inning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: squeeze play - a baseball play where the batter bunts to allow a runner to score from third
