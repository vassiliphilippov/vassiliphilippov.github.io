Sioux

noun
member of Native American group (Sioux)
7.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / suÀê / (AmE), / suÀê / (BrE)
A member of a group of Native American peoples who traditionally inhabited the region from Lake Michigan to the Rocky Mountains and spoke a Siouan language.

Examples

Sioux leaders
Several Sioux leaders signed the treaty in Washington, D.C.
The Sioux fought to preserve their land and way of life during the 19th century.
Today, many Sioux nations maintain their cultural traditions and languages.

Synonyms
