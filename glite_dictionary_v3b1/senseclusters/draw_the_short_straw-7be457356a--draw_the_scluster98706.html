draw the short straw

verb
be chosen for an unpleasant task by chance
8.8
Pronunciation: / drɔ ðə ʃɔrt strɔ / (AmE), / drɔː ðə ʃɔːt strɔː / (BrE)
to be chosen or forced, usually by chance, to do an unpleasant or difficult task

Examples

To draw the short straw first
He drew the short straw and had to present the report on Monday.
In our office, I always seem to draw the short straw when it comes to working late.
Nobody wanted to clean the garage, so they decided to draw the short straw.

Related Terms

  • antonym: draw the long straw - to be chosen for a more favorable or advantageous task
  • synonym: get the short end of the stick - to receive the worst part or least benefit in a situation
