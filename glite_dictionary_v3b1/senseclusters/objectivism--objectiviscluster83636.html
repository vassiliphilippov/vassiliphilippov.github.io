objectivism

noun
philosophy valuing objectivity
7.9
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈdʒek.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /əb.ˈdʒek.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief or practice of basing opinions and actions on objective reality, facts, and reason rather than personal feelings or opinions

Examples

Scientific objectivism
Many philosophers have debated the principles of objectivism and its emphasis on reality.
Her approach to ethics was influenced by objectivism, focusing on facts rather than emotions.
The professor explained how objectivism differs from other philosophical viewpoints.
Good science depends on objectivism to ensure results are not influenced by personal beliefs.
The judge's commitment to objectivism helped guarantee a fair trial for everyone involved.
Many people value objectivism in journalism to avoid biased or misleading information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: objectivity - the quality of being unbiased and based on facts
  • synonym: rationalism - the belief that reason is the chief source of knowledge
  • antonym: subjectivism - the belief that knowledge is based on personal feelings or opinions
