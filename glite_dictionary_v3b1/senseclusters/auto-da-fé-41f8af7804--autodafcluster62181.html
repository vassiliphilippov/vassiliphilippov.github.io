auto-da-fé

noun
public heretic-burning ceremony
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔtoʊ də ˈfeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊ də ˈfeɪ / (BrE)
the public ceremony in which heretics were punished, especially by burning at the stake, during the Spanish Inquisition.

Examples

Historic auto-da-fé ceremony
Many historians have studied the auto-da-fé as a symbol of religious intolerance.
The chilling scenes of an auto-da-fé are described in several novels set during the Inquisition.
The condemned heretic was taken to the square for the auto-da-fé.
