correction

noun
the act of disciplining or punishing
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of punishing or disciplining someone in order to improve their behavior

Examples

Prison correction
Prison authorities believe in strict correction for inmates who break the rules.
Some parents prefer positive reinforcement over correction when guiding their children.
The school uses a system of correction to address repeated misbehavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discipline - the practice of training people to obey rules or a code of behavior, using punishment to correct disobedience
  • antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement, often as an opposite to discipline
