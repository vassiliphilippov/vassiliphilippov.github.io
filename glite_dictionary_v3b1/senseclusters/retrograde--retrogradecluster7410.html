retrograde

verb
move backward or contrary to norm
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtrəˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəˌɡreɪd /; / ˈrɛtrəʊˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
to move backward or in a direction opposite to the usual or expected

Examples

To retrograde along a path
Astronomers observed the comet briefly retrograding along its orbit before resuming its path.
During certain periods, Mercury will retrograde, confusing astrologers and laypeople alike.
The planet appears to retrograde for several weeks each year.
After the tide peaked, the water started to retrograde toward the sea.
As the ice age ended, the massive glaciers began to retrograde across the valley.
In rare cases, some diseases can cause the optic nerve to retrograde instead of growing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward in a purposeful way
  • synonym: recede - to move back or further away; retreat
