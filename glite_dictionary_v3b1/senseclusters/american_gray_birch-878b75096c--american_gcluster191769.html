American gray birch

noun
birch tree of eastern North America
6.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən greɪ bɜːrtʃ/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən greɪ bɜːtʃ/ (BrE)
A medium-sized birch tree (Betula populifolia) native to eastern North America, with white or pale grey bark and low-value wood, often found in second-growth forests.

Examples

The American gray birch tree
Due to its quick growth, the American gray birch often colonizes areas after a forest fire.
The American gray birch is commonly seen in abandoned fields across the northeastern United States.
Unlike other birches, the American gray birch has wood that is not highly valued by the timber industry.
In some regions, the American grey birch quickly colonizes land after a forest fire.
The American grey birch is easily recognized by its distinctive pale bark.
The woods behind our house are filled with American grey birch and young maples.
