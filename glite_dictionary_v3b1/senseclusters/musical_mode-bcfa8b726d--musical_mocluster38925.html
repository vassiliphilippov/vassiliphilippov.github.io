musical mode

noun
type of musical scale or mode
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuzɪkəl moʊd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl məʊd / (BrE)
a type of scale characterized by a fixed sequence of natural notes within an octave, used as the basis for composition in Western music

Examples

Musical mode scale
Learning each musical mode helps musicians understand the character of various Western music traditions.
Medieval chants often make use of different musical modes rather than major or minor scales.
The piece was composed in the Dorian musical mode, giving it a distinctive sound.
