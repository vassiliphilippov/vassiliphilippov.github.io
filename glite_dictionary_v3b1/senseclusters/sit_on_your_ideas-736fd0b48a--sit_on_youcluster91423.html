sit on your ideas

verb
delay sharing or acting on ideas
1.2 5.7
Pronunciation: /sɪt ɑːn jɔːr aɪ.ˈdiː.əz/ /; / /sɪt ɑːn jər aɪ.ˈdiː.əz/ (AmE), /sɪt ɒn jɔː aɪ.ˈdɪ.əz/ /; / /sɪt ɒn jə aɪ.ˈdɪ.əz/ (BrE)
to delay sharing or acting on your ideas, often by keeping them to yourself instead of taking action or telling others

Examples

To sit on your ideas
If you just sit on your ideas, you'll miss out on valuable feedback.
It's unproductive to sit on your ideas when innovation is encouraged.
She regretted sitting on her ideas instead of presenting them to the team.
