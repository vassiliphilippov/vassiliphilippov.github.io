irrebuttable

adjective
cannot be disproved
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈbʌtəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈbʌtəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to disprove or refute, especially in a legal context

Examples

Irrebuttable evidence
The judge considered the witness's statement to be irrebuttable evidence in the case.
Some legal presumptions are irrebuttable and cannot be challenged in court.
The contract included an irrebuttable clause that protected both parties from future disputes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indisputable - impossible to question or doubt
  • antonym: rebuttable - able to be disproved or refuted
