restaff

verb
to reassign employees
7.0
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈstæf/ /; / /rɪ.ˈstæf/ (AmE), /riː.ˈstæf/ /; / /rɪ.ˈstæf/ (BrE)
to reorganize or reassign existing employees to different jobs, roles, or teams within an organization without hiring new people

Examples

To restaff with current workers
The company decided to restaff the customer service department after several employees changed roles.
Following the budget cuts, management had to restaff teams using only current employees.
To improve efficiency, the hospital will restaff its night shift with more experienced nurses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hire - to employ new people for jobs
  • synonym: reorganize - to arrange or structure again in a new way
