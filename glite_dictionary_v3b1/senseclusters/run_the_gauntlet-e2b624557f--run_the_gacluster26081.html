run the gauntlet

verb
endure many challenges or criticisms
Pronunciation: / rʌn ðə ˈɡɔnt.lət / (AmE), / rʌn ðə ˈɡɔːnt.lɪt / (BrE)
to be subjected to a series of difficult or unpleasant experiences or criticisms

Examples

To run the gauntlet of criticism
After revealing his controversial opinion, he had to run the gauntlet of public criticism.
During the campaign, she bravely ran the gauntlet of negative media coverage.
The new employee was forced to run the gauntlet of tough questions from senior staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: face the music - accept unpleasant consequences or criticism
  • synonym: walk on eggshells - act very carefully to avoid upsetting someone or causing problems
