trichiasis

noun
inward-growing eyelashes
Pronunciation: / trɪˈkaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / trɪˈkaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which eyelashes grow inward toward the eye, causing irritation or discomfort

Examples

Trichiasis eye condition
The patient complained of eye pain, and the doctor found trichiasis during the examination.
Surgery may be needed if trichiasis causes repeated irritation or damage to the eye.
People with chronic trichiasis often experience redness and watering of the eyes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blepharitis - inflammation of the eyelids
  • synonym: entropion - a condition where the eyelid turns inward
