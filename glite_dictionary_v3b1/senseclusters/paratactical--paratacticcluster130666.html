paratactical

adjective
side-by-side clauses
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈtæktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈtæktɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the placement of clauses or phrases side by side without using connecting words to show their relationship

Examples

Paratactical style
The writer's paratactical style gives the story a fast, direct rhythm.
In ancient texts, paratactical sentences are common, often lacking conjunctions.
Her essay uses a paratactical structure, linking ideas with simple, short statements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypotactical - relating to the use of subordination in clauses or phrases
