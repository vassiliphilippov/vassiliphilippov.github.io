strike down

verb
cause to fall by a blow or force
4.9 6.8
Pronunciation: / straɪk daʊn / (AmE), / straɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to cause someone to fall, become ill, or die suddenly, especially by force, illness, or disaster

Examples

Illness strikes down workers

Lightning struck down the power lines, leaving the area without electricity.
The old oak tree was struck down during the violent storm.
He was struck down in his prime by a sudden heart attack.
Many soldiers were struck down by the mysterious illness during the winter.
The epidemic struck down entire families within a matter of weeks.
