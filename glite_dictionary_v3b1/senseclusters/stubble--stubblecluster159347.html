stubble

noun
seed husks and stem fragments left after threshing
3.4 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈstʌb.əl/ (AmE), /ˈstʌb.əl/ (BrE)
Material consisting of seed husks and small fragments of stem or leaf that remain after seeds have been separated, often as a byproduct of threshing or processing grain.

Examples

Wheat stubble remains
After the wheat harvest, heaps of stubble were left scattered across the field.
Farmers often burn stubble to clear their fields for the next planting season.
The old mill was filled with stubble and other remnants from last season's crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaff - the husks of grains and grasses separated during threshing
