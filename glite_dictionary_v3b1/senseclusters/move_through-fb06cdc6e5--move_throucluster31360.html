move through

verb
pass through a place or area
1.5
Pronunciation: / muːv θruː / (AmE), / muːv θruː / (BrE)
to pass from one side, part, or area to another as part of a journey or process

Examples

To move through a forest
The hikers moved through the dense forest before reaching the mountain lake.
The proposal must move through several committees before it can become law.
We watched the parade move through the city streets with crowds cheering on both sides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pass through - to go through a place without staying for long
