cheloid

noun
raised scar tissue from excessive healing
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkiːlɔɪd / (BrE)
a raised and thickened area of pinkish scar tissue that forms at the site of an injury due to excessive tissue repair

Examples

A cheloid scar
After her ear piercing became infected, a cheloid developed at the site.
Doctors recommended treatment to reduce the size of the cheloid.
Some people are more prone to developing cheloids after surgery.
