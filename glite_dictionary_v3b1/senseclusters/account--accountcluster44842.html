account

noun
use for advantage or benefit
Pronunciation: / əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
the quality of being important, valuable, or useful; worth or significance

Examples

Put to good account
He always tries to make sure his time is put to best account.
She turned her writing skills to good account in her new career.
The company put new technology to excellent account to improve efficiency.
Although it seemed a minor issue, it soon became a matter of considerable account.
She was a person of great account in the community, respected by all.
The opinion of the committee is of little account in these decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable condition or circumstance
  • synonym: importance - the state or quality of being important
  • antonym: insignificance - the quality of having little or no importance
  • synonym: profit - benefit or advantage, especially financial gain
  • synonym: significance - the quality of being worthy of attention; importance
