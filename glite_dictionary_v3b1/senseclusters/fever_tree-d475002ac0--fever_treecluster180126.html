fever tree

noun
ornamental tree whose bark treats fever
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːvər triː / (AmE), / ˈfiːvə triː / (BrE)
a tree or shrub whose leaves or bark are used to treat fever, or that is believed to indicate areas free from fever, especially in Africa and the US

Examples

A fever tree grove
During our hike, we stumbled upon a grove of blooming fever trees near the riverbank.
The fever tree is famed for the medicinal properties of its bark.
Traditional remedies sometimes use extracts from the fever tree to treat certain illnesses.
Local healers harvested the leaves of the fever tree to prepare herbal remedies.
The plantation included hundreds of fever trees cultivated for their valuable medicinal oils.
Young fever trees are recognizable by their unique bluish foliage along the riverbanks.
During their safari, they camped beneath a tall fever tree by the riverbank.
Some believed that the presence of a fever tree signaled healthy, malaria-free ground.
The bark of the fever tree was once widely used as a remedy for malaria.
