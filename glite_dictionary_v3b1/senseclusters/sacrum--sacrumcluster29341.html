sacrum

noun
triangular pelvic bone at base of spine
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪkrəm / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrəm / (BrE)
The large, triangular bone at the base of the spine, composed of five fused vertebrae, forming the back part of the pelvis and connecting the spine to the hip bones.

Examples

The sacrum bone
After the fall, she experienced pain near her sacrum and lower back.
The human sacrum is made up of vertebrae that are fused together.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's sacrum before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coccyx - the small bone at the base of the spinal column, below the sacrum
