Franklin Delano Roosevelt

proper noun
32nd U.S. President; led during Great Depression and WWII
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋklɪn ˈdɛləˌnoʊ ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈfræŋklɪn ˈdɛlənəʊ ˈruːzəvɛlt / (BrE)
(1882–1945) the 32nd President of the United States, serving four terms; he instituted the New Deal to address the Great Depression and led the nation during World War II.

Examples

Franklin Delano Roosevelt presidency
During World War II, Franklin Delano Roosevelt worked closely with Allied leaders to coordinate military strategy.
Many historians consider Franklin Delano Roosevelt one of the most influential American presidents.
The New Deal policies introduced by Franklin Delano Roosevelt reshaped the U.S. economy and government.
