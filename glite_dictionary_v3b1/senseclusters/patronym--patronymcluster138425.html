patronym

noun
surname from father's name
4.6 7.8
Pronunciation: /pæ.trə.ˈnɪm/ /; / /ˈpæt.roʊ.nɪm/ (AmE), /ˈpæ.trəʊ.nɪm/ (BrE)
A family name derived from the given name of one's father or a paternal ancestor, often formed by adding an affix.

Examples

A patronym surname
In Iceland, a patronym is commonly used instead of a family surname.
Many cultures form a patronym by adding specific prefixes or suffixes to the father's name.
The name 'Johnson' is a classic example of an English patronym.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surname - A hereditary name common to all members of a family
