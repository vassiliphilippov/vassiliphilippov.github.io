inholding

noun
private land within public
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
a privately owned piece of land that is located within the boundaries of public land, such as a national park or forest

Examples

A private inholding
The family owns an inholding in the middle of the national park.
Developers are interested in purchasing the inholding for a new lodge.
Access to the inholding requires a permit from the park authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enclave - a territory or area surrounded by another territory
  • antonym: outholding - a piece of land outside the main area of ownership
