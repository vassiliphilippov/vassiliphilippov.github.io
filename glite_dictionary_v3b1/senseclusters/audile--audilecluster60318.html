audile

noun
person with auditory mental imagery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːdaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdaɪl / (BrE)
a person whose mental imagery is predominantly auditory rather than visual or motor

Examples

An audile person
As an audile, she often remembers information by repeating it to herself.
He identified himself as an audile, finding it easier to imagine sounds than pictures.
Teachers may notice that audiles learn best through listening and discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: motor - a person whose mental imagery is primarily related to movement
  • antonym: visual - a person whose mental imagery is primarily visual
