zirconium

noun
a metallic chemical element (Zr)
Pronunciation: / zɝːˈkoʊniəm / (AmE), / zɜːˈkəʊniəm / (BrE)
a lustrous, grey, strong metallic element, used especially in nuclear reactors and obtained chiefly from the mineral zircon

Examples

Pure zirconium metal
Researchers developed a new alloy by combining zirconium with titanium.
The fuel rods in the reactor are encased in zirconium because of its low absorption of neutrons.
Zircon is the primary mineral from which zirconium is extracted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: titanium - a strong, light, lustrous metallic element, symbol Ti
  • degree lower: zircon - a mineral, main source of zirconium
