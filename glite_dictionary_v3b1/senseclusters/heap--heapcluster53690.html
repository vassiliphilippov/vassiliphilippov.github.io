heap

noun
pile of things on top of each other
1.1 4.93/m
Pronunciation: / hip / (AmE), / hiÀêp / (BrE)
A pile of things placed haphazardly on top of each other.

Examples

A heap of clothes
A heap of old newspapers was stacked in the corner of the room.
Children ran and jumped into the heap of autumn leaves in the garden.
She threw all her clothes into a heap on the floor.

Synonyms

  • pile
    1.9 30/m
    pile is a general word for many things stacked together, while heap suggests a rough or untidy arrangement.
  • mound
    2.1 4.12/m
    mound is a rounded pile often formed on purpose or by nature, while heap is a more untidy pile.
  • agglomerate
    9.1 0.01/m
    agglomerate is a technical or formal word for objects stuck together, while heap is everyday and suggests a loose, messy pile.

  • synonym: pile - an accumulation of objects placed one on top of another
