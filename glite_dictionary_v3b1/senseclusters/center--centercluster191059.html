center

adjective
moderate in politics or thought
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɚ /; / ˈsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə /; / ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
(of politics or intellectual stance) not aligning with either the right or left wing; holding moderate or middle-of-the-road views.

Examples

Center political view
Her political views are generally center, avoiding both extremes.
In many debates, his opinions tend to be center, seeking compromise between both sides.
The party adopted a more center approach to attract undecided voters.
Many economists hold centre views, avoiding extremes of either political side.
She was known as a centre politician, favoring balanced and pragmatic policies.
The party adopted a centre stance to appeal to a broader range of voters.
