type I diabetes

noun
insulin-dependent diabetes, starts in childhood
2.9 2.9
Pronunciation: /taɪp wʌn daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz/ /; / /taɪp wʌn daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs/ (AmE), /taɪp wʌn daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz/ /; / /taɪp wʌn daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs/ (BrE)
a chronic medical condition in which the pancreas produces little or no insulin, typically beginning in childhood or adolescence, requiring lifelong insulin therapy

Examples

Type I diabetes treatment
Insulin injections are an essential part of managing type I diabetes in children and adults.
People diagnosed with type I diabetes must monitor their blood sugar levels carefully each day.
Some early symptoms of type I diabetes include increased thirst, frequent urination, and weight loss.
