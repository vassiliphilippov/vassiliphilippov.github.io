oriental bittersweet

noun
an ornamental Asiatic vine with colorful fruit
Pronunciation: / ɔːriˈɛntəl ˈbɪtərˌswit / (AmE), / ˌɒːriˈɛntəl ˈbɪtəswiːt / (BrE)
a climbing Asiatic vine (Celastrus orbiculatus), grown for its showy orange-yellow fruits with scarlet arils, often considered invasive in North America

Examples

The oriental bittersweet vine
Bright orange fruits distinguish the oriental bittersweet from other vines in the region.
Gardeners are warned that oriental bittersweet can quickly take over native vegetation.
The oriental bittersweet is commonly planted for its decorative berries in the fall.
