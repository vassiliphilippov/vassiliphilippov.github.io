thyrotropic hormone

noun
pituitary hormone that activates the thyroid
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊpɪk ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪrəˈtrəʊpɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the activity of the thyroid gland

Examples

Thyrotropic hormone levels
An imbalance of thyrotropic hormone can lead to either hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.
Doctors may test levels of thyrotropic hormone to diagnose thyroid disorders.
The thyrotropic hormone plays a crucial role in regulating metabolism by stimulating the thyroid gland.
