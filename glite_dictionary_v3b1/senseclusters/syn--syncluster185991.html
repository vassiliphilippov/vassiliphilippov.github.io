syn

noun
TCP connection flag
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
a control flag in the TCP networking protocol used to initiate a connection between computers

Examples

Syn packet sent
The client sends a syn packet to start the TCP handshake with the server.
A firewall may block incoming syn requests to prevent unauthorized access.
Network engineers often analyze syn packets when troubleshooting connection issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ACK - a TCP flag used to acknowledge receipt of data
  • synonym: SYN-ACK - a TCP flag sent in response to a SYN to acknowledge and synchronize
