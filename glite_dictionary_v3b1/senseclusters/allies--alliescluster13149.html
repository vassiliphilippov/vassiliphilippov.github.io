allies

noun
nations joined against a common enemy
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈælaɪz / (AmE), / ˈælaɪz / (BrE)
countries or groups that have joined together for a common purpose, especially to fight against a common enemy

Examples

Allies in war
Several European countries became allies to face the growing military threat.
The Allies emerged victorious in World War II against the Axis powers.
The leaders gathered to strengthen the bond between their allies before the conflict escalated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit
  • antonym: enemy - a person or group actively opposing or hostile to another
