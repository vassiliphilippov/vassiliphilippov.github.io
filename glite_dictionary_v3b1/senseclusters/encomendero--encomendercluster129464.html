encomendero

noun
colonial tribute holder
9.9
Pronunciation: / eŋˌkɔːmɛnˈdɛɾoʊ /; / ɛŋˌkɔmɛnˈdɛroʊ / (AmE), / eŋˌkɒmɛnˈdɛərəʊ /; / ɛŋˌkɒmɛnˈdɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a person in Spanish colonial America who was granted the right by the Spanish crown to collect tribute and demand labor from indigenous people

Examples

The encomendero collects tribute
The encomendero was responsible for collecting tribute from the local population.
During the colonial era, an encomendero often managed large areas of land and labor.
Many indigenous people were forced to work for the encomendero under harsh conditions.
The Spanish crown appointed an encomendero to oversee the local indigenous community.
Each encomendero was expected to protect and convert the people under his control.
The abuses committed by the encomenderos led to reforms in colonial policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonist - a person who settles in a colony
  • synonym: conquistador - a Spanish conqueror of the Americas
  • synonym: vassal - a person granted land in exchange for service
  • antonym: viceroy - a ruler exercising authority in a colony on behalf of a sovereign
