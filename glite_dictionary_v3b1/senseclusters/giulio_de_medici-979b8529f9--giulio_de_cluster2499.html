Giulio de' Medici

proper noun
Italian pope (Clement VII), 1523–1534
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːlioʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːliəʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1478–1534) Italian pope, known as Clement VII, who ruled from 1523 to 1534 and is noted for breaking with Henry VIII after Henry's divorce from Catherine of Aragon and marriage to Anne Boleyn.

Giulio de' Medici became Pope Clement VII at a time of great political turmoil in Europe.
