groundspeed

noun
speed of an aircraft relative to the ground
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌspiːd / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndspiːd / (BrE)
the speed at which an aircraft moves relative to the ground, as measured by an observer on the ground

Examples

The groundspeed of aircraft
Air traffic control monitors the groundspeed of all approaching aircraft.
The pilot calculated the groundspeed to ensure an accurate arrival time.
With a strong tailwind, the airplane's groundspeed increased significantly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: airspeed - the speed of an aircraft relative to the air through which it is moving
