generalized anxiety disorder

noun
chronic anxiety disorder with excessive worry
3.8 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.ə.rə.laɪzd æŋ.ˈzaɪ.ə.tiː dɪs.ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.ə.rə.laɪzd æŋ.ˈzaɪ.ə.tiː dɪs.ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
a mental health condition characterized by persistent, excessive worry and anxiety about various aspects of life, accompanied by physical symptoms such as tension, sweating, or irritability, lasting for more than six months

Examples

Generalized anxiety disorder symptoms
A doctor diagnosed her with generalized anxiety disorder after months of persistent tension and nervousness.
People with generalized anxiety disorder often find it hard to control their worrying, even about everyday matters.
Treatment for generalized anxiety disorder usually includes therapy, medication, or a combination of both.
