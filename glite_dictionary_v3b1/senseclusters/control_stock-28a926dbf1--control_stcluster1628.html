control stock

noun
shares conferring controlling interest in a company
3.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl stɑk / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl stɒk / (BrE)
shares of a company's stock owned by shareholders who have a controlling interest, giving them the ability to influence or direct company decisions

Examples

Control stock shares
Investors competed to purchase control stock during the corporate takeover.
Owning control stock allows shareholders to influence key policy decisions.
The CEO acquired enough control stock to dictate the company's future direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: majority stock - stock that represents the majority of shares held
