anti-submarine rocket

noun
ship-launched weapon against submarines
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti səbˈmɑː.riːn ˈrɑː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti səˈmɑː.rɪn ˈrɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
A rocket-based weapon system mounted on ships, used for attacking submarines from the surface.

Examples

Ship anti-submarine rocket
Modern navies equip their ships with anti-submarine rockets for defensive maneuvers.
The destroyer fired an anti-submarine rocket after detecting movement beneath the waves.
The new anti-submarine rocket system increased the fleet's capability to neutralize underwater threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depth charge - explosive anti-submarine weapon dropped into water
