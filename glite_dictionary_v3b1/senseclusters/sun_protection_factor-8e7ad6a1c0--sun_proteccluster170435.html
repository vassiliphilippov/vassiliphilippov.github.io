sun protection factor

noun
measure of sunscreen's effectiveness
3.2 3.0
Pronunciation: /sʌn prə.ˈtek.ʃən ˈfæk.tər/ (AmE), /sʌn prə.ˈtek.ʃən ˈfæk.tə/ (BrE)
A measure of the effectiveness of a sunscreen in protecting the skin from ultraviolet (UV) rays of the sun.

Examples

A sun protection factor rating
A higher sun protection factor means greater protection against burning.
Many dermatologists recommend using a sun protection factor of at least 30 for daily use.
When choosing sunscreen, check the sun protection factor for the level of defense you need.
