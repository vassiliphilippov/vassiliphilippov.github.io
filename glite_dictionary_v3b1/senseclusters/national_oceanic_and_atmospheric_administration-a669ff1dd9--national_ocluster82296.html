National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration

proper noun
U.S. agency for ocean and atmospheric monitoring
Pronunciation: / ˌnæʃəˈnæl oʊˌʃænɪk ənd ˌætmosˈfɛrɪk ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnaʃəˈnal əʊˌʃænɪk ənd ˌatməsˈfɛrɪk ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a U.S. federal agency within the Department of Commerce responsible for monitoring and conserving ocean and atmospheric resources, providing weather forecasts, and forecasting natural disasters related to weather

Examples

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration forecast
According to the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, the upcoming week will see unusually heavy rainfall.
Scientists at the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration are tracking changes in ocean temperatures.
The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration issued a hurricane warning for the southeastern coast.
