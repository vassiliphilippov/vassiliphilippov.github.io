tally

verb
to record or count scores
Pronunciation: / ˈtæli / (AmE), / ˈtæli / (BrE)
to count, record, or add up numbers, points, or items; to gain or accumulate points or scores

Examples

To tally the score
During the match, she tallied the team's points on a scoreboard.
It's your turn to tally the results for this round of trivia.
They tallied the votes before announcing the winner.
She tallied the number of books sold at the end of the day.
The volunteers are tallying the supplies to ensure nothing is missing.
We need to tally all the votes before announcing the results.
Our school's quiz team hopes to tally more points in the final round.
She tallied twenty-five points by the end of the match.
The team managed to tally three goals in the first half.

Related Terms

  • synonym: count - to say numbers in order or calculate the total
  • synonym: score - to gain points or record points in a game
  • antonym: subtract - to take one number or amount away from another
  • synonym: sum - to add things together to find a total
