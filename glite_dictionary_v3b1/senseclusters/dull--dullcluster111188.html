dull

adjective
not responsive or alert

not alert, quick, or intelligent; slow to notice, react, or learn (used for people or minds)

Examples

Dull student
After staying up all night, she felt dull and unresponsive in the morning meeting.
His eyes had a dull look, as if he was not really present.
The medication left him dull to his surroundings and slow to react.
Despite months of tutoring, he remained dull in most academic subjects.
Her teachers felt she was a bit dull, but she excelled in sports.
The class was designed to help students who were considered dull by the regular curriculum.

Synonyms

  • slow
    4.4 16/m
    slow is commonly used to describe someone not quick to learn or think, while dull suggests a lack of alertness or mental sharpness.
  • obtuse
    7.4 1.09/m
    obtuse sounds formal and can mean slow to understand subtle points, while dull describes general slowness of mind or alertness.
  • dim
    6.5 1.33/m
    dim is an informal, mild way to say someone is not very bright, while dull focuses on slow thinking or notice.
  • dense
    6.8 1.43/m
    dense is a strong, rude word for someone who seems stupid, while dull refers more to slow thinking or low alertness.
