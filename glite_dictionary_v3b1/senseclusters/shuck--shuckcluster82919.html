shuck

noun
outer covering of seeds; seed husk
Pronunciation: / ʃʌk / (AmE), / ʃʌk / (BrE)
the outer covering of a seed or the husk and other small plant pieces separated from seeds, especially after threshing or processing

Examples

A corn shuck
After shelling the beans, he gathered the shuck into a basket.
Farmers use the shuck as animal bedding or compost.
The process left piles of shuck on the ground after the corn was sorted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: husk - the dry outer covering of some fruits or seeds
