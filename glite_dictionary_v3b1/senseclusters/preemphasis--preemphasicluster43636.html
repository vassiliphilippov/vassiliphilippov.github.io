preemphasis

noun
signal boosting process
9.3
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈem.fə.sɪs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈem.fə.sɪs/ (AmE), /priː.ˈem.fə.sɪs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈem.fə.sɪs/ (BrE)
a process or technique that increases the strength of high-frequency signals before transmission or recording to improve quality or reduce noise

Examples

Preemphasis in audio
Audio engineers often use preemphasis to reduce noise in radio transmissions.
The preemphasis circuit boosts high frequencies before the signal is sent.
Without preemphasis, the transmitted audio may sound distorted or unclear.
The audio engineer applied preemphasis to improve the clarity of the recorded music.
Modern FM transmitters often use preemphasis to reduce noise in high-frequency signals.
Without preemphasis, the broadcast would sound dull and lack crispness in the treble range.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deemphasis - the process of reducing high-frequency signals after transmission
  • synonym: equalization - the process of adjusting the balance between frequency components
