turn

verb
change focus or attention
1.0 187/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn / (AmE), / t…úÀên / (BrE)
to direct or change one's attention, interest, or focus toward or away from something

Examples

To turn attention
After years of working as a lawyer, he decided to turn his attention to writing novels.
Many people turn away from social media to focus on their mental health.
When feeling anxious, she often turns to meditation for comfort.
In times of trouble, many turn to their faith for comfort.
People often turn to the internet when they need quick answers.
When he lost his job, he turned to his friends for assistance.
During the interview, she turned a charming smile on the skeptical reporter.
She turned her gaze toward the stranger standing in the doorway.
The teacher turned his attention to the next student in line.

Synonyms

  • reorient
    5.6 1.21/m
    reorient is more formal and often means to change a plan's or person's overall focus, while turn means shift attention in everyday speech.
  • turn to
    2.7 17/m
    turn to is used when you address or ask someone for help, while turn is a general verb for changing attention or focus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divert - to turn aside from a course or direction
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
  • synonym: point - to direct something toward a particular target
  • synonym: shift - to change position or direction, especially attention or focus
  • synonym: train - to aim or point something toward a target
