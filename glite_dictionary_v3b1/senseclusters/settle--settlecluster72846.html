settle

verb
resolve or come to an agreement
4.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈse.təl/ (AmE), /ˈse.təl/ (BrE)
to resolve or bring an argument, dispute, or problem to an end by making a decision or agreement

Examples

To settle an argument
After hours of negotiation, they finally settled their differences.
The two companies agreed to settle the dispute out of court.
We need to settle this issue before moving on to the next agenda item.
Before we can proceed, we need to settle the details of the contract.
The two companies finally settled their differences after weeks of negotiation.
They met to settle the terms of the agreement before signing.
After months of negotiation, the company decided to settle with the former employee out of court.
Rather than go through a lengthy trial, both sides opted to settle and end the dispute.
The opposing lawyers agreed to settle the lawsuit before the case reached the judge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agree - to reach a mutual decision or understanding
  • synonym: decide - to make a decision after considering
  • antonym: dispute - a disagreement or argument, especially one that is prolonged or legal
  • synonym: finalize - to make final; to complete the last details of something
  • antonym: prolong - to make something last longer, delay conclusion
  • antonym: reopen - to open or consider again after being closed or settled
  • synonym: resolve - to find a solution or decide firmly
