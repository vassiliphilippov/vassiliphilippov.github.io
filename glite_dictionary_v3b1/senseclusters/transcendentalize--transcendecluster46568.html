transcendentalize

verb
to treat as a priori
9.9
Pronunciation: /træn.sən.den.ˈtæl.aɪz/ /; / /træn.sen.den.ˈtæl.aɪz/ (AmE), /træn.sən.den.ˈtæl.aɪz/ /; / /træn.sen.den.ˈtæl.aɪz/ (BrE)
to interpret or analyze something as a fundamental or a priori condition for experience, especially in philosophy

Examples

To transcendentalize knowledge
The philosopher tried to transcendentalize the concept of time in his latest book.
Some critics argue that attempts to transcendentalize art remove it from real-world experience.
In her essay, she transcendentalizes perception by focusing on its underlying conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empiricize - to interpret something based on experience or observation
  • synonym: philosophize - to think or reason in a philosophical way
