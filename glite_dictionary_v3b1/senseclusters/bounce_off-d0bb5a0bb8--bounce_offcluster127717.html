bounce off

verb
rebound or ricochet from a surface
3.4 2.62/m
Pronunciation: / baÊŠns É‘f / (AmE), / baÊŠns É’f / (BrE)
(of an object) to rebound or ricochet away from a surface after hitting it.

Examples

To bounce off a wall
Raindrops bounced off the windshield, making it hard to see ahead.
She watched the pebble bounce off the surface of the lake before sinking.
The tennis ball seemed to bounce off the net and land unexpectedly on the court.

Synonyms

  • rebound
    2.9 1.11/m
    rebound is a general verb for springing back after a hit, while bounce off describes something that bounces away from a surface.
  • ricochet
    7.7 1.44/m
    ricochet is used for projectiles that bounce off at an angle, while bounce off is the simple, general idea of rebounding.
  • carom
    9.5 0.05/m
    carom often describes a ball or object striking then bouncing off at an angle, while bounce off is broader and not specific to angle.
  • bound
    4.4 0.14/m
    bound tends to suggest a quick spring or leap by a living thing, while bounce off focuses on objects rebounding after a hit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebound - to spring back after hitting a surface
  • synonym: ricochet - to bounce off a surface and move in another direction
