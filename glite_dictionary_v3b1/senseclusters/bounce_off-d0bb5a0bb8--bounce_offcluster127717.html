bounce off

verb
rebound or ricochet from a surface
Pronunciation: / baʊns ɑf / (AmE), / baʊns ɒf / (BrE)
(of an object) to rebound or ricochet away from a surface after hitting it.

Examples

To bounce off a wall
Raindrops bounced off the windshield, making it hard to see ahead.
She watched the pebble bounce off the surface of the lake before sinking.
The tennis ball seemed to bounce off the net and land unexpectedly on the court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebound - to spring back after hitting a surface
  • synonym: ricochet - to bounce off a surface and move in another direction
