vicinage

noun
local jury district
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪsɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the district or area from which jurors are selected, or the right to be tried by a jury from that locality

Examples

Vicinage jury selection
The law requires that the jury be chosen from the vicinage where the crime occurred.
Debates about the proper vicinage for the trial delayed the proceedings.
He argued that a jury from his own vicinage would better understand the local context.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jurisdiction - the official power to make legal decisions in an area
  • synonym: venue - the location where a trial is held
