brank

noun
medieval punishment device
9.9
Pronunciation: / bræŋk / (AmE), / bræŋk / (BrE)
an old device used to punish and silence people, especially women, by restraining the head and mouth

Examples

A metal brank
The museum displayed a brank once used in medieval punishments.
In the past, a brank was used to silence those accused of gossiping.
Historians studied the cruel effects of the brank on its victims.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scold's bridle - an iron muzzle used to punish and silence women in the past
