copyholder

noun
device that holds copy for printing
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːpiˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈkɒpihəʊldə / (BrE)
a mechanical device used in printing to hold the copy for the compositor to set type

Examples

A copyholder device
He adjusted the copyholder to better see the faded page he was typesetting.
Old printing shops often had a sturdy copyholder next to every compositor's station.
The copyholder kept the manuscript in place for the typesetter to work efficiently.
