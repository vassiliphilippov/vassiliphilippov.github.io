excisional

adjective
removal by cutting
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈsaɪʒənəl / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɪʒənəl / (BrE)
relating to the removal of something completely by cutting, especially in a surgical procedure

Examples

Excisional surgery
The surgeon performed an excisional procedure to remove the entire tumor.
An excisional biopsy is often recommended when a lump needs to be completely removed for testing.
The patient recovered quickly after the excisional removal of the cyst.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incisional - relating to making a cut or incision
  • synonym: surgical - relating to surgery or operations
