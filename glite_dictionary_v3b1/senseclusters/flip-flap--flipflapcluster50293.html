flip-flap

adverb
with repeated flapping noise or movement
3.5
Pronunciation: / flɪp flæp / (AmE), / flɪp flæp / (BrE)
With repeated, quick, flapping sounds or motions.

Examples

Wings flip-flap quickly
Birds' wings beat flip-flap as they took flight from the tree.
The curtains went flip-flap against the open window all night long.
There was something mysterious going flip-flap in the attic during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flap - to move or cause to move with a fluttering or waving motion
  • synonym: flip-flop - to move with a sudden, repeated motion or sound
