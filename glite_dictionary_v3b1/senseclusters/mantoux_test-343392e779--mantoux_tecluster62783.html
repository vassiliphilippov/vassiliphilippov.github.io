Mantoux test

noun
medical test for tuberculosis exposure
4.5
Pronunciation: /mæn.ˈtuː test/ (AmE), /mæn.ˈtuː test/ (BrE)
a medical test in which tuberculin is injected intradermally to check for exposure to tuberculosis, indicated by a skin reaction

Examples

Positive Mantoux test
A positive Mantoux test may indicate either a past infection or a current exposure to tuberculosis.
My doctor recommended a Mantoux test after I returned from a country with high rates of TB.
The Mantoux test is routinely administered to screen for tuberculosis in healthcare workers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tuberculin test - general skin test for tuberculosis using tuberculin
