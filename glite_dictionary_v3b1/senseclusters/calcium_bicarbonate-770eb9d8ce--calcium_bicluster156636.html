calcium bicarbonate

noun
compound causing hard water
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.si.əm baɪˈkɑːr.bəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælsiəm baɪˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a chemical compound, Ca(HCO₃)₂, that contributes to hardness in water and is formed when calcium carbonate dissolves in carbonated water

Examples

Calcium bicarbonate in water
Calcium bicarbonate forms when rainwater absorbs carbon dioxide and reacts with limestone.
Boiling water removes calcium bicarbonate, which helps soften hard water.
High levels of calcium bicarbonate in tap water can make it taste slightly bitter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calcium carbonate - a related compound, CaCO₃, also found in water as a mineral deposit
