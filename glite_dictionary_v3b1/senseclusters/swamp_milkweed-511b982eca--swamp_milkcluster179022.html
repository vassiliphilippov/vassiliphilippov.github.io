swamp milkweed

noun
a wetland perennial plant with colorful flowers
7.0
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈmɪlkˌwid / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈmɪlkˌwiːd / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, with dense branches and clusters of white to crimson or purple flowers, typically found in wetlands.

Examples

Swamp milkweed flowers
In late summer, the vibrant blooms of swamp milkweed add color to riverbanks and marshes.
Monarch butterfly larvae often feed exclusively on swamp milkweed in wetland habitats.
She planted swamp milkweed in her garden to attract pollinators and native birds.
