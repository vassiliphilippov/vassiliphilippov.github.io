shy

verb
start back suddenly, especially from fright
Pronunciation: / ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
to move or start back suddenly, or to avoid something or someone because of fear or nervousness

Examples

To shy away
She shied away from the barking dog, startled by its aggression.
The horse shied at the sudden noise beside the road.
Whenever he saw his own reflection, the deer would shy and dart back into the woods.
Some children shy away from speaking in front of the class.
He would often shy from making eye contact with strangers.
Many animals shy at sudden loud noises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoid - to keep away from something or someone
  • antonym: confront - to face something or someone directly
  • synonym: startle - to cause (someone) to move or jump suddenly in surprise or fright
