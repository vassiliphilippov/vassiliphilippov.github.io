mercantile agency

noun
organization providing business credit ratings
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝːkənˌtaɪl ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkəntaɪl ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an organization that supplies businesses with information about the creditworthiness of other companies

Examples

A mercantile agency report
Before extending credit, the supplier consulted a mercantile agency for a report on the buyer.
Dun & Bradstreet is a well-known mercantile agency that provides credit information on businesses.
Many banks rely on mercantile agencies to assess the financial stability of commercial clients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credit bureau - an agency that collects and reports credit information on individuals
