thiocyanic acid

noun
unstable acid formed from thiocyanate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪ.oʊ.saɪˈæn.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌθaɪ.əʊ.saɪˈæn.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
An unstable acid (HSCN) formed by distilling a thiocyanate salt; used mainly in chemical laboratories.

Examples

Thiocyanic acid in lab
Because thiocyanic acid is unstable, it must be prepared fresh before use in experiments.
Researchers noted that thiocyanic acid rapidly decomposes at room temperature.
The laboratory synthesized thiocyanic acid by distilling potassium thiocyanate.
