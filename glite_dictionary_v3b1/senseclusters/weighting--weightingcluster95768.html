weighting

noun
statistical coefficient showing relative importance
6.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈweɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
(statistics) a coefficient assigned to elements of a set, typically to reflect their relative importance or influence in a calculation or frequency distribution.

Examples

A weighting factor
A proper weighting can make your statistical analysis more accurate and meaningful.
The survey data used a weighting to adjust for the smaller representation of rural participants.
These ratings were averaged with a weighting given to more recent performance.
