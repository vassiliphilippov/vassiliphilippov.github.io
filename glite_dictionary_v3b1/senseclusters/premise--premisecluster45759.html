premise

noun
statement forming basis of an argument
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
A statement or idea that is assumed to be true and that forms the basis for a logical argument or conclusion.

Examples

A logical premise
Every argument must start with at least one premise that is accepted as true.
If your premise is incorrect, your conclusion will also be flawed.
The lawyer questioned whether the premise of the prosecution's argument was actually valid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true without proof
  • antonym: conclusion - a judgment or opinion formed after considering the premises
