factious

adjective
causing or relating to dissension within a group
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈfæk.ʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or inclined to dissent or cause division, especially within a group or organization.

Examples

Factious group members
His factious attitude made it difficult to reach a consensus in the meeting.
History offers many examples of factious groups undermining national unity.
The organization was torn apart by factious disputes among its leaders.
