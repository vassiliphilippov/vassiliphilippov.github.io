Innocent XI

proper noun
Italian pope (1676–1689), canonized saint
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnəsənt ði ˈɛlɛvənθ / (AmE), / ˌɪnəsənt ði ˈɛlɪvənt / (BrE)
(1611–1689) Italian pope from 1676 to 1689, known for his saintliness, struggle with Louis XIV over papal authority, and canonized in 1956.

Examples

Innocent XI pope
Innocent XI was known for his opposition to the policies of Louis XIV.
Many historians consider Innocent XI a reformer within the Catholic Church.
The sainthood of Innocent XI was officially recognized in 1956.
