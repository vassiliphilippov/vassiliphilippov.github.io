slacken

verb
become slower or less active
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈslækən / (AmE), / ˈslækən / (BrE)
to become or make something slower, less active, or less intense

Examples

To slacken the pace
After several hours, the wind began to slacken and the sea calmed.
His pace noticeably slackened as he climbed the steep hill.
Productivity tends to slacken toward the end of the workday.
As the rain continued, the workers decided to slacken their efforts and take a short break.
He began to slacken his pace as he grew tired from the long walk.
Traffic tends to slacken in the late evening, making it easier to drive across town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slow - to reduce speed; move at a slower rate
  • synonym: slow down - to reduce speed or activity
  • antonym: speed up - to increase speed
  • antonym: tighten - to make or become tighter or more tense
