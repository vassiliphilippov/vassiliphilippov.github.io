bis-phossy jaw

noun
jawbone disease from phosphorus exposure
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪs ˈfɑːsi dʒɔ / (AmE), / ˌbɪs ˈfɒsi dʒɔː / (BrE)
a severe medical condition involving necrosis of the jawbone, caused by exposure to white phosphorus, historically affecting workers in the match industry

Examples

Bis-phossy jaw disease
Prolonged exposure to phosphorus in factories could result in bis-phossy jaw among workers.
The surgeon had seen several cases of bis-phossy jaw during his career in industrial towns.
Today, strict regulations prevent outbreaks of bis-phossy jaw in the workforce.
