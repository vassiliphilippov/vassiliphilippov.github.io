crossover

noun
voter switching parties in a primary election
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔˌsoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a voter who is registered with one political party but votes in the primary election of another party

Examples

Crossover voter
In the last election, the party saw a surge in crossover voters from the opposition.
Many candidates adjust their platforms to appeal to potential crossover voters during the primaries.
The influence of crossover voters can sometimes determine the outcome of a close primary race.
