hagiographical

adjective
overly idealizing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhæɡiəˈɡræfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiəˈɡræfɪkəl / (BrE)
describing someone or something in an overly idealized or saintly manner, often ignoring faults

Examples

Hagiographical praise
The documentary took a hagiographical approach, focusing only on the leader's achievements.
Many fans found the film's hagiographical portrayal of the singer unrealistic.
Critics argued that the article was too hagiographical and ignored important controversies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critical - expressing adverse or disapproving comments or judgments
  • synonym: idolizing - admiring or praising someone excessively
