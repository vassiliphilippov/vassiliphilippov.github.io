synchronizer

noun
instrument for synchronizing periodic motions
3.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a device or person that makes things happen at the same time or in coordination; especially an instrument that indicates whether two periodic motions are synchronous, for example to enable a pilot to synchronize the propellers of a multi-engine plane

Examples

Automatic synchronizer device
Modern aircraft are equipped with an automatic synchronizer for propeller control.
The pilot checked the synchronizer to ensure both engines were running in harmony.
Using the synchronizer, she adjusted the propellers to eliminate any vibration.
The synchronizer in the car's transmission helps gears shift smoothly.
A skilled synchronizer ensures that all the dancers move in perfect time.
Modern cameras use a synchronizer to match the flash with the shutter.
A faulty synchroniser can result in uncomfortable noise and uneven engine performance.
Modern aircraft are often equipped with a synchroniser for safer engine operation.
The pilot used the synchroniser to adjust the engines and minimize vibrations.
