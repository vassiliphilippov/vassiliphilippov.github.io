drift

noun
general tendency to change
6.7 3.3
Pronunciation: /drɪft/ (AmE), /drɪft/ (BrE)
a gradual change or tendency in something, such as opinion, language, policy, or data, over time

Examples

A drift in opinion
Over time, a slight drift in company policy became apparent.
The drift of the conversation was towards a more conservative approach.
There is a noticeable drift to the right in public opinion.
Many linguists believe that the drift of English grammar has simplified sentence structures over centuries.
Semantic drift is evident when a word's meaning slowly changes across generations.
The drift from synthetic to analytic structures can be traced in the history of English.
The team detected drift when the model's predictions became less accurate after new user behavior emerged.
To prevent performance loss, engineers monitor data pipelines for drift and retrain models regularly.
Researchers distinguish feature drift from label changes because each requires different fixes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concept drift - the phenomenon where the relationship between inputs and target variables changes over time, affecting model performance
  • synonym: data drift - changes in the distribution of input features over time
  • synonym: language change - the alteration of language structures over time
  • synonym: trend - general direction in which something is developing or changing
