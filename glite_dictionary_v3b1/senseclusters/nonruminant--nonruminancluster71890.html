nonruminant

adjective
not chewing cud; not a ruminant animal
9.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈruː.mɪ.nənt/ /; / /nə.ˈruː.mɪ.nənt/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈruː.mɪ.nənt/ /; / /nə.ˈruː.mɪ.nənt/ (BrE)
(of an animal) not belonging to the group of animals that chew the cud (ruminants); lacking a multi-chambered stomach used for rumination.

Examples

Nonruminant animals
Pigs and horses are considered nonruminant animals because they do not chew cud.
Researchers compared growth rates between the nonruminant rabbits and the ruminant sheep.
The diet of a nonruminant species typically differs from that of a ruminant.
