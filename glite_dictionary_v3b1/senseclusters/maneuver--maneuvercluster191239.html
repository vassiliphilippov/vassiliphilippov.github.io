maneuver

verb
move strategically (military/naval)
4.9 4.7
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈnuː.vər/ (AmE), /mə.ˈnuː.və/ (BrE)
to move, guide, or act skillfully or strategically, especially to achieve a goal or gain an advantage; often used for vehicles, objects, or groups

Examples

To maneuver a car
Enemy forces maneuvered skillfully during the simulated battle exercise.
The general decided to maneuver his troops to higher ground for a tactical advantage.
The navy maneuvered its fleet to create a blockade around the harbor.
He skillfully maneuvered the car through the narrow, winding streets.
She slowly maneuvered the boat away from the crowded dock.
The pilot had to maneuver the plane around a sudden storm.
She is maneuvering the boat through the narrow channel to avoid rocks.
The driver skillfully maneuvered the car around the fallen tree in the road.
He spent several minutes maneuvering the heavy sofa through the doorway.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crash - to collide violently with an obstacle
  • synonym: deploy - to move troops or equipment into position for military action
  • synonym: maneuver - to perform a movement in military or naval tactics (American spelling)
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence cleverly or unfairly
  • synonym: navigate - to plan and direct the course of a ship, aircraft, or other form of transport
  • antonym: retreat - to move away from the enemy to avoid danger or defeat
  • synonym: scheme - to make clever and secret plans to achieve an aim
  • synonym: steer - to guide or control the movement of a vehicle or vessel
