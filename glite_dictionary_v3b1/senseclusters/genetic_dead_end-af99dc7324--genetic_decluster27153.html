genetic dead end

noun
organism/situation with no possibility of future reproduction
7.1
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk dɛd ɛnd / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪk dɛd end / (BrE)
a species, organism, or situation that is unable to reproduce or develop further, closing off future genetic or evolutionary possibilities

Examples

A genetic dead end
Researchers warned that cloning certain species could create a genetic dead end for the lineage.
The isolated island population ultimately became a genetic dead end due to inbreeding.
The new hybrid plant is a genetic dead end, unable to produce fertile seeds.
