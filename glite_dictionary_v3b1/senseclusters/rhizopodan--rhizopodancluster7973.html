rhizopodan

noun
protozoan with pseudopods
Pronunciation: / raɪˈzɑpədən / (AmE), / raɪˈzɒpədən / (BrE)
a protozoan belonging to a group characterized by the presence of pseudopods, used for movement and feeding

Examples

A rhizopodan protozoan
Many rhizopodans play an essential role in aquatic ecosystems.
Scientists observed the feeding behavior of a rhizopodan under the microscope.
The rhizopodan moves by extending its pseudopodia to form temporary projections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amoeba - a type of protozoan that moves using pseudopods
