harrow

verb
to cause distress
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhærəʊ / (BrE)
to cause someone great distress or emotional pain; to torment deeply.

Examples

News harrowed her
The tragic news harrowed her for weeks, making it hard to concentrate at work.
He was harrowed by memories of the difficult times he had endured as a child.
The film's graphic scenes harrowed many viewers, leaving a lasting impression.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to make someone feel better or less distressed
  • synonym: disturb - to upset or worry someone
  • synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering
