realism

noun
accepting facts and preferring practicality
Pronunciation: / ˈriːəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to accept facts as they are and to prefer practical, literal approaches over idealism or abstraction

Examples

Practical realism
A healthy dose of realism is needed when planning such an ambitious project.
Her realism helped the team make practical decisions during the crisis.
His realism prevented him from chasing unrealistic dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealism - the practice of forming or pursuing ideals, often unrealistically
  • synonym: pragmatism - a pragmatic, practical approach to problems and affairs
