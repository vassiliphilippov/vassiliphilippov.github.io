Bolivar

proper noun
Venezuelan leader and liberator; founder of Bolivia
5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑː.lə.vər/ /; / /ˈbɑː.lɪ.vər/ /; / /bə.ˈliː.vɑːr/ (AmE), /bə.ˈliː.vɑː/ /; / /bə.ˈliː.və/ /; / /ˈbɒ.lɪ.və/ (BrE)
Simón Bolívar (1783–1830), the Venezuelan statesman who led the independence movements of several South American countries from Spanish rule and founded Bolivia.

Examples

Bolivar independence leader
Bolivar played a pivotal role in the independence of Venezuela, Colombia, and Ecuador.
Historians often refer to Bolivar as 'El Libertador' for his efforts to free South America from Spanish rule.
Many cities and monuments in South America are named after Bolivar.
