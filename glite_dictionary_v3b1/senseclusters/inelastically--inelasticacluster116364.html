inelastically

adverb
not responsive to change
8.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ɪ.ˈlæs.tɪ.klɪ/ /; / /ɪn.ə.ˈlæs.tɪ.klɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ɪ.ˈlæs.tɪ.klɪ/ /; / /ɪn.ə.ˈlæs.tɪ.klɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that shows little or no response to changes in price or income, especially in economics.

Examples

Demand changes inelastically
When the price of medicine increased, demand changed inelastically because people still needed it.
Essential goods are often purchased inelastically, regardless of price fluctuations.
The market responded inelastically to the new tax, with only a slight drop in sales.
