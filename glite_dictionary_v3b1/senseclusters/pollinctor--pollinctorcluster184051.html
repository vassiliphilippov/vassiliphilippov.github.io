pollinctor

noun
ancient body preparer
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪŋktər / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪŋktɔː / (BrE)
a person who prepares dead bodies for burial, especially by anointing them, in ancient times

Examples

The pollinctor anoints bodies
In ancient Rome, a pollinctor would carefully anoint the deceased before burial ceremonies.
The family hired a pollinctor to prepare the body according to traditional customs.
Historical texts describe the important role of the pollinctor in funeral rites.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embalmer - a person who preserves dead bodies by treating them with chemicals
