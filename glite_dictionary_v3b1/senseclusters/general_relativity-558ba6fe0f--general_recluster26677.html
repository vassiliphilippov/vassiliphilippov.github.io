general relativity

noun
Einstein's theory of gravity and spacetime
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛnərəl rɪˌlætəˈvɪɾi / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəl rɪˌlætɪˈvɪti / (BrE)
A theory of gravitation developed by Albert Einstein, which extends special relativity to include gravity, describing it as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

Examples

General relativity theory
Albert Einstein developed general relativity to explain how gravity affects the fabric of space and time.
Modern GPS systems must account for general relativity to provide accurate positioning information.
The bending of light by massive objects, as predicted by general relativity, was confirmed during a solar eclipse.
