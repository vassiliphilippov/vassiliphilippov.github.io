cDNA

noun
complementary DNA made from RNA
8.4 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / siÀê diÀê Ààen e…™ / (AmE), / siÀê diÀê Ààen e…™ / (BrE)
single-stranded DNA synthesized from a messenger RNA template by the enzyme reverse transcriptase, resulting in DNA complementary to the RNA sequence

Examples

A cDNA clone
A cDNA clone was isolated and sequenced to determine the gene's structure.
Researchers use cDNA libraries to study gene expression in different tissues.
The enzyme reverse transcriptase converts mRNA into cDNA for further analysis.
