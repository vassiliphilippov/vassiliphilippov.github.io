circus

noun
traveling show with acts and animals
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəs / (BrE)
a traveling show or event with acrobats, clowns, trained animals, and other entertainers, usually held in a large tent

Examples

A traveling circus show
During her travels in Europe, she visited a famous old circus in Paris.
The children were thrilled when they saw the circus coming to town.

He dreamed of leaving his small town behind and joining a circus as an acrobat.
Over the years, the traveling circus became famous for its daring trapeze acts.
When the circus came to town, children eagerly lined up to see the elephants and clowns.
After the circus ended, the workers packed away the tent and equipment.
Children cheered as the acrobats performed daring stunts inside the circus.
The circus was set up on the edge of town, drawing large crowds every evening.
