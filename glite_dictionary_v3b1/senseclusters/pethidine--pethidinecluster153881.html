pethidine

noun
pain-relief drug
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛθɪdiːn / (AmE), / ˈpɛθɪdiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used to relieve moderate to severe pain, especially in medical settings

Examples

pethidine injection
The nurse gave the patient pethidine to help control severe pain after surgery.
Doctors sometimes prescribe pethidine for short-term pain relief in hospitals.
Because of its side effects, pethidine is used less frequently than other painkillers.
The doctor administered pethidine to relieve the patient's severe pain after surgery.
Some hospitals use pethidine during childbirth to help manage contractions.
She was given a single dose of pethidine for her injury in the emergency room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meperidine - another name for pethidine, especially in the United States
  • synonym: morphine - a strong pain-relieving drug derived from opium
  • degree lower: paracetamol - a common mild pain reliever and fever reducer
