Han dynasty

noun
imperial Chinese dynasty (206 BC–AD 220)
6.6
Pronunciation: /hæn ˈdaɪ.nə.stiː/ /; / /hɑːn ˈdaɪ.nə.stiː/ (AmE), /hæn ˈdɪn.ə.stiː/ /; / /heɪn ˈdɪn.ə.stiː/ (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 206 BC to AD 220, marked by territorial expansion, strengthened bureaucracy, and cultural achievements

Examples

The Han dynasty rule

Silk Road trade flourished during the Han dynasty, linking China with Central Asia and beyond.
The Han dynasty is considered a golden age in Chinese history due to its advances in politics and culture.
