broad daylight

noun
in full daylight; visible to all
Pronunciation: / brɔd ˈdeɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / brɔːd ˈdeɪlaɪt / (BrE)
in full daylight; during the day when it is bright and visible to everyone, often used to emphasize that something occurred openly or publicly

Examples

Broad daylight robbery
The artwork was taken from the museum in broad daylight, stunning the staff.
The thief was bold enough to steal the car in broad daylight.
They argued loudly in broad daylight in front of everyone at the café.
