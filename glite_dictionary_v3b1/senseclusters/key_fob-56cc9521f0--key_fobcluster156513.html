key fob

noun
electronic access device
Pronunciation: / ki fb / (AmE), / ki fb / (BrE)
A small electronic device, often attached to a key ring, that uses wireless signals or proximity sensing to unlock or start a car or to grant access to a building or system.

Examples

Electronic key fob
She waved her key fob to unlock the car doors from outside the parking lot.
At the office, everyone carries a key fob that lets them enter the secure building after hours.
Manufacturers now embed chips in key fobs so cars can be started without inserting a physical key.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remote (device) - a small wireless device used to control equipment or systems at a distance
  • synonym: transponder - an electronic device that automatically transmits a response signal, often used for vehicle access or identification
