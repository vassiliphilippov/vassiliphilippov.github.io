intervisibility

noun
mutual visibility
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˌvɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌvɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the condition or degree to which two points or locations can see each other without anything blocking the view (especially in surveying or telecommunications)

Examples

The intervisibility between towers
The engineers checked the intervisibility between the two radio towers before starting construction.
Trees growing on the hill reduced the intervisibility between the observation posts.
Good intervisibility is essential for safe communication between the two stations.
Surveyors checked the intervisibility between the two hilltops before installing the signal tower.
Good intervisibility is essential for accurate mapping in mountainous regions.
The engineers improved intervisibility by clearing trees that blocked the line of sight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obscuration - the act of making something less visible or hidden
  • synonym: visibility - the state of being able to see or be seen
