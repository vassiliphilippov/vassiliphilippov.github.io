charmeuse

noun
shiny lightweight fabric
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrˈmuːz / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈmɜːz / (BrE)
a soft, lightweight fabric with a shiny front and dull back, commonly used for clothing such as dresses and lingerie

Examples

Soft charmeuse fabric
The designer chose charmeuse for the evening gown because of its elegant drape.
Her scarf was made of blue charmeuse that shimmered in the light.
Many luxury pajamas are crafted from soft charmeuse for comfort and style.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crepe - a light, thin fabric with a slightly crinkled texture
  • synonym: satin - a smooth, glossy fabric, often used for clothing
