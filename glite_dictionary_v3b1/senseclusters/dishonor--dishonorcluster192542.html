dishonor

verb
bring shame or disgrace
2.8 4.3
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɑnər /; / dɪsˈɑːnər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
to bring shame or disgrace upon someone or something

Examples

To dishonor the family
He dishonored his family by lying under oath.
She feared she would dishonor her school's reputation by failing the exam.
The athlete was dishonored when his cheating was exposed publicly.
His actions dishonoured the entire team in front of the spectators.
The politician feared he would dishonour his oath of office by breaking his promises.
They believe cheating would dishonour their school's reputation.
He felt he had dishonoured his family's name by breaking the law.
The athlete was dishonoured after being caught cheating in the competition.
She would never dishonour her promise to her best friend.
