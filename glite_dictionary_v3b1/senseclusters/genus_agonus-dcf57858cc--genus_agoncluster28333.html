genus Agonus

noun
type genus of Agonidae (marine fishes)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈɡoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs əˈɡəʊnəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Agonidae, a group of bottom-dwelling marine fishes known as poachers.

Examples

genus Agonus fishes
Members of the genus Agonus are recognized by their armored, spiny bodies.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Agonus during their Arctic expedition.
The genus Agonus includes several species of poacher fishes found in cold northern waters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Agonidae - a family of fish known as poachers, to which Agonus belongs
