rifting

noun
formation of crust cracks
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of forming cracks or splits in the Earth's crust, often leading to the separation of landmasses

Examples

Continental rifting
Geologists study rifting to understand how continents break apart over millions of years.
The East African Rift is a famous example of active rifting on land.
Ocean basins can form as a result of prolonged rifting between tectonic plates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faulting - the process where rocks break and move along a fault
  • antonym: uplift - the rising of regions of the Earth's crust
