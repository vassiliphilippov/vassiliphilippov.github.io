neovascularization

noun
abnormal blood vessel growth
9.6
Pronunciation: /niː.oʊ.væs.kjʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /niː.ə.væs.kjʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /niː.əʊ.væs.kjʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /niː.ə.væs.kjʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the abnormal or excessive growth of new blood vessels, often contributing to disease such as tumors or retinal disorders

Examples

Abnormal neovascularization
Uncontrolled neovascularization in the retina can lead to vision loss in diabetic patients.
Doctors monitor for neovascularization when treating certain types of cancer.
The presence of neovascularization is a key factor in diagnosing some eye diseases.
