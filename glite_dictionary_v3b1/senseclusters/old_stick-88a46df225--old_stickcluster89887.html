old stick

noun
conventional, resistant person
Pronunciation: / oʊld stɪk / (AmE), / əʊld stɪk / (BrE)
a person who is old-fashioned, conventional, and resistant to change

Examples

An old stick person
Don't be such an old stick; join us for the dance!
My uncle is an old stick who still refuses to use a smartphone.
She called her boss an old stick for rejecting every new suggestion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fuddy-duddy - a person with old-fashioned ideas and a dislike of change
  • synonym: stick-in-the-mud - a person who is unwilling to try anything new or change their ways
