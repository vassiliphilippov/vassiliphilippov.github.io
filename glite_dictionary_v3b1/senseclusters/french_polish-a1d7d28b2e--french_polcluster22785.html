French polish

noun
a shellac-based wood varnish
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ˈpɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / frentʃ ˈpɒlɪʃ /; / frɛntʃ ˈpɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of varnish made from shellac and alcohol, and the high-gloss finish it produces on wood

Examples

French polish finish
He applied several coats of French polish to achieve a deep, glossy surface on the wooden cabinet.
The antique table was restored carefully using French polish to bring out its shine.
Traditional furniture makers often use French polish to give wooden pieces a luxurious finish.
Over time, the French polish developed a beautiful, deep luster.
Restorers applied several coats of French polish to bring out the wood's rich color.
The antique table gleamed under a fresh French polish.
