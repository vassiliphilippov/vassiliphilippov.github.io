clouded

adjective
showing a troubled or distressed appearance
5.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈklaʊ.dɪd/ (AmE), /ˈklaʊ.dɪd/ (BrE)
not clear or obscured, especially because of worry, confusion, or difficulty in understanding (used for faces, minds, explanations, etc.)

Examples

Clouded mind
A clouded face often betrays inner anxiety or doubt.
Her clouded eyes revealed that she had been crying.
His expression became clouded when he heard the bad news.
After the accident, his mind was clouded and he struggled to remember details.
Fatigue can leave your thoughts clouded and unfocused.
Grief had clouded her judgment, making decisions difficult.
Her explanation was so clouded that nobody could follow what she meant.
His judgment was clouded by anger, making it hard to see the situation clearly.
The true meaning of the poem remained clouded despite several careful readings.
