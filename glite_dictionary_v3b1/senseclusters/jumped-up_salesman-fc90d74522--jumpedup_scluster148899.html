jumped-up salesman

noun
arrogant or self-important person (esp. salesman)
7.1 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒʌmp.tʌp ˈseɪlz.mən/ /; / /ˈdʒʌmp.təp ˈseɪlz.mən/ (AmE), /ˈdʒʌmp.tʌp ˈseɪlz.mən/ /; / /ˈdʒʌmp.təp ˈseɪlz.mən/ (BrE)
A person, especially a salesman, who is arrogant or self-important, often because of a recent promotion or rise in status.

Examples

A jumped-up salesman
Most clients avoided the jumped-up salesman because of his patronizing attitude.
She couldn't stand working with that jumped-up salesman who bragged about his recent promotion.
The manager dismissed the jumped-up salesman's suggestions without a second thought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
  • synonym: upstart - a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or power, often behaving arrogantly
