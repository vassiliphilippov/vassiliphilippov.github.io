poach

verb
take or steal someone's ideas
6.9 7.0
Pronunciation: / poʊtʃ / (AmE), / pəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to illegally or unfairly take someone else's ideas, work, or employees for your own benefit

Examples

To poach workers
She felt betrayed when her colleague began to poach her research ideas for his own presentation.
Some startups are notorious for attempting to poach ideas from more established firms.
The company was accused of trying to poach ideas from its rivals during the conference.
The firm tried to poach several engineers from its competitor by offering higher salaries and benefits.
Several startups have poached senior developers away with generous stock options and flexible schedules.
He warned the board that constant poaching of staff would damage long-term team morale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: headhunt - to try to persuade a person to leave their job and work for another company
  • synonym: recruit - to find and hire new people to work for an organization
  • antonym: retain - to keep employees and prevent them from leaving a company
  • synonym: steal - to take something without permission
