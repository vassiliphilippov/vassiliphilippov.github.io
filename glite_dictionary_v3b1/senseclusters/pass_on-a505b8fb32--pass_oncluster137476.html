pass on

verb
distribute or transmit to others
1.0
Pronunciation: / pæs ɑn / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒn / (BrE)
to give, transfer, or communicate something (such as an object, information, or responsibility) to another person

Examples

To pass on a message
All outdated documents should be shredded rather than passed on to others.
Could you pass on this message to your colleagues?
The manager will pass on the memo to all team members.
Before leaving, he passed on everything he had learned to his successor.
Please pass on this message to your supervisor as soon as possible.
She always passes on important updates to the rest of the team.
It's traditional to pass on family recipes from one generation to the next.
She decided to pass on her grandmother's necklace to her eldest daughter.
The retiring teacher will pass on her classroom resources to a new colleague.

  • synonym: refer - to direct someone to a different person or place for information, help, or action
