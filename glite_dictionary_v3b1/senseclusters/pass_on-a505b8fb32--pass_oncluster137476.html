pass on

distribute or transmit to others

to give, transfer, or communicate something (such as an object, information, or responsibility) to another person

Examples

To pass on a message
All outdated documents should be shredded rather than passed on to others.
Could you pass on this message to your colleagues?
The manager will pass on the memo to all team members.
Before leaving, he passed on everything he had learned to his successor.
Please pass on this message to your supervisor as soon as possible.

The retiring teacher will pass on her classroom resources to a new colleague.

Synonyms

  • pass
    1.0 30/m
    pass is a simple verb for handing or sending something to someone, while pass on often adds the idea of sending or informing further.
  • give
    1.0 783/m
    give is a common everyday verb for handing something directly to someone, while pass on often implies passing or sharing with others.
  • hand
    1.0 68/m
    hand is to place something into another person's hands in person, while pass on often includes passing information or items to someone else.
  • hand over
    2.8 9.21/m
    hand over means giving control or possession, often reluctantly, while pass on is a general transfer of things, information, or duty.
  • turn over
    3.2 7.88/m
    turn over is to give someone to the care or authority of another, while pass on covers both objects and information more generally.
  • pass along
    2.5 3.06/m
    pass along is used for relaying news or items from person to person, while pass on covers direct giving or wider transfers.
  • hand off
    3.7 0.79/m
    hand off means to transfer control or an object, often briefly or in sport, while pass on is a general give or transfer.
