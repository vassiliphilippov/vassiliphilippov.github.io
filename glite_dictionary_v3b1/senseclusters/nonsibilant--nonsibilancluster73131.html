nonsibilant

noun
non-hissing consonant
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɪbɪlənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɪbɪlənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound that does not have a hissing or 's'-like quality, such as 'm', 't', or 'k'

Examples

A nonsibilant like 't'
In English, the sound 't' is a nonsibilant because it does not produce a hissing noise.
Phonetic studies often compare nonsibilant consonants with sibilant ones like 's' and 'sh'.
The teacher asked students to list examples of nonsibilant sounds in different languages.
