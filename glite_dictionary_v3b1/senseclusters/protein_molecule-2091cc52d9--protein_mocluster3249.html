protein molecule

noun
large molecule made of amino acids
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtiːn ˈmoʊlɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiːn ˈməʊlɪkjuːl / (BrE)
a large, complex molecule composed of chains of amino acids joined by peptide bonds, essential for the structure and function of living cells

Examples

A protein molecule chain
Each protein molecule in the body has a unique structure determined by its sequence of amino acids.
Scientists study the folding of a protein molecule to understand diseases like Alzheimer's.
The function of an enzyme depends on the precise shape of its protein molecule.
