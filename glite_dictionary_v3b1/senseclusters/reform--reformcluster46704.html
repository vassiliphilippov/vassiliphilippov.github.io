reform

noun
self-improvement by giving up vice
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
the process of improving one's behavior or morals by abandoning harmful or immoral habits

Examples

Personal reform
Following his release from prison, he dedicated himself to true reform.
Her reform from gambling addiction inspired others to seek help.
The whole community celebrated his reform after years of destructive behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rehabilitation - the process of helping someone return to a healthy or useful life
  • antonym: relapse - a return to previous bad habits or vices after reform
