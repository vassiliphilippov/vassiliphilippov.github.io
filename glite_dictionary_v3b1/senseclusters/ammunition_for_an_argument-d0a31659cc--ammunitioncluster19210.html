ammunition for an argument

noun
evidence or support for a claim or argument
2.6 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæmjəˈnɪʃən fər ən ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˌamjʊˈnɪʃən fə ən ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
information, evidence, or reasons that can be used to support a point or strengthen a case in a discussion or debate

Examples

Ammunition for an argument about policy
Economic statistics often provide ammunition for an argument about government policy.
She collected all the data as ammunition for an argument against her opponent's proposal.
The new report gave the senator plenty of ammunition for an argument during the debate.
