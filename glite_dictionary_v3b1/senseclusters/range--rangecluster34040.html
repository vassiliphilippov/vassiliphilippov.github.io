range

verb
arrange in a line or order
Pronunciation: / reɪndʒ / (AmE), / reɪndʒ / (BrE)
to arrange things in a line or in a particular order

Examples

Range books by size
He ranged his ideas logically to make his point clear.
She carefully ranged the books by author on the shelf.
The soldiers were ranged in a straight line for inspection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a neat, attractive, or required order
  • antonym: disorder - to throw into confusion; to disarrange
