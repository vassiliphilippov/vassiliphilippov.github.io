graph

noun
visual diagram displaying data
Pronunciation: / ɡræf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a diagram that shows the relationship between sets of data, usually using lines, bars, or points on axes

Examples

A line graph
I included a bar graph in my report to make the data easier to understand.
The teacher drew a graph on the board to explain the results.
This graph shows the rise in temperature over the last decade.
Before the presentation, she plotted the survey data on a graph for better visualization.
Our math homework involved drawing a graph of the quadratic equation.
The teacher used a graph to show the increase in temperature over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chart - a sheet of information, often presented as a table or in a visual form such as a graph
  • synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something
  • antonym: table - a set of facts or figures systematically displayed
