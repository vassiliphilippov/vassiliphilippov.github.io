depersonalization neurosis

noun
psychological disorder with sense of unreality
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.pɜːr.sən.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən nʊr.ˈoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /dɪ.pɜːr.sən.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən njuːr.ˈoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /dɪ.pɜː.sən.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən njuː.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /dɪ.pɜː.sən.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən nʊə.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A psychological disorder characterized by persistent or recurrent feelings of detachment from one's self or a sense of unreality, often accompanied by emotional numbing and a loss of personal identity.

Examples

Depersonalization neurosis symptoms
Her experience of depersonalization neurosis made everyday interactions feel distant and unreal.
Some patients with depersonalization neurosis describe feeling as if they are observing themselves from outside their body.
Treatment of depersonalization neurosis often includes psychotherapy and, in some cases, medication.
During episodes of depersonalisation neurosis, ordinary experiences may appear dreamlike or unreal.
People suffering from depersonalisation neurosis often describe themselves as feeling detached from their bodies.
The psychiatrist diagnosed her symptoms as indicative of depersonalisation neurosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depersonalisation disorder - A mental disorder characterized by persistent feelings of detachment or unreality.
  • synonym: depersonalization disorder - a disorder featuring persistent feelings of detachment from oneself
  • degree lower: derealisation - A dissociative disorder with feelings that the external world is unreal.
