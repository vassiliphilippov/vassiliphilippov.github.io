like nobody's business

adverb
to a very great extent or with great intensity
Pronunciation: / laɪk ˈnoʊˌbaɾiz ˈbɪznɪs / (AmE), / laɪk ˈnəʊbədiːz ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something is done with great intensity, efficiency, or enthusiasm.

Examples

Sweat like nobody's business
After he started his fitness routine, he was sweating like nobody's business.
During the holiday sale, customers were shopping like nobody's business.
She can type like nobody's business when facing a tight deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: like mad - with wild or extreme energy or enthusiasm
