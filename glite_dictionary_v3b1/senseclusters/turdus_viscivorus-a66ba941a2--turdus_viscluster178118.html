Turdus viscivorus

noun
large European thrush (mistle thrush)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrdəs vɪˈsɪvərəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs vɪˈsɪvərəs / (BrE)
A large European thrush that feeds primarily on mistletoe berries, also known as the mistle thrush.

Examples

The Turdus viscivorus bird
Due to its diet, the Turdus viscivorus plays a key role in spreading mistletoe seeds.
Ornithologists observed a pair of Turdus viscivorus feeding on the berries of a mistletoe tree.
The Turdus viscivorus is frequently seen in open woodlands across Europe during spring.
