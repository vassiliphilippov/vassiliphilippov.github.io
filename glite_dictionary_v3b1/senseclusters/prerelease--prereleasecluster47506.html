prerelease

verb
to release early
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːrɪˈliːs / (AmE), / ˌpriːrɪˈliːs / (BrE)
to release something, such as a product or media, to a limited audience before its official launch

Examples

To prerelease software
The company decided to prerelease the software to select users for feedback.
They will prerelease the new single to radio stations next month to build interest.
Movie studios sometimes prerelease films to critics before the official premiere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: launch - to officially release a product or service to the public
  • synonym: preview - to show or release something before its official launch
