sonograph

noun
medical imaging instrument using ultrasound
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsɒnəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument that uses ultrasonic waves to create images of internal body organs

Examples

Medical sonograph machine
Advances in sonograph technology have greatly improved medical imaging.
The doctor used a sonograph to examine the patient's heart.
With the help of a sonograph, the technician could monitor the fetus during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: stethoscope - instrument for listening to internal body sounds
  • synonym: ultrasound scanner - instrument using ultrasound waves for medical imaging
