tailed toad

noun
frog with tail-like organ, western North America
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪld toʊd / (AmE), / ˈteɪld təʊd / (BrE)
A western North American frog distinguished by a tail-like copulatory organ.

Examples

The tailed toad frog
Researchers observed a tailed toad using its unique organ during the breeding season.
The tailed toad inhabits fast-flowing mountain streams in the Pacific Northwest.
Unlike most frogs, the tailed toad retains a tail-like structure as an adult.
