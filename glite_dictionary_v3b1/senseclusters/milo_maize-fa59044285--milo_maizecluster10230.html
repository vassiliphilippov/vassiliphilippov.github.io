milo maize

noun
drought-resistant sorghum with large grains
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪloʊ ˈmeɪz / (AmE), / ˈmaɪləʊ ˈmeɪz / (BrE)
A variety of drought-resistant sorghum with large yellow or whitish grains, grown for grain and fodder.

Examples

Milo maize grain
Farmers in dry regions often grow milo maize because it thrives with little water.
The large grains of milo maize are used as animal feed and for food production.
This hybrid of milo maize is prized for its resistance to drought and heat.
