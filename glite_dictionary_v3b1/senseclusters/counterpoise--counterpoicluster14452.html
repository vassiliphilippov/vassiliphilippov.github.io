counterpoise

verb
to act as a counterbalance
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.pɔɪz/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.pɔɪz/ (BrE)
to act as a counterweight or counterbalance to something; to offset or balance by opposing force or influence

Examples

To counterpoise the effect
Checks and balances are intended to counterpoise the powers of government branches.
Her calm reasoning served to counterpoise his passionate arguments during the debate.
The smaller tower was designed to counterpoise the visual weight of the main structure.
