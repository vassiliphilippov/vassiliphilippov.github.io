faithful

noun
loyal adherents of a religion or church
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
the people who are loyal and devoted followers, especially of a religion or cause

Examples

The faithful gather
Every year, thousands of the faithful make a pilgrimage to the holy site.
The faithful gathered outside the cathedral despite the rain.
The priest addressed the assembled faithful during the Sunday service.
Every year, the church holds a special service for the faithful who gather from around the country.
The mayor addressed the faithful at the rally, thanking them for their unwavering support.
Thousands of faithful braved the cold to attend the midnight vigil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: believer - a person who has religious faith
  • synonym: devotee - an enthusiastic follower or admirer
  • antonym: infidel - a person who does not believe in a particular religion
