retrofit

verb
add new parts to something old
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌfɪt / (BrE)
to add new or improved parts, equipment, or technology to something that was made in the past, especially to improve or update it

Examples

To retrofit old buildings
After the earthquake, many buildings were retrofitted to meet new safety standards.
The company decided to retrofit its old factories with energy-efficient lighting.
They plan to retrofit their fleet of buses to run on electric power.
After the earthquake, many homes were retrofitted to withstand future tremors.
The old school was retrofitted with solar panels last year.
They plan to retrofit the office building to make it more energy efficient.
Many homeowners are choosing to retrofit their houses to improve insulation and save on energy bills.
The company decided to retrofit older trains with more energy-efficient engines.
The office building was retrofitted with advanced fire safety systems last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modernize - to make something more modern
  • antonym: replace - to put something new or different in the place of something else
  • synonym: upgrade - to raise something to a higher standard
