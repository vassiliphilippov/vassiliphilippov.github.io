draggy

adjective
resistant to movement
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡi / (AmE), / ˈdræɡi / (BrE)
offering resistance to movement, causing something to move slowly or with difficulty

Examples

Draggy car
The car felt draggy after driving through the muddy road.
His old laptop became draggy and slow after years of use.
The drawer was so draggy that I had to pull hard to open it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sluggish - moving slowly or lacking energy
  • antonym: smooth - moving easily without resistance
