pluralistically

adverb
in a diverse way
8.1
Pronunciation: /plʊr.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /pluːr.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /pluː.rə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /plʊə.rə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
in a way that recognizes, accepts, or supports diversity of people, ideas, or methods

Examples

To act pluralistically
The committee approached the issue pluralistically, considering input from all community members.
A society that functions pluralistically values different cultures and beliefs equally.
The school board decided pluralistically to incorporate various teaching methods.
The committee worked pluralistically to ensure all viewpoints were considered in the final decision.
She approached the curriculum pluralistically, integrating both traditional and modern teaching methods.
The organization operates pluralistically, welcoming members from various cultural and professional backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diversely - in a varied or diverse manner
  • antonym: exclusively - in a way that excludes others or limits participation
  • synonym: inclusively - in a way that includes people of all backgrounds or groups
