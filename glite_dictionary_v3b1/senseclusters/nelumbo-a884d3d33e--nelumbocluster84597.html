Nelumbo

noun
genus of aquatic lotus plants
Pronunciation: / nəˈlʌm.boʊ /; / nəˈlʌmboʊ / (AmE), / nɪˈlʌm.bəʊ /; / nɪˈlʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic plants known as the true lotuses, including the sacred lotus and American lotus, sometimes classified in the water lily family (Nymphaeaceae)

Examples

The Nelumbo plant
Botanists classify the sacred lotus as part of the genus Nelumbo.
In the pond, you can see both water lilies and Nelumbo plants.
Species of Nelumbo are admired for their large, fragrant flowers.
Nelumbo species grow in shallow ponds and slow-moving rivers across Asia and North America.
The sacred lotus, or Nelumbo nucifera, is revered in many religious traditions.
The seeds of the Nelumbo genus are known for their remarkable longevity.
