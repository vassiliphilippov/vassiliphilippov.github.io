bittersweet

adjective
both happy and sad at once
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪt̬.ɚˈswit / (AmE), / ˌbɪt.əˈswiːt / (BrE)
Containing a mixture of both happy and sad feelings.

Examples

Bittersweet memories
Graduation day was a bittersweet moment for the whole family.
The ending of the novel was bittersweet, leaving readers feeling both happy and sad.
They shared a bittersweet farewell, knowing they might never meet again.

Related Terms

  • antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness (opposite in emotional tone)
  • degree lower: melancholy - a feeling of deep, pensive sadness
  • degree higher: poignant - evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret, often mixed with pleasure
