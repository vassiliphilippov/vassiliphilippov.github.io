homograft

noun
tissue or organ transplant from a same-species donor
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊməˌɡræft / (AmE), / ˈhɒməˌɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a tissue or organ graft transplanted from a donor of the same species but with a different genetic makeup than the recipient

Examples

A homograft transplant
Advances in immunosuppression have made homograft procedures more successful.
Following the homograft, the patient was carefully monitored for signs of tissue rejection.
The surgeon chose a homograft rather than a synthetic valve for the heart operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allograft - a tissue graft from a donor of the same species as the recipient but not genetically identical
