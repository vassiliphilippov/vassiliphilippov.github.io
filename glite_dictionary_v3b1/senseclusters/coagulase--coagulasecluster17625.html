coagulase

noun
enzyme involved in coagulation
Pronunciation: / koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪs / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊleɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that causes coagulation, especially of blood or plasma

Examples

A coagulase test
A coagulase test is often used to identify Staphylococcus aureus in clinical samples.
Some bacteria produce coagulase to help them evade the host's immune system.
The presence of coagulase distinguishes pathogenic strains from non-pathogenic ones.
