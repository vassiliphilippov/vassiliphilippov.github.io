agnosticism

noun
belief that ultimate knowledge is unattainable
8.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /æg.ˈnɑːs.tɪ.sɪz.əm/ /; / /əg.ˈnɑːs.tɪ.sɪz.əm/ (AmE), /æg.ˈnɒs.tɪ.sɪz.əm/ /; / /əg.ˈnɒs.tɪ.sɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the belief that it is impossible to know, or that we do not know, whether God or the divine exists

Discussions about religion often include debates between theism, atheism, and agnosticism.

The professor explained the distinction between atheism and agnosticism during the lecture.
