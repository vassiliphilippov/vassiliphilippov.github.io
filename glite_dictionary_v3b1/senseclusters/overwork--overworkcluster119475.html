overwork

verb
use something excessively
4.4 0.22/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo ä.v…öÀàw…ùÀêk / (AmE), / Àå…ô ä.v…ôÀàw…úÀêk / (BrE)
to use something too much or for too long, especially so that it loses its quality or value

Examples

To overwork a story
Critics argue that the director overworked the original story, making it less impactful.
If you overwork the dough, the bread will become tough and dry.
The essay has been overworked with too many complicated ideas.

Synonyms

    7.0 0.01/m

  • overdrive
    6.3 0.04/m
    overdrive pushes a person, machine, or system past normal limits to work very hard, while overwork means using something too much and damaging it.
