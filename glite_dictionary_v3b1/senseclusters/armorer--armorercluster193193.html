armorer

noun
military weapons maintenance specialist
8.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.mər.ər/ /; / /ˈɑːr.mər/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.mə.rə/ (BrE)
a person (especially in the military) who makes, repairs, or maintains weapons or armor; in military contexts, responsible for maintaining and repairing small arms and other weapons.

Examples

Military armorer
After several hours at the range, the armorer cleaned and serviced all the firearms.
As an armorer, he was responsible for ensuring all weapons were safe to use.
The armorer inspected every rifle before the soldiers went on patrol.
During the war, the armourer worked tirelessly to keep the soldiers' weapons in top condition.
She trained for years to become a skilled armourer in her family's factory.
The respected armourer crafted custom rifles for the royal court.
In medieval times, every castle employed an armourer to maintain its weapons and armor.
The armourer carefully repaired the knight's damaged breastplate before the next battle.
The famous English armourer was commissioned to forge a new sword for the king.
