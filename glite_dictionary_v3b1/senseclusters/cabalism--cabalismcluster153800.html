cabalism

noun
esoteric or mystical religious interpretation
8.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈbæl.ɪ.zəm / (AmE), / kəˈbæl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Adherence to esoteric or mystical interpretations of religious doctrine, especially in a traditional or extreme sense.

Examples

Cabalism in religion
His interpretation of the scripture was steeped in cabalism, focusing on hidden meanings.
Scholars debated whether the text reflected genuine mysticism or mere cabalism.
The group's leader promoted a form of cabalism that was often criticized as too extreme.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literalism - strict adherence to the literal meaning of scripture
  • synonym: mysticism - belief in direct spiritual insight beyond orthodox teachings
