portal

noun
website acting as an entry point to others
4.0 2.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…îrt…ôl / (AmE), / Ààp…îÀêt…ôl / (BrE)
(computing) A website that serves as an entry point to other websites, typically offering a range of services such as search engines, email, and news.

Examples

Web portal
Many people set their browser homepage to a portal for convenience.
The company's portal offers users access to news, weather, email, and a variety of online tools.
Yahoo! was one of the first major portals on the internet.

Synonyms

  • portal site
    3.9 0.02/m
    portal site is a website serving as a main entry to many services like search, email, and news, while portal is a general website entry point.
  • portal system
    4.4 0.00/m
    portal system is a centralized platform that gives single access to many services with dashboards and single sign-on, while portal is a website that links to other sites.
