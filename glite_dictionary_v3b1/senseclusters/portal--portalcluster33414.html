portal

noun
website acting as an entry point to others
1.0 4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔrtəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl / (BrE)
(computing) A website that serves as an entry point to other websites, typically offering a range of services such as search engines, email, and news.

Examples

Web portal
Many people set their browser homepage to a portal for convenience.
The company's portal offers users access to news, weather, email, and a variety of online tools.
Yahoo! was one of the first major portals on the internet.
