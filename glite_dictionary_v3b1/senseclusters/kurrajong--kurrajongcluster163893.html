kurrajong

noun
Australian tree yielding fiber and light wood
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜːr.ə.dʒɒŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.ə.dʒɒŋ / (BrE)
a widely distributed Australian tree (genus Brachychiton) known for its tough fiber, light wood, and use as emergency cattle fodder.

Examples

The kurrajong tree
During droughts, farmers feed cattle the foliage of the kurrajong.
Several kurrajongs lined the dusty road through the outback.
The kurrajong is valued for its attractive wood and resilient fiber.
