hold on

verb
persist; continue determinedly
1.0
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to continue doing something with determination; to persist despite difficulties or opposition

Examples

To hold on through trouble
Despite many failures, she continued to hold on to her dreams.
He reminded everyone to hold on and not lose hope during the tough times.
You have to hold on when faced with setbacks if you want to succeed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give up - to stop trying or cease making an effort
  • synonym: persist - to continue firmly despite difficulty
