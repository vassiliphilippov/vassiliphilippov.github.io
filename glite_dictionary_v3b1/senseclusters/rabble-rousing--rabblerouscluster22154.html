rabble-rousing

adjective
inciting or exciting crowds to action
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈræb.əlˌraʊ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræb.əlˌraʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
encouraging ordinary people to become angry, excited, or rebellious, especially for political purposes

Examples

Rabble-rousing political speech
Many criticized the journalist for his rabble-rousing articles during the protests.
Some leaders prefer quiet negotiation to rabble-rousing rhetoric.
The politician's rabble-rousing speeches attracted large, passionate crowds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agitational - intended to arouse people to act, especially against authority
  • antonym: conciliatory - intended to calm or pacify people
  • synonym: incendiary - tending to stir up conflict or rebellion
