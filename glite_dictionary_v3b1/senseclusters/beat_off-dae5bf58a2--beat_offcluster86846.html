beat off

verb
repel or fend off an attacker or annoyance
Pronunciation: / biːt ɔf / (AmE), / biːt ɒf / (BrE)
to succeed in repelling or driving away someone or something, especially when attacked or persistently bothered

Examples

To beat off attackers
Despite being outnumbered, the guards beat off the attackers at the gate.
She used her coat to beat off the aggressive geese in the park.
The villagers managed to beat off the wild animals by making loud noises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fend off - to defend oneself against someone or something
  • synonym: repel - to push back or drive away, especially an attacker
  • antonym: yield to - to give way or surrender to something or someone
