unforfeitable

adjective
not subject to forfeiture
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfɔrfɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːfɪtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be taken away or lost through forfeiture; secure from forfeiture.

Examples

An unforfeitable right
Freedom of expression is often considered an unforfeitable human right.
The contract includes an unforfeitable right to compensation.
Your pension benefits become unforfeitable after a certain number of years of service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forfeitable - liable to be forfeited or lost
