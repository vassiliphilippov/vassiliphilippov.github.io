searcher beetle

noun
large predatory North American beetle
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrtʃər ˌbiːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃə ˌbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a large metallic blue-green beetle native to North America that preys on caterpillars

Examples

A searcher beetle hunts caterpillars
A single searcher beetle can help control pest populations in a vegetable patch.
The shiny blue-green searcher beetle is often spotted under logs or stones in North America.
We found a searcher beetle in the garden, hunting for caterpillars among the leaves.
