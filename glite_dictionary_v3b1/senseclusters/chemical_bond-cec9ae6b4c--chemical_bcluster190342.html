chemical bond

noun
force linking atoms in compounds
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmɪkəl bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl bɒnd / (BrE)
a lasting attraction between atoms or ions that enables the formation of chemical compounds, typically resulting from an electrical force

Examples

A chemical bond between atoms
A water molecule is held together by a chemical bond between hydrogen and oxygen atoms.
Scientists study how a chemical bond can be formed or broken during chemical reactions.
The strength of a chemical bond determines the stability of a compound.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: covalent bond - a type of chemical bond formed by the sharing of electron pairs between atoms
  • degree lower: ionic bond - a type of chemical bond formed through electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions
