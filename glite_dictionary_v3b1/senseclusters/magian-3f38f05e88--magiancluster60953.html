Magian

noun
ancient Persian priest
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒiən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒiən / (BrE)
a member of the Magi, an ancient Persian priest or religious specialist, especially associated with Zoroastrianism

Examples

A Magian priest
The Magian performed rituals at dawn to honor the sacred fire.
Ancient texts describe a Magian as a wise advisor to Persian kings.
During the festival, a Magian led the community in prayers and offerings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform religious ceremonies
  • synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magical powers
