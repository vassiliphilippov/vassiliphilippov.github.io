unbegun

adjective
without beginning
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnbɪˈɡʌn / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈɡʌn / (BrE)
having no beginning; not created or caused, especially used in archaic or theological contexts to mean eternal.

Examples

Unbegun universe
In ancient philosophy, the universe was sometimes described as unbegun and everlasting.
The poem refers to a time unbegun, before the world was made.
Some medieval scholars wrote about the unbegun nature of the divine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: created - brought into existence; having a beginning
  • synonym: eternal - existing forever without beginning or end
