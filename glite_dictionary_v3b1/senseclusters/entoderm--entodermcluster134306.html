entoderm

noun
innermost embryonic germ layer
9.0 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɛntoʊˌdɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɛntədɜːm / (BrE)
the innermost germ layer of an embryo, which develops into the lining of the digestive and respiratory systems

Examples

The entoderm layer
During embryonic development, the entoderm forms before many other organs appear.
Researchers studied the process by which cells differentiate into entoderm in early stages of growth.
The entoderm gives rise to the lining of the gut and associated organs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ectoderm - the outermost germ layer in embryology
  • synonym: endoderm - the innermost germ layer in embryonic development; alternative, more common form
  • antonym: mesoderm - the middle germ layer in embryonic development
