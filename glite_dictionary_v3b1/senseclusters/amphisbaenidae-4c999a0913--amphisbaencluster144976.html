Amphisbaenidae

noun
family of worm lizards
9.9
Pronunciation: / æm.fɪzˈbiː.nɪ.diː / (AmE), / am.fɪzˈbiː.nɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of limbless, burrowing reptiles commonly known as worm lizards, distinguished by their elongated bodies and reduced eyes.

Examples

Amphisbaenidae worm lizards
Scientists have discovered several fossil members of the Amphisbaenidae in South America.
The Amphisbaenidae possess unique adaptations for a subterranean lifestyle.
Unlike snakes, the Amphisbaenidae have distinctive rings encircling their bodies.
