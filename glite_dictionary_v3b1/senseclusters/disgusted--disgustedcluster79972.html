disgusted

adjective
feeling strong distaste or revulsion
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡʌstɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌstɪd / (BrE)
feeling a strong sense of distaste or revulsion, often as a reaction to something offensive or unpleasant

Examples

Disgusted expression
He threw the spoiled food away with a disgusted expression.
Many were disgusted by the politician's behavior.
She looked disgusted when she found out what had happened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delighted - feeling or showing great pleasure
  • synonym: fed up - annoyed or bored, especially after having experienced something for too long
  • synonym: sickened - made to feel disgusted or revolted
