circadian rhythm

noun
biological 24-hour cycle in organisms
8.4 4.9
Pronunciation: /sər.ˈkeɪ.dɪ.ən ˈrɪð.əm/ (AmE), /sə.ˈkeɪ.dɪ.ən ˈrɪð.əm/ (BrE)
The natural, internal process that regulates the sleep-wake cycle and other physiological patterns, repeating roughly every 24 hours in living organisms.

Examples

The circadian rhythm cycle
Disrupting your circadian rhythm can make it harder to fall asleep at night.
Light exposure in the evening can delay the body’s circadian rhythm.
Shift work often causes problems with a person’s circadian rhythm.

  • synonym: biological clock - an internal mechanism controlling biological rhythms
