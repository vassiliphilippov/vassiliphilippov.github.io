Masorite

noun
scholar of the Masorah in Jewish tradition
Pronunciation: / məˈsɔːr.aɪt / (AmE), / məˈsɔː.rʌɪt / (BrE)
a scholar, particularly a Jewish scribe, who is an expert on the Masorah and contributed to its compilation and preservation.

Examples

A Masorite scholar
Every Masorite was meticulous about copying the sacred texts without error.
In her research, she cited the work of a renowned Masorite from the tenth century.
The Masorite scholars played a crucial role in standardizing the Hebrew Bible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Masorete - alternative term for a scholar who contributed to the Masorah
