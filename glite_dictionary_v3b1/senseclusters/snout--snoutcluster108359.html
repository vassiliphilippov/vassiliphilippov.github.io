snout

noun
beaklike projection on some insects' heads
3.6
Pronunciation: / snaʊt / (AmE), / snaʊt / (BrE)
the long, projecting part of an animal's head, especially the nose and jaws (including in insects and other animals)

Examples

A pig's snout
Some insects use their snout to feed by piercing plant tissues.
The scientist examined the shape of the beetle's snout under the microscope.
The weevil's distinctive snout allows it to bore into seeds and fruits.
A crocodile's long snout helps it catch prey at the water's surface.
He watched as the fox poked its snout out of the burrow, sniffing the air.
The pig rooted through the mud with its snout, searching for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muzzle - the projecting part of the face, including the nose and mouth, of an animal
