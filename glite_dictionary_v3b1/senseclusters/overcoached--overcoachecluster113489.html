overcoached

adjective
given excessive coaching
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkoʊtʃt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkəʊtʃt / (BrE)
having received too much coaching or instruction, often resulting in reduced effectiveness or natural ability

Examples

An overcoached athlete
The young athlete seemed overcoached and lost her natural style.
Some players become overcoached and start to overthink every move on the field.
He felt overcoached after too many conflicting instructions from different trainers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overtrained - having trained too much, often leading to negative effects
  • antonym: undercoached - having received too little coaching or instruction
