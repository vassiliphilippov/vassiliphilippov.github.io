insurability

noun
quality of being insurable
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌʃʊrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌʃʊərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being insurable; the conditions or factors that determine whether an insurance company will provide coverage to an applicant.

Examples

The insurability of applicants
Pre-existing medical conditions can affect the insurability of life insurance applicants.
She worried that changes in the law would impact the insurability of her business.
The insurability of the property was questioned after the flood damage was assessed.
