theodicy

noun
defense of God's goodness amid evil
Pronunciation: / θiˈɑːdəsi / (AmE), / θɪˈɒdɪsi / (BrE)
the branch of theology that seeks to defend the goodness and justice of God in the presence of evil in the world

Examples

The study of theodicy
Discussions of theodicy are central to addressing the problem of evil in theology.
Her thesis focused on how different religions construct their own forms of theodicy.
The philosopher wrote extensively on theodicy in an attempt to reconcile faith and reason.
