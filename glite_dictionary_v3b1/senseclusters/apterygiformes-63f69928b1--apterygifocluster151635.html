Apterygiformes

noun
order of flightless birds (kiwis)
Pronunciation: / ˌæptəˌrɪdʒɪˈfɔrmiːz /; / æpˌtɛrɪdʒəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌæptərɪdʒɪˈfɔːmiːz /; / æpˌtɛrɪdʒɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of flightless, ground-dwelling birds with vestigial wings, long bills, and small eyes; known as kiwis

Examples

The Apterygiformes birds

The Apterygiformes are native to New Zealand and include all species of kiwi.
Unlike most birds, Apterygiformes possess only vestigial wings, rendering them unable to fly.
Scientific studies have focused on the evolutionary history of Apterygiformes.
The kiwi is the most well-known member of the Apterygiformes.
There are five recognized species within the bird order Apterygiformes.
