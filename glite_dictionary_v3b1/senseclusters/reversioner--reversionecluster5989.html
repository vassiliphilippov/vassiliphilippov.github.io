reversioner

noun
person entitled to estate in reversion (law)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrʒənər / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃənə / (BrE)
(Law) A person who is entitled to inherit or take possession of property or an estate upon the termination of a particular estate, such as a lease or life estate.

Examples

The reversioner inherits property
If the life tenant dies, the property passes automatically to the reversioner.
The rights of a reversioner can sometimes be affected by the actions of the current tenant.
Under the lease agreement, the reversioner will regain possession of the land after ten years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remainder-man - a person who is entitled to inherit an estate after the end of a prior estate, but not the original owner
