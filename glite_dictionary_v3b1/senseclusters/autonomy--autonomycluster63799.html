autonomy

noun
self-government; political independence
6.7 9.99/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàt…în…ômi / (AmE), / …îÀêÀàt…ín…ômi / (BrE)
the right or condition of self-government, especially in a political context; freedom from external control or influence

Examples

Political autonomy
Cultural autonomy is important for preserving minority languages.
Many former colonies fought for autonomy in the twentieth century.
The region demanded greater autonomy from the central government.

Synonyms

  • self-government
    4.3 1.74/m
    self-government is the system of a group governing itself, while autonomy refers to the right or condition to govern without outside control.
  • self-determination
    4.3 7.02/m
    self-determination is the right to choose and govern oneself, while autonomy is the state or power of self-government.
  • self-rule
    5.8 0.54/m
    self-rule is governing a country or region by its own people, while autonomy can mean that self-government within a larger state.
  • independence
    3.0 35/m
    independence is becoming fully free from another country's control, while autonomy often means partial self-government inside a larger state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: independence - the fact or state of being independent
  • synonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority
  • antonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under domination or control
