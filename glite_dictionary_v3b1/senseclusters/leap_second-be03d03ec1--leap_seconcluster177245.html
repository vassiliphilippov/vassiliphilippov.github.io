leap second

noun
extra time adjustment in official clocks
7.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /liːp ˈsek.ənd/ (AmE), /liːp ˈsek.ənd/ (BrE)
A second added to or subtracted from Coordinated Universal Time to compensate for irregularities in the Earth's rotation.

Examples

A leap second added
A leap second was inserted at midnight last June, briefly making the day longer.
Technical systems sometimes experience glitches when a leap second is added to the world clock.
The international time standard occasionally includes a leap second to keep clocks in sync with Earth's rotation.
