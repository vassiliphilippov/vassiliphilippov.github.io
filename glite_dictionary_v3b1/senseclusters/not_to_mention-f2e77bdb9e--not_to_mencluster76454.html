not to mention

adverb
used to add something even more obvious
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: / nɑt tə ˈmɛnʃən / (AmE), / nɒt tə ˈmɛnʃən / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is even more obvious or important than the previous thing mentioned; let alone.

Examples

Not to mention the cost
He was tired from working all day, not to mention having to help his friend move in the evening.
She can't drive a car, not to mention ride a motorcycle.
The recipe is expensive, not to mention too complicated for beginners.
