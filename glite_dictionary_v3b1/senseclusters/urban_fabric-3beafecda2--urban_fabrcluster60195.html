urban fabric

noun
structure and character of a city
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɝː.bən ˈfæb.rɪk / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bən ˈfæb.rɪk / (BrE)
the physical and social structure that forms the character and layout of a city, including its buildings, streets, and communities

Examples

The urban fabric of cities
Preserving historic buildings helps maintain the unique urban fabric of a neighborhood.
Rapid development can dramatically alter the urban fabric and displace existing communities.
Transportation systems are essential elements of the urban fabric in modern cities.
