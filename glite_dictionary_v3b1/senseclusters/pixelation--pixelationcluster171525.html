pixelation

noun
image blurring effect
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪksəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɪksɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of obscuring or censoring part of an image or video by displaying it as large, visible pixels

Examples

Pixelation for privacy
The news channel used pixelation to protect the identity of the child in the video.
Heavy pixelation made it impossible to recognize the faces in the leaked footage.
Some reality shows use pixelation to cover up brand logos or sensitive content.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - to make an image unclear or less distinct
  • synonym: censor - to remove or block content considered inappropriate or sensitive
