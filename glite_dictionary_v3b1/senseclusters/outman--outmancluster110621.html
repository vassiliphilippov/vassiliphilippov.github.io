outman

verb
to have more people
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈmæn / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈmæn / (BrE)
to have or provide more people than an opponent, especially in a battle or competition

Examples

To outman the enemy
The defending army was quickly outmanned and forced to retreat.
In the debate, the team felt outmanned by the larger group of opponents.
Despite being outmanned, the small crew managed to hold their position for hours.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outnumber - to be greater in number than someone or something
  • synonym: overwhelm - to defeat completely, often by superior force or numbers
  • antonym: underdog - a person or group expected to lose a contest
