pause

verb
stop briefly before continuing
1.0 40/m
Pronunciation: / p…îz / (AmE), / p…îÀêz / (BrE)
to stop or interrupt an action, speech, or process for a short time before continuing

Examples

To pause for a moment
He paused briefly at the door before entering the room.
Let's pause the movie and get some snacks.
She paused to think before answering the question.
Please pause for a moment and think about your answer.
She had to pause the movie to answer the phone.
The teacher paused before explaining the next section of the lesson.

Synonyms

  • take a moment
    3.4 4/m
    take a moment is a very short, deliberate stop to think or notice, while pause is any brief interrupt before continuing.
  • hold on
    1.3 18/m
    hold on asks someone to wait briefly during talk or phone calls, while pause means a short stop in action or speech.
  • leave off
    4.1 4/m
    leave off means stop doing something at a certain point, while pause means interrupt an action briefly before continuing.
  • take a break
    1.4 14/m
    take a break means stop work to rest or relax for a short time, while pause is any quick interruption.
  • break
    1.0 110/m
    break often means a longer or final stop in an activity, while pause refers to a short interruption before resuming.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to interrupt an activity for a period
  • degree higher: halt - to come or bring to a complete stop
  • antonym: resume - to begin again after a pause or interruption
  • degree higher: stop - to cease moving or operating
