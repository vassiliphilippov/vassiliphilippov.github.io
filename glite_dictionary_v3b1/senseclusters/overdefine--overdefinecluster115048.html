overdefine

verb
to define too precisely
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to define something with excessive detail or unnecessary precision, often making it more complicated than needed

Examples

To overdefine the rules
If you overdefine the rules, people may find them hard to follow.
Teachers should avoid trying to overdefine every concept in the lesson.
The manual overdefines simple procedures, making them seem more difficult than they are.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcomplicate - to make something more complicated than necessary
  • antonym: simplify - to make something easier or less complex
