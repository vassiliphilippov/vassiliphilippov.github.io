mile

noun
unit of length (navigation), 1,852 meters
1.0 4/m
Pronunciation: / maÉªl / (AmE), / maÉªl / (BrE)
a unit of length used in navigation, called a nautical mile, equal to about 1,852 meters (including historical British navigation miles)

Examples

A nautical mile
Modern aircraft calculate their speed in nautical miles per hour, rather than kilometers.
One mile on the sea equals exactly 1,852 meters.
The ship was reported to be five miles offshore, according to the navigator.
A ship sailed a full mile before turning northward.
In old British charts, a mile could refer to 6,080 feet, not the statute mile.
The distance was measured as six nautical miles from shore.
Before the metric system, a British nautical mile measured 6,000 feet.
Naval charts from the 19th century often used the old mile as a standard unit.
Navigation instructors once taught students to calculate distances using the traditional mile.
