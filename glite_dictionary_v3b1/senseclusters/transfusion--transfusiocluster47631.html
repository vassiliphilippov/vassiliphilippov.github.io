transfusion

noun
introduction of blood into a vein or artery
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process of introducing blood or blood plasma into a vein or artery, usually as a medical treatment

Examples

Blood transfusion
After surgery, the patient required a transfusion to replace lost blood.
Doctors performed a transfusion to treat the severe anemia.
The availability of safe transfusion services is vital in emergency situations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blood transfusion - the direct transfer of blood into a person's circulation
