palisading

noun
fence of stakes
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪˈseɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈseɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a fence or barrier made of closely set strong stakes or posts, used for defense or enclosure

Examples

Wooden palisading
The settlers built palisading around their camp to protect themselves from wild animals.
Archaeologists discovered remnants of ancient palisading at the edge of the village site.
The wooden palisading was reinforced to withstand attacks during the siege.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier - a structure that blocks or limits movement
  • synonym: palisade - a strong fence made of stakes driven into the ground
  • synonym: wall - a solid structure that encloses or divides an area
