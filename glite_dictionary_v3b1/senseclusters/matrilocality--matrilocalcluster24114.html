matrilocality

noun
female-based residence
Pronunciation: / ˌmætrɪloʊˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪləʊˈkælɪti / (BrE)
a social or animal behavior pattern in which females remain in or near their birthplace after maturity, often forming groups with female relatives

Examples

Animal matrilocality groups
In some primate species, matrilocality helps maintain strong bonds among female relatives.
Anthropologists study matrilocality to understand how family groups are organized in different cultures.
The tradition of matrilocality can influence inheritance and social roles within a community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: matrilineal - tracing descent through the mother's line
  • antonym: patrilocality - a pattern where males remain in or near their birthplace
