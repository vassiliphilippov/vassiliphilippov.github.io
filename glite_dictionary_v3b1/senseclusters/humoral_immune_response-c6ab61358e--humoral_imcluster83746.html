humoral immune response

noun
immune response involving antibodies produced by B cells
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmərəl ɪmˈjun rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmərəl ɪmˈjuːn rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
the part of the immune response that is mediated by B cells and involves the production of antibodies in body fluids, mainly targeting bacteria and toxins

Examples

The humoral immune response fights bacteria
A weakened humoral immune response can increase susceptibility to bacterial diseases.
The humoral immune response involves antibodies circulating in the bloodstream to neutralize pathogens.
Vaccines are designed to stimulate the humoral immune response to help protect against certain infections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cell-mediated immune response - immune response involving T cells attacking infected cells
