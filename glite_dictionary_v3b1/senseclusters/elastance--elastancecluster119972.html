elastance

noun
ability to return shape
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæstəns / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstəns / (BrE)
the property of a structure that measures its ability to return to its original shape after being deformed

Examples

Lung elastance
The elastance of the lung tissue is important for normal breathing.
Doctors measure the elastance of blood vessels to assess cardiovascular health.
A decrease in elastance can make it harder for organs to function properly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliance - the ability of a structure to stretch or yield in response to force
  • synonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed
