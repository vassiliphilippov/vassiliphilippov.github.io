school of law

noun
graduate institution for law studies
Pronunciation: / skuːl əv lɑː / (AmE), / skuːl əv lɔː / (BrE)
a graduate-level institution offering advanced study leading to a law degree, typically preparing students to become lawyers.

Examples

A school of law
Many judges began their careers by attending a top-ranked school of law.
She applied to a prestigious school of law after finishing her undergraduate studies.
The school of law hosted a seminar on international human rights last week.
