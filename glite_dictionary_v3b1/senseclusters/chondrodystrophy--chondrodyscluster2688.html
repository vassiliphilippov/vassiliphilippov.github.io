chondrodystrophy

noun
inherited skeletal disorder causing dwarfism
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːndroʊˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkɒndrədɪsˈtrɒfi / (BrE)
An inherited disorder affecting bone and cartilage development, resulting in abnormal stature and skeletal deformities, often leading to dwarfism.

Examples

Chondrodystrophy disorder
People with chondrodystrophy may require specialized orthopedic care during growth.
Researchers are studying new treatments for inherited disorders such as chondrodystrophy.
The doctor diagnosed the child with chondrodystrophy after reviewing the results of genetic testing.
