pseudocyesis

noun
false pregnancy (medical condition)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.doʊ.saɪˈiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌsjuː.dəʊ.saɪˈiː.sɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a woman displays symptoms of pregnancy despite not actually being pregnant.

Examples

pseudocyesis symptoms
After several tests, the rare diagnosis of pseudocyesis was confirmed by the medical team.
Cases of pseudocyesis can include weight gain, missed periods, and even a sensation of fetal movement.
The doctor explained that her symptoms were due to pseudocyesis, not actual pregnancy.
