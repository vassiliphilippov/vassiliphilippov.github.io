fatigue

noun
temporary loss of strength and energy
4.2 9.15/m
Pronunciation: / f…ôÀàti…° / (AmE), / f…ôÀàtiÀê…° / (BrE)
A temporary loss of strength and energy caused by hard physical or mental work.

Examples

Physical fatigue
After running the marathon, a wave of fatigue washed over her.
Mental fatigue can make it hard to concentrate at work.
The team's fatigue was evident in their slow, uncoordinated movements.

Synonyms

  • tiredness
    1.0 1.02/m
    tiredness is a normal, short-lived lack of energy after effort, while fatigue sounds more formal and medical.
  • weariness
    3.1 2.41/m
    weariness is a steady feeling of tiredness after long effort, while fatigue means a temporary loss of strength and energy.
  • exhaustion
    3.6 7.22/m
    exhaustion is an extreme, overwhelming tiredness that can stop activity, while fatigue means a less severe, temporary drop in energy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: exhaustion - extreme tiredness; complete depletion of energy
  • degree lower: tiredness - the feeling of needing rest; less intense than fatigue
  • synonym: weariness - physical or mental exhaustion; tiredness
