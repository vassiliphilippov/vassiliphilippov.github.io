social democracy

noun
political ideology supporting gradual socialism through democracy
2.7 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.ʃəl dɪ.ˈmɑː.krə.siː/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.ʃəl dɪ.ˈmɒ.krə.siː/ (BrE)
A political ideology that advocates for a gradual transition from capitalism to socialism through democratic means, supporting both social justice and a mixed economy.

Examples

Social democracy movement
Many European countries adopted social democracy after World War II to balance economic growth with social welfare.
She studied the history of social democracy in Scandinavia for her political science thesis.
The party's commitment to social democracy was evident in its progressive social policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capitalism - economic system based on private ownership and profit
  • synonym: democratic socialism - political system mixing socialist goals with democratic processes
  • degree higher: socialism - economic and political system where the means of production are collectively owned
