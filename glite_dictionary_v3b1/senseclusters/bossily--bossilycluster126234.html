bossily

adverb
in a domineering way

in a manner that shows a tendency to order people around or act like a boss

Examples

To speak bossily
He bossily told everyone what to do without listening to their ideas.
The manager spoke bossily to the staff during the meeting.
She bossily arranged the chairs and expected everyone to follow her instructions.

Synonyms

  • domineeringly
    8.1 0.00/m
    domineeringly is very controlling and tries to rule others' actions, while bossily means ordering people around in a blunt, informal way.
  • overbearingly
    7.9 0.02/m
    overbearingly is forceful and ignores others' wishes to control a situation, while bossily is more about boss-like ordering in daily talk.
  • imperiously
    8.6 0.3/m
    imperiously gives orders in a proud, superior way, while bossily is more about acting like a boss and telling people what to do.
  • high-handedly
    8.2 0.01/m
    high-handedly means acting without asking others and ignoring their views, while bossily focuses on ordering people in a blunt personal way.
  • dictatorially
    9.1 0.03/m
    dictatorially is like a strict leader who forces rules and punishments, while bossily is less formal and often just tells others what to do.
