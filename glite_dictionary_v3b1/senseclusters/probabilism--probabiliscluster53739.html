probabilism

noun
Catholic doctrine about acting on probable opinions
4.6 9.7
Pronunciation: /prə.bə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /prə.bə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The ethical doctrine in Roman Catholic casuistry that allows a person to follow any opinion that has solid probability, even if another opinion is more probable, when expert views differ.
