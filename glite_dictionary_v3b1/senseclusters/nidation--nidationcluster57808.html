nidation

noun
implantation of a fertilized egg in uterus
Pronunciation: / naɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
(embryology) The process by which a fertilized egg becomes implanted in the lining of the uterus, especially in placental mammals.

Examples

nidation of embryo
Some fertility treatments aim to encourage proper nidation of the embryo.
Successful nidation is crucial for a pregnancy to progress beyond the earliest stages.
The doctor explained that nidation usually occurs about a week after fertilization.
