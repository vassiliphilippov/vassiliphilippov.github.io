ealdorman

noun
Anglo-Saxon noble
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈældəˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɔːldəˌmæn / (BrE)
a high-ranking noble or official who governed a region in Anglo-Saxon England on behalf of the king

Examples

The ealdorman governed
The ealdorman was responsible for maintaining order and collecting taxes in his territory.
During the council, the ealdorman advised the king on matters of law and defense.
After the battle, the victorious ealdorman was rewarded with new lands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earl - a British nobleman ranking below a marquess and above a viscount
  • antonym: king - the male ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth
