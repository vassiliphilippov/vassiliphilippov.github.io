logic gate

noun
electronic device performing logical operations
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlɑː.dʒɪk geɪt/ (AmE), /ˈlɒdʒ.ɪk geɪt/ (BrE)
an electronic circuit that has two or more inputs and produces an output based on a specific logical function of the inputs

Examples

Logic gate circuit
Engineers use a variety of logic gates to build complex circuits.
The AND logic gate only outputs a signal when all inputs are active.
You can design a half-adder using just two types of logic gate.
