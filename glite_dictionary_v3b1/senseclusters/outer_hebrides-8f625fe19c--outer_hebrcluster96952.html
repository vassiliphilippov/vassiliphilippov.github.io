Outer Hebrides

proper noun
group of Scottish islands
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtər ˈhɛbrɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌaʊtə ˈhɛbrɪdiːz / (BrE)
a group of islands forming an archipelago to the northwest of mainland Scotland, known for their rugged landscapes and Gaelic culture

Examples

The Outer Hebrides islands
Many visitors travel to the Outer Hebrides to experience its unique blend of natural beauty and history.
She spent her summer hiking across several islands in the Outer Hebrides.
The wildlife in the Outer Hebrides includes rare birds and flourishing marine life.
