cavetto

noun
concave quarter-circle molding
Pronunciation: / kəˈvɛtoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈvɛtəʊ / (BrE)
A concave molding with a cross section shaped like a quarter circle, commonly used in architecture and decorative design.

Examples

A cavetto molding
In classical architecture, a cavetto molding often decorates the tops of columns.
Restorers carefully replicated the damaged cavetto to match the building's original style.
The cornice featured a pronounced cavetto just below the roofline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ogee - an architectural molding with an S-shaped profile
