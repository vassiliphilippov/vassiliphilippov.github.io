coat of arms

noun
official heraldic symbol or design
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊt əv ɑːrmz / (AmE), / ˌkəʊt əv ˈɑːmz / (BrE)
the distinctive heraldic design on a shield or escutcheon, or on a surcoat or tabard, used to identify individuals, families, states, or organizations

Examples

A coat of arms design
Each knight bore his own coat of arms on his shield during tournaments.
The royal family's coat of arms is displayed above the palace gates.
The university incorporated a symbolic lion into its coat of arms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crest - a component or symbol atop a coat of arms
