cataleptic

noun
person suffering from catalepsy
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from catalepsy, a medical condition characterized by a trance or seizure with a loss of sensation and consciousness accompanied by rigidity of the body

Examples

A cataleptic patient
A support group was formed for families of cataleptics.
During the episode, the cataleptic remained unresponsive for several minutes.
The doctor observed the cataleptic in a rigid, motionless state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalepsy - a medical condition involving loss of sensation with motor rigidity
