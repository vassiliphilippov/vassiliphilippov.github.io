nominative

noun
grammatical case of the subject
8.1 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ëÀêm…ôn…ôt…™v / (AmE), / Ààn…ím…™n…ôt…™v / (BrE)
(in grammar) the case of a noun, pronoun, or adjective that is used as the subject of a verb.

Examples

Nominative case
English rarely marks the nominative case except in pronouns like 'he' and 'she'.
In Latin, the nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence.
The teacher asked the students to identify the nominative in each sentence.

Synonyms

  • nominative case
    8.6 0.01/m
    nominative case is the full formal term used in grammar books, while nominative is the shorter label often used in tables and notes.
  • subject case
    7.0 0.05/m
    subject case is a plain descriptive name for the case used by subjects, while nominative is the technical grammatical term.

Related Terms
