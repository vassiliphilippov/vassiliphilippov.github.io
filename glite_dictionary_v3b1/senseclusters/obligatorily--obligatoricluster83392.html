obligatorily

adverb
in an obligatory manner
2.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈblɪɡ.ə.tər.ɪ.li/ /; / /ə.ˈblɪɡ.ə.tɔːr.ɪ.li/ (AmE), /ə.ˈblɪɡ.ə.tə.rɪ.li/ /; / /ə.ˈblɪɡ.ə.tɔː.rɪ.li/ (BrE)
in a way that is required by law, rule, or necessity; because it is obligatory

Examples

To obligatorily follow rules
All employees must obligatorily complete the safety training before starting work.
Attendance at the meeting is obligatorily required for all department heads.
Masks were obligatorily worn in all public spaces during the outbreak.
Employees were obligatorily enrolled in the new pension scheme by law.
The safety training must be obligatorily completed before starting work.
Vaccinations for schoolchildren are obligatorily administered in some countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: optionally - in a manner that is not obligatory; as an option
  • antonym: voluntarily - in a way that is done by choice, not forced
