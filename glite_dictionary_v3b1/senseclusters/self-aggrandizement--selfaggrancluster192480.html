self-aggrandizement

noun
the act of exaggerating one's own importance
8.3 8.1
Pronunciation: /self.ə.ˈgræn.dɪz.mənt/ /; / /səlf.ə.ˈgræn.dɪz.mənt/ (AmE), /self.ə.ˈgræn.dɪz.mənt/ /; / /səlf.ə.ˈgræn.dɪz.mənt/ (BrE)
The act of promoting oneself as more important, powerful, or influential than one really is.

Examples

Self-aggrandizement behavior
His constant self-aggrandizement made him unpopular among his colleagues.
Instead of focusing on teamwork, she engaged in self-aggrandizement during the meeting.
The biography criticized the politician for his public self-aggrandizement.
Critics accused the politician of self-aggrandisement during her campaign.
His constant self-aggrandisement annoyed his colleagues, who preferred humility in the workplace.
The speech was seen as an exercise in self-aggrandisement rather than genuine leadership.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boastfulness - talking a lot about one's achievements or abilities
  • antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance
  • antonym: modesty - the quality of having a moderate view of one's own importance
  • synonym: self-promotion - the act of promoting oneself, one's abilities, or one's achievements
