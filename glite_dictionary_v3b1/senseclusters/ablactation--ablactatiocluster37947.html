ablactation

noun
weaning from mother's milk
Pronunciation: / ˌæb.lækˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌablækˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of gradually substituting other food for a mother's milk in the diet of an infant or young mammal; weaning.

Examples

Ablactation of a baby
Ablactation is an important milestone in a baby's development and should be approached gradually.
The practice of ablactation varies widely between cultures and families.
Veterinarians monitor the ablactation process in young animals to ensure healthy growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: weaning - the process of accustoming an infant or young animal to food other than its mother's milk
