debutant

noun
first-time performer
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪbjutænt /; / ˈdeɪbjuˌtænt / (AmE), / ˈdeɪbjuːtænt /; / ˈdeɪbjuːtɑːnt / (BrE)
a person who is making their first public appearance in a particular role, activity, or performance

Examples

A debutant performer
The young debutant impressed everyone at the art exhibition.
As a debutant in the theater, she was nervous before her first performance.
The coach praised the debutant for his outstanding effort in his first match.
The young debutant was nervous before stepping on stage.
As a debutant in the art world, she was eager to impress the critics.
The coach gave the debutant a chance to play in the final minutes of the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: novice - a person new to a job or activity
  • antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field
