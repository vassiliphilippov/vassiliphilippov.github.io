eagre

noun
sudden high tidal wave in a river or estuary
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈiːɡə / (BrE)
a sudden, high wave caused by a strong tidal flow, often occurring in rivers or estuaries and sometimes dangerous

Examples

A eagre wave
An eagre swept upriver, making navigation difficult for small boats.
Locals warned tourists to watch out for the powerful eagre during the new moon tides.
The sudden arrival of the eagre startled the fishermen along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tidal bore - a strong surge of water in a river or estuary caused by incoming tide
