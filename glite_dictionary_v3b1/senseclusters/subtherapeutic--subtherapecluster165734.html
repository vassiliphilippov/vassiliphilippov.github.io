subtherapeutic

adjective
below effective dose
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (BrE)
describing a dose or level of treatment that is too low to achieve the intended medical effect

Examples

A subtherapeutic dose
The doctor warned that a subtherapeutic dose might not control the infection effectively.
If the medication is subtherapeutic, the patient may not experience any improvement.
Researchers found that subtherapeutic antibiotic use can contribute to drug resistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ineffective - not producing the intended result
  • antonym: therapeutic - having a healing or beneficial effect
