canzona

noun
Renaissance instrumental piece
Pronunciation: / kænˈzoʊnə / (AmE), / kanˈtsəʊnə / (BrE)
an instrumental musical composition from the Renaissance or Baroque period, often lively and based on vocal models

Examples

Baroque canzona
The musicians played a beautiful canzona during the early part of the concert.
Many composers of the Baroque era wrote at least one canzona for brass instruments.
The lively rhythms of the canzona made it a favorite at Renaissance courts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ricercar - an early type of instrumental composition from the Renaissance
  • synonym: sonata - a composition for one or more instruments, typically in several movements
