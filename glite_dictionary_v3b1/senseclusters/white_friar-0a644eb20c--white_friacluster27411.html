White Friar

noun
Carmelite friar; Roman Catholic mendicant in white cloak
9.8
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈfraɪər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈfraɪə / (BrE)
a member of the Carmelite order of Roman Catholic friars, distinguished by their white cloaks and known as mendicant preachers

Examples

White Friar order
Legend says a White Friar still haunts the abbey ruins after sunset.
The White Friars were renowned for their preaching and charitable works across Europe.
The old monastery was once home to a community of White Friars in the Middle Ages.
