pick-me

noun
person seeking approval or attention
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪk.mi / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.mi / (BrE)
a person, especially a woman, who seeks approval or attention by acting in ways intended to appeal to others, often at the expense of their own interests or dignity

Examples

A pick-me person
He was called a pick-me after repeatedly putting down his friends to gain attention.
Many social media users criticize the pick-me mentality for encouraging unhealthy competition among women.
Try not to act like a pick-me just to fit in with that group.
