vitrectomy

noun
surgical removal of eye's vitreous humor
Pronunciation: / vɪˈtrɛktəmi / (AmE), / vɪˈtrɛktəmi / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which the vitreous humor is removed from the eye and replaced with a saline solution.

Examples

A vitrectomy surgery
After her vitrectomy, she experienced improved vision and a quicker recovery than expected.
Complications from diabetes can lead to procedures like a vitrectomy to restore or preserve eyesight.
The ophthalmologist recommended a vitrectomy to treat the patient's severe retinal detachment.
