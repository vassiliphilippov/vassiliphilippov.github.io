black spruce

noun
a North American coniferous tree (Picea mariana)
Pronunciation: / blæk sprus / (AmE), / blak spruːs / (BrE)
a small coniferous tree of northeastern North America (Picea mariana), with spreading branches, dense foliage, and wood considered inferior; typically found in boggy areas

Examples

The black spruce tree
Birds and mammals rely on the seeds and shelter provided by the black spruce forests.
Lumber from black spruce is often used for pulp rather than construction due to its lower quality.
The black spruce thrives in cold, boggy areas across Canada and Alaska.
