keeper

noun
person responsible for care or supervision
2.5 6.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkiÀêp…ö /; / ÀàkiÀêp…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàkiÀêp…ô / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for taking care of something or someone

Examples

Zoo keeper
Each building has its own keeper responsible for maintenance and security.
She worked for years as a keeper at the city zoo.
The lion keeper feeds and looks after the big cats every morning.
As the team keeper, she was trusted to guide new members and ensure everyone worked together.
He felt like a keeper to his younger siblings after their parents passed away.
Many religions teach that you are your brother's keeper and should help others in need.

Synonyms

  • caretaker
    1.9 3.07/m
    caretaker is often an employed person who lives on site to look after a building or person, while keeper is any responsible caregiver.
  • custodian
    3.8 2.74/m
    custodian is a formal term for someone who protects and maintains property or records in an organization, while keeper is more general.
  • guardian
    2.8 16/m
    guardian is a legal protector often responsible for a child's welfare or property rights, while keeper means a general caretaker.
  • tender
    5.1 0.27/m
    tender is a rare job title for a person who cares for animals or equipment, while keeper is the common general term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guardian - a person who protects or cares for someone or something
  • synonym: warden - a person responsible for the supervision of a particular place or group
