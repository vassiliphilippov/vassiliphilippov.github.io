canvas

noun
fabric used as a sail on ships
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.vəs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs / (BrE)
a strong, heavy fabric used for sails, tents, or as a surface for painting

Examples

Canvas tent
The captain ordered the canvas to be hoisted as the wind picked up.
The old ship's canvas billowed gracefully in the evening breeze.
Years at sea had left the canvas worn and patched in several places.
Sails constructed from sturdy canvas helped the ship withstand harsh sea conditions.
The artist stretched the canvas tightly across the wooden frame before beginning to paint.
The old tent was made of thick canvas, which kept out wind and rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linen - a fabric made from flax fibers, used for sheets and clothing
  • synonym: sail - a sheet of strong cloth used to catch wind on a sailing vessel
