A method of psychotherapy in which the client guides the direction and pace of sessions, emphasizing their personal experience and agency.

Examples

Client-centered therapy approach
Client-centered therapy empowers individuals to lead the conversation and focus on their own concerns.
In client-centered therapy, the therapist shows empathy and unconditional positive regard for the client.
Many people find client-centered therapy helpful for building self-awareness and achieving personal growth.
