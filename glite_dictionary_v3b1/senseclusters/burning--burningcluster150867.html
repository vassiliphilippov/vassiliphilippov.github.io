burning

noun
execution by fire as punishment
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or practice of executing someone by setting them on fire, especially as a form of capital punishment historically

Examples

Public burning of criminals
A detailed account of the burning in the town square was recorded in the old chronicles.
Historically, burning was used as a form of capital punishment for heresy.
The witch was sentenced to burning at the stake for her alleged crimes.
