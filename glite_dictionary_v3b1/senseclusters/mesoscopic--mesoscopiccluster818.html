mesoscopic

adjective
intermediate physical scale
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsəˈsɑpɪk /; / ˌmɛsəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəˈsɒpɪk /; / ˌmɛsəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
describing or relating to a size or scale that is between microscopic and macroscopic, especially in science

Examples

A mesoscopic scale
Researchers observed mesoscopic effects in the material, where quantum behavior influenced its electrical properties.
At the mesoscopic scale, particles can show both classical and quantum characteristics.
The device operates in a mesoscopic regime, making it useful for studying quantum transport phenomena.
The scientist studied mesoscopic particles that were larger than molecules but smaller than grains of sand.
At the mesoscopic level, materials can show properties different from both bulk and atomic scales.
New technologies allow researchers to manipulate mesoscopic systems for advanced electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: macroscopic - large enough to be seen with the naked eye
  • degree lower: microscopic - too small to be seen without a microscope
