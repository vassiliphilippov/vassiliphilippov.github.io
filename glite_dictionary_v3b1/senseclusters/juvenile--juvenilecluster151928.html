juvenile

noun
young person not fully developed
3.9 4.85/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íuÀêv…ôÀåna…™l / (AmE), / Ààd íuÀêv…ôna…™l / (BrE)
a young person who is not yet fully grown or legally an adult

Examples

A juvenile offender
As a juvenile, he was tried in family court rather than criminal court.
Libraries often have a special section for juveniles containing age-appropriate books.
The police questioned the juvenile about the incident in the park.
Many juveniles enjoy spending time at the community center after school.
The court treats juvenile offenders differently from adults.
The new policy aims to reduce juvenile crime in urban areas.

Synonyms

  • young person
    1.0 42/m
    young person is a neutral term for any underage individual in everyday speech, while juvenile sounds more legal and formal.
  • minor
    2.8 8.64/m
    minor is a person under the legal age, while juvenile is often used in law or court to refer to that person.
  • nonadult
    4.6 0.01/m
    nonadult is a formal legal label for someone not of age, while juvenile carries similar legal meaning but sounds rarer.
  • adolescent
    3.5 21/m
    adolescent is a person in puberty and teenage years, while juvenile covers any underage person and often implies legal context.
  • youth
    1.0 9.47/m
    youth often means a young person or group, especially males, while juvenile more clearly points to an underage individual.
  • child
    1.0 1056/m
    child usually means a younger kid below puberty, while juvenile can include teenagers and also appears in legal or formal talk.
  • youngster
    1.0 15/m
    youngster is a friendly, informal word for a child or young person, while juvenile is more formal and legally charged.
