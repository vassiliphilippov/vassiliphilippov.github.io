juvenile

noun
young person not fully developed
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl / (BrE)
a young person who is not yet fully grown or legally an adult

Examples

A juvenile offender
As a juvenile, he was tried in family court rather than criminal court.
Libraries often have a special section for juveniles containing age-appropriate books.
The police questioned the juvenile about the incident in the park.
Many juveniles enjoy spending time at the community center after school.
The court treats juvenile offenders differently from adults.
The new policy aims to reduce juvenile crime in urban areas.
