homotopic

adjective
can be continuously deformed
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊməˈtɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒməˈtɒpɪk / (BrE)
describing two functions, shapes, or spaces in topology that can be continuously deformed into each other

Examples

Two homotopic functions
In algebraic topology, two functions are homotopic if one can be smoothly transformed into the other.

If two paths are homotopic, they represent the same element in the fundamental group.
In algebraic topology, two loops are homotopic if one can be deformed into the other without breaking.
