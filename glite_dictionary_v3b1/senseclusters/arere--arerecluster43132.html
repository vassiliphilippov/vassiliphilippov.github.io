arere

noun
West African tree with lobed leaves and pale wood
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈrɛəreɪ / (AmE), / ɑːˈrɛəreɪ / (BrE)
A large West African tree with palmately lobed leaves, cymose clusters of small white flowers, and soft pale wood.

Examples

The arere tree
Local craftsmen used arere branches to carve intricate ceremonial masks.
The rainforest expedition documented several mature arere trees among the native flora.
The wood of the arere is prized for furniture making due to its softness and pale yellow color.
