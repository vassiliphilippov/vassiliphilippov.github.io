necrophilic

adjective
feeding on dead matter
9.9
Pronunciation: /ne.krə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ (AmE), /ne.krə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ (BrE)
attracted to, living on, or feeding on dead or decaying organic matter, especially in ecological or biological contexts

Examples

Necrophilic insects
Certain necrophilic insects play a vital role in breaking down animal remains in forests.
The biologist observed a necrophilic fungus growing rapidly on the decaying log.
Some beetles are known for their necrophilic behavior, colonizing carcasses soon after death.

Related Terms

  • antonym: necrophobic - having an aversion to dead or decaying matter
  • synonym: saprophytic - feeding on or deriving nourishment from decaying organic matter
