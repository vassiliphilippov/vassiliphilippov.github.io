large margin

noun
a significant difference in results
4.9
Pronunciation: / lɑrdʒ ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
a significant or substantial difference between two amounts, scores, or results

Examples

A large margin increase
Profits increased by a large margin this quarter compared to last year.
The candidate won the election by a large margin, securing over two-thirds of the votes.
Their team defeated the opposition by a large margin in the championship final.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrow margin - a small difference between results or amounts
