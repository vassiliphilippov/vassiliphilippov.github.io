mythos

noun
traditional stories
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪθoʊs / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɒs / (BrE)
the collection of traditional stories or myths that explain the beliefs, customs, or origins of a group, culture, or society

Examples

Greek mythos
The ancient Greeks developed a rich mythos to explain the origins of the world.
Modern films often draw on the superhero mythos to create new stories.
Each culture has its own unique mythos that shapes its traditions and values.
The mythos of the American West has shaped the nation's identity for generations.
Many films draw on the mythos of superheroes to tell stories of good versus evil.
The festival celebrates the ancient mythos of the local people through music and dance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fact - something that is known or proven to be true
  • synonym: folklore - the traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community
  • synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes regarded as historical but not authenticated
  • synonym: myth - a traditional story explaining natural or social phenomena
  • synonym: mythology - a body of myths, especially those belonging to a particular religious or cultural tradition
