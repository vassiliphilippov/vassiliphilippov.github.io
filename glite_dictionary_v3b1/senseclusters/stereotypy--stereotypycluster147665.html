stereotypy

noun
use of stereotypes
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛriəˌtaɪpi / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəˌtaɪpi / (BrE)
the tendency to form or apply fixed, generalized ideas or stereotypes about groups of people or things

Examples

Cultural stereotypy
The research focused on how stereotypy influences people's perceptions of different cultures.
Teachers are trained to recognize and avoid stereotypy in the classroom.
Efforts to reduce stereotypy can help create a more inclusive environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individuality - the quality of being an individual, not defined by group traits
  • synonym: stereotyping - the act of assigning generalized traits to a group
