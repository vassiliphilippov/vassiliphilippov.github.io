yardang

noun
wind-eroded desert ridge
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːrdæŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdæŋ / (BrE)
a long, narrow ridge of rock or sand formed by wind erosion in a desert

Examples

A yardang ridge
The explorers photographed a massive yardang rising above the desert floor.
Wind and sand slowly shaped the yardang over thousands of years.
Scientists studied the unique structure of each yardang to learn about the region's climate history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dune - a hill of sand formed by wind in a desert or on a beach
  • synonym: mesa - a flat-topped hill with steep sides, found in deserts
