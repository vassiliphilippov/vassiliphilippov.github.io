trephining

noun
skull surgery procedure
9.9
Pronunciation: / trəˈfaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / trɪˈfaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making a hole in the skull using a surgical instrument, usually for medical reasons

Examples

Surgical trephining procedure
In ancient times, trephining was performed to treat head injuries and relieve pressure.
Archaeologists have found skulls showing evidence of trephining in prehistoric societies.
Modern surgeons rarely use trephining except in specific emergency situations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: craniotomy - a surgical operation in which a bone flap is removed from the skull
