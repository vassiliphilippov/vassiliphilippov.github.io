Louis XIII

proper noun
King of France, 1610–1643
Pronunciation: / ˌluː.i θɜrˈtiːnθ / (AmE), / ˌluː.i ˌθɜːˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
King of France from 1610 to 1643, known for centralizing royal power and relying heavily on Cardinal Richelieu.

Examples

Louis XIII of France
Louis XIII became king of France at the age of nine after his father's assassination.
Many French historical dramas feature Louis XIII as an important character during the early 17th century.
The reign of Louis XIII marked the strengthening of royal authority with Cardinal Richelieu's support.
