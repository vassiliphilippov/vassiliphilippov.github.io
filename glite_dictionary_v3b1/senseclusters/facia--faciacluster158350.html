facia

noun
fibrous tissue band in the body
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪʃ(i)ə / (AmE), / ˈfæʃiə / (BrE)
A sheet or band of fibrous connective tissue that separates, supports, or binds together muscles, organs, or other structures in the body.

Examples

A facia sheet
A torn facia can cause pain and restrict movement after an injury.
During surgery, the doctor carefully separated the muscle from the surrounding facia.
Inflammation of the plantar facia is a common cause of heel pain.
