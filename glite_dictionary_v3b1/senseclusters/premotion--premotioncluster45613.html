premotion

noun
prior movement or change
9.9
Pronunciation: / priːˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / priːˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
a movement or change that happens before another action or event, often as a cause or preparation

Examples

A premotion before change
The philosopher discussed the concept of premotion in relation to causality.
Some theories suggest that every action is preceded by a kind of premotion.
In the debate, she argued that premotion is necessary for understanding the sequence of events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consequence - something that follows as a result of an action
  • synonym: precursor - something that comes before and leads to another
