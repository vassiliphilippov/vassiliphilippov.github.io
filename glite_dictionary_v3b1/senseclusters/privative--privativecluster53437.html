privative

noun
negating morpheme
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvətɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪvətɪv / (BrE)
a morpheme, especially a prefix, that indicates negation, absence, or removal of a quality or state

Examples

An privative morpheme
The prefix 'un-' in 'unknown' is a privative that negates the meaning of the word.
Linguists study how a privative changes the sense of a root word.
In the word 'dislike', 'dis-' acts as a privative to show the opposite meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: augmentative - a morpheme that increases or intensifies meaning
  • synonym: negative prefix - a prefix that gives a word a negative meaning
