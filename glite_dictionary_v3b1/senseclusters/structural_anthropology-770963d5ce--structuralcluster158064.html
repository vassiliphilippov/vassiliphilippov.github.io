structural anthropology

noun
theory about underlying social structures in anthropology
Pronunciation: / ˈstrʌktʃərəl ænˌθrɑːpəˌlɑːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl ænˌθrəpəˈlɒdʒi / (BrE)
An anthropological theory proposing that unobservable social structures underlie and generate observable cultural and social phenomena.

Examples

Structural anthropology theory
Claude Lévi-Strauss is closely associated with the development of structural anthropology in the twentieth century.
Critics of structural anthropology argue that it overlooks historical and individual variation in cultures.
Students of structural anthropology analyze myths to uncover patterns underlying human societies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: functionalism - an anthropological theory focusing on the roles social institutions play in maintaining society
  • synonym: structuralism - a theoretical paradigm asserting that elements of culture must be understood in terms of their relationship to a broader structure
