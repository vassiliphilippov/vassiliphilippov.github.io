chiasm

noun
crossing of structures in X shape
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪˌæzəm / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.az(ə)m / (BrE)
an intersection or crossing of two tracts, such as nerves or vessels, in the shape of the letter X

Examples

The chiasm crossing
Damage to the chiasm can result in vision problems or loss of peripheral vision.
The distinctive X-shape of a neural chiasm is important in anatomical diagrams.
The optic chiasm is where the optic nerves partially cross in the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chiasma - a crossing or intersection of two tracts, especially in anatomy
