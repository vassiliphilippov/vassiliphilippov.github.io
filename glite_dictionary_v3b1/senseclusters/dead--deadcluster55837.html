dead

adjective
lacking bounce; unresponsive (of objects, materials)
8.2
Pronunciation: / dɛd / (AmE), / dɛd / (BrE)
lacking bounce or resonance; dull or lifeless in sound or movement

Examples

Dead ball
After hours of play, the dead ball barely rebounded from the court.
He complained that the drum sounded dead and needed to be tightened.
The guitar string felt dead and had to be replaced before the concert.
Some compact discs have a surprisingly dead sound quality compared to vinyl records.
The dead wall panels absorbed most echoes and reverb in the room.
The studio's dead acoustics make it ideal for voice recording.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bouncy - having a lot of bounce; able to rebound easily
  • antonym: resonant - producing a deep, full, or reverberating sound
