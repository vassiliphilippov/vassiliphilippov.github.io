scienter

noun
legal knowledge of wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / saɪˈɛntər / (AmE), / saɪˈɛntə / (BrE)
knowledge that an act is illegal or wrong, especially as required to prove legal responsibility

Examples

Scienter requirement
The prosecution must prove the defendant acted with scienter to establish fraud.
Without evidence of scienter, the court could not convict her of intentional wrongdoing.
In civil cases, demonstrating scienter is often necessary to show liability for damages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
  • synonym: mens rea - the intention or knowledge of wrongdoing in criminal law
