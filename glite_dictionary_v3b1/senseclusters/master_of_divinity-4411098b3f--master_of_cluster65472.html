Master of Divinity

noun
postgraduate degree in religion
Pronunciation: / ˈmæs.tər əv dɪˈvɪn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stər əv dɪˈvɪn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a postgraduate academic degree in religion, typically awarded to those preparing for ministry or advanced study in theology.

Examples

A Master of Divinity degree
Applicants to the program must already hold a Master of Divinity or an equivalent degree.
Many seminaries require a Master of Divinity for ordination into ministry.
She earned her Master of Divinity before becoming a pastor at the local church.
