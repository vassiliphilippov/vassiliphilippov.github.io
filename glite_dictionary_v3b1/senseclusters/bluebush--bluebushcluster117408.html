bluebush

noun
Australian shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
an Australian shrub with bluish leaves, commonly found in dry inland regions

Examples

The bluebush shrub
The sheep grazed on the bluebush that covered the arid landscape.
Farmers rely on bluebush as a source of food for their livestock during dry seasons.
We spotted several bluebush plants while driving through the Australian outback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saltbush - a type of Australian shrub adapted to salty soils
