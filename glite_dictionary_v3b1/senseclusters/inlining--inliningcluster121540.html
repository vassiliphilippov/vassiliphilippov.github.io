inlining

noun
embedding resources directly
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of embedding external resources, such as code or images, directly within a document instead of linking to them separately

Examples

Inlining images in HTML
Web developers often use inlining to improve the loading speed of their websites.
The inlining of images in emails can help ensure they display correctly without external downloads.
By inlining small CSS files, the team reduced the number of server requests needed for each page.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embedding - the act of placing content directly within another file or document
  • antonym: linking - the act of connecting to external resources rather than including them directly
