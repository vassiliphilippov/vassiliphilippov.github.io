perigonium

noun
outer flower parts (calyx and corolla)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛr.ɪˈɡoʊ.ni.əm / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˈɡəʊ.ni.əm / (BrE)
the collective outer parts of a flower, consisting of the calyx and corolla, which enclose the stamens and pistils

Botanists examined the texture of the perigonium during their research.
In some plant species, the perigonium is brightly colored to attract pollinators.
The perigonium consists of both the sepals and petals of the flower.
