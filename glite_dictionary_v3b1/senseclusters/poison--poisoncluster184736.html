poison

noun
harmful or deadly substance
2.2 1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪ.zən / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪ.zən / (BrE)
A substance that is capable of causing harm, illness, or death to a living organism when introduced or absorbed.

Examples

Rat poison
Keep all cleaning chemicals out of reach, as they may contain poison.
Some plants produce natural poison to protect themselves from insects.
The detective suspected that the victim had died from poison placed in his drink.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts a poison
  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms
