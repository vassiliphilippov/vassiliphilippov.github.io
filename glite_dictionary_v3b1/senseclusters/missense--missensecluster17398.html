missense

adjective
mutation changing amino acid
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌsɛns / (BrE)
describing a genetic mutation that results in the substitution of one amino acid for another in a protein

Examples

Missense mutation
A missense mutation can lead to a nonfunctional protein and cause disease.
Researchers identified a missense variant in the gene responsible for the disorder.
The doctor explained that the patient's condition was due to a missense change in the DNA sequence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonsense - describing a mutation that introduces a premature stop codon in a gene
  • antonym: silent - describing a mutation that does not change the amino acid sequence of a protein
