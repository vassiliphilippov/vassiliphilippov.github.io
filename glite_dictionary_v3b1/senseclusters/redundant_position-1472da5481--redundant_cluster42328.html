redundant position

noun
unneeded job role after restructuring
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌn.dənt pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌn.dənt pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A job role that is no longer necessary, often because of organizational restructuring, downsizing, or technological changes.

Examples

A redundant position after automation
After automation, her role became a redundant position and she was offered severance pay.
He found out his job was classified as a redundant position during the company’s cost-cutting phase.
The restructuring resulted in several redundant positions across different departments.
