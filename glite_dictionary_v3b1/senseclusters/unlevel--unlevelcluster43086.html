unlevel

adjective
unequal or unfair
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
not equal or fair in status, opportunity, treatment, or conditions; unequal or uneven.

Examples

Unlevel playing field
The new regulations created an unlevel playing field for small businesses.
Many argued that the competition was unlevel due to the different starting conditions.
She felt the hiring process was unlevel and favored those with connections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: level - equal or fair in status, opportunity, or treatment
  • synonym: unequal - not the same in amount, value, or status
  • synonym: unfair - not just or impartial
