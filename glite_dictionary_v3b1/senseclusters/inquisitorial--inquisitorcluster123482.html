Synonyms

  • investigational
    7.1 0.05/m
    investigational is used for official inquiries by police, health boards, or government, while inquisitorial usually names judge-led judicial questioning.
  • inquisitional
    8.9 0.02/m
    inquisitional suggests harsh, often historical inquests and persistent questioning, while inquisitorial refers to formal judicial or court-based investigation.
  • investigatory
    6.8 0.29/m
    investigatory is a formal adjective for official fact-finding in government or police probes, while inquisitorial highlights judge-led legal inquiry.
  • investigative
    5.1 9.11/m
    investigative describes detailed research by journalists or teams, while inquisitorial implies an authority-led, judicial search for facts.
  • inquisitory
    8.9 0.00/m
    inquisitory means searching and probing in a careful way, while inquisitorial emphasizes an official, judge-led investigation with authority.
