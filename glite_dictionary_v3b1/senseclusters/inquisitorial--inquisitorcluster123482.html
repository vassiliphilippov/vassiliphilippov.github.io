inquisitorial

adjective
relating to judicial investigation by a judge
6.3 7.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkwɪzəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to official or judicial investigation, especially where an authority actively investigates the facts

Examples

Inquisitorial system
Critics argue that the inquisitorial method can undermine the rights of the accused.
Many civil law countries have adopted inquisitorial procedures in their criminal justice system.
The court followed an inquisitorial process, with the judge actively interrogating witnesses.
He resented the inquisitorial authority the investigator seemed to wield over ordinary citizens.
Some legal systems are based on the inquisitorial rather than the adversarial model of justice.
The committee's inquisitorial approach made the witnesses nervous during the hearing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accusatorial - relating to a judicial process in which the parties present evidence before a judge, who acts as an impartial arbiter
  • synonym: investigative - engaged in or given to investigation; especially in journalism or official matters
