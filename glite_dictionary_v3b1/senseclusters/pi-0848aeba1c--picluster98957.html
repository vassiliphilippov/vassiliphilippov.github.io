PI

noun
private investigator (detective)
3.7 0.67/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpiÀêÀàa…™ / (AmE), / ÀåpiÀêÀàa…™ / (BrE)
a private investigator; someone who can be employed as a detective to collect information and assist in criminal investigations

Examples

Hire a PI
She hired a PI to find her missing brother when the police ran out of leads.
The PI followed the suspect for several days before gathering enough evidence.
The movie features a tough PI solving a mysterious case in Los Angeles.

Synonyms

  • private investigator
    3.7 1.81/m
    private investigator is the full formal term used in reports and ads, while PI is the short initialism used in speech.
  • private detective
    3.4 0.89/m
    private detective often sounds slightly old-fashioned and emphasizes investigation work, while PI is a modern, neutral label for that role.
  • private eye
    4.2 0.80/m
    private eye is an informal, storybook name often used in fiction or casual talk, while PI is neutral and professional.
  • shamus
    9.9 0.07/m
    shamus is a dated slang term found in old detective stories or film, while PI is the current professional term.

Related Terms
