carbineer

noun
soldier armed with a carbine
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrbɪˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a soldier, especially formerly a mounted one, armed with a carbine.

Examples

A carbineer soldier
In the 18th century, a carbineer was a key member of cavalry regiments.
The carbineer rode ahead to scout the enemy’s position.
The museum displayed a uniform once worn by a French carbineer.
