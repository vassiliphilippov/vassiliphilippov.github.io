undermine

verb
weaken or hinder (especially gradually)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈmaɪn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈmaɪn / (BrE)
to damage or weaken (someone or something), often gradually or secretly; or to hinder normal operations or destroy the integrity of something

Examples

To undermine confidence
Failure to maintain the equipment could undermine normal operations and lead to costly breakdowns.
Recent cyber attacks have undermined the company's computer systems, causing significant disruption.
The spies tried to undermine the enemy's supply routes during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy, damage, or obstruct something, especially for political or military advantage
