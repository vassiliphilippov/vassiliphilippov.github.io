overscore

verb
to mark above
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈskɔr / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈskɔː / (BrE)
to mark or write a line or symbol above a letter, number, or character

Examples

To overscore a symbol
In some languages, you overscore certain letters to indicate a different pronunciation.
The teacher asked students to overscore the important numbers in their calculations.
To show the repeating part of a decimal, you should overscore those digits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overline - to draw a line above text or a character
  • antonym: underline - to draw a line under a word or character
