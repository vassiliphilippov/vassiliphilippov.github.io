zeal

noun
great enthusiasm or eagerness
5.8 4.30/m
Pronunciation: / ziÀêl /; / zil / (AmE), / ziÀêl / (BrE)
great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective

Examples

Great zeal for causes
His zeal for environmental protection inspired many others to get involved.
She approached her new job with remarkable zeal and dedication.
The volunteers worked with zeal to clean up the neighborhood park.
He approached each new challenge with remarkable zeal.
Her zeal for learning impressed all her teachers.
Sometimes, too much zeal can become overwhelming for others.
He attacked the new project with remarkable zeal, completing his work ahead of schedule.
Her zeal for volunteer work inspired others to get involved in community service.
The teacher's zeal for education was evident in every lesson she taught.
