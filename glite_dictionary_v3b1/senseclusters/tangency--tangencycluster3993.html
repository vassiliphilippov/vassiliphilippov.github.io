tangency

noun
the state of being tangent
9.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtæn.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒən.si / (BrE)
the state of being tangent; the condition in which a curve or surface touches another at a single point or along a line without crossing it

Examples

Circle tangency point
A chord and a tangent line have only one point of tangency on a circle.
In geometry, tangency describes the precise point where a circle meets a straight line.
The engineer examined the tangency of the two surfaces to ensure they only touched at one point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tangent - a straight line or plane that touches a curve or curved surface at a point, but does not cross it
