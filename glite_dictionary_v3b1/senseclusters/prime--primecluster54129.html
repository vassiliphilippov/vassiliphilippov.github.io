prime

noun
number divisible only by 1 and itself
2.1
Pronunciation: / praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
a natural number that has exactly two distinct natural number divisors: 1 and itself

Examples

A prime number
Mathematicians study the distribution of primes among natural numbers.
The number 7 is a prime, while 8 is not.
Two is the only even prime number.

Related Terms

  • antonym: composite number - a natural number that is not prime, having more than two divisors
