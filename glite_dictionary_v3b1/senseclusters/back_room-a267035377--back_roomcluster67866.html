back room

noun
room at rear, often private or restricted
5.3
Pronunciation: / bæk ɹuːm / (AmE), / bak ruːm / (BrE)
A room situated at the rear of an establishment, often used for private meetings or activities accessible only to select individuals.

Examples

The back room meeting
Only the managers are allowed in the back room during business hours.
Rumors spread that important decisions were made in the back room rather than the boardroom.
They discussed the deal privately in the back room away from customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front room - a room at the front of a building, often open to the public or guests
