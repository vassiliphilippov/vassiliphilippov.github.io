deformability

noun
ability to be deformed
Pronunciation: / dɪˌfɔrməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌfɔːməˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a material or object to be deformed or changed in shape without breaking

Examples

The deformability of metals
The deformability of metals is important in manufacturing processes like forging and rolling.
Scientists study the deformability of biological tissues to understand how they respond to stress.
Rubber's high deformability makes it ideal for products that need to stretch or compress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not easily bent or changed
