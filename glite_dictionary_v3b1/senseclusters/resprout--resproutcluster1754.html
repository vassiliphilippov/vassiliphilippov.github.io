resprout

verb
to grow again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈspraʊt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈspraʊt / (BrE)
to grow new shoots or leaves after being cut, damaged, or destroyed

Examples

To resprout after fire
After the wildfire, many bushes began to resprout from their roots.
If you cut back the plant in spring, it will quickly resprout new leaves.
Some trees can resprout even after severe storm damage.
