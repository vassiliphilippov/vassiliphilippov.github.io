cinch

verb
to secure or ensure achievement of
7.7
Pronunciation: / sɪntʃ / (AmE), / sɪntʃ / (BrE)
to secure or guarantee something, especially to make certain of winning or achieving something

Examples

To cinch a win
A strong performance in the final round could cinch a victory for her team.
After weeks of hard work, they finally cinched the contract with the client.
She managed to cinch the promotion by impressing her supervisors during their meeting.
A last-minute goal would cinch a victory for the team.
He studied extra hard to cinch a passing grade.
She hoped to cinch the deal with one final proposal.
