ill-treatment

noun
cruel or inhumane treatment
6.4 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªlËˆtritmÉ™nt / (AmE), / ÉªlËˆtriËtmÉ™nt / (BrE)
cruel or inhumane treatment of a person or animal, especially over a period of time.

Examples

Reports of ill-treatment
Reports of ill-treatment in the institution led to a public outcry.
She spoke out against the ill-treatment of animals in entertainment.
The investigation exposed years of ill-treatment suffered by workers at the factory.

Synonyms

  • maltreatment
    7.0 0.87/m
    maltreatment describes cruel, harmful treatment that causes physical or emotional harm, while ill-treatment emphasizes long-term or repeated cruelty.
  • inhuman treatment
    8.3 0.03/m
    inhuman treatment means deliberate acts that cause great pain or suffering, while ill-treatment is any cruel treatment happening over a period.
  • abuse
    3.2 44/m
    abuse is violent or repeated harmful treatment, while ill-treatment highlights cruel or inhumane treatment possibly happening over time.
  • mistreatment
    4.0 1.71/m
    mistreatment is treating someone badly or unfairly in action or practice, while ill-treatment stresses cruel or inhumane behaviour over time.
  • cruelty
    3.2 7.64/m
    cruelty is the quality or act of causing pain on purpose, while ill-treatment refers to cruel treatment carried out over a period.
  • inhumanity
    6.6 0.76/m
    inhumanity is cruel behaviour or a very cruel act showing lack of compassion, while ill-treatment names ongoing cruel treatment.
  • ill-usage
    9.9 0.00/m
    ill-usage means harsh or abusive conduct and sounds old-fashioned, while ill-treatment is the common modern term for cruel treatment.
