fall on a grenade

verb
make a personal sacrifice for others
Pronunciation: / fɔl ɑn ə ɡrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒn ə ɡrəˈneɪd / (BrE)
to make a significant personal sacrifice, often accepting blame or risk, for the benefit or protection of others

Examples

To fall on a grenade
He didn't hesitate to fall on a grenade so his colleagues wouldn't get in trouble.
Sarah decided to fall on a grenade by volunteering for the toughest assignment.
Sometimes a leader must fall on a grenade to protect the rest of the group from criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: take one for the team - to accept an unpleasant task or responsibility for a group's benefit
