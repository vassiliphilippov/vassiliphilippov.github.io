sacrifice

noun
act of accepting a loss when giving up something
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪfaɪs / (BrE)
the act of giving up something valued or selling it at less than its worth, resulting in a loss

Examples

A sacrifice sale
Selling his house for such a low price was a major sacrifice.
She regarded moving to a smaller apartment as a necessary sacrifice after losing her job.
The company made a sacrifice by accepting a smaller profit to keep its employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loss - the state of no longer having something or as much of something; damage resulting from giving up something
