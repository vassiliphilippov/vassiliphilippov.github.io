multilocus

adjective
involving multiple loci
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈloʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈləʊkəs / (BrE)
relating to or involving more than one genetic locus

Examples

Multilocus genetic study
The scientists used a multilocus approach to better understand the genetic structure of the population.
A multilocus analysis can reveal more about hereditary diseases than studying a single gene.
Researchers compared multilocus data from different species to investigate evolutionary relationships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polygenic - involving many genes
  • antonym: single-locus - involving only one genetic locus
