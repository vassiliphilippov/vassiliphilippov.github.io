hemimetabolic

adjective
undergoing incomplete insect metamorphosis
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmɪməˈtæbəlɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪməˈtæbəlɪk / (BrE)
(of certain insects) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, where the young do not resemble the adult and develop through a series of molts.

Examples

Hemimetabolic insects
Dragonflies are hemimetabolic insects, developing from aquatic nymphs to winged adults without a pupal stage.
Mayflies exhibit a hemimetabolic life cycle distinct from butterflies, which are holometabolic.
The hemimetabolic development of grasshoppers lacks the dramatic transformation seen in moths.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holometabolic - (of insects) undergoing complete metamorphosis, with larvae and adults looking very different
