melodramatize

verb
to exaggerate drama
7.7
Pronunciation: /mel.ə.ˈdræ.mə.taɪz/ /; / /mə.lə.ˈdræ.mə.taɪz/ (AmE), /mel.ə.ˈdrɑː.mə.taɪz/ /; / /mə.lə.ˈdrɑː.mə.taɪz/ (BrE)
to present or treat something in an exaggerated, overly dramatic way, as if it were a melodrama

Examples

To melodramatize a story
The director decided to melodramatize the true story to make it more appealing to audiences.
Some news outlets tend to melodramatize events to attract more viewers.
She tried not to melodramatize the situation, but her emotions got the better of her.
He tends to melodramatize every minor inconvenience as if it were a disaster.
The director chose to melodramatize the story, making the emotions seem much more intense.
Try not to melodramatize the situation; it is not as serious as it appears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it really is
  • antonym: understate - to present something as less important or serious than it really is
