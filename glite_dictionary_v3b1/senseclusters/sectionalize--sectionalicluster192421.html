sectionalize

verb
divide into sections, often geographically
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛk.ʃə.nəˌlaɪz /; / ˈsɛkʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ʃə.nə.laɪz /; / ˈsɛkʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to divide something into sections, especially along geographic or administrative lines

Examples

To sectionalize a country
Attempts to sectionalize the country often lead to regional rivalries.
He argued that we should not sectionalize our society by drawing artificial boundaries.
The new law will sectionalize the city into distinct administrative zones.
Some historians argue that colonial borders served to sectionalise communities that had once lived together.
The company chose to sectionalise its market strategy by region to better address local needs.
The government decided to sectionalise the territory for administrative purposes.

  • antonym: unify - make a whole; bring together into a single unit
