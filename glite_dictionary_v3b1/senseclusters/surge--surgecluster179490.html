surge

noun
a large sea wave or swell
4.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /sɜːrdʒ/ (AmE), /sɜːdʒ/ (BrE)
a sudden and powerful forward or upward movement or flow, especially of water, a crowd, or emotion

Examples

A surge of water
A powerful surge of water flooded the low-lying villages after the storm.
Fishermen were warned of the dangerous surge approaching the coast.
The sudden surge crashed over the seawall, drenching everyone nearby.
A surge of excitement swept through the crowd as the band appeared.
Hospitals experienced a surge in patients during the flu season.
There was a sudden surge of water after the dam broke.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ebb - the movement of the tide out to sea; a reduction of a flow
  • synonym: rush - a sudden fast movement, especially of liquid or crowds
  • synonym: wave - a moving ridge on the surface of water, especially the sea
