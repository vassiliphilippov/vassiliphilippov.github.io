shun

verb
to deliberately avoid or exclude
3.3 3.7
Pronunciation: /ʃʌn/ (AmE), /ʃʌn/ (BrE)
to deliberately avoid or reject someone or something

Examples

To shun a person
After the scandal, the community began to shun him completely.
Members who break the rules may be shunned by the entire group.
She felt hurt when her colleagues started to shun her at work.
After the scandal, many former friends began to shun him in public.
She tried to shun publicity and preferred to live a quiet life.
Vegetarians often shun dishes that contain any form of meat or animal by-products.
