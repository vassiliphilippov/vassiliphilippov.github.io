inrush

noun
a sudden inflow or influx
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.rʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden inflow or influx, especially of liquid, people, or other substances

Examples

An inrush of water
He felt an inrush of energy after hearing the good news.
The sudden inrush of water flooded the lower levels of the building.
There was an inrush of customers as soon as the store opened.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influx - an arrival or entry of large numbers of people or things
