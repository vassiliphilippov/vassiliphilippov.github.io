order Cypriniformes

noun
an order of freshwater fishes (carps, loaches, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ saɪˌprɪnɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə saɪˌprɪnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of freshwater fishes that includes carps, minnows, loaches, and suckers, sometimes considered a suborder of Ostariophysi.

Examples

Order Cypriniformes fishes
Biologists study the diversity and distribution of the order Cypriniformes across Asian rivers.
Loaches, which belong to the order Cypriniformes, are popular in the aquarium trade.
The order Cypriniformes contains many familiar freshwater fish, including carp and goldfish.
