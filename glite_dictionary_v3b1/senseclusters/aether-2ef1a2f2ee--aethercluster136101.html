Aether

proper noun
Greek mythological personification of upper sky/air
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.θər/ /; / /ˈeɪ.θər/ (AmE), /ˈiː.θə/ /; / /ˈeɪ.θə/ (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the personification of the upper sky or pure upper air, often considered a deity; son of Erebus and Night or of Chaos and Darkness.

Examples

Aether in mythology
According to some ancient myths, Aether was born from Erebus and Night.
In Greek mythology, Aether represented the pure upper air that the gods breathed.
Many poets describe Aether as the bright, shining light of the heavens.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Erebus - personification of darkness in Greek mythology, father of Aether
  • degree lower: Nyx - personification of night in Greek mythology, mother of Aether
