tract housing

noun
rows of similar houses built together
Pronunciation: / trækt ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / trækt ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of residential development in which similar or identical houses are built together on a large area of land, often as a subdivision

Examples

Tract housing development
After World War II, tract housing became popular in growing suburbs across the United States.
Many families moved into the new tract housing development on the edge of town.
The uniform appearance of tract housing can make neighborhoods look monotonous.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subdivision - an area of land divided into plots for homes, often with similar houses
