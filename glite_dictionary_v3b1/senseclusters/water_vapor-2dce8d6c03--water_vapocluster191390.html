Examples

Water vapor in air
Clouds form when water vapor in the atmosphere condenses into tiny droplets.
Cold air holds less water vapor than warm air, which is why it feels drier in winter.
The greenhouse effect is partially caused by water vapor trapping heat in the Earth's atmosphere.
Clouds form when water vapour in the atmosphere cools and condenses into droplets.
In hot weather, the water vapour in the air increases, making it feel humid.
