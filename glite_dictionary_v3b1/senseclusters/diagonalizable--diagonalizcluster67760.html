diagonalizable

adjective
able to be transformed into a diagonal matrix
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæɡənəˌlaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / daɪˈaɡənəˌlaɪzəbəl / (BrE)
(of a matrix) capable of being transformed into a diagonal matrix by a similarity transformation.

Examples

A diagonalizable matrix
A diagonalizable matrix simplifies computations in linear algebra.
Not every square matrix is diagonalizable over the real numbers.
The matrix is diagonalizable if there exists a basis of eigenvectors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-diagonalizable - (of a matrix) not capable of being transformed into a diagonal matrix
