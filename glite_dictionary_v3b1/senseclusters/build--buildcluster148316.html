Synonyms

  • construct
    3.5 9/m
    construct is a formal word for making large things like bridges or houses, while build is more general and common.
  • make
    1.0 1100/m
    make is a broad everyday verb for creating things in many ways, while build focuses on putting parts together.
  • put together
    2.8 50/m
    put together means joining parts to form something often small, while build often refers to constructing larger objects or buildings.
  • assemble
    2.9 8/m
    assemble means fitting parts to form a device or group, while build covers creating whole objects or buildings.
  • put up
    3.0 3/m
    put up means to erect or place something quickly, often a temporary structure, while build suggests longer planned construction.
  • raise
    3.7 2/m
    raise means to lift up or erect a structure over time, often for buildings, while build covers the full making process.
  • fabricate
    5.5 1/m
    fabricate usually means to manufacture parts or goods in a workshop or factory, while build is more general and means creating things.
  • frame
    3.2 2/m
    frame means to make the basic supporting skeleton of something, while build means to finish the whole object or building.
