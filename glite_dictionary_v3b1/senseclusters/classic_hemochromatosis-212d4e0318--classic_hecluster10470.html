classic hemochromatosis

noun
inherited iron overload disorder
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪk ˌhiːmoʊˌkroʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈklasɪk ˌhiːməʊˌkrəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an inherited disorder causing excessive absorption and accumulation of iron in the body, often leading to organ damage

Examples

Classic hemochromatosis disorder
Regular phlebotomy is the standard treatment for patients with classic hemochromatosis to remove excess iron.
The doctor diagnosed him with classic hemochromatosis after a series of blood tests and genetic screening.
Untreated classic hemochromatosis can lead to serious complications such as liver cirrhosis and diabetes.
