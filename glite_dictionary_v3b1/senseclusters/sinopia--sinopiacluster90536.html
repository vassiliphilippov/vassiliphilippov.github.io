sinopia

noun
red ocher pigment
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnoʊpiə / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊpiə / (BrE)
A reddish-brown natural earth pigment, consisting of red ocher, formerly used for underdrawings and frescoes.

Examples

Red sinopia pigment
Ancient murals often reveal traces of sinopia beneath the surface layer.
Restorers carefully examined the sinopia to understand the original composition.
The artist sketched the fresco's outlines in sinopia before applying the final colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: red ocher - a natural earth pigment containing iron oxide, giving a reddish hue
