reconnoitering

noun
exploring to gather information (military)
8.5 7.4
Pronunciation: /re.kə.ˈnɔɪ.tər.ɪŋ/ /; / /rə.kə.ˈnɔɪ.tər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /re.kə.ˈnɔɪ.tə.rɪŋ/ /; / /re.kə.ˈnɔɪ.trɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of exploring or surveying an area, especially for military or strategic purposes, to gather information

Examples

To reconnoiter enemy positions
Reconnoitering enemy positions required caution and stealth in the dense forest.
Extensive reconnoitering helped the team map out a safe route through hostile territory.
The platoon was tasked with reconnoitering the area before the main force advanced.
Accurate maps were produced after days of reconnoitring the surrounding hills.
The soldiers spent hours reconnoitring the forest before moving into enemy territory.
Their mission involved reconnoitring the coastline for any signs of hostile activity.
