metacarpus

noun
bones between wrist and fingers
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
the group of five bones in the hand situated between the wrist (carpus) and the fingers (phalanges)

Examples

The metacarpus bones
A fracture in the metacarpus can significantly impact hand movement.
The metacarpus connects the wrist bones to the finger bones.
The surgeon examined the metacarpus to determine the extent of the injury.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: carpus - the cluster of bones forming the wrist
  • degree higher: phalanges - the bones in the fingers and toes
