glazing

noun
window glass layer
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgleɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgleɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
glass or similar material fitted into windows or doors, or a transparent coating applied for protection or decoration

Examples

Window glazing
The architect recommended triple glazing to improve the building's energy efficiency.
After the storm, several glazings in the old house needed to be replaced.
Modern glazing can help reduce outside noise in busy city areas.
The new windows feature double glazing for better insulation.
The artist applied a thin glazing to give the painting a glossy finish.
Modern buildings often use special glazing to reduce heat loss and noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coating - a thin layer covering a surface
  • synonym: glass - a hard, transparent material used for windows and bottles
  • synonym: pane - a single sheet of glass in a window or door
  • synonym: window - an opening in a wall fitted with glass to let in light or air
