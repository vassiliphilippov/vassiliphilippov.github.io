pacu

noun
South American fish
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɑːˈkuː / (AmE), / pɑːˈkuː / (BrE)
a South American freshwater fish related to the piranha, known for its flat, human-like teeth used for eating plants and fruit

Examples

The pacu fish
The pacu is often found in rivers and lakes throughout the Amazon basin.
Unlike the piranha, the pacu mainly eats fruit and nuts that fall into the water.
Aquarium enthusiasts sometimes keep a pacu because of its unique teeth and peaceful nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: piranha - a South American freshwater fish known for its sharp teeth and carnivorous diet
