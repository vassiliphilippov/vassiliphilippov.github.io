irony

noun
incongruity between expectation and reality
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrəni / (AmE), / ˈaɪərəni /; / ˈaɪrəni / (BrE)
a situation where what actually happens is the opposite of what you expected, often in a surprising or humorous way

Examples

A cruel irony
It was a cruel irony that the fire station itself burned down.
The irony of the situation was lost on no one in the audience.
There is an irony in how the fastest runner tripped at the starting line.
He smiled at the irony that his safety training caused him to break his arm.
The irony of her quitting right after being promoted was not lost on anyone.
There is a certain irony in calling a huge man 'Tiny'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
