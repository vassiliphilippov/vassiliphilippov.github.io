warren

noun
overcrowded residential area
9.3 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔːr.ən/ /; / /ˈwɑːr.ən/ (AmE), /ˈwɒr.ən/ (BrE)
An area or building that is crowded with many people living closely together, often in poor or cramped conditions.

Examples

A crowded warren
During the industrial revolution, many workers lived in dark, unhealthy warrens near the factories.
The city's old quarter is a labyrinth of narrow streets and warrens packed with families.
The refugees were forced to stay in makeshift warrens, with little space or privacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slum - a squalid and overcrowded urban street or district inhabited by very poor people
