prebiotic

noun
substance for gut bacteria
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːbaɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriːbaɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
a substance, typically a type of fiber, that promotes the growth of beneficial bacteria in the gut

Examples

A prebiotic supplement
Many nutritionists recommend adding prebiotic foods like garlic and onions to your diet.
A daily supplement containing prebiotic fiber can help maintain a healthy digestive system.
Yogurt often contains both prebiotic and probiotic ingredients for better gut health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antibiotic - a medicine that destroys or inhibits the growth of bacteria
  • synonym: probiotic - live bacteria that are beneficial for health, especially the digestive system
