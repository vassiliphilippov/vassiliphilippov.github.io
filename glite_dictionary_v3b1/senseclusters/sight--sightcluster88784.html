sight

noun
the act of seeing or observing
Pronunciation: / saɪt / (AmE), / saɪt / (BrE)
the ability, act, or experience of seeing; something that is seen, especially something notable or impressive

Examples

A sight to see
After hours in the forest, finally the hikers were treated to the sight they had hoped for.
He caught his first sight of the ocean from the hilltop.
The rare bird was gone before we could get a sight of it.
At the carnival, every sight and sound seemed new and exciting.
The children were amazed by the sight of the snow-covered mountains.
The parade was a sight to behold, with its vibrant costumes and floats.
It was a beautiful sight to see the mountains covered in snow.
The children laughed at the strange sight of the parade elephants marching down Main Street.
Tourists gathered to take photos of the famous sight during their city walk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blindness - the condition of lacking visual perception
  • synonym: glance - a quick look or act of seeing briefly
  • synonym: scene - a place, event, or incident viewed by a person
  • antonym: sightless - without the ability to see; blind
  • antonym: sound - something that is heard, as opposed to something seen
  • synonym: spectacle - a visually striking performance or display
  • synonym: view - the ability to see something or the act of seeing
  • synonym: vision - the faculty or state of being able to see
