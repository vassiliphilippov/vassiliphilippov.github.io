transcendental

noun
abstract concept
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænˌsɛnˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌtrænˌsɛnˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
a concept or quality that goes beyond ordinary experience or understanding, especially in philosophy or mathematics

Examples

A transcendental number
In mathematics, a transcendental cannot be expressed as the root of any algebraic equation.
Philosophers have long discussed the meaning of a transcendental in understanding reality.
The concept of infinity is often considered a transcendental in both philosophy and science.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concrete - existing in a material or physical form; not abstract
  • synonym: ideal - a perfect or most suitable example of something
