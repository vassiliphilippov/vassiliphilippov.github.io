sustainability

noun
ability to be maintained; endurance
1.5 3.6
Pronunciation: /sə.steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /sə.steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /sə.steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /sə.steɪ.nə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability to be maintained at a certain rate or level; the capacity to endure or continue without depleting resources or causing harm, especially in environmental, economic, or social contexts

Examples

Environmental sustainability
Investors are increasingly concerned with the sustainability of businesses they support.
Many cities are developing policies to ensure long-term sustainability in energy and water usage.
The company is committed to improving the sustainability of its manufacturing processes.
