corrosion

noun
gradual destruction by chemical action
3.2 4.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈroʊ.ʒən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrəʊ.ʒən/ (BrE)
the gradual destruction or deterioration of a material, especially metal, by chemical action (such as exposure to air or moisture)

Examples

Corrosion of metal
Engineers inspected the bridge for signs of corrosion before approving its use.
Preventing corrosion is essential to maintaining the safety of metal structures.
The steel beams were weakened by corrosion after years of exposure to saltwater.
Regular maintenance prevents corrosion from damaging industrial machinery.
The bridge was closed due to severe corrosion on its metal supports.
The salty air accelerates the corrosion of vehicles near the coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erosion - the process by which something is worn away
  • synonym: oxidation - the process of oxidizing, especially causing loss of electrons in materials
  • synonym: rust - reddish-brown coating formed by the oxidation of iron
