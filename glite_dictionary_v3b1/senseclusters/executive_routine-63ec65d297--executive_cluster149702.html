executive routine

noun
managing program coordinating subroutines
9.2 8.1
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛk.jə.tɪv ruˈtin / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛk.jʊ.tɪv ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a computer program or subroutine that manages and coordinates the execution of other routines or subroutines within a system.

Examples

The executive routine manages tasks
Debugging often involves checking how the executive routine coordinates calls between tasks.
Every time a subroutine completes, the executive routine determines which subroutine will run next.
The executive routine oversees the flow of control between various user processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scheduler - a program that determines the order in which tasks are executed
