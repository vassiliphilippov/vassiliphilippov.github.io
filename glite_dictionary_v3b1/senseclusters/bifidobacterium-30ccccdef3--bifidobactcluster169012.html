Bifidobacterium

proper noun
intestinal bacteria genus
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪfɪdoʊbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌbɪfɪdəʊbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a genus of beneficial bacteria commonly found in the intestines of humans and animals, often used in probiotics

Examples

Bifidobacterium genus
Many yogurts contain Bifidobacterium to promote digestive health.
Scientists study Bifidobacterium to understand its role in the human gut microbiome.
Some probiotic supplements list Bifidobacterium as a key ingredient for supporting digestion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Escherichia coli - a genus of bacteria, some strains of which can cause illness
  • synonym: Lactobacillus - a genus of beneficial bacteria often found in the digestive system
