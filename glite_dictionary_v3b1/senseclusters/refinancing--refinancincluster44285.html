refinancing

noun
replacing a loan
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfaɪnænsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfaɪnɑːnsɪŋ /; / ˌriːˈfaɪnænsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of replacing an existing loan with a new loan, usually to get better terms or lower payments

Examples

Loan refinancing
The company secured a $100 million refinancing to replace its maturing bonds.
Homeowners often consider refinancing when interest rates drop to save money on their mortgage.
After years of high payments, she finally completed the refinancing of her student loans.
After losing his job, he looked into refinancing his mortgage to reduce his monthly payments.
The company announced a major refinancing to manage its growing debt more effectively.
Many students consider refinancing their loans when interest rates drop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loan modification - a change to the terms of an existing loan
  • synonym: repayment - the act of paying back money borrowed
  • synonym: restructuring - the process of changing the structure or terms of a loan or debt
