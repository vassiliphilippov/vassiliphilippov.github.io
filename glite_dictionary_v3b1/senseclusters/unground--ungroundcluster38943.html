unground

verb
to remove grounding
8.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to disconnect an electrical device or conductor from its grounding (earth) connection

Examples

To unground a device
The technician needed to unground the equipment before performing maintenance.
Always unground the circuit carefully to avoid electrical hazards.
After repairs, the engineer made sure not to unground any essential safety connections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ground - to connect an electrical device to the earth for safety
