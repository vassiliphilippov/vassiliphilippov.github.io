skeptic

noun
person who doubts accepted beliefs
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪk / (BrE)
a person who habitually doubts or questions accepted beliefs or claims

Examples

A skeptic questions claims
As a skeptic, she questioned the validity of every claim made in the debate.
Many skeptics remain unconvinced about the effectiveness of the treatment.
The skeptic in the group demanded solid evidence before accepting the new theory.
As a lifelong sceptic, she questioned every claim made by the politicians.
The conference attracted both supporters and sceptics of the new technology.
While many accepted the story at face value, several sceptics demanded more evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: believer - a person who accepts a particular set of beliefs unquestioningly
  • synonym: doubter - a person who doubts or lacks faith
  • synonym: skeptic - Alternative (especially American) spelling of 'sceptic', meaning a person who doubts accepted beliefs.
