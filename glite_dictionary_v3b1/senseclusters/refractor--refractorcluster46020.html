refractor

noun
light-bending material
6.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktər / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktə / (BrE)
a material or device that bends light as it passes through, changing its direction

Examples

Glass refractor
The scientist used a refractor to study how light changes direction in different materials.
A glass prism acts as a refractor, splitting white light into a rainbow of colors.
Modern telescopes often use a refractor to focus distant starlight for observation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lens - a curved piece of glass or plastic that bends light
  • antonym: reflector - a device that reflects light instead of bending it
