licorice

noun
plant with sweet, edible roots
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkərɪʃ /; / ˈlɪkərɪs / (BrE)
A deep-rooted plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe and Asia, with pinnately compound leaves and blue flowers, cultivated for its long, sweet roots used in confectionery and medicine.

Examples

Licorice plant
Blue flowers bloom on the licorice in early summer.
Farmers in southern Europe cultivate licorice for commercial purposes.
The licorice plant is grown for its sweet root, often used in candies and herbal teas.
Liquorice is often used to make traditional sweets and candies enjoyed around the world.
Some herbal teas contain liquorice root for its natural sweetness and medicinal qualities.
The liquorice plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils of the Mediterranean region.
