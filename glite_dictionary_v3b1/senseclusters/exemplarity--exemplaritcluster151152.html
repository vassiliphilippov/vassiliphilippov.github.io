exemplarity

noun
quality as a warning
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˌzɛmpˈlærəti / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɛmpˈlærəti / (BrE)
the quality of serving as a warning or model to others, especially by making an example of someone or something

Examples

Exemplarity in punishment
The judge's decision was marked by a level of exemplarity meant to discourage similar crimes.
Some critics argued that the punishment's exemplarity was excessive and unfair.
The principal emphasized the exemplarity of the suspension to prevent future rule-breaking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deterrence - the act of discouraging an action through fear of consequences
  • antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected
