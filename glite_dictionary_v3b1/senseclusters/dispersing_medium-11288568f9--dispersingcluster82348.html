dispersing medium

noun
substance in which another is colloidally dispersed
5.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɜrsɪŋ ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsɪŋ ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A substance in which another substance is colloidally dispersed, especially in the context of colloids.

Examples

The dispersing medium in smoke
In smoke, air serves as the dispersing medium for fine solid particles.
The choice of dispersing medium affects the properties and stability of the resulting colloidal system.
Water is a common dispersing medium in many everyday colloids, such as milk or paint.
