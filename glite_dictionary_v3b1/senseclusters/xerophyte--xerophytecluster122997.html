xerophyte

noun
plant adapted to dry conditions
Pronunciation: / ˈzɪrəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛərəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
a plant adapted to survive in environments with very little water, such as deserts

Examples

A xerophyte plant
Botanists study how xerophytes manage to survive where most plants cannot.
Many xerophytes have thick leaves to store water during dry periods.
The cactus is a classic example of a xerophyte, thriving in arid desert regions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrophyte - plant adapted to living in water
  • antonym: mesophyte - plant adapted to environments with a moderate supply of water
