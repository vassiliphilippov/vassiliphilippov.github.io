neuropod

noun
invertebrate appendage
Pronunciation: / ˈnʊrəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a limb or appendage associated with the nervous system in certain invertebrate animals

Examples

Worm neuropod
The marine worm uses its neuropods to crawl along the ocean floor.
Scientists observed that damage to the neuropod affected the animal's movement.
Each segment of the worm's body has a pair of neuropods for locomotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parapodium - a paired lateral appendage in some invertebrates
