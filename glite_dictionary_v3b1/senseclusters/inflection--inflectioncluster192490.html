inflection

noun
change in a word's form for grammar
8.4 5.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈflek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈflek.ʃən/ (BrE)
a change in the form of a word, typically by adding a suffix, to indicate a change in grammatical function such as tense, number, or case

Examples

Inflection of verbs
In Latin, the inflection of verbs provides information about tense and person.
The English language uses fewer inflections than many other languages.
The change from "walk" to "walked" is an example of verb inflection.
English verbs undergo inflexion to indicate tense, such as adding '-ed' for the past.
In Latin, noun inflexion shows the grammatical case and number.
She studied the patterns of inflexion in ancient Greek language texts.
