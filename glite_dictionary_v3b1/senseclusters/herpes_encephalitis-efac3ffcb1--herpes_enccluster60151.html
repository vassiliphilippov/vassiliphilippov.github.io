herpes encephalitis

noun
brain inflammation caused by herpes simplex virus
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrpiːz ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɜːpiːz ɛnˌsɛf əˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
An acute viral inflammation of the brain, usually caused by herpes simplex virus type 1, often affecting the temporal and frontal lobes.

Examples

Herpes encephalitis brain infection
MRI scans revealed lesions in the temporal lobe typical of herpes encephalitis.
Prompt treatment is crucial when herpes encephalitis is suspected, to prevent severe brain damage.
The patient was diagnosed with herpes encephalitis after showing sudden confusion and fever.
