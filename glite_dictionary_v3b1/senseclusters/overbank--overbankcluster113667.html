overbank

noun
floodplain sediment deposit
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌbæŋk / (BrE)
a deposit of fine sediment left on a river's floodplain after the river overflows its banks

Examples

River overbank deposit
The geologist collected samples from the overbank to analyze the history of flooding in the area.
After the heavy rains, a thick overbank covered the fields near the river.
Farmers noticed that crops grew better in the rich soil of the overbank region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - sediment deposited by flowing water, especially in a riverbed or floodplain
  • antonym: levee - a natural or artificial embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
