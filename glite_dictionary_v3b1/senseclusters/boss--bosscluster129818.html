boss

noun
person in charge; employer or manager
Pronunciation: / bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
a person who is in charge of a group, organization, or workers; someone with authority over others

Examples

My boss at work
After the interview, the boss called her to offer her the job.
The boss decided to expand the team by hiring two new designers.
You should ask your boss for permission before taking any time off.
My new boss is very supportive and encourages us to share ideas.
The boss called an early morning meeting to discuss the new project.
You should talk to your boss before taking time off from work.
After the boss retired, the party struggled to find a new unifying figure.
Many citizens fought against the corruption of the party boss and his allies.
The city was once dominated by a single political boss who decided every major appointment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: employee - a person employed for wages or salary, especially at non-executive level
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads a group or organization
  • synonym: manager - a person who is responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of others; not a boss
  • synonym: supervisor - a person who supervises others at work
