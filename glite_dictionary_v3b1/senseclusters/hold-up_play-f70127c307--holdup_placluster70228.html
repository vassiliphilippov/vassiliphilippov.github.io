hold-up play

noun
tactic to delay or mislead in sports
4.2 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊld.ʌp pleɪ/ /; / /ˈhoʊld.əp pleɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊld.ʌp pleɪ/ /; / /ˈhəʊld.əp pleɪ/ (BrE)
a tactic in team sports, especially football, where a player deliberately delays play to allow teammates to move into better positions or to mislead opponents

Examples

Strong hold-up play
Coaches often praise players with strong hold-up play for their tactical awareness.
She used clever hold-up play to maintain possession and slow down the opposition.
The striker's hold-up play gave his teammates time to join the attack.
