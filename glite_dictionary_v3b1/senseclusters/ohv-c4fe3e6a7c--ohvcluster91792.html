OHV

adjective
overhead-valve engine
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ ˌeɪtʃ ˈvi / (AmE), / ˌəʊ ˌeɪtʃ ˈviː / (BrE)
relating to an engine or engine part that uses an overhead-valve design, where the valves are located above the combustion chamber

Examples

OHV engine design
The mechanic recommended an OHV engine for its durability and simple maintenance.
Classic cars often feature OHV designs instead of more modern engine types.
Many small trucks use OHV engines because they provide good low-end torque.

Related Terms

  • antonym: OHC - relating to an engine with overhead camshaft design
