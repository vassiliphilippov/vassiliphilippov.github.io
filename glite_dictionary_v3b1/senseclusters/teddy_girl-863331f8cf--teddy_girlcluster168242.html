Teddy girl

noun
female youth in 1950s-60s Britain adopting Edwardian style
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛdi ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈtɛdi ɡɜːl / (BrE)
A young woman, especially in the 1950s and 1960s in Britain, who adopted a style based on Edwardian fashion and was often associated with youth subculture and rebellious behavior.

Examples

Teddy girl style
As a teenager, she proudly dressed as a Teddy girl and frequented the local dance halls.
Photographs from the era show groups of Teddy girls gathered on London streets.
The Teddy girl movement challenged traditional gender roles with its bold fashion and attitude.
