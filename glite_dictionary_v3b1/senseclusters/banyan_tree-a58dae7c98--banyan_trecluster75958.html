banyan tree

noun
East Indian fig tree with aerial roots
Pronunciation: / ˈbæn.jən triː / (AmE), / ˈbæn.jən triː / (BrE)
an East Indian fig tree (Ficus benghalensis) whose spreading branches produce aerial roots that grow down into the ground to form new trunks.

Examples

The banyan tree roots
Children enjoy playing under the massive canopy of the old banyan tree.
Legend says a great banyan tree grew at the village center for centuries.
The banyan tree spread its roots wide across the temple courtyard.
