undomesticable

adjective
cannot be tamed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndoʊˈmɛstɪkəbl / (AmE), / ˌʌndəʊˈmɛstɪkəbl / (BrE)
not able to be tamed or adapted to live with humans

Examples

An undomesticable animal
Some wild animals are considered undomesticable despite repeated attempts.
The zookeeper explained that wolves are generally undomesticable compared to dogs.
Scientists found that certain species remain undomesticable even after generations of captivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: domesticable - able to be tamed or adapted for living with humans
  • synonym: wild - living in a natural state, not tamed
