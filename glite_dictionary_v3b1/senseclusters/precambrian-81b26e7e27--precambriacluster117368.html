Precambrian

noun
early geological eon before the Phanerozoic
9.9 9.0
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈkæm.briː.ən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈkæm.briː.ən/ (AmE), /priː.ˈkæm.brɪ.ən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈkæm.brɪ.ən/ (BrE)
The vast span of geological time from about 3,800 million to 544 million years ago, preceding the Phanerozoic eon.

Examples

The Precambrian era
Fossils from the Precambrian are rare because most life forms were microscopic.
Many of Earth's oldest rocks date back to the Precambrian.
Scientists study the Precambrian to understand the origins of life and the planet's early history.
