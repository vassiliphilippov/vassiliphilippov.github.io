Aegilops triuncalis

noun
a species of barbed goatgrass
Pronunciation: / ˌiːdʒɪˌlɒps traɪˈʌŋkəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌiːdʒɪˌlɒps traɪˈʌŋkælɪs / (BrE)
A species of European grass, known as barbed goatgrass, that has become a weed in North America; its sharp-pointed seeds can injure livestock when eaten.

Examples

Aegilops triuncalis grass species
Aegilops triuncalis has become an invasive weed in parts of California's grasslands.
Efforts to control Aegilops triuncalis focus on preventing its sharp-pointed seeds from contaminating pastures.
Farmers are concerned about the spread of Aegilops triuncalis because its seeds can harm sheep and cattle.
