sweeping

adjective
covering a wide area or range
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈswipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
covering or affecting a wide area or many things; very general or extensive

Examples

Sweeping changes
His sweeping generalizations often miss important details.
She made a sweeping gesture that took in the whole room.
The new law is expected to have sweeping effects on the entire industry.
Avoid making sweeping statements that ignore important differences between groups.
Many critics dismissed his theory as a sweeping generalization lacking nuance.
The government announced sweeping reforms that would impact all sectors of society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broad - covering a large area or scope
  • synonym: general - not specific; including or affecting all or most people, things, or places
  • antonym: narrow - covering a small area or limited range
  • antonym: specific - clearly defined or identified; precise; opposite of general
