prose

noun
ordinary or dull expression
Pronunciation: / proʊz / (AmE), / prəʊz / (BrE)
writing or speech that is ordinary, unremarkable, or lacking in poetic or imaginative qualities; commonplace or dull expression.

Examples

Dull prose
His speech was nothing but prose, lacking any inspiration or excitement.
Some critics dismissed the novel’s style as mere prose, finding it unimaginative.
The meeting’s minutes were recorded in a dry, factual prose.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poetry - writing that expresses ideas in expressive, imaginative, or rhythmic language
