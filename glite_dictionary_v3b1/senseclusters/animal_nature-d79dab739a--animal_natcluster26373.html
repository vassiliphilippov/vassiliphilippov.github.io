animal nature

noun
instinctive or physical side of a person
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪməl ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
the instinctive, physical, or sensual aspects of a person, as distinct from the intellect or spirit

Examples

His animal nature
Despite his calm demeanor, his animal nature showed when he acted impulsively.
In moments of anger, his animal nature sometimes overpowers his better judgment.
The philosopher argued that our animal nature is inseparable from our higher reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spiritual nature - the spiritual or intellectual aspects of a person
