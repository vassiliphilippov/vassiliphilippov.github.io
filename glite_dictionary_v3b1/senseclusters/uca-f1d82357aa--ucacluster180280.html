Uca

noun
genus of fiddler crabs
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈjuː.kə/ /; / /ˈuː.kə/ (AmE), /ˈjuː.kə/ /; / /ˈuː.kə/ (BrE)
A genus of small, mainly tropical crabs commonly known as fiddler crabs, characterized by the extreme size difference between their two front claws in males.

Examples

Uca fiddler crabs

The genus Uca includes many species of fiddler crabs found in mangrove swamps.
Tidal changes greatly influence the feeding patterns of Uca crabs.
