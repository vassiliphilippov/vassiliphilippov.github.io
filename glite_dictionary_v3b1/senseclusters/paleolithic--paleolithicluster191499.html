paleolithic

adjective
relating to the early Stone Age
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊˈlɪθɪk /; / ˌpeɪ.li.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊˈlɪθɪk /; / ˌpæl.i.əʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the earliest period of the Stone Age, characterized by the use of stone tools

Examples

Paleolithic stone tools
Archaeologists found paleolithic tools at the excavation site.
Many paleolithic cave paintings have been discovered in France and Spain.
The paleolithic diet is based on foods eaten by early humans during the Stone Age.
Archaeologists discovered palaeolithic tools buried in the cave.
The museum features a remarkable collection of palaeolithic artifacts.
These palaeolithic paintings reveal the early development of artistic expression.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: mesolithic - relating to the middle period of the Stone Age
  • degree higher: neolithic - relating to the last period of the Stone Age, when ground or polished stone weapons and implements prevailed
