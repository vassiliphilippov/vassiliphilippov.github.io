unstabilized

adjective
not made stable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪbɪlaɪzd / (BrE)
not made stable or not treated to prevent change, breakdown, or deterioration

Examples

An unstabilized chemical
The unstabilized chemical must be handled with care in the laboratory.
Photographs printed on unstabilized paper may fade more quickly over time.
Engineers warned that the unstabilized structure could collapse during strong winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stable - firmly fixed or not likely to change or fail
  • synonym: unstable - likely to change, fall, or break down; not stable
