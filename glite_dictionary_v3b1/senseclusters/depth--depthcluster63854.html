depth

noun
seriousness or profundity of thought
Pronunciation: / dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
the quality of having serious thought, insight, or complexity (intellectual or emotional profundity)

Examples

Depth of thought
Despite his youth, he demonstrated remarkable depth in his analysis of complex issues.
Her depth of understanding impressed the entire panel of judges.
The novel is praised for the depth of its philosophical insights.
Her writing shows remarkable depth and insight into human nature.
The philosopher was admired for the depth of his understanding.
There is little depth to his arguments, making them easy to challenge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: profundity - the quality of being profound; intellectual depth
  • antonym: shallowness - lack of intellectual or emotional depth
  • antonym: superficiality - lack of thoroughness or depth in thought
