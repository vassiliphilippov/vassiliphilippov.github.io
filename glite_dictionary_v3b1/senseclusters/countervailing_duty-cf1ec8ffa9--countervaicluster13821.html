countervailing duty

noun
import tax to offset foreign subsidies
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊn.tɚˈveɪ.lɪŋ ˈduː.ti / (AmE), / ˌkaʊn.təˈveɪ.lɪŋ ˈdjuː.ti / (BrE)
A tax imposed on imported goods to counteract subsidies provided to producers in the exporting country.

Examples

A countervailing duty tax
A countervailing duty may be applied when foreign exporters receive unfair state subsidies.
The World Trade Organization regulates the use of countervailing duties among its member countries.
The government imposed a countervailing duty on steel imports to protect domestic manufacturers.
