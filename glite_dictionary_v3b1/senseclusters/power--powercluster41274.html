power

noun
ability to control or influence
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊɚ /; / ˈpaʊər /; / ˈpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə /; / ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
the ability or right to control, influence, or direct people or events (including political, governmental, or supernatural authority, or a powerful country)

Examples

Political power
His power to inspire people was evident in every speech he gave.
She has enormous power within the organization and can influence every major decision.
The new law gave the government greater power over the financial sector.
She felt a benevolent power watching over her during difficult times.
Some stories speak of invisible powers guiding human destiny.
They believed an evil power controlled the fate of the village.
Britain was the leading colonial power in the nineteenth century.
Emerging economies are challenging established powers on the world stage.
The United States is considered a global power due to its military and economic influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders, make decisions
  • synonym: force - a power or influence, often with coercive strength
  • synonym: influence - the capacity to have an effect on the character or behavior of someone or something
  • degree lower: minor power - a state with less international influence
  • antonym: subordination - the state of being under the authority or control of another
  • degree higher: superpower - a very powerful and influential nation
  • antonym: weakness - lack of power or strength
