shift

to move or change something or someone from one place, position, state, or focus to another (physically or abstractly)

Examples

To shift position
During the meeting, the manager shifted his chair closer to the table for a better view.
He shifted the books from one shelf to another to make more space.
She quickly shifted her gaze from the window to her phone when he entered the room.
After parking, he shifted the car into reverse and backed out of the driveway.
She shifted uncomfortably in her seat during the long meeting.
The wind shifted to the east, bringing cooler temperatures.
After an hour, the drivers shifted so the tired one could rest.
She shifted from leading the meeting to taking notes instead.
The team shifted their responsibilities after the project changed direction.

Synonyms

  • switch
    2.0 40/m
    switch means changing quickly from one thing to another, while shift can mean either moving position or changing focus more broadly.
  • transition
    3.8 5/m
    transition describes a process of moving from one state to another, while shift can mean both quick moves and small changes.
  • change over
    3.9 1/m
    change over means switching systems or methods in a planned way, while shift covers many kinds of moving or changing.
  • transfer
    2.7 40/m
    transfer moves someone or something to a new place or group, while shift also covers abstract changes in focus or state.
  • reorient
    5.6 1/m
    reorient means change the direction or focus of something, while shift can mean moving place or altering focus more generally.
  • reposition
    3.9 2/m
    reposition means move something to a different physical place or position, while shift can mean physical or abstract change.
  • resituate
    7.4 0.02/m
    resituate means place someone or something in a new location, while shift also includes changing focus or state, not only location.
