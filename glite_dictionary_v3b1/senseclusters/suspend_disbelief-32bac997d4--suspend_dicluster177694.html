suspend disbelief

verb
willingly ignore doubts to enjoy fiction
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛnd dɪsˈbliːf / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd dɪsˈbliːf / (BrE)
to deliberately set aside one's doubts about what is realistic in order to enjoy a story, film, or other artistic work

Examples

To suspend disbelief willingly
In order to enjoy magic tricks, audiences willingly suspend disbelief.
It can be difficult to suspend disbelief when a film's special effects look unrealistic.
Viewers must suspend disbelief to become immersed in a fantasy world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suspension of disbelief - the process or state of willingly suspending disbelief to engage with fiction
