kayak

noun
a small, narrow, watertight boat
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪ.æk / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.æk / (BrE)
a small, narrow boat with a light frame and watertight covering, traditionally made from animal skins, propelled with a double-bladed paddle; originally used by Inuit and other Arctic peoples

Examples

A kayak trip
A traditional kayak is covered with sealskin to keep out the water.
She learned how to maneuver a kayak during her summer in Alaska.
They paddled their kayak down the river, enjoying the beautiful scenery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canoe - a light, narrow boat pointed at both ends and usually open on top, propelled by paddles
