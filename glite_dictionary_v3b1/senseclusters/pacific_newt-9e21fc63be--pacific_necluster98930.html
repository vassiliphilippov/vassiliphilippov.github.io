Pacific newt

noun
rough-skinned newt of western North America
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈnuːt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈnjuːt / (BrE)
any of several species of rough-skinned newts native to western North America, particularly of the genus Taricha

Examples

The Pacific newt species
Biologists conducted a study on the habitat of the Pacific newt in Northern California.
Several hikers spotted a Pacific newt near the edge of the stream.
The Pacific newt is known for the toxins in its skin that deter predators.
