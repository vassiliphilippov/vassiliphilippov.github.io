belemnite

noun
fossil of an extinct cephalopod
7.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbe.ləm.naɪt/ (AmE), /ˈbe.ləm.naɪt/ (BrE)
a conical, tapering fossil of an extinct cephalopod, typically with a pointed end and a chambered cavity, found widely in marine rocks

Examples

A belemnite fossil
A belemnite fossil can often be found in limestone formations from the Jurassic period.
Scientists study belemnites to understand the evolution of ancient cephalopods.
The pointed shape of a belemnite resembles a dart or bullet.
