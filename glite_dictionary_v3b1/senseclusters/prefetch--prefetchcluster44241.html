prefetch

verb
to load in advance
Pronunciation: / priːˈfɛtʃ / (AmE), / priːˈfɛtʃ / (BrE)
to load data or resources in advance before they are actually needed, often to improve performance

Examples

To prefetch data
Modern web browsers can prefetch links to make websites load faster for users.
The app will prefetch data while you are connected to Wi-Fi to save mobile data later.
Engineers decided to prefetch images so that the gallery would display instantly.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: fetch - to retrieve or get data or resources
  • synonym: preload - to load something before it is needed
