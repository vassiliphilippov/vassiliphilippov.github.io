Church of Scientology

noun
religious movement founded by L. Ron Hubbard
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɝtʃ əv ˌsaɪənˈtɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃ əv ˌsaɪənˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a religious movement founded by L. Ron Hubbard in 1955, emphasizing the ability of an individual's spirit to overcome past traumas through self-knowledge and spiritual development

Examples

The Church of Scientology movement
Many celebrities have been public supporters of the Church of Scientology.
The Church of Scientology was founded in the United States in 1955 by L. Ron Hubbard.
The beliefs and practices of the Church of Scientology have been widely discussed and sometimes criticized.
