immunoglobulin A

noun
an antibody class in mucosal tissues
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmjunoʊˌɡlɑbəˈlɪn eɪ / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˌɡlɒbjʊˈlɪn eɪ / (BrE)
a class of antibody found mainly in the membranes of the gastrointestinal and respiratory tracts, important for mucosal immunity

Examples

Immunoglobulin A antibody
Immunoglobulin A plays a critical role in defending mucous membranes against infection.
Doctors often measure immunoglobulin A to assess immune function in the digestive tract.
Low levels of immunoglobulin A can make a person more susceptible to respiratory illnesses.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: immunoglobulin G - the most abundant class of antibody in the blood
  • degree lower: immunoglobulin M - an antibody class mainly produced in an initial immune response
