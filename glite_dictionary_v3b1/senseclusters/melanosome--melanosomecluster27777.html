melanosome

noun
cell pigment structure
8.9
Pronunciation: / məˈlænəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / mɛˈlanəsəʊm / (BrE)
a small structure inside a cell where melanin pigment is produced and stored

Examples

A melanosome in cells
The scientist observed the melanosome under a powerful microscope to study pigment formation.
Differences in melanosome size can affect the color of animal fur and feathers.
A mutation in the gene can disrupt normal melanosome development, leading to pigmentation disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melanin - the pigment responsible for color in skin, hair, and eyes
  • synonym: organelle - a specialized structure within a cell
