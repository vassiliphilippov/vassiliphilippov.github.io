honor

verb
accept a check or payment instrument
5.4 3.40/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ën…ö /; / Àà…ëÀên…ôr / (AmE), / Àà…ín…ô / (BrE)
(of a bank or business) to accept or pay the amount of a check, bill, or other payment instrument when presented.

Examples

Bank honors a check
If the signature is missing, the company may refuse to honor the draft.
The bank will honor this check if you present it today.
We always honor valid payment orders from our clients.
Her payment was delayed because the company refused to honour the check.
Not all institutions are willing to honour foreign drafts due to security concerns.
The bank will honour your cheque as long as there are sufficient funds in your account.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dishonor - refuse to accept or pay a check, bill, etc.
  • antonym: dishonour - to refuse to accept or pay a cheque or bill
