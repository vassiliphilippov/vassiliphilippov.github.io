obligately

adverb
by obligation
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.blɪ.ɡət.lɪ/ /; / /ə.ˈblɪ.ɡeɪt.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.blɪ.ɡət.lɪ/ /; / /ə.ˈblɪ.ɡeɪt.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that is required by law, duty, or necessity; because one is obliged to do something

Examples

Obligately follow rules
She obligately attended the meeting to fulfill her duties as treasurer.
The company obligately follows all safety regulations to protect its workers.
He obligately paid his taxes every year, even when it was difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compulsorily - in a way that is required or mandatory
  • antonym: voluntarily - in a way that is done by choice or free will
