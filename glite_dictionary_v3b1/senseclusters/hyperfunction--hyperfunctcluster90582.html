hyperfunction

noun
excessive organ activity
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
excessive or above-normal activity or function of an organ or system in the body

Examples

Hyperfunction of glands
The doctor explained that hyperfunction of the adrenal glands can cause serious health problems.
Certain medications are used to control hyperfunction in patients with thyroid disorders.
Researchers are studying the causes of hyperfunction in various endocrine systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overactivity - excessive activity or functioning
