proximally

adverb
in a near manner
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːksɪməli / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪməli / (BrE)
in a way that is nearer in origin, development, or as an immediate or primary factor.

Examples

Proximally to the joint
The injury occurred proximally to the elbow, making treatment more complicated.
The economic crisis was proximally caused by sudden changes in government policy.
In anatomy, the femur is proximally connected to the hip joint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: directly - in a way that is immediate or without anything in between
  • antonym: distally - in a way that is farther from the point of origin or attachment
