overvaluation

noun
the act of valuing something too highly
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərvælˈjuːeɪʃən /; / ˌoʊvərvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəvælˈjuːeɪʃən /; / ˌəʊvəljuːˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of valuing something as worth more than it really is

Examples

Stock overvaluation
Analysts warned that overvaluation of tech stocks could lead to a market correction.
Her appraisal resulted in the overvaluation of the painting, which later sold for much less.
The recent housing bubble was fueled by the overvaluation of real estate in many areas.
Due to overvaluation, the property's price far exceeded its actual worth.
The auditor warned that the overvaluation of assets could mislead investors.
The recent stock market bubble was caused by the overvaluation of technology companies.
