calcium octadecanoate

noun
insoluble calcium salt of stearic acid
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkælsiəm ˌɑːktədəˈkeɪnoʊˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˌɒktədəˈkeɪnəʊˌeɪt / (BrE)
an insoluble calcium salt of stearic acid, often formed as scum when soap is mixed with hard water containing calcium ions

Examples

Calcium octadecanoate scum
Chemists analyzed the scum and identified it as calcium octadecanoate.
The buildup of calcium octadecanoate in pipes is a common problem in areas with hard water.
When hard water is used with soap, calcium octadecanoate forms as an insoluble residue.
