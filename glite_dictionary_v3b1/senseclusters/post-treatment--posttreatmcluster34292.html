post-treatment

noun
therapy after treatment
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈtriːtmənt / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈtriːtmənt / (BrE)
a therapy, procedure, or period that takes place after an initial treatment to improve outcomes or ensure recovery

Examples

A post-treatment checkup
The doctor scheduled a post-treatment checkup to monitor the patient's recovery.
Patients often receive post-treatment care to reduce the risk of the disease returning.
Proper post-treatment support can make a significant difference in long-term health outcomes.
The nurse explained the importance of post-treatment care to ensure a full recovery.
Regular check-ups are scheduled during the post-treatment phase to monitor progress.
Patients often receive detailed instructions for post-treatment activities at the end of their therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aftercare - care or treatment given after a main medical procedure
  • synonym: follow-up - an additional appointment or treatment after the main one
  • antonym: pre-treatment - a therapy or procedure given before the main treatment
