coureur de bois

noun
French Canadian fur trapper
Pronunciation: / kuˌrɜr də ˈbwɑ / (AmE), / kuːˌrɜː də ˈbwa / (BrE)
A French Canadian woodsman or fur trapper, especially one engaged in trading with Indigenous peoples during the 17th and 18th centuries.

Examples

A coureur de bois trader
As a young man, he dreamed of becoming a coureur de bois exploring the vast forests of New France.
Many coureurs de bois played a key role in the early fur trade between Canada and Europe.
The legend of the coureur de bois is woven into Canadian history and folklore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: voyageur - a French Canadian who transported furs by canoe during the fur trade era
