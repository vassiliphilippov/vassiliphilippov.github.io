coureur de bois

noun
French Canadian fur trapper
Pronunciation: / kuˌrɜr də ˈbwɑ / (AmE), / kuːˌrɜː də ˈbwa / (BrE)
A French Canadian woodsman or fur trapper, especially one engaged in trading with Indigenous peoples during the 17th and 18th centuries.

The legend of the coureur de bois is woven into Canadian history and folklore.
