trip

noun
an exciting or stimulating experience
4.5
Pronunciation: / trɪp / (AmE), / trɪp / (BrE)
an exciting or unusual experience, especially one involving hallucinations or altered perceptions (often from drugs)

Examples

A wild trip
His stories are such a trip that I sometimes wonder if they’re true.
That movie was a total trip; it left everyone in the theater speechless.
The concert last night was a real trip—I've never seen anything like it.
He had a frightening trip after taking LSD last night.
Many musicians credit a psychedelic trip with inspiring their creativity.
She described her trip as both terrifying and enlightening.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - a dull or tedious experience
  • synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
  • synonym: thrill - a sudden feeling of excitement and pleasure
