trypanosomiasis

noun
parasitic disease
Pronunciation: / traɪˌpænəsoʊˈmaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / trɪˌpænəʊsəˈmaɪəsɪs /; / traɪˌpænəsəˈmaɪəsɪs /; / traɪˌpænəsəʊˈmʌɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a disease in humans or animals caused by infection with trypanosome parasites, often spread by biting insects in tropical regions

Effective prevention of trypanosomiasis requires managing the population of biting flies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Chagas disease - a type of trypanosomiasis found in the Americas
  • synonym: nagana - a form of trypanosomiasis affecting livestock
  • synonym: sleeping sickness - a form of trypanosomiasis affecting humans, causing fever and sleep disturbances
