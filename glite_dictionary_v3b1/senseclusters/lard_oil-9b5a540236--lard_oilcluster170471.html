lard oil

noun
oil rendered from lard, used as lubricant
Pronunciation: / lɑrd ɔɪl / (AmE), / lɑːd ɔɪl / (BrE)
An oil obtained mainly from lard, composed chiefly of olein, used especially as a lubricant, cutting oil, or illuminant.

Examples

lard oil lubricant
Historically, lard oil was used in lamps before more modern alternatives became widespread.
Proper lubrication with lard oil can extend the life of cutting tools.
The machinist applied lard oil to the gears to prevent them from overheating.
