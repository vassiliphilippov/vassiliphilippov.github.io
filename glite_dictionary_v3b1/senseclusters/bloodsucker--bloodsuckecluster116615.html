bloodsucker

noun
bloodsucking aquatic or terrestrial worm
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌsʌkə / (BrE)
an animal or creature that feeds on the blood of other animals, such as a leech or mosquito

Examples

A bloodsucker leech
Scientists studied the feeding habits of the aquatic bloodsucker in their laboratory.
She shrieked when she realized a bloodsucker had latched onto her ankle in the lake.
The swamp was teeming with bloodsuckers clinging to the legs of wading animals.
A mosquito is a common bloodsucker that can spread diseases to humans.
Vampires are legendary bloodsuckers found in many myths and stories.
The doctor explained how a leech acts as a natural bloodsucker in medical treatments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leech - a bloodsucking aquatic worm with suckers at both ends
  • synonym: parasite - an organism that lives on or in another and benefits at its host's expense
