eve

noun
day or time before an event
6.4 2.1
Pronunciation: /iːv/ (AmE), /iːv/ (BrE)
the day or period of time just before an event, especially a festival or important occasion

Examples

Christmas eve
Many families gather together on Christmas eve for a special dinner.
Protests erupted on the eve of the government election.
The company announced its decision on the eve of the big merger.
On the eve of the concert, the musicians did final rehearsals.
Tensions rose on the eve of the election as people awaited the results.
We moved to the new house on the eve of my birthday, making it extra special.
