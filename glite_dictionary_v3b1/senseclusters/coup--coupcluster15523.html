coup

noun
illegal takeover of a government
8.1 27/m
Pronunciation: / kuÀê / (AmE), / kuÀê / (BrE)
a sudden, illegal, and often violent takeover of a government or political power, typically by a small group

Examples

Military coup
After the coup, the country's constitution was suspended.
News of the coup shocked international leaders and prompted widespread condemnation.
The military staged a coup and seized control of the capital overnight.

Synonyms

  • coup d'etat
    8.5 0.00/m
    a coup d'etat is a formal phrase used in news and books for an illegal government takeover, while coup is the common short word.
  • golpe
    9.9 0.04/m
    golpe is a Spanish loan often used in reports about Latin America or history, while coup is the standard English term.
  • putsch
    9.9 0.34/m
    putsch is often used for a sudden attempted overthrow that may fail, while coup describes a direct seizure of government power.
  • takeover
    5.4 4.99/m
    takeover is a broad word for seizing control in business or politics, while coup specifically means an illegal seizure of government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: putsch - a sudden attempt to overthrow a government, typically by a small group
  • synonym: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order in favor of a new system
