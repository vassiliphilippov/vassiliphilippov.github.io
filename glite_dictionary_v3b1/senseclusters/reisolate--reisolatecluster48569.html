reisolate

verb
to isolate again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈaɪsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈaɪsəleɪt / (BrE)
to isolate something again, especially after it has already been isolated once before

Examples

To reisolate the virus
After contamination was detected, the team had to reisolate the virus from the samples.
Researchers decided to reisolate the compound to ensure its purity before further testing.
If results are unclear, scientists may need to reisolate the organism for confirmation.
