EMG

noun
test measuring electrical muscle activity
6.9
Pronunciation: / iː ɛm dʒiː / (AmE), / iː ɛm dʒiː / (BrE)
a test that records the electrical activity produced by skeletal muscles to diagnose neuromuscular disorders

Examples

EMG test results
After reviewing the EMG results, the neurologist diagnosed a nerve disorder.
An EMG is often used to detect problems with nerve and muscle function.
The doctor ordered an EMG to evaluate the patient's muscle weakness.
