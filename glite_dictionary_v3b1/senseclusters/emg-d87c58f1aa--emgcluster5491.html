EMG

noun
test measuring electrical muscle activity
6.9 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / iÀê …õm d íiÀê / (AmE), / iÀê …õm d íiÀê / (BrE)
a test that records the electrical activity produced by skeletal muscles to diagnose neuromuscular disorders

Examples

EMG test results
After reviewing the EMG results, the neurologist diagnosed a nerve disorder.
An EMG is often used to detect problems with nerve and muscle function.
The doctor ordered an EMG to evaluate the patient's muscle weakness.

Synonyms

  • electromyography
    8.4 0.16/m
    electromyography is the medical procedure that records muscle electrical activity to find nerve or muscle problems, while EMG is the test abbreviation.
  • electromyogram
    9.1 0.05/m
    electromyogram is the recorded display of muscle electrical activity made during testing, while EMG refers to the test itself.
