concurrent negligence

noun
independent negligence by multiple parties

Pronunciation: /kən.ˈkɜːr.ənt ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /kəŋ.ˈkɜːr.ənt ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /kən.ˈkɜːr.ənt ˈneɡ.lə.dʒəns/ (AmE), /kən.ˈkʌr.ənt ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /kəŋ.ˈkʌr.ənt ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /kɒŋ.ˈkʌr.ənt ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ (BrE)
(law) Negligence by two or more parties acting independently, each of which contributes to the same harm or injury, allowing the plaintiff to sue any or all parties.

Examples

Concurrent negligence cases
In cases of concurrent negligence, a plaintiff can choose to sue either one or both responsible parties.
Legal experts discussed how concurrent negligence affects the allocation of damages in civil lawsuits.
The court found that both drivers were guilty of concurrent negligence in causing the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comparative negligence - degree of fault assigned to each party in a negligence suit
  • synonym: contributory negligence - negligence by the injured party that contributes to their own harm
