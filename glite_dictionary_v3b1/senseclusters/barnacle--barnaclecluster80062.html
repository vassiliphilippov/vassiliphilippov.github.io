barnacle

verb
to cling persistently
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːr.nə.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈbɑː.nə.kəl/ (BrE)
to cling persistently and closely to someone or something, often to gain advantage or support (figurative use).

Examples

Barnacled to the boss
He barnacled himself to the new manager, hoping to get a promotion.
The reporter barnacled to the celebrity throughout the event, never missing a moment.
She barnacled onto the project, eager to share in its success.
