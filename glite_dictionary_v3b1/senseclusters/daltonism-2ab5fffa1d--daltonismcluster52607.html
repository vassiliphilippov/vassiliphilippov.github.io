Daltonism

noun
type of color blindness affecting green vision
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îlt…ônÀå…™z…ôm / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêlt…ôn…™z…ôm / (BrE)
a type of color blindness characterized by reduced sensitivity to green light, making it difficult to distinguish between green and purplish-red hues

Examples

Daltonism green blindness
Daltonism often makes it hard for individuals to tell the difference between green and purple colors.
Because of Daltonism, the artist uses labels to identify paint colors.
He was diagnosed with Daltonism after struggling to differentiate traffic lights.

Synonyms

  • green-blindness
    8.4 0.00/m
    green-blindness describes reduced sensitivity to green light that makes greens and purplish-reds hard to tell apart, while Daltonism is the named condition.
  • deuteranopia
    9.9 0.00/m
    deuteranopia causes lowered green sensitivity and trouble seeing red-green differences, while Daltonism refers to this same green-sensitive color blindness.
