Hebrew Scripture

noun
the Jewish scriptures (Torah, Prophets, Writings)
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːbru ˈskrɪptʃər / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː ˈskrɪptʃə / (BrE)
the Jewish sacred writings consisting of three divisions: the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings; also known as the Hebrew Bible or Tanakh.

Examples

The Hebrew Scripture texts
Many passages in the Hebrew Scripture are referenced in both Jewish and Christian traditions.
Scholars have dedicated years to studying the language and history of the Hebrew Scripture.
The Hebrew Scripture is central to understanding the roots of Judaism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Old Testament - the first part of the Christian Bible, corresponding largely to Hebrew Scripture
  • synonym: Tanakh - the canonical collection of Jewish texts, the Hebrew Bible
