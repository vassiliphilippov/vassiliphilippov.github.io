proportionately

adverb
in proportion; correspondingly
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɔrʃənətli /; / prəˈpɔr.ʃə.nət.li / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənətli /; / prəˈpɔː.ʃən.ət.li / (BrE)
in a way that matches or corresponds in amount, size, or degree to something else; in proportion

Examples

Increase proportionately
If the price increases, the cost will rise proportionately.
The penalty for late submission will decrease proportionately the sooner you turn in the assignment.
The rewards will be proportionately distributed based on each member's contribution.
If you double the number of hours worked, your pay will rise proportionately.
The bonus was distributed proportionately among the staff based on their performance.
Your rent will increase proportionately if you decide to rent a larger office.
If attendance declines, the budget for the event will be reduced proportionately to reflect the lower demand.
The employees' bonuses were distributed proportionately based on their performance and contribution to the project.
When the recipe was doubled, the ingredients were increased proportionately to maintain the flavor.
