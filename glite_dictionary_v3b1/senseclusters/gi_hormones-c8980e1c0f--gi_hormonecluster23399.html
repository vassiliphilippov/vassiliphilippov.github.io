GI hormones

noun
hormones affecting the digestive system
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːˈaɪ ˈhɔrˌmoʊnz / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːˈaɪ ˈhɔːməʊnz / (BrE)
Hormones that regulate the functions of the gastrointestinal tract, such as digestion and absorption.

Examples

GI hormones regulate digestion
Certain medications can impact the production of GI hormones in the digestive system.
Scientists are studying how GI hormones affect appetite and metabolism.
The levels of GI hormones can influence how quickly food moves through the intestines.
