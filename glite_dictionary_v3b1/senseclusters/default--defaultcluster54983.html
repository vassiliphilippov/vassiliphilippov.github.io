default

noun
failure to meet financial obligation
4.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔlt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt / (BrE)
failure to fulfill a financial obligation, especially to repay a loan or meet a debt payment

Examples

A loan default
A history of default can damage your credit rating significantly.
If you fall into default on your mortgage, the bank may foreclose on your home.
The company went into default after missing several loan payments.
A wave of mortgage defaults triggered the financial crisis.
If you fail to pay your loan, you risk default and legal action.
The company declared default on its bonds after missing several payments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bankruptcy - legal status of being unable to repay debts
  • synonym: delinquency - failure to make payments on time
  • antonym: repayment - the action of paying back money owed
