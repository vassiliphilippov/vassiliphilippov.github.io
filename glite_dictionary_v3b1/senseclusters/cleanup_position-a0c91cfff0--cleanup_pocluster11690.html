cleanup position

noun
fourth batting spot in baseball lineup
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkliːn.ʌp pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈkliːn.ʌp pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Baseball) the fourth position in the batting order, typically assigned to the team's strongest hitter.

Examples

Batting in the cleanup position
Batting in the cleanup position, she drove in three runs during the game.
He was moved to the cleanup position after hitting several home runs this season.
The manager selected their power hitter for the cleanup position to maximize scoring chances.
