overclocked

adjective
running above standard speed
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈklɑːkt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklɒkt / (BrE)
operating at a higher speed than the manufacturer's standard setting, especially for computer components

Examples

Overclocked processor
The overclocked processor allowed the computer to complete tasks much faster than before.
He noticed that his overclocked laptop became hotter during long gaming sessions.
Many enthusiasts use special cooling systems to keep their overclocked hardware stable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boosted - increased in power or performance
  • antonym: underclocked - operating at a lower speed than the standard setting
