trial balloon

noun
an attempt to test public opinion
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪəl bəˈluːn /; / ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl bəˈluːn /; / ˈtraɪ.əl bəˈluːn / (BrE)
an idea or proposal suggested to test how people will react before making a final decision

Examples

Political trial balloon
His comments about restructuring the company were just a trial balloon to gauge employees' opinions.
Sometimes politicians float a trial balloon before committing to a controversial decision.
The government released the policy as a trial balloon to see how the public would react.
Her announcement about cutting taxes was widely seen as a trial balloon to judge voter response.
Launching a trial balloon can minimize risk by revealing potential objections early.
The company floated a trial balloon to see if the public would accept the new product.

  • synonym: test case - an instance used to test or evaluate something
