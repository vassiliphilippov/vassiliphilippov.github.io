concentration gradient

noun
difference in solute concentration across a distance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən ˈɡreɪ.di.ənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.sənˈtreɪ.ʃən ˈɡreɪ.di.ənt / (BrE)
a gradual change in the concentration of solutes in a solution as a function of distance through a solution

Examples

A concentration gradient in solution
Cells often rely on a concentration gradient to transport nutrients across their membranes.
Oxygen moves into the blood along its concentration gradient.
The rate of osmosis increases with a steeper concentration gradient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diffusion - the movement of particles from an area of higher to lower concentration
