self-generated

adjective
produced by oneself or itself
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtɪd /; / ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnɚˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
produced or created by itself, without external influence or cause

Examples

Self-generated movement
Many plants exhibit self-generated movements in response to light and gravity.
She felt a self-generated sense of motivation that kept her going.
The machine was powered by a self-generated electrical current.
A self-generated idea can sometimes be more powerful than one suggested by others.
His motivation was entirely self-generated, not the result of external pressure.
The company relies on self-generated electricity to power its operations.
