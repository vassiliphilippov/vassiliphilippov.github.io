tympanoplasty

noun
surgical repair of the eardrum or middle ear bones
Pronunciation: / tɪmˈpæn.əˌplæsti / (AmE), / tɪmˈpan.əˌplɑːsti / (BrE)
A surgical procedure to repair defects or injuries in the eardrum or the small bones of the middle ear.

Examples

Tympanoplasty surgery
Advances in tympanoplasty techniques have increased the success rate of ear surgeries.
After the accident, the surgeon recommended a tympanoplasty to restore his hearing.
The patient's recovery after tympanoplasty was smooth, and her hearing improved significantly.
