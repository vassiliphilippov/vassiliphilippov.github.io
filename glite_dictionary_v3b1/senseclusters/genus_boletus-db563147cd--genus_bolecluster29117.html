genus Boletus

noun
genus of pore fungi, includes boletes
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs boʊˈliːtəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs bəˈliːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of soft, early-decaying fungi in the family Boletaceae, some species of which are edible and others poisonous; includes many well-known boletes.

Examples

The genus Boletus mushrooms
Several edible species in the genus Boletus are prized by mushroom hunters.
Some mushrooms in the genus Boletus are toxic, so proper identification is crucial.
The genus Boletus includes both gourmet and inedible varieties.
