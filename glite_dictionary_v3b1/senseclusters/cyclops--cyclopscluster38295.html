cyclops

noun
minute freshwater crustacean with one eye
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɑps / (AmE), / ˈsaɪklɒps / (BrE)
A minute free-swimming freshwater crustacean with a single large eye, belonging to the genus Cyclops, often serving as an intermediate host for parasitic worms.

Examples

Tiny cyclops crustacean
Fish feed on cyclops, making these tiny crustaceans important in aquatic ecosystems.
Scientists study cyclops to understand freshwater food chains and their role as disease vectors.
The pond water sample contained several cyclops swimming near the surface.
