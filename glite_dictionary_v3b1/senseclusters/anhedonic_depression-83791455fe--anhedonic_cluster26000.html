anhedonic depression

noun
depression marked by inability to feel pleasure
3.1 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.hɪˈdɑː.nɪk dɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌan.hɪˈdɒn.ɪk dɪˈprɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
A type of depression characterized by a person's inability to experience pleasure in normally enjoyable activities.

Examples

Anhedonic depression symptoms
A period of anhedonic depression left him feeling empty and detached from the world.
Psychiatrists often differentiate anhedonic depression from other subtypes based on loss of pleasure.
The patient struggled with anhedonic depression, finding no joy in activities she once loved.
