endermatic

adjective
acting by absorption through the skin
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.dərˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or treatment) acting by absorption through the skin.

Examples

An endermatic treatment
Applying an endermatic ointment can be useful for patients who have difficulty swallowing pills.
Some medications are designed for endermatic delivery, allowing them to enter the body through the skin.
The doctor recommended an endermatic cream to treat the rash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transdermal - (of medication) administered through the skin, typically by a patch.
