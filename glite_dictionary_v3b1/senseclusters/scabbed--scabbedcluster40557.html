scabbed

adjective
covered with a scab
Pronunciation: / skæbd / (AmE), / skæbd / (BrE)
covered with a dry, rough layer that forms over a healing wound or sore

Examples

Scabbed skin
After scraping his elbow, a scabbed patch soon appeared over the wound.
The dog's nose was scabbed from scratching it on the fence.
Her hands were scabbed and rough after a week of gardening.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bleeding - losing blood from a wound
  • degree higher: healed - having recovered from a wound or injury
