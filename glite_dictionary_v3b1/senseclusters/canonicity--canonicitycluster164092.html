canonicity

noun
state of being canonical
Pronunciation: / ˌkænəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌkænəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being in a canonical or standard form, especially where a unique or normalized representation is required

Examples

Canonicity of a formula
The canonicity of the mathematical expression ensures that every solution is represented in the same way.
In computer science, canonicity helps prevent duplicate data by enforcing a unique format.
The researchers debated the canonicity of the text before including it in the official collection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ambiguity - the quality of having more than one possible meaning or form
  • synonym: normalization - the process of converting data or structures to a standard form
  • synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being the only one of its kind
