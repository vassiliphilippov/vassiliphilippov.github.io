volley

verb
discharge or send rapidly together
9.3 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈvɑː.liː/ /; / /ˈvɑː.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈvɒl.iː/ /; / /ˈvɒl.ɪ/ (BrE)
to send, fire, or utter things rapidly and forcefully, especially in quick succession (such as bullets, questions, or words)

Examples

To volley questions
He volleyed questions at the speaker during the heated debate.
Spectators gasped as the protesters volleyed stones at the building.
The soldiers volleyed arrows toward the advancing enemy.
Frustrated by the delay, he volleyed complaints at the customer service agent.
She volleyed questions at the witness, barely giving him time to answer.
When challenged, the politician volleyed accusations at his opponents without hesitation.
Cheers volleyed across the stadium after the final goal was scored.
Gunfire suddenly volleyed from the opposite side of the street, scattering the crowd.
Questions volleyed from the reporters as the minister entered the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrage - a rapid, concentrated outpouring of something, such as shots or questions
