volley

verb
discharge or send rapidly together
6.3 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ëÀêli /; / Ààv…ëli / (AmE), / Ààv…íli / (BrE)
to send, fire, or utter things rapidly and forcefully, especially in quick succession (such as bullets, questions, or words)

Examples

To volley questions
He volleyed questions at the speaker during the heated debate.
Spectators gasped as the protesters volleyed stones at the building.
The soldiers volleyed arrows toward the advancing enemy.
Frustrated by the delay, he volleyed complaints at the customer service agent.
She volleyed questions at the witness, barely giving him time to answer.
When challenged, the politician volleyed accusations at his opponents without hesitation.
Cheers volleyed across the stadium after the final goal was scored.
Gunfire suddenly volleyed from the opposite side of the street, scattering the crowd.
Questions volleyed from the reporters as the minister entered the room.

Synonyms

  • let fly
    5.8 0.26/m
    let fly sends or throws something suddenly and can mean launching angry words, while volley implies many items fired in quick succession.
  • blaze away
    7.7 0.04/m
    blaze away means to shoot rapidly and repeatedly with a gun, while volley can also mean many words or questions fired together.
  • pump
    6.8 0.67/m
    pump means to fire bullets rapidly with strong force, often by repeating action, while volley highlights a burst of many shots or words.
  • blaze
    7.7 0.74/m
    blaze means to shoot rapidly in heavy bursts, often with loud gunfire, while volley stresses a quick grouped discharge or stream of words.
  • fire
    1.0 49/m
    fire means to shoot or discharge a weapon generally or once, while volley stresses many items sent quickly and in close succession.
