vernix

noun
white protective substance on newborn skin
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrnɪks / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪks / (BrE)
A white, cheese-like protective substance that covers the skin of a fetus before birth.

Examples

The vernix caseosa
Doctors believe that vernix helps protect the fetus from amniotic fluid.
Nurses gently wiped the vernix from the newborn's body after delivery.
The baby's skin was covered in vernix immediately after birth.
