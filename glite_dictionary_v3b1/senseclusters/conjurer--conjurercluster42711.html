conjurer

noun
person who performs magic or conjury
9.0 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀênd í…ôr…ôr / (AmE), / Ààk…índ í…ôr…ô / (BrE)
A person who performs magic or practices conjury, especially one believed to use supernatural powers as a witch doctor.

Examples

Tribal conjurer
Stories of a powerful conjurer who protected the tribe have been passed down for generations.
The conjurer performed secret rituals believed to ward off evil spirits.
The village conjurer was called upon to heal the sick child.

Synonyms

  • conjuror
    8.6 0.04/m
    conjuror is an alternate spelling seen in older texts or names, while conjurer is the common modern form for the same person.
  • sorcerer
    7.9 4.29/m
    sorcerer practices strong supernatural magic and often appears in stories, while conjurer can mean a magic worker tied to witch doctor traditions.
  • enchanter
    8.7 0.23/m
    enchanter casts spells to charm or control people, while conjurer is a broader term for someone who practices magic or conjury.
  • magician
    3.6 7.68/m
    magician performs tricks for an audience and sometimes seems to have powers, while conjurer is often seen as a supernatural or witch doctor figure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: witch doctor - a traditional healer or practitioner of magic among some peoples
