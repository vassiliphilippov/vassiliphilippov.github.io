chaser

noun
drink that follows another drink
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪ.sə / (BrE)
A drink taken immediately after another drink, usually to follow a strong or unpleasant-tasting liquor.

Examples

A beer chaser
A cold soda makes a good chaser for spicy cocktails.
He ordered a whiskey with a chaser of beer to soften the burn.
She always needs a chaser after drinking tequila shots.
