flock

verb
gather or move in a large group
2.0 5.18/m
Pronunciation: / flÉ‘k / (AmE), / flÉ’k / (BrE)
to gather or move together in a large group

Examples

People flock together
The protesters were flocking outside the government building by early morning.
Tourists flock to the city every spring to see the cherry blossoms.
Young people flocked to the concert, eager to see their favorite band perform.
Fans began to flock to the stadium hours before the match started.
In spring, birds flock north to their breeding grounds.
Shoppers flocked to the new store as soon as it opened.

Synonyms

  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather is a general verb for bringing people or things together, while flock means moving together in a large group.
  • congregate
    4.1 2.55/m
    congregate means coming together often for a purpose, while flock suggests people or animals move together in a large group.
  • assemble
    3.3 11/m
    assemble is to bring people together for a planned purpose, while flock means to gather and move together in a large group.
  • throng
    6.4 0.78/m
    throng is to crowd closely in a place, often pressing for space, while flock means to gather or move together as a group.
  • cluster
    3.5 6.08/m
    cluster is to form a tight group or bunch, often in one spot, while flock means to move or gather in large groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disperse - to scatter in different directions
  • synonym: gather - to come together in a group
