flock

verb
gather or move in a large group
4.8 1.7
Pronunciation: /flɑːk/ (AmE), /flɒk/ (BrE)
to gather or move together in a large group

Examples

People flock together
The protesters were flocking outside the government building by early morning.
Tourists flock to the city every spring to see the cherry blossoms.
Young people flocked to the concert, eager to see their favorite band perform.
Fans began to flock to the stadium hours before the match started.
In spring, birds flock north to their breeding grounds.
Shoppers flocked to the new store as soon as it opened.
