knife-edge

noun
narrow or critical boundary
2.9
Pronunciation: / naɪf ɛdʒ / (AmE), / naɪf ɛdʒ / (BrE)
a very narrow or critical boundary between two possible outcomes or states, especially where a slight change can have significant consequences

Examples

Balanced on a knife-edge
Scientists say the ecosystem is balanced on a knife-edge and could collapse without intervention.
The political situation remained on a knife-edge for weeks after the election.
Their relationship was always on a knife-edge, ready to break at any moment.
