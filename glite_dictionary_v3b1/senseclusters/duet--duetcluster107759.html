duet

noun
social-media split-screen collaboration
Pronunciation: / duˈet / (AmE), / djuˈet / (BrE)
a short social-media video format or feature in which two users appear together (often side-by-side) to perform, respond to, or collaborate with an existing clip.

Examples

Social media duet
The app's new duet feature lets two creators perform together in split-screen.
She posted a duet with her friend singing the chorus alongside the original clip.
Brands often encourage fans to make duets responding to product videos for increased engagement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reaction video - a video in which a creator records their response to another clip; often paired with the original.
  • synonym: split-screen video - a video presentation where two clips are shown side-by-side, allowing creators to collaborate or compare.
