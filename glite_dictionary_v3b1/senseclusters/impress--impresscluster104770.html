impress

verb
to force into military or naval service
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɛs / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to seize someone and force them to serve, especially in the military or on a ship, against their will

Examples

To impress sailors
In the 18th century, naval officers would impress men from port towns into service.
Several villagers were impressed onto ships during the war and never returned home.
The fear of being impressed kept many young men away from busy harbors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shanghai - to force someone to join a ship by drugging or trickery
