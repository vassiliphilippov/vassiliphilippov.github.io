Pronunciation: / i ˌplʊrɪbəs ˈjuːnəm / (AmE), / i ˌplʊərɪbəs ˈjuːnəm / (BrE)
a Latin phrase meaning 'out of many, one', used as a motto of the United States to express unity from diversity

Examples

E pluribus unum motto
The phrase E pluribus unum is found on many U.S. coins and official documents.
Americans often refer to E pluribus unum when discussing the country's diverse origins.
The motto E pluribus unum symbolizes the unity of the United States.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divided - separated into parts or groups
  • synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose
