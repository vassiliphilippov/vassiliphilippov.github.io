dally

verb
to consider or treat casually
Pronunciation: / ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈdæli / (BrE)
to consider or deal with something in a casual or unserious way; to toy with an idea or possibility without commitment

Examples

To dally with ideas
Don't dally over the idea if you have no intention of following through.
He would often dally with plans for travel, never committing to any.
She tends to dally with different career options without making a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: toy - to consider something lightly or playfully, not seriously
  • synonym: trifle - to treat someone or something without seriousness or respect
