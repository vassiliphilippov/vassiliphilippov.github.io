earthquake

noun
major disruption or upheaval
Pronunciation: / ˈɝːθˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌkweɪk / (BrE)
a sudden and major disruption or upheaval, especially in a social, political, or organizational context

Examples

A political earthquake
Her shocking revelation was an earthquake in the world of politics.
The CEO's resignation created an earthquake within the organization.
The policy change sent an earthquake through the tight-knit community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a period or state of tranquility; the absence of disturbance
  • synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption
