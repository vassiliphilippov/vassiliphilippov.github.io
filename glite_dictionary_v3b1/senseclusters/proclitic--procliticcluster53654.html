proclitic

noun
clitic joined to following word
9.8 9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈklɪtɪk / (AmE), / prəʊˈklɪtɪk / (BrE)
(in linguistics) a clitic that is pronounced together with and closely linked to the following word, rather than preceding word.

Examples

A proclitic word
In some languages, the definite article is a proclitic attached to the following noun.
The word 'a' in 'a book' is considered a proclitic in English.
When analyzing the sentence, the linguist identified the prefix as a proclitic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enclitic - a clitic that attaches to the preceding word
