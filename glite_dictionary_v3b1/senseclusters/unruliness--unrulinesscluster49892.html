unruliness

noun
disobedience; lack of discipline
7.2 0.06/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàruÀêlin…ôs / (AmE), /  ånÀàruÀêl…™n…™s / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult to control or manage; disobedience and lack of discipline

Examples

Persistent unruliness
His persistent unruliness led to frequent detentions.
Teachers often struggle with unruliness in large classrooms.
The unruliness of the crowd made it difficult for security to maintain order.

Synonyms

  • indiscipline
    7.2 0.11/m
    indiscipline shows a clear lack of training or control in people or groups, while unruliness shows active disobedience and hard-to-manage behavior.
  • disobedience
    4.2 3.65/m
    disobedience is refusing to follow rules or orders, while unruliness is broader and includes general lack of discipline and control.
  • rebelliousness
    6.7 0.34/m
    rebelliousness means openly resisting authority or rules, while unruliness covers both resistance and general failure to be controlled.
  • fractiousness
    9.9 0.07/m
    fractiousness means being easily upset and hard to manage, while unruliness focuses more on rule-breaking and lack of discipline.
  • obstreperousness
    9.9 0.01/m
    obstreperousness describes loud, stubborn, and often aggressive noisy behavior, while unruliness covers difficulty to control without always being noisy.
  • waywardness
    7.5 0.04/m
    waywardness means unpredictable, stubborn behavior that ignores rules, while unruliness emphasizes general lack of discipline and control.
  • willfulness
    6.8 0.28/m
    willfulness means acting by strong personal choice and stubborn will, while unruliness focuses on poor discipline and difficulty to manage.
  • disorderliness
    6.0 0.01/m
    disorderliness means messy, chaotic conduct and lack of order, while unruliness more often implies disobedience and active resistance to control.
  • rowdiness
    4.5 0.07/m
    rowdiness is noisy, rough, and boisterous public behavior, while unruliness is the broader state of being hard to control or discipline.
