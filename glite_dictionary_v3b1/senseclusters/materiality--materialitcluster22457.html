materiality

noun
importance or relevance requiring attention
6.2 6.7
Pronunciation: /mə.tɪ.riː.ˈæl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /mə.tɪ.riː.ˈæl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being relevant and significant, especially in a way that warrants careful attention or consideration

Examples

Materiality of evidence
Before making her decision, she assessed the materiality of each argument presented.
Courts often debate the materiality of facts when determining if a case should proceed.
The auditor questioned the materiality of the minor accounting errors in the report.
