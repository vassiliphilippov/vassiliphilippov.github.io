chain-smoke

verb
smoke cigarettes one after another
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪnˌsmoʊk / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪnˌsməʊk / (BrE)
to smoke cigarettes continuously, lighting each new one from the end of the last

Examples

To chain-smoke cigarettes

He used to chain-smoke during deadlines, barely stopping to eat or drink.
You could tell he'd been chain-smoking by the overflowing ashtray on his desk.
