let loose

verb
allow to be free; behave freely
2.1
Pronunciation: / lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to become free, or to relax and act without restraint

Examples

To let loose and dance
After the big exam, everyone wanted to let loose and have some fun.
At the party, she finally let loose and danced without worrying about anyone watching.
They let loose the dogs into the garden to run freely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: release - to set someone or something free
  • antonym: restrain - to keep something under control or within limits
