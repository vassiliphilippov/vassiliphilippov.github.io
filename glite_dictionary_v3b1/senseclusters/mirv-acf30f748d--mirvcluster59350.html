MIRV

noun
missile with multiple warheads
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪrv /; / mɜːrv / (AmE), / ˈmɜːv /; / ˈmɪːv /; / mɪəv / (BrE)
a missile or warhead system that can carry and deliver several warheads, each aimed at a different target independently

Examples

A MIRV missile
The new submarine is armed with several MIRVs to increase its deterrence capability.
During the arms race, countries developed MIRVs to overcome missile defense systems.
A single MIRV can deliver multiple warheads to different targets in one launch.
The development of MIRVs significantly increased the destructive power of nuclear arsenals.
During arms control negotiations, the deployment of MIRVs was a major point of discussion.
Modern strategic missiles often carry MIRVs to strike multiple targets simultaneously.
The military unveiled a new MIRV capable of striking several targets at once.
Each MIRV can carry multiple warheads, increasing its effectiveness in warfare.
The treaty aims to limit the number of MIRVs deployed by each country.
