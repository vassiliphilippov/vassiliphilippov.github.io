flowline

noun
factory production system
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfləʊˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a production system in which products move continuously through a sequence of machines or workstations in a factory

Examples

Factory flowline
The new flowline allowed the factory to increase its output and reduce production time.
Engineers redesigned the plant to include a modern flowline for assembling electronic devices.
By switching to a flowline system, the company improved efficiency and lowered costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assembly line - a manufacturing process where parts are added in sequence as the product moves along
  • antonym: batch production - a manufacturing method where products are made in groups rather than continuously
