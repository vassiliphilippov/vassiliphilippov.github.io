diagenesis

noun
sediment transformation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the physical and chemical changes that sediments undergo after deposition, leading to the formation of sedimentary rock

Examples

Diagenesis of sediments
The color and hardness of the rock changed due to diagenesis over millions of years.
Geologists study diagenesis to understand how sedimentary rocks are formed and altered.
During diagenesis, minerals in the sediment can dissolve and re-form, changing the rock's structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lithification - the process of turning sediment into rock
  • antonym: weathering - the breaking down of rocks and minerals at Earth's surface
