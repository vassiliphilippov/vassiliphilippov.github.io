overspin

verb
to spin excessively
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈspɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɪn / (BrE)
to cause something to spin or rotate more than is intended, safe, or necessary

Examples

To overspin the wheels
If you overspin the wheels on a wet road, you might lose control of the car.
The tennis player tried not to overspin the ball during her serve.
He accidentally overspun the top, causing it to wobble and fall quickly.

  • synonym: spin - to turn around quickly on a central point
  • antonym: underrotate - to rotate less than intended or required
