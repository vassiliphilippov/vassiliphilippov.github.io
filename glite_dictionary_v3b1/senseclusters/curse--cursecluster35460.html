curse

verb
use offensive language angrily
4.1 10/m
Pronunciation: / k…úrs /; / k…ùs / (AmE), / k…úÀês / (BrE)
to use offensive or obscene language, especially in anger

Examples

To curse at people
He was so angry that he cursed loudly at the driver who cut him off.
She cursed under her breath when she realized she had missed the last train.
The coach cursed at his team after their poor performance in the first half.
Drivers often curse at each other when stuck in heavy traffic.
She was so angry at the delay that she couldn't help but curse under her breath.
When he stubbed his toe, John began to curse loudly.

Synonyms

  • swear
    4.3 8.23/m
    swear is the common word for using offensive language in speech, while curse often suggests stronger, angrier words and more emphasis.
  • cuss
    8.1 1.35/m
    cuss is an informal US word meaning to use swear words in frustration, while curse is the standard term for swearing especially in anger.
