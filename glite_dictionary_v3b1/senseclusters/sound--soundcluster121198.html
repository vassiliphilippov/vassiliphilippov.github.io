sound

verb
emit a particular noise or tone
Pronunciation: / saʊnd / (AmE), / saʊnd / (BrE)
to make or produce a noise; to cause something to be heard

Examples

To sound a horn
Her voice sounds hoarse after cheering at the game all night.
The alarm clock sounded loudly at six in the morning.
The bell sounded three times to signal the end of class.
If there is a fire, the system will sound an alarm automatically.
Please sound the horn if you see any danger ahead.
The referee sounded the whistle to mark the end of the game.
He asked the organist to sound a low note during the ceremony.
Please sound the alarm if there is an emergency.
The church will sound the bell every hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emit - to produce or discharge (a sound, light, etc.)
  • synonym: produce - to cause a particular result or effect, often used for sounds
  • synonym: ring - to make a bell or similar object produce sound
  • synonym: signal - to send a signal or warning
  • antonym: silence - the absence of any sound or noise
