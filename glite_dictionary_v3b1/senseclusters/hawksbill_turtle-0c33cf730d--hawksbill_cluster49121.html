hawksbill turtle

noun
tropical sea turtle with a hawk-like beak
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑksˌbɪl ˈtɜrtəl / (AmE), / ˈhɔːksbɪl ˈtɜːtəl / (BrE)
a tropical sea turtle with a hawk-like beak, known for its beautiful tortoiseshell and endangered status

Examples

The hawksbill turtle shell
Many cultures once valued the shells of hawksbill turtles for making ornaments and jewelry.
Snorkelers sometimes spot the distinctive beak of a hawksbill turtle among coral reefs.
The hawksbill turtle is critically endangered due to hunting and habitat loss.
