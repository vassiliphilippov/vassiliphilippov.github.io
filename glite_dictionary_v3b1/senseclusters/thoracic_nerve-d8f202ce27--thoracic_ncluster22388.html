thoracic nerve

noun
spinal nerve from thoracic region
7.3
Pronunciation: /θɔːr.ˈæs.ɪk nɜːrv/ /; / /θə.ˈræs.ɪk nɜːrv/ (AmE), /θə.ˈræs.ɪk nɜːv/ /; / /θɔː.ˈæs.ɪk nɜːv/ (BrE)
any of the twelve pairs of spinal nerves that emerge from the thoracic region of the spinal cord

Examples

Thoracic nerve pair
Each thoracic nerve supplies both motor and sensory fibers to specific areas of the chest and back.
Injuries to a thoracic nerve can result in weakness or loss of sensation in the torso.
The surgeon identified the damaged thoracic nerve before beginning the procedure.
