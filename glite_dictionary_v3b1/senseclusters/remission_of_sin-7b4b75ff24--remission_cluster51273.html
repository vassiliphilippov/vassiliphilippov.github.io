remission of sin

noun
forgiveness of sin in a religious context
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪʃ.ən əv sɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃ.ən əv sɪn / (BrE)
the formal forgiveness or pardon of sin, especially as granted by a priest in the sacrament of penance

Examples

The remission of sin granted
Early Christians believed baptism brought about the remission of sin.
The doctrine of remission of sin is central to many religious traditions.
The priest declared the remission of sin after the penitent confessed sincerely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolution - formal forgiveness of sins, especially in a religious context
  • synonym: penance - a sacrament expressing sorrow for sin and seeking forgiveness
