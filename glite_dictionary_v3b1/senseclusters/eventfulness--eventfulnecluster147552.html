eventfulness

noun
quality of having events
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvɛntfəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈvɛntfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of consisting of or being characterized by distinct, individual events rather than a continuous process

Examples

Eventfulness of the plot
The eventfulness of the story kept the audience engaged from start to finish.
Scientists analyzed the eventfulness of the data to identify significant changes over time.
Her life was marked by a surprising eventfulness despite her quiet personality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuity - the state of being continuous or unbroken
  • synonym: episodicity - the quality of being made up of separate episodes or events
