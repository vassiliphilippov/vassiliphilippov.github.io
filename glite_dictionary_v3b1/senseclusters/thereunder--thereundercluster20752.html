thereunder

adverb
below that place
9.1
Pronunciation: / ðɛrˈʌndər / (AmE), / ðeərˈʌndə /; / ðeəˈʌndə / (BrE)
under or beneath the thing, provision, or condition just mentioned (often used in legal or formal contexts)

Examples

Hidden thereunder papers
The document was placed on the table, and the contract was hidden thereunder for safekeeping.
They discovered a secret compartment thereunder after removing the old floorboards.
The cat found a warm spot thereunder when the blanket was draped over the chair.
The parties agree to fulfill all obligations set forth thereunder in the agreement.
Any rights granted by the contract and duties imposed thereunder shall remain valid.
The law and the penalties prescribed thereunder apply to all citizens equally.
