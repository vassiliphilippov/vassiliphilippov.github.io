liege

adjective
owing feudal allegiance; loyal to a lord
7.3
Pronunciation: / liːdʒ / (AmE), / liːdʒ / (BrE)
(historical, archaic) Owing or owed feudal allegiance and service; loyal to a feudal superior.

Examples

Liege loyalty
He swore to remain a liege subject of his king until death.
In ancient times, peasants served as liege men to powerful nobles.
The knights gathered before their liege lord to pledge loyalty and service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sovereign - a supreme ruler, especially a monarch
  • synonym: subject - a person under the authority of a ruler; one who owes allegiance
