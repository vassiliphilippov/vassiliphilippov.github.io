bleed out

verb
lose a large amount of blood
7.7 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / bliÀêd a ät / (AmE), / bliÀêd a ät / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to lose so much blood from an injury that there is a risk of death or serious harm, especially if untreated.

Examples

To bleed out from a wound
If they had arrived any later, she might have bled out on the sidewalk.
The medic rushed over before the victim could bleed out from the deep wound.
We need to apply pressure here so he doesn't bleed out before the ambulance gets here.

Synonyms

  • exsanguinate
    9.9 0.05/m
    exsanguinate is a formal medical verb meaning to drain almost all blood and often cause death, while bleed out is used for dying from heavy blood loss.
  • hemorrhage
    7.8 1/m
    hemorrhage is a medical verb for heavy, often uncontrolled bleeding, while bleed out focuses on dying because blood loss becomes severe and untreated.
