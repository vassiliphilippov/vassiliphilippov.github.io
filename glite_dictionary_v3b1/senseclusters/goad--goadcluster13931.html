goad

something that urges or encourages someone to take action, especially a remark or incentive designed to provoke a response.

Examples

A goad for change
Her words served as a goad to push him out of his comfort zone.
His refusal to help acted as a goad for her to prove him wrong.
Sometimes a little goad is all it takes to motivate someone to succeed.

Synonyms

  • spur
    4.1 0.94/m
    a spur is a quick remark that encourages action in the moment, while a goad is often harsher and more persistent.
  • prod
    4.4 0.13/m
    a prod is a light nudge used to overcome reluctance, while a goad is stronger and meant to provoke a clear response.
  • inducement
    6.3 1.02/m
    an inducement is an offered reward or motive to persuade someone, while a goad often pressures or teases to cause action.
  • incitation
    8.6 0.00/m
    incitation is formal urging that often implies wrongdoing or public provocation, while a goad can be any remark or incentive to prompt action.
