goad

noun
stimulus or encouragement to act
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /goʊd/ (AmE), /gəʊd/ (BrE)
something that urges or encourages someone to take action, especially a remark or incentive designed to provoke a response.

Examples

A goad for change
Her words served as a goad to push him out of his comfort zone.
His refusal to help acted as a goad for her to prove him wrong.
Sometimes a little goad is all it takes to motivate someone to succeed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deterrent - something that discourages someone from doing something
  • synonym: incentive - a thing that motivates or encourages someone to do something
  • synonym: spur - a thing that encourages someone to take action
