glib-tongued

adjective
persuasively fluent in speech, often insincere
Pronunciation: / ɡlɪb tʌŋd / (AmE), / ɡlɪb tʌŋd / (BrE)
Having a fluent and persuasive way of speaking, often suggesting insincerity or superficiality.

Examples

Glib-tongued speaker
His glib-tongued explanations managed to defuse the heated argument.
She distrusted glib-tongued salespeople and always read the fine print carefully.
The politician was so glib-tongued that people sometimes doubted his sincerity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunt - Plain-spoken, not subtle or insincere in speech
  • synonym: silver-tongued - Having a way with words; eloquent and persuasive
  • synonym: smooth-tongued - Having smooth and persuasive speech, often insincere
