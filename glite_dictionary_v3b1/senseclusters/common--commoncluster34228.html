common

adjective
shared by two or more parties
1.0 119/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀê.m…ôn /; / Ààk…ëm.…ôn / (AmE), / Ààk…ím.…ôn / (BrE)
shared by two or more people, groups, or things; belonging to or shared by all members of a community

Examples

Common interests
These organizations have a common goal to improve education.
They worked together for their common benefit.
We have a common friend who introduced us.
During the meeting, we discussed issues of common concern to all residents.
Historically, the village had a common pasture for farmers to graze their animals.
The park is a common space where everyone in the neighborhood can relax.

Synonyms

  • communal
    3.9 6.46/m
    communal is used or owned by a whole community for everyone's use, while common can mean shared by people or groups generally.
  • shared
    1.0 0.14/m
    shared is used or held by more than one person or group, while common means belonging to several people or things.
  • mutual
    2.8 34/m
    mutual is felt or done by two or more people equally, while common simply means shared among people or groups.
  • joint
    3.5 39/m
    joint is shared or carried out by two or more people or groups together, while common means shared more generally.
  • collective
    3.9 26/m
    collective is done or shared by a group's members acting together, while common is a general idea of being shared.

Related Terms

  • synonym: public - relating to or affecting all the people or the community
