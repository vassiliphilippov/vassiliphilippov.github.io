mechanical phenomenon

noun
physical event involving mechanics
3.4
Pronunciation: / məˈkænɪkəl fəˈnɑːməˌnɑːn / (AmE), / məˈkænɪkəl fəˈnɒmɪˌnən / (BrE)
a physical event or process associated with the equilibrium or movement of objects, especially as studied in mechanics.

Examples

A mechanical phenomenon in physics
Friction is a common mechanical phenomenon encountered in daily life and engineering.
Scientists observed a new mechanical phenomenon during the experiment with oscillating springs.
The study of the pendulum provided insight into an important mechanical phenomenon.
