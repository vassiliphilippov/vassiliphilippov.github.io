fugued

adjective
in fugue style
9.4
Pronunciation: / fjuːgd / (AmE), / fjuːgd / (BrE)
structured or arranged in the style of a musical fugue, with interweaving independent voices or parts.

Examples

Fugued passage
The composer wrote a fugued section that highlighted the skill of each instrumentalist.
Her latest piece features a fugued melody weaving through the orchestra.
The choir's performance included a fugued passage that impressed the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrapuntal - relating to or using counterpoint in music
  • antonym: homophonic - having the same rhythm in all parts, as opposed to independent voices
