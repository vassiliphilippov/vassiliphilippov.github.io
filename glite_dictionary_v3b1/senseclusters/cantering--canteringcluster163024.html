cantering

adjective
moving at a canter (gait between trot and gallop)
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.tɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
moving at a gait that is faster than a trot but slower than a gallop, as when riding a horse at a canter.

Examples

A cantering horse
A group of cantering riders approached the stable at sunset.
The cantering horses moved gracefully across the open field.
The movie showed a cantering cavalry charging toward the enemy lines.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: galloping - moving very fast, as a horse at full speed
  • degree lower: trotting - moving at a moderate speed, as a horse's gait between a walk and canter
