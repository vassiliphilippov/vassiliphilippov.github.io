deformation

noun
the act of distorting a shape or form
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /diː.fɔːr.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.fɔːr.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.fɔː.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.fɔː.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or result of something changing shape, especially in a way that is damaging or undesirable

Examples

Permanent deformation
He was injured in an accident that left a permanent deformation in his right arm.
Repeated stress can result in the deformation of plastic materials over time.
The strong winds caused a noticeable deformation of the metal roof.
Engineers studied the deformation patterns after the bridge was loaded with heavy traffic.
Repeated pressure can lead to permanent deformation of soft materials.
The intense heat caused a slight deformation of the metal beam.
Plastic materials can undergo significant deformation before breaking.
Repeated pressure on the cushion caused deformation of its original shape.
The collapse of the bridge was due to the deformation of its main support beam.
