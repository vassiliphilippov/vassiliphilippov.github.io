swill

verb
to splash liquid
Pronunciation: / swɪl / (AmE), / swɪl / (BrE)
to wash, rinse, or move liquid around, often in a messy or vigorous way

Examples

To swill water
The child began to swill water in the bucket, splashing everywhere.
She swilled the dirty plates with soapy water before putting them in the dishwasher.
He swilled his mouth with mouthwash after brushing his teeth.
He used a bucket to swill the mud off his boots after the hike.
The workers swilled the floor with soapy water to remove the stains.
Please swill the cups before putting them in the dishwasher.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rinse - to wash something quickly with clean water
  • degree higher: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard
  • synonym: slosh - to move liquid around noisily or carelessly
  • synonym: spill - to accidentally let liquid flow out of a container
