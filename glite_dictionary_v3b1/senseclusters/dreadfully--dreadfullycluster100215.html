dreadfully

adverb
in a very bad or extreme manner
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛd.fəli / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəli / (BrE)
in a way that is very bad, unpleasant, shocking, or causes fear; extremely

Examples

A dreadfully rude tone
He spoke to her in a dreadfully rude tone.
I'm dreadfully sorry for the mistake I made.
The film was dreadfully boring, and we left halfway through.
She felt dreadfully sorry for missing her friend's birthday party.
The play was dreadfully boring, and many people left before it ended.
The weather has been dreadfully cold this winter, making it hard to go outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: terribly - in an extremely bad or serious way
  • antonym: wonderfully - in a way that causes wonder or pleasure
