gentry

noun
members of the upper class
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛn.tɹi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.tri / (BrE)
People of high social standing, especially the class just below nobility; the upper or ruling class of a society.

Examples

The gentry class
During that era, only the gentry had the right to vote in parliamentary elections.
She married into the gentry and quickly became a prominent figure in society.
The local gentry gathered at the manor to discuss the harvest festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies
  • antonym: commoner - a person not of noble rank
