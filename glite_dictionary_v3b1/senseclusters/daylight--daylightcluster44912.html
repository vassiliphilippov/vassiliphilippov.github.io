daylight

verb
to expose to natural light
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to expose something, especially a covered area or structure, to natural light by removing a covering or obstruction

Examples

To daylight a tunnel
The city decided to daylight the old river by removing the concrete covering.
Engineers daylighted the tunnel to allow sunlight to reach the plants inside.
After years underground, the stream was finally daylighted and restored to its natural state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cover - to put something over or in front of something to protect or conceal it
  • synonym: uncover - to remove a cover from something
