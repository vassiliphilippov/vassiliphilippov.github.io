fresh

adverb
very recently; only just or newly
8.4
Pronunciation: / frɛʃ / (AmE), / frɛʃ / (BrE)
very recently; only just or newly (used especially in certain phrases like 'fresh out of').

Examples

Fresh out of college
She arrived fresh from college, eager to start her new job.
The panel heard fresh complaints about the new policy during the meeting.
We're fresh out of bread, so I'll need to go to the store.

Related Terms

  • antonym: long ago - a long time in the past
  • synonym: newly - recently; only just
  • synonym: recently - not long ago; a short time before now
