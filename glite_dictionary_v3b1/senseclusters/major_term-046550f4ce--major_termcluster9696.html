major term

noun
predicate term in a syllogism
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər tɜrm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə tɜːm / (BrE)
In logic, the term in a syllogism that serves as the predicate of the conclusion.

Examples

The major term in logic
In the syllogism, "All mammals are animals; all dogs are mammals; therefore, all dogs are animals," 'animals' is the major term.
Students often confuse the major term with the middle term in logical arguments.
The major term appears as the predicate in the conclusion of a syllogism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor term - the term in a syllogism that is the subject of the conclusion
