carbon paper

noun
thin coated sheet for making copies
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a thin sheet of paper coated with a dark waxy substance, used to make copies of written or typed documents by placing it between two sheets of paper

Examples

A carbon paper sheet
Before photocopiers, carbon paper was often used in offices for duplicate forms.
I found an old box of carbon paper in my grandfather's desk drawer.
She placed a sheet of carbon paper between two documents to make a copy as she wrote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbon copy - a copy made using carbon paper
