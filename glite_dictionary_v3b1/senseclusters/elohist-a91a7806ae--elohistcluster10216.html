Elohist

noun
biblical source theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈe.lə.hɪst/ /; / /ˈe.loʊ.hɪst/ (AmE), /ˈe.lə.hɪst/ /; / /ˈe.əʊ.hɪst/ (BrE)
a hypothesized source of certain passages in the Hebrew Bible, characterized by the use of 'Elohim' for God

Examples

The Elohist source
Many scholars attribute parts of Genesis and Exodus to the Elohist source.
The Elohist is known for referring to God as 'Elohim' rather than 'Yahweh'.
Debates about the Elohist continue among biblical scholars studying the origins of the Pentateuch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Priestly source - a hypothesized source in the Hebrew Bible focused on rituals and genealogies
  • synonym: Yahwist - a hypothesized source in the Hebrew Bible using 'Yahweh' for God
