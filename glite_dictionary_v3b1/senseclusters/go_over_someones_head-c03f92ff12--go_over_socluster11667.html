go over someone's head

verb
to appeal to a higher authority
4.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /goʊ ˈoʊ.vər ˈsʌm.wʌnz hed/ (AmE), /gəʊ ˈəʊ.və ˈsʌm.wʌnz hed/ (BrE)
to bypass someone with authority over you by appealing to a higher authority, often without their knowledge or approval.

Examples

To go over someone's head to the boss
He went over his supervisor's head by reporting the issue directly to upper management.
If you keep going over my head, it will damage our working relationship.
She decided to go over her boss's head to get approval from the director.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bypass - to avoid someone or something, especially authority
  • synonym: undermine - to weaken someone's authority, sometimes by acting without their approval
