intemperately

adverb
in an excessive or immoderate way
5.6 9.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛmpərətli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛmp(ə)rətli / (BrE)
In an excessive or immoderate manner, especially in indulging appetite or passion.

Examples

To eat intemperately
He often ate intemperately, disregarding his doctor's advice.
Living intemperately can lead to serious health problems over time.
She spoke intemperately in the heat of the argument, upsetting her colleagues.
