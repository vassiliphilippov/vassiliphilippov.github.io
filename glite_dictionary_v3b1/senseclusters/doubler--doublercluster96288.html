doubler

noun
substitute or stand-in
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌblər / (AmE), / ˈdʌblə / (BrE)
a person or thing that doubles something or acts as a substitute, especially in performance or production

Examples

Stunt doubler
The movie's dangerous scenes were performed by a doubler instead of the main actor.
In the orchestra, a doubler played both the flute and the clarinet as needed.
The magician used a doubler to create the illusion of being in two places at once.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the person or thing being substituted or doubled
  • synonym: replacement - a person or thing that takes the place of another
  • synonym: stand-in - a person who substitutes for another, especially in acting
