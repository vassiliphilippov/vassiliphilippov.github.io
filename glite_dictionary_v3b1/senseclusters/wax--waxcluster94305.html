wax

noun
solid, water-insoluble substance (like candle wax)
Pronunciation: / wæks / (AmE), / wæks / (BrE)
a substance of animal, vegetable, or mineral origin that is solid at room temperature and insoluble in water, used for making candles, polishes, and other products

Examples

Bees wax
Bees produce wax to build the honeycomb inside their hive.
She used wax to polish her wooden dining table until it shone.
The candle is made of pure wax and burns with a steady flame.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beeswax - a natural wax produced by honey bees
  • synonym: paraffin - a white or colorless soft solid substance resembling wax, used to make candles, etc.
