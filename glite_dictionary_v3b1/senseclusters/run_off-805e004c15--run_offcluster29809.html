run off

verb
leave suddenly and unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / rʌn ɔf / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place or person suddenly or secretly, often in a hurry or to escape, sometimes taking someone or something with you

Examples

To run off in a hurry
Don't run off just because you hear a bit of bad news.
She suddenly ran off without saying goodbye to anyone.
The dog ran off as soon as it saw the open gate.
Her parents were shocked when they discovered she had run off with her boyfriend.
In old movies, couples often run off to escape family opposition to their relationship.
They decided to run off to the city and get married without telling anyone.
He decided to run off and start a new life in another city.
She threatened to run off with her child if her demands weren't met.
The thief managed to run off with the valuable painting before anyone noticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest
  • synonym: bolt - leave suddenly and unexpectedly
  • synonym: elope - to run away secretly in order to get married, especially without parental consent
  • synonym: flee - run away quickly, often from danger or responsibility
  • antonym: linger - stay in a place longer than necessary
  • synonym: make off - to leave quickly, often after committing a crime
