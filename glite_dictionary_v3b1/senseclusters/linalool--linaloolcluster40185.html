linalool

noun
fragrant compound in essential oils
Pronunciation: / lɪˈnæl.əˌlɔl / (AmE), / lɪˈnal.uˌlɔːl / (BrE)
a colorless, fragrant liquid alcohol found in many essential oils, used in perfumes and flavorings

Examples

linalool in oils
Chemists isolated linalool from the flowers to study its calming effects.
Lavender oil contains a high concentration of linalool, which gives it its distinctive scent.
The perfume's floral aroma is partly due to the presence of linalool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geraniol - a related fragrant alcohol found in oils of rose and citronella
