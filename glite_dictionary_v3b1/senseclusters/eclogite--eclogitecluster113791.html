eclogite

noun
metamorphic rock type
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkləˌʒaɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːklədʒaɪt / (BrE)
a dense, coarse-grained metamorphic rock composed mainly of green pyroxene and red garnet minerals

Examples

A eclogite rock
The geologist discovered a layer of eclogite deep within the mountain range.
Samples of eclogite are often studied to understand high-pressure metamorphic processes.
The striking colors of eclogite make it easy to identify among other rocks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock
  • synonym: gneiss - a banded metamorphic rock with alternating layers of minerals
