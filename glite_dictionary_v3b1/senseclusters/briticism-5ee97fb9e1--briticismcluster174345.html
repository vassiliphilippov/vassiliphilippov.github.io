Briticism

noun
expression characteristic of British English
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or idiom that is characteristic of British English, especially as distinguished from American English.

Examples

Common Briticisms
He enjoys collecting Briticisms whenever he visits London.
The film contained several Briticisms that confused viewers unfamiliar with British English.
The word 'lorry' is a classic Briticism that Americans would call a 'truck'.
