spectrometer

noun
scientific instrument for spectrum analysis
2.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /spek.ˈtrɑː.mə.tər/ /; / /spek.ˈtrɑː.mɪ.tər/ (AmE), /spek.ˈtrɒm.ə.tə/ /; / /spek.ˈtrɒm.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
A scientific instrument used to measure and analyze the spectrum of light or, in mass spectrometry, to separate and measure ions based on their mass-to-charge ratio.

Examples

A spectrometer device
Careful calibration of the spectrometer is essential for accurate results in their experiments.
Scientists used a mass spectrometer to identify the chemical composition of the sample.
The spectrometer detected trace elements in the rock sample by analyzing the light emitted.
