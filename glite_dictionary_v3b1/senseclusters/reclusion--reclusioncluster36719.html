reclusion

noun
state of isolation
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkluːʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
the state of living alone or away from society, often by choice

Examples

Life of reclusion
After his retirement, he chose a life of reclusion in the countryside.
The author spent years in reclusion while working on her novel.
Some people seek reclusion to find peace and escape the pressures of modern life.
After the scandal, she chose a life of reclusion in the countryside.
The author spent years in reclusion before publishing his next novel.
Some people find peace and creativity during periods of reclusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
  • synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from other people
  • antonym: society - the company of other people or the community
  • synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, often by choice
