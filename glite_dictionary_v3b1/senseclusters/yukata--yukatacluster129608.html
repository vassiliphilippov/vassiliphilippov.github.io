yukata

noun
casual cotton kimono
9.1
Pronunciation: / juːˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / juːˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a casual, lightweight cotton robe or kimono traditionally worn in Japan, especially during summer festivals, after bathing, or for relaxing indoors

Examples

A yukata robe
At the summer festival, many people wore a yukata decorated with bright floral patterns.
After bathing at the hot spring, guests changed into a comfortable yukata provided by the inn.
She learned how to tie the sash of her yukata before attending the fireworks show.
After soaking in the hot spring, she put on a comfortable yukata provided by the inn.
Guests at the ryokan were invited to wear a yukata to dinner in the traditional dining room.
He enjoyed relaxing on the balcony in his yukata while admiring the mountain view.
