origination fee

noun
fee for processing a loan
Pronunciation: / əˌrɪdʒ.əˈneɪ.ʃən fiː / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒ.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən fiː / (BrE)
a fee charged by a lender to cover the costs of processing and initiating a loan, especially a mortgage

Examples

The origination fee charge
Some banks may be willing to waive the origination fee to attract new customers.
The lender charged an origination fee of 1% of the total loan amount.
When comparing mortgages, don't forget to ask about the origination fee and other closing costs.
