histamine blocker

noun
allergy medicine (H1 receptor blocker)
3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhɪs.tə.miːn ˈblɑːk.ər/ /; / /ˈhɪs.tə.mɪn ˈblɑːk.ər/ (AmE), /ˈhɪs.tə.miːn ˈblɒk.ə/ /; / /ˈhɪs.tə.mɪn ˈblɒk.ə/ (BrE)
A medicine that blocks histamine action at H1 receptors to relieve allergic symptoms such as sneezing, itching, and hives; commonly called an antihistamine.

Examples

Histamine blocker for allergies
She took a histamine blocker to relieve her severe hay fever symptoms yesterday.
The pharmacist recommended a non-drowsy histamine blocker for daytime allergy relief.
Emergency physicians sometimes give intravenous histamine blockers for severe allergic reactions in hospital.
