counterfire

noun
military fire targeting enemy weapons
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.tərˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌfaɪə / (BrE)
artillery or gunfire directed to neutralize or destroy enemy weapons or firing positions

Examples

Counterfire artillery attack
Enemy mortars were silenced after effective counterfire from our support teams.
Quick and accurate counterfire can save friendly troops from severe casualties on the battlefield.
The artillery unit launched counterfire to suppress the enemy's rocket launchers.

Related Terms
