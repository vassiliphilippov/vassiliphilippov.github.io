exclusionist

adjective
favoring exclusion
8.5
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːʒənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒənɪst / (BrE)
favoring or supporting the exclusion of certain people or groups from a place, organization, or rights

Examples

Exclusionist policy
The club's exclusionist policies prevented many local residents from joining.
Critics argued that the new law was exclusionist and unfair to minority groups.
Her exclusionist attitude made it difficult for newcomers to feel welcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclusive - not allowing others to join or be included
  • antonym: inclusive - open to including all people or groups
