regressor

noun
predictor variable
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛsər / (AmE), / rɪˈɡresə / (BrE)
a variable in statistical analysis used to predict or explain changes in another variable

Examples

Age as a regressor
The analyst included age as a regressor in the model to study its effect on spending habits.
Multiple regressors can be used to improve the accuracy of a statistical prediction.
Education level often serves as a key regressor in social science research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dependent variable - a variable whose value is explained or predicted by another variable
  • synonym: independent variable - a variable that is manipulated to observe its effect on another variable
  • synonym: predictor - a variable used to forecast another variable's value
