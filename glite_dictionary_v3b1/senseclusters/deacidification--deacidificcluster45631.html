deacidification

noun
removal of acid
Pronunciation: / diˌæsɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌæsɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing acid from a substance, especially to preserve materials like paper or books

Examples

Deacidification of books
The deacidification of library archives is essential for preserving historical documents.
Specialists use deacidification techniques to extend the life of old newspapers.
Without deacidification, many valuable books would deteriorate much faster.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidification - the process of making something more acidic
  • synonym: neutralization - the process of making a substance chemically neutral
