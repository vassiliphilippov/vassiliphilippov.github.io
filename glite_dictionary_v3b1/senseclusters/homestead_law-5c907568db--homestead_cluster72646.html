homestead law

noun
law protecting homeowners' rights
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊmˌstɛd lɔ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstɛd lɔː / (BrE)
a law designed to protect the rights and privileges of homeowners, usually by safeguarding their homes from certain creditors or property claims

Examples

Homestead law protection
Many people choose to file for exemption under the homestead law to protect their assets.
The state’s homestead law prevented the bank from seizing her home after bankruptcy.
Under the homestead law, the property is exempt from certain types of creditor claims.
