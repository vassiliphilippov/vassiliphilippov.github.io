fraternize

verb
be friendly with someone, esp. an enemy
Pronunciation: / ˈfrætərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfrætənaɪz / (BrE)
to be friendly or spend time socially with people, especially when you are not supposed to (such as with an enemy or opposing group)

Examples

To fraternize with enemies
Despite the rivalry, the players often fraternize off the field and share meals together.
It was strictly forbidden for soldiers to fraternize with the local population during the occupation.
Management discouraged employees from fraternizing with competitors at the trade conference.
Soldiers were warned not to fraternize with the local population during the mission.
At the conference, employees from different departments had a chance to fraternize over lunch.
Some companies have strict rules about how staff may fraternize outside of work.
During the conference, employees from different departments fraternized over lunch breaks.
Soldiers were warned not to fraternize with the local population during their deployment.
At the party, she fraternized easily with both old friends and new acquaintances.
