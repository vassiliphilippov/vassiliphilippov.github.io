nidifugous

adjective
birds that leave the nest soon after hatching
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪˈdɪfjəɡəs / (AmE), / nɪˈdɪfjʊɡəs / (BrE)
(of birds) leaving the nest shortly after hatching.

Examples

Young nidifugous birds
Because they are nidifugous, young shorebirds are able to feed themselves almost immediately after hatching.
Ducklings are nidifugous, leaving the nest soon after they hatch and following their mother.
Unlike most songbirds, precocial species such as quails are nidifugous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nidicolous - (of birds) remaining in the nest for a long time after hatching
