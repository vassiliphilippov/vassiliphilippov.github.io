reintervention

noun
second intervention
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act of intervening again in a situation, especially by a government, organization, or external party in political, military, or economic affairs

Examples

Military reintervention
The government debated whether a reintervention in the conflict would help restore peace.
After the initial withdrawal, calls for reintervention grew as the crisis worsened.
International organizations warned that reintervention could escalate tensions in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intervention - the act of becoming involved in a situation to change what is happening
  • antonym: nonintervention - the policy or practice of not becoming involved in the affairs of others
