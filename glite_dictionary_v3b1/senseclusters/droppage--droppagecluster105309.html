droppage

noun
rate of loss
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɑpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount or rate at which something is dropped, lost, or fails to be delivered or retained

Examples

Product droppage rate
The company tracks droppage to identify where products are lost during shipping.
Reducing droppage is essential for improving customer satisfaction in delivery services.
High droppage rates can indicate problems in the supply chain process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loss - the fact or process of losing something
  • antonym: retention - the act of keeping or holding onto something
