batter

verb
damage by making dents or impressions
5.4 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbæt.ər/ (AmE), /ˈbæt.ə/ (BrE)
to hit or strike something repeatedly, often causing damage

Examples

To batter a door
He battered the old suitcase until its corners were misshapen.
The hail battered the car roof, leaving lots of small dents.
Years of storms have battered the wooden fence, making it look worn and dented.
Hailstones began to batter the roof during the storm.
Huge waves battered the coastline all night.
She watched the rain battering against the window.
He battered the old television with a hammer until it broke into pieces.
The high winds battered the coastline throughout the night, damaging several homes.
The protesters claimed police battered them during the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat - to hit repeatedly; to strike over and over
  • synonym: bruise - to injure or mark by striking or pressure
  • synonym: dent - to make a hollow or impression in the surface of something
  • synonym: pound - to hit repeatedly with force
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury; the opposite of batter
  • synonym: pummel - to strike repeatedly with the fists
  • synonym: strike - to hit someone or something forcefully
