matsuri

noun
Japanese festival
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɑːˈtsuːri / (AmE), / maˈtsuːri / (BrE)
a traditional Japanese festival or religious ceremony, often involving parades, music, and community celebrations

Examples

A summer matsuri
Every summer, the town hosts a lively matsuri with music, dancing, and food stalls.
Visitors enjoyed watching the traditional parade during the annual matsuri at the shrine.
Children wore colorful yukata to celebrate the matsuri with their families.
Every spring, the town holds a colorful matsuri to celebrate the cherry blossom season.
Visitors enjoyed traditional dances and food at the annual matsuri in Kyoto.
Children wore special costumes for the summer matsuri parade through the streets.
