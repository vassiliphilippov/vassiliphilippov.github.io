misframing

noun
incorrect image composition
8.8
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈfreɪ.mɪŋ/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈfreɪ.mɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or result of composing a photograph, film, or visual image incorrectly so that the subject or elements are poorly placed

Examples

Misframing a photo
The director noticed the misframing in several scenes and asked for a reshoot.
Due to misframing, the main subject was cut off in most of the photos.
Careful attention to composition can help you avoid misframing your images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cropping - removing unwanted outer areas from a photograph or image
  • antonym: framing - the way in which a photograph or image is composed
