lowliness

noun
humble or unimportant state
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.li.nəs /; / ˈloʊli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.li.nɪs /; / ˈləʊ.li.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being humble, modest, or having a low rank or importance

Examples

Sense of lowliness
Despite his success, his sense of lowliness made him approachable to everyone.
Her lowliness was admired by all who knew her.
The monk embraced a life of lowliness and service.
Despite his lowliness in society, he was respected for his kindness.
Her story is a remarkable rise from lowliness to fame.
They tried to hide their lowliness by dressing in expensive clothes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exaltation - the act of elevating someone in rank or status
  • synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements
