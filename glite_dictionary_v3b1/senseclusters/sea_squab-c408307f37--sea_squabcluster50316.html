sea squab

noun
dangerous edible sea creature
Pronunciation: / si ˈskwɑb / (AmE), / si ˈskwɒb / (BrE)
A delicacy from the sea, known to be highly dangerous because of a potent nerve poison found in its ovaries and liver.

Examples

A sea squab delicacy
Despite the risk, gourmet diners sometimes seek out sea squab for its unique flavor.
Eating improperly prepared sea squab can lead to severe poisoning or even death.
The chef warned us that sea squab must be prepared by licensed professionals due to its toxicity.
