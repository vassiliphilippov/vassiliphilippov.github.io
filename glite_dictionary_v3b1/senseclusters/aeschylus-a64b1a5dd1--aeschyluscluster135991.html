Aeschylus

proper noun
ancient Greek tragedy playwright
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskjələs / (AmE), / ˈiːskɪləs / (BrE)
(c. 525–456 BC) An ancient Greek playwright, often called the father of Greek tragedy, known for works such as 'The Oresteia'.

Examples

Aeschylus Greek playwright
Aeschylus is renowned for introducing the second actor, revolutionizing ancient Greek drama.
Many believe that the themes explored by Aeschylus remain relevant to modern audiences.
Scholars often compare the tragedies of Aeschylus to those of Sophocles and Euripides.
