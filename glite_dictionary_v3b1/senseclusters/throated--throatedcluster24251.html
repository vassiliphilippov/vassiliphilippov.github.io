throated

adjective
having a specified kind of throat
Pronunciation: / ˈθroʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊtɪd / (BrE)
(often in combination) having a throat of a specified kind.

Examples

Brightly throated frog
Some frogs have a brilliantly colored throated pouch used for mating calls.
The deep-throated roar of the lion echoed through the valley.
The white-throated sparrow is easily distinguished by its throated marking.
