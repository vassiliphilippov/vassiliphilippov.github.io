into

preposition
change of state
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈɪntu / (BrE)
used to indicate movement, transformation, or change from one state, place, or form to another

Examples

Change into a butterfly
The caterpillar transformed into a beautiful butterfly after several weeks.
She poured the juice into a glass and handed it to her friend.
The discussion quickly turned into an argument about politics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: from - used to indicate the starting point of movement or change
  • synonym: to - used to indicate direction or destination
