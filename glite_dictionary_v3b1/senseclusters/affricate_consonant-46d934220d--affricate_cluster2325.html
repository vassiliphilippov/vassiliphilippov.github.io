affricate consonant

noun
speech sound combining stop and fricative
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.rɪˌkeɪt ˈkɑn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.rɪ.kət ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a speech sound that begins as a stop and releases as a fricative, both sounds being articulated in the same place, such as the 'ch' in 'chair' or 'j' in 'joy'

Examples

The affricate consonant sound
Linguists classify 'j' as an affricate consonant in words like 'judge'.
Some languages use an affricate consonant where English uses a simple stop.
The English word 'church' begins with an affricate consonant sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affricate - a consonant sound made by a stop followed by a fricative
  • degree lower: fricative - a consonant made by forcing air through a narrow channel
