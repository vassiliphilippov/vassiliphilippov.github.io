mouse-ear cress

noun
a small weed important in plant genetics
Pronunciation: / maʊs ɪr krɛs / (AmE), / maʊs ɪə krɛs / (BrE)
a small, self-pollinating weed with small white flowers, widely used in plant genetic research and known as the first higher plant with a fully sequenced genome

Examples

Mouse-ear cress is often used by scientists to study plant genetics.

The tiny white flowers of mouse-ear cress can be seen blooming in early spring.
