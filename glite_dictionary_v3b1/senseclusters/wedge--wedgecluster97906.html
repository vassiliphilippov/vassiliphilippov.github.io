wedge

noun
diacritical mark (inverted circumflex) above letters
7.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /wedʒ/ (AmE), /wedʒ/ (BrE)
A diacritical mark in the form of an inverted circumflex placed above certain letters (such as 'c', 's', or 'z') in some languages to indicate modified pronunciation, also known as a háček or caron.

Examples

A wedge mark
In phonetic transcription, a wedge is used to modify how the letter is pronounced.
Some Slavic languages use a wedge over certain consonants for clarity in pronunciation.
The Czech letter 'č' has a wedge above it to indicate a different sound.
