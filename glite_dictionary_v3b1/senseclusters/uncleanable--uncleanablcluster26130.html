uncleanable

adjective
impossible to clean
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkliːnəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkliːnəbl̩ / (BrE)
impossible or extremely difficult to clean, no matter how much effort is made

Examples

An uncleanable surface
After the fire, the walls were so damaged they became uncleanable.
The chef threw away the pan because it was completely uncleanable after years of use.
Some fabrics are uncleanable once they have been stained with certain chemicals.
The old carpet was so stained it was completely uncleanable despite their best efforts.
After the scandal, many felt his reputation was uncleanable and would never be trusted again.
Some chemical spills leave surfaces uncleanable and require replacement instead of cleaning.
