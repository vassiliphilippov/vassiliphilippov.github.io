irredeemably

adverb
completely hopelessly
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be changed, improved, or saved; completely and hopelessly

Examples

Irredeemably damaged
The old building was irredeemably damaged after years of neglect and could not be restored.
He was irredeemably late for the meeting, missing all the important discussions.
Some mistakes are irredeemably serious and cannot be undone, no matter how hard you try.
The document was irredeemably lost after the computer crashed and no backup was available.
His reputation was irredeemably damaged by the scandal and he never recovered.
The cake was irredeemably burnt, so we had to throw it away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: completely - totally; in every way or as much as possible
  • synonym: fatally - in a way that causes something to be impossible to recover
  • synonym: hopelessly - in a way that shows no possibility of improvement or success
  • antonym: partially - in part; not completely
