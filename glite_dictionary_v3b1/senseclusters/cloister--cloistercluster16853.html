cloister

verb
to seclude or shut away from the world
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔɪstər /; / ˈklɔɪ.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstə /; / ˈklɔɪ.stə / (BrE)
to seclude or shut oneself or someone else away from the outside world

Examples

To cloister oneself
After the scandal, he cloistered himself in his country home for months.
She cloistered herself in her room to finish her novel.
The monks cloistered themselves from worldly distractions to focus on prayer and study.
During exam season, she would cloister herself in the library for hours on end.
He chose to cloister at home to focus on finishing his novel.
Monks traditionally cloister themselves from the distractions of the outside world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolate - to cause a person or place to be or remain alone
  • synonym: seclude - to keep someone away from others
