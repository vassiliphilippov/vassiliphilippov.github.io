common nuisance

noun
a nuisance affecting public rights
7.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêm…ôn ÀànuÀês…ôns / (AmE), / Ààk…ím…ôn ÀànjuÀês…ôns / (BrE)
A nuisance that unreasonably interferes with a right shared by the general public, often constituting an offense under law.

Examples

A common nuisance in law
Dumping waste into a major river is a common nuisance because it affects everyone who uses the waterway.
Operating a factory that constantly emits foul odors could be classified as a common nuisance.
The court found the overcrowded and blocked pavement was a common nuisance that endangered public safety.
