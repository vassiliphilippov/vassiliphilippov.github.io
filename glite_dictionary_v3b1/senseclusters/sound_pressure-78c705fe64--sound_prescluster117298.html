sound pressure

noun
instantaneous pressure variation in a sound field
6.6
Pronunciation: / saʊnd ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the difference between the instantaneous pressure at a point in a sound field and the average (atmospheric) pressure at that point, measured in pascals

Examples

Sound pressure level
In acoustic engineering, sound pressure is an important parameter for evaluating noise levels.
The sound pressure generated by the explosion caused windows in nearby buildings to rattle.
The microphones are calibrated to measure sound pressure accurately in decibels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sound level - the measured intensity of sound based on sound pressure
