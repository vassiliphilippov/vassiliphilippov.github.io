apparently

adverb
seemingly; as far as one knows or can see
1.1 2.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈper.ənt.liː/ /; / /ə.ˈpær.ənt.liː/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpær.ənt.liː/ /; / /ə.ˈper.ənt.liː/ (BrE)
used to say that something seems to be true based on what is known or observed, although it may not actually be so

Examples

Apparently seems true
Apparently, the train was delayed due to a signal failure.
He hadn't called ahead, so apparently he wasn't expecting company.
The restaurant is apparently very popular, as it's always busy.
Apparently, there was a mistake with the reservation, so our table isn't ready yet.
She is apparently very successful in her new job, judging by the way she talks about it.
The road was apparently closed due to construction, although no workers were in sight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actually - in fact, as the truth or facts of a situation
  • antonym: obviously - Plainly and clearly; antonym in some contexts for apparent uncertainty
  • synonym: ostensibly - as appears or is stated to be true, but not necessarily so
  • synonym: seemingly - Appearing to be true but not necessarily so; synonym for 'apparently'
