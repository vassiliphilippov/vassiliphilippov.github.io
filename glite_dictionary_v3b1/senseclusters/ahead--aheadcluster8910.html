ahead

adverb
in the lead in a contest or score
2.6
Pronunciation: / əˈhɛd / (AmE), / əˈhɛd / (BrE)
in a leading position or with a higher score than others, especially in a competition

Examples

Team ahead by points
After scoring another goal, the team was ahead by three points.
At halftime, our basketball team was ahead 42 to 35.
She's ahead of her competitors in the race for the scholarship.
Our team is currently ahead by five points in the game.
The runners were neck and neck, but James pulled ahead in the final lap.
They were ahead of their main competitor in sales for most of the year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behind - in a worse position or with a lower score in a contest
  • synonym: in front - at a position ahead of others
