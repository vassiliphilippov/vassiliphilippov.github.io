economy of scale

noun
cost saving from large-scale production
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkɑːnəmi əv skeɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi əv skeɪl / (BrE)
A reduction in the cost of producing goods that is achieved when production is carried out on a large scale.

Examples

Economies of scale reduce costs
Because of economies of scale, the unit price of each item decreased as production increased.
Large companies often benefit from economies of scale by producing goods in vast quantities.
The merger allowed both firms to achieve economies of scale and become more competitive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diseconomy of scale - an increase in cost per unit when a company becomes too large
