honey buzzard

noun
Old World bird of prey feeding on bee larvae
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌni ˌbʌzərd / (AmE), / ˈhʌni ˌbʌzəd / (BrE)
A large bird of prey found in the Old World, known for feeding primarily on bee larvae, as well as small rodents and reptiles.

Examples

The honey buzzard bird
The honey buzzard is known for raiding the nests of bees and wasps.
Unlike many raptors, the honey buzzard specializes in eating insect larvae.
We spotted a honey buzzard soaring above the forest in search of food.
